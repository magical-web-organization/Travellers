Introduction to Nikko

Nestled in the Tochigi prefecture of Japan, Nikko is a small town rich in culture and history. Known for its UNESCO World Heritage Sites, Nikko boasts numerous shrines and temples that offer a glimpse into Japan’s past.

But Nikko isn’t just about ancient architecture. The town is surrounded by scenic mountain trails, waterfalls, and hot springs, making it an ideal destination for outdoor enthusiasts. This travel guide covers everything you need to know to plan your perfect trip to Nikko.

List of Places to Visit in Nikko

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Toshogu Shrine: The most famous shrine in Nikko, Toshogu is dedicated to Tokugawa Ieyasu, the founder of the Tokugawa shogunate that ruled Japan for over 250 years.
  • Futarasan Shrine: This shrine is dedicated to the three deities of Nikko’s mountains: Mount Nantai, Mount Nyoho, and Mount Taro.
  • Rinno-ji Temple: One of the most important temples in Nikko, Rinno-ji houses three large statues of Buddhist deities.
  • Nikko Tamozawa Imperial Villa Memorial Park: This villa was a summer residence for the Japanese royal family and is now open to the public as a museum.

Natural Attractions of Nikko: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Kegon Falls: A 97-meter waterfall that is particularly stunning during autumn foliage season.
  • Senjogahara Marshlands: A picturesque area perfect for hiking that offers breathtaking views of Lake Chuzenji.
  • Mount Nantai: A dormant volcano that offers a challenging hike with stunning views of Nikko and Lake Chuzenji.
  • Kinugawa River: This river is a popular spot for onsen (hot spring) resorts and water activities like rafting and kayaking.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Nikko

Nikko has numerous hiking and biking opportunities to explore its beautiful scenery. Some of the best routes include:

  • The Oku-Nikko Area: This area offers many trails including the Odashirogahara Marsh Trail and the popular Mount Nasu Trek.
  • Lake Chuzenji: This lake has a 25-kilometer scenic road that circles it and is perfect for biking or walking.

Best Things to Do in Nikko: Activities and Tours

  • Visit an onsen: Nikko is famous for its hot springs, which can be enjoyed at various spas and resorts. Try Kinugawa Onsen, Nikko Yumoto Onsen, or Yunishigawa Onsen.
  • Participate in a tea ceremony: Nikko is a great place to learn about traditional Japanese tea ceremonies. Check out the Kanaya Hotel’s tea ceremony.
  • Take a day trip to Edo Wonderland: This theme park is dedicated to the Edo period of Japan and features samurai shows, ninja training, and traditional crafts.

Week-Long Itinerary for Nikko

Day 1: Visit Toshogu Shrine and explore the surrounding area. Day 2: Visit other shrines and temples in Nikko, like Futarasan Shrine and Rinno-ji Temple. Day 3: Take a scenic drive around Lake Chuzenji and visit Kegon Falls. Day 4: Hike Mount Nantai for a challenging but rewarding trek. Day 5: Relax in one of Nikko’s many onsen resorts. Day 6: Take a day trip to Edo Wonderland. Day 7: Explore the Oku-Nikko Area with a guided tour or rent a bicycle to explore the area on your own.

Food and Drink Scene of Nikko

Must-Try Dishes

  • Yuba: Soft tofu that is a local delicacy in Nikko.
  • Yumomi Soba: Soba noodles that are mixed in a bowl of ice water before eating, a traditional method of cooling the dish.
  • Shimotsukare: A dish made with hot soba noodles in a thick soup, often served with tempura.

Local Beverages

  • Nikko Beer: Microbrewery that offers a variety of craft beers.
  • Kozuchi Shochu: A traditional Japanese liquor made from sweet potatoes.

Where to Stay in Nikko: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Hotel Hoshino Yado: This luxurious hotel offers hot springs and an outdoor pool.
  • The Classic Hotel: A Western-style hotel in the heart of Nikko.
  • Nikkorisou Backpackers: A budget option that offers Japanese-style dormitory rooms.

What to Buy in Nikko: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Yuba: Soft tofu that is a local delicacy in Nikko.
  • Kamakura-bori: Hand-carved wooden boxes or items.
  • Yukata: Japanese casual summer clothing.

Local Culture and Customs of Nikko

Nikko is a traditional town, and it’s important to respect local customs. Dress modestly at shrines and temples, and remove your shoes before entering buildings. Greeting locals with a bow instead of a handshake is also appreciated.

Best Time to Visit Nikko

The best times to visit Nikko are in the autumn and spring. During autumn, the colorful foliage adds a breathtaking dimension to the historical sites, and in the spring, the cherry blossoms bloom and create a stunning atmosphere around the shrines and temples.

Do’s and Don’ts of Nikko

Do:

  • Respect local customs when visiting shrines and temples.
  • Try local foods and drinks.
  • Explore the outdoors with a guided tour.

Don’t:

  • Wear shoes inside traditional buildings.
  • Touch or disturb historical artifacts.
  • Speak too loudly or behave disrespectfully at shrines and temples.

Safety and Health

Safety in Nikko

Nikko is generally a safe destination. However, it’s always important to practice common sense safety measures, like using a lock for your valuables and being aware of your surroundings.

Health in Nikko

Nikko has many hot springs that have both physical and psychological benefits. However, it’s important to follow onsen etiquette, including taking a shower before entering the water.

Getting to Nikko: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Nikko

  • Narita International Airport (NRT): The largest airport in Japan, located 130 kilometers away from Nikko.
  • Haneda International Airport (HND): Located in Tokyo and 155 kilometer away from Nikko.

Bus Stations Near Nikko

  • Tobu Nikko Station: A major bus station that offers direct services to major cities in Japan.
  • JR Nikko Station: A small terminal with limited services but does have connections to other destinations in Japan.

Other Options

Train is the most efficient way to get to Nikko from Tokyo. The journey takes around two hours and fifteen minutes. Another option is to rent a car and drive yourself, which will take around three hours.

How to Get Around in Nikko

The best way to get around Nikko is by car, especially if you plan on exploring the surrounding mountains and forests. Alternatively, you can hire a bicycle, use taxis, or rely on public transportation.

Where to Go Next from Nikko: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Oya stone museum: Located in nearby Utsunomiya, this museum showcases unique Oya stone.
  • Sano Premium Outlets: A shopping center with luxury brands, located in Sano.
  • Kiryu: A city famous for its textile industry, located in Gunma prefecture.

Conclusion

Whether you’re interested in cultural heritage or outdoor adventures, Nikko has something to offer. Plan your visit carefully, follow local customs and etiquette, and you’re sure to leave with unforgettable memories of this historic town.
