Introduction to Kinugawa

Nestled in the Tochigi prefecture in Kanto, Kinugawa is a serene and tranquil escape from the bustling city of Tokyo. Known for its abundance of natural beauty, the stunning landscapes and mesmerizing views of Kinugawa make it a popular destination for tourists from all over the world. From adventurous outdoor activities to cultural and historical attractions, Kinugawa offers something for everyone.

List of Places to Visit in Kinugawa

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Kinugawa is replete with opportunities to get a taste of Japanese history and culture. Some of the most significant places to visit are:

  • Kinugawa Onsen Fureai Museum: Exhibiting the history and culture of Onsen culture in Kinugawa.
  • Kinugawa Kanaya Hotel History House: Learn about the rich history of Kinugawa Kanaya Hotel, built in 1873.
  • Nikko Tosho-gu Shrine: A UNESCO World Heritage Site, this shrine is famous for its elaborate carvings and decorations.
  • Futarasan Shrine: A Shinto shrine located near Nikko Tosho-gu Shrine, where visitors can learn about Japanese mythology.

Natural Attractions of Kinugawa: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Kinugawa offers a plethora of opportunities for outdoor activities, including:

  • Kinugawa River Boat Tour: A tranquil boat ride on the Kinugawa River offers a perfect chance to take in the scenic beauty of Kinugawa.
  • Kinugawa Valley: A beautiful valley that is popular for hiking in summer and viewing autumn leaves in the fall.
  • Kinugawa Onsen Ropeway: A cable car ride to the top of Mount Maruyama provides breathtaking views of Kinugawa Valley.
  • Kegon Falls: One of Japan’s three most beautiful waterfalls, Kegon Falls is an awe-inspiring destination that should not be missed.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Kinugawa

Kinugawa has a variety of hiking and biking trails for people of all skill levels:

  • Mount Maruyama: Hike to the top for stunning views or take a cable car.
  • Senjogahara: A beautiful plateau with gentle hills that offer easy to moderate hiking.
  • Ryuokyo Gorge Trail: A beautiful path through the stunning valley.
  • Kinugawa Valley Trail: A beautiful trail along the Kinugawa River, perfect for a relaxing bike ride.

Best Things to Do in Kinugawa: Activities and Tours

For those looking for more customized experiences, Kinugawa offers a range of activities and tours, including:

  • Onsen Hot Springs Experience: Enjoy a relaxing soak in one of many traditional hot springs in Kinugawa.
  • Nikko National Park Tour: Explore the stunning Nikko National Park and take in the beautiful sights of Mount Nantai, Lake Chuzenji, and Kegon Falls.
  • Kinugawa Traditional Crafts Workshop: Learn to make pottery, woodblock prints or calligraphy from expert instructors.
  • Kinugawa Wine and Cheese Tasting Tour: Sample local wines and cheeses in a beautiful and scenic setting.

Food and Drink Scene of Kinugawa

Must-Try Dishes

Kinugawa offers a range of delicious dishes made from fresh and local ingredients, including:

  • Yuba: A traditional Japanese dish made from tofu skin, served as sashimi or grilled.
  • Shojin Ryori: Vegetarian dishes that are typically prepared by Zen monks in Buddhist temples.
  • Ayu no taimeshi: Sweetfish served over rice.

Local Beverages

Kinugawa is famous for its sake, a traditional Japanese rice wine, but there are also delicious local beers and wines to enjoy.

Where to Stay in Kinugawa: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Kinugawa has a variety of accommodation options for all budgets, from the traditional Japanese-style Ryokans to modern hotels and guesthouses. Some popular options include:

  • Kinugawa Kanaya Hotel: A historic hotel that was built in 1873.
  • Yubaeri no Yado Kashiwaya: A beautiful Ryokan located on the banks of the Kinugawa River.
  • Kinugawa Onsen Hotel: A traditional Ryokan with rooms that offer beautiful views of Kinugawa Valley.
  • Nikko Station Hotel Classic: A modern hotel located near the Kinugawa Onsen Ropeway.

What to Buy in Kinugawa: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Kinugawa offers plenty of options for souvenirs, including:

  • Yuba: Take home some Yuba, a traditional Japanese delicacy.
  • Crafts: Bring home some pottery or calligraphy from the Kinugawa Traditional Crafts Workshop.
  • Sake: Take home some Kinugawa sake, a perfect gift for family and friends.

Local Culture and Customs of Kinugawa

One of the unique features of Kinugawa’s culture is its emphasis on Onsen hot springs culture. Guests are typically required to bathe naked in public baths, so it’s important to be aware of the customs and etiquette surrounding this practice.

Best Time to Visit Kinugawa

The best time to visit Kinugawa is during the autumn months of September to November when the leaves turn brilliant shades of red and orange. Spring, from March to May, is also an excellent time to visit when the cherry blossom trees are in bloom.

Do’s and Don’ts of Kinugawa

Do:

  • Be respectful of traditional Japanese customs and etiquette.
  • Take time to learn about the local culture and history.
  • Enjoy the natural beauty while being mindful of the environment.

Don’t:

  • Wear shoes indoors. Most places in Japan require visitors to remove their shoes before entering.
  • Be loud or disruptive in public areas.
  • Take pictures of people without their permission.

Safety and Health

Safety in Kinugawa

Japan is generally known for being a safe country for tourists, and Kinugawa is no exception. Just be mindful of your belongings, and always practice common sense when travelling.

Health in Kinugawa

Healthcare in Japan is generally excellent, and there are several medical facilities in Kinugawa. However, it’s important to have comprehensive travel insurance, since medical costs in Japan can be expensive.

Getting to Kinugawa: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Kinugawa

The nearest airport to Kinugawa is Narita International Airport, which is around 170 km away. You can take a train from Narita airport to Kinugawa, which takes around 2.5 hours.

Bus Stations Near Kinugawa

There are several bus services that run from Tokyo to Kinugawa, which take around 2.5 hours.

Other Options

You can also take a train from Tokyo to Kinugawa, which takes around 2 hours.

How to Get Around in Kinugawa

There are plenty of transportation options to get around Kinugawa, including buses, taxis, and rental cars. You can also rent bicycles to explore Kinugawa at your own leisure.

Where to Go Next from Kinugawa: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

There are several nearby destinations that are worth exploring, such as:

  • Nikko: A scenic mountain town that is home to several UNESCO World Heritage sites.
  • Kawaji Onsen: A small town famous for its hot springs and skiing opportunities.
  • Edo Wonderland Nikko Edomura: A popular theme park that recreates traditional Japanese culture and history.

Conclusion

Kinugawa is a must-visit destination for those looking for a break from the hustle and bustle of everyday life. With its combination of natural beauty, cultural attractions, and delicious local cuisine, Kinugawa offers a unique and unforgettable experience for tourists of all ages.
