Introduction to Tochigi (Prefecture)

Tochigi Prefecture is located in the Kanto region of Japan, known for its steaming onsen (hot springs), temples, shrines, and vast natural spaces. Home to some of the most awe-inspiring national parks, breathtaking waterfalls, and serene lakes, Tochigi Prefecture is a nature and adventure lover’s paradise. History buffs and culture enthusiasts also have plenty of options to see, with the area being rich in history and traditional Japanese culture.

Best Things to Do in Tochigi (Prefecture): Activities and Tours

Whether you want to explore the region’s natural beauty, delve into its history and culture, or indulge in some local culinary delights, Tochigi Prefecture has plenty to offer. Here are some of the best things to do:

  1. Visit the UNESCO World Heritage Site of Nikko Toshogu Shrine
  2. Soak in the hot springs of Kinugawa Onsen
  3. Admire the seasonal blooms at Ashikaga Flower Park
  4. Explore the underground world of Oya Stone Museum
  5. Take a tour of Sano Yakuyoke Daishi Temple

Local Culture and Customs of Tochigi (Prefecture)

Tochigi Prefecture offers a glimpse into traditional Japanese culture, with several festivals and traditions celebrated throughout the year. The area is also known for its art and craftwork, including pottery, lacquerware, and woodwork.

Best Time to Visit Tochigi (Prefecture)

Tochigi Prefecture can be enjoyed throughout the year, with each season offering its own unique charm. Spring is the best time to visit for the cherry blossom season, with the Ashikaga Flower Park offering a spectacular display of wisteria blooms. Fall is also an excellent time to visit, with vibrant autumn foliage in the region’s many parks and hiking trails.

Do’s and Don’ts of Tochigi (Prefecture)

Do:

  1. Do try the local delicacies and craft products.
  2. Do follow basic etiquette and respect local customs.
  3. Do carry cash, as some small towns and shops may not accept credit cards.

Don’t:

  1. Don’t litter or damage the natural surroundings.
  2. Don’t enter restricted areas in national parks and historical sites.
  3. Don’t be noisy in public places.

Safety and Health

Safety in Tochigi (Prefecture)

Tochigi Prefecture is generally safe, with low crime rates. It is always best to take basic safety precautions, such as securing valuables and avoiding deserted areas at night.

Health in Tochigi (Prefecture)

Tochigi Prefecture does not have any major health concerns, and medical facilities are readily available throughout the area.

How to Get Around in Tochigi (Prefecture)

Tochigi Prefecture offers various transportation options for getting around, including trains, buses, and taxis. Rental cars are also available, with English GPS navigation.
