Introduction to Ashikaga

Located in Tochigi prefecture in Kanto region, Ashikaga is a charming city that boasts a rich history, breathtaking natural beauty, and a vibrant cultural scene. It is a popular day trip destination from Tokyo, thanks to its convenient location and easy accessibility.

Ashikaga is best known for its iconic Ashikaga Flower Park, which draws visitors from all over the world, especially during the wisteria season in late April and early May. However, there is more to Ashikaga than just flowers. From historic temples and shrines to scenic hiking trails and mouth-watering cuisine, Ashikaga has something to offer for every traveler.

List of Places to Visit in Ashikaga

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Ashikaga School Museum: This museum is dedicated to the history of Ashikaga School, a renowned school of Confucian philosophy that was established in the city in the 17th century. The museum features exhibits on the school’s history, philosophy, and prominent alumni.

  • Banna-ji Temple: Founded in the 8th century, Banna-ji is a tranquil temple that is famous for its beautifully preserved wooden structures and serene garden.

  • Ashikaga Yoshiki Park: This park is home to the ruins of Ashikaga Yoshiki Castle, which was built in the 16th century. The castle’s hilltop location offers stunning views of the city, and the park is a popular spot for cherry blossom viewing in the spring.

Natural Attractions of Ashikaga: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Ashikaga Flower Park: This world-famous park is home to over 350 wisteria trees, which bloom in a riot of colors during the spring. The park also features other seasonal flowers, as well as a botanical garden, a maze, and various food and souvenir stalls.

  • Mount Kurobi: Rising to a height of 2,140 meters, Mount Kurobi is the highest peak in the Kanto region. Hiking to the summit takes around 7-8 hours, but the panoramic views of the surrounding mountains and valleys are well worth the effort.

  • Tambara Lavender Park: This park is a must-visit for anyone who loves lavender. Located in neighboring Numata city, it is home to around 50,000 lavender plants that bloom from late June to early August each year.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Ashikaga

  • Ashikaga Gakuen Trail: This hiking trail runs through the grounds of Ashikaga Gakuen, a prestigious high school that dates back to 1887. The trail is easy and suitable for all fitness levels, and offers scenic views of the school’s historic buildings and the surrounding forest.

  • Ashikaga Fudo Waterfall Trail: This hiking trail leads to the beautiful Ashikaga Fudo Waterfall, which is located in a lush valley deep in the mountains. The trail is moderately difficult and takes around 2 hours to complete round-trip.

Best Things to Do in Ashikaga: Activities and Tours

  • Wisteria Festival: Held annually from mid-April to mid-May at Ashikaga Flower Park, this festival is a celebration of the park’s iconic wisteria flowers. Visitors can enjoy various attractions and events, including light-up evenings, concerts, and traditional Japanese performances.

  • Asobi no Sato: This outdoor adventure park offers various activities such as bungee jumping, zip lining, ATV riding, and hot air balloon rides. It is a great place to challenge yourself and have fun, surrounded by nature.

Food and Drink Scene of Ashikaga

Must-Try Dishes

  • Ashikaga yaki: This local specialty is a savory pancake made with flour, eggs, cabbage, and other ingredients, topped with various toppings such as pork or seafood.

  • Nikudango: Often referred to as “Ashikaga’s soul food”, this dish consists of meatballs made with ground beef and pork, seasoned with garlic and ginger, then fried and served in a sweet and savory sauce.

  • Soba: Ashikaga is home to several soba (buckwheat noodle) restaurants, offering delicious and healthy noodle dishes.

Local Beverages

  • Ashikaga sake: Made with locally grown rice and fresh spring water, Ashikaga sake is known for its smooth and rich flavor.

  • Shochu: Similar to sake, shochu is a distilled alcohol that is popular in Japan. Ashikaga has several shochu distilleries that offer tours and tastings.

Where to Stay in Ashikaga: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Ashikaga Plaza Hotel: This modern hotel is conveniently located near Ashikaga Station, offering comfortable rooms and a variety of amenities, including a restaurant and a hot spring bath.

  • Ryokan Tanaka: This traditional Japanese inn is situated in a historic building that dates back to the Edo period. Guests can enjoy the authentic atmosphere, Japanese-style rooms, and delicious kaiseki-style meals.

  • Ashikaga City Hotel: This budget-friendly hotel is a good option for those who want to save money without compromising comfort. It features clean and cozy rooms, as well as a restaurant and a sauna.

What to Buy in Ashikaga: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Ashikaga yaki pottery: This unique pottery is made using a traditional technique that results in a distinctive texture and color.

  • Wisteria-themed products: From postcards and keychains to cosmetics and snacks, there is no shortage of wisteria-themed souvenirs in Ashikaga.

  • Local sake and shochu: Bring home a bottle of Ashikaga’s finest sake or shochu to share with friends and family.

Local Culture and Customs of Ashikaga

  • Respect for nature: Ashikaga is blessed with beautiful nature and greenery, and locals take pride in preserving and cherishing their environment. Show respect for nature by not littering, staying on hiking trails, and following park rules.

  • Onsen etiquette: Hot springs are a popular attraction in Ashikaga, and visitors are expected to follow proper onsen etiquette, such as washing themselves thoroughly before entering the bath and not wearing swimsuits.

Best Time to Visit Ashikaga

The best time to visit Ashikaga depends on what you want to see and experience. For wisteria viewing, the best time is late April and early May, during the Ashikaga Flower Park’s Wisteria Festival. For autumn foliage, the best time is in November, when the mountains surrounding Ashikaga turn into a sea of colors. For outdoor activities, such as hiking and camping, the best time is in the summer, when the weather is mild and pleasant.

Do’s and Don’ts of Ashikaga

Do:

  • Do take off your shoes when entering a traditional Japanese house or ryokan.

  • Do try local specialties such as Ashikaga yaki and Nikudango.

  • Do explore the city’s many temples and shrines.

Don’t:

  • Don’t eat or drink while walking on the street.

  • Don’t be loud or disruptive in public places.

  • Don’t tip at restaurants or hotels, as it is not a custom in Japan.

Safety and Health

Safety in Ashikaga

Ashikaga is considered a safe city with low crime rates. However, it is always a good idea to take common sense precautions, such as locking your valuables in your hotel room, being aware of your surroundings, and avoiding secluded areas at night.

Health in Ashikaga

Healthcare in Japan is generally of high quality and easily accessible. There are several hospitals and clinics in Ashikaga, and most doctors and medical staff speak at least basic English. It is recommended to have travel insurance that covers medical emergencies.

Getting to Ashikaga: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Ashikaga

The closest airport to Ashikaga is Tokyo’s Haneda Airport, which is about 2 hours away by train. Narita Airport is also an option, but it takes longer (around 3 hours) to get to Ashikaga.

Bus Stations Near Ashikaga

Ashikaga Station is the main bus station in the city, with regular connections to Tokyo, Nikko, and other nearby cities.

Other Options

The easiest way to get around Ashikaga is by train, as the city is served by the JR Ryomo Line and the Tobu Isesaki Line. It is also possible to rent a car and explore the surrounding countryside at your own pace.

How to Get Around in Ashikaga

The best way to get around Ashikaga is on foot, as many attractions and restaurants are located within walking distance of each other. Public transportation, such as buses and trains, is also available.

Where to Go Next from Ashikaga: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Nikko: Famous for its UNESCO-listed shrines and temples, as well as its scenic beauty, Nikko is a must-see destination for travelers to Japan.

  • Kinugawa Onsen: This hot spring resort town is located in the neighboring Gunma prefecture, and offers a wide range of outdoor activities, such as skiing, hiking, and rafting.

  • Mashiko: Known for its pottery tradition and rustic charm, Mashiko is a quaint town that is a great place to unwind and appreciate the beauty of rural Japan.

Conclusion

Ashikaga is a hidden gem that offers a blend of nature, culture, and adventure. Whether you are interested in hiking to mountain peaks or soaking in a hot spring, visiting historic temples or savoring local cuisine, Ashikaga has something to offer for everyone. With this comprehensive travel guide, you can plan the perfect trip to Ashikaga and make memories that will last a lifetime.
