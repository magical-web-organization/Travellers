Introduction to Kirifuri Highlands

Kirifuri Highlands is a stunning natural wonder located in Tochigi, Japan. This is a perfect destination for those who want to get away from the hustle and bustle of the city and immerse themselves in nature. Kirifuri Highlands is rich in natural beauty and offers an array of cultural and outdoor experiences. This guide will help you plan your trip to Kirifuri Highlands and ensure that you don’t miss out on any of the amazing things that this place has to offer.

List of Places to Visit in Kirifuri Highlands

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Kirifuri Waterfall: This 75-meter tall waterfall is a must-visit destination in Kirifuri Highlands. Located at the entrance of Kirifuri National Park, this waterfall is framed by stunning greenery and offers a refreshing and rejuvenating experience.
  • Shrines and Temples: The area around Kirifuri Highlands is rich in shrines and temples, each with its own unique history and architecture. Some of the most popular ones are the Takahata Fudoson, the Kinugawa Onsen Futarayama Shrine, and the Tosho-gu Shrine.
  • Kirifuri Highland Observatory: This observatory offers a breathtaking panoramic view of the Kirifuri Highlands and the surrounding areas. You can take in the scenery while enjoying a cup of coffee or tea at the observatory’s cafe.

Natural Attractions of Kirifuri Highlands: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Kirifuri National Park: This national park covers a vast area and is home to some of the most beautiful natural scenery in Japan. From scenic vistas to waterfalls and mountains, the park has something for everyone.
  • Hiking/Biking Trails: Kirifuri Highlands has an extensive network of hiking and biking trails that offer an exhilarating outdoor experience. Some of the most popular trails are the Mt. Takahara Trail, the Mt. Kurikoma Trail, and the Kirifuri Highlands Nature Trail.
  • Kirifuri Plain: The Kirifuri Plain is a vast expanse of greenery that is at its best during the summer season. The plain is home to colorful wildflowers and is a popular destination for outdoor picnics and camping.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Kirifuri Highlands

Kirifuri Highlands has some of the best hiking and biking trails in Japan. Here are a few of the most popular ones:

  • Mt. Takahara Trail: This trail is a moderately challenging 4.4 km hike that takes you to the summit of Mt. Takahara. From the top, you can enjoy a panoramic view of the Kanto Plain and the surrounding peaks.
  • Mt. Kurikoma Trail: This hike is a challenging 10.7 km trek that takes you to the summit of Mt. Kurikoma. The trail is steep and rocky in some places, but the stunning views from the top make it all worth it.
  • Kirifuri Highlands Nature Trail: This trail is a leisurely 6.7 km walk that takes you through some of the most beautiful natural scenery in Kirifuri Highlands. The trail is easy to navigate and offers breathtaking views of waterfalls, forests, and scenic vistas.

Best Things to Do in Kirifuri Highlands: Activities and Tours

  • Whitewater Rafting: The Kinugawa River, located near Kirifuri Highlands, is perfect for whitewater rafting. This is an exhilarating activity that is popular with adventure seekers.
  • Skiing: Kirifuri Highlands is a popular skiing destination during the winter season. The area has several ski resorts that offer a thrilling skiing experience.
  • Koyo (Autumn Leaves) Viewing: The Kirifuri Highlands are particularly stunning during the autumn months when the leaves turn golden-red. This is the best time to visit Kirifuri Highlands for a picturesque and relaxing getaway.
  • Kirifuri Highland Observatory Tour: The Kirifuri Highland Observatory offers guided tours that take you through the history and culture of the area.

Food and Drink Scene of Kirifuri Highlands

Must-Try Dishes

  • Nikkou Yuba: Yuba is a soy-based delicacy that is made by boiling soy milk and skimming the surface layer. Nikkou Yuba is a locally produced variety that is particularly delicious.
  • Imo-kenpi: Imo-Kenpi is a sweet potato snack that is a popular souvenir item in the area. This crispy snack is made by thinly slicing sweet potatoes and baking them to perfection.
  • Soba Noodles: Soba Noodles are a regional specialty and are made from buckwheat flour. Kirifuri Highlands has several soba noodle restaurants that serve scrumptious noodles.

Local Beverages

  • Sake: Tochigi Prefecture is known for its sake production. Several local sake breweries offer tastings of their delicious brews.
  • Juices and Smoothies: Because of the abundance of fresh fruits in the area, there is no scarcity of juice and smoothie shops in Kirifuri Highlands.

Where to Stay in Kirifuri Highlands: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Kirifuri Highlands Hotel: This hotel is located in the heart of Kirifuri Highlands and offers an array of services and amenities.
  • Kinugawa Onsen Hotel: This hotel is located near the Kinugawa River and offers scenic views of the surrounding areas. The hotel has several hot springs and traditional Japanese-style rooms.
  • Cozy Guesthouses: Kirifuri Highlands has several cozy guesthouses that offer a more intimate and homely experience.

What to Buy in Kirifuri Highlands: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Imo-Kenpi: This sweet potato snack makes for a perfect souvenir or gift to bring back home.
  • Handcrafted Pottery: Kirifuri Highlands has several pottery studios that make and sell beautiful handcrafted pottery.
  • Sake: Tochigi Prefecture is known for its sake production, and bringing back a bottle of locally produced sake is a great way to remember your trip to Kirifuri Highlands.

Local Culture and Customs of Kirifuri Highlands

Kirifuri Highlands has a rich cultural history that is reflected in its temples, shrines, and festivals. Visitors are expected to respect local customs and etiquette. It is customary to remove shoes before entering temples and shrines, and to clean oneself before entering a hot spring or bathhouse.

Best Time to Visit Kirifuri Highlands

The best time to visit Kirifuri Highlands is during the autumn months (September-November) when the leaves turn golden-red and the weather is mild.

Do’s and Don’ts of Kirifuri Highlands

Do:

  • Respect local customs and etiquette.
  • Take advantage of the outdoor activities and scenic beauty.
  • Try the local food and drink.

Don’t:

  • Wear shoes inside temples and shrines.
  • Litter in natural areas or in public spaces.
  • Engage in disruptive behavior.

Safety and Health

Safety in Kirifuri Highlands

Kirifuri Highlands is a relatively safe destination, but visitors are advised to take precautions while hiking or engaging in outdoor activities.

Health in Kirifuri Highlands

There are several medical facilities in the area for emergencies, and visitors should ensure that they have adequate health insurance coverage before traveling to the area.

Where to Go Next from Kirifuri Highlands: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Nikko: Located just a short distance from Kirifuri Highlands, Nikko is a UNESCO World Heritage site and offers stunning natural scenery, temples, and shrines.
  • Nasu-Shiobara: This area is known for its hot springs and is located just over an hour’s drive from Kirifuri Highlands.

Conclusion

Kirifuri Highlands is a must-visit destination for anyone who loves the outdoors, culture, and natural beauty. With its breathtaking scenery, rich cultural history, and abundance of outdoor activities, Kirifuri Highlands is sure to leave a lasting impression on any traveler.
