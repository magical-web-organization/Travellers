Introduction to Nasu

Nestled in the mountainous region of Tochigi Prefecture, Nasu is a hidden gem in Japan waiting to be discovered. It is a perfect destination for travelers who desire to experience the beauty and tranquility of nature, while also immersing themselves in the rich culture and traditions of Japan.

From its breathtaking scenery, hot springs, and delicious food to its culturally-rich landmarks and activities, Nasu has a lot to offer to its visitors. This travel guide provides a comprehensive list of attractions and activities in Nasu to help you plan your itinerary and make the most out of your trip.

List of Places to Visit in Nasu

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Nasu is home to several culturally significant landmarks that offer visitors an insight into the rich history of the area. Here are some must-visit places to learn more about the local traditions and culture:

  • Nasu Imperial Villa
  • Nasu Folk Museum
  • Nasunogahara Museum
  • Nasushiobara City Museum

Natural Attractions of Nasu: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Besides its cultural attractions, Nasu is also known for its natural beauty, including mountains, lakes, and forests. Outdoor adventure activities are also popular in the area. Here are some of the top natural attractions and outdoor activities in Nasu:

  • Mount Nasudake
  • Nasu Flower World
  • Nasu Safari Park
  • Nasu Yumoto Onsen
  • Nasu Heisei-no-Mori Forest

Hiking/Biking Routes of Nasu

Nasu is an excellent destination for nature and outdoor enthusiasts. If you love hiking, trekking, or pedaling, Nasu has several routes to explore. Some of the hiking and biking routes are:

  • Mount Nasudake Hiking Trail
  • Nasu Rindo Forest
  • Nasu Plateau Cycling Road

Best Things to Do in Nasu: Activities and Tours

Nasu has several activities and tours that cater to all interests and ages. Whether you want to relax in hot springs, enjoy sightseeing tours, participate in cultural activities or go on a wildlife safari, you will not be disappointed. Here are some of the best things to do in Nasu:

  • Nasu Alpaca Farm
  • Nasu Animal Kingdom
  • Horseback Riding at Nasu Highland Park
  • Nasu Onsen Nature Trail
  • Nasu Highland Park
  • Strawberry Picking at Ichinaka Farm

Week-Long Itinerary for Nasu

If you have more than a day to explore Nasu, you are in for a treat. Nasu has so much to offer, and a week-long itinerary is a perfect way to see the best sights and activities the area has to offer.

Day 1: Nasu Imperial Villa, Nasunogahara Museum, and Nasushiobara City Museum Day 2: Mount Nasudake, Nasu Flower World, and Nasu Safari Park Day 3: Nasu Rindo Forest Hiking, Atago Shrine, and Nasu Shrine Day 4: Nasu Heisei-no-Mori Forest, Nasu Trick Art Museum, and Nasu Teddy Bear Museum Day 5: Horseback Riding at Nasu Highland Park, Onsen at Nasu Yumoto Onsen, and Soba Noodle Making Classes Day 6: Ichinaka Farm Strawberry Picking, Nasu Animal Kingdom, and Nasu Garden Outlet Day 7: Nasu Alpaca Farm, Nasu Stained Glass Museum, and Nasu Ropeway

Food and Drink Scene in Nasu

Nasu has a diverse culinary scene that combines local traditions with modern twists. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages while visiting Nasu:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Nasu Katsu: Deep-fried eggplant with breadcrumbs and sauce
  • Green Tea Soba: Soba noodles with green tea powder
  • Yuba: Deluxe tofu skin boiled in broth
  • Sano Ramen: Ramen noodles in a shoyu (soy sauce) soup

Local Beverages

  • Nasu Beer: Brewed with natural spring water from the Nasu mountains
  • Nasu Wine: Made from locally grown grapes
  • Hotspring water: Refreshing and perfect for hydration

Where to Stay in Nasu: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Nasu has several accommodation options to suit all budgets and preferences. Whether you want luxurious hotels or simple guesthouses, Nasu has it all. Here are some top-rated places to stay in Nasu:

  • Nasu Rindoh-Tei
  • Nasu Kogen Hotel
  • Hotel Epinard Nasu
  • Nasu Highland Resort

What to Buy in Nasu: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Nasu is famous for its unique and quality souvenirs and local products. Some of the must-buy items while in Nasu include:

  • Nasu Sasa Kamaboko: Traditional fish cakes made with bamboo shoots
  • Nasu Wooden Chopsticks
  • Nasu Reifū Tea: Made with locally grown tea leaves
  • Nasu Yuki Tsumugi: Traditional Japanese silk fabric

Local Culture and Customs of Nasu

Respect for local customs and traditions is vital when visiting Nasu. Some of the customs and etiquettes to keep in mind include;

  • Removing shoes before entering houses, temples, or shrines.
  • Jam-packed public transport, even during peak season. Respect other passengers’ personal space.
  • Avoid littering and maintain cleanliness.

Best Time to Visit Nasu

Nasu is an all-year-round destination with each season offering a different experience. However, the best time to visit Nasu is from April to June or from September to November when the weather is mild, and the scenic views are spectacular.

Do’s and Don’ts of Nasu

Here are some do’s and don’ts to keep in mind when in Nasu;

Do:

  • Respect for local customs and etiquettes.
  • Try the various dishes and local beverages while in Nasu.
  • Learn a few Japanese phrases to communicate effectively.

Don’t:

  • Don’t tip in restaurants or cafes. It’s not part of the Japanese culture.
  • Avoid loud conversation or speaking on the phone while on public transport.
  • Don’t take photos of locals, especially without their consent.

Safety and Health

Safety and health are crucial when traveling to a new destination. Here are some safety tips and health information to keep in mind when visiting Nasu;

Safety in Nasu

  • Nasu is safe; however, always be vigilant, especially when in crowded places.
  • Drink tap water, which is safe and readily available in Nasu.

Health in Nasu

  • Nasu has clean air and a healthy environment, but be careful not to exert too much during outdoor activities, especially during summer.
  • Nasu has excellent medical facilities in case of any health emergencies.

Getting to Nasu: Transportation Options and Logistics

Nasu is accessible from several points via public transport, including trains, buses, and taxis. Here are some of the transportation options to get to Nasu;

Airports Near Nasu

  • Tokyo Narita International Airport (NRT)
  • Tokyo Haneda International Airport (HND)

Bus Stations Near Nasu

  • Nasushiobara Station
  • Kuroiso Station

Other Options

  • Rent a car, which is an excellent option if you want to explore Nasu’s surrounding areas independently.

How to Get Around in Nasu

Nasu is a small area with excellent public transportation. The best way to get around Nasu is by shuttle bus, taxi, or rental car.

Where to Go Next from Nasu: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

If you have some extra time, there are several excellent destinations to explore around Nasu. Here are some of them:

  • Nikko National Park
  • Kinugawa Onsen
  • Kegon Falls
  • Utsunomiya City

Conclusion

Nasu is an excellent destination to experience Japan’s beauty and tranquility. With its cultural attractions, natural beauty, activities, and delicious food, Nasu offers an exciting and unforgettable experience for all visitors. Use this comprehensive guide to plan your trip and make the most out of your time in Nasu.
