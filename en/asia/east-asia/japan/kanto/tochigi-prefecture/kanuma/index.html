Introduction to Kanuma

Kanuma is a small town located in the Tochigi prefecture of Japan. It’s a hidden gem in the Kanto region, known for its scenic views, outdoor adventures, and rich cultural history. The town is surrounded by mountains, rivers, and forests, making it the perfect destination for nature lovers.

Kanuma is also home to several cultural and historical sites, including museums and temples, that offer visitors a glimpse into Japan’s past. With its relaxed atmosphere and friendly locals, Kanuma is the perfect destination for those seeking an authentic and off-the-beaten-path experience in Japan.

List of Places to Visit in Kanuma

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Kanuma has a rich cultural history, and visitors can learn more about it by visiting its museums and historical sites. The following are some of the most important places to visit:

Kanuma City Museum

The Kanuma City Museum is dedicated to preserving and displaying the town’s cultural heritage. The museum has several exhibits, including artifacts from the Jomon period, Edo era, and Meiji era. Visitors can learn about the town’s history, culture, and traditions.

Tochigi Prefectural Museum of Fine Arts

The Tochigi Prefectural Museum of Fine Arts is located in Kanuma and has a large collection of paintings and sculptures from both Japanese and Western artists. The museum also hosts special exhibitions throughout the year.

Natural Attractions of Kanuma: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Kanuma is the perfect destination for nature lovers and outdoor enthusiasts. The town is surrounded by mountains, forests, and rivers, making it the perfect place for hiking, biking, and exploring the great outdoors. The following are some of the most popular outdoor attractions in Kanuma:

Shiobara Canyon

Shiobara Canyon is a beautiful and scenic attraction located near Kanuma. The canyon is known for its natural hot springs and waterfalls. Visitors can take a dip in the hot springs or hike through the canyon to see its many waterfalls.

Nikko National Park

Nikko National Park is located near Kanuma and is known for its natural beauty and scenic views. The park is home to several hiking trails, including the popular Mt. Nantai hiking trail, which offers stunning views of the surrounding mountains and forests.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Kanuma

Kanuma is known for its many hiking and biking trails. The following are some of the most popular routes:

Mt. Kurikoma Hiking Trail

Mt. Kurikoma is a popular hiking trail located near Kanuma. The trail is about 17 kilometers long and takes about six hours to complete. Visitors can enjoy stunning views of the surrounding mountains and forests while hiking the trail.

Kanuma Cycling Route

The Kanuma Cycling Route is a popular biking and hiking trail that runs along the Kinugawa River. The route is 17.5 kilometers long and takes about three hours to complete. Visitors can enjoy scenic views of the river while biking or hiking the trail.

Best Things to Do in Kanuma: Activities and Tours

Kanuma offers visitors a wide variety of activities and tours. The following are some of the best things to do in Kanuma:

Kanuma Kimono Experience

Visitors to Kanuma can experience traditional Japanese culture by renting a kimono and walking around the town. Many shops in Kanuma offer kimono rentals at affordable prices.

River Rafting on the Kinugawa River

The Kinugawa River is a popular destination for river rafting. Visitors can enjoy the rapids and scenic views of the river while rafting with a tour guide.

Week-Long Itinerary for Kanuma

The following is a sample week-long itinerary for visitors to Kanuma:

Day 1: Visit the Kanuma City Museum and learn about the town’s culture and history.

Day 2: Take a hike along the Mt. Kurikoma Hiking Trail and enjoy the stunning views of the surrounding mountains and forests.

Day 3: Rent a kimono and explore the town while dressed in traditional Japanese clothing.

Day 4: Visit the Shiobara Canyon and take a dip in the natural hot springs.

Day 5: Take a river rafting tour on the Kinugawa River.

Day 6: Visit the Tochigi Prefectural Museum of Fine Arts and admire the paintings and sculptures.

Day 7: Take a day trip to nearby Nikko National Park and hike the Mt. Nantai trail.

Food and Drink Scene of Kanuma

Must-Try Dishes

Kanuma is known for its delicious local cuisine. The following are some of the best dishes to try:

Yuba

Yuba is a local specialty made from the skin that forms on top of soy milk when it’s heated. It’s a popular snack in Kanuma and can be enjoyed in several ways, including grilled, boiled, or fried.

Soba

Kanuma is known for its soba noodles, which are made from buckwheat flour. The noodles are often served in a hot broth with vegetables and meat.

Local Beverages

Kanuma is also known for its sake, a type of Japanese rice wine. The following are some of the best local breweries to visit:

Kikusui sake brewery

The Kikusui sake brewery is located in Kanuma and offers tours and tastings. Visitors can sample different types of sake and learn about the brewing process.

Where to Stay in Kanuma: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Kanuma offers visitors a variety of accommodations, from traditional guesthouses to modern hotels. The following are some of the best places to stay:

Kanuma Yumoto Onsen

Kanuma Yumoto Onsen is a traditional Japanese hot spring hotel located in the town. It offers visitors a relaxing and authentic Japanese experience.

Tonegawa Onsen Sato no Yu

Tonegawa Onsen Sato no Yu is another hot spring hotel located in Kanuma. It offers visitors a variety of hot springs and spa services.

What to Buy in Kanuma: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Kanuma is known for its local products, including yuba and soba noodles. The following are some of the best souvenirs to bring home:

Yuba

Yuba can be purchased from several shops in Kanuma, and it makes a great gift for friends and family back home.

Soba Noodles

Kanuma’s soba noodles are another popular souvenir. They can be purchased from several shops in the town.

Local Culture and Customs of Kanuma

Kanuma has a rich cultural history, and visitors can learn about it by visiting its museums and temples. The town is also famous for its traditional festivals and events, including the Kanuma Grand Festival, which is held every year in August.

Best Time to Visit Kanuma

The best time to visit Kanuma is during the spring and autumn months when the weather is mild and the foliage is at its peak. The town is also beautiful during the winter months, and visitors can enjoy winter sports such as skiing and snowboarding.

Do’s and Don’ts of Kanuma

Do:

  • Respect local customs and traditions.
  • Try the local cuisine and beverages.
  • Take off your shoes when entering a traditional Japanese home.

Don’t:

  • Be loud or disrespectful in public places.
  • Wear shoes inside a traditional Japanese home.
  • Forget to tip when dining out - it’s not customary in Japan.

Safety and Health

Safety in Kanuma

Kanuma is a safe town with low crime rates. Visitors should always take normal safety precautions, such as keeping an eye on their personal belongings and avoiding walking alone at night.

Health in Kanuma

Kanuma has several medical facilities, including hospitals and clinics. Visitors should purchase travel insurance before visiting to ensure they’re covered in case of any accidents or illnesses.

Getting to Kanuma: Transportation Options and Logistics

Kanuma is located about two hours north of Tokyo by train. The following are some of the transportation options:

Airports Near Kanuma

  • Narita International Airport: Located about two hours south of Kanuma by train.
  • Tokyo Haneda Airport: Located about two hours south of Kanuma by train.

Bus Stations Near Kanuma

  • Tobu Nikko Station: Located about 30 minutes north of Kanuma by bus.

Other Options

  • Renting a car is also an option for visitors who want to explore the surrounding area independently.

How to Get Around in Kanuma

Kanuma is a small town, and visitors can easily get around on foot or by bike. Taxis are also available, but they can be expensive.

Where to Go Next from Kanuma: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Kanuma is located near several other popular destinations, including Nikko and Tokyo. The following are some of the best places to visit:

Nikko

Nikko is located about 30 minutes north of Kanuma by train. It’s a popular destination known for its temples and shrines, including the famous Toshogu Shrine.

Tokyo

Tokyo is located about two hours south of Kanuma by train. It’s the capital of Japan and offers visitors a wide range of attractions, including museums, restaurants, and shopping districts.

Conclusion

Kanuma is a hidden gem in Japan’s Tochigi prefecture, offering visitors a unique and authentic travel experience. With its natural beauty, cultural history, and friendly locals, Kanuma is the perfect destination for those looking to escape the crowds and discover a different side of Japan.
