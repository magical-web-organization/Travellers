Introduction to Chofu

Chofu is a city in Tokyo Prefecture, located in the western part of the metropolitan area. It is situated against the backdrop of the Chichibu-Tama-Kai National Park and offers visitors a unique blend of modernity and traditional charm. With a population of approximately 230,000, Chofu is a perfect city for those who seek a quieter vacation with rich cultural experiences.

In this guide, we’ll take you on a tour of Chofu’s culturally significant historical and natural attractions, provide you with hiking and biking routes, and suggest fun activities to do during your stay. Additionally, we’ll share our recommendations for where to stay, what to eat and drink, and highlight some unique souvenirs and local products to bring home.

List of Places to Visit in Chofu

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Chofu is steeped in Japanese culture and history. You can explore museums and historical sites that offer an insight into the city’s unique past. Here are a few must-visit places:

  • The Chofu City Museum of History: this museum showcases the rich history of Chofu and its neighboring areas from prehistoric times to modern-day.
  • The Akishima Folk Museum: this museum is dedicated to preserving and promoting the city’s traditional culture and heritage and features a range of exhibits, including artifacts, tools, and objects used in daily life in olden times.
  • The Jindai-ji Temple: founded in the year 733, it is one of the oldest temples in Japan and is renowned for its tranquil gardens and the “100 Soumen Noodle” dish, a local specialty.

Natural Attractions of Chofu: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Nature lovers will find plenty of outdoor activities to enjoy in Chofu. Here are some of the best natural attractions:

  • Tama Forest Science Garden: This 40-hectare botanical garden features over 1,300 plant species from around the world.
  • Fuchu Forest Park: It is a massive public park that includes amenities such as baseball fields, a cycling course, and a nature museum.
  • Mt. Mitake: Located in the Chichibu-Tama-Kai National Park, Mt. Mitake offers great hiking trails and some spectacular views of the surrounding mountains.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Chofu

Hiking and biking enthusiasts will love Chofu’s trails and routes, providing fantastic chances to explore the city’s natural beauty. These are some of the best hiking and biking routes to try:

  • Cherry Blossom River Course: This 5.5 km cycling and walking path runs parallel to the Tamagawa River, with fantastic scenery and dynamic Japanese delights throughout the year.
  • Chofu Kaido Walk: This walking route from the Jindaiji Temple to Chofu Station passes through tea fields and rural scenery, offering a glimpse of the city’s richness heritage.
  • Sengawa Valley Nature Walk: This 3.5 km circular walk presents visitors with a fantastic opportunity to admire the city’s natural beauty, especially in autumn when the leaves change color.

Best Things to Do in Chofu: Activities and Tours

Apart from its natural and cultural landmarks, Chofu presents visitors with several exciting activities to try. Following are a few recommendations:

  • Try Archery: Experience traditional Japanese archery techniques in a modern way with a Japanese archery experience, showcasing the artistry of the Japanese bow.
  • Visit an Onsen: Relax and rejuvenate in a natural hot spring or spa for a truly authentic Japanese experience.
  • Have a Karaoke Night: Sing your hearts out in one of the many karaoke rooms that Tokyo is known for – perfect for a night out with friends or family.

Food and Drink Scene of Chofu

Chofu offers some delectable local food and beverage options that are a must-try for visitors. Here are some of them:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Soba Noodles: Chofu is known for these homemade Japanese buckwheat noodles, made with Tsukumo-nan or powdered salt.
  • Okonomiyaki: A savory Japanese pancake-like dish, it includes veggies, meat, and eggs topped with Okonomi sauce.
  • Monjayaki: a dish that is particularly popular in the Konohana-tei Okonomiyaki restaurant, it’s a savory pancake-like dish containing finely diced ingredients and batter.

Local Beverages

  • Japanese Sake: Try a cup of sake in the sake bars located throughout the city, often with the added bonus of being able to chat to locals.
  • Green Tea: Tea ceremony experiences can be had across Chofu. You can also sample fresh and premium loose-leaf teas in local stores.

Where to Stay in Chofu: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Chofu offers a range of accommodation options to suit different budgets and preferences. Here are a few we suggest:

  • Chofu Creston Hotel: Located right next to Chofu Station, this hotel offers comfortable accommodations and fantastic city views of Chofu.
  • SGE (Sasaduka Green Heights Estate): This modern and chic apartment-style accommodation is perfect for those looking for a more independent stay, features kitchenettes for longer stays.
  • Hostel Takamatsu: A great budget option located just a short walk from the Chofu train station, offering both comfort and affordability.

What to Buy in Chofu: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Chofu offers some unique and locally produced products that make for great souvenirs to take back home. Here are a few ideas:

  • Chofu Handmade Umbrellas: These are made from traditional Japanese paper, Washi, and come in several patterns and styles.
  • Chofu Sushi: Sushi can be bought for takeaway from the rooftop Cafeteria of the Chofu Creston Hotel, often prepped on the spot.
  • Chofu Local Sake: Sip on local sake as a memento, with several types and strains readily available.

Local Culture and Customs of Chofu

Japanese culture and customs are no different in Chofu, with many traditional customs being followed. Here are a few things you should keep in mind while visiting:

  • Respect: Dress appropriately when visiting temples, shrines and other public areas, and always bow when entering and leaving stores or places.
  • Tipping: Tipping isn’t expected in Chofu or the rest of Japan, as it is considered impolite.
  • Shoes off: Shoes are typically taken off when entering people’s homes, apartments, or restaurants. Make sure to check shoe arrangements and the presence of slippers.

Best Time to Visit Chofu

The best time to visit Chofu is from March to May, when the weather is pleasant and cherry blossoms are in full bloom, adding picturesque scenic beauty. September to November is great for those who love foliage and autumn foliage.

Do’s and Don’ts of Chofu

Here are some do’s and don’ts to keep in mind while visiting Chofu:

Do:

  • Carry Cash: Japan is still a cash-based society, and many establishments don’t accept cards.
  • Use Public Transportation: Japan’s public transportation system is efficient and reliable, making it the best way to get around.
  • Be Polite: Always remember to say “Arigato Gozaimasu” (“Thank you very much”) or “Sumimasen” (“Excuse me”) when interacting with locals.

Don’t:

  • Eat and walk: Eating while walking in public in Japan is considered impolite.
  • Be loud: Speaking loudly or playing music on public transportation is frowned upon, so keep it down.

Safety and Health

Safety and health hazards are rarely a concern in Chofu, making it a great travel destination. That being said, visitors should keep in mind the following:

Safety in Chofu

  • Traffic safety: bicycles and cars common and can be dangerous, ensure extra care when crossing the road.
  • Theft: As with any location, visitors should maintain vigilance when it comes to their personal belongings.

Health in Chofu

  • Natural Disasters: Japan lies on the earthquake-prone Pacific Ring of Fire, which can pose potential natural hazards to visitors. Always be aware of emergency evacuation routes.

Getting to Chofu: Transportation Options and Logistics

Getting to Chofu is relatively straightforward, with various options available from Tokyo city. Here are a few we suggest:

Airports Near Chofu

The closest airport to Chofu is Tokyo Haneda Airport. From there, you can take the train, bus, or taxi to reach the city.

Train Stations Near Chofu

Chofu Station, located on the Keio Line and the Chuo Line, is the main train station in the city.

Other Options

  • Bus: From the central Tokyo area, take the Tokyo Shuttle Bus, which departs regularly and provides service to three locations in Chofu: Tamagawajosui, Chofu Station, and Fuda Station.
  • Taxi: Taxis are widely available at any time, providing convenience and privacy for those who prefer it. However, taxis can be expensive in Japan.

How to Get Around in Chofu

Chofu provides visitors with several convenient ways to get around, including by train, bus, and walking. Here are a few transportation options to keep in mind:

  • Trains: Chofu Station is on several train lines, including the Keio Line and the Chuo Line, making it a convenient way to get around.
  • Buses: The Tamagawajosui Bus Terminal is the main bus terminal, with buses operating throughout Chofu.
  • Walking and Cycling: Chofu is relatively small and easy to navigate on foot, with bike rentals and cycling tours offering other options to explore the city.

Where to Go Next from Chofu: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Chofu is located near many significant cities and destinations, making it an excellent base for day trips and excursions. Here are a few options:

  • Mount Takao: One of Tokyo’s more popular hiking destinations, Mount Takao is only 30 minutes from Chofu by train and provides visitors with fantastic scenic beauty.
  • Shinjuku: One of Tokyo’s most vibrant neighborhoods, it’s a short train ride away from Chofu, and is known for its shopping, dining, and nightlife.
  • Kawasaki: Located a short distance from Chofu, this industrial city is home to several theme parks and museums that showcase some of Japan’s technology and innovation.

Conclusion

In conclusion, Chofu is a city with rich cultural significance, natural beauty, and unique experiences. From relaxing at hot springs to exploring botanical gardens to trying out traditional Japanese archery techniques, there is something to suit everyone’s interest. Plan your next trip to Chofu with the help of this comprehensive guide, and we are sure you will have an unforgettable vacation.
