Introduction to Machida

Machida is a city located in the Tokyo prefecture of Japan, known for its breathtaking natural beauty, rich history, and diverse cultural scene. It was once a small rural town but has now grown into a bustling city with a population of over 430,000 people. Located just 45 minutes south-west of Tokyo by train, Machida offers visitors the perfect blend of modern city life and traditional Japanese culture.

List of Places to Visit in Machida

There’s plenty to see and do in Machida. Here are some of the top places to visit:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Machida City Museum of Graphic Arts: This museum is dedicated to Japanese printmaking and displays works from artists across the country.
  • Machida Squirrel Garden: This is a unique park where visitors can feed and interact with cute squirrels.
  • Yakushi Ike Park: This park has a beautiful pond with walking trails, and is home to a variety of waterfowl and small animals.
  • Machida Shrine: This shrine is dedicated to the god of agriculture and has been a popular place of worship for locals for centuries.
  • Sagamihara Prefectural Museum of Modern Art: This museum houses a collection of modern and contemporary art from Japanese and international artists.

Natural Attractions of Machida: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Tama Hills Nature Park: This is a great place for hiking, camping, and picnicking. Visitors can enjoy scenic views of the surrounding mountains and forests.
  • Ikuta Ryokuchi Park: This large park features beautiful gardens, a rose garden, and a small amusement park for families.
  • Nagaike Shinsui Park: This park has a beautiful lake, walking trails, and a cherry blossom festival in the spring.
  • Machida Central Park: This park has a playground and a large open space for playing sports or having a picnic.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Machida

Machida has beautiful hiking and biking routes that allow visitors to explore its scenic beauty. Some of the best trails include the Tama River Bicycle Path, the Tobogasaki Forest Park Trail, and the Shiroyama Park Trail. Visitors can also take the Mt. Takao Pilgrimage Trail to enjoy stunning views of the area.

Best Things to Do in Machida: Activities and Tours

There are plenty of activities and tours to enjoy in Machida, including:

  • Sushi making workshops: Learn how to make delicious sushi rolls from expert chefs.
  • Tea ceremonies: Experience a traditional Japanese tea ceremony with a local host.
  • Craft workshops: Try your hand at making pottery or fabric-dyeing.
  • Karaoke: Sing your heart out at one of the local karaoke bars.
  • Nightlife: Enjoy live music, drinks, and dancing in Machida’s vibrant nightlife.

Week-Long Itinerary for Machida

For visitors planning to spend a week in Machida, we recommend the following itinerary:

Day 1-2: Visit the Machida City Museum of Graphic Arts, the Machida Squirrel Garden, and the Yakushi Ike Park.

Day 3-4: Explore the Tama Hills Nature Park, the Ikuta Ryokuchi Park, and the Nagaike Shinsui Park.

Day 5-6: Take the Mt. Takao Pilgrimage Trail, visit the Machida Shrine, and enjoy a sushi making workshop.

Day 7: Spend the day at the Machida Central Park and indulge in some tea ceremonies or craft workshops.

Food and Drink Scene of Machida

Machida has a diverse food and drink scene with many tasty options to choose from. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Ramen: Machida is home to some of the best ramen shops in Tokyo, including the famous Kamukura Ramen.
  • Sushi: Visit Sushiko Meieki, a local sushi restaurant that serves fresh and delicious sushi rolls.
  • Okonomiyaki: This savory pancake dish is a popular Japanese comfort food. Head to Okonomiyaki Sagarinoha for an authentic taste.
  • Yakitori: Grilled chicken skewers are a local favorite. Try them at any of the local yakitori shops.

Local Beverages

  • Sake: Machida is located near the famous sake-producing region of Nada, and locals enjoy delicious and refreshing sake from local breweries.
  • Craft beer: Many microbreweries have opened up in Machida in recent years, serving unique and delicious craft beer.
  • Green tea: Visitors can enjoy high-quality green tea in many tea houses and cafes around the city.

Where to Stay in Machida: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Machida has plenty of options for accommodation, ranging from budget-friendly hostels to luxurious hotels. Some of the best places to stay include the APA Hotel Machida-Kanagawa-University, the Hotel Kizuna, and the Machida Washington Hotel.

What to Buy in Machida: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

When in Machida, make sure to pick up some souvenirs and local products to bring back home. Some popular options include:

  • Japanese pottery: Machida has several pottery workshops where visitors can buy beautiful handcrafted Japanese pottery.
  • Traditional sweets: Try some of the local Japanese sweets, such as wagashi or mochi, which make great souvenirs.
  • Green tea: Buy some high-quality Japanese green tea to bring back home.
  • Japanese knives: Machida is home to some of the best Japanese knife makers, and visitors can buy high-quality knives at local shops.

Local Culture and Customs of Machida

Machida has a strong cultural heritage, and visitors can experience traditional Japanese customs and culture throughout the city. Visitors should be respectful of local customs, including removing shoes before entering a home or temple, and bowing when greeting someone.

Best Time to Visit Machida

Machida is a great place to visit year-round, but the best time to visit is during the spring (March to May) and autumn (September to November) when temperatures are mild, and the foliage is beautiful. Cherry blossom season in the spring and the autumn foliage in November are particularly popular times to visit.

Do’s and Don’ts of Machida

Do:

  • Remove shoes before entering homes or temples.
  • Try the local cuisine and beverages.
  • Practice common courtesies, such as saying “hello” and “thank you” in Japanese.
  • Respect local customs and traditions.

Don’t:

  • Be disrespectful of local customs and traditions.
  • Be late for appointments or meetings.
  • Speak loudly in public places.
  • Litter or be disrespectful of the environment.

Safety and Health

Safety in Machida

Machida is a relatively safe city, and visitors should not encounter any major safety concerns. However, it is always wise to take normal precautions, such as keeping valuables safe, locking hotel room doors, and being aware of surroundings.

Health in Machida

Machida has excellent healthcare facilities, including hospitals and clinics, but it is recommended that visitors purchase travel health insurance before arrival. Tap water is safe to drink, but visitors may prefer to drink bottled water.

Getting to Machida: Transportation Options and Logistics

Machida is easily accessible by train or bus. Visitors can take the JR Lines, the Odakyu Line, or the Keio Line to reach Machida from Tokyo or other nearby cities.

Airports Near Machida

Machida is about an hour from Haneda Airport and about two hours from Narita International Airport.

Bus Stations Near Machida

Machida has several bus stations, including the Machida Bus Terminal and the Odakyu Bus Station.

Other Options

Visitors can also take a taxi, rent a car, or rent a bicycle to explore the city.

How to Get Around in Machida

Machida is a relatively small city, and visitors can easily get around on foot or by bicycle. Public transport, including trains and buses, is also readily available and convenient.

Where to Go Next from Machida: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Machida is located in the Tokyo prefecture and is surrounded by several exciting destinations, including Tokyo, Yokohama, Kamakura, Hakone, and Mt. Fuji.

Conclusion

Machida is a hidden gem in the Tokyo prefecture, offering visitors a unique blend of natural beauty, cultural heritage, and modern city life. From the scenic hikes to the delicious local cuisine, there’s something for everyone in this charming city. Use our ultimate guide to plan your trip and get the most out of your Machida experience.
