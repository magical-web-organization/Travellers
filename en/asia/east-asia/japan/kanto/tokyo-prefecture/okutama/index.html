Introduction to Okutama

Located in the western part of Tokyo Prefecture, Okutama is a serene retreat away from the hustle and bustle of the city. Its picturesque scenery, rich culture, and outdoor activities make it a popular destination for both locals and tourists. The town is surrounded by lush green mountains, clear freshwater streams, and scenic views of the Tama River. The calmness of the area is a stark contrast to the bustling and crowded city of Tokyo.

List of Places to Visit in Okutama

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Okutama Museum of History and Folklore: Discover the history and traditions of Okutama through various exhibits, artifacts, and displays.

  • Hatonosu Valley Kiyose River Museum: Learn about the unique ecosystem found in the Kiyose River and the Hatonosu Valley area.

  • Musashi Mitake Shrine: A 2,000-year old shrine that is considered a sacred site in Japan. Visitors can hike up to the shrine and enjoy panoramic views of the surrounding mountains.

Natural Attractions of Okutama: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Mt. Mitake: One of the most famous mountains in the Tokyo area, Mt. Mitake offers stunning views of the surrounding valley and is the perfect place to hike or explore.

  • Okutama Lake: A beautiful lake that offers a variety of water sports, including kayaking and fishing.

  • Tama River: A popular spot for fishing, picnicking, and water sports, the Tama River is a tranquil escape from the city.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Okutama

There are many hiking and biking routes in Okutama that offer breathtaking views of the surrounding mountains and valleys. Some of the most popular routes include:

  • Mt. Mitake Trail: This trail leads hikers to the top of Mt. Mitake and offers stunning views of the valley below.

  • Shiromaru Dam Trail: A 6km trail that offers scenic views of the Shiromaru Dam and surrounding area.

  • Kamosawa Valley Trail: A beautiful trail that leads hikers along a clear stream, through forests, and past waterfalls.

Best Things to Do in Okutama: Activities and Tours

  • White Water Rafting: Adventure-seekers can enjoy rafting down the Tama River.

  • Orienteering: Orienteering courses are available for those wanting to explore the beauty of the forests of Okutama.

  • Cycling Tours: Experience the scenic scenery of Okutama on a guided cycling tour.

Food and Drink Scene of Okutama

Must-Try Dishes

  • Nishikawa Soba: A locally made soba noodle dish that is famous in Okutama.

  • Grilled Ayu fish: A local fish that is grilled and served with rice.

  • Kajika soup: A traditional Japanese soup made with a local, freshwater fish called the stone moroko.

Local Beverages

  • Sake: Okutama is home to several sake breweries that take advantage of the local, fresh water.

  • Green tea: The lush greenery surrounding Okutama provides an ideal environment for growing tea leaves.

Where to Stay in Okutama: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Okutama Grand Hotel: A luxury hotel that offers stunning views of the surrounding mountains and valley.

  • Riverside Cottage: A cozy lodging option that is situated along the Tama River.

  • Okutama Youth Hostel: A budget-friendly option for travelers who want to make the most of their outdoor adventures.

What to Buy in Okutama: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Shinrin-yoku certified products: Okutama is home to a forest bathing certification program and offers various beauty products to take home.

  • Local sake and green tea: Take home a taste of Okutama with locally made beverages.

Local Culture and Customs of Okutama

Okutama is known for its friendly locals and traditional Japanese culture. Visitors are expected to bow when greeting locals, and it is customary to remove your shoes before entering homes and temples. It is essential to be respectful of local customs and uphold the community’s values.

Best Time to Visit Okutama

The best time to visit Okutama is during the spring and autumn seasons when the weather is mild, and the leaves change colors. The summer months offer plenty of outdoor activities, while the winter months are great for winter sports like skiing and snowboarding.

Do’s and Don’ts of Okutama

Do:

  • Respect local customs and traditions.
  • Be friendly and courteous to locals and other visitors.
  • Take care of the environment and do not litter.

Don’t:

  • Use flash photography in temples or shrines.
  • Leave valuables unattended.
  • Take photos without asking for permission.

Safety and Health

Safety in Okutama

Okutama is generally a safe place to visit, and tourists should not have any problems. It is essential to take basic safety precautions, such as not leaving valuables unattended.

Health in Okutama

Most visitors to Okutama will not experience any health issues. However, it is essential to be cautious when engaging in outdoor activities and to wear proper clothing and equipment. Visitors should also be aware of any allergies they may have, as certain foods and plants may cause an allergic reaction.

Getting to Okutama: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Okutama

  • Narita International Airport: The closest international airport to Okutama, located approximately 100 km away.

  • Haneda Airport: A domestic airport in Tokyo located approximately 100 km away.

Bus Stations Near Okutama

  • Musashi-itsukaichi Station: A train and bus station that provides access to Okutama from central Tokyo.

  • Okutama Station: A train and bus station located in central Okutama.

Other Options

  • Taxi: Taxis are available from nearby train or bus stations for transportation to preferred locations.

  • Rental Car: Renting a car is also an option to help visitors traverse through the valley and surrounding areas with ease.

How to Get Around in Okutama

Walking and cycling are the best ways to explore Okutama, taking advantage of the scenic views and natural beauty of the region. Local buses and taxis are also available, providing visitors with easy access to surrounding areas or more remote locations.

Where to Go Next from Okutama: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Kawagoe: A historic city located just an hour from Okutama that offers various cultural landmarks, great shopping, and delicious food.

  • Nikko: Home to various UNESCO World Heritage sites, this quiet mountain town is a great place to explore traditional Japanese culture and history.

Conclusion

Okutama is a serene retreat that is perfect for outdoor enthusiasts, history buffs, and anyone wanting to experience traditional Japanese culture. From hiking and biking to exploring museums and local food and drink, Okutama offers something for everyone. With its accessibility from central Tokyo and various lodging options, it is a great place to experience Tokyo’s countryside.
