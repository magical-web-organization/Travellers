Introduction to Nishitokyo

Nishitokyo is a city located in Tokyo Prefecture, Japan. The city is known for its rich cultural heritage, natural beauty, and outdoor adventures. It is a perfect destination for travelers who want to explore the traditional side of Japan while taking in the modern city vibe.

List of Places to Visit in Nishitokyo

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

1. Wada House

Wada House is a traditional Japanese house that represents the architectural style of the Edo Period (1603-1868). The house has been well preserved, and visitors can take a tour of the rooms and gardens to learn about Japanese history and culture.

2. Fudaten Shrine

Fudaten Shrine is a Shinto shrine located in the heart of the city. It is a popular spot for locals to pray and participate in traditional festivals throughout the year.

3. Tamarokuto Science Center

The Tamarokuto Science Center is a science museum that features interactive exhibits and displays about the universe, earth, and technology. It is a great place for travelers who want to learn and have fun at the same time.

Natural Attractions of Nishitokyo: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

1. Takahatafudoson Kongoji Temple

The Takahatafudoson Kongoji Temple is a Buddhist temple located in a quiet area of the city. Visitors can enjoy the peaceful atmosphere, take a stroll around the beautiful gardens, and try the temple’s vegetarian cuisine known as shojin ryori.

2. Showa Kinen Park

Showa Kinen Park is a spacious park that boasts lush greenery, gardens, and a lake. The park offers a variety of activities, such as cycling, boating, and picnicking. During the cherry blossom season in late March to early April, it is a popular spot for locals to enjoy hanami (flower viewing).

3. Nishitokyo Nature Park

Nishitokyo Nature Park is a nature reserve located in the western part of the city. The park offers hiking trails, birdwatching, and stunning views of the surrounding mountains.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Nishitokyo

If you are into hiking and biking, Nishitokyo has plenty of options to offer. Some recommended routes are:

  • Musashino Plateau Cycling Course: a 15 km course that takes you through beautiful scenery of nature and urban spaces.
  • Shiromaru Dam Hike: a 9 km hike that leads you through scenic views of lakes and forests.
  • Takahatafudoson Kongoji Temple Walk: a gentle walk that takes you through a residential area and the beautiful temple gardens.

Best Things to Do in Nishitokyo: Activities and Tours

1. Hot Air Balloon Ride

Enjoy the beautiful landscapes of Nishitokyo from a different perspective with a hot air balloon ride. This unique experience is available throughout the year, weather permitting.

2. Soba Making Workshop

Learn the art of soba making, a traditional Japanese noodle, with a workshop. You will learn the traditional method and enjoy your homemade soba at the end of the session.

3. Nishitokyo Cycling Tour

Explore the city on two wheels with a guided cycling tour. You will visit the city’s historical sites and scenic areas while enjoying the beautiful landscapes.

Week-Long Itinerary for Nishitokyo

Day 1: Visit Wada House and Fudaten Shrine followed by a lunch break at Takahatafudoson Kongoji Temple. Day 2: Explore the beautiful gardens of Showa Kinen Park and enjoy a picnic. Day 3: Take a hot air balloon ride followed by a soba making workshop. Day 4: Enjoy hiking at Shiromaru Dam. Day 5: Visit the Tamarokuto Science Center followed by a scenic ride on the Musashino Plateau Cycling Course. Day 6: Take a leisurely walk around Takahatafudoson Kongoji Temple. Day 7: Spend your last day exploring the Nishitokyo Nature Park and enjoy birdwatching.

Food and Drink Scene of Nishitokyo

Must-Try Dishes

1. Soba

Soba is a traditional Japanese noodle made with buckwheat flour. You can find soba in many restaurants in Nishitokyo, but trying the homemade soba at Takahatafudoson Kongoji Temple is a must.

2. Yakitori

Yakitori is a popular Japanese snack that consists of skewered chicken pieces grilled over charcoal. You can find many yakitori restaurants in Nishitokyo, especially in the downtown area near the train station.

Local Beverages

1. Sake

Sake is a traditional Japanese rice wine that is brewed all over Japan. In Nishitokyo, you can try the local breweries’ sake, which has a unique taste and aroma.

2. Green Tea

Green tea is a staple in Japan and is often served in restaurants and cafes. You can find high-quality green tea in Nishitokyo, especially at the Tamarokuto Science Center and Showa Kinen Park.

Where to Stay in Nishitokyo: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

1. Hotel Mets Tachikawa

Hotel Mets Tachikawa is a modern hotel located near Nishitokyo. It offers comfortable rooms, dining options, and easy access to transportation.

2. Hotel Route-Inn Tachikawa

Hotel Route-Inn Tachikawa is a business hotel that offers clean rooms, excellent hospitality, and a complimentary breakfast buffet.

3. Guesthouse Kichijoji

Guesthouse Kichijoji is a traditional Japanese guesthouse located in a quiet neighborhood in the city. It offers private and dormitory-style rooms and a cozy atmosphere.

What to Buy in Nishitokyo: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

1. Sake

Bring home a bottle of the local sake as a souvenir for your loved ones.

2. Japanese Lacquerware

Japanese lacquerware, also known as urushi, is a traditional craft made from the sap of the lacquer tree. You can find beautiful and unique pieces of lacquerware in Nishitokyo.

Local Culture and Customs of Nishitokyo

Nishitokyo is a traditional and culturally rich city. Visitors should show respect for local customs, such as removing shoes before entering temples or private homes, and avoid being too loud or disruptive in public spaces.

Best Time to Visit Nishitokyo

The best time to visit Nishitokyo is in the spring from mid-March to early May when the cherry blossoms are in full bloom, and the weather is mild. The city is also beautiful in autumn when the leaves change color in October and November.

Do’s and Don’ts of Nishitokyo

Do:

  • Observe local customs and traditions.
  • Try the local culinary specialties.
  • Explore the beautiful natural landscapes of Nishitokyo.

Don’t:

  • Be loud or disruptive in public spaces.
  • Ignore the local etiquette.
  • Leave trash in natural areas or public spaces.

Safety and Health

Safety in Nishitokyo

Nishitokyo is a safe city, and visitors should not encounter any major safety concerns. However, travelers should still exercise normal precautions, such as being aware of pickpockets in crowded areas and following traffic rules.

Health in Nishitokyo

Nishitokyo has excellent medical facilities, and visitors can find pharmacies and clinics throughout the city. It is recommended to have travel insurance and carry a first-aid kit if you plan on doing outdoor activities.

Getting to Nishitokyo: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Nishitokyo

The closest airport to Nishitokyo is Haneda Airport, which is located approximately 30 minutes away by train.

Bus Stations Near Nishitokyo

Nishitokyo does not have a bus station, but there are several bus stops throughout the city that connect to other areas in Tokyo.

Other Options

The most convenient way to get to Nishitokyo is by taking the train. The city has several train stations, including Nishi-Tachikawa Station, which is the busiest station in the city and a hub for transportation.

How to Get Around in Nishitokyo

The easiest way to get around Nishitokyo is by taking the train or bus. Taxis are also available, but they can be expensive. Biking is a popular option for exploring the city’s scenic areas, and bike rentals are available throughout the city.

Where to Go Next from Nishitokyo: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

1. Tokyo

Tokyo is a world-renowned city that has something for everyone, from shopping and entertainment to cultural experiences and historic sites. Tokyo is only a short train ride from Nishitokyo.

2. Mount Takao

Mount Takao is a popular hiking destination located west of Tokyo. The mountain offers scenic views, hiking trails, and a cable car that takes visitors to the top.

Conclusion

Nishitokyo is a hidden gem in Tokyo Prefecture, Japan, that offers a unique blend of traditional and modern culture. The city’s natural beauty and outdoor adventures make it an excellent destination for travelers looking to explore Japan’s traditional side. With this comprehensive travel guide, you can plan your trip to Nishitokyo with ease and make unforgettable memories.
