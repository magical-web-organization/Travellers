Introduction to Kichijoji

Kichijoji is a vibrant district in Tokyo with a distinct bohemian charm that attracts visitors from all over the world. It is located in the western part of the city, which means that it is a bit more laid back compared to the more fast-paced districts in central Tokyo. Kichijoji is also home to one of Tokyo’s largest parks, the Inokashira Park, which offers plenty of green spaces, a lake, and a zoo.

Kichijoji is a melting pot of cultures, with plenty of restaurants serving various cuisines like Thai, Italian, and Korean. The district also has a thriving nightlife, with numerous bars, cafes, and clubs that cater to all tastes. If you are looking for a unique Tokyo experience, Kichijoji is the place to be.

List of Places to Visit in Kichijoji

To help you plan your trip to Kichijoji, we have compiled a list of the top attractions and must-visit places in the neighborhood.

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Ghibli Museum: If you are a fan of the famous Japanese animation studio Studio Ghibli, a visit to its museum in Kichijoji is a must. The museum houses some of the studio’s iconic works and provides a behind-the-scenes look at the animation process.

  • Inokashira Park: The Inokashira Park is one of the largest and most popular parks in Tokyo. The park is home to a lake, a zoo, and plenty of walking paths that offer scenic views of the surrounding area.

  • Kichijoji Art Museum: This museum is dedicated to the works of famous Japanese artists, including Yasuo Kazuki and Jiro Yoshihara.

Natural Attractions of Kichijoji: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Inokashira Park: As mentioned earlier, the Inokashira Park is a must-visit destination in Kichijoji. Take a stroll around the lake or rent a pedal boat to explore the park from the water.

  • Nakamichi Road: This picturesque road is one of the most popular spots in Kichijoji for its vibrant foliage during autumn. Walk along the road and enjoy the scenic views of the colorful leaves.

  • Kichijoji Station Square: This bustling square is the heart of Kichijoji and offers plenty of opportunities to shop, dine, and people-watch.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Kichijoji

Kichijoji is home to many walking and cycling routes that offer stunning views of the surrounding area. Some of the most popular routes include:

  • Inokashira Park Avenue: This scenic avenue stretches from the park’s entrance to the gazebo and offers great views of the cherry blossoms in spring.

  • Tamagawa Josui Road: This walking trail runs parallel to the Tamagawa-Josui Aqueduct and is known for its beautiful cherry tree-lined roads and scenic views of the surrounding hills.

Best Things to Do in Kichijoji: Activities and Tours

Kichijoji offers plenty of activities and tours that cater to all tastes. Here are some of the best things to do in Kichijoji:

  • Experience Tokyo’s nightlife: Kichijoji’s bars and clubs offer a unique Tokyo experience. Try out some of the local drinks and dance the night away in one of the many clubs in the neighborhood.

  • Try some local delicacies: Kichijoji has plenty of restaurants that serve some of the most delicious authentic Japanese cuisine. Don’t miss out on the opportunity to try the famous tonkatsu or the mouth-watering soba.

Food and Drink Scene of Kichijoji

Kichijoji has a thriving food and drink scene, with plenty of restaurants and bars offering various cuisines and beverages. Here are some of the must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes:

  • Tonkatsu: This deep-fried pork cutlet is a Kichijoji specialty and should not be missed.

  • Soba: Kichijoji has plenty of soba restaurants that serve some of the most delicious soba noodles in the city.

Local Beverages:

  • Sake: Kichijoji is home to several sake breweries that produce some of the most unique and flavorful sake in the city.

  • Craft beer: Kichijoji has a growing craft beer scene, with several bars and breweries that serve some of the best craft beers in Tokyo.

Where to Stay in Kichijoji: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Kichijoji has plenty of accommodation options to choose from, including hotels, hostels, and guesthouses. Here are some of our top picks:

  • Kichijoji Tokyu REI Hotel: This hotel is located just a few minutes’ walk from Kichijoji Station and offers comfortable and affordable rooms.

  • K’s House Tokyo Oasis: This hostel is located near Inokashira Park and offers clean and spacious rooms at an affordable price.

What to Buy in Kichijoji: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Kichijoji has plenty of specialty stores that offer unique souvenirs and local products. Here are some of the best places to shop:

  • Nakamichi Shopping Street: This shopping street is known for its unique and quirky stores that offer everything from vintage clothing to handmade crafts.

  • Harmonica Yokocho: This pedestrian street is known for its vibrant nightlife and numerous bars, but it also has several stores that offer traditional Japanese crafts and souvenirs.

Local Culture and Customs of Kichijoji

Kichijoji has a unique culture that is influenced by its bohemian vibe and diverse population. Here are some of the local customs and traditions to keep in mind when visiting Kichijoji:

  • Respect for others: Japanese people value respect for others, so be mindful of your behavior and avoid causing any inconvenience to others.

  • Shoes off: In many places in Japan, including many homes, you are expected to take off your shoes before entering. Be sure to look out for signs that indicate this custom.

Best Time to Visit Kichijoji

The best time to visit Kichijoji is in spring when the cherry blossoms are in bloom or in autumn when the leaves turn vibrant shades of red and orange. However, Kichijoji is enjoyable all year round, with winter being the only season where you may want to pack extra layers to stay warm.

Do’s and Don’ts of Kichijoji

Here are some important do’s and don’ts to keep in mind when visiting Kichijoji:

Do:

  • Try some local cuisine and beverages.

  • Explore the beautiful parks and gardens in Kichijoji.

Don’t:

  • Be loud or rude in public places.

  • Smoke in prohibited areas.

Safety and Health

Safety in Kichijoji

Kichijoji is generally safe, but it is always important to take precautions when in a new environment. Here are some tips to keep in mind:

  • Keep an eye on your valuables, especially in crowded areas.

  • Stay aware of your surroundings and avoid walking alone late at night.

Health in Kichijoji

Kichijoji is a relatively safe area in terms of health hazards. However, it is important to take the usual precautions, such as staying hydrated and carrying any necessary medication with you.

Getting to Kichijoji: Transportation Options and Logistics

Kichijoji is easily accessible by public transport. Here are some transportation options to consider:

  • Airports Near Kichijoji: The closest airport to Kichijoji is Haneda Airport, which is located about 30 minutes away by train.

  • Bus Stations Near Kichijoji: There are several bus stations in Kichijoji that serve different destinations in and around Tokyo.

  • Other Options: Tokyo’s extensive subway system makes it easy to get around the city. Take the JR Chuo Line to Kichijoji Station, and you’ll be right in the heart of the neighborhood.

How to Get Around in Kichijoji

Kichijoji is a relatively small neighborhood that you can easily explore on foot or by bicycle. You can also use the public transport, such as buses and trains, to get around.

Where to Go Next from Kichijoji: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Kichijoji is located near several other interesting destinations that are worth a visit. Here are some suggestions:

  • Shibuya: This vibrant district is famous for its bustling shopping and entertainment scene, including the iconic Shibuya Crossing.

  • Shinjuku: Another popular district in Tokyo, Shinjuku is known for its towering skyscrapers, bustling nightlife, and beautiful parks.

Conclusion

Kichijoji is a unique Tokyo district that offers a perfect blend of culture, nature, and urban attractions. With plenty of activities and sights to see, Kichijoji is a must-visit destination on any Tokyo itinerary.
