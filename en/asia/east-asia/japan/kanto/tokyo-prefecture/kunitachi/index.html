Introduction to Kunitachi

Kunitachi is a small town located in the western part of Tokyo prefecture. The town is nestled between the Tama Hills and the Musashino Plateau, offering spectacular scenic views. Kunitachi is known for its laid-back atmosphere, friendly locals, and cultural significance. The town is home to several prestigious universities, making it a hub for students from all over Japan.

List of Places to Visit in Kunitachi

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

National Astronomical Observatory of Japan

The National Astronomical Observatory of Japan is the perfect place for astronomy enthusiasts. This research facility houses the world’s largest telescope, the Subaru Telescope, and offers stargazing events for visitors.

Kunitachi Fudōson Temple

Kunitachi Fudōson Temple is a Buddhist temple that dates back to the 8th century. The temple is known for its 2.6-meter tall Fudō Myō-ō statue, which is one of the largest stone statues in Japan.

Tama Art University Museum

The Tama Art University Museum is a contemporary art museum located on the campus of Tama Art University. The museum hosts several exhibitions and has an impressive collection of modern Japanese art.

Natural Attractions of Kunitachi: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Tama Hills

The Tama Hills are a mountain range that offers fantastic trekking and hiking opportunities. The hills are easy to access from Kunitachi, and the views of the surrounding countryside are breathtaking.

Musashino Plateau

The Musashino Plateau is a vast plain that stretches across several towns in Tokyo Prefecture. The plateau has several walking trails, and visitors can rent bicycles to explore the area.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Kunitachi

Tama Hills Hiking Trail

The Tama Hills Hiking Trail is a popular trekking route that starts from the Tama Hills Recreation Area. The trail passes through dense forests, streams, and small waterfalls, providing a peaceful and refreshing escape from city life.

Tamagawa Cycling Road

The Tamagawa Cycling Road is a 24km-long cycling route that runs parallel to the Tamagawa River. The route passes through several towns, including Kunitachi, and has plenty of rest areas with picturesque views.

Best Things to Do in Kunitachi: Activities and Tours

Tama River Boat Tours

Tama River Boat Tours are a unique way to explore the beauty of Kunitachi’s surroundings. The tour takes visitors down the Tamagawa River, offering stunning views of the surrounding landscapes.

Local Food and Cooking Classes

Kunitachi is known for its delicious cuisine, and visitors can take cooking classes to learn how to prepare local dishes like Tonkatsu (crumbed pork cutlets) and Takikomi Gohan (Japanese mixed rice).

Food and Drink Scene of Kunitachi

Must-Try Dishes

Tonkatsu

Tonkatsu is a local specialty that consists of a pork cutlet dipped in panko breadcrumbs and deep-fried until crispy. The dish is usually served with a side of shredded cabbage and a tangy sauce.

Takikomi Gohan

Takikomi Gohan is a dish made with Japanese mixed rice cooked with vegetables, seafood, and meat. The dish is flavorful and often served in special occasions.

Local Beverages

Sake

Sake is a Japanese rice wine that’s commonly served with meals. Kunitachi has several local breweries that produce high-quality sake, and visitors can take sake tasting tours to learn more about this traditional beverage.

Where to Stay in Kunitachi: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Kunitachi has several accommodation options ranging from luxury hotels to budget-friendly hostels. Some popular options include Kunitachi Hotel, Smile Hotel Tokyo Tama Center, and Capsule Inn Tamagawa.

What to Buy in Kunitachi: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Kunitachi Pottery

Kunitachi is known for its traditional pottery, particularly Nakazato Taroemon Kiln. Visitors can purchase handmade pottery at several shops in the town.

Local Culture and Customs of Kunitachi

Kunitachi has a rich cultural heritage and is known for its vibrant festivals and events throughout the year, such as Kunitachi Sakura Festival and Kunitachi Autumn Festival. Locals are typically warm and welcoming, and visitors are encouraged to respect local customs and etiquette.

Best Time to Visit Kunitachi

The best time to visit Kunitachi is from late March to early May, when the cherry blossoms bloom and the town is covered with pink petals. The autumn months of September and October are also ideal for visiting, with pleasant temperatures and colorful foliage.

Do’s and Don’ts of Kunitachi

Do:

  • Take off your shoes before entering traditional Japanese establishments like temples and traditional restaurants.
  • Respect local customs and traditions.
  • Try local cuisine and beverages.

Don’t:

  • Talk loudly or create a disturbance in public places.
  • Ignore traffic laws or signs.
  • Smoke or litter in public areas.

Safety and Health

Safety in Kunitachi

Kunitachi is a safe town, with low crime rates and a friendly community. Visitors should take standard precautions like carrying valuables with them and observing traffic laws.

Health in Kunitachi

Kunitachi has several hospitals and medical facilities that offer high-quality care. Visitors should have proper travel insurance and take basic precautions like washing hands regularly and staying hydrated.

Getting to Kunitachi: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Kunitachi

The closest airport to Kunitachi is Tokyo Haneda International Airport, which is approximately 40km away. Visitors can take a train or bus from the airport to reach Kunitachi.

Bus Stations Near Kunitachi

Kunitachi has several bus stations that connect the town to nearby destinations. Some popular bus stations include Kunitachi Station, Higashi-Kunitachi Station, and Kunitachi Civic Hall Station.

Other Options

Visitors can also reach Kunitachi by train, with the town being accessible via the JR Chuo Line and the Seibu Tamagawa Line.

How to Get Around in Kunitachi

Kunitachi is a relatively small town, and most attractions are easily accessible on foot or by bicycle. Visitors can also take buses or taxis to reach more distant places.

Where to Go Next from Kunitachi: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Visitors can explore nearby towns and cities like Mitaka, Chofu, and Shinjuku. The Tama Hills and the Musashino Plateau offer several outdoor activities like trekking, hiking, and biking.

Conclusion

Kunitachi is a gem of a town that deserves to be on every Japan traveler’s itinerary. From its historical landmarks to its outdoor adventures and mouth-watering cuisine, Kunitachi has something to offer everyone. With this comprehensive travel guide, visitors can explore the beauty of this charming little town and create unforgettable memories.
