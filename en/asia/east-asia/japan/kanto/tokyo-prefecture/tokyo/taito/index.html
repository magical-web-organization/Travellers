Introduction to Taito

Taito is a historical district located in Tokyo, the capital city of Japan. The district is a testament to the country’s rich culture and history, with numerous temples, shrines, and museums dotting its landscape. The district has a population of approximately 180,000 people and is easily accessible through several transportation options, including buses and trains.

This guide will provide you with a comprehensive overview of Taito’s must-visit places, food and drink scene, where to stay, and other important information to make your trip to Taito an unforgettable experience.

List of Places to Visit in Taito

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Sensoji Temple

Sensoji Temple, also known as Asakusa Temple, is a must-visit destination in Taito, Tokyo. It is one of the oldest and most famous temples in the city and attracts millions of tourists every year. Visitors can explore the temple’s gardens, observe traditional Japanese ceremonies, and participate in cultural events throughout the year.

Tokyo National Museum

The Tokyo National Museum is one of the largest and most comprehensive museums in Japan. The museum has a vast collection of artworks and artifacts that represent Japan’s history, culture, and heritage, including samurai weapons, Buddhist artifacts, and Ukiyo-e prints, among others.

Ueno Park

Ueno Park is a massive green space that houses several museums, shrines, and gardens. It is a popular destination among locals and tourists alike, and you can spend an entire day exploring the park’s attractions. The park also has a zoo, which is ideal for families with children.

Natural Attractions of Taito: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Skytree Tower

Skytree Tower is the tallest observation tower in Japan, offering panoramic views of Tokyo and its surroundings. The tower has two observation decks, and visitors can marvel at the breathtaking scenery while enjoying drinks and snacks at the cafe and restaurant.

Sumida River

Sumida River is a serene and peaceful river that flows through the heart of Tokyo. Visitors can enjoy a leisurely boat ride or take a stroll along the riverbank and enjoy the sights and sounds of Tokyo.

Yanaka Cemetery

Yanaka Cemetery is a peaceful and picturesque cemetery that houses several famous and historic graves. The cemetery is a serene place to escape the hustle and bustle of Tokyo and has several temples and shrines within its boundaries.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Taito

Arakawa River

Arakawa River is an ideal destination for biking and hiking enthusiasts. The river runs through several districts of Tokyo, including Taito, and has a well-maintained cycling and walking path that runs alongside it. Visitors can enjoy the scenic views of Tokyo while cycling or walking along the river.

Ueno Park

Ueno Park has several walking and hiking paths that take visitors through its many attractions, including shrines, temples, and museums. The park also has several gardens showcasing different types of plants and flowers.

Best Things to Do in Taito: Activities and Tours

Sake Tasting Tour

Taito is known for its rich sake production, and visitors can take a sake tasting tour to sample various sake brands and learn about the sake-making process.

Ninja Experience

Taito has several activity centers that offer visitors a chance to experience the traditional art of ninja. Visitors can put on ninja costumes and participate in various ninja-themed activities and lessons.

Food and Drink Scene of Taito

Must-Try Dishes

Okonomiyaki

Okonomiyaki is a famous Japanese dish that is available in many restaurants in Taito. It is a savory pancake that typically contains shredded cabbage, meat, seafood, and other vegetables.

Takoyaki

Takoyaki is another popular Japanese dish that is available in many street food stalls in Taito. It is a ball-shaped snack that contains diced octopus, savory batter, and various seasonings.

Local Beverages

Sake

Sake is a traditional Japanese alcoholic drink made of fermented rice. Taito is known for its rich sake production, and visitors can taste various sake brands produced in the district.

Where to Stay in Taito: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Hotel Sardonyx Ueno

Hotel Sardonyx Ueno is a 3-star hotel located near Ueno Station. The hotel has various room types, including twin and deluxe rooms, and offers several amenities, such as free Wi-Fi, massage services, and a restaurant.

Anne Hostel Asakusabashi

Anne Hostel Asakusabashi is a budget-friendly hostel located near Asakusabashi Station. The hostel has various room types, including dormitory and private rooms, and offers several amenities, such as free Wi-Fi, luggage storage, and a shared kitchen.

What to Buy in Taito: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Yukata

Yukata is a traditional Japanese garment that is worn during summer festivals and other cultural events. It is available in many shops in Taito, and visitors can buy them as souvenirs.

Ukiyo-e Prints

Ukiyo-e prints are traditional Japanese woodblock prints that depict various aspects of Japanese culture and history. Taito has several shops that sell Ukiyo-e prints, and visitors can buy them as souvenirs.

Local Culture and Customs of Taito

Taito is known for its rich and vibrant culture, and visitors are expected to show respect and appreciation for its customs and traditions. Some of the customs and traditions include bowing as a gesture of respect, taking off shoes before entering temples and shrines, and following proper table manners.

Best Time to Visit Taito

The best time to visit Taito is during spring (March to May) or autumn (September to November) when the weather is pleasant, and there are several cultural events and festivals taking place throughout the district.

Do’s and Don’ts of Taito

Do:

  • Show respect to the local culture and customs
  • Carry cash as some stores and restaurants do not accept credit cards
  • Use the public transportation system

Don’t:

  • Eat or drink while walking in public
  • Smoke in non-designated areas
  • Take photographs of people without their permission

Safety and Health

Safety in Taito

Taito is a safe district, and visitors can explore its attractions without worrying about crimes or theft. However, visitors are advised to take necessary precautions, such as keeping their valuables safe and being aware of their surroundings.

Health in Taito

Taito has several hospitals and clinics that provide medical assistance to visitors in case of emergencies. Visitors should have medical insurance and carry their medications with them.

Getting to Taito: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Taito

Haneda Airport and Narita International Airport are the two airports near Tokyo. Haneda Airport is closer to Taito and is well-connected through trains, taxis, and buses.

Bus Stations Near Taito

Taito has several bus stations that provide connectivity to surrounding districts and prefectures. The most popular bus stations in Taito are Asakusa Bus Station and Ueno Okachimachi Bus Station.

Other Options

Visitors can travel to Taito through trains and taxis as well. Taito has several train stations that provide connectivity to other districts in Tokyo, and taxis are available 24/7 throughout the district.

How to Get Around in Taito

The most popular transportation option in Taito is the metro system. Visitors can buy day passes or travel cards to save money while using the metro to visit different attractions. Taxis and buses are also available for visitors who prefer more private transportation.

Where to Go Next from Taito: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Akihabara

Akihabara is a neighboring district of Taito that is famous for its electronic gadgets, anime and manga products, and gaming arcades.

Asakusa

Asakusa is a neighboring district of Taito that is famous for Sensoji Temple, Asakusa Shrine, and Nakamise shopping street. Visitors can explore traditional Japanese architecture, culture, and heritage in Asakusa.

Conclusion

Taito is a district that perfectly represents the traditional and modern aspects of Japanese culture. Visitors can enjoy the district’s numerous attractions, taste its delicious food, and explore its vibrant culture during their visit. With this comprehensive tour guide, visitors can plan their trip to Taito with ease and have an unforgettable experience.
