Introduction to Shibuya

Located in the heart of Tokyo, Shibuya is a bustling neighborhood known for its trendy fashion, lively entertainment, and bustling nightlife. Home to the famous Shibuya Crossing, a symbol of Tokyo’s modernity, Shibuya attracts millions of visitors each year.

But Shibuya is more than just a bustling commercial hub. This neighborhood has a rich history and cultural heritage that is visible in its museums, temples, and shrines, making it a must-visit destination for travelers who want to experience the best of Tokyo’s past and present.

In this comprehensive guide, we’ll explore all there is to know about Shibuya, from its cultural attractions and outdoor adventures, to its food scene, accommodations, and transportation options. Whether you’re a first-time visitor or a seasoned traveler, this guide will help you make the most of your trip to Shibuya.

List of Places to Visit in Shibuya

No trip to Shibuya is complete without visiting its cultural attractions, exploring its natural beauty, and trying its diverse cuisine. Here are some of the top places to visit in Shibuya:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Shibuya Crossing: A symbol of Tokyo’s modernity, the Shibuya Crossing is one of the busiest pedestrian crossings in the world, attracting thousands of visitors each day.

  • Meiji Jingu Shrine: One of Tokyo’s most famous shrines, Meiji Jingu Shrine was built in honor of Emperor Meiji and Empress Shoken. Visitors can enjoy serene walks through the forested grounds and witness traditional Shinto rituals.

  • Yoyogi Park: With over 134 acres of greenery, Yoyogi Park is one of Tokyo’s largest parks. It’s a great spot for picnics, jogging, and people-watching.

  • Bunkamura: This complex of museums and performance venues hosts a variety of exhibitions and events throughout the year, showcasing art, music, and theatre.

  • NHK Studio Park: This television studio offers a glimpse behind the scenes of Japan’s most famous programs and is a great spot for families with children.

Natural Attractions of Shibuya: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Mount Takao: Just outside of Shibuya, Mount Takao is a popular hiking spot that offers stunning views of Tokyo and its surroundings.

  • Jingu Gaien Ginkgo Avenue: A beautiful street lined with ginkgo trees that turn golden in the fall.

  • Yoyogi Hachimangu Shrine: This traditional shrine is known for its sumo wrestling events and its beautiful autumn foliage.

  • Todoroki Valley: Located in the quieter parts of Shibuya, Todoroki Valley is a peaceful retreat from the city, with a small river and walking paths surrounded by trees and flowers.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Shibuya

Shibuya is a great place for outdoor enthusiasts who want to explore the city on foot or on bike. Here are some of the best hiking and biking routes in Shibuya:

  • Shibuya to Shinjuku Walking Route: This 6.5-kilometer route takes you through some of Tokyo’s most vibrant neighborhoods, including Shibuya, Harajuku, and Shinjuku.

  • Shibuya to Yoyogi Park Cycling Route: This 4-kilometer route takes you through quiet backstreets and along the Yamanote Line, leading to one of Tokyo’s most beautiful parks.

  • Mount Takao Hiking Route: This 6.5-kilometer trail leads to the summit of Mount Takao, offering stunning views of Tokyo and its surroundings.

Best Things to Do in Shibuya: Activities and Tours

Shibuya is a hub of activity and entertainment, with plenty of options for fun and adventure. Here are some of the best things to do in Shibuya:

  • Karaoke: Japan’s favorite pastime, karaoke bars are ubiquitous in Shibuya, offering hours of entertainment for groups of friends and families.

  • Shopping: Shibuya is known for its trendy fashion and shopping, from luxury department stores like Shibuya 109 to quirky boutiques and vintage shops.

  • Izakaya: These casual Japanese pubs offer a chance to enjoy good food, drinks, and conversation with friends.

  • Robot Restaurant: This kitschy entertainment venue features futuristic performances, featuring robots and dancers.

Week-Long Itinerary for Shibuya

If you have a week to explore Shibuya and its surroundings, here’s a sample itinerary to guide your trip:

Day 1: Explore Shibuya’s cultural attractions, including the Meiji Jingu Shrine, the Yoyogi Park, and Bunkamura.

Day 2: Take a day trip to Mount Takao for hiking and scenic views.

Day 3: Visit the NHK Studio Park for a behind-the-scenes look at Japanese television, followed by a shopping trip in Shibuya.

Day 4: Explore the food scene of Shibuya, with visits to traditional Izakaya and trendy restaurants.

Day 5: Take a day trip to Kamakura, a historic town known for its temples and beaches.

Day 6: Experience the fast-paced nightlife of Shibuya, with visits to karaoke bars, nightclubs, and the famous Shibuya Crossing.

Day 7: Relax and enjoy some of Shibuya’s peaceful green spaces, like Todoroki Valley or Jingu Gaien Ginkgo Avenue.

Food and Drink Scene of Shibuya

Shibuya is a food lover’s paradise, with a diverse range of local and international cuisine. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Ramen: Japan’s famous noodle soup, with various styles and flavors available in Shibuya.

  • Sushi: A classic Japanese dish, with fresh and delicious sushi available at Shibuya’s many sushi restaurants.

  • Takoyaki: A snack made of octopus balls filled with batter, often served at food stalls around Shibuya.

  • Yakitori: Grilled skewers of chicken and vegetables, often enjoyed with drinks at Izakaya.

Local Beverages

  • Sake: Japan’s famous rice wine, available in a variety of flavors and styles, from dry to sweet.

  • Umeshu: A sweet and fruity plum wine, often served as a digestif.

  • Shochu: A distilled spirit made from barley, potatoes, or other grains, often served with izakaya food.

Local Culture and Customs of Shibuya

Shibuya is a modern and cosmopolitan neighborhood, but it still retains its traditional values and customs. To show respect for the local culture, here are some important things to keep in mind:

  • Bowing: In Japan, bowing is a sign of respect and appreciation. It’s common to bow when greeting someone or expressing gratitude.

  • Shoes: In Japan, it’s customary to take off your shoes before entering homes, temples, and other traditional spaces.

  • Language: Although many people in Shibuya speak English, it’s always appreciated when travelers make an effort to learn a few basic phrases in Japanese.

Best Time to Visit Shibuya

Shibuya is a year-round destination, but the best time to visit depends on your preferences and interests. Here are some tips:

  • Spring (March to May): Cherry blossoms bloom, and the weather is mild and pleasant.

  • Summer (June to August): Temperatures are hot and humid, but outdoor festivals and events are plentiful.

  • Fall (September to November): The weather is crisp and cool, and the autumn foliage is beautiful.

  • Winter (December to February): Tokyo can be chilly in the winter, but holiday decorations and events add to the festive atmosphere.

Do’s and Don’ts of Shibuya

To help you navigate Shibuya like a local and show respect for its customs and culture, here are some do’s and don’ts:

Do:

  • Bow when greeting someone or expressing gratitude.
  • Take off your shoes before entering traditional spaces.
  • Follow local customs and traditions.
  • Try the local cuisine and beverages.
  • Explore both the well-known and hidden gems of Shibuya.

Don’t:

  • Be loud and disruptive in public spaces.
  • Wear shoes in traditional spaces.
  • Take photographs of people without permission.
  • Leave trash or litter on the streets.

Safety and Health

Shibuya is generally a safe and clean neighborhood, but it’s always important to take precautions to protect yourself and maintain good health.

Safety in Shibuya

  • Watch out for pickpockets in crowded areas like Shibuya Crossing.
  • Be aware of your surroundings, especially at night.
  • Use caution when crossing busy streets and intersections.
  • Follow local laws and regulations.
  • Keep personal belongings secure.

Health in Shibuya

  • Be mindful of food allergies and dietary restrictions
  • Stay hydrated in Tokyo’s humid climate
  • Pack sunscreen and protective clothing for outdoor activities
  • Consult with a doctor or medical professional if you have any health concerns

Getting to Shibuya: Transportation Options and Logistics

Shibuya is easily accessible via public transportation, with a variety of options to choose from.

Airports Near Shibuya

  • Haneda Airport: Tokyo’s closest airport, located about 25 minutes from Shibuya by train.
  • Narita Airport: The main airport in Tokyo, located about 75 minutes from Shibuya by train.

Bus Stations Near Shibuya

  • Shibuya Bus Terminal: A major bus terminal in Tokyo, located in the heart of Shibuya.

Other Options

  • Train: Shibuya is served by several train lines, including the JR Yamanote Line, the Ginza Line, and the Hanzomon Line.
  • Taxi: Taxis are readily available in Shibuya, but they can be expensive.
  • Bicycle: Shibuya is a bike-friendly neighborhood, with plenty of rental options available.

How to Get Around Shibuya

Shibuya is a compact neighborhood that is easy to navigate on foot or by bicycle. However, if you need to cover longer distances, here are some transportation options:

  • Train: Shibuya is served by several train lines, including the JR Yamanote Line, the Ginza Line, and the Hanzomon Line.

  • Bus: Buses are another affordable option for traveling within Shibuya and its surrounding neighborhoods.

  • Taxi: Taxis are readily available in Shibuya, but they can be expensive.

Where to Go Next from Shibuya: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Shibuya is a great starting point for exploring Tokyo and its surrounding areas. Here are some nearby destinations and excursions to consider:

  • Harajuku: A trendy and fashionable district known for its street fashion and quirky shops.

  • Shinjuku: Tokyo’s business district, with a lively nightlife and plethora of restaurants.

  • Kamakura: A historic town known for its temples and beaches, located just a short train ride from Shibuya.

Conclusion

From its cultural attractions and outdoor adventures to its food scene, accommodations, and transportation options, Shibuya has something for every traveler. By following this comprehensive guide, you’ll be able to experience the best of Shibuya and make the most of your trip to Tokyo. Whether you’re visiting for the first time or the tenth, Shibuya’s vibrancy and energy are sure to leave a lasting impression.
