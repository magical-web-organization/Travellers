Introduction to East

East, Tokyo is a dynamic region nestled in the heart of the Kanto area, in the eastern part of Japan. It boasts a unique blend of ancient culture and modernization, vibrant cities and idyllic landscapes, that attracts millions of visitors from all over the world every year. With rich cultural heritage, stunning natural beauty, and plenty of thrilling activities, East is a must-visit destination for travelers seeking to discover the best of Japan.

List of Places to Visit in East

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Senso-ji Temple: The oldest and most famous temple in Tokyo, famous for its imposing gate and Nakamise-dori street.
  • Edo-Tokyo Museum: A fascinating museum that tells the story of Tokyo’s rich history, culture, and art.
  • Tokyo National Museum: Home to a vast collection of Japanese art and artifacts, including pottery, textiles, and samurai swords.
  • Imperial Palace: The residence of the Imperial Family of Japan, surrounded by lush gardens and moats.
  • Ueno Park: A sprawling park with countless attractions, such as the Tokyo Zoo, the National Science Museum, and several temples.

Natural Attractions of East: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Mount Takao: A scenic mountain located in western Tokyo, which offers stunning views and several hiking trails.
  • Tokyo Bay: A picturesque bay with a plethora of water activities, such as sailing, kayaking, and wind surfing.
  • Ogasawara Islands: A UNESCO World Heritage Site, consisting of a chain of volcanic islands, offering superb snorkeling and diving opportunities.
  • Chichibu-Tama-Kai National Park: A vast national park that stretches over four prefectures, with endless opportunities for outdoor activities, such as camping, hiking, and skiing.

Hiking/Biking Routes of East

  • Tama River Cycling Road: A scenic route that follows the banks of Tama River, stretching over 50 kilometers.
  • Mount Mitake: A popular hiking destination, with stunning views and sacred temples.
  • Okutama Mukashi Michi Trail: A scenic trail that follows the old Tokaido road, offering a glimpse into Japan’s ancient past.
  • Mt. Tsukuba: A breathtaking mountain with several hiking trails, including a cable car and a ropeway.

Best Things to Do in East: Activities and Tours

  • Sumo Wrestling: As Japan’s national sport, watching a sumo wrestling match is an experience not to be missed.
  • Traditional Tea Ceremony: Experience the art of the Japanese tea ceremony, a centuries-old tradition that is steeped in culture and history.
  • Visit the Ghibli Museum: Dedicated to showcasing the works of legendary animation studio Ghibli, this museum is a must-see for fans of Miyazaki’s films.
  • River Cruise: Take a relaxing river cruise along Tokyo Bay, while enjoying the stunning skyline and iconic landmarks.
  • Visit an Onsen: A traditional hot spring bath that is deeply rooted in Japanese customs, offering a soothing and relaxing experience.

Food and Drink Scene of East

Must-Try Dishes

  • Sushi: East is home to some of the best sushi restaurants in Japan, serving fresh and delicious sushi made with local ingredients.
  • Unagi: Grilled eel, served on a bed of rice, is a popular and delicious dish in East.
  • Ramen: Japan’s beloved noodle dish comes in many variations, and East has some of the best ramen shops in the world.
  • Tempura: Light and crispy fried seafood and vegetables, dipped in a flavorful sauce, makes for a delightful meal in East.

Local Beverages

  • Sake: Japan’s national drink, made from fermented rice, comes in many varieties, and East has some of the best sake breweries in the country.
  • Shochu: A distilled spirit, made from a variety of ingredients, such as barley, sweet potatoes, and rice.
  • Matcha: A vividly green tea powder, used in traditional tea ceremonies, and considered a superfood with many health benefits.

Where to Stay in East: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Park Hyatt Tokyo: A luxurious hotel with stunning views of Tokyo and impeccable service.
  • Sakura Hostel Asakusa: A budget-friendly hostel with a convenient location, comfortable rooms, and friendly staff.
  • Traditional Ryokan Seikanso: A traditional Japanese guesthouse, with elegant tatami rooms and a peaceful garden.

What to Buy in East: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Tokyo Banana: A sponge cake filled with banana cream, and wrapped in a cute anime-like packaging.
  • Hakone Yosegi Zaiku: Beautifully crafted wooden puzzles and boxes, made using traditional Japanese techniques.
  • Kamakura Bori: Intricately carved wooden pieces made in the town of Kamakura, perfect for decorating your home.

Local Culture and Customs of East

  • Respect the Elders: Showing respect for elders is a traditional Japanese custom, and is highly valued in East as well.
  • Take off your Shoes: It is customary to take off your shoes before entering homes, temples, and some restaurants in Japan.
  • Learn some Japanese Phrases: Even if it’s just basic phrases, such as “arigato” (thank you) and “sumimasen” (excuse me), it will go a long way in showing your appreciation for Japanese culture.

Best Time to Visit East

The best time to visit East is between March and May, during the cherry blossom season, when the region is covered in pink and white hues, and celebrates the iconic hanami (flower viewing) tradition.

Do’s and Don’ts of East

Do:

  • Respect the local customs and traditions
  • Try local foods and drinks
  • Carry cash, as some places do not accept credit cards

Don’t:

  • Disrespect temples and shrines by sitting on the altar or disturbing the peace
  • Talk loudly on public transport
  • Take pictures without permission

Safety and Health

Safety in East

East is generally a safe region, with low crime rates, and a high standard of safety and hygiene. However, it is always advisable to take basic safety precautions, such as keeping your valuables safe, and being aware of your surroundings at all times.

Health in East

Japan has an excellent healthcare system, with modern facilities and highly-trained medical professionals. Visitors are advised to get comprehensive travel insurance before their visit, and to carry any necessary medications with them.

Getting to East: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near East

  • Narita International Airport: The main international airport serving Tokyo, located about 70 kilometers from East.
  • Haneda Airport: A domestic and international airport, located about 20 kilometers from East.

Bus Stations Near East

  • Tokyo Station: A major transportation hub, with bus services to several destinations in and around Tokyo.

Other Options

  • Train: Tokyo has an extensive train network, consisting of several lines and stations, making it convenient and easy to get around.

How to Get Around in East

East is well-connected by train, bus, and subway, providing easy and affordable transportation options for visitors. The Tokyo Metro and Toei Subway lines cover most of East, and a prepaid Suica or Pasmo card is a convenient option for travel.

Where to Go Next from East: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Nikko: A UNESCO World Heritage Site, famous for its scenic beauty and historical landmarks, such as the Nikko Toshogu Shrine.
  • Yokohama: Japan’s second-largest city, located just south of Tokyo, offering a mix of modern and traditional attractions, such as the striking Minato Mirai and the historic Chinatown.
  • Kamakura: A charming coastal town, famous for its Shinto shrines and Buddhist temples, and picturesque hiking trails.

Conclusion

East, Tokyo offers a unique and fascinating travel experience, that combines ancient cultural heritage and modern amenities, scenic natural beauty and thrilling adventures. Whether you’re a history buff, an outdoor enthusiast, a foodie, or a culture geek, East has something for everyone, making it an unforgettable destination for travelers seeking to discover the best of Japan.
