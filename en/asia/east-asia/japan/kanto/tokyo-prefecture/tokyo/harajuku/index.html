A Complete Guide to Harajuku: Best Things to Do, Places to Visit, and More

Located in the heart of Tokyo, Harajuku is a vibrant and exciting neighborhood that is home to the city’s most unique street fashion, delicious food, and fascinating culture. With so many things to see and do in Harajuku, it can be overwhelming to plan a trip here. That’s why we’ve created this comprehensive guide to help you make the most of your trip to Harajuku.

Introduction to Harajuku

Harajuku is a district in the Shibuya ward of Tokyo, Japan. Known for its colorful street fashion, Harajuku is a hub of Japanese youth culture and is home to some of the city’s most popular tourist attractions. The area is also known for its cozy cafes, specialty boutiques, and vintage record shops, making it a hub for culture and creativity.

List of Places to Visit in Harajuku

Below is a list of the top places to visit in Harajuku:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  1. Meiji Jingu Shrine - A beautiful shrine surrounded by a lush forest that is said to house the spirits of Emperor Meiji and Empress Shoken.

  2. Togo Shrine - A famous Shinto shrine dedicated to Admiral Togo Heihachiro, who is known for his role in the Russo-Japanese War.

  3. Nezu Museum - A serene museum showcasing various artworks from Japan and East Asia, surrounded by a beautiful Japanese garden.

Natural Attractions of Harajuku: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  1. Yoyogi Park - A beautiful park stretching over 134 acres, surrounded by forests and featuring walking paths, a jogging track, and open spaces.

  2. Mount Takao - A popular hiking destination with trails of varying difficulty levels, excellent views of the city, and a beautiful temple at the top.

  3. Shibuya Crossing - Located just south of Harajuku, this is famously the busiest pedestrian crossing in the world.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Harajuku

  1. Harajuku to Omotesando - A beautiful tree-lined street that’s perfect for a gentle stroll or a bike ride.

  2. Komazawa Olympic Park - A large park area dedicated to the 1964 Tokyo Olympics, featuring beautiful gardens and walking paths.

  3. Shinjuku Gyoen National Garden - A beautiful park with lush gardens, tranquil ponds, and ample space to walk, bike or relax.

Best Things to Do in Harajuku: Activities and Tours

  1. Cat Street - A stylish alleyway lined with trendy boutiques and cafes that leads from Harajuku to Shibuya.

  2. Takeshita Street - Known for its unique street fashion, Takeshita Street is a must-visit destination for all fashion enthusiasts.

  3. Onden Shrine - Known for its charming red torii gate entrance.

Week-Long Itinerary for Harajuku

Day 1: Visit Meiji Jingu Shrine and explore Yoyogi Park Day 2: Take a bike tour of Harajuku, visit Takeshita Street, and Onden Shrine Day 3: Head to Mount Takao for a day of hiking and beautiful views of Tokyo Day 4: Tour the Nezu Museum and visit the nearby Togo Shrine Day 5: Take a walk along Cat Street and browse the stores and cafes on the way Day 6: Visit Shibuya Crossing and explore Komazawa Olympic Park Day 7: Spend a day relaxing in Shinjuku Gyoen National Garden

Food and Drink Scene of Harajuku

Harajuku is home to several incredible restaurants and cafes, so here are a few dishes and local beverages that you should try while here:

Must-Try Dishes

  1. Totti Candy Factory - Offering an array of excellent sweets and cotton candy.

  2. Kawaii Monster Cafe - Try their rainbow-colored burgers and milkshakes.

Local Beverages

  1. Harajuku Brewery Taproom - Excellent craft beer from around Japan.

  2. Sugamo Aji no Mingei - Famous for its Shibuya Honey Lemonade.

Where to Stay in Harajuku: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Harajuku has a variety of options for travelers, including hostels, hotels, and guesthouses.

Top Hotels:

  1. Park Hyatt Tokyo - A luxury hotel with stunning views of the city and an excellent location.

  2. The Millennials Shibuya - Ultra-modern capsule hotel that offers private en suite showers, relaxation pods, and incredible technology.

Mid-Range Hotels:

  1. The B Tokyo Sangenjaya - A cozy capsule hotel for budget travelers.

  2. DG Budget Hotel - An affordable hotel with an excellent location.

Guesthouses:

  1. Oedo Guesthouse - A comfortable and affordable guesthouse that is located close to many of the attractions in Harajuku.

  2. Oak Hostel - A trendy and modern hostel that offers private rooms and shared dormitories.

What to Buy in Harajuku: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Harajuku is a great place to shop for unique souvenirs and local products. Here are some things you should consider bringing home:

  1. Vintage clothing - Harajuku is known for its vintage clothing stores, which offer unique and upcycled pieces.

  2. Kawaii items - Japanese culture is full of kawaii (cute) items so you could consider bringing back items like character hats and plushies.

Local Culture and Customs of Harajuku

Harajuku is known for its vibrant street fashion and youth culture, so be sure to respect the customs and dress respectfully when visiting. Taking pictures is generally allowed, but be sure to ask permission first.

Best Time to Visit Harajuku

Harajuku is a great place to visit any time of year, although the best times to go are during the spring (cherry blossom season) or fall (beautiful foliage).

Do’s and Don’ts of Harajuku

Do:

  1. Be respectful of local customs and dress codes.

  2. Visit different areas of Harajuku to get a sense of the neighborhood’s unique culture.

Don’t:

  1. Take pictures of people without their permission.

  2. Litter or vandalize public spaces.

Safety and Health

Safety in Harajuku

Harajuku is generally a safe area, but be aware of pickpockets in crowded areas like Takeshita Street.

Health in Harajuku

Be sure to stay properly hydrated and bring sunscreen during the summer months as temperatures and humidity can be high.

Getting to Harajuku: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Harajuku

  1. Narita International Airport - Located in Tokyo’s neighboring city, Narita, about 1 hour and 30 minutes away from Harajuku.

  2. Haneda International Airport - Located in Tokyo, about 30 minutes away from Harajuku.

Bus Stations Near Harajuku

Frequent buses are available from both airports that run toward different parts of Tokyo.

Other Options

Taking a taxi or ride-hailing service (Uber or Lyft) is a good option if you don’t want to deal with public transportation.

How to Get Around in Harajuku

Walking is the best way to explore Harajuku, although you can also rent bikes to discover the area if you prefer cycling.

Where to Go Next from Harajuku: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  1. Shibuya - Located just south of Harajuku, Shibuya is famous for its fashion, nightlife, and street crossings.

  2. Shinjuku - One of Tokyo’s largest business districts that is full of nightlife, restaurants, and shopping.

Conclusion

Harajuku is a unique and exciting neighborhood that is worth visiting in Tokyo. With its vibrant street fashion, delicious food, and fascinating culture, Harajuku has something for everyone. We hope that this guide has helped you plan your trip and find the best things to do, places to visit, where to stay, and more.
