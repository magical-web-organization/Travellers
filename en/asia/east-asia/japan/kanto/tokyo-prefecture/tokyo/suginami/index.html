Introduction to Suginami

Suginami is a special ward in Tokyo, Japan, located in the western part of the city. It is known for its unique blend of modern and traditional Japanese culture. The neighborhood is surrounded by lush greenery, temples, shrines, and museums that attract locals and tourists alike. If you’re planning a trip to Suginami and want to make the most of your visit, then this comprehensive travel guide is just what you need.

List of Places to Visit in Suginami

Suginami has something to offer to everyone, whether you’re a history buff, a nature lover or a foodie. Here are some must-visit places in Suginami:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Suginami Animation Museum: Explore the history of Japanese animation and its impact on popular culture.
  • Tokyo Fuji Art Museum: Discover a vast collection of Japanese art and artifacts, including paintings, ceramics, and sculptures.
  • Ghibli Museum: The museum features exhibits that showcase the works of legendary Japanese filmmaker Hayao Miyazaki.
  • Koenji Temple: This temple, located in the center of Koenji, is dedicated to the Buddhist goddess Fudo Myoo.

Natural Attractions of Suginami: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Zenpukuji Park: One of the largest parks in Suginami, has walking trails, a boathouse, a playground, and a large pond.
  • Tama Forest Science Garden: The Park features various species of trees and flowers and has several walking trails.
  • Ogikubo Sun Road: This shopping street is known for its quirky shops, vintage stores, and hidden bars.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Suginami

Suginami is known for unique and secluded paths perfect for outdoor adventures. Here are some of the best hiking/biking routes of Suginami:

  • Koenji to Nakano: The scenic trail is 2.7 km long and takes you through quiet residential areas.
  • Nogata to Mitakadai: This trail is about 5.2km long and passes by the beautiful Zenpukuji Park.
  • Nature Walk in Ogikubo: The trail is 1.3 km long and takes you through the forests and shrines.

Best Things to Do in Suginami: Activities and Tours

Suginami is full of exciting activities and tours that cater to all interests. Here are some of the best things to do in Suginami:

  • Anime Tour: Explore the world of Japanese anime by visiting the famous Akihabara Electric Town.
  • Tokyo Street Food Tour: Sample the best local cuisines in Suginami and get a taste of the local food culture.
  • Flower Arrangement Class: Learn the ancient Japanese art of Ikebana from masters in Suginami.

Week-Long Itinerary for Suginami

Here’s a week-long itinerary you can follow to make the most of your visit to Suginami:

  • Day 1: Visit the Suginami Animation Museum, Koenji temple and spend the evening exploring Harmony Avenue.
  • Day 2: Explore the Tama Forest Science Garden, go for a nature walk in Ogikubo, and visit the Ghibli Museum.
  • Day 3: Take an anime tour, visit the Nakano Broadway mall, and end your day at the Izakaya bars in Koenji.
  • Day 4: Spend a day at Zenpukuji Park, rent a boat, and have a picnic at the park.
  • Day 5: Take a flower arrangement class and visit the Koenji Fureai Park.
  • Day 6: Go for a bike ride from Nogata to Mitakadai and visit the Tokyo Fuji Art Museum.
  • Day 7: Visit the Ogikubo Sun Road, shopping street and try some local street food.

Food and Drink Scene of Suginami

Suginami is known for its amazing food culture, from delicious street food to traditional Japanese cuisine. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages in Suginami:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Tonkatsu at Tonki: The Tonkatsu at Tonki is one of the best in Tokyo, where they serve crispy pork fillet with cabbage and rice.
  • Tempura at Tenko: Head to Tenko for a perfect tempura experience in Suginami that is made from fresh shrimp and vegetables.
  • Ramen at Afuri: Afuri has the best Ramen in Suginami, which is made from a light chicken broth topped with spring onions and wakame seaweed.

Local Beverages

  • Sake: Suginami has a variety of Sake bars that serve a range of local and imported sake.
  • Shochu: Japanese distilled spirit Shochu can be found in many bars and restaurants in Suginami.
  • Local Craft Beers: Bars and breweries in Suginami serve a range of local craft beers and international beers.

Where to Stay in Suginami: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Suginami has a range of accommodation options to suit all budgets, from luxurious hotels to budget-friendly hostels and guesthouses. Some popular options include:

  • Ryokan Takemine: A traditional Japanese Inn that has a serene ambiance.
  • Hotel Roncieres: A luxurious hotel located in the heart of Suginami.
  • Guest house Kabu: A budget-friendly guest house close to Zenpukuji Park.

What to Buy in Suginami: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Suginami has several shops where you can buy local souvenirs and products to bring back home, such as:

  • Suginami Station Souvenir Shops: These shops sell a range of souvenirs and local snacks like Kit Kats.
  • Kinto: Suginami’s stylish shop sells unique and trendy homewares, ceramics and glassware.
  • Shimokitazawa: A popular shopping hub that sells vintage clothing and records.

Local Culture and Customs of Suginami

Suginami is steeped in Japanese culture and traditions. The Japanese believe in expressing gratitude and respect towards others. It is important to follow the cultural norms and respect the local customs. Bowing when greeting is customary, and it is important to remove your shoes before entering homes or traditional establishments.

Best Time to Visit Suginami

The best time to visit Suginami is from late March to early May or October to November when the weather is mild, and the cherry blossoms bloom. Suginami also attracts tourists during the summer months when it hosts several cultural events and festivals.

Do’s and Don’ts of Suginami

Do:

  • Observe traditional Japanese customs and practices.
  • Try different kinds of Japanese food and drinks.
  • Be respectful towards locals and fellow travelers alike.

Don’t:

  • Litter or smoke in public areas.
  • Speak loudly or engage in disruptive behavior in quiet areas.
  • Tip waiters or service staff; it is not customary to tip in Japan.

Safety and Health

Safety in Suginami

Suginami is a safe neighborhood, and the crime rate is low. However, it is always recommended to be careful and take general safety precautions in crowded areas, especially during peak hours.

Health in Suginami

Suginami has several medical facilities and clinics that cater to locals and tourists alike. Carry prescribed medications and check the hospital and clinic information in advance to ensure quick access.

Getting to Suginami: Transportation Options and Logistics

Suginami is easily accessible via multiple transportation options, including:

Airports Near Suginami

  • Haneda Airport: Haneda airport is about a 30-minute drive from Suginami and has several flights connecting to domestic and international destinations.
  • Narita Airport: Narita airport is approximately a 90-minute drive to Suginami and has several international flights.

Bus Stations Near Suginami

  • Suginami Central Building Bus Terminal: The terminal connects several local and intercity buses.

Other Options

  • Train: Suginami is well-connected through several train stations.
  • Taxi: Taxis in Tokyo are safe and easy to find, but they can be a little expensive compared to other modes of transport.

How to Get Around in Suginami

Suginami is relatively small, and most attractions are easily accessible on foot or by bike. Walking is a great way to explore the neighborhoods of Suginami, but for longer journeys, take advantage of public transport services like trains and buses.

Where to Go Next from Suginami: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Suginami’s location in Tokyo makes it an ideal point to explore other nearby destinations and cities such as:

  • Shinjuku: A popular tourist attraction and a shopping hub for both locals and tourists.
  • Shibuya: A fashion-centric area, known for designer boutiques and trendy cafes.
  • Mt. Takao: A scenic spot known for its natural beauty and hiking trails.

Conclusion

Suginami offers a unique glimpse into traditional Japanese culture and modern artistry. Whether you want to explore the rich traditional heritage or soak up the contemporary culture, there’s something for everyone in Suginami. With this comprehensive travel guide, you’re all set to explore this vibrant Tokyo neighborhood to its fullest.
