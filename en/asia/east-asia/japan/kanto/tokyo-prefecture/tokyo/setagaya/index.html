Located in the southwestern part of Tokyo, Setagaya is one of the city’s most residential wards. Despite its humble status, this suburban area boasts a rich history and vibrant culture that attracts many visitors each year. In this comprehensive travel guide, we’ll take a closer look at everything that Setagaya has to offer, from its cultural attractions and hiking routes, to its food and drink scene and where to stay.

Introduction to Setagaya

Setagaya is situated in the western part of Tokyo and is the city’s largest ward by area with a population of more than 910,000 people. It was named after the Setagaya Village, which existed during the Edo period. Today, Setagaya is a suburban area with a laid-back atmosphere and numerous parks, making it an ideal destination for outdoor activities.

List of Places to Visit in Setagaya

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Setagaya is a treasure trove of cultural attractions. Here are some of the top museums and historical places to check out:

  • Setagaya Art Museum - This contemporary art museum houses a collection of more than 9,000 works of art from the 20th century to the present day.

  • Setagaya Hachimangu Shrine - As one of Tokyo’s oldest and most prestigious shrines, Setagaya Hachimangu is a must-visit attraction for those interested in learning about Japanese history and culture.

  • Setagaya Literary Museum - This museum is dedicated to the history of literature in Setagaya, showcasing a wide variety of books, manuscripts, and literary memorabilia.

Natural Attractions of Setagaya: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Setagaya also offers a range of natural attractions for those looking to spend time outdoors. Here are some of the top places to visit:

  • Todoroki Valley Park - This serene park in the middle of Setagaya is perfect for a scenic stroll or a picnic. Its highlight is the Todoroki Valley, a natural gorge in the heart of the city.

  • Kinuta Park - This spacious park is ideal for jogging, cycling, or having a barbecue with friends. It also features a beautiful rose garden that blooms in May and June.

  • Gotokuji Temple - As the birthplace of the “maneki-neko” or the “beckoning cat,” Gotokuji Temple is a must-see attraction for cat lovers. The temple grounds feature hundreds of ceramic cats, each with its unique design.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Setagaya

Setagaya is also known for its scenic hiking and biking routes. Here are some of the top routes to check out:

  • Sakura Cycling Path - This bike path follows the Tamagawa-Josui waterway and is especially popular in the spring when the cherry blossoms are in full bloom.

  • Takao-san Hiking Trail - Located in nearby Hachioji, this hiking trail offers stunning views of Setagaya and the surrounding mountains. It’s a must-visit for outdoor enthusiasts.

Best Things to Do in Setagaya: Activities and Tours

Aside from the places mentioned above, there are other exciting things to do in Setagaya. Here are some of the best activities and tours:

  • Visit the Local Markets - Setagaya is home to several local markets, including the Setagaya Boroichi and the Setagaya Flea Market. These markets are perfect for finding souvenirs or trying out delicious street food.

  • Take a Cooking Class - Trying new foods is one of the best ways to experience a new culture, and what better way to do that than to learn how to cook local dishes? There are several cooking classes available in Setagaya, including sushi-making classes and traditional Japanese cooking classes.

Week-Long Itinerary for Setagaya

If you have a full week to spend in Setagaya, here’s a suggested itinerary:

Day 1 - Visit Setagaya Art Museum and Setagaya Hachimangu Shrine

Day 2 - Go on a bike ride along the Sakura Cycling Path

Day 3 - Explore the Todoroki Valley Park and Kinuta Park

Day 4 - Visit the Setagaya Literary Museum and take a cooking class

Day 5 - Hike the Takao-san Hiking Trail

Day 6 - Visit Gotokuji Temple and explore the Setagaya Flea Market

Day 7 - Relax in one of Setagaya’s cafes or bars

Food and Drink Scene of Setagaya

Must-Try Dishes

Setagaya is known for its diverse culinary scene. Here are some must-try dishes:

  • Soba Noodles - These thin noodles made from buckwheat flour are a Japanese staple and can be found in many restaurants around Setagaya.

  • Tonkatsu - A breaded and deep-fried pork cutlet that is crispy on the outside and tender on the inside.

Local Beverages

  • Sake - Sake is a traditional Japanese alcoholic drink made from fermented rice. Setagaya has several sake breweries, including the Otokoyama Honten Sake Brewery, which offers tours and tastings.

  • Craft Beer - Setagaya has a growing craft beer scene, with several breweries and craft beer bars in the area. Some notable breweries include TDM 1874 Brewery and Ise Kadoya Brewery.

Where to Stay in Setagaya: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Setagaya offers several accommodation options for visitors, ranging from budget-friendly hostels to luxury hotels. Here are some top options:

  • Kyoka-an Guesthouse - This charming guesthouse offers traditional Japanese-style rooms, complete with tatami mat flooring and futon beds.

  • Tokyu Stay Yoga - A modern hotel with stylish rooms and convenient access to public transportation.

  • Hostel WALKER - A budget-friendly hostel with basic amenities and a welcoming atmosphere.

What to Buy in Setagaya: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

If you’re looking for souvenirs or local products to bring home, here are some top options:

  • Traditional Crafts - Setagaya is known for its traditional crafts, including pottery, ceramics, and textiles. You can find many local crafts at the Setagaya Boroichi market.

  • Local Snacks - A must-try local snack is the “Omanju,” a sweet bean-paste-filled cake that’s popular in Setagaya.

Local Culture and Customs of Setagaya

In Setagaya, it’s important to respect local customs and learn about the culture. Some traditions to be aware of include:

  • Respect for Elders - In Japanese culture, respect for elders is a core value. It’s important to show respect to older people and be mindful of their needs.

  • Shoes Off Indoors - In many Japanese homes and establishments, it’s customary to remove shoes before entering. Look for shoe racks or clues like slippers to know if you should remove your shoes.

Best Time to Visit Setagaya

The best time to visit Setagaya is during the spring (March to May) and fall (September to November) when the weather is pleasant and the foliage is at its most beautiful. The cherry blossom season in Tokyo usually falls in late March to early April, so plan accordingly if you want to see this spectacular sight.

Do’s and Don’ts of Setagaya

Do:

  • Do bow when greeting someone. It’s a common and respectful greeting in Japanese culture.

  • Do learn basic Japanese phrases. It’s appreciated when foreigners make an effort to speak the local language.

Don’t:

  • Don’t tip at restaurants, as it’s not customary in Japan.

  • Don’t talk loudly on public transportation or in public spaces, as it’s considered rude.

Safety and Health

Safety in Setagaya

Setagaya is generally a safe area, but it’s always best to take precautions to ensure your safety. Be sure to lock your doors and windows when leaving your hotel room or apartment and avoid flashing expensive jewelry or electronics in public.

Health in Setagaya

Japan is known for its excellent healthcare system, and Setagaya is no exception. There are several hospitals and clinics in the area, and English-speaking doctors are available if needed.

Getting to Setagaya: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Setagaya

The closest airport to Setagaya is Haneda Airport, which is approximately 20 kilometers away. It’s the only airport that is fully located within Tokyo and offers flights to domestic and international destinations.

Bus Stations Near Setagaya

Setagaya has several bus stations that provide transportation to nearby destinations. Some of the bus stations include Sangen-Jaya Bus Terminal and Futako-Tamagawa Station Bus Terminal.

Other Options

The most popular way to get around Setagaya is by train. There are several train stations in the area that offer easy access to other parts of Tokyo.

How to Get Around in Setagaya

Setagaya is a large area with many attractions spread out throughout the ward. The best way to get around Setagaya is by using public transportation, such as the train or bus. Taxis are also available but can be expensive.

Where to Go Next from Setagaya: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

If you’re looking to explore other areas of Tokyo, some popular nearby destinations include Shibuya, Shinjuku, and Harajuku. These popular areas offer a range of shopping, dining, and entertainment options.

Conclusion

Setagaya is a unique and diverse destination that offers a range of cultural attractions, outdoor adventures, and delicious cuisine. Whether you’re interested in exploring its historic sites or trying new foods, Setagaya has something for everyone. Use this comprehensive travel guide to make the most out of your visit to Setagaya and experience Tokyo like a local.
