Introduction to Toshima

Located in the Izu Islands of Tokyo Prefecture, Toshima is a small volcanic island that offers an authentic glimpse into traditional Japanese culture and natural beauty. With a population of only 400 people and an area of 4.12 km², Toshima is a hidden gem that offers a tranquil escape from the hustle and bustle of Tokyo. The island is known for its scenic views, outdoor activities, cultural attractions, and delicious local cuisine.

List of Places to Visit in Toshima

If you’re planning a trip to Toshima, it’s important to know what to expect from this picturesque island. Here is a comprehensive list of the top things to do and see in Toshima:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Toshima Folklore Museum: Learn about the island’s history and culture, including traditional clothing, fishing techniques, and folklore.
  • Fukumitsu Shigemi Memorial Hall: Discover the life and work of Fukumitsu Shigemi, a famous Japanese artist who lived on the island.
  • Kannon-do Temple: Visit this picturesque temple, located on a rocky hill overlooking the sea, and enjoy breathtaking views of the surrounding landscape.
  • Toshima Shrine: This picturesque shrine, located on the highest point of the island, offers stunning views of the sea and surrounding mountains.

Natural Attractions of Toshima: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Mount Sakurajima: Hike to the summit of this inactive volcano and enjoy breathtaking views of the island and the sea.
  • Yugashima Beach: Relax on this beautiful white sand beach and admire the crystal clear waters of the Pacific Ocean.
  • Nagata Matsubara: Take a stroll along this picturesque pine tree-lined promenade and admire the stunning views of the sea.
  • Fishing and Snorkeling: Enjoy a fishing or snorkeling excursion and explore Toshima’s rich marine life.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Toshima

Toshima offers a range of scenic hiking and biking routes that are suitable for both beginners and experienced hikers. Here are some of the best routes:

  • Mt. Sakurajima Hiking Trail: This challenging trail takes you to the summit of Mt. Sakurajima and offers stunning views of the island and the sea.
  • Yugashima Beach Hiking Trail: Stroll along this picturesque trail and enjoy breathtaking views of the ocean and surrounding landscapes.
  • Nagata Matsubara Biking Trail: Explore the island’s scenic coastline on this easy biking trail that takes you through pine tree forests and along sandy beaches.

Best Things to Do in Toshima: Activities and Tours

Toshima offers a range of activities and tours that cater to different interests and preferences. Here are some of the best things to do in Toshima:

  • Whale Watching Tour: Take a boat tour and spot humpback whales and dolphins in the Pacific Ocean.
  • Cultural Tour: Explore Toshima’s rich culture and history with a guided tour of the island’s museums and temples.
  • Fishing Excursion: Experience Toshima’s rich marine life with a fishing excursion, and catch your own dinner.
  • Snorkeling Tour: Discover the underwater world of Toshima with a snorkeling excursion and admire the colorful coral reefs and marine life.

Week-Long Itinerary for Toshima

If you’re planning a week-long trip to Toshima, here’s a suggested itinerary that covers the island’s top attractions and activities:

Day Activities
Day 1 Arrive at Toshima, check into your hotel, explore the village, and take a relaxing stroll along Nagata Matsubara
Day 2 Take a hiking tour of Mt. Sakurajima and enjoy breathtaking views of the sea and mountain ranges
Day 3 Go on a cultural tour and visit Toshima Folklore Museum, Fukumitsu Shigemi Memorial Hall, and Toshima Shrine
Day 4 Enjoy a snorkeling excursion and discover Toshima’s rich marine life, coral reefs, and colorful fish.
Day 5 Take a fishing excursion and catch your own dinner
Day 6 Relax on Yugashima Beach, enjoy the sun, swim, and sample local cuisine
Day 7 Departure day. Pack up, check-out, and head off to your next destination.

Food and Drink Scene of Toshima

Toshima is known for its delicious local cuisine, which includes fresh seafood, vegetables, and fruits, and traditional Japanese dishes. Here are some must-try dishes:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Tuna Sashimi: Enjoy fresh tuna sashimi, caught locally in the Pacific Ocean
  • Shirasu-don: Try this delicious rice bowl, topped with fresh whitebait and vegetables
  • Izu Shamoji Rice: Taste the island’s famous rice, made from the locally grown and harvested white rice grains

Local Beverages

  • Amazake: A traditional sweet rice drink, served hot or cold, and made from fermented rice.
  • Shochu: A popular Japanese spirit made from sweet potatoes, rice, or barley.
  • Sake: A popular Japanese rice wine, available in a range of varieties and flavors.

Where to Stay in Toshima: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Toshima has a range of accommodation options that cater to different budgets and preferences. Here are some of the best places to stay in Toshima:

  • Yamagami Ryokan: A traditional Japanese ryokan that offers comfortable rooms, delicious local cuisine, and a relaxing hot spring bath.
  • Tobira Onsen Myosen: A cozy and affordable guesthouse that offers comfortable rooms, and a hot spring bath with a view of the sea.
  • Kazenoko: A friendly hostel that offers dormitory rooms and private rooms, and a cozy common area.

What to Buy in Toshima: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Toshima offers a range of local products and souvenirs that are perfect for taking home as gifts or reminders of your trip. Here are some of the best products to buy in Toshima:

  • Island Hiking Stick: These handmade wooden hiking sticks are made from the local cherry blossom trees and feature intricate carvings.
  • Izu Shamoji Rice: Take home a bag of Toshima’s famous white rice grains, harvested locally.
  • Local Sake: Taste and buy sake made by the island’s local breweries.

Local Culture and Customs of Toshima

Toshima has a rich cultural heritage that is reflected in its traditional festivals, customs, and traditions. Visitors are encouraged to respect local customs and etiquette, including bowing when meeting someone new, removing your shoes before entering someone’s home, and avoiding loud or disruptive behavior in public places.

Best Time to Visit Toshima

The best time to visit Toshima is during the spring and fall seasons, when the weather is mild and pleasant, and the island’s natural beauty is at its peak. The summer season can be hot and humid, while the winter season can be chilly and rainy.

Do’s and Don’ts of Toshima

Here are some important do’s and don’ts to keep in mind when visiting Toshima:

Do:

  • Respect local customs and etiquette
  • Try local cuisine and products
  • Take care of the environment and help keep the island clean

Don’t:

  • Make noise or act inconsiderately in public places
  • Litter or damage the environment
  • Ignore safety warnings and instructions

Safety and Health

Safety in Toshima

Toshima is a safe and peaceful island that offers a welcoming and friendly environment for visitors. However, visitors are advised to take necessary precautions, such as wearing appropriate footwear when hiking or biking, avoiding swimming in strong currents or rough seas, and being aware of the risk of sunburn and dehydration.

Health in Toshima

Toshima offers basic healthcare facilities, including a small medical clinic and a pharmacy. Visitors are advised to take necessary precautions, such as bringing any necessary medications or health supplies with them, and staying hydrated in the island’s hot and humid climate.

Getting to Toshima: Transportation Options and Logistics

Toshima is accessible by boat and ferry from Tokyo and other nearby areas. Here are some transportation options to get to Toshima:

Airports Near Toshima

  • Tokyo Haneda Airport: The largest airport in Tokyo, which offers domestic flights to nearby islands and destinations.

Bus Stations Near Toshima

  • Toshima Terminal: The island’s main bus terminal, located in the village center, which offers bus connections to nearby destinations on the island.

Other Options

  • Ferry from Tokyo: Visitors can take a ferry from Tokyo to Toshima, which takes around three hours.

How to Get Around in Toshima

Toshima is a small island that can be explored on foot, by bike, or by car. Visitors can rent bikes or cars from local rental services and explore the island’s scenic routes and attractions. Buses are also available, which connect the village center with nearby destinations and attractions.

Where to Go Next from Toshima: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Toshima is located in the Izu Islands, which offer a range of nearby destinations and attractions to explore. Visitors can take a day trip to nearby islands, such as Hachijojima, Miyakejima, or Mikurajima, and explore their unique culture, history, and natural beauty.

Conclusion

Toshima is a hidden gem of the Izu Islands, which offers a tranquil escape from the bustling city of Tokyo. This picturesque island offers a range of cultural attractions, outdoor adventures, scenic views, and delicious local cuisine. By following this travel guide, visitors can explore the best things to do and see in Toshima, and plan a memorable trip to this hidden gem in the Pacific Ocean.
