Introduction to Kozushima

Located in the Izu Islands, Kozushima is a hidden gem in Japan’s Tokyo prefecture. With its pristine beaches, lush greenery, and stunning mountain landscapes, Kozushima is an ideal destination for eco-conscious travelers looking for a peaceful and quiet escape from the hustle and bustle of Tokyo. The island is also famous for its fresh seafood and traditional hot springs, making it a popular destination for foodies and wellness enthusiasts.

List of Places to Visit in Kozushima

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Kozushima Folklore Museum: Learn about the island’s history, culture, and traditions through a collection of artifacts, photographs, and documents.
  • Kozushima Shrine: Located on a hilltop overlooking the sea, Kozushima Shrine is a Shinto shrine that dates back to the 11th century. The shrine’s main hall features a beautiful wooden gate, a sacred tree, and a large torii gate.
  • Hachimangu Shrine: Another important Shinto shrine on the island, Hachimangu Shrine is dedicated to the god of war and is believed to protect the island from evil spirits.

Natural Attractions of Kozushima: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Mt. Tenjo: The highest peak on the island, Mt. Tenjo offers breathtaking views of the surrounding sea and mountains. Visitors can hike to the top of the mountain along a scenic trail that passes through forests, fields, and rocky terrain.
  • Kozushima Beach: With its clear turquoise waters and white sandy shores, Kozushima Beach is a paradise for swimmers, sunbathers, and water sports enthusiasts.
  • Futatsura Bay: This secluded bay on the north side of the island is a great spot for snorkeling and exploring the vibrant coral reef.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Kozushima

Kozushima has a number of hiking and biking trails that offer stunning views of the island’s rugged terrain and natural beauty. Some of the most popular trails include:

  • Tenjo-Kurasaki Trail: This scenic trail takes hikers to the summit of Mt. Tenjo and offers panoramic views of the island and the surrounding sea.
  • Mita-Tenjo Trail: This trail starts in the village of Mita and takes hikers through rolling hills and dense forests before reaching the summit of Mt. Tenjo.
  • Coastal Cycling Route: This cycling route follows the coastline of the island and offers stunning views of the rugged cliffs and turquoise sea.

Best Things to Do in Kozushima: Activities and Tours

  • Hot Springs: Kozushima is home to several hot springs where visitors can soak in mineral-rich waters while admiring the island’s natural beauty.
  • Fishing: The island’s crystal-clear waters are teeming with fish, making Kozushima a popular destination for fishing enthusiasts.
  • Snorkeling and Diving: Visitors can explore the colorful coral reefs and underwater landscapes of Kozushima’s surrounding waters.

Food and Drink Scene of Kozushima

Must-Try Dishes

  • Kozushima Tuna: This local delicacy is served fresh and raw in sushi or sashimi form.
  • Kozushima Island Chicken: This free-range chicken is known for its tender meat and rich flavor.
  • Shojin Ryori: This traditional Buddhist cuisine is made with fresh vegetables, mushrooms, and tofu and is served in local temples and restaurants.

Local Beverages

  • Kozushima Sake: This locally produced sake is made from locally grown rice and is known for its light and refreshing taste.
  • Kozushima Green Tea: This locally grown green tea is famous for its rich aroma and delicate flavor.
  • Kozushima Shochu: This locally distilled spirit is made from sweet potatoes and is a popular drink among locals.

Where to Stay in Kozushima: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Kozushima Guesthouse: This cozy guesthouse offers simple and affordable accommodations with stunning sea views and easy access to hiking trails and beaches.
  • Kozushima Seaside Hotel: This hotel offers comfortable and modern rooms with ocean or mountain views, on-site hot springs, and a variety of dining options.
  • Kozushima Campsite: This campsite offers a unique and affordable way to experience the island’s natural beauty and outdoor adventures.

What to Buy in Kozushima: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Kozushima Island Chicken: Visitors can purchase fresh or processed Island Chicken meat and products at local stores and markets.
  • Amulet Charms: These traditional Japanese good luck charms can be purchased at Kozushima Shrine or Hachimangu Shrine.
  • Kozushima Island Green Tea: Visitors can purchase locally grown and harvested green tea at local stores and souvenir shops.

Local Culture and Customs of Kozushima

Kozushima is known for its strong cultural heritage and traditions, which can be seen through its various shrines and temples, traditional cuisine, and festivals. The island is also home to an active fishing community, and visitors are encouraged to learn about the local fishing practices and fishing-related events.

Best Time to Visit Kozushima

The best time to visit Kozushima is from late spring to early autumn (March to November) when the weather is mild and the island’s natural beauty is at its peak. The island can become crowded during peak holiday periods, so visitors are advised to book accommodations well in advance.

Do’s and Don’ts of Kozushima

Do:

  • Respect the island’s natural environment and avoid littering.
  • Visit the island’s shrines and temples and learn about local customs and traditions.
  • Try the locally grown and harvested produce and seafood.

Don’t:

  • Engage in any activities that may harm the island’s flora or fauna.
  • Disrupt the peace and quiet of the local community.
  • Ignore any warning signs or advice from local authorities.

Safety and Health

Safety in Kozushima

Kozushima is a safe place to visit, with relatively low crime rates and a welcoming and friendly local community. Visitors are advised to take necessary precautions when engaging in outdoor activities and to respect local customs and traditions.

Health in Kozushima

Kozushima has a well-developed healthcare system and several medical facilities that can provide basic medical care and emergency services. However, visitors with pre-existing medical conditions or specific medical needs should consult with their doctor before traveling to Kozushima.

Getting to Kozushima: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Kozushima

The closest airport to Kozushima is Tokyo International Airport (Haneda), which has regular flights to Shimoda Airport on the nearby Izu Peninsula. From Shimoda Airport, visitors can take a ferry or a fast boat to Kozushima.

Bus Stations Near Kozushima

Several bus companies operate regular services to Kozushima from major cities in Japan, including Tokyo, Yokohama, and Nagoya.

Other Options

Visitors can also reach Kozushima by ferry or fast boat from nearby islands in the Izu Islands chain, including Miyakejima and Hachijojima.

How to Get Around in Kozushima

Kozushima is a relatively small island and can be easily explored on foot or by bicycle. Visitors can also rent cars or scooters from local vendors, although driving can be challenging due to the island’s narrow and winding roads.

Where to Go Next from Kozushima: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Miyakejima: This volcanic island is famous for its unique geology and underwater landscape, making it a popular destination for diving and snorkeling.
  • Hachijojima: This remote island is known for its rugged terrain, hot springs, and lush forests, making it a popular destination for eco-tourists and hikers.
  • Shimoda: This charming seaside town on the Izu Peninsula is known for its beautiful beaches, historic sites, and delicious seafood.

Conclusion

Kozushima is an off-the-beaten-path destination that offers visitors a unique and authentic Japanese experience. With its natural beauty, cultural heritage, and outdoor adventures, Kozushima is an ideal destination for travelers looking for a peaceful and relaxing escape from the city.
