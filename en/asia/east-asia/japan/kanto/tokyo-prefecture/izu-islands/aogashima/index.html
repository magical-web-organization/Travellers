Introduction to Aogashima

Aogashima is a volcanic island located in the Izu Islands, part of Tokyo prefecture, Kanto region, Japan. The island is remote, with a population of just over 200, and is accessible only by boat or helicopter. Aogashima is formed by two volcanic calderas, with the smaller one inside the larger one, and is a natural wonderland that is perfect for outdoor adventure enthusiasts and history buffs. In this comprehensive travel guide, we will cover everything you need to know to plan your perfect trip to Aogashima.

List of Places to Visit in Aogashima

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Aogashima has a rich cultural and historical heritage, and there are several museums and historical places that showcase the island’s unique history. The Aogashima Folklore Museum is a great place to learn about the island’s culture and traditions, including its festivals, music, and crafts. The Aogashima Shoten Museum is a historic shop that has been preserved in its original form, and offers visitors a glimpse into the past. Additionally, the island boasts several shrines and temples, including the Aogashima Shrine, which dates back to the 7th century and is dedicated to the god of the sea.

Natural Attractions of Aogashima: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Aogashima is a paradise for outdoor enthusiasts, with its rugged coastline, stunning cliffs, and pristine beaches. Hiking is a popular activity on the island, and there are several trails that offer breathtaking views of the volcanic landscape, such as the Mt. Maruyama Trail and the Mt. Takatsuka Trail. Snorkeling and scuba diving are also popular activities, with the crystal-clear waters providing excellent visibility and an abundance of marine life. The island’s beaches offer a relaxing escape from the hustle and bustle of city life and the perfect place to bask in the sun.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Aogashima

Aogashima is a hiker’s paradise, with several trails that offer stunning views of the island’s natural beauty. Among the most popular trails are the Mt. Maruyama Trail and the Mt. Takatsuka Trail, both of which take visitors through dense forest and offer panoramic views of the island. For those who prefer biking, the island’s scenic roads offer a unique cycling experience, and visitors can rent bikes from local stores.

Best Things to Do in Aogashima: Activities and Tours

In addition to hiking and biking, Aogashima offers a plethora of activities and tours that cater to all interests. Visitors can take a helicopter tour of the island for a bird’s eye view of the volcanic landscape. Additionally, fishing tours are a great way to experience the island’s bountiful marine life, and there are several local operators that offer fishing charters. For those looking for an adrenaline rush, skydiving is available on the island, offering a unique perspective of the volcano and the island.

Week-Long Itinerary for Aogashima

Day 1: Arrive and check-in at your accommodation. Day 2: Visit the Aogashima Folklore Museum, Aogashima Shoten Museum, and Aogashima Shrine. Day 3: Hike the Mt. Maruyama Trail or Mt. Takatsuka Trail. Day 4: Go snorkeling or scuba diving and relax at one of the island’s beaches. Day 5: Take a helicopter tour of the island or choose a fishing charter. Day 6: Visit the island’s hot springs and relax. Day 7: Departure from Aogashima.

Food and Drink Scene of Aogashima

Must-Try Dishes

Aogashima is known for its fresh seafood, and visitors can indulge in a variety of seafood dishes such as sashimi, grilled fish, and tempura. The island is also known for its beef and pork dishes, with local farms producing high-quality meat. Additionally, visitors can’t miss trying the island’s specialty, ‘Aka-tama,’ a local egg dish that is made with soy sauce and mirin.

Local Beverages

The island of Aogashima does not have any breweries or distilleries, but visitors can try local beverages such as ‘shochu,’ a distilled spirit that is popular in Japan, and ‘umeshu,’ plum wine that is sweet and refreshing.

Where to Stay in Aogashima: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

While there are limited accommodation options on Aogashima, visitors can choose from a variety of budget-friendly and comfortable hotels, hostels, and guesthouses. Most accommodations are traditional Japanese-style inns, with tatami mats and futon bedding. Some of the popular options include ‘Minshuku Gensenkan’ and ‘Aogashima Pica Lodge.’

What to Buy in Aogashima: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Visitors to Aogashima must bring home the local specialty, ‘Aka-tama’ soy sauce. Additionally, visitors can also purchase local handicrafts such as pottery and textiles, and can also indulge in the island’s delicious seafood.

Local Culture and Customs of Aogashima

Aogashima is a remote island with a population that practices traditional Japanese culture and customs. Visitors should be respectful of these customs and follow appropriate etiquette, such as removing shoes before entering homes or temples, and speaking in a soft tone in public places.

Best Time to Visit Aogashima

The best time to visit Aogashima is during the spring and fall months, which offer mild temperatures and comfortable weather. Visitors should avoid visiting during the summer months, as the island can get crowded with tourists and temperatures can be hot and humid.

Do’s and Don’ts of Aogashima

Safety and Health

Safety in Aogashima

Aogashima is a safe destination, with a low crime rate and friendly locals. However, visitors should be cautious when hiking and follow proper safety protocols, such as informing someone of their hiking plans and packing essential hiking gear.

Health in Aogashima

There are no medical facilities on Aogashima, so visitors should bring all necessary medications and first-aid supplies. Visitors should also be aware of the local wildlife, such as venomous snakes and insects, and take proper precautions.

Getting to Aogashima: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Aogashima

The closest airport to Aogashima is Tokyo International Airport, also known as Haneda Airport, which is located in Tokyo. From there, visitors can take a domestic flight to Hachijojima Airport and catch a ferry to Aogashima.

Bus Stations Near Aogashima

There are no bus stations on Aogashima, as the island is too small for public transportation.

Other Options

Visitors can also reach Aogashima by taking a ferry or helicopter from mainland Japan.

How to Get Around in Aogashima

Aogashima is a small island that can be easily navigated on foot or by bike. Visitors can also take a taxi or hire a car, but these options can be expensive.

Where to Go Next from Aogashima: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

From Aogashima, visitors can explore nearby destinations such as Hachijojima Island, an idyllic island that is known for its natural hot springs and scenic views. Visitors can also visit other nearby destinations such as Tokyo, Yokohama, and Kamakura.

Aogashima is a hidden gem that is waiting to be discovered. With its stunning natural beauty, rich cultural heritage, and endless outdoor activities, it is the perfect destination for travelers looking for a unique and off-the-beaten-path experience.
