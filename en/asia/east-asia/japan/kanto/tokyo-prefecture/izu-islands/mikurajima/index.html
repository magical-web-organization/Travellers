Are you looking for an off-the-beaten-path destination that offers natural beauty with life-changing experiences? Then Mikurajima, a small island in southern Tokyo, is the perfect place for you. Mikurajima might be a tiny island, but its picturesque scenery, outdoor adventures, and cultural history attract thousands of tourists every year. So why wait? Let’s dive into Mikurajima’s natural beauty and unique culture.

Introduction to Mikurajima

Mikurajima is a small island located in the Izu Islands, approximately 200 kilometers from Tokyo. It is one of the volcanic islands of Tokyo’s islands and home to an array of flora and fauna. The island is specifically known for its beautiful coral reefs, crystal waters, and friendly dolphins that come to its shores to play. Mikurajima’s natural landscape offers outdoor enthusiasts endless opportunities to explore, hike, and bike their way through its scenic routes. The island is small in size, so visitors will have plenty of opportunities to experience its culture, people, and natural beauty.

Local Culture and Customs of Mikurajima

Mikurajima’s culture is heavily influenced by its natural beauty and the lifestyle of the locals. The island’s customs and traditions are closely tied to fishing and agriculture, and Mikurajima celebrates its cultural heritage through various festivals throughout the year.

For instance, the island celebrates the Kamezaki Shishimai Festival in November, where performers dressed in lion costumes perform around town. Visitors can participate in the festival and enjoy the traditional music, food, and arts of Mikurajima.

Best Time to Visit Mikurajima

The best time to visit Mikurajima is from April to June and September to November when the island has a temperate climate and fewer visitors. During these months, the island’s natural beauty is more visible, and it’s an ideal time to explore the outdoors.

Do’s and Don’ts of Mikurajima

Do:

  • Respect the island’s natural environment and wildlife
  • Follow the standard hiking and snorkeling rules
  • Dress appropriately while visiting shrines, temples, or other cultural sites

Don’t:

  • Litter or leave traces while hiking or exploring the island
  • Chase or harass the dolphins and other wildlife
  • Engage in activities that might harm the island’s flora and fauna

Safety and Health

Safety in Mikurajima

Mikurajima is a safe island with a low crime rate. However, visitors should take the necessary precautions during outdoor activities and avoid straying off the designated tracks.

Health in Mikurajima

Visitors should avoid drinking tap water and stick to bottled water during their stay in Mikurajima. Also, since the island is relatively remote, it’s recommended you carry any necessary medicine and have appropriate travel insurance.

Getting to Mikurajima: Transportation Options and Logistics

Since Mikurajima is an island, visitors need to use ferries or boats to reach it.

Here are some transportation options to reach Mikurajima:

Airports Near Mikurajima

The nearest airport to Mikurajima is Haneda Airport in Tokyo.

Bus Stations Near Mikurajima

Visitors can take a bus to reach the port Nishisato in the town of Minamiizu, which is approximately two hours by bus from Tokyo Station.

Other Options

Visitors who wish to experience a thrilling ride can take a helicopter shuttle from Tokyo to Mikurajima.

How to Get Around in Mikurajima

Mikurajima is a small island, and visitors can easily explore the island by walking or renting bicycles. Visitors can also use the island’s taxi service to travel from one point to another.
