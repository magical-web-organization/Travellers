Introduction to Izu Ōshima

Izu Ōshima is a volcanic island situated in the Izu Islands, a chain of islands located south of Tokyo in the Philippine Sea. The island is part of Tokyo (prefecture), in Kanto, Japan, and is a popular destination for its hot springs, outdoor activities, and scenic views.

Despite its close proximity to Tokyo, Izu Ōshima remains a relatively undiscovered gem that attracts mostly domestic travelers seeking a quiet and peaceful escape from the bustling city. Visitors can easily reach the island by ferry or plane from Tokyo or nearby cities, making it a convenient destination for a day trip or a longer stay.

This travel guide will take you through the best places to visit in Izu Ōshima, including culturally important attractions, natural wonders, and outdoor adventures. You’ll also find information on the best things to do, where to stay and eat, local culture and customs, and practical tips on transportation, safety, and health.

List of Places to Visit in Izu Ōshima

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Izu Ōshima has a rich history and culture that is still prevalent today. The island is home to a number of museums and historical places that offer insights into its past and present.

1. Mt. Mihara Observatory

Located at the summit of Mt. Mihara, the volcano that dominates the island’s skyline, this observatory offers stunning views of the island and its surroundings. Visitors can take a cable car to the top and enjoy panoramic views from an altitude of 758 meters (2,485 feet).

2. Oshima Folklore Museum

This museum showcases the island’s history, culture, and customs, with exhibits on traditional crafts, costumes, and festivals. Visitors can learn about the island’s fishing and farming traditions, as well as the role of volcanoes in shaping its landscape.

3. Komuroyama Park

This hilltop park offers a panoramic view of the island and is a popular spot for cherry blossom viewing in the spring. It also has a statue of Momotaro, a legendary hero from Japanese folklore.

Natural Attractions of Izu Ōshima: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Izu Ōshima is known for its natural beauty and outdoor activities, including hiking, biking, and hot spring bathing. The island boasts stunning scenery that includes volcanic peaks, rocky coastlines, and lush forests.

1. Mt. Mihara Hiking Trail

For adventurous hikers, Mt. Mihara offers a challenging trek that rewards with stunning views of the island and its surroundings. The trail is steep and rugged, covering a distance of 8.5 kilometers (5.3 miles) from the trailhead to the summit.

2. Motomachi Coast

This scenic coastline stretches for 4 kilometers (2.5 miles) along the eastern side of the island, offering panoramic views of the Pacific Ocean and the island’s rugged terrain. Visitors can take a leisurely stroll or cycle along the coast, stopping to admire the views or soak in the natural hot springs dotted along the way.

3. Okada Port Area

Located on the western side of the island, the Okada port area is a popular spot for fishing and boating. Visitors can take a boat tour around the island and enjoy the stunning views of the rocky coastline and towering cliffs.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Izu Ōshima

Izu Ōshima is a paradise for outdoor enthusiasts, with a range of hiking and biking routes that offer stunning scenery and challenging terrain. Whether you’re a seasoned hiker or a casual cyclist, you’ll find plenty of trails to explore on the island.

1. Mt. Mihara Hiking Trail

As mentioned earlier, this trail offers a challenging hike that rewards with stunning views of the island and its surroundings.

2. Okada Forest Road

This scenic forest road runs for 9 kilometers (5.6 miles) along the western side of the island, offering stunning views of the Pacific Ocean and the island’s rugged terrain. The road is paved and relatively easy to cycle, making it a popular route for cyclists.

3. Motomachi Coast Walk

This scenic walk along the eastern side of the island offers stunning views of the Pacific Ocean and the island’s rugged coastline. The walk is relatively easy and can be completed in an hour or two, depending on how many stops you make along the way.

Best Things to Do in Izu Ōshima: Activities and Tours

Izu Ōshima offers a range of activities and tours that cater to all ages and interests. From cultural experiences to outdoor adventures, there’s something for everyone on the island.

1. Soak in a Hot Spring

Izu Ōshima is known for its natural hot springs, or onsen, which are believed to have healing properties. Visitors can experience the island’s hot springs at various locations, including hotels, public baths, and outdoor springs.

2. Go Fishing or Boating

The island’s rich fishing and boating culture provides visitors with ample opportunities to go fishing or take a boat tour around the island. You can charter a boat or join a tour from the Okada port area or other locations around the island.

3. Take a Cultural Tour

Izu Ōshima’s rich history and culture provide the perfect backdrop for cultural tours that offer a glimpse into the island’s past and present. Visitors can explore the island’s museums, traditional crafts, and festivals, or even take a cooking class to learn how to make local dishes.

Week-Long Itinerary for Izu Ōshima

A week-long itinerary for Izu Ōshima could look like this:

Day 1: Arrive on the island, check into your hotel, and explore the local area.

Day 2: Take a boat tour around the island to see the stunning coastline and natural wonders.

Day 3: Go hiking on the Mt. Mihara Hiking Trail and soak in the natural hot springs at the top.

Day 4: Take a cultural tour of the island, visiting museums, historic sites, and traditional crafts workshops.

Day 5: Go fishing or boating from the Okada port area, or take a bike ride along the coast.

Day 6: Relax and unwind at a local hot spring or spa.

Day 7: Depart from the island.

Food and Drink Scene of Izu Ōshima

Izu Ōshima offers a unique culinary experience that reflects its rich history and culture. From fresh seafood to local delicacies, visitors can taste the best of what the island has to offer.

Must-Try Dishes

1. Izu Ōshima Tuna

The island’s fresh and succulent tuna is a must-try for seafood lovers. Visitors can sample it at local restaurants or even catch their own on a fishing tour.

2. Izu Ōshima Shochu

This locally produced liquor is made from sweet potatoes and other grains and is a favorite among locals. Visitors can try it at local bars and restaurants or purchase it to bring home as a souvenir.

3. Izu Ōshima Island Vegetables

The island’s volcanic soil produces some of the freshest and most flavorful vegetables in Japan. Visitors can try them at local restaurants or even participate in a cooking class to learn how to prepare them.

Local Beverages

1. Oshima Tea

This tea is produced from tea leaves grown on the island and has a unique flavor that reflects the soil and climate of the region. It can be found at local tea shops or purchased as a souvenir.

2. Amazake

This sweet, non-alcoholic beverage is made from rice and is a popular winter drink on the island. Visitors can try it at local restaurants or purchase it from local vendors.

Where to Stay in Izu Ōshima: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Izu Ōshima offers a range of accommodation options that cater to all budgets and preferences. From luxury hotels to traditional Japanese inns, there’s something for everyone on the island.

1. Izu Ōshima Prince Hotel

This luxurious hotel offers stunning views of the Pacific Ocean and Mt. Mihara and has a range of amenities, including hot springs, restaurants, and a pool.

2. Pension Sky Blue

This charming guesthouse offers comfortable and affordable rooms with stunning views of the Pacific Ocean. It also includes a shared hot spring bath and a restaurant that serves locally sourced ingredients.

3. Izu Ōshima Youth Hostel

This hostel is ideal for budget travelers and offers dormitory-style rooms and private rooms at affordable rates. It also has a shared kitchen and lounge area, as well as a communal hot spring bath.

What to Buy in Izu Ōshima: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Izu Ōshima offers a range of souvenirs and local products that reflect its unique culture and heritage. From locally produced liquor to handcrafted pottery, visitors can find something to bring home as a reminder of their trip.

1. Izu Ōshima Shochu

As mentioned earlier, this locally produced liquor is a popular souvenir among visitors. It can be found at local liquor stores or purchased at the airport or ferry terminal.

2. Izu Ōshima Pottery

The island’s rich clay soil produces some of the finest pottery in Japan, and visitors can purchase handmade pottery from local artists and shops.

3. Izu Ōshima Island Vegetables

If you’re a fan of fresh and flavorful vegetables, you can purchase locally grown vegetables from farmers’ markets and shops around the island.

Local Culture and Customs of Izu Ōshima

Izu Ōshima has a rich and unique culture that reflects its history and heritage. Visitors can experience this culture through traditional crafts, festivals, and customs.

One of the island’s most famous festivals is the Oshima Tsubaki Festival, which takes place in February and celebrates the island’s famous camellia flowers. Visitors can see traditional dances, music, and other performances, as well as sample local food and drink.

Other customs and traditions include fishing and farming practices that have been passed down for generations, as well as the island’s unique dialect, which is different from the standard Japanese language.

Best Time to Visit Izu Ōshima

The best time to visit Izu Ōshima is from April to November, when the weather is warm and sunny and the island’s outdoor activities and festivals are in full swing.

However, visitors can still enjoy the island’s hot springs and indoor activities during the winter months from December to March, when the island is less crowded and hotel rates are lower.

Do’s and Don’ts of Izu Ōshima

Do:

  • Respect local customs and traditions, such as removing your shoes before entering homes and temples.
  • Try local dishes and drinks, and engage with locals to learn more about the island’s culture and heritage.
  • Pack appropriate clothing and gear for outdoor activities, including hiking shoes, sunscreen, and insect repellent.

Don’t:

  • Litter or damage the natural environment, and respect wildlife and marine life.
  • Wear revealing clothing or swimwear outside of designated areas.
  • Be loud or disruptive in public areas or late at night, and respect others’ privacy and space.

Safety and Health

Safety in Izu Ōshima

Izu Ōshima is generally a safe and peaceful destination, with low crime rates and a friendly and welcoming locals. Visitors should take normal precautions to ensure their safety, such as locking doors and valuables in hotel rooms, and avoiding dark or deserted areas late at night.

The island is also prone to volcanic eruptions and earthquakes, and visitors should stay informed of any alerts or warnings issued by local authorities.

Health in Izu Ōshima

Izu Ōshima has a good standard of healthcare facilities, and visitors can seek medical assistance at local clinics and hospitals. It’s recommended that visitors bring any necessary prescription medication with them, and obtain appropriate travel insurance before arriving on the island.

Getting to Izu Ōshima: Transportation Options and Logistics

Izu Ōshima can be easily reached from Tokyo or other nearby cities by ferry or plane.

Airports Near Izu Ōshima

The closest airport to Izu Ōshima is Tokyo Haneda Airport, where visitors can take a short domestic flight to the island.

Bus Stations Near Izu Ōshima

Visitors can also take a bus from Tokyo to Atami or Shimoda, where they can then board a ferry to Izu Ōshima. Buses depart from Tokyo Station and other locations around the city.

Other Options

Visitors can also take a train from Tokyo to Atami or Shimoda, where they can then board a ferry to Izu Ōshima. Trains depart from Tokyo Station and other major stations around the city.

How to Get Around in Izu Ōshima

Getting around Izu Ōshima is relatively easy and convenient, with a range of transportation options available.

Bus

The island has a bus system that connects major destinations and attractions, making it easy for visitors to get around without a car.

Taxi

Taxis are readily available on the island, and can be hailed from the airport or ferry terminal, or by calling a local taxi service.

Bike

Visitors can rent bikes from local stores and hotels, and explore the island’s scenic routes and coastal paths at their own pace.

Where to Go Next from Izu Ōshima: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Izu Ōshima is part of the Izu Islands chain, and visitors can easily explore other nearby islands and destinations.

1. Izu Peninsula

The Izu Peninsula is a popular destination for its natural hot springs, beaches, and scenic coastline. Visitors can take a ferry from Izu Ōshima to Atami or Shimoda, and then explore the peninsula by car or bus.

2. Hachijojima

Hachijojima is another volcanic island in the Izu Islands chain, known for its hot springs, hiking trails, and scenic views. Visitors can take a ferry or plane from Izu Ōshima to Hachijojima and explore its unique culture and heritage.

Conclusion

Izu Ōshima is a hidden gem that offers visitors a unique cultural and natural experience in close proximity to Tokyo. From stunning scenery to outdoor adventures, traditional culture to local cuisine, the island offers something for everyone. Plan your trip today and discover the beauty and charm of Izu Ōshima for yourself.
