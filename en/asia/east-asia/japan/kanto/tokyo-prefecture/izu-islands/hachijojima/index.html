Introduction to Hachijojima

Hachijojima is a volcanic island located in the Philippine Sea. It is one of the eight Izu Islands and is part of Tokyo Prefecture. The island is known for its beautiful nature, crystal-clear waters, and rich cultural heritage. Hachijojima is a popular destination for outdoor enthusiasts who come here to hike, bike, and explore the island’s rugged terrain.

List of Places to Visit in Hachijojima

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Hachijo-Fuji Museum: This museum features artifacts related to the island’s history and culture. It is located near Mount Hachijo-Fuji, which is a UNESCO World Heritage Site.

  • Hachijojima Folk Museum of History and Folklore: This museum is dedicated to preserving the island’s rich cultural heritage. It features exhibits related to traditional crafts, music, and dance.

  • Nishi-no-Omotejima Island: This island is a popular destination for snorkeling and diving enthusiasts. It is also home to several historical sites, including a lighthouse and a shrine.

Natural Attractions of Hachijojima: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Mount Hachijo-Fuji: This is the highest peak on the island and offers stunning views of the surrounding area. It is also a popular location for hiking and mountain climbing.

  • Higashi-no-Omotejima Island: This island is known for its beautiful scenery and pristine beaches. It is a popular destination for swimming, sunbathing, and surfing.

  • Tamaudun Beach: This is one of the most popular beaches on the island. Its crystal-clear waters and white sand make it an ideal location for swimming and snorkeling.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Hachijojima

  • Mt. Hachijojima Course: This course takes you to the summit of Mt. Hachijojima and offers an incredible view of the island. The trail is challenging, but the stunning scenery is well worth the effort.

  • Yokodake Course: This course is a challenging hike that takes you to the Yokodake Observatory. The view from the observatory is breathtaking and is the perfect spot to take in the beauty of the island.

Best Things to Do in Hachijojima: Activities and Tours

  • Snorkeling and Diving: The clear waters around Hachijojima are perfect for snorkeling and diving. You can see a variety of marine life, including colorful tropical fish and sea turtles.

  • Fishing: Fishing is a popular activity on the island, and you can catch a variety of fish, including tuna, Mahi-Mahi, and yellowtail.

  • Hot Springs: Hachijojima is home to a variety of natural hot springs. The waters are said to have therapeutic properties and are an excellent way to relax after a long day of hiking or sightseeing.

Food and Drink Scene of Hachijojima

Must-Try Dishes

  • Hachijojima Flying Fish: This local delicacy is a must-try for seafood lovers. The fish is marinated in vinegar and served with rice.

  • Hachijojima Pudding: This dessert is made with local milk and is known for its creamy texture and rich flavor.

Local Beverages

  • Sake: Hachijojima is known for its sake, which is made with locally grown rice.

  • Shochu: This traditional Japanese liquor is also popular on the island.

Where to Stay in Hachijojima: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Hachijojima Hotel: This hotel is located in the heart of Hachijojima and offers comfortable rooms and stunning views of the ocean.

  • Hachijojima Pension Naminori: This guesthouse is located near Tamaudun Beach and is known for its friendly staff and cozy atmosphere.

  • Hachijojima Youth Hostel: This hostel is popular among budget travelers and offers affordable accommodations in a great location.

What to Buy in Hachijojima: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Hachijojima T-Shirts: These shirts feature unique designs inspired by the island’s culture and nature.

  • Hachijojima Sake: This locally produced sake makes the perfect gift for friends and family back home.

Local Culture and Customs of Hachijojima

Hachijojima has a unique culture that is influenced by its isolation from the mainland. The locals are known for their hospitality and welcoming nature. Traditional festivals and events are an important part of island life, and visitors are welcome to participate in these celebrations.

Best Time to Visit Hachijojima

The best time to visit Hachijojima is from April to November when the weather is warm and dry. The island can get crowded during the summer months, so it’s best to visit during the shoulder seasons.

Do’s and Don’ts of Hachijojima

Do:

  • Respect local customs and traditions: Be respectful of local customs and traditions and participate in traditional festivals and events.

  • Take care of the environment: Hachijojima is known for its beautiful nature, so be sure to take care of the environment and respect the natural beauty of the island.

Don’t:

  • Leave trash behind: Be sure to dispose of your trash properly and leave no trace behind.

  • Ignore safety warnings: Follow safety warnings and guidelines and be sure to stay safe when exploring the island.

Safety and Health

Safety in Hachijojima

Hachijojima is generally a safe place to visit, but visitors should be aware of potential hazards, such as dangerous hiking trails and rough waters. Follow safety warnings and guidelines and be sure to take appropriate precautions when exploring the island.

Health in Hachijojima

The island has a small medical center, but visitors with serious health problems should seek medical help on the mainland. Tap water on the island is safe to drink, but visitors may prefer to drink bottled water.

Getting to Hachijojima: Transportation Options and Logistics

  • Air: The easiest way to get to Hachijojima is to take a flight from Tokyo Haneda Airport to Hachijojima Airport. There are several flights per day, and the flight takes approximately 50 minutes.

  • Boat: There is a ferry service that runs from Takeshiba Pier in Tokyo to Hachijojima. The journey takes approximately 12 hours.

How to Get Around in Hachijojima

The best way to get around Hachijojima is by rental car or scooter. Bicycles are also available for rent and are a popular mode of transportation on the island.

Where to Go Next from Hachijojima: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Izu Islands: The Izu Islands are a chain of volcanic islands located south of Tokyo. They are known for their beautiful nature, hot springs, and excellent snorkeling and diving opportunities.

  • Tokyo: Tokyo is well connected to Hachijojima by air and is a great place to continue your Japanese adventure. The city is known for its exciting nightlife, excellent cuisine, and rich cultural heritage.

Conclusion

Hachijojima is a beautiful and unique destination that offers something for everyone. Whether you’re an outdoor enthusiast, a history buff, or a foodie, you’re sure to find something to love on this incredible island. Plan your trip today and discover all that Hachijojima has to offer!
