Introduction to Izu Islands

Izu Islands is a group of volcanic islands in Tokyo (prefecture), in Kanto, Japan, located south of the Izu Peninsula. These islands are blessed with breathtaking natural beauty, rich history, and unique cultural traditions. They offer a perfect escape from the hustle and bustle of the city and a wide range of exciting activities for travelers of all ages. In this comprehensive guide, we will help you plan your trip to Izu Islands and make the most out of it.

List of Places to Visit in Izu Islands

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Izu Islands have a rich and diverse cultural heritage that dates back thousands of years. The following museums and historical places are worth visiting to learn more about the island’s unique history and traditions:

Shikinejima Island Museum

The Shikinejima Island Museum showcases the island’s natural history, cultural heritage, and traditional crafts. It has a large collection of artifacts, including pottery, textiles, and folk art, that are worth exploring.

Oshima Folk Museum

The Oshima Folk Museum features the traditional lifestyle of the island’s residents, including fishing, agriculture, and handicrafts. It also exhibits local art, music, and dance.

Mt Omuro

Mt Omuro is an extinct volcano that offers stunning panoramic views of the island. Visitors can enjoy a cable car ride and a hike up to the summit for a breathtaking view of the island and the Pacific Ocean.

Natural Attractions of Izu Islands: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Izu Islands are famous for their natural beauty and offer a wide range of outdoor activities that are perfect for nature lovers and adventure seekers. Here are some must-visit natural attractions on Izu Islands:

Koganezaki Furofushi Onsen

The Koganezaki Furofushi Onsen is a hot spring resort located on the northern tip of the Izu Peninsula. It boasts stunning views of the Pacific Ocean and rugged rocky cliffs. Visitors can relax in the hot springs while enjoying the ocean breeze and the sounds of the waves.

Izu Shaboten Zoo

Izu Shaboten Zoo is a unique zoo that features a wide variety of cactus plants. It also has an animal park where visitors can see lions, tigers, chimpanzees, and other animals.

Kawazu Seven Waterfalls

Kawazu Seven Waterfalls is a scenic hiking trail that features seven waterfalls and a beautiful river. It is a popular destination for nature lovers and hikers.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Izu Islands

Izu Islands offer a wide range of hiking and biking routes that take visitors through stunning natural scenery and cultural heritage sites. Here are some of the most popular hiking and biking routes on Izu Islands:

Oshima Island Hiking Trail

The Oshima Island Hiking Trail is a 10-km trail that takes visitors through the island’s volcanic landscape and offers stunning views of the Pacific Ocean.

Niijima Island Biking Trail

The Niijima Island Biking Trail is a 15-km route that winds through the island’s beautiful beaches, forests, and hills. It is a popular route for bikers and nature lovers.

Best Things to Do in Izu Islands: Activities and Tours

There are many exciting activities and tours that visitors can enjoy on Izu Islands. Here are some of the best things to do on Izu Islands:

Snorkeling and Scuba Diving

Izu Islands are famous for their crystal-clear waters and abundant marine life. Visitors can enjoy snorkeling and scuba diving to explore the beautiful underwater world.

Surfing and Windsurfing

Izu Islands has many beautiful beaches that are perfect for surfing and windsurfing. Visitors can rent equipment and take lessons from local instructors to enjoy the sport.

Island Hopping

Visitors can explore the unique features of each island by taking a ferry or a hydrofoil. Each island has its own unique charm and attraction that is worth discovering.

Week-Long Itinerary for Izu Islands

Here is a sample week-long itinerary for visitors who want to explore the best of Izu Islands:

Day 1: Arrive at Oshima Island and visit the Shikinejima Island Museum.

Day 2: Explore the Mt. Omuro and enjoy the panoramic views of the island.

Day 3: Take a ferry to Niijima Island and go for a cycling tour.

Day 4: Enjoy snorkeling and scuba diving on Niijima Island.

Day 5: Visit the Kawazu Seven Waterfalls and enjoy a hike.

Day 6: Take a ferry to Toshima Island and enjoy a day at the beach.

Day 7: Depart from Toshima Island.

Food and Drink Scene of Izu Islands

Must-Try Dishes

Izu Islands offer a wide range of local delicacies that are worth trying. Here are some of the must-try dishes on Izu Islands:

Izu Beef

Izu beef is a premium beef that is known for its tender texture and rich flavor. It is often served as steak or shabu-shabu.

Maguro Sashimi

Maguro sashimi is a delicacy made of fresh, raw tuna. It is characterized by its buttery texture and sweet, mild taste.

Local Beverages

Izu Islands also have a unique beverage culture. Here are some popular local beverages on Izu Islands:

Shochu

Shochu is a traditional Japanese spirit made from sweet potato, barley, or rice. It has a higher alcohol content than sake and is often served on the rocks.

Amazake

Amazake is a sweet, non-alcoholic drink made from fermented rice. It is traditionally served during festivals and celebrations.

Where to Stay in Izu Islands: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Izu Islands offer a wide range of accommodations, including hotels, hostels, and guesthouses. Here are some of the best places to stay on Izu Islands:

Oshima Island: Hotel Okada

Hotel Okada is a luxurious hotel located on Oshima Island. It features hot springs, a spa, and a pool, and offers stunning views of the Pacific Ocean.

Niijima Island: Guesthouse Tapan

Guesthouse Tapan is a cozy guesthouse located on Niijima Island. It features a communal kitchen, a lounge, and a rooftop terrace with a great view of the ocean.

Toshima Island: Izu Shoto Kominkan

Izu Shoto Kominkan is a traditional Japanese inn located on Toshima Island. It offers traditional Japanese-style rooms and hot springs.

What to Buy in Izu Islands: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Izu Islands offer a wide range of unique souvenirs and local products that visitors can bring home. Here are some of the best things to buy on Izu Islands:

Kihachi Tea

Kihachi tea is a type of green tea that is grown on Izu Islands. It has a unique taste and aroma and is often sold in decorative tins.

Mokume Glassware

Mokume glassware is a type of glassware that is made by layering different-colored glass together to create a unique pattern. It is a popular souvenir among visitors.

Local Culture and Customs of Izu Islands

Izu Islands have a rich and unique culture that is worth exploring. Here are some of the local customs and traditions on Izu Islands:

Kojiki Festival

Kojiki Festival is a traditional festival held on Oshima Island. It celebrates the island’s cultural heritage and features parades, music, dancing, and fireworks.

Yugawara Onsen

Yugawara Onsen is a hot spring resort that has been famous for over 1,200 years. It offers a traditional Japanese-style bath with hot spring water.

Best Time to Visit Izu Islands

The best time to visit Izu Islands is from April to November when the weather is mild and pleasant. However, each island has its own unique features and attractions that can be enjoyed all year round.

Do’s and Don’ts of Izu Islands

Do:

  • Respect local customs and traditions.
  • Take your shoes off when entering a Japanese-style room.
  • Prepare cash as some places may not accept credit cards.

Don’t:

  • Ignore safety warnings when swimming or hiking.
  • Be loud or disruptive in public spaces.
  • Leave trash behind when visiting natural attractions.

Safety and Health

Safety in Izu Islands

Izu Islands are generally safe for travelers. However, visitors should be aware of the following safety tips:

  • Check the weather forecast before going on outdoor activities.
  • Follow the instructions of local guides and signs.
  • Be careful when swimming or snorkeling, and avoid dangerous or unguarded areas.

Health in Izu Islands

Izu Islands have a high standard of healthcare, and medical facilities are readily available. However, visitors should bring their own medication and travel insurance.

Getting to Izu Islands: Transportation Options and Logistics

Izu Islands can be accessed by ferry or hydrofoil from Tokyo, Shimoda, or Atami. Visitors can also take a flight to the islands from Tokyo Haneda Airport.

Airports Near Izu Islands

The closest airport to Izu Islands is Oshima Airport, located on Oshima Island. Flights are available from Tokyo Haneda Airport and other major airports in Japan.

Bus Stations Near Izu Islands

Bus stations are available in Shimoda and Atami. Visitors can take a bus to reach the ferry or hydrofoil terminal to get to Izu Islands.

Other Options

Visitors can also reach Izu Islands by car, taxi, or rental car. However, traffic on the islands can be congested during peak season.

How to Get Around in Izu Islands

The best way to get around Izu Islands is by rental car or scooter. Bicycles are also available for rent on many islands. Visitors can also use the local bus services to explore the islands.

Where to Go Next from Izu Islands: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

If visitors have more time to explore Japan, they can visit nearby destinations and attractions from Izu Islands. Here are some of the nearby locations that are worth visiting:

Hakone

Hakone is a popular hot spring resort town located near Mt. Fuji. It offers stunning views of the mountain and boasts hot springs, museums, and other attractions.

Tokyo

Tokyo is the bustling capital of Japan and a hub for culture, entertainment, and shopping. Visitors can explore the city’s landmarks, museums, and restaurants.

Conclusion

Izu Islands offer visitors a unique blend of natural beauty, cultural heritage, and outdoor adventures. This comprehensive guide has provided information on the best places to visit, things to do, accommodations, transportation, and more to help visitors plan their trip to Izu Islands. We hope this guide inspires travelers to visit Izu Islands and experience their beauty firsthand.
