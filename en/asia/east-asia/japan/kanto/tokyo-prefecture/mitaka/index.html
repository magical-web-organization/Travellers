Introduction to Mitaka

Located in the western part of Tokyo, Mitaka is a suburban city that offers a mix of cultural and natural attractions to its visitors. It is home to the famous Ghibli Museum, which showcases the works of renowned Japanese animation studio Studio Ghibli. Mitaka also boasts several parks and hiking trails, making it an ideal destination for nature lovers. In this guide, we will take a look at the top places to visit, things to do, and where to stay in Mitaka.

List of Places to Visit in Mitaka

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Mitaka has a rich cultural heritage, and here are some of the most important cultural attractions that must be on your list:

  • Ghibli Museum: A museum dedicated to the world of Studio Ghibli, where you can see original sketches, animation cells, and learn about the creative process behind the famous animations such as Spirited Away and My Neighbor Totoro.
  • Mitaka City Gallery of Art: A modern art museum featuring exhibitions by both Japanese and international artists.
  • National Astronomical Observatory of Japan: A research facility that features a planetarium and a museum with exhibits related to Astronomy.
  • Jindai-ji Temple: Established in 733, this is Tokyo’s second oldest temple. It is located in Chofu, which is adjacent to Mitaka.

Natural Attractions of Mitaka: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

For those who want to escape the hustle and bustle of Tokyo, here are some natural attractions that will take your breath away:

  • Inokashira Park: A beautiful park covering 400,000 square meters that features a large pond, where you can rent paddle boats, and walkways that are perfect for a stroll in the park.
  • Mitaka City Park: A spacious park that features several playgrounds, fountains, and green spaces where you can relax and enjoy a picnic with your friends and family.
  • Musashino Forest Sport Plaza: A state-of-the-art indoor sports facility with a beautiful rooftop garden that offers a panoramic view of the surrounding area.
  • Mitaka Green Road: A 7 km-long paved trail that is perfect for hiking, jogging, and cycling, featuring beautiful views of the forest, river, and temples.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Mitaka

Mitaka is a heaven for outdoor enthusiasts who prefer to explore the city on foot or by bike. Here are some of the best hiking and biking routes in Mitaka:

  • Tamagawa Green Road: A scenic bike path that follows the Tamagawa River for 34 km, featuring beautiful views of the river and surrounding mountains.
  • Musashino Plateau Trail: A 50.4 km-long hiking trail that runs across 10 municipalities, including Mitaka, and features beautiful views of the Tama River and the Chichibu Mountains.
  • Mitaka Green Road: A 7 km-long trail that offers excellent views of the forest and river, and is perfect for walking, jogging, and cycling.
  • Inokashira Park: A beautiful park with walking paths and paved roads that are perfect for a leisurely stroll.

Best Things to Do in Mitaka: Activities and Tours

Here are some activities and tours that you can do in Mitaka to make the most of your trip:

  • Visit the Ghibli Museum and learn about the creative process behind the famous Studio Ghibli animations.
  • Take a boat ride in the Inokashira Park pond and enjoy the picturesque scenery.
  • Attend a concert or a sports event at the Musashino Forest Sport Plaza.
  • Explore the Mitaka Green Road on foot or bike and enjoy the scenic views of the forest and river.
  • Join a guided tour of Jindai-ji Temple and learn about the history of one of Tokyo’s oldest temples.

Week-Long Itinerary for Mitaka

Here is an itinerary to help you plan your week in Mitaka:

Food and Drink Scene of Mitaka

Must-Try Dishes

Mitaka is famous for its local specialties and traditional dishes. Here are some must-try foods in Mitaka:

  • Soba noodles: Mitaka is famous for producing high-quality soba noodles that are made from buckwheat flour and served either hot or cold.
  • Tonkatsu: Breaded pork cutlets that are deep-fried until crispy and served with rice, miso soup, and tsukemono (pickled vegetables).
  • Yakitori: Grilled chicken skewers that are marinated in a soy sauce and served with salt or tare (a sweetened soy sauce).
  • Okonomiyaki: A savory pancake that is made with a variety of ingredients such as cabbage, meat, seafood, and topped with sauce, mayonnaise, and bonito flakes.

Local Beverages

Mitaka is home to several breweries and sake distilleries that produce high-quality drinks. Here are some local beverages that you must try:

  • Azuma Brewery: Known for its rich and flavorful craft beer, Azuma brewery is a must-visit for beer enthusiasts.
  • Kirin Brewery: Founded in 1907, Kirin brewery is one of the most popular breweries in Japan, producing a wide range of beers.
  • Sake: Mitaka is home to several sake breweries that produce high-quality sake, such as Kaetsu Shuzo and Takashima Brewery.

Where to Stay in Mitaka: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Mitaka offers a range of accommodation options to suit every budget. Here are some of the best places to stay in Mitaka:

  • Kichijoji Daiichi Hotel: A modern hotel located near the Kichijoji Station, offering comfortable rooms and excellent amenities.
  • Mitaka City Hotel: A budget-friendly option located near the Mitaka Station, offering basic amenities and clean rooms.
  • Feels Like Home Mitaka: A guesthouse located in a residential neighborhood, offering cozy rooms and a friendly atmosphere.
  • Capsule Hotel Anshin Oyado: A unique experience for solo travelers, this capsule hotel offers private sleeping pods and communal spaces.

What to Buy in Mitaka: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Here are some souvenirs and local products that you can buy in Mitaka to bring home:

  • Studio Ghibli merchandise: The Ghibli Museum has a gift shop that sells a range of toys, t-shirts, and other products featuring famous characters from Studio Ghibli animations.
  • Soba noodles: You can buy high-quality soba noodles from local shops as a gift or souvenir.
  • Craft beer: Mitaka is famous for its craft beer scene, and you can buy high-quality beers from several breweries.
  • Local sake: Mitaka is home to several sake breweries that produce high-quality sake, which makes an ideal souvenir to take home.

Local Culture and Customs of Mitaka

The local culture and customs of Mitaka are influenced by the traditional values of Japan. Here are some customs and ettiquettes that you should be aware of:

  • Bowing: Bows are a common form of greeting in Japan, and they express gratitude, respect, and apology.
  • Shoes: You should remove your shoes before entering homes, temples, and some restaurants, and wear socks or slippers provided by the establishment.
  • Public behavior: It is important to be respectful of others in public spaces, avoid speaking loudly on phones, and eating or drinking in public transportation.

Best Time to Visit Mitaka

The best time to visit Mitaka is during the spring and autumn seasons, from March to May and September to November respectively. The weather during these seasons is mild and pleasant, and the colorful foliage adds a charm to the city’s parks and gardens. However, Mitaka can be visited throughout the year.

Do’s and Don’ts of Mitaka

Here are some things that you should and shouldn’t do in Mitaka:

Do:

  • Take off your shoes before entering homes and some establishments.
  • Try the local cuisine and drink.
  • Respect local customs and traditions.

Don’t:

  • Smoke in public places as there are strict laws against it.
  • Litter or leave garbage in public spaces.
  • Ignore the custom of bowing when greeting others.

Safety and Health

Safety in Mitaka

Mitaka is a safe city with low crime rates. However, it is important to remain vigilant and aware of your surroundings, especially in crowded areas and at night. It is also recommended to keep your valuables secure and not to carry large amounts of cash.

Health in Mitaka

Mitaka has a high-quality healthcare system, but medical expenses can be expensive for foreigners who do not have insurance. Carry travel insurance and avoid risky activities that can lead to injuries. It is also advisable to carry any necessary medication and consult with your doctor before traveling.

Getting to Mitaka: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Mitaka

The closest airport to Mitaka is the Haneda Airport, which is located approximately 25 km away. Shuttles, taxis, and trains are readily available from the airport to Mitaka.

Bus Stations Near Mitaka

There are several bus stations in and around Mitaka, providing easy and cheap transportation to nearby cities and towns. The major bus station is Mitaka Bus Terminal, which offers services to destinations such as Mount Fuji and Kawaguchiko.

Other Options

  • Train: Mitaka is served by several train lines such as the JR Chuo Line, the Keio Inokashira Line, and the Tokyo Metro Tozai Line.
  • Taxi: Taxis are available in Mitaka, but they can be expensive compared to other forms of transportation.
  • Car: Renting a car is possible, but not recommended due to the high cost of parking in Tokyo.

How to Get Around in Mitaka

Getting around Mitaka is easy, mainly due to its excellent public transportation system. Here are some convenient modes of transportation in Mitaka:

  • Trains: Mitaka is served by several train lines, including the JR Chuo Line, Keio Inokashira Line, and Tokyo Metro Tozai Line. All lines interconnect at Mitaka Station, making it easy to navigate.
  • Buses: Buses in Mitaka are cheap, reliable, and operate throughout the city. The Mitaka Bus Terminal serves as the main bus hub for the city.
  • Taxis: Taxis in Mitaka are expensive, but can be convenient for shorter trips or late-night travel.
  • Bicycles: Renting a bicycle is a great way to explore Mitaka on your own pace, and there are several rental shops available throughout the city.

Where to Go Next from Mitaka: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Mitaka is surrounded by several popular destinations and attractions that make for great day trips:

  • Kichijoji: A lively district that is a popular shopping and dining destination for locals and tourists alike, located just 1 station away from Mitaka Station.
  • Tama Hills: A beautiful destination for outdoor activities such as hiking, cycling, and golfing, located about 30 minutes away from Mitaka by train.
  • Mount Takao: A popular hiking destination that offers stunning views of the surrounding countryside and is located about 45 minutes away from Mitaka.

Conclusion

Mitaka is a wonderful destination for those looking for a mix of cultural and natural attractions. From the world-famous Ghibli Museum to the beautiful parks and hiking trails, there’s something for everyone in Mitaka. Use this guide to plan your trip and make the most of your time in this beautiful Tokyo suburb!
