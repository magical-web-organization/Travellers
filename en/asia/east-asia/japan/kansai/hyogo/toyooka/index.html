Introduction to Toyooka

Nestled in the northern part of Hyogo prefecture, Toyooka is a charming city with a rich history and a stunning landscape. Surrounded by mountains and facing the Sea of Japan, it offers a unique blend of outdoor adventures, cultural attractions, and culinary delights. If you’re looking for a less crowded destination in Japan, with a friendly local community, Toyooka should definitely be on your travel list.

List of Places to Visit in Toyooka

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Kinosaki Onsen

A visit to Toyooka is not complete without experiencing the famous natural hot springs of Kinosaki Onsen. This historic onsen town boasts seven public bathhouses, some of which are more than 1300 years old. You can stroll around the town in a traditional yukata robe, sample local sweets, and relax in the soothing waters of the hot springs.

Izushi Castle Town

Located just a short train ride away from Toyooka, Izushi Castle Town is a well-preserved historical district that takes you back to the Edo period. Its streets are lined with white-walled buildings, traditional shops, and temples. Don’t miss the chance to try “sara soba,” a unique dish of thin buckwheat noodles served on small, portable bamboo plates.

Takeda Castle Ruins

If you’re looking for a scenic spot with a touch of mystery, Takeda Castle Ruins should be on your list. Perched on a mountain peak, the remains of this castle look like they’re floating above a sea of clouds on clear days. It’s a challenging hike to get there, but the reward is worth it.

Natural Attractions of Toyooka: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Hyonosen-Ushiroyama-Nagisan National Park

Stretching across the border of Hyogo and Tottori prefectures, this national park is a playground for nature lovers. Its rugged peaks, dense forests, and marshland offer a variety of hiking trails, camping sites, and wildlife watching opportunities. You can also visit the Hyonosen-Enchi herb garden to learn about medicinal plants and enjoy a panoramic view of the surrounding mountains.

San’in Kaigan Geopark

The San’in Kaigan Geopark covers a coastal area in northern Hyogo and western Tottori. It features unique geological formations, such as towering cliffs, sea caves, and sea stacks. You can explore the park by foot or by sea kayak. Keep an eye out for the rare black-tailed gulls, who nest in the area.

Maruyama River

The Maruyama River is a hidden gem in Toyooka, perfect for a leisurely stroll or a picnic. Its clear waters run through a forested valley, where you can see wild flowers, birds, and insects. You can also try “ayu-no-shioyaki,” a local dish of grilled sweetfish seasoned with salt and served with rice.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Toyooka

Daisen-Oki National Park

Daisen-Oki National Park covers a vast area in Tottori and Shimane prefectures, and extends to the northern part of Hyogo as well. Its trails offer breathtaking views of the sea, the mountains, and the islands. You can choose from easy walks to demanding climbs, depending on your level of fitness and experience.

Tanba Mountains

The Tanba Mountains are a range that runs through the southern part of Hyogo. They offer a less challenging but no less enjoyable hike than the ones in Daisen-Oki. You can see lush forests, small waterfalls, and expansive rice paddies. Stop by a local village and try “yama-no-imo,” a type of mountain yam that’s a specialty of the area.

Yumura-Onsen Village

Yumura-Onsen Village is a hot spring resort with a difference – it’s situated in the middle of mountains, with hiking trails branching out from it. You can soak in the hot springs after a long day of hiking or explore the caves and waterfalls nearby. Don’t forget to sample the local sake, which is made from the pure spring water.

Best Things to Do in Toyooka: Activities and Tours

Kinosaki Marine World

Kinosaki Marine World is an aquarium that showcases the marine life of the Sea of Japan. Its most famous attraction is the dolphin show, where you can see the agile creatures perform impressive stunts. You can also see sea otters, penguins, and other animals up close. Children will love the touch pool and the ride on the mini-submarine.

Toyooka + α

Toyooka + α is a tour company that offers various activities and experiences, such as pottery-making, fishing, and cycling. You can join a guided tour, with English-speaking staff, and learn about the local culture and customs. They also have a rental shop where you can rent bicycles, fishing gear, and other equipment.

Tajima Yaotsu Canyoning

Tajima Yaotsu Canyoning is a unique and exciting way to explore the natural beauty of Toyooka. You’ll be equipped with a wetsuit, a helmet, and a life jacket, and slide down waterfalls, jump into pools, and swim in rapids. Don’t worry if you’re a beginner – the guides will teach you everything you need to know.

Food and Drink Scene of Toyooka

Must-Try Dishes

Tajima Beef

Tajima beef is a premium brand of wagyu beef that’s raised in Hyogo prefecture. It’s known for its tenderness, marbling, and rich flavor. You can try it in various forms, such as shabu-shabu, yakiniku, and steak. Make sure to pair it with some sake or red wine.

Matsuba Crab

Matsuba crab is a local delicacy that’s only available during the winter months. It’s a type of snow crab with a sweet and delicate taste. You can have it boiled, grilled, or in a hot pot. Don’t forget to dip it in some soy sauce mixed with grated daikon radish.

Sasa-dango

Sasa-dango is a sweet dumpling made with rice flour and sweet red bean paste. What makes it unique is that it’s wrapped in a bamboo leaf, which gives it a subtle aroma and a chewy texture. You can find it in traditional sweet shops in Kinosaki Onsen and other places.

Local Beverages

Tajima Sake

Tajima sake is a type of Japanese rice wine that’s brewed with water from the mountains in northern Hyogo. It’s known for its smoothness, aroma, and versatility. You can try it in various styles, such as junmai, ginjo, and daiginjo. Visit a local brewery to see the brewing process and do some tasting.

Toyooka Wine

Toyooka wine is a relatively new addition to the local beverage scene, but it’s gaining popularity. It’s made from grapes that are grown in the sandy soil of the coastal area. It has a fruity and refreshing flavor, and pairs well with seafood and pasta dishes. Visit a winery to do some tasting and buy some bottles to bring home.

Where to Stay in Toyooka: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Kinosaki Onsen

Kinosaki Onsen has a wide range of accommodations, from luxury ryokans to budget hostels. Ryokans are traditional Japanese inns that offer tatami rooms, futon beds, and hot spring baths. Some of the most popular ones are Kawaguchiya Honkan, Mikuniya, and Ichinoyu Katei.

Toyooka City

If you prefer a more modern style of hotel, Toyooka city has several options to choose from. Hotel New Tanaka and Green Ridge Hotel are located near the train station and offer comfortable rooms and convenient access to restaurants and bars.

Yumura-Onsen Village

Yumura-Onsen Village has few accommodations, but they offer a unique experience. Kureya Ryokan is situated in the middle of mountains and has a natural hot spring bath from which you can enjoy a panoramic view. It also serves organic meals made from local ingredients.

What to Buy in Toyooka: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Kasuri Fabric

Kasuri is a type of textile that’s woven with dyed yarns to create a pattern. It’s a traditional craft of northern Hyogo, and Toyooka is one of the centers of production. You can find various items made from kasuri, such as scarves, bags, and tablecloths. They make great souvenirs and gifts.

Sandaika Wooden Bowls

Sandaika is a type of wood that’s unique to the San’in region. It’s hard and durable, and has a striped pattern. Sandaika wooden bowls are handmade by local craftsmen, and come in various shapes and sizes. They’re not only beautiful but also functional, as they can be used for serving food or as decor.

Tajima Beef Jerky

Tajima beef jerky is a snack that’s both delicious and healthy. It’s made from thin slices of beef that are marinated in soy sauce, sake, and other seasonings, and then dried. It’s perfect for munching on the go or as a gift for carnivorous friends. You can find it in souvenir shops in Kinosaki Onsen and other places.

Local Culture and Customs of Toyooka

Toyooka has a rich cultural heritage that’s still preserved by the local community. Some of the customs and traditions that you can observe are:

  • Wearing yukata robes in Kinosaki Onsen
  • Trying out “sara” plates in Izushi Castle Town
  • Making pottery in Tanba
  • Eating sweetfish in the Maruyama River
  • Watching kabuki performances in Yumura-Onsen Village

Respect the local customs and follow the rules, such as taking off your shoes when entering a tatami room and separating your trash properly.

Best Time to Visit Toyooka

The best time to visit Toyooka depends on your preference and interest. Here are some suggestions:

  • Spring (March to May): Cherry blossoms bloom in Kinosaki Onsen and other places. The weather is mild.
  • Summer (June to August): You can enjoy outdoor activities, such as hiking and swimming. The weather is hot and humid.
  • Autumn (September to November): The leaves change color in the mountains, providing a stunning view. The weather is cool.
  • Winter (December to February): You can soak in the hot springs and eat warming dishes, such as nabe hot pot and oden. Matsuba crab is in season.

Keep in mind that Kinosaki Onsen is crowded during peak seasons, such as the New Year holidays and the Golden Week in May. If you prefer a quieter atmosphere, avoid those times.

Do’s and Don’ts of Toyooka

Do:

  • Take off your shoes when entering a tatami room or a private house.
  • Bow when greeting someone.
  • Use chopsticks properly.
  • Follow the rules in hot spring baths.
  • Be quiet in public places, such as train stations and buses.

Don’t:

  • Use your mobile phone in public transportation.
  • Toss away trash in the wrong bin.
  • Speak loudly in public places.
  • Wear revealing clothes in temples or shrines.
  • Tip the service staff.

Safety and Health

Safety in Toyooka

Toyooka is generally a safe place to travel, with low crime rates and a helpful local community. However, you should be aware of the following:

  • Follow traffic rules when driving or cycling.
  • Watch out for wild animals, such as monkeys and bears, when hiking in the mountains.
  • Beware of slippery roads and icy conditions during the winter.
  • Use common sense when withdrawing money from ATMs.

Health in Toyooka

Toyooka has good medical facilities and a high standard of hygiene. However, you should keep in mind the following:

  • Bring sufficient medication and prescription documents, if you have any chronic conditions.
  • Wear sunscreen and a hat, and drink plenty of water during hot weather.
  • Be aware of food allergies and ask for help if needed.
  • Follow the guidelines for preventing the spread of COVID-19.

Getting to Toyooka: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Toyooka

The nearest major airport to Toyooka is Kansai International Airport, which is about 170 kilometers away. You can take a bus or a train to get to Toyooka from the airport.

Bus Stations Near Toyooka

Toyooka has a bus terminal, which is located next to the train station. It’s served by several bus companies, such as JR Bus and Toyooka City Bus. You can check the schedule and buy tickets online or at the terminal.

Other Options

The most convenient way to travel to Toyooka is by train. It’s connected to major cities, such as Osaka, Kyoto, and Kobe, by the JR Sanin Line and the Kinosaki Limited Express. You can also rent a car or a bicycle to explore the area.

How to Get Around in Toyooka

The best way to get around Toyooka is by public transportation. The train and bus networks cover most of the area. You can buy a one-day pass or a multi-day pass for unlimited rides. Taxis are also available, but they’re relatively expensive. Cycling is a good option for short distances, as the roads are well-maintained and safe.

Where to Go Next from Toyooka: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Tottori

Tottori prefecture is located to the north of Hyogo prefecture, and is known for its sand dunes, hot springs, and sake. You can visit the Tottori Sand Dunes, which are the only ones in Japan, and ride a camel. You can also soak in the hot springs of Misasa Onsen or visit the local breweries.

Kyoto

Kyoto is a cultural capital of Japan, known for its temples, gardens, and cuisine. You can take a train from Toyooka to Kyoto station, and then explore the city by bus or subway. Don’t miss the iconic sites, such as Kinkakuji Temple, Fushimi Inari Shrine, and Gion district.

Conclusion

Toyooka offers a unique and unforgettable travel experience, with its scenic landscapes, cultural attractions, and culinary delights. Whether you’re a solo traveler, a couple, or a family, there’s something for everyone. Follow this guide to make the most of your trip and create lasting memories.
