Introduction to Kobe

Kobe is a picturesque city in Japan situated along the coast of the Seto Inland Sea. It is the sixth-largest city of Japan and features a blend of traditional and modern culture. Known for its fine dining, hot springs, and stunning views of the sea and surrounding mountains, Kobe is a must-visit place in Japan for tourists.

In this travel guide, we will explore the best tourist destinations of Kobe, local culture and customs, and the top things to do in this beautiful city.

List of Places to Visit in Kobe

Without any doubt, the following tourist destinations must be on your list while planning a trip to Kobe.

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  1. Kobe City Museum: Learn about the history and culture of Kobe, including the Great Hanshin Earthquake of 1995.

  2. Hyogo Prefectural Museum of Art: This museum showcases a range of art styles from the 19th century to current times.

  3. Kobe Port Tower: This iconic red tower is a must-visit place to witness the panoramic view of the city.

  4. Meriken Park: It features notable places such as the Kobe Maritime Museum and Kobe Port Earthquake Memorial Park.

  5. Nunobiki Falls: These scenic waterfalls are a natural beauty and a perfect spot for a nature lover.

Natural Attractions of Kobe: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  1. Mount Rokko: Visit Mount Rokko to witness panoramic views of Kobe and the Bay Area.

  2. Akashi Kaikyo National Government Park: This park features a cycling path, playgrounds, and various outdoor activities for adventure enthusiasts.

  3. Arima Onsen: A famous hot spring to relax and refresh your mind and body.

  4. Suma Beach: A perfect place for sea, sun, and sand lovers.

  5. Kobe Animal Kingdom: A unique zoo featuring exotic animals, birds, and plants.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Kobe

Kobe offers fantastic options for adventurous hikers and bike riders.

  1. Mount Maya: A popular hiking spot, Mount Maya offers a stunning view of the city and the sea.

  2. Akashi Kaikyo Bridge: Take a biking tour and ride across the longest suspension bridge in the world.

  3. Rokko Alpine Botanical Garden: A beautiful garden featuring various plant species and hiking trails.

Best Things to Do in Kobe: Activities and Tours

  1. Sake Brewery Tour: Taste the local brew and learn about the traditional process of sake making.

  2. Kobe Beef Dining: Indulge in the world-renowned Kobe Beef, a delicacy of Kobe.

  3. Kobe Harborland: A trendy shopping and entertainment district featuring restaurants, shops, and outdoor performances.

  4. Kobe Luminarie: A spectacular light festival held annually to honor the victims of the Great Hanshin Earthquake.

  5. Kobe City Suma Football Stadium: Check out the home of the Vissel Kobe football club.

Week-Long Itinerary for Kobe

Here’s a suggested itinerary for a week-long trip to Kobe:

Day 1 Visit the Kobe City Museum and Meriken Park
Day 2 Explore the Mount Rokko and eat Kobe beef for dinner
Day 3 Take a Sake Brewery tour and visit Nunobiki Falls
Day 4 Enjoy the beach at Suma Beach and relax at Arima Onsen
Day 5 Witness the panoramic views from Kobe Port Tower
Day 6 Take a biking tour of the Akashi Kaikyo Bridge
Day 7 Watch a Vissel Kobe football match at Suma Football Stadium

Food and Drink Scene of Kobe

Kobe has a thriving food and drink scene that offers a wide range of options. Here are some of the must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  1. Kobe Beef: Known for its exceptional marbling, Kobe beef is a Japanese delicacy that is recognized worldwide for its taste and quality.

  2. Sushi: Indulge in the finest sushi made with fresh seafood.

  3. Takoyaki: A popular Japanese street food made mainly of octopus.

  4. Okonomiyaki: A savory pancake made with various ingredients, including seafood, vegetables, and meat.

  5. Onigiri: A traditional Japanese rice ball with various stuffing and toppings.

Local Beverages

  1. Sake: A traditional Japanese alcoholic drink made from fermented rice.

  2. Shochu: A distilled Japanese spirit made with various grains and vegetables.

  3. Plum Wine: A sweet and sour wine made with Japanese plums.

  1. Hotel La Suite Kobe Harborland

What to Buy in Kobe: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Kobe is famous for various handicrafts and local products that make perfect souvenirs for tourists. Some of the must-buy products from Kobe are:

  1. Handmade Japanese blades: Known as “Kobe Swords,” these are traditionally crafted swords made in Kobe.

  2. Kobe Leatherware: High-quality leather bags and wallets made with the finest leather.

  3. Kitano Tenmangu Shrine Accessories: These accessories feature the art of “Yuzen Zome,” a beautiful dyeing technique.

Local Culture and Customs of Kobe

When visiting Kobe, it’s essential to be mindful of local customs and etiquette. Here are some of the customs and traditions that you should know:

  1. Japanese bow: Japanese people bow as a form of greeting and showing respect.

  2. Take off your shoes: Guests are expected to remove their footwear in many Japanese homes and traditional restaurants, so make sure to check before entering.

  3. Learn a few Japanese phrases: If possible, learn some basic Japanese phrases to communicate with the locals and make the most of your trip.

Best Time to Visit Kobe

The best time to visit Kobe is from March to May and September to November. During this time, the weather is mild and comfortable, and the city is less crowded than other seasons.

Do’s and Don’ts of Kobe

Here are some of the do’s and don’ts you should keep in mind while traveling to Kobe:

Do:

  1. Respect the local culture and customs.

  2. Carry cash as many small shops and restaurants do not accept credit cards.

  3. Try local cuisines and beverages.

  4. Carry a travel guide or a map.

Don’t:

  1. Avoid tipping as it is not customary in Japan.

  2. Do not smoke in public areas.

  3. Avoid loud conversations on public transport.

Safety and Health

Safety in Kobe

Kobe is a very safe city for travelers. However, it’s essential to take normal precautions to ensure your safety, such as:

  1. Keep your belongings safe and secure.

  2. Take a registered taxi late at night.

  3. Avoid walking alone in isolated areas at night.

Health in Kobe

Japan has an excellent healthcare system, and medical facilities in Kobe are no exception. If you plan to travel to Kobe, it’s recommended to have travel insurance that covers medical emergencies.

Getting to Kobe: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Kobe

  1. Kansai International Airport: Located about 75 kilometers from Kobe, it is the largest airport near Kobe.

  2. Osaka International Airport: Located about 30 kilometers from Kobe, it’s a convenient option for domestic flights.

Bus Stations Near Kobe

  1. JR Kobe Station: It is the primary transport hub in Kobe for local, regional, and intercity buses.

  2. Sannomiya Bus Terminal: Located in the city center, it connects with various cities in western Japan.

Other Options

  1. Shinkansen: Japan’s high-speed train, the Shinkansen, stops at Shin-Kobe Station and connects various cities in Japan.

  2. Ferry: Ferries run between Kobe and other nearby cities, including Shikoku and Kyushu.

How to Get Around in Kobe

Kobe has a well-connected public transportation system that includes buses, trains, and subways. You can also rent bicycles and electric bikes in many places around the city. Getting around in Kobe is relatively easy and convenient.

Where to Go Next from Kobe: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

If you have extra time in your trip, you can consider visiting these nearby destinations and take a day trip:

  1. Kyoto: One of the most famous tourist destinations in Japan, Kyoto, is just an hour’s train ride away from Kobe.

  2. Himeji Castle: Himeji Castle, a UNESCO World Heritage Site, is one of Japan’s most beautiful castles, located about an hour from Kobe.

  3. Osaka: The vibrant city of Osaka is just 40 minutes away from Kobe by train and features a wide range of local attractions such as Osaka Castle and Universal Studios Japan.

Conclusion

Kobe is a perfect destination for travelers looking for a mix of traditional and modern Japanese culture. From natural beauty to unique local cuisines, there’s something for everyone in Kobe. Follow this comprehensive guide to plan your trip to Kobe and explore this fascinating city with ease.
