Introduction to Himeji

Himeji is a charming city located in the Hyogo Prefecture of Japan, in the Kansai region. Known for its magnificent castle, Himeji attracts tourists from all over the world, and is a must-see destination for those travelling to Japan. Apart from the castle, the city has a lot to offer in terms of culture, nature, food, and drink. In this comprehensive travel guide, we will explore the best things to see and do in Himeji, as well as provide you with practical information about the city.

List of Places to Visit in Himeji

Here’s a list of must-see attractions in Himeji:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Himeji Castle: Himeji Castle, also known as the White Heron Castle, is the most iconic landmark of the city. It’s a UNESCO World Heritage site and is considered one of the best-preserved castles in Japan.
  • Koko-en Garden: This Japanese style garden is located next to Himeji Castle and offers stunning views of the castle and its surroundings.
  • Engyo-ji Temple: A Buddhist temple located on Mt. Shosha, in the outskirts of Himeji. The temple is known for its striking architecture and offers a great view of the city from the top.
  • Himeji City Museum of Art: The museum has a vast collection of Japanese art, including paintings and ceramics.

Natural Attractions of Himeji: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Mt. Takamikura: This mountain is located in Himeji’s northern outskirts and is popular among hikers. It offers a breathtaking view of the city and the surrounding mountains.
  • Hyogo Prefectural Tajima Park: A great place to relax and picnic, this large park offers stunning views of the city and the sea.
  • Shoshazan Engyo-ji Temple: Located on Mt. Shosha, this temple is known for its scenic views and scenic hiking trails.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Himeji

Besides Mt. Takamikura and Mt. Shosha, Himeji offers many other hiking and biking trails. Some of the most popular routes include the Himeji Castle Loop, the Kokoen Garden Path, and the Hyogo Forest Path. These routes offer stunning views of the city and its surroundings, and are perfect for those who love nature and adventure.

Best Things to Do in Himeji: Activities and Tours

When it comes to things to do in Himeji, there’s no shortage of options. Some of the best activities and tours include:

  • Himeji Castle Night Tour: Explore the castle at night and enjoy its illuminated beauty.
  • Sake Brewery Tours: Himeji is known for its delicious sake, and taking a brewery tour is a great way to learn more about the local brewing process and sample some of the best sake in Japan.
  • Kimono Rental and Photoshoot: Dress up in a traditional Japanese kimono and take a photoshoot in front of Himeji Castle.

Week-Long Itinerary for Himeji

If you’re planning a week-long trip to Himeji, here’s a sample itinerary for you:

  • Day 1: Visit Himeji Castle and Kokoen Garden.
  • Day 2: Hike Mt. Takamikura and visit Hyogo Prefectural Tajima Park.
  • Day 3: Take a sake brewery tour and try some local sake.
  • Day 4: Visit Engyo-ji Temple on Mt. Shosha and enjoy the scenic views.
  • Day 5: Take a kimono rental and photoshoot in front of Himeji Castle.
  • Day 6: Explore the city’s food and drink scene.
  • Day 7: Relax and unwind at a local hot spring, like the Himeji City Tegarayama Hot Spring.

Food and Drink Scene of Himeji

Himeji is known for its delicious food and drink options. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Oden: A traditional Japanese stew made with various fish cakes, tofu, and other ingredients.
  • Himeji Castle Rice Crackers: A popular snack made from rice flour and featuring Himeji Castle’s image.
  • Himeji Burger: A locally made burger with a beef patty, cheese, lettuce, tomato, and a special sauce.

Local Beverages

  • Sake: Himeji is known for its delicious sake, and there are many local breweries that offer tours and tastings.
  • Akashiyaki: A local specialty made with octopus, batter, and green onions, served with a sweet sauce.

Where to Stay in Himeji: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Himeji offers a range of accommodation options to suit all budgets. Whether you’re looking for a luxury hotel or a budget-friendly hostel, you’re sure to find something suitable in the city. Some of the best options include:

  • Himeji Castle Grandvrio Hotel: A luxurious hotel located near Himeji Castle, featuring spacious rooms and a rooftop bar.
  • Himeji 588 Guesthouse: A budget-friendly guesthouse located in a historic building, featuring traditional Japanese-style rooms.
  • J-Hoppers Himeji: A hostel located close to Himeji Station, featuring dorms and private rooms.

What to Buy in Himeji: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

If you’re looking for souvenirs and local products to bring home, here are some great options:

  • Himeji Castle Rice Crackers: A popular snack made from rice flour and featuring Himeji Castle’s image.
  • Himeji Castle Keychains and Postcards: A great way to remember your visit to the castle.
  • Sake: Himeji is known for its delicious sake, and there are many local breweries that offer bottles to take home.

Local Culture and Customs of Himeji

Himeji has a rich cultural heritage, and the locals take pride in preserving their traditions. Here are some customs and etiquettes to keep in mind when visiting:

  • Bowing: Bowing is a common way of greeting people in Japan, and you should do it when meeting someone for the first time.
  • Removing Shoes: When entering a temple or a Japanese-style house, you’re expected to remove your shoes.
  • Chopsticks: Do not stick your chopsticks in your rice bowl, as it is considered bad manners.

Best Time to Visit Himeji

The best time to visit Himeji is during spring (March to May) and autumn (September to November), when the weather is mild and pleasant, and the cherry blossoms and autumn leaves are in full bloom. However, Himeji is a year-round destination, and you can visit any time of the year, depending on your preferences.

Do’s and Don’ts of Himeji

When visiting Himeji, here are some do’s and don’ts to keep in mind:

Do:

  • Respect the local customs and traditions.
  • Try the local food and drink.
  • Take off your shoes when entering a temple or a Japanese-style house.

Don’t:

  • Smoke in public places.
  • Litter or damage the environment.
  • Wear revealing clothing when visiting temples or religious places.

Safety and Health

Safety in Himeji

Himeji is a safe city, and violent crimes are rare. However, like any other city, it’s important to take precautions and stay aware of your surroundings.

Health in Himeji

Himeji has a good healthcare system, and there are many hospitals and clinics in the city. However, it’s important to have travel insurance that covers medical emergencies.

Getting to Himeji: Transportation Options and Logistics

Getting to Himeji is easy, and there are many transportation options available.

Airports Near Himeji

The closest airport to Himeji is Kansai International Airport, located about 100 km away. From the airport, you can take a train or a bus to Himeji.

Bus Stations Near Himeji

Himeji Bus Terminal is located near Himeji Station, and offers buses to various destinations in the Hyogo Prefecture and beyond.

Other Options

If travelling from Tokyo or Osaka, taking the Shinkansen is the easiest and fastest option.

How to Get Around in Himeji

Himeji is a small city, and most of the attractions are located within walking distance of each other. However, you can also use public transportation, such as buses and trains, to get around the city.

Where to Go Next from Himeji: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

If you have more time in Japan, here are some destinations you can visit from Himeji:

  • Kobe: A vibrant port city known for its beef and scenic views of Mount Rokko.
  • Osaka: A bustling city known for its street food, nightlife, and shopping.
  • Nara: A historic city known for its temples, shrines, and deer.

Conclusion

Himeji is a beautiful city that offers a blend of culture, nature, and adventure. Whether you’re interested in visiting its iconic castle, hiking its scenic trails, or tasting its delicious sake, Himeji is a must-visit destination for anyone travelling to Japan. We hope this comprehensive travel guide has given you a good idea of what to see and do in Himeji, as well as practical information about the city.
