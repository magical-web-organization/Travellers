Introduction to Akashi

Located in Hyogo prefecture in the Kansai region of Japan, Akashi is a small coastal town that offers a blend of captivating natural beauty and cultural landmarks. Often referred to as a hidden gem of the country, Akashi is located on the coast of the Seto Inland Sea and is famous for its iconic Akashi Kaikyo Bridge, known to be the world’s longest suspension bridge.

List of Places to Visit in Akashi

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Akashi Castle

Akashi Castle is a restored 16th-century building that once served as a military stronghold. Today, it stands as a symbol of the town’s rich history and culture. Visitors can explore the castle’s impressive architecture and soak in the stunning views of the surrounding city.

Akashi Municipal Planetarium

The Akashi Municipal Planetarium is a must-visit attraction for science enthusiasts. The planetarium offers an immersive experience that allows visitors to explore the galaxies, learn about astronomy and the history of the universe.

Natural Attractions of Akashi: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Okura Beach

Located on the coast of the Seto Inland Sea, Okura Beach is a beautiful and serene spot for relaxation, sunbathing, and swimming. The beach’s calm waters and gentle breeze make it a delightful spot for a day trip picnic with family and friends.

Rokko Mountain

Rokko Mountain is considered to be one of the most beautiful scenic spots in the Kansai region. The mountain features an observatory with panoramic views of the Seto Inland Sea and the Akashi Kaikyo Bridge. In the winter months, visitors can enjoy a scenic cable car ride up to the mountain peaks and indulging in the beautiful snowy vistas.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Akashi

Akashi has several hiking and biking trails for visitors to explore.

Akashi Kaikyo Bridge Walkway

The Akashi Kaikyo Bridge Walkway is the world’s longest pedestrian suspension bridge. The walkway stretches over 3.5 km, allowing visitors to experience jaw-dropping views of the Seto Inland Sea.

Akashi Seaside Park

Akashi Seaside Park is a waterfront park with biking and walking trails. Visitors can rent bicycles here and ride along the scenic coastline.

Best Things to Do in Akashi: Activities and Tours

Coastal Cruise

Take a scenic cruise along the Seto Inland Sea and witness the stunning views of the Akashi Kaikyo Bridge, the Rokko Mountains, and other famous landmarks.

Sake Brewery Tours

Akashi is famous for its sake breweries. Take a tour of one of the many breweries and learn about the brewing process of this traditional Japanese drink.

Week-Long Itinerary for Akashi

Day 1-2: Explore the town of Akashi, visit the castle and the planetarium.

Day 3: Spend the day at Okura Beach and indulge in some water sport activities.

Day 4-5: Take a hiking trip to the Rokko Mountain or bike along the Seaside Park.

Day 6: Take a coastal cruise along the Seto Inland Sea and witness the stunning views of the Akashi Kaikyo Bridge and other landmarks.

Day 7: Take a sake brewery tour and enjoy a traditional Japanese meal.

Food and Drink Scene of Akashi

Must-Try Dishes

  • Akashi-yaki: a type of ball-shaped Japanese snack filled with octopus, shrimp, or vegetables.
  • Akashiyaki soba: a bowl of steaming hot noodles in a warm broth that is popularly served with Akashi-yaki.
  • Tako senbei: A Japanese rice cracker dish made with octopus.

Local Beverages

Akashi is famous for its sake breweries. Sake is a traditional Japanese alcoholic beverage made from fermented rice. Other popular local beverages include locally brewed beer and fruit juices.

Where to Stay in Akashi: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

There are several accommodation options in Akashi, ranging from luxurious hotels and resorts to budget-friendly hostels and guesthouses.

  • Akashiya (luxury ryokan)
  • Okura Hotel (mid-range hotel)
  • The Trestle Hostel (budget-friendly hostel)

What to Buy in Akashi: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Sake: Akashi is known for its sake breweries; try some local varieties.
  • Akashi-yaki: Purchase some Akashi-yaki to relish as you reminisce about your trip.
  • Traditional Japanese souvenirs, such as wooden chopsticks, Japanese tea sets, and more.

Local Culture and Customs of Akashi

Akashi is known for its strong cultural heritage and celebrations of traditional festivals. Visitors should take note of the following etiquette and customs:

  • Visitors should remove their shoes when entering homes, temples, and shrines.
  • Bowing is a common form of greeting in Japan.
  • Keep your voice low when visiting public places.

Best Time to Visit Akashi

The best time to visit Akashi is during the spring season (March to May) when the cherry blossoms are in full bloom, and the weather is pleasant. Autumn (September to December) is also a great time to visit the region when the leaves of the trees turn golden-hued.

Do’s and Don’ts of Akashi

Do:

  • Learn some basic Japanese expressions before your trip.
  • Bow, show gratitude, and respect for the local customs.
  • Carry cash; not all shops and establishments accept card payments.

Don’t:

  • Don’t take photos or videos of people without their permission.
  • Don’t eat or smoke while walking.
  • Don’t tip; it is not customary in Japan.

Safety and Health

Safety in Akashi

Akashi is a safe city to visit. However, visitors should take the usual precautions, such as not leaving their belongings unattended and staying alert in crowded areas.

Health in Akashi

Akashi offers excellent healthcare facilities. Visitors should ensure they have international health insurance in case of medical emergencies.

Getting to Akashi: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Akashi

The nearest airport is Kansai International Airport, located in Osaka. From here, visitors can take a train or bus ride to reach Akashi.

Bus Stations Near Akashi

Akashi Station is the major bus station where visitors can board local buses to various parts of the town.

Other Options

Visitors can take a train to Akashi from major cities in Japan, such as Tokyo and Kyoto.

How to Get Around in Akashi

Akashi has an efficient public transportation system comprising buses and trains. Taxis are also available.

Where to Go Next from Akashi: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Kobe

Kobe is a neighboring city famous for its charming streets and delicious cuisine. It is known for various tourist spots such as the Kobe Port Tower, Mount Rokko and the delicious Kobe beef.

Himeji

Himeji is a city famous for the magnificent Himeji Castle, a UNESCO world heritage site. The castle’s breathtaking beauty is an excellent stop for history buffs and is known as one of the most beautiful castles in Japan.

Conclusion

Whether you’re looking to explore the cultural landmarks or indulge in outdoor adventures, Akashi has something for everyone. Use our comprehensive travel guide to plan your visit to this hidden gem on the coast of the Seto Inland Sea. We hope you have a great time in Akashi, Japan!
