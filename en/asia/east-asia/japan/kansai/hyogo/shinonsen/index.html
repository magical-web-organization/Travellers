Introduction to Shinonsen

Shinonsen is a lesser-known town located in Hyogo, a prefecture in the Kansai region of Japan. This charming town is a hidden gem, renowned for its natural hot springs and beautiful scenery. Shinonsen spans across three peninsulas, each with its unique tourist attractions, making it a perfect destination for nature lovers and adventure seekers alike.

List of Places to Visit in Shinonsen

The town of Shinonsen is a small, quaint area that offers a range of tourist attractions. Here are some of the must-visit places in Shinonsen:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

1. Arayu

Arayu is a public bathhouse that has been operational since the Edo period. The building’s distinctive architecture and interior design add historical significance to the bathhouse. One can experience a traditional Japanese bath and relax in pure natural hot springs.

2. Shinonsen History Museum

The Shinonsen History Museum is known for its exhibits that depict the history of the town. This museum has an impressive collection of displays and artifacts that hold significance to the region’s history.

Natural Attractions of Shinonsen: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

1. Tateiwa Observation Deck

Tateiwa Observation Deck is located on top of Hyonosen. It offers a mesmerizing panoramic view of the surrounding peaks and valleys, including the captivating Kumihama Bay.

2. Tendaki Waterfall

Takekari Waterfall is a sight to behold. It is a must-visit location offering both peaceful scenery and tranquil ambiance.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Shinonsen

1. Hyonosen Futatabi

Hyonosen Futatabi is a circular hiking route that takes you around Hyonosen. It is a 6 km walking trail that takes 2-3 hours to complete. This hike offers magnificent forest and mountain views.

2. The Mochigase Cycling Road (Mochi Road)

The Mochigase Cycling Road (Mochi Road) is the perfect biking route for beginners. The cycling road offers a smooth path, surrounded by mesmerizing rice paddies, fields, and mountain views, that can also be enjoyed on foot.

Best Things to Do in Shinonsen: Activities and Tours

1. Go for a River Cruise

One can take a tranquil boat ride along the Maruyama River that passes through the dense forest of Shinonsen. It is the perfect weekend getaway for friends and family.

2. Rent a Bike or Scooter

Scooter and bike rental shops are present all over Shinonsen, offering exciting alternatives to explore the region. It is arguably the best way to cover long distances by sightseeing and stopping wherever you please.

Week-Long Itinerary for Shinonsen

Day Activity
Day 1 Visit Arayu public bathhouse, followed by a relaxing afternoon in Takekari waterfall.
Day 2 Go for a bike ride on Mochi road and witness mesmerizing vistas of rice paddies and scenic mountain ranges.
Day 3-4 Hike around Hyonosen and embark on a refreshing river cruise through the dense forests
Day 5 Visit the Shinonsen History Museum and Grab a tasty bite at some of the best local eateries
Day 6-7 Enjoy the soothing hot spring baths of Arayu, and catch a stunning view from Tateiwa observation deck.

Food and Drink Scene of Shinonsen

Must-Try Dishes

1. Kani Monaka

Kani monaka is a kind of sweeter cake made by wrapping sweet bean paste in a thin layer of fried dough with the shape of a crab.

2. Shirozake

Shirozake is another traditional sweet and tangy rice wine from Shinonsen commonly served during festivals.

Local Beverages

1. Tajima-San-In Yume-dokei Beer

Tajima-San-In Yume-dokei Beer is a locally brewed beer appreciated regionally for its unique and distinct taste.

2. Taisetsu Sake

Taisetsu Sake is another locally produced drink that has a distinct, smooth, and slightly sweet taste.

Where to Stay in Shinonsen: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Shinonsen has all kinds of accommodation options that cater to different budgets and preferences. Here are some of the best-guaranteed options:

1. Arayu

Arayu is ideal for those looking for traditional accommodation. This public bathhouse also provides rooms offering a very traditional feel.

2. Oohashi Ryokan

Located near the Mochi Road, this Ryokan offers budget-friendly accommodation options for guests looking for convenience on their itinerary.

What to Buy in Shinonsen: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

1. Hyonosen Vegetable

Hyonosen vegetables are known for their unique method of cultivation in molded plastic. One can purchase a variety of seasonal vegetables at the local markets for a fair price.

2. Local crafts

Shinonsen is known for its unique pottery, art, and craft. There is no shortage of options for artwork enthusiasts that appreciate buying unique products.

Local Culture and Customs of Shinonsen

Shinonsen values the significance of community and togetherness. It has healthy lifestyle choices, such as regular walking, hiking, and cycling, to help foster strong neighborliness.

Best Time to Visit Shinonsen

The best time to visit Shinonsen is during the months of April to July, and September till December; spring, summer, and fall bring comfortable temperatures and fewer crowds, making it a perfect opportunity to explore the hidden gem of Hyogo.

Do’s and Don’ts of Shinonsen

Do:

  • Remove your shoes before entering traditional Japanese inns, restaurants, and temples.
  • Respect public property.
  • Try to be eco-friendly at all times.

Don’t:

  • Ignore essential cultural customs.
  • Consume food and drink in public or on transportation.
  • Speak loudly or behave offensively in public.

Safety and Health

Safety in Shinonsen

Shinonsen, like most small Japanese towns, experiences little crime. It is, however, essential to maintain caution when traveling alone at night.

Health in Shinonsen

Shinonsen has medical centers and hospitals that cater to the general health needs of its inhabitants and visitors. However, it is always recommended to carry a proper travel insurance policy before setting foot on your adventure trail.

Getting to Shinonsen: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Shinonsen

The nearest major airport to Shinonsen is Kansai International Airport, located about 107 km away. It is a popular international hub that accommodates numerous airlines.

Bus Stations Near Shinonsen

The local train station services Shinonsen. The Ako Line connects the town with Himeji, Okamoto, and other cities in the region.

Other Options

Taxis are easily available, but to fully appreciate the scenic beauty of Shinonsen, it is recommended to rent a car, bike or scooter.

How to Get Around in Shinonsen

The best way to move around Shinonsen is to use local transportation offered by local travel providers.

Where to Go Next from Shinonsen: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

1. Himeji Castle

Himeji Castle is one of Japan’s most impressive castles and a UNESCO World Heritage Site located about 60 km from Shinonsen.

2. Amanohashidate

Amanohashidate is one of Japan’s three scenic views, located about 64 km away, famous for its stunning scenery and outdoor adventures.

Conclusion

Shinonsen is a charming town offering a blend of history, culture, and natural splendor. This comprehensive tourist handbook was designed to provide information on the best places to visit, major cultural events, and local customs to help you make the most of your Shinonsen experience.
