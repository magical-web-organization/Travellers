Introduction to Awaji Island

Awaji Island is a beautiful and serene destination located in Hyogo, in Kansai, in Japan. It is situated between Honshu and Shikoku islands and connected to the Kobe city by the Akashi-Kaikyo Bridge, which is the longest suspension bridge in the world. Awaji Island is renowned for its stunning natural scenery, including picturesque bays, beaches, mountains, and forests. It is also rich in culture, history, and traditions, making it an ideal destination for travelers looking to explore Japan beyond the beaten track.

List of Places to Visit in Awaji Island

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Awaji Castle Ruins

Awaji Castle Ruins are a must-visit for history enthusiasts. This castle was built in the 14th century and played a vital role in the local feudal system for several centuries. Visitors can explore the castle ruins, walk around the moat, and enjoy the panoramic views of the island.

Awaji Yumebutai

Awaji Yumebutai is a multi-purpose complex that houses a conference center, hotel, and botanical garden. The garden has over 1,000 species of plants, including a large collection of rare flowers, and is a perfect place for nature lovers to visit.

Awaji Puppet Theater

Awaji Puppet Theater is one of the most well-known puppet theaters in Japan. Visitors can watch traditional puppet shows, which are unique to the Awaji Island region, and learn about the history of the art of puppetry.

Natural Attractions of Awaji Island: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Sumotoyama Park

Sumotoyama Park is located on the highest peak of Awaji Island, at 407 meters above sea level. Visitors can hike to the top of the mountain, which offers mesmerizing panoramic views of the Seto Inland Sea and the surrounding islands.

Tsunami Memorial Hall

The Tsunami Memorial Hall was built to commemorate the Great Hanshin Earthquake, which struck the region in 1995. The museum showcases the devastating effects of the earthquake and the subsequent tsunami, and serves as a reminder of the importance of disaster preparedness.

Naruto Whirlpools

The Naruto Whirlpools are formed by the collision of tidal currents in the Naruto Strait. Visitors can take a boat tour to see the whirlpools up close and learn about the geography and marine life of the area.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Awaji Island

Awaji Island offers a network of hiking and biking trails that cater to all skill levels. Some popular routes include the Summit Trail, which takes hikers to the top of Mount Eboshidake, and the Coastal Trail, which offers stunning views of the Seto Inland Sea.

Best Things to Do in Awaji Island: Activities and Tours

Awaji Farm Park England Hill

Awaji Farm Park England Hill is a picturesque park that recreates the British countryside. Visitors can enjoy a range of activities such as berry picking, flower viewing, and animal feeding.

Awaji Hanasajiki Garden

Awaji Hanasajiki Garden is known for its beautiful flower fields that bloom all year round. Visitors can stroll around the gardens, take a guided tour, and participate in flower arranging workshops.

Awaji World Park Onokoro

Awaji World Park Onokoro is a theme park that features replicas of famous monuments from around the world, including the Eiffel Tower, the Great Wall of China, and the Pyramids of Egypt. Visitors can enjoy rides, shows, and games, making it an ideal destination for families.

Food and Drink Scene of Awaji Island

Must-Try Dishes

Awaji Beef

Awaji Island is renowned for its high-quality beef, which is known for its excellent marbling and tenderness. Be sure to try the Awaji Beef steak or yakiniku during your visit.

Kurozu (Black Vinegar)

Kurozu is a type of vinegar made from fermented rice that has been aged for several years. It is rich in antioxidants, and its tangy flavor makes it an essential ingredient in many local dishes.

Local Beverages

Namban Sake

Namban Sake is a unique type of sake that is brewed using black malted rice, which gives it a dark color and a rich flavor. It is known for its smooth texture and pairs well with the local cuisine.

Awaji Island Beer

Awaji Island has several microbreweries that produce a range of craft beers. Visitors can enjoy local beers at the breweries or at one of the many bars and restaurants on the island.

What to Buy in Awaji Island: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Awajishima Crafts

Awajishima Crafts are handmade pottery and ceramics that are unique to Awaji Island. Visitors can buy beautiful vases, bowls, and plates, all of which reflect the island’s natural beauty.

Awajishima Onions

Awajishima onions are sweet and fragrant onions that are grown on the island. Visitors can buy them fresh or in the form of onion-flavored snacks, such as onion crackers and onion rings.

Local Culture and Customs of Awaji Island

Awaji Island has a rich cultural heritage, with several festivals and traditions that are unique to the island. Some popular festivals include the Awaji Island Onion Festival and the Festival of the Sea, which celebrates the island’s maritime history.

Best Time to Visit Awaji Island

The best time to visit Awaji Island is from April to June and from September to November when the weather is mild, and the island is at its most beautiful. The island can be crowded during the summer months, especially in August, when the locals celebrate the Obon Festival.

Do’s and Don’ts of Awaji Island

Do:

  • Respect the local customs and traditions of the island.
  • Take off your shoes when entering traditional Japanese-style buildings.
  • Try the local cuisine and beverages.

Don’t:

  • Ignore the rules and regulations of the national parks and hiking trails.
  • Leave your trash behind. The island prides itself on being eco-friendly.

Safety and Health

Safety in Awaji Island

Awaji Island is a safe destination, but visitors should take necessary precautions, such as staying hydrated during hikes and bike rides, and following traffic laws when renting scooters or bicycles.

Health in Awaji Island

Visitors should carry their necessary medication and follow basic hygiene practices, such as washing hands regularly, to prevent the spread of illnesses.

Getting to Awaji Island: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Awaji Island

The nearest international airports to Awaji Island are Kansai International Airport and Kobe Airport. Both airports offer regular shuttle services to the island.

Bus Stations Near Awaji Island

Visitors can take a bus to Awaji Island from Osaka, Kobe, and other cities in the region. The island has several bus stations, including Sumoto Bus Terminal and Awaji Bus Terminal.

Other Options

Visitors can also take a ferry to Awaji Island from Kobe or Naruto.

How to Get Around in Awaji Island

Awaji Island is easy to navigate, with a well-connected network of buses and taxis. Visitors can also rent bicycles or scooters to explore the island at their leisure.

Where to Go Next from Awaji Island: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Awaji Island is an excellent base for exploring the Kansai region. Some nearby destinations include the cities of Kobe and Osaka, as well as the Kii Peninsula, which is home to several UNESCO World Heritage Sites.

Conclusion

Awaji Island is a hidden gem of the Kansai region of Japan, offering visitors a unique blend of natural beauty, culture, and cuisine. Whether you’re interested in hiking, biking, or just relaxing on the beach, there’s something for everyone on this beautiful island.
