Introduction to Nara

Located in the Kansai region of Japan, Nara is a city rich in history and tradition. Known as the cradle of Japanese civilization, it was once the country’s capital from 710 to 794, and is home to some of Japan’s most significant cultural treasures. With its beautiful temples, stunning gardens, and scenic landscapes, Nara is a must-visit destination for anyone interested in Japanese history and culture.

List of Places to Visit in Nara

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Todai-ji Temple and the Great Buddha

One of the most iconic sights in Nara, Todai-ji Temple is a UNESCO World Heritage Site and the largest wooden structure in the world. The temple houses a giant bronze statue of Buddha which is over 50 feet tall and is a must-see for any visitor to Nara.

Nara National Museum

The Nara National Museum is an essential stop for anyone interested in Japanese art and history. Its collection includes both Japanese and Asian art, with a focus on Buddhist art from the Nara period, and features displays of paintings, sculptures, and traditional objects.

Kasuga Taisha Shrine

One of Japan’s most famous shrines, Kasuga Taisha Shrine is known for its thousands of lanterns that light up the surrounding forest. The shrine was established in the 8th century and is a fascinating example of Shinto architecture.

Natural Attractions of Nara: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Nara Park

Located next to Todai-ji Temple, Nara Park is home to over 1,200 wild deer that roam freely throughout the park. Visitors can purchase deer crackers to feed them and take memorable photos with them.

Yoshino-Kumano National Park

Yoshino-Kumano National Park is one of the most beautiful national parks in Japan, with mountains, waterfalls, and forests full of cherry blossoms in the spring. The park offers many outdoor activities such as hiking, camping, and birdwatching.

Mount Wakakusa

Mount Wakakusa, also known as Mount Mikasa, offers stunning views of Nara from its summit. Visitors can hike to the top or participate in a nighttime torch-lit walk, which is held during the winter months.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Nara

Nara is an excellent destination for outdoor enthusiasts, with plenty of hiking and biking trails to explore. Some popular routes include:

  • Yamato Koriyama hiking course
  • Totsukawa mountain bike course
  • Mount Kasuga primeval forest trail

Best Things to Do in Nara: Activities and Tours

Nara Tea Ceremony

The Nara Tea Ceremony is a traditional Japanese cultural experience that offers a glimpse into the art of tea-making. Visitors can participate in a tea ceremony at the Kikugetu-an tea house located within the Isuien Garden.

Try On a Kimono

For a truly authentic cultural experience, visitors can try on a traditional Japanese kimono and experience what it feels like to wear such a beautiful garment. There are many shops in Nara where visitors can rent a kimono for a few hours or a whole day.

Explore Nara’s Nightlife

Nara has a bustling nightlife scene, with plenty of bars and clubs catering to both locals and tourists. Some popular spots include Bar Capri, Club Garam, and Irish Pub Doolin’s.

Food and Drink Scene of Nara

Must-Try Dishes

Kakinoha Zushi

Kakinoha Zushi is a traditional sushi dish that uses persimmon leaves to wrap the sushi rice and fish, giving the dish a sweet, earthy flavor.

Somen

Somen is a type of thin noodle made from wheat flour that is popular during the summer months. It is served cold and usually dipped into a soy sauce-based sauce.

Kaki no Ha Sake

Kaki no Ha Sake is a sake that is brewed with persimmon leaves, giving it a unique, slightly sweet flavor.

Local Beverages

Nara Beer

Nara has a thriving craft beer scene, with many breweries producing high-quality, delicious beers. Some popular local breweries include Akutagawa Brewery, Harushika Brewery, and Fujinami Brewery.

Sake

Sake is a traditional Japanese alcoholic beverage made from fermented rice. In Nara, many breweries produce outstanding sake, including the premium brand Dewazuru.

Where to Stay in Nara: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Nara has plenty of accommodation options for visitors, including:

  • Nara Hotel, a historic hotel with a stunning garden
  • Kotonoyado Musashino, a stylish boutique hotel located in the heart of the city
  • Guesthouse Naramachi, a budget-friendly option located in a traditional Japanese-style building

What to Buy in Nara: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Deer-related Souvenirs

As Nara is famous for its wild deer, there are plenty of deer-related souvenirs available, including deer keychains, deer plush toys, and deer-shaped cookies.

Sake

Sake is a popular souvenir to take home from Nara, with many sake breweries offering tours and tastings.

Local Culture and Customs of Nara

Visitors to Nara should be aware of the following cultural customs:

  • Visitors should bow slightly when greeting someone, preferably with their hands clasped in front of them.
  • Shoes should always be removed before entering someone’s house or a temple.
  • It is considered polite to say “itadakimasu” before eating and “gochisou sama deshita” after finishing a meal.

Best Time to Visit Nara

The best time to visit Nara is in the spring or autumn. In the spring, visitors can enjoy the stunning cherry blossoms, and in the fall, the autumn foliage is equally beautiful.

Do’s and Don’ts of Nara

Do:

  • Do try a traditional Japanese tea ceremony.
  • Do be respectful of the local customs and traditions.
  • Do feed the wild deer in Nara Park, but do it safely and responsibly.

Don’t:

  • Don’t litter or leave trash in public places.
  • Don’t touch or harass the wild deer, as they are a protected species.
  • Don’t be loud or disruptive in public places.

Safety and Health

Safety in Nara

Nara is a relatively safe city, with low levels of crime. Visitors should be aware of their surroundings and take standard safety precautions, such as avoiding unlit areas at night.

Health in Nara

Nara has a high standard of healthcare, with several hospitals and medical clinics located throughout the city.

Getting to Nara: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Nara

The nearest international airport to Nara is Kansai International Airport, which is located approximately 70 km west of Nara.

Bus Stations Near Nara

Nara has two main bus stations: Nara Kotsu Bus Center, which serves local and regional destinations, and JR Nara Bus Terminal, which offers bus routes to other parts of Japan.

Other Options

Nara is also accessible by train, with several train lines serving the city. The closest train station to Nara is Kintetsu Nara Station, which is located in the city center.

How to Get Around in Nara

Nara is a compact city that is easy to navigate on foot or by bicycle. Bicycles can be rented from several locations throughout the city and are a convenient and fun way to get around.

Where to Go Next from Nara: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Kyoto

Kyoto is a famous tourist destination, located approximately 45 minutes away by train from Nara. It is famous for its stunning temples and shrines, traditional ryokan accommodation, and fantastic food scene.

Osaka

Osaka is located approximately 1 hour away by train from Nara and is a bustling city with a vibrant nightlife and food scene. Visitors can enjoy delicious street food, explore the famous Osaka Castle, and shop at the many trendy boutiques and department stores.

Conclusion

Nara is a fascinating city with a rich history and culture, stunning natural landscapes, and delicious food and drink experiences. Whether you’re interested in learning about Japanese history, exploring the great outdoors, or trying local delicacies, Nara has something for everyone. Start planning your trip today, and discover all that this incredible city has to offer.
