Introduction to Nara (Prefecture)

Located in the Kansai region, Nara (prefecture) is one of Japan’s historic regions that played a significant role in the development of the country. With ancient temples, beautiful gardens, and natural wonders, Nara offers visitors a glimpse of Japanese history and culture. While a visit to Nara can be a bit overwhelming, our comprehensive travel guide will help you plan your trip and make the most out of your visit to this beautiful region.

List of Places to Visit in Nara (Prefecture)

If you’re planning a trip to Nara, make sure to visit these must-see attractions:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Todai-ji Temple
  • Nara National Museum
  • Kasuga-taisha Shrine
  • Naramachi
  • Horyu-ji Temple

Natural Attractions of Nara (Prefecture): Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Yoshino-Kumano National Park
  • Mount Yoshino

  • Mount Kasuga Primeval Forest
  • Nara Park

Best Things to Do in Nara (Prefecture): Activities and Tours

There are plenty of things to do and explore in Nara. Here are some of the best:

  • Visit Nara’s traditional festivals
  • Participate in tea ceremonies and pottery making
  • Try your hand at dressing up in a kimono or yukata
  • Go on a guided tour around the famous temples of Nara
  • Enjoy traditional Japanese arts and performances at the Nara Arts Festival in October

Local Culture and Customs of Nara (Prefecture)

While traveling to Nara, it’s essential to familiarize yourself with the local culture and customs of the region:

  • Shoes must always be removed before stepping on tatami flooring.
  • Bowing is a common way of showing respect.
  • Simply tipping is not customary in Japan.
  • Bringing chocolates and souvenirs is a common way of showing appreciation and respect.

Best Time to Visit Nara (Prefecture)

The best time to visit Nara is in the fall months of September to November, when the weather is mild, and the autumn colors are at their peak. Winters are also a popular time to visit Nara, albeit chilly. Spring is another popular time for cherry blossom viewing and festival attendance.

Do’s and Don’ts of Nara (Prefecture)

Here are some essential do’s and don’ts to keep in mind when visiting Nara:

Do:

  • Respect the local customs and traditions.
  • Bow when greeting and thanking people.
  • Try the local food and drinks.
  • Take off your shoes when entering traditional Japanese establishments.
  • Follow cleanup policies by disposing of trash properly.

Don’t:

  • Touch or feed the deer, especially during the mating season.
  • Enter restricted areas unless accompanied by an official guide.
  • Smoke in public areas unless designated smoking areas.
  • Shout, make loud noises or play loud music in public areas.

Safety and Health

While Nara is a reasonably safe destination, it’s essential to take care of your health and safety seriously.

Safety in Nara (Prefecture)

  • Keep a copy of your passport and other identification documents with you at all times.
  • Take extra care when walking around at night.
  • Keep valuables secure at all times.
  • Always follow traffic laws and signals.

Health in Nara (Prefecture)

  • Make sure to drink plenty of water and stay hydrated at all times.
  • Have a basic grasp of first aid and know how to call emergency services if needed.
