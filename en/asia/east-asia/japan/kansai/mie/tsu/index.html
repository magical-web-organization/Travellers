Introduction to Tsu

Located in the Mie prefecture of Kansai, Tsu is a charming city in Japan that offers an exciting blend of traditional and modern Japanese culture. With a population of over 280,000 people, Tsu is the capital of Mie and home to several historical landmarks, great food choices, natural wonders, and activities.

This guide will help you plan your trip to Tsu by giving you an idea of the best things to do in the city, where to stay, what to eat, and what to buy. It will also highlight the important cultural practices and etiquette in Tsu, as well as essential information like transportation options and safety concerns.

List of Places to Visit in Tsu

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Tsu Castle: A splendid historical site and one of the grandest castles of Mie Prefecture. The castle was built in the early 15th century and offers stunning views of the city from the top.
  • Mie Prefecture Museum: Located in Tsu city, the Mie Prefecture Museum is a museum of natural history and cultural heritage. There are three floors of exhibits showcasing the art, history, and natural world of the region.
  • Terashima Kurando Samurai House: This traditional Japanese house belonged to the Terashima family, who were samurai warriors in the Edo period (1600-1868). The well-preserved house features tatami-matted rooms, shoji doors, and Japanese gardens.

Natural Attractions of Tsu: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Ichishi Reservoir: Nestled in the mountains of Tsu, the Ichishi Reservoir is a popular spot for fishing and boating. The reservoir also offers stunning views of the surrounding forests and mountains.
  • Sakakibara Hot Spring: Located in the city’s outskirts, the Sakakibara Onsen is one of the most famous hot springs in Japan. The water is rich in minerals and known for its therapeutic properties.
  • Kusu Castle: A small castle perched on top of a hill overlooking the city. It offers breathtaking views and a relaxing atmosphere.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Tsu

  • Iseji Route: A 170 km hiking route that connects Meotoiwa, the famous married rocks, to Kumano Sanzan. It is a part of the Kumano Kodo pilgrimage route and offers stunning views of the countryside and the coast.
  • Amakawa Valley: A beautiful hiking trail that meanders along a river with crystal-clear water. It is a great spot to witness the changing leaves in autumn and has several waterfalls along the way.
  • Oodo River Cycling Path: A popular cycling route that stretches for 17 km along the Oodo River. The path is flat, easy, and offers beautiful views of the river and the city.

Best Things to Do in Tsu: Activities and Tours

  • Experience Samurai Culture: Visitors can take part in a samurai experience at the Terashima Kurando Samurai House, where they can dress up in Japanese armor, learn about sword fighting techniques, and pose for pictures.
  • Enjoy Japanese Sweets: Tsu is home to several traditional Japanese sweets shops, where visitors can taste delicious treats like daifuku, mochi, and manju.
  • Watch the Tsu Festival: Tsu Matsuri is one of the most famous festivals in Mie Prefecture, held every autumn. The festival features floats decorated with traditional lanterns, performances by local groups, and a stunning fireworks display.

Food and Drink Scene of Tsu

Must-Try Dishes

  • Ise Lobster: A special type of rock lobster that is caught off the coast of Mie Prefecture. It is boiled and served with soy sauce, ginger, and wasabi.
  • Tekonezushi: A local specialty of Tsu, Tekonezushi is a kind of sushi that consists of fresh tuna sashimi, rice, and wasabi. The tuna is marinated in soy sauce and is renowned for its rich flavor.
  • Tsu Kappa Maki: A type of sushi roll that is wrapped in cucumber instead of seaweed. It is a refreshing and light dish that is perfect for summer.

Local Beverages

  • Ise-Shima Craft Beer: A local beer that is brewed in the Ise-Shima region of Mie Prefecture. The beer is made with local ingredients and is known for its crisp, clean taste.
  • Matsusaka Wine: A red wine that is made from grapes grown in Japan. Matsusaka Wine is a favorite of locals and is available in several wineries in the region.

Where to Stay in Tsu: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Tsu Miyako Hotel: A hotel located in the heart of the city, offering comfortable rooms and a great view of the city.
  • Toyota Hotel Okiekansai: A hotel located in the suburbs of Tsu, great for those looking for a quiet and peaceful stay.
  • Ryokan Takemine: A traditional Japanese inn that offers a comfortable stay in a serene environment. The ryokan has hot spring baths, Japanese-style rooms, and a delicious breakfast.

What to Buy in Tsu: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Ise Tea: A type of green tea that is grown in Mie Prefecture. The tea has a refreshing taste and is perfect for green tea lovers.
  • Ise Udon: A type of udon noodle that is made from local wheat and has a chewy texture. It is a popular souvenir among visitors.
  • Ise Katagami: A traditional craft of Mie Prefecture, Ise Katagami is a stencil paper used for dying textiles. It is perfect for those interested in traditional Japanese crafts.

Local Culture and Customs of Tsu

  • Respect: Respect is a fundamental aspect of Japanese culture. Visitors should always show respect to the local people, their customs, and traditions.
  • Etiquette: In Japan, it is customary to take off shoes when entering a home, a temple, or a shrine. Visitors should also bow when meeting someone and avoid loud conversations in public places.
  • Language: Japanese is the official language of Japan. Although many people in urban areas speak English, visitors should learn some basic Japanese phrases to communicate with locals.

Best Time to Visit Tsu

The best time to visit Tsu is during spring (March to May) or autumn (September to November). During these seasons, the weather is mild, and the city is in full bloom with cherry blossoms or vibrant fall foliage. Summers can be hot and humid, while winters are cold and occasionally snowy.

Do’s and Don’ts of Tsu

Do:

  • Observe local customs and practices.
  • Take off your shoes when entering someone’s house or a temple.
  • Try local food and drinks.
  • Learn some basic Japanese phrases.

Don’t:

  • Speak loudly in public places.
  • Wear shoes inside a home or shrine.
  • Tip in restaurants or taxis.
  • Smoke in public places.

Safety and Health

Safety in Tsu

Japan is known for being one of the safest countries in the world, and Tsu is no exception. The city is relatively crime-free, and visitors can feel safe walking around at any time of day or night. However, visitors should still be aware of their surroundings, especially in crowded areas.

Health in Tsu

Mie Prefecture has a good medical system, and visitors can get medical assistance if needed. Japan is also known for its cleanliness, and visitors should take care to maintain good hygiene to avoid getting sick.

Getting to Tsu: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Tsu

  • Chubu Centrair International Airport: The nearest international airport to Tsu, located about 70 km away. Visitors can take a bus, train, or taxi to get to Tsu.

Bus Stations Near Tsu

  • Tsu Station: The main bus station in the city, located in the city center. Visitors can take a bus from the station to other parts of the city.

Other Options

  • Train: Visitors can take a train from Tokyo or Osaka to Tsu. The journey takes about 3 hours from both cities.
  • Car Rental: Visitors can rent a car and drive to Tsu from nearby cities. There are several car rental companies in Japan, and visitors should have a valid driver’s license and an international driving permit.

How to Get Around in Tsu

Visitors can use public transportation to get around Tsu. The city has a well-developed bus system that covers all major tourist destinations. Visitors can also rent a bicycle to explore the city. Taxis are also available, but they can be expensive.

Where to Go Next from Tsu: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Ise Shrine: A famous Shinto shrine located in Ise city, about 30 km away from Tsu. The shrine is dedicated to the sun goddess Amaterasu and is one of the most important shrines in Japan.
  • Nagoya: The fourth-largest city in Japan, located about 60 km away from Tsu. Nagoya is known for its castle, museums, and shopping districts.
  • Kumano Kodo: A series of ancient pilgrimage trails in Wakayama Prefecture, located about 80 km away from Tsu. The trails lead to three major shrines in the Kumano region and are a UNESCO World Heritage Site.

Conclusion

Tsu is a beautiful city situated in the heart of Japan, offering visitors an opportunity to immerse themselves in traditional Japanese culture. With a variety of cultural landmarks, outdoor adventures, and delicious local cuisine, Tsu is a great destination for anyone interested in exploring Japan beyond the typical tourist destinations.
