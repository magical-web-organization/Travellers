Introduction to Kumano

Kumano is a city located in the Mie prefecture of Japan, known for its rich cultural and spiritual heritage, breathtaking scenery, and outdoor adventures. Located in the southern part of the Kii Peninsula, Kumano is blessed with vast natural expanses ranging from rocky coasts and dense forests to glistening waterfalls and hot springs. Kumano is also renowned for its Shinto-Buddhist pilgrimage routes and UNESCO-listed sacred sites that have attracted travelers from all over the world.

List of Places to Visit in Kumano

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Kumano Hongu Taisha: One of the three holiest shrines in Kumano, Kumano Hongu Taisha is the spiritual heart of the city, with a rich history dating back to the 12th century. The structure itself is awe-inspiring and features a massive torii gate and gorgeous gardens.

  • Kumano Hayatama Taisha: Another of the three holiest shrines in Kumano, Kumano Hayatama Taisha is situated on the banks of the Kumano River and offers stunning Shinto-Buddhist architecture and a tranquil retreat from the bustling city.

  • Kumano Kodo Pilgrimage Routes: The Kumano Kodo Pilgrimage Routes is a series of trails that have been used for centuries by pilgrims as a means of seeking spiritual enlightenment. The pilgrimage routes are an exceptional way to reconnect with nature while also exploring the city’s history and culture.

  • Seigantoji Temple: Situated beside Nachi Falls, Seigantoji Temple is a must-visit destination for those who appreciate Japan’s traditional architecture and spiritual sites.

  • Nachi Falls: Cascading down from a height of over 400 feet, Nachi Falls is one of the tallest waterfalls in Japan, offering spectacular views and peaceful surroundings.

  • Shima Spain Village: This Spanish-themed amusement park offers fascinating Spanish-replica buildings, attractions, and exhibitions, making it a great destination for families and culture enthusiasts alike.

Natural Attractions of Kumano: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Kumano Sea Kayaking: Venture out to the Pacific coast for a unique perspective of Kumano by sea. This activity offers a chance to witness the city’s coastline, wildlife, and rock formations illuminated by the peaceful evening light, which is unforgettable.

  • Kumano River Rafting: Explore the scenic views and gentle rapids of the Kumano River by inflatable raft or traditional wooden boats, with a knowledgeable local guide who offers guests a unique perspective on the city’s history and geology.

  • Yunomine Onsen: Visit Kumano’s most popular hot springs and treat yourself to a hot soak, surrounded by natural scenery and traditional Shinto-Buddhist architecture.

  • Yoshino Kumano National Park: This national park stretches over 1,400 acres and offers a range of wildlife, iconic landscapes, and outdoor activities such as hiking, fishing, and kayaking throughout the seasons.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Kumano

  • Nakahechi Route: The Nakahechi Route, the most popular hiking route and the best for first-timers. This trail typically takes three to five days to finish and leads adventurers to various shrines and natural wonders across Kumano.

  • Kohechi Route: This route is the spiritually inclined advanced hikers’ preferred trail. The Kohechi Route links Koyasan and Kumano Hongu Taisha, with a total length of 62.1 miles, and can take up to ten days to complete.

  • Ise-ji Route: It is a series of trails linking various cultural and spiritual sites, including the famed Grand Shrine of Ise. It offers a great opportunity for those who love hiking at their own leisurely pace as well.

Best Things to Do in Kumano

  • Participate in a Traditional Tea Ceremony: Experience a Japanese tea ceremony in a tranquil Zen garden, typically held by the spiritual guides of the Shinto-Buddhist traditions.

  • Enjoy Kumano’s Local Festivals: Throughout the year, Kumano hosts numerous festivals to highlight its spiritual practices and rich history. The Nachi-no-otaki Fire Festival, the Beloved Bridges Festival, and the Hayatama Taisha Festival are some of the most popular festivals.

  • Tour Kumano’s Sake Breweries: Sake brewing in Kumano dates back to ancient times, and visitors can experience it via a guided tour which demonstrates the art and history behind Kumano’s sake production.

  • Take a Photography Tour: Kumano’s natural beauty is perfect for photographers. Visitors can engage in a personalized tour of the city with professionals who offer insights on the best locations for great shots and guides on photography techniques.

Week-Long Itinerary for Kumano

Day 1: Visit Kumano Hayatama Taisha and Kumano Hongu Taisha Day 2: Explore Kumano’s hiking trails and enjoy an onsen at Yunomine Day 3: Head to the Pacific Ocean for a Sea Kayaking adventure Day 4: Visit museums and historical sites in Kumano, including the Seigantoji Temple and Nachi Falls Day 5: Enjoy another day of hiking or venture out on the Kohechi Route Trail. Day 6: Take it easy and enjoy Kumano’s beaches, markets or go to a local festival. Day 7: Shop for souvenirs at Kumano’s markets or for locally produced sake

Food and Drink Scene of Kumano

Must-Try Dishes

  • Tekone-zushi: This dish is made up of sushi rice, sashimi-style bonito, and vegetables.

  • Ise-ebi: This seafood delicacy comprises fresh lobster served either grilled or boiled

  • Akafuku: This mochi dessert features a soft, sweet Mochi covered in smooth red bean paste.

Local Beverages

  • Sake: Kumano is known for producing excellent sake, made with the best water in Japan.

  • Green Tea: This drink is produced from locally-grown tea leaves and can be found in many restaurants and markets throughout the city.

Where to Stay in Kumano: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Hotel Urashima: This hotel offers stunning views of the Pacific Ocean and unlimited access to the natural hot springs near the beach.

  • Kodo-no-Yado Hotel TAKI: This traditional accommodation offers authentic Japanese architecture, delicious meals, and an onsen.

  • Kawayu-onsen Fujiya: Located close to Yunomine, this onsen offers guests relaxation and meditation, making it an ideal retreat for those who are looking to spend some alone time.

What to Buy in Kumano: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Kumano’s famous sake and tea make great souvenirs for both yourself and your loved ones.

  • Kumano’s ornately crafted and beautiful chopsticks, wooden spoons, and bowls are very common and make memorable souvenirs.

Local Culture and Customs of Kumano

  • Visitors are advised to learn and respect Japanese customs, such as taking off shoes before entering temples or shrines and bowing during prayers or greetings.

  • Taking photographs at sensitive locations or in temples and shrines is inappropriate and disrespectful.

Best Time to Visit Kumano

Spring and Fall seasons are the best times to visit Kumano due to clear and mild weather. The height of the tourist season is late April and early May, so expect crowds during that time. Late September and early October are also excellent alternatives.

Do’s and Don’ts of Kumano

Do:

  • Wear comfortable hiking or walking shoes due to Kumano’s rugged terrain.

  • Learn a few Japanese phrases for simple communication.

Don’t:

  • Carry a portfolio of things to see and do in Kumano, take it slow, and enjoy the city’s natural beauty and spirituality.

  • Visit Kumano without learning and respecting Japan’s cultural history and customs.

Safety and Health

Safety in Kumano

Kumano is a very safe city. However, tourists are advised to be cautious and vigilant about their belongings and surroundings, especially in crowded areas.

Health in Kumano

Kumano is a safe destination with excellent medical facilities. Please have basic health insurance and carry your prescription with you at all times.

Getting to Kumano: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Kumano

  • Kansai-Hiroshima International Airport.

  • Nagoya-Chubu makes for an excellent alternative for international visitors who are considering Kumano as part of a larger Japan itinerary.

Bus Stations Near Kumano

  • Kumano is well-connected to other parts of Japan by railway and bus service.

Other Options

  • Trains are the most convenient and comfortable way to reach Kumano from other parts of the country. It usually takes 2 to 3 hours to reach Kumano from Osaka, Kyoto, and Nagoya.

How to Get Around in Kumano

Kumano is a relatively small city, so public buses, taxis, or rental cars are the most common modes of transportation within the city.

Where to Go Next from Kumano: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Shirahama, located about an hour away from Kumano, is a coastal city known for its beaches and hot springs.

  • Wakayama city, the capital of the prefecture, offers a fascinating blend of history, culture, and natural beauty.

  • Osaka, Kyoto, and Nara are popular destinations connecting with Kumano due to their proximity and as iconic Japanese cities.

Conclusion

Kumano is a city that takes pride in its heritage, spirituality, and natural beauty. It is a great destination for those who love hiking, outdoor adventures, and history. Plan to stay for a few days to get lost in the city’s magic, rejuvenating and revitalizing yourself with its vibrant culture, food, and fresh air.
