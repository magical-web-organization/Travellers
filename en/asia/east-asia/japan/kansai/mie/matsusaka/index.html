Introduction to Matsusaka

Matsusaka, sometimes known as the “Wagyu Capital of Japan,” is renowned for its premium quality beef, which is considered one of the best in the world. But it’s not just its beef that makes Matsusaka special – the city is also steeped in history and culture, with many museums and historical sites to explore. Nature lovers will be in awe of the city’s scenic beauty, with hiking trails and other outdoor adventures waiting to be discovered. Matsusaka is also home to many temples and shrines, providing visitors with a glimpse into the city’s rich cultural heritage. With a population of around 170,000, the city is small enough to explore on foot, making it an ideal destination for those who want an authentic Japanese experience off the beaten path.

Local Culture and Customs of Matsusaka

Matsusaka is a city steeped in tradition, and visitors are encouraged to respect local customs and cultural practices. Visitors should be aware of the following:

  • When entering a temple or shrine, be sure to remove your shoes.
  • When dining out, it is customary to say “itadakimasu” before eating and “gochisosama deshita” after finishing your meal.
  • Be respectful of others’ personal space on public transport and in crowded areas.
  • It is considered rude to blow your nose in public.

Best Time to Visit Matsusaka

Matsusaka can be visited year-round, but the best time to go is in the spring (March to May) and autumn (September to November), when the temperatures are mild, and the foliage is at its most beautiful. Summers can be hot and humid, while winters can be cold but offer a chance to see the snow-capped mountains in the distance.

Do’s and Don’ts of Matsusaka

Do:

  • Try Matsusaka beef at a local restaurant.
  • Explore the city’s historical sites and museums.
  • Respect local customs and culture.

Don’t:

  • Be loud or boisterous in public places.
  • Wear shoes inside a shrine or temple.
  • Take photographs without permission in private areas.

Safety and Health

Safety in Matsusaka

Matsusaka is a safe city to visit, with low levels of crime. Visitors should still take standard safety precautions, such as keeping an eye on their belongings and being cautious in crowded areas.

Health in Matsusaka

The city has modern medical facilities, and visitors can access healthcare services if necessary. Tap water is safe to drink in Matsusaka, but it is recommended that visitors carry a water bottle with them and stay hydrated throughout the day.

Getting to Matsusaka: Transportation Options and Logistics

Matsusaka can be reached by train or bus from most major cities in Japan. The nearest airport is Chubu Centrair International Airport in Nagoya, which is approximately 100km away from Matsusaka. It is recommended that visitors take the train from Nagoya to Matsusaka, which takes approximately two hours.

How to Get Around in Matsusaka

Matsusaka is a small city, and most of the main attractions are located within walking distance of each other. Visitors can also use public transportation, such as buses and trains, to get around the city.
