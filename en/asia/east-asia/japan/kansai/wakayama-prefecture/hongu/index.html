Hongu, located in Wakayama prefecture in Kansai, Japan, is a hidden gem with a rich cultural and natural heritage. This small town, nestled in the heart of the Kumano Kodo pilgrimage routes, offers visitors a unique opportunity to experience traditional Japanese culture, sample local delicacies, and explore lush green forests and mountains. In this comprehensive guide, we’ll take you on a tour of Hongu’s top attractions, accommodations, and must-try dishes.

Introduction to Hongu

Hongu is a small town located in the southeastern part of Wakayama prefecture. The town is famous for being the center of the Kumano Kodo pilgrimage routes, which has been a UNESCO World Heritage Site since 2004. The Kumano Kodo is a network of ancient pilgrimage routes that have been traveled by Japanese emperors, samurais, and commoners for over a thousand years. The area offers visitors a chance to experience traditional Japanese culture, sample delicious local foods, and explore the stunning natural scenery.

List of Places to Visit in Hongu

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

One of the main attractions of Hongu is the Kumano Hongu Taisha, the head shrine of the Kumano Kodo pilgrimage routes. The shrine has been a place of spiritual significance for over a thousand years and is dedicated to the deities of Kumano, which include over 3,000 gods and goddesses. Visitors can also explore the nearby Kumano Hayatama Taisha, a UNESCO World Heritage Site known for its beautiful torii gate, shrines, and sacred rocks.

Another must-see attraction in Hongu is the Hosshinmon-Oji Shrine. This shrine is located at the halfway point of the Kumano Kodo pilgrimage routes and is famous for its stunning mountain views and unique architecture.

Visitors interested in history will enjoy the Hongu History Museum, which showcases the town’s rich cultural and natural heritage. The museum contains exhibits on the Kumano Kodo pilgrimage routes, the area’s flora and fauna, and the historic town of Hongu.

Natural Attractions of Hongu: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Hongu is famous for its beautiful natural scenery, which includes lush green forests, rivers, waterfalls, and mountains. One of the most popular attractions is the Nachi Falls, which is the tallest waterfall in Japan and is located near the Kumano Nachi Taisha Shrine. Visitors can also hike to the nearby Daimon-zaka, a picturesque cobblestone stairway that leads up to the Kumano Nachi Taisha Shrine.

Another popular destination for nature lovers is the Yunomine Onsen, a natural hot spring that has been used for over 1,800 years. The area features a variety of hot springs, including indoor and outdoor baths, which are believed to have therapeutic properties that help heal various ailments.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Hongu

Hiking and biking are popular activities in Hongu, with the town offering numerous routes that cater to different fitness levels and preferences. The Kumano Kodo pilgrimage routes are a popular choice, with trails ranging from leisurely walks to challenging hikes that pass through forests, temples, and shrines.

For those looking for a more leisurely activity, the town also offers cycling tours that showcase the local culture, food, and scenery. These tours are available year-round and offer visitors a chance to explore the town at a relaxed pace.

Best Things to Do in Hongu: Activities and Tours

Apart from hiking and biking, Hongu offers visitors a variety of cultural and outdoor activities. One popular activity is attending the Kumano Hongu Taisha’s daily rituals, which include prayers, dances, and cleansing ceremonies.

For those interested in Japanese arts and crafts, the town has several workshops that offer classes in traditional crafts such as calligraphy, pottery, and dyeing.

Another popular activity is taking a cultural tour of the town, which includes visiting historical sites, tasting local delicacies, and meeting locals. These tours offer visitors a chance to learn about the town’s rich history and culture in a fun and engaging way.

Food and Drink Scene of Hongu

Must-Try Dishes

Hongu is famous for its local delicacies, which include freshwater fish, pickled vegetables, and soba noodles. One must-try dish is the Kumano Sanzan Soba, which is buckwheat noodles served in a flavorful broth made from fresh mountain vegetables and locally farmed chicken.

Another popular dish is the Kawayuka fish, which is caught fresh from the nearby rivers and grilled to perfection. Visitors can also sample the local specialty of pickled vegetables, which are fermented in barrels using traditional methods.

Local Beverages

Hongu’s local beverage is sake, a Japanese rice wine that is brewed using locally grown rice and water. Visitors can try several varieties of sake at the local breweries, which offer tours and tastings.

Where to Stay in Hongu: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Hongu offers a range of accommodations to suit different budgets and preferences. For those looking for budget-friendly options, guesthouses and hostels are available that offer comfortable rooms and communal spaces perfect for socializing with other travelers.

Those seeking more luxurious accommodations can stay in one of the town’s ryokans, traditional Japanese inns that offer a unique experience of sleeping on futons on tatami mats, enjoying traditional meals, and soaking in Japanese-style baths.

What to Buy in Hongu: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

One popular souvenir to bring home from Hongu is Kumano Kodo-themed merchandise, such as T-shirts, mugs, and key chains. Visitors can also purchase locally made crafts such as pottery, calligraphy items, and handmade soba noodles.

Local Culture and Customs of Hongu

Hongu is a town steeped in traditional Japanese culture, with visitors expected to respect local customs and etiquette. Bowing is a common way of greeting others, and visitors should avoid being loud or disruptive in public places.

Best Time to Visit Hongu

The best time to visit Hongu is during the spring and fall seasons when the weather is mild, and the scenery is at its peak. The cherry blossoms in spring and the autumn foliage in fall offer stunning backdrops for hiking and sightseeing.

Do’s and Don’ts of Hongu

Do:

  • Respect local customs and etiquette
  • Try the local cuisine
  • Explore the Kumano Kodo pilgrimage routes
  • Attend the Kumano Hongu Taisha’s daily rituals

Don’t:

  • Be loud or disruptive in public places
  • Wear shoes indoors
  • Take photographs of people without permission

Safety and Health

Safety in Hongu

Hongu is relatively safe, with low crime rates and a friendly local population. Visitors should take standard safety precautions such as keeping valuables locked up and securing accommodation before arriving.

Health in Hongu

Hongu has clean drinking water and basic medical facilities, with larger hospitals located in nearby towns. Visitors with pre-existing medical conditions should carry necessary medication and inform their travel companions of their conditions.

Getting to Hongu: Transportation Options and Logistics

Hongu is located approximately four hours from Osaka by train and bus. Visitors can take the JR Kuroshio Limited Express train from Shin-Osaka Station to Kii-Tanabe Station and then transfer to a local bus that goes directly to Hongu.

Airports Near Hongu

The closest airport to Hongu is Kansai International Airport, which is located approximately three hours away by train and bus.

Bus Stations Near Hongu

Local and express buses to Hongu are available from Kii-Tanabe Station and other nearby cities.

Other Options

Visitors can also hire a rental car or take a private shuttle from Osaka to Hongu. However, it is recommended to research local driving laws and conditions before driving in Japan.

How to Get Around in Hongu

Renting a bicycle or motorbike is a great way to travel around Hongu and explore the town’s natural beauty. Buses and taxis are also available for those who prefer not to drive.

Where to Go Next from Hongu: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Hongu’s proximity to other tourist destinations makes it an excellent base for exploring the wider Wakayama prefecture. Visitors can take a day trip to nearby attractions such as the Kumano Nachi Taisha Shrine, the Shirahama Beach, and the Kii-Katsuura Whale Watching Center.

Conclusion

Hongu is a town that offers visitors a unique opportunity to experience traditional Japanese culture, sample local delicacies, and explore lush green forests and mountains. From hiking and biking to cultural tours and hot springs, there is something for everyone in this hidden gem of Japan. Plan your trip today and experience the magic of Hongu for yourself.
