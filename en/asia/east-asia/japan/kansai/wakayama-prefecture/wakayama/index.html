Introduction to Wakayama

Wakayama is a prefecture located in the Kansai region of Japan, known for its rich cultural history and natural beauty. The prefecture is known for its many temples and shrines, including the famous Kumano Kodo pilgrimage route, which has been designated a UNESCO World Heritage site. Visitors to Wakayama can also enjoy hiking through the beautiful mountains, relaxing in hot springs, and experiencing local festivals and culinary delights.

List of Places to Visit in Wakayama

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Wakayama has a rich cultural history, which can be seen in the many museums and historical attractions throughout the prefecture. Some of the must-visit attractions include:

  • Wakayama Castle: A well-preserved castle that dates back to the Edo period.
  • Kishi Station and Tama Cafe: A small train station made famous by the cat Tama, who served as the station master for many years.
  • Koyasan: A small town with more than 100 temples, including the famous Kongobu-ji Temple.
  • Nachi Falls: A picturesque waterfall that is considered one of the three most beautiful in Japan.
  • Kumano Kodo: A network of ancient pilgrimage trails that lead to three Grand Shrines of Kumano, also known as the “Kumano Sanzan.”

Natural Attractions of Wakayama: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Wakayama is known for its beautiful natural scenery, which includes mountains, forests, beaches, and hot springs. Some of the best places to experience the natural beauty of Wakayama include:

  • Mount Koya: A sacred mountain that is home to many temples and is a popular destination for hiking and meditation.
  • Yoshino-Kumano National Park: A beautiful park that covers the Kii Peninsula and includes rugged mountains and beautiful coastline.
  • Shirahama Beach: A pristine beach with crystal-clear water and white sand that is perfect for swimming and sunbathing.
  • Yunomine Onsen: A hot spring town that is said to have been visited by the famous Buddhist monk Kukai.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Wakayama

Wakayama is a fantastic destination for hiking and biking enthusiasts. Some of the best routes include:

  • Kumano Kodo: A network of ancient pilgrimage trails that lead to the Grand Shrines of Kumano. The routes vary in difficulty, with some suitable for beginners and others reserved for experienced hikers.
  • Mount Koya: There are several hiking trails that lead to the top of this sacred mountain. The routes offer stunning views of the surrounding landscape.
  • Shirahama: This coastal town has a beautiful cycling path that runs along the coastline.
  • Daito Islands: A group of islands off the coast of Wakayama that offer hiking trails and beautiful beaches.

Best Things to Do in Wakayama: Activities and Tours

There are many exciting activities and tours to enjoy in Wakayama, including:

  • Boat tours: Take a boat tour along the Kumano River or around the islands of Wakayama for a unique view of the prefecture.
  • Caving and spelunking: Explore the underground caves and tunnels in Wakayama, including the Kumano Sanzan Nachi Cave.
  • Temple and shrine tours: Visit the many temples and shrines in Wakayama, including the famous Nachi Taisha and Kumano Hayatama Taisha.
  • Food tours: Indulge in Wakayama’s famous cuisine on a food tour that will take you to local markets, restaurants, and food stalls.
  • Coastal adventures: Enjoy water activities such as surfing or paddleboarding on the scenic beaches of Wakayama.

Week-Long Itinerary for Wakayama

Day 1: Visit Wakayama Castle and Marina City for shopping and dining.

Day 2: Explore Koyasan and its many temples.

Day 3: Take a hiking tour of the Kumano Kodo pilgrimage route.

Day 4: Enjoy outdoor activities in Yoshino-Kumano National Park, such as hiking or biking.

Day 5: Relax in a hot spring in Yunomine Onsen.

Day 6: Visit the beautiful Nachi Falls and the nearby Nachi Taisha shrine.

Day 7: Head to Shirahama to relax on the beautiful beach and enjoy water activities such as swimming or surfing.

Food and Drink Scene of Wakayama

Must-Try Dishes

Wakayama is known for its delicious cuisine, which includes:

  • Kujira steak: A dish made from whale meat that is said to have a texture similar to beef.
  • Tai-meshi: A dish made from sea bream and rice.
  • Kishu plum wine: A sweet and sour wine made from local plums.
  • Mikan oranges: A type of mandarin orange that is grown in Wakayama and is known for its sweet and juicy flavor.

Local Beverages

Besides Kishu plum wine, there are other popular local beverages in Wakayama such as:

  • umeshu: A Japanese liqueur made from ume fruits.
  • sake: A traditional Japanese rice wine.

Where to Stay in Wakayama: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

There are many accommodation options available in Wakayama, from luxury hotels to budget-friendly hostels. Some popular choices include:

  • Koyasan Shukubo: Temple lodgings that offer a unique experience for travelers.
  • Wakayama Marriott Hotel: A luxury hotel located in the heart of Wakayama City.
  • Hanayagi no Sho Keizan: A traditional Japanese inn with hot spring baths.
  • Backpackers Hotel Toyo: A popular hostel for backpackers and budget travelers.

What to Buy in Wakayama: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Wakayama is famous for its local products, including:

  • Kishu lacquerware: A traditional craft made from wood and coated in a special lacquer.
  • Kiiroitori: A yellow bird character from the popular Sanrio franchise.
  • Kishu binchotan charcoal: A type of activated charcoal that is made in Wakayama and is used for cooking and purifying water.
  • Wakayama mikan oranges: A juicy and sweet type of mandarin orange.

Local Culture and Customs of Wakayama

Wakayama has a rich cultural heritage and many customs that are still observed today. Some of the things to be aware of when visiting include:

  • Respect for temples and shrines: Visitors should be respectful when visiting temples and shrines and should follow the appropriate customs and dress codes.
  • Tea ceremony: The tea ceremony is an important part of Japanese culture and is often performed in Wakayama.
  • Kumano Kodo pilgrimage: Many locals and visitors undertake the Kumano Kodo pilgrimage, which is a deeply spiritual and traditional experience.

Best Time to Visit Wakayama

The best time to visit Wakayama is during the spring and autumn seasons, when the weather is mild and comfortable. The spring season, from March to May, is ideal for viewing cherry blossoms, while the autumn season, from September to November, is perfect for enjoying the fall foliage.

Do’s and Don’ts of Wakayama

Do:

  • Bow when greeting someone: It is customary to bow when greeting someone in Japan.
  • Remove your shoes: In many temples, shrines, and traditional Japanese inns, visitors are expected to remove their shoes before entering.
  • Carry cash: Many small businesses in Wakayama do not accept credit cards, so it is best to carry cash.

Don’t:

  • Eat while walking: It is considered rude to eat while walking in Japan, so it is best to find a place to sit and enjoy your food.
  • Wear your shoes on tatami mats: Tatami mats are traditional Japanese flooring made of woven straw, and it is customary to remove your shoes before stepping on them.
  • Tip: Tipping is not customary in Japan, so it is not necessary to leave a tip at restaurants or hotels.

Safety and Health

Safety in Wakayama

Wakayama is generally a safe place to visit, with low crime rates and a friendly local population. However, visitors should take the usual precautions when traveling, such as keeping their valuables safe and being aware of their surroundings.

Health in Wakayama

Food and water in Wakayama are generally safe to consume, but visitors should still exercise caution and follow basic hygiene practices, such as washing their hands regularly. It is also important to have travel insurance in case of any medical emergencies.

Getting to Wakayama: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Wakayama

The closest airport to Wakayama is Kansai International Airport, which is located approximately 40 miles from Wakayama City.

Bus Stations Near Wakayama

Wakayama Bus Terminal is the main bus station in the city, with regular connections to other destinations in the prefecture and beyond.

Other Options

Visitors can also reach Wakayama by train, with the Kuroshio Limited Express connecting Wakayama City to Osaka and other major cities in the region.

How to Get Around in Wakayama

The best way to get around Wakayama is by train or bus, with regular services connecting major cities and tourist destinations throughout the prefecture. Taxis and rental cars are also available for those who prefer more flexibility.

Where to Go Next from Wakayama: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Some nearby destinations and excursions from Wakayama include:

  • Osaka: A vibrant city known for its food, shopping, and nightlife, located just a short train ride from Wakayama City.
  • Nara: A historic city with many impressive temples and shrines.
  • Kyoto: A cultural hub and former capital of Japan, located within easy reach of Wakayama by train.

Conclusion

Wakayama offers a unique blend of cultural heritage, natural beauty, and outdoor adventure that is sure to appeal to any traveler. Whether you’re interested in exploring ancient temples, hiking through rugged mountains, or indulging in delicious local cuisine, Wakayama has something to offer. With this comprehensive travel guide, you’re sure to make the most of your visit to this beautiful prefecture in Japan.
