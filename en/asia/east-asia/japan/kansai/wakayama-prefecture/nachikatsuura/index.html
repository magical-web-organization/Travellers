Introduction to Nachikatsuura

Nestled in Wakayama Prefecture of Kansai, Japan, Nachikatsuura is a picturesque fishing town situated on a rugged coastline with the Pacific Ocean. The town is famous for Nachi Falls, a UNESCO world heritage site, and Kumano Kodo pilgrimage routes. Its abundance of natural beauty, rich cultural heritage, and modernized amenities make it a must-visit destination.

List of Places to Visit in Nachikatsuura

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums, and Historical Places

  • Seigantoji Temple: Located near Nachi Falls, is a sacred temple in the Kumano Kodō pilgrimage route built-in 918.
  • Nachi Falls: A UNESCO heritage site and Japan’s tallest waterfall at 133 meters. Visitors in December can witness a unique winter illuminations show.
  • Kumano Nachi Taisha: Nachi Shrine is a Shinto shrine that reveres Nachi Falls and has been a place of worship for the past 1,500 years.
  • Katsuura Fish Market: Enjoy fresh seafood straight from the Japanese ocean in a traditional fish market.

Natural Attractions of Nachikatsuura: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Doro-no-taki Falls: A stunning waterfall also known as “Muddy Waterfalls,” as it can be covered in a mist of water carrying soil from the surrounding slopes.
  • Kushimoto Marine Park: A popular spot for scuba diving enthusiasts with thriving marine life and crystal-clear waters.
  • Nachikatsuura Beach: A sandy beach with azure waters ideal for swimming and sunbathing.
  • Nachi-Katsuura Hot-spring: Relaxing hot-spring located near Seigantoji Temple with panoramic views of Nachi Falls.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Nachikatsuura

  • Kumano Kodo: A network of ancient pilgrimage routes that are world heritage sites that connect Nachikatsuura to three other grand shrines located in the Kumano region.
  • Ogigahama Route: A trail featuring an excellent view of the Shimoda Bay that leads to a secluded beach.

Best Things to Do in Nachikatsuura: Activities and Tours

  • Fishing Excursions: Enjoy fishing in the Pacific Ocean and close-up views of the coastline from the sea.
  • Whale Watching Tours: See the whales travelling north to south during their migratory period.
  • Kumano Kodo Guided Tours: Explore the ancient pilgrimage site with knowledgeable guides.

Week-Long Itinerary for Nachikatsuura

1 Visit Kumano Nachi Taisha and enjoy Nachi Falls

Food and Drink Scene of Nachikatsuura

Must-Try Dishes

  • Kujira Tataki: Seared whale meat with soy sauce and ginger slices.
  • Tai-meshi: A rice bowl with generous portions of fresh sea bream on top.
  • Ise-ebi: A type of lobster native to Kumano Kodo region.

Local Beverages

  • Wakayama Sake: A traditional alcoholic beverage of Japan made using fermentation from locally grown rice.
  • Yuzu Tea: A refreshing tea made from yuzu, a citrus fruit grown in the region.

Where to Stay in Nachikatsuura: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Nachi Katsuura Hotel: A luxurious hotel with hot springs and beautiful sea views.
  • Kumano Kodo Kan Pilgrimage Guesthouse: A budget-friendly guesthouse located on the Kumano Kodo trail.
  • Minshuku Inn: A traditional-style inn that offers immersive cultural experiences.

What to Buy in Nachikatsuura: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Kumano Kodo Pilgrimage Trail Stamp Book: Collect Kumano Kodo pilgrim trail stamps as a unique souvenir.
  • Meharizushi: A special type of sushi stone made from a type of stone found in the region.

Local Culture and Customs of Nachikatsuura

Respect for tradition and nature is deeply embedded in Nachikatsuura’s culture. Visitors should follow local customs such as taking off their shoes before entering a place of worship, and no excessive noise in/on trains, buses, and other public places. Locals are welcoming and hospitable, and a simple bow of thanks is appreciated.

Best Time to Visit Nachikatsuura

The best time to visit Nachikatsuura is during the autumn season(September-November) when the weather is mild, and there is less rainfall. March-April is another good time to visit as the region is coloured pink and white with cherry blossoms. December adds unique charm to Nachikatsuura When it hosts vivid illumination shows for Nachi Falls.

Do’s and Don’ts of Nachikatsuura

Do:

  • Wear comfortable shoes when hiking.
  • Try the local cuisine.
  • Respect nature, culture, and local customs.

Don’t:

  • Be loud in/on public places.
  • Take photographs of the locals without permission.
  • Leave trash or litter anywhere in Nachikatsuura.

Safety and Health

Safety in Nachikatsuura

Nachikatsuura is a safe destination with low crime rates. However, visitors should be mindful of the tides and waves at the beach and follow basic safety guidelines. Be careful while walking or cycling on the hilly terrains and busy streets of the town.

Health in Nachikatsuura

Visitors should ensure good personal hygiene and carry basic medication, including insect repellants for forested areas.

Getting to Nachikatsuura: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Nachikatsuura

The nearest airports to Nachikatsuura are Kansai International Airport and Nanki Shirahama Airport.

Bus Stations Near Nachikatsuura

Kumano Kotsu Bus is a popular bus service that connects Nachikatsuura to Kumano Kodo pilgrimage sites and other nearby towns.

Other Options

Renting a car or taking a taxi is an excellent option for exploring the region at your own pace.

How to Get Around in Nachikatsuura

The best way to explore Nachikatsuura is on foot, bike or by using public transportation like trains and buses. Taxis and rental cars are also available for visitors who want more flexibility.

Where to Go Next from Nachikatsuura: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Wakayama City: A charming city with historical and cultural landmarks such as Wakayama Castle and Kimiidera Temple.
  • Mount Koya: A mystical mountain that’s home to Koyasan temple, a UNESCO world heritage site.
  • Shirahama hot springs resort: A popular resort known for its natural hot springs and beach.

Conclusion

Nachikatsuura is a paradise for nature lovers and cultural enthusiasts with its natural wonders and ancient pilgrim trails. From picturesque waterfalls, seaside scenery to exploring Japanese traditions and local cuisine, Nachikatsuura offers something unique for everyone.
