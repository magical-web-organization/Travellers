Introduction to Ryuo

Ryuo is a quaint town located in the Shiga Prefecture of Japan, which is situated in the famous Kansai region. Known for its natural beauty and ancient cultural sites, the town is a destination that offers a serene escape from the loud hustle and bustle of Japan’s larger cities. The town has a population of just over 17,500 people and offers a glimpse into Japan’s rich cultural heritage.

List of Places to Visit in Ryuo

If you are planning to visit Ryuo, here are the top destinations you should consider:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • The Ryuo Castle Ruins Park: The Ryuo Castle Ruins Park is located on a hill overlooking the town. The park is a famous historical landmark and offers a panoramic view of the town.

  • Pagoda of Ishiyamadera Temple: The Ishiyamadera Temple is one of the most famous and oldest temples in Shiga. The five-story tall pagoda provides excellent views of the surrounding areas.

  • Shigaraki Kogen Roman Art Museum: The museum is famous for its Roman sculptures and artworks.

Natural Attractions of Ryuo: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Mount Ibuki: Mount Ibuki is one of the tallest mountains in Japan, standing at 1,377 meters tall. It is a popular destination for hiking and skiing.

  • Lake Biwa: Lake Biwa is an enormous freshwater lake in Japan and is the country’s largest lake. Visitors can enjoy fishing, swimming, and hiking along the surrounding trails.

  • Makino Highlands: Makino Highlands is known as the Swiss Alps of Japan, thanks to its rolling hills and picturesque views. Travelers can enjoy hiking, birdwatching, and skiing in the winter months.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Ryuo

Ryuo is a great destination for hikers and bikers. Here are some of the most popular routes:

  • Ibukiyama Mount Oyama Hike: The hike is about 8.5 km long and is moderate to difficult. The route leads to the summit of Mount Ibuki, where hikers can enjoy beautiful views of the surrounding countryside.

  • Ishiyamadera Temple and Lake Biwa Trail: This trail is 14 km long and follows a path that begins at the Ishiyamadera Temple and ends on the shores of Lake Biwa.

Best Things to Do in Ryuo: Activities and Tours

  • Hiking and Biking: Ryuo is a beautiful destination for hiking and biking. Take a scenic route, enjoy beautiful views and capture memories to last a lifetime.

  • Art and Culture: Ryuo is home to many cultural and historical landmarks. Art lovers should also check out Shigaraki pottery and attend festivals like the Biwako Hatto Matsuri.

  • Onsen and Spas: Ryuo is known for its hot springs. Relax and unwind at one of the many spas, such as Hisui No Yu, and enjoy a rejuvenating, healing experience.

Food and Drink Scene in Ryuo

Must-Try Dishes

  • Omi Beef: Omi Beef is a type of Wagyu beef that is produced in Shiga. It is known for its high-quality marbling and exceptional taste.

  • Saba Sushi: This is a traditional Japanese dish made with mackerel and sushi rice.

  • Sake: Sake is a traditional Japanese rice wine, and one of the best places to try it is at the sake breweries located in Ryuo.

Local Beverages

  • Shiga Craft Beer: Shiga prefecture now prides itself on local breweries, including Jyaezo, Baeren and Tama no Megumi. You can find the beers at many restaurants and bars around Ryuo.

  • Matcha: Matcha is a famous Japanese green tea that is famous for its strong flavor and health benefits. Also, you can enjoy matcha sweets such as matcha ice cream or matcha candies.

Where to Stay in Ryuo: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Ryuo offers a variety of accommodations, including hotels, hostels, and guest houses.

  • Ryokan Kinmata: This traditional Japanese guesthouse offers private rooms with tatami flooring and futon beds. They have excellent food and offer access to the onsen.

  • Hotel Route-Inn: Hotel Route-Inn Ryuo offers comfortable rooms with modern amenities.

  • Ryuo Youth Hostel: The Ryuo Youth Hostel offers budget-friendly accommodation, ideal for travelers on a budget.

What to Buy in Ryuo: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Shigaraki Pottery: Shigaraki is a type of pottery that is produced in Shiga. It is known for its unique, rustic style and is widely available in local shops.

  • Omi Beef: Preserved beef is a popular souvenir item that you can bring home as a gift.

Local Culture and Customs of Ryuo

In Ryuo, as in the rest of Japan, it is important to follow the custom of bowing upon meeting someone. Also, remove shoes when entering restaurants, temples, or private homes.

Best Time to Visit Ryuo

The best time to visit Ryuo is during the spring (March to May) when the cherry blossoms are in full bloom, or during the fall (September to November) when the trees turn red and gold.

Do’s and Don’ts of Ryuo

Do:

  • Respect the local customs and traditions.
  • Be punctual.
  • Take off your shoes when entering a home, temple, or restaurant.

Don’t:

  • Don’t speak loudly or rudely in public.
  • Don’t tip the services. Tipping is frowned upon in Japan.
  • Don’t wear revealing clothing when visiting temples.

Safety and Health

Safety in Ryuo

Ryuo is a safe town for tourists. However, it is always good to take precautions, such as:

  • Keep valuables in a safe place.
  • Learn some basic Japanese phrases or carry a translation app with you.
  • Follow the traffic rules when driving or cycling.

Health in Ryuo

Visitors should take the necessary health precautions while traveling, such as applying sunscreen and staying hydrated to avoid heat stroke.

Getting to Ryuo: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Ryuo

The nearest airport to Ryuo is Osaka International Airport, which is about a two-hour drive from the town.

Bus Stations Near Ryuo

Ryuo is well-connected by bus services from most major cities in Japan. The Ryuo Bus Station is located in the center of the town.

Other Options

Trains are an excellent option for travelers who want to visit Ryuo efficiently. The JR Tokaido Shinkansen Line connects Ryuo Station to major cities in Japan.

How to Get Around in Ryuo

The easiest way to explore Ryuo is by using transportation services like Ryuo Bus, rent-a-car, or hire a taxi. Bicycles are also an excellent option for those who would like to explore the town at a leisurely pace.

Where to Go Next from Ryuo: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Kyoto: Kyoto is only an hour away by train and is Japan’s cultural capital. Visit the countless shrines, statues, temples, and more.

  • Takayama: Takayama is a beautiful town located in the Japanese Alps. It is famous for its historical districts and festivals.

Conclusion

Ryuo offers a unique blend of culture, history, and nature, making it the perfect destination for travelers looking for an immersive experience. From exploring castles and temples to hiking and biking on scenic routes, Ryuo has something to offer everyone. With this guide, you’re all set to plan your perfect trip, enjoy your stay in Ryuo, and make unforgettable memories that will last a lifetime.
