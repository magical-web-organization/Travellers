Introduction to Otsu

Located in the Shiga Prefecture of Japan’s Kansai region, Otsu is a charming city that offers visitors a rich cultural experience and breathtaking natural scenery. Otsu is situated on the southeastern shore of Lake Biwa, which is the largest freshwater lake in Japan. The city is easily accessible from Kyoto and Osaka, which makes it a perfect destination for a day trip, weekend getaway, or an extended stay. In this comprehensive travel guide, we will explore everything you need to know about visiting Otsu, from the must-see places and activities to the best hotels and local cuisine.

List of Places to Visit in Otsu

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Otsu has a rich cultural heritage with several historical places and museums that showcase the city’s traditions and history. The following are the must-visit museums and historical sites in Otsu:

  • Otsu City Museum of History
  • Miho Museum
  • Ishiyama-dera Temple
  • Hiyoshi Taisha Shrine
  • Enryaku-ji Temple (Mt. Hiei)

Natural Attractions of Otsu: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Otsu is a paradise for nature lovers, with several outdoor activities and scenic views. Some of the top attractions in Otsu are:

  • Mt. Hiei
  • Lake Biwa
  • Biwako Valley
  • Ogoto Onsen

Hiking/Biking Routes of Otsu

For hiking and biking enthusiasts, Otsu has several scenic routes that are perfect for a day trip. The following are the top hiking and biking routes in Otsu:

  • Mt. Hiei hiking trail
  • Biwa-ichi cycling road
  • Otsu cycling road

Best Things to Do in Otsu: Activities and Tours

If you are looking for unique experiences and tours, Otsu has plenty to offer. Whether you are interested in local culture or outdoor adventures, there is something for everyone. Some of the top activities and tours in Otsu are:

  • Tea ceremony experience
  • Fishing on Lake Biwa
  • Sake brewery tour

Week-Long Itinerary for Otsu

If you are planning to spend a week in Otsu, here is a sample itinerary that covers the best places and activities in and around the city:

Day 1: Ishiyama-dera Temple and Hieizan Enryaku-ji Temple

Day 2: Miho Museum and Ogoto Onsen

Day 3: Hiking at Mt. Hiei and tea ceremony experience

Day 4: Lake Biwa cruise and fishing tour

Day 5: Biwako Valley and Sake brewery tour

Day 6: Otsu City Museum of History and Hiyoshi Taisha Shrine

Day 7: Departure from Otsu

Food and Drink Scene of Otsu

Must-Try Dishes

Otsu is famous for its local cuisine, which features fresh seafood, rice, and vegetables. Some of the must-try dishes in Otsu are:

  • Omi beef
  • Biwako Oden
  • Funazushi
  • Jibuni
  • Shiga-style sushi

Local Beverages

Otsu is famous for its sake, which is made from high-quality rice and pure water from Lake Biwa. Some of the best sake breweries in Otsu are:

  • Kinoshita Shuzo
  • Kamotsuru Shuzo
  • Ōmi Shuzō

Where to Stay in Otsu: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Otsu has plenty of accommodation options for all budgets, from luxury hotels to budget-friendly hostels and guesthouses. Some of the top places to stay in Otsu are:

  • Biwako Hotel
  • Lake Biwa Otsu Prince Hotel
  • Guesthouse HANA
  • Ryokan Beniayu

What to Buy in Otsu: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

If you are looking for souvenirs and local products to bring home, Otsu has plenty to offer. Some of the best places for souvenirs in Otsu are:

  • Otsu Machikado Kadoya
  • Otsu Shukuba no Daidokoro
  • Hikone Castle Museum

Local Culture and Customs of Otsu

Otsu has a rich cultural heritage and several local customs that are still in practice today. Some of the notable cultural customs of Otsu are:

  • Tea ceremony
  • Biwako fireworks festival
  • Jidai Matsuri festival

Best Time to Visit Otsu

The best time to visit Otsu is from April to June and from September to November when the weather is mild, and the crowds are smaller. The cherry blossoms in April and autumn leaves in November are particularly beautiful.

Do’s and Don’ts of Otsu

Do:

  • Respect local customs and traditions
  • Try the local cuisine
  • Sample the local sake

Don’t:

  • Speak loudly in public places
  • Wear shoes inside shrines and temples
  • Touch the Geisha or Maiko’s hair or attire without permission

Safety and Health

Safety in Otsu

Otsu is a safe city, and tourists can explore the city without any major safety concerns. However, it is always wise to take basic precautions like keeping an eye on your belongings and avoiding deserted areas at night.

Health in Otsu

Otsu has several hospitals and medical facilities that offer quality healthcare services. Tourists can also seek help from their embassy in case of any emergency.

Getting to Otsu: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Otsu

The nearest major airports to Otsu are Kansai International Airport and Chubu Centrair International Airport, which are both approximately 2 hours away.

Bus Stations Near Otsu

Otsu is well-connected by buses from major cities like Kyoto, Osaka, and Nagoya. The main bus terminal in Otsu is Otsu Station, which is served by several bus companies.

Other Options

Tourists can also reach Otsu by train or car. Otsu Station is a major train station in the city and can be reached by train from major cities in Japan. Visitors can also rent a car and explore the city on their own.

How to Get Around in Otsu

Otsu is well-connected by public transportation like buses and trains. Visitors can also rent a bicycle and explore the scenic routes on their own. Taxis are also available for short trips and getting around the city quickly.

Where to Go Next from Otsu: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Otsu is surrounded by several beautiful destinations that are perfect for a day trip or weekend getaway. Some of the must-visit places near Otsu are:

  • Kyoto
  • Nara
  • Hikone
  • Omihachiman

Conclusion

If you are looking for a unique cultural experience in a beautiful and scenic setting, then Otsu is the perfect destination for you. With its rich history, natural attractions, local cuisine, and traditional customs, Otsu is sure to leave a lasting impression on anyone who visits. Whether you are planning a day trip or a week-long stay, Otsu has plenty to offer for all types of travelers.
