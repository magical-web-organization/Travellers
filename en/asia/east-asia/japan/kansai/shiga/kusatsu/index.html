Introduction to Kusatsu

Located in the Kansai region of Japan, Kusatsu is a breathtaking destination renowned for its natural hot springs, lush forests, and scenic hiking trails. This town in Shiga is an ideal destination for adventure seekers and history buffs alike, and offers a unique combination of outdoor recreation and traditional Japanese culture.

List of Places to Visit in Kusatsu

Whether you’re interested in visiting culturally important attractions, experiencing the natural beauty of Kusatsu, or discovering the iconic hiking and biking routes, this guide has everything you need to plan your trip. Check out some of the top places to visit in Kusatsu:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Kusatsu History Museum: This museum offers fascinating insights into the history of the town and its role in the development of the wider region.
  • Jingu-ji Temple: Established in 850, this temple is renowned for its stunning architecture and serene atmosphere.
  • Hiyoshi Taisha Shrine: Located on the slopes of Mount Hiei, this shrine offers breathtaking views of the surrounding area.

Natural Attractions of Kusatsu: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Kusatsu Hot Springs: One of the main attractions of Kusatsu, these natural hot springs have been used for their healing properties for centuries.
  • Mount Hiei: This towering peak offers some of the best hiking and mountain biking trails in the region, as well as stunning views of the surrounding area.
  • Biwako Valley: Located in nearby Otsu, Biwako Valley is a popular skiing and snowboarding destination, and offers stunning views of Lake Biwa.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Kusatsu

Kusatsu is renowned for its stunning hiking and biking trails, which offer some of the most breathtaking views in the region. Some of the best hiking and biking routes in Kusatsu include:

  • Kusatsu-Juku Trail: This historic trail passes through Kusatsu, and offers a unique glimpse into the town’s rich cultural heritage.
  • Mount Hiei Trail: This challenging trail offers stunning views of the surrounding area, and is a popular destination for hiking enthusiasts.
  • Lake Biwa Circuit: This scenic route circles Lake Biwa, and offers stunning views of the lake and surrounding mountains.

Best Things to Do in Kusatsu: Activities and Tours

In addition to hiking, biking, and exploring the town’s cultural attractions, there are plenty of other things to do in Kusatsu. Some of the best activities and tours in Kusatsu include:

  • Kusatsu Hot Springs Tour: This tour takes visitors to some of the best natural hot springs in the town, and offers a unique opportunity to experience this traditional Japanese activity.
  • Biwako Valley Skiing and Snowboarding: This popular winter activity is a must-do for snow sports enthusiasts, and offers stunning views of Lake Biwa.
  • Kusatsu Fireworks Festival: Held every August, this festival is one of the most popular events in Kusatsu, and features stunning nighttime fireworks displays.

Week-Long Itinerary for Kusatsu

If you’re planning a week-long trip to Kusatsu, consider the following itinerary:

Day Activity
1 Explore Kusatsu town and visit the Kusatsu History Museum
2 Hike the Kusatsu-Juku Trail and visit Jingu-ji Temple
3 Take a tour of the Kusatsu Hot Springs
4 Explore Mount Hiei and hike the Mount Hiei Trail
5 Visit Biwako Valley for skiing or snowboarding
6 Shop for souvenirs and local products in Kusatsu
7 Relax and enjoy the scenic views of Lake Biwa and the surrounding area

Food and Drink Scene of Kusatsu

No trip to Kusatsu is complete without sampling the local food and drink scene. Some of the top dishes and beverages to try in Kusatsu include:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Omi Beef: This local specialty is some of the best beef in Japan, and is typically served in the form of steak or sukiyaki.
  • Omi Rice: Made from locally grown rice, Omi Rice is a delicious and comforting dish that is perfect for cold winter days.
  • Biwako Buri: This local freshwater fish is a popular ingredient in many traditional Japanese dishes, and is typically served grilled or fried.

Local Beverages

  • Shiga Craft Beer: This local brew is made using traditional brewing methods and local ingredients, and is some of the best craft beer in the region.
  • Sake: Sake is a traditional Japanese rice wine that is popular throughout the country, and is typically served hot or cold.
  • Japanese Tea: Kusatsu is famous for its green tea, which is served in many of the town’s tea houses and restaurants.

Where to Stay in Kusatsu: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

There are plenty of accommodation options in Kusatsu, ranging from budget-friendly hostels to luxury hotels. Some of the top hotels, hostels, and guesthouses in Kusatsu include:

  • Kiyotaki Ryokan: This traditional Japanese inn offers authentic tatami rooms, hot springs, and delicious local cuisine.
  • Urban Hotel Kusatsu: This modern hotel is located in the heart of Kusatsu, and offers comfortable rooms and convenient access to the town’s attractions.
  • Kusatsu Onsen Hotel Resort: This luxurious hotel features hot springs, spa services, and stunning views of the surrounding mountains.

What to Buy in Kusatsu: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Kusatsu is renowned for its local products and souvenirs. Some of the best things to buy in Kusatsu include:

  • Omi Beef: Take home some of the best beef in Japan and cook it up as a special treat when you return home.
  • Shiga Craft Beer: Bring home a few bottles of this delicious local brew to share with friends and family.
  • Kusatsu Hot Springs Products: From bath salts to soap, there are plenty of products available that use the healing properties of Kusatsu’s hot springs.

Local Culture and Customs of Kusatsu

Kusatsu has a rich cultural heritage, and visitors are encouraged to learn about and respect the town’s customs and traditions. Some things to keep in mind while visiting Kusatsu include:

  • Take off your shoes before entering a traditional Japanese home or ryokan.
  • Be respectful of religious sites, and follow any posted rules or regulations.
  • When visiting a hot spring, be sure to follow any posted guidelines and respect the privacy of other bathers.

Best Time to Visit Kusatsu

The best time to visit Kusatsu depends on what you’re looking for. The summer months (June-August) offer warm weather and plenty of outdoor activities, while the winter months (December-February) offer skiing and snowboarding. Spring (March-May) and fall (September-November) offer cooler temperatures and beautiful foliage.

Do’s and Don’ts of Kusatsu

When visiting Kusatsu, there are a few things to keep in mind to make your trip as enjoyable as possible. Some key do’s and don’ts include:

Do:

  • Take advantage of the town’s stunning natural beauty and outdoor activities.
  • Learn about the local culture and customs, and be respectful of religious sites.
  • Try the local food and drink scene, and don’t be afraid to ask locals for recommendations.

Don’t:

  • Be disrespectful of religious sites or cultural traditions.
  • Ignore posted guidelines or rules regarding hot springs or other natural attractions.
  • Be rude or disruptive to other travelers or locals.

Safety and Health

Kusatsu is a safe destination, but visitors should still take some basic precautions to ensure a comfortable and enjoyable trip.

Safety in Kusatsu

  • Be aware of your surroundings, especially when traveling alone or at night.
  • Keep valuables such as passports and money in a secure location at all times.
  • Take precautions when hiking or participating in other outdoor activities.

Health in Kusatsu

  • Be aware of the effects of hot springs on your body, and be sure to follow any posted guidelines or rules.
  • Stay hydrated and wear sunscreen when participating in outdoor activities.
  • In case of emergency, dial 119 (emergency services) for immediate assistance.

Getting to Kusatsu: Transportation Options and Logistics

Kusatsu is easily accessible from major cities in Japan, and there are a variety of transportation options available.

Airports Near Kusatsu

  • Kansai International Airport: Located in Osaka, this is the largest airport in the region and offers international flights.
  • Chubu Centrair International Airport: Located in Nagoya, this airport offers domestic and international flights.

Bus Stations Near Kusatsu

  • JR Kusatsu Station: This train station is located in the heart of Kusatsu, and offers convenient access to the town’s attractions.
  • Kyoto Station: Located in nearby Kyoto, this train station offers bus connections to Kusatsu and other destinations in the region.

Other Options

  • Private Car or Taxi: Private cars and taxis are available for hire, and offer a convenient way to travel to and from Kusatsu.

How to Get Around in Kusatsu

Kusatsu is a small town, and many attractions are within walking distance of each other. However, there are also a variety of transportation options available.

  • Walking: Many attractions in Kusatsu are within walking distance of each other, and walking is a great way to experience the town’s beauty.
  • Biking: Bike rentals are available throughout the town, and offer a fun and eco-friendly way to explore Kusatsu.
  • Taxi: Taxis are readily available, and offer a convenient way to travel to and from attractions outside the town.

Where to Go Next from Kusatsu: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

If you’re looking to extend your trip, there are plenty of nearby destinations and excursions to consider. Some of the top destinations near Kusatsu include:

  • Kyoto: Located just a short train ride away, Kyoto is one of Japan’s most popular destinations, and features stunning temples, shrines, and gardens.
  • Osaka: Known for its vibrant nightlife and street food scene, Osaka is also home to some of the country’s top attractions and cultural sites.
  • Nara: A former capital of Japan, Nara is renowned for its stunning temples and Shrines, as well as its friendly deer population.

Conclusion

Kusatsu is a stunning destination that offers a unique combination of outdoor recreation and traditional Japanese culture. Whether you’re interested in exploring the town’s cultural attractions, experiencing the natural beauty of the region, or simply relaxing in the healing waters of the hot springs, Kusatsu has something for everyone. With this comprehensive travel guide, you’ll be able to plan the perfect trip to this breathtaking destination.
