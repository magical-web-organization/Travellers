Introduction to Hikone

Nestled in the Shiga Prefecture of Kansai region, Hikone is a beautiful city that boasts natural beauty, rich cultural heritage, and delectable cuisine. The city is home to one of Japan’s most well-preserved castles - Hikone Castle - which attracts visitors from all over the world. Hikone is also known for its traditional festivals, stunning gardens, and picturesque landscapes.

With this comprehensive travel guide, you will explore the best that Hikone has to offer.

List of Places to Visit in Hikone

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Hikone has an exceptional cultural significance, with several museums and historical places that offer visitors a peek into the city’s rich history. Famed attractions include:

  • Hikone Castle
  • Shiga Prefectural Museum of History
  • Genkyu-en Garden
  • Nagahama Castle Historical Museum
  • Yonban-cho Square

Natural Attractions of Hikone: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Hikone’s natural beauty is captivating, and there are many places where visitors can enjoy the great outdoors. Popular natural attractions include:

  • Lake Biwa
  • Mount Hiei
  • Himure Hachimangu Shrine
  • Hikone Green Hill Park

Hiking/Biking Routes of Hikone

Hikone is the perfect destination for outdoor enthusiasts. The city offers several biking and hiking routes that provide an opportunity to explore the scenic beauty of the region. Some of the best routes include:

  • Biwako Cycling Road
  • Mount Hiei Trail
  • Hachiman Mountain Trail
  • Mt. Ryuo Nature Park

Best Things to Do in Hikone: Activities and Tours

Hikone has many things to do for visitors whether they are interested in cultural or outdoor activities. Some of the top things to do in Hikone include:

  • Enjoying a boat ride on Lake Biwa
  • Visiting Hikone Castle Museum
  • Exploring the Yonban-cho Square and Museum
  • Attending Hikone Castle’s annual cherry blossom festival

Week-Long Itinerary for Hikone

Hikone has a wide range of attractions to explore. A week-long itinerary will allow visitors to explore the best of Hikone, and nearby destinations.

Day Destination
Day 1 Explore Hikone Castle and Museum
Day 2 Visit Shiga Prefectural Museum of History and Nagahama Castle Historical Museum
Day 3 Take a Boat Ride on Lake Biwa
Day 4 Hike in Mount Hiei or Hachiman Mountain Trail
Day 5 Visit Genkyu-en Garden and Hikone Green Hill Park
Day 6 Discover the culture of Yonban-cho Square and surrounding areas
Day 7 Excursion to Kyoto’s temples and shrines

Food and Drink Scene of Hikone

Must-Try Dishes

Hikone is a culinary hotspot, and visitors can enjoy a wide range of traditional Japanese delicacies. Some of the must-try dishes include:

  • Omi Beef
  • Saba Misoni
  • Funazushi
  • Oden
  • Hikone Sweets

Local Beverages

Hikone is also known for its local beverages, including:

  • Sake
  • Hikone Beer
  • Hikone Quince Tea

Where to Stay in Hikone: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Hikone has several lodging options for travelers of all budgets. Depending on your needs, you can choose from:

  • International and Business Hotels
  • Budget-Friendly Hostels
  • Ryokans (Traditional Inns)

What to Buy in Hikone: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Hikone has a rich culture that is reflected in the souvenirs and local products made by local artisans. Consider buying:

  • Omi Traditional Handicrafts
  • Japanese Traditional Sweets
  • Hikone Quince Tea
  • Kokufu-ten Orchid Flowers
  • Hikone Castle Miniatures

Local Culture and Customs of Hikone

Hikone is a city with a rich culture and customs. Visitors should observe the following:

  • Remove your shoes when entering homes and temples
  • Bow slightly when greeting someone
  • Respect privacy and personal space

Best Time to Visit Hikone

Hikone is a year-round destination, but the best time to visit is spring and autumn when the weather is mild, and the scenery is stunning.

Do’s and Don’ts of Hikone

Do:

  • Respect the local culture and customs
  • Carry cash as some shops and restaurants do not accept cards
  • Dress appropriately, especially when visiting temples or shrines.

Don’t:

  • Speak loudly in public areas
  • Eat or drink while walking in public areas
  • Tip at restaurants or hotels.

Safety and Health

Safety in Hikone

Hikone is a relatively safe city. Visitors should take their normal precautions, such as:

  • Keep your belongings safe at all times
  • Avoid walking alone at night
  • Be aware of your surroundings in crowded places.

Health in Hikone

Hikone has excellent health facilities, and visitors can access medical services without any issues. However, it is advisable to have medical insurance coverage before visiting.

Getting to Hikone: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Hikone

The nearest airport to Hikone is Kansai International Airport, which is served by various international airlines. From there, visitors can take a train or bus to Hikone.

Bus Stations Near Hikone

Hikone Bus Terminal is the main bus station in the city, and it is serviced by various buses from nearby destinations.

Other Options

Visitors can also get to Hikone by train, taxi or rental car.

How to Get Around in Hikone

Hikone is a relatively small city, and visitors can easily explore it on foot or by bicycle. Public transport options include buses, trains, and taxis.

Where to Go Next from Hikone: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Hikone is a gateway to some of the most exciting destinations in Japan. Some of the best nearby destinations and excursions include:

  • Kyoto
  • Nagoya
  • Osaka
  • Nara

Conclusion

Hikone is a city that is rich in culture, natural beauty, and history. With its traditional festivals, stunning gardens, and picturesque landscapes, Hikone is a must-visit destination. Plan your trip to Hikone today and discover the hidden gems of this Japanese gem.
