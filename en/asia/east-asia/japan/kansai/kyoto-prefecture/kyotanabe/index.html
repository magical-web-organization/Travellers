Kyotanabe is a charming city located in the southern part of Kyoto Prefecture, Japan. It is a hidden gem that offers a peaceful escape from the bustling city of Kyoto while still showcasing the rich cultural traditions and natural beauty of the Kansai region. With its scenic views, exciting outdoor adventures, and historical landmarks, Kyotanabe is a must-visit destination for anyone planning a trip to Japan. Here’s a comprehensive guide to help you plan your visit.

Introduction to Kyotanabe

Kyotanabe is a small city located in the southern part of Kyoto Prefecture, Japan. It has a population of approximately 65,000 people and covers an area of 37.96 square kilometers. The city is surrounded by lush green mountains, making it a popular destination for outdoor enthusiasts who love hiking, cycling, and other outdoor activities. Kyotanabe is also home to several cultural and historical landmarks, including temples, museums, and shrines that offer a glimpse into the rich cultural traditions of the region.

List of Places to Visit in Kyotanabe

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  1. Higashi-Otani Tomb: This ancient tomb is believed to be the burial site of Emperor Keitai, who ruled Japan from 507 to 531. The tomb is located in a park and is surrounded by beautiful cherry blossom trees.
  2. Nara Sho Museum: This museum showcases the cultural significance of Nara Sho, a traditional Japanese rice cake that is made in Kyotanabe. The museum features exhibits on the history of Nara Sho and offers workshops where visitors can learn how to make the rice cake.
  3. Otokuni-dera Temple: This temple was built in the 8th century and is the oldest temple in Kyotanabe. It is known for its beautiful gardens and traditional architecture.
  4. Old Shimoda Family Residence: This traditional Japanese house was built in the Edo period and offers a glimpse into the daily life of a wealthy family during that time.

Natural Attractions of Kyotanabe: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  1. Mount Satsuki: This mountain is the highest peak in Kyotanabe, standing at 466 meters. It offers breathtaking views of the surrounding landscape and is a popular destination for hiking and mountain climbing.
  2. Yamakawa Gorge: This scenic gorge is located in the southern part of Kyotanabe and offers stunning views of the surrounding mountains and forests. It is a popular destination for hiking and camping.
  3. Naraigawa River: This river flows through Kyotanabe and offers opportunities for fishing, boating, and other water sports.
  4. Nagamine Park: This park offers beautiful views of the cherry blossoms in the spring and is a great spot for a picnic or a relaxing walk.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Kyotanabe

  1. Mount Satsuki Hiking Trail: This hiking trail leads to the summit of Mount Satsuki and offers stunning views of the surrounding mountains and forests.
  2. Yamakawa Gorge Hiking Trail: This hiking trail follows the Yamakawa Gorge and offers breathtaking views of the surrounding landscape.
  3. Naraigawa River Biking Trail: This 10-kilometer biking trail follows the Naraigawa River and offers scenic views of the river and surrounding mountains.
  4. Kyotanabe Green Road: This 16-kilometer paved biking and walking path connects several parks and other attractions in Kyotanabe.

Best Things to Do in Kyotanabe: Activities and Tours

  1. Nara Sho Making Workshop: Learn how to make traditional Japanese rice cakes at the Nara Sho Museum.
  2. Temple and Shrine Tour: Visit some of Kyotanabe’s most famous shrines and temples, including Otokuni-dera Temple and Ninoen Shrine.
  3. Outdoor Adventures: Go hiking, mountain climbing, or camping in the beautiful mountains and gorges of Kyotanabe.
  4. Cherry Blossom Viewing: Visit Nagamine Park or Higashi-Otani Tomb in the spring to see the beautiful cherry blossoms in bloom.

Week-Long Itinerary for Kyotanabe

Day 1: Visit Higashi-Otani Tomb and Nagamine Park for cherry blossom viewing. Day 2: Go hiking or mountain climbing in Mount Satsuki. Day 3: Take a Nara Sho making workshop at the Nara Sho Museum. Day 4: Visit Otokuni-dera Temple and other historic landmarks in the city. Day 5: Explore Yamakawa Gorge and go camping or hiking in the area. Day 6: Take a temple and shrine tour of Kyotanabe. Day 7: Biking tour along the scenic Kyotanabe Green Road.

Food and Drink Scene of Kyotanabe

Must-Try Dishes

  1. Nara Sho: This traditional Japanese rice cake is made with glutinous rice and has a sweet and savory flavor.
  2. Kyotanabe Manju: This sweet pastry is filled with sweetened red bean paste and is a popular snack in the region.
  3. Obanzai: This is a traditional Kyoto-style home-cooked meal that consists of several small dishes, including pickles, vegetables, and tofu.

Local Beverages

  1. Sake: Kyoto Prefecture is known for its high-quality sake, and Kyotanabe is no exception. You can try different types of sake at local bars and restaurants.
  2. Ume-shu: This sweet plum wine is a popular drink in the region.
  3. Green Tea: Kyoto Prefecture is also known for its high-quality green tea, which is grown mainly in the Uji region.

  2. Kyotanabe Guesthouse: This guesthouse is a great option for budget travelers who want to experience Japanese hospitality and culture.

What to Buy in Kyotanabe: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  1. Nara Sho: You can buy Nara Sho, the traditional Japanese rice cake, at the Nara Sho Museum or in local shops around Kyotanabe.
  2. Kyotanabe Manju: This sweet pastry makes a great souvenir or gift for friends and family.
  3. Green Tea: You can buy high-quality green tea from local shops and tea houses in the region.

Local Culture and Customs of Kyotanabe

Kyotanabe, like the rest of Kyoto Prefecture, has a rich cultural heritage that is deeply ingrained in the daily life of its people. Visitors should respect local customs, such as removing their shoes before entering temples and respecting the silence in outdoor spaces. Visitors can also experience traditional Japanese hospitality by staying at a local guesthouse or participating in cultural activities like Nara Sho making workshops.

Best Time to Visit Kyotanabe

The best time to visit Kyotanabe is in the spring or fall when the weather is mild and the landscape is at its most beautiful. The cherry blossoms in Nagamine Park and Higashi-Otani Tomb bloom in early spring, and the autumn leaves in the mountains and gorges are a sight to behold in the fall.

Do’s and Don’ts of Kyotanabe

Do:

  • Respect local customs and traditions.
  • Enjoy the natural beauty of the region.
  • Try the local cuisine and beverages.
  • Visit the historic temples and shrines.

Don’t:

  • Litter or damage the natural environment.
  • Cause disruption or noise in public spaces.
  • Disrespect traditional customs or dress inappropriately in religious sites.

Safety and Health

Safety in Kyotanabe

Kyotanabe is a safe city, and visitors can feel comfortable exploring the region on their own. However, like all cities in Japan, visitors should be aware of their surroundings and exercise caution when traveling alone at night or in unfamiliar areas.

Health in Kyotanabe

Kyotanabe has adequate medical facilities and services, and visitors can rest assured that they will receive quality care if needed. Visitors should bring any necessary medications with them and take proper precautions to avoid illness or injury while traveling.

Getting to Kyotanabe: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Kyotanabe

The closest airport to Kyotanabe is Kansai International Airport, which is located approximately 80 kilometers from the city. Visitors can take the train or bus from the airport to reach Kyotanabe.

Bus Stations Near Kyotanabe

Kyotanabe is serviced by several bus lines that connect the city to other destinations in Kyoto Prefecture and beyond.

Other Options

Visitors can also reach Kyotanabe by train, with several local and regional lines servicing the city.

How to Get Around in Kyotanabe

The best way to get around Kyotanabe is by car or bicycle, as public transportation options can be limited in some areas. Visitors can rent a car or bicycle from local shops or hotels.

Where to Go Next from Kyotanabe: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  1. Kyoto: This bustling city is located just 20 kilometers north of Kyotanabe and is known for its historic temples and shrines.
  2. Nara: This historic city is located approximately 50 kilometers south of Kyotanabe and is home to several UNESCO World Heritage sites, including the Todai-ji temple and Kasuga-taisha shrine.
  3. Osaka: This vibrant city is located approximately 60 kilometers west of Kyotanabe and is known for its delicious food and bustling nightlife.

Conclusion

Kyotanabe in Kyoto Prefecture is a hidden gem that offers a unique blend of natural beauty and cultural richness. Visitors can explore historic temples and shrines, go hiking in the beautiful mountains and gorges, and sample the delicious local cuisine and beverages. With this comprehensive guide, visitors can plan an unforgettable trip to this charming city and create lasting memories.
