Introduction to South

Located in the southern region of Kyoto, South is one of the most popular destinations in Japan. The area is rich in history, culture, and natural beauty, making it a must-visit for anyone traveling to Kyoto or the Kansai region.

South is known for its stunning temples, traditional architecture, and beautiful gardens. The area also offers plenty of outdoor activities for visitors to enjoy, such as hiking, cycling, and hot springs. If you’re looking for a place to relax and unwind, South is the perfect destination.

List of Places to Visit in South

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Fushimi Inari Taisha

Fushimi Inari-Taisha is one of the most famous Shinto shrines in Kyoto. The shrine is known for its thousands of torii gates, which create a path up the mountain behind the shrine. Visitors who hike the path to the top will be rewarded with stunning views of Kyoto.

Tofuku-ji Temple

Tofuku-ji Temple is a beautiful, large Zen temple with stunning gardens. The temple is famous for its beautiful autumn foliage and the Tsutenkyo Bridge, which offers beautiful views of the gardens.

Kiyomizu-dera Temple

Kiyomizu-dera is a UNESCO World Heritage Site and one of the most famous temples in Kyoto. The temple is known for its stunning views of the city and beautiful architecture.

Natural Attractions of South: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Arashiyama

Arashiyama is a district located in the western part of Kyoto that is known for its beautiful bamboo groves, hiking trails, and river cruises. The area is especially beautiful during the autumn season when the leaves change color.

Mount Atago

Mount Atago is the highest mountain in Kyoto and is known for its beautiful views of the city. Visitors can hike to the top of the mountain or take a cable car for stunning views of the surrounding area.

Sagano Scenic Railway

The Sagano Scenic Railway is a train that runs from Arashiyama to Kameoka and offers stunning views of the Hozugawa River and the surrounding mountains.

Hiking/Biking Routes of South

Fushimi Inari Taisha Hiking Trail

Visitors to Fushimi Inari Taisha can take a hiking trail that leads up to the top of the mountain behind the shrine. The trail is lined with thousands of torii gates and offers stunning views of the surrounding area.

Kyoto Cycling Tour

The Kyoto Cycling Tour offers visitors the opportunity to explore the city by bike. The tour takes visitors to some of Kyoto’s most famous attractions, as well as some lesser-known spots.

Best Things to Do in South: Activities and Tours

Tea Ceremony

Kyoto is famous for its tea traditions, and visitors can participate in a traditional tea ceremony at one of the city’s many tea houses.

Sake Tasting

Sake is a traditional Japanese drink made from fermented rice. Visitors can taste different types of sake at local breweries and learn about the drink’s history and production process.

Food and Drink Scene of South

Must-Try Dishes

Yuba

Yuba is a traditional Kyoto dish made from soybeans. The dish is often served with vegetables and rice and is a must-try for anyone visiting the city.

Tofu

Tofu is another traditional Kyoto dish that is popular with visitors. The dish is made from soybeans and can be served in a variety of ways, such as fried or grilled.

Local Beverages

Sake

Sake is a traditional Japanese drink made from fermented rice. Kyoto is known for its high-quality sake, and visitors can taste different types of sake at local breweries.

Where to Stay in South: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Kyoto Guesthouse

Kyoto Guesthouse is a popular budget accommodation option in South. The guesthouse offers private rooms and dormitory-style accommodation, as well as a shared kitchen and lounge area.

Hotel Gran Ms Kyoto

Hotel Gran Ms Kyoto is a mid-range hotel located in the heart of South. The hotel offers comfortable rooms, a restaurant, and a spa.

The Ritz-Carlton, Kyoto

The Ritz-Carlton, Kyoto is a luxury hotel located in South. The hotel features beautiful rooms, a spa, and multiple restaurants.

What to Buy in South: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Kyo Yuzen Fabric

Kyo Yuzen is a traditional dyeing technique that is used to create beautiful fabrics. Visitors can purchase Kyo Yuzen fabrics at local markets and shops.

Kyoto Pottery

Kyoto is famous for its pottery, which is known for its beautiful designs and high quality. Visitors can purchase Kyoto pottery at local shops and studios.

Local Culture and Customs of South

Kyoto is known for its traditional culture, and visitors should be aware of local customs and etiquette. It is important to be respectful when visiting temples and shrines, and to remove shoes when entering someone’s home or a traditional ryokan.

Best Time to Visit South

The best time to visit South is in the autumn season (September-November) when the leaves change color and the weather is cool and comfortable.

Do’s and Don’ts of South

Do:

  • Be respectful when visiting temples and shrines.
  • Take off your shoes when entering someone’s home or a traditional ryokan.
  • Try local food and drink, like sake and tofu.
  • Participate in traditional activities, like tea ceremonies and pottery classes.

Don’t:

  • Wear shoes in temples and shrines.
  • Be loud or disruptive in public places.
  • Take photos of people without their permission.
  • Leave trash in public areas.

Safety and Health

Safety in South

South is a safe area for travelers, but visitors should still take precautions, such as keeping valuables close and being aware of their surroundings. It is also important to follow local laws and customs to avoid any issues.

Health in South

South is a relatively clean and healthy area, but visitors should still be careful when it comes to food and water. It is important to drink bottled or boiled water and to be cautious when trying new foods.

Getting to South: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near South

The nearest airport to South is Osaka International Airport, which is about an hour’s drive away. Visitors can also fly into Kansai International Airport, which is about two hours away.

Bus Stations Near South

There are several bus stations in South, including Kyoto Station and Gion Bus Stop. Visitors can take buses to and from other parts of Kyoto and the surrounding area.

Other Options

Visitors can also take trains or taxis to South, or rent a car and drive themselves.

How to Get Around in South

Visitors can get around South using public transportation, such as buses and trains. Bikes are also a popular option for getting around the area.

Where to Go Next from South: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Nara

Nara is a historic city located near Kyoto that is known for its beautiful temples and shrines. Visitors can take a day trip to Nara from South to explore the city.

Osaka

Osaka is a bustling city located near Kyoto that is known for its food and nightlife. Visitors can take a day trip to Osaka from South to experience the city’s culture.

Conclusion

South is a beautiful and historic destination that offers something for everyone. With stunning temples, beautiful gardens, and plenty of outdoor activities, visitors to South will find plenty to see and do. Whether you’re looking to relax and unwind, or to explore the beauty of Kyoto, South is the perfect destination for your next trip.
