Introduction to Arashiyama

Arashiyama is a must-visit destination for anyone travelling to Kyoto. Located in the western outskirts of the city, it is known for its stunning natural beauty and rich culture. Whether you’re interested in exploring historic sites, taking in scenic views, or enjoying outdoor adventures, Arashiyama has something for everyone. In this comprehensive travel guide, we’ll help you plan your trip to Arashiyama, covering everything from must-visit locations to do’s and don’ts.

List of Places to Visit in Arashiyama

Arashiyama is home to several attractions that are worth a visit. Here is a list of the top places to visit in Arashiyama:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Tenryu-ji Temple
  • Bamboo Forest
  • Jojakko-ji Temple
  • Nison-in Temple
  • Gio-ji Temple
  • Okochi-Sanso Villa
  • Adashino Nenbutsu-ji Temple

Natural Attractions of Arashiyama: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Katsura River
  • Mt. Arashiyama
  • Iwatayama Monkey Park
  • Sagano Scenic Railway
  • Hozu-gawa River Cruise
  • Arashiyama Park

Hiking/Biking Routes of Arashiyama

Arashiyama is known for its beautiful hiking and biking trails that offer stunning views of the surrounding mountains, forests, and rivers. Here are some of the best routes to explore:

  • Okochi-Sanso Villa Trail
  • Kiyotaki River Trail
  • Kameoka-Tamba Cycling Road
  • Arashiyama to Takao Hiking Trail
  • Mt. Atago Trail

Best Things to Do in Arashiyama: Activities and Tours

Apart from visiting popular attractions and hiking/biking, there are several activities and tours you can enjoy in Arashiyama. Here are a few of the best things to do:

  • Tea ceremony experience
  • Kimono rental and photoshoot
  • River rafting
  • Fishing
  • Zen meditation experience
  • Local sake tasting

Food and Drink Scene of Arashiyama

Arashiyama offers a variety of food and drink options that showcase the local cuisine and culture. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Yudofu (tofu hot pot)
  • Hozu-gawa Fish (grilled river fish)
  • Kaiseki Ryori (traditional multi-course Japanese meal)
  • Arashiyama Noodles (soba or udon noodles served in a hot broth)

Local Beverages

  • Matcha (green tea)
  • Sake (Japanese rice wine)
  • Shochu (Japanese distilled alcohol)
  • Yuzu Juice (citrus fruit juice)

Where to Stay in Arashiyama: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Arashiyama offers a variety of accommodation options, ranging from luxury hotels to budget guesthouses. Here are some of the best places to stay:

  • Hoshinoya Kyoto (luxury resort)
  • Suiran Kyoto (luxury hotel)
  • Arashiyama Benkei (ryokan-style hotel)
  • Guesthouse Arashiyama (budget guesthouse)
  • First Cabin Kyoto Arashiyama (capsule hotel)

What to Buy in Arashiyama: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Arashiyama is famous for its traditional handicrafts, local snacks and sweets, and other unique items. Here are some souvenirs and local products you should consider buying:

  • Tenugui (traditional Japanese hand towel)
  • Yatsuhashi (cinnamon-flavored sweet)
  • Sagano bamboo craft products
  • Local sake and other spirits
  • Matcha and other tea products

Local Culture and Customs of Arashiyama

Arashiyama has a rich cultural heritage that is deeply rooted in Japanese history and tradition. Here are some customs and etiquettes that you should be aware of when visiting:

  • Respect local customs and traditions, such as bowing and removing your shoes when entering temples and other sacred places.
  • Observe proper table manners and etiquette when dining.
  • Do not litter or smoke in public places.
  • Ask for permission before taking photos of people or private property.

Best Time to Visit Arashiyama

The best time to visit Arashiyama depends on your preferences and interests. Here are some recommendations based on the season:

  • Spring (March to May): Cherry blossom season, with plenty of outdoor activities and festivals.
  • Summer (June to August): Warm weather, ideal for hiking and enjoying outdoor adventures.
  • Fall (September to November): Beautiful autumn foliage, with cooler temperatures and fewer crowds.
  • Winter (December to February): Snowy landscapes, with seasonal festivals and cozy indoor activities.

Do’s and Don’ts of Arashiyama

Here are some do’s and don’ts to keep in mind when visiting Arashiyama:

Do:

  • Try local food and drinks.
  • Take part in cultural experiences and activities.
  • Show respect for local customs and traditions.
  • Dress appropriately for the season and occasion.

Don’t:

  • Smoke in public places.
  • Litter or disturb the natural environment.
  • Take photos of people or private property without permission.
  • Engage in disruptive or disrespectful behavior.

Safety and Health

Safety in Arashiyama

Arashiyama is a safe destination for travelers, with low crime rates and well-maintained public spaces. Here are some safety tips to keep in mind:

  • Be aware of your surroundings, especially at night or in crowded areas.
  • Take precautions to prevent heatstroke and dehydration during the summer months.
  • Wear comfortable and appropriate clothing and footwear for outdoor activities.

Health in Arashiyama

Arashiyama has modern medical facilities and services, including hospitals and clinics. Here are some tips for staying healthy:

  • Drink bottled water or tap water that has been boiled or filtered.
  • Take precautions to prevent insect bites and sunburn.
  • Bring any necessary medications or prescriptions with you.

Getting to Arashiyama: Transportation Options and Logistics

Arashiyama is easily accessible by public transportation and offers several options for getting around the area.

Airports Near Arashiyama

The nearest airports to Arashiyama are the Kansai International Airport and the Osaka International Airport, both of which offer direct flights from major cities in Asia, Europe, and North America.

Bus Stations Near Arashiyama

There are several bus stations in and around Arashiyama, including the Arashiyama Bus Station, which is located near the popular attractions and offers bus services to other parts of Kyoto.

Other Options

Alternatively, you can take a taxi or rent a car to get around Arashiyama and the surrounding areas.

How to Get Around in Arashiyama

Arashiyama is a walkable and bike-friendly destination, with several rental shops offering bicycles for hire. Alternatively, you can take public transportation such as buses and trains, or use taxis and rental cars.

Where to Go Next from Arashiyama: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Arashiyama is a great starting point for exploring other destinations in Kansai and beyond. Here are some nearby places that you should consider visiting:

  • Kyoto City Center
  • Nara Park
  • Osaka City Center
  • Kobe City Center
  • Hiroshima City Center

Conclusion

Arashiyama is a beautiful and culturally rich destination that offers something for everyone. Whether you’re interested in outdoor activities, historic sites, or food and drink, Arashiyama has it all. Use this comprehensive travel guide to plan your trip and make the most of your time in this stunning part of Kyoto.
