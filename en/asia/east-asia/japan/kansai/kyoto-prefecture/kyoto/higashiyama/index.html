Introduction to Higashiyama

Located in the heart of Kyoto, Higashiyama is a district that is deeply rooted in Japan’s rich cultural history. The district is home to some of the most beautiful temples, shrines, and historic sites in all of Japan. Visitors can immerse themselves in traditional Japanese culture and take a step back in time as they explore the narrow streets of this charming district.

List of Places to Visit in Higashiyama

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Higashiyama is known for its beautiful temples and shrines, which have been designated as UNESCO World Heritage sites. Among the most popular attractions are the Kiyomizu-dera Temple, the Yasaka Shrine, and the Ginkaku-ji Temple. Additionally, there are several museums in Higashiyama that showcase the history and art of the region. The Kyoto National Museum, the Museum of Kyoto, and the Kyoto Municipal Museum of Art are just a few that are worth visiting.

Natural Attractions of Higashiyama: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Higashiyama offers visitors a unique opportunity to reconnect with nature. The Sannenzaka and Ninenzaka sloping streets are lined with traditional shops and restaurants and offer stunning views of the city. At the base of the Higashiyama mountains, visitors can also take a stroll through the beautiful Maruyama Park. In addition, visitors can rent bikes and explore the district or take a leisurely hike through the mountains.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Higashiyama

One of the best ways to explore Higashiyama is on foot or by bike. The Philosopher’s Path is a beautiful tree-lined pedestrian path that runs along a small canal from Ginkaku-ji Temple to Nanzen-ji Temple. The Kyoto Circuit Trail is another popular trail, which offers stunning views of the city and takes visitors through several temples and shrines. Guided tours are also available for those interested in exploring Higashiyama’s natural beauty.

Best Things to Do in Higashiyama: Activities and Tours

Higashiyama offers a variety of activities and tours for visitors to enjoy. Those interested in traditional Japanese culture can participate in a tea ceremony or learn the art of calligraphy. For a unique experience, visitors can take a rickshaw ride through the streets of Higashiyama. Additionally, there are several guided tours available that will take visitors to the most popular attractions in the district.

Week-Long Itinerary for Higashiyama

Visitors to Higashiyama can easily spend a week exploring all the district has to offer. Some must-see attractions include the Kiyomizu-dera Temple, the Yasaka Shrine, and the Ginkaku-ji Temple. Visitors can also take a bike tour of the district and explore the natural beauty of Higashiyama. Conclude the trip by enjoying traditional Japanese cuisine and shopping for souvenirs in the local markets.

Food and Drink Scene of Higashiyama

Must-Try Dishes

Higashiyama is known for its delicious traditional Japanese cuisine. Some must-try dishes include yudofu (boiled tofu), matcha (Japanese green tea), and soba noodles. Another must-try dish is kaiseki, a traditional multi-course Japanese meal that is served at some of the best restaurants in the district.

Local Beverages

Higashiyama is home to several fantastic sake breweries, including Gekkeikan Okura Sake Museum, where visitors can learn about the history and production of sake. Beer lovers can also visit the Kyoto Beer Brewery for a taste of locally brewed beer.

Where to Stay in Higashiyama: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Higashiyama offers a variety of accommodation options to suit every budget. From luxurious hotels, like the Hyatt Regency Kyoto, to budget-friendly hostels and guesthouses, there is something for everyone. For a more unique experience, visitors can also book a stay in a traditional Japanese ryokan.

What to Buy in Higashiyama: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Higashiyama is known for its beautiful traditional crafts and souvenirs. Visitors can shop for handmade pottery, lacquerware, and textiles. Local sweets, like Kyoto-style yatsuhashi (sweet rice cakes), are also popular souvenirs. Additionally, visitors can bring home a beautiful kimono or yukata as a reminder of their trip to Higashiyama.

Local Culture and Customs of Higashiyama

Japanese culture places a strong emphasis on respect and etiquette. Visitors should be mindful of their behavior when visiting temples and shrines, and should remove their shoes when entering a traditional Japanese home or establishment. Additionally, visitors should avoid loud or disruptive behavior in public places.

Best Time to Visit Higashiyama

The best time to visit Higashiyama is during the spring and fall seasons. In spring, visitors can enjoy the cherry blossoms, while in fall, the leaves take on beautiful shades of red and gold. However, Higashiyama is beautiful year-round, so visitors can enjoy the district’s charm no matter what time of year they visit.

Do’s and Don’ts of Higashiyama

Do:

  • Wear comfortable shoes for walking and hiking
  • Be respectful when visiting temples and shrines
  • Visit local museums and art exhibits
  • Try traditional Japanese cuisine

Don’t:

  • Use loud or disruptive behavior in public places
  • Take pictures of people without their permission
  • Leave trash or litter on the streets

Safety and Health

Safety in Higashiyama

Higashiyama is a relatively safe district, but visitors should be mindful of their belongings when traveling through crowded areas. Petty crime, such as pickpocketing, can occur in popular tourist areas.

Health in Higashiyama

Higashiyama is generally safe and clean. However, visitors should take precautions to prevent heatstroke and dehydration during the hot summer months. It is also important to practice good hygiene and wash hands frequently to prevent the spread of illnesses.

Getting to Higashiyama: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Higashiyama

The closest airport to Higashiyama is Osaka International Airport. Visitors can take a direct train or bus from the airport to Kyoto, and then take a local bus or taxi to Higashiyama.

Bus Stations Near Higashiyama

The Kyoto City Bus and the Keihan Bus both stop at various locations throughout Higashiyama. Additionally, visitors can take the JR Train to Kyoto Station and then transfer to a local bus or taxi.

Other Options

Visitors can also rent a car or bike to explore Higashiyama and the surrounding areas. However, parking can be difficult to find in crowded areas.

How to Get Around in Higashiyama

The best way to get around Higashiyama is on foot or by bike. Visitors can rent bikes from various shops in the district or take a bike tour. Additionally, Higashiyama is well-connected by bus and taxi.

Where to Go Next from Higashiyama: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Some popular destinations near Higashiyama include the Fushimi Inari Shrine, the Arashiyama Bamboo Grove, and Nara Park. Visitors can take a day trip or overnight stay to explore these nearby attractions.

Conclusion

Higashiyama is a must-visit destination for anyone interested in Japanese culture and history. From the beautiful temples and shrines to the natural beauty of the district, Higashiyama offers something for everyone.
