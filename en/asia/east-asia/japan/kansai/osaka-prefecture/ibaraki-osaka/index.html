Introduction to Ibaraki (Osaka)

Ibaraki is a suburb located in northern Osaka prefecture, Japan. While originally, it is a small city, it is now an integral part of Osaka’s metropolitan area. Unlike downtown Osaka with its vibrant atmosphere, Ibaraki stands out with its quiet and serene environment, making it an ideal place for a weekend getaway or a peaceful retreat. The city is famous for its cultural attractions, historical landmarks, and natural beauty, attracting tourists from all over the world.

List of Places to Visit in Ibaraki (Osaka)

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Ibaraki boasts a rich historical background and culture that is reflected in its numerous attractions:

  • Tadao Ando Gallery: A must-visit attraction for contemporary art lovers, located on the campus of the Hyogo Prefectural Museum of Art. The gallery boasts a collection of modern art pieces designed by the famous Japanese architect Tadao Ando.
  • Ibaraki Nature Museum: Located in the lush green foliage of Ibaraki’s outskirts. This museum gives visitors an in-depth insight into the area’s diverse flora and fauna.
  • Abeno Harukas: A skyscraper built at the heart of Osaka in the Kintetsu Abeno Harukas building. The observation deck on the top floor offers a panoramic view of the entire city.

Natural Attractions of Ibaraki (Osaka): Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Undoubtedly, the natural beauty of Ibaraki is incomparable with any other city in Japan. Below are some of the natural attractions that will give you a glimpse into Ibaraki’s majestic beauty:

  • Makiochi Natural Park: This park offers stunning views of mountains and valleys and is a perfect place for a picnic or a leisurely stroll with friends and family.
  • Minoo Park: A vast park with spectacular landscapes, waterfalls, and green meadows. This park is a perfect escape from the hustle and bustle of the city.
  • Matsuo Basho Memorial: A memorial park dedicated to the famous Japanese poet and travel writer, Matsuo Basho. The park’s well-kept gardens and serene atmosphere makes it a perfect place to relax and appreciate the beauty of nature.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Ibaraki (Osaka)

Ibaraki’s location is ideal for a nature lover. It offers several trails that visitors can explore with their friends and family, including:

  • Minoo Park Hiking Trail: This trail features a view of the majestic Meiji Waterfall.
  • Okukiharadai Hiking Trail: This hiking trail offers an excellent view of the Osaka Bay from the top of Okukiharadai mountain.

Best Things to Do in Ibaraki (Osaka): Activities and Tours

Ibaraki offers visitors several activities that will make their stay worthwhile. Below, you will find some of the best things to do in Ibaraki:

  • Ibaraki Sarusawa Ice Skating Rink: A perfect spot for winter sports enthusiasts. The ice skating rink is open in winters and can accommodate several individuals.
  • Osaka Pasta Making Class & Gyoza Lunch: Visitors can enjoy making their pasta and dumplings from scratch and enjoy them afterward.
  • Osaka Pub Crawl: A perfect opportunity to explore Osaka’s nightlife with other travelers and locals. The crawl takes visitors through different local bars, restaurants and gives visitors a chance to interact with the locals.

Food and Drink Scene of Ibaraki (Osaka)

Ibaraki boasts a vast array of dining options that offer both Japanese and international cuisine. Visitors can find everything from traditional Japanese dishes to international fusion cuisine in the numerous restaurants, cafes, and bars scattered around the city.

Must-Try Dishes

Below, you will find some of the must-try dishes in Ibaraki:

  • Okonomiyaki: A savory Japanese pancake that is a must-try when visiting Osaka.
  • Takoyaki: A ball-shaped snack filled with diced octopus, pickled ginger, and green onion.
  • Soba Noodles: A traditional buckwheat noodle dish that is served piping hot in a broth made from soy sauce.

Local Beverages

Ibaraki’s nightlife is vibrant, and visitors can enjoy a range of drinks, both alcoholic and non-alcoholic. Below are some of the popular drinks in Ibaraki:

  • Sake: A traditional Japanese alcoholic drink made from fermented rice.
  • Umeshu: A plum wine that is sweet and sour, often served as an aperitif.
  • Matcha Latte: A matcha flavored latte made by pouring hot milk over whipped matcha powder.

Where to Stay in Ibaraki (Osaka): Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Ibaraki has several accommodation options, including hotels, hostels, and guesthouses. Visitors can choose from different price ranges and styles of accommodation. A few of the best places to stay include:

  • Osaka Fukishima House: Located in the heart of Osaka, offering affordable accommodation to visitors.
  • Arietta Hotel: A traditional Japanese hotel located close to Ibaraki’s natural parks.
  • Sakura House Osaka: A guesthouse situated close to the city center and local attractions.

What to Buy in Ibaraki (Osaka): Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Visitors can get a taste of Ibaraki’s culture by bringing home some of the local souvenirs and products. Below are some of the items that are worth purchasing:

  • Hankyu Umeda Fountain: A miniature version of the famous Hankyu Umeda station fountain.
  • Tsukemono (pickles): A famous local delicacy that visitors can take home to enjoy.
  • Takoyaki Pan Set: Visitors can purchase a set of takoyaki pans, which are a great way to bring a piece of Osaka home.

Local Culture and Customs of Ibaraki (Osaka)

Ibaraki’s culture is deeply rooted in its traditions and customs, which make up its daily activities. Some of Ibaraki’s local culture includes:

  • Famous festivals: Ibaraki hosts many festivals throughout the year, celebrating its rich cultural heritage.
  • The tea ceremony: A traditional Japanese ceremony that involves the ritualistic preparation and consumption of tea.
  • Sake brewing: Ibaraki is known for its sake brewing, which visitors can learn about and even taste when visiting.

Best Time to Visit Ibaraki (Osaka)

The best time to visit Ibaraki is during late spring to autumn when the weather is pleasant and mild. During this time, visitors can enjoy the natural scenery, outdoor activities without being hindered by the harsh winters.

Do’s and Don’ts of Ibaraki (Osaka)

Do:

  • Respect the traditional customs and culture of Ibaraki.
  • Dress appropriately when visiting religious sites.
  • Learn basic Japanese phrases to interact with the locals.

Don’t:

  • Litter the streets or natural parks.
  • Make excessive noise or disrupt the tranquil environment of Ibaraki.
  • Eat or drink in public spaces.

Safety and Health

Safety in Ibaraki (Osaka)

Ibaraki is a relatively safe city, with minimal criminal activity. Visitors can wander about the city at ease without worrying about their safety.

Health in Ibaraki (Osaka)

Ibaraki’s health system is relatively efficient, with a range of hospitals and clinics catering to the local population’s health needs. Visitors are advised to purchase travel insurance before visiting the city.

Getting to Ibaraki (Osaka): Transportation Options and Logistics

Ibaraki is easily accessible by various modes of transportation.

Airports Near Ibaraki (Osaka)

The Kansai International Airport is the nearest international airport to Ibaraki. Visitors can take a shuttle or taxi to reach their accommodation in the city.

Bus Stations Near Ibaraki (Osaka)

There are numerous bus stations in Ibaraki, making travel within the city a breeze.

Train Stations Near Ibaraki (Osaka)

Ibaraki has several train stations, including Hankyu-Ibaraki Station and Osaka Metro Midosuji Line Ibaraki-shi Station, making it easy to explore the city via train.

How to Get Around in Ibaraki (Osaka)

The most convenient modes of transportation in Ibaraki are buses and taxis. Visitors can purchase a one-day bus pass to explore the city’s different attractions.

Where to Go Next from Ibaraki (Osaka): Nearby Destinations and Excursions

After exploring Ibaraki, visitors can explore nearby destinations such as:

  • Kyoto, the ancient capital of Japan.
  • Nara, famous for its numerous UNESCO World Heritage sites, including Todai-Ji temple and Kasuga-Taisha shrine.

Conclusion

Ibaraki is a charming city that is worth exploring. Its rich cultural heritage and natural attractions make it an ideal place for people seeking a break from their busy life. Visitors can enjoy everything from outdoor adventures, cultural sites and festivals to dining in numerous restaurants and cafes. We hope this travel guide will help visitors plan their trip to Ibaraki (Osaka) and make their stay memorable.
