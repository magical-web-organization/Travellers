Introduction to Tennoji

Tennoji is a vibrant neighborhood located in the southern part of Osaka, Japan. It’s a historical and cultural hub, featuring an eclectic array of attractions, outdoor activities, and delicious food. The area is also famous for its access to transportation, making it a popular destination for both locals and tourists. Tennoji is home to the Tennoji Park, the Tennoji Zoo, Shitenno-ji Temple, and numerous shopping centers, department stores, and restaurants.

Tennoji is accessible via the JR Osaka Loop Line and the Osaka Municipal Subway. Visitors to the area will find an endless array of things to see and do, from exploring historical sites and museums to experiencing the frenetic energy of the entertainment districts. This comprehensive guide provides an overview of the best things to see and do in Tennoji.

List of Places to Visit in Tennoji

Tennoji offers an impressive variety of cultural and natural attractions, each with its unique charm and appeal. Here is a list of must-visit places in Tennoji.

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Shitenno-ji Temple

Shitenno-ji Temple is the oldest officially administered temple in Japan, with a history that dates back over 1,400 years. Located in Tennoji, the temple’s stunning design and beautiful grounds make it an ideal destination for visitors interested in Buddhist architecture and culture. The temple is home to a number of historic buildings, including a five-story pagoda, a central gate, and an archive museum.

Osaka City Museum of Fine Arts

The Osaka City Museum of Fine Arts is the second-largest art museum in Osaka Prefecture. It features a collection of over 8,000 works of art, including paintings, sculptures, and other fine arts. The museum also hosts regular exhibitions from local and international artists, making it a must-visit destination for art lovers.

Natural Attractions of Tennoji: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Tennoji Park

Tennoji Park is a delightful spot for picnicking, jogging, or taking a leisurely stroll. It’s also home to the Tennoji Zoo, which houses over 1,000 animals from around the world. Visitors to the park can enjoy a variety of activities, like feeding animals at the zoo, taking a walk through the seasonal flower gardens or enjoy a nice picnic with a stunning view of the Osaka skyline.

Harukas Observation Deck

Harukas Observation Deck is the tallest building in Japan, standing at 300 meters tall. It provides panoramic views of Osaka from the 58th floor, making it an ideal location for sunset watching. The observation deck also offers several restaurants and cafes to enjoy the view while having delicious food.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Tennoji

Tennoji has many hiking and biking routes that are perfect for outdoor enthusiasts. Here are some of the best options.

Abeno Harukas Twilight Walk

The Abeno Harukas Twilight Walk is a hiking route that takes in the stunning views of Osaka by night. The trail starts at the base of the Abeno Harukas building and follows a well-marked path up to the observation deck. Along the way, visitors can enjoy the city’s beautiful nighttime scenery and fresh air.

Tennoji Park Cycling Route

Tennoji Park’s cycling path is the perfect way to explore the expansive park, boasting breathtaking views of the city, natural beauty, and pleasant wildlife sightings. The path circles the park, with various landmarks and lookouts along the way. Hop on a bicycle and enjoy the sights, sounds, and smells of Tennoji in a relaxing, unhurried pace.

Best Things to Do in Tennoji: Activities and Tours

Tennoji is a bustling city with an exciting variety of activities and tours. Here are some of our top picks:

Shop Local at Tennoji Station.

Tennoji Station is a train station that doubles as a shopping destination. Visitors can find a plethora of shops, including clothing stores, convenience stores and various restaurant options. The station is also home to several department stores, featuring traditional Japanese souvenirs and food.

Take a Walk through Tennoji Park

Tennoji Park is a sprawling oasis, located right in the middle of the city. There are things to see and do all year round, making it an ideal destination for sightseeing, picnicking or strolling. The park is home to several historical landmarks, such as the Tennoji Zoo and the Museum of Fine Arts.

Week-Long Itinerary for Tennoji

Tennoji is a vibrant and action-packed neighborhood. For those who want to see and do it all, you can spend a whole week in Tennoji. Here is a suggested itinerary.

Day 1: Explore Shitenno-ji Temple

Take a stroll through Shitenno-ji Temple and pay homage to the country’s oldest temple. The temple is peaceful and serene, with a lot of historical significance.

Day 2: Hit the Shops

Spend your second day exploring the commercial district of Tennoji, with plenty of department stores and central shopping centers. This area is full of international brands, making it a popular location for shoppers to get souvenirs, accessories and clothes.

Day 3: Tennoji Zoo and Park

Spend your third day at the Tennoji Zoo and Park, home to many animals from around the world. Visitors can enjoy a picnic after a quick visit to the zoo at the park, bringing their food to the large open grass areas.

Day 4: Osaka City Museum of Fine Arts

Osaka City Museum of Fine Arts is a beautifully curated collection of a range of art styles, and artwork. It is home to work from some of the best-known artists across generations.

Day 5: Harukas Observation Deck

The Harukas Observation deck offers great panoramic views of the city, perfect for finding an ideal spot for sunset watching.

Day 6: Osaka Castle

Explore the architectural marvel of the Osaka Castle while getting to know its history. The castle features stunning views of the city from its towers, and visitors can learn about Japanese history as they explore the castle.

Day 7: Dōtonbori

Dōtonbori is Osaka’s famous entertainment district. Spend the day visiting cafes and restaurants or try local cuisine such as takoyaki, grilled squid, or Okonomiyaki.

Food and Drink Scene of Tennoji

Must-Try Dishes

Osaka is famous for its delicious street food scene. So, the top dishes to try include:

Takoyaki

Takoyaki is a street food staple in Osaka, made up of small balls of wheat flour with a piece of octopus inside, all mixed with green onions, and garnished with sweet sauce and mayonnaise. Super fluffy and flavorful.

Okonomiyaki

Okonomiyaki is a savory pancake served with a wide array of ingredients, from meat to vegetables, eggs, seafood, and even cheese. The dish is then topped with various sauces and mayonnaise, making it a unique and truly delicious option.

Local Beverages

Osaka’s drinking culture is both social and exciting, and there are many local beverages to try, from Japanese sake to beer and even unique cocktails.

Local Culture and Customs of Tennoji

Local culture in Tennoji includes practices that reflect the city’s rich history and traditions. Osaka’s motto is Kuidaore, which means “eat until you drop.” A lot of Osaka’s cuisine is street food, meant to keep people energized and always on the go. Visitors should maintain a sense of respect for local customs, traditions, and culture during their stay in Tennoji.

Best Time to Visit Tennoji

The best time to visit Tennoji is during the spring season when seasonal flowers like sakura and wisteria bloom. But travelers can visit throughout the year due to its moderate climate.

Do’s and Don’ts of Tennoji

It’s essential to follow guidelines and practice etiquette when visiting Tennoji.

Do:

  • Bow when you greet someone
  • Say thank you and excuse me.
  • Try Japanese food, especially the street food which is both cheap and delicious

Don’t:

  • Carry food or drink in public spaces.
  • Litter
  • Be overly loud or talk with a loud voice in public.

Safety and Health

Tennoji is generally safe. Visitors should take basic precautions such as safeguarding their belongings and keeping an eye on their bags.

Safety in Tennoji

Pickpocketing can happen in crowded areas or popular landmarks. Visitors should avoid solo trips to certain areas of the city, which can be dangerous at night.

Health in Tennoji

Visitors should always carry health insurance or travel insurance when visiting Tennoji, and take extra precautions when it comes to COVID-19 as per the guidelines offered by WHO and the Japanese government.

Getting to Tennoji: Transportation Options and Logistics

Traveling to Tennoji is easy due to its sheer variety of transportation options.

Airports Near Tennoji

The Kansai International Airport, located about 50 kilometers away from the city, is the closest international airport serving Tennoji. Visitors can also travel to Osaka using Osaka International Airport, located just a few minutes away from the city center.

Bus Stations Near Tennoji

Osaka’s extensive network of bus routes readily serves Tennoji. Visitors can opt to travel by intercity buses or take the airport shuttles to avoid city traffic.

Other Options

Tennoji is well-connected to the Osaka Municipal Subway line, with numerous stops in the area, such as the Tennoji Station.

How to Get Around in Tennoji

Tennoji is a walkable city but also has several transportation options. Visitors can move by trains, buses, taxis, or bicycles. The buses allow for better mobility, while the train system is fast and efficient. Taxis are also a good way to move around but can be a bit expensive.

Where to Go Next from Tennoji: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Tennoji is well-connected to some of the best nearby destinations, including the following:

  • Namba, a bustling shopping and entertainment district
  • Dotonbori, the food capital of Osaka
  • Shinsekai, a historical neighborhood known for its traditional atmosphere.

Conclusion

With so much to see and do, Tennoji is an ideal destination for those who love traveling and discovering something new. The area’s diverse attractions and natural beauty make it an excellent choice for travelers looking to enjoy the best of Osaka. Understanding local customs, exploring the city’s cultural centers, and tasting the culinary delights are some of the highlights that make Tennoji an unforgettable destination.
