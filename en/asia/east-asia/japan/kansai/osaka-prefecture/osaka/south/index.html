Introduction to South

South, located in Osaka (prefecture), in the Kansai region of Japan, is a wonderful destination for travelers looking to immerse themselves in Japanese culture and history while also enjoying outdoor adventures and scenic views. This area of Osaka is known for its rich cultural heritage and natural beauty, offering visitors a unique mix of urban and rural experiences.

In this comprehensive guide to South, we will cover everything you need to know to plan your trip, including the top attractions, activities, accommodations, local culture, and more.

List of Places to Visit in South

South is a diverse region with a range of attractions to suit different preferences. Here are some top places to add to your itinerary:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • The Museum of Oriental Ceramics: This museum is dedicated to traditional Japanese ceramics from the 6th to 14th centuries.
  • Osaka Museum of History: Learn about the history of Osaka and its people through interactive exhibits and displays.
  • Daisen Park: A beautiful park with a castle, Japanese garden, and a museum displaying the history of the castle.

Natural Attractions of South: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Sumiyoshi Taisha Shrine: One of Japan’s oldest shrines with a beautiful garden.
  • Sakaihama Beach Park: An excellent place to take a walk along the beach and enjoy scenic views of Osaka Bay.
  • Minoo Park: A place to enjoy hiking and see the famous Minoo Waterfall.
  • The Summit: A popular place for skiing and snowboarding during winter, and hiking during summer.

Hiking/Biking Routes of South

South is home to some of the best hiking and biking routes in Japan. Here are some routes that you should consider:

  • Koya Park: A great place to hike and bike, experiencing the beauty of the region’s nature and culture.
  • Kumano Kodo Trail: An ancient pilgrimage route with stunning scenery and historic sites.
  • Osakajo Park: Offers beautiful views from the park and great scenery for walking and cycling.

Best Things to Do in South: Activities and Tours

  • Experience Traditional Japanese Tea Ceremony: South is home to many tea houses where you can experience a traditional Japanese tea ceremony.
  • Try Local Food at Street Vendors: Street vendors are a must-visit in Osaka, and South has some of the best food to offer.
  • Take a Walk Through Famous Universal Studios Japan: Universal Studios Japan theme park is located in South and has many rides and attractions for both adults and children.
  • Historical Bus Tour of Temples and Shrines: Take a historical bus tour to explore some of the famous temples and shrines in South.

Week-Long Itinerary for South

Here is a suggested week-long itinerary to help you maximize your trip to South:

Day Activity
Day 1 Visit the Osaka Museum of History and enjoy a traditional Japanese tea ceremony in a nearby tea house.
Day 2 Explore the Sumiyoshi Taisha Shrine and its beautiful garden, followed by a street vendor food tour.
Day 3 Hike in Minoo Park to see the famous Minoo Waterfall, then enjoy a hot spring bath at a nearby ryokan.
Day 4 Take a bus tour of historical temples and shrines in South, including the famous Shitennoji and Isshinji Temples.
Day 5 Visit the Museum of Oriental Ceramics and experience the beauty of Koya Park.
Day 6 Take a trip to Universal Studios Japan for a day of entertainment and fun.
Day 7 Relax at Sakaihama Beach Park and enjoy the beautiful views of Osaka Bay.

Food and Drink Scene of South

One of the best things about South is its delicious local cuisine. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages to add to your foodie tour:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Takoyaki: A famous Osakan dish made of octopus and batter, shaped into small balls and grilled on a hot plate.
  • Okonomiyaki: A Japanese-style pancake made with various ingredients topped with savory sauces.
  • Kushikatsu: Deep-fried skewered meat, seafood, or vegetables coated in a crispy bread crumb batter.

Local Beverages

  • Saké: A Japanese rice wine that’s a must-try while visiting Japan, especially in South.
  • Shochu: A Japanese distilled liquor that comes in many flavors and is often paired with sashimi or sushi.

Where to Stay in South: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

South has numerous accommodation options, including hotels, hostels, and guesthouses. Here are some top options to consider:

  • Hotel Sunroute Sakai: A modern and comfortable hotel located near major attractions in South.
  • Hostel Mundo: A budget-friendly hostel with excellent amenities, including a game room and a shared kitchen.
  • Guesthouse Nonno Sakai: A cozy guesthouse with a homely feel and a beautiful garden in a quiet neighborhood.

What to Buy in South: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Don’t forget to bring some local souvenirs from South to bring back with you. Some popular souvenirs include:

  • Sakai Knives: Handcrafted knives from Sakai that are known for their quality and precision.
  • Tenugui: A type of Japanese hand towel, often used as decoration or as a gift.
  • Baranashi: A traditional Japanese cloth used for wrapping and storing ceramics.

Local Culture and Customs of South

Here are some tips to help you understand the local culture and customs of South:

  • Take Your Shoes Off: It’s common to take your shoes off when entering homes, temples, and some restaurants in Japan.
  • Bow Instead of Handshake: A bow is the traditional Japanese greeting, and it’s a sign of respect.
  • Don’t Tip: Tipping is not customary in Japan, and it’s sometimes considered rude.

Best Time to Visit South

The best time to visit South is in the spring (March to May) or fall (September to November) when the weather is mild and pleasant. The cherry blossoms in the Osaka Castle Park are particularly beautiful in the spring.

Do’s and Don’ts of South

Here are some do’s and don’ts to keep in mind during your stay in South:

Do:

  • Try Local Food: South has amazing street vendors that offer lots of delicious local dishes.
  • Experience Japanese culture: Join in traditional experiences like tea ceremonies, hot spring baths, and other activities.

Don’t:

  • Be Loud: It is polite to keep your voice down, even when in public places.
  • Take Calls on Public Transportation: It is considered bad manners to take phone calls or talk loudly while on public transportation.

Safety and Health

Here are some tips to ensure your safety and health while visiting South:

Safety in South

  • Carry Cash: Have some cash on you at all times, as some restaurants and shops may not accept credit cards.
  • Stay in Safe Areas: Be aware of your surroundings and avoid secluded areas at night.

Health in South

  • Japan Rail Pass: Purchase a Japan Rail Pass before you arrive in the country, which offers unlimited travel on most rail lines.
  • Small Portions: While enjoying street vendor food or eating out, it’s best to eat small portions to avoid overeating.

Getting to South: Transportation Options and Logistics

South is easily accessible from Osaka’s main transportation hubs, including Osaka International Airport and Osaka Station. Here are some transportation options:

Airports Near South

  • Osaka International Airport: This airport is just 20-minutes away from South by train or taxi.

Bus Stations Near South

  • Kongo Bus Station: This bus station is located in Kongo, a town in South and offers bus services to various destinations.

How to Get Around in South

Public transportation in South is efficient and convenient, with buses and trains covering the region. You can also rent a car if you prefer to explore the area on your own.

Where to Go Next from South: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

If you want to explore other parts of Japan, here are some nearby destinations and excursions:

  • Osaka: Osaka is a bustling city with lots to see and do, including a famous aquarium, a giant Ferris wheel, and lots of street food vendors.
  • Kobe: The city of Kobe is known for its excellent beef, stunning views of the sea and mountains, and beautiful parks.
  • Kyoto: This ancient capital city of Japan is famous for its many temples, shrines, and traditional architecture.

Conclusion

South, Osaka, is a beautiful region in Japan that offers an excellent mix of culture, history, outdoor adventure, and scenic views. We hope this comprehensive guide helps you plan your trip and make the most of your stay in South. Happy travels!
