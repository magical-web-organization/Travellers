Introduction to Shikokuchuo

Shikokuchuo is a small yet beautiful town located in the Ehime prefecture of Shikoku, Japan. It is a hidden gem that offers a unique blend of cultural and natural attractions, making it a must-visit destination. The town’s small size ensures a relaxed and peaceful atmosphere, meaning it is the perfect place to escape from the hustle and bustle of the city. The town is famous for its local cuisine, natural hot springs, and cultural landmarks.

List of Places to Visit in Shikokuchuo

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Shikokuchuo has a rich cultural history that dates back to the Edo period. The town is home to several museums and historical places that showcase the town’s traditional arts and crafts. A must-visit attraction is the Shikokuchuo Folk Museum, which features exhibits highlighting the town’s history and culture. Other cultural landmarks include the Shikokuchuo Shrine, which is famous for its elaborate architecture and intricate details, and the Tsuji Moriya Memorial Museum, a tribute to the renowned artist whose art is featured throughout the town.

Natural Attractions of Shikokuchuo: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Shikokuchuo is surrounded by picturesque mountains providing striking natural scenery. Visitors can take a hike or bike through the countryside and enjoy breathtaking views from its peaks. One of the most popular spots is the Komatsu-Hirano Waterfall, which features a charming landscape and crystal-clear water. Visitors can also explore the lush greenery of the Hoso River Park and the Ozuchijuku valley.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Shikokuchuo

Shikokuchuo is a paradise for outdoor adventure enthusiasts. The town’s community promotes outdoor activities, and there are several hiking/biking trails perfect for exploring the surrounding countryside. Some of the most popular trails include the Shirakuchi Hiking Trail and the Oku-Iyo-Michi Trail, both offering fantastic views of the town’s natural beauty.

Best Things to Do in Shikokuchuo: Activities and Tours

Visitors to Shikokuchuo are not short of things to do. They can participate in a traditional tea ceremony or experience the town’s famous hot springs, known for their therapeutic benefits. Other popular activities include attending a pottery-making class at one of the local studios or exploring the town’s traditional architecture on a guided walking tour. For those seeking an adrenaline rush, Shikokuchuo offers whitewater rafting, kayaking and paragliding.

Week-Long Itinerary for Shikokuchuo

Day 1: Arrive at Shikokuchuo and explore the town’s cultural landmarks, including the Shikokuchuo Folk Museum and the Tsuji Moriya Memorial Museum.

Day 2: Hike through the scenic trails of the Shirakuchi Hiking Trail and Oku-Iyo-Michi Trail.

Day 3: Relax at one of the town’s hot springs or attend a pottery-making class.

Day 4: Take a guided tour of the town’s traditional architecture and visit the Shikokuchuo Shrine.

Day 5: Participate in a tea ceremony or try whitewater rafting on the Yoshino River.

Day 6: Visit the Komatsu-Hirano Waterfall and enjoy the scenic views of the Ozuchijuku valley.

Day 7: Depart from Shikokuchuo and continue your journey to other exciting parts of Japan.

Food and Drink Scene of Shikokuchuo

Must-Try Dishes

Shikokuchuo is known for its unique and exotic cuisine. Visitors should try tasting dish like Saba-zushi (mackerel sushi), Aoyaki (green pancakes), Iyo Jidori (local chicken) and various sea food dishes, including clams and seaweed.

Local Beverages

The town is famous for its local sake, Iyo-shochu and Mitsuya cider, which is an ideal refreshment after a long day’s activities.

Where to Stay in Shikokuchuo: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Shikokuchuo offers a wide range of accommodation options, including hotels, hostels, and guesthouses. Visitors can choose between traditional or modern accommodation facilities, catering to all their needs. One of the best places to stay is the traditional Japanese Ryokan Taiyo no Sato, which offers excellent lodging facilities, comfortable rooms with traditional bedding and magnificent views of the surrounding mountains.

What to Buy in Shikokuchuo: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Shikokuchuo is famous for its pottery and ceramics, made by skilled artisans in the town. Visitors can buy souvenirs such as handcrafted mugs, bowls, and plates, or try there hand at making pottery at the local studios. Other items include traditional Japanese crafts, handmade baskets and textiles.

Local Culture and Customs of Shikokuchuo

Shikokuchuo has a unique culture and customs that are deeply rooted in its historical and spiritual heritage. Visitors are encouraged to learn about and respect these customs, including proper manners at the hot springs or when visiting religious sites.

Best Time to Visit Shikokuchuo

The best time to visit Shikokuchuo is from March to early May, when the cherry blossoms are in full bloom, creating a stunning atmosphere all around. Another great time to visit is during autumn between September to November when the foliage turns into beautiful hues of red, yellow, and orange.

Do’s and Don’ts of Shikokuchuo

Do:

  • Respect the local customs and beliefs.
  • Try the local food and drinks.
  • Be courteous when using the hot springs and other public facilities.

Don’t:

  • Litter in public places.
  • Enter places with shoes on, especially inside homes or traditional establishments.
  • Disrespect any religious sites.

Safety and Health

Safety in Shikokuchuo

Shikokuchuo is a relatively safe place with low crime rates. However, visitors should still take precautions like locking doors and not leaving valuables in plain sight.

Health in Shikokuchuo

Visitors need not worry about health concerns in Shikokuchuo. The town’s medical facilities are well equipped to handle any emergencies. Visitors should also take note of any allergies or dietary restrictions before consuming the town’s food and drinks

Getting to Shikokuchuo: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Shikokuchuo

The nearest airport is Matsuyama airport, which is situated about 95 kilometers from Shikokuchuo. Visitors can take a taxi or a bus from outside the airport terminal to Shikokuchuo.

Bus Stations Near Shikokuchuo

There are several long-distance bus lines that connect Shikokuchuo with other Japanese cities, namely Matsuyama and Kochi. Visitors can board a direct bus from these bus stations and ferries to Shikokuchuo.

Other Options

Visitors can rent a car or motorcycle to explore the surrounding area. Additionally, some tour operators offer guided tours that cover the town’s most exciting sites.

How to Get Around in Shikokuchuo

The best way to explore the town is on foot or by bicycle. Visitors can rent bikes from local stores or hotels. Taxis are also available for quick and easy transportation. Cars can be rented for day trips outside of Shikokuchuo.

Where to Go Next from Shikokuchuo: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Visitors can explore other towns and cities in Shikoku, such as Matsuyama, Kochi, and Tokushima, known for their unique cultural and natural attractions. A popular excursion is to take the ferry to the nearby island of Oshima, famous for its stunning beaches and surfing spots.

Conclusion

Shikokuchuo is a hidden gem that offers a unique blend of cultural and natural attractions. The town’s serene atmosphere, rich cultural history, and breathtaking scenery make it the perfect destination for nature lovers, outdoor adventurers, and culture enthusiasts alike. A trip to Shikokuchuo is sure to be a memorable experience, and visitors should take the time to explore everything that this charming town has to offer.
