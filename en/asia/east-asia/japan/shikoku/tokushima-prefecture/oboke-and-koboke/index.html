Introduction to Oboke and Koboke

Oboke and Koboke are two picturesque gorges located in the Tokushima Prefecture, one of the four prefectures in Shikoku Island, Japan. The gorges are famous for their crystal-clear waters, rugged cliffs, and stunning rock formations. Oboke and Koboke offer a unique blend of natural beauty and cultural attractions, making them a popular destination for both domestic and international travelers.

List of Places to Visit in Oboke and Koboke

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Iya Valley

Iya Valley is one of the most beautiful and remote areas in Japan, located in the western part of Tokushima. It is known for its mountainous landscapes and historic sites such as the Ochiai Village, where ancient Japanese architecture is still preserved. Iya Valley is also home to the famous vine bridge, which is a suspended bridge made of vines, and the Udatsu Old Street, a preserved merchant street from the Edo Period.

Oboke Gorge Sightseeing Boat Tour

Take a relaxing boat ride through the Oboke Gorge and see the beautiful scenery of the cliffs and towering rocks. The tour takes around 30 minutes and is the perfect way to admire the natural beauty of the gorge.

Koboke Gorge Hiking

For adventure seekers, a challenging hike through the Koboke Gorge is a great way to experience the natural beauty of the area. The hiking trail is around 8 km long and offers stunning views of the river and surrounding mountains.

Mount Tsurugi

Mount Tsurugi is the second-highest peak on Shikoku Island and is popular among hikers for its stunning panoramic views of the surrounding areas. The mountain has several hiking trails, ranging from beginner to advanced levels.

Natural Attractions of Oboke and Koboke: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Iya-no-Kazurabashi Vine Bridge

Located near the Ochiai Village in the Iya Valley, the Iya-no-Kazurabashi Vine Bridge is a unique suspension bridge made of vines. The bridge is about 45 meters long and is suspended high above the Iya River. Crossing the bridge is a thrilling experience and a must-try for adventure enthusiasts.

Oboke and Koboke Rivers

The Oboke and Koboke rivers are two of the most beautiful rivers in Japan. The clear water, surrounded by towering cliffs, creates a breathtaking landscape that is perfect for canoeing or rafting. The rivers also offer fishing opportunities for those who enjoy angling.

Kazura-bashi (Iya Valley) Vine Suspension Bridge

The Kazura-bashi Vine Suspension Bridge is another iconic attraction in the Iya Valley. The bridge is made entirely of vines and stretches over the Iya River. It is 45 meters long and 2 meters wide, and it sways gently as you cross over it. Walking on the bridge provides beautiful views of the surrounding valley.

Iya-no-Kazurabashi Vined Bridge

The Iya-no-Kazurabashi Vined Bridge is one of the most popular attractions in Oboke and Koboke. The bridge is made entirely of vines and spans across the Iya River. It is known for its incredible views and unique structure.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Oboke and Koboke

Oboke and Koboke are known for their hiking and biking trails, which provide beautiful views of the surrounding landscape.

Oboke Gorge Walking Trail

The Oboke Gorge walking trail is a 1.2 km long trail that offers beautiful views of the gorge. The trail is well maintained and easy to navigate, making it a great option for families with children or beginner hikers.

Koboke Gorge Hiking Trail

The Koboke Gorge hiking trail is an 8 km long trail that offers stunning views of the valley and river. The trail is of moderate difficulty, with steep inclines and rocky terrain. Hikers should come prepared with sturdy footwear and plenty of water.

Cycling in Oboke and Koboke

Rent a bicycle and cycle the scenic routes of Oboke and Koboke. The area has several bike rental shops, and the routes are well-marked and maintained.

Best Things to Do in Oboke and Koboke: Activities and Tours

Rafting in the Rivers

The Oboke and Koboke rivers provide some of the best rafting opportunities in Japan. Several tour companies offer half-day or full-day rafting tours that cater to both beginners and experienced rafters.

Canyoning

Canyoning is a fun and exhilarating activity that involves navigating through canyons by walking, climbing, and jumping. In Oboke and Koboke, several tour companies offer canyoning tours that explore the gorges and waterfalls in the area.

Hot Spring Bathing

The Tokushima Prefecture is famous for its hot springs or onsen. There are several onsen resorts in Oboke and Koboke that offer a relaxing experience after a day of activities and tours.

Week-Long Itinerary for Oboke and Koboke

Day 1: Explore the Oboke Gorge and take a boat tour.

Day 2: Hike the Koboke Gorge hiking trail or bike the scenic routes of Oboke and Koboke.

Day 3: Visit Iya Valley and walk across the Iya-no-Kazurabashi Vine Bridge.

Day 4: Go rafting or canyoning in the Oboke and Koboke rivers.

Day 5: Climb Mt. Tsurugi for stunning panoramic views of the area.

Day 6: Relax and unwind at an onsen resort in the area.

Day 7: Shop for souvenirs and local products to bring back home.

Food and Drink Scene of Oboke and Koboke

Must-Try Dishes

Tairyomushi Soba

Tairyomushi Soba is a popular dish in Oboke and Koboke. It is a bowl of buckwheat noodles topped with a variety of fresh vegetables, mushrooms, and locally sourced proteins.

Iya Konnyaku

Iya Konnyaku is a traditional dish made from the konjac plant, native to the Iya Valley. The thick and chewy noodles are served with a variety of dipping sauces and toppings such as grated radish or soy sauce.

Local Beverages

Awashima Sake

Awashima Sake is a high-quality sake made in Tokushima Prefecture. The sake is known for its smooth texture and fruity flavors.

Sudachi Juice

Sudachi Juice is a refreshing citrus drink made from sudachi fruits, a specialty of the Tokushima Prefecture. The juice is tart and tangy, making it a great accompaniment to meals or as a refreshing drink on a hot day.

Where to Stay in Oboke and Koboke: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

There are several accommodation options in Oboke and Koboke, ranging from budget-friendly hostels to luxurious onsen resorts.

Budget-Friendly Hostels

Oboke Hostel

Oboke Hostel is a modern and stylish hostel in the heart of Oboke. The hostel offers both dorm rooms and private rooms, a communal kitchen, and a lounge area.

Iya Valley Youth Hostel

Iya Valley Youth Hostel is a charming hostel located in the Iya Valley. The hostel offers dorm rooms, private rooms, a communal kitchen, and a cozy lounge area.

Mid-Range Hotels

Pin-pon-na

Pin-pon-na is a cozy and comfortable hotel located near the Oboke Gorge. The hotel offers both Japanese-style and Western-style rooms, a restaurant, and a hot spring bath.

Hotel de Urashima

Hotel de Urashima is a luxurious hotel located near the Koboke Gorge. The hotel offers spacious and elegant rooms, a wide range of amenities, and a hot spring bath.

What to Buy in Oboke and Koboke: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Iya Knives

Iya Knives are a traditional Japanese knife made in the Tokushima Prefecture. The knives are made using ancient forging techniques and are known for their sharpness and durability.

Iya Weaving

Iya Weaving is a traditional Japanese textile made by weaving together natural fibers such as cotton and silk. The intricate patterns and designs of the textiles are unique to the Iya Valley and make for great souvenirs.

Local Culture and Customs of Oboke and Koboke

Respect for Nature

Oboke and Koboke have a deep respect for nature and the environment. Visitors are encouraged to follow this philosophy by not littering or damaging the pristine natural surroundings.

Buddhist Temples

Buddhism is an important part of the local culture in Oboke and Koboke. Visitors are encouraged to visit the numerous Buddhist temples and shrines in the area.

Best Time to Visit Oboke and Koboke

The best time to visit Oboke and Koboke is from April to October when the weather is mild and pleasant. The spring and fall seasons are particularly beautiful, with vibrant foliage and blooming flowers.

Do’s and Don’ts of Oboke and Koboke

Do:

  • Respect the local culture and customs.
  • Dress appropriately when visiting temples and shrines.
  • Carry a water bottle when hiking or biking.
  • Try the local cuisine and beverages.

Don’t:

  • Litter or damage the natural environment.
  • Wear shoes into temples and shrines.
  • Ignore safety precautions when participating in outdoor activities.

Safety and Health

Safety in Oboke and Koboke

Oboke and Koboke are generally safe areas for visitors. However, visitors should be aware of their surroundings and take necessary precautions when participating in outdoor activities such as hiking, rafting or canyoning.

Health in Oboke and Koboke

Japan has an excellent healthcare system, and visitors can access quality medical care in Oboke and Koboke. Travelers should carry medical insurance and prescription medications when visiting.

Getting to Oboke and Koboke: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Oboke and Koboke

The closest airport to Oboke and Koboke is Tokushima Airport. The airport is around a 90-minute drive from Oboke and Koboke, and several shuttle bus services are available from the airport.

Bus Stations Near Oboke and Koboke

There are several bus stations near Oboke and Koboke, including the JR Oboke Station, the JR Awa-Ikeda Station, and the Sadamitsu Bus Terminal. Several bus routes operate between these stations and the surrounding areas.

Other Options

Visitors can also rent a car or hire a private taxi to travel to Oboke and Koboke. This is a good option for visitors who want to explore the area at their own pace.

How to Get Around in Oboke and Koboke

The best way to get around in Oboke and Koboke is by car or by bicycle. Several bike rental shops are available in the area, and the roads are well-maintained and marked. Visitors can also take advantage of the local bus services, which operate between the major tourist destinations in the area.

Where to Go Next from Oboke and Koboke: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Naruto Whirlpools

The Naruto Whirlpools are a natural phenomenon caused by the tides and the narrow strait between the Naruto and Awaji Islands. Visitors can take a boat tour to see the whirlpools up close.

Ritsurin Garden

Ritsurin Garden is a beautiful Japanese garden located in Takamatsu City, Kagawa Prefecture. The garden is known for its unique landscaping, tea houses, and stone bridges.

Conclusion

Oboke and Koboke are two of the most beautiful and unique destinations in Japan. The area offers a perfect blend of natural beauty and cultural attractions, making it a must-visit destination for travelers looking for an authentic Japanese experience. With a wide range of activities, attractions, and accommodation options, Oboke and Koboke are the perfect destination for a memorable vacation.
