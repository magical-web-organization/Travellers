Introduction to Iya Valley

Nestled in the mountains of Tokushima prefecture, on the island of Shikoku in Japan, lies a hidden gem called Iya Valley. Known for its magnificent natural beauty, rich cultural history, and outdoor adventures, Iya Valley offers an escape from the hustle and bustle of urban living. Visitors to this secluded paradise can expect to be awe-inspired by its lush green forests, gorges, and rivers. In this guide, we will explore the best attractions, things to do and see, accommodations, and transport options for your next visit to Iya Valley.

List of Places to Visit in Iya Valley

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

History buffs and culture enthusiasts will appreciate the wide range of museums and historical sites that Iya Valley has to offer. Some of the must-visit attractions include:

  • Ochiai Village: A secluded village located deep in the mountains of Iya Valley. Here, you can get a glimpse of traditional Japanese culture where the village’s inhabitants still wear traditional attire.
  • Iya Castle Ruins: Located on a hill overlooking the valley, this castle was built some 400 years ago.
  • Oku-Iya Niju Kazura Bridge: This vine bridge was used by the local people for centuries to access the neighboring villages.

Natural Attractions of Iya Valley: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Iya Valley is a haven for nature lovers and outdoor enthusiasts. Some of the must-visit natural attractions include:

  • Iya-no-Kazurabashi: Voted as one of Japan’s three most beautiful bridges, this vine bridge is a must-visit attraction in Iya Valley.
  • Mt. Tsurugi: The second-highest peak in Shikoku, Mt. Tsurugi is a popular destination for hikers.
  • Oboke Gorge: Enjoy a boat ride down the Oboke Gorge river where you can marvel at the stunning scenery of towering cliffs.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Iya Valley

Iya Valley is a paradise for hikers and bikers. Some of the recommended trails are:

  • Iya Valley Trail: This trailcovering is approximately 60 km, and takes visitors through several villages, mountain passes, and historical sites.
  • Mt. Tsurugi Trail: This trail to the summit of Mt. Tsurugi is challenging and requires moderate to high levels of fitness.
  • Kazurabashi Trail: This scenic trail leads to the vine bridge featured in the natural attractions section.

Best Things to Do in Iya Valley: Activities and Tours

The best things to do in Iya Valley include:

  • Rafting: The Oboke Gorge and Yoshino River are two popular rafting spots in Iya Valley.
  • Zipline: The Oku-Iya Zipline boasts the longest zipline in Japan.
  • Hot Springs: The Iya Valley is known for its natural hot springs, where visitors can relax and unwind after a long hike or bike ride.

Food and Drink Scene of Iya Valley

Must-Try Dishes

Some of the must-try dishes in Iya Valley include:

  • Soba Noodles: This is a popular dish throughout Japan, but Iya Valley’s soba noodles are made using water from the clear mountain streams and have a unique flavor.
  • Kei-chan: Marinated chicken that is boiled, then grilled and served with a special sauce.
  • Wild boar: A local delicacy, often served skewered or as a stew.

Local Beverages

Some of the local beverages to try in Iya Valley include:

  • Sake: Iya Valley is known for producing high-quality sake using traditional methods.
  • Shiso Juice: A refreshing drink made from the shiso plant that grows abundantly in the area.
  • Green Tea: Iya Valley is known for its exceptional quality green tea, which can be tasted at several tea houses or purchased to take home.

Where to Stay in Iya Valley: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

There is a wide range of accommodation options in Iya Valley to suit various budgets and preferences. Some of the recommended places to stay include:

  • Iya Valley Ryokan: This traditional Japanese inn offers comfortable rooms, hot springs, and an on-site restaurant.
  • Iya Onsen Hotel: A luxury hotel offering natural hot springs and stunning views of the valley.
  • Camping Grounds: Visitors who prefer camping can pitch a tent at one of the designated camping areas.

What to Buy in Iya Valley: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

When in Iya Valley, don’t forget to bring home some souvenirs and local products such as:

  • Iya Soba Noodles: Take home a pack of delicious soba noodles as a tasty souvenir.
  • Handmade Crafts: There are several stores in the area selling handmade crafts, such as pottery and textiles.
  • Iya Valley Sake: If you enjoyed the local sake, bring home a bottle or two as a souvenir.

Local Culture and Customs of Iya Valley

The people of Iya Valley have preserved their traditional way of life, and visitors are expected to respect their customs and traditions. Some of the cultural practices in the area include:

  • Wearing traditional attire: Many villagers still wear traditional attire, and visitors are welcome to dress up in borrowed attire for photos.
  • Respect for nature: Iya Valley residents have a strong bond with nature and are committed to preserving the natural environment. Visitors are required to follow the “Leave No Trace” principle.
  • Manners and Etiquette: Politeness is highly valued in Japan, and visitors are expected to show respect and consideration to others at all times.

Best Time to Visit Iya Valley

The best time to visit Iya Valley is during the spring (March to May) or autumn (September to November) when the weather is mild, and the foliage is spectacular. Summer months (June to August) can be hot and humid, while winter (December to February) can be bitterly cold, with snowfall hindering outdoor activities.

Do’s and Don’ts of Iya Valley

Do:

  • Respect local customs and practices.
  • Carry enough cash as some establishments only accept cash payments.
  • Hire a local guide for hiking and trekking in the area.

Don’t:

  • Litter or damage the environment.
  • Disrespect other visitors or locals.
  • Ignore safety protocols.

Safety and Health

Safety in Iya Valley

Iya Valley is generally safe for tourists. Visitors are advised to:

  • Carry a fully charged phone at all times in case of emergencies.
  • Dress appropriately when hiking or trekking in the area.
  • Follow safety guidelines when engaging in adventure activities.

Health in Iya Valley

Visitors to Iya Valley are advised to:

  • Carry enough prescription medication when traveling to the area.
  • Stay hydrated, especially when hiking or biking in the area.
  • Seek medical attention immediately in case of any health emergencies.

Getting to Iya Valley: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Iya Valley

The nearest airport to Iya Valley is Tokushima Airport.

Bus Stations Near Iya Valley

Buses from Tokyo or Osaka run to the city of Tokushima from where you can catch a local bus to Iya Valley.

Other Options

Driving to Iya Valley is an option for those who prefer a more scenic route.

How to Get Around in Iya Valley

The best way to get around Iya Valley is by rental car or taxi. However, public transport is also available, including local buses and trains, although they may not run as frequently or as conveniently as rental cars.

Where to Go Next from Iya Valley: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Some of the nearby destinations and excursions from Iya Valley include:

  • Tokushima City: Explore the city’s cultural and historical sites, such as the Awa Odori Kaikan museum.
  • Naruto Whirlpools: Experience the natural wonder of the Naruto Whirlpools, a tidal phenomenon found in the Naruto Strait.
  • Kochi City: Visit one of Japan’s most scenic cities, with a rich history and cultural heritage.

Conclusion

Iya Valley is a hidden gem in Japan, offering visitors a chance to escape the hustle and bustle of urban life and immerse themselves in nature and culture. With its many attractions, hiking trails, outdoor activities, and stunning scenery, it is no wonder that Iya Valley has become a favorite destination among visitors to Japan. We hope this guide has been helpful in planning your next adventure to Iya Valley.
