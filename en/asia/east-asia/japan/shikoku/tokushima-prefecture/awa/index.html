Introduction to Awa

Serenely located in Tokushima prefecture, Shikoku, Japan, lies a tranquil paradise known as Awa. Far from the tourist crowds of Japan’s major cities, Awa retains its unique culture and stunning natural beauty. Awa is a hidden gem that’s waiting to be explored by intrepid travelers.

List of Places to Visit in Awa

Awa has numerous attractions catering to tourists who enjoy history, culture, and the great outdoors.

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Awa has a rich cultural history, and there are several museums and historical sites to visit.

  • Awa Folk Museum: This museum gives insight into the local life of the people of Awa. Exhibits showcase traditional crafts, including kettle making, weaving, and indigo dyeing.

  • Hiwasa Turtle Museum: This museum is dedicated to the preservation of sea turtles in Japan. Visitors can experience the life cycle of turtles and have the opportunity to see loggerhead turtles up close.

  • Ichinomiya Temple: This temple is known as one of the Three Grand Shrines of Shikoku, and it’s where the famous Hydrangea Festival takes place.

Natural Attractions of Awa: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Awa is blessed with picturesque landscapes and scenic views, making it perfect for outdoor enthusiasts.

  • Naruto Whirlpools: The Naruto Whirlpools are a natural phenomenon created by the powerful tidal currents that occur within the Naruto Strait. Visitors can take a boat tour to view the whirlpools.

  • Kamiyama Skyline Drive: This scenic drive showcases the beauty of Awa’s mountainous landscapes. Visitors can enjoy the drive or go for a hike on one of the many trails.

  • Otsuka Art Museum: With a focus on contemporary art, the Otsuka Art Museum showcases the works of renowned Japanese artists. One can enjoy the mountainous view from the windows of this unique gallery space which spans across four stories.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Awa

Awa has numerous hiking and biking trails that cater to outdoor enthusiasts.

  • Mt. Bizan: This mountain offers excellent hiking trails with some amazing views of Awa.

  • Oboke-Iya: There are many biking trails that wind through the picturesque countryside and mountains of Oboke-Iya.

Best Things to Do in Awa: Activities and Tours

Awa offers several exciting activities and tours for visitors.

  • Rafting on the Yoshino River: This activity is for the thrill-seekers who enjoy an adrenaline rush. The Yoshino River is one of the best places in Japan for white-water rafting.

  • Shimanami Kaido Cycling Tour: This tour extends across the Seto Inland Sea to connect Honshu and Shikoku. It offers stunning views of the sea and is a great option for those who want to experience Awa’s natural beauty up close.

Week-Long Itinerary for Awa

Day 1: Arrival in Awa and relaxation at onsen

Day 2: Visit the Awa Folk Museum, Ichinomiya Temple, and Hiwasa Turtle Museum

Day 3: Hike Mt. Bizan or take a drive on the Kamiyama Skyline Drive

Day 4: Enjoy breakfast at Otsuka Art Museum, explore the gallery and its surroundings

Day 5: Rafting on the Yoshino River

Day 6: Cycling tour on the Shimanami Kaido

Day 7: Visit the Naruto Whirlpools

Food and Drink Scene of Awa

Awa is famous for its fresh seafood, traditional sake, and citrus fruits.

Must-Try Dishes

  • Irodori-zushi: A unique type of sushi in which the raw fish is laid over the vinegared rice instead of being wrapped.

  • Jakoten: Lightly fried fish cakes made with pureed white fish and flour. It is then shaped into sticks and fried.

  • Sanuki Udon: A kind of Japanese noodle that is famous for its thick white texture, served in a soup or cooked yaki (grilled) style with sauce.

Local Beverages

  • Awagami Sake: This sake is a local and traditional drink of Awa, made with the pure waters of the Yoshino River.

  • Iyokan Juice: A type of citrus fruit that is famous in Awa for its sweet and sour taste.

Where to Stay in Awa: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Awa offers a range of accommodation options that cater to various preferences and budgets.

  • Yoshino Onsen: An onsen (hot spring) with traditional Japanese-style rooms, offering magnificent views of the Otoyocho and surrounding mountainous landscapes.

  • Awa Royal Hotel: A luxurious stay with spacious rooms, providing western dinners of authentic Japanese cuisine.

  • Sanyoso: A traditional inn that offers delicious Japanese breakfast and dinners in a comfortable and friendly atmosphere.

What to Buy in Awa: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Yoshino Emon Kettle: A traditional ibushi-gin (smoked silver) kettle made in Yoshino, Awa’s central town. It has a distinct aroma and is said to improve the taste of water when used.

  • Naruto Kurosaki Stone: Used for sharpening Japanese knives, this high-quality stone is a product of Naruto in Tokushima prefecture.

Local Culture and Customs of Awa

  • Awa-Odori: A traditional Japanese dance festival that takes place in August in Tokushima city.

  • Shikoku Island Pilgrimage: This pilgrimage involves visiting 88 temples around the entire Shikoku island, and it’s considered one of the most significant Buddhist pilgrimages in Japan.

Best Time to Visit Awa

The best time to visit Awa is during the spring (April to May) and autumn (September to November) to experience its natural beauty.

Do’s and Don’ts of Awa

Do:

  • Research and respect local customs while visiting a shrine or temple.

  • Try the local cuisine of Awa, including fresh seafood and sake.

Don’t:

  • Take photographs without permission at local festivals, especially during the Awa-Odori.

  • Walk around town or public places in revealing clothing.

Safety and Health

Safety in Awa

Awa is a safe city with a low crime rate. Ensure you have travel insurance and notify your embassy about your trip in case of any emergency.

Health in Awa

It’s important to have a travel health insurance policy that covers medical evacuation as well as any pre-existing health conditions. Make sure to carry your prescription medication as it may not be available in local pharmacies.

Getting to Awa: Transportation Options and Logistics

Awa can be reached by air or land. Tokyo and Osaka are the major starting points for traveling to Awa.

Airports Near Awa

  • Tokushima Awaodori Airport: It’s located in the heart of Awa and is served by airlines from major cities in Japan.

Bus Stations Near Awa

  • Hiwasa Bus Stop: Regular buses run to and from Hiwasa to Tokushima, Nagano, and other destinations in Awa.

  • Tokushima Bus Terminal: It’s the central hub for local buses and buses to the neighboring prefectures.

Other Options

  • Train: Get off at Tokushima Station and take a bus or taxi from there to reach Awa.

How to Get Around in Awa

Awa can be explored by bus, car, or bicycle.

By Bus

Buses are a popular mode of transportation in Awa. The Tokushima bus terminal provides services to and from various destinations in the prefecture.

By Car

A car rental is convenient for visitors who want to explore Awa at their pace. There are several rental companies in Awa.

By Bicycle

Biking is popular to explore the scenic countryside of Awa. Rental bikes are available, and many hotels also provide them to their guests.

Where to Go Next from Awa: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Awa is located in Shikoku, and there are many nearby destinations to explore, including:

  • Matsuyama: Famous for Dogo Onsen, one of the oldest hot springs in Japan

  • Kochi: Known for its delicious seafood and scenic landscapes

  • Takamatsu: Famous for its traditional gardens and udon noodles.

Conclusion

Awa is a hidden gem away from the hustle and bustle of a typical tourist destination. It’s a place where visitors can immerse themselves in the rich cultural history and embrace the natural beauty of Japan. Whether you’re looking to hike, raft, or indulge in local cuisine, Awa is an unforgettable experience for every traveler.
