Introduction to Naruto (Tokushima)

Located in the southwestern part of Tokushima Prefecture, Naruto is a beautiful city with a rich cultural heritage and some of Japan’s most stunning natural attractions. Known for its whirlpools, beautiful temples, scenic views, and delicious cuisine, Naruto is a perfect destination for all types of travelers.

List of Places to Visit in Naruto (Tokushima)

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Otsuka Museum of Art

The Otsuka Museum of Art is Japan’s largest museum of Western art, with over 1,000 works by 190 artists from 25 countries. The museum’s most unique feature is its use of ceramic reproductions of famous paintings.

Ryozen-ji Temple

Ryozen-ji Temple is the starting point of the famous Shikoku Pilgrimage, a 1,200 km long trek of 88 Buddhist temples. Built in 733, this temple is considered one of the most important temples in Japan.

Naruto Otsuka Museum of Art

The Naruto Otsuka Museum of Art features over 1,000 works of art by 190 artists from 25 countries, reproduced in ceramic.

Natural Attractions of Naruto (Tokushima): Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Naruto Whirlpools

The Naruto Whirlpools are one of Naruto’s most famous natural attractions. These powerful whirlpools are created by tidal currents as they rush through the narrow strait between Naruto and Awaji Island.

Naruto Park

Naruto Park is a popular spot for hiking and picnicking, with several hiking trails and scenic views of the Naruto Strait.

Mount Bizan

Mount Bizan is a beautiful mountain located in the heart of Tokushima City. The top of the mountain offers stunning views of the city and the surrounding landscape.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Naruto (Tokushima)

Tokushima Prefectural Nature Park

Tokushima Prefectural Nature Park is a beautiful natural area located in the northern part of Tokushima Prefecture. The park features several hiking trails through stunning landscapes filled with rich flora and fauna.

Naruto Park Trail

The Naruto Park Trail is a beautiful hiking trail that takes you through some of Naruto’s most scenic areas, including the Naruto Strait and Mount Kamegamori.

Best Things to Do in Naruto (Tokushima): Activities and Tours

Naruto Whirlpool Boat Tour

The Naruto Whirlpool Boat Tour is a popular activity for visitors to Naruto. These tours take you on a boat ride through the Naruto Strait, where you can witness the incredible power of the Naruto Whirlpools up close.

Naruto Uzushio Power Plant

The Naruto Uzushio Power Plant is an incredible feat of engineering located in the Naruto Strait. The plant harnesses the power of the whirlpools to generate electricity for the surrounding areas.

Food and Drink Scene of Naruto (Tokushima)

Must-Try Dishes

Sudachi

Sudachi is a type of small, sour citrus fruit that is a specialty of Tokushima Prefecture. It is typically used as a garnish for fish dishes, but can also be used in dressings and sauces.

Awaodori Chicken

Awaodori Chicken is a local brand of chicken that is raised in Tokushima Prefecture. It is known for its superior taste and texture, and is a popular ingredient in many local dishes.

Local Beverages

Sudachishu

Sudachishu is a local liqueur made from sudachi fruit. It has a sweet, tangy taste and is often served as an aperitif or digestif.

Tokushima Sake

Tokushima Sake is a type of Japanese rice wine that is brewed using locally grown rice. It has a smooth, mellow flavor that is perfect for pairing with local dishes.

What to Buy in Naruto (Tokushima): Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Sudachi Products

Sudachi products are a popular souvenir for visitors to Naruto. You can find a wide range of sudachi-related items, including jam, dressing, and even sudachi-flavored potato chips.

Awaodori Chicken

Awaodori Chicken is another popular local product that makes for a unique and delicious souvenir. Look for chicken jerky or dried chicken meat to bring home with you.

Local Culture and Customs of Naruto (Tokushima)

Naruto is part of the Shikoku region of Japan, which is known for its traditional customs and practices. One of the most well-known of these is the Shikoku Pilgrimage, which attracts thousands of visitors every year.

Best Time to Visit Naruto (Tokushima)

The best time to visit Naruto is during the spring and fall months, when the weather is mild and comfortable. In the spring, you can witness the beautiful cherry blossoms in full bloom, while in the fall you can enjoy the stunning autumn leaves.

Do’s and Don’ts of Naruto (Tokushima)

Do:

  • Try the local cuisine, including sudachi and Awaodori chicken.
  • Visit the Naruto Whirlpools for an unforgettable experience.
  • Take part in the Shikoku Pilgrimage if you have the chance.

Don’t:

  • Wear shoes inside temples or other sacred areas.
  • Talk loudly or make a scene in public places.
  • Ignore local customs or traditions.

Safety and Health

Safety in Naruto (Tokushima)

Naruto is a relatively safe city, with low rates of crime and violence. However, visitors should still take basic safety precautions, such as staying alert and avoiding dark, isolated areas at night.

Health in Naruto (Tokushima)

Naruto has good healthcare facilities and services, including several hospitals and clinics throughout the city. Visitors should still have health insurance and take basic precautions to avoid illness, such as washing hands frequently and staying hydrated.

Getting to Naruto (Tokushima): Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Naruto (Tokushima)

The closest airport to Naruto is Tokushima Airport, which is located about 30 minutes away by car. The airport has several daily flights to and from Tokyo and Osaka.

Bus Stations Near Naruto (Tokushima)

Naruto is well-connected by bus, with several bus stations throughout the city. The most popular bus stations include Naruto Station, Otsuka Museum of Art Station, and Naruto Park Station.

Other Options

Naruto is also easily accessible by train or car. The city is located on the Tokushima Railway line, which connects it to other cities throughout Tokushima Prefecture and beyond.

How to Get Around in Naruto (Tokushima)

Naruto is a relatively small city, and is easy to navigate on foot or by bicycle. There is also a local bus service that connects the city’s major attractions and neighborhoods.

Where to Go Next from Naruto (Tokushima): Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Awaji Island

Awaji Island is located just across the Naruto Strait and is a popular destination for day trips from Naruto. The island is known for its beautiful nature, delicious cuisine, and hot springs.

Tokushima City

Tokushima City is the capital of Tokushima Prefecture and is located about 45 minutes away from Naruto by train. The city is known for its vibrant culture, delicious food, and beautiful nature.

Conclusion

Naruto is a beautiful city with a rich culture and stunning natural attractions. Whether you’re interested in outdoor adventures, cultural experiences, or delicious food, Naruto has something to offer everyone. Plan your visit today and discover the beauty of one of Japan’s most unique destinations.
