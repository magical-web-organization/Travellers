Introduction to Miyoshi (Tokushima)

Miyoshi is a small town in Tokushima prefecture, located in Shikoku, the smallest and least populated of Japan’s four main islands. The town is situated in a scenic valley and surrounded by the picturesque mountains of Shikoku. Miyoshi is known for its dense forests, swift rivers, and natural hot springs. The town is also rich in culture and history, with a number of museums and historical landmarks to explore.

List of Places to Visit in Miyoshi (Tokushima)

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Iya Valley The Iya Valley is one of Japan’s most remote and isolated regions, with traditional thatched-roof houses and stunning natural scenery. It is also the site of the famous Vine Bridge, a suspension bridge made of vines that stretches across a deep gorge.
  • Ochiai Village Ochiai is a small historic village that is known for its well-preserved traditional houses and beautiful gardens. The village is a great place to experience Japan’s rural culture and architecture.
  • Awa Odori Museum The Awa Odori Museum is dedicated to the region’s famous dance festival, which takes place in August. Visitors can learn about the history and cultural significance of the dance, and watch performances throughout the year.

Natural Attractions of Miyoshi (Tokushima): Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Mt. Tsurugi Mt. Tsurugi is one of the highest peaks in Shikoku, and is a popular destination for hikers and climbers. The mountain offers stunning views of the surrounding area, and in the winter, it is a great place to ski or snowboard.
  • Niju Kazurabashi The Niju Kazurabashi are two suspension bridges made of vines that span a deep gorge. The bridges are a popular destination for tourists, and visitors can cross the bridges and hike through the surrounding forest.
  • Iya Onsen Ryokan The Iya Onsen Ryokan is a traditional Japanese inn that is located in a secluded valley. Visitors can relax in the natural hot springs and enjoy the stunning views of the surrounding mountains and forests.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Miyoshi (Tokushima)

  • Mt. Tsurugi Hike The Mt. Tsurugi hike is a challenging but rewarding climb up one of the highest peaks in Shikoku. The hike takes about 5-6 hours round trip and offers stunning views of the surrounding area.
  • Oboke Gorge Trail The Oboke Gorge Trail is a scenic hiking trail that follows the Yoshino River through a deep and narrow gorge. The trail is known for its beautiful views and wildlife.
  • Koboke Canyon Cycling Trail The Koboke Canyon Cycling Trail is a scenic route that follows the Yoshino River through stunning rock formations and breathtaking natural scenery.

Best Things to Do in Miyoshi (Tokushima): Activities and Tours

  • Rafting and Canyoning The fast-flowing rivers and deep gorges of Miyoshi make it an ideal destination for water activities like rafting and canyoning. A number of tour companies offer guided excursions for all skill levels.
  • Awa Odori Dance Festival The Awa Odori Dance Festival is a lively and colorful event that takes place every August. Visitors can watch traditional dance performances and join in the celebrations.
  • Visit a Sake Brewery Miyoshi is home to a number of sake breweries, which offer tours and tastings. Visitors can learn about the traditional brewing process and sample a variety of sake.

Food and Drink Scene of Miyoshi (Tokushima)

Must-Try Dishes

  • Sobagaki Sobagaki is a traditional dish made from pounded buckwheat noodles that is often served with soy sauce and grated daikon radish.
  • Katsuo no Tataki Katsuo no Tataki is a local delicacy made from seared bonito tuna that is sliced thinly and served with onions, garlic, and ginger.
  • Sanuki Udon Sanuki Udon is a type of thick and chewy udon noodle that is made from locally grown wheat and served in a variety of styles.

Local Beverages

  • Sake The Miyoshi region is known for its traditional sake, which is brewed using local rice and water. Visitors can tour sake breweries and sample a variety of different types of sake.
  • Shochu Shochu is a distilled beverage that is made from a variety of different ingredients, including sweet potatoes, barley, and rice. It is often served with ice or mixed with other drinks.

Where to Stay in Miyoshi (Tokushima): Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Iya Onsen Ryokan The Iya Onsen Ryokan is a traditional Japanese inn that offers comfortable rooms with private hot spring baths and stunning views of the surrounding mountains and forests.
  • Ochiai Shuraku Ochiai Shuraku is a traditional village that offers visitors the chance to stay in a traditional Japanese house and experience rural life in Miyoshi.
  • Kazurabashi Guesthouse Kazurabashi Guesthouse is a cozy and comfortable guesthouse that offers a variety of rooms and easy access to the Niju Kazurabashi suspension bridges and other outdoor activities.

What to Buy in Miyoshi (Tokushima): Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Awamori Awamori is a type of distilled liquor that is made in Okinawa, Japan. It is known for its unique flavor and is often used in cocktails or as a base for other drinks.
  • Iya kaya (millet) Iya kaya is a local specialty that is made from millet and is often used in traditional dishes like sobagaki.
  • Naruto Kintoki Sweet Potato Chips Naruto Kintoki Sweet Potato Chips are a popular snack made from locally-grown sweet potatoes. They are crispy and sweet, and make a great souvenir or gift.

Local Culture and Customs of Miyoshi (Tokushima)

Miyoshi is a town that is steeped in tradition and has a rich cultural history. Visitors should take care to respect local customs and traditions, such as removing their shoes before entering a traditional Japanese house or temple, and being quiet and respectful during performances or ceremonies.

Best Time to Visit Miyoshi (Tokushima)

The best time to visit Miyoshi is during the spring and autumn months, when the weather is mild and the scenery is at its most beautiful. The summer months can be hot and humid, but are a great time to see the Awa Odori Dance Festival. Winter is also a good time to visit, especially for skiing and snowboarding on Mt. Tsurugi.

Do’s and Don’ts of Miyoshi (Tokushima)

Do:

  • respect local customs and traditions
  • try local cuisine and drinks
  • be courteous and polite to locals and other travelers
  • take care of the natural environment and preserve its beauty

Don’t:

  • litter or damage the natural environment
  • disrespect local customs and traditions
  • be loud or disruptive during ceremonies or performances
  • take photographs of people without permission

Safety and Health

Safety in Miyoshi (Tokushima)

Miyoshi is a safe town with a low crime rate. However, visitors should take care when hiking or engaging in outdoor activities, and should avoid swimming in fast-moving rivers or deep water.

Health in Miyoshi (Tokushima)

Miyoshi has modern medical facilities and is a safe place to visit. However, visitors should take care to protect themselves from the sun and insects, and should be conscious of any food allergies.

Getting to Miyoshi (Tokushima): Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Miyoshi (Tokushima)

The nearest airport to Miyoshi is Tokushima Airport (TKS), which is located about 60 kilometers away. The airport offers flights to and from several major cities in Japan, including Tokyo, Osaka, and Fukuoka.

Bus Stations Near Miyoshi (Tokushima)

Miyoshi is accessible by bus from major cities in Shikoku, including Takamatsu, Kochi, and Tokushima. The town is served by several bus companies, including JR Bus and Tokushima Bus.

Other Options

Visitors can also rent a car or take a taxi to travel to Miyoshi. However, a car is recommended for exploring the surrounding area and accessing some of the more remote attractions.

How to Get Around in Miyoshi (Tokushima)

Miyoshi is a small town that can be easily explored on foot or by bicycle. However, visitors may want to rent a car or take a taxi to access some of the more remote attractions.

Where to Go Next from Miyoshi (Tokushima): Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Kochi City Kochi City is the capital of Kochi Prefecture, and is known for its beautiful castle and rich cultural heritage.
  • Takamatsu City Takamatsu City is the capital of Kagawa Prefecture, and is famous for its udon noodles and traditional Ritsurin Garden.
  • Shimanto River The Shimanto River is the longest and most beautiful river in Shikoku, and is a great destination for canoeing and other water activities.

Conclusion

Miyoshi is a hidden gem in the heart of Shikoku, offering stunning natural scenery, rich culture and history, and a variety of outdoor activities and adventures. With its traditional villages, hot springs, and festivals, Miyoshi is a must-visit destination for travelers looking for an authentic Japanese experience.
