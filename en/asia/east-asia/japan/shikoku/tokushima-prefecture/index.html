Food and Drink Scene of Tokushima (prefecture)

Must-Try Dishes

1. Sudachi citrus fruits

Sudachi is a small, green citrus fruit that grows in Tokushima. It is used to flavor many local dishes, including sashimi, street food, and drinks.

2. Oboke Udon

Oboke Udon is a thick, chewy noodle dish served in a hot soup made of soy sauce, seaweed, and bonito flakes. It is a popular dish among locals and visitors alike.

Local Beverages

1. Sake

Tokushima is famous for its high-quality sake. The most popular varieties are Awa Sake and Manzairaku Sake, both of which have won numerous awards.

2. Japanese Green Tea

Tokushima’s climate and unique soil have made it an ideal place to grow green tea. Taste the fresh brew at one of the local tea houses.

Where to Stay in Tokushima (prefecture): Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

1. Hotel Clement Tokushima

Hotel Clement Tokushima is a modern and comfortable hotel located in the heart of Tokushima. It offers a wide range of facilities and amenities, including a spa and an on-site restaurant.

2. Tokushima Guesthouse Buono

Tokushima Guesthouse Buono is a cozy and affordable guesthouse with a friendly atmosphere. It is located near the train station and offers dormitory-style rooms with shared facilities.

What to Buy in Tokushima (prefecture): Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

1. Awa Indigo Textiles

Tokushima is famous for its Awa Indigo dyeing technique. You can buy textiles and garments made with this vibrant blue color.

2. Naruto Kintoki Sweet Potato

Naruto Kintoki is a sweet potato variety that grows in Tokushima. It has a unique flavor and texture, and it is used to make various local sweets.

Local Culture and Customs of Tokushima (prefecture)

Tokushima is a proud and diverse region that cherishes its traditions and values. The locals are friendly and welcoming, and they appreciate visitors who show respect and interest in their culture.

Best Time to Visit Tokushima (prefecture)

The best time to visit Tokushima is during the summer months, between June and August. This is when the weather is warm and pleasant, and when most of the festivals and activities take place.

Do’s and Don’ts of Tokushima (prefecture)

Do:

  • Learn a few Japanese phrases and greetings to show respect towards the locals.
  • Take off your shoes and wear slippers when entering a traditional Japanese house or temple.
  • Try the local food and drinks, and get involved in cultural activities.

Don’t:

  • Use your phone or talk loudly on public transportation.
  • Take pictures of people without their permission.
  • Be disrespectful towards religious or cultural symbols.

Safety and Health

Safety in Tokushima (prefecture)

Tokushima is a relatively safe region with a low crime rate. However, visitors should take basic safety precautions, such as not leaving valuables unattended and being aware of their surroundings.

Health in Tokushima (prefecture)

Tokushima has an excellent healthcare system with modern hospitals and clinics. Visitors should consider purchasing travel insurance and bringing any necessary medication and vaccines.

Getting to Tokushima (prefecture): Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Tokushima (prefecture)

The nearest airport to Tokushima is Tokushima Awaodori Airport. It offers domestic flights to major Japanese cities such as Tokyo and Osaka.

Bus Stations Near Tokushima (prefecture)

The main bus station in Tokushima is Tokushima Bus Terminal, which is located next to the train station.

Other Options

You can also reach Tokushima by train or rental car. The city is connected to major Japanese cities via the JR Tokaido-Sanyo Shinkansen bullet train.

How to Get Around in Tokushima (prefecture)

The most convenient way to get around Tokushima is by train or bus. The local transport system is reliable and efficient, and it covers most of the prefecture’s main attractions.

Where to Go Next from Tokushima (prefecture): Nearby Destinations and Excursions

1. Osaka

Osaka is a vibrant and bustling city located about two hours away from Tokushima. Known for its food scene and attractions such as Osaka Castle and Universal Studios Japan, Osaka is a must-visit destination in Japan.

2. Takamatsu

Takamatsu is a beautiful coastal city located in Kagawa Prefecture. It is known for its traditional gardens, such as Ritsurin Park, and its signature dish, Sanuki Udon.

Conclusion

Tokushima is a beautiful and unique travel destination with something to offer for everyone. With its rich cultural heritage, stunning natural landscapes, and mouth-watering local cuisine, it is an experience that will stay with you forever.
