Introduction to Sakaide

Nestled in the heart of Kagawa, Sakaide is a picturesque city that offers a perfect blend of urban life and natural beauty. Known for its exquisite landscapes, elaborate museums, and unique cultural landmarks, it’s no wonder that Sakaide attracts visitors from all over the world. In this travel guide, we’ll explore the best of Sakaide, including must-visit tourist attractions, excellent food, local customs, and more. So pack your bags and get ready to explore Japan’s hidden gem!

List of Places to Visit in Sakaide

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Sakaide History Museum: Explore the city’s rich cultural history and learn about the local traditions and crafts at the Sakaide History Museum.
  • Sanuki Kid’s Kingdom: Take your kids to the Sanuki Kid’s Kingdom, a theme park complete with a ferris wheel, roller coaster, and numerous other fun rides and attractions.
  • Kanamaruza: Visit Kanamaruza, Japan’s oldest surviving Kabuki theatre that dates back to the Edo period.

Natural Attractions of Sakaide: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Kotohira-gu Shrine: Hike up to the Kotohira-gu Shrine and be rewarded with a stunning view of the city from atop a scenic mountain.
  • Seto Ohashi Memorial Park: Enjoy a peaceful walk through the Seto Ohashi Memorial Park and take in the breathtaking views of the Seto Inland Sea.
  • Mt. Yashima: A great place for hiking and breathtaking views of the Seto Inland Sea, Mt. Yashima offers a unique experience with local temples and scenic beauty.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Sakaide

  • Shiwaku Cycling Road: Hop on a bike and take a ride along the Shiwaku Cycling Road, a trail that spans 30 miles and leads to the top of Mount Noro.
  • Goshikidai Plateau Hiking Course: Take a walk along the Goshikidai Plateau Hiking Course, and enjoy magnificent views of the island and sea.

Best Things to Do in Sakaide: Activities and Tours

  • Sake Tasting Tour: Get a taste of the local culture and sample some of the best sake with a sake tasting tour.
  • Local Food Tour: Sakaide is known for its delicious food, so why not indulge in a local food tour and try some of the local delicacies.
  • Shakunage Valley Sightseeing: The Shakunage Valley Sightseeing is a popular spot for cherry blossoms and admired for its beautiful waterfall.

Week-Long Itinerary for Sakaide

  • Day 1: Visit the Sakaide History Museum and the Sanuki Kid’s Kingdom for a fun-filled day.
  • Day 2: Hike up to the Kotohira-gu Shrine and explore the local temples.
  • Day 3: Visit Kanamaruza, Japan’s oldest surviving Kabuki theatre that dates back to the Edo period.
  • Day 4: Take a bike ride along the Shiwaku Cycling Road and enjoy the stunning views.
  • Day 5: Experience a sake tasting tour and indulge in some of the local delicacies.
  • Day 6: Take a walk along the Goshikidai Plateau Hiking Course and enjoy the beautiful views.
  • Day 7: Visit the Seto Ohashi Memorial Park and enjoy a peaceful walk, taking in the breathtaking views of the Seto Inland Sea.

Food and Drink Scene of Sakaide

Must-Try Dishes

  • Sanuki Udon: Sanuki Udon is a must-try dish in Sakaide, made with a unique and traditional method. You can taste it at local restaurants such as Marugame Seimen.
  • Kamaboko: Kamaboko is a fish cake that can be eaten in many different ways. Kamaboko is available at any grocery store and market of Sakaide.
  • Olives: Olives have been grown in Sakaide for more than 100 years, and are renowned for their unique flavor.

Local Beverages

  • Sake: Sake is a popular beverage in Sakaide, and a trip to Sakaide wouldn’t be complete without tasting some of the best local sake at a sake brewery.
  • Shochu: Shochu is a distilled beverage that is similar to vodka and is made from barley, sweet potatoes or rice. It’s also produced in Sakaide.

Where to Stay in Sakaide: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • JR Hotel Clement Sakaide: The JR Hotel Clement Sakaide is a great choice for those looking for a luxurious stay in a prime location.
  • *Nio No Yakata: The Nio No Yakata is a charming Japanese-style guesthouse that offers a traditional experience in a quiet location.
  • Shodoshima Business Hotel: The Shodoshima Business Hotel is a budget-friendly option with comfortable rooms and excellent amenities.

What to Buy in Sakaide: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Olives: Olives are a specialty of Sakaide and can be found in various forms and at the local shops and markets.
  • Kamaboko: Kamaboko is a popular local dish that you can take home with you for a taste of Sakaide.
  • Pottery: Sakaide has a rich pottery culture, with famous restaurants and ceramic production sites in the area. You can buy pottery products at some of the local stores and factories.

Local Culture and Customs of Sakaide

Sakaide has a rich and unique culture, known for its pottery, olives, and local festivals. The city is also famous for its Kabuki theatre, which is a blend of music, drama, and dance. Sakaide values hard work, respect, and hospitality, so be sure to embrace these values when visiting this charming city in Japan.

Best Time to Visit Sakaide

The best time to visit Sakaide is during the cherry blossom season (late March to early April) and the autumn foliage season (late November to early December). During these times, the city is enveloped in natural beauty, with vibrant pink and orange hues. However, Sakaide is a year-round destination, with various events taking place throughout the year.

Do’s and Don’ts of Sakaide

Do:

  • Respect the local culture and customs.
  • Be punctual and observant.
  • Try the local food and drink.

Don’t:

  • Wear shoes indoors.
  • Speak too loudly or aggressively in public.
  • Handle food with your hands, unless it is a finger food.

Safety and Health

Safety in Sakaide

Sakaide is a safe and peaceful city with a low crime rate. However, like any other city, it’s essential to be aware of your surroundings and belongings.

Health in Sakaide

Medical treatment is readily available, and emergency services are efficient and responsive. It’s always advisable to have health insurance and carry a basic medical kit with you on any trip.

Getting to Sakaide: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Sakaide

  • Takamatsu Airport: Takamatsu Airport located approximately 35 kilometers from Sakaide, is the closest airport to Sakaide. You can take a bus or taxi from the airport to the city center.

Bus Stations Near Sakaide

  • Sakaide Station: Sakaide Station is the main bus station in the city, with frequent services to other cities in the region.

Other Options

  • Train: Sakaide is accessible by train, and the city has two train stations: JR Sakaide Station and Manyo-Mahoroba Station.

How to Get Around in Sakaide

Sakaide is relatively small, and most attractions are within walking distance. However, if you prefer not to walk, you can take the local bus or taxi. Bicycle rentals are also available.

Where to Go Next from Sakaide: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Kurashiki: Kurashiki is a historic city located approximately 30 kilometers from Sakaide, famous for its traditional Japanese architecture and picturesque canal area.
  • Takamatsu: Takamatsu is a small port city approximately 35 kilometers from Sakaide, known for its beautiful gardens and infamous Udon noodles.
  • Naoshima Island: Naoshima Island is renowned for its art museums and modern sculptures and is located approximately 50 kilometers from Sakaide.

Conclusion

Sakaide is a hidden gem of Japan, filled with natural beauty, rich culture, and warm hospitality. With something for everyone, from foodies to outdoor enthusiasts, Sakaide is a perfect destination for your next trip to Japan. Use this comprehensive guide to plan your itinerary and explore all that Sakaide has to offer.
