The Ultimate Guide to Kotohira, Kagawa, Japan

Sitting in the northeastern area of Kagawa Prefecture in Shikoku Island, Kotohira is a small town brimming with natural beauty and cultural heritage. The town is known for its rich history, natural hot springs, and the famous Kompira-san Shrine, which draws thousands of visitors each year.

In this ultimate guide to Kotohira, we’ll tell you everything you need to know about the town. From the best things to do and see to the local culture and customs, this guide has it all. So let’s dive in!

Introduction to Kotohira

Kotohira, also known as Konpira, is a small town located on the coastline of the Seto Inland Sea in Kagawa Prefecture. The town is famous for its stunning natural beauty, historical landmarks, and delicious local cuisine. It has a population of about 10,000 people and is easily accessible by train or bus from other parts of Japan.

List of Places to Visit in Kotohira

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Kompira-san Shrine: A famous Shinto shrine located on Mt. Zozu, which features 1,368 stone steps to the main shrine gate.
  • Kotohira-gu Shrine: A smaller, but equally important shrine located near Kompira-san Shrine.
  • Kagawa Prefectural Cultural Property Center: This historical museum has a vast collection of artwork and artifacts from the Kagawa region.
  • Kotohira History Museum: This museum provides an insightful look into the history of Kotohira and its culture.

Natural Attractions of Kotohira: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Sanuki Mannou National Park: This park is a nature lover’s paradise with various hiking trails and scenic views.
  • Kotohira-gawa River: A beautiful river that flows through the town, offering activities like fishing and boat rides.
  • Kotohiragu Geopark: An educational park where visitors can learn about the geological history of the Kotohira region.
  • Zentsu-ji Temple: This temple is a perfect place to relax and enjoy the peaceful surroundings.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Kotohira

  • Mt. Zozu Trail: This trail leads to Kompira-san Shrine and offers a stunning view of the Seto Inland Sea.
  • Mannou Park Nature Trail: This trail goes through a wooded area and is great for birdwatching and wildlife observation.
  • Kokubunji Temple Trail: This trail is an easy hike that leads to the Kokubunji Temple.

Best Things to Do in Kotohira: Activities and Tours

  • Onsen: Kotohira is home to various hot springs that are perfect for relaxing and unwinding.
  • Shiawase no Megumi Restaurant: This restaurant serves traditional Japanese cuisine, including the local specialty, Sanuki Udon.
  • Kotohira Brewery: Visitors can tour this brewery and sample some of the local sake.
  • Horse Riding: Visitors can take a horseback ride through the scenic countryside.

Food and Drink Scene of Kotohira

Must-Try Dishes

  • Sanuki Udon: A local specialty and one of the most popular dishes in the Kagawa Prefecture.
  • Mentaiko Tempura: Fried tempura with spicy cod roe filling.
  • Hamo (Pike Conger) Dishes: A freshwater eel that is a popular delicacy in Kotohira.

Local Beverages

  • Sake: The Kotohira and Kagawa region are famous for their sake. Be sure to sample some of the local brews.
  • Kagawa Craft Beer: The prefecture has a burgeoning craft beer scene that’s worth exploring.
  • Green Tea: Kotohira is famous for its refreshing green tea.

Where to Stay in Kotohira: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Kotohira Kadan: A luxurious ryokan with beautiful outdoor hot springs.
  • Kotohira Grand Hotel Sakura no Sho: A modern hotel perfect for families and couples.
  • Hostel Sora: A budget-friendly hostel with great views of Mount Zozu.

What to Buy in Kotohira: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Sanuki Udon: Visitors can take home a package of the local specialty to enjoy at home.
  • Hamo Products: Various eel products are available, including dried and canned options.
  • Chirimen Craft Goods: Traditional Japanese fabric crafts that make great souvenirs.

Local Culture and Customs of Kotohira

Kotohira has a rich cultural heritage, and visitors are encouraged to participate in local customs and festivities. One such event is the Kompira-san Shrine festival held in late April, where locals participate in traditional dances and celebrations.

Best Time to Visit Kotohira

The best time to visit Kotohira is during the spring months (March to May) when the cherry blossoms are in bloom, and the temperatures are moderate. October and November are also great times to visit when the autumn foliage is at its peak.

Do’s and Don’ts of Kotohira

Do:

  • Respect local customs and traditions.
  • Observe the rules and etiquette at the shrines and temples.
  • Try the local cuisine.

Don’t:

  • Eat while walking or on trains/buses.
  • Be too loud or disruptive at shrines and temples.
  • Leave trash or litter in public places.

Safety and Health

Safety in Kotohira

Kotohira is a relatively safe town with low crime rates. However, visitors should still exercise caution when walking alone at night and be aware of their surroundings.

Health in Kotohira

Kotohira has various medical clinics and hospitals that provide quality healthcare to residents and visitors. Visitors should have adequate travel insurance and be aware of any potential health risks when traveling to Japan.

Getting to Kotohira: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Kotohira

The nearest airport to Kotohira is Takamatsu Airport, which is about 30 km away. Other options include Okayama Airport and Hiroshima Airport.

Bus Stations Near Kotohira

Various bus companies operate within Kotohira, including Kotoden Bus, which offers service to major tourist destinations in the area.

Other Options

The most convenient way to get to Kotohira is by train, with the JR Kotohira Station and Kotoden Kotohira Station being the two main train stations in the town.

How to get around in Kotohira

The best way to get around in Kotohira is by bike or rental car. Visitors can also use the local train system or take a taxi.

Where to Go Next from Kotohira: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Takamatsu: A bustling city known for its delicious udon and beautiful Ritsurin Garden.
  • Naoshima: An island famous for its contemporary art installations and museums.
  • Matsuyama: A historical city with an impressive castle and hot springs.

Conclusion

From its rich cultural heritage to its stunning natural beauty, Kotohira has something to offer everyone. This ultimate guide will help you make the most of your trip to this enchanting town. Everything from hiking trails to local customs has been covered, ensuring you have a fantastic and fulfilling trip.
