Introduction to Zentsuji

Zentsuji is a small city located in Kagawa prefecture on the island of Shikoku, Japan. Known as one of the most sacred places in Japan, Zentsuji houses the oldest temple on the island, as well as many other historical and cultural landmarks. Travelers can explore ancient history and natural beauty in this charming town, which offers a mix of urban and rural experiences.

List of Places to Visit in Zentsuji

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Zentsuji Temple: The main attraction in Zentsuji, this temple is known for being the birthplace of Kobo Daishi, the founder of Shingon Buddhism. Visitors can explore the grounds and architecture of the temple, which dates back over 1,200 years.
  • Zentsuji History Museum: Learn more about the history of the temple and the city at this museum, located just next to the temple gate. Exhibits are available in English and Japanese and showcase artifacts and artworks from the region.
  • Kobo Daishi Gallery: Located in the same building as the temple, this gallery displays beautiful artwork and religious items from the temple’s collection.
  • Zentsuji Shujiro Memorial Museum: Shujiro was a famous calligrapher from Zentsuji, whose works are now on display at this museum. Learn about his life and art in this peaceful and stunning gallery.

Natural Attractions of Zentsuji: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Mt. Yashima: This mountain offers beautiful views of the surrounding area, as well as hiking and biking trails. Visit the temple at the top of the mountain for an even more stunning view.
  • Kotohiki Park: A beautiful park that features a pagoda as well as a Japanese garden. Perfect for a leisurely afternoon walk or a picnic.
  • Takamatsu Park: Located on the outskirts of town, this park offers hiking trails, a playground, and a beautiful view of the sea.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Zentsuji

  • Mt. Yashima Hiking Trail: This trail takes you up the mountain to the main temple, where you can enjoy stunning views of the surrounding area. The trail is well-maintained and easy to follow.
  • Zentsuji Cycling Course: This 21km course is perfect for cycling enthusiasts. The course takes you through the city and surrounding countryside, offering beautiful scenery along the way.

Best Things to Do in Zentsuji: Activities and Tours

  • Zen Meditation: Experience traditional Japanese meditation in the serene surroundings of Zentsuji Temple.
  • Kotohiki Beach: Spend a day at this beautiful beach located just a short drive from Zentsuji. Enjoy swimming, sunbathing, and soaking up the sun.
  • Sake Tasting: Visit a local sake brewery and taste some of the best rice wine made in the area. Learn about the history of sake and how it’s made.

Food and Drink Scene of Zentsuji

Must-Try Dishes

  • Sanuki Udon: A type of thick noodle that is famous in Kagawa prefecture. Can be served hot or cold and often served with a variety of toppings.
  • Green Onion Tempura: This unique tempura is made with whole green onions and is a popular snack in the area.
  • Iriko Dashi: A type of fish broth made with dried sardines that is commonly used as a seasoning for soups and stews.

Local Beverages

  • Sake: Kagawa prefecture is known for producing high-quality sake, and many local breweries offer tastings and tours.
  • Awamori: A distilled liquor made from rice that is popular in Okinawa, but can also be found in Zentsuji.

Where to Stay in Zentsuji: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Zentsuji Grand Hotel: A luxury hotel in the heart of the downtown area. Offers comfortable rooms, on-site dining, and a rooftop terrace with views of the temple.
  • Hikawa Ryokan: A traditional Japanese inn that features tatami rooms and hot springs. Located a short drive from Zentsuji.
  • Guesthouse Ouka: A budget-friendly guesthouse that offers clean and comfortable rooms with shared bathrooms. Located near the downtown area.

What to Buy in Zentsuji: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Sanuki Udon: Bring home a package of local udon noodles to cook at home.
  • Green Onion Tempura: Available in souvenir stores throughout the city.
  • Kobo Daishi Goods: Pick up a souvenir featuring Kobo Daishi’s image in the temple gift shop.

Local Culture and Customs of Zentsuji

  • Shingon Buddhism: Zentsuji Temple is a sacred site for Shingon Buddhism, and visitors should be respectful of the religious customs and practices.
  • Honoring Kami: Zentsuji is in the heart of a region with many Shinto shrines, and honoring the kami, or spirits, is an important part of Japanese culture.
  • Traditional Crafts: Zentsuji is known for traditional crafts such as pottery, calligraphy, and lacquerware, which can be seen at local markets and shops throughout the city.

Best Time to Visit Zentsuji

The best time to visit Zentsuji is in the spring or fall. The weather is mild, and the cherry blossoms and fall foliage are stunning. However, Zentsuji has something to offer visitors year-round, each season showcasing its unique beauty.

Do’s and Don’ts of Zentsuji

Do:

  • Respect the religious customs and traditions of the temple.
  • Be polite and courteous to locals and fellow travelers.
  • Use chopsticks when eating meals.

Don’t:

  • Wear shoes inside the temple or other religious sites.
  • Speak loudly or disturb the peaceful atmosphere of the temple.
  • Leave a chopstick standing up in a bowl of rice, as it is considered bad luck in Japanese culture.

Safety and Health

Safety in Zentsuji

Zentsuji is a safe city, with low crime rates and a friendly atmosphere. However, visitors should still practice standard safety measures, such as keeping an eye on their belongings and staying aware of their surroundings at all times.

Health in Zentsuji

Zentsuji has modern hospitals and clinics, and visitors can feel comfortable seeking medical attention if needed. However, it’s always a good idea to have travel insurance when visiting Japan.

Getting to Zentsuji: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Zentsuji

The nearest airport to Zentsuji is Takamatsu Airport (TKS), which is about 48km away. From there, travelers can take a bus, taxi, or train to Zentsuji.

Bus Stations Near Zentsuji

Zentsuji Bus Terminal is located in the downtown area, with many bus routes connecting to nearby cities and other areas of Shikoku.

Other Options

Travelers can reach Zentsuji by train, with the nearest train station being JR Zentsuji Station. From there, visitors can take a taxi or bus to their destination.

How to Get Around in Zentsuji

The easiest way to get around Zentsuji is by car or taxi, but bicycles are also available for rent at hotels and tourist information centers.

Where to Go Next from Zentsuji: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Kotohira: Located just a short distance from Zentsuji and home to the famous Kotohira Shrine.
  • Takamatsu: The largest city in Kagawa prefecture with many sightseeing spots and a beautiful seaside park.
  • Shodoshima: Known as the “Olive Island,” Shodoshima is a beautiful island just off the coast of Kagawa prefecture.

Conclusion

Zentsuji is a charming town that offers a mix of cultural and natural attractions. From exploring ancient history to hiking beautiful mountains, there is something for everyone in Zentsuji. Be sure to try the local food, visit a sake brewery, and bring home some unique souvenirs. With its beautiful scenery and friendly atmosphere, Zentsuji is a must-visit destination in Japan.
