Introduction to Shodoshima

Shodoshima is a beautiful island located in the Kagawa province, in the Shikoku region of Japan. Known as “The Olive Island,” Shodoshima is famous for its production of high-quality olive oil. The island is easily accessible by ferry from Takamatsu city, which is the largest city in Kagawa.

Shodoshima is a popular destination for both domestic and international travelers, offering plenty of attractions such as beaches, scenic cycling routes, stunning views, hot springs, and many cultural and historical sites of interest. Additionally, Shodoshima has a vibrant culinary scene, and visitors can try local specialties, including the island’s famous olive oil.

In this comprehensive travel guide, we’ll be exploring the best things to do and see in Shodoshima, where to stay, what to eat and drink, and much more.

List of Places to Visit in Shodoshima

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Shodoshima is a perfect destination for those interested in Japanese culture and history. The island is home to several museums and historical sites, including:

  • Olive Park: A theme park dedicated to the history and production of olive oil, featuring an olive tree maze, outdoor olive oil pool, and olive oil tasting experiences.
  • Twenty-four Eyes Movie Studio: A studio that was used during the filming of a famous Japanese movie called “Twenty-four Eyes.” Today, visitors can see the movie sets and film props used in the movie.
  • Kankakei Gorge: A must-visit spot in Shodoshima, offering a unique gorge with beautiful scenic views that changes throughout the year, from the autumn foliage to the spring cherry blossoms.
  • Shodoshima Olive Garden: A park that hosts a variety of unique flowers and trees, including over 2000 olive trees, making it the perfect spot to have a picnic while enjoying Shodoshima’s beautiful scenery.

Natural Attractions of Shodoshima: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Shodoshima is a paradise for outdoor enthusiasts, offering many breathtaking natural attractions for tourists to explore. Here are some of the must-sees:

  • Angel Road: A natural sandbar that appears only at low tide, creating a path appearing as if it leads to the sky, rendering it a popular spot for romantic walks or proposals.
  • Kankakei Ropeway: A cable car system taking visitors to the top of the mountain, offering spectacular views of the Seto Inland Sea and lush green valleys below.
  • Uchinomi Bay: Perfect for swimming, water sports, or just enjoying the beautiful sandy beach overlooking the Seto Inland Sea.
  • Shodoshima Furusato Mura: A traditional village that has been restored with thatched roofs and features local craftsmen demonstrating traditional handicrafts such as weaving and pottery making.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Shodoshima

Shodoshima is also an ideal place for hiking and cycling enthusiasts. The island offers a variety of hiking trails each providing different experiences such as the stunning mountain sides, beautiful beaches, and even the rich herb fields.

Here are some of the popular routes that visitors can explore:

  • Kankakei Trail: A must-do trek, provides beautiful views of the Kankakei Gorge and the ocean.
  • Olive Line: A cycling route that takes visitors around Shodoshima’s magnificent olive groves.
  • Tonosho Hills Trail: A trek that provides awe-inspiring views of the Tonosho port and the Seto Inland Sea.

Best Things to Do in Shodoshima: Activities and Tours

In addition to its natural and cultural attractions, Shodoshima offers a variety of tours and activities to visitors. Here are some popular things to do in Shodoshima:

  • Sunset Cruise: Sail around Shodoshima on a beautiful 65-foot sailing ship while enjoying the beautiful sunset views over the Seto Inland Sea.
  • Olive Oil Factory Visit: Visit one of Shodoshima’s olive oil factories to learn about the history and production process of the island’s famous olive oil.
  • Kayaking and Snorkeling Tour: Explore the island’s clear blue waters by kayaking or snorkeling around the stunning coastline.
  • Sunset Yoga: Relax and unwind with a sunset yoga session overlooking the beautiful Inland Sea of Japan.

Week-Long Itinerary for Shodoshima

Visitors can spend a whole week descending upon Shodoshima’s incredible attractions. Here’s a suggested itinerary to help you take full advantage of your trip to Shodoshima:

Day 1: Arrival on the island, check-in at the hotel. Day 2: Start your day exploring Olive Park, and enjoy a brief tour of the olive oil museum. Head to Nijushi no Hitomi Movie Studio in the afternoon. Day 3: Take the Kankakei Ropeway to the top of the Kankakei Gorge and hike around the area. Day 4: Visit Angel Road and then spend some time on Uchinomi beach, enjoying a swim or other water sports activities. Day 5: Rent a bike and head out on the Olive Line cycling route, stopping at local restaurants and cafes along the way. Day 6: Take a sunset yoga class in a coastal jungle setting or kayak around the stunning coastline of Shodoshima. Day 7: Check out Tonosho Hills and spend an afternoon at Shodoshima Olive Garden before returning to your hotel.

Food and Drink Scene of Shodoshima

Shodoshima is known for its olive oil production, but the island is also home to a wide range of traditional and modern Japanese cuisine. Here are some of the dishes and local beverages that visitors must try when on the island:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Olive Beef: One of the most famous dishes on the island, known for its tender and juicy marbled beef.
  • Udon: Kagawa is known as the “udon prefecture,” and Shodoshima has some of the best udon dishes in the region.
  • Sea Bream Rice: A popular rice bowl dish that features delicious local sea bream.

Local Beverages

  • Olive Juice: A unique drink made from Shodoshima’s famous olive oil.
  • Shodoshima Beer: A light and refreshing beer brewed locally on the island.
  • Shochu: A traditional Japanese liquor made on the island and usually paired with izakaya-style dishes.

Where to Stay in Shodoshima: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Shodoshima has a range of accommodation options suitable for different budgets and needs. Here are some of the top-rated places to stay on the island:

  • Shodoshima International Hotel: A luxurious hotel featuring over 30 guest rooms, an outdoor swimming pool, and an on-site restaurant serving local cuisine.
  • Guest House Shodoshimatoya: A traditional Japanese-style guesthouse that provides a warm, homey atmosphere along with comfortable and cozy accommodation.
  • Olivean Shodoshima: An affordable and comfortable hotel located close to many of the island’s main attractions.

What to Buy in Shodoshima: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

When exploring Shodoshima, visitors can find a wide range of souvenirs and local products to take home. Some popular items to buy include:

  • Olive Oil: Shodoshima is known for producing high-quality olive oil, and visitors can find various types to bring home.
  • Udon Noodles: Kagawa is the “udon prefecture,” and Shodoshima produces some of the best udon noodles in the region.
  • Shodoshima Lemon: A citrus fruit that is unique to the island with a slightly sour flavor and can be made into delicious drinks or desserts.

Local Culture and Customs of Shodoshima

Shodoshima is known for its warm hospitality, and visitors should respect the cultural customs of the island. Here are some local customs to observe while exploring the island:

  • Shoes off indoors: When entering someone’s home or traditional establishments like ryokans, remove your shoes.
  • Bowing: Bowing is a sign of respect and an essential part of Japanese culture.
  • Separating Trash: Japan is known for separating their trash into different categories, and Shodoshima implements the same standard.

Best Time to Visit Shodoshima

Shodoshima can be visited year-round, with each season bringing unique attractions and experiences. The best time to visit is during the autumn season (October to November), when the foliage turns into the beautiful hues of red, orange, and yellow.

Do’s and Don’ts of Shodoshima

Do:

  • Do try the famous olive oil and local cuisine.
  • Do be respectful of local customs and traditions.
  • Do be prepared to hike and cycle while touring the island.
  • Do bring a camera to take stunning photos of the scenery.

Don’t:

  • Don’t litter, as the island keeps its environment clean.
  • Don’t use outdoor hot springs facilities without showering first.
  • Don’t disrespect local customs or tradition.

Safety and Health

Safety in Shodoshima

Shodoshima is relatively safe, with low crime rates. Visitors should take normal safety measures such as securing personal belongings and locking hotel rooms.

Health in Shodoshima

Shodoshima has reliable medical facilities, including hospitals and clinics. Visitors should ensure they have adequate travel insurance covering medical emergencies.

In case of emergency, contact the Japanese equivalent of 911 by dialing 110.

Getting to Shodoshima: Transportation Options and Logistics

Shodoshima can be accessed by ferry or car, offering a unique way to explore the island. Here are some transport options available for visitors:

  • Ferry: Visitors can take the ferry from Takamatsu port to various ports on Shodoshima, with regular departures throughout the day.
  • Car: Visitors can rent a car from major cities like Osaka, Kobe or Tokyo, to drive around the island.

Airports Near Shodoshima

The nearest airports to Shodoshima are:

  • Takamatsu Airport (Kagawa Prefecture)
  • Okayama Airport (Okayama Prefecture)

Bus Stations Near Shodoshima

  • Kojima Bus Station (Okayama Prefecture)
  • Takamatsu Bus Station (Kagawa Prefecture)

Other Options

  • Train: Visitors can take a train to JR Takamatsu Station or JR Kojima Station followed by a bus or taxi to reach Shodoshima.
  • Taxi: Visitors can take a taxi from Takamatsu port or other major cities for more convenience.

How to Get Around in Shodoshima

Shodoshima is a small island that can be explored either by renting a car, motorcycle or bicycle. Visitors can also utilize the local bus services with well-scheduled bus routes that take you to most of the island’s popular tourist locations. Explore Shodoshima with Jinriki (human-powered transportation), an old-fashioned cart pushed by a vendor.

Where to Go Next from Shodoshima: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Shodoshima is a good base for exploring nearby destinations and excursions. Visitors can enjoy day trips to nearby locations such as the historic port town of Takamatsu or Mizushima, where world heritage sites are located.

Conclusion

Shodoshima is a beautiful island with much to offer visitors. From stunning natural attractions to rich cultural experiences, visitors are sure to have a fantastic time exploring the island. We hope this comprehensive travel guide has provided useful insights and tips for exploring all that Shodoshima has to offer.
