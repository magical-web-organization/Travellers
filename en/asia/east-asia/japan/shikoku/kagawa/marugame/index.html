Introduction to Marugame

Marugame is a quaint city located in the Kagawa prefecture of Shikoku island, Japan. Known for its historical landmarks, natural beauty and artistic culture, the city is a must-visit destination for those who love to explore. Marugame is especially famous for its majestic Marugame Castle, built in the 17th century, located in the heart of the city – which attracts tourists from all over the world. Apart from the ancient castle, Marugame boasts a plethora of attractions – from scenic hiking trails to locally-sourced cuisine – making it an excellent location to spend a fun-filled, culturally enriching holiday.

List of Places to Visit in Marugame

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Marugame Castle

Marugame Castle, which dates back to the 17th century, is one of the most popular tourist destinations in Marugame. The magnificent edifice, also known as “Crow Castle” due to its black color, is a symbol of strength and fortitude. Visitors can take a guided tour of the castle to explore the vast historical and cultural significance the castle holds.

Marugame Genichiro Inokuma Museum of Contemporary Art

Marugame Genichiro Inokuma Museum of Contemporary Art is the first major museum of contemporary art on Shikoku island. The impressive collection of modern art features works from numerous artists all across the globe. Anyone interested in art must not miss out on this world-class museum.

Natural Attractions of Marugame: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Mount Iino

Mount Iino is the highest mountain in the Marugame region, and hiking up to the peak is an excellent way to spend a day. The hike is challenging but rewarding, as the highest peak provides a breathtaking panorama of the surrounding area.

Kōnomineji Temple

Kōnomineji Temple is located amidst the lush green forests of Marugame, and is a great place to find some serenity in nature. The temple’s serene environment is ideal for meditation and relaxation and attracts visitors seeking inner peace.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Marugame

Marugame Castle, Mount Iino, and Kōnomineji Temple all have fantastic hiking trails – making Marugame an ideal location for hiking enthusiasts. In addition, there are various biking routes that provide a unique perspective of the city, and offer an enjoyable way to explore the city.

Best Things to Do in Marugame: Activities and Tours

Marugame has numerous activities that offer visitors an interactive experience of the city’s rich history and culture. Here are a few popular options worth checking out:

Castle Town Walking Tour

Taking a castle town walking tour is an excellent way to get a feel of the local culture and heritage in Marugame. This intriguing walking tour is guided, giving tourists a chance to see the main sights and learn from a local guide.

Art Studios and Workshops Tour

Marugame has a significant creative scene, with several art workshops and studios throughout the city. A guided “Art Studios and Workshops Tour” tour takes visitors inside the artist’s studios and provides the opportunity to learn and explore the creation process of unique works of art.

Week-Long Itinerary for Marugame

Here’s an example itinerary of how to spend a week in Marugame:

Day 1: Explore Marugame Castle, and marvel at its magnificent architecture.

Day 2: Hike up Mount Iino and enjoy the panoramic views.

Day 3: Visit Kōnomineji Temple to find inner peace.

Day 4: Book a Castle Town Walking Tour to learn about the city’s culture and heritage.

Day 5: Visit the Marugame Genichiro Inokuma Museum of Contemporary Art for a unique, world-class art experience.

Day 6: Take a guided “Art Studios and Workshops Tour” and explore the city’s unique artistic culture.

Day 7: Sample the local delicacies of Marugame before departing the picturesque city.

Food and Drink Scene of Marugame

Must-Try Dishes

Sanuki Udon

Sanuki udon is a famous type of udon noodle created in Kagawa Prefecture, which is the location of Marugame. With thin and firm noodles and a simple broth, Sanuki udon is a local staple and must-try dish.

Tamamo Sushi

Tamamo sushi is made from seasoned, vinegared rice, and includes an array of sushi toppings such as shrimp, egg, and various vegetables – this is perfect for those who love sushi.

Local Beverages

Kiku Wine

The Kagawa Prefecture is famous for Kiku wine, a unique drink that combines local pears and grapes into a region-specific, crisp-tasting wine. A must-try for wine lovers!

Shikoku Beer

Brewed with local water, Shikoku Beer is an excellent beverage for those who love the taste of Japanese beer. This beer has a unique taste that pairs perfect;y with local Marugame cuisine.

Where to Stay in Marugame: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Marugame has a variety of accommodation options ranging from budget-friendly hostels to luxury hotels. Here are a few suggestions:

Marugame Guesthouse

Marugame Guesthouse is a cozy and comfortable hostel located near the city center. The friendly staff provide an excellent atmosphere, and a fantastic way to meet other travelers.

Dormy Inn Marugame

Dormy Inn Marugame is an excellent choice for those looking for a mid-range hotel experience. Located near the city center, guests can enjoy comfortable rooms and convenient amenities.

Grand Hotel Takamatsu

Grand Hotel Takamatsu is a little far from the city center, but it’s a great option for those seeking luxury accommodation. This hotel is equipped with an indoor pool, spa, and gym, providing guests with a memorable experience.

What to Buy in Marugame: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Japanese Traditional Crafts

Marugame has a rich collection of Japanese traditional crafts, including pottery, paper lanterns, and kutani ware. Souvenir shops within the city center offer a range of unique pieces perfect for bringing the traditional Japanese culture back home.

Uchiwa Fans

Uchiwa fans are a unique Japanese product – a fan made from traditional bamboo and paper that features unique designs specific to Marugame. These fans make an excellent souvenir to remember your trip by.

Local Culture and Customs of Marugame

Marugame is a historically rich city with a unique culture and customs. It’s important to follow proper etiquette and respect the local culture. It’s worth noting that Japanese culture is based on mutual respect – it’s best to be respectful to locals, and they will return the favor with overwhelming hospitality and kindness.

Best Time to Visit Marugame

Marugame can be visited year-round, with each season offering something unique. However, the best time to visit is during the spring and autumn seasons, when the weather is mild and pleasant.

Do’s and Don’ts of Marugame

Do:

  • Do be polite and respectful toward the locals, and always use polite forms of expression.

  • Do remove your shoes when entering homes or temples, this is a custom in Japan.

  • Do try street food and local products.

Don’t:

  • Don’t litter anywhere, and always dispose of trash in provided bins.

  • Don’t wear shoes indoors in Japanese homes, temples, and other establishments.

  • Don’t talk loudly in public, as it is considered rude in Japanese culture.

Safety and Health

Safety in Marugame

Marugame is a safe city; however, precautions should be taken to ensure a smooth trip. Always be aware of your belongings and keep them in a secure location at all times. It’s essential to remain alert, especially in crowded areas of the city.

Health in Marugame

Marugame is known for its healthy cuisine and lifestyle. However, health concerns like any other city should be considered and visitors should take necessary precautions, such as carrying any necessary medication with them, checking the water sources, and making sure to avoid harmful activities.

Getting to Marugame: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Marugame

The nearest airport to Marugame is Takamatsu Airport (TAK), located approximately 30 kilometers from Marugame. Visitors can take a bus or taxi to reach Marugame from the airport.

Bus Stations Near Marugame

The best way to travel within and around Marugame is through the bus network. There are several bus stations around the city, and getting around is relatively simple and affordable.

Other Options

Visitors also have the option of travel via train, which links Marugame to neighboring cities and major urban centers.

How to Get Around in Marugame

Marugame is a compact city, and getting around is relatively easy. The bus network is reliable and has routes all over the city, making it the best way to get around.

Where to Go Next from Marugame: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Marugame is an excellent location to explore the surrounding areas of Shikoku Island. A few interesting nearby destinations include:

  • Kotohira-gu Shrine in Kotohira
  • Ritsurin Garden in Takamatsu
  • Ōkubo-ji Temple in Mitoyo

Conclusion

Marugame is a culturally memorable and relaxing city that offers travelers an experience unlike any other. The city’s vibrancy lies in its rich history, cultural offerings, and natural beauty. A trip to Marugame is the perfect destination to slow down, relax, and experience authentic Japanese culture.
