Introduction to Tanjung Resang

Nestled in the southern region of Johor, Malaysia, Tanjung Resang is a picturesque coastal town located next to the South China Sea. This laid-back destination lures visitors with its pristine beaches, diverse cultural attractions, natural landmarks, and delectable cuisine.

Travelers seeking a quiet escape to enjoy nature and fascinating history will find much to explore in Tanjung Resang. The town provides a perfect combination of relaxation and adventure, making it an excellent getaway for families, couples, and solo travellers.

In this comprehensive travel guide, we will take you on a journey through the best things to do, places to visit, and where to stay in Tanjung Resang. You’ll also encounter fun activities and tours, along with must-try dishes and local culture. From the best hiking and biking routes to exciting excursions, you’ll find practical tips to make the most of your trip.

List of Places to Visit in Tanjung Resang

Whether you’re an adventure-seeker or a history buff, Tanjung Resang has something entertaining for every individual. Here’s a list of places to visit in Tanjung Resang:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Muzium Melayu

Muzium Melayu, also known as the Malay Museum, is a heritage house located in Kampung Melayu Resam, showcasing the traditional Malay way of life. Visitors can view various historical artefacts, including traditional Malay weapons, textiles, furniture, and household items. The museum allows visitors to learn about Malayan traditions and customs and is an excellent opportunity to immerse oneself in Malaysian culture.

Pengkalan Balak Historical Complex

The Pengkalan Balak Historical Complex is a cluster of six traditional wooden houses supported on stilts, situated in the fishing village of Pengkalan Balak. The complex provides a genuine glimpse into the Malay way of life, and visitors can learn about the ancient fishing traditions of Malay culture. Experience and observe the fishing and boat-making process and taste some seafood delicacies – fresh from the fishermen’s catch.

Natural Attractions of Tanjung Resang: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Taman Negara Endau - Rompin

Taman Negara Endau-Rompin is a vast natural reserve that stretches through Johor and Pahang. It is home to numerous plant and animal species, including tigers, elephants, and the endangered Sumatran rhinoceros. Visitors can explore the virgin rainforest, go trekking through challenging terrain, and even set up overnight camps in the heart of the jungle.

Endau River

The Endau River is a stunning natural area, featuring crystal-clear waters and surrounded by lush greenery. Visitors can enjoy a river cruise up the Endau River and enjoy scenic views of the dense jungle, see various bird species and primates. At the river’s mouth, visitors can take a leisurely boat ride through the mangrove forests and marvel at the various creatures that call the area home.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Tanjung Resang

Hiking and biking in Tanjung Resang provide the perfect way to explore nature and burn off some calories. So pack up your trekking shoes and cycling gear and discover some of the best trails.

Gunung Pulai

Gunung Pulai, located about 30 minutes from Tanjung Resang, is an enjoyable trekking spot, featuring lush greenery and stunning vistas. The ascent takes around two to three hours and is moderately challenging. Hikers can enjoy great panoramic views of the surrounding forest, Johor Bahru city, and even Singapore in the distance.

Endau Rompin National Park

Endau Rompin National Park, a protected UNESCO World Heritage Site, is a natural wonderland featuring crystal-clear rivers, ancient rainforests, and towering waterfalls. The park offers various trekking routes that cater to different skill levels, taking visitors along riverbanks, through dense forests, and over hilltops. Cyclists can enjoy various trails along scenic routes of the national park and learn about flora and fauna.

Best Things to Do in Tanjung Resang: Activities and Tours

River Cruise

Take a leisurely afternoon river cruise along the Endau River and discover the mangroves’ wonders and the area’s unique wildlife. Enjoy the tranquil surroundings as the boat winds its way through the dense forest, and experience the unique connection between the river and mangroves.

Volunteer at a Marine Conservation Project

Help protect the marine environment by volunteering at a turtle sanctuary project. Here, visitors can learn about sea turtle protection and conservation. An experience that is both enlightening and enthralling to be part of.

Week-Long Itinerary for Tanjung Resang

Here is a detailed seven-day itinerary to make the most of your trip to Tanjung Resang:

Day 1

Arrive at Tanjung Resang, check-in to your hotel, and enjoy some leisure time by the beach. Take a stroll and enjoy the surroundings or read a book while relaxing on the beach; unwind and let go of the stress from your daily routine.

Day 2

Discover the local fishing village in Pengkalan Balak and visit the historical complex. Learn about the local fishing culture and traditions and try fresh seafood dishes in local dining settings.

Day 3

Visit the Endau River for a river cruise and take in the breathtaking views of the natural environment. Observe local wildlife and learn about the intricate ecosystem.

Day 4

Explore the Tanjung Resang beaches. Take a swim or go snorkeling to discover the underwater world.

Day 5

Hike or Bike Gunung Pulai, enjoy trekking through the lush greenery and experience panoramic views of the surrounding forest and cities.

Day 6

Visit Taman Negara Endau-Rompin and explore the unique and pristine nature reserve.

Day 7

Volunteer at a marine conservation project and learn about sea turtle protection and conservation. Then, in the evening, enjoy a relaxing evening with local entertainment.

Food and Drink Scene of Tanjung Resang

The food in Tanjung Resang offers a unique blend of local Malay, Chinese, and Indian cuisines, which are both tangy and tasty.

Must-Try Dishes

Nasi Lemak, a beloved Malaysian dish consisting of coconut rice, sambal, and a variety of toppings, is a must-try. Try some traditional and exotic seafood dishes as well, like ikan bakar, otak-otak, and satay.

Local Beverages

Try the local Kopi-Tiam coffee shops and enjoy specialty Malaysian coffee and teas.

Where to Stay in Tanjung Resang: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Tanjung Resang provides visitors with a wide range of accommodation options, from beachside hotels, guesthouses, and renting private villas.

Tiara Beach Resort

Tiara Beach Resort is a family-friendly resort located on Tanjung Resang’s coast. The resort offers a broad range of amenities, including freshwater swimming pools, massage services, and Western and Oriental-style restaurants.

Tanjung Inn

If you’re on a budget, Tanjung Inn offers affordable, comfortable rooms with easy access to the beach. Enjoy the serene surroundings and a pleasant view of the ocean.

What to Buy in Tanjung Resang: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Visit the nearby Kampung Melayu Resam market and shop for traditional handmade batik souvenirs with intricate designs. You can also buy some tasty food items to bring back home to share with your friends and family.

Local Culture and Customs of Tanjung Resang

The local culture of Tanjung Resang is rooted in the Malay customs and traditions, which blend with the Chinese and Indian cultures. Visitors should always remember to respect local customs and dress accordingly, taking off shoes in religious buildings.

Best Time to Visit Tanjung Resang

The peak season to visit Tanjung Resang starts from December to February and again from June to August. The climate is mostly sunny with occasional rainfall, making it suitable for outdoor activities.

Do’s and Don’ts of Tanjung Resang

Do:

  • Respect local customs and traditions.
  • Dress appropriately while visiting religious shrines and places of worship.
  • Try the local delicacies.
  • Visit the beaches and take part in various activities available.

Don’t:

  • Litter or create noise pollution in public places.
  • Show public displays of affection openly.
  • Disrespect nature by littering or destroying natural environments.

Safety and Health

Safety in Tanjung Resang

Tanjung Resang is generally a safe destination with a low crime rate. But as with any destination, visitors should always stay alert and be aware of their surroundings.

Health in Tanjung Resang

The medical facilities in Tanjung Resang are decent, but visitors should carry essential medications and receive vaccinations before their trip.

Getting to Tanjung Resang: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Tanjung Resang

The nearest airport is Senai International Airport, located 89 km away. Visitors can take a taxi or rent a car from the airport to get to Tanjung Resang.

Bus Stations Near Tanjung Resang

The nearest bus station is located in Mersing, where visitors can take a bus to Tanjung Resang or rent a car.

Other Options

Visitors can also take a taxi or rent their transport to get to Tanjung Resang.

How to Get Around in Tanjung Resang

Renting a car or scooter is the best way to get around Tanjung Resang. Visitors can also use taxis or chartered boats for transport.

Where to Go Next from Tanjung Resang: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Tanjung Resang is a great base to explore nearby destinations and excursions. Here are some suggestions:

  • Pulau Tioman, a paradise Island located south of Tanjung Resang.
  • Johor Bahru city, a vibrant city with fascinating history and culture.
  • Desaru Beach, a beautiful beach on Malaysia’s eastern coast.

Conclusion

Tanjung Resang is a fascinating destination that offers a mix of history, culture, and natural beauty. Visitors can explore the many historical sites, hike through lush greenery, and relax on splendid golden beaches.
