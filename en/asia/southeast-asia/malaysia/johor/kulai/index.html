Introduction to Kulai

Kulai is a town in Johor, Malaysia, located in Southeast Asia, with a population of approximately 243,671 people. The town is known for its historical sites, vibrant culture, beautiful natural scenery, and delicious food and drinks. The town has attracted a large number of tourists from all over the world who come to enjoy the excitement and the magnetism that this city offers. In this comprehensive travel guide to Kulai, we will highlight the best places and activities to experience in this charming town.

List of Places to Visit in Kulai

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  1. Kulai Heritage Centre: where visitors can learn about the history and culture of the town through interactive exhibits, artifacts, and photographs.
  2. Kulai Old Street: a traditional street with Chinese architecture and shops that take visitors back in time to traditional old Malaysia.
  3. Kota Tinggi Museum: a natural history museum that provides insight into the flora and fauna of the area.
  4. Sultan Abu Bakar Memorial Museum: a former palace museum exhibiting the royal collections and offers some of the richest insights into the state’s history.

Natural Attractions of Kulai: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  1. Gunung Pulai Recreational Forest: a green oasis highlighted by various waterfalls and natural landscapes perfect for short hiking or trekking.
  2. Senai-Desaru Highway Scenic Viewpoint: A perfect spot for nature lovers to take a rest and appreciate nature while enjoying stunning views overlooking the Senai-Desaru highway.
  3. Pulai Springs Resort: One of the most peaceful and scenic places in Kulai. This resort offers a golf course, spa and stunning natural scenery, making it the perfect romantic getaway for couples and nature lovers.
  4. Forest City Golf Course: Known for its world-class golf courses, this beautiful location also offers breathtaking views of the ocean.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Kulai

  1. Gunung Pulai Recreational Forest: As mentioned earlier, the forest offers hiking and trekking opportunities and its lush greenery and natural waterfall only makes these activities more refreshing.
  2. Kulai Rail Trail: Built over an old railway line, the trail is perfect for a quiet walk, bike ride, or enjoying natural surroundings.

Best Things to Do in Kulai: Activities and Tours

  1. Visit the Johor Premium Outlet: a luxury shopping mall that provides retail therapy for shopaholics in Kulai.
  2. Indulge in Local Street Food: Kulai is known for its delicious street food, such as Otak-Otak, Laksa Johor, and Kacang Pool, which are must-try savory dishes that will ignite your taste buds and leave you wanting more.
  3. Experience the Nightlife: Kulai comes alive at night, with many bars and pubs offering live music, karaoke, or comedy acts, making it perfect for bar-hopping and nightlife enthusiast.
  4. Join a Cooking Class: This class is perfect for visitors who want to learn to cook their favorite Malaysian dishes.

Food and Drink Scene of Kulai

Must-Try Dishes

  1. Otak-Otak: A spicy fish cake made from ground fish, spices, and herbs, wrapped in banana leaves.
  2. Laksa Johor: A type of spicy noodle soup made with fish broth, chili paste, and coconut milk.
  3. Kacang Pool: A local favorite served with bread and boiled eggs, consists of boiled beans in a spicy gravy.
  4. Mee Bandung: A noodle dish with a spicy, sweet and sour broth and beef or shrimp.

Local Beverages

  1. Teh Tarik: Malaysia’s famous tea is a brewed milk tea with a perfect blend of tea and milk.
  2. Air Barli: A refreshing barley water drink that can be found throughout Kulai’s hawker stalls.
  3. Kopi: A local coffee that is brewed with sweetened condensed milk and is served hot or cold.

Where to Stay in Kulai: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

There are plenty of accommodations to choose from in Kulai, Johor. Some options are:

  • Pulai Springs Resort: One of the most luxurious resorts in Kulai, offering sophisticated rooms with natural surroundings.
  • Creator Hotel: A budget-friendly hotel that offers comfortable rooms to accommodate any budget.
  • Cornor Stay: This guesthouse is located in the heart of Kulai and provides excellent facilities and services.

What to Buy in Kulai: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  1. Pewterware and Silverware: Kulai is famous for its handmade pewter and silver products, such as trays, cups, and jewelry.
  2. Wooden Products: Kulai is also famous for its wooden crafts, such as combs and home decor items.
  3. Customized T-Shirts: Custom-made T-Shirts with Kulai’s landmarks or images or messages make for great souvenirs.

Local Culture and Customs of Kulai

Best Time to Visit Kulai

Kulai can be visited all year round, but the best time to visit the town is from December to January when the weather is dry and cool, making it the perfect season for outdoor activities such as hiking and cycling.

Do’s and Don’ts of Kulai

Do:

  1. Be respectful of the local culture and traditions.
  2. Try to speak in the local language or learn a few basic phrases.
  3. Dress modestly when visiting religious sites.
  4. Give donations when visiting any religious sites.

Don’t:

  1. Don’t engage in or encourage illegal or inappropriate behavior.
  2. Don’t litter and keep the environment clean.
  3. Don’t disrespect the local culture, customs, and traditions.

Safety and Health

Safety in Kulai

Kulai is generally a safe place to visit, but visitors should still take precautions such as being aware of their surroundings, not flashing valuables, and keeping personal belongings safe from theft or pickpockets.

Health in Kulai

Visitors should be aware of the weather as it can be hot and humid in Kulai. Visitors should also stay hydrated and bring sunscreen when outdoors for extended periods.

Getting to Kulai: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Kulai

  • Senai International Airport: the nearest airport to Kulai, located approximately 18km away.

Bus Stations Near Kulai

  • Kulai Bus Station: the primary bus station in Kulai, with regular bus services connecting to various parts of Johor.

Other Options

Visitors can also get around Kulai by hiring a car, taxi, or using ride-sharing services.

How to Get Around in Kulai

Getting around Kulai is relatively easy and affordable. Visitors can rent bikes or scooters or use taxis or ride-sharing services. For a more budget-friendly option, visitors can opt for buses or local trains.

Where to Go Next from Kulai: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Johor Bahru: located approximately 31 km south of Kulai, Johor Bahru is a bustling city that boasts many cultural and historical sites, shopping, and dining experiences.
  • Singapore: located approximately 50 km south of Kulai, Singapore is a popular tourist destination that offers a wide range of attractions, shopping, and dining experiences.

Conclusion

Kulai has a rich cultural heritage, beautiful natural scenery, and delicious food and drinks. The town is easy to get around and well-connected to other Malaysian destinations. Whether you are looking for outdoor adventure, delicious food, or cultural experiences, Kulai is the perfect destination for you.
