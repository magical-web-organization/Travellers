Introduction to Johor Bahru

Johor Bahru is the capital of the state of Johor, located in the southern part of Malaysia, near the border with Singapore. Known as the “Southern Gateway to Malaysia,” it is the third-largest city in the country and has emerged as a hub of economic growth and development. From the historical landmarks to the theme parks, Johor Bahru offers a unique blend of traditional Malay culture and modern lifestyle that is fascinating for visitors.

List of Places to Visit in Johor Bahru

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Johor Bahru has a rich history and several museums and monuments that showcase its cultural and architectural heritage. The following are some of the must-visit cultural attractions in Johor Bahru:

  • Sultan Abu Bakar State Mosque: This iconic landmark of Johor Bahru is an impressive piece of architecture inspired by both Moorish and Victorian styles. It is the largest mosque in Johor and can accommodate up to 2,000 worshippers.

  • Istana Bukit Serene: It is the official residence of the Sultan of Johor, located on a hilltop near the Johor Causeway. The palace was built in 1933, and its vast grounds of 53 hectares are open to the public.

  • Johor Bahru Old Chinese Temple: Also known as the Thean Hou Kong, this temple is more than 140 years old and dedicated to the patron goddess of the Hokkien Chinese, Thean Hou. It is one of the oldest Taoist temples in Malaysia, and its intricate design is worth taking a look at.

  • Johor Bahru Chinese Heritage Museum: This museum showcases the everyday life of the Chinese community living in Johor Bahru during the 19th and early 20th centuries. Exhibits include period furniture, clothing, decorative trinkets, and other artifacts.

Natural Attractions of Johor Bahru: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Apart from the culturally significant places, there are several natural attractions in Johor Bahru that are worth exploring. Here are some of the top spots for tourists:

  • Gunung Pulai: It is a popular hiking spot located about 45 minutes from the city center of Johor Bahru. The peak stands at 654m and offers breathtaking views of the surrounding forest and hills.

  • Istana Garden: This beautiful garden is located in the compound of Istana Bukit Serene and offers a serene atmosphere with a variety of trees, flowers, and well-manicured lawns. It is a perfect place to relax and unwind.

  • Desaru Beach: Located on the east coast of Johor Bahru, this seaside area is home to some of the best beaches in Malaysia. Stretching over 22km, Desaru is known for its clear waters, pristine sand, and beach activities.

  • Danga Bay: A popular waterfront entertainment and recreational complex located in the western part of Johor Bahru. The tranquil seafront offers a boardwalk, retail outlets, and waterfront dining options which cater to both tourists and locals.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Johor Bahru

Johor Bahru has several hiking trails that enthusiasts can explore to experience the natural beauty of the area. Many of the routes are accessible via public transport. Here are some popular trails:

  • Gunung Pulai: This is a well-maintained trail that can be done in half a day. It is known for its challenging terrain and steep slopes.

  • Kota Tinggi Waterfall: The trail comprises of cascading falls and dense vegetation, making it a popular spot among hikers and nature enthusiasts.

  • Mawai Eco Park: It is an eco-tourism park that offers a variety of trekking trails and cycling routes. Visitors can take their bikes on a 15km-long adventure trail that snakes through the forest.

Best Things to Do in Johor Bahru: Activities and Tours

Johor Bahru is a hub of activity, from theme parks and nature reserves to shopping and food tours. Here are some of the best things to do in Johor Bahru:

  • Legoland Malaysia: It is the first Legoland in Asia and offers thrilling roller coasters, water rides, and other attractions. The park is suitable for all ages and promises an unforgettable experience.

  • Hello Kitty Town: This theme park is dedicated to all things Hello Kitty, including themed rides and interactive exhibits. It is a must-visit spot for families with young children.

  • Johor Bahru City Square: It is a large shopping mall located in the heart of the city. Visitors can shop for souvenirs, clothes, and electronic gadgets. There are also several restaurants and cafes for dining options.

  • Johor Bahru Night Food Market Tour: It is a guided tour that takes visitors through the best street food stalls in Johor Bahru. The local cuisine is a unique blend of Malay, Chinese, and Indian spices, offering flavorsome treats.

Week-Long Itinerary for Johor Bahru

A week-long itinerary in Johor Bahru can be a perfect blend of traditional culture, outdoor activities, and shopping. Here’s a sample itinerary:

Day 1: Visit Sultan Abu Bakar State Mosque, Istana Bukit Serene, and Johor Bahru Chinese Heritage Museum.

Day 2: Explore the natural beauty of Gunung Pulai.

Day 3: Head to Legoland Malaysia for a day of adventure.

Day 4: Visit Hello Kitty Town and Johor Bahru City Square for shopping and dining options.

Day 5: Discover the street food scene of Johor Bahru with a food tour.

Day 6: Visit the Desaru Beach and relax by the seaside.

Day 7: Visit nearby destinations like Singapore or other parts of Malaysia.

Food and Drink Scene of Johor Bahru

Must-Try Dishes

Johor Bahru’s culinary scene is influenced by Malay, Chinese, and Indian cuisines, making it a gastronomical adventure. Here are some must-try dishes:

  • Nasi Lemak: It is a Malaysian classic dish made with fragrant coconut rice, sambal, ikan bilis, peanuts, and boiled egg. The dish is a perfect start to the day and widely available in local eateries and food courts.

  • Otak-otak: This is a traditional fish cake made with ground fish, coconut milk, herbs, and spices. The mixture is wrapped in a banana leaf and grilled. Otak-otak can be eaten as a side dish or a main dish.

  • Mee Bandung: It is a spicy noodle dish made with chicken, prawn, chili, and tomato paste. The dish originated from Muar, a town near Johor Bahru, and is a signature dish of the state.

  • Kaya Toast: It is a breakfast delicacy consisting of thin slices of crispy toast spread with homemade kaya jam, made of coconut milk, sugar, and eggs.

Local Beverages

Johor Bahru has a vibrant café culture, and visitors can enjoy a variety of drinks alongside their meals. Here are some notable beverages:

  • Teh Tarik: It is a sweet and creamy milk tea that is a staple in Malaysian cuisine. The tea is poured back and forth between two vessels to create a frothy layer on top.

  • Kopi: This is traditional Malaysian coffee made with a blend of coffee beans and sweetened condensed milk. It is a popular breakfast beverage in Johor Bahru.

  • Bandung: It is a refreshing rose syrup mixed with milk and ice, a sweet concoction that is perfect for the hot and humid weather.

Where to Stay in Johor Bahru: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Johor Bahru has several accommodation options to suit every budget and preference. Here are some of the top picks:

  • Thistle Johor Bahru: A luxury hotel located in the city center, offering an outdoor pool, spa, and restaurant.

  • Container Gardenstay: A unique hostel built from shipping containers, providing a cozy and unique experience at affordable prices.

  • The Westin Desaru Coast Resort: A beachfront property located in the Desaru area, offering spacious rooms, multiple dining options, and a kids’ club.

What to Buy in Johor Bahru: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Johor Bahru offers several souvenir options perfect for bringing back memories of your trip. Here are some of the must-buys:

  • Batik Fabric: It is a traditional fabric with intricate designs and patterns. It is available in various colors, and you can choose from pre-made clothes, handkerchiefs, scarves, or even by the meter.

  • Kueh: It is a popular Malaysian snack made from rice flour, sugar, and coconut milk. The snacks are available in different colors and shapes and are perfect for a quick snack.

  • Pewter: It is a metal alloy made from tin, copper, and antimony. The metal is used to make decorative items like bowls, vases, and figurines. It is widely available in Johor Bahru and makes for an excellent souvenir.

Local Culture and Customs of Johor Bahru

Johor Bahru is a multicultural city, and visitors should be respectful of the local customs and traditions. Here are some things to keep in mind:

  • Malaysians follow Islamic teachings, so visitors should dress modestly when visiting religious sites.

  • It is customary to remove shoes before entering homes, mosques, and some restaurants.

  • Malaysians respect their elders and are a little more reserved, so visitors should avoid being too loud and boisterous in public areas.

Best Time to Visit Johor Bahru

The best time to visit Johor Bahru is from March to September, when the weather is dry and sunny. However, visitors should also be prepared for occasional sporadic showers, so they should pack appropriately.

Do’s and Don’ts of Johor Bahru

Do:

  • Dress modestly when visiting religious sites.

  • Be respectful of the local culture and customs.

  • Carry Malaysian currency with you at all times.

Don’t:

  • Touch people on the head, as it is a sign of disrespect in Malay culture.

  • Point with your feet in any direction, as it is considered impolite.

  • Bring drugs or other prohibited substances into the country, as it is a severe offense punishable by law.

Safety and Health

Safety in Johor Bahru

Johor Bahru is a relatively safe city with low crime rates. However, visitors should take necessary precautions to ensure their safety.

  • Avoid walking alone in deserted areas at night.

  • Keep your documents, passports, and other valuables in a safe place.

  • Always use licensed taxis or ride-sharing services when traveling around the city.

Health in Johor Bahru

Visitors should be aware of the health risks when traveling to Johor Bahru and take necessary precautions.

  • Drink bottled water instead of tap water.

  • Apply insect repellent to avoid mosquito-borne diseases like dengue fever.

  • Visit a travel clinic before traveling to Johor Bahru to get the necessary vaccinations.

Getting to Johor Bahru: Transportation Options and Logistics

Johor Bahru is easily accessible via different modes of transportation from Kuala Lumpur, Singapore, and other parts of Malaysia.

Airports Near Johor Bahru

  • Senai International Airport: Located about 30km north of Johor Bahru City, this airport is served by regional and domestic airlines.

Bus Stations Near Johor Bahru

  • Larkin Bus Terminal: The largest bus terminal in Johor Bahru and a transit hub for various regional destinations.

Other Options

  • Train services are available to Johor Bahru from Kuala Lumpur and Singapore.

  • Visitors can also take a taxi or rent a car to reach Johor Bahru from Singapore.

How to Get Around in Johor Bahru

Public transportation options such as buses and trains are available in Johor Bahru. Visitors can also use ride-hailing services like Grab or rent a car for more flexibility.

Where to Go Next from Johor Bahru: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

There are several destinations near Johor Bahru that are worth exploring:

  • Singapore: The city-state of Singapore is only about an hour’s drive from Johor Bahru and is a great destination for a day trip or a weekend getaway.

  • Melaka: It is a historic city located 200km northwest of Johor Bahru, known for its UNESCO World Heritage sites, street art, and museums.

  • Desaru: A tourist hotspot that is located about 45 minutes away. Visitors can indulge in several beach and water activities along with various attractions.

Conclusion

Johor Bahru is an exciting cultural and urban destination with much to offer, from historical buildings and outdoor adventures to delicious food and regional shopping opportunities. With so many fantastic activities and destinations, it’s no wonder that Johor Bahru has become a popular destination for tourists from all over the world.
