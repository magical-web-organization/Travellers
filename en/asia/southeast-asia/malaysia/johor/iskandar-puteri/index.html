Introduction to Iskandar Puteri

Iskandar Puteri, formerly known as Nusajaya, is a rapidly-developing city in Johor, Malaysia. It is part of the larger Iskandar Malaysia economic zone, which encompasses the southernmost tip of the Malay Peninsula and is a major hub for tourism, education, and business.

With its modern infrastructure, beautiful natural scenery, and close proximity to Singapore, Iskandar Puteri has become a popular destination for travelers looking to discover a unique blend of modern amenities and traditional culture. This travel guide will provide you with everything you need to know to plan your trip to Iskandar Puteri.

List of Places to Visit in Iskandar Puteri

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Iskandar Puteri has a rich history dating back to the 16th century when it was the center of the Johor Sultanate. Visitors can explore this history through a variety of museums and historical sites:

  • Johor Bahru Old Chinese Temple: Also known as the Thean Hock Keong Temple, this temple is located in the heart of Johor Bahru and is the oldest Chinese temple in the area. It is dedicated to the worship of the Taoist god of prosperity, Tua Pek Kong.
  • Kota Iskandar: The administrative center of the state government of Johor, Kota Iskandar is a stunning complex of buildings and gardens that showcases both modern and traditional Johor architectural styles.
  • Sultan Abu Bakar State Mosque: This mosque is one of the most beautiful in Malaysia, featuring a blend of Moorish and Victorian architecture. It was completed in 1900 and can accommodate up to 2,000 worshippers.
  • Royal Abu Bakar Museum: This museum is located within the Sultan Abu Bakar State Mosque complex and comprises two separate buildings: one housing the personal memorabilia of the Sultan, and the other showcasing the history of Johor.
  • Muzium Tokoh Johor: This museum honors the contributions of Johor’s founding fathers to the development of the state and showcases their personal artifacts and memorabilia.

Natural Attractions of Iskandar Puteri: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Iskandar Puteri is home to a wide array of natural attractions, from gorgeous beaches to lush tropical forests:

  • Puteri Harbour: A bustling marina and entertainment complex, Puteri Harbour is home to a variety of restaurants, bars, and shops. It is also a departure point for boats heading to nearby islands.
  • Danga Bay: A popular spot for weekend family picnics, Danga Bay is a large waterfront park that features a variety of recreational activities, including fishing, boating, and cycling.
  • Gunung Pulai: For nature lovers and adventure seekers, Gunung Pulai offers a challenging hike to a summit that offers breathtaking views of the surrounding rainforest.
  • Tanjung Piai National Park: Located at the southernmost point of mainland Asia, this park is a designated UNESCO Biosphere Reserve and is home to unique mangrove forests and a diverse array of flora and fauna.
  • Desaru Beach: One of Malaysia’s most popular beaches, Desaru offers miles of sandy coastline, pristine waters, and plenty of watersports activities.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Iskandar Puteri

For those who want to explore the natural beauty of Iskandar Puteri at a slower pace, the city offers a variety of hiking and biking trails:

  • Plaza Sentosa Trail: A scenic trail located on the outskirts of Johor Bahru, this path winds through lush forests and past rushing streams.
  • Ulu Pulai Trail: For a more challenging hike, Ulu Pulai takes you through Gunung Pulai Forest Reserve to a stunning waterfall.
  • Bukit Timbalan Loop Trail: Located in the heart of Johor Bahru, this trail takes you through the city’s historic sites and cultural landmarks.
  • Puteri Harbour Bike Trail: A popular trail for cyclists and leisurely riders alike, this 7-kilometer trail takes you around the picturesque marina and entertainment district of Puteri Harbour.

Best Things to Do in Iskandar Puteri: Activities and Tours

There are many things to do in Iskandar Puteri that cater to all interests. These include:

  • Legoland: A theme park for the whole family, Legoland features Lego-themed rides, attractions, and even a hotel.
  • Hello Kitty Town: Another family-friendly attraction, Hello Kitty Town is an indoor theme park that features rides, games, and interactive exhibits featuring everyone’s favorite cat.
  • Pasar Karat: This street market located in downtown Johor Bahru offers a wide array of handmade crafts, vintage items, and delicious street food.
  • Johor Bahru Night Market: Every night, the streets near Komtar JBCC come alive with vendors selling everything from clothes to electronics to delicious local delicacies.
  • Iskandar Puteri Adventure Park: An outdoor adventure park located in the heart of Iskandar Puteri, featuring high ropes courses, zip lines, and other outdoor adventure activities.

Week-Long Itinerary for Iskandar Puteri

A one-week trip to Iskandar Puteri might include the following itinerary:

  • Day 1: Explore the city of Johor Bahru, including the Sultan Abu Bakar State Mosque and Royal Abu Bakar Museum.
  • Day 2: Visit Legoland Malaysia and spend the day exploring the park and all its rides and attractions.
  • Day 3: Take a day trip to the Tanjung Piai National Park to explore the unique mangrove ecosystem and hike through the lush rainforest.
  • Day 4: Visit Danga Bay and spend the day relaxing on the beach, fishing, boating, and cycling.
  • Day 5: Take a tour of Kota Iskandar, the state government administrative center, and explore its stunning architecture and gardens.
  • Day 6: Explore Puteri Harbour, including Hello Kitty Town and the marina. Take a boat trip to nearby islands for snorkeling and sightseeing.
  • Day 7: Spend the day at Iskandar Puteri Adventure Park, enjoying the outdoor activities and beautiful scenery.

Food and Drink Scene of Iskandar Puteri

Must-Try Dishes

Iskandar Puteri offers a melting pot of cuisines, with influences from China, India, and Malay cultures. Some must-try dishes include:

  • Nasi Lemak: A fragrant rice dish cooked in coconut milk and served with a variety of sides, including fried chicken and sambal chili sauce.
  • Roti Canai: A flaky, crispy flatbread served with curry sauce for dipping.
  • Laksa Johor: A spicy, sour noodle soup that is unique to the Johor region, made with a fish-based broth and served with shrimp, cucumber, and chili paste.
  • Kacang Pool: A hearty bean stew served with bread, eggs, and shavings of cheese.
  • Mee Goreng Mamak: A spicy noodle dish made with Indo-Malay spices and served with fresh lime and potato crisps.

Local Beverages

To quench your thirst and cool off from the tropical heat, be sure to try these local beverages:

  • Teh Tarik: A sweet, creamy tea served hot or iced and poured back and forth between two cups to create a frothy, pleasing texture.
  • Bandung: A refreshing pink drink made from evaporated milk and rose syrup.
  • Lime Juice: A tart and refreshing drink made with fresh lime juice, water, and sugar.
  • Air Mata Kucing: A traditional Chinese drink made from longan fruit and served over ice.

Where to Stay in Iskandar Puteri: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

There are numerous accommodation options in Iskandar Puteri, ranging from budget-friendly hostels to luxury hotels. Some popular options include:

  • Hotel Jen Puteri Harbour: A modern and stylish hotel located in the heart of Puteri Harbour, offering stunning views of the marina and easy access to nearby attractions.
  • Amari Johor Bahru: A sleek and modern hotel located in the heart of Johor Bahru, offering easy access to the city’s attractions and shopping areas.
  • Zoom Inn Boutique Hotel: A budget-friendly hotel located in the heart of the city, offering simple but comfortable rooms at an affordable price.
  • Eco Bed & Breakfast: A cozy guesthouse located in the heart of Iskandar Puteri, offering simple but comfortable rooms in a lush garden setting.

What to Buy in Iskandar Puteri: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Some popular souvenirs to bring home from Iskandar Puteri include:

  • Handmade crafts: From intricately-beaded purses to intricate wood-carvings, there are many unique and handmade crafts to be found in the street markets of Iskandar Puteri.
  • Spices and herbs: Malaysia is famous for its spices and herbs, and you’ll find a wide variety of them in local markets and shops.
  • Batik textiles: Intricately designed and often brightly colored, batik textiles are a popular souvenir to bring home from Malaysia.
  • Durian-based products: Known as the “king of fruits” in Malaysia, durian is a unique and beloved flavor used in a variety of sweets, snacks and other products.

Local Culture and Customs of Iskandar Puteri

Iskandar Puteri is a melting pot of cultures, with Malays, Chinese, Indians, and a variety of other ethnic groups living and working in harmony. Visitors to Iskandar Puteri should respect local customs and traditions, such as dressing modestly when visiting religious sites and removing shoes before entering homes.

Best Time to Visit Iskandar Puteri

The best time to visit Iskandar Puteri is from November to February, when the weather is generally dry and cool. However, Iskandar Puteri can be visited year-round due to its tropical climate.

Do’s and Don’ts of Iskandar Puteri

Do:

  • Respect local customs and traditions.
  • Learn a few words of Malay to better communicate with locals.
  • Try the local cuisine and drinks.
  • Take your shoes off before entering homes or religious buildings.

Don’t:

  • Display affection in public.
  • Wear revealing clothing when visiting religious sites.
  • Touch or point with your feet.
  • Hesitate to ask for directions.

Safety and Health

Safety in Iskandar Puteri

Iskandar Puteri is a safe city, but visitors should exercise common sense precautions such as avoiding poorly-lit areas and carrying only essential valuables. It is also important to be aware of traffic laws and exercise caution when crossing streets.

Health in Iskandar Puteri

Travelers to Iskandar Puteri should be up-to-date on routine vaccinations and may consider additional vaccinations for hepatitis A and B, typhoid, and Japanese encephalitis. Visitors should also take care to avoid tap water and street food that may not be properly prepared.

Getting to Iskandar Puteri: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Iskandar Puteri

The nearest major airport to Iskandar Puteri is the Senai International Airport (JHB), which is about 30 minutes north of Johor Bahru by car. From the airport, visitors can take taxis or ride-sharing services to Iskandar Puteri.

Bus Stations Near Iskandar Puteri

The main bus station in Johor Bahru is the Larkin Sentral bus terminal, which provides connections to other parts of Malaysia and Singapore. Visitors can also take local buses, mini-buses, or taxis within Iskandar Puteri and Johor Bahru.

Other Options

Visitors can also take private cars or ride-sharing services from Singapore to Iskandar Puteri via the Second Link Expressway.

How to Get Around in Iskandar Puteri

The best way to get around in Iskandar Puteri is by car or taxi, although visitors can also use local buses and ride-sharing services. Many attractions are also within walking distance of each other.

Where to Go Next from Iskandar Puteri: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Iskandar Puteri offers easy access to a variety of nearby destinations and excursions, including:

  • Singapore: Just across the causeway from Iskandar Puteri, Singapore is a global city-state known for its blend of modernity and traditional culture.
  • Malacca: A historic city located about two hours north of Iskandar Puteri, Malacca is a UNESCO World Heritage Site and is known for its colonial-era architecture and vibrant street markets.
  • Desaru Coast: A luxury resort destination located about an hour east of Iskandar Puteri, Desaru Coast offers beautiful beaches, championship golf courses, and a variety of watersports and outdoor activities.

Conclusion

Iskandar Puteri is a vibrant and dynamic city that offers something for every type of traveler. From its bustling night markets to its stunning natural attractions, visitors will find plenty of things to see and do in this beautiful part of Malaysia. Whether you’re seeking relaxation or adventure, Iskandar Puteri is a destination not to be missed.
