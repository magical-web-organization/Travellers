Introduction to Igan

From natural attractions and outdoor adventures to culturally significant sites and heritage trails, Igan has everything to please the travellers’ thirst for exploration. A charming town located in Sarawak, Malaysia, Igan is known for its serene countryside, traditional way of life, and irresistible local cuisine. It’s an off-the-beaten-path destination that promises authentic experiences away from the usual tourist crowds.

List of Places to Visit in Igan

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Igan Heritage Trail

The Igan Heritage Trail offers visitors the opportunity to explore the town’s deep cultural and historical roots. The trail consists of several stops, including the Poh Hua Kiong Temple, the Tomb of Dato Banyang, and the Igan Museum.

Igan Museum

Located in the heart of the town, Igan Museum is a must-visit for those interested in the history and culture of the region. The museum houses a collection of local artefacts and displays that showcase the traditional lifestyle of the local people.

Natural Attractions of Igan: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Bawang Assan Beach

Bawang Assan Beach is located south of Igan town and is known for its crystal-clear and turquoise waters. Visitors can relax, swim, sunbathe, or try their hand at fishing.

Pulau Seduku

Pulau Seduku is a small island surrounded by lush greenery and beautiful scenery. It’s an excellent place for a picnic, swimming, or snorkelling.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Igan

Igan’s abundant natural beauty means outdoor enthusiasts can take advantage of several hiking and biking trails. The popular routes are the Bawang Assan Trail and the Igan River Trail, which provide stunning views of the surrounding landscape.

Best Things to Do in Igan: Activities and Tours

Igan Sunset Cruise

Take in the beautiful sunset and explore the Igan River aboard a sunset cruise. The tour includes a local guide who will share insights into the region’s rich history and culture.

Traditional Cooking Class

Discover the secrets of local cuisine and learn how to prepare traditional dishes by taking a cooking class by one of the locals.

Food and Drink Scene of Igan

Must-Try Dishes

Ikan Terubok Masin

Ikan Terubok Masin is a local delicacy that’s a must-try when visiting Igan. It’s a type of salted fish that’s dried and smoked, resulting in a unique flavour that’s truly delicious.

Sarawak Laksa

Sarawak Laksa is a flavourful noodle soup with a tangy and spicy taste, making it a favourite among Malaysians.

Local Beverages

Tuak

Tuak is a traditional Iban rice wine that’s generally served during festivals and celebrations. It’s a sweet and mildly alcoholic beverage that’s perfect for those who prefer lighter drinks.

Durian Shake

Durian Shake is a refreshing drink made with the flesh of the world-famous fruit, durian, and a few other ingredients that make it a delicious and healthy beverage.

Where to Stay in Igan: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Riverfront Inn Igan

Riverfront Inn Igan is a budget-friendly option located along the Igan Riverbank, providing an excellent view of the river and its surroundings.

Sebauh Inn

Sebauh Inn is a mid-range hotel located at the heart of the town and offers comfortable accommodation and excellent service at a reasonable price.

Sunset Homestay

Sunset Homestay is a perfect option for those looking for a homely and relaxing option. It’s a laid-back accommodation that provides an excellent view of the sunset.

What to Buy in Igan: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Igan Rice

Igan is known for the rice cultivation, and visitors can take home some locally-grown Igan rice as a souvenir.

Handmade Bags and Baskets

The local people of Igan are skilled in weaving, so handmade bags and baskets are an excellent souvenir option.

Local Culture and Customs of Igan

Being in Sarawak, Igan’s customs and traditions are heavily influenced by the Iban culture, including music, dance, food, and clothing. Visitors may want to learn more about the tradition and customs by participating in local festivals such as Gawai Dayak and Kaamatan.

Best Time to Visit Igan

The best time to visit Igan is during the dry season from March to September, as the weather is pleasant, and outdoor activities are more appealing during this time.

Do’s and Don’ts of Igan

Do:

  • Respect local customs and traditions
  • Bring loose, comfortable clothing, and appropriate footwear if you plan on hiking or cycling
  • Try the local cuisine and delicacies

Don’t:

  • Wear revealing clothing while visiting religious or cultural sites and temples
  • Pluck flowers or disturb the natural habitat while hiking or exploring
  • Bargain excessively while shopping

Safety and Health

Safety in Igan

Igan is generally a safe place, but visitors should always be aware of their surroundings, especially at night. Avoid walking alone in poorly lit streets or deserted areas and keep your valuables safe.

Health in Igan

Visitors should ensure that they are up to date with their routine vaccinations and should take precautions to protect themselves against mosquito-borne diseases such as dengue fever and malaria.

Getting to Igan: Transportation Options and Logistics

The nearest airport to Igan is Sibu Airport, which has regular flights from major cities. Visitors can also take a bus or drive from Kuching or Sibu to Igan.

Airports Near Igan

Other Options

Visitors can also take a boat from nearby towns such as Sarikei or Tanjung Manis.

How to Get Around in Igan

The best way to get around Igan is by renting a car or motorcycle. Taxis are also available for short distances, or visitors can take a leisurely stroll through the town’s streets.

Where to Go Next from Igan: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Kuching

Kuching is a bustling city located within easy reach of Igan. It’s known for its vibrant culture, historical landmarks, and savoury cuisine.

Bintulu

Bintulu is a coastal town located a few hours from Igan, known for its serene beaches, outdoor activities, and diverse culture.

Conclusion

Igan is an ideal destination for those seeking off-the-beaten-path experiences and authentic cultural and natural attractions. Visitors can explore the town’s rich history and culture, dine on traditional cuisine, and enjoy outdoor activities amidst beautiful scenery. This comprehensive tour guide provides everything from places to visit, where to stay, things to eat and buy, and safety tips to ensure visitors have a hassle-free and enjoyable stay in Igan, Sarawak, Malaysia.
