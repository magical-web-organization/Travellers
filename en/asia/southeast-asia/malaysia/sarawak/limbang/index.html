Introduction to Limbang

Limbang is a charming town located in the northern region of Sarawak, Malaysia. It is the capital of the Limbang District and is situated on the banks of the Limbang River. Limbang is known for its beautiful natural attractions, rich cultural heritage, and mouth-watering local cuisine. This small town may be often overlooked, but it is definitely worth a visit.

List of Places to Visit in Limbang

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

The Limbang Museum, located in the town center, is a must-visit for history enthusiasts. Here, you can learn about the cultural and historical significance of Limbang and its people through a collection of artifacts, photographs, and exhibits.

The Old Limbang Fort, built in the 1890s, is another historical attraction worth visiting. The fort served as a police station during the Brooke era and was used as a prison by the Japanese during World War II.

Natural Attractions of Limbang: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

The Pulong Tau National Park, located 30 km from Limbang, is a great destination for adventure seekers and nature lovers. The park has several hiking trails, camping spots, picnic areas, and waterfalls that make for great day trips.

The Bakelalan Highlands, a four-hour drive from Limbang, is a beautiful highland region known for its tranquil villages, scenic views, and cool weather. The region is ideal for hiking and exploring the local culture.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Limbang

The Bukit Mas Gading National Park, located 60 km from Limbang, offers several hiking and biking routes. The park is home to the rare Rafflesia flower, which blooms only once a year.

Best Things to Do in Limbang: Activities and Tours

For an unforgettable experience, take a river cruise on the Limbang River and experience the beauty of the town from a different perspective.

Another popular activity is visiting one of the local longhouses. Longhouses are traditional communal living quarters where several families live together. Most longhouses are located in rural areas around Limbang.

Week-Long Itinerary for Limbang

Day 1 Arrival in Limbang and check-in at hotel. Explore the town center and visit the Limbang Museum.
Day 2 Visit the Old Limbang Fort and take a river cruise on the Limbang River.
Day 3-4 Head out to the Pulong Tau National Park for a two-day camping trip.
Day 5-6 Explore the Bakelalan Highlands and visit the local villages.
Day 7 Take a day trip to one of the nearby longhouses for a cultural experience.

Food and Drink Scene of Limbang

Must-Try Dishes

Limbang is known for its delicious local cuisine, which has a unique blend of Malay, Chinese, and indigenous flavors. Some must-try dishes include:

  • Ayam Pansoh: Chicken cooked in bamboo shoots and lemongrass.
  • Laksa Sarawak: A spicy noodle soup with a variety of toppings.
  • Belacan Bee Hoon: Stir-fried rice noodles with belacan (shrimp paste) and vegetables.

Local Beverages

For a refreshing drink, try the local Teh C Special. This is a sweetened iced tea with evaporated milk and a dash of salt.

Where to Stay in Limbang: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Limbang has a variety of accommodation options for all budgets. Some popular choices include:

  • Limbang Plaza Hotel: A four-star hotel located in the town center.
  • Happy Garden Inn: A budget-friendly guesthouse with clean and comfortable rooms.
  • Kenyalang Backpackers Hostel: A backpacker hostel with a lively atmosphere and friendly staff.

What to Buy in Limbang: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Limbang is known for its handmade crafts, including woven baskets, mats, and traditional clothing. Other popular souvenirs include local coffee, tea, and snacks.

Local Culture and Customs of Limbang

Limbang is home to several ethnic groups, including the Iban, Malay, and Chinese. Respect for local customs and traditions is imperative, so be sure to dress modestly when visiting religious and cultural sites. Additionally, it is customary to remove your shoes when entering someone’s house.

Best Time to Visit Limbang

The best time to visit Limbang is during the dry season, from March to October. However, keep in mind that the weather can be unpredictable, so be prepared for sudden rain showers.

Do’s and Don’ts of Limbang

Do:

  • Respect the local customs and traditions.
  • Dress modestly in religious and cultural sites.
  • Try the local cuisine.

Don’t:

  • Disrespect the local culture.
  • Be too loud and disruptive in public places.
  • Wear revealing clothing in public.

Safety and Health

Safety in Limbang

Overall, Limbang is a safe place to visit. However, it is always advisable to take precautions and stay alert, especially when traveling alone at night.

Health in Limbang

It is always important to keep in mind basic health precautions such as avoiding tap water and getting vaccinated before traveling. In the case of an emergency, there is a hospital and several clinics available in Limbang.

Getting to Limbang: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Limbang

The nearest airport to Limbang is the Miri International Airport, which is located approximately 140 km away. From the airport, you can take a direct bus or taxi to Limbang.

Bus Stations Near Limbang

Limbang has a bus station located in the town center that connects to other major cities in Sarawak.

Other Options

Alternatively, you can hire a taxi or rent a car to get around Limbang and its surrounding areas.

How to Get Around in Limbang

Limbang is a small town, and most places are easily accessible by foot or bicycle. Taxis and rental cars are also available for those who prefer to drive.

Where to Go Next from Limbang: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

There are several nearby destinations and excursions you can explore after your visit to Limbang. Some popular choices include Miri, Brunei, and the Niah Caves.

Conclusion

In conclusion, Limbang is a hidden gem in Malaysia that offers a mix of natural beauty, cultural heritage, and mouth-watering cuisine. Plan your trip to Limbang today to experience the beauty of this charming town for yourself.
