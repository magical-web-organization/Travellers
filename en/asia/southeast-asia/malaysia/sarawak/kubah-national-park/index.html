Introduction to Kubah National Park

Kubah National Park is located in Sarawak, Malaysia, and is one of the smallest national parks in the state. Despite its size, it boasts a diverse range of flora and fauna, as well as unique geological formations. It is a popular destination for nature lovers and outdoor enthusiasts, offering hiking and biking trails, scenic views, and cultural attractions. This comprehensive travel guide provides all the information you need to plan your trip to Kubah National Park.

List of Places to Visit in Kubah National Park

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Matang Wildlife Centre: A wildlife rehabilitation center that allows visitors to see rare animals, such as the orangutan, sun bear, and hornbill.
  • Sarawak Cultural Village: A living museum that showcases the cultural diversity of Sarawak, including traditional music, dance, and crafts.
  • Kuching Waterfront: A scenic promenade along the Sarawak River that features historic buildings, food stalls, and street performances.

Natural Attractions of Kubah National Park: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Waterfall Trail: A hiking trail that leads to the park’s waterfall, which is surrounded by lush rainforest and offers a refreshing swim.
  • Frog Pond Trail: A hiking trail that takes visitors through the jungle to a pond that is home to a variety of colorful frogs.
  • Palm Loop Trail: A hiking trail that winds through palm oil plantations and offers stunning views of the surrounding mountains.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Kubah National Park

  • Bukit Sungai Pueh Summit Trail: A challenging hike that leads to the summit of Bukit Sungai Pueh, offering panoramic views of the park and beyond.
  • Gunung Serapi Trail: A moderate hiking trail that leads to the summit of Gunung Serapi, offering scenic views of Kuching city and the South China Sea.
  • Kubah Summit Trail: A short but steep hike to the summit of Kubah Mountain, offering stunning views of the park and surrounding area.

Best Things to Do in Kubah National Park: Activities and Tours

  • Night Safari: A guided tour of the park at night, offering the opportunity to see nocturnal animals such as the slow loris and flying squirrel.
  • Birdwatching: Kubah National Park is home to over 200 species of birds, making it a popular destination for birdwatchers.
  • Jungle Trekking: Explore the rainforest on foot, accompanied by a knowledgeable guide who can point out the park’s flora and fauna.

Food and Drink Scene of Kubah National Park

Must-Try Dishes

  • Sarawak Laksa: A spicy noodle soup that is a popular local dish.
  • Kolo Mee: A traditional noodle dish topped with minced meat and fried onions.
  • Ayam Pansuh: Chicken cooked in bamboo, often served with rice.

Local Beverages

  • Teh C Peng Special: A popular iced tea drink with a combination of evaporated milk, palm sugar, and red tea.
  • Tuak: A traditional rice wine served during special occasions and cultural events.
  • Kek Lapis: A multilayered cake made of butter, flour, and flavorings, such as pandan or chocolate.

Where to Stay in Kubah National Park: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Kubah National Park Hostel: Basic accommodations within the park, with bunk beds and shared facilities.
  • Damai Beach Resort: A luxury resort located a short drive from Kubah National Park, offering beachfront rooms and world-class amenities.
  • RJ Ayam Penyet Homestay: A local homestay that provides a unique cultural experience for visitors.

What to Buy in Kubah National Park: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Sarawak Pepper: A popular spice that is grown locally and used in many traditional dishes.
  • Handicrafts: Sarawak is known for its skilled artisans, who produce a variety of handmade goods, such as pottery, weaving, and woodcarvings.
  • Sarawak Layer Cake: A popular sweet treat made up of many layers of colorful cake, often bought as a souvenir for loved ones.

Local Culture and Customs of Kubah National Park

The people of Sarawak are known for their warm hospitality, and visitors are often welcomed with open arms. It is important to be respectful of local customs and traditions, such as removing shoes before entering a home or temple, and dressing modestly in public places.

Best Time to Visit Kubah National Park

The best time to visit Kubah National Park is from March to October, which is the dry season in Sarawak. The weather is cooler and less humid during this time, making it more comfortable for outdoor activities.

Do’s and Don’ts of Kubah National Park

Do:

  • Dress appropriately for the weather and terrain.
  • Bring a refillable water bottle to minimize plastic waste.
  • Respect the park’s rules and regulations, such as refraining from littering.

Don’t:

  • Disturb wildlife or plant life in the park.
  • Smoke or use fire in the park.
  • Take souvenirs from the park, such as rocks or plants.

Safety and Health

Safety in Kubah National Park

Kubah National Park is generally a safe destination, but visitors should take precautions such as staying on designated trails and avoiding isolated areas at night. It is also important to be aware of potential hazards such as poisonous plants and insects, and to treat all cuts or wounds promptly to prevent infection.

Health in Kubah National Park

Visitors should take necessary health precautions such as getting vaccinated before traveling and bringing insect repellent and sunscreen. Tap water in Sarawak is generally safe to drink, but visitors may prefer to stick to bottled water to avoid any issues with unfamiliar bacteria.

Getting to Kubah National Park: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Kubah National Park

Kuching International Airport is the nearest airport to Kubah National Park, located approximately 30 kilometers away. Visitors can take a taxi or shuttle service from the airport to the park.

Bus Stations Near Kubah National Park

Kubah National Park is accessible by bus from Kuching city center. Visitors can take a local bus or taxi to the park, or book a private car or shuttle service.

Other Options

Visitors can also hire a car or motorbike to reach Kubah National Park, or join a guided tour that includes transportation.

How to Get Around in Kubah National Park

The park is relatively small and can be easily navigated on foot. Visitors can also rent bicycles or hire a guide for hiking and trekking expeditions.

Where to Go Next from Kubah National Park: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Bako National Park: A nearby nature reserve that is home to rare wildlife such as the proboscis monkey and Bornean bearded pig.
  • Semenggoh Nature Reserve: A wildlife sanctuary that is home to the endangered orangutan, and features a rehabilitation program to reintroduce them to the wild.
  • Kuching City: The capital of Sarawak, and a vibrant cultural hub with a rich history and unique architecture.

Conclusion

Kubah National Park is a hidden gem in Sarawak, offering a range of outdoor activities and cultural attractions in a stunning natural setting. Whether you are interested in hiking, wildlife, or local culture, there is something for everyone to enjoy. Use this travel guide to plan your trip and make the most of your visit to Kubah National Park.
