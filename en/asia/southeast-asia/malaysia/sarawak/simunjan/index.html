Introduction to Simunjan

Nestled in the heart of Sarawak, Simunjan is a quaint little town that’s filled with natural wonders and cultural heritage. This small but lively town is a perfect destination for those looking to experience the beauty and charm of Malaysia, away from the hustle and bustle of big cities.

Simunjan is known for its incredible natural beauty, with lush green forests, crystal-clear rivers, and breathtaking waterfalls. The town is also rich in cultural heritage, with a number of museums and historical landmarks that offer a glimpse into the country’s fascinating history.

Whether you’re looking for outdoor adventures, cultural experiences, or just a relaxing getaway, Simunjan has something for everyone. In this comprehensive tour guide, we’ll explore everything this town has to offer, from must-see attractions to local cuisine and customs.

List of Places to Visit in Simunjan

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Simunjan is home to a number of cultural landmarks and museums that offer a fascinating glimpse into the region’s history and heritage. Here are some must-visit attractions for history lovers:

  • Simunjan Town Mosque: This stunning mosque is one of the largest in the region, and it’s a great place to learn more about Islamic culture and traditions. The mosque is also a popular spot for photography, thanks to its stunning architecture and beautiful surroundings.
  • Simunjan Heritage Trail: The Simunjan Heritage Trail is a great way to explore the town’s rich history and culture. This self-guided tour takes you through some of Simunjan’s most important landmarks, including the town square, the old courthouse, and the Chinese temple.

Natural Attractions of Simunjan: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Simunjan is blessed with incredible natural beauty, and there are plenty of outdoor activities and scenic views to enjoy. Here are some must-visit natural attractions:

  • Kubah National Park: Located just a short drive from Simunjan, Kubah National Park is a nature lover’s paradise. The park is home to a stunning array of flora and fauna, including pitcher plants, jungle fowl, and proboscis monkeys. Hiking trails and picnic areas are also available for visitors.
  • Mount Penrissen: This impressive mountain peak is one of the highest in Sarawak, and it offers stunning views of the surrounding landscape. Visitors can hike to the summit for breathtaking panoramas, or take a leisurely stroll through the nearby forests.
  • Talang-Talang Island: This beautiful island is a popular spot for snorkeling and diving, thanks to its crystal-clear waters and diverse marine life. Visitors can also enjoy leisurely walks along the beach, or simply soak up the sun and enjoy the island’s tranquil atmosphere.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Simunjan

Simunjan is a great place for outdoor enthusiasts, and there are plenty of hiking and biking trails to explore. Here are some popular routes:

  • Kubah National Park Trails: Kubah National Park has several hiking trails that lead through lush rainforest and past scenic waterfalls. The popular Palmetum Trail takes hikers past a wide variety of palm species, while the Selang Trail offers breathtaking views of the park’s mountains and valleys.
  • Mount Penrissen Trail: The Mount Penrissen trail is a challenging but rewarding hike that leads to the summit of Sarawak’s highest mountain. The trail offers stunning views of the surrounding landscape, and visitors can enjoy a well-deserved rest at the summit’s rest house.
  • Simunjan Heritage Trail: While not a traditional hiking trail, the Simunjan Heritage Trail is a great way to explore the town’s history and culture on foot. The trail takes visitors past important landmarks and cultural sites, and it offers a rare glimpse into Simunjan’s storied past.

Best Things to Do in Simunjan: Activities and Tours

In addition to the natural and cultural attractions, Simunjan has plenty of activities and tours that visitors can enjoy. Here are some popular options:

  • River Safari: Take a boat ride along the Simunjan River for a chance to spot wildlife such as proboscis monkeys, crocodiles, and hornbills.
  • Hot Springs: The nearby Kuching Hot Springs are a great place to relax and rejuvenate. Visitors can soak in the naturally-heated pools, or enjoy a massage or spa treatment.
  • Cultural Tours: Many local guides offer tours that showcase Simunjan’s rich cultural heritage, including visits to museums, temples, and traditional longhouses.

Food and Drink Scene of Simunjan

No trip to Simunjan is complete without experiencing the local cuisine. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Sarawak Laksa: This iconic Malaysian dish features thick noodles in a rich, spicy broth that’s flavored with coconut milk and tamarind.
  • Kolo Mee: Kolo Mee is a popular breakfast dish that consists of thin noodles, minced pork, and a variety of spices and sauces.
  • Belacan Bee Hoon: This flavorful dish is made with rice vermicelli noodles and shrimp paste, and it’s often served with fresh vegetables and spicy chili paste.

Local Beverages

  • Teh C Peng: This refreshing iced tea is a popular drink throughout Malaysia. It’s made with black tea, evaporated milk, and sweetened condensed milk.
  • Sarawakian Rice Wine: This locally-produced wine is made from glutinous rice, and it has a sweet, slightly sour flavor. It’s often served during special occasions and festivals.

Where to Stay in Simunjan: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Simunjan has a range of accommodation options to suit every budget and preference. Here are some of the most popular hotels, hostels, and guesthouses in the area:

  • Simunjan Jaya Hotel: This modern hotel offers comfortable rooms and a range of amenities, including free Wi-Fi and a 24-hour front desk.
  • Iban Longhouse Stay: For a unique cultural experience, consider staying in a traditional Iban longhouse. These communal houses offer basic accommodations, but they provide an authentic glimpse into local culture.
  • Simunjan Homestay: This cozy guesthouse is run by a friendly local family, and it offers comfortable rooms and home-cooked meals.

What to Buy in Simunjan: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

No trip is complete without a souvenir or two! Here are some local products that make great gifts and keepsakes:

  • Sarawak Pepper: This famous spice is grown throughout the region, and it’s renowned for its unique flavor and aroma. Look for pepper harvested from the nearby Gunung Penrissen range.
  • Handmade Crafts: Simunjan is known for its skilled artisans, who produce a wide variety of handmade crafts and souvenirs. Look for intricately-carved wooden sculptures, handmade pottery, and woven baskets.
  • Traditional Clothing: For an authentic souvenir, consider purchasing traditional Iban clothing, such as handmade sarongs or woven headgear.

Local Culture and Customs of Simunjan

Simunjan is home to a diverse and vibrant community, and visitors should be respectful of local customs and traditional practices. Here are some tips for immersing yourself in the local culture:

  • Learn the Language: While many locals speak English, learning some basic Bahasa Malaysia phrases can help you better connect with the community.
  • Respect Local Traditions: Simunjan is home to a number of different ethnic groups, each with their own unique traditions and customs. Visitors should be respectful of these traditions, and seek to learn more about them.
  • Try Local Cuisine: Food is an important part of Malaysian culture, and trying local dishes is a great way to experience the country’s rich culinary heritage.

Best Time to Visit Simunjan

Simunjan is a year-round destination, but the best time to visit is from May to September, when the weather is dry and sunny. Temperatures during this time range from 24-30°C, making it a perfect time to explore the town’s outdoor attractions.

Do’s and Don’ts of Simunjan

Do:

  • Do dress modestly, especially when visiting places of worship or cultural sites.
  • Do respect local customs and traditions.
  • Do try the local cuisine and immerse yourself in the local culture.

Don’t:

  • Don’t litter or damage the natural environment.
  • Don’t touch or disturb local wildlife.
  • Don’t make excessive noise or disruptive behavior in public places.

Safety and Health

Safety in Simunjan

Simunjan is generally safe for visitors, but it’s always important to take basic safety precautions, such as:

  • Staying alert and aware of your surroundings, especially when in crowded or unfamiliar areas.
  • Avoiding carrying large sums of money or valuable items when traveling.
  • Taking extra precautions when traveling at night or in remote areas.

Health in Simunjan

Visitors to Simunjan should take basic health precautions, such as:

  • Checking with your doctor or travel clinic to ensure that you’re up-to-date on recommended vaccinations.
  • Using insect repellent to avoid mosquito-borne illnesses.
  • Drinking only bottled or boiled water to avoid water-borne illnesses.

Getting to Simunjan: Transportation Options and Logistics

Simunjan is easily accessible by a variety of transportation options. Here are some ways to get to the town:

Airports Near Simunjan

The nearest airport to Simunjan is Kuching International Airport, which is approximately 70km away. Visitors can take a taxi or bus from the airport to reach Simunjan.

Bus Stations Near Simunjan

Simunjan has several bus stations that offer connections to nearby cities and towns. The most popular stations are the Simunjan Bus Terminal and the Serian Bus Terminal.

Other Options

Visitors can also reach Simunjan by taking a taxi or renting a car. Many local guides and tour companies also offer transportation services for visitors.

How to Get Around in Simunjan

Simunjan is a small town that is easily walkable, but visitors can also take a taxi or rent a car or motorbike to explore the surrounding areas.

Where to Go Next from Simunjan: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Simunjan is located in a beautiful part of Malaysia that is full of nearby destinations and excursions. Here are some popular options:

  • Kuching: Located just 70km from Simunjan, Kuching is a vibrant city that’s known for its rich history and beautiful architecture.
  • Bako National Park: This stunning national park is located just a short drive from Kuching, and it’s home to a variety of wildlife and natural attractions.
  • Santubong: This picturesque fishing village is known for its beautiful beaches and laid-back atmosphere.

Conclusion

Simunjan is a hidden gem in Malaysia that’s full of natural wonders and cultural heritage. Whether you’re looking for outdoor adventures, cultural experiences, or just a relaxing getaway, Simunjan has something for everyone.
