Introduction to Rejang River

Rejang River is the longest river in Sarawak, Malaysia, spanning over 640 km in length. It is known for its vibrant culture, breathtaking scenery, and outdoor adventures. The river has been an integral part of the life and culture of the Rejang people for centuries. This guide will provide you with all the information you need to plan the perfect trip to Rejang River.

List of Places to Visit in Rejang River

Rejang River is dotted with several attractions that cater to different tastes and interests. Here are some of the top places to visit:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  1. Bangunan Diraja Sibu - A historic building that used to be the administrative center of Sibu.
  2. Sibu Heritage Centre - A museum that showcases the rich cultural heritage of the Rejang people.
  3. Wong Nai Siong Memorial Park - A park dedicated to the Chinese revolutionary who played a vital role in the development of Sibu.

Natural Attractions of Rejang River: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  1. Batang Ai National Park - A protected rainforest area with lush greenery and diverse wildlife.
  2. Bakun Dam - A hydroelectric dam known for its magnificent view of the surrounding mountains.
  3. Nanga Sumpa Longhouse - A traditional longhouse that offers a glimpse into the daily life of the Iban people.
  4. Pelagus Rapids - A series of rapids that offer an exciting rafting experience.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Rejang River

Rejang River also offers some of the best hiking and biking trails in Sarawak. Here are some popular routes:

  1. Borneo Highlands Trail - A challenging hiking trail that takes you through stunning mountain landscapes.
  2. Batang Ai Trek - A moderate hiking trail that takes you through the lush rainforest of Batang Ai National Park.
  3. Ngemah Mountain Biking Trail - An exhilarating trail that takes you through some of the most scenic routes in Rejang River.

Best Things to Do in Rejang River: Activities and Tours

Rejang River is not just about breathtaking scenery and cultural attractions. There are plenty of activities and tours that one can indulge in. Here are some of the best things to do in Rejang River:

  1. River Safari - Take a boat tour along the river and explore the local wildlife and vegetation.
  2. Caving Exploration - Explore the hidden caves in the surrounding mountains.
  3. White Water Rafting - An adrenaline-pumping adventure that takes you through the rapids of the river.

Food and Drink Scene of Rejang River

No trip to Rejang River is complete without trying out the local food and drinks. Here are some must-try dishes:

Must-Try Dishes

  1. Manok Pansoh - A traditional Iban dish that consists of chicken cooked with bamboo shoots.
  2. Laksa Sarawak - A spicy noodle soup that is famous in Sarawak.
  3. Kek Lapis - A multi-layered cake usually served during festive seasons.

Local Beverages

  1. Tuak - A type of rice wine made by the Iban people.
  2. Teh C Peng - A popular iced tea drink that is widely consumed in Sarawak.

Where to Stay in Rejang River: Hotels, Hostels, Guesthouses

There are numerous options for accommodation in Rejang River. Here are some of our recommendations:

  1. Tanahmas Hotel - A luxurious hotel located in Sibu.
  2. Riverside Majestic Hotel - A four-star hotel located in Kuching.
  3. Longhouse Bed and Breakfast - A budget-friendly option that offers a traditional longhouse experience.

What to Buy in Rejang River: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Rejang River is known for its vibrant handicrafts and local products. Here are some items you should consider bringing home:

  1. Pua Kumbu - A traditional Iban textile that is commonly used for clothing or as a decorative piece.
  2. Sarawak Pepper - Known as the king of spices, Sarawak pepper is highly sought after for its unique flavor.
  3. Borneo Beads - Colorful beads that are handcrafted by the native tribes of Borneo.

Local Culture and Customs of Rejang River

The people of Rejang River have their unique cultural practices and beliefs. Visitors are encouraged to learn about their customs and traditions during their stay. Here are some cultural practices you should be aware of:

  1. Gawai Dayak - A festival celebrated by the Dayak people to mark the end of the rice harvesting season.
  2. Iban Weddings - A traditional Iban wedding is an elaborate celebration that can last for several days.
  3. Respectful Behavior - Visitors are expected to show respect towards the local customs and traditions.

Best Time to Visit Rejang River

The best time to visit Rejang River is between the months of March and September when the weather is dry and pleasant.

Do’s and Don’ts of Rejang River

Here are some do’s and don’ts to keep in mind when visiting Rejang River:

Do:

  1. Respect the local traditions and customs.
  2. Bring mosquito repellent as the area is known for mosquitoes.
  3. Be mindful of your belongings and keep them in a safe place.

Don’t:

  1. Do not litter as it can harm the environment and wildlife.
  2. Do not take pictures of the locals without their permission.
  3. Do not wear revealing clothing in conservative areas.

Safety and Health

Safety in Rejang River

Rejang River is generally safe for visitors. However, it is always advisable to take appropriate precautions while traveling.

Health in Rejang River

Visitors are advised to take necessary vaccinations and precautions against malaria and other mosquito-borne diseases. It is also advisable to carry insect repellent and protect yourself from the sun.

Getting to Rejang River: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Rejang River

The nearest airport to Rejang River is Sibu Airport. Airlines such as AirAsia, Malaysia Airlines, and MASwings fly regularly to Sibu from major cities in Malaysia.

Bus Stations Near Rejang River

Sibu Bus Terminal is the primary bus station that connects to various cities in Sarawak.

Other Options

Taxis and rental cars are also available to explore the surrounding areas.

How to Get Around in Rejang River

The best way to explore Rejang River is by hiring a private car or by joining a tour. Some of the attractions are located in remote areas, and the roads can be challenging, so it is always better to have a dependable vehicle.

Where to Go Next from Rejang River: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Rejang River’s vicinity offers several other popular destinations worth a visit. Here are some of our recommendations:

  1. Miri - A vibrant city known for its natural attractions and cultural heritage.
  2. Kuching - The capital of Sarawak, known for its colonial-era architecture and vibrant food scene.

Conclusion

Rejang River is a hidden gem in Sarawak that offers an amazing travel experience. With its stunning scenery, vibrant culture, and outdoor adventures, it is an ideal destination for those who seek new and exciting adventures. This comprehensive guide will help you plan your trip to Rejang River and make your vacation an unforgettable one.
