Belaga is located in the Rejang River of Sarawak, Malaysia, amidst lush rainforests and stunning waterfalls. A hidden gem in Southeast Asia, Belaga offers a unique experience to those looking to immerse themselves in nature and traditional culture. This travel guide to Belaga covers all you need to know to plan your trip to this off-the-beaten-path destination.

Introduction to Belaga

Belaga is a small town located at the heart of Sarawak’s remote interior. It is home to the Kayan people, one of the main indigenous groups of Sarawak. The town is surrounded by dense rainforests and undulating mountains that offer some of the most breathtaking views in Malaysia. The ethnic diversity, stunning landscapes, and rich cultural heritage make Belaga an ideal destination for adventure enthusiasts, nature lovers, and culture aficionados alike.

List of Places to Visit in Belaga

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Belaga is home to a plethora of museums and historical attractions that showcase the town’s rich cultural heritage, which dates back to the pre-colonial period. Here are some of the must-visit cultural attractions in Belaga:

  • Kayan Longhouse: The Kayan longhouse is a traditional communal dwelling that can host up to 20 families. This unique architectural structure forms the hub of Kayan social life and is a must-visit attraction in Belaga.

  • Bakun Dam: The Bakun Dam is a hydroelectric dam that provides electricity to most of Sarawak and is one of the biggest dams in Southeast Asia. The dam offers stunning views of the surrounding forests and surreal landscapes.

  • Belaga Bazaar: The Belaga bazaar is a vibrant market where visitors can buy traditional handicrafts, souvenirs, and local produce.

Natural Attractions of Belaga: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Nature has blessed Belaga with some of the most beautiful landscapes in Malaysia. From dense rainforests to tranquil lakes, Belaga offers endless opportunities for outdoor enthusiasts to explore. Here are some of the best natural attractions in Belaga:

  • Kapit River: The Kapit River is a popular destination for kayaking and rafting enthusiasts and is renowned for its clear waters and serene surroundings.

  • Kayan Mentarang National Park: The Kayan Mentarang National Park is one of the most biodiverse regions in Southeast Asia and is home to over 1500 plant species and 300 animal species.

  • Batu Lawi: Batu Lawi is a mountain peak located in the Kelabit Highlands and offers spectacular views of the surrounding rainforests and valleys.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Belaga

Belaga is a paradise for hikers and bikers who like to explore the great outdoors. Here are some of the best hiking/biking routes in Belaga:

  • Belaga to Bakun Dam: The Belaga to Bakun Dam hiking trail is a moderately challenging 20 km trail that offers stunning views of the surrounding landscapes and forests.

  • Kapit River Loop: The Kapit River loop is a 35 km bike trail that takes you through rural villages and dense forests.

  • Gunung Mulu National Park: The Gunung Mulu National Park is a UNESCO World Heritage Site and offers numerous trails, from easy strolls to challenging hikes, that lead to stunning caves, rivers, and waterfalls.

Best Things to Do in Belaga: Activities and Tours

Belaga is a haven for adventure and culture lovers. From experiencing traditional longhouses to exploring lush rainforests, there is no shortage of things to do in Belaga. Here are some of the best activities and tours to do in Belaga:

  • Explore Kayan Longhouses: Kayan longhouses are the pride of Belaga, and visitors to the town should not miss the opportunity to visit a traditional longhouse.

  • Visit the Bakun Dam: The Bakun Dam is a marvel of engineering and offers fantastic views from the top.

  • Jungle Trekking: Trekking through the dense rainforests of Belaga is an adventure of a lifetime. Experienced guides are available to take you on jungle treks that offer a unique perspective of the region’s biodiversity.

Week-Long Itinerary for Belaga

To ensure you don’t miss any of the fantastic attractions and activities in Belaga, we have put together a comprehensive seven-day itinerary. Here is what you can expect:

Day 1: Arrive in Belaga and spend the day exploring the town’s cultural attractions.

Day 2: Take a hiking or biking trail and explore the surrounding forests.

Day 3: Venture into the Kayan Mentarang National Park and witness the stunning biodiversity of the region.

Day 4: Visit the Bakun Dam and enjoy breathtaking views of the surrounding landscapes.

Day 5: Spend the day exploring the various shops and markets in Belaga, where you can buy unique traditional handicrafts and souvenirs.

Day 6: Embark on a guided jungle trek and experience the region’s topography and biodiversity.

Day 7: Spend your last day in Belaga exploring the nearby attractions, such as Batu Lawi or Gunung Mulu National Park.

Food and Drink Scene of Belaga

Belaga is a melting pot of different cultures, and this is reflected in its culinary scene. The town offers a wide range of local and international dishes that will satisfy any foodie’s palate. Here are some must-try dishes and beverages in Belaga:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Umai: Umai is a traditional dish made with raw fish marinated in lime juice and chili. It is a staple in Kayan cuisine.

  • Pansoh: Pansoh is a Kayan dish cooked in bamboo, usually with chicken or fish, and is a must-try when visiting Belaga.

  • Manok Pansoh: Manok Pansoh is a Kayan dish made with chicken cooked in bamboo, seasoned with lemongrass, and is a favorite among locals and tourists alike.

Local Beverages

  • Tuak: Tuak is a popular alcoholic beverage in Sarawak, made from fermented rice. It is a must-try for those who want to experience the local culture.

  • Teh C Peng: Teh C Peng is a refreshing iced tea with evaporated milk and is available in most coffee shops in Belaga.

Where to Stay in Belaga: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Belaga offers a wide range of accommodation options, from budget hostels to luxurious hotels. Here are some popular options:

  • Belaga Riverside Hotel: A modern hotel with comfortable rooms, stunning views, and excellent facilities.

  • Belaga Inn: A budget guesthouse that offers comfortable rooms and basic amenities.

  • Sarawak Longhouse Lodging: For a truly authentic experience, visitors can opt to stay in a Kayan longhouse.

What to Buy in Belaga: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Belaga is home to various shops and markets that sell traditional handicrafts, souvenirs, and local products. Below are some must-buy items that you can bring back as souvenirs:

  • Kayan Weavings: Hand-woven baskets, mats, and bags made from natural materials are a popular purchase among tourists.

  • Pua Kumbu: Pua Kumbu is traditional Kayan cloth with intricate designs and patterns woven on a backstrap loom.

  • Local Produce: Visitors can purchase local produce such as Sarawak pepper, Bario rice, and wild honey, among other things.

Local Culture and Customs of Belaga

Belaga is a multicultural town, and the local customs and traditions reflect this. Visitors should be respectful of these customs and adhere to the following:

  • Dressing: Visitors are advised to wear modest clothing when visiting cultural attractions or religious sites.

  • Greeting: It is customary to greet locals by employing the local language, such as saying “Selamat Pagi!” (Good morning) or “Apa Khabar?” (How are you?).

  • Taboos: Certain foods are considered taboo for some ethnic groups, and visitors should be respectful of these customs.

Best Time to Visit Belaga

The best time to visit Belaga is during the dry season, which runs from May to September. During this season, the weather is mild, making it ideal for outdoor activities such as hiking and trekking. The wet season, which runs from October to April, can be challenging for outdoor enthusiasts as roads may be muddy and some rivers may be impassable.

Do’s and Don’ts of Belaga

Do:

  • Respect local customs and traditions.
  • Try the local cuisine.
  • Explore the surrounding nature.
  • Buy local products as souvenirs.

Don’t:

  • Disrespect local customs and traditions.
  • Litter in the natural environment.
  • Ignore the advice of experienced tour guides.
  • Overstay your visa.

Safety and Health

Safety in Belaga

Belaga is generally a safe town, and visitors can explore the surrounding areas without any major safety concerns. However, visitors should take the necessary precautions, such as:

  • Avoid walking alone at night.
  • Ensure you have the necessary equipment when embarking on outdoor activities.
  • Stay up-to-date with weather conditions.

Health in Belaga

Visitors to Belaga should be wary of water-borne illnesses, and it is advisable to drink bottled water. It is also recommended that visitors consult a medical professional before traveling to Malaysia and get the necessary vaccinations.

Getting to Belaga: Transportation Options and Logistics

Belaga is a remote town that is accessible by road, river, or air. The most common way to reach Belaga is by bus or taxi from Bintulu or Miri. Alternatively, visitors can take a boat ride from Kapit to Belaga. Here are some transportation options to consider:

Airports Near Belaga

The nearest airport to Belaga is in Miri, which is approximately 300 km away from Belaga.

Bus Stations Near Belaga

The nearest bus station to Belaga is in Bintulu or Miri, offering daily services to and from the town.

Other Options

Alternatively, visitors can take boats from Kapit to Belaga, which run several times a day.

How to Get Around in Belaga

Belaga is a small town that can be easily explored on foot. However, visitors can also hire taxis, motorbikes, or boats to explore the surrounding areas.

Where to Go Next from Belaga: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Belaga is well connected to various nearby destinations that visitors can explore. Some of these nearby destinations include:

  • Mulu National Park: A UNESCO World Heritage Site located 200 km from Belaga, offering a fascinating cave system and stunning landscapes.

  • Bario: A highland town renowned for its rice fields, offering excellent trekking and cultural experiences.

  • Kuching: The capital city of Sarawak province, located approximately 700 km from Belaga.

Conclusion

Belaga offers a unique and authentic Malaysian experience, offering breathtaking landscapes, rich culture, and vibrant local life. With this comprehensive travel guide, visitors can explore and immerse themselves in the heart of Sarawak’s rainforest and come back with memories that will last a lifetime.
