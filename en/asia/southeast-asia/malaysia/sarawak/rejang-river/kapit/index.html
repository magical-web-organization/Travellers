Introduction to Kapit

Kapit, located in Sarawak, Malaysia, is a town that provides travelers with an opportunity to experience and explore Malaysia’s more rugged and authentic side. Situated in the Rejang River Basin, Kapit is established as the commercial and administrative center of the Baleh region. The region is home to the Iban tribe which is an ethnic group known for its exquisite murals, cultural traditions, and longhouses that provide visitors with a fascinating glimpse into the everyday life of the locals.

There are plenty of things to do and see in Kapit, from experiencing the local culture and customs to exploring the area’s breathtaking nature hikes and scenic views.

List of Places to Visit in Kapit

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Kapit has several unique cultural sites that showcase the town’s long-standing history and rich cultural heritage. The well-known Kapit Museum, located in the middle of the town, is a must-see, as it features artifacts and murals that capture the cultural legacy of the local Iban tribe. Tourists can also visit the Kapit Longhouse, where they can explore the traditional architecture and living habits of this ethnic group in Malaysia. Additionally, the Batu Bungan Tepu is a cave painting that tourists can visit and admire in Kapit.

Natural Attractions of Kapit: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Kapit is a town surrounded by spectacular and takes the breath away scenery and natural beauty. Visitors can head to the Tunoh National Park, which is notable for its flourishing flora and fauna, scenic views, and the opportunity to participate in some outdoor activities, such as hiking and wildlife watching. Travellers can also take boat tours and explore the Rejang River, from which they can catch a glimpse of the lush rainforest and wildlife in the area.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Kapit

Kapit has numerous hiking trails and biking routes, which are perfect for travelers seeking outdoor escapades. The “Jebak Puyuh Waterfall” (translated as “Quail trap waterfall”) is one of the popular trekking sites around Kapit. A 2-hour long journey through the dense jungle will take travelers to this fantastic waterfall site.

Best Things to Do in Kapit: Activities and Tours

There is a wide range of activities and tours available in Kapit. From heritage tours like Iban Longhouse Tours to thrilling outdoor adventures such as kayaking, trekking, and caving.

Week-Long Itinerary for Kapit

Here’s an example week-long itinerary that covers the best of Kapit:

Day Itinerary
Day 1 Arrival in Kapit and check-in to hotel or guest house; walk around Kapit Town to get acquainted with the area.
Day 2 Hiking at Jebak Puyuh Waterfall; swimming and enjoying the scenic views.
Day 3 Participate in an Iban Longhouse Tour; visit the Batu Bungan Tepu cave paintings.
Day 4 Riverboat tour down the Rejang River. On the way stop to visit fishermen villages, take photos, and observe the local flora and fauna.
Day 5 Explore the Tunoh National Park, hiking and bird watching.
Day 6 Participate in an outdoor adventure, like kayaking, caving, or trekking.
Day 7 Depart Kapit.

Food and Drink Scene of Kapit

Must-Try Dishes

Kapit offers foodies a unique dining experience that stimulates the appetite. Must-try local dishes include “Manok Pansuh,” which is chicken cooked in bamboo, with local herbs and spices. “Ketupat Sarawak” is another must-try dish, which is rice cooked in coconut milk, served in a triangle-shaped package.

Local Beverages

The local beverage that tourists should never leave Kapit without trying is the “Tuak” - a fermented rice drink that locals make from sticky rice, sugar, and water.

Where to Stay in Kapit: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Kapit provides a reasonably limited range of accommodations, but there are several decent guesthouses, hostels, and hotels tourists can choose from. The most popular ones include:

  • Express Point Hotel
  • The Rajang Inn Hotel
  • KEM’s Lodge

What to Buy in Kapit: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Some of the most popular items to buy and take home as a souvenir from Kapit include woven textiles, woodcarvings, silverware, and traditional costume, such as the Pua Kumbu.

Local Culture and Customs of Kapit

Kapit has a rich cultural heritage, and tourists should take time to experience the local Iban tribe’s traditional way of life. The tribe is known for its longhouses, a communal living space housing several families under the same roof, several activities like farming, weaving, and various other cultural traditions.

Best Time to Visit Kapit

The best time to visit Kapit is during the dry season, which runs from March until September. During this time, the weather is perfect for outdoor activities with fewer chances of rain or downpours, and the climate is mild and pleasant.

Do’s and Don’ts of Kapit

Do:

  • Respect the locals’ cultural practices and traditions.
  • Try out the significant local dishes and drinks, such as Manok Pansuh and Tuak.
  • Listen to the local tour guides to learn about Kapit’s history and traditions.

Don’t:

  • Never touch anything in the Longhouses without your tour guide’s permission.
  • Smoke in inappropriate areas

Safety and Health

Safety in Kapit

Kapit is generally a safe place, and tourists should not be worried about any safety threats while in the area. However, travelers should keep their essential belongings safe and close to them at all times.

Health in Kapit

Before traveling to Kapit, it’s advisable to visit your doctor to receive vaccinations recommended for the area.

Getting to Kapit: Transportation Options and Logistics

Kapit is accessible through various modes of transportation, including air travel and watercraft. The most comfortable way to get to Kapit is by taking a flight from Kuala Lumpur International Airport to Sibu. From there, tourists can take a boat to Kapit.

Airports Near Kapit

Sibu Airport, also recognized as the Sibu/Jelong Airport, is the nearest airport to Kapit. It’s approximately 190 km away.

Bus Stations Near Kapit

There are no direct bus services to Kapit. However, tourists can take a bus to Sibu and, from there, take a boat or car rental to Kapit.

Other Options

Another option travelers can use to access Kapit is to take a boat from Kuching or Sibu to Kapit. The option is perfect for nature enthusiasts who wish to enjoy the views of the Rejang River during their trip.

How to Get Around in Kapit

Tourists can move around Kapit using various modes of transportation, including walking, biking, hire a private car service, or use the public transport services like taxis or tuk-tuks.

Where to Go Next from Kapit: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Some of the popular destinations near Kapit include:

  • Bintulu, which is well-known for its beaches and seafood cuisine.
  • The city of Kuching, located 200 km southwest of Kapit, which is home to many exciting attractions, including museums, riverside cafes, and historic landmarks.

Conclusion

Kapit is a unique town that offers visitors a chance to experience an authentic and robust part of Malaysia’s culture and history. From the local cuisine, the exciting outdoor activities, and the historic landmarks, Kapit is a worthwhile destination for any traveler seeking an unforgettable adventure.
