The Ultimate Travel Guide to Lawas, Sarawak

Nestled in the northernmost tip of Sarawak, Malaysia, the small town of Lawas is a hidden gem waiting to be discovered. With its lush rainforests, pristine rivers, and rich cultural heritage, Lawas offers an authentic travel experience for those seeking to explore off-the-beaten-path destinations. In this comprehensive guide, we’ll take you on a virtual tour of Lawas and show you the best attractions, activities, and local secrets of this charming town.

Introduction to Lawas

Located in the northernmost part of Sarawak, Lawas is a small town with a population of approximately 40,000 people. It is known for its natural beauty, cultural heritage, and friendly locals. The town is surrounded by lush rainforests, rivers, and mountains, making it a paradise for adventurers. Lawas is also the gateway to the beautiful Ba’Kelalan highlands, a hidden gem that offers stunning views and a unique cultural experience.

List of Places to Visit in Lawas

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • History Museum: Located in the heart of Lawas, the History Museum is dedicated to preserving the town’s rich cultural heritage. It features exhibits and artifacts that showcase the history and culture of the local community.
  • Batu Danau Archaeological Park: This park is home to a significant archaeological site that dates back to over 3,000 years ago. The park offers a glimpse into the ancient history of the local communities, including the Punan and the Lun Bawang.

Natural Attractions of Lawas: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Sg. Mentarang National Park: This national park is home to some of the most beautiful rainforests in Sarawak. It features stunning waterfalls, pristine rivers, and lush jungles that are perfect for trekking, hiking, and wildlife spotting.
  • Pasar Awam Lawas: This local market is a great place to explore the local cuisine and culture. It offers a wide variety of local delicacies, fresh produce, and handmade crafts that are unique to the region.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Lawas

  • Mount Murud Trail: This challenging trail takes you through some of the most beautiful rainforests and mountains in Sarawak. It offers stunning views of the local landscape and a unique adventure for those seeking a real challenge.
  • Ba’Kelalan Highlands Trail: This trail takes you through the scenic Ba’Kelalan highlands, offering stunning views of the local rice paddies, forests, and valleys. It is a relatively easy hiking trail that is perfect for beginners or those seeking a more leisurely adventure.

Best Things to Do in Lawas: Activities and Tours

  • White Water Rafting: Lawas is home to some of the best white water rafting in Sarawak. The Lawas river offers a challenging and exciting adventure for those seeking an adrenaline rush.
  • Cultural Village Tour: This tour takes you to the nearby Ba’Kelalan highlands, where you can experience the unique culture and traditions of the local community. You will visit the longhouses, learn about their customs and try the local cuisines.

Food and Drink Scene of Lawas

Must-Try Dishes

Lawas is known for its unique local cuisine, which features a fusion of traditional Malay, Chinese, and indigenous flavors. Here are some of the must-try dishes:

  • Laksa Sarawak: This spicy noodle soup is a favorite of the locals and a must-try for visitors. It features a complex blend of flavors and spices that make it unique to Sarawak.
  • Umai: This traditional dish is made from raw fish that is marinated in lime juice and chili. It is a refreshing and flavorful dish that is perfect for hot weather.

Local Beverages

  • Teh C Peng: This iced tea is a favorite of the locals and a great way to cool down on a hot day. It is made from black tea, condensed milk, and ice.
  • Tuak: This traditional rice wine is a staple at local celebrations and a popular drink among the locals.

Where to Stay in Lawas: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

There are several accommodation options in Lawas, ranging from budget-friendly guesthouses to luxurious hotels. Here are some of our recommendations:

  • Lawas Hotel: This hotel offers comfortable rooms and excellent facilities, including a swimming pool, restaurant, and bar.
  • Purnama Hotel: This budget-friendly hotel offers clean and comfortable rooms, and a great location in the heart of Lawas town.

What to Buy in Lawas: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Handmade Crafts: Lawas is known for its traditional handicrafts, including handwoven baskets, beaded jewelry, and carvings.
  • Local Produce: Lawas is home to several local farms that produce fresh fruits and vegetables, including the famous Sarawak pineapple and durian.

Local Culture and Customs of Lawas

The local community in Lawas is a unique blend of Malay, Chinese, and indigenous cultures. The locals are known for their friendly and welcoming nature, and they take pride in their rich cultural heritage. Visitors to Lawas are encouraged to respect the local customs and traditions, including dress codes and social etiquette.

Best Time to Visit Lawas

The best time to visit Lawas is between March and September, when the weather is dry and sunny. However, visitors should be prepared for occasional rain showers, as the rainforest climate can be unpredictable.

Do’s and Don’ts of Lawas

Do:

  • Respect the local customs and traditions
  • Try the local cuisine and participate in cultural activities
  • Take precautions against mosquito-borne diseases

Don’t:

  • Wear revealing clothing in public
  • Disrespect the local culture or religious practices
  • Litter or damage the natural environment

Safety and Health

Safety in Lawas

Lawas is generally a safe place for travelers, but visitors should take precautions against petty crime, such as pickpocketing and theft. It is also important to be aware of the risks of wildlife encounters and to take precautions, such as avoiding feeding wild animals and keeping a safe distance.

Health in Lawas

Visitors to Lawas should take precautions against mosquito-borne diseases, such as dengue fever and malaria. It is also important to stay hydrated and to take precautions against heat stroke and dehydration.

Getting to Lawas: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Lawas

The nearest airport to Lawas is the Lawas Airport, which serves domestic flights from Kuala Lumpur and Kuching.

Bus Stations Near Lawas

There are several bus stations near Lawas, including the Lawas Bus Terminal and the Lawas Express Bus Terminal. Visitors can take buses from Kota Kinabalu, Kuching, and other major cities in Sarawak and Sabah.

Other Options

Visitors can also reach Lawas by car or taxi from nearby towns and cities, such as Miri and Limbang.

How to Get Around in Lawas

The best way to get around Lawas is by car or motorcycle. Visitors can also take taxis or hire private drivers to explore the town and its surroundings.

Where to Go Next from Lawas: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Ba’Kelalan Highlands: Located just a few hours’ drive from Lawas, the Ba’Kelalan highlands offer stunning views and a unique cultural experience. Visitors can explore the local rice paddies, visit traditional longhouses, and learn about the unique customs and traditions of the Lun Bawang people.
  • Limbang: This nearby town is known for its stunning natural beauty and rich cultural heritage. Visitors can explore the Limbang Museum, visit the nearby Batu Lawi peak and waterfalls, and try the local cuisine and handicrafts.

Conclusion

Lawas is a hidden gem in Sarawak, offering travelers a unique cultural and natural experience. With its lush rainforests, pristine rivers, and rich cultural heritage, Lawas is a destination that is not to be missed. We hope that this comprehensive guide has inspired you to explore the best of Lawas and discover the local secrets of this charming town.
