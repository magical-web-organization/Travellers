Introduction to Sarikei

Located in the state of Sarawak in Malaysia, Sarikei is a small town known for its charming waterfront and vibrant fruit market. While it may not be as well-known as other destinations in Malaysia, Sarikei has much to offer visitors looking to experience the beauty and culture of Southeast Asia.

List of Places to Visit in Sarikei

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Sarikei is steeped in history and culture, making it a must-visit destination for those interested in learning more about Malaysia’s past. Here are a few recommended stops:

  • Sarikei Heritage Square - Located in the heart of Sarikei, this square is home to several restored historic buildings that showcase the town’s rich cultural heritage.

  • Sarikei Civic Centre - This cultural centre is a great place to learn about the local people and their history through exhibits, performances, and workshops.

  • Wong Nai Siong Memorial Park - This park pays homage to Wong Nai Siong, a notable figure in Sarikei’s history who helped establish the local Hokkien community.

Natural Attractions of Sarikei: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

For those seeking adventure and stunning scenery, Sarikei has plenty of options. Here are a few recommendations:

  • Bukit Aup Jubilee Park - This park offers stunning views of Sarikei’s landscape and is great for hiking, picnicking, and wildlife watching.

  • Ikan Bakar Batu Bersurat - Located on the banks of the Batang Rajang river, this famous local spot is known for its fresh seafood and beautiful views.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Sarikei

If you’re looking to explore Sarikei’s natural beauty on foot or bike, here are a couple of recommended routes:

  • Bukit Aup Trail - This hiking trail is challenging but rewarding, providing stunning views of the surrounding jungle and hills.

  • Sarikei Waterfront Trail - This flat, scenic trail is perfect for a leisurely bike ride or stroll along the river.

Best Things to Do in Sarikei: Activities and Tours

Beyond the outdoor adventures and cultural sites, there are plenty of other things to do in Sarikei. Here are some ideas for activities and tours:

  • Sarikei Fruit Farm Tour - Experience the local fruit industry firsthand with a visit to a local fruit farm, where you can taste and learn about the production process.

  • Batang Rajang Cruise - Take a relaxing boat tour along the Batang Rajang river to soak up the town’s stunning surroundings.

  • Sarikei Night Market - Sample delicious local food and browse goods from vendors at this bustling street market.

Week-Long Itinerary for Sarikei

To help you plan your visit to Sarikei, here’s a suggested week-long itinerary:

Day Activity
1 Visit Sarikei Heritage Square and Civic Centre
2 Hike Bukit Aup Trail and visit Ikan Bakar Batu Bersurat for dinner
3 Take a day trip to Wong Nai Siong Memorial Park and nearby fruit farms
4 Explore the Batang Rajang river with a boat tour or riverfront stroll
5 Visit local museums and art galleries
6 Take a cooking class or food tour and explore the Sarikei Night Market
7 Relax at Bukit Aup Jubilee Park or nearby beaches

Food and Drink Scene of Sarikei

No trip to Sarikei is complete without sampling some of the local cuisine. Here are a few must-try dishes and beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Belacan Bee Hoon - A traditional Sarikei dish made with rice vermicelli noodles, bean sprouts, and spicy shrimp paste.

  • Ikan Bakar - Grilled fish served with rice and dipping sauces.

  • Fruit Rojak - A fruit salad made with local fruits and drizzled with sweet and savory sauce.

Local Beverages

  • Tuak - A traditional rice wine made by the Iban people in Sarawak.

  • Kopi O - A strong, black coffee commonly found at local kopitiams (coffee shops).

Where to Stay in Sarikei: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

While Sarikei may not have many accommodation options, there are a few places to stay that offer great value and comfort:

  • Grand Palace Hotel - A modern hotel with comfortable rooms and great amenities, including a pool and fitness centre.

  • Wawasan Inn - A budget-friendly hotel in the heart of Sarikei that offers basic but comfortable rooms.

  • Sarikei River Lodge - A cozy guesthouse located right on the Batang Rajang river, perfect for those looking for a more unique experience.

What to Buy in Sarikei: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Whether you’re looking for gifts or just want to bring a piece of Sarikei home with you, here are a few recommendations:

  • Rattan and Bamboo Products - Handmade baskets and home goods made with locally sourced materials.

  • Local Fruits and Snacks - Sarikei is famous for its fruit production, so be sure to pick up some fresh produce or local snacks to take home.

Local Culture and Customs of Sarikei

Sarikei is a melting pot of different cultures, including Malay, Iban, Chinese, and Orang Ulu. Visitors should be respectful of local customs and traditions, and dressing modestly is recommended.

Best Time to Visit Sarikei

The best time to visit Sarikei is during the dry season, which typically runs from April to September. However, as it is a tropical climate, rain is common year-round.

Do’s and Don’ts of Sarikei

Do:

  • Try the local cuisine and sample some of the fresh fruits grown in the area.

  • Respect the local culture and customs.

Don’t:

  • Disrespect religious sites or cultural symbols.

  • Ignore warnings about local wildlife, such as crocodiles or monkeys.

Safety and Health

Safety in Sarikei

Sarikei is generally a safe place to visit, but visitors should take precautions to avoid petty theft or scams. Be aware of your surroundings and keep your belongings close to you, especially in crowded areas.

Health in Sarikei

Visitors should make sure to have up-to-date vaccinations before arriving in Malaysia, and take precautions against mosquito-borne illnesses such as dengue fever.

Getting to Sarikei: Transportation Options and Logistics

Sarikei is accessible by air, bus, and car. Here are a few transportation options:

Airports Near Sarikei

  • Sibu Airport - located about 45 minutes from Sarikei

Bus Stations Near Sarikei

  • Sarikei Bus Terminal

Other Options

  • Private car or taxi

How to Get Around in Sarikei

Sarikei is a relatively small town, and most major attractions are within walking distance of each other. Taxis and rental cars are also available for those who prefer to drive.

Where to Go Next from Sarikei: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

If you’re looking to explore more of Sarawak and Malaysia, here are a few nearby destinations worth visiting:

  • Kuching - The capital of Sarawak and a bustling city filled with culture, history, and natural beauty.

  • Bako National Park - A stunning nature reserve known for its plant and animal life, as well as its stunning coastline.

  • Miri - A coastal city located near the border between Sarawak and Brunei, known for its beaches and offshore oil industry.

Conclusion

Sarikei may be a small town, but it is packed with culture, history, and stunning natural beauty. Whether you’re interested in exploring the outdoors, learning about local culture, or trying new foods, Sarikei has something to offer everyone. With this comprehensive guide, you’ll be ready to plan an unforgettable trip to this hidden gem in Malaysia.
