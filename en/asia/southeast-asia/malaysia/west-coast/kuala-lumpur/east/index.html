Introduction to East

Welcome to East, a hidden gem of Kuala Lumpur located in the West Coast of Malaysia. East is famous for its natural beauty, cultural attractions, delicious food, and friendly locals. Whether you’re an outdoor enthusiast, history buff, or a foodie, East has something for everyone. In this tour guide, we’ll cover everything you need to know to plan your ultimate trip to East.

List of Places to Visit in East

Here are some of the must-visit places in East:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • National Museum of Malaysia: Learn about Malaysia’s history, culture, and art at the National Museum of Malaysia.
  • Islamic Arts Museum: Explore Islamic art and culture at the Islamic Arts Museum.
  • National Monument: Pay tribute to the fallen heroes of Malaysia’s struggle for independence at the National Monument.
  • Thean Hou Temple: Visit the beautiful Thean Hou Temple, a six-tiered Chinese temple dedicated to the goddess Tian Hou.

Natural Attractions of East: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Batu Caves: Climb the 272 steps to reach the Batu Caves, a series of limestone caves and Hindu shrines.
  • Bukit Gasing Forest Reserve: Hike through the lush Bukit Gasing Forest Reserve and enjoy scenic views of Kuala Lumpur.
  • FRIM Kepong: Enjoy an outdoor adventure at the Forest Research Institute of Malaysia (FRIM) Kepong, where you can explore the tropical rainforest, canopy walk, and waterfall.
  • Taman Eko Rimba Kuala Lumpur: Spot wildlife, go on a jungle trek, or have a picnic at Taman Eko Rimba Kuala Lumpur, also known as the Kuala Lumpur Forest Eco Park.

Hiking/Biking Routes of East

For outdoor enthusiasts, East offers many hiking and biking routes to explore. Some of the best ones are:

  • Bukit Tabur: Enjoy a challenging hike with amazing views of the Klang Gates Quartz Ridge and the surrounding jungle at Bukit Tabur.
  • Kuala Lumpur City Centre Park: Have a leisurely walk or bike ride through the lush Kuala Lumpur City Centre Park, a green oasis in the heart of the city.
  • Kiara Hills: Hike through the lovely Kiara Hills and enjoy stunning views of the city skyline and surrounding hills.

Best Things to Do in East: Activities and Tours

Besides hiking and biking, there are many other things to do in East, such as:

  • Exploring the local street art scene at Publika and Solaris Dutamas.
  • Sampling local cuisine at Jalan Alor, a street food haven.
  • Visiting the Central Market, a heritage building that sells local products, artwork, and souvenirs.
  • Taking a cultural tour of Little India or Chinatown.

Food and Drink Scene of East

East is a foodie’s paradise with a diverse mix of Malay, Chinese, Indian, and Western cuisine. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Nasi Lemak: A fragrant coconut rice dish served with fried chicken, sambal, and various toppings.
  • Char Kway Teow: Stir-fried noodles with prawns, cockles, bean sprouts, and egg.
  • Roti Canai: A flaky Indian flatbread served with curry dipping sauce.
  • Satay: Grilled meat skewers served with peanut sauce.

Local Beverages

  • Teh Tarik: A creamy, frothy milk tea pulled from a height to mix and cool it down.
  • Kopi O: A strong black coffee served with sugar.
  • Bandung: A sweet rose syrup drink mixed with milk.
  • Air Mata Kucing: A refreshing longan and winter melon drink.

Where to Stay in East: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

East has many accommodation options to suit different budgets and preferences. Here are some of our top picks:

  • Luxury: The Majestic Hotel Kuala Lumpur, a colonial-style hotel with a pool, spa, and fine dining.
  • Mid-range: Aloft Kuala Lumpur Sentral, a trendy hotel with a rooftop bar and pool.
  • Budget: Backhome KL, a cozy hostel with stylish decor and a communal kitchen.

What to Buy in East: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Some popular souvenirs and local products to bring back from East are:

  • Batik apparel and accessories: A traditional cloth embellished with intricate patterns, used to make clothing and accessories such as bags and scarfs.
  • Pewter items: A malleable metal alloy made of tin, copper, and antimony, used to make decorative items such as vases and sculptures.
  • Durian-flavored snacks: A tropical fruit with a pungent smell, enjoyed as a dessert or snack in various forms, such as chips, ice cream, and sweets.
  • Instant coffee and tea: Malaysia is famous for its coffee and tea blends, such as White Coffee and Teh Tarik.

Local Culture and Customs of East

Malaysia is a multi-ethnic and multi-religious country with a rich cultural heritage. Here are some tips on local etiquette:

  • Dress modestly, especially in places of worship.
  • Remove your shoes before entering someone’s home or a mosque.
  • Use your right hand for eating, giving, and receiving objects, as the left hand is deemed impure.
  • Avoid pointing your feet at someone or touching someone’s head, as they are considered disrespectful.

Best Time to Visit East

The best time to visit East is from March to October, when the weather is warm and dry with occasional rain showers. The monsoon season runs from November to February, which can cause flooding and disrupt transportation.

Do’s and Don’ts of East

Here are some do’s and don’ts to keep in mind when visiting East:

Do:

  • Learn a few local phrases such as “Terima Kasih” (Thank you) and “Sama-sama” (You’re welcome).
  • Bargain politely when shopping at the markets.
  • Try local delicacies and street food.
  • Be respectful of local customs and traditions.

Don’t:

  • Drink tap water, stick to bottled water.
  • Get involved in illegal activities or drugs.
  • Show public displays of affection, as it’s frowned upon in public.

Safety and Health

Safety in East

East is generally a safe destination for tourists, but it’s always best to take precautions such as:

  • Avoid carrying large amounts of cash or valuables.
  • Be aware of your surroundings especially in crowded areas or at night.
  • Keep your passport and important documents in a safe place.

Health in East

Most visitors do not require any special vaccinations or health precautions before visiting East. However, it’s recommended to have travel insurance and be aware of the following:

  • Air pollution is a problem in Kuala Lumpur, especially during the dry season, so bring a face mask if you have breathing problems.
  • Mosquito-borne diseases such as dengue fever and Zika virus are prevalent, so use insect repellent and wear long-sleeved clothing in mosquito-prone areas.
  • Tap water is not safe to drink, stick to bottled water.

Getting to East: Transportation Options and Logistics

East is easily accessible by various modes of transportation. Here are some options:

Airports Near East

  • Kuala Lumpur International Airport (KUL): The main airport in Kuala Lumpur, located 60km south of East.
  • Sultan Abdul Aziz Shah Airport (SZB): A domestic airport located 18km west of East.

Bus Stations Near East

  • Hentian Duta Bus Terminal: The main bus terminal in Kuala Lumpur, located 10km north of East.
  • Bandar Tasik Selatan Bus Terminal: A bus terminal located 23km south of East, serving several destinations in Malaysia and Singapore.

Other Options

  • Taxi: Taxis are widely available in Kuala Lumpur, and ride-hailing apps such as Grab are also popular.
  • Train: The KTM Komuter and LRT systems serve various destinations in Kuala Lumpur and surrounding areas.

How to Get Around in East

Getting around East is easy with various transportation options such as:

  • Public transport: RapidKL buses and trains serve many popular destinations within Kuala Lumpur and surrounding areas.
  • Taxi: Taxis are available throughout Kuala Lumpur, but make sure to use a licensed taxi or a ride-hailing app.
  • Grab Bike: Rent a motorbike through the Grab app, which is a quick and affordable way to get around.
  • Walking: Exploring on foot is a great way to discover the local neighborhoods and soak in the atmosphere.

Where to Go Next from East: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

East is located in a prime location for exploring nearby destinations and excursions:

  • Genting Highlands: A popular hill resort town with a world-class casino and theme park, located 50km north of East.
  • Batu Ferringhi: A beach destination famous for its water sports, nightlife, and street markets, located 350km northwest of East.
  • Cameron Highlands: A scenic hill station known for its tea plantations and hiking trails, located 200km north of East.
  • Langkawi: A tropical island paradise with stunning beaches, waterfalls, and wildlife, located 400km north of East.

Conclusion

East is a fascinating destination with something for everyone, from its natural beauty to its cultural attractions and delicious cuisine. We hope this tour guide has helped you plan your ultimate trip to East and made your journey more memorable. Happy travels!
