Introduction to North of City Centre

North of City Centre is home to some of the most impressive historical attractions, scenic views, and outdoor adventures in Kuala Lumpur. It is located in the West Coast area, one of Malaysia’s major regions, which is known for its bustling cities, beautiful beaches, and lush tropical rainforests. This part of Kuala Lumpur is renowned for its culturally significant museums, galleries, and historical buildings, as well as its natural reserves, which are perfect for adventurous activities like hiking, trekking, and bird-watching.

Best Time to Visit North of City Centre

The best time to travel to North of City Centre is from March to September, when the weather is dry and sunny, and the air quality is usually decent. Avoid visiting during the monsoon season, which is between November and February, when the rainfall and humidity are at their highest levels.

Do’s and Don’ts of North of City Centre

Do:

  • Cover your shoulders and knees
  • Learn a few polite phrases in Malay
  • Try street food
  • Bring a reusable water bottle
  • Shop at local markets

Don’t:

  • Touch someone’s head or point with your feet
  • Drink tap water
  • Use your left hand to eat or give
  • Disrespect the royal family
