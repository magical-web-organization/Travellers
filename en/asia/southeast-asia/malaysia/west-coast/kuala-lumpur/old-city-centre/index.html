Introduction to Old City Centre

Old City Centre, also known as Kuala Lumpur’s historic centre, is a popular tourist destination in Malaysia. This is an area that is steeped in history and culture, featuring grand colonial-era buildings intermingled with modern skyscrapers. The district is located in the heart of Kuala Lumpur, and is bordered by the Klang River on one side, and Jalan Tun Razak on the other. With its rich history, cultural significance, and beautiful architecture, there is something for everyone in Old City Centre.

List of Places to Visit in Old City Centre

There are plenty of places to visit in Old City Centre, from culturally important museums and historical sites to natural outdoor attractions. Here’s a list of the top places to visit in Old City Centre:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  1. National Museum of Malaysia - This museum houses an impressive collection of historical and cultural artifacts, spanning Malaysia’s history from prehistoric times to present day.
  2. Kuala Lumpur City Gallery - A great place to learn about the history of Kuala Lumpur, and to pick up souvenirs.
  3. Merdeka Square - This is where Malaysia declared independence from British colonial rule in 1957. Today it is a popular spot for locals and tourists, with many events and festivals taking place here throughout the year.
  4. Sultan Abdul Samad Building - A stunning piece of architecture that was once the administrative headquarters of the British colonial government.

Natural Attractions of Old City Centre: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  1. KL Forest Eco Park - Located in the heart of Kuala Lumpur, this forest reserve covers 9.3 hectares and features nature trails, camping sites, and breathtaking views of the cityscape.
  2. KL Bird Park - Home to over 3,000 birds from over 200 species, this park is a must-visit for nature lovers and bird enthusiasts.
  3. Menara Kuala Lumpur - This iconic tower is one of the tallest in Southeast Asia, offering panoramic views of the city from its observation deck.
  4. Batu Caves - While not technically in Old City Centre, the Batu Caves are only a short train ride away. These limestone caves are home to numerous temples and shrines, as well as a giant golden statue of Lord Murugan.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Old City Centre

For those who prefer to explore the city on foot or bike, there are several great routes available in Old City Centre. Here are a few:

  1. KL Heritage Trail - This self-guided walking tour takes visitors through some of the most historic and culturally significant sites in Old City Centre.
  2. River of Life - This scenic cycling route takes riders along the Klang River, showcasing the city’s stunning riverside scenery.
  3. Bukit Nanas Forest Reserve - This urban tropical rainforest is perfect for hikers, with well-marked trails and breathtaking views of the city.

Best Things to Do in Old City Centre: Activities and Tours

Beyond visiting the popular attractions, there are plenty of unique things to do in Old City Centre. Here are some ideas:

  1. Take a food tour - Sample some of the best local dishes from street vendors and food stalls in Old City Centre.
  2. Explore the street art - Take a walking tour of the city’s murals and graffiti, which can be found in some of the most unexpected places.
  3. Attend a cultural performance - Witness traditional Malay dance, music, and theatre at the iconic Istana Budaya.

Food and Drink Scene of Old City Centre

One of the best things about visiting Old City Centre is the incredible food and drink scene. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  1. Nasi Lemak - A fragrant rice dish cooked in coconut milk and served with spicy sambal, peanuts, and anchovies.
  2. Char Kway Teow - A popular street food made with stir-fried flat rice noodles, shrimp, egg, bean sprouts, and sometimes sausage or chicken.
  3. Roti Canai - A flaky, crispy flatbread served with spicy curry gravy.
  4. Chendol - A refreshing dessert made with shaved ice, coconut milk, red beans, and green jelly noodles.

Local Beverages

  1. Teh Tarik - A sweet milky tea beverage that is Malaysia’s national drink.
  2. Milo Dinosaur - A Milo drink topped with a thick layer of powdered Milo.
  3. Limau Ais - A refreshing lime juice served with ice.

Where to Stay in Old City Centre: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

There are several accommodation options in Old City Centre, ranging from budget-friendly hostels to luxurious hotels. Here are some options:

  1. Travelodge City Centre - A budget-friendly hotel that is centrally located and offers comfortable accommodations.
  2. Regalia Suites - An upscale hotel suite that provides stunning views of the city.
  3. BackHome - a popular hostel that provides a budget-friendly option with a great atmosphere.

What to Buy in Old City Centre: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

If you’re looking for unique souvenirs and local products to bring back home, Old City Centre has plenty to offer. Here are a few ideas:

  1. Traditional Malay clothing - Beautiful batik shirts and sarongs are widely available in the local markets.
  2. Local crafts - Handmade handicrafts, wood carvings, and woven baskets make great souvenirs.
  3. Durian candy - Durians are known as the king of fruits in Malaysia, and the candy is a unique and sweet souvenir.

Local Culture and Customs of Old City Centre

When it comes to local culture and customs, Old City Centre has a rich and diverse history. Both Malay and Chinese cultures are prominently featured throughout the district. Visitors should be respectful of local customs such as removing shoes before entering a home or temple, and dressing modestly while visiting religious sites.

Best Time to Visit Old City Centre

The best time to visit Old City Centre is during the dry season, which runs from December to February. The temperatures during this time are pleasant and rainfall is minimal. However, do be aware that as this is peak tourist season, prices may be higher and crowds may be larger.

Do’s and Don’ts of Old City Centre

Here are some essential do’s and don’ts to keep in mind when visiting Old City Centre:

Do:

  • Respect local customs and dress modestly while visiting religious sites.
  • Try the local cuisine.
  • Take a river cruise along the Klang River.

Don’t:

  • Touch or disturb wildlife in the forest reserves.
  • Take photos of people without their permission.
  • Agitate or feed the monkeys at Batu Caves.

Safety and Health

When visiting Old City Centre, it’s important to take necessary safety precautions and consider your health. Here are some things to be aware of:

Safety in Old City Centre

  • Be aware of pickpockets and keep valuables safe and secure.
  • Take precautions to ensure safe drinking water and food.
  • Avoid travelling alone at night in darkly lit areas.

Health in Old City Centre

  • Get vaccines and medication recommended by your doctor before arriving.
  • Prevention from mosquito bites is always a good idea.
  • Always use sunscreen and stay hydrated especially during the day.

Getting to Old City Centre: Transportation Options and Logistics

Old City Centre is easily accessible by various public transportation options.

Airports Near Old City Centre

  1. Sultan Abdul Aziz Shah Airport (Subang airport).
  2. Terminal Bersepadu Selatan (TBS) - Major coach and bus station.

Bus Stations Near Old City Centre

  1. Terminal Bersepadu Selatan (TBS).
  2. Puduraya.
  3. Hentian Putra

Other Options

Metered taxis, Grab or public buses are available on-site and around the city.

How to Get Around Old City Centre

Old City Centre is best explored on foot by walking or on bikes. Various transportations such as buses, taxis, and the MRT are also available for your convenience.

Where to Go Next from Old City Centre: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Old City Centre is a gateway to several other parts of Kuala Lumpur, as well as other nearby destinations. Here are some suggestions:

  1. Petronas Twin Towers - This iconic pair of skyscrapers are only 15 minutes away by taxi or public transportation.
  2. Thean Hou Temple - A popular Chinese temple that is only a short drive away from Old City Centre.
  3. Genting Highlands - A popular mountain resort that is ideal for a day trip from Old City Centre.

Conclusion

Old City Centre is a must-visit destination in Kuala Lumpur for anyone interested in history, culture, and architecture. With numerous attractions, stunning natural scenery and delicious local food, it’s an experience that you won’t soon forget.
