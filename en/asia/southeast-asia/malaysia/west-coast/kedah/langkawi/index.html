Introduction to Langkawi

Langkawi is a group of 99 islands on the west coast of Malaysia, located in the state of Kedah. With its pristine beaches, stunning landscapes and diverse culture, this place has been designated as a UNESCO Global Geopark since 2007. Langkawi offers a unique combination of natural and man-made attractions, from historic sites and museums to outdoor adventures and scenic views.

List of Places to Visit in Langkawi

If you’re planning a trip to Langkawi, here are some of the must-visit places:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Mahsuri’s Tomb and Museum: A tribute to the legend of Mahsuri, an innocent woman accused of infidelity and put to death in the 17th century.
  • Galeria Perdana: A museum featuring over 9,000 gifts and awards presented to former Malaysian Prime Minister, Tun Dr. Mahathir Mohamad, during his tenure.
  • Langkawi Craft Complex: A handicraft centre showcasing traditional Malaysian crafts and souvenirs.

Natural Attractions of Langkawi: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Langkawi Sky Bridge: A 125-metre curved pedestrian bridge suspended 660 metres above sea level that offers a breathtaking view of the surrounding mountains, rainforests and the sea.
  • Telaga Tujuh Waterfalls: A series of seven waterfalls located in a lush jungle environment in the north-western part of the island. Hikers will love the trek to the top of this awe-inspiring natural attraction.
  • Kilim Karst Geoforest Park: A protected area that’s also a UNESCO-listed geo-park. It features mangrove swamp, towering limestone cliffs and the chance to see some of Langkawi’s unique wildlife.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Langkawi

Langkawi is also an excellent destination for outdoors enthusiasts, offering an array of hiking and biking trails throughout the island’s interior. Here are some of the best routes:

  • Gunung Raya: The highest peak on Langkawi island (881 metres). The climb is challenging but the views are well worth it.
  • Durian Perangin Waterfall: A trek through lowland rainforest to a cascading waterfall.
  • Kampung Bohor Tempoyak: A 20km mountain biking trail through the scenic padi fields and local Malay villages.

Best Things to Do in Langkawi: Activities and Tours

Langkawi has plenty to offer in terms of activities and tours. Here are a few:

  • Island Hopping Tour: A half-day boat tour of the surrounding islands, including stops at the Pulau Dayang Bunting and Pulau Beras Basah.
  • Sunset Dinner Cruise: A romantic dinner cruise that takes you through the stunning Langkawi sunset while enjoying live music and good food.
  • Mangrove Kayaking: An eco-friendly tour that takes you through Langkawi’s mangrove forest and allows you to see local wildlife up close.

Food and Drink Scene of Langkawi

No trip to Langkawi would be complete without sampling the local cuisine. Here are a few must-try dishes and beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Nasi Lemak: A Malaysian national dish consisting of coconut rice, cucumber, boiled egg, ikan bilis, roasted peanuts and sambal.
  • Satay: A skewered meat dish (usually chicken, beef or lamb) served with peanut sauce and cucumber.
  • Laksa: A spicy, sour noodle soup with fish, vegetables and herbs.

Local Beverages

  • Sirap Bandung: A sweet, pink drink made with rose syrup and milk.
  • Teh Tarik: A hot milk tea that’s commonly found in Malaysia and other parts of Southeast Asia.
  • Coconut Shake: A refreshing drink made with coconut water, ice cream and sweetened condensed milk.

Where to Stay in Langkawi: Hotels, Hostels and Guesthouses

There are plenty of accommodations options to choose from on Langkawi, ranging from luxury resorts to budget hostels. Here are some suggestions:

  • The Datai Langkawi: A five-star resort set in the rainforest with an award-winning golf club, beach-side restaurants and a secluded spot for luxe travellers.
  • The St. Regis Langkawi: A luxurious experience complete with private beaches, elegant rooms and a spa.
  • Temple Tree at Bon Ton: A collection of antique Malay houses converted into a boutique hotel, perfect for a touch of traditional charm.

What to Buy in Langkawi: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

When it comes to shopping in Langkawi, you’ll find a range of locally-produced items, including:

  • Handicrafts: Langkawi Craft Complex is the perfect place to find handmade batik clothing, ceramics, and traditional carvings as well as souvenirs like keychains and magnets.
  • Duty-Free Goods: Langkawi is a duty-free island, meaning that alcohol, cigarettes and chocolates are tax-free.
  • Local Products: The island also offers a variety of products such as honey, batik clothing and coffee that is produced by locals.

Local Culture and Customs of Langkawi

Langkawi has a rich cultural heritage that is reflected in its architecture, food, and festivals. Malay culture is predominant, but there are also elements of Chinese and Indian culture that have influenced the island’s traditions and customs. Some things to keep in mind when visiting include:

  • Dress modestly when visiting mosques or other Islamic places of worship.
  • The majority of the population in Langkawi is Muslim and alcohol is not commonly served in local restaurants and cafes.
  • The traditional greeting is a slight bow with hands together, similar to the Indian Namaste.

Best Time to Visit Langkawi

The best time to visit Langkawi is between November and March when the temperatures are cooler and the skies are clear. This is also the peak season, so expect more visitors and higher prices. The monsoon season is from April to October, which is when the island receives most of its rainfall and windy weather.

Do’s and Don’ts of Langkawi

Do:

  • Respect the local customs and traditions
  • Carry an umbrella or raincoat as sudden downpours are common
  • Try the local food and drink
  • Visit the nearby islands

Don’t:

  • Litter on the beaches or anywhere on the island
  • Drink and drive
  • Wear revealing or inappropriate clothing in public places

Safety and Health

Safety in Langkawi

Langkawi is a safe destination for travellers, but it’s always best to exercise caution and common sense. Here are some tips:

  • Be aware of your surroundings, particularly at night.
  • Keep an eye on your belongings in crowded places.
  • Use reputable tour operators for activities and tours.

Health in Langkawi

No vaccinations are required before entering Langkawi, but it’s still recommended to have up-to-date tetanus and hepatitis A immunisations. It’s also important to be cautious when consuming food and water outside of recognised restaurants and hotels.

Getting to Langkawi: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Langkawi

Langkawi International Airport (LGK) is the only airport in Langkawi and is located on the island of Langkawi itself. The airport is connected to a number of domestic and international destinations.

Bus Stations Near Langkawi

Langkawi doesn’t have a bus station but there are regular bus services connecting other Malaysian cities to Kuala Perlis, where you can board a ferry to Langkawi.

Other Options

Ferries are also a popular way to get to Langkawi as the island is connected to Kuala Perlis and Kuala Kedah on the mainland. Another option is to take a taxi or hire a car and drive your way to Langkawi by using the ferry crossing.

How to Get Around in Langkawi

The easiest way to get around in Langkawi is to rent a car or motorbike, which can be arranged through your hotel or at the airport. Taxis are also readily available and are metered so be sure they turn on their meters, which are calculated based on distance.

Where to Go Next from Langkawi: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

If you are looking to visit the nearby destinations and excursions from Langkawi here are few suggestions:

  • Penang: Known as the “culinary capital” of Malaysia, Penang is famous for its street food and colonial architecture.
  • Koh Lipe, Thailand: A small island in Thailand that’s just a short ferry ride away from Langkawi and offers a unique blend of Thai and Malay culture.
  • Lang Tengah Island: One of the most pristine islands in Malaysia that’s known for its clear waters and coral reefs.

Conclusion

Langkawi is a truly unique destination that offers a mix of natural beauty and cultural attractions. Its pristine beaches, lush jungle interiors and traditional Malay culture make it an ideal destination for travellers interested in history, art, nature and adventure. With so much to do and see on this island, Langkawi is an excellent choice for a tropical holiday.
