Introduction to Kedah

Kedah is a state located on the West Coast of Malaysia in Southeast Asia. Known as the “Rice Bowl of Malaysia,” Kedah is famous for its paddy fields, stunning natural attractions, rich history, and delicious cuisine.

Kedah has a lot to offer visitors, from its beautiful beaches to its impressive museums, cultural attractions, and outdoor adventures. Whether you’re looking for an action-packed trip or a more relaxing vacation, Kedah has something for everyone.

Week-Long Itinerary for Kedah

Here is a seven-day itinerary for exploring Kedah:

Day 1: Visit Alor Setar Tower and Kedah State Museum. Day 2: Explore the Bujang Valley Archaeological Museum and Gunung Jerai. Day 3: Take a tour of the Mahathir Birthplace Museum and drive to Langkawi Island. Day 4: Relax on Langkawi Island’s beaches and visit Pulau Payar Marine Park. Day 5: Explore Ulu Muda Eco Park and Sedim River Recreation Park. Day 6: Take a mangrove tour and visit Rumah Merdeka. Day 7: Hike the Langkawi mountains and explore the island’s food scene.

Local Culture and Customs of Kedah

Kedah has a rich cultural heritage, and visitors should take the time to learn about its customs and traditions. Some important aspects of Kedah’s culture include its emphasis on respect for elders, strong family ties, and the importance of religion.

Best Time to Visit Kedah

The best time to visit Kedah is between December and April when the weather is sunny and dry.

Do’s and Don’ts of Kedah

Do:

  • Dress modestly when visiting religious sites.
  • Follow proper etiquette when eating with your hands.
  • Try to learn some basic Malay phrases to communicate with locals.

Don’t:

  • Disrespect local customs and traditions.
  • Engage in public displays of affection.
  • Drink alcohol in public places.

Safety and Health

Safety in Kedah

Kedah is generally a safe destination for visitors. However, travelers should take the usual precautions to avoid theft and scams, especially in crowded tourist areas.

Health in Kedah

Healthcare in Kedah is of a good standard, but it’s always recommended to have updated health insurance before traveling. Visitors should also take precautions against mosquito-borne diseases by using insect repellent and wearing long sleeves and pants.

Getting to Kedah: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Kedah

  • Langkawi International Airport
  • Sultan Abdul Halim Airport

Bus Stations Near Kedah

  • Alor Setar Bus Terminal
  • Jitra Bus Terminal

Other Options

  • Train: There are no train stations in Kedah, but visitors can take a train to a nearby state.
  • Ferry: Travelers can take a ferry from Malaysia or Thailand to Langkawi Island.

How to Get Around in Kedah

The easiest way to get around Kedah is by car or motorbike rental. Alternatively, visitors can take a taxi or use ride-hailing apps.
