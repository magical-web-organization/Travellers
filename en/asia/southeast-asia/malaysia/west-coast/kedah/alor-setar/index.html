Introduction to Alor Setar

Alor Setar, the capital city of the state of Kedah, is a gem located in the west coast of Malaysia. It’s a charming city filled with history and culture, with a mix of traditional and modern attractions. Alor Setar is also known as “rice bowl of Malaysia”, because of its vast paddy fields. Here, you’ll find a variety of fun things to do, cultural landmarks, and delicious cuisine that will make your visit to Malaysia unforgettable.

List of Places to Visit in Alor Setar

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Balai Besar - built in the 18th century, this Malay royal palace was used as Kedah’s administrative centre until recent years. It’s now a popular tourist attraction and venue for cultural events.
  • Balai Nobat - this iconic building contains the sacred instruments of the royal orchestra. You can witness a traditional performance of the royal orchestra every Friday.
  • Pekan Rabu Complex - a traditional Malay market located in the heart of the city, offering local delicacies, handicrafts, and clothing items.
  • Zahir Mosque - this stunning mosque is one of the most significant landmarks in Alor Setar. It was built in 1912 with five large black domes symbolizing the five principles of Islam.
  • Kedah State Museum - this museum showcases the history, culture, and heritage of the Kedah state. You can find artifacts, exhibits, and collectibles from the ancient times of Kedah Sultanate up until contemporary Malaysia.

Natural Attractions of Alor Setar: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Gunung Keriang Recreational Forest - located on the outskirts of Alor Setar, this nature reserve offers scenic views of the surrounding hills and forests. You can go hiking, camping, and picnic with family and friends.
  • Menara Alor Setar - this tower is the tallest in Kedah and also the 6th highest telecommunication tower in Malaysia. You can witness a spectacular panoramic view of Alor Setar from the top.
  • Sungai Petani Hot Spring - located 30 minutes away from Alor Setar, this natural hot spring is perfect for relaxing and rejuvenating your body. The water is believed to have healing properties that can soothe joint and muscle pains.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Alor Setar

  • Sungai Korok Hiking Trail - located in Sungai Korok Forest Reserve, this trail is perfect for hikers who want to enjoy nature and see exotic flora and fauna.
  • Jalan Putra Biking Route - this pathway offers a scenic viaduct along the Kedah River, making it perfect for cyclists who want to enjoy the beautiful riverside views.

Best Things to Do in Alor Setar: Activities and Tours

  • Cycling Tour of Alor Setar - you can take a guided tour and cycle around the city, stopping at various landmarks and attractions. This is a great way to see the sights and sounds of Alor Setar while getting some exercise.
  • Kedah Paddy Tour - you can take a guided tour of the paddy fields in Alor Setar, learn about the history and culture of paddy planting, and enjoy a unique experience of being in the great outdoors.
  • Night Market Tour - experience the nightlife of Alor Setar by joining a night market tour. You can sample local delicacies and taste authentic street food while getting a glimpse of the local culture.

Food and Drink Scene of Alor Setar

Must-Try Dishes

  • Nasi Lemak Kukus - a classic Malaysian dish with steamed rice cooked in coconut milk. You can eat it with spicy sambal, ikan bilis, fried chicken, or boiled egg.
  • Laksa Kedah - a popular traditional dish made with vermicelli noodles, coconut milk, fish gravy, and a variety of herbs and spices.
  • Nasi Tomato - a delicious dish made with tomato rice, accompanied by spicy chicken, sambal, and egg.
  • Putu Mayam - light and fluffy rice flour noodles served with sugar and grated coconut.

Local Beverages

  • Teh Tarik - a popular hot beverage made from black tea and condensed milk, brewed through a process of “pulling” the tea with both hands from one container to another.
  • Kopi O - a strong, black coffee, without sugar, served typically in small cups.
  • Air Batu Campur (ABC) - a refreshing local dessert made of shaved ice, red beans, corn, jelly, and evaporated milk.

Where to Stay in Alor Setar: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • The Leverage Business Hotel - a modern hotel located in the heart of Alor Setar. It offers comfortable rooms with excellent amenities and is perfect for business travelers who want to stay in the city centre.
  • The Jerai Hotel - a grand hotel with a classic colonial theme, overlooking the Gunung Jerai Mountain. It offers an authentic traditional experience, with spacious rooms and excellent service.
  • The Regency Hotel Alor Setar - a luxurious hotel located minutes from the city’s landmarks. It offers a range of luxurious amenities, including spa and wellness facilities.
  • Sakura Guest House - a budget-friendly guesthouse located in the city centre. It offers basic amenities, comfortable rooms, and is perfect for backpackers and budget travellers.

What to Buy in Alor Setar: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Traditional Handicrafts - you can find a variety of traditional handicrafts, including batik, weaving, and woodcarving. You can purchase them as souvenirs or gifts for friends and family.
  • Local Delicacies - you can purchase traditional Malay sweets, snacks, and desserts, which are unique to the Kedah region.
  • Paddy Products - Kedah is known as the “rice bowl of Malaysia,” so it’s only fitting that you purchase some paddy products such as rice, crackers, or even paddy-derived cosmetic products.

Local Culture and Customs of Alor Setar

  • Religion - Malaysia is predominantly Muslim, so it’s essential to respect the local customs and dress conservatively when visiting religious sites.
  • Language - The official language in Malaysia is Bahasa Malaysia, though English and Chinese are also widely spoken.
  • Etiquette - Malaysians are known for their hospitality. It’s essential to practice courtesy, respect, and politeness when interacting with locals.

Best Time to Visit Alor Setar

The best time to visit Alor Setar is during the dry season, which is between November to February when the weather is much cooler and less humid.

Do’s and Don’ts of Alor Setar

Do:

  • Dress appropriately when visiting religious sites.
  • Explore the local culture and try new things.
  • Respect the local customs and traditions.

Don’t:

  • Don’t disregard the local etiquette by being rude or disrespectful.
  • Don’t litter or damage the environment in any way.
  • Don’t be a part of illegal activities.

Safety and Health

Safety in Alor Setar

Alor Setar is generally safe, and the crime rate is low. However, it’s always best to take precautions and avoid carrying large amounts of cash in crowded areas.

Health in Alor Setar

It’s essential to stay hydrated during the hot months and wear sunscreen to prevent sunburns. It’s also recommended that you have travel insurance and get vaccinated to avoid getting sick.

Getting to Alor Setar: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Alor Setar

  • Alor Setar International Airport - located in Kepala Batas, approximately 20 km north of Alor Setar. It is well connected to all major cities in Malaysia.

Bus Stations Near Alor Setar

  • Alor Setar Bus Station - located in the city centre, offers regular bus services to all major cities in Malaysia.

Other Options

  • **Train ** - you can take a train from Kuala Lumpur to Alor Setar, which takes approximately 6 hours.
  • Car rental - Car rental agencies are available for you to rent a car for private transportation.

How to Get Around in Alor Setar

The best way to get around Alor Setar is by taxi, Grab, or car rental. Some other options include local buses, which are frequent and affordable, and cycling, which is an excellent way to explore the city on your own terms.

Where to Go Next from Alor Setar: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Langkawi Island - a popular tropical island getaway, just an hour ferry ride away from Alor Setar.
  • Penang Island - a UNESCO World Heritage site, known for its street art and iconic dishes, easily accessible by bus or car.
  • Kuala Lumpur - the Malaysian capital city, located approximately 400 km away from Alor Setar, accessible by bus or flight.

Conclusion

Alor Setar may not be as popular as other cities in Malaysia, but it offers a unique travel experience that’s worth exploring. This charming city is packed with historical landmarks, natural attractions, and exciting activities that will leave you feeling enchanted. From cycling tours to traditional handicraft shopping, you’re sure to find something that appeals to you in this beautiful city. Go ahead and plan your Alor Setar trip today!
