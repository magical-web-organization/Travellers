Introduction to Kulim

Kulim is a town located in the state of Kedah, Malaysia, on the west coast of Peninsular Malaysia. This quaint town was once a thriving tin-mining town, but now, it is known for its serene beauty and cultural significance. With a rich history, Kulim attracts visitors from around the world to explore the historical landmarks, natural beauty, and culinary delights.

List of Places to Visit in Kulim

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

1. Kulim Gallery of Royal Portraits

The Kulim Gallery of Royal Portraits houses a collection of portraits of the Malaysian rulers from the past to the present. The gallery showcases the evolution of Malaysia’s political system and the lives of the country’s monarchs.

2. Kulim Art Gallery

The Kulim Art Gallery is home to a permanent collection of works by local artists. The gallery features a series of rotating exhibitions throughout the year, showcasing paintings, sculptures, and installations.

3. Kedah Royal Museum

The Kedah Royal Museum, located in Alor Setar, is just a short drive from Kulim. The museum is housed in a 19th-century palace that showcases the historical and cultural heritage of the Kedah Sultanate.

Natural Attractions of Kulim: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

1. Gunung Jerai

Gunung Jerai is a famous hiking spot in Kulim. The mountain is a popular destination for both locals and tourists, offering stunning views of the surrounding hills and valleys.

2. Sedim River

Sedim River is a popular destination for white water rafting enthusiasts. The river meanders through a dense forest, offering an adrenaline-pumping experience.

3. Sedim Tree Top Walk

The Sedim Tree Top Walk is a unique experience that offers stunning views of the surrounding forest from above the canopies. The walkway is over 1km long and reaches a height of up to 50 meters.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Kulim

Kulim is a paradise for outdoor enthusiasts, and hiking and biking are popular activities. Gunung Jerai is the most popular hiking destination, and there are several trails to choose from, ranging from easy to advanced. For biking enthusiasts, there are several scenic routes, including the Kulim Bike Trail and the Kulim Mountain Bike Park.

Best Things to Do in Kulim: Activities and Tours

1. Sedim River White Water Rafting

White water rafting along the Sedim River is an exhilarating experience that offers stunning views of the surrounding forest.

2. Kulim Night Market

Kulim Night Market is a popular destination for locals and tourists alike. The market offers a wide variety of local delicacies, including grilled seafood, satay, and other street foods.

3. Kulim Wetlands

The Kulim Wetlands is a nature reserve that offers stunning views of the surrounding forest and marshes. Visitors can enjoy bird watching, hiking, and kayaking in the reserve.

Food and Drink Scene of Kulim

Must-Try Dishes

1. Nasi Kerabu

Nasi Kerabu is a popular dish in the state of Kedah. It is a type of blue rice that is served with fried chicken, fish, or beef, and various vegetables. The rice is colored blue using butterfly pea flowers.

2. Laksa

Laksa is a popular Malaysian noodle dish that is made with a spicy coconut milk-based soup. It is often served with fish or shrimp.

3. Satay

Satay is a staple dish in Malaysia and is typically made with grilled meat on skewers. It is often served with a spicy peanut sauce.

Local Beverages

1. Teh Tarik

Teh Tarik is a popular Malaysian tea that is made with black tea and condensed milk. The tea is frothed up by being poured back and forth between cups, giving it a creamy and frothy texture.

2. Kopi-O

Kopi-O is a strong black coffee that is brewed with sugar and served hot.

3. Sirap Bandung

Sirap Bandung is a sweet and creamy drink that is made with rose syrup and condensed milk.

Where to Stay in Kulim: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Kulim offers various accommodations, including hotels, hostels, and guesthouses. There are several budget-friendly accommodations available, as well as high-end resorts. Some of the top accommodation options in Kulim include the Grand Alora Hotel, the Kulim Park Inn, and the Kulim Forest Chalet.

What to Buy in Kulim: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Kulim is known for its local handicrafts, including batik, wood carvings, and textiles. Some of the best shopping destinations in Kulim include the Kulim Landmark Central, the Tesco Kulim, and the Kulim Hi-Tech Park.

Local Culture and Customs of Kulim

Kulim is a melting pot of different cultures, including Malay, Chinese, and Indian. The local culture is deeply rooted in tradition, and visitors are encouraged to respect local customs. Islam is the predominant religion in Kulim, and it is important to dress modestly when visiting mosques or religious sites.

Best Time to Visit Kulim

The best time to visit Kulim is between December and February, when the weather is dry and it’s not too hot. It’s best to avoid visiting during the monsoon season from September to November, as heavy rain and flooding can occur during this time.

Do’s and Don’ts of Kulim

Do:

  • Respect local customs and traditions
  • Dress modestly when visiting religious sites
  • Try the local cuisine
  • Explore the natural beauty of Kulim

Don’t:

  • Engage in public displays of affection
  • Disrespect religious or cultural sites
  • Overstay your visa

Safety and Health

Safety in Kulim

Kulim is generally a safe destination for tourists. However, visitors should take precautions to avoid theft and pickpocketing in crowded areas.

Health in Kulim

Kulim is a relatively healthy destination, but visitors should take precautions to avoid food and waterborne illnesses. It’s best to drink bottled water and avoid ice in drinks.

Getting to Kulim: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Kulim

The nearest airport to Kulim is the Penang International Airport, which is approximately 51km away from Kulim.

Bus Stations Near Kulim

Kulim is well-connected to other parts of Malaysia by bus. The Kulim Bus Terminal is the main bus station in Kulim.

Other Options

Visitors can also take a taxi or rent a car to get to Kulim from other parts of Malaysia. However, it’s important to note that traffic in Malaysia can be congested.

How to Get Around in Kulim

Kulim is a relatively small town, and it’s easy to get around on foot or by bicycle. Taxis and Grab are also available for visitors who prefer not to walk or bike.

Where to Go Next from Kulim: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Kulim is located in a strategic location, and there are several nearby destinations that visitors can explore. Some of the top nearby destinations include Penang, Langkawi, and Ipoh.

Conclusion

Kulim is a hidden gem in Malaysia and offers visitors a unique blend of culture, history, and natural beauty. From exploring the local museums and historical sites to hiking Gunung Jerai and kayaking in Sedim River, there is something for everyone in Kulim. With a vibrant food and beverage scene and plenty of accommodations, Kulim is the perfect destination for visitors looking for a laid-back and authentic Malaysian experience.
