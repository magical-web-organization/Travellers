Taiping is a charming town located in the state of Perak, on the West Coast of Malaysia. Taiping offers a perfect blend of history, culture, nature, and adventure, which makes it an ideal destination for travelers seeking an off-the-beaten-track experience. In this comprehensive travel guide, we will reveal the best things to do, see, and experience in Taiping.

Introduction to Taiping

Taiping is a town that has been blessed with a rich history and culture. The name “Taiping” means “peace” in Chinese, and the town is famous for being the first city in Malaysia to have a railway line. Taiping is also known for its lush greenery, natural attractions, hiking/biking trails, and vibrant food scene.

List of Places to Visit in Taiping

If you are planning to visit Taiping, here is a list of places that you should definitely add to your itinerary.

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Taiping is known for its rich history and culture, which is reflected in its numerous museums and historical sites. Here are some of the must-visit cultural attractions:

  • Taiping Museum: A museum that showcases the history and cultural heritage of Taiping through various exhibits and artifacts.

  • Perak Museum: One of the oldest museums in Malaysia, the Perak Museum houses a vast collection of artifacts and exhibits from the state of Perak.

  • Colonial Buildings: Taiping has many colonial buildings that are worth visiting, including the Old Residency, the Federated Malay States (FMS) Railway Building, the Taiping Court, and the All Saints’ Church.

Natural Attractions of Taiping: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Taiping is a nature lover’s paradise, with its lush greenery and natural attractions. Here are some of the top natural attractions that Taiping has to offer:

  • Taiping Lake Gardens: The Taiping Lake Gardens is a massive park that features beautiful gardens, lakes, and recreational activities. It is an ideal place for a picnic, morning jog, or leisurely stroll.

  • Bukit Larut (Maxwell Hill): Known for its scenic views and cool weather, Bukit Larut is a popular hiking destination for locals and tourists alike.

  • Taman Negara Bintang: Located just outside of Taiping, Taman Negara Bintang is a pristine rainforest that is home to a diverse range of flora and fauna.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Taiping

For outdoor enthusiasts, Taiping offers many hiking and biking trails that are worth exploring. Here are some of the best routes:

  • Maxwell Hill Trail: A challenging but rewarding trail that takes you to the summit of Bukit Larut.

  • Pancur Falls Trail: A scenic trail that passes by beautiful waterfalls and streams.

  • Taiping Heritage Trail: A self-guided walking trail that takes you through the historic town center of Taiping.

Best Things to Do in Taiping: Activities and Tours

Apart from its natural and cultural attractions, Taiping offers many fun activities and tours that cater to all types of travelers. Here are some of the best things to do in Taiping:

  • Night Safari Taiping: A guided tour that takes you through the Taiping Zoo at night to observe nocturnal animals in their natural habitat.

  • Street Food Tour: Sample some of the best local food that Taiping has to offer with a guided street food tour.

  • Horse Riding: Take a leisurely horse ride around the Taiping Lake Gardens and enjoy the scenic views.

Week-Long Itinerary for Taiping

Here’s a suggested itinerary for a week-long trip to Taiping:

Day Activity
Day 1 Visit the Taiping Museum and explore the historic town center
Day 2 Hike Bukit Larut and enjoy the scenic views
Day 3 Take a day trip to Taman Negara Bintang and go for a nature walk
Day 4 Explore the Taiping Lake Gardens and its recreational activities

Day 6 Take a cooking class and learn to make traditional Malaysian dishes
Day 7 Go souvenir shopping and sample local delicacies before departing

Food and Drink Scene of Taiping

Taiping has a vibrant food and drink scene, where you can sample delicious local delicacies and drinks. Here are some of the must-try food and drink items in Taiping:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Nasi Lemak: A fragrant rice dish cooked in coconut milk and served with various sides such as sambal, peanuts, and fried anchovies.

  • Laksa: A spicy noodle soup made from fish or chicken broth, served with rice noodles and a variety of garnishes.

  • Popiah: A fresh spring roll filled with vegetables, bean sprouts, tofu, and shrimp, wrapped in light rice paper.

Local Beverages

  • White Coffee: A popular coffee that originated in Ipoh, made from roasted beans and condensed milk.

  • Teh Tarik: A sweet, creamy tea that is prepared by “pulling” the tea from one cup to another, creating a frothy and delicious beverage.

  • Cendol: A refreshing dessert consisting of shaved ice, coconut milk, palm sugar, and green jelly noodles.

Where to Stay in Taiping: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Taiping offers a variety of accommodation options to suit all budgets and preferences. Here are some of the best places to stay in Taiping:

  • The Haven Resort Hotel & Residences: A luxury resort with spacious suites and villas, set amidst lush greenery and serene lakes.

  • Novotel Taiping: A modern hotel located in the heart of Taiping, offering comfortable rooms and easy access to the town’s attractions.

  • Taiping Homestay: A budget-friendly guesthouse that offers clean and comfortable rooms, with a homely and welcoming atmosphere.

What to Buy in Taiping: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

If you are looking for unique souvenirs and local products to bring back from Taiping, here are some of the best items to consider:

  • Gula Melaka: A type of palm sugar that is commonly used in Malaysian desserts.

  • Handwoven products: Taiping is known for its handwoven products such as baskets, mats, and bags, which make great souvenirs.

  • Antique items: Taiping has many antique shops that sell unique and rare items such as old coins, watches, and jewelry.

Local Culture and Customs of Taiping

Taiping is a multi-cultural town that is home to various ethnic groups such as Chinese, Malay, and Indian. Here are some of the local customs and cultural practices that you should be aware of:

  • Respect for elders: In Malaysian culture, elders are highly respected, and it is customary to address them with terms of respect such as “Aunty” or “Uncle.”

  • Religious practices: Malaysia is a predominantly Muslim country, and it is important to respect Islamic practices and traditions.

  • Dress code: While there is no strict dress code in Taiping, it is advisable to dress modestly when visiting religious sites and when in public places.

Best Time to Visit Taiping

The best time to visit Taiping is from May to October, when the weather is dry and pleasant, and the natural attractions are at their best. However, be prepared for occasional showers as Taiping is known for its rainfall throughout the year.

Do’s and Don’ts of Taiping

Here are some do’s and don’ts that travelers should be aware of when visiting Taiping:

Do:

  • Do try the street food: Taiping has a vibrant street food scene, and it is a great opportunity to sample some of the best local delicacies.

  • Do respect local customs and traditions: It is important to respect local customs and traditions, especially when visiting religious sites.

  • Do take care of the environment: Taiping is known for its lush greenery and natural attractions, and it is important to take care of the environment to preserve its beauty.

Don’t:

  • Don’t litter: Littering is a serious offense in Malaysia, and travelers should dispose of their waste properly.

  • Don’t disrespect religion: Malaysia is a multi-religious country, and it is important to show respect towards all religions and their practices.

  • Don’t be disrespectful towards elders: In Malaysian culture, elders are highly respected, and it is important to show them respect.

Safety and Health

Safety in Taiping

Taiping is generally a safe town to visit, but travelers should take basic precautions such as avoiding unlit areas at night and keeping valuables secure. It is also advisable to use only registered taxis and to be cautious when using public transportation.

Health in Taiping

Tap water in Taiping is generally safe to drink, but it is advisable to drink bottled water to be cautious. Travelers should also take standard health precautions such as using mosquito repellent and wearing long-sleeved clothing to avoid mosquito bites.

Getting to Taiping: Transportation Options and Logistics

Taiping is located approximately 70 km from Ipoh, the capital city of Perak. Here are some of the transportation options available to travelers:

Airports Near Taiping

  • Sultan Azlan Shah Airport (IPH): Located in Ipoh, approximately 70 km from Taiping.

Bus Stations Near Taiping

  • Taiping Bus Station: Located in the town center, servicing local and intercity buses.

  • Kamunting Bus Station: Located 7 km from Taiping, with connections to Kuala Lumpur and Penang.

Other Options

  • Train: Taiping’s railway station services trains to and from Kuala Lumpur and other major cities in Malaysia.

  • Car: Renting a car is a good option for travelers who want to explore Taiping and its surrounding areas.

How to Get Around in Taiping

Taiping is a small and compact town, which makes it easy to get around on foot or by bicycle. Taxis and local buses are also available, but these may not be as frequent or reliable as in larger cities.

Where to Go Next from Taiping: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

If you have more time to explore, here are some nearby destinations and excursions that are worth considering:

  • Ipoh: A charming city located 70 km from Taiping, known for its street art, cave temples, and food scene.

  • Kuala Kangsar: A historic royal town located 40 km from Taiping, home to the Ubudiah Mosque and the Istana Kenangan Palace.

  • Pulau Pangkor: A beautiful island located off the west coast of Perak, known for its pristine beaches and water sports.

Conclusion

Taiping is a hidden gem that offers a unique and authentic Malaysian experience. Whether you are a nature lover, history buff, foodie, or outdoor enthusiast, Taiping has something for everyone. We hope this comprehensive travel guide will help you plan your trip to Taiping and make the most of your stay.
