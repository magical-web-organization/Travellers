Introduction to Pangkor

Pangkor Island is a beautiful and peaceful tropical island located off the west coast of Malaysia’s state of Perak. With its crystal-clear water, golden sandy beaches, traditional fishing villages, and mouth-watering seafood, it is one of Malaysia’s hidden gems. Pangkor Island offers an array of activities such as swimming, snorkeling, fishing, jungle trekking, and water sports.

List of Places to Visit in Pangkor

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  1. Dutch Fort - Considered to be the oldest fort in Malaysia, it was built by the Dutch in the 17th century.
  2. Fu Lin Kong Temple - This popular Chinese temple, known as the “Wealth Temple”, features a nine-story pagoda, carved pillars, and intricate roof decorations.
  3. Kota Belanda (Dutch Fort) Historical Complex - This historical complex houses the remains of a Dutch fort, ammunition storage, and a British outpost.
  4. Galeri Sultan Azlan Shah - A small gallery featuring an exhibition of paintings and sculptures, as well as displays documenting the life of the Sultan Azlan Shah.

Natural Attractions of Pangkor: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  1. Teluk Nipah Beach - The most popular beach on Pangkor Island, ideal for watersports like banana boat rides and parasailing.
  2. Coral Bay - A secluded beach that offers spectacular views of the sunset.
  3. Pasir Bogak Beach - An ideal beach for swimming, sunbathing and enjoying water sports activities.
  4. Hornbill Feeding Ground - A bird-watching haven to spot the iconic hornbills of Malaysia.
  5. Pangkor Hill - Trekking up this hill provide panoramic views of the island and the sea.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Pangkor

There are several hiking and biking routes around Pangkor Island. One of the most scenic routes is the Pangkor Hill trek that takes around 2 hours. Alternatively, you could rent a bike and explore the island at your own pace. Exploring the fishing villages and capturing the stunning views is an experience that you will treasure.

Best Things to Do in Pangkor: Activities and Tours

  1. Snorkelling and Diving - The reefs surrounding the island offer great opportunities for a personal underwater experience.
  2. Island Hopping - Go on a day trip and explore the nearby islands of Pangkor Laut, Pulau Jarak, and Pulau Sembilan.
  3. Fishing - Take a tour with a local fisherman and learn about traditional fishing techniques.
  4. Sunset Cruise - Get on a boat and watch the sun dip into the horizon, the red and orange hues in the sky are something to behold.
  5. Jet Skiing & Banana Boat Riding - Enjoy the adrenaline rush by jet skiing or riding a banana boat.

Week-Long Itinerary for Pangkor

Day 1: Arrive at Pangkor Island. Explore the Dutch Fort and nearby museums. Day 2: Spend the day relaxing on Teluk Nipah Beach and trying exhilarating watersports activities. Day 3: Discover Pangkor’s dense jungle by visiting the Hornbill Feeding Ground and jungle trekking to the Pangkor Hill for panoramic views. Day 4: Visit the Coral Bay and have a sunset massage on the beach. Day 5: Take a day trip to Pulau Sembilan and go on an island hopping adventure. Day 6: Go fishing with the local fishermen in their traditional boats. Day 7: Spend the morning at Pasir Bogak Beach and depart from Pangkor Island.

Food and Drink Scene of Pangkor

Must-Try Dishes

  1. Nasi Lemak - A Malaysian delicacy that is served as a complete meal with rice, spicy sambal, and anchovies.
  2. Asam Pedas - A sour and spicy fish stew that is a Perak speciality.
  3. Mee Goreng - A popular noodle dish with a spicy and tangy sauce, vegetables and meat, garnished with peanuts and fried shallots.
  4. Satay - Malaysian meat skewers, grilled over an open fire and served with peanut sauce.

Local Beverages

  1. Sirap Bandung - Rose-flavoured milkshake, blended with ice, served in tall glasses.
  2. Teh Tarik - A hot tea beverage with milk, sugar and cinnamon.
  3. Kopi-O - A strong black coffee that is a favourite drink among the locals.

Where to Stay in Pangkor: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  1. Pangkor Laut Resort - A luxurious resort with overwater villas and an array of activities.
  2. The Island Hotel - A boutique hotel with air-conditioned rooms and free Wi-Fi.
  3. Pangkor Bay View Beach Resort - A budget-friendly resort that is just a minute’s walk away from Teluk Nipah Beach.

What to Buy in Pangkor: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  1. Belacan - A pungent shrimp paste used in traditional Malaysian cooking.
  2. Keropok Lekor - A popular snack made of fish and sago flour.
  3. Handicrafts - Look out for traditional Malaysian crafts such as batik, wood carvings, and colourful woven mats.

Local Culture and Customs of Pangkor

The locals on Pangkor Island are friendly and welcoming people who predominantly rely on fishing and tourism as their main sources of income. Respectful behaviour is encouraged in public spaces, especially at places of worship and traditional villages.

Best Time to Visit Pangkor

The best time to visit Pangkor Island is between the months of April and September when the weather is dry and sunny, and the sea is generally calm.

Do’s and Don’ts of Pangkor

Do:

  1. Respect the local culture and customs.
  2. Try the local delicacies and seafood dishes.
  3. Book your accommodation in advance during peak season.

Don’t:

  1. Litter on the beaches or streets.
  2. Leave your valuables on display or unsecured in public areas.
  3. Swim in unguarded areas.

Safety and Health

Safety in Pangkor

Pangkor Island is generally safe for visitors, but like any other tourist destination, precautions should be taken while travelling. It is advisable to keep enough cash with you to pay for services, lock your rooms when leaving, and avoid isolated areas at night.

Health in Pangkor

Although there are no major health concerns associated with traveling to Pangkor Island, it is essential to drink bottled water and use sunscreen while enjoying outdoor activities.

Getting to Pangkor: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Pangkor

The closest airport to Pangkor Island is the Sultan Azlan Shah Airport in Ipoh. From there, you can take a taxi or bus to Lumut Jetty. A ferry service runs regularly from Lumut Jetty to Pangkor Island.

Bus Stations Near Pangkor

The closest bus station is the Lumut Bus Terminal, and the journey from Kuala Lumpur takes approximately four hours.

Other Options

Private taxi services and car hires are also available for direct transfers to the jetty of Pangkor Island.

How to Get Around Pangkor

The island is small enough to explore on foot, and there are also motorcycle rentals available for a more adventurous experience.

Where to Go Next from Pangkor: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Perak has plenty of scenic and cultural destinations to visit nearby Pangkor, including Ipoh, Gua Tempurung, Taiping Lake Gardens, and Kellie’s Castle.

Conclusion

Pangkor Island is a slice of paradise in Malaysia. With its beautiful beaches, historical attractions, delicious food, and friendly locals, it is a destination worth exploring. This travel guide gives you an insight into the various fun activities, accommodation options, cultural experiences, and transportation options to make your trip to Pangkor Island a memorable one.
