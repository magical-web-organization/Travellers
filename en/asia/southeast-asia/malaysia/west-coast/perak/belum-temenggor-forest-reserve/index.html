Introduction to Belum Temenggor Forest Reserve

Belum Temenggor Forest Reserve is a vast, undisturbed rainforest covering an area of 300,000 hectares in Perak, West Coast, Malaysia. It is said to be over 130 million years old and is home to an incredible array of wildlife, including tigers, Asian elephants, tapirs, and many more. Visitors to this beautiful forest reserve can enjoy outdoor adventures, scenic views, cultural attractions, and much more.

List of Places to Visit in Belum Temenggor Forest Reserve

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Orang Asli Museum: This museum showcases the traditional way of life of the Orang Asli, the indigenous people of Malaysia. Visitors can learn about their culture, customs, and beliefs.

  • Belum Discovery Centre: A hub of knowledge for everything related to the Belum Temenggor Forest Reserve, this center offers educational displays, interactive exhibits, and a wealth of information to visitors.

Natural Attractions of Belum Temenggor Forest Reserve: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Royal Belum State Park: This section of the forest reserve is home to a diverse population of flora and fauna. Visitors can go on a guided tour to explore the area and spot wildlife.

  • Temenggor Lake: The second largest man-made lake in Malaysia, Temenggor Lake is a peaceful spot for fishing, canoeing, and bird watching.

  • Mesmerizing Waterfalls: Discover the enchanting beauty of the waterfalls, including the Sungai Ruok Waterfall, which is easily accessible, and the Temenggor Waterfall, which requires a bit of a hike but is worth it for the breathtaking scenery.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Belum Temenggor Forest Reserve

Belum Temenggor Forest Reserve has plenty of hiking and biking trails that vary in difficulty level. Visitors can choose from a range of jungle trekking excursions, such as:

  • Tertiary Trek: A challenging trek through the dense jungle that takes visitors to scenic spots such as rivers, waterfalls, and hot springs.

  • Sira Gajah Trek: A moderate trek that takes visitors upstream to a refreshing waterfall.

Best Things to Do in Belum Temenggor Forest Reserve: Activities and Tours

Belum Temenggor Forest Reserve offers plenty of activities for visitors:

  • Wildlife Safari Tour: Local guides take visitors on an adventurous ride through the rainforest in search of rare and exotic wildlife.

  • Boat tours on Temenggor Lake: Visitors can take in the breathtaking views while cruising on a boat.

Week-Long Itinerary for Belum Temenggor Forest Reserve

A week-long itinerary for Belum Temenggor Forest Reserve is as follows:

Day 1: Arrival and check-in

Day 2: Visit to Orang Asli Museum

Day 3: Trek to Sungai Ruok Waterfall

Day 4: Wildlife Safari Tour

Day 5: Boat tour on Temenggor Lake

Day 6: Explore the Royal Belum State Park

Day 7: Departure

Food and Drink Scene of Belum Temenggor Forest Reserve

Must-Try Dishes

  • Ipoh Hor Fun: This dish of flat rice noodles and shredded chicken is a must-try local delicacy.

  • Satay: Skewered and grilled meat served with peanut sauce is a popular street food.

Local Beverages

  • Teh Tarik: A popular sweet milk tea

  • Air Kelapa: Fresh coconut water straight from the fruit.

Where to Stay in Belum Temenggor Forest Reserve: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

There are several accommodation options available for visitors:

  • Belum Rainforest Resort: This resort offers luxurious accommodations to visitors looking for a comfortable stay.

  • Bamboo Chalets: These eco-friendly accommodations offer a unique experience for visitors who want to stay in the jungle.

What to Buy in Belum Temenggor Forest Reserve: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Handwoven baskets

  • Traditional Orang Asli crafts

Local Culture and Customs of Belum Temenggor Forest Reserve

The Orang Asli, the indigenous people of Malaysia, are part of the culture of Belum Temenggor Forest Reserve. Visitors are encouraged to learn about their culture and customs and to respect their way of life.

Best Time to Visit Belum Temenggor Forest Reserve

The best time to visit is between March and October when the weather is dry and the temperature is pleasant.

Do’s and Don’ts of Belum Temenggor Forest Reserve

Do:

  • Respect the local culture and customs

  • Follow the guidelines set by tour guides and park officials

Don’t:

  • Harm or disturb the wildlife

  • Litter or damage the environment

Safety and Health

Safety in Belum Temenggor Forest Reserve

  • Visitors should always follow the instructions and guidelines set by park officials

  • Visitors should avoid trekking alone and should always stay on marked trails

Health in Belum Temenggor Forest Reserve

  • Visitors should take necessary safety precautions against mosquito-borne diseases such as dengue fever and malaria.

Getting to Belum Temenggor Forest Reserve: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Belum Temenggor Forest Reserve

The nearest airport is Sultan Azlan Shah Airport in Ipoh, which is about 2.5 hours away.

Bus Stations Near Belum Temenggor Forest Reserve

Buses from Kuala Lumpur to Gerik are available daily. From Gerik, visitors can take a taxi to Belum Temenggor Forest Reserve.

How to Get Around in Belum Temenggor Forest Reserve

Visitors can hire a guide or take a boat tour to explore the forest reserve.

Where to Go Next from Belum Temenggor Forest Reserve: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Cameron Highlands: A popular hill station for its tea plantations and strawberry farms.

  • Penang: Known as the food capital of Malaysia, Penang is a delightful destination for those who love street food and historic architecture.

Conclusion

Belum Temenggor Forest Reserve is a stunning destination for visitors who love nature, wildlife, and cultural attractions. With plenty of outdoor adventures, scenic views, and cultural experiences, this forest reserve is a must-visit destination for travelers.
