Introduction to Padang Besar

Padang Besar is a small town located in Perlis, on the West Coast of Malaysia. It is known for its cultural attractions, outdoor adventures, and local food scene. The town is also an important transportation hub, with a train station that connects Malaysia and Thailand.

List of Places to Visit in Padang Besar

From historical landmarks to natural wonders, there are plenty of places to explore in Padang Besar. Here are some must-visit attractions:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

1. Kota Kayang Museum - This museum displays the history and culture of Perlis and its people. Visitors can learn about the local traditions, crafts, and architecture.

2. Gua Kelam Cave - Located in a limestone hill, this cave was once used as a hideout by Malaysian freedom fighters. Visitors can explore the cave and see the natural formations, including a river that runs through it.

3. Istana Arau - This palace is the official residence of the Raja of Perlis. Visitors can admire the architecture and learn about the royal family’s history and traditions.

Natural Attractions of Padang Besar: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

1. Wang Kelian State Park - This park is home to several hiking trails that lead to scenic views of the surrounding hills and forests. Visitors can also see the Wang Kelian Market, one of the largest markets in the region.

2. Bukit Ayer State Park - This park is known for its limestone formations and caves, including the Elephant Cave and the Tiger Cave. Visitors can also go on a jungle trek or bird watching tour.

3. Padang Besar Lake - This lake is a popular spot for fishing and picnicking. Visitors can rent boats or kayaks to explore the lake and its surroundings.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Padang Besar

Padang Besar has several hiking and biking routes for outdoor enthusiasts to explore. Some popular routes include:

  • Wang Kelian to Bukit Hijau trail
  • Bukit Ayer to Bukit Kubu trail
  • Padang Besar to Kaki Bukit trail

Best Things to Do in Padang Besar: Activities and Tours

Padang Besar offers many fun and exciting activities for visitors. Here are some of the best things to do:

  • Visit the local night market, where you can try delicious Malaysian street food and buy souvenirs.
  • Take a river cruise and explore the local flora and fauna.
  • Join a cultural tour and learn about the local traditions and history.
  • Attend a local festival or event, such as the Harvest Festival or the Merdeka Day celebrations.

Food and Drink Scene of Padang Besar

One of the best things about Padang Besar is its delicious local cuisine. Here are some must-try dishes and beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Nasi Kandar - A popular Malaysian rice dish served with a variety of curries and side dishes.
  • Satay - Grilled skewers of meat served with peanut sauce.
  • Laksa - A spicy noodle soup served with seafood or meat.

Local Beverages

  • Teh Tarik - A sweet milk tea that is popular throughout Malaysia.
  • Sirap Bandung - A refreshing drink made with rose syrup and milk.
  • Dragonfruit Juice - A healthy and refreshing juice made from locally grown dragonfruits.

Where to Stay in Padang Besar: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Padang Besar has several options for accommodation, ranging from budget hostels to luxury hotels. Here are some recommendations:

  • Hotel Seri Malaysia Perlis - A comfortable hotel located in the town center, with easy access to transportation and attractions.
  • BP Guesthouse - A budget-friendly guesthouse located near the train station and the night market.
  • Sutra Beach Resort Perlis - A beachfront resort located 30 minutes away from Padang Besar, with beautiful views of the sea and the sunset.

What to Buy in Padang Besar: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Padang Besar is a great place to buy souvenirs and local products. Here are some recommendations:

  • Batik fabric - Elegant and colorful fabrics that are a signature of Malaysian craftsmanship.
  • Traditional snacks - Locally made snacks, such as kuih and dodol, are delicious and make great gifts.
  • Handicrafts - Perlis is known for its intricately designed woodcarvings, pottery, and silverware.

Local Culture and Customs of Padang Besar

The people of Padang Besar are friendly and welcoming. It is important to respect the local customs and traditions, such as dressing modestly and removing shoes before entering homes and places of worship.

Best Time to Visit Padang Besar

The best time to visit Padang Besar is from November to March when the weather is dry and cool. The town is also bustling with festivals and events during this time.

Do’s and Don’ts of Padang Besar

Here are some do’s and don’ts to keep in mind when visiting Padang Besar:

Do:

  • Dress modestly and respectfully, especially when visiting religious sites.
  • Try the local food and drink, and immerse yourself in the local culture.
  • Respect the wildlife and natural surroundings when going on outdoor adventures.

Don’t:

  • Engage in disrespectful or illegal activities, such as littering or vandalism.
  • Ignore local customs and traditions.
  • Be disrespectful towards the local people or their beliefs.

Safety and Health

Safety in Padang Besar

Padang Besar is a safe town for visitors. However, it is important to take precautions and practice common sense, such as keeping valuables out of sight and staying in well-lit areas at night.

Health in Padang Besar

Tourists should bring their own medications and consult with a doctor before traveling to Padang Besar. It is also important to stay hydrated and protect yourself from the sun and insects when going on outdoor adventures.

Getting to Padang Besar: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Padang Besar

The closest airport to Padang Besar is the Sultan Abdul Halim Airport, located in Alor Setar, about an hour’s drive away.

Bus Stations Near Padang Besar

Padang Besar has a bus station that connects it to neighboring towns and cities. Travelers can also take a bus from Kuala Lumpur or Penang to Padang Besar.

Other Options

Visitors can also take a train from Kuala Lumpur or Bangkok to Padang Besar. The town is a major transportation hub for the northern region of Malaysia.

How to Get Around in Padang Besar

The easiest way to get around Padang Besar is by car or motorbike. Visitors can also hire a taxi or use a ride-hailing service like Grab. For those who prefer to walk, the town center is small and easily navigable.

Where to Go Next from Padang Besar: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

There are several nearby destinations worth exploring from Padang Besar, including:

  • Langkawi Island - A popular tourist destination known for its beaches, mangrove forests, and duty-free shopping.
  • Perlis State Park - A nature reserve with hiking trails and scenic views of the surrounding hills and forests.
  • Songkhla, Thailand - A bustling city known for its cultural attractions, beaches, and delicious food.

Conclusion

Padang Besar is a charming town that offers a blend of cultural attractions, outdoor adventures, and delicious food. With this comprehensive travel guide, visitors can plan their itinerary and make the most out of their trip to this hidden gem of Malaysia’s West Coast.
