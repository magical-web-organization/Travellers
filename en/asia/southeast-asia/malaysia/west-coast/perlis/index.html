Introduction to Perlis

Perlis is a state located in West Coast Malaysia, bordering Thailand. It is the smallest state in Malaysia, both in terms of land area and population, but it is packed with scenic views, outdoor adventures, and cultural experiences. Perlis offers visitors a diverse range of attractions, from scenic rice paddies and limestone hills to museums and historical landmarks. In this comprehensive travel guide, we will explore the top attractions, activities, cultural experiences, food and drink scenes, where to stay, what to buy, local culture and customs, and safety tips for your next trip to Perlis.

List of Places to Visit in Perlis

Perlis has something for everyone, from outdoor enthusiasts to history buffs. Here’s a list of the top attractions to visit in Perlis:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Kota Kayang Museum: This museum is housed in the former palace of the Raja of Perlis, showcasing the state’s royal history and culture.
  • Gua Kelam: A limestone cave that houses a historical tin mine that dates back to the 19th century.
  • Kuala Perlis Ferry Terminal: Embark on a ferry trip to Langkawi Island for a day trip.
  • Masjid Alwi: A mosque with a unique architecture design.
  • Al-Hussain Mosque: A mosque reminiscent of Middle Eastern architecture.

Natural Attractions of Perlis: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Bukit Kubu Forest Reserve: A beautiful forest reserve with several hiking options for all levels of hikers.
  • Perlis State Park: Known for its scenic views of limestone hills and rice paddies.
  • Wang Kelian State Park: A verdant and peaceful park with several trails to catch a glimpse of the diversity of flora and fauna.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Perlis

Perlis offers some of the best hiking and biking trails in Malaysia. Here are some of the best routes:

  • Gua Kelam Recreational Park: This park offers a 1.5-kilometer hiking trail that takes you through the historical tin mine and a limestone cave.
  • Bukit Ayer Recreational Park: The park offers a 1.5-kilometer hiking trail that takes you through a forest trail and uphill to a scenic view of the surrounding landscape.
  • Perlis State Park Trek: A 5-kilometer hiking trail that takes you through scenic limestone hills of Perlis.

Best Things to Do in Perlis: Activities and Tours

Perlis offers visitors a range of exciting activities and tours. Here are some of the best:

  • Take a ferry ride to Langkawi Island: A day trip to Langkawi can be easily arranged from Kuala Perlis.
  • Explore Gua Kelam: This limestone cave offers a historical dimly lit walk through narrow pathways and tunnels.
  • Visit Wang Kelian Market: A popular market situated near the border of Thailand where you can buy cheap goods.
  • Spend a day at Perlis State Park: This park offers various activities such as camping, trekking, and picnic activities.
  • Visit agricultural farms: Perlis is known for its rice paddies and fruit orchards such as mango, rambutan, durian, and more.

Week-Long Itinerary for Perlis

Day 1: Arrival in Perlis and check-in to your accommodation. Spend the rest of your day relaxing and exploring the town.

Day 2: Visit Kota Kayang Museum and Al-Hussain Mosque.

Day 3: Spend the day at Bukit Kubu Forest Reserve. Choose from one of the hiking trails according to your fitness level.

Day 4: Explore Gua Kelam and visit the tin mine.

Day 5: Take a ferry ride to Langkawi Island for a day trip.

Day 6: Spend a day at Perlis State Park, and enjoy a picnic or camping activity.

Day 7: Visit Wang Kelian Market and do souvenir shopping. Departure from Perlis.

Food and Drink Scene of Perlis

Perlis is known for its traditional food and unique drinks. Here are some dishes you must try:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Laksa Perlis: A spicy noodle soup with a local twist.
  • Nasi Kandar: A rice dish with a variety of meats and curries.
  • Roti Canai: A light and fluffy bread you can enjoy with curry or sweet syrup.

Local Beverages

  • Sirap Bandung: A refreshing drink made with rose syrup, milk, and ice.
  • Teh Tarik: A lightly sweetened milk tea that is one of Malaysia’s most popular drinks.

Where to Stay in Perlis: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Perlis offers several accommodations options for visitors ranging from budget-friendly hostels to luxurious hotels. Here are some of the best accommodations in Perlis:

  • Seri Malaysia Hotel Perlis: A reputable budget hotel in the heart of Kangar.
  • The Putra Regency Hotel: A three-star hotel located in Arau, close to Bukit Kubu Recreational Park.
  • Azahari Homestay: A homestay that offers clean rooms and is situated near several local attractions

What to Buy in Perlis: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Here are some souvenirs and local products to buy when visiting Perlis:

  • Songket Fabric: A traditional handwoven fabric that reflects the state’s unique culture and heritage.
  • Handmade crafts: Perlis has a thriving handicraft industry, including woodcarving and pottery.
  • Local food products: Buy some local fruits such as mangoes, durians and nuts.

Local Culture and Customs of Perlis

Perlis, like the rest of Malaysia, is a Muslim state with deep cultural roots and traditions. Visitors should be respectful of the local customs, such as modest dress and removing shoes before entering a mosque. It’s also advisable to refrain from public displays of affection.

Best Time to Visit Perlis

The best time to visit Perlis is from November to February when the weather is mild. However, it may be crowded during holidays, festivals and school holidays.

Do’s and Don’ts of Perlis

Here are some do’s and don’ts to keep in mind when visiting Perlis:

Do:

  • Remove your shoes before entering a mosque.
  • Dress modestly, especially when visiting a mosque.
  • Be respectful of local customs and traditions.

Don’t:

  • Engage in public displays of affection, as it is frowned upon in Malaysia.
  • Wear revealing clothing in public areas.
  • Drink alcohol or smoke in public areas.

Safety and Health

Perlis is a relatively safe state with low crime rates. However, visitors should take the usual precautions to avoid petty theft, such as keeping their valuables safe and not displaying them in public.

Safety in Perlis

  • Don’t walk alone at night, especially in quiet areas or empty streets.
  • Avoid carrying large sums of money or valuables.

Health in Perlis

Perlis has several primary care clinics and a government hospital, in line with other states in Malaysia.

Getting to Perlis: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Perlis

  • Alor Setar International Airport: The closest airport to Perlis, 45 minutes by car.

Bus Stations Near Perlis

  • Kangar Bus Station.
  • Kuala Perlis Bus Station.

Other Options

  • Ferry ride from Langkawi Island to Kuala Perlis Terminal.

How to Get Around in Perlis

Bus

  • Local buses are the most popular mode of transportation for visitors to Perlis.
  • Taxis are also available, but they can be expensive.

Car

  • Car rentals are available in Kuala Lumpur and Alor Setar.
  • Cars can be rented for RM50 to RM100 per day.

Where to Go Next from Perlis: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

If you’re looking to extend your trip beyond Perlis, here are some nearby destinations worth exploring:

  • Langkawi Island: A beautiful island known for its beaches, resorts, and cable car ride. It can be reached via a ferry ride from Kuala Perlis Terminal.
  • Kedah: Known for its traditional Malay villages, paddy fields, and industrial development. It is located south of Perlis.
  • Penang: A popular tourist destination known for its cultural and historical landmarks, street art, and food scene. It is located south of Perlis.

Conclusion

Perlis may be small, but it packs a punch when it comes to natural beauty, cultural attractions, and outdoor adventures. Whether you’re a history buff or an outdoor enthusiast, there’s something for everyone in Perlis. This comprehensive travel guide serves as an informative guide that will help you plan your trip to Perlis and make the most of your time there. So pack your bags and get ready for an unforgettable trip to this charming state of Malaysia.
