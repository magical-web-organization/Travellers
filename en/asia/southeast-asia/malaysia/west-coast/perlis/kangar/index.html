Introduction to Kangar

Perched on the northernmost part of Perlis, Kangar is a small yet bustling town that offers a sneak peek into Malaysia’s rural lifestyle. The town is fringed by verdant stretches of paddy fields, quaint villages, and stretches of mesmerizing beaches in the nearby region of Kuala Perlis. Kangar is a perfect getaway destination, away from the hustle and bustle of city life. The town is a melting pot of cultures, and its people are warm and welcoming.

List of Places to Visit in Kangar

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

1. Kota Kayang Museum

Kota Kayang Museum is a piece of living history in Kangar. The museum is housed in a 200-year-old fort that was once home to the Perlis royalty. The museum features a collection of ancient artifacts, including traditional weapons, jewelry, and ceramics, that depict the history and culture of the Perlis province.

2. Alwi Mosque

The Alwi Mosque is one of the oldest mosques in Kangar. The mosque’s unique architectural style makes it a significant landmark in Kangar. Alwi Mosque has a peaceful atmosphere, making it a perfect place to meditate and reflect.

Natural Attractions of Kangar: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

1. Kuala Perlis Jetty

Kuala Perlis Jetty offers sweeping views of the Andaman sea and the beautiful Perlis coastline. The Jetty is the main port that connects Perlis and Langkawi Island, making it a popular spot for fishing, cruising, and diving.

2. Perlis State Park

Perlis State Park is a serene natural reserve that consists of verdant forests, raging rivers, and a range of rare flora and fauna. The park is an ideal spot for hiking, picnics, and bird watching.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Kangar

Kangar is an ideal destination for hiking and biking enthusiasts. The town is surrounded by verdant hills and lush green forests that offer stunning panoramic views of the Andaman sea. Some of the popular routes for hiking and biking include:

  • Wang Gua Damai Trail
  • Bukit Kubu Trail
  • Bukit Air Rebus Trail

Best Things to Do in Kangar: Activities and Tours

There are endless activities and tours that one could enjoy in Kangar. Some of the popular ones include:

  • Island Hopping in Langkawi
  • Exploring the Perlis State Park
  • Visiting the Titiwangsa Lake Gardens

Week-Long Itinerary for Kangar

Day 1: Arrive in Kangar and take a tour of the town’s historical sites.

Day 2: Visit the Perlis State Park and admire the natural beauty of the area.

Day 3: Take a day trip to Langkawi Island and enjoy the white sandy beaches.

Day 4: Enjoy outdoor activities such as hiking or biking in the Bukit Kubu Trail.

Day 5: Visit the famous Paddy Museum in Kuala Perlis and learn about the region’s rice-growing culture.

Day 6: Take a trip on a cruise on Kuala Perlis Jetty and admire the stunning scenery of Perlis province.

Day 7: Shop for souvenirs and enjoy local cuisine before departing from Kangar.

Food and Drink Scene of Kangar

Must-Try Dishes

Kangar’s food scene is a melting pot of cultures, with Indian, Chinese, and Malay cuisine dominating the culinary scene. Some of the must-try dishes include:

  • Nasi Lemak (Coconut Rice)
  • Satay (grilled meat skewers)
  • Kuih-Muih (assorted traditional cakes)
  • Roti Canai (Malay flatbread)

Local Beverages

The people of Kangar love their tea and coffee. One of the best places to enjoy a cup of tea or coffee is in a local Kopitiam. Kopitiams are traditional coffee shops that serve a range of Malaysian traditional beverages such as:

  • Teh Tarik (Pulled Tea)
  • Kopi-O (Strong Black Coffee)

Where to Stay in Kangar: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Kangar offers a range of accommodation options, from budget-friendly hostels to luxury hotels. Some of the popular hotels and guesthouses in Kangar include:

  • Hotel Seri Malaysia Kangar
  • Putra Regency Hotel
  • Jaring @ Residensi 1.6

What to Buy in Kangar: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Kangar is known for its exquisite hand-woven textiles, such as Songket, a traditional Malay fabric. Other popular souvenirs to bring home include:

  • Handmade Pottery
  • Batik Prints
  • Local Spices

Local Culture and Customs of Kangar

The people of Kangar are deeply rooted in their history and customs. Visitors should be mindful of local customs such as removing shoes before entering a mosque or temple and dressing modestly.

Best Time to Visit Kangar

The best time to visit Kangar is from November to February. During these months, the weather is pleasantly cool and dry, making it ideal for outdoor activities.

Do’s and Don’ts of Kangar

Do:

  • Respect local customs and traditions
  • Bargain when buying items from the local markets
  • Try local cuisine

Don’t:

  • Disrespect religious sites and practices
  • Wear revealing clothing

Safety and Health

Safety in Kangar

Kangar is a relatively safe destination. However, visitors should be vigilant and aware of their surroundings during their travels. It is always a good practice to keep your valuable items in a secure place.

Health in Kangar

Visitors should take the necessary precautions, including staying hydrated and applying sunscreen to avoid sunburns. It is advisable to drink bottled water to avoid water-borne diseases.

Getting to Kangar: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Kangar

The nearest airport to Kangar is Sultan Abdul Halim Airport, located in Kepala Batas, about 50 kilometers away.

Bus Stations Near Kangar

Kangar is well-connected by buses from various cities across Malaysia. The Kangar bus station is the central hub for buses that operate in and out of the town.

Other Options

Visitors can also rent cars or use ride-hailing services such as Grab to get around Kangar.

How to Get Around in Kangar

Visitors can use taxis or book a car rental service to get around Kangar. Some of the popular taxi services include MyCar and Grab.

Where to Go Next from Kangar: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Perlis province is surrounded by a string of enchanting destinations that offer a range of excursions for visitors. Some of the popular destinations include:

  • Langkawi Island
  • Penang Island
  • Taiping Lake Gardens

Conclusion

Kangar is a hidden gem of Perlis province, packed with an abundance of natural beauty, culture, and history. If you are looking to explore the rustic charm of Malaysia, Kangar should be on your itinerary. Use this comprehensive guide to plan your trip to Kangar and discover all that the town has to offer.
