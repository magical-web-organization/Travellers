Arau is a charming town in the state of Perlis, located in West Coast Malaysia. Known for its historical significance and natural beauty, Arau offers visitors a range of exciting attractions and activities. This comprehensive tour guide will provide you with all the information you need to plan your trip to Arau, including the best things to do, places to visit, where to stay, what to eat, local culture and customs, safety tips, transportation options and nearby destinations.

Introduction to Arau

Arau is the royal town of Perlis, and it is home to the Istana Arau, the residence of the Raja of Perlis. The town is located in the northwest part of Malaysia, close to the border with Thailand. One of the main attractions of the town is the Masjid Negeri Arau, a grand mosque that dominates the landscape. The town also has a thriving tourist industry, with numerous attractions and activities that cater to visitors from all over the world.

List of Places to Visit in Arau

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Istana Arau: This grand palace is the residence of the Raja of Perlis and is an iconic landmark in the town. Visitors can view the exterior of the palace, which boasts a unique blend of traditional Malay and European architecture, and learn about the history of the royal family.
  • Galeri Sultan Abdul Halim Mu’adzam Shah: This museum showcases the history and culture of Perlis and the surrounding regions. Exhibits include traditional musical instruments, costumes and artifacts, as well as photographs and artworks that illuminate the town’s history.

Natural Attractions of Arau: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Gua Kelam Recreational Park: This park is a popular destination for nature lovers, with scenic trails that wind through the limestone hills and along the river. Visitors can explore the fascinating cave system and admire the impressive rock formations.
  • Perlis State Park: This protected area is home to a diverse range of flora and fauna, as well as stunning natural landscapes. Visitors can enjoy hiking trails, birdwatching, camping and other activities while taking in the natural beauty of the region.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Arau

Arau is a great place for outdoor enthusiasts, with many hiking and biking trails to explore. Some popular routes include:

  • Bukit Buluh: This hill is located on the outskirts of town and offers a challenging uphill hike. The reward is stunning views of the surrounding countryside and a peaceful forested area at the top.
  • Gua Kelam Recreational Park: The park also has a number of hiking trails that wind through the hills and along the river. Visitors can take a leisurely stroll or a more challenging hike, depending on their preference.

Best Things to Do in Arau: Activities and Tours

  • River Cruise: Take a leisurely boat ride along the river and enjoy the scenic views of the surrounding countryside.
  • Cultural Tour: Learn about the history and customs of the town with a guided tour of the main attractions and landmarks.
  • Food Tour: Indulge in the local delicacies and street food with a culinary tour of the town.

Week-Long Itinerary for Arau

Day 1: Arrive in Arau and check into your hotel.

Day 2: Visit Istana Arau and Galeri Sultan Abdul Halim Mu’adzam Shah.

Day 3: Explore Gua Kelam Recreational Park and enjoy a river cruise.

Day 4: Take a cultural tour of the town and enjoy some local cuisine.

Day 5: Hike up Bukit Buluh and take in the stunning views of the countryside.

Day 6: Visit Perlis State Park and enjoy outdoor activities.

Day 7: Depart from Arau.

Food and Drink Scene of Arau

Must-Try Dishes

  • Laksa Perlis: A spicy noodle soup with fish, herbs, and vegetables, this dish is a local specialty.
  • Nasi Kandar: A Malaysian Indian dish consisting of steamed rice and a selection of curries and side dishes.

Local Beverages

  • Teh Tarik: A hot or cold milk tea that is frothed and mixed by tapping it between two containers.
  • Air Bandung: A refreshing drink made from rose syrup and milk.

Where to Stay in Arau: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Hotel Seri Malaysia Arau: A budget-friendly hotel with comfortable rooms and a convenient location.
  • Perlis Wellness Resort: A luxury resort with spa facilities and stunning views of the surrounding hills.

What to Buy in Arau: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Songket: A traditional Malay cloth made from silk or cotton, often woven with gold threads.
  • Handicrafts: Perlis is known for its skilled artisans who produce beautiful wood carvings and other handicrafts.

Local Culture and Customs of Arau

As a predominantly Muslim town, visitors are advised to dress modestly and respect local customs and traditions. Alcohol is not widely available, and visitors should refrain from consuming it in public.

Best Time to Visit Arau

The best time to visit Arau is between November and March when the weather is cool and dry, and the scenery is at its most beautiful.

Do’s and Don’ts of Arau

Do:

  • Dress modestly and respect local customs.
  • Try the local cuisine and street food.
  • Take a cultural tour to learn more about the town’s history.

Don’t:

  • Drink alcohol in public.
  • Be disrespectful to the local culture and customs.
  • Ignore safety precautions when taking part in outdoor activities.

Safety and Health

Safety in Arau

Arau is a safe town, but visitors should take precautions when traveling alone at night or in isolated areas. Follow standard safety guidelines, such as locking doors and windows, and keeping valuables out of sight.

Health in Arau

There are no major health risks in Arau, but visitors should take precautions against mosquito-borne diseases and drink bottled water.

Getting to Arau: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Arau

The nearest airport to Arau is the Sultan Abdul Halim Airport in Alor Setar, about an hour’s drive away.

Bus Stations Near Arau

Arau has a bus terminal that provides services to other towns and cities in the area.

Other Options

Visitors can travel to Arau by train, with stops at nearby stations in Kangar and Kuala Perlis.

How to Get Around in Arau

Arau is a small town that can easily be explored on foot or by bicycle. Taxis and buses are also available for longer journeys.

Where to Go Next from Arau: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Langkawi: A popular tourist destination with stunning beaches and a relaxed island vibe.
  • Alor Setar: The capital city of Kedah, just a short drive from Arau.

Conclusion

Arau is a delightful town with a blend of historical and cultural attractions, natural beauty, delicious food, and friendly locals. Whatever your interests may be, there is something for everyone in this charming town. We hope this comprehensive tour guide has helped you plan your trip to Arau and given you a taste of the many experiences and adventures that await you.
