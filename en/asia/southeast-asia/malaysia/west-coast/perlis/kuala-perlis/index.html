Kuala Perlis, or “Royal Capital of Perlis,” is a small town located in the northernmost state of Malaysia. Bordering Thailand on the west coast, this town boasts a culturally rich heritage and natural beauty that is waiting to be explored. In this comprehensive travel guide, we’ll take you through the best that Kuala Perlis has to offer.

Introduction to Kuala Perlis

Located in the north of Peninsular Malaysia, Kuala Perlis is the gateway to the state of Perlis and other nearby attractions such as the tropical islands of Langkawi. As a significant trading port and fishing village in the past, Kuala Perlis has evolved into a charming town featuring a mix of modern developments and traditional Malay cultural heritage. One of the most notable features of Kuala Perlis is its scenic waterfront, lined with small boats and restaurants serving fresh seafood.

List of Places to Visit in Kuala Perlis

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Kuala Perlis is home to many cultural and historical sites that shed light on the town’s rich heritage, such as:

  1. Masjid Alwi: Known as Perlis’ highest Islamic architecture with golden domes that symbolize the king’s sovereignty.

  2. Kota Kayang Museum: An excellent place to learn about the history and culture of Perlis and its early days of the Malay Kingdom.

Natural Attractions of Kuala Perlis: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Kuala Perlis may be a small town, but it’s packed with stunning natural beauty, including:

  1. Kuala Perlis Waterfront: An excellent spot to enjoy gorgeous views of the sunset and the sea.

  2. Herbal Park: A beautiful garden consisting of more than 800 species of herbs.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Kuala Perlis

Kuala Perlis is surrounded by beautiful mountains and countryside, making it an ideal destination for nature lovers. Some of the popular hiking and biking routes in Kuala Perlis include:

  1. Perlis State Park: Home to various exciting trails that offer scenic views of the surrounding landscape, including Bukit Kubu Trail and Padang Bulateh Trail.

  2. Bintong Recreational Forest: This beautiful forest reserve is perfect for hiking, camping, and picnicking.

Best Things to Do in Kuala Perlis: Activities and Tours

Kuala Perlis offers a range of activities and tours for visitors to enjoy, such as:

  1. Fisherman Village Tour: A glimpse into the traditional way of life of the local fishermen.

  2. Kayaking Tours: An exciting way to explore the mangrove forests and waterways around Kuala Perlis.

Week-Long Itinerary for Kuala Perlis

Day 1: Visit Masjid Alwi, explore the town’s fishing village, and enjoy fresh seafood at the waterfront. Day 2: Head to Perlis State Park and hike Bukit Kubu Trail or Padang Bulateh Trail. Day 3: Check out Bintong Recreational Forest and enjoy a picnic by the beautiful waterfall. Day 4: Take a kayaking tour of the mangrove forests and waterways around Kuala Perlis. Day 5: Visit Kota Kayang Museum and learn about the history and culture of Perlis. Day 6: Visit the Herbal Park and learn about the various herbs grown in the garden. Day 7: Relax and unwind at the Kuala Perlis Waterfront and watch the sunset.

Food and Drink Scene of Kuala Perlis

Kuala Perlis may be small, but it has a vibrant food and drink scene. Some must-try dishes include:

Must-Try Dishes

  1. Ikan Bakar: Grilled fish usually served with spicy sauce, rice, and vegetables.

  2. Laksa Perlis: A spicy noodle dish with fish broth.

Local Beverages

  1. Teh Tarik: A sweet and creamy tea that is popular throughout Malaysia.

  2. Air Kelapa: Fresh coconut juice served in the husk.

Where to Stay in Kuala Perlis: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Kuala Perlis offers a range of accommodation options, from luxury hotels to budget-friendly guesthouses. Some of the most popular options include:

  1. Putra Brasmana Hotel
  2. Seri Malaysia Hotel Kuala Perlis
  3. Homestay Kuala Perlis

What to Buy in Kuala Perlis: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Kuala Perlis is known for its unique handicrafts and local products, such as:

  1. Songket: A traditional Malay fabric handwoven using gold or silver threads.

  2. Keropok: A type of snack made from fish or prawn crackers.

Local Culture and Customs of Kuala Perlis

Kuala Perlis has a rich cultural heritage that is heavily influenced by its Malay roots. Here are some things to keep in mind when visiting:

  1. Dress modestly, particularly when visiting religious sites.

  2. Remove your shoes when entering a mosque or someone’s home.

Best Time to Visit Kuala Perlis

The best time to visit Kuala Perlis is between October and March, when the weather is typically cool and dry. However, Kuala Perlis has a pleasant tropical climate year-round and can be visited any time of the year.

Do’s and Don’ts of Kuala Perlis

Do:

  1. Try the local dishes and beverages.

  2. Respect local customs and traditions.

Don’t:

  1. Ignore dress codes when visiting religious sites.

  2. Drift away from your group when hiking or exploring the town at night.

Safety and Health

Safety in Kuala Perlis

Kuala Perlis is a generally safe town. However, visitors should take standard precautions, such as not carrying large amounts of cash, avoiding deserted areas at night, and keeping a close eye on their belongings.

Health in Kuala Perlis

Kuala Perlis has excellent healthcare facilities, and visitors can find pharmacies and clinics throughout town.

Getting to Kuala Perlis: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Kuala Perlis

The closest airport to Kuala Perlis is Langkawi International Airport, which is approximately 45 minutes away by car.

Bus Stations Near Kuala Perlis

Visitors can take a bus to Kuala Perlis from various locations, including Kuala Lumpur and Penang.

Other Options

Visitors can also rent a car or take a taxi to Kuala Perlis.

How to Get Around in Kuala Perlis

Kuala Perlis is a small town that is easily navigable on foot. However, visitors can also take a taxi or rent a car to get around.

Where to Go Next from Kuala Perlis: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Kuala Perlis is an excellent base for exploring the nearby attractions, such as:

  1. Langkawi: A tropical paradise featuring beautiful beaches, water sports, and nature reserves.

  2. Satun, Thailand: A picturesque town on the border of Malaysia and Thailand, featuring a range of cultural sites.

Conclusion

Kuala Perlis may be a small town, but it packs a cultural and natural punch. From its rich history to its beautiful landscapes and adventurous activities, there’s something for everyone in this charming Malaysian town. So, what are you waiting for? Pack your bags and start exploring Kuala Perlis today!
