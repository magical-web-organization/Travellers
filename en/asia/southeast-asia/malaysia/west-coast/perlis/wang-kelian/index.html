Introduction to Wang Kelian

Wang Kelian is a small town situated in the Perlis state of Western Malaysia. This little-known gem of a place offers visitors an opportunity to explore the rich cultural heritage and natural beauty of Southeast Asia. Wang Kelian is famed for its outdoor adventures and hiking trails, and the stunning scenic views will leave you awestruck. The town is popular among tourists who are looking for a break from the hustle of city life, seeking to rediscover themselves surrounded by pristine beauty and tranquility.

List of Places to Visit in Wang Kelian

Wang Kelian is full of attractions for visitors of diverse interests. The town’s attractions can be broadly divided into two categories – culturally significant and natural.

Culturally Important Attractions

Museums and Historical Places

There are several museums and historical places worth visiting in and around Wang Kelian. These include the Lembah Bujang Archaeological Museum, the Alor Setar Tower, the Pekan Rabu Market, and the Kota Kuala Kedah Fort. Walk through the museums and historical places, each of which preserves a unique strand of Malay history and culture.

Natural Attractions of Wang Kelian

Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Wang Kelian offers an array of options for outdoor enthusiasts. The town is home to Gunung Jerai, a magnificent mountain offering stunning panoramic views of the surrounding area. It is a popular hiking spot throughout the year. The Wang Kelian State Park houses numerous forests and waterfalls that you can explore. You can also enjoy a leisurely trek to the Guan Yin Temple, located on a mountaintop, which offers awe-inspiring views of the town below. There are several smaller hills surrounding Wang Kelian that offer trekking adventures.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Wang Kelian

Wang Kelian is a hiker’s paradise, with beautiful trails that are a mix of easy, moderate, and difficult. Some of the best routes include the Jerai Forest Reserve, the Terap Eco Park, the Pasir Panjang Recreational Park, and the Mount Ledang. These areas are perfect for all levels of hikers and bikers, with trails well-marked and ready for exploration.

Best Things to Do in Wang Kelian: Activities and Tours

The attractions and activities in Wang Kelian might be plentiful, but the tours offered in the town are outstanding. Visitors can take part in a plethora of adventures such as exploring the UNESCO World Heritage site at Gunong Mulu National Park, river tubing, cycling, exploring the 400-year-old historical center of Melaka, and much more.

Week-Long Itinerary for Wang Kelian

Visitors looking for an itinerary that explores Wang Kelian’s highlights can expect to spend at least one week in the town. Check out the two-day outdoor adventure tour, the three-day cultural exploration tour, and the four-day tour of the best trekking trails in the region. With these tours, you can get to know the true essence of Wang Kelian, and take home memories that will last a lifetime.

Food and Drink Scene of Wang Kelian

No matter where you go in Malaysia, the food doesn’t disappoint. Wang Kelian is no exception. Malaysian cuisine is an amalgamation of Indian, Chinese and indigenous cultures. Wang Kelian offers visitors a range of delicious authentic dishes that must be tried.

Must-Try Dishes

Some of the dishes you should sample include Nasi Lemak, a traditional Malay dish made from rice cooked in coconut milk, mixed with chili sauce, fried crispy anchovies, and fried egg. Another dish is Satay, marinated, grilled meat skewers served with a spicy peanut sauce.

Local Beverages

Explore the town’s beverage scene and try some authentic local drinks such as Teh Tarik (pulled tea) and Air Mata Kucing (a fruit drink made from Chinese longan fruit). These are two popular drinks among the locals.

Where to Stay in Wang Kelian: Hotels, Hostels, and Guest Houses

Wang Kelian offers a range of hotels, hostels, and guesthouses catering to varying budgets. Visitors can choose from a wide range of accommodation options, from boutique hotels to budget guesthouses. Some of the best places to stay in Wang Kelian include the Putra Palace Hotel, Anjung Warn (a budget-friendly hostel), and Arivina Homestay.

What to Buy in Wang Kelian: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Wang Kelian’s market is where you’ll find a range of items to take home as souvenirs. You can find intricate Malaysian handicrafts, handwoven fabrics, pottery, and a range of delicious snacks to munch on while you explore the town. Be sure to check out the night markets to get your hands on some of the best souvenirs.

Local Culture and Customs of Wang Kelian

Malaysia is a multi-ethnic nation, and Wang Kelian reflects this diversity. Visitors should expect to encounter a blend of Indian, Chinese, and Malay cultures with cultures and traditions of indigenous peoples. Show respect for each other’s traditions, learn something new about other cultures, and you will leave with a richer experience.

Best Time to Visit Wang Kelian

The best time to visit Wang Kelian is between the months of November and February when the weather is dry and pleasant. The town becomes crowded from April to October, and it is still enjoyable; however, accommodation rates tend to go up, and outdoor activities are uncomfortable in the heat.

Do’s and Don’ts of Wang Kelian

Do:

  • Respect the local customs and traditions.
  • Wear conservative clothing while visiting temples and religious sites.

Don’t:

  • Do not smoke in public places.
  • Do not litter or disturb the natural environment.

Safety and Health

Safety in Wang Kelian

Wang Kelian is generally a safe town, with low levels of crime. Follow standard precautions and be aware of your surroundings while travelling around the town.

Health in Wang Kelian

Wang Kelian is a relatively healthy place to visit, with no major health concerns. Nonetheless, visitors should drink bottled water and avoid tap water, and always practice safe hygiene measures to reduce the risk of illness.

Getting to Wang Kelian: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Wang Kelian

The nearest airports to Wang Kelian are the Sultan Abdul Halim Airport in Alor Setar and the Penang International Airport. From these airports, you can take a taxi or rent a car to reach Wang Kelian.

Bus Stations Near Wang Kelian

Wang Kelian has a small bus station that connects to nearby towns and cities, such as Kangar, Arau, and Alor Setar.

Other Options

Visitors can also consider hiring a private driver or rent a car to explore the town and its surrounds comfortably.

How to Get Around Wang Kelian

The best way to get around Wang Kelian is by renting a car or scooter to explore the town. Visitors can also use public transport in the form of taxis or buses, but they aren’t as convenient.

Where to Go Next from Wang Kelian: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

From Wang Kelian, visitors can explore the nearby cities such as Alor Setar, the capital city of Perlis, Kuala Lumpur, and Penang. Visitors can explore the historic center of Melaka (a UNESCO World Heritage Site), or the beautiful Cameron Highlands.

Conclusion

Wang Kelian is simply a must-visit destination in Malaysia. From outdoor adventures and natural beauty to its delightful food scene and traditional cultures and customs, the town has everything you need to immerse yourself in the beauty of Southeast Asia. The next time you’re planning your travels in Malaysia, make sure to put Wang Kelian on your itinerary.
