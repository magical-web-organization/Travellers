Introduction to Puchong

Puchong is a bustling suburban town in the Selangor state of Malaysia. Located in the West Coast region of the country, it is known for its delicious food, vibrant culture, and a wide array of outdoor activities. Puchong is just a 25-minute drive from Kuala Lumpur, making it a popular tourist destination.

If you are looking for a place to visit that offers a perfect mix of urban and rural experiences, Puchong might just be the destination of your dreams. This travel guide will highlight the top attractions, food and drink scenes, accommodations, and transportation options of the region.

Local Culture and Customs of Puchong

The culture in Puchong is diverse, with Malay, Chinese, and Indian influences. Respect for elders and customs is key to the region’s culture, so make sure you familiarize yourself with them before your visit.

Best Time to Visit Puchong

Puchong has a tropical climate with high temperatures and humidity year-round. The best time to visit is during the dry season, which runs between June to August and December to February.

Do’s and Don’ts of Puchong

Do:

  1. Respect local culture and traditions
  2. Bargain when shopping at street markets
  3. Carry an umbrella or light jacket due to unpredictable weather

Don’t:

  1. Wear revealing clothing in public places
  2. Disrespect religious sites and traditions
  3. Ignore traffic rules and regulations

Safety and Health

Safety in Puchong

Puchong is a relatively safe place to visit, but it is always recommended to stay cautious and take safety precautions.

Health in Puchong

Malaysia is known for its spicy and flavorful food, so make sure you know your tolerance levels before trying it. The most common health issue among travelers to the region is food poisoning, so make sure to only eat at reputable food establishments.
