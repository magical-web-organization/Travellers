Introduction to Pulau Ketam

Pulau Ketam is a small island in the state of Selangor, Malaysia. It is a fishing village, and its name means “crab island” in Malay. The island is only accessible by boat, and there are no roads or cars on Pulau Ketam, making it a peaceful and unique destination.

List of Places to Visit in Pulau Ketam

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

There are no museums on Pulau Ketam, but visitors can explore some of the island’s historical landmarks and cultural sites. The Tin Hau Temple is the oldest temple on the island and is a popular attraction for both locals and visitors. The temple is dedicated to Ma Zu, the Chinese goddess of the sea, and is a place of worship for the island’s fishermen. Other historical landmarks include the Toong Fara and Chee Yok Teng temples, which have interesting traditional Chinese architecture.

Natural Attractions of Pulau Ketam: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Pulau Ketam is known for its beautiful mangrove forests, which provide a habitat for a wide range of unique wildlife. Visitors can take a boat tour to explore the forests and observe animals such as monkeys, mudskippers, and otters. The island also has several beautiful beaches, including Tai Bak and Taman Pesisir Pantai, which are perfect for relaxation and swimming. Another popular activity on the island is crabbing, where visitors can catch crabs in the muddy mangrove forests.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Pulau Ketam

Biking is a popular activity on Pulau Ketam, as there are no cars on the island, making it a peaceful and safe place to explore on two wheels. There are several cycling routes on the island, including the scenic coastal road that provides beautiful views of the island’s beaches and mangrove forests.

Best Things to Do in Pulau Ketam: Activities and Tours

Besides visiting cultural landmarks and engaging in outdoor activities, visitors can also take part in several tours, including fishing tours, boat tours, and even a seafood tour. The seafood tour is especially popular, as the island is famous for its fresh seafood dishes. Visitors can sample local delicacies such as crab, prawns, and fish in various restaurants and food stalls around the island.

Food and Drink Scene of Pulau Ketam

Must-Try Dishes

The seafood scene on Pulau Ketam is one of the main attractions. Visitors can try local dishes such as chili crab, butter prawns, seafood noodles, and fish dishes. Many restaurants also offer traditional Chinese dim sum dishes, such as steamed dumplings and buns.

Local Beverages

Visitors can try local drinks such as coconut water, which is available at various street stalls and juice bars around the island. Other drinks include Chinese tea and coffee, which can be enjoyed in the island’s cafes and restaurants.

Where to Stay in Pulau Ketam: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Visitors can choose from a wide range of accommodation options on Pulau Ketam, including budget-friendly hostels, guesthouses, and mid-range hotels. Some popular choices include the Hotel de Art and the Pulau Ketam Leisure Park.

What to Buy in Pulau Ketam: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Visitors can buy various souvenirs on Pulau Ketam, including traditional handicrafts, such as Chinese lanterns and fans. Local products from the island include seafood snacks, such as dried shrimp and fish, and homemade sweet treats.

Local Culture and Customs of Pulau Ketam

The local culture of Pulau Ketam is heavily influenced by the island’s fishing heritage and traditional Chinese customs. Visitors can also learn about the island’s unique Hokkien dialect, which is a blend of Chinese and Malay languages.

Best Time to Visit Pulau Ketam

The best time to visit Pulau Ketam is during the dry season, which usually runs from June to August. During this time, visitors can enjoy the island’s outdoor activities and beautiful scenery without being affected by rainfall.

Do’s and Don’ts of Pulau Ketam

Do:

  • Respect local customs and traditions
  • Try the local seafood dishes
  • Explore the island’s mangrove forests

Don’t:

  • Litter or damage the island’s natural environment
  • Wear revealing clothing in public places
  • Disrespect cultural and religious landmarks

Safety and Health

Safety in Pulau Ketam

Pulau Ketam is a safe destination, as crime rates are low on the island. However, visitors should still take sensible precautions to protect their belongings.

Health in Pulau Ketam

Visitors should bring mosquito repellent, as mosquitoes are prevalent on the island. The tap water is not drinkable, so visitors should only drink bottled water.

Getting to Pulau Ketam: Transportation Options and Logistics

Visitors can reach Pulau Ketam by taking a ferry from Port Klang, which leaves every 30 minutes. The journey takes around 40 minutes and costs RM7 per person. Visitors can also take a taxi or Grab from Kuala Lumpur, which takes around an hour.

Airports Near Pulau Ketam

The closest airport to Pulau Ketam is Kuala Lumpur International Airport, which is around 50 kilometers away.

Bus Stations Near Pulau Ketam

Visitors can take a bus from Kuala Lumpur to Port Klang, which takes around an hour.

Other Options

Visitors can also reach Pulau Ketam by taking a private boat or chartering a boat from Port Klang.

How to Get Around in Pulau Ketam

Visitors can explore Pulau Ketam on foot, but the island is also ideal for biking, as there are no cars on the island. Visitors can rent bikes at various rental shops around the island.

Where to Go Next from Pulau Ketam: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Visitors can explore nearby destinations such as Kuala Lumpur, which is only around an hour’s drive away. Other nearby attractions include the Batu Caves and Sunway Lagoon.

Conclusion

Pulau Ketam is a unique and peaceful island that offers a wide range of activities and cultural experiences for visitors. Whether you’re interested in exploring the island’s natural beauty, trying local seafood dishes, or learning about the island’s fishing heritage, Pulau Ketam is a destination worth considering for your next holiday.
