Introduction to Balik Pulau

Located on the southwest side of Penang Island, Balik Pulau is a tranquil and traditional town that is often overlooked by tourists. Its name means “back of the island” in Malay, and it is known for its scenic views, rich culture, and delicious food.

Balik Pulau is a great place for those who want to escape the hustle and bustle of city life and experience the charm of a small town in Malaysia.

List of Places to Visit in Balik Pulau

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Balik Pulau has some of the most unique and significant places to visit in Penang, including:

1. Balik Pulau Museum

The Balik Pulau Museum is a must-visit for those interested in the history and culture of the town. It showcases the traditional lifestyle of the locals, from their houses, clothing, and food.

2. Kek Lok Si Temple

Kek Lok Si Temple is the largest Buddhist temple in Southeast Asia. It boasts a stunning architecture and is famous for its statue of Kuan Yin, the goddess of mercy.

3. Penang Hill Railway

Take a scenic ride on the Penang Hill Railway, the oldest funicular railway in Southeast Asia. You’ll be rewarded with breathtaking views of the town and the surrounding areas.

Natural Attractions of Balik Pulau: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Balik Pulau is blessed with beautiful natural attractions that are perfect for outdoor adventures. Here are some of our favorite spots:

1. Teluk Bahang Beach

This secluded beach is the perfect escape from the crowded tourist attractions of Penang. Its clear waters and sandy shores make it a great place to relax and unwind.

2. Bukit Genting Hill

Bukit Genting Hill offers some of the best views of Balik Pulau and the surrounding areas. You can hike up the hill or drive up to the top for a stunning panoramic view.

3. Balik Pulau Fruit Farm

Take a tour of the Balik Pulau Fruit Farm and learn about the fruits that are native to the region. You can also taste some of the freshest and most delicious fruits in Malaysia.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Balik Pulau

Balik Pulau is the perfect place for outdoor enthusiasts who enjoy hiking and biking. Here are some of the most popular trails:

1. Balik Pulau to Teluk Bahang Trail

This scenic trail takes you through the hills of Balik Pulau and ends at the beautiful Teluk Bahang Beach.

2. Balik Pulau to Balik Pulau Dam Trail

This trail takes you to the Balik Pulau Dam, where you can enjoy beautiful views of the surrounding hills and forests.

3. Balik Pulau to Penang Hill Trail

This challenging trail takes you from Balik Pulau to the top of Penang Hill, offering stunning views along the way.

Best Things to Do in Balik Pulau: Activities and Tours

In addition to the natural attractions and outdoor adventures, Balik Pulau offers a variety of activities and tours that are perfect for all kinds of travelers. Here are some of our favorite things to do:

1. Cooking Classes

Learn how to cook traditional Malaysian dishes at one of the many cooking classes available in Balik Pulau. You’ll get to shop for ingredients at a local market and cook with the guidance of a local chef.

2. Fruit Tasting Tours

Take a fruit tasting tour and sample some of the freshest and most delicious fruits in Malaysia. You’ll get to learn about the different varieties and how the locals use them in their cooking.

3. Heritage Tour

Take a heritage tour of Balik Pulau and learn about the history and culture of the town. You’ll get to visit some of the most significant landmarks and buildings in the area.

Week-Long Itinerary for Balik Pulau

Here is a suggested itinerary for a week-long trip to Balik Pulau:

Day 1: Arrival and check-in at your accommodation Day 2: Explore Balik Pulau town and visit the Balik Pulau Museum Day 3: Take a cooking class and learn how to make traditional Malaysian dishes Day 4: Visit the Kek Lok Si Temple and ride the Penang Hill Railway Day 5: Go on a hiking or biking trail in the morning, and relax on Teluk Bahang Beach in the afternoon Day 6: Take a fruit tasting tour and visit the Balik Pulau Fruit Farm Day 7: Departure and check-out

Food and Drink Scene of Balik Pulau

Balik Pulau is famous for its delicious food, especially its fruits and traditional Malay dishes. Here are some of the must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

1. Laksa

Balik Pulau’s laksa is made with a spicy and tangy broth, rice noodles, and a variety of fresh seafood.

2. Durian

The king of fruits is best enjoyed in Balik Pulau, where you’ll find some of the freshest and most delicious durians in Malaysia.

3. Nasi Lemak

This traditional Malay dish consists of rice cooked in coconut milk, served with spicy sambal sauce, fried chicken, egg, and peanuts.

Local Beverages

1. Nutmeg Juice

Nutmeg juice is a refreshing drink that is made from the fruit of the nutmeg tree, which is native to Balik Pulau.

2. Soursop Juice

Soursop juice is a sweet and tangy drink that is made from the fruit of the soursop tree.

3. Coconut Water

Balik Pulau is known for its fresh and delicious coconut water, which is the perfect way to quench your thirst on a hot day.

Where to Stay in Balik Pulau: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Balik Pulau offers a variety of accommodation options, from luxury hotels to budget-friendly guesthouses. Here are some of the best places to stay:

Luxury Hotels

1. Tadom Hill Resorts

This eco-friendly resort offers luxurious rooms and beautiful views of the surrounding hills and forests.

2. Desa Murni Batik House

This traditional Malay house has been converted into a cozy and comfortable guesthouse, offering a unique and authentic experience.

Budget-Friendly Guesthouses

1. Balik Pulau BookLodge

This cozy guesthouse is perfect for budget-conscious travelers who still want a comfortable and convenient place to stay.

2. Amzan Homestay

Stay with a local family and experience the warm hospitality and traditional lifestyle of Balik Pulau.

What to Buy in Balik Pulau: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Here are some of the best souvenirs and local products to bring home from Balik Pulau:

1. Nutmeg Products

Nutmeg is one of the most popular fruits in Balik Pulau, and the locals have found many uses for it, including making soaps, lotions, and jams.

2. Batik

Batik is a traditional Malaysian fabric that is hand-dyed with intricate patterns. You can find beautiful batik products at the local markets and shops in Balik Pulau.

3. Sweets and Snacks

There are many local sweets and snacks that are unique to Balik Pulau, such as Tambun Biscuits, Mueh Kueh, and Heh Bee Hiam.

Local Culture and Customs of Balik Pulau

Balik Pulau is a traditional town with a rich culture and customs that are worth experiencing. Here are some things you should know before visiting:

  • The locals are mostly Malay, and Islam is the dominant religion. Visitors should dress modestly and respect local customs and beliefs.
  • Balik Pulau is known for its traditional Malay houses, which have a unique architecture and design. Many of these houses are still in use today and have been passed down from generation to generation.
  • Respect for elders and family values are important in Malaysian culture. Visitors should be courteous and respectful to the locals, especially to older people.

Best Time to Visit Balik Pulau

The best time to visit Balik Pulau is from December to February, when the weather is cooler and drier. Avoid visiting during the monsoon season, which runs from September to November, as heavy rain and strong winds can disrupt outdoor activities.

Do’s and Don’ts of Balik Pulau

Here are some do’s and don’ts to keep in mind while visiting Balik Pulau:

Do:

  • Respect local customs and traditions
  • Dress modestly when visiting religious sites
  • Use your right hand for eating and passing items
  • Be courteous and polite to the locals

Don’t:

  • Touch someone’s head, it is considered disrespectful in Malaysian culture
  • Point with your finger, use your hand instead
  • Use your left hand for passing items or shaking hands
  • Disrespect the local culture and traditions

Safety and Health

Safety in Balik Pulau

Balik Pulau is a safe town with low crime rates. However, visitors should still take necessary precautions, such as not leaving valuables unattended and avoiding walking alone at night.

Health in Balik Pulau

Tap water is not recommended for drinking in Balik Pulau, as it may cause stomach problems. Visitors should stick to bottled or filtered water and avoid eating raw or unpeeled fruits and vegetables.

Getting to Balik Pulau: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Balik Pulau

The nearest airport to Balik Pulau is the Penang International Airport, which is about 30 kilometers away. From there, you can take a taxi or a bus to Balik Pulau.

Bus Stations Near Balik Pulau

The main bus station in Balik Pulau is the Balik Pulau Bus Terminal, which connects to towns and cities across Penang Island and mainland Malaysia.

Other Options

You can also take a taxi or hire a private car to get to Balik Pulau. Alternatively, you can rent a car or motorbike and explore the town and surrounding areas at your own pace.

How to Get Around in Balik Pulau

The best way to get around Balik Pulau is by car, motorbike, or bicycle. Taxis are also available, but they can be expensive. Walking is another option, especially if you’re staying in the town center.

Where to Go Next from Balik Pulau: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Here are some nearby destinations and excursions that you can visit from Balik Pulau:

1. Penang Island

Penang Island has many attractions and places to visit, such as the heritage center of George Town, the beaches of Batu Ferringhi, and the Penang National Park.

2. Langkawi Island

Langkawi Island is a popular tourist destination that is known for its beautiful beaches, duty-free shopping, and outdoor activities.

3. Ipoh

Ipoh is a historic town that is famous for its colonial architecture, street art, and food scene. It’s a great place to experience the old-world charm of Malaysia.

Conclusion

Balik Pulau is a hidden gem in Penang that offers a unique and authentic experience for travelers who want to explore the lesser-known areas of Malaysia. From the natural attractions and outdoor adventures to the rich culture and delicious food, Balik Pulau has something for everyone.
