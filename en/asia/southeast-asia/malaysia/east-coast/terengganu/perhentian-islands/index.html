Introduction to Perhentian Islands

Perhentian Islands is a stunning archipelago located in Terengganu, on the east coast of Peninsular Malaysia. It includes two islands, Perhentian Besar and Perhentian Kecil, both of which are surrounded by crystal-clear waters and offer pristine beaches of white sand. The islands are a must-visit for those looking for adventure, relaxation, and a chance to reconnect with nature.

List of Places to Visit in Perhentian Islands

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

When visiting Perhentian Islands, be sure to explore its rich culture and history. The following are some of the must-visit cultural attractions on the islands:

  • Pulau Perhentian Marine Park - This park is home to a diverse range of marine species, including sea turtles, reef sharks, and colourful fish. Visitors can explore the park through snorkelling and diving.

  • Abdul Rahman Sulaiman Cultural Centre - This centre showcases the traditional way of life of the local Malay community. It includes exhibitions of traditional Malay clothing, architecture, crafts, and music.

  • Long Beach Mosque - This mosque is unique in that it is built entirely out of wood. It sits on the western side of Long Beach and is a beautiful sight to behold, especially during sunset.

Natural Attractions of Perhentian Islands: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Perhentian Islands are known for their stunning natural beauty, including pristine beaches, coral reefs, and hiking trails. Here are some of the top natural attractions on the islands:

  • Teluk Kekeb Beach - This beach is often referred to as “turtle bay” due to the frequent appearances of turtles on its shores. Visitors can also go snorkelling to explore the surrounding coral reefs.

  • Turtle Sanctuary Beach - This beach is home to a turtle conservation project aimed at protecting the endangered sea turtles that nest on its shores. Visitors can learn about the project and view the turtles up close.

  • Bukit Che Long - This hill offers breathtaking views of the islands from its summit. The hike up the hill is challenging but rewarding, and visitors can explore the surrounding forest along the way.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Perhentian Islands

Perhentian Islands offer several hiking and biking routes that are perfect for those looking to explore the islands’ natural beauty on foot or by bike. Some of the top routes include:

  • Perhentian Island Hiking Trail - This trail spans the entire length of the Perhentian Besar island and offers stunning views of the surrounding beaches and forests along the way.

  • Kampung Sungai Renggis to Teluk KK Trail - This trail takes visitors through the lush rainforests of Perhentian Kecil and offers stunning views of the surrounding islands.

Best Things to Do in Perhentian Islands: Activities and Tours

Perhentian Islands offer a variety of activities and tours that cater to all types of travellers. Some of the top things to do on the islands include:

  • Snorkelling and Diving - Perhentian Islands are home to some of the world’s most beautiful coral reefs. Visitors can explore these reefs through guided snorkelling and diving tours.

  • Kayaking and Stand-Up Paddleboarding - Visitors can rent kayaks and stand-up paddleboards to explore the islands’ beautiful shores and secluded coves.

  • Fishing Tours - Those looking to experience the local fishing culture can take a guided fishing tour and learn about the traditional fishing methods used by the local Malay community.

Week-Long Itinerary for Perhentian Islands

Day 1 - Arrive on Perhentian Besar and check into your chosen accommodation. Spend the rest of the day exploring the island’s southern beaches.

Day 2 - Take a snorkelling tour of the islands’ coral reefs.

Day 3 - Go on a hiking or biking tour of Perhentian Besar.

Day 4 - Visit the turtle sanctuary on Perhentian Kecil and learn about the local conservation efforts.

Day 5 - Explore the local Malay culture at the Abdul Rahman Sulaiman Cultural Centre.

Day 6 - Take a kayaking or stand-up paddleboarding tour of the islands.

Day 7 - Relax on the beach and enjoy the natural beauty of Perhentian Islands.

Food and Drink Scene of Perhentian Islands

Must-Try Dishes

Perhentian Islands offer a variety of Malay and international dishes that cater to all types of tastes. Some of the must-try dishes on the islands include:

  • Nasi Lemak - A traditional Malay dish made with rice cooked in coconut milk, served with peanuts, dried anchovies, sambal, and boiled egg.

  • Keropok Lekor - A deep-fried fish snack made from a mixture of fish meat, sago flour, and seasoning.

  • Pasembor - A popular salad dish made with shredded cucumber, potatoes, bean sprouts, and deep-fried fritters, served with a sweet and spicy sauce.

Local Beverages

Visitors to Perhentian Islands can enjoy a variety of refreshing beverages, including:

  • Teh Tarik - A traditional Malay tea made with condensed milk and black tea, poured back and forth repeatedly to create a frothy texture.

  • Lemon Grass Juice - A refreshing drink made from lemongrass, sugar syrup, and ice.

  • Coconut Shake - A popular drink made from coconut water and coconut flesh blended with ice.

Where to Stay in Perhentian Islands: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Perhentian Islands offer a variety of accommodation options that cater to all types of travellers. Some of the top places to stay on the islands include:

  • Perhentian Island Resort - A luxury beachfront resort on Perhentian Besar, offering spacious rooms, a spa, and on-site dining options.

  • Alunan Resort - A boutique resort on Perhentian Kecil, offering stunning sea views, a restaurant, and a rooftop lounge.

  • The Barat Perhentian - A budget-friendly hostel on Perhentian Besar, offering dormitory and private rooms, a restaurant, and a bar.

What to Buy in Perhentian Islands: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Perhentian Islands offer a variety of souvenirs and local products that make for great gifts and mementos. Some of the top items to buy on the islands include:

  • Batik Clothing - A traditional Malaysian textile art, often used to make clothing and accessories.

  • Handmade Jewellery - Made from locally sourced shells, coral, and beads, these unique pieces of jewellery make for great souvenirs.

  • Traditional Malay Snacks - Made from a variety of ingredients, including coconut, sugar, and rice flour, these snacks are a delicious reminder of the local cuisine.

Local Culture and Customs of Perhentian Islands

Perhentian Islands are home to a predominantly Malay community, and visitors are encouraged to be respectful of the local culture and customs. Some of the key aspects of the local culture and customs include:

  • Islam - The local community is predominantly Muslim, and visitors are expected to dress modestly when visiting mosques and other religious sites.

  • Language - The official language of Malaysia is Malay, but most locals in Perhentian Islands also speak English.

  • Etiquette - Visitors are expected to remove their shoes before entering homes or temples, and it is considered impolite to point with the feet or touch someone’s head.

Best Time to Visit Perhentian Islands

The best time to visit Perhentian Islands is from mid-March to October, when the weather is dry and sunny. The islands are closed to visitors from November to February due to the monsoon season.

Do’s and Don’ts of Perhentian Islands

Do:

  • Do respect the local culture and customs.

  • Do try the local cuisine and drinks.

  • Do take your trash with you and dispose of it properly.

Don’t:

  • Don’t litter or disturb the natural habitats of the local wildlife.

  • Don’t engage in activities that harm the local environment.

  • Don’t consume drugs or engage in any illegal activities.

Safety and Health

Safety in Perhentian Islands

Perhentian Islands are generally safe for visitors. However, visitors should take the following precautions:

  • Avoid leaving valuables unattended on the beach.

  • Avoid swimming during monsoon season or in areas with strong currents.

  • Follow the instructions of tourist guides and tour agencies for safe activities.

Health in Perhentian Islands

Visitors to Perhentian Islands should take the following health precautions:

  • Use insect repellent to avoid mosquito bites and the risk of dengue fever.

  • Use sunscreen to avoid sunburn.

  • Drink only bottled water to avoid waterborne diseases.

Getting to Perhentian Islands: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Perhentian Islands

The closest airport to Perhentian Islands is Sultan Mahmud Airport in Kuala Terengganu, which is served by several domestic airlines, including AirAsia, Malaysia Airlines, and Firefly.

Bus Stations Near Perhentian Islands

The nearest bus station to Perhentian Islands is in Kuala Besut, which is a two-hour drive from Kuala Terengganu.

Other Options

Visitors can also take a taxi or rent a car from Kuala Terengganu to Kuala Besut.

How to Get Around in Perhentian Islands

Perhentian Islands are best explored on foot or by boat. Visitors can also rent bicycles and motorbikes to explore the islands at their own pace.

Where to Go Next from Perhentian Islands: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Perhentian Islands are a great base for exploring other nearby destinations, including:

  • Redang Island - A larger island located to the south of Perhentian Islands, offering stunning beaches and snorkelling opportunities.

  • Taman Negara National Park - A vast rainforest and wilderness area located on the mainland, offering opportunities for jungle trekking, wildlife spotting, and river rafting.

Conclusion

Perhentian Islands offer a unique blend of adventure, relaxation, culture, and natural beauty that makes it a must-visit destination in Southeast Asia. Whether you’re looking for a chance to explore pristine coral reefs or simply relax on a pristine beach, Perhentian Islands has something to offer for every type of traveller.
