Introduction to Kuala Besut

Located on the East Coast of Malaysia, Kuala Besut is a hidden gem that is often overlooked by travelers. While it may not be the most famous destination, it offers an authentic Malaysian experience with its white sandy beaches, crystal clear waters, and traditional villages. Kuala Besut is the gateway to the Perhentian Islands, which offer some of the most beautiful beaches in the world. In this comprehensive tour guide, we’ll explore the best things to do in Kuala Besut, where to stay, what to eat, and much more.

List of Places to Visit in Kuala Besut

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Kuala Besut is rich in history and culture, and there are several museums and historical landmarks to visit. Here are some of the must-visit places:

  • Kuala Besut Jetty - This is the gateway to the Pehentian Islands and a bustling hub of activity. You’ll find plenty of shops, restaurants, and tour operators here.
  • Terengganu State Museum - This is the largest museum in Malaysia and a must-visit for history lovers. It has a vast collection of artifacts and exhibits that showcase the state’s culture and heritage.
  • Sultan Mahmud Bridge - This is a beautiful landmark in Kuala Terengganu that offers panoramic views of the city and river.
  • Kampung Cina - This is a traditional Chinese village that dates back to the 19th century. It’s a great place to explore the local culture and architecture.

Natural Attractions of Kuala Besut: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

If you love the outdoors, Kuala Besut has plenty of natural attractions to offer. From scenic hikes to water sports, there’s something for everyone. Here are some of the best natural attractions in Kuala Besut:

  • Perhentian Islands - These beautiful islands are a short boat ride from Kuala Besut and offer pristine beaches, crystal-clear waters, and incredible marine life. You can enjoy water activities such as snorkeling, diving, and kayaking.
  • Bukit Keluang - This is a beautiful hill that offers stunning panoramic views of the coast and surrounding islands. It’s a popular spot for hiking and picnics.
  • Duyung Island - This island is home to the Turtle Conservation Centre, where you can learn about and interact with turtles. You can also go snorkeling or kayaking around the island.
  • Kuala Besut River Cruise - This is a peaceful and scenic boat ride through the mangrove forests and small villages along the river.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Kuala Besut

Kuala Besut is a great place for hiking and biking enthusiasts. Here are some of the most popular routes:

  • Bukit Keluang Trail - This is a moderate hiking trail that takes you to the top of Bukit Keluang for stunning views.
  • Pulau Perhentian Trail - This is a beautiful trail that takes you through the lush jungle and along the beach on the Perhentian Islands.
  • Kampong Sg Keluang Trail - This is a challenging trail that takes you through dense forests and steep hills, but the breathtaking views make it all worth it.

Best Things to Do in Kuala Besut: Activities and Tours

If you’re looking for things to do in Kuala Besut, here are some exciting activities and tours to check out:

  • Snorkeling or Diving in the Perhentian Islands - These islands offer some of the best snorkeling and diving experiences in the world, with colorful coral reefs and a variety of marine life.
  • Camping on the Beach - Spend a night under the stars on one of the Perhentian Islands’ pristine beaches.
  • Exploring Local Villages - Visit traditional fishing villages, learn about the local culture and lifestyle, and enjoy fresh seafood.
  • River Cruise - Take a peaceful boat ride along the Kuala Besut River and explore the mangrove forests and wildlife.
  • Kayaking or Paddleboarding - Rent a kayak or paddleboard and explore the coastline at your own pace.

Week-Long Itinerary for Kuala Besut

Here’s a suggested itinerary for a week-long trip to Kuala Besut:

Day 1: Arrive in Kuala Besut, explore the town, and enjoy local street food.

Day 2: Take a boat ride to the Perhentian Islands and snorkel or dive in the crystal-clear waters.

Day 3: Hike Bukit Keluang for sunrise and enjoy the panoramic views. Spend the afternoon relaxing on the beach.

Day 4: Explore the local villages and learn about the traditional fishing methods. Enjoy fresh seafood for lunch.

Day 5: Take a river cruise along the Kuala Besut River and explore the mangrove forests and wildlife.

Day 6: Rent a kayak or paddleboard to explore the coastline at your own pace.

Day 7: Relax on the beach and soak up the sun before departing Kuala Besut.

Food and Drink Scene of Kuala Besut

Kuala Besut offers a range of local delicacies that are sure to satisfy any foodie. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Nasi Dagang - A traditional Malaysian dish of rice cooked with coconut milk and served with fish curry.
  • Keropok Lekor - Grilled fish sausages made from fish paste and sago flour.
  • Laksam - A type of noodle dish served with fish gravy, vegetables, and herbs.

Local Beverages

  • Kopi-O - A strong black coffee served with sugar.
  • Teh Tarik - A sweet milk tea that is frothed and pulled to mix the ingredients.

Where to Stay in Kuala Besut: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Kuala Besut offers plenty of accommodation options to suit every budget and preference. Here are some top choices:

  • Perhentian Island Resort - Located on the Perhentian Islands, this resort offers comfortable rooms and plenty of amenities.
  • Tuna Bay Island Resort - Another popular resort on the Perhentian Islands, offering gorgeous beach views and water sports activities.
  • Kuala Besut Homestay - A budget-friendly option for travelers who want to experience the local culture and lifestyle.
  • D’Perhentian Hotel - A luxury beachfront hotel with a swimming pool, spa, and restaurant.

What to Buy in Kuala Besut: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Here are some must-buy souvenirs and local products to bring home from Kuala Besut:

  • Batik Textiles - Traditional Malaysian fabrics with intricate designs.
  • Wau Bulan - Beautiful Malaysian kites that are traditionally flown during festivals.
  • Keropok Lekor - Delicious grilled fish sausages that make the perfect snack.

Local Culture and Customs of Kuala Besut

The local culture of Kuala Besut is strongly influenced by the Islamic religion. Visitors should dress modestly and respect the local customs and traditions. It’s also common to remove shoes before entering homes and places of worship.

Best Time to Visit Kuala Besut

The best time to visit Kuala Besut is between April and September when the weather is warm and sunny. However, the peak tourist season is between June and August, and prices may be higher.

Do’s and Don’ts of Kuala Besut

Do:

  • Respect the local customs and dress modestly.
  • Try the local cuisine and learn about the culture.
  • Explore the natural attractions and go on outdoor adventures.

Don’t:

  • Disrespect the local culture and religion.
  • Litter or pollute the natural environment.
  • Engage in illegal activities.

Safety and Health

Safety in Kuala Besut

Kuala Besut is a relatively safe destination for tourists. However, visitors should take normal precautions such as not flashing valuables, avoiding isolated areas at night, and being aware of your surroundings.

Health in Kuala Besut

Visitors to Kuala Besut should ensure they are up to date with routine vaccinations. You should also take precautions against mosquito bites and drink bottled water to avoid getting sick.

Getting to Kuala Besut: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Kuala Besut

The nearest airport to Kuala Besut is the Sultan Mahmud Airport in Kuala Terengganu. From there, visitors can take a taxi or bus to Kuala Besut.

Bus Stations Near Kuala Besut

Kuala Besut Bus Terminal is the main hub for buses traveling to Kuala Besut from other parts of Malaysia.

Other Options

Visitors can also take a ferry from the nearby islands to Kuala Besut.

How to Get Around in Kuala Besut

The best way to get around Kuala Besut is by taxi or rental car. Taxis can be hailed on the street, or visitors can book through their accommodation. Scooters and bicycles are also available for rent.

Where to Go Next from Kuala Besut: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Here are other nearby destinations and excursions to add to your itinerary:

  • Kuala Terengganu - The capital of Terengganu state, home to several museums, markets, and landmarks.
  • Kenyir Lake - A beautiful lake with crystal-clear waters and rich biodiversity. Visitors can enjoy water activities, jungle treks or bird watching.
  • Redang Island - The third-largest island off the East Coast of Peninsular Malaysia, known for its crystal-clear waters and coral reefs.

Conclusion

Kuala Besut may not be as famous as other destinations in Malaysia, but it offers a unique and authentic Malaysian experience. With its beautiful natural attractions, traditional villages, and delicious cuisine, Kuala Besut has something for every traveler. We hope this comprehensive tour guide has helped you plan your next trip to Kuala Besut and has made your journey more enjoyable.
