Introduction to Kuala Terengganu

Kuala Terengganu is the capital of the Terengganu state in Malaysia, located on the east coast of the country. Known for its rich cultural heritage, stunning landmarks, and beautiful natural attractions, Kuala Terengganu is a must-visit destination that combines history and modernity in perfect harmony.

List of Places to Visit in Kuala Terengganu

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Terengganu State Museum - One of the largest museums in Southeast Asia that showcases the cultural and historical heritage of The Terengganu state. The museum houses ethnographic, Islamic, and Royal galleries, exhibiting a rich collection of artefacts and arts that reflect the unique Terengganu culture.
  • Taman Tamadun Islam - A park that exhibits the replicas of 22 most beautiful Islamic monuments in the world, including Taj Mahal, Hagia Sophia, and Masjid Al-Haram. The park is surrounded by beautiful scenery, making it a must-visit location to appreciate Islamic art and architecture.
  • Crystal Mosque - A mosque made entirely of steel and crystal, featuring unique, modern architecture. This stunning mosque looks even more breathtaking at night when it is illuminated in an array of bright colours.

Natural Attractions of Kuala Terengganu: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Redang Island - A tropical paradise that boasts crystal clear waters, coral reefs, and pristine white sand beaches. Redang Island is a perfect spot for snorkelling, scuba diving, and other water activities.
  • Tengku Tengah Zaharah Mosque - A mesmerising floating mosque, built over a man-made island in the middle of a lake. Its unique design and peaceful surroundings make it a popular tourist attraction for visitors of all ages.
  • Chendering Lake - A tranquil lake surrounded by beautiful scenery that offers various recreational activities like fishing, boating, cycling, and picnicking.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Kuala Terengganu

  • Jungle trekking in Kenyir Lake - Embark on a hiking trail that leads to the highest peak in Lake Kenyir, offers an awe-inspiring view of the lake and lush greenery that surrounds it.
  • Bukit Puteri Trail - A popular hiking track that leads to the scenic viewpoint above the Bukit Puteri Fort. The trail itself is well-paved, and you can witness the stunning sunset view from the top.
  • Terengganu Heritage Trail - A cycling route that allows you to explore the cultural and historical side of Kuala Terengganu that passes through 12 landmarks, including the State Museum, the Royal Mausoleum, and the Tengku Tengah Zaharah Mosque.

Best Things to Do in Kuala Terengganu: Activities and Tours

  • Snorkeling and Scuba Diving at Redang Island - Discover the beauty of marine life by indulging in activities like snorkelling and scuba diving at Redang Island.
  • Pasar Payang (Central Market) - A must-visit location if you want to indulge in local food and handicraft shopping during your stay in Kuala Terengganu.
  • Kampung Cina Walking Tour - Take a walking tour of the lively and vibrant Chinatown area that showcases traditional wooden houses, delicious Chinese cuisine, and local handicraft stores.

Food and Drink Scene of Kuala Terengganu

Must-Try Dishes

  • Keropok Lekor - A delicious traditional Terengganu dish made of fish/meat and sago flour, served with hot chilli sauce.
  • Nasi Dagang - A popular breakfast dish, consists of steamed glutinous rice cooked with coconut milk and fish curry sauce.
  • Lontong - Another popular breakfast dish made of compressed rice served in savoury coconut milk broth.

Local Beverages

  • Teh Tarik - A hot milk tea beverage that is pulled (tarik) to create a frothy finish.
  • Air Kelapa Bakar - Coconut water mixed with sweetened syrup and grilled coconut pulp.

Where to Stay in Kuala Terengganu: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Grand Continental Hotel Kuala Terengganu - A luxury hotel with spacious rooms, an outdoor pool, and sauna, located near the city’s attractions.
  • The Regency Waterfront Hotel - A four-star hotel located on the banks of Terengganu River, providing picturesque views and good location for a relaxing stay.
  • The Kuala Terengganu Boutique Hotel - A budget-friendly hotel located in the city centre, perfect for those seeking affordable accommodation with good amenities.

What to Buy in Kuala Terengganu: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Terengganu Batik - A traditional Terengganu textile art that involves dyeing and printing on a cotton fabric to create a unique design.
  • Keropok Lekor - A popular traditional Terengganu snack made of fish and sago flour, perfect as a souvenir.
  • Songket - A hand-woven silk or cotton cloth with intricate designs and gold thread, used to make traditional Malay attire.

Local Culture and Customs of Kuala Terengganu

  • Dress Code - As Kuala Terengganu is a conservative Muslim city, it is recommended that you dress modestly to respect the local customs and traditions. Women should cover their heads, wear long skirts/trousers, and long-sleeved shirts while visiting mosques or other religious places.
  • Etiquette - Greeting someone with Salam (hello) is customary. It is also not polite to touch anybody with your foot in Malaysia.
  • Ramadan - The holy month of Ramadan is observed in Kuala Terengganu, and you should avoid eating, drinking or smoking in public places during daylight hours.

Best Time to Visit Kuala Terengganu

The best time to visit Kuala Terengganu is between March and September, during the dry season when the weather is warm, and the sea is calm. But if you prefer less-crowded places, it is recommended that you visit during the Monsoon season (November to February) when it’s slightly cooler and much quieter.

Do’s and Don’ts of Kuala Terengganu

Do:

  • Respect local culture and customs.
  • Wear appropriate clothing while visiting Islamic sites.
  • Greet locals with a smile, and say “Salam”.
  • Keep the environment clean and use the proper trash bins.

Don’t:

  • Avoid eating in public during the Holy month of Ramadan.
  • Don’t point your finger or feet to anyone, as it is considered impolite in the local culture.
  • Do not litter or spit in public places.

Safety and Health

Safety in Kuala Terengganu

Kuala Terengganu is a safe and welcoming city for tourists, but visitors should take normal safety precautions, such as being aware of their surroundings and not leaving their important belongings unattended.

Health in Kuala Terengganu

It is recommended that you have all the necessary vaccinations before traveling to Kuala Terengganu. Always drink bottled water and use insect repellent when going outside to avoid mosquito bites.

Getting to Kuala Terengganu: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Kuala Terengganu

Sultan Mahmud Airport is the primary airport located in Kuala Terengganu that caters to both local and international airlines.

Bus Stations Near Kuala Terengganu

The express bus station located in the town centre, offers frequent bus services from major cities such as Kuala Lumpur, Penang, and Singapore.

Other Options

If you prefer a more scenic route, you can take a train from Kuala Lumpur that passes through lush green countryside and scenic coastline towards Kuala Terengganu.

How to Get Around in Kuala Terengganu

Public transportation such as buses and taxis are available in Kuala Terengganu, but the most convenient way to get around is by renting a car/bike or hiring a private driver.

Where to Go Next from Kuala Terengganu: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Pulau Kapas - A stunning island known for its beautiful white sand beaches and crystal clear waters.
  • Pulau Perhentian - Another beautiful island located on the east coast of Malaysia, known for its snorkelling and diving spots.
  • Kenyir Lake - A beautiful lake located near Kuala Terengganu, offering plenty of recreational activities like jungle trekking and boating.

Conclusion

Kuala Terengganu is a city that has much to offer for visitors of all ages. Plan your perfect vacation by exploring the cultural, historical, and natural attractions while indulging in delicious local cuisine and experiencing the customs and traditions of Terengganu. Safe travels, and we hope you have a memorable time in Kuala Terengganu!
