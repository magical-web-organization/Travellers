Introduction to Kemaman

Kemaman is a district located in Terengganu, on the east coast of Malaysia. It is a small town but boasts a lot of natural attractions. Kemaman is known for its beautiful beaches, gorgeous islands, and the famous Chukai town. It is a great place to visit and explore the traditional Malay culture of the region.

List of Places to Visit in Kemaman

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Kemaman Museum: A great place to start the exploration of Kemaman’s rich heritage is the Kemaman Museum. The museum features historical artifacts, traditional costumes, and other memorabilia that showcase the town’s past.
  • Lanjut Fort: The Lanjut Fort is a well-preserved ancient fort located in Kemaman. It was previously used as a defense post that helped the locals defend their town against the pirates.
  • Gong Badak Monument: Another historical landmark located in Kemaman is the Gong Badak monument. It was built to commemorate an 1839 treaty signed between Terengganu and the British.

Natural Attractions of Kemaman: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Pantai Kelulut: Pantai Kelulut is a gorgeous beach located in Kemaman. It is a great place to relax and soak up the sun. The beach features serenity, crystal clear waters, and a calming atmosphere.
  • Taman Tamadun Islam: This Islamic park is a theme park that highlights Islamic architecture and its contributions to humanity. Visitors can explore the different museums and exhibits that showcase Islamic civilization, learn Islamic beliefs, and try on traditional clothing.
  • Pulau Cempedak: A tiny island located in Kemaman, Pulau Cempedak, is an island paradise. It boasts clear blue waters and beautiful coral reefs, making it a popular spot for snorkeling and diving.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Kemaman

  • Teluk Mak Nik: A scenic hiking spot located in Kemaman, Teluk Mak Nik is a great spot for a day out in nature. The hike is moderate, with exhilarating views of the sea as you walk along the coastal pavement.
  • Cherating Beach: Cherating Beach is a popular destination for surfers. With its gentle waves, it’s a great place to catch some waves and soak in the sun. Visitors can also relax with a picnic or take a boat ride around the nearby islands.

Best Things to Do in Kemaman: Activities and Tours

  • Water Sports: For water sports enthusiasts, Kemaman has plenty of activities to offer. Visitors can go snorkeling, scuba diving, kayaking, and more along its beautiful coastal areas.
  • Boat Tours: Another great way to explore Kemaman is by taking on boat tours. Visitors can do sunset cruises and experience a laid-back local life of sea villages while soaking up the sun.
  • Night Markets: Kemaman has many night markets where visitors can enjoy local food and buy souvenirs. The Sher Sungai Lembing night market is a must-visit, selling everything from street food to handmade crafts.

Food and Drink Scene of Kemaman

Must-Try Dishes

  • Keropok Lekor: A famous local snack, Keropok Lekor is a fish sausage-like treat made from fish and sago flour.
  • Nasi Dagang: A popular dish that consists of steamed rice and fish curry, nasi dagang is a must-try in Kemaman.
  • Otak-Otak: Another famous snack, Otak-otak is made of fish paste, spices, and coconut milk wrapped in banana leaves and grilled over an open flame.

Local Beverages

  • Air Kelapa: Fresh coconut water is a popular drink in Kemaman. It is refreshing to drink in the hot and humid weather.
  • Sirap Bandung: Sirap Bandung is a great drink to try, especially if you have a sweet tooth. It is a mixture of rose syrup, milk, and ice.

Where to Stay in Kemaman: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Hyatt Regency Kuantan Resort: A luxurious resort, the Hyatt Regency Kuantan Resort offers guests breathtaking views of the sea. It is perfect for travelers who want to relax in complete comfort and style.
  • Tanjung Inn: A great option for those with an eye for nature. Tanjung Inn overlooks the charming Kuala Kemaman river and nature reserve. It offers cozy and comfortable traditional Malay-style rooms.
  • Serambi Beach Resort: A quiet and beautiful resort located on a white sandy beach near Kemaman. It is perfect for a relaxing vacation with outstanding facilities and breathtaking views.

What to Buy in Kemaman: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Kemaman Keropok Lekor: As mentioned before, Keropok Lekor is a famous snack in Kemaman. Visitors can buy them easily in the local markets to bring home as a souvenir.
  • Traditional Batik Clothing: Kemaman is famous for its beautiful hand-painted batik clothing. The locals produce them in different styles and design.

Local Culture and Customs of Kemaman

Kemaman is a traditional Malay town, and visitors should respect local customs. Dress conservatively when visiting temples and mosques, and always ask permission before taking pictures.

Best Time to Visit Kemaman

The best time to visit Kemaman is between March and September when the weather is hot and sunny. However, it is worth noting that the monsoon season in Kemaman runs from October to February.

Do’s and Don’ts of Kemaman

Do:

  • Respect the local customs and beliefs.
  • Experience the local culture by trying traditional foods and participating in cultural events.
  • Wear comfortable clothing and footwear when exploring outdoor activities.

Don’t:

  • Disrespect or litter in the natural environment.
  • Dress inappropriately when visiting religious sites.
  • Haggle excessively when shopping in the markets or stores.

Safety and Health

Safety in Kemaman

Kemaman is generally a safe place to visit. However, visitors should always take necessary precautions such as securing their belongings and avoiding walking alone at night.

Health in Kemaman

Visitors to Kemaman should ensure they have all their necessary vaccinations before visiting. Medical facilities can be limited in small towns, so it’s always best to carry a first-aid kit.

Getting to Kemaman: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Kemaman

The nearest airport to Kemaman is the Sultan Mahmud Airport in Kuala Terengganu, which is about 60 kilometers away from the town.

Bus Stations Near Kemaman

Visitors from Kuala Lumpur can take a bus ride to Kemaman. The Hentian Duta Bus Terminal is the central bus terminal, and buses depart frequently to Kemaman.

Other Options

Visitors can also opt to rent a car or taxi from Kuala Lumpur to Kemaman, which is about a 274-kilometer drive. Ferries also run between Kemaman and nearby islands.

How to Get Around in Kemaman

Taxis and buses are the most common mode of transportation in Kemaman. However, renting a car is a great option for visitors looking to have more independence and flexibility.

Where to Go Next from Kemaman: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Kuala Terengganu: Kuala Terengganu is the state capital of Terengganu and is located just an hour’s drive from Kemaman. It is famous for its markets and traditional crafts.
  • Pekan: Pekan is a town located in Pahang, just a 2-hour drive from Kemaman. Visitors can explore the many historical sites and attractions or visit the Pahang Royal Museum to learn about the region’s royal family.

Conclusion

Kemaman is a spectacular destination for those who love to explore the natural beauty of Malaysia. With its rich culture, friendly people, and delicious food, Kemaman is a destination that is sure to give visitors an unforgettable experience. Whether it’s exploring the local culture or soaking up the sun on its beautiful beaches, Kemaman is a one-of-a-kind destination that should be on every traveler’s bucket list.
