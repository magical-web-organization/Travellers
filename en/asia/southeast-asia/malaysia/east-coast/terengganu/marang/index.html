Introduction to Marang

Marang is a quaint coastal town located in the district of Kuala Nerus, Terengganu, in East Coast Malaysia. Unlike other popular tourist destinations in Malaysia, Marang offers a more laid-back atmosphere and off-the-beaten-path experiences to visitors. The town boasts a scenic coastline, serene fishing villages, and a rich cultural heritage that dates back centuries. In this comprehensive guide, we will explore the various attractions, activities, dining options, accommodations, and logistical information on how to get around Marang.

List of Places to Visit in Marang

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Terengganu State Museum

Terengganu State Museum is the largest museum complex in South East Asia where visitors can learn about the region’s history, culture, and art. Located in Bukit Losong, Kuala Terengganu, the museum complex spans over 27 hectares and features 4 main galleries: the Islamic Heritage Gallery, the Royal Gallery, the Historical and Cultural Gallery, and the Natural History Gallery.

Crystal Mosque

The Crystal Mosque, also known as Masjid Kristal in Malay, is a modern mosque made of steel and glass that stands out for its stunning architecture. The mosque, located in nearby Kuala Terengganu, is named after its crystal-like structure that glows beautifully at night. The mosque can accommodate up to 1,500 worshippers and features a breathtaking view of the nearby river.

Rantau Abang Turtle Sanctuary

Rantau Abang Turtle Sanctuary is a conservation site that aims to protect the endangered sea turtles from extinction. The sanctuary, located 30 minutes drive from Marang, is open to the public during the nesting season from May to September. Visitors can watch the turtles lay their eggs on the beach and witness the hatching of baby turtles at the hatchery.

Natural Attractions of Marang: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Pulau Kapas

Pulau Kapas, or Cotton Island in Malay, is a small, lush island located just a short boat ride from Marang’s jetty. The island features pristine beaches, crystal-clear waters that are perfect for snorkeling and diving, and scenic hiking trails that lead to the island’s interior. Visitors can also rent kayaks, go fishing, or simply relax on the beach.

Bukit Puteri

Bukit Puteri is a hill that offers panoramic views of the Marang, Terengganu River, and the nearby mountains. Visitors can hike up the hill or take a cable car to the top to enjoy the breathtaking views. The hill is also home to the Puteri Palace, the residence of the Sultan’s daughter, and a traditional Malay kampung or village.

Sekayu Waterfall

Sekayu Waterfall is a natural wonder located in the Sekayu Recreational Forest, about 20 kilometers from Marang. The waterfall features several tiers, each with a pool that visitors can swim in. The area around the waterfall is surrounded by lush tropical rainforest, offering a refreshing escape from the hot and humid weather.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Marang

Bukit Bading Hill Trail

Bukit Bading Hill Trail is a popular hiking trail that takes visitors on a scenic trek up the hill. The trail is about 1.5 kilometers long and takes approximately 1 hour to complete. The trail is well-maintained and features several lookout points that offer spectacular views of the surrounding countryside.

Marang River Trail

Marang River Trail is a flat and easy biking trail that follows the Marang River’s scenic route. Visitors can rent bicycles to explore the winding riverside path, which passes through traditional Malay villages, mangrove forests, and paddy fields. The trail is about 10 kilometers long and takes about 2 hours to complete.

Best Things to Do in Marang: Activities and Tours

Island Hopping Tours

Marang is an ideal starting point for island-hopping tours to nearby islands such as Pulau Kapas, Pulau Gemia, and Pulau Redang. Visitors can book a tour with local tour operators, who will transport them by boat to the islands, provide snorkeling gear, and organize other activities like kayaking, fishing, or simply enjoying the beach.

Fishing Tours

Fishing enthusiasts can charter a boat from Marang’s jetty for a day of fishing in the Terengganu River or the nearby sea. The tour operators provide all the necessary equipment, snacks, and drinks for a fun and relaxing day.

Week-Long Itinerary for Marang

Visitors can create a week-long itinerary to explore Marang and its neighboring towns, or use it as a base to venture out to nearby destinations like Kuala Terengganu, Kuala Besut, or the Perhentian Islands. Here’s a sample itinerary:

Day Activity
1 Arrive in Marang, check into hotel, relax on the beach
2 Visit Rantau Abang Turtle Sanctuary, hike Bukit Bading Hill Trail
3 Island-hopping tour to Pulau Kapas
4 Explore Marang River Trail on a bike, visit Sekayu Waterfall
5 Go fishing with a local tour operator

7 Check out of hotel, depart from Marang

Food and Drink Scene of Marang

Must-Try Dishes

Nasi Dagang

Nasi Dagang is a popular traditional dish in Terengganu made of glutinous rice cooked in coconut milk and served with a flavorful fish curry. The dish is usually eaten for breakfast or lunch and can be found in local food stalls and restaurants throughout Marang.

Keropok Lekor

Keropok Lekor is a popular snack in Terengganu made of fish paste and sago flour. The snack is usually boiled or fried, and served with a spicy dipping sauce. Visitors should not miss trying this popular snack at local marketplaces or keropok lekor specialty shops.

Local Beverages

Air Kelapa

Air Kelapa, or coconut water in English, is a natural hydrating drink that is widely available in Marang. The coconut water is sweet, refreshing and an ideal drink to quench thirsts on hot and humid days.

Teh Tarik

Teh Tarik, or pulled tea in English, is a sweet and creamy tea that is a popular drink among locals in Malaysia. The drink is made by pulling the tea back and forth between two cups to create a frothy consistency, and is considered as a cultural icon in Malaysia. Visitors should not miss trying this delicious tea at local coffee shops or food stalls in Marang.

Where to Stay in Marang: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Visitors can choose to stay in Marang or nearby Kuala Terengganu, which offers more lodging options. Here are some recommended places to stay in Marang:

Terrapuri Heritage Village

Terrapuri Heritage Village is a unique boutique hotel that preserves the traditional Malay houses or kampong in the region. The hotel features 29 antique houses, each with its own modern amenities and private beach access. The hotel offers guests an opportunity to experience the traditional architecture and culture of Terengganu.

Duyong Marina and Resort

Duyong Marina and Resort is a luxury hotel located in nearby Kuala Terengganu, adjacent to the picturesque Duyong Island. The hotel offers guests a wide range of facilities and amenities, including an outdoor swimming pool, tennis court, spa services, and water sports activities.

What to Buy in Marang: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Batik Fabric

Batik fabric is a traditional Malay fabric that features intricate designs and patterns. Visitors can find a variety of batik fabrics at local markets and shops in Marang, ranging from simple cotton fabrics to more elaborate silk and velvet designs.

Keropok Lekor

Keropok Lekor is a popular local snack that makes an ideal gift to bring back home. Visitors can find a variety of packaged Keropok Lekor at local markets and souvenir shops, which can be easily stored in luggage before departure.

Local Culture and Customs of Marang

Terengganu is a traditional and conservative Malay state, visitors should respect the local culture and customs, including dressing modestly and avoiding public displays of affection. Visitors should also observe Muslim practices during Ramadan, such as fasting, extra prayers, and not eating or drinking in public during daylight hours.

Best Time to Visit Marang

The best time to visit Marang is from March to September, when the weather is dry and sunny. From November to February, the rainy season brings heavy downpours and occasional flooding, it is advisable to avoid traveling during this period.

Do’s and Don’ts of Marang

Do:

  • Respect the local culture and customs
  • Dress modestly, especially when visiting cultural sites and religious places.
  • Carry enough cash, as some places might not accept credit cards.
  • Try the diverse local cuisine and snacks offered by street food vendors.

Don’t:

  • Drink alcohol in public, as it is considered taboo in the Muslim culture.
  • Take photos of people or places without consent.
  • Litter or pollute the environment
  • Be disrespectful to the locals.

Safety and Health

Safety in Marang

Marang is a generally safe town. However, visitors should take basic safety precautions like avoiding dark alleys and deserted places, keeping important documents in secure places, and not leaving belongings unattended. Visitors should also be mindful of the strong currents at sea when swimming in the sea, particularly during the monsoon season.

Health in Marang

Visitors are advised to drink bottled or filtered water to avoid waterborne diseases and to take preventive measures against mosquito-borne diseases, especially during the rainy season. Visitors should also bring personal medication and a good medical insurance policy when traveling to Marang.

Getting to Marang: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Marang

The nearest airport to Marang is the Sultan Mahmud Airport in Kuala Terengganu. From there, visitors can hire a taxi or take a bus to Marang, which is about 30 kilometers away.

Bus Stations Near Marang

Marang Bus Terminal is located in the town center, and visitors can take a taxi or walk to the various hotels and attractions in the area.

Other Options

Visitors can also hire a car, either self-drive or with a driver, to explore Marang and its surrounding areas. Alternatively, visitors can book a private transfer service or a tour operator who will provide transportation, tour guides, and activities.

How to Get Around in Marang

Visitors can rent a car or motorcycle to explore Marang and its nearby attractions. Alternatively, visitors can hire a taxi or use Grab, which is a ride-hailing app that operates in Marang. Visitors can also take a bus or a boat to access the nearby islands or go on a river cruise.

Where to Go Next from Marang: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Visitors can venture out to nearby destinations and take advantage of Marang’s central location. Here are some recommended nearby excursions:

Kuala Terengganu

Kuala Terengganu is the state capital of Terengganu and is located about 20 kilometers away from Marang. Visitors can explore the traditional Chinese heritage sites, the vibrant markets, and the impressive Terengganu State Museum.

Kuala Besut

Kuala Besut is a small town located on the East Coast of Peninsular Malaysia and is the gateway to the idyllic Perhentian Islands. Visitors can take a boat from the Kuala Besut Jetty to the beautiful islands and enjoy the pristine beaches and crystal-clear waters.

Taman Negara National Park

Taman Negara National Park is one of the oldest rainforests in the world, located about 250 kilometers away from Marang. Visitors can experience the lush tropical rainforest, scenic trails, and diverse wildlife.

Conclusion

Marang offers visitors a unique and authentic experience that reflects the traditional Malay way of life. Visitors can explore the town’s cultural heritage, indulge in the diverse local cuisine, soak in the natural beauty of the land and sea, and venture out to nearby destinations. This guide has provided an extensive overview of the various attractions, activities, dining options, accommodations, and logistical information needed to plan a trip to Marang successfully.
