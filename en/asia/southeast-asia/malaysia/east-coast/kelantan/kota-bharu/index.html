Introduction to Kota Bharu

Kota Bharu, the capital city of Kelantan, is a fascinating destination that offers a unique blend of cultures and traditions. Located in the northeast of Peninsular Malaysia, it is often referred to as the ‘cultural heart’ of the country, with a rich heritage and history.

From its old-world charm to modern amenities, Kota Bharu has plenty of attractions and activities to offer visitors. This guide will give you an idea of the best things to see and do in the city, as well as helpful travel tips to make your visit a memorable one.

List of Places to Visit in Kota Bharu

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Kota Bharu is a city steeped in history and tradition, evident in its collections of museums and historical sites:

  • Muzium Negeri Kelantan - This museum houses a collection of artifacts reflecting the state’s cultural heritage, including traditional clothing, crafts, and weaponry.
  • Istana Jahar - A magnificent, traditional palace that dates back to the 1800s and features outstanding Malay architecture.
  • Istana Balai Besar - Another palace built on the foundation of Kota Bharu’s ancient castle, it now serves as a cultural center that exhibits the rich history of the Malay royal family.
  • Kampung Laut Mosque - This mosque, built in the 1600s, is the oldest mosque in Kelantan and features unique architecture that fuses Islamic and local styles.

Natural Attractions of Kota Bharu: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

For those who appreciate the great outdoors, Kota Bharu has plenty to offer, including:

  • Pantai Cahaya Bulan - Located along the coast about 10 km from the city center, this breathtaking beach with crystal-clear waters is suitable for swimming, water sports, and other beach activities.
  • Gunung Stong State Park - A natural rainforest park with stunning mountain scenery and several hiking trails.
  • Bukit Bintang - A hill located in the center of Kota Bharu that offers beautiful views of the city skyline.
  • Kampung Laut Beach - A secluded, picturesque beach that’s perfect for swimming, sunbathing, and relaxing.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Kota Bharu

Kota Bharu has several beautiful hiking and biking routes for adventurous travelers, including:

  • Jelawang Jungle Trek - A challenging trail that takes hikers through lush rainforests and offers panoramic views of Gunung Stong State Park.
  • Kelantan River Cycling Trail - A scenic cycling trail with gentle slopes that follows the banks of the Kelantan River, offering a glimpse into the daily life of the local people.
  • Kampung Laut Coastal Walk - A leisurely walk that takes visitors along the stunning coastline of Kota Bharu, with great sunset views and photo opportunities.

Best Things to Do in Kota Bharu: Activities and Tours

Apart from visiting the museums, temples, and natural attractions, here are some other fun things to do in Kota Bharu:

  • Explore the Night Markets - Kota Bharu is known for its vibrant night markets, where you can try the local street food, shop for souvenirs, and soak up the lively atmosphere.
  • Learn Traditional Malay Cooking - Experience the flavors of Kelantan by joining a cooking class that teaches traditional Malay recipes, such as curry puffs and nasi kerabu.
  • Join a Batik Painting Workshop - Learn about the art of batik printing, a traditional textile technique that’s unique to Southeast Asia, and create your own masterpiece to take home.
  • Take a River Cruise - See Kota Bharu from a different perspective by taking a leisurely river cruise and observe local village life along the banks.

Week-Long Itinerary for Kota Bharu

For a hassle-free week-long itinerary, here’s a sample program that covers the top attractions and activities:

  • Day 1 - Explore the City: Istana Jahar, Kampung Laut Mosque, Central Market, and the Royal Museum.
  • Day 2 - Nature Day Trip: Gunung Stong State Park and several hiking trails.
  • Day 3 - Leisurely Day: Pantai Cahaya Bulan Beach, relaxing and beach activities.
  • Day 4 - Tour the Countryside: Visit the Kota Bharu Countryside and local villages.
  • Day 5 - Cultural Immersion: Take a batik workshop and Malay cooking class.
  • Day 6 - Night Market Adventure: Experience the vibrant nightlife in Kota Bharu’s night markets.
  • Day 7 - Departure

Food and Drink Scene of Kota Bharu

No trip to Kota Bharu is complete without trying its delicious food and drinks. Here are some dishes and beverages you must try:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Nasi Kerabu - A unique rice dish with blue color, flavored with herbs and spices, served with a variety of sides.
  • Laksam - A popular local dish made of rolled rice noodles, served with rich coconut gravy and vegetables.
  • Ayam Percik - Grilled chicken marinated with a blend of spices and served with peanut sauce.
  • Kuih Muih - Assorted Malaysian sweets, made of glutinous rice, palm sugar, and coconut milk.

Local Beverages

  • Sirap Selaseh - A refreshing drink made of pandan leaves and basil seeds, sweetened with sugar syrup.
  • Kopi and Teh Tarik - Two classic Malaysian beverages served with condensed milk and hot tea or coffee.
  • Pulut Durian - A traditional dessert made of glutinous rice flavored with durian, a pungent fruit native to Southeast Asia.

Where to Stay in Kota Bharu: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Kota Bharu has a wide range of accommodations to suit all budgets and preferences. Here are some options:

  • Grand Riverview Hotel - A luxury hotel located by the river, with spacious rooms, swimming pool, and spa facilities.
  • Idaman Guesthouse - A comfortable guesthouse with charming family-style rooms and affordable prices.
  • The Room Hotel - A modern boutique hotel with stylishly furnished rooms and a central location in the city.

What to Buy in Kota Bharu: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Kota Bharu is known for its handicrafts, textiles, and traditional artifacts. Here are some souvenirs and local products to buy:

  • Songket Fabric - A hand-woven local fabric made of silk or cotton, with intricate patterns and designs.
  • Wooden Crafts - Hand-carved wooden items, such as traditional Malay houses, masks, and figurines.
  • Keropok Lekor - A popular snack made of fish paste, dried and fried as a crispy roll, and often served with dipping sauce.
  • Seri Mahkota Durian - A premium durian chocolate, made from high-quality durian and cocoa butter.

Local Culture and Customs of Kota Bharu

Kota Bharu is a conservative city, and visitors should respect local culture and customs. Here are some tips:

  • Dress modestly and avoid skimpy clothing, especially when visiting temples or mosques.
  • Remove your shoes before entering any religious or private establishments.
  • Ask for permission before taking photos of locals, especially women.
  • Do not consume alcohol or food during the month of Ramadan, as a sign of respect to Muslim locals.

Best Time to Visit Kota Bharu

The best time to visit Kota Bharu is from March to September when the weather is dry and sunny, and the temperature ranges from 25-30°C. October to February is also a good time to visit, with slightly cooler weather and occasional rainfall.

Do’s and Don’ts of Kota Bharu

Do:

  • Respect the local culture and customs.
  • Try local food and drinks.
  • Learn some basic Malay phrases.
  • Haggling is common in street markets.

Don’t:

  • Wear revealing clothing.
  • Public displays of affection are not allowed.
  • Touch or pose with animals, such as monitor lizards and turtles found in the markets.

Safety and Health

Safety in Kota Bharu

Kota Bharu is generally a safe and friendly city. However, visitors should be careful of pickpockets and petty theft in crowded areas, such as night markets and bus stations. Avoid walking alone at night in secluded areas.

Health in Kota Bharu

Kota Bharu has adequate medical facilities and pharmacies. Visitors should take appropriate measures against mosquito-borne diseases, such as dengue and malaria, and drink only bottled or boiled water.

Getting to Kota Bharu: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Kota Bharu

The nearest airport to Kota Bharu is Sultan Ismail Petra Airport. There are several daily flights connecting Kota Bharu to Kuala Lumpur, Penang, and other major cities in Malaysia.

Bus Stations Near Kota Bharu

There are several bus stations in Kota Bharu, serving destinations within the city and throughout Peninsular Malaysia. The most popular bus terminals are Kota Bharu Bus Terminal and Mydin Mall Bus Terminal.

Other Options

Visitors can also rent a car from the airport or city center, or take a taxi to get around Kota Bharu.

How to Get Around in Kota Bharu

Kota Bharu has several transportation options, such as:

  • Taxis - The most convenient way to get around Kota Bharu, which can be easily hailed from the street or booked through mobile apps.
  • Buses - A cheap and efficient way to travel around Kota Bharu, with regular services to major destinations and attractions.
  • Trishaws - Traditional bicycle rickshaws, which offer a unique and eco-friendly way to explore the city.

Where to Go Next from Kota Bharu: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Kota Bharu is an excellent base for exploring the surrounding area of Kelantan and other nearby destinations, such as:

  • Perhentian Islands - A stunning archipelago of islands with crystal-clear waters and white-sand beaches.
  • Taman Negara National Park - A vast rainforest reserve and the oldest national park in Malaysia with rich biodiversity and wildlife.
  • Cameron Highlands - A scenic hill station with cool climate and rolling tea plantations.

Conclusion

Kota Bharu is a unique and fascinating destination that offers a glimpse into the rich cultural heritage and natural beauty of Malaysia. With its mix of museums, historical sites, outdoor adventures, and local delicacies, there’s something for everyone to enjoy. So, pack your bags, and head to Kota Bharu for an unforgettable travel experience.
