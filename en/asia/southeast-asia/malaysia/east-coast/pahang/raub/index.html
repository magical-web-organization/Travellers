Introduction to Raub

Nestled in the heart of Pahang, Raub is a quaint town that boasts awe-inspiring natural landscapes and rich cultural heritage. Known as the durian capital of Malaysia, Raub boasts dense forests, tranquil lakes, and beautiful waterfalls. The food is heavenly, and the people are friendly. Whether you are looking for outdoor adventures or a cultural experience, Raub has something for everyone.

List of Places to Visit in Raub

Here are the top places to visit in Raub:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Pahang State Museum: A must-visit for history buffs, the Pahang State Museum showcases the rich cultural heritage and history of the region. The museum comprises four galleries that feature various exhibits and artifacts, including ancient weaponry, traditional costumes, and historical documents.
  • Laman Padi: This living museum offers visitors an opportunity to experience the various stages of rice cultivation and learn about the importance of paddy in the Malaysian diet.
  • Balai Besar: This majestic building is a reminder of Raub’s colonial past. Constructed in the late 19th century, the Balai Besar was once frequented by British officials and is now used for official events.

Natural Attractions of Raub: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Bukit Fraser: This stunning hill station offers a respite from the hustle and bustle of city life. Visitors can enjoy hiking trails, nature walks, and bird watching.
  • Chamang Waterfall: One of the most beautiful waterfalls in Pahang, the Chamang Waterfall offers a refreshing retreat for visitors. You can swim in the crystal-clear water or simply bask in the natural beauty that surrounds it.
  • Sungai Klah Hot Springs: Located in the foothills of the Titiwangsa Mountains, the Sungai Klah Hot Springs offer natural healing properties and a relaxing atmosphere. Visitors can soak in the various pools, ranging in temperature from 36°C to 46°C, and enjoy the surroundings.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Raub

Raub’s natural beauty lends itself well to outdoor adventures such as hiking and biking. Here are some of the best trails to explore:

  • Jeram Tengkek: This eight-kilometer trail is ideal for beginners and offers stunning views of the Titiwangsa Mountains.
  • Gunung Bunga Buah: A challenging hike, Gunung Bunga Buah is popular among experienced hikers. Visitors can expect to be rewarded with a panoramic view of the surrounding area.
  • Sungai Tahan Trail: A backpacker’s paradise, the Sungai Tahan Trail is a multi-day trek that takes hikers through dense forests, waterfalls, and rivers.

Best Things to Do in Raub: Activities and Tours

Whether you’re interested in exploring the natural beauty of Raub or getting a glimpse of local culture, there are plenty of activities and tours to choose from. Here are some of the best:

  • Fruit Farm Tours: Raub is known as the durian capital of Malaysia, and visitors can take a tour of the local fruit farms and indulge in some durian tasting.
  • Rafting Adventures: The rivers around Raub offer exciting rafting adventures for thrill-seekers.
  • Night Market: The Raub Night Market, held every Saturday, is a must-visit for foodies. You can sample a variety of local delicacies and soak in the vibrant atmosphere.

Week-Long Itinerary for Raub

Here is a sample itinerary for a week-long stay in Raub:

Day Activities
Day 1 Explore the Pahang State Museum and Laman Padi, and enjoy a traditional Malay lunch at a local eatery
Day 2 Trek up the Jeram Tengkek trail and enjoy a picnic lunch at Bukit Fraser
Day 3 Take a fruit farm tour and indulge in some durian tasting
Day 4 Rafting adventure in one of the rivers in Raub
Day 5 Visit the Sungai Klah Hot Springs and indulge in some relaxation time
Day 6 Explore the Chamang Waterfall and enjoy a traditional dinner at Raub Night Market

Food and Drink Scene of Raub

Raub’s food and drink scene is a delight for the senses. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Nasi Dagang: A dish made with fish curry and rice served with pickled vegetables and hard-boiled eggs.
  • Laksa: A spicy noodle soup made with fish and various spices
  • Koay Teow Kia: A noodle soup made with pork and a variety of herbs and spices.

Local Beverages

  • Teh Tarik: A sweetened milk tea that is a favourite among Malaysians.
  • Kopi O: A traditional, dark coffee served with sugar and condensed milk.
  • Air Batu Campur: This refreshing drink is made with shaved ice, syrup and various toppings including red beans, sweet corn, and palm seeds.

Where to Stay in Raub: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Raub caters to all kinds of travellers, ranging from luxurious hotels to budget-friendly guesthouses. Here are some top choices:

  • Hotel Jelai Raub: A luxurious hotel located in the heart of Raub. Offers modern amenities, including a swimming pool and fitness center.
  • Sri Pahang Hotel: This budget-friendly hotel is located close to the centre of town and offers comfortable rooms and breakfast services.
  • Koleq 88 Guest House: A guesthouse situated in a colonial building that offers a dormitory as well as private rooms.

What to Buy in Raub: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Raub is famous for its local delicacies and handicrafts. Here are some souvenirs and local products to bring home:

  • Durian-based products: Raub is known for its durians, and visitors can bring home durian-based products such as durian coffee and durian tarts.
  • Keropok: A traditional Malaysian snack made with fish or prawn and dried to a crisp.
  • Handicrafts: Visitors can bring home locally made handicrafts such as rattan baskets and wooden carvings.

Local Culture and Customs of Raub

Raub’s local culture is influenced by the Malay and Chinese communities. Visitors should be respectful of local customs, dress appropriately when visiting religious sites, and remove their footwear while entering homes. Visitors should also be mindful of local customs around greetings and avoid public displays of affection.

Best Time to Visit Raub

The best time to visit Raub is between December and February when the weather is cool and dry. Visitors should avoid visiting during the rainy season between April and November as the trails and natural attractions in the region may be closed due to safety concerns.

Do’s and Don’ts of Raub

Do:

  • Be respectful of local customs and traditions.
  • Dress appropriately when visiting religious sites.
  • Try local cuisine and indulge in some durian tasting.
  • Plan ahead if you want to trek, hike, or participate in outdoor activities.
  • Have small change in cash, as not all vendors accept credit/debit cards.

Don’t:

  • Public displays of affection are not common or appreciated.
  • Wear shoes inside homes or religious sites.
  • Disrespect local cultural norms and customs.
  • Litter, as Raub has a strong commitment to sustainable tourism.

Safety and Health

Safety in Raub

As with any destination, visitors should be mindful of their belongings and avoid walking alone at night in areas with low visibility. Be aware of the presence of monkeys in natural attractions that may snatch your food items.

Health in Raub

Visitors should be vaccinated against Hepatitis A and B as well as Typhoid before travelling. It is best to carry mosquito repellent if you visit during the rainy season.

Getting to Raub: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Raub

The Kuala Lumpur International Airport (KLIA) is the closest airport to Raub, located approximately 150 kilometres away.

Bus Stations Near Raub

Visitors can take a bus to Raub from the Terminal Bersepadu Selatan (TBS) in Kuala Lumpur.

Other Options

Those who prefer to drive can rent a car from Kuala Lumpur. There is also the option of taking a taxi or Grab ride from any major city in Malaysia to Raub.

How to Get Around in Raub

The best way to explore Raub is by car or motorbike. Visitors can hire a car or motorbike from a rental service in town. Taxis are also available but are relatively expensive.

Where to Go Next from Raub: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Raub’s proximity to other popular destinations makes it a perfect base to explore other parts of Pahang. Here are some nearby destinations to consider:

  • Taman Negara: One of the oldest tropical rainforests in the world, Taman Negara is known for its natural treasures. Visitors can trek, hike, or take a river safari to explore the park’s rich biodiversity.
  • Genting Highlands: A popular highland resort approximately 70 km away from Raub, Genting Highlands offers casinos, theme parks, and shopping malls.
  • Frasers Hill: Also known as Bukit Fraser, Frasers Hill is a hill station that offers visitors a respite from the hot and humid lowlands. Visitors can explore the bird sanctuary and enjoy scenic views.

Conclusion

Raub is a hidden gem in the state of Pahang, offering breathtaking natural landscapes and a rich cultural heritage. With something for every kind of traveller, Raub is a destination that should be on every travel enthusiast’s list. Indulge in local delicacies, explore the natural beauty of the region, and immerse yourself in the local culture with this comprehensive travel guide.
