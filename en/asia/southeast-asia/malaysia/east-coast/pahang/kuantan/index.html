Introduction to Kuantan

Kuantan is the largest city in the state of Pahang, located on the east coast of Peninsular Malaysia. It is a popular destination for domestic and international tourists due to its beautiful beaches, cultural attractions, and outdoor activities.

There are many things to do and see in Kuantan, which makes it a great option for a serene getaway for solo travelers, couples, and families with kids.

This article will help you plan your trip to Kuantan, including a list of must-visit places, hiking routes, best things to do, itinerary, food and drinks, accommodation options, local culture and customs, safety and health concerns, transportation options, and nearby destinations.

List of Places to Visit in Kuantan

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  1. Masjid Negeri Sultan Ahmad Shah - Also known as the State Mosque, this iconic landmark is a popular tourist spot and is known for its stunning Islamic architecture.
  2. Sultan Abu Bakar Museum - A restored palace turned museum that showcases the cultural heritage and history of Kuantan.
  3. Kuantan Art Gallery - This is an excellent spot to view and purchase traditional Malay artwork, and handmade crafts.

Natural Attractions of Kuantan: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  1. Teluk Chempedak Beach - A popular spot for tourists and locals, this beach has crystal-clear water and an array of water sports activities, making it a perfect spot to spend an entire day.
  2. Taman Esplanade Kuantan - This tranquil park has an easy walking trail and offers an excellent place for a morning jog.
  3. Kuantan River Cruises - A riverboat ride through the picturesque Kuantan River, which offers stunning views of the mangroves, local wildlife, and traditional fishing villages.
  4. Bukit Gambang Water Park - This water park has the largest wave pool in Southeast Asia and boasts an array of exciting water rides for all ages.
  5. Berjaya Hills - Nestled amidst the lush rainforest, Berjaya Hills Resort is an ecotourism destination and offers a pristine rainforest as your backdrop while playing golf, cycling among other outdoor adventures and visiting the Japanese Village.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Kuantan

Kuantan offers a plethora of hiking and biking opportunities that cater to hikers and cyclists’ different skill levels. Here are our top picks:

  1. Pelindung Hill Trail - This trail offers a stunning view of Kuantan’s skyline and renowned for its best sunrise views. A manageable 40-minute hike from the foot of the hill.
  2. Bukit Pelindung Forest Reserve Trail - A haven for travelers looking for an adventurous and active experience. Hikers trek through rainforests, waterfalls, and caves, making it an ideal family activity.
  3. Sungai Pandan Waterfall Trail - An extension of the Kuantan River, a short drive from the city center, has a beautiful waterfall that offers a refreshing swim after the hike.

Best Things to Do in Kuantan: Activities and Tours

  1. Kuantan Sunset River Cruise - Enjoy a leisurely riverboat ride during sunset, where you can watch spectacular hues of orange, gold, and red in the sky.
  2. Fishing Charters - Experience the thrill of deep-sea fishing by booking a fishing charter or joining a group boat tour.
  3. Kuantan River Cruise - A riverboat ride through the picturesque Kuantan River
  4. Mangrove Tour of Sungai Sepat - Explore the mangrove swamp, home to a variety of wildlife, group tours, and specific packages guide tourists this tour.
  5. Kuantan Food Tour - Try the famous local cuisines of Kuantan, a perfect way to have a memorable culinary adventure!

Week-Long Itinerary for Kuantan

Here is our suggested week-long itinerary for Kuantan:

Day 1 - Visit the Masjid Negeri Sultan Ahmad Shah and Sultan Abu Bakar Museum. In the evening, join the Kuantan River Cruise for a peaceful riverboat ride.

Day 2 - Spend a day on the Teluk Chempedak Beach. Enjoy a variety of water sports activities, local food, and magnificent views in the evening.

Day 3 - Trek Pelindung Hill and have breakfast on top of the hill. Afterwards, head to Berjaya Hills Resort region and spend the rest of the day exploring the Japanese Village, near the famous Resort.

Day 4 - From beaches to jungles today, visit the Sungai Pandan Waterfall trail and enjoy a serene afternoon swimming in the freshwater falls and be surrounded by nature’s calming sounds.

Day 5 - Head to the Bukit Pelindung Forest Reserve Trail, complete the trek, and head to Kuantan Food Tour for the best food experience in town.

Day 6 - Go fishing by joining a group or booking a private fishing charter experience in Kuantan’s deep sea waters.

Day 7 - Wrap up your trip with a relaxing mangrove tour at Sungai Sepat, A tranquil boat ride through the mangrove, known for its untouched nature and wildlife sightings.

Food and Drink Scene of Kuantan

Must-Try Dishes

  1. Nasi Lemak - Rice cooked in coconut milk, served with crispy fried chicken, and spicy sauce.
  2. Satay - Grilled meat skewers served with a peanut sauce dip
  3. Ikan Bakar Petai - Grilled seafood such as stingrays or fish with petai (stink beans) in a marinade of tamarind and chillies
  4. Laksa - A spicy noodle soup made with fish or shrimps broth, sour spices, and dried chili paste.
  5. Keropok Lekor - A fish cracker snack enjoyed with spicy chili sauce.

Local Beverages

  1. Teh Tarik - Also known as Pulled Tea. A popular Malaysian sweet milky tea, stretched between two pots by a skilled tea-maker.
  2. Air Mata Kucing - A sweet drink made from the fruit of the Longan tree, which has a mild lychee-like flavor.
  3. Cendol - A sweet icy drink served with coconut milk, sweetened red beans, and pandan-flavored jelly noodles.

Where to Stay in Kuantan: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Kuantan offers many accommodation options for travelers that cater to every budget, from top-end resorts to budget-friendly guesthouses.

Luxury / Resorts

  1. The Zenith Hotel - One of the best hotels in town with luxurious, modern amenities.
  2. Hyatt Regency Kuantan Resort - A luxurious resort located adjacent to Teluk Chempedak Beach.

Budget-Friendly / Hostels

  1. Qube Hotel - A budget hotel locate in the city center who offers a great location and accommodating staff.
  2. CitiHoTel Comfortable Stay - located in the city of Kuantan, a budget-friendly hotel, offers clean rooms and accommodating staff.

What to Buy in Kuantan: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Kuantan is well- known for its handicrafts made by local artisans. Here are our top picks for souvenirs to buy:

  1. Woven Baskets - Handcrafted baskets made from local materials such as bamboo and rattan make a great souvenir for yourself or as a gift.
  2. Traditional Malay Masks - These masks depict characters from Malaysian folklore and make a unique and colorful addition to any home.
  3. Batik Fabrics - Batik is a traditional technique of dyeing and printing fabrics, and Kuantan is known for its batik prints and designs.
  4. Wood Carvings - Wooden figurines and sculptures crafted from local woods such as teak, rosewood make for an excellent souvenir to bring back home.

Local Culture and Customs of Kuantan

Kuantan is a Muslim town, and it’s important to respect local customs and be mindful of the dress code while traveling around the city. Shorts and sleeveless tops may not be suitable for some cultural sites, so it’s better to wear a cover-up in such cases.

Best Time to Visit Kuantan

The best time to visit Kuantan is between March to October as the weather is dry during these months. The monsoon season spans from November to February, which might interfere with outdoor activities and exploring nature.

Do’s and Don’ts of Kuantan

Do:

  1. Respect the local culture and customs.
  2. Sample the delicious local cuisine and try the fresh seafood.
  3. Use sunscreen and take plenty of water to stay hydrated while enjoying outdoor adventures.
  4. Bargain when shopping at local markets and craft shops.

Don’t:

  1. Engage in public display of affection, especially during the holy month of Ramadan.
  2. Refuse food or drink offered as a sign of hospitality from locals.
  3. Disrespect the local authorities and their customs.
  4. Wear revealing clothing in public spaces.

Safety and Health

Safety in Kuantan

Like any other city, tourists must be careful and take necessary safety precautions to ensure a safe and enjoyable trip to Kuantan. Avoid traveling alone after dark and always keep your valuables close.

Health in Kuantan

The tap water in Kuantan is not recommended for drinking, so it is best to stick to bottled water or filtered water. Mosquitoes are prevalent, so it’s advisable to wear loose-fitting clothing and use mosquito repellent.

Getting to Kuantan: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Kuantan

Kuantan is approx. 30 minutes drive from Sultan Ahmad Shah Airport (KUA), accessible by a taxi ride, private transfers, or rental cars.

Bus Stations Near Kuantan

Kuantan is accessible by bus from key cities in Malaysia. The main bus terminal is located at Terminal Sentral Kuantan.

Other Options

Car rentals and taxis are a common option to commute around the city, and reliable services are readily available for tourists to use.

How to Get Around in Kuantan

Kuantan is relatively small and is easy to navigate around on foot or using public transportation. Taxis and buses are readily available for tourists to commute.

Where to Go Next from Kuantan: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

If you’re looking to explore more of Malaysia, here are some great destinations that are easily accessible from Kuantan:

  1. Tioman Island - This island in the South China Sea is known for its stunning beaches, crystal-clear waters, coral reefs, and popular for diving and snorkeling.
  2. Cherating Beach - Located 45 minutes north of Kuantan, this laid-back beach with clear waters and white sands is a popular holiday destination for travelers.
  3. Fraser’s Hill - Nestled in the Titiwangsa Mountains, a two-hour drive from Kuantan, this hill resort is a perfect escape for those who love nature, enjoy trekking, and bird-watching.
  4. Lake Chini - Located about an hour’s drive from Kuantan, and is the second-largest freshwater lake in Malaysia and is a popular place for kayaking, fishing, cruising, and bird watching.

Conclusion

Kuantan is a beautiful city with many things to offer for tourists of various preferences. Whether you’re an adventure-seeker, history buff, water sports enthusiast, or nature lover, Kuantan has something for you.
