Introduction to Pekan

Pekan is a small and charming town located in Pahang, on the east coast of Malaysia, Southeast Asia. Known as the Royal Town of Pahang, Pekan is rich in culture and history and offers visitors a glimpse into the traditions and customs of Malaysia. The town is located on the banks of the Pahang River and is surrounded by lush tropical rainforests, making it an ideal destination for nature lovers. In this guide, we will take you on a journey of discovery, exploring the highlights of Pekan and providing you with all the information you need to plan your trip.

List of Places to Visit in Pekan

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Pekan offers visitors a wealth of cultural attractions to explore. One of the most popular attractions is the Istana Abu Bakar Royal Palace, which was built in 1902 and served as the residence of the Sultan of Pahang until 1955. Visitors can take a guided tour to learn more about the history and architecture of the palace.

Another must-visit attraction is the Sultan Abu Bakar Museum, which was once a palace and is now a museum that showcases the history and culture of Pekan and the surrounding area. The museum is home to a collection of royal regalia, artifacts, and exhibits detailing the life and reign of the sultans of Pahang.

If you’re interested in learning more about the local culture and traditions, the Pekan Heritage Museum is the perfect place to start. The museum offers visitors a glimpse into the daily life of the local people, including their customs, clothing, and beliefs.

Natural Attractions of Pekan: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

For outdoor enthusiasts and nature lovers, Pekan offers a wide range of natural attractions. The Sungai Pahang River, which runs through the town, is a popular spot for river activities such as fishing, kayaking, and rafting.

The nearby Endau-Rompin National Park is a pristine rainforest that is home to a diverse range of flora and fauna. Visitors can explore the park’s trails, waterfalls, and caves, or take a guided tour to learn more about the local wildlife.

For stunning panoramic views of Pekan and the surrounding area, head to Bukit Pelindung, a picturesque hilltop that overlooks the town and the Pahang River.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Pekan

Pekan and the surrounding area offer a variety of hiking and biking trails for all skill levels. One of the most popular trails is the Sungai Semantan Tropical Forest Trail, which winds through the Endau-Rompin National Park and offers breathtaking views of the rainforest and its inhabitants.

Another popular option is the Bukit Pelindung Hiking Trail, which takes visitors on a steep climb to the top of the hill, offering spectacular views of Pekan and the surrounding area.

Best Things to Do in Pekan: Activities and Tours

In addition to exploring the town’s cultural and natural attractions, there are many other activities and tours to enjoy in Pekan.

Visitors can take a river tour of the Pahang River, stopping at local fishing villages and other points of interest along the way. You can also experience the thrill of whitewater rafting on the rapids of the Sungai Pahang.

For a taste of local culture, visitors can participate in traditional activities such as batik painting and kite making. Or, you can take a cooking class and learn how to make traditional Malay dishes.

Week-Long Itinerary for Pekan

Day 1: Visit the Istana Abu Bakar Royal Palace and the Sultan Abu Bakar Museum Day 2: Explore the Pekan Heritage Museum and take a river tour of the Pahang River Day 3: Go whitewater rafting on the rapids of the Sungai Pahang Day 4: Hike the Bukit Pelindung Hiking Trail and visit the Bukit Pelindung Scenic Area Day 5: Take a cooking class and learn how to make traditional Malay dishes Day 6: Explore the Endau-Rompin National Park and its waterfalls and caves Day 7: Enjoy local culture by participating in traditional activities such as batik painting and kite making.

Food and Drink Scene of Pekan

Must-Try Dishes

Pekan is known for its delicious and unique cuisine which offers a blend of Malay, Chinese, and Indian influences. One of the most popular dishes is Nasi Dagang, which is rice cooked in coconut milk and served with fish curry, pickled vegetables, and hard-boiled eggs.

Another must-try dish is the traditional Malay breakfast dish, Nasi Lemak, which consists of coconut rice, crispy anchovies, peanuts, cucumber, and a spicy sambal sauce.

Local Beverages

For a taste of local beverages, try Teh Tarik, a sweet and creamy tea that is a popular morning beverage. Another unique beverage to try is Sirap Bandung, a rose-flavored milk drink that is sweet and refreshing.

Where to Stay in Pekan: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Pekan offers a variety of accommodation options to suit all budgets and preferences. For a luxury experience, stay at the Zenith Hotel, located in the heart of Pekan and featuring world-class amenities.

There are also many budget-friendly options, including guesthouses and hostels such as the Pekan Backpackers Hostel and the NUR Guesthouse.

What to Buy in Pekan: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

When it comes to souvenirs and local products to bring home, Pekan offers a range of options. One of the most popular items is traditional Malay artwork and handicrafts such as batik fabrics and woodcarvings.

You can also find local products such as traditional Malay sweets like kuih and dodol, which make great gifts for friends and family.

Local Culture and Customs of Pekan

Pekan is known for its rich cultural traditions and customs. One of the most prominent customs is the royal culture, which can be seen in the architecture and history of the town. Visitors are encouraged to respect local customs and observe the dress code when visiting religious sites.

Best Time to Visit Pekan

The best time to visit Pekan is between April and October, which is the dry season. During this time, the weather is mild and pleasant, making it perfect for outdoor activities and sightseeing.

Do’s and Don’ts of Pekan

Do:

  • Dress modestly when visiting religious sites
  • Respect local customs and traditions
  • Learn a few words of Malay to communicate with locals

Don’t:

  • Refuse food or drink if offered by locals
  • Disrespect the royal culture and customs
  • Engage in public displays of affection

Safety and Health

Safety in Pekan

Pekan is generally a safe place to visit, with low crime rates. However, visitors are advised to take standard safety precautions such as not carrying large amounts of cash, avoiding walking alone at night in unfamiliar areas, and locking valuables in hotel safes.

Health in Pekan

Visitors are advised to drink only bottled water, avoid street food if you have a sensitive stomach, and take precautions against mosquito-borne illnesses such as dengue fever.

Getting to Pekan: Transportation Options and Logistics

Pekan is well-connected to other parts of Malaysia via a variety of transportation options. The nearest airport is Sultan Haji Ahmad Shah Airport, which is located in the nearby city of Kuantan.

There are also several bus stations near Pekan, providing connections to other cities throughout Malaysia. For those looking for a more leisurely option, taking a river cruise is an excellent way to explore the area and arrive in Pekan in style.

How to Get Around in Pekan

Getting around Pekan is easy and affordable. The town is small enough to explore on foot, or you can rent a bicycle or motorbike to get around. Taxis and ride-sharing services are also available for those who prefer to travel by car.

Where to Go Next from Pekan: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Pekan offers visitors a range of nearby destinations and excursions to explore. One of the most popular is Kuantan, which is home to beautiful beaches and a vibrant nightlife.

Other nearby attractions include the Cameron Highlands, a scenic hill station with tea plantations and strawberry farms, and the Taman Negara National Park, a pristine rainforest that is home to a variety of wildlife.

Conclusion

Pekan is a hidden gem in Malaysia, offering visitors a unique blend of culture, history, and natural beauty. Whether you’re interested in exploring the town’s cultural attractions, hiking through the rainforest, or simply relaxing by the river, Pekan has something for everyone. We hope this comprehensive guide has provided you with all the information you need to plan your trip and make the most of your time in Pekan.
