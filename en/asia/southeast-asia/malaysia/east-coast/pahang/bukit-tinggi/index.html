Introduction to Bukit Tinggi

Bukit Tinggi is a picturesque town located in the state of Pahang, on the East Coast of Malaysia. It offers a uniquely tranquil escape from the bustling cities of the region and is a wonderful place to visit for nature lovers, outdoor enthusiasts, and cultural explorers. The town is known for its lush landscapes, beautiful botanical gardens, and its unique blend of traditional Malay and Western architecture. Its close proximity to popular tourist spots like Kuala Lumpur and Genting Highlands makes it a fantastic destination for a day trip or a weekend getaway.

List of Places to Visit in Bukit Tinggi

Bukit Tinggi is home to numerous attractions, both natural and man-made. Here are some of the must-visit places in Bukit Tinggi.

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Colmar Tropicale: This charming French-themed village is a major attraction in Bukit Tinggi and is inspired by the quaint European villages found in the Alsatian region. Admire the picturesque architecture and explore the beautiful gardens, cafes, and shops.
  • Japanese Village: Built as a replica of a typical Japanese village, this attraction offers visitors a glimpse into the country’s traditional way of life and architectural styles. The village includes a teahouse, a Japanese garden, and a temple.
  • Bentong Gallery: Home to a vast collection of authentic and exquisite art and craft, the Bentong Gallery showcases traditional Malaysian arts and crafts as well as a range of modern and contemporary art.
  • Pusat Kraftangan: Showcasing the rich handicrafts of Pahang, the Pusat Kraftangan houses a collection of traditional Malaysian crafts, including weaving, ceramics, and woodcarving.

Natural Attractions of Bukit Tinggi: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Chrysanthemum Farm: Located in nearby Kuala Lumpur, this farm is home to stunning flower gardens with thousands of colorful chrysanthemums.
  • Bukit Tinggi Botanical Garden: A haven for nature lovers, this garden features an array of exotic plants and flowers, making it one of Bukit Tinggi’s most popular attractions. Enjoy a leisurely stroll and admire the scenery.
  • Berjaya Hills Golf and Country Club: Surrounded by stunning views of the tropical rainforests, this 18-hole golf course is a golfer’s paradise.
  • Bukit Tinggi Hill Trek: A challenging hike through lush vegetation, the Bukit Tinggi Hill Trek offers stunning views of the town and surrounding landscapes.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Bukit Tinggi

Bukit Tinggi offers a range of hiking and biking trails for nature enthusiasts and adventure seekers. Here are some of the best routes to explore while in Bukit Tinggi:

  • Berjaya Hills Trail: A moderate trek through lush rainforests, this trail is perfect for nature lovers and offers beautiful views of the surrounding landscapes.
  • Bukit Tinggi Hill Trek: A challenging hike through lush rainforests with beautiful views of the surrounding landscapes.
  • Genting Sempah to Bukit Tinggi Trail: This bike route takes riders through the lush tropical rainforest of Pahang, passing through villages and waterfalls along the way.

Best Things to Do in Bukit Tinggi: Activities and Tours

  • Indoor and Outdoor Activities: Berjaya Hills Golf & Country Club offers golfing and other outdoor activities and the self-contained resort offers indoor facilities including a game room that includes bowling, snooker, and pool tables.
  • Spa and Massage: Treat yourself to a relaxing spa day at the Colmar Tropicale Spa.
  • Cultural Tours: Take a tour of the Japanese Village to learn about the culture, architecture, and traditional customs of Japan.

Week-Long Itinerary for Bukit Tinggi

Here’s a suggested week-long itinerary for exploring Bukit Tinggi:

Day Activity
Day 1 Explore Colmar Tropicale, visit the Japanese Village
Day 2 Take a guided tour of the Pusat Kraftangan, continue to Berjaya Hills Golf & Country Club for golfing
Day 3 Take the Bukit Tinggi Hill Trek for scenic views of the town and surroundings
Day 4 Visit the Chrysanthemum Farm in Kuala Lumpur
Day 5 Relax at the Colmar Tropicale Spa and enjoy a massage
Day 6 Explore the Bukit Tinggi Botanical Garden
Day 7 Take time to enjoy the indoor facilities of Berjaya Hills Resort like bowling, snooker, and pool, and shopping for souvenirs in nearby Bentong

Food and Drink Scene of Bukit Tinggi

Bukit Tinggi offers a variety of local and international cuisine, reflecting the town’s diverse cultural influences. Here are some must-try dishes:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Nasi Lemak: A traditional Malay dish consisting of rice cooked in coconut milk and served with fried chicken, anchovies and peanuts.
  • Satay: Grilled skewers of meat served with peanut sauce
  • Laksa: A spicy noodle soup
  • Kimchi Soup: A spicy Korean soup

Local Beverages

  • Teh Tarik: A popular beverage in Malaysia, this sweet tea is mixed with condensed milk to create a foamy texture.
  • Sirap Bandung: A refreshing drink made with rose syrup and milk.

What to Buy in Bukit Tinggi: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Bukit Tinggi is known for its traditional Malaysian handicrafts and art. Visitors can buy a range of products, including:

  • Handwoven fabrics and garments, including batik and songket.
  • Ceramics and pottery
  • Woodcarvings: Especially the traditional masks used in cultural performances
  • Artwork and paintings depicting the landscapes and cultures of the region

Local Culture and Customs of Bukit Tinggi

Bukit Tinggi is a culturally rich town, home to a diversity of traditions, customs, and beliefs. Visitors should respect the local customs, religion, and dress code. In Islam, women are advised to wear modest clothing, and men should dress appropriately in public places.

Best Time to Visit Bukit Tinggi

Bukit Tinggi enjoys a tropical climate, with warm temperatures and occasional rainfall throughout the year. The best time to visit is between March to May and September to November, when the weather is drier and cooler.

Do’s and Don’ts of Bukit Tinggi

Do:

  • Respect local customs and traditions
  • Wear appropriate clothing in public places
  • Carry enough cash, as some establishments may not accept credit cards

Don’t:

  • Disrespect the local customs and traditions
  • Wear revealing clothing in public
  • Drink tap water, always carry bottled water

Safety and Health

Safety in Bukit Tinggi

Bukit Tinggi is a relatively safe destination for tourists. Visitors should take normal precautions, such as keeping their belongings safe and secure and avoiding walking alone at night in unlit areas.

Health in Bukit Tinggi

Visitors should take precautions against mosquito bites and stay hydrated due to the hot and humid weather. Visitors should also check with their doctors regarding their vaccination status and carry essential medications with them.

Getting to Bukit Tinggi: Transportation Options and Logistics

Bukit Tinggi is easily accessible by various modes of transportation.

Airports Near Bukit Tinggi

The closest airport to Bukit Tinggi is Sultan Abdul Aziz Shah Airport in Subang, which is approximately an hour’s drive away.

Bus Stations Near Bukit Tinggi

Pekeliling Bus Terminal in Kuala Lumpur is the closest bus station to Bukit Tinggi. Visitors can take a taxi or a bus from here to reach the town.

Other Options

Visitors can also rent a car or take a taxi to reach Bukit Tinggi from Kuala Lumpur.

How to Get Around in Bukit Tinggi

Visitors can easily get around using taxis, rental cars, or by biking and walking. Taxis are readily available in the town and offer an affordable mode of transport for short trips. Rental cars are an excellent option for exploring the surrounding area in comfort. Biking and walking are perfect for those who wish to explore at a leisurely pace.

Where to Go Next from Bukit Tinggi: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Bukit Tinggi is surrounded by other attractions and destinations that are worth exploring, including:

  • Genting Highlands: A popular hill resort located just a short drive from Bukit Tinggi.

  • Kuala Lumpur: The capital city of Malaysia is just an hour’s drive from Bukit Tinggi and offers a range of attractions, including the Petronas Twin Towers, Batu Caves, and Chinatown.

  • Fraser’s Hill: A hill station surrounded by lush rainforests, Fraser’s Hill is known for its diverse wildlife, bird watching, and nature trails.

Conclusion

Bukit Tinggi is a hidden gem located in the heart of Malaysia that is worth exploring. With its picturesque landscapes, cultural attractions, and wide range of activities, Bukit Tinggi is a perfect place to relax, unwind, and immerse oneself in the local customs and traditions.
