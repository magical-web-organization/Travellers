Introduction to East Coast

The East Coast of Malaysia is a stunning region that encompasses the states of Kelantan, Terengganu, and Pahang. Over the years, the area has become a popular destination for tourists, thanks to its crystal-clear waters, beautiful beaches, and cultural landmarks.

Whether you’re interested in exploring the region’s rich history or just want to soak up the sun on a sandy beach, the East Coast of Malaysia has something to offer everyone. In this comprehensive tour guide, we’ll take a closer look at the best places to visit, things to do, and where to stay in the East Coast.

Local Culture and Customs of East Coast

The East Coast of Malaysia has rich cultural traditions, and it’s important for visitors to respect local customs. Handshakes are the most common form of greeting, and it’s best to dress conservatively, especially when visiting a mosque.

Best Time to Visit East Coast

The best time to visit the East Coast of Malaysia is between March and October, when the weather is mostly dry and sunny. However, visitors should keep in mind that as the area has a tropical climate, sudden rains and storms may occur any time of the year.

Do’s and Don’ts of East Coast

Do:

  • Learn a few phrases in Bahasa Melayu to communicate with locals.
  • Dress modestly when visiting mosques or religious sites.
  • Follow cultural etiquette, like removing your shoes before entering a home.

Don’t:

  • Disrespect local customs and beliefs.
  • Carry too much cash, especially in secluded areas.
  • Litter or harm the environment.

Safety and Health

Safety in East Coast

The East Coast of Malaysia is safe for tourists, though it’s advisable to keep valuables and cash secure. When travelling by taxi or car, avoid flagging down unlicensed vehicles. Visitors should also be cautious while trekking and hiking, especially during the rainy season.

Health in East Coast

Travellers should carry basic medication and personal care products for common illnesses. It’s essential to carry mosquito repellents and cover up to protect against mosquito-borne diseases like dengue fever.
