Introduction to Malaysia

Malaysia is a multicultural country in Southeast Asia, bordered by Thailand, Singapore, and Indonesia. The country is known for its diverse culture, stunning natural landscapes, and vibrant cities. Exploring Malaysia is a unique experience, with visitors enjoying a mix of colonial architecture, lush rainforests, beautiful beaches, and delicious cuisine. Our comprehensive tour guide for Malaysia will give you an insider’s glimpse of everything the country has to offer.

List of Places to Visit in Malaysia

Malaysia is a tourist’s paradise with so many fascinating places to visit. Here is a list of the top places to visit in Malaysia.

Malaysia is known for its rich history and culture, which is reflected in its museums and historical sites. Some of the popular cultural attractions and historical places in Malaysia include:

  • National Museum of Malaysia
  • Penang Museum and Art Gallery
  • Islamic Arts Museum Malaysia
  • Georgetown Historic Center
  • Malacca Historic City

Local Culture and Customs of Malaysia

Malaysia is a multicultural country with diverse ethnic groups such as Malays, Chinese and Indians staying together. Understanding the local culture and customs is essential to make your trip hassle-free. It is customary to remove your shoes before entering someone’s home or temple, and it is respectful to dress modestly and conservatively. It is also polite to greet anyone you meet in Malaysia, and the traditional greeting is “Selamat Datang”.

Best Time to Visit Malaysia

The best time to visit Malaysia is between March and October, when the weather is dry and sunny. However, it can be crowded during public holidays and festivals.

Do’s and Don’ts of Malaysia

Do:

  • Dress modestly
  • Remove shoes before entering temples and mosques
  • Respect the local culture and customs
  • Greet people with “Selamat datang”

Don’t:

  • Disrespect the royal family or religion
  • Smoke in prohibited areas
  • Litter in public areas

Safety and Health

Safety in Malaysia

Malaysia is generally a safe country to visit. Violent crimes are relatively rare, but petty crimes like pickpocketing and bag snatching can occur in crowded areas. It is advisable to avoid political gatherings and protests happening across the country.

Health in Malaysia

Malaysia has good healthcare facilities and medical clinics across the country. It is advisable to have comprehensive travel insurance before your trip. Do not drink tap water and check if the restaurant meets cleanliness standards before having food there.
