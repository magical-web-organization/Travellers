Introduction to Tawau

Nestled in Sabah, Malaysia, Tawau is a hidden gem of Southeast Asia. Once known primarily as a center for the timber industry, today, Tawau is a vibrant city that attracts visitors from all over the world with its beautiful natural landscapes, delicious cuisine, and rich cultural heritage. Here, visitors can explore everything from pristine rainforests and stunning beaches to fascinating museums, local markets, and hiking trails galore. This comprehensive travel guide will help you get the most out of your trip to Tawau, providing recommendations for things to do, see, eat, and experience during your visit.

List of Places to Visit in Tawau

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

No trip to Tawau would be complete without exploring some of the city’s intriguing museums and historical sites. Here are a few of the best:

Tawau Japanese War Memorial

The Tawau Japanese War Memorial is a somber but fascinating tribute to the lives lost during World War II. Visitors can explore the museum’s collections of photographs, artifacts, and other memorabilia related to the occupation of Sabah by Japanese forces.

The Teck Guan Cocoa Museum

Chocolate lovers won’t want to miss a visit to the Teck Guan Cocoa Museum. Here, visitors can learn all about the chocolate making process, from the growth of the cocoa plant to the final product. And of course, there are plenty of free samples to savor!

The Tawau Bell Tower

The Tawau Bell Tower is a historical landmark that was built in 1912. The tower has been designated as a heritage site by the Sabah Museum and is a must-visit spot for anyone interested in local history and architecture.

Natural Attractions of Tawau: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

One of the main reasons visitors come to Tawau is to explore the city’s incredible natural landscapes. Here are some top natural attractions to check out:

Tawau Hills Park

Tawau Hill Park is a vast nature reserve that is home to an abundance of exotic wildlife, including pythons and orangutans. The park also features several hiking trails that wind through lush rainforest, providing visitors with breathtaking views of the surrounding landscape.

Visit the Maliau Basin Conservation Area (MBCA)

The Maliau Basin Conservation Area is a pristine wilderness that has been designated a protected area by the Malaysian government. Visitors can explore the park’s dense jungle, cascading waterfalls, and unique wildlife - it’s a once-in-a-lifetime experience.

Sipadan Island

Sipadan Island, a part of the Turtle Islands Park an hour’s distance away from Tawau, offers pristine diving and snorkeling opportunities for sea lovers. The island is abundant with marine life, making it a must-visit attraction for those who are fascinated by marine creatures.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Tawau

Tawau is a haven for outdoor enthusiasts, and there are plenty of hiking and biking routes in and around the city worth exploring. Here are some of the most popular routes

Gunung Magdalena

Gunung Magdalena is the highest peak near Tawau, standing at an elevation of 1,310 meters. The hike to the summit is challenging but rewarding, offering visitors breathtaking views of the surrounding forests and mountain ranges.

Sungai Kunjungan Nature Trail

The Sungai Kunjungan Nature Trail is a relaxing hike that is perfect for families. Visitors can trek through the lush rainforest along the river, a sight to behold.

Best Things to Do in Tawau: Activities and Tours

There is no shortage of fun things to do in Tawau. Whether you are an adrenaline junkie or a history buff, there’s something for everyone. Here are some top picks:

Visit the Tawau Market

The Tawau Market is a vibrant hub of activity, offering visitors the chance to shop for local handicrafts, fashion, and souvenirs at bargain prices.

Snorkeling at Tun Sakaran Marine Park

For those who love to snorkel, Tun Sakaran Marine Park is a paradise. The park is home to a stunning array of marine creatures, including manta rays, sharks, and sea turtles.

Catch the Sunset at Sim-Sim Water Village

Heading to Sim-Sim Water Village to catch the sunset is a must-do activity for visitors to Tawau. The village, which is built entirely on stilts over the water, offers a beautiful and unique view of the sun setting over the bay.

Week-Long Itinerary for Tawau

To make the most of your week-long visit to Tawau, we recommend the following itinerary:

Day 1-2: Explore the natural beauty of Tawau Hills Park, Maliau Basin Conservation Area, and Gunung Magdalena.

Day 3: Visit the Tawau Japanese War Memorial, Teck Guan Cocoa Museum, and Tawau Bell Tower.

Day 4-5: Go snorkeling or scuba diving at Sipadan Island.

Day 6: Explore the Tawau Market and try some local cuisine.

Day 7: Catch the sunset at Sim-Sim Water Village.

Food and Drink Scene of Tawau

No visit to Tawau would be complete without trying some of the delicious local cuisine. Here are some must-try dishes and beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

Tawau Loh Mee

Tawau Loh Mee is a local specialty that consists of thick noodle soup served with seafood, meat, and vegetables. The dish has a unique savory flavor and is a must-try for anyone visiting Tawau.

Udang Galah

Udang Galah, or giant river prawns, are a Tawau delicacy that should not be missed. These prawns are cooked in a variety of ways, but the most popular method is to steam them with garlic and ginger - delicious!

Local Beverages

Tawau Coffee

Tawau is famous for its coffee, which is grown locally and has a unique nutty flavor. Visitors can sample coffee in cafes all over the city.

Teh Tarik

Teh tarik, or “pulled tea,” is a popular Malaysian beverage made by mixing hot milk and tea leaves together in a process that creates a frothy, silky texture.

Where to Stay in Tawau: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

There is no shortage of accommodation options in Tawau, ranging from budget-friendly hostels to high-end hotels. Some of the best include:

Heritage Hotel Tawau

Heritage Hotel Tawau is a highly-rated mid-range hotel that offers comfortable rooms, free WiFi, and a swimming pool.

Promenade Hotel Tawau

Promenade Hotel Tawau is a luxury hotel that features an outdoor pool, spa, and fitness center, making it the perfect choice for those looking for a bit of indulgence during their visit.

What to Buy in Tawau: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Tawau is known for its handicrafts, traditional textiles such as Songket, and exotic fruits from the nearby plantations. A visit to the Tawau Market is a great place to find souvenirs to take home.

Local Culture and Customs of Tawau

Tawau has a rich and diverse cultural heritage, and visitors are sure to encounter local traditions and customs during their stay.

Best Time to Visit Tawau

The best time to visit Tawau is during the dry season, which runs from April to October. The weather is mild and comfortable during these months, making it the ideal time for outdoor activities like hiking and snorkeling.

Do’s and Don’ts of Tawau

Do:

  • Respect local customs and traditions
  • Cover yourself respectfully while visiting mosques and other religious sites
  • Try the local cuisine

Don’t:

  • Disrespect or litter at any of the natural bodies and landscapes
  • Wear revealing clothing in public areas
  • Engage in illegal activities

Safety and Health

Safety in Tawau

Tawau is generally a safe destination, but visitors should still take precautions to ensure their safety:

  • Avoid walking alone at night.
  • Take care of your valuables during your trip.
  • Stay updated on weather conditions and check for any natural disaster warnings before heading out.

Health in Tawau

Visitors should take precautions to maintain their health and well-being during their visit to Tawau:

  • Carry mosquito repellent with you, as Sabah is infamous for malaria and dengue cases.
  • Drink only bottled or filtered water to avoid waterborne illnesses.
  • Be aware of the risk of sunburn and heat stroke, and take necessary protective measures.

Getting to Tawau: Transportation Options and Logistics

Tawau is easily accessible by air and road. Some of the popular transport options include:

Airports Near Tawau

Tawau Airport is the main airport serving the city, and it offers domestic flights to and from Kuala Lumpur, Kota Kinabalu, and Sandakan.

Bus Stations Near Tawau

Tawau Bus Terminal is the main long-distance bus station, where visitors can find buses going to other cities in Malaysia.

Other Options

Visitors can also opt to rent a car or hire a private driver to explore the city and its surroundings at a leisurely pace.

How to Get Around in Tawau

Tawau is a relatively small city, and the most convenient means of transport is by taxi or ride-hailing service. Visitors can also rent a car or scooter if they prefer to explore on their own.

Where to Go Next from Tawau: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Some popular destinations near Tawau include:

Semporna

Semporna is a renowned spot for snorkeling and diving, making it perfect for adventurous travelers.

Kota Kinabalu

Kota Kinabalu is the capital city of Sabah and offers visitors a blend of natural beauty, history, and modern amenities.

Labuan

Labuan is an island just off the coast of Sabah that is known for its duty-free shopping, beautiful beaches, and fascinating history.

Conclusion

Tawau is a unique and fascinating destination that offers visitors a blend of culture, adventure, and relaxation. With so much to do and see, this comprehensive travel guide will be your go-to reference as you plan your trip to this beautiful city in Southeast Asia.
