Introduction to Mataking

Mataking Island is one of the most idyllic islands located in Sabah, Malaysia. Known for its crystal-clear waters and white sandy beaches, this island gem is a popular spot for honeymooners, divers, and those who are looking for a peaceful retreat. The island is also surrounded by beautiful coral reefs, making it an ideal destination for snorkeling and diving enthusiasts.

If you are planning to visit this beautiful island, this complete travel guide has everything you need to know to make your trip to Mataking enjoyable and unforgettable.

List of Places to Visit in Mataking

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Mataking is not only known for its stunning natural beauty, but the island also has a rich culture and history. Here are some of the culturally important attractions that you should visit on the island:

  • Mataking Island History Gallery - This gallery displays artifacts, photos, and stories about the island’s history and cultural heritage.
  • Survivor Memorial Monument - The memorial monument was erected to pay tribute to the survivors of the World War II shipwrecked Japanese soldiers who made it to the island.
  • Bajau Laut Village - This traditional village is home to the Bajau Laut tribe who are known as the “sea gypsies” because of their nomadic lifestyle on the water.

Natural Attractions of Mataking: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Apart from its stunning beaches, Mataking is also home to various natural attractions that are worth visiting. Here are some of the best natural attractions that you can visit during your trip:

  • Mataking Reef - As one of the most beautiful dive spots in the world, this breathtaking reef should not be missed. Divers from all over the world come here to explore the diverse marine life, including sharks, turtles, and various species of fish.
  • Island Hopping Tour - Discover the surrounding islands of Mataking, such as Pandanan Island, which is famous for its sandy beaches and snorkeling sites.
  • Turtle Playground - Located near Mohd Azmi Jetty, this site is named after its frequent visitors, green and hawksbill sea turtles. Here, you can observe these sea creatures up close in their natural habitat.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Mataking

Mataking Island is a small island, but it is perfect for short hikes and bike rides. Here are some of the best hiking and biking routes on the island:

  • Jungle Trekking - Explore the dense jungles of Mataking Island through various trekking routes equipped with canopy walkways, suspension bridges, and viewing decks.
  • Mountain Biking - The island’s terrain is gentle, making it a great destination for mountain biking enthusiasts. Rent a bike and take a ride to explore the island’s hidden treasures.

Best Things to Do in Mataking: Activities and Tours

Mataking Island offers various activities and tours to suit any interest. Here are some of the best things to do during your stay on the island:

  • Diving and Snorkeling - Mataking is known for its stunning coral reefs, making it one of the best diving and snorkeling destinations in the world.
  • Spa Treatments - Relax and unwind in one of the island’s spas or get a traditional massage to rejuvenate your mind and body.
  • Sunset Cruise - End your day with a romantic, picturesque sunset cruise around the island.

Week-Long Itinerary for Mataking

For those who want to experience everything Mataking has to offer, below is a week-long itinerary:

Day 1: Arrival and check-in at the resort. Day 2: Snorkeling and Island hopping tour. Day 3: Jungle trekking and mount biking. Day 4: Diving and spa treatments. Day 5: Turtle Playground and beach picnic. Day 6: Sunset cruise and dinner by the beach. Day 7: Departure from the island.

Food and Drink Scene of Mataking

Mataking has a variety of dining options ranging from traditional Malaysian cuisine to international fare. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Nasi Lemak - One of the most popular Malaysian dishes that includes rice cooked with coconut milk, served with sambal, fried anchovies, peanuts, and hard-boiled eggs.
  • Laksa - A spicy noodle soup with coconut milk, served with shrimp, chicken, or fish.

Local Beverages

  • Teh Tarik - A traditional Malaysian milk tea that is pulled or stretched to create a frothy texture.
  • Sirap - A refreshing drink made with rose syrup and mixed with water.

Where to Stay in Mataking: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Mataking Island offers a wide range of accommodation options to suit all budgets. Below are some of the best hotels, hostels, and guesthouses on the island:

  • Mataking Reef Dive Resort - A luxury resort that boasts a private jetty and offers a range of activities for guests to enjoy.
  • Mataking Island Lodge - A budget-friendly lodge located on the beach that is perfect for backpackers and budget-conscious travelers.
  • Seaventures Dive Rig - Converted from an oil rig, this unique accommodation option includes diving packages for guests.

What to Buy in Mataking: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Mataking Island offers various souvenirs and local products that are unique to the island. Below are some recommended items to bring home:

  • Pearls - Sabah is famous for its pearl farms, and Mataking Island offers visitors the chance to buy some of the finest pearls in the region.
  • Handmade Crafts - Mataking offers a range of handmade crafts, including batik prints, wood carvings, and traditional woven baskets and bags.

Local Culture and Customs of Mataking

Mataking Island has a diverse culture and customs that visitors should be aware of. Here are some basic customs to keep in mind:

  • Respect for Religious Places - Sabah is home to many Muslims, and visitors should be respectful when visiting mosques and other places of worship.
  • Cover Up - It is important to dress modestly when visiting local villages and religious sites.

Best Time to Visit Mataking

The best time to visit Mataking Island is from March to October when the weather is dry and the seas are calm, making it ideal for water-related activities.

Do’s and Don’ts of Mataking

Do:

  • Respect the Environment - Keep the island clean and do not litter.
  • Try the Local Cuisine - Experience Mataking’s authentic flavors.

Don’t:

  • Take Coral or Marine Life - Do not touch or take anything from the coral reefs and marine life as it could cause damage to the ecosystem.
  • Sunbathe Topless - Public nudity is not allowed in Malaysia.

Safety and Health

Safety in Mataking

Mataking Island is generally safe for tourists, but as with any trip, there are a few things to keep in mind:

  • Mosquitoes - Bring mosquito repellent as mosquitoes can be quite prevalent on the island.
  • Water Sports - Always be cautious when participating in water sports and ensure that you have proper safety equipment and certified guides.

Health in Mataking

Visitors to Mataking Island should ensure they have the following:

  • Vaccinations - Visitors should ensure they are up-to-date with vaccination requirements for Malaysia.
  • Travel Insurance - Ensure that you have adequate travel insurance that covers medical emergencies while traveling.

Getting to Mataking: Transportation Options and Logistics

Getting to Mataking Island can be done through various transportation options:

Airports Near Mataking

  • Tawau Airport - Located in Tawau, this is the nearest airport to Mataking, and it is approximately 90 minutes away by car.

Bus Stations Near Mataking

  • Tawau Bus Terminal - Buses from Kota Kinabalu and Sandakan arrive at Tawau Bus Terminal.

Other Options

  • Taxi - It is recommended to pre-book your taxi or arrange for airport transfers with your hotel.
  • Ferry - Visitors can take a ferry from Semporna jetty to Mataking Island.

How to Get Around in Mataking

The best way to get around Mataking Island is by foot, bike, or boat. Most accommodations have bicycles for rent, and boat rentals are available for island-hopping and diving excursions.

Where to Go Next from Mataking: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Mataking Island is near various destinations that visitors can explore before continuing their journey to other parts of Malaysia. Here are some of the nearby destinations:

  • Semporna - A coastal town known for its diving sites and seafood.
  • Tawau - A bustling port town with various shopping and dining options.
  • Sepilok Orangutan Rehabilitation Center - Located in Sandakan, this sanctuary is home to orphaned orangutans and other wildlife.

Conclusion

Mataking Island is a tropical paradise that offers stunning natural beauty, cultural heritage, and so much more to visitors. With a variety of activities, accommodations, and dining options, this island is sure to cater to any traveler’s interest. Follow this travel guide to make the most out of your trip to Mataking Island in Sabah, Malaysia.
