Introduction to Ranau

Ranau is a quaint town situated in the northern part of Sabah, Malaysia, known for its rolling hills, serene lakes, and lush greenery. This charming town is surrounded by verdant valleys, towering mountains, and cascading waterfalls, making it a perfect destination for nature lovers and outdoor enthusiasts.

Ranau is steeped in history, with a fascinating culture that has helped shape Sabah’s multicultural society. The Kadazan Dusun community here is known for their musical instruments, traditional dance, and vibrant festivals. Home to some of the most majestic landmarks in Sabah, Ranau should definitely be included in your list of must-visit destinations in Malaysia.

List of Places to Visit in Ranau

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

War Memorial - The War Memorial in Kundasang is dedicated to the British and Australian soldiers who died in the Sandakan Death Marches during World War II.

Sabah Tea Garden - The Sabah Tea Garden is a 5,000-acre organic tea plantation that offers guided tours showcasing how tea is processed from the tea plants before ending in a tea-tasting session.

Kundasang Memorial Park - The Kundasang Memorial Park is created to commemorate the victims of the World War II death marches and also features exhibits and information on the local Dusun people.

Natural Attractions of Ranau: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Mount Kinabalu - At 4,095 meters, Mount Kinabalu is the highest mountain in Southeast Asia and is a sight to behold. Hiking to the summit will involve walking across rocky terrain, but the stunning views from the top make it worth your while.

Desa Cattle Dairy Farm - This farm is home to hundreds of dairy cows and offers a scenic view of Mount Kinabalu. Visitors can take a guided tour, feed the calves, and try fresh milk and cheese produced on the farm.

Poring Hot Springs - The cool highland air in Ranau makes it a perfect place to unwind and enjoy a hot soak in Poring Hot Springs. You can also take a walk through the forest canopy on the suspension bridge, which provides a stunning view of the surrounding rainforest.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Ranau

For hiking enthusiasts, the Ranau Trail is a must-do. The 7.5 km trail starts at Liwagu Restaurant and ends at the Timpohon Gate of Mount Kinabalu National Park. The trail offers spectacular views of the surrounding mountains and valleys. For those seeking a cycling adventure, the road from Kundasang to Ranau is a popular route, with stunning scenery throughout the 14 km journey.

Best Things to Do in Ranau: Activities and Tours

River Rafting - The Kiulu River is a great location for white-water rafting and is only a 30-minute drive from Ranau. The river is classified as Grade 2 to 3 and is perfect for both beginners and experienced rafters.

Paragliding - Paragliding over the Kundasang highlands and Mount Kinabalu is a thrilling activity for those looking for an adrenaline rush.

Farm Tour - The Desa Dairy Farm offers a guided tour where you can learn where you can experience the life of a local farmer in Sabah and interact with the cute animals at the farm, such as goats and horses. Additionally, the farm is also an excellent location for photography.

Food and Drink Scene of Ranau

Must-Try Dishes

Nonsom Bambangan (Bambangan Pickle) - Made from the Bambangan fruit and mixed with bird’s eye chili, this dish is a perfect condiment for any dish.

Hinava - Hinava is a popular traditional dish made of sliced raw fish and mixed with fresh lime, chili, and onions.

Ambuyat - Ambuyat is a local staple food made from the sago starch mixed with water and eaten with various side dishes of fish, vegetables, and soup.

Local Beverages

Teh Tarik - This drink is a local favorite and can be found at any local restaurant or coffee shop. It is made out of black tea, sweetened condensed milk, and hot water, which is “pulled” and mixed until frothy.

Lihing - Lihing is a traditional rice wine made by the Kadazan Dusun community in Sabah.

Kopi Tenom - This coffee from Tenom has a unique taste and aroma and is worth trying while in Sabah.

Where to Stay in Ranau: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Sutera Sanctuary Lodges at Kinabalu Park - This lodge is the perfect choice for visitors who wish to be close to Mount Kinabalu. Surrounded by lush greenery, the lodges are comfortable and offer fantastic views of the mountain.

J Residence - J Residence is a hotel located in Kundasang, close to popular attractions such as the Desa Dairy Farm and the Kundasang War Memorial.

Rose Cabin - If you’re looking for a more budget option, Rose Cabin provides comfortable and affordable accommodations in Ranau.

What to Buy in Ranau: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Labu Sayong - This is a traditional water jug made from clay, and the design dates back to the Islamic era. It is a perfect souvenir for those wanting to take home a piece of Malaysia’s rich history.

Sabah Tea - Sabah Tea is a popular souvenir item for visitors to Ranau. The Sabah Tea Garden produces some of the world’s finest organic tea and is a perfect gift for tea lovers.

Dabai Fruit - The dabai fruit is a delicacy in Sabah and can only be found during certain times of the year in local markets. It is a must-try fruit for foodies visiting the region.

Local Culture and Customs of Ranau

The Kadazan Dusun community is the largest indigenous group in Sabah, and their traditional dance, music, and festivals play a vital role in the state’s multicultural society. Visitors to Ranau should respect the local culture and dress appropriately when visiting cultural sites. When attending festivals, it is essential to follow the traditions and customs, such as removing your shoes when entering someone’s house.

Best Time to Visit Ranau

The best time to visit Ranau is during the dry season, from May to September. The rain is less frequent during this period, and the weather is generally warm and sunny. The temperature can dip quite low during the night due to the cooler mountain climate, so it’s essential to bring warm clothes, especially if you plan to hike.

Do’s and Don’ts of Ranau

Do:

  • Respect the local culture and traditions.
  • Dress conservatively when visiting cultural sites.
  • Try the local food and drinks.

Don’t:

  • Litter in public areas.
  • Disrespect the local traditions and customs.
  • Engage in activities that harm the environment or animals.

Safety and Health

Safety in Ranau

Ranau is generally a safe place for visitors, but it is important to exercise caution when traveling outside of the city center. It is always best to keep your belongings close, especially when visiting crowded places like markets or festivals.

Health in Ranau

Visitors to Ranau should ensure that they have received the necessary vaccinations before arriving in Sabah. It is crucial to stay hydrated and wear sunscreen and insect repellent when hiking or spending time outdoors.

Getting to Ranau: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Ranau

The nearest airport to Ranau is Kota Kinabalu International Airport, which is about 3 hours’ drive away from Ranau. From the airport, you can take a taxi to Ranau town or rent a car to drive yourself.

Bus Stations Near Ranau

There are no bus stations in Ranau town, but there are bus services that run to and from Kota Kinabalu, the city center of Sabah.

Other Options

The most convenient way to travel around Ranau is by rented car. Car rentals are available at the Kota Kinabalu International Airport, and the drive to Ranau takes about 3 hours. Other transportation options include taxis or hiring a private driver.

How to Get Around in Ranau

Ranau town is small enough to navigate on foot, but it’s more convenient to hire a taxi or rent a car to explore the surrounding area.

Where to Go Next from Ranau: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Kota Kinabalu - The capital city of Sabah is only a 2-hour drive away from Ranau and has a mix of modern and natural attractions, such as beaches, islands, and shopping malls.

Sandakan - Sandakan is the second-largest city in Sabah and is known for its historical sites, such as the Sandakan Memorial Park and the Sandakan Heritage Trail.

Sepilok Orangutan Rehabilitation Centre - Located about 4 hours away by car, the Sepilok Orangutan Rehabilitation Centre is one of the top attractions in Sabah where visitors can see orangutans up close and learn about their conservation efforts.

Conclusion

Ranau is a hidden gem in Sabah that offers visitors a glimpse into the state’s unique culture and stunning natural beauty. Visitors can spend their days hiking, visiting historical landmarks, trying local delicacies, or interacting with the local community. This travel guide is the perfect starting point for anyone planning a trip to Ranau, offering recommendations for must-visit attractions, activities, accommodations, and more.
