A Comprehensive Guide to Kudat, Sabah, Malaysia

Kudat is a stunning coastal town in Sabah, Malaysia, located in the northern region of Borneo island. Its beautiful beaches, crystal-clear waters, and abundant wildlife make it a popular destination for nature lovers. This guide will provide you with everything you need to know to plan a memorable and exciting trip to Kudat.

Introduction to Kudat

Kudat is a small town in Sabah, located about 190 kilometers from the state capital Kota Kinabalu. The town is known for its laid-back atmosphere, pristine beaches, and lush tropical forests. Kudat is home to several indigenous communities, including the Rungus people, who are famous for their unique culture and traditional longhouses.

List of Places to Visit in Kudat

Here is a list of the top places to visit in Kudat:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Kudat Museum: The Kudat Museum is a small museum that showcases the cultural heritage of the Rungus people. It features traditional costumes, handicrafts, and musical instruments, among other exhibits.
  • Batu Gong: Batu Gong is a series of large rocks with natural hollows that produce musical tones when struck with a stick. It is believed to have been used for ceremonial purposes by the Rungus people.
  • Gong Making Village: This village is where the Rungus people make gongs, one of their traditional handicrafts.

Natural Attractions of Kudat: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Tanjung Simpang Mengayau: Also known as the Tip of Borneo, Tanjung Simpang Mengayau is a scenic coastal area where the South China Sea meets the Sulu Sea. It offers panoramic views of the sunset and is a popular spot for photography.
  • Kudat Bird Sanctuary: The Kudat Bird Sanctuary is home to more than 100 species of birds, including migratory birds that come from as far away as Siberia.
  • Gombizau Honey Bee Farm: The Gombizau Honey Bee Farm is a family-run farm that produces high-quality honey using traditional methods. Visitors can learn about the honey-making process and sample different types of honey.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Kudat

For adventure enthusiasts, Kudat offers several hiking and biking routes. The most popular one is the trek up Mount Marai, which offers spectacular views of the surrounding jungle and coastline.

Best Things to Do in Kudat: Activities and Tours

Here are some of the best activities and tours to enjoy in Kudat:

  • Snorkeling and Diving: Kudat boasts some of the best snorkeling and diving sites in Malaysia, including the Mantanani Islands and Banggi Island.
  • Island Hopping: Visitors can hop on a boat to explore the secluded islands around Kudat, such as Kalampunian Island and Balambangan Island.
  • Cultural Tours: Visitors can take a cultural tour to learn about the Rungus peoples’ way of life, such as visiting their longhouses and trying their traditional food.

Week-Long Itinerary for Kudat

Here is a sample week-long itinerary for Kudat:

Day 1: Visit Kudat Museum, Batu Gong, and Gong Making Village. Day 2: Explore the Tip of Borneo and Tanjung Simpang Mengayau, enjoy the sunset. Day 3: Go snorkeling or diving at Mantanani Islands. Day 4: Hike up Mount Marai. Day 5: Visit the Kudat Bird Sanctuary and Gombizau Honey Bee Farm. Day 6: Take a cultural tour to a Rungus longhouse. Day 7: Island hop to Kalampunian or Balambangan Island.

Food and Drink Scene of Kudat

Kudat is known for its excellent seafood, traditional Rungus cuisine, and locally produced honey. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Tuhau: Tuhau is a spicy pickled vegetable that is popular among the Rungus people.
  • Ambuyat: Ambuyat is a sticky, starchy food made from sago palm that is typically eaten with fish or meat.
  • Fish Satay: Fish Satay is a skewered fish dish that is marinated in spices and grilled.

Local Beverages

  • Tape: Tape is a popular alcoholic beverage made from fermented rice or cassava.
  • Tenom Coffee: Tenom Coffee is a popular coffee variety produced in the neighboring town of Tenom.

Where to Stay in Kudat: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Kudat has several accommodation options for tourists, including hotels, hostels, and guesthouses. Here are some of the best options:

  • Tip of Borneo Resort: This beachfront resort offers spacious rooms and spectacular sea views.
  • Nim’s Inn: Nim’s Inn is a budget-friendly guesthouse that offers comfortable rooms and friendly service.
  • Kudat Riviera: Kudat Riviera is a luxurious beach resort that features a private beach, pool, spa, and restaurant.

What to Buy in Kudat: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Here are some souvenirs and local products to buy in Kudat:

  • Beaded Jewelry: The Rungus people are known for their intricate beadwork, and visitors can buy beautiful beaded jewelry as souvenirs.
  • Wooden Handicrafts: The Rungus people are also skilled in woodworking and create handmade wooden products such as masks, dolls, and sculptures.
  • Honey: Kudat is famous for its natural, high-quality honey, which is available for purchase at local markets and honey farms.

Local Culture and Customs of Kudat

The Rungus people, the largest indigenous group in Kudat, have a unique culture and customs that visitors can learn about. Some of their customs include living in traditional longhouses, wearing traditional clothing, and practicing traditional handicrafts.

Best Time to Visit Kudat

The best time to visit Kudat is between March and October when the weather is dry and sunny. However, visitors should be prepared for occasional rain showers.

Do’s and Don’ts of Kudat

Here are some do’s and don’ts to keep in mind when visiting Kudat:

Do:

  • Respect the local culture and customs of the Rungus people.
  • Dress modestly, especially when visiting villages and religious sites.
  • Try the local cuisine and beverages.

Don’t:

  • Engage in any activities that may harm the local wildlife or environment.
  • Enter any private property without permission.
  • Disrespect the local culture or customs.

Safety and Health

Safety in Kudat

Kudat is generally a safe destination for tourists. However, visitors should take basic precautions, such as not leaving valuables unattended and avoiding isolated areas at night.

Health in Kudat

Visitors to Kudat should take proper precautions, such as getting vaccinated and taking out travel insurance. They should also avoid drinking tap water and eating uncooked or undercooked food.

Getting to Kudat: Transportation Options and Logistics

Kudat is accessible by several transportation options:

Airports Near Kudat

  • Kota Kinabalu International Airport: This airport is the nearest international airport to Kudat and is located about 190 kilometers away.
  • Kudat Airport: This is a small domestic airport that serves only a few airlines and flights.

Bus Stations Near Kudat

  • Kota Kinabalu Bus Station: Buses from Kota Kinabalu to Kudat are available daily.

Other Options

Visitors can also hire private taxis or rent a car to reach Kudat from Kota Kinabalu.

How to Get Around in Kudat

Visitors can hire taxis or motorbikes to get around Kudat. However, the best way to explore Kudat is by hiring a local guide who can show them around the town and surrounding areas.

Where to Go Next from Kudat: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Here are some nearby destinations and excursions that visitors can take from Kudat:

  • Kota Kinabalu: Kota Kinabalu is the state capital of Sabah and a popular tourist destination known for its rich cultural heritage and natural beauty.
  • Maliau Basin: Maliau Basin is a pristine rainforest reserve that offers visitors an unforgettable nature experience.
  • Mount Kinabalu: Mount Kinabalu is the tallest mountain in Malaysia and a popular destination for hiking enthusiasts.

Conclusion

Kudat is a hidden gem in Sabah, Malaysia, that offers a unique blend of culture, natural beauty, and adventure. With so many things to see and do, visitors are sure to have an unforgettable experience in Kudat.
