Introduction to Pom Pom Island

Located in the southern part of Sabah, Malaysia, Pom Pom Island is a paradise for travelers seeking a secluded and serene getaway. The island boasts crystal-clear waters, sandy beaches fringed with palm trees, and colorful marine life, making it an ideal destination for divers, snorkelers, and beach lovers.

List of Places to Visit in Pom Pom Island

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Although Pom Pom Island is mostly known for its natural beauty and outdoor activities, there are still a few places that visitors can explore to learn more about the island’s history and culture. The most noteworthy include:

  • Bukit Tengkorak Archaeological Site: This ancient burial ground represents the history of the local community, showcasing a mix of Orthodox and non-Orthodox types of burials.

  • Tun Sakaran Marine Park: This protected marine park is named after the former Sabah governor, Tun Sakaran Dandai. The park’s main draw is its rich marine biodiversity, including over 250 species of corals and 450 species of fish.

Natural Attractions of Pom Pom Island: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Pom Pom Island is a nature lover’s paradise, with stunning landscapes and abundant wildlife. Here are some of the top natural attractions to visit:

  • Pom Pom Island Beach: This is a perfect destination for sunbathing and swimming, with soft white sand and crystal clear waters. It’s also home to various sea life like turtles.

  • Pom Pom Island Reef: The surrounding waters boast an array of colorful corals and tropical fish, making it an excellent spot for snorkeling and diving.

  • Pom Pom Island Mangrove Forest: Take a boat tour through the dense mangrove forest to spot otters, kingfishers, and other wildlife species.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Pom Pom Island

Pom Pom Island is not large enough for significant hikes or biking routes. However, visitors can take walks around the island and explore its natural beauty.

Best Things to Do in Pom Pom Island: Activities and Tours

From water sports to cultural experiences, here are some of the best things to do in Pom Pom Island:

  • Island Hopping: Take a boat ride to nearby islands to marvel at stunning natural landscapes and secluded beaches.

  • Scuba Diving: Explore the vibrant coral reefs and marine life, including turtles, reef sharks, and colorful fish.

  • Snorkeling: With equipment provided, enjoy an easy-to-access activity and spot turtles, cuttlefish, and all the colorful fish.

  • Sunset Cruise: Sit back and relax with a drink as you watch the sunset over the waters.

Week-Long Itinerary for Pom Pom Island

Day 1: Arrival and Check-in Day 2: Island Hopping Day 3: Scuba Diving or Snorkeling Day 4: Mangrove Forest Tour Day 5: Relax on Pom Pom Island Beach Day 6: Sunset Cruise Day 7: Departure

Food and Drink Scene of Pom Pom Island

Pom Pom Island’s food scene is limited to the resorts and local eateries on the island. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Fresh Seafood: Take advantage of the island’s proximity to the sea and indulge in fresh seafood dishes such as fish, calamari, and prawns.

Local Beverages

  • Coconut Water: Served fresh, coconut water is a refreshing treat on a hot day.

  • Teh Tarik: A popular Malaysian hot milk tea drink.

Where to Stay in Pom Pom Island: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Pom Pom Island is home to a handful of resorts and guesthouses. Visitors can choose between luxury accommodations or more affordable options. Here are some of the top choices:

  • Pom Pom Island Resort: A luxury resort located on the island, boasting a private beach, spa, and a beautiful infinity pool.

  • Pom Pom Guesthouse: Affordable accommodation option offering clean and comfortable rooms, only a short walk from the beach.

What to Buy in Pom Pom Island: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Local shops and stalls on Pom Pom Island are limited, and souvenir options are quite scarce. However, visitors can buy handicrafts and souvenirs during their stay on the nearby Semporna town.

Local Culture and Customs of Pom Pom Island

The local community on Pom Pom Island follows a traditional Malay lifestyle, with fishing and tourism as the primary source of income. Visitors are expected to dress modestly, especially when visiting cultural attractions, and always ask for permission before taking photographs of people.

Best Time to Visit Pom Pom Island

The best time to visit Pom Pom Island is from March to October, during the dry season. Visitors can expect plenty of sunshine and clear waters during this period.

Do’s and Don’ts of Pom Pom Island

Do:

  • Respect the local community and their culture when visiting tourist attractions.

  • Respect the environment by practicing responsible tourism and picking up trash.

  • Take part in the beach clean-up activities that some resorts organize.

Don’t:

  • Litter the beaches or the waters.

  • Touch or stand on the corals during snorkeling or diving.

  • Disrupt the natural habitat of the island’s wildlife.

Safety and Health

Safety in Pom Pom Island

Pom Pom Island is generally a safe destination for visitors. However, it’s always advised to take precautions and keep an eye on your belongings. Visitors are also asked to follow the safety guidelines of the activities they participate in, such as snorkeling and diving.

Health in Pom Pom Island

Visitors are advised to take extra precautions when it comes to hygiene and cleanliness, especially when consuming street food. It’s also recommended to bring mosquito repellent to prevent mosquito-borne illnesses.

Getting to Pom Pom Island: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Pom Pom Island

The nearest airport is Tawau Airport, located around 1 hour and 30 minutes from Semporna town, the nearest point to Pom Pom Island. From there, visitors can take a taxi or shuttle bus to the Semporna jetty, where they can catch a boat ride to the island.

Bus Stations Near Pom Pom Island

There are no bus stations near Pom Pom Island as the only way to reach the island is by boat.

Other Options

Visitors can book transportation packages from resorts or local travel agencies, providing a one-stop-shop for the journey.

How to Get Around in Pom Pom Island

As Pom Pom Island is a small island, visitors typically walk around to explore it on foot. Speedboats also trail the island for hopping to the nearby islands or to transfer guests to different resorts.

Where to Go Next from Pom Pom Island: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Sipadan Island: Just a short boat ride away from Pom Pom Island, Sipadan Island is one of the best diving destinations in the world.

  • Bohey Dulang Island: This is a pristine, deserted island, located in the east of Semporna which gives a panoramic view of Semporna and neighboring islands

Conclusion

Pom Pom Island is a must-visit destination for travelers who are looking for a peaceful and scenic escape. With its white sand beaches, clear blue waters, and diverse marine life, visitors can enjoy many activities and opportunities to relax during their stay.
