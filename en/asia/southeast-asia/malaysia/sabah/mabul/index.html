Introduction to Mabul

Mabul is a small island located off the southeastern coast of Sabah, Malaysia, in the Celebes Sea. The island is well-known for its exquisite scuba diving and snorkeling sites, including the famous Sipadan Island, one of the world’s top dive sites. But Mabul offers more than just underwater exploration. The island is a melting pot of cultures, with its unique Bajau Laut community and their vibrant stilt villages. This comprehensive travel guide will help you plan your trip to Mabul and make the most of your island adventure.

List of Places to Visit in Mabul

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  1. Mabul Bajau Laut Village: Explore the stilt houses and marketplaces of the Bajau Laut community, one of the last nomadic sea tribes in the world.
  2. Mabul Island Mosque: Be amazed by the floating mosque on stilts built by the local community.
  3. Malaysian Borneo Scuba Dive Center: Learn about the diverse marine life in the Celebes Sea and the Sipadan Island.

Natural Attractions of Mabul: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  1. Sipadan Island: This UNESCO-listed site boasts some of the world’s most beautiful coral reefs and diverse sea creatures, including turtles, sharks, and barracudas.
  2. Mabul Island Beach: Relax on the powdery white sand and enjoy the crystal-clear turquoise waters.
  3. Kapalai Island: This small island is popular for its water activities, such as kayaking, snorkeling, and diving in the clear blue waters.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Mabul

Mabul is a small island, and there are no hiking or biking trails available.

Best Things to Do in Mabul: Activities and Tours

  1. Scuba Diving and Snorkeling: Explore the colorful marine life of Mabul and Sipadan Island with the help of experienced guides.
  2. Island Hopping: Visit other nearby islands, such as Kapalai and Mataking Island, and experience their unique cultures and natural attractions.
  3. Cultural Tours: Learn about the fascinating history and tradition of the Bajau Laut community through cultural exchange programs and homestays.

Food and Drink Scene of Mabul

Must-Try Dishes

  1. Nasi Lemak: A signature Malaysian dish made of coconut rice, fried anchovies, peanuts, egg, and sambal, a spicy sauce made with chili paste and shrimp paste.
  2. Laksa: A spicy noodle soup with seafood, bean sprouts, and herbs.
  3. Satay: Skewered and grilled meat served with peanut sauce and cucumber.

Local Beverages

  1. Teh Tarik: A hot milk tea beverage that is sweetened and frothed by being pulled back and forth between two containers.
  2. Kopi-O: A black coffee traditionally served with sugar or condensed milk.
  3. Fresh Coconut Juice: A refreshing and healthy drink, especially in the tropical climate of Mabul Island.

Where to Stay in Mabul: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  1. Sipadan Mabul Resort: A luxury resort with spacious rooms and villas offering breathtaking ocean views and direct beach and reef access.
  2. Billabong Scuba: A budget-friendly accommodation that provides dorm and private rooms with basic facilities and free Wi-Fi.
  3. Arung Hayat Homestay: An authentic homestay experience with local cultural exchange programs and comfortable rooms and facilities.

What to Buy in Mabul: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  1. Sea Pearl and Shell Handicrafts: Find beautiful jewelry and decorative items made of sea pearls, seashells, and coral.
  2. Batik Fabrics: Observe the traditional batik-making process and buy colorful batik fabrics, clothing, and accessories as souvenirs.
  3. Bajau Laut Hand-Woven Mats: Take home the intricately woven mats made by the Bajau Laut community, which can be used for flooring, wall decor, and as a mat.

Local Culture and Customs of Mabul

The Bajau Laut community is the predominant ethnic group of Mabul Island, and they have a distinct way of life and beliefs. They practice a nomadic lifestyle, living in stilt houses on the sea and depending on fishing and bartering for their livelihood. Visitors to Mabul should respect their culture and traditions, including dress modestly, avoid pointing with fingers or feet, and ask permission before taking photographs.

Best Time to Visit Mabul

The best time to visit Mabul is from March to October, during the dry season with pleasant weather and good visibility for scuba diving and snorkeling. The island can be visited year-round, but the monsoon season from November to February may bring heavy rainfall and rough sea conditions.

Do’s and Don’ts of Mabul

Do:

  1. Respect the local culture and customs.
  2. Wear sunscreen and protective clothing against the sun and sea.
  3. Bring cash as there are no ATMs or credit card facilities on the island.

Don’t:

  1. Take anything from the sea or damage corals.
  2. Use single-use plastics or litter on the island.
  3. Engage in unlicensed fishing or activities that harm the environment.

Safety and Health

Safety in Mabul

While Mabul Island is generally safe, visitors should be aware of the potentially dangerous rip currents and strong waves that can occur, especially during the monsoon season. It is advisable to always swim and snorkel with a life jacket and seek guidance from experienced guides.

Health in Mabul

Visitors to Mabul Island are advised to check their vaccination status before traveling and take precautions against mosquito-borne diseases, such as dengue fever and Zika virus. Bringing a mosquito repellent and treating clothing with permethrin can help reduce the risks.

Getting to Mabul: Transportation Options and Logistics

Mabul Island is located off the southeastern coast of Sabah, Malaysia, and can be reached by a combination of air, land, and sea transportation.

Airports Near Mabul

The nearest airport to Mabul Island is Tawau Airport, which is served by daily domestic flights from Kuala Lumpur, Kota Kinabalu, and other major cities in Malaysia. From Tawau, visitors can take a taxi or a pre-arranged shuttle service to Semporna jetty, the gateway to Mabul and nearby islands.

Bus Stations Near Mabul

Semporna Bus Terminal is the main bus station that connects Semporna town to other major cities in Sabah, such as Kota Kinabalu, Sandakan, and Lahad Datu. From the bus terminal, visitors can take a taxi or a short walk to Semporna jetty.

Other Options

There are also private chartered flights, speedboats, and ferry services available from major cities in Sabah, such as Kota Kinabalu and Sandakan, to Mabul Island.

How to Get Around in Mabul

Mabul Island is a small island that can be explored on foot or by boat. Visitors can also hire snorkeling or diving boats or join island-hopping tours to reach other nearby islands and attractions.

Where to Go Next from Mabul: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  1. Semporna: A bustling town on the mainland that offers more cultural, historical, and natural attractions, such as the Bajau Laut Museum and Tun Sakaran Marine Park.
  2. Kinabatangan River: A wildlife sanctuary and river cruise destination that offers a chance to spot Borneo’s endangered proboscis monkeys, orangutans, and pygmy elephants.
  3. Danum Valley: A pristine rainforest reserve that offers eco-tourism activities, such as jungle trekking, bird-watching, and canopy walks.

Conclusion

Mabul Island is a treasure trove of cultural, natural, and adventure attractions that will surely leave travelers spellbound. Whether you are a diving enthusiast or seeking an authentic cultural experience, Mabul offers something for everyone. Follow this ultimate travel guide to plan your trip to Mabul and explore the best of the island’s beauty and charm.
