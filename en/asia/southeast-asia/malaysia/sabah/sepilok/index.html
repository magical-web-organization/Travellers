Introduction to Sepilok

Sepilok offers travelers an immersive cultural and natural experience in the heart of Sabah, Malaysia’s second largest state. The region is home to rainforests and mangroves, with an array of wildlife including orangutans, proboscis monkeys, crocodiles, and pygmy elephants. In this comprehensive travel guide, we’ll take you through the best things to do, adventure activities, where to stay, and local food and culture of Sepilok so that you can make the most of your trip here.

List of Places to Visit in Sepilok

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  1. Sandakan Memorial Park: This park commemorates those who perished during World War II in Sandakan. It has a peaceful ambience and is a must-visit for history enthusiasts.
  2. Agnes Keith House: Agnes Keith was an American author who wrote extensively about her life in Sabah during British colonial times. Her former residence is now a museum where visitors can catch a glimpse of her life.
  3. Tun Sakaran Museum: The Tun Sakaran Museum aims to preserve and promote the traditional culture and history of Semporna. It’s a perfect way to immerse yourself in the culture of the region.

Natural Attractions of Sepilok: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  1. Kinabatangan River: Take a boat ride along the Kinabatangan River to spot wildlife, including pygmy elephants, orangutans, and proboscis monkeys.
  2. Sun Bear Conservation Center: Established in 2008, the Sun Bear Conservation Center is dedicated to preserving and protecting the endangered sun bear. Visitors can learn about the center’s conservation efforts and even get a chance to see some of the bears up close.
  3. Rainforest Discovery Center: The Rainforest Discovery Center offers nature trails, bird-watching opportunities, and spectacular views of the jungle. It’s a great spot for anyone interested in eco-friendly tourism.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Sepilok

  1. Sepilok Laut Trail: Follow the Sepilok Laut Trail through the jungle and enjoy picturesque views of the sea from the top of a nearby hill.
  2. Kabili-Sepilok Forest Reserve: Take a guided tour of the Kabili-Sepilok Forest Reserve and witness different types of flora and fauna in their natural habitat.
  3. Puh Jih Syh Buddhist Temple: Hike to the Puh Jih Syh Buddhist Temple and soak in the panoramic views of Sandakan Bay while enjoying the tranquility of the temple’s surroundings.

Best Things to Do in Sepilok: Activities and Tours

  1. Orangutan Rehabilitation Center: Visitors can witness the effort to reintroduce these endangered primates into their natural habitat.
  2. Proboscis Monkey Sanctuary: Take a boat to Klias Wetland and enjoy watching proboscis monkeys in their natural habitat.
  3. Snorkeling and Diving: Explore the underwater treasures of the region by snorkeling or diving. Sipadan Island is considered one of the best dive sites in the world.

Food and Drink Scene of Sepilok

Must-Try Dishes

  1. Beef Rendang: This slow-cooked beef dish is often served at special occasions such as weddings or religious ceremonies.
  2. Nasi Goreng: A traditional Indonesian-style fried rice dish, which is commonly taken as breakfast. Perfect for a quick bite before heading out for the day.
  3. Laksa: A spicy noodle soup dish that is popular among locals. It’s a must-try dish while you’re in Sabah.

Local Beverages

  1. Teh Tarik: A sweet and frothy tea mixture made with condensed milk and black tea. Don’t leave Sabah without trying this delicious beverage.
  2. Kopi O: A strong black coffee that is often served with a layer of foam on top. It’s a great way to kickstart your morning or give yourself a caffeine boost during the day.

Where to Stay in Sepilok: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  1. Sepilok Nature Resort: If you’re looking to immerse yourself in the natural beauty of the region, this is an excellent choice. They have an outdoor pool, bar/lounge, and restaurant.
  2. My Nature Resort: Located near Sepilok Laut Trail, it’s the perfect spot for anyone who loves to hike. The resort has a swimming pool and a restaurant serving local cuisine.
  3. Sabah Hotel Sandakan: This hotel is located in the heart of Sandakan and offers easy access to all the major attractions in the area. They also offer a 24-hour fitness center and a rooftop terrace.

What to Buy in Sepilok: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  1. Hand-woven baskets: Made by local artisans, these baskets are perfect for storing fruits and vegetables or as decorative pieces.
  2. Pearl Jewelry: Sabah is famous for its pearls, so why not take home some beautiful jewelry as a souvenir?
  3. Tenmoku Pottery: These beautiful black-glazed ceramics are a specialty of Sabah and are perfect as gifts or souvenirs.

Local Culture and Customs of Sepilok

Sabah is a melting pot of cultures, with various ethnic groups living in harmony. When visiting Sepilok, it is important to be respectful of local customs. Dress modestly when visiting temples, and avoid pointing your feet towards religious statues.

Best Time to Visit Sepilok

The best time to visit Sepilok is between March and October when the weather is warm and dry. In November and December, it’s still a good time to visit, but be prepared for occasional rain showers.

Do’s and Don’ts of Sepilok

Do:

  1. Respect local customs and traditions.
  2. Carry a water bottle and insect repellent while going out on outdoor activities.
  3. Try local cuisine.

Don’t:

  1. Litter in public places.
  2. Touch or feed the wild animals.
  3. Disrespect religious sites and customs.

Safety and Health

Safety in Sepilok

Sepilok is generally safe for tourists. However, it is advisable not to walk alone in dimly lit areas at night and to take precautions against petty crime. Make sure you keep your belongings with you and don’t leave valuables unattended.

Health in Sepilok

It is always wise to consult your physician if you’re not feeling well before traveling to Sepilok. Malaria and dengue fever are the primary concerns in the region, so take appropriate precautions and carry necessary medication.

Getting to Sepilok: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Sepilok

  1. Sandakan Airport: Located just 25 minutes away from Sepilok, this airport has regular flights from Kuala Lumpur and Kota Kinabalu.
  2. Kota Kinabalu International Airport: A major airport in Malaysia, this airport is located three hours away from Sandakan.

Bus Stations Near Sepilok

  1. Sandakan Express Bus Terminal: It’s the primary bus station in Sandakan, with buses connecting to various parts of Sabah and across the border to Indonesia.
  2. Labuk Road Bus Station: This station is closest to Sepilok and has regular buses connecting to other parts of Sandakan.

Other Options

  1. Private Taxis: Taxis are widely available in Sandakan and can be the most comfortable option for travelers.
  2. Car Rentals: Car rentals are available in Sandakan and Kota Kinabalu for those who prefer to drive themselves. However, make sure you have a valid driving license and are comfortable driving on the left side of the road.

How to Get Around in Sepilok

Getting around Sepilok is easy, with plenty of transportation options available. Taxis, rental cars, and buses are readily available, and many hotels offer shuttle services for their guests.

Where to Go Next from Sepilok: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  1. Turtle Island Park: Just a few hours from Sepilok, Turtle Island Park is a great place to see turtles in their natural habitat and enjoy some outdoor activities.
  2. Mount Kinabalu: A popular spot for hikers, Mount Kinabalu is the highest peak in Southeast Asia and offers breathtaking views of the surrounding area.
  3. Danum Valley Conservation Area: Another great spot for eco-tourism, Danum Valley Conservation Area offers nature walks, bird-watching, and treks through the nearby rainforest.

Conclusion

Sepilok is a charming destination that offers a unique cultural and natural experience. From the rainforest discovery center to tiger exploration, there is something for everyone in this beautiful region. Don’t miss the opportunity to immerse yourself in the local culture and beauty of Sepilok on your next trip to Sabah, Malaysia!
