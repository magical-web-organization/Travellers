Introduction to Labuan

Labuan, a Federal Territory, is a small group of islands located in the South China Sea, off the coast of Sabah, Malaysia. It is known as an offshore financial centre and a duty-free haven, which offers a unique mix of cultures and a variety of natural attractions. Labuan is a popular destination for tourists, where they can explore museums and historical sites, enjoy outdoor adventures, relax on beautiful beaches, and indulge in duty-free shopping.

List of Places to Visit in Labuan

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Labuan has a rich history and an interesting cultural mix. Some of the most prominent cultural attractions include:

Labuan Museum

Labuan Museum is one of the top places to visit if you want to learn about Labuan’s history and culture. It is located in a historic building from the 1800s and features exhibits on Labuan’s history, culture, customs, and traditions. Some of the highlights of the museum include displays of Labuan’s natural history, prehistoric artifacts, ancient coins, and photos and documents from the World War II era.

Chimney Museum

The Chimney Museum is another cultural attraction worth visiting in Labuan. It is located in a restored chimney that was part of the Royal Dutch Shell’s refinery in the early 1900s. The museum features exhibits on the history of the refinery and the early oil industry in the region.

Peace Park

If you are looking for a serene experience, Peace Park, located in Tanjung Batu, is a perfect choice. The park features a memorial dedicated to the victims of World War II, beautifully landscaped gardens, and a pavilion with stunning views of the sea.

Natural Attractions of Labuan: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Labuan’s natural attractions are among the best in the region, and visitors are advised to add these scenic views and outdoor adventures to their bucket list.

Chimney Trail

The Chimney Trail is a popular hiking trail that takes you through lush greenery and leads you to some of the most stunning views of Labuan Island.

Layang-Layangan Beach

Layang-Layangan Beach is one of the most beautiful beaches in Labuan. It’s an ideal spot for swimming, sunbathing, or just relaxing by the sea. The clear blue waters and white sand make it one of the must-visit places in Labuan.

Labuan Bird Park

Labuan Bird Park is home to over 580 bird species, including the famous Hornbill bird, which is a protected species in Malaysia. Visitors to the park can observe these birds in their natural habitat and can enjoy the scenic views of the park.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Labuan

Labuan is known for its fantastic hiking and biking routes which are perfect for outdoor enthusiasts.

Layang-Layangan Trail

The Layang-Layangan Trail is a scenic route that runs along the coastline and is a popular cycling and hiking route for both locals and tourists.

Bukit Kubong Trail

Bukit Kubong Trail is another must-visit trail on Labuan Island, known for its challenging terrain. The trail takes you through dense forests and steep hills, providing an exciting and adventurous experience.

Best Things to Do in Labuan: Activities and Tours

Labuan offers a wide range of activities and tours to choose from, here are some of the most popular ones:

Island-Hopping Tour

The Island-Hopping Tour is one of the best ways to explore the beautiful surrounding islands of Labuan. You can enjoy swimming, snorkelling, and diving in the crystal-clear waters and experience the breathtaking views of stunning corals and marine life.

Scuba Diving

Labuan is a prime location for scuba diving. There are many diving sites with fascinating wrecks, such as the American and British warships, which make for an unforgettable adventure, and you can also explore the coral reefs and see colorful marine life.

Food and Drink Scene of Labuan

Labuan’s food scene is a blend of Malay, Chinese, and Western influences. Here are some of the best dishes and drinks to try:

Must-Try Dishes

Laksa

Laksa is a spicy noodle soup made with fish or chicken broth that is a popular dish in Labuan. The dish is topped with herbs, chili paste, and a hard-boiled egg.

Sambal Seafood

Sambal seafood is a fast-food dish that consists of spicy stir-fried prawns, squid, and fish with a mix of chili, garlic, and onions.

Local Beverages

Tiger Beer

Tiger Beer is the most popular beer in Labuan, and you’ll find it in most bars and restaurants on the island.

Where to Stay in Labuan: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Labuan offers a wide range of accommodation options for visitors, including hotels, hostels, and guesthouses. Some of the top hotels in the area are:

Dorsett Grand Labuan

Dorsett Grand Labuan is a five-star hotel that offers stunning sea views. The hotel features an outdoor pool, a fitness center, and spa facilities.

Tiara Labuan Hotel

Tiara Labuan Hotel is a four-star hotel that features modern rooms and facilities. The hotel provides a fitness center, pool, and restaurant, and is a great choice for those looking for a luxury stay in Labuan.

What to Buy in Labuan: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Labuan is known for its duty-free shopping, and there are many products and souvenirs that visitors can purchase. Some of the best items to take home from the island include:

Handmade Crafts

Handmade crafts made by local artisans are great souvenirs to bring home. They are made from local materials such as rattan and bamboo and include items such as baskets, bags, and mats.

Local Snacks and Products

Labuan’s markets offer a vast selection of local snacks such as dried seafood, pineapple cookies, and other treats.

Local Culture and Customs of Labuan

Labuan is a melting pot of cultures with influences from the Chinese, Malay, and European cultures. Islam is the dominant religion in the area, and visitors are expected to dress modestly and respect the local customs and traditions.

Best Time to Visit Labuan

The best time to visit Labuan is between January and August when the weather is mild and pleasant, and rainfall is minimal.

Do’s and Don’ts of Labuan

Do:

  • Respect the local customs and traditions.
  • Dress modestly when visiting religious sites.
  • Be courteous and polite to the locals.

Don’t:

  • Wear revealing clothing in public.
  • Disrespect the local culture and traditions.
  • Litter or damage the natural sites.

Safety and Health

Safety in Labuan

Labuan is a safe destination for tourists. However, visitors are advised to be cautious and take precautions when visiting crowded areas and tourist spots.

Health in Labuan

The medical facilities in Labuan are adequate, but visitors are advised to have travel insurance and take precautions such as vaccinations before visiting.

Getting to Labuan: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Labuan

Labuan Airport is the main airport in the area, and visitors can fly to the island from major airports in Malaysia, Singapore, and Brunei.

Bus Stations Near Labuan

There are regular bus services from Kota Kinabalu and Brunei to Labuan.

Other Options

Another alternative to getting to Labuan is by ferry from the nearby cities of Kota Kinabalu and Menumbok.

How to Get Around in Labuan

Getting around Labuan is easy, and visitors can either take a taxi or rent a car, motorbike, or bicycle.

Where to Go Next from Labuan: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Kota Kinabalu

Kota Kinabalu is a vibrant city located in Sabah, and is known for its beautiful beaches, bustling markets, and rich history.

Brunei

Brunei is a small nation located on the north coast of Borneo, and is a great place to visit to experience the local culture and customs. Visitors can explore the majestic mosques, historic landmarks, and traditional villages in the area.

Conclusion

Labuan is a unique destination that offers a mix of culture, history, and natural attractions. With its beautiful beaches, delicious food, and adventure activities, Labuan is a great choice for visitors looking for an authentic Southeast Asian experience.
