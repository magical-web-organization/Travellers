A Complete Travel Guide to Lahad Datu, Sabah, Malaysia

Lahad Datu is a city located on the east coast of Sabah, Malaysia. It is a popular destination for tourists who are seeking outdoor adventures, cultural experiences, and a taste of Malaysia’s traditional cuisine. In this comprehensive travel guide, we will explore the best places to visit, things to do, food to eat, and tips for safety and transportation while visiting Lahad Datu.

Introduction to Lahad Datu

Lahad Datu is a city that was founded in 1947. It is located in the eastern part of Sabah, which is known for its diverse wildlife, tropical rainforests, and pristine beaches. The city is surrounded by numerous natural attractions, such as the Tabin Wildlife Reserve, Danum Valley Conservation Area, and Maliau Basin Conservation Area. It is also home to several cultural sites, such as the Madai Caves and the Lahad Datu Museum.

List of Places to Visit in Lahad Datu

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

The Lahad Datu Museum is a must-visit destination for those interested in learning about the history and culture of Sabah. The museum showcases various artifacts, including traditional musical instruments, costumes, and weapons. Visitors can also learn about the city’s history and development through exhibits and interactive displays.

Another culturally significant attraction is the Madai Caves. Located about 10 kilometers from the city center, the caves are believed to be home to the spirits of the ancestors. Visitors can explore the various chambers of the caves and view the ancient rock paintings.

Natural Attractions of Lahad Datu: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

One of the top attractions in Lahad Datu is the Tabin Wildlife Reserve, which is a haven for nature lovers and wildlife enthusiasts. The reserve is home to a vast array of flora and fauna, including the endangered Bornean pygmy elephant, proboscis monkey, and orangutan.

Danum Valley Conservation Area is another must-visit destination for those who enjoy outdoor adventures. The conservation area features a tropical rainforest, picturesque waterfalls, and an abundance of wildlife. Visitors can take guided hikes, canopy walks, and night safaris to experience the natural beauty of the area.

Maliau Basin Conservation Area is a pristine rainforest that is considered one of the most complex ecosystems in the world. Visitors can explore the area’s unique flora and fauna while hiking through the rainforest and viewing the stunning waterfalls.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Lahad Datu

There are various hiking and biking trails around Lahad Datu that provide visitors with opportunities to explore the city’s stunning natural beauty. Some popular routes include the Danum Valley Trek, the Baturong Mangrove Boardwalk, and the Maliau Basin Trek.

Best Things to Do in Lahad Datu: Activities and Tours

Some of the best things to do in Lahad Datu include:

  • Take a river cruise to explore the city’s mangroves and spot wildlife
  • Visit the Tabin Wildlife Reserve and go on a guided jungle trek
  • Go on a night safari in search of nocturnal animals
  • Explore the Maliau Basin Conservation Area and view the stunning waterfalls
  • Visit the Lahad Datu Museum to learn about the city’s culture and history

Week-Long Itinerary for Lahad Datu

A week-long itinerary for Lahad Datu could look something like this:

Day 1: Arrive in Lahad Datu and explore the city center. Day 2-3: Spend two days exploring the Tabin Wildlife Reserve and participating in guided jungle treks. Day 4: Visit the Lahad Datu Museum and the Madai Caves. Day 5-6: Explore the Maliau Basin Conservation Area and take guided hikes to view the waterfalls. Day 7: Take a river cruise to explore the city’s mangroves and spot wildlife.

Food and Drink Scene of Lahad Datu

Must-Try Dishes

Lahad Datu is known for its delicious cuisine, which features a mix of flavors and influences from Malay, Chinese, and Indian cuisine. Some popular dishes to try include:

  • Nasi Lemak: A fragrant coconut rice dish served with various side dishes, such as fried chicken, sambal, and anchovies.
  • Satay: Grilled skewered meats served with a spicy peanut sauce.
  • Laksa: A spicy noodle soup with a coconut milk base and a mix of seafood and vegetables.
  • Teh Tarik: A sweet, spicy, and milky tea served hot or cold.

Local Beverages

One of the most popular local beverages in Lahad Datu is the Milo Dinosaur, which is a thick chocolate milkshake topped with Milo powder. Other popular drinks include Teh Tarik and coffee.

Where to Stay in Lahad Datu: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

There are various accommodation options in Lahad Datu that cater to different budgets and preferences. Some recommended options include:

  • My Inn Hotel Lahad Datu: A budget-friendly hotel located in the city center
  • Silam Dynasty Hotel: A mid-range hotel with a swimming pool and restaurant
  • Danum Valley Conservation Area: An eco-resort located within the Danum Valley Conservation Area

What to Buy in Lahad Datu: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Some popular souvenirs to buy in Lahad Datu include traditional handicrafts, such as woven baskets and wooden carvings. Visitors can also purchase local products such as Sabah tea and bird’s nest products.

Local Culture and Customs of Lahad Datu

The local culture of Lahad Datu is a mix of Malay, Chinese, and Indian influences. Visitors should dress modestly and refrain from drinking alcohol in public spaces. It is also customary to remove shoes before entering a mosque or someone’s home.

Best Time to Visit Lahad Datu

The best time to visit Lahad Datu is between January and April when the weather is dry and sunny. Visitors should avoid traveling during the monsoon season, which occurs from November to February.

Do’s and Don’ts of Lahad Datu

Do:

  • Dress modestly
  • Try the local cuisine
  • Respect local customs and traditions

Don’t:

  • Drink alcohol in public spaces
  • Wear revealing clothing

Safety and Health

Safety in Lahad Datu

Lahad Datu is generally a safe destination for travelers. However, visitors should exercise caution when taking nature hikes and be aware of their surroundings at all times. It is also recommended to travel with a local guide.

Health in Lahad Datu

Visitors should make sure they are up to date on all vaccines before traveling to Lahad Datu. It is also recommended to bring mosquito repellent and to drink plenty of water to avoid dehydration.

Getting to Lahad Datu: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Lahad Datu

The nearest airport to Lahad Datu is the Lahad Datu Airport, which is a 30-minute drive from the city center. Visitors can also fly into the Kota Kinabalu International Airport and take a bus or taxi to Lahad Datu.

Bus Stations Near Lahad Datu

The Lahad Datu Bus Terminal is located in the city center and offers regular bus services to destinations across Sabah.

Other Options

Visitors can also rent a car or take a taxi to explore the city and surrounding areas.

How to Get Around in Lahad Datu

The best way to get around Lahad Datu is by taxi or rental car. Visitors can also explore the city center on foot or by bicycle.

Where to Go Next from Lahad Datu: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Some popular destinations to visit after exploring Lahad Datu include:

  • Sandakan: A city located on the east coast of Sabah, known for its wildlife and natural attractions.
  • Kota Kinabalu: The capital city of Sabah, known for its beaches, seafood, and cultural landmarks.
  • Mount Kinabalu: A popular hiking destination that is home to the highest mountain in Malaysia.

Conclusion

Lahad Datu offers visitors a unique blend of natural beauty, cultural experiences, and delicious cuisine. From exploring the city’s museums and historical sites to hiking through the Tabin Wildlife Reserve and enjoying a cup of Teh Tarik, there is something for everyone in Lahad Datu. Follow this comprehensive travel guide to make the most out of your visit to this beautiful Malaysian city.
