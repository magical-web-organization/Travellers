Semporna Travel Guide: Explore Sabah’s Natural Wonders

Semporna, located on the southeastern coast of Sabah in Malaysia, is a must-visit destination for those who love the great outdoors. Known for its stunning coral reefs, turquoise waters, and biodiverse marine life, Semporna attracts adventure seekers, culture enthusiasts, and diving aficionados from all over the world.

In this comprehensive travel guide, we’ll show you the best things to see and do in Semporna, from exploring natural wonders to trying delicious local dishes. Let’s get started!

Introduction to Semporna

Located a two-hour drive from the city of Tawau, Semporna is a picturesque town situated on the coast of the Celebes Sea. The town is home to a diverse mix of ethnic groups, including the Bajau, Chinese, and Malays, who have helped shape the region’s unique cultural heritage. Visitors to Semporna can look forward to stunning natural scenery, rich cultural experiences, and some of the best dive sites in the world.

List of Places to Visit in Semporna

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  1. Bajau Parang Making Village - A visit to this village offers an in-depth look at the traditional art of Bajau parang-making, where skilled artisans craft beautiful, handmade swords.
  2. Bukit Tengkorak Archaeological Site - This site contains ancient burial grounds believed to be over 2000 years old. Visitors can explore the remains of the longhouse structures and view artifacts from the period.
  3. Semporna Cultural Museum - This museum showcases the region’s unique cultural heritage, with exhibits focusing on the traditions of the Bajau and other ethnic groups in the area.

Natural Attractions of Semporna: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  1. Tun Sakaran Marine Park - This protected park is made up of eight islands and offers some of the best snorkeling and diving opportunities in the region. Visitors can spot turtles, reef sharks, colorful fish, and a variety of coral formations.
  2. Bohey Dulang - A popular destination for hikers, Bohey Dulang offers breathtaking views from the top of its volcanic peak, which is over 300 meters tall.
  3. Mud Vulcanoes - This geological formation is made up of bubbling mud pots and fumaroles, creating a unique landscape that is fun to explore.
  4. Pom Pom Island - One of the most beautiful islands in Semporna, Pom Pom is known for its pristine beaches, crystal-clear waters, and turtle conservation efforts.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Semporna

  1. Bohey Dulang - The hike to the peak of Bohey Dulang takes around 45 minutes to an hour, depending on fitness levels. It’s a relatively challenging trail, but the views from the top make it well worth the effort.
  2. Maliau Basin - This pristine wilderness area in Sabah is home to a network of hiking trails that range from easy to challenging. Visitors can explore rainforests, waterfalls, and mountain ridges on a range of guided hikes.
  3. Tawau Hills Park - This park is home to over 700 species of plants and a variety of wildlife. Trails range in difficulty, from 30-minute walks to multi-day treks.

Best Things to Do in Semporna: Activities and Tours

  1. Dive in Sipadan Island - One of the most famous dive sites in Malaysia, Sipadan Island is a must-visit for those who love underwater exploration. Visitors can expect to see turtles, reef sharks, barracudas, and schools of colorful fish.
  2. Go Island Hopping - With so many beautiful islands in the area, island hopping is a popular activity in Semporna. Visitors can explore uninhabited islands, snorkel, and relax on pristine beaches.
  3. Visit a Bajau Laut Village - The Bajau Laut are a tribe of seafaring people who live in stilt houses in the sea. Visitors can visit these unique villages and learn about their traditional lifestyles.

Food and Drink Scene of Semporna

Must-Try Dishes

  1. Hinava - A popular dish from the Bajau people, Hinava is made from raw fish mixed with sliced onions, chillies, and lime juice.
  2. Sambal Udang - This spicy dish features prawns cooked in a spicy sambal sauce.
  3. Nasi Lemak - A popular Malaysian dish, Nasi Lemak is rice cooked with coconut milk and served with a variety of toppings, including fried chicken, sambal, and peanuts.

Local Beverages

  1. Sabah Tea - Sabah is known for its tea plantations, and a cup of Sabah tea in the morning is a must for tea lovers.
  2. Teh Tarik - A popular Malaysian beverage, Teh Tarik is black tea brewed with condensed milk and poured back and forth between two cups to create a frothy texture.

Where to Stay in Semporna: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  1. Sipadan Mangrove Resort - This eco-friendly resort is situated on the edge of a mangrove forest and offers stunning views of the sea.
  2. Scuba Tiger Semporna Hostel - A great option for budget travelers, this hostel provides clean, comfortable accommodations and is within walking distance of the town center.
  3. Seaventures Dive Rig - This unique accommodation is a converted oil rig that now serves as a dive resort. It offers stunning views of the sea and unforgettable diving experiences.

What to Buy in Semporna: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  1. Handmade parangs - These traditional swords make great souvenirs and can be purchased at the Bajau Parang Making Village.
  2. Sabah Tea - Take home a bag of Sabah’s famous tea as a gift for a friend (or for yourself!).
  3. Batik cloth - This colorful and intricate fabric can be found in shops throughout Semporna.

Local Culture and Customs of Semporna

The Bajau people are the dominant ethnic group in Semporna, and visitors should be respectful of their customs and traditions. Visitors should dress modestly when visiting villages and cultural sites, and ask for permission before taking photos.

Best Time to Visit Semporna

The best time to visit Semporna is from April to October, when the weather is dry and sunny. This is also the peak season for diving, so visitors should book accommodations and tours well in advance.

Do’s and Don’ts of Semporna

Do:

  1. Respect local customs and traditions.
  2. Try the local cuisine.
  3. Be mindful of the environment and avoid littering or damaging coral reefs.

Don’t:

  1. Wear revealing clothing in public.
  2. Interfere with wildlife or coral reefs.
  3. Leave valuables unattended on the beach.

Safety and Health

Safety in Semporna

Semporna is generally a safe destination, but visitors should take some precautions to avoid theft or other crimes. Avoid walking alone at night and keep valuables locked up in accommodations.

Health in Semporna

Visitors should be up to date on routine vaccinations and consult a health professional regarding any additional vaccinations required for travel to the area. Visitors should also be aware of the risk of mosquito-borne illnesses such as dengue fever and take appropriate precautions, such as using insect repellent and wearing long clothing.

Getting to Semporna: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Semporna

The nearest airport to Semporna is Tawau Airport, which is served by domestic airlines such as Malaysia Airlines and AirAsia.

Bus Stations Near Semporna

Buses from Kota Kinabalu to Tawau are available daily, with a travel time of around eight hours.

Other Options

Taxis and private car transfers from Tawau to Semporna are available for visitors who prefer more comfortable travel.

How to Get Around in Semporna

Semporna is a small town, and most attractions are within walking distance. For those who prefer not to walk, taxis and motorbike rentals are also available.

Where to Go Next from Semporna: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  1. Danum Valley Conservation Area - This protected area is home to some of Sabah’s most pristine rainforests and a wide variety of wildlife, including orangutans, proboscis monkeys, and clouded leopards.
  2. Kinabatangan River - Known for its abundant wildlife, including crocodiles, hornbills, and orangutans, the Kinabatangan River is a must-visit destination for nature lovers.

Conclusion

Semporna is a destination that offers something for everyone, whether you’re looking for outdoor adventure, cultural experiences, or simply a place to relax on a beautiful beach. With so much to see and do, it’s easy to see why Semporna is one of Malaysia’s hidden gems!
