Kapalai is a small island located in Sabah, Malaysia, on the east coast of Borneo. It is known for its crystal clear waters, abundant marine life, and white sandy beaches. If you are looking for a paradise getaway, then Kapalai is the place for you! Here is a comprehensive travel guide to help you plan your trip to Kapalai.

Introduction to Kapalai

Kapalai Island is a small sand cay, which is only a few feet above sea level and situated on the shallow waters of the Ligitan Reefs. The island is home to a dive resort that has gained popularity over the years, attracting tourists from all over the world. Whether you are a solo traveler, honeymooners, or families with kids, Kapalai offers a unique experience that is worth seeing and experiencing.

List of Places to Visit in Kapalai

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Kapalai Island is not rich in history or arts and culture, but that doesn’t mean there are no sights to see. However, In Malaysia, Sabah Museum is one of the state’s two main museums, located in the state capital city of Kota Kinabalu. It houses the state’s comprehensive collection of natural history, ceramics, ethnography, and archeology. Here, visitors can learn about the region’s Islamic, Chinese, and indigenous cultures through displays that illustrate daily life in the region.

Natural Attractions of Kapalai: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Kapalai Island’s primary attraction is its marine environment, which makes it popular to divers and snorkelers. Visitors can experience the beauty of the coral reef and its aquatic inhabitants firsthand. There are also various resorts on the island that offer activities such as jet-skiing, kayaking, and paddle boarding. Furthermore, you may walk around the island during low tide to spot exotic marine life living in pools between the coral formations.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Kapalai

Kapalai Island doesn’t have any mountains or forests to provide hiking or mountain biking opportunities. Instead, visitors can take leisurely walks around the island to enjoy the beautiful surroundings.

Best Things to Do in Kapalai: Activities and Tours

There are a variety of things to do on Kapalai Island. Here are some of the top activities and tours:

  • Diving and Snorkeling: Kapalai Island is famous for its extraordinary diving and snorkeling sites, which offer views of a variety of marine life, including sharks, turtles, and fish of various species.
  • Relaxing on the Beaches: Visitors can relax on the white sandy beaches, reading a book, and enjoying the beautiful surroundings.
  • Boat Tours: There are various boat tours around the Kapalai Island that allow visitors to sail around the island’s beautiful waters.
  • Kayaking: Rent a kayak at one of the resorts and paddle around the island at your leisure.

Week-Long Itinerary for Kapalai

Day 1: Arrive on the Island and settle in at your stay Day 2: Go diving or snorkeling in the morning before relaxing on the beach in the afternoon. Day 3: Take a boat tour around the Island Day 4: Go kayaking in the morning before an afternoon of relaxation. Day 5: Visit Sabah Museum and discover the local culture. Day 6: Participate in resort-organized activities such as jet-skiing and paddle boarding. Day 7: Bask in the sun one last time before departing on your trip home.

Food and Drink Scene of Kapalai

Since most visitors come for the marine environment, there are limited dining options on Kapalai Island. However, there are some excellent local dishes that visitors must try.

Must-Try Dishes

  • Nasi Kerabu: A rice dish served with fish or chicken with a blue hue due to the use of butterfly pea flower in the recipe.
  • Soto: A spicy soup made with coconut milk, turmeric, and coriander served with chicken or beef.
  • Ayam Percik: Grilled chicken marinated in coconut milk, chili, and ginger.

Local Beverages

  • Teh Tarik: Milk-infused tea served hot
  • Kopi: Strong, brewed coffee.

Where to Stay in Kapalai: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Kapalai Island is renowned for its all-inclusive resorts, which offer guests access to a long list of activities and tours to make their stay even more enjoyable. Here are a few of the best resorts to consider:

  • Kapalai Dive Resort
  • Pom Pom Island Resort
  • Mataking Island Resort

What to Buy in Kapalai: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Since Kapalai Island is a small island, there are limited shopping options. However, tourists can find plenty of souvenir items at the resort shops and local markets, including:

  • Traditional Sarongs
  • Handmade Jewelry
  • Batik Shirts

Local Culture and Customs of Kapalai

Malaysia is a predominantly Muslim country and has various traditional customs and culture that visitors must respect. Visitors should dress conservatively while on the island, covering their knees and shoulders, especially when leaving the resort. During the month of Ramadan, visitors should be aware of Muslims who are fasting from sunrise to sunset.

Best Time to Visit Kapalai

The best time to visit Kapalai Island is between March and October, when the waters are calm, and the visibility is excellent for diving and snorkeling.

Do’s and Don’ts of Kapalai

Do:

  • Respect the local culture and customs.
  • Apply reef-friendly sunblock to avoid harming the marine life while swimming and diving.

Don’t:

  • Don’t touch or break corals while snorkeling or diving.
  • Don’t take any shells or marine life home as souvenirs.

Safety and Health

Safety in Kapalai

Kapalai Island is a safe place to visit, but visitors should be aware of their surroundings and use common sense. Snorkelers and divers should remain cautious and be aware of their location since the area has strong currents.

Health in Kapalai

Visitors should use bottled water for drinking and avoid tap water. Visitors should also take antimalarial medication and apply mosquito repellent to avoid mosquito bites.

Getting to Kapalai: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Kapalai

The nearest airport to Kapalai Island is Tawau Airport, located 45 minutes from the port in Semporna. From Tawau, visitors can take a taxi to Semporna, and from there, they can reach Kapalai Island via speedboat.

Bus Stations Near Kapalai

A variety of buses travel from Kota Kinabalu to Tawau, but the journey takes approximately eight hours.

Other Options

Visitors who want to avoid buses can book a private transfer service or a flight from Kota Kinabalu to Tawau.

How to Get Around in Kapalai

Kapalai Island doesn’t have any roads, and visitors can navigate the island on foot or hire kayaks. For long-distance travel, visitors can rent a boat or book a tour through their resort.

Where to Go Next from Kapalai: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

After exploring Kapalai Island, visitors can also visit other nearby destinations, such as:

  • Sipadan Island
  • Mabul Island
  • Tun Sakaran Marine Park

Conclusion

Kapalai Island is a paradise that offers visitors an unforgettable experience with its natural beauty and marine life. This comprehensive travel guide provides visitors with the information they need to plan the perfect trip to Kapalai Island. From diving and snorkeling to relaxing on the beach, Kapalai Island has something to offer every traveler.
