Introduction to Poring Hot Springs

Nestled in the foothills of Mount Kinabalu, Poring Hot Springs is a hidden gem of Sabah, Malaysia. The natural hot springs have been popular among tourists and locals alike since the 1980s, and today the area is known for its relaxing hot baths, scenic hiking trails, and lush rainforest terrain. Whether you’re seeking relaxation, adventure, or a combination of both, Poring Hot Springs is the perfect destination.

List of Places to Visit in Poring Hot Spring

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

One of the must-see attractions in Poring Hot Springs is the Sabah Agriculture Park, where you can learn about the local flora and fauna through interactive exhibits and guided tours. Additionally, the nearby Kundasang War Memorial is a poignant reminder of Malaysia’s World War II history, featuring a memorial garden and museum dedicated to those who fought and lost their lives.

Natural Attractions of Poring Hot Spring: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

For outdoor enthusiasts, Poring Hot Springs is an eco-tourism hub. Adventurers can explore over twenty hiking trails, bird watching, and jungle trekking. The highlight of the area is the treetop canopy walkway, a 175-meter-long suspension bridge that takes you through the treetops for stunning panoramic views. Other natural attractions include butterfly and orchid gardens and a picturesque waterfall.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Poring Hot Spring

The hiking trails of Poring Hot Springs offer something for everyone, from novices to seasoned hikers. The trail difficulty ranges from easy to advanced, and many routes offer stunning views of Mount Kinabalu and the surrounding forest. Cyclists can also enjoy scenic routes around the hot springs and nearby Kundasang.

Best Things to Do in Poring Hot Spring: Activities and Tours

In addition to hiking and soaking in the hot springs, there is an array of activities and guided tours to choose from. The zip line adventure takes you through the jungle, on one of the most thrilling experiences. Or you can test your courage at the hill slide by experiencing the twists and turns on a long ride down the hill. The area also offers rock climbing, river rafting, and off-road ATV tours.

Week-Long Itinerary for Poring Hot Spring

Day 1: Arrival and check-in at your chosen accommodation Day 2: Explore the Sabah Agriculture Park and Kundasang War Memorial Day 3: Soak in the hot springs and relax Day 4: Hike the Mount Kinabalu trail Day 5: Visit the Poring Orchid Conservation Center and waterfall Day 6: Participate in an adventure activity such as river rafting or zip lining Day 7: Departure day

Food and Drink Scene of Poring Hot Spring

Must-Try Dishes

Sabah is famous for its seafood specialties such as Tuaran Mee, Bambangan, and Hinava. Other local delicacies include sang nyuk mian, which is pork noodle soup, and tarap fruit.

Local Beverages

The area is known for its plantations of tea, coffee, cocoa, and fruit trees. It’s an opportunity to try some of the trendiest farm-to-cup coffee shops within the Poring region, gives new meaning to “good morning.”

Where to Stay in Poring Hot Spring: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

There are many types of accommodations available in Poring Hot Springs, from luxurious resorts to budget-friendly guesthouses. The Poring Hot Springs Resort is one of the popular options; it has full hot spring facilities along with a souvenir shop and restaurants within the resort. Another recommended option would be the Spring Lodge, which also provides hot spring facilities.

What to Buy in Poring Hot Spring: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Local products include traditional handicrafts such as baskets made from rattan or bamboo, silverware, and local products such as honey and tea. Borneo Tea is the perfect souvenir to bring back home.

Local Culture and Customs of Poring Hot Spring

Sabah is known for its diverse cultures and rich heritage, adding to the charm and authenticity of Poring Hot Springs. Respect the local culture by dressing modestly when visiting cultural sites and temples and avoid pointing with your feet. Instead of tipping, expressing appreciation by saying “terima kasih” which means “thank you” in Malay.

Best Time to Visit Poring Hot Spring

The best time to visit Poring Hot Springs is from March to October, when the weather is dry and comfortable with temperatures ranging from 20°C to 32°C. The area is generally less crowded although the rainy season may pose as a possible challenge with trails becoming slippery and difficult to navigate.

Do’s and Don’ts of Poring Hot Spring

Do:

  • Respect the environment and wildlife
  • Dress modestly while exploring cultural sites
  • Follow safety guidelines when hiking or participating in adventure activities
  • Carry insect repellant and sunscreen

Don’t:

  • Litter or trespass
  • Disturb wildlife
  • Take pictures of people without their permission
  • Use flash when taking pictures of the hot springs

Safety and Health

Safety in Poring Hot Spring

Visitors should exercise caution when hiking or engaging in adventure activities around Poring Hot Springs. It is recommended to follow safety guidelines, keep in contact with the appropriate authorities during the activity, and stay within your limits.

Health in Poring Hot Spring

Although there has been no serious issue reported, visitors should be cautious of gastrointestinal distress when consuming street food or from untreated water. It’s important to carry a water bottle and bottled water as drinking water. Apply insect repellant to avoid mosquito bites.

Getting to Poring Hot Spring: Transportation Options and Logistics

Poring Hot Springs is located approximately 136km from Kota Kinabalu International Airport. One option to travel to the area would be to hire a cab directly from the airport, or another option would be to rent a car. Otherwise, Shyang Travels bus station located near the Kota Kinabalu area offers bus times from Kota Kinabalu to Ranau, with a stop at Poring Hot Springs.

Airports Near Poring Hot Spring

  • Kota Kinabalu International Airport

Bus Stations Near Poring Hot Spring

  • Shyang Travels Bus Station
  • Ranau Bus Station

How to Get Around in Poring Hot Spring

Poring Hot Springs is a small area that can easily be explored on foot or by bicycle. Alternatively, car rentals can also be availed which makes travelling more hassle-free if one wanted to explore the nearby areas.

Where to Go Next from Poring Hot Spring: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

After having a memorable stay at Poring Hot Springs, you can continue your journey further to Kundasang. The scenic drive takes only 30 minutes, where you can explore the scenic beauty of the area, visit the Kundasang vegetable market and Kinabalu Park.

Conclusion

Poring Hot Springs has something for everyone, from outdoor enthusiasts to cultural enthusiasts. Follow our guide to plan your dream trip and make the most out of your time in this tropical paradise.
