Introduction to Sentosa and Harbourfront

Sentosa Island, located in the southern part of Singapore, is a popular holiday destination for both locals and tourists. The island boasts a plethora of things to do and see, including world-class attractions, exciting outdoor activities, and stunning beaches. The neighbouring Harbourfront, located on the mainland, is a bustling hub that serves as the gateway to Sentosa and a gateway to amazing shopping, dining, and entertainment experiences. Get ready to explore two dynamic areas that are sure to delight any visitor.

List of Places to Visit in Sentosa and Harbourfront

There are many places to see and activities to do on Sentosa and Harbourfront. Here’s a list of the must-visit sites and attractions for your next trip.

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Sentosa and Harbourfront are home to several culturally significant landmarks that showcase Singapore’s rich history and diverse culture. Here are some of the must-visit museums and historical places:

1. Universal Studios Singapore

Universal Studios Singapore is one of the top-rated theme parks in Asia, offering an array of thrilling rides, attractions, and entertainment for visitors of all ages. The park is divided into seven themed zones, each with its unique set of shows, rides, and experiences.

2. S.E.A. Aquarium

S.E.A. Aquarium is one of the world’s largest aquariums, featuring over 100,000 marine animals from 1,000 different species. Visitors can explore the aquarium’s various zones, which replicate marine habitats from across the world, including the Open Ocean, the Red Sea, and the Bay of Bengal.

3. Fort Siloso

Fort Siloso is an historical attraction located on Sentosa Island that offers insight into Singapore’s military history. Built in the late 19th century, the fort served as an important military base throughout World War II.

Natural Attractions of Sentosa and Harbourfront: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Sentosa Island is known for its idyllic beaches, lush foliage, and scenic views. Here are some of the must-do activities and natural attractions.

1. Sentosa Beaches

Sentosa Island is home to several stunning beaches, including Siloso Beach, Palawan Beach, and Tanjong Beach. Each beach has its unique character and offers a variety of water sports and beachside activities.

2. Mount Faber

Mount Faber is the highest point in Singapore and offers panoramic views of the city skyline and the surrounding islands. Visitors can take a cable car up to the top or hike up on of the many trails.

3. Sentosa Nature Discovery

Located in Imbiah Lookout, Sentosa Nature Discovery is an interactive exhibition that showcases the flora and fauna of Sentosa Island and educates visitors on the importance of conservation and the environment.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Sentosa and Harbourfront

Sentosa Island and Harbourfront offer a variety of walking and cycling trails that let visitors get up close and personal with nature. Here are some of the best trails:

1. Sentosa Nature Trails

The Sentosa Nature Trails are a network of walking paths that traverse through the island’s lush forests, mangroves, and coastal areas. The trails offer a chance to see many of Sentosa’s animal inhabitants up close.

2. Labrador Nature Reserve

Located in Harbourfront, the Labrador Nature Reserve is a peaceful enclave with walking paths that offer respite from the bustling city. The area is home to several species of birds, including kingfishers, herons, and eagles.

3. Henderson Waves

Henderson Waves is a stunning pedestrian bridge that stretches across the southern ridges of Singapore. The bridge offers spectacular views of the city skyline and the lush forests of the area.

Best Things to Do in Sentosa and Harbourfront: Activities and Tours

Sentosa and Harbourfront offer a plethora of exciting activities and tours that are sure to delight visitors of all ages. Here are some of the best things to do and see:

1. Adventure Cove Waterpark

Adventure Cove Waterpark is an exciting water theme park that features thrilling water slides, a wave pool, and snorkeling with marine life such as sharks and rays.

2. Cable Car Ride

The Singapore Cable Car ride is a fun and exciting way to see the city skyline and Sentosa Island from above. The ride offers unparalleled views of the coastal areas and the lush foliage of the island.

3. Marina Bay Sands Observation Deck

The Marina Bay Sands Observation Deck is situated on the 56th floor of the iconic Marina Bay Sands Hotel. The deck offers stunning panoramic views of the city skyline and the surrounding areas.

Week-Long Itinerary for Sentosa and Harbourfront

Here’s a week-long itinerary that lets you see the best of what Sentosa and Harbourfront has to offer:

Day 1: Visit Universal Studios Singapore and see the various zones

Day 2: Explore the beaches on Sentosa Island, including Siloso Beach and Palawan Beach

Day 3: Visit the S.E.A. Aquarium and experience the diverse marine life

Day 4: Hike up Mount Faber and take in the breathtaking views

Day 5: Visit the cable car and take a ride to see Sentosa and the city skyline

Day 6: Explore the nature trails on Sentosa and meet the island’s residents up close

Day 7: Visit Adventure Cove Waterpark and experience the thrill rides and the snorkeling adventure

Food and Drink Scene of Sentosa and Harbourfront

Sentosa and Harbourfront are home to a diverse range of cuisines, ranging from classic local dishes to international fare. Here are some of the must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

1. Bak Kut Teh

Bak Kut Teh is a peppery pork rib soup that originates from Malaysia but has become a popular dish in Singapore as well.

2. Hainanese Chicken Rice

Hainanese Chicken Rice is a dish that consists of poached chicken and rice cooked in chicken broth. It is often served with cucumber slices and chili sauce.

3. Laksa

Laksa is a noodle soup that features a coconut milk-based broth with spicy curry and seafood.

Local Beverages

1. Teh Tarik

Teh Tarik is a sweet and frothy milk tea that is a popular drink in Southeast Asia.

2. Singapore Sling

Singapore Sling is a cocktail that was invented in the historic Long Bar of the Raffles Hotel in Singapore. The drink consists of gin, cherry liqueur, and various juices and syrups.

3. Kopi

Kopi is a strong, sweet coffee that is a staple drink in Singapore and Malaysia.

Where to Stay in Sentosa and Harbourfront: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Sentosa and Harbourfront offer a range of accommodation options to suit every budget and preference. Here are some of the best places to stay:

1. Shangri-La’s Rasa Sentosa Resort and Spa

Located on Sentosa Island, this resort offers luxurious rooms, a private beach, and a range of dining and recreational options.

2. Festive Hotel

Located in the heart of the Resorts World Sentosa complex, Festive Hotel offers affordable rooms with modern amenities.

3. Harbour Ville Hotel

Harbour Ville Hotel is located in Harbourfront and offers clean and comfortable rooms at an affordable price.

What to Buy in Sentosa and Harbourfront: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Sentosa and Harbourfront offer plenty of opportunities to get your hands on unique souvenirs and local products. Here are some of the best things to buy:

1. Merlion Souvenirs

The Merlion is a symbol of Singapore, and you can find a range of souvenirs featuring the iconic half-lion, half-fish statue in Sentosa and Harbourfront.

2. Batik Clothing

Batik is a traditional Indonesian textile art that produces vibrant patterns. You can find a range of batik clothing, from kaftans to shirts, in Sentosa and Harbourfront.

3. Straits Chinese Porcelain

Straits Chinese Porcelain is a decorative art that originated in Malacca, Malaysia, and combines Chinese motifs with bright colors. You can find a range of porcelain plates, vases, and teapots in Sentosa and Harbourfront.

Local Culture and Customs of Sentosa and Harbourfront

Singapore is a diverse and multicultural society, and it’s essential to understand and respect its customs and traditions. Here are some important customs to keep in mind:

1. Dress Appropriately

Singaporeans dress conservatively, and it’s important to dress modestly in public areas.

2. Respect the Local Customs

Singapore has a diverse population that follows various religious traditions. It’s important to be respectful of the customs and traditions of the locals.

3. Mind Your P’s and Q’s

Singaporeans are polite and courteous, and it’s essential to be polite and respectful in interactions.

Best Time to Visit Sentosa and Harbourfront

Singapore has a tropical climate, and the best time to visit Sentosa and Harbourfront is from February to April and from July to September. These months offer moderate temperatures, low humidity, and minimal rainfall.

Do’s and Don’ts of Sentosa and Harbourfront

Do:

  • Respect the local culture
  • Dress modestly in public areas
  • Stay hydrated in the humid weather

Don’t:

  • Litter in public spaces
  • Smoke in public areas, including beaches
  • Disrespect the local customs

Safety and Health

Safety in Sentosa and Harbourfront

Sentosa and Harbourfront are generally safe areas, and the crime rate is quite low. However, it’s essential to be vigilant in crowded areas, as pickpocketing can occur.

Health in Sentosa and Harbourfront

Singapore has a high standard of healthcare, and visitors can expect quality medical care in the event of any health issues. However, it’s essential to stay hydrated in the humid weather and apply sunscreen to protect against sunburn.

Getting to Sentosa and Harbourfront: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Sentosa and Harbourfront

The closest airport to Sentosa and Harbourfront is Changi Airport, which is a 20-minute drive or a 40-minute MRT ride away.

Bus Stations Near Sentosa and Harbourfront

The Singapore Cruise Centre is located in Harbourfront, and visitors can take buses from the centre to Sentosa Island and downtown Singapore.

Other Options

Visitors can also take the MRT to Harbourfront, which connects to several other parts of the city.

How to Get Around in Sentosa and Harbourfront

Sentosa Island is easily accessible by walking, cycling, or scooters. Harbourfront has excellent connectivity with the rest of the city via MRT, buses, and taxis.

Where to Go Next from Sentosa and Harbourfront: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Singapore is a small island nation, but it offers plenty of day trip excursions and nearby destinations. Some of the popular nearby destinations include:

1. Marina Bay

Marina Bay is a bustling hub of activity that boasts several world-class attractions, including Gardens by the Bay, Marina Bay Sands, and the Merlion Statue.

2. Chinatown

Chinatown is a vibrant cultural enclave that is home to various shops, restaurants, and temples that showcase Chinese culture.

3. Clarke Quay

Clarke Quay is a riverside precinct that offers a range of dining and entertainment options, including live music and nightlife.

Conclusion

Sentosa and Harbourfront offer a captivating blend of natural beauty, cultural landmarks, exciting activities, and vibrant culinary culture. With its excellent connectivity and compact size, it’s easy to explore the various attractions on offer. Whether you’re a nature lover, an adventure enthusiast, or a culture buff, Sentosa and Harbourfront have something for everyone.
