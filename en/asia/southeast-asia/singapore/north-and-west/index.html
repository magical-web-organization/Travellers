Introduction to North and West

Singapore’s North and West regions are an ideal destination for tourists who want to experience the best of this vibrant city-state. These areas are home to a wide array of tourist attractions, natural wonders, and cultural landmarks. With its vibrant mix of Asian cultures, Singapore offers visitors the opportunity to traverse through different customs, tastes, and architecture styles. Whether you’re a first-timer or a frequent traveler, there’s something here for everyone.

List of Places to Visit in North and West

Here are some of the most popular tourist spots to visit when in North and West:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Singapore Discovery Centre: The Singapore Discovery Centre is dedicated to showcasing the nation’s story from its founding to its present-day achievements.

  • Changi Chapel and Museum: This museum is a reminder of the horrors of the Japanese Occupation during World War II.

  • Lee Kong Chian Natural History Museum: Visitors can get a glimpse of Singapore’s natural history and see over 2,000 specimens of flora and fauna.

  • Sun Yat Sen Nanyang Memorial Hall: This museum was established to commemorate Dr. Sun Yat Sen who was a key figure in the Chinese Revolution.

  • Asian Civilizations Museum: The Asian Civilizations Museum showcases over 1,300 exhibits that highlight different cultures in Asia.

Natural Attractions of North and West: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Bukit Timah Nature Reserve: Bukit Timah Nature Reserve is a popular spot for hiking and biking trails that offers scenic views of the city from the top.

  • Singapore Botanic Gardens: This is a UNESCO World Heritage Site and is home to more than 10,000 species of plants and flowers.

  • Jurong Bird Park: Jurong Bird Park is the largest bird park in Asia and is home to over 5,000 birds including 400 species.

  • Night Safari: The Night Safari is a unique experience that offers visitors the chance to observe nocturnal animals up close.

  • Hindhede Nature Park: This park is located near the Bukit Batok Town Park and offers hiking trails that lead through lush forest areas.

Hiking/Biking Routes of North and West

For outdoor enthusiasts, the North and West regions of Singapore offer several scenic hiking and biking trails. Here are some popular options:

  • MacRitchie Reservoir Trail: This trail is a 10km loop that takes you around the reservoir and through the forest.

  • Bukit Timah Hill Trail: This trail leads to the top of the Bukit Timah hill and offers stunning views of the surrounding areas.

  • Chestnut Nature Park: Chestnut Nature Park has multiple trails that offer a range of difficulty levels, from easy walks to challenging treks.

Best Things to Do in North and West: Activities and Tours

There are plenty of activities and tours to enjoy when in North and West, such as:

  • Marina Bay Sands: The Marina Bay Sands is one of the most iconic buildings in Singapore, housing a variety of attractions and activities such as the infinity pool, observation deck and Art Museum.

  • Water Sports: Singapore’s North and West regions offer many kinds of water sports, such as kayaking and paddleboarding around the local islands, which offer scenic views of the city-state.

  • The Singapore Zoo: The Singapore Zoo is world-famous and perfect for a day out with the family. Here, you can see over 2,800 specimens of animals.

  • Art and Culture Tour: Many art galleries and exhibitions are springing up around Singapore, and North and West offer plenty of options. Take a trip to Gillman Barracks or wander down the Art Walk in West Coast Park.

Week-Long Itinerary for North and West

If you’re planning on visiting Singapore, a week-long itinerary of North and West could look something like this:

  • Day 1: Visit the Singapore Zoo and Night Safari
  • Day 2: Take a stroll through the Singapore Botanic Gardens
  • Day 3: Go hiking at Bukit Timah Hill and visit the nearby Nature Reserve
  • Day 4: Explore the museums in North and West
  • Day 5: Try some water sports around the island; take a trip to Jurong Bird Park
  • Day 6: Take an Art and Culture Tour around the city
  • Day 7: Go souvenir shopping before your flight

Food and Drink Scene of North and West

Singapore has plenty of delicious local food to try, and North and West regions offer some unique dining experiences as well:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Chilli Crab: It’s a must-try dish in Singapore, and Jumbo Seafood serves some of the best in North and West.

  • Biryani: Indian-Muslim food is considered a must-try in Singapore, and You Never Have Enough 24 Hours is a popular restaurant for it.

  • Satay: Any visit to Singapore is incomplete without trying Satay. The Satay Club at Lau Pa Sat offers delicious skewers and other local street food.

Local Beverages

  • Soya Bean Milk: This refreshing drink is popular in Singapore, including the North and West regions, and it’s available from many local hawker centers and food courts.

  • Teh Tarik: This sweet, milky tea is a must-try for tea lovers, and is easily found at many hawker centers and food courts around town.

Where to Stay in North and West: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

There are many options for accommodation around the North and West regions of Singapore:

  • Luxury Hotels: Marina Bay Sands and Four Seasons Hotel are popular options for a luxurious stay.

  • Budget Accommodation: Hostels and guesthouses are becoming increasingly popular in Singapore, and the North and West regions have many options such as QUALITY HOSTELS.

What to Buy in North and West: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

If you’re looking to take home a little piece of Singapore, here are some things to consider:

  • Souvenir T-Shirts: Hawker centers and markets offer a wide variety of clothing and items, including some tongue-in-cheek Singaporean tees.

  • Local Products: Supermarkets carry various local products such as chili sauce, black pepper sauce, and curry powder.

Local Culture and Customs of North and West

Singapore has a diverse mix of cultures, religions, and ethnicities. Exploring the different neighborhoods of North and West regions is the best way to experience the local culture, customs, and traditions. Take a stroll through Chinatown, Little India, and Kampung Glam for a true Singaporean experience.

Best Time to Visit North and West

The best time to visit North and West regions is between February and April when the weather is dry and less humid. The temperatures are also temperate and pleasant during this time, making it ideal for outdoor activities.

Do’s and Don’ts of North and West

Here are some essential do’s and don’ts to keep in mind when exploring the North and West regions:

Do:

  • Respect the local customs and traditions.
  • Try the local food and drinks.
  • Dress modestly when visiting religious sites.
  • Carry a water bottle and sunscreen.

Don’t:

  • Litter in public spaces.
  • Smoke in public areas.
  • Chew gum in public areas.

Safety and Health

Safety in North and West

Singapore is a safe city, but it’s always wise to take necessary precautions. Here are some tips:

  • Keep an eye on your belongings and important documents.
  • Use common sense, and be aware of your surroundings.
  • Follow traffic rules when cycling and walking.
  • Don’t carry large sums of cash.

Health in North and West

Singapore has excellent healthcare facilities that cater to visitors’ medical needs. It’s mandatory to have travel insurance when visiting, and visitors should always carry their policy documents with them.

Getting to North and West: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near North and West

  • Changi Airport is the gateway to Singapore, and it’s located in the East region.
  • Seletar Airport is a private airport that caters to private jets and is located in the North region.

Bus Stations Near North and West

  • The Woodlands Bus Interchange is in the North region.
  • Boon Lay Bus Interchange is in the Western region.

Other Options

  • Taxis and ride-hailing services like Grab are available throughout Singapore.

How to Get Around in North and West

There are several modes of transport to get around North and West:

  • MRT: Singapore’s subway, is a quick and efficient way to move around Singapore.
  • Buses: Singapore has an extensive bus network, and most buses cover North and West regions.
  • Taxis and Private Hire Cars: Hiring a taxi or private hire car is also an option and is widely available.

Where to Go Next from North and West: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

When you’re done exploring North and West regions, here are some other nearby destinations and excursions for more exploring:

  • Sentosa Island: Sentosa Island is a popular tourist destination and is home to attractions such as Universal Studios and other theme parks.

  • Pulau Ubin: Pulau Ubin offers a peaceful and serene escape from city life and has preserved the traditional Malay Kampung lifestyle.

  • Marina Bay: Marina Bay is in the Central region offers great views of the city’s skyline and iconic attractions like the Merlion and Gardens by the Bay.

Conclusion

North and West regions of Singapore offer a wide variety of options for travelers. From natural wonders to cultural landmarks, scenic hiking and biking trails to food indulgence, one can experience the best of Singapore here. Keep our guide handy and enjoy this unique city-state experience to its fullest.
