Introduction to Kampong Cham

Located in the Mekong Lowlands and Central Plains of Cambodia, Kampong Cham is a charming city with a vibrant culture and history. It is situated on the banks of the Mekong River and is home to various historical landmarks, cultural attractions, and spectacular natural sites. Kampong Cham is the largest province in Cambodia and is a popular destination for travelers looking to experience the country’s rich cultural heritage and natural beauty.

List of Places to Visit in Kampong Cham

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Phnom Srei: This hilltop temple offers stunning panoramic views of Kampong Cham and its surrounding areas.
  • Wat Nokor: A unique temple that combines both Hindu and Buddhist architecture.
  • Koh Pen Beach: A beautiful, secluded beach with crystal clear waters that offers a peaceful escape from the city.
  • French Colonial Architecture: Take a stroll through the city’s old quarter to admire the stunning French colonial architecture.
  • Phnom Hanchey: A sacred hilltop temple that offers a breathtaking view of the Mekong River and the surrounding plains.

Natural Attractions of Kampong Cham: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Mekong River: Take a relaxing boat cruise on the Mekong River and witness the local life along its banks.
  • Banteay Prei Nokor: A peaceful, ancient temple situated among beautiful rice paddies.
  • Kizuna Bridge: A beautiful bridge that spans the Mekong River, offering spectacular views of the surrounding area.
  • Phnom Daun Penh: A scenic hill that offers stunning views of the city and its surroundings.
  • Tonlé San River: A river that is popular for its beautiful scenery, fishing, and water activities.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Kampong Cham

  • Bike Tour of Kampong Cham: Rent a bike and explore the city and its beautiful countryside at your own pace.
  • Biking Along Mekong River: A scenic bike ride along the Mekong River that offers beautiful riverside scenery.
  • Kampong Cham Countryside Tour: A tour that takes you through the beautiful countryside of Kampong Cham.

Best Things to Do in Kampong Cham: Activities and Tours

  • Visit Traditional Villages: Take a guided tour to the nearby traditional villages to experience the rural life of Cambodia.
  • Explore the Temples: Visit the ancient temples and learn about their history and significance.
  • Boat Cruise on the Mekong River: Take a relaxing boat cruise on the Mekong River and witness local life along its banks.
  • Relax on the Beach: Enjoy a relaxing day on the beautiful Koh Pen Beach.
  • Explore the City: Take a leisurely stroll through the city and admire the stunning French colonial architecture.

Food and Drink Scene of Kampong Cham

Must-Try Dishes

  • Amok: A traditional Khmer dish made with fish or meat, coconut milk, and spices, steamed and served in a banana leaf.
  • Lok Lak: A popular Cambodian dish made with beef, stir-fried with onions, tomatoes, and peppers and served with rice and a fried egg.
  • Fish Curry: A spicy and flavorful curry made with locally caught fish and served with rice.

Local Beverages

  • Cambodian Beer: Try the locally brewed Cambodian beer, such as Angkor beer, which is light and refreshing.
  • Sugar cane juice: Made by juicing freshly harvested sugar cane, this drink is sweet, refreshing, and a perfect choice for a hot day.

Where to Stay in Kampong Cham: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Mekong Hotel: A luxurious hotel situated along the Mekong River.
  • River Dolphin Hotel: A beautiful hotel located in the center of Kampong Cham.
  • Rajabori Villas Resort: A peaceful resort located in the countryside, offering beautiful scenery and quiet surroundings.

What to Buy in Kampong Cham: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Krama: A traditional Cambodian scarf made of cotton, silk, or wool.
  • Sampot: A traditional Cambodian garment worn by both men and women.
  • Silver Jewelry: Kampong Cham is known for its beautiful silver jewelry, which makes for a great souvenir.

Local Culture and Customs of Kampong Cham

  • Buddhism: Cambodia is largely Buddhist, and it is important to respect local customs and traditions while visiting Buddhist temples and other religious sites.
  • Etiquette: It is important to dress modestly and remove shoes before entering temples or other religious sites.
  • Greetings: Cambodians are known for their friendly and welcoming nature, and it is customary to greet people with a smile and a polite nod.

Best Time to Visit Kampong Cham

The best time to visit Kampong Cham is between November and March when the weather is dry and pleasant, and the temperature is comfortable.

Do’s and Don’ts of Kampong Cham

Do:

  • Respect local customs and traditions.
  • Dress modestly when visiting religious sites.
  • Carry a copy of your passport and visa at all times.

Don’t:

  • Touch someone’s head, especially children, as it is considered disrespectful.
  • Raise your voice or show aggression in public.
  • Take photographs of people without their permission.

Safety and Health

Safety in Kampong Cham

Kampong Cham is a relatively safe destination for travelers. However, it is important to take basic safety precautions such as keeping your valuables secure and being aware of your surroundings while walking in crowded areas.

Health in Kampong Cham

It is important to take precautions when it comes to food and water while visiting Kampong Cham. Drink only bottled or boiled water and avoid eating raw, uncooked food. It is also recommended to get vaccinated before traveling to Cambodia.

Getting to Kampong Cham: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Kampong Cham

The nearest airport to Kampong Cham is Phnom Penh International Airport, located approximately 125 km. away from the city. From there, you can catch a bus or taxi to Kampong Cham.

Bus Stations Near Kampong Cham

There are several bus stations located in Kampong Cham, which offer bus services to various destinations across Cambodia.

Other Options

You can also reach Kampong Cham by taxi or hire a private car with a driver. This option can be expensive but offers more privacy and flexibility.

How to Get Around in Kampong Cham

The best way to get around Kampong Cham is by tuk-tuk or motorbike taxi, which are readily available throughout the city.

Where to Go Next from Kampong Cham: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Phnom Penh: The capital city of Cambodia, Phnom Penh, is a bustling city that offers plenty of cultural and historical attractions.
  • Siem Reap: Home to the famous Angkor Wat, Siem Reap is a popular destination for travelers.
  • Kratié: Located along the Mekong River, Kratié is a quiet and peaceful town that offers a glimpse into traditional Cambodian life.

Conclusion

Kampong Cham is a beautiful and charming city that offers travelers a unique experience of Cambodia’s rich cultural heritage and stunning natural beauty. With its ancient temples, beautiful countryside, and scenic vistas, Kampong Cham is the perfect destination for those looking to explore the country’s rural charm and authentic culture. So pack your bags and get ready for an unforgettable journey to Kampong Cham.
