Introduction to Kampong Thom

Located in the Mekong Lowlands and Central Plains of Cambodia, Kampong Thom is a hidden gem that boasts of breathtaking natural landscapes, historical sites, and cultural attractions. It is often overlooked by tourists but remains a popular destination amongst locals.

The city is divided into eight districts, each offering its unique charm and character. Visitors are often drawn to the ancient ruins of Sambor Prei Kuk, which is a UNESCO World Heritage Site.

Travelers who enjoy the path less traveled will find Kampong Thom an exciting destination. It is easy to fall in love with the small-town vibe as the locals are friendly and welcoming. Kampong Thom is a destination for travelers looking for a more authentic Cambodian experience.

List of Places to Visit in Kampong Thom

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Sambor Prei Kuk

Sambor Prei Kuk is an ancient city located 30 kilometers north of Kampong Thom town. Dating back to the 6th century, it is now listed as a UNESCO World Heritage Site. Visitors can explore the numerous temples and shrines with intricate carvings and sculptures.

Prasat Andet

Prasat Andet is a small temple complex that dates back to the 7th century. It is located seven kilometers north-west of Kampong Thom and features well-preserved carvings that depict various Hindu deities.

Wat Kampong Thom

Wat Kampong Thom is a relatively new temple, built-in 1955. It is a visually stunning temple filled with intricate carvings and sculptures. Visitors can see its famous reclining Buddha statue as well.

Natural Attractions of Kampong Thom: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Boeng Peae Wildlife Sanctuary

Boeng Peae Wildlife Sanctuary is a protected area located in the southern part of Kampong Thom. Established in 1993, the sanctuary offers a perfect mix of wetlands, savannas, and forests that is home to a variety of flora and fauna, including endangered bird species.

Prey Pros

Prey Pros is a popular picnic and camping spot that is located near Stung Saen river. Visitors can enjoy swimming, kayaking, or even just relaxing in the tranquil atmosphere.

Steung Chinit

Steung Chinit is a beautiful waterfall located 20 kilometers northeast of Kampong Thom. Visitors can opt for a refreshing dip in the cascade pool or trek their way to the top for panoramic views of the surrounding jungle.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Kampong Thom

Kampong Thom’s stunning natural landscapes and historical sites offer fantastic opportunities for hikers and bikers. Visitors can choose from a range of short treks to multi-day bike tours.

Sambor Prei Kuk to Prasat Kampong Thom

A popular route among hikers and worth every step. The route covers over 150 ancient temples, traditional Cambodian villages, and breathtaking jungle landscapes.

Kampong Thom Loop

The Kampong Thom Loop is an excellent option for those looking for a multi-day bike tour that covers Kampong Thom’s best natural and cultural attractions. Visitors can explore Sambor Prei Kuk, Boeng Peae Wildlife Sanctuary, and the stunning Steung Chinit waterfall.

Best Things to Do in Kampong Thom: Activities and Tours

Guided Tours

Guided tours are a popular option for those who want to learn more about Kampong Thom’s history and culture. Visitors can choose from a range of tours, including temple tours, bike tours, and nature tours.

Cooking Classes

One way to immerse oneself in Cambodian culture is by taking part in a traditional Khmer cooking class. Visitors can learn about local ingredients and flavors while preparing delicious dishes.

Week-Long Itinerary for Kampong Thom

Kampong Thom’s location makes it an ideal stopover for travelers heading to Siem Reap or Phnom Penh. A week-long itinerary can cover Kampong Thom’s major attractions and offer travelers plenty of time to explore at a leisurely pace.

Day 1 - Sambor Prei Kuk

Begin your trip by exploring the ancient ruins of Sambor Prei Kuk, a UNESCO World Heritage Site.

Day 2 - Prasat Andet

Visit the small temple complex of Prasat Andet and marvel at the intricate carvings and sculptures.

Day 3 - Boeng Peae Wildlife Sanctuary

Take a nature tour or hike through Boeng Peae Wildlife Sanctuary and discover Cambodia’s rich wildlife.

Day 4 - Prey Pros

Spend the day relaxing, picnicking, or kayaking at Prey Pros.

Day 5 - Steung Chinit Waterfall

Trek your way to the top of Steung Chinit Waterfall for panoramic views of the surrounding jungle.

Day 6 - Guided Tours

Take part in a guided tour to learn about Kampong Thom’s history and culture.

Day 7 - Cooking Class

End your week-long trip with a traditional Khmer cooking class and indulge in delicious dishes.

Food and Drink Scene of Kampong Thom

Must-Try Dishes

Samlor Korko

Samlor Korko is a flavorful Cambodian soup filled with vegetables, meats, and herbs.

Num Banh Chok

Num Banh Chok is a popular Cambodian breakfast dish that consists of rice noodles and fish sauce.

Local Beverages

Sugar Cane Juice

Sugar cane juice is a refreshing drink made from pressed sugarcane. It is a popular drink amongst locals and a great way to cool down in the hot weather.

Angkor Beer

Angkor Beer is Cambodia’s leading beer brand, and a great way to indulge in the local beer scene.

Where to Stay in Kampong Thom: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Sambor Village Hotel

Sambor Village Hotel is a luxury eco-friendly hotel that offers guests unique Khmer-style accommodations that are surrounded by lush tropical gardens.

Sovannphum Guest House

Sovannphum Guest House offers guests comfortable and affordable accommodation in the heart of Kampong Thom.

What to Buy in Kampong Thom: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Handicrafts

Kampong Thom is known for its intricate handicrafts made from local silk, bamboo, and rattan.

Kramas

Kramas are traditional Cambodian scarfs that are made from cotton and come in various colors and patterns.

Local Culture and Customs of Kampong Thom

Kampong Thom is a predominantly rural province, and the locals are welcoming and friendly. Visitors are encouraged to take their shoes off before entering temples, and it is considered disrespectful to touch people’s heads.

Best Time to Visit Kampong Thom

November to January is the ideal time to visit Kampong Thom, as the climate is temperate and offers visitors the perfect weather for outdoor activities.

Do’s and Don’ts of Kampong Thom

Do:

  • Dress modestly when visiting temples and public areas.
  • Take part in a traditional Khmer cooking class.
  • Learn Khmer words and phrases.

Don’t:

  • Disrespect the locals and their customs.
  • Touch people’s heads.
  • Use your feet to point or touch anything.

Safety and Health

Safety in Kampong Thom

Kampong Thom is a safe destination, with low rates of crime against tourists. Visitors are urged to take standard safety precautions, such as keeping an eye on their belongings and avoiding unlit areas at night.

Health in Kampong Thom

It is essential to take standard health precautions when traveling to Kampong Thom. Visitors are advised to drink bottled or boiled water, wear insect repellent, and get vaccinated against common diseases such as hepatitis A and B, and typhoid fever.

Getting to Kampong Thom

Airports Near Kampong Thom

Siem Reap International Airport is the nearest airport to Kampong Thom, approximately 90 kilometers away.

Bus Stations Near Kampong Thom

There are several bus stations in Kampong Thom that provide affordable and convenient transportation options.

Other Options

Taxis, tuk-tuks, and motorbikes are available for hire in Kampong Thom.

How to Get Around in Kampong Thom

Tuk-tuks, taxis, and motorbikes are the most popular transportation options in Kampong Thom.

Where to Go Next from Kampong Thom: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Siem Reap

Siem Reap is the gateway to the world-renowned Angkor Wat, which is a UNESCO World Heritage Site. Visitors can explore ancient temple ruins and immerse themselves in Khmer culture.

Phnom Penh

Phnom Penh is the capital city of Cambodia and a bustling metropolis filled with historical sites, cultural attractions, and a vibrant nightlife.

Conclusion

Kampong Thom remains one of Cambodia’s best-kept secrets that offer travelers the perfect mix of culture, history, and nature. From the ancient ruins of Sambor Prei Kuk to the scenic beauty of Boeng Peae Wildlife Sanctuary, Kampong Thom is a magical destination that should not be missed.
