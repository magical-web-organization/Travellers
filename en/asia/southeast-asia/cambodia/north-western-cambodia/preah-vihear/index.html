A Comprehensive Travel Guide to Preah Vihear, Cambodia

Preah Vihear is a magnificent site located in the North-western region of Cambodia. It is a temple dedicated to Lord Shiva and was built during the Khmer Empire. The temple stands on the edge of a plateau, offering panoramic views of the surrounding countryside. Preah Vihear has become one of Cambodia’s major tourist destinations and is a UNESCO World Heritage site. In this comprehensive travel guide, we will explore the best places to visit, things to do, where to stay, and more in Preah Vihear, Cambodia.

Introduction to Preah Vihear

Preah Vihear is located in the Dangrek Mountains within the Preah Vihear Province along the border between Cambodia and Thailand. It is one of Cambodia’s most impressive ancient sites and is situated on a spectacular cliff that offers a breathtaking view of the surroundings. There are two ways to access the temple, either from the Thai or the Cambodian side, but the most common and safest way is from Cambodia. The temple is renowned for its extraordinary carvings and intricate lintels that depict scenes from Hindu mythology.

List of Places to Visit in Preah Vihear

Preah Vihear is a vast site that offers a range of attractions for visitors to explore. You can explore the historically significant sites or dive into natural wonders. Following are the places one must visit while in Preah Vihear:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  1. The Preah Vihear Temple: This ancient temple is the main attraction in Preah Vihear. It is located on the plateau of Dangrek Mountain and is a UNESCO World Heritage Site.

  2. Koh Ker Temple: Located 100 km northeast of Siem Reap, it is a recently opened temple that was built during the Khmer Empire.

  3. Koh Ker Temple City: One of the great ancient cities of the 10th century features the famous Prang Temple. It covers an area of ​​about 81 hectares.

  4. Bakan Sanctuary: One of the most mysterious and remote sanctuaries on the outskirts of the ancient Khmer Khmer Empire that was just recently opened to the public.

Natural Attractions of Preah Vihear: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  1. The Dangrek Mountains: The Dangrek Mountains offer a great opportunity for hiking, bird-watching, and photography.

  2. Veal Prais Mountain: Veal Prais Mountain offers enjoyable trekking through lush green forests.

  3. River canoeing tours: The Mekong and Tonle Sap rivers offer an excellent chance for visitors to enjoy canoeing tours and see the natural sights and wildlife.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Preah Vihear

Preah Vihear is an ideal place for hiking and biking lovers. The jungle surrounding the temple has some excellent hiking and biking trails that offer stunning views of the surrounding countryside.

Best Things to Do in Preah Vihear: Activities and Tours

  1. Take a tour of the Preah Vihear Temple: Take a guided tour of the temple and explore its intricate carvings and statues.

  2. Explore the Dangrek Mountains: Take a hiking tour of the Dangrek Mountains and explore the beautiful natural scenery that surrounds the temple.

  3. Visit the Bakan Sanctuary: Take a guided tour of the Bakan Sanctuary and learn about the site’s historical significance.

Week-Long Itinerary for Preah Vihear

Day 1: Explore the Preah Vihear Temple Day 2: Hike the Dangrek Mountains Day 3: Visit the Bakan Sanctuary Day 4: Go on a canoeing tour of the Mekong and Tonle Sap rivers Day 5: Take a bike tour of the surrounding countryside Day 6: Visit Koh Ker Temple City Day 7: Relax and shop at the local market

Food and Drink Scene of Preah Vihear

The food culture of Preah Vihear is heavily influenced by the Khmer culture. Here are some of the must-try dishes and local beverages you must try while visiting:

Must-Try Dishes

  1. Amok Fish Curry: It is one of the signature dishes of Cambodia, made with fish, coconut milk, and various spices.
  2. Num Banh Chok: Rice noodles served with a fish-based broth.
  3. Lok Lak: A beef stir-fry with oyster sauce served with vegetables and rice.

Local Beverages

  1. Angkor Beer: Cambodia’s most famous beer, brewed locally.
  2. Palm Wine: A traditional alcoholic beverage made from palm trees.

What to Buy in Preah Vihear: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Here are some of the unique souvenirs and local products you must buy while visiting Preah Vihear:

  1. Traditional Khmer silk: It is a beautiful and high-quality fabric that comes in various colors and textures.

  2. Handicrafts: There are many handicrafts available in the local markets, such as wooden sculptures, hand-woven baskets, and pottery.

Local Culture and Customs of Preah Vihear

The Khmer culture and traditions heavily influence the local culture of Preah Vihear. There are some etiquettes that one must follow while in Preah Vihear:

  1. Remove shoes before entering temples or someone’s house.
  2. Always dress appropriately, and cover your shoulders and knees while entering religious places.
  3. Greeting each other with your hands placed together, fingers pointing upward regardless of age, social class, or gender.

Best Time to Visit Preah Vihear

The best time to visit Preah Vihear is during the dry season, from November to March. The temperature is mild, and the humidity is low. Moreover, the temple is open year-round, but visiting during the rainy season can be challenging and can spoil your trip.

Do’s and Don’ts of Preah Vihear

Do:

  1. Respect the local culture and traditions.
  2. Carry cash as ATMs are not readily available in the area.
  3. Keep yourself hydrated while exploring the temple.

Don’t:

  1. Don’t use temples and other religious sites as a backdrop for photography.
  2. Don’t touch or move any artifacts or exhibits.
  3. Don’t take photos with monks without their permission.

Safety and Health in Preah Vihear

Safety in Preah Vihear

Preah Vihear is considered a safe destination for travelers, but still, one must exercise caution in public places and take care of your belongings.

Health in Preah Vihear

Preah Vihear is a malaria-free zone but is at risk for dengue fever. Travelers must take measures like using DEET-based mosquito repellent to avoid getting ill.
