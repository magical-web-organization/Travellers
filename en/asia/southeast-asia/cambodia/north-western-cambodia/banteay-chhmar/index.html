Introduction to Banteay Chhmar

Located in northwestern Cambodia, Banteay Chhmar is a hidden gem that offers visitors a chance to step back in time and explore the rich history and culture of Cambodia. This small town is home to one of the largest and most impressive Angkorian temples outside of Angkor Wat, making it a must-visit destination for anyone interested in ancient architecture and history.

List of Places to Visit in Banteay Chhmar

There are plenty of sights to see and activities to do in Banteay Chhmar. Here are just a few:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Banteay Chhmar Temple - this 12th-century temple is the main attraction in town and is known for its intricate carvings and rich history.
  • Jayavarman VII Hospital - also known as Ta Prohm, this hospital and Buddhist monastery was built during the reign of Jayavarman VII.
  • Banteay Top - this small temple is located on top of a nearby hill and offers stunning views of the surrounding countryside.
  • Banteay Chhmar Community-Based Tourism - this organization offers visitors the chance to learn about local culture and traditions through guided tours and homestays.

Natural Attractions of Banteay Chhmar: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • K5 Ecotourism - this organization offers eco-tours of the surrounding forests and wetlands, which are home to a wide variety of wildlife and bird species.
  • Banteay Neang - this small temple is located on the banks of a tranquil lake and offers a peaceful retreat from the hustle and bustle of town.
  • Phnom Lovea - a nearby natural attraction with stunning waterfall and pools, perfect for swimming and relaxing.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Banteay Chhmar

Banteay Chhmar and its surrounding countryside offer plenty of opportunities for hiking and biking enthusiasts. Here are some of the best routes:

  • Banteay Chhmar Temple Circuit - a 17km loop around the temple complex, which takes visitors through small villages and scenic countryside.
  • The Ancient Trail - a 26km route that connects Banteay Chhmar with another nearby temple complex, Banteay Torp.
  • The Peace Trail - a 14km route that takes visitors on a journey through the region’s turbulent past, with stops at former Khmer Rouge strongholds and mass grave sites.

Best Things to Do in Banteay Chhmar: Activities and Tours

There are plenty of activities and tours to keep visitors busy during their stay in Banteay Chhmar:

  • Homestays with Local Families - offers a chance to immerse yourself in local culture and traditions, and learn about Cambodian cuisine.
  • Banteay Chhmar Community-Based Tourism - offers guided tours of the temple complex and surrounding countryside, as well as cooking classes and handicraft workshops.
  • Biking and Hiking Tours - explore the region’s natural beauty while getting some exercise.

Food and Drink Scene of Banteay Chhmar

Cambodian cuisine is often overlooked when compared to its better-known neighbours, but it is incredibly delicious and unique. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Amok - a popular Cambodian curry made with coconut milk, fish or meat, and spices, served with rice.
  • Bai Sach Chrouk - grilled pork marinated in garlic and coconut milk, served with rice and pickled vegetables.
  • Nom Banh Chok - a breakfast dish made with rice noodles, fish gravy, and fresh vegetables.

Local Beverages

  • Angkor Beer - Cambodia’s most popular beer, often enjoyed with meals.
  • Fruit Shakes - made with a variety of fresh fruits, including mango, pineapple, and banana, mixed with ice and sweetened condensed milk.
  • Sugar Cane Juice - a refreshing drink made by squeezing sugar cane stalks and mixing the juice with ice and lime juice.

Where to Stay in Banteay Chhmar: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

There are a variety of accommodation options in Banteay Chhmar, ranging from budget-friendly guesthouses to luxury hotels:

  • Banteay Chhmar Homestay - for an immersive cultural experience, stay with a local family and experience life in a traditional village.
  • Bunlin Guesthouse - a budget-friendly option located near the temple complex.
  • Soksabike - a unique accommodation option that also offers tours and experiences, including biking tours of the surrounding area.

What to Buy in Banteay Chhmar: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Banteay Chhmar is known for its handicrafts and textiles, which make great souvenirs to bring home:

  • Silk Scarves - made with locally sourced silk, in a variety of colors and designs.
  • Handwoven Baskets - made from natural materials, such as bamboo and rattan, and used for a variety of purposes, including carrying crops and goods.
  • Hand-Painted Silk - local artisans create one-of-a-kind pieces of art on silk fabric, which can be framed or used as a tablecloth.

Local Culture and Customs of Banteay Chhmar

Cambodian culture is rich and diverse, with ancient traditions still practiced today. Here are a few things to keep in mind when visiting Banteay Chhmar:

  • Dress Modestly - Cambodian culture places an emphasis on modesty, so it’s best to cover your shoulders and legs when visiting temples and other culturally significant places.
  • Remove Shoes - it’s customary to remove your shoes before entering homes and temples.
  • Be Respectful - Cambodian people are known for their kindness and hospitality, but it’s important to be aware of cultural norms and show respect for local traditions.

Best Time to Visit Banteay Chhmar

The best time to visit Banteay Chhmar is during the dry season, from November to April. The weather is mild and dry during this time, making it ideal for outdoor activities and sightseeing. However, if you don’t mind a bit of rain, the wet season can also be a good time to visit, as the countryside becomes lush and green.

Do’s and Don’ts of Banteay Chhmar

To have the best possible experience in Banteay Chhmar, here are some do’s and don’ts to keep in mind:

Do:

  • Learn a Few Words of Khmer - the local language is Khmer, and locals appreciate visitors who make an effort to speak a few words.
  • Try Local Cuisine - Cambodian cuisine is unique and delicious, so don’t be afraid to try something new.
  • Support Local Businesses - many businesses in Banteay Chhmar are locally owned and operated, so by supporting them, you’re helping to support the local economy.

Don’t:

  • Disrespect Religious Sites - always be mindful of the fact that you’re visiting culturally significant sites, and act accordingly.
  • Overtip - while tipping is appreciated, it’s not necessary to tip excessively.
  • Be Overly Familiar - Cambodian culture tends to be more formal than Western culture, so be aware of cultural norms and show respect for local customs.

Safety and Health

Banteay Chhmar is a safe destination for tourists, but it’s always important to take certain precautions when traveling abroad.

Safety in Banteay Chhmar

  • Petty Theft - as with any tourist destination, be aware of your surroundings and keep an eye on your belongings to avoid theft.
  • Transportation - use only licensed taxis and tuk-tuks, and avoid traveling at night if possible.
  • Wildlife - if hiking or exploring the nearby forests, be aware of potential wildlife hazards, such as snakes and spiders.

Health in Banteay Chhmar

  • Vaccinations - make sure you’re up-to-date on routine vaccinations before traveling to Cambodia, and consider getting a Hepatitis A and Typhoid vaccine.
  • Food and Water Safety - avoid drinking tap water and be wary of eating street food that has been sitting out for a long time.

Getting to Banteay Chhmar: Transportation Options and Logistics

Banteay Chhmar is a bit off the beaten path, but it’s still fairly easy to get to.

Airports Near Banteay Chhmar

  • Siem Reap International Airport - the nearest international airport, located about 160km away.
  • Banteay Meanchey Airport - a smaller airport located about 30km away from Banteay Chhmar, with limited flights to other cities in Cambodia.

Bus Stations Near Banteay Chhmar

  • Poipet Bus Station - located about 70km away from Banteay Chhmar, this bus station offers connections to other cities in Cambodia as well as Bangkok, Thailand.
  • Sisophon Bus Station - located about 45km away from Banteay Chhmar, this bus station offers connections to other cities in Cambodia.

Other Options

  • Private Car - hire a driver to take you from Siem Reap or Phnom Penh to Banteay Chhmar.
  • Motorbike - rent a motorbike from a nearby city and explore the countryside on your own.

How to Get Around in Banteay Chhmar

The best way to get around Banteay Chhmar is by tuk-tuk or bicycle.

  • Tuk-Tuk - a common mode of transportation in Cambodia, tuk-tuks are available for hire in Banteay Chhmar, and are a convenient way to get around town and the temple complex.
  • Bicycle - many hotels and guesthouses offer bicycle rentals, which is a great way to explore the surrounding countryside at your own pace.

Where to Go Next from Banteay Chhmar: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

If you’re looking to continue your Cambodian adventure, here are a few nearby destinations worth visiting:

  • Siem Reap - home to the world-famous Angkor Wat temple complex, as well as a vibrant nightlife and restaurant scene.
  • Battambang - a charming city known for its French colonial architecture and cultural sites.
  • Phnom Penh - the capital city of Cambodia, which offers a mix of history, culture, and modern amenities.

Conclusion

Banteay Chhmar may not be as well-known as some of its neighboring cities, but it offers visitors a unique opportunity to explore Cambodian culture and history in a peaceful and authentic setting. From the impressive temple complex to the natural beauty of its surrounding countryside, there is plenty to see and do for travelers of all interests. With this comprehensive guide in hand, you’ll be sure to have an unforgettable trip to Banteay Chhmar.
