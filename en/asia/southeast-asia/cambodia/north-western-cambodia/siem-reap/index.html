Introduction to Siem Reap

Siem Reap is a city located in northwestern Cambodia and is considered to be the gateway to Angkor Wat, a UNESCO World Heritage Site and one of the world’s most famous temples. Siem Reap has become a popular tourist destination due to its rich cultural history and beautiful natural attractions. The city is known for its vibrant nightlife, street markets, and delicious food. Siem Reap is an excellent destination for backpackers, families, and luxury travelers alike.

List of Places to Visit in Siem Reap

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Siem Reap is home to many historical sites and museums that tell the story of Cambodia’s rich and complex culture.

Angkor Wat

Angkor Wat, built in the 12th century, is the largest religious monument in the world and one of Cambodia’s proudest cultural treasures. A visit to Siem Reap is not complete without seeing the stunning temple complex.

Angkor Thom

Angkor Thom was once the capital of the Khmer Empire and is now a breathtaking archaeological site with many impressive temples and palaces.

Bayon Temple

Bayon Temple, located in Angkor Thom, is known for its 54 towers and more than 200 carved stone faces, making it one of the most intriguing and stunning temples in Siem Reap.

Preah Khan

Preah Khan served as a monastery and was later used as a royal palace. The temple was constructed in the 12th century and is known for its beautiful carvings and sculptures.

National Museum of Cambodia

The National Museum of Cambodia is located in Siem Reap and showcases the country’s rich cultural heritage, from the pre-Angkorian period to modern times.

Natural Attractions of Siem Reap: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Siem Reap also offers a wealth of natural attractions for outdoor enthusiasts and nature lovers.

Tonle Sap Lake

Tonle Sap Lake is the largest freshwater lake in Southeast Asia and offers breathtaking views of rural Cambodia.

Phnom Kulen

Phnom Kulen is a sacred mountain located in the heart of Siem Reap and is known for its beautiful waterfalls and ancient temple ruins.

Biking or Hiking the Siem Reap Countryside

The countryside around Siem Reap is dotted with rice paddies, small villages, and Buddhist temples. Hiking or biking through the countryside is an excellent way to experience rural Cambodia.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Siem Reap

Siem Reap has a variety of hiking and biking routes to suit all experience levels.

Tonle Sap Lake

The lake is a popular spot for kayaking and fishing, and there are many organized tours available for those who are interested in experiencing the area’s natural beauty.

Angkor Wat Temple Complex

The Angkor Wat complex has many paths and trails that are perfect for hiking and biking. Visitors can explore the temples alone or hire a guide to take them on a tour of the ruins.

Kulen Mountain

Phnom Kulen is a beautiful and sacred mountain that is perfect for hiking. The mountain has several waterfalls, including the famous Champey waterfall, and ancient temple ruins that are worth exploring.

Best Things to Do in Siem Reap: Activities and Tours

There are many things to do in Siem Reap, from exploring temples and museums to enjoying the city’s vibrant nightlife.

Angkor Wat Sunrise Tour

Watching the sunrise over Angkor Wat is an unforgettable experience. Many tour companies offer early morning tours of the temple complex, complete with a guide and transportation.

Khmer Cooking Class

Learn how to cook traditional Khmer dishes in a cooking class with a local chef. This is an excellent way to learn about the local cuisine and culture.

Cambodian Circus

The Phare Circus is a nonprofit that provides opportunities for emerging Cambodian performers. The circus is known for its high-energy performances and impressive acrobatics.

Week-Long Itinerary for Siem Reap

A week in Siem Reap allows visitors to experience the city’s main attractions and natural beauty. Recommended itinerary:

Day 1: Angkor Wat Sunrise Tour and Exploring the Temples

Start your trip with a sunrise tour of Angkor Wat, followed by exploring the temple complex.

Day 2: Biking or Hiking Through the Siem Reap Countryside

Rent a bike and explore the Siem Reap countryside or go hiking in Phnom Kulen.

Day 3: Visiting the National Museum of Cambodia

Learn about Cambodian history and culture at the National Museum.

Day 4: Exploring Angkor Thom and Bayon Temple

Visit the ancient walled city of Angkor Thom and the stunning Bayon Temple.

Day 5: Tonle Sap Lake

Take a boat tour of Tonle Sap Lake and visit one of the floating villages.

Day 6: Cambodian Cooking Class

Learn how to cook traditional Khmer dishes in a cooking class with a local chef.

Day 7: Shopping in Siem Reap

Explore the city’s markets and buy some souvenirs before leaving Siem Reap.

Food and Drink Scene of Siem Reap

Cambodian cuisine is a blend of French, Thai, Chinese, and Vietnamese influences, creating a unique culinary experience. Siem Reap is known for its street food and night markets.

Must-Try Dishes

  • Fish Amok: A traditional Khmer dish made with freshwater fish, coconut milk, and lemongrass.
  • Beef Lok Lak: A stir-fry made with tender beef and served with rice and a fried egg.
  • Khmer Curry: A spicy, coconut-based curry made with chicken, potatoes, and vegetables.
  • Num Pang: A Cambodian sandwich made with crusty bread, meat, vegetables, and spices.

Local Beverages

  • Angkor Beer: A popular Cambodian beer.
  • Sugar Cane Juice: A refreshing and sweet drink made with fresh sugar cane.
  • Cambodia Coffee: A strong, sweet coffee made with condensed milk.

Where to Stay in Siem Reap: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Siem Reap offers a range of accommodation options to suit all budgets.

  • High-End Hotels: Raffles Grand Hotel d’Angkor, Belmond La Residence d’Angkor, Park Hyatt Siem Reap
  • Mid-Range Hotels: Tara Angkor Hotel, Shinta Mani Shack, Borei Angkor Resort & Spa
  • Budget Hostels and Guesthouses: Onederz Hostel, Siem Reap Pub Hostel, Viroth’s Villa

What to Buy in Siem Reap: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Siem Reap is known for its unique handicrafts, textiles, and artwork. Popular souvenirs include:

  • Silk Scarves and Clothing
  • Handwoven Baskets and Bags
  • Handmade Ceramics and Pottery
  • Carved Wooden Statues and Figurines

Local Culture and Customs of Siem Reap

Cambodian culture is heavily influenced by Buddhism, and visitors should dress conservatively and show respect for local customs and traditions. Photography is allowed at most historical sites, but visitors should be mindful of their surroundings and not disrupt any religious ceremonies.

Best Time to Visit Siem Reap

The best time to visit Siem Reap is from November to February, when the weather is cool and dry. March to May can be hot, and the rainy season lasts from June to October.

Do’s and Don’ts of Siem Reap

Do:

  • Dress modestly at temples and religious sites.
  • Show respect for local customs and traditions.
  • Bargain at the markets, but be polite and respectful.
  • Try the local cuisine and drinks.

Don’t:

  • Disrespect the Buddha or other religious symbols.
  • Take any artifacts or souvenirs from archaeological sites.
  • Criticize the monarchy or government.
  • Overstay your visa.

Safety and Health

Safety in Siem Reap

Siem Reap is generally a safe city, but visitors should be cautious when wandering alone at night and avoid carrying large amounts of cash. It is also important to watch out for pickpockets at crowded markets and tourist attractions.

Health in Siem Reap

Visitors should drink bottled water and avoid tap water, which may be contaminated. It is also recommended to get up-to-date on routine vaccinations before visiting Cambodia.

Getting to Siem Reap: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Siem Reap

  • Siem Reap International Airport (REP)

Bus Stations Near Siem Reap

  • Siem Reap Bus Station
  • Phnom Penh Sorya Transport
  • Giant Ibis Transport

Other Options

  • Tuk-tuks and taxis are readily available in Siem Reap and provide a convenient way to get around the city.

How to Get Around in Siem Reap

Tuk-tuks and bicycles are the most popular modes of transportation in Siem Reap. Visitors can also rent motorbikes, cars, or book a private car with a driver.

Where to Go Next from Siem Reap: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Phnom Penh: The capital city of Cambodia is home to many historical sites and museums, including the Royal Palace and the Killing Fields.
  • Kampot: A charming riverside town famous for its pepper plantations and beautiful natural scenery.
  • Sihanoukville: A seaside port city and popular tourist destination with many beautiful beaches.

Conclusion

Siem Reap is a must-visit destination for anyone interested in history, culture, and natural beauty. The city is welcoming and friendly, and visitors will enjoy discovering its unique cuisine, handicrafts, and art. With numerous attractions and activities, there is never a dull moment in Siem Reap. Plan your trip today and experience the magic of this fascinating Cambodian city.
