Introduction to Pursat

Located in the Cardamom and Elephant Mountains in Cambodia, Pursat is a charming and tranquil destination for travelers seeking an authentic experience. It is home to rich cultural heritage, natural attractions, and outdoor adventures. Agriculture is the backbone of this town with rice paddies, sugar cane, fruits, and vegetables.

List of Places to Visit in Pursat

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

The Vat Sala Kpaos

The Vat Sala Kpaos temple, located in the town center, is a must-visit attraction for history buffs. This Buddhist temple, built in the early 19th century, showcases beautiful Khmer architecture, intricate carvings, and exquisite sculptures.

The Phnom Ba Klas

Another historical site that you must visit is the Phnom Ba Klas temple located on top of a mountain. This temple is an important spiritual site for the locals and offers a breathtaking view of the surrounding landscapes.

The Canal des Étangs

The Canal des Étangs is a historic canal constructed in the late 13th century. This canal was once used to transport rice and other goods to other parts of the country. Today, it is a great spot for a leisurely walk, picnic, or boat ride.

Natural Attractions of Pursat: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

The Cardamom Mountains

The Cardamom Mountains, located to the west of Pursat, offer a variety of outdoor adventures. Hiking trails, mountain biking areas, and wildlife sanctuaries make it a nature lover’s paradise. The waterfalls at the base of the mountains offer a beautiful and refreshing respite from the heat.

Pursat River

Pursat River is another natural attraction that is worth exploring. Take a boat ride along the river, and you will be awed by the beautiful scenery and wildlife along the banks. The river is also popular for fishing and kayaking.

The Tonle Sap Lake

The Tonle Sap Lake is the largest freshwater lake in Southeast Asia and is located 60 kilometers north of Pursat. The lake is home to a diverse range of fish, and it is fascinating to watch the local fishermen at work.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Pursat

Pursat has several hiking and biking routes that offer stunning natural scenery, wildlife sightings, and outdoor adventures. The Cardamom Mountains and its surroundings have numerous trails for hikers and bikers of different skill levels.

Best Things to Do in Pursat: Activities and Tours

Apart from hiking and biking, Pursat offers several exciting activities and tours. From visiting silk weaving factories to cooking classes to visiting floating villages, there is something for everyone.

Week-Long Itinerary for Pursat

Day 1: Arrival and exploring around Pursat town center Day 2-3: Trekking in the Cardamom Mountains Day 4: Boat ride and picnic along the Canal des Étangs Day 5-6: Visit Tonle Sap Lake and the floating villages Day 7: Departure

Food and Drink Scene of Pursat

Must-Try Dishes

Amok

Amok is a traditional Cambodian dish made with fish or meat, coconut milk, and spices. It is steamed and garnished with sliced chili and fresh herbs.

Beef Skewers

If you are a meat lover, try some beef skewers seasoned with spices and served with a peanut sauce. These skewers are a popular street food in Pursat.

Local Beverages

Palm Wine

Palm wine is a local alcoholic beverage made from the sap of palm trees. It has a sweet and sour taste and is best enjoyed chilled.

Cold Coffee

Cold coffee is a popular drink in Pursat, especially during the hot summer months. Locally grown coffee beans are roasted and served with condensed milk and ice.

Where to Stay in Pursat: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Pursat has a range of accommodations, including budget-friendly guesthouses, mid-range hotels, and upmarket resorts. The town center and its surrounding areas offer a variety of options.

What to Buy in Pursat: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Pursat is known for its silk weaving, and you can buy beautiful silk scarves, clothing, and accessories as souvenirs. Other popular items include natural wood and bamboo products, silver jewelry, and traditional Khmer art pieces.

Local Culture and Customs of Pursat

Pursat is a predominantly Buddhist community, with many temples and wats scattered throughout the town. The locals are friendly and welcoming, and it is customary to greet them with a polite “chum rearp suor” (hello) or “arkun” (thank you).

Best Time to Visit Pursat

The best time to visit Pursat is from November to April when the weather is dry and mild. The rainy season from May to October is when the landscape is lush and green, but it is not ideal for outdoor activities.

Do’s and Don’ts of Pursat

Do:

  • Dress modestly when visiting temples and other religious sites.
  • Try the local cuisine and beverages.
  • Show respect to the locals and their customs.

Don’t:

  • Disrespect the temples or any religious sites.
  • Take photographs of locals without their permission.
  • Engage in drugs or other illegal activities.

Safety and Health

Safety in Pursat

Pursat is a safe town for travelers, but like any other place in the world, it is essential to take precautions. Stay alert, avoid carrying valuables, and be careful when walking around late at night.

Health in Pursat

It is recommended to take precautions against malaria and other mosquito-borne diseases. Pack insect repellent and wear long sleeves and pants when venturing into the natural areas. Make sure to stay hydrated and carry a water bottle at all times.

Getting to Pursat: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Pursat

The nearest international airport is Phnom Penh International Airport, which is approximately 170 kilometers from Pursat.

Bus Stations Near Pursat

Several bus companies operate routes to and from Pursat. The most popular ones are Giant Ibis, Capitol, and Mekong Express.

Other Options

Private car hire and tuk-tuk taxis are also available for hire at affordable prices.

How to Get Around in Pursat

The best way to get around Pursat is by renting a motorbike or a bicycle. Tuk-tuk taxis are also available for hire if you prefer not to drive.

Where to Go Next from Pursat: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Battambang

Battambang is a charming town located northwest of Pursat. It is famous for its French colonial architecture, cultural institutions, and, most importantly, the bamboo train ride.

Kep

Kep is a seaside town located southeast of Pursat. It is known for its delicious seafood, beautiful beaches, and pepper farms.

Conclusion

Pursat is an excellent destination for travelers who want to immerse themselves in nature and culture. This comprehensive guide will help you plan your trip to this charming town in Cambodia.
