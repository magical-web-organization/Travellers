Introduction to Koh Kong

Koh Kong is a picturesque province in the southwestern part of Cambodia, located in the Cardamom and Elephant Mountains. The province is home to dense jungles, pristine beaches, waterfalls, and an abundance of wildlife. Travelers from all over the world visit Koh Kong to experience its natural beauty and explore the diverse cultural attractions.

List of Places to Visit in Koh Kong

Koh Kong has something to offer for everyone, whether you prefer outdoor adventures, cultural immersion, or simply enjoying relaxing on a beach. Below are the must-visit places in Koh Kong:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Koh Kong Museum: This museum provides a glimpse into the history and culture of Koh Kong. Visitors can learn about the province’s past through exhibits and artifacts.

  • Tatai Waterfall: This marvelous waterfall is located in the Cardamom Mountains and is a popular destination for visitors interested in the local culture.

Natural Attractions of Koh Kong: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Koh Kong Island: This island is perfect for those who want to escape to a secluded tropical paradise.

  • Koh Kong Bay: Take a boat trip to explore the stunning coastline of Koh Kong and discover hidden coves and beaches.

  • Botum Sakor National Park: This park is home to a wide range of flora and fauna species, including tigers, Asian elephants, and gibbons.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Koh Kong

  • Cardamom Elephant Mountains: This area offers a great choice of biking and hiking trails for adventurous travelers who want to discover the natural beauty of Koh Kong.

  • Koh Poi Mountain: This mountain offers a challenging but rewarding hike, with stunning views of the surrounding countryside.

Best Things to Do in Koh Kong: Activities and Tours

  • River Safari: Take a river safari tour to see the unique natural beauty of Koh Kong up close and personal.

  • Kayak through Mangrove Forest: Visitors can rent kayaks and explore the mangrove forests, which are home to a wealth of wildlife.

Week-Long Itinerary for Koh Kong

Day 1: Arrival and settling in at the hotel

Day 2: Explore the Koh Kong Island

Day 3: Hike/Bike in the Cardamom Elephant Mountains

Day 4: Visit the Koh Kong Bay

Day 5: Discover the Botum Sakor National Park

Day 6: Take a River Safari or Kayak Through Mangrove Forest

Day 7: Departure

Food and Drink Scene of Koh Kong

Must-Try Dishes

  • Amok: A traditional Cambodian dish. Fish, chicken, or pork, cooked in coconut milk and spices, and served with rice.

  • Lok Lak: Very popular among the locals, this dish includes diced meat served with fresh vegetables and rice.

Local Beverages

  • Angkor Beer: A very popular local beer, Angkor Beer is frequently paired with Cambodian dishes.

  • Palm Juice: Made from the sap of the palm tree, this drink is refreshing and a must-try for visitors.

Where to Stay in Koh Kong: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Koh Kong Resort: A luxury hotel that offers superior accommodations to guests, with an exquisite in-house restaurant, private beach access, and a range of on-site facilities.

  • Rajabori: This guesthouse offers a comfortable stay with beautiful views of the Cardamom Mountains.

What to Buy in Koh Kong: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Cambodian Scarfs: These beautifully-designed scarfs are a must-buy for people interested in the local handicrafts.

  • Krama: A traditional Cambodian scarf that can also be used as a multi-purpose cloth is a perfect souvenir for visitors.

Local Culture and Customs of Koh Kong

  • Language: Cambodian or Khmer is the official language spoken in Koh Kong but English is also commonly spoken.

  • Clothing: Visitors are expected to dress modestly when visiting religious or cultural attractions.

Best Time to Visit Koh Kong

The dry season usually starts in November and lasts until April, which is the best time to visit Koh Kong. However, visitors can still enjoy their stay during the rainy season, which starts in May and ends in October.

Do’s and Don’ts of Koh Kong

Do:

  • Remember to be respectful of the local culture.

  • Dress appropriately when visiting religious or cultural places.

Don’t:

  • Bring drugs into Koh Kong or use them while visiting.

  • Purchase parts and products made from endangered animals.

Safety and Health

Safety in Koh Kong

Koh Kong is generally a safe place for visitors. However, it is always a good idea to take some necessary precautions to ensure personal safety, such as avoiding going out alone at night or in areas that are off-limits for tourists.

Health in Koh Kong

Visitors need to take appropriate measures to avoid being bitten by mosquitos and other insects. It is also advisable to use mosquito nets, wear long sleeves, and use insect repellent.

Getting to Koh Kong: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Koh Kong

  • Phnom Penh International Airport: The major gateway to Cambodia, with a range of international and domestic flights.

  • Siem Reap International Airport: A major international airport, with several daily flights from neighboring countries.

Bus Stations Near Koh Kong

  • Phnom Penh Bus Station: A major bus station that connects Koh Kong with Phnom Penh city and other destinations in Cambodia.

  • Siem Reap Bus Station: Connects Koh Kong with Siem Reap city and other nearby destinations.

Other Options

Visitors can also take a private car, motorbike, or even a ferry to Koh Kong, depending on their preferences and budget.

How to Get Around in Koh Kong

Visitors can choose from a range of transportation options such as motorbike taxis, tuk-tuks, and local buses to get around Koh Kong.

Where to Go Next from Koh Kong: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Siem Reap: Home to the famous Angkor Wat temple, Siem Reap is one of the most popular tourist destinations in Cambodia.

  • Phnom Penh: The capital city of Cambodia, with a fascinating history and cultural landmarks.

Conclusion

Koh Kong is a fascinating place, full of natural beauty and cultural attractions. Visitors can immerse themselves in the local culture, explore the stunning landscapes, or simply enjoy the laid-back lifestyle unique to Cambodia. With this guide, visitors can make the most of their trip to Koh Kong and have an unforgettable experience.
