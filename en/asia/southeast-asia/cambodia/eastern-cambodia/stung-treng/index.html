Introduction to Stung Treng

Stung Treng is a small city located in Eastern Cambodia. It is the capital of the Stung Treng province and is situated on the Mekong River. The city provides a gateway to some of the best eco-tourism activities in the region, including waterfall hikes, bird-watching, and kayaking.

List of Places to Visit in Stung Treng

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Stung Treng doesn’t have many cultural attractions, but there are a few museums and historical buildings worth visiting. The Preah Ko Temple is one of the oldest temples in Cambodia, dating back to the 9th century.

The Stung Treng Museum is a small museum showcasing the history and culture of the Khmer people. It has exhibits on the local wildlife, geology, and traditional Khmer houses. Another historical place is the Wat Phnom Svay, a 16th century Buddhist temple located on a hill near the city.

Natural Attractions of Stung Treng: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

The Stung Treng province is known for its incredible natural attractions. Here are some places to visit:

  • Tatai Waterfall - A 10-meter high waterfall that cascades through the jungle. The waterfall is located in the province, about 50 km from the city center. It offers a great opportunity to enjoy a refreshing swim in the plunge pool or go for a hike.
  • Mekong River - A boat trip along the Mekong River is a good way to explore the area. You can spot rare species of freshwater dolphins living in the area. Floating villages and other local communities can also be seen.
  • Irrawaddy Dolphin Conservation Area - Home to the rare Irrawaddy dolphin, which is threatened with extinction. You can take a boat trip to the conservation area and learn about the importance of preserving this unique species.
  • O’Svay Waterfall - A scenic location with a waterfall, natural pools, and plenty of greenery, perfect for hikes or swimming.
  • Virachey National Park - Farther away, but worth it is the Virachey National Park, which has some of the most amazing biodiversity in Cambodia. The park is home to elephants, tigers, gibbons, and a vast array of birds.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Stung Treng

The province of Stung Treng offers plenty of hiking and biking routes for nature enthusiasts. The magnificent landscapes of the region can be best appreciated by foot or on a bike. Exploring the dirt tracks and back roads is an excellent way of engaging with the local people and learning about their way of living.

Some of the popular trails are:

  • Sesan River Route - A 15 km track along the edge of the Sesan River, which is a great way of experiencing the rural life of the area.
  • Virachey National Park Route - A multi-day trek through the dense forest of the national park.
  • Trips to remote villages - A guided tour of the smaller and more remote villages near Stung Treng can give you an insight into local life.

Best Things to Do in Stung Treng: Activities and Tours

For those who want a more organized activity or tour, below are some of the best things to do in Stung Treng:

  • Irrawaddy Dolphin Tour - A fantastic way of experiencing these rare creatures and helping to conserve them.
  • Mekong Sunset Cruise - The Mekong River is beautiful, especially during the sunset hours.
  • Tatai Waterfall Day Trip - A day trip to the nearby waterfall provides an opportunity to enjoy nature.
  • Local Cooking Classes - Learn how to cook some Khmer cuisine and take home some useful skills.

Week-Long Itinerary for Stung Treng

Day 1: Visit Preah Ko Temple and explore the city center

Day 2: Take a boat trip on the Mekong River and visit the Irrawaddy Dolphin Conservation Area.

Day 3: Hike to O’Svay Waterfall.

Day 4: Take a day trip to the Tatai Waterfall.

Day 5: Rent a bike and explore the countryside neighborhoods.

Day 6: Visit the Stung Treng Museum to learn about the province’s history and culture.

Day 7: Take a peaceful sunset cruise on the Mekong River for a relaxing end to the week.

Food and Drink Scene of Stung Treng

Must-Try Dishes

Stung Treng is known for its freshwater fish, which can be found in most restaurants. Some traditional Khmer dishes worth trying include:

  • Fish amok - A curry dish served with rice or noodles.
  • Lok Lak - a dish consisting of stir-fried beef with a salty, sweet, and tangy taste.
  • Bai Sach Chrouk - A popular breakfast dish of grilled pork served with rice and pickled vegetables.

Local Beverages

  • Palm Wine - A locally brewed wine made from the sap of palm trees.
  • Numpang - A refreshing drink made from sugar cane juice, served over ice.

Where to Stay in Stung Treng: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Stung Treng has various types of accommodations available for travelers. Ranging from affordable hostels to luxurious hotels, and homestays. Some of the best choices are:

  • Green Guesthouse - A minimalist property located in the provincial city center.
  • Thavry Hotel Stung Treng - A luxurious place close to the Sesan River.
  • Sekong Guesthouse - A more affordable place run by a local family.

What to Buy in Stung Treng: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Some of the best souvenirs and products from Stung Treng are:

  • Hand-woven textiles - Including scarves and tablecloths made using traditional techniques.
  • Silverware - Stung Treng is known for its silverware made from an alloy of silver and other metals.
  • Palm sugar - A locally made sweetener that goes great in coffee or tea.

Local Culture and Customs of Stung Treng

Stung Treng has a primarily rural population, with most people living in small villages or communities outside the city center. The province is home to various ethnic groups, with the Khmer being the most prominent. Fisherman and farmers make up a large portion of the workforce.

Best Time to Visit Stung Treng

The best time to visit Stung Treng is during the dry season, from November to April, as it’s the most comfortable weather and suitable for outdoor activities. The wet season, from May until October, can be challenging for trekking and exploring, due to the heavy rains and the often muddy terrain.

Do’s and Don’ts of Stung Treng

Do:

  • Respect the local customs and dress modestly.
  • Learn some of the Khmer language.
  • Engage with the locals and learn about their culture and way of life.

Don’t:

  • Disrespect religious monuments or areas.
  • Take photographs of people without asking for their permission.
  • Touch wild animals or plant life.

Safety and Health

Safety in Stung Treng

Stung Treng is generally a safe city for tourists, especially during the daylight hours. However, travelers should take precautions, such as the following:

  • Avoid walking alone or in dark places at night.
  • Keep your belongings close to you and watch out for pickpockets.
  • Always wear a helmet if you rent a motorbike.

Health in Stung Treng

Malaria and Dengue fever are common in the region, so it’s recommended to take prophylaxis medication and use insect repellent. Tap water is not safe to drink, so it’s best to stick to bottled water or boil tap water before drinking.

Getting to Stung Treng: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Stung Treng

The nearest airport is the Stung Treng Airport (TNX), which is just 6 km northeast of the city.

Bus Stations Near Stung Treng

There are various bus stations in Stung Treng. The Stung Treng Bus Station is located in the city center and offers connections to destinations within the province and to neighboring Laos.

Other Options

Taxis and tuk-tuks are also available for hire in the city.

How to Get Around in Stung Treng

Bikes are the best way to get around the city, explore the countryside and enjoy nature. Taxis, tuk-tuks, and motorbikes are available for hire within the city.

Where to Go Next from Stung Treng: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

The neighboring provinces offer other destinations to visit once you have explored Stung Treng. The following places are some of the top places to visit nearby:

  • Kratie - Home to a significant population of the Irrawaddy dolphins.
  • Ratanakiri - Known for its beautiful waterfalls, hills, and diverse eco-tourism opportunities.
  • Mondulkiri - With beautiful rainforests, waterfalls and stunning views, this province is a must-visit for trekking lovers.

Conclusion

Stung Treng is an amazing destination for anyone looking for adventure and to experience life off the beaten path. With its natural wonders, amazing food, and friendly locals, it is the perfect place to explore a side of Cambodia that is often overlooked.
