Introduction to Kon Tum

Kon Tum is a hidden gem of the Central Highlands, Vietnam, located on the Dak Bla River. The town, established in the early 1800s, has a rich cultural heritage due to its location at the crossroads of different ethnic groups, such as Banhar, Jrai, and Gie-Trieng. Apart from its cultural and historical significance, Kon Tum is renowned for its scenic beauty, which draws hikers, bikers, and nature enthusiasts from around the world. This comprehensive travel guide offers all the information you need to plan your trip to Kon Tum.

List of Places to Visit in Kon Tum

Kon Tum has several culturally important attractions, natural wonders, and exciting outdoor adventures. Here is a list of the must-visit places:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  1. Kon Tum Museum: The Kon Tum Museum showcases the history and culture of the different ethnic minorities residing in the region.
  2. Kon Tum Wooden Church: The 100-year-old Kon Tum Wooden Church is a significant historical and architectural landmark.
  3. Nguyen Dynasty’s Kon Tum Citadel: This citadel seats in the center of Kon Tum and was built by the Nguyen dynasty in the late 1800s.
  4. Traditional Kon Tum Village: Take a stroll through the traditional Banhar village to learn about their unique customs and traditions.

Natural Attractions of Kon Tum: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  1. Kon Klor Suspension Bridge: The Kon Klor Suspension Bridge is the longest one in Vietnam, offering a spectacular view of the Kon Klor River.
  2. Kon Tum Nature Reserve: The constantly thriving jungle of the Kon Tum Nature Reserve offers a unique hiking experience.
  3. Dak To Hot Springs: Relax in the mineral-rich natural hot springs and get a rejuvenating experience.
  4. Dak Glun Waterfall: This beautiful waterfall in a dense jungle is perfect for a refreshing dip.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Kon Tum

Kon Tum is a paradise for hikers and bikers as it has unexplored trails and scenic routes. Here are some of the best ones:

  1. Kon Tum - Sa Thay Loop: This 55km loop passes through several Banhar villages, a suspension bridge, and a hydroelectric dam.
  2. Kon Tum - Kham Duc trail: This 220km biking trail offers an ultimate experience of the Central Highlands beauty passing through lush jungles, hills and dotted with beautiful waterfalls.
  3. Rattan - Kon Tum trail: This 45 km trail boasts a great view of the valley and the river.

Best Things to Do in Kon Tum: Activities and Tours

Apart from hiking and biking, Kon Tum has several other activities and tours that attract travelers worldwide. Here are some of our top picks:

  1. Visit Pơ Thi, a traditional Banhar village, to learn about their customs and traditions.
  2. Explore the cultural museum in Kon Tum to learn about the different ethnic minorities residing in the region.
  3. Catch a glimpse of the unique Bahnar architecture by visiting the Kon Tum Wooden Church and the Nguyen Dynasty’s Kon Tum Citadel.
  4. Relax in the mineral-rich Dak To hot pool.
  5. Go trekking in the Kon Tum Nature Reserve.
  6. Watch traditional dances and music performances at local cultural shows.

Food and Drink Scene of Kon Tum

The Banhar cuisine of Kon Tum is a culinary delight, with plenty of local and traditional dishes that involve different meats, herbs, and spices. Here are some must-try dishes:

Must-Try Dishes

  1. Grilled pork strips with rice papers (nem nướng)
  2. Banh trang tron, rice paper salad with eggs, herbs, and pork
  3. Steamed sticky rice stuffed with chicken (xoi ga)
  4. Traditional grilled beef (thit nuong)

Local Beverages

  1. Can wine: A locally brewed rice wine served in bamboo tubes and best when paired with grilled dishes.
  2. Arabica coffee: This region is known for its delicious coffee, so enjoy a cup of locally brewed Arabica coffee.

What to Buy in Kon Tum: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Kon Tum offers a wide variety of unique souvenirs and local products. Here are some ideas:

  1. Handwoven brocade: Buy Banhar handwoven brocade, which is made with different patterns and colors and unique to the region.
  2. Wild honey: The Central Highlands are famous for wild honey made by traditional methods, which makes it a great souvenir.
  3. Handcrafted knives: Kon Tum is famous for making knives, and you can buy one as a souvenir.
  4. Arabica coffee: Don’t forget to buy locally brewed Arabica coffee, which is the region’s specialty.

Local Culture and Customs of Kon Tum

Kon Tum has a rich cultural heritage and is home to several ethnic groups. The Banhar, Jrai, Gie-Trieng, and Rmeet people make up most of the population. Here are some customs and traditions that you should know about:

  1. The Banhar language is the most commonly spoken language in the region.
  2. The Banhar people are famous for their handwoven brocade, which has unique patterns and colors.
  3. The Rmeet people are famous for their skillful knife-making.
  4. Traditionally, the Banhar people live in communal houses, which are known as rong.

Best Time to Visit Kon Tum

Kon Tum’s weather is tropical and humid throughout the year, with an average temperature of 25℃ (77℉). The best time to visit Kon Tum is from September to December when the temperature is cooler and the rainfall is low.

Do’s and Don’ts of Kon Tum

Do:

  1. Take your time to explore and learn about the region’s rich cultural heritage.
  2. Respect the local culture and customs.
  3. Dress appropriately when visiting cultural and religious sites.
  4. Carry enough cash, as some places do not accept cards.

Don’t:

  1. Don’t litter.
  2. Don’t take pictures of the local people without their permission.
  3. Don’t touch or move traditional cultural items or artifacts.

Safety and Health

Safety in Kon Tum

Kon Tum is a safe place to travel but be mindful of these tips:

  1. Beware of pickpockets in crowded areas.
  2. Use a helmet while biking or trekking.
  3. Avoid traveling alone at night.

Health in Kon Tum

Kon Tum has clean drinking water, but it’s always recommended to carry a portable water filtration system or buy bottled water. Precautions should be taken during the rainy season to avoid mosquito-borne diseases.

Getting to Kon Tum: Transportation Options and Logistics

Kon Tum is easy to access via different modes of transportation. Here are the most common ways to reach Kon Tum:

Airports Near Kon Tum

The nearest airport is Pleiku Airport (PXU), which is 45 km from Kon Tum and has daily flights from Ho Chi Minh City and Hanoi.

Bus Stations Near Kon Tum

Buses from Ho Chi Minh City and Hanoi run frequently to Kon Tum. You can also catch local buses from cities such as Buon Ma Thuot, Da Lat, and Quy Nhon.

Other Options

You can also hire a private car from any major city in the Central Highlands, explore Kon Tum on your driving.

How to Get Around in Kon Tum

Kon Tum is a small town, and most of the tourist destinations are within walking distance. You can also rent a motorbike from any of the rental shops. Taxis and Shared minivans are other alternatives for traveling longer distances.

Where to Go Next from Kon Tum: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

If you’re planning to stay longer, here are some nearby destinations for a weekend getaway:

  1. Buon Ma Thuot: Home to the world’s largest coffee farms and is the largest city of the Central Highlands
  2. Hoi An: Visit the historic town of Hoi An, a UNESCO World Heritage site famous for its beautifully preserved ancient architecture.
  3. Quy Nhon: Quy Nhon boasts long sandy beaches, seafood, and the charming Old Town.

Conclusion

Kon Tum is a hidden gem of the Central Highlands, Vietnam, that offers visitors a unique experience of adventure, culture, and natural beauty. We hope this travel guide will help you plan a memorable trip to Kon Tum!
