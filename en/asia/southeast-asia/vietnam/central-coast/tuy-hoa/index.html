Introduction to Tuy Hòa

Tuy Hòa is a hidden gem located in Vietnam’s Central Coast, specifically in the south-central province of Phu Yen. It is less crowded and less known compared to popular tourist destinations in Vietnam like Ho Chi Minh City or Hanoi, but it boasts stunning natural scenery, beautiful beaches, and rich cultural heritage.

List of Places to Visit in Tuy Hòa

If you’re planning to visit Tuy Hòa, here’s a list of places that you shouldn’t miss:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • The 1,000 Year Old Cham Tower: Also called “Pô Nagar,” this tower was built around the 8th or 9th century and reflects the architecture of the Cham people who once ruled the central coast of Vietnam.
  • Tuy Hoa Airbase: The airbase was used by the US military during the Vietnam War and is now a tourist attraction.
  • The Provincial Museum: This museum showcases Phu Yen Province’s history, culture, and traditions, from colonial times to the present day.

Natural Attractions of Tuy Hòa: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Ganh Da Dia: Known as the “Organ Rocks,” Ganh Da Dia is a geological masterpiece made of black basalt cliffs. The stunning natural formation has won accolades from many modern architects.
  • O Loan Lagoon: Famous for its picturesque view and bountiful seafood, O Loan Lagoon is a must-visit spot for nature and adventure enthusiasts. You can rent a boat and explore the beauty of the lagoon or try your hand at fishing.
  • Mui Dien Lighthouse: Located at the southernmost tip of the province, Mui Dien Lighthouse is an ideal place to enjoy a fascinating view of the Vietnamese coastline.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Tuy Hòa

Tuy Hòa is also a haven for fitness enthusiasts and nature lovers. You can try hiking or biking on the many natural trails that Tuy Hòa has to offer. Some of the popular hiking and biking trails are:

  • Phu Yen National Park: With an area of 81 hectares, the park is home to 1,000 species of animals and rare plants. It’s one of the best places to hike in Tuy Hòa, with breathtaking landscapes and great mountain views.
  • Da Bia Mountain: Da Bia Mountain is a nearby destination, which can be reached via motorbike or bike ride. It is a perfect spot for trekking as it rewards trekkers with stunning panoramic views from the top of the mountain.

Best Things to Do in Tuy Hòa: Activities and Tours

If you’re looking for things to do in Tuy Hòa, here are some of the best activities and tours to try:

  • Phu Yen River Cruise: Cruise on the serene river to see Tuy Hoa’s natural heritage, ancient fishing villages and behold the breathtaking beauty of the region’s countryside
  • Diving: Tuy Hòa’s coastline is pristine and offers some of the country’s best diving sites. Marine life includes seahorses, lionfish, blue-spotted stingrays, and octopuses.
  • Vietnamese Cooking Classes: Immerse yourself in Tuy Hòa’s culinary scene and go back home with new gastronomic experiences.

Food and Drink Scene of Tuy Hòa

Vietnamese cuisine is known for its bold flavors, fresh herbs, and spices. Here are some of the must-try dishes and local beverages in Tuy Hòa:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Bánh xèo: a crispy, savory pancake filled with pork, shrimp, bean sprouts, and herbs.
  • Bún bò: a popular Southern Vietnamese noodle soup dish made with beef and rice vermicelli noodles.
  • Gỏi cá và tôm: a healthy salad made with fish and shrimp, mixed with fresh herbs, lime, and chili.

Local Beverages

  • Trà đá: Proper tea is served hot, but during hotter seasons, you can enjoy it over ice with a liberal dose of sugar.
  • Nước mía: Also known as sugarcane juice, Nước mía is a refreshing drink made from fresh sugarcane juice.
  • Bia hơi: a fresh, unpasteurized lager that is brewed daily and only lasts for a day or two.

  • Fleur De Vie Bungalows & Homestay: A European-style homestay with a private beach and beautiful gardens.
  • Nhat Minh Hotel: A budget-friendly option located in the heart of the city.

What to Buy in Tuy Hòa: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Some local crafts and food products you could bring back from Tuy Hòa include:

  • Xôi bắp: a traditional sticky rice dish, which is enjoyable during festivals or family gatherings.
  • Nón lá: a conical hat that is made from palm leaves, popular amongst Asian communities, and serves as a great souvenir.
  • Bamboo products: Handicrafts made from bamboo like baskets, mats or trays, sold in local markets, make excellent items to bring home

Local Culture and Customs of Tuy Hòa

Vietnam has an array of diverse cultural, religious, and historical backgrounds, and Tuy Hòa reflects that. When visiting Tuy Hòa, it is best to be mindful of the local traditions and customs:

  • Language: Vietnamese is the official language. However, in tourist areas, workers may speak some English.
  • Religion: Minimizing noise around temples and pagodas is appreciated as well as willful donations.
  • Clothing: Dress appropriately when visiting temples and other religious sites. Always cover your knees and shoulders.

Best Time to Visit Tuy Hòa

The best time to visit Tuy Hòa is from February to September when the temperature is moderate, and you can enjoy long days of sunshine. North Vietnam’s winter is Tuy Hoa’s cool and dry season, while summer is hot and rainy.

Do’s and Don’ts of Tuy Hòa

When traveling to Tuy Hòa, here are some do’s and don’ts to keep in mind:

Do:

  • Try the local cuisine and street food.
  • Respect the local culture and traditions.
  • Shop at local markets and support small businesses.

Don’t:

  • Litter or dispose of trash haphazardly.
  • Touch the head of someone or hand them something with your feet.
  • Disrespect the national flag or national anthem.

Safety and Health

Safety in Tuy Hòa

Tuy Hòa is a relatively safer area in Vietnam, but you should always be aware of your surroundings and maintain good safety habits. Avoid walking alone or carrying expensive items in view.

Health in Tuy Hòa

It’s always essential to have travel insurance when visiting Vietnam, you never know what might happen. Consult your doctor before traveling and make sure that all your vaccines are up-to-date. You should also have a basic medical kit on hand for minor emergencies, including diarrhea, dehydration, and heat stroke.

Getting to Tuy Hòa: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Tuy Hòa

Tuy Hòa has a domestic airport that offers daily flights from Ho Chi Minh City and Hanoi.

Bus Stations Near Tuy Hòa

The province has a central bus station located in Tuy Hòa City that services most domestic routes.

Other Options

If you want to explore the countryside and take in the natural beauty of Tuy Hòa, you can rent a motorbike or bike from various rental services in the city.

How to Get Around in Tuy Hòa

Tuy Hòa’s central area can be explored by walking, but if you want to visit other places in town, you can get around by motorbike taxi or car taxi. Additionally, you can rent a car or a motorbike from rental services and have a more personalized experience.

Where to Go Next from Tuy Hòa: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

There are many options if you want to explore more of Vietnam after Tuy Hòa, such as:

  • Nha Trang: a popular seaside resort town located 134 km from Tuy Hòa.
  • Hoi An: a well-preserved ancient town that is designated a UNESCO World Heritage Site and is about 350 km from Tuy Hoa.
  • Da Nang: an upcoming city located 415 km from Tuy Hòa, that serves as a hub for many travelers who want to explore the neighboring towns and villages.

Conclusion

From beautiful beaches to stunning natural scenery, rich cultural heritage to delicious Vietnamese cuisine, Tuy Hòa has it all.
