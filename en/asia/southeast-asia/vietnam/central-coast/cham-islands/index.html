Introduction to Chàm Islands

Chàm Islands is a group of eight small islands located in the central coast of Vietnam. This stunning archipelago boasts white sandy beaches, crystal clear waters, and an abundance of coral reefs and marine life, making it a popular destination for beach lovers and snorkeling enthusiasts. The islands offer a perfect mix of natural beauty, history, culture, and adventure, making it an ideal destination for all types of travelers.

The islands are also home to a small fishing community, the Chàm people, who have preserved their traditional way of life and culture. These islands were recognized by UNESCO as a World Biosphere Reserve in 2009.

In this comprehensive tour guide, we’ll explore the best places to visit, hiking and biking routes, activities and tours, food and drink scene, accommodation options, local culture and customs, safety tips, and transportation options to help you plan your trip to Chàm Islands.

List of Places to Visit in Chàm Islands

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Chàm Island Maritime Museum: This museum showcases the maritime history and culture of the Chàm people. You’ll get to learn about fishing techniques, trading, boat building, and other aspects of their seafaring lifestyle.

  • Po Nagar Cham Towers: These temples were built by the Chàm people between the 7th and 12th centuries to honor the Hindu goddess Po Nagar. The towers are well-preserved and offer a glimpse into the Chàm’s religious and architectural heritage.

  • Ong Temple: This temple is dedicated to the Whale God, who is believed to protect fishermen at sea. The temple showcases the unique blend of Vietnamese and Chàm cultures.

Natural Attractions of Chàm Islands: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Bai Chong Beach: This is the largest and most popular beach on Chàm Islands. It offers a picturesque setting of white sand, crystal clear waters, and palm trees. You can swim, sunbathe, and snorkel here.

  • Bai Lang Beach: This is a lesser-known beach but equally beautiful. It has fewer tourists, which makes it a perfect spot for a peaceful getaway. You can bike or walk here from the main village.

  • Hon Tai: This rocky island is located in the north of the main island and offers panoramic views of the sea and surrounding islands. It’s a popular spot for hiking and photography.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Chàm Islands

Chàm Islands offer a range of hiking and biking routes that take you through scenic routes of natural beauty and cultural significance. Some of the popular trails are:

  • Bai Bac – Bai Ong: This is a scenic coastal route that takes you through two beautiful beaches, Bai Bac and Bai Ong. You’ll get to see fishing villages, rice fields, and coconut plantations along the way.

  • Hon Lao – Bai Huong: This is a challenging hiking trail that takes you to the highest peak on the island, Hon Lao. The trail offers stunning views of the sea and surrounding islands.

  • Bai Chong – Bai Lang: This is an easy biking route that takes you through the two most popular beaches on the island, Bai Chong and Bai Lang. You’ll get to see the traditional way of life of the Chàm people en route.

Best Things to Do in Chàm Islands: Activities and Tours

  • Snorkeling and Diving: Chàm Islands offer some of the best snorkeling and diving spots in Vietnam. You’ll get to see an abundance of marine life, including colorful corals and fish, sea turtles, and even seahorses.

  • Island Hopping: You can hire a boat and explore the surrounding islands of Chàm. Each island has its unique charm, and you’re sure to find a hidden gem.

  • Fishing Tours: You can go on a fishing tour with the Chàm fishermen and experience their way of life. You’ll get to catch fish, cook and eat them on the boat.

Food and Drink Scene of Chàm Islands

Must-Try Dishes

  • Cao Lầu: This is a local noodle dish that’s made with pork, local herbs, and vegetables. It’s a must-try dish when in Chàm Islands.

  • Bánh Mì This is a Vietnamese-style sandwich that’s made with a baguette, pork, pâté, pickled vegetables, and herbs. You can find it in most street vendors.

  • Nem Nướng: This is a grilled pork sausage that’s served with a variety of herbs, lettuce, and dipping sauce. It’s a popular snack in Chàm Islands.

Local Beverages

  • Sugarcane Juice: You can find sugarcane juice in most street vendors in Chàm Islands. It’s a refreshing drink made with fresh sugarcane juice, lime, and ice.

  • Bia Hoi: This is a local beer that’s made fresh every day. It’s light and refreshing and perfect for hot weather.

Where to Stay in Chàm Islands: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Chàm Islands offer limited accommodation options, most of which are basic guesthouses and a few mid-range hotels. Here are some of the popular options:

  • Chàm Oasis: This is a mid-range hotel that offers comfortable rooms, restaurant, and pool. It’s located near Bai Chong Beach.

  • Tam Hải Resort: This is a luxury resort that offers spacious rooms, private beach, and spa. It’s located in the north of the main island.

  • Champa Island Resort: This is a high-end resort that offers beachfront accommodation, restaurant, and pool. It’s located on Bai Xep Beach.

What to Buy in Chàm Islands: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Chàm Textiles: You can find beautiful textiles made by the Chàm people at the local markets. These textiles are made from local materials and showcase the unique patterns and colors of their culture.

  • Seafood: You can buy fresh seafood from the local fishermen at the pier. You can either cook it yourself or ask the restaurant to cook it for you.

Local Culture and Customs of Chàm Islands

The Chàm people are a minority ethnic group in Vietnam who have preserved their traditional way of life and culture. They have their language, customs, and religion, which are influenced by Hinduism and Islam. It’s essential to respect their culture and customs when visiting Chàm Islands.

Best Time to Visit Chàm Islands

The best time to visit Chàm Islands is from April to August when the weather is sunny and dry, and the sea is calm. Avoid visiting during the monsoon season from September to December as the island experiences heavy rainfall and strong winds.

Do’s and Don’ts of Chàm Islands

Do:

  • Dress modestly when visiting religious sites.

  • Respect the local culture and customs.

  • Ask for permission before taking photos with the Chàm people.

Don’t:

  • Take coral reefs or shells from the beach.

  • Litter or damage the environment.

  • Swim or snorkel in restricted areas.

Safety and Health

Safety in Chàm Islands

Chàm Islands are generally safe, and the crime rates are low. However, it’s advisable to take standard precautions like not carrying too much cash and valuables and not walking late at night.

Health in Chàm Islands

There are no medical facilities on the island, so it’s essential to carry a first aid kit and any necessary medication. Use insect repellent to avoid mosquito bites that can cause dengue fever or malaria.

Getting to Chàm Islands: Transportation Options and Logistics

Chàm Islands are accessible by boat from Hội An or Cửa Đại beach. You can also fly into Danang International Airport and take a taxi or bus to Hội An. From Hội An, you can hire a boat to Chàm Islands.

Airports Near Chàm Islands

  • Danang International Airport: This is the nearest airport to Chàm Islands, located 38 km away.

Bus Stations Near Chàm Islands

  • Hội An Bus Station: This is the nearest bus station to Chàm Islands.

Other Options

  • Private Transfer: You can hire a private car or taxi from Danang to Hội An and then hire a boat to Chàm Islands.

How to Get Around in Chàm Islands

The best way to get around Chàm Islands is on foot, bike, or motorbike. You can rent bikes or motorbikes from the guesthouses or hotels on the island.

Where to Go Next from Chàm Islands: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Hội An

Hội An is a charming UNESCO World Heritage Site located 15 km from Chàm Islands. The ancient town is known for its beautiful architecture, lanterns, and vibrant nightlife.

Danang

Danang is a bustling city with a beautiful beach and world-class attractions like the Marble Mountains and the Ba Na Hills.

Conclusion

Chàm Islands are a hidden gem in Vietnam that offers a perfect escape from the bustling city life. Whether you’re looking for outdoor adventures, cultural experiences, or just a peaceful beach getaway, Chàm Islands have it all. Plan your trip to Chàm Islands using this comprehensive tour guide and create memories that will last a lifetime.
