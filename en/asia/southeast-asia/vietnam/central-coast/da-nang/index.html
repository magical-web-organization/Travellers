Da Nang Travel Guide - A Comprehensive Tour Guide for Da Nang

Welcome to Da Nang - a beautiful coastal city in Central Vietnam. From natural scenic views to vibrant nightlife, delicious food, friendly people, and exciting adventures, Da Nang has something for everyone. Whether you’re a budget backpacker or luxury traveler, single or with family, this comprehensive guide has got you covered. Let’s explore the best things to do in Da Nang and nearby destinations with safety tips, must-try food, and drink, culture, and customs.

List of Places to Visit in Da Nang

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Da Nang is a city rich in culture and history. Here are some of the best museums and historical places to visit:

  • Da Nang Museum of Cham Sculpture: Explore the history and culture of the Cham people who lived in Central Vietnam from the 4th to the 14th century. The museum has over 300 Cham sculptures and artifacts.
  • Marble Mountains: Located 8 km from Da Nang, Marble Mountains is a cluster of five hills representing the five elements of the universe - water, wood, fire, earth, and metal. You can climb the stairs and caves to reach beautiful temples, panoramic views, and natural caves.
  • Hoi An Ancient Town: Hoi An is a UNESCO World Heritage site, located 30 km from Da Nang. It’s a charming town with a blend of Vietnamese, Chinese, Japanese, and French architecture. You can visit ancient houses, temples, museums, tailor shops, and try delicious local food, such as Quang noodles or Cao Lau noodles.

Natural Attractions of Da Nang: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Da Nang has a diverse landscape with mountains, beaches, rivers, and forests. Here are some of the best natural attractions to visit:

  • My Khe Beach: One of the most beautiful beaches in Vietnam, My Khe has white sand, clear water, and gentle waves. You can go swimming, surfing, or sunbathing and try fresh seafood at the local beachside restaurants.
  • Son Tra Mountain: Also known as Monkey Mountain, Son Tra has a lush jungle, green hills, rare wildlife, and breathtaking views of the city and the sea. You can go hiking, biking, or jeep tours and visit the stunning Linh Ung Pagoda.
  • Ba Na Hills: Ba Na is a hill station located 40 km from Da Nang, with a French colonial-style village, fantasy park, cable cars, and panoramic views. You can go on a day trip and experience different climates, such as spring, summer, autumn, and winter, in one place.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Da Nang

Da Nang has some fantastic hiking and biking routes that take you through the city’s nature and culture, such as:

  • Han River Bike Path: A 10 km bike path along the Han River that connects several city landmarks, such as Dragon Bridge, Love Bridge, and Song Han Bridge.
  • Monkey Mountain Hike: A challenging 14 km hike that takes you to the peak of Son Tra Mountain, passing through the jungle trail, and visiting the historic radar station.
  • Cycling to Hoi An: A scenic 30 km bike ride from Da Nang to Hoi An, passing green rice fields, peaceful villages, and the Thu Bon River.

Best Things to Do in Da Nang: Activities and Tours

Da Nang has many exciting activities that you can enjoy, such as:

  • Dragon Bridge Show: A unique spectacle of a fire-breathing dragon bridge that lights up every Saturday and Sunday night at 9 pm. You can watch it from the riverside or book a boat tour for a closer view.
  • Da Nang Food Tour: A culinary adventure that takes you to the best street food stalls, local markets, and restaurants to taste traditional dishes, such as Banh Xeo, Banh Mi, and Bun Cha Ca.
  • Hoi An Lantern Tour: A romantic evening walk through the ancient town of Hoi An, where you can make your lantern and float it on the river while listening to traditional music.

Week-Long Itinerary for Da Nang

If you’re planning to spend a week in Da Nang, here’s a sample itinerary that you can follow:

  • Day 1: Arrival in Da Nang, check-in hotel, visit Dragon Bridge and Han River at night.
  • Day 2: Explore the city center, visit Da Nang Museum of Cham Sculpture, and have lunch at the local market. In the afternoon, visit Marble Mountains and watch the sunset at My Khe Beach.
  • Day 3: Take a day trip to Ba Na Hills and enjoy the French village, fantasy park, and panoramic views.
  • Day 4: Rent a bike and bike to Hoi An, explore the ancient town, and have lunch at a riverside restaurant. In the afternoon, visit An Bang Beach and return to Da Nang before sunset.
  • Day 5: Join a cooking class in Da Nang and learn how to make traditional Vietnamese dishes. In the afternoon, relax at My Khe Beach and have a street food dinner.
  • Day 6: Take a Jeep tour to Son Tra Mountain, visit the Linh Ung Pagoda, and have lunch with a view. In the afternoon, visit Han Market and go shopping.
  • Day 7: Departure from Da Nang.

Food and Drink Scene of Da Nang

No trip to Vietnam is complete without trying the local food and drink. Da Nang has some delicious dishes and beverages that you can’t miss, such as:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Banh Mi: A Vietnamese sandwich made with a crispy baguette, various fillings such as pork, chicken, or tofu, mayonnaise, pate, fresh herbs, and vegetables.
  • Banh Xeo: A crispy pancake made with rice flour, turmeric powder, coconut milk, prawns or meat, bean sprouts, and herbs. You wrap it with lettuce, herbs, and dip in fish sauce.
  • Mi Quang: A regional noodle dish with turmeric yellow noodles, sliced pork or chicken, shrimp, peanuts, rice crackers, herbs, and vegetables. You can eat it dry or with broth.

Local Beverages

  • Bia Hoi: A fresh beer brewed daily with low alcohol content, served in plastic cups, and enjoyed on the streets with friends and local snacks.
  • Tra Da: Iced tea is a ubiquitous drink in Vietnam, especially in summer. You can find it in most cafes and restaurants and add sugar or lime to your liking.
  • Ca Phe Sua Da: Vietnamese iced coffee is made with strong coffee, sweet condensed milk, and ice. You mix it well and enjoy a perfect balance of bitterness and sweetness.

Where to Stay in Da Nang: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Da Nang has a wide range of accommodation options to suit various budgets and preferences. Here are some of the best places to stay in Da Nang:

  • Luxury Hotels: InterContinental Danang Sun Peninsula Resort, Fusion Maia Danang, Pullman Danang Beach Resort, and Hyatt Regency Danang Resort and Spa.
  • Mid-Range Hotels: Vinpearl Condotel Riverfront Da Nang, A La Carte Da Nang Beach Hotel, Premiere Danang Resort, and Mercure Danang French Village.
  • Budget Hostels: Memory Hostel, Da Nang Center Hostel, and Funtastic Danang Hostel.

What to Buy in Da Nang: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Da Nang has some unique souvenirs and local products that you can buy as a memory of your trip, such as:

  • Silk Products: Vietnam is famous for its silk, and Da Nang has many shops and markets selling silk scarves, dresses, and shirts.
  • Marble Handicrafts: Marble Mountains is known for its marble handicrafts, such as statues, vases, and home decor.
  • Seafood Products: Da Nang is a seafood paradise, and you can buy dried squid, fish sauce, shrimp chips, and other seafood products as gifts for your loved ones.

Local Culture and Customs of Da Nang

Vietnamese people are friendly and welcoming, but it’s always good to know some local customs and etiquette to avoid miscommunication or offense, such as:

  • Greeting: Vietnamese people usually greet each other with a smile and a bow, and they use titles or honorifics such as “anh” for a male older brother and “chi” for a female older sister.
  • Shoes: Vietnamese people take off their shoes when entering a temple, a person’s house, or a restaurant. It’s a sign of respect and hygiene.
  • Gifts: When giving a gift, it’s best to wrap it nicely and give it with both hands. It’s also polite to refuse a gift at first to show modesty and persistence to accept it later.

Best Time to Visit Da Nang

The best time to visit Da Nang is from February to May when the weather is dry and mild, with a high average temperature of 30 degrees Celsius and low humidity. June to August is the peak tourism season, but the weather can be hot and humid, with occasional rain. September to January is the rainy and typhoon season, with a high risk of floods and storms.

Do’s and Don’ts of Da Nang

Do:

  • Try Local Food: Vietnamese cuisine is diverse and delicious, and Da Nang has some unique dishes that you can’t find elsewhere. Don’t be afraid to try new things.
  • Bring Sun Protection: Da Nang has a tropical climate, and the sun can be intense. Bring sunscreen, hat, and sunglasses to protect your skin and eyes.
  • Learn Some Vietnamese Words: Vietnamese language is not easy, but locals appreciate it when foreigners try to speak a few words like “xin chao” for hello, “cam on” for thank you, and “bao nhieu tien” for how much money.

Don’t:

  • Touch People’s Head: Vietnamese people consider the head as the highest and most sacred part of the body. Don’t touch someone’s head or ruffle a child’s hair, as it’s considered impolite.
  • Haggle Too Hard: Haggling is common in Vietnam, but don’t go too hard or be disrespectful. Bargain with a smile, and if you can’t agree on a price, it’s okay to walk away gracefully.
  • Disrespect Temples or Pagodas: Vietnam has many temples and pagodas that are sacred to Buddhists and locals. When visiting these places, dress modestly, don’t make noise or take photos without permission.

Safety and Health

Safety in Da Nang

Da Nang is a safe city with low crime rates, but it’s always good to take some precautions to ensure your safety, such as:

  • Traffic: Traffic in Vietnam can be chaotic and dangerous, especially for pedestrians or motorbike riders. Always look both ways before crossing the street, and if in doubt, follow a local.
  • Scams: Scams are rare, but they can happen in touristy areas. Be aware of overcharging, fake products, and unofficial tour guides. Book tours and activities from reputable companies.
  • Beaches: Be careful when swimming in the sea, as the waves can be strong and the currents unpredictable.

Health in Da Nang

Da Nang has a good public health care system, but it’s always good to have travel insurance and take some measures to avoid common health risks, such as:

  • Food and Water: Drink only bottled or boiled water, and avoid raw or undercooked food, street food, or food from unreliable sources.
  • Mosquitoes: Da Nang is a dengue fever area, especially during the rainy season. Wear long-sleeved clothes, use mosquito repellent, and sleep under a mosquito net.
  • Sunstroke: Da Nang has a hot and humid climate, which can cause sunstroke or dehydration. Drink plenty of water, avoid staying in the sun for too long, and wear loose and comfortable clothes.

Getting to Da Nang: Transportation Options and Logistics

Da Nang is a central city in Vietnam, with good transportation connections to other cities and countries. Here are some options to get to Da Nang:

Airports Near Da Nang

  • Da Nang International Airport: Located in the city center, Da Nang Airport has flights to major cities in Vietnam, such as Ho Chi Minh City, Hanoi, and Nha Trang, and international destinations such as Singapore, Seoul, and Taipei. You can take a taxi or a shuttle bus to the city center.

Bus Stations Near Da Nang

  • Da Nang Central Bus Station: Located in the city center, Da Nang Bus Station has buses to nearby cities such as Hue, Hoi An, Quy Nhon, and Nha Trang, as well as long-distance buses to Ho Chi Minh City and Hanoi. You can buy tickets at the station or book online.

Other Options

  • Train: Da Nang Railway Station is a major train station in Vietnam, with daily trains to major cities such as Hue, Hoi An, Nha Trang, and Ho Chi Minh City.
  • Car: You can rent a car or hire a private driver to take you from other cities to Da Nang, such as Hue or Hoi An.

How to Get Around in Da Nang

Da Nang is a compact city with many transportation options to get around, such as:

  • Taxi: Taxis in Da Nang are metered and reliable, but make sure to negotiate the price before getting in.
  • Motorbike: Motorbike rental is cheap, and it’s an excellent way to explore the city’s narrow streets and alleys. Make sure to wear a helmet and get a valid license.
  • Bicycle: Bicycle rental is available in many places, and it’s a fun and eco-friendly way to explore the city’s parks and beaches.
  • Bus: Da Nang has a public bus system with many routes, but it can be crowded and confusing for foreigners.

Where to Go Next from Da Nang: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Da Nang is a gateway to many nearby destinations and excursions, such as:

  • Hue: A former imperial city and a UNESCO World Heritage site, Hue is located 100 km from Da Nang, with a rich history, culture, and cuisine. You can visit the Imperial City, tombs of the Nguyen Dynasty, Perfume River, and try Hue-style noodle dishes.
  • Hoi An: As mentioned earlier, Hoi An is a charming ancient town located 30 km from Da Nang, with a blend of Vietnamese, Chinese, Japanese, and French architecture. You can visit ancient houses, temples, museums, tailor shops, and try delicious local food.
  • My Son Sanctuary: My Son is a cluster of Hindu temple ruins from the Champa Kingdom, located 60 km from Da Nang, in a valley surrounded by mountains and rivers. You can visit the temples, learn about Champa culture and history, and enjoy the tranquil nature.

Conclusion

Da Nang is a beautiful city that offers a mix of nature, culture, cuisine, and adventure. Whether you’re a solo traveler, couple, or family, you’ll find plenty of things to do and see in Da Nang and nearby destinations. We hope this comprehensive guide has helped you plan your trip and enjoy your stay in Da Nang. Safe travels!
