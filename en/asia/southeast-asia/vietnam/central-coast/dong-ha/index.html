Introduction to Dong Ha

Located in Central Coast Vietnam, Dong Ha is a vibrant city that has many cultural and natural attractions. It is the capital of Quang Tri province and is situated close to the Vietnamese Demilitarized Zone. Dong Ha has significant historical significance as it was the site of the 1972 Easter Offensive during the Vietnam War.

This guide will help you plan your trip to Dong Ha and discover the best places to visit, best things to do, local food and drink, where to stay, and more.

List of Places to Visit in Dong Ha

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Vinh Moc Tunnels: Located 6km from the city center, Vinh Moc Tunnels is a complex of tunnels dug during the Vietnam War as a shelter for the locals. It was used as a temporary hospital, living quarters, and school during the war.

  • Hien Luong Bridge: The historic bridge crosses the Ben Hai River and was used as a demarcation line during the Vietnam War, separating North and South Vietnam.

  • Quang Tri Ancient Citadel: The citadel was a significant military stronghold during the Vietnam War and was destroyed during the Easter Offensive. It is now a historical site that provides insight into the war years.

Natural Attractions of Dong Ha: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Cua Tung Beach: Located 15km from Dong Ha, Cua Tung Beach is a beautiful white sand beach that is perfect for swimming, sunbathing, and watching the sunset.

  • Hang Thuy Tien Cave: Located 10km from Dong Ha, Hang Thuy Tien Cave is a stunning natural cave that is worth a visit for its impressive rock formations and clear water.

  • Dakrong Nature Reserve: Located in the western part of Quang Tri Province, Dakrong Nature Reserve is home to many rare and endangered wildlife species, including the saola, a new antelope species that was discovered in 1992.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Dong Ha

Dong Ha has several routes for hiking and biking enthusiasts. Some of the popular routes include:

  • Ho Chi Minh Trail: This trail runs through the provinces of Quang Binh, Quang Tri, and Thua Thien Hue and is suitable for both hikers and bikers.

  • Dakrong Valley: This valley offers scenic views of rice paddies, mountains, and the Dakrong River. It is a perfect place for hiking, biking, and camping.

  • Khe Sanh Combat Base: This former military base offers a unique hiking experience as it has preserved the trenches and buildings from the Vietnam War.

Best Things to Do in Dong Ha: Activities and Tours

  • Boat Tour on the Ben Hai River: Take a boat tour to explore the Ben Hai River and learn more about its historical significance during the Vietnam War.

  • Visit Dong Ha Market: Explore the vibrant Dong Ha Market to see the local products and to try the delicious street food.

  • Visit a Local Village: Take a tour to a local village to learn about the daily life of the locals and to try some of the local specialties.

Food and Drink Scene of Dong Ha

Must-Try Dishes

  • Bun Bo Hue: A hot and spicy beef noodle soup that originates from the nearby city of Hue.

  • Banh Xeo: A savory pancake filled with shrimp, pork, and bean sprouts.

  • Com Hen: A dish made of rice, baby clams, peanuts, and herbs.

Local Beverages

  • Coffee: Vietnam is known for its delicious coffee, and Dong Ha is no exception. Try the local coffee with condensed milk for a unique blend.

  • Beer: Dong Ha has many local breweries that offer a wide range of beers, including the popular Huda beer.

Where to Stay in Dong Ha: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Saigon Dong Ha Hotel: Located in the city center, this hotel offers comfortable rooms and excellent amenities.

  • Hillside Hostel: A budget-friendly option that offers clean and cozy rooms.

  • Phuong Anh Guesthouse: A family-run guesthouse that provides a personal touch to your stay.

What to Buy in Dong Ha: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Vietnamese Coffee: Bring home some authentic Vietnamese coffee beans or pre-ground packs as a souvenir.

  • Handwoven Textiles: Dong Ha is known for its handwoven textiles made by local artisans. These make unique souvenirs.

Local Culture and Customs of Dong Ha

Dong Ha has a rich cultural heritage, and the locals are known for their warm hospitality. Visitors are expected to dress modestly when visiting cultural and religious sites and to respect the local customs and traditions.

Best Time to Visit Dong Ha

The best time to visit Dong Ha is between February and May when the weather is dry and pleasant. However, the city can be visited throughout the year, but visitors should be prepared for the occasional rain and high humidity.

Do’s and Don’ts of Dong Ha

Do:

  • Try the local food and drink scene

  • Dress modestly when visiting cultural and religious sites

  • Learn a few Vietnamese phrases to communicate with locals

Don’t:

  • Be disrespectful of the local customs and traditions

  • Overlook the outdoor adventures and natural attractions in Dong Ha

  • Waste food when eating at a local restaurant, Vietnamese tradition emphasizes keeping food waste to a minimum

Safety and Health

Safety in Dong Ha

Dong Ha is a safe city, and visitors are advised to take the usual precautions. Avoid walking alone late at night, especially in isolated areas, and be aware of pickpockets in crowded areas.

Health in Dong Ha

Visitors should take precautions against insect bites as there is a risk of mosquito-borne diseases in Vietnam. It is advisable to use mosquito repellent and to cover skin when outdoors. It is also recommended to drink only bottled water to avoid any chance of contracting waterborne diseases.

Getting to Dong Ha: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Dong Ha

Dong Ha does not have its airport. Visitors can fly to Da Nang or Hue airport and can take a bus or private transfer to Dong Ha.

Bus Stations Near Dong Ha

Dong Ha has a central bus station that connects the city to other major cities in Vietnam.

Other Options

Visitors can also reach Dong Ha by train or private car.

How to Get Around in Dong Ha

The easiest way to get around Dong Ha is by motorbike taxi or by renting a motorbike. There are also local buses and taxis available.

Where to Go Next from Dong Ha: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Hue: A beautiful city that is known for its rich cultural heritage and ancient citadels.

  • Phong Nha-Ke Bang National Park: Famous for its massive cave system and underground rivers.

Conclusion

Dong Ha is a city that offers a unique combination of cultural and natural attractions.
