Introduction to Hue

Hue is a city located in Central Coast, Vietnam. It is a well-known tourist destination in Vietnam, thanks to its rich history, culture, and natural beauty. The city served as the capital of Vietnam during the Nguyen Dynasty from 1802 to 1945.

Visitors to Hue can explore numerous historical sites such as the Imperial City, numerous royal tombs, and old temples. The Perfume River is also famous for its scenic beauty and is the perfect spot for a relaxing boat ride. Hue is also known for its delicious food, with various street food markets and restaurants to taste traditional Vietnamese dishes.

Read on to discover everything you need to know to plan your trip to Hue.

List of Places to Visit in Hue

Hue has a plethora of attractions, from culturally important sites to natural wonders. Here are some of the must-visit places in Hue:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Imperial City

The Imperial City was the home of Vietnam’s emperors during the Nguyen Dynasty, and it served as the political center of the country. The city includes a Forbidden Purple City, which was the living quarters of the emperor and his family. Visitors can explore palaces, temples, and gardens in the Imperial City.

Royal Tombs

Hue is home to numerous royal tombs, which are the final resting places of Vietnam’s emperors. These tombs represent an aspect of Vietnamese history and culture and are fascinating to explore. Tombs such as the Minh Mang Tomb, Tu Duc Tomb, and Khai Dinh Tomb are among the most popular ones.

Thien Mu Pagoda

The Thien Mu Pagoda is the tallest pagoda in Vietnam and sits on the north bank of the Perfume River. Visitors can marvel at the seven-story tower and see temple artifacts and religious relics. The temple also houses a car that belonged to a monk who set himself on fire to protest against the South Vietnamese government in 1963.

Hue Museum of Royal Fine Arts

The Hue Museum of Royal Fine Arts is located in the Citadel complex and showcases the art and artifacts from the Nguyen Dynasty. The museum has a collection of antiques, ceramics, and furniture. Visitors can learn more about the traditions and customs of the Nguyen Dynasty through the museum’s exhibits.

Natural Attractions of Hue: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Perfume River

The Perfume River is not only scenic but also has historical significance in Hue. Visitors can take a boat ride along the river and enjoy the stunning views of the surrounding landscapes. The river is especially beautiful during sunset, when it glows with golden light.

Bach Ma National Park

Bach Ma National Park is located 40 km south of Hue. The park is home to numerous hiking and trekking trails, waterfalls, and lush forests. Visitors can also enjoy stunning views of the surrounding mountains and coastline.

Lang Co Beach

Lang Co Beach is a picturesque beach located an hour’s drive from Hue. The beach is known for its clean white sand and crystal-clear water, making it a perfect spot for a relaxing beach day.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Hue

Hue has numerous hiking and biking routes that allow visitors to explore the city’s natural beauty and historical sites.

Hai Van Pass

The Hai Van Pass is a scenic mountain route that connects Hue and Da Nang. The route is famous for its stunning views of the ocean on one side and the mountains on the other. Visitors can rent a motorbike or bicycle to explore the pass’s beauty.

Tam Giang Lagoon

Tam Giang Lagoon is located 15 km southeast of Hue. The lagoon is home to numerous bird species, and visitors can take a bike ride along the lagoon to enjoy the scenic views.

Best Things to Do in Hue: Activities and Tours

Hue Street Food Tour

Hue is known for its delicious street food. Visitors can explore the city’s food scene by taking a street food tour. These tours take visitors to various street food stalls and restaurants, allowing them to taste traditional Vietnamese dishes such as Bun Bo Hue (noodle soup).

Perfume River Boat Cruise

A boat cruise on the Perfume River is the perfect way to explore the city’s scenic beauty. Visitors can enjoy the stunning views of the river and the surrounding landscape.

Hue Countryside Bike Tour

Hue is surrounded by lush countryside, and visitors can explore it by taking a bike tour. These tours take visitors through villages and rural landscapes, allowing them to learn more about the local culture and lifestyle.

Food and Drink Scene of Hue

Hue’s food scene is known for its traditional Vietnamese dishes. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

Bun Bo Hue

Bun Bo Hue is a spicy beef noodle soup that originated in Hue. The broth is made from pork bones, beef shanks, and lemongrass, giving it a unique flavor.

Banh Khoai

Banh Khoai is a Hue style crepe made from rice flour, shrimp, pork, and bean sprouts. The crepe is typically served with a savory dipping sauce.

Local Beverages

Hue Beer

Hue Beer is a popular beer brand in Hue. It is a refreshing lager that is perfect for quenching thirst on a hot day.

Hue Sweet Soup

Hue Sweet Soup is a dessert soup made with beans, coconut, and tapioca pearls. The soup is often enjoyed as a refreshing sweet treat after a meal.

Mid-Range

Budget

Hong Thien Ruby Hotel

The Hong Thien Ruby Hotel is a budget-friendly option that offers clean and comfortable rooms. The hotel is located in the city center, making it convenient for exploring Hue’s attractions.

What to Buy in Hue: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Hue has numerous souvenir shops that sell traditional Vietnamese products. Here are some recommendations:

Conical Hats

Conical Hats are a popular Vietnamese souvenir item. They are traditional Vietnamese hats made from bamboo and palm leaves and are perfect for keeping the sun out of your face.

Traditional Vietnamese Clothing

Traditional Vietnamese clothing such as the Ao Dai and Non La are popular souvenir items. Visitors can buy these clothes from local markets and shops.

Local Culture and Customs of Hue

Hue has a rich history and culture, and it’s essential to respect the local customs and traditions. Here are some things to keep in mind:

  • Dress modestly when entering temples and pagodas
  • Remove shoes before entering religious sites and homes
  • Greet locals by saying “Xin Chao” (pronounced sin chow)
  • Respect the local culture and traditions

Best Time to Visit Hue

The best time to visit Hue is from March to May and September to November. During these months, the weather is mild and comfortable, making it perfect for exploring the city’s attractions.

Do’s and Don’ts of Hue

Here are some Do’s and Don’ts to keep in mind during your trip to Hue:

Do:

  • Try the local food
  • Visit the historical sites and cultural landmarks
  • Respect the local culture and customs
  • Explore the natural beauty of the surrounding areas

Don’t:

  • Disrespect the local culture and customs
  • Be loud and disruptive in public places
  • Leave trash behind in public places

Safety and Health

Safety in Hue

Hue is a relatively safe city, but visitors should take precautions to ensure their safety. Here are some tips:

  • Avoid walking alone at night in quiet areas
  • Keep valuables in a safe place
  • Be aware of pickpockets in crowded areas

Health in Hue

Visitors should take precautions to ensure their health during their trip to Hue. Here are some tips:

  • Drink only bottled or boiled water
  • Use sunscreen to protect against the sun’s harmful rays
  • Be aware of the risk of dengue fever and take appropriate measures to avoid mosquito bites

Getting to Hue: Transportation Options and Logistics

Hue is easily accessible via several modes of transportation.

Airports Near Hue

The closest international airport to Hue is Da Nang International Airport, which is approximately 2 hours from Hue by car.

Bus Stations Near Hue

Hue has a central bus station that connects to major cities in Vietnam.

Other Options

Visitors can also rent a car or motorbike to travel to Hue or take a train from Hanoi to Hue.

How to Get Around in Hue

Hue is a small city, and visitors can easily get around on foot, bicycle, or motorbike. Taxis and Grab (a ride-hailing service popular in Southeast Asia) are also available.

Where to Go Next from Hue: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Hue is a great base for exploring other destinations in Central Vietnam. Here are some nearby destinations and excursions to consider:

Da Nang

Da Nang is a coastal city located 2 hours south of Hue. The city has numerous attractions such as the Marble Mountains, Son Tra Peninsula, and My Khe Beach.

Hoi An

Hoi An is a charming old town located 3 hours south of Hue. The town is known for its well-preserved old architecture and colorful lanterns.

Conclusion

Hue is a fantastic destination in Vietnam, with a rich culture, history, and natural beauty. Visitors can explore numerous attractions, taste delicious local food, and enjoy the city’s relaxed atmosphere. With this comprehensive guide, planning your trip to Hue has never been easier.
