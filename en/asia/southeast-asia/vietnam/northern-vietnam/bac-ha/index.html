Introduction to Bac Ha

Bac Ha is a small town located in the northern mountains of Vietnam. It is famous for its stunning landscapes, rich culture, and regional markets, making it the perfect destination for travelers seeking an authentic experience. Bac Ha is a melting pot of ethnic groups, which results in diverse cultures, food, and traditions.

List of Places to Visit in Bac Ha

Bac Ha offers a wide range of attractions from historical sites, scenic outdoor views, and various activities. Below is a list of must-visit spots in Bac Ha:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Bac Ha Market: A popular market known for its vibrant colors and diverse cultures.
  • Hoang A Tuong Palace: A French-style castle, built-in 1914, reflects the history of the Hmong people.
  • Can Cau Market: Known for selling handmade textiles and animals for local farmers.
  • Lung Phin Market: Offers authentic and traditional products, including medicinal plants, livestock, and tea.

Natural Attractions of Bac Ha: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Ban Pho Village: Surrounded by terraced fields, Ban Pho Village offers rich cultural experiences and beautiful scenery.
  • Bac Ha White Plateau: Home to many hillside tribes, the plateau boasts of fascinating scenery, rolling hills, and calm lakes.
  • Hoang Su Phi Rice Terraces: A vast landscape of rice terraces, which changes colors according to the season.
  • Bac Ha Waterfall: A stunning waterfall encapsulated by lush greenery.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Bac Ha

  • Ta Van Chu trail: A stunning mountain range that provides unbeatable views of Northern Vietnam.
  • The China-Vietnam border trail: A challenging trail that leads you to the heart of the dense forests.

Best Things to Do in Bac Ha: Activities and Tours

  • Participate in a farming activity: Get hands-on experience and learn from locals’ sustainable farming practices.
  • Trekking and hiking: Discover the gorgeous terraced fields and explore hillsides.
  • Visit ethnic minority villages: Experience the authentic culture and lifestyle of hill tribes.
  • Soak in the natural hot springs: Relax and rejuvenate in a natural mineral spring.

Week-Long Itinerary for Bac Ha

Day 1: Visit Hoang A Tuong Palace and Bac Ha Market. Day 2: Trek in Ban Pho Village and explore the surrounding waterfalls. Day 3: Hike through terraced fields and visit ethnic minority villages. Day 4: Discover the beauty of Ta Van Chu Trail and enjoy the sunset at a local cafe. Day 5: Take a scenic drive and visit Lung Phin Market and Can Cau Market. Day 6: Explore the rice terraces of Hoang Su Phi or relax in the natural hot springs. Day 7: Depart from Bac Ha and head to your next destination.

Food and Drink Scene of Bac Ha

Bac Ha’s culinary culture is influenced by its diverse ethnic groups and its location close to the Chinese border. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages worth tasting:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Thang Co: An iconic dish of Bac Ha, made of horse meat and organs and cooked with added savory herbs.
  • Com lam: Vietnamese sticky rice cooked inside bamboo, usually served with grilled meat or vegetables.
  • Banh chung: Vietnamese sticky rice cake filled with mung bean, pork belly, and spices.

Local Beverages

  • Corn wine: Made from fermented corn and is a popular drink in Bac Ha.
  • Shan Tuyet Tea: A unique tea, grown in the high altitude mountains, offering a complex and rich flavor.

Where to Stay in Bac Ha: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Bac Ha provides various accommodation options that suit every budget and preference. Here are some recommended options:

  • Sao Mai Hotel: A well-maintained hotel offering basic amenities and a convenient location.
  • An Binh Hotel: A small hotel with comfortable rooms and friendly staff.
  • Bac Ha Hostel: A cozy hostel offering a warm and welcoming environment.

What to Buy in Bac Ha: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Bac Ha is known for its handicrafts and textiles, making it a perfect place to purchase souvenirs. Here are some recommended items:

  • Embroidered clothes: Traditional clothes with delicate embroidery that reflects the unique Hmong culture.
  • Medicinal herbs: herbs used for medicinal purposes, and due to Vietnam’s long history of herbal medicine, they are famous for their effectiveness.
  • Handmade crafts and jewelry: Handcrafts and jewelry are always popular options for souvenirs, and Bac Ha offers a wide range of options to choose from.

Local Culture and Customs of Bac Ha

While Bac Ha is a tourist-friendly place, one must respect the local culture and customs. Here are some notes:

  • Dress conservatively, particularly when visiting ethnic minority villages.
  • Avoid eating and drinking in front of locals who are fasting during Ramadan.
  • When visiting a Buddhist temple, dress neatly and avoid taking off your shoes.

Best Time to Visit Bac Ha

The best time to visit Bac Ha is from September to November when the weather is cool and dry, making it comfortable to explore the natural surroundings. However, it is also an ideal place to visit year-round.

Do’s and Don’ts of Bac Ha

The following are some do’s and don’ts to keep in mind when visiting Bac Ha:

Do:

  • Respect the local culture and customs.
  • Try the local cuisine.
  • Shop at Bac Ha’s vibrant markets.

Don’t:

  • Litter or damage public property.
  • Disrespect religious sites or traditions.
  • Take photos of locals without their permission.

Safety and Health

Safety in Bac Ha

Bac Ha is a generally safe place to travel. Visitors are advised to take necessary precautions such as keeping a close eye on personal belongings, avoiding dark and isolated places, and being vigilant during crowded events.

Health in Bac Ha

Visitors visiting Bac Ha should take necessary measures to protect themselves from common travel-related illnesses. It is essential to stay hydrated and avoid eating street food. Visitors should also check with their doctor to ensure up-to-date vaccines and antimalarials.

Getting to Bac Ha: Transportation Options and Logistics

While Bac Ha is only around 70km from Lao Cai, there are several routes depending on which direction you are coming from. Here are some options:

Airports Near Bac Ha

The nearest airport to Bac Ha is Hanoi’s Noi Bai Airport, which is around 300km away.

Bus Stations Near Bac Ha

Bac Ha has limited bus stations. However, the Lao Cai bus station, which has several routes, is close by.

Other Options

Taxis, car rentals, or private cars are other options available for transportation to Bac Ha.

How to Get Around in Bac Ha

Walking is a popular option in Bac Ha, as most attractions are nearby. Taxis or motorbike rentals are also available for those who want to explore Bac Ha’s surroundings.

Where to Go Next from Bac Ha: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Sapa: A popular tourist destination known for its stunning landscapes and diverse culture, located around 100km from Bac Ha.
  • Ha Giang Loop: Home to some of the most breathtaking sceneries in Vietnam, located around 80km from Bac Ha.
  • Hanoi: Vietnam’s capital city, located around 300km from Bac Ha.

Conclusion

Bac Ha offers an authentic and cultural experience that tourists won’t forget. With an array of attractions, food, and scenery, Bac Ha is a must-visit destination when exploring Vietnam’s northern region. With proper planning, visitors can experience Bac Ha to the fullest.
