Introduction to Cuc Phuong National Park

As the oldest national park in Vietnam, Cuc Phuong National Park offers a diverse range of experiences for outdoor enthusiasts and nature lovers. The park, located in Ninh Binh Province, is home to various species of flora and fauna, including several rare and endangered ones. The park also features historical and cultural sites, making it an excellent destination for those interested in Vietnam’s history and culture.

List of Places to Visit in Cuc Phuong National Park

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Cuc Phuong Visitor Center: Get an overview of the park’s history, flora, and fauna before starting your exploration. The center also features several educational displays and exhibits.

  • Eco Trail: Discover the historical and cultural significance of Cuc Phuong village, located within the park. Explore the traditional houses and learn about the daily life of the local people.

  • Prehistoric Cave: Visit the prehistoric cave and learn about the ancient history of the park. The cave contains several fascinating fossils and artifacts, including stone tools and human fossils.

Natural Attractions of Cuc Phuong National Park: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • The Tien Cave: Take a guided tour to explore the stalactites and stalagmites of the Tien cave, located in the heart of the park. The cave is famous for its unique and diverse formations.

  • The Endangered Primate Rescue Center: The rescue center is dedicated to protecting several endangered species of primates, including the Delacour’s Langur and the Black Crested Gibbon. Visitors can observe the primates in a natural habitat and learn about conservation efforts.

  • The Botanical Garden: Stroll through the gardens and explore the various species of trees, flowers, and plants that call the park home. The park’s botanical gardens offer breathtaking views and a chance to experience the park’s natural beauty.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Cuc Phuong National Park

  • The Valley Route: This moderate hiking route takes you through the lush greenery of the park, offering stunning views and a chance to spot wildlife, including several species of primates.

  • The Muong Village Route: This easy biking route takes you through the scenic countryside of Cuc Phuong Park, including Muong Village. Along the way, you’ll not only enjoy stunning views of the surrounding landscape but also learn about the local culture and daily life.

Best Things to Do in Cuc Phuong National Park: Activities and Tours

  • Wildlife Spotting Tour: Take a guided tour to spot several species of primates, including the critically endangered Delacour’s Langur.

  • Night Safari Tour: Explore the park after dark, watching as the wildlife come to life amidst the shadows. The night safari tour allows visitors to observe several species of nocturnal animals.

Food and Drink Scene of Cuc Phuong National Park

Must-Try Dishes

  • Ninh Binh Rice Pancakes: A popular local dish made with rice flour, ground pork, and shrimp. The pancakes are served with a dipping sauce made of fish sauce, vinegar, and sugar.

  • Goat Meat Dish: A traditional dish consisting of tender goat meat in a rich and flavorful broth. The dish is often accompanied by herbs and fresh vegetables.

Local Beverages

  • Moc Chau Tea: A locally produced tea made from the leaves of the Moc Chau tea plant. The tea is known for its smooth and crisp taste.

  • Ninh Binh Wine: A fortified wine made from sticky rice. The wine is often enjoyed as an aperitif or dessert wine.

Where to Stay in Cuc Phuong National Park: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Cuc Phuong Resort & Spa: A luxury resort featuring an outdoor pool, spa, and restaurant.

  • Tuan Anh Guest House: A budget-friendly guest house offering basic amenities.

  • La Belle Vie Tam Coc Homestay: A charming homestay located in the nearby town of Tam Coc, offering an authentic Vietnamese experience.

What to Buy in Cuc Phuong National Park: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Handicrafts: Handmade crafts and souvenirs made by local artisans, including embroidered clothing, pottery, and woodcarvings.

  • Herbal Tea: Locally produced herbal teas, including lemongrass and ginger tea, are popular souvenirs.

Local Culture and Customs of Cuc Phuong National Park

  • Modest clothing is recommended when visiting local temples and cultural sites.

  • It is considered impolite to touch someone’s head or show the soles of your feet.

Best Time to Visit Cuc Phuong National Park

The best time to visit Cuc Phuong National Park is between October and December, when the weather is cooler and drier, providing optimal conditions for hiking and outdoor activities.

Do’s and Don’ts of Cuc Phuong National Park

Do:

  • Respect the environment and avoid littering.

  • Follow the park’s rules and regulations.

  • Hire a local guide for hiking and wildlife spotting tours.

Don’t:

  • Disturb the wildlife or their natural habitat.

  • Pick flowers or plants from the park.

  • Take home any stones or other natural items from the park.

Safety and Health

Safety in Cuc Phuong National Park

  • Avoid hiking alone.

  • Wear appropriate footwear and clothing.

  • Use insect repellent to prevent mosquito bites and other insect-borne illnesses.

Health in Cuc Phuong National Park

  • Drink only bottled water.

  • Avoid street food if you have a sensitive stomach.

  • Carry any necessary medication with you.
