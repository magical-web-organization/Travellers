Introduction to Halong

Located in Ha Long Bay, Northern Vietnam, Halong is a picturesque destination known for its stunning natural beauty, crystal clear waters, and iconic limestone karsts. This charming town attracts visitors from all over the world who wish to witness the breathtaking scenery, explore the local culture and history, and engage in various outdoor activities. Whether you’re a nature lover, food enthusiast, or adventure seeker, Halong has something for everyone.

List of Places to Visit in Halong

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Halong Bay Marine Heritage Museum: This museum provides visitors with an insight into the history and culture of the region. It houses an impressive collection of artifacts, photos, and exhibits showcasing the geology, ecology, and history of Halong Bay.
  • Quang Ninh Museum and Library: This modern and interactive museum showcases the natural, cultural, and historical highlights of Quang Ninh Province, including Halong Bay. It’s an excellent place to learn about the local culture and identity.
  • Poem Mountain: Also known as Bai Tho Mountain, this peak offers fantastic panoramic views of Halong Bay. It’s a popular destination for locals and visitors alike, and the scattered poems and inscriptions on the mountain walls make it even more special.
  • Dau Go Cave: Dau Go Cave is a magnificent cave complex featuring incredible stalactites, stalagmites, and rock formations, illuminated by colorful lights. According to legend, it was once a hiding place for General Tran Hung Dao’s troops during an invasion.

Natural Attractions of Halong: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Halong Bay: Halong Bay is undoubtedly the star attraction of this region, famous for its towering limestone karsts that rise out of the emerald waters. There are various ways to explore the bay, including a traditional junk boat ride, kayaking, or a seaplane tour.
  • Cat Ba Island: Located in the southwestern part of Halong Bay, Cat Ba Island is the largest island in the archipelago. It’s the perfect place to escape the crowds and indulge in some outdoor activities like hiking, rock climbing, or cycling.
  • Bai Tu Long Bay: Bai Tu Long Bay is often referred to as the off-the-beaten-path alternative to Halong Bay. It’s a secluded and pristine area featuring similar limestone karsts, caves, and beaches with fewer tourists.
  • Tuan Chau Island: Tuan Chau Island is a small island that has been transformed into a world-class resort destination. It features white sandy beaches, luxurious hotels, and various water sports and entertainment activities.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Halong

  • Sung Sot Cave Hike: This easy to moderate hike takes you through a scenic trail to the majestic Sung Sot Cave. The hike takes approximately 45 minutes to an hour, and once you reach the cave, you’ll be mesmerized by its grandeur and beauty.
  • Cat Ba National Park Hike: This 15 km trek takes you through the dense tropical forest of Cat Ba Island, and the summit offers breathtaking views of Halong Bay. The hike can take anywhere between three to six hours, depending on your pace and fitness level.
  • Bai Tu Long Bay Bike Route: This scenic route takes you through the idyllic countryside of Bai Tu Long Bay, passing through quaint fishing villages, rice paddies, and lush greenery. The route is suitable for all levels, and it’s an excellent way to explore the local culture and scenery.

Best Things to Do in Halong: Activities and Tours

  • Indulge in the Local Cuisine: Halong is known for its fresh seafood, delicious street food, and unique local dishes like Banh Cuon Cai Rong, a rice noodle roll with pork filling.
  • Take a Junk Cruise: Taking a junk boat cruise is the best way to explore the bay and its incredible limestone karsts.
  • Kayaking in Halong Bay: Kayaking is a great way to explore the hidden coves and lagoons of Halong Bay.
  • Scuba Diving: Halong Bay is one of the few places in the world where you can scuba dive among limestone karsts and discover the underwater world.
  • Tai Chi on Deck: Early morning Tai Chi on the boat deck is an excellent way to start the day and connect with the serene surroundings.
  • Squid Fishing: Squid fishing is a unique and exciting activity that allows you to catch squid with your own hands using fishing rods and lures.

Food and Drink Scene of Halong

Must-Try Dishes

  • Fresh Seafood: Halong is known for its fresh seafood, and you can find it cooked in various ways, including steamed, grilled, and fried.
  • Banh Cuon Cai Rong: A popular local dish, it’s a rice noodle roll filled with pork and served with a side of fish sauce.
  • Fried Spring Rolls: These delicious and crispy spring rolls are stuffed with vegetables and meat and served with a sweet chili sauce.
  • Nem Cua Be: These are deep-fried crab spring rolls that are crunchy on the outside and juicy on the inside.
  • Bun Ca: A type of fish noodle soup that is a popular breakfast option in Halong.

Local Beverages

  • Bia Hoi: This is a type of Vietnamese beer that is brewed fresh daily and served in small plastic cups. It’s a popular beverage among locals and a great way to cool off on a hot day.
  • Tra Duong: A traditional Vietnamese sweet tea that is brewed with cinnamon and ginger. It’s a comforting and refreshing drink that is perfect for relaxing after a long day of exploring.

Where to Stay in Halong: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Wyndham Legend Halong Hotel: This is a luxurious hotel that offers stunning views of Halong Bay and an array of amenities.
  • Novotel Halong Bay: This hotel offers comfortable rooms, excellent service, and a beautiful rooftop pool with views of the bay.
  • Halona Hotel: This hotel is budget-friendly and offers clean and comfortable rooms with friendly staff.
  • Central Backpackers Hostel: This hostel offers affordable and clean accommodation with a social atmosphere and central location.

What to Buy in Halong: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Pearls: Halong is known for its freshwater pearls, and you can find various shops selling them in different grades and sizes.
  • Embroidered Fabric: There are various types of embroidered fabric in Halong, including linen, silk, and cotton, featuring beautiful patterns and designs.
  • Vietnamese Coffee: Vietnamese coffee is famous for its strong, bold flavor and unique preparation. You can find it in different blends and roasts in various shops in Halong.
  • Bamboo Products: Bamboo products like chopsticks, baskets, and bowls are ubiquitous in Halong. They are eco-friendly, durable, and make great souvenirs.

Local Culture and Customs of Halong

The local culture of Halong is influenced by the people who call it home, including the Vietnamese, Chinese, and French. Halong is predominantly a fishing town, and fishing is a way of life for many locals. The locals are friendly and hospitable, and they take pride in their culture and traditions, including the famous Halong Bay Boat Festival. As a visitor, it’s important to respect the local customs and culture, dress appropriately, and be mindful of the environment and wildlife.

Best Time to Visit Halong

The best time to visit Halong is from October to May when the weather is dry and cool, with plenty of sunshine and less humidity. The peak tourist season is from December to February, and it’s advisable to book your accommodation and tours in advance during this time.

Do’s and Don’ts of Halong

Do:

  • Respect the local culture and customs
  • Dress modestly, especially when visiting temples or religious sites
  • Try the local cuisine
  • Engage with the locals and learn about their way of life
  • Practice responsible tourism and be mindful of the environment

Don’t:

  • Take any artifacts or souvenirs from historical or cultural sites
  • Disrespect the environment and litter
  • Ignore the safety guidelines for activities like kayaking or scuba diving
  • Haggle too aggressively while shopping
  • Take photos of locals or their homes without their permission

Safety and Health

Safety in Halong

Halong is a relatively safe destination, but it’s always wise to be cautious and take precautions. It’s advisable to avoid traveling alone at night and to keep your valuables in a safe place. When engaging in outdoor activities like kayaking or hiking, it’s essential to listen to the guides and follow their instructions.

Health in Halong

It’s recommended to consult with a healthcare professional before traveling to Halong and to ensure that all necessary vaccinations are up to date. It’s also important to carry insect repellent, sunscreen, and other necessary medication. It’s essential to stay hydrated, especially during the hot season, and to avoid tap water, drink only bottled or filtered water.

Getting to Halong: Transportation Options and Logistics

Halong is located around three hours’ drive from Hanoi, the capital city of Vietnam. Here are some transportation options to consider:

Airports Near Halong

  • Noi Bai International Airport (HAN): This is the closest airport to Halong Bay, and it’s located in Hanoi, around 150 km away.

Bus Stations Near Halong

  • Luong Yen Bus Station (Hanoi): Regular buses leave from Luong Yen Bus Station in Hanoi to Halong Bay, taking around 4-5 hours.
  • Halong Bay Bus Station (Halong): Halong Bay Bus Station is located in the main town of Halong Bay and offers various bus services to different parts of Vietnam.

Other Options

  • Private Car/Minivan: Private cars or minivans can be hired from Hanoi, and the travel time is around 3-4 hours depending on the traffic.
  • Motorbike: Motorbikes are available for rent in Hanoi, and it’s a fun way to explore the scenic countryside of Vietnam, including Halong Bay.

How to Get Around in Halong

  • Taxis: Taxis are available in Halong, and they are relatively cheap. Be sure to use a reputable taxi company, insist on using the meter, and agree to the price before boarding.
  • Motorbikes: Motorbikes are available for rent in Halong, and they are an excellent way to explore the town and its surroundings.
  • Bicycles: Bicycles can be rented from hotels and guesthouses, and they are perfect for short trips around town.

Where to Go Next from Halong: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Sapa: Sapa is a charming hill station located in the northwest region of Vietnam, known for its stunning rice terraces, ethnic minority groups, and hiking trails.
  • Hanoi: The bustling capital city of Vietnam is a must-visit destination, featuring ancient temples, street food, and French colonial architecture.
  • Ha Giang: Ha Giang is an off-the-beaten-path destination located in the northernmost region of Vietnam known for its dramatic landscapes, colorful markets, and cultural diversity.

Conclusion

Halong Bay is undoubtedly one of the most mesmerizing destinations in Vietnam and Southeast Asia. It’s a place of incredible natural beauty, rich culture and history, and exciting adventure opportunities. From exploring hidden caves and lagoons to indulging in delicious seafood and kayaking on the clear waters, Halong is a true paradise for any traveler. Use this comprehensive guide to plan an unforgettable trip to Halong Bay.
