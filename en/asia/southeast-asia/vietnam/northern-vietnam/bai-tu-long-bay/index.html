Introduction to Bai Tu Long Bay

Located in Northern Vietnam, Bai Tu Long Bay is often considered as the quieter and less crowded alternative to Halong Bay. This stunning bay boasts over 3000 limestone islands, which rise majestically from the sea, creating incredible landscapes and breathtaking views. Bai Tu Long Bay is a great place to relax, unwind, and immerse yourself in the beauty of nature.

List of Places to Visit in Bai Tu Long Bay

Bai Tu Long Bay has several culturally rich attractions, natural wonders, and outdoor adventures. Here’s a list of our top picks:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Vung Vieng Fishing Village: A floating village with a unique way of living, where you can see the daily life of local fishermen and try their fresh seafood.
  • Cong Dam Island: A small island with beautiful beaches, pristine water, and rock formations that are millions of years old.
  • Cua Van Fishing Village: A floating village situated in a tranquil lagoon with stunning scenery, where you can see the local way of life and fishing techniques.

Natural Attractions of Bai Tu Long Bay: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Bai Tu Long National Park: A protected area with a diverse range of flora and fauna, including monkeys, bats, and hornbills, and numerous hiking trails to explore.
  • Thien Canh Son Cave: A beautiful cave with colorful stalactites and stalagmites that reflect the light in interesting ways.
  • Cap La and Tra San: Two picturesque fishing villages with untouched natural beauty, traditional lifestyle, and fresh seafood.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Bai Tu Long Bay

Bai Tu Long Bay has some of the best trekking and cycling routes in Vietnam, with many paths leading through the hills, lush forests, and rice paddies. Some of the popular routes include:

  • Quan Lan Island Cycling Tour: A three-hour tour of Quan Lan Island’s stunning landscapes and unique culture, with stops at the local market, pagoda, and the island’s lighthouse.
  • Tra Ban Trekking Tour: A six-hour tour through the forest and fields of Tra Ban Island, where you can see the local farming, fishing, and weaving practices.
  • Bai Tu Long Bay Kayak Tour: A day-long tour of the bay, where you can explore the hidden lagoons, caves, and floating villages, while paddling through crystal clear water.

Best Things to Do in Bai Tu Long Bay: Activities and Tours

Bai Tu Long Bay offers a range of exciting activities and tours for visitors. Here are some of our favorites:

  • Island Hopping Tour: A day tour of the bay, where you can visit several different islands and explore their unique landscapes and cultures.
  • Cooking Class: A fun and informative experience, where you can learn how to make traditional Vietnamese dishes, using fresh local ingredients.
  • Overnight Cruise: A luxurious way to experience the beauty of Bai Tu Long Bay, where you can relax on board a boat, enjoy delicious food, and admire the incredible scenery.

Week-Long Itinerary for Bai Tu Long Bay

Bai Tu Long Bay is a great destination for a week-long vacation. Here’s our suggested itinerary for an unforgettable trip:

Day 1-2: Explore the natural wonders of Bai Tu Long Bay, including island hopping, kayaking, and visiting Thien Canh Son Cave.

Day 3-4: Immerse yourself in local culture, by visiting fishing villages like Vung Vieng and Cua Van, attending a cooking class, and trying some delicious seafood.

Day 5-7: Unwind and relax, by enjoying an overnight cruise, visiting the tranquil beaches of Tra San and Cap La, and hiking or cycling through the national park.

Food and Drink Scene of Bai Tu Long Bay

The food scene of Bai Tu Long Bay is diverse and flavorful, with a particular emphasis on fresh seafood and local produce. Here are some dishes and drinks you shouldn’t miss:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Banh Cuon: A steamed rice cake filled with minced pork, mushrooms, and shallots, served with sweet fish sauce.
  • Cha Ca: A tasty fish dish, marinated in turmeric and ginger, and served with herbs, noodles, and peanuts.
  • Pho: A Vietnamese noodle soup, made with beef or chicken broth, rice noodles, and various herbs.

Local Beverages

  • Ruou Can: A traditional Vietnamese rice wine, served in bamboo tubes and usually mixed with herbs and honey.
  • Ca Phe Trung: A famous Vietnamese egg coffee, made with egg yolks, coffee, sugar, and milk, and served hot or cold.
  • Tra Da: A refreshing iced tea, made with green tea leaves and sometimes flavored with fruits or herbs.

Where to Stay in Bai Tu Long Bay: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Bai Tu Long Bay has a wide range of accommodation options to suit all budgets and preferences. Whether you’re looking for luxurious resorts, cozy guesthouses, or backpacker hostels, you’ll find plenty of choices. Here are some of our favorite options:

  • Vinpearl Discovery 3 Ha Long Bay: A stunning resort with ocean-view rooms, a private beach, a spa, and several restaurants.
  • Dragon Bay Hotel: A stylish and modern hotel with comfortable rooms, a rooftop pool, and a great location.
  • Cong Doan Bai Tu Long Resort: A rustic and charming resort with bungalows and villas, a private beach, and a restaurant that serves excellent seafood.

What to Buy in Bai Tu Long Bay: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Bai Tu Long Bay is known for its rich culture and traditional handicrafts. Here are some souvenirs and local products you can bring back home:

  • Pearl Jewelry: Bai Tu Long Bay is famous for its high-quality pearls, which come in various colors, sizes, and shapes.
  • Hand-woven Rattan Products: Local artisans make beautiful and functional baskets, hats, bags, and furniture from rattan.
  • Quan Lan Island Wine: A delicious and unique wine, made from the fruits of a local tree called Vai.

Local Culture and Customs of Bai Tu Long Bay

Bai Tu Long Bay has a rich cultural heritage and deep-rooted customs that are worth exploring and respecting. Here are some facts and tips that can help you understand the local culture:

  • Bai Tu Long Bay is home to many ethnic groups, such as the Tay, Dao, and San Chi, who have their own language, traditions, and beliefs.
  • The locals are friendly and hospitable, but also proud and independent, and they appreciate visitors who show respect and cultural sensitivity.
  • Traditional festivals and ceremonies, such as the Mid-Autumn Festival, the Lunar New Year, and the Buffalo Fighting Festival, are essential parts of the local culture and attract many visitors.

Best Time to Visit Bai Tu Long Bay

The best time to visit Bai Tu Long Bay is from October to May, when the weather is dry and mild, and the water is calm and clear. The peak season is from December to February, when the weather is cool and comfortable, and the sky is blue and sunny. The summer months from June to September can be hot and humid, with occasional typhoons and storms, but also quieter and less crowded.

Do’s and Don’ts of Bai Tu Long Bay

Here are some do’s and don’ts to keep in mind when visiting Bai Tu Long Bay:

Do:

  • Respect the local culture, customs, and traditions.
  • Dress modestly and appropriately, especially when visiting temples and other religious sites.
  • Try the local food, drinks, and handicrafts, and support the local economy.

Don’t:

  • Litter or pollute the environment, and always dispose of your trash properly.
  • Touch or remove any marine life, especially coral reefs.
  • Engage in any illegal or unethical activities, such as gambling, drugs, or prostitution.

Safety and Health

While Bai Tu Long Bay is generally safe and peaceful, there are some safety and health concerns that visitors should be aware of.

Safety in Bai Tu Long Bay

  • Be cautious when swimming or participating in water activities, as the water can be deep and the currents can be strong.
  • Make sure to book your tours and activities with reputable and licensed operators, and check the safety equipment and procedures beforehand.
  • Avoid wandering alone at night, especially in remote or unfamiliar areas, and always keep an eye on your belongings.

Health in Bai Tu Long Bay

  • Make sure to bring mosquito repellent and sunscreen, as the sun and pests can be intense.
  • Drink only bottled or boiled water, and avoid eating raw or undercooked food.
  • Seek medical attention immediately if you experience any symptoms of illness or injury, and make sure to have adequate health insurance coverage.

Getting to Bai Tu Long Bay: Transportation Options and Logistics

Bai Tu Long Bay is easily accessible from Hanoi, which is about 165 kilometers away. Here are some transportation options and logistics to consider:

Airports Near Bai Tu Long Bay

  • Noi Bai International Airport: The closest airport to Bai Tu Long Bay, located in Hanoi, about 3-4 hours away by car or bus.
  • Cat Bi International Airport: A larger and more convenient airport than Noi Bai, located in Hai Phong, about 2-3 hours away by car or bus.

Bus Stations Near Bai Tu Long Bay

  • My Dinh Bus Station: A major bus station in Hanoi, with regular services to Bai Tu Long Bay and other destinations.
  • Luong Yen Bus Station: Another bus station in Hanoi, with services to Bai Tu Long Bay and other parts of northern Vietnam.

Other Options

  • Private Transfer: You can book a taxi, car rental, or private transfer from Hanoi to Bai Tu Long Bay, which offers more comfort and flexibility but can be more expensive.
  • Cruise Package: Many cruise operators offer all-inclusive packages that include transportation, accommodation, meals, and activities, which can be a convenient and luxurious way to explore the bay.

How to Get Around in Bai Tu Long Bay

The best way to get around Bai Tu Long Bay is by boat, which allows you to explore the islands, caves, and fishing villages at your own pace. You can rent a kayak or join a guided tour to visit the scenic spots and hidden corners of the bay. Alternatively, you can cycle, hike, or walk on some of the islands or the national park, which offer stunning views and fresh air.

Where to Go Next from Bai Tu Long Bay: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Bai Tu Long Bay is surrounded by many other attractions and destinations in northern Vietnam, which are worth a visit. Here are some of the top picks:

  • Halong Bay: The most famous and popular bay in Vietnam, located next to Bai Tu Long Bay, with even more limestone islands and caves.
  • Sapa: A charming mountain town with rich ethnic minority culture and picturesque rice terraces, about 250 kilometers away from Bai Tu Long Bay.
  • Ha Giang: A stunning and less touristy province with breathtaking landscapes, winding roads, and colorful ethnic markets, about 300 kilometers away from Bai Tu Long Bay.

Conclusion

Bai Tu Long Bay is a hidden gem of Vietnam, with its breathtaking scenery, rich culture, and diverse activities. Whether you’re looking for relaxation, adventure, or cultural immersion, you’ll find plenty of things to do and see in this beautiful bay. Follow our recommendations, and plan your trip today!
