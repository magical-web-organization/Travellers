Introduction to Mong Cai

Mong Cai is located in the northernmost part of Quang Ninh Province, Vietnam, bordering China’s Guangxi Province. This city is known for its bustling border crossing, vibrant culture, and historical landmarks. The surrounding area offers visitors a wide range of exciting outdoor activities and breathtaking natural scenery. In this travel guide, we will explore the best places to visit, things to do, food and drink scenes, culture and customs, safety and health tips, transportation options, and nearby destinations in Mong Cai, Vietnam.

List of Places to Visit in Mong Cai

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Nguom Ngao Cave: This 2.1km-long cave is located in Gun Village, Dam Thuy Commune. Visitors can marvel at the stunning stalactites and stalagmites formations and unique stone carvings created by nature over millions of years.
  • Truc Lam An Tam Zen Monastery: This serene Buddhist temple is located at the foot of Dong Liem Mountain, on Tran Nhan Tong Street. Visitors can take part in meditation classes and admire the beautiful architecture and intricate decorations.
  • Mong Cai Border Gate: This is the busiest border gate between Vietnam and China. Visitors can witness the hustle and bustle of cross-border trading and immerse themselves in the vibrant atmosphere of the marketplaces on both sides.

Natural Attractions of Mong Cai: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Yen Phu Lake: This peaceful lake is located in Yen Phu Village, Hai Son Commune. Visitors can take a leisurely stroll in the surrounding park or rent a boat to explore the tranquil waters.
  • Mount Cao Xiem: This mountain is the highest peak in Mong Cai, standing at 314m high. Visitors can enjoy a breathtaking panoramic view from the top and take in the fresh mountain air.
  • Tra Co Beach: This 17km-long beach is one of the most beautiful beaches in Vietnam, located in Tra Co Ward. Visitors can indulge in sunbathing, swimming, and enjoying fresh seafood at the nearby restaurants.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Mong Cai

  • Hiking route: Visitors can explore Mount Cao Xiem and enjoy the scenic views along the way. The route is quite challenging, and visitors should bring plenty of water and proper hiking gear.
  • Biking route: Visitors can follow the coastal road from Tra Co Beach to Dongxing, China. The route is relatively easy and offers breathtaking views of the sea and surrounding countryside.

Best Things to Do in Mong Cai: Activities and Tours

  • Border crossing excursion: Visitors can witness the busy border crossing between Vietnam and China and immerse themselves in the vibrant atmosphere of the marketplaces on both sides.
  • Seafood dining experience: Visitors can savor the fresh seafood delicacies at the local restaurants, such as grilled oysters, steamed scallops, and butterflied shrimps.
  • Island hopping tour: Visitors can take a boat to visit the stunning uninhabited islands surrounding Mong Cai and enjoy the peaceful scenery and fresh sea air.

Food and Drink Scene of Mong Cai

Must-Try Dishes

  • Grilled oysters: Freshly harvested oysters are grilled on charcoal and served with a tangy dipping sauce.
  • Steamed scallops: Local scallops are steamed and served with garlic and vermicelli noodles.
  • Butterflied shrimps: Fresh shrimps are butterflied and deep-fried and served with chili and lemongrass dipping sauce.

Local Beverages

  • Rice wine: This traditional Vietnamese spirit is made from fermented rice and is often infused with medicinal herbs.
  • Egg coffee: This unique coffee recipe involves beating egg yolks with condensed milk and hot coffee to create a decadent and creamy drink.

Where to Stay in Mong Cai: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Mong Cai International Hotel: This luxury hotel offers stunning sea views and amenities such as swimming pool, spa, and fitness center.
  • Kieu Anh Guesthouse: This cozy guesthouse offers affordable rooms and is located within walking distance of the city center and marketplaces.
  • Full House Hostel: This hostel provides a friendly and welcoming atmosphere, comfortable rooms, and free breakfast.

What to Buy in Mong Cai: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Chinese tea: Visitors can buy high-quality Chinese tea from the marketplaces on both sides of the border.
  • Shell crafts: Local artisans create intricate and beautiful crafts from shells collected from nearby beaches.
  • Vietnamese coffee: Visitors can buy premium-quality Vietnamese coffee beans from local shops and markets.

Local Culture and Customs of Mong Cai

  • Respect for elders: Vietnamese culture places great importance on showing respect and deference to older generations, so visitors should be mindful of this custom.
  • Tipping: Tipping is not a common practice in Vietnam but is appreciated in the hospitality industry.
  • Dress code: Visitors should dress modestly and conservatively when visiting temples and other religious sites.

Best Time to Visit Mong Cai

The best time to visit Mong Cai is from September to November and from March to May when the weather is mild and pleasant, and the skies are often clear. Avoid visiting during the summer months when the weather is hot and humid and during the winter months when temperatures can drop to freezing.

Do’s and Don’ts of Mong Cai

Do:

  • Practice social etiquette: Vietnamese culture values politeness and humility, so visitors should be courteous and respectful at all times.
  • Try the local cuisine: Vietnamese cuisine is famous for its fresh ingredients and bold flavors, so visitors should try as many local dishes as possible.
  • Learn some Vietnamese phrases: A few basic Vietnamese phrases such as “xin chao” (hello) or “cam on” (thank you) can go a long way in showing respect and making connections.

Don’t:

  • Disrespect local customs and traditions: Visitors should be aware of and respect local customs and traditions, such as removing shoes before entering temples and not touching people’s heads.
  • Take photographs without permission: Visitors should always ask for permission before taking photographs of people or religious sites.
  • Over haggling: Haggling is a common practice in Vietnamese markets, but visitors should do so respectfully and not overdo it.

Safety and Health

Safety in Mong Cai

Mong Cai is generally a safe city, but visitors should still take simple precautions such as keeping valuables secured and being aware of their surroundings. Visitors should also be cautious of pickpocketing and petty theft in crowded areas and marketplaces.

Health in Mong Cai

Visitors should make sure that their vaccinations are up-to-date before traveling to Vietnam. It is also recommended to drink only bottled or filtered water, avoid street food from unhygienic places, and apply mosquito repellant to avoid mosquito-borne diseases such as dengue fever.

Getting to Mong Cai: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Mong Cai

The nearest airport to Mong Cai is Cat Bi International Airport, which is approximately 160km southwest of the city. From there, visitors can take a bus or rent a car to travel to Mong Cai.

Bus Stations Near Mong Cai

The main bus station in Mong Cai is the Mong Cai Bus Station, which offers regular bus services to various destinations in Vietnam. Visitors can also take a bus to Dongxing, China, from the Mong Cai Bus Station.

Other Options

Visitors can also take a train from Hanoi to Halong and then take a taxi or bus to Mong Cai. Another option is to hire a private car or take a guided tour from Hanoi to Mong Cai.

How to Get Around in Mong Cai

The most convenient way to get around in Mong Cai is by taxi or motorbike taxi. Visitors can also rent a bicycle or motorbike to explore the city and surrounding areas on their own.

Where to Go Next from Mong Cai: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Halong Bay: This UNESCO World Heritage Site is located approximately 130km south of Mong Cai and offers visitors stunning natural scenery and exciting outdoor activities.
  • Yen Tu Mountain: This sacred mountain is located approximately 90km west of Mong Cai and offers visitors the chance to explore stunning landscapes and ancient temples.
  • Lao Cai: This city is located approximately 190km north of Mong Cai and offers visitors a glimpse into traditional Vietnamese culture and history.

Conclusion

Mong Cai is a hidden gem in northern Vietnam, offering visitors breathtaking natural scenery, exciting outdoor activities, vibrant markets, and historical landmarks. With this comprehensive travel guide, visitors can make the most of their time in this beautiful city and immerse themselves in the local culture and traditions.
