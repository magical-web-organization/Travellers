Can Tho, situated in Southern Vietnam, is a vibrant city known for its beautiful landscapes, historical sites, and local culture. It is one of the largest cities in the Mekong Delta region and a popular destination for travelers seeking an authentic Vietnamese experience. In this comprehensive travel guide, we’ll take you through everything you need to know about Can Tho, from its must-visit attractions and cultural hotspots to its incredible food and drink scene.

Introduction to Can Tho

Can Tho is a vibrant city located in the Mekong Delta region of Southern Vietnam. It is known for its stunning landscapes, vibrant culture, historical significance, and bustling markets. The city is surrounded by beautiful rice paddies, fruit orchards, and rivers, which make it one of the most scenic destinations in the country. Can Tho is home to a thriving community of locals who are happy to share their way of life with visitors.

List of Places to Visit in Can Tho

Whether you’re interested in history, culture, or nature, Can Tho offers plenty of attractions to explore. Here are some of the must-visit places:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Can Tho Museum: Take a step back in time at this fascinating museum, which showcases the history and culture of the Mekong Delta region.

  • Binh Thuy Ancient House: This 150-year-old house, built-in French colonial style design, is considered one of the most beautiful houses in the region and a testament to the country’s ancient architectural beauty.

  • Ong Pagoda: A historic Buddhist temple dedicated to the Chinese merchant Quang Trieu. The temple complex is a popular location for religious practices and rituals and is considered as one of the city’s must-visit landmarks.

Natural Attractions of Can Tho: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Cai Rang Floating Market: No trip to Can Tho is complete without a visit to this famous floating market. Observe the bustling market as you float down the winding river and sample delicious local cuisine, fresh fruits, and vegetables at the market stalls.

  • Bang Lang Stork Sanctuary: Bird lovers will enjoy visiting the Bang Lang Stork Sanctuary, home to a range of migratory birds. The sanctuary is situated about 45 minutes away from the city centre.

  • My Khanh Tourist Village: A picturesque village showcasing the region’s flora and fauna through various activities such as boat rides, fishing, folk games, and fruit picking.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Can Tho

For those who love the great outdoors, Can Tho offers a wide variety of biking or hiking experiences through the surrounding landscapes and rustic villages. Some popular options include:

  • Biking along the Mekong River: Rent a bike and pedal through the charming villages and rice paddies that line the Mekong River.

  • Hiking in Soai Nou National Park: Located less than an hour from the city, this national park offers hiking trails and plenty of opportunities to spot local wildlife.

  • Biking in My Khanh Tourist village: Explore the idyllic countryside of the Can Tho region by bike on a tranquil ride through scenic roads and paths fringed by paddy fields.

Best Things to Do in Can Tho: Activities and Tours

Apart from sightseeing and outdoor activities, Can Tho offers many other interesting things to see and do. A few notable ones include:

  • Can Tho Countryside Tour: Experience the local way of living in Can Tho by visiting the traditional villages and sampling local delicacies with a tour operator’s help.

  • Traditional Vietnamese Cooking Class: Learn how to cook Vietnamese cuisine with local ingredients, a popular activity among tourists.

  • Can Tho Photography Tour: Capture the picturesque scenery of Can Tho with a professional photographer and learn new photography techniques.

Food and Drink Scene of Can Tho

Can Tho’s food scene is well-known for its flavorful and vibrant dishes and drinks. Here are some must-try dishes and drinks to add to your travel list:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Banh Xeo: A savory pancake made from rice flour, filled with a variety of meat options, bean sprouts, and other vegetables.

  • Bun Rieu: A rich, tangy noodle soup consisting of tomato broth-based sour soup with a delicious mix of crab meat, prawns, and minced pork.

  • Hu Tieu: A noodle soup that has its base from pork or beef broth. It consists of many ingredients such as seafood, pork, beef, quail eggs, and more.

Local Beverages

  • Coconut Juice: Refreshing and slightly sweet, straight from the coconut shell;

  • Cincau Drink: A refreshing drink consisting of cincau grass jelly served with sugar syrup, coconut milk, and ice; or

  • Tra Da: Arguably Vietnam’s national drink, Tra da is the simple, humble form of iced tea served with lime and sugar upon request.

Where to Stay in Can Tho: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Can Tho’s accommodation scene can cater to any traveler’s preferences and budget, ranging from luxury hotels to backpacker hostels. Some of our top picks include:

  • Vinpearl Can Tho Hotel: An opulent stay that offers world-class amenities, positioned on Ninh Kieu River in the city center.

  • Nam Bo Boutique Hotel: Situated atop a hill, this welcoming guesthouse provides a picturesque view of Can Tho from the balcony.

  • Cantho Hotel: A budget-friendly option situated directly in the center of the city, offering easy access to main attractions and activities.

What to Buy in Can Tho: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Can Tho has a range of traditional souvenirs to bring home. Here are a few popular options:

  • Silk Products: Can Tho is known for its high-quality silk, and you can find traditional clothing, tablecloths, and accessories made from this luxurious fabric at the local markets.

  • Phong Canh Pictures: These unique pictures are created by stretching silk over a wooden frame, painting the scenery, and lighting the art from the back, giving the scenery a three-dimensional depth.

  • Can Tho Rice: Known for the delicious rice that comes from the Mekong Delta, locals consider high-quality rice a must-buy product from the Can Tho region.

Local Culture and Customs of Can Tho

Can Tho is a city steeped in local culture and customs, with a strong sense of community and hospitality. If you want to make the most of your visit, it is worth familiarizing yourself with some of the local customs and etiquette. Some tips to keep in mind include:

  • Dress modestly while visiting temples and other sacred sites.

  • Remove your shoes before entering someone’s home.

  • Tipping is not generally expected, but it is appreciated if you receive excellent service.

Best Time to Visit Can Tho

The best time to visit Can Tho is from August to November when the weather is cooler and less humid. During this period, you can also witness the superb rice harvest season. Summer months can be quite hot, and the rainy season from May to October can make some areas difficult to access.

Do’s and Don’ts of Can Tho

While visiting Can Tho, it is essential to respect local customs and traditions. Here are some do’s and don’ts to remember:

Do:

  • Respect local customs and dress codes, especially when visiting temples and other sacred sites.

  • Try local cuisine and drinks at the street stalls and cafes.

  • Learn basic Vietnamese phrases, such as “Hello” and “Thank you.”

Don’t:

  • Take photographs of people without their permission.

  • Point with your feet or touch someone on the head.

  • Touch or pick flowers or fruit from private gardens.

Safety and Health in Can Tho

Can Tho is generally a safe place to visit, but it is always important to take precautions when traveling to a new place. Here are some tips to keep in mind:

Safety in Can Tho

  • Keep your valuables secure and be aware of your surroundings, especially in crowded areas.

  • Use extra caution when crossing busy roads; traffic can be hectic.

  • Take care of your personal belongings and keep them close to you at all times.

Health in Can Tho

  • Drink only bottled water and avoid ice in your drinks.

  • Carry mosquito repellent as dengue fever is prevalent in the region.

  • Check with your doctor before traveling to get updated regarding vaccination requirements and other health advisories.

Getting to Can Tho: Transportation Options and Logistics

Getting to Can Tho from other parts of the country is quite easy. Here are some of the transportation options you can choose from:

Airports Near Can Tho

  • Can Tho International Airport: Located about eight kilometers from the city centre.

Bus Stations Near Can Tho

  • Can Tho Bus Terminal: Located on No.91, Cach Mang Thang Tam Street, An Phu Ward, Ninh Kieu District.

  • Mien Tay Bus Station: Located in district 6 in Ho Chi Minh City.

Other Options

  • Private Car: You can hire a private car from a reputable transportation company operating from Ho Chi Minh City.

  • Boat: If you’re cruising from other riverside cities, you can hire a boat to take close to Can Tho Ferry Terminal, 10 km outside of the city center

How to Get Around in Can Tho

The most common transportation option for tourists in Can Tho is a taxi, but there are other options like the cyclo or bike rental. Traffic conditions are generally lighter than in many other Vietnamese cities.

Where to Go Next from Can Tho: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Can Tho is just one of the gems of the Mekong Delta; from here, you can plan your excursion to a range of nearby destinations:

  • Chau Doc: Known as Vietnam’s “rice bowl,” Chau Doc is a picturesque city situated on the Cambodian border known for its cultural attractions.

  • Vinh Long: A prosperous province on the banks of the Mekong, with a comfortably small town vibe.

  • Phong Dien Floating Market: Besides the Cai Rang Floating Market, Phong Dien is another typical floating market on the peaceful river, famous for its authenticity of daily life in the Mekong Delta.

Conclusion

Can Tho is a vibrant and culturally rich destination in Vietnam that offers something for everyone, from beautiful landscapes and outdoor adventures to historical and cultural sites that visitors can discover and explore. Whether you’re traveling solo, with your family, or with friends, you are sure to have an unforgettable experience in one of the most beautiful cities in Southeast Asia.
