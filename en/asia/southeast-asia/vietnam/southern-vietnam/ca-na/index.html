Introduction to Ca Na

Ca Na is a picturesque coastal town situated in the Southern region of Vietnam. While it may not be as popular as other tourist destinations in the country, it is rapidly gaining recognition for its stunning scenery, untouched beaches, and serene atmosphere. The town is named after a bright red lobster (called “Ca Na” in Vietnamese) that is recorded to be abundant in the area.

Ca Na is best known for its pristine beaches, crystal-clear water, and natural landscapes. There are plenty of outdoor activities to indulge in, ranging from hiking, biking, snorkeling, diving, and exploring the deserted wilderness in this region. In this guide, we will provide a comprehensive list of the best places to explore, eat, sleep, and have fun in Ca Na.

List of Places to Visit in Ca Na

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Po Klong Garai Towers

The Po Klong Garai Towers have been standing for over 700 years since they were built in honor of King Po Klong Garai, a Champa ruler. These towers mainly served as places of worship and rituals for the Champa people. Visitors can climb the towers to enjoy a panoramic view of the surrounding areas.

The Long Thanh Gallery

The gallery was founded by the nationally renowned artist, Nguyen Son. It is home to one of the most comprehensive collections of contemporary artwork in Vietnam. It provides visitors with a unique platform to admire the country’s rich cultural heritage.

Natural Attractions of Ca Na: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Ca Na Beach

This beach is undisputedly the pride of Ca Na. The beach is known for its crystal-clear waters, verdant reefs, and white sand. Visitors can participate in water sports such as swimming, paddle-boarding, and snorkeling. It is also an excellent place to bask in the sun while sipping on a refreshing drink.

Hon Cau Island

Hon Cau Island is an uninhabited landmass set in the midst of the vast ocean. Visitors can hire a boat to explore the island and enjoy an array of water sports on offer. The island is famous for its unusual rock formations, which are reminiscent of a giant bee’s nest.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Ca Na

Ca Na to Cu Lao Cau

Hiking or biking from Ca Na to Cu Lao Cau is an excellent way to explore this region. This route takes you through picturesque fishing villages and pastures while basking in serene seascapes. The trek culminates at the beautiful Cu Lao Cau Island, which features a unique contrast of rugged rocks and stunning beaches.

Best Things to Do in Ca Na: Activities and Tours

Diving and Snorkeling

Diving and snorkeling are considered must-do activities when visiting Ca Na. These activities provide an excellent opportunity to explore the underwater world and witness natural beauty firsthand.

Try Local Cuisine

Vietnam is renowned for its delicious cuisine, and Ca Na is no exception. Visitors must try fresh seafood from local vendors.

Week-Long Itinerary for Ca Na

Below is a suggested week-long itinerary for travelers visiting Ca Na.

Day Itinerary
1 Arrival in Ca Na. Exploring the town and visiting Po Klong Garai Towers.
2 A day trip to Cu Lao Cau Island, exploring the island and its beaches.
3 Renting a scooter and riding to nearby fishing villages.
4 Visiting the Long Thanh Gallery and learning more about contemporary Vietnamese art.
5 Diving and snorkeling day trip to deeper parts of the sea.
6 Enjoying a day on Ca Na Beach and indulging in water sports.
7 Departure from Ca Na.

Food and Drink Scene of Ca Na

Must-Try Dishes

Banh Xeo

Banh Xeo is a Vietnamese dish that consists of a savory crepe made using rice flour, water, and turmeric and is usually stuffed with pork, shrimp, and vegetables. It is served with fish sauce and is a popular breakfast food in Ca Na.

Ca Na Lobster

As the name suggests, Ca Na Lobster is the quintessential dish of this town. The lobster is best enjoyed when it is barbecued or stir-fried and served with stir-fried noodles and a healthy side of vegetables.

Local Beverages

Sugarcane juice

Sugarcane juice is a refreshing and sweet drink that is freshly made by local vendors. It is usually served with a scoop of ice and enjoyed on a hot summer day.

Where to Stay in Ca Na: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Mia Resort

Mia Resort is a luxurious resort located on the Ca Na coast. It features bungalows that have their own private pools, making it an excellent spot for couples and honeymooners.

The Cliff Resort & Residences

The Cliff Resort is an eco-luxury resort with all the amenities that one would expect from a lavish resort. Located on the edge of Ca Na, it boasts stunning views of the ocean.

What to Buy in Ca Na: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Lobster Snacks

These snacks are a perfect memento of the town’s emblematic “Ca Na” lobster. They are bite-sized biscuits made with lobster flavor, butter, and other traditional ingredients.

Local Culture and Customs of Ca Na

The people in Ca Na are hospitable, friendly, and welcoming to travelers. It is considered a traditional fishing village, and its people are known for their fishery skills. The most widely spoken language in the town is Vietnamese, and visitors should take note of the local customs and etiquette.

Best Time to Visit Ca Na

The best time to visit Ca Na is between December and June as the weather conditions are quite favorable, and the sea is calm and clear.

Do’s and Don’ts of Ca Na

Do:

  • Respect local customs and traditions.
  • Learn a few basic phrases in Vietnamese.
  • Explore the outdoors and indulge in outdoor activities.

Don’t:

  • Litter in the ocean and on the beach.
  • Ignore local customs and behavior.

Safety and Health

Safety in Ca Na

Ca Na is generally considered a safe place to visit. However, visitors should be cautious and keep their belongings safe while exploring the town.

Health in Ca Na

Visitors should take the necessary precautions as the area is prone to mosquito-borne diseases such as malaria and dengue.

Getting to Ca Na: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Ca Na

The nearest airport to Ca Na is the Cam Ranh International Airport, which is located about 115 km from the town.

Bus Stations Near Ca Na

Buses to Ca Na are available from most cities in Vietnam. A few options include:

  • Sleeper Bus: Many companies such as Phuong Nam, Hanh Cafe, and Mai Linh Taxi provide this service.
  • Public Bus: Public buses are available from the tourist center of Nha Trang.

Other Options

One can also hire a private car, or taxi car to reach the town.

How to Get Around in Ca Na

Visitors can rent a scooter or motorbike to explore the town and its surroundings. Taxis are also available to move around town.

Where to Go Next from Ca Na: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Nha Trang

Nha Trang is a popular tourist destination in Vietnam known for its chain of beaches and serene atmosphere.

Mui Ne

Mui Ne is renowned for its sand dunes, making it an excellent spot for sandboarding and quad-biking.

Conclusion

Ca Na is a charming coastal town that offers excellent opportunities for outdoor adventures, relaxing beach days, cultural experiences, and gastronomical delights. We hope this travel guide has been helpful in planning your trip to Ca Na. Enjoy your time in this hidden gem of Vietnam!
