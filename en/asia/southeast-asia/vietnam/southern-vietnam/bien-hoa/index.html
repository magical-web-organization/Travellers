The Ultimate Travel Guide to Biên Hòa, Vietnam

Nestled in Southern Vietnam, Biên Hòa is a thriving city that boasts a unique blend of natural beauty, rich history, vibrant culture, and delicious cuisine. It is the perfect destination for those looking to immerse themselves in authentic Vietnamese experiences, away from the tourist crowds. In this comprehensive travel guide, we will help you discover the best places to visit, things to do, where to stay, what to eat, and more in Biên Hòa, Vietnam.

Introduction to Biên Hòa

Biên Hòa is the capital of Đồng Nai Province, located in the southeastern part of Vietnam. The city is famous for its greenery, along with its traditional architecture, making it a wonderland for nature lovers and history buffs. Biên Hòa used to be a key staging area during the Vietnam War, and as such, it offers a number of historical sites worth visiting.

List of Places to Visit in Biên Hòa

Visitors to Biên Hòa will have plenty of options to choose from, with a variety of attractions like museums, parks, temples, and outdoor adventures. Here are some of the attractions worth exploring:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Đồng Nai Museum of History and Culture: This museum offers visitors a chance to learn about the history and culture of the Đồng Nai Province. The museum has numerous exhibits on the various ethnic groups who lived in the area, along with information on the province’s agricultural and industrial development.
  • Ỷ Lan Temple: This ancient temple dates back to the 18th century and is dedicated to the goddess of mercy. The temple is a cultural landmark and is a great example of traditional Vietnamese architecture.
  • Biên Hòa Airbase: The air base played a crucial role in the Vietnam War, and visitors can see the display of helicopters, aircraft, and armaments used during the war.
  • Hòa Bình Culture Square: It is the hub of social, cultural activities and public entertainment in Biên Hòa. Cultural events and activities, traditional music, and puppet shows are regularly performed here.

Natural Attractions of Biên Hòa: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Hầm Rồng Mountain: Hiking up Hầm Rồng Mountain offers a glorious view of the city and the beautiful scenery around it. A popular spot to watch the sunrise or sunset.
  • Đồng Nai River: The river has a soothing and peaceful atmosphere that attracts visitors who want to escape the city’s chaos. Tourists can take a boat trip, go fishing, or just relax on the riverbank and take in the scenery.
  • Vườn Xuân Eco-Tourism Site: This 300-hectare site is located at the foot of Hầm Rồng Mountain. The place offers visitors an opportunity to explore the natural beauty of Biên Hòa, with an abundance of flowers, orchids, bonsai trees, and a giant fish pond to see.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Biên Hòa

If you’re looking for outdoor activities, Biên Hòa provides excellent hiking and biking routes for all levels of fitness. Here are some of the best routes to explore:

  • Hầm Rồng Mountain: The mountain is a popular hiking destination with a distance of 1500 meters from the foot to the top. There are a number of routes on this mountain depending on your fitness level, and the view from the top is worth the climb.
  • Ỷ Lan Temple Trail: This 2 km trail is a peaceful and scenic walk from Thanh Sơn Bridge to Ỷ Lan Temple, offering great views of the surrounding countryside.
  • Hoà Bình Culture Square: The square is a popular place to rent a bike and explore the city’s scenic beauty. The trail is an easy ride, with several stops along the way to view the attractions.

Best Things to Do in Biên Hòa: Activities and Tours

Biên Hòa has plenty of activities to offer its visitors, from exploring the city’s rich history to taking part in adventure sports. Here are some of the best things to do:

  • Explore War Tunnels: The Cu Chi Tunnels, located near Biên Hòa, were used by the Viet Cong to hide from American troops during the Vietnam War. Visitors can crawl through the underground network of tunnels and gain a unique insight into the war.
  • Visit the Floating Market: Near Biên Hòa are the Cái Bè floating market and the Phong Nam village market, where visitors can explore the bustling markets on boats.
  • Hot springs: Soak in the hot springs in the nearby town of Vũng Tàu for a relaxing getaway.

Biên Hòa has a lot to offer tourists, and with so many options to choose from, we have put together a sample week-long itinerary for your convenience:

Food and Drink Scene of Biên Hòa

Vietnamese cuisine is renowned worldwide for its exotic flavors and freshness. Biên Hòa is no exception, with an array of local delicacies that you must taste during your visit.

Must-Try Dishes

  • Bánh mì: Baguette sandwiches filled with fresh vegetables, herbs, and a choice of meats, such as grilled pork or chicken.
  • Bún vịt: Rice noodles with duck soup, served hot or cold, with bean sprouts, onions, and herbs on top.
  • Bánh xèo: A savory Vietnamese pancake filled with pork, shrimp, and bean sprouts. They are eaten wrapped in lettuce leaves and dipped in sweet and sour fish sauce.
  • Nui Goi: Rice noodles with mixed vegetables and meat (beef/ pork/ chicken) then served with fish sauce.

Local Beverages

  • Cà phê sữa đá: Vietnamese iced coffee with condensed milk, perfect for the hot Biên Hòa weather.
  • Trà đá: Freshly brewed and sweetened iced tea, available everywhere.

Where to Stay in Biên Hòa: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Biên Hòa offers visitors a range of accommodation options to suit all budgets. Here are some of the top-rated ones:

  • Riverside Suites: A luxury hotel located near the Đồng Nai River.
  • Thanh Xuan Hotel: A budget hotel located in the city center.
  • Charm House: A cozy bed and breakfast with a homey atmosphere.

What to Buy in Biên Hòa: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Here are some popular local products worth purchasing during your visit:

  • Pottery: Biên Hòa is famous for its pottery, and you can find traditional glazed ceramics in various shops.
  • Wooden items: Hand-carved wooden items such as chopsticks, bowls, and trays are commonly sold in local markets and shops.
  • Traditional lacquer products: Biên Hòa is renowned for its outstanding traditional lacquer products such as boxes, dishes, and vases.

Local Culture and Customs of Biên Hòa

Vietnamese culture is vibrant and unique, influenced by its long-standing history and diverse ethnic groups. Here are some aspects of local culture and customs to be aware of during your visit to Biên Hòa:

  • Respect to elders: Vietnam has a strong culture of respect for the elderly, so visitors should show politeness and courtesy to older people.
  • Dress modestly when visiting religious sites: When visiting religious sites, visitors should dress modestly and remove shoes, hats, and other accessories.
  • Tipping etiquette: In Vietnam, tipping is not mandatory, but tipping for good service is appreciated. Tipping around 10-15% of the total bill is appropriate.

Best Time to Visit Biên Hòa

The best time to visit Biên Hòa is between December and April, when the weather is cooler and drier. The rainy season typically starts from May to November and can make outdoor activities more difficult.

Do’s and Don’ts of Biên Hòa

Here are some things to keep in mind while visiting Biên Hòa:

Do:

  • Try local food and drinks.
  • Respect local customs and traditions.
  • Learn a few Vietnamese phrases like “Xin chào” (hello) and “Cám ơn” (thank you).

Don’t:

  • Touch someone’s head or feet, as it is considered impolite.
  • Lose your temper in public.
  • Use drugs or engage in any other illegal activities.

Safety and Health

Safety in Biên Hòa

Biên Hòa is a relatively safe city for tourists. However, visitors should take precautions such as keeping their valuables safe and avoiding walking alone at-night.

Health in Biên Hòa

Visitors should drink bottled water and avoid ice in drinks to avoid any stomach issues. When travelling to Biên Hòa, it’s recommended to have up-to-date vaccinations for typhoid, hepatitis A and B.

Getting to Biên Hòa: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Biên Hòa

  • Tan Son Nhat International Airport: The closest airport to Biên Hòa is in Ho Chi Minh City, approximately 30 minutes away.
  • Lien Khuong Airport: Located about 225km away from Biên Hòa, in Da Lat.

Bus Stations Near Biên Hòa

  • Biên Hòa Bus Station: The central station provides regular bus service to nearby cities and provinces.

Other Options

  • Taxis: Taxis are widely available in Biên Hòa and are a convenient mode of transportation.
  • Trains: Saigon Railway Station is the closest train station to Biên Hòa, providing regular services to other provinces in Vietnam.

How to Get Around in Biên Hòa

Getting around Biên Hòa is easy, with various modes of transportation available:

  • Motorbike: Renting a motorbike is one of the most cost-effective ways of getting around Biên Hòa.
  • Taxi: Taxis are widely available and offer safe and convenient transportation. You can book a taxi through popular ride-share apps like Grab.
  • Public transportation: Local buses and shuttle buses are available to reach nearby towns and provinces.
  • Walking: Exploring Biên Hòa on foot is a great way to experience the city’s culture and architecture.

Where to Go Next from Biên Hòa: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

When you’re done exploring Biên Hòa, check out these nearby attractions:

  • Ho Chi Minh City: Located about 30 minutes from Biên Hòa, Ho Chi Minh City is the largest city in Vietnam and is famous for its history, culture, shopping, and nightlife.
  • Vũng Tàu: A coastal city located about 50 km from Biên Hòa, Vũng Tàu is famous for its beaches, seafood, and French colonial architecture.
  • Phú Quốc Island: A beautiful tropical island located near Cambodia and Thailand, offering visitors pristine beaches, warm waters and rich marine life.

Conclusion

Biên Hòa is a vibrant city that offers visitors a unique experience of Vietnamese culture, natural beauty, and history. With this travel guide, you’re now well-equipped to explore Biên Hòa and everything it has to offer. Don’t forget to try the local cuisine, explore the historical sites, and take part in outdoor adventures. Happy travels!
