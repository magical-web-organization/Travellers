Introduction to Con Dao

Con Dao, an archipelago located off the coast of Southern Vietnam, is the country’s hidden gem. Known for its pristine beaches, turquoise waters, lush forests, and rich history, Con Dao is an ideal destination for travelers seeking natural beauty and a historical experience.

While the Con Dao archipelago consists of 16 islands, only the largest island, Con Son, is open to tourists. Despite this restriction, there is still plenty to explore on Con Son, making it an ideal destination for travelers who want to get off the beaten path.

In this comprehensive guide, we’ll provide you with everything you need to know about Con Dao, including the best places to visit, fun things to do, where to stay, and more.

List of Places to Visit in Con Dao

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Con Dao Prison Museum: A solemn reminder of the island’s dark history as a prison used by the French colonialists and later by the South Vietnamese government.
  • Hang Duong Cemetery: A peaceful hillside cemetery dedicated to the thousands of Vietnamese Independence activists who died during the French occupation.
  • Con Dao Temple: A serene place of worship and pilgrimage for the locals.

Natural Attractions of Con Dao: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Dam Trau Beach: A stunning beach with white sand, blue water, and great swimming conditions.
  • Lo Voi Beach: A secluded beach surrounded by nature, great for a peaceful retreat.
  • Con Dao National Park: A diverse biosphere reserve with hiking trails, wildlife viewing, and beautiful views of the island.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Con Dao

  • Con Son Island’s traverse: A strenuous hike that takes you through forests and mountains, following the trail that connects the various historical sites.
  • Biking the island perimeter: A milder way to cover most of the island’s beaches and attractions. Biking is available for rent on the island.

Best Things to Do in Con Dao: Activities and Tours

  • Island boat tour: A scenic tour of the island that takes you to secluded coves and hidden beaches, some only accessible by boat.
  • Snorkeling and diving: Con Dao’s clear waters and vibrant marine life make it an ideal location for snorkeling and diving.
  • Visiting the night market: A chance to taste some local food, enjoy some drinks, and shop for souvenirs.

Week-Long Itinerary for Con Dao

Day 1: Arrival and settling in, visit Con Dao Temple and the Night Market. Day 2: Relax on Dam Trau Beach and climb Con Son Peak. Day 3: Hike the traverse and visit Hang Duong Cemetery. Day 4: Take a boat tour to the surrounding islands and snorkel or dive. Day 5: Visit Con Dao National Park and explore its hiking trails. Day 6: Take a bike ride around the island, stopping at Lo Voi Beach and local cafes. Day 7: Departure.

Food and Drink Scene of Con Dao

Must-Try Dishes

  • Shark’s fin soup: A traditional Con Dao delicacy, made with fresh shark fins and chicken broth.
  • Seafood platter: Fresh seafood caught daily, served with rice and dipping sauces.
  • Bánh xèo: A crispy crepe filled with shrimp, bean sprouts, and herbs.

Local Beverages

  • Coconut water: A refreshing drink found across the island, made from fresh coconuts.
  • Rice wine: A strong alcoholic beverage made from fermented rice, a staple of any Vietnamese celebration.

Where to Stay in Con Dao: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Six Senses Con Dao: A luxury resort on a secluded beach, with villas, private pools, and breathtaking ocean views.
  • Poulo Condor Boutique Resort & Spa: A boutique resort with a tropical garden, a pool, and a small beach.
  • Con Dao Camping: A budget-friendly lodging option that includes tents, hammocks, and simple bungalows.

What to Buy in Con Dao: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Con Dao honey: A sweet souvenir made on the island.
  • Pearls and seashells: The island’s sustainable pearl farming is an ideal gift or souvenir.
  • Souvenir t-shirts: Available at the night market, these t-shirts make a stylish reminder of your trip to Con Dao.

Local Culture and Customs of Con Dao

The Con Dao locals value their tradition and history, and the island’s slow-paced lifestyle reflects that. Visitors are expected to respect the local customs, dress appropriately when visiting temples and shrines, and avoid loud or disruptive behavior.

Best Time to Visit Con Dao

The best time to visit Con Dao is from April to September when the weather is warm, sunny, and the waters are calm. The off-season months from October to March offer possibly fewer crowds and a more relaxed experience, but the weather can be rainy and unpredictable.

Do’s and Don’ts of Con Dao

Do:

  • Respect the local customs and traditions.
  • Dress appropriately when visiting temples and shrines.
  • Participate in eco-tourism activities to help preserve the island’s natural beauty.

Don’t:

  • Litter or damage the environment.
  • Disrespect the local culture or traditions.
  • Take coral or sea creatures from the waters.

Safety and Health

Safety in Con Dao

Con Dao is a safe destination, but it’s always good to take precautions while traveling. Visitors should avoid going out after dark alone and be aware of their belongings when in public areas.

Health in Con Dao

Visitors should be up-to-date on their vaccines before traveling to Con Dao. Mosquito-borne diseases such as dengue fever are endemic in the area, and visitors should take appropriate precautions such as wearing long clothes or using mosquito repellent.

Getting to Con Dao: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Con Dao

The Con Dao Airport serves the island for domestic flights only, departing from Ho Chi Minh City, Can Tho, and other major cities.

Bus Stations Near Con Dao

There are no direct bus routes to Con Dao. Visitors can take a bus to the ferry port in Vung Tau and then take a ferry to Con Dao.

Other Options

A private car service can be arranged from Ho Chi Minh City or Can Tho to the port in Vung Tau.

How to Get Around in Con Dao

Visitors can rent scooters, bicycles, or motorbikes to explore the island. Taxis are available but not as common.

Where to Go Next from Con Dao: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Ho Chi Minh City: The largest city in Vietnam, with a rich history, delicious food, and a vibrant nightlife.
  • Mekong Delta: A vast waterway system that provides home to thousands of fish farmers and picturesque fruit orchards.
  • Nha Trang: A coastal city renowned for its turquoise waters and white sand beaches.

Conclusion

Con Dao is an oasis of natural beauty and history, and it’s worth the effort to explore its treasures. The archipelago’s stunning landscapes, serene beaches, and quiet culture offer visitors a sanctuary away from bustling cities and tourist traps. With this comprehensive guide, you’ll be well on your way to a memorable Con Dao experience.
