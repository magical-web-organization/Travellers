Introduction to Cat Tien National Park

Cat Tien National Park is a protected area located in the southern part of Vietnam, covering over 70,000 hectares. The park serves as one of the most essential conservation areas in the country, home to several endangered species such as the white-shouldered ibis, Javan rhinoceros, Siamese crocodile, and more. Apart from its stunning fauna and flora, the park’s landscapes, rivers, and waterfalls offer visitors an excellent opportunity to experience nature at its finest.

List of Places to Visit in Cat Tien National Park

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

If you’re interested in learning the history and cultural significance of Cat Tien National Park, the following attractions are a must-visit:

  • Dao Tien Endangered Primate Species Centre: A sanctuary for several endangered primates such as gibbons and langurs.
  • Cat Tien National Park Museum: A museum displaying the history and culture of the Park’s local people and wildlife.
  • Ta Lai Longhouse: A traditional longhouse built by the Ma and S’tieng ethnic groups.

Natural Attractions of Cat Tien National Park: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Cat Tien National Park is a paradise for outdoor enthusiasts, with plenty of trails and natural wonders to explore. Here are some of the must-see attractions in the park:

  • Crocodile Lake: A stunning lake in the middle of the forest, home to several Siamese crocodiles.
  • Bau Sau Swamp: A wetland part of the park and home to several bird species like storks and herons, Vietnamese pond turtle, and other animals.
  • Nam Cat Tien Nature Trail: Explore the rich flora and fauna while biking or hiking through the trails. Multiple trails are available, covering a range of distances and difficulty levels.
  • Giant Tung Tree: A towering tree that is the oldest in the park, estimated to be over 1000 years old, standing 48 meters tall with a diameter of nearly 3 meters.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Cat Tien National Park

Cat Tien National Park offers visitors a chance to hike or bike through several breathtaking trails. Some of the recommended routes include:

  • Troong Gia Hill Trail: A short, easy trail offering hillside views of the surrounding area.
  • Bau Sau Loop Trail: A challenging 5.8 km loop offering stunning views of Bau Sau Swamp and its inhabitants.
  • Nam Cat Tien Nature Trail: A moderate trek covering 5 km through the park’s diverse landscapes.

Best Things to Do in Cat Tien National Park: Activities and Tours

Apart from hiking and exploring the park’s natural landscapes, visitors can also participate in other activities. Here are some of the best things to do in Cat Tien National Park:

  • Night Safari: Explore the park at night and observe nocturnal animals like civets, porcupines, and more.
  • Ecotourism Tour: Experience the local culture and learn about the park’s local flora and fauna with a guided tour.
  • Bird Watching: Given its diverse range of bird species, Cat Tien National Park is an ideal location for bird watching.

Week-Long Itinerary for Cat Tien National Park

With so much to see and do in Cat Tien National Park, here is a recommended itinerary for a week-long visit:

Day Activity
1 Arrive at Cat Tien, visit the park’s museum
2 Explore the surroundings with a half-day ecotourism tour
3 Trek the Troong Gia Hill Trail, bike to the Giant Tung Tree
4 Discover the park’s wildlife at the Dao Tien Endangered Primate Species Centre
5 Take a bird watching tour, visit the Ta Lai Longhouse
6 Hike the Nam Cat Tien Nature Trail, go on a night safari
7 Depart for the next destination

Food and Drink Scene of Cat Tien National Park

Must-Try Dishes

Vietnamese cuisine is a joy to explore, and Cat Tien National Park has many food options available to visitors. Here are some of the must-try dishes in the park:

  • Elephant Ear Fish: A regional specialty, made with fish bones and sweet and sour sauce.
  • Banh Xeo: A crispy, turmeric-flavored pancake filled with bean sprouts, pork, and shrimp.
  • Nem Nuong: Grilled pork sausage, served with rice noodles and vegetables.

Local Beverages

Vietnam is known for its warm hospitality, and visitors to Cat Tien National Park will be pleased with the host of refreshing drinks available. Here are two of the most popular:

  • Tra Da: A refreshing iced tea, commonly served with honey and lime.
  • Ca Phe Sua Da: A traditional iced coffee, prepared with sweetened condensed milk.

Where to Stay in Cat Tien National Park: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Visitors to Cat Tien National Park can choose from a vast range of accommodations to suit every preference and budget. Here are some of the best options:

  • Cat Tien Jungle Lodge: A comfortable and eco-friendly lodge in the heart of the park.
  • Green Cat Tien Hotel: A budget-friendly hotel with proximity to the park entrance.
  • Dormitory Cat Tien National Park: An affordable option with shared dormitories in a traditional Vietnamese house.

What to Buy in Cat Tien National Park: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Visitors to Cat Tien National Park can take home local, handmade souvenirs and products that make great gifts for family and friends. Here are some recommended souvenirs:

  • Handmade bamboo handicrafts.
  • Local honey and bee products, available at the park’s information center.
  • Hand-woven S’tieng textiles.

Local Culture and Customs of Cat Tien National Park

Cat Tien National Park is home to several ethnic groups, each with its unique culture and customs. Visitors are asked to respect the local people, their traditional values, and demonstrate responsible and sustainable tourism practices.

Best Time to Visit Cat Tien National Park

The best time to visit Cat Tien National Park is between December and April, outside the rainy season, when the weather is mild and pleasant. That being said, the park is open all year round, so visitors can plan their visit according to their preferences.

Do’s and Don’ts of Cat Tien National Park

Do:

  • Stay on marked trails.
  • Carry sufficient water and food while hiking.
  • Use ecological bicycles available near the park entrance to reduce your carbon footprint.

Don’t:

  • Feed wild animals.
  • Smoke inside the park area.
  • Litter the park’s environment.

Safety and Health

Safety in Cat Tien National Park

Cat Tien National Park is a safe place to visit for tourists. However, visitors need to be cautious while hiking or exploring the park’s wilderness. The park wildlife is dangerous, and visitors should only observe them from a safe distance.

Health in Cat Tien National Park

Visitors should carry insect repellent and take necessary precautions against mosquito-borne illnesses such as Dengue fever. Visitors should also ensure the water they drink is safe for consumption.

Getting to Cat Tien National Park: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Cat Tien National Park

The nearest airport to Cat Tien National Park is Tan Son Nhat International Airport in Ho Chi Minh City, located approximately 135 km from the park.

Bus Stations Near Cat Tien National Park

Tourists can take a bus from Ho Chi Minh City to Cat Tien National Park. The bus journey takes around 4 hours.

Other Options

Visitors can take a taxi or book a private car to reach Cat Tien National Park from Ho Chi Minh City.

How to Get Around in Cat Tien National Park

Visitors can indulge in numerous walking trails inside Cat Tien National Park or go on a guided tour by a licensed operator. Alternatively, visitors can rent a bicycle to explore the park’s natural beauty and wildlife at their leisure.

Where to Go Next from Cat Tien National Park: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

After exploring the beauty of Cat Tien National Park, visitors can embark on a journey to neighboring locations such as:

  • Dalat: An exquisite hill station city located approximately 180 km north of Cat Tien National Park.
  • Phan Thiet: A coastal city famous for its stunning beaches and sand dunes located approximately 144 km east of Cat Tien National Park.

Conclusion

Cat Tien National Park offers an unforgettable experience to those seeking to discover the diverse natural surroundings of Vietnam. The park’s stunning landscapes, cultural significance, and wildlife attract eco-tourists from around the world. Visitors are encouraged to keep in mind the sustainable tourism practices and preserve the beauty of the park for future generations.
