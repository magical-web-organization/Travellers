A Comprehensive Travel Guide to Ha Tien, Vietnam

If you’re looking for an off-the-beaten-path destination in Vietnam, then Ha Tien is the perfect place to explore. Located in the southern part of the country, near the border with Cambodia, Ha Tien is a charming town that is rich in history, culture, and natural beauty. In this travel guide, we will take you through the best places to visit, things to do, where to eat, stay, and more in Ha Tien.

Introduction to Ha Tien

Ha Tien is a small town in the Kien Giang Province of Vietnam, located about 100 km from Can Tho, a major city in the region. This town was historically an important trading hub between Vietnam and Cambodia, and it is characterized by its French-colonial architecture, old temples, and stunning natural scenery. Ha Tien is still a relatively undiscovered destination in Vietnam, which makes it a perfect place to escape the crowds and soak up the local culture.

List of Places to Visit in Ha Tien

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Mac Cuu Tombs: These tombs are the final resting place of the Mac family, who were major political figures in Vietnamese history. The tombs are located on the top of a hill, which offers breathtaking views of the surrounding countryside.

  • Ngoc Tien Monastery: This Zen monastery is said to be one of the most beautiful in Vietnam, and it features stunning gardens, colorful pagodas, and intricate statues and artwork.

  • Ha Tien Market: This bustling market is the perfect place to experience local life in Ha Tien. You can find everything from fresh produce and seafood to handmade crafts and souvenirs.

Natural Attractions of Ha Tien: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Thach Dong Cave: This limestone cave is located just outside Ha Tien, and it features beautiful stalactites and stalagmites. Visitors can explore the cave on foot, and also enjoy stunning views of the surrounding mountains and forests.

  • Phu Tu Islet: This islet is a natural wonder, with crystal-clear waters and pristine white-sand beaches. Visitors can go swimming, snorkeling, or just relax and soak up the sun.

  • Binh San Mountain: This mountain is located just outside Ha Tien, and it offers breathtaking views of the surrounding countryside. Visitors can hike to the top, and enjoy a picnic or watch the sunset.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Ha Tien

Ha Tien is a great destination for active travelers, with plenty of hiking and biking opportunities. The following routes are recommended:

  • Thach Dong Cave Trail: This trail takes visitors through scenic forests and limestone cliffs, before arriving at the entrance to Thach Dong Cave. The trail is about 3 km long, and it is suitable for all levels of hikers.

  • Binh San Mountain Trail: This trail takes visitors to the top of Binh San Mountain, where they can enjoy panoramic views of the surrounding countryside. The trail is about 5 km long, and it is suitable for intermediate-level hikers.

Best Things to Do in Ha Tien: Activities and Tours

  • Take a boat tour of the Mekong Delta: This tour takes visitors on a journey through the Mekong Delta, where they can see local villages, floating markets, and stunning natural scenery.

  • Visit the Fish Sauce Factory: Fish sauce is an important ingredient in Vietnamese cuisine, and in Ha Tien, visitors can see how it is made at a local factory.

Week-Long Itinerary for Ha Tien

Day 1: Arrive in Ha Tien and explore the town Day 2: Visit the Mac Cuu Tombs and Ngoc Tien Monastery Day 3: Take a boat tour of the Mekong Delta Day 4: Visit Thach Dong Cave and Binh San Mountain Day 5: Relax at Phu Tu Islet or take a cooking class Day 6: Explore the local market and visit the Fish Sauce Factory Day 7: Depart Ha Tien

Food and Drink Scene of Ha Tien

Must-Try Dishes

  • Bun ca: This is a noodle soup that is made with fish broth and served with fish meatballs and fresh herbs.

  • Banh khot: These are small pancakes that are made with rice flour and coconut milk, and are topped with shrimp, scallions, and peanuts.

Local Beverages

  • Nuoc mia: This is a refreshing juice made from fresh sugarcane.

  • Tra da: This is a popular iced tea that is served all over Vietnam.

Where to Stay in Ha Tien: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Victoria Nui Sam Lodge: This is a luxurious hotel that is located on a hill, with stunning views of the surrounding countryside.

  • Ha Tien Vegas Hotel: This is a budget-friendly hotel that is located in the town center, with easy access to all the local attractions.

  • The River House - Mekong: This is a guesthouse that is located on the river, with peaceful views and a relaxing atmosphere.

What to Buy in Ha Tien: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Fish sauce: This is a staple of Vietnamese cuisine, and it is made locally in Ha Tien.

  • Handicrafts: Ha Tien is known for its handmade crafts, including pottery, wood carvings, and silk products.

Local Culture and Customs of Ha Tien

  • Respect for elders: In Vietnamese culture, respect for elders is very important. Visitors should always greet older people first, and show deference to their wishes.

  • Bargaining: Bargaining is a common practice in Vietnam, and visitors should negotiate prices when shopping at local markets.

Best Time to Visit Ha Tien

The best time to visit Ha Tien is between November and April, when the weather is dry and mild.

Do’s and Don’ts of Ha Tien

Do:

  • Dress conservatively when visiting temples or other religious sites.
  • Try the local cuisine, including fresh seafood and traditional noodle dishes.
  • Respect local customs and traditions.

Don’t:

  • Don’t touch someone’s head, as it is considered disrespectful in Vietnamese culture.
  • Don’t drink tap water, as it may not be safe for visitors to drink.

Safety and Health

Safety in Ha Tien

Ha Tien is generally a safe destination for visitors, but they should take precautions and keep their valuables in a safe place.

Health in Ha Tien

Visitors should take precautions against mosquito-borne illnesses, including dengue fever and malaria. They should also drink bottled water, and avoid eating raw or undercooked food.

Getting to Ha Tien: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Ha Tien

The nearest airport to Ha Tien is Can Tho International Airport, which is about 100 km away.

Bus Stations Near Ha Tien

There are regular buses from nearby cities like Can Tho, Ho Chi Minh City, and Phnom Penh that arrive in Ha Tien.

Other Options

Visitors can also rent a car or motorbike to travel to Ha Tien, but should be aware of the local traffic rules and road conditions.

How to Get Around in Ha Tien

The best way to get around Ha Tien is on foot or by bicycle. Visitors can also hire a motorbike or car if they wish to explore the surrounding areas.

Where to Go Next from Ha Tien: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Phu Quoc Island: This tropical island is located off the coast of Ha Tien, and is known for its stunning beaches and clear waters.

  • Chau Doc: This small city is located on the Cambodian border, and is known for its famous pagodas and floating villages.

Conclusion

Ha Tien is a hidden gem in Southern Vietnam, with a rich history, stunning natural scenery, and a unique local culture. We hope that this travel guide has given you a taste of what to expect when visiting this charming town, and that it has inspired you to plan your next adventure in Vietnam.
