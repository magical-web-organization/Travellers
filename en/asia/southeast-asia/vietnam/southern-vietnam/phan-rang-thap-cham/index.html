Introduction to Phan Rang–Tháp Chàm

Phan Rang–Tháp Chàm is a city located in southern Vietnam, nestled between the provinces of Ninh Thuận and Khánh Hòa. This scenic city boasts of breathtaking beaches, stunning temples, scenic views, rich history, and a vibrant food scene. It’s a perfect getaway for those who want to experience authentic Vietnamese culture and explore the country’s natural beauty.

List of Places to Visit in Phan Rang–Tháp Chàm

Phan Rang-Thap Cham is home to a variety of culturally significant attractions, natural wonders, and hiking trails. Here’s a list of some of the places you must visit when in Phan Rang-Thap Cham:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Po Klong Garai Cham Temple complex: A group of three towers and two temples, built between the 13th and 14th centuries, it is the most elaborate surviving complex of the Champa Kingdom.
  • Ninh Chu Beach: It is known for its pristine white sand, turquoise water, and beautiful scenery.
  • Hang Rai: A series of cliff-side caves featuring natural rock formations like stalactites and stalagmites.

Natural Attractions of Phan Rang–Tháp Chàm: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Núi Chúa National Park: It is a protected area that covers over 29,000 hectares with an abundance of diverse flora and fauna and beguiling scenery.
  • Bau Truc Pottery Village: A quaint village that’s known for its distinctive pottery in Vietnam.
  • Nam Cuong Sand Dunes: Towering beige sand dunes that will transport you into another world.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Phan Rang–Tháp Chàm

Phan Rang-Thap Cham is a paradise for hikers and bikers as the city offers some exceptional and astonishing routes for adventurous people. Some of the best hiking and biking trails in Phan Rang-Thap Cham are:

  • Nui Chua National Park Hike
  • Nam Cuong Sand Dune Bike Ride
  • The Path of Health Trek

Best Things to Do in Phan Rang–Tháp Chàm: Activities and Tours

Phan Rang–Tháp Chàm offers a variety of activities and tours that will make your trip worthwhile. Some of the best things to do in Phan Rang–Tháp Chàm are:

  • Explore the Po Klong Garai Cham Temple
  • Take a trip to the Ninh Chu Beach
  • Visit the Bau Truc Pottery Village
  • Experience the sunset at Nam Cuong Sand Dunes
  • Trek through the Nui Chua National Park
  • Admire the natural beauty of Hang Rai

Food and Drink Scene of Phan Rang–Tháp Chàm

Phan Rang–Tháp Chàm’s food and drink scene is something not to be missed while visiting the city. Here are some of the must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Bánh căn: Vietnamese pancakes that are cooked in small clay pots with quail eggs, meat, and herbs
  • Bún cá sứa: A spicy seafood soup made with jellyfish and widely popular in Southeast Asian countries
  • Bún sứa: A jellyfish salad mixed with herbs, cucumber, and chili
  • Cơm tấm: A local favourite, it is a Vietnamese dish made of broken rice
  • Gỏi tôm: A prawn salad made with a mixture of herbs and vegetables known for its sour and spicy flavours

Local Beverages

  • Cà phê: Vietnamese coffee, usually served with condensed milk
  • Sữa chua: A traditional Vietnamese yoghurt made with coconut milk
  • Nước mía: Sugar cane juice, a refreshing drink served cold

Where to Stay in Phan Rang–Tháp Chàm: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Phan Rang–Tháp Chàm has a variety of accommodation options depending on your budget. Here are some of the top-rated places to stay in the city:

  • Giang Son 2 Hotel: A 3-star hotel located in the heart of the city, perfect for those who want to explore Phan Rang–Tháp Chàm on foot.
  • Nghia Lo guesthouse: A budget-friendly family-run guesthouse that offers clean and comfortable rooms.
  • Aniise Villa Resort: A luxury 4-star resort located near Ninh Chu beach offering beautiful views and excellent facilities.

What to Buy in Phan Rang–Tháp Chàm: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Phan Rang-Thap Cham is known for its local crafts and souvenirs. Here’s what you should consider buying:

  • Bau Truc pottery: Beautiful traditional pottery made by locals.
  • Rượu nếp: A local rice-based liquor that comes in different flavours and is a perfect gift.
  • Dried seafood: With its coastal location, Phan Rang–Tháp Chàm has a variety of dried seafood to choose from, including cuttlefish and shrimp.

Local Culture and Customs of Phan Rang–Tháp Chàm

Phan Rang–Tháp Chàm is predominantly a Buddhist city with locals practicing traditional Vietnamese customs. It’s essential to understand and respect the culture and customs of the place you’re visiting.

Best Time to Visit Phan Rang–Tháp Chàm

The best time to visit Phan Rang–Tháp Chàm is between December to April when the weather is predominantly dry and sunny, making it ideal for outdoor activities.

Do’s and Don’ts of Phan Rang–Tháp Chàm

Here are some Do’s and Don’ts to follow when visiting Phan Rang–Tháp Chàm:

Do:

  • Respect Buddhist temples and local customs.
  • Wear appropriate clothing when visiting temples or holy sites.
  • Engage with locals and immerse yourself in their culture.

Don’t:

  • Disrespect local customs or religious beliefs.
  • Litter or harm the environment.
  • Take photographs without asking for permission.

Safety and Health

While Phan Rang–Tháp Chàm is generally safe for travellers, being vigilant while travelling is always a good idea.

Safety in Phan Rang–Tháp Chàm

  • Be cautious of pickpocketing and theft in crowded areas.
  • Avoid walking alone in unfamiliar places.
  • Always wear your helmet when biking.

Health in Phan Rang–Tháp Chàm

  • Drink bottled or filtered water.
  • Use mosquito nets and insect repellents during the rainy season.
  • Be careful when eating street food or raw seafood.

Getting to Phan Rang–Tháp Chàm: Transportation Options and Logistics

Phan Rang–Tháp Chàm is well-connected to major cities in Vietnam. Here are the transportation options:

Airports Near Phan Rang–Tháp Chàm

  • Cam Ranh International Airport, located 60 km away from Phan Rang–Tháp Chàm, is the closest airport.

Bus Stations Near Phan Rang–Tháp Chàm

  • Sinh Tourist and Phan Rang Bus Station are the two main bus stations in the area.

Other Options

  • Taxis and private cars are also available for hire.

How to Get Around in Phan Rang–Tháp Chàm

Phan Rang-Thap Cham is a small city, and it’s easy to explore the city on foot or bike. Taxis and motorbikes are also available for hire if you’re trying to get further distances.

Where to Go Next from Phan Rang–Tháp Chàm: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Ninh Thuận Province is home to Núi Chúa National Park, Po Klong Garai Cham Temple, and Bau Truc Pottery Village.
  • Nearby Khánh Hòa Province is home to the famous beach destinations of Nha Trang and Cam Ranh.

Conclusion

Phan Rang–Tháp Chàm is a destination that offers diverse experiences for every type of traveller. It’s a must-visit for anyone looking for a unique cultural, culinary, and adventure-filled experience in Vietnam.
