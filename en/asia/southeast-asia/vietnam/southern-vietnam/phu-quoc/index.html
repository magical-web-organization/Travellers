Introduction to Phu Quoc

Located in the southern part of Vietnam, Phu Quoc island is part of the Kien Giang province in the Mekong Delta region. Considered as the largest island in Vietnam, Phu Quoc boasts of white sandy beaches, turquoise waters, lush jungles and a rich culture. The island has seen a surge in tourism over the past decade and has become a popular destination for both international and domestic travelers.

In this guide, we will take you on a journey to explore the beauty and charm of Phu Quoc. Whether you are a backpacker or luxury traveler, a nature lover or a culture enthusiast, there’s something for everyone on this enchanting island.

List of Places to Visit in Phu Quoc

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Coi Nguon Museum

Located on Tran Hung Dao Street, Coi Nguon Museum is a little gem that depicts the history and culture of Phu Quoc island. The museum has a collection of ancient artifacts, photographs, and displays that showcase the island’s development from its early fishing village days to its current tourism industry.

Phu Quoc Prison

Once a brutal prison during the Vietnam War, Phu Quoc Prison is now a museum that serves as a reminder of the atrocities that took place. The museum displays the torture methods used on the prisoners during the war, and also shows the many escape plots that were attempted.

Dinh Cau Temple

Located at the mouth of the Duong Dong River, Dinh Cau Temple is a popular attraction for travelers. The temple is considered one of the most revered temples on the island and offers stunning views of the sea.

Natural Attractions of Phu Quoc: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Sao Beach

Sao Beach is arguably one of the most beautiful beaches on Phu Quoc island. The waters are a pristine blue, and the sand is white and powdery. The beach is lined with palm trees, and there are many beachside shacks that offer drinks and snacks.

Phu Quoc National Park

Phu Quoc National Park occupies more than half of the island’s total area. The park is home to diverse flora and fauna, including the famous Phu Quoc Ridgeback dog, a breed that is unique to the island. Visitors can hike through the rainforest, swim in the waterfalls, and explore the cave systems.

Ham Ninh Fishing Village

Located on the eastern coast of the island, Ham Ninh Fishing Village is a great spot for travelers to experience the local way of life. The village has a long pier that extends out into the sea, and small fishing boats bob up and down in the water. Visitors can enjoy fresh seafood dishes from the local restaurants.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Phu Quoc

Phu Quoc is a great destination for hiking and biking enthusiasts, as the island offers stunning natural scenery. Here are a couple of routes to give you an idea of what to expect:

Ganh Dau Trail

This 10-kilometer trail takes hikers through the northern part of Phu Quoc island. The trail starts at Ganh Dau beach, and passes through forested areas, mangrove swamps, and fishing village settlements. The hike is relatively easy and can be completed in half a day.

Southern Island Loop

This 60-kilometer road loop is perfect for biking and takes travelers through an array of landscapes such as paddy fields, lush forests, and coastline. The loop starts at Duong Dong city and passes through An Thoi, Sao Beach, and Ham Ninh Village.

Best Things to Do in Phu Quoc: Activities and Tours

Snorkeling and Diving

Phu Quoc is famous for its vibrant marine life and coral reefs. Travelers can take a snorkeling or diving tour to explore the underwater world. Some of the best spots include An Thoi Islands, Turtle Island, and Fingernail Island.

Sunset Cruise

Experience an unforgettable sunset over the Gulf of Thailand onboard a sunset cruise. Sip on a tropical cocktail while taking in the panoramic views of the sea.

Night Market

The Dinh Cau Night Market is a bustling market that is open every evening. Travelers can explore the market and sample delicious local street food, shop for souvenirs, and witness live fish auctions.

Food and Drink Scene of Phu Quoc

Must-Try Dishes

Bun Quay

A popular dish in Phu Quoc, Bun Quay is a rice noodle soup topped with meat, seafood, and vegetables. The dish is usually served with a side of rice crackers.

Ca Mai

This dish is essentially marinated fish that is grilled to perfection. Ca Mai is served with a variety of dipping sauces and is a great dish to enjoy with friends.

Bun Khot

A traditional dish in Phu Quoc, Bun Khot is a type of rice cake that is crispy on the outside and soft on the inside. It is served with shredded coconut, sliced cucumber, and a special sweet and sour sauce.

Local Beverages

Sim Wine

Sim Wine is a local specialty that is made from the fruit of the rose myrtle tree. The wine has a sweet and tangy taste and is usually served chilled.

Nuoc Mia

Nuoc Mia is a popular drink that is made from sugarcane juice. The juice is extracted from sugarcane and mixed with ice and a dash of lemon juice.

Where to Stay in Phu Quoc: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Best Luxury Hotels

JW Marriott Phu Quoc Emerald Bay

With stunning views of the sea and a private beach, the JW Marriott Phu Quoc Emerald Bay is the perfect choice for travelers looking for luxury accommodations. The hotel boasts of a design that is inspired by French colonial architecture, and amenities include a spa, several restaurants, and an infinity pool.

Salinda Resort Phu Quoc Island

Nestled on the shores of the Gulf of Thailand are the Salinda Resort Phu Quoc Island. The resort has an eco-friendly design and offers spacious rooms with balconies, and private beach access. Guests can enjoy the infinity pool, spa, and restaurants.

Best Mid-Range Hotels

Sea Sense Resort

Located on the southern tip of the island, the Sea Sense Resort offers bungalows with sea views, a private beach, and a pool. The resort has a laid-back ambiance and is perfect for travelers looking for a relaxing holiday.

Cassia Cottage

Cassia Cottage is situated on the pristine Long Beach and features garden rooms, beachfront bungalows, and luxury villas. The resort offers a swimming pool, a spa, and a beachfront bar.

Best Budget Accommodations

Sen’s House

Sen’s House is a cozy guesthouse located in Duong Dong. The guesthouse offers clean rooms, free Wi-Fi, and a friendly atmosphere.

Phu Quoc Backpacker

Phu Quoc Backpacker is a great budget option for travelers looking for clean and comfortable accommodations. The hostel offers dormitory-style rooms, free Wi-Fi, and a common area.

What to Buy in Phu Quoc: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Phu Quoc Fish Sauce

Arguably one of the most famous products from Phu Quoc, the fish sauce is considered one of the best in the world. Visitors can buy the fish sauce in local markets or specialty stores on the island.

Sim Fruit

Sim fruit is a plant that is unique to the island of Phu Quoc. The fruit is tangy and sweet and is used in several local dishes. Visitors can buy Sim fruit in local markets on the island.

Local Culture and Customs of Phu Quoc

When traveling to Phu Quoc, it’s important to respect the local culture and customs. Here are a few things to keep in mind:

  1. Wear appropriate clothing when visiting temples and religious sites.
  2. Remove shoes before entering a temple or a local’s home.
  3. Greet the locals with a smile and show respect.
  4. Bargain when shopping in the market, but don’t go too low.

Best Time to Visit Phu Quoc

The best time to visit Phu Quoc is during the dry season, which runs from November to April. The weather is mild, with lower humidity, and the sea is calm.

Do’s and Don’ts of Phu Quoc

Do:

  1. Spend time exploring the natural wonders of the island.
  2. Sample the local food and drink.
  3. Respect the local culture and customs.
  4. Take a sunset cruise.
  5. Bargain when shopping in the local markets.

Don’t:

  1. Litter or damage the environment.
  2. Wear inappropriate clothing when visiting religious sites.
  3. Disrespect the local culture or customs.
  4. Carry valuables around with you.
  5. Travel without adequate sunscreen and mosquito repellent.

Safety and Health

Safety in Phu Quoc

Phu Quoc is a relatively safe destination for travelers. However, it’s important to stay cautious and aware of your surroundings, especially when traveling alone or at night.

Health in Phu Quoc

Before traveling to Phu Quoc, it’s recommended that travelers get vaccinated for Hepatitis A and B and have up-to-date Tetanus shots. Travelers should also be cautious when consuming food and drinks.

Getting to Phu Quoc: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Phu Quoc

Phu Quoc International Airport is the main airport on the island, and several airlines operate flights to and from the island.

Bus Stations Near Phu Quoc

There are several bus stations in Ho Chi Minh City, and travelers can take a bus to Ha Tien or Rach Gia, and then take a ferry to Phu Quoc.

Other Options

Travelers can also take a boat or ferry from Rach Gia or Ha Tien to Phu Quoc. The boat ride takes about two hours from Ha Tien and three hours from Rach Gia.

How to Get Around in Phu Quoc

Travelers can rent a motorbike or hire a taxi to get around the island. Metered taxis are available at the airport and in Duong Dong city. For those who prefer a more eco-friendly mode of transport, bicycles are available for rent, and there are also many private tour operators that offer customized tours of the island.

Where to Go Next from Phu Quoc: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

If you are looking for more adventures after exploring Phu Quoc, there are many nearby destinations to explore, such as the Mekong Delta, Ho Chi Minh City, or the nearby islands of Con Dao and Nam Du.

Conclusion

Phu Quoc is a beautiful island destination that offers a mix of natural beauty, rich cultural history, and stunning vistas. Whether you are looking for a relaxing beach vacation or an adventure-filled getaway, Phu Quoc has something for everyone. So, pack your bags, and come experience the magic of Phu Quoc island!
