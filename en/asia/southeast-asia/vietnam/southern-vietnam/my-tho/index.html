Introduction to My Tho

My Tho is a charming city located in the Tien Giang province, deep in the Mekong Delta of Southern Vietnam. The city is known as the gateway to the Mekong Delta and offers a perfect starting point for exploring the waterways, villages, and rice paddies of this vibrant region. My Tho is also a cultural and historical hub with several important temples and museums to explore.

List of Places to Visit in My Tho

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

My Tho has a wealth of cultural and historical attractions to explore. Here are some of the top must-visit places:

  • Vinh Trang Pagoda: This ancient Buddhist temple is said to be the largest and most significant Buddhist temple in the Mekong Delta. This temple’s beautiful architecture reflects a fusion of Chinese, European, and Vietnamese styles.

  • Dong Tam Snake Farm: This is a unique place where tourists can see and learn about the slithering creatures. The farm’s main mission is to extract venom for medicinal purposes.

  • Museum of the Vietnamese Revolution: The museum is dedicated to the Vietnamese Revolution, with displays about Ho Chi Minh and artefacts from the war.

Natural Attractions of My Tho: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

One of the main attractions of My Tho is the natural beauty of the surrounding area. Here are some popular places to visit and outdoor activities in My Tho:

  • Thoi Son Island: This charming island is a part of the Mekong Delta. The island is famous for its lush greenery, wildlife, and local craft villages.

  • Tan Thach village: This picturesque village is surrounded by lush green fields and features a beautiful pagoda overlooking the river.

  • Can Tho Bridge: This bridge is one of the longest cable-stayed bridges in Southeast Asia. Visitors can enjoy a stunning view of the Mekong Delta and the city of Can Tho from here.

Hiking/Biking Routes of My Tho

The best way to explore My Tho’s natural beauty is by biking or hiking. Here are some popular routes to explore:

  • Nguyen Dinh Chieu Pedestrian Street: A peaceful, picturesque walking area in the heart of the city.

  • Tan Thach Village to Phu An Bamboo Bridge: This scenic route takes you through sleepy towns and lush green fields.

Best Things to Do in My Tho: Activities and Tours

Here are some of the best things to do in My Tho:

  • Take a boat trip: A boat ride on the Mekong Delta is one of the best ways to explore the region’s natural beauty. Visitors can hire a boat with a driver or take a group boat tour.

  • Visit local markets: Visitors can explore the Chau Doc or Cai Be floating markets, which are unique and lively.

Week-Long Itinerary for My Tho

For visitors who want to spend a whole week exploring My Tho, here is a suggested itinerary:

Day 1: Visit Vinh Trang Pagoda and Dong Tam Snake Farm

Day 2: Explore Thoi Son Island and surrounding area

Day 3: Visit the Museum of the Vietnamese Revolution and check out the local markets

Day 4: Go for a biking or hiking tour in Tan Thach Village

Day 5: Take a boat trip to Cai Be floating market and explore the stunning scenery of the Mekong Delta

Day 6: Relax and recharge by the Can Tho Bridge

Day 7: Say goodbye to My Tho and catch a bus to the next destination.

Food and Drink Scene of My Tho

Must-Try Dishes

Vietnamese cuisine is renowned worldwide, and there is no dearth of food options in My Tho. Here are some must-try dishes:

  • Hu Tieu My Tho: A noodle soup dish that involves chewy rice noodles, a clear savoury broth, and a mix of meats and vegetables.

  • Banh Xeo: This savoury pancake with prawns, pork, and bean sprouts is a must-have.

Local Beverages

  • Coconut juice: Fresh coconut juice is a great refreshing drink for the hot and humid weather of My Tho.

  • Ca phe sua da: This traditional Vietnamese iced coffee with condensed milk is a great way to enjoy the local beverage scene.

Where to Stay in My Tho: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

My Tho has a diverse range of lodging options. Here are some of our top picks:

  • Mekong Riverside Boutique Resort & Spa: A luxurious resort located on the banks of the Mekong River.

  • Minh Kieu Park View Hotel: A mid-range hotel with an excellent location opposite of the Tien River.

  • Song Tien Hotel: This budget-friendly option has clean rooms and is located in the city center.

What to Buy in My Tho: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

My Tho is known for its delicious coconut candy, local spices, and various handicrafts. Here are some must-buy souvenirs:

  • Coconut candy: A delicious, sweet treat made from locally grown coconut.

  • Handicrafts: My Tho has several local craft villages that produce beautiful handicrafts such as hats, baskets, and pottery.

Local Culture and Customs of My Tho

My Tho is a Vietnamese city with a rich culture and local customs. Here are some important local traditions to keep in mind:

  • Dress conservatively: As Vietnam is a conservative country, it’s best to dress modestly.

  • Respect local customs: Respect the local customs while visiting the religious and historical sites.

Best Time to Visit My Tho

The best time to visit My Tho is during the dry season, from November to March. The weather is mild, and rainfall is less frequent during this time.

Do’s and Don’ts of My Tho

Do:

  • Use your bargaining, haggling, and people skills while shopping in the local markets.

  • Respect the religion and culture of the locals.

Don’t:

  • Do not give money or gifts to children, as this leads to begging, and it creates a cycle of poverty.

  • Do not ignore warnings about the safety of certain areas.

Safety and Health

Safety in My Tho

My Tho is widely considered a safe city. Apart from the usual precautions while traveling, tourists should take care of pickpockets while in crowded areas.

Health in My Tho

Health risks in My Tho are similar to those in the rest of Southeast Asia. Visitors should stay hydrated, wear sunscreen, and use mosquito repellent.

Getting to My Tho: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near My Tho

The nearest airport is in Ho Chi Minh City, about 70 km (43 miles) away from My Tho. Visitors can take a taxi or bus to reach the city.

Bus Stations Near My Tho

My Tho has several bus stations with connections to Ho Chi Minh City and other cities in the region. Buses are a cost-effective way to travel to and from My Tho.

Other Options

Visitors can take a taxi or hire a car with a driver to reach My Tho from Ho Chi Minh City or other nearby cities.

How to Get Around in My Tho

The best ways to get around My Tho are by taxi, bike, or motorbike. Visitors can hire a bike or motorbike to explore the city and nearby villages.

Where to Go Next from My Tho: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

My Tho is an excellent base for exploring the Mekong Delta region. Here are some places to visit near My Tho:

  • Can Tho: Another bustling town in the Mekong Delta with a vibrant riverside market.
  • Ben Tre: A small, sleepy town that is known for its coconut industry and laid-back atmosphere.

Conclusion

My Tho is a charming city with a rich history and culture. The city’s central location in the Mekong Delta makes it an ideal starting point for exploring the region’s natural beauty and unique villages. Visitors should make sure to try the local cuisine, buy some traditional souvenirs, and explore the surrounding towns and villages while in My Tho.
