Introduction to Apo Kayan

Located in East Kalimantan, Indonesia, Apo Kayan is a hidden gem waiting to be explored. It is a beautiful destination that offers a perfect blend of cultural and natural attractions. Home to various ethnic groups, including Kenyah, Kayan, and Punan, Apo Kayan is a melting pot of cultures and traditions. You will find traces of these traditions in their colorful festivals, traditional music, dance, and art.

List of Places to Visit in Apo Kayan

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Leak Libung Cultural Museum: This museum is a must-visit place in Apo Kayan if you are interested in learning about the local culture and traditions. It showcases traditional attire, ancient weapons, musical instruments, and other cultural artifacts.

  • Rumah Betang: This is a traditional longhouse, which is still home to many Kayan families. It is a fascinating place to visit as it gives you a glimpse of the life of the local people.

  • Batu Kajang: This is a megalith site where you can see ancient stones arranged in different formations. It is believed to be an ancient burial site and is considered sacred by the local people.

Natural Attractions of Apo Kayan: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Loksado River: This river offers an incredible adventure that you should not miss. You can go rafting, kayaking, or tubing while enjoying the scenic views of the surrounding rainforest.

  • Mount Kelam: Hiking to the top of this mountain is a memorable experience. You will encounter various flora and fauna along the way, and the view from the peak is breathtaking.

  • Kayan Mentarang National Park: It is the largest national park in Indonesia and is home to various endangered species such as orangutans, sun bears, and clouded leopards.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Apo Kayan

  • Mount Kelam Summit Hike: It is a challenging yet rewarding hike to the peak of Mount Kelam. It takes around 6-7 hours to reach the summit, but the stunning panoramic view of the valley is worth the effort.

Best Things to Do in Apo Kayan: Activities and Tours

  • Visit the Kayan Mentarang National Park: A guided tour through this national park gives you the opportunity to explore the rich biodiversity of the area. You will also get to interact with the local communities and learn about their lifestyle.

  • Explore Loksado River: Exploring this river through rafting, kayaking, or tubing is a fun and exciting activity that you should not miss.

Food and Drink Scene of Apo Kayan

Must-Try Dishes

  • Ulam: This is a traditional salad made from a variety of vegetables and herbs that is served as a side dish to the main meal.

  • Timus: It is a potato-like root vegetable and is a staple food in Apo Kayan. It is usually boiled or fried and served with sambal or chili sauce.

  • Pansoh: It is a traditional Kayan dish made from chicken or fish cooked with various herbs in a bamboo tube.

Local Beverages

  • Tuak: It is a locally brewed rice wine that is enjoyed during traditional festivals and celebrations.

  • Teh C Peng: It is a traditional iced tea that is sweetened with condensed milk and is a refreshing drink for hot days.

Where to Stay in Apo Kayan: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Jewel of Kalimantan Resort: This resort is located near the Loksado River and provides a memorable experience for its guests with its modern amenities, traditional design, and natural surroundings.

  • Riam Jeram Guesthouse: It is a budget-friendly guesthouse that is located near the town center and provides comfortable rooms and excellent service.

What to Buy in Apo Kayan: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Handmade Baskets: These are made by the local women and are used to store rice and other produce.

  • Traditional Weavings: These are made from handwoven cotton and are available in various colors and designs.

Local Culture and Customs of Apo Kayan

The local people in Apo Kayan are known for their hospitality and are eager to share their culture and traditions with visitors. Some of the cultural practices that you may encounter during your visit include traditional dancing, music, and art demonstrations.

Best Time to Visit Apo Kayan

The best time to visit Apo Kayan is between April and November when the weather is dry and pleasant for outdoor activities. The rainy season is from December to March, which can make it challenging to enjoy outdoor activities.

Do’s and Don’ts of Apo Kayan

Do:

  • Respect the local culture and traditions.
  • Dress modestly when visiting cultural and religious sites.
  • Try the local cuisine and beverages.
  • Take part in cultural events and festivals.

Don’t:

  • Litter in the national park or other outdoor areas.
  • Disrespect the local customs and traditions.
  • Take anything from the national park, including plants and animals.

Safety and Health

Safety in Apo Kayan

Apo Kayan is generally safe for tourists, but it is recommended to take the necessary precautions, such as carrying enough cash, avoiding walking alone at night, and keeping your valuables in a safe place.

Health in Apo Kayan

It is advised to get the required vaccinations and take the necessary precautions to avoid mosquito bites as there is a risk of malaria and other mosquito-borne diseases.

Getting to Apo Kayan: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Apo Kayan

The nearest airport is Sepinggan International Airport in Balikpapan, which is around 13 hours away from Apo Kayan by car.

Bus Stations Near Apo Kayan

There are regular buses from Samarinda, the capital city of East Kalimantan, to Long Pujungan, which is around 2 hours away from Apo Kayan.

Other Options

Renting a car with a driver is the most convenient option to reach Apo Kayan as public transportation services are limited.

How to Get Around in Apo Kayan

The best way to get around in Apo Kayan is by renting a car with a driver as public transportation services are limited.

Where to Go Next from Apo Kayan: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Derawan Islands: These islands are known for their stunning beaches, crystal-clear waters, and vibrant marine life.

  • Sangatta: It is a city in East Kalimantan that is known for its coal mining industry and scenic landscapes.

Conclusion

Apo Kayan is a destination that offers a perfect blend of cultural and natural attractions. From exploring the ancient megalith sites and traditional longhouses to hiking to the peak of Mount Kelam and exploring the Loksado River, there is something for everyone in Apo Kayan. Plan your trip now and experience the magic that this hidden gem of Indonesia has to offer!
