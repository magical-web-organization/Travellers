Introduction to Samarinda

Samarinda, the capital of East Kalimantan, is a vibrant city located on the banks of the Mahakam River. Despite being a relatively small city, it is an important hub for trade, industry, and tourism in Indonesia. Samarinda is a melting pot of cultures, with various ethnic groups and religions living side by side in harmony.

If you’re planning to visit Samarinda, there are many things to see and do to make your trip unforgettable. In this travel guide, we’ll help you discover the best places to visit, things to do, and where to stay during your visit.

List of Places to Visit in Samarinda

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Samarinda has a rich cultural heritage, and there are many historical and cultural attractions to explore in the city. Here are some of the must-visit places:

1. The Museum of East Kalimantan

The Museum of East Kalimantan is the oldest museum in Samarinda and offers a fascinating insight into the history and culture of the region. The museum has an impressive collection of traditional costumes, weapons, household items, and artifacts from the Dayak tribes.

2. Kutai Sultan Palace

The Kutai Sultan Palace is a historical landmark and home to the Kutai Sultanate, one of the longest-reigning kingdoms in Indonesia. The palace is a beautiful example of traditional architecture, and the lush gardens surrounding it are a peaceful retreat from the bustle of the city.

3. Mulawarman Museum

Mulawarman Museum is another great place to learn about the history and culture of East Kalimantan. The museum showcases various archaeological finds, including ancient pottery, artifacts from the Kutai Kingdom, and historical relics from the Indonesian Revolution.

Natural Attractions of Samarinda: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Samarinda is home to some of the most beautiful natural attractions in Indonesia. Here are some of the best places to explore:

1. Bukit Soeharto

Bukit Soeharto is a conservation area located just outside of Samarinda. The lush forest is home to various wildlife, including orangutans, gibbons, and deer. The area is a popular destination for hiking and birdwatching.

2. Mahakam River

The Mahakam River is the lifeblood of Samarinda and the surrounding region. The river offers stunning views of the city and is a popular spot for river cruises.

3. Teluk Lerong Ilir Mangrove Forest

The Teluk Lerong Ilir Mangrove Forest is a beautiful protected area with a boardwalk that allows visitors to explore the mangrove trees and observe the local birdlife.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Samarinda

Samarinda is a great place for outdoor enthusiasts and offers various hiking and biking routes to explore. The following routes are worth checking out:

  • Kawa Putih Trail
  • Gn Arsakbuntalan Trail
  • Bukit Bangkirai Trail

Best Things to Do in Samarinda: Activities and Tours

Aside from outdoor activities, there are many other things to do in Samarinda. Here are a few ideas:

  • Take a city tour to explore the historical and cultural attractions
  • Visit the night market to sample the local food and experience the bustling night scene
  • Join a river cruise to discover the Mahakam River and experience the local way of life

Food and Drink Scene of Samarinda

Samarinda has a diverse culinary scene, and there are many delicious dishes and drinks to try.

Must-Try Dishes

Here are some of the most popular local dishes to try:

  • Nasi Kuning: a fragrant turmeric rice dish served with meat or fish and savory condiments.
  • Nasi Goreng Kambing: a spicy fried rice dish with goat meat and vegetables.
  • Sate Padang: a skewered meat dish with a spicy peanut sauce.

Local Beverages

Here are some local beverages to try:

  • Es Cendol: a sweet, refreshing drink made with coconut milk, green jelly noodles, and palm sugar.
  • Teh Tarik: a tea-based drink with condensed milk.
  • Bajigur: a hot and sweet drink made with coconut milk, brown sugar, and ginger.

  • Swiss-Belinn Samarinda: a modern hotel with a rooftop pool and city views.
  • Mantra Sakala Resort & Beach Club: a beachfront resort with luxurious amenities.

What to Buy in Samarinda: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

If you’re looking for unique souvenirs to bring back home, consider buying some of these items:

  • Dayak crafts and textiles, such as woven baskets and textiles
  • Samarinda River Pearls
  • Kain Kaltim (East Kalimantan Fabric)

Local Culture and Customs of Samarinda

Samarinda is a melting pot of cultures, with various ethnic groups and religions living side by side in harmony. The people of Samarinda are friendly and welcoming, and visitors are expected to show respect for local customs and traditions.

Best Time to Visit Samarinda

The best time to visit Samarinda is between June and August when the weather is dry and sunny. During this season, many festivals are held, and the city is bustling with activity.

Do’s and Don’ts of Samarinda

Here are some do’s and don’ts to keep in mind while visiting Samarinda:

Do:

  • Dress modestly when visiting religious sites and public places.
  • Sample the local cuisine and try new dishes.
  • Greet people with a smile and show respect to elders.

Don’t:

  • Wear revealing clothing in public places.
  • Touch people’s heads, as it is considered rude.
  • Point with your feet, as it is considered impolite.

Safety and Health

Safety in Samarinda

Samarinda is generally a safe city, but it is always advisable to take precautions while traveling. Here are a few tips:

  • Avoid walking alone at night in dark alleys or deserted areas.
  • Keep your valuables in a safe place and carry only essential items.
  • Use a reputable taxi or ride-sharing service when traveling at night.

Health in Samarinda

While Samarinda does not have many health risks, it is recommended to take precautions:

  • Drink only bottled water and avoid tap water.
  • Use insect repellent to prevent mosquito bites.
  • Check current vaccination recommendations before traveling to Samarinda.

Getting to Samarinda: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Samarinda

The nearest airport to Samarinda is Sultan Aji Muhammad Sulaiman Airport (SRI), which is located approximately 20 kilometers south of the city center.

Bus Stations Near Samarinda

The main bus station in Samarinda is Terminal Kota Samarinda, which is located in the city center.

Other Options

You can also take a ferry from other nearby destinations such as Balikpapan or Tanjung Selor.

How to Get Around in Samarinda

The best way to get around Samarinda is by taxi or ride-sharing apps such as Grab. Most taxis and ride-sharing services are metered, but it’s always a good idea to agree on a price before getting into the vehicle.

Where to Go Next from Samarinda: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

If you’re planning to stay longer in Indonesia, consider visiting the following destinations:

  • Kalimantan Barat: for jungle trekking and wildlife spotting
  • Derawan Islands: for pristine beaches and world-class diving

Conclusion

Samarinda is a unique destination that offers a mix of history, culture, and stunning natural scenery. From exploring the city’s museums and historical landmarks to hiking in the lush forests and river cruising, Samarinda has something to offer for every type of traveler. With this comprehensive guide, you’re sure to have an unforgettable trip to Samarinda, Indonesia.
