Introduction to Derawan

Derawan Island is a remote tropical paradise located in the province of East Kalimantan, Indonesia. It boasts of pristine beaches, crystal-clear waters, and a rich marine life that attracts divers and snorkelers from all over the world. Apart from its natural attractions, Derawan is also home to interesting cultural landmarks and local traditions.

List of Places to Visit in Derawan

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Derawan may be a small island, but it is not lacking in cultural significance. Here are some of the must-visit attractions:

  • Kampung Bajo Museum: This museum is dedicated to the history and culture of the Bajo people, a seafaring community that has inhabited the coastal areas of Indonesia for centuries.
  • Kampung Budaya Derawan: Visitors can learn about the traditional dance, music, and crafts of the local communities in this cultural center.
  • Apu Island: This island was formerly a sea gypsy settlement and has a unique cemetery, where its inhabitants buried their dead in tree trunks.

Natural Attractions of Derawan: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Derawan is best known for its natural attractions, which offer plenty of opportunities for outdoor activities and scenic views. Here are some of the top natural attractions:

  • Derawan Island: The main island of Derawan is a popular spot for sunbathing, swimming, and snorkeling. It is home to several resorts, restaurants, and dive shops.
  • Maratua Island: This secluded island is known for its breathtaking sceneries, including its lake-like lagoon, sandy beaches, and towering limestone cliffs. It is also a popular spot for diving and snorkeling.
  • Kakaban Island: This island has a unique brackish lake where visitors can swim with stingless jellyfish. It is also home to diverse marine life and coral reefs.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Derawan

Derawan Island is relatively small, and there are no designated hiking or biking trails. However, visitors can explore the island’s paved roads and dirt paths, which lead to the island’s various attractions.

Best Things to Do in Derawan: Activities and Tours

Apart from swimming, snorkeling, and diving, here are some other popular activities and tours in Derawan:

  • Island Hopping Tour: Visitors can go on a boat tour to explore the nearby islands of Maratua, Kakaban, and Sangalaki. The tour includes a snorkeling or diving session in each island.
  • Turtle Watching Tour: Visitors can join a guided tour to watch sea turtles lay their eggs and hatchlings make their way to the sea.
  • Sunset Cruise: Visitors can watch the sunset while cruising the waters around Derawan Island.

Food and Drink Scene of Derawan

Derawan may be a small island, but it has a surprisingly diverse food and drink scene. Here are some local dishes and beverages visitors should try:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Ikan Bakar: Grilled fish served with rice and sambal sauce.
  • Sate Kelapa: Shrimp or chicken skewers cooked with grated coconut and spices.
  • Lumpia Basah: Fresh spring rolls filled with vegetables and meat, served with spicy peanut sauce.

Local Beverages

  • Kelapa Muda: Young coconut juice, served fresh.
  • Kopi Susu: Indonesian-style coffee with milk and palm sugar.

Where to Stay in Derawan: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Derawan Island has several accommodation options, ranging from budget-friendly homestays to luxury resorts. Here are some of the recommended places to stay:

  • Derawan Dive Resort: This resort offers comfortable rooms, a private beach, and diving and snorkeling packages.
  • Sea Turtle Resort Derawan: This family-run resort has a relaxing atmosphere, a restaurant, and a turtle conservation program.
  • Homestay Mawar Derawan: This budget-friendly homestay is located near the harbor and offers clean rooms and friendly service.

What to Buy in Derawan: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Visitors can bring home these souvenirs and local products as a memento of their trip to Derawan:

  • Pearls: Derawan Island is known for its high-quality pearls. Visitors can buy pearl necklaces, earrings, and other accessories from local vendors.
  • Borneo Crafts: Visitors can buy handmade crafts made from local materials, such as bamboo, rattan, and wood.
  • Kain Tenun: Traditional woven fabrics made by the Dayak tribes in Kalimantan.

Local Culture and Customs of Derawan

Visitors to Derawan should be respectful of the local culture and customs. Here are some tips:

  • Dress modestly when visiting cultural landmarks and religious sites.
  • Ask for permission before taking photos of the locals.
  • Greet the locals with a smile and a nod, especially elders and those in authority.

Best Time to Visit Derawan

Derawan has a tropical climate, with a rainy season from November to March and a dry season from April to October. The best time to visit is during the dry season, when the weather is sunny and clear.

Do’s and Don’ts of Derawan

Here are some general do’s and don’ts to ensure a safe and enjoyable trip to Derawan:

Do:

  • Wear sunscreen and protective clothing when swimming or snorkeling.
  • Bring cash, as there are limited ATMs on the island.
  • Be adventurous and try the local food and drink.

Don’t:

  • Litter in the ocean or on the beach.
  • Touch or disturb the marine life, including the stingless jellyfish.
  • Disrespect the local culture and traditions.

Safety and Health

Safety in Derawan

Derawan is generally safe for visitors, but it is still advisable to take precautions. Here are some safety tips:

  • Lock your valuables in a safe or a secure location.
  • Avoid walking alone at night.
  • Take caution when swimming or snorkeling, especially during high tide or strong currents.

Health in Derawan

Visitors should take the necessary health precautions before coming to Derawan. Here are some tips:

  • Consult your doctor before traveling and get the necessary vaccinations.
  • Use mosquito repellent and sleep under a mosquito net to prevent mosquito-borne illnesses.
  • Drink bottled or boiled water to avoid stomach illnesses.

Getting to Derawan: Transportation Options and Logistics

Visitors can get to Derawan Island by air and sea. Here are the transportation options:

Airports Near Derawan

  • Juwata International Airport: This is the primary airport in East Kalimantan, located in the city of Tarakan, 121 km away from Derawan Island.

Bus Stations Near Derawan

  • Tarakan Bus Station: This is the main bus station in Tarakan, which has daily bus services to major cities in Kalimantan, such as Balikpapan and Banjarmasin.

Other Options

  • Private Boat: Visitors can hire a private boat from Tarakan or Tanjung Selor to go to Derawan Island.

How to Get Around in Derawan

Derawan Island is relatively small, and visitors can explore the island on foot or by bicycle. Visitors can also hire a motorcycle or a boat to explore the nearby islands or go island hopping.

Where to Go Next from Derawan: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Here are some nearby destinations and excursions visitors can go to after their trip to Derawan:

  • Samarinda: This city is the capital of East Kalimantan and has several cultural and historical landmarks, such as the Kutai Sultan’s Palace and the Mulawarman Museum.
  • Berau: This town is the gateway to the Derawan Islands and has several natural attractions, such as the Batu Payung Beach and the Labuan Cermin Lake.
  • Kutai National Park: This national park is home to diverse flora and fauna, as well as traditional Dayak villages.

Conclusion

Derawan Island offers visitors a unique blend of natural beauty, cultural significance, and authentic local experience. Whether you want to explore its waters, relax on its beaches, or immerse yourself in its culture, Derawan has something for everyone. Plan your trip now and discover the hidden gem of Indonesia!
