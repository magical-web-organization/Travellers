Introduction to Bontang

Bontang is a city in East Kalimantan, Indonesia, known for its stunning natural beauty and rich cultural heritage. The city is home to a diverse population of locals and visitors alike, making it a popular tourist destination for those seeking authentic experiences. From exploring museums and historical sites to embarking on outdoor adventures, there’s something for everyone in Bontang. In this travel guide, we’ll take you through some of the best things to see, do, and experience in this charming city.

List of Places to Visit in Bontang

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Bontang is steeped in history and culture, and there are numerous museums and historical attractions that offer a glimpse into the city’s past. Some of the must-visit cultural attractions in Bontang include:

  • Museum Kayu Madak, which showcases traditional houses and wooden sculptures from the Dayak and Kutai tribes.
  • Taman Tepian Teluk, a waterfront park that offers stunning views of the ocean and the city.
  • Monument of Dayak, a landmark statue that symbolizes the Dayak tribe’s contribution to the development of Bontang.
  • Masjid Agung Baitul Makmur, the city’s largest mosque, which boasts beautiful architecture and intricate carvings.

Natural Attractions of Bontang: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Bontang is home to a diverse range of landscapes, including dense forests, pristine beaches, and rolling hills. Natural attractions in Bontang include:

  • Semayang Lake, a scenic lake that’s perfect for boating and fishing.
  • Bukit Bangkirai, a lush rainforest that’s home to a variety of wildlife and hiking trails.
  • Kutai National Park, a UNESCO World Heritage Site that’s home to endangered species such as orangutans and proboscis monkeys.
  • Batu Putih Beach, a white sandy beach that’s perfect for swimming and sunbathing.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Bontang

Bontang has no shortage of hiking and biking trails that offer stunning views of nature and exercise for the body. The top hiking and biking routes of Bontang include:

  • Bukit Bangkirai Trail, a one-of-a-kind elevated canopy walkway between trees in the rainforest.
  • Batu Lamampu Beach Trail, a great route and an absolute adventurer’s paradise, tight jungle canopy, and coastal cliffs are bound to captivate trekkers.
  • Ladang Panjang Trail, a picturesque river and tranquil rice fields are the highlights of this route.

Best Things to Do in Bontang: Activities and Tours

Bontang is a vibrant city with a range of activities and tours available for visitors. Some of the best things to do in Bontang include:

  • Explore local markets, such as Pasar Baru, where you can find local handicrafts and souvenirs.
  • Take a city tour of Bontang to learn more about the city’s history and culture.
  • Enjoy watersports such as jet skiing, wakeboarding, and banana boating at Batu Putih beach.
  • Visit Bontang State Plantation, a historic site that’s home to rubber and oil palm plantations.

Week-Long Itinerary for Bontang

If you’re planning a trip to Bontang, here’s a week-long itinerary to help you make the most of your stay:

Day Activity
1 Explore Taman Tepian Teluk and the Monument of Dayak
2 Visit Bukit Bangkirai and take a canopy walk

Food and Drink Scene of Bontang

No trip to Bontang is complete without trying some of the local cuisine. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Nasi Kuning Bontang, a yellow rice dish that’s typically served with fried chicken and sambal.
  • Sayur Asem, a sour vegetable soup that’s made with tamarind and vegetables.
  • Martabak Manis, a sweet dessert pancake that’s filled with chocolate and cheese.

Local Beverages

  • Es Kelapa Muda, a refreshing drink made with coconut water and ice.
  • Teh Tarik Susu, a sweet milk tea that’s perfect for pairing with local snacks.
  • Sabai, a traditional Dayak rice wine that’s often served during celebrations and ceremonies.

Where to Stay in Bontang: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

There are several accommodation options available in Bontang to suit all budgets and preferences. Some of the top places to stay in Bontang include:

  • Hotel Grand Dafam Bontang, a 4-star hotel with a pool and fitness center.
  • RedDoorz @ Jalan Kenari Bontang, a budget hotel with clean and comfortable rooms.
  • Guest House Lapangan Sepak Bola, a cozy guesthouse with friendly staff and a central location.

What to Buy in Bontang: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Bontang is known for its handicrafts, textiles, and souvenirs that are perfect for bringing home as a memory of your trip. Some of the top items to buy in Bontang include:

  • Dayak handicrafts, such as wood carvings and woven baskets.
  • Traditional textiles, such as tenun and songket.
  • Batik Bontang, a unique local batik that’s made with natural dyes and wax.

Local Culture and Customs of Bontang

Bontang is a city with a rich cultural heritage, and visitors should be mindful of local customs and traditions. Some of the local customs of Bontang include:

  • Shaking hands is a common greeting between people of the same gender. For opposite genders, bowing with hands clasped is more appropriate.
  • Modest dress is encouraged when visiting mosques or other religious sites.
  • Removing shoes before entering a home or religious site is a sign of respect.

Best Time to Visit Bontang

The best time to visit Bontang is during the dry season, which runs from May to October. The weather is usually sunny and warm, making it perfect for outdoor activities and sightseeing. It’s best to avoid the rainy season, which runs from November to April, as some roads may be flooded and outdoor activities may be limited.

Do’s and Don’ts of Bontang

Here are some important do’s and don’ts to keep in mind when visiting Bontang:

Do:

  • Dress modestly when visiting religious sites or attending local events.
  • Be respectful of local customs and traditions.
  • Learn some basic phrases of Bahasa Indonesia.

Don’t:

  • Disrespect local customs or traditions.
  • Be too loud or disruptive in public places.
  • Litter or damage the environment.

Safety and Health

Bontang is generally a safe city for tourists, but it’s always important to take necessary precautions when traveling. Here’s what you need to know about safety and health in Bontang:

Safety in Bontang

  • Petty crime, such as pickpocketing, can occur in crowded places, so it’s essential to keep an eye on your belongings.
  • Avoid walking alone at night and be cautious when using public transportation.

Health in Bontang

  • Tap water in Bontang is not potable, so it’s best to drink bottled water.
  • Mosquitoes can be a problem in some areas, so it’s recommended to wear mosquito repellent and use bed nets.

Getting to Bontang: Transportation Options and Logistics

Bontang is accessible by air, land, and water transportation. Here are the main transportation options:

Airports Near Bontang

  • Sultan Aji Muhammad Sulaiman Airport in Balikpapan is the closest airport to Bontang, and it’s about a two-hour drive from the city.

Bus Stations Near Bontang

  • Bontang Bus Terminal is the city’s main bus station, serving both regional and long-distance buses.

Other Options

  • Boat transportation is available between Bontang and nearby islands such as Sebatik and Tarakan.

How to Get Around in Bontang

Bontang is a relatively small city, and it’s easy to get around using public transportation, such as taxis or minivans. However, renting a car or motorbike may offer more flexibility and ease of travel.

Where to Go Next from Bontang: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

There are several destinations that are easily accessible from Bontang and offer additional opportunities for adventure and exploration:

  • Samarinda, the capital city of East Kalimantan, is known for its authentic Dayak culture and vibrant culinary scene.
  • Derawan Islands, an archipelago that’s home to pristine beaches and some of the best snorkeling and diving spots in Indonesia.
  • Mahakam River, the longest river in East Kalimantan, is a scenic and culturally significant destination.

Conclusion

Bontang is a hidden gem in East Kalimantan, offering visitors a unique blend of natural beauty, culture, and adventure. This travel guide outlines the top places to see, things to do, and tips for a safe and enjoyable trip to Bontang. Whether you’re interested in history, culture, or outdoor activities, there’s something for everyone in this charming city.
