Introduction to Balikpapan

Balikpapan, a city located in East Kalimantan, Indonesia, is the gateway to a myriad of stunning natural attractions that are waiting to be explored. Its rich history, warm culture, and friendly locals make Balikpapan a tourist destination that attracts people from all parts of the world. Plan a trip to Balikpapan, and you’ll get to hike in pristine rainforests, bask in the sun on beautiful beaches, and soak in the local culture.

List of Places to Visit in Balikpapan

Plan your itinerary with these must-see attractions in Balikpapan.

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  1. Balikpapan Museum
  2. Japan-Balikpapan War Museum
  3. Monument Perjuangan Rakyat (People’s Struggle Monument)

Natural Attractions of Balikpapan: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Best Things to Do in Balikpapan: Activities and Tours

Week-Long Itinerary for Balikpapan

Day 1: Explore the Bukit Bangkirai (Bangkirai Hill) Nature Park Day 2: Go for a city tour and visit the Japanese-Balikpapan War Museum, Balikpapan Oil Museum, and Monument Perjuangan Rakyat (People’s Struggle Monument) Day 3: Visit the Balikpapan Mangrove Tourist Park Day 4: Explore the Samboja Lestari Day 5: Take a Traditional Dayak Village tour Day 6: Visit the Sungai Wain Protection Forest Day 7: Relax at the beach and enjoy some local food.

Local Culture and Customs of Balikpapan

  1. Dress modestly when visiting temples or other cultural attractions.
  2. Always ask for permission before taking a picture of locals.
  3. Use your right hand for giving and receiving things.

Best Time to Visit Balikpapan

The best time to visit Balikpapan would be between May and November when the weather is dry and sunny.

Do’s and Don’ts of Balikpapan

Do:

  1. Respect the local culture and customs, such as taking off your shoes before entering a temple or other cultural attractions.
  2. Cover yourself appropriately when visiting religious sites.
  3. Take plenty of water when you go hiking or trekking.

Don’t:

  1. Damage or litter in nature reserves or sacred sites.
  2. Wear shoes inside someone’s home.
  3. Touch someone’s head, as it’s considered rude in the local culture.

Safety and Health

Safety in Balikpapan

Balikpapan is generally a safe city for tourists. However, be aware of pickpockets and be cautious when using public transportation.

Health in Balikpapan

Some of the health concerns when travelling to Balikpapan include malaria and dengue fever. Make sure to consult your doctor about preventive measures before travelling.

Getting to Balikpapan: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Balikpapan

The nearest airport is Balikpapan International Airport.

Bus Stations Near Balikpapan

The Balikpapan bus station has town and long-distance buses.

Other Options

You can also take a taxi or rent a car to get to Balikpapan.

How to Get Around in Balikpapan

The best way to get around Balikpapan is by car, as public transportation is limited. Taxis and ride-hailing apps are readily available for comfortable and safe travel.

Where to Go Next from Balikpapan: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Here are a few nearby destinations and excursions to consider:

  1. Derawan Islands

  3. Kutai National Park

Conclusion

Balikpapan is beautiful and must-visit tourist destination offering a glimpse of Indonesia’s rich culture and biodiversity. The city boasts natural wonders and historical landmarks that cater to all types of travelers. Ensure you plan a trip to this city and get to experience the magic it offers.
