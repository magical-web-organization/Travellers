Introduction to Kalimantan

Kalimantan is the Indonesian part of the island of Borneo, which is the third-largest island in the world. It is located in Southeast Asia and shares borders with Malaysia and Brunei. Kalimantan comprises four provinces, including East Kalimantan, South Kalimantan, Central Kalimantan, and West Kalimantan. The island is known for its verdant jungle forests, diverse wildlife, and unique cultural heritage. In this comprehensive guide, we will explore the best places to visit, things to do, and local culture and customs of Kalimantan.

List of Places to Visit in Kalimantan

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  1. Museum Mulawarman (Samarinda, East Kalimantan)
  2. Palace of the Sultan of Kutai Kartanegara (Tenggarong, East Kalimantan)
  3. Dayak Longhouses (among the tribes in West and Central Kalimantan)
  4. Benuaq Heritage Museum (Sangatta, East Kalimantan)
  5. Sanggam Museum (Palangka Raya, Central Kalimantan)

Natural Attractions of Kalimantan: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  1. Tanjung Puting National Park (Central Kalimantan)
  2. Derawan Islands (East Kalimantan)
  3. Mahakam River (East Kalimantan)
  4. Mount Meratus (South Kalimantan)
  5. Bukit Raya National Park (Central Kalimantan)

Hiking/Biking Routes of Kalimantan

  1. Mount Kinabalu (East Kalimantan)
  2. Mount Merabu (Central Kalimantan)
  3. Mahakam River Trek (East Kalimantan)
  4. Cempaka Diamond Mines (South Kalimantan)
  5. Bukit Raya Trek (Central Kalimantan)

Best Things to Do in Kalimantan: Activities and Tours

  1. Jungle trekking and wildlife spotting in Tanjung Puting National Park.
  2. Exploring the underwater world in Derawan Islands.
  3. Visiting the traditional Dayak longhouses in West and Central Kalimantan.
  4. Exploring the diamond mines in Cempaka, South Kalimantan.
  5. River cruising on the Mahakam River, East Kalimantan.

Day 3 Travel to Sangatta and explore Benuaq Heritage Museum.

Local Culture and Customs of Kalimantan

Kalimantan is home to various Dayak ethnic groups who follow their traditional lifestyle and beliefs, which include animism and shamanism. Visitors can witness traditional ceremonies, such as the Gawai Dayak (Harvest Festival) and Adat Dangdut (Traditional Dance) in Central Kalimantan. It is important to respect local customs and traditions when visiting Kalimantan.

Best Time to Visit Kalimantan

The best time to visit Kalimantan is during the dry season, which is from May to September. This is the best time to explore the outdoor natural attractions and hike the mountain trails. The wet season, from October to April, can be challenging due to heavy rainfall and flooding in some areas.

Do’s and Don’ts of Kalimantan

Do:

  1. Respect local customs and traditions.
  2. Learn some basic Indonesian language.
  3. Keep an open mind and be adventurous.
  4. Wear appropriate clothing in the religious and cultural sites.
  5. Use mosquito repellent when exploring the jungle.

Don’t:

  1. Touch the head of a local person.
  2. Point fingers or feet at people or holy things.
  3. Wear revealing clothing in public places.
  4. Litter in the natural and cultural sites.
  5. Disrespect people’s religion or beliefs.

Safety and Health

Safety in Kalimantan

Kalimantan is generally a safe place to visit, but visitors should pay attention to their surroundings and take typical precautions. There have been reports of petty crimes in some urban areas, so visitors should exercise caution when carrying valuables. It is also advisable to use a local guide when exploring the countryside.

Health in Kalimantan

Visitors are advised to take precautions against mosquito-borne diseases, such as malaria and dengue fever, by using mosquito repellent and wearing protective clothing. It is also advisable to drink bottled water and avoid consuming raw food in the rural areas of Kalimantan.

  3. Landasan Ulin Bus Station (South Kalimantan)

Other Options

Visitors can take the ferry from Malaysia or Brunei to Kalimantan’s ports. There are also river cruise options for exploring the Mahakam River in East Kalimantan.

How to Get Around in Kalimantan

Transportation options in Kalimantan include car rentals, taxis, buses, and boats. Visitors can use local guides and tours to explore the more remote areas. It is also possible to explore the natural attractions on foot or by bicycle.

Conclusion

Kalimantan is a hidden gem in Southeast Asia, offering natural wonders, cultural heritage, and unique experiences. With its diverse flora and fauna, traditional villages, and natural outdoor adventures, Kalimantan is an excellent destination for any traveler looking for a unique and exciting experience. This comprehensive guide to Kalimantan will help you plan your visit and make the most of your time on the island.
