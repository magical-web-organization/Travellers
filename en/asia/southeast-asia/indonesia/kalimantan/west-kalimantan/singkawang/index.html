Singkawang is a city located in the province of West Kalimantan, Indonesia. It is known for its ethnic Chinese population and its rich cultural heritage. Visitors to Singkawang can experience a mix of Chinese and Indonesian culture, taste local delicacies, and explore scenic natural attractions. In this guide, we will provide you with everything you need to know to plan your visit to Singkawang.

Introduction to Singkawang

Singkawang, also known as the “City of a Thousand Temples,” has a population of approximately 220,000 people and is located on the west coast of Borneo Island. The city is known for its vibrant Chinese architecture and culture, and it is an important port for local trade. Visitors to Singkawang will find a wide variety of attractions, including museums, temples, parks, and markets.

List of Places to Visit in Singkawang

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Singkawang is known for its rich cultural heritage, and visitors can explore several museums and historical places throughout the city.

  • Lembah Hijau Nature Tourism Park: This park is located on the outskirts of Singkawang and is a popular spot for hiking, birdwatching, and picnics.
  • Vihara Dewi Kwan Im: This Chinese temple is one of the most popular attractions in Singkawang and is dedicated to the goddess of mercy, Kwan Im.
  • Jembatan Pasir Panjang: This bridge is an icon of Singkawang and connects the city to the nearby islands of Borneo.
  • Sekolah Tionghoa Khe Hian: This is one of the oldest Chinese schools in West Kalimantan, and it houses a museum dedicated to the history of Chinese education in Indonesia.

Natural Attractions of Singkawang: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Singkawang is home to several scenic natural attractions that visitors won’t want to miss.

  • Taman Wisata Alam Bukit Batu: This nature reserve offers breathtaking views of the city, and visitors can hike through the dense jungle to reach its peak.
  • Danau Teratai: This lotus pond is located in the center of Singkawang and is a popular spot for picnics and photoshoots.
  • Pantai Pasir Panjang: This beach is the perfect spot for swimming and sunbathing, and it offers stunning views of the sunset over the ocean.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Singkawang

Singkawang is surrounded by lush jungle and natural beauty, making it a great destination for hiking and biking enthusiasts.

  • Bukit Batu Hiking Trail: This trail leads visitors through the dense jungle to the peak of Bukit Batu, where they can enjoy panoramic views of Singkawang.
  • Danau Teratai Hiking Trail: This trail takes visitors around the beautiful lotus pond of Danau Teratai.
  • Bicycle Tour of Singkawang: Visitors can rent a bike and explore the city on two wheels, stopping at various attractions and scenic spots along the way.

Best Things to Do in Singkawang: Activities and Tours

Singkawang offers a range of activities and tours for visitors to enjoy.

  • Chinese Cultural Tour: This tour takes visitors through the heart of Singkawang’s Chinese culture, visiting temples, markets, and museums.
  • River Cruise: Visitors can take a cruise down the Kapuas River, the longest river in Indonesia, and enjoy a scenic view of the city and the surrounding jungle.
  • Shopping at Pasar Turi: This traditional market is located in the heart of Singkawang and offers a variety of souvenirs, clothing, and local delicacies.

Week-Long Itinerary for Singkawang

Day 1: Arrival and city tour, including Vihara Dewi Kwan Im and Pantai Pasir Panjang Day 2: Visit to Taman Wisata Alam Bukit Batu Day 3: Bicycle tour of Singkawang Day 4: Chinese cultural tour, including Sekolah Tionghoa Khe Hian and Pasar Turi Day 5: River cruise and sunset at Danau Teratai Day 6: Hike at Lembah Hijau Nature Tourism Park Day 7: Departure

Food and Drink Scene of Singkawang

Must-Try Dishes

Singkawang is known for its unique blend of Chinese and Indonesian cuisine, and visitors can enjoy a variety of delicious dishes.

  • Bakmi Singkawang: This is a type of noodle soup that is typically served with chicken or pork and vegetables.
  • Bubur Pedas: This spicy porridge is made with chicken or seafood and spices.
  • Kue Semprong: This popular Indonesian snack is made with crispy, thin wafers.

Local Beverages

  • Kopi Singkawang: This strong coffee is popular in Singkawang and is typically served with condensed milk and sugar.
  • Tuak: This local drink is made from the sap of the palm tree and is fermented to create an alcoholic beverage.

Where to Stay in Singkawang: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Singkawang offers a variety of accommodations to suit every budget and style.

  • Singkawang Grand Hotel: This hotel is located in the heart of Singkawang and offers modern amenities and comfortable rooms.
  • Tiong Lay Hotel: This hotel is located near the Pasir Panjang Beach and offers stunning views of the ocean.
  • Homestay Teck Huat: This guesthouse is located in a traditional Chinese house and offers an authentic Singkawang experience.

What to Buy in Singkawang: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Visitors to Singkawang can find a variety of souvenirs and local products to take home.

  • Kerupuk Palembang: This crispy snack is made from fish and is a popular local delicacy.
  • Sarung Tenun: This traditional Indonesian sarong is handwoven and comes in a variety of colors and patterns.
  • Bakso Kalimantan: This spicy meatball soup is a popular local dish that can be taken home in a dry mix form.

Local Culture and Customs of Singkawang

Singkawang is known for its unique blend of Chinese and Indonesian culture, and visitors should be aware of local customs and traditions.

  • Respect for elders: Respect for elders is an important part of Chinese culture, and visitors should show deference to older people.
  • Temples and religious sites: Visitors should dress modestly and remove their shoes before entering temples and religious sites.
  • Language: The most commonly spoken languages in Singkawang are Indonesian and Chinese.

Best Time to Visit Singkawang

The best time to visit Singkawang is during the dry season, which runs from April to October. The weather is sunny and pleasant, making it the perfect time to explore the city’s outdoor attractions.

Do’s and Don’ts of Singkawang

Do:

  • Do try local delicacies like Bakmi Singkawang and Bubur Pedas.
  • Do respect local customs and traditions.
  • Do visit the city’s many temples and religious sites.

Don’t:

  • Don’t litter or damage natural attractions.
  • Don’t wear revealing clothing when visiting religious sites.
  • Don’t argue with locals or use disrespectful language.

Safety and Health

Safety in Singkawang

Singkawang is a generally safe city to visit, but visitors should take precautions to avoid pickpockets and petty theft.

Health in Singkawang

Visitors should take precautions to avoid mosquito bites, as there is a risk of dengue fever and malaria in Singkawang. Visitors should also avoid drinking tap water and only consume bottled water.

Getting to Singkawang: Transportation Options and Logistics

Singkawang is accessible by air, land, and sea.

Airports Near Singkawang

The nearest airport to Singkawang is the Supadio International Airport in Pontianak, approximately 150 kilometers away.

Bus Stations Near Singkawang

There are several bus stations in nearby cities that offer transportation to Singkawang, including the Pontianak Bus Station and the Sintang Bus Station.

Other Options

Visitors can also take a ferry from nearby cities like Pontianak and Jakarta.

How to Get Around in Singkawang

The best way to get around Singkawang is by renting a motorbike, taxi, or minivan. Visitors can also explore the city on foot or by bicycle.

Where to Go Next from Singkawang: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Pontianak: This city is the capital of West Kalimantan and offers a variety of attractions, including museums, temples, and markets.
  • Gunung Palung National Park: This expansive national park is located approximately 80 kilometers from Singkawang and offers hiking, birdwatching, and wildlife watching opportunities.

Conclusion

Singkawang is a city rich in culture and natural beauty, offering visitors a unique blend of Chinese and Indonesian traditions. Whether you’re looking for outdoor adventures, cultural experiences, or delicious food, Singkawang has something for everyone. Plan your trip today and experience the beauty and charm of this vibrant city.
