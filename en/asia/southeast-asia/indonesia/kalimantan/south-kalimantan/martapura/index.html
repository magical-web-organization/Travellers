Introduction to Martapura

Martapura, a town in South Kalimantan, Indonesia, is known for its rich cultural heritage and natural attractions. Located on the banks of the Martapura River, the town is famous for its traditional diamond mining and handicraft industry. Martapura is home to a number of temples, museums, and other cultural landmarks that offer a glimpse into the region’s fascinating history.

List of Places to Visit in Martapura

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Martapura has a number of museums and historical sites that provide a glimpse into the region’s rich cultural heritage. The most important of these include:

  • Taman Purbakala Keraton - A park and museum complex that showcases the history of South Kalimantan’s royal families. The park features intricately designed statues, traditional buildings, and lush gardens.
  • Museum Lambung Mangkurat - A museum that highlights the history and culture of South Kalimantan. The museum features exhibits on traditional arts and crafts, local wildlife, and the region’s diamond-mining industry.
  • Siring Banjarmasin - A historic site that dates back to the 17th century. The site features the Siring Palace, which was once the residence of the sultan of Banjar, and a number of temples and shrines.

Natural Attractions of Martapura: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Martapura is surrounded by breathtaking natural scenery, offering visitors a range of outdoor activities. The most popular natural attractions in the area include:

  • Bukit Raya - A mountain located on the border of Kalimantan and East Kalimantan. At 2,278 meters tall, it is the highest peak in Kalimantan and offers stunning trekking and hiking opportunities.
  • Sungai Bakar - A pristine river known for its clear waters and scenic views. The river is ideal for those who enjoy swimming, fishing, and rafting.
  • Pulau Kaget - A small island located in the Martapura River. The island boasts beautiful white sandy beaches, crystal clear waters, and is an excellent spot for snorkeling and water sports.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Martapura

Martapura is a hiker’s paradise with an abundance of trails that showcase the natural beauty of the region. Some of the best hiking and biking routes in Martapura include:

  • Gunung Besar - A popular hiking trail that leads to the top of a 600-meter-tall mountain. The route offers picturesque views of the surrounding landscape and is well-suited for novice hikers.
  • Barito Trail - A scenic route that runs along the banks of the Barito River. The trail is ideal for cycling and offers stunning views of the river and surrounding countryside.
  • Bukit Raya Trek - An arduous trek that requires physical fitness, stamina, and a good pair of hiking boots. The trail passes through dense forests, steep ridges, and rocky terrain, culminating in a spectacular view from the mountain’s summit.

Best Things to Do in Martapura: Activities and Tours

Martapura offers visitors a range of activities and tours that cater to different interests:

  • Diamond Mine Tour - A visit to the traditional diamond mine in Martapura is a must. Visitors can witness the diamond-mining process and learn about the history and culture of diamond mining in the region.
  • River Cruise - Take a leisurely boat ride along the Martapura River and savour the stunning views of the town and the surrounding natural beauty.
  • Cultural Tours - Experience the rich cultural heritage of the region with a visit to the traditional villages, temples and local markets.

Food and Drink Scene of Martapura

Must-Try Dishes

Martapura is known for its delicious traditional cuisine, which is characterized by a blend of spicy, sweet, and sour flavors. Some of the must-try dishes include:

  • Soto Banjar - A hearty soup made with beef, coconut milk, and spices.
  • Pempek Palembang - A popular fish cake dish that is served with a spicy vinegar sauce.
  • Bakso - A popular Indonesian meatball soup dish.

Local Beverages

Martapura is home to a range of refreshing drinks, including:

  • Bir Pletok - A sweet and spicy traditional drink made from ginger, lemongrass, and palm sugar.
  • Es Kelapa Muda - A refreshing drink made with young coconut and ice.
  • Teh Talua - A tea made from tea leaves, egg yolk, and sugar.

Where to Stay in Martapura: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Martapura has a range of accommodation options, from budget-friendly guesthouses to luxurious hotels. Some of the most recommended options include:

  • Swiss-Belhotel - A luxurious hotel that boasts a range of amenities, including an outdoor pool, spa, and fitness center.
  • City Hotel Martapura - A mid-range hotel that is known for its comfortable rooms and excellent customer service.
  • Guesthouse Damai - A budget-friendly guesthouse that is ideal for travelers who are looking for a comfortable and affordable place to stay.

What to Buy in Martapura: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Martapura is famous for its diamond mining and handicraft industry. Some of the popular souvenirs and local products that visitors can bring home include:

  • Diamonds - Martapura is known for producing high-quality diamonds, and visitors can purchase these precious stones at a range of local shops.
  • Handicrafts - Visitors can purchase a range of local handicrafts, including batik textiles, woodcarvings, and traditional ceramics.
  • Snacks - Visitors can take home a range of traditional snacks and sweets, including Martapura’s famous dodol sweets and kueh lapis.

Local Culture and Customs of Martapura

Martapura has a rich cultural heritage that is reflected in its traditional art, music, and dance performances. Visitors are encouraged to respect the local customs and traditions, including dressing modestly when visiting temples and other religious sites, taking off shoes before entering homes, and asking for permission before taking photographs of locals.

Best Time to Visit Martapura

The best time to visit Martapura is during the dry season, which runs from May to September. During this time, the weather is dry and sunny, making it ideal for outdoor activities. The wet season, from October to April, brings heavy rains and high humidity, which can make visiting the natural attractions more challenging.

Do’s and Don’ts of Martapura

Do:

  • Respect the local customs and traditions.
  • Dress modestly when visiting religious sites.
  • Try the local cuisine and beverages.
  • Learn a few basic phrases in Bahasa Indonesia.

Don’t:

  • Disrespect the local customs and traditions.
  • Wear revealing clothing when visiting religious sites.
  • Hike or trek alone in remote areas.
  • Carry large amounts of cash or valuable items in public places.

Safety and Health

Safety in Martapura

Martapura is generally safe for visitors, but it is always advisable to take precautions to avoid theft and other petty crimes. Visitors are encouraged to carry a photocopy of their passport and leave the original copy in a safe place. It is also advisable to use reputable tour operators when embarking on outdoor activities.

Health in Martapura

Martapura is a relatively low-risk area when it comes to health concerns, but visitors are advised to take standard health precautions. Ensure that vaccinations are up to date, drink only bottled water, and avoid eating foods from street vendors unless they are cooked thoroughly.

Getting to Martapura: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Martapura

The nearest airport to Martapura is Syamsudin Noor International Airport, located in Banjarmasin, which is about 45 minutes away by car. The airport is served by several domestic airlines, including Garuda Indonesia and Lion Air.

Bus Stations Near Martapura

Martapura is well connected by road, and visitors can take a bus from Banjarmasin’s main bus station to Martapura. The journey takes around an hour, and buses run regularly throughout the day.

Other Options

Visitors can also travel to Martapura by car or motorcycle, which is the most convenient way to explore the region at their own pace. Car rental services are available at Syamsudin Noor International Airport, making it easy for visitors to get around.

How to Get Around in Martapura

Martapura is a small town, and most of the attractions are within walking distance. Visitors can also use local taxis or rent a car or motorcycle to get around. Motorbikes are the most common mode of transportation in the town, and visitors can hire them at affordable rates from local shops.

Where to Go Next from Martapura: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Martapura is surrounded by several other towns and cities that are worth a visit. Some of the nearby destinations include:

  • Banjarmasin - A bustling city known for its floating market and historic landmarks.
  • Pulau Laut Kepulauan - A group of islands known for its beautiful beaches and crystal-clear waters.
  • Tanah Laut - A small regency that is home to a number of traditional villages and natural attractions.

Conclusion

Martapura offers visitors a unique blend of cultural and natural attractions that are sure to leave lasting memories. From exploring the traditional diamond-mining industry to hiking up Bukit Raya, visitors can experience the very best of Indonesia’s cultural and natural diversity. So, pack your bags and get ready to embark on a journey of a lifetime in Martapura, Indonesia.
