Introduction to Amuntai

Amuntai is a town located in South Kalimantan province in Indonesia. Surrounded by lush rainforests, waterfalls, and rivers, Amuntai offers an ideal destination for outdoor enthusiasts keen on exploring the beauty of Indonesia.

List of Places to Visit in Amuntai

Whether you are a culture buff, a nature lover, or an adventurous spirit, Amuntai offers an array of options for every traveler. Here is a list of places to visit while in Amuntai:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Museum Lambung Mangkurat: A museum that showcases the rich history of South Kalimantan through a collection of traditional handicrafts, sculptures, and historical artifacts.

  • Raden Saleh Mosque: A historical mosque renowned for its intricate carvings, stained glass window panels, and Arabic calligraphy.

  • Istana Amuntai: A former palace that showcases the traditional architecture of South Kalimantan.

Natural Attractions of Amuntai: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Riam Durian Waterfall: A stunning waterfall located in the deep rainforest of Gunung Mangkurat that offers a perfect spot for swimming, hiking, and picnicking.

  • Gunung Mangkurat: A nature reserve that covers an area of 424,000 hectares and offers an abundance of wildlife, including orangutans, gibbons, and hornbills.

  • Riam Kuning Lake: A picturesque lake surrounded by rainforest that offers picnic spots, fishing, and bird watching.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Amuntai

Amuntai offers a range of trekking and biking routes that lead through the scenic landscape of the town. Some of the popular routes include:

  • Gunung Mangkurat Trek: A two-day trek that takes you through a lush rainforest and offers stunning views of Amuntai.

  • Batapang Biking Trail: A four-hour biking trail that takes you through local villages and rice paddies.

Best Things to Do in Amuntai: Activities and Tours

  • River Rafting: Paddle down the rapids of the Barito River and experience the thrill of white water rafting.

  • Village Tour: Explore the local culture and customs of Amuntai by visiting nearby villages, participating in traditional dances and trying local delicacies.

Week-Long Itinerary for Amuntai

Day 1: Arrive at Amuntai and explore the town center

Day 2-3: Trek to Gunung Mangkurat and explore the nature reserve

Day 4: Take a village tour and try local cuisine

Day 5: Experience the thrill of rafting on Barito River

Day 6: Visit Riam Kuning Lake and enjoy a picnic by the water

Day 7: Departure from Amuntai

Food and Drink Scene of Amuntai

Must-Try Dishes

  • Soto Banjar: A traditional soup made with chicken or beef, rice noodles, and a spicy broth.

  • Pempek Palembang: A deep-fried fish cake made with tapioca flour and served with sweet and sour sauce.

Local Beverages

  • Kandangan Coffee: A sweet and smooth coffee served with condensed milk.

Where to Stay in Amuntai: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Hotel Barito: A luxury hotel with modern amenities and a swimming pool.

  • Homestay Danaraden: A cozy guesthouse with shared facilities run by a friendly local family.

What to Buy in Amuntai: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Handicrafts: Local artists create intricate wooden carvings, textiles, and pottery that make for unique souvenirs.

Local Culture and Customs of Amuntai

  • Dress modestly when visiting mosques and other religious places.

  • Always remove your shoes before entering a local’s house or a mosque.

Best Time to Visit Amuntai

The best time to visit Amuntai is between May and October when the weather is dry and comfortable, making it ideal for outdoor adventures.

Do’s and Don’ts of Amuntai

Do:

  • Respect the local culture and customs.

  • Ask for permission before taking photographs of locals.

Don’t:

  • Drink tap water.

  • Wear revealing clothing in public places.

Safety and Health

Safety in Amuntai

  • Avoid walking alone at night.

  • Beware of pickpockets and street crime in busy tourist areas.

Health in Amuntai

  • Make sure to get all necessary vaccinations before traveling to Indonesia.

  • Drink bottled water and avoid eating street food to prevent food-borne illnesses.

Getting to Amuntai: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Amuntai

The nearest airport to Amuntai is Syamsudin Noor International Airport, located in Banjarmasin, which is about 3 hours’ drive from Amuntai.

Bus Stations Near Amuntai

Amuntai Bus Station is located in the town center and offers regular bus connections to cities across South Kalimantan.

Other Options

Taxis and private transfers are available from nearby cities to Amuntai.

How to Get Around in Amuntai

The best way to get around Amuntai is by renting a motorbike or car. Shared taxis and motorbikes are also available for shorter distances.

Where to Go Next from Amuntai: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Banjarmasin: A bustling city that offers insight into traditional life in South Kalimantan.

  • Tanjung Puting National Park: A nature reserve that is home to orangutans, gibbons, and other endemic wildlife.

Conclusion

Amuntai offers an unforgettable travel experience with its fascinating culture and abundant natural beauty. With this comprehensive guide, you can plan your travel itinerary and make the most of your time in this beautiful town.
