Introduction to Kandangan

Kandangan is a small town located in South Kalimantan, one of the provinces on the island of Borneo in Indonesia. As a prime example of rural Indonesian life, Kandangan has many hidden gems that travellers should discover. A town that’s brimming with history, culture and unique experiences, Kandangan is a must-visit destination for anyone visiting the Kalimantan region.

List of Places to Visit in Kandangan

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

One of the must-visit sites in Kandangan is the “Taman Makam Pahlawan Nasional Sultan Suriansyah,” a national cemetery complex of the Sultan Suriansyah in Banjarmasin. It is the burial site of some warriors whose names are etched in South Kalimantan’s history. Visitors can learn not only about the history of South Kalimantan, but can also enjoy scenic riverside views.

In addition to the sultan’s national cemetery, the Banua Niha Keriso Church is the oldest Christian church in South Borneo, located in Talawang. For those interested in exploring the traditional houses of South Kalimantan, the Dayak traditional village of Kayuh Baimbai, which is about an hour drive from Kandangan city centre, is a great experience. The village showcases the Dayak elders’ longhouses as well as their culture.

Natural Attractions of Kandangan: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Another must-visit natural attraction is the waterfall of Loksado located approximately an hour away from Kandangan. Travellers could enjoy the safari experience and bamboo rafting in Loksado. The Meratus mountain range and rainforests around Loksado offer plenty of opportunities for hiking, trekking, and adventures through challenging terrains that are suitable for any level of experience.

The Paring Rata eco-tourism site located in the Banjar Regency is another natural attraction that should not be missed. Visitors can experience a unique Indonesian landscape in the comfort of bamboo huts. The resort offers exotic fruit tasting, farming activities, and tours to the Banjarese village, a fascinating perspective into locals’ daily livelihoods.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Kandangan

Fans of biking or hiking can find trails that exceed their expectations in Kandangan. One of the most popular hikes is Gunung Pingai, which is suitable for amateur hikers and outdoor enthusiasts alike. Gunung Pingai features a trail through forests and steep terrains and offers an excellent view of the town centre and beautiful sunset views.

Cycling enthusiasts will enjoy the ride from Banjarmasin to Kandangan through the Road of 1000 Bridges. The route is surrounded by the lush greenery of rice paddies and acres of palm groves, and it’s a fantastic way to get to know the South Kalimantan region’s countryside better.

Best Things to Do in Kandangan: Activities and Tours

Bamboo Rafting at Loksado and Betung Bungalow, which offers stunning views of the safe river and rainforest freshness, is a must-do activity. For anyone looking for a culinary experience, join the cooking class in Banar Cocoon and experience local cuisine or explore the local markets and try some exotic street foods at Jalan Hardjodjokarto.

Week-Long Itinerary for Kandangan

Here’s an itinerary for a week exploring Kandangan and nearby towns:

  • Day 1: Arrive in Banjarmasin. Head for Talawang and visit the Banua Niha Keriso Church. Arrive in Kandangan and prepare for the next few days.

  • Day 2: Start by hiking to Gunung Pingai and then relax at Mandiangin Waterfall.

  • Day 3: Travel to Loksado and take an unforgettable bamboo rafting experience

  • Day 4: Head to Paring Rata Eco Village and witness the unique Indonesian landscape.

  • Day 5: Take a day trip to Kayuh Dayak Traditional House and explore the world of Bornean tribes.

  • Day 6: Return to Banjarmasin and sample the city’s street food or try the culinary experience at Banar Cocoon.

  • Day 7: End the trip with a visit to Taman Makam Pahlawan Nasional Sultan Suriansyah, then relax on a Banjarmasin’s tour while enjoying the city’s famous floating market.

Food and Drink Scene of Kandangan

Must-Try Dishes

One of the dishes that travellers should try in South Kalimantan is Soto Banjar, which is a traditional soup dish made of chicken or beef and noodles topped with slices of tomato, potato, bean sprouts, and other vegetables. Another authentic dish to try is the spicy Ayam Tangkap, which is crunchy chicken cooked with a mix of spicy herbs and serves with lime juice.

Local Beverages

For drinks, the “Kelapa Muda,” which is a young coconut drink, is refreshing and perfect for hot days, and it’s available in nearly all places all across the town. While Kopi Banjar, a traditional coffee made with palm sugar and using unique brewing techniques, is worth a try.

Where to Stay in Kandangan: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Kandangan has several affordable accommodation options, including hotels, guesthouses, and homestays. Betung Bungalow in Loksado is an excellent option for those looking for a more luxury experience. Meanwhile, Wisma Dayak, located in Kandangan city centre, offers homely rooms and is budget-friendly.

What to Buy in Kandangan: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Travellers looking for local souvenirs and products could shop for Kandangan handicraft items, such as woven bags, traditional hats, or wicker furniture. Batik shirts, scarves, and dresses are also excellent local products to bring home, showcasing the intricate patterns commonly found in souvenir stalls and boutiques.

Local Culture and Customs of Kandangan

When travelling in Kandangan, it’s essential to respect the local culture and customs. Visitors should always dress respectfully, following the local customs’ norms, and follow the local people’s lead. Travellers should also avoid discussing sensitive subjects such as religion, politics or gender in public spaces.

Best Time to Visit Kandangan

The best time to visit Kandangan is from May to September, during the dry season. The weather is mild, making it easier for outdoor activities like hiking, biking or trekking. However, visitors are advised to check weather forecasts before travelling as sudden rainfalls can occur at any time.

Do’s and Don’ts of Kandangan

Do:

  • Dress modestly and follow the local customs’ norms.
  • Try local cuisine like Soto Banjar and Ayam Tangkap.
  • Learn about the local Dayak tribes, their culture and traditions.
  • Bring mosquito repellent and sun protection.

Don’t:

  • Discuss sensitive subjects like religion or politics in public spaces.
  • Smoke in public places or near religious institutions.
  • Take any natural resources such as plants or animals from the area.

Safety and Health

Safety in Kandangan

Kandangan is generally a safe place to visit, but travellers should always take basic precautions such as keeping a copy of their passport, keeping their valuable items in a safe place, and avoiding isolated areas at night.

Health in Kandangan

Visitors are advised to get vaccinated against Hepatitis A and B before travelling. In addition, visitors should drink bottled water and avoid eating foods from shady street vendors to prevent illnesses such as diarrhea.

Getting to Kandangan: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Kandangan

The nearest airport for travellers visiting Kandangan is the Syamsudin Noor airport, located in Banjarmasin, approximately 102 km away.

Bus Stations Near Kandangan

Travellers can take a bus from Banjarmasin bus station to Kandangan, which takes approximately three hours.

Other Options

Taxis and car rental services are available from Banjarmasin to Kandangan if you prefer a more private mode of transportation.

How to Get Around in Kandangan

Travellers can easily navigate Kandangan’s town centre and nearby attractions by motorbike or local public transport. Bicycle and car rental services are also readily available for visitors looking for a more comfortable and hassle-free travel experience.

Where to Go Next from Kandangan: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Travellers visiting Kandangan should explore other fascinating destinations in the South Kalimantan province like the Banjarmasin floating market or the Diamond Karst of Banjarbaru before leaving the area. Dayak Meratus and Pulau Kaget Island are also worth visiting islands near Kandangan.

Conclusion

Kandangan is a hidden gem in South Kalimantan, Borneo. Offering a blend of stunning natural beauty and rich cultural heritage, it is an ideal destination for travellers looking to enjoy unique experiences in Indonesia. Whether hiking to Gunung Pingai, bamboo rafting in Loksado or exploring traditional houses in Kayuh Baimbai, visitors will discover endless possibilities for adventure, relaxation, and more in this charming town.
