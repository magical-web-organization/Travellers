Introduction to Banjarbaru

Banjarbaru is a city located in South Kalimantan, Indonesia. Known for its tropical rainforests and numerous tourist attractions, Banjarbaru has become a popular tourist destination over the years. In this comprehensive travel guide to Banjarbaru, we will dive into the city’s top attractions, food and drink scene, local culture, safety and health, transportation options, and much more.

List of Places to Visit in Banjarbaru

Banjarbaru boasts a wide range of attractions that are perfect for travelers of all ages and interests. Whether you’re interested in exploring the city’s cultural history or getting up close and personal with nature, Banjarbaru has something for everyone. Here are some of the top attractions that you should not miss:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Sabilal Muhtadin Grand Mosque: This is one of the largest mosques in Indonesia and a must-visit attraction in Banjarbaru. Located in the center of the city, this mosque has a beautiful interior and exterior design that represents the culture and architectural style of South Kalimantan.
  • Lambung Mangkurat Museum: This museum showcases the traditional way of life and cultural heritage of Banjarbaru and the wider South Kalimantan region. Here, you’ll find various exhibits that display the history, cultural practices, and artworks of the Banjar and Dayak tribes.
  • Kampung Sasirangan: This village is known for its unique textile art, called sasirangan. Here, you’ll witness the making process of this beautiful textile art, learn about its history, and even try it yourself.

Natural Attractions of Banjarbaru: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Kebun Raya Banua: This botanical garden is a great destination for nature lovers. With over 300 types of tropical plants and flowers, you can spend hours exploring this beautiful garden.
  • Riam Kanan Dam: This dam is a great destination for photography enthusiasts. The sight of water cascading through the dam is quite a sight, and you can also get up close to the river to enjoy the peaceful atmosphere.
  • Tambak Besar River Cruise: Take a boat tour down the river and enjoy the stunning views of Banjarbaru’s natural surroundings. The river and its banks are rich with flora and fauna, and you might even see some monkeys and rare birds.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Banjarbaru

Banjarbaru has some excellent hiking and biking trails that are perfect for trekkers. Here are a few of the top trails:

  • Mount Tangkiling National Park: Located in the north of Banjarbaru, this park has several hiking trails that will take you through the lush tropical rainforest. You might even be able to spot some wildlife, such as deer and macaques.
  • Bukit Batu: This is a popular hiking and trekking destination in Banjarbaru for those who want to enjoy panoramic views of the city. The climb can be challenging but is worth it once you reach the top.
  • Sungai Rangas: This river is perfect for kayaking and rafting. With its fast-flowing current, rapids, and calm stretches, Sungai Rangas is a perfect setting for adventure seekers.

Best Things to Do in Banjarbaru: Activities and Tours

If you’re looking for activities during your stay in Banjarbaru, here are a few of the best things to do:

  • Have a Spa Day: There are several spas in Banjarbaru that offer affordable spa treatments. This is a great way to relax and rejuvenate during your trip.
  • Try the Local Food: Banjarbaru has a reputation for delicious food, and you’ll find that the local food is a blend of Indonesian and Malay influences. Try local dishes such as Soto Banjar, Ketupat Kandangan, or Pempek Palembang.
  • Join a Cultural Tour: Cultural tours are a great way to learn about the local culture of Banjarbaru. You will get to visit traditional villages, explore historical landmarks, and learn about the local way of life.

Food and Drink Scene of Banjarbaru

Banjarbaru has a thriving food and drink scene that combines the flavors of Indonesia and Malaysia. Here are a few must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Soto Banjar: This is a chicken soup dish that is popular in South Kalimantan. It is served with rice, lime, and chili sauce.
  • Ketupat Kandangan: This is a traditional rice cake dish that is steamed and served with chicken, beef, or fish curry.
  • Pempek Palembang: This is a fishcake that is made from ground fish and tapioca flour. It is served with vinegar and chili sauce.

Local Beverages

  • Bir Pletok: This is a herbal drink that is made from ginger, cinnamon, lemongrass, and palm sugar. It has a unique taste and is perfect for cooling down on a hot day.
  • Kacang Ijo: This is a sweet green bean juice that is a popular drink in Banjarbaru. It is served cold and is quite refreshing.

What to Buy in Banjarbaru: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Here are some souvenirs and local products that you can bring home from Banjarbaru:

  • Sasirangan Textiles: These are traditional textiles that are unique to Banjarbaru. They are colorful and have intricate designs.
  • Keripik Banjar: This is a type of crispy snack made from cassava or sweet potatoes. It is a popular snack in Banjarbaru and can be found in most supermarkets and markets.
  • Lontong Kandangan: This is a traditional snack made from rice cakes that are stuffed with savory meat and vegetable fillings.

Local Culture and Customs of Banjarbaru

If you’re interested in learning about local culture and customs in Banjarbaru, here are a few things to keep in mind:

  • Greeting: Indonesians usually greet one another by shaking hands, followed by a slight bow. It is also common to use greetings like “selamat pagi” (good morning) or “selamat sore” (good afternoon).
  • Dress Code: Indonesians are generally conservative in their dress and behavior. It is recommended to dress modestly when visiting religious sites and when interacting with locals.
  • Respect: Indonesians value respect for elders and hierarchy. It is important to show respect to elders and those in positions of authority.

Best Time to Visit Banjarbaru

The best time to visit Banjarbaru is from May to September, which is the dry season. During this time, the weather is generally sunny and pleasant, making it ideal for outdoor activities and exploring the city’s attractions.

Do’s and Don’ts of Banjarbaru

Here are a few do’s and don’ts to keep in mind during your stay in Banjarbaru:

Do:

  • Respect the local culture and customs.
  • Try the local food and drinks.
  • Learn a few basic Indonesian phrases to interact with the locals.

Don’t:

  • Engage in public displays of affection.
  • Insult or belittle the local culture or customs.
  • Carry large sums of money or valuable items.

Safety and Health

Safety in Banjarbaru

Banjarbaru is a safe city, but it is important to take precautions like you would in any other city. Here are a few safety tips to keep in mind:

  • Be aware of your surroundings and stick to well-lit areas at night.
  • Keep your valuable possessions secure and out of sight.
  • Watch your belongings in crowded areas like markets and bus terminals.

Health in Banjarbaru

While there are no specific health concerns in Banjarbaru, it is recommended to take standard precautions like drinking bottled water, using insect repellent, and getting vaccinated before traveling. If you need medical treatment during your stay in Banjarbaru, there are several hospitals and clinics in the city that offer excellent care.

Getting to Banjarbaru: Transportation Options and Logistics

Banjarbaru is accessible by air, land, and water. Here are a few transportation options to consider:

Airports Near Banjarbaru

  • Syamsudin Noor International Airport: This is the nearest airport to Banjarbaru and is located about 30 minutes away from the city center. It offers flights to several major cities in Indonesia.

Bus Stations Near Banjarbaru

  • Banjarbaru Bus Terminal: This bus terminal is located in the center of the city and offers services to several cities in the Kalimantan region and other parts of Indonesia.

Other Options

  • Taxis: Taxis are a popular option for getting around the city, and there are several taxi companies in Banjarbaru.
  • Motorcycle Taxis: Motorcycle taxis, or ojeks, are a cheaper alternative to regular taxis and are accessible throughout the city.

How to Get Around in Banjarbaru

Here are a few options for getting around Banjarbaru:

  • Taxis: Taxis are a convenient way to get around the city, and there are several taxi companies operating in Banjarbaru.
  • Motorcycle Taxis: Ojeks are a cheaper alternative to taxis and are readily available throughout the city.
  • Public Transportation: Public transportation in Banjarbaru mainly consists of buses and minibusses, which are affordable and reliable.

Where to Go Next from Banjarbaru: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

If you’re looking to explore other destinations in the Kalimantan region, here are a few places to consider:

  • Banjarmasin: This is the largest city in South Kalimantan and is known for its floating markets and traditional canals.
  • Martapura: This is a small town known for its traditional diamond and gemstone markets.
  • Loksado: This is a village located in the mountains and is known for its bamboo rafting activities and stunning landscapes.

Conclusion

Banjarbaru is a city that is rich in culture and natural beauty, and it offers a wide range of attractions and activities for visitors. From exploring cultural landmarks to embarking on outdoor adventures, there is always something to do in Banjarbaru.
