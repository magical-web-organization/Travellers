Introduction to Banjarmasin

Banjarmasin is a city located in South Kalimantan, Indonesia. It is known as the “River City” because the city is situated around many waterways and canals. The city is also known for its floating markets that are located on canals. Banjarmasin offers a blend of cultural experiences, outdoor adventures, and natural beauty that attracts both domestic and international travelers.

List of Places to Visit in Banjarmasin

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Museum Negeri Propinsi Kalimantan Selatan, a museum that showcases the cultural diversity and history of South Kalimantan and Banjarmasin.
  • Keraton Banjar, a historical palace that was built in the 17th century and served as the residence of the Banjar Sultanate.
  • Taman Sari Mosque, a prominent mosque that dates back to the 18th century.

Natural Attractions of Banjarmasin: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Lok Baintan Floating Market, one of the largest and most vibrant floating markets in the city that offers a unique cultural experience.
  • Bukit Keluang, a hilltop viewpoint that offers stunning panoramic views of the city and surrounding areas.
  • Monumen Likuang Bumi, a monument that commemorates the historic legend of the seven princesses of South Kalimantan.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Banjarmasin

  • Gunung Batu Gadung, a popular hiking trail that offers scenic views of the city and the surrounding landscape.

Best Things to Do in Banjarmasin: Activities and Tours

  • Take a boat tour of the floating markets and the canals of Banjarmasin
  • Visit the traditional production of diamond carving
  • Attend a cultural performance at the Sasirangan Village

Food and Drink Scene of Banjarmasin

Must-Try Dishes

  • Soto Banjar, a traditional soup made with chicken, vegetables, and coconut milk.
  • Ketupat Kandangan, a local dish of steamed rice cake served with shredded chicken and spicy chilli sauce.
  • Sate Banjar, skewers of grilled meat typically made from beef or chicken.

Local Beverages

  • Teh Kahwa, traditional cinnamon tea served with peanuts
  • Bubur Pedas, a sweet and spicy dessert made with rice and coconut milk

Where to Stay in Banjarmasin: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Hotel Mercure Banjarmasin, a modern and comfortable hotel located in the city center.
  • Amaris Hotel Banjar, a budget-friendly option located near the airport.
  • OYO 507 Rumbia Hotel, a guesthouse offering comfortable amenities at an affordable price.

What to Buy in Banjarmasin: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Sasirangan, traditional handmade fabric with vibrant colors and patterns.
  • Diamond Carving, ornate carvings made from precious stones.
  • Lulut, a traditional Banjar instrument made from bamboo.

Local Culture and Customs of Banjarmasin

  • As a predominantly Muslim area, visitors should be respectful of local customs and dress modestly.
  • It is considered impolite to touch someone’s head or point with your feet.
  • Indonesian is the official language, but Banjarese is also spoken in the area.

Best Time to Visit Banjarmasin

The best time to visit Banjarmasin is during the dry season from April to September when the weather is warm and dry, and there is little rainfall. However, as it is a tropical destination, expect some rain throughout the year.

Do’s and Don’ts of Banjarmasin

Do:

  • Bargain at the floating markets and local shops.
  • Learn some basic Indonesian phrases, such as “terima kasih” (thank you) or “tidak” (no).
  • Try the local cuisine and beverages.

Don’t:

  • Dress inappropriately or offensively.
  • Take photos of people without their permission.
  • Engage in public displays of affection.

Safety and Health

Safety in Banjarmasin

  • Pickpocketing and petty crime can be a problem in crowded areas, so keep valuables in a secure place.
  • Avoid areas where demonstrations or large gatherings are taking place.

Health in Banjarmasin

  • It is recommended to take precautions against mosquito bites, as dengue fever is prevalent in the area.
  • Drink bottled or filtered water, and avoid consuming raw or undercooked foods.

Getting to Banjarmasin: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Banjarmasin

  • Syamsudin Noor International Airport, located approximately 22 kilometers from the city center.

Bus Stations Near Banjarmasin

  • Terminal Pahandut, a bus terminal in the city center that serves several destinations in the region.

Other Options

  • Taxi and ride-sharing services are available in the city, but be sure to negotiate the fare before getting in the vehicle. Motorbike rentals are also available in the city.

How to Get Around in Banjarmasin

  • Public transportation such as buses and taxis are readily available.
  • Bicycles and motorbikes are available for rent in the city.
  • Boat tours along the waterways and canals are a unique way to explore the city.

Where to Go Next from Banjarmasin: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Loksado, a charming village located approximately 2 hours from Banjarmasin that is known for its natural beauty and outdoor activities.
  • Martapura, a town approximately 30 minutes from Banjarmasin known for its diamond mines and traditional diamond carving industry.

Conclusion

Banjarmasin offers a unique blend of cultural experiences, outdoor adventures, and natural beauty that attract travelers from all over the world. With its fascinating history, vibrant culture, and warm hospitality, it is a destination that offers something for everyone.
