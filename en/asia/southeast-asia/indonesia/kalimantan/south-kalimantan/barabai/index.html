Barabai is a scenic town in South Kalimantan, located in the Indonesian part of Kalimantan island. It has several natural and cultural attractions that make it an ideal destination for travelers who seek outdoor adventures and cultural experiences. In this travel guide, we will explore the best places to visit, things to do, where to stay and eat, local culture and customs, safety and health, and transportation options in Barabai, South Kalimantan.

Introduction to Barabai

Barabai is a small town that lies on the eastern bank of the Barito River, one of the longest rivers in Indonesia. It is surrounded by lush tropical forests, and the climate is tropical, with high humidity and temperatures ranging between 78°F and 88°F throughout the year. The town has a rich culture and history, with a mix of Banjar, Malay, and Dayak traditions, which can be seen from its architecture, customs, and cuisine.

List of Places to Visit in Barabai

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Barabai has several historical and cultural attractions that reflect the town’s rich history and traditions. Some of the must-visit places include:

  • Kaharingan Traditional House
    • It is a traditional Dayak house that represents the Kaharingan religion, which is the indigenous faith of the Dayak people. The house has a unique architecture and decoration that reflects the Dayak’s beliefs and way of life.
  • Museum Negeri Lambung Mangkurat
    • It is a cultural museum that displays various artifacts, documents, and objects related to the local culture and history of South Kalimantan. It also has a collection of traditional clothes, weapons, and musical instruments that reflect the local customs and traditions.
  • Pasar Terapung Barabai
    • It is a floating market situated on the Barito River. The market offers a range of traditional foods and products, including freshly caught fish, fruits, and vegetables. It is an excellent place to experience the local culture and try some of the traditional cuisines of Barabai.

Natural Attractions of Barabai: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Barabai is surrounded by lush tropical forests and scenic views that offer a range of outdoor activities and adventures. Here are some of the must-visit natural attractions in Barabai:

  • Karasikan Natural Park
    • It is a tropical rainforest located in the southern part of Barabai. The park offers a range of outdoor activities, including trekking, camping, and bird watching. It is home to various species of flora and fauna, including the rare orangutan and proboscis monkey.
  • Air Terjun Loksado
    • It is a beautiful waterfall situated in Loksado village, which is about 3 hours drive from Barabai. The waterfall is surrounded by lush green forests and offers a refreshing swimming experience. Visitors can also enjoy trekking and hiking in the nearby hills.
  • Barito River
    • It is the longest river in South Kalimantan and offers a range of activities, including river cruising, kayaking, and fishing. Visitors can hire a boat and explore the river’s mangrove forests, see the traditional floating houses, and witness the local way of life.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Barabai

Barabai has several hiking and biking trails that offer scenic views and outdoor adventures. The primary activities include:

  • Gunung Botak
    • It is a hill situated in the middle of Barabai that offers panoramic views of the town and surrounding areas. It is an excellent destination for hiking and trekking, and visitors can also enjoy the scenic sunrise and sunset views.
  • Pengonakan Village
    • It is a traditional Banjar village located about 30 minutes drive from Barabai. The village has several hiking and biking trails that offer scenic views of the surrounding hills and rice fields.

Best Things to Do in Barabai: Activities and Tours

Barabai offers a range of activities and tours that cater to different interests and preferences. Here are some of the best things to do in Barabai:

  • Explore the Culture
    • Visitors can explore the local culture and history of Barabai by visiting the museums, traditional villages, and cultural events that take place throughout the year.
  • Outdoor Adventures
    • Barabai offers several outdoor adventures, including trekking, hiking, biking, river cruising, and wildlife watching. Visitors can hire a guide or join a tour to explore the natural beauty of the town and its surroundings.

    • Barabai has a rich culinary tradition that reflects the mix of Banjar, Malay, and Dayak cuisines. Visitors can try some of the local dishes, including Ayam Penyet, Soto Banjar, and Pempek Palembang.

Food and Drink Scene of Barabai

Barabai has a rich culinary tradition that reflects the mix of Banjar, Malay, and Dayak cuisines. Visitors can try some of the local dishes, including:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Ayam Penyet
    • It is a traditional Banjarese dish that consists of fried chicken served with sambal, rice, and vegetable.
  • Soto Banjar
    • It is a traditional Banjarese soup made with beef, noodles, and spices. It also comes with a side of rice cakes and crackers.
  • Pempek Palembang
    • It is a traditional Indonesian snack made with fish and tapioca starch. It is served with a sweet and sour sauce.

Local Beverages

  • Teh C Peng Sisik
    • It is a traditional iced tea with a unique design that resembles fish scales. It is made with tea, sugar, and lime juice.
  • Tuak
    • It is a traditional Dayak beverage made from fermented rice. It has a sweet and sour taste and is best consumed fresh.

What to Buy in Barabai: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Barabai offers several local products and handicrafts that reflect the town’s culture and traditions. Here are some of the best souvenirs to buy in Barabai:

  • Lok Baintan Weaving
    • It is a traditional Banjar handloom weaving that produces beautiful fabrics and textiles. Visitors can buy various products made from the Lok Baintan weaving, including bags, clothes, and accessories.
  • Dayak Crafts
    • Visitors can buy various Dayak handicrafts, including traditional masks, baskets, and carvings.
  • Diamond Mining Stones
    • Barabai has a rich mining history, and visitors can buy various products made from the local diamond mining stones, including jewelry and ornaments.

Local Culture and Customs of Barabai

Barabai has a rich culture and history that reflects the mix of Banjar, Malay, and Dayak traditions. Visitors are expected to respect the local customs and traditions, including wearing appropriate clothing when visiting the religious sites and avoiding public display of affection.

Best Time to Visit Barabai

The best time to visit Barabai is between May and September when the weather is dry, and the temperature is relatively cool. Visitors can enjoy the outdoor activities and natural beauty of the town without experiencing the high humidity and rainfall of the wet season.

Do’s and Don’ts of Barabai

Do:

  • Dress modestly when visiting religious sites and places.

  • Respect the local customs and traditions.

  • Try some of the local cuisines and drinks.

Don’t:

  • Public display of affection is not allowed.

  • Do not litter the surroundings with trash.

  • Do not touch or disturb the wildlife.

Safety and Health

Safety in Barabai

Barabai is a relatively safe town to visit. However, visitors are advised to take necessary precautions, including:

  • Travel in groups and avoid walking alone at night.

  • Keep valuables and passports in a secure place.

  • Avoid traveling during the rainy season, as the roads can be slippery and dangerous.

Health in Barabai

Visitors are advised to take necessary health precautions, including:

  • Drink only bottled or purified water, and avoid tap water.

  • Use insect repellent to avoid mosquito bites, which can spread malaria and dengue fever.

  • Carry necessary medication and first aid kits.

Getting to Barabai: Transportation Options and Logistics

Barabai can be reached by various transportation options, including:

Airports Near Barabai

The Banjarmasin Syamsudin Noor International Airport is the nearest airport, located about 2 hours drive from Barabai. Visitors can hire a taxi or a private car to reach their destination.

Bus Stations Near Barabai

Barabai has a bus terminal located in the city center, which offers various bus services to different parts of South Kalimantan.

Other Options

Visitors can also hire a private car or taxi to reach Barabai from Banjarmasin or other nearby towns.

How to Get Around in Barabai

  • Visitors can hire a taxi or a private car to explore the town and its surroundings.

  • Motorbike rentals are also available in the town.
