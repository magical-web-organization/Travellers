Introduction to Kumai

Located in Central Kalimantan, on the southern coast of Borneo, Kumai is a small town that serves as the gateway to Tanjung Puting National Park. Known for its lush rainforests, natural wonders, and abundant wildlife, Kumai offers an unforgettable travel experience that combines adventure, relaxation, and cultural immersion. Whether you are a nature lover, an outdoor enthusiast, or a history buff, Kumai has something for everyone.

List of Places to Visit in Kumai

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Istana Kota Waringin: Also known as the Sultan’s Palace, this historic building served as the residence of the sultan of Kotawaringin in the 19th century. Today, it is a popular attraction that showcases the local culture and architecture.
  • Tugu Khatulistiwa: This monument marks the equator line that runs through Central Kalimantan and is located in nearby Pangkalan Bun, just a short drive from Kumai.
  • Rumah Betang: A traditional Dayak longhouse that features unique architecture and cultural artifacts, it is a must-see for anyone interested in indigenous culture.

Natural Attractions of Kumai: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Tanjung Puting National Park: Home to one of the largest wild orangutan populations in the world, Tanjung Puting National Park is a UNESCO World Heritage Site that offers an unforgettable jungle experience. Visitors can explore the park on foot, by boat, or via a guided tour.
  • Kumai Bay: This picturesque bay is a great spot for swimming, sunbathing, and watching the sunset.
  • Klotok River Cruise: A river tour on a traditional wooden boat, known as a klotok, is a popular activity in Kumai. The tour takes visitors along the Sekonyer River, where they can see proboscis monkeys, crocodiles, and other wildlife.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Kumai

  • Gunung Pancar: This mountain is a popular hiking destination in Kumai, offering scenic views of the surrounding rainforest.
  • Bukit Tengkorak: This hill, located within Tanjung Puting National Park, is known for its unique rock formations and is a popular spot for trekking.
  • Biking around town: Renting a bike is a great way to explore the local area at your own pace. Ask your hotel or guesthouse for recommendations on the best routes.

Best Things to Do in Kumai: Activities and Tours

  • Orangutan Trekking: A once-in-a-lifetime opportunity to see these majestic creatures up close. Hire a local guide for the best experience.
  • Nighttime Jungle Tour: Explore the rainforest after dark and see nocturnal creatures like owls, flying squirrels, and slow lorises.
  • Village Tour: Learn about local culture and traditions by visiting nearby villages.

Week-Long Itinerary for Kumai

Day 1 Explore Tanjung Puting National Park
Day 2 Visit Istana Kota Waringin and Tugu Khatulistiwa
Day 3 Go on a klotok river cruise
Day 4 Do a nighttime jungle tour
Day 5 Rent a bike and explore Kumai
Day 6 Visit Rumah Betang and nearby villages
Day 7 Relax on Kumai Bay

Food and Drink Scene of Kumai

Must-Try Dishes

  • Soto: A traditional Indonesian soup made with chicken, noodles, and vegetables.
  • Lontong Sayur: A rice cake dish served with vegetables in a spicy coconut milk broth.
  • Ikan Bakar: Grilled fish served with rice and sambal.

Local Beverages

  • Kelapa Muda: Young coconut water - refreshing and hydrating on a hot day!
  • Teh Botol: A sweetened iced tea popular throughout Indonesia.
  • Bintang Beer: A local lager that pairs well with spicy food.

Where to Stay in Kumai: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Kumai Backpackers: A budget-friendly hostel that offers dorms and private rooms.
  • Green Lodge: A mid-range hotel with comfortable, modern rooms and an on-site restaurant.
  • Rimba Ecolodge: An eco-friendly resort located within Tanjung Puting National Park that offers a unique jungle experience.

What to Buy in Kumai: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Dayak Crafts: Indigenous crafts such as woven baskets, carved wooden sculptures, and beaded jewelry.
  • Kris: A traditional Indonesian weapon with a distinctive curved blade.
  • Batik: A type of cloth that is decorated with wax and dye and is popular throughout Indonesia.

Local Culture and Customs of Kumai

  • The local culture is a blend of Malay, Dayak, and Javanese influences.
  • Islam is the predominant religion in Kumai, though other faiths are also represented.
  • Respect for elders and authority figures is highly valued in local culture.

Best Time to Visit Kumai

The best time to visit Kumai is during the dry season, which typically lasts from May to September. This is also the peak season for tourism, so be prepared for higher prices and larger crowds. The rainy season (October to April) can be an excellent time to visit if you don’t mind the occasional downpour, as the landscape is lush and green and the wildlife is more active.

Do’s and Don’ts of Kumai

Do:

  • Dress modestly and respectfully when visiting temples and other religious sites.
  • Respect local customs and traditions.
  • Try to learn a few words of Indonesian - the locals will appreciate the effort!

Don’t:

  • Don’t litter or damage the environment.
  • Don’t touch or disturb the wildlife.
  • Don’t disrespect elders or authority figures.

Safety and Health

Safety in Kumai

Kumai is a relatively safe town, but as with any destination, it’s important to take basic safety precautions. Avoid walking alone at night, keep valuables out of sight, and be aware of your surroundings.

Health in Kumai

A few health precautions to take before visiting Kumai:

  • Check with your doctor to see if any vaccinations are recommended or required for your visit.
  • Use insect repellent to protect against mosquito-borne illnesses.
  • only drink bottled or boiled water.

Getting to Kumai: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Kumai

  • Iskandar Airport (PKN), located in Pangkalan Bun, is the closest airport to Kumai, with regular flights to other major cities in Indonesia like Jakarta and Surabaya.

Bus Stations Near Kumai

  • Bus routes connect Kumai with other cities in Kalimantan, including Palangkaraya and Banjarmasin.

Other Options

  • Taxis and private car rentals are also available for those who prefer more direct transportation.

How to Get Around in Kumai

Getting around Kumai is relatively easy and affordable.

  • Taxis and motorbike rentals are available.
  • Bicycling is a popular way to get around and see the local area.

Where to Go Next from Kumai: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Palangkaraya: the capital city of Central Kalimantan, is the nearest major city and offers a range of cultural and historical attractions.
  • Borobudur: A UNESCO World Heritage Site located on the island of Java, is a must-visit for anyone interested in Indonesian culture and history.
  • Pulau Derawan: This stunning island, located off the coast of East Kalimantan, is known for its pristine beaches, crystal-clear waters, and diverse marine life.

Conclusion

Kumai is truly a traveler’s paradise, offering a unique blend of natural beauty, cultural richness, and adventure. Whether you’re seeking an eco-friendly jungle retreat, a cultural immersion, or simply a relaxing vacation, Kumai has something for everyone. With this comprehensive travel guide, you’re now equipped with all the information you need to plan an unforgettable trip to Kumai.
