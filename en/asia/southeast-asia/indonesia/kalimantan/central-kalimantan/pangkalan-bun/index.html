Introduction to Pangkalan Bun

Pangkalan Bun is a small town located in the province of Central Kalimantan, on the island of Borneo in Indonesia. The town is best known as a gateway to Tanjung Puting National Park, which is home to some of the world’s largest populations of wild orangutans. Pangkalan Bun is also famous for its rich culture and traditions, delicious food, and stunning natural beauty, making it a must-visit destination in Indonesia.

List of Places to Visit in Pangkalan Bun

If you’re planning a trip to Pangkalan Bun, make sure to include these attractions in your itinerary:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Istana Kuning (Yellow Palace): Built in the early 20th century by Dutch colonialists, this palace showcases the architectural blend of European and Javanese styles.
  • Keraton Kotawaringin: This historical site is a royal palace and mosque, which dates back to the 15th century. Visitors can explore the palace rooms and learn about the history and culture of the region.
  • Danau Sembuluh: This lake is home to floating houses and boasts stunning views, especially during sunrise and sunset.

Natural Attractions of Pangkalan Bun: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Tanjung Puting National Park: This park is home to around 6,000 orangutans and several other animal species, including gibbons, proboscis monkeys, and crocodiles. The park has three feeding stations where visitors can see the orangutans up close.
  • Camp Leakey: This is a research and conservation center for orangutans, where visitors can see the primates in their natural habitat and learn about conservation efforts.
  • Sungai Waringin: This river is perfect for a scenic boat tour, offering the opportunity to see mangrove forests, wildlife, and traditional floating houses.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Pangkalan Bun

If you’re a nature lover and enjoy hiking or biking, make sure to explore these routes in Pangkalan Bun:

  • Sangkulirang-Mangkalihat Wildlife Reserve: This is a tropical rainforest reserve that is home to several endangered species, including orangutans and proboscis monkeys.
  • Bukit Rawi: This hill offers stunning views of the surrounding forests and rivers, and is also great for bird-watching.
  • Arut River: This river offers an adventurous rafting experience, with stunning scenery and challenging rapids.

Best Things to Do in Pangkalan Bun: Activities and Tours

Apart from exploring the cultural and natural attractions, here are some other things to do in Pangkalan Bun:

  • Try the local cuisine: Sample traditional dishes such as Nasi Kuning (yellow rice), Ikan Bakar (grilled fish), and Soto Banjar (spicy soup).
  • Go on a river cruise: Take a slow boat tour along the rivers and soak up the stunning views of the mangrove forests and local wildlife.
  • Visit the night market: In the evening, the market offers an amazing variety of local handicrafts, souvenirs, and street food.

Week-Long Itinerary for Pangkalan Bun

Here’s an example itinerary for a week-long trip to Pangkalan Bun:

Day Activities

Food and Drink Scene of Pangkalan Bun

When it comes to food, Pangkalan Bun offers a variety of unique dishes and local beverages that you don’t want to miss.

Must-Try Dishes

  • Nasi Kuning: This is a popular breakfast dish made with turmeric-flavored rice and various toppings such as chicken, tofu, and vegetables.
  • Betutu: This is a traditional Balinese dish of smoked chicken or duck, served with spices and vegetables.
  • Ikan Bakar: This is a grilled fish dish, usually served with steamed rice and vegetables.

Local Beverages

  • Teh Talua: This is tea made with egg yolk, sugar, and lime juice. It’s a popular drink for breakfast or as a refreshing drink during hot days.
  • Kelapa Muda: This is coconut water served straight from the fruit. It’s a popular drink for its refreshing and hydrating properties.
  • Bajigur: This is a Javanese hot drink made with coconut milk, brown sugar, ginger, and pandan leaf. It’s usually served with roasted peanuts.

Where to Stay in Pangkalan Bun: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

There are several accommodation options in Pangkalan Bun, ranging from budget-friendly hostels to luxury hotels. Here are some of the best:

  • Swiss-Belinn Pangkalan Bun: This is a modern and comfortable hotel, located in the heart of Pangkalan Bun. The hotel features an outdoor pool and a spa.
  • Aston Niu Manokwari Hotel & Conference Center: This is a 4-star hotel, located close to the airport. The hotel offers a fitness center, meeting rooms, and a restaurant.
  • Holi House: This is a guesthouse with a traditional Javanese-style house. The guesthouse features a lovely garden and a terrace.

What to Buy in Pangkalan Bun: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

If you’re looking for souvenirs or gifts, make sure to check out these local products:

  • Batik Fabric: This is a traditional textile made with a wax-resist dyeing technique. You can find batik fabric in many patterns and colors.
  • Dayak Handicrafts: Dayak is an indigenous community in Borneo, and their handicrafts are often made from natural materials such as wood and rattan.
  • Kopi Luwak: This is a famous coffee that is made from the droppings of civet cats. It’s a unique and expensive coffee, but it’s worth trying.

Local Culture and Customs of Pangkalan Bun

Pangkalan Bun has a rich culture and traditions, and visitors should respect and observe them. Here are some customs to keep in mind:

  • Islam is the dominant religion in the area, so visitors should dress modestly and respect local customs during Ramadan.
  • Most areas in Pangkalan Bun are not touristy, so visitors should be respectful and considerate, especially in rural areas.
  • Visitors should not touch or feed the wildlife in Tanjung Puting National Park, as it’s an important conservation area.

Best Time to Visit Pangkalan Bun

The best time to visit Pangkalan Bun is during the dry season from May to October, when the weather is more pleasant and there’s less chance of rain. However, be prepared for high temperatures and humidity during this time. The rainy season from November to April can be a challenge, especially for outdoor activities.

Do’s and Don’ts of Pangkalan Bun

Here are some do’s and don’ts to keep in mind when visiting Pangkalan Bun:

Do:

  • Try the local cuisine and explore the natural attractions.
  • Respect local customs and cultural practices.
  • Learn some Bahasa Indonesia phrases and have a conversation with the friendly locals.

Don’t:

  • Touch or feed the wildlife in Tanjung Puting National Park.
  • Wear revealing clothing or bikinis, as it’s not culturally appropriate.
  • Take photos of people without asking for their permission.

Safety and Health

Pangkalan Bun is generally a safe place to visit, but visitors should take precautions and follow these safety and health tips:

Safety in Pangkalan Bun

  • Be careful when exploring the national park and other natural attractions, as they can be challenging for inexperienced hikers or boaters.
  • Take precautions against mosquito bites and use insect repellent, especially during the rainy season.

Health in Pangkalan Bun

  • Visitors should get vaccinated before traveling and take precautions against malaria.
  • Drink bottled water to avoid stomach problems and other illnesses.
  • Be careful with street food and make sure it’s freshly cooked and hot.

Getting to Pangkalan Bun: Transportation Options and Logistics

If you’re planning a trip to Pangkalan Bun, here’s how to get there:

Airports Near Pangkalan Bun

  • Pangkalan Bun Airport (PKN): This is the main airport in Pangkalan Bun, located only 3.5 km from the town center. There are several daily flights connecting Pangkalan Bun with Jakarta and other major cities in Indonesia.

Bus Stations Near Pangkalan Bun

  • PELNI Bus Terminal: This is the main bus station in Pangkalan Bun, located in the town center. There are several daily buses connecting Pangkalan Bun with other cities in Kalimantan.

Other Options

  • Riverboat: You can also reach Pangkalan Bun by riverboat from Kumai, a small port town. The journey takes around 1 hour and offers stunning views of the mangrove forests along the river.

How to Get Around in Pangkalan Bun

Pangkalan Bun is a small town, and most of the attractions are within walking distance. However, if you want to explore further or have limited time, you can use these transportation modes:

  • Ojek: This is a motorcycle taxi, which is a fast and cheap way to get around. Make sure to negotiate the price before getting on.
  • Kijang: This is a shared taxi that operates like a mini-bus. They are usually cheaper than taxis and more comfortable than ojeks.
  • Car rental: You can also rent a car with a driver to explore the town and nearby attractions.

Where to Go Next from Pangkalan Bun: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

If you have more time, there are several nearby destinations that are worth visiting:

  • Palangkaraya: This is the capital city of Central Kalimantan, which is famous for its Dayak culture and attractions such as the Taman Wisata Alam Bukit Batu nature reserve.
  • Sampit: This is a harbor town and a gateway to the Seruyan River, where you can see wild orangutans and other wildlife.
  • Banjarmasin: This is the biggest city in South Kalimantan, which is famous for its riverboat tours and floating markets.

Conclusion

Pangkalan Bun is a hidden gem in Indonesia, offering a unique blend of cultural, natural, and wildlife attractions. With its welcoming locals, delicious cuisine, and stunning scenery, it’s a destination that shouldn’t be missed.
