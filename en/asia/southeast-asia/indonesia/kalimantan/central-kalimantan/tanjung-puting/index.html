Introduction to Tanjung Puting

Tanjung Puting is a national park located in Central Kalimantan, Indonesia, known for its stunning jungle landscapes, diverse wildlife, and vibrant indigenous culture. The park is home to over 6000 orangutans, making it the largest population of captive orangutans in the world. The park also features a wide range of bird and mammal species, making it an unforgettable destination for animal lovers and nature enthusiasts. Tanjung Puting is a must-visit destination for anyone traveling to Indonesia, offering visitors an authentic glimpse into the country’s rich and vibrant culture.

List of Places to Visit in Tanjung Puting

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Dr. Birute Galdikas Orangutan Conservancy: A foundation aimed at raising awareness about orangutan conservation and educating tourists about the work being done to protect these incredible primates.
  • Museum Tanjung Puting: A small museum dedicated to showcasing the unique culture and heritage of the indigenous people of Central Kalimantan.
  • The Crocodile Lake: A stunning natural attraction located within the park, famous for its impressive population of crocodiles.

Natural Attractions of Tanjung Puting: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Camp Leakey: The oldest and most famous orangutan research and rehabilitation center in the world.
  • Sekonyer River: A scenic river that runs through the heart of the park, offering visitors the chance to spot a wide variety of wildlife, including proboscis monkeys and crocodiles.
  • Tanjung Harapan: A stunning beach located on the edge of the park, offering visitors crystal clear water and a peaceful escape from the hustle and bustle of the city.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Tanjung Puting

  • Tanjung Puting Wildlife Reserve: A beautiful reserve that offers visitors the chance to hike through dense jungle and spot a variety of wildlife. This is also a great spot for birdwatching, with over 200 species found in the area.
  • Rimba Orangutan Eco Lodge: A great spot for mountain biking, with well-maintained trails that run through the jungle and offer stunning views of the surrounding landscape.

Best Things to Do in Tanjung Puting: Activities and Tours

  • Orangutan Trekking: A once-in-a-lifetime experience that involves hiking through the jungle with an experienced guide and coming face-to-face with orangutans in their natural habitat.
  • River Cruise: The best way to explore the park’s waterways and spot wildlife, with many boat tours available that offer comfortable seating and refreshments.
  • Night Safari: A unique opportunity to explore the park at night, when many of its nocturnal animals come out to play.

Food and Drink Scene of Tanjung Puting

Must-Try Dishes

  • Nasi Padang: A famous Indonesian dish that consists of rice served with a variety of meats, vegetables, and spicy sauces.
  • Sate Ayam: Grilled chicken skewers, a popular street food in Indonesia.
  • Mie Godog: A noodle soup that is a favorite among locals, featuring a savory broth and fresh vegetables.

Local Beverages

  • Kopi Luwak: The most famous coffee in Indonesia, made from beans that have been eaten and then excreted by civet cats.
  • Teh Tarik: A sweet and creamy milk tea that is a popular drink throughout Indonesia.
  • Bintang Beer: The most popular beer in Indonesia, known for its refreshing taste and crisp finish.

Where to Stay in Tanjung Puting: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Rimba Orangutan Eco Lodge: A sustainable eco-lodge that puts a premium on conservation and sustainability, offering guests the chance to stay in comfortable, modern accommodations while enjoying stunning views of the jungle.
  • Palm Lodge: A budget-friendly guesthouse that offers comfortable accommodations, friendly staff, and a convenient location in the heart of Pangkalan Bun.
  • Kumai Beach Resort: A beachside resort that offers guests easy access to Tanjung Harapan and a variety of on-site amenities, including a swimming pool, spa, and restaurant.

What to Buy in Tanjung Puting: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Wooden Carvings: Hand-carved wooden statues and ornaments are a popular souvenir in Indonesia, especially in the Central Kalimantan region.
  • Batik Fabric: A type of traditional Indonesian fabric that is made using wax and dye, creating unique and intricate designs.
  • Kopi Luwak: The famous Indonesian coffee makes a great gift to take home and share with friends and family.

Local Culture and Customs of Tanjung Puting

  • Traditional Dress: The indigenous people of Central Kalimantan wear traditional dress that is characterized by bold colors and intricate patterns.
  • Traditional Music and Dance: Music and dance are an important part of Indonesian culture, with many traditional performances taking place throughout the year.
  • Etiquette: It is important to show respect for local customs and traditions, including removing your shoes before entering a home or temple and dressing modestly.

Best Time to Visit Tanjung Puting

The best time to visit Tanjung Puting is during the dry season, which runs from May to October. During this time, rainfall is minimal, and the weather is pleasant and warm, making it the perfect time to explore the park’s outdoor attractions.

Do’s and Don’ts of Tanjung Puting

Do:

  • Do dress modestly and show respect for local culture and customs.
  • Do bring insect repellent to protect yourself from mosquitoes and other insects.
  • Do stay hydrated and carry plenty of water with you at all times.

Don’t:

  • Don’t feed the animals, including orangutans, as this can disrupt their natural behavior and cause harm to the animals.
  • Don’t litter or leave trash behind in the park, as this can harm the environment and its inhabitants.
  • Don’t take any plant or animal specimens out of the park, as this is illegal and can harm the park’s delicate ecosystem.

Safety and Health

Safety in Tanjung Puting

Tanjung Puting is a safe destination for travelers, with a low crime rate and friendly locals. However, it is always important to take necessary safety precautions while traveling, including keeping your valuables safe and not traveling alone at night.

Health in Tanjung Puting

Before traveling to Tanjung Puting, it is important to consult with a healthcare professional to ensure you are up-to-date on all necessary vaccinations. While in the park, be sure to drink only bottled or boiled water and take necessary precautions to avoid mosquito bites.

Getting to Tanjung Puting: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Tanjung Puting

  • Pangkalan Bun Airport: The closest airport to Tanjung Puting, located just a short distance from the park.

Other Options

  • Boat: Many visitors choose to arrive in Tanjung Puting via a riverboat, offering a unique and picturesque way to travel.
  • Bus: Buses run from all major cities in Central Kalimantan to Pangkalan Bun and other nearby destinations.

How to Get Around in Tanjung Puting

The best way to get around Tanjung Puting is on foot or by boat. Many of the park’s attractions are accessible only by foot, while boat tours offer a great way to explore the park’s waterways and spot wildlife.

Where to Go Next from Tanjung Puting: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Borobudur Temple: One of the most famous ancient monuments in Indonesia, offering visitors a glimpse into the country’s rich cultural history.
  • Yogyakarta: The cultural capital of Indonesia, featuring a range of museums, galleries, and cultural attractions.
  • Bali: A world-renowned destination famous for its stunning beaches, vibrant nightlife, and unique culture.

Conclusion

Tanjung Puting is a destination that truly has something for everyone, whether you’re a nature lover, an animal enthusiast, or simply looking to immerse yourself in Indonesian culture. With its stunning landscapes, unique attractions, and friendly locals, it’s no wonder that Tanjung Puting has become one of the most popular destinations in Southeast Asia. So, pack your bags and get ready for an unforgettable adventure in one of the world’s most beautiful and diverse destinations.
