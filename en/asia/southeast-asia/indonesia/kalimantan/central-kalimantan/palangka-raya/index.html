Introduction to Palangka Raya

Palangka Raya is a city located in the heart of Central Kalimantan, Indonesia. It is known for its lush rainforests, cultural attractions, and outdoor adventures. The city is accessible via air and land transportation and offers a range of accommodations to suit every budget.

List of Places to Visit in Palangka Raya

Palangka Raya is home to several culturally important attractions and natural wonders. Here are some of the best things to see and do in the city:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Museum Balanga: This museum showcases traditional Dayak life, the indigenous people of Borneo.
  • Kaharingan Museum: This museum features the Kaharingan religion, which is unique to the Dayak people.
  • Tjilik Riwut Cultural Park: This park is a cultural center that hosts traditional dance performances and exhibitions.

Natural Attractions of Palangka Raya: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Bukit Tangkiling: This hill offers panoramic views of the city and surrounding rainforest.
  • Sabangau National Park: This park is home to orangutans, proboscis monkeys, and other wildlife.
  • Danau Semayang Lake: This lake is located in the middle of the city and offers boat rides and lakeside walks.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Palangka Raya

Palangka Raya is surrounded by rainforests and offers several hiking and biking trails. Some popular routes include:

  • Bukit Rawi: This hiking trail leads to an observation deck with views of the city and the Kahayan River.
  • Rian Jayapura: This hiking trail leads to a waterfall and natural pool for swimming.
  • Nyaru Menteng: This bike trail leads through a traditional Dayak village and past rice paddies.

Best Things to Do in Palangka Raya: Activities and Tours

Palangka Raya offers a range of activities and tours for all ages and interests:

  • Dayak Longhouse Tour: This tour takes you through a traditional Dayak longhouse to learn about the Dayak way of life.
  • River Cruise: This cruise takes you along the Kahayan River to see wildlife and enjoy the scenic views.
  • Night Market Tour: This tour takes you to the local night market to try traditional Indonesian street food.

Week-Long Itinerary for Palangka Raya

Here’s a suggested itinerary for a week-long trip to Palangka Raya:

Day 1: Arrive in Palangka Raya and check into your hotel. Take a stroll along Danau Semayang Lake and enjoy sunset views.

Day 2: Visit Museum Balanga and learn about traditional Dayak life. In the afternoon, take a bike ride through a traditional Dayak village.

Day 3: Explore Sabangau National Park and see orangutans, proboscis monkeys, and other wildlife.

Day 4: Take a river cruise along the Kahayan River and see the city from a different perspective.

Day 5: Visit the Kaharingan Museum and learn about the unique Kaharingan religion. In the afternoon, hike to Bukit Rawi and enjoy panoramic views of the city.

Day 6: Take a day trip to Tangkiling Nature Reserve and see more of Borneo’s rainforest.

Day 7: Visit the Tjilik Riwut Cultural Park and witness traditional dance performances. In the evening, take a night market tour to try traditional Indonesian street food.

Food and Drink Scene of Palangka Raya

Palangka Raya boasts a range of traditional Indonesian dishes and unique local snacks:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Ayam Betutu: This spicy chicken dish is marinated in fragrant spices and grilled to perfection.
  • Maranggi: This grilled beef dish is served with a sweet and spicy sauce.
  • Lempok Durian: This local snack is a sweet and chewy durian-based cake.

Local Beverages

  • Kopyor: This sweet, creamy coconut drink is a local favorite.
  • Bir Pletok: This traditional spiced beer is made with ginger and local herbs.
  • Teh Tanjung: This tea is made with freshwater from Lake Tahai and has a unique taste.

Where to Stay in Palangka Raya: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Palangka Raya offers a range of accommodation options, from budget-friendly hostels to luxury hotels:

  • Swiss-Belhotel Danum Palangka Raya: This hotel offers modern amenities in a central location.
  • Zen Rooms Palangka Raya: This budget-friendly hotel has clean, comfortable rooms and a convenient location.
  • Villa Kayu Raja: This luxury hotel offers villas with private pools and lush gardens.

What to Buy in Palangka Raya: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Palangka Raya is known for its traditional handicrafts and unique products:

  • Dayak Weaving: Hand-woven textiles made by the Dayak people, featuring intricate patterns and designs.
  • Lokta Paper Products: Handmade paper products made from eco-friendly materials.
  • Air Drying Clay: A type of air-drying clay used by the Dayak people to create traditional sculptures and figurines.

Local Culture and Customs of Palangka Raya

Palangka Raya is a multicultural city, with influences from the Dayak people and other ethnic groups. Here are some local customs to keep in mind:

  • Respect local customs and dress appropriately when visiting temples and other cultural sites.
  • The Dayak people believe in “adat,” which governs behavior and relationships within the community.
  • Handshakes are a common greeting, but wait for the other person to initiate physical contact.

Best Time to Visit Palangka Raya

The best time to visit Palangka Raya is during the dry season from April to September. The weather is cooler and drier, making it perfect for outdoor activities. However, the city can be visited throughout the year as the temperature remains stable throughout the year.

Do’s and Don’ts of Palangka Raya

Here are some do’s and don’ts to keep in mind when visiting Palangka Raya:

Do:

  • Try traditional Indonesian food and snacks.
  • Respect local customs and dress appropriately.
  • Learn about the unique Dayak culture and history.

Don’t:

  • Touch or disturb wildlife in natural parks.
  • Disrespect local customs and beliefs.
  • Litter or pollute the environment.

Safety and Health

Safety in Palangka Raya

Palangka Raya is a relatively safe city, but you should take precautions as you would in any other place you visit.

  • Carry a copy of your passport or ID with you at all times.
  • Be aware of your surroundings, especially at night.
  • Keep valuables hidden and secure.

Health in Palangka Raya

The tap water in Palangka Raya is not safe for drinking. Stick to bottled water or bring a reusable water bottle with a filter. Additionally, make sure you get vaccinated before your trip and bring necessary prescription medication with you.

Getting to Palangka Raya: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Palangka Raya

Palangka Raya has one airport located approximately 6 miles outside of the city:

  • Tjilik Riwut Airport (PKY)

Bus Stations Near Palangka Raya

Several bus companies operate in Palangka Raya, with most buses departing from the Central Bus Station:

  • Central Bus Station Palangka Raya: this bus station has several daily departures to other cities within Kalimantan.

Other Options

Taxis, car rental, and motorcycle rentals are also available in Palangka Raya.

How to Get Around in Palangka Raya

Palangka Raya has several transportation options:

  • Ojek (motorcycle taxi): This is a popular and affordable option for short-distance travel.
  • Taxis: Taxis are available throughout the city, and ride-hailing apps such as Go-Jek and Grab are also available.
  • Car Rental: Several car rental companies operate in Palangka Raya.

Where to Go Next from Palangka Raya: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Palangka Raya is close to several other destinations in Central Kalimantan:

  • Bukit Raya: This mountain offers challenging hikes and scenic views.
  • Tangkiling Nature Reserve: This reserve has several hiking trails, waterfalls, and caves to explore.
  • Tanjung Puting National Park: This park is home to the largest population of wild orangutans in the world.

Conclusion

Palangka Raya is a hidden gem located in the heart of Central Kalimantan. With its rich culture, lush rainforests, and outdoor adventures, it’s a must-visit destination for travelers looking to get off the beaten path in Indonesia. Plan your trip today to explore the best that Palangka Raya has to offer.
