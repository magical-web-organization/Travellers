Introduction to Probolinggo

Nestled in the heart of East Java, Probolinggo is a charming city that offers visitors a unique experience. Boasting incredible natural beauty, rich history, and vibrant culture, this Indonesian gem is well worth a visit. Whether you are looking to explore the city’s cultural sites, embark on an outdoor adventure, or immerse yourself in local customs, Probolinggo has something for everyone.

List of Places to Visit in Probolinggo

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Probolinggo is home to a number of fascinating museums and historical sites. Visit the Probolinggo City Museum to learn about the city’s past, or tour the Sumberbening Palace, a grand historical residence that dates back to the Dutch colonial era. The Probolinggo State Library is also worth a visit for history buffs, boasting a wide range of rare manuscripts and books.

Natural Attractions of Probolinggo: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

For lovers of the outdoors, Probolinggo offers a range of activities and scenic spots to explore. Visit the stunning Bromo Tengger Semeru National Park, home to the famous Mount Bromo, an active volcano that offers spectacular views. Don’t miss the Madakaripura Waterfall, a natural wonder that is considered to be one of the most beautiful in East Java. For those who enjoy hiking, the Argopuro mountain range offers a range of challenging and rewarding routes to explore.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Probolinggo

For those who love hiking or biking, Probolinggo offers a range of breathtaking trails. The Bromo Tengger Semeru National Park offers a variety of routes that cater to different levels of ability, and the Argopuro mountain range boasts some of the most challenging but rewarding routes in the area. Hire a local guide to get the most out of your hiking or biking experience.

Best Things to Do in Probolinggo: Activities and Tours

In addition to outdoor activities, Probolinggo offers a range of other fun and exciting things to do. Take a cooking class to learn how to prepare traditional Indonesian dishes, or embark on a river rafting adventure down the Progo River. For a more relaxing experience, visit one of the city’s hot springs for a soothing soak.

Week-Long Itinerary for Probolinggo

For those looking to experience all that Probolinggo has to offer, consider following this week-long itinerary:

  • Day 1: Visit the Probolinggo City Museum and Sumberbening Palace.
  • Day 2: Explore the Bromo Tengger Semeru National Park and hike Mount Bromo.
  • Day 3: Relax and soak in one of the city’s hot springs.
  • Day 4: Take a cooking class and sample traditional Indonesian cuisine.
  • Day 5: Tour the Madakaripura Waterfall.
  • Day 6: Embark on a river rafting adventure down the Progo River.
  • Day 7: Visit the Argopuro mountain range and hike one of its challenging routes.

Food and Drink Scene of Probolinggo

Must-Try Dishes

Probolinggo is known for its delicious cuisine, which blends traditional Indonesian flavors with local specialties. Don’t miss the sate ayam, a flavorful chicken skewer dish that is grilled over an open flame. Try the nasi kebuli, a fragrant rice dish that is cooked with aromatic spices and lamb. Those with a sweet tooth should try the banana kolak, a dessert made with coconut milk, palm sugar, and ripe bananas.

Local Beverages

Indonesia is famous for its coffee, and Probolinggo is no exception. Sample the local brew and savor its rich, full-bodied flavor. For something stronger, try the arak, a potent local liquor made from fermented rice or coconut.

Where to Stay in Probolinggo: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Probolinggo offers a range of accommodation options to suit all budgets and preferences. For luxury travelers, the Bromo Cottages and Plataran Bromo Resort & Venue offer comfortable rooms and stunning views. For those on a budget, there are a number of affordable guesthouses and hostels in the city that offer clean and comfortable accommodations.

What to Buy in Probolinggo: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

When visiting Probolinggo, be sure to pick up some of the city’s unique souvenirs and local products. Batik fabrics, with their intricate designs and bold colors, make for great gifts, as do traditional Indonesian puppets. Don’t miss the chance to try some of the local delicacies, such as tempeh or kripik, a type of savory snack made from cassava.

Local Culture and Customs of Probolinggo

Indonesia is a country with deep-seated traditions, and Probolinggo is no exception. Visitors are encouraged to dress modestly and show respect for local customs and traditions. During your stay, take the time to learn about the local way of life, participate in cultural events, and interact with the friendly locals.

Best Time to Visit Probolinggo

The best time to visit Probolinggo is between May and September, when the weather is dry and pleasant. Be aware that the area can get quite crowded during peak season, so plan your visit accordingly.

Do’s and Don’ts of Probolinggo

Do:

  • Dress modestly and show respect for local customs and traditions.
  • Try the local cuisine and coffee.
  • Take the time to learn about the local way of life.

Don’t:

  • Visit during peak season without booking accommodation and transportation in advance.
  • Engage in disrespectful behavior or disregard local customs and traditions.
  • Take off your shoes when entering a mosque or somebody’s home.

Safety and Health

Safety in Probolinggo

Overall, Probolinggo is a safe city for visitors. However, travelers should be aware of pickpockets and theft, especially in crowded areas. It’s also important to exercise caution when hiking or biking in rural areas, as there may be hazards such as loose rocks or uneven terrain.

Health in Probolinggo

Visitors to Probolinggo should be up-to-date on routine vaccinations and should consider getting vaccinated against hepatitis A and B. Mosquito-borne illnesses such as dengue fever and malaria are a risk, so travelers should take steps to prevent mosquito bites.

Getting to Probolinggo: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Probolinggo

The closest airport to Probolinggo is the Juanda International Airport in Surabaya, which is located approximately 57 kilometers away. From there, visitors can take a taxi, bus or train to reach Probolinggo.

Bus Stations Near Probolinggo

Probolinggo has a central bus station with connections throughout the region. There are several bus companies operating in the city, offering both local and long-distance transportation.

Other Options

Travelers who prefer to drive themselves can rent a car at the Juanda International Airport in Surabaya or in Probolinggo. Taxis and ride-sharing services are also available for those who prefer not to drive.

How to Get Around in Probolinggo

Probolinggo is a compact city, and many attractions are within walking distance of each other. Taxis and ride-sharing services are also widely available, and there are local buses and minibusses that connect different parts of the city.

Where to Go Next from Probolinggo: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Probolinggo is a great base for exploring the wider East Java region. Nearby attractions include the city of Surabaya, home to the famous House of Sampoerna museum, and the stunning Ijen Crater, a volcanic crater lake that glows blue at night. The city of Malang, known for its colonial architecture and trendy cafes and restaurants, is also worth a visit.

Conclusion

Probolinggo offers visitors a unique and exciting experience, with its rich history, stunning natural beauty, and vibrant culture. Whether you are a history buff or an outdoor enthusiast, a foodie or a cultural traveler, Probolinggo has something for everyone. Plan your visit today and experience this Indonesian gem for yourself.
