Introduction to Bondowoso

Located in the Horseshoe area of East Java, in Indonesia, Bondowoso is a small town that preserves the traditional Javanese way of life. The town is surrounded by forests and mountains, making it an ideal destination for nature lovers and adventurers. Bondowoso is also rich in Javanese culture and history, evident in its monuments, museums, and traditional festivals.

List of Places to Visit in Bondowoso

Bondowoso has many cultural and natural attractions that are worth visiting. Here is a list of the most popular places to visit:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Soekarno Bondowoso Museum: A museum dedicated to Indonesia’s first president, Soekarno, who spent time in Bondowoso during the Dutch Colonial period.
  • Giri Arjuna Temple: A Hindu temple built during the Majapahit Kingdom period, located on the slopes of Mount Raung.
  • Ijen Crater: A popular hiking spot that leads to a volcanic crater lake.
  • Japanese Caves: A network of tunnels and caves that were used by Japanese soldiers during World War II.
  • Kampung Jawa: A traditional Javanese village where you can see the daily life of local people.
  • Taman Nasional Meru Betiri: A national park that is home to rare species like the Javan rhinoceros, Javan leopard, and Javanese eagle.

Natural Attractions of Bondowoso: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Mount Raung: An active volcano and the second-highest mountain in East Java.
  • Alas Purwo National Park: A protected tropical forest with wildlife, birdlife, and hiking trails.
  • Sukamade Beach: A secluded black-sand beach that is a nesting site for sea turtles.
  • Tamansari Waterfall: A serene and picturesque waterfall surrounded by lush greenery.
  • Kalibendo Waterfall: A scenic two-tiered waterfall that is a popular spot for hiking and picnicking.
  • Sumber Pitulung Adventure Park: A family-friendly park with outdoor activities like camping, canoeing, and archery.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Bondowoso

Bondowoso is perfect for hiking and biking enthusiasts, with many trails that offer breathtaking views of the surrounding mountains and forests. Some popular routes are:

  • Mount Raung Summit Trail: A challenging trek that takes you to the peak of Mount Raung, offering stunning panoramic views.
  • Alas Purwo National Park Trail: A network of trails that lead to the park’s waterfalls, beaches, and viewpoints.
  • Kalibendo Waterfall Trail: A moderate hike that takes you through lush jungle and across rivers to reach the waterfall.
  • Sumber Pitulung Adventure Park Trail: A scenic route through the park’s forest and bamboo groves, suitable for hiking and mountain biking enthusiasts.

Best Things to Do in Bondowoso: Activities and Tours

Besides hiking and biking, Bondowoso offers many other activities and tours that cater to different interests:

  • Sukamade Turtle Tour: A guided tour that takes you to Sukamade Beach and lets you observe sea turtles laying eggs.
  • Traditional Javanese Cultural Tour: A tour that exposes you to Javanese culture and traditions through visits to batik-making workshops, gamelan music performances, and traditional markets.
  • Coffee Plantation Tour: A tour that takes you to local coffee plantations, where you can learn about the coffee-making process and taste some of the world-famous Indonesian coffee.
  • Overnight Camping and Bonfire: A fun activity that lets you experience the outdoors by camping overnight in the wilderness and enjoying a bonfire under the stars.

Week-Long Itinerary for Bondowoso

Here’s a sample itinerary to help you plan your week-long trip to Bondowoso:

Day Activities
Day 1 Arrive in Bondowoso, check into your accommodation, and explore the town’s cultural sites like Soekarno Museum and Giri Arjuna Temple.
Day 2 Hike the Mount Raung Summit Trail and enjoy the stunning views of the surrounding mountains and forests.
Day 3 Take a Sukamade Turtle Tour and witness the natural wonder of sea turtles laying eggs on the beach.

Food and Drink Scene of Bondowoso

Bondowoso offers a diverse range of traditional Javanese cuisine and local beverages that will satisfy your taste buds. Here are some must-try dishes and beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Nasi Bebek: Rice topped with flavorful duck, vegetables, and spices.
  • Rawon: A beef soup with a unique black color, made from black nuts.
  • Soto: A chicken soup with coconut milk and turmeric.
  • Gado-gado: A salad made with boiled vegetables, tofu, tempeh, and peanut sauce.
  • Sate: Skewered grilled meat that comes in various varieties like chicken, beef, or goat.

Local Beverages

  • Javanese Coffee: Indonesia is famous for its coffee, and Bondowoso is no exception. Try the local coffee from nearby plantations and coffee shops.
  • Wedang Jahe: A ginger tea with a sweet tangy taste, perfect for cold weather.
  • Es Dawet: A sweet dessert made with coconut milk, palm sugar, and pandan-flavored jellies.

Where to Stay in Bondowoso: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Bondowoso has various accommodation options that cater to different budgets and preferences. Here are some recommended hotels, hostels, and guesthouses:

  • Hotel Lestari: Located in the town center, this hotel provides cozy rooms with air conditioning, Wi-Fi, and an on-site restaurant.
  • Kost Catur Wulan: A budget-friendly hostel with private and shared rooms and basic amenities like Wi-Fi and TV.
  • Kusuma Sari Guesthouse: A charming guesthouse with a traditional Javanese design, spacious rooms, and a beautiful garden.
  • Graha Selaras Hotel: A mid-range hotel with modern facilities like a rooftop bar, a swimming pool, and a gym.

What to Buy in Bondowoso: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Want to take some local products and souvenirs back home? Here are some ideas:

  • Batik Clothes: Traditional Javanese batik is a UNESCO heritage, and you can find various types of batik clothing in selling in shops and markets across Bondowoso.
  • Coffee and Tea: As mentioned before, Bondowoso is home to some excellent Indonesian coffee and tea plantations, so don’t forget to bring some home.
  • Wooden Crafts: Bondowoso is known for its wooden crafts like masks, sculptures, and furniture.
  • Traditional Snacks: Bondowoso and the surrounding areas have a lot of traditional snacks like kemplang, emping, and klepon, which are perfect for taking back home as souvenirs.

Local Culture and Customs of Bondowoso

Bondowoso is a small town that has preserved the traditional Javanese culture, norms, and values. These are some of the customs and rituals that are observed in Bondowoso:

  • Respect for Elders: The Javanese people are known for their respect for elders, and this is also observed in Bondowoso.
  • Traditional Clothing: You can still see people wearing traditional Javanese clothing like batik sarongs and kebayas in Bondowoso.
  • Sego Sambel: Sego Sambel is the most popular local dish in Bondowoso, and it reflects the town’s love for spicy food.
  • Traditional Festivals: Bondowoso also has many traditional festivals that showcase local dances, music, and customs, like the Bondowoso Festival and the Tutur Cup Festival.

Best Time to Visit Bondowoso

The best time to visit Bondowoso is during the dry season, between April to October. During this period, the weather is clear, and the skies are blue, making it perfect for activities like hiking and outdoor adventures. The rainy season, between November to March, can be wet and muddy, but it’s also a good time to visit the region’s lush greenery and waterfall.

Do’s and Don’ts of Bondowoso

Here are some do’s and don’ts for travelers visiting Bondowoso:

Do:

  • Respect local customs and traditions, such as removing your shoes before entering a temple or a house.
  • Try the local food and beverages, especially the spicy dishes and the coffee.
  • Learn some basic Javanese phrases like greetings and thank you, as locals appreciate your efforts to engage with their culture.

Don’t:

  • Wear revealing clothing, especially when visiting religious sites.
  • Take photographs of people without their consent.
  • Litter or disrespect the environment.

Safety and Health

Safety in Bondowoso

Bondowoso is generally safe for travelers, but it’s always recommended to take basic precautions like:

  • Travel with a buddy, especially when hiking or exploring unfamiliar areas.
  • Avoid carrying large amounts of cash or valuables in crowded places.
  • Keep your belongings safe, especially on public transportation and crowded areas.

Health in Bondowoso

Travelers to Bondowoso should take some medical precautions to ensure their health and safety:

  • Make sure your vaccinations are up-to-date, especially for Hepatitis A and B, typhoid, and tetanus.
  • Drink only bottled or sterilized water and avoid street food, especially if you have a sensitive stomach.
  • Bring mosquito repellent and wear proper clothing to protect against mosquito bites, which can transmit diseases like dengue fever and malaria.

Getting to Bondowoso: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Bondowoso

The nearest airport to Bondowoso is Juanda International Airport in Surabaya, which is around 200 kilometers away. From the airport, you can take a taxi or shuttle bus to Bondowoso.

Bus Stations Near Bondowoso

Bondowoso is well-connected to other major cities in Java through buses. The main bus station in Bondowoso is Bondowoso Terminal, which has regular buses to Surabaya, Malang, and other major cities.

Other Options

If you prefer private transportation, you can rent a car or a motorbike in Surabaya or Malang and drive to Bondowoso. Another option is to hire a private driver or a taxi.

How to Get Around in Bondowoso

In Bondowoso, you can get around by:

  • Walking: Bondowoso is a small town, so you can explore most of it on foot.
  • Motorbike or Bicycle Rent: You can rent a motorbike or a bicycle to explore the surrounding areas like Mount Raung, Alas Purwo National Park, and Kalibendo Waterfall.
  • Local Transportation: If you prefer public transportation, angkot (minibuses) are available in Bondowoso, but they are often crowded and uncomfortable.

Where to Go Next from Bondowoso: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Bondowoso is a great base for exploring other nearby destinations and excursions in East Java:

  • Mount Bromo: A famous volcanic mountain that is a must-visit for nature lovers and adventurers.
  • Ijen Crater: An active crater lake that is popular for its blue flames.
  • Malang: A vibrant city that is famous for its Dutch colonial architecture, street food, and art scene.
  • Surabaya: The second-largest city in Indonesia, which offers a mix of modern and traditional attractions like shopping malls, museums, and historic sites.

Conclusion

Bondowoso is a hidden gem in East Java that offers a unique blend of natural beauty, cultural richness, and traditional lifestyle. With this travel guide, you can plan your trip to Bondowoso and explore all that it has to offer. Pack your bags, bring a sense of adventure, and experience the wonder of Bondowoso for yourself.
