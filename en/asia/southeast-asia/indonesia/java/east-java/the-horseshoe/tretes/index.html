Introduction to Tretes

Tretes is a small town located in the Horseshoe region of East Java, Indonesia. The town is nestled at the foothills of the Arjuno-Welirang mountain range, providing awe-inspiring views and refreshing natural beauty. Tretes serves as a popular weekend getaway for locals and a tranquil escape from the bustling cities of East Java. In this comprehensive travel guide, we will help you explore and experience Tretes in the best way possible.

List of Places to Visit in Tretes

Tretes may be a small town, but it is rich with diverse attractions that cater to everyone’s interests. We have grouped the attractions into two categories: Culturally Important Attractions and Natural Attractions of Tretes.

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

1. Taman Dayu Golf Course and Resort

Taman Dayu is a world-class golf course and resort located only 15 minutes from Tretes. With its well-manicured greens, stunning views, and luxurious amenities, Taman Dayu is a must-visit destination for golf enthusiasts and travelers seeking high-end accommodations.

2. Trowulan Temple

Trowulan Temple is a historical site located about an hour drive from Tretes. The temple dates back to the Majapahit Kingdom, which ruled Indonesia from the 13th to 16th century. The temple ruins provide a glimpse into Indonesia’s rich history and cultural heritage.

Natural Attractions of Tretes: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

1. Mount Arjuno

Mount Arjuno stands at 3,339 meters above sea level and is one of the highest peaks in East Java. It is a popular hiking destination, and the trek takes approximately 7-8 hours. The journey to the top is challenging, but the stunning views are worth it.

2. Tretes Hidden Valley

Tretes Hidden Valley is a natural park filled with lush greenery, streams, and waterfalls. Visitors can relax in its natural hot springs or take a dip in the cool waters of the refreshing mountain streams.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Tretes

Tretes is a perfect destination for outdoor enthusiasts who love hiking and biking. Some of the popular hiking and biking routes in Tretes are:

  • Mount Arjuno hiking trail
  • Sumber Brantas waterfall trek
  • Kaliandra Adventure Park biking trails

Best Things to Do in Tretes: Activities and Tours

The following are the best things to do in Tretes that will cater to various interests:

  • Relaxing in hot springs

Food and Drink Scene of Tretes

Tretes has excellent options for foodies, and the following are the must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Rawon Tretes: A beef soup with black nuts that give a unique flavor and aroma.
  • Bakso Tretes: A meatball soup that uses locally sourced meat and has an authentic Indonesian flavor.

Local Beverages

  • Wedang Uwuh: A drink made of boiled herbs and spices that provides comfort and relief.
  • Es Kacang Merah: A refreshing drink made of red beans, milk, and shaved ice.

Where to Stay in Tretes: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Here are the best options for accommodation in Tretes:

  • The Singhasari Resort: A luxurious resort situated on a hilltop that provides a breathtaking view of the surrounding mountains.
  • Kaliandra Sejati Eco Resort and Farm: An eco-resort that provides a peaceful and serene environment to relax and escape the bustling city life.
  • Sapadia Hotel Tretes: A budget-friendly option that provides comfortable accommodation near the top attractions of Tretes.

What to Buy in Tretes: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Local Handicrafts: Tretes offers a variety of handicrafts, such as batik, wooden carvings, and pottery.
  • Jamu: A herbal mixture that serves as a natural remedy for various ailments.
  • Local Snacks: Savor Tretes flavor with the local snacks, such as Otap, Bingka Ambon, Karedok, and Pempek.

Local Culture and Customs of Tretes

Tretes has a rich cultural heritage, and the locals are proud of their traditions and lifestyle. It is essential to respect their customs and traditions, such as dressing modestly, avoiding public displays of affection, and not touching someone’s head.

Best Time to Visit Tretes

The best time to visit Tretes is during the dry season, which spans from May to September. The weather is pleasant, and the sky is clear, providing an ideal environment for outdoor activities.

Do’s and Don’ts of Tretes

Do:

  • Respect the local customs and traditions
  • Dress modestly
  • Try the local food and beverages

Don’t:

  • Engage in public displays of affection
  • Touch someone’s head
  • Disrespect the nature and environment of Tretes

Safety and Health

Safety in Tretes

Tretes is a relatively safe town, but tourists should take precautions to avoid any unfortunate incidents. It is advisable to keep your valuables safe and be vigilant at night.

Health in Tretes

It is recommended to get vaccinated for common illnesses and diseases before traveling to Tretes. Tourists should drink bottled water and avoid tap water. They should also carry sunscreen, insect repellent, and first-aid kits to be prepared for any emergencies.

Getting to Tretes: Transportation Options and Logistics

Tretes can be reached via various modes of transportation, and the following are the details:

Airports Near Tretes

The nearest airport is Juanda International Airport in Surabaya, which is about a two-hour drive from Tretes.

Bus Stations Near Tretes

The nearest bus station is in Surabaya, which connects Tretes with other cities and towns of East Java.

Other Options

Tourists can also rent a car or hire a private driver to reach Tretes or explore the surrounding areas.

How to Get Around in Tretes

The best way to explore Tretes is by renting a motorcycle or car. Tourists can also hire a taxi or a private driver for a more comfortable and hassle-free experience.

Conclusion

Tretes is a hidden gem of East Java that provides a blend of natural beauty, cultural heritage, and outdoor adventure. If you’re planning to visit East Java, make sure to include Tretes in your itinerary and experience its charm and magic.
