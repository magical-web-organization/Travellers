Travel Guide to Surabaya: Explore the City of Heroes

Nestled in the heart of East Java, Surabaya is a vibrant city that can be visited all year round. It is not only East Java’s largest city, but also the second-largest city in the country after Jakarta. Known as the City of Heroes, Surabaya is rich in culture, history, and natural attractions that will leave you spellbound. Whether you are interested in exploring museums, hiking trails, or trying the local cuisine, Surabaya has something in store for you. This comprehensive travel guide will help you plan your trip to Surabaya and make the most out of your visit.

Local Culture and Customs of Surabaya

Surabaya is a culturally rich city with its own unique customs and traditions. Here are some cultural insights to help you better understand this vibrant city:

  • The locals in Surabaya speak Bahasa Indonesia, but many of them also speak Javanese.
  • The city has a strong Islamic influence, and many locals are devout Muslims.
  • The most common greeting in Surabaya is “Selamat Pagi/Siang/Sore/Malam,” which means “Good Morning/Afternoon/Evening/Night” respectively.

Best Time to Visit Surabaya

Surabaya can be visited all year round, but the best time to visit is during the dry season from May to September. The weather during this time is mostly sunny, making it the perfect time for outdoor activities and exploring the city.

Do’s and Don’ts of Surabaya

Do:

  • Respect the local customs and traditions.
  • Learn some basic Indonesian phrases to communicate with the locals.
  • Dress modestly, especially when visiting religious sites.

Don’t:

  • Haggle too aggressively when shopping from street vendors.
  • Drink tap water or eat uncooked food from street vendors.
  • Touch people’s heads or point your feet at them.

Safety and Health

Safety in Surabaya

Surabaya is generally a safe city for travelers, but it’s always better to exercise caution and be aware of your surroundings. Here are some safety tips to keep in mind:

  • Use common sense when walking at night and avoid poorly lit areas.
  • Be cautious when using public transportation, as pickpocketing is common.
  • Keep a close eye on your belongings at all times.

Health in Surabaya

Travelers to Surabaya should take precautions to ensure they stay healthy during their trip. Here are some health tips to keep in mind:

  • Drink plenty of water to stay hydrated.
  • Use mosquito repellent to avoid mosquito-borne diseases.
  • Use sunblock to protect yourself from the sun’s harmful rays.

Getting to Surabaya: Transportation Options and Logistics

Surabaya is easily accessible by air, bus, and train. Here are some transportation options to get to Surabaya:

Airports Near Surabaya

  • Juanda International Airport

Bus Stations Near Surabaya

  • Terminal Purabaya
  • Terminal Bungurasih

Other Options

  • Train - Surabaya is connected by train to other major cities in Java.
  • Car - It is possible to hire a car and driver, but be aware of the traffic and driving conditions.

How to Get Around in Surabaya

Surabaya has an extensive public transportation network, including buses, taxis, Grab, and Gojek. The most convenient way to get around the city is by using Grab or Gojek. Taxis are also widely available, but make sure to use only official taxis and agree on the price before your trip.

Conclusion

Surabaya is a fascinating city that offers something for every traveler. With this comprehensive travel guide, you’ll be able to plan your trip and make the most out of your visit. Whether you’re exploring the city’s cultural landmarks, embarking on an outdoor adventure, or indulging in delicious Indonesian cuisine, Surabaya promises an unforgettable experience.
