Introduction to Sidoarjo

Located in the Surabaya Metropolitan Area of East Java, Indonesia, Sidoarjo is a charming city that offers a mix of cultural attractions and natural wonders. The city is famous for its shrimp crackers, so it is also called “Kota Asri” or the “Beautiful City of Shrimp Crackers.” It is a perfect destination for travelers who want to explore Javanese culture.

List of Places to Visit in Sidoarjo

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Lapindo Mudflow Museum: This museum showcases the impact of Lapindo mud flow disaster, which occurred in Sidoarjo in 2006. The museum has interactive exhibits, videos, and photos, to educate visitors on the disaster and its aftermath.
  • Sari Temple: This is one of the oldest temples in East Java and is a must-visit for those interested in Javanese architecture and culture.
  • Sidoarjo Grand Mosque: This mosque was built in the traditional Javanese style and is a great place to learn about Islamic culture in Indonesia.

Natural Attractions of Sidoarjo: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Air Mancur Taman Mayangkara: This is a beautiful park with a lake, a fountain, and a jogging track. It’s a perfect place for a picnic or to have a relaxing afternoon.
  • Kampoeng Potean: This traditional village is a perfect example of the rural lifestyle of Javanese people. It’s a great place to learn about Javanese culture and traditions.
  • Kali Porong: This river is a popular spot for white water rafting, kayaking, and fishing.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Sidoarjo

  • Bukit Tengger: This hill is a great place to hike and see the panoramic view of Sidoarjo. The hike to the top takes less than an hour, but it’s a bit steep.
  • Sidoarjo Riverbanks: This route is perfect for leisure biking. The beautiful view of the river and the green paddy fields will make you forget about the time.

Best Things to Do in Sidoarjo: Activities and Tours

  • Shrimp Crackers Factory Tour: Sidoarjo is famous for its shrimp crackers, so taking a tour of the factory is a must-do activity. You can learn how the crackers are made and even try your hand at making them.
  • Sidoarjo Culinary Tour: Sampling the local food is a must-do activity when visiting Sidoarjo. Take a culinary tour to try some of the local delicacies, such as “lontong sayur”, “tahu campur”, and “rojak cingur”.
  • Lapindo Mudflow Tour: Take a guided tour of the Lapindo mudflow disaster site to learn about the disaster and its impact on the local community.

Food and Drink Scene of Sidoarjo

Must-Try Dishes

  • Lontong Sayur: This is a popular breakfast food in Sidoarjo, consisting of rice cakes, mixed vegetables, and peanut sauce.
  • Tahu Campur: A mix of fried tofu, noodles, bean sprouts, and vegetables. It’s usually served with thick peanut sauce.
  • Rujak Cingur: This is a traditional salad dish consisting of sliced vegetables, shrimp, and cow snout.

Local Beverages

  • Es Dawet: A sweet iced drink made from coconut milk, pandanus juice, and sugar.
  • Wedang Uwuh: A hot herbal drink made from ginger, lemongrass, cloves, and cinnamon.

Where to Stay in Sidoarjo: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Swiss-Belinn Manyar: A four-star hotel that offers modern rooms, a fitness center, and an outdoor pool.
  • Krowi Inn: A budget-friendly guesthouse located in the city center. It offers clean and comfortable rooms and free Wi-Fi.
  • MaxOne Hotel: A three-star hotel that offers quirky and colorful rooms, a restaurant, and a bar.

What to Buy in Sidoarjo: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Shrimp Crackers: Sidoarjo is famous for its shrimp crackers, so buying a pack of these crackers is a must.
  • Bakpia: A sweet pastry filled with mung bean paste that is a popular snack in Sidoarjo.
  • Batik: This traditional Javanese textile is a popular souvenir and is available in various designs and colors.

Local Culture and Customs of Sidoarjo

  • Batik: Batik is an important part of Javanese culture, and in Sidoarjo, you can see batik designs in traditional clothing, accessories, and artwork.
  • Respect for elders: In Javanese culture, elders are highly respected, so it’s important to show respect and courtesy to them.
  • Islamic Culture: Sidoarjo has a strong Islamic culture, so it’s important to dress modestly and to respect Islamic customs and practices.

Best Time to Visit Sidoarjo

The best time to visit Sidoarjo is during the dry season, from April to October. The weather during this time is usually warm and sunny, perfect for outdoor activities and sightseeing.

Do’s and Don’ts of Sidoarjo

Do:

  • Dress modestly when visiting religious places.
  • Try the local food and drinks.
  • Respect the local customs and traditions.

Don’t:

  • Wear revealing clothing in public places.
  • Disrespect elders or ignore their advice.
  • Publicly display affection with a partner.

Safety and Health

Safety in Sidoarjo

Sidoarjo is generally a safe city, but it’s important to take common sense precautions such as keeping your valuables safe and not walking alone at night.

Health in Sidoarjo

It’s advisable to drink only bottled water in Sidoarjo, and to bring along any necessary medication that you might need during your visit.

Getting to Sidoarjo: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Sidoarjo

  • Juanda International Airport: This airport is located in Surabaya and is the nearest airport to Sidoarjo. It’s approximately 30 minutes away by car.

Bus Stations Near Sidoarjo

  • Pondok Jati Bus Station: This bus station serves destinations throughout Java, including Sidoarjo.
  • Indrapura Bus Station: This bus station is located in the city center of Sidoarjo and serves destinations throughout Java.

Other Options

  • Car rental: Renting a car is a good option if you want to explore the surrounding areas of Sidoarjo independently.
  • Taxi: Taxis are readily available in Sidoarjo and are a convenient option for getting around the city.

How to Get Around in Sidoarjo

Most of the attractions in Sidoarjo are accessible by car or motorbike. Taxis and motorbike taxis are readily available, but it’s important to agree on a price before beginning your journey.

Where to Go Next from Sidoarjo: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Surabaya: Indonesia’s second-largest city, Surabaya is famous for its history, shopping, and night markets.
  • Mount Bromo: A famous volcano located in East Java, Mount Bromo is a popular destination for hikers and outdoor enthusiasts.
  • Trowulan: The former capital of the Majapahit Empire, Trowulan is an important archaeological site and is a great place to learn about Javanese history and culture.

Conclusion

Sidoarjo is a charming city that offers a mix of cultural attractions and natural wonders. From exploring the Lapindo mudflow site to hiking Bukit Tengger, there is something for everyone in Sidoarjo. Don’t forget to try the local shrimp crackers, and take a culinary tour to experience the full flavor of Javanese cuisine. With our comprehensive guide, you’ll have everything you need to plan an unforgettable trip to Sidoarjo, East Java, Indonesia.
