Introduction to Trawas

Trawas is a charming small town located in Surabaya Metropolitan Area, in East Java, Indonesia. Known for its scenic natural beauty and cultural landmarks, Trawas is a popular destination for nature enthusiasts and history buffs alike. The town’s strategic location between Surabaya and Malang also makes it a perfect base for exploring the region.

This comprehensive guide will give you all the information you need to plan an unforgettable visit to Trawas. From must-see attractions to insider tips, we’ve got you covered.

List of Places to Visit in Trawas

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Trawas has a rich cultural heritage that is reflected in its many museums and historical landmarks. Here are some places to add to your itinerary:

Trowulan Museum

The Trowulan Museum is a must-visit for anyone interested in Javanese history. Located in Mojokerto, about 30-minutes drive from Trawas, this museum displays a stunning collection of ancient artifacts from the Majapahit Empire, which once ruled Java.

Singosari Temple

The Singosari Temple is a beautiful Hindu temple located in Malang, about an hour’s drive from Trawas. Built in the 13th century, this temple is famous for its intricate carvings and stunning architecture.

Lawang Sewu

Lawang Sewu, located in Semarang, about 3-hour drive from Trawas, is a colonial-era building that served as a railway office in the Dutch colonial period. Today, it is a popular landmark and museum, with exhibits that showcase the building’s history.

Natural Attractions of Trawas: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Trawas is known for its lush greenery and breathtaking natural landscapes. Here are some places to explore in Trawas:

Mount Penanggungan

Mount Penanggungan is a 3,000-foot volcano located in the heart of Trawas. This majestic mountain is a popular destination for hikers and offers stunning views of the surrounding landscape.

Taman Dayu

Taman Dayu is a world-class golf resort located on the slopes of Mount Arjuno. In addition to golf, the resort offers a range of outdoor activities, such as mountain biking, hiking, and horseback riding.

Tretes Waterfall

Tretes Waterfall is a hidden gem that is located just a short drive from Trawas. Nestled in the lush forests of the region, this waterfall offers a refreshing escape from the city.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Trawas

Trawas is a hiker’s paradise, offering miles of scenic trails that wind through lush forests and ascend steep mountain peaks. Here are some popular hiking and biking routes in Trawas:

  • Mount Penanggungan
  • Mount Arjuno
  • Gunung Welirang
  • Taman Dayu Resort

Best Things to Do in Trawas: Activities and Tours

Trawas offers a range of activities and tours for visitors of all ages. Here are some of the top things to do in Trawas:

  • Enjoy a round of golf at Taman Dayu Resort
  • Go hiking or mountain biking on the many trails around Trawas
  • Take a scenic drive through the picturesque countryside
  • Explore the town’s cultural landmarks, such as the Trowulan Museum and Singosari Temple

Week-Long Itinerary for Trawas

Day 1: Visit the Trowulan Museum in Mojokerto, followed by a trip to the nearby Singosari Temple Day 2: Explore the scenic trails of Mount Penanggungan Day 3: Take a day trip to Lawang Sewu in Semarang Day 4: Enjoy a round of golf at Taman Dayu Resort Day 5: Go hiking on Mount Arjuno Day 6: Visit the picturesque Tretes Waterfall Day 7: Take a scenic drive through the countryside and explore the town’s cultural landmarks

Food and Drink Scene of Trawas

Must-Try Dishes

Trawas boasts a rich culinary scene, thanks to its proximity to Surabaya and Malang. Here are some dishes you must try:

  • Sate Ayam: Grilled chicken skewers served with peanut sauce
  • Rawon: A hearty beef soup made with black nuts, served with rice
  • Rujak Cingur: A spicy fruit salad served with cow’s nose cartilage
  • Bakso Malang: Meatball soup, a local specialty

Local Beverages

  • Es Cendol: A sweet, creamy dessert made with coconut milk, green jelly, and palm sugar
  • Es Teler: A refreshing fruit cocktail made with shaved ice, coconut milk, and a combination of fruits such as avocado, jackfruit and coconut.
  • Kopi Tapanuli: A strong and sweet coffee, brewed with sugar and milk

Where to Stay in Trawas: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Trawas offers a variety of accommodation options for travelers, ranging from budget-friendly hostels to luxurious resorts. Here are some top choices:

  • Trawas Hotel and Restaurant: One of the town’s top-rated hotels, with clean and comfortable rooms and a great restaurant that serves local specialties.
  • Kanaan Hotel Trawas: A five-star hotel with luxurious amenities such as an outdoor pool, a spa, and a fine-dining restaurant.
  • Belana Hotel: A mid-range hotel with well-equipped rooms and a lovely garden area.

What to Buy in Trawas: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Trawas is known for its handicrafts and traditional products. Here are some items to consider bringing home:

  • Batik fabric: Colorful, handmade fabrics that are unique to Java
  • Kris: A ceremonial dagger with a unique design, often crafted by local artisans
  • Local snacks: Trawas is known for its delicious snacks such as shrimp crackers and banana chips

Local Culture and Customs of Trawas

Trawas is a friendly and welcoming town, with a deeply ingrained cultural heritage. Visitors are expected to respect local customs and traditions, such as:

  • Removing shoes before entering a temple or mosque
  • Dressing modestly when visiting cultural landmarks
  • Greeting locals with a smile and a nod

Best Time to Visit Trawas

The best time to visit Trawas is during the dry season, which typically runs from April to October. The weather is cool and comfortable during this time, making it ideal for outdoor activities and sightseeing.

Do’s and Don’ts of Trawas

Do:

  • Try the local cuisine
  • Be respectful of local customs and traditions
  • Go hiking on the scenic trails

Don’t:

  • litter
  • Wear revealing clothing when visiting cultural landmarks
  • Disrespect local customs

Safety and Health

Safety in Trawas

Trawas is generally a safe town, but visitors should be cautious when traveling alone, especially at night. Keep valuables out of sight and avoid walking in unlit areas after dark.

Health in Trawas

It is advisable to bring insect repellent, as there are mosquitoes in the area. Drinking bottled water is recommended, as the tap water is not always safe to drink.

Getting to Trawas: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Trawas

The nearest airport to Trawas is Juanda International Airport in Surabaya, which is about an hour’s drive away.

Bus Stations Near Trawas

The public bus is the cheapest way to travel to Trawas, but it can be crowded and uncomfortable. The nearest bus station is in Surabaya, from where you can take a taxi or hire a car to Trawas.

Other Options

Private cars or tours are also available from Surabaya or Malang. Renting a car is also an option, but be aware that traffic in Java can be chaotic.

How to Get Around in Trawas

The best way to get around Trawas is by hired car or motorbike. Trawas is a small town, and most major attractions are within easy driving distance.

Where to Go Next from Trawas: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Trawas is a great base for exploring the surrounding region. Here are some nearby destinations and excursions to consider:

  • Malang: A vibrant university town with a rich artistic scene and beautiful colonial architecture.
  • Surabaya: The second-biggest city in Indonesia, Surabaya is a bustling metropolis that boasts great food, shopping, and nightlife.
  • Mount Bromo: One of the most iconic destinations in Indonesia, Mount Bromo is an active volcano that attracts hikers and adventurers from around the world.

Conclusion

Trawas is a hidden gem that offers visitors a unique blend of natural beauty and cultural heritage. Whether you’re exploring its scenic trails, sampling its delicious cuisine, or immersing yourself in Javanese history, Trawas is sure to leave a lasting impression. With this comprehensive guide, you’re ready to plan the ultimate Trawas adventure.
