Introduction to Bawean

Bawean, a remote island located in the Java Sea, is a hidden gem of East Java, Indonesia, situated 130 km north of Gresik. It is a land of tropical beauty with pristine beaches, dense forests, and scenic mountains. The unique culture, friendly locals, and abundance of natural resources make Bawean a must-visit destination on your next travel bucket list.

List of Places to Visit in Bawean

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Bawean Island has rich culture and history that you can learn about by visiting the following places:

  • Museum Di Bale Pemuda: This museum is dedicated to the history of Bawean Island, showcasing a collection of cultural objects from the past, including antique wooden statues and traditional textiles.
  • Forrest Lasidang: Located in Sangkapura, this forest is a historical site where the kingdom of Bawean was once situated. You can explore the old fort, ancient trees, and witness the unique architecture of traditional Baweanese houses.
  • Candi Sumber Anyar: This 14th-century temple is situated in Tambak Village. It is an ancient site that was once used for meditation, and it features beautiful carvings and decorations with enormous historical significance.

Natural Attractions of Bawean: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Bawean Island is a must-visit destination for nature lovers. Here are some of the top natural attractions you wouldn’t want to miss, including:

  • Pulau Noko: This small island located off the coast of Bawean is sensational. You will be enchanted by the crystal clear water and white sand beach. You can enjoy swimming, snorkeling, diving, and sunbathing while enjoying the beautiful scenery around the island.
  • Gua Batu Kapal: This limestone cave is situated in Sangkapura. It features a unique natural rock formation that resembles a ship. You can explore breathtaking rock formations, stalactites, and stalagmites here.
  • Taman Nasional Bawean: This national park protects the biodiversity of Bawean Island. It is home to unique animal species like Bawean deer, Bawean monkey, and many rare bird species. You can explore the park by taking a guided tour or trekking the trails yourself.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Bawean

Bawean Island is full of natural beauty and scenic views that you can explore through hiking and biking routes. Here are some of the best routes to explore:

  • Mount Sangkapura: This is the highest mountain on Bawean Island with its summit reaching 1,950 meters above sea level. The trek to the top is challenging but rewarding. You can enjoy bird’s eye views of the island and the Java Sea from the top.
  • Mount Batu Lawang: This mountain is situated on the north coast of Bawean with a height of 640 meters above sea level. You can enjoy beautiful views of the coastline and the surrounding islands from the top.
  • Biking around the Island: You can explore Bawean Island on a bicycle. There are a variety of routes you can take, and depending on what you are interested in, you can easily rent a bike and explore the island on a self-guided tour.

Best Things to Do in Bawean: Activities and Tours

There are plenty of things to do on Bawean Island, and here are some of the best activities you can enjoy:

  • Snorkeling and Diving: Bawean is home to coral reefs and tropical fish species that you can explore through snorkeling or diving. You can take a guided tour to witness the magical underwater world.
  • Fishing: Bawean Island is well-known for fishing. You can join the locals in fishing by renting a boat with a guide to catch fish like yellowfin tuna, mahi-mahi, and more.
  • Leisurely walks: Bawean Island is perfect for leisurely walks along the beaches and hiking trails. You can find hidden views and soak in the island culture while strolling around.

Week-Long Itinerary for Bawean

Here is a seven-day itinerary for exploring Bawean Island:

Day Activity
1 Arrive in Bawean, explore Sangkapura and its historical landmarks
2 Trek to the peak of Mount Sangkapura, and explore traditional Baweanese houses
3 Snorkel or dive in the coral reefs of Gili Island
4 Rent a bike and explore Bawean Island’s hidden beaches and surrounding villages
5 Take a boat tour to the dramatic limestone cliffs of Watu Kodok and the fishing village of Laccar
6 Enjoy a fishing trip with a local guide and cook your catch for dinner.
7 Visit the wildlife at the Bawean National Park before departing Bawean.

Food and Drink Scene of Bawean

Must-Try Dishes

Bawean cuisine offers an abundant variety of flavors with unique ingredients like coconut milk, turmeric, and lemongrass. Some must-try dishes include:

  • Sate Bawean: Bawean-style satay consists of marinated meat that is skewered and grilled over charcoal. It is served with peanut sauce or soy sauce and is a popular street food option.
  • Tahu Telor: This dish consists of a fried bean cake or tofu with an egg, served with sweet soy sauce and peanut sauce. It is a popular snack or appetizer.
  • Lopper: A traditional Baweanese breakfast dish that consists of a savory pancake made with coconut milk, turmeric, and shrimp or crabmeat, served with a chili or fish sauce.

Local Beverages

Bawean Island offers a variety of beverages, including fresh coconut water, tea, and local alcohol. Try some of the local beverages, such as:

  • Baweanese Coffee: This coffee is locally grown and has a unique aroma, body, and flavor that will delight every coffee lover.
  • Arak: This is a palm wine alcoholic drink that you can find in Bawean. It has a unique flavor and is perfect for sipping in the warm Bawean nights.

Where to Stay in Bawean: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Bawean Island provides accommodation options for all budgets, from budget-friendly hostels to high-end resorts. Here are some of the best accommodation options to choose from:

  • The Lida Hotel Bawean: A modern and comfortable hotel that provides stunning ocean views, located near Bawean Port.
  • Bawean Beach Resort: A luxurious beach resort with traditional Baweanese design and exceptional hospitality services.
  • B&B Kaali: A budget-friendly guesthouse located in Sangkapura, perfect for backpackers and budget travel.

What to Buy in Bawean: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Bawean Island is famous for its handicrafts, traditional herbs, and seafood. Here are some souvenirs and local products you can consider bringing back home:

  • Batik Bawean: Traditional Bawean batik is famous for its unique design and color scheme. You can find batik at the local market in Sangkapura and many other towns in Bawean.
  • Fish crackers: Bawean Island is famous for producing fish crackers (krupuk ikan). It is a perfect traditional snack to bring back home.
  • Traditional Herbs: Bawean Island has a wide variety of traditional herbs that are known for its health benefits. You can find them at local markets near traditional medicine sellers.

Local Culture and Customs of Bawean

Baweanese culture is a mixture of various cultures, including Chinese, Malay, and Javanese. The people of Bawean follow the Islamic religion, and they have unique customs and traditions, including:

  • Selamatan: A traditional religious ceremony done to celebrate various events, including births, weddings, and homecomings.
  • Sekaten: A week-long festival that celebrates the birthday of Prophet Muhammad, where traditional foods and music are served.
  • Nyadran Ritual: A cultural ritual that is held annually, where locals pay a visit to their ancestors’ graves and offer offerings.

Best Time to Visit Bawean

The best time to visit Bawean is between April to September when the weather is dry and sunny. It is also a perfect time to explore the beaches, go snorkeling and diving, and enjoy the flora and fauna of the island. The rainy season is between October to March, and it is still possible to visit during this time, but outdoor activities may be limited.

Do’s and Don’ts of Bawean

Do:

  • Respect the local culture and customs.
  • Dress modestly when visiting religious sites.
  • Always ask for permission before taking photographs of local people.

Don’t:

  • Engage in public displays of affection.
  • Consume alcohol in public places.
  • Disrespect the local customs, including chewing betel nut.

Safety and Health

Safety in Bawean

Bawean Island is generally a safe place to visit, but it is essential to take precautions to avoid unwanted incidents. Always stay alert and follow simple safety guidelines:

  • Keep your valuables secure and carry them with you.
  • Don’t wander around the island after dark.
  • Be careful when swimming in the sea and follow safety guidelines.

Health in Bawean

Bawean Island has a limited healthcare system, and it is recommended to bring a first aid kit and any necessary medication. Always drink bottled water to avoid getting ill. Mosquito-borne illnesses are also prevalent, and it is essential to use insect repellent to avoid getting bitten.

Getting to Bawean: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Bawean

The nearest airport to Bawean Island is Juanda International Airport in Surabaya, which is the capital city of East Java. From Surabaya, you can take a bus, car, or taxi to Gresik Seaport, and then take a ferry to Bawean Island.

Bus Stations Near Bawean

You can also take a bus from Surabaya or Gresik to Sangkapura, the capital city of Bawean Island.

Other Options

Another way to get to Bawean Island is by private car or rental car, but it can be more challenging for non-locals to navigate the roads.

How to Get Around in Bawean

One of the best ways to get around Bawean Island is by renting a motorcycle or bicycle, which you can find at rental shops in Sangkapura. Alternatively, you can also hire a private car or use public transportation, including angkot (minibus) and ojek (motorcycle taxi).

Where to Go Next from Bawean: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Bawean Island is a perfect destination for those who love nature and adventure. Here are some nearby destinations and excursions to complement your adventures:

  • Mount Bromo: One of Indonesia’s most famous natural attractions, located in East Java.
  • Madura Island: A neighboring island famous for its cultural and traditional heritage.
  • Surabaya: The capital of East Java, a bustling city with diverse experiences to offer.

Conclusion

Bawean Island is an isolated but beautiful destination worth visiting. Whether you are interested in culture and history, nature adventures, or leisurely walks and scenic views, Bawean Island caters to all.
