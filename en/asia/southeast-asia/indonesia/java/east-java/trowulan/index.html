Introduction to Trowulan

Trowulan is a small town located in Mojokerto Regency, East Java, Indonesia. It is renowned for its rich history and ancient ruins of the Majapahit Kingdom that once ruled the region. The town is also surrounded by lush green fields, making it a perfect destination for nature lovers.

List of Places to Visit in Trowulan

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Majapahit Museum

The Majapahit Museum is a must-visit for anyone interested in the rich history of the Majapahit Kingdom. The museum houses a collection of artifacts and relics from the era, including ancient coins, ceramics, stone sculptures, and weapons.

Candi Tikus

Candi Tikus is a subterranean temple complex that served as the royal bathing place during the Majapahit era. The structure, built in the shape of a rat, was discovered in the 20th century and has since undergone extensive restoration.

Bajang Ratu

Bajang Ratu is a charming historical site known for its intricate stone carvings and Hindu-style architecture. The site is believed to have served as the entrance gate to a palace built by the Majapahit rulers.

Natural Attractions of Trowulan: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Wringin Lawang Beach

Wringin Lawang Beach is a serene and secluded spot surrounded by lush greenery. Visitors can enjoy swimming, sunbathing, and exploring the nearby caves and cliffs.

Mount Penanggungan

Mount Penanggungan is a dormant volcano located near Trowulan. The mountain is renowned for its scenic hiking trails, which offer stunning views of the surrounding countryside.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Trowulan

Mount Penanggungan Trail

The Mount Penanggungan Trail is a challenging trek that takes hikers through lush forests and past ancient temples. The trail offers spectacular views of the surrounding hills and mountains.

Trowulan Cycling Tour

The Trowulan Cycling Tour is a great way to explore the town and its surrounding countryside. The tour takes riders through rice paddies, small villages, and past historical sites.

Best Things to Do in Trowulan: Activities and Tours

Majapahit Kingdom Tour

The Majapahit Kingdom Tour is a guided tour that takes visitors through the historical sites and relics of the Majapahit era. The tour includes visits to the Majapahit Museum, Candi Tikus, and Bajang Ratu.

Rice Field Trekking

Rice Field Trekking is a unique Trowulan experience that takes visitors through the lush green rice paddies of the surrounding countryside. Visitors can see firsthand the traditional farming methods used by the local farmers.

Food and Drink Scene of Trowulan

Must-Try Dishes

Soto Trowulan

Soto Trowulan is a local specialty made with chicken broth, shredded chicken, and noodles. The dish is served with a variety of traditional herbs and spices.

Tahu Campur

Tahu Campur is a delicious street food made with tofu, noodles, shrimp, and soy sauce. The dish is topped with crunchy crackers and served with a spicy peanut sauce.

Local Beverages

Wedang Uwuh

Wedang Uwuh is a hot herbal drink made with a variety of traditional Javanese herbs and spices. The drink is believed to have many health benefits, including boosting the immune system and aiding digestion.

Es Campur

Es Campur is a refreshing iced dessert made with a variety of fruit, jelly, and condensed milk. The dessert is perfect for a hot day in Trowulan.

Where to Stay in Trowulan: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Hotel Tugu

Hotel Tugu is a luxurious hotel located in the heart of Trowulan. The hotel offers spacious rooms, a swimming pool, and a restaurant serving traditional Indonesian cuisine.

Omah Heritage Hotel

Omah Heritage Hotel is a boutique hotel located in a traditional Javanese house. The hotel offers comfortable rooms, a garden, and a restaurant serving local and international dishes.

What to Buy in Trowulan: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Batik

Batik is a traditional Indonesian fabric that is often used to make clothing and accessories. Visitors can shop for unique batik designs at local markets and shops in Trowulan.

Keris

A Keris is a traditional Indonesian dagger that is often used for ceremonial purposes. Visitors can purchase Keris knives from local craftsmen in Trowulan.

Local Culture and Customs of Trowulan

The people of Trowulan are known for their warm hospitality and traditional values. Visitors should respect local customs, such as removing shoes before entering homes or religious sites.

Best Time to Visit Trowulan

The best time to visit Trowulan is between April and October, during the dry season. The town can be busy during the peak tourist season in July and August, so visitors should plan ahead and book accommodations in advance.

Do’s and Don’ts of Trowulan

Do:

  • Dress modestly when visiting religious sites
  • Learn a few basic phrases in Bahasa Indonesia to communicate with the locals
  • Try the local cuisine and delicacies

Don’t:

  • Engage in public displays of affection
  • Disrespect local customs and culture
  • Photograph or touch people without their permission

Safety and Health

Safety in Trowulan

Trowulan is a safe destination for travelers. However, visitors should take precautions like securing their valuables and avoiding unlit areas at night.

Health in Trowulan

Visitors should drink bottled water and avoid tap water. Mosquito repellent is also recommended to prevent mosquito-borne diseases such as dengue fever.

Getting to Trowulan: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Trowulan

The nearest airport to Trowulan is Juanda International Airport, located in Surabaya. From there, visitors can take a taxi or bus to Trowulan.

Bus Stations Near Trowulan

The closest bus station to Trowulan is in Mojokerto. From there, visitors can take a local bus or taxi to Trowulan.

Other Options

Visitors can also hire a private car or motorcycle to travel to Trowulan from nearby cities.

How to Get Around in Trowulan

Visitors can easily get around Trowulan on foot or by bicycle. Motorbike rentals are also available for those who want to explore the surrounding countryside.

Where to Go Next from Trowulan: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Surabaya

Surabaya, the second-largest city in Indonesia, is just a few hours’ drive from Trowulan. The city is known for its colorful history, vibrant night markets, and delicious cuisine.

Malang

Malang is a charming city located in East Java, known for its scenic natural landscapes and colonial architecture. Visitors can hike to Mount Bromo, visit the local tea plantations, or explore the city’s unique art scene.

Conclusion

Trowulan is a hidden gem in East Java that offers visitors a unique glimpse into Indonesia’s rich history and culture. From exploring ancient temples to trekking through rice paddies, there is something for everyone in Trowulan.
