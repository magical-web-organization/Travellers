Introduction to East Java

Located in the eastern part of Java Island, East Java is not only a populous area but also home to a diverse range of natural and cultural attractions. From towering active volcanoes to sandy beaches, from ancient Hindu temples to thriving modern cities, East Java is a smorgasbord of delights for every type of traveler.

List of Places to Visit in East Java

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

East Java has a rich cultural heritage, and several museums and historical places preserve the region’s history and traditions. Here are some of the must-visit attractions:

  • Majapahit Hotel - A colonial-era hotel that has been renovated and converted into a museum showcasing East Java’s history and culture.
  • Trowulan - This ancient capital of the Majapahit Kingdom has numerous temples, palaces, and old buildings which are extremely well-preserved.
  • Surabaya Submarine Monument - A Soviet-built submarine was later converted into a museum in tribute to the sailors who died during the Battle of Surabaya, in which Indonesian naval forces fought against Dutch aggressors.

Natural Attractions of East Java: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

East Java boasts some of the most breathtaking scenery in Indonesia. Here are some of the key natural attractions:

  • Mount Bromo - Arguably the most famous natural attraction in East Java, Mount Bromo offers spectacular views of sunrise and sunset that you can enjoy during a sunrise trek.
  • Ijen Crater - One of the few sulfur mines still in operation in the world. You can take a guided tour and witness the natural phenomena of the blue flames.
  • Madakaripura Waterfall - A 200 meters high waterfall surrounded by steep cliffs and a rocky cave that you can explore.

Hiking/Biking Routes of East Java

East Java offers various hiking and biking routes, depending on your experience and fitness level. Here are some of our favorite routes:

  • Tanjung Papuma Beach - The beach is known for the scenic view and clean air that comes from the Indian Ocean. It’s a popular spot for jogging and cycling in the area.
  • Gunung Arjuna - A good hiking challenge with stunning views of plantations and forests, and ample camping spots available
  • Mount Semeru - Southeast Asia’s most active volcano that tourists can climb.

Best Things to Do in East Java: Activities and Tours

Here are some more ideas to add to your itinerary to help fill your time:

  • Surfing - A popular activity in G-Land Reserve.
  • Culinary Adventure - Try out the rich assortment of traditional dishes like Nasi Rawon or Rujak Cingur in Surabaya.
  • Recreational Parks - Taman Safari Prigen and Bromo Tengger Semeru National Park are popular choices for families and outdoor enthusiasts.
  • Beach lounging and swimming - Populated beaches such as Tamban, Balekambang, and Watu Leter are great for lounging or taking a dip.

Food and Drink Scene of East Java

The cuisine in East Java is a blend of distinct flavors and ethnic groups. Here are some of the most authentic dishes and beverages to try:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Rawon - A beef soup with black-colored broth that comes from the use of keluak nuts.
  • Rujak Cingur - A mixed salad dish with rice cakes, beef, and shrimp, flavored with sweet peanut sauce, and soup made of crushed ginger and keluak.

Local Beverages

  • Wedang Jahe - Ginger root tea is a traditional drink in East Java that can boost your immune system and has warming effects in colder weather.
  • Tuak - An alcoholic drink made from the sap of a palm tree that can be found in various locations throughout East Java.

Where to Stay in East Java: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

East Java caters to all types of tourists with various accommodation options available. Here are some of the best places to stay:

  • Bromo Terrace Hotel - Located near Mount Bromo, this hotel offers beautiful views of nature along the hotel bedrooms.
  • Hotel Santika Premiere Gubeng - A more modern luxury hotel near the heart of Surabaya city.
  • Krowi Inn - Located in the heart of Trowulan, budget-friendly amenities that will suit backpackers.

What to Buy in East Java: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

East Java has many cultural souvenirs to choose from:

  • Batik - Indonesian textile, particularly cotton or silk cloth with a design drawn totally or partly in wax and then dyed, sold in various levels of class and price.
  • Majapahit Souvenirs - Various souvenirs that bear the mark of the ancient Majapahit Kingdom, from handheld fans to embroidered handbags.

Local Culture and Customs of East Java

East Java people are a mix of ethnic groups - predominantly Javanese, then Chinese, Madurese and some smaller tribes. When visiting East Java, it’s important to know what is considered respectful or not.

Key local customs include bowing with your hands clasped in front of you when greeting someone, avoiding spicy food if you can’t handle it, and removing shoes and washing feet before entering a mosque.

Best Time to Visit East Java

The best time to visit East Java is during the dry season, from May to September. During this time, you can enjoy clear skies, ideal hiking weather, and warm temperatures for swim in the beach.

Do’s and Don’ts of East Java

Here are some of the most important things to keep in mind when visiting East Java:

Do:

  • Dress appropriately when visiting sacred spots like temples or mosques.
  • Be respectful to locals- they will appreciate it.
  • Bargain when you shop in markets or street stalls.

Don’t:

  • Don’t consume alcohol in public places as it is not recommended in Indonesia.
  • Don’t litter or leave trash, keep the environment clean at all times.
  • Don’t be too demanding or aggressive.

Safety and Health

Safety in East Java

East Java has a reasonable safety rate for tourists. However, you should keep your eyes on your belongings and avoid wandering in the dark or unfamiliar places.

Health in East Java

It’s advisable to take necessary health precautions while visiting East Java. Bring along insect repellent, sunscreen, anti-diarrhea medication, and any other necessary medications.

Getting to East Java: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near East Java

  • Juanda International Airport - The main gateway for travelers to East Java, located in Surabaya City. Many airlines fly to Juanda International Airport from various locations worldwide.

Bus Stations Near East Java

  • Purabaya Bus Station - The main bus station in Surabaya city that operates nationwide.
  • Terminal Bungurasih - Another main bus station, based on Sidoarjo county and near to Juanda International Airport.

Other Options

  • Train - If you are planning to visit the city of Surabaya, you can utilize Surabaya’s railway station. Regular trains depart from various cites throughout Java, have one train station in Surabaya and split up for various directions.
  • Car Rental - Rental cars are often affordable and offer a considerable level of ease in traveling to unfamiliar places.

How to Get Around in East Java

The most common transportation mode in East Java is by taxi or ride-hailing services like Gojek or Grab. However, for tourists, hiring a taxi or renting a car is highly advisable.

Where to Go Next from East Java: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

If you have more time, here are some nearby destinations worth visiting:

  • Bali - A popular island paradise that with fine beach and cultural traditions, and easily accessible from East Java by a short flight.
  • Yogyakarta - A bustling city on Java Island, well-known for its historical temples, art, and Javanese cuisine.
  • Malang - a town in East Java famous for its cooler weather, well-preserved colonial buildings, and delicious local dishes.

Conclusion

East Java is a region with a lot to offer, providing a fascinating mix of adventure, adventure, and local culture. So go ahead and plan your trip, and experience firsthand the region’s natural and cultural offerings.
