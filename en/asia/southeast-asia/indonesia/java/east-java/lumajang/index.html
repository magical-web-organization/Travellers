Introduction to Lumajang

Lumajang is a picturesque regency located in the southern part of East Java, Indonesia. The regency is known for its natural beauty, with towering mountains, cascading waterfalls, and lush green forests. Lumajang is the perfect destination for travelers seeking adventure, outdoor activities, and cultural experiences.

List of Places to Visit in Lumajang

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Lumajang has a rich history and culture, and there are several museums and historical sites worth visiting:

  • Lumajang Square: Located in the heart of Lumajang, Lumajang Square is a popular spot for locals and tourists alike. The square features a giant statue of Srikandi, a legendary female warrior from the ancient Hindu epic Mahabharata.

  • Candi Kedung Jati: Candi Kedung Jati is an ancient temple dating back to the Majapahit Kingdom in the 14th century. The temple is located in the middle of a forest, and visitors can hike to the top of the temple for breathtaking views of the surrounding area.

  • Museum Lumajang: Museum Lumajang is a small museum dedicated to the history and culture of Lumajang. The museum features exhibits on local traditions, art, and cultural heritage.

Natural Attractions of Lumajang: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Lumajang is home to several natural wonders, including mountains, waterfalls, and forests. Some of the top natural attractions include:

  • Mount Semeru: Mount Semeru is the highest mountain in Java and one of the most popular hiking destinations in Indonesia. Visitors can hike to the top of the mountain for stunning views of the surrounding area.

  • Coban Pelangi Waterfall: Coban Pelangi Waterfall is a beautiful cascade waterfall located in Pronojiwo village. Visitors can swim in the cool, clear water at the base of the waterfall and enjoy the stunning views.

  • Bromo Tengger Semeru National Park: Bromo Tengger Semeru National Park is a must-visit destination in Lumajang. The park is home to several active volcanoes, including Mount Bromo, which visitors can hike to the top of for amazing views.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Lumajang

Lumajang is a hiker’s and biker’s paradise, with several trails and routes to explore:

  • Ranu Pani Hiking Trail: The Ranu Pani Hiking Trail is a challenging but rewarding trek that takes visitors through some of the most beautiful natural landscapes in Lumajang.

  • Candi Kedung Jati Hiking Trail: The Candi Kedung Jati Hiking Trail is an easy to moderate hike that leads to the ancient temple of the same name. Along the way, visitors can see stunning views of the surrounding forests and mountains.

  • Sukamade Beach Cycling Tour: The Sukamade Beach Cycling Tour is a fun and scenic way to explore the beaches and forests of Lumajang. Visitors can bike through rural villages and see local life up close.

Best Things to Do in Lumajang: Activities and Tours

There’s no shortage of things to do in Lumajang, from outdoor adventures to cultural experiences. Some of the top things to do include:

  • Visit the Waterfalls: Lumajang is home to several beautiful waterfalls, including Coban Pelangi Waterfall, Tumpak Sewu Waterfall, and Coban Sewu Waterfall.

  • Climb Mount Semeru: Mount Semeru is the highest mountain in Java and a popular hiking destination. Visitors can hike to the summit for amazing views of the surrounding area.

  • Visit Bromo Tengger Semeru National Park: Bromo Tengger Semeru National Park is a must-visit destination in Lumajang. Visitors can hike to the top of Mount Bromo for stunning views, and explore the park’s other natural attractions.

Food and Drink Scene of Lumajang

Must-Try Dishes

Lumajang is known for its delicious food, and there are several dishes that visitors must try:

  • Soto Lamongan: Soto Lamongan is a traditional Indonesian soup made with chicken broth, meat, and eggs. It’s a popular breakfast dish in Lumajang.

  • Nasi Kuning: Nasi Kuning is a rice dish cooked with turmeric and coconut milk, and served with chicken or beef. It’s a popular lunch option in Lumajang.

  • Gethuk Goreng: Gethuk Goreng is a traditional snack made with cassava, coconut milk, and sugar. It’s a popular dessert in Lumajang.

Local Beverages

Lumajang is known for its refreshing and healthy drinks. Some of the top local beverages include:

  • Es Dawet: Es Dawet is a sweet and creamy drink made with coconut milk, pandan leaves, and palm sugar. It’s a popular drink in Lumajang, especially during hot weather.

  • Wedang Uwuh: Wedang Uwuh is a traditional Javanese herbal drink made with ginger, lemongrass, and other spices. It’s believed to have medicinal properties and is a popular drink in Lumajang.

Where to Stay in Lumajang: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Lumajang has several accommodation options to suit all budgets and preferences:

  • Hotel Semeru Ijen: Hotel Semeru Ijen is a three-star hotel located in the heart of Lumajang, offering comfortable rooms and great amenities.

  • Pondok Indah Guesthouse: Pondok Indah Guesthouse is a budget-friendly guesthouse located in the charming village of Ranuyoso, just a short drive from Lumajang.

  • Hostel D’Java: Hostel D’Java is a stylish and affordable hostel located in the heart of Lumajang, with comfortable dorms and private rooms.

What to Buy in Lumajang: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Visitors to Lumajang can find several unique souvenirs and local products to bring home, such as:

  • Batik Tulungagung: Batik Tulungagung is a traditional Indonesian textile art that originated in Lumajang. Visitors can buy authentic and beautifully designed batik textiles in Lumajang.

  • Biji Salak: Biji Salak is a traditional Indonesian dessert made with sweet potato and coconut milk. Visitors can buy this delicious dessert in Lumajang and take it home as a souvenir.

Local Culture and Customs of Lumajang

Lumajang has a rich cultural heritage, and visitors should be aware of local customs and traditions. Some of the most important customs include:

  • Respect for elders: Respect for elders is an important value in Lumajang. Visitors should always show respect to the elders and ask for their permission before doing anything.

  • Dress modestly: Indonesians are generally conservative, and visitors should dress modestly when in public places.

Best Time to Visit Lumajang

The best time to visit Lumajang is during the dry season, which runs from May to September. During this time, the weather is sunny and clear, making it perfect for outdoor activities.

Do’s and Don’ts of Lumajang

Do:

  • Learn some basic Indonesian: Most locals in Lumajang speak Indonesian, so visitors should try to learn some basic phrases and greetings.

  • Respect local customs: Visitors should respect local customs and traditions, including dress codes and social etiquette.

Don’t:

  • Don’t litter: Visitors should never litter in public places and should dispose of their trash properly.

  • Don’t touch sacred objects: Visitors should refrain from touching or taking anything from sacred objects or sites.

Safety and Health

Safety in Lumajang

Lumajang is considered a safe destination for tourists. However, visitors should take standard precautions such as securing valuables, staying in well-lit areas at night, and being aware of their surroundings.

Health in Lumajang

Visitors to Lumajang should take standard health precautions, such as staying hydrated, protecting themselves from mosquito bites, and wearing sunscreen. Visitors should also consider getting vaccinated before their trip.

Getting to Lumajang: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Lumajang

The closest airport to Lumajang is Abdul Rachman Saleh Airport in Malang, which is approximately 75 kilometers away from Lumajang.

Bus Stations Near Lumajang

Lumajang has several bus stations that connect the city to other destinations in East Java, such as Pasar Turi Bungurasih, and Arjosari terminal.

Other Options

Visitors can also hire a private car, a taxi, or a motorcycle to get to Lumajang.

How to Get Around in Lumajang

Visitors can get around Lumajang by using public transportation, hiring a private car or taxi, or renting a motorcycle. It’s also possible to hire a guide or join a tour group to explore the city and its surroundings.

Where to Go Next from Lumajang: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

There are several nearby destinations and excursions that visitors to Lumajang should consider:

  • Mount Bromo: Mount Bromo is one of the most popular tourist destinations in Indonesia, and it’s located just a few hours away from Lumajang.

  • Malang: Malang is a charming city located north of Lumajang, known for its Dutch colonial architecture, art galleries, and museums.

Conclusion

Lumajang is a must-visit destination in East Java, Indonesia, with its rich culture, natural beauty, and outdoor activities. Visitors to Lumajang will never run out of things to do, from exploring natural wonders to learning about local customs and traditions. Whether you’re traveling solo or with family and friends, Lumajang has something for everyone.
