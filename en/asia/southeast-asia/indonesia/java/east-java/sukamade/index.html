Introduction to Sukamade

Nestled in the eastern part of Java, Indonesia, Sukamade offers a unique and off-the-beaten-path experience for travelers seeking adventure and nature. The village is particularly known for its turtle conservation program and beautiful beaches. In this guide, we’ll share everything you need to know before visiting Sukamade, including the must-visit attractions, cultural experiences, and outdoor adventures.

List of Places to Visit in Sukamade

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Sukamade is not particularly known for its museums or historical landmarks, but there are some culturally important sites you shouldn’t miss:

  • Pancengro Cave: This cave is believed to be used as a sacred site by the local people for hundreds of years. The cave is located in a beautiful forest, and visitors can see ancient inscriptions and carvings on the cave’s walls.

  • Jangkar Waterfall: This beautiful waterfall is located near Sukamade beach and is an ideal place for a picnic. Visitors can enjoy the beautiful scenery and refreshing pool.

Natural Attractions of Sukamade: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Sukamade is a haven for outdoor enthusiasts and nature lovers. Here are the must-visit natural attractions in Sukamade:

  • Sukamade Beach: The main attraction of Sukamade is its beautiful beach. The beach is famous for its turtle conservation program, where travelers can witness the incredible sight of giant turtles laying eggs and hatching. Visitors can also take a stroll on the beach and enjoy the scenic views.

  • Meru Betiri National Park: This vast national park covers an area of around 57,000 hectares and is home to a wide variety of flora and fauna. Visitors can enjoy trekking in the park and spotting rare animals and birds such as Javanese eagle, leopard, and banteng.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Sukamade

Sukamade offers some great hiking and biking routes for those seeking adventure. The national park is the best place to enjoy trekking and biking. Some of the popular trekking and biking trails include:

  • Teluk Hijau Trail: This is one of the most popular hiking trails in Sukamade, which leads to a beautiful beach known as Teluk Hijau. The trail starts from the national park’s entrance and is around 9 km long.

  • Surabrata Mountain Trail: This trail is perfect for more experienced hikers who want a challenge. The trail leads to the top of Surabrata Mountain, and the views from the top are stunning.

Best Things to Do in Sukamade: Activities and Tours

Apart from the natural attractions and outdoor adventures, Sukamade offers many exciting things to do. Here are some of the best activities and tours to try:

  • Turtle Conservation Tour: Don’t miss the chance to witness the incredible sight of giant turtles laying eggs and hatching. The tour starts at night, and visitors can watch the turtles’ process from a safe distance.

  • Campfire and Stargazing: Sukamade’s clear skies offer an ideal opportunity for stargazing. Visitors can enjoy the views while sitting around a campfire.

If you’re planning a week-long trip to Sukamade, here is an itinerary to consider:

Food and Drink Scene of Sukamade

Sukamade’s food culture is influenced by the local Javanese traditions. Here are the must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Sate Klatak: This is a delicious grilled beef or chicken skewer served with spicy sauce and rice.

  • Gudeg: This sweet dish is made of young jackfruit cooked with palm sugar, coconut milk, and spices. It is usually served with rice and chicken.

Local Beverages

  • Wedang Jahe: This is a hot ginger tea that’s perfect for cold nights.

  • Es Dawet: A refreshing drink served with coconut milk, palm sugar, and jelly-like pieces made of rice flour.

Where to Stay in Sukamade: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Sukamade offers plenty of affordable accommodation choices. Here are some of the best options:

  • Sukamade Lodge: This hotel is located near the beach and offers comfortable rooms with modern amenities.

  • Jelita 2 Homestay: This homestay offers basic rooms with a fan and shared bathrooms.

What to Buy in Sukamade: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Sukamade doesn’t have a significant shopping scene, but visitors can buy some unique souvenirs and local products, such as:

  • Batik Cloth: Sukamade’s batik is famous for its unique prints and patterns.

  • Wayang Kulit: This traditional puppet is made of leather and is a popular souvenir.

Local Culture and Customs of Sukamade

Sukamade’s local culture is heavily influenced by Javanese traditions. Visitors are expected to dress modestly and be respectful of local customs.

Best Time to Visit Sukamade

The best time to visit Sukamade is from June to September, which is the dry season. The weather during this time is pleasant and ideal for outdoor activities and beach visits.

Do’s and Don’ts of Sukamade

Do:

  • Dress modestly and be respectful of local customs.

  • Try the local food and beverages.

  • Take part in the turtle conservation program.

Don’t:

  • Take pictures of the turtles with flashlights or disturb them in any way.

  • Litter on the beach or in the national park.

Safety and Health

Safety in Sukamade

Sukamade is generally safe, but visitors should take the usual precautions, such as avoiding walking alone at night and not leaving their belongings unattended.

Health in Sukamade

Visitors should be up-to-date on their routine vaccinations and malaria prophylaxis.

Getting to Sukamade: Transportation Options and Logistics

Sukamade is remote and not easily accessible, but it worth it when you get there. Here are the transportation options:

Airports Near Sukamade

The nearest airports to Sukamade are Blimbingsari Airport in Banyuwangi and Juanda International Airport in Surabaya.

Bus Stations Near Sukamade

The nearest bus station to Sukamade is Jember Bus Station.

Other Options

Visitors can hire a car or a taxi from Jember or Banyuwangi to reach Sukamade.

How to Get Around in Sukamade

The best way to get around Sukamade is on foot, but visitors can also rent a bike or a car for excursions.

Where to Go Next from Sukamade: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Sukamade is an ideal base to explore some of the nearby destinations, such as:

  • Banyuwangi: This vibrant city is famous for its natural attractions, such as Ijen Crater and Menjangan Island.

  • Mount Bromo: This active volcano is around 160 km away from Sukamade and can be reached by a car or a bus.

Conclusion

Sukamade offers a unique and authentic experience for travelers seeking nature, adventure, and cultural immersion. With its stunning beaches, national park, and wildlife, Sukamade is a hidden gem in East Java, Indonesia, and worth adding to your travel plans.
