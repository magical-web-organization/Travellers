Malang is a charming city located in Southern Mountainous in East Java, Indonesia. It boasts a rich culture, welcoming locals, and magnificent natural scenery. Whether you’re an adventure seeker, culture enthusiast, or foodie, Malang has something for everyone. This comprehensive guide will help you plan your visit to Malang by showcasing the city’s top attractions, hiking routes, food scene, and travel logistics.

List of Places to Visit in Malang

Malang has numerous culturally important attractions and natural wonders.

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Malang is known for its rich cultural heritage, so it’s no surprise that the city is home to a plethora of museums and historical sites. Some of the must-visit attractions include:

  • Museum Angkut: This transportation museum houses an impressive collection of vintage cars, bikes, and airplanes.
  • Balai Kota Malang: This government building, built-in 1889, is a great example of Dutch colonial architecture.
  • Singosari Temple: This ancient temple, located in the Singosari district, is a great example of Hindu-Javanese architecture.

Natural Attractions of Malang: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Malang’s natural beauty is one of its main selling points. Here are some of the top natural attractions in the city:

  • Mount Bromo: This famous active volcano is a must-visit for adventure seekers. You can hike or rent a jeep to catch an unforgettable sunrise view.
  • Coban Rais: This waterfall is nestled amidst the lush greenery of the Trawas Mountains.
  • Batu Night Spectacular: This theme park offers a range of fun rides, shows, and attractions perfect for families.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Malang

Malang is a hiker’s paradise with plenty of trails for all levels of experience. Some of the popular hiking/biking routes are:

  • Gunung Arjuno-Welirang: This challenging hike offers a stunning view of two peaks and is best suited for experienced hikers.
  • Taman Langit Cemoro Kandang: This scenic hike takes you through a forest and a hilltop meadow, perfect for a picnic or camping trip.
  • Taman Hutan Raya Brawijaya: This park offers a range of trails for all levels of hikers, from easy to moderate.

Best Things to Do in Malang: Activities and Tours

Malang has plenty of activities to offer for all types of visitors. Here are some of the best things to do in Malang:

  • Visit the Malang Night Market: A great place to sample local food, interact with locals and shop for souvenirs.
  • Take a bike tour through the city: Touring the city on two wheels is a great way to explore the local lifestyle and architecture.
  • Learn to make Batik: This traditional textile-making technique is an important part of Javanese culture, and you can learn the art of Batik at the several batik-making centers in Malang.

Week-Long Itinerary for Malang

Here is an example of a week-long itinerary for Malang:

Day Activities
1 Arrival, check-in at a guesthouse, and explore Malang’s local markets and street food.
2 Take a hike on the Arjuno-Welirang trail, then spend the afternoon relaxing at Batu Secret Zoo.
3 Visit the historic Singosari Temple in the morning, then attend a traditional wayang puppet show in the evening.
4 Take a tour of the Malang city on a bicycle and visit the Malang Town Hall, Ijen Boulevard and Taman Rekreasi Kota.
5 Travel to the Bromo Tengger National Park, catch sunrise from the mountaintops, and then hike around the volcanoes.
6 Learn the art of Batik and visit some of the local artisanal crafts stores. End the day at Jatim Park 3, an amusement park.
7 Wrap up with a relaxing day at the Coban Rais waterfall before departure.

Food and Drink Scene of Malang

Malang’s food and drink scene is rich and diverse, characterized by a blend of traditional Indonesian and Dutch colonial influences. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages to sample on your visit:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Bakso Malang: This meatball soup is a signature dish in Malang.
  • Sate Ayam: A skewered chicken dish marinated in sweet soy sauce.
  • Lontong Kupang: A rice cake dish topped with mussels and peanut sauce.

Local Beverages

  • Wedang Jahe: A traditional ginger tea served with crushed peanuts and ginger slices.
  • Es Kelapa Muda: A refreshing drink made with young coconut water and meat.
  • Cingcau Hijau: A herbal jelly drink made of grass jelly and coconut milk.

Where to Stay in Malang: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Malang offers a plethora of accommodation options to fit your budget and preferences. Here are some popular options:

  • Hotel Tugu: A luxury hotel located in the heart of the city.
  • Krowi Inn: A popular hostel with a laid-back atmosphere and friendly staff.
  • Rumah Kertajaya: A traditionally styled guesthouse in a quiet residential area.

What to Buy in Malang: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

If you’re looking for unique souvenirs and local products to bring back home, here are some popular purchases:

  • Batik: Handmade batik textiles are popular in Java and make great gifts.
  • Wayang puppets: These traditional Javanese puppets make great souvenirs and art pieces.
  • Snacks: Malang is famous for its traditional kue (cakes) and snacks.

Local Culture and Customs of Malang

Malang, like the rest of Indonesia, is a diverse country with distinct customs and traditions. It is important to be respectful of local culture and customs. Here are some tips on Malang’s local culture and customs:

  • Dress modestly in public places.
  • Remove your shoes before entering a mosque or temple.
  • Greet people with “Salam” (hello) and “Sampai Jumpa” (goodbye).

Best Time to Visit Malang

The best time to visit Malang is between May and August when the weather is mild and dry. However, if you prefer fewer crowds, you can visit the city from September to December. Avoid visiting during the rainy season (January to April) as the trails are slippery and difficult to navigate.

Do’s and Don’ts of Malang

Here are some do’s and don’ts to keep in mind when visiting Malang:

Do:

  • Try to learn a few basic phrases of Indonesian.
  • Respect the environment and avoid making unnecessary noise.
  • Agree upon prices before buying any souvenirs or products.

Don’t:

  • Don’t smoke in public places.
  • Don’t show affection in public.
  • Don’t touch or disturb underwater marine life.

Safety and Health

While Malang is generally safe, it’s important to take precautions to stay healthy and avoid accidents.

Safety in Malang

  • Keep a close watch on your belongings, especially in crowded areas.
  • Avoid walking alone in less populated areas at night.
  • Be cautious of scams targeting tourists.

Health in Malang

  • Drink bottled water and avoid consuming unwashed fruit or street food.
  • Apply mosquito repellent or wear long sleeves and pants to prevent mosquito bites.
  • Be prepared for the hot and humid weather by packing plenty of fluids and sunscreen.

Getting to Malang: Transportation Options and Logistics

There are several ways to get to Malang:

  • Fly: You can take a direct flight to Juanda International Airport in Surabaya and then take a taxi or bus to Malang.
  • Bus: You can catch a bus from major cities to Malang’s terminals.
  • Train: You can take a train from major cities to Malang’s railway station.

How to Get Around in Malang

Malang has a reliable and affordable public transportation system. Here are some convenient modes of transport:

  • Angkot: These small public minibusses are the most popular mode of transportation in Malang.
  • Taxi: You can find several taxis in the city center and at airports and train stations.
  • Ojek: These motorbike taxis are useful for short distances.

Where to Go Next from Malang: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Here are some nearby destinations and excursions that can be combined with your Malang trip:

  • Surabaya: The second-largest city in Indonesia offers a range of shopping and cultural activities.
  • Mount Ijen: This active volcano is home to mesmerizing blue flames and a turquoise-colored lake.
  • Yogyakarta: A cultural and historical city that offers temple complexes and traditional handicrafts.

Conclusion

Malang is a unique city that offers a mix of natural beauty, cultural heritage, and modern entertainment. Plan your visit with this comprehensive guide and enjoy the best the city has to offer. Whether you prefer hiking up stunning mountains or sampling street food, there’s something for everyone in Malang.
