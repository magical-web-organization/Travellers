Introduction to Kediri

Kediri is a city located in the Southern Mountainous region of East Java, Indonesia. The city is known for its rich cultural heritage, outdoor adventures, and natural wonders. It has a diverse range of attractions, from historical sites and museums to scenic hiking trails and waterfalls. Kediri also boasts a vibrant food and drink scene, with a variety of local dishes and beverages to try.

List of Places to Visit in Kediri

Here is a list of must-visit places in Kediri:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Kediri Kingdom Museum: This museum houses artifacts and relics from the Kediri Kingdom, which dominated the region in the 12th century. It showcases the history and culture of the kingdom through exhibits and displays.
  • Singosari Temple: This ancient Hindu temple dates back to the 13th century and is a UNESCO World Heritage Site. It features intricate carvings and sculptures that reflect the art and culture of the Singosari Kingdom.
  • Puh Sarang Church: This Dutch-colonial style church was built in the 19th century and is a popular pilgrimage site for Catholics in the area.

Natural Attractions of Kediri: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Mount Kelud: This active volcano offers stunning panoramic views from its summit. It is a popular hiking destination for adventure enthusiasts.
  • Tirta Nirwana Kampoeng Tritis Waterfall: This stunning waterfall is located in a rural area outside the city. It features a natural pool where visitors can swim and relax.
  • Selomangleng Cave: This limestone cave system is located in the foothills of Mount Kelud. It is known for its unique rock formations and underground river.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Kediri

Kediri is a hiker’s paradise, with a variety of trails and routes to explore. The most popular hiking and biking routes include:

  • Mount Kelud Summit Trail: This trail is a challenging hike that takes visitors to the summit of Mount Kelud. The trail offers stunning views of the city and the surrounding countryside.
  • Tawangmangu Mountain Trail: This trail is a moderate hike that takes visitors through the lush forests of the Tawangmangu Mountains. The trail is known for its scenic views and diverse wildlife.
  • Karanganyar Cycling Route: This route is a leisurely bike ride through the scenic countryside. It takes visitors through rice fields, rural villages, and historical sites.

Best Things to Do in Kediri: Activities and Tours

Kediri offers a range of activities and tours for visitors to enjoy. Here are some of the best things to do in Kediri:

  • Kampoeng Sriti Cultural Village Tour: This tour takes visitors through a traditional Javanese village, where they can learn about the local customs and culture.
  • Rafting Adventure on the Brantas River: This adventure tour takes visitors on a thrilling rafting ride along the Brantas River. The tour offers scenic views of the surrounding countryside and a taste of local adventure.
  • Night Market Tour: This tour takes visitors through the vibrant night markets of Kediri, where they can sample local street food and shop for souvenirs.

Week-Long Itinerary for Kediri

Here is a sample itinerary for a week-long trip to Kediri:

  • Day 1: Visit the Kediri Kingdom Museum and explore the Singosari Temple.
  • Day 2: Hike to the summit of Mount Kelud and enjoy the panoramic views.
  • Day 3: Take a cultural village tour and learn about the local customs and traditions.
  • Day 4: Explore the Selomangleng Cave and Tirta Nirwana Kampoeng Tritis Waterfall.
  • Day 5: Take a leisurely bike ride through the countryside and visit the Puh Sarang Church.
  • Day 6: Try white-water rafting on the Brantas River and visit the night markets for dinner and shopping.
  • Day 7: Depart Kediri for your next destination.

Food and Drink Scene of Kediri

Kediri has a vibrant food and drink scene, with a variety of local specialties to try.

Must-Try Dishes

  • Sate Kelinci: This local specialty consists of rabbit skewers grilled over charcoal and served with a peanut sauce.
  • Rawon: This hearty beef soup is cooked with spices and served with rice and vegetables.
  • Lontong Kupang: This seafood dish features mussels served with rice cakes in a flavorful broth.

Local Beverages

  • Jamu: This traditional Indonesian drink is made with a blend of herbs and spices.
  • Bir Pletok: This local beer is brewed with traditional ingredients like ginger and lemongrass.
  • Wedang Jahe: This hot ginger drink is a popular cure for colds and flu.

What to Buy in Kediri: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Kediri has a range of unique souvenirs and local products for visitors to take home. Here are some of the best things to buy in Kediri:

  • Batik Fabric: Kediri is known for its vibrant batik fabric, which features unique designs and patterns.
  • Wood Carvings: Local craftsmen in Kediri create beautiful wood carvings, from masks and figurines to furniture and home decor.
  • Kris: This traditional Indonesian dagger is a popular souvenir item and a symbol of Javanese culture.

Local Culture and Customs of Kediri

Kediri has a rich cultural heritage, with a blend of Javanese, Hindu, and Islamic influences. Visitors should be respectful of local customs and traditions, such as covering their shoulders and legs when visiting religious sites.

Best Time to Visit Kediri

The best time to visit Kediri is during the dry season, which runs from May to October. The weather is mild and pleasant during this time, making it ideal for outdoor activities and sightseeing.

Do’s and Don’ts of Kediri

Here are some do’s and don’ts to keep in mind when visiting Kediri:

Do:

  • Dress appropriately when visiting religious sites.
  • Try the local food and beverages.
  • Learn about the local customs and traditions.

Don’t:

  • Disrespect local customs and traditions.
  • Litter or damage natural attractions.
  • Engage in illegal activities.

Safety and Health

Safety in Kediri

Kediri is a relatively safe city, but visitors should take precautions when traveling, such as avoiding unfamiliar areas at night and keeping valuables secure.

Health in Kediri

Visitors to Kediri should be up to date on their routine vaccines and take precautions against mosquito-borne illnesses like dengue fever and malaria.

Getting to Kediri: Transportation Options and Logistics

Kediri is accessible by air, bus, and train. The nearest airport is Juanda International Airport in Surabaya, which is a two-hour drive from the city. Buses and trains also connect Kediri to other cities in East Java and beyond.

Airports Near Kediri

  • Juanda International Airport, Surabaya: 142 km (2 hours by car)

Bus Stations Near Kediri

  • Terminal Kediri: located in the city center
  • Terminal Ngasem: located on the outskirts of the city
  • Terminal Selomangleng: located on the road to Mount Kelud

Other Options

  • Private Car: Visitors can hire a private car or taxi to travel to Kediri from other cities.
  • Motorcycle Rental: Visitors can rent motorcycles to explore Kediri and the surrounding areas.

How to Get Around in Kediri

Kediri is a relatively small city, and many attractions are within walking distance of each other. Visitors can also use public transportation in the form of bemos (minivans), taxis, and motorcycle taxis.

Where to Go Next from Kediri: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Kediri is a great starting point for exploring the surrounding region. Some nearby destinations and excursions include:

  • Tawangmangu: This mountain resort offers stunning views of the surrounding landscape.
  • Mount Bromo: This active volcano is a popular destination for adventure enthusiasts.
  • Malang: This charming colonial town offers a blend of history and culture.

Conclusion

Kediri offers a unique blend of culture, adventure, and natural beauty. From hiking to scenic waterfalls, exploring museums and temples to sampling local cuisine, there is something for everyone in Kediri. Plan your trip today and experience all that this hidden gem of East Java has to offer.
