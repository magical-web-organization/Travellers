Introduction to Trenggalek

Nestled in the southern mountainous region of East Java, Trenggalek is a hidden gem in Indonesia waiting to be explored. Known for its stunning natural attractions, rich cultural heritage, and welcoming locals, Trenggalek offers a unique and authentic experience for travelers seeking off-the-beaten-path destinations. In this comprehensive travel guide, we will help you plan your visit to Trenggalek by sharing the best places to visit, things to do, where to stay, and more.

List of Places to Visit in Trenggalek

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Tarakanita Museum: This museum is dedicated to the history and culture of Trenggalek, showcasing various local artifacts, traditional clothes, and ancient weapons.
  • Kamulan Temple: A 12th-century temple that reflects the ancient Majapahit Kingdom’s architectural style is a must-visit attraction for history lovers.
  • Jeddih Lebur Temple: This is another temple that you should not miss while in Trenggalek. It is known for its unique carvings and ancient artifacts.

Natural Attractions of Trenggalek: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Prigi Beach: Prigi Beach is a stunning beach with crystal clear water and breathtaking views. It is an ideal spot for swimming, sunbathing, surfing, and fishing.
  • Tanjung Papuma: Tanjung Papuma Beach boasts serene, turquoise waters and white sandy beaches. It is an ideal place to escape the crowds and relax in peace.
  • Tlogo Agung Hill: This is the highest point in Trenggalek, and a hike up to the summit rewards visitors with picturesque views of the surrounding landscapes.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Trenggalek

Trenggalek boasts several hiking and biking routes, offering unparalleled opportunities to discover the region’s natural beauty better. Here are some popular ones:

  • Mount Lakip: This mountain trek takes you through lush forests, delightful streams, and magnificent waterfalls.
  • Larangan Trail: Experience Trenggalek’s rural beauty on this trail through strawberry gardens, paddy fields, and coffee plantations.

Best Things to Do in Trenggalek: Activities and Tours

  • Berkah Waduk Selorejo: Enjoy a boat ride on Selorejo Lake and take in the peaceful scenery with panoramic mountain views.
  • Sukolilo Natural Wonders Exploration: This tour takes you through authentic, off-the-beaten-path destinations where you will discover waterfalls, lakes, and hidden caves in Trenggalek’s scenic beauty.
  • Watu Lawang Adventure: Trek through unique rock formations and stunning panoramic views of the Blitar Mountains at sunrise on this adventure that’s sure to take your breath away.

Week-Long Itinerary for Trenggalek

Here’s a sample week-long itinerary for exploring Trenggalek:

Day 1 Explore Tarakanita Museum and Kamulan Temple
Day 2 Take a hike to Mount Lakip and explore the surrounding waterfalls
Day 3 Visit Tlogo Agung Hill and enjoy the breathtaking mountain vistas
Day 4 Enjoy a relaxing day at Tanjung Papuma Beach
Day 5 Go on a bike tour through rural Trenggalek
Day 6 Take a boat ride on Selorejo Lake
Day 7 Explore Trenggalek’s local culture and shop for souvenirs

Food and Drink Scene of Trenggalek

Must-Try Dishes

  • Nasi Kuning: A classic Indonesian rice dish, Nasi Kuning is steamed rice cooked with turmeric, served alongside spicy chicken and sambal (hot sauce).
  • Gado-Gado: A popular Indonesian salad, Gado-Gado consists of boiled vegetables and boiled egg, paired with peanut sauce.
  • Sate: Indonesia’s most loved street food, it is marinated grilled meat served with peanut sauce.

Local Beverages

  • Kopi Jombang: This is a local coffee variant that has a unique flavor and aroma, produced locally in the Jombang region.
  • Es Dawet Telasih: A refreshing drink, it’s made from fresh pandan-infused rice flour jelly and served with coconut milk.

Where to Stay in Trenggalek: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Trenggalek has several accommodation options that cater to all budgets, preferences, and travel types. Some of the best hotels, hostels, and guesthouses in Trenggalek include:

  • Royal Hotel Trenggalek: A luxurious four-star hotel situated in the heart of the city, known for its incredible hospitality, modern facilities, and stunning views.
  • Wijaya Guest House: A mid-range guest house located in the city center, offering modern amenities and a homely atmosphere.
  • Rumah Boedi Private Residence: A budget-friendly guest house, known for its cleanliness, exceptional hospitality, and central location.

What to Buy in Trenggalek: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Madura Sheets: A famous Madura handicraft, these colorful sheets can be used as tablecloths, bedspreads, or even wall hangings.
  • Kain Tenun: Trenggalek is known for producing high-quality woven fabric, locally known as Kain Tenun.
  • Batik Trenggalek: Witness the traditional craft of batik printing in Trenggalek and purchase beautiful handmade batik fabrics or clothing.

Local Culture and Customs of Trenggalek

Trenggalek is home to a vibrant blend of Javanese, Madura, and Bali cultures. Like many other regions in Indonesia, religion and local customs play a crucial role in Trenggalek’s culture. Visitors should dress modestly and be respectful of local customs and traditions.

Best Time to Visit Trenggalek

The best time to visit Trenggalek is during the dry season, which runs from May to September. During this season, the weather is pleasant, and the chances of rain are minimal. However, travelers may still visit Trenggalek during the wet season (October to April) when the rainfall is heavy, but the climate remains comfortable.

Do’s and Don’ts of Trenggalek

Do:

  • Respect the local customs and traditions.
  • Dress modestly when visiting religious sites.
  • Try out the local cuisine and beverages.
  • Explore the natural wonders of Trenggalek.

Don’t:

  • Engage in public displays of affection.
  • Wear revealing clothing in public areas.
  • Disrespect local customs or religious beliefs.

Safety and Health

Safety in Trenggalek

Trenggalek is a relatively safe destination for tourists, but visitors should still exercise caution and be aware of pickpockets in crowded areas.

Health in Trenggalek

Travelers are advised to drink only bottled water, avoid street food stalls and carry anti-mosquito repellent while visiting Trenggalek.

Getting to Trenggalek: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Trenggalek

  • Abdul Rachman Saleh Airport: The nearest airport to Trenggalek is the Abdul Rachman Saleh Airport in Malang, situated about 106 km away.

Bus Stations Near Trenggalek

  • Terminal Bus Trenggalek
  • Terminal Bus Karangan
  • Terminal Bus Kampak

Other Options

  • Visitors can also take a taxi or a private car from Malang or Surabaya, which is about 200 km away.

How to Get Around in Trenggalek

The most effective way to get around Trenggalek is by renting a car, motorbike, or using the Ojek (motorbike taxi). Public transportation such as buses and minivans is also available, but they may not be as frequent or reliable.

Where to Go Next from Trenggalek: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Malang: A beautiful city famous for its coffee plantations and stunning landscapes is just a short drive away from Trenggalek.
  • Bromo Tengger Semeru National Park: A UNESCO World Heritage Site, this volcanic national park with its stunning landscapes, and scenic views is just a few hours’ drive from Trenggalek.

Conclusion

Trenggalek is a hidden gem in East Java, Indonesia, waiting to be explored. It is full of natural wonders, cultural richness, and warm hospitality that makes it an irresistible destination for travelers seeking an off-the-beaten-path experience. We hope this comprehensive travel guide has equipped you with all the necessary information to plan your trip to Trenggalek and make the most out of your visit.
