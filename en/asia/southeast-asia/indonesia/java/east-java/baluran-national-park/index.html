Introduction to Baluran National Park

Located in the province of East Java, Baluran National Park is a nature lover’s paradise. Spread over an area of 250 square kilometers, it is home to a vast array of flora and fauna. The park boasts of a unique ecosystem, housing savannas, mangrove forests, and evergreen forests.

Baluran National Park offers a range of activities, from hiking and wildlife safaris to beach walks and cultural exploration. It is an ideal destination for adventure-seekers, nature enthusiasts, and culture vultures.

List of Places to Visit in Baluran National Park

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Bama Beach Museum: This museum showcases the history and culture of the local Bajawan people.
  • Bekol Savannah Museum: Located in the heart of the park, this museum is a treasure trove of information about the park’s flora and fauna.
  • Radar Tower: This tower offers a panoramic view of the entire park and its surroundings.

Natural Attractions of Baluran National Park: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Bekol Savannah: The vast grassland savannah is home to various animal species, including water buffaloes, deer, and wild boars.
  • Mangrove Forest: Covering an area of 4,000 hectares, this coastal forest is a breeding ground for several marine species, like crabs and shrimps.
  • Bama Beach: The white-sand beach is an excellent place for swimming and relaxing, and offers scenic views of the park.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Baluran National Park

Baluran National Park offers several hiking and biking routes of varying difficulty levels. Some of the popular trails include:

  • Sejati Hill Trek: This trek takes you through a dense forest and rewards you with a stunning view of the Sejati Hill.
  • Bekol-Four Lake Trek: This trek takes you past the savannah to four lakes, where you can enjoy birdwatching and fishing.
  • Bekol-Sadengan Trek: This trek takes you through the savannah and the mangrove forest to Sadengan, where you can spot the iconic Baluran banteng (wild water buffalo).

Best Things to Do in Baluran National Park: Activities and Tours

  • Wildlife Safari: Join a guided safari tour to spot the park’s unique wildlife, such as the banteng, deer, and peafowl.
  • Beach Walk: Take a leisurely stroll along the stunning Bama Beach and soak in the beauty of the surroundings.
  • Birdwatching: Baluran National Park is home to over 200 species of birds, making it a paradise for bird watchers.
  • Cultural Tour: Learn more about the local Bajawan culture and traditions by visiting the Bama Beach Museum and interacting with the locals.

Week-Long Itinerary for Baluran National Park

Day 1: Arrive at Baluran National Park and check-in to your hotel. Spend the rest of the day relaxing and exploring the nearby area.

Day 2: Take a guided wildlife safari tour and spot the park’s unique wildlife.

Day 3: Go on a hiking or biking tour of the park and enjoy the stunning scenery.

Day 4: Visit the Bekol Savannah Museum and learn about the park’s flora and fauna.

Day 5: Take a cultural tour and learn more about the local Bajawan culture and traditions.

Day 6: Relax and unwind at Bama Beach or go on a beach walk.

Day 7: Explore the nearby area and check out some of the local markets and restaurants.

Food and Drink Scene of Baluran National Park

Must-Try Dishes

  • Rawon Nguling: A traditional East Javanese dish made with beef and black nuts. It is usually served with rice and vegetables.
  • Sego Sambal Kemangi: A simple dish of rice served with sambal (spicy sauce) and kemangi (basil leaves).
  • Soto Daging: A traditional Indonesian soup made with beef, potatoes, and spices.

Local Beverages

  • Wedang Uwuh: A traditional Javanese herbal drink made with ginger, lemongrass, and other spices.
  • Kopi Jawa: A strong and rich coffee made with locally grown coffee beans.

Where to Stay in Baluran National Park: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

There are several accommodation options available in and around Baluran National Park, ranging from budget guesthouses to luxurious resorts. Some popular options include:

  • Wisma Baluran: A budget guesthouse located within the park, offering basic but comfortable rooms and a convenient location.
  • Baluran Hill Resort: A luxurious resort situated on a hill, offering stunning views of the park and a range of facilities like a swimming pool and spa.

What to Buy in Baluran National Park: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Batik: Traditional Javanese cloth that has been decorated using wax-resist dyeing.

  • Wayang Kulit: Traditional Javanese shadow puppets made from leather.

Local Culture and Customs of Baluran National Park

The local Bajawan people have a rich culture and tradition that they are proud of. Visitors to Baluran National Park should respect the customs and traditions of the locals, including dress codes and behavior in public spaces.

Best Time to Visit Baluran National Park

The best time to visit Baluran National Park is from June to September, during the dry season. The weather is pleasant, and the wildlife is more visible during this period.

Do’s and Don’ts of Baluran National Park

Do:

  • Respect the park’s wildlife and natural environment.
  • Follow the park’s rules and regulations.
  • Dress appropriately, covering your shoulders and knees when visiting temples or other cultural sites.

Don’t:

  • Litter and leave trash in the park.
  • Harm the wildlife or disturb their natural habitat.
  • Disrespect local culture and customs.

Safety and Health

Safety in Baluran National Park

Visitors to Baluran National Park should take necessary precautions to ensure their safety, such as:

  • Staying within marked trails and avoiding areas that are known to be hazardous.
  • Hiring a local guide when visiting remote areas of the park.
  • Keeping valuables safe and secured.

Health in Baluran National Park

Visitors are advised to take necessary health precautions, such as:

  • Getting vaccinations and immunizations before traveling to Indonesia.
  • Using insect repellent to avoid mosquito bites and other insect-borne diseases.
  • Drinking bottled water and avoiding tap water.

Getting to Baluran National Park: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Baluran National Park

The nearest airport to Baluran National Park is Juanda International Airport in Surabaya, about 4 hours away by car.

Bus Stations Near Baluran National Park

The nearest bus station to Baluran National Park is in Banyuwangi, about 2 hours away by car.

Other Options

Visitors can also hire a private car or taxi to travel to Baluran National Park.

How to Get Around in Baluran National Park

There are several ways to get around Baluran National Park, including:

  • Guided Tours: Join a guided tour of the park, where you can explore the various attractions with a local guide.
  • Walking: The park is relatively small, and it’s easy to explore on foot.
  • Biking: Rent a bike and explore the park’s trails and scenic routes at your own pace.

Where to Go Next from Baluran National Park: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Ijen Crater: A popular tourist destination known for its blue flames and sulfur mining.
  • Mount Bromo: A picturesque active volcano known for its stunning sunrise views.
  • Taman Safari Indonesia: A safari park located near Prigen that’s home to various animal species.

Conclusion

Baluran National Park is a must-visit destination for anyone seeking a unique and adventurous experience. With its diverse ecosystem, rich cultural heritage, and stunning natural beauty, it offers something for everyone. Whether you’re looking for a relaxing beach vacation or a thrilling wildlife safari, Baluran National Park has it all.
