Introduction to Java

Java, the most populous island in Indonesia, is a vibrant mix of culture, history, and natural beauty. Home to more than half of Indonesia’s population, Java offers travelers a unique blend of modernity and tradition, from ancient temples to bustling cities. Easily accessible by air or ferry, Java is a must-see destination for anyone visiting Southeast Asia. With this comprehensive travel guide, you’ll discover the best places to visit, things to do, food and drink scene, local culture, and safety and health tips for your trip to Java.

List of Places to Visit in Java

Whether you’re interested in history, nature, or both, Java has plenty of options for every traveler. Here are the top places you should consider visiting during your stay:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Borobudur Temple
  • Prambanan Temple
  • Kraton Ngayogyakarta Hadiningrat (Sultan’s Palace)
  • Taman Sari Water Castle
  • National Museum of Indonesia
  • House of Sampoerna

Natural Attractions of Java: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Mount Bromo
  • Ijen Crater
  • Kawah Putih
  • Green Canyon
  • Madakaripura Waterfall
  • Mount Merbabu

Best Things to Do in Java: Activities and Tours

Java has a variety of activities and tours that cater to different interests. Here are some of the best things to do in Java:

  • Explore Borobudur and Prambanan temples
  • Rafting at Elo River
  • Sunrise Tours in Mount Bromo and Borobudur
  • Cooking Classes
  • Wildlife Tours in Ujung Kulon National Park

Week-Long Itinerary for Java

If you’re planning on spending a week in Java, here’s a sample itinerary to make the most of your trip:

Day 1: Arrive in Jakarta Day 2: Explore Jakarta and visit National Museum of Indonesia Day 3: Take a train to Yogyakarta Day 4: Visit Borobudur and Prambanan temples Day 5: Trek Mount Bromo Day 6: Visit Ijen Crater Day 7: Return to Jakarta

Local Culture and Customs of Java

Java has a rich cultural heritage, with a mix of Javanese and Islamic traditions. Here are some customs and practices that you should be aware of during your visit:

  • Dress modestly while visiting religious sites
  • Remove your shoes before entering a home or temple
  • Avoid pointing with your feet
  • Greet people using the polite term “selamat”, followed by the appropriate time of day

Best Time to Visit Java

The best time to visit Java is during the dry season, from June to September. However, some parts of Java such as the Bromo Tengger Semeru National Park are best visited during the rainy season when the waterfalls are at their peak.

Do’s and Don’ts of Java

Do:

  • Learn a few phrases in Bahasa Indonesia
  • Try the local cuisine
  • Respect religious customs and traditions

Don’t:

  • Get offended by bargaining in the markets
  • Touch or disturb wildlife
  • Disrespect local customs or religion

Safety and Health

Safety in Java

Java is generally a safe place to visit, but travelers should exercise caution when it comes to pickpocketing and petty theft in crowded areas and public transportation.

Health in Java

Visitors should be up-to-date on their routine vaccinations, hepatitis A and B, and typhoid. It is highly recommended to take malaria prophylaxis medication if you are visiting certain parts of Java such as Kalimantan.
