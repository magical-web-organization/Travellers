Pekalongan is a charming city located in Central Java, Indonesia. Known for its vibrant batik industry, this city has a rich cultural heritage that makes it a must-visit destination for anyone traveling through Central Java. In this comprehensive travel guide, we will help you discover the best things to do, see, eat, and where to stay in Pekalongan.

Introduction to Pekalongan

Pekalongan is a vibrant city brimming with life and culture. Located along the coast, it has a rich history that can be seen in its architecture, streets, and markets. The city is known for its batik industry, which produces some of the finest batik in Indonesia. Pekalongan is also home to some of the best seafood in the region, making it a foodie’s paradise. The people of Pekalongan are friendly and welcoming, making it a great destination for solo travelers and families alike.

List of Places to Visit in Pekalongan

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Pekalongan is home to several museums and historic places that are worth visiting. Some of the must-visit places include:

  • Batik Museum, which houses a vast collection of batik fabrics from all over Indonesia.
  • Pekalongan Museum, which displays various cultural artifacts and exhibits of the city’s history.
  • Pekalongan Great Mosque, a historic mosque with beautiful architecture and a serene atmosphere.
  • Java Taman Mini Indonesia Indah, a miniature park that showcases the different cultures of Indonesia.

Natural Attractions of Pekalongan: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Pekalongan has plenty of natural attractions that offer outdoor adventures and scenic views. Some of the top places to visit include:

  • Goa Jepang, a historic cave that was used as a hideout during World War II.
  • Curug Setawing, a stunning waterfall nestled in the hills of Pekalongan.
  • Pantai Pasir Kencana, a beautiful beach with crystal-clear waters and soft sand.
  • Bukit Cinta, a hilltop overlooking the city with breathtaking views.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Pekalongan

Pekalongan has some great hiking and biking routes that offer a chance to experience the beauty of the city’s landscapes up close. Some popular routes include:

  • Gunung Malang Trekking, a steep hike up to the peak of Gunung Malang that rewards hikers with panoramic views of the city.
  • Pantai Pasir Kencana Trail, a scenic trail that leads to the beach and takes around 3 hours to complete.
  • Pekalongan City Biking Tour, a cycling tour that takes you through the streets of Pekalongan and highlights the city’s most notable landmarks.

Best Things to Do in Pekalongan: Activities and Tours

Apart from visiting the cultural attractions and natural wonders, Pekalongan has many fun activities and tours to offer. Here are a few suggestions:

  • Taste the seafood at Pekalongan Fish Market, where you can buy fresh seafood and have it cooked on the spot.
  • Book a batik workshop and learn how to make your own batik fabric.
  • Take a boat tour of the Segara Anakan Estuary and explore the mangrove forests.
  • Join a city tour and visit the local markets, mosques, and landmarks with a guide.

Food and Drink Scene of Pekalongan

Must-Try Dishes

Pekalongan is known for its seafood and traditional Javanese dishes. Here are a few must-try dishes:

  • Nasi Krawu, a beef dish with rice, coconut, and spices.
  • Gado-gado, a vegetable salad with peanut sauce.
  • Soto, a soup with chicken, beef or fish, and vegetables.
  • Grilled fish, fresh from the sea and cooked on the spot.

Local Beverages

Try the traditional beverages of Pekalongan, such as Es Dawet Telasih, a sweet drink made from coconut milk, and ice, with basil seeds. You can also sample Es Cendol, a popular drink made from rice flour and palm sugar mixed in coconut milk.

Where to Stay in Pekalongan: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Pekalongan has plenty of accommodation options ranging from budget-friendly hostels to luxury hotels. Some of the best places to stay are:

  • Pesona Krakatau Hotel, a modern hotel with a pool and a restaurant.
  • Santai Retreat, a guesthouse with a quiet and relaxing atmosphere.
  • OYO 231 Graha Wisata Homestay, a budget-friendly homestay in the heart of the city.

What to Buy in Pekalongan: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

When in Pekalongan, you’ll want to bring some souvenirs back home with you. Here are some items to consider:

  • Batik fabrics, the most iconic product of Pekalongan.
  • Local snacks such as Tempe Mendoan and getuk, made from cassava.
  • Traditional crafts such as wooden puppets and pottery.

Local Culture and Customs of Pekalongan

Pekalongan’s culture is deeply rooted in its batik industry, and most of the local customs and traditions revolve around it. The people of Pekalongan are friendly and helpful towards visitors, and expect the same in return.

Best Time to Visit Pekalongan

The best time to visit Pekalongan is between April and September when the weather is dry and sunny.

Do’s and Don’ts of Pekalongan

Do:

  • Try the local cuisine.
  • Respect the local culture and customs.
  • Shop for souvenirs from local vendors.

Don’t:

  • Wear revealing clothing in conservative areas.
  • Bring illegal drugs.
  • Disrespect local customs.

Safety and Health

Safety in Pekalongan

Pekalongan is a relatively safe city, and travelers should exercise the same caution as they would in any other city. Avoid walking alone at night, and keep your valuables safe.

Health in Pekalongan

Make sure to receive all recommended vaccinations before traveling to Pekalongan. Drink plenty of water to stay hydrated, and avoid street food that might be contaminated. Mosquito-borne diseases are prevalent in the region, so make sure to apply insect repellent and wear long sleeves and pants.

Getting to Pekalongan: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Pekalongan

The nearest airport to Pekalongan is Ahmad Yani International Airport in Semarang, about 75 km away from the city. From there, you can take a taxi to Pekalongan.

Bus Stations Near Pekalongan

Pekalongan has two main bus stations: Terminal Bus Pekalongan and Terminal Bus Wirakencana. Buses run to and from several destinations in Central Java and Indonesia.

Other Options

You can also take the train to Pekalongan, as the city has a railway station that connects it to cities like Jakarta, Surabaya, and Yogyakarta.

How to Get Around in Pekalongan

The best way to get around Pekalongan is by taxi or motorcycle taxi. You can also rent a car or motorbike to explore the city.

Where to Go Next from Pekalongan: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Pekalongan is a great base from which to explore other parts of Central Java. Some nearby destinations and excursions include:

  • Semarang, a historic colonial city with a rich culture and cuisine.
  • Borobudur, the largest Buddhist temple in the world.
  • Dieng Plateau, a highland with stunning scenery and ancient temple ruins.

Conclusion

Pekalongan is a hidden gem of Indonesia, a city rich in culture, history, and natural beauty. With this comprehensive travel guide, you are now ready to plan your trip to Pekalongan and experience all that this charming city has to offer. Safe travels!
