Introduction to Purbalingga

Purbalingga is a regency in Banyumas, located in Central Java, Indonesia. Situated along the southern foothills of Mount Slamet, Purbalingga is home to many natural wonders and cultural attractions that make it an ideal destination for travelers looking for an off-the-beaten-path experience.

Although Purbalingga might not be as popular as other tourist destinations in Java, it has a unique charm of its own. Visitors can expect to be greeted with warm hospitality, hike picturesque trails, explore ancient temples, and indulge in delicious local cuisine.

List of Places to Visit in Purbalingga

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Gua Lawa: A historical site located in the village of Baturaden. This cave was used by the Japanese as a hiding place during World War II. Visitors can explore the cave and learn about the history of the site.

  • Peninggalan Kerajaan Purbasora: A museum that showcases the history of Purbalingga. Visitors can see ancient artifacts and learn about the cultural heritage of the region.

  • Purbalingga Palace: Once the residence of the royal family of Purbalingga, this majestic palace is now open to the public. Visitors can admire the intricate architecture and learn about the rich history of the palace.

Natural Attractions of Purbalingga: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Baturaden: A popular tourist spot known for its scenic beauty and hot springs. Visitors can enjoy a soothing bath in the hot water pools while overlooking the beautiful landscape.

  • Bukit Cipendok: A hill in Purbalingga that offers stunning views of the surrounding area. Visitors can hike to the top of the hill and enjoy the breathtaking scenery.

  • Curug Cipendok: A waterfall located in Tambak district. Visitors can hike to the waterfall and enjoy the cool, refreshing water.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Purbalingga

  • Mount Slamet: As the highest peak in Central Java, Mount Slamet provides a fantastic hiking and biking experience for adventurers. Visitors can explore lush forests, spot wildlife, and take in the breathtaking views from the top.

  • Bukit Cinta: A hill in Purbalingga that offers scenic hikes and biking trails. Visitors can see rice fields, farms, and natural beauty on this route.

  • Jalur Sepeda Purbalingga: A biking trail that offers amazing views of Mount Slamet, rice fields, and local villages. Visitors can also stop and taste some traditional foods along the way.

Best Things to Do in Purbalingga: Activities and Tours

  • Explore Ancient Temples: Purbalingga is home to some of the oldest and most beautiful temples in Indonesia, including Candi Cetho, Candi Sukuh, and Candi Kethek.

  • Visit Local Villages: Visitors can immerse themselves in the local culture by visiting traditional villages like Desa Wisata Ketenger.

  • Taste Local Cuisine: Purbalingga offers a variety of delicious local dishes like Nasi Liwet, Tempe Goreng, and Pecel.

Food and Drink Scene of Purbalingga

Must-Try Dishes

  • Nasi Liwet: a rice dish cooked with coconut milk, lemongrass, and other spices, served with tempeh, chicken or seafood.

  • Tempe Goreng: a popular snack made from fermented soybeans, deep-fried in oil and usually seasoned with salt and chili powder.

  • Pecel: a Javanese mixed vegetable salad served with peanut sauce and usually accompanied by fried tofu or tempeh.

Local Beverages

  • Wedang Ronde: a sweet, warming drink made from glutinous rice balls, ginger, and palm sugar, served in a hot, sweet soup.

  • Kopi Purbalingga: locally grown coffee, medium roasted and ground to perfection. Purbalingga coffee beans are known for their unique taste, so don’t miss out on trying it during your visit.

Where to Stay in Purbalingga: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Baturaden: a popular tourist spot with many lodging options like bungalows, chalets, and hotels.

  • Cipanas: a favorite spot for hot spring lovers with many resorts and guesthouses to choose from.

  • Purbalingga City Center: the perfect option for visitors looking for a more urban experience, with a variety of accommodation options from budget to luxury hotels.

What to Buy in Purbalingga: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Salak Pondoh: a tropical fruit that is famous in Purbalingga. Visitors can buy fresh fruits or chips to bring home.

  • Bambu Kuning: a type of bamboo handicraft that is made by local craftsmen in Purbalingga. Visitors can buy various souvenirs like coasters, lanterns, and bags.

Local Culture and Customs of Purbalingga

  • Respect: Respect for locals, their culture, and beliefs are highly valued in Purbalingga. Visitors should dress modestly when entering sacred temples, and not use flash photography when taking pictures.

  • Mitoni Ceremony: This is a traditional ceremony held to celebrate the seventh month of pregnancy. Usually, it is held at the bride’s parent’s house.

Best Time to Visit Purbalingga

Purbalingga has a tropical rainforest climate, with temperatures ranging from 22°C to 32°C throughout the year. The best time to visit is during the dry season, which runs from April to October. However, visitors can still visit during the rainy season, which runs from November to March.

Do’s and Don’ts of Purbalingga

Do:

  • Wear comfortable clothing and footwear when hiking or exploring natural attractions.

  • Respect the culture and customs of the local people.

  • Exchange currency at authorized money changers, banks, or hotels to avoid fraud.

Don’t:

  • Don’t litter in public places or throw garbage in rivers or waterfalls.

  • Don’t touch or climb ancient temple structures, as they are delicate and could easily be damaged.

  • Don’t take pictures of locals without permission.

Safety and Health

Safety in Purbalingga

Purbalingga is a safe destination for travelers. However, visitors should still take precautions like locking their belongings and valuables in a safe place, especially when staying in a public area.

Health in Purbalingga

Visitors should be up-to-date on routine vaccines before traveling, and they should also be cautious of mosquito-borne illnesses like dengue fever and malaria. Drinking bottled water is recommended, and visitors should also practice good hygiene by washing their hands frequently and avoiding undercooked food.
