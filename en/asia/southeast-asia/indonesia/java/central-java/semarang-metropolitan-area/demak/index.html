Introduction to Demak

Demak is a small city located in the Semarang Metropolitan Area in Central Java, Indonesia. The city is best known for its history, culture, and religion. It is considered the oldest Muslim kingdom in Java, with a rich history that dates back to the 15th century. Demak is a vibrant city that showcases a blend of Islamic and Javanese culture through its landmarks, cultural attractions, and cuisine. This comprehensive guide will explore what you can see and do in Demak, including the best places to visit, food to try, and cultural customs to keep in mind.

List of Places to Visit in Demak

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  1. Great Mosque of Demak - The Great Mosque of Demak is a cultural and religious landmark that dates back to the 15th century. It is believed to be the first mosque ever built in Java and served as the center of Islam in Java for several centuries.
  2. Museum of Demak Kingdom - The Museum of Demak Kingdom showcases a collection of historical artifacts, cultural exhibits, and displays that tell the story of Demak’s history and Islam in Java.
  3. Tomb of Raden Patah - The Tomb of Raden Patah is the burial site of the first sultan of Demak. It is located near the Great Mosque of Demak and serves as a historical and religious attraction.
  4. Makam Syekh Jumadil Kubro - Makam Syekh Jumadil Kubro is a tomb complex of a prominent Islamic figure in Demak. It is located in Kecapi, Demak.

Natural Attractions of Demak: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  1. Pantai Kartini Beach - The Pantai Kartini Beach is a beautiful shoreline that stretches along the Java Sea. It is named after an Indonesian national hero, Kartini, and offers a serene environment to relax and enjoy the sunset.
  2. Kambang Putih Beach - Kambang Putih Beach is another beautiful shoreline that offers a serene environment with white sands and clear blue waters. It is located in the northern part of Demak Province.
  3. Mount Muria - Mount Muria is the highest mountain in Central Java with a height of 1625 meters. It offers a challenging hike, and visitors will be rewarded with picturesque views of the surrounding area.
  4. Karanghawu Reservoir - The Karanghawu Reservoir is a freshwater lake that offers various water activities such as paddle boating, jet skiing, and fishing.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Demak

  1. Mount Muria Trail - The Mount Muria trail is a challenging hiking route that takes visitors to the highest point in Central Java. It is a 2-day hike with a 20-kilometer trail.
  2. Karanghawu Reservoir Trail - The Karanghawu Reservoir Trail is a scenic hiking route that offers beautiful views of the surrounding area. It is a 1-hour hike with a 5-kilometer trail.
  3. Pantai Kartini Beach Trail - The Pantai Kartini Beach Trail is a leisurely bike route that offers a beautiful scenic ride along the coastline. It is a 1-hour bike ride with a 10-kilometer trail.

Best Things to Do in Demak: Activities and Tours

  1. Walking Tour of Demak - A walking tour of Demak is the best way to explore the city’s historical and cultural landmarks. Visitors can take a guided tour of the Great Mosque of Demak, Museum of Demak Kingdom, Tomb of Raden Patah, and Makam Syekh Jumadil Kubro.
  2. Sunset at Pantai Kartini Beach - Witness the spectacular sunset at Pantai Kartini Beach and enjoy a serene environment. Visitors can also take a stroll along the coastline and relax with a coconut drink.
  3. Cycling Tour in Demak - Visitors can rent a bike and take a cycling tour of Demak. They can explore the city’s beautiful landmarks, cultural attractions, and scenic views.
  4. Fishing at Karanghawu Reservoir - Fishing at Karanghawu Reservoir is a popular activity among locals and visitors alike. It offers a serene environment and is home to several fish species.

Food and Drink Scene of Demak

Must-Try Dishes

  1. Soto Demak - Soto Demak is a traditional chicken soup that is made with coconut milk and various spices. It is a staple of Central Javanese cuisine and is a must-try dish when visiting Demak.
  2. Nasi Liwet - Nasi Liwet is a rice dish that is cooked with coconut milk, herbs, and spices. It is served with various side dishes such as chicken, tofu, and vegetables.
  3. Getuk Goreng - Getuk Goreng is a fried cassava dish that is served as a snack or dessert. It is made with grated cassava, sugar, and coconut milk.
  4. Es Dawet - Es Dawet is a refreshing dessert made with coconut milk, palm sugar, and pandan-flavored rice flour jelly.

Local Beverages

  1. Jamu - Jamu is a traditional herbal drink that is made with various medicinal plants and spices. It is believed to have health benefits and is a popular drink among locals.
  2. Wedang Jahe - Wedang Jahe is a hot ginger drink that is served with sugar and spices. It is a popular drink during the rainy season and is believed to have health benefits.

Where to Stay in Demak: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  2. Grand Dian Hotel - Grand Dian Hotel is a mid-range hotel that offers modern facilities and a convenient location.
  3. Griya Guest House - Griya Guest House is a charming guesthouse that provides a homely environment and personalized services.

What to Buy in Demak: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  1. Batik - Batik is a traditional Javanese fabric that is hand-dyed with various patterns and designs. It is a unique souvenir that showcases the local culture and traditions.
  2. Keris - Keris is a traditional Javanese dagger that is believed to have mystical properties. It is a popular souvenir for collectors and enthusiasts.
  3. Gethuk - Gethuk is a traditional snack that is made with cassava cake and sweet fillings such as coconut, chocolate, and cheese. It is a unique souvenir for food lovers.

Local Culture and Customs of Demak

  1. Islam - Demak is a predominantly Muslim city, and visitors are expected to dress modestly and respect the local customs and traditions.
  2. Greeting - Indonesians are known for their warm and friendly greeting, and visitors are expected to reciprocate the same.
  3. Elders - Indonesians hold great respect for their elders, and visitors are expected to show reverence to the elderly.

Best Time to Visit Demak

The best time to visit Demak is during the dry season, which runs from April to September. The weather is pleasant, with low humidity and comfortable temperatures. However, visitors should avoid the peak season, which falls in July and August, as the city can be crowded and prices may be higher.

Do’s and Don’ts of Demak

Do:

  • Dress modestly in public places, especially around religious sites.
  • Respect the local customs and traditions.
  • Learn some basic Indonesian phrases to communicate with the locals.

Don’t:

  • Disrespect the local customs and traditions.
  • Touch or climb on religious monuments and statues.
  • Drink tap water, always drink bottled water.

Safety and Health

Safety in Demak

Demak is generally a safe place to visit. However, visitors should take customary precautions such as keeping an eye on their belongings, being aware of their surroundings, and avoiding isolated areas at night.

Health in Demak

Visitors should drink bottled water and avoid eating raw street food to avoid stomach problems. It is also recommended to get routine immunizations and consult a doctor before traveling.

Getting to Demak: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Demak

The nearest airports to Demak are Ahmad Yani International Airport in Semarang and Adisumarmo International Airport in Surakarta. Both airports have domestic and international flights.

Bus Stations Near Demak

The main bus stations near Demak are Semarang Poncol Station and Semarang Tawang Station. From the stations, visitors can take a taxi or bus to Demak.

Other Options

Visitors can also rent a car or hire a driver to travel to Demak from nearby cities.

How to Get Around in Demak

The best way to get around in Demak is by renting a motorbike or a bicycle. Visitors can also take a taxi or ojek (motorbike taxi) to travel short distances.

Where to Go Next from Demak: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  1. Semarang - Semarang is the capital city of Central Java and is located less than an hour from Demak. It is known for its Dutch colonial architecture, Chinese influences, and bustling food scene.
  2. Borobudur Temple - Borobudur Temple is a UNESCO World Heritage Site that is located two hours from Demak. It is the largest Buddhist temple in the world and offers a fascinating insight into the history and culture of Java.
  3. Mount Merbabu - Mount Merbabu is another popular hiking destination that is located three hours from Demak. It offers a challenging hike and picturesque views of Central Java.

Conclusion

Demak is a hidden gem in Central Java that offers a unique blend of Islamic and Javanese culture. Visitors can explore the city’s historical and cultural landmarks, enjoy scenic natural attractions, and savor delicious local cuisine. With this comprehensive guide, visitors can plan their adventure and make the most of their trip to Demak.
