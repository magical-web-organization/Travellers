Introduction to Jepara

Located in Central Java, Indonesia, Jepara is a coastal town known for its rich cultural heritage, natural beauty, and outdoor activities. Jepara is easily accessible and is only a few hours away from major cities like Semarang and Yogyakarta, making it a perfect getaway for tourists seeking an off-the-beaten-path destination. In this travel guide, we will walk you through the best places to visit, activities to do, food to try, and accommodations to book in Jepara.

List of Places to Visit in Jepara

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Museum RA Kartini: This museum showcases the life and achievements of Kartini, a famous Indonesian feminist and national heroine.
  • Puri Maerokoco: A museum that displays a replica of traditional Javanese houses, including the royal palace and traditional crafts workshops.
  • Masjid Agung Jepara: A historic mosque built in the 16th century, with intricate Islamic architectural design.

Natural Attractions of Jepara: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Karimunjawa Islands: A group of 27 coral islands with crystal clear waters, vibrant marine life, and white sand beaches. Popular activities include snorkelling, diving, and island hopping.
  • Teluk Awur Beach: A favourite spot to watch the sunrise or sunset, with golden sand and calm waters. Perfect for swimming or relaxing.
  • Ungaran Waterfall: An impressive three-tier waterfall with panoramic views of lush forestry.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Jepara

  • Mount Muria: A popular hike for its challenging terrain, offering panoramic views of the surrounding plains and rice fields.
  • Karimunjawa National Park: The park boasts several hiking trails that lead to various scenic spots, including the crown jewel of the park, the Royal Island of Menjangan Besar.

Best Things to Do in Jepara: Activities and Tours

  • Traditional Batik Workshop: Join a batik-making workshop to learn the traditional technique of wax-resist dyeing.
  • Mangrove Forest Tour: Take a boat tour through the mangrove forest to appreciate the rich biodiversity of the area.
  • Culinary Tour: Sample the local cuisine through a cooking class or a food tour, where you can try signature dishes like nasi liwet, seafood, and burnt coconut ice cream.

Food and Drink Scene of Jepara

Must-Try Dishes

  • Nasi Liwet: A rice dish cooked in coconut milk and served with chicken, tofu, tempeh, and chili sauce.
  • Seafood: Jepara is a coastal town that is known for its fresh seafood dishes, such as grilled prawns, squid, and fish.
  • Burnt Coconut Ice Cream: A popular dessert made from burnt coconut flakes and rich coconut milk.

Local Beverages

  • Jamu: A traditional Indonesian herbal drink with ingredients that vary from turmeric, ginger, to tamarind.
  • Arak: A distilled alcoholic beverage made from fermented coconut sap and is often mixed with other drinks and fruits.

Where to Stay in Jepara: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • The Gecho Inn Country Side: A rustic accommodation with a beautiful garden and swimming pool, offering a tranquil retreat for visitors.
  • Kura-Kura Resort: A seaside resort on Karimunjawa Island that offers a wide range of activities, including snorkelling and island hopping.
  • The Shanka Villa: A luxurious villa with a private pool and splendid views that are perfect for a romantic getaway.

What to Buy in Jepara: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Jepara Furniture: The town is known for its well-crafted wooden furniture and antique pieces.
  • Batik: The traditional Indonesian fabric that is decorated with intricate designs and patterns that tell stories and legends.
  • Local Snacks: Pick up some snacks like wingko babat or kue gaplek, made of delicate coconut and cassava that make for great souvenirs.

Local Culture and Customs of Jepara

The majority of the population in Jepara is Muslim, with a handful of Hindu and Buddhist minorities. Visitors are expected to respect the local culture and wear modest clothing when visiting religious attractions.

Best Time to Visit Jepara

The best time to visit Jepara is from April to August, during the dry season, when you can enjoy the outdoor activities and beautiful scenery. Avoid visiting in December and January, which experience frequent downpours and rough sea conditions.

Do’s and Don’ts of Jepara

Do:

  • Learn a few basic phrases in Indonesian to communicate with the locals.
  • Explore local markets and try the street food.
  • Respect the local customs and traditions.

Don’t:

  • Wear revealing clothing when visiting religious sites.
  • Litter or damage the natural attractions and their surrounding environment.
  • Disrespect the culture and customs of the locals.

Safety and Health

Safety in Jepara

Jepara is a relatively safe destination. Visitors should exercise the same caution as in any other place and be aware of petty crimes like pickpocketing and scams.

Health in Jepara

Ensure that you have all the necessary vaccines before travelling to Jepara. Visitors are discouraged from drinking tap water and should opt for bottled water instead.

Getting to Jepara: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Jepara

Achmad Yani International Airport (SMG) in Semarang is the nearest airport to Jepara.

Bus Stations Near Jepara

There are several bus operators that offer a direct route between Jakarta and Jepara, including Laju Prima, Kramat Jati, and ASD. You can catch a bus from notable cities in Java like Yogyakarta, Semarang, or Surabaya.

Other Options

You can also hire a private car or a taxi service that will take you directly to Jepara.

How to Get Around in Jepara

The best way to get around Jepara is by renting a motorbike, which is readily available in the town centre. You can also take a taxi or a becak, a traditional Indonesian cycle-rickshaw, which is ideal for trips within the city.

Where to Go Next from Jepara: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Semarang: A bustling city located an hour’s drive from Jepara and offers a range of attractions, including the historical district of Kota Lama, the Lawang Sewu building, and the Sam Poo Kong temple.
  • Yogyakarta: A cultural hub in Central Java that offers visitors a chance to explore the famous Borobudur temple, the Kraton Museum, and the colourful street art scene in the city.

Conclusion

Jepara is a hidden gem in Indonesia that combines cultural richness, natural beauty, and outdoor activities. We hope that this travel guide has given you an overview of everything Jepara has to offer and inspires you to plan an unforgettable trip to this coastal town.
