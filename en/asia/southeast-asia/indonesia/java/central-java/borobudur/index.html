Introduction to Borobudur

Borobudur is an ancient Buddhist temple complex located in Central Java, Indonesia. It was built in the 9th century during the reign of the Sailendra dynasty and is now considered one of the world’s largest Buddhist temples. Borobudur is a UNESCO World Heritage Site and a must-visit destination in Indonesia. This tour guide will help you plan your visit to Borobudur by providing information on places to visit, hiking and biking routes, best things to do, food and drink scene, where to stay, safety and health, getting to Borobudur, and nearby destinations.

List of Places to Visit in Borobudur

Borobudur is divided into three levels: the first level represents the world of desire, the second level represents the world of form, and the third level represents the world of formlessness.

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  1. Borobudur Temple: The main attraction here, the temple is the largest Buddhist temple in the world and has been recognized as a UNESCO World Heritage Site since 1991.
  2. Samudra Raksa Museum: This museum features artifacts and collections related to the history of Borobudur and the Buddhist religion.

Natural Attractions of Borobudur: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  1. Menoreh Hills: Hiking trails in Menoreh Hills offer breathtaking views of the surrounding countryside.
  2. Punthuk Setumbu: A popular viewpoint to watch the sunrise over Borobudur.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Borobudur

Biking and hiking are great ways to explore Borobudur and its surrounding areas. Here are some hiking and biking routes you can explore.

  1. Gombong Mountain Trail: A tough but rewarding mountain bike trail that leads through villages, bamboo forests and up to Mount Gombong.
  2. Chicken Church Hiking Trail: A 30-minute hike through a dense forest leads to the iconic “chicken church” a unique church-shaped like a chicken is the perfect spot for photography.

Best Things to Do in Borobudur: Activities and Tours

  1. Sunrise Tour: Watch the sunrise from Punthuk Setumbu.
  2. Sunset Tour: Admire the stunning sunset view from Candi Pawon area.
  3. Borobudur Cycling Tour: Experience the natural beauty of Borobudur while cycling through rice paddies, and traditional Javanese villages.

Week-Long Itinerary for Borobudur

Day 1: Arrive in Yogyakarta and check in at hotel. Day 2: Tour Borobudur temple and Samudra Raksa Museum. Day 3: Go on a cycling tour around Borobudur area. Day 4: Visit the Mendut temple and Candi Pawon area. Day 5: Hike to the Chicken Church and explore the Menoreh hills. Day 6: Visit Kulon Progo and go on a river-rafting adventure. Day 7: Depart from Yogyakarta.

Food and Drink Scene of Borobudur

Must-Try Dishes

  1. Gudeg: A traditional Javanese dish made from young unripe jackfruit, usually served with rice.
  2. Soto: A soup made from chicken or beef with noodles, beans sprouts, and limes.

Local Beverages

  1. Wedang Ronde: A hot drink made from ginger, peanut, and glutinous rice balls.
  2. Es Dawet: A refreshing drink made from coconut milk, palm sugar, and pandan-flavored jelly balls.

Where to Stay in Borobudur: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  1. Plataran Heritage Borobudur Hotel: A luxurious 5-star hotel located in the middle of rice fields with a view of Borobudur Temple.
  2. Manohara Resort: The only resort within the temple complex, offers stunning views and convenient access to Borobudur Temple.

What to Buy in Borobudur: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  1. Wayang Puppet: Traditional Javanese shadow puppets made from leather or banana trees.
  2. Batik: Handmade fabric with Javanese motifs.

Local Culture and Customs of Borobudur

  1. Dress Appropriately: When visiting Borobudur temple complex, respect the local culture by dressing modestly and covering your shoulders and knees.
  2. Respect the Monks: Pay respect to the monks and refrain from taking photos of them without their permission.

Best Time to Visit Borobudur

The best time to visit Borobudur is during the dry season from May to September, where visitors can enjoy the sunrise and sunset without being disrupted by rainy weather.

Do’s and Don’ts of Borobudur

Do:

  1. Keep your ticket stub with you at all times.
  2. Follow the signs and don’t climb on the temple.

Don’t:

  1. Bring food or drink inside the temple complex.
  2. Wear revealing clothing inside the temple complex.

Safety and Health

Safety in Borobudur

  1. Be aware of petty crime, such as pickpocketing in crowded areas.
  2. Do not leave your valuables unattended.

Health in Borobudur

  1. Be aware of the tropical climate and stay hydrated.
  2. Wear sunscreen and mosquito repellent to protect yourself from insect bites.

Getting to Borobudur: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Borobudur

  1. Adisutjipto International Airport: Yogyakarta’s main airport, located 42 km from Borobudur Temple.
  2. Soekarno-Hatta International Airport: Jakarta’s main airport, located 540 km from Borobudur Temple.

Bus Stations Near Borobudur

  1. Jombor Bus Terminal: Located in Yogyakarta, buses to Borobudur are available every 30 minutes.
  2. Adaar Arjosari Bus Terminal: Located in Semarang, buses to Borobudur are available every hour.

Other Options

  1. Private Car Rental: Guests can rent a car with a driver to take them to Borobudur.
  2. Train: The closest train station to Borobudur is Tugu Station in Yogyakarta, takes 90 minutes by car to get to Borobudur.

How to Get Around in Borobudur

The best way to get around Borobudur is by renting a bike or motorcycle. Walking is also a good option as most attractions are located close to each other.

Where to Go Next from Borobudur: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  1. Yogyakarta: The cultural center of Java, located 42 km from Borobudur, known for its beautiful temples and traditional Javanese puppet shows.
  2. Prambanan: A beautiful Hindu temple complex, located 57 km from Borobudur.

Conclusion

Borobudur is one of Indonesia’s main tourist destinations and a must-visit location for those interested in exploring the country’s rich culture and heritage. From the centuries-old Buddhist temple complex to beautiful natural landscapes and outdoor adventures, Borobudur has something for everyone. Plan your visit accordingly, respect the local culture, and follow the safety guidelines to ensure a safe and enjoyable trip.
