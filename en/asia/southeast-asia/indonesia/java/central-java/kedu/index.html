Introduction to Kedu

Located in Central Java, Kedu is a popular region in the heart of Indonesia. Boasting of many cultural and natural attractions, Kedu offers tourists a chance to experience Java’s rich cultural heritage and immerse themselves in its natural beauty.

List of Places to Visit in Kedu

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Kedu is rich in history and culture and has many museums and historical places to explore, such as:

  1. Borobudur: This UNESCO World Heritage site is the world’s largest Buddhist temple, featuring intricate carvings and statues.

  2. Mendut Temple: Another Buddhist temple built in the 9th-century, Mendut is well-known for its three-meter tall statue of the Buddha.

  3. Pawon Temple: A temple dedicated to the goddess of fertility and agriculture, known for its intricate carvings and design.

  4. Prambanan Temple: A UNESCO World Heritage site, Prambanan is the largest Hindu temple in Indonesia, dedicated to the gods Shiva, Vishnu, and Brahma.

Natural Attractions of Kedu: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Kedu offers some of the best outdoor experiences in Central Java, such as:

  1. Mount Merbabu: A popular hiking destination, offering a challenging trek to the summit and stunning views of the surrounding landscape.

  2. Selogriyo Temple: An ancient temple located amidst lush green forests and scenic views, Selogriyo is popular amongst hikers and trekkers.

  3. Sumbing-Sindoro Geopark: A region of important geological and cultural sources where many mountaineers and hikers explore.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Kedu

Kedu offers many hiking and biking routes for tourists to explore, such as:

  1. Merbabu Trekking: A two-day trek to the summit of Mount Merbabu, providing a challenging but rewarding experience and picturesque scenery.

  2. Sumbing-Sindoro Circuit: A moderate trek that allows hikers to explore the stunning natural beauty of the Sumbing-Sindoro Geopark.

  3. Cycling Tour: A leisurely cycling tour through Kedu’s countryside, visiting local villages, and exploring the region’s natural beauty.

Best Things to Do in Kedu: Activities and Tours

There are many activities and tours available in Kedu, such as:

  1. Sunrise Borobudur Tour: A once-in-a-lifetime experience, watching the sunrise over the Borobudur Temple.

  2. Cultural Discovery Tour: A guided tour through Kedu’s cultural and historical sites, providing an insight into Java’s rich heritage.

  3. White Water Rafting Tour: A thrilling white water rafting adventure on the Elo river, passing through lush jungles and stunning scenery.

Week-Long Itinerary for Kedu

Day 1: Visit Borobudur and surrounding areas. Day 2: Explore Prambanan Temple and Ratu Boko Palace. Day 3: Trek Mount Merbabu or Sumbing-Sindoro. Day 4: Visit Selogriyo Temple and surrounding areas. Day 5: Take cycling tour around Kedu’s countryside. Day 6: Go for white water rafting on Elo river. Day 7: Relax and enjoy a spa day in Kedu’s luxurious resorts.

Food and Drink Scene of Kedu

Must-Try Dishes

Kedu offers a range of tasty dishes to try, such as:

  1. Soto Kedu: A spicy chicken soup served with rice cakes.

  2. Tahu Petis: A tofu dish served with fermented shrimp paste and chili.

  3. Gudeg Kandangan: A jack fruit stew served with rice.

Local Beverages

  1. Wedang Ronde: A hot, sweet drink made with ginger, peanuts, and sticky rice balls.

  2. Jamu: A traditional herbal drink made from natural ingredients, believed to have medicinal properties.

Where to Stay in Kedu: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Kedu offers various accommodations for tourists, such as:

  1. Plataran Borobudur Resort and Spa: A luxurious resort located near Borobudur Temple, offering stunning views and amazing amenities.

  2. Amata Borobudur Resort: A tranquil resort located near Borobudur Temple, offering stylish design and outstanding services.

  3. Omah Garengpoeng: A restored traditional Javanese house offering cozy accommodation with modern amenities.

What to Buy in Kedu: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Tourists can find many souvenirs and local products to buy. Some of the best things to buy in Kedu are:

  1. Batik artwork: A traditional Indonesian fabric featuring vibrant patterns and designs.

  2. Pottery and Ceramics: Kedu is famous for its unique pottery and ceramics, made from local clays and techniques.

  3. Kopi Luwak: A rare and expensive coffee made from the droppings of the Asian Palm Civet.

Local Culture and Customs of Kedu

Kedu is rich in history and culture, and it is essential for tourists to respect local customs and traditions. Some of the customs and traditions in Kedu are:

  1. Javanese Culture: Kedu is home to one of the most dominant ethnic groups in Indonesia, the Javanese.

  2. Respect for Elders: Respect and honor for elders are essential in Javanese culture.

Best Time to Visit Kedu

The best time to visit Kedu is between June and September, which is the dry season. During the dry season, visitors can experience favorable weather conditions for outdoor activities and sightseeing.

Do’s and Don’ts of Kedu

Do:

  1. Respect local customs and traditions.
  2. Dress appropriately when visiting religious sites.
  3. Try the local cuisine and explore the local markets.

Don’t:

  1. Disrespect local customs and traditions.
  2. Wear revealing clothing when visiting religious sites.
  3. Use your left hand to give, receive, or eat food.

Safety and Health

Safety in Kedu

Kedu is generally safe, but tourists should take regular precautions, such as:

  1. Use reputable tour operators and transportation services.
  2. Keep valuables safe and secure.
  3. Avoid walking alone at night.

Health in Kedu

Tourists should take precautions to prevent illnesses such as:

  1. Drink bottled water only.
  2. Use sunscreen and insect repellent.
  3. Consult a travel doctor for vaccination requirements before visiting Kedu.

Getting to Kedu: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Kedu

The nearest airport to Kedu is Adisucipto International Airport in Yogyakarta, approximately an hour’s drive away.

Bus Stations Near Kedu

There are many bus stations in Yogyakarta, with regular bus services to Kedu.

Other Options

Taxis and private cars are also available, and many hotels and resorts offer airport and bus station transfers.

How to Get Around in Kedu

Tourists can get around Kedu by:

  1. Hiring a car or motorcycle.
  2. Using local transportation such as buses, taxis, or ride-sharing services.
  3. Renting a bicycle to explore the countryside.

Where to Go Next from Kedu: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  1. Yogyakarta: A cultural and historical city filled with museums and temples.

  2. Mount Merapi: An active volcano, located just outside of Kedu.

  3. Solo: A bustling city renowned for its batik industry and historic palaces.

Conclusion

Kedu is an excellent destination for tourists seeking to explore Indonesia’s rich culture and natural beauty. With a vast range of attractions, activities, accommodations, and delicious cuisine, Kedu promises to provide an unforgettable experience for all travelers.
