Introduction to Solo

Located in Central Java, Indonesia, Solo (also known as Surakarta) is a hidden gem for travelers looking for an authentic Javanese experience. Solo offers a unique blend of traditional Javanese culture and modern city life, and is home to some of the best culinary and artistic scenes in the region. Despite being overshadowed by its neighboring city, Yogyakarta, Solo is definitely worth a visit if you want to explore the heart of Java.

List of Places to Visit in Solo

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Keraton Kasunanan

The Keraton Kasunanan, also known as the Surakarta Palace, is one of the oldest surviving Javanese palaces and is still home to the royal family. While it is still a functioning palace, visitors can explore the pavilions and museums inside, which give insight into Javanese history, culture and way of life.

Radya Pustaka Museum

Built in 1890, the Radya Pustaka Museum is the oldest museum in Indonesia. It boasts a collection of over 44,000 items, including manuscripts, historical documents, and cultural artifacts from all over Java. Visitors can explore 2 floors of exhibits and rooms, including a library with an impressive collection of rare books.

Mangkunegaran Palace

Built in the 18th century, the Mangkunegaran Palace is another important cultural landmark in Solo. The palace is open to the public, and visitors can see the beautiful architecture of the palace buildings, the Javanese art collection, and performances of classical Javanese music and dance.

Natural Attractions of Solo: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Tawangmangu Waterfall

Located about 30 km from the city center, Tawangmangu Waterfall is a beautiful natural wonder nestled in the mountains. The waterfall has a height of about 80 meters and is surrounded by lush trees and greenery. Visitors can hike the trails and take in the stunning views of the waterfall and surrounding landscape.

Mount Lawu

For those seeking a more strenuous outdoor adventure, Mount Lawu is a great option. It is the highest mountain in Central Java and offers incredible hiking and biking routes, as well as picturesque views of the surrounding landscape. There are several routes to choose from, ranging from easy to challenging.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Solo

Flora Hill

The Flora Hill route is a great option for those seeking an easy, scenic hike. The trail is about 3 km long and takes visitors through a stunning botanical garden, showcasing the natural beauty of Solo.

Ngarsopuro Night Market

If you’re looking for a more low-key adventure, biking through the Ngarsopuro Night Market is a great way to experience the local culture. The market is famous for its street food, street performers, and affordable souvenirs.

Best Things to Do in Solo: Activities and Tours

Batik Workshop

One of the most famous handicrafts of Java is Batik art. Several workshops in Solo offer classes in traditional Batik dyeing techniques. It’s a fun and unique way to learn about Javanese culture and create your own souvenir.

Village Cycling Tour

The villages surrounding Solo are known for their traditional Javanese way of life. A cycling tour is a great way to explore these areas, meet locals, and sample traditional Javanese cuisine,

Food and Drink Scene of Solo

Must-Try Dishes

Gudeg

Gudeg is a traditional Javanese dish made from young jackfruit, coconut milk, and spices. It is often served with chicken, boiled eggs, and steamed rice.

Sate Buntel

Sate Buntel is a unique variation of Indonesia’s famous satay. It is made with ground minced meat, wrapped around a stick and grilled over a charcoal fire.

Local Beverages

Bir Pletok

Bir Pletok is a traditional Indonesian drink made from a blend of herbs and spices. It is served hot and is a great way to warm up on a cold night in Solo.

Wedang Ronde

Wedang Ronde is a hot dessert drink made of ginger, glutinous rice balls, and peanuts, perfect for warming up on a chilly afternoon.

Where to Stay in Solo: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Alila Solo

Alila Solo is a luxury hotel located in the heart of Solo. The hotel offers spacious rooms and suites, as well as a rooftop bar, swimming pool, and fitness center.

Roemahkoe Heritage Hotel

Roemahkoe Heritage Hotel is a charming boutique hotel housed in a colonial-era building. The hotel offers beautifully decorated rooms, a swimming pool, and a garden area for guests to relax.

What to Buy in Solo: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Batik

As mentioned before, Batik is a traditional art form in Java, and Solo is known for producing high-quality Batik cloth. Visit the Pasar Klewer market for a wide range of Batik products, including handbags, clothes, and home decor.

Wayang Puppets

Wayang Puppets are a traditional Javanese art form and are great souvenirs to bring home. There are several shops around Solo that sell high-quality puppets and also offer puppet-making classes.

Local Culture and Customs of Solo

Javanese culture is known for its emphasis on harmony, respect, and community. Visitors to Solo should dress modestly when visiting religious or cultural sites, and always ask for permission before taking photos of locals.

Best Time to Visit Solo

The best time to visit Solo is between May and October when the weather is dry and pleasant. It is important to note that Solo can get quite busy during peak season, so book your accommodations and activities in advance.

Do’s and Don’ts of Solo

Do:

  • Discover the local culinary scene
  • Learn about traditional Javanese arts and crafts
  • Respect local customs and cultures

Don’t:

  • Wear revealing clothing when visiting religious or cultural sites
  • Do not litter in public areas
  • Do not disregard local customs and culture

Safety and Health

Safety in Solo

Solo is generally a safe city for travelers. However, it is important to take usual precautions such as avoiding unlit areas at night and being aware of pickpockets in busy areas.

Health in Solo

Visitors to Solo should drink bottled or boiled water to avoid stomach upsets. It’s also a good idea to bring insect repellent to protect against mosquito-borne illnesses.

Getting to Solo: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Solo

Solo is served by the Adisumarmo International Airport, and there are several direct flights to and from major cities in Indonesia, such as Jakarta and Bali.

Bus Stations Near Solo

The city has two main bus terminals, Tirtonadi and Solo Balapan. From these terminals, travelers can easily reach other cities around Java.

Other Options

There are also several trains that run from Yogyakarta to Solo every day, making it an easy and affordable option for travelers.

How to Get Around in Solo

The best way to get around Solo is by taxi or the local mode of transportation, the becak (a three-wheeled bicycle taxi). Bikes and motorcycles are also available for rental, making it easy for visitors to explore the city on their own.

Where to Go Next from Solo: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Yogyakarta

Located only an hour’s drive from Solo, Yogyakarta is a great destination for those looking to explore more of Java. The city is home to famous cultural landmarks, such as Borobudur and Prambanan Temple.

Dieng Plateau

Dieng Plateau is a group of volcanic mountains, located in the central highlands of Java. The area is known for its stunning natural landscapes and is a great destination for hiking, camping, and exploring traditional Javanese culture.

Conclusion

Solo is a hidden gem in Central Java, offering a unique blend of traditional Javanese culture and modern city life. Whether you’re interested in exploring historical landmarks, outdoor adventures, or trying out traditional Javanese cuisine, there’s something for everyone in Solo. With this comprehensive guide, you’ll be able to plan the perfect trip to this fascinating city.
