Introduction to Gunung Kidul

Nestled in the the province of Yogyakarta Special Region, Central Java, Indonesia, Gunung Kidul is an enchanting travel destination. Boasting of limestone hills, azure coastlines, and serene waterfalls, it’s not hard to see why this region attracts visitors from different parts of the world. The district’s name “Gunung Kidul” itself means “south mountain” and the region is a rugged, hilly terrain often compared to a scene from “Jurassic Park” with its greenery and dense jungle canopy.

Gunung Kidul’s unique combination of natural landscapes and rich cultural heritage makes it the ideal location for adventurers, history enthusiasts, and nature lovers.

List of Places to Visit in Gunung Kidul

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Kretek Museum - A museum dedicated to the production and history of kretek cigarettes in Indonesia.
  • Sri Gethuk Waterfall - A waterfall with a rich cultural history with the locals even considering it sacred. It’s believed that those who swim in its pool will have their wishes granted.
  • Pindul Cave and River Tubing - A must-try adventure in Gunung Kidul where visitors can explore a cave and enjoy a floating journey through underground rivers.
  • Yogyakarta Keris Museum - Another museum, this time dedicated to the history and significance of the keris (Indonesian traditional dagger).
  • Candi Prambanan - A UNESCO World Heritage Site and one of the largest Hindu temples in Southeast Asia.

Natural Attractions of Gunung Kidul: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Jomblang Cave - A cave with a unique entrance that requires visitors to rappel down 60 meters to reach its floor. Visitors can admire the ancient geological wonders and the “lights of heaven” from the sunlight’s reflection on the cave’s underground river.
  • Timang Beach - An Instagram-worthy beach with a unique attraction—the gondola ride that takes visitors to a small island located 20 meters from the coast, and back.
  • Bukit Bintang - A hill with a stunning view of the southern coast, where visitors can easily have a picnic or watch the sunrise/sunset.
  • Sindoro-Manggolo Nature Reserve - A nature reserve with a variety of flora and fauna including the endangered Javan gibbons.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Gunung Kidul

Gunung Kidul is a haven for outdoor enthusiasts who love to explore the great outdoors. Here are some of the best hiking and biking routes to explore:

  • Gunung Api Purba Nglanggeran - A 700-meter high volcano with spectacular views, and an excellent spot to watch the sunrise or sunset.
  • Nglinggo Tea Plantation - A trek through this plantation provides visitors picturesque vistas of tea plantations, rivers, and flourishing hills.
  • Kiskendo Cave - A cave that can only be accessed by hiking for 45 minutes through the jungle, offering visitors a glimpse of Gunung Kidul’s unique flora and fauna.

Best Things to Do in Gunung Kidul: Activities and Tours

  • Paragliding - Take the skies on paragliding trips over Gunung Kidul’s stunning terrain.
  • White Water Rafting - An adventure that shouldn’t be missed. Gunung Kidul offers several packages for white-water rafting often with a picnic on the riverside.
  • Horseback Riding - A chance to explore Gunung Kidul’s countryside in a different way.
  • Caving - Crawling through crevices, and scaling steep inclines are perfect ways to explore the region’s unique geography.

Week-Long Itinerary for Gunung Kidul

Day 1: Arrive and settle in the city, explore the surrounding area.

Day 2: Visit Kretek Museum, learn about the history and production of the famous clove cigarettes.

Day 3: Caving adventure in Pindul Cave and River Tubing.

Day 4: Visit Candi Prambanan and surrounding areas.

Day 5: A day of outdoor adventure which includes paragliding or horseback riding at Bukit Bintang or Kiskendo Cave Trek.

Day 6: Timang Beach and Gondola ride.

Day 7: Departure day.

Food and Drink Scene of Gunung Kidul

Must-Try Dishes

  • Gudeg Yu Djum - Jackfruit stewed in a mixture of coconut milk and spices served with rice, chicken or fried eggs.
  • Sate Klathak - Skewered meat, usually lamb or beef, cooked over hot charcoal fire.
  • Kupat Glabed - Rice cakes wrapped in banana leaves and served with peanut sauce, shredded chicken, and soybean cake.

Local Beverages

  • Wedang Uwuh - A drink made of various herbs like lemongrass, ginger and pandan leaves, often known as the traditional Jamu drink.
  • Luwak Coffee - Dubbed as the world’s most expensive coffee produced through a unique process by the civet cats of Java, this coffee has a distinctive aroma and taste.

Where to Stay in Gunung Kidul: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Gunung Kidul offers several accommodation options to suit all budgets, such as:

  • Erga Bed And Breakfast - A small b&b located at the top of the mangrove tree with stunning views of the South Sea.
  • Mangunan Homestay - A homestay surrounded by rice fields and considerable views of the surrounding hills.
  • Queen of the South Resort - A resort located in one of Gunung Kidul’s main towns, offers guests a pool, spa facility and other amenities.

What to Buy in Gunung Kidul: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Batik Cloth - A traditional textile featuring intricate patterns and designs.
  • Lulur - A herbal mixture used for traditional Javanese spa treatments.
  • Sandalwood and other locally produced aromatic oils.

Local Culture and Customs of Gunung Kidul

Respect for the local customs and traditions is best observed while in Gunung Kidul. Visitors are encouraged to dress modestly and take off their shoes when entering homes or temples. Punctuality is valued, and visitors should arrive on time for social gatherings/events.

Best Time to Visit Gunung Kidul

Visitors can explore Gunung Kidul throughout the year, but the best time to visit is during the dry season (May to September) when the weather is warm and dry.

Do’s and Don’ts of Gunung Kidul

Do:

  • Accept the locals’ warm hospitality and respect their customs and traditions.
  • Be adventurous, explore the local attractions, and try the local food.
  • Carry cash since most small shops and vendors do not accept cards.

Don’t:

  • Litter during your visit. Keep the environment clean and respect the locals and their culture.
  • Wear revealing clothing in religious or culturally significant places.

Safety and Health

Safety in Gunung Kidul

It is important to stay updated on the weather patterns in the region. Flash floods and landslides are common during the rainy season. Visitors are advised to avoid traveling to Gunung Kidul during these periods.

Health in Gunung Kidul

Visitors are encouraged to get vaccinated against common diseases such as typhoid and hepatitis A. Tap water in Gunung Kidul is not safe to drink. It’s recommended to always carry a bottle of water and insect repellent while exploring the region.

Getting to Gunung Kidul: Transportation Options and Logistics

Gunung Kidul is located about 60 kilometers south of Yogyakarta.

Airports Near Gunung Kidul

Adi Sutjipto International Airport in Yogyakarta is the closest airport to Gunung Kidul.

Bus Stations Near Gunung Kidul

Terminal Giwangan in Yogyakarta has regular buses that go to Gunung Kidul.

Other Options

Taxis and hired cars are available in Yogyakarta to take visitors to Gunung Kidul.

How to Get Around in Gunung Kidul

The best way to explore Gunung Kidul is through hired taxis, rental vehicles, or motorbikes. Visitors can also opt for a guided tour to enjoy a convenient and hassle-free experience.

Where to Go Next from Gunung Kidul: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Visitors to Gunung Kidul can explore the surrounding regions, such as Yogyakarta, Solo, and Semarang, which have a rich cultural heritage and offer plenty of places to eat, shop, and explore.

Conclusion

Gunung Kidul’s natural beauty and cultural significance make it an unforgettable travel destination. From its caving, paragliding adventures, iconic beaches, and premium accommodations, there is something for every traveler in Gunung Kidul. Embrace the local customs, try malty cuisines in the region, and be prepared for an adventure of a lifetime.
