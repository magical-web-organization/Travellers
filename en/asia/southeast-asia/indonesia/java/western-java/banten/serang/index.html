Introduction to Serang

Serang is a historic city in the province of Banten, located in Western Java, Indonesia. Situated on the banks of the Cidurian River and surrounded by lush greenery, this charming city is known for its natural beauty, rich history, and cultural significance.

List of Places to Visit in Serang

Serang is home to numerous attractions that cater to all kinds of travelers, from nature lovers to history buffs. Here are some of the best places to visit in Serang:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Old Town Serang

Take a stroll through the historic Old Town Serang and witness the remnants of the colonial-era in Indonesia. The area is home to well-preserved Dutch-era buildings, including the former residency of the Banten Regent and the former Dutch Governor’s office.

Benteng Heritage Museum

Explore the city’s history - from its pre-colonial days to the present - at Benteng Heritage Museum, located in Benteng Pendem, about 5 miles from Serang City.

Cidanghiang Mosque

This picturesque mosque dates back to the 17th century and features unique architecture and intricate detailing. It is a must-visit for those interested in Islamic architecture.

Natural Attractions of Serang: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Ujung Kulon National Park

Witness the beauty of tropical rainforests, beaches, and coral reefs at the Ujung Kulon National Park, located approximately 100 km west of Serang. It’s home to the critically endangered Javan rhinoceros and other wildlife, making it a popular spot for nature lovers.

Mount Krakatau

Situated in the Sunda Strait, Mount Krakatau is an active volcano that last erupted in 2018. The volcano is accessible via boat tours and offers breathtaking views of the surrounding sea and nearby islands.

Curug Bitung Waterfall

Located in Sumur Village, approximately 33 km south of Serang City, the Curug Bitung Waterfall is a stunning three-level waterfall surrounded by lush vegetation. It’s a great spot for those seeking an adventure in nature.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Serang

Serang is a haven for outdoor enthusiasts looking to explore nature through hiking or biking. Two of the most popular trails are:

Gunung Pulosari Trail

A moderate hiking trail, Gunung Pulosari Trail is approximately 4 km and takes around 2-3 hours to reach the summit. The trail offers enchanting views of the city and beautiful natural surroundings.

Kanci Trail

Another popular trail is the Kanci Trail, a 10 km biking track that starts from Kanci Village and finishes in Cidahu Village. The trail offers beautiful views of rice fields, mountains, and local villages along the way.

Best Things to Do in Serang: Activities and Tours

There are plenty of activities you can enjoy in Serang that cater to all kinds of travelers. Here are some of the best things to do in Serang:

Tsunami Memorial

Located in Banten’s capital city of Serang, the Tsunami Memorial is dedicated to the 2004 Indian Ocean tsunami victims. The monument honors the more than 168,000 people who lost their lives in the disaster.

Explore Local Markets

The city is home to many local markets, including Pasar Anyar and Pasar Serang, where visitors can find local produce, handicrafts, and souvenirs to take back home.

Take a Cooking Class

Learn to cook local dishes at one of the city’s cooking schools, such as The Cookery or The Sambal Kitchen, and take home the skills to impress your friends and family with your newfound culinary expertise.

Week-Long Itinerary for Serang

Here’s a sample week-long itinerary for Serang, giving you an idea of what to expect in this beautiful city:

Day Activity
1 Explore Old Town Serang and Benteng Heritage Museum
2 Trek to the top of Gunung Pulosari or spend the day exploring natural attractions around the city such as Curug Bitung

Food and Drink Scene of Serang

Serang offers a variety of delicious food and drink options, blending traditional Indonesian and local dishes with international flavors.

Must-Try Dishes

Empal Gentong

A traditional beef soup with rich, spicy flavors, Empal Gentong is a local favorite.

Soto Betawi

Soto Betawi is a soup dish made with beef, potatoes, and a variety of spices, making it a tasty and hearty meal.

Local Beverages

Es Dawet Selasih

A sweet drink made with rice flour, coconut milk, and pandan leaves, Es Dawet Selasih is a delicious and refreshing local beverage.

Teh Poci

Teh Poci is a traditional Indonesian tea served in small clay teapots that add unique flavors to the tea.

Where to Stay in Serang: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Serang offers a variety of accommodation options to suit every traveler’s unique needs and preferences. Here are some of the best places to stay in Serang:

Aston Anyer Beach Resort

Located in nearby Anyer, approximately 30 km from Serang, the Aston Anyer Beach Resort offers stunning views of the ocean and provides a luxurious and relaxing retreat for guests.

Amaris Hotel Serang

For budget travelers, the Amaris Hotel Serang offers affordable and comfortable accommodation in the heart of the city.

What to Buy in Serang: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Serang is known for producing unique handicrafts and local products that make the perfect souvenirs to take back home.

Batik Banten

Batik Banten is a traditional Indonesian textile with stunning patterns and colors. Visitors should check out the Pasar Anyar market for an array of batik options.

Wayang Golek

Wayang Golek is a traditional wooden puppet that is handmade in Serang. Visitors should check out the Benteng Heritage Museum to learn more about the art of wayang golek.

Local Culture and Customs of Serang

Serang is a city that strongly values its cultural and religious customs. Visitors should be respectful of these customs and dress modestly when visiting mosques and other cultural sites.

Best Time to Visit Serang

The best time to visit Serang is between May and September, when the weather is dry and sunny. However, visitors should be prepared for occasional rain showers throughout the year.

Do’s and Don’ts of Serang

Do:

  • Be respectful of local customs and traditions.
  • Dress modestly when visiting mosques and other cultural sites.
  • Try the local food and beverages.

Don’t:

  • Disrespect religious customs and practices.
  • Wear revealing clothing or short shorts in public areas.
  • Engage in public displays of affection.

Safety and Health

Safety in Serang

Serang is a relatively safe city for tourists. However, visitors should avoid walking alone late at night and take appropriate precautions when traveling outside the city.

Health in Serang

Visitors traveling to Serang should consult their physician for any necessary vaccinations and medications. It’s also recommended to drink bottled water and practice good hygiene habits to avoid any potential health issues.

Getting to Serang: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Serang

The nearest airport to Serang is Soekarno-Hatta International Airport, which is approximately 75 km away. From there, travelers can take a taxi or private car to Serang.

Bus Stations Near Serang

Serang has a central bus station, Terminal Serang, that offers several bus routes to Jakarta and other nearby cities in Banten.

Other Options

Private car hires and taxis are also available to travel to Serang from nearby locations.

How to Get Around in Serang

Local transportation in Serang includes buses, taxis, and online ride-hailing services such as Grab and Go-Jek. Renting a car or motorbike is also a popular option for travelers seeking more independence and freedom to explore the city and surrounding areas.

Where to Go Next from Serang: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Serang is situated in a prime location, with several nearby destinations and excursions that offer breathtaking views and unique experiences. Here are some of the best nearby destinations:

Krakatau Islands

A group of islands located in the Sunda Strait, near the volcano of the same name. These islands offer stunning beaches, hiking trails, and water sports.

Pelabuhan Ratu

Located approximately 70 km southwest of Serang, Pelabuhan Ratu is a popular beach destination for surfers and those seeking a relaxing beach getaway.

Conclusion

Serang offers a unique blend of history, culture, and natural beauty that beckons travelers from all around the world. With this comprehensive travel guide, you’ll have all the information you need to plan an unforgettable trip to this hidden gem of western Java.
