Introduction to Banjar

Located in East Parahyangan, Banjar is a small town nestled in the mountains of Western Java. This town is known for its natural beauty, cultural heritage, and outdoor adventures. The town is a popular destination for both domestic and international tourists who want to explore the best of Indonesia. In this comprehensive guide, we will take you through the must-visit attractions, things to do, where to stay, and local customs in Banjar.

List of Places to Visit in Banjar

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

1. Museum of Banjar Heritage

The Museum of Banjar Heritage is a must-visit for history buffs. The museum showcases the culture and heritage of the Sundanese people which is the native people of Banjar. The museum features historical artifacts, traditional musical instruments, and traditional costumes of the Sundanese people.

2. Suryakencana Museum

The Suryakencana Museum is another cultural attraction you should not miss. The museum showcases the history and culture of the Priangan region which is Western Java. The museum features a collection of artifacts including traditional weapons, clothing, and pottery.

Natural Attractions of Banjar: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

1. The Green Canyon

The Green Canyon is one of the most popular natural attractions in Banjar. This is a beautiful canyon surrounded by lush green forests and rocky cliffs. Visitors can enjoy boat trips through the canyon, swim in the crystal clear water, and explore the surrounding natural beauty.

2. Cikawao and Cikaso Waterfalls

Banjar is home to two stunning waterfalls, Cikawao and Cikaso. Both waterfalls offer breathtaking views of cascading waterfalls surrounded by the lush greenery of the forested areas.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Banjar

Banjar also offers plenty of trails for hiking and biking enthusiasts. The following are some of the best routes in Banjar:

  • Gunung Sanggabuana
  • Gunung Lalakon
  • Gunung Adeng
  • Cipanas Soreang

Best Things to Do in Banjar: Activities and Tours

1. Green Canyon Tour

As mentioned earlier, the Green Canyon is one of the best natural attractions in Banjar. A boat trip through the canyon is a must-do experience, and there are several tour operators that offer this experience.

2. Waterfall Tour

A tour of the Cikawao and Cikaso waterfalls is also a popular activity among visitors. This tour lets visitors hike through beautiful nature to reach the waterfalls.

Week-Long Itinerary for Banjar

Here’s a suggested week-long itinerary for Banjar:

Day 1: Arrival in Banjar, check-in at your hotel, and explore the town. Day 2: Visit the Museum of Banjar Heritage and Suryakencana Museum. Day 3: Enjoy a boat tour through the Green Canyon. Day 4: Go on a waterfall tour and enjoy the natural beauty of Cikawao and Cikaso. Day 5: Explore the hiking trails of Gunung Sanggabuana and Gunung Lalakon. Day 6: Take a stroll along Cipanas Soreang and enjoy the beautiful views. Day 7: Fitness - Yoga or a Spa day then after that say goodbye to Banjar.

Food and Drink Scene of Banjar

Must-Try Dishes

1. Sate Maranggi

Sate Maranggi is a popular dish in Banjar that you must-try. It is a grilled beef dish that is served with a spicy dipping sauce.

2. Gado-Gado

Gado-Gado is a traditional Indonesian dish that is very popular in Banjar. It is a vegetable salad with peanut sauce dressing.

Local Beverages

1. Bandrek

Bandrek is a sweet and spicy drink made from ginger, brown sugar, and spices.

2. Es Degan

Es Degan is a refreshing drink made from young coconut juice flavored with sugar.

Where to Stay in Banjar: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

There are plenty of accommodation options in Banjar that cater to all budgets. Here are some of the best places to stay:

  • Santika Hotel Banjar
  • Pesona Alam Resort & Spa
  • Royal Safari Resort

What to Buy in Banjar: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

1. Batik Fabric

Indonesia is famous for the traditional Batik fabric, and Banjar offers plenty of shops where you can buy authentic and handwoven Batik.

2. Kris Sword

Kris Sword is an Indonesian weapon that is known for its unique design and craftsmanship. You can buy a Kris Sword from the local markets in Banjar as a souvenir or for display.

Local Culture and Customs of Banjar

The Sundanese people, the native people of Banjar, are known for their traditional culture and customs. One of the most prominent customs is the use of traditional music instruments such as angklung, rebab, and kecapi. Local festivals and traditional ceremonies are an excellent way to experience the local culture.

Best Time to Visit Banjar

The best time to visit Banjar is from April to October when the weather is dry and pleasant. However, the weather can change quickly, so it is essential to carry appropriate clothing and gear.

Do’s and Don’ts of Banjar

Here are some guidelines to follow during your trip to Banjar:

Do:

  • Respect the local customs and traditions.
  • Carry a good sunscreen, water bottle, and appropriate gear for outdoor activities.
  • Learn basic Indonesian Phrases like “terima kasih” which means Thank you

Don’t:

  • Wear revealing clothing when visiting religious places.
  • Litter or cause any harm to the environment.
  • Show disrespect to locals.

Safety and Health

Safety in Banjar

Banjar is generally considered safe for tourists. However, it is always recommended to take precautions when traveling to a new place. Avoid walking alone or in deserted areas at night, and be mindful of your belongings.

Health in Banjar

Healthcare facilities in Banjar are minimal, so it’s recommended to take proper precautions. Carry a first-aid kit, insect repellent, and be up-to-date with vaccinations.

Getting to Banjar: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Banjar

Husein Sastranegara International Airport in Bandung is the closest airport to Banjar, which is about two hours away by road.

Bus Stations Near Banjar

Banjar is well-connected to other cities in Indonesia via the bus network. The main bus station is Banjar Terminal.

Other Options

Taxis, private cabs, and rental cars are also available for transportation in Banjar.

How to Get Around in Banjar

The most common mode of transportation in Banjar is public transport such as taxis, trains, and buses. However, hiking and biking are also popular ways of exploring the town and its surroundings.

Where to Go Next from Banjar: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Banjar is a great base camp for exploring nearby attractions such as Keraton Kasepuhan Cirebon and Taman Safari Indonesia.

Conclusion

Banjar is a hidden gem in Western Java, with a unique blend of culture, history, and natural beauty. We hope this comprehensive guide has helped you plan your trip to Banjar and explore the best of Indonesia. Remember to respect and follow the local customs and traditions, and take proper precautions to have a safe and enjoyable trip.
