Introduction to Cimahi

Cimahi is a charming city located in the Parahyangan region of Western Java, Indonesia. It is surrounded by verdant hills and picturesque natural attractions, making it an ideal destination for nature-lovers and adventure-seekers. With its rich cultural heritage, historical landmarks, and mouth-watering cuisine, there is plenty to explore in Cimahi. Discover the best places to visit and things to do in this comprehensive tour guide for Cimahi, Indonesia.

List of Places to Visit in Cimahi

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Kampoeng Tulip - An open-air museum that showcases traditional Sundanese architecture and culture. Visitors can explore various traditional houses, watch cultural performances, and participate in workshops.

  • Cimahi Railroad Museum - A fascinating museum that exhibits the history of railroad transportation in Indonesia. The museum houses old steam trains, locomotives, and railway equipment.

  • Gedung Sate - A historical building that served as the headquarters of the Dutch East Indies government in the early 20th century. Visitors can admire the magnificent architecture and take a guided tour to learn about its history.

Natural Attractions of Cimahi: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Tangkuban Perahu - A popular natural attraction located in the northern outskirts of Cimahi. It is an active volcano that offers stunning panoramic views of the surrounding landscape.

  • Ciater Hot Springs - A natural hot spring located in the nearby town of Ciater. Visitors can relax in the warm waters and enjoy the scenic views of the surrounding mountains.

  • Walini Tea Plantation - A picturesque tea plantation that offers guided tours, tea-tasting sessions, and scenic walks through the plantations.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Cimahi

  • Hutan Raya Djuanda - A large natural park that offers various hiking trails and stunning views of the Bandung basin.

  • Babakan Siliwangi - A pristine forest and watershed that offers various hiking and biking trails. Visitors can also explore the nearby waterfall and enjoy the scenic views of the surrounding hills.

Best Things to Do in Cimahi: Activities and Tours

  • Sundanese Cooking Class - Learn how to cook traditional Sundanese dishes like Gado-gado, Sate Maranggi, and Soto Bandung.

  • Rafting Adventure - Enjoy a thrilling rafting adventure down the Citarik River, which offers a mix of adrenaline-pumping rapids and scenic views.

  • Night Market Tour - Explore the vibrant and bustling night market of Cimahi, where you can taste various local delicacies and shop for souvenirs.

Food and Drink Scene of Cimahi

Must-Try Dishes

  • Sate Maranggi - A grilled beef dish marinated in a mix of sweet soy sauce, galangal, garlic, and red chili.

  • Gado-gado - A mixed vegetable salad with peanut sauce dressing.

  • Soto Bandung - A traditional chicken soup with potatoes, bean sprouts, and rice.

Local Beverages

  • Bandrek - A hot and spicy beverage made with ginger, palm sugar, and cinnamon.

  • Sekoteng - A hot and sweet beverage made with ginger, lemongrass, and palm sugar.

Where to Stay in Cimahi: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Green Hill Hotel - A cozy hotel located in the city center, offering modern amenities and friendly service.

  • Sajan Villa Resort & Convention - A luxurious resort located on the outskirts of Cimahi, offering scenic views and a tranquil atmosphere.

  • Cimahi Homestay - A budget-friendly stay option with a cozy and comfortable atmosphere.

What to Buy in Cimahi: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Batik Fabric - Cimahi is known for its traditional batik fabric, which is made using a wax-resist dyeing technique.

  • Kue Cimahi - A traditional pastry that is sweet and savory, made with flour, eggs, sugar, and grated coconut.

  • Keripik Tempe - A popular snack made from thinly sliced tempeh, which is marinated in a mix of spices and deep-fried.

Local Culture and Customs of Cimahi

Cimahi is predominantly a Muslim city, and visitors are advised to dress modestly and respect local customs and traditions. The Sundanese culture is prevalent in Cimahi, and visitors can witness various traditional ceremonies and performances throughout the year.

Best Time to Visit Cimahi

The best time to visit Cimahi is from May to October, when the weather is dry and pleasant, and the natural attractions are at their best.

Do’s and Don’ts of Cimahi

Do:

  • Respect local customs and traditions, including dress codes and religious practices.

  • Try the local cuisine and beverages.

  • Explore the natural and cultural attractions of Cimahi.

Don’t:

  • Disrespect local beliefs and customs.

  • Engage in illegal activities, including drug use and gambling.

Safety and Health

Safety in Cimahi

Cimahi is generally a safe city for tourists, but visitors are advised to take precautions and be aware of their surroundings, especially at night. Pickpocketing and petty theft can occur in crowded areas, so it’s important to keep your belongings safe.

Health in Cimahi

Visitors to Cimahi are advised to take precautions against mosquito-borne illnesses like dengue fever and malaria. It’s also important to stay hydrated and avoid consuming tap water, which may not be safe for drinking.

Getting to Cimahi: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Cimahi

The nearest airport to Cimahi is the Husein Sastranegara International Airport, located in the nearby city of Bandung.

Bus Stations Near Cimahi

Cimahi has a central bus station that connects it to various cities in the region, including Jakarta, Bogor, and Bandung.

Other Options

Visitors can also reach Cimahi by train, as it has a train station that connects it to various cities in the region.

How to Get Around in Cimahi

The best way to get around in Cimahi is by car or motorcycle, which can be rented from various rental services in the city. Public transportation options like buses and taxis are also available.

Where to Go Next from Cimahi: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Bandung - A vibrant city known for its shopping, street food, and nightlife.

  • Pangandaran - A beach town located in the southern coast of Java, known for its stunning beaches and natural attractions.

  • Garut - A mountainous region located in the eastern part of Western Java, known for its natural hot springs and picturesque landscapes.
