Introduction to North

North, located in Parahyangan, Western Java, is a hidden gem on the island of Java, Indonesia. It is known for its rich cultural heritage, natural beauty, and outdoor adventures. North is home to picturesque landscapes, historical sites, and an exciting food scene, making it a must-visit destination for any avid traveler. This comprehensive guide will take you through the best places to visit, things to do, food to try, and where to stay in North, Bandung.

List of Places to Visit in North

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  1. Gedung Sate - The landmark building of Bandung, it is intricately designed like a sate with the skewer as the tower. The building houses the Governor’s office, but the main attraction is the architecture.
  2. Tangkuban Perahu - One of the most popular tourist attractions in Parahyangan, the active volcano is located 30 kilometers north of Bandung. It is known for its unique shape resembling an “upside-down boat” and stunning view of the surrounding area.
  3. Museum Sri Baduga - A museum dedicated to the Sundanese culture, showcasing the history, art, and craftsmanship of the Sundanese people.
  4. Kampung Gajah Wonderland - A family-friendly attraction that features playgrounds, fun rides, and an animal safari.

Natural Attractions of North: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  1. Dago Pakar - A hilly area formed from the eruption of Tangkuban Perahu. It is a popular spot for recreation, and outdoor activities like hiking and paragliding.
  2. Kawah Putih - A stunning crater lake located 50km from Bandung, it boasts a striking turquoise color due to the high sulfuric content. Visitors can soak in the beauty of the lake by taking a guided tour or trekking through the surrounding hills.
  3. Sapu Lidi Resort - An eco-resort that highlights the beauty of its natural surroundings with its rustic and traditional architecture. The resort is surrounded by forests and rice fields, offering a tranquil escape from the city life.
  4. Maribaya Waterfall - Located in Lembang, this waterfall is known for its natural beauty, crystal-clear water, and picturesque surroundings.

Hiking/Biking Routes of North

  1. Maribaya Nature Reserve Hiking Trail - This hiking trail is located in Lembang and is known for the scenic views of pine trees and coffee plantations. The trail leads to a stunning waterfall.
  2. Dago Endah Hiking Trail - A moderately challenging hiking trail that takes you through pine forests, tea plantations, and cinnamon trees. The trail is known for its stunning views of the city.
  3. Batu Cinta Cycling Track - Located near Jatinangor, it is a popular 8km cycling track that winds through rice fields, villages, and forests. The track is suitable for beginners and experienced cyclists.

Best Things to Do in North: Activities and Tours

  1. Rafting in Citarik River - One of the longest and most challenging rivers in Java, it is known for its stunning scenery and rapids of class III-IV. Visitors can enjoy the thrills of whitewater rafting with a guide.
  2. Hot Air Balloon Ride - A unique and unforgettable experience, visitors can take a hot air balloon ride over the picturesque landscape of Parahyangan. The flight offers stunning views of the surrounding countryside.
  3. Paragliding in Dago - An adrenaline-filled activity, visitors can take a tandem paragliding flight with a guide and enjoy the bird’s eye view of Bandung.
  4. Kelinci Village Tour - A guided tour to the Kelinci Village, it is known for its fluffy rabbits, picturesque vegetable gardens, and a unique presentation of traditional Sundanese handicrafts.

Week-Long Itinerary for North

Day 1: Arrival in Bandung, checking into the hotel, and a short visit to Gedung Sate. Day 2: A day trip to Kawah Putih, followed by a visit to Museum Sri Baduga. Day 3: A tour of Tangkuban Perahu, followed by a hot air balloon ride. Day 4: A day of outdoor adventures, including rafting in the Citarik River and a paragliding flight over Dago. Day 5: A visit to Maribaya Waterfall, followed by a hike in Maribaya Natural Reserve. Day 6: A visit to the Kelinci Village, followed by a bike ride through Batu Cinta Cycling Track. Day 7: Departure from Bandung, with a quick stop at the Sapu Lidi Resort.

Food and Drink Scene of North

Must-Try Dishes

  1. Lontong Kari Kepala Ikan - This is a traditional Indonesian dish of fish head curry served with lontong (rice cake). It is a must-try dish from Parahyangan.
  2. Sate Taichan - A variation of the original sate, it is a healthier option that involves chicken breast skewers marinated with chilies and tamarind sauce.
  3. Bakso - A popular Indonesian soup dish served with meatballs, rice noodles, and vegetables. It is a quick and hearty meal often served as a street food snack.
  4. Nasi Goreng - A classic Indonesian dish made with fried rice, vegetables, and meats. It is often served with a fried egg on top.

Local Beverages

  1. Bandrek - A traditional Sundanese hot beverage made with ginger, cinnamon, and palm sugar. It is often served as a hot drink during the rainy season.
  2. Teh Poci - A traditional Sundanese tea served with a clay teapot called poci. The tea is brewed with pandan leaves and is known for its refreshing taste.
  3. Bajigur - A traditional Sundanese hot beverage made with palm sugar, coconut, and ginger. It’s perfect for cold winter nights.

Where to Stay in North: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  1. Hilton Bandung - A luxurious and modern hotel located in the heart of Bandung. It offers world-class amenities and is perfect for leisure and business travelers.
  2. The Puncak - A cozy boutique hotel located near Tangkuban Perahu. The hotel offers stunning views of the surrounding area and is known for its warm hospitality.
  3. Wijaya Guesthouse - An affordable guesthouse located in Dago, it offers cozy rooms, a garden, and a shared kitchen. It is popular among backpackers and budget travelers.

What to Buy in North: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  1. Sundanese Batik - A traditional cloth made with intricate patterns and designs. It’s perfect as a gift or as a memorabilia to bring home.
  2. Angklung - A traditional Sundanese musical instrument made of bamboo. It is a unique gift to bring home and is often used in cultural performances.
  3. Keripik Tempe - A crispy and spicy soybean snack originating from Parahyangan. It is often sold in local markets as souvenirs.

Local Culture and Customs of North

In North, Sundanese culture is prevalent, and locals are proud of their heritage. Sundanese is a significant ethnic group in Parahyangan, and their customs and traditions are visible in their daily life. It is customary to greet people with a smile and a handshake, and displaying respect to elders is a sign of good manners. In mosque and temples, visitors are expected to wear appropriate attire and remove their shoes before entering.

Best Time to Visit North

The best time to visit North is between June to September, the dry season, when the weather is cool and pleasant, making outdoor activities enjoyable. During the rainy season, which starts from October to May, visitors can still explore the area, but some outdoor activities might get affected due to rainfall.

Do’s and Don’ts of North

Do:

  1. Dress modestly while visiting places of worship.
  2. Try the local food and interact with locals.
  3. Bargain while shopping in the local markets.
  4. Use a reputable tour company for outdoor activities.

Don’t:

  1. Avoid public display of affection.
  2. Do not litter in public places.
  3. Do not insult local customs or religious beliefs.
  4. Do not forget to carry sunscreen and a hat while exploring outdoors.

Safety and Health

Safety in North

North is generally safe for travelers. However, it is advised to take necessary precautions like avoiding isolated areas after dark, staying alert about your belongings, and keeping emergency numbers handy. It is also recommended to consult with your hotel or tour guide to avoid any safety hazards.

Health in North

Travelers are advised to carry adequate medication and travel insurance before visiting North. Medical facilities in Parahyangan are limited, and hospitals are often crowded, so visitors are recommended to take necessary precautions, including staying hydrated, avoiding street food, and using mosquito repellents to prevent any health issues.

Getting to North: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near North

The nearest international airport to North is Soekarno-Hatta International Airport in Jakarta, which is approximately 150 km away. Another option is Husein Sastranegara International Airport, located in Bandung city, which is well connected with the major cities in Indonesia.

Bus Stations Near North

Visitors can use public transportation buses, which are easily available in Bandung city. The main bus stations near North are Cicaheum and Ledeng Terminal.

Other Options

Visitors can also hire a taxi or a private car to reach North, which is the most convenient option.

How to Get Around in North

Visitors can hire a taxi or a private car to explore North. However, public transportation options are also available in the form of buses and shared minivans called angkot. Motorbike taxis are also popular among locals and are suitable for shorter distances.

Where to Go Next from North: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  1. Pangandaran Beach - Located approximately 230 km from North, it is an idyllic beach town known for its sandy beaches, surfing, and snorkeling spots.
  2. Yogyakarta - Located around 400 km from North, Yogyakarta is home to the famous temples of Borobudur and Prambanan, and it is known for its art and cultural attractions.
  3. Jakarta - Located around 150 km from North, Jakarta is the bustling capital city of Indonesia, famous for its shopping district, nightlife, and museums.

Conclusion

North in Parahyangan is a unique and exciting destination that offers visitors a glimpse into the rich cultural heritage and natural beauty of Java. With its picturesque landscapes, historical sites, outdoor activities, and scrumptious food, North is indeed a perfect destination for travelers seeking an adventure.
