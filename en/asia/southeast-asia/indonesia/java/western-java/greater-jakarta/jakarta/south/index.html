Introduction to South

South is a bustling district located in Greater Jakarta, in Western Java, Indonesia. It is famous for its vibrant culture, delicious cuisine, and scenic landscapes. The district is a popular tourist destination among local and foreign visitors alike. In this guide, we will provide you with a comprehensive tour of South, including places to visit, hiking and biking routes, best things to do, and more.

List of Places to Visit in South

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

National Museum of Indonesia

The National Museum of Indonesia is a must-visit for history buffs. The museum houses over 140,000 historical, prehistoric, and ethnographic artifacts from all over Indonesia. The exhibits include ancient weapons, textiles, ceramics, and musical instruments.

Museum Nasional Keprajuritan

The Museum Nasional Keprajuritan is dedicated to the Indonesian military. The museum has a vast collection of military equipment, including tanks, airplanes, and weapons. The exhibits are well-maintained, and the guides are knowledgeable about Indonesian military history.

Jakarta History Museum

The Jakarta History Museum is located in the old town area of Jakarta. It was once the residence of the Dutch Governor-General during the colonial era. Today, it serves as a museum, showcasing the city’s history, culture, and architecture.

Natural Attractions of South: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Ragunan Zoo

Ragunan Zoo is Indonesia’s oldest and largest zoo. It is home to over 3,500 animals, representing more than 270 species. The zoo is located in a lush forested area, providing visitors with an opportunity to experience nature up close.

Cilandak Town Square

Cilandak Town Square is a beautiful park located in South Jakarta. The park features a lake, jogging track, and picnic areas. It is an excellent spot for a relaxing afternoon picnic or a morning jog.

Taman Mini Indonesia Indah

Taman Mini Indonesia Indah is a cultural park that showcases Indonesia’s diverse cultures and traditions. The park features traditional houses, museums, and galleries displaying textiles, handicrafts, and other cultural artifacts.

Hiking/Biking Routes of South

Ciliwung Trail

The Ciliwung Trail is a popular hiking trail that runs along the Ciliwung River from Bogor to Jakarta. The trail offers beautiful views of the river and surrounding landscapes. It is a great option for outdoor enthusiasts looking for a challenging hike.

Sudirman-Thamrin Route

The Sudirman-Thamrin Route is a scenic bike route that runs through Jakarta’s business district. The route offers stunning views of the city’s modern architecture and skyscrapers. It is an excellent option for cyclists looking for an urban adventure.

Best Things to Do in South: Activities and Tours

Explore Jakarta’s Nightlife

South Jakarta is home to some of the best nightclubs and bars in the city. Visitors can enjoy live music, dance performances, and excellent food and drinks.

Shopping at Pasar Santa

Pasar Santa is a hipster market that offers a wide variety of locally made goods, such as clothes, accessories, and food. It is an excellent spot to buy unique souvenirs and support local businesses.

Visit Local Batik Producers

Indonesia is famous for its batik textiles. Visitors can take a tour of local batik producers and learn about the intricate process of creating batik fabrics. They can also purchase authentic batik products to bring home.

Food and Drink Scene of South

Must-Try Dishes

Ayam Goreng Kalasan

Ayam Goreng Kalasan is a traditional Javanese fried chicken dish. It is marinated in coconut water and served with sambal and rice.

Nasi Uduk Betawi

Nasi Uduk Betawi is a popular breakfast dish in Jakarta. It consists of steamed rice cooked with coconut milk and served with fried chicken, fried tofu, and peanut sauce.

Local Beverages

Es Cendol

Es Cendol is a refreshing Indonesian drink made with coconut milk, jelly noodles, and palm sugar.

Teh Botol Sosro

Teh Botol Sosro is a bottled iced tea that is a popular beverage in Indonesia. It is made with black tea and sweetened with cane sugar.

What to Buy in South: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Indonesia is famous for its handicrafts, textiles, and batik fabrics. Visitors can purchase souvenirs such as batik shirts, traditional puppets, and intricately carved wooden masks. They can also buy locally made products such as tea, coffee, and spices.

Local Culture and Customs of South

Indonesia is a diverse country with a rich cultural heritage. Visitors should respect the local customs and traditions and dress modestly when visiting places of worship. It is also appropriate to remove shoes before entering someone’s home.

Best Time to Visit South

The best time to visit South is from May to September when the weather is dry and pleasant. The district can be visited year-round, but visitors should be prepared for heavy rainfall during the monsoon season (October to April).

Do’s and Don’ts of South

Do:

  • Respect the local customs and traditions.
  • Carry a copy of your passport and visa at all times.
  • Bargain when shopping at local markets.

Don’t:

  • Drink tap water.
  • Take photographs of people without their permission.
  • Disrespect public property.

Safety and Health

Safety in South

South Jakarta is a relatively safe district, but visitors should take precautions to avoid pickpocketing and theft. They should also be careful when crossing the street as traffic can be heavy.

Health in South

Visitors should drink bottled water and avoid eating street food that is not cooked thoroughly. They should also get vaccinated for Hepatitis A and B and typhoid fever before visiting Indonesia.

Getting to South: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near South

The Soekarno-Hatta International Airport is the primary airport serving Jakarta. It is located approximately 30 kilometers from South Jakarta.

Bus Stations Near South

The Jakarta International Bus Terminal is located in the nearby city of Depok. Buses operate from the terminal to various cities in Indonesia.

Other Options

Visitors can also take a taxi or use a ride-sharing service such as Grab to get around South Jakarta.

How to Get Around in South

Public transportation in South Jakarta includes buses, taxis, and ride-sharing services. Visitors can also rent a car or a motorcycle to explore the district.

Where to Go Next from South: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

South Jakarta is a gateway to many nearby destinations, including:

Conclusion

South Jakarta is a vibrant and exciting district, offering visitors a diverse range of cultural, historical, and natural attractions. From exploring Jakarta’s nightlife to hiking scenic trails, South Jakarta has something for everyone. Plan your visit today and enjoy all that this amazing district has to offer!
