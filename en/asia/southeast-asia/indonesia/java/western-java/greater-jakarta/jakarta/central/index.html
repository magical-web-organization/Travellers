Introduction to Central

Central is a bustling district located in the heart of Jakarta, the capital city of Indonesia. With its strategic location, it serves as the gateway to many popular tourist destinations in the region, such as Bandung, Bogor, and Puncak. Central is also home to numerous cultural and historical sites, scenic views, and exciting outdoor adventures. Whether you’re a history buff, nature lover, or simply looking for a unique travel experience, Central has something for everyone.

Local Culture and Customs of Central

Jakarta is a melting pot of different cultures, and as such, it’s important to respect the diverse customs and traditions. Here are some of the local customs to keep in mind:

  • Remove your shoes before entering someone’s home.
  • Always use your right hand when shaking hands or passing objects.
  • Modest dressing is recommended when visiting mosques or religious sites.
  • Bargaining is common in markets and street vendors.

Best Time to Visit Central

The best time to visit Central is during the dry season, which runs from May to September. The weather is generally pleasant, with low humidity and minimal rainfall. The high season for tourism is also during these months. However, if you prefer fewer crowds and cheaper travel costs, visiting during the off-season (October to April) is a good option, but be prepared for the wet season’s daily rain showers.

Do’s and Don’ts of Central

Here are some do’s and don’ts to keep in mind while traveling to Central:

Do:

  • Carry small change for local transportation and street vendors.
  • Learn basic Indonesian phrases, including greetings and polite phrases.
  • Respect the local customs and traditions.

Don’t:

  • Drink tap water or eat raw fruits and vegetables.
  • Wear revealing clothing when visiting religious sites.
  • Touch someone’s head, as it is considered disrespectful in Indonesian culture.

Safety and Health

Safety in Central

While Central is generally safe for travelers, it’s always important to take precautions and be aware of your surroundings. Here are some tips to stay safe while exploring Central:

  • Avoid walking alone at night.
  • Keep your belongings secure and be cautious of pickpockets in crowded places.
  • Use a reputable taxi or ride-hailing service instead of hailing a taxi on the street.

Health in Central

Travelers to Central should be up to date on routine vaccinations, particularly those for hepatitis A and B, typhoid, and rabies. Mosquito-borne diseases such as dengue fever are also prevalent, so make sure to protect yourself by using insect repellent and wearing protective clothing.

Getting to Central: Transportation Options and Logistics

Central is easily accessible from Jakarta’s Soekarno-Hatta International Airport, located about 21 miles away. Here are some transportation options to get to Central:

Airports Near Central

  • Soekarno-Hatta International Airport (CGK)
  • Halim Perdanakusuma Airport (HLP)

Bus Stations Near Central

  • Kampung Rambutan Bus Terminal
  • Cililitan bus terminal

Other Options

  • Taxi or ride-hailing service
  • Private car rental with a driver

How to Get Around in Central

The most convenient and efficient way to get around Central is by using a taxi or ride-hailing service such as Grab, Gojek or Blue Bird Taxi. If you prefer public transportation, buses and commuter trains are available but the language barrier can be challenging to navigate.
