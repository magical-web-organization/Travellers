Introduction to Bekasi

Bekasi is a city located in Greater Jakarta, in Western Java, Indonesia. It is one of the most populated cities in the country, with a population of around 3 million people. Bekasi is known for its vibrant culture, historical landmarks, natural attractions, and delicious food.

List of Places to Visit in Bekasi

If you plan to visit Bekasi, here are some of the top places to visit:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Gedung Juang 45 Museum: A museum that displays the struggle for independence of Indonesia through the collection of weapons, photographs, documents and dioramas of Gedung Juang 45.
  • Masjid Al-Azhar Bekasi: One of the biggest mosques in Bekasi, Indonesia
  • Kampung Naga: A unique and traditional Sundanese village located in Nagrak, Bogor, about 30km away from Bekasi.

Natural Attractions of Bekasi: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • The Great Mosque of Al Azhar Bekasi: This mosque is known for its stunning architecture, and beautiful green gardens where visitors can relax while enjoying the view.
  • Kali Bekasi: This is a river located in the eastern part of Bekasi, offering scenic views and opportunities for outdoor activities like kayaking and fishing.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Bekasi

Bekasi offers an excellent hiking and biking experience to those who visit the city. Some of the best hiking/biking routes include:

  • Mount Pancar: This mountain offers a challenging yet rewarding hiking experience, with beautiful scenic views on top.
  • Ciawi-Babakan Madang: This biking route is located in the south of Bekasi and is perfect for those who enjoy mountain biking.

Best Things to Do in Bekasi: Activities and Tours

Bekasi offers plenty of activities and tours that cater to everyone’s preferences.

  • Waterboom Lippo Cikarang: An excellent water park for family fun.
  • Bekasi Traditional Market: A popular tourist spot for those who enjoy shopping and food tasting.

Week-Long Itinerary for Bekasi

Day 1: Visit Gedung Juang 45 Museum, Masjid Al-Azhar Bekasi, and Kampung Naga Day 2: Go on a scenic boat ride on Kali Bekasi Day 3: Take a hike to Mount Pancar Day 4: Biking tour to Ciawi-Babakan Madang Day 5: Visit The Great Mosque of Al Azhar Bekasi and Lippo Cikarang Waterboom Day 6: Explore the Bekasi Traditional Market Day 7: Relax at Jababeka Golf Club

Food and Drink Scene of Bekasi

Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages while in Bekasi:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Nasi Uduk: a traditional Indonesian rice dish cooked in coconut milk and served with fried chicken or beef, fried tempeh, and sambal.
  • Sate Ayam: This is a popular Indonesian dish, which features grilled chicken skewers, often served with rice cakes and peanut sauce.

Local Beverages

  • Es Teler: a refreshing drink with avocado, coconut meat, jackfruit, and condensed milk.
  • Es Doger: A drink made from red beans, coconut milk, palm sugar, and jelly.

Where to Stay in Bekasi: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Aston Imperial Bekasi Hotel & Conference Center: Luxury hotel with excellent facilities, located in the heart of Bekasi.
  • Horison Ultima Bekasi: A mid-range hotel located on the outskirts of Bekasi.
  • Whiz Prime Hotel Bekasi: Mid-range hotel located near Bekasi Train Station.

What to Buy in Bekasi: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Keripik Bawang: A popular snack made from fried garlic, often sold in markets or street stalls.
  • Kain Batik: Traditional Indonesian cloth that is often used for clothes or home decorations.

Local Culture and Customs of Bekasi

People in Bekasi are known for being friendly, welcoming, and polite. Indonesia is a predominantly Muslim country, and visitors should dress modestly when visiting religious places.

Best Time to Visit Bekasi

The best time to visit Bekasi is during the dry season, between May to October. The weather is cooler and drier, making it perfect for exploring the city’s outdoor attractions and adventures.

Do’s and Don’ts of Bekasi

Do:

  • Dress modestly when visiting religious places.
  • Respect the local culture and customs.

Don’t:

  • Do not drink tap water. Only drink bottled water or purified water.
  • Do not talk loudly in public places.

Safety and Health

Safety in Bekasi

Bekasi is generally a safe city, but it is always best to take precautions. Avoid going out late at night and be aware of your surroundings when walking in busy areas.

Health in Bekasi

Visitors should make sure they are up-to-date with their vaccinations before traveling to Indonesia. It is also essential to bring mosquito repellent to protect against diseases such as dengue fever and malaria.

Getting to Bekasi: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Bekasi

The nearest airport to Bekasi is Soekarno-Hatta International Airport in Jakarta.

Bus Stations Near Bekasi

Bekasi has several bus terminals, including the Bekasi Bus Terminal and the Kalimalang Bus Terminal.

Other Options

Visitors can also take a train to Bekasi Train Station, located in the center of the city.

How to Get Around in Bekasi

Bekasi offers several transportation options, including taxis, buses, and train. Visitors should make sure to negotiate the price of a taxi ride before getting in.

Where to Go Next from Bekasi: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Jakarta: The capital city of Indonesia is only about 30km from Bekasi, and offers countless attractions and iconic landmarks to visit.
  • Bandung: A city of art and culture located about 2 hours from Bekasi.

Conclusion

Bekasi is a vibrant city located in Western Java, Indonesia. It offers plenty of attractions and activities for everyone, from natural wonders to cultural landmarks, and delicious food. Ensure your trip to Bekasi is a memorable one by using this comprehensive travel guide to plan your itinerary.
