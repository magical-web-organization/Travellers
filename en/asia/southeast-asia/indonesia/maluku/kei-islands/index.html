Introduction to Kei Islands

Kei Islands, a cluster of small islands in Maluku, Indonesia, is a hidden gem that boasts crystal clear waters, white sandy beaches, and a rich cultural heritage. With over 20 islands in the archipelago, Kei Islands are a perfect destination for beach lovers, adventure seekers, and travelers interested in learning about local cultures.

List of Places to Visit in Kei Islands

If you’re planning a trip to Kei Islands, here are some must-visit places to add to your itinerary.

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Benteng Victoria Fort: Built by the Dutch in the 17th century, this fort is a symbol of the Dutch colonization of the region.
  • Museum Siwalima: The museum showcases the unique culture and traditions of the Kei people, including art, handicrafts, and traditional clothing.
  • Taman Wisata Alam Naulu: This natural park is home to unique bird species, including the Kei parrot and the white cockatoo. In addition to bird watching, the park offers nature walks and camping opportunities.

Natural Attractions of Kei Islands: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Ngurbloat Beach: Also known as Pasir Panjang, this 5-kilometer-long beach is a paradise for beachgoers and is perfect for swimming, sunbathing, and beach volleyball.
  • Hajar Karst: This unique geological formation features impressive limestone cliffs, caves, and sinkholes, and is a popular destination for rock climbing.
  • Pulau Kei Kecil: This island is the perfect spot for snorkeling and diving, as it is home to a diverse range of marine life, including colorful corals and schools of fish.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Kei Islands

If you’re looking for a more active way to explore Kei Islands, consider hiking or biking on one of the island’s many trails.

  • Bukit Batu: This 45-meter-high hill offers stunning panoramic views of Kei Kecil island and is the perfect spot for sunset watching.
  • Desa Ohoililir: This small village is located on the southern part of Kei Besar and has a range of hiking trails that will take you through lush forests and colorful traditional villages.

Best Things to Do in Kei Islands: Activities and Tours

  • Snorkeling and Diving: With some of the richest marine life in Indonesia, Kei Islands are the perfect destination for snorkeling and diving.
  • Island Hopping: Explore the different islands and beaches of Kei Islands on an organized tour.
  • Local Culture Tours: Learn about the unique culture of the Kei people by visiting sacred sites, traditional villages, and attending cultural events.

For a comprehensive Kei Islands experience, here is a suggested week-long itinerary.

Food and Drink Scene of Kei Islands

Kei Islands offer a variety of local cuisine that is a mix of Indonesian and Kei cultures.

Must-Try Dishes

  • Sagu Keju: A dish made from sago flour and grated cheese.
  • Ikan Woku: Spicy fish soup cooked with various herbs and spices.
  • Rujak: A salad made of fruits and vegetables served with spicy peanut sauce.

Local Beverages

  • Saguer: A traditional alcoholic drink made from fermented sago palms.
  • Es Kelapa Kopyor: A refreshing drink made from coconut water mixed with kopyor (young coconut flesh).

Where to Stay in Kei Islands: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Kei Islands offer a range of accommodation options to suit different budgets and preferences.

  • Luxury Hotels: The most upscale options include Maluku Resort & Spa and Kei Beach Resort.
  • Mid-Range Hotels: Hotel Santai, Ohoijang Beach Hotel, and Swallow Guest House are affordable options that still offer modern amenities.
  • Budget Hostels: Kei Kecil Hostel and Pulse Hostel offer dormitory-style accommodations for budget travelers.

What to Buy in Kei Islands: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Here are some unique souvenirs and local products to bring back home with you after your trip to Kei Islands.

  • Sago Flour: Made from sago palms, this flour is used in many local dishes.
  • Handicrafts: The Kei people are known for their traditional basket weaving and wood carving.
  • Pearls: Kei Islands are also known for their pearl farms, producing high-quality pearls that make a great souvenir.

Local Culture and Customs of Kei Islands

Kei people have unique traditions and customs that visitors should be aware of and respect.

  • Clothing: Modest clothing is appreciated, especially when visiting villages or sacred sites.
  • Greetings: A smile and a nod is the typical greeting; a handshake is not always expected.
  • Religion: The majority of Kei people are Christian, and visitors should be respectful of their religious traditions and practices.

Best Time to Visit Kei Islands

The best time to visit Kei Islands is between July and September when the weather is dry, and the sea is calm. Avoid visiting during the rainy season from December to February, as some roads and beaches may be inaccessible.

Do’s and Don’ts of Kei Islands

Do:

  • Respect local customs and traditions.
  • Carry enough cash, as ATMs are limited on the islands.
  • Have mosquito repellent with you.

Don’t:

  • Use plastic bags as they are banned in Kei Islands.
  • Swim in areas with strong currents.
  • Leave garbage on the beaches or in public areas.

Safety and Health

While Kei Islands are generally safe for tourists, it’s important to take some precautions to ensure a comfortable and enjoyable trip.

Safety in Kei Islands

  • Be mindful of your surroundings, especially at night.
  • Avoid walking alone at night in poorly lit areas.
  • Secure valuables and keep them out of sight.

Health in Kei Islands

  • Carry your prescription medications with you.
  • Use sunscreen and stay hydrated to avoid heatstroke and dehydration.
  • Check with your doctor for any recommended vaccines or precautions before traveling to Kei Islands.

Getting to Kei Islands: Transportation Options and Logistics

Kei Islands can only be accessed via air or sea.

Airports Near Kei Islands

  • Pattimura Airport in Ambon is the nearest airport, where you can catch a domestic flight to Langgur Airport in Kei Islands.

Sea Transport

  • Pelni ships connect the Kei Islands with other islands in Indonesia.
  • Passenger boats are also available for travel between the different islands of Kei Islands.

How to Get Around in Kei Islands

The best way to get around Kei Islands is by hiring a motorcycle or car. Taxis and public transportation are also available, but may be less reliable and more expensive.

Where to Go Next from Kei Islands: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

After exploring Kei Islands, consider visiting nearby destinations and excursions, such as

  • Ambon: The capital of Maluku province and has a rich colonial history and natural attractions.
  • Banda Islands: A small archipelago that is known for its nutmeg plantations and Dutch colonial landmarks.

Conclusion

Kei Islands offer a perfect mix of stunning natural scenery, outdoor activities, and cultural experiences, making it a great destination for travelers seeking adventure and relaxation in an off-the-beaten-path location. With this comprehensive guide, you’re all set to plan the perfect getaway to Kei Islands.
