Introduction to Morotai Island

Morotai Island, located in the northern part of Maluku province in Indonesia, is a hidden gem waiting to be explored. Known as the Island of Warriors, it was a strategic location during World War II and has an interesting history to discover. Nowadays, visitors come to Morotai Island to enjoy its pristine beaches, clear blue waters, rich culture, and diverse natural beauty.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Morotai Island

There are several trails in Morotai Island for hiking and biking enthusiasts. From easy to challenging trails, you can explore the natural beauty of the island while enjoying some physical activity. Some of the most popular trails include:

  • Dodola to Kulaba track
  • Kolorai Waterfall trek
  • Lalibela Trek
  • Runu Binoluho to Linaon Hill trek

Local Culture and Customs of Morotai Island

Morotai Island has a rich culture and history that visitors should respect and appreciate. The local people are friendly and welcoming, and visitors are often greeted with a smile. Some customs and traditions to be aware of include:

  • Covering shoulders and legs when visiting religious sites or attending traditional events
  • Removing shoes before entering homes or places of worship
  • Expressing gratitude and respect for elders and local customs
  • Avoiding public displays of affection

Best Time to Visit Morotai Island

The best time to visit Morotai Island is from May to September when the weather is dry and sunny. However, the island can be visited year-round as the temperatures remain warm throughout the year.

Do’s and Don’ts of Morotai Island

Do:

  • Respect local customs and traditions
  • Use environmentally friendly practices, such as reducing plastic waste
  • Wear sunscreen and bring insect repellent
  • Try local food and drink

Don’t:

  • Take coral reefs or other underwater flora and fauna
  • Litter or damage the natural environment
  • Disrespect local beliefs or customs

Safety and Health

Safety in Morotai Island

Morotai Island is generally a safe place for visitors. However, visitors should take standard precautions such as safeguarding their valuables and being mindful of their surroundings. Swimming in the open sea can also be risky and caution should be taken.

Health in Morotai Island

Visitors are recommended to have their routine vaccinations up to date such as measles-mumps-rubella (MMR) vaccine, diphtheria-tetanus-pertussis vaccine, varicella (chickenpox) vaccine, and polio vaccine. They are also advised to bring antimalarial drugs due to mosquitoes possibly spreading the disease.
