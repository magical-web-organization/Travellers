Introduction to Saparua

Saparua is a small and secluded island located in the Maluku archipelago, which is a part of Indonesia’s eastern provinces. The island is surrounded by crystal clear blue waters and a lush forest canopy that creates a serene, untouched landscape. Despite being off-the-beaten-path, the island is well-worth exploring for its natural beauty, rich history, and unique culture that has been influenced by visitors over the centuries.

List of Places to Visit in Saparua

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

One of the best ways to learn about Saparua’s history is to visit its cultural attractions, which hold storied narratives from the past. The island has several museums, such as the Museum Daerah Kabupaten Maluku Tengah, which features artifacts and exhibitions of traditional materials used by the villagers of Saparua over the centuries. Another museum not to be missed is the Saparua Marine and Wildlife Reserve, which is home to a range of marine flora and fauna, including sea turtles, reef fish, and coral gardens. In addition, the old Dutch fort, Fort Duurstede, built-in 1656, offers a captivating insight into the island’s colonial history.

Natural Attractions of Saparua: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

One of Saparua’s attractions is its lush equatorial rainforest, which offers several hiking and biking trails, perfect for nature enthusiasts. The island is also home to several idyllic beaches such as Ora and Nusa Laut, which are popular for sunbathing and swimming. A popular tourist destination is the Banda Sea, known for its rich marine life, the tropical sceneries, and the opportunity to go on underwater diving expeditions.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Saparua

The forests of Saparua offer several hiking and biking routes for explorers to immerse themselves in the natural beauty of the island. For a four-hour adventure, take the trek from Benteng Duurstede Village to the pine forest. The reward is a stunning panoramic view of the Banda sea. Another option is strolling through the village of Haria, where you can witness traditional house designs and explore the local marketplace.

Best Things to Do in Saparua: Activities and Tours

There’s no shortage of activities and tours to choose from on Saparua. For those looking to experience the local culture, attend a war dance, Cakalele, performed by villagers, and learn about its significance in their history. Island hopping and snorkeling are also popular activities among visitors. Moreover, guided tours that take you to the most thrilling sites such as, Imas-Waha, white sand beaches, and the teeming marine reserve.

Food and Drink Scene of Saparua

Must-Try Dishes

Saparua offers a unique culinary experience with dishes that are not found anywhere else in the world. One of the must-try dishes is the local seafood, served fresh, and always prepared with a local twist. On the sweeter side, try the local delicacy of sweet potato chips, a local favorite snack.

Local Beverages

The Maluku Islands are known for their spices, and Saparua produces locally sourced spices that are used to make spice-infused cocktails. Also, the island’s local beverage is Sari duren, a juice made from locally grown durian fruits which makes for a refreshing drink after a long day of exploring.

Where to Stay in Saparua: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Finding accommodation in Saparua is not an issue as the island has an array of choices ranging from luxury villas to basic guesthouses, at any budget range. The Ora Beach Resort and Manusela Resort are high-end options that offer picturesque ocean views. Alternatively, the Moluccas Cottages and Aksara Hotel are more affordable options for budget hunters.

What to Buy in Saparua: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Saparua is famed for its locally produced products such as spices, handmade textiles, wood carvings, and pottery. These make for excellent souvenirs to take home and remember the island by.

Local Culture and Customs of Saparua

The culture of Saparua is a mix of traditional beliefs, colonial influences, and modern-day customs. The island is predominantly Muslim, but there are also Christian minorities. When visiting the island, it’s important to be respectful of the locals and their traditions.

Best Time to Visit Saparua

The best time to visit Saparua is from April to September when the weather is dry and perfect for outdoor activities. Although there may be occasional rainfall, the island remains warm and humid all year round.

Do’s and Don’ts of Saparua

Do:

  • Respect the island’s culture and traditions.
  • Take part in local activities to immerse yourself in the local culture.
  • Keep the environment clean and free from garbage.

Don’t:

  • Wear revealing clothing in public as it may offend locals.
  • Take rocks or artifacts from the island to preserve its natural beauty and cultural heritage.
  • Be disrespectful to the locals or their customs.

Safety and Health

Safety in Saparua

Saparua is generally safe, but visitors should practice caution when exploring the island’s natural areas and forests. Always take a local guide or seek assistance from tour operators. Avoid visiting the island during the rainy season when the currents become stronger.

Health in Saparua

Visitors should bring mosquito repellent as the island is home to these pesky insects all year round. Consider getting vaccinated for Hepatitis A and Typhoid as well.

Getting to Saparua: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Saparua

The nearest airport to Saparua is Pattimura International Airport in Ambon. From here, visitors have to take a taxi to Tulehu jetty. The journey from the airport to the jetty takes 45 minutes. Once at the jetty, hop on a boat to Saparua.

Bus Stations Near Saparua

A more affordable and longer travel option would be to take a bus from Jakarta to Ambon. The journey takes around 32 hours. From Ambon, take a taxi to the jetty and then a boat ride to Saparua.

Other Options

For a more luxurious experience, charter a private yacht to Saparua from Ambon.

How to Get Around in Saparua

To get around the island, take either a motorbike or a rented scooter from the locals. Visitors can also explore the island on foot and enjoy the scenic beauty of the forests and the coastline.

Where to Go Next from Saparua: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Exploring the Maluku archipelago’s other hidden gems is worth a visit, such as the nearby islands of Haruku and Nusa Laut, which have beautiful beaches and marine reserves. Or continue to Seram Island, one of the Maluku archipelagos’ largest islands, with its mountainous regions and diverse flora and fauna.

Conclusion

Saparua offers visitors a chance to explore Indonesia’s remote and unique hidden gems with its cultural significance, natural beauty, and adventures waiting for those looking to explore every corner of this island. Come to Saparua to get lost in the island’s wonder and create memories that will last a lifetime.
