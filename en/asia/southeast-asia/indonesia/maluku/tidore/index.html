Introduction to Tidore

Tidore is a small island located in the province of Maluku, Indonesia. It is known for its panoramic views of the Tidore and Ternate islands, as well as its rich historical culture. The island’s main attraction is Mount Kie Matubu, an active volcano that offers stunning views of the surrounding landscape. Tidore is also known for its traditional spice trade, which has been ongoing for centuries.

List of Places to Visit in Tidore

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Kedaton Tidore - A historic palace and cultural hub of Tidore. You can learn about the history of Tidore and the spice trade at this site.
  • Torre Fortress - A Portuguese-built fortress that once served as the primary defense for Tidore against invaders.
  • Benteng Tolukko - A historic fort on Tidore operated by the sultanate in the past, and now functions as a museum.
  • Kastela Fort - A fortress built in 1529 that played an important role in the Portuguese-Spanish war.

Natural Attractions of Tidore: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Mount Kie Matubu - The main attraction of Tidore, an active volcano that offers unrivaled scenic views of the surrounding islands.
  • Tara-Tara Hill - A hill located near Kedaton Tidore with panoramic views of Tidore.
  • Sopi-Sopi Waterfall - A stunning waterfall located in the lush forests of Tidore.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Tidore

Tidore offers several hiking and biking routes.

  • Mount Kie Matubu Hike - A difficult trail that takes about 5 hours to reach the summit. The trail provides breathtaking views of Tidore Island.
  • Tara-Tara Hill Hike - A moderate hiking trail that takes approximately 30 minutes to reach the top of Tara-Tara Hill.

Best Things to Do in Tidore: Activities and Tours

  • Explore the Spice Markets - Tidore is known for its traditional spice markets that sell various spices such as cloves, cinnamon, and nutmeg.
  • Snorkeling and Diving - Tidore offers excellent snorkeling and diving spots with stunning underwater views.
  • Boat Tour - Take a boat tour around the island to explore its scenic views.

Week-Long Itinerary for Tidore

Day 1 - Arrival and Spice Market Tour Day 2 - Mount Kie Matubu Hike Day 3 - Tara-Tara Hill Hike and Kedaton Tidore Museum Visit Day 4 - Boat Tour around Tidore Island Day 5 - Snorkeling and Diving Day 6 - Visit to Torre Fortress and Benteng Tolukko Day 7 - Departure

Food and Drink Scene of Tidore

Must-Try Dishes

  • Papeda - A traditional staple food which consists of sago flour and fish soup.
  • Grilled Fish - Freshly caught fish from Tidore Island grilled to perfection with a blend of spices.

Local Beverages

  • Kopi Kapal - A traditional coffee made from Robusta beans and cooked in a pot over a fire stove.
  • Saguer - A fermented drink made from the sap of the sago palm.

Where to Stay in Tidore: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Grand Mosque Hotel - A luxurious hotel equipped with modern amenities located in the heart of Tidore.
  • Puncak Homestay - A budget-friendly homestay located on the foothills of Mount Kie Matubu.
  • Bali Hai Beach Resort - A beachfront resort located on Obi Island, just a short boat ride from Tidore.

What to Buy in Tidore: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Tidore is known for its traditional spice trade, making its spices the perfect souvenir to bring home. You’ll also find handicrafts made by the Tidore people such as traditional woven fabrics, sculptures, and wooden carvings.

Local Culture and Customs of Tidore

The culture of Tidore is heavily influenced by its rich history and spice trade. The Tidore people are known for their hospitality and friendliness, making it easy for visitors to feel welcome.

Best Time to Visit Tidore

The best time to visit Tidore is from May to October, during the dry season when the weather is warm and the skies are clear.

Do’s and Don’ts of Tidore

Do:

  • Learn a few words in the local language, Bahasa Indonesia.
  • Respect the local customs and traditions.
  • Try the local cuisine and explore the traditional spice markets.

Don’t:

  • Wear revealing clothing when visiting cultural sites.
  • Smoke in public places.
  • Disrespect the local culture and traditions.

Safety and Health

Safety in Tidore

Tidore is generally safe for tourists, but it’s advisable to take the usual precautions when traveling, such as securing your valuables and avoiding unlit areas at night.

Health in Tidore

It’s recommended to get vaccinated against common diseases such as typhoid and hepatitis A before visiting Tidore. Make sure to drink bottled water and avoid consuming raw foods when traveling in the region.

Getting to Tidore: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Tidore

  • Sultan Babullah Airport - The nearest airport to Tidore, located on Ternate Island, around 25 km away from Tidore.

Bus Stations Near Tidore

There are no bus stations on Tidore Island. Instead, visitors can take a ferry or rented boat from Ternate Island.

Other Options

Visitors can also take a boat or ferry from other nearby islands such as Sulawesi, Java, and Bali.

How to Get Around in Tidore

The best way to get around Tidore is by rented motorbike or car.

Where to Go Next from Tidore: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Ternate Island - Another island located near Tidore known for its historic sites and scenic views.
  • Morotai Island - A scenic island located in the northern part of Maluku province known for its beaches and World War II history.

Conclusion

Tidore is a paradise waiting to be explored. With its rich history and culture, stunning views, and friendly locals, it’s no wonder why visitors to Indonesia add Tidore to their travel itinerary. Make sure to refer to our comprehensive guide to make the most out of your time in Tidore.
