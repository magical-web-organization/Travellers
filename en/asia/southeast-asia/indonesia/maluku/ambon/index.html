Introduction to Ambon

Located in Maluku, Indonesia, Ambon is known for its beautiful beaches and rich cultural history. Despite its reputation as a quiet and remote destination, Ambon offers a wealth of experiences for visitors. The island is home to several historic sites, natural wonders, and vibrant culinary traditions. Whether you’re interested in outdoor adventures or cultural exploration, Ambon has something for you.

List of Places to Visit in Ambon

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Museum Siwalima - This museum is dedicated to the history and culture of Maluku. The exhibits showcase the region’s diverse ethnic groups and their traditional costumes, weapons, and musical instruments.
  • Fort Amsterdam - Built by the Dutch in 1637, this fort was an important trading post in colonial times. Today, the fort is a popular tourist attraction with sweeping views of the island.
  • Pintu Kota Beach - This beach is home to a beautiful monument carved to commemorate the arrival of the first Europeans in Ambon. The sculpture depicts a Portuguese ship and a Dutch ship sailing towards each other.

Natural Attractions of Ambon: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Karang Hatta Island - A secluded paradise island with crystal-clear waters and white sand beaches.
  • Mount Salahutu - A hike to the top of this dormant volcano rewards visitors with breathtaking views of the island.
  • Natsepa Beach - A popular beach with locals, this beach offers clear water and white sand. Visitors can also enjoy a range of water sports, including snorkeling, diving, and jet skiing.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Ambon

  • Salahutu Trail - A challenging six-hour hike that leads to the summit of Mount Salahutu.
  • Pohon Waai Trail - A two-hour hike through a forest of towering trees and lush vegetation. Visitors can also explore prehistoric cave art along the trail.

Best Things to Do in Ambon: Activities and Tours

  • Snorkeling and Diving - Ambon is home to some of the world’s best dive sites, including the famous Ambon Bay. Visitors can explore a diversity of marine life, including colorful corals, reef sharks, and rays.
  • Cooking Class - Visitors can learn how to cook traditional Maluku dishes with a local chef.
  • Island Hopping - Ambon is surrounded by smaller islands that offer pristine beaches and untouched natural beauty.

Food and Drink Scene of Ambon

Must-Try Dishes

  • Papeda - A traditional Maluku dish made from sago flour and served with a range of toppings, including fish and vegetables.
  • Kohu-kohu - A salad made from raw vegetables and peanuts.
  • Sambal Roa - A spicy sauce made from dried fish and chili peppers.

Local Beverages

  • Sagu Woka - A refreshing drink made from sago pearls.
  • Es Kelapa Muda - A chilled coconut water drink.

Where to Stay in Ambon: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Swiss-Belhotel Ambon - A luxurious hotel with a rooftop pool and ocean views.
  • Pondok Suada - A budget-friendly option with clean rooms and friendly staff.
  • Amans Hotel - A boutique hotel near the city center.

What to Buy in Ambon: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Ambon T-shirts - A souvenir that highlights Ambon’s unique history and culture.
  • Spices and Herbs - Maluku is famous for its spices, including nutmeg, mace, and cloves.
  • Handicrafts - Local artisans produce intricate wood carvings, batik fabric designs, and woven baskets.

Local Culture and Customs of Ambon

Ambon has a rich cultural heritage, with influences from Dutch, Portuguese, and Malay cultures. Visitors should take care to respect local customs and dress modestly when visiting religious sites. Tipping is not expected but appreciated.

Best Time to Visit Ambon

Ambon can be visited year-round, but the dry season (May to October) is the most comfortable time to visit, with less rain and lower humidity.

Do’s and Don’ts of Ambon

Do:

  • Respect local customs and traditions.
  • Dress modestly when visiting religious sites.
  • Try the local cuisine and learn about Maluku’s food traditions.

Don’t:

  • Engage in public displays of affection.
  • Take photographs of people without their permission.
  • Ignore local customs and traditions.

Safety and Health

Safety in Ambon

Ambon is generally a safe destination for tourists, although visitors should take precautions against petty theft and scams.

Health in Ambon

Visitors to Ambon should take care to drink only bottled or boiled water and take precautions against mosquito-borne illnesses like dengue fever.

Getting to Ambon: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Ambon

  • Pattimura Airport - The only airport in Ambon, with domestic flights from Jakarta, Makassar, and Surabaya.

Bus Stations Near Ambon

  • There are no major bus stations in Ambon.

Other Options

  • Ferries - Visitors can take a ferry from nearby islands like Seram and Saparua.
  • Private Boats - Private boats can be hired to explore the surrounding islands.

How to Get Around in Ambon

Public transportation is limited in Ambon, but visitors can hire taxis or ojeks (motorcycle taxis) to get around the island. Some hotels also offer shuttle services.

Where to Go Next from Ambon: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Banda Islands - A group of ten small islands famous for their nutmeg plantations and vibrant coral reefs.
  • Saparua Island - A small island near Ambon known for its white sand beaches and traditional villages.
  • Ternate Island - A historic island known for its sultanate and ancient forts.

Conclusion

In conclusion, Ambon is a destination that offers a unique blend of natural beauty and cultural heritage. With its pristine beaches, historic sites, and vibrant cuisine, Ambon is a must-see destination for travelers seeking an off-the-beaten-path destination in Indonesia.
