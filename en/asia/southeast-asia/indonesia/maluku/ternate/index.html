Introduction to Ternate

Ternate is a small island located in the Maluku province of Indonesia, also known as the Moluccas Islands. This historical island is a must-visit for anyone interested in exploring the history, culture, and natural beauty of Indonesia.

With its rugged terrain and stunning scenery, Ternate is the perfect destination for outdoor enthusiasts, while history buffs can explore the island’s rich cultural heritage with its museums, forts, and ancient mosques. The island is also a great place to taste some delicious local cuisine and experience the unique hospitality of the Ternate people.

In this comprehensive tour guide for Ternate, we’ll cover everything you need to know to plan your perfect trip – from where to go and what to see, to where to stay and how to get around.

List of Places to Visit in Ternate

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Fort Oranje

Fort Oranje, located on the west coast of Ternate, is a historic Dutch fort built during the 16th century. This fort served as the center of the Dutch East India Company trade in the Moluccas, and played an important role in shaping the history of Indonesia. Today, it’s a popular spot for history buffs, with its stunning architecture and spectacular views of the surrounding sea.

Kedaton Sultanate Palace

Kedaton Sultanate Palace is the former palace of the Sultan of Ternate, built in the 17th century. The palace is a striking example of Ternatean architecture and design, featuring intricate carvings and beautiful murals. Visitors can learn about the rich cultural heritage of Ternate and its royal history, while admiring the beautiful artwork and design.

Tolukko Fort

Tolukko Fort is another historical fort located on the south coast of Ternate. Built in the 18th century, the fort was used as a military outpost by the Portuguese, Dutch, and Sultanate of Ternate. The site now houses a museum, where visitors can learn about the military history of the fort and its role in shaping the island’s past.

Natural Attractions of Ternate: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Mount Gamalama

Mount Gamalama is an active volcano located on the northern side of Ternate. Visitors can hike up to the top for a breathtaking view of the surrounding landscape. The trail is challenging, but the view from the top is well worth the effort.

Sulamadaha Beach

Sulamadaha Beach is a stunning white-sand beach located on the western coast of Ternate. Visitors can enjoy swimming, sunbathing, and snorkeling in the crystal-clear waters. The beach is also a great place to try some of the local cuisine, with many small restaurants serving fresh seafood and traditional dishes.

Ternate Bay

Ternate Bay is a beautiful natural harbor located on the eastern coast of Ternate. Visitors can take a boat tour to explore the bay and its surrounding islands, including the popular island of Tidore.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Ternate

Ternate is a great place for outdoor enthusiasts, with many opportunities for hiking and biking. Some of the most popular trails include:

  • Mount Gamalama – a challenging hike to the top of the volcano.
  • Fort Tolukko Trail – a scenic trail that leads to the Tolukko Fort and its museum.
  • Ternate City Trail – a biking trail that takes visitors through the heart of Ternate’s old town.

Best Things to Do in Ternate: Activities and Tours

  • Take a boat tour of Ternate Bay and surrounding islands.
  • Visit the historic forts of Oranje and Tolukko for a glimpse into the island’s past.
  • Hike up Mount Gamalama for a stunning view of the island.
  • Explore the traditional market of Ternate and try some of the local cuisine.
  • Visit the Kedaton Sultanate Palace to learn about the island’s royal history.

Week-Long Itinerary for Ternate

Day 1: Arrive in Ternate and explore the city center, including the traditional market and Kedaton Sultanate Palace.

Day 2: Take a boat tour of Ternate Bay and surrounding islands.

Day 3: Visit the historic Fort Oranje and learn about the island’s role in the Dutch East India Company trade.

Day 4: Hike up Mount Gamalama for a stunning view of the island.

Day 5: Explore the Tolukko Fort and its museum, learning about the island’s military history.

Day 6: Relax on the beautiful Sulamadaha Beach and try some of the local cuisine.

Day 7: Depart Ternate, taking some local souvenirs and memories with you.

Food and Drink Scene of Ternate

Must-Try Dishes

  • Ikan Tude – a traditional Ternate dish made with dried fish cooked in a mixture of spices and coconut milk.
  • Sambal Roa – a spicy fish dish made with smoked mackerel and chili sauce.
  • Nasi Kuning – yellow rice served with various side dishes, including chicken, beef, and vegetables.

Local Beverages

  • Sagu Dedere – a sweet drink made with sago pearls, coconut milk, and brown sugar.
  • Teh Sereh – a refreshing lemongrass tea popular in Indonesia.
  • Bika Ambon – a traditional Indonesian cake made with eggs, sugar, and tapioca flour.

Where to Stay in Ternate: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Grand Dafam Bela Ternate – a luxurious hotel located in the heart of Ternate’s old town.
  • Kurnia Guesthouse – a budget-friendly option with a great location near the city center.
  • Sari Beach Resort – a beautiful beachfront resort with stunning views of Sulamadaha Beach.

What to Buy in Ternate: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Cloves – Ternate is known as the “Cloves Island,” and visitors can purchase high-quality cloves and other spices at the local markets.
  • Banda Nutmeg – Ternate is also famous for its nutmeg, which is grown on nearby Banda Island and can be purchased locally.
  • Ternate Batik – the island’s traditional textile art, featuring intricate designs and patterns.

Local Culture and Customs of Ternate

  • When visiting mosques, visitors should dress modestly and remove their shoes before entering.
  • Tipping is not expected in Ternate, but is appreciated for good service.
  • Visitors should always ask permission before taking photos of people or culturally sensitive areas.

Best Time to Visit Ternate

The best time to visit Ternate is during the dry season, from April to October. The weather is warm and sunny during this time, making it perfect for outdoor activities and sightseeing.

Do’s and Don’ts of Ternate

Do:

  • Learn a few words of Indonesian to show respect for the local culture.
  • Try the local cuisine and interact with the friendly locals.
  • Explore the island’s rich cultural heritage and natural beauty.

Don’t:

  • Wear revealing clothing when visiting religious sites.
  • Disrespect the local customs and traditions.
  • Be careless when hiking or exploring the island’s rugged terrain.

Safety and Health

Safety in Ternate

Ternate is generally a safe destination for tourists, but visitors should exercise common sense and take precautions to avoid pickpocketing and other petty crimes. Solo travelers should avoid walking alone at night, and should stick to well-lit and busy areas.

Health in Ternate

Visitors to Ternate should ensure they are up-to-date on routine vaccinations, and should take precautions to avoid mosquito-borne illnesses such as dengue fever and malaria. Tap water is not potable in Ternate, so visitors should drink bottled water or use water purification tablets.

Getting to Ternate: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Ternate

  • Sultan Babullah Airport - This is the main airport serving Ternate, and is located approximately 10 km from the city center. Airlines serving the airport include Garuda Indonesia and Lion Air.

Bus Stations Near Ternate

  • Pasar Mardika Bus Terminal - This is the main bus terminal in Ternate, and serves buses to many destinations on the island.

Other Options

  • Travel by Sea - Ferries connect Ternate to other islands in Maluku and other parts of Indonesia.
  • Private Car Rental – Visitors can rent a car for exploring the island’s rugged terrain.

How to Get Around in Ternate

Visitors can get around Ternate by:

  • Ojek – motorcycle taxis that are popular in Indonesia.
  • Becak – bicycle rickshaws that are a popular form of transportation in Ternate.
  • Car Rental – Visitors can rent a car for exploring the island’s rugged terrain.

Where to Go Next from Ternate: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Tidore Island – A small island located just a few kilometers from Ternate, known for its traditional architecture and beautiful beaches.
  • Banda Islands – A group of 10 small islands located approximately 160 km southeast of Ternate, famous for its nutmeg plantations and historical significance.
  • Makassar – A bustling port city located in Sulawesi, with a vibrant nightlife and cultural scene.

Conclusion

Ternate is a hidden gem that offers a unique mix of history, culture, and outdoor adventure. From the island’s stunning beaches and rugged terrain, to its rich cultural heritage and delicious cuisine, Ternate has something for every traveler. With this comprehensive tour guide, visitors can plan their perfect trip to Ternate, exploring all the island has to offer while experiencing the warm hospitality of its people.
