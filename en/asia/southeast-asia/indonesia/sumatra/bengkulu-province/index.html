Introduction to Bengkulu (province)

Located in the west coast of Sumatra island, Bengkulu is a province in Indonesia that boasts of its natural attractions and rich cultural heritage. The province’s historical significance dates back to the 18th century when it was a hub for spice trading and a Dutch colony.

Today, Bengkulu offers travelers adventures in natural wonders, culturally significant sites, and a food and drink scene that will tantalize your taste buds.

List of Places to Visit in Bengkulu (province)

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Fort Marlborough: This historical fort was built by the British East India Company in the 18th century and is now a museum that showcases Bengkulu’s past.
  • Thomas Parr Monument: This monument is a symbol of friendship between the English and the people of Bengkulu.
  • Soekarno’s House: This traditional house was once the home of the first president of Indonesia, Soekarno.
  • Bung Karno’s Exile House: Another house where Soekarno lived during his exile in Bengkulu.
  • Museum Rumah Gajah: A museum dedicated to the history and culture of Bengkulu.

Natural Attractions of Bengkulu (province): Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Danau Dendam Tak Sudah: A tranquil lake offering scenic views and a peaceful atmosphere.
  • Tanjung Setia Beach: A hidden paradise with white sand, clear water, and perfect waves for surfers.
  • Curup Tenang Waterfall: A stunning waterfall located in a dense forest, perfect for hiking and nature lovers.
  • Air Manis Beach: A picturesque beach with rock formations and a unique history related to the legend of Malin Kundang.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Bengkulu (province)

  • Taba Penanjung Nature Reserve: A protected area with lush forests, hiking trails, and a variety of flora and fauna.
  • Bungo Tebo National Park: An ideal destination for trekking and hiking enthusiasts with stunning landscapes, hot springs, and caves to explore.
  • Lembah Anai Nature Reserve: A beautiful park filled with hiking trails, waterfalls, and an abundance of flora and fauna.

Best Things to Do in Bengkulu (province): Activities and Tours

  • Visit the traditional markets of Bengkulu City and experience the local way of life.
  • Take a guided tour to learn about Bengkulu’s history and heritage.
  • Go on a nature walk to explore the rich flora and fauna of Bengkulu province.

Week-Long Itinerary for Bengkulu (province)

Day 1: Arrive in Bengkulu City and explore the local markets. Day 2: Visit Fort Marlborough, Thomas Parr Monument, and Soekarno’s House. Day 3: Take a day trip to Taba Penanjung Nature Reserve. Day 4: Visit Bung Karno’s Exile House and Museum Rumah Gajah. Day 5: Go on a nature walk at Lembah Anai Nature Reserve. Day 6: Visit Curup Tenang Waterfall and Tanjung Setia Beach. Day 7: Spend the day relaxing at Air Manis Beach.

Food and Drink Scene of Bengkulu (province)

Must-Try Dishes

  • Pempek Lenjer: A traditional dish made with fish, tapioca starch, and spices.
  • Mie Celor: A noodle dish with a creamy coconut milk sauce and fresh seafood.
  • Nasi Goreng Ikan Teri: Fried rice with anchovies and vegetables.

Local Beverages

  • Es Degan: A refreshing drink made with coconut water and palm sugar.
  • Es Teh Bengkulu: A sweet iced tea made with pandan leaves and lime.

Where to Stay in Bengkulu (province): Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Santika Bengkulu: A four-star hotel in the heart of Bengkulu that offers modern amenities and convenience.
  • Hotel Nakita: A budget-friendly option with comfortable rooms and friendly staff.
  • Wisma Boedi: A cozy guesthouse with a homely atmosphere and excellent service.

What to Buy in Bengkulu (province): Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Batik Bengkulu: Traditional handcrafted fabric made with wax-resistant dyeing.
  • Kris: A traditional weapon with a wavy blade and a unique design.
  • Bengkulu Kopi: High-quality coffee beans grown in Bengkulu province.

Local Culture and Customs of Bengkulu (province)

  • Bengkulu’s people are known for their kind and hospitable nature.
  • Visitors are encouraged to dress modestly when visiting mosques and other religious sites.
  • Togathering and sharing meals are some of the common ways to socialize with locals.

Best Time to Visit Bengkulu (province)

The best time to visit Bengkulu is from April to October, which is the dry season. The temperature is cooler, and outdoor activities can be enjoyed without the interruption of rain.

Do’s and Don’ts of Bengkulu (province)

Do:

  • Respect the local customs and traditions.
  • Try the local food and interact with the locals.
  • Wear comfortable clothes and shoes suitable for outdoor activities.

Don’t:

  • Disrespect cultural and religious beliefs.
  • Litter and deface natural wonders.

Safety and Health

Safety in Bengkulu (province)

Bengkulu is a safe place to visit, but visitors should exercise caution at night and avoid areas that are known for criminal activity.

Health in Bengkulu (province)

Visitors are advised to drink bottled water and take precautions against mosquito bites to avoid malaria and dengue fever.

Getting to Bengkulu (province): Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Bengkulu (province)

Fatmawati Soekarno Airport is located in Bengkulu city and offers domestic flights to several cities in Indonesia.

Bus Stations Near Bengkulu (province)

Bengkulu bus station is the main bus terminal offering transportation to several destinations within Bengkulu province.

Other Options

Visitors can also reach Bengkulu by ferry or private car from nearby cities and provinces.

How to Get Around in Bengkulu (province)

Visitors can get around Bengkulu province through taxis, motorcycle taxis, private cars, and public buses.

Where to Go Next from Bengkulu (province): Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Lampung: A neighboring province famous for its beautiful beaches and natural wonders.
  • Padang: A city in West Sumatra known for its spicy food and rich culture.
  • Palembang: The capital city of South Sumatra with a rich history and cultural heritage.

Conclusion

Bengkulu province is a hidden gem in Sumatra island that offers natural wonders, cultural heritage, and a food and drink scene that will make your trip a memorable one. With this comprehensive guide, visitors can plan their travel itinerary, immerse themselves in the local culture, and indulge in the province’s many offerings.
