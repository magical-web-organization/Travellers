Introduction to South Sumatra

Situated in the southern part of Sumatra Island, South Sumatra is one of the largest provinces in Indonesia. Home to diverse ethnicities who possess distinct cultures, traditions, and languages, South Sumatra is a fascinating blend of history, culture, and natural beauty.

This travel guide will take you through the best of South Sumatra, from its culturally significant museums and historical sites to the outdoor adventures and scenic views. Learn about the must-try local dishes, best places to stay, transport options, and much more.

List of Places to Visit in South Sumatra

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

South Sumatra is steeped in history and culture, which is evident from its numerous museums and historical sites. Some of the must-visit places for history buffs include:

  • Museum Keraton Kuto Besak: Located in Palembang, the museum is a testament to the ancient Sriwijaya Kingdom that once ruled South Sumatra.
  • Museum Sultan Mahmud Badaruddin II: This museum showcases the rich cultural heritage of the Malay people of Palembang, including their traditional attire and handicrafts.
  • Ampera Bridge: An iconic bridge that connects the two banks of the Musi River in Palembang, the Ampera Bridge offers stunning sunset views.

Natural Attractions of South Sumatra: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

South Sumatra is a haven for nature lovers, offering a wide range of outdoor activities and scenic views. Some of the most recommended places for adventure seekers and nature enthusiasts include:

  • Bukit Siguntang: With an elevation of 45 meters, this hill offers a panoramic view of Palembang and the surrounding areas.
  • Mount Dempo: The highest mountain in South Sumatra, Mount Dempo offers a breathtaking view of green hills and picturesque villages.
  • Air Terjun Serelo: A beautiful waterfall that is perfect for a refreshing dip and surrounded by lush greenery.

Hiking/Biking Routes of South Sumatra

South Sumatra has some of the best hiking and biking routes in Indonesia. Some of the most popular options include:

  • Mount Dempo Trek: Hike the highest mountain in South Sumatra, through the lush rainforest and coffee plantations.
  • Bukit Dinding: A popular hiking spot with a stunning view of Palembang city.
  • Cycling along the Musi River: Rent a bike and cycle along the Musi River to enjoy the beautiful view of the river.

Best Things to Do in South Sumatra: Activities and Tours

From cultural tours to adventure activities, there’s something for everyone in South Sumatra. Here are some of the best things to do:

  • Heritage Tour of Palembang: Learn about the history and culture of Palembang, including stops at the Sriwijaya Museum, Kuto Besak Fort, and other cultural sites.
  • River Rafting: Experience the thrill of white water rafting along the Lematang River in Lahat.
  • Visit Pagaralam: This small town is located in the mountains and offers spectacular views of Bukit Barisan and tea plantations.

Week-Long Itinerary for South Sumatra

Here is a sample itinerary for a week-long trip to South Sumatra:

  • Day 1: Arrive in Palembang and explore the city’s cultural landmarks.
  • Day 2: Hike up Bukit Siguntang and visit Museum Keraton Kuto Besak.
  • Day 3: Take a day trip to Air Terjun Serelo and explore the nearby villages.
  • Day 4: White water rafting along the Lematang River in Lahat.
  • Day 5: Visit Pagaralam and explore the tea plantations.
  • Day 6: Climb Mount Dempo and catch the sunset.
  • Day 7: Explore Palembang’s culinary scene and shop for souvenirs.

Food and Drink Scene of South Sumatra

Must-Try Dishes

South Sumatra is known for its unique and flavorful cuisine that blends traditional Malay and Chinese influences. Here are some dishes you must try:

  • Pempek: Also known as fish cake, this dish is made from ground fish, sago starch, and spices and served with a sour and spicy sauce.
  • Tekwan: A traditional soup made from fish meatballs, mushrooms, and vegetables, topped with crispy crackers.
  • Mie Celor: A dish of noodles in a thick gravy made of coconut milk, shrimp, and spices.

Local Beverages

Don’t leave South Sumatra without trying these refreshing drinks:

  • Teh Kandangan: A refreshing tea made from ginger and pandan leaves.
  • Bir Pletok: A local ginger beer that is sweet and spicy.
  • Es Kacang Merah: A sweet and cold dessert made from red beans, syrup, and shaved ice.

Where to Stay in South Sumatra: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

South Sumatra has a range of accommodation options to suit different budgets and preferences. Here are a few recommended hotels, hostels, and guesthouses:

  • The Alts Hotel: A luxurious hotel located in the heart of Palembang city.
  • Airy Rambang Lubai Timur 17 Palembang: A budget-friendly hotel located close to the city center.
  • Rumah Limas Palembang: A traditional guesthouse that provides a glimpse into the local culture and architecture.

What to Buy in South Sumatra: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Some of the items you should consider purchasing as souvenirs of your trip to South Sumatra include:

  • Songket: A traditional handwoven fabric made from silk or cotton, decorated with gold or silver thread.
  • Handicrafts: These include wooden carvings, ceramics, and batik fabric.
  • Spices: Sumatra is known for its aromatic spices like cloves, cinnamon, and nutmeg.

Local Culture and Customs of South Sumatra

South Sumatra’s unique culture is shaped by the diverse ethnicities that reside here. Some cultural practices and customs to keep in mind include:

  • Greetings: It is customary to greet elders with a nod or a bow.
  • Dress Modestly: Dress modestly while visiting mosques and other religious sites.
  • Respect for Elders: Show respect to elders as they are highly regarded in South Sumatran culture.

Best Time to Visit South Sumatra

The best time to visit South Sumatra is during the dry season, which runs from June to September. The weather is pleasant, making it perfect for outdoor activities and sightseeing.

Do’s and Don’ts of South Sumatra

Do:

  • Learn some of the basic Malay phrases to engage with the locals.
  • Try the local food and drinks.
  • Respect local customs and traditions.

Don’t:

  • Disrespect religious sites and traditions.
  • Display public affections.
  • Carry too much money or valuables on you in crowded areas.

Safety and Health

Safety in South Sumatra

South Sumatra is generally safe for tourists, but it is always important to take precautions:

  • Be aware of pickpockets and keep your belongings safe.
  • Avoid traveling alone at night.
  • Consult with your tour operator before going on any adventure activities.

Health in South Sumatra

Make sure you are up to date on your vaccinations before travelling to South Sumatra. Mosquito-borne diseases like dengue and malaria are prevalent. Carry mosquito repellent and sleep under mosquito nets where possible.

Getting to South Sumatra: Transportation Options and Logistics

Getting to South Sumatra is relatively easy, and you can choose from various transportation modes:

Airports Near South Sumatra

  • Sultan Mahmud Badaruddin II Airport in Palembang, the main airport in South Sumatra.

Bus Stations Near South Sumatra

  • Palembang Bus Terminal: A central bus terminal that serves various destinations in the region.
  • Lahat Bus Terminal: A bus terminal that serves the Lahat region.

Other Options

  • Taxi: Taxis are readily available in Palembang and other major cities.
  • Train: A train runs between Palembang and Bandung.

How to Get Around in South Sumatra

Getting around South Sumatra is relatively easy, and you can choose from various transportation options:

  • Ojek: A motorcycle taxi that is fast and efficient.
  • Becak: A cycle rickshaw that can carry two passengers.
  • Bus: Buses run frequently between towns and cities.

Where to Go Next from South Sumatra: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

South Sumatra is well-connected to other regions in Indonesia, and there are plenty of nearby destinations worth exploring. Some of the nearby destinations include:

  • Krakatoa: A volcanic island known for its epic 1883 eruption.
  • Jakarta: The capital city of Indonesia with a rich history and culture.
  • Bali: An island paradise with stunning beaches and traditional culture.

Conclusion

South Sumatra is a hidden gem in Indonesia, with a rich history, culture, and natural beauty. From outdoor adventures to cultural landmarks, there’s plenty to explore in South Sumatra. Plan your trip with this comprehensive guide to experience the best of this region.
