Introduction to Lhokseumawe

Being the second-largest city in Aceh, Lhokseumawe is a bustling metropolis located on the north coast of Sumatra Island, Indonesia. It is a land of untamed natural wonders, hidden cultural treasures, and unbeatable hospitality. Travelers looking to spend some time exploring the lesser-known parts of Indonesia must put Lhokseumawe on their destination list.

List of Places to Visit in Lhokseumawe

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Lhokseumawe offers significant insights into Aceh’s culture and history through its various museums and historical places. Some of the must-visit places include:

  • Museum Aceh: This is one of the largest museums in Sumatra, featuring an extensive collection of historical and cultural artifacts from the Aceh region.

  • Museum Rumoh Aceh: It is an elegant wooden house that provides a glimpse into traditional Acehnese architecture and lifestyle.

  • Jami Mosque: Located on the Jenderal Sudirman road, it is one of the oldest and most iconic mosques in Aceh, known for its impressive architecture.

Natural Attractions of Lhokseumawe: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Lhokseumawe is blessed with pristine forest reserves, waterfalls, and beaches, making it an outdoor lover’s paradise. Visitors can relish numerous outdoor activities and breathtaking views, some of which include:

  • Kuala Pangan Beach: This is a secluded beach that offers a serene and remote escape from the city. The rocky coastline, a small strip of sand, and crystal clear water make it a perfect place to relax.

  • Ujung Pancu Waterfall: This beautiful waterfall boasts three tiers, one of which is 30 metres high and is easily accessible through a short hike.

  • Lhokseumawe Forest Reserve: This reserve covers over 50,000 hectares and is home to several endangered species of flora and fauna. Travelers may spot elephants and tigers while trekking through the dense jungle.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Lhokseumawe

Lhokseumawe offers a range of hiking and biking routes that can be enjoyed by nature enthusiasts and adventurous tourists. Some of the popular ones include:

  • Geunpase Trail: This trail runs through the Lhokseumawe Forest Reserve and offers hikers a chance to spot rare flora and fauna along with scenic views.

  • Seuneubok Bayu Trail: It is a less challenging hike that runs through a peaceful forest and offers several viewpoints making it a picturesque trail.

Best Things to Do in Lhokseumawe: Activities and Tours

There is no shortage of unique activities or tours in Lhokseumawe, making it a dream destination for adventure seekers and culture lovers alike. Some of the best things to do include:

  • Visit the Geureudong Volcano: This active volcano offers a challenging but exciting climb to the summit. At the top, one can see a stunning panoramic view of the city.

  • Kayaking in the Seuneubok Bayu river: This activity is perfect for adventurous travelers looking to explore the lush greenery surrounding Seuneubok Bayu river.

  • Visit the traditional markets: The bustling traditional markets of Lhokseumawe offer tourists a chance to experience the local culture, taste delicious Indonesian dishes, and purchase unique souvenirs.

Week-Long Itinerary for Lhokseumawe

Here is a suggested itinerary for a week-long trip to Lhokseumawe:

  • Day 1: Arrive in Lhokseumawe and visit the Museum Aceh and Museum Rumoh Aceh.

  • Day 2: Explore the Seuneubok Bayu trail, enjoy kayaking in the river, and discover the traditional markets.

  • Day 3: Visit the impressive Jami mosque, hike through the Lhokseumawe forest reserve and enjoy a picnic at Ujung Pancu Waterfall.

  • Day 4: Go on a city tour- visit the historical places, such as the Panglima Polem Fort and the Islamic Center, and relax at the Kuala Pangan Beach.

  • Day 5: Climb the captivating Geureudong volcano and explore the greenery surrounding it.

  • Day 6: Spend the day strolling around the town, shopping for souvenirs, and trying out local delicacies.

  • Day 7: Depart from Lhokseumawe.

Food and Drink Scene of Lhokseumawe

Acehnese cuisine blends Indonesian spices with Arab and Indian influences, making it unique and delicious. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages when visiting Lhokseumawe:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Mie Aceh: This spicy noodle dish is a popular choice amongst tourists and locals alike.

  • Nasi Goreng Kambing: A spicy goat fried rice that is a staple dish in Lhokseumawe.

  • Sate Matang: The dish comprises beef skewers served with spicy sauce and Ketupat (rice cakes)

Local Beverages

  • Aceh Gayo Coffee: Known for its bold and strong flavour, Aceh Gayo Coffee is a must-try for all coffee lovers.

  • Teh Sireh: It is a popular drink brewed with betel leaves, which is said to have medicinal properties.

Where to Stay in Lhokseumawe: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Lhokseumawe has several accommodation options ranging from luxury hotels to budget-friendly guesthouses. Some of the best options include:

  • Hotel Grand Arabia: A luxurious hotel located in the heart of the city.

  • OYO 3611 Penginapan Surga: A budget-friendly hotel with comfortable rooms and basic amenities.

What to Buy in Lhokseumawe: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Visitors can take home several souvenirs and local products from Lhokseumawe, some of which include:

  • Acehnese Coffee: Packets of coffee make for a great souvenir for coffee enthusiasts.

  • Traditional Fabric: Lhokseumawe is known for its traditional Batik and Songket fabric, which can be purchased from local markets.

Local Culture and Customs of Lhokseumawe

The traditional Acehnese culture is deeply rooted in Islam, and as a result, residents of Lhokseumawe follow conservative norms. It is essential to dress modestly while visiting religious sites or areas.

Best Time to Visit Lhokseumawe

The best time to visit Lhokseumawe is between June and September when the weather is dry and pleasant, making it ideal for indulging in outdoor activities and sightseeing.

Do’s and Don’ts of Lhokseumawe

Do:

  • Respect the local traditions and customs.

  • Try the local cuisine and beverages.

  • Spend time supporting local businesses and vendors.

Don’t:

  • Dress inappropriately when visiting religious sites and areas.

  • Touch any of the historical or cultural artifacts without permission.

  • Ignore the safety protocols when hiking or trekking.

Safety and Health

Travelers should take measures to ensure their safety and health while visiting Lhokseumawe.

Safety in Lhokseumawe

  • Travelers should avoid visiting the city during the monsoon season when roads are prone to landslides.

  • Take precautions while trekking and hiking, such as hiring a local guide and carrying necessary equipment.

Health in Lhokseumawe

  • Drink only bottled or boiled water.

  • Carry your insect repellents and mosquito nets.

Getting to Lhokseumawe: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Lhokseumawe

  • Sultan Iskandar Muda International Airport in Aceh operates several domestic and international flights to and from Lhokseumawe.

Bus Stations Near Lhokseumawe

  • Lhokseumawe has a bus terminal that operates several services to other destinations in Sumatra Island.

Other Options

  • Visitors can hire taxis or private cars from nearby cities to reach Lhokseumawe.

How to Get Around in Lhokseumawe

Travelers can hire taxis, motorbikes, or scooters to get around Lhokseumawe. However, it is recommended to negotiate the fare beforehand.

Where to Go Next from Lhokseumawe: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Lhokseumawe is located close to several stunning destinations that are worth exploring. Some of the places you can visit include:

  • Banda Aceh: The capital city of Aceh, located two and a half hours away from Lhokseumawe.

  • Pulau Weh: A small tropical island with crystal-clear waters ideal for diving and snorkelling.

Conclusion

Lhokseumawe is a hidden gem of Aceh that offers an unforgettable travel experience. With its rich culture, natural wonders, and peaceful atmosphere, Lhokseumawe will undoubtedly make your Indonesia vacation an unforgettable one.
