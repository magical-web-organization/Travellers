Introduction to Bangka-Belitung

Located in Sumatra, Indonesia, the province of Bangka-Belitung is known for its stunning beaches, crystal-clear waters, and rich cultural heritage. Comprising two main islands – Bangka and Belitung – the region has become a popular destination for tourists in Southeast Asia. In this travel guide, we’ll take you on a journey through the most breathtaking and unique experiences that Bangka-Belitung has to offer.

List of Places to Visit in Bangka-Belitung

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • The Museum of Tin: Learn about the history of the mining industry in Bangka-Belitung and discover how tin became such an important resource in the area.
  • The Kaolin Lake: This former mining site is now a renowned tourist spot, thanks to its serene lake and stunning surrounding scenery.
  • The Old Temple at Penyabong Beach: A beautiful temple that dates back to the 19th century, nestled in the picturesque Penyabong Beach.

Natural Attractions of Bangka-Belitung: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Lengkuas Island: A small but picturesque island surrounded by crystal-clear waters, with a historic lighthouse that visitors can climb for breathtaking sea views.
  • Tanjung Kelayang Beach: Located on Belitung Island, this stunning beach is known for its granite rock formations, crystal-clear water, and a variety of water activities such as snorkelling and diving.
  • Matras Island: This uninhabited island is perfect for those looking for some seclusion and relaxation. The island is known for its coral reefs and white sandy beaches.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Bangka-Belitung

  • Trikora Beach: An excellent seafront in Bangka Island with rolling hills. It’s a great place to relax and enjoy the views or trek through the woodland trails.
  • Rambak Mountain: A popular destination for hiking enthusiasts, this mountain has trails that range from easy to difficult; as you progress higher, the views become increasingly more breathtaking.
  • Pulau Subar Island: The island is only accessible by boat, but visitors can take bike tours around the island to see its stunning natural beauty.

Best Things to Do in Bangka-Belitung: Activities and Tours

  • Snorkelling Tour: Explore the colorful underwater world of Bangka-Belitung by taking a guided snorkeling tour to see tropical fish, turtles, and coral reefs up close and personal.
  • Island Hopping Tour: Take a day trip to visit some of the most popular islands in Bangka-Belitung and experience their unique beauty and charm.
  • Culinary Tour: One of the best ways to experience the local culture and customs is through food. Take a tour of the local markets and taste some of the best dishes that the region has to offer.

Week-Long Itinerary for Bangka-Belitung

Day Activities
1 Arrive at Pangkal Pinang Airport and travel to Pasir Padi Beach, one of the most popular beaches in Bangka Island
2 Visit the Museum of Tin in Pangkal Pinang and explore the Kaolin Lake
3 Take a boat tour of Lengkuas Island and enjoy the crystal-clear water and scenic views
4 Go island hopping to see some of the most stunning islands in the region, such as Matras and Parai Tenggiri
5 Take a guided tour of Penyabong Beach, one of the most picturesque beaches on the island, and visit the Old Temple
6 Trek the beautiful Rambak Mountain and explore the surrounding woodlands
7 Visit the Tanjung Kelayang Beach and go snorkelling to explore the beautiful underwater world

Food and Drink Scene of Bangka-Belitung

Must-Try Dishes

  • Mie Belitung: A traditional Belitung dish made with flour noodles, prawn, egg, and seafood sauce.
  • Sop Ikan: A soup dish made with fish, tomatoes, and shallots, served with rice or noodles.
  • Ikan Bakar: A grilled fish dish that is popular throughout the region.

Local Beverages

  • Es Kacang: A popular Indonesian shaved ice dessert topped with condensed milk, syrup, and a variety of toppings such as jelly and fruit.
  • Lemongrass Tea: A refreshing tea made with lemongrass and ginger, known for its calming properties.
  • Kopi Tubruk: A strong local coffee usually served with sweetened condensed milk.

Where to Stay in Bangka-Belitung: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Aston Hotel Pangkal Pinang: Located in the heart of Pangkal Pinang, this four-star hotel offers modern amenities and easy access to some of the most popular attractions in the area.
  • Airy Syariah Hotel: A budget-friendly option located in Bangka Island, with comfortable rooms and friendly staff.
  • Bumi Asih Bangka: A traditional-style guesthouse located in Belitung Island with spacious rooms, garden views, and excellent service.

What to Buy in Bangka-Belitung: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Tin Products: Bangka Island is famous for its tin mining industry, so be sure to check out local shops selling tin-based products such as keychains, jewelry, and home decor.
  • Belitung Woven Products: Belitung Island is known for its traditional weaving techniques, producing handmade baskets, bags, and home decor items.
  • Kain Songket: This traditional fabric is hand-woven and embroidered with intricate designs. It makes an excellent souvenir or gift from Bangka-Belitung.

Local Culture and Customs of Bangka-Belitung

  • Respect for Elders: In Indonesian culture, elders are greatly respected, so bear this in mind when interacting with the locals.
  • Conservative Dress Code: Indonesians are conservative in their dress, so avoid wearing revealing clothing when in public.
  • Hospitality: The locals have a strong culture of hospitality, making visitors feel welcomed and valued.

Best Time to Visit Bangka-Belitung

The best time to visit Bangka-Belitung is from May to October when the weather is relatively cooler and drier. However, expect higher tourist traffic during this time. It’s best to avoid the monsoon season from November to March, as the rain can lead to travel disruptions and delays in outdoor activities.

Do’s and Don’ts of Bangka-Belitung

Do:

  • Learn a few basic Indonesian phrases to show respect for the local culture.
  • Respect local customs and traditions, such as removing your shoes before entering a home or temple.
  • Try the local food and beverages to immerse yourself into the local culture.

Don’t:

  • Don’t disrespect the local culture or customs.
  • Don’t dispose of trash in public spaces; always use designated trash bins.
  • Don’t swim or bathe in unsupervised areas, especially during the monsoon season.

Safety and Health

Safety in Bangka-Belitung

Bangka-Belitung is generally safe for tourists, but it’s essential to be careful with your possessions and stay aware of your surroundings when traveling in public areas, especially during the night. Be careful when swimming or snorkelling in the beaches, always ask for the latest tide report.

Health in Bangka-Belitung

Be sure to consult with your doctor before visiting Bangka-Belitung to ensure that you have all necessary vaccinations and are aware of any potential health risks in the area. It’s best to drink bottled water, and avoid street food that is not adequately cooked.

Getting to Bangka-Belitung: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Bangka-Belitung

  • Depati Amir Airport: Located in Pangkal Pinang, this is the principal airport in Bangka Island.

Bus Stations Near Bangka-Belitung

  • Ferry Port of Bangka-Belitung: This ferry port serves as a gateway for visitors arriving in Bangka-Belitung by sea.

Other Options

Options include chartered cars and water taxis are available to travel between the islands in Bangka-Belitung.

How to Get Around in Bangka-Belitung

Public transportation options may be limited, so it’s recommended to hire a private car or taxi or join a group tour to visit the main attractions comfortably.

Where to Go Next from Bangka-Belitung: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Bangka-Belitung is a gateway to other must-see destinations in Sumatra. Some must-see places include:

  • Bukit Tinggi: A stunning highland town known for its quaint Dutch architecture and history.
  • Lake Toba: Southeast Asia’s largest natural lake located in North Sumatra province, famous for its picturesque views and cultural significance

Conclusion

Bangka-Belitung is a breathtaking destination that is perfect for those looking to immerse themselves in the cultural vibrancy of Indonesia. The region offers a unique blend of natural beauty and cultural heritage, providing visitors with a truly unforgettable travel experience. Use this comprehensive travel guide, and explore some of the hidden gems of Bangka-Belitung on your next adventure.
