Introduction to Kundur

Kundur is a small island located in the Riau Islands province of Sumatra, Indonesia. Known for its pristine beaches, lush forests, and cultural attractions, Kundur is a hidden gem waiting to be explored. The island is home to numerous historical and cultural sites, natural wonders, and outdoor adventures that make it an ideal destination for travelers seeking an authentic experience.

List of Places to Visit in Kundur

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Kilang Minyak - a historic oil refinery built during the Dutch colonial period that now serves as a museum showcasing the island’s oil industry history.
  • Keramat Sultan Mahmud - a sacred site dedicated to the legendary Sultan Mahmud who is believed to have brought Islam to Kundur.
  • Lamin Tamo - a traditional house that offers insights into the local Malay architecture and lifestyle.
  • Kampung Dabo Singkep Museum - a museum dedicated to the history of the Singkep Island located off the coast of Kundur.

Natural Attractions of Kundur: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Bukit Merah - a scenic hill located in the northern part of the island offering panoramic views of the sea and the surrounding forests.
  • Sungai Pinang Muka Forest Reserve - a protected area featuring diverse wildlife, including hornbills, eagles, and monkeys, and hiking trails leading to stunning waterfalls.
  • Pantai Tamban - a secluded, picturesque beach with clear blue water and white-sand beaches ideal for swimming and sunbathing.
  • Kampung Jawa Waterfall - a stunning waterfall located in the middle of the island accessible through a short jungle trek.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Kundur

  • Pantai Tamban - Bukit Merah - a 10-kilometer hike through the forest and over the hill for the most stunning views.
  • Sungai Pinang Muka Forest Reserve - multiple trails are varying lengths that take you through the reserve with its stunning wildlife and waterfalls.
  • Coastal bike ride - rent a bike or scooter and take a scenic ride along the coast with stops at beaches along the way.

Best Things to Do in Kundur: Activities and Tours

  • Snorkeling and diving - experience the vibrant underwater world and the abundance of marine life in the crystal-clear waters surrounding the island.
  • Local village tours - take a guided tour of the island’s nearest villages to witness the local lifestyle and learn about the traditional crafts and cuisine.
  • Fishing trips - join the local fishermen on their boats and catch your lunch while enjoying the scenic views of the waters.
  • Island-hopping tours - explore the nearby islands and lose yourself to their natural beauty and stunning beaches.

Week-Long Itinerary for Kundur

Day Activities
1 Arrival and check-in at the hotel or guesthouse. Relax at the beach and watch the sunset.
2 Visit Kilang Minyak and Keramat Sultan Mahmud. Take a village tour and learn about the local’s lifestyles.
3 Island-hopping tour - explore the nearby islands of Singkep and Siantan.

Food and Drink Scene of Kundur

Must-Try Dishes

  • Gonggong - a type of sea-snail boiled and served with a spicy sauce.
  • Gulai Daun Singkong - a coconut milk curry made with cassava leaves and various spices.
  • Ikan Bakar - grilled fish served with sambal, a spicy chili sauce.

Local Beverages

  • Air Kelapa - coconut water served fresh from the fruit.
  • Teh Tarik - sweet and milky tea served hot or cold.

Where to Stay in Kundur: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Kundur offers various options for accommodation, from budget-friendly hostels to luxury resorts. Some popular ones include:

  • Kundur Island Resort - a luxury resort located on Pantai Tamban, offering private beaches, a pool, and a stunning view of the sea.
  • Pantai Cottage - a small but comfortable guesthouse located near the beach, offering traditional fare and a family-friendly atmosphere.
  • Cempaka Homestay - a budget-friendly guesthouse in the heart of Kundur Town, close to the main attractions and ideal for travelers seeking a local cultural experience.

What to Buy in Kundur: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Handicrafts - the island is known for its traditional crafts made of materials like rattan, coconut shells, and textiles.
  • Spices - Kundur produces a wide range of spices like nutmeg, cloves, and cinnamon, popularly sold in local markets.
  • Oil-based products - the island’s oil industry has led to the development of locally made oil-based products like soap, lotions, and aromatherapy products.

Local Culture and Customs of Kundur

The locals are predominantly Malay and are primarily Muslims. Visitors are advised to dress modestly and respect the local traditions and practices. Friday and Saturday are observed as the weekend, and some restaurants and shops may be closed on these days.

Best Time to Visit Kundur

The best time to visit Kundur is from April to October when the weather is favorable, and outdoor activities can be enjoyed without disruption from the monsoons.

Do’s and Don’ts of Kundur

Do:

  • Respect the local customs and traditions.
  • Wear comfortable and modest clothing that covers the knees and shoulders when visiting religious sites.
  • Learn some basic Malay phrases and words to communicate with the locals.

Don’t:

  • Wear revealing clothing in public places or religious sites.
  • Engage in activities that may harm the local environment, such as littering or collecting coral.
  • Get too close to monkeys or other wild animals.

Safety and Health

Safety in Kundur

Kundur is relatively safe, and the crime rate is low. Visitors should take basic precautions like avoiding walking alone at night and keeping an eye on personal belongings.

Health in Kundur

Visitors should take precautionary measures to protect themselves from mosquito-borne diseases like dengue fever, particularly during the wet season. It is recommended you seek health advice from your doctor before visiting Kundur.

Getting to Kundur: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Kundur

The nearest international airport is the Hang Nadim Airport in Batam Island, which offers regular flights to and from major cities in Indonesia and Singapore. From there, visitors can catch a ferry or a speedboat to Kundur.

Bus Stations Near Kundur

The Riau Islands have a comprehensive bus network that links major cities, towns, and islands, including Kundur.

Other Options

Visitors can also take a ferry or speedboat from neighbouring islands.

How to Get Around in Kundur

Kundur has well-maintained roads, and there are plenty of options for getting around, including motorbike rentals, taxis, and rental cars.

Where to Go Next from Kundur: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Singkep Island - another island in the Riau Islands with fantastic beaches, diving spots, and rainforests.
  • Tanjung Pinang - the capital city of the Riau Islands Province, known for its colorful markets and cultural landmarks.
  • Batam Island - known for its beaches, nightlife, and an array of family-friendly activities.

Conclusion

Kundur is a great destination for those seeking a break from the hustle and bustle of city life and a chance to reconnect with nature and authentic local life. With its scenic views, cultural landmarks, and outdoor adventures, this island paradise is a must-visit for any traveler visiting Sumatra.
