Introduction to Barelang Islands

Barelang Islands is a group of six islands located in Batam, Riau Islands, Sumatra, Indonesia. The name “Barelang” is an abbreviation of Batam, Rempang, and Galang Islands. These islands are known for their picturesque natural landscapes, historical sites, and cultural significance. With plenty of outdoor adventures and water activities, Barelang Islands attract both domestic and international travelers looking for a unique vacation experience.

Food and Drink Scene of Barelang Islands

Barelang Islands have a diverse culinary scene, showcasing both local Indonesian cuisine and international flavors. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Otak-otak: grilled fish cake wrapped in banana leaves
  • Sate Padang: grilled beef skewers with spicy sauce
  • Gado-gado: mixed vegetables with peanut sauce

Local Beverages

  • Teh Botol: sweetened tea in a bottle
  • Es Cendol: sweet coconut milk with green jelly and palm sugar syrup
  • Bintang Beer: an Indonesian favorite beer

Local Culture and Customs of Barelang Islands

Barelang Islands have a hybrid culture derived from various ethnic and religious influences. Indonesians are known to be hospitable and friendly, with a strong sense of community. Dress modestly, and respect local customs while visiting the islands.

Best Time to Visit Barelang Islands

The best time to visit Barelang Islands is from May to October. However, visitors can enjoy the islands year-round, with occasional rainfall and tropical thunderstorms during the rainy season.

Do’s and Don’ts of Barelang Islands

Do:

  • Respect local customs and dress modestly
  • Try the delicious Indonesian cuisine
  • Dive and explore the underwater life
  • Interact with locals and learn about their way of life

Don’t:

  • Disrespect religious and cultural customs
  • Damage the natural and cultural resources
  • Over-tip excessively

Safety and Health

Safety in Barelang Islands

Barelang Islands are generally safe and secure for travelers. However, visitors should exercise caution and avoid traveling alone during the night. Always keep your valuables safe and secure.

Health in Barelang Islands

Visitors should take necessary precautions and carry mosquito repellents and sunscreen. Drink only bottled water to avoid food and water-borne illnesses. Seek medical attention in case of emergencies.

Getting to Barelang Islands: Transportation Options and Logistics

The nearest airport to Barelang Islands is Hang Nadim International Airport in Batam, with frequent flights from Jakarta and other major cities in Indonesia. Visitors can take a ferry or boat ride to Barelang Islands from Batam Island.

Airports Near Barelang Islands

  • Hang Nadim International Airport, Batam

Bus Stations Near Barelang Islands

  • Batam Center Bus Terminal

Other Options

  • Ferry and boat rides from Batam Island

How to Get Around in Barelang Islands

Visitors can easily get around Barelang Islands by renting a car, motorcycle, or bicycle. Taxis and local public transportation, including minibusses and angkot (public vans), are also available.
