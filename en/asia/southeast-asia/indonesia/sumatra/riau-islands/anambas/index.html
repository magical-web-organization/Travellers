Introduction to Anambas

Anambas is a stunning archipelago in Riau Islands, Sumatra, Indonesia. Comprised of over 255 islands, only a few of them are inhabited, making it a peaceful and secluded destination. The islands are home to natural beauty, an abundance of marine life and beautiful coral reefs, and people who are proud of their traditions and culture.

Tourism is developing at a slow pace in Anambas, and while that may seem like a disadvantage, it is quite the opposite. It means that visitors can feel as though they have discovered a hidden gem that has yet to be overrun by tourism.

List of Places to Visit in Anambas

Discover the must-visit places in Anambas:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Jebung Village: One of the oldest villages in Anambas, you can tour the village, admire the unique architecture, and learn about local traditions.
  • Jemaja Island: This island has a long history of being a trading center before the arrival of the Dutch. The island is now home to a lively seafood market, where you can watch fishers bring in their catches from the sea.

Natural Attractions of Anambas: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Tarempa Town: The largest town in Anambas, offers a scenic view of the ocean and nearby islands. It is also an excellent location to enjoy the sunset.
  • Air Asukur Waterfall: This 25-meter-tall waterfall is a hidden gem in Anambas with an incredible hike to reach the lush greenery where the waterfall flows.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Anambas

For adventure enthusiasts who love hiking or biking, there are scenic trekking routes that offer a unique glimpse of the island life. Some of the popular routes include:

  • Situ Buaya Viewpoint: This viewpoint can be accessed via a 2-kilometer trail on Terempa Island. Once you reach the top, you will be treated to breathtaking views over the island and the ocean.
  • Pulau Penjalin Trek: This trek is located on the uninhabited Penjalin Island and offers a view of the stunning coral reef system surrounding the island.

Best Things to Do in Anambas: Activities and Tours

There are plenty to see and do in Anambas; here is a list of the top things to do:

  • Island Hopping: With over 255 islands, island hopping to explore nearby islands is a must-do activity in Anambas.
  • Scuba Diving: Anambas is a well-known diving hotspot, with some of the best dive sites in Asia, boasting vibrant coral and marine life.

Week-Long Itinerary for Anambas

Suppose you’re planning to visit Anambas for a week. Here is an itinerary to make the most of your time:

Day 1: Reach Tarempa Town and explore the town. Day 2: Take a boat ride to Penjalin Island for a day trip. Day 3: Explore traditional villages, such as Jebung and Jemaja. Day 4: Enjoy a hiking route of your choice. Day 5: Go island hopping to explore nearby islands. Day 6: Experience scuba diving in one of the best dive sites in Anambas. Day 7: Relax, and take in the scenic beauty of Tarempa Town before returning home.

Food and Drink Scene of Anambas

Discover the unique flavors and cuisine of Anambas:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Ikan Kuah Asam: A tangy fish soup made with fresh tamarind and tuna that locals highly recommend.
  • Ayam Botak: The name Ayam Botak refers to the prepared chicken, which has its hair plucked or shaved off.

Local Beverages

  • Air Kelapa: Coconut water is a refreshing local drink that is widely available in Anambas.
  • Sop Kambing: A spiced goat soup made with local ingredients.

Where to Stay in Anambas: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Anambas has a limited number of commercial accommodations to choose from, but they are charming and comfortable:

  • Siantau Island Resort: With a prime location on Siantau Island, it is one of the most popular resorts in Anambas.
  • Anambas Beach Bungalows: Located on Jemaja Island, the bungalows offer stunning ocean views and a peaceful getaway from the city life.

What to Buy in Anambas: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Souvenirs and local products to buy from Anambas include:

  • Wooden Carvings: The locals have artisan skills in wood carving, which you can purchase as souvenirs.
  • Batik Clothes: Batik clothes are prevalent throughout Indonesia, and Anambas is no exception.

Local Culture and Customs of Anambas

The locals of Anambas are proud of their culture and history. Respectful tourism is welcomed, and visitors should dress conservatively and follow local customs, including:

  • Islam is the Religion in Anambas: Visitors should respect Islamic culture and customs, such as dressing modestly.
  • Cleanliness and Footwear: Locals practice cleanliness and believe that footwear should not be worn inside the house.

Best Time to Visit Anambas

The best time to visit Anambas island is between April to November when the weather is dry and sunny. The rainy season starts in December, which can result in a slower pace of outdoor activities.

Do’s and Don’ts of Anambas

Here are some do’s and don’ts to follow during your stay:

Do:

  • Respect local customs and traditions.
  • Dress conservatively, especially while visiting traditional villages.

Don’t:

  • Leave waste and litter around.
  • Disrespect the culture and religion of locals.

Safety and Health

Safety in Anambas

Anambas is safe for tourists, but travelers are advised to take precautions such as:

  • Travel with authorized travel agencies and registered tour guides.
  • Ensure there is a lifeguard on duty before going swimming.
  • Always wear a helmet while biking or cycling.

Health in Anambas

The standard of health care facilities in Anambas is limited, so it is advisable to:

  • Carry your medication and first-aid kit if you have a pre-existing medical condition.
  • Drink bottled water.

Getting to Anambas: Transportation Options and Logistics

There are three ways to reach Anambas:

Airports Near Anambas

  • Hang Nadim Airport: It is the nearest airport to Anambas, and you can take a flight from Singapore to reach this airport. From there, take a ferry to reach Anambas.

Bus Stations Near Anambas

Anambas is on an island, and there are no bus stations within the island.

Other Options

  • Ferry: A ferry from Batam or Bintan Island is the easiest way to access Anambas.

How to Get Around in Anambas

The best way to get around Anambas is via boats, either public boats or chartered boats available for rent at the Tarempa Harbour. Bicycles can also be rented for exploring the islands.

Where to Go Next from Anambas: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

After the island vacation in Anambas, here are other places to visit:

  • Bintan Island: A popular destination for island hopping and beach activities.
  • Redang Islands: Located in Malaysia, it is renowned for marine life and beach activities.

Conclusion

Anambas is a slice of paradise on earth with unparalleled natural beauty, rich history, and vibrant culture. With this comprehensive tour guide, you can plan your visit to experience the best of Anambas Island and take home a lifetime of memories.
