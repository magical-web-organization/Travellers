Introduction to Sumpat

Sumpat is a hidden gem on the Indonesian island of Bintan, located in the Riau Archipelago. Legendary tales accompany this place and make it one of the most traditional destinations in Bintan. It is famous for its pristine beaches, rich historical past, extensive tropical forests, magnificent waterfalls, and the variety of adventure activities. Throughout the year, thousands of tourists from around the world visit Sumpat for an unforgettable experience.

In this comprehensive travel guide, we will explore the best things to see and do, places to stay, and delicious food to try in Sumpat.

List of Places to Visit in Sumpat

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Sumpat Village - A traditional village that reflects the local Minangkabau people’s culture. It is located near a forest and offers a calm and relaxed atmosphere to visitors.

  • Tanjung Uban Fort - This fort was built by the Portuguese in the 16th century, and it is one of the historical landmarks of Sumpat. Visitors can take a guided tour and learn about its historical significance.

  • Raja Haji Fisabilillah Monument - It is a monument built to commemorate the bravery of Raja Haji Fisabilillah, who fought against the Dutch colonizers. The monument offers a glimpse into the local culture and history.

Natural Attractions of Sumpat: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Grouse Mountain - It is an ideal place for hiking enthusiasts. The summit provides a panoramic view of the surrounding hills and the sea.

  • Gunung Bawah Waterfall - It is a stunning waterfall located near the heart of the forest. The waterfall has several stages and is popular for its gorgeous scenery.

  • Pulau Penyengat - This small island is famous for its white sandy beaches, crystal-clear water, and coral reefs. Visitors can indulge in activities like snorkeling, swimming, and beach hopping.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Sumpat

  • Sumpat to Sungai Lekop Waterfall Trail - This route covers a distance of 7 km and is popular among hikers. The trail leads visitors through the dense forest, small hills, and streams before reaching the breathtaking waterfall.

  • Rindu Alam & Pemancingan Lake Trail - This 9 km trail takes visitors on a scenic journey through the dense forest and vibrant flora of Sumpat. Visitors can also indulge in fishing at Pemancingan Lake.

Best Things to Do in Sumpat: Activities and Tours

  • Island Hopping Tour - Visitors can experience the beauty of the untouched islands around Sumpat on this tour. The tour takes visitors to several islands, including Pulau Penyengat and Pulau Mapur.

  • Waterfall Adventure Tour - This tour takes visitors on a journey through the forest to explore the best waterfalls in Sumpat, including Gunung Bawah and Sungai Lekop Falls.

  • Sunset Cruise - Visitors can take a sunset cruise and enjoy the magnificent scenery of the coastline and the ocean.

Food and Drink Scene of Sumpat

Must-Try Dishes

  • Nasi Padang - It is one of Indonesia’s most popular dishes and is made up of steamed rice served with various curries, vegetables, and meat.

  • Keropok Belinjo - It is a traditional Indonesian snack made from the roots of the belinjo tree. It is crispy and is commonly served with spicy sauce.

  • Lemang - It is a glutinous rice cooked inside a bamboo tube and served with chicken or beef curry.

Local Beverages

  • Teh Tarik - A popular local drink made by pouring hot milk tea from one container to another repeatedly. It is frothy and sweet.

  • Kopi Tubruk - It is a strong Indonesian coffee made with boiling water and sugar. It is usually enjoyed with local biscuits.

Where to Stay in Sumpat: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Sumpat Indonesia Resort - A luxurious resort located near Grouse Mountain, offering visitors an elegant experience.

  • Bintan Sayang Resort - A budget-friendly resort with a prime location just minutes away from the beach.

  • Sumpat Guesthouse - A homely guesthouse located in the heart of Sumpat village, equipped with all necessities for a comfortable stay.

What to Buy in Sumpat: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Batik Fabric - It is a traditional Indonesian fabric that comes in a wide variety of colors and patterns. Popular items include batik shirts, sarongs, and bags.

  • Keropok Belinjo - It is a traditional Indonesian snack that is commonly brought back as a souvenir.

  • Local Handicrafts - Visitors can choose from a wide variety of local handicrafts, including wood carvings, rattan baskets, and woven textiles.

Local Culture and Customs of Sumpat

  • Minangkabau Culture - The Minangkabau culture predominates in Sumpat. Visitors can enjoy getting immersed in the local customs, traditions, and clothing.

  • Local Festivals - Numerous festivals and celebrations around the year provide visitors with the opportunity to experience the local heritage and culture.

Best Time to Visit Sumpat

Sumpat can be visited throughout the year. However, the best time to visit is from April to October, when the temperature is the most moderate and there are fewer chances of rainfall.

Do’s and Don’ts of Sumpat

Do:

  • Respect the local culture and traditions.

  • Dress modestly in religious places and when visiting small villages.

  • Take shoes off before entering homes or places of worship.

Don’t:

  • Engage in PDA in public areas.

  • Use your left hand when greeting, eating, or receiving items from others.

  • Touch people’s heads, especially children’s heads, as it is considered rude.

Safety and Health

Safety in Sumpat

Sumpat is considered a safe destination for tourists. However, visitors are advised to take necessary precautions, such as securing their belongings, especially in crowded areas.

Health in Sumpat

Visitors should take necessary precautions against mosquito bites, especially during rainy seasons. They should also consume bottled water to avoid any water-borne diseases.

Getting to Sumpat: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Sumpat

The nearest airport is Raja Haji Fisabilillah International Airport, which serves direct flights from Jakarta, Singapore, and Kuala Lumpur. From Tanjung Pinang ferry terminal, visitors can take a taxi or bus to Sumpat.

Bus Stations Near Sumpat

Buses from Tanjung Pinang Bus Station stop in Sumpat village. Visitors can take buses from Pekanbaru, Batam, Singapore, and Johor Bahru.

Other Options

Ferries are available from Singapore, Johor Bahru, and Tanjung Pinang to Bintan Island. Visitors can then take a taxi or bus to reach Sumpat.

How to Get Around in Sumpat

Visitors can rent a car or motorbike to explore the area. Taxis and buses are also available, but the schedules might not be reliable.

Where to Go Next from Sumpat: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Tanah Merah Beach - A peaceful and secluded beach located at the end of the Bintan Island.

  • Mangrove Forest Escapade - An eco-tourism destination that offers visitors exciting encounters with the wildlife.

Conclusion

Sumpat is a hidden gem tucked away in the Riau Islands Archipelago. With its vibrant culture, historical landmarks, pristine beaches, and adventurous activities, it offers visitors an unforgettable experience. Plan your trip today and get ready to explore this enchanting destination.
