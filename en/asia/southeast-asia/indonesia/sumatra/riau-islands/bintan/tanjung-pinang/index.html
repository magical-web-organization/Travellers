Introduction to Tanjung Pinang

Tanjung Pinang is a charming city situated on the western coast of Bintan Island in the Riau Islands of Indonesia. Being the capital of the Riau Islands Province, Tanjung Pinang is known for its rich culture, history, and natural beauty. The city attracts tourists from all over the world who come here to explore its beautiful temples, historic sites, stunning beaches, and lively markets.

List of Places to Visit in Tanjung Pinang

If you are planning a trip to Tanjung Pinang, make sure to include the following attractions in your itinerary:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Rumah Bakar Museum

The Rumah Bakar Museum is a unique museum that showcases traditional Malay architecture. It is a great place to learn about the culture and history of the Malay people.

Bintan Island Heritage Museum

The museum features an impressive collection of artefacts that tell the story of Bintan Island’s rich history.

Penyengat Island

Penyengat Island is an island located off the coast of Tanjung Pinang. It is home to several historical sites, including the Royal Mosque and the Istana Raja Haji Fisabilillah palace.

Natural Attractions of Tanjung Pinang: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Trikora Beach

One of the most popular beaches in Tanjung Pinang, Trikora Beach offers crystal clear blue waters and powdery white sand.

Gunung Bintan

Gunung Bintan is the highest peak on Bintan Island. Visitors can enjoy hiking or biking to the peak while taking in the island’s natural beauty.

Senggarang Island

Senggarang Island is known for its stunning views and natural beauty. Visitors can enjoy kayaking, snorkelling, and diving in the island’s crystal clear waters.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Tanjung Pinang

Bintan Island offers some of the best hiking and biking routes in Indonesia. Visitors can explore the island’s jungle, rice paddies, and hills by foot or by bike.

Best Things to Do in Tanjung Pinang: Activities and Tours

Indonesian Cultural Village

The Indonesian Cultural Village is a great way to learn about the country’s rich culture and history. Visitors can enjoy traditional dance performances, art exhibitions, and cultural workshops.

Mangrove Forest Tour

Explore Tanjung Pinang’s natural beauty by taking a mangrove forest tour. Visitors can spot local flora and fauna, including monkeys, birds, and more.

Island Hopping

Explore the beautiful islands around Tanjung Pinang on a day trip. Visitors can enjoy snorkelling, diving, and relaxing on the pristine beaches.

Week-Long Itinerary for Tanjung Pinang

Day 1: Visit the Rumah Bakar Museum and the Bintan Island Heritage Museum

Day 2: Explore Penyengat Island and its historical sites

Day 3: Hike or bike to Gunung Bintan

Day 4: Relax on Trikora Beach

Day 5: Take a mangrove forest tour

Day 6: Explore the nearby islands on a day trip

Day 7: Shop for souvenirs at the local markets

Food and Drink Scene of Tanjung Pinang

Must-Try Dishes

Otak-Otak

A popular snack made with fish paste and spices wrapped in banana leaves and grilled.

Soto

A delicious soup made with chicken or beef broth, rice, and vegetables.

Rendang

A spicy beef dish made with coconut milk and a combination of spices.

Local Beverages

Teh Botol

A popular Indonesian drink made with black tea and sweetened condensed milk.

Kopi Luwak

One of the most expensive coffees in the world, kopi luwak is made from coffee beans that have been eaten and excreted by civet cats.

Where to Stay in Tanjung Pinang: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Tanjung Pinang offers a wide variety of accommodation options to suit every budget. From luxury hotels to budget hostels, visitors can easily find an option that fits their needs.

What to Buy in Tanjung Pinang: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Tanjung Pinang is known for its handicrafts, including traditional batik fabrics, woven baskets, and wooden carvings. Visitors can also shop for local snacks, including dried fish and crackers.

Local Culture and Customs of Tanjung Pinang

When visiting Tanjung Pinang, it’s important to respect local customs and traditions. Visitors should dress modestly when visiting temples and mosques, and avoid public displays of affection.

Best Time to Visit Tanjung Pinang

The best time to visit Tanjung Pinang is between April and October, when the weather is generally dry and sunny.

Do’s and Don’ts of Tanjung Pinang

Do:

  • Respect the local customs and traditions
  • Try the local food and drinks
  • Bargain at the local markets

Don’t:

  • Wear revealing clothing when visiting temples and mosques
  • Litter on the streets or beaches
  • Disrespect the local culture

Safety and Health

Safety in Tanjung Pinang

Tanjung Pinang is a safe city, but visitors should be cautious when walking alone at night and keep their valuables safe at all times.

Health in Tanjung Pinang

Visitors should drink bottled water and avoid drinking tap water. They should also apply insect repellent to avoid mosquito bites and other insect-borne illnesses.

Getting to Tanjung Pinang: Transportation Options and Logistics

Visitors can fly into the Raja Haji Fisabilillah International Airport in Tanjung Pinang or take a ferry from Singapore or other nearby destinations.

Visitors can get around Tanjung Pinang by taking a bus, taxi, or renting a motorbike.

Where to Go Next from Tanjung Pinang: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Visitors can explore other parts of Bintan Island or take a short ferry ride to nearby destinations, including Batam and Singapore.

Conclusion

Tanjung Pinang is a hidden gem in Indonesia, offering visitors the perfect combination of history, culture, and natural beauty. Plan your trip today and discover all that this beautiful city has to offer.
