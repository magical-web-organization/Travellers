Introduction to Tanjung Uban

Tanjung Uban is a charming town located on the northern coast of Bintan Island in the Riau Islands of Indonesia. The town is known for its laid-back atmosphere, pristine beaches, and fascinating culture. It has many beautiful natural attractions, including lush forests, mangrove swamps, and crystal-clear waters. Tanjung Uban is an ideal destination for anyone looking for a relaxing vacation away from the hustle and bustle of city life.

List of Places to Visit in Tanjung Uban

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Museum Bahari Tanjungpinang: A museum that showcases the maritime history of the Riau Islands. The museum displays traditional boats, fishing equipment, and other objects related to the sea.
  • Bintan Island Heritage Site: An archaeological site that dates back to the 16th century. It consists of ancient tombs, temples, and ruins that reflect the rich cultural heritage of the island.
  • Raja Haji Fisabilillah Monument: A monument that honors the brave warrior who fought against the Dutch colonizers. The monument depicts his heroic struggle for independence and serves as a reminder of the country’s fight against colonization.

Natural Attractions of Tanjung Uban: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Pulau Marawang Island: A small island that hosts breathtaking panoramic views of the crystal clear waters. The island is perfect for a relaxed day trip or snorkeling around the coral reefs.
  • Gunung Bintan: The highest peak in Bintan island where you can see a stunning view of Tanjung Uban. You can hike the mountain and immerse yourself in the tranquil nature while enjoying the serenity.
  • Penyengat Island: An island known for its unique architecture and heritage site, including the royal graveyard, sultan’s palace, and Masjid Raya Sultan Riau. Visitors can take a stroll around the island and take in the captivating beauty of the landscape.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Tanjung Uban

  • Bukit Bintan: A hill with hiking and biking trails that offer stunning views of the surrounding landscape. The hill trail is beginner-friendly, but your hiking gear is important.
  • Penyengat Island: A small island with a network of pathways that allow visitors to explore the island and discover historical sites and beautiful scenery.
  • Lagoi Bay: A beachfront area that offers a range of outdoor activities, including hiking and biking trails through the lush forest.

Best Things to Do in Tanjung Uban: Activities and Tours

  • Island Hopping: Take a tour to other islands around Tanjung Uban. Take guided tours or rent boats to roam around the islands and unleash the calmness, and have fun all-day sea adventure.
  • Dolphin Watching: Watch beautiful dolphins swim in the clear waters of Tanjung Uban. Boats have licensed tourism guides who will help you navigate and enjoy dolphin sightings.
  • Water Adventure Sports: Kayaking, canoeing, paddleboarding, and banana boat rides can make your vacation exciting.

Food and Drink Scene of Tanjung Uban

Must-Try Dishes

  • Asam Pedas: A spicy and sour fish soup that uses ingredients like tamarind, tomato, chili pepper, and shrimp paste for flavor.
  • Sate Lilit: A Balinese chicken or fish skewer that is grilled over charcoal and served with a peanut sauce.
  • Nasi Goreng: A traditional Indonesian stir-fried rice dish that is often served with vegetables, egg, and chicken or shrimp.

Local Beverages

  • Teh Botol: A sweet and refreshing bottled iced tea beverage that is popular throughout Indonesia.
  • Cendol: A sweet and creamy coconut milk-based drink that contains traditional food in Indonesia like fruits and rice.
  • Es Kelapa Muda: A popular Indonesian drink made with coconut water and ice.

Where to Stay in Tanjung Uban: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Bintan Island Resorts: A luxurious resort with stunning views in Lagoi Bay that offers world-class facilities and various land and sea activities.
  • Cassia Bintan: A perfect family-friendly destination with spacious, modern rooms and apartments in stylish and comfortable settings.
  • Chill Cove at Treasure Bay Bintan: A budget-friendly hideaway that offers a waterfront experience with an affordable price tag.

What to Buy in Tanjung Uban: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Lampu Hias: Exquisite handmade colorful lamps made by the local people out of the seashells, giving you a flashy colorful vibe.
  • Chips and Snacks: Get the taste of sweetness and saltiness by buying Banana chips, jackfruit chips, and “kerupuk udang” (Shrimp crackers).
  • Batik Cloth/Clothes: The traditional textile art cloth is a perfect souvenir, family-gifts with unique cultural heritage, and beauty. Find a stunning array of colors and shapes.

Local Culture and Customs of Tanjung Uban

  • Respectfully Greeting: Greeting the elders and the people at a higher rank to show respect when visiting notable sites and important events.
  • Religion: Tanjung Uban largely follows the Islam religion, and it is advised to dress modestly and avoid public displays of affection.
  • Silaturahmi: A culture of exchanging pleasantries and special gifts between Indonesians to maintain good relationships.

Best Time to Visit Tanjung Uban

The best time to visit Tanjung Uban is from May to August when the weather is dry, and temperature ranges from 30-35° C. The rainy season falls from November to January, which reduces the outdoor activities prices. It is also a pleasant time to visit if you’re a budget traveler.

Do’s and Don’ts of Tanjung Uban

Do:

  • Respect Local Culture: Learn about the local traditions and customs and adhere to them.
  • Be Adventurous: This small town has a lot of natural beauty to explore. Plan outdoor activities and tours to experience the charm better.
  • Practice Environmental Awareness: Clean up the trash after activities, use refill-bottles, support eco-friendly initiatives.

Don’t:

  • Littering: Avoid littering in the area and rivers or oceans. Have the consciousness to follow proper waste disposal.
  • Harassment: Being respectful and courteous is important in Tanjung Uban. Do not harass, and respect people as you would like to receive to avoid misunderstandings and culture shock.
  • Speeding: Stick to the designated speed limit when driving, to avoid fines, accidents and contributing to general pollution.

Safety and Health

Safety in Tanjung Uban

Tanjung Uban is a generally peaceful town, but it is advised to stay aware of your surroundings, avoid walking alone in isolated areas, and beware of pickpockets, especially in public places.

Health in Tanjung Uban

Visitors traveling to Tanjung Uban are advised to take the necessary precautions, including getting vaccinated for hepatitis A and Typhoid fever. Drinking bottled water is recommended, and it is best to avoid drinking tap water.

Getting to Tanjung Uban: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Tanjung Uban

  • Raja Haji Fisabilillah International Airport (TNJ), Tanjung Pinang, Bintan is the nearest airport and just one hour away from Tanjung Uban.

Bus Stations Near Tanjung Uban

  • Sri Bintan Pura Ferry Terminal, just a few minutes from Tanjung Uban, and the terminal is well connected with other popular islands such as Batam Island and Singapore.

Other Options

Visitors can also take ferries from other Indonesian cities in the Sumatra region such as Batam, Batu Ampar, and Punggur to Bintan Island.

How to Get Around in Tanjung Uban

The best way to get around Tanjung Uban is by renting a car or hiring a taxi. Visitors can also rent a motorcycle and bicycle to explore the island on their own. Boats and public transportation are limited.

Where to Go Next from Tanjung Uban: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Mangrove Forest of Sebung River: A stunning spot for nature-lovers where you can explore the mangrove forests and take riverboat rides through the serene waters.
  • Batam Island: A neighboring island that offers shopping, nightlife, and entertainment.
  • Singapore: A world-renowned destination 45 minutes away by ferry and offers iconic landmarks, shopping, and cuisine.

Conclusion

Tanjung Uban is a small town with a lot of natural charm, contoured by the rich maritime history of the Riau Islands. The town has a lot to offer in terms of adventure, sightseeing, and the chance to immerse yourself in Indonesia’s culture and lifestyle. With this comprehensive travel guide, visitors can plan and make the most of their stay with practical tips for transportation, food, and unique experiences. Come and discover Tanjung Uban, a true gem of Indonesia, waiting for people to reveal its magnificence.
