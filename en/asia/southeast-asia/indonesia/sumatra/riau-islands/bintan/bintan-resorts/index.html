Introduction to Bintan Resorts

Nestled in the Riau Islands of Sumatra, Indonesia, Bintan Resorts is a stunning destination that offers endless opportunities for adventure, exploration, and relaxation. This beautiful island is known for its pristine beaches, crystal-clear waters, lush rainforests, and rich cultural heritage. Bintan Resorts is an oasis of calm and tranquility in an otherwise bustling Southeast Asian region.

In this comprehensive tour guide, we will introduce you to the top places to visit, the best things to do, where to stay and eat, and helpful tips for planning your trip to Bintan Resorts.

List of Places to Visit in Bintan Resorts

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Bintan Resorts is steeped in history and culture, and there are several museums and historical places that you must visit during your trip. Here are some of the top attractions:

  • Penyengat Island: This island is home to several historic sites, including the Sultan Riau Mosque and the royal tombs of the Riau Sultanate.
  • Banyan Tree Temple: This ancient temple is a beautiful example of Chinese architectural style and is situated in a lush tropical garden.
  • Tanah Lot Temple: This magnificent temple is situated on a rocky outcropping overlooking the sea and is an excellent spot to enjoy a beautiful sunset.

Natural Attractions of Bintan Resorts: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Bintan Resorts is known for its stunning natural beauty and offers endless opportunities for outdoor adventures and scenic views. Here are some of the top natural attractions you must visit:

  • Treasure Bay Bintan: This resort offers a wide range of water sports activities, including jet skiing, parasailing, and banana boat rides.
  • Mt. Bintan: This mountain offers breathtaking views of the island and is perfect for a day of hiking or biking.
  • Lagoi Bay: This area is home to several beautiful beaches that offer excellent swimming, sunbathing, and snorkeling.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Bintan Resorts

Bintan Resorts is a hiker’s and biker’s paradise, with several beautiful trails that wind through lush rainforests and rolling hills. Here are some of the top hiking and biking routes:

  • Nature Trail: This trail is a 3-kilometer loop that winds through the rainforest and offers stunning views of the surrounding hills and valleys.
  • Lagoi Bay Bike Trail: This trail is a 7-kilometer loop that follows the coastline and offers beautiful views of the ocean and nearby islands.
  • Jalan Pantai Pasir Panjang Trail: This trail follows the shoreline and offers excellent views of the beach and the surrounding landscape.

Best Things to Do in Bintan Resorts: Activities and Tours

In addition to hiking, biking, and water sports, Bintan Resorts offers a wide range of activities and tours that you must experience during your trip. Here are some of the top things to do in Bintan Resorts:

  • Golfing: Bintan Resorts is home to several world-class golf courses, including the Ria Bintan Golf Club and the Bintan Lagoon Golf Course.
  • Spa Day: Bintan Resorts is known for its luxurious spa resorts, and a spa day is a must-do activity while you’re here.
  • Mangrove Tour: This tour takes you on a scenic boat ride through the mangroves and offers a glimpse of the local wildlife, including colorful birds and playful monkeys.

Food and Drink Scene of Bintan Resorts

Bintan Resorts is a foodie’s paradise and offers a wide range of delicious local and international cuisine. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Nasi Padang: This is a traditional Indonesian dish that features steamed rice served with a variety of side dishes, such as curried beef, fried fish, and spicy chili paste.
  • Gado-gado: This is a salad that features boiled vegetables, tofu, tempeh, and boiled eggs, topped with a peanut sauce dressing.
  • Laksa: This is a spicy noodle soup that is popular in Southeast Asia and features a rich, flavorful broth and a variety of toppings, such as shrimp, chicken, or tofu.

Local Beverages

  • Teh Tarik: This is a sweet, milky tea that is popular throughout Southeast Asia and is made by pulling the tea between two cups to create a frothy layer on top.
  • Bintang Beer: This is a popular Indonesian beer that is light and refreshing and pairs well with spicy dishes.
  • Milo Dinosaur: This is a sweet, chocolatey drink that features Milo powder and milk, topped with a generous serving of Milo powder on top.

Where to Stay in Bintan Resorts: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Bintan Resorts offers a wide range of accommodation options, from luxurious beachfront villas to budget-friendly hostels. Here are some top recommendations:

  • Banyan Tree Bintan: This resort features private villas with their own pools and stunning views of the ocean.
  • Nirwana Gardens: This resort offers a range of accommodation options, from basic hotel rooms to private villas with butler service.
  • Mayang Sari Beach Resort: This hotel is located on a white sandy beach and offers comfortable, affordable rooms with beautiful views.

What to Buy in Bintan Resorts: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Bintan Resorts offers a wide range of souvenirs and local products that you can bring home to remember your trip. Here are some top recommendations:

  • Batik Fabric: This is a traditional Indonesian fabric that features intricate patterns and designs and makes an excellent souvenir or gift.
  • Coffee: Bintan Resorts is known for its excellent coffee, and you can buy bags of locally grown beans to bring home.
  • Handmade Crafts: You can find a wide range of handmade crafts, such as pottery, wood carvings, and woven baskets, at local markets and shops.

Local Culture and Customs of Bintan Resorts

While Bintan Resorts is a modern and cosmopolitan destination, it is important to respect the local culture and customs. Here are some tips to keep in mind:

  • Dress modestly when visiting religious sites or attending cultural events.
  • Always remove your shoes before entering homes, temples, or mosques.
  • Don’t touch someone’s head or point your feet at them, as these are considered disrespectful gestures.

Best Time to Visit Bintan Resorts

The best time to visit Bintan Resorts is from May to September, when the weather is dry and sunny. The monsoon season runs from October to April and brings heavy rains and strong winds, which can disrupt outdoor activities.

Do’s and Don’ts of Bintan Resorts

Do:

  • Respect the local culture and customs.
  • Pack sunscreen, insect repellent, and comfortable shoes.
  • Stay hydrated and drink plenty of water.

Don’t:

  • Engage in public displays of affection, as this is considered inappropriate.
  • Walk in the streets after dark, as the lighting can be poor and crime can occur.
  • Swim in areas that are marked with warning signs.

Safety and Health

Safety in Bintan Resorts

Bintan Resorts is a safe destination for travelers, but it is always important to exercise caution and take necessary precautions. Here are some safety tips:

  • Keep your valuables in a safe or secure location when not in use.
  • Always use a reputable taxi service or pre-book transportation through your hotel.
  • Be aware of your surroundings and avoid carrying large sums of cash or valuable items.

Health in Bintan Resorts

Bintan Resorts is a relatively healthy destination, but travelers should take necessary precautions to avoid getting sick. Here are some health tips:

  • Drink only bottled water and avoid drinking tap water.
  • Use insect repellent to protect against mosquito-borne illnesses like dengue fever.
  • Practice good hand hygiene and wash your hands frequently.

Getting to Bintan Resorts: Transportation Options and Logistics

Bintan Resorts is accessible by air, sea, and land and can be reached from several major cities in Southeast Asia. Here are some transportation options:

Airports Near Bintan Resorts

The closest airport to Bintan Resorts is Raja Haji Fisabilillah Airport in Tanjung Pinang, which is served by several domestic airlines. You can also fly into Singapore Changi Airport, which is a two-hour ferry ride away from Bintan Resorts.

Bus Stations Near Bintan Resorts

There are several bus stations in Tanjung Pinang, which connect the city to major cities in Sumatra and other islands in Indonesia.

Other Options

You can also reach Bintan Resorts by ferry from Singapore or Malaysia. There are several ferry operators that run daily services to and from Bintan Resorts.

How to Get Around in Bintan Resorts

The best way to get around Bintan Resorts is by taxi, which is readily available and relatively inexpensive. You can also rent a car or motorbike if you prefer to explore the island on your own.

Where to Go Next from Bintan Resorts: Nearby Destinations and Excursions
