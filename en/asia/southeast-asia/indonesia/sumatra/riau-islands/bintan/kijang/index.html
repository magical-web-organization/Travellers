Introduction to Kijang

Kijang is a secluded getaway located in Bintan, Riau Islands, Sumatra, Indonesia. Named after the Kijang deer that roams the area, Kijang is nestled in the midst of lush tropical forests, sparkling beaches, and breathtaking landscapes. Characterized by warm hospitality, and an unspoiled natural environment, Kijang offers an immersive Indonesian cultural experience. Whether you are a hiker, a biker, a foodie, or history buff, Kijang has something for everyone. In this comprehensive travel guide, we will showcase the best attractions, accommodations, food and drink scene, local culture and customs, safety and health, and transportation options.

List of Places to Visit in Kijang

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Preserving the rich history of the area, Kijang has several museums and historical places that shouldn’t be missed:

Penyengat Island

Home to the royal family of the Johor-Riau Sultanate in the 19th century, Penyengat Island is steeped in history. Visitors can explore the ruins of the palace, tomb of the sultan, and visit the Masjid Raya Sultan Riau, the oldest mosque in Riau.

Trikora Monument

The Trikora Monument, situated in the nearby town of Tanjungpinang, honors the bold and inspiring Indonesian independence hero Bung Tomo. Visitors can view the impressive statue, as well as a recreation of his bunker that was used during the struggle for independence.

Museum Bahari

Museum Bahari is dedicated to the maritime history of Indonesia, with an impressive collection of ship models and artifacts. Visitors can learn about Indonesia’s seafaring culture and how it influenced its history and development.

Natural Attractions of Kijang: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Kijang has an abundance of natural wonders, including stunning cliffs, unspoiled beaches, and crystal clear waters. Here are some of the must-see natural attractions in Kijang:

Pantai Trikora

Pantai Trikora is a three-kilometer stretch of white sandy beaches, crystal clear waters, and lush greenery. Visitors can explore the beach, take a dip in the ocean, and watch the beautiful sunset.

Banyan Tree Beach

Banyan Tree Beach, located in Tanjungpinang, is a hidden gem with a serene atmosphere and calm waters. The beach is surrounded by lush mangrove forests and is the perfect spot for a picnic or a day of swimming and relaxation.

Gunung Bintan

Gunung Bintan is an active volcano that towers over Kijang, offering stunning panoramic views of the surrounding landscape. Visitors can hike up the volcano and explore the paths that run through its dense forest.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Kijang

Kijang is a nature lover’s paradise, with plenty of hiking and biking opportunities. Some of the best routes include:

Gunung Bintan Trek

The Gunung Bintan Trek is a challenging climb to the top of the volcano. The trail features steep inclines and rugged terrain, but the payoff is worth it for the stunning views.

Banyan Tree Beach Hike

This hiking trail follows the coastline of Banyan Tree Beach and takes visitors through mangrove forests and along the beach.

Kijang Coastal Bike Path

This bike path runs along the coastal route, offering breathtaking views of the sea and coastline. It’s perfect for leisurely biking and exploring the surrounding area.

Best Things to Do in Kijang: Activities and Tours

Kijang offers a range of activities and tours to suit any interest. Here are some of the best things to do in Kijang:

Batam Island Day Trip

Visitors can take a day trip to Batam Island, located just a short boat ride away from Kijang. On the island, visitors can explore the city, try local cuisine, and shop for souvenirs.

Sea Kayaking

Sea kayaking is a fun and adventurous way to explore the coastline of Kijang. Visitors can rent kayaks and explore the coastal waters at their own pace.

Cooking Classes

Cooking classes are a great way to learn about Indonesian cuisine and culture. Visitors can learn how to make traditional Indonesian dishes, including nasi goreng and gado-gado.

Food and Drink Scene of Kijang

Indonesian cuisine is known for its bold flavors and unique combinations of herbs and spices. In Kijang, visitors can sample some of the region’s best dishes and beverages.

Must-Try Dishes

Nasi Goreng

Nasi Goreng is a popular Indonesian dish of fried rice, mixed with vegetables, and often served with a fried egg on top.

Gado-gado

Gado-gado is a vegetable salad, featuring boiled potatoes and assorted vegetables, covered in a peanut sauce.

Ayam Bakar

Ayam Bakar is a grilled chicken dish, marinated in a sauce made from chili, garlic, and other spices.

Local Beverages

Teh Botol

Teh Botol is a sweetened tea drink, sold in a bottle. It is a refreshing and popular drink in Indonesia.

Bintang Beer

Bintang Beer is a popular Indonesian beer, brewed locally and known for its crisp and light taste.

Where to Stay in Kijang: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Kijang offers a range of accommodations to fit any budget. Here are some of the best options:

Bintan Lagoon Resort

Bintan Lagoon Resort offers luxurious beachfront accommodations, world-class dining, and a range of recreational activities.

Riau Beachfront Resort

Riau Beachfront Resort provides beachfront bungalows with scenic views and is perfect for a relaxing getaway.

Grand Lagoi Hotel

Grand Lagoi Hotel offers modern, stylish rooms with stunning sea views, as well as a range of dining and recreational activities.

What to Buy in Kijang: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Kijang offers a range of local products to bring home, including:

Handicrafts

Visitors can find handmade bracelets, baskets, and other intricate handicrafts made by locals.

Batik Clothing

Batik clothing is a traditional Indonesian garment featuring intricate designs. Visitors can purchase Batik clothing in a range of styles and colors.

Local Culture and Customs of Kijang

Indonesia is known for its warm hospitality, and Kijang is no exception. It’s important to respect the local culture and customs, which include:

Dress Modestly

Indonesians tend to dress modestly, so visitors should avoid revealing clothing.

Take Off Your Shoes

When entering a home or a mosque, it’s customary to take off your shoes and leave them outside.

Best Time to Visit Kijang

The best time to visit Kijang is from May to September, when the weather is warm, and rainfall is low.

Do’s and Don’ts of Kijang

Do:

  • Respect the local culture and customs.
  • Try local cuisine and beverages.
  • Be adventurous and explore the natural wonders of Kijang.

Don’t:

  • Wear revealing clothing in public places.
  • Litter or damage the natural environment.
  • Disrespect local customs or beliefs.

Safety and Health

Safety in Kijang

Kijang is a relatively safe destination, but visitors should exercise caution when traveling alone at night, and avoid carrying large amounts of cash or valuables.

Health in Kijang

Visitors should ensure that their vaccines are up to date before traveling to Kijang. It’s also important to drink bottled water and avoid ice cubes in drinks to avoid stomach upset.

Getting to Kijang: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Kijang

The nearest airport to Kijang is Raja Haji Fisabilillah International Airport in Tanjungpinang.

Bus Stations Near Kijang

There are several bus stations near Kijang, including Batam Center Ferry Terminal and Tanjungpinang Ferry Terminal.

Other Options

Visitors can also reach Kijang by ferry from Batam Island. There are several ferry companies that operate daily services.

How to Get Around in Kijang

Visitors to Kijang can get around by bike, taxi, or rental car. Biking is a popular option and is a great way to explore the natural scenery of the area.

Where to Go Next from Kijang: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Batam Island

Visitors can take a short ferry ride from Kijang to Batam Island, where they can explore the vibrant city and indulge in some shopping.

Pulau Penyengat

Pulau Penyengat is an island located near Kijang and is home to historic ruins and a beautiful mosque.

Conclusion

Kijang has something to offer for every type of traveler. From outdoor adventures and natural wonders to cultural sites and delicious cuisine, Kijang has it all. Whether you are looking for a tropical getaway or an immersive cultural experience, Kijang is the perfect destination.
