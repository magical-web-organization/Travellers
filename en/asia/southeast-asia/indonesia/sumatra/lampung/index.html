Introduction to Lampung

Located in the southernmost part of Sumatra Island, Lampung Province offers a perfect blend of cultural and natural attractions. Known for its pristine beaches, historical sites, and lush forests, Lampung is a must-visit destination for travelers seeking an off-the-beaten-path experience in Indonesia.

List of Places to Visit in Lampung

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Mount Krakatoa Museum: Visit this museum to learn about the infamous 1883 eruption of Mount Krakatoa.
  • Balaputradeva Museum: Explore the history of Lampung at this museum, which houses artifacts and exhibits related to the region’s past.
  • Tirtayasa Water Castle: This historical site was once an important palace for the Sultan of Lampung.

Natural Attractions of Lampung: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Way Kambas National Park: Known for its elephant conservation program, this national park is also home to various species of birds, primates, and reptiles.
  • Kiluan Bay: Take a boat tour to see dolphins and enjoy the stunning views of the bay.
  • Puncak Mas Tea Plantation: Experience the beauty of Lampung’s countryside with a visit to this tea plantation, where you can learn about the tea-making process and enjoy a cup of local tea.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Lampung

  • Tanggamus Mountain: Hike to the top of this mountain for panoramic views of Lampung’s beautiful landscape.
  • Way Kambas Elephant Training Center: Take a guided tour of the elephant training center and bike through the surrounding area.

Best Things to Do in Lampung: Activities and Tours

  • Surfing: Enjoy some of Indonesia’s best waves at beaches like Krui and Balo.
  • Snorkeling and Diving: Explore the underwater world at beaches like Pahawang, Tanjung Setia, and Sebuku Island.
  • Traditional Weaving Workshops: Learn about Lampung’s traditional weaving techniques and create your own souvenir to take home.

Week-Long Itinerary for Lampung

Day Activities
1 Visit Mount Krakatoa Museum and Balaputradeva Museum
2 Explore Tirtayasa Water Castle and hike to Tanggamus Mountain
3 Take a boat tour of Kiluan Bay and visit Puncak Mas Tea Plantation

7 Relax at Lampung’s beautiful beaches and enjoy the local cuisine

Food and Drink Scene of Lampung

Must-Try Dishes

  • Gulai Ikan Kepala: A spicy fish head curry dish, usually made with freshwater fish.
  • Empek-Empek: A deep-fried fishcake served with sweet and sour sauce.
  • Sate Clydon: Skewered chicken or beef served with spicy peanut sauce.

Local Beverages

  • Kopi Baginda: Lampung’s famous coffee, known for its strong and rich flavor.
  • Es Kelapa Muda: A refreshing drink made with young coconut water and meat.
  • Gula Aren Juice: A sweet drink made from palm sugar.

Where to Stay in Lampung: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Novotel Lampung: A 4-star hotel in the heart of Bandar Lampung, offering comfortable rooms and a rooftop bar with stunning views.
  • Liberty Hotel: A budget-friendly option in Bandar Lampung, with modern amenities and a central location.
  • Krui Surf Camp: A beachfront hostel in Krui, perfect for surfers and those looking for a laid-back atmosphere.

What to Buy in Lampung: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Lampung Traditional Fabrics: Handwoven fabrics with intricate designs, often used for clothing and home decor.
  • Krakatoa Souvenirs: Keepsakes related to Mount Krakatoa, including t-shirts and keychains.
  • Lampung Coffee: Bring home a bag of Lampung’s famous coffee, freshly roasted and packed.

Local Culture and Customs of Lampung

  • Traditional Dance: Lampung is known for its traditional dances, which often involve graceful movements and colorful costumes.
  • Wedding Ceremony: Lampung’s wedding ceremonies are elaborate affairs, with pre-wedding ceremonies lasting up to a week.
  • Rambu Solo Ceremony: A traditional funeral ceremony unique to Lampung, involving a series of rituals and preparations.

Best Time to Visit Lampung

The best time to visit Lampung is between April and September, when the weather is dry and sunny. However, keep in mind that the peak tourist season is in July and August, so be prepared for crowds and higher prices.

Do’s and Don’ts of Lampung

Do:

  • Respect Local Customs: Familiarize yourself with the local culture and customs, and be respectful of them.
  • Try Local Food: Lampung has a vibrant food scene, so be sure to sample local dishes and street food.
  • Engage with Locals: Strike up a conversation with locals to learn more about Lampung’s culture and way of life.

Don’t:

  • Disrespect Religious Sites: Lampung is home to several important religious sites, so be respectful when visiting them.
  • Litter: Help keep Lampung’s natural attractions beautiful by disposing of your trash properly.
  • Disrespect Local Traditions: Avoid making negative comments about local traditions or customs.

Safety and Health

Safety in Lampung

Lampung is generally a safe destination for travelers. However, exercise caution when traveling alone at night and be aware of pickpocketing in crowded areas.

Health in Lampung

Make sure to pack any necessary medication and consult a doctor before traveling to Lampung. Mosquito-borne diseases such as dengue fever are a concern, so take precautions such as using insect repellent and wearing protective clothing.

Getting to Lampung: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Lampung

  • Radin Inten II International Airport: Located in Bandar Lampung, this airport serves domestic and international flights.

Bus Stations Near Lampung

  • Terminal Rajabasa: A major bus station in Bandar Lampung, with connections to Jakarta and other destinations in Sumatra.

Other Options

  • Ferry: Ferries operate between Merak in Java and Bakauheni in southern Sumatra, with frequent departures throughout the day.
  • Car Rental: Renting a car is a convenient option for exploring Lampung’s many attractions at your own pace.

How to Get Around in Lampung

  • Taxi: Taxis are available in Bandar Lampung and other major cities in Lampung.
  • Ojek: Motorcycle taxis are a popular option for short-distance travel.
  • Angkot: Angkot is a public transportation system consisting of modified minibuses or vans, often used for longer distances.
  • Car Rental: Renting a car is a good option for exploring Lampung’s rural areas and natural attractions.

Where to Go Next from Lampung: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Krakatoa Island: Take a boat tour to visit the site of the infamous 1883 eruption of Mount Krakatoa.
  • Bukit Barisan Selatan National Park: Explore this national park, which is home to endangered species such as Sumatran tigers and rhinos.
  • Bali: Fly to Bali to experience its beautiful beaches, vibrant culture, and famous cuisine.

Conclusion

Lampung offers a unique travel experience for those looking to explore Sumatra’s rich culture and natural beauty. Whether you’re interested in hiking, surfing, or simply relaxing on the beach, Lampung has something to offer for every type of traveler. With this comprehensive guide, you’ll be well-equipped to plan your dream trip to this beautiful and underrated destination in Indonesia.
