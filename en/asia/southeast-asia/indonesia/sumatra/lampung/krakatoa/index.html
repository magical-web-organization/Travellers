Introduction to Krakatoa

Krakatoa, also known as Krakatau, is a volcanic island located in the Sunda Strait between the Java and Sumatra islands of Indonesia, in Southeast Asia. Its eruption in 1883 was notorious for being one of the deadliest and most destructive volcanic eruptions in history. Today, Krakatoa is a popular destination for adventure-seekers and nature lovers, offering breathtaking views, outdoor activities, and rich cultural experiences.

List of Places to Visit in Krakatoa

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Krakatoa has a rich history and culture that is reflected in many of its museums and historical attractions. Here are some of the must-visit places:

  • Krakatau Museum: Located on the mainland of Sumatra, this museum displays artifacts related to the 1883 eruption of Krakatoa, as well as the geology and biodiversity of the surrounding region.

  • Anak Krakatoa: This volcanic island is the youngest and most active part of the Krakatoa volcano. Visitors can take a boat tour to see the volcano up close and witness its periodic eruptions.

  • Ujung Kulon National Park: This park on the western tip of Java Island is home to the last remaining wild population of the Javan rhinoceros, as well as other rare wildlife.

Natural Attractions of Krakatoa: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Krakatoa’s stunning landscapes and natural wonders make it a paradise for outdoor enthusiasts. From hiking to diving, here are some of the best natural attractions to explore:

  • Krakatau National Park: Established to protect the volcano and its surroundings, this park offers visitors the opportunity to observe the flora and fauna of the region, as well as to hike and camp on the island.

  • Snorkeling and Diving: The waters around Krakatoa are renowned for their biodiversity and crystal-clear visibility. Visitors can snorkel or dive in the area to discover a whole new underwater world.

  • Rakata Island: The largest island of the Krakatoa archipelago, Rakata Island offers visitors stunning views of the surrounding sea and the nearby Anak Krakatoa volcano.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Krakatoa

Krakatoa’s stunning landscapes and diverse terrain make it a great place for hiking and biking. Here are some of the most popular routes:

  • Krakatau Volcano Trail: This challenging hike takes visitors to the top of the volcano, offering breathtaking views of the surrounding sea and islands.

  • Circling the Island: This route takes visitors around the entire Krakatoa island, offering a diverse range of terrains and landscapes to explore.

Best Things to Do in Krakatoa: Activities and Tours

Aside from hiking, biking, and exploring the island’s natural and cultural attractions, Krakatoa offers visitors a range of activities and tours to make the most of their trip. Here are some of the best things to do:

  • Boat Tours: Various operators offer boat tours of the region, taking visitors to the nearby islands, waterfalls, and other scenic spots.

  • Fishing: The waters surrounding Krakatoa are filled with fish, making it a great destination for fishing enthusiasts.

  • Camping: Visitors can camp on the island and enjoy the natural beauty of Krakatoa in the evening and night.

Week-Long Itinerary for Krakatoa

For those looking to spend a week in Krakatoa, here’s a sample itinerary:

Day Activities
1 Arrive in Jakarta, travel to Lampung
2 Visit Krakatau Museum and hike around the mainland
3 Take a boat tour to Anak Krakatoa and enjoy snorkeling
4 Explore Ujung Kulon National Park
5 Hike to the top of Krakatau volcano
6 Take a boat tour of the surrounding islands
7 Relax on the beach and depart to Jakarta

Food and Drink Scene of Krakatoa

Krakatoa’s food scene is a mix of traditional Indonesian cuisine and seafood dishes that reflect the island’s coastal location. Here are some of the must-try dishes and beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Gado-gado: A traditional Indonesian salad made from boiled vegetables, tofu, and tempeh, served with a peanut sauce.

  • Sate: Grilled meat skewers served with a spicy peanut sauce and rice cakes.

  • Seafood: The waters around Krakatoa are filled with seafood, including fish, shrimp, and crabs, which are prepared using various local recipes.

Local Beverages

  • Tehbotol Sosro: A popular bottled tea drink that is sweetened with sugar.

  • Kopi Luwak: A famously expensive coffee that is made using beans that have been eaten and digested by Asian palm civets.

Where to Stay in Krakatoa: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Krakatoa offers a range of accommodations to suit different budgets and preferences. Here are some of the best options:

  • Krakatau Nirwana Resort: A luxury resort located on the mainland that offers stunning views of Krakatoa.

  • Krakatoa Eco Resort: An eco-friendly resort located on the Krakatoa island that offers guests a chance to live in harmony with nature.

  • Guesthouses and Homestays: There are several guesthouses and homestays on the island that offer affordable accommodation with local families.

What to Buy in Krakatoa: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Visitors to Krakatoa can take home some unique souvenirs and local products that reflect the island’s culture and natural beauty. Here are some of the best options:

  • Krakatau Volcano Rocks: Rocks that have been ejected from Krakatoa’s volcanic eruptions can be found on the island, and visitors can take some of them home as a unique souvenir.

  • Local Crafts: Local artisans create handmade crafts, such as baskets, bags, and clothing, that reflect the traditional culture of the region.

Local Culture and Customs of Krakatoa

Krakatoa has a rich cultural heritage that is deeply influenced by its location and its natural surroundings. Visitors should be respectful of local customs and traditions, such as covering up when visiting religious sites and avoiding littering on the island.

Best Time to Visit Krakatoa

The best time to visit Krakatoa is during the dry season, which runs from May to September. The weather is dry and sunny during this time, making it ideal for outdoor activities and sightseeing.

Do’s and Don’ts of Krakatoa

Do:

  • Respect local customs and traditions.

  • Bring sunscreen, insect repellent, and comfortable walking shoes.

  • Take proper precautions when hiking or exploring the island’s rugged terrain.

Don’t:

  • Litter on the island or in the sea.

  • Damage the natural surroundings or disturb the local wildlife.

  • Ignore warnings or advice from local authorities regarding safety.

Safety and Health

Safety in Krakatoa

While Krakatoa is generally a safe destination, visitors should take proper precautions when hiking or exploring the island’s natural attractions. Here are some tips:

  • Hire a guide or join a tour group when hiking or exploring the island’s rugged terrain.

  • Follow all instructions and warnings from local authorities regarding safety.

  • Be aware of the risk of volcanic eruptions and follow all evacuation orders if necessary.

Health in Krakatoa

Visitors to Krakatoa should take proper precautions to ensure their health and well-being during their trip. Here are some tips:

  • Drink bottled water or use a water purifier to avoid getting sick from contaminated water.

  • Apply insect repellent and wear protective clothing to avoid getting bitten by mosquitoes or other pests.

  • Pack any necessary medications or medical supplies, as pharmacies may be limited on the island.

Getting to Krakatoa: Transportation Options and Logistics

Krakatoa is located in the Sunda Strait between Sumatra and Java islands. Here are some transportation options and logistics:

Airports Near Krakatoa

  • Soekarno-Hatta International Airport, Jakarta: The nearest international airport that serves major airlines from around the world.

  • Radin Inten II Airport, Lampung: The nearest domestic airport that serves flights from major cities in Indonesia.

Bus Stations Near Krakatoa

  • Kalianda Bus Station: This bus station is located in the town of Kalianda in Lampung province.

Other Options

  • Boat: Visitors can reach the island of Krakatoa by taking a boat trip from various ports around Sunda Strait.

How to Get Around in Krakatoa

The best way to get around Krakatoa is by boat, as the island is relatively small and has rugged terrain. Visitors can also hike or bike around the island.

Where to Go Next from Krakatoa: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

If you have more time to explore the region, here are some nearby destinations and excursions that are worth visiting:

  • Bandar Lampung: The capital city of Lampung province, known for its colonial architecture and beaches.

  • Jakarta: The capital city of Indonesia, known for its vibrant culture, shopping, and cuisine.

  • Yogyakarta: A city on Java Island, known for its ancient temples and traditional arts and crafts.

Conclusion

Krakatoa is a unique and exciting destination that offers a range of natural and cultural attractions for visitors to explore. By following the tips and recommendations in this tour guide, you’ll be able to make the most of your trip to this beautiful part of Indonesia.
