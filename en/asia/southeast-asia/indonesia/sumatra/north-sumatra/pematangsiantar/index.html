Introduction to Pematangsiantar

Pematangsiantar is a bustling city located in the heart of North Sumatra. Known for its rich cultural heritage and beautiful landscapes, the city is a popular destination for tourists from all over the world. Pematangsiantar is home to friendly locals, delicious cuisine, and a vibrant art scene, making it an excellent destination for travelers seeking an authentic Sumatran experience.

In this comprehensive travel guide, we’ll cover everything you need to know to plan your perfect trip to Pematangsiantar, including where to stay, what to eat, and how to get around. Let’s get started!

List of Places to Visit in Pematangsiantar

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Pematangsiantar is steeped in history and culture. Visitors can immerse themselves in the city’s past by visiting its many museums and historical landmarks. Here are a few places you won’t want to miss:

  • Museum Simalungun: This museum displays the culture and history of the Simalungun people, an ethnic group that has lived in this region for centuries.
  • Gereja GKPB Sion: This historic church, built in 1899, is one of the oldest buildings in Pematangsiantar. It features beautiful stained glass windows and an impressive pipe organ.
  • Taman Huta Siallagan: This park is home to traditional Batak houses, showcasing the architecture and lifestyle of the Batak people. Visitors can tour the houses and learn about Batak culture and traditions.

Natural Attractions of Pematangsiantar: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Pematangsiantar is also known for its stunning natural beauty. From rolling hills to crystal-clear lakes, there’s no shortage of breathtaking landscapes to explore. Here are some of the must-visit natural attractions in the area:

  • Taman Wisata Iman (TWI): This park is popular among locals and tourists alike for its stunning views and tranquil atmosphere. Visitors can take a leisurely stroll through the gardens, enjoy a picnic, or climb the hill for panoramic views of the city.
  • Air Terjun Sipiso-Piso: This spectacular waterfall is located just outside of Pematangsiantar. It’s a popular spot for hiking and photography, offering great views of the surrounding mountains and forests.
  • Danau Toba: No trip to Pematangsiantar is complete without a visit to Lake Toba, the largest volcanic lake in the world. Visitors can take a boat tour of the lake, swim in its crystal-clear waters, or relax on its sandy beaches.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Pematangsiantar

Pematangsiantar is also an excellent destination for outdoor enthusiasts. Visitors can explore the area’s stunning natural landscapes by hiking or biking through its many trails. Here are some of our favorite routes:

  • Taman Wisata Iman (TWI): The park offers several hiking trails that wind through its gardens and up the hill for panoramic views of the city.
  • Bukit Marsudirini: This hill offers excellent hiking trails and stunning views of Lake Toba and the surrounding mountains.
  • Tea Plantation: This is an excellent place for biking, with paths that run through the rolling hills covered in verdant tea plants.

Best Things to Do in Pematangsiantar: Activities and Tours

In addition to its natural beauty and rich cultural heritage, Pematangsiantar offers plenty of activities and tours for visitors to enjoy. Here are some of the best things to do in the area:

  • Traditional Batak Dance Show: Visitors can enjoy an authentic Batak dance performance and learn about the culture and traditions of the Batak people.
  • Shopping in Pasar Pematang: This bustling market is a great place to shop for souvenirs and sample local cuisine. Visitors can also check out the many stalls selling clothes, handicrafts, and other goods.
  • Explore the Nightlife: Pematangsiantar has a lively nightlife scene, with plenty of bars and clubs to choose from. Visitors can enjoy live music, dancing, and karaoke until the early hours of the morning.

Week-Long Itinerary for Pematangsiantar

If you’re planning to spend a week in Pematangsiantar, here’s a sample itinerary to help you make the most of your time:

  • Day 1: Arrive in Pematangsiantar and check into your hotel. Spend the afternoon exploring the city center and trying some local cuisine.
  • Day 2: Visit Museum Simalungun and Gereja GKPB Sion in the morning. In the afternoon, head to TWI for a leisurely stroll through its gardens.
  • Day 3: Take a day trip to Lake Toba, where you can relax on the beach or take a boat tour of the lake.
  • Day 4: Spend the morning hiking Bukit Marsudirini and then head to Pasar Pematang for some shopping in the afternoon.
  • Day 5: Explore Pematangsiantar’s nightlife scene by visiting some of the city’s bars and clubs.
  • Day 6: Take a tour of the traditional Batak houses in Taman Huta Siallagan and then head to Air Terjun Sipiso-Piso for a scenic hike.
  • Day 7: Depart from Pematangsiantar.

Food and Drink Scene of Pematangsiantar

No trip to Pematangsiantar would be complete without sampling some of the local cuisine. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Babi Panggang Karo: This is a slow-roasted pork dish that’s marinated in spices and wrapped in banana leaves. It’s a staple of Karo cuisine, which is indigenous to the region.
  • Arsik: This is a fish dish that’s cooked with traditional Karo spices and herbs. It’s typically served with vegetables and rice.
  • Bolu Meranti: This is a traditional cake that’s made from rice flour and coconut cream. It’s a popular dessert in Pematangsiantar.

Local Beverages

  • Kopi Tubruk: This is a traditional Sumatran coffee that’s brewed with hot water and sugar. It’s typically served with a small, crunchy snack.
  • Tuak: This is a traditional Batak drink that’s made from fermented palm wine. It’s typically served in a bamboo cup and has a sweet, slightly alcoholic taste.

Where to Stay in Pematangsiantar: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Pematangsiantar offers a wide range of accommodation options to suit all budgets and preferences. Here are some of the best places to stay in the area:

  • Horison Sky Pematangsiantar: This is a luxury hotel located in the heart of the city. It features a rooftop pool, spa, and fitness center.
  • Bukit Lawang Cottage: This is a budget-friendly option located just outside of the city center. It’s a great choice for backpackers and budget-conscious travelers.
  • Samosir Villa Resort: This is a high-end resort located on Samosir Island, offering luxurious amenities and stunning lake views.

What to Buy in Pematangsiantar: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Pematangsiantar is known for its handicrafts and traditional arts. Visitors can find a wide range of souvenirs and local products to bring home, including clothing, jewelry, and handicrafts. Some of the most popular items include:

  • Ulos: This is a traditional cloth made from cotton or silk. It’s typically woven by hand and decorated with geometric patterns. It makes an excellent souvenir or gift.
  • Batak Jewelry: The Batak people are known for their intricate jewelry, which often features traditional motifs and symbols. Visitors can find a wide range of necklaces, bracelets, and earrings in the local markets.
  • Handwoven Bags: Pematangsiantar is also known for its handwoven bags, which are made from natural fibers and feature colorful patterns.

Local Culture and Customs of Pematangsiantar

Pematangsiantar is a deeply traditional city, and visitors should be respectful of local customs and practices. Here are a few things to keep in mind:

  • Dress Appropriately: Visitors should dress modestly, especially when visiting religious sites or traditional villages. Avoid wearing short shorts or revealing clothing.
  • Remove Your Shoes: It’s customary to remove your shoes before entering someone’s home or a religious site.
  • Respect Elders: The Batak people have a deep respect for their elders. Visitors should be polite and deferential when interacting with older people.

Best Time to Visit Pematangsiantar

The best time to visit Pematangsiantar is during the dry season, which runs from June to September. During this time, the weather is mild and pleasant, and there’s less rainfall. It’s also easier to explore the city’s natural attractions during the dry season.

Getting to Pematangsiantar: Transportation Options and Logistics

Pematangsiantar is located in North Sumatra, and there are several ways to get there. Here are some transportation options for getting to Pematangsiantar:

Airports Near Pematangsiantar

The closest airports to Pematangsiantar are:

  • Kualanamu International Airport: This airport is located in Medan, approximately 2 hours from Pematangsiantar by car.
  • Sibisa Airport: This is a small airport located in Pematangsiantar, offering domestic flights to Jakarta, Medan, and other Indonesian cities.

Bus Stations Near Pematangsiantar

Pematangsiantar is well-connected by bus to other major cities in Sumatra. Here are some bus stations near Pematangsiantar:

  • Terminal Amplas: This is the main bus terminal in Medan, offering services to Pematangsiantar and other cities in Sumatra.
  • Terminal Dolok Sanggul: This is a bus terminal within Pematangsiantar, offering services to other cities in the region.

Other Options

Visitors can also arrange for private transportation, such as a taxi or hired car, to get to Pematangsiantar from other cities in Sumatra.

How to Get Around in Pematangsiantar

Pematangsiantar is a relatively small city, and many of its attractions are within walking distance of each other. Visitors can also use public transportation or hire a car or motorcycle to get around. Here are some options:

  • Ojek: Ojeks are motorcycle taxis that are available throughout the city. Visitors can negotiate a fare with the driver.
  • Angkot: Angkots are small minibusses that are a popular form of public transportation in Pematangsiantar. They follow designated routes and are relatively cheap.
  • Car Rental: Visitors can rent a car or motorcycle to get around Pematangsiantar. Rental agencies are available throughout the city.
