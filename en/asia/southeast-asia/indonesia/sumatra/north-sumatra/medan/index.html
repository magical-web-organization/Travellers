Introduction to Medan

Medan is the capital of North Sumatra, Indonesia and is the largest city outside of Java. It is a bustling city with a rich cultural heritage, delicious cuisine, and scenic views. Despite not being as popular as other Indonesian destinations, such as Bali and Jakarta, Medan still offers a plethora of attractions and activities for travelers.

List of Places to Visit in Medan

Whether you’re interested in history, culture, or nature, Medan has something to offer for everyone. Here’s a list of the must-visit attractions in Medan:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  1. Maimun Palace - a beautiful palace built in the 19th century by the Sultan of Deli
  2. Tjong A Fie Mansion - a historic mansion built in the 1890s by a Chinese businessman
  3. Rahmat International Wildlife Museum and Gallery - a museum that houses an impressive collection of wildlife specimens from all over the world
  4. Great Mosque of Medan - one of the largest mosques in Indonesia and a prime example of Islamic architecture

Natural Attractions of Medan: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  1. Lake Toba - a picturesque lake known for its scenic viewpoints and water activities such as swimming, boating, and fishing
  2. Mount Sibayak - an active volcanic mountain that offers stunning panoramic views of the surrounding landscape and hiking opportunities
  3. Bukit Lawang - a natural reserve that allows visitors to view orangutans in their natural habitat
  4. Berastagi - a leisurely hill station known for its beautiful volcano and traditional markets

Hiking/Biking Routes of Medan

Medan is home to numerous hiking and biking trails, ranging from easy to moderate. Here are some of the best:

  1. Gunung Leuser National Park - a scenic park with numerous trails for hiking and trekking to explore the rainforest
  2. Sipiso-piso Waterfall - a moderate-to-difficult hiking trail that rewards visitors with stunning panoramic views of Medan and the surrounding landscape

Best Things to Do in Medan: Activities and Tours

  1. Visit the Snake River Temple - a historic temple that has been in existence for more than a century and is the perfect place to learn about the local culture.
  2. Take a culinary tour - a perfect way to experience the tasty local cuisine and food culture of Medan
  3. Attend a traditional dance performance - an excellent opportunity to witness the vibrant dance and music culture of Medan
  4. Explore the city’s night markets - a great way to sample different local foods and buy souvenirs

Week-Long Itinerary for Medan

Here’s a week-long itinerary that highlights the best of Medan:

  • Day 1: Visit the Great Mosque in Medan and explore the historic Maimun Palace.
  • Day 2: Take a day trip to Berastagi, a beautiful hill station that offers scenic views and a chance to explore the local markets.
  • Day 3: Hike to the Sipiso-piso waterfall and then visit Tjong A Fie Mansion.
  • Day 4: Explore the lush Gunung Leuser National Park and go trekking.
  • Day 5: Take a day trip to the picturesque Lake Toba and go swimming, boating, or fishing.
  • Day 6: Visit the Rahmat Museum and Gallery, followed by a culinary tour of the city.
  • Day 7: Depart from Medan or take a day trip to Bukit Lawang and explore the natural reserve.

Food and Drink Scene of Medan

Medan is famous for its diverse cuisine, blending traditional Indonesian flavors with Chinese and Indian influences. Here are some of the must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  1. Sate Padang - a savory meat dish with a spicy sauce, made from local spices and beef
  2. Nasi Padang - a hearty rice dish served with a variety of side dishes such as fried shrimp, egg, and chicken
  3. Bika Ambon - a sweet pancake-like dessert made with flour, eggs, and sugar
  4. Soto Medan - a traditional Indonesian soup made with chicken or beef, vegetables, and herbs.

Local Beverages

  1. Teh Tarik - a hot milk tea, pulled to create a frothy texture
  2. Bajigur - a sweet, hot beverage made from coconut milk, brown sugar, and ginger.

Where to Stay in Medan: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Medan offers a variety of accommodations, ranging from luxury hotels to budget-friendly guesthouses.

  1. Grand Aston City Hall Hotel & Serviced Residences - a luxurious hotel located in the heart of the city
  2. Garuda Plaza Hotel - a mid-range hotel located in the central business district
  3. OYO 354 Boston Inn Hotel - a budget-friendly option located in the city center.
  4. Una Hotel Medan - a comfortable and affordable hotel located in East Medan

What to Buy in Medan: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  1. Batik clothing - traditional Indonesian clothing decorated with unique patterns and designs
  2. Ulos cloth - a woven cloth from Sumatra that is known for its intricate patterns and traditional motifs
  3. Sarung - a versatile and comfortable piece of clothing that can be worn as a dress, skirt, or pants
  4. Batak knife - a traditional knife used by the Batak tribe of North Sumatra.

Local Culture and Customs of Medan

The official language of Medan is Bahasa Indonesia, but English is widely spoken. The locals are friendly and respectful of visitors’ privacy. Religion plays a crucial role in Medan’s culture, with Islam mosques and temples frequenting the scene.

Best Time to Visit Medan

The best time to visit Medan is between June and September, during the dry season. The temperature is mild and pleasant during this time, and there are fewer chances of rain.

Do’s and Don’ts of Medan

Here are some of the do’s and don’ts to keep in mind when visiting Medan:

Do:

  1. Dress modestly when visiting religious places.
  2. Carry cash when visiting local markets or street vendors.
  3. Try the local food and drink.
  4. Be respectful towards the locals and their customs.

Don’t:

  1. Wear revealing clothing in public.
  2. Touch someone’s head since this is considered a sign of disrespect.
  3. Take pictures without permission.
  4. Use your left hand to greet someone.

Safety and Health

Safety in Medan

Medan is generally a safe place to visit, but it’s always best to stay alert and be cautious of pickpockets. It’s also recommended to avoid walking alone at night and avoid some areas of the city where pickpockets are prevalent.

Health in Medan

Medan is a high-disease risk area. It’s essential to visit a doctor before traveling and take the necessary vaccinations. Drinking only bottled or boiled water is the best way to prevent water-borne illness.

Getting to Medan: Transportation Options and Logistics

Medan is well-connected to other parts of the world through its international airport, Kualanamu International Airport, located only 23km from the city center. Visitors can travel within the city through taxis, buses, or rented motorcycles.

Airports Near Medan

  1. Kualanamu International Airport (Medan)

Bus Stations Near Medan

  1. Amplas Bus Terminal (Medan)
  2. Pinang Baris Bus Terminal (Medan)

Other Options

  1. Taxis
  2. Bajaj (three-wheeled vehicles)
  3. Renting a motorcycle

How to Get Around in Medan

The best way to navigate Medan is by taxi or renting a motorcycle. It’s also possible to use public transportation, including buses, which are cheap but can be confusing for first-timers.

Where to Go Next from Medan: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

If you have additional time, here are some nearby destinations you should consider visiting:

  1. Aceh
  2. Lake Toba
  3. Tanjung Puting National Park
  4. Bukit Lawang

Conclusion

Medan is a unique and exciting destination for tourists interested in culture, history, nature, and delicious cuisine. With this comprehensive guide, you’re now better equipped to plan your trip to this beautiful Indonesian city. Remember to observe the local customs and always stay safe. Happy travels!
