Introduction to Pekanbaru

Pekanbaru is an Indonesian city located in the province of Riau, in Sumatra Island. It is one of the largest cities in Sumatra and offers a blend of traditional and modern attractions. Known as the “City of Love,” Pekanbaru presents a rich cultural experience, with its distinct Malay influences. The city’s development has been shaped by its past as a major trading hub and its current status as a busy commercial center. This comprehensive travel guide provides the ultimate guide to exploring the culture, attractions, cuisine, accommodations, and transportation options in Pekanbaru.

List of Places to Visit in Pekanbaru

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • An-Nur Grand Mosque – This is the most significant mosque in Pekanbaru, with its beautiful architecture, artwork, and large prayer space. Visitors are allowed to enter but must dress modestly.
  • Rumah Gadang Riau – The Rumah Gadang represents traditional Minangkabau architecture. It’s a type of traditional house found in West Sumatra. It houses a museum with various historical and cultural belongings.
  • Sultan Syarif Kasim II Museum – The museum, which is named after the last Sultan, houses a display of royal regalia, clothing, weapons, and other historical artifacts.
  • Taman Budaya Riau – This is a cultural and arts center that features traditional music, dance, and theater performances.

Natural Attractions of Pekanbaru: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Air Mertua Waterfall – A perfect destination for hikers and outdoor enthusiasts. This waterfall is situated in a dense forest and is about four meters tall.
  • Pelangi Waterfall – Another waterfall that offers a natural swimming pool, hiking trails, and scenic views that attract tourists from all over the world.
  • Tesso Nilo National Park – A protected forest that spreads across 38,000 hectares, making it one of the most significant lowland forest conservation areas in Indonesia.
  • Sungai Kampar Adventure – A great place for rafting activities, offering rapids of different grades of difficulty.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Pekanbaru

  • Rumbai Green Valley Park – A park that encompasses various trails that can cater to both leisure walkers and hikers alike.
  • Taman Alam Merbah – The park offers a beautiful landscape of hills, forests, and lakes for cycling enthusiasts.
  • Harapan Island – This island can be accessed by ferry and offers various biking trails to explore its stunning landscapes.

Best Things to Do in Pekanbaru: Activities and Tours

  • Riau Fantasi Theme Park – A theme park that offers thrilling rides for all ages.
  • Pekanbaru City Tour – The city tour covers the most popular attractions of Pekanbaru, accompanied by an experienced guide.
  • Shopping in Pekanbaru – The city has plenty of shopping centers, malls, and traditional markets, providing visitors with a shopping experience like no other.

Week-Long Itinerary for Pekanbaru

Day Activities
1 Visit An-Nur Grand Mosque and Rumah Gadang Riau.
2 Spend time at Taman Budaya Riau.
3 Go hiking or biking at Rumbai Green Valley Park or Taman Alam Merbah.
4 Visit Tesso Nilo National Park and Sungai Kampar Adventure.
5 Spend the day indulging in tours and activities.

7 Visit the Sultan Syarif Kasim II Museum and shopping centers/malls.

Food and Drink Scene of Pekanbaru

Must-Try Dishes

  • Asam Pedas – A sour and spicy fish soup that includes various herbs and spices, sometimes with vegetables and meat added as well.
  • Pindang Ikan Patin – A creamy and sour soup made with Pangasius fish, considered one of the signature dishes in Riau.
  • Soto Riau – A meat soup made using beef or poultry broth, mixed with noodles, bean sprouts, eggs, and spices.
  • Lemang – Rice cooked in bamboo tube over a charcoal fire, served with chicken curry or spicy beef rendang.

Local Beverages

  • Teh Tarek – Tea mixed with condensed milk and poured back and forth in a container to froth it up.
  • Kopi Tubruk – An Indonesian-style black coffee with coarse grounds and boiled water in a cup.
  • Kelo – A sweet and nutty drink made with coconut milk, sugar, and pandan leaves.

Where to Stay in Pekanbaru: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Grand Tjokro Pekanbaru – A four-star hotel that has an outdoor pool, a fitness center, and sauna facilities.
  • UFF Hotel Pekanbaru – A three-star hotel located near the city center and close to shopping and dining establishments.
  • Pesonna Hotel Pekanbaru – A modern hotel that offers a free shuttle to nearby shopping centers and has an exquisite rooftop terrace.

What to Buy in Pekanbaru: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Batik Trusmi Riau – Traditional cloth made using a wax-resistant dyeing technique, available in vibrant colors and elegant designs.
  • Songket Riau – A hand-woven textile that requires a great deal of skill and intricate designs, made using gold and silver threads.
  • Tenun Pahang Diraja Riau – A unique type of hand-woven cloth made using silk or cotton fibers. It is often used for traditional costumes and wedding ceremonies.

Local Culture and Customs of Pekanbaru

  • Traditional Attire – The traditional dress for men is the Baju Melayu, while women wear the Baju Kurung. Both outfits use batik fabrics.
  • Language – While Bahasa Indonesia is the official language, the local language in Pekanbaru is Malay.
  • Respectful Behavior – It is essential to show respect when visiting the various religious and cultural sites in Pekanbaru by dressing modestly and refraining from any disrespectful behavior.

Best Time to Visit Pekanbaru

The best time to visit Pekanbaru is between April and October, where the weather is dry and pleasant. The rainfall is relatively high between November and February, so it is best to avoid visiting during this period.

Do’s and Don’ts of Pekanbaru

Do:

  • Dress modestly when visiting religious and cultural attractions.
  • Greet locals with a smile and a respectful bow.
  • Try the local cuisine.

Don’t:

  • Drink tap water as it is not potable.
  • Engage in any illegal or immoral activities.
  • Litter in public areas.

Safety and Health

Safety in Pekanbaru

Pekanbaru is a relatively safe city, with low crime rates. However, travelers should always take precautions and be aware of their surroundings, especially when wandering around at night.

Health in Pekanbaru

It is essential to have updated vaccinations before visiting Pekanbaru. The city has a tropical climate that can cause dehydration and heat exhaustion. It is recommended to carry bottled water and stay hydrated.

Getting to Pekanbaru: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Pekanbaru

  • Sultan Syarif Kasim II International Airport – located 8km from Pekanbaru city center, serves domestic and international flights.

Bus Stations Near Pekanbaru

  • Tipe Ngarai – located near the Pasar Pagi Tipe Ngarai Market.
  • Bandar Raya Payung Sekaki Bus Terminal – located in the heart of Pekanbaru.

Other Options

  • Taxis and ride-hailing services are widely available in Pekanbaru.
  • Car rental services are also available.

How to Get Around in Pekanbaru

  • Taxis and ride-hailing services such as Grab and Gojek are common modes of transportation in Pekanbaru.
  • Ojek, a motorcycle taxi, can be an option for quick trips around the city.
  • Public transportation such as buses and the angkot (local mini-buses) are cheaper but usually full.

Where to Go Next from Pekanbaru: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Riau Islands – A stunning archipelago with white sandy beaches, pristine lakes, and historical landmarks.
  • Bukittinggi – A historical town located in West Sumatra, known for its beautiful landscapes and preserved colonial architecture.
  • Medan – A bustling city in North Sumatra that offers a blend of modernity and traditional influences.

Conclusion

Pekanbaru offers a unique travel experience by showcasing a rich blend of culture, history, nature, and modernity. With this comprehensive travel guide, visitors can maximize their stay in Pekanbaru and explore the beautiful city’s attractions, food, and culture.
