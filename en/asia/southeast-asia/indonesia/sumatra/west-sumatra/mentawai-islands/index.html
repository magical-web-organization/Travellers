Introduction to Mentawai Islands

Mentawai Islands is a hidden gem located in the West Sumatra province of Indonesia. This group of islands is known for its natural beauty, rich culture, and world-class surf breaks. The islands are home to the indigenous Mentawai tribe, who have preserved their culture and traditions for centuries.

List of Places to Visit in Mentawai Islands

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  1. Museum Mentawai: The Museum Mentawai displays traditional costumes, hunting gear, and art from the Mentawai tribe.
  2. Traditional Mentawai Village: A visit to a traditional Mentawai village provides an insight into the prehistoric way of life of the indigenous tribe.

Natural Attractions of Mentawai Islands: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  1. Siberut National Park: This national park is home to a variety of rare animals such as the Mentawai macaque, giant squirrel and the endemic Mentawai hornbill.
  2. Awera Island: Awera Island is known for its stunning beaches and coral reefs, ideal for snorkeling and diving.
  3. Sipora Island: Sipora Island is famous for its world-class surf breaks and is a must-visit destination for surf lovers.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Mentawai Islands

  1. Mount Madobak: This peak is the highest mountain in the Mentawai Islands, and offers a challenging hike with stunning panoramic views of the surrounding islands.
  2. Trek to Traditional Mentawai Villages: Take a hike or bike ride through the lush jungle to reach the traditional Mentawai villages and learn about their way of life.

Best Things to Do in Mentawai Islands: Activities and Tours

  1. Surfing: Mentawai Islands are awarded as the one of the top surfing destinations in the world.
  2. Snorkeling and Diving: Explore the coral reefs and the rich marine life of the Mentawai Islands.
  3. Cultural Tours: Join a guided tour to learn about the unique culture and traditions of the Mentawai tribe.
  4. Fishing: Charter a boat or join a tour for a day of fishing and catch some of the best fish in the area.

Food and Drink Scene of Mentawai Islands

Must-Try Dishes

  1. Pala-pala: A traditional Mentawai dish made with grilled fish, coconut milk, and chili.
  2. Sagu: A staple food made from a sago palm that is usually served with fish or vegetables.

Local Beverages

  1. Saguer: A local drink commonly made from fermented sago.
  2. Tuak: A traditional drink made from palm sap.

Where to Stay in Mentawai Islands: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  1. Kandui Villas: Located in Sipora Island, offers a luxurious villa experience with world-class surfing waves at its doorstep.
  2. Aloita Surf Resort & Spa: A family-friendly resort with a wide range of activities, including surfing, diving, fishing, and spa services.
  3. Pulau Siberut Guesthouse: Located in Siberut Island, this guesthouse offers traditional bamboo accommodation in the middle of the jungle.

What to Buy in Mentawai Islands: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  1. Mentawai Traditional Art: The Mentawai tribe is known for their intricate woodcarving and weaving, and you can purchase them as souvenirs.
  2. Sago Flour: Sago flour is a unique local product that makes an ideal souvenir for culinary enthusiasts.

Local Culture and Customs of Mentawai Islands

The Mentawai tribe is known for their unique culture that has been preserved through generations. Visitors are encouraged to respect their culture and observe their customs, such as wearing appropriate attire when visiting traditional villages and seeking permission prior photography. In general, the Mentawai people are known to be hospitable and warm to guests.

Best Time to Visit Mentawai Islands

The best time to visit Mentawai Islands is between March to November when the weather is dry, and the waters are calmer, providing ideal surfing conditions. December to January is when the monsoon rains hit and can make traveling to and around the islands more challenging.

Do’s and Don’ts of Mentawai Islands

Do:

  1. Respect the local customs and beliefs.
  2. Seek permission before taking photographs of the Mentawai people.
  3. Be mindful of the environment and avoid littering.

Don’t:

  1. Wear clothing that may offend local customs and beliefs.
  2. Disturb the flora and fauna in the Siberut National Park.
  3. Take shells or coral from the beaches.

Safety and Health

Safety in Mentawai Islands

Mentawai islands are safe to travel to, and the crime rate is relatively low. However, travelers are advised to take standard precautions such as being vigilant in crowded areas and avoid showing valuables in public.

Health in Mentawai Islands

Visitors are advised to be up-to-date on their routine vaccinations and take preventive measures against Malaria, a common disease in the region, through medication and the use of mosquito nets.

Getting to Mentawai Islands: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Mentawai Islands

There are two airports near the Mentawai Islands - Padang Airport on the mainland and Sikakap Airport on Sipora Island. Sikakap Airport serves as the main hub for domestic flights to the Mentawai Islands.

Bus Stations Near Mentawai Islands

Padang is the main bus hub to the nearby cities and towns.

Other Options

Ferries, private boats, and speedboats are other options to reach Mentawai Islands from Padang.

How to Get Around in Mentawai Islands

The best way to get around Mentawai Islands is by a chartered boat or speedboat. These are easily available at most places and allow for navigability between the islands.

Where to Go Next from Mentawai Islands: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  1. Bukittinggi: This charming town is known for its unique architecture, including traditional Minangkabau houses.
  2. Lake Maninjau: A stunning natural lake located in the West Sumatra region.

Conclusion

Mentawai Islands offer a unique travel experience and are a must-visit destination for adventure enthusiasts and cultural enthusiasts alike. With its natural beauty, rich culture, and world-class surf breaks, Mentawai Islands promise to be the highlight of any traveler’s journey in Southeast Asia.
