Introduction to Lombok

Lombok is one of Indonesia’s hidden gems, located in the Nusa Tenggara region. The island promises to fascinate you with its verdant hills, long sandy beaches, and crystal-clear waters. The cultural and natural attractions, along with the peaceful surroundings, are guaranteed to leave you with an unforgettable experience.

This tourist handbook is your ultimate guide to Lombok. It comprises everything from tourist attractions to the best places to eat, stay, and shop. Our goal is to help you make the most out of your stay on the island.

List of Places to Visit in Lombok

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Lombok’s rich cultural heritage is highlighted in its many museums and historical landmarks. The attractions here reflect the island’s long and storied past.

  • Mayura Water Palace: A perfect blend of historical and natural attractions, the palace is a stunning piece of architecture that sits in a beautiful man-made pond.
  • Sukarara Village: Set in the heart of Lombok, this traditional village showcases local handicrafts and also organizes cultural dance performances.
  • Narmada Park: Built in the 17th century as a replica of Bali’s Mount Rinjani, the park is home to an artificial lake and a palace with stunning architectural features.
  • Pura Lingsar: This Hindu temple is revered by both the Balinese and the Sasak tribes and is the epitome of religious harmony.
  • Sade Village: A must-visit place for anyone interested in Lombok’s traditional lifestyle, this village gives an insight into the Sasak’s unique way of life.

Natural Attractions of Lombok: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Lombok’s natural attractions leave a lasting impression on all visitors. The island’s mountains, waterfalls, and beaches make it one of Indonesia’s unmissable destinations, and there’s plenty to explore, whether it’s trekking or simply soaking up the scenery.

  • Mount Rinjani: This active volcano is a crucial part of Lombok’s geography and spirituality. The surrounding national park is a UNESCO World Heritage Site and is a haven for trekkers and nature enthusiasts.
  • Tanjung Aan Beach: A crescent-shaped beach that produces a stunningly picturesque view of turquoise waters, Tanjung Aan Beach is perfect for those who want to spend time sunbathing and swimming.
  • Pink Beach: Known for its blush-colored sand (created by minuscule organisms), Pink Beach is tranquil, and its coral reefs make it an ideal place for snorkeling.
  • Benang Kelambu Waterfall: The twin falls surrounded by lush greenery make it an excellent place to experience Lombok’s tropical nature.
  • Gili Islands: These three coral-fringed island off the northwestern coast of Lombok are entirely car-free, and each of them has its own unique style and charm.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Lombok

Lombok is known for its rugged terrain and picturesque landscape, making hiking and biking two of the island’s most popular activities. Here are some of the best trials to consider:

  • Mount Rinjani trails: With 3,726 meters, Mount Rinjani is the second-highest mountain in Indonesia. The tough hike up to the summit is an incredible experience, and it rewards you with an unforgettable view.
  • Tetebatu trails: Surrounded by lush green rice fields, traditional villages, and forests, the Tetebatu hiking trails are perfect for those who want to get up close and personal with nature.
  • Loyok trails: A great spot for biking enthusiasts, Loyok’s off-road paths take you through a range of terrain, from water crossings to tough hills.

Best Things to Do in Lombok: Activities and Tours

The island of Lombok offers countless activities for tourists to get the most out of their time here. Here are some of the best things to do:

  • Surfing: Lombok has some of the best surf spots in Indonesia, and the excellent conditions throughout the year make it an ideal destination for beginners or seasoned surfers.
  • Snorkeling: Lombok’s crystal-clear waters make for the perfect snorkeling experience, with the Gili Islands being a fantastic spot to admire the coral reefs and marine life
  • Island hopping: Lombok is surrounded by stunning islands that are perfect for day trips.
  • Camping: Lombok’s mountains, forests, and beaches make it an excellent place to camp, and many camping companies provide comfortable tents with spectacular views.
  • Traditional handicraft workshops: Lombok is famous for its handicrafts, ranging from weaving to pottery. There are plenty of workshops that offer visitors the opportunity to create their own piece of art.

Food and Drink Scene of Lombok

Lombok’s cuisine is famous for its spicy and vibrant flavors that pay tribute to both land and sea. Here are some of the best dishes and local beverages that you should try:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Ayam Taliwang: This spicy chicken dish is a specialty of Lombok, cooked with multiple spices and condiments.
  • Plecing Kangkung: It is a salad made from water spinach and chili paste, mixed with shrimp paste and lime juice.
  • Sate Bulayak: A dish consisting of marinated meat skewers typically made from pork or chicken.
  • Gado-Gado Lombok: A dish containing mixed vegetables with peanut sauce.

Local Beverages

  • Es Kelapa Muda: Refreshingly fresh and sweet coconut water served in a coconut.
  • Wedang Ronde: A warm, soothing dessert drink made of glutinous rice balls in ginger-infused brown sugar syrup.
  • Kopi Lombok: Lombok’s famous coffee is often compared to Sumatran coffee, known for its smooth and mild taste.

Where to Stay in Lombok: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Lombok’s accommodation is varied to suit different budgets and tastes. From luxury hotels to budget-friendly hostels, you’ll find what you’re looking for. Here are some of the best places to stay in Lombok:

  • Jeeva Klui Resort: A luxurious beachfront resort located in Klui Beach. The private suites overlook the tropical gardens and the crystal-clear waters.
  • Rinjani Lodge: A boutique hotel tucked away in the forest area, the upscale property offers an unobstructed view of Mount Rinjani.
  • Gili Castle: A budget-friendly hostel in Gili Trawangan where you can sleep in a treehouse or private room.
  • Lakey Peak Inn: A surfer’s paradise located near Lakey Peak, a surfing hotspot.

What to Buy in Lombok: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Lombok is a treasure trove of local handicrafts and souvenirs. Here are some of the must-buy items that you should consider:

  • Pottery: Lombok’s pottery is famous all over Indonesia, where intricate designs and the high quality of the material used
  • Weaving: Lombok’s traditional fabrics, like Tenun Ikat and Songket, are the product of a complicated and refined weaving process, often featuring beautiful colors and patterns.
  • Pearls: Lombok is the hub of Indonesia’s pearl industry, and you can find freshwater and South Sea pearls in many sizes, shapes, and colors.
  • Lombok Coffee: The coffee from Lombok is a fragrant blend of Arabica and Robusta, and it is a not-to-be-missed souvenir for coffee aficionados.
  • Lombok Honey: A must-try for anyone with a sweet tooth, the honey produced here is organic, fresh, and deliriously delicious.

Local Culture and Customs of Lombok

Lombok’s local culture reflects its blend of Hindu and Islamic traditions, as well as the Sasak tribe’s unique way of life. Visitors should respect the local culture, and we recommend wearing modest clothing when visiting a mosque or local village. It is customary to take your shoes off when entering homes and temples, and it is always better to ask for permission before taking photographs.

Best Time to Visit Lombok

The best time to visit Lombok is during the dry season, which runs from May to September. The weather is excellent, and there is little to no rainfall. The weather also makes it great for outdoor activities like hiking and surfing. However, it’s essential to book accommodation and activities well in advance, as the peak season gets crowded.

Do’s and Don’ts of Lombok

Do:

  • Try the local cuisine and beverages.
  • Dress modestly when visiting a mosque or village.
  • Take your shoes off before entering temples or homes.
  • Learn a few basic phrases in Bahasa Indonesia to help you communicate with the locals.
  • Respect the local culture and customs.

Don’t:

  • Don’t litter or leave your trash behind.
  • Don’t walk around in a bikini outside the beach area.
  • Don’t touch or disrupt religious artifacts or offerings.
  • Don’t criticize the local customs or beliefs.

Safety and Health

Safety in Lombok

Lombok is generally a safe place for tourists to visit, but just like any other destination, tourists need to exercise caution. Petty crime is rare, but it’s always good to carry your valuables with you and avoid flaunting your wealth.

Health in Lombok

There are no major health concerns in Lombok, but it’s best to take precautions when it comes to food and water. Stick to bottled water, and make sure you check that any food you eat is adequately cooked before consuming it.

Getting to Lombok: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Lombok

Lombok has an international airport, Lombok International Airport (LOP), located in Central Lombok. The airport has domestic and international travel to destinations like Bali, Jakarta, and Singapore.

Bus Stations Near Lombok

Traveling by bus is possible, with many operators traveling to different parts of Lombok. The most prominent bus terminal is Mandalika Terminal, located in Central Lombok.

Other Options

If you’re traveling to Lombok from Bali, you can take a fast boat from Bali to Lombok. They depart from Padang Bai, Sanur, and Serangan.

How to Get Around in Lombok

Taxis, motorbikes, and private cars are the most common transportation options available to tourists in Lombok. For those who like a more adventurous mode of transportation, cycling is an ideal way to explore the island. Motorbike rental is also an option, but it’s important to note that the roads can be unpredictable. Hiring a driver for the day is also a popular option for those who want to explore Lombok at their own pace.

Where to Go Next from Lombok: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Lombok is surrounded by other beautiful destinations that are worth checking out. Here are some top options to consider:

  • Bali: Just a 25-minute flight from Lombok, Bali is an excellent place to visit next, and it is home to a range of attractions, from pristine beaches to scenic rice terraces.
  • Sumbawa: Another Indonesian island in the east of Lombok, Sumbawa is best known for its perfect waves for surfers and astounding beauty.
  • Labuan Bajo: Located in the east of Lombok, it’s a charming fishing town situated in front of Komodo Island, which is home to the mythical Komodo Dragon.

Conclusion

Lombok is a unique island with a lot to offer, from stunning nature to rich cultural traditions. We hope this comprehensive travel guide has provided you with all the information you need to make your stay on the island an unforgettable one. With so many beautiful attractions and destinations to explore, Lombok is undoubtedly a place that every traveler should visit at least once in their lifetime, so don’t wait and start planning your trip today!
