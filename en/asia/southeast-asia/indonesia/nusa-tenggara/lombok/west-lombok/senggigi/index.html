Introduction to Senggigi

Senggigi is a popular tourist destination in West Lombok, Indonesia, known for its stunning sunsets, pristine beaches, and lively nightlife. Located about 20 minutes away from Lombok International Airport, it’s the perfect base for exploring the island’s natural and cultural attractions and is often referred to as the gateway to the Gili Islands. Whether you’re a solo traveler, couple, or family, Senggigi has something to offer everyone. This travel guide will help you plan your trip by highlighting the best attractions, accommodation, food, culture, safety, and more.

List of Places to Visit in Senggigi

Senggigi has much to offer to visitors beyond the stunning beaches. Here are some of the most remarkable places to visit:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Mayura Water Palace

Mayura Water Palace, constructed in the 18th century, is a mesmerizing and historical place to visit. The palace was a vital location where the ruling Balinese kingdom would have dancing ceremonies, both ritualistic and entertains.

Pura Batu Bolong

Perched on the cliffside a few minutes north of Senggigi, Pura Batu Bolong is a Hindu temple with beautiful views of the coastline. The temple is famous for a hole in the reef rock that the temple sits atop, where it is believed to house good spirits.

Natural Attractions of Senggigi: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Mount Rinjani

Mount Rinjani is an active volcano that towers over Lombok. It’s popular among hiking enthusiasts as it’s the second-highest volcano in Indonesia and provides many wonderful viewpoints.

Gili Islands

The Gili Islands are a group of three islands known for their turquoise waters, white sandy beaches, and fantastic diving spots. Visitors to the Gili Islands can enjoy a range of activities such as snorkeling, scuba diving, paddleboarding, and horseback riding on the beach.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Senggigi

If you’re looking for an adventure, Senggigi is an excellent place for biking or hiking. Here are some popular routes to explore:

Tinombawan Rice Terraces

The Tinombawan Rice Terraces is the Spian Village – a 45-minute hike above Senggigi. It offers a glimpse into the local way of life and the stunning surrounding scenery will keep you intrigued.

Lendang Bajur Traditional Village

Lendang Bajur Village, located about 8 kilometers east of Mataram City, is known for its unique crafts and stunning landscape. Visitors can witness handicrafts such as weaving and woodcarving, and also take part in cultural events.

Best Things to Do in Senggigi: Activities and Tours

Senggigi has much to offer besides nature and adventure. Here are some of the top things to do:

Explore the local beaches

The most prominent beach in Senggigi is Senggigi Beach, which offers a range of watersports, including snorkeling, kayaking, and surfing. Other beaches to check out include Mangsit Beach, the Coral Reefs of Gili Nanggu and the Kuta Beach in southern Lombok.

Enjoy a sunset cruise

One of the best ways to enjoy stunning views of the Lombok coastline is aboard a sunset cruise. Numerous companies offer tours, charter boats, and dinner cruises to explore the island’s stunning scenery.

Food and Drink Scene of Senggigi

Senggigi offers a range of dining options, including local and international cuisine. Here are some must-try dishes and beverages during your visit.

Must-Try Dishes

Ayam Taliwang

Originating from Lombok, Ayam Taliwang is a grilled chicken dish marinated in shrimp paste, sweet soy sauce, and lime juice. It’s a spicy dish that is excellent for those who like a bit of heat.

Plecing Kangkung

A traditional Lombok dish made of water spinach or Kankung (a leafy green vegetable), sambal terasi (shrimp paste sauce), and fried peanuts.

Local Beverages

Es Kelapa Muda

Es Kelapa Muda is a refreshing coconut drink made from young coconut meat and juice, commonly served in a coconut shell.

Tuak

Tuak is a traditional alcoholic beverage made from fermented palm sap. It’s popular among locals and is often consumed during social gatherings and celebrations.

Where to Stay in Senggigi: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Senggigi has various lodging options, ranging from budget-friendly hostels to luxury resorts. Some of the best accommodations in Senggigi include:

The Sheraton Senggigi Beach Resort

Located on Senggigi Beach, The Sheraton Senggigi Beach Resort is an upscale resort ideal for a luxury escape. The resort offers several dining options, a lagoon-style pool, and a range of spa treatments.

Puri Mas Boutique Resort and Spa

Puri Mas is a boutique resort offering beachfront bungalows and villas and three outdoor pools. The resort is famous for its art collection and regularly hosts cultural performances.

What to Buy in Senggigi: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Senggigi is known for its striking handicrafts, textiles, and pottery. Some of the best souvenirs to bring home from Senggigi include:

  • Handwoven textiles
  • Traditional wooden masks
  • Pottery

Local Culture and Customs of Senggigi

The locals of Senggigi are predominantly Muslim, and visitors should dress modestly when outside of the beach resort areas. It’s common for visitors to encounter locals at markets and temples, and it’s essential to respect local customs and beliefs. Visitors should also avoid drinking tap water and opt for bottled water and other treated beverages to prevent diarrhea and other water-borne diseases.

Best Time to Visit Senggigi

The best time to visit Senggigi is between April and October when the weather is warm and dry, with little rainfall. It is also the peak season, and prices are generally higher. Low season months are November to March and are the wetter season with a higher chance of rain, but it’s a quiet time, and prices are more affordable.

Do’s and Don’ts of Senggigi

Do:

  • Dress modestly when visiting temples and interacting with locals
  • Carry bottled water and other treated beverages to avoid water-borne diseases
  • Explore the local culture and customs and take part in traditional activities
  • Bring mosquito repellent and sunblock

Don’t:

  • Get involved in drugs/alcohol trafficking; drug punishment penalties in Indonesia can involve life imprisonment or the death penalty.
  • Disrespect the local customs and beliefs
  • Leave valuables unattended in public places
  • Litter the beaches and waste environment

Safety and Health

Safety in Senggigi

Senggigi is a relatively safe destination, but it is always essential to take precautions. Some tips to stay safe during your visit include:

  • Avoid carrying too much cash and valuables when walking on the street
  • Take authorized taxis instead of street-side taxis
  • Avoid walking alone at night
  • Be cautious when swimming in the sea and always swim between red and yellow flags

Health in Senggigi

Senggigi is located in a malarial region, and visitors should take the necessary precautions to prevent mosquito bites. Dengue fever is also prevalent in the area, and visitors should wear long sleeves and use mosquito repellent.

Getting to Senggigi: Transportation Options and Logistics

Senggigi is easily accessible via air, sea, and land. Here are the transportation options available:

Airports Near Senggigi

Lombok International Airport (LOP) is the closest airport to Senggigi and is located about 45 minutes away from the town. From the airport, visitors can take a taxi to Senggigi.

Bus Stations Near Senggigi

Senggigi is well-connected via local and intercity buses to various parts of Lombok. The primary bus station in Lombok is Mandalika Terminal, which is located in Mataram.

Other Options

Visitors can also hire a private driver or rent a scooter to explore the island. Ferry services are also available from Bali to Lombok.

How to Get Around in Senggigi

Senggigi is a small town and is easily navigable on foot or via bicycles. Taxi services are also available, and visitors can hire scooters for easy access to nearby attractions.

Where to Go Next from Senggigi: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Senggigi is an excellent starting point to explore the surrounding areas of Lombok. Here are some of the best places to visit:

Three Gili Islands

The Gili Islands consist of three islands, Gili Trawangan, Gili Meno, and Gili Air, and are the epitome of a paradise island experience. The islands can be accessed via speedboat or ferry from Senggigi, and visitors can enjoy various water sports or relax on the golden beaches.

Mount Rinjani

Mount Rinjani is Lombok’s iconic landmark and offers breathtaking views from the summit. Visitors can take a two- or three-day trek to the top of the volcano, and it is essential to book with authorized tour companies.

Conclusion

Senggigi is undoubtedly one of the most beautiful and unforgettable places to visit in Indonesia. Planning your trip with this guide will ensure that you make the most of your stay. Take advantage of its many natural and cultural attractions, savor the local cuisine, and relax in its charming accommodations. Whether you’re planning an adventure-packed trip or a laid-back retreat with family and friends, Senggigi is a must-visit destination.
