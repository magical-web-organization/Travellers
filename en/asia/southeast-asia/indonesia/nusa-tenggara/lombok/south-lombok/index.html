Introduction to South Lombok

South Lombok is a hidden treasure in Indonesia, known for its pristine beaches, lush rice terraces, and rural villages that preserve the authentic Sasak culture. Unlike its bustling neighbour Bali, South Lombok offers a tranquil escape for those seeking to recharge their body and soul. From waterfalls and volcanoes to surfing and snorkeling, South Lombok has something for everyone.

List of Places to Visit in South Lombok

To help plan your trip, here are the must-see places in South Lombok:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Sade Village: A traditional Sasak village that showcases the daily life, rituals, and crafts of this ancient culture.
  • Mayura Water Palace: A historical site where the kings of Lombok used to hold court and entertain guests.
  • Narmada Water Park: Originally built as a replica of Mount Rinjani, Narmada Water Park is now a serene garden and bathing pool where locals come to pray and relax.

Natural Attractions of South Lombok: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Tanjung Aan Beach: A scenic white sand beach that offers a picturesque sunset view.
  • Mawun Beach: A secluded cove with turquoise waters and a limestone cliff backdrop.
  • Benang Kelambu Waterfall: A two-tiered waterfall that is accessible by a scenic hike through rice paddies and forests.
  • Mount Rinjani National Park: A challenging trek that rewards hikers with breathtaking views of the caldera and the summit sunrise.
  • Gili Islands: A trio of stunning islands (Gili Trawangan, Gili Meno, and Gili Air) known for their crystal-clear waters, coral reefs, and laid-back vibe.

Hiking/Biking Routes of South Lombok

If you’re into outdoor activities, you’ll love these hiking and biking routes in South Lombok:

  • Rice fields biking tour: A leisurely bike ride through the rural countryside, passing verdant rice terraces and local villages.
  • Mount Rinjani Trekking Tour: A multi-day trek that takes you to the summit of Mount Rinjani (3,726 meters), the second-highest volcano in Indonesia.
  • Pengsong Temple cycling route: A scenic bike ride through the rural villages to visit the Pengsong temple and see the beautiful Lombok landscape

Best Things to Do in South Lombok: Activities and Tours

South Lombok offers a range of activities and tours that cater to different interests and budgets:

  • Surfing: South Lombok is a paradise for surfers, with consistent waves and less crowded breaks compared to Bali. Some of the best surf spots are Gerupuk, Ekas, and Tanjung Aan.
  • Snorkeling and Diving: The Gili Islands and Sekotong Bay are popular destinations for snorkeling and diving, with healthy coral reefs and diverse marine life.
  • Fishing and Sunset Cruise: Enjoy a traditional wooden boat ride to explore the coast and try your luck at fishing while sipping cocktails and watching the sunset.
  • Cultural Tours: Learn about the Sasak culture by visiting local villages and attending traditional ceremonies and dances.
  • Yoga and Wellness: South Lombok has a burgeoning wellness scene with yoga retreats, meditation centers, and massage parlors.

Week-Long Itinerary for South Lombok

Here’s a suggested itinerary for a week-long trip to South Lombok:

Day Activity
1 Arrival and check in at hotel in Kuta. Visit Tanjung Aan Beach for sunset.
2 Morning surf lesson at Gerupuk. Afternoon visit to Sade Village and Mayura Water Palace.
3 Full-day excursion to Gili Islands for snorkeling and beach hopping.
4 Trek to the Benang Kelambu Waterfall and visit Taman Narmada Water Park.
5 Sunrise trek to the summit of Mount Rinjani. Return to hotel for a relaxing massage and spa.
6 Cycling tour to rice paddies and rural villages. Afternoon visit to Pura Lingsar temple.
7 Departure from Lombok.

Food and Drink Scene of South Lombok

South Lombok offers an array of culinary delights that reflect the diversity of Indonesian cuisine:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Ayam Taliwang: A spicy grilled chicken dish served with plecing kangkung (water spinach salad) and white rice.
  • Beberuk Terong: A mixed vegetable salad made of eggplant, tomato, chili, and shrimp paste.
  • Sate Bulayak: A skewered grilled meat dish served with rice cakes and peanut sauce.
  • Sate Ikan Tanjung: A seafood variation of sate (skewered meat) made of tuna or snapper and served with sweet soy sauce.

Local Beverages

  • Es Jeruk: A refreshing orange juice mixed with ice and sugar.
  • Brem: A traditional rice wine that is sweet and sour with a mild alcohol content.
  • Kopi Lombok: A local coffee brand known for its strong aroma and bold flavor.

Where to Stay in South Lombok: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

There are various accommodation options in South Lombok to suit different preferences and budgets:

  • Kuta Lombok: The main tourist hub with a range of hotels, hostels, and villas. Recommended options include Novotel Lombok, Ashtari Lounge and Kitchen, and Selong Selo Resort.
  • Tanjung Aan: A secluded beach with a few beachfront resorts and homestays. Recommended options include Jivana Resort, G’Day Inn, and Baba Bungalow.
  • Gerupuk: A fishing village that has become a popular surf spot with basic accommodation and homestays. Recommended options include Gerupuk Homestay and Sari Inn.

What to Buy in South Lombok: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Here are some of the best souvenirs and local products to buy in South Lombok:

  • Sasak Weavings: Handmade textiles and tapestries that feature traditional motifs and colors. Famous Sasak weavings include songket, tenun ikat, and sasirangan.
  • Pottery and Ceramics: Lombok has a rich tradition of pottery making, with unique designs and techniques. Some of the best places to buy pottery are Penujak Village and Banyumulek Village.
  • Pearls and Jewelry: South Lombok is known for its high-quality pearls and exquisite jewelry made from silver and gold. Some recommended jewelry shops are Lombok Pearl and Pearl of Lombok.

Local Culture and Customs of South Lombok

Sasak culture is deeply rooted in South Lombok, and visitors can learn about the ancient traditions and beliefs by visiting local villages and participating in cultural events. Here are a few customs to keep in mind:

  • Dress modestly: Sasak people are conservative, so it’s best to cover your shoulders and knees when visiting villages and temples.
  • Remove shoes and hats: It’s customary to take off your shoes and hats before entering a Sasak house or temple.
  • Respect elders and monks: Sasak people value hierarchy and respect for authority, so be polite and courteous to elders and religious figures.
  • Ask permission: If you want to take photos or participate in a ceremony, always ask for permission first.

Best Time to Visit South Lombok

The best time to visit South Lombok is from May to October, which is the dry season. The weather is sunny and warm, and the sea is calm and clear. The peak season is from July to August, so expect higher prices and more crowds. If you prefer a quieter and more affordable trip, consider visiting from November to April, which is the rainy season.

Do’s and Don’ts of South Lombok

Here are some quick do’s and don’ts to follow when visiting South Lombok:

Do:

  • Learn basic Indonesian phrases: Learning a few words of Indonesian will help you communicate better with locals and show your respect for their culture.
  • Support local businesses: Patronize local shops, restaurants, and tour operators to contribute to the local economy and community.
  • Follow eco-friendly practices: Lombok’s natural resources are precious, so avoid littering, wasting water, or damaging coral reefs.

Don’t:

  • Disrespect local customs: Avoid wearing revealing clothes in public places or showing affection in public.
  • Buy souvenir made from animal products: Avoid purchasing souvenirs made from ivory, turtle shells, or other endangered species.
  • Engage with touts: Some locals may offer you overpriced tours or services, so be discerning and do your research before making a purchase.

Safety and Health

Safety in South Lombok

South Lombok is generally safe for tourists, but there are a few precautions to keep in mind:

  • Be aware of pickpockets: Keep your valuables close to you in crowded places like markets or festivals.
  • Avoid walking alone at night: Stick to well-lit areas and take taxis or motorbikes instead of walking alone at night.
  • Watch out for natural hazards: Some places like Mount Rinjani or the sea can be dangerous if you’re not prepared, so follow the guidelines and advice of your guide or mentor.

Health in South Lombok

Lombok has a few health risks, mainly related to food and water hygiene. Here are some tips to stay healthy:

  • Drink only bottled or boiled water: Don’t drink tap water or ice cubes that may contain bacteria or viruses.
  • Eat at reputable restaurants: Look for restaurants that follow proper food safety practices and have a high turnover of customers.
  • Use mosquito repellent: There are cases of dengue fever and malaria in Lombok, so use mosquito repellent and wear long sleeves and pants during dusk and dawn hours.

Getting to South Lombok: Transportation Options and Logistics

South Lombok is accessible by various modes of transportation, depending on your budget and time constraints:

Airports Near South Lombok

  • Lombok International Airport: The closest airport to South Lombok, located in Praya, Central Lombok. It offers direct flights from Jakarta, Bali, Kuala Lumpur, and Singapore.

Bus Stations Near South Lombok

  • Mandalika Bus Station: The biggest bus station in Kuta, Lombok, with daily buses to other cities in Lombok and Bali.
  • Mataram Bus Terminal: The main bus station in Mataram, the capital of Lombok, with connections to other cities in Nusa Tenggara and Java.

Other Options

  • Private Car: You can hire a private car or taxi to get around South Lombok, although it can be expensive compared to other modes of transport.
  • Motorbike Rental: Renting a motorbike is a popular option for travelers who want more freedom and flexibility, but it requires a valid international driving license and a cautious attitude.

How to Get Around in South Lombok

South Lombok has various options for transportation, depending on your comfort level and budget:

  • Public Transportation: Buses, minivans, and bemos (shared taxi) are affordable ways to get around Lombok, but they can be crowded and slow.
  • Charter Car: Hiring a car with a driver is convenient if you want to visit multiple sights in a day, but it can be expensive for solo travelers.
  • Motorbike Rental: Renting a motorbike is the most popular option for backpackers and adventurous travelers, but it requires good driving skills and wearing helmets.

Where to Go Next from South Lombok: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

South Lombok is a great starting point to explore other parts of Lombok or nearby islands:

  • Senggigi Beach: A lively beach town in West Lombok with a range of accommodations, nightlife, and water activities.
  • Mount Tambora: A remote volcano in West Nusa Tenggara that offers a unique trekking experience and a stunning panorama of the sea.
  • Bali Island: A world-famous tourist destination known for its beaches, temples, and nightlife. You can reach Bali by plane or boat from Lombok.

Conclusion

South Lombok is a hidden gem that has much to offer for travelers seeking an authentic and relaxing holiday. From culture and nature to adventure and relaxation, South Lombok has it all. Use this guide to plan your trip wisely and make the most of your time in Lombok. Remember to respect the local culture, support the local economy, and practice responsible tourism. Selamat datang di Lombok! (Welcome to Lombok!)
