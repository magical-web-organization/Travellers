Introduction to Ruteng

Located on the island of Flores in Nusa Tenggara, Ruteng is a charming town in Indonesia that is perfect for visitors seeking a blend of natural beauty and cultural attractions. Surrounded by lush hills and picturesque rice paddies, Ruteng is a popular destination for outdoor enthusiasts, history buffs, and those interested in local customs. This guide will provide you with all the information you need to plan your trip to Ruteng.

List of Places to Visit in Ruteng

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Ruteng Pu’u Traditional Village: This authentic Manggarai village is a perfect place to learn about the traditional customs and living conditions of the local people.
  • Liang Bua Cave: This cave was made famous by the discovery of the Homo floresiensis, also known as the “Hobbit” species of human. Its importance makes it one of the most popular tourist attractions in Ruteng.
  • Ruteng Catholic Church: The Ruteng Catholic Church is one of the most beautiful and historic Catholic churches in the region. It is renowned for its stunning architecture and ornate decorations.

Natural Attractions of Ruteng: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Cunca Rami Waterfall: Located about an hour away from Ruteng, this magnificent waterfall is perfect for swimming, picnicking, and hiking.
  • Golo Lusang Rice Terrace: This picturesque rice terrace offers stunning views of the surrounding countryside and makes for a great photo opportunity.
  • Ranamese Lake: This serene lake is surrounded by lush hills and is perfect for those seeking tranquility and scenic beauty.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Ruteng

Ruteng is a great destination for outdoor enthusiasts, offering several hiking and biking routes through its stunning hills and valleys. Some of the most popular routes include:

  • Wae Rebo Trek: A day hike to the traditional village of Wae Rebo located in the mountains of Flores. This trek takes you through dense forests, shallow rivers, and beautiful hills and valleys.
  • Melolo Trek: This two to three-day trek takes you through the mountainous terrain of Flores, passing through traditional villages and offering stunning views of the surrounding valleys.
  • Ranamese-Caldera Trek: This 2-3 day trek takes visitors through the stunning natural beauty of the caldera of Mt. Ranaka, including its picturesque Danau Ranamese lake.

Best Things to Do in Ruteng: Activities and Tours

  • Traditional Village Tour: Take a tour of a traditional village near Ruteng to learn about local customs and living conditions.
  • Culinary Tour: Experience the local cuisine of Flores with a culinary tour of Ruteng, where you can sample traditional dishes like Ikat rice, sop konro, and Lepet Ketan.
  • Cultural Performance: Catch a traditional dance or musical performance to learn more about the culture of Flores.

Week-Long Itinerary for Ruteng

Food and Drink Scene of Ruteng

Must-Try Dishes

  • Ikat Rice: A traditional dish made from a mixture of rice, coconut, and various beans and spices.
  • Sop Konro: A hearty beef soup made with spices and served with rice and crackers.
  • Lepet Ketan: A sweet and sticky rice cake served with coconut milk and palm sugar.

Local Beverages

  • Kopi Flores: A strong and flavorful coffee made from beans grown in the region.
  • Sopi: A popular local liquor made from the sap of the lontar palm.

Where to Stay in Ruteng: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Hotel Susteran MBC: A budget hotel located in the heart of Ruteng.
  • Ruteng Homestay: A cozy guesthouse with a traditional atmosphere located in the center of town.
  • Wae Rebo Homestay: A homestay located in the traditional village of Wae Rebo, about an hour from Ruteng.

What to Buy in Ruteng: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Ikat Textiles: A traditional woven textile that is unique to the region.
  • Lontar Palm Handicrafts: Handicrafts made from the leaves of the Lontar palm, which are common in the area.
  • Coffee Beans: High-quality coffee beans grown and processed in Flores make for a great souvenir and are also a fantastic gift for friends and family.

Local Culture and Customs of Ruteng

The people of Ruteng are Manggarai, a distinctive and unique ethnic group with their own customs and traditions. It is customary for visitors to remove their shoes before entering a Manggarai home or a traditional village and to dress modestly when visiting religious sites.

Best Time to Visit Ruteng

The best time to visit Ruteng is from May to October, which is the dry season. During this time, visitors can enjoy the beautiful natural scenery of the area without having to worry about excessive rain.

Do’s and Don’ts of Ruteng

Do:

  • Respect the local culture and traditions of the Manggarai people.
  • Try the local cuisine and delicacies.
  • Learn about the history and culture of the area.

Don’t:

  • Wear revealing clothing when visiting traditional villages or religious sites.
  • Disrupt the local wildlife and natural environment.
  • Ignore the local customs and traditions of the area.

Safety and Health

Safety in Ruteng

Ruteng is a relatively safe place for visitors, but it is still recommended to take the usual precautions when traveling in unfamiliar areas.

Health in Ruteng

Visitors are advised to drink only bottled or boiled water and to take precautions to avoid mosquito bites to prevent malaria and dengue fever.

Getting to Ruteng: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Ruteng

  • Maumere Airport: The closest airport to Ruteng with direct flights to Bali and other parts of Indonesia.
  • Ende Airport: Located about two hours away from Ruteng with direct flights to Bali.

Bus Stations Near Ruteng

  • Ruteng Bus Station: The main bus station in Ruteng with connections to other parts of Flores.
  • Labuan Bajo Bus Station: Located about five hours away from Ruteng with connections to Bali and other parts of Indonesia.

Other Options

Private cars and taxis are also available for hire from larger cities like Bali and Labuan Bajo.

How to Get Around in Ruteng

The best way to get around Ruteng and its surrounding areas is by private car or motorcycle, which can be rented at various locations in town. Taxis and bemos (small vans used for public transportation) are also available for hire.

Where to Go Next from Ruteng: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Kelimutu National Park: Located about four hours away from Ruteng, Kelimutu is famous for its three-colored crater lakes.
  • Labuan Bajo: A fishing town located about five hours away from Ruteng with plenty of activities and attractions like snorkeling and visits to the Komodo National Park.
  • Bajawa: A town located about four hours away from Ruteng with traditional villages and scenic landscapes.

Conclusion

Ruteng is a hidden gem in Indonesia, offering visitors the opportunity to experience the rich culture and stunning natural beauty of the country. With this comprehensive tour guide, you will be well-equipped to explore all that Ruteng has to offer.
