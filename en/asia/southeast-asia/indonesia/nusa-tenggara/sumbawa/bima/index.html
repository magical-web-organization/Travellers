Introduction to Bima

Located in the eastern part of Indonesia, Bima is a hidden gem that is perfect for those who want to experience an unspoiled island paradise. Bima is an idyllic town in the Sumbawa region, Nusa Tenggara, known for its beautiful beaches, cultural heritage, and rugged terrain. Bima is isolated from its neighboring towns, which makes it a unique destination for travelers who want to escape the crowds.

List of Places to Visit in Bima

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

The cultural heritage of Bima can be seen in its museums and historical places. The Bima Museum is a must-visit as it provides a glimpse of the local history and culture of Bima. Another popular attraction is the Sultan Salahuddin Mosque, a stunning architectural masterpiece built-in 1812. The ancient Hindu temple, Batu Meringin is another must-visit where visitors can learn about the Hindu culture of Bima.

Natural Attractions of Bima: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Bima is a great destination for outdoor enthusiasts as it offers numerous natural attractions. Tambora National Park is a popular destination where visitors can hike to the summit of Mount Tambora and enjoy the stunning views of the surrounding hills and beaches. The Segara Anak Lake is another natural attraction that is perfect for trekking and exploring the flora and fauna of the region.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Bima

For adventure seekers, Bima has numerous hiking and biking routes that offer unique and unforgettable experiences. The Padolo Waterfall trail is a popular hiking route, which takes visitors through the lush forests and ends at the mesmerizing Padolo Waterfall.

Best Things to Do in Bima: Activities and Tours

There are plenty of things to do in Bima, from exploring the natural wonders to experiencing the local culture. Visitors can take a boat ride to Tanjung Ringgit, where they can witness breathtaking sunsets or visit the traditional markets of Bima to taste the local delicacies.

Week-Long Itinerary for Bima

Day 1: Visit the Bima Museum and Sultan Salahuddin Mosque Day 2: Trek to Mount Tambora Day 3: Explore Segara Anak Lake Day 4: Relax at Tente Beach Day 5: Visit Tanjung Ringgit for a sunset experience Day 6: Explore the traditional markets and try the local delicacies Day 7: Hike to Padolo Waterfall

Food and Drink Scene of Bima

Must-Try Dishes

The cuisine in Bima is a fusion of Malay, Indonesian, and Chinese flavors. One of the famous dishes in Bima is Ayam Kampung Taliwang, a grilled chicken dish that is cooked with spicy seasoning. Sate Bima, a beef satay dish, is also popular among locals and tourists.

Local Beverages

The local beverage in Bima is the traditional drink, Tuak which is made from fermented palm wine. Another popular beverage in the region is Es Kacang Merah, a refreshing drink made from red beans.

Where to Stay in Bima: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Bima offers a range of accommodation options, from budget-friendly hostels to luxurious hotels. Kencana Beach Hotel is a popular choice for visitors who want to stay close to the beach, while Hotel Amba offers a more luxurious experience. Backpackers can stay at the Bima Backpackers Hostel, which offers a comfortable stay at an affordable price.

What to Buy in Bima: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Tourists can bring home souvenirs from the traditional markets of Bima, including handcrafted souvenirs, batik, and traditional woven fabrics. The local product that tourists should not miss is the woven sarongs made of tenun ikat, a traditional art of weaving practiced by the people of Bima.

Local Culture and Customs of Bima

The people of Bima have a unique culture that is reflected in their way of life and customs. The Bimanese people are predominantly Muslims and therefore, visitors should adhere to the local dress code and customs. It is customary to greet the locals with “Salamat Datang,” which means “welcome.”

Best Time to Visit Bima

The best time to visit Bima is from May to November when the weather is dry and sunny, perfect for outdoor excursions. The monsoon season in Bima is from December to April, which is characterized by heavy rainfall and winds.

Do’s and Don’ts of Bima

Do:

  • Dress modestly to respect the local culture and beliefs
  • Try the local dishes and beverages
  • Take a boat ride to witness the stunning sunsets in Tanjung Ringgit

Don’t:

  • Disrespect the local customs and traditions
  • Engage in activities that harm the environment and wildlife
  • Leave trash or litter in public areas

Safety and Health

Safety in Bima

Bima is generally a safe destination for tourists. However, visitors should take precautions, such as avoiding isolated areas, keeping valuables out of sight, and staying informed about weather conditions.

Health in Bima

Travelers should take precautions against mosquito bites by using mosquito repellent and wearing long-sleeved clothing. Visitors should also be aware of the potential for waterborne diseases and should only drink bottled water.

Getting to Bima: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Bima

The nearest airport to Bima is Sultan Muhammad Kaharuddin III Airport, which is located around 30 kilometers from the town center. From the airport, visitors can take a taxi or rent a car to reach their destination.

Bus Stations Near Bima

Bima has two bus terminals, Bima Bus Terminal and Waepolo Bus Terminal, which offer frequent connections to neighboring towns and cities.

Other Options

Visitors can also take a ferry or boat to reach Bima from the neighboring island of Lombok.

How to Get Around in Bima

The most convenient way to get around in Bima is by renting a car or a motorcycle. Visitors can also hire a taxi or use public transportation, which includes bemos and angkutan (minibusses).

Where to Go Next from Bima: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Bima is an excellent base for exploring the surrounding islands and regions. Visitors can take a ferry to Lombok, a neighboring island known for its stunning beaches and surf spots. Another popular destination is the Komodo National Park, where visitors can witness the famous Komodo Dragons.

Conclusion

Bima is a hidden gem that offers a unique combination of natural, cultural, and historical attractions. From hiking to Mount Tambora to trying the traditional dishes of Bima, visitors are sure to have an unforgettable experience in this idyllic town in Eastern Indonesia.
