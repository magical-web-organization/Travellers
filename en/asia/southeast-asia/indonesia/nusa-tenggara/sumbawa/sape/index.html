Introduction to Sape

Sape is an under-the-radar destination located on the island of Sumbawa, Indonesia. Although it’s not as well-known as other places in the country, Sape is home to some of the most beautiful natural attractions, tasty local cuisine, and interesting cultural sites. If you’re looking for a refreshing getaway off the beaten track, this is the place to be.

List of Places to Visit in Sape

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Sape has a rich history that is worth exploring. A great way to understand its past better is to visit some of its museums and historical sites. Some of the interesting places to visit include:

  1. La Village - The famous traditional village that has an iconic drum-shaped house where locals perform traditional music and dance.

  2. Ketekesu Village - Home of the Sasak tribe, known for their beautiful ikat weaving.

  3. Kriyamaha Sembalun Agro Tourism Village - This village offers an insight into the rural lifestyle and agriculture economy of the area.

  4. Moyo Island - Known for its stunning beaches and traditional villages, Moyo Island is worth a visit if you have the time.

Natural Attractions of Sape: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

When it comes to natural attractions, Sape has an abundance of options. From hiking and trekking to waterfall chasing, you’ll be spoilt for choice. Here are some of the top natural attractions to put on your list:

  1. Tambora National Park - An active volcano that sits 2850 meters above sea level.

  2. Siti Batu Preservation Forest - A breathtaking natural forest that is home to some of Indonesia’s endangered wildlife.

  3. Lakey Beach - A beautiful spot for surfing, sunbathing, or snorkeling, Lakey Beach is a must-visit spot.

  4. Air Terjun Bilu and Bena waterfall - A picturesque waterfall located around an hour’s drive from Sape.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Sape

Sape has some of the best hiking and biking routes for outdoor enthusiasts looking to explore the natural beauty of the region. Here are some of the most popular routes to consider:

  1. Tambora Volcano Trek - This is a challenging trek that takes you to the top of Tambora, the highest active volcano on the island.

  2. Lombok Explorer - A biking tour that takes you through the countryside and offers some amazing natural landscapes.

Best Things to Do in Sape: Activities and Tours

Sape has something for everyone, from culture buffs and nature lovers to adventurers. Here are some of the best things to do:

  1. Snorkeling and diving - Sape is home to some of the most beautiful coral reefs and marine life in the region. Explore the underwater world through diving or snorkeling.

  2. Surfing - Lakey Beach is one of the best spots for surfing, and there are plenty of surfing schools in the region to take you to the best waves.

Week-Long Itinerary for Sape

A week in Sape will give you the opportunity to experience the region’s many attractions fully. Here’s a suggested itinerary:

Day 1: Explore Tambora National Park and take the Tambora Volcano Trek. Day 2: Head to Lakey Beach for some surfing, sunbathing, and snorkeling. Day 3: Visit the traditional La Village and Ketekesu Village. Day 4: Take a tour of Siti Batu Preservation Forest and explore the natural landscapes. Day 5: Spend a day in Moyo Island. Day 6: Visit the Kriyamaha Sembalun Agro Tourism Village for a glimpse of rural life and agriculture. Day 7: Relax and enjoy the local food and drink scene.

Food and Drink Scene of Sape

One of the unique aspects of Sape is its diverse cuisine, influenced by its mixed cultures. Here are some of the must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  1. Ayam Taliwang - A grilled chicken dish with spicy seasonings that originates from neighboring Lombok island.

  2. Sate Babi - Grilled pork skewers marinated in a sweet soy sauce.

Local Beverages

  1. Arak - A potent local rice liquor that is often mixed with fruit juice or soda.

  2. Kopi Kuali - A unique local coffee that is roasted in clay pots, resulting in a distinct flavor.

Where to Stay in Sape: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Sape has a variety of accommodation options available, ranging from budget hostels to luxurious resorts. Here are some of the best options to consider:

  1. Lakey Beach Inn - A budget-friendly hostel located close to the stunning Lakey Beach.

  2. Hotel Sinar Tambora - An affordable hotel that offers comfortable rooms and easy access to Tambora National Park.

  3. The Rinjani Lodge - A luxurious resort that offers stunning views of the nearby volcano and natural landscapes.

What to Buy in Sape: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

If you’re looking to bring a piece of Sape back home with you, there are several unique souvenirs and local products to consider, including:

  1. Handicrafts and traditional woven textiles, such as ikat or songket.

  2. Locally produced coffee or rice wine.

Local Culture and Customs of Sape

Sape is a region with a mixed culture, drawing influences from Sasak and Balinese culture. It is essential to respect local customs and etiquette while exploring the region. Here are some important things to keep in mind:

  1. Dress modestly, especially when visiting temples or other cultural sites.

  2. Remove your shoes before entering someone’s home.

Best Time to Visit Sape

The best time to visit Sape is during the dry season from May to October, when the weather is sunny and dry, making it easy to explore the outdoors. However, do note that temperatures can reach up to 30°C, so pack accordingly.

Do’s and Don’ts of Sape

Do:

  1. Embrace the local culture and explore traditional villages.

  2. Try the local cuisine and beverages.

Don’t:

  1. Disrespect local customs, especially at religious sites.

  2. Litter or damage the natural environment.

Safety and Health

Safety in Sape

Sape is generally a safe destination for tourists. However, it is advisable to take precautions, such as:

  1. Avoid solo travel at night, especially in quiet areas.

  2. Keep your valuables safe and secure while exploring.

Health in Sape

While in Sape, it is important to stay hydrated and protect yourself from the sun. Additionally, it’s important to take precautions against mosquito bites, as the region is prone to mosquito-borne diseases like dengue fever.

Getting to Sape: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Sape

The nearest airport is Sultan Muhammad Kaharuddin III Airport in Sumbawa Besar, which is around a four-hour drive from Sape.

Bus Stations Near Sape

There are several bus stations located around Sape that provide connections to other parts of Sumbawa Island and beyond.

Other Options

If you’re looking for a more comfortable and private transfer, consider hiring a car or taking a taxi from Sumbawa Besar.

How to Get Around in Sape

The easiest way to explore Sape is by hiring a car or motorbike. Additionally, there are taxis and public buses available for sparser routes.

Where to Go Next from Sape: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

If you’re looking to explore more of Sumbawa Island, there are several popular destinations to consider, including:

  1. Mount Rinjani National Park

  2. Komodo National Park

Conclusion

Sape may not be the most well-known destination in Indonesia, but it is a hidden gem worth exploring. From its stunning natural landscapes and picturesque villages to its diverse cuisine and rich cultural heritage, Sape has something to offer everyone. Plan your visit today and start exploring this unique corner of Indonesia.
