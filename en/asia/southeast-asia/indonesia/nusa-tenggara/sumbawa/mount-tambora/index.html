Introduction to Mount Tambora

Mount Tambora, a towering stratovolcano located on Sumbawa Island in Indonesia, is a must-visit destination for nature lovers and adventure seekers. This imposing natural wonder is known for its spectacular views, rich cultural heritage, and dramatic history. It gained worldwide attention in the early 19th century for the catastrophic eruption that wiped out entire villages and caused significant climate impacts worldwide. Since then, Mount Tambora has been a popular destination for hikers and explorers.

List of Places to Visit in Mount Tambora

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

If you’re fascinated by history and culture, several places in Mount Tambora are worth visiting. Here are some must-visit destinations:

  • Tambora Museum: The museum houses a collection of artifacts and photographs that document the history and stories of the 1815 eruption, as well as the local culture and traditions.

  • Old Town Bima: Located in the nearby town of Bima, this historic district offers a glimpse of the traditional way of life in Sumbawa. Here, you can see traditional architecture, visit local shops and markets, and watch cultural performances.

Natural Attractions of Mount Tambora: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Mount Tambora is a paradise for nature enthusiasts and adrenaline junkies. Here are some of the best natural attractions you shouldn’t miss:

  • Tambora National Park: This vast national park features diverse wildlife, stunning landscapes, and rare flora and fauna. It’s also home to several trekking routes that offer breathtaking views of the surrounding mountains and valleys.

  • Tambora beach: This beautiful and secluded beach is an ideal spot for swimming, relaxing, and enjoying stunning sunsets.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Mount Tambora

Mount Tambora offers several excellent hiking and biking routes that take you through dense forests, vast savannas, and scenic valleys. Here are some of the most popular trails:

  • Tambora Summit Trail: This challenging but rewarding route leads you to the summit of Mount Tambora, offering sweeping views of the surrounding landscapes.

  • Pondok Tangga Trail: This intermediate trek takes you through dense forests and steep inclines, leading to a hilltop offering panoramic views of the surrounding area.

Best Things to Do in Mount Tambora: Activities and Tours

Mount Tambora offers visitors several exciting activities and tours. Here are some of the best things to do:

  • Hiking and Trekking: Mount Tambora is a paradise for hikers and trekkers, with several trails of varying difficulty levels.

  • Cultural Tours: Visitors can explore the local culture and traditions through guided tours and visits to museums and historic sites.

Week-Long Itinerary for Mount Tambora

Here’s a sample itinerary for a week-long trip to Mount Tambora:

Day 1: Arrive in Bima, the nearest town to Mount Tambora, and rest. Day 2: Visit Tambora Museum and Old Town Bima. Day 3: Trek the Pondok Tangga Trail and enjoy the stunning views. Day 4: Relax at Tambora beach and watch the sunset. Day 5: Trek the Tambora Summit Trail and reach the top of Mount Tambora. Day 6: Explore Tambora National Park and its wildlife and flora. Day 7: Depart from Bima.

Food and Drink Scene of Mount Tambora

Must-Try Dishes

Sumbawa cuisine is known for its spicy, flavorful dishes. Here are some must-try dishes when in Mount Tambora:

  • Ayam Taliwang: A signature dish of Sumbawa, this spicy grilled chicken is served with chili sauce and rice.

  • Babi Guling: Grilled suckling pig served with rice and spicy sauce.

Local Beverages

Sumbawa is famous for its local coffee, which is grown in the nearby hills. You can also try traditional drinks like

  • Tuak: A sweet fermented drink made from palm sap.

Where to Stay in Mount Tambora: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

There are several accommodation options in Bima, the nearest town to Mount Tambora:

  • Luxury/Resorts: Some of the top luxury resorts in Bima include the Ayana Resort and Spa, the Novotel Lombok Resort and Villas, and the Qunci Villas Hotel.

  • Mid-Range: Mid-range options abound, such as the Hotel Santika Bima City, the Hotel D’mahitra, and the Hotel Lambitu.

  • Budget: Hostels and guesthouses are also available for budget travelers, such as the Hotel Bali Nusa Indah, the OYO 1868 Siwah Hotel, and the Eka Hotel.

What to Buy in Mount Tambora: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

There are several souvenirs and local products you can bring home from Mount Tambora, including:

  • Sumbawa Coffee: Bring home a bag or two of fresh, locally grown coffee.

  • Handicrafts and Weavings: Pick up some traditional handicrafts, such as woven baskets, mats, and textiles.

Local Culture and Customs of Mount Tambora

Sumbawa has a rich cultural heritage, with its own language, traditions, and beliefs. Some of the customs and traditions include:

  • Harvest Celebrations: The Sasak people, the indigenous population of Sumbawa, celebrate several harvest festivals throughout the year.

  • Traditional Dances: Sumbawa has several unique dance forms, such as Gendang Beleq, a traditional drum dance, and Rejang Dewa, a ritual dance.

Best Time to Visit Mount Tambora

The best time to visit Mount Tambora is during the dry season, from May to October. During this time, the weather is warm and sunny, and trekking conditions are optimal.

Do’s and Don’ts of Mount Tambora

Here are some essential do’s and don’ts when visiting Mount Tambora:

Do:

  • Respect local customs and traditions.
  • Make sure to carry enough water and snacks while trekking or hiking.
  • Wear comfortable and sturdy shoes.

Don’t:

  • Litter or damage the environment.
  • Disrespect the local culture or beliefs.
  • Leave valuables unattended.

Safety and Health

Safety in Mount Tambora

Mount Tambora is relatively safe for visitors, but it’s always advisable to take precautions, such as:

  • Trek or hike with a guide or group.
  • Avoid trekking or hiking alone, especially at night.
  • Check weather conditions and forecast before setting out.

Health in Mount Tambora

Sumbawa has several hospitals and clinics. However, it’s advisable to take necessary vaccinations before visiting and carry mosquito repellents and first aid kit.

Getting to Mount Tambora: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Mount Tambora

The nearest airport to Mount Tambora is Sultan Muhammad Kaharuddin III Airport in Bima. You can take a taxi or rent a car from the airport to reach the mountain.

Bus Stations Near Mount Tambora

Bima has a bus terminal where buses from nearby towns and cities arrive and depart. You can take a bus or minivan to reach the mountain from Bima.

Other Options

Many tour operators and travel agencies arrange tours and treks to Mount Tambora.

How to Get Around in Mount Tambora

The best way to get around in Mount Tambora is by car or motorcycle rental. Taxis and public transport are also available, though not frequently.

Where to Go Next from Mount Tambora: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

There are several nearby destinations you can explore from Mount Tambora, including:

  • Komodo Island: Visit the world-famous Komodo Dragon and explore the vibrant marine life surrounding the island.

  • Lombok Island: Explore the natural beauty and cultural heritage of nearby Lombok Island.

Conclusion

Whether you’re a nature lover, history buff, or culture enthusiast, Mount Tambora offers something for everyone. Plan your trip and experience the adventure and beauty of this Indonesian gem.
