Introduction to Ubud

Ubud is a town located in the central part of Bali, Indonesia. Known as the cultural heart of Bali, Ubud is a place of traditional crafts, religious sites, and rich history. It’s a perfect destination for those seeking to experience the serene and peaceful environment and escape the bustling cities.

List of Places to Visit in Ubud

Ubud has many fascinating tourist spots for visitors to enjoy. Here are some of the best places to visit in Ubud:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Ubud boasts several museums and historical sites that showcase the unique culture of Bali. Here are a few:

Agung Rai Museum of Art

This museum is the perfect place to see Balinese art, culture and architecture all in one place. It houses permanent collection of paintings, sculptures, traditional textiles, shadow puppets, and wood carvings.

Puri Saren Palace

The palace was the residence of the Balinese royalty in Ubud. It is surrounded by beautiful gardens and offers visitors a glimpse of the traditional Balinese architecture.

Sacred Monkey Forest Sanctuary

This sanctuary is home to over 600 Balinese long-tailed monkeys. It is also houses three ancient temples with historical and religious significance.

Natural Attractions of Ubud: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Ubud offers a variety of outdoor activities for nature lovers. Here are some of the best natural attractions:

Tegalalang Rice Terrace

One of the most popular tourist destinations in Ubud is the Tegalalang Rice Terrace. The green terraces create a spectacular landscape for visitors to marvel.

Campuhan Ridge Walk

For stunning views of the lush valleys and serene landscapes, take a walk along the Campuhan Ridge Trail.

Ayung River Rafting

Experience the thrill of white water rafting with spectacular views of the tropical forest while cruising along the Ayung River.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Ubud

Ubud offers excellent mountain biking and hiking trails for those seeking a little bit of adventure. Here are some of the most popular routes:

Mount Batur

Climb up the lush mountainside of Mount Batur to catch a breathtaking sunrise view of the Bali Island.

Ubud Rice Terrance Trekking Tour

Take a trekking tour through Ubud’s picturesque Rice Terrace and experience the traditional Bali countryside.

Best Things to Do in Ubud: Activities and Tours

Ubud offers a wide range of activities and tours that appeal to every kind of traveler. Here are just a few worth checking out:

Balinese Cooking

Take a traditional Balinese cooking course and learn how to cook the most delicious Balinese cuisine.

Cultural and Spiritual Tours

Experience the traditional and spiritual side of Bali with a variety of tours, including temples, ceremonies, and traditional performances.

Cycling Tour

Discover Bali’s charm and breathtaking beauty on a cycling tour through Ubud and its surrounding areas.

Food and Drink Scene of Ubud

Ubud has a vibrant and dynamic food and drink scene, with plenty of local and organic produce. Here are some of the amazing dishes and drinks you must try:

Must-Try Dishes

Babi Guling

Babi Guling, or Suckling Pig, is a favourite traditional Balinese food. It is usually served at large ceremonies or gatherings and consists of a whole roasted pig stuffed with spices and herbs.

Nasi Campur

This is a mixed rice dish that includes a variety of meat, vegetables, and spices. It’s a perfect introduction to Balinese cuisine.

Satay

Satay is a traditional Balinese dish that includes marinated and grilled meat with peanut sauce on the side.

Local Beverages

Bintang beer

A local beer that is famous amongst tourists coming to Bali. The beer is light and refreshing.

Kopi Luwak

Kopi Luwak is the famous coffee made out of Civet’s feces. It is an exotic Balinese coffee and is reputed to be the most expensive coffee in the world.

What to Buy in Ubud: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Ubud is famous for its handicrafts and traditional arts. Here are some of the best things to buy as a souvenir:

Handmade Batik Fabric and Clothes

Batik is Indonesia’s traditional textile art, and Ubud is a perfect place to buy handmade batik fabrics and clothing.

Silver Jewelry

Ubud is also popular for its handcrafted silver jewelry; there are many shops and workshops where you buy unique designs and styles.

Local Culture and Customs of Ubud

Ubud has a rich and vibrant culture that visitors must respect and appreciate. Here are some cultural and customs facts to keep in mind:

  • Bali is mostly Hindu, and thus, many landmarks celebrate Hinduism
  • The Balinese people practice various traditional ceremonies and daily offerings to their gods.
  • The Balinese people are friendly and hospitable, so it is essential to respect them along with their culture.

Best Time to Visit Ubud

The best time to visit Ubud is between May and July, during Bali’s dry season. Good weather makes it ideal for outdoor activities and tourism.

Do’s and Don’ts of Ubud

Do:

  • Try local food and drinks
  • Respect the Hindu traditions and culture
  • Experience Balinese culture by attending performances and participating in cultural events

Don’t:

  • Get involved in political discussions
  • Wear revealing clothing at religious sites
  • Interrupt religious services

Safety and Health

Safety in Ubud

Ubud is a generally safe town for tourists. However, visitors should take necessary precautions to avoid any mishaps.

  • Avoid late-night walking on dimly lit streets
  • Always keep an eye on your belongings, especially during busy tourist seasons.

Health in Ubud

It is advisable to take Malaria pills if you are visiting the remote places of Bali. Do not drink the tap water, and bring sunscreen and insect repellent to protect yourself from the heat and mosquitos.

Getting to Ubud: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Ubud

Ngurah Rai International Airport, Bali, is the closest airport to Ubud.

Bus Stations Near Ubud

The Perama Tour and Travel agency has buses departing frequently to Ubud from Kuta, Seminyak, and other Bali destinations.

Other Options

Private taxis and rental cars or motorbikes are also available options to travel within Bali.

How to Get Around in Ubud

The best way to experience Ubud is on foot or bike. Renting a scooter is also an option as it is a popular mode of transportation in Bali.
