Introduction to Nusa Lembongan

Nusa Lembongan is a small island located southeast of Bali, Indonesia. Despite its small size, it is a popular tourist destination due to its stunning natural beauty, pristine beaches, and rich cultural heritage. Visitors can enjoy a variety of outdoor activities like surfing, snorkeling, and hiking, as well as explore the island’s vibrant arts and music scene. If you’re planning a trip to Nusa Lembongan, this comprehensive tour guide will help you make the most of your visit.

List of Places to Visit in Nusa Lembongan

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Nusa Lembongan has a rich cultural history, and there are several museums and historical sites on the island that offer visitors a glimpse into its past. Some of the must-visit attractions in this category are:

  • Pura Puncak Sari Temple: A beautiful Balinese Hindu temple with stunning views of the island from atop a hill.
  • Gala-Gala Underground House: An intricate network of tunnels and caves that were hand-carved by a local man as a place to hide during times of war.
  • Nusa Lembongan Mangrove Forest: A protected area that encompasses lush mangroves and a unique species of jellyfish found only in this area.

Natural Attractions of Nusa Lembongan: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

One of the main draws of Nusa Lembongan is its breathtaking natural beauty. From pristine beaches to lush jungles and crystal-clear waters, there is no shortage of outdoor adventures to be had on the island. Some of the top natural attractions to visit include:

  • Dream Beach: A secluded beach with powdery white sand and turquoise waters surrounded by soaring cliffs.
  • Devil’s Tear: A dramatic cliffside with crashing waves that create a spectacular spray and mist.
  • Blue Lagoon: A calm, crystal-clear bay perfect for snorkeling and diving with an abundance of colorful marine life.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Nusa Lembongan

For those who love to explore on foot or bike, Nusa Lembongan offers several scenic routes that offer stunning views of the island’s natural beauty. Some of the top hiking and biking routes to check out include:

  • Ceningan Ridge Walk: A scenic walking route with panoramic views of the island’s jungles, beaches, and coral reefs.
  • Mangrove Forest Trail: A nature trail that winds through the island’s protected mangrove forests and is home to a variety of wildlife.
  • Jungut Batu Beach to Mushroom Beach: A scenic beach walk that takes you along the island’s stunning coastline with breathtaking views at every turn.

Best Things to Do in Nusa Lembongan: Activities and Tours

Beyond the natural and cultural attractions, Nusa Lembongan also offers visitors a variety of activities and tours to keep them entertained. These are some of the most popular things to do on the island:

  • Surfing: The island’s waves are famous among surfers, with breaks suitable for all levels of experience from beginner to advanced.
  • Snorkeling and Diving: Nusa Lembongan is home to a vibrant coral reef ecosystem, where visitors can see a wide variety of colorful marine life.
  • Yoga: There are several studios on the island that offer yoga classes in stunning outdoor settings, perfect for a relaxing retreat.

Week-Long Itinerary for Nusa Lembongan

With so much to see and do in Nusa Lembongan, it can be tough to know where to start. Here’s a week-long itinerary that will help you make the most of your time on the island:

Day Activity
1 Arrive and settle in. Relax on the beach and enjoy the sunset.
2 Take a snorkeling or diving tour of the island’s coral reef ecosystem.
3 Rent a bike and explore the island’s scenic routes. Stop at scenic attractions like Dream Beach and Devil’s Tear.
4 Visit the island’s cultural attractions, like Pura Puncak Sari Temple and Gala-Gala Underground House.
5 Learn to surf with a local surf school.
6 Take a yoga class in one of the island’s scenic outdoor studios.
7 Spend the day relaxing on the beach and soaking up the sun before departing.

Food and Drink Scene of Nusa Lembongan

Must-Try Dishes

Nusa Lembongan is known for its delicious Indonesian cuisine, with a variety of local specialties that should not be missed. Some of the must-try dishes on the island include:

  • Nasi Campur: A traditional Indonesian dish that is a mix of rice, veggies, and meat.
  • Satay: A popular street food dish made of grilled meat on skewers served with peanut sauce.
  • Gado Gado: A salad made with steamed vegetables, tofu, and peanut sauce.

Local Beverages

Indonesia is famous for its coffee, and Nusa Lembongan is no exception. Visitors can find a variety of local coffee shops selling delicious brews. Other local beverages to try include:

  • Bintang: A popular Indonesian beer often enjoyed on the beach.
  • Jamu: A traditional Indonesian herbal drink made from natural ingredients like turmeric and ginger.

Where to Stay in Nusa Lembongan: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Nusa Lembongan offers a variety of accommodations to suit every type of traveler, from luxury resorts to budget-friendly guesthouses and hostels. Some of the top accommodations on the island include:

  • Indiana Kenanga Luxury Villas Spa & Restaurant: A luxury resort with private villas, a spa, and a seafood restaurant.
  • Hai Tide Beach Resort: A beachfront resort with stunning ocean views and a variety of water activities.
  • The Cubang Hut’s Lembongan: A budget-friendly guesthouse with simple, clean rooms and friendly staff.

What to Buy in Nusa Lembongan: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

When visiting Nusa Lembongan, there are several souvenirs and local products that make for great gifts or keepsakes. These include:

  • Seashell jewelry: The island is famous for its variety of seashells, which are often turned into beautiful jewelry pieces.
  • Batik clothing: A traditional Indonesian fabric, batik is used to make clothing and other textiles often sold in local shops.
  • Coffee: Nusa Lembongan has several coffee shops that sell locally grown and roasted coffee beans.

Local Culture and Customs of Nusa Lembongan

Indonesia is a diverse country with a rich cultural heritage, and Nusa Lembongan is no exception. Visitors should be respectful of local customs and traditions, like covering shoulders and knees when visiting temples, and being mindful of noise levels in residential areas.

Best Time to Visit Nusa Lembongan

The best time to visit Nusa Lembongan is from April to October when the weather is dry and sunny. However, keep in mind that this is peak tourist season, so prices will be higher, and crowds will be larger.

Do’s and Don’ts of Nusa Lembongan

Do:

  • Do try local cuisine and drinks
  • Do explore the island’s natural and cultural attractions
  • Do engage with locals and learn about their culture

Don’t:

  • Don’t litter or damage the natural environment
  • Don’t disrespect local customs or traditions
  • Don’t pet or disturb local wildlife

Safety and Health

Safety in Nusa Lembongan

Nusa Lembongan is a generally safe place to visit, with a low crime rate. Visitors should take basic precautions like securing valuables and being aware of their surroundings.

Health in Nusa Lembongan

Visitors should be cautious about drinking tap water and be sure to bring insect repellent to protect against mosquitos, which can carry diseases like dengue and malaria.

Getting to Nusa Lembongan: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Nusa Lembongan

The nearest airport to Nusa Lembongan is Ngurah Rai International Airport in Bali. From here, visitors can take a ferry or speedboat to the island, which takes around 30 minutes.

Bus Stations Near Nusa Lembongan

There are no bus stations on Nusa Lembongan. However, visitors can take a taxi or shuttle bus from Bali to one of the island’s ferry terminals.

Other Options

For those looking to avoid the crowds, Nusa Lembongan can also be accessed by private boat or yacht, rented from Bali.

How to Get Around in Nusa Lembongan

Nusa Lembongan is a small island that can easily be explored on foot or by bicycle. However, visitors can also rent a scooter or take advantage of local taxis or shuttle buses.

Where to Go Next from Nusa Lembongan: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

If you’re looking to extend your trip beyond Nusa Lembongan, there are several nearby destinations worth exploring, including:

  • Nusa Penida: A nearby island that is home to stunning cliffs, crystal-clear waters, and wildlife like manta rays and sea turtles.
  • Bali: Indonesia’s most famous island is just a short ferry ride away and offers a wealth of cultural and natural attractions.
  • Lombok: A nearby island known for its beautiful beaches and natural attractions like waterfalls and volcanoes.

Conclusion

Nusa Lembongan is a beautiful and unique destination that offers visitors a wealth of natural and cultural attractions to explore. Whether you’re looking to relax on the beach or engage with local culture and traditions, this island has something for everyone. Use this comprehensive tour guide to plan your trip and make the most of your visit to Nusa Lembongan.
