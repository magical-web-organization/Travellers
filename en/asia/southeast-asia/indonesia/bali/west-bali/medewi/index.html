Introduction to Medewi

Medewi is a small fishing village located on the west coast of Bali. Known for its world-renowned waves and stunning sunsets, it is the perfect location for travelers looking to escape the crowds and find a more relaxing side of Bali. While the area is lesser-known than other parts of Bali, Medewi offers lush landscapes, rugged coastlines, and rich cultural experiences.

List of Places to Visit in Medewi

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Yeh Embang Temple: This temple was built in the 11th century and is dedicated to the sea goddess. Located on the coast, it offers breathtaking views and a unique spiritual experience.
  • Pura Rambut Siwi Temple: Another important temple in the area is Pura Rambut Siwi. This is the largest temple in West Bali and is known for its impressive architecture and peaceful atmosphere.
  • Medewi Bay Fishing Village: Get a glimpse into the daily life of the locals by visiting the Medewi Bay fishing village. Here, you can watch fishermen in action and even try your hand at traditional fishing techniques.

Natural Attractions of Medewi: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Medewi Beach: Surfing enthusiasts flock to Medewi Beach for its world-class waves. But even if you’re not a surfer, the stunning sunset views and tranquil atmosphere make it a great spot to relax and recharge.
  • Jatiluwih Rice Terraces: Located just a short drive from Medewi, the Jatiluwih Rice Terraces are a UNESCO World Heritage site and offer breathtaking views of Bali’s lush green landscapes.
  • Medewi Canyon: For the adrenaline junkies, exploring the Medewi Canyon is a must-do activity. With waterfalls, natural pools, and challenging cliff jumps, it’s the perfect place to get your blood pumping.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Medewi

  • Mount Batukaru Trek: For the experienced hikers, the Mount Batukaru Trek offers a challenging ascent to the peak of this active volcano. From the summit, you’ll be rewarded with panoramic views of Bali.
  • Butterfly Park and Botanical Garden Trail: For a more leisurely hike, the Butterfly Park and Botanical Garden Trail offers a scenic nature walk with stunning botanical and butterfly exhibits.

Best Things to Do in Medewi: Activities and Tours

  • Medewi Surf Lesson: Experience the thrill of surfing with a professional lesson at Medewi Beach. Suitable for beginners and experienced surfers alike, this activity is a must-do in Medewi.
  • West Bali National Park Tour: Get up close and personal with Bali’s stunning wildlife, including deer, monkeys, and sea turtles, by taking a tour of the West Bali National Park.
  • Watersport Activities: Kayaking, snorkeling, and stand-up paddleboarding are just a few of the water sports available in Medewi. With calm and clear waters, it’s the perfect place to try something new.

Food and Drink Scene of Medewi

Must-Try Dishes

  • Sate Lilit: This local dish is made from minced fish and spices, formed into a sausage shape, and grilled on a stick. It’s a flavor-packed snack that you won’t want to miss.
  • Nasi Goreng: A staple of Indonesian cuisine, Nasi Goreng is a fried rice dish with a delicious mix of spices, meat or shrimp, and vegetables.

Local Beverages

  • Bintang: This local lager is a perfect pairing for a day on the beach or a relaxing evening at your accommodation.
  • Kopi Luwak: This coffee is made from beans that have been digested and excreted by civet cats, resulting in a unique and flavorful brew.

Where to Stay in Medewi: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Medewi Beach Cottage: Located just a stone’s throw from the beach, these cottages are the perfect place to relax and enjoy the tranquil atmosphere.
  • Bali Lush: Offering stunning ocean views and a pool, this guesthouse is the perfect place to unwind after a day of exploring.
  • Medewi Bay Retreat: Nestled in a lush garden, this retreat offers peaceful accommodations with an emphasis on sustainability and community.

What to Buy in Medewi: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Traditional Weavings: Bali is known for its beautiful and intricate woven fabrics, and Medewi offers some great opportunities to purchase handcrafted pieces.
  • Incense and Essential Oils: Take a piece of Bali’s fragrant atmosphere home with you by purchasing some of the locally-made incense or essential oils.

Local Culture and Customs of Medewi

Bali is known for its rich cultural heritage, and Medewi is no exception. While exploring this area, it’s important to be respectful of the locals and their customs. This means dressing modestly when visiting temples, removing your shoes before entering a home or temple, and being mindful of noise levels in residential areas.

Best Time to Visit Medewi

The best time to visit Medewi is during the dry season, which runs from April to October. During this time, you’ll be able to make the most of Medewi’s outdoor activities without worrying about rain or humidity.

Do’s and Don’ts of Medewi

Do:

  • Respect the local culture and customs.
  • Try the local dishes and beverages.
  • Explore the stunning nature and landscapes of the area.

Don’t:

  • Wear revealing clothing when visiting temples.
  • Disrespect the environment by littering or damaging natural landmarks.
  • Photograph locals without their permission.

Safety and Health

Safety in Medewi

Medewi is a relatively safe area, but travelers should still be cautious and take basic safety precautions. These include keeping an eye on personal belongings, being aware of surroundings, and avoiding unlit areas at night.

Health in Medewi

The tap water in Medewi is not suitable for drinking, so travelers should stick to bottled water. Additionally, it’s always a good idea to pack insect repellent to protect against mosquito-borne illnesses like dengue fever and malaria.

Getting to Medewi: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Medewi

The closest airport to Medewi is Ngurah Rai International Airport (DPS) in Denpasar. From there, it’s a 2-3 hour drive to Medewi.

Bus Stations Near Medewi

Buses and minivans run regularly from Denpasar and other major cities to West Bali, including Medewi.

Other Options

Alternatively, travelers can also rent a car or scooter to get around the area. However, be aware that driving in Bali can be challenging for inexperienced drivers.

How to Get Around in Medewi

The best way to get around Medewi is by scooter or bicycle. These can be rented from one of the many rental shops in the area. Walking is also an option for those who prefer to take things at a slower pace.

Where to Go Next from Medewi: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Menjangan Island: Located just a short boat ride from West Bali, Menjangan Island is a small, uninhabited island known for its incredible snorkeling and diving.
  • Pemuteran: Just north of Medewi, Pemuteran is a quiet beach town with a thriving diving industry and stunning coral reefs.
  • Ubud: For a change of pace, head to Ubud, Bali’s cultural center. With its stunning rice terraces, traditional performances, and art galleries, it’s a great place to soak up Bali’s rich history and culture.

Conclusion

Medewi offers a perfect blend of adventure, culture, and relaxation, making it a great destination for those looking to experience a different side of Bali. With its stunning natural landscapes, rich cultural heritage, and welcoming locals, Medewi is a must-visit for any traveler exploring Bali.
