North Bali is a unique travel destination located on the island of Bali in Indonesia. The region is known for its rich cultural heritage, stunning natural attractions, and warm hospitality. If you are planning a trip to North Bali, this comprehensive travel guide will help you make the most of your visit.

Introduction to North Bali

North Bali is less developed than other parts of Bali, but that is what makes it attractive to many visitors. The region is known for its serene ambiance, a slower pace of life, and authentic Balinese culture. North Bali comprises of the districts of Buleleng and Singaraja, and it is home to many charming towns and villages. It is located about 80 kilometers from Bali’s Ngurah Rai International Airport.

List of Places to Visit in North Bali

North Bali has something for everyone, from culture enthusiasts to adventure seekers. Here are some of the top places to visit in North Bali:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Brahmavihara Arama Buddhist Monastery: This stunning Buddhist temple is located on a hilltop and offers excellent views of the surrounding countryside.
  • Sangsit Village: This small village is home to Pura Beji, a beautiful Hindu temple with intricate carvings depicting different aspects of Balinese life.
  • Singaraja: This former Dutch colonial capital is a bustling town with a long history of trade and culture. You can visit Gedong Kirtya, a museum dedicated to Balinese literature, and the Singaraja Heritage Walk, a self-guided tour of the town’s historic buildings.

Natural Attractions of North Bali: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Lovina Beach: This black sand beach is known for its calm waters and the chance to see wild dolphins in their natural habitat.
  • Gitgit Waterfall: This 35-meter waterfall is located in the middle of Bali’s lush jungle and is a popular spot for photos and swimming.
  • Mount Batur: Take a sunrise trek up Bali’s most famous volcano, and you’ll be rewarded with stunning views of the sunrise.

Hiking/Biking Routes of North Bali

North Bali is a nature lover’s paradise, and there are many opportunities for hiking and biking. Here are some of the best routes to explore:

  • Sekumpul Waterfall Trek: This challenging trek takes you through several stunning waterfalls, including the highest one in Bali, with an altitude of 80 meters.
  • Jatiluwih Rice Terrace Walk: This easy hike takes you through Bali’s famous rice terraces, with beautiful views of the surrounding mountains and jungle.
  • Bali Jungle Mountain Trek: This challenging trek takes you through Bali’s lush jungle and up to Mount Lesung, with panoramic views of the island.

Best Things to Do in North Bali: Activities and Tours

There is a lot to see and do in North Bali beyond just the cultural attractions and natural wonders. Here are some of the best tours and activities to add to your itinerary:

  • Snorkeling and Diving: North Bali has some of the best snorkeling and diving sites in Bali, including the beautiful Menjangan Island.
  • Traditional Cooking Class: Learn how to cook authentic Balinese dishes, such as Nasi Goreng and Sate Lilit, from a local family.
  • Balinese Massage: Relax and unwind with a traditional Balinese massage or spa treatment.

Week-Long Itinerary for North Bali

If you’re planning a week-long trip to North Bali, here’s a suggested itinerary:

Day Activity
Day 1 Relax and explore Lovina Beach, then take a sunset dolphin watching tour.
Day 2 Experience traditional Balinese culture with a visit to Singaraja and the Brahmavihara Arama Buddhist Monastery.
Day 3 Trek to Sekumpul Waterfall in the morning, then relax in the afternoon with a Balinese massage.
Day 4 Dive or snorkel at Menjangan Island, followed by a sunset bike ride through the rice paddies.
Day 5 Take a cooking class and learn how to prepare a traditional Balinese feast.
Day 6 Trek Mount Batur for sunrise, followed by a visit to Gitgit Waterfall.
Day 7 Visit local markets to buy souvenirs and relax on the beach before departing North Bali.

Food and Drink Scene of North Bali

North Bali offers a unique culinary experience that blends Balinese, Indonesian, and international flavors.

Must-Try Dishes

  • Sate Lilit: Minced fish or meat mixed with coconut, lime, and spices, skewered and grilled over charcoal.
  • Nasi Campur: A mixed rice dish served with various side dishes such as vegetables, meat, and sambal.
  • Jajanan Pasar: Traditional Balinese sweets made from rice flour, coconut, and palm sugar.

Local Beverages

  • Bali Coffee: Grown in the highlands of northern Bali, Bali coffee is a full-bodied, aromatic coffee with a unique taste.
  • Arak: A traditional Balinese liquor made from fermented rice or palm sap.

Where to Stay in North Bali: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

North Bali offers a range of accommodations to suit every budget and preference. Here are some of the best options:

  • Adirama Beach Hotel: A beachfront hotel with traditional Balinese architecture and a laid-back ambiance.
  • Munduk Moding Plantation Nature Resort & Spa: A luxury resort in the heart of Bali’s jungle, with stunning views of the surrounding rice paddies.
  • Bali Lovina Beach Cottages: A budget-friendly option located right on Lovina Beach, with ocean views and a swimming pool.

What to Buy in North Bali: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

North Bali is known for its unique arts and crafts, including wood carvings, silver jewelry, and traditional textiles. Here are some of the best places to shop for souvenirs in North Bali:

  • Krisna Oleh-Oleh: A chain of souvenir shops selling Balinese crafts, clothing, and snacks.
  • Seririt Market: A traditional market selling fresh produce, spices, and locally made handicrafts.

Local Culture and Customs of North Bali

Balinese culture is deeply rooted in tradition and religion, and visitors are expected to show respect for local customs and beliefs. The island has a unique Hindu culture, and you’ll often see offerings, ceremonies, and festivals taking place throughout North Bali.

Best Time to Visit North Bali

The best time to visit North Bali is during the dry season from April to October. The weather is sunny and warm during the day, with cooler temperatures at night. However, Bali can be visited year-round as the weather is relatively stable.

Do’s and Don’ts of North Bali

Do:

  • Dress modestly when visiting temples and other religious sites.
  • Take your shoes off when entering someone’s home.
  • Try Balinese food and drinks.

Don’t:

  • Touch someone’s head, as it is considered sacred in Balinese culture.
  • Disrespect Indonesian laws, such as drug possession or trafficking.
  • Take photos of people without asking for their permission.

Safety and Health

Safety in North Bali

North Bali is generally a safe destination, but visitors should still take normal precautions to keep themselves and their belongings safe. Petty theft and burglaries do occur, especially in busy tourist areas, so keep an eye on valuables and be careful when traveling at night.

Health in North Bali

Bali is a tropical destination, so visitors should take precautions against mosquito-borne illnesses such as malaria and dengue fever. It’s also a good idea to have travel insurance that covers medical emergencies.

Getting to North Bali: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near North Bali

The closest airport to North Bali is Ngurah Rai International Airport in Denpasar. From the airport, you can take a taxi or hire a driver to get to North Bali.

Bus Stations Near North Bali

There are bus services connecting North Bali with other parts of Bali, such as Kuta and Ubud. However, bus travel is not recommended due to safety concerns.

Other Options

Another option to get to North Bali is to hire a car and driver to take you there. This is a popular option for those who want to explore Bali’s countryside at their own pace.

How to Get Around in North Bali

The best way to get around North Bali is by hiring a driver or renting a scooter. Taxis and ride-hailing services are also available, but they are less common compared to other parts of Bali.

Where to Go Next from North Bali: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

North Bali is a great starting point for exploring other parts of Bali. Here are some of the top destinations to visit after North Bali:

  • Ubud: Known for its traditional arts and crafts, yoga studios, and holistic wellness centers.
  • Seminyak: A popular upscale resort town with stylish beach clubs, boutiques, and restaurants.
  • Sidemen: A peaceful town located in Bali’s rural heartland, surrounded by rice paddies and mountains.

Conclusion

North Bali is a hidden gem that offers a unique Balinese experience. Whether you’re looking to explore cultural attractions, natural wonders, or just relax on the beach, North Bali has something to offer everyone. Use this comprehensive travel guide to plan your visit and make the most of your time in this beautiful part of the world.
