Introduction to Singaraja

Singaraja is a charming coastal city located in North Bali, Indonesia. It used to be the capital of Bali before it was moved to Denpasar. Despite being a lesser-known destination in Bali, it offers visitors a diverse range of cultural and natural attractions. Travelers who venture to Singaraja will find stunning natural landscapes, significant historical sites, and a unique local culture. This travel guide aims to provide you with all the necessary information to plan your trip to Singaraja, Bali.

List of Places to Visit in Singaraja

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

The Museum Buleleng

Located in the heart of downtown Singaraja, the Museum Buleleng is a must-visit attraction for those interested in Bali’s rich history and culture. The museum houses a collection of traditional Balinese artifacts, including ancient weapons, musical instruments, and textiles.

Brahma Vihara Arama Buddhist Monastery

Brahma Vihara Arama Buddhist Monastery is the largest monastery in Bali and is located on top of a hill in the village of Banjar. Visitors can enjoy panoramic views of the surrounding rice terraces and mountains, as well as explore the ornate temple buildings and tranquil meditation rooms.

Pura Meduwe Karang

Located in the village of Kubutambahan, Pura Meduwe Karang is one of the oldest temples in Bali, dating back to the Majapahit era. The intricate carvings and sprawling courtyard make it a popular destination for locals and tourists alike.

Natural Attractions of Singaraja: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Gitgit Waterfall

Gitgit Waterfall is a stunning 40-meter waterfall located in the village of Gitgit. Visitors can hike down to the base of the waterfall and cool off in the refreshing pool. The surrounding area is also an excellent spot for photography.

Lovina Beach

Lovina Beach is a black sand beach popular for its calm waters and dolphins. Visitors can take a boat ride in the early morning to see the playful dolphins jump out of the water. There are also many beachside cafes and bars that offer fresh seafood and drinks.

Munduk Waterfall

Munduk Waterfall is a hidden gem located in the village of Munduk. It is accessible by a short hike through the lush rice paddies and jungle. The waterfall is smaller than Gitgit, but it is equally impressive and has a serene atmosphere.

Hiking/Biking Routes in Singaraja

Singaraja offers many hiking and biking experiences that allow visitors to explore Bali’s natural beauty up close. Some popular trails include Mount Batur Sunrise Trek, Munduk Rice Terraces Trek, and Jatiluwih Rice Terraces Bike Tour. These tours are usually led by experienced guides, and they provide information on the local flora and fauna.

Best Things to Do in Singaraja: Activities and Tours

Dolphin Watching Tour

The Dolphin Watching Tour is a popular activity in Singaraja, and it takes place at sunrise. Visitors can witness the playful dolphins jumping and swimming in the calm waters of the Bali Sea.

Banjar Hot Springs

Banjar Hot Springs is a natural hot spring located in the village of Banjar. Visitors can relax and soak in the warm waters while admiring the lush rainforest scenery.

Sekumpul Waterfall Tour

Sekumpul Waterfall is a collection of seven waterfalls located in the village of Sekumpul. Visitors can book guided tours that take them through the dense jungle and gorgeous rice terraces to see these magnificent waterfalls.

Week-Long Itinerary for Singaraja

Here is a suggested week-long itinerary for those visiting Singaraja, Bali:

  • Day 1: Visit the Museum Buleleng and Pura Meduwe Karang.
  • Day 2: Go on a Munduk Rice Terraces Trek.
  • Day 3: Explore the Brahma Vihara Arama Buddhist Monastery and Banjar Hot Springs.
  • Day 4: Take a Dolphin Watching Tour and spend the day at Lovina Beach.
  • Day 5: Go on a Sekumpul Waterfall Tour.
  • Day 6: Relax at your hotel and enjoy the local food and drinks.
  • Day 7: Take a day trip to nearby destinations like Ubud or Seminyak.

Food and Drink Scene of Singaraja

Must-Try Dishes

Bebek Betutu

Bebek Betutu is a Bali-style roasted duck that has been marinated in a blend of spices and herbs. It is usually served with rice and vegetables and makes for a flavorful and filling meal.

Babi Guling

Babi Guling is a Balinese-style roast pig that has been stuffed with a mixture of spices and vegetables. It is a local specialty and is usually served with rice and sambal sauce.

Sate Lilit

Sate Lilit is a type of Balinese satay made from minced meat mixed with grated coconut, garlic, ginger, and spices. It is served on skewers and makes for a delicious and spicy snack.

Local Beverages

Bintang Beer

Bintang Beer is the most popular beer in Bali, and it is a refreshing drink to enjoy after a day of sightseeing or outdoor adventures.

Kopi Luwak

Kopi Luwak is a unique coffee that is made from coffee beans that have been digested and excreted by civet cats. While it may sound unappetizing, it is said to be one of the best-tasting coffees in the world.

  • The Lovina Bali Resort
  • The Damai
  • Aditya Beach Resort

What to Buy in Singaraja: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Handicrafts

Singaraja is known for its handmade crafts and textiles. Visitors can browse the shops and markets to find unique items like wooden carvings, batik fabrics, and handwoven baskets.

Local Products

Singaraja is also known for its coffee and spices, which make for great souvenirs or gifts to bring home. Visitors can buy locally-made products like Kopi Luwak, Balinese vanilla, and Balinese sea salt.

Local Culture and Customs of Singaraja

Balinese culture is deeply rooted in religion, and visitors should show respect for local customs and traditions. Visitors should dress appropriately when visiting temples and other religious sites and consider hiring a local guide for insights into the culture.

Best Time to Visit Singaraja

The best time to visit Singaraja is during the dry season, which falls between April and October. The weather is pleasant, and the skies are clear, making it ideal for outdoor activities.

Do’s and Don’ts of Singaraja

Do:

  • Dress appropriately when visiting temples and other religious sites.
  • Respect local customs and traditions.
  • Hire a local guide for insights into the culture.

Don’t:

  • Show public displays of affection, as it is considered disrespectful.
  • Point with your feet, as it is considered impolite.

Safety and Health

Safety in Singaraja

Singaraja is generally safe for visitors, but it is always a good idea to take the necessary precautions like not walking alone at night and keeping valuables in a safe place.

Health in Singaraja

Visitors should be up-to-date with their vaccinations before visiting Bali. It is also recommended to drink bottled water and use insect repellent to prevent mosquito-borne illnesses like dengue fever.

Getting to Singaraja: Transportation Options and Logistics

Singaraja is located about 90 kilometers north of Denpasar, the capital of Bali. Visitors can reach Singaraja by:

Airports Near Singaraja

The closest airport to Singaraja is Ngurah Rai International Airport in Denpasar. From there, visitors can take a taxi or private car to Singaraja.

Bus Stations Near Singaraja

Singaraja has two major bus terminals: Gilimanuk Bus Terminal and Mengwi Bus Terminal. Visitors can take a bus from the terminals to Singaraja.

Other Options

Visitors can also rent a car or hire a private driver to reach Singaraja from other parts of Bali.

How to Get Around in Singaraja

Singaraja is a relatively small city, and many of the attractions are located within walking distance. Visitors can also rent a bicycle or motorbike to explore the surrounding areas.

Where to Go Next from Singaraja: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Singaraja is the perfect place to explore the northern part of Bali, and visitors can plan day trips to nearby destinations like:

  • Bedugul
  • Menjangan Island
  • Lovina

Conclusion

Singaraja is an off-the-beaten-path gem that offers visitors a unique glimpse into Bali’s rich culture, history, and natural beauty. Travelers who venture to Singaraja will find themselves enchanted with its tranquil atmosphere, stunning landscapes, delicious food, and warm locals.
