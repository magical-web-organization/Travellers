Introduction to Tanah Lot

Tanah Lot is a popular tourist destination in Bali, Indonesia, known for its stunning seaside temple perched on a rocky outcrop surrounded by the Indian Ocean. The temple, which was built in the 15th century, is dedicated to the sea gods and is one of the seven sea temples in Bali.

Apart from the temple, Tanah Lot is also home to many natural and cultural attractions, making it an ideal destination for travelers seeking a mix of adventure, relaxation, and cultural experiences. In this guide, we’ll explore the best places to visit, things to do, where to stay, and more in Tanah Lot.

List of Places to Visit in Tanah Lot

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Tanah Lot Temple: The main attraction of Tanah Lot, this temple offers spectacular views of the ocean and the sunset. Visitors can explore the temple complex and witness the daily rituals performed by the temple priests.

  • Puri Agung Tabanan: A historical royal palace located in Tabanan, which showcases the royal Balinese architecture and is surrounded by beautiful gardens and water features.

  • Taman Ayun Temple: A beautiful Hindu temple in Mengwi, built in the 17th century, which features stunning wooden carvings and traditional Balinese architecture.

  • Bali Museum: Located in Denpasar, this museum showcases the history and culture of Bali, including traditional art, music, and dance.

Natural Attractions of Tanah Lot: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Tanah Lot Beach: A beautiful beach with black sand, perfect for swimming, sunbathing, and surfing.

  • Alas Kedaton Monkey Forest: A beautiful forest sanctuary that is home to hundreds of monkeys and several Hindu temples.

  • Jatiluwih Rice Terraces: The largest rice terrace in Bali, located in a village in Tabanan, that offers stunning views of the green fields and mountain backdrop.

  • Mount Batukaru: An active volcano that offers scenic views of the surrounding hills and forests.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Tanah Lot

  • Batukaru Nature Reserve: This reserve offers hiking trails through a pristine rainforest, exotic wildlife, and the opportunity to explore some of the island’s more remote areas.

  • Bali Jungle Trekking: An eco-friendly company that provides guided tours and treks through the beautiful Balinese jungle.

Best Things to Do in Tanah Lot: Activities and Tours

  • Sunset Dinner at Tanah Lot: Experience breathtaking sunsets while enjoying Balinese cuisine and live traditional dance performances.

  • Tanah Lot Seafood Dinner: Savor fresh seafood dishes in restaurants near the temple and enjoy the stunning views of the ocean.

  • Surfing: Tanah Lot Beach is a popular spot for surfing, with challenging waves that are suitable for experienced surfers.

  • Balinese Massage: Relax and rejuvenate with a traditional Balinese massage, using techniques that have been passed down through generations.

Week-Long Itinerary for Tanah Lot

Day Activities
1 Visit Tanah Lot Temple and watch the sunset.
2 Explore Puri Agung Tabanan and Taman Ayun Temple.
3 Go surfing at Tanah Lot Beach.
4 Take a guided tour of Jatiluwih Rice Terraces.
5 Hike in Batukaru Nature Reserve.

Food and Drink Scene of Tanah Lot

Must-Try Dishes

  • Nasi Campur: A traditional Balinese dish that consists of rice, vegetables, and your choice of meat or seafood.

  • Babi Guling: A roasted suckling pig that is considered a delicacy in Bali.

  • Sate Lilit: Minced meat satay made with spices, coconut milk, and lemongrass.

  • Lawar: A salad made with vegetables, coconut, and meats or seafood, that is rich in flavors and textures.

Local Beverages

  • Bintang Beer: A popular beer in Indonesia that goes perfectly with a seafood dinner or a relaxing sunset view.

  • Kopi Luwak: A unique coffee made from beans that have been eaten and excreted by palm civets.

Where to Stay in Tanah Lot: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Pan Pacific Nirwana Bali Resort: A luxurious resort with a golf course, spa, and beach access, situated near Tanah Lot temple.

  • Beraban Beach Inn: A budget-friendly hotel that offers clean and comfortable rooms, situated near Tanah Lot beach.

  • Pondok Nyoman Bagus Homestay: An affordable guesthouse with a beautiful garden, situated near Taman Ayun temple.

What to Buy in Tanah Lot: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Traditional handicrafts: Balinese people are skilled in traditional crafts, such as weaving, wood carving, and pottery.

  • Batik Clothing: Batik is a traditional fabric that is dyed with intricate patterns and designs.

  • Coffee and Tea: Bali is known for its high-quality coffee and tea.

Local Culture and Customs of Tanah Lot

Balinese people are hospitable and friendly, and it is expected that visitors show respect for their culture and customs. Dress modestly when visiting temples and other cultural sites, and take off your shoes before entering a temple.

Best Time to Visit Tanah Lot

The best time to visit Tanah Lot is during the dry season from April to October. The weather is sunny, and the ocean is calm, making it an ideal time for surfing and swimming.

Do’s and Don’ts of Tanah Lot

Do:

  • Respect the local culture and customs.

  • Haggle when shopping at the markets.

  • Visit Tanah Lot temple during sunset for a stunning view.

Don’t:

  • Wear revealing clothing.

  • Touch or disturb the monkeys

  • Disrespect the local traditions, beliefs, and customs.

Safety and Health

Safety in Tanah Lot

Tanah Lot is a generally safe destination, and visitors are advised to take the same precautions as they would in any other tourist destination. Be aware of your belongings and avoid carrying valuables with you on the street.

Health in Tanah Lot

Make sure to drink only bottled water, and avoid tap water. Bali is a malaria-prone area, and visitors are advised to take necessary precautions, such as wearing long-sleeved shirts, using mosquito repellent, and taking anti-malaria medication.

How to Get Around in Tanah Lot

The best way to get around in Tanah Lot is by scooter or taxi. Scooters can be rented from several locations, and taxis are readily available at hotels and in the street.

Where to Go Next from Tanah Lot: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Ubud: A cultural and artistic town located in the center of Bali, famous for its traditional crafts, art galleries, and rice terraces.

  • Kuta: A popular tourist destination known for its nightlife, shopping, and beaches.

  • Mount Agung: The highest mountain in Bali, located in the east of the island, which offers stunning views and challenging hiking trails.

Conclusion

Tanah Lot is a perfect destination for travelers looking for a mix of culture, nature, and adventure. With its stunning temple, beautiful beaches, natural attractions, and rich cultural heritage, Tanah Lot has something for everyone. Explore this magical destination to experience the beauty and magic of Bali.
