Introduction to Canggu

Located in South Bali, Canggu is a coastal village known for its scenic rice fields, black sand beaches, and laid-back surfer lifestyle. The village combines the traditional culture of Bali with a modern edge, making it a popular destination for both relaxation and adventure. In this comprehensive travel guide, we will explore the best things to see and do in Canggu, where to eat, stay, and shop, and local customs and safety tips for a memorable trip.

List of Places to Visit in Canggu

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Pura Tanah Lot: Known for its picturesque location on a rocky ocean platform, Pura Tanah Lot is a 16th-century temple that boasts stunning sunset views.
  • Pura Taman Ayun: This temple is surrounded by water and lush greenery and features intricate carvings and a peaceful garden.
  • Museum Pasifika: Located in nearby Nusa Dua, this museum showcases the art and culture of the Pacific islands, Asia, and Southeast Asia.

Natural Attractions of Canggu: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Echo Beach: Known for its black sand and surf breaks, Echo Beach is a popular spot for surfers and beachgoers.
  • Tegal Wangi Beach: This secluded beach is known for its stunning sunsets and natural rock pools.
  • Jatiluwih Rice Terraces: These lush green rice fields offer scenic hiking trails and panoramic views of Bali’s countryside.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Canggu

  • Mount Batur Sunrise Trekking: An early morning start to trek up to Mount Batur to catch the sunrise with panoramic views of Bali.
  • Campuhan Ridge Walk: A scenic trail that offers stunning views of lush green hills, rice paddies, and coconut groves.
  • Hidden Canyon Beji Guwang: A thrilling hike through narrow canyons and rocky streams.

Best Things to Do in Canggu: Activities and Tours

  • Surfing Lessons: With world-renowned surf breaks, Canggu offers plenty of opportunities for beginners and pros to ride the waves.
  • Yoga Classes: Canggu is known for its yoga scene, with various studios and retreats offering classes for all levels.
  • Cooking Classes: Learn to cook authentic Balinese cuisine with local ingredients in a traditional home kitchen.

Food and Drink Scene of Canggu

Must-Try Dishes

  • Nasi Goreng: Fried rice mixed with vegetables, spices, and meat topped with a fried egg is a popular Indonesian dish.
  • Babi Guling: Roasted suckling pig is a traditional Balinese dish served with rice and spicy sambal sauce.
  • Gado Gado: A vegetarian dish made with boiled vegetables and tempeh, boiled eggs, and a peanut sauce.

Local Beverages

  • Bali Kopi: Bali coffee is rich and full-bodied, great served hot or iced with milk.
  • Bintang Beer: The most popular beer in Bali, it’s light and refreshing, and perfect to have chilled on a hot day.
  • Arak: A traditional Balinese spirit made from coconut palm sap, it is usually mixed with fruit juices.

Where to Stay in Canggu: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Hotel Tugu Bali: A luxurious boutique hotel with a mix of Balinese and Javanese traditional architectural styles.
  • The Slow: A modern-design boutique hotel that offers a complete relaxation atmosphere.
  • The Escape Hostel: A budget-friendly hostel located in Canggu’s main street, Batu Bolong.

What to Buy in Canggu: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Handicrafts: Bali is known for its intricate wood carvings, silver jewelry, and colorful textiles.
  • Souvenir T-Shirts: You can find various styles and designs in the souvenir shops in Canggu.
  • Bali Coffee: Bring home a package of local coffee beans, freshly roasted for a true taste of Bali.

Local Culture and Customs of Canggu

  • Most people practice Balinese Hinduism, with daily offerings made at public and private altars
  • Be respectful of the local customs and dress modestly when visiting temples and other religious sites
  • Haggling is acceptable at markets and souvenir shops, but be polite and respectful in the process.

Best Time to Visit Canggu

The best time to visit Canggu is during the dry season, which runs from April to September, with less rainfall and more sunshine. The peak tourist season is June to August, with crowded beaches, and higher prices. Low season, from October to March, has more rain, but fewer tourists, making it a good option for budget travelers.

Do’s and Don’ts of Canggu

Do:

  • Respect the local culture and customs, especially at religious sites and during ceremonies
  • Drink bottled water to avoid illness.
  • Try local cuisine and drink Bali’s traditional beverages.

Don’t:

  • Touch people’s heads: It is considered disrespectful and is against local customs.
  • Sunbathe nude or topless: It’s against Indonesian law and considered offensive.
  • Take drugs: Indonesia imposes strict laws against drug possession and trafficking.

Safety and Health

Safety in Canggu

Canggu is generally safe for tourists, but it’s best to be cautious and aware of your surroundings. Be aware of pickpockets, especially in crowded places, and take precautions when walking alone at night. Watch out for dangerous surf currents when swimming in the ocean, and always follow the instructions of lifeguards.

Health in Canggu

Drink only bottled water and beware of ice, salads or raw vegetables sold in less reputable food vendors, as this sometimes can cause stomach discomfort or more severe illnesses. It’s important to protect yourself from mosquito bites, which can transmit diseases such as dengue fever or malaria.

Getting to Canggu: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Canggu

  • Ngurah Rai International Airport: Located in Denpasar, Bali’s capital city, it’s around a 45-minute drive to Canggu.

Bus Stations Near Canggu

  • Perama Tour: Provides shuttle bus service from Kuta and Seminyak to Canggu.
  • Kura-Kura Bus: Offers a hop-on hop-off bus service to various destinations in Bali, including Canggu.

Other Options

  • Private Car or Taxi: You can hire a private car or taxi from the airport or book through a ride-hailing app like Grab.
  • Scooter Rental: Scooters are available for rent throughout Canggu and provide a convenient and inexpensive way to explore the area.

How to Get Around in Canggu

There are various options to get around in Canggu:

  • By walking or biking: Canggu is a small village that can be easily explored on foot or by bicycle.
  • By private car or taxi: You can rent a private car or take a taxi to explore the surrounding areas or visit nearby attractions.
  • By Canggu Shuttle: A free shuttle service available in the village with multiple pickup and drop-off points.

Where to Go Next from Canggu: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Ubud: The cultural heart of Bali, known for its beautiful temples, art markets, and lush rice paddies.
  • Kuta: Famous for its long sandy beach, shopping malls, and lively nightlife.
  • Seminyak: Known for its high-end restaurants and bars, designer boutiques, and luxury resorts.

Conclusion

Canggu offers a unique blend of traditional Balinese culture and modern amenities, making it a perfect destination for those seeking to experience Bali’s natural beauty, enjoy outdoor adventures, or just relax on the beach. With this comprehensive travel guide, you are now equipped with all the necessary information to make the most out of your trip to Canggu. So, pack your bags, grab your surfboard, and explore the beauty that Bali has to offer in Canggu.
