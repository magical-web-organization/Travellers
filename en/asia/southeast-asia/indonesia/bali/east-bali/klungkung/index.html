Introduction to Klungkung

Located in East Bali, Indonesia, Klungkung is a cultural haven that boasts some of the most remarkable historical and natural wonders of Bali. The town is a delight for tourists looking for an escape from Bali’s busy tourist hotspots.

List of Places to Visit in Klungkung

From ancient palaces and museums to breathtaking natural landscapes, Klungkung offers an array of exciting destinations for tourists. Here is a detailed overview:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Klungkung is a historically rich town showcasing Bali’s customs and culture. That said, the following museums and historical places should be on your list:

  • Kerta Gosa - A pavilion built in the early 18th century, Kerta Gosa has pretty paintings on its ceilings that depict the cycle of life and death.
  • Goa Lawah - A 11th-century temple complex featuring a cave with a small stream and thousands of bats.
  • Taman Kertha Gosa - A palace complex featuring artwork and statues depicting Balinese legends and myths.
  • Puputan Klungkung Monument - A monument that commemorates the battle of Klungkung where the Balinese royals were killed by the Netherlands.
  • Bukit Jambul Rice Terrace - Explore the breathtaking countryside landscape at Bukit Jambul, which features rice paddies, valleys, and small rivers.

Natural Attractions of Klungkung: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Apart from serene landscapes, Klungkung has adventure-packed activities for adventure seekers. Here are the top natural attractions in the town:

  • Nusa Penida Island - One of the most-visited islands of Klungkung, Nusa Penida is blessed with a serene atmosphere and turquoise blue water.
  • Taman Ujung Water Palace - A scenic palace that features a big pond with a variety of fish and dozens of traditional Balinese pavilions.
  • Tirta Gangga Water Palace - One of Bali’s most famous water palaces right outside of Klungkung, known for its gardens, scenic setting and water fountains.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Klungkung

If you’re an adventure enthusiast and love hiking and biking in the scenic countryside of Bali, choose one of the following routes:

  • Trekking in Mount Agung - One of Bali’s best tourist attractions, Mount Agung hike levels up to 3,032 meters above the sea level.
  • Biking in Bukit Asah - Go on a biking expedition that showcases the beautiful countryside of Klungkung, and also, the less-traveled Bukit Asah village.

Best Things to Do in Klungkung: Activities and Tours

Aside from museums, palaces, natural sites, and trekking expeditions, there are other things to do in Klungkung. Here they are:

  • Cooking Classes - Learn to cook Balinese dishes with an expert chef, and enjoy a meal in a traditional setting.
  • Art Workshops - Learn batik painting, woodcarving, or jewelry-making at an art workshop, and take home a unique souvenir.
  • Private Tours - Tour private workshops of local artisans and discover local life in a more traditional and authentic way.

Week-Long Itinerary for Klungkung

Klungkung has a lot to offer, making it a great destination for a week-long trip. Here is an itinerary for your week-long trip:

Day 2 Tour in Bali’s Countryside
Day 3 Visit Sacred Temples and Palaces

Day 7 Departure Day - Travel Home

Food and Drink Scene of Klungkung

Balinese food is famous for its spices, and Klungkung is no exception. Here are some of the must-try dishes and local beverages to savor in Klungkung:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Nasi Campur - A popular Balinese dish that comprises steamed rice, beans, vegetables, and meat.
  • Bebek Betutu - A traditional dish made from slow-cooked duck with a Balinese blend of spices wrapped in banana leaves.
  • Gado-Gado - A vegetable salad typically served with peanut sauce, boiled egg, and freshwater shrimp crackers.

Local Beverages

  • Bali Coffee - One of Indonesia’s signature drinks, Bali coffee is a must-try for coffee lovers.
  • Arak - A type of local alcoholic beverage typically made from coconut or palm sugar.

Where to Stay in Klungkung: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Klungkung offers an array of accommodation options to meet your preferences and budgets. Here are some of the top-rated hostels, hotels, and guesthouses:

  • The Maha Boutique Resort and Restaurant - Rated as the best hotel in Klungkung, the Maha Resort is perfect for honeymooners and travelers who love luxury.
  • Bunga Bungalow - A comfy, bungalow-style guesthouse located within the natural landscapes of Bali.
  • Rama Homestay and Spa - A homestay that offers traditional Balinese hospitality along with a modern spa.

What to Buy in Klungkung: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Klungkung offers a broad range of Balinese handicrafts that make perfect souvenirs or gifts. From souvenirs to textiles, here are some of the popular products to bring home:

  • Wayang Puppets - Wooden shadow puppets that depict ancient Balinese legends and myths.
  • Batik textiles - Colorful textiles featuring indigenous Balinese motifs and patterns.
  • Wood Carvings - Handcrafted wooden objects such as masks, figurines, bowls, and even furniture.

Local Culture and Customs of Klungkung

Klungkung is a deeply religious town with a rich cultural heritage. The locals value and honor their religious practices and rituals. So, be respectful in their town and adhere to their customs and culture.

Best Time to Visit Klungkung

The best time to visit Klungkung is during the dry season, which runs from April to October. The weather is very pleasant and comfortable during this time of the year.

Do’s and Don’ts of Klungkung

Do:

  • Be respectful to the locals and follow their customs and traditions.
  • Stay local and support the business community of Klungkung.
  • Dress appropriately when visiting temples or religious sites.

Don’t:

  • Drink tap water, as it may lead to health problems.
  • Don’t take pictures of locals without asking permission.
  • Don’t damage any historical or cultural sites or monuments.

Safety and Health

Safety in Klungkung

Klungkung is generally safe for travelers; however, tourists must:.

  • Protect their valuables and keep them in a secure place.
  • Avoid traveling at night or in unfamiliar areas.
  • Follow safety guidelines provided by guides or local authorities.

Health in Klungkung

Klungkung does not have any major health concerns, though tourists should take necessary precautions to avoid potential health risks. One should:

  • Drink bottled water or use a filter to avoid water-borne diseases.
  • Wear insect repellent to prevent mosquito bites.
  • Bring necessary medicines along and check the required vaccinations before traveling.

Getting to Klungkung: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Klungkung

The nearest airport to Klungkung is Ngurah Rai International Airport that is located in the south of Bali. From the airport, one can take a taxi, rent a car or hire a private car.

Bus Stations Near Klungkung

Another way to get to Klungkung from other parts of Bali is by taking a bus. Buses to Klungkung operate from all major bus terminals across the island. Regular buses from Denpasar operate every day.

Other Options

One can choose from several transportation options to get around Klungkung. Taxis, motorbikes, and rented cars are available in Klungkung to explore the town.

How to Get Around in Klungkung

To explore Klungkung, tourists can use several modes of transportation:

  • Taxi - A comfortable mode of transportation that is easy to find and navigate around Klungkung.
  • Motorbike - Ideal for adventure and quick trips around the town.
  • Local Buses - The cheapest mode of transportation around Klungkung.

Where to Go Next from Klungkung: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Klungkung is strategically located and an excellent base to explore nearby places of interest. Some of the popular destinations around Klungkung include:

  • Ubud - A cultural town known for its art and craft galleries, yoga retreats, and ancient temples.
  • Sanur Beach - A top beach destination that is only 20 minutes drive from Klungkung, loved for its calm sea and beautiful sunrise.
  • Nusa Lembongan - An island that is only a 25-minute boat ride from Bali’s southeast coast.

Conclusion

Klungkung, Bali’s cultural haven, is a destination that has a lot to offer to tourists. From historical sites to natural wonders and outdoor adventures, the town caters for all types of travelers. Use this guide as a reference point to plan your visit to Klungkung, and get ready to embark on an adventurous journey.
