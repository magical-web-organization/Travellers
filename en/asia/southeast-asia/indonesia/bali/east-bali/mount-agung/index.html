Introduction to Mount Agung

Mount Agung, also known as Gunung Agung, is the highest mountain in Bali and is located in the eastern part of the island. It is an active volcano that last erupted in 1963, which makes it a sacred and important place for the people of Bali.

If you are a nature lover and looking for a thrilling outdoor adventure, then Mount Agung should be on your bucket list. This mountain boasts stunning scenery, challenging hiking trails, and a rich cultural heritage that will leave you spellbound.

List of Places to Visit in Mount Agung

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Besakih Temple
  • Goa Lawah Temple
  • Tirta Gangga Water Palace

Besakih Temple, also known as the Mother Temple, is the most important temple in Bali and is located on the slopes of Mount Agung. It is a complex of more than 80 temples and shrines and is considered the spiritual center of Bali.

Goa Lawah Temple, also known as the Bat Cave Temple, is one of the most important cultural attractions in Bali, located on the southeastern coast of Bali. The temple is built around a cave inhabited by thousands of bats and is believed to be a sacred place for meditation.

Tirta Gangga Water Palace is a former royal palace located in the eastern part of Bali. It is famous for its beautifully landscaped gardens, intricate statues, and lotus ponds. The water palace is surrounded by rice fields and Mount Agung.

Natural Attractions of Mount Agung: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Mount Agung Summit
  • Telaga Waja River Rafting
  • Tukad Cepung Waterfall

Mount Agung Summit is the highest peak in Bali and is a popular hiking destination. The hike is challenging and requires physical fitness, but it offers breathtaking views of Bali from the summit.

Telaga Waja River Rafting is a must-do outdoor adventure for adrenaline junkies. The river flows through the beautiful Telaga Waja Valley, and a rafting tour will give you stunning views of Bali’s countryside and Mount Agung.

Tukad Cepung Waterfall is a hidden gem in Bali and is located in the heart of a jungle. The waterfall has a unique cave-like structure, and the sun rays that seep through create a mesmerizing scene that will leave you spellbound.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Mount Agung

There are two main hiking routes to the summit of Mount Agung, the Besakih Route, and the Pasar Agung Route. The Besakih Route is considered the easier route, while the Pasar Agung Route offers a more challenging climb. Both routes require a guide.

Cycling tours around the foot of Mount Agung are also popular among tourists. You can cycle through the beautiful Balinese countryside and see the local way of life.

Best Things to Do in Mount Agung: Activities and Tours

  • Sunrise Hike to Mount Agung Summit
  • Besakih Temple Tour
  • Telaga Waja River Rafting Tour
  • Tirta Gangga Water Palace Tour
  • Cultural Village Tours

A sunrise hike to Mount Agung Summit is one of the best things to do in Bali. The view from the summit at sunrise is breathtaking, and it is a unique experience that you won’t forget.

Besakih Temple Tour will give you a deep understanding of the Balinese culture and religion. The tour will take you to the most important temple in Bali, and you will learn about the history and customs of the Balinese people.

Telaga Waja River Rafting Tour is an adventurous and thrilling experience that you shouldn’t miss. You will ride through the river’s rapids and see Bali’s stunning countryside and wildlife.

Tirta Gangga Water Palace Tour is perfect for those who love gardens and architecture. You will see beautiful landscapes, intricate statues, and lotus ponds.

Cultural Village Tours will immerse you in the Balinese culture and traditions. You will see how the locals live, and you will learn about their customs and rituals.

Food and Drink Scene of Mount Agung

Must-Try Dishes

  • Babi Guling (Suckling Pig)
  • Nasi Campur (Mixed Rice with Meat and Vegetables)
  • Pisang Goreng (Fried Banana)

Babi Guling is the most famous traditional Balinese dish, and you can find it in almost every restaurant in Bali. It is a spit-roasted piglet that is stuffed with herbs and served with rice.

Nasi Campur is a mixed rice dish that is also popular in Bali. It is a combination of rice, meat, vegetables, and condiments, and it is a flavorful and filling dish.

Pisang Goreng is a fried banana that is a popular snack in Bali. It is a sweet and savory snack that is perfect for when you need a quick energy boost.

Local Beverages

  • Bintang Beer
  • Arak

Bintang Beer is Bali’s most popular beer and is perfect for hot days. It is refreshing and has a light flavor.

Arak is a local liquor that is made from rice or coconut. It is traditionally used for ceremonial purposes, but it is also enjoyed as a beverage.

Where to Stay in Mount Agung: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Amankila
  • Bloo Lagoon Village
  • Puri Agung Karangasem

Amankila is a luxurious resort that is located on a hillside overlooking the Lombok Strait. It has a beautiful infinity pool, spa, and outdoor activities.

Bloo Lagoon Village is a unique and eco-friendly resort that is built on a hillside overlooking the ocean. It has stunning views, healthy organic food, and a relaxing atmosphere.

Puri Agung Karangasem is a family-run hotel that is located near Mount Agung. It has comfortable rooms, a garden, and a swimming pool. The hotel also offers hiking tours and cultural trips.

What to Buy in Mount Agung: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Batik Clothing
  • Handicrafts

Batik clothing is a traditional Indonesian art that uses wax and dye to create intricate patterns on cloth. You can find batik clothing in almost every market in Bali.

Handicrafts are also popular in Bali, and they include wood carvings, woven baskets, and pottery. These items make great souvenirs and are perfect for decorating your home.

Local Culture and Customs of Mount Agung

The people of Bali are predominantly Hindu, and their culture is deeply rooted in religion and tradition. It is important to respect their customs and dress appropriately when visiting temples or other cultural sites.

Balinese people are friendly and welcoming, and they value family and community. They have many ceremonies and rituals that are deeply connected to their religion and way of life.

Best Time to Visit Mount Agung

The best time to visit Mount Agung is during the dry season, which runs from April to September. The weather is usually sunny and dry during this time, making it perfect for outdoor activities and hiking.

Do’s and Don’ts of Mount Agung

Do:

  • Dress appropriately when visiting temples or cultural sites
  • Respect the customs and traditions of the Balinese people
  • Try the local cuisine and experience the culture
  • Book tours through reputable tour companies

Don’t:

  • Disrespect the local culture or religion
  • Leave trash or litter wherever you go
  • Touch or disturb the wildlife

Safety and Health

Safety in Mount Agung

Hiking to the summit of Mount Agung can be dangerous, and it is important to hire a guide and follow their instructions. The route can be challenging and slippery, and there have been accidents in the past.

The area around Mount Agung is also prone to earthquakes, and it is important to listen to local authorities and follow their instructions in case of an emergency.

Health in Mount Agung

The water in Bali is not potable, and it is important to drink bottled or filtered water. It is also recommended to get vaccinated before traveling to Bali and to take precautions against mosquito bites.

Getting to Mount Agung: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Mount Agung

The nearest airport to Mount Agung is Ngurah Rai International Airport in Denpasar, Bali. From here, you can take a taxi or rent a car to get to Mount Agung.

Bus Stations Near Mount Agung

The main bus station in Bali is the Ubung Bus Station in Denpasar. There are also shuttle buses that run between major tourist destinations in Bali, including Mount Agung.

Other Options

You can also rent a car or motorbike to explore Bali. Just make sure to have an international driver’s license and to wear a helmet when riding a motorbike.

How to Get Around in Mount Agung

The best way to get around in Mount Agung is by hiring a car or motorbike. You can also hire a driver for a day or book tours through a reliable tour company.

Where to Go Next from Mount Agung: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Ubud

Ubud is a beautiful town located in central Bali and is known for its art and culture. It is a popular destination for yoga retreats, and it has many galleries and museums.

Nusa Penida

Nusa Penida is a beautiful island located southeast of Bali. It is famous for its stunning beaches, crystal-clear waters, and snorkeling and diving opportunities.

Conclusion

Mount Agung is an incredible destination that offers a unique combination of natural beauty, cultural heritage, and outdoor adventures.
