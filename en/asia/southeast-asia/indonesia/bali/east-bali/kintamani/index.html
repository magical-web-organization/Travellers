Introduction to Kintamani

Located in East Bali, Kintamani is a tranquil highland region known for its scenic views of Mount Batur and Lake Batur. This picturesque destination is perfect for travelers looking for a peaceful getaway and outdoor adventures. In this travel guide, we’ll explore the top attractions, activities, and must-try dishes in Kintamani.

  2. Penelokan Village
  3. Batu Kursi Temple
  4. Pura Ulun Danu Batur Temple

Food and Drink Scene of Kintamani

Must-Try Dishes

  1. Babi Guling (Suckling Pig)
  2. Bebek Betutu (Balinese Roasted Duck)
  3. Nasi Campur (Mixed Rice)
  4. Lawar (Balinese Spicy Salad)

Local Beverages

  1. Kopi Luwak (Civet Coffee)
  2. Jamu (Traditional Herbal Drink)
  3. Brem (Sweet Rice Wine)

Best Time to Visit Kintamani

The best time to visit Kintamani is during the dry season, which is from April to September. The weather is pleasant, and you can enjoy outdoor activities without any hindrance.

Do’s and Don’ts of Kintamani

Do:

  1. Respect the Local Culture and Traditions
  2. Dress Appropriately while Visiting Temples and Holy Sites
  3. Learn Basic Balinese Greetings and Phrases
  4. Try Local Foods and Drinks

Don’t:

  1. Wear Immodest Clothing in Public Places
  2. Touch Balinese Offerings or Statues without Permission
  3. Step on Offerings or Temples

Safety and Health

Safety in Kintamani

Kintamani is generally a safe place to visit, but it’s always good to take precautions when traveling.

Health in Kintamani

  1. Bring Medications and Hygiene Products with You
  2. Drink Bottled Water
  3. Wear Sunscreen and Mosquito Repellent
