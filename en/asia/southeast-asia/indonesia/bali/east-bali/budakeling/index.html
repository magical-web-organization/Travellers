Introduction to Budakeling

Budakeling is a small village located in East Bali, an area in Indonesia that boasts stunning natural scenery, a rich cultural heritage, and plenty of opportunities for outdoor adventures. With its peaceful atmosphere and welcoming locals, Budakeling makes for the perfect destination for those looking to escape the hustle and bustle of Bali’s more touristy areas.

In this comprehensive guide to Budakeling, we’ll take you on a tour of the village’s most popular attractions, recommend the best places to stay and eat, and share insider tips on how to make the most of your trip.

List of Places to Visit in Budakeling

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Budakeling is home to several must-visit cultural attractions, including the Budakeling Temple, which dates back to the 14th century and boasts stunning architectural features and intricate carvings. Visitors can also explore the nearby Puri Agung Karangasem Palace, which was once home to the royal family and now serves as a museum showcasing the area’s rich history and culture.

Natural Attractions of Budakeling: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

One of the biggest draws of Budakeling is its incredible natural beauty. Visitors can hike to the top of Mount Agung, Bali’s highest peak, for panoramic views of the surrounding countryside, or explore the nearby rice paddies and lush forests on a guided trek. For those seeking a more adrenaline-fueled adventure, there are plenty of opportunities for white-water rafting, zip-lining, and other outdoor activities.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Budakeling

For avid hikers and bikers, Budakeling has many trails to explore. The Mount Agung trek, which takes roughly four hours to complete, is the most popular and offers breathtaking views of the surrounding countryside. Other trails include the Tirta Gangga to Ujung Water Palace trail, which passes through serene rice fields, and the Sidemen to Besakih Temple trail, which takes visitors on a journey through some of Bali’s most picturesque villages.

Best Things to Do in Budakeling: Activities and Tours

Other popular activities in Budakeling include taking a cooking class to learn about traditional Balinese cuisine, visiting an organic farm to learn about sustainable farming practices, and taking a yoga or meditation class to unwind and relax. Visitors can also take a guided tour of the local coffee plantations to learn about the process of growing and harvesting coffee beans.

Day 2: Hike to the top of Mount Agung and take in the stunning views

Day 3: Take a cooking class and learn about Balinese cuisine

Day 4: Visit the nearby Puri Agung Karangasem Palace and learn about the area’s rich history

Food and Drink Scene of Budakeling

Must-Try Dishes

No trip to Bali is complete without trying some of the local cuisine, and Budakeling is no exception. Must-try dishes include nasi goreng (fried rice), satay (grilled meat skewers), and babi guling (spit-roasted pork). Visitors can also indulge in delicious vegetarian and vegan dishes made with fresh, locally sourced ingredients.

Local Beverages

Bali is known for its delicious coffee and tea, and Budakeling is no exception. Visitors can try Kopi Luwak, one of the world’s most expensive and exclusive coffees, or enjoy a cup of Bali’s famous ginger tea. There are also plenty of opportunities to try locally produced wines and spirits.

Where to Stay in Budakeling: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Budakeling offers a variety of accommodation options to suit every traveler’s needs and budget. From luxurious resorts with stunning views of Mount Agung to charming guesthouses nestled in rice paddies, there’s something for everyone. Some popular options include the Lihat Sawah Homestay, the D’Alas Warung Homestay, and the Sideman Homestay and Spa.

What to Buy in Budakeling: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Some popular souvenirs and local products to bring home from Budakeling include handmade pottery and ceramics, traditional Balinese masks and artwork, coffee and tea, and locally produced spices and seasonings. Visitors can also purchase locally made sarongs, batik clothing, and other traditional Balinese clothing and accessories.

Local Culture and Customs of Budakeling

Budakeling is home to a rich and vibrant culture, which is deeply rooted in Hinduism. Visitors should be respectful of local customs and dress modestly when visiting temples and other cultural sites. It’s also a good idea to learn a few key phrases in Indonesian, the local language, to show respect and connect with the locals.

Best Time to Visit Budakeling

The best time to visit Budakeling is during the dry season, which runs from May to October. During this time, the weather is warm and sunny and there’s very little rainfall. It’s important to note that Bali can get very crowded during peak season (July-August) so visitors should plan accordingly.

Do’s and Don’ts of Budakeling

Do:

  • Respect local customs and traditions

  • Dress modestly when visiting temples and other cultural sites

  • Learn a few key phrases in Indonesian

  • Try the local cuisine and beverages

Don’t:

  • Disrespect local customs and traditions

  • Wear revealing clothing when visiting temples and other cultural sites

  • Take photos of locals without their permission

Safety and Health

Safety in Budakeling

Budakeling is a safe and welcoming area, but visitors should exercise caution when traveling. It’s important to keep an eye on personal belongings and be aware of any potential scams or thefts. Visitors should also take precautions when engaging in outdoor adventures like hiking or rafting, and always follow safety guidelines given by tour operators.

Health in Budakeling

Visitors to Budakeling should be up to date on routine vaccinations such as measles, mumps, and rubella (MMR) and tetanus. It’s also a good idea to speak with a healthcare provider about additional vaccinations or preventive measures, especially if traveling during the rainy season.

Getting to Budakeling: Transportation Options and Logistics

Budakeling is located approximately two hours from Denpasar, Bali’s capital city, and is easily accessible by car or taxi. For those flying into Bali, the closest airport is Ngurah Rai International Airport, which is located in Denpasar. Visitors can also take a bus from major cities in Bali to nearby towns and then arrange for transportation to Budakeling.

How to Get Around in Budakeling

The easiest way to get around Budakeling is by hiring a local driver or renting a motorbike. Visitors can also explore the village on foot or take a guided tour of the area’s most popular attractions.

Where to Go Next from Budakeling: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Budakeling’s location in East Bali makes it a great starting point for exploring the surrounding area. Popular nearby destinations include the town of Amed, which is known for its stunning beaches and coral reefs, and the ancient village of Tenganan, which is home to the Bali Aga people and is famous for its traditional ikat weaving.

Conclusion

Budakeling is a hidden gem in Bali, offering visitors a chance to experience the island’s natural beauty and rich cultural heritage without the crowds and tourist traps of other areas. With its stunning scenery, variety of activities, and welcoming locals, Budakeling is the perfect destination for travelers looking for an authentic Balinese experience.
