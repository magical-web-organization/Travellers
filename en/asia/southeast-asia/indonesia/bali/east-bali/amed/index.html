Ultimate Guide to Amed, Bali

Located in East Bali, Amed is a seaside community that offers travelers a unique blend of natural beauty, outdoor adventures, cultural immersion, and relaxation. If you’re seeking a destination that is off the beaten path, then Amed is the perfect place for you.

List of Places to Visit in Amed

Whether you’re seeking cultural attractions or outdoor adventures, Amed has something for everyone. Here are some of the must-visit places in Amed:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Amed Shipwreck: This shipwreck, located just off the coastline, offers an unforgettable underwater experience for divers and snorkelers.
  • Lempuyang Temple: One of the oldest and most revered temples in Bali, the Lempuyang Temple commands a breathtaking view of Mount Agung.
  • Tirta Gangga Water Palace: Built in 1946, this stunning water palace is a testament to Bali’s rich history and culture.

Natural Attractions of Amed: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Amed Beach: Relax on the black sand or go snorkeling in the crystal-clear waters of this beautiful beach.
  • Mount Agung: Bali’s highest mountain, Mount Agung is a must-visit for outdoor enthusiasts who want to hike to the summit.
  • Taman Tirtagangga: This stunning water garden is surrounded by lush green hills and features multiple swimming ponds and a bathing area.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Amed

Amed offers a wide variety of hiking and biking routes that showcase the stunning natural beauty of the area. Some of the most popular routes include:

  • Amed Hill: This 2-hour hike offers stunning views of the coastline and provides a glimpse into local culture.
  • The Tirta Gangga Circuit: This 12-kilometer bike ride will lead you through beautiful rice paddies and traditional Balinese villages.

Best Things to Do in Amed: Activities and Tours

If you’re looking for things to do in Amed, here are some of the best activities and tours:

  • Scuba Diving: Amed offers some of the best diving spots in Bali, with opportunities to see colorful fish, sharks, and even shipwrecks.
  • Snorkeling: Explore the beautiful coral reefs and underwater world that lies just off the coast of Amed.
  • Fishing: Take a local fishing tour and experience the traditional fishing techniques used by Balinese fisherman.

Food and Drink Scene of Amed

The food and drink scene in Amed is a blend of traditional Balinese cuisine and international dishes. Here are some of the must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Babi Guling: Bali’s famous roast suckling pig, served with rice and vegetables.
  • Sate Lilit: Minced fish satay, grilled over an open flame and served with a spicy peanut sauce.
  • Nasi Campur: A mixed rice dish with a variety of toppings, such as satay, vegetables, and egg.

Local Beverages

  • Bintang Beer: Bali’s most popular beer, perfect for hot days on the beach.
  • Arak: A traditional Balinese spirit made from fermented rice, often mixed with fruit juices.
  • Jamu: A traditional Indonesian herbal drink made with turmeric, ginger, and honey.

Where to Stay in Amed: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Amed offers a variety of accommodations to suit any traveler’s needs and budget. Here are some options:

  • Santai Hotel: This beachfront hotel offers comfortable rooms and stunning views of the ocean.
  • Bali Dream House: A budget-friendly option that offers private rooms and a communal kitchen.
  • Jukung Dive Resort: This resort offers spacious villas with private pools and direct access to the beach.

What to Buy in Amed: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

When it comes to souvenirs and local products, here are some of the best things to buy in Amed:

  • Handmade Balinese textiles, such as batik and ikat.
  • Traditional Balinese handicrafts, such as woodcarvings and silver jewelry.
  • Local spices and coffee, sourced directly from the nearby plantations.

Local Culture and Customs of Amed

The people of Amed are warm and welcoming, and their culture reflects Bali’s unique blend of Hinduism and Buddhism. Visitors should dress modestly when visiting temples, and asking for permission before taking photos is appreciated.

Best Time to Visit Amed

The best time to visit Amed is during the dry season, which runs from May to September. This time of year offers clear skies, warm temperatures, and perfect conditions for scuba diving and hiking.

Do’s and Don’ts of Amed

Do:

  • Respect local customs and traditions.
  • Learn a few basic phrases in Balinese, such as “thank you” and “hello.”
  • Try the local cuisine and beverages.

Don’t:

  • Wear revealing clothing when visiting temples or other cultural sites.
  • Disrespect local traditions, such as leaving offerings or participating in local ceremonies.
  • Take coral or other natural resources from the area.

Safety and Health

Safety in Amed

Amed is generally a safe destination for travelers, but it’s always important to take precautions. Here are some tips:

  • Always carry a copy of your passport and travel documents.
  • Be aware of your surroundings, especially when walking or traveling alone at night.
  • Only use licensed tour operators and transportation providers.

Health in Amed

Bali has a high prevalence of mosquito-borne illnesses, such as dengue fever and malaria. Visitors should take precautions such as wearing mosquito repellent and sleeping under mosquito nets.

Getting to Amed: Transportation Options and Logistics

Amed is located about 2.5 hours from Bali’s international airport in Denpasar. Here’s how to get to Amed:

Airports Near Amed

  • Ngurah Rai International Airport in Denpasar is the main airport that serves Bali.

Bus Stations Near Amed

  • The closest bus station to Amed is in Amlapura, about 20 kilometers away.

Other Options

  • Taxis and private drivers are available for hire throughout Bali.
  • Local transportation providers, such as bemos and motorbike taxis, are also available.

How to Get Around in Amed

The best way to get around in Amed is by renting a motorbike or hiring a driver. Many of the attractions in the area are spread out and not accessible by foot.

Where to Go Next from Amed: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

If you’re looking to explore more of Bali, here are some nearby destinations and excursions to consider:

  • Ubud: Bali’s cultural capital, located about 2 hours away from Amed.
  • Nusa Lembongan: A beautiful island off the coast of Bali, known for its crystal-clear waters and world-class diving.
  • Gili Islands: A group of 3 small islands off the coast of Lombok, known for their pristine beaches and laid-back vibe.

Conclusion

If you’re looking for a destination that offers a unique blend of natural beauty, outdoor adventures, and cultural immersion, then Amed is the perfect place for you. With its stunning beaches, lush green hills, and rich cultural heritage, Amed is sure to captivate any traveler.
