Introduction to Northern Sulawesi

Located in the Sulawesi region of Indonesia, Northern Sulawesi is undoubtedly an upcoming tourist destination that promises travelers to offer everything needed for an unforgettable vacation. From stunning beaches to bustling cities, from magnificent majestic mountains to exciting crater lakes, Northern Sulawesi is a hub of cultural and natural tourism. With this comprehensive tour guide, we will take you through the must-visit places, exciting outdoor activities, food scene, cultural values, safety tips, and everything else you need to know when you plan your trip to Northern Sulawesi.

List of Places to Visit in Northern Sulawesi

Northern Sulawesi is full of cultural and natural treasures that are waiting for you to discover them.

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Northern Sulawesi proudly preserves the rich culture of its ancestors, and there are many places where you can witness this and learn more about them. Here are some of the top culturally significant attractions to explore:

  • Waruga Cemetery: Located in Airmadidi, Waruga Cemetery is famous for the unique funerary customs where the Minahasan would bury their ancestors in stone sarcophagi with elaborate decorations.
  • Toraget Hot Spring: A hidden gem nestled in the rainforest of Tomohon, these naturally occurring hot springs offer an authentic and traditional experience, making them a must-visit cultural attraction.
  • Christ Blessing Statue: Towering at 50 meters, this impressive and iconic statue stands proudly at the peak of Krueng Raya Hill and is considered the tallest Jesus Christ statue in Indonesia.

Natural Attractions of Northern Sulawesi: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Northern Sulawesi is famous for its natural splendor, ranging from scenic landscapes to wilderness areas. Some of the top natural attractions to explore are:

  • Tangkoko Batuangus Nature Reserve: One of the main attractions of Northern Sulawesi is Tangkoko Batuangus Nature Reserve, home to unique wildlife including the tarsier and black monkey of Sulawesi. It offers an exceptional experience for trekking enthusiasts.
  • Dumoga Bone National Park: Located in Bolaang Mongondow, Dumoga Bone National Park features rainforests, fascinating waterfalls, and Sulawesi’s iconic wildlife species.
  • Sangihe Islands: Off the coast of Sulawesi, Sangihe Islands offers stunning beaches and unforgettable diving opportunities to explore the marine life in its crystal-clear waters.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Northern Sulawesi

Northern Sulawesi offers great outdoor activities to visitors. Below are the best hiking/biking routes:

  • Tondano Lake: A beautiful hiking trail around Tondano Lake provides an opportunity to enjoy the fresh air and stunning scenery of Northern Sulawesi.
  • Mount Lokon: Mount Lokon is a fabulous ecosystem that offers great trekking opportunities in a lush mountainous environment, with its peak at 1580 meters.
  • Woloan Village: Located in Tomohon, Woloan Village offers fascinating insights into the Minahasan culture, and visitors can enjoy cycling and walking tours to explore the agricultural fields, flower nurseries, and natural resources in the area.

Best Things to Do in Northern Sulawesi: Activities and Tours

Northern Sulawesi offers an endless array of activities and tours to travelers.

  • Diving: Northern Sulawesi is famous for its marine life diversity, and diving into the turquoise waters around the Sangihe Islands offers many different species of marine flora and fauna.
  • Culinary Tours: Northern Sulawesi offers a variety of culinary tours to visitors, allowing them to taste the local cuisine. Make sure to taste Tinutuan, a flavorful porridge dish made from mixed vegetables, pumpkin, and other local ingredients.
  • Rafting: Northern Sulawesi is a hub of thrilling river adventures for adventurous travelers, with rapids ranging from level two to four on the beautiful Nimanga or Timbukar Rivers.

Food and Drink Scene of Northern Sulawesi

Northern Sulawesi has a delectable and authentic local cuisine that reflects the region’s cultural and ethnic diversity.

Must-Try Dishes

During your visit to Northern Sulawesi, you must try these local dishes:

  • Ayam/Babi Rica-Rica: This spicy chicken or pork dish is a local classic that is prepared with chili, shallots, garlic, and tomato spices.
  • Tinutuan: A delicious porridge made with mixed vegetables, pumpkin, and coconut milk, served hot for breakfast with pan-fried eels and plantains.
  • Klapertart: A dessert that consists of soft custard sweetened with pandan leaves and topped with coconut flesh and raisins.

Local Beverages

Northern Sulawesi features various refreshing beverages:

  • Saguer: This palm wine is a local favorite and is made from fermented sap that produces mild alcohol content.
  • Iced Tea: Called Teh Cap Gelas on every menu, is a refreshing and popular cold beverage that comes with a distinct sweetened flavor.

Where to Stay in Northern Sulawesi: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Northern Sulawesi has scores of accommodation options for visitors, from budget hotels to luxurious resorts. Here are a few popular options:

  • Gardenia Country Inn: Located in Tomohon and surrounded by lush greenery, Gardenia Country Inn offers a comfortable and serene atmosphere that features a garden, a terrace, and a restaurant.
  • Murex Dive Resort: Located in Manado and offers premium accommodation with sea views. The resort is perfect for diving aficionados looking for a luxurious base to explore the underwater world.
  • Evergreen Guest House: Located in the heart of Manado, Evergreen Guest House is a decent, affordable, and budget-friendly option for travelers who want a comfortable stay with facilities like air conditioning, hot showers, and free Wi-Fi.

What to Buy in Northern Sulawesi: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Northern Sulawesi has some unique souvenir options that visitors can take back to remind them of their trip:

  • Knitted Orchids: Handmade from a light-gauge wire, these knitted orchids are perfect for gifting or decorating your house. They come in various colors and sizes and can be customized to your preference.
  • Minahasan Traditional Clothing: Made of colorful batik and woven fabrics, traditional clothing is eye-catchy and vibrant, showcasing the Minahasan culture that you can witness throughout your visit to the Northern Sulawesi.

Local Culture and Customs of Northern Sulawesi

Northern Sulawesi is a region with cultural and ethnic diversity rich with customs and traditions. As a visitor to the region, make sure to respect and follow these customs and traditions. For example,

  • Indonesians Speak Bahasa Indonesia: Indonesians speak Bahasa Indonesia, and the people of Northern Sulawesi also speak Manado Malay. Try to learn some basic phrases before your visit to communicate with the locals.
  • Minahasan Tombs: The Minahasan people hold elaborate funerary customs, where the tombs are usually found in the front yards of family homes, or in the Waruga Cemetery.
  • Greeting Rituals: The people of North Sulawesi adore nature and treat it with respect. The people of North Sulawesi greet visitors with a handshake and ‘Selamat Datang,’ indicating welcome in Northern Sulawesi.

Best Time to Visit Northern Sulawesi

Northern Sulawesi’s climate is tropical, with high temperatures and humidity throughout the year. The best time to visit Northern Sulawesi is the dry season between May to September. During this period, the weather is pleasant and ideal for outdoor activities.

Do’s and Don’ts of Northern Sulawesi

Here are some essential do’s and don’ts you should know about when you visit Northern Sulawesi:

Do:

  • Respect the Culture: The people of Northern Sulawesi have an incredible cultural heritage, so respect their customs and treat them as you would expect to be treated.
  • Try New Foods: Northern Sulawesi’s cuisine is unique and reflects the region’s ethnic diversity, so try new foods and explore new flavors.
  • Pack Light: The terrain in Northern Sulawesi is rugged, so pack light, comfortable clothing and suitable footwear for trekking and hiking.

Don’t:

  • Litter the Environment: Northern Sulawesi offers natural scenery, so be sure to dispose of waste properly and keep the environment clean.
  • Wear Revealing Clothes: Exposing too much skin in Northern Sulawesi is not well-accepted, so try to cover your knees and shoulders to show respect for the culture and customs.
  • Forget to Bargain: Bargaining is customary in Northern Sulawesi’s markets, so always haggle prices.

Safety and Health

Safety in Northern Sulawesi

Northern Sulawesi is a safe place to visit, with friendly locals and low crime rates. However, it’s always advisable to follow safety precautions while traveling, so:

  • Avoid Walking Alone at Night: Walking alone at night is never safe, especially if you are not familiar with the area.
  • Avoid Carrying Large Amounts of Cash: Be cautious when carrying cash, and keep your valuables hidden from public view.

Health in Northern Sulawesi

Visitors should take the necessary health precautions before entering the region, such as:

  • Vaccinations: Check with your doctor whether you need any vaccines before your trip, such as hepatitis A and B, typhoid, and tetanus.
  • Hydration: Northern Sulawesi’s climate is tropical, so drink plenty of water to stay hydrated.
  • Mosquito Bite Prevention: Northern Sulawesi is a malaria-prone area, so carry mosquito repellent and use mosquito nets when necessary.

Getting to Northern Sulawesi: Transportation Options and Logistics

Visitors can reach Northern Sulawesi by air, land, and sea through the options mentioned below:

Airports Near Northern Sulawesi

  • Sam Ratulangi International Airport: Located in Manado, this is the primary airport that serves Northern Sulawesi.
  • Melongguane Airport: Located in Melongguane, it’s the key airport for visitors travelling to the Sangihe Islands.

Bus Stations Near Northern Sulawesi

  • Manado Terminal Bus: Located in Manado, connecting to several destinations such as Gorontalo, Tomohon, and Bitung.

Other Options

  • Ferries and Speedboats: Visitors can travel to Sulawesi’s coastal regions via ferries and speedboats. The Pelni ship offers long-distance, affordable transport options.

How to Get Around in Northern Sulawesi

  • By Taxi: The city of Manado and Tomohon have metered taxis that cover most areas.
  • By Minibus: ‘Mikrolet’ are mini-buses that serve the main urban areas, offering economical transport options.
  • By Ojek: Visitors can hire motorbike taxis, known as ‘Ojek,’ which are widespread in Northern Sulawesi.

Where to Go Next from Northern Sulawesi: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Northern Sulawesi is well connected to other regions, offering exciting excursions to visitors. Here are some of the top destinations:

  • Bunaken Island: Just off the coast of Manado, this island paradise has some of the world’s best diving sites.
  • Gorontalo: Known for its stunning beaches, cool mountain ranges, and exquisite cave formations, Gorontalo is just a few hours away from Northern Sulawesi.
  • Togean Islands: Located southeast of Manado, Togean Islands is a mecca for snorkel and dive enthusiasts with abundant and diverse marine life.

Conclusion

Northern Sulawesi is an incredible destination that promises to provide a plethora of experiences to visitors. With stunning landscapes, rich culture, delicious cuisine, and plenty of outdoor activities, Northern Sulawesi is an ideal choice for a memorable trip. At the same time, visitors must follow safety precautions, respect the customs and traditions, and preserve the environment, making it a responsible tourism destination.
