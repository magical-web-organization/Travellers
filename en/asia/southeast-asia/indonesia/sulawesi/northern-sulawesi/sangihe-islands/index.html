Introduction to Sangihe Islands

Sangihe Islands is a hidden gem located in Northern Sulawesi, a province in Indonesia. The archipelago comprises of 58 islands, with the two main islands being Sangihe and Talaud. These islands boast a perfect blend of tropical landscapes, shimmering beaches, and rich cultural heritage, making them a must-visit destination for nature lovers, outdoor enthusiasts and culture seekers.

As a tourist, you will enjoy the warm hospitality of the local residents who are friendly and welcoming. The people here have long maintained their unique culture and practices, which are reflected in their daily lives.

List of Places to Visit in Sangihe Islands

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Sangihe Islands is rich in history, and there’s no better way to immerse yourself in it than by visiting the cultural landmarks. The following are some of the top places to visit to get a glimpse of the local culture:

  • Tamsis Museum: The Tamsis Museum is an eclectic museum showcasing the cultural heritage of the people of Sangihe Islands. Among the artifacts are traditional clothing, musical instruments, and weaponry.

  • Tabukan Monument: The Tabukan Monument is a war monument dedicated to the soldiers who died in World War II. It’s an excellent place to learn about the history and heritage of Sangihe Islands.

Natural Attractions of Sangihe Islands: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Sangihe Islands boasts fantastic natural scenery, with clear waters teeming with marine life, pristine beaches, and lush forests. Here are a few must-visit sights in Sangihe Islands:

  • Mount Awu: Rising to over 1300 meters, Mount Awu is one of the most iconic peaks in Sangihe Islands. With its breathtaking views and challenging trails, it’s a popular spot among hikers and nature enthusiasts.

  • Siau Island: Located in the heart of Sangihe Islands, Siau Island, offers various outdoor activities, such as scuba diving and snorkeling, amidst diverse marine life from black-tip sharks to dolphins and whales.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Sangihe Islands

Sangihe Islands has many hiking and biking trails that offer visitors an unforgettable adventure through the natural beauty of the islands. Here are a few select routes to check out:

  • Mount Sahendaruman: A great hike to take in the island of Sangan, with the trail leading to the summit from the village of Tahaang.

  • Bicycle Route: A leisurely bike ride around the island of Sangihe, taking in the beaches, rice paddies, and small communities. Rent a bike from a local shop or hotel.

Best Things to Do in Sangihe Islands: Activities and Tours

A tour of Sangihe Islands presents visitors with a wealth of fun activities, including water sports, visits to local communities, and cultural experiences such as traditional dances. Here are a few select things to do:

  • Snorkeling and diving: Enjoy diving and snorkeling off the coral reefs to discover the diversity of marine life inhabiting the sea, such as turtles, sharks, rays, and fish.

  • Visit Local Villages: A visit to the local villages of Sangihe Island offers visitors a chance to experience the culture, people, and best of all, the food! A local guide can show you around.

Week-Long Itinerary for Sangihe Islands

A week-long itinerary is enough to cover a lot of Sangihe Islands’ major attractions while also leaving you with time to relax and enjoy the tropical scenery. Here is a sample itinerary for you:

Day 1

  • Arrive in Sangihe Islands: Get settled in at one of the island’s many accommodations.

Day 2

  • Visit cultural landmarks such as the Tamsis Museum and Tabukan Monument; explore the local cuisine, and relax on the beach.

Day 3

  • Head to Mount Awu to enjoy a challenging hiking trail followed by a relaxing soak in the hot springs.

Day 4

  • Visit the black sand beach of Pulisan and spend the day relaxing by the shore while enjoying the natural scenery.

Day 5

  • Explore Siau Island and engage in outdoor activities such as scuba diving or interact with the local community and try their food.

Day 6

  • Take a trip to the nearby town of Manado, known for its seafood and markets.

Day 7

  • Soak in the hot springs at Lirung, ending your week-long vacation with relaxation and rejuvenation.

Food and Drink Scene of Sangihe Islands

Indonesian cuisine is renowned worldwide, and Sangihe Islands are no exception. The islands offer a vast wealth of tasty traditional foods influenced by their local herbs and spices. You can taste the following must-try dishes:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Tinutuan: A porridge-style dish that is made from various vegetables like pumpkins, corn, cassava, and rice. It is often served with fish, sambal, or shrimp paste.

  • Nasi Jaha: This sticky rice dish is steamed inside a bamboo stem, giving it a unique flavor and texture. It’s best enjoyed with meat or fish wrapped in banana leaf.

Local Beverages

  • Saguer: A sweet, refreshing drink extracted from the sap of the sugar palm tree. It’s an excellent complement to spicy dishes and seafood.

  • Daun Singkong: A drink made from cassava leaves, pandan, and sugar.

Where to Stay in Sangihe Islands: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Sangihe Islands has many options for visitors to choose from, ranging from high-end resorts to more cozy, locally run guesthouses. Here are a few recommendations:

  • Sangihe Paradise Resort: A popular upscale resort located in Tahuna, offering a private beach and top-notch facilities.

  • Sangihe Talaud Hotel: A three-star hotel that offers air-conditioned rooms and essential amenities, catering more to the budget-oriented visitors.

What to Buy in Sangihe Islands: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Sangihe Islands has a selection of souvenirs, artwork, and crafts to bring back home. Some of the popular things to buy include:

  • Sangihe Traditional Woven Cloth: This traditional cloth is known as Kain Sopi, made of woven cotton and is available in various bright colors and designs.

  • Clove and Nutmeg: A popular spice in the islands, they can be purchased as souvenirs or even brought back home and used to make your dishes.

Local Culture and Customs of Sangihe Islands

Sangihe Islands have a unique culture influenced by both the Javanese and Chinese. The people of Sangihe Islands are primarily fishermen with a deep respect for the sea. Some of the cultural practices include the following:

  • Tabuik ritual ceremony: Celebrated on the 10th day of Muharram, it marks the day that the grandson of Prophet Muhammad was martyred in the battle of Karbala. Local residents perform dances in traditional clothing.

  • Food offering ceremony: Offerings are made to ancestors who are believed to have a significant influence on their daily lives, particularly when it comes to food.

Best Time to Visit Sangihe Islands

The best time to visit Sangihe Islands is during the dry season, from April to September. During this time, the weather is generally sunny and dry, perfect for all outdoor activities.

Do’s and Don’ts of Sangihe Islands

Do:

  • Respect the local culture
  • Carry cash as credit cards are not widely accepted
  • Dress modestly and avoid revealing clothing while visiting the villages

Don’t:

  • Get involved in illegal activities, such as drug trafficking or trafficking in wildlife products
  • Litter or waste the natural beauty of the islands
  • Ignore safety regulations while engaging in outdoor activities

Safety and Health

Safety in Sangihe Islands

Sangihe Islands is a safe place to visit, but visitors should take the usual precautions to avoid encounters and stay safe. For example:

  • Do not wander alone at night in isolated places

  • Avoid tourist traps, especially when purchasing goods and services
  • Beware of pickpockets and bag snatchers in crowded places

Health in Sangihe Islands

It is advisable to take necessary precautions for your health while on Sangihe Islands.

  • Drink only bottled water and check if the seal is intact. Avoid tap water or drinking from streams or wells.

  • Carry essential medication with you, especially if you have a pre-existing condition.

Getting to Sangihe Islands: Transportation Options and Logistics

Sangihe Islands is relatively remote and can be challenging to reach. However, there are transport options available, including the following:

Airports Near Sangihe Islands

  • Naha Airport: Serves as the nearest airport to Sangihe Island and is accessible from Jakarta or Bali.

Bus Stations Near Sangihe Islands

  • Terminal Bersepadu Selatan: A terminal in Kuala Lumpur, offering bus services to Indonesia.

Other Options

  • You can travel by ferry or speedboat from Manado.

How to Get Around in Sangihe Islands

Getting around in Sangihe Islands can be challenging, with limited transport services available. But you can travel by public transportation, find a taxi or rent a bicycle.

Where to Go Next from Sangihe Islands: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Sangihe islands is close to other tourist destinations such as the famous Bunaken Marine Park. Here are a few other nearby destinations worth visiting:

  • Manado: A bustling city known for its seafood, volcanoes, and markets.

  • Bunaken Island: Home to the Bunaken Marine Park, which offers world-class diving and spectacular coral reefs.

Conclusion

Sangihe Islands is a mesmerizing destination that offers visitors a blend of tropical nature, rich culture, and unique experiences. Whether you are an outdoor enthusiast, food lover, or culture seeker, there is something for everyone. With this comprehensive guide, you are ready to enjoy everything Sangihe Islands has to offer.
