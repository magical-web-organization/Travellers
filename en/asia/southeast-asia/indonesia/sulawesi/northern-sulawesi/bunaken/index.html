Introduction to Bunaken

Bunaken is a small island located in Northern Sulawesi, Indonesia. It is surrounded by crystal-clear waters and pristine coral reefs, making it a haven for diving enthusiasts from all over the world. The island has a rich biodiversity with over 3000 fish species, 70 coral genera, and five turtle species. Besides diving, there are plenty of other attractions and activities that visitors can enjoy on Bunaken. This comprehensive guide will cover everything you need to plan your dream vacation to Bunaken.

List of Places to Visit in Bunaken

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Bunaken Timur Village: a traditional village where visitors can experience the local way of life and learn about the customs and traditions of Bunaken.
  • Siladen Island: this island is home to some of the oldest tombs in the region. Visitors can view the ancient burial grounds and learn about the unique burial rituals of the people of Bunaken.
  • Waruga Burial Site: located in Airmadidi, this is one of the most important historical sites in Bunaken. The site features ancient tombs that date back to the 14th century.

Natural Attractions of Bunaken: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Bunaken National Marine Park: this park is home to an array of marine life and coral reefs. Visitors can enjoy snorkeling, diving, and swimming in the clear waters of the marine park.
  • Tangkoko Nature Reserve: a protected area that is home to a variety of wildlife, including the endangered tarsier and black macaque monkeys.
  • Mount Lokon: an active volcano that offers breathtaking views of the surrounding region. Visitors can enjoy hiking to the summit to experience the stunning landscape.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Bunaken

  • Mount Lokon Hike: a challenging hike to the summit of the active volcano, offering breathtaking views of the landscape.
  • Bunaken Mangrove Forest: a leisurely walk through the forest, where visitors can spot various species of monkeys and birds.

Best Things to Do in Bunaken: Activities and Tours

  • Diving and Snorkeling: Bunaken is famous for its pristine waters, and visitors can enjoy diving and snorkeling to explore the colorful coral reefs and marine life.
  • Island Hopping: visitors can go on a tour of the nearby islands, which offers scenic views of the coast and the clear waters.
  • Kayaking: visitors can rent a kayak to explore the coast and nearby islands at their own pace.
  • Fishing: visitors can go fishing and enjoy some fresh seafood that Bunaken is famous for.

Food and Drink Scene of Bunaken

Must-Try Dishes

  • Ikan Bakar: grilled fish served with rice and vegetables, a signature dish of Bunaken.
  • Tinutuan: a traditional porridge dish made with vegetables, corn, and smoked fish that is commonly eaten for breakfast.
  • Spicy Seafood Soup: a hearty soup made with a variety of seafood and spices.

Local Beverages

  • Kunyit Asam: a refreshing drink made with turmeric, tamarind, and sugar.
  • Kopi Toraja: a famous coffee from the region, known for its bold and rich taste.
  • Bajigur: a hot drink made with coconut milk, palm sugar, and spices.

Where to Stay in Bunaken: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Bunaken Oasis Dive Resort and Spa: a luxurious resort located on the island, offering diving and spa services.
  • Bunaken Kuskus Resort: a beachfront resort that offers diving, snorkeling, and other water activities.
  • Bunaken Cha Cha Nature Resort: a budget-friendly option that provides comfortable accommodation and organizes tours and activities.

What to Buy in Bunaken: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Local Handicrafts: Bunaken is known for its traditional handicrafts such as woven baskets, wooden carvings, and traditional clothing.
  • Pearl Jewelry: Bunaken is home to some of the finest pearl farms in the region, and visitors can buy a range of pearl jewelry here.
  • Spices: Sulawesi is known for its unique spices such as cloves, nutmeg, and cinnamon that are grown and sold in the region.

Local Culture and Customs of Bunaken

The people of Bunaken are predominantly Christian with a strong animist influence. The locals are friendly and welcoming to visitors, and visitors are expected to respect their culture and traditions. Tipping is not customary, although it is appreciated for good service.

Best Time to Visit Bunaken

The best time to visit Bunaken is from April to October when the weather is dry and sunny, and the seas are calm for diving and snorkeling. However, visitors can enjoy the island’s beauty all year round.

Do’s and Don’ts of Bunaken

Do:

  • Respect the local culture and customs.
  • Take care of the environment and avoid littering.
  • Try the local cuisine and buy locally made products.

Don’t:

  • Wear revealing clothing in public places.
  • Damage the coral reefs and marine life.
  • Take photos of the locals without their permission.

Safety and Health

Safety in Bunaken

Bunaken is generally safe for visitors, but it is always recommended to take precautions like locking valuables and not leaving them unattended on the beach or in public places. Visitors should also be aware of their surroundings and avoid walking alone at night.

Health in Bunaken

Bunaken has no major health issues, but visitors are advised to take precautions like drinking bottled water, wearing sunscreen and insect repellent, and seeking medical attention in case of any medical emergency.

Getting to Bunaken: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Bunaken

The nearest airport to Bunaken is Sam Ratulangi International Airport in Manado. From the airport, visitors can take a taxi to the port and then catch a boat to Bunaken.

Bus Stations Near Bunaken

The most common way to get to Bunaken is by taking a bus from Manado to the port and then boarding a boat to Bunaken.

Other Options

Visitors can also hire a private car or arrange for a shuttle service from Manado to the port and then take a boat to Bunaken.

How to Get Around in Bunaken

The best way to get around in Bunaken is by sea. Visitors can rent a boat or hire a guide for island hopping, fishing, or exploring nearby attractions.

Where to Go Next from Bunaken: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Manado: the capital city of North Sulawesi, known for its vibrant seafood markets and cultural attractions.
  • Lembeh Island: a popular diving destination in North Sulawesi, known for its unique underwater creatures.
  • Tangkoko Nature Reserve: a protected area that is home to unique wildlife like tarsiers and black macaque monkeys.

Conclusion

Bunaken is a paradise for nature lovers and adventure seekers. From its pristine coral reefs and crystal-clear waters to its lush green forests and scenic mountains, Bunaken has something for everyone. With this comprehensive guide, visitors can plan their dream vacation to this beautiful island with ease. Plan your trip wisely, respect the environment and culture, and have an amazing time in Bunaken!
