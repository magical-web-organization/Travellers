Muara, a small town located in the northeast of Brunei, is rapidly transforming into a tourist destination with beautiful beaches, welcoming locals, and rich cultural heritage. With diverse landscapes, ranging from coral reefs to dense forests, Muara offers an array of outdoor adventures and scenic views, making it an ideal destination for travelers seeking new experiences. In this travel guide, we will explore the list of places to visit, food and drink scene, where to stay, local culture, safety and health, transportation options, and nearby destinations to make your trip memorable and exciting.

Introduction to Muara

Muara is a small town located near the capital city of Bandar Seri Begawan, Brunei’s capital. Known as the gateway to Brunei, Muara is home to Brunei’s largest port and is the primary entrance point for most visitors to Brunei. Its strategic location makes it an ideal place to explore the Brunei’s natural beauty and history.

Food and Drink Scene of Muara

Muara offers a fantastic food scene with unique and delicious culinary treats. Here are some must-try dishes in Muara:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Ambuyat: A traditional Bruneian dish, made from sago starch and eaten with a variety of different dips
  • Nasi Katok: A dish consisting of simple ingredients like steamed rice, fried chicken, and sambal, which makes it a classic Bruneian dish.
  • Sate: A dish consisting of skewered and grilled meat, served with a peanut sauce.

Local Beverages

  • Sirap Bandung: A refreshing, sweet, and milky beverage made from rose syrup and evaporated milk, which is perfect for Brunei’s hot weather.
  • Teh Tarik: A classic and popular tea-based beverage in Brunei, made from tea leaves, milk, and sugar.
  • Air Katira: A refreshing and sweet summer drink made from the sap of a plant with the same name.

What to Buy in Muara: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Muara offers a wide range of souvenirs and local products, reflecting the Brunei’s culture and heritage. Here are some of the unique items to buy in Muara:

  • Terendak: A traditional Bruneian hat made from bamboo leaves or pandan leaves.
  • Kris: A traditional Bruneian dagger or sword, with a unique shape and design, symbolizing bravery and honor.
  • Bruneian handicrafts: A wide range of handicrafts are available, ranging from woven baskets and mats to intricate silver jewelry and wood carvings.

Local Culture and Customs of Muara

Brunei’s culture is heavily influenced by Islam and Malay customs, and Muara is no exception. Travelers should dress modestly when visiting religious sites and remove shoes before entering mosques, temples, or other religious places. Additionally, it would help if you greeted people using their formal title, such as Haji and Datin, to show your respect.

Best Time to Visit Muara

The best time to visit Muara is between December and February, during the dry season when the weather is mild and sunny. Additionally, the period between April and June is also suitable for travelers, but it may be a bit hotter with occasional rain. However, travelers should avoid visiting Muara during the monsoon season from September to November, as heavy rains and floods may occur.

Do’s and Don’ts of Muara

Do:

  • Dress modestly when visiting religious sites
  • Respect local traditions and customs
  • Remove shoes before entering homes, mosques, or temples
  • Be polite and respectful to the locals

Don’t:

  • Display public affection
  • Engage in smoking or drinking in public places
  • Insult the Royal Family or Islam
  • Disrespect the locals or their religious beliefs
