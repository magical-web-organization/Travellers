Introduction to Bislig

Located in Caraga, in Mindanao region of the Philippines, Bislig is a picturesque coastal city with stunning natural attractions, including vast rainforests, cascading waterfalls, and mesmerizing beaches. This hidden gem is a must-visit destination for adventure seekers, nature lovers, and culture enthusiasts. Here is a comprehensive travel guide to help you explore the best of Bislig.

List of Places to Visit in Bislig

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Tinuy-an Falls – A multi-tiered waterfall that is hailed as the “Niagara Falls of the Philippines.”
  • Ocean View Park - A popular picnic spot located on a hilltop, offering spectacular views of the Bislig Bay.
  • International Doll House – A museum featuring a vast collection of dolls from different countries, including traditional wear.
  • Bislig Public Market - A bustling marketplace where you can explore local crafts, clothes, and food.

Natural Attractions of Bislig: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Hagonoy Island – A hidden gem boasting crystal-clear waters and powdery white sand for the perfect seaside escape.
  • Boslon Island – A picturesque island known for its flourishing coral reefs and colorful marine life, making it ideal for snorkeling and diving.
  • Togonan Falls – A majestic waterfall nestled in the middle of lush rainforest, forming a natural pool for swimming.
  • Magkawas Falls – A less crowded waterfall located in the heart of Bislig’s countryside, ideal for a peaceful and refreshing dip.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Bislig

Bislig is a hiker’s paradise, with vast areas of unspoiled rainforests to explore. Here are some of the most popular hiking and biking routes in Bislig:

  • Cebuano Trails – A challenging 11-kilometer hiking trail that starts from the Bislig City proper and leads to the Tinuy-an Falls.
  • Pangi to Nalsoc Cave – A scenic bike route that passes through the lush forest and agricultural fields, ending at the stunning Nalsoc Cave.
  • Togonan Falls Trail – A moderate 3-kilometer hike that takes you through the rainforest and leads to the breathtaking Togonan Falls.

Best Things to Do in Bislig: Activities and Tours

  • Surfing in Hinayagan – Catch some epic waves at Hinayagan beach, known for its consistent surf breaks and uncrowded beach.
  • Beach Camping on Boslon Island – Enjoy a serene and secluded beach camping experience on the breathtaking Boslon Island.
  • River Tubing on Awasian River – Brave the rapids of Awasian River on an adrenaline-pumping river tubing adventure.
  • Explore the Unspoiled Rainforest – Discover the pristine rainforests teeming with wildlife, rare flora, and stunning waterfalls, on a trek led by a local guide.

Week-Long Itinerary for Bislig

Day One: Arrival and explore Bislig city proper. Day Two: Visit Tinuy-an Falls and Ocean View Park. Day Three: Beach hop on Hagonoy Island and Boslon Island. Day Four: Take on a moderate hike to Togonan Falls. Day Five: Explore the charming countryside of Bislig and visit the Magkawas Falls.
Day Six: Discover the unspoiled rainforest and go on a river tubing adventure. Day Seven: Depart from Bislig.

Food and Drink Scene of Bislig

Must-Try Dishes

  • Pako Salad – A refreshing salad made from fern leaves, tomatoes, onions, and salted eggs.
  • Kinilaw na Isda – A Filipino ceviche made from fresh raw fish marinated in vinegar and spices.
  • Grilled Fish – Freshly grilled fish caught from the nearby ocean, served with garlic rice and salad.

Local Beverages

  • Tuba – A local alcoholic drink made from coconut sap.
  • Barako Coffee – A strong and aromatic coffee sourced from the nearby Batangas province.

Where to Stay in Bislig: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Here are some of the best accommodations in Bislig:

  • Eastwood Lodge – A budget-friendly lodge that offers comfortable rooms and a peaceful garden.
  • Microtel by Wyndham Bislig – A modern hotel that offers ocean-view rooms, an outdoor pool, and a rooftop bar.
  • Paper Country Inn – A charming guesthouse that offers cozy rooms and delicious local breakfast.

What to Buy in Bislig: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • T’nalak Fabric – A traditional handwoven fabric made by the T’boli tribe that features intricate patterns and striking designs.
  • Abaca Slippers – Comfortable and durable slippers made from Abaca fiber, a locally-grown material in Bislig.

Local Culture and Customs of Bislig

Bislig is known for its rich cultural heritage, influenced by different tribes that coexist in the city. Some of the customs and traditions that you might encounter during your stay are the T’boli’s elaborate dances, festivals, and traditional cuisine.

Best Time to Visit Bislig

Bislig has a tropical climate, which means that you can visit the city all year round. However, the best time to visit Bislig is from October to May, when the weather is dry and sunny.

Do’s and Don’ts of Bislig

Do:

  • Respect the local culture and customs.
  • Bring appropriate clothing for outdoor activities.
  • Try local food and drinks.

Don’t:

  • Engage in activities that harm the natural environment, such as littering.
  • Disrespect the local people.
  • Wear revealing clothing when visiting cultural sites or events.

Safety and Health

Safety in Bislig

Bislig is generally a safe and peaceful city. However, it’s always best to take necessary precautions and be aware of your surroundings, especially when exploring the countryside or hiking.

Health in Bislig

Bislig has several hospitals and clinics to cater to your healthcare needs. Make sure to bring appropriate medications and consult with your doctor before embarking on any outdoor activities.

Getting to Bislig: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Bislig

The nearest airport to Bislig is Bancasi Airport in Butuan City, which is approximately three hours away by car or bus.

Bus Stations Near Bislig

Bislig Bus Terminal is located in the city proper and serves various routes to major cities in the region.

Other Options

You can also hire a private car or rent a motorbike to explore the city and its surroundings.

How to Get Around in Bislig

The most common way to get around Bislig is by tricycle, which is readily available in any part of the city.

Where to Go Next from Bislig: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Siargao Island – A world-renowned destination for surfing, located north of Bislig, which can be reached by bus and ferry.
  • Butuan City – A historical city that is home to several museums, including the National Museum-Butuan Branch, which is approximately two hours away from Bislig.
  • Surigao del Sur – The neighboring province of Bislig, which boasts several attractions, including Enchanted River, Britania Islands, and Tinago Falls.

Conclusion

Bislig is a hidden gem that offers a perfect blend of adventure, culture, and natural beauty. Explore the city’s rich heritage, stunning beaches, lush rainforests, and unique cuisine on your next trip to the Philippines. Follow this comprehensive travel guide to get the best out of Bislig.
