Introduction to Socorro (Surigao del Norte)

Located in the Caraga region of Mindanao in the Philippines, Socorro is a small, picturesque town blessed with off-beat natural locations. Known as the ‘Jewel of Surigao del Norte,’ Socorro is a hidden gem in the Philippines. The town’s warm and friendly people are waiting to greet you and show you around.

List of Places to Visit in Socorro (Surigao del Norte)

Socorro offers an ideal combination of natural beauty, cultural heritage, and adventure activities. Below are the must-visit places in Socorro:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • San Nicolas de Tolentino Parish Church – Located infront of the town plaza, it is a beautiful church dating back to 1872. The interior still preserved its original design.
  • Socorro Beach – A popular beach that offers crystal clear water and white sand.

Natural Attractions of Socorro (Surigao del Norte): Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Bucas Grande Island – A great spot for scuba diving, snorkeling and swimming.
  • Sohoton Cove – a must-visit when exploring Bucas Grande Island. The cove has crystal clear waters, hidden caves, and a lush mangrove forest.
  • Tiktikan Lake – A stunning lake that offers a natural pool of emerald green water.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Socorro (Surigao del Norte)

Socorro offers great hiking and biking routes that tourists can explore. The famous routes are:

  • Bucas Grande Island Trek
  • Jellyfish Lagoon
  • Sohoton Cove Cave Exploration

Best Things to Do in Socorro (Surigao del Norte): Activities and Tours

Socorro offers a range of fun-filled activities and tours that you should not miss. The following are some of the top activities and tours to experience in Socorro.

  • Kayaking and Paddle-boarding: The crystal clear waters offer the perfect opportunity for Kayaking and Paddle-boarding.
  • Island Hopping: A boat ride around Bucas Grande Island will lead you to secluded lagoons, coral gardens, and the stingless jellyfish sanctuary.
  • Scuba Diving and Snorkeling: A great spot for scuba diving, snorkeling and swimming.
  • Fishing: With access to unspoiled waters, fishing is an activity that tourist should not miss.

Day 1: Arrival Day 2: Explore Socorro Beach Day 3: Bucas Grande Island Trek Day 4: Jellyfish Lagoon Tour Day 5: Sohoton Cove Exploration Day 6: Scuba diving and snorkeling Tour Day 7: Departure

Local Culture and Customs of Socorro (Surigao del Norte)

The culture and customs in Socorro are deeply ingrained in the local lifestyle. The locals are very friendly and hospitable towards visitors. Respectful behavior is highly encouraged when visiting churches and other sacred places.

Best Time to Visit Socorro (Surigao del Norte)

Socorro can be visited throughout the year, but the best time to visit is during the dry season from February to May.

Do’s and Don’ts of Socorro (Surigao del Norte)

Do:

  • Respect the culture and customs of locals
  • Carry enough cash as there are no ATMs

Don’t:

  • Don’t leave your belongings unattended
  • Don’t litter

Safety and Health

Safety in Socorro (Surigao del Norte)

Socorro is a safe place to visit, but tourists should always take precautions and be aware of their surroundings.

Health in Socorro (Surigao del Norte)

Tourists are advised to carry necessary medications, sunscreen, and mosquito repellents, as medical facilities in the town are limited.

Getting to Socorro (Surigao del Norte): Transportation Options and Logistics

Socorro can be reached by air or water transport. Below are the transportation options and logistics:

Airports Near Socorro (Surigao del Norte)

  • Surigao del Norte Airport (SUG) - The nearest airport located over an hour away from Socorro.

Bus Stations Near Socorro (Surigao del Norte)

  • Butuan Bus Terminal - The bus terminal is approximately three hours away from Socorro.

Other Options

  • Ferry Services - Ferries dock in nearby towns like Dapa, which can be reached via a 2-hour boat ride from Siargao Island.

How to Get Around in Socorro (Surigao del Norte)

Tourists can get around Socorro via habal-habal (motorcycles offered by local drivers) or by renting a motorbike.

Where to Go Next from Socorro (Surigao del Norte): Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Socorro is surrounded by natural beauty and has nearby destinations and excursions tourists can visit. The following are some of the top destinations and excursions that tourists can visit:

  • Dapa and General Luna (Siargao Island) - World-renowned for its surfing beaches.
  • Bucas Grande Island
  • Surigao City

Conclusion

Socorro is a hidden gem in the Philippines that offers a unique experience for tourists. Offering warm hospitality, adventure activities, rich culture, and natural beauty, Socorro is a must-visit destination. Plan your trip to Socorro today and experience the town’s unspoiled natural beauty and mesmerizing culture.
