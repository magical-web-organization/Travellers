Siargao Travel Guide: A Comprehensive Tour Guide

Siargao is a beautiful island located in the Caraga region of Mindanao, Philippines. Known for its world-class surfing and stunning natural beauty, Siargao is quickly becoming one of the most popular tourist destinations in Southeast Asia. This comprehensive tour guide will help you plan your dream trip to Siargao, from top attractions and things to do, to transportation options, food and drink scene, safety tips, and more.

Introduction to Siargao

Siargao is located in the southern part of the Philippines in the province of Surigao del Norte. It’s known for its beautiful beaches, crystal clear waters, and world-class surfing spots. With its laid-back atmosphere and friendly locals, Siargao is perfect for travelers looking for a relaxing and rejuvenating vacation.

List of Places to Visit in Siargao

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Siargao is a relatively new travel destination, which means it doesn’t have many historical or cultural attractions. However, there are still a few places worth visiting if you’re interested in learning more about the island’s history.

  • Makahambus Hill Cave: This cave is located in the town of Cagayan de Oro City and is one of the most important historical sites in the Philippines. It is where Filipino soldiers hid during the Philippine-American War.

  • Taktak Falls: This waterfall is located in the town of Antipolo City and is a popular spot for locals to escape the city’s hustle and bustle.

Natural Attractions of Siargao: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Siargao is known for its beautiful natural attractions, including beaches, lagoons, and rock formations.

  • Cloud 9: This is one of the most popular surfing spots in the world and is known for its powerful waves.

  • Magpupungko Beach and Tidal Pools: This beach is located in the town of Pilar and is famous for its stunning rock formations and natural tidal pools.

  • Sugba Lagoon: This lagoon is located on the island of Del Carmen and is known for its crystal clear waters and lush green surroundings.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Siargao

Siargao has plenty of hiking and biking routes that are perfect for adventure seekers.

  • Coconut Hill: This is a popular spot for cycling and hiking, and offers stunning views of the island’s coastline.

  • Tayangban Cave: This cave is located in the town of Pilar and is a popular spot for spelunkers and adventure seekers.

Best Things to Do in Siargao: Activities and Tours

There’s no shortage of activities and tours to enjoy in Siargao, from surfing lessons to island hopping tours.

  • Surfing: Siargao is known for its world-class surfing spots, including Cloud 9, Jacking Horse, and Stimpy’s.

  • Island Hopping Tour: Siargao has many beautiful islands and islets, including Naked Island, Guyam Island, and Daku Island.

  • Snorkeling and Diving: Siargao has many beautiful coral reefs and is a popular spot for snorkeling and diving.

Week-Long Itinerary for Siargao

If you’re planning to spend a week in Siargao, here’s a suggested itinerary that will help you make the most of your time:

Day Activity
1 Arrival, check-in, and relax at your hotel
2 Surfing lessons at Cloud 9 and sunset at Magpupungko Tidal Pools
3 Island hopping tour of Naked Island, Guyam Island, and Daku Island
4 Snorkeling and diving at the Coral Garden and visiting the Maasin River
5 Relaxing day at General Luna Beach and exploring the town center
6 Hiking at Coconut Hill and kayaking at Sugba Lagoon
7 Departure from Siargao

Food and Drink Scene of Siargao

Siargao has a vibrant food and drink scene, with plenty of local delicacies and international cuisine options to choose from.

Must-Try Dishes

  • Kinilaw: a Filipino ceviche made with raw fish, coconut vinegar, and spices

  • Adobo: a traditional Filipino dish made with pork, chicken, or fish in a soy sauce and vinegar marinade

  • Lechon: a roasted pig that’s popular at festivals and special occasions

Local Beverages

  • Calamansi Juice: a refreshing citrus juice made from calamansi (a type of Philippine lime)

  • Buko Juice: a coconut water drink that’s popular throughout the Philippines

Where to Stay in Siargao: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Siargao offers a range of accommodations, from luxurious resorts to budget-friendly hostels and guesthouses.

  • Luxury Resorts: Nay Palad Hideaway, Siargao Bleu Resort, and Isla Cabana Resort

  • Mid-Range Hotels: Bravo Beach Resort, Siargao Inn Beach Resort, and Del Carmen Beach Villas

  • Budget-Friendly Hostels and Guesthouses: Harana Surf Resort, Ocean 101 Beach Resort, and Paglaom Hostel

What to Buy in Siargao: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

If you’re looking to bring home some souvenirs or local products from Siargao, here are a few suggestions:

  • Handmade Jewelry: Siargao is known for its handmade jewelry, particularly pieces made from shells and sea glass.

  • Local Coffee: The Philippines is known for producing high-quality coffee, and Siargao has many coffee shops that sell locally grown beans.

Local Culture and Customs of Siargao

The people of Siargao are warm, friendly, and welcoming to visitors. The island is predominantly Catholic, and many of the local customs and traditions revolve around the church and religious holidays.

Best Time to Visit Siargao

The best time to visit Siargao is between March and November, when the weather is dry and sunny. The peak season is between June and September, when the surf is at its best.

Do’s and Don’ts of Siargao

Do:

  • Respect the local culture and customs

  • Practice responsible tourism

  • Be mindful of the environment and avoid littering

Don’t:

  • Take anything from the beach or damage the local wildlife

  • Disrespect the locals or their traditions

  • Engage in any illegal activities

Safety and Health

Safety in Siargao

Siargao is generally a safe destination for travelers, but it’s always important to take precautionary measures when traveling.

  • Be mindful of your belongings and avoid leaving valuables unattended

  • Use caution when swimming or participating in water activities, as rip currents can be dangerous

Health in Siargao

Siargao has basic medical facilities, but it’s always a good idea to bring a basic first aid kit and any necessary medications with you when traveling.

Getting to Siargao: Transportation Options and Logistics

Siargao can be reached by plane, bus, or boat.

Airports Near Siargao

  • Siargao Airport (IAO) is the main airport on the island and is served by several airlines, including Cebu Pacific and Philippine Airlines.

Bus Stations Near Siargao

  • There are several bus stations in Surigao City, which is the closest city to Siargao Island.

Other Options

  • You can also reach Siargao by boat from Surigao City, which takes about two hours.

How to Get Around in Siargao

The best way to get around Siargao is by renting a motorbike or a scooter. You can also hire a tricycle, which is a popular mode of transportation in the Philippines.

Where to Go Next from Siargao: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

If you have some extra time in the Philippines, here are a few nearby destinations and excursions worth considering:

  • Bucas Grande Island: Known for its stunning lagoons and pristine beaches.

  • Camiguin Island: A volcanic island known for its natural beauty, hot springs, and waterfalls.

  • Sohoton Cove: A beautiful cove located in the town of Bucas Grande, known for its stunning rock formations and clear waters.

Conclusion

Siargao is a destination that offers something for everyone, from world-class surfing to stunning natural beauty and delicious cuisine. With this comprehensive tour guide, you can plan your dream trip to Siargao and make the most of everything this beautiful island has to offer.
