Introduction to Butuan

Located in the Caraga region of Mindanao, Philippines, Butuan is a historically and culturally significant city known for its archaeological sites, museums, natural attractions, and food scene. As the region’s economic hub, Butuan offers visitors an array of exciting activities, from exploring ancient sites to trying local delicacies. This comprehensive travel guide will help you plan your trip to Butuan and make the most of your stay.

List of Places to Visit in Butuan

When it comes to things to see and do in Butuan, visitors will have no shortage of options. Here is a list of some of the city’s most popular attractions:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Butuan is home to a range of historical and cultural sites that provide insight into the city’s rich heritage:

  • Balangay Shrine Museum - This museum is dedicated to the ancient Balangay boats that were used by the first Filipino sea traders to sail across the Southeast Asian seas.

  • Butuan National Museum - This spacious museum houses a vast collection of artifacts related to Butuan’s pre-colonial and colonial history, showcasing the former glory of the Ancient Kingdom of Butuan.

  • Magellan’s Landing Site - As the spot where Portuguese explorer Ferdinand Magellan first landed in the Philippines, this historical landmark serves as a reminder of the various European colonizations that have impacted the city’s history.

Natural Attractions of Butuan: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Butuan’s magnificent natural surroundings make it an ideal spot for outdoor enthusiasts. Here are some of the city’s most beautiful natural attractions:

  • Mount Mayapay - This 980-meter mountain offers a challenging yet rewarding trekking experience. Its summit provides breathtaking views of the city and the surrounding lush green hills.

  • Lake Mainit - Found in the northern part of Mindanao, Lake Mainit offers a tranquil retreat surrounded by stunning landscapes from the air and by boat.

  • Delta Discovery Park - Located just a few minutes from the city center, this park showcases the beauty of the Agusan River Delta. Visitors can bike, hike, or take a boat ride to explore the surrounding mangroves and wildlife.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Butuan

Exploring Butuan’s breathtaking landscapes on foot or by bike is an excellent way to see the city’s stunning natural scenery. Here are some of the best hiking and biking routes in Butuan:

  • Mt. Mayapay Trail - This hiking trail begins at Barangay Bading and boasts spectacular views of Butuan Bay. The journey takes about four to six hours, depending on the level of experience.

  • Delta Discovery Park Trail - This bike trail allows visitors to explore the lush mangrove forest surrounding the Agusan River Delta. The route covers around 14 kilometers, followed by delicious traditional snacks like suman-latik and native cacao hugos.

Best Things to Do in Butuan: Activities and Tours

Whether you’re looking for adventure or relaxation, Butuan has plenty to offer. Here are some of the best things to do in Butuan:

  • Visit the Orchid Farm - This flower farm is home to over 500 orchid species and other exotic plants. Visitors can join a guided tour and learn about the different species before relaxing in the garden’s serene surroundings.

  • Take a River Cruise - A river cruise along the Agusan River is a peaceful and scenic way to explore the city. The one-hour journey provides breathtaking views of the surrounding landscape and local culture.

  • Learn to Cook Local Dishes - From adobo to lechon, Filipino cuisine is packed with delicious flavors. Attend a cooking class for a chance to learn how to cook typical dishes from the region and other popular Philippine dishes, then enjoy your creations with fellow travelers.

Week-Long Itinerary for Butuan

For visitors planning to spend a week in Butuan, here is a suggested itinerary:

Day 1: Visit the Butuan National Museum and the Balangay Shrine Museum.

Day 2: Explore the natural beauty of Mt. Mayapay and take a hike to its summit.

Day 3: Experience the thrill of whitewater rafting on the Agusan River.

Day 4: Take a relaxing tour of the city’s notable churches.

Day 5: Discover the breathtaking landscape of Delta Discovery Park and the surrounding mangrove forests.

Day 6: Attend a local cooking class and learn how to make delicious Filipino delicacies.

Day 7: Take a day trip to the nearby island of Camiguin, known for its stunning beaches, waterfall, and hot springs.

Food and Drink Scene of Butuan

Butuan offers visitors a wide variety of food and drink experiences, from delicious local dishes to refreshing beverages.

Must-Try Dishes

  • Adobo - A Philippine staple, this dish is made with meat or vegetables stewed in vinegar, soy sauce, and spices.

  • Lechon - A whole roasted pig, served with spicy vinegar and rice.

  • Sikwate - This local beverage is made from freshly ground cacao, mixed with water or milk, and usually served with suman-latik.

Local Beverages

  • Lambanog - Also known as coconut wine, this local favorite is made from the sap of coconut trees. It has a sweet taste and a high alcohol content.

  • Tanduay - Another popular drink, Tanduay is a rum distilled from sugarcane that packs a serious punch.

Where to Stay in Butuan: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Butuan offers plenty of accommodation options to suit every budget and preference. Here are some of the city’s most popular hotels, hostels, and guesthouses:

  • Almont City Hotel - A luxurious hotel located in the city center, Almont City Hotel features an outdoor pool and a restaurant serving international cuisine.

  • Go Hotels Butuan - A budget-friendly hotel with comfortable rooms in a convenient location near the city center.

  • Butuan Grand Palace Hotel - A mid-range hotel with spacious rooms, a pool, and a restaurant serving local and international cuisine.

What to Buy in Butuan: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Butuan is known for its unique souvenirs and local products, including:

  • Butuanon Bikolnon Coffee - This locally produced coffee boasts a rich and distinct aroma, best enjoyed with a side of kakanin.

  • Weaved Fabrics and Textiles - Butuan is known for its intricate weaving traditions. Visitors can buy handmade bags, table runners, and other textiles in local markets.

Local Culture and Customs of Butuan

The people of Butuan place a high value on their culture and traditions. Visitors should respect the locals and their customs, including modest dressing and asking permission before taking photos.

Best Time to Visit Butuan

The best time to visit Butuan is between December and February when the weather is cooler and drier, perfect for exploring the city’s outdoor attractions. The months of March to May have hotter temperatures, while the rainy season starts from June to November.

Do’s and Don’ts of Butuan

Here are some essential do’s and don’ts for visitors to Butuan:

Do:

  • Respect the local culture - Visitors should act accordingly when visiting culturally or religiously significant sites.

  • Try the local food - Butuan has plenty of delicious local delicacies you won’t want to miss.

  • Pack appropriate clothing - Visitors should dress modestly and appropriately, especially when visiting religious sites.

Don’t:

  • Litter - Visitors should respect the environment and dispose of trash accordingly.

  • Disrespect the locals - Visitors should be respectful and courteous towards the locals and their customs.

  • Haggle too much - While haggling is common in local markets, visitors should do it with patience and not try to get the lowest price possible.

Safety and Health

Safety in Butuan

Butuan is a safe city for tourists to visit. However, visitors should take precautionary measures and avoid bringing valuable items where they can be easily seen. Also, visitors must always wear face masks and face shields in public spaces, especially now during the pandemic.

Health in Butuan

Visitors should carry all required vaccinations and medications when traveling to Butuan. Visitors should also take precautions to avoid consuming contaminated food and water by sticking to bottled or filtered water and avoiding street food.

Getting to Butuan: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Butuan

The nearest airport to Butuan is the Butuan Airport (BUT), which serves several flights from cities in the Philippines, including Manila and Cebu.

Bus Stations Near Butuan

Butuan has several bus terminals that provide transportation to cities in the Northern Mindanao and Caraga regions, including Davao, Cagayan de Oro, Surigao, and Butuan itself.

Other Options

Visitors can also reach Butuan by ferry or private vehicle. Major port cities with ferry routes to Butuan include Cebu, Bohol, and Dumaguete.

How to Get Around in Butuan

Butuan offers various public transportation options like jeepneys, tricycles, and buses which visitors can use around the city. Visitors should also bear in mind that metered taxis do not operate in the city.

Where to Go Next from Butuan: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

After visiting Butuan, visitors can explore the nearby cities of Davao, Surigao, and Cagayan de Oro, known for their stunning natural attractions and cultural landmarks. Visitors can also take a day trip to the nearby island of Camiguin, which is accessible by ferry from Butuan.

Conclusion

Butuan is a city that’s rich in history and natural beauty. With a plethora of museums, natural attractions, outdoor adventures, and food scene, visitors are sure to enjoy exploring this captivating city in Mindanao, Philippines. Use this comprehensive travel guide to plan your trip and make the most of your stay in Butuan.
