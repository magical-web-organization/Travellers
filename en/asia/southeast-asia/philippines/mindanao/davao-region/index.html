A Comprehensive Guide to Davao Region - Explore the Best of Mindanao

Are you looking for a destination that combines natural wonders, rich culture, and exciting adventures? Davao Region, located in the southern part of the Philippines, is a perfect vacation spot for you. This quaint province has a lot to offer: from pristine beaches and verdant mountains to historical landmarks and delicious cuisine. Here’s your ultimate guide to exploring Davao Region.

List of Places to Visit in Davao Region

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Davao has a wealth of cultural and historical landmarks that reflect the province’s rich heritage and diversity. Step back in time by visiting these must-see sites:

  • Museo Dabawenyo: This museum showcases the province’s history, culture, and natural resources through interactive exhibits and artifacts.

  • San Pedro Cathedral: One of the oldest churches in Mindanao, San Pedro Cathedral is a testament to the province’s strong Catholic faith.

  • Davao Crocodile Park: This wildlife park features a wide variety of crocodile species and other animals. Perfect for kids and adults!

Natural Attractions of Davao Region: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Davao is home to a myriad of breathtaking natural wonders, from enchanting waterfalls to stunning mountains. Here are the top outdoor activities you can try:

  • Mount Apo: The highest peak in the Philippines, Mount Apo offers a challenging but rewarding hiking experience. Enjoy panoramic views of the lush forest and the surrounding landscape.

  • Samal Island: A quick ferry ride from Davao City brings you to this beautiful island paradise. Enjoy sunbathing, swimming, and island-hopping.

  • Philippine Eagle Centre: Witness the majestic Philippine Eagle up close and personal at this conservation center located in Malagos, Baguio District.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Davao Region

For avid hikers and bikers, Davao Region has a treasure trove of trails and routes to explore. Here are some of the most popular options:

  • Mount Apo Trail: The most famous hiking trail in Davao, Mount Apo offers a challenging yet rewarding experience for experienced hikers.

  • Calinan-Buda Trail: This scenic route passes through fruit orchards, lush forests, and refreshing streams.

  • Davao City to Tagum City: A long yet scenic route that passes through rural villages, rivers, and verdant mountains.

Best Things to Do in Davao Region: Activities and Tours

There’s no shortage of fun and exciting things to do in Davao. Here are some of the best activities and tours to add to your itinerary:

  • Island Hopping: Visit the stunning islands surrounding Davao like Samal Island, Talicud Island, and Vanishing Island.

  • River Cruising: Explore the breathtaking waterways of Davao River and Talomo River.

  • Food Tour: Discover the diverse culinary offerings of Davao, from seafood and fruits to street food and durian.

Week-Long Itinerary for Davao Region

If you’re planning to stay in Davao for a week, here’s a sample itinerary you can try:

Day Itinerary
1 Explore Davao City, visit Museo Dabawenyo and San Pedro Cathedral
2 Go to Mount Apo, enjoy a day hike
3 Discover the natural wonders of Samal Island, island-hopping
4 Visit the Philippine Eagle Center, Malagos Garden Resort
5 Take a river cruise, visit Davao Crocodile Park
6 Check out the food scene, visit local markets and street food vendors
7 Relax and unwind, spend the day at your hotel or resort

Food and Drink Scene of Davao Region

The food scene in Davao is a potpourri of flavors influenced by different cultures and traditions. Here are some popular local dishes and beverages to try:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Kinilaw: A raw seafood dish that’s marinated in vinegar, ginger, onions, and chili peppers.

  • Durian: A fruit unique to Southeast Asia, known for its pungent aroma and custard-like texture.

  • Chicken Inasal: Grilled chicken that’s marinated in milk and special spices, then grilled to perfection.

Local Beverages

  • Tubâ: A refreshing and healthy drink made from coconut water and sap.

  • Barako Coffee: A strong and robust coffee that’s grown in the highlands of Batangas and served in different cafes and restaurants in Davao.

  • Mango Shake: Davao is known for its sweet and juicy mangoes, and they make the perfect drink in a tropical setting.

Where to Stay in Davao Region: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Davao offers a wide range of accommodation options that cater to different budget and preference. Here are some recommendations:

  • Marco Polo Davao: A luxury hotel located in the heart of the city, with world-class amenities and breathtaking views of Mount Apo.

  • Bahay Ni Tuding Inn: A charming and cozy guesthouse in the historic district of Davao, perfect for budget-savvy travelers.

  • Hofsgrund Mountain Resort: A relaxing resort located in the highlands of Davao, with stunning views of the verdant hills and relaxing ambiance.

What to Buy in Davao Region: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Shopping in Davao is a real treat, with various markets and shopping centers selling local handicrafts, native delicacies and souvenir items. Here are some recommendations:

  • Malong and Batik: These intricate fabrics are hand-woven and dyed by local artisans, making them a unique and thoughtful souvenir item.

  • Pomelo and Durian Products: Davao is known for its sweet and juicy Pomelos, and it’s also the Durian capital of the Philippines. You can bring home jams, candies, and other treats made from these fruits.

  • Indigenous Handicrafts: Shop for colorful baskets, mats, and other native handicrafts made by the indigenous tribes of Davao.

Local Culture and Customs of Davao Region

Davaoeños are known for their hospitality, kindness, and respect for their cultural heritage and traditions. Here are some things to keep in mind when visiting Davao:

  • Respect the Philippine flag and national anthem.

  • Remove your shoes before entering a house or a temple.

  • Practice proper etiquette when eating with your hands and using Chopsticks.

Best Time to Visit Davao Region

Davao’s climate is tropical, with dry and rainy seasons throughout the year. The best time to visit Davao is from December to February, when the weather is cool and dry. Another good time to visit is from March to May, when the weather is still dry, but temperatures are starting to rise.

Do’s and Don’ts of Davao Region

Do:

  • Learn some basic Tagalog phrases before your trip.

  • Try the local cuisine and delicacies.

  • Wear comfortable clothes and footwear when hiking or doing outdoor activities.

Don’t:

  • Wear revealing clothes when visiting religious sites.

  • Litter or damage the environment.

  • Haggle too much when shopping in markets.

Safety and Health

Safety in Davao Region

While Davao is generally a safe province, it’s still recommended to take necessary precautions to ensure your safety. Here are some tips:

  • Avoid walking in dark, isolated areas at night.

  • Leave your valuables in your hotel or accommodation.

  • Always carry a copy of your passport or ID.

Health in Davao Region

It’s essential to take care of your health when traveling to Davao, especially when engaging in outdoor and adventure activities. Here are some tips:

  • Drink only bottled water or purified water

  • Wear sunscreen and insect repellant when going outdoors.

  • Bring basic medicines and first aid kit.

Getting to Davao Region: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Davao Region

Francisco Bangoy International Airport (DVO) serves as the main gateway to Davao City and the rest of the province. The airport is located in Barangay Buhangin, just a few kilometers from the city center.

Bus Stations Near Davao Region

Major bus companies like Yellow Bus Line, Bachelor Express, and Philtranco ply routes from Manila and other cities in Visayas and Mindanao to Davao.

Other Options

There are also ferry services from nearby islands like Samal Island and General Santos City to Davao.

How to Get Around in Davao Region

There are various ways to get around Davao, depending on your budget and preference. Here are some options:

  • Taxi: Metered taxis and ride-hailing services like Grab are available in Davao.

  • Jeepney: The most common mode of transportation in Davao, jeepneys are cheap and convenient.

  • Tricycle: A motorized three-wheeled vehicle that’s common in rural areas and short distances.

Where to Go Next from Davao Region: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Davao Region is just the tip of the iceberg when it comes to exploring the southern part of the Philippines. Here are some nearby destinations and excursions you can try:

  • Siargao: Known as the surfing capital of the Philippines, Siargao is only a short flight from Davao and offers crystal-clear waters and world-class reefs.

  • Camiguin: A small island province in Northern Mindanao, Camiguin boasts of beautiful beaches, historical landmarks, and hot springs.

  • Cagayan de Oro: Known as the “Gateway to Northern Mindanao,” Cagayan de Oro offers adventure activities like white-water rafting, zip-lining, and rappelling.

Conclusion

Davao Region is a true gem in the southern part of the Philippines, offering a range of experiences for every type of traveler. From stunning natural wonders and exciting outdoor activities to rich culture and history, Davao should definitely be on your must-visit list. So pack your bags and get ready for a memorable adventure in Davao Region.
