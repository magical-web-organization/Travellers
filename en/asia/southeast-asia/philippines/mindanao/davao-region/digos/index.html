Introduction to Digos

Located in the province of Davao del Sur in Mindanao, Philippines, Digos is a city known for its culture, natural beauty, and friendly locals. At first glance, Digos may seem like a quiet town, but it offers unique attractions and adventures for travelers who are willing to explore beyond the surface.

List of Places to Visit in Digos

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Digos has a rich history that is worth exploring. Here are some of the cultural attractions you must visit:

  1. Digos Cathedral - Also known as the San Jose Parish Church, this is a century-old church situated in the heart of the city. It is known for its intricate architecture and religious significance to the locals.

  2. Digos City Hall and Freedom Park - The City Hall building is an architectural marvel with its colorful facades and intricate designs. The Freedom Park is the perfect place to relax and take a break from your Digos adventure.

  3. Digos Museum - This museum showcases the province’s history, culture, and art. It is a perfect place for history buffs and art enthusiasts.

Natural Attractions of Digos: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Digos is also famous for its natural attractions. Here are some must-visit outdoor and scenic destinations:

  1. Mount Apo - Known to be the highest peak in the Philippines, Mount Apo is located in Davao, which is near Digos. It is an ideal destination for trekking enthusiasts.

  2. Tamborong Cold Spring - This natural spring is perfect for those who want to cool down and relax amidst nature. It is a great destination for families and friends who want to have a picnic.

  3. Lion’s Park - It is an ideal place for nature lovers. It is known for its beautiful scenery, flowers, and wildlife.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Digos

Digos is an ideal destination for hikers and bikers who love to explore the outdoors. Here are some of the best hiking and biking trails:

  1. Mount Apo Trail - This trail offers an excellent opportunity for trekkers who want to explore Digos’ mountainous terrain.

  2. Blessed Hill Retreat House - This hill offers an excellent hiking experience with an incredible view of Digos City.

  3. Lion’s Park Trail - This trail is perfect for both beginner and experienced hikers who want to explore Digos’ natural beauty up close.

Best Things to Do in Digos: Activities and Tours

Here are some of the must-do activities and tours in Digos:

  1. Water Rafting Adventure - This is the perfect adventure for adrenaline junkies who want to challenge themselves to a thrilling ride.

  2. Canyoneering Adventure - This adventure includes hiking, swimming and jumping into natural pools. It is perfect for those who want to venture into Digos’ natural beauty and experience fun and excitement.

  3. Fruit Picking Tour - Digos is known for its vast fruit plantations, and a fruit picking tour is the perfect opportunity to experience the local lifestyle and pick fruits fresh from the trees.

Week-Long Itinerary for Digos

Here’s a suggested itinerary for a week’s stay in Digos:

Food and Drink Scene of Digos

Must-Try Dishes

Digos is famous for its local delicacies such as:

  1. Durian - The king of fruits is a must-try when in Digos.

  2. Buko Halo-halo - A refreshing dessert that consists of shaved ice, milk, and fruits.

  3. Grilled Tuna Belly - Freshly caught tuna served grilled, Digos-style.

Local Beverages

Digos is also known for its refreshing and unique beverages such as:

  1. Coconut Water - A refreshing drink that is loved by locals.

  2. Durian Shake - A sweet and creamy shake made from the infamous durian fruit.

  3. Barok Coffee - A local brew that comes from arabica coffee beans. It is perfect for coffee lovers who want to try something unique.

Where to Stay in Digos: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Here are some of the best accommodations in Digos:

  1. Green Valley Farm Resort - A resort surrounded by greeneries, perfect for those who want to experience nature while staying in a comfortable place.

  2. Digos Goodwill Budget Inn - This budget-friendly hostel is perfect for backpackers who want to save their expenses while exploring Digos.

  3. Saverah Inn and Suites - A hotel that offers comfort and luxury at an affordable price.

What to Buy in Digos: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Digos is known for its vast plantations and local delicacies that you can take home as souvenirs such as:

  1. Durian candies, pastillas, and jam - This is a must-buy when in Digos as it is a staple food in the province.

  2. Marang - A sweet and creamy fruit that is native to the Philippines.

  3. Coconut Shell Products - Coconut shell has multiple uses and is famous for its unique handicrafts, souvenirs, and decorations.

Local Culture and Customs of Digos

Digos is a friendly and welcoming city, and being aware of its customs and practices can help you in getting to know more about its culture.

Best Time to Visit Digos

The best time to visit Digos is during its festivities such as:

  1. Pasko sa Digos (Christmas in Digos) - A month-long celebration of Christmas that features parades, concerts, and other festive activities.

  2. Kadayawan sa Digos (Kadayawan Festival) - A festival that celebrates the diverse culture of Davao.

Do’s and Don’ts of Digos

Do:

  1. Respect the locals, their customs, and practices.

  2. Dress appropriately when visiting religious sites and other cultural landmarks.

  3. Try the local cuisine and experience the lifestyle of the locals.

Don’t:

  1. Litter and dispose of waste inappropriately.

  2. Engage in activities that can adversely affect the natural beauty of Digos.

  3. Disrupt the peace and order of the place.

Safety and Health

Safety in Digos

Digos is a relatively safe city, but it is still important to exercise caution, especially when exploring nature and other outdoor activities. Make sure to follow the instructions of your tour guide and take necessary precautions before embarking on any adventure.

Health in Digos

It is important to stay hydrated and bring sunscreen when exploring Digos under the sun. To avoid getting sick, make sure to drink bottled water and avoid drinking tap water. Consult with a medical professional if you feel unwell.

Getting to Digos: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Digos

The nearest airport to Digos is Francisco Bangoy International Airport, which is approximately 56 kilometers away from Digos.

Bus Stations Near Digos

Buses available from Davao City to Digos, and the travel time is approximately 1 hour and 30 minutes.

Other Options

You can also rent a private car or hire a taxi to get to Digos, and it is approximately an hour’s drive away from Davao City.

How to Get Around in Digos

Jeepneys and tricycles are the most common modes of transportation in the city.

Where to Go Next from Digos: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Here are some nearby destinations and excursions you can explore:

  1. Davao City - Approximately an hour’s drive away from Digos, Davao City offers more adventures, attractions, and sights to explore.

  2. Sibulan River Cruise - An eco-tourism destination that offers a scenic river cruise experience.

Conclusion

Digos, Mindanao is a hidden gem that is worth exploring. From its natural beauty to its cultural significance, Digos has plenty to offer, making it an excellent destination for travelers who want to experience all that Mindanao has to offer. So plan your trip to Digos, and make unforgettable memories.
