Introduction to Tagum

Tagum is located in the province of Davao del Norte in the Davao Region of Mindanao, Philippines. It is known for its vast banana plantations, scenic views, and diverse cultural heritage. Whether you are a nature lover or a history buff, Tagum has something to offer for everyone. In this travel guide, we will take you through the best places to visit, eat, stay, and explore in Tagum.

List of Places to Visit in Tagum

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Magugpo Japanese Tunnel: Explore the site where the Japanese army hid during World War II and learn about its significant role in the country’s history.
  • Tagum City Hall: Admire the classic architecture of the Tagum City Hall, which dates back to the American colonial period.
  • Bonguyan Cultural Center: Immerse yourself in the traditional music, dance, and art of the Mandaya tribe at the Bonguyan Cultural Center.

Natural Attractions of Tagum: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Banana Beach: Relax in the stunning beach that sits amidst the lush banana plantations and enjoy local delicacies at the nearby restaurants.
  • Nawasa Lake Sidelights: Go on a scenic tour of the Nawasa Lake and explore the surrounding tropical forest with a local guide.
  • Can-umantad Falls: Take a dip in the cool waters of Can-umantad Falls, which is one of the most spectacular waterfalls in Tagum.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Tagum

  • Mt. Mirador: Climb the majestic peak of Mt. Mirador, which offers an unforgettable panoramic view of the city and its surroundings.
  • Cateel Trail: Bike through the picturesque trail that connects Tagum to the tranquil town of Cateel, which is known for its pristine beaches and vibrant culture.
  • Magtuod-Tanasan Hike: Go on a challenging hike to the top of Magtuod and Tanasan peaks, which are popular among adventure seekers for their rugged terrain and scenic beauty.

Best Things to Do in Tagum: Activities and Tours

  • Biking and Boat Tours: Take a guided tour of Tagum’s beautiful landscape by a combination of bike rides and boat trips that offer a unique perspective of the city’s attractions.
  • Fruit Picking: Pick fresh fruits, including bananas, pineapples, and durians at the local farms, and learn about the cultivation process of these delicious delicacies.
  • Mandaya Weaving Workshop: Learn the finer skills of weaving and textile art from the expert weavers of the Mandaya tribe and create your traditional souvenir to take home.

Food and Drink Scene of Tagum

Must-Try Dishes

  • Durian: Try the infamous fruit of Mindanao, which is famous for its pungent smell but sweet taste.
  • Sinuglaw: Enjoy the delicious grilled pork and tangy fish ceviche dish, which is a must-try for seafood lovers.
  • Lutong-upaw: Savor the classic Filipino dish of Tinolang Manok served in bamboo shoots that is slow-cooked over fire.

Local Beverages

  • Barako Coffee: Sip on a strong and fragrant cup of local coffee made from Liberica beans that is known for its distinct flavor.
  • Lubi-lubi Wine: Try the unique and sweet wine made from the sap of the Lubi-lubi plant, which is a popular choice among locals and tourists.

Where to Stay in Tagum: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Big 8 Corporate Hotel: Enjoy the modern amenities and luxurious comfort of this four-star hotel located in the heart of the city.
  • La Filipina Pensionne: Stay in this budget-friendly guesthouse that offers a cozy and friendly atmosphere to its guests.
  • Eagle View Resort: Relax in the peaceful and rustic charm of this eco-resort located in the foothills of Mt. Apo.

What to Buy in Tagum: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Banana Chips: Stock up on the delicious and crispy banana chips, which are available in various flavors and sizes.
  • Abaca Products: Get handcrafted bags, placemats, and other home décor items made of abaca fiber- a plant endemic to the Philippines.
  • Barong Tagalog: Shop for the traditional Filipino formal wear made of delicate fabrics such as silk, piña, and jusi.

Local Culture and Customs of Tagum

Tagum is a melting pot of various cultures and ethnic communities, including the Mandaya, Tagalog, and Visayan. The city is known for its colorful festivals such as the Musikahan, a celebration of music and dance that takes place annually in February.

Best Time to Visit Tagum

The best time to visit Tagum is between November and April when the weather is dry and pleasant, making it ideal for outdoor adventures and sightseeing. The months of May to October are the rainy season, and it is not recommended to travel during this time.

Do’s and Don’ts of Tagum

Do:

  • Respect the local customs and traditions.
  • Dress appropriately, especially when visiting religious sites and cultural events.
  • Try the local food and drinks to immerse in the local culture.

Don’t:

  • Litter or damage the environment.
  • Display any public affection.
  • Wear shoes when visiting homes or establishments.

Safety and Health

Safety in Tagum

Tagum is generally safe for travelers, but it is always best to stay alert and cautious in crowded and busy areas. Avoid carrying large amounts of cash and jewelry and keep your valuables safe. It is also recommended to use reputable tour guides for hiking and outdoor activities.

Health in Tagum

Make sure to bring any necessary medications, especially if you have any pre-existing medical conditions. It is also recommended to stay hydrated, use mosquito repellent, and practice good hygiene to avoid any illnesses.

Getting to Tagum: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Tagum

  • Francisco Bangoy International Airport: Located in Davao City, it is the closest airport to Tagum, approximately 53 kilometers away.

Bus Stations Near Tagum

  • Ecoland Terminal: Located in Davao City, it is the main bus terminal for trips to Tagum.

Other Options

  • Car and Van Rental: Renting a car or van is a convenient and reliable way to get to Tagum from nearby destinations such as Davao City or Surigao.

How to Get Around in Tagum

Tricycles, taxis, and jeepneys are the most common modes of transportation in Tagum. Most attractions and destinations are easily accessible by public transport or walking.

Where to Go Next from Tagum: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Davao City: Visit the bustling metropolis of Davao City, known for its shopping, dining, and entertainment options.
  • Samal Island: Escape to the serene and beautiful Samal Island, which is famous for its white sandy beaches and crystal-clear waters.
  • Mount Apo: Go on an adventure to climb the highest peak in the Philippines, Mt. Apo, which offers a spectacular view of the surrounding landscapes.

Conclusion

Tagum is a hidden gem in the Davao Region, offering a unique blend of culture, history, and natural beauty. With this travel guide, you can discover the best attractions, activities, accommodations, and local customs that the city has to offer. Plan your visit to Tagum today and explore this hidden paradise in Mindanao, Philippines.
