Introduction to Davao Oriental

Located in the Davao Region of Mindanao, Davao Oriental is a province in the Philippines that boasts stunning natural beauty, rich culture, and a laid-back vibe. With a coastline that stretches for over 200 kilometers and a diverse landscape that ranges from rugged mountains to tranquil beaches, Davao Oriental is a paradise for beach lovers, hikers, and adventure seekers.

List of Places to Visit in Davao Oriental

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • San Salvador del Mundo Church - a centuries-old church located in Caraga that is known for its intricate architectural details and beautiful paintings.
  • Mati Art Gallery - showcases artworks created by local artists that depict the beauty and culture of Davao Oriental.
  • Mati National Museum - a hub of cultural artifacts that provide insight into the history and culture of Davao Oriental.

Natural Attractions of Davao Oriental: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Cape San Agustin - a rugged coastline that offers panoramic views of the Celebes Sea and breathtaking rock formations.
  • Aliwagwag Falls - one of the most beautiful waterfalls in the Philippines, with 84 cascading falls.
  • Mt. Hamiguitan - a UNESCO World Heritage Site and home to various wildlife and rare plants.
  • Sleeping Dinosaur Island - a stunning rock formation that resembles a sleeping dinosaur, located in Mati.
  • Pusan Point - a popular beach in Governor Generoso that features clear turquoise waters and pristine white sands.
  • Subangan Museum - showcases the rich biodiversity and cultural heritage of Davao Oriental.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Davao Oriental

  • Mt. Hamiguitan Traverse - a 3 to 4 day hike that takes trekkers through lush forests, scenic ridges, and diverse wildlife.
  • Aliwagwag Falls Trail - a 10-kilometer hike that leads to the majestic Aliwagwag Falls.
  • San Victor Island Circuit - a 4-kilometer bike trail that takes cyclists through the rustic coastline of San Victor Island.

Best Things to Do in Davao Oriental: Activities and Tours

  • Island Hopping Tour - explore the scenic beaches and islands that surround Davao Oriental aboard a traditional Filipino boat.
  • Fishing Tour - experience the traditional way of fishing in the province and enjoy the freshest seafood.
  • Whale Watching Tour - witness the majestic whales and dolphins that inhabit the waters of Davao Oriental.

Week-Long Itinerary for Davao Oriental

Day Activity
1 Arrival in Mati, check-in at your preferred accommodation, and explore the city center
2 Discover the beaches of Mati, including Dahican Beach and Pujada Bay
3 Climb Mt. Hamiguitan and camp overnight
4 Explore the San Victor Island
5 Visit the Aliwagwag Falls and the surrounding forests
6 Hop on a boat and go island hopping
7 Visit the Subangan Museum and travel back to Davao City

Food and Drink Scene of Davao Oriental

Must-Try Dishes

  • Kinilaw - a dish made of raw fish flavored with vinegar and spices.
  • Lechon Kawali - deep-fried pork belly that is crispy on the outside and tender on the inside.
  • Tabon-tabon - a local fruit used as a natural souring agent in many dishes.

Local Beverages

  • Durian Shake - a delicious and creamy shake made with the infamous durian fruit.
  • Coconut Juice - refreshing juice made from fresh coconuts.

Where to Stay in Davao Oriental: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Costa Marina Beach Resort - located in Tagabakid, it offers beachfront accommodations and several activities.
  • Pacific Blue Resort - a laid-back resort located in Dahican that offers a private beach and cozy rooms.
  • Bahay ni Tuding Inn - a charming bed and breakfast located in Mati City.

What to Buy in Davao Oriental: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Malagos Chocolate - a world-renowned chocolate made in Davao City.
  • Banig Products - handwoven mats made by local artisans.
  • T’nalak Fabric - intricately woven cloth made by the T’boli tribe of Mindanao.

Local Culture and Customs of Davao Oriental

Respect for elders and authorities is highly valued in Davao Oriental. Visitors are expected to dress modestly and be mindful of their behavior.

Best Time to Visit Davao Oriental

The best time to visit Davao Oriental is during the dry season, which lasts from November to May. This is when the weather is warm and dry, making it perfect for outdoor activities.

Do’s and Don’ts of Davao Oriental

Do:

  • Respect the local culture and traditions.
  • Dress modestly when visiting churches and other cultural attractions.

Don’t:

  • Litter in public places.
  • Display public displays of affection, as it is frowned upon by locals.

Safety and Health

Safety in Davao Oriental

Davao Oriental is a generally safe province, but visitors should still exercise caution and avoid traveling alone at night. It is also advisable to keep valuables in a safe place.

Health in Davao Oriental

Visitors should always ensure that they have appropriate travel insurance, and vaccines for diseases such as typhoid and hepatitis A. It is also important to stay hydrated and avoid drinking tap water.

Getting to Davao Oriental: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Davao Oriental

Francisco Bangoy International Airport in Davao City is the main airport for domestic and international flights to Davao Oriental.

Bus Stations Near Davao Oriental

Ecoland Bus Terminal and Sta. Ana Wharf Terminal in Davao City are the main hubs for buses bound for Davao Oriental.

Other Options

Visitors can also rent a car or hire a private van for transportation to Davao Oriental.

How to Get Around in Davao Oriental

Jeepneys and tricycles are the most popular means of transportation in Davao Oriental, but visitors can also rent a motorbike or hire a private car.

Where to Go Next from Davao Oriental: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Davao City - a cosmopolitan city that is known for its beaches, museums, and parks.
  • Samal Island - a scenic island located near Davao City that offers white sand beaches and crystal clear waters.
  • Tandag City - a city located in the neighboring province of Surigao del Sur that is known for its beautiful waterfalls.

Conclusion

Davao Oriental is a must-see destination for those looking for a laid-back and scenic getaway. With its rich cultural heritage, stunning natural beauty, and hospitable locals, it is a true gem of the Philippines. Plan your trip to Davao Oriental today and experience the beauty of this province!
