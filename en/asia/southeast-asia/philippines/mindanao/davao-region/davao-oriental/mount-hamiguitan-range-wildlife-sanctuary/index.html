Introduction to Mount Hamiguitan Range Wildlife Sanctuary

Mount Hamiguitan Range Wildlife Sanctuary is a UNESCO World Heritage Site located in the Davao Oriental Province of the Philippines. The sanctuary is known for its rich and diverse ecosystem that consists of several endemic species, including the iconic pygmy forest, bonsai trees, and pitcher plants.

It is also home to Mount Hamiguitan, which is the highest peak in the Davao Oriental Province and stands at a height of 1,620 meters. The sanctuary has a total area of 6,834 hectares and is a popular destination for nature enthusiasts, hikers, and adventure seekers.

List of Places to Visit in Mount Hamiguitan Range Wildlife Sanctuary

There are several places that visitors can explore in the Mount Hamiguitan Range Wildlife Sanctuary. Here are some of the must-visit places:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Pusan Point - A historical landmark that offers a stunning view of the sanctuary and its surrounding areas.
  • Hamiguitan Cultural Village - A museum that showcases the cultural heritage of the local Agusanon Manobo tribe.

Natural Attractions of Mount Hamiguitan Range Wildlife Sanctuary: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Agong House - A viewpoint that offers a picturesque view of the sanctuary and the surrounding landscapes.
  • Twin Falls - A scenic waterfall that is perfect for swimming and picnicking.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Mount Hamiguitan Range Wildlife Sanctuary

Visitors can explore the sanctuary on foot by following one of the several hiking trails. Some of the popular hiking/biking routes are:

  • Mount Hamiguitan Trail - A challenging hike that takes visitors to the summit of Mount Hamiguitan.
  • Tinagong Dagat Trail - A moderate hike that passes through the Tinagong Dagat Lake and offers stunning views of the sanctuary.

Best Things to Do in Mount Hamiguitan Range Wildlife Sanctuary: Activities and Tours

Here are some of the activities and tours that visitors can enjoy in the sanctuary:

  • Wildlife Watching - Visitors can spot several endemic species, including the Philippine Eagle, the Philippine Tarsier, and the Philippine Warty Pig.
  • Camping - There are several campsites in the sanctuary that visitors can use for overnight stays.
  • Guided Tours - Visitors can hire a guide to explore the sanctuary and learn more about its flora and fauna.

Week-Long Itinerary for Mount Hamiguitan Range Wildlife Sanctuary

Here’s a sample week-long itinerary for visitors to get the most out of their Mount Hamiguitan Range Wildlife Sanctuary experience:

Day 1: Arrival and check-in at the resort/hotel Day 2: Guided tour of the sanctuary Day 3: Hike to Mount Hamiguitan Day 4: Wildlife Watching Day 5: Twin Falls Visit Day 6: Cultural Tour of Local Villages Day 7: Departure

Food and Drink Scene of Mount Hamiguitan Range Wildlife Sanctuary

Visitors can try local delicacies and drinks during their stay in the sanctuary. Here are some must-try dishes and beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Kinilaw na Tuna - A dish made of fresh tuna marinated in vinegar, onions, and spices.
  • Grilled Pork Belly - Pork belly marinated in soy sauce, vinegar, and spices and grilled to perfection.

Local Beverages

  • Coconut Wine - A popular drink made from fermented coconut sap.
  • Barako Coffee - A strong and aromatic coffee made from locally grown beans.

Local Culture and Customs of Mount Hamiguitan Range Wildlife Sanctuary

The local culture in Mount Hamiguitan Range Wildlife Sanctuary is diverse and rich. Visitors can explore the culture by visiting local villages and attending local festivals and celebrations.

The local community is known for their warm hospitality and welcoming nature. Visitors are encouraged to respect local customs and traditions during their stay.

Best Time to Visit Mount Hamiguitan Range Wildlife Sanctuary

The best time to visit Mount Hamiguitan Range Wildlife Sanctuary is from December to May when the weather is dry and pleasant. The sanctuary is open to visitors throughout the year, but the rainy season (June-November) can make hiking and outdoor activities challenging.

Do’s and Don’ts of Mount Hamiguitan Range Wildlife Sanctuary

Visitors are advised to follow some simple rules and regulations during their stay in the sanctuary:

Do:

  • Respect local customs and traditions
  • Protect the environment by following proper waste disposal practices.
  • Hire a guide to explore the sanctuary and learn about its flora and fauna.
  • Wear appropriate gear and footwear for hiking and outdoor activities.

Don’t:

  • Touch or disturb wildlife.
  • Leave trash or litter in the sanctuary.
  • Remove or damage natural resources or artifacts.
  • Smoke or light fires in prohibited areas.

Safety and Health

Safety in Mount Hamiguitan Range Wildlife Sanctuary

Visitors are advised to take proper precautions to ensure their safety during their stay in the sanctuary. Here are some safety tips:

  • Hire a guide for hiking and outdoor activities.
  • Carry a first aid kit and sufficient water and food supplies.
  • Be aware of weather conditions and forecast.

Health in Mount Hamiguitan Range Wildlife Sanctuary

Visitors are advised to take necessary vaccinations and seek medical advice before visiting the sanctuary. They should also carry insect repellent and sunscreen to protect against sunburn and insect bites.

Getting to Mount Hamiguitan Range Wildlife Sanctuary: Transportation Options and Logistics

Visitors can reach Mount Hamiguitan Range Wildlife Sanctuary by air, road, and sea.

Airports Near Mount Hamiguitan Range Wildlife Sanctuary

The nearest airport is the Davao International Airport, from where visitors can hire a taxi or rent a car to reach the sanctuary.

Bus Stations Near Mount Hamiguitan Range Wildlife Sanctuary

Visitors can take a bus from Davao City to Mati City, which is approximately 15 km from the sanctuary. From Mati City, visitors can hire a taxi or take a jeepney to reach the sanctuary.

Other Options

Visitors can also take a ferry from Davao City to Mati City and then take a bus or taxi to reach the sanctuary.

How to Get Around in Mount Hamiguitan Range Wildlife Sanctuary

Visitors can explore the sanctuary on foot or on a bicycle. They can hire a guide for hiking and outdoor activities.

Where to Go Next from Mount Hamiguitan Range Wildlife Sanctuary: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Visitors can explore nearby destinations and enjoy excursions. Here are some popular options:

  • Dahican Beach - A stunning beach near Mati City.
  • Enchanted River - A picturesque river located in Hinatuan, Surigao del Sur.

Conclusion

Mount Hamiguitan Range Wildlife Sanctuary is a beautiful destination that offers a diverse and rich experience for visitors. By following proper rules and regulations and respecting local customs and traditions, visitors can have a memorable and safe stay in the sanctuary.
