Introduction to Mati

Located in the picturesque province of Davao Oriental in the southern Philippines, Mati is a hidden gem that boasts stunning beaches, unspoiled natural parks, and vibrant local culture. As a city filled with friendly people, great food, and recreational activities, Mati is an ideal destination for travelers who desire to experience the true essence of the Philippines. With this comprehensive guide, you can plan your perfect trip to Mati and make the most out of your journey.

List of Places to Visit in Mati

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

If you’re a history buff, Mati has plenty of attractions for you to explore. One of the most popular cultural destinations is the Subangan Museum, which showcases the vast marine biodiversity and socio-cultural traditions of Davao Oriental. Other must-visit museums include the D’Bone Collector Museum, which displays a diverse range of animal specimens, and the Mati Museum, which exhibits the city’s rich history and heritage.

Apart from the museums, Mati also has a number of historical sites you can explore. The Sleeping Dinosaur Island, for example, is a geological formation that resembles a sleeping dinosaur when viewed from above. Another site is the Mati Shrine, a sacred place of worship that offers a stunning panoramic view of the city and the coastline.

Natural Attractions of Mati: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Mati is home to some of the most breathtaking natural attractions in the Philippines. The most popular of these is the Dahican Beach, known for its powdery white sand and crystal-clear waters. You can also go island hopping to explore the nearby Pujada Islands or hike to Mount Hamiguitan Range Wildlife Sanctuary, a UNESCO World Heritage Site that boasts a diverse range of flora and fauna.

For those seeking outdoor adventures, you can try surfing in Dahican or go on a river rafting trip along the Matiao River. Alternatively, you can simply take a relaxing stroll through the Mati Park and Baywalk, which is a popular spot for picnics, family gatherings, and sunset viewing.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Mati

Mati is a haven for outdoor enthusiasts, offering a vast network of hiking and biking trails. One of the most popular routes is the Mount Hamiguitan Range Wildlife Sanctuary trek, which features a stunning collection of rare and endemic flora and fauna. You can also try the Manurigao River tubing adventure, which lets you float down a scenic river while surrounded by a lush canopy of trees.

Best Things to Do in Mati: Activities and Tours

Apart from the outdoor adventures, Mati has plenty of other activities to offer. You can try local delicacies at the local food market or visit the Lawigan Beach, which is known for its breathtaking sunsets. You can also participate in cultural events and festivals that showcase the local traditions and customs, such as the Kaimonan Festival or the Baywalk Festival.

For an immersive travel experience, you can join guided tours that offer a glimpse into the city’s cuisine, culture, and history. Some popular tours include the Subangan Museum Tour or the Dahican Adventure Tour.

Week-Long Itinerary for Mati

Here’s a sample itinerary for a week-long stay in Mati:

  • Day 1: Visit the Subangan Museum and the Mati Museum
  • Day 2: Go island hopping at the Pujada Islands
  • Day 3: Hike at the Mount Hamiguitan Range Wildlife Sanctuary
  • Day 4: Try surfing at the Dahican Beach
  • Day 5: Go river rafting at Matiao River or participate in the Kaimonan Festival
  • Day 6: Visit the Sleeping Dinosaur Island and Mati Shrine
  • Day 7: Join a guided tour or simply relax at the Mati Park.

Food and Drink Scene of Mati

Must-Try Dishes

Mati’s food scene is a gastronomic adventure that blends traditional Filipino cuisine with a modern twist. For breakfast, try the local delicacy, Budbud, a sticky rice dish served wrapped in banana leaves. You can also sample Kinilaw, a local version of ceviche made with fresh fish and vinegar.

For lunch and dinner, try a hearty serving of Boodle Fight, which is a traditional Filipino feast served on a banana leaf and meant to be eaten with your hands. Other must-try dishes include Tinolang Manok, a chicken soup flavored with ginger, and Adobong Pusit, squid cooked in a tangy and savory vinegar sauce.

Local Beverages

For beverages, Mati offers a wide range of refreshing non-alcoholic drinks such as fresh buko juice, a sweet coconut juice drink, and salabat, a ginger tea that is perfect for the cold weather.

If you’re looking for something a bit stronger, you can try the traditional coconut wine called tuba or the local beer called Pale Pilsen.

Where to Stay in Mati: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Mati has plenty of accommodations to meet any traveler’s needs. From luxury hotels to budget-friendly hostels and guesthouses, you can choose based on your budget, location, and preferences.

Some of the most popular hotels to consider include the Dahican Beach Resort, the Mati City Garden Resort, and the Casa Leticia Beach Resort. For those looking for budget-friendly options, the Amihan sa Dahican Surf Resort and Bahay sa Kawayan Backpackers Inn are great choices.

What to Buy in Mati: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

When it comes to souvenirs, Mati has plenty of options to choose from. You can buy local products such as handicrafts, woven bags, and shell accessories, at the local market. You can also bring home a bottle of the traditional coconut wine or the famous crunchy and cheesy Cocoa de Mati, which is a unique take on chocolate bars.

Local Culture and Customs of Mati

Mati’s culture and customs are heavily influenced by the indigenous Mandaya tribe, who are known for their intricate weaving and beadwork. Visitors can witness the tribe’s unique cultural traditions during festivals such as the Kaimonan Festival, which celebrates the tribe’s thanksgiving for a bountiful harvest.

Best Time to Visit Mati

The best time to visit Mati is during the dry season, which runs from November to April. This period offers perfect weather for outdoor activities, such as hiking or beach hopping.

Do’s and Don’ts of Mati

Do:

  1. Respect the local traditions and customs of the indigenous tribes.
  2. Bring sunscreen, insect repellent, and proper hiking gear if you are planning to explore the outdoors.
  3. Support local businesses, such as food stalls and souvenir shops.

Don’t:

  1. Litter or damage the environment, especially in national parks and beaches.
  2. Disrespect sacred places, such as shrines or temples.
  3. Wear skimpy clothing in public places, as it may offend the local culture.

Safety and Health

Safety in Mati

Mati is generally a safe place to travel, but it’s always best to exercise caution and common sense. Make sure to keep your valuables safe and avoid traveling alone at night. When going on outdoor activities, always follow safety guidelines and listen to your tour guide’s instructions.

Health in Mati

To avoid getting sick during your stay in Mati, always drink bottled or boiled water. Keep a hand sanitizer and wash your hands regularly, especially before eating. Make sure to get travel insurance that covers medical expenses and emergency evacuations.

Getting to Mati: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Mati

The nearest airport to Mati is Francisco Bangoy International Airport, also known as Davao International Airport, which is located in Davao City, about 179 kilometers away. From there, you can take a private vehicle or public transportation to Mati.

Bus Stations Near Mati

From Davao City or Manila, you can take a bus to Mati. The biggest bus companies that serve this route include Bachelor Express, Davao Metro Shuttle, and Philtranco.

Other Options

If you don’t want to take a bus, you can also rent a private car or a van for a more convenient and comfortable travel experience.

How to Get Around in Mati

The most common transportation option in Mati is tricycles, which are motorized three-wheeled vehicles that can take you around the city. Taxis and jeepneys are also available, but they are less common. If you’re feeling adventurous, you can also rent a motorbike or a bicycle to explore the city at your own pace.

Where to Go Next from Mati: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

If you’re looking to explore more of Davao Oriental, you can visit the Aliwagwag Falls, a natural wonder with 84 cascading waterfalls that stretch over 1,000 feet. Another destination is the Cape San Agustin, the southernmost point of the Davao Oriental province, which offers stunning views of the Pacific Ocean and the Saranggani Bay.

Conclusion

Mati is a beautiful destination that boasts stunning natural scenery, rich cultural heritage, and exciting outdoor activities. With this comprehensive guide, you can plan your perfect trip and explore the city’s must-visit attractions, food and drink scene, accommodations, and transportation options. Discover the beauty of Mati and immerse yourself in the vibrant local culture that makes the Philippines a beloved travel destination.
