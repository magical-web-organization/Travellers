Introduction to Davao

Welcome to Davao, the largest city on the island of Mindanao in the Philippines. Blessed with natural beauty and cultural diversity, Davao offers a unique blend of leisure and adventure activities that cater to every type of traveler. Whether you’re looking for a relaxing beach vacation, yearning to explore pristine forests and mountain ranges, or seeking to immerse yourself in local culture, Davao has something for everyone.

As a bustling city that prides itself on cleanliness and orderliness, Davao feels safe and welcoming for both locals and tourists alike. Despite its size, the city has managed to preserve its natural wonders and cultural heritage. In this travel guide, we’ll explore the best places to visit, things to do, and tips to ensure your trip to Davao is a memorable one.

List of Places to Visit in Davao

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Davao Museum - Discover the rich cultural heritage of Davao by visiting the Davao Museum. The museum features an extensive collection of artifacts, including pottery, textiles, and jewelry, as well as the history of the Bagobo and Mandaya indigenous tribes.

People’s Park - Located at the heart of Davao City, the People’s Park is more than just a park. It’s a cultural and historical hub that showcases the city’s proud heritage. The park features intricately designed statues and outdoor sculptures, floral gardens, Japanese-themed koi ponds, and a mini-forest with an impressive waterfall.

San Pedro Cathedral - One of the oldest churches in Davao, San Pedro Cathedral was built during the Spanish colonial period in the 1800s. The church is known for its intricate stained-glass windows and impressive bell tower.

Natural Attractions of Davao: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Mount Apo - As the tallest mountain in the Philippines, Mt. Apo is a favorite destination for adventurous visitors. A day trip to the mountain offers a chance to hike, climb, or even camp out overnight. Visitors should hire a local guide or join a hiking tour to ensure their safety and enjoyment.

SAMAL Island - Known for its pristine beaches, Samal Island is a must-visit destination for beach lovers. The island offers a range of beach resorts, water sports adventures such as kayaking, snorkeling, and diving, and island hopping tours to nearby isles.

Crocodile Park - Experience wildlife up-close at the Philippine Crocodile Farm and Nature Park in Davao City. The park is home to a variety of crocodile species, as well as other exotic animals such as snakes, birds, and primates. Visitors can take a guided tour for an educational experience on the conservation of these endangered species.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Davao

Davao to Samal Island - This picturesque route takes travelers through scenic mountains, lush forests, and twists and turns of a well-paved scenic road. The route is perfect for cycling enthusiasts and hiking adventurers alike, with plenty of rest points along the way to enjoy the stunning views.

Laudato Si Trail - Laudato Si is a three-kilometer trail located in the Apo View Hotel Compound that traverses through a forest. The route passes over a hanging bridge over a rivulet and offers hikers a peek into the beautiful flora and fauna of Davao’s tropical rainforest.

Best Things to Do in Davao: Activities and Tours

Eden Nature Park and Resort - Experience the rustic charm and lush beauty of nature at the Eden Nature Park and Resort. Visitors can zip line through the forest, raft down a river, tour a butterfly sanctuary or take a guided walk through orchards and gardens.

Davao City Food and Cultural Tour - Explore the diverse culinary landscape of Davao City on a half-day food tour. The tour takes visitors on a tasting journey, from street food and local snacks to traditional Filipino dishes and exotic fruits.

Food and Drink Scene of Davao

Must-Try Dishes

Kadios Baboy Langka - A traditional Visayan dish, Kadios Baboy Langka is a stew that mixes tender pork with fresh jackfruit and locally-sourced black beans.

Durian - Davao is considered the durian capital of the Philippines. The fruit has a distinct sharp smell and unique taste that leaves an unforgettable experience on the visitor’s taste buds.

Local Beverages

Sikwate - A traditional Filipino hot chocolate made from tablea (pure cacao) mixed with sugar and hot water or milk. It is usually paired with rice cakes and eaten as a staple breakfast food.

Barako Coffee - A strong and flavorful brew made from liberica beans, which are indigenous to the Philippines. Davao is famous for producing some of the best-quality barako coffee in the country.

Where to Stay in Davao: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Marco Polo Davao - A five-star hotel located in the heart of the city, Marco Polo offers luxury amenities and stunning city views.

The Royal Mandaya Hotel - A budget-friendly accommodation option that features comfortable rooms and a central location near major tourist attractions.

What to Buy in Davao: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Malong - A traditional woven blanket, the malong is a popular souvenir in Davao. It can be worn as a sarong, used as a shawl, or hung on the wall as wall art.

Pomelo - Known as the “king of fruits” in Davao, pomelo is a sweet and tangy citrus fruit that is abundant in the region. Visitors can bring home freshly peeled pomelo or opt to purchase bottled pomelo juice.

Local Culture and Customs of Davao

Davaoeno Culture - The culture of Davao City is heavily influenced by its surroundings. The city is home to various indigenous tribes, and their traditions and customs are part of the city’s heritage.

Best Time to Visit Davao

The best time to visit Davao is from December to February or March. The weather during these months is cooler and comfortable, making it perfect for outdoor adventures and exploration of the city. Tourists should avoid visit the region during the rainy months from June to October.

Do’s and Don’ts of Davao

Do:

  • Respect local customs and culture
  • Visit the city’s cultural and historical landmarks
  • Explore outdoor activities available in the region, such as mountain climbing, island hopping, and wildlife sanctuary tours

Don’t:

  • Litter in public spaces
  • Disrespect members of local indigenous communities or make fun of their cultural traditions
  • Wear revealing clothing in cultural and religious sites

Safety and Health

Safety in Davao

Davao City is generally safe for tourists. However, visitors should exercise caution when traveling late at night and avoid less populated areas. While exploring outdoor adventures such as mountain climbing, hiking, or beach tours, it is advisable to follow all safety precautions to avoid accidents.

Health in Davao

Before leaving for Davao, tourists should get vaccinated for hepatitis A, typhoid, and tetanus. This is especially true for tourists who will be exploring outdoor activities or spending time in rural areas.

Getting to Davao: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Davao

Davao International Airport - Located in the heart of Davao City, the airport is the primary air gateway into the region. The airport has international and domestic flights available that operate daily.

Bus Stations Near Davao

Buhangin Bus Station - Located in the city center, Buhangin Bus Station is a popular option for commuting in and out of the city. Buses leave for nearby cities such as General Santos City and Tagum City frequently.

Other Options

Taxis and Grab - Taxis and Grab services in Davao City are widely available and a popular choice for tourists who want to explore the city. Additionally, car rentals and private car services are also available for tourists who prefer to have their transportation on standby.

How to Get Around in Davao

Jeepneys – Jeepneys are a cheap and popular transport option for tourists exploring Davao city. One ride can cost around 8 pesos and can take you anywhere around the city.

Taxis and Grab – Taxis and Grab services operate 24/7 and are a convenient option for travelers who want a comfortable and private ride.

Where to Go Next from Davao: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

CDO – Short for Cagayan de Oro, is a bustling city located north of Davao city, it’s known for river rafting expeditions and is locally referred to as the adventure capital of the Philippines

Siargao – A picturesque island located in Surigao Del Norte, Siargao is known for its magnificent waves, crystal-clear waters, and fascinating lagoons perfect for diving and island hopping.

Conclusion

This concludes our comprehensive travel guide to Davao, the bustling city on the island of Mindanao in the Philippines. From hikes up Mt. Apo to island hopping around Samal Island, from food tours to cultural excursions, Davao offers a blend of activities and experiences that cater to every type of traveler. With its natural beauty, cultural diversity, and welcoming locals, Davao should be on your bucket list of places to explore. We hope that this guide has helped you plan your perfect getaway to Davao, and we look forward to welcoming you to our beautiful city.
