Introduction to Mount Apo

Mount Apo is the highest mountain in the Philippines, with its peak standing at 2,954 meters above sea level. It is located in the Davao Region, in Mindanao, the southernmost island in the Philippines. Mount Apo is a popular destination for hikers, nature enthusiasts, and adventurers because of its breathtaking views of the Davao Gulf, the vast Mindanao landscape, and the volcanic crater filled with hot springs and crystal-clear lake. This travel guide will help you explore Mount Apo and discover the best places to visit, hiking and biking routes, activities, food and drink scene, accommodations, local culture and customs, safety tips, transportation, and nearby destinations.

Week-Long Itinerary for Mount Apo

Day Activity
1 Arrive at Davao International Airport, travel to Kidapawan City.
2 Start a Mt. Apo hike; stay overnight at Tinikaran Campsite.
3 Trekking to peak, manage descent; overnight at mountain lake resort.
4 Lake Agco Hot Springs camping and spa experience.
5 Go on a nature trek at Tamayong Prayer Mountain and tour the Davao Museum of History and Ethnography.
6 Adventure at Camp Sabros Zip line, explore Davao Crocodile Park.
7 Depart from Mount Apo or visit nearby destinations such as Malagos Garden, Samal Island, or Mount Matutum.

Food and Drink Scene of Mount Apo

Must-Try Dishes

  1. Kinilaw - fish ceviche with ginger
  2. Durian - a pungent fruit famous in Mindanao
  3. Adobo - marinated meat with soy sauce and vinegar
  4. Mangosteen - a sweet tropical fruit
  5. Satti - a Muslim dish made of grilled beef or chicken wrapped in banana leaves

Local Beverages

  1. Barako coffee - a famous local blend
  2. Tuba - coconut wine
  3. Pineapple juice - a refreshing drink

What to Buy in Mount Apo: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Local Culture and Customs of Mount Apo

  1. Respect the culture and beliefs of the locals, who are mainly Lumads and Muslims.
  2. Be aware of appropriate dress codes for the vicinity of sacred sites and mosques
  3. Say “thank you” in the local language, which is a form of respect
  4. Do not bring alcohol, pork, or any prohibited substances when visiting sacred sites

Best Time to Visit Mount Apo

  1. The best time to visit Mount Apo is during the dry season, which starts from November to April.

Do’s and Don’ts of Mount Apo

Do:

  1. Bring enough bottled water and pack foods for the hiking trip
  2. Follow the itinerary or the advice of the guide
  3. Respect the environment by not littering and damaging the natural sites
  4. Follow the rules and regulations inside the parks and protected areas

Don’t:

  1. Remove any plants and animals from parks or protected areas
  2. Starting fires within mountain areas
  3. Leave trash on the mountain trails
  4. Bring plastics and other non-biodegradable materials

Safety and Health

Safety in Mount Apo

  1. Always check the weather before heading out for a hike.
  2. Never hike alone and always follow the guide or instructions by authorities.
  3. Bring a first-aid kit when hiking and wear appropriate footwear.
  4. Avoid hiking during bad weather or if there are advisory warnings.

Health in Mount Apo

  1. Prepare for climate changes and keep warm when needed.
  2. Always bring medications if required and enough drinking water to avoid dehydration and altitude sickness.
  3. Do not take any food or drinks offered by strangers, especially during trekking.
