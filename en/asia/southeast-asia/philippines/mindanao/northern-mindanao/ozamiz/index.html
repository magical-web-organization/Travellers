Introduction to Ozamiz

Located in Northern Mindanao, in the southernmost island of the Philippines, Ozamiz is a city known for its rich history, culture, and natural attractions. With a population of around 150,000 people, the city is one of the most visited tourist destinations in Mindanao.

List of Places to Visit in Ozamiz

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  1. Ozamiz City Museum - showcases Ozamiz’s rich cultural heritage and history, including the city’s founding and development, traditional costumes, and musical instruments.
  2. Cotta Fort - a well-preserved Spanish-era fortress built to protect Ozamiz from pirate attacks in the 18th century.
  3. Immaculate Conception Cathedral - a centuries-old Baroque-style church famous for its intricate design and architecture, declared a National Cultural Treasure by the Philippine government.

Natural Attractions of Ozamiz: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  1. Bukagan Hill - a popular spot for hiking and biking with an elevated view of Ozamiz and the surrounding areas.
  2. Mt. Malindang Range Natural Park - a protected area with diverse flora and fauna, natural hot springs, and waterfalls, suitable for trekking and camping.
  3. Malaubang Nature Park - a serene eco-park with a lagoon, children’s playground, and picnic area.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Ozamiz

  1. Bukagan Hill Hike - a two-hour trek to the peak, set amid lush greenery and scenic views of Ozamiz.
  2. Biking to the Malindang Mountain Range - a challenging route that takes you through dense forests, mountain streams, and steep climbs that offer breathtaking views of the mountain range.

Best Things to Do in Ozamiz: Activities and Tours

  1. Explore the City by Tricycle - an experience unique to the Philippines, a tricycle is a motorbike with a sidecar, and it is Ozamiz’s primary mode of transportation.
  2. Visit the Local Wet Market - witness Ozamiz’s vibrant and bustling marketplace, where fresh seafood, fruits, vegetables, and meats are sold.

Week-Long Itinerary for Ozamiz

Day Activity
Day 1 Visit the Fort Santiago Shrine and Museum, followed by a scenic drive to Bukagan Hill
Day 2 Hike to the peak of Bukagan Hill early in the morning. Spend the afternoon exploring the Ozamiz City Museum
Day 3 Visit the historic Immaculate Conception Cathedral and then take a tricycle ride to Ozamiz’s famous wet market.
Day 4 Take a day trip to Malaubang Nature Park and spend the day enjoying picnics, swimming, and the scenic views
Day 5 Bike to the Malindang Mountain Range, a challenging trek that will take the day to complete but offers breathtaking views at the top.
Day 6 Take a break from the long hikes and bike rides by exploring the city’s parks and restaurants
Day 7 Take a day trip to Tangub City, located just an hour’s drive away, and visit the popular Panguil Bay Bridge and replica of the Eiffel Tower

Food and Drink Scene of Ozamiz

Must-Try Dishes

  1. Lechon - Ozamiz’s signature dish, a whole roasted pig stuffed with herbs and spices, with crispy skin and succulent meat.
  2. Buko Pie - a local pastry made with coconut meat and milk, butter, and a flaky crust.

Local Beverages

  1. Siphon-Brewed Coffee - Ozamiz is known for its distinct coffee flavor, brewed using a siphon or vacuum pot. It’s best paired with Buko Pie or other local pastries.

Where to Stay in Ozamiz: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  1. Royal Garden Hotel - a 3-star hotel located in the heart of Ozamiz, with modern facilities and comfortable rooms.
  2. Sunlight Guest Hotel - a budget-friendly option with clean rooms and helpful staff.
  3. Hotel Alron - another affordable option located near the city center, with basic amenities and cozy rooms.

What to Buy in Ozamiz: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  1. Bariw Bags and Baskets - made from the leaves of a local plant called Buri, these bags are sturdy and stylish.
  2. Handwoven Textiles - Ozamiz is famous for its vibrant and colorful fabrics, made using local techniques and materials.

Local Culture and Customs of Ozamiz

Ozamiz’s culture is heavily influenced by its history and geography. The locals are predominantly Catholic and are known for their hospitality and warmth towards visitors.

Best Time to Visit Ozamiz

The best time to visit Ozamiz is between December and May, when the weather is dry and sunny, and the temperatures are not too hot. The rainy season starts in June and lasts until November, so it’s best to avoid traveling during this time.

Do’s and Don’ts of Ozamiz

Do:

  1. Try the local cuisine and coffee.
  2. Respect the local culture and customs.
  3. Dress modestly when visiting religious sites.

Don’t:

  1. Wander around alone at night.
  2. Disrespect the local traditions and beliefs.
  3. Neglect your health and safety.

Safety and Health

Safety in Ozamiz

Ozamiz is a safe city for tourists, but it’s always best to take precautionary measures to avoid any untoward incidents, such as taking taxis or tricycles instead of walking alone at night and avoiding wearing expensive jewelry or carrying large amounts of cash.

Health in Ozamiz

It’s essential to take necessary health precautions such as drinking only bottled water, washing your hands regularly, applying mosquito repellent, and carrying a first-aid kit, especially if you plan to go on outdoor adventures.

Getting to Ozamiz: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Ozamiz

Ozamiz City Airport is the nearest airport, with domestic flights from regional airlines.

Bus Stations Near Ozamiz

Several bus companies serve Ozamiz, connecting it to major cities in Mindanao and other parts of the Philippines.

Other Options

Ferries and boats are also available from nearby cities and ports such as Cagayan de Oro, Iligan, and Zamboanga.

How to Get Around in Ozamiz

The primary mode of transportation is the tricycle, which can take you to most places within the city. Taxis and vans are also available for longer distances.

Where to Go Next from Ozamiz: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  1. Tangub City - visit the Panguil Bay Bridge and see the replica of the Eiffel Tower.
  2. Mount Malindang Natural Park - go on a camping and trekking adventure.
  3. Cagayan de Oro - experience the thrill of whitewater rafting.

Conclusion

Ozamiz is a beautiful city with an abundance of cultural attractions, outdoor adventures, and delicious cuisine. With this comprehensive tour guide, you’re now ready to explore all the best that Ozamiz has to offer.
