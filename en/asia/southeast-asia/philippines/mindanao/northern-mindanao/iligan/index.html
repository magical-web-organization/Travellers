Introduction to Iligan

Located in the Northern Mindanao region of the Philippines, Iligan is a beautiful and picturesque city surrounded by natural wonders and cultural landmarks. Known as the “City of Waterfalls,” Iligan is home to over 20 stunning waterfalls, making it a popular destination for nature lovers and adventure seekers. This travel guide will help you plan your trip to Iligan, with tips on the best places to visit, things to do, where to stay, and how to get around.

Local Culture and Customs of Iligan

Iliganons are known for their religious practices, with most of the population being Catholic. The city is also home to an indigenous tribe called the Higaonons, who have their own unique traditions and customs.

Best Time to Visit Iligan

The best time to visit Iligan is from November to May, when the weather is dry and temperate. The wet season usually lasts from June to October, which can make outdoor activities challenging.

Do’s and Don’ts of Iligan

Do:

  • Dress modestly when visiting religious landmarks
  • Respect the indigenous culture and customs
  • Explore the city’s natural attractions

Don’t:

  • Disrespect the locals
  • Litter or damage the environment
  • Treat the attractions as a playground

Safety and Health

Safety in Iligan

Iligan is a relatively safe city for tourists, with a low crime rate. However, it’s always important to take extra precautions when traveling, such as keeping your valuables secured and being aware of your surroundings.

Health in Iligan

Make sure to get your recommended vaccinations before traveling to the Philippines, and be cautious when eating street food. Drink only bottled water, and use mosquito repellent to prevent mosquito-borne illnesses.

How to Get Around in Iligan

The best way to get around Iligan is by renting a motorbike or hiring a tricycle driver. There are also jeepneys and buses available for transport, but they can be crowded and uncomfortable.
