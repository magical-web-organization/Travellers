Valencia (Bukidnon) is a serene and beautiful municipality nestled in the heart of Northern Mindanao, Philippines. This rustic countryside is famous for its scenic views, natural attractions, and cultural heritage. In this comprehensive travel guide, we’ll delve into the must-visit attractions, food and drink scene, hiking and biking routes, and local customs, making your trip to Valencia (Bukidnon) an unforgettable experience.

Introduction to Valencia (Bukidnon)

Valencia (Bukidnon) is a town located in the central part of Bukidnon, a province in the Philippines. It is home to breathtaking views of lush forests, rolling hills, verdant plains, and majestic mountains that will leave you awestruck. Valencia is fondly called the “City of Golden Friendship” as its people are known for their warmth, hospitality, and kindheartedness, and they’re eager to welcome visitors from all over the world.

List of Places to Visit in Valencia (Bukidnon)

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Valencia is rich in cultural heritage, and there are plenty of museums and historical sites that you should visit. One of them is the Valencia Museum, which showcases the town’s history, traditions, and culture. You will also find the San Agustin Parish Church, which has stood firm amidst the test of time. This beautiful church features a Baroque-style altar and the famous mural paintings by National Artist for Painting, Vicente Manansala.

Natural Attractions of Valencia (Bukidnon): Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Valencia boasts a plethora of natural attractions that will leave visitors spellbound. The must-visit spot is the Mount Kitanglad Range Natural Park. It is a UNESCO World Heritage site and a sanctuary for endangered species such as Philippine Eagles and tarsiers. Additionally, the town features the Pulangi River, a long twisty river that offers an ideal escape for kayaking or river rafting. You can also explore the Malingon Falls, a majestic seven-tiered waterfall that is surrounded by lush greenery.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Valencia (Bukidnon)

Valencia is a hiker’s paradise with several hiking destinations that offer stunning views of the town’s picturesque landscapes. One of the recommended routes is the Valencia Forest Park, with an elevation of 1,150 meters above sea level. For bikers, Valencia offers the iconic impasug-ong-Valencia trail, a beautiful route featuring a mix of paved roads, dirt paths, and forests.

Best Things to Do in Valencia (Bukidnon): Activities and Tours

Valencia has plenty of fun activities and tours that are perfect for anyone who loves adventure, nature, and culture. You can take an eco-friendly tour and learn how to make a traditional Bukidnon cloth called “t’nalak” or witness an enchanting display of fireflies at night. Alternatively, you can visit Honey Bee Farms, where you can sample some of the best honey in the country.

Week-Long Itinerary for Valencia (Bukidnon)

Valencia is a town that deserves more than just a day trip, with plenty of things to do in its beautiful wilderness. A week-long itinerary for Valencia can include hiking, biking, exploring the natural parks and river systems as well as learning the local culture and watching the sunset or star gaze at night at one of the town’s observation decks.

Food and Drink Scene of Valencia (Bukidnon)

Must-Try Dishes

Valencia is famous for its unique local delicacies. Among them is the Bukidnon Rice Cake, a flavorful and slightly sweet rice cake made from glutinous rice and mixed with coconut cream. You can also try the famous “lechon baboy,” a roasted pig that is a staple on special occasions.

Local Beverages

Valencia is famous for its coffee beans, and you can visit one of the coffee farms and roasting stations to experience the town’s coffee culture. Aside from coffee, Valencia is home to some of the best pineapple plantations in the country, and you can enjoy refreshing pineapple juice or cocktails.

Where to Stay in Valencia (Bukidnon): Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Valencia offers plenty of accommodation options to suit different budgets. For backpackers, there are affordable guesthouses and hostels around the town center. The area also features some luxurious hotels and resorts with fantastic views of the surrounding mountains, countryside, and forests.

What to Buy in Valencia (Bukidnon): Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Valencia is known for its craftsmanship, and you can find unique handmade souvenirs such as woven baskets, blankets, and trinkets. You can also bring home some of the best coffee, honey, and pineapple products in the country.

Local Culture and Customs of Valencia (Bukidnon)

Valencia honors its indigenous people, and you can witness cultural performances such as the famous “Kaamulan Festival,” showcasing diverse Bukidnon folklore and traditions. Additionally, the locals are known for their friendliness and genuine smiles, so don’t be shy to ask them about their daily lives and experiences in the town.

Best Time to Visit Valencia (Bukidnon)

The best time to visit Valencia is during the town’s festivities. The Kaamulan is celebrated every April, showcasing Bukidnon’s rich culture and traditions, offering a full week of exciting activities and performances.

Do’s and Don’ts of Valencia (Bukidnon)

Do:

  • Respect the locals’ culture and traditions.
  • Try local dishes and products.
  • Take your trash with you and maintain cleanliness.

Don’t:

  • Leave sensitive items in plain sight.
  • Disrespect the environment by littering.
  • Speak ill of local traditions and beliefs.

Safety and Health

Safety in Valencia (Bukidnon)

Valencia is a peaceful and safe town with friendly locals. However, travel with caution, especially in secluded areas, and keep an eye on your belongings at all times. Always follow safety protocols when hiking or exploring natural attractions.

Health in Valencia (Bukidnon)

It is always recommended to have medical insurance when traveling. Valencia has a medical center should you need medical assistance, but it is best to bring any necessary medications or personal hygiene products.

Getting to Valencia (Bukidnon): Transportation Options and Logistics

Valencia is accessible via the main roads and highways, such as the Cagayan de Oro-Bukidnon national highway. There are buses and vans available from the city of Cagayan de Oro that will take you to Valencia in just an hour. Alternatively, you can also hire a car or motorbike and take your time exploring the scenic routes.

Airports Near Valencia (Bukidnon)

The nearest international airport is the Laguindingan Airport, located in Misamis Oriental, around 52 km away from Valencia. From here, you can take a bus or taxi to Valencia.

Bus Stations Near Valencia (Bukidnon)

Buses and vans to Valencia are available from Cagayan de Oro Bus Station.

How to Get Around Valencia (Bukidnon)

Valencia has various modes of transportation such as tricycles, buses, vans, jeepneys, and private cars, which can take you around the town and other nearby destinations.

Where to Go Next from Valencia (Bukidnon): Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Valencia is surrounded by other cities and towns with exciting destinations and activities. You can visit the provincial capital Malaybalay, the refreshing Lake Apo, or the famous white-sand beaches of Iligan.

Conclusion

Valencia (Bukidnon) is an incredible destination in the Philippines, offering a truly unique experience to travelers. Make sure to take note of the things to do, where to stay, the food and drink scene, and local customs and traditions to get the most out of your Valencia adventure. With its beautiful natural landscape, rich culture, and friendly locals, Valencia is a place that you would not want to miss.
