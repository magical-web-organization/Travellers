Introduction to Cagayan de Oro

Cagayan de Oro, or popularly known as CDO, is a charming and vibrant city in the Northern Mindanao region of the Philippines. It is the capital of Misamis Oriental province and is located along the northern coast of Mindanao island. The city is known for its breathtaking natural wonders, rich cultural heritage, and exciting outdoor activities. From visiting historical landmarks to hiking and river rafting, Cagayan de Oro has something to offer for every kind of traveler. If you are planning to visit this delightful city, this comprehensive travel guide will help you make the most out of your trip.

List of Places to Visit in Cagayan de Oro

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Museo de Oro - A small museum showcasing the rich cultural heritage of the Higaonon and other indigenous tribes of Mindanao.

  • Gaston Park - One of the oldest and most significant landmarks in the city, featuring a monument of national hero, Dr. Jose Rizal.

  • St. Augustine Cathedral - A beautiful Neo-Gothic cathedral in the heart of the city, built in honor of St. Augustine of Hippo.

  • Macahambus Hill Cave - A historical site where Filipino revolutionaries won the Battle of Macahambus in 1900 against the Americans during the Philippine-American War.

Natural Attractions of Cagayan de Oro: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Mapawa Nature Park - A nature park with a range of activities such as horseback riding, trekking, ziplining, and swimming in the waterfalls.

  • Dahilayan Adventure Park - A popular adventure park with thrilling outdoor activities such as ziplining, rope courses, and ATV rides.

  • White Water Rafting - Cagayan de Oro is known as the white water rafting capital of the Philippines, offering exciting rafting experiences for beginners and pros.

  • Mount Kitanglad Range Natural Park - A protected area that covers about 47,270 hectares and is home to various wildlife, including endangered species such as the Philippine Eagle.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Cagayan de Oro

  • Mount Maculot - A popular day trip hike with scenic views of Cagayan de Oro and its surrounding mountains.

  • Mt. Balatucan - An easy hike with amazing views of the city and the Cagayan de Oro River.

  • CDO Eco Park - A nature park with various hiking and biking trails and scenic views of the city.

Best Things to Do in Cagayan de Oro: Activities and Tours

  • Red Rafts - A river rafting experience that offers a unique and exciting way to explore the Cagayan de Oro River.

  • Garden of Malasag Eco-Tourism Village - A nature park that showcases the rich cultural heritage of the indigenous tribes of Mindanao.

  • Cagayan de Oro City Tour - A tour of the city’s historical landmarks, museums, and natural attractions.

Food and Drink Scene of Cagayan de Oro

Must-Try Dishes

  • Satti - A popular local dish made of grilled meat skewered and served with a spicy sauce and rice.

  • Lechon de CDO - A roasted pig dish that is known for its crispy skin and flavorful meat.

  • Kinilaw - A local seafood dish that is similar to ceviche, made with fresh fish, vinegar, and spices.

Local Beverages

  • Suman Latik - A local dessert made of sticky rice and coconut milk sauce served with a cup of coffee.

  • Tablea Tsokolate - A hot chocolate drink made of local cacao and brown sugar.

  • Buko Juice - A refreshing drink made of young coconut water and meat.

Where to Stay in Cagayan de Oro: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Limketkai Luxe Hotel - A luxurious hotel that offers comfortable and spacious rooms, a spa, and a pool.

  • Seda Centrio - A modern hotel that offers beautifully designed rooms, a rooftop pool, and a fitness center.

  • CDO Hotel Xentro - A budget-friendly guesthouse that provides clean rooms and a convenient location.

What to Buy in Cagayan de Oro: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Higaonon Weave - A type of woven cloth made by the indigenous people of Bukidnon.

  • Vjandep Pastel - A local pastry filled with sweet yema or custard.

  • Pearls - The Philippines is known for its high-quality pearls, and Cagayan de Oro has various pearl shops that offer authentic and unique designs.

Local Culture and Customs of Cagayan de Oro

  • CDO is a predominantly Catholic city, so always be respectful when visiting churches and other religious sites.
  • The indigenous tribes of Mindanao have a rich cultural heritage, and it is essential to learn about their customs and traditions.
  • Shaking hands and greeting with a smile is a common way to show respect and courtesy.

Best Time to Visit Cagayan de Oro

The best time to visit Cagayan de Oro is from December to February as it is the dry season, and the weather is pleasant. The months of March to May are also good to visit, but it can get very hot during these months. It is advised to avoid the rainy season from June to October as some outdoor activities may be limited due to the weather.

Do’s and Don’ts of Cagayan de Oro

Do:

  • Try local dishes and drinks, such as Satti, Kinilaw, Tablea Tsokolate, and Buko Juice.
  • Learn about the rich cultural heritage of the Indigenous people of Mindanao.
  • Carry a phrasebook or learn a few basic Filipino language phrases to help you communicate with the locals.

Don’t:

  • Don’t litter or leave garbage in public places.
  • Don’t forget to respect local customs and traditions.
  • Don’t forget to always keep your valuables safe and secure.

Safety and Health

Safety in Cagayan de Oro

Cagayan de Oro is generally a safe city for visitors, but it is essential to take necessary precautions such as:

  • Avoid isolated or poorly lit areas, especially at night.
  • Always keep your valuables secure and never leave them unattended.
  • Be vigilant and aware of your surroundings and avoid carrying large sums of money in public.

Health in Cagayan de Oro

It is recommended to have updated vaccinations before visiting Cagayan de Oro. Travelers must also pay attention to hygiene and food safety to avoid getting sick.

Getting to Cagayan de Oro: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Cagayan de Oro

  • Laguindingan Airport - The nearest airport to CDO, located about 46 kilometers away, with daily flights to major cities in the Philippines.

Bus Stations Near Cagayan de Oro

  • Cagayan de Oro Bus Terminal - Located in the heart of the city, with regular trips to various destinations in Northern Mindanao and Visayas.

Other Options

  • Ferries - CDO is accessible by ferry, with trips from nearby islands, such as Camiguin and Bohol.
  • Car rental or private transfers - These options are available in CDO, but it is essential to book in advance.

How to Get Around in Cagayan de Oro

  • Jeepneys and Tricycles - The most common and affordable public transportation in CDO.
  • Taxis and Grab - Available 24/7 in the city and can be booked through mobile applications.
  • Car rental and Motorbikes - These options are also available but are not as commonly used as the other forms of transportation.

Where to Go Next from Cagayan de Oro: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Camiguin Island

A popular island destination with beautiful beaches, stunning waterfalls, hot springs, and volcanic mountains.

Bukidnon Province

Known for its vast pineapple plantations, rolling hills, and unique adventure parks such as the Dahilayan Adventure Park.

Iligan City

Famous for its numerous waterfalls, including Maria Cristina Falls, known as the “twin falls.”

Conclusion

Cagayan de Oro is a must-visit destination in the Philippines, offering a perfect blend of natural wonders, rich cultural heritage, and exciting outdoor activities. Explore the city, indulge in local cuisine, and experience the warm hospitality of locals by following this comprehensive travel guide.
