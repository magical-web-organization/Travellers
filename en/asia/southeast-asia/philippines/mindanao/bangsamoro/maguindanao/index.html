Maguindanao is a province in the Bangsamoro Autonomous Region in Muslim Mindanao (BARMM) of the Philippines. Due to its cultural and historical importance, Maguindanao has become a destination for both local and foreign tourists. From its rich history, stunning natural attractions to vibrant local culture and savory cuisine, this guide will help you discover and explore Maguindanao.

Introduction to Maguindanao

Maguindanao is a landlocked province situated in the hinterlands of Mindanao. It is known for its lush forests, majestic waterfalls and picturesque mountains. Maguindanao is home to the Maguindanaoan people, who have a unique culture and language. It is a predominantly Muslim province and is one of the few places in the Philippines where Sharia law is observed.

List of Places to Visit in Maguindanao

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  1. Tamontaka Church - One of the oldest churches in Mindanao built during the Spanish colonial period.
  2. Sultan Kudarat Monument- A monument built to honor Sultan Kudarat, the last Muslim ruler who opposed the Spanish colonization of Mindanao.
  3. Shariff Aguak Mosque- One of the oldest mosques in the Philippines built in the 16th century.
  4. Datu Piang Marker- A marker that signifies the bravery of the Maguindanao warriors who fought against the American forces.

Natural Attractions of Maguindanao: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  1. Awang Falls- A stunning waterfall with a natural pool, perfect for swimming and relaxing.
  2. Kabakaba Hill- A scenic hill overlooking a vast forest and the picturesque Rio Grande de Mindanao.
  3. Liguasan Marsh- The largest marshland in the Philippines known for its diverse wildlife and stunning sunset views.
  4. Mt. Minandar- A towering mountain that is ideal for hiking and camping, offering a breathtaking view of the surrounding landscape.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Maguindanao

Maguindanao offers several hiking and biking routes with stunning views of nature. Some of the popular routes include:

  1. Kabakaba-Kalidsing Falls Trek- A moderate three-hour hike to see the stunning Kabakaba Falls and Kalidsing Falls.
  2. Tigbaon Falls Trail- A two-hour hike across forested hills leading to the majestic Tigbaon Falls.

Best Things to Do in Maguindanao: Activities and Tours

  1. Explore the cultural heritage sites of Maguindanao by going on a guided tour of Tamontaka Church, Sultan Kudarat Monument, Shariff Aguak Mosque, and Datu Piang Marker.
  2. Swim and relax in the natural pools of Awang Falls.
  3. Go camping and hiking at Mt. Minandar.
  4. Visit Liguasan Marsh and learn about the diverse wildlife and ecological importance of the marsh.
  5. Explore the bustling market in Datu Odin Sinsuat.
  6. Savor the local delicacies.

Week-Long Itinerary for Maguindanao

Here’s a sample itinerary for a week-long holiday in Maguindanao:

Day 1: Arrival at Awang Airport, Explore Tamontaka Church and Sultan Kudarat Monument Day 2: Trek to Kabakaba-Kalidsing Falls Day 3: Explore Shariff Aguak Mosque and Datu Piang Marker Day 4: Discover the beauty of Liguasan Marsh Day 5: Visit the bustling market in Datu Odin Sinsuat Day 6: Climb Mt. Minandar and witness the stunning view from the top Day 7: Departure

Food and Drink Scene of Maguindanao

Must-Try Dishes

  1. Palapa- A savory mixture of grated coconut, chili, and ginger that is served as a condiment.
  2. Pastil- A rice dish wrapped in banana leaves with chicken or beef, served with palapa on the side.
  3. Tiula Itum - A soup made of beef, spices, and burnt coconut meat.

Local Beverages

  1. Kapeng Maguindanao - A local coffee blend made from Liberica beans.
  2. Salabat- A ginger tea that is perfect for cold weather.

Where to Stay in Maguindanao: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Here are some accommodation options in Maguindanao:

  1. Family Country Hotel and Convention Center - A 3-star hotel located in Datu Odin Sinsuat with comfortable rooms and amenities such as a swimming pool, clubhouse, and restaurant.
  2. Awang Airport Hotel - A budget-friendly hotel located near the airport, offering comfortable rooms and a restaurant.
  3. Gaga’s Garden and Resort - A nature resort located in Sultan Kudarat town that features a swimming pool, restaurant, and cozy cottages.

What to Buy in Maguindanao: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Here are some local products that you can bring back home as souvenirs:

  1. Inaul Cloth- A traditional weaving fabric that is made of cotton and silk that can be used for clothing and home decor.
  2. Barter Trade Items- Maguindanaoans participate in barter trade where they exchange goods such as rice, vegetables, and fruits.

Local Culture and Customs of Maguindanao

The Maguindanao people have a unique culture and value system, influenced by the Islamic faith. Hospitality is an essential part of Maguindanaon culture, and they believe in welcoming guests with open arms. During festivals such as the Kalimudan Festival, the Maguindanaoans showcase their traditional dances and music.

Best Time to Visit Maguindanao

The best time to visit Maguindanao is during the cooler months of December to February when the temperature is cooler and dry.

Do’s and Don’ts of Maguindanao

Do:

  1. Respect the local culture and customs.
  2. Dress appropriately when visiting places of worship.
  3. Be courteous and greet people with “Assalamualaikum.”
  4. Get to know the people and learn their way of life.

Don’t:

  1. Drink alcohol in public places as this is prohibited in Islamic faith.
  2. Disrespect the local customs and traditions.
  3. Wander into unfamiliar territories without the guidance of a local guide.
  4. Engage in provocative behavior.

Safety and Health

Safety in Maguindanao

Maguindanao can be considered a safe destination to visit. However, it is best to take precautions such as avoiding public demonstrations or going to places with known security risks.

Health in Maguindanao

Tourists are advised to take necessary precautions against mosquito bites to avoid diseases such as dengue fever and malaria. It is also advisable to drink bottled water and practice good hygiene.

Getting to Maguindanao: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Maguindanao

  1. Awang Airport in Datu Odin Sinsuat town is the primary airport in Maguindanao.

Bus Stations Near Maguindanao

  1. North Cotabato Bus Terminal in Kidapawan City
  2. Isulan Terminal in Sultan Kudarat town

Other Options

  1. Hire a private vehicle from Davao or Cotabato City
  2. Take a boat from Cotabato City to Rio Grande de Mindanao Port

How to Get Around in Maguindanao

The best way to get around in Maguindanao is by hiring a local guide or driver who knows the area. Public transportation such as tricycles and buses are also available in Datu Odin Sinsuat town and nearby towns.

Where to Go Next from Maguindanao: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  1. Cotabato City - Discover more about the history and culture of the Maguindanao people.
  2. Lake Sebu - Explore the natural beauty of South Cotabato’s famous lake.
  3. Davao City - Visit the Philippine Eagle Center, explore the food scene, and find adventure in Davao’s surrounding areas.

Conclusion

Maguindanao is a province full of natural wonders, cultural gems, and vibrant tradition. It is a destination that is worth visiting for those who want to experience the unique beauty of Mindanao. With this guide, you’ll be able to explore Maguindanao’s stunning landscape, learn about its history and culture, savor its local delicacies, and make unforgettable memories.
