Introduction to Sulu Islands

The Sulu Islands is a province in the Bangsamoro Autonomous Region in the Mindanao region situated in the most southern part of the Philippines. It is known for its natural beauty, vibrant culture, and historic significance.

The Sulu Archipelago includes several small islands and islets, each with its distinct natural and cultural attractions. The islands are known for pristine beaches, virgin jungles, and ancient monuments that reflect its cultural influence. Travelers can enjoy different outdoor adventures such as hiking, diving, snorkeling, and island hopping, to name a few, while experiencing the historical and cultural richness of the islands.

List of Places to Visit in Sulu Islands

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  1. Tulay Mosque - a historical mosque built in 1750 and served as a place of worship for the local Muslim community in Sulu.
  2. Rajah Baginda Shrine - a shrine located in Jolo, Sulu, which honors Rajah Baginda, a legendary figure and one of the early rulers of the Sulu Archipelago.
  3. Bud Daho Massacre Site - a historical site located at the summit of Mt. Bud Daho, where a tragic massacre took place in 1906, involving remaining Moro warriors and American soldiers.
  4. Maimbung - a historic town with various ancient architectures and monumental structures, such as the Astana, Batu Tarsila, and the Palace of Maulana, among others.

Natural Attractions of Sulu Islands: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  1. Tandu Banak Island - an uninhabited island featuring white sand beaches, crystal clear waters, and rich marine life.
  2. Ampullong White Beach - an isolated beach in Patikul, Sulu, famous for its white sand, crystal clear waters, and tranquil atmosphere.
  3. Bud Bongao - a mountain popular for trekking and pilgrimage, known for its breathtaking panoramic views of the Sulu Archipelago and its lush forests.
  4. Capual Island - an uninhabited island off the coast of Jolo, offering an exceptional beach experience where visitors can enjoy activities such as swimming, snorkeling, and picnicking.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Sulu Islands

  1. Mount Tumantangis (Tumantangis) - Located in Jolo at the southernmost part of Sulu, this mountain offers a challenging trek and scenic views of the Sulu Archipelago.
  2. Mount Dajo (Bud Dajo) - A dormant volcano located in Jolo, this mountain offers a challenging trek and a glimpse of the rich history of the Sulu archipelago.

Best Things to Do in Sulu Islands: Activities and Tours

  1. Island Hopping - Set sail and visit different islands of the Sulu Archipelago and discover their cultural and natural beauty.
  2. Diving and Snorkeling - Discover the rich marine ecosystem of Sulu Islands and its pristine coral reefs by diving or snorkeling.
  3. Culinary Tour - Explore and taste the unique cuisine of Sulu Islands and learn more about local culinary traditions.
  4. Cultural and Heritage Tour - Visit the different historical and cultural attractions of Sulu Islands and immerse yourself in its rich heritage.

Food and Drink Scene of Sulu Islands

Must-Try Dishes

  1. Satti - a traditional Tausug dish made of barbecued meat and skewered on a stick, served with a spicy dipping sauce and a side of rice.
  2. Tiula itum - a hearty beef soup dish made of burnt coconut and various local spices.
  3. Palapa - a versatile spicy condiment made of finely chopped scallions, chili peppers, and ginger, commonly served with grilled or roasted meats.
  4. Latti - a savory rice cake made with coconut milk, sugar, and pandan leaves.

Local Beverages

  1. Tuba - a sweet, mildly alcoholic drink made from the sap of coconut palms.
  2. Kahawa Sug - a traditional coffee drink featuring roasted coffee beans, ginger, cinnamon, and muscovado sugar.
  3. Kalinga - a homemade refreshing drink made from calamansi juice, honey, and water.

What to Buy in Sulu Islands: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  1. Pis Syabit - a colorful woven fabric made by Tausug women, which can be used as clothing, tablecloths, and other decorative items.
  2. Yakan Weaving - traditional woven fabrics crafted by the Yakan tribe using a backstrap loom, known for its vibrant geometric patterns and colors.
  3. Sulu Kris - a unique Moro bladed weapon, intricately designed and crafted from high-quality metal by skilled artisans.

Local Culture and Customs of Sulu Islands

The Sulu Islands is rich in cultural heritage and influence, shaped by a fusion of various ethnic groups such as the Tausug, Yakan, and Samal. The Sulu culture is predominantly Muslim, with distinct Islamic practices and traditions, such as observance of Ramadan, mosque visits, and gender-specific social norms.

Visitors are advised to dress appropriately and respectfully while visiting cultural and religious sites. It is customary to remove shoes and cover the head when entering mosques.

Best Time to Visit Sulu Islands

The best time to visit Sulu Islands is from December to March when the weather is pleasant and ideal for outdoor activities such as island hopping, beach lounging, and hiking. However, the islands can be visited year-round, but travelers should take note of the rainy season, which falls between June and October.

Do’s and Don’ts of Sulu Islands

Do:

  • Dress appropriately and respectfully when visiting cultural and religious sites.
  • Engage with the locals and learn more about their culture and daily life.
  • Try local cuisine and delicacies.

Don’t:

  • Bring or display any illegal drugs or substances.
  • Wander around unfamiliar areas alone at night.
  • Disrespect local customs and traditions.

Safety and Health

Safety in Sulu Islands

Visitors are advised to exercise caution and vigilance when traveling to Sulu Islands, as there have been reports of kidnapping and terrorist activities in the past. It is advisable to coordinate with local authorities or travel with a reputable tour company when visiting the islands.

Health in Sulu Islands

Visitors must take precautionary measures and vaccination before traveling to Sulu Islands, as the region can be a high-risk area for mosquito-borne illnesses such as dengue fever and malaria.

Getting to Sulu Islands: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Sulu Islands

The closest airport to Sulu Islands is the Jolo Airport, which operates daily flights from Zamboanga City. It is advisable to book flights in advance as schedules can be limited.

Bus Stations Near Sulu Islands

Sulu Islands can be accessed by bus from nearby cities such as Zamboanga and Cotabato. Buses are available daily, but travelers are advised to check the schedules in advance.

Other Options

Travelers can reach Sulu Islands by sea through ferry services such as Weesam Express, Zamboanga Ferry, and Super Shuttle Ferry, which operate daily from Zamboanga City.

How to Get Around in Sulu Islands

Travelers can get around Sulu Islands through various modes of transportation such as tricycles, jeepneys, and vans. It is advisable to coordinate with local travel agencies or tour operators when planning island hopping activities.

Where to Go Next from Sulu Islands: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  1. Zamboanga City - a bustling city in the Zamboanga Peninsula, known for its Spanish colonial architecture, scenic islands, and rich culinary heritage.
  2. Tawi-Tawi Island - an island located at the southernmost part of the Philippines, known for its pristine beaches, virgin jungles, and historical landmarks.
  3. Cotabato City - a city located in the Mindanao region, known for its rich cultural heritage, unique cuisine, and natural attractions.

Conclusion

The Sulu Islands is a perfect destination for travelers who are looking for a unique and attractive place to visit in the Philippines. The islands offer a perfect blend of natural beauty, cultural influence, and historical significance, making each experience unforgettable. With a plethora of outdoor adventures, cultural attractions, and culinary delights, Sulu Islands should be on your bucket list when traveling to the Philippines.
