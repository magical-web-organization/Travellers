Introduction to Basilan

Located in the Bangsamoro region of Mindanao, Philippines, Basilan is a scenic and culturally-rich destination waiting to be explored. Often referred to as the “Island of Risks,” Basilan was once known for its dynamic history of violence and strife, but today it is a peaceful and welcoming land that has so much to offer for travelers. Drawing its beauty from the untainted landscapes and the hospitality of its people, Basilan is a hidden gem worth visiting.

List of Places to Visit in Basilan

Basilan is endowed with both man-made and natural attractions that are worth exploring. From ancient landmarks, pristine beaches, and undisturbed mountain ranges, here are some of the best places to visit in Basilan.

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

If you’re looking to discover the essence of Basilan’s history, here are some must-visit landmark sites:

  • Basilan State College Museum - a museum where you can see significant collections of historical, cultural, and archeological artifacts from the early settlers of the island.
  • Lamitan City Hall - a building that serves as the reminder of the city’s origin and history, it features an impressive Spanish-style architecture built in 1972.
  • Maluso Old Mosque - an antique wooden structure that dates back to the 19th century and one of the oldest mosques in the Philippines. It showcases the traditional architecture of the pre-colonial mosque and a great example of the inter-religious cultural relationship of Basilan’s people.

Natural Attractions of Basilan: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Basilan is home to numerous natural havens that will leave outdoor enthusiasts and nature lovers amazed. The islands have:

  • White Beach - a perfect white sand beach that provides a picturesque view of the sea and a perfect spot for swimming and sunbathing.
  • Bud Tillah Waterfalls - a hidden waterfalls in the heart of the jungle, where visitors can take a refreshing dip in the cool waters of the falls while enjoying the breath-taking scenery.
  • Rubber Plantation - a developing eco-tourism site that features an impressive range of nature-activities where visitors can experience moments of enlightenment with its diverse flora and fauna.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Basilan

Basilan’s mountainous topography is an excellent option for those who love hiking and biking. Here are some famous trekking and biking routes you can enjoy:

  • Mount Tumatangas Hike - known for its high peak lodge and fantastic sunrise views, the hike is one of the best excursions in Basilan. A guided tour is recommended as it can be quite challenging.
  • Lantawan Trail Hike - one of the most popular hikes in Basilan. The trail is well-developed and maintained, providing a picturesque view of the surrounding landscapes.
  • Tairan Mangrove Trail Bike Route - situated on Lantawan Island, where you can bike through the scenic mangrove forest and enjoy the gorgeous view of the sunset on the horizon.

Best Things to Do in Basilan: Activities and Tours

Basilan has numerous ways to help visitors make the most of their stay. Here are some of the top activities and tours you can enjoy:

  • Island Hopping - a chance to explore other islands that are nearby, take a dip in crystal clear waters and walk on virgin beaches.
  • Jungle Camping - an excursion that allows visitors to camp in the heart of the rainforest. Provide an opportunity to see Basilan’s flora and fauna and experience a peaceful time with nature.
  • Beach-Hop and Pass Island Tour - a must-activity when in Basilan. It is a tour that takes visitors to pass-by islands, make your way to the beach, and enjoy the fantastic white sand shorelines.

Week-Long Itinerary for Basilan

Here’s a 7-day itinerary for those who want to explore the beautiful Basilan Islands:

Day 1 - Arrival and check-in at the hotel. Day 2 - Visit Lamitan City Hall, Maluso Mosque, and Basilan State College Museum. Day 3 - Island-hop and Pass-Island tour. Day 4 - Hike at Mount Tumatangas. Day 5 - Relax at White Sand Beach and enjoy the sun and sea. Day 6 - Take a refreshing dip in Bud Tillah Waterfalls. Day 7 - Take a day off and Shop for souvenirs.

Food and Drink Scene of Basilan

One of the unique experiences when traveling to Basilan is exploring its fantastic food and drink scene. Below are the must-try dishes and beverages.

Must-Try Dishes

  • Kambing Kuracha - roasted goat cooked to perfection and a locally famous dish for its unique flavor.
  • Curacha Alavar - a mouth-watering dish made from a special type of crab and a luscious blend of coconut milk and chilies.
  • Piyanggang Manok - a grilled chicken dish, using coconut milk, turmeric and burnt coconut meat for flavor.

Local Beverages

  • Sakurab - a potent coconut wine that is made from the fermented sap of a coconut tree.
  • Lambanog - distilled coconut wine that is either unflavored or infused with different flavors like lime, ginger, and mango.

Where to Stay in Basilan: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

When it comes to accommodation, Basilan offers a wide selection of hotels, hostels, and guesthouses that cater to every traveler’s needs and budget. Below are some recommended options:

  • Lamitan City Lodge - offers clean and budget-friendly rooms that provide a cozy and comfortable stay.
  • Kublai’s Place - a peaceful paradise, located near the beach, offering modern and spacious accommodations with an outdoor pool.
  • Garden Orchid Hotel - a luxury hotel, famous for its well-landscaped gardens and terraces with tranquil sea views.

What to Buy in Basilan: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Basilan has numerous cultural influences, resulting in various locally made handicrafts and products that make impeccable souvenirs to bring back home. Here are some excellent ideas:

  • Yakan Weaving Products - made from handwoven textiles that reflect the rich textile heritage of the Yakan ethnic group in Basilan.
  • Basilan Knives - Basilenos bladesmiths make some of the finest knives in the Philippines.
  • Bukayo - a sweet delicacy made from burnt coconut meat and sugar, which tourists love to bring home as a souvenir.

Local Culture and Customs of Basilan

Basilan is a culturally-rich destination, with various ethnic tribes, religious groups and a unique blend of traditions. Here are some customs and traditions visitors should keep in mind:

  • Respect - during your visit, please respect the customs and cultures of the local people.
  • Attire - dress modestly and appropriately.
  • Greetings - learn basic greetings like “Salam Alaikom” - which means “peace be upon you” in Arabic.

Best Time to Visit Basilan

The optimal period to visit Basilan is from January to April when the weather is dry and breezy, making it ideal for outdoor activities.

Do’s and Don’ts of Basilan

To ensure a memorable and enjoyable trip, here are some do’s and don’ts:

Do:

  • Try Local Delicacies - sample the local cuisine and drinks.
  • Ask Before Photographing People - remain courteous and obtain the locals’ permission for any photographs.
  • Carry Cash - ATMs are not readily available, so carry enough cash to enjoy your trip completely.

Don’t:

  • Disregard the Local Culture - respect local tradition and culture.
  • Wear Revealing Clothing - dress modestly and appropriately.
  • Travel Alone Off the Beaten Path - Always travel with a guide and avoid isolated areas or places that are known to be risky.

Safety and Health

Here are some tips for ensuring your safety during your trip to Basilan.

Safety in Basilan

  • Travel with a Guide - avoid traveling alone and always travel with a knowledgeable guide.
  • Avoid Certain Areas - Certain areas may have uncertain political or civil situations. Try to avoid these areas and check the latest security reports.
  • Stay Alert - Stay aware of your surroundings and trust your instincts.

Health in Basilan

  • Stay Hydrated - Drink plenty of bottled water to avoid dehydration, especially during outdoor activities.
  • Take Precautions Against Mosquito Bites - mosquitos spread diseases like Malaria and Dengue. Wear long-sleeved shirts and pants and use insect repellent.

Getting to Basilan: Transportation Options and Logistics

Here are some ways to get to and from Basilan.

  • Airports Near Basilan - The nearest international airport to Basilan is Zamboanga International Airport, which is a 30-minute flight from Manila.
  • Bus Stations Near Basilan - Buses can take passengers in the Zamboanga peninsula, and from Zamboanga City, passengers can take a boat or ferry ride to Isabela City, Basilan.
  • Other Options - From Jolo or Zamboanga City, you can take a fast ferry to Basilan.

How to Get Around in Basilan

  • Tricycle - Trikes are the most common mode of transportation around Basilan.
  • Habal Habal - A motorbike commonly used to traverse rural areas and provides alternative transportion around Basilan.
  • Jeepney - shared minibusses that ply major roads in Basilan.

Where to Go Next from Basilan: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

After exploring Basilan, here are some nearby destinations you can consider visiting:

  • Zamboanga City - a vibrant city with historical attractions and stunning beaches.
  • Sulu - a group of islands that boast of beaches and culture.
  • Zambonga del Sur - known for the giant Moriones masks, rice terraces and the splendid lakes of the area.

Conclusion

With its history, beauty, and excellent cuisine, Basilan deserves a spot on your travel bucket list. With the help of this guide, you can explore its stunning natural resources, immerse in its rich culture and traditions and create unforgettable memories. Plan your trip wisely, and you’ll discover the wonders that Basilan has in store for you.
