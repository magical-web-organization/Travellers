A Comprehensive Travel Guide to Lake Sebu, Philippines

Lake Sebu is a tranquil and charming town located in the southern part of the Philippines, specifically in the province of South Cotabato, within the region of Soccsksargen. It is known for its breathtaking natural scenery, rich cultural heritage, and indigenous communities. Whether you’re a nature lover, a culture enthusiast, or an adventure seeker, Lake Sebu has something to offer. In this comprehensive travel guide, we will explore the best places to visit, things to do, where to stay, what to eat, and more.

Introduction to Lake Sebu

Lake Sebu is a town that is home to three lakes - Lake Sebu, Lake Seloton, and Lake Lahit. The town itself is surrounded by misty mountains and lush forests, making it a perfect destination for nature lovers. The T’boli people, one of the indigenous groups in the Philippines, inhabit the area and play a significant role in shaping the town’s culture and traditions. The town’s booming tourism industry has helped preserve the T’boli people’s traditions, customs, and folklore.

List of Places to Visit in Lake Sebu

There are many exciting places to visit in Lake Sebu. Here are some of the top attractions:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • T’boli Museum - This museum is dedicated to the T’boli people and their rich cultural heritage. It showcases various T’boli artifacts, clothing, and musical instruments. Visitors can also observe a traditional T’boli dance performance.
  • Seven Falls Zipline - This zipline is a must-try adventure activity in Lake Sebu. It provides a scenic view of the seven waterfalls in the area, including the majestic Hikong Bente.
  • School of Living Traditions - The school’s primary aim is to preserve cultural heritage, especially the T’boli language, dance, and music. Visitors can participate in workshops on traditional weaving and jewelry-making.

Natural Attractions of Lake Sebu: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Lake Sebu - The town’s namesake lake is the largest of the three and one of the country’s most pristine natural lakes. It offers breathtaking views of the surrounding mountains and is perfect for kayaking and fishing.
  • Hikong Bente Falls - This waterfall is the most well-known and largest in the area. Visitors can hike up to the falls and take a dip in the natural pool below.
  • Mountain Bike Trails - Lake Sebu’s rugged terrain makes it a great destination for mountain biking enthusiasts. The local tour operators offer tours and rental bikes.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Lake Sebu

Lake Sebu offers several hiking and biking trails that cater to various skill levels. Here are some of the most popular:

  • Hikong Bente Falls Trail - This trail leads to the Hikong Bente Falls and takes about 45 minutes to an hour to complete. The trail is relatively easy and offers stunning views of the surrounding mountains.
  • Dolores Lake Trail - This trail is perfect for those who want a more challenging hike as it takes roughly three hours to complete. The trail leads to Dolores Lake, and the reward for hikers is panoramic views of the mountain ranges and Lake Sebu.

Best Things to Do in Lake Sebu: Activities and Tours

Lake Sebu offers a range of activities and tours that cater to various interests. Here are some of the best things to do:

  • Try T’boli Cuisine - Lake Sebu is famous for its unique and delicious T’boli cuisine. Visitors can try dishes such as tilapia cooked in bamboo tubes, grilled chicken with turmeric, and pinanggau, a snack made of sweet rice and grated coconut.
  • Go on a T’boli village tour - Visitors can explore the T’boli villages and learn about their culture and traditions. The tour includes a visit to traditional houses, weaving centers, and jewelry-making workshops.
  • Join the T’boli tribe dance festival - This annual event celebrates the T’boli culture and traditions. It features colorful and lively performances, traditional games, and a food fair.

Here’s a week-long itinerary to make the most out of your trip:

Food and Drink Scene of Lake Sebu

Lake Sebu offers a unique and delicious cuisine that reflects the T’boli culture. Here are some must-try dishes and beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Tilapia cooked in bamboo tubes - Tilapia is marinated in spices, wrapped in banana leaves and cooked inside bamboo tubes, resulting in a flavorful and tender dish.
  • Grilled chicken with turmeric - Chicken is marinated in turmeric and other spices before being grilled to perfection.
  • Pinanggau - A snack made of sweet rice and grated coconut that is wrapped in banana leaves and grilled.

Local Beverages

  • T’boli coffee - Locally grown coffee that is brewed using a traditional method and served with coconut milk.
  • Lambanog - A traditional Filipino drink made from coconut tree sap.

Where to Stay in Lake Sebu: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Lake Sebu offers various accommodation options that cater to different budgets and preferences. Here are some recommended places to stay:

  • Mountain Log Resort - A rustic yet cozy resort situated on a mountain overlooking Lake Sebu.
  • Sone’s Place - A hostel located in the town center that offers affordable and comfortable rooms.
  • Punta Isla Lake Resort - A luxurious resort located on the banks of Lake Seloton that offers stunning views.

What to Buy in Lake Sebu: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Lake Sebu is known for its vibrant and unique handicrafts and textiles. Here are some souvenirs and local products to check out:

  • T’boli woven products - The T’boli are known for their intricate and colorful weaving patterns. Visitors can buy bags, blankets, and clothing made from abaca, a type of banana fiber.
  • T’boli brasswork - The T’boli are also skilled in metalworking and create intricate brass bowls, jewelry, and accessories.

Local Culture and Customs of Lake Sebu

The T’boli people have a rich and vibrant culture that has been passed down from generation to generation. Visitors should be respectful of their culture and customs. Here are some things to keep in mind:

  • Ask for permission before taking photos - It is customary to ask for permission before taking photos of the T’boli people or their property.
  • Avoid wearing revealing clothing - The T’boli people are conservative and traditional, so it is important to dress modestly out of respect.
  • Don’t touch or buy ritual objects - The T’boli consider certain objects and artifacts as sacred, so it is polite not to touch or buy them.

Best Time to Visit Lake Sebu

The best time to visit Lake Sebu is from November to April when the weather is cool and dry. It is important to note that Lake Sebu can get chilly, so make sure to bring warm clothing.

Do’s and Don’ts of Lake Sebu

Do:

  • Respect the local culture and customs - The T’boli people have their unique culture and traditions, so it is essential to be respectful of them.
  • Engage with the locals - Lake Sebu’s charming locals are friendly and engaging and are eager to share their knowledge and experiences.
  • Try the local cuisine - Lake Sebu’s T’boli cuisine is unique and delicious and should not be missed.

Don’t:

  • Litter the environment - Lake Sebu’s natural environment is pristine and should be preserved. Visitors should dispose of their trash properly.
  • Touch or disturb the wildlife - Lake Sebu’s natural surroundings are home to various wildlife species. Visitors should refrain from touching or disturbing them.
  • Smoke and drink in public - Smoking and drinking in public areas are prohibited in Lake Sebu.

Safety and Health

Safety in Lake Sebu

Lake Sebu is a safe and welcoming town, and locals are hospitable and friendly. However, visitors should exercise caution and follow standard safety tips like keeping their valuables safe and staying aware of their surroundings.

Health in Lake Sebu

Visitors should have updated vaccinations and carry mosquito repellents as the area is prone to mosquitos. Visitors should also avoid drinking tap water and buy bottled water instead.

Getting to Lake Sebu: Transportation Options and Logistics

Lake Sebu is located in South Cotabato, which is around 54 kilometers from General Santos City, the nearest airport.

Airports Near Lake Sebu

The nearest airport to Lake Sebu is General Santos City International Airport. From there, visitors can take a van or a bus to Lake Sebu.

Bus Stations Near Lake Sebu

The General Santos City Terminal offers bus services to Lake Sebu regularly. Visitors can travel to General Santos City from any major city in the Philippines.

Other Options

Visitors can also opt for a private car rental or hire a van service from General Santos City to Lake Sebu.

How to Get Around in Lake Sebu

Lake Sebu is a small town that can be explored on foot. Visitors can also hire a motorbike to explore the surrounding areas. Local tour operators also offer bike rentals and guided bike tours.

Where to Go Next from Lake Sebu: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Lake Sebu is an excellent base for exploring nearby destinations and exciting excursions.

  • Koronadal City - A bustling city and the capital of South Cotabato province. It is known for its delicious food, local delicacies, and night markets.
  • Tboli town - A small town located near Lake Sebu, known for its lovely waterfalls, rice fields, and swimmable rivers.
  • Surallah town - A small town near Lake Sebu known for its T’nalak, a traditional cloth made by the T’boli people.

Conclusion

Lake Sebu is a charming and culturally rich town that offers visitors an opportunity to immerse themselves in nature and tradition. From natural attractions to cultural experiences, Lake Sebu has something to offer everyone. The above comprehensive travel guide offers a detailed itinerary to help visitors make the most out of their trip. Follow safety guidelines, respect local culture and customs, and enjoy the magical town of Lake Sebu in the Philippines.
