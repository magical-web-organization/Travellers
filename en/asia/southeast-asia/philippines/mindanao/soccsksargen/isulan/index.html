Introduction to Isulan

Isulan is a small and charming municipality located in the province of Sultan Kudarat, in the Soccsksargen region of Mindanao, Philippines. The town is known for its rich culture, beautiful landscapes, and vibrant local festivities.

Whether you’re looking for adventure, cultural immersion, or relaxation, Isulan has something to offer. In this comprehensive travel guide, we’ll introduce you to the best things to do, places to visit, and local cultural insights, making sure you experience the best of what Isulan has to offer.

List of Places to Visit in Isulan

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  1. Sultan Kudarat Provincial Museum - This museum houses artifacts and exhibits related to the history and culture of the province, including ancient burial jars, traditional musical instruments, and indigenous textiles.

  2. Mausoleum of Sultan Kudarat - Located in Barangay Madanding, this mausoleum is the final resting place of the legendary Muslim leader who resisted Spanish colonization in the 17th century.

  3. Isulan Municipal Hall - The municipal hall is a century-old structure that showcases the classic architectural design of “bahay na bato” in the region. It serves as a cultural landmark and a symbol of the town’s history and development.

Natural Attractions of Isulan: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  1. Mt. Busa - This towering mountain is a popular destination for hiking enthusiasts due to its challenging trails and stunning views of Lake Buluan and the surrounding landscapes.

  2. Balut Island - This island is well-known for its white sand beaches and crystal-clear waters ideal for swimming, snorkeling, and island hopping.

  3. Lake Buluan - This is the second-largest freshwater lake in Mindanao, where visitors can enjoy fishing, boating, and bird-watching.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Isulan

  1. Mt. Busa - This mountain is a favorite destination for hikers and mountain climbers. There are several trails available, ranging from moderate to advanced levels.

  2. Isulan Eco Park - The park offers an excellent mountain biking trail that winds through the forest and scenic landscapes of Isulan.

Best Things to Do in Isulan: Activities and Tours

  1. Visit the town plaza during “Kalimudan Festival” - This is the town’s most significant event, celebrated annually in November. The festivity features traditional dances, food fairs, and street parties.

  2. Participate in Lake Buluan Festival - This festival is celebrated in March, where people gather to showcase their crafts, food, and dance performances.

Week-Long Itinerary for Isulan

Day Activities
Day 1 Explore Sultan Kudarat Provincial Museum, Mausoleum of Sultan Kudarat, and Isulan Municipal Hall
Day 2 Trek Mt. Busa or enjoy the mountain biking trail at Isulan Eco Park

Food and Drink Scene of Isulan

Must-Try Dishes

  1. Sinina- A traditional dish made from a mixture of seafood and local spices cooked in a bamboo tube.

  2. Lechon Kawali - A crispy deep-fried pork belly seasoned with garlic, soy sauce, and vinegar.

  3. Beef Kulma - A spiced beef curry dish with creamy coconut milk

Local Beverages

  1. Tubig Kanin - A rice-based drink mixed with cinnamon and sugar.

  2. Halo-halo - A sweet dessert made with shaved ice, milk, and an assortment of fruits, beans, and sweet preserves.

Where to Stay in Isulan: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  1. Hotel Kelex - A mid-range hotel located in the town center, offering comfortable rooms and modern amenities.

  2. Antonio’s Inn - A budget-friendly option with clean and well-maintained rooms, located near the bus terminal.

  3. Mountain Lodge - A charming guesthouse located in the mountains, offering a tranquil and scenic environment.

What to Buy in Isulan: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  1. Woven Fabrics - The T’boli tribes in Isulan are known for their intricate and colorful textiles woven from abaca and pineapple fibers.

  2. Handmade Crafts - Handicrafts made from local materials such as bamboo, coconut husks, and shells make for unique souvenirs.

Local Culture and Customs of Isulan

  1. Respect Local Customs - Isulan is a predominantly Muslim town, so visitors are expected to dress appropriately and respect the local customs and traditions.

  2. Greetings - The traditional Muslim greeting is “Assalamu alaikum,” which means “Peace be upon you.”

Best Time to Visit Isulan

The best time to visit Isulan is between December and May, as the weather is warm, dry, and pleasant during these months. The Kalimudan Festival, celebrated in November, is also an excellent time to visit.

Do’s and Don’ts of Isulan

Do:

  1. Do dress conservatively and appropriately.

  2. Do experience the local culture and traditions.

Don’t:

  1. Don’t disrespect local customs and traditions.

  2. Don’t engage in disrespectful behavior or activities.

Safety and Health

Safety in Isulan

Isulan is a generally safe town, but visitors are advised to avoid areas outside the town center at night, as well as being cautious when traveling alone.

Health in Isulan

Visitors are advised to drink bottled water and take precautions against mosquito-borne illnesses such as dengue fever.

Getting to Isulan: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Isulan

  1. General Santos International Airport is the nearest airport to Sultan Kudarat, located approximately 144 kilometers from Isulan.

  2. Awang Airport is located in Cotabato City, around 190 kilometers from Isulan.

Bus Stations Near Isulan

  1. Yellow Bus Line - Operating daily buses from General Santos and Cotabato City to Isulan.

  2. Husky Bus Company - Operating daily buses from Davao City to Isulan.

Other Options

Private car and van rentals are also available, but visitors should be cautious of driving on Mindanao’s roads and should hire a local driver if possible.

How to Get Around in Isulan

Tricycles are the most common mode of transport in Isulan and can be easily hired around the town center. Jeepneys and buses also operate along the main roads.

Where to Go Next from Isulan: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  1. Cotabato City - A bustling city with historical sites, markets, and cultural attractions.

  2. Gen San - A coastal city famous for its seafood and tuna industry, offering water sports and island hopping activities.

Conclusion

Isulan is a hidden gem in Mindanao, offering visitors a unique blend of culture, adventure, and relaxation. This travel guide has outlined the best things to do and see in Isulan, making it easier for travelers to plan and make the most of their visit. So pack your bags and get ready to explore the beauty of Isulan!
