Introduction to Eastern Visayas

Located in the central part of the Philippines, Eastern Visayas is a region composed of three major islands - Samar, Leyte, and Biliran. It is known for its stunning natural beauty, rich cultural heritage, and warm and friendly locals. Whether you are an adventure seeker or a history buff, Eastern Visayas has something to offer to everyone.

List of Places to Visit in Eastern Visayas

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • San Juanico Bridge - also known as the “Bridge of Love,” this 2.16 km long bridge connects the islands of Samar and Leyte and offers stunning views of the San Juanico Strait.
  • Santo Niño Shrine and Heritage Museum - located in Tacloban City, this museum houses a collection of antique items, religious artifacts, and artworks.
  • MacArthur Landing Memorial National Park - a historical park in Palo that commemorates the landing of General Douglas MacArthur in Leyte during World War II.
  • Calbiga Caves - the largest cave system in the Philippines, located in the town of Calbiga in Samar.
  • Sohoton Natural Bridge National Park - a protected area in Basey, Samar known for its natural bridge formation and limestone cliffs.
  • Cuatro Islas - a group of four islands located off the coast of Inopacan, Leyte. It offers white sand beaches, crystal clear waters, and rich marine life.
  • Biliran Island - a beautiful island province in Eastern Visayas that boasts of waterfalls, hot springs, and stunning views.

Natural Attractions of Eastern Visayas: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Kalanggaman Island - a picturesque island in Palompon, Leyte, known for its long sandbar and turquoise waters.
  • Leyte Landing Memorial Park - located in Dulag, this park is a tribute to the American liberation forces that landed in Leyte during World War II.
  • Lake Danao National Park - a natural park in Ormoc, Leyte that offers various activities such as boating, kayaking, and camping.
  • Samar Island Natural Park - a protected area in Samar that boasts of extensive forests, diverse wildlife, and majestic waterfalls, such as the Tarangban Falls and the Lulugayan Falls.
  • Canigao Island - a beautiful island off the coast of Matalom, Leyte that offers white sand beaches, clear waters, and snorkeling and diving opportunities.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Eastern Visayas

  • Mt. Tres Marias - a challenging hike located in Samar that offers scenic views of the province.
  • Alto Peak - the highest peak in Eastern Visayas, located in Ormoc, Leyte. It offers breathtaking views of the surrounding areas.
  • Karap-agan Falls - a beautiful waterfall in Hinabangan, Samar that requires a short hike to get to.

Best Things to Do in Eastern Visayas: Activities and Tours

  • Island-hopping and beach bumming in Kalanggaman Island and Cuatro Islas.
  • Snorkeling and diving in the crystal-clear waters of Canigao Island and other surrounding islands.
  • Whitewater rafting and kayaking in the Ulot River in Samar.
  • Visiting the historical sites and museums in Tacloban City and Palo.
  • Trekking and camping in the natural parks and peaks in the region.

Week-Long Itinerary for Eastern Visayas

Tres Marias or Alto Peak Day 5: Visit San Juanico Bridge and Sohoton Natural Bridge National Park Day 6: Visit Biliran Island and its stunning waterfalls and hot springs Day 7: Relax in Canigao Island and enjoy its pristine beaches

Food and Drink Scene of Eastern Visayas

Must-Try Dishes

  • Binagol - a dessert made from taro, coconut, and caramel, wrapped in a banana leaf.
  • Suman - a rice cake made from glutinous rice and coconut milk, wrapped in banana leaves.
  • Leyte Dried Fish - a local specialty made from various types of fish, dried under the sun and served as a side dish or main course.
  • Oysters - fresh and delicious oysters from the waters of Eastern Visayas.
  • Bocarillo - a sweet delicacy made from grated coconut and caramelized brown sugar.

Local Beverages

  • Tubâ - a sweet and refreshing alcoholic drink made from coconut sap.
  • Bahalina - a traditional wine made from rice and local herbs.

Where to Stay in Eastern Visayas: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • The Oriental Leyte - a luxurious hotel in Palo, Leyte that boasts of a beachfront location and stunning views of the Leyte Gulf.
  • GV Hotel - budget-friendly hotels located in different parts of Eastern Visayas.
  • GV Pension House - a budget-friendly accommodation option in Tacloban City.

What to Buy in Eastern Visayas: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Banig Products - woven mats and bags made from natural fibers.
  • Abaca Products - bags, wallets, and home decor made from abaca fiber, a local plant fiber.
  • Binagol - a delicious dessert in a jar that can be brought home as a souvenir.
  • Leyte Coffee - a locally-produced coffee variety that is gaining popularity in the international market.

Local Culture and Customs of Eastern Visayas

Eastern Visayas has a strong sense of community and family ties. Respect for elders and hospitality towards visitors are highly valued.

Best Time to Visit Eastern Visayas

The best time to visit Eastern Visayas is from November to May, as these months have the least amount of rainfall and offer the best weather for outdoor activities.

Do’s and Don’ts of Eastern Visayas

Do:

  • Respect the culture and beliefs of the locals.
  • Try the local food and drinks.
  • Bring your own reusable water bottle and eco-bag to help reduce waste.

Don’t:

  • Litter or harm the environment.
  • Disrespect historical sites and museums.
  • Wear revealing clothing in public places.

Safety and Health

Safety in Eastern Visayas

Eastern Visayas is generally safe for tourists. However, it is advised to practice standard safety precautions, such as avoiding dark and isolated areas at night and keeping your valuables safe. It is also recommended to check with local authorities before going on hiking and trekking adventures, as some areas may be unsafe or require a guide.

Health in Eastern Visayas

It is recommended to always have a travel insurance, and to be up-to-date on routine vaccinations such as measles, mumps, rubella, and typhoid. It is also advisable to drink only bottled or boiled water, and to avoid eating raw or undercooked food.

Getting to Eastern Visayas: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Eastern Visayas

There are two major airports that serve Eastern Visayas - Daniel Z. Romualdez Airport in Tacloban City, and Biliran Airport in the province of Biliran. Both airports offer daily flights to and from major cities in the Philippines.

Bus Stations Near Eastern Visayas

Buses from Manila and nearby cities travel to Tacloban City and other major towns in Eastern Visayas regularly.

Other Options

Ferries and boats also operate routes to Eastern Visayas from nearby islands and provinces in the Philippines.

How to Get Around in Eastern Visayas

Jeepneys, tricycles, and taxis are the most common modes of transportation in Eastern Visayas. However, for long-distance trips, it is recommended to rent a car or hire a private van or vehicle.

Where to Go Next from Eastern Visayas: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Cebu - known for its pristine beaches, rich history, and famous lechon.
  • Bohol - home of the Chocolate Hills, tarsiers, and beautiful beaches.
  • Siargao - a popular surfing destination in the Philippines.
  • Camiguin - known as the “Island Born of Fire,” with its stunning waterfalls, hot springs, and volcanic landscapes.

Conclusion

Eastern Visayas is a hidden gem in the Philippines, offering a diverse range of activities, attractions, and experiences for every type of traveler. From its pristine beaches and natural wonders to its rich culture and history, Eastern Visayas is a destination that should not be missed. Plan your trip today and discover the beauty of this stunning region.
