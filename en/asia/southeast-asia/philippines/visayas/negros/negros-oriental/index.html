Introduction to Negros Oriental

Negros Oriental is a province located in Visayas, Philippines. It is known for its rich culture, stunning natural attractions, and friendly locals. The province is surrounded by magnificent beaches, lush forests, and picturesque mountains.

Negros Oriental is a paradise for adventure enthusiasts and nature lovers. Its natural wonders, such as waterfalls, hot springs, and dive sites, are a must-visit for anyone seeking outdoor adventures. The province is also home to historical landmarks, museums, and festivals that offer visitors a glimpse into the local culture and way of life.

If you are planning to travel to Negros Oriental, this comprehensive tour guide will help you navigate your way through the province. From places to visit to where to stay, this guide covers everything you need to know for an unforgettable trip to Negros Oriental.

List of Places to Visit in Negros Oriental

Negros Oriental offers a variety of attractions for visitors to explore. From cultural landmarks to natural wonders, here are some of the must-visit places in the province:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Silliman University – One of the oldest universities in the Philippines, offering a glimpse into the country’s colonial past and modern history.
  • Dumaguete Cathedral – A Roman Catholic Church that serves as one of the landmarks of Dumaguete City.
  • Bais City Museum – A museum dedicated to showcasing the rich culture and heritage of Bais City.
  • Tanjay City Heritage Park and Museum – A park and museum that showcases Tanjay City’s history and culture.
  • Negros Oriental Provincial Capitol – The seat of government of Negros Oriental that features Spanish colonial architecture.
  • Rizal Boulevard – A popular spot in Dumaguete City for strolling, dining, and enjoying the sunset.

Natural Attractions of Negros Oriental: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Apo Island – A world-famous dive site that offers stunning coral reefs and marine life.
  • Twin Lakes – Two crater lakes located in Balinsasayao, surrounded by thick forests, and perfect for trekking and nature walks.
  • Casaroro Falls – A majestic 100-meters waterfall located in Valencia offering stunning views and a refreshing dip in its cool waters.
  • Mabinay Caves – A group of limestone caves located in the town of Mabinay, perfect for spelunking and cave exploration.
  • Mount Talinis – A dormant volcano that offers hiking trails and a stunning view of Negros Oriental.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Negros Oriental

Negros Oriental offers plenty of opportunities for hiking and biking. Here are some of the popular routes for adventure seekers:

  • Dumaguete to Valencia Highway – A scenic route that offers both challenging climbs and easy downhill rides.
  • Mount Talinis Trail – A challenging trail that takes hikers through lush forests and to the summit of the dormant volcano.
  • Dumaguete City to Cuernos de Negros – A route that takes bikers through the mountains, providing stunning views of the surrounding countryside.

Best Things to Do in Negros Oriental: Activities and Tours

Besides hiking and biking, Negros Oriental offers many activities and tours for visitors to enjoy. Here are some of the best things to do in the province:

  • Dolphin Watching – Visitors can take a boat to Tañon Strait and experience the thrill of watching dolphins leap and swim in the wild.
  • Whale Shark Swimming – Visitors can swim with the gentle giants of the sea in Oslob, Cebu, which is just a short ferry ride from Negros Oriental.
  • Waterfall Hopping – Visitors can explore the many waterfalls in the province, including Casaroro Falls in Valencia and Twin Falls in Dumaguete City.
  • Island Hopping – Visitors can explore the nearby islands of Apo Island and Siquijor, known for their stunning beaches and marine life.

Week-Long Itinerary for Negros Oriental

For visitors who want to experience the best of Negros Oriental, we recommend the following week-long itinerary:

Day 1: Arrive in Dumaguete City and explore the city’s bustling markets and historic landmarks.

Day 2: Visit the nearby Apo Island for a day of snorkeling and swimming with sea turtles.

Day 3: Trek to the Casaroro Falls in Valencia and enjoy a refreshing dip in its cool waters.

Day 4: Take a tour of the historic landmarks in Tanjay City and Bais City.

Day 5: Explore the lush forests of Balinsasayao and visit the Twin Lakes.

Day 6: Take a trip to Mabinay Caves and explore the many limestone caves in the area.

Day 7: Relax on the Rizal Boulevard in Dumaguete City and enjoy a sunset dinner.

Food and Drink Scene of Negros Oriental

Negros Oriental offers a variety of delicious local cuisine and refreshing beverages. Here are some of the must-try dishes and drinks:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Sisig Baboy – A dish made from pork parts chopped into small pieces and served on a sizzling plate with onions and chili peppers.
  • Tinolang Manok – A dish made from chicken and a broth flavored with ginger, onions, and other spices.
  • Batchoy – A noodle soup dish made from pork meat, liver, and innards, garnished with chicharon and vegetable toppings.
  • Inasal – A grilled chicken dish marinated in a blend of vinegar, soy sauce, ginger, and other spices.

Local Beverages

  • Tuba – A refreshing drink made from coconut sap, popular at fiestas and gatherings.
  • Barako Coffee – A type of coffee grown in the Philippines and known for its strong taste and aroma.
  • Mango Juice – A refreshing drink made from fresh mangoes, which are abundant in Negros Oriental.

Where to Stay in Negros Oriental: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Negros Oriental offers a variety of accommodation options for visitors. Here are some of the recommended hotels, hostels, and guesthouses in the province:

  • Coco Grande Hotel – A budget-friendly hotel located in the heart of Dumaguete City.
  • The Flying Fish Hostel – A backpacker-friendly hostel located in Dumaguete City.
  • Silliman Beach Resort – A beachfront resort located in Dauin, offering breathtaking views of the sea and nearby islands.
  • Bethel Guesthouse – A family-owned guesthouse located in Dumaguete City, offering cozy rooms and home-cooked meals.

What to Buy in Negros Oriental: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Negros Oriental offers a variety of unique souvenirs and local products that visitors can bring home. Here are some of the recommended items to buy:

  • Peanut Kisses – A local confectionery made from peanuts and sugar, considered a delicacy in Negros Oriental.
  • Hand-woven Products – Visitors can purchase hand-woven bags, baskets, and mats from local vendors and artisans.
  • Tabon-Tabon Fruit – A unique fruit that is native to the Philippines and used in local cooking.
  • Souvenirs from Apo Island and Siquijor – Visitors can purchase souvenirs, such as T-shirts, keychains, and fridge magnets, from the nearby islands of Apo and Siquijor.

Local Culture and Customs of Negros Oriental

The people of Negros Oriental are known for their warm hospitality and love of music and dance. Visiting during one of the province’s festivals is a great way to experience the local culture and traditions. Some of the popular festivals in Negros Oriental include:

  • Sandurot Festival – A celebration of the city of Dumaguete’s founding, featuring street dance performances, food fairs, and other cultural events.
  • Buglasan Festival – A month-long festival that showcases the best of Negros Oriental’s culture and traditions, featuring music, dance, and pageants.

Best Time to Visit Negros Oriental

The best time to visit Negros Oriental is during the dry season, which runs from November to April. During this time, visitors can enjoy sunny skies and pleasant temperatures, making it perfect for outdoor adventures and sightseeing.

Do’s and Don’ts of Negros Oriental

Do:

  • Respect the local culture and customs.
  • Try the local cuisine and beverages.
  • Bring a reusable water bottle and eco-friendly products.
  • Follow responsible tourism practices.

Don’t:

  • Litter or harm the environment.
  • Wear revealing clothing in public places.
  • Disrespect the local community and its traditions.

Safety and Health

Safety in Negros Oriental

Negros Oriental is a safe destination for travelers, but visitors should still exercise caution and practice common sense. Here are some safety tips to keep in mind:

  • Avoid walking alone at night in deserted areas.
  • Keep your valuables and money in a secure place.
  • Follow traffic rules and be cautious when crossing the streets.

Health in Negros Oriental

Visitors to Negros Oriental should take the necessary precautions to stay healthy during their trip. Here are some tips on how to stay healthy:

  • Drink only bottled or filtered water.
  • Wear sunscreen and protect yourself from the sun.
  • Use insect repellent to prevent mosquito bites.

Getting to Negros Oriental: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Negros Oriental

The province is served by two airports:

  • Dumaguete-Sibulan Airport – Located in Dumaguete City, with daily flights to and from Manila, Cebu, and other cities in the Philippines.
  • Bacolod-Silay International Airport – Located in Bacolod City, with daily flights to and from major cities in the Philippines and select international destinations.

Bus Stations Near Negros Oriental

Visitors can also travel to Negros Oriental via land transportation. The province is accessible from nearby cities and towns via bus.

Other Options

Visitors can also travel to Negros Oriental via ferry from nearby islands, such as Cebu and Bohol.

How to Get Around in Negros Oriental

Negros Oriental offers many transportation options for visitors. From tricycles to buses, here are the recommended modes of transportation in the province:

  • Tricycles – A common mode of transportation in Negros Oriental, offering affordable and convenient rides around the city and nearby towns.
  • Jeepneys – A popular mode of transportation for locals, offering a cheap and convenient way to get around the city and province.
  • Buses – A comfortable and affordable option for traveling to nearby towns and cities.

Where to Go Next from Negros Oriental: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Visitors who want to explore more of the Philippines can check out these nearby destinations:

  • Bohol – A nearby island known for its Chocolate Hills, tarsiers, and stunning beaches.
  • Cebu – A bustling metropolis known for its rich history and culture, mouth-watering cuisine, and beautiful beaches.
  • Siquijor – A neighboring island known for its mystical and magical atmosphere, stunning waterfalls, and beautiful beaches.

Conclusion

Negros Oriental is a beautiful province that offers something for everyone. From natural wonders to cultural landmarks and delicious local cuisine, the province has everything you need for an unforgettable adventure. With this comprehensive tour guide, you can explore Negros Oriental with ease, immersing yourself in the local culture and enjoying all that the province has to offer.
