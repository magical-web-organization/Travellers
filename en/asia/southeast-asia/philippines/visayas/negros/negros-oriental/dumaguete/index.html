Introduction to Dumaguete

Dumaguete is a small city located in the Negros Oriental province, in the Visayas region of the Philippines. Also known as the “City of Gentle People”, Dumaguete boasts a laid-back charm, a vibrant cultural scene, and a bustling food and drink scene. It’s a perfect destination for travelers who crave a blend of adventure, relaxation, and cultural immersion.

Here’s your comprehensive guide to the best of Dumaguete.

List of Places to Visit in Dumaguete

Dumaguete offers a plethora of attractions ranging from historical sites, world-class museums, natural wonders, and outdoor adventures. Here are some of the must-visit sites:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Silliman University Anthropology Museum - A world-class museum featuring a vast collection of artifacts, archaeological finds, and ethnographic materials from different cultures in Asia and the Pacific.
  • St. Catherine of Alexandria Cathedral - A historic church built during the Spanish era that features a beautiful baroque facade and stunning interiors.
  • Rizal Boulevard - A scenic boardwalk that’s perfect for an early morning jog or an evening stroll. Lined with restaurants and cafes, this boulevard offers a picturesque view of the sea and the nearby islands.
  • Dumaguete Belfry - A historic structure that served as a watchtower during the Spanish era.

Natural Attractions of Dumaguete: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Apo Island - An idyllic island famous for its breathtaking underwater scenery, where you can swim with sea turtles and explore the colorful coral reefs.
  • Twin Lakes of Balinsasayao and Danao - A pair of serene crater lakes that are perfect for hiking, picnicking, and camping.
  • Casaroro Falls - A magnificent 100-foot waterfall that’s accessible through a challenging hiking trail, offering a picturesque view of the lush forests and rock formations.
  • Bais City Dolphin Watching - A boat tour that takes you to the playground of dolphins and scenic mangroves in the Tañon Strait.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Dumaguete

Dumaguete is also renowned for its hiking and biking routes that cater to outdoor enthusiasts. Here are some of the best routes to explore:

  • Mount Talinis Trail - A challenging trek that takes you to the highest peak of Negros Oriental, offering scenic views of the surrounding mountains, waterfalls, and hot springs.
  • Ocoy River Trail - A peaceful trail that takes you to the heart of the forest, where you can spot exotic wildlife and enjoy the cool river breeze.
  • Bike Route along the Boulevard - A scenic bike path that stretches along Rizal Boulevard, offering a breathtaking view of the sea and the nearby islands.

Best Things to Do in Dumaguete: Activities and Tours

Dumaguete offers a plethora of activities and tours that cater to all types of travelers. Here are some of the best things to do:

  • Scuba Diving in Apo Island - Explore the colorful underwater world of Apo Island, famous for its healthy coral reefs, sea turtles, and tropical fish.
  • City Tour - Explore the cultural landmarks of Dumaguete, including the Silliman University, the St. Catherine of Alexandria Cathedral, and the Dumaguete Belfry.
  • Dolphin Watching in Bais City - Witness the playful dolphins jumping and spinning in the crystal-clear waters of Tañon Strait.
  • Lakeside Camping - Spend a night under the stars and enjoy the tranquil ambiance of the Twin Lakes of Balinsasayao and Danao.
  • Food Tour - Sample the mouth-watering local delicacies and culinary specialties of Dumaguete, including the famous Silvanas, budbud, and balut.

Food and Drink Scene of Dumaguete

One of the best ways to explore Dumaguete is through its culinary scene. Here are some of the must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Silvanas - a buttery layered cookie topped with cream, and sold in packs at various bakeshops around the city
  • Budbud Kabog - A sweet sticky rice cake made of millet and coconut cream wrapped inside a banana leaf
  • Sizzling Tanigue - A fresh fish steak served on a sizzling plate, usually flavored with soy sauce, calamansi, and chili peppers
  • Pancit Molo - A local dumpling soup filled with minced pork, shrimps, and vegetables

Local Beverages

  • Barako Coffee - A rich and flavorful coffee brewed from Liberica beans, a rare coffee variety native to Batangas
  • Tuba - A refreshing and coconut wine that’s often served during the local festivals and fiestas.
  • Buko Juice - A refreshing drink made from young coconut water, perfect for a hot day.

Where to Stay in Dumaguete: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Dumaguete has various accommodation options that cater to all budgets. Here are some of your choices:

  • Bethel Guesthouse - A homely guesthouse located in the heart of Dumaguete, featuring clean and comfortable rooms, and a friendly staff.
  • Harold’s Mansion - A backpacker-friendly hostel that offers dormitory and private rooms, located near Rizal Boulevard and other attractions.
  • The Oriental Plaza Dumaguete - A luxurious hotel that offers modern amenities, a stunning pool, and a panoramic view of the sea.

What to Buy in Dumaguete: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Dumaguete has a vibrant local arts scene that produces beautiful handicrafts, paintings, and textiles. Make sure to check out the following souvenirs and local products:

  • Piña Cloth - A delicate and hand-woven fabric made from pineapple leaves, perfect for dresses, table runners, and napkins.
  • Sculptures and Artwork - A wide range of artworks, ranging from traditional wooden carvings to modern paintings, created by local artists.
  • Crafts and Accessories - a variety of souvenir shops selling custom-made bracelets, necklaces, bags, and ornaments made of wood and other natural materials.

Local Culture and Customs of Dumaguete

Dumaguete is a city that prides itself on its rich cultural heritage, beautiful landmarks and warm hospitality. The locals are friendly and hospitable, embodying the adage “City of Gentle People.” Their traditional festivals revolve around their patron Saint Catherine of Alexandria, where they showcase their colorful and diverse cultures through street dances and culinary indulges.

Best Time to Visit Dumaguete

The best time to visit Dumaguete is during the cooler and drier months of December to February. The city’s local festivals and fiestas are celebrated throughout the year, so it’s always a good idea to check the schedule of events before planning your trip.

Do’s and Don’ts of Dumaguete

Do:

  • Always greet the locals with a smile and a respectful nod.
  • Respect the local customs and traditions, especially when visiting religious sites.
  • Sample the local cuisine and culinary specialties.

Don’t:

  • Wear revealing clothes, especially when visiting churches and other religious places
  • Litter and damage the environment, whether in the city or on a hiking trail.
  • Disrespect the locals, especially during their religious festivals and cultural events.

Safety and Health

Safety in Dumaguete

Dumaguete is generally a safe city. However, it’s still advisable to take precautions, such as avoiding dimly-lit places at night and watching out for pickpockets in crowded areas.

Health in Dumaguete

Dumaguete has public and private hospitals that offer basic healthcare services. It’s advisable to have travel insurance for any emergencies. Make sure to drink plenty of water and use mosquito repellent to avoid mosquito-borne illnesses.

Getting to Dumaguete: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Dumaguete

The nearest airport to Dumaguete is the Dumaguete-Sibulan Airport. It has direct flights from Manila, Cebu and other destinations in the Visayas region.

Bus Stations Near Dumaguete

Dumaguete has two bus terminals that offer routes to major cities and towns around Negros Island and other nearby provinces.

Other Options

Dumaguete is well-connected via ferry and fast craft services to other nearby islands and provinces such as Siquijor, Bohol, and Cebu.

How to Get Around in Dumaguete

The best way to get around Dumaguete is through tricycles, motorcycle taxis, and jeepneys. These modes of transportation are affordable and convenient, especially when you’re exploring the city center and nearby attractions. If you’re looking for more adventure, you can rent a motorbike or a bicycle at one of the local rental shops.

Where to Go Next from Dumaguete: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

After exploring the best of Dumaguete, here are some nearby destinations and excursions to consider:

  • Siquijor Island - A mystical island famous for its natural wonders, pristine beaches, and healing practices.
  • Osmeña Peak - The highest peak in the Cebu province, offering panoramic views of the surrounding islands.
  • Moalboal - A coastal town in Cebu that’s famous for its world-class diving spots and vibrant nightlife.
  • Bohol - A beautiful island province that features the iconic Chocolate Hills, Tarsier Sanctuary, and the historic Baclayon Church.

Conclusion

Dumaguete offers a unique blend of cultural immersion, natural wonders, and outdoor adventures. Its warm and hospitable locals will always welcome you with a smile and a warm greeting. This comprehensive guide aims to help you discover the best of Dumaguete as you embark on your Philippine adventure.
