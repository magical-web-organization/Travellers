Introduction to Guihulngan

Guihulngan is a small city located in Negros Oriental, one of the provinces in the Visayas region of the Philippines. Unlike its neighboring cities, Guihulngan is a hidden gem waiting to be discovered. Despite its size, Guihulngan offers a rich culture and natural beauty that is worth exploring.

List of Places to Visit in Guihulngan

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Guihulngan Museum

The Guihulngan Museum is located in Barangay Planas, and it houses artifacts and exhibits that showcase Guihulngan’s history and culture. Visitors can see the city’s rich cultural heritage brought to life through displays of local handicrafts, traditional clothing, and photographs.

St. Francis of Assisi Church

The St. Francis of Assisi Church is a historical landmark in Guihulngan. This church, built in the 19th century, has witnessed the city’s history and stood the test of time. It is a must-visit for all those interested in religious landmarks.

Natural Attractions of Guihulngan: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Mabinay Caves

Located in the south of Guihulngan, the Mabinay Caves are a series of caves that attract tourists looking for outdoor adventures. These majestic caves are the part of the country’s longest cave system, with at least 400 entrances and over 40 main caves. Visitors can enjoy spelunking, caving, and exploring the caves.

Mt. Talinis

Mt. Talinis is the highest peak in Negros Oriental, and it is a popular destination for hikers and mountaineers. The mountain is blessed with an abundance of natural beauty, from its colourful flora and fauna to its scenic views. Visitors can experience the thrill of trekking to the summit, amid lush green forests and panoramic landscapes.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Guihulngan

Guihulngan offers some of the most beautiful and challenging trails for hiking and biking in the region. Below are some of the most notable routes:

  • Banawe Trail is a popular mountain biking trail that offers breathtaking views of Banawe Lake and its surrounding mountains.
  • Guihulngan-Nabahat Highlands is an excellent spot for hikers and trekking enthusiasts, as it offers a combination of an easy trail and a more challenging one. This route rewards its climbers with stunning views of the mountains and sea.

Best Things to Do in Guihulngan: Activities and Tours

Dolphin Watching

Guihulngan is known for its excellent dolphin watching experiences. Visitors can enjoy an early morning boat ride and see these graceful creatures swimming and playing in the sea.

Diving in Apo Island

Apo Island is a small island located off the coast of Guihulngan. It has a diverse marine life, making it an ideal spot for diving. Visitors can swim with a variety of fish species, explore colourful coral reefs, and encounter turtles and other fascinating marine creatures.

Food and Drink Scene of Guihulngan

Must-Try Dishes

Budbud and Puto Maya

Budbud and Puto Maya are native delicacies that are staples in Guihulngan. These sweet and sticky rice cakes are usually paired with hot chocolate and enjoyed as a breakfast food.

Ginamos

Ginamos is a type of fermented fish paste that is famous in Guihulngan. It is usually served as a condiment for rice and other dishes, and it adds a unique flavour to Filipino cuisine.

Local Beverages

Cerveza Negrense

Cerveza Negrense is a famous beer brand that is brewed in Negros Oriental. This beer is made from local ingredients and is famous for its refreshing taste.

Laksoy

Laksoy is a traditional rice wine that is brewed in Guihulngan. It is served during special occasions and celebrations, and it has a sweet and fruity taste.

Where to Stay in Guihulngan: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Guihulngan offers several accommodation options, ranging from budget-friendly hostels to luxury hotels. Some of the best places to stay in the city include:

Bethel Guesthouse

Bethel Guesthouse is a popular choice among backpackers and budget travelers. It offers comfortable and clean rooms with basic amenities at an affordable price.

Casa Amparo Heritage House

Casa Amparo Heritage House is a charming colonial-style guesthouse that offers a unique and comfortable experience. This heritage house has seven rooms, each of which is uniquely decorated and elegant.

What to Buy in Guihulngan: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Guihulngan is known for its delicious native delicacies, handicrafts, and textiles. Visitors can bring home some of the following souvenirs and local products:

  • Budbud and Puto Maya
  • Ginamos
  • Handwoven textiles
  • Handcrafted items made from bamboo

Local Culture and Customs of Guihulngan

Guihulngan is a culturally rich city, with many traditions and customs that are still practiced today. For instance, during the annual feast of St. Francis of Assisi, the city holds various events and processions to honor their patron saint. The locals are also known for their hospitality and friendliness, making visitors feel at home

Best Time to Visit Guihulngan

The best time to visit Guihulngan is during the dry season, which runs from November to April. Visitors can enjoy the city’s outdoor attractions and activities without worrying about the rain.

Do’s and Don’ts of Guihulngan

Do:

  • Respect local customs and traditions.
  • Try local cuisine and delicacies.
  • Wear appropriate clothing when visiting religious sites.

Don’t:

  • Disrespect religious traditions or customs.
  • Litter in public areas.
  • Engage in illegal activities.

Safety and Health

Safety in Guihulngan

Guihulngan is relatively safe, and crime rates are low. However, it is always best to be cautious, especially when travelling alone or at night.

Health in Guihulngan

It is essential to take the usual health precautions when traveling to Guihulngan. These include drinking only bottled water, avoiding street food (if you have a sensitive stomach), and getting vaccinated before your trip (if necessary).

Getting to Guihulngan: Transportation Options and Logistics

Guihulngan is easily accessible by air, land, and sea.

Airports Near Guihulngan

The nearest airport to Guihulngan is Dumaguete Airport, located approximately 60 kilometers away from the city center. From there, visitors can take a shuttle bus or a private car to reach Guihulngan.

Bus Stations Near Guihulngan

Several bus companies operate daily routes to Guihulngan from major cities like Dumaguete and Cebu. These include Ceres Bus line, Dimple Star Transport, and Vallacar Transit.

Other Options

Visitors can also rent a car or a motorcycle to explore the city on their own. This is an excellent option for those who want to explore Guihulngan at their own pace.

How to Get Around in Guihulngan

Guihulngan has several transportation options, including tricycle, motorcycle taxis, and jeepneys. These options are convenient and affordable, and they ensure visitors can reach their destination.

Where to Go Next from Guihulngan: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Guihulngan is a great base for exploring the nearby towns and cities of Negros Oriental. Some of the destinations that visitors can consider include:

  • Dumaguete City (60 kilometers from Guihulngan)
  • Bais City (67 kilometers from Guihulngan)
  • Siquijor Island (115 kilometers from Guihulngan)

Conclusion

Guihulngan is a destination that should be on every traveler’s itinerary when visiting Negros Oriental. It offers a rich cultural heritage and natural beauty that is worth exploring. We hope this tour guide helps you plan an unforgettable trip to Guihulngan.
