If you’re planning a trip to the Visayas region of the Philippines, make sure to add Dauin to your itinerary. Located in Negros Oriental, Dauin is a small town that offers a unique blend of culture, history, and natural beauty. In this guide, we’ll take a closer look at Dauin and highlight the best attractions, places to eat, things to do, and more.

Introduction to Dauin

Dauin is a town on the eastern coast of the island of Negros. The town is known for its beautiful beaches, diving spots, and marine sanctuaries. Visitors to Dauin can explore the picturesque mountains, lush forests, and scenic countryside. Dauin is located near many other popular tourist destinations in the Philippines, making it a great starting point for exploring the country.

List of Places to Visit in Dauin

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Dauin is home to many culturally important sites that are worth visiting. Here are some of the most notable:

  • Dauin Church: This beautiful church dates back to the 16th century and is a must-visit for anyone interested in religious architecture and history.
  • Bulakawe Forest Reservation: Located in the mountains of Dauin, this forest is home to many native tree species and is a great place to go hiking.
  • Sta. Catalina de Alejandria: A well-known landmark in Dauin, this bell tower dates back to the 18th century and is a popular spot for photography.

Natural Attractions of Dauin: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Dauin is famous for its outdoor activities and natural beauty. Here are some popular places to visit:

  • Apo Island: This island is well-known for its stunning coral reefs and underwater life. Visitors can go snorkeling or scuba diving to explore the reefs and see the abundance of marine life.
  • Malatapay Market: This market is always bustling with activity and is a great place to experience the local culture and customs. It’s a great spot to pick up locally made souvenirs and handicrafts.
  • Mount Talinis: This mountain is the highest peak in Negros Oriental and is a popular spot for hiking and camping.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Dauin

There are many great hiking and biking routes in Dauin. Here are some popular ones:

  • Bacong-Dumaguete Trail: This trail is popular among mountain bikers and offers stunning views of the countryside.
  • Apolong-Ayungon Trail: This trail is great for experienced hikers and is known for its challenging terrain and beautiful views.
  • Twin Lakes Trail: The Twin Lakes Trail is a popular hiking route that takes visitors through the beautiful forests and mountains of Dauin.

Best Things to Do in Dauin: Activities and Tours

There are many exciting things to do in Dauin, from water sports to cultural experiences. Here are some of the top activities and tours:

  • Scuba diving: Dauin is known for its world-class scuba diving spots, and visitors can explore the stunning coral reefs and underwater life.
  • Dolphin watching: Visitors can take a boat tour and watch dolphins swimming in their natural habitat.
  • Island-hopping: Visitors can take a tour of the surrounding islands and explore their unique cultures and natural beauty.

Week-Long Itinerary for Dauin

Visitors to Dauin can easily spend a week exploring the town and its surrounding areas. Here is a sample itinerary:

  • Day 1: Explore Dauin’s cultural sites, including the Dauin Church and the Bulakawe Forest Reservation.
  • Day 2-3: Go scuba diving in Apo Island and explore the underwater marine life.
  • Day 4: Take a trip to Mount Talinis and go hiking and camping.
  • Day 5-6: Explore the surrounding islands and go island-hopping.
  • Day 7: Experience the local culture and customs at the Malatapay Market.

Food and Drink Scene of Dauin

Dauin offers many delicious local dishes and unique beverages. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Adobo: A popular Philippine dish made with meat marinated in soy sauce and vinegar.
  • Lechon: A roasted pig dish that is a staple at Filipino celebrations.
  • Sinigang: A tamarind-based soup that is a comfort food for many Filipinos.

Local Beverages

  • Tuba: A traditional Filipino alcoholic drink made from coconut sap.
  • Barako coffee: A strong coffee made from locally grown beans.
  • Buko juice: Fresh coconut water that is a refreshing drink on a hot day.

Where to Stay in Dauin: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Dauin offers many accommodation options for visitors, from luxury hotels to budget-friendly hostels. Here are some of the top places to stay in Dauin:

  • Bahura Resort and Spa: A luxury resort that offers stunning views of the ocean and the mountains.
  • Liquid Dive Resort: A popular resort among scuba divers that offers comfortable accommodation and diving packages.
  • Dauin Homestay: A budget-friendly option that provides visitors with an authentic experience of Filipino culture and hospitality.

What to Buy in Dauin: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Dauin offers many unique souvenirs and locally made products that visitors can bring home as gifts or keepsakes. Here are some popular items to look out for:

  • Hand-made jewelry: Dauin is known for its beautiful jewelry, made by skilled artisans from local materials such as shells and beads.
  • Local handicrafts: Visitors can find many locally made handicrafts, such as woven baskets and pottery.
  • Barako coffee: Visitors can bring home a bag of locally grown and roasted coffee beans as a souvenir.

Local Culture and Customs of Dauin

Dauin has a rich culture and customs, influenced by its history and population. Here are some things to keep in mind when visiting:

  • Religion: The majority of the population in Dauin is Catholic, and visitors should respect local places of worship and traditions.
  • Filipino hospitality: Filipinos are known for their warm hospitality and friendliness, and visitors should expect to be welcomed and treated like family.
  • Language: The official language of Dauin is English, and most locals speak it fluently. However, learning some basic Tagalog or Visayan phrases can go a long way in connecting with locals.

Best Time to Visit Dauin

Dauin has a tropical climate and can be visited year-round. However, the best time to go is during the dry season, which falls between November and May. During this time, the weather is mild, and the seas are calm, making it a perfect time for outdoor activities and water sports.

Do’s and Don’ts of Dauin

Do:

  • Respect local customs and traditions.
  • Try the local cuisine and beverages.
  • Take care of the environment and wildlife.

Don’t:

  • Wear revealing clothing in public areas.
  • Insult or disrespect locals or their culture.
  • Litter or damage the environment.

Safety and Health

Safety in Dauin

Dauin is a relatively safe place for visitors, but it’s always important to take precautions and be aware of your surroundings. Here are some safety tips to keep in mind:

  • Stay in well-lit areas at night.
  • Keep your valuables safe and secure.
  • Be cautious when swimming or diving and follow all safety guidelines.

Health in Dauin

Dauin has modern healthcare facilities and reputable hospitals. Visitors should take standard precautions such as drinking bottled water and wearing insect repellent to avoid illnesses. Travelers should also make sure to have travel insurance to cover any medical emergencies.

Getting to Dauin: Transportation Options and Logistics

Dauin is easily accessible by air, land, and sea. Here are some transportation options:

Airports Near Dauin

The nearest airports to Dauin are the Dumaguete Airport and the Tagbilaran Airport. Both airports offer regular domestic and international flights.

Bus Stations Near Dauin

Dauin can be reached by bus from major cities such as Cebu, Manila, and Bacolod. The Ceres Liner and Dimple Star buses are reliable and affordable.

Other Options

Visitors can also hire private cars or take a van or taxi service to get to Dauin.

How to Get Around in Dauin

Dauin is a small town that can be easily explored on foot, but visitors can also rent a motorcycle or a bicycle to get around. Tricycles and jeepneys are also common modes of transportation.

Where to Go Next from Dauin: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Dauin is located near many other popular tourist destinations in the Philippines. Here are some nearby places to visit:

  • Dumaguete: A nearby city known for its colonial architecture and vibrant nightlife.
  • Panglao Island: Known for its stunning beaches and vibrant marine life.
  • Bohol: An island province famous for its Chocolate Hills and the Tarsier Sanctuary.

Conclusion

Dauin is a hidden gem in the Philippines that offers visitors a unique experience of culture, natural beauty, and adventure.
