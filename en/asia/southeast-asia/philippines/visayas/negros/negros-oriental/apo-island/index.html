Introduction to Apo Island

Nestled in the southern part of Negros Oriental, Apo Island is a small volcanic island located just off the coast of the larger island of Negros. The island is known for its pristine coral reefs and clear waters, making it a prime destination for snorkeling and diving. Apo Island is also a hub of marine biodiversity, with over 650 documented species of fish and 400 types of corals.

List of Places to Visit in Apo Island

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Apo Island may not be home to many museums or historical landmarks, but the island has a rich cultural heritage that can be explored through interactions with the local community. Visitors can opt for a homestay experience with local families and learn about their traditional way of life. Alternatively, a walk around the island will lead you to an old lighthouse and a small chapel built in the early 1900s.

Natural Attractions of Apo Island: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Apo Island’s natural beauty is its biggest draw. The island is home to a marine sanctuary teeming with coral reefs and colorful fish, making it a popular spot for scuba diving and snorkeling. Visitors can also take a trek up the island’s lush hills to see panoramic views of the surrounding seascape.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Apo Island

Apo Island is a hiker’s paradise, with a number of trails that crisscross the island’s hills and valleys. The most popular route for hiking is the one that leads to the lighthouse and the chapel, which affords stunning views of the sea. Biking is also a great way to explore the island and can be rented for a nominal fee at the small stores near the beach.

Best Things to Do in Apo Island: Activities and Tours

Aside from the natural beauty of the marine sanctuary, there are many activities that visitors can partake in while on Apo Island. For those who love the water, there are scuba diving and snorkeling tours that take visitors to the best spots for observing marine life. One can also try their hand at fishing with local fishermen, or take a boat ride along the island’s coast to search for dolphins and turtles. For those who prefer to stay on land, there are cultural tours to nearby villages, or a trek through the hills to see the stunning views of the island.

Food and Drink Scene of Apo Island

Must-Try Dishes

The food in Apo Island is a mix of Filipino and Western cuisine, with seafood taking center stage. Some of the must-try dishes on the island include kinilaw (ceviche) made with fresh seafood, grilled fish and prawns, and a local dish called “ginamos”, which is a condiment made from fermented fish.

Local Beverages

When it comes to beverages, Apo Island is known for its fresh coconut juice and shakes made from local fruit such as mangoes and bananas. Beer lovers can also enjoy local beer brands such as San Miguel and Red Horse.

Where to Stay in Apo Island: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Accommodation options on Apo Island are limited, with only a handful of small resorts and guesthouses dotting the coastline. While the options may be limited, the quality and level of service is high, with most resorts offering modern amenities such as air conditioning and WiFi.

What to Buy in Apo Island: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Souvenir shops around Apo Island offer a range of locally made products such as handmade jewelry and clothing, woven baskets and bags, and local artwork.

Local Culture and Customs of Apo Island

The local culture of Apo Island is heavily influenced by maritime traditions. Many of the island’s residents make their living through fishing and rely on the sea for food and sustenance. Visitors to the island can take part in home-stays with local families to learn more about their way of life and customs.

Best Time to Visit Apo Island

The best time to visit Apo Island is between March and May when the skies are clear and the waters are calm, making it ideal for snorkeling and diving. However, it is also the peak season, so expect more crowds and higher rates during this time. Alternatively, the months between June and October receive some rainfall but are less crowded and offer slightly lower rates.

Do’s and Don’ts of Apo Island

Do:

  • Respect the natural environment by avoiding stepping on or touching corals and other marine life.
  • Participate in responsible tourism by choosing hotels and tours that prioritize sustainability and conservation.
  • Interact with the local community and respect their customs and way of life.

Don’t:

  • Use plastic or other single-use materials that may end up in the sea and harm marine life.
  • Take souvenirs from the marine sanctuary, including shells, rocks, or pieces of coral.
  • Wear sunscreen or other products that may contain harmful chemicals that damage the environment.

Safety and Health

Safety in Apo Island

Apo Island is generally safe, but visitors are advised to take care when swimming in the sea and to listen to the advice of local guides in regards to currents and other hazards. It’s also important to take precautions against mosquito-borne illnesses by bringing mosquito repellent and wearing long-sleeved clothing.

Health in Apo Island

While there is a small medical center on Apo Island, it is best to come prepared with any necessary medications or treatments that you may need during your visit. Also, bear in mind that fresh water is a limited resource on the island, so be mindful of your water usage and try to keep consumption low.

Getting to Apo Island: Transportation Options and Logistics

Getting to Apo Island requires taking a ferry from the nearby town of Zamboanguita, located on the southeastern tip of Negros Oriental. From there, visitors can take a local tricycle or jeepney to Malatapay, and then take a small boat to Apo Island.

Airports Near Apo Island

The nearest airport to Apo Island is Dumaguete Airport, which is located about 30km away from the ferry terminal in Zamboanguita. Flights to Dumaguete may require a stopover in Manila or Cebu.

Bus Stations Near Apo Island

There are several bus stations in Dumaguete that offer connections to Zamboanguita, from where visitors can take the ferry over to Apo Island.

Other Options

Private transfers or taxis can also be arranged from Dumaguete to Apo Island, although they can be more expensive than taking public transportation.

How to Get Around in Apo Island

Given Apo Island’s relatively small size, the best way to get around is on foot or by renting a bicycle. Tours or boat rides can also be arranged to explore the surrounding areas of the island.

Where to Go Next from Apo Island: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Visitors to Apo Island can explore nearby destinations such as Siquijor Island, known for its waterfalls and swimming holes, or Dumaguete City, a university town with a vibrant food and arts scene.

Conclusion

Apo Island is a true paradise for beach and nature lovers, offering a mix of outdoor adventure, cultural immersion, and relaxation in a pristine natural atmosphere. Whether you’re an avid diver or just looking for a quiet retreat, Apo Island is a must-visit destination in the Philippines.
