Introduction to Basay

Located in Negros Oriental, Basay is a quaint town in the province of Negros in the Visayas region of the Philippines. Blessed with stunning natural beauty, vibrant culture, and friendly locals, Basay is the perfect destination for travelers seeking an off-the-beaten-track experience.

List of Places to Visit in Basay

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Visitors to Basay can immerse themselves in the rich culture and history of the region by visiting some of its museums and historical attractions.

  • Basay Church: Also known as the San Nicholas de Tolentino Church, this majestic structure is a must-visit attraction in Basay. Built-in the early 19th century, the church is known for its distinctive Baroque-style architecture, ornate altar, and impressive bell tower.

  • Basay Public Market: Located in the heart of town, the Basay Public Market is an excellent place to experience local culture and mingle with friendly locals. Visitors can shop for traditional handicrafts, fresh produce, and other locally-made souvenirs.

  • Basay Historical Landmarks: Basay is also home to several significant historical landmarks, including the Basay World War II Marker, The Lim-Peter House, and the Basay Municipal Hall.

Natural Attractions of Basay: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Basay’s natural wonders are awe-inspiring, and visitors will be spoilt for choice.

  • Mambajo Tree Park: Located 11 kilometers from the town proper, Mambajo Tree Park is a beautiful jungle-covered park that has been declared a bird sanctuary. Here, visitors can go hiking, bird watching, and enjoy the stunning views of the surrounding mountains and valleys.

  • Lake Balanan: A serene freshwater lake surrounded by pristine forests, Lake Balanan is a perfect destination for a relaxing weekend getaway. Visitors can enjoy kayaking, swimming, or simply basking in the stunning natural beauty of the area.

  • Cabacungan Cave: For adventure seekers, the Cabacungan Cave is an exciting attraction in Basay. Located inside a lush forest, visitors can explore the cave’s mystifying beauty, crystal-clear waters, and impressive rock formations.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Basay

Basay’s stunning natural beauty offers plenty of opportunities for outdoor enthusiasts to get their adrenaline pumping by exploring the many hiking and biking trails around the area. Some of the most popular trails include:

  • Basay Eco-Adventure Trail: This 15-kilometer trail offers breathtaking views of the mountain ranges, lush forests, and rivers of Basay. Hikers can also enjoy cliff diving and swimming in the river.

  • Mount Talinis: A challenging but rewarding trek, Mount Talinis is the highest peak in Negros Oriental. The trail is approximately 20 kilometers round trip and offers magnificent views of the surrounding landscapes.

Best Things to Do in Basay: Activities and Tours

There is no shortage of activities to enjoy when visiting Basay. From cultural experiences to outdoor adventures, there is something for everyone.

  • Explore Local Markets: For an authentic cultural experience, explore local markets like Basay Market. Visitors can shop for traditional souvenirs, sample local delicacies, and meet friendly locals.

  • Visit Basay’s Historic Landmarks: Basay is home to several significant historical landmarks, including the Basay World War II Marker, The Lim-Peter House, and the Basay Municipal Hall.

  • Have a Picnic Day: Basay’s natural beauty makes the area ideal for picnics and outdoor relaxation. Visitors can enjoy picnic areas like the Mambajo Tree Park and Lake Balanan.

Food and Drink Scene of Basay

Must-Try Dishes

A visit to Basay isn’t complete without trying out some of its delicious local cuisine. Some of the must-try dishes include:

  • Binignit: A popular Visayan sweet stew made with root crops like taro, sweet potatoes, bananas, and tapioca pearls cooked in coconut milk.

  • Ukoy: A local take on shrimp fritters, made with grated vegetable and shrimp.

  • Budbud or Biko: A sticky rice cake served with grated coconut and caramelized sugar.

Local Beverages

Basay is also home to several refreshing local beverages, such as:

  • Buko Juice: Coconut juice served in its natural shell.

  • Barley Water: Made from roasted barley grain, water, and sugar.

  • Sago’t Gulaman: A refreshing drink made with sago pearls, jelly, and brown sugar syrup.

Where to Stay in Basay: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Basay offers a range of accommodation options to suit all budgets and preferences. Some of the best places to stay in Basay include:

  • Basay Coconut Grove Beach Resort: A beachfront resort offering comfortable rooms, modern facilities, and stunning views of the ocean.

  • Basay Proud Lodge: A budget-friendly hotel with clean and tidy rooms, friendly staff, and easy access to nearby attractions.

  • Basay Resthouse: A charming guesthouse with a homey feel and rustic decor, located amid lush greenery.

What to Buy in Basay: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Visitors to Basay can bring home some unique souvenirs and local products, such as:

  • Hand-woven textiles and handicrafts: Basay is known for its intricate weavings and unique handmade crafts.

  • Local delicacies: Pick up some Binignit, Budbud, or other locally made sweets and snacks.

  • Hand-carved wooden furniture: Basay has a strong woodworking industry and is known for producing high-quality furniture.

Local Culture and Customs of Basay

The people of Basay are renowned for their warm hospitality and cultural richness. Some essential customs and etiquettes to keep in mind while visiting Basay include:

  • Respect local traditions and customs.

  • Dress modestly when visiting religious sites.

  • Remove shoes before entering homes or holy establishments.

Best Time to Visit Basay

The best time to visit Basay is between November and May when the weather is sunny, and the skies are clear. The rainy season typically runs from June to October, which can result in muddy and challenging trekking conditions.

Do’s and Don’ts of Basay

Do:

  • Do try local delicacies and immerse yourself in Basay’s culture.

  • Do respect local customs and traditions.

  • Do bring appropriate hiking or outdoor gear when going on excursions.

Don’t:

  • Don’t litter and discard trash properly.

  • Don’t disrespect local customs or traditions.

  • Don’t take photographs or videos without permission.

Safety and Health

Safety in Basay

Basay is generally a safe destination for travelers. However, visitors should take the usual precautions to avoid theft and disturbances.

Health in Basay

Visitors to Basay should ensure that they have updated routine vaccinations and take necessary precautions to avoid mosquito-borne illnesses. It is also essential to carry adequate health insurance, as medical facilities in the region may be limited.

Getting to Basay: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Basay

The closest major airport to Basay is Dumaguete Airport, about 80 kilometers from Basay. Private transport can be arranged upon arrival, or visitors can choose to take a bus or taxi.

Bus Stations Near Basay

Basay can be reached by bus from Dumaguete or Bacolod City. Buses run daily, and have frequent departures from early morning until late afternoon.

Other Options

Visitors can also hire private transport or rent a car to get to Basay. However, be advised that road conditions may be challenging, so it is advised to use experienced local drivers.

How to Get Around in Basay

Basay is a small town that can easily be explored on foot. For longer distances, visitors can choose to rent a motorcycle or hire a tricycle taxi.

Where to Go Next from Basay: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

From Basay, visitors can explore nearby destinations and attractions, including:

  • Dumaguete City: Known for its vibrant student scene, beautiful beaches, and spectacular underwater scenery.

  • Siquijor Island: A beautiful island paradise known for its pristine beaches, beautiful coral reefs, and mystical attractions.

Conclusion

Basay may not be the most popular tourist destination in the Philippines, but it has a lot to offer visitors seeking a unique travel experience. With its stunning natural beauty, rich culture, and friendly locals, Basay deserves a place on every traveler’s list. So pack your bags and start planning your itinerary today!
