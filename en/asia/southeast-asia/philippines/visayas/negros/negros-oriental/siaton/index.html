Introduction to Siaton

Nestled in the heart of Negros Oriental, Siaton is a small town that offers visitors a unique experience with its natural beauty, unique culture, and friendly locals. The town is known for its stunning landscapes that feature a combination of mountains, beaches, and lush forests. Despite its size, it has a vibrant culture, rich history, and a diverse range of activities for tourists to enjoy. Exploring Siaton is like discovering a hidden paradise, far away from the crowds of more popular destinations in the Philippines.

List of Places to Visit in Siaton

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Siaton Parish Church: A centuries-old church that is a popular landmark in town. The church has undergone several renovations and has a beautiful facade, stained glass windows, and impressive architectural features.
  • Balay Negrense Museum: A historical museum that provides an insight into the region’s fascinating history and culture. The museum has displays of the famous Balay Architecture, which uses native materials and techniques to build houses.
  • San Antonio Abad Parish Church: Another religious site that attracts visitors. The church boasts stunning architecture, with intricate carvings and sculptures.

Natural Attractions of Siaton: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Twin Lakes: Located in Lake Balanan, the Twin Lakes are a unique natural attraction that offers spectacular views. The two lakes have different colors, with one being an enchanting emerald green and the other a deep blue. Visitors can go on a boat tour or hike to the top of the mountain for panoramic views.
  • Balanan Lake: An impressive man-made lake surrounded by beautiful mountains. It’s a popular spot to go kayaking, swimming, or hiking.
  • Mount Talinis: One of the highest peaks in the region, Mount Talinis offers visitors a thrilling adventure. Visitors can hike to the summit, which is over 1,900 meters above sea level, or explore the nearby hot springs and waterfalls.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Siaton

Siaton has several scenic hiking and biking trails for visitors to explore. Mount Talinis is a popular destination for hikers and offers a variety of trails for different skill levels. For bikers, the Circumferential Road surrounding the Twin Lakes is a scenic route to take.

Best Things to Do in Siaton: Activities and Tours

  • Water Sports: Visitors can enjoy kayaking and swimming in the various natural springs and lakes in Siaton.
  • Diving: Siaton has several diving spots that offer visitors a chance to explore the marine life and coral reefs.
  • Island Hopping: Visitors can go on an island-hopping tour and explore the nearby islands such as Apo Island, which is known for its pristine beaches and marine diversity.

Week-Long Itinerary for Siaton

Day Activities
Day 1 Arrive in Siaton, check-in at your hotel, and unwind.
Day 2 Visit Siaton Parish Church, Balay Negrense, and San Antonio Abad Parish Church.
Day 3 Hike to the top of Mount Talinis or explore the hot springs.
Day 4 Explore Twin Lakes or go kayaking in Balanan Lake.
Day 5 Take a diving tour or enjoy some water sports.
Day 6 Go on an island hopping tour or explore the nearby beaches.
Day 7 Shop for souvenirs or relax at your hotel before departure.

Food and Drink Scene of Siaton

Must-Try Dishes

  • Inasal: A popular grilled chicken dish that uses a unique marinade and is cooked over charcoal.
  • Kinilaw: A local dish made of raw fish marinated in vinegar, lime juice, and spices.
  • Kare-kare: A Filipino dish made with beef stewed in a peanut-based sauce, vegetables, and served with rice.

Local Beverages

  • Tuba: A traditional alcoholic drink made from fermented coconut sap.
  • Barako Coffee: A strong coffee that is locally grown in the Philippines and known for its bold flavor.

Where to Stay in Siaton: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Siaton has various accommodation options to suit different budgets. Some popular options include:

  • Balay Kogon: A luxury eco-resort with spacious villas and excellent amenities.
  • Apo Island Beach Resort: A beachfront resort that offers visitors a tranquil escape.
  • Living Forest Resort: A mid-range resort that has a variety of rooms and excellent facilities.

What to Buy in Siaton: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Handmade Crafts: Visitors can purchase handmade crafts such as baskets, pottery, and woven textiles that are made by local artisans.
  • Local Coffee: Visitors can take home packs of Barako coffee, which is locally grown and known for its bold flavor.

Local Culture and Customs of Siaton

Siaton is deeply rooted in cultural traditions, and visitors should respect these customs:

  • Visitors should dress modestly when visiting religious sites.
  • Visitors should ask for permission before taking pictures of people or their homes.

Best Time to Visit Siaton

The best time to visit Siaton is during the dry season, which runs from December to May. The weather is ideal for outdoor activities and sightseeing.

Do’s and Don’ts of Siaton

Do:

  • Respect local customs and traditions.
  • Try the local food and drinks.
  • Take time to explore Siaton’s natural beauty.

Don’t:

  • Litter or damage the natural environment.
  • Wear revealing clothing in religious sites or public areas.
  • Disrespect local customs or traditions.

Safety and Health

Safety in Siaton

Siaton is a safe place to visit, but visitors should take some basic precautions:

  • Don’t leave your belongings unattended.
  • Be aware of your surroundings, especially at night.
  • Take licensed taxis or hire a private car for transportation.

Health in Siaton

Visitors should take precautions to avoid getting sick during their stay in Siaton:

  • Drink bottled water.
  • Use insect repellent to avoid mosquito bites.
  • Wear sunscreen and a hat to protect against the sun.

Getting to Siaton: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Siaton

The nearest airport is Dumaguete Airport, which has several flights from Manila and other cities in the Philippines.

Bus Stations Near Siaton

Visitors can take a bus from Cebu City to Dumaguete, then take a bus to Siaton.

Other Options

Visitors can hire a car or take a taxi from Dumaguete to Siaton.

How to Get Around in Siaton

Visitors can take a tricycle, hire a private car, or rent a motorbike to get around Siaton and explore the nearby attractions.

Where to Go Next from Siaton: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Dumaguete: A bustling city that offers visitors a chance to explore Filipino culture and history.
  • Apo Island: A picturesque island that is perfect for a day trip or overnight stay.
  • Siquijor: Known as the Island of Fire, Siquijor is a mystical island surrounded by folklore and legends.

Conclusion

Siaton has a lot to offer visitors, from its stunning natural scenery to its rich cultural traditions. Visitors who take the time to explore this remote corner of the Philippines will be rewarded with unforgettable experiences. Whether you’re seeking adventure, relaxation or a cultural immersion, Siaton has something for everyone.
