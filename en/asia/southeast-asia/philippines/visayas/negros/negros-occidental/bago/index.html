Introduction to Bago

Situated in the province of Negros Occidental, Bago is a charming town in the Visayas region of the Philippines. The town’s rich history, along with its natural splendor, draws in a steady flow of tourists every year. With serene beaches, lush rainforests, world-class hiking trails, and inspiring cultural landmarks, Bago is a must-visit destination for budding travellers and adventurers alike.

List of Places to Visit in Bago

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • St. John the Baptist Church: Built in the 19th century, this impressive church is a prime example of colonial era Baroque-style architecture.
  • Bago City Museum: This museum is a must-visit attraction for culture enthusiasts looking to explore the town’s rich heritage and appreciate art.
  • Mag-aso Falls: Situated on the borders of Bago and Valencia, the Mag-aso Falls is a stunning natural wonder with cascading waterfalls and clear blue pools.

Natural Attractions of Bago: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Mount Kanlaon: Known as the “Crowning Glory of Negros,” Mount Kanlaon is one of the toughest and most rewarding hiking expeditions not just in Bago, but in the entire Visayas region.
  • Bago River: The serene Bago River is a must-visit destination for nature lovers and fishing enthusiasts.
  • Malingin Falls: The hidden gem of Bgy. Mailum, Malingin Falls is a picture-perfect waterfall surrounded by towering cliffs and lush vegetation.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Bago

  • Mount Kanlaon Traverse: This intense hiking experience takes hikers from Bago City up to the summit of the majestic Mount Kanlaon, with stunning views of the Visayan islands and the ocean.
  • Bago River Scenic Bike Ride: This leisurely ride follows the meandering Bago River and takes riders through scenic countryside roads.

Best Things to Do in Bago: Activities and Tours

  • Island Hopping: Take a local boat and explore the surrounding islands, snorkel in tranquil waters, and enjoy fresh seafood prepared by locals.
  • City Tour: Visit the historical landmarks of Bago and soak in the local culture on this guided tour.

Week-Long Itinerary for Bago

Day 1: Visit St. John the Baptist Church and explore the historical past of Bago.

Day 2: Take a scenic bike ride along the Bago River.

Day 3: Experience the majesty of Mount Kanlaon.

Day 4: Visit the Bago City Museum and the icon of the town, Mag-aso Falls.

Day 5: Check out the Malingin Falls and spend the rest of the day island-hopping.

Day 6: Take a day trip to nearby Bacolod City and visit the spectacular food scene.

Day 7: Spend a relaxed day at one of the town’s serene beaches.

Food and Drink Scene of Bago

Must-Try Dishes

  • Inasal: A local grilled chicken dish cooked on charcoal and served with rice.
  • Batchoy: A hearty noodle soup made with beef or pork and garnished with garlic, spring onions, and crushed pork cracklings.
  • Piaya: A flatbread made with flour, sugar, and muscovado.

Local Beverages

  • Iced Tea: Sweetened black tea served over ice - a refreshing drink in the hot Philippine climate.
  • Mango Shake: A popular drink made with fresh mangoes - a perfect thirst quencher after a day of sightseeing.

Where to Stay in Bago: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • L’Fisher Eco Park: A nature-inspired hotel that offers guests a relaxing stay with a luxurious, eco-friendly vibe.
  • Bago City Hostel: Budget accommodation that provides a comfortable stay and convenient access to the town’s major attractions.
  • Caribbean Waterpark and Resotel: A family-friendly resort that features a water park, swimming pools, and a variety of amenities to cater to the needs of all guests.

What to Buy in Bago: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Muscovado Sugar: A unique type of unrefined sugar made from sugarcane juice and popular in this region.
  • Inasal Marinade: A classic marinade used for grilling that features a blend of lemon grass, calamansi, garlic, and other spices.

Local Culture and Customs of Bago

A rich blend of different cultural influences has shaped the unique identity of Bago. The people of Bago are friendly and warm, and their customs reflect their rich heritage.

Best Time to Visit Bago

The perfect time to visit Bago is from December to April when the weather is dry and pleasant. However, tourists can still visit Bago all year round, but they should be prepared for the occasional rainy season from May to November.

Do’s and Don’ts of Bago

Do:

  • Respect the local customs and traditions.
  • Always ask for permission before you take pictures or videos of people and places.
  • Be mindful of your surroundings and keep your valuables safe.

Don’t:

  • Get involved in any illegal activities that may cause harm to the environment or the community.
  • Litter or damage any of the natural or cultural landmarks.

Safety and Health

Safety in Bago

Bago is a peaceful town, but tourists must still take precautions to ensure their safety. They should avoid carrying large sums of money, valuables, or other expensive items with them.

Health in Bago

We recommend tourists to check their vaccinations before travelling to Bago. They should avoid drinking tap water and use only bottled water for drinking and brushing of teeth.

Getting to Bago: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Bago

The closest airport to Bago is the Bacolod-Silay International Airport, which is about 25 kilometres away.

Bus Stations Near Bago

There are several bus stations in Bago, such as the Ceres Bus Terminal, which has regular trips from Bacolod City.

Other Options

Tourists can also rent a private van, jeepney, or tricycle for a more convenient and flexible mode of transportation.

How to Get Around in Bago

Tricycles are a popular mode of transportation in Bago, and tourists can hail one on the street. There are also local buses and jeepneys that travel around the town, making it easy to get to different destinations.

Where to Go Next from Bago: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • The Ruins, Bacolod: A must-visit attraction near Bago, where tourists can explore remnants of an Italian-style mansion built in the early 1900s.
  • Sipalay City: A pristine area with picturesque beaches and stunning coral reefs ideal for diving and snorkelling.

Conclusion

Bago, Philippines, is a beautiful and culturally rich town that attracts tourists from all over the world. It offers an unforgettable experience, from its stunning natural wonders to its historical landmarks and vibrant culture. Plan your perfect getaway and experience the magic of Bago!
