A Comprehensive Travel Guide to Murcia, Philippines

Murcia is a quaint and charming town situated in the Negros Occidental province of the Philippines. With its rolling hills, scenic views, natural attractions, and cultural landmarks, this destination is a must-visit for anyone seeking an authentic Filipino experience. In this guide, we will take you through the top things to see and do in Murcia, where to stay, what to eat, and everything you need to know to plan a memorable trip.

List of Places to Visit in Murcia

Murcia offers an array of tourist attractions, from cultural landmarks to natural wonders. Here are some of the top places to visit in this charming town.

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Murcia Church. Also known as Our Lady of the Immaculate Conception Church, this religious structure is a famous landmark of Murcia and a sacred place for the locals of the town.
  • Hacienda Maria. This historical estate provides insights into the town’s colonial past, giving you a glimpse of the lives of the landlords and tenants that once prospered here.

Natural Attractions of Murcia: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Mambukal Mountain Resort. This popular resort boasts hot springs, waterfalls, and natural pools, perfect for those looking to relax or enjoy time with family and friends.
  • Napleon’s Hill. This hill offers an excellent view of the town and is a perfect spot for photography enthusiasts.
  • Canlaon Volcano National Park. This park is home to Mount Canlaon, the highest peak in central Philippines and a majestic sight. The park also boasts hot springs, geysers, and a lush forest, perfect for hiking and exploring.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Murcia

Murcia is a haven for outdoor enthusiasts, with its rolling hills and scenic views. If you are up for a challenge, these hiking and biking routes are sure to test your endurance.

  • Mambukal Trail. This 7-kilometer trail takes you through a lush forest, passing by hot springs and natural pools along the way.
  • Mt. Marapara. This trail will take you up a steep hill, rewarding you with a panoramic view of the town below.
  • Mount Canlaon. If you are up for an adventure, take on the challenge of hiking to the peak of Mount Canlaon, taking in spectacular views of the national park’s natural beauty.

Best Things to Do in Murcia: Activities and Tours

Murcia offers a range of exciting activities and tours, from exploring historical landmarks to enjoying outdoor adventures.

  • Experience Local Culture. Attend the town’s fiesta celebration in honor of Our Lady of the Immaculate Conception, held every December, to experience traditional parades, dances, and foods.
  • Visit a Hot Spring. Mambukal Mountain Resort offers hot springs perfect for soaking away your worries.
  • Try Adventure Sports. Have an adrenaline-pumping experience at the resort’s zipline, wall climbing, or the outdoor slide obstacle course.

Here’s a week-long itinerary that will help you see the best of Murcia.

Food and Drink Scene of Murcia

Murcia has a diverse and flavorful cuisine, with specialties that often utilize fresh ingredients harvested from the nearby mountain range and farmlands. Don’t leave without trying these must-try dishes and drinks.

Must-Try Dishes

  • Chicken Inasal. This popular grilled chicken dish is a staple in Negros Occidental Cuisine, perfectly paired with rice and atchara.
  • Kadios, Baboy, Langka. A delicious dish made of pigeon pea, pork belly, and jack-fruit.
  • Piaya. Murcia’s sweet treat made of flaky bread and filled with muscovado sugar or ube.

Local Beverages

  • Tuba. Tuba is a sweet, fermented drink made from the sap of coconut trees. Tuba is best served cold, refreshing, and with a hint of sweetness.
  • Mango Piña Shake. Murcia’s famous mix of fresh mangoes and pineapples blended with yoghurt and ice.

Where to Stay in Murcia: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Whether you’re looking for budget-friendly or luxury accommodations, Murcia offers a range of options for all kinds of travelers.

  • Mambukal Mountain Resort. This nature-friendly resort offers a selection of cottages, villas, and lodges located in proximity to most tourist attractions.
  • The Farm Chalet. This rustic farmstay offers cozy rooms with lovely views of the surrounding hills and nearby farmlands.
  • O Hotel. For luxury accommodation, head to O Hotel; it offers excellent rooms, restaurants, and a fitness center.

What to Buy in Murcia: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

If you’re looking for a unique souvenir, Murcia has a range of products that make for great gifts or personal mementos.

  • Muscovado sugar. This unrefined, brown sugar is a famous product in Negros Occidental, made directly from sugarcane juice.
  • Buri Handicrafts. This traditional handicraft of the Philippines which includes baskets, hats, bags, and other items made from buri palm tree.
  • Local Coffee. Negros Occidental has been dubbed the coffee capital of the Philippines. So, grab a bag of freshly roasted beans to bring back home.

Local Culture and Customs of Murcia

Murcia values its traditions and custom, and it’s essential for visitors to know some of their cultural practices and beliefs for a more immersive experience.

The locals are predominantly Catholic, and their cultural practices reflect their faith. Respect the church, religious rituals, and the environment. Always dress modestly while visiting churches, shrines, and other religious sites. When staying in the homes of locals, take your shoes off when entering and do not leave your feet pointing towards anyone; it is considered offensive.

Best Time to Visit Murcia

The optimal time to visit Murcia is during the dry season from November to May, providing you with sunny and pleasant weather to enjoy all outdoor activities.

Do’s and Don’ts of Murcia

Do:

  • Try the local delicacies. Murcia is known for its unique cuisine, which you must taste to understand Philippine food culture.
  • Respect the culture and customs. Filipinos are known for their hospitality; return that hospitality by respecting their culture and customs.
  • Be friendly and smile. Filipinos are friendly people, and a simple smile will bring about a warm welcome.

Don’t:

  • Don’t litter Littering is prohibited, and respect for the environment is paramount.
  • Don’t over-schedule. Take your time and enjoy the serenity of the town.

Safety and Health

Safety in Murcia

Murcia is a relatively safe place, but outsiders should be vigilant, particularly at nighttime and avoid any confrontation with strangers. Petty crimes such as pickpocketing and snatchers also occur in crowded areas, so keep your valuables safe at all times.

Health in Murcia

While there are no major medical facilities within Murcia, there are clinics and hospitals in the nearby towns and cities that offer medical assistance. It’s advisable to have travel insurance upon your arrival.

Getting to Murcia: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Murcia

The closest airport is the Bacolod-Silay International Airport, which is around 35 kilometers away. From there, you can take a bus, van, or rent a car to reach the town.

Bus Stations Near Murcia

The transportation hub in Bacolod City is a few kilometers away from the airport, a short distance from the city center. From here, you can either take a direct bus or a jeepney going to Murcia.

Other Options

If you have a car, drive through the Northern Negros circumferential highway, which offers incredible views of the natural scenery of Negros Occidental. Public jeepneys and tricycles are the most common modes of public transportation in the town.

How to Get Around in Murcia

Tricycles are the most convenient mode of transportation in Murcia, especially when traveling around the town center. Jeeps and passenger vans also ply the main roads, making rounds to neighboring towns and cities.

Where to Go Next from Murcia: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Silay City. A nearby area famous for its well-preserved ancestral homes and heritage museum, showcasing the rich history of the Philippines.
  • Bacolod City. The capital city of Negros Occidental, home of the best inasal restaurants and colorful festivals, including the famous masked street dance, Masskara Festival.
  • Don Salvador Benedicto. A picturesque highland town that provides panoramic views of the natural scenery and a laid-back vibe.

Conclusion

Murcia is a hidden gem in the Negros Occidental province of the Philippines, offering a host of activities and experiences for those looking for an authentic Filipino experience. Whether you are seeking adventure, culture, or relaxation, Murcia has something for everyone, making it a must-visit destination in the country. Plan your trip, follow this guide, and get ready to create unforgettable memories.
