Introduction to Negros Occidental

Negros Occidental is a province located in Visayas, Philippines, known for its rich cultural history, natural attractions, and world-renowned cuisine. The province is home to some of the most celebrated festivals in the country, such as the MassKara Festival in Bacolod City and the Panaad sa Negros Festival in Bago City.

Negros Occidental is also famous for its sugarcane plantations, which contributed to the province’s wealth and heritage. The Panaad sa Negros Festival is a testament to this legacy, where the 13 cities and 19 municipalities showcase their respective products, food, and crafts.

List of Places to Visit in Negros Occidental

When planning a trip to Negros Occidental, it is essential to identify the top attractions and must-visit places in the province. Here is a rundown of the most popular tourist destinations:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  1. Negros Museum - Located in Bacolod City, the Negros Museum is a significant cultural center that houses the history and heritage of the Negrense people. It features various exhibits showcasing their cultural practices, agricultural traditions, and historical events.

  2. Balay Negrense Museum - This museum is located in Silay City and serves as a tribute to the sugar barons who contributed to Negros Occidental’s development and growth. The museum features a replica of a typical sugar baron’s house during the early 1900s.

  3. The Ruins - Dubbed as the “Taj Mahal of Negros,” The Ruins is a famous landmark located in Talisay City. It was built by a sugar baron as a mansion for his wife but was later burned down during the war. Today, only the façade remains, but it is still a stunning sight to behold.

Natural Attractions of Negros Occidental: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  1. Mambukal Mountain Resort - This resort is known for its hot sulfur springs, seven waterfalls, and challenging hiking trails. It offers various activities, including trekking, camping, and bird watching.

  2. Lakawon Island - Lakawon Island is a lesser-known yet equally beautiful beach destination in Negros Occidental. Its main attraction is the Tawhai Floating Bar, a bar that is entirely floating in the sea, offering an unparalleled experience of enjoying a drink while surrounded by the turquoise waters of the Visayan sea.

  3. Mag-aso Falls - Mag-aso Falls is a twin waterfall located in the municipality of Kabankalan. It is situated in a verdant, jungle-like setting that is relatively untouched and unspoiled.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Negros Occidental

Negros Occidental offers various hiking and biking trails that provide breathtaking scenic views of the province. Here are some of the best trails to consider:

  1. Mt. Kanlaon National Park - This majestic volcano stands at over 7000 feet and is the highest peak in Central Philippines. It offers a challenging yet rewarding hiking experience to those who dare to climb it.

  2. Don Salvador Benedicto-San Carlos Heights Route - This 65-kilometer long route is perfect for bikers looking for a scenic ride. It offers stunning views of the Negros Occidental countryside, including lush vegetation, rolling hills, and picturesque landscapes.

Best Things to Do in Negros Occidental: Activities and Tours

Negros Occidental is not only famous for its attractions but also for the various activities and tours that visitors can enjoy. Here are some of the best things to do in the province:

  1. Food Tripping - Negros Occidental is famous for its cuisine, characterized by sweet and savory flavors. It’s worth trying KBL, a stew made of Kadios (pigeon peas), Baboy (pork), and Langka (jackfruit), or Inasal, a grilled chicken dish marinated in vinegar and calamansi.

  2. Festival Hopping - Negros Occidental is home to some of the country’s most celebrated festivals, including Maskara Festival and Panaad sa Negros Festival. Visitors can join the festivities and enjoy colorful parades, cultural performances, and concerts.

Food and Drink Scene of Negros Occidental

No trip to Negros Occidental is complete without indulging in its world-renowned cuisine and local beverages. Here are some of the must-try dishes and drinks:

Must-Try Dishes

  1. Bacolod Chicken Inasal - This grilled chicken dish marinated in calamansi and vinegar is a staple in Negros Occidental’s cuisine. It is usually served with rice, soy sauce, and vinegar dipping sauce.

  2. KBL - A hearty stew made with Kadios (pigeon peas), Baboy (pork), and Langka (jackfruit) in coconut milk, KBL offers a unique flavor that is sweet, savory, and creamy.

Local Beverages

  1. Antique Brew - This coffee brand is made from 100% Arabica beans grown in the mountain ranges of Negros Occidental. It has a smooth, rich flavor and is perfect for coffee lovers.

  2. Basi - This local wine is made from sugarcane juice and fermented in earthen jars for months. It offers a distinctively sweet and sour taste and is usually served during celebrations and special occasions.

Where to Stay in Negros Occidental: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Negros Occidental offers a wide range of accommodation options, from luxurious hotels to budget-friendly guesthouses and hostels. Here are some of the best places to stay in the province:

  1. L’Fisher Hotel - This hotel is located in Bacolod City and offers elegant rooms and suites, an outdoor pool, and a fitness center.

  2. Mayfair Plaza Pension House - This pension house is a budget-friendly option located in downtown Bacolod City. It offers simple yet comfortable rooms and is conveniently located near tourist attractions and transportation hubs.

What to Buy in Negros Occidental: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Negros Occidental is not only known for its cuisine and attractions but also for its unique souvenirs and local products. Here are some of the best items to bring home:

  1. Hand-woven Fabrics - Negros Occidental’s artisan communities produce vibrant, colorful textiles that make for beautiful curtains, fabrics, and tablecloths.

  2. Piaya - This sweet flatbread is one of the most popular treats in Negros Occidental. It is made of muscovado sugar and flour and is perfect to bring home as a gift or snack.

Local Culture and Customs of Negros Occidental

The Negrense culture is deeply rooted in its agricultural heritage and rich history. It is also known for its hospitality and jovial spirit. Visitors are encouraged to respect local customs and manners, especially during festivals and social gatherings.

Best Time to Visit Negros Occidental

The best time to visit Negros Occidental is from November to April, which is the province’s dry season. During this time, visitors can enjoy the cool weather, clear skies, and various festivals and activities.

Do’s and Don’ts of Negros Occidental

Do:

  1. Try the local cuisine and drinks.

  2. Respect local customs and traditions.

  3. Bring home souvenirs and local products.

Don’t:

  1. Litter or violate environmental laws.

  2. Disrespect local culture and manners.

  3. Engage in inappropriate behavior or activities.

Safety and Health

Safety in Negros Occidental

Negros Occidental is generally a safe destination for tourists. However, visitors are advised to exercise caution and common sense, especially when traveling alone or at night.

Health in Negros Occidental

Visitors are encouraged to get vaccinated and take necessary precautions before traveling to Negros Occidental. It is also recommended to bring mosquito repellent and sunscreen, especially when engaging in outdoor activities.

Getting to Negros Occidental: Transportation Options and Logistics

Negros Occidental is accessible through various transportation options, including air and land travel. Here are some of the options:

Airports Near Negros Occidental

  1. Bacolod-Silay Airport - This airport is the closest to Bacolod City and is the busiest airport in Negros Occidental. It has daily flights to Manila and Cebu.

  2. Dumaguete Airport - This airport is located in Negros Oriental and is an alternative option for visitors to Negros Occidental.

Bus Stations Near Negros Occidental

  1. Bacolod Ceres Bus Terminal - This bus terminal is located in downtown Bacolod and offers various routes to other parts of the province.

  2. Dumaguete Ceres Bus Terminal - This bus terminal is located in Dumaguete City and offers routes to nearby cities and municipalities in Negros Occidental.

Other Options

  1. Taxis and Private Cars - Taxis and private cars are available for hire in Negros Occidental and are best for visitors who prefer a more convenient and private mode of transportation.

  2. Jeepneys and Tricycles - Jeepneys and tricycles are the most affordable mode of transportation in the province. They are best for short distances and are ideal for travelers on a budget.

How to Get Around in Negros Occidental

Getting around Negros Occidental is relatively easy, with various transportation options available. Here are some of the options:

  1. Jeepneys - Jeepneys are the primary mode of transportation and are ideal for short distances and commuting within the city.

  2. Tricycles - Tricycles are a popular option for getting around smaller towns and villages. They are best for short distances.

  3. Taxi - Taxis are available in major cities and are ideal for tourists who prefer a more private mode of transportation.

Where to Go Next from Negros Occidental: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Negros Occidental is an excellent starting point for exploring the best destinations in Visayas. Here are some of the nearby destinations and excursions you can consider:

  1. Bais City Dolphin and Whale Watching - Bais City is home to several dolphin and whale species that are best seen from a river cruise along the Tanon Strait.

  2. Siquijor Island - Siquijor Island is a mystical island known for its pristine beaches and traditional folklore.

Conclusion

Negros Occidental is a destination worth visiting, with its rich cultural history, stunning natural attractions, and world-renowned cuisine. By following this comprehensive tour guide, visitors can explore the best of what this province has to offer, from its fascinating museums to its scenic outdoor adventures.
