Silay, located in Negros Occidental, is an off-the-beaten-path destination in the Philippines that is often overlooked by tourists. However, this charming city is a hidden gem that boasts of a rich cultural heritage, natural beauty, and adventure-filled activities. In this comprehensive tour guide, we’ll take you on a journey through Silay, highlighting the must-see places, things to do, food and drink scene, local culture and customs, and safety and health tips.

Introduction to Silay

Silay is a city in the province of Negros Occidental in the Visayas region of the Philippines. It is known as the “Paris of Negros” due to the impressive heritage houses built during the sugar boom of the 19th century that reflects European and American architectural styles. The city is also dubbed the “Arts Capital of Western Visayas” because of its vibrant art scene. With its unique blend of culture, history, and adventure, Silay is a great destination for those who want to take a break from the usual tourist spots.

List of Places to Visit in Silay

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Silay is a city that is steeped in history and culture, and it’s no wonder that the top tourist attractions here reflect this. Here are the culturally important attractions that you should visit in Silay:

  • Balay Negrense Museum: A heritage museum housed in a restored 19th-century mansion that showcases the lifestyle of affluent families during the sugar boom era.
  • Silay Heritage Walk: A walking tour that takes you through the city’s historic streets, passing by beautifully restored colonial houses with European and American architectural influences.
  • San Diego Pro-Cathedral: A historic church built in 1925 that features a stunning altar that is made of silver and gilt.
  • Bernardino Jalandoni Museum: A museum that exhibits the collection of the Jalandoni family, showcasing prehistoric artifacts, antique furniture, and an extensive art collection.
  • Hofileña Ancestral House: A heritage house-turned-museum with a vast collection of antique porcelain, European paintings, and old photographs.

Natural attractions of Silay: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Aside from its cultural attractions, Silay also has natural wonders that are worth exploring. Here are the natural attractions that you can visit in Silay:

  • Patag Mountain Range: A scenic mountain range located at the heart of Silay that offers beautiful views of the city and surrounding landscapes.
  • Mambukal Mountain Resort: A natural resort that features seven waterfalls, hot springs, butterfly gardens, and adventure activities like ziplining and wall climbing.
  • Campuestohan Highland Resort: An amusement park with a variety of attractions like ziplines, rope courses, and a Sky Bike. It also offers an excellent view of the city.
  • Balaring Beach: A beach in Silay with clear waters and white sand. It’s an excellent spot for swimming and relaxing.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Silay

For adventure lovers, Silay has plenty of hiking and biking routes that offer scenic views of the city and the surrounding landscapes. Here are the best routes to explore:

  • Patag Trail: A popular hiking trail that leads to the summit of Patag Mountain, with stunning views of Silay and the surrounding areas.
  • Mambukal Mountain Bike Trail: An adventurous bike trail that zigzags through the natural terrain, giving bikers a thrilling ride through the lush mountainside.
  • Hacienda Triton Mountain Bike Trail: An exciting mountain bike trail that winds through a sprawling sugarcane plantation, passing through forested trails, and rolling hills.

Best Things to Do in Silay: Activities and Tours

Aside from visiting the top tourist spots, Silay also offers activities and tours that will add to your Silay experience. Here are the best things to do in Silay:

  • Silay Art Walk: A tour that showcases the artworks and art galleries around the city, including the artworks featured in heritage houses.
  • Silay Food Tour: A food tour that takes you through the city’s food scene, exploring the local street food and the city’s dining scene.
  • Turtle Island Tour: A tour that takes you to the Turtle Island Wildlife Sanctuary, where you can catch a glimpse of the endangered Hawksbill turtle.
  • Silay Street Dancing Festival: An annual festival that highlights street dancing and colorful parades, showcasing the vibrant culture of Silay.

Food and Drink Scene of Silay

Silay’s food and drink scene is as rich as its cultural heritage. Here are the must-try dishes and local beverages that you should try:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Inasal: A dish made of grilled chicken marinated in a mixture of vinegar, lemon, and spices.
  • Kansi: A soup made with meat, vegetables, and spices, usually beef or pork with bone marrow.

Local Beverages

  • Batuan Fruit Drink: A refreshing drink made from the batuan fruit, a sour fruit grown in Negros.
  • Muscovado Sugar Cane Juice: A sweet drink made from sugarcane juice and muscovado sugar, with a distinct caramel flavor.

Where to Stay in Silay: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Silay is a small city, but it has plenty of accommodation options to choose from. Here are some of the best places to stay in Silay:

  • Seda Capitol Central: A trendy hotel located in Bacolod City, which is only a few kilometers away from Silay.
  • Balay ni Tana Dicang: A heritage guesthouse that is restored to its former glory with Carribean and European influences.
  • Blue Roof Inn Pension House: A budget-friendly inn with clean and comfortable rooms, located in the heart of the city.

What to Buy in Silay: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Silay has unique local products and souvenirs that are worth taking home. Here are some of the best things to buy in Silay:

  • Piaya: A sweet pastry filled with muscovado sugar or Ube jam, a famous souvenir in Negros.
  • Barong Tagalog: A traditional Filipino shirt made from pineapple fiber or silk, popular in Negros.
  • Muscovado Sugar: A type of unrefined brown sugar that is famous in Negros, with a distinct caramel flavor.

Local Culture and Customs of Silay

Silay has a rich cultural heritage that is reflected in its arts, architecture, and food scene. Filipinos are hospitable and friendly people, and visitors can expect a warm welcome when they visit Silay. It’s essential to respect the culture and customs of the locals when visiting the city, especially when visiting religious or historical sites.

Best Time to Visit Silay

The best time to visit Silay is during the dry season, which runs from November to April. The weather is sunny, and there is little to no chance of rain, making it perfect for outdoor activities and exploring the city’s attractions. The peak tourist season is during the Christmas holidays, Sinulog Festival in January, and the Masskara Festival in October.

Do’s and Don’ts of Silay

Do:

  • Dress appropriately, especially when visiting religious sites like churches or temples.
  • Greet locals with a smile and a “hello” in the local dialect or English.
  • Practice sustainable tourism by minimizing your impact on the local environment and culture.

Don’t:

  • Be disrespectful to the locals and culture.
  • Engage in activities that are harmful to the environment, animals, or local communities.
  • Leave your trash behind when visiting natural attractions or city streets.

Safety and Health

Safety in Silay

Silay is a generally safe place to visit, but like any other city, it’s best to remain vigilant and avoid suspicious areas, especially at night. Solo travelers and women should take extra precautions, especially when walking around the city at night.

Health in Silay

Visitors should take precautions against common illnesses like dengue fever and malaria, which are common in some parts of the Philippines. It’s best to bring mosquito repellent and wear long-sleeved clothes to avoid mosquito bites. Tap water is not drinkable, and it’s best to drink bottled water or boiled water.

Getting to Silay: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Silay

The nearest airport to Silay is Bacolod-Silay International Airport, which is located in the nearby city of Bacolod. From the airport, visitors can take a taxi or bus to Silay.

Bus Stations Near Silay

Bacolod City has several bus stations and terminals that serve different parts of the city and nearby towns. Visitors can take a bus or jeepney from Bacolod Bus Terminal to Silay.

Other Options

Visitors can also rent a car or hire a private van for a more convenient trip to Silay. Some resorts and hotels also offer shuttle services to and from the city.

How to Get Around in Silay

Silay is a small city that is easy to navigate on foot or by bike. Jeepneys and tricycles are also available for visitors who wish to explore the city’s attractions in comfort and convenience.

Where to Go Next from Silay: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Silay is an excellent base for exploring other destinations in Negros and nearby islands. Here are some nearby places that you can explore:

  • Bacolod City: A bustling city with a rich culture, food scene, and popular festivals like Masskara Festival.
  • Hinoba-an Beach: A secluded beach located in the southern part of Negros that features white sand and clear waters.
  • Sagay Marine Reserve: A marine reserve located in the northern part of Negros that is home to diverse marine life, including sea turtles and colorful corals.

Conclusion

Silay is a hidden gem in the Philippines that offers a unique blend of culture, history, adventure, and natural beauty. From exploring heritage houses to biking through mountain trails, indulging in local cuisine to participating in colorful festivals, there’s never a shortage of things to do and see in Silay. With this comprehensive tour guide, you can now plan your next adventure to Silay with confidence and enthusiasm.
