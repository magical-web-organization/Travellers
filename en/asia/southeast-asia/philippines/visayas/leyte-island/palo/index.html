Introduction to Palo

Located in Leyte Island, Palo is a quaint town in the Visayas region of the Philippines. It boasts a rich cultural heritage, thriving nature, and an impeccable food scene. This guide will take you through a journey to explore the best of Palo, including the must-visit attractions, best things to do, local food and drink scene, where to stay, and more.

List of Places to Visit in Palo

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  1. MacArthur Landing Memorial National Park - a tribute to General MacArthur and his troops’ arrival on the shores of Leyte during World War II. The park features a statue of MacArthur and a museum showcasing artifacts from the war.
  2. Santo Nino Shrine and Heritage Museum - a grand palace that houses a museum showcasing the history and culture of Palo and Leyte Island. The shrine is also a popular pilgrimage site for Catholics.
  3. The People’s Center and Library - a cultural and educational hub that features various exhibits and programs dedicated to the local history and culture.

Natural Attractions of Palo: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  1. San Juanico Bridge - the longest bridge in the Philippines, connecting Leyte Island to Samar Island with breathtaking views of the San Juanico Strait.
  2. Samar Island Natural Park - a protected reserve that covers more than 33,000 hectares of forestland, waterfalls, and biodiversity. It is also a sanctuary for native species like the Philippine eagle.
  3. Nacpan Beach - a serene and secluded beach boasting serene waters, white sand, and coconut trees. It is a perfect spot for beachfront relaxation, swimming, and picnics.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Palo

  1. Mt. Alto - a challenging but rewarding hiking trail, taking its visitors through a lush forest before reaching the summit. The views offer a scenic glimpse of Palo and beyond.
  2. Tacloban City Circuit - a bike tour that combines historical landmarks, stunning views, and cultural highlights culminating in a fun ride across the San Juanico Bridge.
  3. Tagahawang or The Walls to the River Trek - a popular trekking destination for the adventurous and experienced hikers. The trail offers a combination of forest exploration, river crossing, and cliff jumping.

Best Things to Do in Palo: Activities and Tours

  1. Scuba diving and Snorkeling - Palo’s seas offer a wealth of underwater attractions for marine enthusiasts. Gear up and explore the reefs, shipwrecks and marine life.
  2. Kayaking and Paddle Boarding - Tour the beautiful Palo coastline through kayaking and paddle boarding offering a good workout as well.
  3. Heritage and Historical Tours - guided tours of important landmarks take you to the historical past of the area.
  4. Cultural Workshops - learn about local crafts, dance, and food preparation from seasoned artisans and food experts.

Food and Drink Scene of Palo

Must-Try Dishes:

  1. Linupak - a dessert made of mashed cassava, sugar, and coconut.
  2. Moron - a combination of sweetened sticky rice and meat filling.
  3. Binagol - a sweet delicacy made from grated coconut, gabi, and sugar, infused with rich flavors of chocolate and peanut.

Local Beverages:

  1. Tuba - an alcoholic drink made from coconut sap.
  2. Calamansi Juice - a refreshing citrus drink served cold.
  3. Barako Coffee - a strong and bold brew made from Liberica coffee beans found only in the country.

Where to Stay in Palo: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  1. The Oriental Leyte - a beach resort that offers stunning views of San Juanico Bridge, and a range of modern amenities and facilities.
  2. GV Hotel - a budget-friendly chain hotel that offers clean and comfortable rooms, located in the town center.
  3. Hilltop Guesthouse - a cozy and quaint guest house offering warm Filipino hospitality and panoramic views of the landscape.

What to Buy in Palo: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  1. Banig - a traditional mat made of grass or bamboo, used as a bed or floor covering.
  2. Romblon Marble - intricately carved marble that serves a decorative accent to any home.
  3. Bolo Knife - a long, flat-bladed weapon used by respected Filipino warriors.

Local Culture and Customs of Palo

  1. Religion - Palo is predominantly Catholic, and religious traditions are deeply embedded in daily life.
  2. Festivals - Palo celebrates several cultural festivals, including the Abayan Festival, Holy Week, and the Feast of the Immaculate Conception.
  3. Family - Family values and hospitality are integral to the local culture.

Best Time to Visit Palo

The best time to visit Palo is during the dry season from November to May. The climate is dry and mild, with temperatures ranging from 25°C to 30°C, making it ideal for outdoor activities and beach hopping.

Do’s and Don’ts of Palo

Do:

  1. Observe good manners and respect local customs and traditions.
  2. Bring enough sunscreen and insect repellent to avoid any skin damage.
  3. Support the local economy by buying local products and patronizing locally-owned establishments.

Don’t:

  1. Wear revealing clothing when visiting religious sites or attending cultural events.
  2. Litter or damage nature when trekking or exploring the natural wonders of Palo.
  3. Disrespect elderly people and authority figures.

Safety and Health

Safety in Palo

Palo is generally a safe destination for tourists. However, it is recommended to observe caution in unfamiliar areas, avoid carrying large amounts of cash and valuable items, and stay vigilant in crowded places.

Health in Palo

It is advisable to get vaccinated against common illnesses like tetanus, typhoid, and hepatitis A and B before visiting Palo. Drink bottled or boiled water and use insect repellents to avoid getting sick. In case of a medical emergency, it is advisable to head to the Tacloban City Hospital.

Getting to Palo: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Palo

The airlines servicing the Tacloban D. Z. Romualdez Airport include:

  • Cebu Pacific
  • Philippine Airlines
  • AirAsia
  • SkyJet
  • Philippine Air Force

The airport is about 10 kilometers from downtown Palo. There are taxi services available outside the airport terminals.

Bus Stations Near Palo

  • Duptours Shuttle Service
  • Van-A-Van
  • Super 5 Bus
  • Pintados
  • CUL Transport

Other Options

Hiring a private car is also a popular option for tourists visiting Palo.

How to Get Around in Palo

Tricycles, bicycles, and jeepneys are the most common modes of transport in Palo town proper. Motorcycles are another popular option for getting around the countryside.

Where to Go Next from Palo: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  1. Ormoc City - another bustling city in Leyte that offers a range of natural and cultural attractions and exquisite culinary experiences.
  2. Tinago Falls - a majestic and secluded waterfall located in Biliran, offering a serene and refreshing escape from urban life.
  3. Biri Island - an island off the coast of Northern Samar that offers a stunning landscape of rock formations and a rich marine ecosystem.

Conclusion

Palo offers a wealth of cultural and natural treasures, making it an ideal destination for travelers who seek history, adventure, and relaxation. The town offers warm hospitality, delicious food, and incredible scenery that will make your visit an unforgettable one. Plan your trip now to Palo and experience the best of what this charming town has to offer.
