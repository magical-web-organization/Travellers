Introduction to Abuyog

Located in the southwestern part of Leyte Island, Abuyog is a beautiful town known for its rich cultural heritage, natural attractions, and friendly locals. With its stunning landscapes and recreational activities, it’s a must-visit destination for every traveler to the Philippines.

Here’s a comprehensive guide to help you plan your trip to Abuyog:

List of Places to Visit in Abuyog

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Santo Niño Parish Church

The Santo Niño Parish Church is one of the oldest churches in the Philippines built-in 1740. It is known for its beautiful façade, bell tower, and religious significance. It’s a great place to visit for those who are interested in Philippine history and religious culture.

Casa Real

Casa Real is another historical landmark in Abuyog. It was once a government building during the Spanish colonial era. Today, it serves as a museum that showcases the rich cultural heritage of Abuyog.

Natural Attractions of Abuyog: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Mount Tres Marias

Mount Tres Marias is located in Barangay Mahayag, Abuyog and known as one of the highest peaks in Eastern Visayas. The hike to the summit takes around 5-6 hours, but the breathtaking views of the surrounding mountains and forests are worth the effort.

Acacio Beach Resort

Acacio Beach Resort is a popular beach destination in Abuyog. It is well-known for its white sand beaches, crystal clear waters, and recreational activities such as kayaking, paddle-boarding, and surfing. If you’re looking for a bit of relaxation, this is the perfect spot.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Abuyog

Talisay Trail

The Talisay Trail is a popular hiking and biking route in Abuyog. It is a scenic trail that offers magnificent views of the surrounding mountains and the Tañon Strait.

Best Things to Do in Abuyog: Activities and Tours

Visit Buga Buga Hills

A trip to Buga Buga Hills should be on your travel itinerary when visiting Abuyog. You’ll be able to witness and learn about the site’s interesting history, as well as enjoy magnificent views of Abuyog.

Go on a City Tour

This tour will take you to Abuyog’s iconic landmarks and significant historical sites, including the Santo Niño Parish Church and Casa Real. The tour is customizable, so you can choose the places you want to visit according to your preference.

Week-Long Itinerary for Abuyog

Day 1: Arrive in Abuyog and take a city tour to explore the town’s historic landmarks.

Day 2: Visit the Buga Buga Hills and learn about the area’s history.

Day 3: Hike the Talisay Trail and enjoy the scenic views.

Day 4-5: Relax and enjoy Acacio Beach Resort.

Day 6: Visit Mount Tres Marias for an adventure hike.

Day 7: Shop for local souvenirs, eat local delicacies, and enjoy your last day in Abuyog.

Food and Drink Scene of Abuyog

Must-Try Dishes

Binagol

Binagol is a sweet delicacy made from grated taro, coconut milk, butter and sugar. It is traditionally wrapped in banana leaves and steamed.

Kinilaw

Kinilaw is a traditional Filipino dish that is made from raw fish marinated in vinegar and spices. It’s a refreshing dish with a tangy taste.

Local Beverages

Coconut Wine

Coconut wine or “tuba” is a traditional Filipino alcoholic beverage made from fermented coconut sap. It has a sweet and sour taste and is perfect for pairing with local delicacies.

Where to Stay in Abuyog: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Rueda’s Pension House

Rueda’s Pension House is a budget-friendly accommodation that offers clean and comfortable rooms. They also provide excellent customer service and are happy to help you plan your Abuyog adventures.

Acacio Beach Resort

Acacio Beach Resort is a luxury resort that offers guests a private paradise-like experience. Guests can enjoy the white sand beaches, clear waters, and recreational activities.

What to Buy in Abuyog: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Binagol

Binagol is a famous delicacy in Abuyog and is perfect as a pasalubong. You can find it in local markets and souvenir shops.

Abuyog Handicrafts

Abuyog is known for its intricately crafted handicrafts such as caskets, baskets, and mats made of bamboo, rattan, and nito.

Local Culture and Customs of Abuyog

Festivals

Abuyog celebrates its town fiesta every January 15, in honor of the Sto. Niño. The festival is known for its street dancing, colorful parades, and religious processions.

Best Time to Visit Abuyog

The best time to visit Abuyog is during the dry season, from November to April. During this time, the weather is sunny, and the skies are clear, making it perfect for outdoor activities. The town fiesta in January is also a great time to visit Abuyog and celebrate with the locals.

Do’s and Don’ts of Abuyog

Do:

  • Respect the local culture and customs.
  • Try the local delicacies.
  • Interact with the locals and learn about their way of life.

Don’t:

  • Wear revealing clothing when visiting churches or temples.
  • Litter or damage the environment.
  • Disrespect the locals and their beliefs.

Safety and Health

Safety in Abuyog

Abuyog is a safe town, but it’s always good to be mindful of your surroundings. Make sure to take precautionary measures when hiking or engaging in adventurous activities.

Health in Abuyog

Check with your doctor regarding necessary vaccinations and medications to bring. Make sure to bring mosquito repellent and sunscreen to protect yourself from insect bites and harmful UV rays.

Getting to Abuyog: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Abuyog

The nearest airport to Abuyog is the Daniel Z. Romualdez Airport in Tacloban City. From there, you can take a bus or hire a private car to Abuyog.

Bus Stations Near Abuyog

Buses from Tacloban City and Ormoc City pass through Abuyog.

Other Options

There are also vans that travel to Abuyog from Tacloban City and Ormoc City.

How to Get Around in Abuyog

The most convenient way to get around Abuyog is by tricycle, which is a common mode of transportation in the Philippines. You can hire one to take you around town, or you can rent a motorbike to explore the surrounding areas.

Where to Go Next from Abuyog: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Tacloban City

Tacloban City is the capital city of Leyte Island and is known for its historic landmarks and natural attractions.

Ormoc City

Ormoc City is a bustling city located in western Leyte Island. It is known for its hot springs, beaches, and other recreational activities.

Conclusion

Abuyog is a beautiful town with a rich cultural heritage and natural attractions that make it a must-visit destination in Leyte Island, Philippines. Enjoy the beautiful scenery, the delicious food, and the friendly locals. Plan your trip now and see what Abuyog has to offer.
