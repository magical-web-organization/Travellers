Introduction to Ormoc

Ormoc is a quaint city situated in the western part of Leyte Island in Visayas, Philippines. Known for its natural beauty, Ormoc is a must-visit destination for those who want to experience the countryside and the charm of the Philippines. With its rich cultural heritage and endless natural attractions, there is something for everyone in Ormoc.

List of Places to Visit in Ormoc

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Ormoc City Superdome – This sports complex was once used as a shelter for typhoon Yolanda victims. At present it is being used for hosting various local and national events.

  • Lake Danao National Park – Known for its breathtaking view, Lake Danao is considered a crown jewel of Ormoc. Visitors can go on a boat ride, take a picnic blanket and relax for hours while enjoying stunning views of nature.

  • The Ormoc Museum and Cultural Center – Ormoc’s local museum showcases the city’s past, present, and future. The museum houses the city’s historical collection and modern art.

Natural Attractions of Ormoc: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Tongonan Hotspring National Park – The park is located in the municipality of Kananga, not too far from Ormoc. It is one of the country’s few geothermal fields, and visitors can take a dip in the hot spring pool.

  • Cabintan Hot Spring – If you are the kind of traveler who likes to chase thrill and excitement, then head over to Cabintan Hot Spring. This place is perfect for a relaxing dip in warm, healing spring waters.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Ormoc

  • Ormoc City Nature Park – The park has a diverse terrain that caters to different levels of hikers and mountain bikers. Non-adventurers can enjoy the beautiful view that the park has to offer.

  • Mt. Pangasugan – For adventure seekers and mountaineers, the Pangasugan trek is the perfect activity. Trekking through the mountain can be tough, but the view from the top is worth it.

Best Things to Do in Ormoc: Activities and Tours

  • Kayaking in Lake Danao – Kayaking on Lake Danao is an absolute must-do. It allows you to fully immerse yourself in nature whilst enjoying local cuisine.

  • Tongonan Hotspring National Park Tour – Learn about the history of geothermal power and take a dip in the hot springs and pools.

  • Night Market and Strolling – After a busy day of trekking and sightseeing, spend an evening at the Ormoc Night Market. Get a chance to taste the local flavors of grilled meat and seafood while being entertained by local performers.

Week-Long Itinerary for Ormoc

Day Activity
1 Visit Ormoc City Nature Park and Mt. Pangasugan
2 Explore Lake Danao National Park, take a boat ride and have a picnic
3 Learn about Ormoc’s cultural heritage at the Ormoc Museum and Cultural Center

Food and Drink Scene of Ormoc

Must-Try Dishes

  • Binagol – This is a sticky dessert made of root crops and coconut milk.

  • Adobo – Pork or chicken marinated in a concoction of vinegar, soy sauce, garlic, and bay leaves and served with a heaping of rice.

  • Pancit Canton – Considered a staple among Filipinos, Pancit Canton is a stir-fried noodle dish with meat, vegetables and a variety of sauces.

Local Beverages

  • Coconut Water – A refreshing alternative to water, coconut water has become a sought-after drink for its natural benefits.

  • Tuba – Commonly known as coconut wine, Tuba is one of the popular alcoholic drinks in the Philippines.

Where to Stay in Ormoc: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Ormoc Villa Hotel – Located just behind Ormoc City Hall, this hotel has comfortable rooms with modern amenities.

  • Villafranca Pension House – A budget-friendly accommodation option located in the city center close to shopping malls and restaurants

  • Sugarland Suite Ormoc – This hotel offers spacious and classy rooms perfect for families or couples.

What to Buy in Ormoc: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Chocolate Coated Pineapple – One of the sweetest treats you can buy in Ormoc.

  • Tinghoy Sweets – A milk candy infused with a hint of peanuts.

  • Bananas – The city produces some of the sweetest bananas, making it a great souvenir.

Local Culture and Customs of Ormoc

The Ormocanons are known for their strong faith and strong ties to family. They are a friendly people that pride themselves on hospitality.

Best Time to Visit Ormoc

The best time to visit Ormoc is between March to May when the weather is warm, but not too hot. June to November is also ideal for those who love rainy weather.

Do’s and Don’ts of Ormoc

Do:

  • Dress conservatively when visiting religious sites

  • Use common sense when dealing with strangers

  • Always ask before taking photographs of locals

Don’t:

  • Litter

  • Disrespect religious sites or customs

  • Get involved in illegal activities like drugs or gambling

Safety and Health

Safety in Ormoc

Ormoc is generally a safe city, but always remain vigilant and take precautions like avoiding dark alleys at night.

Health in Ormoc

Make sure to stay hydrated and avoid drinking tap water. Seek medical help if experiencing any health-related issues.

Getting to Ormoc: Transportation Options and Logistics

  • Airports Near Ormoc – The nearest airport to Ormoc is Ormoc Airport. It has daily flights from Manila and Cebu.

  • Bus Stations Near Ormoc – Ormoc Bus Terminal is located near the city center and has buses going to different provinces and cities.

  • Other Options – Private car service is available within Ormoc city and nearby areas.

How to Get Around in Ormoc

The best way to explore Ormoc is on foot or by bike. Motorized tricycles and jeepney transportation is also readily available throughout the city.

Where to Go Next from Ormoc: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Tacloban City – The largest city in Eastern Visayas, Tacloban City is perfect for those who like cityscapes and nightlife.

  • Samar Island – Known for its beautiful beaches and waterfalls, Samar Island is the perfect destination for those who love a good seaside stay.

Conclusion

Ormoc is a beautiful destination with an abundance of natural attractions, unforgettable dining experiences, and an inviting local culture. Whether you want to enjoy outdoor adventures or immerse yourself in cultural experiences, Ormoc has something to offer everyone. With so much to do and see, Ormoc should definitely be on your list of must-visit destinations in the Philippines.
