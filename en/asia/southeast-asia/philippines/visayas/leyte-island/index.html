Introduction to Leyte Island

Leyte Island is located in Visayas, Philippines, which belongs to the Southeast Asian region. This lush paradise offers a mix of natural wonders and cultural landmarks that cater to different interests, making it a great destination for solo travelers, families, and adventure-seekers alike.

List of Places to Visit in Leyte Island

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

MacArthur Landing Memorial National Park

This park is a popular attraction in Leyte Island, commemorating General Douglas MacArthur’s historic return to the Philippines in 1944. There is a large statue of MacArthur at the park, as well as a museum with interesting artifacts from World War II.

Santo Niño Shrine and Heritage Museum

Located in Tacloban City, this museum showcases a collection of religious artifacts and memorabilia from the Marcoses, one of the prominent families in the Philippines.

Natural Attractions of Leyte Island: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Kalanggaman Island

Kalanggaman Island is a remote white-sand island that is perfect for beach lovers and water sports enthusiasts. It features crystal-clear waters and scenic views, making it a popular destination for snorkeling, diving, and camping.

Lake Danao National Park

This natural park covers an impressive 2,193 hectares of land and water, featuring a stunning crater lake that is perfect for kayaking and fishing. The whole area is surrounded by lush vegetation and scenic views that are perfect for hiking and camping.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Leyte Island

Leyte Island is a great destination for outdoor enthusiasts who love to hike and bike. Visitors who want to experience Leyte’s hiking and biking trails can check out the following routes:

  • Leyte Mountain Trail
  • Mahagnao Volcano Natural Park Trail
  • Pagsanghan River Trail
  • Bimtabaan Rice Terraces Trail

Best Things to Do in Leyte Island: Activities and Tours

Aside from hiking and biking, Leyte Island also offers a variety of activities and tours that cater to different interests. Visitors who want to have a more structured itinerary can check out the following activities and tours:

  • Day trip to Kalanggaman Island
  • Island hopping tour
  • Historical and cultural tour of Tacloban City
  • Caving and spelunking tour

Food and Drink Scene of Leyte Island

Leyte Island boasts a diverse food and drink scene that blends local and international flavors. Visitors who want to try out the local cuisine can check out the following must-try dishes:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Binagol - A famous dessert made of coconut milk and sweet potato
  • Moron - A sticky rice cake with chocolate filling
  • Kinilaw - A type of ceviche made with fresh fish, vinegar, and spices
  • Humba - A sweet and savory pork dish that is braised until tender

Local Beverages

  • Suman Latik - A rice cake with sweet coconut caramel sauce
  • Bahalina - A traditional Filipino wine made from nipa palm sap
  • Coconut juice - A refreshing drink made from fresh coconuts

Local Culture and Customs of Leyte Island

The people of Leyte Island are warm and friendly, always welcoming visitors with a smile. Visitors are advised to observe the following customs:

  • Always greet locals with a smile
  • Remove footwear before entering homes and places of worship
  • Modest clothing is appropriate when visiting churches and temples

Best Time to Visit Leyte Island

The best time to visit Leyte Island is during the dry season, which runs from November to May. This is when the weather is most favorable for outdoor activities and sightseeing.

Do’s and Don’ts of Leyte Island

Do:

  • Respect local traditions and customs
  • Be mindful of the environment and dispose of trash properly
  • Follow safety precautions when engaging in outdoor activities

Don’t:

  • Disrespect the privacy of locals
  • Litter or damage natural resources
  • Engage in illegal activities

Safety and Health

Safety in Leyte Island

Leyte Island is generally safe for tourists. Visitors are advised to take the following precautions:

  • Be aware of your surroundings at all times
  • Keep valuables such as wallets and phones secure
  • Use registered taxis or public transportation

Health in Leyte Island

Visitors are advised to drink bottled water and avoid street food to prevent foodborne illnesses. Mosquito-borne diseases such as dengue fever are also a concern, so it is recommended to use insect repellent and wear long sleeves and pants when possible.

Getting to Leyte Island is relatively easy, with several transportation options available. Visitors can get to Leyte Island via:

How to Get Around in Leyte Island

Visitors can explore Leyte Island via public transportation such as tricycles, jeepneys, or taxis. Private car or van rentals are also available for those who want more flexibility in their itinerary.
