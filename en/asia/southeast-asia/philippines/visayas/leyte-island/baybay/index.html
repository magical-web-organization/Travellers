A Comprehensive Travel Guide to Baybay, Philippines

Baybay is a charming town located in Leyte Island, in Visayas, Philippines, and is known for its beautiful natural landscapes, adventurous activities, and rich cultural heritage. Whether you’re a nature lover, a history buff, or a foodie, Baybay has something to offer for everyone. In this guide, we will provide a comprehensive tour of Baybay, including the best places to visit, local cuisine, transportation options, and more.

Introduction to Baybay

Baybay is a small town located on the western coast of Leyte Island in the Philippines. The town is named after a large body of water that flows into the Visayan Sea, which is also called Baybay. The town is home to friendly and hospitable locals who are eager to share their culture with visitors.

List of Places to Visit in Baybay

Baybay has a rich culture and natural beauty that is waiting to be explored. Below, we’ve compiled a list of the best places to visit in Baybay.

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Leyte Normal University Museum - A museum that showcases the history and culture of Leyte Island, including an exhibit on the Leyte Landing during World War II.
  • Baybay City Museum - A museum that highlights the history and culture of Baybay and the surrounding areas.
  • San Agustin Church - A historic church that dates back to the Spanish colonial era and features a unique mix of Spanish and Filipino architecture.

Natural Attractions of Baybay: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Agas-Agas Bridge - The tallest bridge in the Philippines and the perfect spot for thrill-seekers to go bungee jumping or ziplining.
  • Sambawan Island - A stunning island that is perfect for snorkeling, scuba diving, and relaxing on picturesque white sand beaches.
  • Lintaon Peak - A popular spot for hiking and trekking, where visitors can have panoramic views of the surrounding areas.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Baybay

Baybay has many opportunities for hiking and biking enthusiasts. Some of the best hiking and biking routes in Baybay include:

  • Bato-bato Peak - A challenging hike through rocky terrain and steep inclines that rewards visitors with stunning views of the surrounding areas.
  • Kang-ura Rice Field Trail - A leisurely bike ride through lush rice fields and scenic views of the town.
  • Mt. Pangasugan Trail - A moderate hike through tropical forests and panoramic views of the mountain ranges and the sea.

Best Things to Do in Baybay: Activities and Tours

Baybay offers a plethora of activities and tours for visitors to enjoy. Some of the best things to do in Baybay include:

  • Island Hopping Tour - Explore the nearby islands such as Sambawan Island, Maripipi Island, and Digyo Island, and experience their unique beauty and charm.
  • Cultural Tour - Visit the local markets, attend cultural festivals, and learn about the history and culture of Baybay.
  • Adventure Tour - Go bungee jumping, ziplining, or mountain trekking, and experience the adventurous side of Baybay.

Week-Long Itinerary for Baybay

If you’re planning to visit Baybay for a week, below is a suggested itinerary to help you make the most of your time:

Day Activities
Day 1 Explore the city and visit the Leyte Normal University Museum and Baybay City Museum
Day 2 Hike to Lintaon Peak and enjoy the scenic views
Day 3 Take an island-hopping tour and visit Sambawan Island
Day 4 Visit the historic San Agustin Church and go on a food tour to taste the local cuisine
Day 5 Go on an Adventure Tour and enjoy bungee jumping or ziplining at Agas-Agas Bridge
Day 6 Take a cultural tour and learn about the history and local traditions
Day 7 Relax and unwind on the beach or go on a bike ride through the Kang-ura Rice Field Trail

Food and Drink Scene of Baybay

Baybay offers a unique fusion of Spanish, Chinese, and Filipino cuisine, making it a foodie’s paradise. Below are some must-try dishes and local beverages in Baybay.

Must-Try Dishes

  • Adobong Kangkong - A dish made with water spinach sautéed in vinegar, soy sauce, and garlic.
  • Humba - A stew made with pork belly, vinegar, soy sauce, and sugar.
  • Binagol - A sweet delicacy made with taro root, coconut milk, and sugar.

Local Beverages

  • Tuba - A fermented coconut drink that has a slightly sour and sweet taste.
  • Calamansi Juice - A refreshing drink made from local Philippine limes.

Where to Stay in Baybay: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Baybay offers accommodation options for all budgets. Below are some recommended hotels, hostels, and guesthouses in Baybay.

  • Baybay City Hotel - A modern hotel located in the heart of Baybay.
  • Villas by the Sea - A beachfront resort that offers luxurious villas and serene tropical gardens.
  • Baybay Backpackers - A cozy hostel that offers budget-friendly accommodations for backpackers.

What to Buy in Baybay: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Baybay offers many unique souvenirs and local products that you can bring home as a reminder of your trip. Some of the best souvenirs to buy in Baybay include:

  • Binagol - A sweet delicacy made with taro root, coconut milk, and sugar.
  • T-Design - Locally made T-Shirts with a unique design and print that showcases Baybay’s local culture and heritage.
  • Suman - A Filipino rice cake wrapped in banana leaves.

Local Culture and Customs of Baybay

Baybay is a town that is deeply rooted in its culture and traditions. Below are some customs and local practices that visitors should know before traveling to Baybay.

  • When entering a home or visiting someone, it is customary to take off your shoes.
  • Baybay locals are known for being hospitable and friendly, so it is essential to reciprocate their kindness by being respectful and polite.

Best Time to Visit Baybay

The best time to visit Baybay is during the dry season, which runs from December to May. During this time, the weather is pleasant, and the skies are clear, making it perfect for outdoor activities and adventures.

Do’s and Don’ts of Baybay

Do:

  • Consider visiting during the town’s festival, the Kang-ura Festival, which takes place in October, to experience Baybay’s local culture and traditions.
  • Be respectful of the locals and their customs, including taking off your shoes before entering a home or a place of worship.

Don’t:

  • Don’t litter or harm the environment. Baybay takes pride in its natural beauty, and it is essential to maintain its cleanliness.
  • Don’t disrespect the local customs and traditions, even if they differ from your own.

Safety and Health

Baybay is generally a safe town to visit, but it is essential to take necessary precautions to ensure your safety. Below are some tips to consider when traveling to Baybay.

Safety in Baybay

  • Avoid carrying large amounts of cash or valuable items with you when exploring the town.
  • Make sure to obtain all necessary permits and documents before embarking on any adventure activities or tours.

Health in Baybay

  • Bring mosquito repellent and wear long-sleeved clothing to avoid mosquito bites, which can cause dengue fever.
  • Make sure to drink bottled water to avoid water-borne illnesses.

Getting to Baybay: Transportation Options and Logistics

Baybay is accessible by plane, bus, and car. Below are some transportation options to consider when traveling to Baybay.

Airports Near Baybay

The nearest airport to Baybay is Daniel Z. Romualdez Airport in Tacloban City, which is approximately a two-hour drive away.

Bus Stations Near Baybay

There are several bus companies that operate from Tacloban City to Baybay, including Duptours, Philtranco, and CUL Transport.

Other Options

For those who prefer to drive, there are several car rental companies available in Tacloban City, as well as private car transfers.

How to Get Around in Baybay

Baybay is a small town that is easy to navigate by foot or by tricycle, a popular mode of public transportation in the Philippines. Tricycles are affordable and convenient, making them a popular choice amongst locals and tourists alike.

Where to Go Next from Baybay: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Baybay serves as an excellent base to explore the nearby towns and cities. Below are some nearby destinations and excursions to consider:

  • Tacloban City - The capital of Leyte Island, known for its rich history and culture.
  • Kalanggaman Island - A stunning island that is famous for its long sandbars and crystal-clear waters.
  • Ormoc City - Known for its beautiful beaches and hot springs.

Conclusion

Baybay is a town that offers a perfect balance of natural beauty, history, and culture. Whether you’re an adventurer, a foodie, or a history buff, Baybay has something to offer for everyone. We hope this comprehensive travel guide has provided you with all the necessary information to plan your trip to Baybay, Philippines, and make the most of your visit.
