Introduction to Tacloban

Tacloban is the regional center of the Eastern Visayas region in the Philippines. It is located on the eastern coast of Leyte Island and is often referred to as the Gateway to Eastern Visayas. Tacloban is known for its rich history, scenic landscapes, and vibrant local culture. It is also a popular destination for adventure seekers because of its hiking and biking routes, as well as water activities like snorkeling and diving. In this comprehensive tour guide, we will provide you with everything you need to know about Tacloban, including the top places to visit, things to do, where to stay, and more.

List of Places to Visit in Tacloban

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Santo Niño Shrine and Heritage Museum

Santo Niño Shrine and Heritage Museum is a must-visit for those who want to learn more about the local culture and history of Tacloban. The shrine, which is located inside the Redemptorist Church, houses a collection of artifacts and religious items that date back to the Spanish colonial period. The museum, on the other hand, showcases antique furniture, old photographs, and other memorabilia that provide a glimpse into the city’s rich past.

Leyte Landing Memorial Park

The Leyte Landing Memorial Park is dedicated to General Douglas MacArthur, who landed on the shores of Leyte Island in 1944 during World War II. The park features life-size bronze statues of MacArthur and his companions, as well as a museum that houses various items and memorabilia related to the landing.

San Juanico Bridge

The San Juanico Bridge, which connects the islands of Leyte and Samar, is the longest bridge in the Philippines. It spans 2.16 kilometers and is a popular spot for sightseeing and taking photos.

Natural Attractions of Tacloban: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Kalanggaman Island

Kalanggaman Island is a must-visit for those who love the beach and the outdoors. It is a scenic island with clear turquoise waters and white sand beaches. Visitors can enjoy swimming, snorkeling, and diving, as well as camping overnight.

Sohoton Caves and Natural Bridge

The Sohoton Caves and Natural Bridge are natural wonders that are worth a visit. The caves, which are located in Basey, Samar, feature breathtaking rock formations and underground rivers. The natural bridge, on the other hand, is a massive arch of rock that spans over a river.

Lake Danao

Lake Danao is a serene lake that is located in the mountains of Ormoc, Leyte. It is a popular spot for boating, kayaking, and picnicking.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Tacloban

Tacloban has several hiking and biking routes that offer stunning views of the city and its surroundings. Some of the most popular routes include:

  • Calvary Hill
  • Kanhuraw Hill
  • Palo Alto Falls
  • Basey Hills

Best Things to Do in Tacloban: Activities and Tours

Try Local Delicacies

Tacloban is known for its delicious and unique delicacies. Some of the must-try dishes include:

  • Binagol – a sticky rice cake made with coconut milk and taro
  • Suman – a sweet rice cake wrapped in banana leaves
  • Adobong pusit – squid cooked in soy sauce and vinegar

Island Hopping

Island hopping is a popular activity in Tacloban. Visitors can explore the surrounding islands and beaches, such as Kalanggaman Island, Sambawan Island, and Cuatro Islas.

Waray Culture Tour

The Waray Culture Tour is a guided tour that takes visitors to various cultural and historical sites around Tacloban. It includes visits to the Santo Niño Shrine and Heritage Museum, the Leyte Landing Memorial Park, and other important landmarks.

Here’s a week-long itinerary that covers the best places to visit in Tacloban:

  • Day 1: Visit Santo Niño Shrine and Heritage Museum and Leyte Landing Memorial Park
  • Day 2: Go island hopping to Kalanggaman Island
  • Day 3: Take a Waray Culture Tour
  • Day 4: Visit Sohoton Caves and Natural Bridge

Food and Drink Scene of Tacloban

Must-Try Dishes

Binagol

Binagol is a popular delicacy in Tacloban made of grated taro, coconut milk, condensed milk, nuts, and egg yolks that are cooked in a banana leaf. It has a sweet and sticky flavor with a creamy texture.

Suman

Suman is another popular delicacy made of glutinous rice, coconut milk, and sugar. The mixture is wrapped in banana leaves and steamed. It is usually eaten as a snack or dessert.

Adobong Pusit

Adobong pusit is a savory dish where squid is cooked in soy sauce, vinegar, garlic, and other spices. Sometimes, it is also mixed with vegetables like onions and tomatoes.

Local Beverages

Tubâ

Tubâ is a fermented coconut water and is popular all over the Philippines. The drink is usually consumed during celebrations and parties.

Bahalina

Bahalina is a type of wine made of coconut sap and rice. It is a unique drink that has a slightly sweet taste with a hint of coconut.

What to Buy in Tacloban: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Visitors can find a variety of locally-made handicrafts and souvenir items in Tacloban. Some of the popular items to bring home include:

  • Banig – woven mats made of pandan leaves
  • Binakol – handwoven fabric made of abaca fibers
  • Lutos – woven baskets made of bamboo

Local Culture and Customs of Tacloban

Waray culture is deeply ingrained in the everyday lives of the locals of Tacloban. Visitors are expected to show respect for local customs and traditions, such as taking off shoes when entering homes or eating with your right hand only. The people of Tacloban are friendly and hospitable, so don’t hesitate to strike up a conversation and ask for recommendations.

Best Time to Visit Tacloban

The best time to visit Tacloban is during the dry season, which runs from November to April. The weather is dry and sunny, which is perfect for outdoor activities. The wet season runs from May to October, and typhoons are common during this time.

Do’s and Don’ts of Tacloban

Do:

  • Show respect for local customs and traditions
  • Try local delicacies
  • Wear comfortable shoes and clothing for hiking and biking
  • Bring a waterproof bag for your gadgets and valuables when going island hopping and other water activities

Don’t:

  • Wear revealing clothing when visiting churches or religious sites
  • Litter in public places
  • Disrespect the local culture and customs

Safety and Health

Safety in Tacloban

Tacloban is generally a safe place for visitors, but it is still important to take precautions to ensure your safety. Here are some tips to keep in mind:

  • Avoid going out alone at night in dimly-lit areas
  • Keep your valuables in a secure location
  • Be cautious when riding public transportation, especially at night
  • Be aware of your surroundings

Health in Tacloban

Visitors should take necessary precautions to avoid getting sick while in Tacloban. Here are some tips to keep in mind:

  • Drink only bottled water or purified water
  • Use insect repellent when outdoors to prevent mosquito bites
  • Use sunscreen to protect your skin from the sun

Getting to Tacloban: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Tacloban

Tacloban has an airport, the Daniel Z. Romualdez Airport, which is serviced by several local airlines. The airport is located in Barangay San Jose, which is three kilometers away from the city center.

Bus Stations Near Tacloban

Several bus companies operate in Tacloban, which serves as a hub for transportation in Eastern Visayas. Buses travel to nearby areas around Tacloban, including Ormoc, Samar, and Leyte.

Other Options

Visitors can also take a van, taxi, or private car to get around Tacloban and the surrounding areas.

How to Get Around Tacloban

The easiest way to get around Tacloban is by jeepney or tricycle. Jeepneys are the primary mode of transportation in the city, while tricycles are ideal for short distances. Taxis and cars for rent are also available.

Where to Go Next from Tacloban: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Ormoc

Ormoc is a city in Leyte that is known for its hot springs, waterfalls, and beaches. Visitors can explore attractions such as the Lake Danao National Park and the Tongonan Hot Springs National Park.

Biliran

Biliran is an island province near Tacloban that is a popular spot for water activities like swimming, diving, and snorkeling. Visitors can also visit the Tinago Falls, Ulan-Ulan Falls, and other natural attractions.

Conclusion

Tacloban is a city that is rich in culture and history, with many attractions and activities that will satisfy any traveler. Whether you want to explore historical landmarks, enjoy outdoor adventures, or simply relax on the beach, Tacloban has something for everyone. By following this guide, you will be able to explore the best that Tacloban has to offer and have an unforgettable experience in the Philippines.
