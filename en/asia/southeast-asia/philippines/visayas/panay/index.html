Introduction to Panay

Panay, located in the Visayas region of the Philippines, is known for its stunning beaches, natural attractions, and rich historical and cultural heritage. It is the fourth-largest island in the country, and its central location makes it easily accessible from other major cities such as Manila and Cebu. In this travel guide, we’ll explore the best places to visit, things to do, where to stay, and more in Panay.

List of Places to Visit in Panay

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

One of the top cultural attractions in Panay is the Miagao Church, a UNESCO World Heritage Site that showcases the unique Baroque architecture of the Philippines. Other must-visit historical sites include the San Jose Church in Iloilo City, the Balay ni Tana Dicang (the ancestral home of Graciano Lopez Jaena), and Casa Real in Capiz, which was once the home of Spanish officials during colonial times.

In addition to these historical sites, Panay is also home to several museums that celebrate the region’s rich cultural heritage. The Museo Iloilo houses a collection of archeological artifacts, textiles, and religious items, while the Angel Araneta Museum showcases the life and works of the renowned artist Angel Araneta.

Natural Attractions of Panay: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Panay is blessed with an abundance of natural wonders that include stunning beaches, lush forests, and majestic mountains. One of the top natural attractions in the region is the Gigantes Islands, known for their crystal-clear waters and white sand beaches. Other popular destinations include the Bucari Pine Forest in Leon, which offers breathtaking views of the surrounding hills, and the Malumpati Cold Spring in Pandan, where visitors can swim in refreshing and clear waters.

For those seeking adventure, Panay offers plenty of opportunities for hiking, trekking, and camping. The Mt. Napulak trail in Antique is a favorite among hikers, while the Maasin River in Iloilo provides a thrilling kayaking experience.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Panay

Panay’s natural beauty makes it an ideal destination for hiking and biking enthusiasts. Some popular routes include the Mayana Peak in Guimaras, Mt. Baloy in Aklan, and the Jalaur River Trail in Iloilo. These routes offer stunning views of the surrounding mountains, forests, and rivers.

Best Things to Do in Panay: Activities and Tours

Apart from the natural and cultural attractions, Panay also offers various activities that are sure to make your visit unforgettable. Go on a food tour to sample the region’s delicacies, visit local markets to shop for souvenirs, or take a heritage tour to learn about the history and culture of places like San Joaquin and Miagao.

Food and Drink Scene of Panay

Must-Try Dishes

Panay boasts a vibrant food scene that celebrates its rich culinary heritage. Some must-try dishes include the famous La Paz Batchoy, a noodle soup made with pork, liver, and intestines, and the Kadios Baboy Langka, a stew made with pigeon peas, pork, and jackfruit. Other popular dishes include the Pinamalhan nga Manok, a braised chicken dish, and the Kaldo, a comforting fish soup.

Local Beverages

To beat the heat, try some of the region’s refreshing beverages such as the Calamansi Juice, Buko Juice, and the popular San Miguel beer.

Where to Stay in Panay: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Panay offers a variety of accommodations to suit all budgets and preferences. For those seeking luxury, the Shangri-La’s Boracay Resort and Spa is a top pick, while budget travelers can choose from guesthouses and hostels such as the Mango Tree Lodge in Guimaras and the Injap Tower Hotel in Iloilo City.

What to Buy in Panay: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Panay is known for its unique crafts and delicacies, such as the hand-woven Hablon fabric, the famous Barquillos, and the delicious Biscocho. Other popular souvenirs include the colorful Ati-Atihan masks, the Capiz shell products, and the Guimaras mangoes.

Local Culture and Customs of Panay

The region’s rich and diverse cultural heritage is celebrated through various festivals such as the Dinagyang Festival in Iloilo City, the Ati-Atihan Festival in Aklan, and the Manggahan Festival in Guimaras.

Best Time to Visit Panay

The best time to visit Panay is between November and April when the weather is dry and sunny. However, visitors can also enjoy the region’s many attractions during the off-peak season from May to October.

Do’s and Don’ts of Panay

Do:

  • Respect the local traditions and customs.
  • Carry a valid ID card at all times.
  • Try the local delicacies and experience the food scene.

Don’t:

  • Wear skimpy clothing in public places, especially in temples and mosques.
  • Smoke or drink alcohol in public places.
  • Engage in any illegal activities.

Safety and Health

Safety in Panay

Panay is generally a safe place to visit, but it’s always a good idea to take precautions such as avoiding dark alleys at night and keeping your belongings secure. Visitors should also be aware of the potential dangers of typhoons and earthquakes.

Health in Panay

Visitors to Panay should make sure they are up to date with their immunizations and take precautions such as wearing insect repellent to prevent mosquito-borne illnesses.

Getting to Panay: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Panay

The Iloilo International Airport is the main gateway to Panay, and it is served by several local and international airlines.

Bus Stations Near Panay

The island is accessible by bus from other major cities in the Visayas region such as Bacolod and Cebu.

Other Options

Ferries are available to Panay from nearby islands such as Guimaras and Boracay.

How to Get Around in Panay

Visitors can get around the island by hiring a private car or motorcycle, taking taxis or tricycles, or using public transportation such as buses and jeepneys.

Where to Go Next from Panay: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Visitors to Panay can easily extend their trip to nearby destinations such as Boracay, Guimaras, and Negros Occidental.

Conclusion

Panay is an island paradise that offers a perfect blend of natural beauty, rich culture, and fascinating history. Whether you’re seeking adventure, relaxation, or a cultural experience, Panay has something for every traveler. With this comprehensive guide, you’re ready to explore the best of what the island has to offer.
