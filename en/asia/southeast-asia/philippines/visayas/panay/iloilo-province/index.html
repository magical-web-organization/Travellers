Introduction to Iloilo (Province)

Iloilo is a province in the Western Visayas region of the Philippines. It is situated on the southeastern section of Panay Island and is known for its rich history, vibrant festivals, and delectable cuisine. During the Spanish colonial era, Iloilo was one of the most important commercial centers in the country due to its strategic location. Today, it has transformed into a hub of economic growth and tourism, attracting both local and international travelers.

List of Places to Visit in Iloilo (Province)

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  1. Museo Iloilo - showcases the culture and heritage of Iloilo through its extensive collection of artifacts and exhibits.
  2. Jaro Cathedral - one of the oldest and most notable landmarks in Iloilo, known for its grand Baroque architecture.
  3. Miag-ao Church - designated as a UNESCO World Heritage site, this famous church is a perfect example of intricate Baroque architecture.
  4. Molo Church - also known as the St. Anne Parish Church, it is a stunning example of Gothic Renaissance architecture.
  5. Fort San Pedro - a Spanish fortress built in 1607, it is considered as a significant historical landmark in Iloilo.

Natural Attractions of Iloilo (Province): Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  1. Guimaras Island - located just a short boat ride from the city, this island is renowned for its pristine beaches and sweet mangoes.
  2. Gigantes Islands - a group of stunning, unspoiled islands with crystal clear waters, white sandy beaches, and unique rock formations.
  3. Madge Lake - a peaceful and serene lake located in Lambunao, known for its scenic views and cool breeze.
  4. Mt. Napulak - a popular destination for hikers and mountaineers, with a captivating 360-degree view from the summit.
  5. Tigbauan Beach - a beautiful stretch of sandy beach with calm waters, perfect for swimming and relaxation.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Iloilo (Province)

  1. Mt. Napulak Hiking Trail - offers a challenging but rewarding hike to the summit, where you’ll have scenic views of the surrounding landscape.
  2. Bucari Pine Forest Mountain Resort - an ideal location for mountain biking and hiking, with winding trails that take you through verdant forests and pristine waterfalls.
  3. Maasin River Trek - a unique hiking experience where you’ll get to experience the beauty of Maasin River while trekking through it.
  4. Bucari Camping Site - a perfect spot for outdoor enthusiasts and adventurers, with numerous trails to hike and scenic picnic areas to enjoy.
  5. Mt. Kanlaon Trekking - a challenging hike that takes you to the summit of Mt. Kanlaon, the highest peak in the Visayas.

Best Things to Do in Iloilo (Province): Activities and Tours

  1. Dinagyang Festival - one of the biggest and most colorful festivals in the Philippines, featuring a grand street parade and lively cultural performances.
  2. Food Tour - sample Iloilo’s diverse cuisines, including their famous La Paz Batchoy, seafood delicacies, and sweet desserts.
  3. Iloilo River Cruise - a relaxing way to explore the scenic Iloilo River while indulging in delicious local food and drinks.
  4. Sunset Watching - witness the breathtaking sunset views at different locations around the province, including the Tigbauan Church and Miag-ao Church.
  5. City Tour - explore Iloilo’s rich history and culture by visiting significant landmarks such as Molo Church, Jaro Cathedral, and Plaza Libertad.

Food and Drink Scene of Iloilo (Province)

Must-Try Dishes

  1. La Paz Batchoy - a hearty noodle soup made with pork organs, chicken stock, and pork cracklings, an Ilonggo staple and delectable comfort food.
  2. Fresh Seafood - Iloilo is blessed with an abundant supply of fresh seafood, which can be enjoyed in various ways such as grilled, fried, or cooked in soup.
  3. Kansi - a sour and spicy beef soup that’s perfect for chilly days, made with pure beef broth and mixed with batwan fruit and lemongrass.
  4. Pancit Molo - a savory dumpling soup made with pork and chicken filling, wrapped in a thin molo wrapper and served in a chicken or pork broth.
  5. Sinamak - a spicy vinegar that’s a perfect condiment for most Ilonggo dishes and is made with crushed garlic, chili peppers, and a dash of salt.

Local Beverages

  1. Tuba - a locally brewed alcoholic beverage made from coconut sap, with a sweet and slightly sour taste.
  2. Barako Coffee - a robust, full-bodied coffee that’s famous among Ilonggos and is made from Liberica beans.
  3. Mango Shake - with Guimaras Island being the mango capital of the Philippines, it’s no surprise that a refreshing mango shake is a must-try beverage in Iloilo.
  4. Buko Juice - a popular drink made from fresh coconut water, served chilled and perfect for hot and humid days.
  5. Craft Beers - with the growing craft beer scene in Iloilo, you can choose from a variety of locally brewed and imported beers, such as Blacksmith and Katipunan.

Where to Stay in Iloilo (Province): Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  1. Richmonde Hotel Iloilo - a luxurious hotel located in Iloilo Business Park, offering comfortable rooms and state-of-the-art amenities.
  2. Circle Inn Iloilo - a budget-friendly hotel located in the heart of the city, with spacious and clean rooms.
  3. ‘Lil Jayne’s Bed & Breakfast - a charming guesthouse located in La Paz, which serves a lovely breakfast and provides a homely atmosphere.
  4. Injap Tower Hotel - a modern, high-rise hotel located along the Diversion Road, offering panoramic views of the city skyline.
  5. Dragon Lodge - a cozy hostel located in the city center, with dormitory and private rooms available.

What to Buy in Iloilo (Province): Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  1. Barquillos - a light, crispy, and sweet snack, perfect for gifting or as a pasalubong.
  2. Pina Cloth - a delicate and intricate fabric made from pineapple fibers, handwoven by local artisans.
  3. Biscocho - a crispy, buttery bread typically served with coffee or as a snack, best enjoyed at the Iloilo Central Market.
  4. Antique Ceramics - beautiful, handcrafted ceramics made from antique materials and traditional techniques available in the Molo Mansion.
  5. Handwoven Bags - intricately woven bags and baskets made by local weavers from various regions of Iloilo, available in various sizes and designs.

Local Culture and Customs of Iloilo (Province)

Ilonggos are known for their warmth and hospitality, which reflects their strong sense of community and family. Modesty and respect for elders are essential cultural values for Ilonggos. Religion plays a significant role in the daily lives of most Ilonggos, with Catholicism being the predominant religion.

Best Time to Visit Iloilo (Province)

The best time to visit Iloilo is during the dry season from November to April, where weather conditions are favorable and rain is minimal. The most visited time of the year is during the Dinagyang Festival in January, where the streets come alive with colorful parades and cultural events.

Do’s and Don’ts of Iloilo (Province)

Do:

  • Dress modestly, especially when visiting churches and other religious sites.
  • Bring a refillable water bottle to reduce plastic waste and keep yourself hydrated.
  • Show respect for the local culture and customs.
  • Try the local food and drinks.
  • Learn a few Ilonggo phrases to better communicate with the locals.

Don’t:

  • Litter in public places.
  • Swim in prohibited areas.
  • Engage in noisy or disruptive behavior in residential areas.
  • Disrespect the local customs and beliefs.
  • Take advantage of locals or their hospitality.

Safety and Health

Safety in Iloilo (Province)

Iloilo is generally a safe place for travelers, with a low crime rate. Petty theft and pickpocketing may occur in crowded areas, so it’s essential to keep valuables and belongings secure. It’s best to avoid walking alone at night and be aware of surroundings.

Health in Iloilo (Province)

It’s best to bring medication and necessary medical supplies when traveling to Iloilo. It’s also advisable to be updated with the necessary vaccinations when traveling to the Philippines. Drinking bottled water and eating from reputable food establishments can help prevent food poisoning and other related illnesses.

Getting to Iloilo (Province): Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Iloilo (Province)

Iloilo International Airport is the main gateway to the province, with daily direct flights from Manila, Cebu, and other local airports.

Bus Stations Near Iloilo (Province)

Buses from other parts of the island and nearby provinces such as Antique and Capiz have routes that pass through Iloilo City.

Other Options

Ferry services are also available from other islands such as Negros and Cebu.

How to Get Around in Iloilo (Province)

Several transportation options are available around Iloilo, including jeepneys, buses, tricycles, and taxis. It’s best to negotiate fares beforehand and ensure to check if the vehicles are in good condition and registered with the local transport authorities.

Where to Go Next from Iloilo (Province): Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Iloilo is an excellent base for exploring nearby areas, such as:

  1. Bacolod City - also known as the “City of Smiles,” it’s famous for its sweet delicacies and vibrant MassKara Festival.
  2. Antique Province - known for its beautiful natural attractions and unspoiled beaches, such as the Malalison Island.
  3. Capiz Province - known as the “Seafood Capital of the Philippines,” it’s famous for its fresh seafood and Capiz shell products.
  4. Guimaras Island - famous for its sweet mangoes and pristine beaches, perfect for relaxation and island-hopping.

Conclusion

Iloilo (province) is indeed a beautiful destination that offers a lot for every type of traveler, from natural wonders and outdoor adventures to unique cultural experiences and delectable local cuisine. With this comprehensive tour guide, visitors can make the most out of their stay in this vibrant and captivating province.
