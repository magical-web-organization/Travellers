Introduction to Numancia

Situated in the heart of Aklan, in Panay island of Visayas, Numancia is a town full of hidden gems. Blessed with natural wonders and a rich cultural heritage, it remains one of the hidden treasures of the Philippines. From exciting outdoor adventures to fascinating historical landmarks, Numancia has something to offer everyone.

List of Places to Visit in Numancia

Here’s a list of places you can visit in Numancia:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  1. Numancia Church: This old church is the center of the town’s religious life and serves as a precious cultural landmark. Made from coral stone and lime, it was established in 1881 and is worth seeing for its old-world charm.
  2. Numancia Ancestral Houses: These are preserved old houses, each telling the story of Numancia’s history and heritage. A visit to these ancestral homes provides visitors with a glimpse of the town’s traditional way of life.

Natural Attractions of Numancia: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  1. Agta Beach: A pristine beach, Agta, is the perfect place to relax and unwind. With its crystal clear waters, it offers a peaceful escape from the hustle and bustle of the city.
  2. Mount Nangtud: A popular hiking spot, Mt. Nangtud offers incredible panoramic views of Numancia and the surrounding mountains. It is an intermediate climb, and the biodiversity of the mountain will amaze all nature lovers.
  3. Nangtud Eco-Tourism Park: This park is accessible through Mt. Nangtud, and offers incredible tours that showcase Numancia’s biodiversity and local culture.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Numancia

If you are an outdoor enthusiast, then Numancia is the place for you. Here are some of the best hiking and biking routes to explore:

  1. Mt. Nangtud: A popular hiking spot, Mt. Nangtud has numerous trails that lead to the peak.
  2. Bagong-Bayan Trail: This trail leads to the beautiful Bagong Bayan waterfall. It is perfect for trekking and is a great place to have a picnic.
  3. Kalibo Numancia Route: This route is suitable for both hiking and biking, and it connects Kalibo, the capital of Aklan, to Numancia.

Best Things to Do in Numancia: Activities and Tours

Here are some of the most exciting activities and tours to indulge in:

  1. Ecotourism tours: Numancia offers some fantastic tours that showcase the town’s biodiversity and cultural heritage. Explore the mountains on foot or by bike, or embark on a scenic river trip.
  2. Beach hopping: Numancia is packed with beautiful beaches. Visit Agta Beach, Tuburan Beach, and Balete Beach for sun, sand, and sea.
  3. Foodie adventures: Discover the local cuisine of Numancia by trying their delicacies such as binakoe, suman, and the iconic Aklan Latik.

Food and Drink Scene of Numancia

Numancia, like the rest of the Philippines, has a wide range of cuisine. Here are some must-try dishes:

Must-Try Dishes

  1. Binakoe: This is a native Aklanon delicacy, and it’s a must-try in Numancia.
  2. Suman: This is a traditional Filipino rice cake that’s perfect for snacking.
  3. Lechon: This crispy Filipino roasted pig is a staple at any Filipino gathering.

Local Beverages

  1. Tuba: This is a locally made wine from coconut trees, and it’s a must-try.
  2. Calamansi Juice: This refreshing drink is made from the local calamansi, a sour fruit that is native to the Philippines.

Where to Stay in Numancia: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Numancia has several accommodation options ranging from hotels to guesthouses:

  1. Birhen Pension House - A budget-friendly guesthouse
  2. Kalibo Hotel - A mid-range hotel with clean and comfortable rooms
  3. Agta Beach Resort - A beachfront resort that offers stunning views and top-notch amenities.

What to Buy in Numancia: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Numancia is known for producing items made from nipa palm leaves. Here’s what you should buy as souvenirs:

  1. Nipa Hats: These are beautiful hats made from nipa palm leaves that protect you from the sun while keeping you cool.
  2. Baskets: These are made from the nipa palm tree’s leaves and are suitable for use as home decor or storage.

Local Culture and Customs of Numancia

The people of Numancia are known for their hospitality. They have a rich cultural heritage, and their traditional customs and festivals are worth experiencing. The town holds the Bungan Bunga Festival annually, which is celebrated to honor their patron saint.

Best Time to Visit Numancia

The best time to visit Numancia is during the dry season, which runs from November to May. This period offers clear skies for outdoor activities. The wet season lasts from June to October, so if you’re planning on visiting during this time, be prepared for sudden rains.

Do’s and Don’ts of Numancia

Do:

  1. Respect the local customs and traditions.
  2. Try the local delicacies such as binakoe and suman.
  3. Keep the beaches clean and free of garbage.

Don’t:

  1. Disrespect the locals or their way of life.
  2. Litter around the town or on the beaches.
  3. Wear inappropriate clothing when visiting historical landmarks.

Safety and Health

Safety in Numancia

Numancia is generally a safe town, but it is always a good idea to exercise caution. Avoid traveling alone at night and keep your belongings close to you in public places.

Health in Numancia

Make sure to bring mosquito repellent and sunscreen with you, as mosquitoes are prevalent in the area, and the sun can be intense. Also, drink plenty of water to stay hydrated, and avoid drinking tap water.

Getting to Numancia: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Numancia

The closest airport to Numancia is Kalibo International Airport, located around 10 kilometers away. From there, you can take a taxi or a private car to Numancia.

Bus Stations Near Numancia

Ceres Bus Line operates the buses that go to Aklan Province from Manila, and there are several buses daily. From the bus station, you can hire a taxi or tricycle to get to Numancia.

Other Options

You can also hire a private car to get to Numancia. This option provides more flexibility, and it is relatively safer.

How to Get Around in Numancia

The tricycle is the preferred means of transport in Numancia. It is cheap and efficient, and the tricycles can take you anywhere in the town quickly.

Where to Go Next from Numancia: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

If you’re looking to continue your adventure, here are some nearby destinations worth visiting:

  1. Boracay Island: This famous island is located south of Aklan and is regarded as one of the top beach destinations in the world.
  2. Kalibo: The capital of Aklan, Kalibo, is an exciting town that has a lot of cultural and historical landmarks.
  3. Caticlan: Located an hour away from Numancia, Caticlan is a town located at the northern tip of Panay Island.

Conclusion

Numancia is a town full of historic landmarks, natural wonders, and a vibrant culture worth experiencing. From hiking in the mountains to relaxing on the pristine beaches, there is something for everyone in Numancia. Book your trip and experience the delights of this hidden gem in the Philippines.
