Introduction to New Washington

Located in the province of Aklan, in the Panay Island region of the Philippines, New Washington is a small and charming town that offers a glimpse into the country’s rich culture and natural beauty. With a population of about 48,000 people, the town is known for its bustling markets, historical landmarks, and outdoor adventures.

In this comprehensive guide, we will explore the best places to visit, things to do, and where to stay and eat in New Washington. We’ll also offer some helpful travel tips, and a week-long itinerary to help you plan your trip.

List of Places to Visit in New Washington

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Bakhawan Ecopark - a 220-hectare mangrove sanctuary and ecotourism park where you can go on a relaxing stroll along wooden boardwalks, see various species of birds and marine life, and enjoy a refreshing swimming experience.
  • Aklan Freedom Shrine - a monument dedicated to the Aklanons who fought for independence against the Spanish and Japanese colonial periods, and is located in the town’s plaza.
  • Museo It Akean - a museum showcasing the culture, history, and traditions of Aklan and is located in the town’s center.

Natural Attractions of New Washington: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Buruanga Beach - a stunning white-sand beach ideal for sunbathing, swimming, and sea kayaking.
  • Bugang River - a river with crystal-clear waters where you can go swimming and river-tubing.
  • Himbangon Cave - an enormous limestone cave worth exploring and situated near the Bakhawan Eco-park.

Hiking/Biking Routes of New Washington

  • Aklan River Trail - a 7-kilometer cycling and walking trail that runs along the Aklan River, which passes through rice fields and a mangrove forest where you can spot local flora and fauna.
  • Mt. Madjaas - a tall mountain peak that stands over 2,114 meters tall and is considered one of the hardest mountain climbs in the Philippines.

Best Things to Do in New Washington: Activities and Tours

  • Island Hopping - explore Boracay Island with its beautiful beaches and go snorkeling.
  • Cooking Classes - learn how to cook traditional local dishes such as kinilaw, ahi maa, and pinaupong manok.
  • Fishing - go on a local fishing boat and catch your own fish for dinner.

Week-Long Itinerary for New Washington

  • Day 1: Visit Bakhawan Ecopark
  • Day 2: Explore Aklan Freedom Shrine and the Town Plaza
  • Day 3: Take the Aklan River Trail and visit Burianga Beach
  • Day 4: Visit Bugang River and go river-tubing
  • Day 5: Go island-hopping in Boracay and go snorkeling
  • Day 6: Take a cooking class and have dinner at a local home
  • Day 7: Go fishing and have a barbeque on the beach

Food and Drink Scene of New Washington

Must-Try Dishes

  • Kinilaw - a sour and savory seafood dish made of raw fish, onions, ginger, vinegar, and chili peppers.
  • Ahi Maa - a chicken dish with tomato and pepper-based sauce which is cooked and served in a bamboo tube.
  • Pinaupong Manok - a chicken dish cooked and then steamed inside a bamboo tube with various spices.

Local Beverages

  • Tuba - a local drink made from coconut wine
  • Buko Juice - coconut juice

Where to Stay in New Washington: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

There are several places to stay in New Washington, including hotels, hostels, and guesthouses. Here are a few recommendations:

  • Boracay Ocean Club Beach Resort - located in Boracay Island, it offers rooms that overlook the sea.
  • Fairways and Bluewater Resort - located in Aklan, it offers rooms that have spectacular views of the ocean.
  • Bambua Nature Cottages - located in the center of New Washington, it provides comfortable and eco-friendly cottages for an authentic local experience.

What to Buy in New Washington: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Barter Items - colorful and intricately woven fabrics which are perfect for casual or beachwear.
  • Woven Handicrafts - beautiful crafted bags, holders, mats.

Local Culture and Customs of New Washington

The locals of New Washington are hospitable and friendly. Respect their culture, traditions, and way-of-life. Take off your shoes when entering a home, and if invited, join in on local celebrations and festivals!

Best Time to Visit New Washington

The best time to visit New Washington is during the dry months of December to May. The temperature ranges between 26-32°C and provides perfect weather for outdoor exploration. The Wet Monsoon season runs from June to November.

Do’s and Don’ts of New Washington

Do:

  • Bring sunscreen, mosquito repellent, and a hat.
  • Respect the local culture, norms, traditions, and wear appropriate clothing when entering a place of worship.
  • Try local dishes and engage in cultural activities.

Don’t:

  • Litter anywhere, especially in eco-parks and other natural wonders.
  • Disrespect the locals, their property, and privacy.

Safety and Health

Safety in New Washington

New Washington is generally a safe town to explore, but it’s still important to take the necessary precautions. Here are a few safety tips:

  • Don’t walk around alone late at night
  • Don’t leave your belongings unattended while at the beach.
  • Be vigilant when swimming or engaging in outdoor activities.

Health in New Washington

Make sure to get vaccinated for common illnesses like measles, polio, and typhoid before travelling. Carry mosquito repellent, and avoid drinking tap water. Seek medical advice if you feel sick.

Getting to New Washington: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near New Washington

The nearest international airport to New Washington is Godofredo P. Ramos Airport, which is 13.6 kilometers from the town center.

Bus Stations Near New Washington

Buses from Manila stop at Kalibo Bus Terminal, which is approximately 10 kilometers from New Washington town center. Alternatively, shuttles, taxis, and private cars are available for hire and will get you to New Washington in less than an hour.

Other Options

Ferries connect to Caticlan Ferry Terminal via Boracay Island through from major local destinations such as Cebu City, Iloilo City and Manila.

How to Get Around in New Washington

Tricycles or habal-habal motorbikes are the most common modes of transport in New Washington. They’re affordable and have a fixed fare system that ensures you won’t be overcharged. Another option is renting a bicycle or scooter to explore the town’s beautiful scenery by yourself.

Where to Go Next from New Washington: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Boracay Island - a world-famous destination known for its stunning beaches and nightlife.
  • Kalibo - the capital city of Aklan, known for its annual Ati-Atihan Festival, which celebrates the Sto. Niño (Holy Child).
  • Iloilo City - nicknamed the “Queen City of the South,” it’s known for its rich history and culture, as well as its food and shopping scene.

Conclusion

New Washington offers a unique travel experience for those who want to explore a quieter, less touristy place in the Philippines. With its natural landscapes, rich history, and warm locals, it’s an ideal destination for a cultural and outdoor adventure getaway. Plan your visit today and immerse yourself in the beauty of this charming town in the Panay region.
