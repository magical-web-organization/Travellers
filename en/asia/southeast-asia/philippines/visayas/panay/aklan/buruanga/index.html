Introduction to Buruanga

Nestled in the province of Aklan in Panay Island, Buruanga is a town that offers a mix of cultural and natural attractions. This town may not be as popular as other tourist destinations in the Philippines, but it surely has its charm that travelers can appreciate. From its stunning beaches, crystal-clear waters, and lush greenery to its rich history and culture, Buruanga has something to offer to every type of traveler.

List of Places to Visit in Buruanga

Here is a list of the top places to visit in Buruanga:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Buruanga Community Museum - provides a glimpse of the life and culture of the town’s locals. The museum showcases various antique and historical items such as old photographs, traditional costumes, and farming tools.

  • St. Augustine Parish Church - a historical church with a baroque-inspired architecture. This church is the center of the town’s religious practices and cultural activities.

Natural Attractions of Buruanga: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Buruanga Beaches - Buruanga boasts stunning beaches such as Nagubat, Malandog, and Balinghai. These beaches offer a breathtaking view of the ocean, powdery white sand, and clear waters perfect for swimming and snorkeling.

  • Afga Point - located in the western part of Buruanga; it offers an awe-inspiring view of the ocean and sunset.

  • Nabas Wind Farm - offers a magnificent view of wind turbines set amidst rice paddies, providing sustainable energy to the town.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Buruanga

Buruanga has a lot to offer for outdoor enthusiasts, with the following hiking/biking routes:

  • Buruanga Landmark Hill Trek - a scenic trekking route that showcases lush greenery and offers a panoramic view of the Buruanga bay.

  • Buruanga Loop Plaza to Li-O River Trail - a bike trail that offers a scenic view of the ocean and passes through rice paddies, river crossings, and local houses.

Best Things to Do in Buruanga: Activities and Tours

  • Island hopping - rent a boat from Buruanga port and explore the stunning islands surrounding the town such as Maniguin Island and Hambil Island.

  • Snorkeling - Buruanga’s beaches offer clear waters that are perfect for snorkeling and experiencing the marine life.

  • Fishing - rent a small boat and spend an afternoon fishing along Buruanga’s coast.

Food and Drink Scene of Buruanga

Buruanga offers a unique culinary experience with its local dishes and beverages.

Must-Try Dishes

  • Kare-Kareng Kambing - a classic Filipino dish made from goat meat cooked in a savory peanut sauce.

  • Adobong Tambiling - a local Buruangan dish made from mackerel fish cooked in vinegar and soy sauce with spices.

Local Beverages

  • Tuba - a traditional Filipino drink made from sap extracted from coconut palms.

  • Lambanog - a liquor made out of coconut sap.

Where to Stay in Buruanga: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Buruanga offers accommodations that cater to different types of travelers, from budget-friendly hostels to luxurious resorts.

  • Mañana Inn - located in the heart of Buruanga town, offers affordable and comfortable rooms.

  • Villa Caipirinha - a luxurious resort that offers a stunning view of the ocean.

What to Buy in Buruanga: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

When visiting Buruanga, visitors may want to consider buying these souvenirs as a keepsake:

  • Barquillos - a thin rolled wafer often found in boxes and ideal for gift-giving.

  • Malong - a tube-like cloth worn by the locals, which can be used as a beach towel, skirt, or tablecloth.

Local Culture and Customs of Buruanga

Buruanga’s local culture is influenced by its Tagalog and Ati communities. Visitors should adhere to the following customs:

  • Show respect to elders and locals.

  • Seek permission before taking pictures of locals or cultural sites.

  • Dress appropriately when visiting churches or religious sites.

Best Time to Visit Buruanga

The best time to visit Buruanga is from November to April, when the weather is dry and sunny. Buruanga can be experienced throughout the year, but it’s crucial to avoid visiting from June to November, as this is typically typhoon season in the Philippines.

Do’s and Don’ts of Buruanga

Do:

  • Respect the locals and their customs.

  • Try the local food and drinks.

  • Bring appropriate clothing and gear, especially when going island hopping, beach hopping, or trekking.

Don’t:

  • Litter on the beaches or in public areas.

  • Disrespect cultural and religious sites.

  • Wear revealing clothing when visiting religious sites.

Safety and Health

Safety in Buruanga

While Buruanga is considered a safe town, visitors should take extra precautions, especially when exploring the beaches and hiking trails. Visitors should also be aware of pickpockets and scams targeting tourists.

Health in Buruanga

Visitors should ensure they have updated vaccines, especially tetanus and Hepatitis A. It is also crucial to bring insect repellent due to the presence of mosquitoes and other insects.

Getting to Buruanga: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Buruanga

  • Godofredo P. Ramos Airport (also known as Caticlan Airport) - the airport closest to Buruanga, approximately 30-45 minutes by land travel.

  • Kalibo International Airport - located 90 minutes by land travel to Buruanga.

Bus Stations Near Buruanga

  • Ceres Bus Liner - operates daily trips to and from other nearby towns and cities like Kalibo and Caticlan.

Other Options

  • Van rental - travelers may arrange for a van rental or private car for more comfortable and convenient travel.

  • Tricycle - a popular mode of transportation within the town where fares are affordable and convenient for short distances.

How to Get Around in Buruanga

Tricycles are the most common mode of transportation in the town. Visitors can also rent motorbikes and bicycles for more adventurous explorations.

Where to Go Next from Buruanga: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Here are some nearby destinations and excursions to go to after Buruanga:

  • Boracay - The world-renowned white sand beaches of Boracay Island are just a boat ride away from Buruanga.

  • Aklan Nature Park - located in Kalibo is a wildlife sanctuary where visitors can see various flora and fauna.

Conclusion

Buruanga may not be as well-known as other tourist destinations in the Philippines, but it has a lot to offer for travelers looking for a unique adventure. From its stunning beaches, breathtaking views, rich culture, and friendly locals, Buruanga is a place worth exploring. Plan your trip and experience the beauty of this charming town in Aklan, Philippines.
