Introduction to Altavas

Altavas is a hidden gem located in the northern part of Aklan, Philippines. This small town boasts rich cultural heritage, scenic natural attractions, and delicious local cuisines.

List of Places to Visit in Altavas

If you’re planning a trip to Altavas, make sure to visit the following places:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • St. Michael Archangel Parish Church - The oldest Catholic Church in Altavas built in 1794.
  • Altavas Municipal Museum - A museum that features artifacts, collections, and exhibits showcasing the rich history and culture of Aklan.
  • Libertad Watchtower - A well-preserved watchtower that was used as a fortification during the Spanish colonial era.

Natural Attractions of Altavas: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Basang Cave - A popular spot for adventure seekers and nature lovers. It features a freshwater pool and stunning rock formations.
  • Alfonso XIII Waterfalls - A serene waterfalls situated in a lush forest setting.
  • Kawa-Kawa Hill - A scenic hill that offers a panoramic view of the town and surrounding areas.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Altavas

Altavas is a great place for outdoor enthusiasts. The following are some of the best hiking and biking routes to explore:

  • Mount Nausok - This mountain offers a challenging hiking adventure with a stunning view of the town and its surroundings from the viewpoint.
  • Panay Loop - A scenic bicycle trail that loops around the island of Panay.
  • Kalibo-Antique Road - A challenging cycling route that passes through scenic rice fields and small villages.

Best Things to Do in Altavas: Activities and Tours

From cultural immersions to outdoor adventures, Altavas has something for everyone. Here are some of the best activities and tours to experience:

  • Aklan Ati-Atihan Festival - A major festival in Aklan that celebrates the arrival of Halaan, patron saint of the town.
  • Kawa Hot Bath - A unique outdoor spa experience that involves soaking in a kawa (cauldron) heated by firewood.
  • Island Hopping - Explore the nearby islands and experience the beautiful beaches, clear waters, and marine life of Panay.

Week-Long Itinerary for Altavas

Here’s a week-long itinerary to make the most of your trip to Altavas:

Day 1: Arrival and check-in at one of the town’s accommodations

Day 2-3: Cultural immersion with visits to St. Michael Archangel Parish Church, Altavas Municipal Museum, and other historical sites.

Day 4-5: Outdoor adventures with hiking and biking routes and visits to Basang Cave, Alfonso XIII Waterfalls, and Kawa-Kawa Hill.

Day 6: Relaxing day with a visit to the nearby beach and island hopping tours.

Day 7: Departure

Food and Drink Scene of Altavas

Altavas is a foodie’s paradise. Here’s a list of must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Linapay - A dish made from sea cucumber, coconut milk, and vegetables.
  • Tinuom - A traditional Aklan soup made with chicken, lemongrass, and vegetables.
  • Caldo - A local favorite soup made with beef, onions, ginger, and garlic.

Local Beverages

  • Lambanog - A popular coconut liquor in the Philippines.
  • Bugnay Wine - A tart and flavorful wine made from the local bugnay fruit.
  • Buko Juice - Fresh coconut juice served in its shell.

Where to Stay in Altavas: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Altavas offers a range of accommodation options for all types of travelers. Here are some of the best places to stay in Altavas:

  • Ma. Rebecca Village Inn - A budget-friendly hotel located in the heart of Altavas.
  • La Trinidad Pension House - A guesthouse that offers basic amenities and comfortable rooms.
  • Lezo Inn - A mid-range hotel that offers modern amenities and excellent service.

What to Buy in Altavas: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Take a piece of Altavas with you by bringing home some souvenirs and local products. Here are some great options:

  • Capiz Products - Handcrafted capiz shell products including lamps, chandeliers, and ornaments.
  • Ati-Atihan Souvenirs - Traditional costumes, masks, and other souvenirs from the Ati-Atihan festival.
  • Local Delicacies - Bring home some piaya, butterscotch, and other local sweets.

Local Culture and Customs of Altavas

Altavas is a small town that highly values its cultural heritage. It is customary for locals to welcome guests with warm hospitality and share stories about the town’s history and culture.

Best Time to Visit Altavas

The best time to visit Altavas is during the summer season (March to June), when the weather is warm and dry. It is also ideal to plan your trip during the Aklan Ati-Atihan Festival held every January.

Do’s and Don’ts of Altavas

Here are some do’s and don’ts to keep in mind during your trip to Altavas:

Do:

  • Respect cultural practices and traditions.
  • Try the local cuisine and interact with locals.
  • Protect the environment and adhere to responsible tourism practices.

Don’t:

  • Engage in activities that harm the environment or local wildlife.
  • Disrespect cultural practices or traditions.
  • Be insensitive or disrespectful to locals.

Safety and Health

Safety in Altavas

Altavas is a safe town, with low crime rates. However, travelers are advised to take precautionary measures such as keeping their valuables secure and being mindful of their surroundings.

Health in Altavas

Altavas has a health center that provides basic medical services. It is recommended that travelers bring their own prescribed medication and travel insurance.

Getting to Altavas: Transportation Options and Logistics

Traveling to Altavas is easy through various transportation options. Here’s how to get there:

Airports Near Altavas

  • Kalibo International Airport - The nearest international airport, located approximately 40 kilometers from Altavas.
  • Caticlan Airport - A local airport that caters to domestic flights only.

Bus Stations Near Altavas

  • Ceres Bus Terminal - This bus terminal in Kalibo has several trips daily to Altavas.
  • Bus Terminal in Aklan - Buses from Iloilo and other neighboring provinces have routes that pass through Altavas.

Other Options

  • Private Car Rental - Several car rental companies offer self-drive cars or chauffeured service to Altavas.

How to Get Around in Altavas

The most common mode of transportation in Altavas is by tricycle or jeepney. Taxis are also available. Bicycles are available for rent in some accommodations and travel agencies.

Where to Go Next from Altavas: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Altavas is located near popular tourist destinations in Aklan such as Boracay and Kalibo. It is also a gateway to other nearby provinces such as Antique and Iloilo.

Conclusion

Altavas is a hidden gem that offers a rich cultural heritage, scenic natural attractions, and delicious local cuisines. Plan your trip and experience the best of what this small town has to offer.
