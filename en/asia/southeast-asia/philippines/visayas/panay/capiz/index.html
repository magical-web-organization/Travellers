Introduction to Capiz

Capiz is a province located in the Western Visayas region of the Philippines. It is known as the “Seafood Capital of the Philippines” because of its abundance of seafood. Capiz is famous for its white-sand beaches, historical landmarks, and rich cultural heritage. The province is also home to the agile tarsier, one of the world’s smallest primates.

List of Places to Visit in Capiz

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Capiz is a treasure trove of historical landmarks and cultural sites. History buffs will enjoy exploring the following attractions:

  • Panublion Museum - Located in Roxas City, this museum showcases the rich cultural history of Capiz.
  • The Church of Pan-ay - This Baroque-style church is one of the oldest and largest churches in the Philippines. It was built in the 18th century.
  • Santa Monica Church - Known for its ornate stained glass windows, this church in Pan-ay is an important religious site in the province.
  • President Manuel A. Roxas Ancestral House - This heritage house in Roxas City was home to the first President of the Philippines after World War II.

Natural Attractions of Capiz: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Outdoor enthusiasts will love the abundance of natural attractions in Capiz. From beaches to waterfalls, there’s something for everyone:

  • Baybay Beach - A pristine white-sand beach located in Roxas City.
  • Mantalip Falls - A picturesque waterfall located in the town of Jamindan.
  • Mount Tapulao - An outdoor enthusiast’s dream, this mountain offers hiking and biking trails with stunning panoramic views of the province.
  • Olotayan Island - A secluded island paradise with powdery white sand beaches and crystal-clear waters.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Capiz

The rugged terrain of Capiz makes it a popular destination for hiking and biking enthusiasts. Some of the most popular trails include:

  • Mount Nangtud - A challenging hike that rewards the adventurous with breathtaking views of the surrounding countryside.
  • Junction to Amlan Falls - This bike trail takes you through lush forests and past tranquil waterfalls, including the beautiful Amlan Falls.
  • Olotayan Island Circuit - A scenic, low-impact bike ride around the perimeter of the island.

Best Things to Do in Capiz: Activities and Tours

Besides outdoor activities, Capiz offers a wide range of activities and tours that visitors can enjoy. Some of the best things to do in Capiz include:

  • Island Hopping Tour - Explore the surrounding islands of Capiz by taking a boat tour.
  • Seafood Feast - Indulge in the freshest seafood dishes at one of Capiz’s many seafood restaurants.
  • Fishing Tour - Join a local fisherman for a day of fishing and experience the traditional way of life for the rural residents of Capiz.

Week-Long Itinerary for Capiz

For those planning a longer visit, here’s a suggested seven-day itinerary to maximize your time in Capiz:

  • Day 1-3: Explore the rich cultural heritage of Roxas City by visiting the museums and historical landmarks.
  • Day 4: Take a day trip to Olotayan Island for a relaxing day on the beach.
  • Day 5: Hike Mount Nangtud for stunning views of the countryside.
  • Day 6: Explore the Bacolod City in Negros Occidental.
  • Day 7: End your trip with a seafood feast before departing for your next destination.

Food and Drink Scene of Capiz

Must-Try Dishes

Capiz is a haven for seafood lovers, so don’t miss trying out the following dishes:

  • Sinugba - Grilled fish wrapped in banana leaves.
  • Adobong Manok sa Ubad - Chicken stew with ginger and ubod (coconut palm heart).
  • KBL (Kadyos, Baboy, Langka) - Stewed pork, black beans, and jackfruit in a savory broth.
  • Pansit - A Filipino noodle dish which originated from China.

Local Beverages

Capiz has several local drinks that visitors should try. Some popular ones include:

  • Basi - A local wine made from sugarcane and fermented for two to three weeks.
  • Tuba - Coconut wine that is known for its sweet and tangy taste.
  • Barubud - Sweet porridge made from glutinous rice and coconut milk.

Where to Stay in Capiz: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Capiz has plenty of options for accommodations, ranging from budget-friendly hostels to luxurious hotels. Here are some recommended options:

  • San Antonio Resort - This beachfront resort in Roxas City offers spacious rooms with stunning ocean views.
  • Planta Centro Bacolod Hotel and Residences - Located in nearby Negros Occidental, this hotel offers modern and comfortable rooms at an affordable price.
  • The Urban Manor Hotel - A cozy hotel located in the heart of Roxas City.

What to Buy in Capiz: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Make sure to bring home a piece of Capiz by picking up some of the following souvenirs and local products:

  • Tibiao Blanket - A locally-made handwoven blanket from Tibiao.
  • Pina Cloth - A traditional cloth made from pineapple fibers.
  • Seafood - Bring home some fresh seafood to enjoy at home.

Local Culture and Customs of Capiz

Capiz is known for its rich cultural heritage and traditions. It is important to be respectful of local customs, such as removing your shoes before entering someone’s home and dressing modestly when visiting religious sites.

Best Time to Visit Capiz

The best time to visit Capiz is from December to March, which is the dry season. This is the perfect time to enjoy the beaches and explore nature.

Do’s and Don’ts of Capiz

Do:

  • Try the local seafood.
  • Visit the museums and historical landmarks.
  • Respect local customs and traditions.

Don’t:

  • Wear revealing clothing when visiting religious sites.
  • Leave your trash on the beach.

Safety and Health

Safety in Capiz

Capiz is a relatively safe destination for tourists. However, it is always important to take precautions such as not carrying large amounts of cash and keeping your valuables in a safe place.

Health in Capiz

While the tap water in Capiz is generally safe to drink, it is advised to drink bottled water to avoid contracting illnesses. It is also recommended to get vaccinated for typhoid and hepatitis A before your trip.

Getting to Capiz: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Capiz

The Roxas Airport is the nearest airport to Capiz, located in Roxas City. It receives several domestic flights from Metro Manila, Cebu, and Iloilo.

Bus Stations Near Capiz

The Ceres Bus Terminal in Roxas City offers several bus routes to different parts of the Philippines.

Other Options

There are also ferry services available that connect Capiz to other provinces in the Visayas region.

How to Get Around in Capiz

Tricycles, jeepneys, and buses are popular modes of transportation in Capiz. Motorcycles and bicycles can also be rented for those who want to explore the province at their own pace.

Where to Go Next from Capiz: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

After exploring Capiz, visitors can continue their journey by checking out these nearby destinations:

  • Bacolod City - Known as the “City of Smiles”, Bacolod City is famous for its MassKara Festival and delectable cuisine.
  • Iloilo City - A bustling city that is famous for its heritage sites and delectable cuisine.
  • Boracay Island - Located in Aklan, Boracay Island is known for its white-sand beaches and vibrant nightlife.

Conclusion

Capiz is a hidden gem that offers a unique blend of history, culture, and outdoor adventures. Whether you’re a beach lover, history buff, or outdoor enthusiast, there’s something for everyone in this beautiful province.
