Introduction to Ubay

Nestled on the northern coast of Bohol island, Ubay is a charming town that offers an authentic experience of Filipino culture and an array of outdoor adventures. While less popular than other towns in Bohol, Ubay offers a unique blend of lush greenery, scenic beaches, and rich history that attracts visitors from all over the world. In this guide, we will take you through the best places to visit, exciting things to do, local culture and customs, and more, ensuring an unforgettable trip to Ubay.

List of Places to Visit in Ubay

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Ubay has a rich history and culture, and there are several museums and historical places that you ought to visit if you’re interested in learning more about the town’s heritage.

Ubay Museum

The Ubay Museum showcases the town’s history and culture through various exhibits, including artifacts, photographs, and documents. The museum is open from 9 AM to 4 PM and is located at Centro, Poblacion, Ubay.

Our Lady of Immaculate Conception Church

The Our Lady of Immaculate Conception Church, built in 1869, is an excellent representation of Neogothic architecture. The church still holds masses, and visitors are allowed to appreciate its grandeur during the day.

Natural Attractions of Ubay: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

In addition to being rich in history and culture, Ubay has plenty of natural attractions that should be on your itinerary.

Magtangtang Mangrove Boardwalk

Take a leisurely stroll on the Magtangtang Mangrove Boardwalk and enjoy the lush greenery of the mangrove forest. The boardwalk is approximately 600 meters and offers scenic views of the surrounding area.

Can-uba Beach

Can-uba Beach is a serene and picturesque beach that’s perfect for relaxing and enjoying the surrounding natural beauty. The beach has crystal clear waters and golden sands, making it ideal for swimming or sunbathing.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Ubay

Ubay boasts several hiking and biking trails that cater to both beginners and experienced hikers and bikers. Here are some of the most popular trails:

Kinahugan Falls Trail

The Kinahugan Falls Trail, also known as the Ubay Agri-Park Trail, is a 6.28 km trail that features lush greenery, scenic views, and a waterfall at the end of the trail.

Ubay Stock Farm Trail

For those interested in a scenic bike ride, the Ubay Stock Farm Trail is a must-try. The trail is approximately 10 km long and offers stunning views of Ubay’s rolling hills and greenery.

Best Things to Do in Ubay: Activities and Tours

In addition to the above attractions, there are plenty of other activities and tours to enjoy in Ubay.

Island Hopping Tour

Take an island-hopping tour around Ubay’s small islands and visit beaches, coves, and snorkeling spots, including Lapinig Island and Punta Ballo.

Sunset Cruise

Get on board a sunset cruise and watch the sun setting in the horizon while enjoying dinner onboard.

Week-Long Itinerary for Ubay

Day 1: Visit Ubay Museum and Our Lady of Immaculate Conception Church

Day 2: Explore the Magtangtang Mangrove Boardwalk and enjoy a picnic lunch

Day 3: Experience the beach life at Can-uba Beach

Day 4: Take the Kinahugan Falls Trail and enjoy a refreshing swim at the waterfall

Day 5: Go on a scenic bike ride along the Ubay Stock Farm Trail

Day 6: Take an island-hopping tour

Day 7: Relax on a sunset cruise and enjoy dinner onboard

Food and Drink Scene of Ubay

Must-Try Dishes

Ubay is known for its delicious seafood and traditional Filipino cuisine. Here are some of the must-try dishes:

Kinilaw

Kinilaw is a Filipino dish made with raw fish marinated in vinegar and calamansi, served with onions, tomatoes, and chili.

Adobo

Adobo is a Filipino dish made with meat (usually chicken or pork) simmered in soy sauce, vinegar, and other spices.

Local Beverages

Tubâ

Tubâ is a popular Filipino alcoholic drink made from fermented coconut water.

Kape Barako

Kape Barako is a local coffee variety that’s grown in neighboring Batangas and is known for its strong taste and aroma.

Where to Stay in Ubay: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Ubay has several options for accommodation, from budget-friendly to luxurious. Here are some of the best options:

Budget-Friendly Options

Agape Pension House

Agape Pension House is a budget-friendly option located in the town center. The air-conditioned rooms come with free Wi-Fi and cable TV.

ScubaBro House

ScubaBro House offers private rooms with air conditioning and free Wi-Fi at very reasonable prices.

Mid-Range Options

Amihan Resort

Amihan Resort is a beachfront resort with various amenities, including an outdoor pool and restaurant.

Lims Resort

Lims Resort offers clean and comfortable rooms with a private bathroom and balcony overlooking Ubay Bay.

What to Buy in Ubay: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Peanut Kisses

Peanut Kisses are peanut-shaped biscuits made from peanuts, flour, eggs, and sugar, which are a popular snack in Ubay.

Banig Weaving

Banig is a traditional Filipino mat made from the leaves of Pandan or Screw Pine, which are available in Ubay. These mats are perfect for picnics and lounging around while on the beach.

Local Culture and Customs of Ubay

Ubay celebrates several festivals throughout the year, including the Ubay fiesta and the Inuboran Festival. During these festivals, visitors can immerse themselves in the local culture, which includes traditional dances and music, and religious rituals.

Best Time to Visit Ubay

The best time to visit Ubay is between November and April when the weather is dry and sunny, perfect for outdoor activities and exploring the town.

Do’s and Don’ts of Ubay

Do:

  • Respect the locals and their culture.
  • Keep your surroundings clean and avoid littering.
  • Learn some basic Tagalog phrases to communicate with the locals.

Don’t:

  • Don’t insult or argue with locals.
  • Don’t bring pets or other animals to natural areas.

Safety and Health

Safety in Ubay

Ubay is generally safe, but you should still take basic precautions and practice common sense. Avoid carrying too much cash or valuables while exploring and keep them in hotel safety deposits.

Health in Ubay

Visit a travel doctor before your trip and make sure you are up-to-date on all necessary vaccines. Make sure to carry medications for common illnesses such as diarrhea and indigestion.

Getting to Ubay: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Ubay

The nearest airport to Ubay is the Tagbilaran City Airport, located approximately 100 km away.

Bus Stations Near Ubay

Buses that go to Ubay are available from the Tagbilaran City Bus Terminal. The travel time from Tagbilaran to Ubay is about 3 hours.

Other Options

Taxis, private vans, and car rentals are also available as transportation options from the city.

How to Get Around in Ubay

There are several transportation options available in Ubay, including tricycles, motorcycles, and bicycles. You can also rent a car or hire a private van or taxi for your convenience.

Where to Go Next from Ubay: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Anda

Anda is a nearby town famous for its white-sand beaches and crystal-clear waters, perfect for snorkeling and diving.

Panglao Island

Panglao Island is known for its magnificent beaches and some of the best diving spots in the country.

Conclusion

Ubay is truly a hidden gem waiting to be discovered. From its rich culture to its natural wonders, Ubay offers something for everyone, making it a perfect destination for both adventure seekers and those looking for a relaxing getaway. Use this guide, and we’re confident that you’ll have an unforgettable experience in Ubay, Bohol.
