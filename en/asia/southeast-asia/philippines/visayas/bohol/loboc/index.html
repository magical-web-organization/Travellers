Introduction to Loboc

Loboc is a small town located in the province of Bohol, in the Visayas region of the Philippines. It is known for its lush green sceneries, cascading waterfalls, verdant hills, and the Loboc River, making it one of the top tourist destinations in the country. The town is also rich in heritage, with a number of historical landmarks, old churches, and cultural festivals.

List of Places to Visit in Loboc

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Loboc Church: The town’s main church, built in 1602, is one of the oldest churches in Bohol and showcases Spanish colonial architecture.
  • Loboc Museum: The museum features a collection of religious items, antique houses, and local artifacts showcasing the town’s history and culture.
  • Poblacione 1 Watchtower: This historic structure dates back to 1800, and its observation deck offers panoramic views of the town’s surroundings.
  • Ilihan Hill: A historic hill that provides scenic views of the Loboc River and is believed to be the site where Datu Sikatuna made a blood compact with Spanish explorer Miguel Lopez de Legazpi.

Natural Attractions of Loboc: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Loboc River: Take a river cruise and enjoy the scenic views of the Loboc River, lush greenery, and waterfalls.
  • Busay Falls: A waterfall that features a natural swimming pool, hiking trails, and picnic areas.
  • Mag-Aso Falls: Located near the town center, this waterfall is surrounded by lush greenery and boasts a natural swimming pool.
  • Chocolate Hills: These iconic hills are located in the nearby town of Carmen and are famous for their unique brownish color that resembles chocolate during the dry season.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Loboc

  • Loboc Eco-Adventure Park: Offers various activities including ziplining, hiking, biking, and rope courses.
  • Mount Carmel: A popular hiking destination for locals where visitors can enjoy scenic views of the ocean and the town.

Best Things to Do in Loboc: Activities and Tours

  • Loboc River Cruise: Go on a boat tour along the Loboc River and experience the town’s lush greenery and waterfalls.
  • Firefly Watching: Enjoy a unique experience and witness the mesmerizing dance of fireflies at night.
  • Tarsier Sanctuary: Visit the nearby sanctuary and see the world’s smallest primates, the tarsiers, in their natural habitat.
  • Kayak/Stand-up Paddle Boarding: Get active and explore the Loboc River through kayak or stand-up paddle boarding.

Food and Drink Scene of Loboc

Must-Try Dishes

  • Calamay: A sweet rice cake made of glutinous rice, sugar, and coconut milk.
  • Chicharon: A pork rind fried to a crispy texture and served with vinegar.
  • Binignit: A Filipino dessert or snack made with tubers, fruits, and coconut milk.

Local Beverages

  • Tuba: A fermented coconut wine that is sweet or sour depending on the age.
  • Calamansi Juice: A refreshing drink made of calamansi (local citrus fruit) and water with sugar.

Where to Stay in Loboc: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Villa Limpia: A resort that offers modern amenities, scenic views of the town, and easy access to the river.
  • Stay N Save: A budget-friendly guesthouse located in the town center and close to the attractions.
  • Nipa Hut Village: A traditional Filipino lodging experience with nipa huts and bamboo rooms.

What to Buy in Loboc: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Tarsier Souvenirs: Bring home a miniature tarsier or t-shirts and other items with a tarsier design.
  • Coconut Products: Purchase coconut oil, vinegar, or soap made from the locally harvested coconuts.

Local Culture and Customs of Loboc

  • Festival: Loboc is known for its annual Loboc River Festival, which showcases colorful parades, street dances, and cultural performances.
  • Respect for Elders: Filipinos hold deep respect for their elders, and it is customary to say “po” and “opo” as a sign of respect.
  • Conservative Dress Code: When visiting religious sites, it is recommended to dress conservatively and cover your shoulders and knees.

Best Time to Visit Loboc

The best time to visit Loboc is during the dry season, which runs from December to April, as the weather is mild and pleasant. However, the town can be crowded during the peak season, so it is advisable to book accommodations and tours in advance.

Do’s and Don’ts of Loboc

Do:

  • Be respectful to the locals and their culture.
  • Try the local delicacies and traditional drinks.
  • Carry loose change for payment on the river cruise.

Don’t:

  • Litter in the river or other natural attractions.
  • Touch or disturb the tarsiers in their natural habitat.
  • Wear revealing clothing when visiting religious sites.

Safety and Health

Safety in Loboc

Loboc is generally a safe area, but as with any tourist destination, be mindful of your belongings and surroundings. Avoid going out alone at night and always take licensed and reliable transportation.

Health in Loboc

Drink only bottled water and avoid street food that may not be prepared under sanitary conditions. It is always advisable to bring personal medication and consult a doctor before traveling to the area.

Getting to Loboc: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Loboc

The closest airport to Loboc is the Tagbilaran airport, which is located 27 kilometers away. Visitors can take a taxi, bus, or van to reach the town center.

Bus Stations Near Loboc

Loboc has a bus terminal that serves routes to other towns and cities around Bohol. Visitors can also take a bus from the nearby Tagbilaran terminal to reach Loboc.

Other Options

Visitors can also rent a car or motorcycle to reach Loboc or hail a tricycle or jeepney for short distances.

How to Get Around in Loboc

The best way to get around Loboc is by tricycle, motorcycle, or jeepney. Visitors can also walk around the town center to explore the nearby attractions.

Where to Go Next from Loboc: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Chocolate Hills: The iconic hills in the nearby town of Carmen are a must-visit destination.
  • Panglao Island: A popular tourist destination known for its crystal clear waters and white sand beaches.
  • Danao Adventure Park: Located in the nearby town of Danao, this park offers adrenaline-pumping activities like ziplining, caving, and sky cycling.

Conclusion

Loboc offers visitors a unique blend of natural beauty, cultural heritage, and adventure. From the scenic river cruises to the lush hiking trails, visitors can enjoy a wide range of activities and experiences. With this comprehensive travel guide, visitors can plan their trip to Loboc with ease and have a memorable time exploring this charming town in the Philippines.
