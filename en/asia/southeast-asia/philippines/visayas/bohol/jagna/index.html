Jagna Travel Guide: Discovering the Hidden Gems of Bohol

Located in the eastern part of Bohol, Jagna is a quaint town that promises a remarkable and authentic Filipino experience. Known for its rich history, mouth-watering local delicacies, and mesmerizing natural attractions, Jagna offers an escape to a quieter and more genuine side of the Philippines.

In this comprehensive travel guide, we’ll showcase the top tourist attractions, things to do, where to stay, food and drink, culture and customs, and everything you need to know before visiting Jagna.

List of Places to Visit in Jagna

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Jagna Church - Also known as The Immaculate Conception Parish Church, this religious site was founded in 1800 and boasts of a unique Baroque and Rococo architectural style. It is also known to have played a significant role during the Philippine Revolution in 1898.
  • Municipal Museum - Built in 2002, the Municipal Museum houses artifacts and relics from Jagna’s rich history when it was still a Spanish colonial town.
  • Jagna Watchtower - Built in 1830, this historical landmark served as a lookout station and defense fortification against pirates and sea raiders during the Spanish era.

Natural Attractions of Jagna: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Can-Umantad Falls - Known as the tallest waterfall in Bohol, Can-Umantad Falls is a breathtaking natural wonder located in the nearby town of Candijay. It involves a short trek, but the view is worth it.
  • Anda Beach - A stunning, white-sand beach located an hour away from Jagna, Anda Beach is worth the journey and is perfect for sunbathing or a dip in the clear waters.
  • Jagna Sand Bar - Accessible only during low tide, the Jagna Sandbar offers stunning views and a chance to witness local fishermen harvesting shellfish and crabs.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Jagna

  • Jagna to Garcia Hernandez trail - This trail takes around 2-4 hours to hike or bike, depending on your fitness level. It offers stunning views of the sea and nearby mountains and passes several waterfalls and rivers.
  • Mount Balungao - A relatively easy hike, Mount Balungao’s peak offers a stunning view of Jagna and its surroundings.

Best Things to Do in Jagna: Activities and Tours

  • Bohol Bee Farm Tour - Learn about organic beekeeping, and indulge in a healthy and delicious meal made from ingredients harvested from Bohol Bee Farm’s garden.
  • Marine Sanctuary Snorkeling Tour - See the abundant underwater life in Jagna’s marine sanctuary and try your hand at snorkeling.
  • Dolphin Watching - Witness dolphins jumping and playing in their natural habitat at dawn.

Week-Long Itinerary for Jagna

Day 1: Arrival and Check-in at a hotel in Jagna Day 2: Visit Jagna Church, Municipal Museum, and Jagna Watchtower Day 3: Hike/bike the trail from Jagna to Garcia Hernandez and visit Anda Beach afterwards Day 4: Visit Can-Umantad Falls and explore nearby towns Day 5: Visit Marine Sanctuary and enjoy snorkeling Day 6: Take a Bohol Bee Farm Tour and afternoon swimming at Jagna Sandbar Day 7: Departure from Jagna

Food and Drink Scene of Jagna

Must-Try Dishes

  • Binakhaw - A sweet and sticky rice cake made from glutinous rice and coconut milk, cooked traditionally in bamboo tubes.
  • Budbud Kabog - Another variety of rice cake made from millet and coconut milk and wrapped in local banana leaves.
  • Kakanin - A wide array of sticky rice cakes made from various ingredients such as cassava, corn, and purple yam. A popular dessert or snack in the Philippines.

Local Beverages

  • Tuba - A traditional Filipino alcoholic beverage made from fermented coconut juice. Often enjoyed fresh from the tree.
  • Barako Coffee - A rich and strong coffee variety found in the Philippines, particularly in Batangas and Cavite.

Where to Stay in Jagna: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • J&R Residence - A budget-friendly beachfront accommodation that offers rooms with sea views and easy access to Jagna’s shore.
  • Coco Grove Tourist Inn - A comfortable and spacious accommodation within a walking distance of Jagna town plaza and a barbecue restaurant.
  • J&R Residence Inn - A peaceful and cozy accommodation option that offers a relaxing garden and clean, air-conditioned rooms.

What to Buy in Jagna: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Peanut Kisses - Sweet and nutty treats made from peanuts and egg whites, usually found in local bakeries or souvenir shops.
  • Handmade Rattan Products - Local artisans make baskets, bags, and furniture from Rattan.

Local Culture and Customs of Jagna

  • Religion - The town’s patron saint is Our Lady of Immaculate Conception, and apart from the main church, there are chapels dedicated to her in each barangay in Jagna.
  • Festivals - Jagna celebrates its town fiesta annually on 8th December, with street parades and cultural presentations.
  • Dance - A traditional folk dance called Karioka is a popular cultural presentation in Jagna, especially during town festivals.

Best Time to Visit Jagna

The best time to visit Jagna is from November to April, during the cool and dry season. The rest of the year may have occasional rainfall, but it is still possible to travel around Jagna.

Do’s and Don’ts of Jagna

Do:

  • Respect local culture and customs, especially during town fiestas.
  • Dress appropriately inside religious sites.
  • Always ask for permission before taking photos of individuals or their homes.

Don’t:

  • Drink tap water, especially if you’re not accustomed to it, to avoid getting an upset stomach.
  • Litter, as there are no designated trash bins in public areas.
  • Smoke in public places, as smoking is prohibited in certain areas.

Safety and Health

Safety in Jagna

Jagna is a relatively safe place with low crime rates. However, always be mindful of your belongings, especially in crowded areas.

Health in Jagna

It is recommended to drink bottled water or boil tap water before drinking. Also, be mindful of the heat, wear sunscreen, and stay hydrated when doing outdoor activities.

Getting to Jagna: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Jagna

  • Tagbilaran Airport - Located 72 km from Jagna, Tagbilaran Airport is the nearest airport to the town and offers flights from Manila, Cebu, and other cities.

Bus Stations Near Jagna

  • Tagbilaran Central Bus Station - Has a regular bus service that connects Jagna to other places in Bohol, including Tagbilaran City.
  • Cebu South Bus Station - Ships passengers to Tagbilaran City in Bohol via ferry services.

Other Options

  • Private Shuttle Service - Several travel agencies operate shuttle services from Tagbilaran Airport and other nearby towns to Jagna.
  • Car Rentals - Rentals can be booked at Tagbilaran Airport, but it is recommended to book in advance for better rates and availability.

How to Get Around in Jagna

  • Tricycles - A common mode of transportation in Jagna, tricycles can take you from one point to another within the town for a reasonable fee.
  • Habal-habal - Ideal for those who want to explore the nearby countryside, habal-habal is a motorcycle taxi that can take you to remote places that are not accessible by tricycles.

Where to Go Next from Jagna: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Panglao Island and its beaches - Known for its stunning beaches and diving spots, Panglao Island is located an hour’s drive from Jagna.
  • Chocolate Hills - A group of limestone hills located in the nearby town of Carmen, Chocolate Hills is Bohol’s most famous attraction.
  • Loboc River Cruise - An hour and a half drive from Jagna, you can experience a relaxing river cruise while taking in the beauty of nature.

Conclusion

Jagna offers a unique experience for those who want to explore a hidden gem in the Philippines. Its natural attractions, rich history, and delicious local delicacies will definitely leave you wanting to come back for more. With this comprehensive travel guide, you’ll be able to plan your trip to Jagna with ease and have a memorable experience that is truly Filipino.
