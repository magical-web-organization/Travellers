Introduction to Baclayon

Baclayon is a charming municipality in Bohol, Philippines. It is a popular destination for tourists who are looking for a quiet escape away from the hustle and bustle of urban living. Baclayon is known for its rich cultural heritage, natural attractions, and breathtaking views.

List of Places to Visit in Baclayon

Here’s a list of Baclayon’s top attractions grouped into two categories:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Baclayon Church - one of the oldest stone churches in the Philippines
  • Baclayon Museum - a museum located beside Baclayon Church that showcases the municipality’s cultural heritage
  • Casa Rocha - a heritage house that offers a glimpse into the lifestyle of wealthy Filipinos during the Spanish colonial period

Natural Attractions of Baclayon: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Prony the Python - a large python that visitors can hold and take pictures with
  • Hinagdanan Cave - a cave that is surrounded by natural greenery and has a lagoon inside
  • Baclayon Beach - a secluded white sand beach that is perfect for swimming and sunbathing

Hiking/Biking Routes of Baclayon

Baclayon has several hiking and biking routes that allow visitors to explore its natural beauty. Some popular routes include:

  • The Baclayon Boulevard - a scenic route that runs parallel to Baclayon Beach
  • Abatan River Hike - a 5km hike along the Abatan River with breathtaking views of the river and surrounding area
  • Mag-aso Falls Hike - a hike through the lush jungle to reach Mag-aso Falls, a waterfall with crystal-clear water

Best Things to Do in Baclayon: Activities and Tours

There are plenty of activities and tours to choose from in Baclayon. Here are some of the best things to do:

  • Island Hopping Tour - a tour of nearby islands that includes snorkeling, swimming, and sunbathing
  • Dolphin and Whale Watching - a tour to see these magnificent creatures up close and personal in their natural habitat
  • Stand Up Paddle Boarding - a fun and unique way to explore the beauty of Baclayon’s coastline

Food and Drink Scene of Baclayon

Baclayon has a vibrant food and drink scene. Here are some must-try local dishes and beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Adobo - a classic Filipino dish made with chicken or pork marinated in vinegar, soy sauce, and garlic
  • Lechon - a whole roasted pig that is crispy on the outside and tender on the inside
  • Sizzling Sisig - a spicy dish made with chopped pig’s head and liver

Local Beverages

  • Tuba - a fermented coconut drink that is sweet and slightly alcoholic
  • Mango Shake - a refreshing drink made with fresh mangoes and ice cream
  • San Miguel Beer - a popular beer brand in the Philippines

Where to Stay in Baclayon: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Baclayon offers a variety of accommodations to suit any budget. Here are some recommendations:

  • The Peacock Garden - a luxurious hotel with spacious rooms, a spa, and infinity pool
  • Alona Kew White Beach Resort - a beachfront resort with rooms that have a private balcony with sea views
  • Starlight Inn - a budget-friendly guesthouse that is located in the heart of Baclayon

What to Buy in Baclayon: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Some popular souvenirs and local products to bring home from Baclayon include:

  • Shellcraft - intricate handicrafts made from seashells
  • Tarsier T-shirts - T-shirts with the popular Tarsier primate depicted
  • Bohol Peanut Kisses - delectable peanut balls that are a Bohol specialty

Local Culture and Customs of Baclayon

Baclayon is a predominantly Catholic municipality and is home to several historical churches and landmarks. The people of Baclayon are known for their hospitable and friendly nature. Visitors are expected to respect their local customs and values.

Best Time to Visit Baclayon

The best time to visit Baclayon is during the dry season, which is between November and April. The weather during this period is pleasant, with sunny and warm days ideal for outdoor activities.

Do’s and Don’ts of Baclayon

Do:

  • Respect the local culture and customs
  • Dress modestly when visiting churches and other religious landmarks
  • Sample the local cuisine

Don’t:

  • Insult or disrespect locals
  • Wear revealing clothing in public
  • Engage in illegal activities

Safety and Health

Safety in Baclayon

Baclayon is generally a safe place to visit, but visitors are advised to take precautions to avoid becoming victims of crime. Here are some safety tips:

  • Avoid walking alone at night
  • Keep your valuables close to you
  • Use authorized tour guides when exploring natural attractions

Health in Baclayon

Visitors to Baclayon are advised to take precautions against common tropical diseases. Here are some health tips:

  • Drink only bottled or boiled water
  • Apply insect repellent to avoid mosquito bites
  • Seek medical attention if you experience any symptoms of illness

Getting to Baclayon: Transportation Options and Logistics

Visitors can get to Baclayon by plane, bus, or private vehicle. Here are some options:

Airports Near Baclayon

  • Tagbilaran Airport - located just 15 minutes away from the center of Baclayon

Bus Stations Near Baclayon

  • Tagbilaran Bus Terminal - buses depart regularly for Manila and other major cities in the Philippines

Other Options

  • Private Vehicle - visitors can rent a car or hire a private taxi to reach Baclayon

How to Get Around in Baclayon

The best way to get around Baclayon is by tricycle or habal-habal. These are small motorcycles that can carry up to two passengers. Visitors can also rent a car or a bike for a more independent way of exploring the area.

Where to Go Next from Baclayon: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Here are some nearby destinations and excursions that visitors can go on after visiting Baclayon:

  • Chocolate Hills - famous geological formation located in Carmen, Bohol
  • Panglao Island - a popular destination with beautiful beaches and world-class diving spots
  • Loboc River Cruise - a relaxing cruise that takes visitors along Loboc River while dining on local cuisine

Conclusion

Baclayon is an enchanting destination that offers a diverse range of activities and attractions for visitors. With its natural beauty, rich culture, and friendly locals, Baclayon is a must-visit destination for anyone traveling to the Philippines.
