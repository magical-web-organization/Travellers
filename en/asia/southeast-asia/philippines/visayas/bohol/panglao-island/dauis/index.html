Introduction to Dauis

Dauis is a small town located on the Panglao Island in Bohol, Philippines. With its rich cultural heritage, an abundance of natural beauty, and scenic landscapes, Dauis is a popular tourist destination. The town is famous for its old stone church, the Our Lady of the Assumption Church, which was built by the Spanish in the 18th century. If you’re looking for a peaceful getaway from the hustle and bustle of city life, Dauis is the perfect destination.

List of Places to Visit in Dauis

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Our Lady of the Assumption Church - The old stone church is built on top of a hill and offers a great panoramic view of the surroundings.
  • Bohol Bee Farm - The farm showcases a sustainable approach towards agriculture and showcases various honeybee products.
  • Baclayon Museum - This museum houses a collection of religious artifacts and cultural exhibits narrating the history of the Philippines.

Natural Attractions of Dauis: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Alona beach - The white sand beach is perfect for a picnic, sunbathing, and swimming.
  • Hinagdanan Cave - An underground cave filled with a natural pool surrounded by stalactites and stalagmites.
  • Mahogany Forest - This forest is home to a dense grove of mahogany trees that line the road leading to the Chocolate Hills.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Dauis

Explore the scenic beauty of Dauis with a hike or bike tour. Take the trail that leads to the scenic hills surrounding Dauis and take in the panoramic views overlooking the surrounding areas.

Best Things to Do in Dauis: Activities and Tours

  • Paddle Boarding - Rent a paddleboard from Alona Beach and explore the crystal clear waters of the Bohol sea.
  • Dolphin Watching - Take a tour of the Balicasag Island and watch the dolphins in their natural habitat.
  • Island Hopping - Explore various islands around Panglao and experience their unique beauty.

Week-Long Itinerary for Dauis

Day 1: Visit Our Lady of the Assumption Church, Mahogany Forest, and Bohol Bee Farm.

Day 2: Go paddleboarding at Alona Beach, explore the Hinagdanan Caves, and visit the Baclayon Museum.

Day 3: Take a dolphin-watching tour, visit the Virgin Island, and go snorkeling.

Day 4: Explore Danao adventure park, take a bike tour around Dauis.

Day 5: Take a day trip to the Chocolate Hills and Tarsier Conservation Area.

Day 6: Explore more of Dauis’s natural beauty by visiting Balicasag Island.

Day 7: Relax on the beach, go shopping for souvenirs, and indulge in some delicious Filipino cuisine.

Food and Drink Scene of Dauis

Must-Try Dishes

  • Lechon - A Filipino dish consisting of a whole roasted pig.
  • Kare-Kare - A stew made with oxtail and peanut sauce.
  • Sinigang - A soup made with tamarind, vegetables, and either pork, fish, or shrimp.

Local Beverages

  • San Miguel Beer - A famous Filipino beer.
  • Tuba - A fermented coconut juice that is a local favorite.

Where to Stay in Dauis: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Henazz Villa Resort - A luxurious resort located in a quiet and peaceful area of Panglao.
  • Alona Royal Palm Resort - A beachfront resort that offers a great view of Panglao’s pristine beaches.
  • Sun Apartelle Hotel - A budget-friendly hotel located in the center of Dauis.

What to Buy in Dauis: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Peanut Kisses - A popular Filipino snack made from peanuts.
  • Tarsier figurines - Miniature figurines of the Philippine Tarsier, a small primate native to Bohol.
  • Boholano fabrics - Locally woven fabrics made in Bohol.

Local Culture and Customs of Dauis

The town of Dauis is predominantly Catholic, so appropriate clothing should be worn when visiting churches. The locals also place a great emphasis on family values and hospitality, so make sure to respect their culture and traditions.

Best Time to Visit Dauis

The best time of the year to visit Dauis is between November and April when the weather is dry and pleasant.

Do’s and Don’ts of Dauis

Do:

  • Respect the local culture.
  • Wear appropriate clothing when visiting churches.
  • Try the local cuisine.

Don’t:

  • Don’t litter.
  • Don’t disrespect the culture.

Safety and Health

Safety in Dauis

Dauis is a safe and peaceful town. However, it is still advised to take precautions such as keeping an eye on your belongings when traveling and not leaving valuables unattended.

Health in Dauis

Make sure to drink bottled water to avoid stomach illnesses. Dengue fever is also prevalent in the Philippines, so it’s recommended to apply mosquito repellent and carry mosquito nets.

Getting to Dauis: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Dauis

The nearest airport to Dauis is Bohol- Panglao International Airport. The airport is located only 12 km away from Dauis.

Bus Stations Near Dauis

There are bus stations located in Tagbilaran that connects to Dauis.

Other Options

Taxis and tricycles are also available to get to Dauis from Tagbilaran.

How to Get Around in Dauis

The best way to explore Dauis is by renting a motorbike or hiring a tricycle to visit places. You can also rent a car or take a tricycle to navigate the narrow lanes of Dauis.

Where to Go Next from Dauis: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Visit the Chocolate Hills in Carmen.
  • Take a boat ride along the Loboc River.
  • Explore the Tarsier Conservation Area.

Conclusion

Dauis is a hidden gem in the Philippines with a lot of natural beauty, cultural significance, and friendly locals. With its serene beaches, rich history, and delicious cuisine, Dauis is a must-visit destination for anyone traveling to the Philippines.
