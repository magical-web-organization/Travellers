Introduction to Bolod and Libaong

Located in Panglao Island, Bohol, Bolod and Libaong offer a serene and unspoiled tropical paradise with pristine beaches, crystal-clear waters, and teeming marine life. Known for its laid-back atmosphere and hospitable locals, this destination is perfect for travelers looking for a break from the hustle and bustle of urban life.

List of Places to Visit in Bolod and Libaong

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  1. Baclayon Church - A well-preserved coral stone church built-in 1727 that features Baroque and Gothic architecture.
  2. Loboc River Cruise - A scenic cruise along the Loboc River where visitors will experience traditional Filipino cuisine and music.
  3. Blood Compact Site - A historical spot marking the first treaty of friendship between Spanish conquistador, Miguel Lopez de Legazpi, and Filipino chieftain, Sikatuna.
  4. Tarsier Conservation Area - A sanctuary home to the world’s smallest primates.

Natural Attractions of Bolod and Libaong: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  1. Alona Beach - A clean and stunning white sand beach with clear blue-green water. Perfect for swimming, sunbathing, and water sports activities.
  2. Danao Beach - A hidden gem offering a secluded ambiance and a breathtaking view of the ocean.
  3. Hinagdanan Cave - A naturally formed limestone cave with a clear pool of water where visitors can swim.
  4. Bohol Bee Farm - A farm that features beautiful gardens and vegetable plots. It also offers food tours and workshops on organic farming and beekeeping.
  5. Panglao Island Nature Resort and Spa - This luxurious resort offers a private beach, excellent amenities, and a panoramic view of the sea.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Bolod and Libaong

  1. Sikatuna Tree Park Trail - A trail that showcases the biodiversity of Bohol island with over 40 different species of trees and plants.
  2. Chocolate Hills ATV Trail - An adventure ride through the famous Chocolate Hills with ATV vehicles.
  3. Sagbayan Peak Trail - A scenic bike ride through the countryside, passing by rice fields and charming local villages.

Best Things to Do in Bolod and Libaong: Activities and Tours

  1. Dolphin Watching - A must-do activity, where visitors can witness dolphins in their natural habitat.
  2. Island Hopping - Explore the nearby islands and beaches, including Pamilacan Island, Balicasag Island, and Virgin Island.
  3. Snorkeling and Diving - Discover the underwater world with an array of marine life living in the coral reefs.
  4. Beach Combing - Stroll along the beautiful shoreline and collect seashells and other interesting things that wash up from the ocean.

Food and Drink Scene of Bolod and Libaong

Must-Try Dishes

  1. Chicken Inasal - Grilled chicken marinated with garlic, vinegar, and soy sauce.
  2. Pancit Palabok - A noodle dish topped with shrimp sauce, boiled eggs, chicharon, and green onions.
  3. Lechon - Roasted whole pig, often served during celebrations and events.
  4. Calamay - Sticky and sweet coconut dessert made from glutinous rice and coconut milk.

Local Beverages

  1. Tuba - A coconut wine made from the sap of coconut palms.
  2. Sago’t Gulaman - A refreshing drink made from brown sugar syrup, sago pearls, and gulaman jelly.

Where to Stay in Bolod and Libaong: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  1. South Palms Resort Panglao - A luxurious beachfront resort with excellent amenities and a private beach.
  2. Alona Tropical Beach Resort - A budget-friendly resort with a relaxing ambiance.
  3. Libaong White Sand Beach Resort - A secluded resort boasting a private white sand beach.

What to Buy in Bolod and Libaong: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  1. Bohol Chocolate - Locally made chocolate from premium cacao beans.
  2. Shell Accessories - Handcrafted necklaces, bracelets, and earrings made of shells found on the beach.
  3. Weaving Products - Items handwoven from abaca and banana fibers, including bags, placemats, and table runners.

Local Culture and Customs of Bolod and Libaong

The people of Bolod and Libaong are hospitable and warm, and they celebrate several festivals throughout the year.

Best Time to Visit Bolod and Libaong

The best time to visit Bolod and Libaong is from November to May, when the weather is dry and mild.

Do’s and Don’ts of Bolod and Libaong

Do:

  1. Respect local customs and traditions, such as removing shoes before entering someone’s home.
  2. Try the local cuisine and products.
  3. Dress appropriately, especially when visiting churches or other religious places.

Don’t:

  1. Swim in prohibited areas or touch the corals while snorkeling or diving.
  2. Litter or damage the environment.
  3. Disrespect the locals or culture.

Safety and Health

Safety in Bolod and Libaong

Bolod and Libaong are generally safe destinations, but tourists must still take precautions against pickpocketing and other petty crimes. Travelers must also stay vigilant when engaging in outdoor activities and follow safety protocols.

Health in Bolod and Libaong

Tourists are advised to bring their insect repellents as the area is prone to mosquitos and other insects. It is also recommended to get a vaccine for hepatitis A and B, as well as typhoid. Travelers must stay hydrated and observe good hygiene practices.

Getting to Bolod and Libaong: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Bolod and Libaong

Panglao International Airport is the nearest airport to Bolod and Libaong, located approximately 20 kilometers away.

Bus Stations Near Bolod and Libaong

Several bus companies operate in and out of Bohol, with stations located at Tagbilaran City and Ubay Port.

Other Options

Private transfers, including vans and cars, can also be arranged for a more comfortable mode of transportation.

How to Get Around in Bolod and Libaong

Tourists can rent a motorbike, a tricycle, or a bicycle to explore Bolod and Libaong. Walking is also recommended, as some of the best attractions can only be reached on foot.

Where to Go Next from Bolod and Libaong: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  1. Chocolate Hills - A must-visit and iconic destination in Bohol, known for its unusual geological formations.
  2. Anda - A quiet and peaceful town with long white-sand beaches, colorful coral reefs, and natural springs.
  3. Cebu - Another popular Philippine destination, known for its historical churches, cultural landmarks, and local delicacies.
