Introduction to Panglao Island

Panglao Island is a tiny island located in the province of Bohol, in the Visayas region of the Philippines. It boasts of its turquoise-blue waters, white sandy beaches fringed by tall palm trees, and of course, its world-famous diving sites. This island paradise is also home to unique flora and fauna, ancient historical sites, and fascinating local culture.

List of Places to Visit in Panglao Island

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Panglao Watchtower - This 18th-century watchtower is located on the southwestern coast of Panglao Island, and was built by the Spanish to guard against Muslim slave raiders.

  • St. Augustine Church - A stunning church built in the Spanish colonial style, this National Historical Landmark has been standing since the 1800s and still offers mass services today.

  • Baclayon Church - A 16th-century church and declared a National Cultural Treasure in Bohol’s largest town.

Natural Attractions of Panglao Island: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Alona Beach - famous for its powdery white sand, crystal blue waters, and vibrant nightlife.

  • Hinagdanan Cave - This cave is located in Dauis, Panglao Island, and features an underground lagoon with clear green waters.

  • Panglao Island Nature Resort & Spa - An eco-friendly, multi-awarded world-class resort, with a nature reserve, butterfly garden, bird sanctuary, and an organic farm.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Panglao Island

  • Hinagdanan Cave Trail - This hiking trail connects Hinagdanan Cave to the beach; it’s a short but challenging hike that offers rewarding views of the island.

  • Alona Beach to Danao Beach Trail - This biking trail is a relaxing 4 km ride along the scenic southern coast of the island.

Best Things to Do in Panglao Island: Activities and Tours

  • Island Hopping - Island hopping include dolphin watching, snorkeling, and lunch on a sandbar. It is perfect for those who want to experience the beauty of Panglao Island’s neighboring islands.

  • Diving and Snorkeling - Panglao Island has been voted as one of the world’s top ten dive sites, that includes underwater creatures. Diving enthusiasts can try out Dolphin House, Balicasag, and Pamilacan.

  • Firefly Watching - A must-have experience in Bohol, where hundreds of twinkling fireflies light up the night sky.

Week-Long Itinerary for Panglao Island

Day 1: Explore Alona Beach Day 2: Visit the historical landmarks on the southwestern coast. Day 3: Go island hopping and enjoy snorkeling and swimming in Bohol’s crystal clear waters. Day 4: Visit Hinagdanan Cave and Panglao Island Nature Resort & Spa. Day 5: Dive in Balicasag Island and meet dolphins in Pamilacan. Day 6: Firefly watching tour in Abatan River. Day 7: Relax and enjoy the beach and local restaurants in Alona.

Food and Drink Scene of Panglao Island

Must-Try Dishes

  • Calamay - A sweet snack made of sticky rice flour, coconut milk, and brown sugar.

  • Pancit Miki - A savory noodle dish with vegetables, shrimp, and pork.

  • Relyenong Bangus - Stuffed milkfish with tomatoes, onions, and spices.

Local Beverages

  • Tuba - A refreshing, lightly alcoholic beverage made from the sap of coconut trees.

  • Barako Coffee - A deliciously strong coffee made from Liberica Coffee beans.

Where to Stay in Panglao Island: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Amarela Resort - A peaceful resort that offers private beach access and privacy among the palm trees.

  • Panglao Island Nature Resort & Spa - An eco-friendly resort with a nature reserve, garden, bird sanctuary, organic farm, and wellness center.

  • Bohol Bee Farm - A farm-to-table themed resort that offers fresh organic food and exquisite handmade products.

What to Buy in Panglao Island: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Shellcrafts - Handmade crafts made from seashells, which can be purchased at Alona Beach or Island City Mall.

  • Bohol Chorizo - Bohol’s version of sausage, best served with eggs and rice for breakfast.

Local Culture and Customs of Panglao Island

The primary language in Panglao Island is the Cebuano language, although many locals also speak Tagalog and English. The island maintains strong ties to its cultural heritage, particularly its deep Catholic roots, which is celebrated yearly with the month-long Bai’ Lawig Festival during the summer season.

Best Time to Visit Panglao Island

The best time to visit Panglao Island is from November to May when the weather is dry and bright that makes the sea clearer for diving enthusiasts. The island may get crowded from December to February, so it is recommended to book ahead of time.

Do’s and Don’ts of Panglao Island

Do:

  • Respect the local culture and customs.

  • Take a dip in the crystal clear waters.

  • Try the local cuisine.

Don’t:

  • Litter on the beach.

  • Collect and damage coral reefs and other marine life.

  • Wear a bikini when visiting churches or historical sites.

Safety and Health

Safety in Panglao Island

Panglao Island is a quite safe place, but it is still essential to be careful and exercise standard safety precautions. Always keep your valuables secure and avoid walking alone at night.

Health in Panglao Island

Visitors are advised to drink only bottled water and check if it’s sealed before opening to prevent water-borne diseases. Dengue fever may also be a concern, so avoid mosquito bites by wearing long-sleeved clothing or using mosquito repellent.

Getting to Panglao Island: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Panglao Island

The nearest airport to Panglao Island is the recently developed Bohol-Panglao International Airport (TAG), which is approximately 25 kilometers away from the island.

Bus Stations Near Panglao Island

The Tagbilaran City integrated bus terminal services all parts of Bohol, including Panglao Island.

Other Options

There are ferries that connect Bohol to Cebu, specifically Pier 1, Cebu City to Tagbilaran City Port and Tubigon Port.

How to Get Around in Panglao Island

The most convenient way to explore the island is by tricycle or motorbike. Tricycles are more suitable for groups of up to three passengers, while motorbikes can only accommodate two passengers.

Where to Go Next from Panglao Island: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Aside from Panglao Island, Bohol offers many other destinations. Some of the must-visit destinations are:

  • Chocolate Hills - A must-visit attraction in the region, the Chocolate Hills are made up of more than 1,200 symmetrical mounds.

  • Tarsier Sanctuary - The world’s smallest primate calls Bohol home, and visitors can get up close with them in the sanctuary.

Conclusion

Panglao Island is perfect for those who want to explore some of the Philippines’ best beaches and immerse themselves in the local culture and heritage. With beautiful natural sights, unique attractions, and exciting activities, Panglao Island is a perfect destination for travelers seeking adventure or relaxation.
