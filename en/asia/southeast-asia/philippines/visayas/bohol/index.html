Introduction to Bohol

Bohol is a tropical paradise located in the Visayas region of the Philippines. This island province boasts of crystal-clear waters, white sand beaches, cascading waterfalls, limestone cliffs, and diverse wildlife. Bohol also has rich cultural heritage renowned for its Chocolate Hills, tarsiers, and centuries-old churches.

Travelers looking to experience a mix of natural beauty, adventure, and culture, should consider visiting Bohol. In this ultimate travel guide to Bohol, we’ll take you through the top places to visit, must-try activities and tours, where to stay, eat, and drink, and tips on how to stay safe and healthy during your trip.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Bohol

Bohol is a destination for adventure enthusiasts looking for an escape to nature. Exploring the island on foot or bike is a great way to discover the island’s hidden gems. Here are some of the top hiking/biking trails to try in Bohol:

  • Chocolate Hills biking trail
  • Mag-Aso Falls trek
  • Loboc River hiking trail
  • Mt. Carmel Hill trek

Best Things to Do in Bohol: Activities and Tours

Bohol is a hub of adventure, wildlife, and cultural activities. Whether you’re looking for a guided tour or outdoor adventure, Bohol has something for every type of traveler. Here are some of the top things to do in Bohol:

  • Island hopping and snorkeling in Balicasag and Virgin Island
  • Dolphin watching in Pamilacan Island
  • Firefly kayaking in Abatan River
  • Countryside tour
  • Chocolate Hills ATV tour
  • Scuba diving
  • Stand-up paddleboarding

Must-Try Dishes

Local Beverages

Local Culture and Customs of Bohol

Bohol has a rich and diverse culture that sets it apart from other islands in the Philippines. Here are some of the traditions and customs you’ll encounter during your visit:

  • The Loboc Children’s Choir
  • Sinulog Festival in Tagbilaran
  • Sandugo Festival
  • Kalamay-making in Jagna

Best Time to Visit Bohol

The best time to visit Bohol is during the dry season, which lasts from December to May. The weather is generally sunny and dry, making it the perfect time to explore the outdoors and participate in outdoor activities.

Do’s and Don’ts of Bohol

Here are some do’s and don’ts for travelers visiting Bohol:

Do:

  • Respect local customs and traditions
  • Bring appropriate clothing for the weather
  • Try the local cuisine
  • Bring insect repellent
  • Go island-hopping

Don’t:

  • Wear revealing clothing in public places
  • Litter on beaches and tourist spots
  • Touch the tarsiers
  • Do any activities that can harm the environment

Safety and Health

Here are some tips to stay safe and healthy while in Bohol:

Safety in Bohol

  • Be cautious of pickpockets and thieves
  • Be mindful of street vendors
  • Use registered tour operators and guides
  • Don’t travel alone at night
  • Use seat belts or helmets

Health in Bohol

  • Drink only bottled water
  • Use sunscreen and avoid the midday sun
  • Bring insect repellent
  • Get vaccinated before traveling
  • Seek medical attention if you feel unwell

Getting to Bohol: Transportation Options and Logistics

Bohol is accessible by plane, boat, or bus. Here are some of the ways to get to Bohol:

Airports Near Bohol

  • Panglao International Airport
  • Mactan-Cebu International Airport

Bus Stations Near Bohol

  • Tagbilaran City Bus Terminal

Other Options

  • Ferry terminal in Cebu City
  • Private van transfers from Cebu City or Dumaguete City

How to Get Around in Bohol

The best way to get around Bohol is by tricycle or rented motorcycle or car. Tricycles are ideal for short distances, while motorcycles or cars are more suitable for exploring the island at your own pace.

Where to Go Next from Bohol: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Siquijor Island
  • Siargao Island
  • Cebu City
  • Coron Island

Conclusion

Bohol is a must-visit destination in the Philippines for those looking to experience a mix of adventure, culture, and natural beauty. With this extensive travel guide, you’re equipped with the knowledge to plan and execute a memorable trip to the island. Remember to follow local customs and traditions and take necessary precautions to ensure your safety and wellbeing while in Bohol.
