Introduction to Marabut

Marabut is a municipality located in the province of Samar in the Philippines. It is known for its natural wonders such as beaches, waterfalls, and caves, as well as its cultural heritage. Visitors to Marabut can look forward to a combination of outdoor adventures, relaxation, and cultural immersion.

List of Places to Visit in Marabut

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • St. Joseph Church - Built in 1893, this church is a testament to Marabut’s Spanish colonial past. The church features a stunning facade and intricate details that reflect its rich history.
  • Marabut Historical Museum - This small museum is a treasure trove of Marabut’s past. It showcases artifacts from the town’s early settlers as well as items related to its religious and cultural history.

Natural Attractions of Marabut: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Sohoton Natural Bridge National Park - The national park boasts stunning karst formations, caves, and lagoons, making it a popular destination for outdoor enthusiasts. Visitors can explore the park by boat or kayak.
  • Samar Island Natural Park - Covering an area of 333,333 hectares, this natural park is home to various species of flora and fauna. Visitors can hike to the peak of Mt. Taraw, which offers panoramic views of the island.
  • Lulugayan Falls - Located in the heart of Samar Island, this waterfall is a breathtaking natural wonder. Visitors can swim in the cool waters and enjoy a picnic in the nearby park.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Marabut

  • Bulusan Lake to San Pablo - This trail takes visitors through the stunning Bulusan Lake and ends at the San Pablo Falls.
  • Ulot River Trekking - Visitors can explore the dense forests of Samar Island and cross the Ulot River via bamboo rafts.

Best Things to Do in Marabut: Activities and Tours

  • Island Hopping - Explore the nearby islands and white-sand beaches by boat. Visitors can swim, snorkel, and lounge under the sun.
  • Sohoton Cave Exploration - Discover the fascinating caves of Sohoton National Park, including the Hagukan Cave, which features an underground river and stunning rock formations.
  • Lulugayan Falls Adventure Tour - Experience the thrill of rappelling down the waterfalls and swimming in the pools below.

Food and Drink Scene of Marabut

Must-Try Dishes

  • Lupung - A traditional dish made from sticky rice and coconut milk, wrapped in banana leaves.
  • Binagol - A sweet dessert made from taro and coconut.

Local Beverages

  • Tuba - A traditional Filipino liquor made from coconut sap.
  • Samar Island Coffee - Grown in the highlands of Samar Island, this coffee is known for its unique flavor profile.

Where to Stay in Marabut: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Sohoton Homestay - This locally-owned homestay offers comfortable accommodation and authentic Filipino hospitality.
  • Marabut Resthouse - Located near St. Joseph Church, this resthouse offers cozy rooms and peaceful surroundings.

What to Buy in Marabut: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Banig Bags - Handwoven bags made by local artisans.
  • Fruit Jellies - A tasty souvenir made from local fruits such as jackfruit and mango.

Local Culture and Customs of Marabut

  • Sinulog Festival - A religious festival held every third Sunday of January. The festival celebrates the town’s patron saint, Sto. Niño.
  • Barrio Fiestas - Each barangay (township) in Marabut holds its own festival, which often features parades, dance performances, and other exciting activities.

Best Time to Visit Marabut

The best time to visit Marabut is during the dry season, which is from November to April. During this time, visitors can expect sunny weather, calm waters, and pleasant temperatures.

Do’s and Don’ts of Marabut

Do:

  • Respect the local customs and traditions.
  • Take only memories, leave only footprints.
  • try the local delicacies and beverages.

Don’t:

  • Litter or damage the natural surroundings.
  • Wear revealing clothing when visiting religious sites.
  • Haggle excessively when buying from local vendors.

Safety and Health

Safety in Marabut

Marabut is generally a safe destination for tourists. However, visitors are advised to always be mindful of their surroundings and belongings.

Health in Marabut

Travelers to Marabut should make sure to have updated vaccinations and bring mosquito repellent as dengue fever is present in the area.

Getting to Marabut: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Marabut

The closest airport to Marabut is the Daniel Z. Romualdez Airport in Tacloban City, which is approximately 65 kilometers away.

Bus Stations Near Marabut

Several bus companies serve Marabut from Tacloban City and nearby towns. Visitors can catch a bus at the Tacloban City Bus Terminal.

Other Options

Visitors can also rent a car or hire a private van to take them to Marabut from Tacloban City.

How to Get Around in Marabut

The best way to get around Marabut is by habal-habal, which is a motorcycle taxi. Visitors can also rent a motorbike or bicycle to explore the town and nearby attractions.

Where to Go Next from Marabut: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Calbayog City - A bustling coastal city known for its gorgeous beaches and rich history.
  • Tacloban City - The largest city in Eastern Visayas, Tacloban is a vibrant cultural and economic hub that is home to historical landmarks and natural wonders.

Conclusion

Marabut is a hidden gem in Eastern Visayas that offers a unique blend of natural wonders, cultural heritage, and outdoor adventures. With this comprehensive travel guide, visitors to Marabut will surely have an unforgettable experience.
