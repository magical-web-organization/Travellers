Introduction to Calbayog

Nestled in Samar Island within the Visayas region, Calbayog is a captivating city brimming with natural attractions, rich history, and vibrant culture. From breathtaking vistas to historical landmarks, this lesser-known destination is certainly worth exploring. With this comprehensive travel guide, discover the top things to do, places to see, and where to stay in Calbayog.

List of Places to Visit in Calbayog

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Sts. Peter and Paul Cathedral - Built in the 18th century, this impressive stone structure is the biggest church in Eastern Visayas. It also houses the oldest bell in Samar island.
  • Vicente Y. Rosales Ancestral House Museum - The oldest surviving house in Calbayog, this museum features artifacts from the Philippine-American War and showcases the lifestyle of the wealthy Filipino-Chinese Rosales family.
  • Calbayog Freedom Park - This park commemorates the heroic struggle of Calbayognons for freedom during the Japanese occupation in World War II. Attractions include Japanese tunnels, sculptures, and artworks.
  • Kapuluan Vista Point - This historic viewpoint offers panoramic views of Calbayog Bay, Samar Island, and its surrounding islets.

Natural Attractions of Calbayog: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Tarangban Falls - A natural gem hidden deep within the lush forest, Tarangban Falls features a 30-foot drop with a refreshing lagoon beneath.
  • Bangon-Bugtong Falls - Hailed as the King of Samar’s waterfalls, this stunning three-tiered cascade is perfect for picnics and swimming.
  • Binaliw Island - This small island boasts a long stretch of white sand beach, crystal-clear waters, and vibrant marine life perfect for snorkeling and diving.
  • Mt. Guintaguhan - This towering mountain rises to 1,164 meters and is a popular hiking destination among adventurous travelers. The trek offers stunning vistas and a chance to spot endemic flora and fauna.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Calbayog

  • Mt. Kankabato - This challenging mountain peak is a popular destination for hikers and bikers alike. The ascent offers breathtaking views of Calbayog and its surrounding areas.
  • Birhen ng Samar Garden Resort Trail - This trail starts from the resort and takes hikers through the lush jungle, across a hanging bridge, and up to a stunning waterfall.

Best Things to Do in Calbayog: Activities and Tours

  • Island Hopping - Explore Binaliw Island and its surrounding islets with a private boat ride.
  • Kawa-Kawa Adventure - Get your adrenaline pumping with ziplining, rappelling, and obstacle courses at Kawa-Kawa Hill.
  • Birdwatching - Discover the diverse birdlife of Calbayog with a guided tour of the Ulot River National Park.
  • Caving - Experience the thrill of spelunking in Lobo Cave, known for its unique rock formations and underground river system.

Food and Drink Scene of Calbayog

Must-Try Dishes

  • Binagol - A sweet dessert made from mashed taro, coconut milk, and muscovado sugar.
  • Suman and Tudmon - Sticky rice wrapped in banana leaves, served with chocolate spreads or coconut jam.
  • Hito at Alimango sa Buko - A combination of catfish and crab cooked in coconut milk and served in a young coconut shell.

Local Beverages

  • Tuba - A coconut wine fermented in bamboo or plastic bottles and served with skewered meat.
  • Buko Juice - Fresh coconut water straight from the husk, served chilled.
  • Barako Coffee - A strong, locally sourced coffee variant with a rich aroma and taste.

Where to Stay in Calbayog: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Ciriaco Hotel and Resort - A luxury hotel with a pool, garden, and beachfront location.
  • Calbayog Pensionne House - A budget-friendly option with clean and comfortable rooms.
  • Orbit Inn - A mid-range guesthouse conveniently located in the city center.

What to Buy in Calbayog: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Banig - A woven mat made from dried palm leaf strips used for sleeping or as a floor covering.
  • Abaca Cloth - A durable, handwoven fabric produced from the fibers of the abaca plant.
  • Coconut Shell Crafts - A variety of handicrafts made from coconut shells such as bowls, candle holders, and jewelry.

Local Culture and Customs of Calbayog

Calbayog is known for its warm, friendly people and vibrant cultural celebrations. Major festivals include the Feast of St. Peter and Paul in June and the Banigan-Kawayan Festival in September.

Best Time to Visit Calbayog

The best time to visit Calbayog is from November to April when temperatures are cooler, humidity is lower, and rainfall is minimal.

Do’s and Don’ts of Calbayog

Do:

  • Respect local customs and traditions.
  • Try local delicacies and cuisines.
  • Take time to explore the natural attractions of Calbayog.

Don’t:

  • Wear revealing clothing outside of the beach or resort areas.
  • Forget to bring insect repellent, sunscreen, and protective clothing when going outdoors.
  • Litter in public areas.

Safety and Health

Safety in Calbayog

Calbayog is a generally safe city, but travelers are advised to take precautions and be aware of their surroundings. Avoid unlit areas at night and be cautious when traveling to remote natural attractions.

Health in Calbayog

Be mindful of the local food and water, as poor sanitation may lead to gastrointestinal problems. It’s important to stay hydrated and bring necessary medication for any pre-existing conditions.

Getting to Calbayog: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Calbayog

The nearest airport to Calbayog is the Daniel Z. Romualdez Airport in Tacloban City, which is around a three-hour drive away.

Bus Stations Near Calbayog

Several bus companies operate daily trips to/from Manila, Cebu, and other parts of the Visayas region. Buses depart from the Calbayog Bus Terminal located in the city center.

Other Options

Chartered flights and private vehicles can also be arranged from Tacloban City or other nearby cities.

How to Get Around in Calbayog

Tricycles and jeepneys are the most common mode of transportation in Calbayog. Taxis and private cars are also available for rent.

Where to Go Next from Calbayog: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Catarman, Northern Samar - Rich in history and natural resources, Catarman is just a two-hour drive from Calbayog and is known for its beaches, hot springs, and churches.
  • Tacloban City, Leyte - The provincial capital of Leyte is located around three hours from Calbayog and offers a variety of cultural attractions, museums, parks, and shopping centers.

Conclusion

Calbayog is a hidden gem among the Philippines’ many destinations. From historic landmarks to natural attractions, the city promises a memorable and fulfilling travel experience. With this comprehensive travel guide, make the most out of your stay in Calbayog and uncover all the beauty and charm that this city has to offer.
