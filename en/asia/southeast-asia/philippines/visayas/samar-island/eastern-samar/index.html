Introduction to Eastern Samar

Eastern Samar is a province located in Samar Island, which is in the Visayas region of the Philippines. It is blessed with dramatic coastlines, pristine beaches, and rich biodiversity. The province is home to a diverse range of flora and fauna that includes rare and endangered species. Eastern Samar is also known for its vibrant cultural scene, including colorful festivals and traditional dances.

List of Places to Visit in Eastern Samar

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

When it comes to cultural attractions, Eastern Samar has a lot to offer. Visitors can explore historical churches, park and museum complex, and experience the culture and customs of the locals.

One of the most famous cultural attractions in the province is the Lungsodaan Point Lighthouse in Guiuan. It is the second-highest lighthouse in the Philippines, standing 164 feet tall, and it offers breathtaking views of the Pacific Ocean. Another must-visit destination is the Balangiga Encounter Memorial Shrine, which commemorates the Philippine-American War battle that happened in 1901.

For those interested in history, the Guiuan Museum and Archives is worth visiting. It features a vast collection of historical artifacts that give insights into the province’s past. The Holy Rosary Parish Church in Borongan, on the other hand, is an excellent example of colonial architecture and needs to be added to your traveling record.

Natural Attractions of Eastern Samar: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Eastern Samar is a paradise for nature lovers, with a vast array of natural attractions that visitors should not miss. One of the most famous natural attractions is the Sohoton Caves and Natural Bridge National Park. The park is home to fascinating underground caves, emerald green lagoons, and mangroves. It is also known for hosting eco-adventure activities like kayaking, cave exploring, and island hopping.

Another famous destination is the Divinubo Island, a tiny island in Borongan, known for its pristine white sand beaches and crystal clear waters. The island is perfect for a day trip or picnicking with loved ones. Apart from Divinubo Island, another famous beach in the province is Sulangan Beach. It is a secluded beach, famous for its fine white sand and crystal clear water.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Eastern Samar

Eastern Samar is well-known for its stunning coastal roads, providing ideal routes for hiking and biking enthusiasts. The most famous place for hiking/biking is the San Pedro Bay. Its amenities and latest developments for bikers and hiking enthusiasts make it an ideal location to start and finish your hike/bike tour.

Best Things to Do in Eastern Samar: Activities and Tours

Eastern Samar is the perfect getaway for those who seek adventure and excitement. Visitors can explore stunning attractions like the Balangiga Encounter Memorial Shrine. They can also go for snorkeling at the Sulangan Beach to go on an island and cave hopping at the Sohoton Caves and Natural Bridge National Park.

Week-Long Itinerary for Eastern Samar

Day 1: Explore Guiuan and visit the Lungsodaan Point Lighthouse Day 2: Visit the Sohoton Caves and Natural Bridge National Park for cave exploring, kayaking, and island hopping. Day 3: Explore the Holy Rosary Parish Church in Borongan Day 4: Go for a day trip to Divinubo Island and enjoy its pristine beaches. Day 5: Hike or bike around San Pedro Bay. Day 6: Go for snorkeling in Sulangan Beach. Day 7: Spend a relaxing day at the stunning Guinticgan Island.

Food and Drink Scene of Eastern Samar

Must-Try Dishes

Eastern Samar’s food scene is a mix of local and international cuisine. When it comes to local dishes, visitors have to try the tinikling dish. It is a local dish consisting of pork marinated in a blend of herbs and spices and then grilled to perfection. The balu-ca-gat stands for coconut crabs locally caught and served fresh, making it another special delicacy worth a try.

Local Beverages

The province is also known for a wide range of refreshing local beverages. Visitors have to try the fresh coconut juice, which is rich, sweet, and nutritious. They should also try the Binagol, a traditional delicacy made with taro root, coconut, and condensed milk

Where to Stay in Eastern Samar: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Eastern Samar offers a wide range of accommodation options that cater to all budgets and preferences. For luxury travelers, the Belrence Bay Resort & Spa is a great option with six stunning hillside villas and beachfront suites. For backpackers, United Tourist Inn, and SLO Lodge offer affordable accommodation options.

What to Buy in Eastern Samar: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Eastern Samar has a rich arts and crafts scene, and visitors can buy traditional handicrafts, clothing, and other local products as souvenirs. One of the best places to find souvenirs is the town of San Julian, which is known for its local embroidery. Guests can also find handmade crafts in various shops located throughout the province.

Local Culture and Customs of Eastern Samar

Eastern Samar locals have a rich culture and customs, which visitors should not miss experiencing. The locals are known for their traditional dances, colorful festivals, and music. The province celebrates the Salcedo Anniversary, featuring cultural and sporting activities like the Tinikling Festival to pay tribute to the town’s patron saint, St. John.

Best Time to Visit Eastern Samar

The best time to visit Eastern Samar is during the dry season between November and May. During these months, the weather is pleasant, and visitors can enjoy the outdoor recreational activities and attractions in the province.

Do’s and Don’ts of Eastern Samar

Do:

  • Respect local customs and traditions.
  • Explore the natural attractions and beautiful beaches.
  • Experience the local festivals.

Don’t:

  • Litter on beaches or other natural attractions.
  • Disrespect the locals or their culture.
  • Take pictures without permission.

Safety and Health

Safety in Eastern Samar

Eastern Samar is generally considered to be a safe destination. However, visitors should be cautious when in unfamiliar areas, especially at night.

Health in Eastern Samar

Visitors to Eastern Samar should obtain travel and health insurance before traveling to the province. They should also bring mosquito repellent to avoid contracting mosquito-borne diseases like dengue fever.

Getting to Eastern Samar: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Eastern Samar

The nearest airports to Eastern Samar are the Catarman and Borongan airports. They have daily flights from Manila and Cebu.

Bus Stations Near Eastern Samar

Visitors can also reach Eastern Samar by bus from Manila to Tacloban. From Tacloban, they can take another bus to their preferred destination.

Other Options

Tourists can also take a private car or book a ride-sharing service to reach Eastern Samar.

How to Get Around in Eastern Samar

Visitors can explore Eastern Samar’s attractions by hiring a car, motorcycle, boat, or a jeep. There are also public transportation options, such as buses, vans, and jeepneys.

Where to Go Next from Eastern Samar: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Visitors can explore nearby destinations like Cebu, Dumaguete, and Siargao Island after their Eastern Samar adventure.

Conclusion

Eastern Samar is a beautiful and diverse province that has something to offer to every type of traveler. From stunning beaches to cultural attractions, there is plenty to see and experience in this part of the Philippines. Plan your trip well and enjoy your Eastern Samar adventure to the fullest.
