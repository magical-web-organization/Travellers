If you are looking for an off-the-beaten-track destination, Borongan is a hidden gem that you must explore. Located on the eastern coast of Samar Island in the Visayas region of the Philippines, this quaint town is a perfect destination for all kinds of travelers. From natural beauty to cultural heritage, Borongan has something for everyone. Plan your visit with this comprehensive guide that includes everything you need to know about the town.

Introduction to Borongan

Borongan is the capital town of Eastern Samar, a province known for its rugged beauty and unique culture. The town is situated on the east coast of Samar Island facing the Pacific Ocean and is a gateway to various natural attractions in the region. It is a small town with a laid-back vibe and traditional Filipino charm that attracts visitors from all over the world.

List of Places to Visit in Borongan

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

If you are interested in the history and culture of Borongan, there are several places that you should visit. Here are some of the top attractions:

  • Our Lady of the Nativity Church: This church is an important religious and cultural landmark of Borongan. It is a historic church dating back to the Spanish colonial era and has a unique design with a mix of Baroque and Filipino style.

  • Borongan Municipal Museum: This museum showcases the history and culture of Borongan and its neighboring towns. You can see several displays of artifacts, photos, and documents that reflect the vibrant cultural heritage of the region.

  • Borongan Ancestral Houses: Borongan has several old houses that showcase the traditional architecture and design of the region. Some of the most notable ones include Lim-Bacal Ancestral House, Zaragoza Ancestral House, and Del Rosario Ancestral House.

Natural Attractions of Borongan: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

If you love nature and outdoor adventures, Borongan won’t disappoint you. Here are some of the top natural attractions that you should explore:

  • Divinubo Island: This small island is just a short boat ride away from Borongan town and is a perfect destination for those who love nature and swimming. You can also rent a kayak and explore the island’s mangrove forest.

  • Anda Beach: This white sandy beach is one of the most stunning beaches in the region and offers clear blue waters and breathtaking views of the Pacific Ocean.

  • Hegyon Falls: This waterfall is located deep in the forest and can be reached through a hiking trail. It is a perfect destination for adventure seekers and nature lovers who want to explore the countryside.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Borongan

Borongan offers several hiking and biking trails that are perfect for fitness enthusiasts and adventure seekers. Some of the top routes include:

  • Mt. Aminduen Hike: This hiking trail is challenging but worth it for the stunning views of Borongan and the Pacific Ocean from the summit.

  • Biking Loop: This route takes you through several scenic villages and coconut plantations around Borongan and is perfect for a leisurely bike ride.

Best Things to Do in Borongan: Activities and Tours

Besides the natural and cultural attractions mentioned above, there are several other things to do in Borongan that are worth exploring. Here are some of the top activities and tours:

  • Island Hopping Tour: You can take a boat ride and explore the nearby islands and islets of Borongan, including Sabang Island and Calicoan Island.

  • Scuba Diving and Snorkeling: Borongan has several diving and snorkeling spots that offer crystal-clear waters, diverse marine life, and stunning coral reefs.

Week-Long Itinerary for Borongan

If you are planning a week-long trip to Borongan and want to explore the best of the town, here is a suggested itinerary:

Day Activity
Day 1 Explore the town center and visit Our Lady of the Nativity Church and Borongan Municipal Museum.
Day 2 Go island hopping and visit Divinubo Island and Sabang Island.
Day 3 Hike to Mt. Aminduen and enjoy the stunning views.
Day 4 Visit the Hegyon Falls and take a dip in the refreshing water.
Day 5 Go scuba diving or snorkeling and explore the underwater world of Borongan.
Day 6 Take a bike ride around the town and nearby villages.
Day 7 Relax at Anda Beach and enjoy the stunning sunset views over the Pacific Ocean.

Food and Drink Scene of Borongan

Borongan has a unique culinary scene that reflects the region’s traditional flavors and influences. Here are some of the top must-try dishes and local beverages that we recommend:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Binagol: This dessert is made of taro, coconut, condensed milk, and peanuts and is cooked in a coconut shell.

  • Suman: This sweet delicacy is made of sticky rice, coconut milk, and sugar and is usually wrapped in banana leaves.

Local Beverages

  • Gamay: This local alcoholic drink is made of coconut sap and is a unique drink that you must try.

  • Baye-Baye: This drink is made of sticky rice, coconut milk, and sugar and is a refreshing beverage that is perfect for hot summer days.

Where to Stay in Borongan: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Borongan offers various accommodation options that suit all kinds of budgets. Here are some of the top recommended places to stay:

  • Golden Sand Inn: This hotel is located near Anda Beach and offers spacious rooms, great amenities, and stunning views of the ocean.

  • Wakatobi Inn: This guesthouse is located in the town center and offers comfortable rooms, friendly staff, and a great location.

What to Buy in Borongan: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

If you’re looking for souvenirs and local products to bring home from Borongan, here are some of the top things to buy:

  • Abaca Products: Borongan is known for its high-quality abaca products, including bags, hats, and baskets.

  • Tinagak: This local delicacy is made of rice, coconut, and sugar and is a perfect gift for foodies.

Local Culture and Customs of Borongan

Borongan is a traditional Filipino town with a unique culture that reflects the region’s history and influences. The locals are friendly, hospitable, and take pride in their town’s culture and heritage. It is best to learn about the local customs and norms before visiting the town to be respectful and avoid offending anyone.

Best Time to Visit Borongan

The best time to visit Borongan is between December and May when the weather is dry and sunny, and the sea is calm. This period is perfect for outdoor adventures, beach activities, and exploring the town’s cultural and natural attractions.

Do’s and Don’ts of Borongan

Here are some of the do’s and don’ts to keep in mind while visiting Borongan:

Do:

  • Respect the local culture and customs.

  • Be courteous and friendly to the locals.

  • Try the local food and beverages.

Don’t:

  • Engage in activities that harm the environment or wildlife.

  • Disrespect local beliefs or traditions.

  • Leave trash or litter on beaches or natural sites.

Safety and Health

Borongan is generally a safe and peaceful town, but it is always advisable to take basic precautions to ensure your safety and health. Here are some tips to keep in mind:

Safety in Borongan

  • Avoid carrying valuable items or large sums of cash.

  • Stay aware of your surroundings, especially at night.

  • Use a reliable transportation service and avoid getting into unmarked or unlicensed vehicles.

Health in Borongan

  • Drink bottled water and avoid tap water.

  • Use a mosquito repellent to avoid mosquito-borne diseases.

  • Be cautious while trying new food and drinks to avoid an upset stomach.

Getting to Borongan: Transportation Options and Logistics

Borongan is well-connected with various regions through air, land, and sea transportation. Here are some of the options:

Airports Near Borongan

  • Borongan Airport: This is the nearest airport to Borongan, and it serves domestic flights to and from Manila and Cebu.

Bus Stations Near Borongan

  • Borongan Bus Terminal: This bus terminal is located in the town center and offers several bus services to different destinations in the region.

Other Options

  • Ferry: Borongan is accessible by ferry from Tacloban and other nearby ports.

How to Get Around in Borongan

Borongan is a small town that can easily be covered on foot or by renting a tricycle. You can also rent a bike or a motorbike to explore the nearby villages and attractions.

Where to Go Next from Borongan: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

If you have more time to explore the region, here are some of the top destinations and excursions that you can consider:

  • Calicoan Island: This island is a paradise for surfers and nature lovers and offers stunning beaches, mangrove forests, and wildlife.

  • Guiuan: This town is just a short drive away from Borongan and offers beautiful beaches, waterfalls, and historic landmarks.

Conclusion

Borongan is a perfect destination for travelers who want to explore the beauty and culture of Eastern Samar. From stunning beaches to natural attractions and cultural landmarks, this underrated town has something for everyone. We hope this guide helps you plan your trip to Borongan and explore the best of what the town has to offer.
