Introduction to Calbiga

Calbiga is a laid-back municipality on the eastern coast of Samar Island in the Visayas region of the Philippines. It is home to remarkable natural beauty, rich cultural heritage, and hospitable locals. With its diverse tourist attractions, Calbiga offers visitors a glimpse of a unique and authentic travel experience that cannot be found in the usual tourist hotspots.

List of Places to Visit in Calbiga

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Calbiga Ancestral House: Step back in time and visit this 120-year-old ancestral house that showcases the traditional architecture and culture of the Waray people.
  • Calbiga Church: One of the oldest churches in the region, this one boasts of its Baroque style architecture that dates back to the 18th century.
  • Matuguinao Cemetery: A burial ground that is over a century old and features intricate, well-preserved tombstones.

Natural Attractions of Calbiga: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Lulugayan Falls: Hike through the rainforest and discover a magnificent 50-meter drop waterfall that cascades into clear, emerald green pool.
  • Pancil and Bugtong Bato Caves: Get lost in the underground world of these two cave systems, with stalagmites and stalactites that have been forming for centuries.
  • Sohoton Natural Bridge and National Park: Discover plant and animal species that can only be found in the Philippines in this stunning ecotourism hotspot which features cave systems, lagoons, and rock formations.
  • Mount Urdaneta: For those who enjoy trekking, a hike up to the peak of the mountain promises an exceptional 360-degree view of the town and its neighboring terrains.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Calbiga

  • Mount Igcuron: The trek to the peak of Mount Igcuron is worth the climb as the panoramic view from the summit is simply breathtaking.
  • Cave Cluster Trail: This 17-kilometer trek explores the cave systems of Calbiga and takes you through 8 different caves, each with its unique geological features.
  • Talahid Trail: For biking enthusiasts, take on the challenge of the long and winding Talahid Trail and enjoy the picturesque view of the surrounding mountains.

Best Things to Do in Calbiga: Activities and Tours

  • Sohoton Cave Adventure: A once-in-a-lifetime experience that takes you on a tour of the cave systems of Sohoton Natural Bridge and National Park, aboard a small boat.
  • Falls Hopping Tour: Visitors can explore multiple majestic waterfalls, including Lulugayan Falls, as well as forest walks, river crossings, and cliff jumping.
  • Organic Farm Tour: Take a tour of the organic farms in Calbiga and learn more about how the locals live and farm sustainably.

Food and Drink Scene of Calbiga

Must-Try Dishes

  • Chicken Tinola: A traditional Filipino soup made with chicken, ginger, and green papaya.
  • Adobong Pusit: Squid cooked in vinegar, soy sauce, and other spices.
  • Lechon Baboy: Roasted whole pig.

Local Beverages

  • Coconut Wine (Tuba): An alcoholic drink made from the fermented sap of coconut flowers.
  • Barako Coffee: A strong, locally grown coffee, with the signature robust flavor that Filipinos love.

Where to Stay in Calbiga: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Jing’s Homestay and Lulugayan Falls Accommodation: A homestay that is only a few minutes away from the Lulugayan Falls, offering comfortable accommodation and a range of meals.
  • Agta Beach Resort: A beachfront resort with a restaurant that serves local cuisine.
  • Bahay Kubo Homestay: A traditional bamboo home, surrounded by beautiful gardens in the heart of Calbiga.

What to Buy in Calbiga: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Handwoven Mats: Made by the locals using traditional methods, these beautiful and colorful mats are unique to the area.
  • Coconut Shell Crafts: Locals in Samar Island are crafty and resourceful, which is why you’ll find coconut shell crafts around Calbiga.
  • Waray-Waray Costume: This traditional Filipino costume is made of rich and vibrant fabrics and is distinct to the Waray people of Samar.

Local Culture and Customs of Calbiga

The people of Calbiga are welcoming and hospitable to visitors. Giving food or small gifts to the locals is a way of showing respect. Just like in other Filipino cultures, “pagmamano” or kissing the back of the hand of elders is a sign of respect in Calbiga.

Best Time to Visit Calbiga

The best time to visit Calbiga is between November and May when the weather is dry and sunny. The rainy season is from June to October when monsoon rains hit the region.

Do’s and Don’ts of Calbiga

Do:

  • Respect local customs and traditions.
  • Ask permission before taking photos of people or religious sites.
  • Spent time in the natural attractions of Calbiga.

Don’t:

  • Litter in the town or natural attractions.
  • Overstay your welcome in someone else’s home.
  • Damage the local ecosystem or wildlife.

Safety and Health

Safety in Calbiga

Calbiga is a small and peaceful town that poses little, if any, danger to visitors. However, it is important to take the usual precautions when traveling, like securing your personal belongings and money.

Health in Calbiga

There are some hospitals in nearby towns like Catbalogan, but it is recommended that visitors bring their own medication and necessary medical supplies with them.

Getting to Calbiga: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Calbiga

The nearest major airport to Calbiga is Tacloban Daniel Z. Romualdez Airport, which is about 93 km away.

Bus Stations Near Calbiga

From Manila, there are daily flights to Tacloban and Cebu. From there, you can take a van or bus to Calbiga.

Other Options

Private vans and cars can also be hired for more convenience and direct travel to Calbiga.

How to Get Around in Calbiga

Jeepneys and tricycles are the main mode of transportation in the area. Renting a car or motorbike is also an option, which is ideal for longer trips and hikes.

Where to Go Next from Calbiga: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Caving in Capul Island: Visit the limestone caves of Capul Island and discover unique rock formations and crystal-clear waters.
  • Surfing in Calicoan Island: This island has earned a reputation as a surfing hotspot in the Philippines, with a consistently strong wave and a laid-back surfer vibe.
  • Island-hopping in Cuatro Islas: Hop on a boat and explore the four small islands that make up Cuatro Islas.

Conclusion

Calbiga may be a small town in Samar Island, but it offers a truly unique and memorable travel experience. Its stunning natural beauty, rich culture, and hospitable locals make it a hidden gem of the Philippines. From cave exploration to waterfall hikes, organic farm tours to traditional cuisine, Calbiga has something for everyone. Plan your next trip to Calbiga and explore one of the Philippines’ best-kept secrets.
