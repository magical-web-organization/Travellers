Introduction to Northern Samar

Located in Samar Island, in the Visayas region of the Philippines, Northern Samar is a hidden gem waiting to be explored. It boasts stunning natural attractions, a rich cultural heritage, and warm and hospitable locals.

List of Places to Visit in Northern Samar

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • St. Bartholomew Church - A well-preserved Spanish-era church with baroque architecture located in the town of San Roque.
  • Catarman Church Ruins and Museum - A museum that displays artifacts and remnants of the 16th-century church that was destroyed by a volcanic eruption in the 19th century.
  • Capul Island Lighthouse - A Spanish-era lighthouse standing tall on Capul Island, overlooking the San Bernardino Strait.
  • Lavezares Fort - A 17th-century fort that was built to protect the island from pirates and foreign invaders.
  • Lopez Jaena Park and Monument - A park and monument dedicated to Graciano Lopez Jaena, a journalist and national hero who fought for Filipino independence from Spain.

Natural Attractions of Northern Samar: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Calicoan Island - A small island perfect for surfing, island hopping, and beach bumming.
  • Biri Island Rock Formations - A natural wonder featuring towering rock formations created by the strong waves crashing into the island’s shores.
  • Mapanas Cold and Hot Spring - A natural hot spring that boasts therapeutic properties and a cold spring that offers a refreshing dip in its crystal-clear waters.
  • Dao Waterfalls - A series of cascading waterfalls nestled amidst lush greenery and stunning rock formations.
  • Sohoton Natural Bridge National Park - A majestic natural wonder that features a towering rock formation that spans over Sohoton River.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Northern Samar

  • Calbayog City Bike Route - A scenic route that takes bikers through the picturesque landscapes of Calbayog City.
  • Pundaguitan Falls Hiking Trail - A moderate level hiking trail that leads to the stunning Pundaguitan Falls.
  • Mt. Kapayahan and Batag Peak Hiking Trail - A challenging trek that takes hikers to the summit of Mt. Kapayahan and Batag Peak, offering panoramic views of the surrounding mountains and valleys.

Best Things to Do in Northern Samar: Activities and Tours

  • Island Hopping - Explore the many islands of Northern Samar, including Calicoan Island and Capul Island.
  • Surfing - Catch some waves on the beaches of Calicoan Island, known for its world-class surfing spots.
  • Visit the Local Markets - Check out the local markets and taste the many local delicacies of Northern Samar.
  • Hiking and Trekking - Discover the beauty of Northern Samar’s natural attractions by hiking or trekking its many trails.
  • Cultural Tours - Visit the many museums and historical sites that showcase Northern Samar’s rich cultural heritage.

Food and Drink Scene of Northern Samar

Must-Try Dishes

  • Tinagtag - A local delicacy made of sticky rice mixed with coconut milk and wrapped in banana leaves.
  • Binagol - A sweet treat made with taro, coconut milk, and sugar, wrapped in banana leaves and steamed to perfection.
  • Suman - A traditional Filipino rice cake wrapped in banana leaves and served with coconut jam or syrup.
  • Pancit Lomi - A hearty noodle soup made with thick egg noodles, meat, and vegetables, best enjoyed on a chilly day.

Local Beverages

  • Tuba - A local alcoholic drink made from coconut sap.
  • Barako Coffee - A type of coffee grown in the region known for its strong, bold flavor.
  • Tanglad Juice - A refreshing drink made with lemongrass, calamansi, and honey.

Where to Stay in Northern Samar: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • GV Hotel Catarman - A budget hotel located in Catarman town center.
  • Calicoan Villa Resort - A beachfront resort located in Calicoan Island, perfect for surfers and beach enthusiasts.
  • At the Woods Resort - A secluded resort nestled amidst lush greenery, offering a peaceful retreat away from the hustle and bustle of the city.

What to Buy in Northern Samar: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Banig - A locally made mat made from pandan leaves, perfect for picnics or as a decorative piece at home.
  • Binakol - A hand-woven fabric made from abaca fibers, traditionally used for clothing or home décor.
  • Sungka - A board game made of wood, perfect for bringing to family gatherings or game nights.

Local Culture and Customs of Northern Samar

  • Religion - Most of the locals in Northern Samar practice Roman Catholicism, and religious festivals and processions are common in the region.
  • Hospitality - The people of Northern Samar are known for their warm and hospitable nature, and it is customary for them to welcome visitors with open arms.
  • Language - The locals speak a mix of Waray and Cebuano, but many also speak Tagalog and English.

Best Time to Visit Northern Samar

The best time to visit Northern Samar is from November to May when the weather is dry and perfect for outdoor activities. The region experiences rainy season from June to October, which may affect some travel plans.

Do’s and Don’ts of Northern Samar

Do:

  • Respect the local culture and traditions.
  • Try the local delicacies and participate in cultural festivities.
  • Enjoy the stunning natural attractions and outdoor activities that Northern Samar has to offer.

Don’t:

  • Wear revealing clothing when visiting religious sites or festivals.
  • Disrespect the locals or their customs.
  • Engage in any illegal activities, such as drug use or trafficking.

Safety and Health

Safety in Northern Samar

While Northern Samar is generally safe for tourists, it is always best to take necessary precautions such as keeping valuable items secure, sticking to well-known tourist areas and avoiding traveling alone at night.

Health in Northern Samar

Northern Samar has a few public hospitals and medical centers, but it is recommended for tourists to have travel insurance and bring any necessary medication and medical supplies with them during their visit.

Getting to Northern Samar: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Northern Samar

  • Catarman National Airport - The main airport serving Northern Samar, located in Catarman town.

Bus Stations Near Northern Samar

  • Van Terminal in Calbayog City - A popular transport hub that services buses and vans from Manila and other cities.

Other Options

  • Ferry - Ferries are available from Matnog, Sorsogon, and arrive in Allen, Northern Samar.

How to Get Around in Northern Samar

Tourists can easily move around Northern Samar through jeepneys and tricycles. Taxis are also available but are more expensive.

Where to Go Next from Northern Samar: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Bicol - Just a short ferry ride away, Bicol is home to the world-famous Mayon Volcano, spectacular beaches, and delicious cuisine.
  • The Visayan Islands - Explore the many beautiful islands and beaches of the Visayas region, including Boracay, Cebu, and Bohol.

Conclusion

Northern Samar may not be as popular as its neighboring provinces, but it offers a unique travel experience that combines stunning natural attractions and rich cultural heritage. With this comprehensive guide in hand, you’re sure to have an unforgettable trip filled with adventure, relaxation, and new discoveries.
