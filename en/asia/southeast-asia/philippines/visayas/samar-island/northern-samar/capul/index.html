Introduction to Capul

Capul is a small, picturesque island municipality located in the northernmost part of the Samar province in the Visayas region of the Philippines. Known for its rich history, stunning natural beauty, and unique culture, Capul is a must-visit destination for any traveler who seeks to explore the hidden gems of the Philippines.

Local Culture and Customs of Capul

Capul is known for its deep Catholic faith and unique local traditions. Visitors are advised to respect local customs, including attire and behavior when attending religious ceremonies.

Best Time to Visit Capul

The best time to visit Capul is during the dry season from December to May.

Do’s and Don’ts of Capul

Do:

  1. Respect local culture and customs
  2. Pack sunscreen and insect repellant
  3. Try the local cuisine

Don’t:

  1. Wear revealing clothing when visiting churches or other religious sites
  2. Litter or damage natural sites
  3. Disrespect elders or authority figures

Safety and Health

Safety in Capul

Capul is generally a safe place for tourists. However, visitors should take the usual precautions when traveling, especially when going to remote areas or hiking trails.

Health in Capul

It is recommended that visitors get vaccinated against typhoid, hepatitis A, and tetanus before traveling to Capul. Also, make sure to bring any necessary medications and insect repellant.

Getting to Capul: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Capul

The nearest airport to Capul is the Catarman National Airport, located about 70 kilometers south of Capul. From there, you can take a van or bus to Allen Port and then ferry to Capul.

Bus Stations Near Capul

Buses from Manila and other parts of the Philippines stop at the Allen Bus Terminal, which is located at the Allen Port. From there, you can take a ferry to Capul.

Other Options

Visitors can also take a direct ferry from Matnog in Sorsogon province to San Isidro in Northern Samar, which is about 25 kilometers from Capul. Another option is to take a private car or van from Manila or other nearby provinces to Allen Port and then take a ferry to Capul.

How to Get Around in Capul

The best way to get around Capul is on foot, as the island is relatively small. Visitors can also hire a tricycle or motorcycle for a more comfortable mode of transportation.
