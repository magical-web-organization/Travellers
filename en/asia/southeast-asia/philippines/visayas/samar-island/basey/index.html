Introduction to Basey

Basey is a quaint coastal town situated on the eastern coast of Samar Island in the Eastern Visayas region of the Philippines. Known for its picturesque landscapes, rich culture and history, Basey is an ideal retreat for anyone looking to escape the hustle and bustle of city life.

The town is home to some of the nation’s most exquisite cultural and natural wonders, which include historical landmarks, pristine beaches, scenic views, and outdoor adventures. Whether you’re an eager traveler or an avid explorer, Basey has plenty of attractions that cater to your interests and preferences.

List of Places to Visit in Basey

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Basey Church

The Basey Church is one of the most renowned historical landmarks in the town. Built in 1842, the church is a beautiful example of colonial-era architecture. Its walls are adorned with intricate carvings and paintings that recount the town’s religious heritage.

Sohoton Caves

The Sohoton Caves are a network of underground caves located in the foothills of the Sohoton Natural Bridge National Park. The caves were formed over millions of years and are popular among tourists for their breathtaking stalactites and stalagmites, underground rivers, and natural rock formations.

Tingib Beach

Tingib Beach is a serene beach that is known for its soft, white sands and clear waters. It’s a perfect place to relax, unwind, and soak up the sun.

Natural Attractions of Basey: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Sohoton Natural Bridge National Park

The Sohoton Natural Bridge National Park is a must-visit attraction in Basey. It is a protected area that is home to a vast array of flora and fauna, including rare bird species, colorful butterflies, and monkey-eating eagles. Activities in the park include trekking, birdwatching, and swimming.

Pan-as Falls

The Pan-as Falls is a three-tiered waterfall that is nestled deep within the jungles of Basey. The falls cascade over large boulders and are surrounded by lush greenery, making it an ideal spot for nature lovers.

San Rafael River Adventure

The San Rafael River Adventure is a perfect outdoor adventure for thrill-seekers. Activities offered here include kayaking, zip-lining, and rope courses, all while taking in the picturesque views of the Basey River.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Basey

Tarangban Falls

The Tarangban Falls is a beautiful waterfall that is located within the Tarangban Falls Ecopark. The park is an ideal destination for hiking and bike enthusiasts, as it boasts several trails that offer stunning views of the surrounding jungles and waterfalls.

Best Things to Do in Basey: Activities and Tours

Explore the Local Art Scene

Basey is home to many talented artists who produce exquisite artwork. The local art scene is worth exploring, and some of the best places to start include the Artist Village and the Balangiga Encounter Museum.

Visit the Local Markets

Basey’s markets are a must-visit destination for anyone interested in local culture and food. The markets offer everything from fresh seafood to traditional handicrafts.

Take a River Cruise

The Basey River is an ideal destination for nature lovers and adventurers. A river cruise along the Basey River offers excellent views of the surrounding scenery, including mangrove forests, natural rock formations, and wildlife.

Food and Drink Scene of Basey

Must-Try Dishes

Kinilaw

Kinilaw is a traditional fish dish that is typically served as an appetizer. It is made with fresh fish, vinegar, lime juice, and various herbs and spices.

Adobong Manok

Adobong Manok is a popular Filipino chicken dish that is cooked in soy sauce, vinegar, garlic, and pepper. It is usually served with steamed rice.

Local Beverages

Coconut Wine

Coconut wine, or “tuba,” is a popular beverage in the Philippines. It is made from the sap of coconut trees and is known for its sweet, tangy taste.

Barako Coffee

Barako coffee is a native coffee that is grown in Batangas, Philippines. It is known for its strong, bold flavor.

What to Buy in Basey: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Basey is known for its handicrafts, including woven mats and bags, and wooden crafts. Some good places to buy souvenirs are the local markets or the Artist Village. You can also buy locally produced food products such as coconut jam and cashew nuts.

Local Culture and Customs of Basey

Basey is a traditional town that has preserved its religious and cultural heritage over the years. The locals are known for their hospitality, friendliness, and warmth towards tourists. Respect for local customs and traditions is essential when visiting Basey.

Best Time to Visit Basey

The best time to visit Basey is between November and May when the weather is dry and sunny. If you’re a nature enthusiast, then the dry season is perfect for outdoor activities such as hiking, swimming, and river cruises.

Do’s and Don’ts of Basey

Do:

  • Respect the locals and their customs.
  • Try the local delicacies and drinks.
  • Explore the natural and cultural wonders of Basey.

Don’t:

  • Engage in activities that harm the environment or wildlife.
  • Disrespect local customs or traditions.
  • Litter or damage natural or cultural attractions.

Safety and Health

Safety in Basey

Basey is generally a safe destination for travelers. However, travelers are advised to take standard safety precautions such as avoiding walking alone at night and keeping valuable items securely.

Health in Basey

Travelers are advised to take precautions against mosquito bites as they can transmit diseases such as dengue fever and malaria. It is also recommended to drink bottled water or boil tap water before drinking.

Getting to Basey: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Basey

The nearest major airport to Basey is the Daniel Z. Romualdez Airport in Tacloban City, which is about 84 km away. From there, you can take a taxi or rental car to Basey.

Bus Stations Near Basey

Basey can be accessed by bus from major cities in the region, including Tacloban, Ormoc, and Catbalogan.

Other Options

Ferries are also available from various locations in the Philippines to nearby ports in Samar Island, including Calbayog City and Catbalogan City.

How to Get Around in Basey

The best way to get around Basey is by vehicle or motorcycle rental. Tricycles are also available for shorter trips around town. Guided tours are also an option for travelers who want to explore the town’s attractions.

Where to Go Next from Basey: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Basey is an ideal starting point for exploring the rest of Samar Island. Some nearby destinations and excursions include:

  • Calbayog City
  • Samar Island Natural Park
  • Catbalogan City

Conclusion

Basey is a hidden gem that boasts a fascinating blend of natural beauty, rich history, and local culture. This comprehensive travel guide has everything you need to plan a memorable trip to Basey. From hiking and biking routes to cultural attractions and local delicacies, Basey has plenty to offer every type of traveler.
