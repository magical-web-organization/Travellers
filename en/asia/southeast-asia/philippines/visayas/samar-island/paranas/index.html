A Comprehensive Travel Guide to Paranas, Samar Island, Philippines

Paranas, located in the heart of Samar Island in Visayas, Philippines, is a hidden gem waiting to be explored. This small town offers a unique combination of cultural heritage, natural wonders, and adrenaline-pumping outdoor adventures. Paranas is a popular destination for backpackers, nature lovers, hikers, and beach bums. In this comprehensive travel guide, we will explore the top attractions, activities, food, accommodations, and more in Paranas.

Introduction to Paranas

Paranas, formerly known as Wright, is a fifth-class municipality of Samar Island. It is a coastal town that boasts a rich history, diverse culture, and stunning natural beauty. The town has a population of over 12,000 people, who are predominantly engaged in agriculture and fishing. Paranas is located 69 kilometers west of Samar’s capital, Catbalogan. The town is accessible by land and air through various modes of transportation.

List of Places to Visit in Paranas

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Paranas is a town that has a rich cultural heritage, which is evident in its museums and historical places. Here are some of the top cultural attractions in Paranas:

  • Paranas Museum: This museum is a must-visit for visitors who want to learn more about the town’s history. It houses various artifacts, documents, and photos that depict the town’s culture and heritage.
  • San Francisco Javier Church: Known as the “Parish of the Immaculate Conception,” this church is one of the major historical landmarks in Paranas. It was established in the 19th century and has undergone several renovations throughout the years.
  • Cathedral Cave: One of the popular tourist spots in Paranas is the Cathedral Cave, which is located near the Paranas Museum. It is a natural limestone cave that has an altar inside, making it a unique religious attraction.

Natural Attractions of Paranas: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Paranas is a town that is blessed with natural wonders, making it a paradise for outdoor enthusiasts. Here are some of the top natural attractions in Paranas:

  • Langun-Gobingob Caves: This cave system is one of the largest in Asia and is popular among spelunkers and adventure seekers. The cave system has an underground river, magnificent stalactites, and stalagmites, making it a must-visit attraction in Paranas.
  • Sohoton Natural Bridge: This natural bridge is a scenic wonder that connects two mountains. It is located near the Sohoton Caves and Natural Bridge National Park, making it a perfect side trip for travelers who love nature.
  • Sulpan Cave: Another cave system that is worth exploring in Paranas is the Sulpan Cave. It is a unique cave that has a natural pool inside where visitors can take a refreshing dip after exploring the cave.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Paranas

Paranas is a town that has rugged terrain, making it a perfect destination for hiking and mountain biking enthusiasts. Here are some of the top hiking and biking routes in Paranas:

  • Mt. Nacolod: This mountain offers a breathtaking view of the town and the Samar Sea. It is located in Barangay Nacolod and can be accessed by foot or by motorcycle.
  • Gobingob-Langun Cave System: Adventure seekers can also hike to the entrance of the Langun-Gobingob Caves. The hike is challenging, but the scenery is worth it.
  • Biketour: For those who prefer biking, there are several bike tour operators in Paranas that offer scenic bike tours around the town.

Best Things to Do in Paranas: Activities and Tours

Aside from visiting the cultural and natural attractions, Paranas offers a wide range of activities and tours that cater to different interests. Here are some of the best things to do in Paranas:

  • Island hopping: Visitors can take a boat tour to nearby islands such as Marabut and San Roque. The islands offer a pristine beach, crystal clear waters, and relaxing seaside retreats.
  • Whitewater rafting: Paranas is also known for its whitewater rafting experience. The Ulot River is perfect for this activity and offers a thrilling adventure for visitors.
  • Fiesta: Every February, Paranas celebrates its town fiesta, which is a vibrant and colorful event filled with music, food, and cultural performances.

Food and Drink Scene of Paranas

Paranas is also known for its cuisine, which is a combination of Filipino and Visayan flavors. Here are some of the top dishes and local beverages that visitors must try:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Binutong: This delicacy is a rice cake cooked in a bamboo tube and mixed with coconut milk, salt, and sugar.
  • Balut: A popular Filipino dish that consists of a boiled duck embryo, which is a must-try for those who want to experience Filipino culture.
  • Lechon: A roasted pig, which is a traditional Filipino dish that is always served during special occasions.

Local Beverages

  • Tuba: A traditional Visayan beverage that is made from fermented coconut water.
  • Bahalina: A local wine that is made from the sap of the nipa palm tree.
  • San Miguel Beer: A popular Filipino beer that is famous for its taste and quality.

Where to Stay in Paranas: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Paranas offers a diverse range of accommodations that cater to every budget and preference. Here are some of the top hotels, hostels, and guesthouses in Paranas:

  • Bahay Kubo Lodge: A cozy lodge that offers a laid-back atmosphere and affordable rates for travelers.
  • Sohoton Caves and Resort: A luxurious resort that offers spacious rooms, stunning views of the town, and outdoor swimming pools.
  • Marju Krisel Hotel and Resort: A boutique hotel that boasts a beautiful garden, an in-house restaurant, and friendly staff.

What to Buy in Paranas: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Visitors can bring home a piece of Paranas by buying some of the town’s local products. Here are some of the best souvenirs that visitors can buy in Paranas:

  • T’nalak Cloth: A traditional woven cloth that is made by the T’boli tribe in Mindanao.
  • Abaca products: A variety of products such as bags, baskets, and hats made from abaca fibers.
  • Coconut products: A wide range of products such as coconut oil, coconut jam, and coconut vinegar that are all locally made.

Local Culture and Customs of Paranas

Paranas has a rich culture that is a mix of Visayan and indigenous groups. Visitors must respect the local customs and tradition of the town, especially during religious festivals and events. The town is known for its hospitality, so visitors will be welcomed warmly by the locals.

Best Time to Visit Paranas

The best time to visit Paranas is during the dry season, which is from December to April. The weather is perfect for outdoor activities, and the town is less crowded during this time. It is also recommended to avoid visiting during the typhoon season, which is from July to October.

Do’s and Don’ts of Paranas

Do:

  • Respect the local culture and tradition of Paranas.
  • Try the local cuisine and beverages.
  • Bring sunscreen and insect repellent.
  • Take a dip in the natural pools and beaches.

Don’t:

  • Litter or damage natural attractions.
  • Wear inappropriate clothing during religious events or in cultural places.
  • Disrespect the locals or their customs.
  • Ignore safety guidelines during outdoor activities.

Safety and Health

Paranas is a relatively safe town, and visitors can explore the town without worrying about safety concerns. However, visitors must take necessary precautions during outdoor activities such as hiking and rafting. It is recommended to consult a doctor before traveling to Paranas and to get vaccinated for common illnesses.

Getting to Paranas: Transportation Options and Logistics

Paranas is accessible by land and air, and visitors can choose from various transportation options depending on their budget and preference.

Airports Near Paranas

The nearest airport to Paranas is the Calbayog Airport, which is located 39 kilometers away from the town. It serves domestic flights to and from Manila and Cebu.

Bus Stations Near Paranas

Visitors can take a bus ride from Manila or Cebu to Catbalogan City, which is a 2-3 hour ride from Paranas. From Catbalogan, visitors can take a jeepney or tricycle ride to Paranas.

Other Options

Visitors can also hire a private car or van to take them directly to Paranas. This option is more expensive but provides a more convenient and faster way of travelling.

How to Get Around in Paranas

Paranas is a small town that can be explored by walking or taking a tricycle ride. Jeepneys are also available for visitors who want to explore the nearby towns and attractions. Visitors can also rent a motorcycle or bicycle to explore the town at their own pace.

Where to Go Next from Paranas: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Visitors can extend their trip by exploring nearby destinations and excursions. Here are some of the nearby destinations that visitors can consider:

  • Catbalogan: The capital city of Samar Island, which is known for its cultural heritage and local festivals.
  • Calbiga: A town that is known for its caves and natural attractions.
  • Tacloban: The capital city of Leyte Island, which is known for its historical landmarks and scenic views.

Conclusion

Paranas is a hidden gem that offers a unique blend of natural wonders, cultural heritage, and outdoor adventures. This comprehensive travel guide has provided a glimpse of the top attractions, activities, food, accommodations, and more in Paranas. A visit to Paranas is a must for travelers who want to explore off-the-beaten-path destinations in the Philippines.
