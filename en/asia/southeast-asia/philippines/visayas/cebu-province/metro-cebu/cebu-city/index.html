Introduction to Cebu City

Cebu City is a bustling metropolis in the central Visayas region of the Philippines. Known as the “Queen City of the South,” it is a popular destination for both domestic and international tourists because of its rich history, beautiful natural attractions, and vibrant culture.

In this comprehensive tour guide, we will explore the best things to see and do in Cebu City, as well as where to stay, what to eat, and how to stay safe during your visit.

List of Places to Visit in Cebu City

There are many great places to visit in Cebu City, whether you are interested in learning about the city’s history and culture or exploring its natural beauty. Let’s start with some of the most popular attractions:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Basilica del Santo Niño - this is the oldest Roman Catholic Church in the Philippines and home to the country’s most revered religious relic, the Santo Niño de Cebu.
  • Fort San Pedro - this is a military defense structure built by Spanish conquistador, Miguel López de Legazpi.
  • Magellan’s Cross - historically symbolizes the city’s shift to Catholicism and is located in a chapel next to the Basilica del Santo Niño.
  • Casa Gorordo Museum - offers a glimpse into the lifestyle of a wealthy Cebuano family during the Spanish colonial era.
  • Yap Sandiego Ancestral House - a well-preserved 17th-century house with antique furniture and artifacts.

Natural Attractions of Cebu City: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Taoist Temple - this iconic landmark symbolizes the city’s Chinese history and offers a stunning view of Cebu City from its hilltop location.
  • Cebu Taoist Cemetery - A one-of-a-kind experience to witness a cemetery like no other. Complete with extravagant and intricate tomb designs; just make sure to respect the space and its surroundings.
  • Sirao Flower Farm - a popular spot to take photos with rows of blooming celosia flowers in varying shades of pink, red, and yellow.
  • Temple of Leah - inspired by Rome’s Parthenon, the temple is a grand structure dedicated to Leah, the late wife of Cebuano businessman Teodorico Soriano Adarna.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Cebu City

Cebu City has several hiking and biking routes that offer a different perspective on its natural beauty. Take a break from the city life and explore Cebu’s nature trails:

  • Mount Busay - offering a panoramic view of the city.
  • Osmeña Peak - the highest mountain in Cebu.
  • Cansumer Falls - a hidden waterfall in the Badian Mountains.

Best Things to Do in Cebu City: Activities and Tours

Cebu City offers a range of activities and tours to suit every traveler’s interests, from water sports and island hopping to cultural tours and cooking classes.

  • Island Hopping - Explore the crystal-clear waters and white sand beaches of Mactan Island or go island hopping to nearby Olango Island.
  • Scuba Diving and Snorkeling - Cebu’s warm waters are home to a variety of marine life, making it an excellent dive destination.
  • Food Tours - Sample local delicacies like lechon, dried mangoes, and otap crackers with a walking tour of Cebu’s food scene.
  • Cultural Tours - learn about Cebu’s fascinating history and culture with a guided city tour.

Food and Drink Scene of Cebu City

Cebu is known for its unique cuisine influenced by its Spanish, Chinese, and Malay heritage.

Must-Try Dishes

  • Lechon - a roasted suckling pig, considered one of the world’s best dishes.
  • Puso - hanging rice, a staple food in Cebu, woven into triangular pouches of woven coconut leaves.
  • Chorizo de Cebu - the original Spanish sausage recipe introduced by the Spaniards during their colonization.
  • Dried Mangoes - a popular snack made from carabao mangoes grown in Cebu.

Local Beverages

  • San Miguel Beer - a cold beer that locally produced and available in most bars and restaurants.
  • Tubâ - also known as coconut wine, is fermented coconut water alcohol.
  • Cebuano Coffee - Cebu produces high-quality coffee beans, including Barako and Kalinga Robusta.

What to Buy in Cebu City: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Cebu is known for a range of handicrafts, food, and specialty products that make great souvenirs.

  • Cebu guitars - handcrafted acoustic guitars made by Cebuano artisans.
  • Sari-Sari Store halo-halo mix - a halo-halo mix made with locally grown fruits like jackfruit, banana, and avocado.
  • Otap crackers - a light and flaky biscuit that uses baking powder instead of yeast.
  • Fruits - sinfully sweet and plump Carabo mangoes.

Local Culture and Customs of Cebu City

Cebuanos are proud of their heritage and hospitality. During your stay, make sure to say “Salamat Kaayo!” (Thank you very much) or “Maayong Buntag” (Good Morning) to show appreciation for their warmth and friendliness.

Best Time to Visit Cebu City

The best time to visit Cebu City is during the dry season, from November to May, when the weather is mild and dry. If you’re up for joining local festivals, plan your trip in January for the Sinulog Festival, a vibrant cultural celebration.

Do’s and Don’ts of Cebu City

Do:

  • Respect the culture and traditions of the locals.
  • Keep yourself hydrated and wear sunscreen to avoid sun damage.
  • Be cautious when traveling around the city at night.

Don’t:

  • Don’t litter and dispose of trash properly.
  • Don’t disrespect religious institutions and their practices.
  • Avoid getting scammed and always double-check prices and scenarios.

Safety and Health

Safety in Cebu City

While Cebu City has been improving on its safety efforts and becoming a more tourist-friendly city, it is still advised to be cautious and aware of your surroundings.

Health in Cebu City

Visitors to Cebu City should ensure they are up-to-date on routine vaccinations, such as measles, mumps, and rubella (MMR), and hepatitis A and B, and should practice good hygiene to avoid food and water-borne illnesses.

Getting to Cebu City: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Cebu City

Mactan-Cebu International Airport (CEB) is the primary gateway to Cebu City, with daily flights from major cities in Asia, Australia, and the Middle East.

Bus Stations Near Cebu City

The Cebu Bus Station is the main transportation hub for inbound and outbound routes of Cebu City.

Other Options

Ride-hailing apps such as Grab, taxis, and jeepneys can take you around Cebu City. However, be wary of scams and always double-check the prices with the driver.

How to Get Around in Cebu City

Jeepneys, taxis, and motorcycle taxis (“habal-habal”) are the most common modes of transportation around Cebu City.

Where to Go Next from Cebu City: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Cebu City offers many nearby destinations that are worth exploring, including:

  • Bohol Island - a popular destination for its Chocolate Hills and the tarsier, one of the world’s smallest primates.
  • Siquijor Island - known for its beaches and mystical charm and culture.
  • Oslob - one of the best places to swimming with whale sharks in Asia.

Conclusion

Cebu City has something for everyone, whether you’re interested in history, culture, or outdoor adventures. By following our tour guide, you can make the most out of your visit to Cebu. So pack your bags, book your flights, and immerse yourself in the Queen City of the South!
