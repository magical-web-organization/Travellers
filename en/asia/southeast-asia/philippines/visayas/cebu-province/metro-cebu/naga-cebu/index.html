Naga (Cebu) is a charming city located in Metro Cebu, in Cebu Province, in the Visayas region of the Philippines. This peaceful city boasts a variety of cultural and natural attractions, making it a must-visit destination for travelers looking for an authentic Filipino experience. In this travel guide, we’ll take you through the best things to do, places to visit, and tips for traveling to Naga (Cebu), Philippines.

Introduction to Naga (Cebu)

Naga (Cebu) is a city located in the southeastern part of Cebu Province, about 30 kilometers south of Cebu City. With a population of approximately 120,000 residents, Naga (Cebu) is a small but bustling city, known for its rich history and unique culture.

List of Places to Visit in Naga (Cebu)

Here is a list of the top places to visit in Naga (Cebu):

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Naga Boardwalk. Take a stroll along the Naga Boardwalk and enjoy the scenic view of the ocean.
  • Naga City Cultural and Sports Complex. This complex serves as a venue for local festivals and sporting events, and also features a mini-library and museum.
  • Naga City Fish Sanctuary. This is a popular spot for snorkeling and diving enthusiasts. Here, you can see a variety of colorful fish and other marine life.
  • San Fernando Rey Parish Church. This historic church was built in 1857 and showcases Gothic-inspired architecture.

Natural Attractions of Naga (Cebu): Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Mount Naupa. This mountain is a popular hiking trail that offers breathtaking views of the surrounding landscape.
  • Lapos Lapos Cave. Located near Mount Naupa, this cave is accessible only by climbing down a steep cliff. Once there, you can explore the cave’s unique rock formations and cool freshwater pools.
  • Bagacay Point Lighthouse. This lighthouse provides panoramic views of Naga’s coastline and surrounding areas.
  • Naga River. Enjoy a relaxing river cruise or kayak adventure along the Naga River, where you can observe the city’s wildlife and natural scenery up close.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Naga (Cebu)

Naga (Cebu) is a haven for outdoor enthusiasts. Here are some of the best hiking and biking trails in the area:

  • Mount Naupa Trail. This is a challenging hiking trail that takes you to the top of Mount Naupa.
  • Naga River Trail. This trail follows the Naga River and provides a scenic view of the surrounding landscape.
  • Naga City to Aloguinsan Trail. This is a long-distance trail that can be biked or hiked. It takes you from Naga City to the nearby town of Aloguinsan, where you can explore additional natural attractions.

Best Things to Do in Naga (Cebu): Activities and Tours

Naga (Cebu) offers a wide range of activities and tours for visitors. Here are some of the top things to do:

  • Join a city tour. This tour takes you around the city’s historic landmarks and cultural attractions.
  • Go island hopping. Naga (Cebu) is surrounded by islands that offer picturesque views and marine life.
  • Experience night fishing. This immersive tour lets you experience local fishing culture and learn traditional fishing techniques.
  • Try kayaking or stand-up paddleboarding. Take a scenic tour of the Naga River by kayak or stand-up paddleboard.

Food and Drink Scene of Naga (Cebu)

No trip to Naga (Cebu) would be complete without tasting the city’s local dishes and beverages. Here are some of the must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Adobo. This is a classic Filipino dish made with meat (usually chicken or pork), vinegar, soy sauce, and garlic.
  • Kinilaw. This is a local version of ceviche made with fresh fish, vinegar, and spices.
  • Lechon. This is a Filipino-style roasted pig known for its crispy skin and tender meat.
  • Puso. This is a type of rice wrapped in woven coconut leaves.

Local Beverages

  • Buko juice. This refreshing drink is made with fresh coconut water served straight from the coconut.
  • Tuba. This is a type of Filipino wine made from fermented coconut sap.
  • Coffee. Philippines is one of the world’s top coffee producers and is known for its rich and flavorful coffee.

Where to Stay in Naga (Cebu): Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Naga (Cebu) has a variety of accommodations to suit different budgets and preferences. Here are some of the top hotels, hostels, and guesthouses in the area:

  • Seacliff Resort and Spa. This beachfront resort offers luxurious amenities and scenic views of the ocean.
  • San Roque Paradise Hotel. This hotel is located in the city center and offers comfortable rooms and local hospitality.
  • NS Royal Hotel. This 3-star hotel boasts modern amenities and excellent customer service.
  • JJS Guesthouse. This budget-friendly guesthouse offers clean and comfortable rooms with basic amenities.

What to Buy in Naga (Cebu): Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Here are some of the best souvenirs and local products to bring home from Naga (Cebu):

  • Traditional woven products. Naga (Cebu) is known for its intricate woven products made from natural fibers.
  • Handmade pottery. You can find a variety of handmade pottery in Naga City, including vases, bowls, and plates.
  • Local coffee beans. Bring home a bag of freshly roasted coffee beans from the nearby mountains.

Local Culture and Customs of Naga (Cebu)

Naga (Cebu) has a diverse cultural heritage influenced by both Spanish and Filipino cultures. Here are some of the local customs and traditions to keep in mind:

  • Respect religious sites. As a predominantly Catholic country, churches and other religious sites are considered sacred places.
  • Take off your shoes before entering a home. It is customary to remove your shoes before entering someone’s home in the Philippines.
  • Practice proper etiquette when dining. Filipinos are known for their hospitality and generosity, and it is proper etiquette to thank your host and praise their cooking.

Best Time to Visit Naga (Cebu)

The best time to visit Naga (Cebu) is between December and May, when the weather is dry and sunny. However, even during the rainy season (June to November), Naga (Cebu) can be an enjoyable destination with moderate rains and cooler temperatures.

Do’s and Don’ts of Naga (Cebu)

Here are some do’s and don’ts to keep in mind when visiting Naga (Cebu):

Do:

  • Learn some basic Tagalog phrases. Filipinos appreciate when visitors make an effort to speak their language.
  • Bring cash. Credit cards are not widely accepted in Naga (Cebu), so it’s always best to bring cash.
  • Wear sunscreen and insect repellent. The sun can be harsh in Naga (Cebu), and mosquitoes can be present in natural areas.

Don’t:

  • Disrespect local customs or beliefs. It’s important to respect the cultural and religious beliefs of the locals.
  • Litter or damage the natural environment. Naga (Cebu) is known for its natural beauty, and it’s important to preserve it for future generations.
  • Be disrespectful to the locals. Filipinos are friendly and hospitable, and it’s important to treat them with respect and kindness.

Safety and Health

Safety in Naga (Cebu)

Naga (Cebu) is a safe city with low crime rates. However, it’s still important to practice common sense safety measures, such as avoiding unlit areas at night and keeping an eye on your belongings in crowded areas.

Health in Naga (Cebu)

Naga (Cebu) has access to medical facilities and pharmacies. However, it’s important to bring any necessary prescription medications with you, as well as mosquito repellent, sunscreen, and first aid supplies.

Getting to Naga (Cebu): Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Naga (Cebu)

The nearest airport to Naga (Cebu) is Mactan-Cebu International Airport, located in Lapu-Lapu City, about an hour’s drive away from Naga (Cebu). The airport has a variety of domestic and international flights available.

Bus Stations Near Naga (Cebu)

There are several bus stations near Naga (Cebu), including Ceres Bus Terminal in Cebu City and Bato Bus Terminal in Samboan. Buses are an affordable and convenient way to get around the area.

Other Options

Private transfers and car rentals are also available for those who prefer a more personalized transportation experience.

How to Get Around in Naga (Cebu)

The most common mode of transportation in Naga (Cebu) is the tricycle, a three-wheeled vehicle accommodating up to three passengers. For longer distances, buses and private transfers are also available.

Where to Go Next from Naga (Cebu): Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Here are some nearby destinations and excursions that you can visit from Naga (Cebu):

  • Cebu City. Explore the historic landmarks and cultural attractions of the bustling city of Cebu.
  • Oslob. Swim with whale sharks or go diving at the beautiful reefs in Oslob.
  • Bohol. Visit the famous Chocolate Hills and endangered tarsiers in the island of Bohol.
