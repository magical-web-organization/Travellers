Introduction to San Fernando (Cebu)

San Fernando is a third-class municipality in the southwestern part of Cebu Province, Philippines. Known for its unspoiled natural beauty and thriving cultural heritage, San Fernando has become a popular destination among travelers who are looking for a laid-back holiday experience. The town is surrounded by lush green hills, pristine beaches, and rugged cliffs, offering tourists with stunning vistas and diverse outdoor activities. Besides, historical landmarks, museums, and ancient churches, showcase the town’s rich history and culture. San Fernando is an ideal place to unwind and explore the natural and cultural wonders of Cebu.

List of Places to Visit in San Fernando (Cebu)

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Nuestra Señora del Pilar Church
    • One of the oldest churches in Cebu, the Nuestra Señora del Pilar Church was built by Spanish missionaries in the early 16th century. The church is famous for its baroque architecture and ornate interior.
  • Tribu Lapu Lapu
    • Learn about Cebuano history and culture at Tribu Lapu Lapu, an interactive cultural village that showcases the customs and traditions of the different indigenous groups in Cebu.
  • Casa Gorordo Museum
    • Casa Gorordo Museum is a well-preserved ancestral house in Cebu that presents a glimpse of the life of a 19th-century Filipino aristocrat family. The museum features antique furniture, embroidery, and folk art.

Natural Attractions of San Fernando (Cebu): Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Green Mountain Spring Farm
    • Experience farm life in San Fernando at Green Mountain Spring Farm, a sprawling organic farm that produces crops such as lettuce, tomatoes, and strawberries. Tourists can participate in farming activities, nature walks, and picnic outings.
  • Sirao Flower Farm
    • Also known as the “Little Amsterdam of Cebu,” Sirao Flower Farm is a picturesque destination that features vibrant rows of celosia flowers that bloom from September to December. Visitors can walk through the flower beds while taking selfies and enjoying the view.
  • Secret Beach
    • As the name suggests, Secret Beach is a secluded and unspoiled cove that is perfect for swimming and snorkeling. It has crystal-clear waters that portray a stunning blue-green hue.

Hiking/Biking Routes of San Fernando (Cebu)

  • Mt. Mago
    • If you’re looking for a challenging hike, Mt. Mago is an ideal route for you. It stands at 1,000 meters above sea level and takes about five hours to reach the summit. Once you reach the top, you’ll have a panoramic view of the surrounding mountains and coastlines.
  • Tabunan Trail
    • Tabunan Trail is a popular hiking and biking path that takes you through scenic forest trails that lead to waterfalls and hidden streams.

Best Things to Do in San Fernando (Cebu): Activities and Tours

  • Whale Shark Watching and Snorkeling
    • Swim with the gentle giants of the ocean as you go whale shark watching and snorkeling off the coast of Tanawan. It’s a unique and thrilling experience that should not be missed.
  • Cebu Canyoneering
    • Canyoneering is an adrenaline-pumping activity that involves trekking, jumping, and swimming down a canyon or riverbed. Cebu Canyoneering is best done in Badian, where you’ll see magnificent waterfalls and natural rock formations.
  • Sardine Run Snorkeling
    • Moalboal is popular for its sardine run snorkeling experience. Witness vast schools of sardines that fill the ocean floor and create an ethereal, stunning effect.

Food and Drink Scene of San Fernando (Cebu)

Must-Try Dishes

  • Puso
    • Puso, also known as hanging rice, is a staple dish in Cebu. It’s made of cooked rice wrapped in a woven coconut leaf that gives it a unique flavor and aroma.
  • Lechon
    • Cebu is the birthplace of the famous Filipino dish, lechon, a roasted pig that is roasted over an open flame until it’s crispy on the outside and juicy on the inside.
  • Dried Fish
    • Dried fish, or tuyo, is a classic Cebuano breakfast dish that’s best paired with eggs and vinegar. It’s both savory and salty, making it a favorite among Cebuanos.

Local Beverages

  • Tuba
    • Tuba is a coconut wine that’s made from the sap of the coconut tree. It’s sweet and fermented, making it a popular drink among locals.
  • Mango Shake
    • Cebu is also known for its delicious mangoes, which you can often find in the streets being sold as freshly squeezed mango shake.

Where to Stay in San Fernando (Cebu): Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Midori Eco Resort
    • Midori Eco Resort offers tranquil accommodations and an eco-friendly atmosphere. It’s surrounded by lush gardens and forests, making it an ideal place for those who love nature.
  • Palm Beach Resort
    • Palm Beach Resort offers luxurious accommodations and amenities, such as infinity pools and fine dining, that cater to tourists who want a more sophisticated vacation experience.
  • Voda Krasna Resort
    • Voda Krasna Resort is perfect for those who want a more laid-back and budget-friendly place to stay. It has its own private beach and cottage accommodations that offer breathtaking views of the sea.

What to Buy in San Fernando (Cebu): Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Otap
    • Otap is a tasty, crisp, and flaky pastry that’s made of sugar, flour, and oil. It’s a popular delicacy that you can bring home as a pasalubong (gift).
  • Mangoes
    • Cebu is famous for its sweet and juicy mangoes. It’s an ideal pasalubong for your loved ones back home.
  • Handicrafts
    • San Fernando has a thriving handicraft industry, where artisans make various products such as woven mats, baskets, and bags from indigenous materials such as corn husks.

Local Culture and Customs of San Fernando (Cebu)

  • Fiesta
    • San Fernando celebrates various fiestas throughout the year, such as the Feast of St. Isidore Labrador, which showcases colorful street parades, live music, and a feast of traditional Filipino dishes.
  • Eco-tourism
    • San Fernando is committed to promoting eco-tourism and sustainable travel practices. As a tourist, it’s essential to respect the town’s environmental policies and support their community-based tourism initiatives.

Best Time to Visit San Fernando (Cebu)

  • The best time to visit San Fernando is during the dry season, which is from January to April. The weather is mild, and the beaches and outdoor activities are in their prime condition.

Do’s and Don’ts of San Fernando (Cebu)

Do:

  • Respect local customs and traditions.
  • Try the local food and drinks.
  • Take care of the environment and follow the eco-tourism guidelines.

Don’t:

  • Litter or harm the environment in any way.
  • Disrespect cultural and religious sites.
  • Engage in any illegal activities, such as drug use or theft.

Safety and Health

Safety in San Fernando (Cebu)

  • San Fernando is generally safe, but like any other tourist destination, tourists should still exercise caution and be mindful of their belongings.
  • Avoid wearing flashy jewelry and avoid alone in unlit areas at night.

Health in San Fernando (Cebu)

  • Tourists should ensure their vaccines are up to date, especially when travelling abroad.
  • Drink bottled water and be aware of the food and drinks you consume.

Getting to San Fernando (Cebu): Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near San Fernando (Cebu)

  • The nearest airport to San Fernando is Mactan-Cebu International Airport.
  • From there, you can take a bus or private transfer to San Fernando.

Bus Stations Near San Fernando (Cebu)

  • San Fernando is accessible via the South Bus Terminal in Cebu City.
  • Several buses going to San Fernando are available throughout the day.

Other Options

  • Private transfers or taxis can be hired from Cebu City if preferred.

How to Get Around in San Fernando (Cebu)

  • Walking around San Fernando is possible, but it is best to hire a motorcycle or tricycle to get to destinations beyond walking distance.
  • Jeepneys are also available but are less common within the area.

Where to Go Next from San Fernando (Cebu): Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Oslob
    • Oslob is a small town that’s famous for its whale shark watching activity.
  • Sumilon Island
    • Sumilon Island is a beautiful island that’s just a boat ride away from Oslob. It has pristine white sand beaches and a stunning lagoon that’s perfect for swimming and snorkeling.

Conclusion

San Fernando is a tropical wonderland that showcases the best of Cebu’s culture and environment. With its rich heritage, stunning natural scenery, and relaxed way of life, San Fernando has evolved into a popular destination for travelers searching for an authentic Philippine cultural experience. With our comprehensive travel guide, you can enjoy all the best that San Fernando (Cebu) has to offer while ensuring a pleasant and safe trip.
