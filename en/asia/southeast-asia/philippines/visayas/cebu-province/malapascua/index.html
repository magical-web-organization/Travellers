Introduction to Malapascua

Malapascua is a small island located in the northernmost tip of Cebu province, in Visayas, Philippines. Famous for its white sandy beaches, crystal-clear waters, and diverse marine life, Malapascua has become one of the most popular destinations for travelers seeking paradise in the Philippines. Malapascua is mostly known for its thresher shark diving, but there are also many other attractions that make it a must-visit destination.

List of Places to Visit in Malapascua

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Malapascua is a small island and doesn’t have many museums or historical places, but there are a few worth visiting.

  • Tapilon Point - This is where the U.S. troops landed in 1944 during the Japanese occupation of the Philippines, beginning the liberation of Cebu.
  • Sto. Rosario Church - This is one of the oldest churches on the island, dating back to the 19th century.
  • Lighthouse - Located in the northern part of the island, the lighthouse offers a stunning panoramic view of Malapascua and the surrounding islands.

Natural Attractions of Malapascua: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Thresher Shark Diving - Malapascua is globally known for its thresher shark diving. These sharks are usually seen between 4 am to 7 am and can be observed up close.
  • Dive Sites - Malapascua has more than 20 dive sites, including Gato Island, an underwater island that is home to various marine species, soft corals, and reef sharks.
  • Island Hopping - Visit Kalanggaman Island and Carnaza Island to experience the beauty of the surrounding islands.
  • Biking and Hiking - Take a bike or go on foot to explore the island and its hidden gems.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Malapascua

There are several routes to choose from if you want to go hiking or biking on the island. One popular route is to start from the lighthouse and bike or hike to the southern part of the island, where you can find beautiful rock formations and secluded beaches. Another option is to bike or hike from the village to the diving sites to avoid the crowded boats.

Best Things to Do in Malapascua: Activities and Tours

  • Swimming and Snorkeling - Take a dip in the clear waters of Malapascua and snorkel to discover the rich marine life.
  • Star Gazing - With no city lights, the island is the perfect place to appreciate the spectacle of the night sky.
  • Island Hopping and Beach Combing - Cruise to the nearby islands and beaches, such as Carnaza Island and Kalanggaman Island.
  • Caving - Explore the caves of Gato Island, home to sea snakes, turtles, and a variety of coral species.
  • Massage and Yoga - Malapascua offers a range of massage services and yoga classes, perfect for unwinding and relaxation.

Week-Long Itinerary for Malapascua

Day 1: Arrival and check-in at your preferred accommodation, followed by a scenic walk to the beach.

Day 2: Early morning dive to see the famous thresher sharks. Breakfast at the dive shop and then explore the village and try local street foods.

Day 3: Island hopping, visiting Carnaza Island and Kalanggaman Island. Snorkeling, swimming, and beach combing.

Day 4: Hiking and biking to the beautiful rock formations of the island, discovering hidden beaches, and enjoying the stunning views.

Day 5: Visit the Sto. Rosario Church and learn about the history of Malapascua. Followed by a swim or a relaxing time at your resort’s swimming pool.

Day 6: A full day spent diving into some of Malapascua’s most stunning dive sites.

Day 7: Departure day with a morning massage and final swim before leaving for your next destination.

Food and Drink Scene of Malapascua

Must-Try Dishes

  • Lechon Cebuano - This is a famous dish in the Philippines. A whole pig is roasted to perfection, giving it a crispy skin and juicy inside.
  • Kinilaw - Fresh fish is marinated in vinegar, onion, and chili, served raw or slightly cooked.
  • Adobong Talong - Grilled eggplant mixed with vinegar, soy sauce, and garlic.

Local Beverages

  • Tuba - A coconut wine made from fermented coconut sap.
  • Calamansi Juice - A refreshing juice made from the fruit of the calamansi tree, a variety of the Philippine lime.

Where to Stay in Malapascua: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Malapascua offers a variety of accommodation options, from high-end resorts to budget guesthouses.

  • Malapascua Island Resort - A high-end resort with fantastic facilities, including a swimming pool and a beachfront bar.
  • Tepanee Beach Resort - A popular resort with sea-view rooms and a spa.
  • Kokay’s Maldito Dive Resort - A beachfront resort with inland rooms and budget-friendly prices.
  • Moonlight Resort - A family-friendly resort with air-conditioned rooms and traditional Filipino design.

What to Buy in Malapascua: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Hand-woven Items - Buy handmade bags, wallets, and hats made from natural materials like Pandan leaves and rattan.
  • Peanut Products - Malapascua is known for its locally produced peanuts, which come in a variety of flavors, including garlic and spicy.

Local Culture and Customs of Malapascua

Malapascua is a friendly and welcoming community, with residents known for their hospitality and generosity. Visitors should respect local customs by dressing modestly and taking off their shoes before entering any religious or cultural sites.

Best Time to Visit Malapascua

The best time to visit Malapascua is from November to May as it has a dry and sunny climate with little rain and calm seas, perfect for diving and island hopping. However, thresher sharks are present throughout the year, making Malapascua a destination for divers all year round.

Do’s and Don’ts of Malapascua

Do:

  • Respect the customs of the locals and dress modestly.
  • Take off your shoes before entering any religious or cultural sites.
  • Protect and preserve the marine life and the environment.

Don’t:

  • Litter on the beach or ocean.
  • Damage coral reefs and other marine life while diving.
  • Feed or touch the marine life.

Safety and Health

Safety in Malapascua

Malapascua is a safe place to visit, with low crime rates and an open and welcoming community. However, visitors should still use caution and keep their valuables in a safe place while exploring the island.

Health in Malapascua

Visitors should come prepared with sunblock and mosquito repellant as the island is prone to insects and strong sun rays. Visiting divers should also follow the necessary safety protocols to prevent diving accidents.

Getting to Malapascua: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Malapascua

The closest airport to Malapascua is Mactan-Cebu International Airport, which has direct flights from major cities in the Philippines and Asia.

From the airport, visitors can either take a private taxi or use public transportation to reach the Maya port. From there, they can take a boat to Malapascua Island.

Bus Stations Near Malapascua

The Cebu North Bus Terminal in Cebu City is the closest bus station to Malapascua Island. Visitors can take a bus or van to the Maya port, where they can take a boat to Malapascua.

Other Options

Visitors can also arrange a private transfer from Cebu City or the airport to the Maya port. There are many travel agencies that offer this service.

How to Get Around in Malapascua

Malapascua is a small island, and most of the attractions are within walking or biking distance. There are also tricycles or motorbikes available for rent if needed.

Where to Go Next from Malapascua: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Malapascua is located in the northern part of Cebu, and there are many nearby destinations worth exploring, including:

  • Bantayan Island - A beautiful island with white sand beaches and crystal-clear waters.
  • Camotes Island - A group of islands that feature beautiful caves, waterfalls, and lakes.
  • Moalboal - A diving destination located on the southern part of Cebu island.

Conclusion

Malapascua is a fantastic destination for nature lovers, adventurers, and diving enthusiasts. With its beautiful beaches, stunning dive sites, and hospitable people, Malapascua is sure to provide an unforgettable experience for every visitor. Plan your trip and get ready for a memorable adventure in one of the most beautiful islands in the Philippines!
