Introduction to Sabang

Sabang is a town located in the beautiful province of Palawan, Philippines, known for its stunning beaches, crystal clear waters, and rich marine life. The town is famous for the Puerto Princesa Subterranean River National Park, which is one of the New 7 Wonders of Nature. Sabang is an excellent destination for travelers who are looking for an outdoor adventure and a perfect place to unwind away from the city.

List of Places to Visit in Sabang

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

World War II Museum

The World War II Museum in Sabang is a must-visit destination for anyone interested in history. It showcases the Philippines’ role during the war, the hardships experienced by the locals, and the brave soldiers who fought for the country’s freedom.

Sabang Church

Sabang Church is a beautiful and historic church located in the heart of the town. It is one of the oldest churches in Palawan and was built in the late 1800s.

Natural Attractions of Sabang: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Puerto Princesa Subterranean River National Park

The Puerto Princesa Subterranean River National Park is one of the most famous tourist destinations in the Philippines and a UNESCO World Heritage Site. It is home to a vast network of underground rivers, caves, and limestone formations. The park has an abundance of wildlife, and visitors can take a boat ride inside the cave.

Sabang Beach

Sabang Beach is a pristine white sand beach with crystal clear waters and is an ideal place to relax and unwind. You can also go snorkeling here to explore the coral reefs and marine life.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Sabang

Sabang is a perfect location for outdoor activities like hiking and biking. Here are some popular routes you can explore:

  1. Sabang to Buenavista View Deck
  2. Sabang to Nagtabon Beach
  3. Sabang to Ugong Rock Adventures

Best Things to Do in Sabang: Activities and Tours

Underground River Tour

The Underground River Tour is a must-do activity in Sabang. It is an incredible experience, and visitors can take a boat ride inside the cave to marvel at the rock formations inside. The tour also includes a hike to reach the cave, and visitors can see monkeys and other wildlife along the way.

Sabang Zipline

The Sabang Zipline is a thrilling 800-meter ride over a lush forest canopy with magnificent views of the sea and surrounding mountains.

Week-Long Itinerary for Sabang

Day 1: Visit the World War II Museum and Sabang Church. Day 2: Take the Underground River Tour and go to Sabang Beach in the afternoon. Day 3: Go hiking to Nagtabon Beach and try surfing. Day 4: Explore Sabang village and shop for souvenirs. Day 5: Take the Sabang Zipline and go to Ugong Rock Adventures. Day 6: Relax on the beach and go snorkeling. Day 7: Try the famous Palawan delicacies and drinks before leaving Sabang.

Food and Drink Scene of Sabang

Must-Try Dishes

  1. Kinilaw – a popular Filipino dish made with raw fish, vinegar, onions, and ginger.
  2. Chicken Inasal – grilled chicken marinated in a mixture of vinegar and spices.
  3. Lechon – roasted pig cooked over an open flame.

Local Beverages

  1. Buko juice – fresh coconut juice served in the shell.
  2. San Miguel Beer – a popular local beer.

Where to Stay in Sabang: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Sheridan Beach Resort and Spa

The Sheridan Beach Resort and Spa is a luxury resort located near the Underground River. The resort offers lavish accommodations, spa, and excellent dining.

Daluyon Beach and Mountain Resort

The Daluyon Beach and Mountain Resort is a 4-star eco-resort located on Sabang Beach. It offers beachfront accommodations, infinity pool, and a restaurant.

What to Buy in Sabang: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  1. Pearls – Palawan is famous for its high-quality pearls.
  2. Cashew nuts – locally sourced cashew nuts are a popular souvenir
  3. Handicrafts – handmade baskets, mats, and hats made by local artisans

Local Culture and Customs of Sabang

Sabang locals are warm and hospitable. It is considered rude to point with your finger and to use your feet to point at people or things. Eating with your hands is also a cultural norm in the Philippines, but cutlery is provided in restaurants.

Best Time to Visit Sabang

The best time to visit Sabang is from November to May. The weather is warm and dry, and the sea is calm.

Do’s and Don’ts of Sabang

Do:

  1. Interact with the locals.
  2. Respect the culture and customs of the Philippines.
  3. Try the local food and drink.

Don’t:

  1. Litter or damage the environment.
  2. Speak loudly in public places.
  3. Wear revealing clothing when visiting religious places.

Safety and Health

Safety in Sabang

Sabang is a safe town with low crime rates. It is always advisable to be aware of your surroundings and belongings when traveling.

Health in Sabang

The town has a few medical clinics, but visitors are advised to have comprehensive travel insurance. It is also important to stay hydrated and use mosquito repellent to prevent mosquito-borne diseases like dengue fever.

Getting to Sabang: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Sabang

The nearest airport to Sabang is Puerto Princesa International Airport (PPS), which is 80 kilometers away.

Bus Stations Near Sabang

Buses operate from Puerto Princesa to Sabang on a regular basis.

Other Options

Visitors can also hire private transfers to get to Sabang.

How to Get Around in Sabang

Walking or biking around is the best way to explore Sabang. Visitors can also take a tricycle or habal-habal, which are popular modes of transportation in the Philippines.

Where to Go Next from Sabang: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

El Nido

El Nido is a town located in the northern part of Palawan, known for its towering limestone cliffs, lagoons, and beaches. It is a must-visit destination for every traveler visiting Palawan.

Coron

Coron is a town located on Busuanga Island, known for its fabulous beaches, crystal clear waters and rich marine life. It is a popular destination for scuba diving and snorkeling.

Conclusion

Sabang in Palawan, Philippines is a stunning destination with nature, adventure, and culture. Visitors can enjoy the exciting tourist attractions, indulge in the local cuisine, and experience the genuine hospitality of the locals. It is one travel essential that should be added to every traveler’s bucket list.
