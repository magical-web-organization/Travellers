Introduction to Palawan

Located in the western part of the Philippines, Palawan is an archipelago comprised of more than 1,700 islands. Known for its stunning beaches, crystal clear waters, and diverse marine life, it has been named the “Best Island in the World” by various travel magazines.

Palawan has something for everyone - from adventure seekers to culture enthusiasts. It is home to two UNESCO World Heritage Sites, a number of protected areas, and a plethora of activities such as island hopping, diving, hiking, and more.

List of Places to Visit in Palawan

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Palawan has a rich cultural heritage, and visitors can explore the province’s history and traditions through its museums and historical sites. Some of the must-visit cultural attractions include:

  • Palawan Heritage Center: Showcasing Palawan’s culture and heritage through its exhibits, this museum is a great place to learn about the province’s history and traditions.

  • Puerto Princesa Subterranean River National Park: A UNESCO World Heritage Site, this park features the Puerto Princesa Underground River, a natural wonder that has been hailed as one of the New 7 Wonders of Nature.

  • Cuyo Fort and Settlement: This historic fortress, built by the Spanish in the 17th century, is a testament to Palawan’s colonial past.

Natural Attractions of Palawan: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Palawan’s natural wonders are the main draw for visitors, with its pristine beaches, lush forests, and stunning landscapes. Some of the top natural attractions of Palawan include:

  • El Nido: One of the most popular destinations in Palawan, El Nido is known for its crystal-clear waters, towering limestone cliffs, and hidden lagoons.

  • Coron: Famous for its shipwrecks, diving spots, and breathtaking views, Coron is a paradise for adventure seekers and nature lovers.

  • Tubbataha Reef: Another UNESCO World Heritage Site, Tubbataha Reef boasts of a stunning underwater world filled with colorful marine life and coral reefs.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Palawan

Palawan is a great destination for hiking and biking enthusiasts, with its rugged terrain, forest trails, and scenic routes. Some of the best hiking and biking spots in Palawan include:

  • Mount Mantalingahan: The highest peak in Palawan, Mount Mantalingahan is a challenging but rewarding hike, offering panoramic views of the island.

  • Sabang to El Nido: This bike ride takes you through scenic landscapes, small villages, and natural wonders, with plenty of photo stops along the way.

  • Port Barton: This tranquil beach town offers a range of hiking and biking trails, from beach walks to jungle treks.

Best Things to Do in Palawan: Activities and Tours

Apart from exploring the natural beauty of Palawan, visitors can also engage in a range of activities and tours, such as:

  • Island hopping: A must-do activity in Palawan, visiting the various islands allows visitors to discover hidden beaches, lagoons, and other natural wonders.

  • Snorkeling and diving: Palawan’s waters are teeming with marine life, making it an excellent destination for snorkelers and divers.

  • Sunset cruise: Enjoy the stunning sunset views of Palawan while relaxing on a boat with a cold drink in hand.

Week-Long Itinerary for Palawan

To make the most of your visit to Palawan, here’s a week-long itinerary that covers the top attractions and experiences in the province:

Day 1: Arrive in Puerto Princesa and visit the Palawan Heritage Center Day 2: Explore the Puerto Princesa Subterranean River National Park Day 3-4: Island hop in El Nido and visit its beaches, lagoons, and cliffs Day 5: Take a snorkeling or diving tour in Coron Day 6: Hike to Mount Mantalingahan or explore Port Barton Day 7: Take a sunset cruise and depart from Palawan

Food and Drink Scene of Palawan

Must-Try Dishes

Palawan’s cuisine is influenced by its surroundings and cultural heritage, making it both unique and flavorful. Some of the must-try dishes in Palawan include:

  • Tamilok: A local delicacy made of woodworm seasoned with vinegar and chili.

  • Kinilaw: A traditional Filipino dish made of raw fish marinated in vinegar and spices

  • Adobo: A common Filipino dish of meat, poultry, or seafood marinated in vinegar, soy sauce, and garlic.

Local Beverages

Palawan is also known for its refreshing and unique beverages. Here are some of the local drinks you should try:

  • Calamansi juice: Made from the local citrus fruit, calamansi juice is a refreshing and tangy drink that’s perfect for hot weather.

  • Coconut water: Fresh coconuts are abundant in Palawan, and their water is a healthy, hydrating drink.

  • Tanduay Rum: A Filipino rum that is popular among locals and visitors alike, Tanduay Rum is made from sugarcane and aged to perfection.

Where to Stay in Palawan: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Palawan has a variety of accommodation options to suit all budgets and preferences. Some of the best places to stay in Palawan include:

  • El Nido Resorts: This premium resort chain offers luxury accommodations and exclusive amenities in various locations in Palawan.

  • Mad Monkey Hostels: Ideal for budget travelers and backpackers, Mad Monkey Hostels offer affordable and social accommodations with a range of activities and tours.

  • Sheridan Beach Resort and Spa: Located in Sabang, this resort has a range of rooms and suites, a spa, and an infinity pool looking out over the ocean.

What to Buy in Palawan: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Palawan is known for its unique souvenirs and locally made products. Here are some of the best things to buy in Palawan:

  • Palawan pearls: Known for their unique color and luster, Palawan pearls are perfect souvenirs for your loved ones.

  • Banig mats: Made of woven pandan leaves, these colorful mats are great for decorating your home or for beach picnics.

  • Cashew nuts: Palawan is famous for its cashew nuts, which are available in different flavors and varieties.

Local Culture and Customs of Palawan

Palawan has a rich and unique culture influenced by its indigenous tribes and various colonizers, making it a vibrant mix of traditions and customs. Here are some of the interesting customs and practices in Palawan:

  • Tabon Cave Ceremony: A traditional ceremony performed by the Tagbanua tribe to honor their ancestors and ask for blessings.

  • Bayanihan: A Filipino tradition of community work, where neighbors help each other in building houses or preparing for special occasions.

  • Palawan Day: A celebration of the province’s foundation, held every April 21.

Best Time to Visit Palawan

Palawan has a tropical climate, with two main seasons - dry and wet. The best time to visit Palawan would be during the dry season, which runs from November to April, when the weather is sunny and ideal for outdoor activities.

Do’s and Don’ts of Palawan

Do:

  • Respect the local culture and customs
  • Dress appropriately when visiting religious sites and when in public areas
  • Try the local cuisine and beverages
  • Take care of the environment and dispose of rubbish properly

Don’t:

  • Engage in any illegal activities such as drug use or trafficking
  • Disrespect religious customs and practices
  • Litter or damage the natural attractions and heritage sites
  • Drink excessive amounts of alcohol in public areas

Safety and Health

Safety in Palawan

Like any other destination, Palawan has its own share of safety concerns. Here are some tips for staying safe in Palawan:

  • Avoid walking alone at night in unfamiliar areas
  • Be cautious of pickpockets and scam artists
  • Follow the rules and regulations of adventure sports and activities
  • Stay up-to-date on the current safety situations in the area.

Health in Palawan

To ensure a healthy trip to Palawan, here are some important health tips to keep in mind:

  • Drink only bottled or purified water to prevent water-borne diseases
  • Apply mosquito repellent to protect against mosquito-borne diseases such as dengue fever and malaria
  • Check if your vaccinations are up to date and if you need additional ones before traveling.

Getting to Palawan: Transportation Options and Logistics

Getting to Palawan is easy, with various transportation options available for tourists. Here are the main ways to get to Palawan:

Airports Near Palawan

  • Puerto Princesa International Airport (PPS)
  • El Nido Airport (ENI)
  • Busuanga Airport (USU)

Bus Stations Near Palawan

  • Manila to San Jose Terminal in Palawan (18 hours)
  • Puerto Princesa to El Nido (5-7 hours)
  • Puerto Princesa to Sabang (2-3 hours)

Other Options

  • Islas Transport Express: A ferry that operates between El Nido and Coron
  • Shuttle vans and buses: Offered by various tour operators and hotels for transfers and transportation around Palawan.

How to Get Around in Palawan

Public transportation in Palawan is limited, but there are various options available for getting around, such as:

  • Tricycles: These are the most common mode of transportation in Palawan, used for short trips and city tours.

  • Jeepneys: These are a common sight in Palawan and offer cheap transportation between towns and cities.

  • Private transfers: This is the most convenient way to travel around Palawan, with various tour operators offering private transfers between attractions.

Where to Go Next from Palawan: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

If you have some extra time after exploring Palawan, here are some nearby destinations and excursions you can consider:

  • Coron Island: Located north of Palawan, Coron offers stunning island scenery, shipwreck diving sites, and a charming town.

  • Cebu: Known for its beaches, waterfalls, and cultural sites, Cebu is a popular destination for travelers in the Philippines.

  • Bohol: Home to the famous Chocolate Hills and the tarsier sanctuary, Bohol is a great destination for nature and wildlife enthusiasts.

Conclusion

Palawan is a true paradise destination, with its natural beauty, rich culture, and unique experiences. Whether you’re looking for adventure, relaxation, or culture, Palawan has something to offer everyone. So pack your bags, and get ready for an unforgettable trip to the Philippines’ best island!
