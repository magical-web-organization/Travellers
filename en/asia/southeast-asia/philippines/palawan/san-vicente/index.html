San Vicente, located in the northwestern part of Palawan, Philippines, is one of the most picturesque places in the country. The municipality is known for its vast, white-sand beaches, crystal-clear waters, verdant forests, and a plethora of exciting activities that can satisfy every nature lover and adventurous traveler. In this comprehensive travel guide, we will explore all the best things to do, places to visit, where to stay, and other insider tips to help you make the most of your visit to San Vicente.

Introduction to San Vicente

San Vicente is a small town in Palawan known for its lush greenery, unspoiled beaches and picturesque landscapes. The town has a relaxed atmosphere and remains uncommercialized, making it a perfect spot for those looking for a peaceful getaway. San Vicente has a rich history with its indigenous population and is home to several ethnic groups who have lived in the region for centuries. The town’s natural beauty and raw charm make it a popular destination for tourists looking to escape the hustle and bustle of city life.

List of Places to Visit in San Vicente

San Vicente is full of places to explore and there is something for everyone. Whether you’re interested in cultural attractions or natural wonders, there is a wealth of things to see and do. Below is a list of the best places to visit in San Vicente.

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Iwahig Prison and Penal Farm – A historical site built during the pre-American colonial period. A popular tourist attraction in San Vicente that provides visitors a glimpse of the correctional system in the Philippines.
  • Pawikan Conservation Center – A marine wildlife sanctuary that aims to protect and preserve sea turtles and their habitat.
  • Bato ni Ningning – An important cultural site located at the town plaza. It is believed to be the place where the first Aetas settled before the arrival of the Spanish colonizers.

Natural Attractions of San Vicente: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Long Beach - Dubbed as the longest white sand beach in Palawan and one of the country’s most splendid beaches.
  • Port Barton Beach - A serene public beach with clear turquoise waters surrounded by lush greenery.
  • Taytay Sabang Waterfall - An impressive series of cascades surrounded by a dense jungle. It is a popular spot for swimming and nature walks.
  • Coral Garden - An excellent spot for snorkeling and diving, located near German Island. It is famous for its colorful coral reefs and clear waters that harbor various sea creatures like turtles and small fishes.

Hiking/Biking Routes of San Vicente

San Vicente is a paradise for outdoor enthusiasts. Here are some of the best hiking and biking routes in San Vicente:

  • Mt. Capoas Trail - A moderate trek that leads to the summit of Mt. Capoas, one of the highest points in San Vicente. The trail offers a panoramic view of the town and its surroundings.
  • San Vicente Eco Trail - A nature trail that passes through various scenic spots, such as rivers, waterfalls, and dense forests. The trail is excellent for hiking, biking, and bird watching.

Best Things to Do in San Vicente: Activities and Tours

There is no shortage of outdoor activities and tours in San Vicente. Here are some of the best things to do and tours to take in San Vicente:

  • Island Hopping - San Vicente is home to several small islands that offer a variety of activities such as snorkeling, swimming, and beach bumming. Some of the most popular islands for island hopping are Exotic Island, Inaladelan Island, and Elephant Island.
  • Scuba Diving and Snorkeling - The Coral Garden, located near German Island, is a well-known spot for diving and snorkeling. It is said to have the most diverse marine life in the area.
  • Nature Walks and Treks - San Vicente has a wealth of natural resources to explore. The town is surrounded by dense forests, waterfalls, rivers and streams ideal for nature lovers.
  • Sunset Cruises - San Vicente has breathtaking sunsets, and witnessing it during a sunset cruise is a perfect way to end your day.

Food and Drink Scene of San Vicente

San Vicente is a perfect place to indulge in local and international cuisine. The town is dotted with restaurants and eateries serving a wide range of dishes inspired by traditional Filipino, Asian, and international flavors. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages in San Vicente:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Kinilaw - A local ceviche dish made of fresh raw fish marinated in vinegar, lime juice, onions, and chili.
  • Adobo - A classic Filipino dish made of chicken, pork, or both, cooked in soy sauce, vinegar, garlic, and black pepper.
  • Sinigang - A sour soup dish made of meat or fish, vegetables, and tamarind broth.

Local Beverages

  • Tuba - A Filipino drink made from fermented coconut sap. It has a sweet, acidic taste, and a low alcohol content.
  • Buko Juice - A refreshing drink made of fresh coconut water and meat.

Where to Stay in San Vicente: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

San Vicente offers a range of accommodations to suit every budget. From luxury hotels to budget-friendly hostels and guesthouses, the town has something for everyone. Here are some of the best places to stay in San Vicente:

  • Club Agutaya - A luxurious resort that offers modern amenities, private villas, and an infinity pool overlooking the sea.
  • Dolarog Beach Resort - A beachfront resort with a relaxing ambiance, an outdoor pool, and a beach bar.
  • Mad Monkey Hostel - A budget-friendly hostel located near the beach and town center. It offers dormitory rooms, private rooms, and a lively atmosphere.

What to Buy in San Vicente: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

San Vicente is famous for its local handicrafts and products made by its ethnic communities. Here are some souvenirs and local products to bring home:

  • Wooden crafts - Handmade wooden carvings, furniture, and decor are available in San Vicente. The town is known for its colorful, intricate designs.
  • Baskets and Bags - Made from bamboo, rattan, and pandan, these woven baskets and bags are the perfect gift for those in search of eco-friendly souvenirs.
  • Coconut-based Products - San Vicente is the perfect place to buy coconut-based products such as virgin coconut oil, coconut sugar, and coconut vinegar.

Local Culture and Customs of San Vicente

San Vicente has a diverse culture and is home to several ethnic communities. The indigenous population of San Vicente still practices their traditional way of life and customs despite being surrounded by modernization. Visitors should respect their cultural practices and customs.

Best Time to Visit San Vicente

The best time to visit San Vicente is between November and May when the weather is dry and sunny. The temperature ranges between 28 – 33 °C, and there is less rainfall during this period.

Do’s and Don’ts of San Vicente

Do:

  • Respect the local culture and customs.
  • Bring sunscreen and insect repellent.
  • Try the local food and beverages.
  • Enjoy the outdoors and explore the town’s natural beauty.

Don’t:

  • Litter or damage the environment.
  • Disrespect the locals.
  • Wear revealing clothing in places of worship.

Safety and Health

Safety in San Vicente

San Vicente is a safe town, with very low crime rates. However, visitors should always practice caution and common sense to avoid becoming a victim of petty crime like theft.

Health in San Vicente

Visitors should be aware of the health hazards associated with traveling to Palawan. The town has an adequate healthcare system, but it is highly recommended to have travel insurance and get vaccinations before visiting.

Getting to San Vicente: Transportation Options and Logistics

San Vicente is accessible by air, land, and sea. Here are the main transportation options to get to San Vicente.

Airports Near San Vicente

The closest airport to San Vicente is the San Vicente Airport. However, the airport only has flights from Boracay Island. Visitors can also fly into Puerto Princesa City International Airport, which is about a four-hour drive to San Vicente.

Bus Stations Near San Vicente

Daily buses and vans operate from Puerto Princesa to San Vicente.

Other Options

Visitors can rent a car or motorcycle to explore San Vicente, but it is not recommended for those unfamiliar with the terrain.

How to Get Around in San Vicente

Some accommodation providers offer shuttle services for their guests. The most popular modes of transportation in San Vicente are tricycles, motorbikes, and rented bicycles.

Where to Go Next from San Vicente: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

San Vicente is a great starting point to explore other parts of Palawan. Some popular destinations accessible from San Vicente are Port Barton, El Nido, and Puerto Princesa City.

Conclusion

San Vicente, Palawan, is one of the hidden gems in the Philippines yet to be discovered. It offers a perfect blend of natural beauty, cultural attractions, and outdoor adventures that will make your visit worth the while. With this comprehensive travel guide, you can plan your trip to San Vicente with ease and confidence.
