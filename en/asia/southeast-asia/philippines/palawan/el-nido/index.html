El Nido is one of the Philippines’ hidden gems and a must-visit destination in Palawan. It is situated in the Bacuit Bay in the northern part of Palawan, one of the most beautiful islands in the country. This stunning destination is known for its breathtaking scenery, crystal-clear waters, and limestone cliffs. El Nido offers several cultural, natural, and adventurous attractions that will suit the interests of every traveler. This comprehensive tour guide highlights all the things you need to know before visiting El Nido, including culture, activities, food, lodging, safety, and nearby excursions.

Introduction to El Nido

El Nido is a unique and stunning place that will take your breath away. It’s a hidden gem that has been attracting more and more visitors in recent years. El Nido is a cluster of 45 islands, islets, and rock formations situated in Bacuit Bay. This archipelago is known for its crystal-clear water, amazing coral reefs, and limestone cliffs. El Nido is famous for its stunning lagoons, picture-perfect beaches, and world-class diving spots.

List of Places to Visit in El Nido

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Palawan is rich in history and culture. There are several museums and historical places in El Nido that you can explore to get a glimpse of the town’s culture and history.

  • Cuyo Fort - Built during the Spanish era in 1680. It is a significant historical landmark that symbolizes the power and influence of the Spanish empire during that time.

  • El Nido Municipal Hall - A classic example of Spanish colonial architecture. The building has been meticulously restored and showcases the history and culture of El Nido.

Natural Attractions of El Nido: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

El Nido has several natural attractions that tourists can explore. Here are some of the top natural attractions that you should add to your itinerary:

  • The Big and Small Lagoons - The Big Lagoon is an enchanting place located in Miniloc Island. The jagged rocks and emerald green waters make it a perfect spot for kayaking, snorkeling, and swimming.

  • Nacpan Beach - A stunning 4-kilometer-long white sand beach and is hailed as the most beautiful beach in Asia. It is a perfect spot for swimming, snorkeling, and scuba diving.

  • Taraw Cliff - Hiking to the top of Taraw Cliff is one of the best things to do in El Nido. The panoramic views are breathtaking and will take your breath away.

Hiking/Biking Routes of El Nido

El Nido has several hiking and biking trails that tourists can explore. Here are some of the top hiking and biking trails that you should add to your itinerary:

  • Nagkalit-Kalit Waterfall Hike Trail - A scenic hike that leads you through a dense forest and ends up at a hidden waterfall. This hike is challenging but rewarding.

  • Marimegmeg Beach - Cycling along Marimegmeg Beach is one of the best ways to explore El Nido. You can rent a bike from one of the bike rentals located near the beach.

Best Things to Do in El Nido: Activities and Tours

El Nido offers several activities that will suit everyone’s interests - from island hopping to scuba diving. Here are some of the best activities and tours you should try:

  • Island Hopping Tours - Island hopping tours are the most popular activity in El Nido. This tour takes you to some of the best islands, beaches, and lagoons in El Nido.

  • Snorkeling and Scuba Diving - El Nido is known for its crystal-clear waters and amazing coral reefs. This makes it a perfect spot for snorkeling and scuba diving.

Food and Drink Scene of El Nido

The food and drink scene in El Nido is a fusion of Filipno, Spanish, and Asian cuisine. Here are some of the must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Kinilaw - A dish similar to ceviche that consists of raw fish served with coconut vinegar, onions, and chilies.

  • Adobo - A national dish of the Philippines that is made from chicken or pork, vinegar, soy sauce, and garlic.

Local Beverages

  • Tuba - A local alcoholic beverage that is made from coconut sap.

  • Buko Juice - A refreshing drink made from fresh coconut water.

Where to Stay in El Nido: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

There are several options for accommodation in El Nido, from budget hostels to luxurious hotels. Here are some of the best places to stay:

  • El Nido Resorts Lagen Island - A luxurious resort located in an unspoiled island paradise in El Nido.

  • Mad Monkey Hostels - A budget-friendly hostel that offers comfortable rooms and excellent service.

What to Buy in El Nido: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

El Nido has several souvenirs and local products that you can buy as gifts for your loved ones.

  • El Nido Pearls - El Nido is known for its high-quality pearls, which are often sold at affordable prices.

  • Handwoven Items - The markets in El Nido are filled with handwoven items like bags, mats, and hats.

Local Culture and Customs of El Nido

El Nido has a rich culture, and it’s essential to respect the local customs and traditions. Here are some things to keep in mind:

  • Dress Modestly - Filipinos are conservative, and it’s important to dress appropriately when visiting El Nido.

  • Respect the Environment - El Nido is home to several marine life species. It’s essential to respect the environment and keep the beaches and lagoons clean.

Best Time to Visit El Nido

The best time to visit El Nido is from November to May when the weather is dry and the waters are crystal-clear. However, expect crowds during the peak season.

Do’s and Don’ts of El Nido

Do:

  • Join an Island Hopping Tour - Island hopping tours are one of the best ways to explore the hidden gems of El Nido.

  • Rent a Bike - Renting a bike is an excellent way to explore the town and nearby beaches.

Don’t:

  • Litter - El Nido is known for its crystal-clear waters and scenic views. It’s important to keep the beaches and lagoons clean by not littering.

  • Disrespect the Local Culture - Filipinos are warm and welcoming people. It’s important to respect their culture and customs.

Safety and Health

Safety in El Nido

El Nido is a relatively safe town, but it’s essential to take precautions to avoid any mishaps. Here are some tips to stay safe in El Nido:

  • Be Vigilant - Keep an eye out for pickpockets and thieves. Be wary of your surroundings, especially when traveling at night.

  • Stay Hydrated - El Nido can get hot and humid, especially during the summer months. It’s essential to stay hydrated and avoid dehydration.

Health in El Nido

It’s important to take extra precautions with your health when traveling in El Nido. Here are some tips to stay healthy:

  • Drink Bottled Water - Make sure to drink bottled water to avoid getting sick from contaminated water.

  • Use Sunscreen - Since El Nido is hot and sunny, it’s important to use sunscreen to avoid sunburns and skin damage.

Getting to El Nido: Transportation Options and Logistics

El Nido is accessible by air, land, and water. Here are some of the transportation options you can take to get to El Nido:

Airports Near El Nido

  • El Nido Airport - Located in Barangay Buena Suerte, El Nido.

  • Puerto Princesa Airport - Located in Puerto Princesa, Palawan. El Nido is approximately six hours away from Puerto Princesa.

Bus Stations Near El Nido

  • San Jose Terminal - The main terminal in Puerto Princesa where most buses heading to El Nido stop.

  • Corong-Corong Terminal - Located in El Nido town proper.

Other Options

  • Chartered Flights and Boats - If you want a more convenient and faster way to get to El Nido, you can rent a chartered flight or boat from Manila or Puerto Princesa.

How to Get Around in El Nido

The best way to explore El Nido is by motorbike or tricycle. Most tour companies have pick-up and drop-off services. You can also rent a bike to explore the town and nearby beaches.

Where to Go Next from El Nido: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Palawan has several breathtaking destinations that you can explore after visiting El Nido. Here are some of the top destinations:

  • Coron - A cluster of limestone islands known for its stunning lagoons and World War II shipwrecks.

  • Puerto Princesa - Palawan’s capital city is known for its underground river and biodiversity.

Conclusion

El Nido is a must-visit destination in the Philippines. With its stunning beaches, natural attractions, and rich culture, El Nido is a paradise for every traveler. Make sure to follow the tips and suggestions in this comprehensive tour guide to have the best experience in El Nido.
