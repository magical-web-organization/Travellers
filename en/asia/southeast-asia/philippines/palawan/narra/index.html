Introduction to Narra

Narra is a municipality located in the southern region of Palawan, Philippines. It boasts a rich cultural heritage, breathtaking natural attractions, and a laid-back provincial atmosphere that creates a perfect escape for travelers who want to get away from the hustle and bustle of the city. Narra is also known for its banana plantations and is dubbed as the “Banana Capital of Palawan.”

List of Places to Visit in Narra

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Narra Municipal Hall - This municipal hall showcases a beautiful architecture and is a sight to behold.
  • Kaniki Point Resort Caves - The caves and rock formations of Kaniki Point Resort are a must-visit for those looking for a unique experience.
  • Tower of Peace - This tower is a testament to the national government’s peace campaign. It features the names of all municipalities in Palawan.
  • Narra Church - This historical landmark dates back to the Spanish colonial era and is a testament to the town’s Catholic roots.

Natural Attractions of Narra: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Bintuan Mangrove Park - This mangrove park features an extensive network of mangrove trails and walkways that lead to a pristine beach.
  • Panoramic View Deck - This view deck offers a scenic view of the town and is perfect for sunset watching.
  • Tagnaya Hill - Tagnaya Hill is famous for its colorful flowers and scenic views.
  • Pulang Lupa - Pulang Lupa is a mountainous area with a scenic view of the town and surrounding areas.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Narra

Narra is a great spot for hiking and biking enthusiasts. Some of the popular hiking and biking routes are:

  • Narra-Bataraza Road
  • Bosuanga Trail
  • Narra-Araceli Road
  • Narra-Making Trail

Best Things to Do in Narra: Activities and Tours

  • Batak Cultural Immersion - Learn about the customs, traditions, and culture of the Batak tribe in Palawan.
  • Banana Plantations Tour - Take a tour of the banana plantations and learn about the banana production process.
  • Snorkeling and Island Hopping - Palawan is famous for its crystal-clear waters and abundant marine life, so snorkeling is a must-do activity.

Week-Long Itinerary for Narra

Day Activity
Day 1 Arrive in Narra. Take a tour of the town and visit the Narra Municipal Hall, Kaniki Point Resort Caves, and the Tower of Peace.
Day 2 Take a trip to Bintuan Mangrove Park.
Day 3 Go hiking or biking on Narra-Bataraza Road.
Day 4 Explore the Batak Tribal Village and learn about their customs and culture.
Day 5 Take a Banana Plantations tour.
Day 6 Snorkel and go island hopping.
Day 7 Relax and enjoy the scenic views of Narra before departing.

Food and Drink Scene of Narra

Must-Try Dishes

  • Banana Que - sliced bananas coated in caramelized sugar, skewered on a stick
  • Hinalang na Manok - chicken cooked with chili and ginger
  • Bilo-Bilo - glutinous rice balls in coconut milk

Local Beverages

  • Coconut Juice - a refreshing drink made from fresh coconut water
  • Tuba - a locally produced alcoholic beverage made from coconut sap.

What to Buy in Narra: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Bananas - Palawan’s bananas are known for their sweetness and flavor.
  • Coconut Products - Coconut oil, coconut milk, and other coconut-based products are available in the local markets.

Local Culture and Customs of Narra

The Batak tribe is the indigenous people of Narra, and their culture and customs are still alive and celebrated in the town. It is recommended to learn and respect their culture during your visit.

Best Time to Visit Narra

The best time to visit Narra is during the months of November to March when the weather is dry and cool.

Do’s and Don’ts of Narra

Do:

  • Respect the local culture and customs
  • Be respectful to the environment
  • Be mindful of your actions in public areas

Don’t:

  • Disrespect the local culture and customs
  • Harm the environment
  • Litter

Safety and Health

Safety in Narra

Narra is a generally safe place to visit. However, it is recommended to keep your valuables safe and secure. Also, be careful when traveling on some of the mountainous areas as they can be challenging to maneuver.

Health in Narra

Make sure to bring insect repellent and sunscreen when going outdoors to avoid insect bites and sunburn. Also, always have clean drinking water to avoid dehydration and illness.

Getting to Narra: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Narra

  • Puerto Princesa International Airport - located 76 km (47 mi) away from Narra

Bus Stations Near Narra

  • Southern Star Bus - located in Puerto Princesa City and offers buses going to Narra

Other Options

  • Private Van Transfer - for a more convenient and comfortable travel experience, private van transfers are available from Puerto Princesa City to Narra.

How to Get Around in Narra

Traveling by tricycle or private transportation are the best ways to get around Narra.

Where to Go Next from Narra: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Queen Tuna Park - located in Bataraza, which is approximately 1 hour and 30-minute drive from Narra.
  • Balabac Island - located in the southernmost part of Palawan and is known for its clear crystal waters.

Conclusion

Narra is a charming and laid-back town that is perfect for travelers who want to experience the simple and authentic provincial life. With its rich cultural heritage, breathtaking natural attractions, and warm hospitality, Narra is undoubtedly one of the must-visit destinations in Palawan, Philippines.
