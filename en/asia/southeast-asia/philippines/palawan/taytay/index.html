Introduction to Taytay

Taytay is a small town located in the province of Palawan, in the Philippines. Despite being a less popular tourist destination, Taytay is known for its rich history, stunning limestone cliffs, crystal-clear waters, and natural wonders. This travel guide will help you plan your trip to Taytay, covering everything from the best places to visit to what to eat and where to stay.

List of Places to Visit in Taytay

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Fuerza de Sta. Isabel - The landmark fort of Taytay built by the Spanish government in 1667.
  • Taytay Cathedral - The oldest church in Palawan built in 1668.
  • Old Municipal Hall - A Spanish-era building that served as Taytay’s municipal hall and prison during the Spanish period.

Natural Attractions of Taytay: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Lake Manguao - A serene lake surrounded by magnificent limestone cliffs and stunning greenery.
  • Isla Blanca - An uninhabited island with white sandy beaches and clear blue waters.
  • Taytay Falls - A beautiful waterfall set in the lush greens of the Taytay forest.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Taytay

  • Cleopatra’s Needle - A challenging hike that brings you to an impressive limestone formation of Cleopatra’s Needle.
  • Mt. Capoas - A moderate climb that rewards you with a stunning overlook of Taytay Bay, Mogpog Island, and Apurawan peak.

Best Things to Do in Taytay: Activities and Tours

  • Island Hopping - Explore the many small islands around Taytay and enjoy some of the most beautiful beaches in the country.
  • Kuyawyaw Waterfall Adventure - A guided tour to Taytay falls, where you can swim and enjoy a picnic lunch in the idyllic setting.
  • Fishing Village Experience - A chance to interact with local fishermen and experience their daily life.

Week-Long Itinerary for Taytay

Day 1 Visit the Fuerza de Sta. Isabel and explore the old town of Taytay

Food and Drink Scene of Taytay

Must-Try Dishes

  • Kare-kareng Palad - A popular dish that consists of palm hearts with a savory peanut sauce.
  • Sinigang na Sugpo - A classic sour soup with freshwater shrimps, vegetables, and tamarind broth.
  • Adobong Pugita - A tasty dish made with octopus marinated in soy sauce, garlic, and vinegar.

Local Beverages

  • Tuba - A fermented coconut wine that is often enjoyed by locals.
  • Calamansi Juice - A refreshing drink made from calamansi, a local citrus fruit.

Where to Stay in Taytay: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Flower Island Resort - A luxurious beachfront resort located on Flower Island.
  • Apulit Island Resort - A beautiful resort located on Apulit Island that offers a wide range of activities.
  • Taytay Ecovillage - A budget-friendly option located in the heart of Taytay town.

What to Buy in Taytay: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Pandan Bags - Handmade bags made from pandan leaves.
  • Pis Syabit - A traditional Iraya-Mangyan garment made from handwoven cloth.
  • Crispy Wani - A crunchy snack made from dried young ferns.

Local Culture and Customs of Taytay

Taytay is a mix of different cultures, including the Tagalog and Iraya-Mangyan tribes. The town celebrates its rich cultural heritage through various festivals such as the Taytay Karakol and the Feast of Our Lady of Assumption.

Best Time to Visit Taytay

The best time to visit Taytay is between November and May when the weather is dry and sunny, perfect for outdoor activities.

Do’s and Don’ts of Taytay

Do:

  • Respect the local customs and traditions.
  • Take part in local festivals and activities.
  • Bring sunscreen and insect repellent.

Don’t:

  • Litter or damage natural areas.
  • Disrespect religious beliefs.
  • Wear revealing clothing in public places.

Safety and Health

Safety in Taytay

Taytay is generally a safe destination, but tourists should take precautions to avoid theft and pickpocketing in crowded areas. It is also advisable to avoid wandering alone in the city or visiting remote areas at night.

Health in Taytay

Several hospitals and clinics are available in Taytay town, and tourists should take normal precautions against common illnesses and insect bites.

Getting to Taytay: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Taytay

The nearest airport to Taytay is the Puerto Princesa International Airport, which is approximately 146 kilometers away. From here, you can take a bus or van to Taytay town.

Bus Stations Near Taytay

Regular bus services from Puerto Princesa or El Nido are available to Taytay bus station, which is located in the town center.

Other Options

Private cars or vans can also be rented to travel to Taytay.

How to Get Around in Taytay

Tricycles are the most common mode of transportation in Taytay town. You can also rent a motorbike or bicycle to explore nearby areas.

Where to Go Next from Taytay: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • El Nido - A stunning island town known for its beautiful lagoons, beaches, and underwater life.
  • Puerto Princesa - The capital city of Palawan, known for the Puerto Princesa Underground River and other natural wonders.

Conclusion

Taytay is a hidden gem waiting to be discovered by travelers. With its natural beauty, cultural heritage, and warm hospitality, Taytay offers a unique and unforgettable experience for those seeking adventure off the beaten path.
