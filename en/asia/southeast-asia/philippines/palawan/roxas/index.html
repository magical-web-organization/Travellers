Introduction to Roxas

Roxas is a municipality located in the northern part of Palawan, Philippines. Its stunning landscapes and natural attractions attract a lot of tourists every year. Home to breathtaking mountain ranges, sandy beaches, crystal-clear waters, and authentic Filipino culture, Roxas is the perfect destination for adventure-seekers and nature lovers alike.

List of Places to Visit in Roxas

There are numerous sights and attractions to see in Roxas. Here are some of the most culturally significant and beautiful natural attractions:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Linao Cave - This cave served as a shelter to the early inhabitants of Roxas. Visitors can see the different equipment and tools used by the ancient people, such as shells, bones, and utensils.
  • St. Joseph The Worker Parish - A peaceful church situated in Roxas Poblacion, this is a place where tourists can take a moment to meditate and admire its unique architecture.
  • Palawan Environmental and Field Research Station (PEFRS) - This research center provides a hands-on educational opportunity. It focuses on Northeastern Palawan’s unique ecological features and its role in conservation.

Natural Attractions of Roxas: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Roxas Forest Park - A spacious open park situated in the center of Roxas town. This area is suitable for those who like to relax and enjoy a calm atmosphere.
  • Pangitawaan Island - This island is known for its crystal-clear water and provides an excellent backdrop for swimming, snorkeling, and diving.
  • Roxas Peak - Adventurers can hike up to Roxas Peak and enjoy an amazing view of the surrounding areas.
  • Cagayancillo Coral Reefs Natural Park - This park offers a perfect opportunity for snorkeling or diving enthusiasts. Its crystal-clear water and rich marine life attract visitors from all over the world.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Roxas

One of the best ways to explore the natural beauty of Roxas is by hiking or biking. There are several trails that visitors can enjoy, such as Roxas Peak, Linao Cave, Pangitawaan Island, and Roxas Forest Park. Tourists should hire an experienced guide to explore these trails comfortably.

Best Things to Do in Roxas: Activities and Tours

Roxas has something for everyone. Here are some of the best activities and tours in Roxas:

  • Kayaking and Canoeing at Teduray Artesian Waters
  • Mangrove Reforestation, Environmental Advocacy, and Adventure Tour
  • Spelunking and Caving Tour at Linao Cave
  • Gibitngil Island and Pangitawaan Island Island Hopping Tour
  • Snorkeling and Diving in Cagayancillo Coral Reefs Natural Park

Food and Drink Scene of Roxas

Filipino food is known for its unique blend of flavor and spices. Roxas has a wide variety of dishes to offer that keep visitors returning back for more. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Binakol - A traditional Filipino dish made by boiling chicken meat in coconut water.
  • Kinilaw - This dish consists of raw fish or seafood marinated in vinegar and spices.
  • Adobo - This popular Filipino dish is made of meat cooked in soy sauce, vinegar, and spices.

Local Beverages

  • Buko Juice - A refreshing drink made from young, green coconuts.
  • Tuba - A local alcoholic drink made from fermented sap harvested from the coconut tree.

What to Buy in Roxas: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Visitors to Roxas should take the opportunity to buy some souvenirs and local products. These are some of the most popular:

  • Palawan Cashews - The cashews, grown locally, are popularly known for their premium quality.
  • Handmade Accessories - Visitors can purchase handmade bags, accessories, and jewelry at reasonable prices.

Local Culture and Customs of Roxas

Roxas’ local customs and culture are notable for their simple yet warm ambiance. Tourists should remember to dress conservatively when visiting cultural attractions and to respect the local traditions and customs.

Best Time to Visit Roxas

The best time to visit Roxas is from January to May, when the weather is relatively less extreme. It is advised to avoid visiting in monsoon season, which is usually from June to December.

Do’s and Don’ts of Roxas

Here are some things tourists should do and avoid during their stay in Roxas:

Do:

  • Respect the local culture and customs.
  • Dress conservatively during religious visits.
  • Try the local dishes and beverages.

Don’t:

  • Litter on the street or beaches.
  • Disrespect the environment.
  • Harm marine life.

Safety and Health

Safety in Roxas

In general, Roxas is a safe destination for tourists. It’s still recommended to take precautionary measures like keeping valuables close and being aware of surroundings.

Health in Roxas

Tourists should make sure to get vaccinated and take precautions to avoid diseases such as dengue fever, malaria, and typhoid.

Getting to Roxas: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Roxas

  • Puerto Princesa International Airport - The most convenient and closest airport to Roxas.

Bus Stations Near Roxas

  • San Jose Terminal - This terminal is located in the town of San Jose, which is 10 kilometers away from Roxas. It offers a bus service to various destinations within Palawan Province.

Other Options

  • Private Car/Van Rental - This is the most convenient, yet most expensive, option for travelers visiting Roxas.

How to Get Around in Roxas

The most common mode of transport in Roxas is the tricycle, a small motorized vehicle that can seat 2-3 passengers. Visitors can also rent a motorcycle or a bicycle to explore the town at their convenience.

Where to Go Next from Roxas: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Roxas offers several nearby destinations that tourists can visit:

  • El Nido - This is a renowned tourist destination located three hours from Roxas that offers plenty of activities such as island hopping, snorkeling, and diving.
  • Puerto Princesa - The capital city of Palawan Province is located around five hours from Roxas and offers several attractions such as UNESCO’s World Heritage Site, the Puerto Princesa Underground River.
  • Taytay - Taytay is home to Fort Santa Isabel, a 17th-century Spanish fort situated at the top of a hill.

Conclusion

Roxas is a hidden gem located in the north of Palawan. Visitors to Roxas will be greeted with a warm culture, stunning natural beauty, and a wide variety of activities to engage in. With this comprehensive travel guide, tourists can explore the best attractions, activities, accommodations, and more in Roxas and experience all that this town has to offer.
