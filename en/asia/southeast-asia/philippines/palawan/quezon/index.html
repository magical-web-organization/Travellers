Introduction to Quezon

Quezon is a town located in the southern part of Palawan, Philippines. It is bounded on the west by the municipality of Rizal, on the north by the municipality of Narra, on the east by the Sulu Sea, and on the south by the municipality of Bataraza. The town is known for its abundant natural resources, particularly its timber and fisheries industry.

Quezon is also home to various cultural and historical attractions that showcase the town’s rich heritage. This town is an excellent destination for travelers who want to experience the rustic charm of Palawan and immerse themselves in the local culture.

List of Places to Visit in Quezon

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Taytay Sabang Balatik Cultural Village

The Taytay Sabang Balatik Cultural Village is a must-visit destination for travelers who want to learn more about the traditional customs and practices of the Palaweños. It is a living museum that showcases the local culture, architecture, and handicrafts of the different communities in Palawan. Visitors can experience the culture firsthand by trying on traditional clothes, participating in cultural dances, and trying out authentic local cuisine.

Quezon Municipal Museum

The Quezon Municipal Museum preserves the town’s historical artifacts, including vintage photographs, antique pieces of furniture, and other memorabilia. The museum’s exhibits offer visitors a glimpse of life in Quezon during the Spanish colonial era, as well as its eventual transformation into a bustling fishing town.

Natural Attractions of Quezon: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Tabon Caves

The Tabon Caves, located in Quezon’s nearby town of Lipuun Point, are a UNESCO World Heritage site. The caves are believed to be the excavation site of the Tabon man, one of the earliest human beings in the Philippines. Visitors can marvel at the intricate cave formations while learning more about the site’s rich history.

Onok Island

Onok Island is one of Quezon’s most secluded and pristine beaches. The island is accessible by boat and offers visitors a tranquil and picturesque retreat. Visitors can spend the day lounging on the beach, snorkeling in the crystal-clear waters, and enjoying the unspoiled beauty of the island.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Quezon

Quezon Eco-Tourism Trail

The Quezon Eco-Tourism Trail is a 7-kilometer route that takes visitors through the town’s lush forest and scenic hillsides. The trail is perfect for hikers and mountain bikers looking for an adventure. Along the way, visitors can also explore the local wildlife and vegetation.

Best Things to Do in Quezon: Activities and Tours

Island Hopping Tour

Quezon’s closest islands are a few kilometers away from the town proper and offer stunning beaches, clear waters, and abundant marine life. Visitors can book a guided island-hopping tour that takes them to the most beautiful destinations, including Onok Island, Four-Sided Island, and Rasa Island.

Fishing Tour

Quezon is known for its thriving fishing industry, and visitors can experience it firsthand by joining a fishing tour. Visitors can expect to catch a wide variety of fish, including tuna, mackerel, and barracuda.

Food and Drink Scene of Quezon

Must-Try Dishes

Seafood

Quezon is famous for its fresh seafood. Visitors can try a wide variety of seafood dishes, including grilled fish, seafood soup (tinolang isda), and seafood curry.

Badjao Soup

Badjao soup is a popular dish in Palawan that features prawn, fish, and noodles. The broth, made from fish and prawn heads, is flavorful and savory.

Local Beverages

Coconut water

Palawan is known for its sweet and refreshing coconuts. Visitors can purchase coconut water along the beaches and roadside stores.

Buko Juice

Buko Juice is a refreshing drink made from cold coconut water and grated coconut meat. It is a popular beverage during the hot summer months.

Where to Stay in Quezon: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Quezon offers a wide variety of accommodations to suit every budget and preference. Visitors can choose from budget-friendly hostels, family-friendly resorts, and cozy guesthouses.

Budget-friendly Accommodations

Caroline’s Lodge

Caroline’s Lodge offers affordable rooms that are clean, comfortable, and well-appointed. The lodge’s location is convenient for exploring Quezon’s top attractions.

RZR Lodging House

RZR Lodging House offers simple yet functional rooms that come equipped with air-conditioning, cable TV, and free Wi-Fi. The lodging offers a convenient location in the heart of Quezon.

Mid-range Accommodations

Quezon Beach Resort

Quezon Beach Resort is situated along the beach and offers guests a relaxed atmosphere. The resort boasts a range of accommodations, including beachfront cabanas, garden cottages, and suites.

Blue Lagoon Tidal Pool

Blue Lagoon Tidal Pool is a picturesque resort that features a natural tidal pool, an infinity pool, and other recreational facilities. Guests can choose from rooms with a sea or pool view.

What to Buy in Quezon: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Mat-woven products

Quezon is famous for its mat-woven products, including placemats, bags, and baskets. These products are made from hand-woven pandan leaves and are a reflection of the local craftsmanship.

Dried Fish

Quezon is a haven for dried fish lovers. Visitors can purchase a variety of dried fish, including anchovies, mackerel, and squid.

Local Culture and Customs of Quezon

The people of Quezon are proud of their cultural heritage and traditions. Visitors should respect the local customs and be mindful of their behavior and clothing, especially when visiting places of worship.

Best Time to Visit Quezon

The best time to visit Quezon is during the dry months, which typically occur from December to May. The weather during these months is warm and dry, making it ideal for outdoor activities and beach hopping.

Do’s and Don’ts of Quezon

Do:

  • Respect the local customs and traditions
  • Wear appropriate clothing when visiting religious sites
  • Try the local cuisine

Don’t:

  • Engage in littering or other forms of environmental pollution
  • Disrespect the local customs and traditions

Safety and Health

Safety in Quezon

Quezon is generally a safe place to visit, but visitors should take care of their valuables and be mindful of their surroundings. As with other destinations, common sense safety precautions such as locking doors and windows, interrupting strangers with suspicious behavior, and taking care on unfamiliar roads should be taken.

Health in Quezon

Visitors should stay hydrated by drinking plenty of water and wear protective clothing and sunscreen during outdoor activities. It is also advisable to bring insect repellent to protect against mosquito bites.

Getting to Quezon: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Quezon

Puerto Princesa Airport is the nearest airport to Quezon and is approximately 130 kilometers away from town. There are several flights offered daily to Puerto Princesa from major cities throughout the Philippines and Southeast Asia.

Bus Stations Near Quezon

There are no bus stations in Quezon, but visitors can take a bus to Puerto Princesa City, which is approximately four hours from Quezon.

Other Options

Visitors can also rent a car, take a taxi or van, or book a private transfer to get to Quezon from Puerto Princesa City

How to Get Around in Quezon

Visitors can explore Quezon on foot or by renting a motorbike. Tricycles are also available for hire for longer distances.

Where to Go Next from Quezon: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Narra

Narra, a neighboring town to Quezon, is known for its pristine beaches, waterfalls, and eco-tourism sites.

Brooke’s Point

Brooke’s Point is a municipality located approximately 45 kilometers away from Quezon. It is known for its extensive mangrove forest, fishing villages, and the Tabon Cave Complex.

Conclusion

Quezon is a charming and laidback town that offers visitors an off-the-beaten-path experience of Palawan. With its abundant natural beauty, rich cultural heritage, and delicious local cuisine, Quezon is an ideal destination for travelers seeking a unique and authentic experience.
