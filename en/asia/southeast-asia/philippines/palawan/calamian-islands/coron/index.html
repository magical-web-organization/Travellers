Introduction to Coron

Nestled in the Calamian Islands in Palawan, Philippines, Coron is a tropical paradise that boasts stunning crystal-clear waters, white sandy beaches, and unique limestone karst formations. The island offers a range of activities: From outdoor adventures such as scuba diving and island hopping to cultural experiences such as exploring museums and learning about the local way of life, Coron has something for everyone.

This comprehensive travel guide to Coron will help you plan your trip and explore everything the island has to offer. From places to visit and things to do to food and drink recommendations and transportation options, this guide has got you covered.

List of Places to Visit in Coron

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Coron has a rich history that has shaped its culture. If you’re a history buff, you’ll enjoy exploring the following museums and historical places:

  • Coron Island and Cemetery
  • Coron Town Plaza
  • The Tapyas View Deck
  • San Agustin Parish Church
  • Mount Darala
  • Calauit Safari Park
  • Coron Youth Club Japanese Wreck

Natural Attractions of Coron: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Coron’s natural beauty is breathtaking, and the scenic views are mesmerizing. The island offers a range of outdoor activities to help you connect with nature:

  • Twin Lagoon
  • Kayangan Lake
  • Siete Pecados
  • Malcapuya Island
  • Banana Island
  • Maquinit Hot Springs
  • Skeleton Wreck

Hiking/Biking Routes of Coron

Coron isn’t only about water activities, but the island offers several hiking trails and biking routes for you to explore. Some popular ones include:

  • Mount Tapyas Trail
  • Lualhati Park Trail
  • Mount Darala Trail
  • Concepcion Waterfall Trail
  • Black Island Hill Viewpoint Trail

Best Things to Do in Coron: Activities and Tours

If you’re looking for activities and tours, Coron has a lot to offer. Some must-do activities include:

  • Scuba diving
  • Island hopping
  • Snorkeling
  • Paddleboarding
  • Surfing
  • Wildlife Safari

Week-Long Itinerary for Coron

If you’re planning to spend a week in Coron, here’s a sample itinerary to get the most out of your trip:

Day Activity
Day 1 Island hopping, kayaking
Day 2 Scuba diving, snorkeling
Day 3 Hiking Mount Tapyas, Maquinit Hot Springs
Day 4 Wildlife Safari
Day 5 Visiting historical sites and museums
Day 6 Exploring the island by bike or motorcycle
Day 7 Enjoying the beach, relaxing

Food and Drink Scene of Coron

Must-Try Dishes

Coron offers several delicious and unique dishes that you should try when you visit. Some of the best local dishes include:

  • Coconut Curry
  • Sisig
  • Kinilaw
  • Adobo
  • Crispy Pata
  • Lechon

Local Beverages

Coron is home to several refreshing and delicious drinks that you should try:

  • Buko Juice (fresh coconut water)
  • Sago’t Gulaman (sago pearls and jelly drink)
  • Tuba (coconut wine)
  • Calamansi Juice ( fresh sour calamansi fruit juice)

Where to Stay in Coron: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Coron offers a range of accommodations that suit all travel budgets and preferences. Some popular options include:

  • Two Seasons Coron Island Resort & Spa
  • Coron Westown Resort
  • Coron Underwater Garden Resort
  • Darayonan Lodge
  • Coron Ecolodge
  • Hop Hostel

What to Buy in Coron: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Coron has several unique souvenirs that you can buy to remind you of your time on the island. Some of the best souvenirs you can bring home include:

  • Hand-woven bags and hats
  • Pearls and shells
  • Handmade crafts, wood carvings, and accessories
  • Traditional Filipino products like balut and taho

Local Culture and Customs of Coron

Coron is home to a rich Filipino culture that values respect, hospitality, and humility. When visiting the island, it’s crucial to show respect to the locals and their customs.

Best Time to Visit Coron

The best time to visit Coron is between October to June. The weather is clear and sunny, and the temperature is comfortable during these months. It’s peak season starting mid-December till May when warm weather attracts many tourists.

Do’s and Don’ts of Coron

Do:

  • Respect the local culture and traditions.
  • Dress conservatively in religious places, public areas, and markets.
  • Haggle at markets and souvenir shops.
  • Take care of the environment by not littering and keeping the beaches clean.
  • Stay hydrated and always bring a water bottle.

Don’t:

  • Use single-use plastic items.
  • Take any coral, shells, or other marine life from the sea or beaches.
  • Wear shoes inside the house or religious places.
  • Smoke in public areas.

Safety and Health

Safety in Coron

While Coron is safe to visit, it’s essential to take precautious measures to ensure your safety:

  • Always carry a copy of your passport and travel documents.
  • Stay alert and cautious, especially when traveling alone at night.
  • Keep your valuables secure in your hotel room or locker.
  • Follow the instructions of your tour guide and never swim alone.
  • Keep emergency numbers with you.

Health in Coron

To avoid any health issues during your trip to Coron, it’s essential to take some necessary precautions:

  • Drink only bottled or purified water.
  • Avoid consuming food from street vendors and unhygienic places.
  • Always wear sunscreen and a hat to avoid a heatstroke.
  • Seek medical attention immediately if you feel sick or experience any symptoms.

Getting to Coron: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Coron

You can reach Coron by flying to either of the following airports:

  • Francisco B. Reyes Airport (Coron - Busuanga Airport)
  • Puerto Princesa International Airport

Bus Stations Near Coron

Coron can be reached by land from various cities of Palawan, including Puerto Princesa, via local buses and vans.

Other Options

There are several ferry and boat services available for transportation from other surrounding islands in Palawan and nearby areas.

How to Get Around in Coron

There are several transportation options for getting around Coron, including:

  • Tricycles
  • Motorbikes
  • Bicycle rentals
  • Private vans and buses

Where to Go Next from Coron: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Coron is just one of the many beautiful destinations in Palawan. After visiting Coron, you can explore other nearby destinations such as:

  • El Nido
  • Port Barton
  • Sabang
  • San Vicente
  • Taytay

Conclusion

Coron is a beautiful island that offers an unforgettable experience for all its visitors. Whether you’re looking to relax on the beach or explore the island’s outdoors, there’s something for everyone. Plan your trip using this comprehensive travel guide for Coron, and get ready for an amazing adventure.
