Introduction to Calamian Islands

Calamian Islands is a group of islands located in Northern Palawan, Philippines. The islands are known for their pristine beaches and crystal-clear waters, making them a popular destination for tourists who are seeking a relaxing getaway. The main islands in the group are Coron Island, Busuanga Island, and Culion Island.

The islands offer visitors a perfect mix of adventure, culture, and natural beauty. There are plenty of things to do and see on these islands, whether you’re looking to explore the underwater world, hike through the lush forests, or learn about the local culture and traditions.

List of Places to Visit in Calamian Islands

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Coron Island Historical Tour: Join a guided tour that takes you to the World War II shipwrecks and historical landmarks of Coron Island.
  • Culion Museum and Archives: Learn about the leper colony that existed on Culion Island from 1906 to 2006 and its impact on the island’s history.
  • Busuanga Island Cultural Village: This place offers visitors a glimpse of the local culture and traditions through performances, workshops, and exhibits.

Natural Attractions of Calamian Islands: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Coron Island Hopping Tour: Take a boat ride around Coron Island to see its stunning lagoons, beaches, and rock formations.
  • Maquinit Hot Springs: Relax in the natural hot springs on Coron Island, which are heated by nearby volcanic activity.
  • Mt. Tapyas View Deck: Hike up to the view deck to get a panoramic view of Coron town and the surrounding islands.
  • Calauit Safari Park: Visit this wildlife reserve on Calauit Island, which is home to African animals such as giraffes, zebras, and antelopes.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Calamian Islands

  • Mt. Dalara Trail: Hike to the highest peak in Busuanga Island and get stunning views of the surrounding islands.
  • Conception Falls Trail: Hike through the lush forests of Busuanga Island and see the beautiful waterfall at the end of the trail.
  • Calauit Wildlife Sanctuary Trail: Explore the wildlife sanctuary on a bike and spot the diverse wildlife along the way.

Best Things to Do in Calamian Islands: Activities and Tours

  • Scuba Diving and Snorkeling: Explore the diverse marine life of Calamian Islands through scuba diving or snorkeling.
  • Island Hopping Tour: Visit the different islands in Calamian Islands and see the stunning beaches and landscapes.
  • Kayaking: Paddle your way through the clear waters of Calamian Islands and get closer to the limestone cliffs and rock formations.
  • Sunset Cruise: Take a boat ride during sunset and watch the sky change colors over the islands.

Week-Long Itinerary for Calamian Islands

Day Activity

Food and Drink Scene of Calamian Islands

Must-Try Dishes

  • Kinilaw: A Filipino ceviche-style dish made with fresh raw fish or seafood, onion, ginger, and calamansi juice.
  • Adobo: A classic Filipino dish made with marinated chicken or pork in soy sauce, vinegar, and spices.
  • Sinigang: A sour soup dish made with tamarind, onion, tomatoes, and meat/fish.

Local Beverages

  • Coconut juice: Sip on the refreshing coconut juice, straight from the coconut itself.
  • Tuba: A local alcoholic drink made from fermented coconut sap.

Where to Stay in Calamian Islands: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Two Seasons Coron Island Resort & Spa: A luxurious resort on Coron Island with a private beach and overwater villas.
  • The Funny Lion: An eco-friendly boutique hotel in Coron town with a rooftop lounge.
  • Sea Dive Resort: A budget-friendly resort in Coron town with a diving center and a restaurant.

What to Buy in Calamian Islands: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Cashew nuts: Calamian Islands is known for its delicious cashew nuts, which make for a great souvenir or snack.
  • Handwoven bags and baskets: These are made by local communities and are great for carrying groceries or as an eco-friendly alternative to plastic bags.
  • Pearls: Palawan is known for its high-quality pearls, and you can find a selection of them in Coron town.

Local Culture and Customs of Calamian Islands

The people of Calamian Islands are predominantly Catholic, and many of their customs and traditions revolve around their faith. The islands also have a strong indigenous culture that is rooted in their relationship with the environment.

Best Time to Visit Calamian Islands

The best time to visit Calamian Islands is during the dry season, from November to May, when the skies are clear and the waters are calm. The summer months of March to May can be quite hot, so it’s best to come prepared with light clothing and sunscreen.

Do’s and Don’ts of Calamian Islands

Do:

  • Dress appropriately: When visiting churches or other religious sites, it’s important to dress modestly.
  • Learn some basic Tagalog phrases: This will help you communicate with locals and show your appreciation for their culture.
  • Respect the environment: Calamian Islands are a delicate ecosystem, so make sure to dispose of your waste properly and follow eco-friendly practices when possible.

Don’t:

  • Touch or feed the marine life: This can harm them and disrupt their natural behavior.
  • Wear shoes inside homes or establishments: It’s customary to remove shoes before entering someone’s home or a restaurant in the Philippines.
  • Disrespect the local culture and traditions: It’s important to be mindful of the local customs and show respect for the people and their way of life.

Safety and Health

Safety in Calamian Islands

Calamian Islands are generally safe for tourists, but it’s important to take some precautions:

  • Keep valuables and important documents in a safe or a secure place.
  • Avoid walking alone at night, particularly in isolated areas.
  • Be aware of your surroundings, especially in crowded places.

Health in Calamian Islands

It’s important to take necessary health precautions while traveling to Calamian Islands:

  • Drink only bottled or filtered water to avoid getting sick.
  • Apply insect repellent to avoid mosquito bites, which can carry diseases like dengue and malaria.
  • Pack any necessary medication and consult a doctor before your trip.

Getting to Calamian Islands: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Calamian Islands

  • Francisco B. Reyes Airport: The main airport in the region, located on Busuanga Island. It has flights from Manila and Cebu.

Bus Stations Near Calamian Islands

  • El Nido Bus Terminal: This terminal has buses going to and from Puerto Princesa, which is 240 kilometers south of Calamian Islands.

Other Options

  • Ferry: A regular ferry service runs between Coron and El Nido.
  • Private boat charter: This is an option for large groups or those who want a more personalized experience.

How to Get Around in Calamian Islands

  • Tricycles: These are the most common form of transportation in Calamian Islands and can be flagged down on the streets.
  • Motorbikes: Renting a motorbike is a popular option for exploring the islands independently.
  • Taxis: Taxis are available in larger towns like Coron.

Where to Go Next from Calamian Islands: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • El Nido: A popular tourist destination with stunning beaches and lagoons, located about 5 hours from Calamian Islands by ferry or bus.
  • Puerto Princesa: The capital city of Palawan, known for its underground river and wildlife reserve, located about 5-6 hours from Calamian Islands by land.
  • Tubbataha Reef: A UNESCO World Heritage Site and a popular diving destination, located in the middle of the Sulu Sea.

Conclusion

Calamian Islands offer a unique blend of natural beauty, adventure, and culture that is unmatched anywhere else. From hiking to island hopping, scuba diving to relaxing on white sandy beaches, there is something for everyone on these islands. The warm hospitality of the locals and the delicious cuisine are just the cherry on top. Plan your trip to Calamian Islands today and experience the ultimate island getaway.
