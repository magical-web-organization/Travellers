Introduction to Culion Island

Culion Island is a peaceful island located in the Calamian Islands of Palawan in the Philippines. It is known for its rich culture, historical significance, and stunning natural beauty. The island was once a leprosy colony, and while it has a dark past, it is now a popular tourist attraction for travelers from all around the world. With a population of only a few thousand people, the island exudes a peaceful and laidback vibe.

List of Places to Visit in Culion Island

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

There are several museums and historical places on Culion Island. It is a great opportunity for visitors to learn about the island’s past while enjoying magnificent views.

  • Culion Museum and Archives offers visitors a chance to learn about the island’s history. It features photographs, artifacts, and documents about the island’s leprosy colony and the remarkable stories of the patients and the island’s people as they came together to create a new community.

  • Culion Church is a beautiful, historic church built in the 17th century. It is a famous landmark in the island and a place of worship for the locals.

  • Fort Culion is another historic attraction located on the island. It was built in the early 1900s by the Americans as part of their defense system against the Japanese during World War II. It is now abandoned but remains an important part of the island’s history.

Natural Attractions of Culion Island: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

The natural beauty of Culion Island is unparalleled. Visitors can engage in outdoor adventures, experience different water sports activities, and bask in the tranquil scenery of the island.

  • Malcapuya Island is a pristine white sand island with crystal clear waters, perfect for swimming, snorkeling, and sunbathing.

  • Bulog Dos Island also boasts of white sand beaches surrounded by turquoise water. It has an underwater garden that travelers must see.

  • Kayangan Lake is a famous tourist destination in Palawan. It features emerald-green water and towering limestone formations. Visitors can take a dip in the clear and refreshing water of the lake.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Culion Island

The hills on the island provide great opportunities for hiking and biking. Some of the most popular trails are:

  • Mt. Tundalara hike is a moderate hike and rewards visitors with a panoramic view of the island and its surrounding islands.

  • Barangay Halsey Loop is an easy biking trail that takes visitors around the island’s picturesque villages.

Best Things to Do in Culion Island: Activities and Tours

There are lots of things to do in Culion Island. Here are some of the must-try activities:

  • Snorkeling and Scuba Diving offers travelers a chance to explore the breathtaking coral reefs and the marine life of Culion Island.

  • Island Hopping Tours allow visitors to cruise around the islands of Palawan and experience the beauty of Culion Island and its neighboring islands.

  • Sightseeing Tours provided by local tour companies offer visitors a chance to explore the island’s historic attractions and experience the beauty of its natural wonders.

Food and Drink Scene of Culion Island

Culion Island has a rich culinary tradition that reflects the island’s diverse cultural heritage. Here are some of the must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Adobong Pusit is a popular Filipino dish made of squid cooked in soy sauce and vinegar. The dish boasts of bold flavors characteristic of Filipino cuisine.

  • Ginataang Langka is a vegetable dish made with jackfruit and coconut milk. It is creamy and flavorful and is a must-try for vegetarians.

Local Beverages

  • Tuba is a local alcoholic drink that is made from coconut sap. It has a sweet and tangy taste and is often served in coconut shells.

  • Culion Craft Beer is a locally-made beer that offers a taste of the island’s unique flavors.

Where to Stay in Culion Island: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Culion Island offers several accommodation options that cater to different budget and travel styles. Here are some recommendations:

  • Culion Lodge and Dive Resort offers comfortable rooms fitted with modern amenities and has a private beach access.

  • La Estancia Busuanga Resort is a luxurious resort located in nearby Busuanga Island. It offers spacious rooms with stunning views of the sea.

  • Culion Homestay is a budget-friendly option for travelers looking to experience local living. The accommodation offers clean rooms and a home-cooked meal.

What to Buy in Culion Island: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Culion Island also offers several souvenir shops where visitors can buy unique and locally-made products. Shopping for souvenirs is also a great way to support the local economy. Here are some popular souvenir items:

  • Native Bags and Baskets are handmade from natural fibers like palm leaves and nito grass.

  • Pearl Jewelry is another popular souvenir item. Palawan is known for its high-quality pearls, and visitors can find unique and affordable jewelry pieces in souvenir shops around the island.

Local Culture and Customs of Culion Island

Culion Island has a unique culture and customs shaped by its diverse history and cultural influences. Here are some things to keep in mind when visiting the island:

  • Respect the Local Customs Visitors are encouraged to dress modestly and cover up when visiting religious sites and public places.

  • Be Mindful of the Environment Littering and damaging the environment is strongly discouraged, and visitors should always make sure to dispose of their garbage responsibly.

Best Time to Visit Culion Island

November to May is the best time to visit the island when the weather is sunny and dry. June to October is the rainy season in the Philippines and can make travel and outdoor activities challenging.

Do’s and Don’ts of Culion Island

Here are some things to keep in mind when visiting Culion Island:

Do:

  • Do take part in island-hopping tours and explore the natural beauty of the island.

  • Do immerse yourself in the island’s culture and visit its historic attractions.

Don’t:

  • Don’t litter or damage the environment.

  • Don’t disrespect the island’s culture and customs.

Safety and Health

Safety in Culion Island

Culion Island is generally safe for tourists. Visitors are advised to take the usual safety precautions and be mindful of their surroundings and belongings. It is advisable to travel with a companion, especially when engaging in outdoor activities.

Health in Culion Island

Visitors are advised to drink only bottled water and practice good hygiene to avoid getting sick. Although the island has a medical facility, it is advisable to bring necessary medications and supplies.

Getting to Culion Island: Transportation Options and Logistics

Culion Island is accessible through several modes of transportation. Here are some options to consider:

Airports Near Culion Island

The Francisco B. Reyes Airport (USU) in Busuanga Island is the nearest airport to Culion Island. Local airlines such as Cebu Pacific, Philippine Airlines, and SkyJet offer regular flights from Manila to Busuanga.

Bus Stations Near Culion Island

The nearest bus station is in Coron, where visitors can take a ferry to Culion Island. Ferries leave from the ferry terminal in Coron, and the ride takes about 2-3 hours.

Other Options

Visitors can also hire private boats or charter flights from Busuanga Island directly to Culion Island.

How to Get Around in Culion Island

Tricycles are the primary mode of transportation around the island. Visitors can rent tricycles or hire a driver for a full-day tour.

Where to Go Next from Culion Island: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Culion Island is a great starting point for exploring other nearby destinations in Palawan. Here are some recommendations:

  • Coron is a popular tourist destination. It is known for its diving spots, scenic islands, and picturesque scenery.

  • El Nido is another famous tourist destination known for its limestone cliffs, lagoons, and beach coves.

Conclusion

Culion Island is a unique travel destination that offers a glimpse into the rich culture and history of the Philippines. With its natural beauty, historic attractions, and warm hospitality, Culion Island is a must-visit for anyone looking for an off-the-beaten-path travel experience. Plan your trip now and make unforgettable memories in this gem of an island in Palawan.
