Busuanga Island Travel Guide

Busuanga Island is a serene and magical destination located in the Calamian Islands of Palawan, Philippines. The scenic beauty of the island, its pristine beaches, clear waters, and rich marine life, make it a favorite destination for adventure-seekers, nature lovers, and beach enthusiasts alike. In this travel guide, we’ll explore the best that Busuanga Island has to offer and discover why it’s a must-visit destination in Southeast Asia.

List of Places to Visit in Busuanga Island

Here are some of the top attractions and places to visit in Busuanga Island:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Lusong Coral Garden - Explore the coral reefs and snorkel over the shipwrecks from World War II.
  • Calauit Safari Park - Come close to Africa’s exotic wildlife in a natural habitat.
  • Tara Island - Discover the rich history and culture of the Tagbanua people.

Natural Attractions of Busuanga Island: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Kayangan Lake - Swim in the crystal clear waters of the Kayangan Lake surrounded by limestone cliffs.
  • Skeleton Wreck Dive Site - Dive into the historic site of wreck from World War II and explore underwater beauty.
  • Concepcion Falls - Chase waterfalls and hike through lush green forests to reach this hidden gem.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Busuanga Island

Busuanga Island has several hiking and biking routes that offer scenic views and an opportunity to explore the island’s natural beauty. Some of the popular routes include:

  • Mt. Tapyas - Hike to the top of the mountain and enjoy panoramic views of Coron town and Busuanga Island.
  • Calauit Game Preserve - Join a guided hike and explore the savannahs of Calauit Island.
  • Ocam-Ocam Beach - Take a bike ride to the beach and encounter a beautiful scenery of rural areas of Busuanga Island.

Best Things To Do in Busuanga Island: Activities and Tours

  • Island hopping - Rent a boat to explore the hidden coves and beaches of Busuanga Island and enjoy some snorkeling and swimming in the clear waters.
  • Scuba diving - Busuanga Island is a world-renowned scuba diving destination with diving sites like the Lusong Gunboat, Kyokuzan Maru, and Akitsushima wrecks creating excellent dive experiences.
  • Island Safari Adventure - Tour various island attractions, Calauit Safari Park, and rural settlements to get closer to local life and culture.

Week-Long Itinerary for Busuanga Island

Here’s a sample itinerary on how you can make the most of your trip to Busuanga Island:

Day Itinerary
1 Arrive in Busuanga and check-in to your hotel
2 Island-hopping tour and explore the clear waters and beaches
3 Scuba diving to discover the underwater beauty of Busuanga Island
4 Hiking and biking trip to Mt. Tapyas and Calauit Game Preserve
5 Tour to Lusong Coral Garden, Concepcion Falls, and Tara Island
6 Relax at Black Island, Malajon Island, and Buluang Beach
7 Depart from Busuanga Island and head back home

Food and Drink Scene of Busuanga Island

Busuanga Island is well-known for its local delicacies and refreshing drinks that are definitely worth trying out.

Must-Try Dishes

  • Adobo - A famous Filipino dish with flavors of soy sauce, garlic, vinegar, and bay leaf.
  • Kinilaw - A local recipe of raw fish, vinegar, and spices.
  • Sisig - A sizzling dish made from pork cheeks, ears, and liver.

Local Beverages

  • Fresh Buko Juice - Chilled coconut juice with refreshing qualities.
  • Tanduay Rum - A popular rum made from sugarcane that can be paired with cola or simply served chilled over ice.

Where to Stay in Busuanga Island

Busuanga Island offers plenty of options for travelers like hotels, hostels, and guesthouses that cater to all kinds of budgets. Some top-rated places include:

  • Al Faro Cosmio Hotel - A boutique hotel offering a panoramic view of the Busuanga Bay and surrounding islands.
  • KokosNuss Garden Resort - A beachfront hotel located on the sandy shores of Black Island that offers a serene environment.
  • Busuanga Island Paradise - A resort located in Concepcion and providing access to a private beach.

What to Buy in Busuanga Island: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Busuanga Island is famous for its local handicrafts, pearls, and dried seafood products that make great souvenirs to bring back home. Some of the things you can buy include:

  • Black Island Cowl Necklaces - These elegant necklaces are made by local artisans with black pearls from the nearby waters.
  • Kawayan Hats - These handcrafted hats are made using bamboo sourced from the local areas.
  • Dried Fish and Squid - These make great souvenirs for seafood lovers and easy to pack and carry items.

Local Culture and Customs of Busuanga Island

Busuanga Island is home to the Tagbanua people, who have a rich culture and customs that visitors should respect as a part of the local experience. Visitors should dress appropriately, remove shoes when entering homes and be mindful of taking photos of the people living there.

Best Time to Visit Busuanga Island

The best time to visit Busuanga Island is during the dry season from January to May, where the sunny and dry weather is perfect for island hopping, scuba diving, and outdoor adventures. The rainy season from June to November can sometimes lead to typhoons and may cause travel disruptions.

Do’s and Don’ts of Busuanga Island

Do:

  • Respect the local culture and customs.
  • Bring cash as ATMs and credit card facilities can be limited
  • Try the local food and drinks

Don’t:

  • Litter or damage the natural environment
  • Disturb the wildlife or remove any natural resources
  • Disrespect the local residents or their culture

Safety and Health

Safety in Busuanga Island

Busuanga Island is generally a safe place for tourists, but like any other destination, visitors should take necessary precautions to ensure their safety. Avoid going out alone at night especially in isolated areas, keep an eye on belongings and stay aware of the surroundings.

Health in Busuanga Island

Visitors should drink bottled water or boil the tap water before consumption to avoid any water-borne diseases. Bring sunscreen and insect repellant as the weather can be hot and humid leading to sunburns and insect bites.

Getting to Busuanga Island: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Busuanga Island

Francisco B. Reyes Airport is the only airport on Busuanga Island and is served by various airlines like Cebu Pacific, Philippine Airlines, SkyJet, and Air Juan with flights offered daily from Manila and Cebu.

Bus Stations Near Busuanga Island

There are no direct bus stations in Busuanga Island, but there are various transportation companies from Coron Town that offer buses and vans to other destinations in Palawan and the Philippines.

Other Options

There are ferry options from El Nido or Coron Town that can be taken to reach Busuanga Island by sea.

How To Get Around in Busuanga Island

Hiring a private boat, bike rental or tricycle taxi is the easiest way to get around Busuanga Island. Some people prefer taking motorbikes or renting a car, however, it is advised to be mindful of the various road conditions and ensure that you have an experienced driver with you.

Where to Go Next from Busuanga Island: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • El Nido - El Nido is known for its white-sand beaches, clear waters, and limestone cliffs. It’s a three-hour ferry ride from Busuanga Island.
  • Coron Town - Coron Town is another popular destination known for its shipwreck diving sites and breathtaking lagoons. It’s just an hour ferry ride from Busuanga Island.
  • Puerto Princesa - Explore the underground river and limestone caves of Palawan – a six-hour ferry ride away from Busuanga Island.

Conclusion

We hope this guide has given you a glimpse of the wonders of Busuanga Island, from its natural to cultural beauty. There are still many other things to discover and experience while on the island, which is why it’s on the bucket list of many travelers around the globe. Always travel responsibly, be respectful of the local culture and customs, and enjoy your journey to this magical island paradise!
