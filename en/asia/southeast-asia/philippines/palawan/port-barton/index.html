Port Barton Travel Guide: A Comprehensive Tourist’s Handbook

If you’re looking for a tropical paradise getaway, Port Barton in Palawan, Philippines is a highly recommended destination. With its powdery white sand beaches, clear turquoise waters, stunning coral reefs, and lush tropical forests, Port Barton offers the perfect balance of relaxation and adventure.

Introduction to Port Barton

Located in the northern part of Palawan, Port Barton is a small fishing village that has become a popular tourist destination for its pristine beaches and laid-back atmosphere. Despite its growing popularity, Port Barton has managed to maintain its natural beauty and simple way of life.

List of Places to Visit in Port Barton

Here are the must-visit attractions in Port Barton, categorized based on their nature:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  1. Port Barton Marine Park - A protected area that showcases the beauty of Palawan’s marine life, including stunning coral reefs, diverse species of fish, and other sea creatures.

  2. Tao Philippines - A social enterprise that takes visitors on an eye-opening cultural immersion experience, featuring an eco-friendly lifestyle, traditional fishing, and weaving practices, and an off-the-beaten-path tour of Port Barton’s hidden gems.

Natural Attractions of Port Barton: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  1. White Beach - A pristine stretch of sand that stretches for miles, surrounded by coconut trees and the turquoise sea; perfect for swimming, sunbathing, and beach activities.

  2. Inaladelan Island - Also known as German Island, a popular spot for island hopping tours, with its white sand beaches, crystal-clear waters, and a stunning view of the surrounding islands.

  3. Port Barton Waterfalls - A 30-minute trek through the lush forest takes you to a refreshing waterfall cascade that you can swim in.

  4. Tres Marias - A trio of rocky islets off the coast of Port Barton with a mesmerizing turquoise lagoon, abundant marine life, and stunning coral reefs ideal for snorkeling and scuba diving.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Port Barton

Port Barton is also a great destination for outdoor enthusiasts. Here are some of the best hiking and biking trails to explore:

  1. Port Barton to Pamuayan Falls Trek - A 3-hour trek in the mountains in the heart of Palawan, with a refreshing stop-over at the Pamuayan Falls.

  2. Pamoayan Village Ride - A leisurely bicycling route that takes you through the rustic Pamuayan Village, where you can meet locals, learn about their culture and livelihood, and enjoy scenic views of the nearby mountains.

Best Things to Do in Port Barton: Activities and Tours

Aside from the must-visit attractions, here are some of the exciting activities and tours you can enjoy in Port Barton:

  1. Island Hopping - A must-do activity when in Port Barton, with tours that take you to secluded islands with pristine beaches, stunning coral reefs, and abundant marine life.

  2. Snorkeling and Scuba Diving - Port Barton is a top spot for underwater adventures, with various dive sites teeming with colorful corals and marine creatures.

  3. Sunset Cruise - Watch the sunset while cruising the calm waters of Port Barton, with an unlimited supply of cold drinks and snacks from the onboard bar.

Week-Long Itinerary for Port Barton

Here’s a suggested week-long itinerary for Port Barton:

Day 1: Arrive in Puerto Princesa, Palawan, and drive to Port Barton (3 hours) Day 2: Island hopping tour, snorkeling, and beach bumming Day 3: Scuba diving trip to Tres Marias or other nearby dive sites Day 4: Trek to Pamuayan Falls, swimming, and picnic lunch Day 5: Tao Philippines cultural immersion tour Day 6: Relax on the beach, sunset cruise Day 7: Depart Port Barton for your next destination

Food and Drink Scene of Port Barton

Aside from the stunning natural beauty, Port Barton also offers a variety of food and drink options to indulge in.

Must-Try Dishes

  1. Adobong Pusit - Grilled squid simmered in vinegar, soy sauce, and spices.
  2. Kinilaw - A local ceviche dish made with fresh seafood, coconut vinegar, and onions.
  3. Chicken Inasal - Skewered and grilled chicken marinated in lemon grass and spices, served with garlic rice.

Local Beverages

  1. Buko Juice - Coconut water served ice cold straight from the coconut.
  2. San Miguel Beer - The most popular beer in the Philippines, a refreshing choice for hot weather.

Where to Stay in Port Barton: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Port Barton offers various types of accommodations, ranging from high-end resorts to budget-friendly guesthouses. Here are some of the best options:

  1. Deep Moon Resort - A highly recommended beachfront resort with rooms that offer stunning views of the beach.

  2. Ausan Beach Front Cottage - This budget-friendly option offers clean and comfortable rooms that are just steps away from the beach.

  3. Reef Hill - A boutique resort that blends modern luxury with traditional Filipino design, offering an unforgettable experience.

What to Buy in Port Barton: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

If you’re looking for souvenirs to bring back home, here are some of the local products and items to buy:

  1. Handcrafted shell jewelry - Made from shells that are abundant in the beaches of Port Barton, these jewelry items are a great way to bring a piece of Palawan with you.

  2. Woven bags and baskets - Made by the locals using local materials such as Pandan and Abaca.

Local Culture and Customs of Port Barton

Port Barton is a small community that is heavily influenced by Filipino culture. Visitors are expected to respect the local customs, such as dressing modestly, taking your shoes off before entering a home, and avoiding public displays of affection.

Best Time to Visit Port Barton

The best time to visit Port Barton is during the dry season, which runs from December to May. The weather is generally sunny and dry, with occasional rains during the months of January and February.

Do’s and Don’ts of Port Barton

Here are some of the do’s and don’ts when visiting Port Barton:

Do:

  1. Respect the natural beauty of Port Barton, avoid littering and do not damage the environment.
  2. Be respectful and mindful of the local culture, customs and traditions.
  3. Choose eco-friendly tours and accommodations whenever possible.

Don’t:

  1. Do not step on coral reefs or take any marine life from Port Barton.
  2. Do not leave trash on the beach or throw it in the sea.
  3. Do not engage in any illegal activities.

Safety and Health

Safety in Port Barton

Port Barton is generally safe for tourists. However, as with any destinations, it’s important to take certain precautions:

  1. Avoid walking alone at night, especially in remote areas.
  2. Secure your valuables and important documents in a safe place.
  3. Always wear a life vest or a personal floatation device when engaging in water activities.

Health in Port Barton

While Port Barton is generally a safe destination, it’s important to take care of your health and well-being:

  1. Drink only bottled or purified water to avoid getting sick.
  2. Wear sunscreen and insect repellent to protect yourself from the sun and bugs.
  3. If you have a medical condition, bring enough medication and consult with your doctor before traveling.

Getting to Port Barton: Transportation Options and Logistics

Port Barton is located in the northern part of Palawan and can be accessed through various modes of transportation.

Airports Near Port Barton

The nearest major airport to Port Barton is the Puerto Princesa Airport, which is approximately 150 kilometers away. From the airport, there are various van services and private transfers that will take you to Port Barton.

Bus Stations Near Port Barton

There are no direct bus services to Port Barton, but there are several bus stations in Puerto Princesa where you can catch a ride to San Vicente, which is the nearest town to Port Barton. From San Vicente, you can take a tricycle or a motorbike to reach Port Barton.

Other Options

There are also private transfer services that will take you directly from your hotel in Puerto Princesa to Port Barton. Some tour operators also offer packages that include transportation to and from Port Barton.

How to Get Around in Port Barton

The easiest way to get around in Port Barton is by walking, as the town is small and the attractions are located within close proximity to each other. Tricycles are also available for hire, and are convenient for going to places outside the town proper.

Where to Go Next from Port Barton: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Here are some of the nearby destinations you can visit after exploring Port Barton:

  1. El Nido - A popular tourist destination that offers stunning limestone cliffs, hidden lagoons, and secret beaches.

  2. San Vicente - A laid-back beach town that has recently been developed to cater to tourists, with pristine beaches and eco-friendly resorts.

  3. Sabang - Home to the famous Puerto Princesa Subterranean River, a UNESCO World Heritage Site that features a ~5-mile river running through a mountain cave.

Conclusion

Port Barton is a tropical paradise that offers endless opportunities for adventure, relaxation, and cultural immersion. With its stunning natural beauty, friendly locals, and relaxed vibe, Port Barton is a destination you won’t want to leave. Whether you’re a nature lover, a beach bum, or a culture enthusiast, Port Barton has something for you. So pack your bags, surf or paddle along the beaches and coastlines, and explore the hidden gems of the beautiful and scenic Port Barton.
