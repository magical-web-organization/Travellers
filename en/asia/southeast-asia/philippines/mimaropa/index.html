Introduction to Mimaropa

Located in the southern part of Luzon island in the Philippines, Mimaropa stands for the provinces of Mindoro, Marinduque, Romblon, and Palawan. This region boasts natural wonders, rich cultural heritage, and exciting adventure destinations. From stunning beaches, lush forest reserves, beautiful dive sites, religious landmarks, and historical sites, Mimaropa has everything that any tourist could wish for.

Read on for our comprehensive Mimaropa travel guide that will help you plan your trip to this paradise on earth.

List of Places to Visit in Mimaropa

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

1. Boac Museum (Marinduque)

Boac Museum is a must-visit attraction for history buffs. This museum houses a collection of artifacts, photos, and memorabilia that tells the story of Marinduque province. Get a glimpse of the local culture and history by checking out the exhibits featuring prehistoric antiques, Spanish colonialism relics, and World War II artifacts.

2. Luzon Datum of 1911 (Occidental Mindoro)

Located in San Jose town, the Luzon Datum of 1911 is a cultural landmark that served as the basis for the geodetic survey of the Philippines. The marker tells the story of the country’s geodetic history and attracts geodetic engineers, historians, and travelers who are interested in the occult.

Natural Attractions of Mimaropa: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

1. Puerto Princesa Underground River (Palawan)

The Puerto Princesa Underground River is one of the most popular tourist attractions in the Philippines. This natural wonder is a UNESCO World Heritage Site and one of the New Seven Wonders of Nature. The river flows under a limestone cave system and features breathtaking rock formations, diverse flora, and fauna. Visitors can take a scenic boat ride while admiring the majestic scenery.

2. Mt. Halcon (Oriental Mindoro)

Mt. Halcon is a majestic mountain in Oriental Mindoro. This mountain is a haven for hikers and nature lovers because of the panoramic views from the summit. The trek is challenging, and climbers are advised to hire an experienced guide to ensure their safety.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Mimaropa

1. Marinduque Island

Exploring Marinduque Island by bike or hike is a great way to take in the beauty of this island. The roads wind through quaint towns, rolling hills, and pristine beaches. Visitors can stop by the Bathala Caves or Mogpog Antique Houses to learn about the local history and culture.

Best Things to Do in Mimaropa: Activities and Tours

1. Island Hopping in El Nido (Palawan)

El Nido is a beach lover’s paradise. One of the best ways to enjoy the beauty of this island is by doing an island-hopping tour. Experience pristine beaches, secret lagoons, colorful coral gardens, and crystal-clear waters. Relax on white sand beaches, swim in azure waters, and take Instagram-worthy photos.

2. Muog Trek (Marinduque)

The Muog Trek is an exciting activity for adventure seekers who want to immerse themselves in Marinduque’s culture and nature. This trek takes visitors through lush forests, local farms, and indigenous villages. Visitors can learn about the history and culture of the place and enjoy the stunning views from the summit.

Food and Drink Scene of Mimaropa

Must-Try Dishes

1. Lechon (Marinduque)

Lechon is a local specialty dish that is a must-try for food lovers. The dish features a roasted pig, cooked to perfection, with crispy skin. Lechon is often served during special occasions and celebrations.

2. Kinunot na Pagi (Palawan)

Kinunot na Pagi is another must-try dish in Mimaropa. This dish is a spicy specialty made from stingray meat, coconut cream, and chili peppers. It is often served with rice and vegetables and is a favorite among locals and tourists alike.

Local Beverages

1. Calamansi Juice (Mindoro)

Calamansi Juice is a refreshing drink made from Calamansi citrus fruits and can be found in any local restaurant or street vendor. The drink is used to cleanse the pallet and improve digestion.

2. Bignay Wine (Palawan)

Bignay Wine is a popular local drink in Palawan. It is made from bignay berries and is known for its tart and sweet taste. It’s a perfect beverage to enjoy after a long day of sightseeing.

Where to Stay in Mimaropa: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

1. Monti’s Place (Marinduque)

Monti’s Place is a cozy and affordable hotel located in Marinduque. The hotel is popular for its comfortable rooms, friendly staff, and excellent location. It’s perfect for travelers who want to have a peaceful and relaxing stay in Marinduque.

2. El Nido Resorts (Palawan)

El Nido Resorts is one of the best hotels in Palawan. The hotel is luxurious, eco-friendly, and offers breathtaking views of the beach and the ocean. It is a perfect place to stay for honeymooners, couples, and families.

What to Buy in Mimaropa: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

1. Marinduque’s Marble Products

Marinduque is known for its marble products, which are made from high-quality marble. These products are a perfect souvenir to bring home because they reflect the unique culture and heritage of the place.

2. Romblon’s Hand-woven Products

Romblon’s Hand-woven Products are must-buy souvenirs. These products reflect the local craftsmanship of the province and are made from high-quality materials. Visitors can buy bags, coasters, and other items made from the traditional woven materials.

Local Culture and Customs of Mimaropa

Mimaropa is a region with a rich cultural heritage. Visitors are advised to respect local traditions, including religious customs and traditions. The locals are friendly and hospitable but it’s best to ask permission before taking photos.

Best Time to Visit Mimaropa

The best time to visit Mimaropa is during the dry season, from November to April. During this season, the weather is warm and dry, making it perfect for outdoor activities and beach adventures. Peak season starts from December to February, but visitors can still enjoy the beauty of the region outside these months.

Do’s and Don’ts of Mimaropa

Do:

  • Respect the local customs and traditions
  • Behave appropriately in religious places and cultural landmarks
  • Be mindful of the environment

Don’t:

  • Litter or damage the natural environment
  • Wear revealing clothing in religious places
  • Take photos without permission of locals

Safety and Health

Safety in Mimaropa

Mimaropa is generally safe for travelers. However, visitors should take precautions when traveling to remote and isolated locations in the region. Petty crimes such as pickpocketing can occur, so travelers should exercise caution and keep their belongings safe.

Health in Mimaropa

Visitors should make sure to have updated vaccinations before traveling to Mimaropa. Dengue fever is prevalent in some parts of the region, especially during the wet season. Visitors should bring insect repellent and take necessary precautions to avoid mosquito bites. Visitors should also bring sunscreen and stay hydrated to avoid heatstroke.

Getting to Mimaropa: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Mimaropa

The main airports in Mimaropa are Tugdan Airport (Romblon), Marinduque Airport, and Francisco B. Reyes Airport (Palawan). These airports offer daily flights to and from Manila and other major cities in the Philippines.

Bus Stations Near Mimaropa

Bus companies offer trips to Mimaropa from Manila and other major cities in Luzon. Visitors can take a bus to Lucena or Batangas Port and travel via a ferry to Mimaropa.

Other Options

Visitors can also take a private car or hire a van to travel to Mimaropa from Manila or other provinces in Luzon. Tricycles and jeepneys are the main modes of transport within the region.

How to Get Around in Mimaropa

The best way to get around Mimaropa is by renting a car or hiring a van. Visitors can also take public transport such as tricycles or jeepneys to get to nearby towns and attractions.

Where to Go Next from Mimaropa: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

1. Cebu

Cebu is a popular tourist destination in the Philippines known for its rich history, white sand beaches, and beautiful islands. Visitors can take a flight from Francisco B. Reyes Airport in Palawan to Cebu’s Mactan-Cebu International Airport.

2. Visayas

Visayas is a region consisting of several islands in the central part of the Philippines. Visitors can take a ferry or book a flight from Francisco B. Reyes Airport or Marinduque Airport to access Visayas’s destinations such as Bohol, Dumaguete, and Siquijor.

Conclusion

Mimaropa is a hidden gem in the Philippines with its natural beauty, rich culture, and adventure destinations. With its crystal-clear waters, stunning landscapes, and warm hospitality, it is one of the best places to relax, unwind, and enjoy your vacation.
