Introduction to Calaguas Islands

Calaguas Islands is a hidden treasure located in Bicol, a region in Luzon, the Philippines, situated in Southeast Asia. It is one of the most beautiful and unspoiled destinations in the country, known for its white sand beaches, turquoise waters, and picturesque landscape. The islands offer a wide range of activities, from swimming and sunbathing to hiking and camping. Whether you’re looking for a relaxing getaway or an adventure-filled vacation, Calaguas Islands has something for everyone.

List of Places to Visit in Calaguas Islands

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Despite being a natural paradise, Calaguas Islands also has cultural attractions that offer a glimpse into its rich history. The following are some of the most popular cultural destinations to visit in Calaguas:

  • Paracale Church: A centuries-old church that is a significant cultural landmark in the region
  • Paracale Gold Mines: The oldest gold mining site in the Philippines, with a rich history dating back to the Spanish colonial period

Natural Attractions of Calaguas Islands: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Calaguas Islands is known for its natural beauty and offers a plethora of outdoor activities.

  • Mahabang Buhangin Beach: A long stretch of white sand beach, perfect for swimming, sunbathing, and water activities
  • Balagbag Island: A small island with crystal clear waters that is a popular spot for diving and snorkeling
  • Tinaga Island Lighthouse: Provides an incredible panoramic view of the entire area
  • Mountaineering: Treks through the forested trails offer a chance to spot local wildlife and beautiful scenery.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Calaguas Islands

Calaguas Islands offers several hiking trails that provide stunning views of the surrounding landscape.

  • Mountaineering: While the initial climb can be challenging, the trail offers breathtaking views of the sea and the island’s verdant forests.
  • Bike trails: The island also has bike trails that offer a unique way to explore the area.

Best Things to Do in Calaguas Islands: Activities and Tours

Calaguas Islands is an incredible destination that offers an abundance of activities and tours. Some of the best things to do are:

  • Beach hopping: Calaguas Islands has numerous beaches that offer different experiences, including Mahabang Buhangin Beach, Balagbag Island, and Bagasbas Beach.
  • Snorkeling and diving: The island’s clear waters and marine biodiversity make it ideal for underwater adventures.
  • Island hopping tours: A good way to explore the islands and enjoy different activities such as kayaking, swimming, and snorkeling.

Week-Long Itinerary for Calaguas Islands

Here is a suggested week-long itinerary for Calaguas Islands:

  • Day 1-2: Explore the cultural sites such as Paracale Church and Gold Mines.
  • Day 3-4: Spend time at Mahabang Buhangin Beach and Balagbag Island while enjoying activities like snorkeling and swimming.
  • Day 5-6: Go hiking or biking on some of the island’s trails.
  • Day 7: Take an island-hopping tour to see more of the region.

Food and Drink Scene of Calaguas Islands

Must-Try Dishes

Calaguas Islands is known for its seafood-centric dishes, such as:

  • Kinunot: a creamy dish made from coconut milk, local vegetables, and fresh seafood
  • Tuwalya: Grilled giant squid and popular Calaguas snack food
  • Bicol Express: A favorite spicy pork dish made with coconut milk.

Local Beverages

The region offers locally produced beverages, including:

  • Lambanog: A distilled coconut spirit that is popular in the Philippines
  • Bicol Coffee: Also known as Barako Coffee, this strong coffee is made from beans grown in the region

Where to Stay in Calaguas Islands: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Calaguas Islands has various accommodation options, including:

  • Tents: The island offers camping facilities, where travelers can rent a tent and pitch it on the beach.
  • Guesthouses and Homestays: Simple and affordable accommodation with an authentic Filipino touch
  • Resorts: Some of the islands offer modest and luxurious accommodation with restaurants and amenities.

What to Buy in Calaguas Islands: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Travelers can bring home an array of local souvenirs and products from Calaguas Islands. Some popular options include:

  • Bicol handicrafts such as bags made from local fibers/domesticated coconut leaves
  • Fresh seafood like fish crackers, dried fish, or tuyo, which is salted and dried fish or squid
  • Organic products like honeys and bee pollen from different indigenous bees within the region.

Local Culture and Customs of Calaguas Islands

Calaguas Islands is home to several indigenous ethnic groups, each with its unique traditions and customs.

Visitors to Calaguas Island should always observe local customs and etiquette around religious and cultural sites and be aware of the surrounding environment.

Best Time to Visit Calaguas Islands

The best time to visit Calaguas Islands is during the dry season, from November to May, when the weather is warm and sunny. The rainy season, from June to October, can bring thunderstorms, rough seas, and monsoon winds, which can disrupt travel plans.

Do’s and Don’ts of Calaguas Islands

Do:

  • Respect the local customs and traditions
  • Protect the environment by avoiding littering, overfishing, or disturbing marine life
  • Keep hydrated and wear sunscreen as the sun can be strong

Don’t:

  • Take any marine or wildlife with you, such as shells or starfish
  • Venture into unmarked or unfamiliar hiking paths without a guide.

Safety and Health

Safety in Calaguas Islands

Calaguas Islands is generally safe but visitors should always take precautions, such as:

  • Avoid venturing out in the open sea if weather alerts, typhoons, or storms are announced in the area
  • Be mindful of theft and careful when carrying money and valuables

Health in Calaguas Islands

Visitors to Calaguas Islands should bring any necessary medications as pharmacies may not be available on all the islands. Ensure to bring essential supplies, including insect repellents and sunscreen to protect against mosquitoes and strong sunshine.

Getting to Calaguas Islands: Transportation Options and Logistics

The recommended mode of transportation to reach Calaguas Islands is by boat from the Paracale Fish Port, located in Camarines Norte. Visitors have the option to hire private boats to transport them directly to the island, or take public boats that run on a schedule. (As some travelers find difficulty in navigating by themselves in an unfamiliar environment).

Airports Near Calaguas Islands

The nearest airport to Calaguas Islands is the Naga airport, which is a three to four-hour drive from Paracale Port.

Bus Stations Near Calaguas Islands

Several bus companies operate services to Camarines Norte. One can take any of the following bus companies and travel by land; Penafrancia Tours, Philtranco, Superlines, DLTB, and RSL.

Other Options

Visitors can also hire a private car or shuttle service from Naga City or Manila, which takes approximately 7-8 hours.

How to Get Around in Calaguas Islands

Visitors to Calaguas Island can explore the region on foot by hiking or biking. For longer distances, travelers can hire motorcycles or rent bicycles.

Where to Go Next from Calaguas Islands: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

After exploring Calaguas Islands, visitors can also check out additional attractions and places nearby, such as:

  • Caramoan Peninsula: A beautiful peninsula where tourists can enjoy beaches, hikes, and water activities.
  • Mount Isarog: A towering mountain that offers several hiking trails through dense forests.
  • Albay Province: located in Bicol region, it is another Central Luzon province of the Philippines that has world-famous tourist destinations such as the Mayon Volcano, Legaspi Boulevard, and Embarcadero de Legaspi.

Conclusion

Calaguas Islands is a beautiful and pristine destination that offers a combination of natural beauty and cultural significance. With several activities and attractions, it’s a perfect spot for travelers seeking adventure, relaxation, and exploration. This travel guide should help you plan your next visit to this gem of the Philippines.
