Introduction to Bicol

Situated in Luzon, the largest island in the Philippines, Bicol offers a unique travel experience for both adventurers and nature-lovers. Bicol’s stunning natural beauty and rich cultural heritage make it a must-visit destination in the Philippines.

Bicol is known for its spicy cuisine, beautiful beaches, and majestic hills. You can expect to find a mix of modern cities and untouched wilderness in this region. Home to the famous Mayon Volcano, Bicol is also a popular destination for thrill-seekers.

In this comprehensive guide, we’ll take you through everything you need to know about visiting Bicol, from the best places to visit and things to do, to where to stay and what to eat.

Food and Drink Scene of Bicol

Must-Try Dishes

  • Bicol Express - a spicy pork stew
  • Pinangat - a combination of taro leaves, pork, and small shrimps cooked in coconut milk
  • Laing - a spicy dish made of taro leaves and coconut milk
  • Kinunot - a dish made with stingray cooked in coconut milk and chili peppers

Local Beverages

  • Bicol coffee
  • Sili (Chili) Ice Cream

Local Culture and Customs of Bicol

Bicolanos are warm, hospitable, and proud of their heritage. They have a strong connection to their language, Bicolano, which has different dialects depending on the region. One distinctive tradition is the Bicol Express, a dance that honors their patron saint and reflects the experience of Bicolanos under colonial rule.

Best Time to Visit Bicol

The best time to visit Bicol is during the dry season from November to April. This season is sunny and warm, making it perfect for outdoor activities and sightseeing. The wet season from May to October can be unpredictable, with occasional typhoons and heavy rains that can affect travel plans.

Do’s and Don’ts of Bicol

Do:

  • Try the local cuisine.
  • Respect the local culture and customs.
  • Bring a jacket in case the weather changes.
  • Take part in local festivities and traditions.

Don’t:

  • Litter in public places
  • Touch or step on coral reefs during your underwater activities
  • Engage in behavior that may offend the locals.

Safety and Health

Safety in Bicol

Bicol is a safe region for tourists. However, it’s always best to take proper precautions when traveling to ensure your safety. Make sure to keep your belongings safe and be vigilant when outdoors, especially at night.

Health in Bicol

Be prepared for the heat and humidity in Bicol. Bring sunscreen, a hat, and light clothing, and stay hydrated by drinking plenty of water. If you have allergies or a medical condition, carry your medications with you at all times.
