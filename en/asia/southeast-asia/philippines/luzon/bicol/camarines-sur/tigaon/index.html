Introduction to Tigaon

Tigaon is a fifth-class municipality in Camarines Sur, a province in the Bicol Region of Luzon in the Philippines. It is located in the southeastern part of the province, and is surrounded by scenic mountains and verdant forests. Tigaon is known for its rich cultural heritage, natural wonders, and warm hospitality. Whether you are a history buff, an outdoor adventurer, or a foodie, you will surely find something to love about this charming town.

List of Places to Visit in Tigaon

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • St. Nicholas of Tolentino Parish Church - This Baroque-style church, built in the 1800s, is an important cultural and religious landmark in Tigaon. It features intricate carvings and murals, and is one of the most awe-inspiring churches in the province.

  • Sunflower Farm - Located in Barangay San Rafael, this farm is a popular spot for photography enthusiasts who want to capture the stunning beauty of blooming sunflowers.

  • Tigaon Municipal Hall and Park - The municipal hall, built in 1915, is an impressive example of American colonial architecture. The park is a great place to relax and people-watch.

Natural Attractions of Tigaon: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Malabsay Falls - A stunning waterfall nestled in a lush forest, Malabsay Falls is one of the most popular destinations in Tigaon. Visitors can hike to the falls, swim in its crystal-clear waters, and take in the breathtaking view.

  • Mount Isarog National Park - Home to an abundance of flora and fauna, this park is a favorite among hikers, bird-watchers, and nature lovers. It offers several hiking trails ranging from easy to challenging, with breathtaking views of the surrounding mountains and forests.

  • Buhi Lake - A large freshwater lake in the Bicol Region, Buhi Lake is a great place for fishing, swimming, and boating. Visitors can enjoy the stunning scenery, the serene atmosphere, and the fresh seafood.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Tigaon

  • Mount Isarog traverse from Panicuason to Tigaon - A challenging and rewarding hike that takes you through dense forests, steep ridges, and stunning viewpoints. It is recommended to do the hike with a guide.

  • Malabsay Falls trek - A moderate hike that leads to the stunning Malabsay Falls. The trail passes through lush forests and allows visitors to take in the beauty of the waterfall up close.

  • Biking trails around Tigaon - There are several biking trails around Tigaon that cater to different levels of difficulty and terrain. Rent a bike and explore the town and its surroundings at your own pace.

Best Things to Do in Tigaon: Activities and Tours

  • Waterfall-hopping tour - Join a tour that will take you to the different waterfalls in Tigaon. This is a great way to explore the town’s natural wonders and experience the local culture.

  • Historical and cultural tour - Explore the churches, museums, and landmarks that showcase Tigaon’s rich cultural heritage. Learn about the town’s history and traditions from knowledgeable guides.

  • Food tour - Tigaon is known for its delicious seafood, local delicacies, and exotic drinks. Join a food tour and sample the best dishes and drinks that the town has to offer.

Food and Drink Scene of Tigaon

Must-Try Dishes

  • Bicol Express - A spicy dish made with pork, coconut milk, and chili peppers. It is a staple in Bicolano cuisine.

  • Laing - A vegetable dish made with taro leaves, coconut milk, and spices. It is a popular side dish or viand in Bicol.

  • Kinunot na Pagi - A soup made with stingray meat, coconut milk, and chili peppers. It is a specialty of the Bicol Region.

Local Beverages

  • Siling Labuyo Juice - A refreshing drink made from the local chili pepper. It is said to have health benefits and is a must-try for those who love spicy food.

  • Buko Juice - A popular drink made from fresh coconut water. It is sweet, refreshing, and perfect for quenching your thirst on a hot day.

Where to Stay in Tigaon: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Villa del Rey - A popular resort hotel that offers comfortable rooms, swimming pools, and a restaurant.

  • Buhi Resort - A cozy guesthouse by the lake that offers stunning views, friendly hosts, and homey rooms.

  • Tigaon Municipal Hostel - A budget-friendly hostel that offers dormitory-style rooms, a common kitchen, and a great location in the town center.

What to Buy in Tigaon: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Pili Nuts - A delicious and healthy snack made from the local pili nut. It comes in different flavors and variants, such as roasted, sweetened, or coated.

  • Handicrafts - Tigaon is known for its woven handicrafts, such as baskets, mats, and table runners. They are made from local materials and are great souvenirs to bring home.

Local Culture and Customs of Tigaon

  • Bicolano language - The Bicolano language is widely spoken in Tigaon, and is characterized by its melodic intonation and unique vocabulary.

  • Fiesta culture - Tigaon celebrates several fiestas or festivals throughout the year, such as the town fiesta, the Pili Festival, and the Buraburon Festival. These are occasions for feasting, dancing, and showcasing the town’s traditions and customs.

Best Time to Visit Tigaon

The best time to visit Tigaon is during the dry season, which runs from November to May. The weather is pleasant and conducive to outdoor activities, and there are fewer chances of typhoons and heavy rains.

Do’s and Don’ts of Tigaon

Do:

  • Do respect the local culture and customs. Bicolanos are known for their warm hospitality and friendly demeanor, so it pays to be polite and respectful.

  • Do try the local food and drinks. Bicolano cuisine is diverse and delicious, and offers a unique gastronomic experience.

  • Do take care of the environment. Tigaon’s natural attractions are fragile and vulnerable, so be mindful of your impact and follow the principles of eco-tourism.

Don’t:

  • Don’t litter. Keep Tigaon clean and green by disposing of your garbage properly.

  • Don’t disrespect the local traditions and beliefs. Tigaon has a rich cultural heritage, and it is important to respect and appreciate it.

  • Don’t be afraid to try new things. Tigaon offers plenty of opportunities for adventure and discovery, so be open-minded and embrace the unknown.

Safety and Health

Safety in Tigaon

Tigaon is generally a safe and peaceful town, with low crime rates and friendly locals. However, it is always wise to take basic precautions, such as not flashing valuable items or walking alone at night.

Health in Tigaon

Tigaon has a health center and several clinics that offer basic medical services. However, for serious or emergency cases, it is best to go to the nearest hospital in Naga City.

Getting to Tigaon: Transportation Options and Logistics

Tigaon is accessible by land and air. The nearest airport is the Naga City Airport, which is served by several airlines with flights from Manila and other major cities. From the airport, you can take a taxi or a van to Tigaon.

If you are coming from Manila, you can take a bus or a private car to Naga City, and then take a jeepney or a tricycle to Tigaon. The journey takes around 8-10 hours, depending on traffic and road conditions.

Airports Near Tigaon

  • Naga City Airport - located in Pili, Camarines Sur, around 45 minutes from Tigaon.

  • Legazpi City Airport - located in Legazpi City, Albay, around 2 hours from Tigaon.

Bus Stations Near Tigaon

  • Philtranco - located in Cubao, Quezon City, with daily trips to Naga City.

  • Isarog Bus Lines - located in Cubao, Quezon City, with daily trips to Naga City.

  • DLTB Bus Lines - located in Pasay City, with daily trips to Naga City.

Other Options

  • Private Car - You can also hire a private car or van from Manila or Naga City to Tigaon. This is a more expensive option, but offers more flexibility and comfort.

How to Get Around in Tigaon

The most convenient mode of transportation in Tigaon is by tricycle, which is a three-wheeled motorcycle that can accommodate up to four passengers. Tricycles are readily available and can take you anywhere within the town proper. There are also jeepneys and buses that connect Tigaon to other towns and cities in the province.

Where to Go Next from Tigaon: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

If you have more time to explore Camarines Sur and its neighboring provinces, here are some places that you might want to visit:

  • Naga City - A bustling city known for its historical sites, cultural landmarks, and vibrant nightlife.

  • Caramoan Islands - A group of islands off the coast of Camarines Sur that boast pristine beaches, crystal-clear waters, and breathtaking scenery.

  • Legazpi City - The capital of Albay province, located at the foot of the majestic Mount Mayon. It offers a unique blend of nature, culture, and adventure.

Conclusion

Tigaon is a hidden gem in the Bicol Region that offers a wealth of cultural and natural attractions. From stunning waterfalls and scenic mountains to delicious food and warm hospitality, Tigaon has something for everyone. Use this comprehensive travel guide to plan your trip to Tigaon and discover the best that this charming town has to offer.
