Introduction to Virac

Virac is a third class municipality in Catanduanes, an island province in the Bicol Region of the Philippines. The town serves as the capital and commercial center of the province. Virac is known for its scenic landscapes, beaches, and forests, making it a favorite destination for nature lovers and adventure seekers.

List of Places to Visit in Virac

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Virac has a rich cultural and historical heritage, and the following sites attest to this:

  • Catanduanes Museum - houses a collection of artifacts and exhibits that chronicle the history and culture of Catanduanes.
  • Virac Cathedral - a Spanish-era church that boasts beautiful Baroque architecture.
  • Bato Church - another old church with Spanish-inspired architecture, located in the mountainous town of Bato.
  • Talisoy Beach - site of the first landing of Americans in Catanduanes during World War II.
  • Maribina Falls - a natural waterfall with a swimming area and picnic grounds.

Natural Attractions of Virac: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Virac boasts a diverse range of natural attractions that are sure to satisfy any nature lover:

  • Puraran Beach - a popular surfing destination that has both beginner and expert waves.
  • Binurong Point - a stunning viewpoint that offers sweeping views of the Pacific Ocean and the surrounding landscape.
  • Balacay Point - another viewpoint that offers panoramic views of the ocean and coastline.
  • Luyang Cave - a beautiful limestone cave system that is home to numerous rock formations, stalactites, and stalagmites.
  • Aga Beach - a white sand beach surrounded by lush vegetation and rock formations.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Virac

Virac offers several hiking and biking routes that provide stunning views of the island’s landscapes:

  • Mt. Lantad Nature Trail - a two-hour hike that offers panoramic views of the town and the ocean.
  • Cabugao Peak - a steep 30-minute hike that offers majestic views of the town and the Pacific Ocean.
  • Baras Viewdeck - a viewing deck that can be reached via a short hike and offers breathtaking views of the town and the Pacific Ocean.
  • Binurong Point Trail - a three-kilometer trek that leads to the stunning viewpoint of Binurong Point.

Best Things to Do in Virac: Activities and Tours

There are plenty of activities and tours to enjoy in Virac:

  • Surfing Lessons - learn how to surf at Puraran Beach, one of the best surfing spots in the country.
  • Island Hopping - explore the nearby islands of Palumbanes and Balacay for a day of swimming, snorkeling, and picnicking.
  • ATV Tour - explore the rugged terrain around Mt. Cagmasoso on an all-terrain vehicle.
  • Heritage Tour - visit the historical and cultural sites of Virac, including old churches and the museum.
  • Food Tour - discover the local food scene of Virac and taste the delicious dishes and delicacies the town has to offer.

Food and Drink Scene of Virac

Must-Try Dishes

  • Bicol Express - a spicy stew made with pork, coconut milk, chili peppers, and shrimp paste.
  • Laing - a vegetable dish made with taro leaves and coconut milk, often served with fried fish or pork.
  • Grilled Seafood - Virac has a good selection of fresh seafood that can be grilled or cooked in different ways.

Local Beverages

  • Abaca Wine - a locally made wine from the fibers of abaca, a type of banana plant.
  • Buko Juice - a refreshing drink made from young coconut.

Where to Stay in Virac: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

There are several options for accommodations in Virac:

  • Puraran Surf Beach Resort - a beachfront resort with comfortable rooms and a surfing school.
  • Maribina Falls Resort - a resort located near the Maribina Falls that offers cottages and rooms for rent.
  • Sentro del Sur - a budget-friendly hostel located in the town center.
  • Casa Remedios - a guesthouse with comfortable rooms located near the Virac Cathedral.

What to Buy in Virac: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Abaca Products - bags, hats, and other accessories made from abaca fibers.
  • Alibaba Seaweed Crackers - a local snack made from seaweed.
  • Honey from Catanduanes - locally produced honey that comes in different flavors.

Local Culture and Customs of Virac

The people of Virac are known for their hospitality and friendliness. The town celebrates several cultural and religious festivals throughout the year, including the Abaca Festival, the Catandungan Festival, and the Feast of St. John the Baptist. Visitors are encouraged to join in the festivities and immerse themselves in the local culture.

Best Time to Visit Virac

The best time to visit Virac is between December and May, when the weather is dry and sunny. However, the town can also be visited year-round, as its natural and cultural attractions are worth exploring any time of the year.

Do’s and Don’ts of Virac

Do:

  • Do respect the local culture and customs.
  • Do bring sunscreen, mosquito repellent, and appropriate clothing for outdoor activities.
  • Do try the local dishes and delicacies.

Don’t:

  • Don’t litter or damage the natural attractions.
  • Don’t disrespect the local people and their beliefs.
  • Don’t leave valuables unattended or unsecured.

Safety and Health

Safety in Virac

Virac is a relatively safe town, but visitors should still exercise caution and keep their belongings secure. Avoid traveling alone at night and be aware of your surroundings, especially in crowded areas.

Health in Virac

Bring any necessary prescription medications and over-the-counter medicines, as some may not be easily available in Virac. Drink only bottled or purified water to avoid gastrointestinal illnesses.

Getting to Virac: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Virac

Virac Airport is the main airport serving the town, with regular flights from Manila, Cebu, and other major cities in the Philippines.

Bus Stations Near Virac

There are several bus companies that operate regular trips to Virac from Manila and other nearby cities. The bus ride takes around 12 hours, depending on the departure point.

Other Options

Visitors can also take a ferry from Tabaco City in Albay to Virac. The ferry ride takes around four hours.

How to Get Around in Virac

Tricycles and jeepneys are the most common modes of transportation around Virac. Visitors can also rent a motorbike or bicycle to explore the town.

Where to Go Next from Virac: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Bato Church - a historical church located in the mountainous town of Bato.
  • Caramoan Islands - a group of islands with stunning beaches and lagoons, located in the nearby province of Camarines Sur.
  • Legazpi City - a city famous for its perfect view of Mayon Volcano, located in the nearby province of Albay.

Conclusion

Virac is a must-visit destination in Catanduanes, offering a wide range of natural and cultural attractions, outdoor activities, and local delicacies. Whether you’re a nature lover, a history buff, or an adventure seeker, Virac has something in store for you. Plan your trip now and explore the beauty of this off-the-beaten-path destination.
