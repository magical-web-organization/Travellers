Introduction to Catanduanes

Catanduanes is a beautiful, yet often overlooked, island in the Bicol region of the Philippines. Known as the “Land of the Howling Wind,” it is a paradise for outdoor enthusiasts looking to explore the island’s natural beauty. Home to stunning beaches, waterfalls, hiking trails, and rich history and culture, Catanduanes is a must-visit destination for both domestic and international travelers.

Local Culture and Customs of Catanduanes

  • The locals are known as Catandunganons and are friendly and accommodating to visitors.
  • Catandunganon food is known for its strong flavors, particularly the use of coconut milk and chili peppers in dishes like Bicol express and laing.
  • Abaca weaving is an integral part of Catandunganon culture and a source of income for many locals.

Best Time to Visit Catanduanes

The best time to visit Catanduanes is during the dry season, which runs from December to May. During this time, the weather is generally sunny and warm, making it ideal for outdoor activities.

Do’s and Don’ts of Catanduanes

Do:

  • Try the local cuisine, especially the Bicol Express and Kinunot na Pagi.
  • Respect the local customs and culture.
  • Make sure to bring appropriate clothing and gear for outdoor activities.

Don’t:

  • Litter or damage natural attractions.
  • Disrespect religious sites, such as Bato Church.
  • Wear revealing clothing in public places.

Safety and Health

Safety in Catanduanes

Catanduanes is generally safe, and crime rates are low. However, travelers are advised to take normal precautions and to be vigilant when exploring more remote areas.

Health in Catanduanes

The healthcare system in Catanduanes is limited, and medical facilities may be basic. It is recommended that travelers ensure they have adequate health insurance and take precautions against common travel illnesses like diarrhea and insect-borne diseases.

Getting to Catanduanes: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Catanduanes

The nearest airport to Catanduanes is Virac Airport, which is served by Cebu Pacific and Philippine Airlines.

Bus Stations Near Catanduanes

Buses to Catanduanes can be taken from various parts of Bicol region, including Tabaco City, Legazpi, and Naga.

Other Options

Catanduanes can also be accessed by roll-on/roll-off ferry from Tabaco City in Albay to the capital town of Virac.

How to Get Around in Catanduanes

Tricycles and jeepneys are the primary modes of transportation in Catanduanes. Private cars and motorbikes can also be rented from local operators.
