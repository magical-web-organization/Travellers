Introduction to Tabaco

Nestled in the heart of Albay, in the Bicol region of the Philippines, Tabaco is a serene and enchanting travel destination. This charming town is a gateway to the scenic landscapes of Albay, with majestic mountains, breathtaking beaches, and lush greenery. The name “Tabaco” is said to have originated from the Spanish word for tobacco, as the town was once known for its tobacco plantations.

Today, Tabaco is a bustling hub for tourism, known for its cultural and natural treasures. From historical museums and ancient landmarks to outdoor adventures and scenic views, Tabaco has something for every traveler.

List of Places to Visit in Tabaco

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Tabaco City Museum: The Tabaco City Museum is a must-see attraction for history buffs, showcasing the rich culture and heritage of the town. The museum is housed in an 18th-century Spanish colonial building and features a collection of artifacts, artworks, and heritage items.

  • St. John the Baptist Church: This stunning church dates back to the 17th century and is a beautiful example of Baroque architecture. The church features intricate carvings, stained glass windows, and stunning paintings.

  • Tabak Festival: This annual festival celebrates the town’s rich history and heritage, with vibrant performances, parades, and cultural exhibits.

Natural Attractions of Tabaco: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Mayon Volcano: One of the most iconic landmarks in the Philippines, Mayon Volcano is a must-visit attraction for every traveler. This stunning volcano is located in the nearby town of Legazpi City and offers breathtaking views of the surrounding landscapes.

  • Cagraray Island: Located just off the coast of Tabaco, Cagraray Island is a hidden gem for travelers seeking serene beaches and crystal-clear waters. The island is home to several resorts, making it the perfect destination for a relaxing getaway.

  • Jovellar Underground River and Cave System: This natural wonder features a network of underground rivers and caves, offering a unique adventure experience for travelers. The cave system is home to several species of bats and is a must-visit attraction for nature enthusiasts.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Tabaco

  • Mayon ATV Tour: Get up close and personal with Mayon Volcano on an ATV tour. Explore the rugged terrain around the base of the volcano with an experienced guide.

  • Hiking to Kawa-Kawa Hill: This scenic hike is a must-do for travelers seeking breathtaking views of Albay’s landscapes. The hike takes you to the top of Kawa-Kawa Hill, where you can see the stunning panorama of the town’s lush greenery and rolling hills.

  • Biking Along Tabaco’s Coastline: Take a leisurely bike ride along Tabaco’s coastline, and soak in the picturesque views of the town’s quaint villages and tranquil beaches.

Best Things to Do in Tabaco: Activities and Tours

  • Explore the Waterfalls: Tabaco is home to several stunning waterfalls, including Linao Cave and Hoyop-hoyopan Cave, that offer a refreshing break from the tropical climate of the Philippines.

  • Beach Hopping: Take a tour of Tabaco’s pristine beaches, including San Miguel Island, Puro Island, and Malinao White Beach. Each offers something unique, from crystal-clear waters to peaceful hideaways.

  • Try Local Cuisine: Sample the delicious local cuisine of Tabaco, including spicy Bicol dishes and fresh seafood delicacies.

Week-Long Itinerary for Tabaco

Day 1: Explore the town’s historical landmarks, including the Tabaco City Museum and St. John the Baptist Church.

Day 2: Take a bike ride along the coastline and soak in the picturesque views of the town.

Day 3: Go on a waterfall adventure and explore the stunning Linao Cave and Hoyop-hoyopan Cave.

Day 4: Take a day trip to Cagraray Island and relax on its serene beaches.

Day 5: Hike to the top of Kawa-Kawa Hill and enjoy panoramic views of the surrounding landscapes.

Day 6: Take an ATV tour and explore the rugged terrain around Mayon Volcano.

Day 7: Sample the local cuisine of Tabaco and buy souvenirs to take home.

Food and Drink Scene of Tabaco

Must-Try Dishes

  • Bicol Express: A signature dish of the Bicol region, Bicol Express is a spicy stew made with pork, chili peppers, and coconut milk.

  • Pinangat: This dish features coconut milk, shrimp or fish, and taro leaves, wrapped in banana leaves and cooked until tender.

  • Tinutungan Rice: A specialty dish of Tabaco, Tinutungan Rice is made with burnt coconut milk and served with various meats and vegetables.

Local Beverages

  • Sili Shake: This refreshing drink is made with Tabaco’s signature chili peppers, combined with fresh fruits and coconut milk.

  • Buko Juice: Made with fresh young coconuts, Buko Juice is a refreshing and healthy drink that’s a staple of the Philippines.

Where to Stay in Tabaco: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • La Volla Francaise: This charming bed and breakfast is located in the heart of Tabaco and features a rustic French-style architecture.

  • Mayon Backpackers Hostel: This budget-friendly hostel is located in the nearby town of Legazpi City and offers clean and comfortable accommodation.

  • Misibis Bay Resort: This luxury resort is situated on Cagraray Island and offers stunning views of the surrounding landscapes.

What to Buy in Tabaco: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Abaca Products: Tabaco is known for its abaca products, including bags, slippers, and hats made from the sturdy and versatile abaca fiber.

  • Bicolano Spices: Bring home the flavors of Tabaco with Bicolano spices, including the signature chili peppers of the region.

  • Tabaco Embroidery: Tabaco is known for its exquisite embroidery, featuring intricate designs and patterns on fabric.

Local Culture and Customs of Tabaco

Tabaco has a rich and vibrant culture, deeply rooted in the region’s history and heritage. The town celebrates several festivals year-round, including the Tabak Festival and the Feast Day of St. John the Baptist. The locals are friendly and welcoming, and visitors are expected to show respect for the town’s customs and traditions.

Best Time to Visit Tabaco

The best time to visit Tabaco is from November to February, when the weather is mild and pleasant. Avoid visiting during the rainy season, from June to October, as this time can bring in heavy rainfall and strong winds.

Do’s and Don’ts of Tabaco

Do:

  • Respect the local culture and customs of Tabaco.
  • Explore the natural wonders of the town, including Mayon Volcano and the stunning waterfalls.
  • Sample the local cuisine of Tabaco.

Don’t:

  • Disrespect the town’s heritage and culture.
  • Litter or damage the natural environments.
  • Hike or explore unfamiliar areas without a knowledgeable guide.

Safety and Health

Safety in Tabaco

Tabaco is a generally safe and peaceful town, but visitors should exercise caution when traveling alone or at night. It’s always advisable to keep your valuables safe and secure and be aware of your surroundings at all times.

Health in Tabaco

Visitors to Tabaco should take extra precautions against mosquito-borne illnesses, including dengue fever and malaria. It’s advisable to use mosquito repellent and wear protective clothing, especially during the rainy season.

Getting to Tabaco: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Tabaco

The nearest airport to Tabaco is the Legazpi City Domestic Airport, which is about 25 kilometers away from the town center.

Bus Stations Near Tabaco

Tabaco is accessible by bus from several major cities in the Bicol region, including Legazpi City, Naga City, and Manila.

Other Options

Visitors can also take a combination of bus and boat rides to reach Tabaco, especially if coming from nearby islands or provinces.

How to Get Around in Tabaco

The best way to get around Tabaco is by tricycle, a common mode of transportation in the Philippines. Visitors can also rent a bike or motorcycle to explore the town on their own.

Where to Go Next from Tabaco: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Legazpi City: This nearby city is known for its stunning views of Mayon Volcano, as well as its rich culture and heritage.

  • Naga City: Known as the “Queen City of Bicol,” Naga City is a vibrant and bustling metropolis that offers several attractions for travelers.

  • Caramoan Islands: Located in the nearby province of Camarines Sur, the Caramoan Islands offer stunning beaches, crystal-clear waters, and fascinating rock formations.

Conclusion

Tabaco is a hidden gem of Albay, offering a varied mix of cultural, natural, and adventure destinations for travelers. With this comprehensive travel guide, you can make the most out of your trip, exploring the charming town and immersing yourself in its rich heritage and culture. So pack your bags, hop on a plane, and get ready to discover the enchanting town of Tabaco.
