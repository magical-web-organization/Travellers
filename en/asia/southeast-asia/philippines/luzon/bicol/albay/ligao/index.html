Introduction to Ligao

Ligao is a charming city in Albay, Bicol, known for its breathtaking natural scenery and rich cultural heritage. It is home to more than 100,000 people who are mainly engaged in agriculture. Ligao is a popular destination for tourists who want to experience a laid-back provincial lifestyle, while being surrounded by magnificent mountains, rivers, and falls, all waiting to be explored.

List of Places to Visit in Ligao

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Ligao has several museums and historical places that give visitors a glimpse into the city’s past. One of the must-visit attractions is the Ligao City Museum which showcases antique weapons, clothing, and other artifacts. Another place worth checking out is the Spanish Tower which dates back to the 17th century. You can also drop by the St. Stephen Cathedral, which has a beautiful baroque architecture and stunning stained-glass windows.

Natural Attractions of Ligao: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

For nature lovers, Ligao has a lot to offer. There are several mountains in Ligao such as Mayon, Mt. Masaraga, and Mt. Catburawan that offer amazing hiking opportunities for those who love the outdoors. Ligao also has several waterfalls and springs such as Malison Falls, Nabonton Falls, and Blue Lagoon which are perfect for those who love swimming and picnic trips. If you’re looking for a picturesque view of the city, visit the Kapitolyo Hill where you can see the city’s panoramic view.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Ligao

Ligao is apt for adventure enthusiasts who love hiking and biking. Mt. Masaraga, for instance, is a popular spot for hiking with a beautiful view at the top. It is also a preferred spot for those who enjoy off-road biking. Other mountains such as Mt. Catburawan and Mt. Mayon also present challenging hikes that can be rewarding with awe-inspiring vistas.

Best Things to Do in Ligao: Activities and Tours

Aside from hiking and biking, Ligao offers plenty of activities and tours such as river rafting, camping, and ATV rides. For the ultimate adventure, a visit to the eco-park is a must-do activity for all ages. You can also rent kayaks and pedal boats at the nearby river for some leisurely fun.

Week-Long Itinerary for Ligao

Here is a week-long itinerary to maximize your stay in Ligao:

Day Activities
Day 1 City tour - visit Ligao Cathedral, Spanish Tower and Ligao City Museum, and try the local delicacies
Day 2 Hike to Mayon Volcano and witness the sunrise
Day 3 Swim at Malison Falls and feast on grilled fish and meat
Day 4 Visit the eco-park and try the zipline, bungee trampoline, and wall climbing
Day 5 Go camping at Blue Lagoon and enjoy fishing and birdwatching
Day 6 Explore the mountains of Masaraga and Catburawan.
Day 7 Do souvenir shopping at Ligao’s public market and visit a local kitchen to learn how the local products are made.

Food and Drink Scene of Ligao

Must-Try Dishes

If you’re a foodie, Ligao is a must-visit destination in Bicol. The city is known for its spicy and flavorful cuisine that’s rich in coconut milk and chili. Some of the must-try dishes are Pinangat, Bicol Express, and Laing. You can also try the local seafood such as grilled fish and shrimps.

Local Beverages

To complement your meal, try the local beverages such as Bicol coffee, salabat tea, and hot coconut milk.

Where to Stay in Ligao: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Ligao has an array of accommodation options to fit all budgets. For a luxurious stay, try the hacienda-inspired Misibis Bay Resort with an amazing view of the sea that’s an hour away from Ligao. For those on a budget, Nuranz Apartelle and Las Cumbres Inn offer clean and comfortable rooms at affordable rates.

What to Buy in Ligao: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Bring home a piece of Ligao by trying the local products such as pili nuts, chili sauce, pastries filled with pili nuts, and handwoven bags and mats. Take a stroll at Ligao’s public market and find unique souvenirs to bring home.

Local Culture and Customs of Ligao

Ligao is a city with deep cultural roots and traditions. Respect the customs of the city by dressing modestly when visiting religious sites and being mindful of their cultural practices.

Best Time to Visit Ligao

The best time to visit Ligao is during the dry season which runs from December to May. During these months, the weather is dry, severe typhoons are rare, and the mountain trails are safe to traverse.

Do’s and Don’ts of Ligao

Do:

  • Respect the locals and their traditions.
  • Bring sunscreen and wear comfortable clothing when exploring.

Don’t:

  • Wet season, from June to November, might not be the best time to visit as the trails can be muddy and there’s an increased risk of landslides.

Safety and Health

Safety in Ligao

Ligao is generally a safe city, but it is always recommended to be cautious when traveling. Make sure to bring a map or GPS, and avoid walking alone late at night.

Health in Ligao

Make sure to bring a first-aid kit and insect repellent when traveling. Hospitals and clinics are available in the city for medical needs, make sure to secure all necessary documents from medical insurance providers to avoid any problems.

Getting to Ligao: Transportation Options and Logistics

Ligao is easily accessible from Manila, the country’s capital, by land, sea, and air. The nearest airport is the Legazpi airport, which is an hour away from Ligao. Buses and jeepney’s run regularly from Manila or Legazpi to Ligao throughout the day.

Airports Near Ligao

  • Legazpi Airport (around an hour away)

Bus Stations Near Ligao

  • Cubao Bus Terminal (Manila) to Ligao Bus Terminal
  • Legazpi Bus Terminal to Ligao Bus Terminal

Other Options

  • Rent a car or a motorcycle to explore the surrounding areas at your own pace.

How to Get Around in Ligao

To travel around the city, there are jeepneys, buses, and tricycles readily available in the terminal. For farther distances or off-roads, you can also rent a motorcycle or car.

Conclusion

Ligao is a city that is full of surprises. With its natural beauty, rich culture, and flavorful cuisine, Ligao has something to offer for everyone. With this guide, plan your trip and get ready to discover the hidden gem that is Ligao.
