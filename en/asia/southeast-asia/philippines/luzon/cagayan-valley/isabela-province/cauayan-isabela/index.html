Introduction to Cauayan (Isabela)

Located in the Cagayan Valley in the Philippines, Cauayan (Isabela) is a city filled with natural wonders, recreational activities, and a rich cultural heritage. It is the 2nd largest city in the province of Isabela and offers tourist attractions that cater to different interests. From its historical sites and outdoor adventures to its local cuisine and traditions, Cauayan (Isabela) is worth exploring.

List of Places to Visit in Cauayan (Isabela)

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Cauayan (Isabela) has a wealth of museums and historical places that will give you a glimpse of the city’s past and culture. Some noteworthy places to visit are:

  1. Cauayan City Museum – This museum displays the history, culture, and traditions of Cauayan (Isabela) and showcases various artifacts and relics.
  2. Isabela Museum and Library – This museum and library contain a great collection of books, articles, and photographs that showcase the history of Isabela.
  3. Patapat Viaduct – The longest bridge in the country, Patapat Viaduct, spans over the Abra River and connects Ilocos Norte and Cagayan Valley. A must-visit landmark.

Natural Attractions of Cauayan (Isabela): Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Cauayan (Isabela) is blessed with unspoiled natural resources that offer exciting outdoor activities and breathtaking views. Some natural attractions to include in your visit are:

  1. Magat Dam – This is not only a popular landmark but also a source of hydroelectric power in the region. The dam also offers a picturesque view of the surrounding mountains and the Magat River.
  2. Northern Sierra Madre Natural Park – One of the largest protected areas in the country, this natural park covers over 359, 489 hectares and has diverse flora and fauna.
  3. Divilacan Island – One of the most beautiful islands in Cagayan Valley, Divilacan Island offers white sandy beaches, crystal-clear waters, and a tranquil environment.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Cauayan (Isabela)

Hiking or biking is an excellent way to explore the natural beauty of Cauayan (Isabela). The Northern Sierra Madre Natural Park offers numerous trails for hiking and biking, some of the popular ones are:

  1. Mt. Amorong – This hike is perfect for beginners as it only takes 2-3 hours to complete and offers a stunning view of the mountains and the Cagayan Valley.
  2. Palanan to Divilacan Route – This route offers an adventure of a lifetime. The challenging hike takes about 3-5 days and covers over 25 kilometers of the Northern Sierra Madre mountain range.

Best Things to Do in Cauayan (Isabela): Activities and Tours

Aside from the natural attractions and outdoor adventures, Cauayan (Isabela) has a lot of exciting things to do for tourists. Some of the best things to do are:

  1. Water Rafting in Magat River – An exhilarating activity that will let you experience the natural beauty of the Magat River while maneuvering through the rapids.
  2. ATV Ride to Mount Calvary – A fun activity where you can enjoy an off-road ride on an all-terrain vehicle to see the scenic views of Mount Calvary.
  3. Tour the Rice Fields of Isabela – Isabela is known as the rice granary of the Philippines, and touring the rice fields is a must-try activity.

Food and Drink Scene of Cauayan (Isabela)

Must-Try Dishes

Cauayan (Isabela) is famous for its sumptuous and satisfying cuisine. Here are some must-try dishes:

  1. Pancit Cabagan – A popular noodle dish and known as the regional food of Cagayan Valley, Pancit Cabagan is made with thick fresh noodles, meat, and vegetables.
  2. Pinakbet – Pinakbet is a vegetable dish made with squash, okra, eggplant, beans, and other local produce, cooked in shrimp paste sauce.
  3. Bangus Sisig – Bangus Sisig is a variation of the popular Filipino dish Sisig, made with milkfish instead of pork and served sizzling on a hotplate.

Local Beverages

Quench your thirst with these locally made beverages:

  1. Bugnay Wine – A wine made from wild berries and fermented in earthen jars for months. Bugnay wine has a sweet and tangy taste that complements any meal.
  2. Calamansi Juice – Calamansi juice is a refreshing citrus drink made from calamansi fruit, sugar, and water.
  3. Guapinol Malt Drink – A malt drink made from roasted guapinol seeds, sugar, and water. It has a nutty flavor and is perfect for cooling off on a hot day.

Where to Stay in Cauayan (Isabela): Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

There are several accommodations to choose from in Cauayan (Isabela), ranging from budget-friendly hostels to luxurious hotels. Some noteworthy options are:

  1. Isabela Provincial Guest House – This guest house offers comfortable rooms and amenities at an affordable price.
  2. Mango Suites – Mango Suites is a modern and luxurious hotel that offers excellent service and world-class amenities.
  3. Villa Diana Hotel and Restaurant – This hotel and restaurant serves delicious local dishes and provides comfortable rooms with a stunning view of the rice fields.

What to Buy in Cauayan (Isabela): Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Take home some souvenirs and local products that represent Cauayan (Isabela), like:

  1. Banana Chips – Isabela is known for producing high-quality bananas that are used to make delicious banana chips.
  2. Napkin or Tissue Holders – Handwoven napkin or tissue holders made from the palm tree are excellent souvenirs to bring home.
  3. Hats and Bags made from Saliwag Vine – Saliwag Vine is a type of local vine that is used to make bags and hats. These handmade products are unique souvenirs to have.

Local Culture and Customs of Cauayan (Isabela)

Cauayan (Isabela) is known for its rich culture and traditions filled with colorful festivals like the Bambanti Festival, which celebrates the region’s agricultural harvest. Respect for elders, food, and hospitality are essential values that locals uphold.

Best Time to Visit Cauayan (Isabela)

The best time to visit Cauayan (Isabela) is during the dry season, which is from November to April. The weather is more suitable for outdoor activities, and the streets are livelier with festivals and events.

Do’s and Don’ts of Cauayan (Isabela)

Do:

  • Respect local customs and traditions, especially when visiting religious sites.
  • Try the local cuisine and drinks for an authentic experience.
  • Keep the environment clean and respect nature.

Don’t:

  • Wear revealing clothing when visiting religious sites.
  • Damage or vandalize any tourist attractions and natural resources.
  • Disrespect locals and their culture.

Safety and Health

Safety in Cauayan (Isabela)

Cauayan (Isabela) is generally a safe place to visit. However, tourists must always take precautions and be aware of their surroundings, especially when traveling to remote areas.

Health in Cauayan (Isabela)

Tourists must always bring their medication and first-aid kit when visiting Cauayan (Isabela). The city has several hospitals and clinics that provide medical services, but it’s best to be prepared in case of an emergency.

Getting to Cauayan (Isabela): Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Cauayan (Isabela)

Tuguegarao airport, in the neighboring province of Cagayan, is the closest airport to Cauayan (Isabela). From there, tourists can take a bus or hire a private car to travel to Cauayan (Isabela).

Bus Stations Near Cauayan (Isabela)

Several bus companies like Victory Liner, Five Star, and Baliwag Transit have terminals in Cauayan City and provide transport to different parts of Luzon.

Other Options

Tourists can also hire private cars or vans to travel around the city and neighboring areas.

How to Get Around in Cauayan (Isabela)

Jeepneys and tricycles are the most common form of transport in Cauayan (Isabela). There are also car rental services available for tourists who want to explore the city at their own pace.

Where to Go Next from Cauayan (Isabela): Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Cauayan (Isabela) is a perfect jump-off point for exploring other destinations in the region. A short trip to these nearby places is worth the experience:

  1. Tuguegarao City – Famous for its natural caverns and hot springs
  2. Baler, Aurora – Known for its beautiful beaches and surfing spots
  3. Cagayan Holiday and Leisure Resort – A perfect getaway for those who want to relax and enjoy the beach.

Conclusion

Cauayan (Isabela) is a hidden gem in Cagayan Valley that offers a unique travel experience to its visitors. From its natural attractions, outdoor adventures, historical landmarks, local cuisine, and cultural heritage, Cauayan (Isabela) is a city worth exploring.
