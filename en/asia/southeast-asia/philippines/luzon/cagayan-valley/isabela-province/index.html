Introduction to Isabela (Province)

Isabela is an underrated province located in Cagayan Valley, Luzon, Philippines. The province boasts natural wonders such as forests, beaches, and waterfalls, making it an idyllic destination for travelers seeking adventure and relaxation. It is also recognized for its rich culture, tradition, and history.

List of Places to Visit in Isabela (Province)

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Isabela is home to several Museums and Historical Places that showcase the province’s colorful past. Among them are:

  • Ilagan Japanese Tunnel - A maze-like tunnel used by the Japanese soldiers during World War II that spans over 1,000 meters.
  • Museo de Diocesano de Ilagan - A museum located inside the St. Ferdinand Cathedral showcasing religious relics, artworks, and historical artifacts.
  • Tumauini Church - A UNESCO-recognized National Cultural Treasure that features a stunning Baroque design dating back to the Spanish colonial period.
  • Magat Dam - One of the biggest dams in the Philippines, located in Ramon, Isabela. The dam generates electricity and supplies irrigation to farmlands in Isabela and surrounding areas.

Natural Attractions of Isabela (Province): Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Isabela’s natural wonders do not disappoint, and each attraction presents a unique experience. Among the popular natural attractions in the province are:

  • Dibulo Falls - A picturesque cascading waterfall surrounded by lush greenery. It is a popular spot for picnics, swimming, and camping.
  • San Mariano Watershed and Ecological Park - A vast protected forest reserve that is ideal for trekking, bird watching, and nature photography.
  • Mt. Palali and Mt. Cordon - Two mountains found in the eastern part of Isabela that offer a challenging hike and stunning views of the Sierra Madre mountain range.
  • Pasa Island - A small island located in the Pacific Ocean with white sandy beaches, turquoise waters, and diverse marine life.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Isabela (Province)

Isabela offers various hiking and biking trails suitable for both amateur and experienced trekkers. Here are some popular routes to try:

  • Mt. Palali Traverse - A challenging 2-day hike that takes you through a dense forest of mossy giant trees, coconut palms, and panoramic views of the mountains and valleys.
  • Magat Dam to Basilica de Piat - A 60-km bike ride that will take you along a scenic countryside road, rivers, and mountain landscapes.
  • Jardin Elcano to Sta. Victoria Caves - An easy trek popular among locals and visitors because of the fascinating limestone caves and scenic views of the Cagayan Valley.

Best Things to Do in Isabela (Province): Activities and Tours

Isabela’s vast natural resources provide plenty of thrilling adventures and activities for travelers to enjoy. Here are some of the top things to do in the province:

  • Whitewater Rafting in Fuyot Springs National Park - An exciting outdoor adventure that takes you through the challenging rapids and scenic beauty of the Cagayan River.
  • Caving in San Mariano - A unique experience that takes you through the intricate and mysterious cave systems in San Mariano.
  • Fishing in Gamu Fish Breeding Center - A relaxing and enjoyable activity, especially for families, that allows you to catch your fish and have it grilled for lunch.
  • Beach Hopping in Pasa Island - A day trip that takes you to different secluded beaches on the island, where you can swim, sunbathe, and snorkel.

Week-Long Itinerary for Isabela (Province)

To maximize your visit to Isabela, here is a suggested week-long itinerary:

  • Day 1: Arrive in Tuguegarao City and check-in to your hotel. Explore the city’s dining and shopping scene.
  • Day 2: Visit historical attractions such as the Tumauini Church and Ilagan Japanese Tunnel.
  • Day 3: Go on a trek to Mt. Palali and take in the stunning views of the Sierra Madre mountain range.
  • Day 4: Take a trip to Pasa Island and enjoy a beach hopping adventure.
  • Day 5: Go on a whitewater rafting adventure in Fuyot Springs National Park.
  • Day 6: Experience the unique caving adventure in San Mariano and enjoy a relaxing afternoon fishing in Gamu Fish Breeding Center.
  • Day 7: Depart from Tuguegarao City for your next destination.

Food and Drink Scene of Isabela (Province)

Must-Try Dishes

Isabela’s culinary scene is as diverse and rich as its natural resources. Among the must-try dishes are:

  • Pancit Cabagan - A popular noodle dish that uses fresh egg noodles, shredded carabeef, and vegetables.
  • Inasal na manok - A roasted chicken dish marinated with tangy calamansi juice, soy sauce, and garlic.
  • Pinais na hipon - A shrimp dish wrapped in banana leaves and steamed with coconut milk, ginger, and chili.
  • Kalderetang kambing - A goat meat stew slow-cooked in tomato sauce, potatoes, carrots, and bell peppers.

Local Beverages

  • Basi - A local rice wine that is a bit sweet, sour, and effervescent.
  • Palawan Saowi - A concoction of lambanog and extracts from various herbs and fruits.
  • Salabat - A warm ginger tea boiled with turmeric and honey.

Where to Stay in Isabela (Province): Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Isabela offers different types of accommodation suitable for various budgets and preferences. Here are some options:

  • Casa de Anas - A bed and breakfast located in the heart of Tuguegarao City, which offers charming rooms and home-cooked meals.
  • Balai Cagayano - A budget hotel with comfortable rooms and adequate facilities located in the capital city of Ilagan.
  • Villa Diana Hotel and Restaurant - A mid-range hotel that features lush gardens, a swimming pool, and an excellent restaurant.

What to Buy in Isabela (Province): Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Isabela is known for its local products such as:

  • Chichacorn - A popular snack similar to popcorn but made from corn grits, deep-fried, and flavored with salt or garlic.
  • Pasalubong Baskets - A basket selection of Cordillera coffee, Basi, native rice cakes, and other local delicacies.
  • Abel Cloth - Hand-woven cloth that comes in various colors and designs and is suitable for clothing, curtains, and handicrafts.

Local Culture and Customs of Isabela (Province)

The people of Isabela are known for their warm hospitality and friendly nature. They celebrate many festivals and events that reflect their unique traditions and cultures. These include:

  • Bambanti Festival - A grand celebration held every January to honor the province’s patron saint, St. Ferdinand, and showcase the bountiful harvest of the farmers in the region.
  • Tuguegarao City Anniversary - Celebrated every June 9th, the anniversary commemorates the city’s foundation and growth.
  • Luyang Dilaw Festival - Held every December in San Ildefonso, the festival features street dancing, parades, and sumptuous food.

Best Time to Visit Isabela (Province)

The best time to visit Isabela is between December and May when the weather is dry, and temperatures range from 25°C to 33°C. The rainy season is from June to November, making travel and outdoor activities challenging.

Do’s and Don’ts of Isabela (Province)

Here are some helpful tips to keep in mind when visiting Isabela:

Do:

  • Do dress conservatively - Filipinos value modesty, so it’s best to dress appropriately, especially when visiting religious sites.
  • Do respect the local customs and traditions - Filipinos are traditional, and it’s important to be mindful of their customs, such as taking off your shoes before entering a house or temple.
  • Do try the local food and delicacies - The province is known for its unique cuisine and flavors, so make sure to taste some of the must-try dishes and local beverages.

Don’t:

  • Don’t litter - Keep the environment clean and dispose of your rubbish properly.
  • Don’t take anything without permission - It’s essential to ask for permission before taking photos of people, especially in cultural or sacred places.
  • Don’t wander alone at night - As in any other city, it’s best to avoid exploring alone at night and stay in well-lit and crowded areas.

Safety and Health

Safety in Isabela (Province)

Isabela is generally a safe destination, but it’s important to take precautions, especially when traveling to remote areas or engaging in outdoor activities. Here are some tips:

  • Always carry identification - Make sure to have a valid identification card or passport with you at all times.
  • Be aware of your surroundings - Keep an eye on your belongings and avoid dark alleys or secluded areas.
  • Ask for guidance from locals - It’s best to seek advice from the locals on what areas to avoid.

Health in Isabela (Province)

It’s important to take precautions to avoid health risks such as malaria and dengue fever. Here are some tips:

  • Use insect repellent - Mosquitoes are prevalent in the countryside, and using insect repellent can help protect you from bites.
  • Drink bottled or boiled water - It’s best to drink bottled or boiled water to avoid waterborne diseases.

Getting to Isabela (Province): Transportation Options and Logistics

Isabela is accessible by various modes of transportation, and getting around the province is easy.

Airports Near Isabela (Province)

The nearest airports to Isabela Province are:

  • Cauayan Airport
  • Tuguegarao Airport

Bus Stations Near Isabela (Province)

Several bus companies such as Victory Liner, GV Florida, and Dalin Liner have routes to Isabela from Manila, Quezon City, and Baguio.

Other Options

  • Private car - You can rent a car or hire a private vehicle to travel to and around Isabela.
  • Jeepney or Tricycle - Jeepneys and tricycles are the main mode of transportation within the province and are affordable and easy to use.

How to Get Around in Isabela (Province)

Getting around Isabela is relatively easy, and there are many options available:

  • Jeepney - Jeepneys are the most common mode of transportation within the province and are affordable and frequent.
  • Tricycle - Tricycles are ideal for short distances and are best used for navigating narrow streets and alleys.
  • Private Car or Van Rental - If you prefer more privacy or are traveling with a group, renting a car or van is a convenient and efficient way to explore Isabela.

Where to Go Next from Isabela (Province): Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Isabela is surrounded by several destinations that are worth visiting. Here are some examples:

  • Tuguegarao City - Explore the bustling metropolis of Cagayan Valley and indulge in authentic Ilocano cuisine.
  • Cagayan Province - Discover the natural wonders of the “Land of Smiling Beauty,” including Palaui Island and Callao Cave.
  • Baler, Aurora - Head west to the Surfing Capital of the Philippines, and enjoy the long stretches of white sand beaches and high waves.

Conclusion

Isabela is a hidden gem in the Philippines, offering natural beauty, rich history, and unique culture that caters to every traveler’s interests. Plan your trip now and experience the best that Isabela has to offer.
