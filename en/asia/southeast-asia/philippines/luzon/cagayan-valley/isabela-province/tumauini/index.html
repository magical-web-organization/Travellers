Introduction to Tumauini

Tumauini, a third-class municipality located in the province of Isabela in Cagayan Valley, is home to a myriad of cultural and natural attractions that attract tourists from near and far. Established in 1686, Tumauini played a significant role during the Spanish colonization era as an important stopover for traders and merchants. Today, it remains an integral part of the Isabela province due to its rich history, warm locals, and numerous sights and activities.

List of Places to Visit in Tumauini

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Tumauini Church

Built in 1768, the Tumauini Church is a cultural and historical landmark in Tumauini that showcases the town’s rich heritage. Its unique architecture boasts of an impressive floor plan, a magnificent facade, and a stunning bell tower.

Tumauini Museum

Located in the municipal hall complex, the Tumauini Museum is a great place to learn about the town’s history and culture. The museum features artifacts and exhibits that showcase the Ibanag’s way of life, customs, and traditions.

Natural Attractions of Tumauini: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Bintacan Hill

Bintacan Hill, the highest point in Tumauini, offers a panoramic view of the surrounding mountains and valleys. It’s an ideal spot for hiking, picnicking, and sightseeing.

Dipudo Waterfalls

Located in Barangay Dipudo, the Dipudo Waterfalls is a refreshing natural attraction surrounded by lush greens and towering trees. Here, visitors can swim, relax, and enjoy the tranquil ambiance.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Tumauini

Mt. Diwata

Mt. Diwata, towering at 1,186 meters above sea level, is a favorite hiking and biking spot among locals and tourists. The summit offers a scenic view of the nearby towns and peaks, making it a must-visit destination for adventurers and nature enthusiasts.

Best Things to Do in Tumauini: Activities and Tours

Visit the Matalag River

The Matalag River is a prime destination for those who love water activities such as rafting, kayaking, and fishing. Visitors can enjoy some fun water adventures while taking in the beautiful scenery along the riverbanks.

Visit the Isabela State University Agricultural Research Center

The Isabela State University Agricultural Research Center is a fascinating place to learn about agriculture and farming practices in Isabela province. Visitors can tour the research facilities, and learn about the latest techniques and crops being developed.

Food and Drink Scene of Tumauini

Must-Try Dishes

Tumauini is known for its mouth-watering local cuisine that boasts of rich, bold flavors. Here are some dishes you must try:

  • Pinakbet (vegetable stew)
  • Ilocos empanada (deep-fried pastry filled with meat and vegetables)
  • Dinakdakan (grilled pork dish with boiled pig’s head and vinegar)

Local Beverages

For a unique Tumauini experience, try these local beverages:

  • Duhat wine (made from the fruits of a native tree called duhat)
  • Cagayan wine (a sweet red wine made from locally grown grapes)

Local Culture and Customs of Tumauini

Tumauini’s culture and traditions are heavily influenced by its rich history and ethnic background. The locals are friendly and welcoming, and visitors are encouraged to respect their beliefs and practices. A popular cultural event in Tumauini is the Tinumaua Festival, held every November to honor the town’s patron saint, St. Anthony of Padua.

Best Time to Visit Tumauini

The best time to visit Tumauini is from February to May when the weather is relatively cooler and drier. This period is ideal for outdoor activities and sightseeing. However, if you’re interested in attending cultural events like the Tinumaua Festival, it’s best to plan your visit in November.

Do’s and Don’ts of Tumauini

Do:

  • Respect the local culture and customs
  • Engage with the locals and learn about their way of life
  • Try the local cuisine and beverages

Don’t:

  • Disrespect sacred places and traditional practices
  • Leave trash behind or damage natural attractions
  • Speak loudly in public places

Safety and Health

Safety in Tumauini

Tumauini is a generally safe place to visit, but as with any destination, visitors should take precautions to ensure their safety. These include:

  • Avoiding isolated areas at night
  • Being aware of your surroundings and belongings
  • Following safety instructions when engaging in outdoor activities

Health in Tumauini

Visitors should take necessary health precautions when visiting Tumauini. These include:

  • Getting updated vaccinations before traveling
  • Drinking only bottled or filtered water
  • Bringing insect repellents to avoid mosquito-borne diseases like dengue.

Getting to Tumauini: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Tumauini

The nearest airport to Tumauini is Cauayan City Domestic Airport, which offers flights to and from Manila and other major cities in the Philippines.

Bus Stations Near Tumauini

  • Florida Bus
  • Dalin Bus
  • Victory Liner

Other Options

  • Hiring a private car/taxi
  • Riding a tricycle or habal-habal (motorcycle taxi)

How to Get Around in Tumauini

The most convenient way to get around Tumauini is by renting a motorbike, car, or tricycle.
