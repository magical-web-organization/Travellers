A Comprehensive Guide to Aparri, Cagayan, Philippines

If you’re looking for a unique destination that offers a mix of outdoor adventure and cultural experiences, Aparri should be on your radar. Located in the province of Cagayan, Aparri is a beautiful coastal town that offers breathtaking views of the Babuyan Channel and has a rich history.

Introduction to Aparri

Aparri is located on the northernmost tip of Luzon, the largest island in the Philippines. It is bounded on the west by the Babuyan Channel and on the east by the Cordillera Mountains, providing visitors with opportunities for hiking, biking, and exploring its natural wonders. The town’s population is around 65,000 as of 2021, and its major industries are agriculture, fishing, and tourism.

List of Places to Visit in Aparri

Aparri has a rich history and cultural heritage, as well as a stunning natural beauty. Here are some of the must-visit places in Aparri:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  1. St. Philomene Church - a beautiful and historical church that was built in the early 1900s. It is a popular destination for devotees and tourists alike.
  2. Aparri Delta Historical and Cultural Museum - a museum that showcases Aparri’s rich history and culture, such as its notable people, social life, and economy.
  3. Cagayan Provincial Museum and Historical Research Center - a historical museum that houses artifacts, antiques, and relics from Cagayan’s early history.

Natural Attractions of Aparri: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  1. Cape Engaño - located on Palaui Island, this is a picturesque lighthouse that provides a panoramic view of the Babuyan Channel.
  2. Palaui Island - offers visitors with water sports activities such as island hopping, snorkeling, and diving. It was also featured in the reality TV series “Survivor”.
  3. Duba Island - a serene island surrounded by clear blue waters, inviting visitors to enjoy its peacefulness and stunning natural beauty.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Aparri

Aparri boasts several hiking and biking routes that are perfect for nature lovers and adventure seekers. Some of the popular routes are:

  1. Aparri Baywalk - a 1.8-kilometer pathway that runs along the Aparri River and offers stunning views of the mountains and the sea.
  2. Palaui Island Lighthouse Trail - a 3.5-kilometer trail that leads to Cape Engaño Lighthouse and offers a panoramic view of the Babuyan Channel.
  3. Mount Cagua Trail - an 8-kilometer trail that takes hikers through a dense forest and offers a stunning view of the Cagayan Valley.

Best Things to Do in Aparri: Activities and Tours

Aparri offers plenty of activities and tours to cater to its visitors’ diverse interests. Some of the best things to do in Aparri are:

  1. Island-hopping tours to Palaui Island - this tour offers visitors the chance to explore the island’s hidden lagoons, pristine beaches, and historical landmarks.
  2. Caving and spelunking at Sierra Cave - this adventure offers visitors the chance to explore the underground world of the Sierra Cave system.
  3. Fishing tours - fishing is a popular activity in Aparri, especially for those who love to enjoy freshly caught seafood.

Week-Long Itinerary for Aparri

Day 1 - Arrival and check-in Day 2 - Visit St. Philomene Church, Aparri Delta Historical and Cultural Museum, and Aparri Baywalk Day 3 - Explore Palaui Island and visit the Cape Engaño Lighthouse Day 4 - Caving and spelunking at Sierra Cave Day 5 - Fishing tour and sample local seafood delicacies Day 6 - Visit Duba Island and enjoy its pristine beaches and clear waters Day 7 - Departure

Food and Drink Scene of Aparri

The food scene in Aparri is all about fresh seafood and local delicacies. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  1. Buridibud - a soup made with grated cassava, fish, and vegetables.
  2. Paksiw na Bangus - a sour stew made with milkfish, vinegar, and vegetables.
  3. Sungayan - grilled or fried river snails that are popular with the locals.

Local Beverages

  1. Batikuling Juice - a refreshing juice made from the fruit of the batikuling tree, which is sour and slightly sweet.
  2. Bugnay Wine - a locally produced wine made from the fruit of the bugnay tree, which is sweet and tangy.

Where to Stay in Aparri: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Aparri offers several lodging options for various budget ranges. Some of the popular ones are:

  1. Penthouse Hotel - a modern hotel that offers spacious rooms, a swimming pool, and a restaurant.
  2. Casa de Aparri - a cozy guesthouse located near the town center that offers great value for money.
  3. St. Peter Lodge - a budget-friendly lodge that offers clean and comfortable rooms.

What to Buy in Aparri: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Aparri is known for its fresh seafood, handicrafts, and agricultural products. Some of the best souvenirs and local products to buy in Aparri are:

  1. Pasalubong (souvenir) baskets - a basket filled with locally grown coffee, fruits, and rice cakes.
  2. Hand-loomed blankets and tablecloths - handcrafted textiles made by the locals using traditional weaving techniques.
  3. Pearls and shell products - pearls and shell handicrafts that are sold by local vendors.

Local Culture and Customs of Aparri

Aparri has a unique culture that is heavily influenced by its proximity to the sea and its long history. Some of its customs and traditions include:

  1. Mayawmay - a festival celebrated every February to honor the town’s patroness, St. Philomene.
  2. Ka-Aparrihan - a gathering of relatives, friends, and neighbors to celebrate a milestone or occasion.
  3. Pagoda - a sea procession that is celebrated every May to ask for a good harvest and safe voyage.

Best Time to Visit Aparri

The best time to visit Aparri is from December to February when the weather is cool and dry. This is also the time when the town celebrates the Mayawmay festival.

Do’s and Don’ts of Aparri

Do:

  1. Respect the local customs and traditions.
  2. Try the local delicacies.
  3. Be friendly to the locals.

Don’t:

  1. Litter the beaches and natural attractions.
  2. Disrespect the religious institutions.
  3. Wear revealing clothing in public places.

Safety and Health

Safety in Aparri

Aparri is generally a safe town, but tourists should still exercise caution and common sense when in public places. Petty theft is still prevalent, so it is best to keep valuables safe and secure.

Health in Aparri

Aparri has local hospitals and health clinics that can provide basic medical services. However, it is still best to bring your own medicine and consult your doctor before traveling. Visitors should also be mindful of the food they eat and the water they drink to avoid any health issues.

Getting to Aparri: Transportation Options and Logistics

Aparri is accessible by air, land, and sea. Here are the transportation options and logistics:

Airports Near Aparri

The nearest airport to Aparri is Tuguegarao Airport, which is around 60 kilometers away. From Tuguegarao, visitors can take a bus or van to Aparri.

Bus Stations Near Aparri

Several bus and van companies service the route from Manila to Aparri daily. The journey takes around 14 hours.

Other Options

Visitors can also take a private car or hire a van from Tuguegarao to Aparri. There are also ferries that operate from Manila to Aparri via the Babuyan Channel.

How to Get Around in Aparri

The most common mode of transportation in Aparri is the tricycle. Visitors can also rent a bike or a motorbike to explore the town and its nearby attractions.

Where to Go Next from Aparri: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Aparri is located in Cagayan Valley, which has several nearby provinces that offer unique attractions and experiences. Here are some of the nearby destinations and excursions from Aparri:

  1. Tuguegarao - a bustling city that is famous for its hot springs and adventure activities such as caving and spelunking.
  2. Callao Cave - a natural wonder located in Peñablanca, Cagayan, that features impressive rock formations and an underground river.
  3. Ilocos Norte - a province north of Cagayan that offers a mix of natural wonders and historical landmarks.

Conclusion

Aparri, Cagayan, is a destination that is worth visiting for its mix of natural beauty and cultural heritage. It offers visitors a unique and off-the-beaten-path experience that is unparalleled in the Philippines. With this comprehensive travel guide, you can plan your perfect getaway to this charming town.
