Introduction to Southern Nueva Vizcaya

Located in the heart of Cagayan Valley, Southern Nueva Vizcaya is a hidden gem waiting to be discovered by avid travelers. Surrounded by stunning mountain ranges, lush green forests, and natural parks, this destination is perfect for outdoor enthusiasts seeking an adventure. On top of that, Southern Nueva Vizcaya is rich in cultural and historical significance, with numerous museums, landmarks, and attractions that showcase its unique heritage.

In this ultimate travel guide, we’ll take you on a journey to explore the best of Southern Nueva Vizcaya, including the must-visit attractions, outdoor activities, local customs, food and drink scene, where to stay, safety and health, transportation options, and much more.

List of Places to Visit in Southern Nueva Vizcaya

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

For history buffs and culture lovers, Southern Nueva Vizcaya offers a wealth of attractions to explore. Here are some of the top cultural destinations in the region:

  • Museum of Nueva Vizcaya: Located in Bayombong, the capital town of Nueva Vizcaya, this museum houses a variety of exhibits that showcase the province’s history, culture, and natural resources, including a collection of traditional costumes and accessories worn by various indigenous tribes.

  • Bangan Hill National Park: This park is home to the remains of a World War II Japanese Shrine, which includes a series of underground tunnels and rooms that served as shelters during the war. Visitors can explore the site and learn about the park’s rich history and cultural significance.

  • Dupax Del Sur Church: This historical church is one of the oldest structures in the province, built in the early 18th century by Spanish colonizers. The edifice features Baroque and Gothic influences and has been designated as a National Historical Landmark.

  • Nueva Vizcaya Capitol Building: Built in 1918, this government building is a testament to the province’s rich political history and architectural heritage. It features a neoclassical design and is considered one of the most prominent landmarks in the town of Bayombong.

Natural Attractions of Southern Nueva Vizcaya: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Southern Nueva Vizcaya is a nature lover’s paradise, with a variety of breathtaking landscapes and outdoor activities to choose from. Here are some of the top natural attractions in the region:

  • Imugan Falls: Located in Santa Fe, Imugan Falls is a hidden gem waiting to be discovered by visitors seeking an adventure. The falls cascade over a series of terraces and pools, offering a picturesque backdrop for hikers and trekkers.

  • Mt. Ugo: Standing at 2130 meters above sea level, Mt. Ugo is one of the highest peaks in Luzon and a favorite among hikers and mountaineers. The summit offers panoramic views of the surrounding landscape and is a great spot for camping and stargazing.

  • Dalton Pass: Located along the national highway that cuts through the province, this mountain pass offers stunning views of the surrounding valleys and plains. It has been the site of various historical battles and is considered a significant landmark in the region.

  • Ambaguio Agricultural Terraces: These terraces, located in the town of Ambaguio, are a living testament to the Ifugao people’s ancient terracing practices. The site is a designated National Cultural Treasure and offers visitors a glimpse into the region’s rich agricultural heritage.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Southern Nueva Vizcaya

For those seeking an outdoor adventure, Southern Nueva Vizcaya offers a variety of hiking and biking trails that cater to different skill levels and interests. Here are some of the top routes to explore:

  • Mt. Ugo Traverse: This hiking trail is a favorite among experienced hikers and mountaineers. The route takes visitors through lush forests, steep ridges, and rocky terrains, offering breathtaking views of the surrounding mountains and valleys.

  • Imugan-Bagting Waterfall Network Trail: This scenic trail takes visitors to a series of waterfalls, including Imugan and Bagting Falls. The route follows the Imugan River and passes through stunning rice fields, coconut groves, and traditional Ifugao villages.

  • Magat Dam Reservoir Dam Trail: This biking trail is perfect for those seeking a more leisurely adventure. The route follows the perimeter trail of Magat Dam Reservoir, offering stunning views of the lake and its surroundings.

Best Things to Do in Southern Nueva Vizcaya: Activities and Tours

Apart from its natural and cultural attractions, Southern Nueva Vizcaya also offers a variety of activities and tours that cater to different interests and preferences. Here are some of the top things to do in the region:

  • Experience Ifugao Culture: The region is home to various indigenous tribes, including the Ifugao people. Visitors can immerse themselves in the local culture by participating in traditional rituals, attending cultural festivals, and visiting local farms and markets.

  • Try Local Delicacies: Southern Nueva Vizcaya is known for its unique culinary offerings, including “ginataang palaka” (frog cooked in coconut milk), “kilawin” (raw meat marinated in vinegar and spices), and “dudol” (sweet rice cake with coconut milk). Visitors can try these dishes at local restaurants and eateries.

  • Visit the Local Markets: The province has a rich agricultural sector, with various crops and products grown and produced locally. Visitors can explore the local markets and buy fresh produce, handmade crafts, and souvenirs to bring back home.

Week-Long Itinerary for Southern Nueva Vizcaya

If you’re planning to spend a week in Southern Nueva Vizcaya, here’s a sample itinerary to help you make the most of your trip:

  • Day 1: Visit the Museum of Nueva Vizcaya and explore the town of Bayombong

  • Day 2: Hike to Imugan Falls and explore the rice terraces

  • Day 3: Visit Dupax Del Sur Church and explore the town of Dupax del Sur

  • Day 4: Bike along Magat Dam Reservoir and visit the local farms

  • Day 5: Hike Mt. Ugo and explore traditional Ifugao villages

  • Day 6: Experience Ifugao culture and attend a local festival or ritual

  • Day 7: Visit local markets and try traditional delicacies

Food and Drink Scene of Southern Nueva Vizcaya

Must-Try Dishes

Southern Nueva Vizcaya offers a variety of unique and flavorful dishes that are a must-try for foodies and culinary enthusiasts. Here are some of the top dishes to try in the region:

  • Ginataang Palaka: This dish is made from frog cooked in coconut milk, chili, garlic, ginger, and other spices. It’s a traditional delicacy in the region and is usually served during special occasions and events.

  • Pancit Batil Patong: This dish is a popular noodle soup in the province, made from fresh egg noodles, shredded vegetables, meats, and a boiled egg. It’s often served with a side of vinegar and chili.

  • Dudol: This sweet rice cake is a popular dessert in the region, made from glutinous rice, coconut milk, and sugar. It’s often served during celebrations and special occasions.

Local Beverages

Apart from its unique culinary offerings, Southern Nueva Vizcaya also boasts a variety of local beverages that are worth trying. Here are some of the top drinks to sample in the region:

  • Bugnay Wine: This wine is made from the berries of the bugnay tree, which is native to the Philippines. It has a sweet and sour taste and is known for its medicinal properties.

  • Arabica Coffee: Southern Nueva Vizcaya is known for its high-quality Arabica coffee, which is grown in the region’s fertile valleys and hillsides. Visitors can sample the coffee at local cafes and restaurants.

Where to Stay in Southern Nueva Vizcaya: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Southern Nueva Vizcaya offers a variety of accommodation options, ranging from budget-friendly hostels to luxury hotels and resorts. Here are some of the top places to stay in the region:

  • Jubilee Bed and Breakfast: This cozy bed and breakfast is located in the heart of Bayombong and offers comfortable rooms and personalized service. It’s a great option for budget-conscious travelers.

  • Amancio’s Place: This modern hotel is located in Solano and offers spacious rooms, a swimming pool, and a restaurant. It’s a great option for families and couples seeking a relaxing getaway.

  • Mt. Ugo Eco Inn: This eco-friendly inn is located in Itogon and offers stunning views of Mt. Ugo and the surrounding landscape. It’s a great option for hikers and outdoor enthusiasts.

What to Buy in Southern Nueva Vizcaya: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Southern Nueva Vizcaya offers a variety of locally made products and handicrafts that make for great souvenirs and gifts. Here are some of the top products to bring back home:

  • Ifugao Handicrafts: The region is home to various Indigenous tribes, including the Ifugao people, who are known for their intricate handicrafts, including basketry, woodcarvings, and weaving.

  • Coffee: Southern Nueva Vizcaya is famous for its high-quality Arabica coffee, which is grown and produced locally. Visitors can buy bags of coffee beans to bring back home.

  • Bugnay Wine: This sweet and sour wine made from the berries of the bugnay tree is a unique and flavorful gift to bring back from the region.

Local Culture and Customs of Southern Nueva Vizcaya

The province of Nueva Vizcaya is a melting pot of various cultures and traditions, including those of the Ilocano, Ifugao, Kalanguya, and Gaddang people. Visitors can immerse themselves in the local culture and customs by participating in traditional rituals, attending local festivals, and learning about the region’s history and heritage.

Best Time to Visit Southern Nueva Vizcaya

The best time to visit Southern Nueva Vizcaya is during the dry months of November to April when the weather is generally mild and pleasant. The rainy season, which runs from May to October, may hinder outdoor activities and hiking trails.

Do’s and Don’ts of Southern Nueva Vizcaya

Do:

  • Respect the local customs and traditions of the Indigenous tribes that reside in the region.

  • Try the local delicacies and sample the unique beverages, wines, and coffee.

  • Dress modestly and appropriately, especially when visiting religious sites and cultural destinations.

Don’t:

  • Disrespect the local culture and customs by acting inappropriately or engaging in disrespectful behavior.

  • Damage or destroy any natural or historical sites.

  • Ignore safety precautions when participating in outdoor activities and adventures.

Safety and Health

Safety in Southern Nueva Vizcaya

Southern Nueva Vizcaya is generally safe for visitors, but it’s important to take precautions, especially when hiking and participating in outdoor activities. Here are some safety tips to keep in mind:

  • Always inform someone of your itinerary.

  • Bring appropriate gear and equipment when participating in outdoor activities.

  • Seek the advice of local guides and experts when exploring unfamiliar trails and destinations.

Health in Southern Nueva Vizcaya

Visitors to Southern Nueva Vizcaya are advised to take basic health precautions, including:

  • Drinking only bottled or boiled water.

  • Preparing and cooking food properly.

  • Applying insect repellent and using mosquito nets when necessary.

Getting to Southern Nueva Vizcaya: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Southern Nueva Vizcaya

The closest airport to Southern Nueva Vizcaya is Cauayan Airport, located approximately 60 kilometers away. From the airport, visitors can take a taxi or a bus to reach their destination.

Bus Stations Near Southern Nueva Vizcaya

The most convenient way to get to Southern Nueva Vizcaya is by bus. Regular buses run from Manila to Bayombong and Solano, with several stops along the way.

Other Options

Visitors can also rent a car or hire a private car service to reach Southern Nueva Vizcaya. However, it’s important to note that road conditions in the region may be challenging, especially during the rainy season.

How to Get Around in Southern Nueva Vizcaya

The best way to get around Southern Nueva Vizcaya is by tricycle, a three-wheeled vehicle that operates like a taxi. Tricycles are readily available in most towns and can be hired for a reasonable fee.

Where to Go Next from Southern Nueva Vizcaya: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Southern Nueva Vizcaya is located in the heart of Cagayan Valley and is a great starting point for exploring other destinations in the region. Here are some nearby destinations and excursions to consider:

  • Tuguegarao City: Located approximately 100 kilometers from Southern Nueva Vizcaya, Tuguegarao City is the capital of Cagayan and is known for its historical sites and unique culinary offerings.

  • Callao Cave: This cave is located in Peñablanca and is a popular destination for spelunking and exploring.

  • Magat River: This river is a popular spot for fishing, boating, and kayaking, and is located approximately 20 kilometers from Southern Nueva Vizcaya.
