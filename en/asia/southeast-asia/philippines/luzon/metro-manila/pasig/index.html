Introduction to Pasig

Pasig is a city located in Metro Manila, in the Luzon region of the Philippines. It is one of the oldest cities in the country and is known for its historical significance. The city is also home to various natural attractions, cultural landmarks, and exciting outdoor adventures that attract tourists from all over the world.

List of Places to Visit in Pasig

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • The Pasig City Museum: This museum houses various historical artifacts, artworks and exhibits, which tells the story of Pasig’s history, culture, and traditions.
  • The Bahay na Tisa: One of Pasig’s landmark structures, this colonial era building was home to various notable figures in Philippine history.
  • The Immaculate Conception Cathedral: Also known as the Pasig Cathedral, this church is one of the oldest churches in the country and is known for its beautiful architecture and religious significance.

Natural Attractions of Pasig: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • The Marikina River: A popular spot for kayaking, rafting, and jet skiing; the river is also home to various endemic species of flora and fauna.
  • The Pasig Rainforest Park: This 4-hectare ecological park is located at the heart of the city and is home to various species of birds, butterflies, and other wildlife.
  • Mount Maarat: A nature park and conservation area, this mountain offers various hiking trails and scenic views of the city.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Pasig

  • The Pasig Bike Trail: A scenic bike trail that runs along the Marikina River and offers views of the city’s skyline.
  • San Vicente Ridge Trail: A challenging hiking trail that passes through some of Pasig’s most beautiful natural landscapes, including Mount Maarat and the San Vicente River.
  • Fern Trail: A lush and verdant trail that passes through various waterfalls and natural formations, perfect for nature lovers.

Best Things to Do in Pasig: Activities and Tours

  • Urban Adventure Quest in Pasig: A city-wide scavenger hunt that allows visitors to explore Pasig’s streets and famous landmarks, while learning about its culture and history.
  • Pasig River Cruise: A scenic boat ride along the Pasig River, where visitors can see the city’s historic sites from a unique vantage point.
  • Pasig Food Tour: A culinary adventure that allows visitors to sample the city’s local delicacies and street food.

Food and Drink Scene of Pasig

Must-Try Dishes of Pasig

  • Sinigang na Baboy: A popular Filipino sour soup made with pork, tamarind, and various vegetables served with steamed rice.
  • Adobo: The Philippines’ national dish, made with meat or vegetables, seasoned with soy sauce, vinegar, garlic, and other spices.
  • Lechon: A roasted pig traditionally served in festive occasions, it is one of the country’s most iconic dishes.

Local Beverages of Pasig

  • Sago’t Gulaman: A refreshing drink made with tapioca pearls, brown sugar, water, and gulaman or agar-agar.
  • Barako Coffee: A strong coffee made from Liberica coffee beans, which are native to the Philippines and have a distinct flavor.
  • Lambanog: A clear, colorless drink made from coconut sap that is distilled and bottled.

Where to Stay in Pasig: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Discovery Suites Manila: A luxury hotel with spacious rooms, elegant fixtures, and various amenities, including a pool and fitness center.
  • Privato Ortigas: A trendy and modern boutique hotel that offers comfortable rooms at an affordable price. It is known for its rooftop bar and restaurant that offers a panoramic view of the city.
  • Hive Hotel and Convention Place: A mid-range hotel that provides cozy and welcoming rooms and has easy access to various attractions in the city.

What to Buy in Pasig: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Hand-woven Cloth: Pasig is home to various indigenous tribes, who are skilled in weaving different types of fabrics and textiles. Check out the local markets to purchase their work.
  • Filipino Handicrafts and Artworks: From pottery to wood carvings, Pasig’s local markets offer a wide selection of handmade artworks and crafts that make unique souvenirs.

Local Culture and Customs of Pasig

  • Music and Dance: Pasig is known for its unique music and dance culture, including tinikling, pandanggo sa ilaw, and carinosa.
  • Festivals and Celebrations: Pasig celebrates various festivals throughout the year, including the Bañamos Festival, which honors the Immaculate Conception Cathedral and the many other religious festivals.

Best Time to Visit Pasig

The best time to visit Pasig is during the dry season, which runs from November to May. Temperatures are cooler in December to February, making it a perfect time for outdoor activities.

Do’s and Don’ts of Pasig

Do:

  • Respect the local customs and traditions.
  • Keep emergency contact numbers with you at all times.
  • Tip in restaurants and bars.

Don’t:

  • Wear revealing clothing in religious and cultural sites.
  • Engage in public displays of affection.
  • Photograph people without their permission.

Safety and Health

Safety in Pasig

Pasig is generally a safe place to visit. However, visitors must be vigilant and keep an eye on their belongings and surroundings.

Health in Pasig

The city has numerous hospitals and health centers that provide healthcare services to locals and visitors alike. It is also advisable to take necessary precautions concerning food and water hygiene.

Getting to Pasig: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Pasig

The closest major airport to Pasig is Ninoy Aquino International Airport (NAIA) in Manila. From the airport, you can take a taxi or private car to Pasig.

Bus Stations Near Pasig

Various bus lines such as Victory Liner and Genesis Transport offer their services at the Pasig Bus Terminal.

Other Options

Visitors can take a taxi, or if on a budget, jeeps and tricycles to navigate the city.

How to Get Around in Pasig

Pasig has various transportation options, such as taxis, buses, jeepneys, tricycles and ride hailing services, grab and Uber are also available.

Where to Go Next from Pasig: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Antipolo City: A mountainous city known for its majestic panoramic views, art scene, and the Hinulugang Taktak Waterfalls.
  • Tagaytay City: A popular tourist destination known for its cooler climate, scenic views, and Taal Lake and Volcano.
  • Corregidor Island: An island fortress that holds significant historical and cultural significance and is a popular site for hiking, bird watching, and eco-tourism.

Conclusion

Pasig is one of the most interesting destinations in the Philippines, a city that is steeped in history and rich culture, with a vibrant natural landscape, a diverse food and drink scene, and exciting outdoor activities. With this comprehensive travel guide, you can create an itinerary that allows you to experience Pasig in full and leave with lasting memories.
