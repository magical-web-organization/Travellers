Las Piñas in the National Capital Region of the Philippines is a charming city full of surprises. From its cultural heritage to its natural beauty, Las Piñas is a destination that appeals to both locals and tourists alike. In this comprehensive travel guide, we’ll show you the must-see sights, best activities, and local culture that makes Las Piñas unique.

Introduction to Las Piñas

Located in the southern part of Metro Manila and known for its historic bamboo organ instrument, Las Piñas is a vibrant city that offers a perfect blend of urban living and natural beauty. With a population of just over half a million people, Las Piñas maintains a small-town charm while still being a bustling metropolis.

List of Places to Visit in Las Piñas

Whether you’re a history buff, a nature lover, or an adventurous traveler, Las Piñas has something to offer for everyone. Here’s a list of places to visit in Las Piñas:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Bamboo Organ Church: The San Jose Parish Church is known for its 19th-century bamboo organ, one of the oldest and largest of its kind in the world.
  • Sarao Motors: Visit the oldest jeepney factory in the Philippines and see firsthand how these iconic vehicles are made.
  • The Las Piñas Historical Corridor: This 7.2-kilometer stretch of road is lined with historical buildings, including the Casa de Segunda, the ancestral house of Emilio Aguinaldo’s first wife.
  • Venuzuelan Park: A beautiful public park that is home to a famous bronze statue of Apollo, the god of music, located just a few meters away from the Bamboo Organ Church.

Natural Attractions of Las Piñas: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Las Piñas-Parañaque Critical Habitat and Ecotourism Area (LPPCHEA): Also known as the “Bird Sanctuary,” this protected area is a haven for migratory birds and is ideal for bird watching enthusiasts.
  • Alabang-Zapote River: Take a relaxing boat ride along the river and explore the lush mangrove forest and fishing villages.
  • Zapote Bridge: Built in 1844 and once the site of a historic battle, this bridge offers a beautiful view of the river and is perfect for taking photos.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Las Piñas

If you’re an adventure seeker, you’ll love exploring the hiking and biking trails of Las Piñas. Some of the best routes include:

  • Las Piñas-Parañaque Critical Habitat and Ecotourism Area (LPPCHEA): This protected area is home to a few easy hiking trails that offer a unique outdoor experience.
  • Riverwalk Trail: A 7-kilometer scenic trail along the Alabang-Zapote River that’s great for hiking, biking, and jogging.

Best Things to Do in Las Piñas: Activities and Tours

  • Ride a Jeepney: Experience the iconic Filipino jeepney as you explore the city’s streets and landmarks.
  • Learn Filipino Martial Arts: Experience the ancient art of Arnis, a popular form of martial arts in the Philippines.
  • Enjoy Local Delicacies: Take a food tour and savor the delicious flavors of Filipino cuisine, including adobo, pancit, and halo-halo.

Week-Long Itinerary for Las Piñas

Here’s a suggested week-long itinerary for Las Piñas:

Day 1: Visit the San Jose Parish Church and see the Bamboo Organ. Day 2: Take a boat ride along the Alabang-Zapote River and explore the mangrove forest. Day 3: Learn Filipino martial arts and have a unique cultural experience. Day 4: Take a food tour of Las Piñas and experience the flavors of Filipino cuisine. Day 5: Visit the Sarao Motors and get a behind-the-scenes look at how jeepneys are made. Day 6: Take a day trip to the nearby coastal town of Bacoor and explore its historic sites and delicious seafood cuisine. Day 7: Relax and unwind at the Venezuelan Park and enjoy the beautiful scenery.

Food and Drink Scene of Las Piñas

Las Piñas has a vibrant food scene that reflects the city’s rich culture and history. Here are some of the must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Adobo: A popular Filipino dish made with pork or chicken and marinated in soy sauce and vinegar.
  • Pancit: A stir-fried noodle dish mixed with vegetables and meat or seafood.
  • Lumpia: Filipino-style spring rolls filled with vegetables or meat.

Local Beverages

  • Halo-halo: A famous Filipino dessert made with shaved ice, evaporated milk, sweetened beans and fruits, and topped with ice cream.
  • Sago’t Gulaman: A refreshing drink made with a mix of sago pearls, gelatin, brown sugar, and syrup.

Where to Stay in Las Piñas: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Las Piñas offers a variety of accommodation options to suit different budgets and needs. Here are some of the best places to stay:

  • Eurotel Las Piñas: A budget-friendly hotel that offers comfortable rooms and excellent service.
  • Acacia Hotel Manila: A luxurious hotel that offers modern amenities and impeccable service.
  • Casa de Segunda Luxury Suites: A boutique hotel that is housed in a historic building and offers a unique experience.

What to Buy in Las Piñas: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Here are some of the souvenirs and local products to bring home from Las Piñas:

  • Jeepney Keychains: Take home a miniature version of the iconic Filipino jeepney.
  • Handmade Bags: These are made from abaca, a natural fiber that is indigenous to the Philippines.
  • Bamboo Organ Souvenirs: Bring home a bamboo organ souvenir to commemorate your visit to the famous instrument.

Local Culture and Customs of Las Piñas

Filipinos are known for their friendly and hospitable nature, and Las Piñas residents are no exception. When visiting Las Piñas, it’s important to be respectful of the local culture and customs, such as shaking hands or offering a small gift when meeting someone for the first time.

Best Time to Visit Las Piñas

The best time to visit Las Piñas is from December to February when the weather is cooler and drier. The rainy season starts in June and lasts until November, so it’s best to avoid these months if you want to avoid the rain.

Do’s and Don’ts of Las Piñas

Do:

  • Respect local customs and traditions.
  • Try local delicacies and beverages.
  • Explore the city’s natural and cultural attractions.

Don’t:

  • Wear revealing clothing when visiting religious sites.
  • Litter or harm protected species when exploring natural sites.
  • Show disrespect to the locals or their customs.

Safety and Health

Safety in Las Piñas

Las Piñas is considered a relatively safe city with a low crime rate. However, like any other city, it’s important to take precautions to ensure your safety.

Health in Las Piñas

Las Piñas has several hospitals and medical centers that offer quality healthcare services. It’s recommended that travelers get vaccinated before visiting the Philippines and take precautions against mosquito-borne illnesses like dengue fever.

Getting to Las Piñas: Transportation Options and Logistics

Las Piñas is easily accessible by car, bus, or taxi. Here are some transportation options to consider:

Airports Near Las Piñas

The closest airport to Las Piñas is the Ninoy Aquino International Airport (NAIA), which is approximately 9.5 miles away.

Bus Stations Near Las Piñas

The nearest bus stations to Las Piñas are in Pasay and Alabang.

Other Options

Taxis and ride-sharing services like Grab are widely available in Las Piñas and offer a convenient way to get around the city.

How to Get Around in Las Piñas

Public transportation options in Las Piñas include jeepneys and tricycles. Taxis and ride-sharing services are also widely available. Walking or biking is another option for exploring the city’s main attractions.

Where to Go Next from Las Piñas: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Here are some nearby destinations and excursions to consider after your visit to Las Piñas:

  • Tagaytay: A scenic city known for its cool climate, beautiful views, and the famous Taal Volcano.
  • Manila: The bustling capital city of the Philippines famous for its historical landmarks, museums and beaches.
  • Laguna: A province that offers a perfect mix of art, culture, and nature with various activities and attractions such as hot springs, caves, and waterfalls.

Conclusion

Las Piñas is a hidden gem in Manila that offers an authentic and immersive travel experience. With its cultural attractions, natural beauty, and friendly locals, it’s a must-visit destination for travelers who want to see the real Philippines. Use this travel guide to plan your trip to Las Piñas and create unforgettable memories.
