Introduction to Taguig

Located in Metro Manila, Taguig offers a mix of modern and traditional experiences that cater to locals and tourists. Taguig is known for being the location of some of the largest business districts in the country, but it also boasts attractions and experiences that reflect the country’s rich cultural heritage. In this comprehensive travel guide, we will explore the best things to see, do, eat, and experience in Taguig.

List of Places to Visit in Taguig

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • The Philippine Veterans Museum - This museum showcases the bravery of Filipinos during World War II, including the heroic acts of the country’s illustrious veterans.
  • The Heritage Park - A peaceful park in the middle of the hustle and bustle of the city, where you can find the final resting place of some of the country’s prominent figures.
  • Ayala Museum - Located in the posh Bonifacio Global City, this museum features interactive exhibits showcasing the art and history of the Philippines.

Natural Attractions of Taguig: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Tenement Museum - This museum offers a glimpse of how the city’s residents lived in tenement housing back in the day, showing the evolution of urban living.
  • Track 30th - This active park is the perfect place to go jogging or cycling. It also features a gentle slope that is perfect for a leisurely walk or run.
  • The Mind Museum - This interactive science museum is perfect for families and houses various exhibits that cater to children and adults alike.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Taguig

  • The Lawton-Timog Trail - This trail offers some of the best views of the city, showing you the modern metropolis from a unique angle.
  • The Makati to BGC Trail - This trail takes you from Makati to Taguig and highlights some of the city’s most prominent landmarks.

Best Things to Do in Taguig: Activities and Tours

  • Food Tour - Taguig is known for its local delicacies. A food tour in the city will give you a chance to try some of the best dishes that the city has to offer.
  • Art Tour - Taguig’s art scene is thriving. A tour of the city’s art galleries and museums will give you insight into the contemporary art scene in the Philippines.
  • City Exploration - The city is filled with hidden gems that are waiting to be discovered. Exploring the city on foot is the best way to discover the quirky spots and unique experiences that the city has to offer.

Food and Drink Scene of Taguig

Must-Try Dishes

  • Balut - A delicacy that is uniquely Filipino, balut is a fertilized duck egg that is boiled and eaten with salt or vinegar.
  • Adobo - One of the country’s iconic dishes, adobo is a stew made with chicken or pork, and a blend of soy sauce and vinegar.
  • Lechon - A staple dish in most celebrations, this roasted pig is cooked to perfection and served with rice.

Local Beverages

  • San Miguel Beer - A popular beer in the Philippines, San Miguel Beer is a refreshing drink that is perfect for any occasion.
  • Calamansi Juice - A refreshing and tangy drink made from fresh calamansi fruit, this juice is popular all over the country.
  • Sago’t Gulaman - A sweet and refreshing drink made with sago pearls, gulaman jelly, and brown sugar syrup.

Where to Stay in Taguig: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Ascott - Located in Bonifacio Global City, this serviced apartment offers luxurious and spacious accommodations.
  • F1 Hotel Manila - A family-friendly hotel located near Bonifacio Global City, F1 Hotel Manila offers spacious and elegant rooms.
  • Z Hostel - A budget option located in Poblacion, Makati, Z Hostel provides comfortable dormitory-style accommodations for backpackers and budget travelers.

What to Buy in Taguig: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Hagonoy Buntal Hat - This traditional hat is made of woven buri palm leaves and is a stylish souvenir that embodies the city’s heritage.
  • Barong Tagalog - A traditional Filipino shirt made of lightweight material that is perfect for hot weather. This is a great souvenir for those looking for traditional clothing.
  • Philippine-made crafts - From woven baskets to handcrafted jewelry, there are plenty of locally made crafts that are perfect for bringing home as souvenirs.

Local Culture and Customs of Taguig

The city’s culture is influenced by Spanish and American colonization and is known for its warm hospitality. Some important customs to remember when in Taguig include:

  • Respect for elders - As with most cultures in Asia, respect for elders is highly valued in Taguig.
  • No shoes inside the home - It is customary to take off one’s shoes before entering a home.
  • Always offer a handshake when meeting someone new - A firm handshake is seen as a sign of respect and is an essential part of Filipino etiquette.

Best Time to Visit Taguig

The best time to visit Taguig is during the cooler months which fall between November and February, avoiding the hot and humid rainy season as well as the scorching summer months.

Do’s and Don’ts of Taguig

Do:

  • Do be respectful of local customs and traditions
  • Do try the local delicacies
  • Do haggle when shopping in local markets

Don’t:

  • Don’t litter or deface public property
  • Don’t forget to tip the locals, especially the service crew in restaurants

Safety and Health

Safety in Taguig

Taguig is generally safe but, visitors should also exercise caution when wandering around late at night, particularly in the more deserted areas of the city. Visitors should take appropriate safety measures and be mindful of their belongings and surroundings when exploring the city.

Health in Taguig

Visitors should be aware of pre-existing medical conditions and should carry with them medications they might need during their stay. It is also essential to note any potential disease threats in the areas to ensure proper protection against them. Visitors are advised to drink only bottled water.

Getting to Taguig: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Taguig

  • Ninoy Aquino International Airport (MNL) - The airport is located about 30 minutes from Taguig.

Bus Stations Near Taguig

  • JAM Liner Inc. - This bus terminal is located near Pasay City and is accessible through the metro rail transit.
  • Saints Mary’s Transport Services - This transport service is located near the airport.

Other Options

Taxis and ride-hailing services are available throughout the city. Visitors can also use the metro rail transit to navigate the city.

How to Get Around in Taguig

The city can be navigated by foot or through various modes of public transportation. Jeepneys, buses, and taxis are readily available for visitors to use.

Where to Go Next from Taguig: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Puerto Galera - A charming beach town located south of Manila is only a 90-minute drive from Taguig that offers a wide range of water sports and other outdoor activities.
  • Tagaytay - A city located south of Manila is only a 90-minute drive from Taguig with a scenic view of Taal Volcano and offers a variety of adventurous activities like hiking and horseback riding.

Conclusion

Taguig is a unique destination for those looking to experience a mix of modernity and cultural heritage. This comprehensive travel guide has given insight into all the exciting attractions and experiences the city holds, providing an itinerary that can fill in a week-long vacation. However, it is essential to note local customs and show respect when exploring all the city has to offer. With the knowledge acquired through this travel guide, this is your opportunity to visit the exciting city of Taguig!
