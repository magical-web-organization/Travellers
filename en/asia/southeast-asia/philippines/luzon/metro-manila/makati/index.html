Introduction to Makati

Makati is a vibrant city located in Metro Manila, the capital of the Philippines. Known for its bustling business district, Makati also offers an array of cultural, natural, and culinary experiences.

This guide will provide you with everything you need to know to plan a trip to Makati, including the top attractions to visit, hiking and biking routes, where to stay, and what to eat.

List of Places to Visit in Makati

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Makati is home to several museums and landmarks that highlight the rich history and culture of the Philippines.

The Ayala Museum is a must-visit for those interested in Filipino art and history. Here, you’ll find exhibits showcasing Philippine history, pre-colonial gold objects, and contemporary art.

Another cultural attraction is the Museo ng Makati, which features exhibits on Makati’s historical and cultural significance. You can learn about Makati’s evolution from a fishing village into a cosmopolitan city, as well as the customs and traditions of the region’s indigenous peoples.

Visit the Nuestra Señora de Gracia Church, one of the oldest churches in Manila, to admire its stunning Baroque architecture and learn about the church’s role in Philippine history.

Natural Attractions of Makati: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Despite the hustle and bustle of modern city life, Makati has places where you can escape to nature.

Poblacion’s Antipolo Open Air Observatory provides spectacular views of the Metro Manila skyline. Located just 20 minutes from Makati, a visit to this observatory makes for a wonderful day trip.

If you’re a nature lover, head to the La Mesa Eco Park to explore a 33-hectare park filled with hiking trails, picnic areas, and an outdoor pool.

In the heart of the city lies the Ayala Triangle Gardens, an urban oasis that serves as a fantastic spot for a peaceful walk, picnic, or outdoor yoga session.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Makati

Exploring Makati on foot or bike is an excellent way to soak up the city’s sights and sounds.

A popular urban trail is the Makati Greenbelt Park, composed of five interconnected parks which are ideal for a bike ride. There’s no need to bring a bike since bike-sharing services such as OFO are widely available in Makati.

The Rockwell to Ayala Triangle Hike is also an urban walking trail that traverses through Makati’s bustling streets and vibrant neighborhoods. As you explore the city on foot, you’ll encounter an array of shops, restaurants, and local entertainment options.

Best Things to Do in Makati: Activities and Tours

Makati is a destination that caters to travelers of all interests.

Foodies can go on an epicurean adventure by taking a food tour that includes tasting local favorites such as adobo, sinigang, and lechon.

For art enthusiasts, there’s the Pinto Art Museum that showcases contemporary Filipino art. The museum houses over 300 paintings, sculptures, and installations by some of the country’s most celebrated artists.

Shoppers will appreciate the Ayala Center, a shopping and entertainment complex that offers everything from high-end designer brands to local boutiques.

Week-Long Itinerary for Makati

Day 1: Explore the Ayala Museum and nearby Greenbelt Park. Day 2: Visit the Nuestra Señora de Gracia Church and the neighbouring Poblacion district. Day 3: Explore the La Mesa Eco Park and enjoy a picnic lunch. Day 4: Go on a food tour and sample local delicacies. Day 5: Visit the Pinto Art Museum and take a relaxing stroll through Ayala Triangle Gardens. Day 6: Shop at the Ayala Center and adjacent Glorietta complex. Day 7: Take a day trip to Antipolo Open Air Observatory or the nearby Tagaytay highlands.

Food and Drink Scene of Makati

Must-Try Dishes

Filipino cuisine is incredibly diverse and flavourful, and Makati has a wide array of restaurants where you can sample the best of the local cuisine. Some must-try dishes include:

  • Adobo: Pork or chicken cooked in vinegar, soy sauce, and spices.
  • Sinigang: A sour and savoury soup made with meat or fish, tamarind, and vegetables.
  • Lechon: Roasted pork with crispy skin and tender meat.

Local Beverages

San Miguel Beer is the national beer of the Philippines, and it’s widely available throughout Makati. If you’re looking for something non-alcoholic, try a refreshing glass of buko juice or calamansi juice.

Where to Stay in Makati: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Makati has a wide range of accommodation options to suit any budget.

For luxury travellers, the Raffles Makati and the Fairmont Makati are two of the city’s most luxurious hotels, offering world-class amenities and stunning views of the city.

For backpackers, there are several hostels located in the Poblacion district, such as Z Hostel and Lub D Makati. Guesthouses and homestays are also widely available, providing a more local and intimate experience.

What to Buy in Makati: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

For those looking to take a piece of Makati home with them, consider buying some local souvenirs and products.

Some popular souvenirs include handmade handicrafts, such as woven baskets and wooden carvings, as well as traditional Filipino textiles like barongs and sarongs.

Makati is also known for its street markets and boutiques that sell modern Filipino fashion, artisanal chocolates, handwoven bags, and much more.

Local Culture and Customs of Makati

The Philippines is a predominantly Catholic country, and many of the customs and traditions in Makati reflect this.

Always show respect when visiting religious sites such as the Nuestra Señora de Gracia Church and Ayala Chapel. When entering a church, dress modestly, and remove your hat.

Filipinos are known for their hospitality and friendliness, so don’t be shy to strike up a conversation with locals and learn more about their culture and traditions.

Best Time to Visit Makati

Makati has a tropical climate, with high temperatures and humidity year-round. The dry season is from December to May, making this the perfect time to explore the city’s outdoor attractions.

The rainy season starts in June and ends in November, so it’s best to be prepared with umbrellas and rain gear during this time.

Do’s and Don’ts of Makati

Do:

  • Learn some basic Tagalog phrases, the local language in Makati.
  • Always carry a water bottle and stay hydrated.
  • Dress conservatively when visiting religious sites.
  • Take your shoes off when entering someone’s home.

Don’t:

  • Forget to tip service staff in restaurants and hotels.
  • Wear shorts or revealing clothing when visiting temples or churches.
  • Take photos of people without their permission.

Safety and Health

Safety in Makati

Makati is a relatively safe city for tourists, but it’s always important to take precautions. Avoid carrying large sums of cash and valuables with you when you’re out and about.

Use caution when walking at night, especially in unlit areas. Stick to well-lit and populated streets or take a taxi or Grab ride.

Health in Makati

The Philippines is prone to dengue fever and malaria, so it’s always important to take precautions when travelling to the country. Wear long-sleeved shirts and pants, use mosquito repellent, and avoid outdoor activities during dawn and dusk.

Tap water is not safe to drink, so stick to bottled water or boiled tap water.

Getting to Makati: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Makati

The closest airport to Makati is Ninoy Aquino International Airport, located just 20 minutes away from the city. From there, you can take a taxi or Grab ride to reach your destination.

Bus Stations Near Makati

Several bus stations connect Makati to nearby cities and provinces. The main bus terminals are located in Pasay and Cubao, which are both accessible via public transportation or Grab ride.

Other Options

Makati is also accessible via the Manila Light Rail Transit (LRT) system, which connects various parts of Manila to Makati. Jeepneys, buses, and taxis are also widely available for getting around the city.

How to Get Around in Makati

Many of Makati’s attractions are within walking distance of each other, making it an excellent city to explore on foot. For longer distances or if you’re carrying a lot of luggage, taking a taxi or Grab ride is a convenient and affordable option.

Biking is also a great way to get around Makati’s streets and explore the city’s neighbourhoods. Bike-sharing services are available and can be rented for a reasonable price.

Where to Go Next from Makati: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Makati is a great starting point for exploring the surrounding regions of the Philippines. Some popular destinations that are within a day’s trip of Makati include:

  • Tagaytay: A scenic city located about 60 kilometres away from Makati that’s known for its stunning views of Taal Lake and Taal Volcano.
  • Batangas: A province located south of Makati that’s home to several stunning beaches and diving spots.
  • Banaue Rice Terraces: One of the most spectacular rice terraces in the world located in the Cordillera region, about 9 hours away from Makati.

Conclusion

Makati is an incredible destination that offers visitors a vast range of experiences, from cultural attractions and natural adventures to delicious food and luxury accommodations. Planning a trip to Makati is easy and well-worth the experience for travellers of all interests.
