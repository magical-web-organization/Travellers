Introduction to Mandaluyong

Sitting in the heart of Metro Manila, Mandaluyong is a bustling city that offers a mix of urban and natural attractions. Whether it’s hiking, shopping, or exploring cultural sites, Mandaluyong has something to offer for every type of traveler. In this guide, we’ll explore the best things to do, see, and eat in Mandaluyong.

List of Places to Visit in Mandaluyong

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

1. Museo ng Makati

Located within the Makati City Hall compound, the Museo ng Makati features exhibits that showcase the city’s rich history. With displays ranging from pre-colonial times to the present day, the museum is an excellent resource for anyone interested in understanding the Philippines’ cultural heritage.

2. Philippine Army Museum

The Philippine Army Museum & Library is home to an extensive collection of military artifacts, including weapons, uniforms, and documents. Visitors can witness the history of the Philippines’ armed forces throughout the centuries, from the country’s earliest battles to modern-day conflicts.

Natural Attractions of Mandaluyong: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

1. Wawa Dam

The Wawa Dam is a popular destination amongst nature enthusiasts. Located on the border of Mandaluyong and Rodriguez in Rizal, the dam is surrounded by lush forests and craggy rocks. Visitors can enjoy hiking, bird watching, and snapping scenic photos throughout the area.

2. Kapitan Moy Heritage House

The Kapitan Moy Heritage House is a traditional Bahay na Bato, a type of Filipino house made of stone and wood. Built in the 1800s, the house serves as a museum and showcases Philippine architecture and culture.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Mandaluyong

1. La Mesa Watershed Reserve

Located just 30 minutes away from Mandaluyong, the La Mesa Watershed Reserve offers an escape from the hustle and bustle of the city. With its pristine hiking trails, biking routes, and picnic areas, La Mesa is a popular destination for those looking to explore the great outdoors.

Best Things to Do in Mandaluyong: Activities and Tours

1. Kapitolyo Food Tour

Kapitolyo is a foodie’s paradise and a go-to destination for anyone who wants to experience Mandaluyong’s culinary scene. Take a guided walking tour of Kapitolyo and sample some of the city’s best dishes.

2. Greenfield Weekend Market

The Greenfield Weekend Market is a popular destination for food and shopping. With over 50 food vendors and 200 stalls selling clothing, accessories, and home goods, the market is the perfect destination for anyone looking to sample some local flavor or pick up souvenirs.

Food and Drink Scene of Mandaluyong

Must-Try Dishes

1. Sisig

Sisig is a popular Filipino dish made from chopped pork, onions, and spices. It’s typically served on a sizzling plate and topped with an egg.

2. Adobo

Adobo is a classic Filipino dish made of meat marinated in a mixture of vinegar, soy sauce, and garlic. The meat is then slow-cooked until it’s tender and infused with flavor.

Local Beverages

1. San Miguel Beer

San Miguel is a popular Filipino beer that has been brewed since 1890. It comes in several different variations, including Pale Pilsen and Red Horse.

2. Buko Juice

Buko juice, or coconut water, is a refreshing drink that’s perfect for hot summer days. It’s served cold and comes straight from the coconut itself.

Where to Stay in Mandaluyong: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

1. RedDoorz Premium near Mandaluyong City Hall

The RedDoorz Premium is a comfortable and modern hotel that’s perfect for those looking for a budget-friendly stay in Mandaluyong. Rooms are equipped with air conditioning, flat-screen TVs, and private bathrooms.

2. The Legend Villas

For travelers who want a touch of luxury, the Legend Villas is an excellent option. The hotel features well-appointed rooms, a swimming pool, and a fitness center.

What to Buy in Mandaluyong: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

1. Barako Coffee

Barako coffee is a type of coffee that’s grown in the Batangas region of the Philippines. It’s known for its strong, bold flavor and rich aroma.

2. Handmade Crafts

Mandaluyong is home to many talented artisans who create handmade crafts and products. Look for items made of wood, bamboo, and coconut shells.

Local Culture and Customs of Mandaluyong

The Philippines is a country with a rich cultural heritage. Visitors to Mandaluyong can expect to encounter friendly locals, vibrant festivals, and a unique blend of Eastern and Western influences.

Best Time to Visit Mandaluyong

The best time to visit Mandaluyong is between November and February, when the weather is cool and dry. These months are also the peak travel season, so be prepared for crowds.

Do’s and Don’ts of Mandaluyong

Do:

  • Learn a few basic Filipino phrases.
  • Keep an open mind and embrace the local culture.
  • Explore Mandaluyong’s natural attractions.

Don’t:

  • Don’t wear revealing clothing in public places.
  • Don’t forget to bring insect repellent.
  • Don’t forget to carry cash, as some businesses don’t accept credit cards.

Safety and Health

Safety in Mandaluyong

Mandaluyong is generally a safe city, but visitors should still exercise caution and be aware of their surroundings. Be sure to keep your belongings close and avoid walking alone at night.

Health in Mandaluyong

Travelers to Mandaluyong should be up-to-date on all routine vaccinations before their trip. Mosquito-borne illnesses such as dengue and malaria are also present in the Philippines, so be sure to bring insect repellent and wear protective clothing.

Getting to Mandaluyong: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Mandaluyong

The Ninoy Aquino International Airport is the main airport serving Manila, and is located just 30 minutes away from Mandaluyong. From the airport, visitors can take a taxi or ride-sharing service to their destination.

Bus Stations Near Mandaluyong

Several major bus stations are located in Mandaluyong, including the Megamall Bus Station and the Shaw Boulevard Bus Station. These stations serve destinations throughout the region and the Philippines.

Other Options

Visitors to Mandaluyong can also take a taxi, ride-sharing service, or public transportation to get around the city and surrounding areas.

How to Get Around in Mandaluyong

Mandaluyong is a compact city that’s easy to navigate on foot. Visitors can also take a taxi, ride-sharing service, or public transportation to get around the city and surrounding areas.

Where to Go Next from Mandaluyong: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

1. Rizal

Rizal is a province located just east of Mandaluyong. The area is known for its lush forests, hiking trails, and waterfalls.

2. Tagaytay

Tagaytay is a popular destination located south of Mandaluyong. It’s known for its stunning views of Taal Lake and Volcano, as well as its cool climate.

Conclusion

Mandaluyong is a dynamic city that offers something for every type of traveler. Whether it’s hiking through the mountains, exploring cultural landmarks, or sampling local cuisine, visitors are sure to have an unforgettable experience in this vibrant destination.
