Introduction to Marikina

Marikina is a city located in the eastern part of Metro Manila, Philippines. Known as the “Shoe Capital of the Philippines” due to its thriving shoe industry, Marikina is also home to a rich history and culture, as well as natural wonders. Whether you’re a local or a traveler, Marikina has plenty to offer.

List of Places to Visit in Marikina

Whether you’re interested in Marikina’s cultural heritage or looking for outdoor adventures, here’s a list of places to visit:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Marikina Museum
  • Shoe Museum
  • Our Lady of the Abandoned Parish
  • Kapitan Moy Ancestral House

Natural Attractions of Marikina: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Marikina River Park
  • Rodriguez Dam
  • Marikina Heights
  • Marikina Watershed

Hiking/Biking Routes of Marikina

  • Marikina River Park to Quezon Memorial Circle
  • Marcos Highway to Sumulong Highway
  • Marikina Watershed to Mount Oriod
  • Barangka Trail

Best Things to Do in Marikina: Activities and Tours

  • Take a shoemaking workshop at Sapatero Manila
  • Explore Marikina’s street art scene
  • Join a Marikina River cruise
  • Try your hand at fishing in the Marikina River

Week-Long Itinerary for Marikina

Day 1: Visit the Marikina Museum, Shoe Museum, and Kapitan Moy Ancestral House. Day 2: Go hiking or biking at Marikina Watershed or Barangka Trail. Day 3: Take a Marikina River cruise. Day 4: Explore Marikina’s street art scene. Day 5: Visit Our Lady of the Abandoned Parish and Rodriguez Dam. Day 6: Learn shoemaking at Sapatero Manila. Day 7: Try local food and drinks and shop for souvenirs in Downtown Marikina.

Local Culture and Customs of Marikina

Marikina is known for its warm and hospitable people. Be respectful when visiting their cultural attractions and religious sites. Don’t forget to greet locals with “Magandang araw po” or “Good day, sir/ma’am.”

Best Time to Visit Marikina

The best time to visit Marikina is during the dry season, from December to April. This is when you can enjoy outdoor activities with lesser chances of rain. The city also celebrates its Shoe Festival during September.

Do’s and Don’ts of Marikina

Do:

  • Try local dishes and drinks.
  • Visit Marikina’s cultural and natural attractions.
  • Be respectful of local customs and traditions.

Don’t:

  • Litter in public places.
  • Disrespect sacred sites and religious practices.
  • Wear inappropriate clothing in public places.

Safety and Health

Safety in Marikina

Marikina is generally safe, but be cautious when visiting crowded places and public transportation. Keep your valuables in a safe place, and avoid going out late at night.

Health in Marikina

Hospital facilities are available in Marikina, and pharmacies are easily accessible. Stay hydrated and wear protective clothing when doing outdoor activities.

Getting to Marikina: Transportation Options and Logistics

Marikina is easily accessible via different modes of transportation.

Airports Near Marikina

  • Ninoy Aquino International Airport
  • Clark International Airport

Bus Stations Near Marikina

  • Araneta Center Bus Terminal
  • Robinsons Galleria Bus Terminal

Other Options

  • Jeepneys
  • Tricycles
  • Taxis

How to Get Around in Marikina

Marikina has a well-organized transportation system. You can get around the city through jeepneys, tricycles, buses, and taxis. You can also explore Marikina’s hiking and biking routes on foot or by bike.

Conclusion

Marikina offers a unique travel experience that combines culture, nature, and outdoor adventures. Explore the city’s shoe industry, cultural heritage, and local cuisine, and discover its natural wonders through hiking and biking routes. With this comprehensive guide, you’re ready to start your Marikina adventure.
