Introduction to Pasay

Pasay is a bustling city in Metro Manila, located in Luzon, Philippines. It is known for its entertainment hubs, casinos, and shopping centers. Visitors often find themselves attracted to the city’s historic landmarks, scenic views, and vibrant local culture.

List of Places to Visit in Pasay

Here are some of the top places to visit in Pasay:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Cultural Center of the Philippines The Cultural Center of the Philippines is the premier venue for performing arts in the country. It showcases the best of Filipino culture through music, dance, theater, and visual arts.

  • National Museum of Anthropology The National Museum of Anthropology houses an extensive collection of cultural artifacts from the Philippines and various Southeast Asian countries.

Natural Attractions of Pasay: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Roxas Boulevard Roxas Boulevard is a scenic coastal road that offers a beautiful view of Manila Bay. It’s the perfect place for a relaxing stroll or a romantic sunset walk.

  • Manila Baywalk The Manila Baywalk is a pedestrian promenade that runs along the bay. It offers scenic views, street performers, and food stalls.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Pasay

  • Libingan ng mga Bayani Trail The Libingan ng mga Bayani Trail is a peaceful hiking trail with a beautiful view of the Manila Bay. It’s a perfect getaway from the busy city.

Best Things to Do in Pasay: Activities and Tours

  • Star City Star City is an amusement park that offers thrilling rides, arcade games, and a 4D theater experience.

  • Art in Island Art in Island is a 3D museum that offers interactive art experiences. Visitors can take pictures and become part of the artwork.

Week-Long Itinerary for Pasay

Day Plan
1 Visit the Cultural Center of the Philippines and watch a performance
2 Take a tour of the National Museum of Anthropology
3 Enjoy a relaxing walk along Roxas Boulevard and catch a beautiful sunset at the Manila Bay
4 Hike the Libingan ng mga Bayani Trail
5 Have fun at Star City
6 Visit Art in Island and take unique pictures at the interactive art exhibit
7 Shop and dine at one of Pasay’s many entertainment hubs

Food and Drink Scene of Pasay

Filipino cuisine is a mix of Chinese, Spanish, and Malay influences. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages to sample in Pasay:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Adobo Adobo is a savory stew made with chicken, pork, or fish marinated in soy sauce, vinegar, and spices.

  • Sinigang Sinigang is a sour soup made with meat, fish, or vegetables, and tamarind or other sour fruits.

Local Beverages

  • San Miguel Beer San Miguel Beer is the most popular local beer in the Philippines.

  • Calamansi Juice Calamansi Juice is a refreshing, sweet and sour drink made with Philippine lime.

Where to Stay in Pasay: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

There are plenty of budget-friendly and luxurious accommodations in Pasay. Here are some recommendations:

  • Conrad Manila Conrad Manila is a luxurious hotel that offers a beautiful view of Manila Bay.

  • Red Planet Manila Aseana City Red Planet Manila Aseana City is a budget-friendly hotel located near the airport.

  • Kabayan Hotel Kabayan Hotel is a cozy guesthouse with affordable rates and Filipino-inspired design.

What to Buy in Pasay: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Pasay is known for its shopping hubs and street markets. Here are some souvenirs and local products to buy:

  • Pasalubong Treats Pasalubong treats are small food items that make great souvenirs. Examples include crispy dilis, dried mangoes, and polvoron.

  • Handicrafts Handicrafts made by local artisans include woven bags, wooden sculptures, and jewelry made from native materials.

Local Culture and Customs of Pasay

Filipino culture is deeply ingrained with family values, respect for elders, and a strong sense of hospitality. Visitors are expected to be courteous and respectful to locals. It is customary to take off shoes before entering a Filipino home or temple.

Best Time to Visit Pasay

The best time to visit Pasay is between November and February when the weather is cool and dry. Avoid visiting during the rainy season, which is from June to September.

Do’s and Don’ts of Pasay

Here are some important do’s and don’ts when visiting Pasay:

Do:

  • Respect local customs and traditions.

  • Dress modestly when visiting temples and other religious places.

Don’t:

  • Display public affection.

  • Refuse food or drinks offered by locals.

Safety and Health

Safety in Pasay

Like any urban area, Pasay can be prone to pickpocketing and petty theft. Visitors are advised to be vigilant and keep their valuables safe. It is also recommended to avoid walking alone at night and stick to well-lit, crowded areas.

Health in Pasay

Visitors should be cautious when eating street food and only drink bottled water. It is also advisable to bring mosquito repellent as dengue fever is common in the Philippines.

Getting to Pasay: Transportation Options and Logistics

Pasay is easily accessible by air, land, and sea. Here are some transportation options to get to Pasay:

Airports Near Pasay

  • Ninoy Aquino International Airport (NAIA)

Bus Stations Near Pasay

  • Jam Liner

  • JAC Liner

Other Options

  • Taxis

  • Jeepneys

  • Tricycles

How to Get Around in Pasay

The most convenient way to get around Pasay is by taxi or ride-hailing app. Jeepneys and tricycles are also available for short-distance rides.

Where to Go Next from Pasay: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Here are some nearby destinations and excursions to explore from Pasay:

  • Manila Manila is the capital city of the Philippines and is a bustling metropolis that offers a mix of history, culture, and entertainment.

  • Tagaytay Tagaytay is a city located south of Manila that offers a cool climate, scenic views, and local cuisine.

Conclusion

Pasay is a city that blends modernity with tradition. Visitors to Pasay can enjoy the best of Filipino culture and hospitality while experiencing thrilling adventures and activities. With its convenient location and accessible transportation, Pasay is a must-visit destination for travelers looking for an authentic Philippine experience.
