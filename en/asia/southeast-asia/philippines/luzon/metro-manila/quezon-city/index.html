Quezon City is a bustling metropolis and one of the largest cities in Metro Manila, Philippines. It’s a hub for culture, art, and history, with plenty of outdoor adventures and scenic beauty to explore. In this comprehensive travel guide, you’ll learn about the best places to visit, things to do, food to try, and more in Quezon City, Philippines.

Introduction to Quezon City

Quezon City, also known as QC, is the former capital of the Philippines, named after the country’s second president, Manuel Luis Quezon. It’s situated in the northern part of Metro Manila and is one of the most urbanized cities in the country. QC is known for its shopping malls, nightlife, and cultural attractions.

Quezon City offers a variety of attractions for travelers to explore, including museums, historical places, natural attractions, and more. Here’s a list of must-visit places in the city:

Local Culture and Customs of Quezon City

Filipino culture is diverse and deeply rooted in tradition. Here are some customs and traditions you should be aware of when visiting Quezon City:

  • Respect elders and authority figures
  • Take off your shoes when entering homes or establishments
  • Use “po” and “opo” to show respect
  • Avoid pointing with your fingers or feet
  • Exercise modesty

Best Time to Visit Quezon City

Quezon City is a year-round destination, but the best time to visit is between November and February when the weather is cooler and drier.

Do’s and Don’ts of Quezon City

Here are some do’s and don’ts to keep in mind when traveling in Quezon City:

Do:

  • Carry cash with you
  • Try local street food
  • Learn some basic Filipino phrases
  • Dress modestly when visiting religious sites
  • Be respectful of local customs and traditions

Don’t:

  • Walk alone in dark and unfamiliar areas at night
  • Engage in public displays of affection
  • Disrespect local authorities or law enforcement
  • Touch or move religious artifacts

Safety and Health

Quezon City is generally safe for travelers, but it’s always important to exercise caution and common sense when traveling in an unfamiliar area.

Safety in Quezon City

  • Be aware of your surroundings at all times
  • Keep your valuables secure
  • Avoid walking alone in dark or deserted places
  • Use trusted transportation services
  • Be cautious when using ATMs

Health in Quezon City

  • Drink bottled water
  • Use insect repellent to avoid mosquito bites
  • Apply sunscreen to protect your skin from the sun
  • Check for any travel advisories before visiting

Getting to Quezon City: Transportation Options and Logistics

Quezon City is easily accessible through various modes of transportation. Here are some options:

Airports Near Quezon City

Bus Stations Near Quezon City

Other Options

How to Get Around in Quezon City

Quezon City has a well-developed public transportation system, including buses, jeepneys, and trains. However, traffic can be heavy during peak hours, so it’s important to plan your journey accordingly.
