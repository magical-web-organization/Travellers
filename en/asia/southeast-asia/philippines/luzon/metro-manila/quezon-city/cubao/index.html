Introduction to Cubao

Cubao is a bustling district located in Quezon City, Metro Manila, Philippines. It is known for being a transportation hub and commercial center of the city. Cubao is a popular destination for both locals and tourists due to its vibrant nightlife, shopping centers, and various attractions. It is home to some of the country’s most famous landmarks and offers a rich cultural experience to visitors.

List of Places to Visit in Cubao

Cubao is a vibrant district that offers numerous attractions and experiences to visitors.

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Cubao is home to several museums and historical places that educate visitors about the country’s rich history and culture. The following are some of the famous cultural attractions in Cubao:

  • Art in Island
  • Filipino Heritage Library
  • Museo ng Quezon City
  • Ninoy Aquino Parks and Wildlife Center
  • People Power Monument

Natural Attractions of Cubao: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Cubao is not only famous for its man-made attractions but also for its natural beauty. Visitors can take part in various outdoor activities and enjoy scenic views. The following are some of the natural attractions of Cubao:

  • Araneta City Eco Park
  • La Mesa Eco Park
  • Quezon Memorial Circle
  • UP Diliman Campus
  • UP Sunken Garden

Hiking/Biking Routes of Cubao

One of the best ways to explore the natural beauty of Cubao is through hiking and biking. The following are some of the popular hiking and biking routes of Cubao:

  • Marikina Riverbanks to UP Diliman
  • Montalban Ridge
  • Mt. Banahaw
  • Wawa Dam

Best Things to Do in Cubao: Activities and Tours

Visitors can keep themselves engaged with several activities and tours in Cubao. The following are some of the best things to do in Cubao:

  • Attend a concert at the Araneta Coliseum
  • Explore the Cubao Expo
  • Go shopping at Gateway Mall
  • Try the street food
  • Watch a movie at the Araneta City Cineplex

Local Culture and Customs of Cubao

The local culture and customs of Cubao are a blend of Spanish, American, and Filipino influences. Visitors should respect the local customs and traditions when exploring the district.

Best Time to Visit Cubao

Cubao can be visited year-round, but the best time to visit is from December to February, as the weather is cooler and comfortable for outdoor activities.

Do’s and Don’ts of Cubao

Visitors should keep the following do’s and don’ts in mind:

Do:

  • Respect the local culture and customs
  • Keep your valuables safe
  • Try the street food

Don’t:

  • Wear revealing clothing when visiting religious places
  • Get involved in illegal activities
  • Litter the streets

Safety and Health

Cubao is generally safe for tourists, but visitors should keep the following safety and health precautions in mind.

Safety in Cubao

  • Avoid walking alone at night in deserted areas
  • Be aware of pickpockets and snatchers
  • Use reputable transportation services

Health in Cubao

  • Drink only bottled water
  • Apply sunscreen and insect repellent
  • Get vaccinated before your trip (if required)
