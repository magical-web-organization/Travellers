Introduction to Quezon Memorial Circle

Quezon Memorial Circle is a national park and a shrine located in Quezon City, Metro Manila, Philippines. It was built to honor the legacy of President Manuel Quezon, the second president of the Philippines. The park covers 27.29 hectares and is a popular destination for locals and tourists alike.

List of Places to Visit in Quezon Memorial Circle

Here are some of the must-visit places and attractions in Quezon Memorial Circle:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Quezon Memorial Shrine

The Quezon Memorial Shrine is a 66-meter tall monument that houses the remains of President Quezon and his wife. It also features galleries that showcase the life and achievements of the former president.

Quezon Heritage House

The Quezon Heritage House is a replica of the former home of President Quezon and his family. Visitors can learn more about the personal life and stories of the Quezon family while inside the house.

Presidential Car Museum

The Presidential Car Museum displays the luxury vehicles used by previous presidents of the Philippines. It includes cars owned by Ferdinand Marcos, Joseph Estrada, and Corazon Aquino.

Natural Attractions of Quezon Memorial Circle: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Circle of Fun

Circle of Fun is an amusement park located in Quezon Memorial Circle. It features fun rides and attractions for the whole family, such as Ferris wheels, water slides, and bumper cars.

Eco Park

Eco Park is a man-made forest and a bird sanctuary within Quezon Memorial Circle. It features several hiking and biking trails, a butterfly garden, and a fishing lagoon.

Circle of Joy

Circle of Joy is a recreational park that offers various outdoor activities such as ziplining, wall climbing, and paintball games.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Quezon Memorial Circle

Quezon Memorial Circle has a lot of biking and hiking trails that cater to all levels of difficulty. Visitors can explore Eco Park’s scenic Dog Walk Trail, which passes by a forest and a lagoon, and the Japanese Garden Trail, which leads to the Japanese Garden of Peace. Another popular route is the Commonwealth Nature Trail, which offers a view of the Commonwealth Avenue and leads to the UP Arboretum.

Best Things to Do in Quezon Memorial Circle: Activities and Tours

Padyak Tour

A padyak tour is a unique way to explore Quezon Memorial Circle while riding a bike. It’s a guided tour that covers several significant landmarks and attractions, such as the Quezon Memorial Shrine and the Quezon Heritage House. Tour guides share historical facts and trivia about the park and its founder.

MaMon Luk Restaurant

MaMon Luk is a must-visit restaurant in Quezon Memorial Circle. Famous for its mami noodles, siopao, and siomai, it’s an excellent place to taste some of the Philippines’ best comfort food.

Food and Drink Scene of Quezon Memorial Circle

When it comes to food and drinks, Quezon Memorial Circle offers a variety of options that cater to all taste buds. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

Mami Noodles

Mami is a type of Filipino noodle soup that has become a popular comfort food in the country. MaMon Luk in Quezon Memorial Circle serves some of the best mami noodles in the Philippines.

Halo-Halo

Halo-Halo is a popular dessert in the Philippines, especially during summer. It’s a mix of shaved ice, fruits, jellies, and evaporated milk topped with ice cream.

Local Beverages

Sago’t Gulaman

Sago’t Gulaman is a traditional Filipino beverage made with tapioca pearls and jelly served in sweet syrup water.

Coconut Water

Coconut water is a refreshing drink that’s popular in tropical countries like the Philippines. It’s a healthy alternative to sugary drinks and is known for its hydrating properties.

What to Buy in Quezon Memorial Circle: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

If you’re looking for souvenirs and local products to bring back home, here are some things to buy in Quezon Memorial Circle:

Barong Tagalog

Barong Tagalog is the national costume of the Philippines. It’s a formal shirt made from delicate fabric such as banana fiber, pineapple, or cotton.

Handwoven Basket

Handwoven baskets are a popular handicraft in the Philippines. They make for great home decor or gift items.

Local Culture and Customs of Quezon Memorial Circle

Filipinos are welcoming and hospitable people. When visiting Quezon Memorial Circle, here are some customs and etiquettes to keep in mind:

  • Always greet people with “hello” or “hi”.
  • Respect the dress code, especially when visiting religious sites and cultural events.
  • Remove your shoes before entering a Filipino home or temple.

Best Time to Visit Quezon Memorial Circle

The best time to visit Quezon Memorial Circle is during the dry season, from December to May. The weather is pleasant and warm, making it ideal for outdoor activities. Avoid visiting during the rainy season, which runs from June to November.

Do’s and Don’ts of Quezon Memorial Circle

Do:

  • Wear comfortable clothes and footwear when visiting the park.
  • Bring a camera to capture the stunning views and attractions.
  • Follow the rules and regulations set by the park authorities.

Don’t:

  • Litter or leave trash around the park.
  • Smoke in public areas.
  • Vandalize or damage any of the park’s attractions.

Safety and Health

Safety in Quezon Memorial Circle

Quezon Memorial Circle is generally safe for visitors. However, it’s always best to take precautions and avoid carrying too much cash or valuable items. Keep an eye on your belongings and stay away from dimly lit areas at night.

Health in Quezon Memorial Circle

It’s always best to drink bottled water and avoid drinking tap water in Quezon Memorial Circle. Apply insect repellent to avoid mosquito bites, especially during the rainy season.

Getting to Quezon Memorial Circle: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Quezon Memorial Circle

The Ninoy Aquino International Airport is the closest airport to Quezon Memorial Circle. It’s about 14 kilometers away from the park, and travelers can take a taxi or public transportation to get there.

Bus Stations Near Quezon Memorial Circle

There are several bus terminals near Quezon Memorial Circle, such as Victory Liner and Five Star Bus Terminal. From these terminals, visitors can take a taxi or public transportation to get to the park.

Other Options

Visitors can also take the LRT or MRT to Quezon Memorial Circle. The station stops at the Quezon Avenue Station, which is a short walk away from the park.

How to Get Around in Quezon Memorial Circle

Getting around Quezon Memorial Circle is easy and convenient. Visitors can take a padyak tour, rent a bike, or walk around the park to explore its numerous attractions.

Where to Go Next from Quezon Memorial Circle: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

There are several nearby destinations and excursions that visitors can explore after visiting Quezon Memorial Circle. Here are some of the best options:

Ninoy Aquino Parks and Wildlife Center

The Ninoy Aquino Parks and Wildlife Center is a protected area located in Quezon City. It features different flora and fauna, bird watching, and several jogging trails.

La Mesa Eco Park

La Mesa Eco Park is a nature park located in Quezon City. It features picnic areas, biking and hiking trails, and several exciting outdoor activities such as ziplining and rope courses.

Conclusion

Quezon Memorial Circle is a fascinating and exciting destination that offers something for everyone. From cultural landmarks and natural attractions to delicious food and exciting outdoor activities, it’s a must-visit site when in Metro Manila.
