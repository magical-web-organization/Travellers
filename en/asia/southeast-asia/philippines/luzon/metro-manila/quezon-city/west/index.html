Introduction to West

West is a neighborhood in Quezon City, Philippines, known for its rich culture and heritage, outdoor adventures, and delicious food. If you’re planning to explore this exciting area, this comprehensive guide will help you make the most of your trip.

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

West is home to many museums and historical sites that are worth a visit. Here are a few must-see attractions:

  • Araneta Center: This is a popular entertainment complex that offers an array of activities and shopping options.
  • Quezon Memorial Circle: This is a historic monument that commemorates the life and legacy of the former Philippine President, Manuel Quezon. The Circle is also home to several museums.
  • Ninoy Aquino Parks and Wildlife Center: This is a natural park that serves as a sanctuary for many animals and plants. The park also has several exhibits and interactive displays.
  • Art in Island: This is a unique museum that features 3D paintings that create the illusion of a three-dimensional world.
  • UP Diliman Campus: This is a well-known university campus that is home to several historic buildings and landmarks.

Natural Attractions of West: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

West is also home to several outdoor adventures and scenic views. Here are a few must-see natural attractions:

  • La Mesa Ecopark: This is a nature park that’s great for hiking, bird watching, and swimming. It also offers stunning views of the La Mesa Dam.
  • Quezon City Circle Bike Trail: This is a bike trail that runs through the Quezon City Circle. It’s a great way to explore the area and stay active.
  • Diliman Quadrangle: This is a green space that’s perfect for picnics or taking a leisurely stroll. The Quadrangle also has several historic buildings that are worth checking out.
  • National Parks and Wildlife Center: This is a wildlife park that’s home to several species of animals and plants. The park also has several walking trails and interactive exhibits.

Hiking/Biking Routes of West

If you’re looking for some outdoor adventure, West has several hiking and biking routes. Here are a few popular routes:

  • La Mesa Ecopark Trail: This trail is great for hiking and offers stunning views of the La Mesa Dam.
  • Quezon City Circle Bike Trail: This bike trail runs through the Quezon City Circle and offers a scenic route through the city.
  • UP Diliman Trail: This trail takes you through the UP Diliman Campus and offers a glimpse of many of the historic landmarks in the area.

Best Things to Do in West: Activities and Tours

Apart from exploring museums and hiking trails, there are several other things to do in West. Here are a few must-try activities:

  • Shop at the Farmers’ Market: The Farmers’ Market in West offers fresh produce, artisanal products, and local delicacies.
  • Take a Food Tour: West is known for its delicious food, and a food tour is a great way to sample the best dishes in the area.
  • Take a Walking Tour of the Area: This is a great way to explore the area and learn about its rich culture and history.

Food and Drink Scene of West

West is known for its delicious food and drinks. Here are a few must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Sisig: This is a Filipino dish made from chopped pork, typically served with rice. It’s a popular local delicacy in West.
  • Lechon Kawali: This is a popular Filipino dish made from crispy fried pork belly.
  • Adobo: This is a classic Filipino dish made from chicken or pork and is typically served with rice. It’s known for its tangy and savory flavor.

Local Beverages

  • San Miguel Beer: This is a popular Philippine beer that’s often served with local dishes.
  • Buko Juice: This is a refreshing coconut water drink that’s popular in West.

Local Culture and Customs of West

Best Time to Visit West

The best time to visit West is from December to May when the weather is dry and pleasant. Avoid visiting during the rainy season (June to November) as it can get very wet and humid.
