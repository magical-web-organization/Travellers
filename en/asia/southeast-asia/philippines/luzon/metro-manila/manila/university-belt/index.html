The Ultimate Travel Guide to University Belt, Manila

If you’re planning a trip to Manila, the University Belt is a must-visit destination. This vibrant district in the heart of the city is home to some of the country’s most prestigious universities, as well as an array of cultural attractions, outdoor adventures, and delicious food options.

In this comprehensive guide, we’ll explore the top places to visit, the best things to do, and where to eat and stay in University Belt. Read on to plan your perfect trip!

List of Places to Visit in University Belt

No trip to University Belt is complete without exploring its many cultural attractions and natural wonders. Here are some of the top places to add to your itinerary:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • National Museum of Fine Arts: This museum features an extensive collection of Philippine art, with works from the pre-colonial era to the contemporary period.

  • Museo ng Kasaysayang Panlipunan ng Pilipinas: Learn about the country’s social history and cultural heritage at this museum, which showcases Philippine society from pre-colonial times to the present.

  • San Sebastian Church: This stunning gothic church is the only steel structure of its kind in Asia.

Natural Attractions of University Belt: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Manila La Mesa Eco Park: Enjoy a day in nature at this expansive park, which features hiking trails, a butterfly garden, and a zip line.

  • Manila Bay: Take a stroll along the waterfront and watch the sunset over the bay.

Hiking/Biking Routes of University Belt

For outdoor enthusiasts, University Belt offers a variety of hiking and biking routes. Here are some of the best:

  • University of Santo Tomas Eco Park: Explore this lush green space, which features a 400-meter jogging track and plenty of picnic areas.

  • Manila Baywalk: Stroll along the bay and enjoy views of the Manila skyline.

Best Things to Do in University Belt: Activities and Tours

There are plenty of fun things to do in University Belt. Here are some of our top recommendations:

  • Cooking Classes with a Local Chef: Learn how to make authentic Filipino dishes with a local chef, who will guide you through the process and share their favorite culinary tips and tricks.

  • City Tour: Experience the best of Manila on a guided city tour, which will take you to top attractions like the Intramuros, Rizal Park, and more.

Week-Long Itinerary for University Belt

If you have a week to explore University Belt, here’s a sample itinerary to help you plan your trip:

Day Activity
Day 1 Visit the National Museum of Fine Arts and explore the surrounding area
Day 2 Go on a guided hiking or biking tour of the University of Santo Tomas Eco Park
Day 3 Take a cooking class with a local chef
Day 4 Explore the San Sebastian Church and other nearby cultural attractions
Day 5 Visit Manila Bay and enjoy a sunset cruise
Day 6 Go on a guided tour of Intramuros
Day 7 Relax in Manila La Mesa Eco Park and enjoy a picnic lunch

Food and Drink Scene of University Belt

University Belt is home to some of the best food in Manila. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Adobo: A classic Filipino dish of chicken or pork cooked with soy sauce, vinegar, garlic, and bay leaves.

  • Sinigang: A sour soup made with tamarind, meat or fish, and vegetables.

Local Beverages

  • San Miguel Beer: The most popular beer in the Philippines, with a light and refreshing taste.

  • Calamansi Juice: Made from the fruit of the calamansi tree, this juice is sour and sweet, and perfect for hot days.

Where to Stay in University Belt: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

University Belt has a wide range of accommodation options, from budget-friendly hostels to luxurious hotels. Here are some top picks:

  • RedDoorz Plus at San Marcelino: This budget-friendly hotel offers clean and comfortable rooms, as well as a convenient location.

  • Go Hotel Otis-Manila: This mid-range hotel features modern rooms with comfortable beds, as well as a rooftop swimming pool with stunning views.

  • The Bayleaf Intramuros: For a luxurious stay, look no further than The Bayleaf Intramuros. This beautiful hotel offers elegant rooms, a rooftop bar, and a restaurant serving delicious local cuisine.

What to Buy in University Belt: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

If you’re looking for souvenirs or gifts for friends and family, University Belt has plenty of options. Here are some ideas:

  • Baro’t Saya: A traditional Filipino dress that makes for a unique and beautiful souvenir.

  • Bibingka: A sweet and filling rice cake that’s great for snacking on during your travels.

Local Culture and Customs of University Belt

Filipino culture is known for its warmth, hospitality, and love of family. While in University Belt, make sure to respect local customs and traditions, such as saying “po” and “opo” (respectful forms of “yes”) when speaking to elders.

Best Time to Visit University Belt

The best time to visit University Belt is from December to February, when the weather is cool and pleasant. However, keep in mind that this is peak tourist season, so be prepared for crowds and higher prices.

Do’s and Don’ts of University Belt

When visiting University Belt, follow these do’s and don’ts:

Do:

  • Be respectful of local customs and traditions.

  • Try local dishes and beverages.

  • Take time to explore the outdoors and natural wonders.

Don’t:

  • Litter or damage the environment.

  • Wander around alone at night in unfamiliar areas.

  • Disrespect religious or cultural sites.

Safety and Health

University Belt is generally safe for visitors, but it’s still important to take precautions.

Safety in University Belt

  • Be aware of your surroundings and keep an eye on your belongings in crowded areas.

  • Avoid walking alone at night.

  • Only take licensed taxis or ride-hailing services, and negotiate the fare beforehand.

Health in University Belt

  • Drink bottled water, and avoid consuming food or beverages from street vendors.

  • Apply insect repellant to avoid mosquito bites, which may cause Zika virus or dengue fever.

Getting to University Belt: Transportation Options and Logistics

University Belt is centrally located in Metro Manila and is easily accessible by various transportation options.

Airports Near University Belt

The nearest airport to University Belt is Manila Ninoy Aquino International Airport, which is a 30-minute drive away.

Bus Stations Near University Belt

The nearest bus stations to University Belt are located at Legarda Station and Far Eastern University Station.

Other Options

Taxis and ride-hailing services like Grab are widely available in Manila and can be a convenient way to get around the city.

How to Get Around in University Belt

The University Belt is easily navigable on foot or by taxi. You can also rent a bicycle to explore the area at your own pace.

Where to Go Next from University Belt: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

If you’re looking to explore more of the Philippines, here are some nearby destinations to consider:

  • Intramuros: A walled city within Manila that dates back to the Spanish colonial era.

  • Rizal Park: A beautiful public park in central Manila that’s home to several monuments and historical buildings.

  • Luneta Park: A popular park with locals and tourists alike, it features a large lagoon and several walking paths.

Conclusion

University Belt is a vibrant and exciting destination that should be on every traveler’s itinerary when visiting Manila. With its rich cultural heritage, natural beauty, and delicious cuisine, there’s something for everyone in this lively district. Follow our guide to plan the perfect trip and experience all that University Belt has to offer!
