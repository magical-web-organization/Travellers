Introduction to Malate

Malate is one of the most popular areas in Manila, with a thriving art scene, natural beauty, and historical landmarks. The area is located in the heart of Manila, making it easy to access different attractions in the city. With so much to explore, it’s no wonder why Malate attracts both locals and tourists.

List of Places to Visit in Malate

Malate is home to a variety of must-see attractions, including culturally important attractions, natural wonders, and more. Here are just a few of the top places to visit:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Malate is home to a number of museums and historical monuments that showcase the rich history of Manila and the Philippines. Check out these popular spots:

  • National Museum of the Philippines: The National Museum of the Philippines houses an impressive collection of cultural artifacts, including prehistoric pottery, ancient goldsmithing, and modern art.
  • Museo Pambata: This interactive museum is a great spot for families with young children, offering a variety of activities and exhibits that promote learning.
  • Intramuros: This walled city is a historical landmark that dates back to the early days of Manila. Visitors can explore the city’s ancient walls, churches, museums, and shops.
  • Rizal Park: Also known as Luneta, this park is dedicated to the national hero of the Philippines, Dr. Jose Rizal. The park is an ideal spot for picnics, strolls, people-watching, and more.

Natural Attractions of Malate: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Malate also boasts a variety of natural attractions, including some of the most stunning views in Manila. Here are a few of the top outdoor destinations:

  • Manila Bay: With stunning sunset views, Manila Bay is a popular spot for romantic strolls and picnics. Visitors can also hop on a boat ride to explore the surrounding islands.
  • Paco Park: This beautiful park is a peaceful oasis in the heart of Malate. Visitors can stroll through the gardens, enjoy a picnic, or take in the charming chapel and mausoleum.
  • Taal Volcano: Just a few hours south of Manila, Taal Volcano is an impressive natural wonder that can be explored with a guided tour.
  • Mount Pinatubo: For the more adventurous travelers, hiking Mount Pinatubo is a must-do activity. This stunning volcano is located a few hours north of Manila and offers breathtaking views of the surrounding countryside.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Malate

If you’re looking for outdoor adventure, Malate has no shortage of hiking and biking trails. Here are some of the top routes:

  • Manila Baywalk: This scenic waterfront walkway is ideal for a leisurely stroll or bike ride. The path runs for several kilometers and offers stunning views of the sunset.
  • Intramuros Wall: The walls surrounding Intramuros offer a unique hiking experience. Visitors can climb the walls and explore the old city’s ancient structures, all while taking in stunning views of the surrounding area.
  • Mount Makiling: Located south of Malate, Mount Makiling offers challenging hiking routes and scenic views of the lush green landscape.

Best Things to Do in Malate: Activities and Tours

In addition to its natural and cultural wonders, Malate offers a variety of activities and tours for adventurous travelers. Here are some of the top things to do:

  • Food tours: Malate is home to some delicious local cuisine, and a food tour is the perfect way to taste your way through the city’s specialties.
  • Art galleries: Malate is a hub for Manila’s thriving art scene, with a variety of galleries featuring local and international artists.
  • Nightlife: Malate boasts a lively nightlife scene, with plenty of bars, clubs, and live music venues.
  • Spa treatments: For a relaxing day out, check out one of Malate’s many spas and treat yourself to a massage, facial, or other treatment.

Week-Long Itinerary for Malate

Day 1 Explore Intramuros, including the old city walls, Fort Santiago, and San Agustin Church.

Food and Drink Scene of Malate

Malate is home to a variety of delicious local cuisine, as well as some international favorites. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Adobo: a traditional Filipino dish of marinated meat cooked in vinegar, soy sauce, and garlic
  • Lumpia: Filipino-style spring rolls, filled with vegetables, meat, or seafood
  • Sinigang: a sour soup made with tamarind, vegetables, and meat or seafood
  • Lechon: whole roasted pig, crispy on the outside and juicy on the inside

Local Beverages

  • San Miguel Beer: a popular local beer, available in several varieties
  • Buko Juice: fresh coconut juice, often served in the shell
  • Taho: a sweet soy milk drink, often served with tapioca pearls

Where to Stay in Malate: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Malate offers a variety of accommodation options to suit every budget and need. Here are some of the top places to stay:

  • The Henry Hotel Manila: This boutique hotel offers stylish rooms and suites, complete with modern amenities and a charming courtyard.
  • Roger’s Place Hotel: This budget-friendly hotel offers clean and comfortable rooms in the heart of Malate.
  • Red Planet Manila Bay: This modern hotel offers affordable rooms with sleek decor and convenient amenities.

What to Buy in Malate: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

If you’re looking for souvenirs or gifts to bring back home, Malate has a variety of options. Here are some top picks:

  • Hand-woven textiles: Malate is home to some skilled weavers, offering a variety of unique fabrics and designs.
  • Local handicrafts: From woodcarvings to pottery, the local handicraft scene in Malate offers some beautiful, one-of-a-kind pieces.
  • Filipino delicacies: Bring back a taste of Malate with some local delicacies, such as dried mangoes, kalamansi jam, or ube-flavored sweets.

Local Culture and Customs of Malate

The Philippines has a unique culture and set of customs, and Malate is no exception. Here are a few things to keep in mind:

  • Religion is an important part of life in Malate, with a strong Catholic presence. Visitors should respect religious customs and dress modestly when visiting churches or other religious sites.
  • Filipinos are known for their warm hospitality and respect for elders. Be sure to greet people with a smile and show gratitude for their help or service.
  • English and Filipino are both widely spoken in the Philippines, making communication relatively easy for English-speaking visitors.

Best Time to Visit Malate

Malate can be visited year-round, with temperatures ranging from 20-33°C (68-91°F). The peak season runs from December to February, with milder temperatures and less rainfall. However, the summer months (March to May) offer longer daylight hours and plenty of outdoor adventures.

Do’s and Don’ts of Malate

Do:

  • Dress modestly when visiting religious sites or conservative areas
  • Be respectful of local customs and traditions
  • Try to learn a few basic phrases in Tagalog or Filipino to show appreciation for the local culture

Don’t:

  • Give money or engage with begging children, as this can perpetuate a cycle of poverty
  • Touch or step on religious objects, such as statues or offerings
  • Make negative or insensitive comments about the local culture or people

Safety and Health

Safety in Malate

Like any major urban area, Malate has its share of safety concerns. Here are a few tips for staying safe:

  • Avoid walking alone at night, especially in areas that are not well-lit or populated.
  • Keep valuables hidden and be aware of your surroundings, especially when in crowded areas or public transportation.
  • Be cautious when using ATMs or exchanging money, as these areas can be targeted by pickpockets or scammers.

Health in Malate

Visitors to Malate should be up-to-date on routine vaccinations, including measles, mumps, rubella, and flu. In addition, travelers should take precautions to prevent mosquito-borne illnesses, such as dengue fever and Zika virus. Visitors should use insect repellent, wear long sleeves and pants, and take other measures to avoid mosquito bites.

Getting to Malate: Transportation Options and Logistics

Malate is located in the heart of Manila, making it easy to access by a variety of transportation methods. Here are some options:

Airports Near Malate

  • Ninoy Aquino International Airport (MNL): This is the largest airport in the Philippines, located just a few kilometers from Malate.

Bus Stations Near Malate

  • Cubao Bus Terminal: This is a major bus terminal for long-distance trips, located about 10 kilometers from Malate.
  • Pasay Bus Terminal: This is another major bus terminal, located a few kilometers from Malate.

Other Options

  • Taxis and ride-sharing services (such as Grab) are widely available in Manila. Visitors should make sure to use only reputable services and should confirm the fare before beginning the trip.
  • Jeepneys are a form of public transportation in Manila, offering low-cost rides to different parts of the city.

How to Get Around in Malate

Malate is a walkable area, with many attractions located within a few kilometers of each other. Visitors can also use taxis, ride-sharing services, or jeepneys to get around. Additionally, there are sightseeing buses and organized tours available for exploring different parts of Manila and the surrounding areas.

Where to Go Next from Malate: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

If you’re looking to explore more of the Philippines after visiting Malate, there are plenty of nearby destinations and excursions to consider. Here are a few ideas:

  • Tagaytay: This popular destination is just a few hours south of Manila and is known for its stunning views of Taal Volcano and Lake.
  • Batangas: Located south of Manila, Batangas is home to some beautiful beaches, historic sites, and unique cultural experiences.
  • Banaue Rice Terraces: This impressive feat of agricultural engineering is a UNESCO World Heritage Site, located a few hours north of Manila.

Conclusion

Malate is a must-visit destination in Manila, offering a mix of natural beauty, cultural landmarks, and exciting activities. With this comprehensive guide, you’re sure to make the most of your trip to Malate.
