Introduction to Binondo

Welcome to Binondo, the oldest Chinatown in the world! Located in the heart of Manila, Philippines, Binondo is a cultural hub that offers a glimpse of Chinese-Filipino heritage. Walking through its narrow streets will take you on a journey through time, as you discover its historical sites, taste its local food, and immerse yourself in the traditions and customs of its people.

List of Places to Visit in Binondo

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

One of the main attractions of Binondo is its museums and historical landmarks. The Bahay Tsinoy Museum, for instance, is a great starting point for learning the Chinese-Filipino culture. Aside from that, you can visit the popular Quiapo Church, the Seng Guan Temple, the Santa Cruz Church, and the Minor Basilica of San Lorenzo Ruiz.

Natural Attractions of Binondo: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

If you’re looking for outdoor adventures, you can go to the Paco Park and Cemetery. You can also stroll through the charming Jones Bridge or visit the tranquil Rizal Park, where you can relax with a picnic or attend a cultural event.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Binondo

Binondo is a small district, so hiking and biking routes cover only its main streets. You can start from Ongpin Street, where you’ll find food stalls and shops. Then, head to Escolta Street, known for its pre-war buildings and architecture, and end your tour at Plaza San Lorenzo Ruiz.

Best Things to Do in Binondo: Activities and Tours

There are various tours and activities in Binondo you can try. Joining a food tour is highly recommended, as you’ll get to taste the best local dishes, such as lumpia (spring rolls), siomai (dumplings), and pancit (noodles). You can also join a walking tour of Binondo, where a guide will take you to the district’s cultural and historical attractions.

Week-Long Itinerary for Binondo

Day 1: Arrive in Binondo and start exploring Ongpin Street and its food stalls.

Day 2: Join a walking tour of Binondo and visit Bahay Tsinoy Museum, Seng Guan Temple, and Minor Basilica of San Lorenzo Ruiz.

Day 3: Visit Rizal Park and Paco Cemetery for some relaxation and sight-seeing.

Day 4: Rent a bike and tour the streets of Escolta, Jones Bridge, and Plaza San Lorenzo Ruiz.

Day 5: Join a food tour and try out the best local dishes of Binondo.

Day 6: Visit Quiapo Church and Santa Cruz Church for a spiritual and cultural experience.

Day 7: Shop for souvenirs and bring home local products from Binondo before you leave.

Food and Drink Scene of Binondo

Must-Try Dishes

Binondo is known for its delicious and affordable street food. Lumpia Shanghai, Siopao, Beef Mami, and Black Chicken Soup are some of the must-try dishes that you’ll find in the food stalls in Ongpin Street. You can also try the hopia (baked pastry with various fillings), mooncakes, and tikoy (Chinese rice cake) from local bakeries.

Local Beverages

Sago’t Gulaman is a popular local drink in Binondo, made of tapioca pearls and gelatin. You can also try the refreshing fresh buko (young coconut) juice and pineapple juice from street vendors.

Where to Stay in Binondo: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

There are a few accommodations in Binondo, but they are limited to hotels and hostel options. If you’re looking for budget-friendly options, you may check out The Bayleaf Hotel, Hotel Sogo, and Red Planet Manila Binondo.

What to Buy in Binondo: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Binondo offers a range of local products and souvenirs that you can bring home. You can buy Chinese tea, lucky charms, and herbs from traditional Chinese medicine stores. You can also find curated items like antiques, calligraphy, and paintings from art galleries and souvenir shops.

Local Culture and Customs of Binondo

Binondo is a cultural melting pot of Chinese and Filipino traditions. Here, you’ll find locals who still practice Taoism and Buddhism. It’s essential to respect the customs and traditions of the community, such as not touching religious statues and covering your shoulders and knees when inside temples.

Best Time to Visit Binondo

The best time to visit Binondo is from December to February. The weather is cooler, and the Chinese New Year festivities take place during January or February.

Do’s and Don’ts of Binondo

Do:

  • Try the street food and local delicacies.
  • Respect the customs and traditions of the locals.
  • Join a walking tour or bike tour to explore the district.

Don’t:

  • Touch the religious statues in temples.
  • Wear shorts and sleeveless tops when visiting temples.
  • Overlook the signage and noise laws in the district.

Safety and Health

Safety in Binondo

Binondo is generally safe during the day, but it’s advisable to be cautious at night. Avoid carrying large amounts of money or valuables and pay attention to your surroundings.

Health in Binondo

Binondo has a few hospitals and clinics where you can seek medical attention if needed. Make sure to have travel insurance and medication if necessary.

Getting to Binondo: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Binondo

  • Ninoy Aquino International Airport
  • Clark International Airport

Bus Stations Near Binondo

  • Pasay Bus Station
  • Cubao Bus Station

Other Options

You can also take taxis or ride-hailing services to get to Binondo. If you’re already in Manila, you can take the LRT-1 and get off at Carriedo Station or Doroteo Jose Station.

How to Get Around in Binondo

The best way to explore Binondo is on foot or by bike. It’s a small district, and the streets can get crowded during peak hours. You can easily rent a bike from some of the hotels in the area.

Where to Go Next from Binondo: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Binondo is in the heart of Manila, so it’s easy to explore other nearby destinations like Intramuros, Pasay, and Makati. You can also visit other tourist spots in the Philippines, such as Tagaytay, Boracay, and Palawan.

Conclusion

Binondo is a fascinating destination for those who want to explore the Chinese-Filipino culture and heritage. It’s a mix of old and new, with traditional practices and modern developments. We hope that this comprehensive guide will help you explore the best of what Binondo has to offer. Remember to respect the locals, try the food, and immerse yourself in the culture. Safe travels!
