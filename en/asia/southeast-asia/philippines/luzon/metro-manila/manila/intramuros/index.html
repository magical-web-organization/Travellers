Introduction to Intramuros

Intramuros, which means “within the walls” in Latin, is a cultural heritage site located in Manila, Philippines. The site has a rich history that dates back to the Spanish colonization in the 16th century. The walled city, which was built as a fortress to protect the Spanish colonial government in the Philippines, is now a popular tourist destination that combines historical landmarks, cultural sites, and natural attractions.

List of Places to Visit in Intramuros

Intramuros is a magical place that offers a combination of historical landmarks, cultural sites and natural attractions:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Intramuros is home to numerous museums and historic landmarks that will transport you back in time. Here are some of the must-visit sites in the walled city:

  • San Agustin Church
  • The Manila Cathedral
  • Casa Manila Museum
  • The National Museum of the Philippines
  • Fort Santiago
  • Baluarte de San Diego
  • The Rizal Shrine

Natural Attractions of Intramuros: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Intramuros is not just a cultural and historical hub, it is also a place of natural beauty and outdoor adventure. Here are some of the must-visit natural attractions of the walled city:

  • The Pasig River Ferry
  • The bamboo bike tour of Intramuros
  • The Paco Park and Cemetery
  • The Manila Bay Sunset Cruise

Hiking/Biking Routes of Intramuros

Intramuros is a perfect place for hiking and biking enthusiasts. The city offers various routes that you can take and enjoy the scenic views that the city has to offer. Some of the popular trails include:

  • Paco Park and Cemetery Loop
  • Parola Loop
  • Intramuros Walls Loop

Local Culture and Customs of Intramuros

Intramuros has a unique culture and customs that are worth exploring. The locals are known for their friendly and hospitable nature.

Best Time to Visit Intramuros

The best time to visit Intramuros is between November and February when the climate is cooler and drier. However, the city is accessible to tourists all year round.

Do’s and Don’ts of Intramuros

Here are some of the do’s and don’ts to keep in mind when exploring Intramuros:

Do:

  • Dress appropriately when visiting churches and other religious sites
  • Learn some basic Tagalog phrases and greet locals in their language
  • Respect the locals and their customs

Don’t:

  • Wander off alone in the dark alleys of Intramuros
  • Litter or damage any historical or cultural sites

Safety and Health

Intramuros is generally a safe place to visit. However, it is always advisable to take precautions and ensure your safety.

Safety in Intramuros

  • Avoid carrying large amounts of cash and valuables
  • Be cautious when using ATMs, particularly at night

Health in Intramuros

  • It is advisable to get vaccinated for typhoid and hepatitis A before travelling to the Philippines
