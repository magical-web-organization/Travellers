Introduction to Tondo and San Nicolas

Located in Manila, in Metro Manila, in Luzon, in Philippines, in Southeast Asia, and in Asia, Tondo and San Nicolas are two cities that have a rich history, vibrant culture, and a lot of attractions to offer for tourists. Tondo is known for its cultural landmarks and historical places, while San Nicolas is famous for its natural attractions that offer breathtaking scenic views and outdoor adventures.

This comprehensive tour guide will provide you with information on the best places to visit in Tondo and San Nicolas, the food and drink scene, where to stay, and local customs and culture. You will also learn how to travel safely and efficiently around the areas and how to visit nearby destinations and excursions.

List of Places to Visit in Tondo and San Nicolas

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Casa Manila

Located in Intramuros, Casa Manila is a recreation of a 19th-century aristocratic house. The interior design and furniture give an accurate depiction of the lifestyle of the wealthy families during the Spanish era.

San Agustin Church and Museum

San Agustin Church and Museum is a UNESCO World Heritage site and one of the oldest stone churches in the Philippines. Its Baroque design and architecture are well-preserved and the museum features religious artifacts and artworks.

Fort Santiago

Fort Santiago is a famous historical fortification built by the Spanish as a defense against invaders. The museum inside showcases the history of the fort and its previous occupants.

Natural Attractions of Tondo and San Nicolas: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Pasig River

The Pasig River runs through Metro Manila, and it is a great location for scenic river cruises to admire the city skyline or experience a sunset.

Manila Baywalk

Manila Baywalk is a seaside promenade that provides stunning views of Manila Bay and the city skyline.

La Mesa Ecopark

Located in Quezon City, La Mesa Ecopark is an ideal destination for nature lovers. It features a vast area of trees, nature trails, butterfly sanctuary, fishing area, and picnic areas in a quiet and peaceful setting.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Tondo and San Nicolas

Freedom Island

Freedom Island is a haven for bird watchers and eco-tourists. Biking and hiking are popular activities, where you can enjoy the beautiful scenery and the wildlife.

Manila North Green Park

Manila North Green Park features an 11-kilometer bike lane that connects the Quezon City Memorial Circle to the Ninoy Aquino Parks and Wildlife Center. Biking is done around the tree-lined areas and gardens.

Best Things to Do in Tondo and San Nicolas: Activities and Tours

Intramuros Golf Course

Intramuros Golf Course is the perfect attraction for golf enthusiasts. It is situated in the heart of Intramuros and offers a 9-hole golf course with a spectacular view of the city skyline.

Manila Ocean Park

Manila Ocean Park is one of the largest ocean parks in Asia and has an extensive collection of marine and aquatic life. Visitors enjoy the aquariums, sea lion and dolphin shows, and other marine-based activities.

Week-Long Itinerary for Tondo and San Nicolas

This itinerary is designed for visitors with one week to explore Tondo and San Nicolas.

  • Day 1: Casa Manila, San Agustin Church and Museum, and Fort Santiago
  • Day 2: Pasig River cruise, Intramuros Golf Course, and Manila Baywalk
  • Day 3: La Mesa Ecopark, Quezon City Circle, and Butterfly Sanctuary
  • Day 4: Freedom Island for bird watching and hiking/biking
  • Day 5: Manila Ocean Park, Manila Cathedral, and National Museum of Fine Arts
  • Day 6: Robinson’s Magnolia, Greenhills Shopping, and Wack Wack Golf and Country Club
  • Day 7: Rizal Park, Luneta, and Manila Zoo

Food and Drink Scene of Tondo and San Nicolas

Must-Try Dishes

Adobo

Adobo is a famous Filipino dish made of chicken, pork, or a combination of both, cooked in soy sauce, vinegar, garlic, bay leaf, and peppercorn. It is usually served with rice.

Sisig

Sisig is a popular dish made of chopped pig’s head and liver, seasoned with calamansi, onions, and chili peppers. It is usually served as a pulutan (appetizer) and accompanied by beer.

Local Beverages

San Miguel beer

San Miguel beer is the most famous beer in the Philippines. It is a light and refreshing beer that is perfect to pair with grilled dishes.

Tanduay Rum

Tanduay is the most popular rum in the Philippines. It has a complex and rich taste and is perfect for cocktails.

Where to Stay in Tondo and San Nicolas: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

The Bayleaf

The Bayleaf is a deluxe hotel located in Intramuros. It features elegant rooms and suites and offers spectacular views of Manila Bay and Intramuros.

Manila Grand Opera Hotel

Manila Grand Opera Hotel is a budget-friendly hotel located in Santa Cruz, Manila. It features rooms with a classic design and has a casino, restaurant, and function rooms.

What to Buy in Tondo and San Nicolas: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Barong Tagalog

Barong Tagalog is a traditional embroidered shirt that is usually worn during formal events. It is made out of silk or pineapple fibers and comes in different colors and sizes.

Jeepney souvenir

Jeepneys are the most popular mode of public transportation in the Philippines. You can find miniature jeepneys in souvenir shops as a great reminder of your trip to Tondo and San Nicolas.

Local Culture and Customs of Tondo and San Nicolas

Filipino hospitality is a big part of their culture. When visiting Tondo and San Nicolas, it is customary to greet the locals with a smile and a polite “hello.” Respect to elders and authority is also highly valued.

Best Time to Visit Tondo and San Nicolas

The best time to visit Tondo and San Nicolas is from December to May. The weather is dry and sunny, making it ideal for outdoor activities and sightseeing.

Do’s and Don’ts of Tondo and San Nicolas

Do:

  • Wear comfortable clothes and footwear
  • Learn basic Tagalog phrases
  • Take a lot of photos

Don’t:

  • Do not wear revealing clothing or shorts when visiting religious sites
  • Do not litter
  • Do not disrespect local customs and traditions

Safety and Health

Safety in Tondo and San Nicolas

When traveling to Tondo and San Nicolas, it is important to be vigilant of your surroundings. Avoid visiting unsafe areas at night and always keep your belongings secure.

Health in Tondo and San Nicolas

Make sure to have routine vaccines up to date before traveling to the Philippines. Bring sunscreen, insect repellent, and hydration in the hot and humid climate.

Getting to Tondo and San Nicolas: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Tondo and San Nicolas

The Manila Ninoy Aquino International Airport is the primary airport in the Philippines and is located in Pasay City. It is the most accessible airport when traveling to Tondo and San Nicolas.

Bus Stations Near Tondo and San Nicolas

Travelling by bus is an affordable and convenient way to reach Tondo and San Nicolas. The major bus stations in Manila are in Cubao and Alabang.

Other Options

Taxi, Grab, and Uber are also available within the city.

How to Get Around in Tondo and San Nicolas

The easiest mode of transportation in Tondo and San Nicolas is by jeepneys. They are cheap and can get you to any destination within the cities.

Where to Go Next from Tondo and San Nicolas: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

You can travel to nearby destinations such as Binondo, Chinatown, and Divisoria for a unique shopping experience. A popular destination is also Tagaytay, known for its cool weather, scenic views, and Taal Volcano.

Conclusion

Exploring Tondo and San Nicolas will immerse you in Filipino culture and tradition. With historical landmarks, outdoor adventures, traditional cuisine, beautiful scenery, and friendly locals, you will experience an unforgettable trip to this part of the Philippines.
