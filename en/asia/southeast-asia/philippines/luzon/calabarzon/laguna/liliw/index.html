Introduction to Liliw

Liliw is a third-class municipality located in the province of Laguna, Calabarzon in the Philippines. It is known for its thriving shoemaking industry, beautiful natural attractions, rich cultural heritage, and traditional local crafts like embroidery and wood-carving. Despite being a small town, there is an abundance of things to see and do in Liliw that will delight any traveler, whether you’re looking for outdoor adventures or cultural immersion.

List of Places to Visit in Liliw

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Liliw Museum - Located in the heart of town, Liliw Museum is a must-visit attraction for anyone interested in Philippine history and culture. The museum features an extensive collection of artifacts and displays, showcasing the town’s rich heritage and traditions.

St. John the Baptist Church - Constructed in the 1600s, St. John the Baptist Church is one of the oldest and most beautiful churches in the area. The church features a unique baroque-style façade, intricate carvings, and beautiful stained glass windows.

Natural Attractions of Liliw: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Mt. Banahaw - This active volcano is the highest peak in the province of Laguna, offering spectacular views of the surrounding landscape. Visitors can hike to the top of the mountain, which takes approximately 8 hours, and explore the lush green forests and waterfalls along the way.

Taytay Falls - A popular destination for nature enthusiasts, Taytay Falls is a stunning waterfall located in a lush and verdant forest. Visitors can swim in the refreshing and crystal-clear waters, relax on the rocks, or take a refreshing shower under the cascading waters.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Liliw

Mt. Mabilog Trail - This trail is perfect for beginner hikers or those looking for an easy and leisurely hike. The trail takes about two hours to complete and offers scenic views of the surrounding landscape.

Bisal Trail - For more experienced hikers, the Bisal Trail is a challenging but rewarding hike. The trail takes approximately 6 hours to complete and offers breathtaking views of the rolling hills and rugged terrain of the area.

Best Things to Do in Liliw: Activities and Tours

Shoemaking Tour - Liliw is renowned for its shoemaking industry, and visitors can go on a shoemaking tour to see the craftsmen at work. You will learn about the process of making shoes and have the opportunity to purchase a pair of handmade shoes.

Heritage Tour - For those interested in history and culture, a heritage tour of Liliw is a must-do activity. The tour takes you to historical sites, museums and churches, and gives you an insight into the rich heritage and traditions of the town.

Food and Drink Scene of Liliw

Must-Try Dishes

Burong Mustasa - This traditional dish is made from fermented mustard leaves and is a delicious and healthy delicacy that is often served as a side dish.

Pancit Habhab - This popular noodle dish originated in Liliw and is one of the town’s most famous foods. The dish is made with stir-fried noodles, meat and vegetables, and is served on a banana leaf.

Local Beverages

Arroz Caldo - This hearty rice porridge is a popular breakfast food and is made with chicken, ginger, and garlic. It is filling and delicious, perfect for a cold Liliw morning.

Kapeng Barako - This strong and flavorful coffee is a specialty of the region and is made from locally grown beans. It’s the perfect pick-me-up after a long hike or a busy day of exploring.

Where to Stay in Liliw: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Sulo Riviera Hotel - A charming and affordable option located in the town center, The Sulo Riviera Hotel offers comfortable accommodation with all the basic amenities for a pleasant stay.

Liliw Resort and Conference Center - A luxurious option on the outskirts of town, the Liliw Resort and Conference Center offers spacious and well-appointed rooms, onsite dining, and recreational facilities.

What to Buy in Liliw: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Shoes - Liliw is famous for its shoe industry, and you can find a wide range of handmade shoes in the town’s marketplaces. From sandals to boots, there is something for everyone.

Embroidery and Wood-Carved Products - Liliw is also known for its traditional crafts like embroidery and woodcarving. You can find a variety of handcrafted products like bags, table runners, and wooden furniture in the town’s marketplaces.

Local Culture and Customs of Liliw

Liliw is a predominantly Catholic town, and the residents are known for their warm hospitality and friendliness towards visitors. The town’s festivals and fiestas are lively and colorful affairs, with a strong focus on traditions and rituals.

Best Time to Visit Liliw

The best time to visit Liliw is during the dry season, from November to May, when the weather is warm and sunny. However, be aware that the town can get crowded during peak tourist seasons such as Christmas and Holy Week.

Do’s and Don’ts of Liliw

Do:

  • Respect local traditions and customs
  • Try the local cuisine, especially the famous Liliw pancit habhab
  • Support the local shoemaking industry by purchasing a pair of handmade shoes
  • Take your trash with you when visiting natural attractions

Don’t:

  • Wear revealing clothing when visiting churches and other religious sites
  • Litter or damage natural attractions and cultural sites
  • Engage in any illegal or disrespectful activities

Safety and Health

Safety in Liliw

Liliw is a relatively safe town, but it’s always a good idea to take precautions when traveling. Be aware of your surroundings, avoid carrying valuables and always keep an eye on your belongings. Ensure your accommodation has safety measures in place like CCTV, security patrolling, and 24-hour front desk services.

Health in Liliw

Tap water in Liliw is not safe to drink, so always opt for bottled water. It’s also important to take precautions against mosquito-borne diseases like dengue fever by applying mosquito repellent and wearing long sleeves and pants.

Getting to Liliw: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Liliw

The nearest airport to Liliw is Ninoy Aquino International Airport in Manila, which is approximately 2-3 hours away by land travel.

Bus Stations Near Liliw

Buses from Metro Manila to Liliw are available, with terminals located at Buendia-Taft in Pasay City, Alabang, and Cubao. Travel time is approximately 2-3 hours, depending on the traffic and route taken.

Other Options

Private car rentals and taxis can also be arranged.

How to Get Around in Liliw

Walking is a convenient way to explore the town, especially if you’re staying in the town center. Jeepneys and tricycles are also available for transportation to the outskirts of town and nearby attractions.

Where to Go Next from Liliw: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Nearby destinations that can easily be explored from Liliw include Nagcarlan Underground Cemetery, Pagsanjan Falls, and Hulugan Falls. These natural wonders offer an exciting day trip for those looking for scenic views and outdoor adventures.

Conclusion

In conclusion, Liliw is a charming and delightful town that offers visitors a unique glimpse into Philippine heritage and traditions. With its beautiful natural attractions, rich cultural heritage, and delicious local cuisine, Liliw is a must-visit destination for any traveler exploring the Calabarzon region in the Philippines.
