Welcome to Santa Rosa, a vibrant city nestled in the southern part of Laguna province, just about an hour’s drive from Metro Manila. With its cultural significance, natural attractions, and diverse culture, Santa Rosa has become a popular destination among local and foreign tourists alike. In this comprehensive guide, we will introduce you to the best things to see and do, where to stay, what to eat, and everything in between to make your Santa Rosa experience unforgettable.

Introduction to Santa Rosa

Santa Rosa is a thriving city that boasts a rich cultural heritage and a strong economy. It is home to several major industrial parks and is a major contributor to the country’s economy. The city is a melting pot of different cultures, traditions, and beliefs. You will find a blend of modernity and tradition in Santa Rosa, making it an exciting and unique place to explore.

List of Places to Visit in Santa Rosa

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums, and Historical Places

For history buffs and museum-goers, Santa Rosa offers a rich tapestry of cultural attractions, including:

  1. Museo de Santa Rosa - offers a glimpse of the city’s history through its collection of artifacts and exhibits.
  2. Santa Rosa Catholic Church - a beautiful church that dates back to the Spanish colonial period.
  3. Sta. Rosa de Lima Park - a public park that features the bronze statue of St. Rose, the city’s patron saint.
  4. Sulyap Gallery Cafe and Restaurant - housed in a historic ancestral house and features the culture and traditions of Santa Rosa through art and cuisine.

Natural Attractions of Santa Rosa: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Santa Rosa boasts several natural attractions that offer outdoor enthusiasts a range of exciting outdoor activities. Here are some of the top scenic views and outdoor adventures in Santa Rosa:

  1. Nuvali Eco Park - a world-class eco-resort that features a man-made lake, bike trails, camping grounds, and other outdoor activities.
  2. Mount Makiling - a dormant volcano that offers several hiking trails, rivers, waterfalls, and its summit which offers a breathtaking view of the surrounding area.
  3. Paseo de Santa Rosa - a lifestyle destination that offers a mix of shopping, dining, and outdoor activities set in a garden-like environment.
  4. Greenfield City - another eco-friendly leisure park, featuring gardens, sports fields, biking trails, and more.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Santa Rosa

Santa Rosa is a treasure trove of hiking and biking routes. Here are some of them:

  1. The trails of Mount Makiling: Mount Makiling offers several hiking trails ranging from easy to difficult. The perfect biking route is the “Makiling Challenge,” a 60-kilometer mountain bike ride that offers stunning views of Mount Makiling and the surrounding areas.
  2. Nuvali to Tagaytay via Talisay: Nuvali to Tagaytay via Talisay is a mix of off-road and on-road cycling. This biking route offers panoramic views of the Taal Lake and the Tagaytay Ridge.
  3. UPLB (University of the Philippines Los Baños) to Mt. Makiling: This hiking route is perfect for beginners and offers a relaxing hike through lush forest trails to the UPLB College of Forestry and Natural Resources.

Best Things to Do in Santa Rosa: Activities and Tours

Santa Rosa offers a range of activities and tours for travelers of all ages and interests. Here are some must-try activities while in Santa Rosa:

  1. Water sports at Nuvali - Nuvali offers a range of water activities, including kayaking, fishing, and wakeboarding.
  2. Greenfield Weekend Market - an outdoor weekend market that features local crafts, food, and live entertainment.
  3. Urban garden hopping - Santa Rosa is home to several community-run urban gardens, where you can enjoy fresh produce and aromatic herbs.
  4. Gourmet food tours - Take a tour and sample the local delicacies at the Sulyap Gallery Cafe and Restaurant.

Week-Long Itinerary for Santa Rosa

Here’s a week-long itinerary of things to see and do in Santa Rosa:

Day 1: Visit Museo de Santa Rosa and Sta. Rosa de Lima Park. Day 2: Go shopping and dine at Paseo de Santa Rosa. Day 3: Enjoy the outdoor activities at Nuvali. Day 4: Go hiking or biking on Mount Makiling. Day 5: Visit the historic ancestral house and enjoy local cuisine at Sulyap Gallery Cafe and Restaurant. Day 6: Take a gourmet food tour around Santa Rosa. Day 7: Relax at any of the city’s parks or community gardens.

Food and Drink Scene of Santa Rosa

Must-Try Dishes

Santa Rosa’s food scene is a mix of traditional Filipino dishes and international cuisine. You can’t leave the city without trying these famous dishes:

  1. Sinigang - a savory soup made of tamarind, pork, or seafood.
  2. Adobo - the most-loved dish in the Philippines, made from chicken, pork, or beef cooked in vinegar, soy sauce, and other ingredients.
  3. Longganisa - delicious sweet or savory sausages, often eaten for breakfast.
  4. Buko pie - a famous Filipino dessert made of young coconut meat and milk.

Local Beverages

The Philippines is known for its love of beer, but there are also a few local drinks worth trying in Santa Rosa:

  1. Barako Coffee - strong coffee made from Liberica beans grown in Batangas province, near Santa Rosa.
  2. Lambanog - a potent coconut wine that is perfect for a night out with friends.
  3. Samalamig (Sweet Coolers) - refreshing sweet drinks that come in different flavors made from a variety of fruits and ingredients.

Where to Stay in Santa Rosa: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Santa Rosa offers a range of accommodation options that cater to different budgets and amenities. Here are some top options:

  1. El Molino Beach Resort - a beachside resort located in Balibago, Santa Rosa.
  2. Technopark Hotel - conveniently located near the industrial parks.
  3. Seda Nuvali - a deluxe hotel located inside the Nuvali eco-park.
  4. Bamboo Grove - a budget-friendly guesthouse located near the city’s commercial area.

What to Buy in Santa Rosa: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Here are some of the best souvenirs and local products you can bring home from Santa Rosa:

  1. Handwoven Rattan Baskets - made from local materials and crafted by Santa Rosa’s indigenous peoples.
  2. Laguna Cheese - delicious artisanal cheese made from carabao’s milk, a water buffalo endemic to the Philippines.
  3. Buko Pie - Santa Rosa’s famous dessert that can last for several days without refrigeration.
  4. Barako Coffee - authentic coffee from Batangas province, near Santa Rosa.

Local Culture and Customs of Santa Rosa

The people of Santa Rosa are known for their lively community events and vibrant festivals. Here are the most important cultural traditions and customs that you should know:

  1. Santacruzan - a religious procession that re-enacts the search of the Holy Cross by Queen Helena (Saint Helena), the mother of Constantine the Great.
  2. Sinukwan Festival - a cultural event showcasing the ethnic tradition of the Aetas and their importance to the cultural heritage of Santa Rosa.
  3. Karakol - a religious procession that culminates at the Santa Rosa de Lima Park.

Best Time to Visit Santa Rosa

Santa Rosa’s climate is generally hot and humid, with average temperatures ranging from 28°C to 33°C. The best time to visit Santa Rosa is during the months of November to April when the weather is more comfortable, and the air is cooler.

Do’s and Don’ts of Santa Rosa

Do:

  1. Respect the local customs and traditions.
  2. Try the local cuisine and explore the flea markets.
  3. Be aware of your surroundings, especially at night.

Don’t:

  1. Litter or disrespect the environment.
  2. Ignore safety precautions when biking or hiking.
  3. Get involved in illegal activities.

Safety and Health

Safety in Santa Rosa

Santa Rosa is a relatively safe city to visit, but like any tourist destination, you need to take extra precautions to ensure your safety:

  1. Always be aware of your surroundings and avoid venturing on your own at night or in remote areas.
  2. Keep your belongings close to you and avoid flashing expensive items in public.
  3. Be vigilant when using public transportation.

Health in Santa Rosa

The Philippines is known for several endemic diseases such as dengue fever, typhoid fever, and malaria. Here are some precautions you need to take when visiting Santa Rosa:

  1. Get vaccinated before traveling to the Philippines.
  2. Use mosquito repellent and wear protective clothing, especially during the rainy season.
  3. Always have a first-aid kit with you.

Getting to Santa Rosa: Transportation Options and Logistics

Santa Rosa is accessible by different modes of transportation from Manila and nearby cities.

Airports Near Santa Rosa

The nearest international airport to Santa Rosa is Ninoy Aquino International Airport (MNL) in Manila. Domestic airports, such as Clark International Airport, are also nearby.

Bus Stations Near Santa Rosa

Several bus companies, such as Green Star Express and Jam Liner, operate bus routes from different parts of Luzon to Santa Rosa.

Other Options

Car rentals and private transfers are also available for visitors who prefer to travel on their own. Ride-sharing apps like Grab are also accessible in the area.

How to Get Around in Santa Rosa

Santa Rosa is a relatively small city, and you can navigate it easily on foot or by hiring a tricycle (a small motorcab).

Where to Go Next From Santa Rosa: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Santa Rosa is an ideal starting point for exploring the many sites and attractions that the Calabarzon region has to offer. Here are some nearby destinations and excursions:

  1. Tagaytay City - famous for its stunning views of Taal Lake and the Taal Volcano.
  2. Pagsanjan Falls - a popular tourist spot with a scenic waterfall and a thrilling river current ride.
  3. Calamba City - birthplace of Jose Rizal, the Philippines’ national hero.

Conclusion

We hope this comprehensive guide to Santa Rosa has been helpful in planning your trip. Santa Rosa offers a wealth of cultural, natural, and culinary delights, making it a perfect getaway for those who seek to explore the Philippines beyond its capital city of Manila. With its stunning natural landscapes, vibrant community events, and rich history, Santa Rosa has something for everyone.
