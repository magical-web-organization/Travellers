Introduction to Cabuyao

Cabuyao is a delightful city in Laguna, Philippines, which is part of the Calabarzon region in Luzon. It is approximately 40 kilometers south of Manila. Cabuyao is made up of 18 barangays and is known for its bustling economy, vibrant culture, and friendly people. The city’s proximity to several bodies of water, including Laguna de Bay, makes it an excellent choice for outdoor enthusiasts. Whether you want to explore culturally significant attractions, check out natural wonders, hike, bike, or dive into the local food scene, Cabuyao has something to offer to every traveler.

List of Places to Visit in Cabuyao

When it comes to things to do in Cabuyao, visitors are in for a treat. Here are some of the top places to visit:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • St. Polycarp Church: This historic church is over two centuries old and has a rich history dating back to the Spanish colonial period. The church’s stunning architecture and vibrant interiors make it a must-see for any visitor to Cabuyao.

  • Cabuyao Museum: This museum is a treasure trove of the city’s cultural and historical artifacts. Visitors can explore various exhibits, including indigenous and colonial-era antiques, memorabilia, and art pieces.

  • Bucal Sinagtala Cave and Spring Resort: This natural attraction features a breathtaking limestone cave and a picturesque, spring-fed pool, making it an excellent spot for water activities and a fascinating piece of geology.

Natural Attractions of Cabuyao: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Makiling Botanic Gardens: This garden’s sprawling grounds offer a serene escape from the city’s hustle and bustle. Visitors can go on nature walks, birdwatching, and picnic on designated areas around the garden.

  • Ilog Maria Honeybee Farm: Set in a 10-hectare farm, Ilog Maria Honeybee Farm is a unique experience for nature lovers and foodies. Here you can learn about the honey-making process from the farm’s owners and sample some of their delicious honey-based products.

  • Nuvali: This eco-city destination is the perfect spot for outdoor enthusiasts. With activities like kayaking, mountain biking, and a nature walk, visitors can appreciate the landscape’s natural beauty.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Cabuyao

Cabuyao has several exceptional hiking and biking routes:

  • Mount Makiling: This mountain is a popular hiking spot in Cabuyao, and it’s said to have legendary enchanted tales. The trail offers an extensive view of the surrounding city and luscious vegetation, making it an excellent spot for nature buffs and adventurers.

  • Nuvali Wildlife and Bird Sanctuary: The sanctuary is known for its trail for birdwatchers, hikers, and pet owners. It is the home of various bird species, such as the egrets, nocturnals, and other endemic fauna.

Best Things to Do in Cabuyao: Activities and Tours

Cabuyao has a lot to offer its visitors with various activities, including:

  • Food tour: Experience the joy of eating and drinking in Cabuyao with its traditional dishes and cool off with drinks from local vendors and shops.

  • City tour: Exploring the city’s attractions, shopping malls, cultural sites, and other tourist spots within the province

Suppose you’re spending a week in Cabuyao as a traveler. Here’s an itinerary to help you make the most of your time:

Food and Drink Scene of Cabuyao

Cabuyao has a vibrant food scene with many excellent dining options. Whether you are on a budget or want to try gourmet food, here are some of the best places to dine in Cabuyao:

Must-Try Dishes

  1. Adobo: This dish is the national dish of the Philippines and is made with meat (chicken or pork), vinegar, soy sauce, garlic, and black peppercorns.

  2. Kakanin: This dessert is made from a variety of glutinous rice flours, coconut milk, and sugar. They come in different colors, shapes, and sizes like bibingka, sapin-sapin, puto, and others.

  3. Lechon: This popular dish is made with a whole roasted pig, which is crispy on the outside and tender on the inside.

Local Beverages

  1. Sago’t Gulaman: A sweet and refreshing drink made of pearls (sago) flavored with caramel, syrup, lemon, gelatin (gulaman), and ice cubes.

  2. Lambanog: An alcoholic beverage made from coconuts primarily found in the Philippines.

Where to Stay in Cabuyao: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Cabuyao has a good range of accommodations, suitable for various needs and budgets. Here’s an overview of the best accommodation options:

  • Green Glass Boutique Hotel: An eco-friendly hotel located in the heart of Cabuyao, offering modern and spacious rooms and accommodates families and business travelers.

  • Sanctuario de San Pablo Resort and Spa: A resort situated in the nearby city of San Pablo, Laguna, which offers luxury and relaxation, set in its landscaped gardens, and a serene sanctuary.

What to Buy in Cabuyao: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Cabuyao is known for its quality products, including tanning, transport, and food service and sales outsourcing. Here are some of the best things to buy as souvenirs:

  1. Gin Melon: A locally produced alcohol made from melon, gin, and various spices.

  2. Barong Tagalog: A formal shirt made of native pineapple fibers, cotton, or silk that represents Philippine culture.

Local Culture and Customs of Cabuyao

The culture of Cabuyao is a mix of indigenous Filipino and Spanish colonial influences. The people of Cabuyao are welcoming, gracious, and hospitable, with a strong emphasis on family and community values. The city also celebrates several festivals, including Lubid Festival, Arana’t Baluarte Festival, and the Feast of St. Polycarp.

Best Time to Visit Cabuyao

Cabuyao has a tropical climate, and the country is known for its rainy season, which falls between June to November. The best time to visit Cabuyao is during the dry season, between December to May, as many outdoor activities take place during this season.

Do’s and Don’ts of Cabuyao

Do:

  1. Respect the local customs and traditions.

  2. Carry a water bottle at all times due to the warm weather.

  3. Dress appropriately when visiting religious and cultural sites.

Don’t:

  1. Overeat or overdrink during social gatherings.

  2. Display any public displays of affection.

  3. Waste food or water.

Safety and Health

Safety in Cabuyao

Cabuyao is generally a safe city. However, travelers must take the necessary precautions to avoid getting into any dangerous situations, especially at night.

Health in Cabuyao

Cabuyao has several hospitals and medical facilities that offer medical attention to tourists should they need it. The city’s hygiene standards are high, so visitors should avoid street food to prevent foodborne illnesses and be proactive with mosquito control measures.

Getting to Cabuyao: Transportation Options and Logistics

Cabuyao is easily accessible through various means of transportation, such as:

Airports Near Cabuyao

  1. Manila Ninoy Aquino International Airport: The airport is the primary gateway to the Philippines and is approximately 30 kilometers or an hour’s drive to Cabuyao.

  2. Clark International Airport: Located in Pampanga, the airport is approximately two hours away from Cabuyao.

Bus Stations Near Cabuyao

Several bus companies offer daily trips to Cabuyao from other parts of Luzon. These include:

  1. JAM Liner: JAM Liner has daily trips from Manila to Cabuyao.

  2. Green Star Express: Green Star Express has daily trips from Cubao to Cabuyao.

Other Options

Travelers can also rent a car or hire a private van to take them to Cabuyao if they want a more comfortable and customized option.

How to Get Around Cabuyao

Cabuyao’s transportation system comprises jeeps, tricycles, and taxis. Jeepneys are the most popular mode of transportation in the city, offering passengers an economical way of getting around town. Tricycles and taxis are the best options if you want to go to neighboring barangays or towns.

Where to Go Next from Cabuyao: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Cabuyao is an excellent base for day trips to some of Luzon’s famous destinations, such as Tagaytay, Batangas, and Pagsanjan.

Conclusion

In summary, Cabuyao is an excellent tourist destination in the Philippines, with many attractions, natural beauty, and cultural experiences to offer. Whether you’re interested in outdoor adventures, food tours, or exploring museums and historical landmarks, the city has something for everyone. So, pack your bags and discover the beauty of Cabuyao, a hidden gem in the Philippines!
