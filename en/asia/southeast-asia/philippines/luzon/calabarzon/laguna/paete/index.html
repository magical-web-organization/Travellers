Introduction to Paete

Nestled in the foothills of the Sierra Madre Mountains, Paete is a charming town known for its rich culture, history, and art. The town is located in Laguna, Philippines, just 95 km from Manila, making it a popular destination for a day trip or weekend getaway.

Paete is also known as the “Carving Capital of the Philippines” and is home to many talented artisans who create exquisite woodcarvings, sculptures, and other works of art. Though relatively small in size, Paete has a lot to offer in terms of culture, history, and outdoor adventures.

List of Places to Visit in Paete

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Paete Church - Also known as the St. James the Apostle Parish Church, this impressive Baroque-style church was built in 1646 and is considered as one of the oldest churches in the Philippines. It is home to beautiful murals and paintings and is a significant religious and cultural landmark in Paete.
  • Paete Municipal Museum - This museum showcases the town’s history, culture, and art through various displays of antique artifacts, paintings, and other objects.
  • Balaw Balaw Specialty Restaurant and Art Gallery - This unique restaurant serves traditional Filipino dishes with a twist and is also an art gallery that showcases the works of local artists.
  • Tapikan River - This river is not only a source of livelihood for the locals, but also an important cultural icon. It is where locals practice the age-old tradition of “pagsayaw sa bangka,” a dance performed on a bamboo raft.

Natural Attractions of Paete: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Victoria Peak - This peak offers a scenic view of the town and its surrounding mountains and valleys. It is a great spot for hiking and picnicking.
  • Kalbaryo Hill - This hill is an important pilgrimage site and offers a stunning view of the town and its surrounding areas.
  • Baliti Falls - This refreshing waterfall is located just a few kilometers outside of town. Visitors can swim, picnic, and relax in the cool waters.
  • Mount Humarap - This mountain is ideal for hiking and offers a breathtaking view of the surrounding landscape.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Paete

Paete is a nature lover’s paradise, with several hiking and biking trails that offer stunning views of the town and its surrounding mountains and valleys. Some of the popular routes include the Victoria Peak Trail, the Kalbaryo Hill Trail, and the Mount Humarap Trail.

Best Things to Do in Paete: Activities and Tours

  • Woodcarving Workshop - Visitors can take a woodcarving workshop and learn from local artisans how to create their own woodcarving masterpiece.
  • Pagsayaw sa Bangka - Visitors can hire a bamboo raft and experience the traditional dance of “pagsayaw sa bangka” with the locals.
  • Visit Art Galleries - Paete is home to several art galleries that showcase the works of local artists. Visitors can explore these galleries and purchase art pieces as souvenirs.

Food and Drink Scene of Paete

Must-Try Dishes

  • Adobong Laguna - A popular Filipino dish made with chicken or pork, vinegar, and soy sauce, served with rice.
  • Sinigang na buko - A unique Filipino soup made with coconut water, vegetables, and choice of protein.
  • Suman - A traditional Filipino snack made from glutinous rice wrapped in banana leaves.

Local Beverages

  • Lambanog - A Filipino liquor made from coconut sap.
  • Salabat - A traditional Filipino ginger tea.

Where to Stay in Paete: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

There are several accommodation options in Paete that cater to different budgets and preferences.

  • Luisa Ridge Resort - A popular resort with a stunning view of the town and its surrounding areas.
  • Paete Sunrise Garden Resort - A relaxing resort surrounded by lush greenery and a quiet environment.
  • Paete Pension House - An affordable guesthouse in the town center.

What to Buy in Paete: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Woodcarvings - Paete is known as the “Carving Capital of the Philippines,” making it a great place to purchase high-quality woodcarvings.
  • Papier-mache products - These lightweight and colorful handicrafts are made from paper pulp and are perfect for souvenirs.
  • Local delicasies - Visitors can bring home some of the town’s delicious and unique food products such as cashew nuts, coco jam, and pili nuts.

Local Culture and Customs of Paete

Paete is a town steeped in culture and customs, with a strong emphasis on religion and art. It is also known for its friendly and artistic locals who take great pride in their heritage and traditions.

Best Time to Visit Paete

The best time to visit Paete is during the dry season, which lasts from November to April. The town is also famous for its annual Paete Higantes Festival, which is held every November and celebrates the town’s rich culture and traditions.

Do’s and Don’ts of Paete

Do:

  • Respect the town’s culture and heritage
  • Try the local delicacies and participate in cultural activities
  • Visit the town’s art galleries and buy local handicrafts

Don’t:

  • Vandalize or damage any of the town’s cultural or historical landmarks
  • Disrespect the locals or their customs and traditions
  • Litter or damage the town’s natural attractions

Safety and Health

Safety in Paete

Paete is a relatively safe town with low crime rates. Visitors are advised to take necessary precautions, such as keeping an eye on their belongings and avoiding isolated areas at night.

Health in Paete

Paete has a few hospitals and clinics that cater to basic medical needs. Visitors are recommended to have travel health insurance and take necessary precautions such as drinking bottled water and avoiding street food.

Getting to Paete: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Paete

The nearest airport to Paete is the Ninoy Aquino International Airport in Manila.

Bus Stations Near Paete

Buses to and from Manila and nearby towns regularly stop in Paete. Visitors can take a bus from the Cubao or Buendia Bus Terminals in Manila.

Other Options

Visitors can also hire a private car or take a taxi to reach Paete from Manila.

How to Get Around in Paete

Paete is a small town and can easily be explored on foot or by renting a bicycle or motorcycle. Local tricycles and jeepneys are also available for short trips.

Where to Go Next from Paete: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Pagsanjan Falls - A famous waterfall and river cruise destination located just 30 minutes away from Paete.
  • Liliw, Laguna - A town famous for its homemade footwear and picturesque town center, located just 25 km away from Paete.
  • Mt. Makiling - A popular hiking destination with stunning views and a rich biodiversity. Located just 20 km away from Paete.

Conclusion

Paete is a hidden gem in Laguna, Philippines, that offers a unique and enriching experience for those who want to explore the town’s rich culture, history, and natural attractions. From its woodcarving workshops to scenic hiking trails, visitors will surely find something to love in Paete.
