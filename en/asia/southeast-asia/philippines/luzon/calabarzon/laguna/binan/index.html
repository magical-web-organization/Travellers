Introduction to Biñan

Biñan is a first-class, urbanized city in the province of Laguna, Philippines. It is located in Calabarzon, in the south-central part of Luzon Island. Biñan is known for its historical significance, rich culture, delicious cuisine, and beautiful natural attractions. The city’s economy is diverse and vibrant, with thriving industries such as agriculture, manufacturing, and services.

List of Places to Visit in Biñan

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • National Shrine of St. John the Baptist - a significant pilgrimage site for Catholics in the region.
  • Museo de La Salle - a museum showcasing the rich legacy of the La Salle brothers in Biñan.
  • Casa de Biñan (Biñan Museum) - a heritage house that gives visitors a glimpse of the city’s history and culture.
  • Biñan City Hall - an iconic structure that represents the governance and leadership of the city.

Natural Attractions of Biñan: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Mount Malarayat - a popular hiking destination that offers breathtaking views of the nearby towns and cities.
  • Splash Island Water Park - a fun-filled attraction that provides water-themed rides and pools for visitors of all ages.
  • Philippine Rice Research Institute - an agricultural research center that showcases the different varieties of rice grown in the Philippines. Visitors can also enjoy a scenic view of the lush rice fields.
  • Pandin Lake - a serene and peaceful lake surrounded by lush vegetation, perfect for picnics and relaxation.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Biñan

  • Mount Malarayat Trail - a scenic and challenging trail that takes hikers to the peak of Mount Malarayat.
  • Silangan-Mataas na Lupa Connection Trail - perfect for mountain bikers, this trail takes riders from Silangan all the way to Mataas na Lupa.
  • Biñan River Trail - a nature trail that follows the path of the Biñan River, showcasing the natural beauty of the area.

Best Things to Do in Biñan: Activities and Tours

  • Food Trip - Biñan is famous for its delicious culinary offerings. Some must-try dishes include puto biñan, bibingka, and lechon.
  • Heritage Walk - take a leisurely stroll around Biñan’s historical and cultural sites and learn about the city’s rich heritage.
  • Fiesta Tour - experience the vibrant and colorful festivals in Biñan, such as the Feast of St. John the Baptist, which is celebrated every June 24.

Week-Long Itinerary for Biñan

Food and Drink Scene of Biñan

Must-Try Dishes

  • Puto Biñan - a steamed rice cake made with ground glutinous rice, coconut milk, and sugar.
  • Bibingka - a baked rice cake topped with cheese, salted egg, and butter.
  • Lechon - a roasted suckling pig that is a popular main dish for celebrations and special occasions.

Local Beverages

  • Salabat - a ginger tea that is usually served hot, perfect for rainy or cold weather.
  • ** Sago’t Gulaman** - a refreshing drink made of boiled sago pearls, gelatin cubes, and brown sugar syrup.

Where to Stay in Biñan: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Splash Suites Hotel Biñan - a luxurious hotel located near Splash Island.
  • Sampaguita Resort and Events Place - a beach resort-themed hotel that caters to families and couples looking for a relaxing getaway.
  • One Biñan Place - a budget-friendly hotel located in the heart of the city.

What to Buy in Biñan: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Puto Biñan - a staple delicacy in Biñan that makes a perfect pasalubong (souvenir) for friends and family.
  • Handwoven Banig - a traditional mat made of pandan leaves that is commonly used for sleeping or as a floor covering.
  • Barong Tagalog - a traditional Filipino attire that makes for a stylish and unique souvenir.

Local Culture and Customs of Biñan

  • Filipinos are known for their hospitality and friendliness, so visitors can expect warm welcomes from the locals.
  • Biñan is a deeply religious city, with a majority of its residents adhering to Catholicism. Visitors should show respect when visiting religious sites.
  • Indulging in a food trip is a popular social activity among Filipinos, so be sure to join in the fun and sample the local cuisine.

Best Time to Visit Biñan

The best time to visit Biñan is during the dry season, which is from November to April. During this time, visitors can enjoy the cool and pleasant weather, perfect for outdoor activities.

Do’s and Don’ts of Biñan

Do:

  • Try the local cuisine and delicacies
  • Wear appropriate clothing when visiting religious sites
  • Engage with the locals and immerse yourself in the city’s culture

Don’t:

  • Disrespect religious sites and traditions
  • Litter and damage the environment
  • Engage in unsafe or illegal activities

Safety and Health

Safety in Biñan

  • Biñan is generally a safe city. However, visitors should always be vigilant and aware of their surroundings.
  • Avoid walking alone in dimly-lit or unfamiliar areas at night.
  • Secure valuables and important documents in a safe place.

Health in Biñan

  • Visitors should drink only bottled water to avoid getting sick.
  • Bring insect repellent to avoid insect bites and diseases such as dengue fever.
  • Consult a doctor before traveling if you have any special medical conditions or concerns.

Getting to Biñan: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Biñan

  • Ninoy Aquino International Airport - located in Manila, approximately 30 kilometers away from Biñan.

Bus Stations Near Biñan

  • Jam Transit Bus Station - located in Alabang, Muntinlupa City, around 15 kilometers away from Biñan.
  • Dela Rosa Bus Station - located in Pasay City, around 25 kilometers away from Biñan.

Other Options

  • Visitors can also hire a car or take a taxi from Manila to Biñan.

How to Get Around in Biñan

  • Tricycles are a popular mode of transportation in the city, and they are available for hire at affordable prices.
  • Jeepneys and buses also operate in the city, providing transportation to nearby towns and cities.

Where to Go Next from Biñan: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Calamba - a neighboring city known for its hot springs and historical landmarks.
  • San Pablo - a city famous for its seven lakes.
  • Tagaytay - a scenic city known for its cool climate, breathtaking views, and delicious food.

Conclusion

Biñan is a city that offers a mix of historical landmarks, natural attractions, and delicious cuisine. A visit to this city is sure to be a memorable and enriching experience. Whether you’re a nature lover, culture enthusiast, or foodie, Biñan has something to offer to everyone. Plan your trip now and discover the beauty of this wonderful city in the Philippines!
