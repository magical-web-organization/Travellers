Introduction to Calamba

Calamba is a highly urbanized city located in Laguna, Philippines. It is renowned for being the birthplace of National Hero, Dr. Jose Rizal. With a rich cultural heritage, verdant natural landscape, and an abundance of adventure activities, Calamba is must-visit destination in the Philippines.

List of Places to Visit in Calamba

Whether you are interested in history, culture, nature, or adventure, Calamba has something for everyone. Here are some of the top places to visit in Calamba:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Take a nostalgic trip back in time and explore the culturally rich heritage of Calamba through its museums and historical landmarks:

  • Rizal Shrine - The shrine used to be the ancestral home of Jose Rizal, the Philippine’s national hero. Today, it houses a museum featuring Dr. Rizal’s sculptures, memorabilia, artworks, and literary works, which provide an insight into his life, and the politics and society of his era.
  • St. John the Baptist Parish Church - This beautiful church was built during the Spanish colonial period and is a fine example of Baroque architecture in the country. It is famous for its iconic bell tower, beautiful stained glass windows, and intricate designs.
  • The Plaza and Calamba City Hall - The Plaza is situated in the heart of the city, while the city hall sits in front of it. It is a well-manicured public park surrounded by restaurants, shops, museums, and historical monuments.

Natural Attractions of Calamba: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Get ready to be awe-inspired with Calamba’s natural beauty. Here are some of the most beautiful attractions Calamba has to offer:

  • Pandin Lake - This serene lake is nestled amidst the lush forested mountains. You can rent a bamboo raft and glide across the clear, calm waters to enjoy the beautiful sight of majestic mountains and the lake’s marine life.
  • Mount Makiling Natural Reserve - This reserve is a must-visit for adventure seekers and nature lovers. There are hiking trails that will take you through the forested slopes of Mount Makiling, a dormant volcano that is home to a diverse range of flora and fauna.
  • Hidden Valley Springs - This private nature reserve is the epitome of paradise with seven natural water pools and beautiful waterfalls surrounded by lush greenery. The place is perfect for a picnic or a relaxing dip in the hot springs.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Calamba

Calamba is an excellent destination for hiking and biking enthusiasts with its ideal roads and mountain trails. Here are some of the best hiking and biking routes in Calamba:

  • Mount Makiling Traverse - This hike will take you through the mountain’s diverse forests, tranquil streams, and lush landscapes. The trail is approximately 8.5 kilometers long, and the peak is at 1,090 meters above sea level.
  • Biking around Lake Caliraya - This biking route offers stunning views of the surrounding forest, mountains, and Lake Caliraya from all angles. The trail is around 22 kilometers long and is ideal for moderate bikers because of its downhill portions.

Best Things to Do in Calamba: Activities and Tours

If you are looking for some unique local activities and tours, Calamba has plenty to offer:

  • Hot Spring Hopping Tour - Take a tour of the major hot springs in Calamba, including Laguna Hot Springs and the Los Banos Hot Springs. Try them all out and pick your favorite one!
  • Day Tour to Pagsanjan Falls - Experience a scenic boat ride into the heart of Pagsanjan Falls. Walk along the side of the waterfall, and feel the roaring spray powerfully hit your face.
  • Zip-Line Adventure in Nuvali - Get your adrenaline pumping with a zipline adventure in Nuvali. The lines are over 300 meters long, and you can choose either a single or a tandem ride.

Week-Long Itinerary for Calamba

If you are planning on spending a week in Calamba, we have crafted the perfect itinerary for you:

Day Activities
Day 1 Visit the Rizal Shrine, then explore the St. John the Baptist Parish Church and its surrounding old houses.
Day 2 Go hot spring hopping at Laguna Hot Springs and Los Banos Hot Springs.
Day 3 Take a day tour to Pagsanjan Falls.
Day 4 Trek to the top of Mount Makiling Natural Reserve; end the day with a relaxing hot spring bath in any of the resorts in Pansol.
Day 5 Marvel at the beautiful Pandin Lake by renting a bamboo raft.
Day 6 Try zip-line riding in Nuvali, then go shopping at SM Calamba.
Day 7 Take the opportunity to explore the Hidden Valley Springs private nature reserve, then relax and unwind by living the “probinsya life” at the nearby town of Los Baños.

Food and Drink Scene of Calamba

Food and drinks are also a big part of Calamba’s culture, so make sure to try some of their local dishes and beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Buko Pie - A famous creamy pie made with coconut milk and meat from young coconuts presented atop a crust made from flour, sugar, and water.
  • Ginataang Langka - Known for its unique sweet and savory taste made from young jackfruit cooked in coconut milk with a dash of Spices.
  • Bulalo - A famous soup traditionally made with beef marrow bones, some greens, and corn on the cob. It’s often served as a “boodle fight” in a huge serving bowl to be shared among friends and family.

Local Beverages

  • Lambanog - A smooth coconut alcohol often mixed with flavored syrups or fruit juices served in a shot glass.
  • Sago’t Gulaman - A traditional Filipino beverage that consists of small tapioca pearls and jelly cubes suspended in sweetened water infused with panocha or brown sugar topped with shaved ice and evaporated milk.

Where to Stay in Calamba: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Calamba offers a diverse range of accommodations to suit every traveler’s needs. Here are some top options:

  • Seda Nuvali - A luxury hotel located in Sta. Rosa, Laguna, ideal for couples or families that want to explore Nuvali’s recreational and commercial district.
  • Casa Palmeras Calamba - A house rental option with up to six bedrooms, making it ideal for larger groups.
  • Microtel by Wyndham - A budget hotel located in Paseo de Sta. Rosa that is comfortable and has adequately-sized rooms.

What to Buy in Calamba: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Some souvenirs and local products that visitors enjoy bringing back home include:

  • Musical instruments made from bamboo - Tikling, a traditional folk dance accompanied by the namesake bamboo music instrument, has been a celebrated part of Calamba’s art and culture.
  • Coco Jam - A delicious coconut jam made from locally sourced coconuts and Milo.
  • Calamba Buko Pie - A creamy, sweet Filipino dessert pie that mirrors the best tropical fruit in the Philippines.

Local Culture and Customs of Calamba

  • Dress conservatively and modestly, especially when visiting religious landmarks.
  • Filipinos are generally hospitable, respectful, and friendly. Always say “Po” and “Opo,” which are Filipino words that show respect for elders or people in higher authority.
  • It’s common to wash your hands before eating, so expect to see a washbasin before a restaurant or karenderya (Filipino-style dining place).

Best Time to Visit Calamba

Calamba has a tropical climate, which makes it favorable in most months of the year. However, if you want to make the most of the outdoor activities and avoid the rainy season, the best time to visit Calamba is between November and April.

Do’s and Don’ts of Calamba

Do:

  • Respect the locals’ customs, culture, and traditions.
  • Carry cash as most establishments in Calamba don’t accept credit cards.
  • Wear comfortable shoes and clothes to enjoy outdoor activities comfortably.

Don’t:

  • Be too loud or boisterous when walking around public areas. As usual, respect other people’s social boundaries and maintain privacy.
  • Litter on the streets or in any of the tourist spots.

Safety and Health

Safety in Calamba

Calamba is generally safe, but like any other city, it’s best to keep your valuables safe and know your surroundings. Avoid going to areas known for pickpockets or street crimes.

Health in Calamba

Make sure to carry mosquito repellent as dengue occurs in Calamba. Wear long sleeve clothes if you can and protect yourself from the sun to avoid getting sunburnt.

Getting to Calamba: Transportation Options and Logistics

Calamba is easily accessible from Manila and other key cities in the Philippines. Here are the transportation options to choose from:

Airports Near Calamba

The closest airport to Calamba would be the Ninoy Aquino International Airport located in Manila. From there, you can take a bus from the airport to Calamba.

Bus Stations Near Calamba

The bus terminals that service Calamba are the Turbina Terminal and the Calamba Terminal. Both terminals offer bus services to and from different parts of Luzon.

Other Options

  • Take the PNR (Philippine National Railways) from Manila to Calamba Station.
  • Private cars for hire are readily available in Manila for those who want a more comfortable journey.

How to Get Around in Calamba

Tricycles, jeepneys, and buses are the primary modes of transportation in Calamba. Tricycle fares within Calamba are around P50 to P60 per ride while jeepney and bus rides cost around P10 to P25 per person.

Where to Go Next from Calamba: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Apart from Calamba’s well-known attractions and landmarks, there are other nearby destinations to explore:

  • Los Banos - An excellent destination for anyone who loves outdoor activities and adventure as it is known for its hot springs, waterfalls, and hiking trails.
  • Tagaytay - A nearby city in the province of Cavite, famous for its stunning views of Taal Lake and Taal Volcano. You can experience its farm-to-table restaurants serving local cuisine.

Conclusion

Calamba indeed has a lot to offer its visitors, from the culturally significant landmarks to the water adventures, outdoor sports, and natural attractions. A week isn’t even enough to explore every corner of this beautiful city. Whether you are in it for the adventure or food trip, a visit to Calamba will undoubtedly be worth it.
