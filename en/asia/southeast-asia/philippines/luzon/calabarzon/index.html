Introduction to Calabarzon

Calabarzon, also known as Region IV-A, is located in Luzon in the Philippines, in Southeast Asia. This region is composed of five provinces - Cavite, Laguna, Batangas, Rizal, and Quezon. Calabarzon is a diverse region, home to historic landmarks, natural wonders, and culinary delights. With its close proximity to Manila, this region is a popular destination for locals and tourists alike.

List of Places to Visit in Calabarzon

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Calabarzon is rich in history and culture. Visitors can explore the many museums and historical sites in the region to learn more about the Philippines’ past. The following are some of the most culturally important attractions to visit:

  • Barasoain Church is located in Malolos City, Bulacan, and is considered one of the oldest churches in the country.
  • Fort Santiago in Manila is a historic site that served as a Spanish military fortress, detention center, and prison.
  • Pila Heritage Site in Pila, Laguna, is a collection of well-preserved Spanish-era houses and structures.
  • Museo ni Rizal in Fort Santiago, Manila, is a museum dedicated to the life and legacy of National Hero, Jose Rizal.
  • Corregidor Island is a historic World War II site located off the coast of Bataan.

Natural Attractions of Calabarzon: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Calabarzon is also known for its natural wonders. There are plenty of outdoor adventures to enjoy, such as hiking, swimming, and snorkeling. Here are some of the must-visit places in Calabarzon:

  • Taal Volcano in Batangas offers an unforgettable hiking experience, with stunning views of the crater lake.
  • Laguna de Bay is the largest lake in the country, and is ideal for fishing, boating, and other water activities.
  • Tagaytay Highlands is a popular destination for breathtaking views of Taal Lake and the surrounding mountains.
  • Nasugbu Beach in Batangas is famous for its white sandy beaches and crystal-clear waters, perfect for swimming and snorkeling.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Calabarzon

Calabarzon is a great destination for outdoor enthusiasts. It offers many scenic hiking and biking routes. Some of the popular hikes and trails include:

  • Mt. Batulao in Batangas is a great beginner-friendly hike with stunning views of the surrounding landscapes.
  • Mt. Pinatubo in Zambales offers a challenging but rewarding day hike, with unforgettable views of the crater lake.
  • Pico de Loro in Cavite is a popular destination for hikers, with its picturesque views of the surrounding mountains and forests.

Best Things to Do in Calabarzon: Activities and Tours

Aside from outdoor activities, there are plenty of other things to do in Calabarzon. Visitors will never run out of options for fun and excitement. Some of the most popular activities and tours include:

  • Food Tours are a must-do in Calabarzon. Visitors can indulge in local delicacies, like bulalo and tapsilog.
  • Heritage Tours allow visitors to learn about the rich history and culture of the region, by exploring historic landmarks and sites.
  • Theme and Amusement Parks like Enchanted Kingdom in Laguna offer fun and thrilling experiences for the whole family.

Week-Long Itinerary for Calabarzon

For those who want to explore Calabarzon more in-depth, a week-long itinerary is recommended. Here’s a sample itinerary:

Food and Drink Scene of Calabarzon

The food scene in Calabarzon is diverse and unique. Visitors can try local delicacies such as:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Bulalo is a traditional Filipino soup made with beef shanks and bone marrow.
  • Adobo is a classic Filipino dish made with chicken or pork, marinated in vinegar, soy sauce, and garlic.
  • Sinigang is a sour soup made with tamarind, vegetables, and meat or fish.

Local Beverages

  • Lambanog is a popular alcoholic drink made from fermented coconut juice.
  • Barako coffee is a strong and flavorful coffee variety grown in Batangas.

Where to Stay in Calabarzon: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Calabarzon offers many lodging options for visitors. From high-end resorts to budget-friendly hostels, there is something for everyone. The following are some of the best places to stay in Calabarzon:

  • The Farm at San Benito in Batangas is a luxury wellness resort that offers a holistic experience for visitors.
  • Pico Sands Hotel in Nasugbu, Batangas, offers cozy rooms with beautiful sea views.
  • La Luz Beach Resort and Spa in Laiya, Batangas, is a popular beachfront resort that caters to families and groups.
  • Quezon Premier Hotel in Lucena City offers comfortable rooms and is located conveniently close to shopping and dining options.

What to Buy in Calabarzon: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Calabarzon is famous for producing high-quality local products. Visitors can bring home souvenirs such as:

  • Barong Tagalog is the traditional Filipino formal attire worn by men on special occasions.
  • Laguna Leather is a high-quality leather product made in Laguna.
  • Batangas Coffe is a popular souvenir item, known for its rich, bold flavor.

Local Culture and Customs of Calabarzon

The people of Calabarzon are friendly and hospitable. One custom of the region is to greet people with a smile and a warm welcome. It is also customary to use “po” and “opo” when speaking to elders as a sign of respect.

Best Time to Visit Calabarzon

The best time to visit Calabarzon is during the dry season, between November and April. This is when the weather is most pleasant, with cool breezes and sunny skies. Summers can be very hot and humid, while typhoons can occur during the rainy season.

Do’s and Don’ts of Calabarzon

Do:

  • Try the local food and drinks.
  • Respect the customs of the locals.
  • Bring sunscreen and insect repellent.

Don’t:

  • Waste food or leave too much on your plate.
  • Disrespect religious monuments and sites.
  • Flash valuables or money in public places.

Safety and Health

Safety in Calabarzon

Calabarzon, like most destinations, is generally safe. Visitors should be cautious and aware of their surroundings, especially when in crowded areas or at night. Avoid carrying too much cash or valuables with you. In case of an emergency, dial 911.

Health in Calabarzon

Visitors should be mindful of the food and water they consume to avoid getting sick. Drinking bottled water is recommended. Mosquitoes are also prevalent in the region, so wearing long sleeves and pants and using insect repellent is advised.

Getting to Calabarzon: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Calabarzon

The closest airport to Calabarzon is the Ninoy Aquino International Airport (NAIA) in Manila. From the airport, visitors can take a bus or hire a private car to reach their destination in Calabarzon.

Bus Stations Near Calabarzon

Several bus companies operate routes to Calabarzon from Manila and other parts of Luzon. Some of the popular bus companies are JAM Liner, DLTB Co, and Jac Liner.

Other Options

Visitors can also hire a taxi or rent a car to explore Calabarzon. However, driving can be difficult due to traffic and road conditions in some parts of the region. Hiring a private car with a driver is recommended.

How to Get Around in Calabarzon

Getting around Calabarzon is easy thanks to its well-connected road and transportation network. Visitors can take a bus, jeepney, tricycle, or taxi to reach their destination. Alternatively, renting a car or hiring a private driver is another option.

Where to Go Next from Calabarzon: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

There are plenty of nearby destinations and excursions to explore once you’ve seen everything Calabarzon has to offer. Some of the popular destinations include:

  • Tagaytay City is a scenic area near Calabarzon, famous for its stunning views of Taal Lake.
  • Puerto Galera is an excellent destination for beach lovers, with crystal-clear waters and white sandy beaches.
  • Mt. Mayon in Bicol is a beautiful volcano that attracts hikers and outdoor enthusiasts.

Conclusion

Calabarzon is a region full of surprises and adventures, where visitors can experience the rich cultural heritage of the Philippines, hike to the top of a volcano, or relax on a white sandy beach. Visitors can explore the stunning natural wonders, sample delicious local food, and learn about the customs and traditions of the locals. A trip to Calabarzon is sure to be unforgettable.
