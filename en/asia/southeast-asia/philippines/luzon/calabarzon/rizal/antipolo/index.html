Introduction to Antipolo

Antipolo, a town in the Province of Rizal, is known as the Pilgrimage City of the Philippines. It is famous for the Antipolo Church, an important pilgrimage site for Catholics. Aside from its religious significance, Antipolo is also known for its stunning panoramic view of Metro Manila and its surrounding provinces. The town is a perfect destination for those seeking a peaceful escape from the bustling city.

List of Places to Visit in Antipolo

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Pinto Art Museum - A popular art museum that features contemporary Filipino art pieces surrounded by lush gardens.
  • Casa Santa Museum - A museum that showcases various Santa Claus figurines and collections from around the world.
  • Hinulugang Taktak Waterfall National Park - A historical site that served as a bathing spot for the locals during the Spanish Colonial period.

Natural Attractions of Antipolo: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Cloud 9 - A famous viewing deck where you can capture a stunning panoramic view of Metro Manila and its surrounding provinces.
  • Masungi Georeserve - A conservation area that offers various courses, including hiking, caving, and zip-lining while enjoying scenic views.
  • Daranak Falls - A popular waterfall with crystal-clear waters surrounded by lush greenery.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Antipolo

  • Mt. Pamitinan - A popular hiking spot with a moderate level of difficulty. It offers scenic views of local flora and fauna, plus a stunning view of the Sierra Madre mountain range.
  • Pililla Wind Farm - A perfect location for a bike ride, where you can enjoy the scenic views of local rice fields, mountain ranges, and soaring wind turbines.

Best Things to Do in Antipolo: Activities and Tours

  • Visit Antipolo Church - A pilgrimage site known for its miraculous powers and a perfect place to visit for religious purposes.
  • Go on a food trip - Antipolo is known for its sumptuous dishes such as “suman,” a type of Filipino rice cake, and “kasuy,” a local delicacy made from cashew nuts.
  • Join a local tour - Discover Antipolo’s rich history and cultural heritage through local tours that take you around the town’s significant sites.

Week-Long Itinerary for Antipolo

Day 1: Visit the Pinto Art Museum and explore its surrounding gardens.

Day 2: Hike up to Mt. Pamitinan and capture a stunning view of Sierra Madre.

Day 3: Visit the Antipolo Church and other religious sites around the town’s perimeter.

Day 4: Take a bike ride up to the Pililla Wind Farm and enjoy the scenic view of the surrounding areas.

Day 5: Go on a food trip and try out the local delicacies around Antipolo.

Day 6: Take a local tour and explore the historical and cultural landmarks around the town.

Day 7: Visit the majestic Daranak Falls and enjoy the serene surroundings.

Food and Drink Scene of Antipolo

Must-Try Dishes

  • Suman - A type of Philippine rice cake, wrapped in banana leaves and sweetened with coconut milk.
  • Kasuy - A sweet and crunchy treat made from locally sourced cashew nuts.
  • Kakanin - A popular Filipino delicacy made from glutinous rice and coconut milk.

Local Beverages

  • Lambanog - A Filipino alcoholic drink distilled from coconut sap.
  • Barako Coffee - A type of coffee that originated from the province of Batangas known for its rich, bold flavor.

Where to Stay in Antipolo: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Here are some of the best-rated accommodations in Antipolo:

  • Luljetta’s Place Garden Suites - A well-loved accommodation that features Balinese-inspired villas, along with a spa and wellness center.
  • The Citywalk Suites Apartment - An affordable hotel in the heart of Antipolo that provides spacious and well-equipped rooms.
  • Yllana Beach resort at Calayo Beach - A beach resort and diving center that offers beachfront accommodations and a wide range of activities.

What to Buy in Antipolo: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Local food products such as suman, kasuy, and kakanin are great souvenirs and gifts to bring home.
  • Handicrafts - Antipolo is known for its locally made handicrafts, such as woven bags and baskets.

Local Culture and Customs of Antipolo

Antipolo is known for its religious culture, mainly centered on the Antipolo Church. It is a must-visit place for Catholics, especially during the Holy Week. Filipinos in Antipolo are also known for their warm hospitality and love for traditional crafts and cuisine.

Best Time to Visit Antipolo

The best time to visit Antipolo is between November to January, where there is a lower chance of rain and the temperature is cooler. The town can also get busy during the Holy Week.

Do’s and Don’ts of Antipolo

Do:

  • Respect the local customs and traditions.
  • Try out the local delicacies and food products.
  • Visit historical landmarks and pilgrimage sites for a full experience.

Don’t:

  • Litter or throw trash in public areas.
  • Disrespect religious sites and customs.
  • Start non-allowed fires in any of the public parks and open areas.

Safety and Health

Safety in Antipolo

Antipolo is generally safe, but visitors should still be mindful of their belongings in public areas. Pickpocketing and theft at crowded places is a common occurrence, so always be cautious.

Health in Antipolo

Antipolo has decent medical facilities. The town’s proximity to the Philippine capital of Manila also offers access to several world-class hospitals and medical centers in case of an emergency.

Getting to Antipolo: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Antipolo

The nearest airport to Antipolo is the Ninoy Aquino International Airport (MNL) located in Pasay City, Manila.

Bus Stations Near Antipolo

There are several bus companies that offer transportation from Manila to Antipolo, including RRCG Transport, Sienna Bus Lines, and Gitnang Bus Lines.

Other Options

  • Private cars and vans are also available for hire when going to Antipolo.
  • Jeepneys, tricycles, and pedicabs are the primary mode of transportation around the town.

How to Get Around in Antipolo

Jeepneys, tricycles, and pedicabs are the most common modes of transportation around Antipolo. Taxis are also available, but may be hard to find in certain areas.

Where to Go Next from Antipolo: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Marikina City - Known as the Shoe Capital of the Philippines, Marikina City is just a short drive away from Antipolo.
  • Tanay - A municipality that offers beautiful natural attractions such as Daraitan River and Tinipak River.
  • Laguna - A province known for its hot springs, waterfalls, and historical landmarks.

Conclusion

Antipolo offers a lot of natural and cultural attractions that make it a perfect destination, whether for a weekend getaway or a more extended holiday. Visiting this town is a good opportunity to experience the cultural and religious traditions of the Philippines while enjoying beautiful views and exciting outdoor activities.
