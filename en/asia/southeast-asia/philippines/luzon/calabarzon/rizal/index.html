Introduction to Rizal

Rizal is a province in the Calabarzon region, located east of Manila, the capital city of the Philippines. It is named after Dr. Jose Rizal, a hero and national figure, who was born in the town of Calamba. The province is known for its beautiful scenery, natural attractions, historical landmarks, and cultural heritage. Whether you are an outdoor enthusiast, a history buff, or a culture lover, Rizal has something to offer for everyone.

List of Places to Visit in Rizal

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

National Museum of the Philippines - Angono Branch

The National Museum of the Philippines - Angono Branch is located in the town of Angono, known as the “Art Capital of the Philippines”. It houses a collection of prehistoric artifacts, archaeological finds, and artworks by local artists, including the Angono Petroglyphs, the oldest known artwork in the Philippines.

Ynares Center Antipolo

Ynares Center Antipolo is a multi-purpose indoor stadium in the city of Antipolo. It is the largest sports venue in the province and is a popular venue for various events, such as concerts, sports tournaments, and trade fairs.

Regina Rosarii Institute for Contemplation in Asia

Regina Rosarii Institute for Contemplation in Asia is a pilgrimage site in Tanay. It features a 71-foot statue of the Blessed Virgin Mary, a Way of the Cross, and a labyrinth garden. It is a peaceful sanctuary for reflection and contemplation.

Natural Attractions of Rizal: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Masungi Georeserve

Masungi Georeserve is a conservation area in Baras that covers 1,500 hectares of land. It is known for its unique limestone formations, lush forests, and wildlife. Visitors can explore the area through guided tours, which include hiking, caving, and rappelling.

Daranak Falls

Daranak Falls is a popular destination in Tanay, known for its clear blue waters and picturesque surroundings. Visitors can swim, picnic, and enjoy the scenic views of the falls and the surrounding forest.

Mt. Pamitinan

Mt. Pamitinan is a 426-meter high mountain in Rodriguez that offers a challenging but rewarding climb. The trail features rocky terrain, steep slopes, and scenic vistas. The summit rewards hikers with a panoramic view of the surrounding mountains and towns.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Rizal

Tinipak River and Cave

Tinipak River and Cave is a scenic hiking destination in the town of Tanay. The trail leads to the river, which is surrounded by limestone cliffs, and the cave, which features stalactites and stalagmites.

Wawa Dam

Wawa Dam is a reservoir and a popular spot for hiking and biking in the town of Rodriguez. The trail leads to the dam, which offers scenic views of the Sierra Madre mountain range and the Wawa River.

Pililla Wind Farm

Pililla Wind Farm is a sustainable energy facility in Pililla that offers a unique biking experience. The trail leads to the wind turbines, which offer scenic views of the Laguna de Bay and the surrounding mountains.

Best Things to Do in Rizal: Activities and Tours

Visit Pinto Art Museum

Pinto Art Museum is a contemporary art museum in Antipolo, known for its collection of modern artworks and exhibits. The museum features a variety of installations, sculptures, and paintings.

Ride a Traditional Jeepney

Jeepneys are colorful, iconic vehicles that are a common mode of transportation in the Philippines. Riding a jeepney is a unique and exciting way to explore the streets of Rizal and experience local culture.

Join a Food Tour

Rizal is known for its delicious cuisine, such as suman (rice cake), kakanin (sticky rice), and pancit (noodles). Joining a food tour is a great way to experience the local food scene and discover hidden culinary gems.

Food and Drink Scene of Rizal

Must-Try Dishes

Suman

Suman is a traditional Filipino rice cake, made from glutinous rice cooked in coconut milk and wrapped in banana leaves. It is a popular snack or dessert in Rizal and is usually eaten with sugar or coco jam.

Kakanin

Kakanin is a type of Filipino dessert, made from sticky rice, coconut milk, and sugar. It comes in different flavors and varieties, such as sapin-sapin, biko, and puto. It is often served during special occasions or celebrations.

Pancit

Pancit is a Filipino noodle dish that comes in different varieties and flavors. It is usually made from rice noodles, vegetables, meat, and spices. It is considered a staple food in Rizal and is commonly eaten during family gatherings or fiestas.

Local Beverages

Kapeng Barako

Kapeng Barako is a strong and flavorful coffee that originated from Batangas. It is a popular drink in Rizal and is usually served hot or iced. It is made from Liberica coffee beans, which are native to the Philippines.

Lambanog

Lambanog is a potent liquor that is made from fermented coconut sap. It is a traditional drink in Rizal and is often served during festive occasions or celebrations. It has a clear and smooth taste and is usually served cold or at room temperature.

Where to Stay in Rizal: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Thunderbird Resorts and Casinos - Rizal

Thunderbird Resorts and Casinos - Rizal is a luxurious resort and casino in Binangonan. It features a variety of amenities, such as a swimming pool, a spa, and a golf course. It offers comfortable accommodations and excellent service.

Mt. Purro Nature Reserve

Mt. Purro Nature Reserve is an eco-friendly resort in Antipolo. It offers a range of accommodations, such as eco-huts, cabins, and dormitories. It features lush forests, natural springs, and wildlife. It is a perfect destination for nature lovers and adventure seekers.

Seven Suites Hotel Observatory

Seven Suites Hotel Observatory is a boutique hotel in Antipolo. It features a rooftop observatory, which offers a panoramic view of the city and the Manila skyline. It offers elegant and spacious rooms, a restaurant, and a swimming pool.

What to Buy in Rizal: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Hinulugang Taktak Souvenirs

Hinulugang Taktak Souvenirs is a shop in Antipolo that offers a variety of souvenirs, such as keychains, shirts, and magnets. It also sells local delicacies, such as suman and kakanin. It is a perfect place to buy gifts or mementos of your Rizal trip.

Angono Petroglyphs Souvenirs

Angono Petroglyphs Souvenirs is a shop near the National Museum of the Philippines - Angono Branch that offers a range of souvenirs, such as postcards, bookmarks, and crafts. It also sells artworks by local artists, inspired by the Angono Petroglyphs.

Local Culture and Customs of Rizal

Rizal is a province with a rich cultural heritage, shaped by its history, traditions, and people. Filipinos in Rizal are known for their warmth, hospitality, and strong sense of community. They celebrate a variety of festivals and events throughout the year, such as the Higantes Festival in Angono, the Pahiyas Festival in Lucban, and the Feast of Our Lady of Peace and Good Voyage in Antipolo.

Best Time to Visit Rizal

The best time to visit Rizal is during the dry season, from December to May. This is the peak tourist season, with mild temperatures, sunny skies, and low rainfall. The months of January to February are ideal for hiking and outdoor activities, with cooler temperatures during the day and at night.

Do’s and Don’ts of Rizal

Do:

  • Respect local customs and traditions
  • Dress modestly and appropriately, especially when visiting religious sites
  • Try local cuisine and delicacies
  • Greet people with a smile and say “po” and “opo” as a sign of respect

Don’t:

  • Disrespect local culture and customs
  • Engage in illegal activities, such as drugs and gambling
  • Take photos without permission, especially of people and religious objects
  • Litter or damage the environment

Safety and Health

Safety in Rizal

Rizal is generally safe for tourists, but visitors should take precautions to avoid theft, scams, and accidents. It is advisable to keep valuables secure, avoid walking alone at night, and be aware of your surroundings.

Health in Rizal

Rizal has several hospitals and medical facilities that offer quality healthcare services. It is advisable to have travel insurance and to be up-to-date with routine vaccinations, such as hepatitis A and B, typhoid, and tetanus.

Getting to Rizal: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Rizal

The nearest airport to Rizal is Ninoy Aquino International Airport in Manila. From the airport, visitors can take a taxi or a bus to Rizal.

Bus Stations Near Rizal

There are several bus stations near Rizal, such as Araneta Center Bus Terminal, Cubao Bus Terminal, and Pasay Bus Terminal. Visitors can take a bus to Tanay, Antipolo, or other towns in Rizal.

Other Options

Visitors can also take a private car or hire a taxi to Rizal. There are also jeepneys and tricycles available for local transportation within towns.

How to Get Around in Rizal

Visitors can get around Rizal by taking public transportation, such as jeepneys, tricycles, and buses. They can also hire a car or a motorcycle, or join a guided tour.

Where to Go Next from Rizal: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Laguna

Laguna is a province that borders Rizal, known for its hot springs, lakes, and waterfalls. It offers a range of outdoor activities, such as hiking, swimming, and boating.

Batangas

Batangas is a province south of Rizal, known for its beaches, churches, and heritage towns. It offers a variety of activities, such as diving, island hopping, and exploring historical landmarks.

Conclusion

Rizal is a province that has something to offer for everyone, from its natural wonders and scenic views to its cultural heritage and delicious cuisine. It is a perfect destination for those who seek adventure, relaxation, or immersion in local culture. With its proximity to Manila and other nearby destinations, it is a must-visit place in the Philippines.
