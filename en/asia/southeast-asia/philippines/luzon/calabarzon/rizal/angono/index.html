Introduction to Angono

Nestled in the Calabarzon region of the Philippines’ Luzon island is the charming town of Angono. Known for its vibrant art scene and rich cultural heritage, Angono is a popular destination for travelers seeking to immerse themselves in the local history and natural beauty of the Philippines. Angono boasts a vibrant mix of museums, historical landmarks, outdoor adventures, and local cuisine that makes it a perfect destination for anyone looking for a unique and memorable travel experience.

List of Places to Visit in Angono

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

1. Blanco Family Museum

The Blanco Family Museum is a must-visit museum in Angono. The museum features the works of three generations of the Blanco family, one of the most famous artistic families in the Philippines. Visitors can admire vibrant paintings, sculptures, and other works of art while learning about the family’s history and creative process.

2. Nemiranda Arthouse

The Nemiranda Arthouse showcases the artwork of renowned Filipino artist Nemesio Miranda, or “Nemi” as he is affectionately known. Visitors can take a guided tour of the Art House, which features an extensive collection of sculptures, paintings, and other art pieces.

3. Angono Petroglyphs

For history buffs, the Angono Petroglyphs is a must-visit attraction. The petroglyphs date back to around 3000 BC and are recognized as the oldest known work of art in the Philippines. Visitors can take a guided tour to see these fascinating rock carvings.

Natural Attractions of Angono: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

1. Hinulugang Taktak Falls

Located just a short distance from Angono, the Hinulugang Taktak Falls is a stunning natural wonder. Visitors can hike through lush rainforests to reach the falls, which plunge into a sparkling swimming hole.

2. Angono Forest Park

The Angono Forest Park is a sprawling nature reserve that allows visitors to escape the hustle and bustle of the city and immerse themselves in nature. The park features hiking trails, picnic areas, and scenic views of the surrounding countryside.

3. Mount Tagapo

For those looking for a challenging hike, the Mount Tagapo trail is a popular choice. At the summit, hikers are rewarded with stunning vistas of the Laguna de Bay and the surrounding mountains.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Angono

Angono is a paradise for outdoor enthusiasts, with a plethora of hiking and biking trails to explore. From Mount Tagapo to the Angono Forest Park, there is no shortage of scenic routes to choose from. Some of the most popular hiking and biking routes in Angono include the Daranak Falls to Batlag Falls trail, the Angono Forest Park Trail, and the Mount Tagapo Trail.

Best Things to Do in Angono: Activities and Tours

In addition to the attractions mentioned above, Angono offers plenty of other activities and tours to keep visitors entertained. From pottery workshops to food tours, there is something for everyone in this charming town.

Week-Long Itinerary for Angono

Day 1: Explore the Blanco Family Museum and Nemiranda Arthouse

Day 2: Take a guided tour of the Angono Petroglyphs

Day 3: Hike to the Hinulugang Taktak Falls

Day 4: Explore the Angono Forest Park and its hiking trails

Day 5: Climb to the summit of Mount Tagapo

Day 6: Take a pottery workshop or a food tour of Angono

Day 7: Visit local markets and buy souvenirs to take home

Food and Drink Scene of Angono

Must-Try Dishes

No visit to Angono is complete without sampling some of the local cuisine. Some must-try dishes in Angono include:

1. Kakanin

Kakanin is a traditional Filipino dessert made from glutinous rice. Angono is known for its delicious kakanin, which comes in various flavors and shapes, such as bibingka and puto.

2. Adobo

Adobo is a popular Filipino dish that features meat, usually chicken or pork, marinated in soy sauce, vinegar, and spices, and then cooked low and slow. Angono’s version of adobo is a must-try dish for meat-lovers.

3. Balaw Balaw

Balaw Balaw is a unique dish that features fermented rice mixed with various meats and vegetables. It’s a must-try dish for adventurous eaters.

Local Beverages

While Angono is better known for its food, it also boasts some unique local beverages. Visitors should try:

1. Lambanog

Lambanog is a type of Filipino rum made from the sap of coconut trees. It’s a smooth and refreshing drink that’s perfect for sipping on a hot day.

2. Barako Coffee

Barako coffee is a strong and flavorful coffee that’s unique to the Philippines. It’s made from liberica beans and is a must-try for coffee lovers.

Where to Stay in Angono: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Angono offers plenty of accommodation options for travelers of all budgets. Some of the best hotels, hostels, and guesthouses in the area include:

1. Thunderbird Resorts and Casino

Located just a short distance from Angono, Thunderbird Resorts and Casino is a luxurious hotel that offers stunning views of the surrounding landscape. It features a casino, spa, and world-class dining options.

2. Angono Bed and Breakfast

Angono Bed and Breakfast is a cozy and comfortable guesthouse that offers affordable rooms and homemade breakfast.

3. Angono Forest Park Mountain Resort

For nature enthusiasts, the Angono Forest Park Mountain Resort is an excellent choice. Located in the heart of Angono Forest Park, the resort offers easy access to hiking trails and other outdoor activities.

What to Buy in Angono: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Angono is famous for its vibrant art scene and unique local products. Visitors should consider buying:

1. Angono Petroglyphs Souvenirs

Visitors can bring home a piece of history with souvenirs inspired by the Angono Petroglyphs. From t-shirts to coffee mugs, there are plenty of options for souvenirs.

2. Local Artwork

Angono is home to some of the most talented artists in the Philippines. Visitors can bring home a piece of local artwork as a memento of their visit.

Local Culture and Customs of Angono

Angono is a town steeped in culture and tradition. Visitors should be respectful of local customs, such as removing shoes before entering someone’s home. It’s also important to dress modestly when visiting religious sites or museums.

Best Time to Visit Angono

The best time to visit Angono is during the dry season, which typically lasts from November to April. This is when the weather is most favorable for outdoor activities and sightseeing.

Do’s and Don’ts of Angono

  • Do explore the local art scene
  • Do dress appropriately when visiting religious sites or museums

Don’t:

  • Don’t litter
  • Don’t be disrespectful of local customs and traditions
  • Don’t forget to bring comfortable shoes for hiking and exploring

Safety and Health

Safety in Angono

Angono is a relatively safe town, but visitors should still take standard safety precautions, like keeping valuables hidden and not walking alone at night.

Health in Angono

Visitors should take standard precautions, like drinking bottled water and protecting themselves from mosquito bites.

Getting to Angono: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Angono

The closest international airport to Angono is Ninoy Aquino International Airport in Manila.

Bus Stations Near Angono

Angono is well-connected by bus to Manila and other nearby towns in the Calabarzon region.

Other Options

Some visitors opt to hire a private car or take a taxi to reach Angono.

How to Get Around in Angono

The most common modes of transportation in Angono are tricycles and jeepneys, which are inexpensive and easy to use. Visitors can also rent bicycles, especially for exploring the hiking and biking trails around Angono.

Where to Go Next from Angono: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Visitors to Angono can easily explore other towns in the Calabarzon region, such as Antipolo and Tanay. The nearby La Mesa Watershed also offers a beautiful natural escape.

Conclusion

Angono is a charming and culturally rich town that offers visitors an immersive and unique travel experience. With its vibrant art scene, rich history, and stunning natural beauty, Angono is a must-visit destination for anyone exploring the Philippines.
