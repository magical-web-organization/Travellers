Introduction to Tayabas

Located in the Quezon province in Calabarzon, Tayabas is a city with rich culture and history. It is known for its prominent role in the Philippine Revolution and as the site of a significant battle against the Spanish army.

Tayabas also boasts breathtaking natural beauty, with lush forests, stunning waterfalls, and scenic views.

List of Places to Visit in Tayabas

Tayabas has something for everyone, from historically significant sites to outdoor adventures and cultural attractions. Here are some of the top places to visit:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Tayabas Church - Also known as Saint Michael the Archangel Parish Church, this church dates back to the Spanish colonial period and features stunning baroque architecture.
  • Manuel S. Enverga University Museum - This museum features artifacts and memorabilia related to the Philippine Revolution and the city’s history.
  • Old Municipal Hall of Tayabas - This historic building now houses the city library and serves as a venue for cultural events.
  • Mansion Rizalina - This charming colonial house is a historical gem, counting more than 200 years of existence. It is now open to the public for viewing and tours.

Natural Attractions of Tayabas: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Tayabas Bay - The bay boasts stunning sunsets, making it a popular spot for nature lovers and photographers.
  • Villa del Prado Swimming Pool - This freshwater pool is sourced from nearby natural springs and surrounded by lush vegetation, perfect for a relaxing dip or picnic.
  • Malagonlong Bridge - This famous bridge spans the Tayabas River and offers breathtaking views of the surrounding natural landscape.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Tayabas

If you are an adventurous traveler, you will enjoy the trails and routes of Tayabas mountains. Take on the following recommended trails:

  • Mt. Banahaw Hike - This challenging hike takes you through Tayabas’ lush forest and offers stunning views from the peak, including an almost unobstructed view of Laguna Lake.
  • Biking Along The Trails of Tayabas - Explore Tayabas mountains via bike and discover hidden towns and villages nestled within the trees.

Best Things to Do in Tayabas: Activities and Tours

Aside from visiting cultural and natural spots, Tayabas offers a diverse range of activities to suit visitors of all ages and interests. Here are some favorites:

  • Join the Tayabas Heritage Trail Tour - Take part in this guided tour through Tayabas’ historical center, where you can see notable sites such as the Saint Michael the Archangel Parish Church.
  • Visit Pahiyas Festival - Every May, Tayabas comes alive with vibrant colors and lively decorations during this annual festival.
  • Take an Eco-Walking Tour - Explore the natural beauty of Tayabas with a guided walking tour through Villa del Prado and admire its flora and fauna.

Food and Drink Scene of Tayabas

Your trip to Tayabas wouldn’t be complete without trying these must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Budin (Rice Cake) - Made with sticky rice and coconut milk, this sweet treat is a Tayabas specialty.
  • Lucban Longganisa - Similar to other Filipino sausages, Lucban longganisa is characterized by its distinct garlicky flavor. It is normally enjoyed for breakfast with rice and eggs.
  • Sinaing na Tulingan - This slow-cooked dish features fresh fish simmered in coconut milk and spices.

Local Beverages

  • Kamias Shake - Made of kamias fruit, sugar, ice, and water, this shake is a native Tayabas beverage that’s perfect for a hot day.
  • Barako Coffee - Known for its strong flavor, Barako is a popular coffee variety in the region.
  • Lambanog - This traditional alcoholic beverage is distilled from coconut sap and is a must-try for tourists who want to experience local culture.

Where to Stay in Tayabas: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Accommodation in Tayabas ranges from budget-friendly hostels to luxurious hotels. Here are some of the best options:

  • Batis Aramin Resort and Hotel - Located in nearby Lucban, this resort offers luxurious rooms, spa services, and a variety of outdoor activities.
  • Jamelton Hotel - This budget-friendly hotel is located in the heart of Tayabas and offers comfortable and clean rooms.
  • Kamayan sa Palaisdaan - This peaceful property is set alongside a fishpond and has its restaurant serving local delicacies.

What to Buy in Tayabas: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Aside from memories and photographs, you can take home the following:

  • Budin - Take home some of Tayabas’ famous rice cake to share with friends and family.
  • Lucban Longganisa - You can also bring some of these sausage delicacies to share with family and friends.
  • Lambanog - Bring home a bottle or two of this local alcoholic beverage to share with friends back home.

Local Culture and Customs of Tayabas

Respect for elders and traditions is part of Tayabas culture, and it is important to dress appropriately when visiting places of worship. It is also customary to greet locals with “Magandang araw po” (Good day, sir/madam).

Best Time to Visit Tayabas

The best time to visit Tayabas is from January to May when temperatures are pleasant and there is little chance of rain. May is also an excellent time to visit for the annual Pahiyas Festival.

Do’s and Don’ts of Tayabas

Do:

  • Respect local customs and traditions.
  • Dress appropriately, especially when visiting places of worship.
  • Learn some basic Tagalog phrases to communicate with locals.

Don’t:

  • Litter or damage natural and cultural sites.
  • Take pictures of people without their permission.

Safety and Health

Safety in Tayabas

Tayabas is a relatively safe city, but it is always important to remain vigilant and watch your belongings when exploring crowded areas.

Health in Tayabas

The availability of medical facilities in Tayabas is limited, and it is essential to bring any necessary medication with you.

Getting to Tayabas: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Tayabas

The closest airport to Tayabas is Ninoy Aquino International Airport in Manila, which is approximately 120 km away. From the airport, you can take a bus or private car transfer to Tayabas.

Bus Stations Near Tayabas

There are several bus stations in nearby cities such as Lucena and Pagbilao, with regular services to Tayabas and other destinations throughout Quezon Province.

Other Options

Tayabas is accessible by private car or rented vehicle from nearby cities. If you decide to drive yourself, it’s essential to know that Filipino traffic rules may differ from those in other countries.

How to Get Around in Tayabas

Tayabas is a relatively small city, and you can explore most of it on foot or by rented vehicle. Public transportation such as tricycles and jeepneys are also available.

Where to Go Next from Tayabas: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

If you have some spare time to explore beyond Tayabas, here are some nearby destinations and excursions worth visiting:

  • Sariaya - This town is known for its beautiful beach resorts and centuries-old churches.
  • Quezon National Forest Park - Located in nearby Atimonan, this forest park offers a wide range of outdoor activities, including hiking and camping.
  • Lucban - Known for the annual Pahiyas Festival, Lucban is a charming town known for its cultural heritage and colorful decorations.

Conclusion

Tayabas is a city that seamlessly blends history, nature, and culture, making it an exciting and unique destination for any traveler. From exploring ancient churches to taking on thrilling hiking trails, there’s always something to discover in this hidden gem in Quezon province.
