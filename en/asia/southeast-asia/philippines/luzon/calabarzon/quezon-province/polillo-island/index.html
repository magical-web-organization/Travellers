Introduction to Polillo Island

Polillo Island is a hidden gem located in the Philippines’ Quezon province. It belongs to the Calabarzon region, one of the most popular tourist destinations in the country, known for its stunning beaches, lush mountains, and vibrant culture. Polillo Island boasts a more laid-back vibe, making it the perfect destination for those who want to unwind and escape from the city’s chaos.

List of Places to Visit in Polillo Island

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Polillo Island is rich in history, and visitors can appreciate it by visiting its museums and historical places. We recommend these places:

  1. Polillo Island Museum: Learn about the island’s history and culture by visiting the Polillo Island Museum. It houses various artifacts, photos, and documents that showcase the island’s rich past.

  2. St. James the Apostle Parish Church: This church is a must-visit for those who want to witness the island’s religiosity. It dates back to the Spanish colonial era, and its baroque architecture is impressive.

Natural Attractions of Polillo Island: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Polillo Island is blessed with breathtaking natural wonders that have remained unspoiled. Here are some of the must-visit natural attractions in Polillo Island:

  1. Kalanay Cave: One of the most popular attractions in Polillo Island, Kalanay Cave, is a natural wonder that will impress you with its stunning rock formations and clear waters.

  2. Pinaglapatan Falls: This majestic waterfall is a must-visit for nature lovers. It features a 30-feet high waterfall that cascades into a clear lagoon. Visitors can also enjoy swimming and picnicking beside the waterfall.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Polillo Island

Polillo Island is a paradise for hikers and bikers, with its lush mountains and scenic views. Here are some of the best routes to explore:

  1. Mt. Malindig: The highest mountain on the island, Mt. Malindig, is perfect for seasoned hikers who are up for a challenging trek. At its summit, you’ll be rewarded with a breathtaking view of the island and its neighboring islands.

  2. Biking Trails: For bikers, Polillo Island offers various biking trails that range from easy to challenging. You can hire a bike and explore the island’s scenic views.

Best Things to Do in Polillo Island: Activities and Tours

Here are some of the best things to do in Polillo Island:

  1. Island hopping: Explore the island’s stunning beaches and neighboring islands by taking an island hopping tour. You can visit nearby islands like Apuao Grande and Cagbalete Island.

  2. Snorkeling and Diving: Discover Polillo Island’s underwater beauty by going snorkeling or diving. The island is teeming with marine life, including colorful corals and schools of fish.

Week-Long Itinerary for Polillo Island

Here’s a sample week-long itinerary for Polillo Island:

Day 1: Arrival at Polillo Island, check-in at the hotel/hostel/guesthouse, and visit the Polillo Island Museum.

Day 2: Explore Kalanay Cave and Pinaglapatan Falls.

Day 3: Hike Mt. Malindig and witness the stunning panoramic view of the island.

Day 4: Bike around the island and visit nearby fishing villages.

Day 5: Island hopping tour and discover nearby islands like Apuao Grande and Cagbalete Island.

Day 6: Snorkeling and diving to explore the island’s underwater beauty.

Day 7: Departure from Polillo Island.

Food and Drink Scene of Polillo Island

Must-Try Dishes

Polillo Island is known for its fresh seafood and local delicacies. Here are some of the must-try dishes:

  1. Binalot: This is a Filipino dish that consists of rice, various meats or fish, and sometimes vegetables, seasoned with salt or soy sauce, and wrapped in banana leaves. It’s perfect for an on-the-go meal.

  2. Seafood: Polillo Island is famous for its fresh seafood, like shrimp, crabs, and fish. You can buy them from the local markets or try them in one of the island’s many seafood restaurants.

Local Beverages

  1. Lambanog: This is a Filipino alcoholic drink made from coconut sap. It’s a popular drink in Polillo Island and other parts of the Philippines.

  2. Barako Coffee: This is Tagalog for “strong coffee,” and it’s strong indeed! This coffee is made from Liberica coffee beans that are grown in the nearby Batangas province.

Where to Stay in Polillo Island: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Here are some of the best places to stay in Polillo Island:

  1. Sea Breeze Lodge: This is a beachfront guesthouse located in Barangay Polillo. It offers air-conditioned rooms and has a restaurant serving local dishes.

  2. Polillo Island Garden Resort: This beachfront property offers cottages and rooms that are perfect for those who want to stay in nature. It also has a restaurant serving Filipino dishes and a garden where you can relax in.

What to Buy in Polillo Island: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Here are some of the best souvenirs and local products to bring home from Polillo Island:

  1. Bags and Mats Made from Pandan Leaves: These bags and mats are handwoven from pandan leaves, a plant that grows abundantly in the island.

  2. Pearls: Due to its proximity to the famous pearl farms of nearby Palawan, Polillo Island offers high-quality pearls at reasonable prices.

Local Culture and Customs of Polillo Island

Polillo Island is a predominantly Catholic community, and visitors are encouraged to respect the locals’ religious beliefs and customs. The locals are also known for their hospitality and friendliness, so don’t hesitate to strike up a conversation!

Best Time to Visit Polillo Island

The best time to visit Polillo Island is from December to May when the weather is typically dry. However, visitors can still enjoy the island’s beauty year-round, as the temperature rarely drops below 20 degrees Celsius.

Do’s and Don’ts of Polillo Island

Do:

  1. Respect the locals’ customs and beliefs.

  2. Practice responsible tourism, and pack out what you pack in.

Don’t:

  1. Litter or damage the island’s natural wonders.

  2. Disrespect local traditions.

Safety and Health

Safety in Polillo Island

Polillo Island is relatively safe, but visitors should take the necessary precautions. It’s best to avoid traveling alone at night and keep your valuables in a safe place.

Health in Polillo Island

Visitors should drink bottled water and practice good hygiene to avoid any stomach problems. It’s also best to bring mosquito repellents as the island is a breeding ground for mosquitoes.

Getting to Polillo Island: Transportation Options and Logistics

Polillo Island is accessible from Manila by land and sea. Visitors can take a van or bus from Manila to Real, Quezon, and then take a ferry to Polillo Island.

Airports Near Polillo Island

The nearest airport to Polillo Island is Manila’s Ninoy Aquino International Airport.

Bus Stations Near Polillo Island

Buses from Manila to Real, Quezon, depart from Cubao, Pasay, and Buendia bus stations.

Other Options

Some tour operators offer private transfers from Manila to Polillo Island. It’s best to inquire before your trip.

How to Get Around in Polillo Island

The best way to get around Polillo Island is by renting a motorcycle or a tricycle. Visitors can also rent a bike or walk if they prefer.

Where to Go Next from Polillo Island: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Here are some of the nearby destinations that visitors can explore after their trip to Polillo Island:

  1. Lucban, Quezon: This charming town is famous for its Pahiyas Festival, held every May. Visitors can explore its colonial houses, fresh produce markets, and local delicacies.

  2. Baler, Aurora: This town is known for its surfing spots, pristine beaches, and historical sites. It’s perfect for adventure seekers and history buffs alike.

Conclusion

Polillo Island is a beautiful and overlooked destination that visitors should add to their travel bucket list. With its stunning natural beauty, rich history, and warm people, Polillo Island is the perfect destination for those who want to escape the hustle and bustle of city life.
