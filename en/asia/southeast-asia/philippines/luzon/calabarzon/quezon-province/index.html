Introduction to Quezon Province

Quezon Province, named after the late Philippine President Manuel Quezon, is situated in the southern part of Luzon. The province is known for its beautiful white sand beaches, historical landmarks, exciting hiking trails, and rich culinary traditions. Visitors to the province can immerse themselves in the local culture, try delicious local dishes, and indulge in various outdoor activities while exploring the surrounding natural wonders.

List of Places to Visit in Quezon Province

Quezon Province is full of hidden gems from beautiful beaches to historical landmarks. Here’s a list of must-visit places in Quezon Province.

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Quezon Memorial Shrine
  • Jomalig Island Lighthouse
  • Malagunlong Bridge
  • Kamay ni Hesus

Natural Attractions of Quezon Province: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Dampalitan Island
  • Borawan Island
  • Real Quezon River

Hiking/Biking Routes of Quezon Province

Quezon Province is a hiker and biker’s paradise, with numerous trails catering to various levels of difficulty. Whether you want a peaceful nature walk or an adrenaline-pumping adventure, you can find it all here. Some of the popular routes are:

  • Mt. Banahaw via Kinabuhayan Trail
  • Mt. Pinagbanderahan via Barangay Atimonan
  • Mount San Cristobal via Sampaloc Trail

Best Things to Do in Quezon Province: Activities and Tours

While in Quezon Province, there are plenty of fun activities to enjoy. Here are some of the best things to do:

  • Visit the beaches: The province is known for its beautiful white-sand beaches such as Dampalitan and Borawan Island.
  • Island Hopping: Explore the stunning islands of Jomalig and Cagbalete Island
  • Join the Kamay ni Hesus Healing Pilgrimage: This is a religious pilgrimage and a popular activity for many residents and tourists in the area.

Food and Drink Scene of Quezon Province

The culinary scene in Quezon Province is rich in flavor and offers a taste of Filipino culture. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages.

Must-Try Dishes

  • Pancit Habhab: a local noodle dish that is specialty in Lucban
  • Lucban Longganisa: a savory sausage made from native pork in Lucban
  • Suman sa Ibos: sticky rice wrapped in coconut leaves

Local Beverages

  • Lambanog: coconut wine made from the sap of the coconut flower
  • Barako Coffee: a strong coffee sourced from Batangas province

Where to Stay in Quezon Province: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Quezon Province offers various accommodation options catering to different needs and budgets. Some of the best places to stay in the province are:

  • Queen Margaret Lodge
  • The Crystal Beach Resort
  • Costa Pacifica Resort

What to Buy in Quezon Province: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Quezon Province is known for its specialty foods and artisan products. Here are some souvenirs and local products to consider bringing back home:

  • Longganisa: pick up some of the famous Lucban longganisa while in the area
  • Calamay: a sweet rice delicacy originally from the province
  • Hand-woven Bags: woven from natural fibers by artisans in the area

Local Culture and Customs of Quezon Province

Quezon Province is rich in Filipino history and culture, and visitors can learn more by experiencing the local customs, festivals, and traditions. The province is home to many festivals all year round, including the Pahiyas Festival in Lucban and the Kamay ni Hesus Healing Pilgrimage.

Best Time to Visit Quezon Province

The best time to visit Quezon Province is between December and May when the weather is warm and dry with clear skies. If you plan on attending the Pahiyas Festival, it’s held every 15th of May.

Do’s and Don’ts of Quezon Province

Do:

  • Respect the local customs and culture
  • Try the local cuisine
  • Visit the local markets for souvenirs and local products

Don’t:

  • Litter in public places
  • Wear shoes or other footwear inside the Kamay ni Hesus Shrine
  • Use flash photography in the Kamay ni Hesus Shrine

Safety and Health

Safety in Quezon Province

Quezon Province is relatively safe for visitors, but it’s still recommended to take precautions such as avoiding secluded areas at night, keeping valuables out of sight, and being mindful of pickpockets.

Health in Quezon Province

Make sure to drink bottled water and follow good hygiene practices to avoid getting sick. Bring any necessary prescription medications with you, and make sure to have travel insurance.

Getting to Quezon Province: Transportation Options and Logistics

Quezon Province is accessible by various transportation options.

Airports Near Quezon Province:

  • Manila Ninoy Aquino International Airport (MNL)
  • Legazpi International Airport (LGP)

Bus Stations Near Quezon Province:

  • JAC Liner and Jam Transit have daily trips from Manila to Lucena City Bus Terminal

Other Options:

  • Private Car: For a more comfortable and customized trip, hiring a private car or van would be an option.
  • Tricycle: Mode of transport inside some towns of Quezon

How to Get Around in Quezon Province

Getting around Quezon Province is relatively easy with several options for transportation:

  • Bus: The bus is an affordable and popular mode of transport around the province.
  • Jeepneys: These colorful and iconic vehicles offer cheap transportation within the towns of Quezon.
  • Tricycle: Another option for getting around town and more personalized service.

Where to Go Next from Quezon Province: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Quezon Province is an excellent starting point for exploring other parts of Luzon, nearby provinces, and islands such as:

  • Batangas: Known for its beautiful beaches and dive spots.
  • Manila: The capital city of the Philippines is known for its historical landmarks and modern attractions.
  • Marinduque: An island province with pristine beaches, unspoiled nature, and traditional festivals.

Conclusion

Quezon Province is a must-visit destination for those looking for a mix of cultural experiences, natural wonders, delicious cuisine, and adventure activities. Plan your trip using this comprehensive guide, and you’re sure to have an unforgettable experience in this beautiful province.
