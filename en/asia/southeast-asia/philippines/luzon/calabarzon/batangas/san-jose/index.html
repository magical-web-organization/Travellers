San Jose is a popular destination in Batangas, known for its natural and cultural attractions. From hiking and biking to exploring museums and historical landmarks, there is plenty to do in this vibrant city. In this comprehensive tour guide, we’ll cover everything you need to know about San Jose, including the best places to visit, where to stay, and what to eat.

Introduction to San Jose

San Jose is a first-class municipality in the province of Batangas, located in the CALABARZON region of Luzon, in the Philippines. It is accessible via the Southern Tagalog Arterial Road (STAR Tollway) and the South Luzon Expressway (SLEX). San Jose is home to a number of natural and cultural attractions, making it a popular destination for adventure seekers and history buffs alike.

List of Places to Visit in San Jose

When visiting San Jose, there are plenty of places to explore.

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

San Jose has a rich history, and visitors can learn about the town’s past by visiting its museums and historical landmarks. Here are some must-visit cultural attractions in San Jose:

  • San Jose Municipal Museum: located inside the Municipal Hall, this museum showcases artifacts and memorabilia related to the town’s history.
  • San Jose Church: also known as the St. Joseph the Worker Parish Church, the San Jose Church was built in 1969 and is known for its unique structure and design.
  • World War II Memorial Shrine: located in Barangay Sala, this shrine honors the soldiers who died during World War II and also serves as a historical landmark.

Natural Attractions of San Jose: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

For nature lovers, San Jose is a paradise. Here are some of the natural attractions you can visit:

  • Malajibomanok Ridge: this attraction is popular for its scenic views and hiking trails that lead to Talaibon Falls.
  • Kaybiang Tunnel: located at the boundary of San Jose and Ternate, Cavite, this tunnel offers a picturesque view of the Batangas and Cavite coasts.
  • Malaking Bundok: also known as Mount Malipunyo, this mountain is a popular hiking destination in San Jose. It features a rainforest and several waterfalls.

Hiking/Biking Routes of San Jose

San Jose is a haven for outdoor enthusiasts. Here are some of the popular hiking and biking routes you can explore:

  • Malajibomanok Ridge to Talaibon Falls: this hiking route is known for its scenic views of mountains, rice paddies, and waterfalls.
  • Malaking Bundok Trail: this trail leads to the peak of Mount Malipunyo, offering panoramic views of Batangas.
  • San Jose Bike Route: this bike route covers a distance of 15km and takes bikers through the town proper and barangays of San Jose.

Best Things to Do in San Jose: Activities and Tours

Aside from hiking and biking, there are plenty of other things to do in San Jose. Here are some popular activities and tours:

  • Visit the local market: San Jose’s market is bustling with activity, offering visitors a chance to sample local food and products.
  • Try a “shakoy” (deep-fried doughnut): this popular local delicacy is crispy on the outside and soft on the inside.
  • Enjoy a boat tour of Taal Lake: Taal Lake is a beautiful natural attraction and visitors can explore it by boat.

Week-Long Itinerary for San Jose

If you have a week to spend in San Jose, here is a suggested itinerary:

Day 1-2: Explore San Jose’s cultural attractions, including the San Jose Municipal Museum and World War II Memorial Shrine.

Day 3-4: Go hiking or biking along Malajibomanok Ridge or Malaking Bundok.

Day 5-6: Enjoy a boat tour of Taal Lake and visit Kaybiang Tunnel.

Day 7: Shop for souvenirs and local products at San Jose’s market.

Food and Drink Scene of San Jose

San Jose is also known for its delicious food and drinks. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Adobo sa Dilaw: a delicious chicken or pork adobo cooked with turmeric.
  • Sinaing na Tulingan: a Batangas specialty made by simmering tulingan (mackerel tuna) with salt, vinegar, and spices.
  • Bulalo: a hearty beef soup flavored with bone marrow.

Local Beverages

  • Kapeng Barako: a strong, aromatic coffee made from locally-grown Liberica beans.
  • Lambanog: a local alcoholic drink made from distilled coconut sap.

Where to Stay in San Jose: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

San Jose has a range of accommodation options to suit different budgets and preferences. Here are some of the best places to stay:

  • Microtel by Wyndham Sto. Tomas Batangas: a modern hotel located in nearby Sto. Tomas, Batangas.
  • Villa Crisanta Garden Resort: a laid-back resort with an outdoor pool and garden.
  • De La Salle Lipa Hostel: a budget-friendly hostel located in nearby Lipa City.

What to Buy in San Jose: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

San Jose is known for its handicrafts and local delicacies. Here are some of the best souvenirs and local products to bring home:

  • Balisong (butterfly knife): a traditional Filipino knife made in Batangas.
  • Barong Tagalog: a formal, embroidered shirt made from pineapple fiber or silk.
  • Tablea (chocolate tablets): a Batangas specialty made from roasted cacao beans that can be used to make hot chocolate.

Local Culture and Customs of San Jose

San Jose is a predominantly Catholic town, and visitors are expected to dress modestly when visiting churches. It is also customary to remove footwear before entering a Filipino home.

Best Time to Visit San Jose

The best time to visit San Jose is during the dry season from November to April, when the weather is cooler and there is less chance of rainfall. The months of May to October are the rainy season in the Philippines, and visitors should expect heavy rainfall during these months.

Do’s and Don’ts of San Jose

When visiting San Jose, here are some do’s and don’ts to keep in mind:

Do:

  • Respect local customs and traditions.
  • Dress modestly when visiting churches.
  • Try local food and drinks.

Don’t:

  • Litter in public places.
  • Insult or disrespect locals.
  • Neglect to tip service providers such as waiters or tour guides.

Safety and Health

San Jose is a relatively safe destination for tourists. However, visitors should still exercise caution and take the usual safety measures. Here are some tips for staying safe and healthy in San Jose:

Safety in San Jose

  • Avoid walking alone at night.
  • Keep your valuables secure and out of sight.
  • Be wary of scams and pickpockets.

Health in San Jose

  • Drink only bottled or boiled water.
  • Apply mosquito repellent if necessary to avoid mosquito-borne diseases such as dengue fever.
  • Seek medical attention if you experience any symptoms of illness.

Getting to San Jose: Transportation Options and Logistics

San Jose is accessible via various transportation options. Here are some of the best ways to get to San Jose:

Airports Near San Jose

  • Ninoy Aquino International Airport (MNL): located in Manila, this is the nearest international airport to San Jose.
  • Daniel Z. Romualdez Airport (TAC): this airport is located in Tacloban City, Leyte, and is approximately 270km away from San Jose.

Bus Stations Near San Jose

  • Jam Liner: this bus liner has trips to Batangas City from Cubao and Buendia in Metro Manila.
  • DLTB Co.: this bus liner has trips to Batangas City from Pasay and Buendia in Metro Manila.

Other Options

  • Private transfers: you can arrange for a private transfer from Manila to San Jose.
  • Car rental: you can rent a car and drive to San Jose yourself.

How to Get Around in San Jose

The easiest way to get around San Jose is by tricycle, a small three-wheeled vehicle that can seat up to three passengers. Jeepneys and buses are also available for longer distances.

Where to Go Next from San Jose: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

San Jose is located near some of the Philippines’ most popular destinations. Here are some nearby destinations and excursions you can visit:

  • Batangas City: located approximately 23km away, Batangas City is known for its beaches, dive sites, and historic landmarks.
  • Taal Volcano: located in nearby Batangas province, Taal Volcano is a popular tourist destination known for its scenic views and hiking trails.
  • Tagaytay City: located approximately 35km away, Tagaytay is a popular destination for its cool weather and scenic views of Taal Lake and Volcano.

Conclusion

San Jose is a must-visit destination for anyone traveling to Batangas. With its cultural and natural attractions, delicious food and drink, and easy accessibility, this lively city has something for everyone. Use this comprehensive tour guide as a resource for planning your next trip to San Jose.
