Introduction to Lian

Located in western Batangas, Lian is a coastal town that offers a perfect blend of history, culture, and natural beauty. Lian is known for its unspoiled beaches, crystal clear waters, and stunning landscapes. It’s a perfect destination for adventurers, history buffs, foodies, and travelers looking for an off-the-beaten-path experience in the Philippines.

This comprehensive tour guide will take you on a journey through Lian’s culturally important attractions, natural wonders, outdoor adventures, and must-try dishes.

List of Places to Visit in Lian

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Lian Museum – Discover the town’s rich history through the museum’s extensive collection of artifacts, photographs, and memorabilia. The museum showcases various aspects of Lian’s culture, including traditional costumes, agriculture, and religion.
  • San Sebastian Church – Built-in 1831, this church is a prime example of a Spanish colonial-era architecture. The church is adorned with intricate wood carvings, antique furniture, and stained glass windows. This church is not only a religious site, but it also holds a historical significance that reflects the town’s past.

Natural Attractions of Lian: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Malaking River – This river is best known for its enchanting beauty, surrounded by lush greenery and clear water. It’s a perfect spot for canoeing, kayaking, and fishing.
  • Binubusan Beach – If you’re looking for a secluded beach with crystal clear waters, Binubusan Beach is the place to be. It’s a peaceful spot with white sand, turquoise water, and a serene atmosphere.
  • Mount Palay-Palay National Park – This 25,000-hectare national park is known for its scenic views of Taal Lake and the Batangas Bay. It’s a great spot for hiking, camping, bird-watching, and nature photography.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Lian

Lian is surrounded by natural beauty and offers beautiful trails for hiking and biking enthusiasts. Here are some of the popular routes:

  • Mount Pico de Loro – This mountain is one of the top hiking destinations in the Philippines. It offers a challenging yet rewarding climb with stunning views of the surrounding landscape.
  • Malapad na Bundok – This mountain is a popular choice for beginner hikers as it offers a less challenging climb. It’s a great spot for bird-watching and nature photography.
  • Binubusan Trail – This trail takes you through the lush greenery of Binubusan. It’s a perfect spot for biking and hiking, surrounded by breathtaking views of the sea.

Best Things to Do in Lian: Activities and Tours

  • Island Hopping – Explore the nearby islands, such as Tingloy and Sombrero Island, through an organized tour.
  • Snorkeling and Diving – Discover the colorful underwater world of Lian through snorkeling and diving adventures.
  • Food Trip – Lian is a foodie’s paradise, with a diverse range of delicious dishes to try. From seafood to the traditional Filipino delicacies, the town has it all.

Week-Long Itinerary for Lian

Day 1: Arrival and Check-in at a hotel. Day 2: Explore the town’s history and culture with a visit to the Lian Museum and San Sebastian Church. Day 3: Hike Mount Pico de Loro and enjoy the stunning views. Day 4: Island-hopping tour and snorkeling adventure. Day 5: Visit Binubusan Beach and relax on the tranquil sands. Day 6: Food trip and local markets visit. Day 7: Departure and return journey.

Food and Drink Scene of Lian

Must-Try Dishes

  • Adobong Balaw – This dish is a local delicacy where shrimp paste is used instead of soy sauce. It’s stir-fried with garlic, onion, and meat.
  • Litson Manok na Lian – It’s a roasted chicken that’s marinated with secret spices and herbs. It’s juicy and flavorful, a must-try for all foodies.
  • Tawilis – This small fish is abundant in Taal Lake and is a must-try for seafood lovers. It’s crispy-fried and served with vinegar dip.

Local Beverages

  • Barako Coffee – This strong coffee is a famous product of Batangas and is a perfect morning fix. The coffee beans are grown in the highlands of Batangas and have a distinct, bold flavor.
  • Lambanog – It’s a local coconut wine made from the sap of the coconut tree. It’s potent and has a smooth taste that’s bound to excite your taste buds.

Where to Stay in Lian: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Batangas Country Club – It’s a luxury resort that offers comfortable rooms, excellent service, and various amenities, including a golf course, swimming pool, and spa.
  • Kubli Springs Resort – It’s a rustic resort that offers affordable rooms surrounded by lush greenery and breathtaking natural views.
  • Liantie’s Inn and Restaurant – This inn offers clean and comfortable rooms and the added bonus of delightful cuisine in their restaurant.

What to Buy in Lian: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Tapa Lian – It’s dried beef that’s thinly sliced and is a popular breakfast dish in Lian. It’s a good local product to take back home.
  • Handwoven Fabrics – Lian is known for its colorful and beautiful handwoven fabrics. It’s a perfect souvenir to remember your trip to Lian.
  • Handmade Wooden Crafts – The town has skilled craftsmen who make handmade wooden crafts, such as bowls, spoons, and intricate figurines.

Local Culture and Customs of Lian

Lian is a small town with an overwhelmingly friendly and warm community. The locals are proud of their heritage, culture, and traditions. They’re hospitable and welcoming to visitors and embrace travelers with open arms.

Best Time to Visit Lian

The best time to visit Lian is from November to April. These months offer a pleasant temperature, clear skies, and lesser rain, making it perfect for outdoor adventures.

Do’s and Don’ts of Lian

Do:

  • Respect the local culture, customs, and traditions.
  • Try the delicious local cuisine and explore the town’s food scene.
  • Embrace the natural beauty and enjoy the outdoor adventures.

Don’t:

  • Disrespect the local community or their beliefs.
  • Litter the environment.
  • Disrupt the natural habitats and scenic areas.

Safety and Health

Safety in Lian

Lian is a generally safe town. There are no major safety concerns to worry about, but it’s always good to take precautions when exploring outdoors.

Health in Lian

Make sure to pack necessary medication and to stay hydrated when trekking or participating in outdoor activities. If you have a pre-existing medical condition, please consult with your doctor before leaving.

Getting to Lian: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Lian

The nearest airport to Lian is Ninoy Aquino International Airport (MNL), Manila. From there, you can take a bus, car, or van to Lian, which takes around three hours.

Bus Stations Near Lian

  • Cubao Bus Terminal – Quezon City, Manila, via South Luzon Expressway (SLEX).
  • Alabang Bus Terminal – Muntinlupa City, Manila, via SLEX.

Other Options

  • Van or Car Rental – You can rent a van or car to take you directly to Lian.
  • Private Car – If you’re driving, you can take the SLEX and exit towards Nasugbu, Batangas. It’s a two-hour drive from Manila.

How to Get Around in Lian

The best way to get around in Lian is through tricycles or jeepneys. Tricycles are available in the town center and can take you to nearby attractions, while jeepneys connect Lian to the neighboring towns.

Where to Go Next from Lian: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Nasugbu – It’s a nearby town known for its pristine beaches, coves, and hiking trails.
  • Taal Heritage Town – It’s a UNESCO-listed town that reflects the Philippines’ rich architectural and cultural heritage.
  • Tagaytay – It’s a popular tourist destination known for its cool weather, scenic views, and famous dishes.

Conclusion

Lian, Batangas, is a hidden gem that offers travelers a unique cultural and natural experience. From outdoor adventures to delicious local cuisine, Lian has something for everyone. Use this comprehensive tour guide to plan your trip and get ready for an unforgettable experience in one of the Philippines’ best-kept secret destinations.
