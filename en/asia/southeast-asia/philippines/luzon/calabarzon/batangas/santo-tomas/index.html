Introduction to Santo Tomas

Santo Tomas is a first-class municipality in Batangas, Philippines. It is one of the fastest-growing municipalities in the province and serves as a gateway to other tourist destinations in Batangas. It is known for its delicious food, scenic landscapes, and friendly locals.

List of Places to Visit in Santo Tomas

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

There are several culturally important attractions in Santo Tomas that will help you understand its rich history and heritage. Some of the must-visit places include:

  • Tambo Lake - a scenic lake surrounded by mountains, and a popular spot for picnics, swimming, and fishing.
  • Sto. Tomas de Aquino Parish Church - a beautiful church that dates back to the 16th century and is renowned for its impressive baroque architecture.
  • Rizal Shrine - a historical landmark dedicated to the Philippine National Hero, Jose Rizal, and is home to various artifacts and memorabilia from his life.

Natural Attractions of Santo Tomas: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Santo Tomas is blessed with stunning natural scenery and landscapes, perfect for outdoor enthusiasts and nature lovers. Here are some of the best natural attractions in Santo Tomas:

  • Mt. Banahaw - a dormant volcano that is considered a sacred place by the locals. It is a popular destination for hikers and climbers, and you’ll be rewarded with breathtaking panoramic views from the summit.
  • Kaytitinga Falls - a beautiful waterfall surrounded by lush vegetation, and a great place to cool off and take a refreshing swim.
  • Malaking Krus - a scenic viewpoint that offers a panoramic view of Santo Tomas and the surrounding mountains.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Santo Tomas

Santo Tomas is a great place for hiking and biking, with several scenic routes to explore. Some of the most popular routes include:

  • Mt. Banahaw Summit Trail - a challenging but rewarding hike that takes you to the summit of Mt. Banahaw.
  • Kayumanggi Trail - a scenic bike trail that offers breathtaking views of the countryside.
  • Pinagsanjan Falls Trail - a beautiful hike that takes you through lush forests and scenic waterfalls.

Best Things to Do in Santo Tomas: Activities and Tours

If you’re looking for things to do in Santo Tomas, there are plenty of options to keep you busy. Here are some of the best activities and tours to enjoy:

  • Heritage Tour - a tour of the historic sites and landmarks of Santo Tomas, including the St. Thomas Aquinas Parish Church and the Rizal Shrine.
  • Mt. Banahaw Hike - a challenging hike to the summit of Mt. Banahaw, with breathtaking views of the surrounding landscape.
  • Kaytitinga Falls Tour - a tour of the picturesque Kaytitinga Falls, with opportunities for swimming and picnicking.

Week-Long Itinerary for Santo Tomas

If you’re planning a week-long trip to Santo Tomas, here’s a suggested itinerary:

Day 1 - Explore the cultural attractions of Santo Tomas, including the St. Thomas Aquinas Parish Church and Rizal Shrine.

Day 2 - Take a day trip to the nearby Kaytitinga Falls for some swimming and picnicking.

Day 3 - Go hiking up Mt. Banahaw for stunning views of the surrounding landscape.

Day 4 - Rent a bike and take a leisurely ride through the scenic countryside.

Day 5 - Enjoy a heritage tour of Santo Tomas and learn about its rich history and culture.

Day 6 - Relax and unwind at the Tambo Lake, a popular spot for picnics and fishing.

Day 7 - Shop for souvenirs and locally-made products for friends and family back home.

Food and Drink Scene of Santo Tomas

Santo Tomas is famous for its delicious food and drink scene, with plenty of local dishes and beverages to try. Here are some must-try dishes and beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Adobo - a popular Filipino dish made with meat cooked in vinegar, soy sauce, and garlic.
  • Lomi - a traditional noodle soup made with thick egg noodles and various meat and vegetable toppings.
  • Bulalo - a hearty and flavorful beef soup made with bone marrow and vegetables.

Local Beverages

  • Barako Coffee - a strong and bold coffee made from local Liberica beans. A must-try for coffee lovers.
  • Lambanog - a potent coconut wine that is often served during special occasions and celebrations.
  • Sago’t Gulaman - a refreshing drink made with tapioca pearls, jelly, and syrup.

Where to Stay in Santo Tomas: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Santo Tomas has various options for accommodation, from budget-friendly hostels to luxurious hotels. Here are some suggestions:

  • Microtel by Wyndham Sto. Tomas - a highly-rated, mid-range hotel with amenities like an outdoor pool and fitness center.
  • Shercon Resort and Ecology Park - a nature resort that offers various accommodations from traditional Filipino huts to hotel rooms.
  • Papaya Lodge and Restaurant - a budget-friendly option with bunk beds and basic amenities.

What to Buy in Santo Tomas: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Santo Tomas offers a wide range of souvenirs and locally-made products that make perfect gifts for friends and family back home. Some of the best things to buy include:

  • Barako Coffee - a popular beverage made from locally-grown Liberica beans.
  • Handicrafts - beautifully-crafted handicrafts made by local artisans, such as bamboo baskets, wooden figurines, and hand-woven fabrics.
  • Pasalubong - locally-made snacks and delicacies such as yema, polvoron, and bibingka.

Local Culture and Customs of Santo Tomas

The people of Santo Tomas are warm and friendly, and the town has a strong sense of community. Filipinos value respect and politeness, so it’s important to be mindful of your actions and words when interacting with locals.

Best Time to Visit Santo Tomas

The best time to visit Santo Tomas is during the dry season between December to May when the weather is mild and sunny. June to November is the rainy season and can sometimes be prone to flooding and typhoons.

Do’s and Don’ts of Santo Tomas

Do:

  • Be respectful of the local culture and customs.
  • Learn a few basic Tagalog phrases to communicate better with locals.
  • Try the local food and drinks.

Don’t:

  • Litter or damage the environment.
  • Be disrespectful or rude to locals.
  • Wear revealing clothing when visiting religious places.

Safety and Health

Safety in Santo Tomas

Santo Tomas is generally safe, but it’s always important to take precautions when traveling. Here are some safety tips:

  • Keep your valuables secured and out of sight.
  • Be aware of your surroundings, especially at night.
  • Avoid walking alone in unlit or unfamiliar areas.

Health in Santo Tomas

Tap water in Santo Tomas is not potable, so it’s always best to drink bottled water. Mosquito-borne diseases such as dengue fever are also prevalent, so it’s advisable to apply insect repellent when outdoors.

Getting to Santo Tomas: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Santo Tomas

The nearest airport to Santo Tomas is Ninoy Aquino International Airport in Manila, which is approximately 100 kilometers away. From there, you can either rent a car or take a bus to Santo Tomas.

Bus Stations Near Santo Tomas

Several bus companies offer trips to Santo Tomas from different parts of the country, including Metro Manila, Cavite, and Laguna. Some popular bus lines include JAM Liner, Green Star Express, and Silver Star.

Other Options

You can also hire a car or take a taxi from Manila to Santo Tomas, but it’s always best to negotiate the fare beforehand.

How to Get Around in Santo Tomas

The best way to get around Santo Tomas is by tricycle or jeepney, which are the most common modes of transportation in the town. You can also rent a car or motorcycle if you prefer to explore on your own.

Where to Go Next from Santo Tomas: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Santo Tomas serves as a great base for exploring other tourist destinations in Batangas and nearby provinces. Here are some nearby destinations and excursions:

  • Laiya Beach - a popular beach destination known for its white sand and crystal-clear waters.
  • Taal Volcano - a popular hiking destination that offers breathtaking views of the Taal Lake and surrounding areas.
  • Mahogany Market - a bustling marketplace that offers local delicacies, souvenirs, and pasalubong.

Conclusion

Santo Tomas is a must-visit destination in Batangas, Philippines, with its beautiful scenery, rich culture, and delicious cuisine. Whether you’re a nature enthusiast or culture buff, there’s something for everyone in Santo Tomas.
