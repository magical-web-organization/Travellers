Introduction to Tanauan

Located in Batangas, in Calabarzon, in Luzon, in the Philippines, in Southeast Asia, Tanauan is a bustling city that has a lot to offer to visitors. Its rich history and culture mix with natural beauty, making it an ideal destination for those seeking adventure and relaxation.

List of Places to Visit in Tanauan

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Casa Consuelo: a restored ancestral house that showcases the traditional architecture of the Batangas region.
  • Apacible Museum: a museum that displays the personal collections of former Philippine Senator Francisco “Paco” Apacible.
  • Tanauan Museum: showcases the city’s culture and history from pre-colonial times to the present day.
  • Basa Compound: one of the oldest residential compounds in the Philippines, featuring traditional Filipino architecture.

Natural Attractions of Tanauan: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Taft Avenue: a promenade along the shores of Taal Lake with scenic views of Taal Volcano.
  • Mount Makiling: a dormant volcano with several hiking trails available for all skill levels.
  • Pulong Bato: a popular destination for rock climbing and hiking. The summit offers panoramic views of the city and nearby areas.
  • Tanauan City Nature Park: features several walking trails, a butterfly garden, and a gazebo for picnics.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Tanauan

  • Mount Makiling Trail: a 15-kilometer trail that takes you through lush forests and offers a chance to spot different bird species.
  • Monte Maria Pilgrimage Trail: a challenging 6-kilometer trek up steep hills to reach the Monte Maria Chapel at the summit.
  • Pulong Bato Trail: a moderately difficult 5-kilometer hike through a rocky terrain that rewards hikers with a stunning view of the city.

Best Things to Do in Tanauan: Activities and Tours

  • Food tour: A visit to Tanauan is not complete without a food tour to the city’s famous restaurants and food establishments.
  • Visit local farms: a chance to experience farm life and learn about local agriculture and food production.
  • Join a heritage tour: explore the rich history and culture of Tanauan through visits to its museums and historical landmarks.

Food and Drink Scene of Tanauan

Must-Try Dishes

  • Lomi: a noodle soup dish that originated from Batangas. It is a hearty meal with thick egg noodles, meat, and vegetables in a thick broth.
  • Kakanin: a variety of rice cakes made with rice flour, coconut milk, and sugar. Examples include bibingka, puto, and kutsinta.
  • Tapa: thin slices of beef or pork that are marinated and then fried or grilled. It is usually served with garlic rice and egg.

Local Beverages

  • Kapeng Barako: a strong and aromatic Philippine coffee that is grown in Batangas.
  • Lambanog: a potent alcoholic beverage made from coconut sap.
  • Calamansi Juice: a refreshing drink made from the local Philippine citrus fruit.

Where to Stay in Tanauan: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Casa Consuelo: a restored ancestral house that offers bed and breakfast accommodations in a traditional Filipino setting.
  • Asiana Hotel: a modern hotel with comfortable rooms and amenities, including a swimming pool and restaurant.
  • Santomas Suites: a budget-friendly hotel with clean and spacious rooms.

What to Buy in Tanauan: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Barong Tagalog: a traditional Filipino shirt made from pineapple leaf fibers or jusi fabric.
  • Kakanin: bring home some of the delicious rice cakes that are sold in many small shops and markets in Tanauan.
  • Tawilis: a freshwater sardine that is only found in Taal Lake. It is a popular and delicious exotic delicacy.

Local Culture and Customs of Tanauan

  • Religion: Tanauan is a predominantly Catholic city, and the locals take their religious traditions and practices seriously.
  • Language: Tagalog is the official language of the Philippines, and it is widely spoken in Tanauan. English is also commonly used.
  • Respect for elders: Filipinos value respect for their elders and authority figures. Visitors are expected to show respect to the locals and their customs.

Best Time to Visit Tanauan

The best time to visit Tanauan is from November to March when the weather is dry and cool. It is also the season for many fiestas and celebrations, which offer visitors a chance to immerse themselves in the local culture.

Do’s and Don’ts of Tanauan

Do:

  • Respect local customs: Filipinos are friendly and hospitable, but visitors should always be mindful of the local customs and traditions.
  • Be polite: Filipinos value politeness and courtesy, so visitors should always be respectful and greet people properly.
  • Be adventurous: Tanauan offers many opportunities for adventure, so visitors should be willing to try new things and explore the city.

Don’t:

  • Haggle too aggressively: Filipinos are known for their bargaining skills, but visitors should be mindful not to be too aggressive when haggling.
  • Disrespect religious sites and traditions: Visitors should avoid wearing revealing clothing or showing disrespect in any way when visiting religious sites or participating in religious traditions.
  • Litter or damage the environment: Visitors should always be mindful of the environment and avoid littering or damaging the natural beauty of Tanauan.

Safety and Health

Safety in Tanauan

Tanauan is generally a safe city for visitors. However, visitors should always be mindful of their surroundings and avoid isolated areas, particularly at night. Visitors should also keep their valuables safe and be aware of pickpockets and scams.

Health in Tanauan

Visitors should make sure to drink only bottled water and avoid consuming food from questionable sources. It is also recommended to bring insect repellent, as dengue fever is prevalent in some areas of the Philippines.

Getting to Tanauan: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Tanauan

The nearest airport to Tanauan is Ninoy Aquino International Airport, which is approximately 65 kilometers away.

Bus Stations Near Tanauan

There are several bus stations in Manila that offer trips to Tanauan, such as Cubao and Pasay.

Other Options

Visitors can also hire a private car or van to take them to Tanauan from Manila, although this may be more expensive than taking public transportation.

How to Get Around in Tanauan

Jeepneys, tricycles, and buses are the most common modes of transportation in Tanauan. Taxis and private cars are also available, but they may be more expensive.

Where to Go Next from Tanauan: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Taal: a heritage town that is home to several historical landmarks, such as the Basilica of St. Martin de Tours and the Taal Heritage Village.
  • Lipa City: known for its coffee plantations, Lipa City is an ideal destination for those interested in learning about local coffee production.
  • Bauan: a small town with several beaches that offer great opportunities for swimming, snorkeling, and diving.

Conclusion

Tanauan is a city that offers a unique mix of culture, adventure, and natural beauty. With this comprehensive guide, visitors can plan their trip and experience the best that Tanauan has to offer. From hiking trails to food tours, Tanauan is a destination that is sure to leave a lasting impression.
