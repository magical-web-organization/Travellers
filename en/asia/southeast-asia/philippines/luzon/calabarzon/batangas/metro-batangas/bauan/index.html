Introduction to Bauan

Bauan, located in Metro Batangas, in Batangas, in Calabarzon, in Luzon, in the Philippines is a small coastal town that boasts of breathtaking natural wonders, rich cultural heritage, and an array of exciting activities for tourists. It is best known for its religious sites, pristine beaches, and hiking trails with stunning views of the surrounding landscape.

List of Places to Visit in Bauan

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Bauan has an interesting history, and its remnants can still be seen in the town’s various historical sites. The most remarkable of these are:

  • Bauan Museum: This museum is located in the ancestral house of a prominent politician and businessman. It showcases different artifacts, paintings, and photographs that vividly depict the town’s history, culture, and way of life.
  • Bauan Heritage Park: This park is a vast expanse of land dotted with historical sites that hark back to the Spanish colonial period. Its most popular attractions are the Bauan Church and the Holy Family Hills, which offer a great view of the surrounding areas.
  • Mabini Shrine: This shrine is a national museum dedicated to Apolinario Mabini, a revolutionary hero who played a significant role in the Philippines’ struggle for independence against the Spanish and American colonial powers.

Natural Attractions of Bauan: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Bauan’s natural beauty is unmatched, and it’s a perfect destination for nature lovers. Here are some of the most scenic and adventurous attractions to visit:

  • Mt. Gulugod Baboy: This mountain is a jewel in Bauan’s crown, and it offers excellent hiking trails with stunning views of the surrounding landscape. It is also a great spot for camping and stargazing.
  • Tingloy Island: Located off the coast of Bauan, Tingloy Island is a haven for beach lovers, with sparkling turquoise waters and a pristine coastline. It’s great for snorkeling and scuba diving.
  • Pulong Ni Pulo: Pulong Ni Pulo is a hidden gem that offers a unique glimpse into Bauan’s natural beauty. It’s a small island that can be accessed via a boat ride from the town’s port.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Bauan

Bauan is a hiker’s paradise, and there are many routes to choose from depending on your level of experience and stamina. Some of the most popular hiking and biking trails are:

  • Mt. Gulugod Baboy: This mountain is a top-rated destination for hikers and bikers who want to experience the beauty of Bauan’s landscape. The trails are well-marked, making it easy for beginners to hike and bike to the summit.
  • Mt. Maculot: Located a few kilometers away from Bauan, this mountain offers both challenging and easy trails. The trails offer breathtaking views of the surrounding landscape, making it a great spot for photography enthusiasts.

Best Things to Do in Bauan: Activities and Tours

Bauan offers an array of activities and tours that cater to the diverse interests of travelers. Here are the best things to do in Bauan:

  • Island Hopping: Take a boat ride and explore the nearby islands, such as Tingloy Island and Sombrero Island.
  • Snorkeling and Scuba Diving: Dive into the sparkling waters of Tingloy Island and witness the beauty of Bauan’s underwater world.
  • Religious Pilgrimage: Visit the town’s religious sites, such as the Bauan Church and the Holy Family Hills, and attend a mass or join a pilgrimage.
  • Food and Drink Tour: Sample Bauan’s local delicacies and beverages, such as Batangas longganisa, tapa, and kapeng barako.

Food and Drink Scene of Bauan

Must-Try Dishes

Bauan’s food scene is as rich and diverse as its culture. Here are the must-try dishes in Bauan:

  • Batangas Longganisa: A locally-made sausage that is typically paired with garlic fried rice and a fried egg. It’s a breakfast staple in Bauan.
  • Tapa: Thinly-sliced beef or pork that is marinated and then fried. It’s usually served with garlic fried rice and a fried egg.
  • Bulalo: A savory beef stew served hot and best enjoyed on a rainy day.

Local Beverages

Bauan’s beverage scene is dominated by Kapeng Barako, a type of coffee that is grown exclusively in Batangas. It is known for its strong and bold flavor, perfect for jumpstarting your day.

Where to Stay in Bauan: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Bauan has an array of accommodation options that cater to different budgets and preferences. Here are some of the best places to stay in Bauan:

  • Sea Spring Resort: A beachfront resort that offers comfortable rooms, great amenities, and excellent service.
  • Bauan Divers Sanctuary: A cozy and rustic resort that offers excellent diving services and has a great restaurant.
  • Villa Jovita: A luxurious resort that boasts of beautiful rooms, top-notch facilities, and a great spa.

What to Buy in Bauan: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Bauan is known for its excellent agricultural produce, such as coffee, honey, and fruits. It’s also a great place to buy souvenirs that showcase the town’s rich cultural heritage. Here are some of the best souvenirs and local products to take home:

  • Kapeng Barako: A bag of Batangas’ signature coffee.
  • Honey: Pure and organic honey that comes straight from Bauan’s bee farms.
  • Handicrafts: Buy indigenous handicrafts, such as woven baskets, abaca bags, and trinkets that showcase the town’s culture and customs.

Local Culture and Customs of Bauan

Bauan is a predominantly Catholic town, and its religious traditions are deeply ingrained in its culture and customs. It celebrates various festivals throughout the year, such as the Feast of St. Anne, the town’s patron saint, and the Holy Week.

Best Time to Visit Bauan

Bauan is a year-round destination, but the best time to visit is from November to February when the weather is cooler and drier. However, if you want to witness the town’s festivities, visit during the Feast of St. Anne, which is celebrated on the last week of July.

Do’s and Don’ts of Bauan

Do:

  • Respect the town’s religious traditions and practices.
  • Sample Bauan’s local delicacies and beverages.
  • Be mindful of your surroundings and keep it clean.

Don’t:

  • Disrespect the town’s religious practices and traditions.
  • Litter or vandalize historic sites and natural attractions.
  • Overstay your visa or engage in illegal activities.

Safety and Health

Safety in Bauan

Bauan is generally a safe and peaceful town, but it’s important to take precautions and be vigilant, especially when exploring natural attractions. Here are some safety tips to keep in mind:

  • Always bring a map and follow hiking trails.
  • Don’t leave your valuables unattended.
  • Get travel insurance that covers outdoor activities.

Health in Bauan

Bauan has several hospitals and clinics that offer medical services, but it’s still best to bring your own medicines and first-aid kit. It’s also important to stay hydrated and wear sunscreen when exploring outdoor attractions.

Getting to Bauan: Transportation Options and Logistics

Bauan is easily accessible from Manila, and there are several options for transportation:

Airports Near Bauan

The nearest airport to Bauan is Ninoy Aquino International Airport in Manila, which is approximately two hours away by car or bus.

Bus Stations Near Bauan

Bauan has a bus terminal that connects it to various destinations in the country, such as Manila, Batangas City, and Mindoro.

Other Options

There are also several vans and jeepneys that ply the routes to Bauan from Batangas City and the nearby towns.

How to Get Around in Bauan

Tricycles and jeepneys are the most common modes of transportation in Bauan. They are cheap and readily available, making it easy for tourists to explore the town’s attractions.

Where to Go Next from Bauan: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Bauan is a great starting point for exploring nearby destinations and excursions. Here are some of the best places to visit:

  • Batangas City: This city is known for its rich history, cultural landmarks, and excellent beaches.
  • Laiya: A beach destination that is popular among locals for its long coastline, pristine waters, and exciting water activities.
  • Tagaytay: A popular destination for its stunning views of Taal Volcano, cold weather, and charming restaurants and cafes.

Conclusion

Bauan, with its pristine beaches, rich cultural heritage, and adventurous activities, is a hidden gem in Metro Batangas that is brimming with charm and beauty. This comprehensive travel guide has highlighted the best places to visit, things to do, food and drink scene, where to stay, tips, and more to help you plan your trip to this enchanting town.
