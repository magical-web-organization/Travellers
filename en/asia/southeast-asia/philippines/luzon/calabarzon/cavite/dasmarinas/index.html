Introduction to Dasmariñas

Dasmariñas is a first-class municipality located in the province of Cavite, Calabarzon, in the Philippines. It is considered as one of the fastest-growing municipalities in the province, and it has become a hub for commercial and residential developments in the past few years. Despite the urban growth, Dasmariñas still boasts rich history and culture, as well as natural and man-made attractions that cater to all kinds of travelers.

List of Places to Visit in Dasmariñas

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Dasmariñas has several historical and cultural landmarks that travelers can visit to learn more about the town’s past and local traditions.

  1. Museo De La Salle – This museum is located inside the De La Salle University-Dasmariñas and showcases the Philippines’ natural history, culture, and art. It also features the personal collections of the school’s founder, St. John Baptist de La Salle.

  2. Bahay Tisa – This ancestral house was built in the 1930s and is considered as one of the best-preserved bahay na bato or stone houses in Cavite. Visitors can marvel at its intricate woodwork, antique furniture, and vintage decor.

  3. Aguinaldo Shrine – This shrine is the birthplace of General Emilio Aguinaldo, the first president of the Philippine Republic. It houses memorabilia, artifacts, and historical documents related to the country’s independence and revolution.

Natural Attractions of Dasmariñas: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Dasmariñas is also home to several natural attractions that offer adventure and scenic views for travelers who love the great outdoors.

  1. Mount Paliparan – This mountain is part of the Sierra Madre range and offers hiking trails to its peak. From the top, visitors can see a panoramic view of Dasmariñas and the neighboring towns.

  2. Malagasang Falls – Located in Barangay Malagasang, this waterfall is a perfect spot for swimming and picnics. It has a series of cascades that lead to a deep natural pool.

  3. Orchard Golf and Country Club – This golf course is a beautiful green oasis in the middle of the town. It has a challenging layout, scenic views, and top-notch facilities that cater to golfers of all levels.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Dasmariñas

Dasmariñas has several hiking and biking routes that cater to adventure seekers and eco-tourists.

  1. Mount Paliparan – This mountain offers a 5-kilometer hiking trail that passes by lush forests, limestone formations, and stunning viewpoints. It takes around 3 hours to complete.

  2. Dasmariñas Nature Trail – This 3-kilometer trail is perfect for beginners and families. It features a flat terrain, picnic areas, and scenic views of Mount Paliparan, Laguna Bay, and Metro Manila.

  3. Orchard Bike Trail – This biking trail is a 4-kilometer loop that passes through the Orchard Golf and Country Club’s scenic fairways and roughs. It has both easy and technical sections that cater to different skill levels.

Best Things to Do in Dasmariñas: Activities and Tours

Dasmariñas offers various activities and tours that cater to all kinds of travelers. Here are some of the best ones:

  1. Food Tours – Dasmariñas is known for its local delicacies such as pancit pusit, tapang Taal, and bibingkoy. Join a food tour to taste these dishes and learn about their history and preparation.

  2. Golf Clinics – Orchard Golf and Country Club offers golf lessons for beginners and advanced players. Improve your swing and enjoy the beautiful course at the same time.

  3. Horseback Riding – The Philippine Racing Club has a horseracing track in Barangay Langkaan that also offers horseback riding tours. Explore Dasmariñas’ scenery on horseback and feel the wind in your hair.

Week-Long Itinerary for Dasmariñas

Here’s a sample itinerary for a week-long stay in Dasmariñas:

  • Day 1: Explore the town’s historical landmarks such as the Museo De La Salle, Bahay Tisa, and Aguinaldo Shrine.

  • Day 2: Hike Mount Paliparan and enjoy the stunning views from its peak.

  • Day 3: Golf day at Orchard Golf and Country Club. Play a round or take a lesson.

  • Day 4: Tour the local farms and try fresh produce such as pineapple, banana, and mango.

  • Day 5: Visit the Malagasang Falls and have a refreshing dip in its natural pool.

  • Day 6: Horseback riding at the Philippine Racing Club.

  • Day 7: Souvenir shopping at Dasmariñas’ local markets and malls.

Food and Drink Scene of Dasmariñas

Must-Try Dishes

Dasmariñas has a rich culinary tradition that reflects its history and influences. Here are some of the must-try dishes:

  1. Pancit Pusit – A local delicacy made with squid ink noodles, vegetables, seafood, and meat. It has a unique savory flavor and is usually served during fiestas or special occasions.

  2. Tapang Taal – A cured pork dish that originated from the town of Taal in Batangas. It is served with garlic rice and fried egg and is a popular breakfast meal.

  3. Bibingkoy – A dessert made with glutinous rice, coconut milk, sugar, and eggs. It is wrapped in banana leaves and steamed until cooked.

Local Beverages

Dasmariñas is also known for its local beverages that are both refreshing and healthy.

  1. Calamansi Juice – A citrus beverage made with calamansi, honey, and water. It is rich in vitamin C and is a great alternative to soda.

  2. Sago’t Gulaman – A sweet drink made with brown sugar, sago pearls, and gulaman or agar-agar. It is usually served with ice and is perfect for hot summer days.

What to Buy in Dasmariñas: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Dasmariñas has several local products and souvenirs that travelers can buy as gifts or keepsakes. Here are some of the best ones:

  1. Pineapple Fabric – A unique fabric that is made from pineapple fibers. It is lightweight, breathable, and has a distinct texture. It is perfect for making dresses, shirts, and home decors.

  2. Barong Tagalog – The Philippines’ national costume for men. It is made from piña or jusi fibers and is usually worn during formal events or weddings.

  3. Cavite Coffee – A rich and flavorful coffee that is grown in the province of Cavite. It has a distinct taste and aroma and is perfect for coffee lovers.

Local Culture and Customs of Dasmariñas

Dasmariñas is a mix of different cultures and traditions that reflect the town’s history, geography, and people. Here are some local customs that travelers should be aware of:

  1. Respect for elders – Filipinos value respect for elders and authority. It is customary to address older people with “po” or “opo” to show politeness.

  2. Family-oriented – Filipinos are family-oriented and value strong family ties. It is common for extended families to live together in the same household.

  3. Religious traditions – Dasmariñas is predominantly Catholic, and religious practices such as attending mass and celebrating fiestas are deeply ingrained in the local culture.

Best Time to Visit Dasmariñas

The best time to visit Dasmariñas is from December to May when the weather is dry and sunny. This is also the peak season for tourism, so expect higher prices and larger crowds. Avoid the rainy season from June to November when typhoons are frequent.

Do’s and Don’ts of Dasmariñas

Do:

  1. Learn basic Filipino phrases such as “magandang araw” (good day) and “salamat” (thank you) to show politeness.

  2. Dress appropriately when visiting religious sites and institutions. Avoid wearing revealing clothing or shorts and sleeveless tops.

  3. Try local delicacies and beverages to discover the town’s culinary traditions.

Don’t:

  1. Disrespect religious practices or symbols.

  2. Litter or damage natural and cultural landmarks.

  3. Engage in illegal activities such as drug use or trafficking.

Safety and Health

Safety in Dasmariñas

Dasmariñas is generally safe for tourists. However, travelers should practice common sense and take necessary precautions such as:

  1. Avoiding dark and isolated areas at night.

  2. Not flaunting money or valuable possessions.

  3. Using licensed and reputable transportation services.

Health in Dasmariñas

Dasmariñas has several hospitals and clinics that offer healthcare services. Travelers should also take basic health precautions such as:

  1. Drinking bottled or purified water.

  2. Bringing insect repellent and sunscreen to protect against mosquito and sunburn.

  3. Avoiding street food or uncooked food to prevent foodborne illnesses.

Getting to Dasmariñas: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Dasmariñas

  1. Ninoy Aquino International Airport (NAIA) – Manila’s main airport is located around 40 kilometers north of Dasmariñas. Travel time is around 1-2 hours depending on traffic.

  2. Clark International Airport (CRK) – An alternative airport located around 130 kilometers north of Dasmariñas. It serves international and domestic flights.

Bus Stations Near Dasmariñas

  1. DLTB Co. – A bus company that offers trips to Batangas, Laguna, and Quezon.

  2. JAM Liner – A bus company that offers trips to Batangas, Laguna, Manila, and Quezon.

Other Options

  1. Private car hire – Travelers can rent a car or hire a private car with a driver to explore Dasmariñas and nearby destinations.

  2. Jeepneys and tricycles – These are the main modes of transportation within Dasmariñas and neighboring towns. Tricycles are motorcycle-driven vehicles that can accommodate up to three passengers, while jeepneys are larger vehicles that follow specific routes.

How to Get Around in Dasmariñas

The best way to get around Dasmariñas is by tricycle or jeepney. These vehicles can be flagged down along the road, and fares should be negotiated before the ride. For longer distances or private tours, renting a car or hiring a private driver is recommended.

Where to Go Next from Dasmariñas: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Dasmariñas is a great base for exploring nearby destinations and excursions. Here are some of the best ones:

  1. Tagaytay – A popular mountain resort town that offers cool weather, scenic views, and fun activities such as horseback riding, ziplining, and food tripping.

  2. Taal – A heritage town that features well-preserved ancestral houses, historical landmarks, and a volcano crater lake.

  3. Manila – The Philippines’ capital city that offers a mix of modern and traditional attractions such as museums, parks, and shopping malls.

Conclusion

Dasmariñas is a town that offers a unique blend of history, culture, nature, and adventure. Whether you’re a foodie, a golfer, a hiker, or just seeking relaxation, Dasmariñas has something for you. Book your trip now and experience the best of Cavite’s fast-growing municipality.
