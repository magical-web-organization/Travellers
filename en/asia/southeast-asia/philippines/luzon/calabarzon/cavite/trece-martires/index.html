A Complete Travel Guide to Trece Martires, Cavite

Welcome to Trece Martires, a city that is quietly making its way in the tourist scene in the Philippines. It is located in Cavite, a province in the Calabarzon region of Luzon. Surrounded by lush greenery, this city is a treasure trove of natural, cultural, and historic landmarks.

List of Places to Visit in Trece Martires

Trece Martires offers a variety of attractions for visitors to explore. Here are some of the must-visit places in the city.

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

If you’re a history buff or simply fascinated by the Philippine culture, be sure to visit these fascinating museums and historical places:

  • Trece Martires City Museum: This museum showcases the rich history and culture of the city. You can find artifacts, vintage photographs, and other historical memorabilia that help bring the past alive.
  • Gen. Trias Museum and Cultural Center: Located in the nearby city of General Trias, this museum presents the history and culture of Cavite and its people. It also has a library, a gallery, and a souvenir shop.
  • Andres Bonifacio House: The house where the Philippine hero Andres Bonifacio lived is a national treasure and an important cultural landmark.

Natural Attractions of Trece Martires: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Trece Martires is blessed with a picturesque landscape, from mountain ranges to rolling hills, and tranquil rivers to waterfalls. Here are some of the best natural attractions in Trece Martires:

  • Mt. Nagpatong: This peak is a popular destination for hiking and boasts a breathtaking panoramic view of the surrounding region.
  • Balite Falls: Located in the nearby city of Amadeo, this gorgeous waterfall is a must-visit for nature lovers.
  • Tagaytay Ridge: A scenic drive to this nearby ridge takes you to one of the most picturesque views overlooking Taal Lake and the nearby volcano.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Trece Martires

For adventurers, Trece Martires offers several hiking and biking trails that traverse through the natural landscapes of the city. Some popular routes include:

  • Mt. Nagpatong: This mountain has several hiking trails that range from easy to challenging.
  • Makulot Trail: Perfect for mountain bikers, this rugged trail winds its way through the mountains, offering a challenging and rewarding experience.
  • Kaybiang Tunnel: While not exactly a hiking or biking trail, traversing through this 300-meter tunnel offers a unique experience for thrill-seekers.

Best Things to Do in Trece Martires: Activities and Tours

There’s always something to do in Trece Martires. Here are some of the best activities and tours you can add to your itinerary:

  • Visit the Cavite Food and Culinary Tour: This culinary tour takes you on a journey to the best local restaurants and street food stalls in Cavite, including those in Trece Martires.
  • Explore the Local Farming Scene: Cavite is known for its agricultural industry, so why not take a tour of the local farms and learn more about the region’s agriculture?
  • Visit Local Festivals: Catch a glimpse of the vibrant culture of Trece Martires by attending its festivals. The city’s fiestas are often celebrated with colorful parades, street dances, and cultural performances.

Week-Long Itinerary for Trece Martires

Here’s a suggested itinerary for visitors who would like to explore the city for a week:

  • Day 1: Arrival in Cavite and check-in to your accommodation in Trece Martires
  • Day 2: Hiking at Mt. Nagpatong and visiting the city museums
  • Day 3: Join the Cavite Food and Culinary tour
  • Day 4: Explore the farming scene in General Trias and Dasmarinas
  • Day 5: Visit Balite falls and enjoy a refreshing dip
  • Day 6: Gear up for a scenic drive to Tagaytay Ridge
  • Day 7: Departure from Cavite

Food and Drink Scene of Trece Martires

Cavite is famous for its cuisine, and Trece Martires is no exception. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Adobo: This is a Philippine favorite that originated from the Spanish occupation. It’s a tangy, salty, and savory dish made with chicken or pork, soy sauce, vinegar, and spices.
  • Bulalo: Another popular Filipino dish is Bulalo, a hearty soup made with beef shank and marrow bones, vegetables, and spices.
  • Kare-Kare: This is a traditional Filipino stew made from oxtail, tripe, or beef with vegetables and a thick peanut sauce.

Local Beverages

  • Kapeng Barako: This coffee has a distinctive bitter taste, characteristic of Philippine coffee. It’s grown in Batangas and Cavite, and a popular drink among locals.
  • Lambanog: This is a Filipino spirit distilled from coconut. It’s known for its strength, so it’s best to sip slowly and in moderation.

Where to Stay in Trece Martires: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Trece Martires offers a variety of accommodation options to suit every traveler’s budget and preference. Here are some popular choices:

  • Hotel Sogo: This is a popular chain hotel in the Philippines, offering a range of room types, from standard rooms to deluxe suites.
  • Hotel Kimberly Tagaytay: This hotel is located in the nearby city of Tagaytay and offers a stunning view of Taal Lake and Volcano.
  • Treasure Island Resort: This resort is ideal for those looking for a more luxurious stay, with amenities such as a swimming pool, spa, and restaurant.

What to Buy in Trece Martires: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Don’t leave Trece Martires without grabbing some souvenirs or local products. Here are some ideas:

  • Lanzones: These are small, sweet fruit native to the Philippines and are abundant in Cavite.
  • Tablea: A popular dessert in the Philippines. Tablea is chocolate made with cacao beans that are roasted, ground, and molded into bars.
  • Bamboo Furniture: Bamboo is abundant in the Philippines, and Trece Martires is known for its quality bamboo furniture.

Local Culture and Customs of Trece Martires

Trece Martires residents are friendly and hospitable people. It’s common practice to say “Po or Opo” when speaking to someone older as a sign of respect.

Best Time to Visit Trece Martires

The best time to visit Trece Martires is from November to April, when the weather is cooler and drier. Be mindful, that late afternoon thunderstorms are common in the Philippines so be sure to plan ahead.

Do’s and Don’ts of Trece Martires

Do:

  • Respect the culture: The Philippines is a culturally conservative country, so it’s important to be respectful of local culture and customs.
  • Carry cash: Credit cards are not always accepted at smaller establishments, so always carry cash.

Don’t:

  • Forget to bring sunscreen: The weather can be sweltering, so protect yourself from the sun’s harsh rays by wearing sunscreen and a hat.
  • Waste water: As much of the region suffers from water shortages, be sure to turn off taps when not using them to be mindful of water consumption.

Safety and Health

Safety in Trece Martires

Trece Martires is relatively safe, but it’s always best to take precautions. Here are some safety tips:

  • Avoid sketchy areas: As in any other city, there are neighborhoods that are less secure than others, so be sure to familiarize yourself with the area before venturing out.
  • Bring mosquito repellent: Mosquitoes are prevalent in the region, so it’s important to bring mosquito repellent to protect yourself from mosquito-borne diseases like dengue.

Health in Trece Martires

The Philippines requires all visitors to present a negative RT-PCR COVID-19 test result taken 72 hours before entering the country. Should you require any medical assistance, there are several hospitals and clinics in nearby General Trias and Dasmarinas.

Getting to Trece Martires: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Trece Martires

  • Ninoy Aquino International Airport: This is the main airport serving Manila, located about 36 kilometers north of Trece Martires.

Bus Stations Near Trece Martires

  • Southwest Provincial Bus Terminal: Located in Tagaytay City, about 13 km from Trece Martires.
  • SM City Dasmarinas Bus Terminal: Located in Dasmarinas about 8 km from Trece Martires.

Other Options

  • Hire a Private Car Service: This is the most convenient way to travel to Trece Martires from Manila. You can choose from an air-conditioned car, van, or bus.

How to Get Around in Trece Martires

Getting around Trece Martires is easy, there are Jeepneys and tricycles available for hire. Prices are reasonable and are usually dependent on the distance or destination.

Where to Go Next from Trece Martires: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Tagaytay City: This is a city in Cavite known for its scenic views of the Taal Lake and Volcano.
  • Puerto Galera: Located in the nearby province of Oriental Mindoro, this beach town offers numerous water sports activities and is known for its secret beaches.
  • Corregidor Island: This historic island is located in the entrance of Manila Bay and s home to ruins of military barracks and gun emplacements.

Conclusion

Trece Martires is a treasure trove of natural beauty, culture, and history. With its friendly locals, delicious cuisine, and diverse attractions, this city has something to offer for every traveler.
