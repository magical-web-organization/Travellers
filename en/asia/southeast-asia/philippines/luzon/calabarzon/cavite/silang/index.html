Introduction to Silang

Silang is a town located in the province of Cavite, which is a part of Calabarzon region in the Luzon island of the Philippines. Known for its rich history and culture, Silang is a destination worth visiting for anyone who is interested in exploring the roots of Filipino tradition.

List of Places to Visit in Silang

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Some of the must-visit museums and historical places in Silang are:

  1. Silang Catholic Church - A 400-year-old church that was built during the Spanish colonial era and is known for its Baroque-style architecture.
  2. Villa Gregoria Terraces - A restored 19th-century mansion that now serves as a museum showcasing the rich heritage and history of Silang.
  3. Museo de La Salle - A museum that exhibits the life and works of Saint John Baptist de La Salle, who established the first Christian school in the Philippines.

Natural Attractions of Silang: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

For nature lovers and outdoor enthusiasts, Silang offers a variety of scenic spots and adventures. Some of the best natural attractions of Silang are:

  1. Mount Sungay - A popular hiking spot that offers a breathtaking view of Taal Volcano and Tagaytay Ridge.
  2. Silang Central Park and Nature Reserve - A vast park with a beautiful man-made lake, jogging path, playground, and picnic areas.
  3. Olaes River - A scenic spot where you can enjoy kayaking, fishing, and camping.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Silang

For those who are looking for a challenging yet rewarding outdoor adventure, Silang has a variety of hiking and biking trails. Some of the most popular routes are:

  1. Mount Sungay Trail - A 2-3 hour hike that is perfect for beginners and offers a breathtaking view of the surrounding mountains and valleys.
  2. Sitio Bayabasan Trail - A moderate to difficult 5-6 hour hike that takes you through scenic forests, river crossings, and rocky terrains.
  3. Tinamnan Trail - A beginner-friendly mountain biking trail that offers stunning views of the countryside and lush forests.

Best Things to Do in Silang: Activities and Tours

There are plenty of exciting things to do in Silang. Some of the top activities and tours are:

  1. Barako Coffee Farm Tour - A guided tour of a local coffee farm that specializes in producing high-quality Barako coffee.
  2. Heritage Tour of Silang - A guided tour that takes you through the town’s historical and cultural landmarks, including the Silang Catholic Church and Villa Gregoria Terraces.
  3. ATV Adventure - An off-road adventure on an all-terrain vehicle that takes you through the scenic trails of Mount Sungay.

Week-Long Itinerary for Silang

Here’s a suggested itinerary for a week-long trip to Silang:

Day Activities
1 Visit Silang Catholic Church and Villa Gregoria Terraces

Food and Drink Scene of Silang

Silang offers a wide range of traditional Filipino cuisine and local delicacies, including:

Must-Try Dishes

  1. Bulalo - A hearty and savory beef soup that is a staple in Filipino cuisine.
  2. Longganisa - A local sausage made with ground pork and a variety of spices.
  3. Adobo - A flavorful dish made with chicken or pork, soy sauce, vinegar, and garlic.

Local Beverages

  1. Barako Coffee - A strong and flavorful coffee made from Liberica beans, which are grown locally in the region.
  2. Lambanog - A traditional coconut wine that is popular in Luzon.

Where to Stay in Silang: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

There are plenty of options for accommodation in Silang. Some of the top hotels, hostels, and guesthouses are:

  1. Anya Resort - A luxurious resort that features a spa, a fitness center, and an outdoor pool.
  2. Hacienda Solange - A guesthouse that offers a homely and rustic experience of Silang’s countryside.
  3. Maria Divina Farm and Resort - A nature-friendly resort that offers an outdoor swimming pool, a garden, and a restaurant.

What to Buy in Silang: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Silang is known for its handicrafts and local produce. Some of the best souvenirs and local products to bring home are:

  1. Handmade Baskets and Bags - Made using traditional weaving techniques, these baskets and bags are unique and stylish.
  2. Barako Coffee - Bring home some of the locally grown and freshly roasted Barako coffee beans.
  3. Local Fruits and Vegetables - Visit the local markets to buy some fresh and locally sourced fruits and vegetables.

Local Culture and Customs of Silang

Silang is known for its vibrant and colorful celebrations of local festivals and traditions, such as:

  1. Sinukmani Festival - Celebrated every May in honor of Saint Isidore the Farmer, the patron saint of farmers and agriculturists.
  2. La Hermosa Festival - Celebrated every February in honor of Our Lady of Candelaria, the patroness of Silang.

Best Time to Visit Silang

The best time to visit Silang is during the dry season, which runs from December to May. This is the perfect time to enjoy outdoor activities and explore the town’s natural attractions.

Do’s and Don’ts of Silang

Do:

  1. Respect the local traditions and customs.
  2. Try the local cuisine and delicacies.
  3. Explore the natural attractions and outdoor adventures.

Don’t:

  1. Engage in any form of disrespectful behavior, such as littering or vandalizing.
  2. Ignore the local culture and customs.
  3. Treat the locals with disrespect or disregard.

Safety and Health

Safety in Silang

Silang is a relatively safe town, but it is always important to take necessary precautions such as:

  1. Avoid areas that are known for criminal activity.
  2. Stay in well-lit areas at night.
  3. Keep your valuables secure and out of sight.

Health in Silang

Silang has a few healthcare facilities and pharmacies in case of minor injuries and illnesses. However, it is always important to carry any necessary medications and first-aid supplies with you during your trip.

Getting to Silang: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Silang

The nearest airport to Silang is Ninoy Aquino International Airport in Manila, which is about 36 miles away.

Bus Stations Near Silang

There are several bus stations in Manila that offer routes to Silang, including:

  1. JAC Liner - Located in Pasay City, JAC Liner offers trips to Silang via Calamba and Santa Rosa.
  2. DLTBCo - Located in Pasay City, DLTBCo offers trips to Silang via Cavite City.

Other Options

Taxi, Grab, and private car services are readily available for transport to Silang.

How to Get Around in Silang

The best and most practical way to get around Silang is by rented vehicles, such as cars, motorcycles, and bicycles. Local tricycles and jeepneys are also available options for short distance transportation.

Where to Go Next from Silang: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

For those who want to extend their travel in the region, there are plenty of nearby destinations and excursions to enjoy, including:

  1. Tagaytay City - A popular destination that offers a scenic view of Taal Lake and Volcano, and a variety of dining and shopping options.
  2. Batangas - Known for its beaches and diving spots, Batangas is a popular destination for water sports and activities.
  3. Manila - The bustling capital of the Philippines has a lot to offer, from historical landmarks to entertainment and shopping centers.

Conclusion

With a rich history and culture, natural wonders, and exciting adventures, Silang, Cavite is a destination worth exploring. Plan your trip with this comprehensive travel guide and enjoy all that Silang has to offer.
