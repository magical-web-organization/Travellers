Introduction to Imus

Imus City is a small but lovely city in the province of Cavite, in the Calabarzon region of the Philippines. Once an agricultural town, Imus is now a bustling city with a rich cultural heritage, nature attractions, and fascinating gastronomy. Despite its proximity to Metro Manila, Imus remains an off-the-beaten-path destination, offering visitors a more relaxed and authentic experience of the Philippines.

In this comprehensive guide to Imus, you will discover the best places to visit, explore the culture and customs, and find the best things to do. You will also learn how to get there, where to stay, and what to buy as souvenirs.

List of Places to Visit in Imus

Imus has a wealth of attractions, both natural and man-made, that tourists can explore. Here’s a list of the most interesting places to visit in Imus:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Imus Cathedral is a well-known landmark of Imus, built during the Spanish colonial era, and is an impressive architectural masterpiece, featuring a baroque facade and ornate interiors.
  • Emilio Aguinaldo Shrine is a two-story ancestral house-turned-museum that showcases the life and times of General Emilio Aguinaldo, the first president of the Philippines, who declared Philippine Independence in 1898.
  • Museo de Imus is a small yet charming museum that displays the city’s cultural heritage, including traditional costumes, handicrafts, and memorabilia.
  • Birhen ng Lujan Shrine is a religious site that houses a sacred image of the blessed virgin Mary, known for miraculous powers. The shrine welcomes thousands of devotees each year to offer prayers and tributes.

Natural Attractions of Imus: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • The Imus Bamboo Organ is UNESCO-listed and is a fascinating attraction that tourists must see. The pipe organ made almost entirely of bamboo is a unique feature and worth documenting both in pictures and videos.
  • Imus City Plaza is a picturesque park in the heart of Imus that offers a relaxing ambiance for picnics, walking, and jogging. The park also features the Imus City Hall, and the towering Imus City Clock Tower.
  • Greenwood Safari Resort has cable cars that offer breathtaking views of the forest while moving above the canopy. You can also opt for the zip line, wall climbing, and swimming pool day tour all at a reasonable price.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Imus

Imus is a city surrounded by the beautiful countryside, with some nearby natural attractions and scenic trekking routes. Here are some popular routes for hiking and biking around Imus:

  • Daanghari Bike Trail starts in Imus and passes through a scenic route that leads to Daanghari road, a popular road biking route that connects many towns in Cavite.
  • Sparta Garden Resort is situated on a hilly region and offers a hiking trail that goes through the resort. The hiking trail offers a unique view of the garden, allowing tourists to appreciate the lush greenery.
  • Bacoor Boulevard is a newly-opened bike lane that starts in Imus and ends in Coastal Road in Bacoor. The path is safe and cyclist-friendly and features stunning views of the Manila Bay sunset.

Best Things to Do in Imus: Activities and Tours

Imus offers a host of activities and tours that cater to different kinds of tourists, from history buffs to thrill-seekers. Here are some ideas for the best things to do in Imus:

  • Chow down on Delicious Local Foods - Imus is known for producing some of the tastiest delicacies, including adobo sa toyo, puto bumbong, and empanada. Go food tripping and try them all!
  • Go on a Walking Tour in the City - Join a walking tour, and explore the city with a knowledgeable and entertaining local guide. The tour will give you insights into the city’s history and culture.
  • Visit Fort Santiago in Intramuros - Take a short ride to Manila, and immerse yourself in the city’s history by visiting the famous Fort Santiago in the Intramuros district.
  • Go on a Beach Excursion - Head to the nearby coastal towns of Kawit and Noveleta, where you will find many beaches to relax, swim, and sunbathe.

Here’s an itinerary for a week-long stay in Imus:

Food and Drink Scene of Imus

Imus has an exciting food scene, with dishes that showcase the city’s heritage and culture. Here are some local favorites:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Adobo sa toyo - a local variant of adobo, wherein instead of vinegar, toyo (soy sauce) is used, and often served with lots of garlic.
  • Puto bumbong is a famous dessert made with glutinous rice soaked in coconut milk, then steamed and sprinkled with grated coconut and muscovado sugar.
  • Empanada is a savory pastry filled with ground meat and vegetables and usually fried.

Local Beverages

  • Salabat is a traditional Filipino ginger tea with sugar and lemon. The refreshing beverage is known for its medicinal properties and is especially good for the stomach.
  • Calamansi Juice is a refreshing drink made from the small, tart calamansi fruit, which is similar to lime. It is a perfect thirst-quencher for a hot day.

Where to Stay in Imus: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Imus offers a range of accommodations to suit all kinds of travelers, from budget-friendly hostels to luxurious hotels. You can opt for:

  • Microtel by Wyndham – Eagle Ridge, Cavite is located in an upscale subdivision, boasts a stunning view of the mountains and golf course. The hotel features comfortable rooms, free breakfast, and swimming pool facilities.
  • Cavite Lodge caters to budget-savvy travelers, offering value-for-money accommodations. The rooms are clean and well-maintained, and the breakfast is affordable.
  • Villa Ciofano is a guesthouse in a quiet and relaxing setting in Imus. The rooms have balconies that offer views of the garden and sea. The hotel features free Wi-Fi and free parking.

What to Buy in Imus: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Imus has a range of souvenir shops and specialty stores offering unique and locally made products that make perfect gifts. Here are some items to look out for:

  • Imus T-Shirts and Keychains - Show your love for Imus by taking home a T-shirt or a keychain featuring the city’s emblem.
  • Barong Tagalog - A traditional Filipino formal attire made from fine, lightweight fabrics. Imus is known for high-quality Barongs, so it’s an excellent place to buy one for formal occasions.
  • Egg Cakes – also known as egg pies, are a must-try when visiting Imus. They are perfect souvenirs because they are a local delicacy that is not as commonly available elsewhere.

Local Culture and Customs of Imus

Imus is a predominantly Catholic city, and you’ll find many religious practices and customs embedded in its culture. Here are some of the customs to keep in mind:

  • “Mano po” greeting - A common custom in the Philippines is to greet elders by performing the “mano” gesture. It involves taking the hand of the elder and placing it on your forehead as a sign of respect.
  • “Blessing” of the car – in a predominantly Catholic city, car blessings are held frequently, as an attempt to seek the blessings and safety of the patron saint.
  • Fiesta Celebrations - Imus celebrates numerous fiestas throughout the year, where religious activities, street parties, and parades are held to celebrate the city’s patron saint.

Best Time to Visit Imus

The best time to visit Imus is during the dry months, from November to April. However, this is also the peak tourist season, so expect higher prices and bigger crowds.

Do’s and Don’ts of Imus

Here are some Do’s and Don’ts that are crucial to remember when traveling to Imus:

Do:

  • Be Respectful - Filipinos are known for their hospitality and warmth, so when visiting Imus, show respect for the local culture and customs.
  • Learn Basic Filipino Phrases - Even if English is widely spoken, it is appreciated if you communicate using some local Filipino phrases. It will show that you respect the culture and customs of the Filipinos.
  • Be Prepared for Traffic - Imus and nearby towns in Cavite are infamous for having heavy traffic.

Don’t:

  • Do not Litter – Let’s keep Imus streets clean and reflect the warm and hospitable nature of the Filipino people.
  • Do not Wear Inappropriately – Imus is a predominantly Catholic city; please note that female visitors should avoid revealing clothing when visiting religious sites and public areas.
  • Do not Haggle Rigidly – Local vendors might raise or give you an above-average price, but be mindful of haggling, avoid being rude.

Safety and Health

Imus is a safe city for tourists, but like in any other place, exercise caution and common sense when traveling. Here are some tips:

Safety in Imus

  • Avoid Taking Off from Strangers - Imus has few incidents of robbery from people hitching or riding in private vehicles.
  • Secure Belongings - Secure your belongings and use a money belt as tourists are often a target of opportunistic theft.
  • Always Keep Cash and Valuables in Hotels Safe - It is more secure to leave your valuables in the hotel than carry them provided that they have safety deposits.

Health in Imus

  • Drink Bottled and Safe Treated Water – To stay healthy, drink only bottled or treated water to avoid getting sick with stomach ailments.
  • Protect Yourself from Mosquitoes - Imus is not known to be a high-risk area for mosquito-borne diseases, but it helps to come prepared with mosquito repellent and mosquito nets.

Getting to Imus: Transportation Options and Logistics

Getting to Imus is relatively easy, as it is well-connected to Manila and to most of the provincial towns in Cavite. Here’s a breakdown of the transportation options:

Airports Near Imus

  • Ninoy Aquino International Airport (MNL) – located in Pasay City, is the main international gateway to the Philippines. From the airport, you can take a taxi or bus to Imus, which is about a 20- to 30-minute drive.

Bus Stations Near Imus

  • Baclaran Bus Terminal – located in Paranaque City, is the most convenient bus terminal to go to Imus. From the terminal, ride a bus bound for Imus and most of the towns in Cavite.
  • Alabang Transport Terminal – located in Muntinlupa City, is an alternative terminal, where you can also take a bus to Imus and nearby towns.

Other Options

  • Ride-hailing Cars and Taxis – There are various ride-hailing services available in the Philippines that you can use to get to Imus. You can book a ride through Grab or get a taxi from designated stands.

How to Get Around in Imus

Imus is a small city, and you can get around quickly on foot or by taking a tricycle, the most common mode of public transportation. You can also rent a car or motorbike to explore nearby towns and attractions.

Where to Go Next from Imus: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Imus is well-connected to various towns and cities in Cavite and Metro Manila. Here are some nearby destinations for day trips and excursions:

  • Tagaytay City - Head to the famous summer capital of the Philippines. It is known for its scenic views and cool climate.
  • Mangrove River Tour in Noveleta - Explore the Noveleta mangrove forest on a river tour, and learn about the importance and preservation of mangroves in the ecosystem.
  • Villa Excellance Beach Resort – located in Barangay Sampaguita is a picturesque coastal resort that offers relaxing and refreshing ambiance.

Conclusion

Imus has a wealth of attractions to explore, from historical landmarks to outdoor adventures to culinary experiences. Its culture and customs reflect the warmth and hospitality of Filipinos, making it an excellent destination for an immersive Philippine experience. Follow this comprehensive guide and enjoy your stay in Imus, Cavite.
