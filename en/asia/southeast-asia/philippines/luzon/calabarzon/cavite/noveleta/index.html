Introduction to Noveleta

Located in the province of Cavite, Philippines, Noveleta offers a rich mix of cultural heritage, natural beauty, and outdoor adventures. Its proximity to Manila makes it an ideal destination for a quick weekend getaway, while its rich history makes it a must-see destination for history buffs. In this travel guide, we have compiled a list of the best places to visit, things to do, where to stay, and what to eat in Noveleta.

List of Places to Visit in Noveleta

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Noveleta Historic Trail - This trail will take you to various historical landmarks, including the Noveleta Cemetery, Noveleta Church, and Launched Pad Monument.
  • Battle of Noveleta Monument - Erected in commemoration of the Battle of Noveleta in 1897, this monument is a must-visit for history enthusiasts.
  • Noveleta Museum - Showcases the history, culture and traditions of Noveleta, and is housed in a restored colonial-era building.
  • San Pedro Apostol Parish Church - This 400-year-old church is one of the oldest churches in the Philippines and features Baroque and neoclassical designs.

Natural Attractions of Noveleta: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Imus-Noveleta Bikeway - Take a bike ride from Noveleta to Imus city through this scenic route, which is perfect for any nature lover.
  • Gahak River - Enjoy kayaking or swimming in the picturesque Gahak River.
  • Mount Pico de Loro - Conquer this mountain and be rewarded with a breathtaking view of Noveleta and the surrounding areas.
  • Maragondon River - This river offers a serene escape and beautiful views for those who love nature.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Noveleta

  • Mount Marami Trail - A more challenging hike than Pico de Loro, this trail boasts a breathtaking view of Cavite and is perfect for avid hikers.
  • Imus-Noveleta Bikeway - A peaceful and scenic route for cycling enthusiasts.

Best Things to Do in Noveleta: Activities and Tours

  • Heritage Trail Tour - Take a tour of Noveleta’s landmarks and attractions with this guided tour, to learn more about its history and culture.
  • Local Cooking Experience - Learn how to cook traditional Filipino dishes with a local chef in Noveleta.
  • Eco Adventure Tours - Explore the natural beauty of Noveleta with a variety of eco-adventure tours, such as kayaking, hiking, and more.

Week-Long Itinerary for Noveleta

Food and Drink Scene of Noveleta

Must-Try Dishes

  • Adobo - The national dish of the Philippines, consisting of meat marinated in vinegar, soy sauce, garlic, and bay leaves.
  • Sinigang - A savory soup made with tamarind, pork, veggies, and fish.
  • Kaldereta - Slow-cooked goat or beef in tomato sauce, potatoes, green and red bell peppers, onions, and garlic.

Local Beverages

  • Tuba - A coconut wine

Where to Stay in Noveleta: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Mabolo Royal Hotel - A luxury hotel located in Noveleta, offering comfortable rooms and excellent facilities.
  • Luana Hotel and Resort - A mid-range hotel with spacious rooms and a variety of facilities.
  • One Central Hotel and Suites - A budget-friendly option with comfortable, clean rooms and excellent service.

What to Buy in Noveleta: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Barong Tagalog - A traditional Filipino shirt, often made of pineapple or abaca fibers.
  • Banig - A decorative woven mat, perfect for home decoration or picnics.
  • Lambanog - A locally crafted coconut liquor.

Local Culture and Customs of Noveleta

  • Filipinos are known for their hospitality towards guests, so don’t be surprised if you’re warmly welcomed by locals.
  • The official language is Filipino, but English is widely spoken, making communication with locals much easier.
  • It’s customary to remove your shoes when entering someone’s home, so be sure to wear easy-to-remove footwear.

Best Time to Visit Noveleta

The best time to visit Noveleta is from March to May, as the weather is warm, sunny, and dry.

Do’s and Don’ts of Noveleta

Do:

  • Dress modestly when visiting churches and other religious sites.
  • Say “po” and “opo” when speaking to elders, its a sign of respect in Filipino culture.
  • Tip waitstaff around 10% of the total bill amount, as it is customary in the country.

Don’t:

  • Don’t forget to pack sunscreen and insect repellent as the weather can be hot and humid, and there are mosquitos in the area.
  • Don’t be rude or disrespectful towards locals or their customs.
  • Don’t drink tap water in the area, only bottled water is safe.

Safety and Health

Safety in Noveleta

Noveleta is a safe destination for tourists, but it is best to follow the usual precautions against petty theft and carry your valuables with you at all times.

Health in Noveleta

Do not forget to pack sunblock, mosquito repellent, and any prescription medications you may need. It’s a good idea to drink only bottled water to prevent any stomach problems.

Getting to Noveleta: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Noveleta

The nearest airport to Noveleta is Ninoy Aquino International Airport, which is about 25 km from the city center. From there, you can take a taxi or shuttle to reach your destination.

Bus Stations Near Noveleta

Several bus companies offer daily trips to Noveleta from Manila and other cities in the area. You can take a bus from Cubao or Pasay terminals to reach Noveleta.

Other Options

You can also take a private vehicle or rent a car to reach Noveleta from Manila. The drive takes around 1-2 hours, depending on the traffic conditions.

How to Get Around in Noveleta

Tricycles are the most common form of transportation in Noveleta, with jeepneys also being available. Taxis and car rentals are also options if you prefer more convenient transportation.

Where to Go Next from Noveleta: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Tagaytay City - a popular destination known for its cooler climate, scenic views, and delicious local food.
  • Corregidor Island - known for its historical significance in World War II.
  • Las Casas Filipinas de Acuzar - see restored traditional Philippine houses located in Bagac, Bataan.

Conclusion

Noveleta is the perfect place for adventure seekers, history buffs, and nature lovers. This comprehensive guide provides an overview of the best places to visit, how to get around, and what to do while you’re there. With its rich history, culture, stunning outdoor scenery, and delicious food, Noveleta should definitely be on your list of must-visit destinations in the Philippines.
