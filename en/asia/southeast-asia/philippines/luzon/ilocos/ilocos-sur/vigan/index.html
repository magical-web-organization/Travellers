Introduction to Vigan

Located in Ilocos Sur province, in the Ilocos region of Luzon island, in the northern part of the Philippines, Vigan is a charming and historic city known for its well-preserved Spanish colonial architecture. The city was declared a UNESCO World Heritage Site in 1999 for its unique architecture that reflects a fusion of Asian and European influences.

Vigan is a popular destination among tourists who are fascinated with the city’s cultural and historical significance. Boasting a rich history, vibrant cultural scene, and stunning natural scenery, Vigan has something for every type of traveler.

List of Places to Visit in Vigan

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Calle Crisologo - a historic street lined with 18th-century Spanish houses turned into souvenir shops and cafes
  • Crisologo Museum - a museum that houses a collection of memorabilia and artifacts related to the life of a prominent Ilocano family
  • Balaurte - a mini-zoo and museum that features preserved animals and exhibits showcasing the rich culture of the Ilocano people
  • Pagburnayan Pottery Making - a workshop that demonstrates the traditional method of pottery in Vigan

Natural Attractions of Vigan: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Baluarte Zoo - a zoo that contains exotic animals, such as zebras, camels, and tigers, as well as a butterfly farm and a botanical garden
  • Cape Bojeador Lighthouse - a Spanish-colonial lighthouse that offers a stunning view of the South China Sea
  • Bantay Bell Tower - a historic tower that provides a panoramic view of the surrounding areas
  • Hidden Garden - a lush garden that features various exotic plants and herbs

Hiking/Biking Routes of Vigan

  • Abra River - a popular spot for kayaking and rafting, amidst a scenic background of the Cordillera Mountains
  • Bantay-Santa Ana Trail - a challenging trail leading to the Santa Ana Church that offers stunning views

Best Things to Do in Vigan: Activities and Tours

  • Kalesa Ride - a horse-drawn carriage ride that takes you around the city’s historic district
  • Food Tour - a gastronomic experience that allows you to sample local delicacies, such as Vigan longanisa and empanada
  • Cultural Show - a showcase of traditional dance, music, and culture of the Ilocano people
  • Heritage Tour - a guided tour that takes you to some of the city’s historic sites

Week-Long Itinerary for Vigan

Day Activity
1 Arrive in Vigan and explore Calle Crisologo
2 Visit Pagburnayan Pottery Making and Crisologo Museum
3 Take a food tour in the morning, then visit Baluarte and Hidden Garden in the afternoon
4 Explore Bantay Bell Tower in the morning, then hike/bike along the Bantay-Santa Ana Trail
5 Visit Cape Bojeador Lighthouse in the morning, then kayak or raft along Abra River
6 Take a cultural show tour, then relax on a kalesa ride around the city
7 Depart from Vigan

Food and Drink Scene of Vigan

Must-Try Dishes

  • Vigan Longanisa - a garlicky sausage made from ground pork
  • Empanada - a savory pastry filled with minced meat, eggs, and vegetables
  • Sinanglaw - a beef broth with liver, vinegar, and spices
  • Pinakbet - a vegetable stew made with eggplant, okra, tomatoes, and bitter melon

Local Beverages

  • Basi - a local wine made from sugarcane
  • Kamote Juice - a sweet purple yam drink
  • Café Amianan - a coffee blend made from local beans and brewed with a traditional Ilocano method

Where to Stay in Vigan: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Calle Crisologo Hotel - a colonial-style hotel located in the heart of Calle Crisologo
  • Hotel Luna - a luxurious boutique hotel that showcases the city’s heritage
  • Gordion Hotel - a budget-friendly hotel that features a rooftop restaurant

What to Buy in Vigan: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Abel Cloth - a colorful woven fabric made from cotton and silk
  • Burnay Pottery - a traditional pottery made in Vigan using local clay
  • Vigan Longanisa - a popular food souvenir to bring home

Local Culture and Customs of Vigan

The people of Vigan are deeply rooted in their traditions and customs. They are proud of their cultural heritage, evident in their language, cuisine, and festivals. One such festival is the “Semana Santa,” a week-long celebration that commemorates the Passion of Christ.

Visitors are encouraged to respect the local customs and traditions, such as dressing modestly when entering churches and other places of worship, and seeking permission before taking photographs of local people.

Best Time to Visit Vigan

The best time to visit Vigan is from November to February when the weather is mild and dry. Avoid visiting during the rainy season, which lasts from June to October, as it can disrupt your outdoor activities.

Do’s and Don’ts of Vigan

Do:

  • Respect the local customs and traditions
  • Dress appropriately and modestly when visiting churches and other places of worship
  • Try the local food and drink

Don’t:

  • Take photographs of locals without their permission
  • Disrespect religious artifacts and symbols
  • Litter or damage the historical sites

Safety and Health

Safety in Vigan

Vigan is a safe destination for tourists, with a low crime rate. However, it is still important to exercise caution, especially when carrying valuables or traveling alone at night.

Health in Vigan

Visitors are advised to drink bottled water and avoid street foods to prevent foodborne illnesses. It is also recommended to bring medication for common travel illnesses such as diarrhea and motion sickness.

Getting to Vigan: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Vigan

The nearest major airport is located in Manila, approximately 408 kilometers south of Vigan.

Bus Stations Near Vigan

Several bus companies offer daily trips to Vigan from Manila, Laoag, and other major cities in the Ilocos region.

Other Options

Taxis and private cars can also be hired for travel to and from Vigan.

How to Get Around in Vigan

The best way to explore the city is on foot or by taking a kalesa ride. Tricycles and jeepneys are also available for short trips around the city.

Where to Go Next from Vigan: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Laoag - the capital city of Ilocos Norte, known for its sandy beaches, historic structures, and natural attractions
  • Pagudpud - a famous beach destination with long stretches of powdery white sand, crystal-clear waters, and picturesque rock formations
  • Baguio - a mountainous city known for its cool climate, pine forests, and expansive views

Conclusion

Vigan is a travel destination that offers a unique and memorable experience to every visitor. With its well-preserved Spanish colonial architecture, rich history, vibrant cultural scene, and stunning natural scenery, the city has something for everyone. From exploring historic sites and museums to hiking and biking amidst scenic views, sampling local delicacies, and shopping for souvenirs, a trip to Vigan should definitely be on your bucket list.
