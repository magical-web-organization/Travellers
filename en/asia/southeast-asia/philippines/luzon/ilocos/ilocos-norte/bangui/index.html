Introduction to Bangui

Nestled in the province of Ilocos Norte in Luzon, Philippines is the scenic coastal town of Bangui. Known for its towering wind turbines and picturesque beaches, Bangui is a must-visit destination for travelers looking for an authentic experience of Filipino culture and natural beauty.

First inhabited by the Ilocano people, Bangui was established as a town in the late 18th century. Today, it is a vibrant community that features both modern and traditional elements, making it a unique addition to any travel itinerary.

List of Places to Visit in Bangui

Bangui offers a diverse range of attractions that showcase the beauty of the province. Here’s a list of must-visit places that you should add to your itinerary:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Bangui Windmills - One of the most iconic landmarks of Bangui, the wind turbines are the first of their kind in Southeast Asia. The windmills stand tall along Bangui Bay and are a symbol of the town’s dedication to renewable energy.

  • Bangui Watchtower - Built during the Spanish Colonial Period, the Bangui Watchtower is a historical fortification that served as a lookout for pirates during the 17th century.

  • Bangui Heritage Center - The center houses cultural exhibits that showcase the Ilocano heritage and traditions of the people of Bangui.

  • Sinking Bell Tower - A unique attraction that showcases the church structures buried in sand dunes.

Natural Attractions of Bangui: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Pagudpud Beach - A stunning beach with crystal-clear waters and powdery white sand. It’s perfect for swimming, sunbathing, and snorkeling.

  • Kapurpurawan Rock Formation - A natural wonder that features creamy-white limestone formations shaped by natural forces.

  • Patapat Viaduct - A scenic drive that passes through mountains, waterfalls, and magnificent coastal views.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Bangui

Bangui’s rolling hills and stunning beach views make it an ideal destination for outdoor activities. Here are some notable hiking/biking routes to explore on your visit:

  • Bangui Wind Farm Loop - A 10-kilometer loop that takes you through scenic views of the wind turbines and the beach.

  • Sugarcane Road Trail - A 6-kilometer trail that starts at the Bangui Heritage Center and takes you through lush sugarcane fields and mountain ranges.

  • Bangui Park to Bangui View Deck Trail - A 5-kilometer trail that takes you through sprawling parkland and ends at the Bangui View Deck, where you can enjoy panoramic views of the Bangui Bay and the wind turbines.

Best Things to Do in Bangui: Activities and Tours

In addition to the attractions and outdoor adventures, Bangui has a long list of activities that cater to different interests. Here are some fun things to do in Bangui:

  • Island-hopping - Take a boat ride and discover the hidden island paradises near Bangui, such as Bantay Abot Cove and Blue Lagoon.

  • Surfing - Try out some waves in Pagudpud with surf schools such as Blue Shorts Surf and Kuya Jeric’s Surf School.

  • ATV Adventure - Explore the countryside of Bangui on an ATV ride with Adventure Philippines.

  • Nightlife at Hannah’s Beach Resort - Dance the night away at this popular beach resort, which features lively music and cocktails.

Week-Long Itinerary for Bangui

If you have a week in Bangui, here is an itinerary that maximizes your time in the town:

Day 1 Arrive in Bangui and explore the Bangui Heritage Center
Day 2 Go on a windmill tour and visit Kapurpurawan Rock Formation
Day 3 Have fun at Patapat Viaduct and go on an ATV adventure
Day 4 Relax on Pagudpud Beach and go island-hopping
Day 5 Discover the Sinking Bell Tower and visit nearby towns
Day 6 Try out some surfing in Pagudpud and have a bonfire at Blue Lagoon
Day 7 Shop for souvenirs and end the night with some nightlife at Hannah’s Beach Resort

Food and Drink Scene of Bangui

Filipino cuisine is known for its unique blend of sweet, sour, and savory flavors. Bangui offers a variety of restaurants and local food establishments that will make your taste buds burst with excitement. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Bagnet - A crispy pork dish that is deep-fried twice for maximum crispiness.

  • Ilocos Empanada - A deep-fried pastry filled with savory minced meat, vegetables, and egg.

  • Pinakbet - A famous Ilocano vegetable dish that features a mix of different vegetables cooked in shrimp paste.

Local Beverages

  • Basi - A traditional Ilocano drink made from fermented sugarcane juice.

  • Cacao Drink - A warm beverage prepared from local cacao beans and served with sweet bread or biscuits.

Where to Stay in Bangui: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Bangui offers several options for accommodation, ranging from budget-friendly hostels to luxury resorts. Here are some recommended places to stay in:

  • Villa Del Nico Beach Resort - A peaceful and luxurious resort that features cabanas and a swimming pool.

  • Ivory Sands Beach Resort - A budget-friendly option that offers beachfront accommodations and a restaurant serving local favorites.

  • Bangui Bay Lodge - An eco-lodge that features cozy rooms and an on-site restaurant that serves international cuisine.

What to Buy in Bangui: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Take a piece of Bangui with you by purchasing local products and souvenirs. Here are some items worth buying:

  • Abel Iloco - A traditional textile made from handwoven cloth.

  • Basi Wine - A traditional drink made from fermented sugarcane juice.

  • Local sweets - A wide variety of sweet treats, such as tupig (sticky rice cakes) and bibingka (rice cake with cheese).

Local Culture and Customs of Bangui

The Ilocano culture is deeply ingrained in the people of Bangui. Visitors should be aware of some customs and traditions so as not to offend the locals.

  • Pagmamano - A sign of respect where one takes the hand of an elder and places it on their forehead.

  • Ilocano language - Learn some common phrases in Ilocano to communicate with the locals.

Best Time to Visit Bangui

The best time to visit Bangui is from November to February when the weather is cool and dry. During this period, you can enjoy the outdoor activities and attractions without worrying too much about the heat and rain.

Do’s and Don’ts of Bangui

Here are some important do’s and don’ts to remember when visiting Bangui:

Do:

  • Respect the local customs and traditions.

  • Dress modestly when visiting places of worship or religious sites.

  • Try out local cuisine and delicacies.

Don’t:

  • Litter or leave trash on the beach or other natural attractions.

  • Disrespect the environment by damaging or harming the natural surroundings.

Safety and Health

Bangui is a relatively safe destination with low crime rates. However, visitors should still exercise caution and take necessary precautions. Here are some safety and health tips:

Safety in Bangui

  • Be aware of your surroundings and avoid walking alone at night.

  • Keep your valuables safe and secure.

  • Check weather conditions before going on outdoor adventures.

Health in Bangui

  • Drink only bottled or filtered water.

  • Wear sunscreen and stay hydrated to avoid getting a sunburn.

  • Consult your doctor for any necessary vaccinations or medications before traveling to Bangui.

Getting to Bangui: Transportation Options and Logistics

There are several transportation options to get to Bangui. Here are some of the most popular:

Airports Near Bangui

The nearest airport to Bangui is Laoag International Airport, which is about 75 kilometers away. Airlines such as Philippine Airlines and Cebu Pacific offer flights to Laoag from Manila and other major cities in the Philippines.

From the airport, you can take a bus or hire a private vehicle to reach Bangui.

Bus Stations Near Bangui

Several bus companies offer trips to Bangui from Manila, Baguio, and other nearby cities. Victory Liner, Partas Bus, and Philippine Rabbit are some of the most popular bus companies that operate in the region.

Other Options

You can also rent a car or hail a taxi to go to Bangui. However, it’s important to note that public transport is more affordable and convenient for travelers on a budget.

How to Get Around in Bangui

The most common mode of transportation in Bangui is the tricycle, which is a motorcycle with a sidecar. Tricycles can take you anywhere within the town and to nearby attractions, such as the wind turbines and the beaches.

For longer trips, you can rent a car or bike.

Where to Go Next from Bangui: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Bangui is a great starting point for exploring the other scenic destinations in Ilocos Norte. Here are some nearby destinations and excursions that you can visit:

  • Paoay Church - A UNESCO World Heritage Site that features a magnificent Baroque architecture.

  • Cape Bojeador Lighthouse - A Spanish-era lighthouse that offers panoramic views of the South China Sea.

  • Vigan City - A charming colonial town that boasts of well-preserved Spanish-era architecture and cobbled streets.

Conclusion
