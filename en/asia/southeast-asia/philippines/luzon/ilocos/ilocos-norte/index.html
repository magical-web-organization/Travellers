Introduction to Ilocos Norte

Ilocos Norte is a province located in the northern region of Luzon in the Philippines. With a rich history and a vibrant culture, Ilocos Norte is a fascinating destination for travelers seeking to explore the wonders of Southeast Asia. The breathtaking natural landscapes, awe-inspiring cultural and historical attractions, delicious local cuisine, and warm hospitality of the locals make Ilocos Norte an unforgettable destination.

List of Places to Visit in Ilocos Norte

There are so many amazing places to explore in Ilocos Norte. From museums to natural attractions, there is something for every kind of traveler.

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Ilocos Norte is famous for its rich cultural heritage and beautiful historic landmarks. Here are some must-visit cultural attractions:

  • Paoay Church: Known as the “Earthquake Baroque” church due to its unique architectural style.
  • Malacañang of the North: This impressive mansion was once the residence of former Philippine President Ferdinand Marcos.
  • Museum of the North: Located at the University of the Philippines, this museum showcases the diverse history and culture of the Ilocanos.
  • Juan Luna Shrine: Explore the life and works of one of the Philippines’ renowned artists at this shrine.
  • Sinking Bell Tower: Don’t miss out on seeing this iconic landmark that appears to be slowly sinking.

Natural Attractions of Ilocos Norte: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Ilocos Norte has a lot to offer for nature lovers as well. Here are some of the most impressive outdoor attractions:

  • Kapurpurawan Rock Formation: This natural wonder boasts crystal blue waters and an awe-inspiring limestone landscape.
  • Paoay Sand Dunes: Experience the thrill of sandboarding or take a scenic drive through these magnificent sand dunes.
  • Bangui Windmills: These renewable energy windmills are among the largest in Southeast Asia and are a must-see for environmental enthusiasts.
  • Saud Beach: Relax, swim, and enjoy the beautiful views of the South China Sea at this stunning beach.
  • Laoag Sinking Bell Tower: One of the four belfries in Ilocos Norte, this tower is slowly sinking into the ground.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Ilocos Norte

Ilocos Norte is also a perfect destination for outdoor enthusiasts looking to go hiking and biking. Here are some of the best hiking and biking routes to try:

  • Pader River Eco-Park Trail: This trail offers a scenic view of the Pader river, and the lush greenery provides a perfect backdrop for hikers. It is also ideal for mountain biking.
  • Dacu Trail: The Dacu trail is a popular hiking and biking trail that passes through scenic landscapes, narrow paths, and rivers.
  • Malacanang of the North Trail: With a mix of uphill and downhill terrains, this trail is perfect for both beginner and experienced hikers.

Best Things to Do in Ilocos Norte: Activities and Tours

There are many great things to see and do in Ilocos Norte beyond sightseeing. Here are some of the best activities and tours:

  • Heritage Walk and City Tour: Explore the history and culture of Ilocos Norte on foot with the Heritage Walk and City Tour.
  • Paoay Sand Dunes Adventure: Take a thrilling adventure with thrilling sandboarding, or take a 4x4 ride through the sand dunes.
  • Kapurpurawan White Rock Formation Tour: Discover the Kapurpurawan White Rock Formation’s beauty to click perfect shots, making your memory album worth remembering.

Week-Long Itinerary for Ilocos Norte

There’s so much to see and do in Ilocos Norte that it can be challenging to fit everything in. Here’s a possible itinerary to get the most out of your visit:

Day Activity
Day 1 Arrive in Laoag City, explore the city’s cultural heritage sites
Day 2 Visit the Kapurpurawan Rock Formation, Bangui Windmills, and Burgos Lighthouse
Day 3 Drive to Pagudpud and visit Maira-ira Beach, Saud Beach, and Kabigan Falls
Day 4 Go sandboarding in Paoay Sand Dunes and visit the Malacanang of the North and Paoay Church
Day 5 Explore the Dacu Trail and the Sinking Bell Tower
Day 6 Visit the Juan Luna Shrine and Museum of the North
Day 7 Depart from Laoag City

Food and Drink Scene of Ilocos Norte

No trip to Ilocos Norte would be complete without sampling some of the local cuisine and beverages. Here are some of the must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Bagnet: A traditional crispy pork dish, which is a must-try when visiting Ilocos Norte.
  • Pinakbet: This Ilocano vegetable dish features mixed vegetables sauteed in bagoong (fish sauce).
  • Empanada: A popular snack food made with a crispy outer shell and filled with a mixture of vegetables, meats such as chicken and/or veggies like bean sprouts, carrots and so on, and eggs.

Local Beverages

  • Basi: A traditional Filipino wine made from sugarcane.
  • Brewed Coffee: Ilocos Norte is known for producing some of the Philippines’ best coffee, so be sure to try a cup or two.

Where to Stay in Ilocos Norte: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Ilocos Norte offers ample choices for lodging options, from budget hostels to luxury hotels. Here are some of the best options:

  • Fort Ilocandia Resort Hotel: A luxury hotel with excellent facilities, including a spa, swimming pool, and restaurants.
  • Java Hotel: This hotel offers a boutique-style experience with tastefully decorated rooms and outstanding customer service.
  • Amihan Bed and Breakfast: A budget-friendly option that offers charming rooms with simple decor and a cozy atmosphere.

What to Buy in Ilocos Norte: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

There are many great souvenirs and local products to bring back home from Ilocos Norte. Here are some of the best options:

  • Bagnet: You can buy vacuum-sealed bagnet to take home, so you can enjoy this Ilocano delicacy whenever you want.
  • Abel Iloco: A locally produced hand-woven cloth that is perfect for different purposes such as clothing, bags, and table runners.
  • Burnay Pottery: This traditional Ilocano pottery is made by hand and is perfect as a decorative or functional piece in your home.

Local Culture and Customs of Ilocos Norte

Ilocos Norte has a rich cultural heritage influenced by Spanish, Chinese and US colonizers, blended with vibrant local customs. Visitors should note the following:

  • Paying Respect to Elders: Showing respect to elders is essential in Ilocano culture.
  • Wearing Appropriate Clothes: When visiting religious sites and other cultural landmarks, it is advisable to wear modest and conservative clothing.
  • Tipping: Tipping is not customary in the Philippines, but it is a common practice among locals in tourist destinations.

Best Time to Visit Ilocos Norte

The best time to visit Ilocos Norte is from November to February when the weather is cooler and less humid. This time coincides with the peak season, so expect more significant crowds and increased prices. The summer months of March to May are the hottest, while June to October is the rainy season.

Do’s and Don’ts of Ilocos Norte

Here are some basic do’s and don’ts to keep in mind while visiting Ilocos Norte:

Do:

  • Learn a few basic phrases in Tagalog like “Magandang araw” (Good day), and “Salamat” (Thank you) before your trip.
  • Respect the local culture and customs, especially when visiting religious sites and participating in local traditions.
  • Be open to trying new things, whether it’s food, activities, or cultural experiences.

Don’t:

  • Litter or damage the environment, or cultural and historical landmarks
  • Wear inappropriate clothing like swimwear or shorts when visiting places of worship.
  • Over-negotiate when shopping or bargaining in local markets.

Safety and Health

Safety in Ilocos Norte

Ilocos Norte is generally safe for tourists and visitors. However, you should take standard safety precautions, such as keeping an eye on your belongings, especially in crowded places like markets, and avoiding isolated, under-lit areas at night.

Health in Ilocos Norte

Ilocos Norte has well-equipped hospitals and clinics. Visitors are advised to bring any medications they might need and take the necessary precautions like getting the recommended vaccinations and using mosquito repellent to prevent the risk of mosquito-borne diseases like dengue fever and malaria.

Getting to Ilocos Norte: Transportation Options and Logistics

Ilocos Norte is accessible by air, land, and sea transportation. Here are the best options to travel to Ilocos Norte:

Airports Near Ilocos Norte

The Laoag International Airport is the primary airport serving Ilocos Norte, with multiple flights available daily from Manila and Cebu. The airport is around 15 minutes from Laoag City proper.

Bus Stations Near Ilocos Norte

Several bus companies operate routes from Manila to Laoag City and other major towns in Ilocos Norte, including Partas and Florida Buses. The travel time is around 10-12 hours.

Other Options

For those who prefer to take their own car, Ilocos Norte is accessible through the North Luzon Expressway (NLEX) and Subic-Clark-Tarlac Expressway (SCTEX) or via Candon City southern part of Ilocos Norte. You can also rent a car or hire a private van with a driver.

How to Get Around in Ilocos Norte

Some of the best ways to get around Ilocos Norte include tricycles, jeepneys, buses, and private cars. Transportation is generally cheap and affordable, making it an excellent way to get around. Visitors may consider hiring private vans or renting their vehicle for added flexibility.

Where to Go Next from Ilocos Norte: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

If you have extra time to explore, there are many fabulous nearby destinations worth visiting after your Ilocos Norte adventure. Some of the options include:

  • Banaue Rice Terraces: One of the UNESCO World Heritage sites located in Ifugao, about 240 kilometers from Laoag City.
  • Vigan City: Another historic town a few hours south of Laoag, known for its well-preserved Spanish colonial architecture.
  • Baguio City: A mountain resort city famous for its cool climate, stunning nature views, and local delicacies, reachable from Ilocos Norte within five to six hours.

Conclusion

Ilocos Norte is a destination that offers something for everyone, from cultural landmarks to natural wonders and outdoor experiences. Visitors to Ilocos Norte are well-rewarded for their efforts with a wealth of activities, unbeatable natural scenery, delicious local food, and friendly locals who are waiting to welcome them with open arms. We hope this comprehensive guide helps you plan your perfect Ilocos Norte adventure!
