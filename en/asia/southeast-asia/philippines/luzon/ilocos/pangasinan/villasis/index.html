Introduction to Villasis

Villasis is a municipality situated in the province of Pangasinan, in Ilocos Region, Philippines. The town was named after a Spanish friar, Father Antonio Vicente Villasis. Located just 40 kilometers northeast of Dagupan City, Villasis is known for its agricultural products such as rice, corn, and vegetables. The town is also home to some of the country’s historical sites, outdoor adventures, and scenic destinations.

In this comprehensive travel guide, we’ll take you through the must-visit places in Villasis, along with hiking/biking routes of the town, best things to do, week-long itinerary, food and drink scene, accommodation options, souvenirs to buy, local culture and customs, safety and health, transportation options, and nearby destinations to explore.

List of Places to Visit in Villasis

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Villasis Heritage Houses

Located at Barangay Poblacion, Villasis, these heritage houses are declared as historical landmarks by the National Historical Commission of the Philippines. The houses were built in the 1800s and are considered significant as they represent the traditional and cultural lifestyle of the town’s prominent families.

St. Anthony Abbot Parish Church

This historic church was built in the 18th century by the Augustinian Friars. The church is accentuated by its beautiful interior, antique-carved wooden doors, and Saint John Nepomucene statue.

Natural Attractions of Villasis: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Imugan Falls

Located in Barangay Imugan, this impressive waterfall is surrounded by verdant forests and can be reached by a one-hour trek.

Balungao Hilltop Adventure

This place is an ideal destination for adventure-seekers. It offers a scenic view of the entire town. You can enjoy activities such as zip-lining, wall climbing, and trekking.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Villasis

Malico Hiking Trail

This trail is a paradise for hikers and nature-lovers, with fresh air to breathe and amazing scenery to take in. The trail boasts several waterfalls and beautiful tropical foliage.

Best Things to Do in Villasis: Activities and Tours

Vegetable Picking Tour

Experience the natural beauty and richness of the town’s agricultural products through an interactive vegetable picking tour. This tour includes learning the proper way of vegetable handling, farming methods, and the history of the town’s agriculture.

Cycling Tour

Explore Villasis on a bike while cycling through its small and cozy streets, and discovering its natural surroundings.

Week-Long Itinerary for Villasis

Day 1: Visit the Villasis Heritage Houses and St. Anthony Abbot Parish Church Day 2: Explore the Imugan Falls Day 3: Take the Balungao Hilltop Adventure tour Day 4: Hike the Malico Hiking Trail Day 5: Take a cycling tour around the town Day 6: Experience the Vegetable Picking Tour Day 7: Take a day trip to nearby destinations such as Baguio City or Hundred Islands National Park.

Food and Drink Scene of Villasis

Must-Try Dishes

Puto Calasiao

A native delicacy made from rice flour and steamed in a round shape.

Pigar Pigar

A unique Ilocano dish made with beef strips, onions, and a pinch of calamansi (local citrus fruit).

Local Beverages

Basi

A sweet wine made from sugarcane.

Tapuy

A traditional rice wine with a sweet and sour taste.

Bangus (Milkfish)

A delicious and famous fish locally farmed in the town.

Local Culture and Customs of Villasis

The locals in Villasis embrace the Ilocano culture, which is also the predominant culture in Pangasinan. The town’s foundation and traditional harvesting customs are rooted in agriculture as the major source of livelihood.

Best Time to Visit Villasis

The best time to visit Villasis is from December to May when the town experiences dry weather, cool winds, and favorable temperatures. The climate, however, is relatively hot and humid from June to November, with occasional typhoons and heavy rains that may affect your outdoor trip plans.

Do’s and Don’ts of Villasis

Do:

Respect the local customs and traditions. Support the local businesses and buy their products. Try their local delicacies and dishes. Teach and learn from the locals.

Don’t:

Litter around the town and its natural surroundings. Disrespect the cultural values of the locals. Cause any damage to their cultural and historical sites. Take photos without permission.

Safety and Health

Safety in Villasis

Villasis is generally a safe town, with low crime rates. However, always keep a close eye on your possessions and avoid isolated places or streets.

Health in Villasis

The town has primary health centers and hospitals that provide the necessary medical needs of the locals and visitors.

Getting to Villasis: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Villasis

The nearest airport is Clark International Airport, which is 109 kilometers away from Villasis. The second nearest airport is Manila’s Ninoy Aquino International Airport, which is 208 kilometers away from Villasis.

Bus Stations Near Villasis

Several bus lines pass by the town and know it as a stop-over to nearby locations. Victory Liner and Five Star are the most commonly used bus lines.

Other Options

Visitors may also rent cars in either Manila or in nearby cities such as Dagupan, San Carlos, or Urdaneta.

How to Get Around in Villasis

Tricycles and Pedicabs are the most used modes of transportation around the town. Rental bikes and cars are also available for those who want to explore the town further.

Where to Go Next from Villasis: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Baguio City

A city famous for its cool climate, Botanical Garden, Burnham Park, and cultural heritage.

Hundred Islands National Park

An archipelago of 124 islets situated in the Lingayen Gulf offering various water activities such as snorkeling and scuba diving.

Conclusion

In conclusion, Villasis offers an exciting blend of adventure, history, culture, and relaxation. From its natural attractions to historical landmarks, from local delicacies to local products to bring home, the town has something for everyone. Make Villasis your next travel destination and discover its hidden gems for yourself.
