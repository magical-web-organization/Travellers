Introduction to Calasiao

Calasiao is a small, picturesque town located in Pangasinan, in Ilocos, in Luzon, in the Philippines. It is a town that is rich in history and culture. Calasiao is famous for its sumptuous puto, but the town has other attractions that make it a great destination for anyone looking to experience Luzon’s best-kept secrets.

List of Places to Visit in Calasiao

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Calasiao has a rich history and culture that is worth exploring. These top cultural attractions are a must-visit:

  1. Sanctuario De San Antonio - A beautiful church that serves as a sanctuary for devotees and tourists alike. It has a breathtaking interior and exterior that will make you feel closer to the divine.

  2. Calasiao Church and Bell Tower - Also known as Our Lady of the Most Holy Rosary Parish Church, this stunning church is another must-see. The bell tower is famous for being the oldest of its kind in the province of Pangasinan.

  3. Calasiao Heritage Center - This museum is dedicated to preserving the town’s cultural history. The center boasts a collection of artefacts and memorabilia that highlight the rich history of Calasiao and its people.

Natural Attractions of Calasiao: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Calasiao is a nature lover’s paradise. The town has plenty of natural attractions that are worth exploring:

  1. Balungao Hilltop Adventure - A great place for outdoor enthusiasts, this adventure park features a variety of thrilling activities like zip-lining, ATV rides, and wall climbing.

  2. Pangasinan Provincial Capitol Park and Baluangao Park - These parks offer scenic views and are perfect for relaxation and picnics with friends and family.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Calasiao

Calasiao is a great place for hiking and biking enthusiasts. The following routes are perfect for exploring the town on foot or bike:

  1. Bike the Puto Loop - Take a leisurely ride around town while making stops at local shops to sample Calasiao’s famous puto.

  2. Mt. Balungao Hike - This moderate to difficult hike rewards climbers with stunning views of the surrounding countryside.

Best Things to Do in Calasiao: Activities and Tours

There are various fun things to do in Calasiao, such as:

  1. Puto Festival - This annual festival celebrates the town’s famous delicacy, the puto. There’s no better way to experience the local culture than by indulging in the traditional taste of Calasiao.

  2. Hot Air Balloon Festival - This festival is held annually in nearby Clark Air Base and is worth the short trip from Calasiao. The festival is known for its hot air balloon rides and stunning light shows.

Week-Long Itinerary for Calasiao

Calasiao is a great place to spend a week exploring. Here’s a sample itinerary to help you make the most of your visit:

Day 1: Visit Calasiao Church and Bell Tower, Calasiao Heritage Center, and Sanctuario De San Antonio

Day 2: Bike the Puto Loop and try various puto delicacies

Day 3: Visit Balungao Hilltop Adventure for a day of outdoor activities

Day 4: Take a trip to Clark Air Base for the Hot Air Balloon Festival

Day 5: Hike Mt. Balungao

Day 6: Visit Pangasinan Provincial Capitol Park and Balungao Park

Day 7: Try local cuisine and souvenirs at the Calasiao Marketplace

Food and Drink Scene of Calasiao

Must-Try Dishes

Calasiao is famous for its puto, but the town has other delicacies that are worth savoring:

  1. Puto - A rice cake that comes in various flavors and toppings. Do not miss Calasiao’s famous brown puto.

  2. Bangus - Pangasinan is famous for its fresh and delicious bangus (milkfish), and Calasiao is no exception.

Local Beverages

  1. Buko Juice - Fresh coconut juice is an ideal drink to beat the tropical heat.

  2. Tuba - A fermented coconut wine that is mildly alcoholic and perfect for sharing with friends.

Where to Stay in Calasiao: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Calasiao has a wide range of accommodations that cater to various budgets and preferences:

  1. Balungao Hilltop Adventure - Offers camping and glamping options for a unique outdoor experience

  2. AJ’s Inn and Suites - A mid-range hotel located in the heart of town, offering comfortable rooms and amenities.

  3. The Mango Grove Farm and Orchard - A budget-friendly option that features cozy guesthouses nestled in a lush mango orchard.

What to Buy in Calasiao: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Apart from traditional delicacies like puto, Calasiao offers the following local products that would make great souvenirs:

  1. Hand-woven Products - Calasiao is known for its handicrafts made from pandan leaves and other natural materials.

  2. Coconut Vinegar - Tuba is not the only product that comes from coconuts in Calasiao. Coconut vinegar is a staple used in many local dishes and makes a unique and tasty souvenir.

Local Culture and Customs of Calasiao

Calasiao is a friendly and welcoming town that values its cultural heritage. When visiting, respect locals’ customs and traditions, like asking for permission before taking photos of people or cultural events.

Best Time to Visit Calasiao

The best time to visit Calasiao is from November to April when the climate is mild and dry.

Do’s and Don’ts of Calasiao

Do:

  • Observe proper etiquette when visiting churches or other cultural sites like museums and heritage centers
  • Respect the town’s traditions and customs
  • Try the local delicacies and beverages

Don’t:

  • Litter or harm the environment
  • Disrespect people or their way of life
  • Take photos without seeking permission first

Safety and Health

Safety in Calasiao

Calasiao is a generally safe town, but it is still important to remain vigilant when traveling. Always be aware of your surroundings and keep valuables out of sight to avoid being a target of theft.

Health in Calasiao

Make sure to bring mosquito repellent and sunscreen to protect yourself from insect bites and sunburns.

Getting to Calasiao: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Calasiao

The closest airport to Calasiao is Clark International Airport, which is approximately 2 hours away by car.

Bus Stations Near Calasiao

Victory Liner, which has buses running to and from Manila, Baguio, and Dagupan, has a station in Calasiao.

Other Options

Taxis and tricycles are the most common modes of transportation around town.

How to Get Around in Calasiao

The best way to get around town is by tricycle, a common mode of transportation in the Philippines. Tricycles are also available for rent for whole-day tours or transfers to other destinations.

Where to Go Next from Calasiao: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Calasiao is a great starting point to explore other destinations in Pangasinan. Here are some nearby places worth visiting:

  1. Dagupan City - A bustling city known for its bangus and other delicacies, only a 20-minute drive from Calasiao.

  2. Alaminos City - Famous for its Hundred Islands National Park, a 2-hour drive from Calasiao.

Conclusion

Calasiao is a town with a rich history and culture, natural beauty, and amazing cuisine. A trip to Calasiao is a perfect way to experience the local lifestyle and explore the hidden treasures of Luzon.
