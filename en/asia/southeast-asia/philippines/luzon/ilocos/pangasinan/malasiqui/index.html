Malasiqui is a town situated in the eastern part of Pangasinan in Region 1, Northern Luzon, Philippines. This agricultural municipality, locally known for their rice and garlic production, provides a tranquil and offbeat experience for travelers. Despite being relatively unknown, Malasiqui has its gems waiting to be discovered. Here’s a comprehensive guide to help you plan your visit to Malasiqui:

Introduction to Malasiqui

Malasiqui is a fourth-class municipality in the province of Pangasinan. The town’s name originated from a Pangasinense dialect “malasik,” a type of woven bamboo used as a fence. The town’s primary livelihood is agriculture, with rice and garlic as their main products. The municipality’s poblacion, or town center, has basic establishments such as a public market, hospital, schools, and commercial banks.

List of Places to Visit in Malasiqui

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Malasiqui Museum

Located within the town’s public market, the Malasiqui Museum showcases the municipality’s culture and history through various artifacts and memorabilia. Some notable exhibits are agricultural gadgets, traditional clothing, and artifacts that highlight the town’s historical events. The museum opens from Tuesday to Saturday, from 8:00 AM to 12:00 PM and from 1:00 PM to 5:00 PM.

Natural Attractions of Malasiqui: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Balingasay River

Balingasay River is Malasiqui’s primary water source and serves as a popular destination for swimming, fishing, boating, and picnicking. The river’s clean and crystal-clear waters provide visitors with a refreshing experience amidst the summer heat.

Malasiqui Integrated Park

The Malasiqui Integrated Park, also known as the “Melaleuca Tree Park,” is home to hundreds of Melaleuca trees from Australia and attracts locals and visitors for jogging, walking, and biking. The park offers a relaxing ambiance and serves as an excellent location for picnics and family bonding time.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Malasiqui

Mount Balungao

If you’re looking for an adventure, Mount Balungao offers hiking and biking routes with stunning views of the malunggay and acacia trees that surround the mountain. The summit is around 388 meters above sea level and takes about two hours to complete.

Best Things to Do in Malasiqui: Activities and Tours

Malasiqui Garlic Festival

If you’re visiting Malasiqui in May, don’t miss their Garlic Festival celebration filled with garlic-inspired dishes, events, and activities. This festival is a thanksgiving celebration and an opportunity for the town’s garlic farmers to showcase their produce.

Food and Drink Scene of Malasiqui

Must-Try Dishes

Garlic Longganisa

The Malasiqui Garlic Longganisa is a type of sausage seasoned with local garlic grown within the municipality. These sausages are best paired with rice, eggs, and vinegar.

Puto Calasiao

Puto Calasiao, a steamed rice cake, is a Pangasinense delicacy perfect to be eaten as a snack or dessert. These rice cakes are soft, chewy, and made with glutinous rice and sugar.

Local Beverages

Sugarcane Juice

Malasiqui has a vast sugarcane plantation, and one of the popular drinks made from sugarcane is the freshly squeezed sugarcane juice. This beverage is perfect to quench your thirst during hot weather.

Where to Stay in Malasiqui: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Wayfarers’ Overnight Lodge

Located in the heart of Malasiqui’s town proper, Wayfarers’ Overnight Lodge offers a clean and comfortable accommodation that suits any budget.

What to Buy in Malasiqui: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Garlic

Malasiqui Garlic, known for its pungent and distinct flavor, is the town’s most popular product. Garlic stalls can be found along National Highway, where tourists can buy fresh garlic or garlic-related products as souvenirs.

Local Culture and Customs of Malasiqui

The town’s primary religion is Roman Catholic, and their patron saint is St. Joseph. Most of the town’s festivities coincide with the religious events of the Roman Catholic Church.

Best Time to Visit Malasiqui

The best time to visit Malasiqui is from November to May when the dry season sets in, providing the best weather for outdoor activities, farm tours, and garlic harvesting.

Do’s and Don’ts of Malasiqui

Do:

  1. Wear comfortable clothes and footwear suitable for outdoor activities.
  2. Try the local delicacies like Puto Calasiao and Garlic Longganisa.
  3. Respect the locals and their culture.

Don’t:

  1. Litter around and pollute the natural and cultural sites.
  2. Enter religious sites with inappropriate clothing.

Safety and Health

Safety in Malasiqui

Malasiqui is generally a safe town, but always be mindful of your surroundings and belongings, especially in crowded areas.

Health in Malasiqui

It is essential to bring mosquito repellent as mosquitoes thrive in rural areas. Stay hydrated and wear sunscreen to avoid dehydration and sunburn.

Getting to Malasiqui: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Malasiqui

The closest airport to Malasiqui is the Clark International Airport located around 98 kilometers away.

Bus Stations Near Malasiqui

Victory Liner and Five Star Bus have direct trips to Malasiqui from the bus terminals in Pasay City, Cubao, and Dagupan.

Other Options

If you’re coming from Manila, take the NLEX and SCTEX to Tarlac, then exit on Luisita, go straight to Urdaneta City then head to Malasiqui via the National Highway.

How to Get Around in Malasiqui

Tricycles are the primary mode of transportation around Malasiqui and neighboring towns.

Where to Go Next from Malasiqui: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

From Malasiqui, you can visit other charming towns in Pangasinan such as Alaminos City, where you can explore the Hundred Islands National Park, and Bolinao, where you can visit the enchanting Patar Beach.

Conclusion

Malasiqui, Pangasinan, may not be the most popular travel destination in the Philippines, but its people, culture, and natural beauty will leave a lasting impression on visitors who choose to venture off the beaten path. Plan your visit and discover Malasiqui’s gems waiting to be explored.
