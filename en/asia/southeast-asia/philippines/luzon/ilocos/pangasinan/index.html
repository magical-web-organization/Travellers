Introduction to Pangasinan

Pangasinan is a province in the Ilocos Region of Luzon, Philippines, known for its stunning coastline, beautiful beaches, and rich cultural heritage. This beautiful province is home to a number of natural wonders, including the Hundred Islands National Park, the Bolinao Falls, and the Patar Beach, which makes it a favorite destination among both local and international tourists.

List of Places to Visit in Pangasinan

Whether you’re a history buff, an adventure seeker, or simply looking for a relaxing vacation away from the hustle and bustle of big cities, Pangasinan has something to offer for everyone. Here’s a list of some of the must-visit places in Pangasinan, divided into two categories: culturally important attractions and natural attractions.

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Bolinao Museum: This museum is a must-visit for anyone interested in Pangasinan’s rich cultural heritage. It features a collection of artifacts and exhibits that showcase the province’s history and traditions.
  • Bolo Beach: This beach is famous for its unique looking rock formations that make an Instagram-worthy photo opportunity.
  • Bolinao Church, St James the Great Parish: An old church in Bolinao that has history as a self-sustaining fortress called “Kuta” for Spaniards who lived in the area combines history with architecture that’s worth a visit.
  • Hundred Island National Park: The park is a collection of over 100 small islands that are located off the coast of Pangasinan, which is known of its white sands and crystal-clear waters.

Natural Attractions of Pangasinan: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Bolinao Falls: Bolinao Falls is a perfect spot for nature lovers. Take a hike in the jungle, and you’ll be rewarded with a refreshing dip in the cool natural pool beneath the cascading waterfalls.
  • Boracay Island: Boracay Island’s sugar-white beaches and turquoise waters are a beautiful sight to behold. The island has resorts where you can enjoy various activities such as snorkeling, parasailing, jet skiing, and island-hopping.
  • Patar Beach: Located in Bolinao, it’s the white beach in Pangasinan that stretches long and surrounded by lush cliffs, giving an awe-inspiring view that’s worth a visit.
  • Tambobong Beach: Tambobong Beach is a hidden gem tucked away from the beaten path in Dasol, known for its long stretch of clean, golden sand beach.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Pangasinan

Pangasinan isn’t just about beaches and outdoor adventures. It also has a variety of opportunities for hikers and bikers. Here are some of the best routes to explore:

  • Mount Balungao: Mount Balungao provides a great challenge for hikers who are up for the adventure. Its trails offer a great view of the surroundings and the chance to appreciate the vastness of Pangasinan.
  • Lingayen Gulf: This coastal area provides a scenic view of the adjacent hills and landmarks while you enjoy the warmth of the sun and the fresh sea breeze.
  • Bolinao - Anda: This route takes you through beautiful landscapes, natural wonders, and small coastal towns.

Best Things to Do in Pangasinan: Activities and Tours

Apart from the vast number of natural and cultural attractions, Pangasinan also offers a wide range of activities and tours to make your vacation more exciting. Here are some of the best things to do in Pangasinan:

  • Island Hopping: Hundred Islands National Park is the perfect place to go if you enjoy island hopping. You can rent a paddleboat or a motorized boat to take you around the islands.
  • Scuba Diving: Explore the depths of the sea and discover the diverse marine life at places like Bolinao and Patar Beach.
  • Enjoy Water Sports: Have fun with water sports like kayaking, skimboarding, surfing, and fishing. You can enjoy these activities at any of the beaches in Pangasinan.

Food and Drink Scene of Pangasinan

Pangasinan is famous for its seafood dishes and exotic recipes that offer a unique culinary experience. Here are some of the must-try dishes and beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Pigar-Pigar: This dish is made of thinly sliced beef mixed with onions and served with calamansi and hot chili.
  • Bangus: Milkfish is the famous local dish in Pangasinan and is found in various recipes such as grilled, fried, or stewed.
  • Ukoy: An appetizer inspired by tempura but made from shrimp and mixed with tofu, mung bean sprouts, and bean curd.

Local Beverages

  • Basuella: Made from fermented rice, this wine has a sweet taste, and some are bottled while others are aged in a jar.
  • Bagoong: This is a famous condiment and drink that is made of fermented fish or shrimp combined with salt, sugar, and vinegar.
  • Cocoa Wine: Also called “Basi”, it is made from fermented sugar cane, and the sugar cane is soaked in water for a week before being boiled. It is then left for six months to ferment before being bottled.

Where to Stay in Pangasinan: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Pangasinan has a good selection of accommodations, to suit your needs and budget. Here are some of the best places to stay:

  • Puerto Del Sol Resort: Located in Bolinao, it is the most beautiful resort in Pangasinan and has a variety of activities for guests, including kayaking, snorkeling, and island hopping.
  • La Virgen Hotel and Resort: Located in Lingayen, it’s a family-friendly destination offering various facilities and amenities to your liking.
  • El Puerto Marina Beach Resort: A perfect location for families looking for a luxurious beachfront getaway.

What to Buy in Pangasinan: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Pangasinan is known for its unique and delicious delicacies and products. Here are some of the local products and souvenirs that you should consider bringing back home:

  • Bangus: Pangasinan is also known as the home of the best milkfish. You can choose to bring back whole Sinigang kits with soup base, fish sauce, and vegetables.
  • Pangasinan Bagoong: This fermented fish condiment is a must-have for cooking various local dishes, which Pangasinans are known for.
  • Pangasinan Rice Cookies (Kakanin): This gluten-free snack, made of rice and coconut, is delicious and a perfect souvenir that can last for weeks.

Local Culture and Customs of Pangasinan

Pangasinan is a cultural hub with its history and traditions. Here are a few customs and beliefs that is good to know as a visitor:

  • Most Pangasinians are devout Catholics, so respect their churches and religious beliefs.
  • If you’re invited to a local dinner, it’s customary to bring a gift.
  • Pangasinians have their Palamata which means a souvenir given to guests as a token of appreciation.

Best Time to Visit Pangasinan

The best time to visit Pangasinan is from October to March when the weather is at its best with less risk of rainfall. The peak season runs from December to January when everyone is on vacation, so book in advance if you’re planning to visit around that time.

Do’s and Don’ts of Pangasinan

To enjoy a peaceful and hassle-free trip to Pangasinan, here are some tips on what to do and what not to do:

Do:

  • Respect the local culture and customs.
  • Try the local delicacies and food products.
  • Enjoy the natural attractions and outdoor adventures.

Don’t:

  • Litter the beaches, waterfalls, and other natural attractions.
  • Wear revealing clothes in public areas.
  • Disrespect local religious beliefs and practices.

Safety and Health

Pangasinan is a safe destination, but like with any other place, travelers should take precautions and be mindful of their surroundings. Here are some tips for safety and health:

Safety in Pangasinan

  • Avoid carrying too much cash when exploring cities and towns.
  • Be careful when swimming in natural bodies of water, especially during monsoon season, as strong currents can be dangerous.
  • Always use sunscreen, especially when outdoors.

Health in Pangasinan

  • Pangasinan is generally a safe destination with no major health threats, but it’s always good to take precautions.
  • Ensure that you have a sufficient supply of your prescription medication, as some may not be available locally.
  • Carry a first-aid kit with you if you plan to go trekking or do any outdoor activities.

Getting to Pangasinan: Transportation Options and Logistics

For travelers visiting Pangasinan, here are some transportation options and logistics to help you get there:

Airports Near Pangasinan

  • Clark International Airport: Located in Pampanga, Clark International Airport is the nearest airport to Pangasinan, about two to three hours drive from Pangasinan.
  • Ninoy Aquino International Airport: Located in Manila, the capital city, the airport is approximately about 5 hours drive from Pangasinan.

Bus Stations Near Pangasinan

  • Victory Liner: Victory Liner has daily trips to various destinations in Pangasinan such as Bolinao, Lingayen, Alaminos, and Urdaneta.
  • 5-Star Bus: 5-Star Bus offers daily trips from Pasay or Cubao in Metro Manila and other destinations in Pangasinan.

Other Options

  • Van or Private Car: Renting a van or private car is also an option, especially for group travelers. The travel time from Manila to Lingayen is approximately 5-6 hours, depending on traffic.

How to Get Around in Pangasinan

Getting around in Pangasinan is convenient with various transportation options available, such as:

  • Jeepneys: Jeepneys are the most popular mode of transportation in Pangasinan, and they’re affordable and widely available.
  • Tricycles: Tricycles are also commonly used in Pangasinan for short distances, but they also travel to farther destinations.
  • Rental cars and motorbikes: Rental cars and motorbikes are available for travelers who prefer to have their transportation and don’t want to depend on public transportation.

Where to Go Next from Pangasinan: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

If you’re looking for more tourist spots outside of Pangasinan, here are some of the nearby destinations and excursions:

  • Baguio City: famous for its pine trees, strawberry, and other cold mountain produce, it’s a perfect destination if you want to cool off from the tropical heat of Pangasinan.
  • Vigan: Famous for its cobbled streets, Spanish colonial houses, and historic town plaza, Vigan is a UNESCO World Heritage Site that is a must-see when visiting Luzon.
  • La Union: The province is known for its surfing spots, beach resorts, and local wineries.

Conclusion

Pangasinan is a beautiful destination with a ton of things to offer, from natural wonders to beautiful beaches, rich cultural heritage to delicious cuisine, making it an ideal destination for any type of traveler. With this comprehensive travel guide, you’re sure to have an unforgettable experience in Pangasinan.
