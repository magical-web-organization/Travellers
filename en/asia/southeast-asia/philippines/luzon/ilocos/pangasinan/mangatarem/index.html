The Ultimate Travel Guide to Mangatarem, Pangasinan, Philippines

Mangatarem is a town in the province of Pangasinan, situated in the Ilocos Region of Luzon, the largest island in the Philippines. It is home to a diverse range of cultural and natural attractions, making it a favorite destination for travelers seeking to experience the laid-back Filipino lifestyle while immersing themselves in the country’s rich history and breathtaking landscapes.

List of Places to Visit in Mangatarem

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

One of the most essential things to do to learn more about Mangatarem’s history is to explore its museums and historical sites. Among the most popular landmarks to visit are the following:

  • Mangatarem Municipal Building - Built during the American occupation, this structure is a perfect example of neo-classical architecture.
  • Mangatarem Parish Church - Constructed in the early 1770s, this church is known for its remarkable baroque and neoclassical designs.
  • Bani National Forest Reserve - A 7,785-hectare protected forest and watershed that boasts various flora and fauna, including Philippine teak and hawksbill turtle.

Natural Attractions of Mangatarem: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Mangatarem offers several natural wonders that are worth visiting, whether by trekking, hiking, or kayaking. Here are some of the best places to explore:

  • Balungao Hilltop Adventure - Located just a few kilometers away from Mangatarem, this scenic spot offers a 360-degree view of the town’s rolling hills and lush greens.
  • Nalsoc Cave - Limestone cliffs enclose this cave, which is home to bats and opens up to a beached area where the Agno River flows.
  • Bani Sand Dunes - These 2,000-hectare sand dunes offer visitors a stunning view of the South China Sea and lush fields, as well as the best place to sandboard in the Philippines.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Mangatarem

Mangatarem is a haven for outdoor enthusiasts, with its winding roads and trails that provide the best scenery and challenge for trekkers and bikers. Here are the places to consider:

  • Mangatarem-Lian Trail - This straightforward trail covers mostly flat terrain but ends with a challenging uphill climb.
  • Balungao Hilltop Adventure Trail - A hike takes you through lush fields and forested areas before ending in the hilltop summit of Balungao.
  • Bolaoen-Lingayen Trail - This path takes you through a scenic town and rice paddies before leading to the sea.

Best Things to Do in Mangatarem: Activities and Tours

Mangatarem offers several activities that fit different interests, making it a must-visit place in Pangasinan. Here are some of the activities that you should try:

  • Kayaking at Nalsoc Cave - Paddle your way through the cave’s intruding water.
  • Visiting the Mangatarem Municipal Building - Step back in time and marvel at the neoclassical architecture.
  • Experience Sandboarding in Bani Sand Dunes - Explore and have fun in the 2,000-hectare sand dunes.

Food and Drink Scene of Mangatarem

While in Mangatarem, prepare your taste buds as you indulge in the variety of traditional Filipino cuisine that the town has to offer. Here are a few must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Puto - A rice cake that is steamed and served with a small amount of butter.
  • Binakol - A dish made with chicken meat and infused with coconut water and juice.
  • Sinigang na Bangus - The most popular Filipino soup made with fresh milkfish, vegetables, and tamarind.

Local Beverages

  • Palayamnon Wine - Made from sugarcane wine, a specialty drink that is uniquely Filipino.
  • Coffee - Best enjoyed with a slice of cassava cake, another favorite Pasalubong of the Mangatarem locals.

Where to Stay in Mangatarem: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

There are several accommodation options to choose from in Mangatarem, ranging from luxury resorts to budget-friendly guesthouses. Here are some of the best places to stay:

  • Balungao Hilltop Adventure Resort - A family-friendly resort that offers some of the best views of the town’s hillside.
  • Inohaan Backpackers Hostel - A budget-friendly hostel located in the heart of Mangatarem town.
  • Villa Tyco Resort - A spacious resort that boasts a swimming pool and garden for its guests to relax.

What to Buy in Mangatarem: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Here are some of the most popular souvenirs and local products to buy when in Mangatarem:

  • Mangatarem Goodies - Known as the town’s Pasalubong, these delicious treats include cassava cake, kakanin, and suman.
  • Handicrafts - Products made of buri, abaca, rattan, and bamboo, including bags, baskets, and mats, are available in the town.

Local Culture and Customs of Mangatarem

Mangatarem’s cultural practices are best seen during their religious festivals, which happen across the year.

  • Feast of the Holy Family - Celebrated every last weekend of January.
  • Feast of St. Joseph - Celebrated every 19th of March.

Best Time to Visit Mangatarem

The best time to visit Mangatarem is during the dry season, which runs from December to May. During this time, the weather is sunny, and water activities can be enjoyed.

Do’s and Don’ts of Mangatarem

Do:

  • Respect the local customs and traditions, including cultural practices and religious beliefs.
  • Dress conservatively and modestly when visiting the churches or cultural sites.
  • Have cash when visiting the smaller towns and restaurants.

Don’t:

  • Don’t litter or destroy the natural wonders in Mangatarem.
  • Don’t touch or disturb the local wildlife when hiking or exploring.

Safety and Health

Safety in Mangatarem

Mangatarem is a safe place for tourists, but always ensure your safety practices:

  • Always be cautious of your belongings.
  • Be careful and aware of your surroundings, especially when traveling at night.

Health in Mangatarem

It is essential to be mindful of your health conditions when traveling to Mangatarem. Always apply these tips:

  • Bring your medications, especially for chronic diseases.
  • Drink only boiled or bottled water, or carry a portable filter if possible.

Getting to Mangatarem: Transportation Options and Logistics

By air and land, travelers from Manila can reach Mangatarem by the following options:

Airports Near Mangatarem

  • Dagupan Airport in Pangasinan.
  • Clark Airport in Pampanga.

Bus Stations Near Mangatarem

  • Victory Liner in Cubao, Pasay, and Caloocan.
  • Dagupan Bus Lines in Cubao.

Other Options

  • Hire a private car or take a taxi from Manila.
  • Take a bus ride from any town in Pangasinan, such as Alaminos, Dagupan, and Bolinao.

How to Get Around in Mangatarem

Travelers can get around Mangatarem by taking a tricycle, the most common mode of transportation, or by renting a private car or motorcycle.

Where to Go Next from Mangatarem: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Travelers can continue their journey from Mangatarem to the following nearby destinations within Luzon:

  • Hundred Islands National Park in Alaminos, Pangasinan.
  • Tondol Beach in Anda, Pangasinan.
  • Manaoag Church in Manaoag, Pangasinan.
  • Bolinao in Pangasinan.

Conclusion

Mangatarem is a must-visit destination for travelers looking for a taste of the Philippines’ unique and varied culture, history, and stunning landscapes. With its diverse attractions, visitors will never run out of things to do and explore, making it a destination worth experiencing at least once in a lifetime.
