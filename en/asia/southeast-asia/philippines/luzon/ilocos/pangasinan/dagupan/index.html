Introduction to Dagupan

Dagupan is a bustling city situated in the province of Pangasinan, in the Ilocos Region of the Philippines. Famed for being the “Bangus Capital of the Philippines” and the home of the annual Bangus Festival, Dagupan is an excellent destination for those who want to experience beautiful views and authentic Filipino culture.

The city is located 216 km away from Metro Manila, and you can get there by bus or private car. The best time to visit Dagupan is from December to February when the weather is cooler, making it an ideal time for outdoor activities.

List of Places to Visit in Dagupan

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Dagupan offers a rich cultural heritage as it played a significant role in the Philippine Revolution. When visiting, be sure to check out the following:

  • Museum and Gallery of Dagupan City - This museum showcases the city’s rich cultural heritage, artifacts, and traditional clothing worn by residents. It also features photographs of the city’s significant events and historical landmarks.

  • Tondaligan Blue Beach - This beach is not only an important historical landmark, but it’s also a beautiful place to visit. The Tondaligan Blue Beach is part of the World War II Bataan Death March trail and was where the Americans landed during their liberation of Luzon.

Natural Attractions of Dagupan: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Dagupan offers a range of outdoor activities that let you explore nature and the great outdoors. Here are some must-visit places:

  • Pangasinan Provincial Capitol - This historic government building offers a magnificent view of the Agno River and nearby mountains.

  • Dagupan River Cruise - The Dagupan River Cruise takes you on a scenic boat ride that showcases the city’s natural beauty. You can see mangroves, floating restaurants, and friendly wildlife during your trip.

  • Mount Balungao - This active volcano is a fantastic spot for hiking trips. The site also features hot springs, zip-lining, and other outdoor activities.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Dagupan

  • Biking in Dagupan City - Biking is an excellent way to explore the city’s culture and heritage. You can bike around the Dagupan City Plaza and stop by the cathedral and nearby parks.

  • Hiking in Mount Balungao - Mount Balungao offers a range of hiking plans that cater to different levels of experience. The hiking trails take you through scenic forests and hillsides, offering fantastic views of the city.

Best Things to Do in Dagupan: Activities and Tours

  • Join the Bangus Festival – Every April, Dagupan hosts the Bangus Festival, a celebration of the city’s fish industry. Activities include street dancing, parades, cooking competitions, and cultural performances.

  • Visit the Basilica Minore of Our Lady of Manaoag – Located in the town of Manaoag, this church is famous for its miraculous image of the Virgin Mary. The church is a popular pilgrimage site and attracts thousands of devotees every year.

Week-Long Itinerary for Dagupan

Day 1: Explore the city and visit the Dagupan Museum and Gallery and the Dagupan City Plaza.

Day 2: Hike Mount Balungao and relax in one of the hot springs.

Day 3: Take in the scenic views of Tondaligan Blue Beach and the Pangasinan Provincial Capitol.

Day 4: Go kayaking or boating at the Dagupan River and enjoy the scenic views.

Day 5: Visit the Basilica Minore of Our Lady of Manaoag and attend mass.

Day 6-7: Spend the rest of your trip exploring the city’s markets and trying local food.

Food and Drink Scene of Dagupan

Must-Try Dishes

  • Bangus - This dish is a must-try when in Dagupan. It features milkfish prepared in various ways, such as steamed, grilled, roasted, and even in soup.

  • Papaitan - A savory dish made from beef tripe, liver, and broth, with vegetables and chili added to give it a little spice.

Local Beverages

  • Calamansi Juice - A refreshing beverage made with locally grown calamansi fruit juice.

  • Tuba - This traditional alcoholic drink is made with the sap of a coconut tree and is commonly enjoyed in the province of Pangasinan.

Where to Stay in Dagupan: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Dagupan has a range of accommodation options, from budget-friendly guesthouses to luxurious hotels. Here are some of our top picks:

  • Star Plaza Hotel - This hotel is located in the heart of the city and offers comfortable rooms, excellent service, and a restaurant.

  • Lenox Hotel - A modern hotel with elegant guest rooms, a restaurant, and an outdoor pool.

What to Buy in Dagupan: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

When in Dagupan, some local products to look out for include:

  • Puto Calasiao - This is a type of rice cake that originated from Calasiao, a town near Dagupan. It’s made from ground rice, sugar, and water and is perfect for those with a sweet tooth.

  • Binuburan - A savory snack made from rice grits mixed with coconut milk and spices. It is typically served with grilled bangus or other fish.

Local Culture and Customs of Dagupan

Dagupan is home to a mix of different cultures that are evident in its customs and traditions. Some things to keep in mind when visiting:

  • Respect Catholic Traditions - Dagupan is predominantly Catholic, and it’s important to respect local religious traditions and customs.

  • Be Respectful During the Bangus Festival - The Bangus Festival is a significant celebration in Dagupan, and it’s essential to be respectful of the traditions and customs associated with it.

Best Time to Visit Dagupan

The best time to visit Dagupan is from December to February when the weather is cooler and more comfortable for outdoor activities. The Bangus Festival also happens in April, which is a great time to visit if you want to participate in this iconic event.

Do’s and Don’ts of Dagupan

Do:

  • Do try local cuisine: Dagupan is known for its seafood and traditional Filipino dishes.

  • Do respect local customs and traditions.

Don’t:

  • Don’t litter: It’s essential to keep the city clean and litter-free.

  • Don’t wear revealing or inappropriate clothing when visiting religious sites.

Safety and Health

Safety in Dagupan

Dagupan is generally safe for travelers. However, it’s still essential to take precautions such as avoiding unlit areas at night and keeping your valuables secure.

Health in Dagupan

  • Vaccinations: Make sure to get vaccinated for hepatitis A and B, typhoid, tetanus, and rabies before traveling to Dagupan.

  • Water: It’s best to drink bottled water when in Dagupan, as the tap water may not be safe for drinking.

Getting to Dagupan: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Dagupan

The nearest airport to Dagupan is Clark International Airport, which is approximately 87 km away. From there, you can take a bus or hire a private car to reach Dagupan.

Bus Stations Near Dagupan

There are multiple bus stations in Dagupan, including the Victory Liner Terminal, where you can find buses that will take you to other parts of Luzon.

Other Options

If you prefer to drive, you can rent a car and drive to Dagupan from Manila.

How to Get Around in Dagupan

The best way to get around Dagupan is by tricycle or jeepney, which are the primary modes of transportation in the city. Fare prices can be negotiated with the driver, so make sure to bargain for the best price.

Where to Go Next from Dagupan: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

If you have some extra time to spare, there are plenty of nearby destinations worth visiting:

  • Alaminos City - Known for its beautiful Hundred Islands National Park, which features fantastic limestone formations and beaches.

  • Lingayen - A town that offers a rich history, beautiful beaches, and numerous attractions, such as the Lingayen Gulf War Memorial.

Conclusion

Dagupan is a beautiful city with a rich cultural heritage, offering a range of activities that cater to different interests. With this comprehensive travel guide, you’re now ready to plan your perfect trip to Dagupan. Don’t miss out on the Bangus Festival or trying the local dishes when you visit!
