Introduction to Sison

Sison is a town in the province of Pangasinan in the northern part of the Philippines. It is situated in the foothills of the Cordillera Mountains, about 25 kilometers away from the city of Baguio. Sison offers visitors a unique blend of natural beauty, cultural landmarks, and outdoor adventures. With its stunning landscapes and rich cultural heritage, Sison is a must-visit destination for anyone traveling to the Philippines.

List of Places to Visit in Sison

Sison has something to offer for everyone, whether you are a history enthusiast, a nature lover, or an adventure seeker. Here are some of the most popular places to visit in Sison:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Sison Museum

The Sison Museum is a must-visit destination for anyone interested in the history and culture of the town. The museum showcases a huge collection of artifacts and displays, including indigenous crafts, tools, and weapons. You can also learn about the town’s cultural heritage through interactive exhibits and audiovisual presentations.

Sison Church

The Sison Church is a beautiful Spanish colonial-era church that dates back to the 17th century. The church has a stunning Baroque facade with intricate carvings and detailing. Inside, you can admire the beautiful stained-glass windows and religious artwork.

Natural Attractions of Sison: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Mount Amuyao

Mount Amuyao is the highest mountain in the Cordillera Range and offers visitors a challenging but rewarding hike. The trek to the summit takes about 2-3 days and offers stunning views of the surrounding landscape, including the rice terraces of Batad and Banaue.

Tondol Beach

Tondol Beach is a hidden gem in Sison and is one of the most beautiful beaches in the Philippines. The beach has crystal-clear waters and soft white sand, making it the perfect place to relax and unwind.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Sison

Sison has some excellent hiking and biking routes that offer visitors a chance to explore the surrounding landscape. Here are some of the most popular routes:

Mount Balungao

Mount Balungao is a popular hiking destination in Sison. The trail is challenging but rewarding, with stunning views of the surrounding countryside and a chance to see the famous Balungao Hot Springs.

Tondol to Lingayen Bike Route

The Tondol to Lingayen Bike Route is a scenic biking route that takes you through beautiful coastal villages and stunning rice paddies. The route is relatively easy and is perfect for beginner cyclists.

Best Things to Do in Sison: Activities and Tours

Sison has a range of activities and tours available that cater to all ages and interests. Here are some of the best things to do in Sison:

Visit a Coffee Farm

Sison is famous for its coffee, and there are several coffee farms in the region that offer tours and tastings. You can learn about the coffee-making process and try some of the best coffee in the Philippines.

Explore the Waterfalls

Sison has several beautiful waterfalls that are worth a visit. One of the most popular is the Turod-Patadon Twin Waterfalls, which is located in the nearby town of San Nicolas. The waterfall is easily accessible and is a great spot for swimming and picnicking.

Food and Drink Scene of Sison

Sison has a range of delicious local dishes and drinks that are worth trying. Here are some must-try dishes and beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

Dinakdakan

Dinakdakan is a traditional Ilocano dish made from boiled and grilled pig’s head and ears. It is often served with onions, chili peppers, and vinegar.

Longganisa

Longganisa is a type of sausage that is popular in the Philippines. It is made from ground pork, garlic, and vinegar and is often served for breakfast with rice and eggs.

Local Beverages

Kapeng Barako

Kapeng Barako is a type of coffee that is unique to the Philippines. It is made from beans that are grown in the Batangas region and has a strong, distinct flavor.

Lambanog

Lambanog is a traditional Filipino liquor made from coconut sap. It is often served with ice or mixed in cocktails.

Where to Stay in Sison: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Sison has a range of accommodation options that cater to all budgets and preferences. Here are some of the best places to stay in Sison:

Budget Accommodation

Sison Plaza Hotel

The Sison Plaza Hotel is a budget-friendly hotel that offers clean and comfortable rooms. It is located in the town center, making it a convenient base for exploring the region.

Mid-Range Accommodation

Balungao Hilltop Adventure

Balungao Hilltop Adventure is a mid-range hotel that offers comfortable rooms and stunning views of the surrounding countryside. It also has an on-site restaurant and outdoor pool.

Luxury Accommodation

The Manor at Camp John Hay

The Manor at Camp John Hay is a luxurious hotel located in nearby Baguio City. It has beautifully appointed rooms, a range of amenities, and stunning views of the countryside.

What to Buy in Sison: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Sison is known for its high-quality coffee and rich cultural heritage, making it the perfect place to pick up some unique souvenirs and gifts. Here are some of the best things to buy in Sison:

Coffee

Sison’s coffee is some of the best in the Philippines and makes the perfect souvenir or gift. You can buy whole beans or ground coffee at local markets and coffee shops.

Handicrafts

Sison is known for its traditional handicrafts, including woven baskets, pottery, and woodcarvings. You can buy these items at local markets and souvenir shops.

Local Culture and Customs of Sison

Sison has a rich cultural heritage, with strong Ilocano traditions and customs. Visitors are expected to respect these traditions and customs during their stay in the town. Here are some things to keep in mind:

  • Dress modestly when visiting churches and other cultural landmarks
  • Avoid public displays of affection
  • Be respectful of local customs and traditions

Best Time to Visit Sison

The best time to visit Sison is during the dry season, which runs from November to April. This is when the weather is most pleasant, and outdoor activities are more enjoyable.

Do’s and Don’ts of Sison

  • Respect local customs and traditions

  • Engage in public displays of affection

Safety and Health

Safety in Sison

Sison is a relatively safe town, but visitors should take normal precautions while traveling. This includes staying in well-lit and populated areas, keeping valuables out of sight, and being aware of their surroundings.

Health in Sison

Sison has a range of medical facilities, including clinics and hospitals. Visitors should ensure they have adequate health insurance before traveling and take normal precautions, such as avoiding tap water.

Getting to Sison: Transportation Options and Logistics

Sison is easily accessible by a range of transportation options. Here are some of the most common ways to get to Sison:

Airports Near Sison

The nearest airport to Sison is the Clark International Airport in Angeles City, which is about 3 hours away by car.

Bus Stations Near Sison

There are several bus companies that serve Sison, including Victory Liner, Dagupan Bus, and Five Star Bus. Buses depart from major cities including Manila, Baguio, and Dagupan.

Other Options

Visitors can also hire a private car or take a taxi to reach Sison. This is a convenient option for those traveling in larger groups or with young children.

How to Get Around in Sison

Sison is a small town that is best explored on foot or by bicycle. Visitors can also hire a tricycle to get around town or a private car or van for longer journeys.

Where to Go Next from Sison: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Sison is located in the beautiful northern region of the Philippines and is a great base for exploring other nearby destinations. Here are some of the best places to visit:

  • Sagada: a charming mountain town famous for its caves and stunning scenery
  • Batad: home to the famous rice terraces and a UNESCO World Heritage Site
  • Baguio: a city known for its cool climate, stunning views, and cultural landmarks

Conclusion

Sison is a hidden gem in the northern part of the Philippines, offering visitors a unique blend of natural beauty, cultural landmarks, and outdoor adventures. With its stunning landscapes and rich cultural heritage, Sison is a must-visit destination for anyone traveling to the Philippines.
