Introduction to La Union

La Union is a province located in the northwestern part of Luzon Island in the Philippines. It is known for its stunning beaches, perfect waves, and lush mountains. La Union is a popular destination for both local and international tourists who seek relaxation, adventure, and cultural immersion.

List of Places to Visit in La Union

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

La Union is rich in history and culture. Here are the must-visit museums and historical places:

  • La Union Museum: Located in Agoo, this museum showcases the province’s cultural heritage, from pre-colonial times to the present.
  • St. William The Hermit Cathedral: This grand cathedral in San Fernando is a popular pilgrimage site. It is one of the largest churches in the region and features intricate Neo-Gothic architecture.
  • Balay na Bato: This ancestral house-turned-museum in Luna showcases the traditional lifestyle of the Ilocanos, the province’s predominant ethnic group.
  • Pindangan Ruins: These ruins in San Fernando are the remains of a Spanish watchtower and an old church. They offer a glimpse into the province’s turbulent colonial past.

Natural Attractions of La Union: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

La Union boasts several natural wonders that are sure to awe visitors. Here are the best outdoor adventures and scenic views:

  • Tangadan Falls: Located in San Gabriel and surrounded by lush greenery, this waterfall is a natural wonder that is worth the trek. The falls have a natural pool that’s perfect for swimming.
  • Bauang Beach: This beach in the town of Bauang is one of La Union’s most popular destinations. It offers long stretches of golden sand and clear waters perfect for swimming and other water sports.
  • Ma-Cho Temple: Located in San Fernando, this temple is considered a cultural landmark. It features ornate architecture and intricate wood carvings, and offers panoramic views of the coastline.
  • Poro Point: This promontory in San Fernando offers stunning views of the South China Sea. It is home to several luxury resorts, as well as a golf course and a lighthouse.

Hiking/Biking Routes of La Union

La Union is a hiker’s and biker’s paradise. Here are the best trails:

  • Mount Darala: Located in San Fernando, this mountain offers several trails for beginners and experienced hikers alike. The view at the summit is worth the effort.
  • Bakun Trio: This trail includes three peaks in the Bakun Mountains: Mt. Kabunian, Mt. Tenglawan, and Mt. Lobo. It is a challenging hike that rewards with amazing views of mountain ranges, terraced rice fields, and vegetable farms.
  • La Union Cycling Route: This 34-kilometer route passes through several municipalities in the province. It offers a scenic view of beaches, mountains, and rice fields. Some of the key stops include the Tangadan Falls and the Ma-Cho Temple.

Best Things to Do in La Union: Activities and Tours

La Union has plenty of things to offer to visitors, from relaxing at the beach to trying out unique outdoor activities. Here are the must-try things to do:

  • Surfing: La Union is known as the surfing capital of the northern Philippines. The surfing season runs from October to April, and the best spots are Urbiztondo and San Juan. There are plenty of instructors and surf schools for beginners.
  • Island-hopping: Take a boat tour to explore La Union’s nearby islands, such as Pagudpud and Bolinao. These islands boast of pristine beaches, crystal-clear waters, and dramatic rock formations.
  • Wine-tasting: La Union is known for producing excellent wines, such as the Tapuey (rice wine) and Bugnay (wild berry wine). Take a tour of the vineyards and wineries and taste some of the best wines in the country.
  • Caving: Explore the numerous caves in La Union, such as Bessang Cave in Luna and Pindangan Cave in San Fernando. These caves are home to unique rock formations and crystal-clear pools.

Week-Long Itinerary for La Union

Day 1: Arrive in San Fernando, check into a hotel, and explore the town center. Visit St. William The Hermit Cathedral and the La Union Museum.

Day 2: Start the day with a trip to Tangadan Falls. In the afternoon, surf or swim at Bauang Beach.

Day 3: Take a day trip to the nearby town of Agoo to visit the Pindangan Ruins. In the afternoon, take a bike tour of the La Union Cycling Route.

Day 4: Explore the town of Luna and visit the Balay na Bato museum. In the afternoon, take a dip in the beaches of Luna.

Day 5: Take a tour of the vineyards and wineries of La Union, such as the Gapuz Grape Farm and the Lomboy Farms. Sample some delicious wines.

Day 6: Explore the nearby town of San Juan and hit the waves. Surfing lessons are available for beginners.

Day 7: Depart from La Union.

Food and Drink Scene of La Union

La Union is a foodie’s paradise. Here are the must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Pinakbet: This classic Ilocano dish is a vegetable stew made with native vegetables and shrimp paste.
  • Dinengdeng: This soup dish originated from Pangasinan, but is widely available in La Union. It is made with fish, vegetables, and spices.
  • Ar-arosep: This is a porridge dish made with sticky rice and coconut milk. It is typically served with mango slices and coconut strips.

Local Beverages

  • Tapuey: This is a traditional Ilocano rice wine that’s usually served during celebrations and special occasions.
  • Bugnay wine: This is a specialty wine made from wild berries and is only available in the northern part of the country.

Where to Stay in La Union: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

La Union offers a wide range of accommodations that cater to different budgets and preferences. Here are some top picks:

  • Thunderbird Resorts & Casino: This luxury resort in San Fernando offers stunning ocean views, a casino, and a spa.
  • La Union Surf Resort: This budget-friendly beachfront resort in Urbiztondo is perfect for surfers and beach lovers.
  • Urbiz Garden Bed and Breakfast: This cozy guesthouse in San Juan offers a peaceful retreat surrounded by a lush garden.

What to Buy in La Union: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

La Union is known for its unique handicrafts and local products that make for great souvenirs. Here are some must-buy items:

  • Blankets: These blankets are hand-woven by the Ilocanos and come in different colors and patterns.
  • Pottery: The town of Sto. Tomas is known for producing traditional earthenware pottery, such as the “burnay” jars and “anay” pots.
  • Anting-anting: These amulets are believed to offer protection and good luck. They are often made of indigenous materials and come in various shapes and sizes.

Local Culture and Customs of La Union

La Union is home to the Ilocano ethnic group, which has a distinct culture and tradition. Visitors are encouraged to respect local customs, such as:

  • Taking off your shoes: It is polite to take off your shoes when entering someone’s home.
  • Using the right hand: The left hand is considered unclean and should be avoided when eating or passing items.
  • Respecting the elders: It is customary to show respect to elders by addressing them with the appropriate titles.

Best Time to Visit La Union

The best time to visit La Union is from October to April, when the weather is dry and the waves are perfect for surfing.

Do’s and Don’ts of La Union

Do:

  • Try surfing: La Union is known as the surfing capital of the North and is a great place to learn or improve your skills.
  • Respect local customs: Be mindful of local customs and traditions and show respect to the locals.
  • Take caution when swimming: Some beaches have strong currents, so always be cautious when swimming.

Don’t:

  • Litter: Be mindful of your trash and dispose of it properly to help preserve the natural beauty of the province.
  • Disrespect religious sites: Be respectful when visiting religious sites and refrain from making loud noises or taking inappropriate photos.
  • Engage in illegal activities: Always follow the law and avoid engaging in illegal activities.

Safety and Health

Safety in La Union

La Union is generally a safe place to visit, but as with any destination, visitors should take precautions to ensure their safety. Here are some tips:

  • Be aware of your surroundings: Always keep an eye out for pickpockets and other petty criminals.
  • Take caution when swimming: Some beaches have strong currents, so always be cautious when swimming.
  • Drive with caution: Some roads in the province are narrow and winding, so be careful while driving or hire a local driver.

Health in La Union

La Union has several hospitals and clinics, but visitors should take extra precautions to avoid illnesses. Here are some tips:

  • Stay hydrated: The hot and humid weather can easily dehydrate you, so make sure to drink plenty of water.
  • Protect yourself from the sun: Apply sunscreen and wear a hat or sunglasses to protect yourself from the sun.
  • Avoid eating street food: To avoid foodborne illnesses, stick to clean restaurants and avoid eating street food.

Getting to La Union: Transportation Options and Logistics

La Union is accessible by land and air. Here are some transportation options:

Airports Near La Union

  • Laoag International Airport: This airport is located in Ilocos Norte, about 100 kilometers north of La Union.
  • Clark International Airport: This airport is located in Pampanga, about 124 kilometers south of La Union.

Bus Stations Near La Union

  • Partas Bus Station: This bus company has several terminals located throughout La Union, and offers daily trips to and from Manila, Baguio, and other nearby provinces.
  • Dominion Bus Lines: This bus company has terminals in Cubao and Pasay in Manila, and offers trips to and from San Fernando in La Union.

Other Options

  • Private car rental: Visitors can also rent a car or hire a local driver to get around La Union and nearby destinations.
  • Shared vans: Shared vans are also available for hire in major towns and cities in the province.

How to Get Around in La Union

The best way to explore La Union is by hiring a tricycle, which is a common form of transportation in the province. Visitors can also rent a car or a scooter. Walking and biking are also good options for exploring smaller towns and beaches.

Where to Go Next from La Union: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

La Union is a great place to start exploring the other provinces of Ilocos. Here are some nearby destinations and excursions:

  • Vigan: This historic town in Ilocos Sur is known for its Spanish colonial architecture and rich culture.
  • Pagudpud: This coastal town in Ilocos Norte offers stunning beaches, waterfalls, and lagoons.
  • Bolinao: This town in Pangasinan offers picture-perfect beaches, stunning caves, and a lighthouse.

Conclusion

La Union is a destination that offers a blend of nature, culture, and adventure. From its stunning beaches and world-class surf spots, to its rich history and culture, La Union has something for every type of traveler. Plan your trip now and experience the best of what this province has to offer.
