A Comprehensive Travel Guide to Boac, Philippines

Boac is a hidden gem found on the island of Marinduque. Nestled in a valley surrounded by green hills, it is a town that showcases Marinduque’s complex historical background, rich culture, and natural attractions. If you’re looking for a destination that’s rich in culture, beautiful landscapes, and adventure, Boac is the perfect place to go. Here’s a comprehensive travel guide that sheds light on everything Boac has to offer.

Introduction to Boac

Boac is a town located on the island of Marinduque in the Mimaropa region, also known as Region IV-B, in the Philippines. It has a population of approximately 60,000 people. Boac is a town known for its rich history, culture, and natural attractions.

Local Culture and Customs of Boac

Boac is home to Marinduque’s unique culture and customs. One of the most notable customs in Boac is the “Moriones Festival”. This festival is celebrated annually during Holy Week. During the festival, locals wear colorful masks and costumes, portraying biblical figures.

Best Time to Visit Boac

The best time to visit Boac is from February to April. During this time, the weather is perfect for outdoor activities, and the town is bustling with the “Moriones Festival” celebrations.

Do:

  • Explore the natural attractions.

Don’t:

  • Litter on beaches or any tourist spots.
  • Disrespect locals.
  • Wear revealing clothes in public areas.

Safety and Health

Safety in Boac

Boac is a safe town to travel to. However, like any other destination, visitors should take safety precautions, such as:

  • Avoid walking in dimly-lit areas during night time.
  • Always be aware of your surroundings.
  • Don’t carry too much cash and expensive jewelry.

Health in Boac

While there are no major diseases prevalent in Boac, visitors should take preventive measures, such as drinking only bottled water, applying mosquito repellent, and seeking medical attention if necessary.

Getting to Boac: Transportation Options and Logistics

Boac is accessible through different modes of transportation. Visitors can get to Boac through:

Airports Near Boac

  • Marinduque Airport (via Manila or Cebu)

Bus Stations Near Boac

  • Jac Liner Terminal in Cubao, Quezon City (via Batangas Port to Boac Port)
  • JAC Liner Terminal in Pasay City (via Batangas Port to Boac Port)

Other Options

  • Private vehicles
  • Renting a car or motorcycle

How to Get Around Boac

Getting around Boac is easy and affordable. Visitors can use tricycles, jeepneys, and Habal-Habal. Car rental services are also available for tourists who prefer private transportation.
