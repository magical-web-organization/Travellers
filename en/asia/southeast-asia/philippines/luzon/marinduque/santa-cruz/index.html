Introduction to Santa Cruz

Santa Cruz is a charming town located in the province of Marinduque, in the heart of the Philippines. This beautiful town is situated on the island of Luzon, which is part of Southeast Asia. Santa Cruz is known for its cultural and historical significance, as well as its natural beauty and outdoor adventures. The town is home to beautiful beaches, scenic views, and fascinating landmarks that are just waiting to be explored.

List of Places to Visit in Santa Cruz

Whether you’re a first-time visitor or a seasoned traveler, Santa Cruz offers plenty of amazing places to explore. Here are some of the top attractions that you shouldn’t miss:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Santa Cruz Church: This historic church is over 200 years old and is a must-visit for anyone interested in local history and culture.
  • Marinduque Museum: Learn about the rich cultural heritage of Santa Cruz and the Marinduque province by visiting this fantastic museum.
  • Boac Cathedral: This stunning cathedral boasts incredible architecture and is an important religious landmark in Marinduque.

Natural Attractions of Santa Cruz: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Poctoy White Beach: One of the most beautiful beaches in Santa Cruz, Poctoy White Beach offers pristine white sand and crystal clear water. Perfect for swimming, sunbathing, and relaxation.
  • Bathala Caves: Visit these awe-inspiring caves to see stunning rock formations, unique biodiversity, and learn about the history of the area.
  • Mount Malindig: Hike to the top of this majestic mountain to enjoy breathtaking views of the surrounding countryside.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Santa Cruz

Santa Cruz is an ideal destination for outdoor enthusiasts who love hiking and biking. Whether you’re a seasoned adventurer or a beginner, there are plenty of trails in and around Santa Cruz that offer a range of difficulty levels.

  • Mount Malindig: This challenging hike takes you to the summit of Mount Malindig, offering panoramic views of the Marinduque province.
  • Maniwaya Island: Biking around Maniwaya Island is a popular activity that will take you to beautiful sites such as Palad Sandbar, Ungab Rock Formation, and the Mangroves.

Best Things to Do in Santa Cruz: Activities and Tours

  • Island hopping: Discover some of the most beautiful islands around Santa Cruz and enjoy snorkeling, swimming, and breathtaking views.
  • Marinduque Moriones: Witness the famous Marinduque Moriones, an annual religious festival in Marinduque Province.
  • Visiting Gasan Beach: Enjoy the white sandy beach in Gasan which is only 30 minutes away.

Food and Drink Scene of Santa Cruz

Must-Try Dishes

Santa Cruz is known for its rich and flavorful cuisine. Here are some dishes that you shouldn’t miss:

  • Adobo: Pork or chicken marinated in vinegar, soy sauce, and garlic, cooked until tender and served with rice.
  • Sinigang: A sour soup made with pork, fish, or shrimp and vegetables such as tomatoes, onions, and taro.
  • Suman: A traditional Filipino dessert made from glutinous rice wrapped in banana leaves.

Local Beverages

  • Coconut water: Freshly harvested coconut water is available throughout Santa Cruz.
  • Lambanog: A Filipino coconut wine made from the sap of coconut flowers.

Where to Stay in Santa Cruz: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Santa Cruz offers a range of accommodation options to suit every budget. Here are some top picks:

  • Bellarocca Island Resort and Spa: An upscale resort offering luxurious accommodations, private beaches, and breathtaking views.
  • Pherradula Lodge: A budget-friendly guesthouse located in the heart of Santa Cruz.
  • Casa de Don Leandro: A charming guesthouse with a relaxing garden and easy access to local attractions.

What to Buy in Santa Cruz: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Marinduque’s traditional kudong: A type of hat made from abaca fiber and woven in a centuries-old ritual.
  • Pina Cloth: A handwoven cloth from pineapple fibers, usually made into a Barong Tagalog shirt.

Local Culture and Customs of Santa Cruz

The people of Santa Cruz are known for their warm hospitality and deeply rooted cultural traditions. It’s important to respect the customs of the locals, which includes taking off your shoes before entering a home and dressing appropriately when visiting religious sites.

Best Time to Visit Santa Cruz

The best time to visit Santa Cruz is during the dry season which starts in November and ends in May. During this time, the weather is pleasant and ideal for outdoor activities.

Do’s and Don’ts of Santa Cruz

Do:

  • Always ask permission before taking a photo of someone.
  • Respect the customs and traditions of the locals.

Don’t:

  • Don’t litter. Keep Santa Cruz clean and beautiful.
  • Don’t disrespect religious sites.

Safety and Health

Safety in Santa Cruz

Santa Cruz is a generally safe place to visit, but it’s always wise to take precautions:

  • Don’t carry large amounts of cash
  • Avoid wandering alone late at night
  • Use an authorized guide for trekking and outdoor activities.
  • Refrain from displaying expensive items in public places.

Health in Santa Cruz

  • Ensure that you have a complete set of vaccines before visiting any tropical destination
  • Always carry mosquito repellent and use it regularly.
  • Drink only bottled water from a reputable supplier.

Getting to Santa Cruz: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Santa Cruz

The closest airport to Santa Cruz is Marinduque Airport, which is serviced by regional airlines and is located about 24 kilometers away from Santa Cruz

Bus Stations Near Santa Cruz

Buses from Manila to Santa Cruz are readily available. Cubao Bus Station in Quezon City and Buendia Bus Terminal in Manila have daily trips going to Santa Cruz.

Other Options

How to Get Around Santa Cruz

Walking is the best way to explore Santa Cruz, but if you need transportation, there are plenty of tricycles for hire in the town center.

Where to Go Next from Santa Cruz: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Maniwaya Island: A beautiful island that offers tranquil beaches and crystal-clear waters.
  • Gasan: A nearby town that boasts a rich history, scenic views, and delicious cuisine.
  • Tres Reyes Islands: A group of small uninhabited islands that offer perfect for snorkeling and diving.

Conclusion

Santa Cruz is a fantastic destination that offers plenty to see and do. Whether you’re interested in culture, history, or outdoor adventures, Santa Cruz has something for everyone. With this comprehensive guide, you’re ready to explore all that Santa Cruz has to offer.
