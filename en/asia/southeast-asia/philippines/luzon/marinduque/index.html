Introduction to Marinduque

Marinduque is a serene and charming island province located in Luzon, in the Philippines. Known for its world-renowned Moriones Festival, Marinduque is often referred to as the heart of the Philippines. With its pristine beaches, lush forests, and diverse culture, Marinduque is an ideal destination for both local and foreign tourists.

List of Places to Visit in Marinduque

Marinduque is home to a plethora of attractions for visitors to enjoy. The following list provides a comprehensive guide to the top tourist spots in Marinduque.

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  1. Boac Cathedral - One of the oldest and most famous churches in Marinduque, the Boac Cathedral features a beautiful facade of old Spanish architecture.
  2. Bellarocca Island Resort and Spa - A luxurious Mediterranean-style resort, Bellarocca is a great place to relax and explore the island’s natural wonders.
  3. Moriones Festival - This weeklong event is the highlight of the year in Marinduque, attracting tourists and locals alike with its colorful parades and reenactment of the story of the Passion of Christ.

Natural Attractions of Marinduque: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  1. Tres Reyes Islands - A group of three scenic islands with crystal-clear waters and white sandy beaches, the Tres Reyes Islands are a must-visit for beach lovers.
  2. Mount Malindig - A popular hiking destination for both local and foreign tourists, Mount Malindig offers breathtaking views of the surrounding countryside.
  3. Kawa-Kawa Falls - A natural waterfall tucked away in the lush forests of the island, Kawa-Kawa Falls is perfect for a refreshing swim or picnic.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Marinduque

Marinduque is a great destination for hiking and biking enthusiasts, with its picturesque trails and beautiful scenery. Some of the most popular routes include:

  1. Mt. Malindig Traverse - A challenging hike that takes you to the summit of Mount Malindig, this route offers stunning views of the island’s landscape.
  2. Poctoy - Bellarocca Loop - A scenic biking trail that takes you through some of the most breathtaking spots on the island.
  3. Bathala Caves - A challenging spelunking adventure, the Bathala Caves hiking trail takes you through a series of caves and underground rivers.

Best Things to Do in Marinduque: Activities and Tours

Marinduque offers plenty of activities and tours for visitors of all ages and interests. Here are some of the best:

  1. Island Hopping - Visit the stunning Tres Reyes Islands and surrounding islets with a guided island hopping tour.
  2. Snorkeling and Diving - Discover the beauty of Marinduque’s underwater world with a guided snorkeling or diving tour.
  3. ATV Adventure - Explore the rugged countryside of Marinduque with an ATV tour.

Week-Long Itinerary for Marinduque

For those planning a week-long visit to Marinduque, here’s a suggested itinerary:

Day Activities

Food and Drink Scene of Marinduque

Marinduque’s cuisine is a blend of various influences, from Spanish to Filipino and Chinese. Here are some of the must-try dishes and beverages:

Must-Try Dishes:

  1. Sizzling Sinigang na Lechon Kawali - A unique twist on the classic Filipino dish, sinigang, this dish features crispy lechon kawali served on a sizzling plate with hot, sour soup and vegetables.
  2. Pansit Saute - A delicious stir-fried noodle dish that’s perfect for lunch or dinner.
  3. Adobo sa Gata - A regional Marinduque specialty, this dish adds coconut milk to the traditional Filipino adobo recipe for a creamy and flavorful twist.

Local Beverages:

  1. Barako Coffee - A strong and rich coffee variety that’s grown exclusively in the Philippines.
  2. Lambanog - A potent coconut wine that’s a favorite among locals.

Where to Stay in Marinduque: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Marinduque offers a range of accommodations to suit every budget and taste. Here are some of the best options:

  1. Bellarocca Island Resort and Spa - A luxurious Mediterranean-style resort located on a private island.
  2. Boac Hotel - A budget-friendly hotel that offers comfortable rooms and basic amenities.
  3. Vitton Beach Resort - A relaxing beachfront resort with stunning views and top-notch facilities.

What to Buy in Marinduque: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Marinduque is known for its vibrant arts and crafts scene, with a variety of unique products and souvenirs available for purchase. Here are some of the top items to bring home:

  1. Moriones Mask - A colorful and intricately designed mask that’s a symbol of Marinduque’s rich culture.
  2. Pineapple Fiber Cloth - A fine cloth made from locally sourced pineapple fibers.
  3. Lambanog Bottle - A decorative bottle filled with traditional coconut wine, perfect as a gift or souvenir item.

Local Culture and Customs of Marinduque

Marinduque is steeped in rich cultural traditions, such as the Moriones Festival and the practice of hilot healing. Visitors are encouraged to respect these customs and interact respectfully with locals.

Best Time to Visit Marinduque

The best time to visit Marinduque is from February to April when the weather is cool and dry. The Moriones Festival also takes place during this period, providing visitors with a unique cultural experience.

Do’s and Don’ts of Marinduque

Do:

  1. Respect Marinduque’s culture and customs.
  2. Try the local cuisine, including the regional specialties.
  3. Bring appropriate clothing and gear for outdoor activities.

Don’t:

  1. Litter or damage the natural environment.
  2. Disrespect local traditions or customs.
  3. Engage in illegal activities.

Safety and Health

Safety in Marinduque

Marinduque is generally a safe place for tourists, but visitors are advised to take normal precautions. Be aware of your surroundings, avoid flashing valuables, and stay in well-lit areas at night. Don’t leave your belongings unattended, especially near beaches or tourist areas.

Health in Marinduque

Marinduque has basic healthcare facilities, but medical services may not be as comprehensive as those found in urban areas. Visitors are advised to bring any necessary medication and seek medical attention immediately in case of an emergency.

Getting to Marinduque: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Marinduque

The nearest airport to Marinduque is Marinduque Airport, which offers domestic flights to and from Manila.

Bus Stations Near Marinduque

Buses operated by several companies, such as JAC Liner and Ceres, travel to Marinduque from Manila and other nearby provinces.

Other Options

Ferries operated by Montenegro Lines and other companies travel to Marinduque from Lucena City, Batangas, and Manila.

How to Get Around in Marinduque

Marinduque is a fairly small island, but visitors may still need transportation to get around. Tricycles and jeeps are the most common means of transport in the island.

Where to Go Next from Marinduque: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Located in the center of the Philippines, Marinduque is an ideal base for exploring other nearby destinations, such as:

  1. Boracay Island - A popular tourist destination famous for its white sandy beaches and crystal-clear waters.
  2. Palawan Island - Known for its picturesque geological formations, turquoise waters, and rich marine life, Palawan is a must-visit for nature lovers and adventure seekers.
  3. Banaue Rice Terraces - A UNESCO World Heritage Site, these ancient rice terraces offer a glimpse into the rich cultural heritage of the Philippines.

Conclusion

Marinduque is truly a paradise waiting to be explored. With its stunning natural wonders, rich cultural traditions, and hospitable locals, it’s no wonder why tourists keep coming back to this charming island province. We hope this comprehensive travel guide has provided you with all the information you need to plan your dream Marinduque getaway.
