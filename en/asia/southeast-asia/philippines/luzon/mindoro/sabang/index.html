Introduction to Sabang

Sabang is a small coastal town located on the northwestern part of the island of Mindoro, in the Philippines. It is known for its beautiful beaches, crystal clear waters, and diverse marine life, making it a popular destination for tourists seeking an affordable tropical getaway.

List of Places to Visit in Sabang

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Mangyan Village: Learn about the indigenous people of Mindoro and their way of life at the Mangyan Village.
  • Tabon Cave: Visit the Tabon Cave, a UNESCO World Heritage Site that is known as the “Cradle of Philippine Civilization,” and marvel at the ancient human remains and artifacts found within the cave.

Natural Attractions of Sabang: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Puerto Galera Island Hopping: Go island hopping and visit the nearby islands of Verde Island, Ilin Island, and the White Beach Island.
  • Tamaraw Falls: Take a dip in the cool waters of the Tamaraw Falls, a 20-meter high waterfall located just outside of Sabang.
  • Underground River: Experience a unique boat ride tour of the Puerto Princesa Underground River, one of the New 7 Wonders of Nature.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Sabang

  • Mount Halcon: Hike to the summit of Mount Halcon, the highest peak on Mindoro Island, and enjoy breathtaking views of the surrounding landscape.
  • Pandin Lake: Cycle through the rural countryside to reach Pandin Lake, a scenic spot known for its peaceful surroundings.

Best Things to Do in Sabang: Activities and Tours

  • Snorkeling and Diving: Explore the colorful underwater world of Sabang by going on a snorkeling or diving adventure.
  • Water Sports: Try your hand at kayaking, paddleboarding, or windsurfing at the various water sports centers located throughout Sabang.
  • Nightlife: Enjoy the lively nightlife scene in Sabang by visiting the various beach bars and dance clubs in the area.

Food and Drink Scene of Sabang

Must-Try Dishes

  • Sinigang na Baboy: A sour soup made with pork, vegetables, and tamarind.
  • Adobo: A popular Filipino dish made with chicken or pork marinated in vinegar, soy sauce, and garlic.

Local Beverages

  • San Miguel Beer: The most popular beer in the Philippines.
  • Tuba: A traditional alcoholic drink made from coconut sap.

Where to Stay in Sabang: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Some of the best places to stay in Sabang are:

  • El Galleon Beach Resort: A beachfront resort offering comfortable accommodation and various water sports activities.
  • Badladz Beach Resort: A budget-friendly option located just a few steps away from Sabang Beach.
  • Amami Beach Resort: A luxurious resort featuring an outdoor pool, a restaurant, and a spa.

What to Buy in Sabang: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Pearls: The Philippines is known for producing some of the world’s finest pearls.
  • Barako Coffee: A strong and flavorful coffee blend that is popular among locals.

Local Culture and Customs of Sabang

  • Religion: The majority of the people in Sabang are Roman Catholic.
  • Language: The official language of the Philippines is Filipino, but many locals also speak English.
  • Respect for Elders: It is customary to show respect to elders, particularly by addressing them using their proper titles.

Best Time to Visit Sabang

The best time to visit Sabang is between November and April when the weather is dry and pleasant.

Do’s and Don’ts of Sabang

Do:

  • Respect the local customs and traditions.
  • Dress appropriately when visiting religious sites and temples.

Don’t:

  • Litter on the beach or in public areas.
  • Engage in any illegal activities.

Safety and Health

Safety in Sabang

Sabang is generally a safe destination for tourists, but it is still advisable to take necessary precautions.

  • Always carry a copy of your passport and other important documents with you.
  • Avoid walking alone late at night in secluded areas.

Health in Sabang

  • It is recommended to get vaccinated before traveling to Sabang.
  • Use insect repellent and wear long sleeves and pants to protect against mosquito-borne illnesses.

Getting to Sabang: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Sabang

  • Puerto Princesa International Airport: The closest airport to Sabang, located about 140 km away.

Bus Stations Near Sabang

  • Calapan Bus Terminal: The main bus terminal in Mindoro, located in the capital city of Calapan.

Other Options

  • Ferry: There are frequent ferry services from Batangas Port to Calapan, which takes about 2.5 hours.
  • Private Transfer: You can arrange for a private transfer from Puerto Princesa to Sabang through your hotel or a travel agency.

How to Get Around in Sabang

The best way to get around in Sabang is to walk or to hire a tricycle. There are no taxis or public transportation services available in the town.

Where to Go Next from Sabang: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Puerto Galera: Another popular beach destination located about 30 minutes away from Sabang by boat.
  • Coron Island: An idyllic island paradise known for its beautiful beaches and crystal-clear waters.

Conclusion

Sabang is a beautiful destination that offers a wide range of activities and attractions for travelers of all ages and interests. From relaxing on the beach to exploring the natural wonders of the region, there is something for everyone in this charming little town on the island of Mindoro. So start planning your trip today and get ready to experience the best of Sabang!
