Introduction to Calapan

Nestled in the heart of Mindoro Island, Calapan is a charming coastal city that boasts some of the most stunning natural landscapes in the Philippines. This lively city offers a diverse range of attractions and activities, from hiking and biking to sightseeing and cultural experiences. Whether you’re looking for a relaxing beach vacation or an action-packed adventure, Calapan has something for everyone.

List of Places to Visit in Calapan

Culturally-Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Calapan City Museum - Located in the heart of the city, Calapan City Museum showcases the rich history and cultural heritage of the province. The museum houses a vast collection of artifacts, photographs, and artworks, providing a fascinating insight into the region’s history and tradition.

  • Rizal Shrine - The Rizal Shrine is one of the most significant historical landmarks in Calapan City. The Shrine, located in Barangay Ilaya, commemorates Dr. Jose Rizal, the Philippines’ national hero. Visitors can learn about his life and contributions to the country while enjoying the serene atmosphere and beautiful views.

Natural Attractions of Calapan: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Tukuran Falls - Tukuran Falls is a breathtaking waterfall located in Barangay Tukuran. This stunning natural wonder features crystal-clear waters cascading down the lush green cliffs and into the refreshing pool below. Visitors can hike through the jungle and swim in the cool waters, making it a perfect spot for outdoor enthusiasts.

  • Mt. Malasimbo - Mt. Malasimbo, standing at 1,003 meters above sea level, is the highest mountain in Oriental Mindoro province. It is a popular destination for hikers and trekkers who can enjoy the beautiful views from the peak. The summit also hosts an annual music and arts festival that features a wide range of performances.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Calapan

  • Malasimbo Circuit trail - Malasimbo Circuit trail starts from the Malasimbo Amphitheater and passes through the scenic La Suerte mountain range. The trail offers breathtaking views of the surrounding hills, forests, and rice terraces.

  • Buktot River trail - The Buktot River trail is a perfect place for nature lovers. The trail takes you along the Buktot River, where you can see diverse wildlife such as birds, butterflies, monkeys, and even monitor lizards.

Best Things to Do in Calapan: Activities and Tours

  • Island-hopping - Calapan’s stunning coastline makes it a paradise for island-hopping enthusiasts. Take a guided tour around Tikling Island, Mystic Island, and other nearby islands and discover their hidden beaches and lagoons.

  • Beach activities - With several beautiful beaches around the city, visitors can enjoy a range of water sports activities such as kayaking, snorkeling, fishing, and windsurfing. Sabang Beach, Sitio Baybay, and Baco Beach are some of the most popular beaches in the region.

Food and Drink Scene of Calapan

Must-Try Dishes

  • Calapan Lomi - A hearty noodle soup with a rich meat broth, Calapan Lomi is a popular comfort food in the region. The dish is made with fresh noodles, chicken, pork, and vegetables, making it a perfect meal for those chilly evenings.

  • Inihaw na Baboy - Grilled pork is one of the most popular dishes in the Philippines, and Calapan City has some of the best. Inihaw na Baboy is marinated pork skewers cooked over an open flame and served with a side of spicy vinegar.

Local Beverages

  • Coconut Wine - Known locally as “tuba,” coconut wine is a popular alcoholic drink in Calapan City. The drink is made from fermented coconut sap and has a slightly sweet and sour flavor.

  • Barako Coffee - Calapan City is known for its Barako coffee, a strong and aromatic coffee variety grown in the region. The coffee is a must-try for coffee lovers who appreciate a bold and full-bodied brew.

Where to Stay in Calapan: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Filipiniana Calapan Resort Hotel - A stunning beachfront resort located in Barangay Tawiran, Filipiniana Calapan Resort Hotel features luxurious rooms with breathtaking views, a private beach, and an outdoor pool.

  • Mimaropa Hotel - Located in Barangay Centro East, Mimaropa Hotel is a budget-friendly accommodation option. It is equipped with all the basic amenities for a comfortable stay, including an on-site restaurant and free Wi-Fi.

What to Buy in Calapan: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Coconut products - Calapan City is known for its coconut products such as virgin coconut oil, coconut sugar, and snacks made from coconut meat. These make great souvenirs to bring back home.

  • Tamaraw products - The tamaraw is a rare buffalo species endemic to Mindoro Island, and their products, such as shirts and keychains, make an excellent souvenir item.

Local Culture and Customs of Calapan

Calapan City is a culturally diverse city, with a mix of Tagalog, Visayan, and Mangyan tribes. Respect for elders and hospitality towards guests are highly valued in the culture. Visitors are advised to dress modestly when attending religious or cultural events.

Best Time to Visit Calapan

The best time to visit Calapan City is between December and April, when the weather is dry and sunny. During this time, visitors can enjoy outdoor activities such as hiking, swimming, and island hopping without experiencing heavy rains.

Do’s and Don’ts of Calapan

Do:

  • Respect the local culture and traditions.
  • Dress modestly when attending religious or cultural events.
  • Explore the natural attractions and join outdoor activities.

Don’t:

  • Disrespect the local customs and traditions.
  • Litter or damage the environment.
  • Disrespect the local wildlife and fishing laws.

Safety and Health

Safety in Calapan

Calapan City is generally safe for tourists and visitors. However, like any other city in the world, it is still advisable to be cautious and aware of the surroundings. Visitors are encouraged to take care of their belongings, especially in crowded areas.

Health in Calapan

It is advisable to bring mosquito repellent and sunblock when traveling to Calapan City. Visitors should also be up-to-date on all their routine vaccinations before visiting the region.

Getting to Calapan: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Calapan

The nearest domestic airport to Calapan City is San Jose Airport, located in Occidental Mindoro. From there, visitors can take a van or bus to Calapan City.

Bus Stations Near Calapan

Calapan City can be easily reached by bus from Manila. The journey takes around 3-4 hours, and there are several bus companies that provide daily trips from Cubao, Pasay, and Alabang.

Other Options

There are also ferry services that connect Calapan City to Batangas City. The ferry ride takes around 2-3 hours.

How to Get Around in Calapan

The most convenient way to get around Calapan City is by tricycle. These small motorized vehicles can carry up to three passengers and can be found on almost every street corner. Jeepneys are also available, but they tend to be crowded and can be challenging to navigate for first-time visitors.

Where to Go Next from Calapan: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Puerto Galera - Known for its stunning beaches and dive sites, Puerto Galera is a popular tourist destination located just a short ferry ride from Calapan City.

  • Sablayan - Located in the western part of Mindoro Island, Sablayan is known for its wildlife reserve, diving sites, and beautiful beaches.

Conclusion

Calapan City is a charming destination that offers a perfect blend of natural beauty, cultural richness, and thrilling adventures. From hiking and island hopping to indulging in mouth-watering local cuisine, there are endless opportunities for visitors to immerse themselves in the local culture and create lasting memories. The city remains relatively undiscovered by tourists, making it an ideal destination for those seeking an authentic travel experience.
