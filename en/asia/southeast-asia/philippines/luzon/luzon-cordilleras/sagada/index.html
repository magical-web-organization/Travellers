Introduction to Sagada

Sagada is nestled in the Cordillera mountain range in northern Luzon, Philippines. It is a popular tourist destination for its natural wonders, outdoor activities, indigenous culture, and hospitable locals. Sagada is renowned for its hanging coffins, rice terraces, caves, waterfalls, natural hot springs while it also offers biking and hiking trails with stunning views of the surrounding peaks.

List of Places to Visit in Sagada

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Sagada has several museums and historical sites that reflect its rich cultural heritage. Here are the must-visit attractions:

  • Sagada Weaving - visit this community-based initiative where you can observe traditional weaving techniques and shop for handmade textiles.
  • Sagada Pottery - located in the main town, you can learn how pottery has been crafted for generations using traditional techniques and purchase some wonderful pottery souvenirs.
  • St. Mary’s Episcopal Church - built-in 1904, this picturesque chapel is one of the oldest religious sites in Sagada.
  • Bontoc Museum - a 45-minute drive from Sagada, it houses an impressive collection of artifacts and exhibits related to the traditional lifestyle of the Igorot people.

Natural Attractions of Sagada: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Sagada is blessed with natural wonders that attract outdoor enthusiasts and nature lovers. Here are some of the popular ones:

  • Sagada Rice Terraces - a scenic drive away from town, these extensive rice paddies offer breathtaking views as well as insights into how the Igorot culture relied on agriculture
  • Lumiang Cave - discover the stunning limestone caves and its ancient coffins. Dated back to the 14th century, the walls of the cave are adorned with “burial blankets” that were spread over mummies (tightly wrapped corpses) as part of the funeral ritual.
  • Sumaguing Cave - explore this massive cave chamber and its crystal-clear underground streams for an exhilarating caving adventure.
  • Bomod-Ok Falls - a 45-minute hike from Banga-an village along a scenic trail through the terraced rice fields, this stunning waterfall features tiered pools that are perfect for swimming on hot days.
  • Mt. Kiltepan - notable for offering the best creative sunrise pictures, the summit is a perfect spot to view the sunrise and the spectacular sea of clouds.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Sagada

Sagada’s mountainous terrain and trails offer an adventurous experience for bikers and hikers. Here are some popular options:

  • Echo Valley Trail - a short walk from the main town, this trail leads to the hanging coffins where local Igorot people laid their loved ones to rest.
  • Marlboro Country Trail - a much longer trail but greatly rewarding with the magnificent panoramic view it offers, of Sagada and the nearby towns of Besao and Bontoc.
  • Kiltepan Trail - truly mesmerizing mountainous trail that leads to the top of one of Sagada’s most breathtaking viewpoints.
  • The Blue Soil Hills - hike this scenic trail to get a better view of Sagada’s cultural heritage and experience the surreal shades of blue unique to these hills.

Best Things to Do in Sagada: Activities and Tours

Apart from its natural wonders, Sagada is also famous for its leisure activities and cultural tours engaging every traveler. Here is what to try while in Sagada:

  • Sagada Lemon Pie House - to satisfy your sweet tooth driving your cravings, try the sumptuous fresh pies, made from natural ingredients and locally sourced fruits.
  • Bike Adventure Tour - experience the backpacking life of Sagada and its surrounding natural beauty through a bike tour that provides spectacular views of the rice fields and the mountains.
  • Sagada Orange Farm Tour - a special visit to an orange farm located 20 minutes from Sagada where you get to learn about orange farming and harvested orange products, the locals consider this one of the best experiences to try out.
  • Culture and Heritage Tour - culture enthusiasts and history buffs will appreciate this walking tour through Sagada’s iconic spots where the tour guide provides a fascinating introduction to the town’s rich past.
  • Sagada Bonfire and Stargazing - a popular activity where travelers enjoy the company of others, sitting around a bonfire and gazing at the breathtaking night sky atop a mountain.

Food and Drink Scene of Sagada

Must-Try Dishes

Sagada provides an amazing local cuisine that visitors should not miss. Here are some of the notable dishes to taste;

  • Pinikpikan: a signature dish in the Cordillera region that is a traditional method of preparing chicken where it is slowly beaten to death to tenderize the meat before cooking.
  • Sagada Lemon Pie: baked right from locally grown fruit, this is Sagada’s signature dessert that is zesty, delicious, and a must-try for visitors.
  • Pancit Habhab: this pancit variant uses thin noodles that visitors can only find in Sagada and served on banana leaves.
  • Tuyo Pasta: an innovative fusion of Italian and Filipino cuisine, noodle tossed with sun-dried fish, garlic, and olive oil.
  • Pinikpikan Soup: traditional Igorot chicken soup featuring the smoked pinikpikan meat

Local Beverages

Sagada has several local beverages that offer an authentic taste of the region’s culture and lifestyle. Here are some of the most popular drinks;

  • Sagada Coffee: known for its mild taste, woodsy notes, and smooth finish, Sagada’s coffee is famous amongst the coffee connoisseurs worldwide.
  • Tapuy: this traditional rice wine is made by fermenting red or white rice with yeast.
  • Basi: another popular rice wine with a distinctive sour taste, basi has been a favorite in the region for generations.

Where to Stay in Sagada: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Sagada has several accommodations to cater to every visitor’s need, whether high-end luxury or budget-friendly. Here are some of the best options;

  • Sagada Heritage Village - a charming, family-run guesthouse with traditional Cordillera architecture, and a pleasant garden setting.
  • Kanip-aw Pines Lodge - situated in Banga-an village, this cozy homestay offers scenic views of Sagada and is a perfect place for quiet retreats.
  • Residential Lodge - located in the heart of Sagada, this budget-friendly lodge offers clean rooms, friendly staff, and a prime location close to good restaurants and attractions.
  • SaGGaDa SaKkada Homestay - this homestay features spacious rooms with a fireplace, making it the best option for those seeking a cozy and relaxing ambiance.
  • Sagada Ecotourism Cottages - situated within the peaceful setting of Banga-an village, these cozy cottages offer an ideal environment for travelers looking for a quiet retreat and nature getaway.

What to Buy in Sagada: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Sagada offers some of the most unique souvenirs and local products that visitors can take home as a memory. Some of these include;

  • Sagada Weaving - as mentioned, visitors can purchase handmade textiles like bags, blankets, scarves, and shawls, all woven using the traditional backstrap loom technique.
  • Pottery - Visitors can buy traditional handmade pottery featuring ethnic designs and intricate patterns.
  • Honey - Visitors can purchase natural honey made from the surrounding farms and family beekeeping operations and native fruits like wild blueberries and raspberries.

Local Culture and Customs of Sagada

Sagada boasts a unique and ancient culture that visitors should respect and experience to understand the local way of life. Here are some of the customs and traditions to expect;

  • Tong-Tong- hand-beaten metal gongs suspended in tree trunks, used for a variety of purposes such as signaling ceremonies, traditional Igorot dance, and important gatherings.
  • Sagada Language - the area’s language is Kankana-ey, which is an official language in the Philippines.
  • Paghichik - A traditional style of attic consisting of a wooden box and a string, used to store goods and valuables.

Best Time to Visit Sagada

The best time to visit Sagada is during the dry season, from November to May. The weather is pleasant, with clear skies, mild temperatures, and no rainfall, making it perfect for trekking, hiking, caving, and outdoor activities. June through October is considered the rainy season, and while it’s the best time for photographing the lush green hills and waterfalls, travel can be challenging due to the heavy rains and mudslides.

Do’s and Don’ts of Sagada

Do:

  • Respect the environment and leave no trace as you traverse through the trails and attractions.
  • Wear appropriate clothing while hiking and caving activities to ensure safety.
  • Learn and appreciate the local indigenous culture and heritage of Sagada and its people

Don’t:

  • Litter within the attractions and trails
  • Take or disturb any artifacts or cultural belongings within the sites.

Safety and Health

Safety in Sagada

Sagada is generally safe and has a low crime rate, but travelers should still take precautions to ensure their safety, especially when hiking, caving, or engaging in outdoor activities.

Health in Sagada

Visitors should stay hydrated, wear appropriate clothing, and take precautions to protect themselves against the sun’s harmful rays, especially when engaging in outdoor activities. Safe drinking water is accessible and readily available within the towns. There are no hospitals within the town, so it is important to come prepared with a first aid kit.

Getting to Sagada: Transportation Options and Logistics

Visitors can reach Sagada by land and air. Here are the best options:

Airports Near Sagada

The closest airport is in Baguio Airport, which is 75 miles away from Sagada. A more popular option is to fly into Manila Airport and then taking a bus to Sagada

Bus Stations Near Sagada

There are several bus lines that connect Sagada from major cities in the Luzon region like the Dangwa Bus or the Cable Tours. Some buses depart at night, and you can choose either air-conditioned or non-airconditioned coaches.

Other Options

Travelers can also hire a van or car with a driver from Baguio and take a scenic three-hour road trip across the beautiful winding roads and mountain passes to reach Sagada within the Cordillera range

How to Get Around in Sagada

Exploring Sagada can be easily done on foot, but you can also rent a motorcycle or hire a tricycle or jeepney to reach specific attractions and nearby towns.

Where to Go Next from Sagada: Nearby Destinations and Excursions.

Sagada’s proximity to other destinations within the Luzon region makes it a perfect base for further exploration of the Philippines. Visitors can check out other places beyond Sagada, such as;

  • Banaue Rice Terraces
  • Baguio City
  • Vigan City

Conclusion

Sagada is a perfect destination for travelers who love outdoor adventure, cultural experiences, and natural beauty. With its stunning rice terraces, unique cave systems, and fascinating indigenous culture, Sagada is a place that will linger in the memory of every traveler. The tips, recommendations, and information provided in this guide will ensure a fantastic trip that meets all your expectations.
