Introduction to Buscalan

Buscalan is a small village located in the mountainous region of the Luzon Cordilleras in the Philippines. Known for its rich cultural heritage and scenic views, Buscalan has become a popular tourist destination in recent years. The village is famously known for being home to the Kalinga people, a group known for their exceptional tattooing traditions.

List of Places to Visit in Buscalan

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Kalinga Museum

The Kalinga Museum is a great place to start your exploration of Buscalan. The museum houses a wide range of artefacts and exhibits showcasing the rich culture, traditions, and history of the Kalinga people. Visitors can learn about the village’s tattooing traditions, see traditional clothing and jewellery, and learn about the different Kalinga tribes that call Buscalan their home.

Kalinga Houses

The traditional houses in Buscalan are a sight to behold. These homes are made entirely of wood and are adorned with intricate carvings and intricate details. Visitors can marvel at the architecture, learn about the building techniques used by the Kalinga people, and see how these houses are still in use today.

Natural Attractions of Buscalan: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Rice Terraces

The rice terraces in Buscalan are some of the most beautiful in the Philippines. The terraces have been meticulously carved into the mountainside and create a series of jaw-dropping levels that are simply breathtaking. Visitors can take a hike through the terraces and enjoy the stunning views.

Waterfalls

Buscalan is home to a number of beautiful waterfalls that are worth checking out. Some of the most popular waterfalls in the area include Ngibat Falls, Tangadan Falls, and Bomod-ok Falls. These waterfalls are perfect for relaxing and cooling off after a long day of hiking or exploring.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Buscalan

Mount Kupapey

Mount Kupapey is a popular hiking destination in Buscalan. The hike to the summit takes about an hour and a half, but the effort is well worth it. At the top, visitors can enjoy stunning views of the surrounding mountains, rice terraces, and villages.

Marlboro Country

Marlboro Country is another great hiking destination in Buscalan. The hike to Marlboro Country takes about three hours but offers some of the best views in the area. The name Marlboro Country comes from the fact that the area was used in a Marlboro cigarette commercial in the 1980s.

Best Things to Do in Buscalan: Activities and Tours

Tattooing

Buscalan is famous for its tattooing traditions. Visitors can witness the traditional hand-tapping of tattoos, which involves using a bamboo needle to puncture the skin and add ink. Many visitors choose to get tattoos of their own while in Buscalan.

Local Village Tour

A local village tour is a great way to learn about the culture and traditions of the Kalinga people. Visitors can see traditional homes, learn about the different tribes in the area, and even participate in local customs and traditions.

Food and Drink Scene of Buscalan

Must-Try Dishes

Pinikpikan

Pinikpikan is a traditional Kalinga dish that involves killing and preparing a chicken in a unique way. The chicken is hit until it is almost unconscious, but is not killed in the process. The chicken is then cooked and served, and is said to have a unique flavour and texture due to the preparation process.

Kinuday

Kinuday is a smoked meat dish that is popular in Buscalan. The meat can be made from a variety of animals, including pork, chicken, and beef. The meat is smoked for several hours, giving it a unique flavour and texture.

Local Beverages

Tapuy

Tapuy is a traditional rice wine that is made in Buscalan. The wine is made using glutinous rice and a yeast that is found in the surrounding forests. The fermentation process takes several days, and the resulting wine is sweet and slightly alcoholic.

Kalinga Coffee

Kalinga Coffee is another local beverage that is worth trying while in Buscalan. The coffee is grown in the surrounding area and is known for its rich, full-bodied flavour.

Where to Stay in Buscalan: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Kalinga Village Homestay

The Kalinga Village Homestay is a great way to experience traditional Kalinga life. Visitors can stay in one of the traditional houses in the village and learn about the culture from the locals. The homestay offers basic amenities and meals.

Buscalan Lodge

The Buscalan Lodge is a great option for those looking for more modern amenities. The lodge offers comfortable rooms with private bathrooms and hot water. The lodge also has a restaurant and bar on-site.

What to Buy in Buscalan: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Handwoven Textiles

Handwoven textiles are a popular souvenir in Buscalan. The textiles are made by the Kalinga people and are known for their intricate designs and bright colours. The textiles can be used as wall hangings, tablecloths, or even as clothing.

Tribal Jewelry

Tribal jewelry is another popular souvenir in Buscalan. The jewelry is made by the Kalinga people and is often made from silver or brass. The jewelry features intricate designs and is a great way to take home a piece of Kalinga culture.

Local Culture and Customs of Buscalan

The Kalinga people are known for their strong sense of community and their rich cultural heritage. Visitors to Buscalan should be respectful of the local customs and traditions, including the ban on smoking and drinking alcohol in public places. Visitors should also dress conservatively and remove their shoes before entering traditional homes.

Best Time to Visit Buscalan

The best time to visit Buscalan is during the dry season, from December to May. During this time, the weather is cooler and the skies are clear, making it easier to see the stunning views in the area.

Do’s and Don’ts of Buscalan

Do:

  • Dress conservatively
  • Remove your shoes before entering traditional homes
  • Respect the local customs and traditions
  • Be friendly and open to learning about the local culture

Don’t:

  • Smoke or drink alcohol in public places
  • Disrespect the local customs and traditions
  • Leave your garbage behind

Safety and Health

Safety in Buscalan

Buscalan is a relatively safe place to visit, but visitors should still take precautions. Visitors should be aware of their surroundings and avoid travelling alone at night. Visitors should also be careful when hiking and stick to established trails.

Health in Buscalan

Visitors to Buscalan should be up to date on their routine vaccinations, including measles-mumps-rubella (MMR), diphtheria-tetanus-pertussis, varicella (chickenpox), polio, and flu. Visitors should also take precautions to prevent mosquito bites, as malaria is present in the area.

Getting to Buscalan: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Buscalan

The nearest airport to Buscalan is the Laoag International Airport, which is about 234 kilometers away.

Bus Stations Near Buscalan

The nearest major bus terminal is the Victory Liner terminal in Baguio City, which is about 140 kilometers away.

Other Options

Visitors can also hire a private car or take a public bus to get to Buscalan from neighboring cities and towns.

How to Get Around in Buscalan

The best way to get around in Buscalan is on foot. Many of the attractions in the area are within walking distance of each other. Visitors can also hire a motorcycle or jeepney to get around the area.

Where to Go Next from Buscalan: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Sagada

Sagada is a popular destination in the Luzon Cordilleras. The town is known for its stunning caves, waterfalls, and hiking trails.

Banaue

Banaue is another popular destination in the Luzon Cordilleras. The town is known for its rice terraces, which are some of the most beautiful in the Philippines.

Conclusion

Buscalan is a unique and beautiful destination in the Philippines. With its rich culture and stunning natural beauty, it is a must-visit for anyone traveling to the Luzon Cordilleras. Whether you’re interested in hiking, cultural heritage or just experiencing a traditional way of life, Buscalan is definitely worth a visit.
