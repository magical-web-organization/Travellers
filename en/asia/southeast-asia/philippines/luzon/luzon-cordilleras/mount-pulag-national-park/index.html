Introduction to Mount Pulag National Park

Mount Pulag National Park is a breathtaking destination situated in Luzon Cordilleras in the northeastern region of Luzon, Philippines. Its summit, standing at 2,922 meters, is the highest peak in Luzon, contributing to its fame as the “playground of the gods”. Mount Pulag is a culturally and ecologically rich park, boasting an incredible biodiversity that includes the Oriental bird, cloud rat, and the endangered Philippine Brown deer. It has also served as a hunting and food-gathering ground for indigenous communities in the region for hundreds of years.

If you are looking for a unique experience filled with history and adventure, Mount Pulag National Park is a must-visit! In this guide, we will introduce you to the best places to visit, things to do, hiking routes, accommodations, local culture, and more.

List of Places to Visit in Mount Pulag National Park

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Ambangeg Trail

The Ambangeg Trail is a path frequented by many visitors as it is the easiest among the three trails to the summit. Along the way, visitors can find a number of historical places and local cultural gems, such as the Kubo Sulfur Hot Springs, the Babalak Marang Trail where visitors can see terraced rice paddies, and the Sayac-An Indigenous Community where visitors can interact with residents and learn about their culture and customs.

Benguet Museum

The Benguet Museum in Kabayan showcases the province’s culture, history, and heritage through a variety of exhibits and interactive displays. It features ancient mummies, human remains, and artifacts of the famous Kabayan burial caves.

Mount Pulag Summit

The summit of Mount Pulag is one of the most popular attractions in the park. Visitors can see the “sea of clouds” that is surrounded by the breathtaking view of the mountains, use the famous Marlboro campsite as a photo op, explore the mossy forest, and more.

Natural Attractions of Mount Pulag National Park: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Akiki Trail

Akiki Trail, a more challenging route to the summit, is the best hike for those who want to experience the beauty of nature with fewer crowds. Hiking along the Akiki trail offers visitors a gorgeous and vibrant landscape, including stunning waterfalls and old-growth forests.

Ambaguio Trail

Ambaguio Trail offers visitors a chance to enjoy the park’s lowlands without compromising the scenic views of the Cordillera Mountains. Expect to see wildflowers, ferns, lichen, and mosses as you hike the trail’s path through the lush untouched Cordillera forest.

Cabacungan Local Falls

Cabacungan Local Falls is one of the park’s hidden yet popular gems. A short hike to the falls leads visitors to a natural swimming pool where they can freshen up before continuing their adventure.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Mount Pulag National Park

The Akiki Trail

The Akiki Trail is the most challenging route to Mount Pulag’s summit, starting from Akiki Village with a distance of 16.8 km. The trail passes through a dense forest, offering a variety of flora and fauna, and a clear stream of flowing water. It passes through a rocky path where hikers have to handle the slippery surface and steep ascents. With proper management, the Akiki Trail is an experience that hikers will surely cherish. Tents can be pitched on the saddle campsite, and from here, the climb to the summit begins.

Ambangeg Trail

The Ambangeg trail is the most accessible route to the summit, perfect for novice hikers and those who prefer a less steep climb. Along the trail, visitors can enjoy breathtaking views, a variety of photographic landscapes, and rustic-style villages. The Ambangeg trail measures 9.4 km, and tents can be pitched on the grassland campsite, allowing visitors to witness the famous sea of clouds early in the morning.

Best Things to Do in Mount Pulag National Park: Activities and Tours

Sunset and Sunrise Viewing

One of the best things to do in Mount Pulag is to watch the sunrise and sunset from the summit. The contrast of color between the sky and mountain changes each minute, making it a must-see view. Visitors particularly enjoy watching the sunrise through the horizon and above the low clouds from the summit’s highest area.

Birdwatching

Birdwatching, particularly for Philippine Endemic birds, is a popular activity in Mount Pulag National Park. Many different species of birds live in the park, including Luzon Water Redstart, Green-backed Tit, Golden-crowned Flowerpecker, and more.

Camping

Camping at Mount Pulag National Park is one of the most sought-after activities among visitors. There are several camping grounds in the park, either rented or independent, and one of it is the area where visitors can experience the “sea of clouds,” which adds up to the charm of the experience.

Week-Long Itinerary for Mount Pulag National Park

Day 1: Arrive at Baguio city.

From Baguio, take a jeep to Bokod town passing by Atok. From Bokod town, hire a private vehicle to reach Mount Pulag National Park’s preferred trailheads. Settle in and get registered for the hike.

Day 2: Acclimatization Day

Spend the day acclimatizing to the altitude by exploring treks like the Ambangeg trail or the Benguet trail, which are less excruciating and might get you some of the gorgeous views the park has to offer.

Day 3: Climb to the Top

Start climbing early in the morning with your scheduled guide through one of the preferred and registered trailheads, either Akiki or Ambangeg. Camping is available on the path, with an optional stay at the summit.

Day 4: Descend and Go Spelunking at the Kabayan Burial Caves

Spend the day strolling and trekking back to the park’s starting point, afterward head to the Kabayan Burial Caves for an immersive experience, learning about the magnetizing past of the Ibaloi people.

Day 5: Relax and Visit Local Attractions

Take the day off after the previous day’s climb and explore local attractions in nearby towns like Sagada or Baguio City, spots for shopping, and restaurants like Ketchup Food Community in Baguio or Yoghurt house in Sagada.

Day 6: Tent City Night

If not done yet, camp in Tent City and witness the “sea of clouds” during sunrise.

Day 7: Final Departure

Pack up, say goodbyes to the trailhead, and take a van or roundtrip jeep back to Baguio or Sagada, depending on where you’re heading next.

Food and Drink Scene of Mount Pulag National Park

Must-Try Dishes

Pinikpikan

Pinikpikan is a traditional dish made by slaughtering and preparing a chicken that has been beaten with a stick until it dies. The chicken is then smoked to preserve it for a long time.

Kiniing

Kiniing is a type of meat that is first salted, then smoked over wood to pickle it.

Local Beverages

Barako Coffee

Barako coffee is the local coffee of the Philippines and is grown in Batangas. It is strong and full-bodied and is known for its bold flavor.

Benguet Blends

The Benguet Highlands has become a haven for coffee lovers who want to experience their traditional coffee blends. The coffee beans are organically grown, roasted, and processed in various establishments around the region.

Where to Stay in Mount Pulag National Park: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Homestays

One of the best ways to experience the local way of life is by staying with locals from the community near the trailheads. It’s also a great way to help support the local community’s tourism industry.

Hostels

There are a few hostels near the park’s immediate area that cater to visitors, such as Baban’s Homestay, where visitors can sample and experience local culture while enjoying affordable accommodations.

Guesthouses

Guesthouses are available for those who want to experience comfort and privacy while staying in the area, such as the Mount Pulag Eco-Park Guesthouse.

What to Buy in Mount Pulag National Park: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Handmade Clothing, Arts, and Crafts

Most shops around the village sell handmade clothing, art, and crafts where you can bring home and appreciatively remember your stay.

Local Coffee Blends

Local coffee blends can be purchased from Benguet’s various establishments, including Sagada, where their coffee is called Sagada Brew.

Local Culture and Customs of Mount Pulag National Park

Tribal Culinary Heritage

The Ibaloi people’s culinary heritage is prominent in the Cabbage Capital of the Philippines. Pinikpikan is one of its most remarkable, and Agri-ecotourism is one of the town’s emerging practices.

Farming and Agriculture

The town’s main livelihood is agriculture, and these are showcased in the area’s preserved farms, which are accessible via a trek through the beautiful and lush surrounding mountains.

Best Time to Visit Mount Pulag National Park

The best time to visit Mount Pulag National Park is between February to April, where the climate is typically cool and temperatures can be as low as 0 degrees Celsius. During these months, the temperature at the summit rises considerably during sunset, creating the park’s famous “sea of clouds” phenomenon.

Do’s and Don’ts of Mount Pulag National Park

Do:

  • Register to the park ranger for your hike.
  • Hike during the day and avoid hiking at night.
  • Respect the park’s wildlife and cultural heritage.
  • Bring thermal insulation gear to prepare for the cold temperature.
  • Pack everything you will need for your hike, including food, water, and sunscreen.

Don’t:

  • Leave trash in the park and always observe “leave no trace” policies.
  • Smoke or light a fire in non-designated areas.
  • Damage or harm the flora and fauna of the park.
  • Hike alone, especially in the dark.
  • Pee anywhere in the trail - Dig holes or use the designated comfort rooms (CR).

Safety and Health

Safety in Mount Pulag National Park

Mount Pulag is regarded as a safe and friendly destination. Visitors should keep in mind that it gets quite cold and misty during hiking, making it critical to wear appropriate clothing and prepare necessary gear and adding thermal insulation. Hikers should be in good health and manage their breath during the climb to avoid altitude sickness. It is also best to hire a guide and have a knowledgeable local will help make the hike safer.

Health in Mount Pulag National Park

Hikers should be aware of the risks of Acute Mountain Sickness (AMS), an issue that occurs when the body fails to adjust to the lower air pressure and oxygen level found at high altitudes. Symptoms of AMS include headaches, nausea, dizziness, and shortness of breath. It is possible to prevent AMS by acclimatizing at a lower altitude before climbing, avoiding alcohol and caffeine, drinking plenty of water, and taking acetazolamide, with medical consultation and follow up.

Getting to Mount Pulag National Park: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Mount Pulag National Park

The two closest airports to Mount Pulag National Park are the Loakan Airport in Baguio, where you can take a jeepney ride to Bokod Town. The Clark Airport in Angeles City, where visitors can travel to the park via private vehicles from Dau Bus Terminal.

Bus Stations Near Mount Pulag National Park

Visitors can get to the Mount Pulag National Park from Baguio City, where buses depart from Baguio Dangwa Bus Terminal, with the passenger drop-off point at Bokod Town.

Other Options

Visitors can also reach the park via private vehicle or van rental, with parking offered at the trailheads’ starting points. It’s also possible to hire a tour guide or join group tours from travel agencies in Baguio City.

How to Get Around in Mount Pulag National Park

The ideal way to explore the park is by foot, hiking on various trails that offer breathtaking views and natural beauty. Visitors can also hire private vehicles to travel around the area or take tricycles from one town to another.

Where to Go Next from Mount Pulag National Park: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Sagada

Sagada is a mountain town located in the Cordillera Mountains, near Mount Pulag. It is famous for its hanging coffins, limestone caves, and stunning rice terraces.

Baguio City

Baguio City is a popular tourist destination in the Philippines, referred to as the “City of Pines.” Visitors can enjoy various attractions, including historical landmarks, souvenir shops, art centers, and unique restaurants.

Conclusion

Mount Pulag National Park has proven itself as a destination offering ecological beauty and cultural richness that are hard to match. We hope this guide has helped you to understand more about the park, the activities you can do, places to stay, and the things you should try and avoid. Enjoy your trip, and most importantly, respect the culture, ecology, and heritage of the area to ensure its continued existence and build friendly lasting connections with the regional community. Happy travels!
