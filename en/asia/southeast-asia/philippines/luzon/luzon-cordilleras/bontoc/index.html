Introduction to Bontoc

Bontoc is a small town located in the heart of the Cordillera range in Northern Luzon, Philippines. It’s the capital and center of the Mountain Province, and it’s commonly known for its rich cultural heritage, breathtaking landscapes, and outdoor adventures.

If you’re looking for a place to escape the bustling city life and immerse yourself in nature and culture, Bontoc is the perfect place to visit. From hiking and biking trails to historical landmarks and colorful festivals, there is a lot to see and do in Bontoc.

In this comprehensive travel guide, we’ll cover everything you need to know about Bontoc, from the best places to visit to the do’s and don’ts of the local culture. So, let’s dive in!

List of Places to Visit in Bontoc

Here are some of the must-visit places in Bontoc:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Bontoc is known for preserving its rich cultural heritage. Here are some of the museums and historical places to visit:

  • Bontoc Museum: This museum showcases traditional clothes, rituals, and ceremonies of the Bontoc people.

  • Samoki Village: A small village that takes you back in time and lets you experience the culture of the Bontoc Tribe.

  • Bontoc Tribal Museum / Bascoy Museum: This museum has a collection of artifacts and photographs of the Bontoc tribe from the Spanish colonization to the present day.

Natural Attractions of Bontoc: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Bontoc is also known for its beautiful natural attractions. Here are some of the outdoor adventures and scenic views to visit:

  • Maligcong Rice Terraces: One of the most picturesque rice terraces in the Philippines is located in Maligcong, Bontoc.

  • Mount Polis: The highest mountain in Mountain Province, Philippines. It is a popular destination for hikers.

  • Bontoc Indigenous Peoples World Heritage Site: This site offers a breath-taking view of the surrounding mountains, rice terraces, and a glimpse of the Bontoc way of life.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Bontoc

For the adventurers, Bontoc has many hiking and biking trails that will tickle your fancy. Here is a list of the best routes:

  • Mount Polis Hike: It takes 5-7 hours, depending on your level of fitness.

  • Bontoc to Kalinga Bike Trail: You’ll get to see the stunning scenery and experience the culture of Bontoc and Kalinga.

  • Bontoc to Maligcong Hike: This hike is perfect for beginners as it’s an easy hike and is only approximately 2.5 hours.

Best Things to Do in Bontoc: Activities and Tours

Besides visiting the places mentioned above, Bontoc offers a plethora of activities for every traveler. Here are some of the best things to do in Bontoc:

  • Attend the Lang-ay Festival: This festival is held in Bontoc every April and is an excellent opportunity to witness the culture and traditions of the Bontoc Tribe.

  • Experience the local cuisine: Sample the local food, which is heavily influenced by the neighboring provinces like Ilocos and Kalinga.

  • Go on a Cultural Tour: There are many local tour operators that offer a cultural tour of Bontoc, which includes visits to local villages and historical sites.

Week-Long Itinerary for Bontoc

Here’s a week-long itinerary for Bontoc:

Day 1: Arrival in Bontoc, visit Bontoc Museum, and explore the town center.

Day 2: Hike Mount Polis.

Day 3: Bike from Bontoc to Kalinga and visit Tinglayan and Buscalan Whang Od.

Day 4: Explore Samoki Village.

Day 5: Visit Maligcong Rice Terraces.

Day 6: Go on a cultural tour and visit the Bascoy Museum, Tinglayan Museum, and the nearby villages.

Day 7: Attend the Lang-ay Festival or experience the local cuisine.

Food and Drink Scene of Bontoc

Here are some of the must-try dishes and local beverages in Bontoc:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Pinikpikan: A traditional Kalinga dish that is prepared by beating a live chicken with a stick before cooking it.

  • Pancit Miki Bihon: A local noodle dish made with egg and rice noodles, vegetables, and meat.

Local Beverages

  • Tapuey: a local rice wine made by fermenting rice in earthen jars.

  • Kalinga Coffee: A specialty coffee grown in the surrounding Kalinga mountains.

Where to Stay in Bontoc: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Here are some of the best accommodations in Bontoc:

  • Alapo’s View Inn: This inn is located in a quiet area with a view of the surrounding mountains.

  • Rice Homestay: This homestay offers a unique experience as it’s located in a local village and offers a glimpse of the local way of life.

What to Buy in Bontoc: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Here are some of the local souvenirs and products to buy in Bontoc:

  • Woven Bags and Baskets: The weavers in Bontoc produce intricate patterns and designs using indigenous materials.

  • Shirts and Long-Sleeve Blouses: These clothes are made of indigenous material like abaca, cotton, and pineapple fibers.

Local Culture and Customs of Bontoc

  • Respect the Bontoc culture by following their customs, traditions, and beliefs.

  • Ask for permission before taking pictures of the locals.

Best Time to Visit Bontoc

The best time to visit Bontoc is during the summer months, from March to May, when the weather is hot and dry. The Lang-ay Festival is also held in April.

Do’s and Don’ts of Bontoc

Do:

  • Respect the local culture and customs

  • Dress modestly when attending cultural events

Don’t:

  • Take pictures of the locals without their permission

  • Disrespect the environment and the local way of life

Safety and Health

Safety in Bontoc

Bontoc is generally a safe place to visit, but it’s always best to take precautions, like avoiding dark and deserted streets at night.

Health in Bontoc

Bontoc has a rural health unit that provides primary healthcare services. Remember to bring enough prescribed medicines.

Getting to Bontoc: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Bontoc

The nearest airport to Bontoc is Cauayan Airport, which is about 185 kilometers away.

Bus Stations Near Bontoc

There are buses available in Baguio City going to Bontoc, which takes approximately 6-7 hours.

Other Options

Alternatively, you may hire a private car to take you to Bontoc from Manila or Baguio City.

How to Get Around in Bontoc

The easiest way to get around Bontoc is by hiring a tricycle or jeepney.

Where to Go Next from Bontoc: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Here are some of the nearby destinations and excursions from Bontoc:

  • Sagada: A nearby town known for its hanging coffins, caves, and waterfalls.

  • Banaue Rice Terraces: One of the most iconic rice terraces in the Philippines.

  • Kalinga: A province on the eastern coast of Luzon, known for its rich cultural heritage and traditional tattooing.

Conclusion

Bontoc is a charming town located in the heart of the Cordillera range, perfect for travelers who want to experience the local culture and natural attractions. From its museums and historical landmarks to its outdoor adventures and scenic views, Bontoc has something to offer every type of traveler. So, pack your bags and experience the beauty of Bontoc!
