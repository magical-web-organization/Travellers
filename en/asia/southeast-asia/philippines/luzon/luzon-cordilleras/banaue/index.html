Introduction to Banaue

Banaue, located in Luzon Cordilleras, Philippines, is home to world-famous rice terraces that have been recognized as a UNESCO World Heritage Site. These stunning rice terraces have been built over two thousand years ago and continue to be used by the local farmers today. In addition to the rice terraces, Banaue offers breathtaking views of the mountains, fresh air, natural hot springs, and cultural experiences unique to the region. Here’s a comprehensive travel guide to help you plan your trip to Banaue.

List of Places to Visit in Banaue

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  1. Banaue Museum - A museum that showcases the culture and history of the Ifugao people. It features different Ifugao artifacts such as clothing, weapons, and musical instruments.

  2. Banaue Ethnic Village and Pine Forest Resort - A re-creation of a typical Ifugao village where visitors can learn about the lifestyle of the Ifugao people, their traditions, and customs.

  3. Tam-An Village - A village that provides visitors with an authentic experience of Ifugao culture. Visitors can witness traditional Ifugao weaving and interact with the locals.

Natural Attractions of Banaue: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  1. Batad Rice Terraces - A UNESCO Heritage Site, this rice terrace is considered by many as the most stunning site in the region. The hike can be challenging, but the view from the top is definitely worth it.

  2. Tappiya Falls - A beautiful waterfall located in Batad that requires a short hike. Visitors can take a refreshing swim in the cool waters or soak up the sun on the rocks.

  3. Mount Amuyao - The highest peak in the Ifugao province. Hiking to the summit requires a decent level of fitness, but you will be rewarded with panoramic views of the surrounding mountains and rice terraces.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Banaue

  1. Batad Rice Terraces hiking trail - A 4-hour round trip trek that leads to the Batad Rice Terraces

  2. Mount Amuyao hiking trail - A demanding hike that takes around 2 days of trekking to complete, but the views at the top are worth the effort.

  3. Biking tour - A scenic tour that takes you through the rice fields and the villages. You can rent a bike or join a guided tour.

Best Things to Do in Banaue: Activities and Tours

  1. Hot Springs - Relax and unwind in the natural hot springs located in nearby towns such as Bontoc and Tinglayan.

  2. Cultural Activities - Visit the Ifugao Museum or join a cultural tour to get a deeper understanding of the region’s culture and customs.

  3. Trekking – Visit the Batad Rice Terraces, Mount Amuyao, Tappiya Falls, or other hiking trails for an outdoor adventure.

Week-Long Itinerary for Banaue

Day 1 Arrive in Manila and transfer to your hotel
Day 2 Travel to Banaue and visit the Banaue Museum
Day 3 Trek to Batad Rice Terraces
Day 4 Hike to Tappiya Falls
Day 5 Climb Mt. Amuyao
Day 6 Relax in the natural hot springs in Bontoc or Tinglayan
Day 7 Depart Banaue

Food and Drink Scene of Banaue

Must-Try Dishes

  1. Pinikpikan - A local dish made of chicken meat that is often beaten to death and plucked before being cooked.

  2. Etang - A vegetable soup made with pork, beans, and sweet potato leaves.

  3. Pakbet - A dish made of mixed vegetables and sometimes pork or fish.

Local Beverages

  1. Tapuy - A rice wine that is brewed by the locals.

  2. Barako coffee - A locally grown coffee that has a strong taste and is often served with rice cakes.

  3. Pineapple Juice - A refreshing drink made with locally grown pineapples.

Where to Stay in Banaue: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  1. Banaue Hotel and Youth Hostel - A budget-friendly hostel that offers a fantastic view of the rice terraces.

  2. Banaue Homestay - A family-owned guesthouse situated in the middle of the rice terraces that offer a unique cultural experience.

  3. Green View Lodge - A mid-range hotel that has stunning views of the rice terraces and other natural landmarks.

What to Buy in Banaue: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  1. Handmade baskets - These baskets are intricately woven and often colorful. They make for great souvenirs or gifts.

  2. Locally made rice wine - A unique souvenir that will remind you of your trip to Banaue.

  3. Traditional Ifugao clothing - Buy traditional clothing such as G-string and other woven garments made by locals.

Local Culture and Customs of Banaue

The Ifugao people have a rich culture that centers around the rice terraces. Religion and ancestor worship play an important role in their community. Men are expected to take part in the field work and other agricultural activities, while women take care of the household. Visitors are expected to respect the local customs and traditions of the community.

Best Time to Visit Banaue

The best time to visit Banaue is during the dry season from November to May. The rice terraces are green and vibrant during the summer months from April to May. The rainy season from June to October can make hiking trails and trekking challenging.

Do’s and Don’ts of Banaue

Do:

  1. Dress appropriately when visiting temples or other significant religious sites.

  2. Respect the local culture and customs

  3. Try local food, drinks, and products to support the community.

Don’t:

  1. Wear revealing clothes

  2. Disrespect the local culture and tradition

  3. Leave trash behind or damage natural landmarks and resources.

Safety and Health

Safety in Banaue

Banaue is generally safe for tourists. However, it is important to take precautions such as avoiding solo hiking and staying with your group. Visiting with organized tours is recommended.

Health in Banaue

It is essential to drink bottled water and to use sunscreen and bug repellent. Visitors are advised to consult a doctor before traveling to Banaue.

Getting to Banaue: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Banaue

The nearest airport to Banaue is the Cauayan Airport located 3 hours away by car.

Bus Stations Near Banaue

Genesis and Ohayami are the two most popular bus companies that ply the Manila-Banaue route. Travel time may take 6-9 hours depending on traffic conditions.

Other Options

There are also taxi services, private vans, and motorcycle rentals available.

How to Get Around in Banaue

The best way to get around Banaue is by walking or hiking. Taxis and tricycles are available for longer trips.

Where to Go Next from Banaue: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  1. Sagada - A town known for its fascinating caves, hanging coffins, and limestone formations.

  2. Batad - A nearby village considered to have the most beautiful rice terraces in the region.

  3. Baguio City - A mountain city known for its mild climate, cultural attractions, and pine-tree setting.

Conclusion

Banaue offers a unique cultural experience that is unmatched anywhere else in the world. From the magnificent rice terraces to the natural hot springs, this hidden gem is waiting to be discovered. Hopefully, this comprehensive travel guide will help you plan an unforgettable trip to Banaue.
