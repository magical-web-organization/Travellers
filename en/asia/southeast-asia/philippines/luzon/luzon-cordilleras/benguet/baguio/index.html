Introduction to Baguio

Baguio, also known as the City of Pines, is a mountain resort town located in the northern region of the Philippines. With its cool climate, lush pine trees, and scenic views, Baguio has been a favorite destination among locals and tourists alike.

The city was established by Americans in the early 1900s as a hill station and became known as the Summer Capital of the Philippines. Since then, Baguio has evolved into a bustling city with a thriving tourism industry.

List of Places to Visit in Baguio

Baguio has numerous attractions and activities that cater to different interests. Here is a list of must-visit places in Baguio:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Baguio Museum - showcases the history and culture of Baguio and the Cordillera region.
  • Tam-Awan Village - an artist village that features reconstructed traditional Cordilleran houses and cultural shows.
  • Bell House - the former residence of the American Governor General, now serves as a museum showcasing Baguio’s history.
  • Lourdes Grotto - a popular pilgrimage site that houses a replica of the Lourdes Grotto in France.

Natural Attractions of Baguio: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Burnham Park - Baguio’s premier park that features a man-made lake, athletic tracks, bike lanes, and various recreational activities.
  • Mines View Park - offers scenic views of the surrounding mountains and the old gold and copper mines.
  • The Mansion - the official summer residence of the Philippine President and a popular photo spot for tourists.
  • Botanical Garden - features various plant species and a beautiful Japanese-style garden.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Baguio

Baguio is also known for its hiking and biking routes. Here are some popular trails:

  • Mt. Ulap - a beginner-friendly hike that offers breathtaking views of the Cordillera Mountains.
  • Mt. Pulag - the highest mountain in Luzon, popular among experienced hikers.
  • Lion’s Head - a 2-hour bike ride or a 20-minute drive from the city center that offers scenic views and a photo opportunity with a giant lion head statue.

Best Things to Do in Baguio: Activities and Tours

Baguio offers a wide variety of activities and tours for adventure seekers and culture enthusiasts. Some of the best things to do in Baguio are:

  • Strawberry Picking - go to nearby La Trinidad to pick fresh strawberries and other produce.
  • Night Market - shop for unique souvenirs, local products, and street food at the night market along Harrison Road.
  • Horseback Riding - explore scenic horse trails at Wright Park and Mines View Park.
  • Food Tour - discover the local food scene and try various delicacies such as Baguio longganisa, ube jam, and pinikpikan.

Week-Long Itinerary for Baguio

Here’s a sample itinerary to maximize your stay in Baguio:

Day 1: Explore the city center and visit Burnham Park, Lourdes Grotto, and The Mansion. Day 2: Go on a hike and visit Mt. Ulap or Mt. Pulag. Day 3: Head to La Trinidad for strawberry picking and visit Tam-Awan Village. Day 4: Rent a bike and ride to Lion’s Head, Mines View Park, and Botanical Garden. Day 5: Visit historical sites such as Bell House and Baguio Museum. Day 6: Take a food tour and enjoy the local cuisine. Day 7: Relax and unwind at a spa or visit nearby hot springs.

Food and Drink Scene of Baguio

Baguio is known for its unique local cuisine that reflects Cordilleran culture. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Baguio Longganisa - a slightly sweet and garlicky sausage that is perfect for breakfast.
  • Ube Jam - a purple yam spread that is perfect for bread or as a dessert.
  • Pinikpikan - a traditional Cordilleran dish made from chicken that is beaten to death with a stick before cooking.

Local Beverages

  • Strawberry Taho - a sweet and refreshing combination of soybean curd and strawberry syrup.
  • Benguet Coffee - a locally grown coffee that is known for its rich and bold flavor.

Where to Stay in Baguio: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Baguio has a wide range of accommodation options that cater to different budgets and preferences. Here are some of the best places to stay in Baguio:

  • The Manor at Camp John Hay - a luxurious hotel that offers modern amenities and scenic views.
  • Azalea Residences - a family-friendly hotel with spacious rooms and a kid’s play area.
  • Baguio Forest House - a cozy guesthouse with a rustic feel and a beautiful garden.

What to Buy in Baguio: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Baguio is famous for its locally made products that are perfect for souvenirs or gifts. Here are some of the best things to buy in Baguio:

  • Strawberry Jams and Wines - made from the freshest strawberries harvested from La Trinidad.
  • Weaving Products - handcrafted products such as bags, blankets, and scarves made from traditional weaving techniques.
  • Silver Jewelry - locally made jewelry pieces that feature unique designs inspired by Cordilleran culture.

Local Culture and Customs of Baguio

Baguio is home to various ethnic groups, including the Ibaloi, Kankanaey, and Ifugao. Visitors are encouraged to respect their culture and practices, which include:

  • Respect for Ancestors - locals believe in ancestor worship and perform rituals to show respect for their ancestors and the gods.
  • Weaving Tradition - weaving is an important part of Cordilleran culture, and visitors should appreciate the intricacy and skill involved in making woven products.
  • Festivals - Baguio celebrates various festivals throughout the year, such as the Panagbenga Festival in February and the Amung di Kabunian in April.

Best Time to Visit Baguio

Baguio has a cool climate all year round, but the best time to visit is from November to February when the weather is at its coolest and driest. The peak season is during the Panagbenga Festival in February when the city is bustling with events and activities.

Do’s and Don’ts of Baguio

Do:

  • Respect the local culture and customs.
  • Bring warm clothing as Baguio can get chilly especially at night.
  • Try the local cuisine and delicacies.
  • Support local businesses and buy locally made products.

Don’t:

  • Litter in public places.
  • Disrespect religious sites and practices.
  • Touch or remove artifacts in museums or historical sites.
  • Engage in illegal or inappropriate activities.

Safety and Health

Safety in Baguio

Baguio is a generally safe city, but visitors should still take precautions to avoid incidents such as pickpocketing or theft. Here are some safety tips:

  • Be aware of your surroundings and keep your valuables secure.
  • Avoid walking alone at night in dimly lit areas.
  • Use designated crosswalks when crossing the street.
  • Follow traffic rules and regulations when driving or riding a bike.

Health in Baguio

Baguio has modern medical facilities and hospitals that offer quality healthcare services. Visitors should also take the following precautions to stay healthy:

  • Drink bottled water or boiled water to avoid catching water-borne diseases.
  • Use insect repellent to avoid mosquito bites and prevent dengue or malaria.
  • Practice proper hygiene, such as washing your hands regularly.

Getting to Baguio: Transportation Options and Logistics

Baguio is accessible by various modes of transportation, such as:

Airports Near Baguio

  • Loakan Airport - a small airport that may only cater to chartered flights.

Bus Stations Near Baguio

  • Victory Liner Terminal - offers regular trips to and from Baguio to Manila, Subic, Tarlac, and other destinations.

Other Options

  • Private Car or Van - can be arranged through numerous car rental companies or can be arranged through hotel accommodations.

How to Get Around in Baguio

Baguio has various transportation options that visitors can choose from, such as:

  • Jeepneys and Taxis - a popular mode of transportation in Baguio.
  • Horse-Drawn Carriages - also known as kalesas, offers a unique experience with scenic views of the city.
  • Rent a Bike or Motorcycle - a convenient way to explore Baguio at your own pace.

Where to Go Next from Baguio: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Baguio is surrounded by other popular tourist destinations that visitors can add to their itinerary. Here are some nearby destinations and excursions:

  • Banaue Rice Terraces - a world-famous UNESCO World Heritage Site carved out of the mountain slopes by the Ifugao people.
  • Sagada - a town famous for its beautiful caves, waterfalls, and hanging coffins.
  • La Union - a coastal town known for its surfing spots and beach resorts.

Conclusion

Baguio is a beautiful city that offers a unique blend of culture, history, and nature. With its cool climate, stunning views, and endless activities, Baguio is the perfect destination for those seeking adventure and relaxation. Whether you’re a solo traveler, a family, or a group of friends, there’s something for everyone in Baguio.
