Introduction to Atok

Atok is a town nestled in the province of Benguet, located in the Cordillera Region of the northern Philippines. It is home to gorgeous mountain views and rich cultural heritage. Visitors can catch a glimpse of the local life and enjoy a range of outdoor adventures, including hiking, biking, and exploring natural wonders.

List of Places to Visit in Atok

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Atok has a number of museums and historic sites that give visitors a glimpse into the town’s rich culture and history. The Yamashita Shrine is a memorial for the soldiers who fought and died during World War II. The Old Benguet Road is also a must-visit, as it offers a glimpse into the region’s colonial past. Finally, the Monastery of the Transfiguration is a serene destination that tourists can visit for some peace and quiet.

Natural Attractions of Atok: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Atok is a nature lover’s paradise. There are a number of must-see natural attractions, such as the Northern Blossom Flower Farm, which offers visitors a chance to see a kaleidoscope of colorful flowers. The Sagada Rice Terraces and Mount Timbak are also worth a visit for the panoramic views.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Atok

Atok offers many hiking and biking routes for adventurous travelers. Visitors can take the trail to the summit of Mount Pulag, the third-highest peak in the Philippines. Another popular destination is the Lobo Cave, which offers visitors a chance to explore a hidden cave filled with stunning rock formations.

Best Things to Do in Atok: Activities and Tours

Atok offers a variety of activities and tours for visitors. One popular activity is strawberry picking at the Northern Blossom Flower Farm. Another unique experience is exploring the underground river at the Sagada Cave. For a relaxing day, tourists can visit the hot springs at Mount Timbac and soak in the mineral-rich water.

Week-Long Itinerary for Atok

Day 1-2: Visit the museums and historical sites in the town.

Day 3-4: Explore the natural attractions, such as the Sagada Rice Terraces and Mount Timbak.

Day 5-6: Take on a hiking or biking activity and visit the Lobo Cave.

Day 7: Relax at the hot springs at Mount Timbac before heading home.

Food and Drink Scene of Atok

Must-Try Dishes

Atok is famous for its strawberry dishes, which come in a variety of forms such as jams, tarts, and shakes. Aside from strawberries, locals also enjoy the native coffee and “pinikpikan” soup, a regional delicacy made with traditional ritualistic chicken preparation.

Local Beverages

Visitors can opt for hot barako, an aromatic Philippine coffee, or cold strawberry shakes when exploring Atok. For a more adventurous palate, try the Igorot wine, a traditional wine made from fermented rice or sugarcane.

Where to Stay in Atok: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Atok has many accommodation options for all budgets and preferences, from modern hotels to rustic guesthouses. Visitors can stay in a traditional Igorot house, or opt for a more conventional hotel. The Green Grass Homestay blends modern comforts with traditional design, offering a unique cultural experience ideal for those who wish to immerse in the local way of living.

What to Buy in Atok: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Strawberry jams, pearls, and Igorot handicrafts such as baskets, hand-woven textiles, and woodcarvings are just some of the items visitors can bring home to remember their trip to Atok.

Local Culture and Customs of Atok

Atok is home to the Igorot people, who maintain a rich cultural heritage. Visitors can join in on traditional celebrations such as the Kankana-ey Festival, or visit the series of homestays managed by Igorot families to learn the craftsmanship of basket weaving or textile making.

Best Time to Visit Atok

The best time to visit Atok is during the months of December to April, which offer the best weather and clear mountain views. This is also the season for strawberry picking and some of the most popular festivals, such as the Strawberry Festival of La Trinidad.

Do’s and Don’ts of Atok

Do:

  • Respect local customs and traditions.
  • Dress appropriately when visiting religious sites.
  • Try the local food and drink.
  • Learn the local language, Kankana-ey.

Don’t:

  • Take photos of people without their permission.
  • Litter in public places.
  • Disrupt local traditions and rituals.

Safety and Health

Safety in Atok

Atok is generally safe for tourists, but it is still advisable to travel with caution, especially during evening hours. Visitors must be mindful of the changing weather conditions and dress appropriately for outdoor activities.

Health in Atok

Atok is a remote area and visitors with medical concerns are advised to bring adequate supplies. Due to the high altitude and low air pressure, some visitors may experience altitude sickness, so it’s important to stay hydrated and take necessary precautions.

Getting to Atok: Transportation Options and Logistics

Visitors can reach Atok by land travel from Baguio City, which takes around two hours. From Manila, visitors can take a bus travel which takes around six to seven hours. It’s also possible to charter private vehicles but it’s important to check if the vehicle is licensed and fully insured before departing.

Airports Near Atok

The nearest airport is Loakan Airport in Baguio City, approximately 45 minutes away from Atok.

Bus Stations Near Atok

Visitors can take a Victory Liner bus to Baguio City and then take a van or jeepney to Atok.

Other Options

From Baguio City, visitors can hire a car or motorbike to get around Atok. Public transportation includes jeepneys, which can be taken from the town center.

How to Get Around Atok

Visitors can hire a bike or take a jeepney or a tricycle to travel around Atok. Walking and hiking are also great, low-cost options for visitors who want to explore at their own pace.

Where to Go Next from Atok: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

After exploring Atok, visitors can travel to the nearby town of Sagada, which is known for its scenic views and adventure tours such as caving and trekking. Banaue is also a popular destination and is home to the world-famous rice terraces.

Conclusion

Atok is a hidden gem that offers visitors a chance to explore the untainted culture and nature of Benguet. With rich cultural heritage, stunning natural scenery, and unique local cuisine, Atok is not to be missed.
