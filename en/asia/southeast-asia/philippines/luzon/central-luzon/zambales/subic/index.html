Introduction to Subic

Subic is a municipality located in the province of Zambales in Central Luzon, Philippines. It is home to several natural attractions, outdoor activities, and cultural landmarks. Formerly a United States military base, Subic has undergone a significant transformation into a thriving tourism destination. With a variety of experiences on offer, from adventurous activities to relaxing getaways, Subic has something to offer for every type of traveler.

List of Places to Visit in Subic

If you’re planning a trip to Subic, make sure to include some of these top places in your itinerary.

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Subic has a rich history and culture, and there are several museums and historical landmarks worth visiting. These include:

  • Subic Bay Exhibition and Conference Center - This center is a former US Air Force base that has been converted into a space for conferences, exhibits, and performances.
  • Subic Bay Naval Base - The former naval base is now a museum that showcases the history of the US Navy in the area.
  • All Hands Beach - A popular beach made famous by the US sailors of Subic Bay, it is still a must-visit location for swimming and picnicking.

Natural Attractions of Subic: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Subic is full of natural beauty, including a vast variety of outdoor activities and scenic views. These include:

  • Ocean Adventure - A theme park that features various shows, and activities such as swimming with dolphins and encounters with sea lions.
  • Zoobic Safari - A wildlife park showcasing an array of animals that can be seen while riding a jeepney.
  • Mount Pinatubo - A volcanic crater that started the largest eruption in the 20th century. Today, it is a popular trekking destination, with a scenic view of the turquoise water from the crater lake.
  • Barracuda Lake - A hidden gem in Subic, this diving spot offers turquoise waters and a unique dive experience.
  • Tree Top Adventure Subic - An eco-adventure park that offers various activities such as zipline, canopy rides, and hanging bridge.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Subic

If you’re on an adventure-seeking trip, then Subic has several hiking and biking routes worth exploring. These include:

  • Argonaut Trail - A scenic hiking trail that starts at the Subic Gate.
  • JEST Camp Jungle Survival Training Tour - An adventure course that includes rappelling along cliff faces and hiking through the jungles of Subic.
  • Subic Bay Loop Trail - A 28 km bike route along the coast that offers scenic views of the Subic Bay.

Best Things to Do in Subic: Activities and Tours

Besides hiking and biking, Subic has a diverse range of other activities and tours worth trying. These include:

  • Island Hopping - Explore the untouched islands of Subic Bay.
  • Jet Skiing and Parasailing - Rushing through the waters is an adrenaline high.
  • Sun Cruises’ Sailor’s Grill - Experience the show under the stars while enjoying freshly grilled seafood.
  • Inflatable Island - A giant blow-up playground where visitors can jump, slide and have fun in the sun.

Week-Long Itinerary for Subic

Here’s how you can spend a week in Subic, Philippines:

Day 1: Arrival and check-in to your accommodation. Day 2: Visit Zoobic Safari, the Tiger Safari and the Crocodile Farm. Day 3: Go on a hike at Mount Pinatubo. Day 4: Visit the Ocean Adventure theme park and participate in animal encounters. Day 5: Rent a boat and go island hopping. Day 6: Try out the inflatables at Inflatable Island. Day 7: Enjoy a relaxing day swimming or sunbathing on the beaches of All Hands Beach.

Food and Drink Scene of Subic

Subic has a thriving food and drink scene. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Kare-Kare - A Filipino stew that is a mix of oxtail, peanut sauce, and vegetables served with rice.
  • Sisig - A sizzling plate of chopped pork, garlic, onions, and chilies.
  • Adobo - The national dish of the Philippines, a stew made from chicken or pork marinated in vinegar, soy sauce, garlic and other spices.

Local Beverages

  • San Miguel Beer - The most popular beer in the Philippines, it is brewed in several varieties.
  • Fresh Coconut Juice - A refreshing drink that’s perfect after a warm day in Subic.

Where to Stay in Subic: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Subic has an array of accommodation options to suit a range of travelers, from budget-friendly hostels to luxurious resorts.

  • Subic Grand Seas Resort - A luxurious beachfront hotel with several amenities and facilities such as a spa, restaurant, and bar.
  • By The Sea Resort Hotel- A mid-range beachfront hotel that provides comfortable rooms and has a lively atmosphere.
  • Hertha Bayfront Resort Subic - A budget-friendly resort with a swimming pool and beach access.

What to Buy in Subic: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

If you’re looking for souvenirs to bring back home, Subic has several unique options, including:

  • Bamboo Products - Subic has several shops that sell eco-friendly bamboo products like reusable straws and cutlery.
  • Local Coffee Beans - The Philippines is known for its coffee and a perfect souvenir to bring back home.
  • Handmade Beaded Jewelry - Subic has a variety of handmade jewelry made of shells and beads which would make an ideal gift.

Local Culture and Customs of Subic

The Philippines has diverse cultures and customs, and it’s essential to be respectful and mindful of them. Here are some tips to help you navigate the local culture and customs of Subic:

  • Filipinos are generally hospitable and friendly. Respect their customs and traditions. Greet locals with a smile and say “Mabuhay” (which means welcome!).
  • Take your shoes off when entering homes and leave your shoes outside establishments.
  • Tipping is often practiced, and rounding up to the nearest peso is appreciated.

Best Time to Visit Subic

The best time to visit Subic is during the dry period, from December to April, when temperatures are comfortable and rainfall is low.

Do’s and Don’ts of Subic

Here are some things to keep in mind during your trip to Subic:

Do:

  • Have plenty of water and sunscreen to prevent dehydration and sunburn.
  • Dress conservatively when visiting religious sites and other cultural landmarks.
  • Carry cash in smaller denominations as some establishments do not accept large bills.

Don’t:

  • Litter in public areas.
  • Take pictures of Philippine government and military installations without permission.
  • Wear revealing clothing when visiting places of worship or public areas.

Safety and Health

Safety in Subic

Subic is generally safe, but travelers should take standard precautions to ensure their safety. These include:

  • Keep valuables in a secure place and be mindful of pickpockets.
  • Avoid traveling alone at night in secluded areas.
  • Hire accredited tour guides and don’t venture into unknown areas.

Health in Subic

Subic has modern medical facilities and hospitals. However, visitors should take standard hygiene precautions to avoid illnesses such as:

  • Drink bottled water instead of tap water.
  • Wash your hands regularly and carry a hand sanitizer.

Getting to Subic: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Subic

The nearest international airport to Subic is Clark International Airport, located about 81 km away. From there, visitors can take a taxi or a bus to Subic.

Bus Stations Near Subic

Where to Go Next from Subic: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

If you’re looking to explore more nearby destinations, here are some suggestions:

  • Angeles City - A former U.S. military base, now the entertainment capital of Central Luzon, located only an hour away from Subic.
  • Olongapo City - A city located outside Subic which is rich with historical value and several local markets selling unique souvenirs.

Conclusion

Subic has something for everyone; with its natural beauty, cultural landmarks, and outdoor activities, it is a must-visit destination in the Philippines. Remember to plan your trip in advance to make the most of your adventure in Subic.
