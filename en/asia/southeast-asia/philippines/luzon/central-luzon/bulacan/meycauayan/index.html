Introduction to Meycauayan

A hidden gem of Bulacan, Meycauayan offers a unique blend of historic charm and modern convenience that will captivate travelers from all walks of life. Known as the “jewelry capital of the Philippines,” this bustling municipality is home to stunning natural landscapes, rich cultural attractions, and mouthwatering delicacies that will surely leave visitors feeling amazed and satisfied.

Whether you’re a history buff, nature lover, or an adventurous traveler, there’s something for everyone in Meycauayan. Let’s dive into the many wonders this magical place has to offer.

List of Places to Visit in Meycauayan

From culturally significant attractions to breathtaking natural landscapes, the list of things to explore in Meycauayan is extensive. Here are a few highlights that you shouldn’t miss on your trip:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Bahay na Tisa - This well-preserved ancestral home provides a glimpse into the daily lives of wealthy Filipino families during the Spanish colonial period.
  • Meycauayan Museum - Located inside a centuries-old church, this museum houses a fascinating collection of artifacts that tell the story of Meycauayan’s rich history.
  • Kapitan Moy Ancestral House -Step into this iconic home, which is now a museum, for a glimpse into the life of Kapitan Moy, the town’s founder, and the father of Philippine silverwork.

Natural Attractions of Meycauayan: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Monte Maria Shrine with Grotto - Amidst luscious vegetation, the impressive Monte Maria Shrine with Grotto stands tall and is known as the tallest statue of Mary in the world, with a towering height of 303 feet.
  • Marcelo H. del Pilar National Park - Take a break from the cityscape and enjoy an outdoor picnic or hike through this vast parkland in Meycauayan. Filled with lush greenery and tranquil waterways, it’s the ideal spot to escape into nature.
  • Saltahan Falls - A natural waterfall hidden in the middle of the town. Witness its beauty as streams of water fall from great heights down into a basin pool, ideal for swimming or simply immersing oneself in its beauty.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Meycauayan

Meycauayan is an ideal destination for outdoor enthusiasts. The region offers a fantastic selection of trails and bike paths, providing visitors with ample opportunities to explore the beautiful outdoors. Here are a few must-visit spots:

  • Marcelo H. del Pilar Park Trail - This scenic trail is perfect for families looking for a gentle hike through nature. The trail includes lush tree tunnels and winding pathways that lead to stunning views of the park.
  • Panicuhan Trail - This little-known trail is perfect for more advanced hikers and mountain bikers. It winds through rugged terrain and passes through beautiful stretches of lush vegetation.
  • Barangay Longos Bike Path - Covering a vast network of bike pathways and trails, this path is perfect for city travelers who appreciate a bit of adventure.

Best Things to Do in Meycauayan: Activities and Tours

Here are some engaging activities and tours to get to know Meycauayan better:

  • Silversmithing Workshop - Learn the intricacies of traditional Filipino silverwork by taking a workshop at one of Meycauayan’s many jewelry shops.
  • History Tour - Book a private or group walking tour and stroll through the historical streets of Meycauayan, soaking in all the town’s significant landmarks.
  • Culinary Tour - Meycauayan has numerous delectable delicacies to offer, from homegrown cuisine to street food. Take the opportunity to sample the many flavours the town has to offer.

Week-Long Itinerary for Meycauayan

To make the most out of your trip to Meycauayan for a week, start with:

Day 1: Arrival and check into your hotel, get an early dinner at a local restaurant. Day 2: Start the day with a visit to Monte Maria Shrine with Grotto, then go to Marcelo H. del Pilar National Park for a picnic or hike. Day 3: Attend a SilverSmithing Workshop, grab lunch at a local eatery, and head to Saltahan Falls for an afternoon swim. Day 4: Take a history tour of Meycauayan’s significant landmarks Day 5: Explore the many biking/hiking routes produced by Meycauayan. Day 6: Take a unique culinary tour of Meycauayan’s food scene. Day 7: Relax and spend a lazy day by the Meycauayan River

Food and Drink Scene of Meycauayan

Meycauayan’s food and drink scene is a cornucopia of flavors, featuring vibrant and tasty dishes. Here are a few local options that you should try:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Lechon - Roasted whole pig made with special herbs and spices and is a staple at most Filipino celebrations.
  • Empanada - This delicious pastry is filled with meat, potatoes, carrots, and spices, often served as snacks or starters.
  • Tinola - Chicken soup dish consisting of chicken stock flavoured with ginger, garlic, onions and fish sauce with green papaya, and chilli leaves.

Local Beverages

  • Buko Juice - This refreshing coconut water is a popular drink in Meycauayan.
  • Sago’t Gulaman - A sweet herbal drink made with gelatinous tapioca pearls and sago.

Where to Stay in Meycauayan: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Meycauayan is packed with great accommodations to fit any budget. Here are a few options to consider:

  • The Royal Mandaya Hotel - A premier hotel with luxurious rooms and amenities such as a swimming pool, fitness center, and dining restaurant.
  • The Pink House - A guesthouse with comfortable rooms that are affordable and simple, perfect for a quick stay.
  • Green Mountains Resort - A charming and rustic vibe that makes it feel like you’re in the middle of the forest while still enjoying modern amenities

What to Buy in Meycauayan: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

If you’re looking for unique souvenirs to capture the magic of Meycauayan, the town is known for its outstanding silver craftsmanship. You can either visit one of the many shops along the jewellery street to have something custom-made or purchase something pre-made. Besides that, the town also produces Lychees, a tropical fruit similar to a grape, and other handicrafts like rattan baskets.

Local Culture and Customs of Meycauayan

Meycauayan has a rich and diverse culture born out of its complex history. People here are usually warm and hospitable to visitors. Filipinos are known for their best behaviour, and it is always polite to greet someone with a smile.

Best Time to Visit Meycauayan

The best time to visit Meycauayan would be the dry months, from November to April. The weather during these months is relatively dry and mild and perfect for outdoor activities.

Do’s and Don’ts of Meycauayan

Do:

  • Wear lightweight and comfortable clothes and footwear as the weather can be hot and humid.
  • Carry cash as many establishments don’t have the option of online payment.
  • Bargain while shopping or purchasing anything.
  • Be respectful to the locals’ religious practices and beliefs.

Don’t:

  • Littering
  • Do not neglect to visit the destinations’ significant cultural landmarks
  • Don’t be hesitant to try local food

Safety and Health

Safety in Meycauayan

The town is generally safe for visitors and tourists. However, it is always advisable to be extra cautious and aware of your surroundings. Avoid secluded and unfamiliar places, and do not carry too much valuable property. Keep a copy of your passport and travel documents with you at all times.

Health in Meycauayan

Malaria and Dengue fever have been present in the Philippines, we recommend visiting your doctor or healthcare provider with travel information to stay up to date with vaccine recommendations. Always practice clean and safe eating habits, and remain hydrated throughout your travels.

Getting to Meycauayan: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Meycauayan

If you’re flying, the closest international airport is the Ninoy Aquino International Airport in Manila. From there, it’s a short taxi ride towards Meycauayan. Locally, there is also an airport located in the city of Malolos, which is a little bit closer.

Bus Stations Near Meycauayan

Meycauayan can be quickly reached by bus from Manila or the neighboring cities of Bulacan. Victory Liner, Baliwag Transit, and Genesis Transport are among the popular bus lines that service the city.

Other Options

Taxis, tricycles, and jeepneys are all readily available and make the cheapest mode of transportation around Meycauayan.

How to Get Around in Meycauayan

While exploring Meycauayan, the easiest way to move around is by tricycle or jeepney, the town’s most prominent mode of public transportation. Taxis are also available, though they could be more expensive than other transport options within the town.

Where to Go Next from Meycauayan: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Meycauayan is conveniently located near several popular tourist attractions, many of which show the same charm as Meycauayan. Here are a few places to explore next:

  • Obando - A town located south of Meycauayan and is home to some of the oldest Spanish colonial churches, including the Obando Church.
  • Angat - A town known for its beautiful dam used for supplying water to surrounding irregular areas. Angat is perfect for a fulfilling day trip.
  • Manila -A bustling Filipino metropolis that’s home to stunning cultural landmarks such as the famous Intramuros district and the many museums of Manila, perfect for an educational and historical trip.

Conclusion

Meycauayan is a beautiful and serene destination that offers a wide array of activities and attractions. Indulging in local cuisine, exploring cultural landmarks, and relaxing in nature are just a few things that visitors can expect while exploring this hidden gem of Bulacan.
