Introduction to Marilao

Marilao, a town located in the province of Bulacan in the Philippines, is a hidden gem waiting to be explored. It is a growing industrial town, with many factories and plants scattered throughout the area. However, Marilao also has a rich cultural and natural heritage that is worth exploring. The town’s close proximity to Metro Manila makes it an ideal destination for those who want to escape the city’s hustle and bustle without having to travel far.

List of Places to Visit in Marilao

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Barasoain Church - located in the nearby town of Malolos, this historical church played a significant role in the Philippines’ struggle for independence.
  • Angat Rainforest and Eco Park - a nature reserve with hiking trails, scenic views, and a variety of flora and fauna.
  • Balagtas Museum - a small museum dedicated to the town’s most famous son, Francisco Balagtas, a prominent Filipino poet and playwright.
  • Pinagrealan Cave Complex - located in Norzagaray, this complex of limestone caves is a popular destination for spelunking enthusiasts.

Natural Attractions of Marilao: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Mount Balagbag - this mountain offers breathtaking views of Marilao and the surrounding towns. Hiking trails leading to the summit are available.
  • Meycauayan River - this river is a popular destination for kayaking and whitewater rafting activities.
  • Biak na Bato National Park - located in the nearby town of San Miguel, this park features several caves, waterfalls, and a rich biodiversity of flora and fauna.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Marilao

  • Mount Balagbag Trail - a challenging hike with an elevation gain of over 4,000 feet, this trail offers rewarding views of the surrounding towns.
  • Bike Route Marilao - a 25-kilometer bike route that passes through the town’s scenic rice fields, farms, and rural areas.

Best Things to Do in Marilao: Activities and Tours

  • Plant Tours - Marilao is home to several industrial plants and factories, and many offer guided tours to visitors.
  • Farm Tours - the town’s rural landscape and proximity to Metro Manila make it an ideal destination for agritourism.
  • Food Tours - Marilao is known for its local delicacies and food scene, and a guided tour of the town’s food establishments is a must-do for foodies.

Food and Drink Scene of Marilao

Must-Try Dishes

  • Lechon Manok - a popular roasted chicken dish that’s cooked with a special blend of herbs and spices.
  • Bulalo - a hearty beef soup with tender beef shanks and bone marrow.
  • Adobo - a beloved Filipino dish made with either pork or chicken stewed in a blend of soy sauce, vinegar, and other spices.
  • Pinakbet - a vegetable stew made with mixed vegetables and shrimp paste.

Local Beverages

  • Kapeng Barako - a strong, flavorful coffee grown and produced in Batangas, a neighboring province.
  • Lambanog - a Filipino distilled coconut liquor.

Where to Stay in Marilao: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • The Greenery Resort - a resort with cottages and villas inspired by Balinese architecture and design.
  • Ritz Hotel - a budget-friendly hotel located in the town center.
  • Marilao Suites - a guesthouse that offers comfortable rooms and excellent service.

What to Buy in Marilao: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Casa San Miguel - a store that sells handmade crafts, pottery, and artwork made by local artisans.
  • Punong Gary’s - a shop that sells homegrown produce and agricultural products.
  • Bulacan Sweets - a shop that sells various sweets and delicacies produced in the province.

Local Culture and Customs of Marilao

  • Traditions - the town celebrates several festivals and events throughout the year, including the Marilao Foundation Day and the Feast of San Vicente Ferrer, the town’s patron saint.
  • Language - the people of Marilao speak Tagalog, the national language of the Philippines, as well as the local dialect of Bulacan.
  • Religion - the majority of the town’s population is Roman Catholic.

Best Time to Visit Marilao

The best time to visit Marilao is during the dry season, which runs from November to April. The weather is generally sunny and dry, making it ideal for outdoor activities and sightseeing.

Do’s and Don’ts of Marilao

Do:

  • Respect the local culture and customs.
  • Try the local delicacies and traditional dishes.
  • Practice proper hygiene and sanitation.

Don’t:

  • Litter or damage the environment.
  • Be disrespectful to the locals or cultural sites.
  • Engage in any illegal or unethical activities.

Safety and Health

Safety in Marilao

Overall, Marilao is a safe place for tourists to visit. However, it’s still important to take precautions to keep yourself safe. Avoid walking alone at night, and don’t carry large amounts of cash or expensive items with you in public areas. Make sure to stay aware of your surroundings and be cautious when using public transportation.

Health in Marilao

It’s essential to take the necessary precautions to avoid getting sick while traveling in Marilao. The town’s tap water is not safe to drink, so make sure to only consume bottled or filtered water. Wear insect repellent to avoid mosquito-borne illnesses like dengue fever, which is common in the Philippines.

Getting to Marilao: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Marilao

  • Ninoy Aquino International Airport - located in Manila, approximately 22 kilometers from Marilao.

Bus Stations Near Marilao

  • Victory Liner - a bus company that operates routes to and from Marilao.

Other Options

  • Taxi - taxis are widely available in Metro Manila and can take you to Marilao.
  • Private Car - Marilao is easily accessible by private car. Expressways connect the town to Manila and other nearby cities.

How to Get Around in Marilao

The best way to get around Marilao is by tricycle, a motorized vehicle similar to a rickshaw that can accommodate up to three passengers. Tricycles are a cheap and convenient way to explore the town’s attractions.

Where to Go Next from Marilao: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Malolos - a nearby town that is home to Barasoain Church and several other historical sites.
  • Manila - the Philippines’ capital city, which offers a variety of attractions and activities for tourists.
  • Bataan - a nearby province that offers beautiful beaches, hiking trails, and other outdoor activities.

Conclusion

Marilao is a town that is worth exploring, with its rich culture, natural landmarks, and delicious local cuisine. From hiking and cycling to plant and farm tours, there’s something for everyone to enjoy in Marilao. Whether you’re a backpacker or traveling with family, Marilao is a destination that should be on your list. Plan your trip now and discover the beauty of this charming town in Bulacan, Philippines.
