Introduction to Capas

Located in Tarlac, Central Luzon, Capas is a charming town surrounded by beautiful mountain ranges and lush green fields. Capas has a rich cultural heritage and is known for its historical significance in the Philippines. The town is blessed with natural wonders, including hot springs, mountains, and rivers. Capas makes for an exciting getaway for tourists looking to immerse themselves in the local culture and natural beauty.

List of Places to Visit in Capas

Capas is full of attractions that cater to different interests, including history, culture, nature, and adventure. Here are some of the must-visit places in Capas:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Capas National Shrine
  • Capas Freedom Park
  • Capas Train Station
  • Capas Municipal Hall
  • Capas Aranguren Ancestral House

Natural Attractions of Capas: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Local Culture and Customs of Capas

Capas locals are friendly and hospitable. However, visitors should keep the following customs and traditions in mind:

  • Show respect to elders and authority figures.
  • Dress modestly especially when visiting religious sites.
  • Remove shoes before entering homes or places of worship.

Best Time to Visit Capas

Visitors can visit Capas any time of the year, but the best time to visit is during the dry season, from November to April, when the weather is sunny and warm.

Do’s and Don’ts of Capas

To make your visit to Capas a success, follow these do’s and don’ts:

Do:

  • Respect the local culture and traditions.
  • Wear comfortable and modest clothing.
  • Carry cash as not all places accept credit cards.
  • Be mindful of your surroundings.

Don’t:

  • Disrespect the local culture or traditions.
  • Wear revealing clothing.
  • Litter or damage the environment.
  • Walk around alone at night.

Safety and Health

Visitors should be aware of certain safety and health precautions when visiting Capas.

Safety in Capas

  • Avoid carrying excess cash or valuables.
  • Keep belongings close to you.
  • Be cautious when hiking or biking and follow safety guidelines.

Health in Capas

  • Make sure to drink bottled water and avoid tap water.
  • Bring bug spray as there may be mosquitoes and other insects.

Getting to Capas: Transportation Options and Logistics

Capas is easily accessible via various modes of transportation.

Airports Near Capas

The closest airport to Capas is Clark International Airport, which is about 41 km away. Another option is Ninoy Aquino International Airport in Manila, which is approximately 116 km away.

Bus Stations Near Capas

Visitors can take a bus from Manila to Tarlac City and then transfer to a jeepney or tricycle to Capas.

Other Options

Visitors can also take a shuttle service or book a private car rental.

How to Get Around in Capas

Visitors can get around Capas easily by tricycle or jeepney.
