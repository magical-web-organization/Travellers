Introduction to Tarlac

Tarlac is a province located in the heart of Central Luzon, Philippines. Known for its vast sugarcane plantations, rice fields, and vast greenery, Tarlac is a perfect destination for nature lovers. It is also home to plenty of historical, cultural, and religious landmarks, making it a great educational destination.

Food and Drink Scene of Tarlac

Must-Try Dishes

  • Batutay (Marinated grilled beef)
  • Adobo (Cooked meat in vinegar and soy sauce, served with rice)
  • Buro (Fermented rice with fish)
  • Longganisang Guagua (Tarlac-style sausage)

Local Beverages

  • Kapeng Barako (Strong coffee made from Liberica beans)
  • Sago’t Gulaman (Sweetened drink made with tapioca pearls and jelly)
  • Calamansi Juice (Juice made from the local citrus fruit)

Local Culture and Customs of Tarlac

  • Tarlac is predominantly a Catholic province, and religious beliefs and traditions heavily influence the local culture and customs.
  • The province celebrates plenty of festivals, such as the Feast of San Sebastian and the Piat Sambali Festival.
  • The locals speak Kapampangan and Pangasinense, aside from Tagalog and English.

Best Time to Visit Tarlac

The best time to visit Tarlac is from November to February when the weather is cooler and more pleasant. However, it is also the peak season, so expect crowds and higher prices during this time.

Do’s and Don’ts of Tarlac

Do:

  • Respect the local culture and customs.
  • Dress appropriately when visiting religious sites.
  • Try the local cuisine and drinks.

Don’t:

  • Disrespect religious monuments or images.
  • Litter or damage the environment.
  • Disrespect locals or their traditions.

Safety and Health

Safety in Tarlac

Tarlac is generally safe for tourists, but it is always best to be cautious and vigilant of your surroundings. Avoid walking alone at night in unfamiliar areas and keep your valuables safe.

Health in Tarlac

It is advisable to drink bottled or purified water during your stay in Tarlac. Mosquito-borne diseases such as dengue fever and malaria are prevalent in the Philippines. Therefore, it is always best to carry and use insect repellent.

Getting to Tarlac: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Tarlac

  • Clark International Airport (57 km)

Bus Stations Near Tarlac

  • Victory Liner Bus Terminal (Tarlac City)

Other Options

How to Get Around in Tarlac

  • Tricycles and jeepneys are the most common modes of transportation in Tarlac.
  • Car rentals and taxis are also available but can be more expensive.
