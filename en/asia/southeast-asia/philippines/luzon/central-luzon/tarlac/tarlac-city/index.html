Introduction to Tarlac City

Tarlac City is a bustling metropolis located in the province of Tarlac, in Central Luzon Region of the Philippines. A center for commerce, trade, and tourism, Tarlac City offers a rich cultural, historical, and natural heritage, making it a popular destination among locals and international visitors alike.

This comprehensive travel guide will walk you through the best attractions, activities, and experiences Tarlac City has to offer, from the culturally important museums and historical places to the breathtaking natural wonders, hiking and biking routes, food and drink scene, accommodation options, local culture, and safety and health tips.

List of Places to Visit in Tarlac City

Tarlac City boasts a range of attractions that cater to different interests and preferences. Here are some of the must-visit places in the city:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Luisita Museum: This museum displays the rich history of Tarlac, including the sugar industry, World War II, and the Hacienda Luisita.
  • Aquino Center and Museum: A tribute to the late senator Benigno Aquino Jr. and his wife, former president Corazon Aquino, the center features various exhibits, such as their personal belongings, photos, and videos, that showcase their contribution to the Philippines’ democracy.
  • San Sebastian Cathedral: Tarlac’s oldest church, built in the 1800s, features a mix of Spanish and American colonial architecture and is home to the miraculous image of St. Sebastian, the city’s patron saint.

Natural Attractions of Tarlac City: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Mount Pinatubo: A popular hiking destination that’s renowned for its stunning views, a crater lake, and the experience of trekking through the lahar canyons.
  • Tarlac Recreational Park: Offers various outdoor activities, such as zip-lining, horseback riding, and fishing, and is surrounded by lush greenery.
  • Bamban Falls: A series of waterfalls located inside a forest, popular for swimming, picnics, and trekking.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Tarlac City

Tarlac City is a haven for outdoor enthusiasts, offering various hiking and biking routes that cater to different levels of difficulty and scenic preferences. Here are some of the popular ones:

  • Mount Pinatubo Trek: A 4x4 ride, followed by a 2-hour trek, that leads to a scenic viewpoint overlooking the crater lake.
  • Tarlac Recreational Park Trek: A leisurely hike through lush greenery that offers scenic views of the park’s surroundings.
  • Tarlac-Lanad hike: A challenging hike that takes you through steep terrain and offers panoramic views of the nearby provinces of Pampanga, Tarlac, and Zambales.

Best Things to Do in Tarlac City: Activities and Tours

Apart from the above-mentioned attractions, Tarlac City offers a range of activities and tours that enable you to explore the city’s beauty, culture, and history. Here are some of the top things to do:

  • Heritage Tour: Explore the city’s rich history by taking a tour that covers the museums, churches, ancestral houses, and other historical places.
  • Culinary Tour: Discover Tarlac’s gastronomic scene by visiting the local markets, trying local dishes, and taking a cooking class.
  • Adventure Tour: Go on an adrenaline-pumping adventure by trying out zip-lining, horseback riding, and other outdoor activities offered at the Tarlac Recreational Park.

Food and Drink Scene of Tarlac City

Tarlac City’s food and drink scene is a blend of traditional and modern cuisine, with flavors that are sure to tantalize your taste buds.

Must-Try Dishes

  • Adobo: A dish made of chicken or pork cooked in vinegar, soy sauce, garlic, and other spices, and served with rice.
  • Longganisa: A type of Filipino sausage that’s flavored with garlic, paprika, and other spices, and served with rice and egg.
  • Sisig: A popular dish that’s made of chopped pig’s face and ears, seasoned with chili, vinegar, and soy sauce, and served as a pulutan (appetizer) or main course.

Local Beverages

  • San Miguel Beer: A famous Filipino beer that’s available in different flavors, such as Pale Pilsen, Premium All-Malt, and Super Dry.
  • Kapeng Barako: A strong and bold coffee made from Liberica beans, which are grown in Batangas, a nearby province.
  • Calamansi Juice: A refreshing drink made from calamansi, a local citrus fruit, and sugar or honey.

Where to Stay in Tarlac City: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Tarlac City offers various accommodation options that cater to different budgets and preferences, ranging from budget-friendly guesthouses to luxurious hotels. Here are some of the top-rated ones:

  • La Maja Rica Hotel: A mid-range hotel located in the heart of the city, featuring modern amenities and facilities, such as a rooftop pool, restaurant, and fitness center.
  • The Farmhouse by EDL: A rustic-themed guesthouse located near Mount Pinatubo, featuring spacious and cozy rooms, a cafe, and a garden.
  • Microtel by Wyndham Tarlac: A budget-friendly hotel located near the Luisita Industrial Park, featuring clean and comfortable rooms, a restaurant, and a lounge.

What to Buy in Tarlac City: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Tarlac City offers various souvenirs and local products that make for great gifts or tokens of remembrance. Here are some of the best ones to buy:

  • Tinapa: A type of smoked fish that’s a popular delicacy in Tarlac, available in different flavors, such as spicy and sweet.
  • Bakya: A traditional wooden slipper that’s handcrafted by local artisans, available in different designs and sizes.
  • Kakanin: A type of Filipino dessert that’s made of glutinous rice and coconut milk, and is available in different flavors, such as ube, mango, and pandan.

Local Culture and Customs of Tarlac City

Tarlac City’s local culture and customs are deeply rooted in the Filipino traditions and values, and are influenced by its rich history and diverse population. Here are some of the customs and traditions to keep in mind:

  • Respect for elders: Filipinos value respect for elders, and it’s customary to address them with titles, such as Tito (uncle), Tita (aunt), Lolo (grandfather), and Lola (grandmother).
  • Hospitality: Filipinos are known for their warm and hospitable nature, and it’s customary to be welcoming and accommodating towards guests.
  • Religious traditions: Majority of the Filipinos are Catholics, and it’s customary to attend Mass on Sundays and during special occasions, such as Christmas and Easter.

Best Time to Visit Tarlac City

The best time to visit Tarlac City is during the dry season, which runs from November to May, when the weather is pleasant and conducive for outdoor activities and tours. The wet season, which runs from June to October, is characterized by frequent rainfall and typhoons, which may hamper your travel plans.

Do’s and Don’ts of Tarlac City

Before visiting Tarlac City, here are some of the do’s and don’ts to keep in mind:

Do:

  • Respect the local customs and traditions.
  • Carry a map and a guidebook when exploring the city.
  • Dress appropriately, especially when visiting religious sites or museums.

Don’t:

  • Engage in illegal activities, such as drugs and alcohol abuse.
  • Take photos without permission, especially of people and private property.
  • Disrespect religious sites and symbols.

Safety and Health

Safety in Tarlac City

Tarlac City is generally safe for travelers, and incidents of crime or violence are rare. However, it’s advisable to take the following precautions:

  • Avoid walking alone in secluded or poorly lit areas, especially at night.
  • Keep your valuables, such as cash, jewelry, and gadgets, in a safe place.
  • Be vigilant and aware of your surroundings, especially in crowded areas and tourist spots.

Health in Tarlac City

Tarlac City has various healthcare facilities, such as hospitals, clinics, and pharmacies, that offer quality medical services. However, it’s advisable to take basic precautions, such as:

  • Drink bottled or boiled water to prevent waterborne diseases.
  • Use mosquito repellent to prevent dengue fever and other mosquito-borne illnesses.
  • Seek medical attention immediately if you experience any unusual symptoms or illness.

Getting to Tarlac City: Transportation Options and Logistics

Tarlac City is easily accessible by different modes of transportation, including air, land, and sea. Here are some of the options:

Airports Near Tarlac City

  • Clark International Airport: Located in Pampanga province, approximately 40 kilometers from Tarlac City, and serves local and international flights.

Bus Stations Near Tarlac City

  • Victory Liner: Offers daily trips to Tarlac City from different cities and provinces, such as Manila and Baguio.
  • Philtranco: Offers daily trips to Tarlac City from different cities and provinces, such as Legazpi and Cubao.

Other Options

  • Car rental: You can rent a car from local or international car rental companies, which offer various packages and options.
  • Taxi or Grab: You can hail a taxi or use the Grab app, which offers safe and convenient rides.

How to Get Around in Tarlac City

Tarlac City offers various modes of transportation that cater to different budgets and preferences, such as:

  • Jeepneys: A colorful and iconic mode of transportation in the Philippines, that follows designated routes and makes frequent stops.
  • Tricycles: A motorcycle with a sidecar, that serves as a taxi service and can take you to specific destinations.
  • Taxi or Grab: You can hail a taxi or use the Grab app, which offers safe and convenient rides.

Where to Go Next from Tarlac City: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Tarlac City is surrounded by various destinations and excursions that are worth a visit, such as:

  • Angeles City: A vibrant city located in Pampanga province, known for its nightlife, culinary scene, and historical landmarks.
  • Baguio City: A mountain resort city located in Benguet province, known for its cool climate, scenic views, and cultural attractions.
  • Subic Bay: A former US naval base turned into a commercial and leisure zone, located in Zambales province, known for its beach resorts and adventure activities.

Conclusion

Tarlac City is a gem of a destination, offering a rich blend of culture, history, nature, and adventure, which are sure to leave you awe-inspired and rejuvenated. Whether you’re a solo traveler, a couple, or a family, Tarlac City has something for everyone, and this comprehensive travel guide aims to help you plan your trip and make the most of your stay.
