Introduction to Clark

Located in the heart of Central Luzon, Clark is a popular destination for both local and international travelers. The city’s rich history, natural attractions, outdoor adventures, and booming nightlife scene make it a must-visit destination for anyone looking to experience the best of the Philippines. In this guide, we’ll take you on a journey through some of the top things to do, see, and experience in Clark.

List of Places to Visit in Clark

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Clark has a long and fascinating history that’s waiting to be explored. For those who are interested in learning about the city’s past, there are several museums and historical places that should be on your itinerary. A few notable examples include:

  • Museo ning Angeles: This museum showcases the rich cultural heritage of the city, including its colonial past, local arts and crafts, and more.

  • Nayong Pilipino Clark: This park features replicas of various famous Filipino landmarks and monuments, such as the Banaue Rice Terraces and the Chocolate Hills.

  • Fort Stotsenburg: This former US military base is steeped in history and features several interesting landmarks, such as the Stotsenburg Marker and the Parade Grounds.

Natural Attractions of Clark: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Clark is surrounded by stunning natural scenery, ranging from the lush forests of the nearby mountains to the tranquil waters of the South China Sea. Whether you’re an adrenaline junkie or just looking to relax and unwind, there’s something for everyone in Clark’s great outdoors. Some must-see natural attractions include:

  • Mount Pinatubo: This active volcano is a popular destination for hikers and adventure-seekers, offering stunning views of the surrounding landscape.

  • Sandbox Adventure Park: This park offers a range of outdoor activities, such as zip-lining, ATV riding, and wall climbing.

  • Aetas Forest Park: This protected area is home to the indigenous Aeta community and offers visitors a glimpse into their way of life.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Clark

Clark is a hiker’s and biker’s paradise, with countless trails and routes weaving through its mountains and forests. Some popular options for outdoor enthusiasts include:

  • Sacobia River Eco-Trail: This 14-kilometer trail takes you through the scenic Sacobia River, offering views of the surrounding wildlife and lush forests.

  • Mount Arayat National Park: This park features several hiking trails that lead to the summit of Mount Arayat, which offers breathtaking views of the entire Clark region.

Best Things to Do in Clark: Activities and Tours

Clark offers a range of activities and tours that cater to all interests and budgets. From cultural tours to adventure activities, there’s always something to see and do. Some top picks include:

  • Hot air balloon ride: This is one of the most popular attractions in Clark, allowing visitors to experience the stunning sunrise over the city from high above.

  • Spelunking in Capas: Explore the underground rivers and caves of Capas, just a short drive away from Clark.

  • Party at Walking Street: Walking Street is the hub of Clark’s nightlife scene, featuring a range of bars, clubs, and restaurants that stay open late into the night.

Week-Long Itinerary for Clark

With so much to see and do, it’s easy to get overwhelmed when planning a trip to Clark. To help you make the most of your time in the city, we’ve put together a sample itinerary for a week-long trip:

Day Activities
Day 1 Exploring the cultural attractions, such as Museo ning Angeles and Nayong Pilipino Clark
Day 2 Hiking or biking at Mount Arayat National Park
Day 3 Visiting the natural attractions, such as Mount Pinatubo and Aetas Forest Park
Day 4 Trying out adventure activities, such as zip-lining and ATV riding at Sandbox Adventure Park
Day 5 Exploring the nearby towns of Pampanga and Subic Bay
Day 6 Relaxing at one of the beaches in the area
Day 7 Partying at Walking Street

Food and Drink Scene of Clark

Must-Try Dishes

No trip to Clark is complete without trying the local cuisine. Some of the most popular dishes in the region include:

  • Sisig: A traditional Filipino dish made from parts of a pig’s head, seasoned and served on a sizzling plate.

  • Adobo: A staple of Filipino cuisine, made from marinated chicken or pork cooked in garlic, soy sauce, and vinegar.

  • Pancit luglug: A noodle dish that’s popular in Pampanga, made with thick rice noodles in a rich shrimp sauce.

Local Beverages

In addition to delicious food, Clark is also known for its refreshing beverages. Some local drinks to try include:

  • Kapampangan beer: This beer is brewed in Pampanga and is a popular choice among locals and visitors alike.

  • Buko juice: A refreshing drink made from fresh coconut juice, often served in half a coconut shell.

Where to Stay in Clark: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Clark has a range of accommodations to suit all budgets and preferences. From luxurious hotels to budget-friendly hostels, there’s something for everyone. Some top picks include:

  • Midori Clark Hotel and Casino: This hotel offers upscale amenities, such as a casino, a pool, and several restaurants.

  • Prime City Resort Hotel: This budget-friendly hotel is ideal for travelers on a tight budget, offering comfortable rooms and basic amenities.

  • Maharajah Hotel: This budget-friendly hotel offers comfortable rooms and is perfect for travelers who want to stay in the heart of the city.

What to Buy in Clark: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

There are several souvenirs and local products that are unique to the Clark region. Some must-buys include:

  • T-shirts and keychains with the Clark logo

  • Handcrafted items, such as pottery and wood carvings

  • Kapampangan delicacies, such as tocino and longganisa

Local Culture and Customs of Clark

The locals in Clark are known for their warm hospitality and welcoming nature. To show your respect and appreciation for their culture, be sure to:

  • Dress conservatively when visiting religious sites

  • Remove your shoes when entering someone’s home

  • Use the proper hand gestures when communicating with locals

Best Time to Visit Clark

The best time to visit Clark is during the dry season, which runs from November to April. During this time, the weather is pleasant and the skies are clear, making it perfect for outdoor activities and sightseeing.

Do’s and Don’ts of Clark

Do:

  • Try the local cuisine
  • Dress modestly when visiting religious sites
  • Learn a few basic Filipino phrases to communicate with the locals

Don’t:

  • Disrespect the local culture
  • Drink tap water
  • Wear revealing clothing in public

Safety and Health

Safety in Clark

Clark is generally a safe destination for tourists, but like any other city, it’s important to take certain precautions to ensure your safety. Some tips to keep in mind include:

  • Always keep an eye on your belongings, especially in crowded areas
  • Avoid walking alone at night
  • Use reputable transportation services

Health in Clark

It’s always a good idea to pack a basic first aid kit when traveling to Clark. In case of emergencies, there are several hospitals in the city that offer medical care, such as the Angeles University Foundation Medical Center and the Sacred Heart Medical Center. It’s also recommended that travelers get vaccinated against certain diseases, such as typhoid and hepatitis A.

Getting to Clark: Transportation Options and Logistics

Clark is easily accessible by several modes of transportation, including:

Airports Near Clark

  • Clark International Airport: This is the main airport in the city and is served by several airlines, such as Philippine Airlines and Cebu Pacific.

  • Manila Ninoy Aquino International Airport: This airport is located approximately 90 kilometers away from Clark and is served by several international airlines.

Bus Stations Near Clark

  • Dau Bus Terminal: This is the main bus terminal in Clark and is served by several bus companies, such as Victory Liner and Five Star.

Other Options

  • Private car/taxi: This is a convenient option for travelers who want to explore the surrounding areas at their own pace.

How to Get Around in Clark

Getting around Clark is easy and convenient, with several transportation options to choose from, such as:

  • Jeepneys: These are the most common mode of transportation in the city, and they run on fixed routes.

  • Tricycles: These are motorcycle taxis that are ideal for short trips around the city.

  • Taxis: These are readily available throughout the city and are perfect for longer trips or traveling in comfort.

Where to Go Next from Clark: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Clark is located in central Luzon, making it a great starting point for exploring other parts of the region. Some nearby destinations and excursions include:

  • Mount Pinatubo trekking
  • Subic Bay Freeport Zone
  • Mount Arayat National Park

Conclusion

Clark is a city that has something for everyone, from cultural attractions and natural wonders to adrenaline-pumping adventures and vibrant nightlife. With this comprehensive travel guide, you’re now ready to plan your trip to Clark and experience the best of the Philippines. Happy travels!
