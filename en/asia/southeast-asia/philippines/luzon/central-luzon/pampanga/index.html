Introduction to Pampanga

Pampanga is a province in the Central Luzon region of the Philippines known for its rich history, culinary delights, and natural beauty. It is located in the northern part of the island of Luzon and is bordered by the provinces of Tarlac, Nueva Ecija, Bulacan, and Bataan. The capital city of Pampanga is San Fernando, which is known for its Giant Lantern Festival held every December.

List of Places to Visit in Pampanga

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Holy Rosary Parish Church

The Holy Rosary Parish Church is a 19th-century Baroque-style church located in Angeles City. It is known for its stunning stained-glass windows and ornate interiors that showcase the Spanish colonial influence in the Philippines.

Museo ning Angeles

Museo ning Angeles is a museum dedicated to showcasing the heritage and culture of Angeles City and the Pampanga province. It features exhibits that highlight the province’s ancient history, the American colonial period, and the contemporary art scene.

Natural Attractions of Pampanga: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Mount Pinatubo Trek

One of the most popular attractions in Pampanga is the Mount Pinatubo Trek, a volcanic crater lake nestled on top of a mountain. The trek itself, while challenging, is an exciting adventure that takes visitors through scenic views of the lush greenery and the majestic volcano.

Nayong Pilipino Clark

Nayong Pilipino Clark is a nature theme park located in the Clark Freeport Zone. The park offers visitors a chance to experience the various landscapes of the Philippines, showcasing replicas of historic landmarks, flora, and fauna, as well as cultural shows.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Pampanga

Sacobia River Eco-Trail

The Sacobia River Eco-Trail is a hiking and biking trail located in the Clark Freeport Zone. The trail offers stunning views of the river and the mountains. Visitors can see local flora, birds, and other wildlife along the way.

SandBox

SandBox is an adventure park located in the Clark Freeport Zone. It offers a variety of outdoor activities, including hiking, biking, zip-lining, and wall climbing. This destination is perfect for adventure-seekers and families who want a fun-filled day of activities.

Best Things to Do in Pampanga: Activities and Tours

Culinary Tours

Pampanga is known for its unique and delectable cuisine, and taking a culinary tour is a great way to experience it. Visitors can try delicacies such as sisig (pork dish) and tocino (cured meat).

Carabao Riding

Carabao riding is a fun and unique way to explore the rural areas of Pampanga. Riders can experience the traditional modes of transportation for farmers in the Philippines and take in the beautiful countryside scenery.

Week-Long Itinerary for Pampanga

Day 1: Visit Angeles City and explore Holy Rosary Parish Church.

Day 2: Go on a culinary tour and try local delicacies.

Day 3: Trek Mount Pinatubo and visit the crater lake.

Day 4: Explore Museo ning Angeles and admire the contemporary art scene.

Day 5: Experience SandBox and try out different outdoor activities.

Day 6: Visit Nayong Pilipino Clark and discover the diverse landscapes of the Philippines.

Day 7: Go on a carabao riding tour and witness the rural beauty of Pampanga.

Food and Drink Scene of Pampanga

Must-Try Dishes

Sisig

Sisig is a popular Filipino dish that originated in Pampanga. It is made from grilled pork pieces, onions, and spices, served on a sizzling plate.

Tocino

Tocino is a sweet and savory cured meat that is popular in the Philippines. The meat is traditionally made from pork belly, seasoned with sugar, salt, and various spices.

Local Beverages

Kapampangan Craft Beer

Kapampangan Craft Beer is a popular local beer that is brewed in Pampanga. Visitors can try different flavors such as mango and coffee.

Tsokolate

Tsokolate is a Filipino hot chocolate drink made from cacao beans. This beverage is rich, creamy, and perfect for cozying up during a cold evening.

Where to Stay in Pampanga: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Hotel Stotsenberg

Hotel Stotsenberg is located in the Clark Freeport Zone, offering guests amenities such as an outdoor pool, spa services, and its casino. The hotel has a modern vibe with spacious rooms and a fitness center.

Victoria Court San Fernando

Victoria Court San Fernando is a unique hotel that offers guests themed rooms. Each room has a distinct and playful design, making for a memorable stay.

What to Buy in Pampanga: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Pastillas de Leche

Pastillas de Leche is a local sweet that is made from milk and sugar. These candies are creamy, chewy, and make for a perfect souvenir.

Chicharon

Chicharon is a Filipino pork snack that is popular in Pampanga. It is a crispy, fried snack that can be stored for a long time, making it an ideal gift for people back home.

Local Culture and Customs of Pampanga

Filipinos, including those from Pampanga, are known for their hospitality and warmth towards visitors. It’s customary to greet locals with “Kumusta?” which means “How are you?” in Filipino. It’s also common to see residents gathering for fiestas, which are religious or cultural celebrations, resulting in vibrant festivals with great energy.

Best Time to Visit Pampanga

Pampanga can be visited year-round, but the best time to visit is during the dry season, from November to April. The temperatures are more comfortable, and there is less chance of rain.

Do’s and Don’ts of Pampanga

Do:

  • Learn a few Filipino phrases. Locals will appreciate the effort and will be more likely to help you out.

  • Try local cuisine dishes. Some may be unfamiliar, but the taste is always worth it.

Don’t:

  • Do not go directly to a local’s house without prior permission.

  • Do not touch religious relics present in churches.

Safety and Health

Safety in Pampanga

Pampanga is relatively safe, but visitors should still take the necessary precautions such as being aware of their surroundings, especially when travelling at night.

Health in Pampanga

Visitors should drink bottled water and be careful with food such as street food. They should also visit a doctor or a travel clinic for advice on vaccinations.

Getting to Pampanga: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Pampanga

The Clark International Airport serves as the main airport for the province, while Ninoy Aquino International Airport in Metro Manila also services the area.

Bus Stations Near Pampanga

Bus terminals such as Genesis Bus Terminal in Pasay and Victory Liner in Cubao offer daily scheduled trips to Pampanga.

Other Options

Car rentals and private car hires are also available for those who want to explore the province at their convenience.

How to Get Around in Pampanga

Jeepneys and tricycles are the main means of public transportation in Pampanga and are readily available almost everywhere. Taxis and car rentals are also options for those who prefer more comfort and privacy.

Where to Go Next from Pampanga: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Subic

Subic is located in Zambales, about 70km from Pampanga. It’s a former US naval base that was converted into a tourism and economic hub. Visitors can enjoy water sports, duty-free shopping, and visit the Zoobic Safari.

Manila

Manila is the capital city of the Philippines, located a few hours away from Pampanga. Visitors can explore the historic district of Intramuros, the popular shopping district of Divisoria, and indulge in the local cuisine.

Conclusion

Pampanga is a destination that offers a diverse range of attractions, including cultural landmarks, scenic views, and culinary experiences. Visitors can participate in outdoor adventures, immerse themselves in the local culture, and indulge in delightful local cuisine. Take time to visit Pampanga and experience the Philippines beyond its summer beach escapes.
