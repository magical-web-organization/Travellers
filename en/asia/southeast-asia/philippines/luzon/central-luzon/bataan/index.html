Introduction to Bataan

Bataan is a province situated in Central Luzon, on the western side of Luzon Island in the Philippines. The place is known for its historical significance, natural beauty, delicious cuisine, and warm hospitality. Bataan was one of the most important battlefields during the Second World War and played a crucial role in the Allied forces’ victory against the Japanese. Today, Bataan is an exceptional travel destination that showcases the Filipino culture, history, and natural landscapes.

List of Places to Visit in Bataan

There are several places and attractions worth visiting in Bataan. Below is a list of some of the most popular ones:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  1. Bataan World War II Museum - A history museum that showcases the wartime experiences of Filipinos and Americans during the Second World War.
  2. Mount Samat National Shrine - A towering structure commemorating the bravery and sacrifice of Filipino and American soldiers during the Battle of Bataan.
  3. Philippine-Japan Friendship Tower - A symbol of friendship between the Philippines and Japan, built on the site where the infamous Death March started in 1942.

Natural Attractions of Bataan: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  1. Las Casas Filipinas de Acuzar - A heritage resort that features Spanish-colonial-era houses that were carefully preserved and restored.
  2. Dunsulan Falls - A stunning natural attraction located in the town of Pilar, where visitors can immerse themselves in the refreshing waters and luscious natural scenery.
  3. Sisiman Bay - A picturesque bay located in Mariveles that offers a dramatic view of the sea and its surrounding mountains.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Bataan

Bataan boasts numerous trails that are perfect for hiking and biking enthusiasts. Here are some of the best routes:

  1. Tarak Ridge to Mariveles Five Finger - A challenging hike that rewards hikers with a stunning view of the Mariveles coastline.
  2. Mount Samat Trek - This trail takes you to the Mount Samat National Shrine, where visitors can witness the breathtaking beauty of Bataan’s lush forests and scenic landscapes.
  3. Bataan Nuclear Power Plant Trek - This hiking route will take you to the abandoned Bataan Nuclear Power Plant and offers an excellent view of the surrounding landscapes.

Best Things to Do in Bataan: Activities and Tours

Bataan offers various activities and tours that cater to every traveler’s interests. Below are some of the must-try ones:

  1. Island Hopping - Explore the beautiful and uninhabited beaches of Bataan’s nearby islands.
  2. Food Trip - Savor the unique and delicious flavors of Bataan’s local cuisine, such as Sisig, Adobo, and Pampanga tocino.
  3. Sunset Cruise - Experience the romantic and breathtaking sunset views of Manila Bay aboard a catamaran-style boat.

Food and Drink Scene of Bataan

Bataan is known for its delicious and unique cuisine that reflects its cultural heritage and history. Here are some of the must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  1. Sisig - A popular dish made of pig’s head and liver, seasoned with calamansi and onions.
  2. Adobo - A classic Filipino dish made of chicken or pork, cooked in soy sauce, vinegar, garlic, and black pepper.
  3. Pampanga Tocino - A sweet and savory cured meat that is distinctly Filipino.

Local Beverages

  1. Kapeng Barako - A strong and flavorful coffee that is a must-try for coffee-lovers.
  2. Calamansi Juice - A refreshing and tangy drink made from calamansi, a local citrus fruit.
  3. Sago’t Gulaman - A sweet and refreshing drink made of tapioca pearls and jelly cubes.

Where to Stay in Bataan: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Bataan offers various accommodation options that cater to different budgets and preferences. Here are some of them:

  1. The Oriental Bataan - A luxury resort situated in Mariveles that offers stunning views of the sea and surrounding landscapes.
  2. Camaya Coast Hotel - A 5-star hotel located in Mariveles that boasts world-class amenities and stunning views of the mountains and the sea.
  3. Sunflower Bed and Breakfast - A cozy and affordable guesthouse located in Balanga that offers a warm and homely atmosphere.

What to Buy in Bataan: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Bataan offers several unique and locally made products that are perfect as souvenirs or gifts. Below are some of them:

  1. Kakanin - A rice-based dessert that comes in different shapes, sizes, and flavors.
  2. Bataan Handicrafts - Handmade products such as baskets, hats, mats, and decor made from local materials such as bamboo and rattan.
  3. Bataan’s Peanut Butter - A delicious and creamy peanut butter made from freshly roasted peanuts.

Local Culture and Customs of Bataan

Bataan’s local culture and customs are deeply rooted in its heritage and history. Filipinos are known to be hospitable, warm, and friendly. Visitors are encouraged to respect and appreciate the local culture and traditions, such as removing their shoes when entering a Filipino home and addressing elders as “po” and “opo.”

Best Time to Visit Bataan

The best time to visit Bataan is during its dry season, which runs from November to May. The weather during these months is warm and sunny, and the skies are usually clear. However, visitors should expect crowded beaches and tourist spots during the peak season, especially during the Christmas and Easter holidays.

Do’s and Don’ts of Bataan

Do:

  • Respect the local culture and customs.
  • Try the local cuisine and delicacies.
  • Bring sunscreen, insect repellent, and comfortable clothing for outdoor activities.

Don’t:

  • Litter or damage the environment.
  • Wear revealing or inappropriate clothing in religious or cultural sites.
  • Be rude or disrespectful to the locals.

Safety and Health

Safety in Bataan

Bataan is a relatively safe place for tourists. However, visitors are advised to remain vigilant and cautious, especially when traveling alone or at night. Visitors are also advised to keep their valuables safe and secured at all times. It is recommended to bring a copy and keep your passport safe in your hotel room.

Health in Bataan

Visitors are advised to drink only bottled or boiled water and practice good hygiene and sanitation practices to avoid health risks. It is also recommended to get updated vaccinations against diseases such as tetanus, hepatitis, and typhoid fever before traveling to the Philippines.

Getting to Bataan: Transportation Options and Logistics

Traveling to Bataan is quite easy, and there are various transportation options available.

Airports Near Bataan

The nearest airport to Bataan is the Clark International Airport, located in Pampanga, which offers both domestic and international flights.

Bus Stations Near Bataan

Several bus companies offer regular trips to Bataan from Manila and other cities in Central Luzon, such as Victory Liner, Five Star Bus, and Bataan Transit.

Other Options

Visitors can also reach Bataan by private cars, vans, and taxis. Car rental services are also available in Manila and nearby cities.

How to Get Around in Bataan

Getting around Bataan is relatively easy, and there are several transportation options available:

  • Jeepneys and Tricycles are the most common modes of transportation in Bataan.
  • Taxis are also available but can be a bit expensive.
  • Rental vehicles and motorcycles are available for tourists who wish to explore the place at their own pace.

Where to Go Next from Bataan: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Bataan is a great starting point for exploring other nearby destinations and excursions. Here are some of them:

  1. Subic Bay - A former US military base that has been converted into a free port zone and a popular tourist destination that offers various activities such as water sports, diving, and gaming.
  2. Zambales - A coastal province known for its scenic beaches, stunning landscapes and offer various activities like Island hopping, Beach volleyball, Surfing, and more.
  3. Angeles City - A city located in Pampanga known for its nightlife, culinary scene, and historical landmarks.

Conclusion

Bataan is a beautiful and culturally rich travel destination that offers various activities and attractions. Visitors can immerse themselves in its long history, natural beauty, delicious cuisine, and warm hospitality. With this comprehensive travel guide to Bataan, you are now ready to explore and experience the best that this province has to offer.
