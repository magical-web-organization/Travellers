Introduction to Balanga

Balanga is a city located in the province of Bataan, Central Luzon, Philippines. It is a destination for history enthusiasts, nature lovers, and foodies. The city’s name “Balanga” is derived from the Tagalog word “banga” which means pot. The city is popularly known for its geothermal energy sources, delicious cuisine, and its role during World War II.

List of Places to Visit in Balanga

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  1. Plaza Mayor de Balanga - The center of the city where the Balanga Cathedral and the capitol are located.
  2. Bataan World War II Museum - A museum showcasing the history of Bataan during the Japanese occupation.
  3. Philippine-Japanese Friendship Tower - A tower located in the historic Mt. Samat in honor of the alliance of the Philippines and Japan.
  4. Santo Domingo de Guzman Church - A church founded in 1714 with beautiful Baroque architecture.

Natural Attractions of Balanga: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  1. Balanga Wetland and Nature Park - A place with a variety of bird species for bird watching and a scenic biking trail.
  2. Tala View Deck and Eco Park - A park with a peaceful atmosphere that offers stunning views of the surroundings.
  3. Pawikan Conservation Center - A sanctuary for sea turtles that also offers educational tours.
  4. Mount Samat National Shrine - A monument dedicated to the Battle of Bataan, with a museum showcasing the history and timeline of the battle.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Balanga

  1. Balanga Wetland and Nature Park Bike Trail - A bike trail that offers a scenic view of the wetlands and birdwatching opportunities.
  2. Tukon Bike Trail - A challenging bike trail with a scenic view of Tukon Hill and the surrounding area.
  3. Pawikan Trail - A trail that passes through the Pawikan Conservation Center and nearby hills.

Best Things to Do in Balanga: Activities and Tours

  1. Island Hopping in the Five Fingers Cove - Enjoy swimming, snorkeling, and island hopping in the Five Fingers Cove.
  2. Food Tour in Balanga’s Heritage Village - Sample get to taste delicious cuisine while exploring the historic Heritage Village.
  3. Spa and Massage in Las Casas Filipinas de Acuzar - Relax and unwind at the French-inspired spa and massage center in Las Casas Filipinas de Acuzar.

Food and Drink Scene of Balanga

Must-Try Dishes

  1. Bataan’s Famous Chicken Inasal - A dish of grilled chicken, marinated in a blend of coconut vinegar, garlic, ginger, and calamansi juice.
  2. Pastel de Lengua - A savory dish made of ox tongue, mushrooms, and pickles served in a puff pastry.
  3. Tinapang Balo-Balo - A classic Kapampangan dish consisting of smoked fish (tinapa) with fermented rice (balao-balao).
  4. Tamales - A popular snack made of sticky rice, meat, and peanuts or beans, enclosed in a banana leaf and steamed until cooked.

Local Beverages

  1. Kamias Shake - A refreshing drink made of kamias or bilimbi fruit blended with ice and sugar.
  2. Sago’t Gulaman - A sweet beverage made of sago pearls, jelly cubes (gulaman), and brown sugar syrup mixed with water and ice.
  3. Calamansi Juice - A citrusy drink made of calamansi mixed with sugar or honey and water.

Where to Stay in Balanga: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  1. The Plaza Hotel Balanga - A 4-star hotel located in the center of the city.
  2. Crown Royale Hotel Balanga - A 3-star hotel with modern amenities located near the Wetland and Nature Park.
  3. Stilts Calatagan Beach Resort - A beach resort located in nearby Calatagan, Batangas that offers a scenic view of the sea.

What to Buy in Balanga: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  1. Peanut Butter from Orion - A mouth-watering peanut butter that comes with unique flavors such as ube, mocha, and espresso.
  2. Bataan-made Hats and Accessories - Buy locally-made hats and accessories made of native materials like abaca fiber and bamboo.
  3. Cashew Products - Bataan is famous for its cashew nuts. In Balanga, you can buy cashew brittle, cashew cream, and roasted cashews.

Local Culture and Customs of Balanga

  1. Locals speak Tagalog and a few people speak English, but many residents are bilingual in Tagalog and Kapampangan.
  2. The Bataan dialect is unique and has similarities with Kapampangan, Tagalog, and Ilocano.
  3. Balanga celebrates the Pawikans (sea turtles) every year, usually during the third week of November.
  4. The city also celebrates the Balangay Festival every April to celebrate the historic culture of the people of Balanga.

Best Time to Visit Balanga

The best time to visit Balanga is between December and April when the weather is dry and cool.

Do’s and Don’ts of Balanga

Do:

  1. Respect the local culture and norms.
  2. Explore the city on a bike or hike to appreciate the scenic view.
  3. Book a tour guide or hire a driver for a hassle-free tour experience.
  4. Try the local cuisine and delicacies.

Don’t:

  1. Litter in public places.
  2. Ignore public dress codes in cultural and religious places.
  3. Engage in prohibited activities.
  4. Damage the natural habitat and scenic spots.

Safety and Health

Safety in Balanga

Balanga is generally safe, but as with any travel, take extra precautions, avoid wandering alone in secluded areas, and be aware of your surroundings.

Health in Balanga

Healthcare in Balanga is available, and several clinics and hospitals operate in the city. Additionally, there are many pharmacies and drugstores.

Getting to Balanga: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Balanga

  1. Clark International Airport - approximately an hour and a half drive
  2. Ninoy Aquino International Airport - approximately two and a half hours drive

Bus Stations Near Balanga

  1. Dau Bus Terminal - approximately an hour and a half drive
  2. Pasay and Cubao Bus Stations - approximately two and a half hours drive

Other Options

  1. Hire a private car or a taxi.
  2. Travel by ferryboat to/from Manila.

How to Get Around in Balanga

Options include hiring a taxi, renting a car, driving a personal vehicle, hiring a tricycle, or hiring a bike tour guide.

Where to Go Next from Balanga: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  1. Mount Pinatubo - A popular destination for hiking and trekking.
  2. Las Casas Filipinas de Acuzar - A historic and picturesque village located in Bataan’s nearby beach town.
  3. Corregidor Island - A historic island with remnants of World War II.

Conclusion

Balanga is a city that offers visitors an opportunity to experience the historical and scenic views of Bataan. This complete travel guide to the city will help you plan your itinerary and discover its natural and cultural attractions while experiencing its food and drink scene.
