Introduction to Sittwe

Sittwe is a small yet vibrant city located on the Bay of Bengal in the Rakhine State of Western Myanmar. Formerly known as Akyab, the city is a melting pot of different cultures, reflecting its diverse past and present.

Sittwe is home to many attractions that showcase its rich history and culture, including pagodas, museums, and cultural festivals. Additionally, its natural beauty attracts outdoor enthusiasts, making Sittwe the perfect destination for adventure seekers and cultural travelers alike.

List of Places to Visit in Sittwe

Here are some of the top attractions to visit in Sittwe:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Rakhine State Cultural Museum: This museum showcases the art, history, and culture of the Rakhine State and its people, including ancient artifacts, textiles, and jewelry.

  • Shwe Zedi Kyaung: One of the most important Buddhist monasteries in Sittwe, this historic site features intricate woodcarvings and gilded Buddha statues.

  • Sittwe U Ottama Park: This park is home to the Sittwe War Memorial, which commemorates the lives lost during World War II.

Natural Attractions of Sittwe: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • The Viewpoint: Situated atop a hill overlooking the city, this viewpoint provides breathtaking panoramic views of Sittwe and the surrounding landscapes.

  • Ngapali Beach: Although a bit of a drive from Sittwe, Ngapali Beach is a must-visit attraction for those seeking a day of relaxation, sunbathing, and swimming in crystal-clear waters.

  • Sunset Cruises: Take a leisurely boat ride along the Bay of Bengal and watch as the sun sets over the horizon.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Sittwe

Sittwe is home to many beautiful routes for hiking and biking. Some popular options include:

  • Sittwe Beach to Ponnagyun: This scenic route takes visitors along the coast, through jungle paths, and past local villages.

  • Sittwe to Maruk U: A challenging ride that takes you through the hills and jungles of the Rakhine State.

Best Things to Do in Sittwe: Activities and Tours

  • Sittwe River Tour: Take a guided boat tour along the Kaladan and Mayu Rivers, with opportunities to see the local fishing industry and visit nearby villages.

  • Snorkeling and Diving: Ngapali Beach and nearby islands offer great options for snorkeling and diving, with colorful coral reefs and diverse marine life.

Week-Long Itinerary for Sittwe

Here’s a suggested itinerary for a week-long visit to Sittwe:

  • Day 1-2: Explore Sittwe’s historic sites, including the Rakhine State Cultural Museum, Shwe Zedi Kyaung, and the War Memorial.

  • Day 3-4: Take a boat tour of the Kaladan and Mayu Rivers and visit local villages.

  • Day 5-6: Head to Ngapali Beach for two days of relaxation, beachcombing, snorkeling, or diving.

  • Day 7: Take a sunset cruise on the Bay of Bengal.

Food and Drink Scene of Sittwe

Must-Try Dishes

  • Mohinga: A traditional Myanmar breakfast dish consisting of rice noodles in fish broth, typically served with hard-boiled egg and vegetables.

  • Rakhine Montin: A spicy fish stew made with tomatoes and a variety of spices.

Local Beverages

  • Myanmar Beer: This locally brewed beer is a popular drink in Sittwe and throughout the country.

  • Green Tea: This mild and refreshing tea is a popular beverage throughout Myanmar.

Where to Stay in Sittwe: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Shwe Thazin Hotel: This mid-range hotel is centrally located and offers clean rooms, a restaurant, and professional staff.

  • Oway Guesthouse: A budget-friendly accommodation in the heart of Sittwe, providing comfortable rooms and a friendly atmosphere.

  • Mrauk U Princess Resort: A luxury resort located near the historic town of Mrauk U, offering spacious rooms, a swimming pool, and views of the surrounding mountains.

What to Buy in Sittwe: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Rakhine Longyi: A traditional Myanmar garment made with locally-woven cotton or silk, perfect for a souvenir or gift.

  • Burmese Lacquerware: A traditional craft of Myanmar that produces beautiful, intricate bowls, vases, and other decorative items.

Local Culture and Customs of Sittwe

  • Dress Code: Visitors should dress modestly and respect local customs, avoiding revealing clothing when visiting temples or other religious sites.

  • Greeting Etiquette: The traditional greeting in Myanmar is a slight bow accompanied by a handshake, avoiding physical contact with members of the opposite sex.

Best Time to Visit Sittwe

The best time to visit Sittwe is between November and February, when the weather is cooler and drier, and outdoor activities are more enjoyable.

Do’s and Don’ts of Sittwe

Do:

  • Respect local customs and traditions

  • Ask before taking photographs of local people

Don’t:

  • Bring or use drugs

  • Disrespect religious sites or customs

Safety and Health

Safety in Sittwe

Sittwe is generally a safe city for travelers, but visitors should be aware of pickpocketing and petty theft, especially in crowded areas.

Health in Sittwe

Visitors to Sittwe should take precautions to prevent mosquito bites and other insect bites, and consider vaccinations for hepatitis A and B, typhoid, and Japanese encephalitis.

Getting to Sittwe: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Sittwe

The nearest airport to Sittwe is the Sittwe Airport, which offers limited domestic flights.

Bus Stations Near Sittwe

Buses from Yangon to Sittwe run daily, with travel times ranging from 18-24 hours depending on route and operator.

Other Options

Ferries from Yangon to Sittwe run several times per week, and private taxis can be hired for visitors seeking a more comfortable and personalized journey.

How to Get Around in Sittwe

Taxis and rental cars are available for visitors seeking to explore the city and surrounding areas, but walking or cycling is a great way to experience the local culture and scenery.

Where to Go Next from Sittwe: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Mrauk U: One of the most important archeological sites in Myanmar, featuring ancient temples, pagodas, and other historic landmarks.

  • Bagan: Another of Myanmar’s most significant cultural destinations, featuring thousands of ancient temples and pagodas spread across a vast landscape.

Conclusion

Sittwe is a vibrant and culturally rich city that offers visitors a unique and authentic experience of Myanmar’s diverse landscapes, people, and history. Whether seeking outdoor adventures or cultural insights, Sittwe is the perfect destination for any traveler seeking to explore Myanmar’s Western region.
