Introduction to Inle Lake

Nestled in the eastern part of Myanmar, Inle Lake is a freshwater lake renowned for its scenic beauty, serene atmosphere, and rich culture. It is one of the top tourist destinations in Southeast Asia. The lake is surrounded by lush green hills, and is home to nearly 20,000 local Intha people, whose unique way of life has evolved over centuries.

List of Places to Visit in Inle Lake

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  1. Phaung Daw Oo Pagoda - One of the most sacred pagodas in Myanmar, it houses five Buddha statues that are adorned with gold leaf by devotees.
  2. Shwe Indein Pagoda Complex - A cluster of over 1,000 ancient pagodas featuring intricate carvings and sculptures that depict the history of the region.
  3. Nyaung Ohak - A fascinating archaeological site that features over 200 stupas and other religious structures from the 16th century.
  4. Inthar Heritage House - A museum that showcases the culture, traditions, and lifestyle of the local people.

Natural Attractions of Inle Lake: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  1. Balloon Rides - Take to the skies for a sensational panoramic view of the lake and surrounding hills.
  2. Hiking Trails - There are many walking trails that lead through forests, villages, and fields offering great adventures and stunning views.
  3. Kayaking/Tubing - Rent a kayak or inner tube and explore the calm waters of the lake at your leisure.
  4. Hot Springs - Relax in some of the best hot springs in Southeast Asia and soak up the stunning mountain scenery.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Inle Lake

  1. Red Mountain Estate Vineyards and Winery - A popular spot with scenic trails through grapevines and stunning views of the valley.
  2. Khaung Daing Village - Explore the town and its surrounding waterfalls, hot springs, and nearby rice paddies.
  3. The Lady Lagoon - Cross the lake to reach the Lady Lagoon where a trekking expedition awaited, with scenic views and local coffee plantations.
  4. Taunggyi Hiking Trail - Hike through deep forests leading to serene lake views.

Best Things to Do in Inle Lake: Activities and Tours

  1. Inle Lake Boat Tour - Cruise the lake and explore its many attractions.
  2. Five-Day Market Tour - Immerse yourself in the local market experience, a colorful and bustling affair held on different days in different towns.
  3. Inthein (Indein) Stupa Tour - Visit the impressive Inthein (Indein) Stupa complex, a collection of ruins that date back to the 17th century.
  4. Cooking Classes - Learn how to cook delicious traditional Myanmar cuisine with local chefs.

Week-Long Itinerary for Inle Lake

Day 1: Arrival in Inle Lake and check-in to hotel. Day 2: Inle Lake boat tour, visiting Phaung Daw Oo Pagoda, Nyaung Ohak, and other historical attractions. Day 3: Red Mountain Winery and hiking tour in the morning, followed by a visit to Inthar Heritage House and cooking class in the afternoon. Day 4: Cycling to Khaung Daing village, visiting hot springs, waterfalls and rice paddies. Day 5: Cruise to the Lady Lagoon, followed by trekking and scenic views of coffee plantations. Day 6: Visit the Inthein (Indein) Stupa complex and explore the vibrant local markets. Day 7: Depart Inle Lake.

Food and Drink Scene of Inle Lake

Must-Try Dishes

  1. Shan Noodles - A popular dish made from rice noodles and mixed with pork or chicken curry, deep-fried tofu, and spicy tomato sauce.
  2. Inle Wetland Tomatoes - These tomatoes have a unique tangy twist to them, unique to Inle Lake. Often used in salads.
  3. Burmese Tea - A tea delicacy made popular my Myanmar’s hold on tea production, can be enjoyed as a part of a meal, or alone.
  4. Shan-style Tofu Noodle Soup - A soup made with delicate tofu and vegetables, often served with Shan noodles.

Local Beverages

  1. Shan Rice Wine - If you are looking for an adventure into the local culture, this sweet wine made from fermented rice is a must-try., often served with local meals.
  2. Mandalay Beer - One of the most popular Myanmar beers, made with local hops and barley, refreshing, cool, and perfect for a warm sunny day!
  3. Lahpet - A popular Myanmar Tea that impresses the taste bud with its rich, complex flavor.

Where to Stay in Inle Lake: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  1. Sofitel Inle Lake Myat Min - A lavish resort situated on the shore of the lake offering breathtaking views, luxury amenities, and superb service.
  2. Inle Princess Resort - A beautiful resort that exemplifies elegance and style, nestled between rice paddies and the lake.
  3. Paramount Inle Resort- A modest resort that offers eco-friendly sustainability in both resort design and service.
  4. Joy Hotel - A quaint hotel with excellent service at a budget-friendly cost.

What to Buy in Inle Lake: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  1. Shan Traditional Dresses and Clothes - The intricately designed and brightly colored traditional garments are a great souvenir from Inle Lake.
  2. Locally Produced Lotus and Silk Shirts - Soft and delicate, these garments highlight the intricate art of Myanmar weaving.
  3. Artworks and Handicrafts - Inle Lake is renowned for its skilled artisans who create beautiful handicrafts, such as lacquerware, engraved silver bowls, and silk textiles.
  4. Shan Chili Powder - A spicy flavoring that is unique to the Shan region of Myanmar, this spice mix is great for foodies who want to add extra zest to their dishes.

Local Culture and Customs of Inle Lake

The Intha people have a rich and unique culture that is different from the rest of the country. A few customs that you may want to learn about before your visit include:

  1. Don’t touch people on the head. It is considered disrespectful.
  2. Remove shoes before entering homes and temples.
  3. Dress modestly when visiting religious sites.
  4. Myanmar culture is generally one of respect, so extending common courtesies like “hello” and “thank you” is generally a good practice in any social setting!

Best Time to Visit Inle Lake

The ideal time to visit Inle Lake is the end of the rainy season (October-February) when the weather is cool, and the landscapes are lush. This is also high season, so expect more tourists. The summer months (March-June) can be hot and humid, but air-conditioned accommodations and fans are widely available. Monsoon season (July-September) is not an ideal time to visit due to heavy rains.

Do’s and Don’ts of Inle Lake

Do:

  1. Respect the local customs and traditions.
  2. Try the local cuisine and beverages.
  3. Book a boat tour and visit the lake’s cultural and natural attractions.
  4. Take your time to explore the region’s rich culture and breathtaking landscapes.

Don’t:

  1. Engage in illegal activities or disrespect the local way of life.
  2. Leave litter or damage the natural surroundings.
  3. Touch religious artifacts in temples and pagodas without permission.
  4. Expect to haggle on prices in supermarkets, restaurants, or other businesses, as this custom is not widespread here.

Safety and Health

Safety in Inle Lake

Inle Lake is generally a safe destination for tourists. However, use common sense when traveling and avoid walking alone at night, particularly in less populated areas.

Health in Inle Lake

Before traveling to Inle Lake, it is important to check with your doctor and have all necessary vaccinations. Inle Lake has several hospitals that can provide medical care. Many visitors are advised not to drink tap water, and some prefer purchasing mineral water. Additionally, ensure that you have packed all necessary ethical medicines as required.

Getting to Inle Lake: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Inle Lake

The nearest airport is Heho Airport, which is approximately 40 minutes from Inle Lake by car.

Bus Stations Near Inle Lake

There are several bus stations near Inle Lake, including the Shwenyaung Bus Station, which is the most commonly used one for visitors.

Other Options

Train and taxi services are available as well. Most hotels, hostels, and guesthouses offer pick-up services from the airport or bus station.

How to Get Around in Inle Lake

The best way to explore Inle Lake is by boat. Most hotels, hostels, and guesthouses in the area offer boat tours. However, cycling, motorbiking, and walking are great options to explore the surrounding countryside and enjoy the stunning views of the lake and nearby hills. Taxi and tuk-tuk services are readily available, making it easy to move around at any time.

Where to Go Next from Inle Lake: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  1. Bagan - Famous for its ancient temples, ruins, and breathtaking views of the Irrawaddy River, Bagan is a must-visit destination near Inle Lake located 250km away.
  2. Mandalay - A bustling city renowned for its royal palaces, pagodas, and scenic hilltop views, just about 250km from Inle Lake.
  3. Naypyitaw - Myanmar’s administrative capital is also worth a visit with its impressive architecture, golden pagodas, and lush green parks and lakes - just 300km north of Inle Lake.

Conclusion

Inle Lake is a magical destination filled with natural and cultural wonders. The lake offers a unique and peaceful atmospheres you won’t find anywhere else. With a plethora of attractions and activities, it is perfect for all kinds of travelers. Plan your trip today and embark on an unforgettable journey to one of the most impressive places in Southeast Asia.
