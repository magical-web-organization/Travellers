Introduction to Loikaw

Located in Eastern Myanmar, Loikaw is a hidden gem that’s gaining popularity among travelers. It is the capital city of Kayah state, and it’s known for its unique culture and traditions. The area is home to diverse ethnic groups such as Kayan, Kayah, and Shan. Loikaw is surrounded by beautiful mountains and boasts a variety of natural attractions, cultural sites, and adventure activities that will make your trip unforgettable.

List of Places to Visit in Loikaw

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Loikaw boasts a rich cultural heritage that is reflected in the various museums and historical places. Some of the notable cultural attractions include:

  • Kayah State Museum: This museum provides an insight into the history and culture of Kayah state.
  • Manuha Pagoda: This pagoda dates back to the 14th century and is known for its unique architecture.
  • Thiri Mingalar Taung Kwe Pagoda: This temple on top of a hill offers panoramic views of the city.
  • Demawso Market: This bustling market is a great place to interact with the locals and try local delicacies.

Natural Attractions of Loikaw: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Loikaw is known for its stunning natural beauty. The area offers a variety of outdoor activities and scenic views that will leave you mesmerized. Some of the must-visit natural attractions include:

  • Taung Kwe Pagoda: This pagoda is located on top of a hill and offers stunning views of the surrounding mountains.
  • Pan Pet Village: This village is known for its scenic rice paddies and is a great place to take a relaxing walk.
  • Loikaw Lake: A picturesque lake in the center of the city that is a popular spot for picnicking.
  • Hte Nget Pyaw Taw Pagoda: A pagoda that sits on top of a mountain with stunning views of the city.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Loikaw

Loikaw is a great destination for adventure enthusiasts. The area offers a variety of hiking and biking trails that will take you through unspoiled natural beauty. Some of the best hiking and biking routes in Loikaw include:

  • Loikaw-Mawchi Road: This road runs through the hills and offers stunning views of the surrounding countryside.
  • Hta Nee La Leh: A scenic hiking trail that takes you through the jungle and offers a glimpse into the local wildlife.
  • Demawso-Yangon Road: This road is great for a leisurely bike ride and offers panoramic views of the mountains.

Best Things to Do in Loikaw: Activities and Tours

Loikaw offers a variety of activities and tours that will give you an insight into the local culture and traditions. Some of the must-do activities in Loikaw include:

  • Visit Kayan Villages: Kayan villages are known for their long-necked women. This is a unique opportunity to interact with the locals and learn about their culture.
  • Try Local Cuisine: Loikaw is known for its delicious cuisine that is influenced by its diverse ethnic groups. Be sure to try local delicacies such as Khaung Yar, a type of rice wine, and Khao Suey, a Burmese noodle dish.
  • Attend Festivals: Loikaw hosts a variety of festivals throughout the year such as the Kayan New Year Festival and the Kayah State Day. These festivals offer an insight into the local traditions and culture.
  • Take a Boat Ride: A boat ride on the Bilu Chaung river is a great way to explore the region’s natural beauty.

Food and Drink Scene of Loikaw

Must-Try Dishes

Loikaw is known for its delicious cuisine that reflects the local culture and traditions. Some of the dishes that you must-try in Loikaw include:

  • Khaung Yar: a type of rice wine that is made from glutinous rice and is a common beverage in Kayah state.
  • Khao Suey: a Burmese noodle dish that is served with a variety of toppings such as coconut, chicken, and onions.
  • Mohinga: a type of noodle soup that is made from fish broth and is a popular breakfast dish in Myanmar.
  • Shan-style Tofu: a vegetarian dish that is made from soybeans and is a staple food in Myanmar.

Local Beverages

Apart from Khaung Yar, Loikaw offers a variety of local beverages that are worth trying. Some of the popular local beverages include:

  • Laphat: a type of tea that is famous in Myanmar and is served with condensed milk.
  • Mandalay Beer: a popular beer that is brewed in Myanmar and is available in most bars and restaurants.
  • Black Tea: a type of tea that is served with sugar and is a common beverage in Myanmar.

What to Buy in Loikaw: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Loikaw offers a range of souvenirs and local products that make for great gifts and keepsakes. Some of the popular items to buy in Loikaw include:

  • Longyi: A traditional Burmese garment that is worn by both men and women.
  • Cheroots: A type of cigar that is handmade and comes in a variety of flavors.
  • Handicrafts: Loikaw is known for its handmade products such as baskets, bags, and jewelry.
  • Tea Leaves: Myanmar is known for its tea, and Loikaw produces some of the best tea leaves in the country.

Local Culture and Customs of Loikaw

Loikaw is known for its unique culture and traditions, which are reflected in the local festivals, markets, and food. The area is home to diverse ethnic groups, and each group has its own customs and traditions. Visitors are advised to respect the local customs, dress modestly, and seek permission before taking photographs of the locals.

Best Time to Visit Loikaw

The best time to visit Loikaw is between November and February when the weather is pleasant and cool. The area experiences a monsoon season from June to September, which can make travel difficult. March to May can be hot, with temperatures reaching up to 40°C.

Do’s and Don’ts of Loikaw

Do:

  • Respect the local customs and traditions.
  • Dress modestly.
  • Seek permission before taking photographs of the locals.
  • Try the local cuisine.
  • Learn basic Burmese phrases.

Don’t:

  • Touch people’s heads.
  • Point your feet towards people or Buddha statues.
  • Disrespect the local traditions and culture.
  • Take photographs of the military or government buildings.

Safety and Health

Safety in Loikaw

Loikaw is a relatively safe destination for travelers. However, visitors are advised to take precautions, such as avoiding crowded areas, not carrying high-value items, and keeping their passports and documents safe.

Health in Loikaw

Visitors are advised to get vaccinated for hepatitis A and typhoid before traveling to Loikaw. Malaria is prevalent in some areas, and visitors are advised to take preventive measures such as using mosquito nets and repellent.

Getting to Loikaw: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Loikaw

The nearest airport to Loikaw is the Heho airport, which is located about 206 kilometers away. Another option is the Naypyitaw International airport, which is located about 250 kilometers away.

Bus Stations Near Loikaw

Loikaw is well-connected by buses to neighboring cities such as Yangon, Mandalay, and Bagan. Some of the popular bus companies include Elite Express, JJ Express, and Shwe Mandalar.

Other Options

Private taxis and cars are available for hire in Loikaw. Visitors can also rent motorbikes and bicycles to explore the area.

How to Get Around in Loikaw

Loikaw is a small town that can easily be explored on foot. Visitors can also use motorcycles and bicycles to explore the area. Taxis and private cars are also available for hire.

Where to Go Next from Loikaw: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Some of the nearby destinations and excursions from Loikaw include:

  • Inle Lake: A picturesque lake that is known for its floating villages, markets, and temples.
  • Kalaw: A scenic hill station that offers a range of outdoor activities such as hiking, trekking, and cycling.
  • Mrauk U: An ancient city that is home to several temples, pagodas, and historical sites.

Conclusion

Loikaw is a hidden gem that offers a unique blend of culture, adventure, and natural beauty. The town is surrounded by stunning mountains and boasts a variety of cultural and natural attractions that will leave you mesmerized. Whether you’re a culture enthusiast, an adventure lover, or a nature lover, Loikaw has something for everyone.
