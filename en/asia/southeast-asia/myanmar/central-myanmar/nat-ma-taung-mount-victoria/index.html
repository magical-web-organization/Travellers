Introduction to Nat Ma Taung (Mount Victoria)

Nat Ma Taung, also known as Mount Victoria, is a designated protected area located in Chin State, Myanmar. With an altitude of 3,053 meters above sea level, it is the highest peak in the Chin Hills and one of the top destinations for outdoor enthusiasts in Myanmar. This mountain is surrounded by a beautiful series of forests, which are home to diverse species of flora and fauna that provide more to explore beyond the main peak of the mountain.

List of Places to Visit in Nat Ma Taung (Mount Victoria)

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Nat Ma Taung has a rich culture and history, making it an excellent destination to immerse yourself in the local way of life in visiting any of the following historical places:

  • Htan Pyi Daw

Situated at the base of the mountain, Htan Pyi Daw is a historic village known for its traditional bamboo houses and craft workshops. It is also a popular venue for the Chin Cultural Festival.

  • Kanpetlet Lay

Kanpetlet Lay is an old British Hill station located at the base of Nat Ma Taung. Here, you can visit the historic British Clock Tower and enjoy a walk through the charming town center.

Natural Attractions of Nat Ma Taung (Mount Victoria): Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Nat Ma Taung has a range of breathtaking natural attractions that offer a range of outdoor activities for the adventurous travelers, including:

  • Nat Ma Taung National Park

Nat Ma Taung National Park covers an area of 722 square kilometers that are home to diverse flora and fauna, including pines, rhododendron, and the rare and endangered birds. Visitors can explore the park’s trails and waterfalls, go birdwatching, and experience the unique biodiversity of Chin State.

  • Mount Victoria

The main attraction of Nat Ma Taung is the mountain peak, where visitors can trek to the top of the mountain, enjoy the stunning panoramic views from the summit, and experience the wild beauty of the surrounding landscape. Nature lovers can also explore the various hiking trails around the mountain as you take in the breathtaking scenery of the Chin Hills.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Nat Ma Taung (Mount Victoria)

Nat Ma Taung offers a range of hiking trails through its stunning scenery for those passionate about trekking. Experienced travellers are advised to engage with a guide or join one of the trekking groups to enjoy safe and memorable expeditions.

Khaw Nu Soum to Milepost 14

This is one of the most popular trekking routes in Chin State. The trail starts at Khaw Nu Soum Village and passes through a series of forested hills before reaching its destination.

Mt. Rungkhwa

This single-day hike along Mt. Rungkhwa offers a stunning panoramic view of the mountain range.

Best Things to Do in Nat Ma Taung (Mount Victoria): Activities and Tours

Nat Ma Taung offers some fun activities and tours that visitors can indulge in which will make their trip more memorable, including:

  • Chin Cultural Festival

The Chin Cultural Festival is held annually in February and takes place in Htan Pyi Daw. Here, visitors can experience traditional Chin dance performances, local cuisines, crafts, and games.

  • Wildlife Conservation

Nat Ma Taung extends several activities that nature lovers can engage to conserve the endangered species. Activities such as bird watching and butterfly discovery tours are common.

Food and Drink Scene of Nat Ma Taung (Mount Victoria)

Must-Try Dishes

Nat Ma Taung cuisine has heavy influences from the neighbouring regions and countries. One of the popular dishes is:

  • Kalay Pauk

This traditional dish is a rice substitute cooked with fried rice noodles, tomatoes, and peanuts. It is flavored with lemon juice and served with a choice of meat or vegetable

Local Beverages

One of the popular local drinks is:

  • Myanmar Beer

The crisp, refreshing taste of Myanmar beer has made it the most popular alcoholic drink in the region and a refreshing beverage when you need to recharge after a long day of adventure and activities.

Where to Stay in Nat Ma Taung (Mount Victoria): Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Nat Ma Taung provides several options for accommodation according to your preferences:

  • Mountain Oasis Resort

Mountain Oasis Resort is the best all-round option. It’s reasonably priced and is located a few minutes drive from Mt. Victoria National Park.

  • Pinewood Guesthouse

Pinewood Guesthouse provides overnight stays in traditional bamboo houses for a more enjoyable cultural experience.

What to Buy in Nat Ma Taung (Mount Victoria): Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Nat Ma Taung offers several affordable and unique souvenirs to bring back with you as a reminder of your amazing trip. You can purchase:

  • Traditional Shan Backpacks

These beautifully designed bags are both unique and practical and are the ideal souvenir for practical backpack enthusiasts.

Local Culture and Customs of Nat Ma Taung (Mount Victoria)

Most of the residents in the region live in small villages in the mountains. Their culture and practices reflect a very traditional way of life with significant links to their local heritage and customs. Politeness and modesty are an essential aspect of their daily lives, so visitors are advised to respect this when they visit the various villages.

Best Time to Visit Nat Ma Taung (Mount Victoria)

The best time to visit Nat Ma Taung is from November to January when the weather is mild, and there is lesser rainfall. It is also the ideal time to trek and explore the beautiful surrounds. February to April is also an option, but it is generally hotter, though still suitable for travel.

Do’s and Don’ts of Nat Ma Taung (Mount Victoria)

Do:

  • Dress modestly

  • Respect the local customs and way of life

  • Take a local guide if going hiking or trekking

Don’t:

  • Harm the local flora and fauna

  • Disrespect the local customs and traditions

  • Engage in any illegal activities

Safety and Health

Safety in Nat Ma Taung (Mount Victoria)

Nat Ma Taung is a very safe place to visit. However, visitors are recommended to take the necessary precautions to stay safe, such as:

  • Always hike in groups

  • Observe park regulations

  • Stay within marked sections of the park

Health in Nat Ma Taung (Mount Victoria)

Visitors are recommended to speak to their doctors before traveling to Nat Ma Taung to ensure they are up to date with their vaccination requirements.

Getting to Nat Ma Taung (Mount Victoria): Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Nat Ma Taung (Mount Victoria)

The closest international airport is Yangon International Airport, and it is approximately a 40-hour bus drive to Mount Victoria.

Bus Stations Near Nat Ma Taung (Mount Victoria)

Buses are available from Yangon, Mandalay and Bagan to Kanpetlet Township daily. It is advisable to book well in advance during peak times.

Other Options

Transport options such as private cars and taxis are available but can be quite expensive.

How to Get Around in Nat Ma Taung (Mount Victoria)

Visitors have a range of ways to explore the beauty of Nat Ma Taung, including hiring a car, riding a scooter, or renting a bicycle.

Where to Go Next from Nat Ma Taung (Mount Victoria): Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Bagan

Less than 220 miles away, Bagan, also known as the land of thousand temples, is one of Myanmar’s most popular tourist destinations, known for its ancient architecture.

  • Mandalay

Mandalay, about 187.1 miles away from Nat Ma Taung, is a cultural center featuring ancient temples and pagodas and the famous Irrawaddy River.

Conclusion

Nat Ma Taung (Mount Victoria) offers a rich cultural heritage and stunning natural beauty, ensuring all visitors will have a range of exciting experiences that will last a lifetime. With its unique culture, breathtaking scenery and outdoor activities, Nat Ma Taung sets itself apart as an ideal destination for adventurers and nature enthusiasts.
