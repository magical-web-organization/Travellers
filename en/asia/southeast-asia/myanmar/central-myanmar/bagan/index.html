Introduction to Bagan

Bagan, located in central Myanmar, is a city that is renowned for its historical significance and beauty. The city is home to the largest concentration of Buddhist temples, pagodas, and ruins in the world. Bagan was once the capital of the ancient Kingdom of Pagan, and it flourished between the 9th and 13th centuries. Today, Bagan’s grand temples and pagodas continue to attract tourists from all over the world, making it a must-visit destination when traveling to Myanmar.

List of Places to Visit in Bagan

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Ananda Temple: One of the most significant and well-preserved temples in Bagan. Built in the 12th century during the reign of King Kyanzittha, this temple houses four standing Buddha statues made of teak wood.
  • Bagan Archaeological Museum: This museum showcases the history of the Bagan kingdom and its temples. It is a great place to learn about the architecture and art of the region.
  • Dhammayangyi Temple: One of the largest and most massive temples in Bagan, this temple is known for its mysterious history and hidden passageways
  • Shwezigon Pagoda: A magnificent structure that is considered to be the prototype for Burmese stupas. Built in the 11th century, the pagoda is adorned with beautiful and intricate carvings.

Natural Attractions of Bagan: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Mount Popa: A volcanic mountain that is believed to be the home of Myanmar’s most powerful spirits. The mountain offers a great opportunity for hiking and enjoying stunning panoramic views of Bagan and the surrounding countryside.
  • Irrawaddy River: This river runs through Bagan and is a great place to take a boat tour or indulge in watersports like kayaking and fishing.
  • Bagan Viewing Tower: For a breathtaking bird’s eye view of the city, head to the Bagan Viewing Tower. The tower stands at 20 stories and offers exceptional views of the stunning Bagan skyline.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Bagan

  • Temple Circuit: A popular route that covers most of the famous temples, starting from Nyaung-U and passing through Old Bagan and New Bagan. This route is suitable for biking and walking.
  • Dry Zone Trails: For a more adventurous and off-the-beaten-track experience, head to the Dry Zone trails. This route consists of rural tracks, stupas, and villages, giving riders a chance to explore the countryside and meet locals.

Best Things to Do in Bagan: Activities and Tours

  • Take a hot air balloon ride: This is a must-do when in Bagan, as it provides a mesmerizing view of the city’s stupas and pagodas.
  • Watch the sunset/sunrise over the temples: Enjoy a peaceful and surreal experience as the sun rises or sets over the grand Bagan temples.
  • Join a cooking class: Dive into the local cuisine by taking a traditional Burmese cooking class.

Food and Drink Scene of Bagan

Must-Try Dishes

  • Mohinga: A traditional Burmese breakfast dish consisting of rice noodles served in fish broth.
  • Shan-style noodles: Thin rice noodles served with pork or chicken broth and mixed with fresh vegetables and herbs.
  • Bagan-style tofu: A delicious organic tofu dish, which is famous for its fresh taste and served with different sauces.

Local Beverages

  • Mandalay beer: The most popular and widely consumed beer in Myanmar. This beer is crisp and refreshing, and perfect to accompany spicy Burmese dishes.
  • Palm wine: A mild alcoholic drink made by fermenting sap from palm trees. It is a popular drink in Myanmar and is enjoyed by locals and tourists alike.

Where to Stay in Bagan: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Bagan Lodge: A luxurious hotel that offers excellent amenities and services in a serene and peaceful environment.
  • Ostello Bello: A popular hostel that offers comfortable accommodations and a range of activities to engage guests.
  • Zfreeti Hotel: A budget-friendly hotel located in the heart of Nyaung-U, which is perfect for travelers on a tight budget.

What to Buy in Bagan: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Wooden crafts: Bagan is well-known for its exquisite wooden carvings, including sculptures and furniture pieces.
  • Lotus silk: This is a unique silk fabric that is handmade by local artisans and dyed with natural colors.
  • Lacquerware: A traditional handicraft that is popular in Myanmar. Lacquerware items are made of wood and are coated with a layer of lacquer before being decorated with intricate patterns.

Local Culture and Customs of Bagan

  • Dress modestly: Myanmar is a conservative country, and it is essential to dress appropriately when visiting temples and other religious sites.
  • Respect the Buddha: When entering a temple or pagoda, remove your shoes, and do not touch Buddha statues or make loud noises.
  • Greet with hands together: The traditional greeting in Myanmar is to put your hands together in prayer position and bow slightly.

Best Time to Visit Bagan

The best time to visit Bagan is from November to February when the weather is dry, and the temperature is mild. March to May is considered to be the peak season, but it can be extremely hot and humid during this time. From June to October, the weather is wet, and many tourist sites may be closed off due to flooding.

Do’s and Don’ts of Bagan

Do:

  • Do try local cuisine and beverages.
  • Do be respectful of local culture and customs.
  • Do take your shoes off when entering a temple or religious site.

Don’t:

  • Don’t touch Buddha statues or make loud noises in religious sites.
  • Don’t litter or leave trash in the streets or tourist sites.
  • Don’t dress immodestly when visiting temples or religious sites.

Safety and Health

Safety in Bagan

Bagan is generally a safe destination for tourists. However, it is always essential to be aware of your surroundings and belongings. Avoid walking alone at night and taking unregistered taxis.

Health in Bagan

Make sure to get travel vaccinations and take precautions against mosquito bites to prevent diseases such as dengue fever and malaria. It is also important to stay hydrated and drink bottled or boiled water to avoid water-borne illnesses.

Getting to Bagan: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Bagan

The nearest airports to Bagan are the Nyaung-U Airport and the Mandalay International Airport. Both airports have direct flights from major cities in Asia, including Bangkok, Singapore, and Kuala Lumpur.

Bus Stations Near Bagan

Buses are a cheap and convenient way to get to Bagan. The nearest bus stations are in Nyaung-U and Mandalay.

Other Options

Train travel is another popular way to get to Bagan, with daily trains running from Yangon and Mandalay. Private taxis and cars are also available for hire.

How to Get Around in Bagan

The best way to get around in Bagan is by bike or e-bike, which can be rented from various shops and hotels. Taxis and private cars are also available for hire.

Where to Go Next from Bagan: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Mandalay: A city famed for its royal palaces and ancient cultural traditions.
  • Inle Lake: A serene lake located in the heart of Myanmar’s Shan State, which is famous for its floating gardens, stilted villages, and leg-rowing fishermen.
  • Yangon: Myanmar’s largest city and former capital, which boasts a mix of colonial and traditional architecture and a lively street food scene.

Conclusion

A visit to Bagan is a journey through history and culture. With its stunning temples, beautiful landscapes, and delicious cuisine, there is something for everyone in this remarkable city. Whether you’re an adventurous traveler or a history buff, Bagan is sure to leave you enchanted and wanting more.
