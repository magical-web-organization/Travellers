    • Introduction to Amarapura

    Amarapura, a former capital of Myanmar, is a small township located in the Mandalay Region. Known for its ancient palaces, magnificent pagodas, and scenic beauty, Amarapura is a must-visit destination for anyone looking to explore Myanmar’s rich history and culture. The city gets its name from the Sanskrit ‘Amarapura’ which means the ‘City of Immortal’. During the Konbaung Dynasty, Amarapura was the royal capital of Myanmar, and today it attracts tourists from all over the world.

    List of Places to Visit in Amarapura

    Amarapura is full of historical and cultural attractions, each with its own unique story to tell. Here is our list of places to visit in Amarapura:

    Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

    • U Bein Bridge
    • Pahtodawgyi Pagoda
    • Mahagandayon Monastery
    • Bagaya Monastery

    U Bein Bridge is a UNESCO world heritage site that stands as a spectacular wooden bridge, 1.2-kilometers long, built in 1782. The bridge crosses the Taungthaman Lake and is the longest teakwood bridge in the world. Walk the bridge and capture the breathtaking reflection of the sunset on the Taungthaman Lake making it picture-perfect for your Instagram.

    Pahtodawgyi Pagoda is a breath-taking temple with exciting architectural designs. Built-in the year 1820, the temple served as the royal palace for King Bagyidaw at one point. The white and gold structure of the temple reflects the tradition of Burmese architecture and is a sacred site worshipped by the locals.

    Mahagandayon Monastery is an extraordinary religious establishment where the monks apply the teachings of Buddha to their daily routine. The monastery was founded in 1914 and is home to around 2,000 monks who follow the strict ethical discipline of the Buddhist teaching.

    Bagaya Monastery is renowned for the intricate wood carvings that adorn its walls and ceilings that date back to the 18th century. The main hall features a Buddha statue with thousands of intricate carvings surrounding it from top to bottom.

    Natural Attractions of Amarapura: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

    • Taungthaman Lake
    • Mahamuni Pagoda
    • Sagaing Hill
    • Ava

    Taungthaman Lake provides an idyllic waterfront retreat for visitors looking for a serene spot to relax and enjoy nature. A two-hour boat ride around the lake is a popular mode of transportation for locals and tourists alike.

    Mahamuni Pagoda is one of the oldest and most revered temples in Amarapura, which houses one of the five revered Buddha images in Myanmar, made of pure gold. The temple also features beautiful frescoes painted in the traditional Burmese style.

    Sagaing Hill offers a panoramic view of the Irrawaddy River and the surrounding countryside. The hill is home to numerous temples and pagodas, and the tranquil setting makes it a perfect place for meditation.

    Ava Located on an island formed by a loop of the Irrawaddy River, Ava was the seat of the Konbaung Dynasty in Myanmar. Visitors can explore the ancient ruins on bicycle or horsecart tours.

    Best Things to Do in Amarapura: Activities and Tours

    Here are some of the greatest things to do in Amarapura:

    • Take a sunset boat tour of the Taungthaman Lake.
    • Experience an early morning alms offering at Mahagandayon Monastery.
    • Visit a traditional silk-weaving workshop.
    • Take a horse cart tour around Ava.
    • Enjoy a traditional Burmese dance performance at a local theater.

    Local Culture and Customs of Amarapura

    The locals in Amarapura are predominantly devout Buddhists, and visitors should be respectful of local culture. It is customary to remove shoes before entering pagodas and temples.

    Best Time to Visit Amarapura

    The best time to visit Amarapura is from November to February when the weather is dry and mild. However, Amarapura can be visited year-round.

    Do’s and Don’ts of Amarapura

    Do:

    • Dress modestly when visiting religious sites.
    • Respect local customs and traditions.
    • Remove your shoes before entering temples and pagodas.
    • Be mindful when taking photographs of locals or religious sites.

    Don’t:

    • Inappropriately touch or photograph monks or nuns.
    • Disrespect or damage ancient monuments.
    • Wear revealing clothing when visiting religious sites.

    Safety and Health

    Safety in Amarapura

    Amarapura is a relatively safe city for travelers, but visitors should be aware of their surroundings and keep an eye on their valuables.

    Health in Amarapura

    Travelers to Amarapura are advised to drink bottled water and avoid consuming street food. It is always recommended to consult with a healthcare professional before traveling to ensure you have the necessary vaccinations and take precautions against disease-carrying insects.

    Getting to Amarapura: Transportation Options and Logistics

    Amarapura is easily accessible from Mandalay and other major cities in Myanmar.

    Airports Near Amarapura

    Mandalay International Airport is the nearest airport, located just 30 kilometers away from Amarapura.

    Bus Stations Near Amarapura

    Several bus stations in Mandalay offer direct services to Amarapura, including Chan Mya Shwe Pyi Highway Bus Station, Taung Myint Gyi Express Highway Bus Station, and Aung Mingalar Highway Bus Station.

    Other Options

    Visitors can also reach Amarapura by taxi and private car.

    How to Get Around in Amarapura

    The best way to get around Amarapura is by bicycle or motorbike, which can be rented from local shops. Taxis and horse-carts are also available for hire.

    Amarapura is a fascinating destination that offers travelers a glimpse into Myanmar’s rich cultural heritage and natural beauty. From exploring ancient temples and pagodas to hiking and biking through beautiful landscapes, Amarapura will leave you with cherished memories that last a lifetime.
