Introduction to Northern Myanmar

Northern Myanmar, also known as Upper Myanmar, is a region filled with breathtaking landscapes, ancient architecture, and unique cultural experiences. It is a less-traveled area of the country that promises an authentic, off-the-beaten-path adventure to travelers looking to explore beyond tourist hotspots.

Local Culture and Customs of Northern Myanmar

  • Buddhism is the primary religion in Myanmar, and visitors should respect religious practices.
  • Remove footwear before entering religious monuments or any homes.
  • Dress modestly, particularly women. Sleeveless tops and shorts may be seen as disrespectful.
  • Show deference to monks and nuns, and do not touch a monk if you are not one.

Best Time to Visit Northern Myanmar

The best time to visit Northern Myanmar is between November and February, when the weather is dry and cool.

Do’s and Don’ts of Northern Myanmar

Do:

  • Learn some Burmese words.
  • Wear sunscreen and a hat when trekking.
  • Stay hydrated.
  • Try local street food.

Don’t:

  • Wear shoes in any religious buildings or homes.
  • Touch anyone’s head, as it is considered sacred.
  • Disrespect Buddhist culture.

Safety and Health

Safety in Northern Myanmar

Northern Myanmar is a safe place for travelers, with low levels of crime or violence. However, it is still important to take precautions such as avoiding isolated areas during nighttime.

Health in Northern Myanmar

Travel vaccinations are recommended before visiting Myanmar. Mosquito-borne diseases such as dengue fever and malaria are a risk, so use insect repellent, and sleep under mosquito nets where possible.

Getting to Northern Myanmar: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Northern Myanmar

  • Mandalay International Airport
  • Heho Airport
  • Putao Airport

Bus Stations Near Northern Myanmar

  • Mandalay Bus Station
  • Lashio Bus Station
  • Taunggyi Bus Station

Other Options

  • Train travel: the Mandalay-Myitkyina rail route.
  • Private car hires: available from Mandalay or Yangon.

How to Get Around in Northern Myanmar

  • Taxis or private cars.
  • Motorbikes for hire.
  • Local buses and shared taxis.

Where to Go Next from Northern Myanmar: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Yangon: the largest city in Myanmar and home to the incredible Shwedagon Pagoda.
  • Inle Lake: a famous scenic destination with floating gardens, freshwater fish and traditional village life.
  • Hoi An: a charming town in Vietnam, about an hour by flight.

Conclusion

Northern Myanmar’s unspoiled nature and vibrant culture provide adventure and tranquility in equal measure. With this comprehensive travel guide, you’ll be well-equipped to discover all the wonders of this amazing region.
