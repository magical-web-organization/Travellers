Introduction to Myitkyina

Myitkyina is a city located in Kachin State, Northern Myanmar, bordering the countries of India and China. It is renowned for its rural landscapes, natural attractions, and rich cultural heritage. Visitors who wish to experience authentic Myanmar life should consider making Myitkyina their next travel destination.

List of Places to Visit in Myitkyina

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Kachin Missionary Museum: The museum showcases the history of the Kachin people and their relationship with Christian missionaries.

  • Fortified City Moat: A historic moat surrounding the inner city of Myitkyina built during World War II.

Natural Attractions of Myitkyina: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Myit Sone: The confluence of the Maykha and Malikha rivers, which form the Ayeyarwady River, offers stunning views and an opportunity to admire the power of the natural world.

  • Indawgyi Lake: The largest lake in Myanmar, it is a bird-watching paradise and a great location for kayaking and canoeing.

  • Putao: A scenic town surrounded by mountains, it is a starting point for trekking tours around the nearby National Parks and nature reserves.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Myitkyina

  • Malikha River Trail: Experience the beauty of the Malikha River as you hike or bike through lush forests and small villages.

  • Tawadum Bum Trail: A challenging trek that leads to a hilltop offering magnificent views of the surrounding landscape.

Best Things to Do in Myitkyina: Activities and Tours

  • Mahamuni Pagoda: A major pilgrimage site in Myitkyina that features a golden statue of Buddha.

  • Kachin Manao Tea: Learn about the history and production of tea in Kachin State and enjoy tasting different brews.

Week-Long Itinerary for Myitkyina

Day Activity
Day 1 Explore the city and visit Kachin Missionary Museum and the Fortified City Moat.
Day 2 Take a boat tour on the Ayeyarwady River and visit Myit Sone.
Day 3-4 Embark on a trekking tour to the nearby National Parks and nature reserves.
Day 5 Visit Indawgyi Lake for bird-watching and kayaking.
Day 6 Participate in a tea-tasting session and visit local markets.
Day 7 Participate in a cooking class and learn about local cuisine before departing.

Food and Drink Scene of Myitkyina

Must-Try Dishes

  • Kachin-style Chili: A spicy dish with different types of meat.

  • Manao tea salad: A salad mixed with fermented tea leaves, nuts, and other ingredients.

Local Beverages

  • Kachin Beer: A locally-brewed beer made with rice and barley.

  • Kachin Manao tea: A popular tea in Kachin State that is fermented for months, giving it a distinctive smell and flavor.

Where to Stay in Myitkyina: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Myitkyina Guesthouse: A budget-friendly accommodation option.

  • Wun Zinn Hotel: A mid-range hotel with modern amenities.

  • Kachinland Hotel: A luxury hotel known for its excellent service and spacious rooms.

What to Buy in Myitkyina: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Kachin Traditional Clothing: The colorful and intricately-decorated clothing is a unique souvenir.

  • Manao Tea: Packets of the fermented tea leaves make perfect gifts for tea lovers.

Local Culture and Customs of Myitkyina

The Kachin people are known for their friendliness and hospitality towards visitors. It is customary to remove your shoes before entering homes and temples. Dress modestly and be respectful of local traditions and customs.

Best Time to Visit Myitkyina

The best time to visit Myitkyina is during the dry season, which spans from November to February. Temperatures are cool and comfortable during this time, making it perfect for outdoor activities.

Do’s and Don’ts of Myitkyina

Do:

  • Respect local customs and traditions.

  • Dress modestly, especially when visiting religious sites.

  • Try local food and tea.

Don’t:

  • Disrespect local traditions or beliefs.

  • Refuse an invitation to a meal or tea.

  • Discard trash improperly.

Safety and Health

Safety in Myitkyina

While Myitkyina is generally safe for visitors, it is important to be mindful of your surroundings and take precautions when traveling alone at night. Avoid carrying large amounts of cash and keep important documents secure.

Health in Myitkyina

Travelers should ensure that they receive all necessary immunizations and take measures to avoid mosquito bites, as malaria is prevalent in the area. Drink bottled or boiled water to avoid getting sick.

Getting to Myitkyina: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Myitkyina

  • Myitkyina Airport: The city’s main airport located seven kilometers from the city center.

Bus Stations Near Myitkyina

  • Myitkyina Bus Terminal: Located in the city center, the bus terminal offers connections to other cities in the Kachin region.

Other Options

Taxis and private cars are also available for hire.

How to Get Around in Myitkyina

Taxis and motorbikes are readily available for hire in Myitkyina. Bicycles can also be rented for a more budget-friendly mode of transportation.

Where to Go Next from Myitkyina: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Bhamo: A town located on the Ayeyarwady River known for its majestic waterfalls and historic temples.

  • Putao: A town surrounded by the Himalayan Mountains, it is the starting point for trekking tours to nearby National Parks and nature reserves.

Conclusion

Myitkyina is a beautiful and unique travel destination with a rich cultural heritage and breathtaking natural attractions. From hiking and biking to tea-tasting and bird-watching, there is something for every type of traveler to enjoy and experience in Myitkyina.
