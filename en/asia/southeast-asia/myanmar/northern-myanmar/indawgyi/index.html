Introduction to Indawgyi

Indawgyi is a hidden gem located in Myanmar’s northern region, in Southeast Asia. This scenic destination is known for its stunning natural beauty and rich cultural heritage, making it a favorite among nature enthusiasts and adventure seekers. Indawgyi is home to the country’s largest freshwater lake, surrounded by lush green forests and towering mountains. With so much to explore, this travel guide will help you plan your itinerary, and discover the best of Indawgyi.

List of Places to Visit in Indawgyi

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Indawgyi Lake Wildlife Sanctuary

The Indawgyi Lake Wildlife Sanctuary is one of the most popular tourist destinations in Indawgyi. This protected area covers an area of about 8,620 hectares, and is home to over 100 species of birds, including the rare Baer’s Pochard and the Black Eagle. The sanctuary also features various hiking trails that offer visitors stunning views of the lake and the surrounding mountains.

Shwe Myitzu Pagoda

The Shwe Myitzu Pagoda is one of the oldest and most revered temples in Indawgyi. The pagoda is located on the east banks of the Indawgyi Lake, and is said to date back to the 17th century. The temple is known for its intricate architecture, and the stunning views it offers of the lake and surrounding mountains.

Natural Attractions of Indawgyi: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Htamanthi Wildlife Sanctuary

The Htamanthi Wildlife Sanctuary is a haven for wildlife lovers. The sanctuary covers an area of around 1,500 square kilometers and is home to over 100 species of mammals, including tigers, leopards, deer, and elephants. Visitors can explore the sanctuary on guided tours and spot rare wildlife species and enjoy beautiful views of the surrounding forests and mountains.

Htamanthi Waterfall

The Htamanthi Waterfall is a breathtaking natural wonder located in the Htamanthi Wildlife Sanctuary. The waterfall is about 20 meters tall and is surrounded by lush green forests. Visitors can take a dip in the cool waters and enjoy a picnic by the falls.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Indawgyi

Hiking and Trekking Trails

Indawgyi offers some of the best hiking and trekking trails in Myanmar. The trails vary in difficulty level and duration, ranging from a few hours to multi-day treks. The most popular hiking trails in Indawgyi include the Htamanthi Wildlife Sanctuary Trail, the Shwe Myitzu Pagoda Trail, and the Indawgyi Lake Trail.

Biking Routes

Biking is an excellent way to explore the hidden gems of Indawgyi, with beautiful scenery and rugged terrain that offers a unique biking experience. The most popular biking routes include the Shwe Myitzu Pagoda Trail, which offers scenic views of the lake and the mountains, and the Htamanthi Wildlife Sanctuary Trail, which is slightly more challenging and offers its visitors a chance to spot some rare wildlife species.

Best Things to Do in Indawgyi: Activities and Tours

Boat Tours

A boat tour is the perfect way to explore the stunning Indawgyi Lake and experience the natural beauty of the area. Visitors can take a sunset boat tour to see the lake at its most serene, or a guided tour to explore the small islands on the lake and spot the rare waterbirds that call it home.

Wildlife Safaris

Indawgyi is known for its diverse wildlife, and visitors can go on guided safaris to spot native species like elephants, tigers, and deer. The Htamanthi Wildlife Sanctuary is home to some of the rarest species in the region, making it a must-visit destination for wildlife enthusiasts.

Food and Drink Scene of Indawgyi

Must-Try Dishes

Indawgyi has a rich culinary heritage, with dishes that are unique to the region. Some of the must-try dishes in Indawgyi include:

  • Mohinga – A popular breakfast dish made with rice noodles in a fish-based broth
  • Ohn No Khao Swae – A spicy coconut milk-based soup, often served with chicken or pork
  • Shan-style Noodles – Rice noodles served in a broth made with tomatoes, garlic, and soy sauce

Local Beverages

Myanmar is known for its unique local beverages that are perfect for refreshing oneself after a long day exploring Indawgyi. Some of the must-try beverages in Indawgyi include:

  • Myanmar Beer – A refreshing lager beer and one of the most popular beers in Myanmar
  • Mandalay Rum – A dark rum distilled in the Mandalay region of Myanmar
  • Palm Wine – A light and sweet alcoholic beverage made from fermented palm sap

Where to Stay in Indawgyi: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Indawgyi has many accommodation options for visitors to choose from, ranging from budget-friendly guesthouses to high-end hotels. Some of the best places to stay in Indawgyi include:

  • Indawgyi Resort – A luxurious resort with stunning views of the Indawgyi Lake
  • Adventure Garden Lodge – A budget-friendly accommodation option that offers clean and comfortable rooms
  • Indawgyi Viewpoint Eco Lodge – A unique eco-lodge that offers visitors a chance to experience the local culture and way of life.

What to Buy in Indawgyi: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Indawgyi offers its visitors many unique and locally made products, perfect for souvenirs and gifts. Some of the must-buy products in Indawgyi include:

  • Handwoven textiles – Traditional handwoven textiles, including Shan bags and shoulder bags, are popular in the region and make great souvenirs.
  • Lacquerware – Myanmar is known for its intricate and colorful lacquerware, and visitors can find many locally made pieces in Indawgyi.
  • Local crafts – Indawgyi is known for its handcrafted products, including bamboo products, pottery, and wood carvings.

Local Culture and Customs of Indawgyi

Indawgyi is home to a rich cultural heritage and unique customs that visitors can experience during their stay. Some of the customs and beliefs in Indawgyi include:

  • Buddhism is the dominant religion in the region, and visitors should respect religious customs and practices.
  • Burmese is the official language spoken in the region, with many locals speaking other minority languages.
  • Respect for elders is important in the local culture, and visitors should address them with proper honorifics and manners.

Best Time to Visit Indawgyi

The best time to visit Indawgyi is from October to February, during the dry season when the weather is mild and pleasant. The rainy season runs from June to September when heavy downpours can limit outdoor activities.

Do’s and Don’ts of Indawgyi

Do:

  • Dress modestly and appropriately when visiting religious sites.
  • Explore the local culture and customs while respecting the local way of life.
  • Carry insect repellent and sunscreen when exploring the area.

Don’t:

  • Disrespect religious sites or practices.
  • Litter or pollute the natural environment.
  • Take photos of locals or religious sites without permission.

Safety and Health

Safety in Indawgyi

Indawgyi is a relatively safe destination, but visitors should be cautious and take necessary precautions. Some safety tips for visitors include:

  • Be aware of your surroundings, especially when venturing into remote areas.
  • Don’t carry large amounts of cash or valuables when out and about.
  • When hiking or trekking, always carry enough food and water to stay nourished.

Health in Indawgyi

Visitors to Indawgyi should take necessary preventive measures to ensure good health during their stay. Some basic health tips for visitors include:

  • Invest in a high-quality water filter or bottled water to avoid getting sick from contaminated water.
  • Make sure to wear insect repellent at all times to avoid contracting mosquito-borne illnesses.
  • Carry a basic first aid kit with any necessary medications or supplies.

Getting to Indawgyi: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Indawgyi

The nearest airport to Indawgyi is Myitkyina Airport, which is about a 2-hour drive from Indawgyi. Visitors to Indawgyi can take a taxi or rent a car to get from the airport to Indawgyi.

Bus Stations Near Indawgyi

Myitkyina is the largest city near Indawgyi, and visitors can find several bus stations offering daily bus services to the region.

Other Options

Visitors can also hire a private car or a taxi to get to Indawgyi from nearby cities.

How to Get Around in Indawgyi

The best way to get around Indawgyi is by hiring a motorbike, bicycle, or car, depending on your preference for the type of activity you will engage in. Visitors can also arrange for private tours, boats, or taxis if they prefer.

Where to Go Next from Indawgyi: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Bhamo

Bhamo is a popular tourist destination located about 150 kilometers from Indawgyi. Visitors can explore the city’s vibrant culture and rich history, including the ancient city walls of Sampanago, the Bhamo Museum, and the historic Bhamo Market.

Myitkyina

Myitkyina is the capital of the Kachin state and is a bustling city located about 120 kilometers from Indawgyi. Visitors can explore the city’s heritage and cultural attractions, including the Sutaungpyae Pagoda, the Kachin Manaw Festival, and the Myitkyina Municipal Market.
