Introduction to Thanlyin

Thanlyin, also known as Syriam, is a small town located in southern Myanmar, southeast of the Yangon River. It is a port city with a rich history, where the Dutch and Portuguese first settled in the 17th century. Today, Thanlyin is a popular tourist destination, offering visitors a glimpse of its historical past and natural beauty. This comprehensive tour guide will help you plan an unforgettable trip to Thanlyin, Myanmar.

List of Places to Visit in Thanlyin

There are many places to visit in Thanlyin, from cultural and historical landmarks to natural attractions and outdoor adventures.

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

For history buffs and those interested in the local culture, Thanlyin offers several culturally significant museums and historical places.

  • Kyaik-Khauk Pagoda: One of the most revered pagodas in Myanmar, located on a hilltop offering stunning views of the town.
  • Ye-Le Pagoda: A lesser-known but equally beautiful pagoda, offering a quiet and peaceful environment with intricate designs and decorations.
  • Kyauktan Pagoda: Located on a small island in the Kyauktan Township, the pagoda is accessible by boat and features a beautiful temple surrounded by lush greenery.
  • Yele Paya: A historic ancient Buddhist temple with great architecture and a unique history.

Natural Attractions of Thanlyin: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Nature lovers will also find plenty to explore in Thanlyin, with some scenic views and outdoor activities.

  • Thanlyin Bridge: This bridge connects the town to Yangon city, offering sweeping views of the river and surrounding areas.
  • Kyaik-Hmaw-Wun Elephant Camp: A unique opportunity to get up close and personal with the majestic creatures, interact with elephants, and learn about their natural habitat.
  • Kyaik-Khauk Waterfall: A hidden gem accessible by a short hike through the forest, offering spectacular views of the waterfall and the river.
  • Yay-Le-Pagoda Wildlife Sanctuary: A great place to birdwatch and spot rare bird species, this sanctuary also houses a variety of animals and reptiles.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Thanlyin

Thanlyin is an ideal place for both hiking and biking enthusiasts. Here are some popular trekking and cycling routes to explore the city:

  • Thone-Htat-Tan Trail: This trail starts from the Kyaik-Khauk Pagoda and ends at the Thone-Htat-Tan Pagoda, offering picturesque views of the city’s rural landscapes and undulating terrain.
  • Kyauktan-Dala-Hlaingtharyar Trail: This trail takes you through scenic rural villages, paddy fields, and across the Thanlyin River to Yangon.
  • Thanlyin River Trail: This trail starts and ends at Thanlyin Bridge and offers a scenic ride along the river, providing picturesque views of the cityscape and scenic landscapes.

Best Things to Do in Thanlyin: Activities and Tours

Whether you’re interested in exploring the city’s historical landmarks, trying out new cuisine or engaging in outdoor activities, Thanlyin has something for everyone.

  • Syriam Golf Course: For golf enthusiasts, the Syriam Golf Course offers a unique golfing experience with well-manicured greens, challenging holes, and stunning views of the city.
  • Thanlyin River Cruise: Take a leisurely day trip along Thanlyin River and explore the city’s landmarks on a relaxing cruise.
  • Indigo Weaving Tour: Visit the local weaving shops and learn about the traditional art of dyeing with natural indigo.
  • Kayaking on the Thanlyin River: Rent a kayak and explore the river with stunning views of pagodas.

Week-Long Itinerary for Thanlyin

Here is a suggested itinerary for a week-long trip to Thanlyin Myanmar:

  • Day 1: Visit Kyaik-Khauk Pagoda and explore the town
  • Day 2: Hike Thone-Htat-Tan Trail and visit local markets
  • Day 3: Take a boat ride to Kyauktan Pagoda and Wildlife Sanctuary
  • Day 4: Take a day trip to Yangon City for sightseeing
  • Day 5: Visit Thanlyin Bridge and enjoy the scenic views
  • Day 6: Explore Indigo Weaving Tour and kayaking on Thanlyin River
  • Day 7: Relax and chill on the beach at the Thanlyin River side

Food and Drink Scene of Thanlyin

Thanlyin offers an array of delicious local dishes and traditional Myanmar cuisine. Here are some must-try dishes and drinks:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Mohinga: A delicious traditional breakfast soup made with rice noodles and fish broth.
  • Laphet Thoke: An iconic Myanmar salad made with pickled tea leaves, cabbage, and other vegetables.
  • Shan Noodles: A popular dish made with rice noodles, chicken, pork, and an assortment of vegetables.

Local Beverages

  • Yenwejan: A popular beverage made from sweetened tamarind juice
  • Lahpet Yeijwei: A refreshing tea served with pickled tea leaf salad (Laphet Thoke)
  • Palm juice: A sweet drink made from palm trees available at the local markets in Thanlyin.

Where to Stay in Thanlyin: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

There are several affordable and comfortable accommodations in Thanlyin, ranging from hotels to hostels and guesthouses, to suit all budget ranges.

  • Aureum Palace Hotel & Resort: A luxurious resort with spacious rooms and scenic views of the countryside.
  • Family Treasure Thanlyin Hotel: A budget-friendly hotel with cozy accommodations and modern amenities.
  • Ocean Pearl Inn – Thanlyin: A guesthouse located in the heart of the town, providing comfortable accommodation at an affordable price.

What to Buy in Thanlyin: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Thanlyin offers a wide range of local products and souvenirs that make for excellent gifts and reminders of your trip. Here are some of the best souvenirs and local products to buy:

  • Traditional Myanmar fabrics: Hand-woven fabrics made from cotton and silk, showcasing intricate designs and patterns.
  • Antique Myanmar pottery: Handmade pottery that represents the local culture and heritage.
  • Native wooden handicrafts: Unique wooden handicrafts made by local artisans that beautifully represent the country’s rich culture and heritage.

Local Culture and Customs of Thanlyin

Myanmar culture is deeply rooted in Buddhist traditions, and this is evident in Thanlyin’s local customs. Visitors are expected to dress modestly at religious places, take off their shoes before entering temples and pagodas and speak softly and politely to locals.

Best Time to Visit Thanlyin

The best time to visit Thanlyin Myanmar is during the drier months from November to February when the weather is cool and pleasant.

Do’s and Don’ts of Thanlyin

To ensure a smooth and enjoyable trip, here are some do’s and don’ts in Thanlyin Myanmar:

Do:

  • Dress conservatively while visiting religious places like temples and pagodas
  • Respect the local culture and customs
  • Take off your shoes before entering holy places such as temples and pagodas

Don’t:

  • Don’t disrespect the national flag and symbols of the country
  • Don’t engage in conversation about politics and religion with locals
  • Smoking or drinking in the religious site is strictly prohibited.

Safety and Health

Safety is a concern for travelers in any destination, and Thanlyin Myanmar is no exception.

Safety in Thanlyin

Thanlyin is relatively safe throughout the year. However, visitors are advised to take caution and avoid walking alone in dark alleys at night. Also, take care of personal belongings and valuables, an keep an eye on your belongings in crowded areas.

Health in Thanlyin

Travelers should take precautions to stay healthy while traveling to Thanlyin. It is necessary to consult a physician for vaccinations and medications specific to the country.

Getting to Thanlyin: Transportation Options and Logistics

Thanlyin is easily accessible by air, rail, and road. Here are some transportation options to reach Thanlyin.

  • By Air: The nearest airport is Yangon International Airport, which serves domestic and international flights.
  • By Rail: Visitors can take trains from Yangon Central railway station to Thanlyin
  • By Road: Thalyin can be reached by roadways, visitors can take a taxi from any location in Yangon, which is the nearest city.

Airports Near Thanlyin

  • Yangon International Airport: 15 km from the city center, a 45-minute drive, and the nearest airport to Thanlyin

Bus Stations Near Thanlyin

  • Kyauktan Bus Station: Located near Kyauktan Pagoda and serves buses to nearby towns and regions.

Other Options

  • Boat Ride: Visitors can take a boat ride from Yangon to Thanlyin, which takes approximately one hour.

How to Get Around in Thanlyin

There are few options for getting around in Thanlyin, including:

  • Taxis and cars: Rent a taxi or car to explore the city and nearby destinations.
  • Buses: Visitors can travel by local buses operated by the city’s government

Where to Go Next from Thanlyin: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

For travelers who want to take an additional trip around the area, there are many nearby destinations and excursions to visit.

  • Bago: A historic city known for its stunning pagodas and temples, located approximately two hours from Thanlyin.
  • Golden Rock: A sacred site, and popular pilgrimage destination, located approximately five hours from Thanlyin.
  • Mawlamyine: A charming town that served as the first capital of British Burma, located about four hours from Thanlyin.

Conclusion

Thanlyin Myanmar is a unique destination that offers visitors a glimpse of the country’s rich history, natural beauty and cultural heritage. With its beautiful pagodas, lush green forests, and scenic hiking and biking trails, the city has something for everyone.
