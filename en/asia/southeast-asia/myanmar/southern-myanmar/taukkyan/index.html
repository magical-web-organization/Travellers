Introduction to Taukkyan

Welcome to Taukkyan, an off-the-beaten-path village located just 20 kilometers northeast of Yangon, the largest city and former capital of Myanmar. Taukkyan is a hidden gem waiting to be discovered by adventurous travelers.

The village boasts a unique blend of historical and cultural sites, natural wonders, adventure activities, and a welcoming local community. In this comprehensive travel guide, we will help you plan your trip to Taukkyan, from the best things to do and places to visit to where to stay and eat.

So, let’s start exploring!

List of Places to Visit in Taukkyan

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  1. Taukkyan War Cemetery
  2. Shwesettaw Pagoda
  3. Taukkyan Archeological Site
  4. Lawka Chanthar Buddhist Monastery
  5. Kyauk Htat Gyi Pagoda

Taukkyan War Cemetery

The Taukkyan War Cemetery is a British Commonwealth cemetery that serves as the resting place for thousands of allied soldiers who died in Myanmar during World War II. A peaceful, serene atmosphere surrounds the cemetery, making it a solemn yet beautiful place to visit.

Shwesettaw Pagoda

The Shwesettaw Pagoda, also known as the Golden Hill, is a religious site with a rich historical and cultural significance. It is believed that the pagoda was built over 2,500 years ago. The complex features a massive Buddha statue surrounded by a serene garden and a beautiful lake that offers fantastic views of the surrounding hills.

Taukkyan Archeological Site

The Taukkyan Archeological Site is another fascinating historical landmark worth a visit. Built in the 11th century, the site is home to ancient stupas and monasteries carved from stone. It offers a glimpse into the rich history of Myanmar’s religious traditions.

Lawka Chanthar Buddhist Monastery

The Lawka Chanthar Buddhist Monastery is a beautiful and serene religious complex that features a pagoda, meditation halls, and stunning gardens. Visitors can participate in guided meditation sessions or simply enjoy the peaceful and tranquil surroundings.

Kyauk Htat Gyi Pagoda

Kyauk Htat Gyi Pagoda is another historical and cultural site with a rich history and spiritual significance. This 70-meter-long reclining Buddha is one of the largest in Myanmar and a must-visit for any traveler interested in the country’s religious heritage.

Natural Attractions of Taukkyan: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  1. Moegoke Valley
  2. Bago Mountains
  3. Ye-indwin Taung Pagoda
  4. Toe Gyi Waterfalls
  5. Twante Canal

Moegoke Valley

Moegoke Valley is a beautiful scenic and natural attraction located near Taukkyan. The valley is famous for its ruby mines; visitors can take tours around the mines and go gem hunting.

Bago Mountains

The Bago Mountains offer spectacular views of Myanmar’s countryside. The mountains are also home to a variety of flora and fauna, great for nature enthusiasts.

Ye-indwin Taung Pagoda

Ye-indwin Taung Pagoda is a popular Buddhist pilgrimage site that offers fantastic views of the surrounding hills and countryside.

Toe Gyi Waterfalls

Toe Gyi Waterfalls, also known as Hmaw Yaw Gyi Waterfalls, is a stunning natural attraction located near Taukkyan. The waterfalls are surrounded by lush green forests and offer a refreshing escape from the city.

Twante Canal

The Twante Canal is a historic and scenic waterway that connects the Yangon River with the Irrawaddy Delta. Visitors can rent boats and cruise down the canal, enjoying the breathtaking views.

Local Culture and Customs of Taukkyan

Myanmar’s culture is a blend of various ethnic and religious traditions. The people are friendly and welcoming; it is customary to greet people by saying “Mingalaba” with folded hands. Visitors are also expected to remove their shoes before entering religious sites and dress modestly.

Best Time to Visit Taukkyan

The best time to visit Taukkyan is from November to February, when the weather is pleasant, with warm sunny days and cooler nights.

Do’s and Don’ts of Taukkyan

Do:

  • Be respectful toward the local culture and customs.
  • Bargain when shopping in the markets.
  • Try local dishes and beverages.
  • Remove your shoes when entering religious sites.

Don’t:

  • Disrespect religious sites or traditions.
  • Wear revealing or inappropriate attire in public places.
  • Use your left hand to touch food or give anything to anyone, as it is considered impolite.

Safety and Health

Safety in Taukkyan

Taukkyan is generally a safe destination for travelers. However, tourists are advised to exercise caution when traveling alone at night and avoid carrying large sums of money or valuable items.

Health in Taukkyan

Travelers are recommended to receive vaccinations at least four to six weeks before their trip. It is also advisable to drink bottled water, avoid street food and raw fruits/vegetables, and take precautions against mosquito bites.

Getting to Taukkyan: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Taukkyan

The closest airport to Taukkyan is the Yangon International Airport, located about 20 kilometers southwest of the village.

Visitors can also take a taxi or rent a car to reach Taukkyan.

How to Get Around in Taukkyan

Visitors can explore Taukkyan by taxi, bicycle, or motorbike. Renting a bike or motorbike is a cost-effective and efficient way to get around the village and explore the surrounding countryside.
