Introduction to Ngwe Saung

Ngwe Saung is a beautiful beach town located in the Ayeyarwady Region of Myanmar, widely regarded as one of the best beach destinations in the country. With its picturesque white sand beaches, crystal clear waters, and stunning sunsets, Ngwe Saung is a perfect location for relaxing, enjoying outdoor activities, or experiencing the local culture.

List of Places to Visit in Ngwe Saung

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Ngwe Saung Pagoda: This beautiful pagoda is located on a hill that overlooks the town and beach. Visitors can climb to the top of the pagoda for an amazing view of the area.
  • Ngwe Saung Market: A bustling market full of local vendors selling everything from fresh seafood to handmade crafts.
  • Sanctuary of Buddha’s Tooth Relic: A beautiful Buddhist temple with a much-revered tooth relic of the Buddha.

Natural Attractions of Ngwe Saung: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • White Sand Beach: The main beach of Ngwe Saung is a pristine white sand beach, perfect for swimming and sunbathing.
  • Lover’s Island: A beautiful and secluded island with crystal-clear waters that can be reached by a short boat trip.
  • Water Sports: Visitors can enjoy activities such as snorkeling, diving, jet skiing, and parasailing.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Ngwe Saung

  • Ngwe Saung Forest Reserve: A beautiful, protected area of forest that offers hiking and mountain biking opportunities.

Best Things to Do in Ngwe Saung: Activities and Tours

  • Ngwe Saung Sunset Cruise: A relaxing and enjoyable way to experience the stunning sunset over the ocean.
  • Cooking Classes: Learn how to cook authentic Burmese dishes in a beautiful and friendly setting.
  • Day Trips: Explore nearby attractions such as the charming riverside town of Pathein and its famous umbrella workshops.

Week-Long Itinerary for Ngwe Saung

Day Activities
Day 1 Arrival, relaxation on the beach, and sunset dinner
Day 2 Scuba diving or snorkeling
Day 3 Jungle safari adventure to the Ngwe Saung Forest Reserve
Day 4 Excursion to Pathein and visit to its umbrella workshops
Day 5 Boat tour to explore surrounding islands
Day 6 Kayaking or paddleboarding on the calm waters of the beach
Day 7 Departure, shopping for souvenirs

Food and Drink Scene of Ngwe Saung

Must-Try Dishes

  • Mohinga: Myanmar’s national dish, a delicious fish-based broth served with rice noodles and other savory ingredients.
  • Tea leaf salad: A unique and flavorful salad made with fermented tea leaves, nuts, and vegetables.

Local Beverages

  • Myanmar Beer: A popular local beer with a crisp flavor that pairs well with local cuisine.
  • Palm wine: A sweet and refreshing alcoholic beverage made from the sap of the palm tree.

Where to Stay in Ngwe Saung: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Bay of Bengal Resort: A luxurious beachfront resort with private pools and villas.
  • Eskala Resort: An elegant and spacious resort with a spa, fitness center, and ocean view rooms.
  • Sea Queen Guesthouse: A comfortable and budget-friendly option with rooms that overlook the sea.

What to Buy in Ngwe Saung: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Umbrellas: Pathein is well-known for its handcrafted umbrellas, which make a great souvenir or gift to take home.
  • Hand-woven textiles: Beautiful handmade textiles, including fabrics, scarves, and shawls, are produced by local artisans.

Local Culture and Customs of Ngwe Saung

  • Dress modestly: Myanmar has conservative and modest dress customs, so it’s important to dress respectfully, especially when visiting temples or other sacred sites.
  • Remove your shoes: In many local customs, it’s customary to remove your shoes before entering homes, temples, and some restaurants.

Best Time to Visit Ngwe Saung

The best time to visit Ngwe Saung is from November to February when the weather is cooler and less humid.

Do’s and Don’ts of Ngwe Saung

Do:

  • Respect the local culture and customs.
  • Stay hydrated and bring sunscreen to protect your skin from the sun.
  • Follow sustainable tourism practices to help preserve the beauty of the area for future generations.

Don’t:

  • Take photos without permission from locals, especially when taking pictures of people in their homes or workplaces.
  • Litter or damage the local ecosystem, including the beaches and waterways.
  • Disrespect the local culture, including local customs and traditions.

Safety and Health

Safety in Ngwe Saung

Ngwe Saung is generally a safe place to visit. However, visitors should take standard safety precautions such as not walking alone at night and securing valuables while at the beach.

Health in Ngwe Saung

Visitors should be up to date on vaccinations and avoid drinking tap water. It’s also recommended to bring mosquito repellent to avoid mosquito-borne illnesses.

Getting to Ngwe Saung: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Ngwe Saung

The closest airport to Ngwe Saung is Pathein Airport, which is about 15 km away.

Bus Stations Near Ngwe Saung

Buses to Ngwe Saung depart from Yangon and Pathein regularly, with a travel time of approximately 6-7 hours.

Other Options

Taxis and private cars can also be hired for the journey from Yangon or Pathein.

How to Get Around in Ngwe Saung

The best way to get around Ngwe Saung is by foot or bicycle. Visitors can also rent motorbikes or scooters for longer trips or excursions.

Where to Go Next from Ngwe Saung: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Pathein: A charming riverside town known for its umbrella workshops and markets.
  • Chaung Tha Beach: Another popular beach destination located about an hour’s drive from Ngwe Saung.
  • Yangon: The bustling and dynamic former capital city known for its colonial architecture and vibrant culture.

Conclusion

Ngwe Saung is an idyllic beach destination that offers something for everyone, from stunning beaches, outdoor activities, cultural attractions, and delicious local cuisine. Start planning your trip to Ngwe Saung today for a truly unforgettable experience.
