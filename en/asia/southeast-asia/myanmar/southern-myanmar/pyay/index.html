Introduction to Pyay

Pyay, also known as Prome, is a charming town located in Southern Myanmar, on the banks of the Ayeyarwady River. Considered one of the country’s hidden gems, Pyay offers a wealth of cultural attractions, natural wonders, and outdoor activities for visitors to enjoy. In this guide, we’ll explore the best things to see and do in Pyay, as well as provide recommendations for accommodations, local cuisine, and transportation options.

List of Places to Visit in Pyay

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Shwesandaw Pagoda: This golden pagoda is one of Myanmar’s most famous Buddhist sites and features stunning views of the surrounding countryside.

  • Bawbawgyi Pagoda: An impressive stupa built in the fifth century and considered one of Myanmar’s oldest surviving monuments.

  • Bebe Pagoda: Another ancient stupa located on a hill that offers panoramic views of the Ayeyarwady Valley.

  • The Pyay Museum: This museum houses a collection of artifacts, antiques, and traditional clothing that provide insight into Pyay’s history and culture.

Natural Attractions of Pyay: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Akauk Taung: A forested hillside dotted with life-size images of Buddha carved into the cliff face. The site is only accessible by boat and offers stunning views of the Ayeyarwady River.

  • Htukkant Thein Temple: This underground temple, built in the seventh century, offers a unique glimpse into ancient Burmese architecture.

  • Shwedaung Forest: A scenic forest located outside of Pyay, home to a variety of wildlife and an excellent spot for hiking and bird watching.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Pyay

For outdoor enthusiasts, Pyay offers several hiking and biking routes that allow visitors to explore the natural beauty of the area. Some of the best routes include:

  • Mount Pyae Taung: A challenging hike that rewards visitors with stunning views of the town and its surroundings.

  • Ayeyarwady River Trail: A moderate hike that allows hikers to explore the beautiful Ayeyarwady River and its lush riverbanks.

Best Things to Do in Pyay: Activities and Tours

  • Ayeyarwady River Cruise: Take a scenic river boat ride around Pyay and enjoy the stunning views of the surrounding countryside.

  • Hot Air Balloon Ride: Experience Pyay from above by taking a hot air balloon ride over the town and its surroundings.

Week-Long Itinerary for Pyay

Day 1: Visit the Shwesandaw Pagoda and Bawbawgyi Pagoda

Day 2: Explore the Pyay Museum and the Bebe Pagoda

Day 3: Take a hike up Mount Pyae Taung

Day 4: Visit Htukkant Thein Temple and Shwedaung Forest Reserve

Day 5: Relax with a scenic Ayeyarwady River cruise and visit Akauk Taung

Day 6: Take a hot air balloon ride over Pyay and its surroundings

Day 7: Visit the local markets and indulge in some traditional Burmese cuisine

Food and Drink Scene of Pyay

Must-Try Dishes

  • Mohinga: A traditional Burmese rice noodle soup with a savory fish broth.

  • Shan Noodles: Flat rice noodles served with a light tomato-based sauce and an assortment of toppings.

  • Tea Leaf Salad: A popular Burmese dish made with fermented tea leaves, peanuts, sesame seeds, and other crunchy vegetables.

Local Beverages

  • Myanmar Beer: A refreshing beer made in Myanmar and available at most restaurants and bars in Pyay.

  • Palm Wine: A popular local drink made from palm tree sap that is sweet and slightly alcoholic.

Where to Stay in Pyay: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

There are several accommodation options in Pyay, ranging from budget-friendly guesthouses to upscale hotels. Some of the best options include:

  • The Royal Kaytumadi Hotel: A luxurious hotel located downtown, with comfortable rooms, a pool, and a spa.

  • The Mingalar Garden Resort: A peaceful and serene resort located outside the town, offering guests a tranquil oasis away from the hustle and bustle.

What to Buy in Pyay: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Shan Shoulder Bag: Local bags made from colorful fabrics and hand-woven by the Shan tribe.

  • Lacquerware: Traditional Burmese handicrafts made from bamboo and decorated with intricate and colorful designs.

Local Culture and Customs of Pyay

  • Dress conservatively when visiting temples and other religious sites.

  • Take your shoes off before entering any temple or pagoda.

Best Time to Visit Pyay

The best time to visit Pyay is between November and February, when the weather is at its coolest and driest. March to May are the hottest months, and June to October is the rainy season.

Do’s and Don’ts of Pyay

Do:

  • Try the local cuisine.

  • Make a point of exploring Pyay’s cultural attractions.

Don’t:

  • Wear revealing clothing when visiting religious sites.

  • Behave disrespectfully towards local customs and traditions.

Safety and Health

Safety in Pyay

Pyay is a relatively safe destination for visitors. However, it is always wise to keep your valuables secure and be aware of your surroundings, particularly at night.

Health in Pyay

Visitors to Pyay should take precautions to avoid mosquito bites, including using insect repellent and wearing long sleeves and pants during the evening. It is also advisable to drink bottled water or purify your tap water before drinking it.

Getting to Pyay: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Pyay

The nearest airport to Pyay is at Yangon, and visitors can catch a connecting flight from there or take the bus or train.

Bus Stations Near Pyay

Buses to and from Pyay run from the Yangon Bus Station, and tickets can be purchased at the station or through a tour company.

Other Options

Visitors to Pyay can also take the train from Yangon, Mandalay, or Bagan.

How to Get Around in Pyay

Getting around Pyay is relatively easy, with options including buses, taxis, and tuk-tuks. Walking or biking between sites is also a popular option, particularly outside of the town.

Where to Go Next from Pyay: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Visitors looking to continue their Myanmar adventure after Pyay may wish to explore some nearby destinations, including Magwe, Bagan, and Mandalay.

Conclusion

Pyay offers visitors a rich cultural experience, from ancient temples to natural wonders, delicious cuisine, and warm hospitality. With this comprehensive guide in hand, you’ll be ready to explore all that Pyay has to offer and create unforgettable memories.
