Introduction to Bago

Located in the Southern part of Myanmar, Bago is a charming town that is rich in history and culture. Also known as Pegu, this ancient capital of the Bamar dynasty has a unique charm that attracts travelers from all over the world.

Once a powerful city and a regional trade hub, Bago today boasts a fascinating blend of rich cultural heritage and natural beauty. This comprehensive travel guide will introduce you to the best places to visit, things to do, and local food and drink of Bago, along with tips on transportation, safety, and health.

List of Places to Visit in Bago

Here are some of the top tourist attractions to include in your Bago itinerary:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Shwemawdaw Pagoda: One of the most important Buddhist shrines in Myanmar, this 114-meter high pagoda is said to contain hairs of Buddha and is a must-visit attraction.
  • Kanbawzathadi Palace: This restored palace complex showcases the grandeur of the ancient Bamar kings. Visitors can explore the throne hall, living quarters, royal bath, and other sections of the palace to learn about the glorious past of Bago.
  • Shwethalyaung Buddha: Reputed to be one of the largest reclining Buddha statues in the world, this 55-meter long and 16-meter wide statue is a sight to behold.
  • Bago Archaeological Museum: This small museum houses a collection of artifacts and relics from the Bagan Period, showcasing the art, culture, and history of the region.
  • Mahazedi Pagoda: Built in the 16th century, this pagoda contains a sacred relic related to the Buddha and is a popular pilgrimage site.

Natural Attractions of Bago: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Moeyungyi Wetlands: A paradise for birdwatchers, this protected wetland area is home to hundreds of species of migratory birds, such as waterfowls, egrets, and herons.
  • Kyaikhtiyo Pagoda (Golden Rock): Located in the nearby town of Kyaikhto, this unique pilgrimage site teeters on the edge of a cliff and offers impressive sunset views.
  • Htauk Kyant War Cemetery: A somber reminder of the horrors of war, this cemetery is the final resting place for over 6,000 Allied soldiers who died during World War II in Myanmar.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Bago

Bago has several hiking and biking routes that offer a chance to explore the natural beauty and rural life of the region. Here are some of the best routes to consider:

  • Hiking in Moeyungyi Wetlands: This easy trail takes you through the scenic wetland area, where you can spot various bird species and wild animals.
  • Cycling to the Kyaikhtiyo Pagoda: The 70-km route to the Golden Rock pagoda is a challenging but rewarding experience, with stunning views of rural Myanmar along the way.
  • Hiking to the Kanbawzathadi Palace: This short hike (about 2 km) takes you through picturesque farmland to the restored palace complex, offering a glimpse of local life and history.

Best Things to Do in Bago: Activities and Tours

Apart from the historical sites and outdoor adventures, Bago offers many other activities and tours to keep you engaged. Here are some suggestions:

  • Attend a Traditional Puppet Show: Bago has a long tradition of puppetry, and you can catch a live performance of puppet shows that depict local legends and folklore.
  • Visit a Local Market: The bustling markets of Bago offer a chance to sample local food, buy souvenirs, and interact with the friendly locals.
  • Take a Hot Air Balloon Tour: Ballooning over the scenic countryside of Bago is an unforgettable experience that lets you see the region from a unique perspective.

Here’s a suggested itinerary for a week-long trip to Bago:

Food and Drink Scene of Bago

No trip to Bago is complete without experiencing the local food and drink scene. Here are some must-try dishes and beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Mohinga: A classic Myanmar noodle soup that includes rice noodles, fish broth, and an array of toppings such as fried garlic, boiled eggs, and coriander.
  • Ohn-no-kauk-swe: A rich curry noodle dish made with coconut milk, chicken, potatoes, and topped with crispy fried noodles.
  • Pennywort Salad (Myin Kwa Yuet Saung): A refreshing salad made with pennywort leaves, roasted peanuts, crispy garlic, and a tangy dressing.

Local Beverages

  • Myanmar Beer: The most popular beer in Myanmar, this light lager goes well with spicy food.
  • Palm Wine (Toddy): A sweet alcoholic drink made from fermented sap of palm trees, this drink is favored by many locals.
  • Green Tea: Myanmar’s version of green tea has a distinct flavor and is often served in small cups with a side of sugar or milk.

Where to Stay in Bago: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Bago has a range of accommodation options to suit all budgets and preferences. Here are some recommendations:

  • Mhaw Myaing Hotel: A luxurious hotel that offers spacious rooms, a swimming pool, and a spa, along with excellent service.
  • San Francisco Guesthouse: A friendly and affordable guesthouse that offers clean rooms, helpful staff, and serves delicious breakfast.
  • Royal Guesthouse: A budget option that offers simple and comfortable rooms, along with a convenient location near the town center.

What to Buy in Bago: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Bago is famous for its lacquerware, woodcarvings, and handmade paper umbrellas. Some other souvenir ideas include:

  • Longyi: A traditional Myanmar cloth used as a skirt or a wrap, available in various colors and patterns.
  • Teakwood Furniture: Bago is known for its high-quality teakwood furniture, including tables, chairs, and cabinets.
  • Gemstones: Myanmar is a major source of precious stones such as rubies, sapphires, and jade, so Bago is an excellent place to shop for these items.

Local Culture and Customs of Bago

Myanmar is a predominantly Buddhist country, and Burmese culture is heavily influenced by Buddhist traditions. Visitors should be respectful of local customs and practices, such as removing shoes before entering temples and pagodas, dressing modestly, and using the right hand for eating and giving and receiving items.

Best Time to Visit Bago

The best time to visit Bago is during the cool and dry season, from November to February. The weather is pleasant, and there is less rainfall. However, this is also the peak tourist season, so expect crowds and higher prices. The shoulder season from March to May and from September to October can also be a good time to visit, with fewer tourists and lower prices.

Do’s and Don’ts of Bago

Do:

  • Do respect local customs and traditions, especially when visiting religious sites.
  • Do dress modestly and avoid revealing attire.
  • Do try local food, such as mohinga and ohn-no-kauk-swe.
  • Do bring suitable clothing and footwear for outdoor activities, such as hiking and cycling.

Don’t:

  • Don’t be disrespectful to the Buddhist religion or the royal family.
  • Don’t litter or damage the natural environment.
  • Don’t photograph people without their permission.
  • Don’t give money to child beggars or engage in unethical practices.

Safety and Health

Safety in Bago

Bago is a relatively safe town, and violent crime is rare. However, visitors should be cautious of pickpockets and petty thefts, especially in crowded areas and markets. It’s also advisable to carry a copy of your passport and other important documents with you at all times.

Health in Bago

Myanmar is a developing country, and medical facilities may not meet international standards. Visitors are advised to take precautions such as drinking only bottled water, avoiding street food, and carrying a basic first-aid kit. It’s also recommended to get vaccinated for diseases such as typhoid, hepatitis A and B, and Japanese encephalitis.

Getting to Bago: Transportation Options and Logistics

The nearest international airport to Bago is in Yangon, which is about 1.5 hours away by car or bus. Domestic flights from Yangon to Bago’s nearest airport in Nay Pyi Taw are also available.

Airports Near Bago

  • Nay Pyi Taw International Airport: Located about 1.5 hours away from Bago, this airport offers domestic flights to major Myanmar cities.

Bus Stations Near Bago

  • Hlawga Bus Station: This is the main bus station in Yangon, and buses to Bago are available throughout the day.
  • Bago Bus Terminal: Located near the town center, this bus station offers connections to nearby destinations such as Kyaikhto and Mawlamyine.

Other Options

Private taxis, shared taxis, and minivans are also available for hire from Yangon to Bago.

How to Get Around in Bago

Bago is a small town, and most of the attractions can be reached on foot or by bicycle. Motorbike rentals are also available in some guesthouses and hotels. Taxis and private cars can be hired for day trips and excursions.

Where to Go Next from Bago: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Bago is a convenient base for exploring nearby attractions and destinations, such as:

  • Kyaikhto: Famous for its Golden Rock Pagoda and scenic beauty.
  • Mawlamyine: A colonial-era town that inspired Rudyard Kipling’s poem “Mandalay.”
  • Yangon: Myanmar’s largest city, which boasts a rich mix of historical, cultural, and modern attractions.

Conclusion

Bago is a hidden gem in Myanmar that offers a unique blend of history, culture, and natural beauty. This comprehensive travel guide has provided you with all the information you need to plan your trip, including the top attractions, outdoor activities, local food, and transportation options. Whether you’re a history buff, nature lover, or just looking for an off-the-beaten-path destination in Southeast Asia, Bago has something to offer for everyone.
