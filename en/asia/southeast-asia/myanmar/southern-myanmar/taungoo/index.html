Taungoo Travel Guide - A Comprehensive Tourist’s Handbook

Are you looking for an off-beat destination in Southeast Asia? Taungoo might be an excellent choice for you! Taungoo is a small town located in Southern Myanmar, nestled among the rolling hills and verdant forests. Known for its rich cultural heritage, lush greenery, and friendly people, Taungoo has become a popular destination for tourists seeking a unique experience. In this comprehensive travel guide, we’ll explore everything you need to know about Taungoo, including the best places to visit, the must-try dishes, and much more.

Introduction to Taungoo

Taungoo is a small town located in Bago Region, Southern Myanmar. Formerly known as Toungoo, it was an important city during the Burmese Empire, serving as the capital of the Toungoo Dynasty in the 16th century. Taungoo’s historical significance is still evident in its architecture, museums, and cultural sites. The town has a population of around 100,000 and is known for its vibrant markets, lively festivals, and delicious food.

List of Places to Visit in Taungoo

When it comes to tourist attractions, Taungoo has plenty to offer. From ancient temples to scenic mountain trails, Taungoo is a treat for travelers. Here are some of the must-visit places in Taungoo:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Shwesandaw Pagoda: A historical pagoda built during the Toungoo Dynasty, this religious site is a must-visit for those interested in Burmese history. In 1561, King Bayint Naung built the temple in honor of his mother. Visitors can explore the impressive pagoda’s intricate design and take in the panoramic views from the top.
  • Taungoo Palace: Situated in the heart of the town, the Taungoo Palace was once the royal residence of the Toungoo Dynasty. The palace reflects a mix of Burmese and European designs and attracts many tourists curious about Myanmar’s royal history.

Natural Attractions of Taungoo: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Than Daung Gyi: A beautiful mountain range which offers breathtaking panoramic views of the surrounding valley. It’s a perfect spot for adventure enthusiasts and is a popular place for hiking and trekking in the region’s verdant forests.
  • Kyauk Ta Lone Pagoda: Located beside the Than Daung Gyi mountain range, the pagoda’s location provides an excellent vantage point for the beautiful landscape views. Also, it’s a place full of spiritual significance, and Buddhist pilgrims frequently visit it.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Taungoo

Taungoo’s lush greenery and hilly terrain make it an excellent destination for hiking and biking enthusiasts. The following routes are recommended:

  • Than Daung Gyi: The mountain range offers a range of hiking and trekking trails to suit your fitness levels. Some trails range between 2 and 3 days miles hiking through dense forests, tranquil streams, and panoramic views from the summit.
  • Yedashe: The scenic beauty of Yedashe’s countryside is perfect for mountain biking enthusiasts. The biking route begins near the Taungoo Palace and takes you through lush green paddy fields and historical villages.

Best Things to Do in Taungoo: Activities and Tours

Taungoo has plenty to offer if you’re looking for a unique travel experience.

  • Visit Local Markets: Taungoo’s markets are famous throughout Myanmar and are full of handicrafts, local produce, and authentic Myanmar cuisine.
  • Join Festivals: Taungoo is home to several festivals, including The Thingyan Water Festival or the Golden Palace festival, which you can join if you are lucky enough to be visiting during the relevant times.
  • Take a Cooking Class: The traditional Burmese cooking style includes spices, herbs, and ingredients that create mouth-watering experience. Learn to cook Burmese cuisines like “mohinga” or “oh no kauk swe” from the locals.

Week-Long Itinerary for Taungoo

If you’re planning to visit Taungoo, here’s our recommended itinerary exploring the best of the town’s attractions:

Day 1-2 Shwesandaw Pagoda and Kyauk Ta Lone Pagoda
Day 3 Taungoo Palace and local market visit
Day 4-5 Than Daung Gyi mountain trails hiking
Day 6 Traditional Cooking Classes
Day 7 Explore nearby villages and handicrafts

Food and Drink Scene of Taungoo

Myanmar’s cuisine is rich and diverse, and Taungoo is known for its unique and flavorful delicacies. Here are some dishes and beverages you shouldn’t miss:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Mohinga: A popular Burmese breakfast dish consisting of rice noodles and fish soup containing lemongrass, pea, and garlic.
  • Oh No Kauk Swe: Delicious rice noodles served with chicken curry, coconut cream, and turmeric.
  • Kyae Oh: A noodle dish with boiled egg, quail egg, chicken or pork.

Local Beverages

  • Beer: The locally produced Yoma beer and the well-known Myanmar Beer are popular beer choices.
  • Laphet yay: A traditional Myanmar tea made with fermented tea leaves and mixed with peanuts, sesame seeds, and garlic.

Where to Stay in Taungoo: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Taungoo has a range of accommodation types, including hotels, hostels, and guesthouses. Here are some recommended options:

  • Taungoo Motel: A budget motel option that’s clean and comfortable.
  • Royal Kaytumadi Hotel: A luxury option featuring a swimming pool and spa services.
  • Oasis Hotel: A mid-range hotel option with an in-house restaurant.

What to Buy in Taungoo: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Taungoo is renowned for its handicrafts, weaving, and ceramics. Here are some recommended souvenirs to bring back home:

  • Longyi: A traditional Burmese garment made of fine cotton or silk is a perfect gift to bring back home for yourself or loved ones.
  • Shan-bag: A popular handbag made from Shan textiles is trendy among many travelers
  • Pottery: Taungoo is famous for its intricate handmade pottery designs, including traditional tea sets and decorative plates.

Local Culture and Customs of Taungoo

In Taungoo, traditional values, customs, and etiquette are highly respected. Burmese culture is heavily influenced by Theravada Buddhism. Here are some cultural practices you should keep in mind:

  • Shoes Off Policy: It’s standard to remove your shoes before entering a home, temple, or other religious sites.
  • Greeting Etiquette: Greet people with “Mingalabar,” a Myanmar greeting that expresses respect and politeness.
  • Clothing Etiquette: Dress modestly by covering your knees and shoulders and taking care not to wear clothing that resembles monk’s robes.

Best Time to Visit Taungoo

The ideal time to visit Taungoo is during the dry season, which happens from December to February. During this time, the weather is cooler, making it comfortable to explore Taungoo’s outdoor attractions. However, if you prefer to attend the various festivals, summer, which is from April to September, would be the best time to visit.

Do’s and Don’ts of Taungoo

Before planning your trip to Taungoo, be aware of the following do’s and don’ts:

Do:

  • Be respectful of the local culture and traditions.
  • Dress appropriately when visiting religious sites.
  • Try to learn a few phrases of the Burmese language.

Don’t:

  • Do not litter.
  • Avoid using your left hand for eating or giving or receiving things.
  • Do not criticize the Myanmar military regime.

Safety and Health

We wish for all traveler’s safety and prosperity while in Taungoo. Here are a few tips that can help keep you healthy and safe.

Safety in Taungoo

  • Hiking trails should be exercised with caution, always with a guide.
  • Take care of your belongings like cash, mobile, and important documents.
  • Follow travel advisories and keep your embassy in the loop.

Health in Taungoo

  • Use bottled water only and brush your teeth with it as well.
  • Use mosquito repellent, and wear long-sleeved clothes to prevent mosquito-borne diseases such as dengue fever.
  • Bring sunscreen with you to keep yourself protected from the sun.

Getting to Taungoo: Transportation Options and Logistics

Here are some of the transportation options to Taungoo, Myanmar:

Airports Near Taungoo

  • Yangon International Airport: Yangon is the primary international gateway for Myanmar. From there, visitors can take a domestic flight to the nearest airport in Bago Region named NayPyiTaw International, which takes about an hour and a half to reach Taungoo.

Bus Stations Near Taungoo

  • Aung Zeya Bus Station: The main bus station for Taungoo, operating buses to Yangon and several other destinations.

Other Options

  • The train is an option for travelers to Myanmar, but please be aware that it can be unpredictable.

How to Get Around in Taungoo

The most convenient way to explore Taungoo is through motorbikes, but it requires an international driving permit. Be aware that rush-hour traffic can cause significant delays in the town.

Where to Go Next from Taungoo: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Here are some nearby destinations and excursions that you can enjoy when traveling through Myanmar:

  • Bago: This small town is known for its magnificent temples, including the Shwemawdaw Pagoda, the tallest pagoda in Myanmar.
  • Kalaw: A picturesque town located in the Shan highlands, known for its hiking trails and ethnic villages.
  • Inle Lake: Home to the Intha people who live in stilt houses and famous for their iconic long-necked women and floating markets.

Conclusion

Taungoo is a hidden gem waiting to be discovered. This comprehensive travel guide should help you plan your itinerary, explore the town’s attractions, and experience the local culture. This vibrant town would be a perfect destination to experience the unique mix of Myanmar’s historical heritage, outdoor activities, and local cuisines. We hope this guide will inspire you to pack your bags and embark upon a Taungoo adventure.
