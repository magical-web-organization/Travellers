Introduction to Myeik

Myeik, formerly known as Mergui, is a bustling coastal city situated in southeastern Myanmar. It is known for its vibrant street markets, rich cultural heritage, and stunning natural landscapes. Myeik is also the gateway to the Myeik Archipelago, a group of over 800 islands with crystal-clear waters, white sand beaches, and pristine coral reefs, making it a popular destination for diving and snorkeling enthusiasts.

This comprehensive travel guide will provide you with all the necessary information to plan a memorable trip to Myeik. From the best things to do and places to visit to the food scene, transportation, and safety tips, discover all that Myeik has to offer.

List of Places to Visit in Myeik

Myeik offers a diverse range of attractions, from cultural treasures to natural wonders. Here are some of our top picks:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Seik Taingyi Pagoda: This pagoda is known for its iconic 54-metre-long reclining Buddha, which is said to date back to the 1900s.
  • National Museum, Myeik: Explore the history and culture of Myeik and the surrounding areas through a collection of art and artifacts.
  • Myeik Archipelago: Discover the unique culture and lifestyle of the Moken, a seafaring ethnic group that has inhabited the Myeik Archipelago for centuries.

Natural Attractions of Myeik: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Myeik Waterfall: Take a refreshing dip in the cool waters of this picturesque waterfall surrounded by lush greenery.
  • Tanintharyi Nature Reserve: Immerse yourself in the diverse flora and fauna of this protected reserve, which also houses several endemic bird species.
  • Lampi Island Marine National Park: Explore the stunning marine life of the Andaman Sea with a guided snorkeling or diving tour around Lampi Island.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Myeik

  • Myeik-Thaik Chaung: This scenic 15-km route offers breathtaking views of the surrounding hills and countryside.
  • Myeik-Potaw Gyi: This 10-km trail is perfect for mountain bikers, offering a thrilling downhill ride with stunning vistas of the Bay of Bengal.

Best Things to Do in Myeik: Activities and Tours

  • Snorkeling and Diving: Explore the rich marine life of the Myeik Archipelago with a guided snorkeling or diving tour.
  • Island-Hopping: Take a boat tour around the Myeik Archipelago and discover its secluded beaches, hidden coves, and pristine coral reefs.
  • Shopping: Visit the bustling street markets of Myeik to experience the local culture and pick up some unique souvenirs, including traditional textiles, wooden carvings, and gemstones.

Food and Drink Scene of Myeik

Must-Try Dishes

  • Mohinga: A popular breakfast dish made with rice noodles, fish broth, and a variety of herbs and spices.
  • Kyar Zan Hin: A traditional Burmese chicken curry with a tangy tomato sauce.
  • Laphet Thoke: A refreshing salad made with pickled tea leaves, tomatoes, and peanuts.

Local Beverages

  • Myeik Hta Min: A sweet and fruity wine made from locally-grown grapes.
  • Myanmar Beer: A popular local beer made from high-quality hops and malt.

Where to Stay in Myeik: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Myeik has a wide range of accommodations to suit every budget and taste. Here are some of our top picks:

  • Grand Jade Hotel: A luxurious hotel located in the heart of Myeik’s city center, offering modern amenities and exceptional service.
  • Shwe Yee Guesthouse: A comfortable and affordable guesthouse located near the Myeik Market, offering clean rooms and friendly staff.
  • Mergui Archipelago Resort: A secluded resort located on a private island, offering stunning views and direct access to the beach.

What to Buy in Myeik: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Pearls and Gems: Myeik is famous for its high-quality pearls and gemstones, so be sure to check out the local jewelry shops.
  • Textiles: Myeik is home to several weaving communities, so you can find a wide variety of traditional textiles and fabrics.
  • Wooden Carvings: Myeik is known for its skilled wood carvers, who create beautiful sculptures, masks, and furniture.

Local Culture and Customs of Myeik

Myeik is a multicultural city with a diverse range of customs and traditions. Here are some tips to help you navigate the local culture:

  • Dress modestly when visiting religious sites or attending formal events.
  • Remove your shoes before entering a home or temple.
  • Always ask for permission before taking photos of people or religious sites.

Best Time to Visit Myeik

The best time to visit Myeik is from November to February when the weather is cool and dry, making it perfect for outdoor activities and sightseeing. Avoid visiting from June to September when heavy monsoon rains can lead to flooding and landslides.

Do’s and Don’ts of Myeik

Do:

  • Respect the local customs and traditions.
  • Try the local cuisine and immerse yourself in the local culture.
  • Bargain when shopping at street markets.

Don’t:

  • Disrespect Buddhist traditions or religious sites.
  • Drink tap water or eat street food that has been sitting out for too long.
  • Touch or interfere with wildlife, especially in protected areas like the Myeik Archipelago.

Safety and Health

Safety in Myeik

Myeik is generally a safe city, but it’s still important to take precautions to ensure your safety. Here are some tips:

  • Avoid walking alone at night, especially in isolated areas.
  • Keep your belongings secure and in your sight at all times.
  • Use caution when swimming or diving, especially in unfamiliar waters or during strong currents.

Health in Myeik

Before traveling to Myeik, be sure to consult with your doctor regarding necessary vaccinations and precautions. Avoid drinking tap water and pay special attention to food hygiene when eating out.

Getting to Myeik: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Myeik

The nearest international airport to Myeik is Yangon International Airport, which is around 550 km away. The Myeik Airport is a domestic airport that connects to several cities in Myanmar, including Yangon, Mandalay, and Dawei.

Bus Stations Near Myeik

Myeik has a central bus station that connects to several nearby towns and cities, including Dawei, Mawlamyine, and Hpa-An.

Other Options

A popular way to get to Myeik is by boat from Kawthaung, which is on the border with Thailand. Several tour companies offer boat tours of the Myeik Archipelago that depart from Myeik or Kawthaung.

How to Get Around in Myeik

Taxis and tuk-tuks are the most convenient ways to get around Myeik, with fixed rates for most destinations. Motorbike rentals are also available, but be sure to wear a helmet and drive defensively.

Where to Go Next from Myeik: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Dawei: A charming colonial town located 200 km north of Myeik, known for its pristine beaches, ancient temples, and unique local culture.
  • Kawthaung: A bustling port city located on the border with Thailand, offering unique seafood dishes, local crafts, and a vibrant street market.
  • Hpa-An: A scenic town located 350 km north of Myeik, known for its stunning limestone karsts, hidden caves, and Buddhist shrines.

Conclusion

Myeik is a hidden gem of Southeast Asia, offering a unique blend of cultural and natural attractions that are sure to leave any traveler in awe. Armed with this comprehensive travel guide, you can now plan a memorable trip to Myeik and discover all that it has to offer.
