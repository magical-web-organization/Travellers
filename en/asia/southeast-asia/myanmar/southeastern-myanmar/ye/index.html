Introduction to Ye

Welcome to Ye, a small town in Southeastern Myanmar that’s considered to be one of the country’s hidden gems. While it may not be as popular as other tourist destinations in Myanmar, Ye boasts stunning natural attractions, a rich history, and a vibrant local culture that’s waiting to be explored.

This guide will give you an overview of the best places to visit, the top things to do, where to stay, and where to eat in Ye. Whether you’re a solo traveler, a couple, or a family, this guide will help you plan an unforgettable trip to this charming little town.

List of Places to Visit in Ye

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Ye has a rich history that dates back to the Mon kingdoms that ruled the area in the 9th century. As such, there are several historical places and museums that are worth exploring for those interested in local culture and history.

1. Ye Htut Lwin Pagoda

One of the most significant places of worship in Ye is the Ye Htut Lwin Pagoda. This pagoda is believed to have been built during the Mon period and has several Buddha images, including a large reclining Buddha statue.

2. Palace of Taungoo Dynasty

The Palace of the Taungoo Dynasty is a grand palace that was originally built in the 16th century by King Bayint Naung. The palace didn’t survive the test of time, and what remains today are the ruins of the grand palace built with bricks

Natural Attractions of Ye: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Ye’s natural landscape is stunning and varied. There are several outdoor attractions that you can visit to enjoy the beauty of this town fully.

1. Kyeintali Lake

One of the most beautiful natural attractions in Ye is the Kyeintali Lake. It’s a picturesque lake surrounded by lush green hills, and it’s a popular spot for swimming, boating, and fishing.

2. Namtok Waterfall

Namtok Waterfall is another natural attraction that’s worth visiting in Ye. The waterfall is located in the lush jungle, and it’s a perfect spot for those who enjoy hiking and trekking. The waterfall itself is tall and impressive, and there’s a pool at the base where you can swim.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Ye

Ye is a perfect destination for outdoor enthusiasts who enjoy hiking and biking. There are several picturesque routes that are ideal for hiking and biking, including:

1. Ye-Taunggoo

This route takes you through some of Ye’s most beautiful countryside, including paddy fields, forests and a lake dam & reservoir. It’s one of the most popular bike routes in Ye.

2. Yè-Shawdaw-Chan

This 28 km (17 mi) trail is one of the must-do trails for hikers in Ye. It passes through some of the most picturesque villages and rural areas in Ye while also giving hikers the opportunity to witness the way of life of the local people.

Best Things to Do in Ye: Activities and Tours

1. Take a Boat Ride on Kyeintali Lake

Kyeintali Lake is one of Ye’s most beautiful natural attractions, and one of the best ways to explore it is by taking a boat ride. You can rent a boat by the hour and enjoy the stunning scenery of the lake and the surrounding hills.

2. Visit a Local Village and Learn about Traditional Weaving

Ye is famous for its traditional weaving, and visiting a local village to learn about this craft is a must-do activity for those interested in the local culture. You can learn about the different methods of weaving and even try your hand at making your own fabric.

Food and Drink Scene of Ye

Must-Try Dishes

1. Mon Hin Ga

Mon Hin Ga is a traditional noodle soup that’s popular in Ye. It’s made with a fish broth and is served with rice vermicelli, onions, chili, and lemongrass.

2. Lahpet Thohk

Lahpet Thohk is a traditional Burmese dish made with tea leaves, tomatoes, and peanuts. It’s a unique dish that’s worth trying, especially if you’re a foodie.

Local Beverages

1. Thanakha

Thanakha is a traditional Burmese drink made by grinding the bark of the Thanakha plant and mixing it with water. It’s a refreshing drink that’s perfect for hot days in Ye.

2. Myanmar Beer

Myanmar Beer is Myanmar’s popular beer that’s worth trying during your trip to Ye.

Where to Stay in Ye: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Ye has a range of accommodation options that cater to different budgets and preferences. Some of the best places to stay in Ye include:

1. Shwe Phyu Hotel

Shwe Phyu Hotel is a budget-friendly hotel that offers clean rooms and basic amenities. It’s located in the town center and is a perfect base for exploring Ye.

2. Ye Chaung Thar Hotel

Ye Chaung Thar Hotel is a mid-range hotel that offers comfortable rooms, a swimming pool, and a restaurant. It’s located on the outskirts of Ye, offering a peaceful and tranquil stay.

What to Buy in Ye: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

1. Traditional Weaving

Ye is famous for its traditional weaving, and there are several shops and markets where you can buy handmade fabrics and textiles.

2. Shan Bags

Shan Bags are traditional Burmese bags made with colorful fabrics and embroidery. They’re perfect for carrying everyday items and make a fantastic souvenir to bring home.

Local Culture and Customs of Ye

Ye’s culture is deeply rooted in the Mon and Myanmar traditions. Monks and pagodas play a vital role in the local culture, and it’s essential to show respect and dress suitably if you visit these places.

Best Time to Visit Ye

The best time to visit Ye is from November to February when the weather is dry and cool.

Do’s and Don’ts of Ye

Do:

  • Try the local food and drink
  • Dress modestly when visiting religious sites
  • Show respect to the local customs and culture

Don’t:

  • Disrespect the monks and religious sites
  • Take pictures without permission
  • Leave trash behind

Safety and Health

Safety in Ye

Ye is a safe town to visit, but it’s always advisable to take precautions when traveling, such as keeping your valuables safe and avoiding isolated areas at night.

Health in Ye

Make sure to drink bottled water during your trip to Ye and avoid street food that may upset your stomach. It’s always a good idea to bring any necessary medication with you.

Getting to Ye: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Ye

The nearest airport to Ye is in Yangon, the capital city of Myanmar. From there, you can take a bus or taxi to Ye.

Bus Stations Near Ye

Ye has a bus station that offers connections to other cities in Myanmar.

Other Options

You can also take a private car or taxi from Yangon to Ye, but it can be more expensive. Another option is to take a train from Yangon to Dawei and then a bus to Ye.

How to Get Around in Ye

The best way to get around in Ye is by bike or on foot.

Where to Go Next from Ye: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

If you have some extra time in Myanmar, there are several nearby destinations and excursions that are worth visiting after your trip to Ye, including:

  • Dawei
  • Mawlamyine
  • Hpa-An

Conclusion

Ye is a beautiful town in Myanmar that’s worth visiting if you’re looking for a unique cultural experience and stunning natural scenery. With this guide, you’ll have all the information you need to plan an unforgettable trip to Ye.
