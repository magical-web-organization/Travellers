Introduction to Three Pagodas Pass

Located in Southeastern Myanmar, Three Pagodas Pass is a place of significant historical and cultural importance. It is surrounded by lush green forests, magnificent mountain ranges, and scenic landscapes that make it an ideal destination for travelers. The pass connects Myanmar to Thailand and is named after the three ancient pagodas that lie within its borders. Myanmar is a remote and off-beaten destination, which makes it an exciting place for explorers. This travel guide will help you plan your trip to Three Pagodas Pass, whether you’re a history buff, adventure seeker, or just looking for an escape from the city.

List of Places to Visit in Three Pagodas Pass

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Three Pagodas Pass Heritage Centre: A museum exhibiting artefacts and photographs relevant to the Second World War and the civil war from 1948-2012.
  • Death Railway Museum: A museum located at the River Kwai Bridge, which provides information about the construction of the railway during World War II and its impact on prisoners of war.
  • Thanbyuzayat War Cemetery: A war cemetery with graves of prisoners who died while building the Burma Railway.

Natural Attractions of Three Pagodas Pass: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Pha-an caves: A complex cave network, famous for its unique architecture, stupa-shaped rocks, and intricate carvings on walls.
  • Kyane Hnit waterfall: A picturesque waterfall at the base of Mount Zwekabin, which is surrounded by lush greenery and beautiful landscapes.
  • Mount Zwekabin: The second-highest mountain in Karen State, which offers stunning views of the surrounding landscapes, the town of Hpa-an, and the lush green forests of the region.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Three Pagodas Pass

Hiking and biking are popular activities in Three Pagodas Pass. Some of the popular routes for hiking and biking are:

  • The Kawkareik-Kyaikto route: A 200 km long route that connects Kawkareik to Kyaikto, passing through the scenic landscapes of Karen state and Mon state.
  • The Win Sein Taw Ya route: A 20 km scenic route that takes you through rice fields, fish ponds, and rubber plantations to the Win Sein Taw Ya Reclining Buddha.

Best Things to Do in Three Pagodas Pass: Activities and Tours

  • Visit the local markets to experience the local culture and taste some of the local cuisine.
  • Take a boat ride on the Moei River to enjoy the beautiful landscapes and witness the daily life of the local people.
  • Attend a traditional Karen dance performance to learn about the local culture and traditions.

Food and Drink Scene of Three Pagodas Pass

Must-Try Dishes

  • Mohinga: A traditional breakfast dish of Myanmar, made with fish soup and rice noodles.
  • Shan-style noodles: A dish made with flat rice noodles, served with chicken or pork broth.
  • Khao Soi: A Burmese-inspired dish of curry noodles.

Local Beverages

  • Green tea: A popular drink in Myanmar with a unique flavor.
  • Beer: Myanmar’s most famous beer is Myanmar Beer, which is available everywhere in the country.

Local Culture and Customs of Three Pagodas Pass

The Karen people are the largest ethnic group in the region, and their unique culture and traditions can be seen throughout Three Pagodas Pass. One of the most notable customs is their traditional dance performances, which are often accompanied by traditional instruments such as drums and flutes. Visitors are encouraged to respect the local customs and traditions and engage with the local people in a respectful and meaningful way.

Best Time to Visit Three Pagodas Pass

The best time to visit Three Pagodas Pass is between November and February when the weather is relatively cool and dry. The monsoon season, which lasts from June to September, can make outdoor activities difficult due to heavy rains and floods.

Do’s and Don’ts of Three Pagodas Pass

Do:

  • Engage with the local people respectfully and meaningfully.
  • Learn about the local culture and traditions.
  • Try the local cuisine.

Don’t:

  • Disrespect the local people, customs, and traditions.
  • Wear revealing clothing when visiting temples or other religious sites.
  • Engage in any illegal activities.

Safety and Health

Safety in Three Pagodas Pass

Three Pagodas Pass is generally a safe place for tourists, but it’s always a good idea to stay aware of your surroundings and take necessary precautions to avoid any unpleasant situations.

Health in Three Pagodas Pass

It’s recommended to get vaccinated for hepatitis A and B, typhoid, and polio before visiting Myanmar. It’s also important to carry antimalarial medication if you plan to stay in rural areas for an extended period.

Bus Stations Near Three Pagodas Pass

Other Options

  • Train: There are several trains that run from Yangon to Mawlamyine, with stops at Kawkareik and other towns along the way.

How to Get Around in Three Pagodas Pass

The best way to get around Three Pagodas Pass is by private car or on foot/bicycle. Car rental services are available at several hotels and guesthouses, and most attractions are within walking or cycling distance.

Where to Go Next from Three Pagodas Pass: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Mawlamyine: A charming colonial town with historic architecture and a bustling street market.
  • Dawei: A seaside town with stunning beaches, waterfalls, and an ancient temple complex.
