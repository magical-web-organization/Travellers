Introduction to Bolaven Plateau

Bolaven Plateau is a picturesque region situated in Southern Laos, known for its lush landscapes, cascading waterfalls, charming villages, and thriving coffee plantations. The plateau lies at an altitude of 1,000 meters above sea level and covers an area of 10,000 square kilometers, making it a popular destination among nature lovers and adventure seekers. Bolaven Plateau boasts a unique blend of natural and cultural attractions that appeal to travelers of all types.

List of Places to Visit in Bolaven Plateau

If you’re planning a trip to Bolaven Plateau, be sure to include the following must-see destinations in your itinerary:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Pakxong Coffee Plantation: Take a guided tour of this organic coffee estate and learn about the history and production process of Laotian coffee.
  • Tad Fane Waterfall: Marvel at the stunning beauty of the twin waterfalls that cascade down a 120-meter cliff amid lush greenery.
  • Wat Phou Salao: Visit this hilltop temple and enjoy panoramic views of Pakse and the Mekong River.
  • That Kong/ Konglor Cave: Discover the natural wonders of Laos by taking a boat through the underground river of Khonglor cave.

Natural Attractions of Bolaven Plateau: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Tad Yuang Waterfall: Explore the lush nature around the cascading torrent of Tad Yuang waterfall and its scenic surroundings.
  • Tad Champi Waterfall: Take a dip in the refreshing natural pool of Tad Champi found along the Bolaven plateau route.
  • Coffee Plantations: Visit some of the many coffee plantations that dot the Bolaven plateau and try some of the finest and freshest coffee beans in Southeast Asia.
  • Phu Asa Mountain: Hike up the 1500m mountain and take in the panoramic view of the region’s lush landscape and many scenic spots.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Bolaven Plateau

Bolaven Plateau offers a variety of hiking and biking routes suitable for both beginners and experienced travelers who wish to explore the area on foot or by bike. You can rent bikes and hike one of the many designated trails in the region or join a guided tour if you prefer a more structured experience. Here are some of the best routes to try:

  • Bolaven Plateau Loop: The Bolaven Plateau loop, a 3-4 day loop, is a popular cycling and biking route that offers a spectacular ride through the dense forest and scenic views of countless waterfalls.
  • Tad Fane Trail: Located in Dong Hua Sao National Park, this trail runs through a dense forest and offers great views of Tad Fane waterfalls and its scenic surroundings.

Best Things to Do in Bolaven Plateau: Activities and Tours

Bolaven Plateau has plenty of activities and tours to offer, including:

  • Elephant Riding: Explore the lush jungle of Bolaven Plateau on the back of an elephant and experience a unique view of the region’s natural beauty.
  • Kayaking and Canoeing: Enjoy the scenic views of Bolaven Plateau’s rivers and waterfalls by kayaking and canoeing along the Mekong and Nam Xong rivers.
  • Coffee Tours: Take a guided tour of coffee plantations and learn about the production process of some of Southeast Asia’s most exquisite coffee blends.

Week-Long Itinerary for Bolaven Plateau

Here’s a suggested week-long itinerary to help you experience the best of Bolaven Plateau:

Day 1: Arrive in Pakse and visit the Pakxong Coffee Plantation Day 2: Hike to Tad Fane Waterfall and explore Dong Hua Sao National Park Day 3: Take a guided tour of some coffee plantations, and visit Tad Yuang and Tad Champi waterfalls Day 4: Kayak or Canoe on the Nam Xong River Day 5: Visit Wat Phou Salao and take in the stunning views of the Mekong River Day 6: Take a guided hike to Phu Asa Mountain Day 7: Enjoy a day of relaxation and sampling local cuisine or visit some markets and buy souvenirs to bring home with you.

Food and Drink Scene of Bolaven Plateau

Laotian cuisine is a treasure trove of unique flavors and culinary delights you should explore on your trip to Bolaven Plateau. Some must-try dishes include:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Khao Niao: A popular staple dish made from sticky rice that is eaten with hands and traditionally used to scoop up other dishes.
  • Larb: A spicy and sour meat salad made of minced meat, herbs, and chilies.
  • Papaya Salad: A flavorful and healthy dish made of unripe papaya, tomatoes, peanuts, and fish sauce.
  • Khao Piak Sen: A hearty soup made of rice noodles, meat, and broth that comes in a variety of flavors and spice levels.

Local Beverages

  • Laos Coffee: Laotian coffee is famous for its smooth taste, rich aroma, and organic quality. You can find coffee shops and plantations throughout Bolaven Plateau where you can sample some of the finest blends.
  • Laos Beer: BeerLao is a popular beer brand in Laos and is a refreshing accompaniment to any meal.

Where to Stay in Bolaven Plateau: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

There are plenty of accommodation options to choose from while visiting Bolaven Plateau:

  • Khamphouy Guesthouse: A budget-friendly guesthouse in Pakse city center, conveniently located near popular attractions.
  • Sengahloune Resort: A mid-range resort nestled amid lush green forests with outdoor pools and serene gardens.
  • The River Resort: A luxurious 5-star riverside hotel with stunning views of the Mekong river and first-class amenities.

What to Buy in Bolaven Plateau: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

When in Bolaven Plateau, don’t forget to check out the following souvenirs and local products:

  • Coffee beans and blends from any of the many plantations along the way, for a special taste of Laos at home.
  • Handcrafted textiles and weavings from village markets, made by skilled and talented artisans.
  • Tribal handmade jewelry and trinkets that uniquely represent the cultural heritage of Laos.

Local Culture and Customs of Bolaven Plateau

Laotian culture is rich and diverse, an amalgamation of influences from its neighbors such as China, Thailand, and Vietnam. While in Bolaven Plateau, it is important to respect local customs by dressing modestly and remaining calm and respectful in public spaces. Learning a few phrases in Laotian will go a long way in bridging the language barrier and showing respect for local culture.

Best Time to Visit Bolaven Plateau

The best time to visit Bolaven Plateau is during the dry season, between October and April, when the weather is warm and dry, and the skies are clear. The wet season, from May to September, is also a good time to visit, as the lush landscape is greener and the waterfalls are in full force. However, be prepared for muddy trails and occasional downpours during this period.

Do’s and Don’ts of Bolaven Plateau

Do:

  • Respect local culture and customs
  • Try local food and coffee
  • Be prepared for outdoor activities with appropriate and comfortable clothing
  • Learn some basic Laotian phrases
  • Be open to new experiences and meeting locals

Don’t:

  • Take photos of people without their permission
  • Display affection in public
  • Leave trash and litter in natural and cultural sites
  • Wear revealing clothing in religious sites
  • Engage in illegal activities such as drugs or wildlife trafficking.

Safety and Health

Safety in Bolaven Plateau

While Bolaven Plateau is generally safe, there are a few precautions travelers should take:

  • Wear a helmet when biking or motorcycling to avoid serious injury in case of an accident.
  • Carry enough cash or credit cards as ATMs are not widely available in rural areas.
  • Secure your valuables in a safe within your accommodation to prevent theft.

Health in Bolaven Plateau

Before traveling to Bolaven Plateau, make sure you have the following vaccinations:

  • Hepatitis A and B
  • Typhoid fever
  • Measles, mumps, and rubella (MMR)
  • Tetanus, Diptheria, and Pertussis (Tdap)

Getting to Bolaven Plateau: Transportation Options and Logistics

You can reach Bolaven Plateau by either air, bus, or private transportation.

Airports Near Bolaven Plateau

The nearest airport to Bolaven Plateau is Pakse International Airport, which is about an hour’s drive from the plateau. The airport is served by regional airlines such as Bangkok Airways and Lao Airlines.

Bus Stations Near Bolaven Plateau

There are several bus stations in Pakse that depart daily to Bolaven Plateau. The bus ride takes about 2-3 hours, depending on the destination, and tickets can be purchased at the bus stations.

Other Options

You can also rent a car, motorbike, or bicycle to explore Bolaven Plateau at your own pace, but make sure you have navigation available since road signs can be scarce in rural areas.

How to Get Around in Bolaven Plateau

The best way to explore Bolaven Plateau is by renting a car or motorbike. You can also hire a local guide to take you around the area or join a guided tour if you prefer a more structured experience.

Where to Go Next from Bolaven Plateau: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

If you have more time, consider exploring other destinations in Laos, including:

  • Vientiane: The capital city of Laos characterized by its French-inspired architecture, serene temples, and vibrant night markets.
  • Luang Prabang: A UNESCO World Heritage site known for its rich cultural heritage and stunning mountainous landscape.
  • Si Phan Don: A laidback island destination located in the southern part of Laos that offers natural scenery and river adventure activities.

Conclusion

Bolaven Plateau is a hidden gem in Southern Laos, offering visitors a unique blend of natural and cultural attractions, delicious local cuisine, and warm hospitality. Whether you’re seeking adventure, relaxation, or cultural immersion, Bolaven Plateau has something for everyone. With our comprehensive guide, you are ready to embark on an unforgettable journey into the heart of Laos.
