Introduction to Don Khong

Don Khong, located in the Si Phan Don or Four Thousand Islands area of southern Laos, is the largest island in the Mekong River and an unspoiled gem of Southeast Asia. The island is known for its serene natural beauty, historic temples, and traditional way of life. In this comprehensive travel guide, we’ll show you the top things to do in Don Khong, from exploring culturally important museums and temples to hiking and biking its scenic trails. Get ready to immerse yourself in the authentic Lao culture and marvel at stunning landscapes.

List of Places to Visit in Don Khong

Here are some of the best places to visit in Don Khong:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Wat Si Moungkhoun: This ancient Buddhist temple is one of the most significant religious structures in Don Khong.
  • Wat Khon Tai: Another notable temple located on the island and often referred to as the Old temple of Don Khong.
  • Khone Phapheng Waterfall: Located on the southern tip of the island, the Khone Phapheng waterfall is the largest waterfall in Southeast Asia, which flows from the Mekong River.

Natural Attractions of Don Khong: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Li Phi Waterfall: This breathtaking waterfall, also known as the Devil’s Corridor, offers a range of outdoor activities such as swimming and jumping.
  • Traditional Villages: Get a glimpse of the traditional Lao lifestyle by visiting local villages like Ban Houayxai and Ban Huaypong.
  • Dolphin Watching: You can witness the rare Irrawaddy dolphins in their natural habitat by going on a boat ride along the Mekong River.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Don Khong

Don Khong is famous for its adventurous hiking and biking routes. Here are some of the best routes to try:

  • The loop road around Don Khong is an ideal route for cycling, which offers beautiful scenery of the Mekong River
  • Explore the island’s eastern part, which includes a dirt road leading to the 2000 meter-long earth embankment, to see the beautiful rice fields and traditional villages.

Best Things to Do in Don Khong: Activities and Tours

Don Khong offers several activities to keep you engaged during your visit. Here are a few options:

  • Explore the local markets with an expert guide to get first-hand experience of the Lao cuisine and the local lifestyle.
  • Take a kayak trip down the Mekong River to experience the local flora and fauna while getting a full-body workout.
  • Go on a traditional longtail boat ride to witness the stunning Li Phi Falls, where you can swim and jump into the blue pools surrounded by lush vegetation.

Week-Long Itinerary for Don Khong

Here’s a sample itinerary to help you make the most of your week in Don Khong:

DAY 1: Arrive in Don Khong and check-in at your accommodation. DAY 2: Visit the historic temples, Wat Si Moungkhoun and Wat Khon Tai, followed by a bicycle ride around the island to experience the local lifestyle and traditional architectures. DAY 3: Go on a boat ride to witness the rare Irrawaddy dolphins playing in the Mekong River. After that, head to Li Phi Waterfall for a swim and dip in the blue pools. DAY 4: Explore the eastern side of the island, which included a dirt road leading to the 2000-meter-long earth embankment, and enjoy the rustic scenery and the rice paddies. DAY 5: Take a kayak trip down the Mekong River and take in the panoramic views of the surrounding nature. DAY 6: Explore the local markets and indulge in Lao cuisine while learning about the local lifestyle and traditions. DAY 7: Depart from Don Khong.

Food and Drink Scene of Don Khong

Don Khong’s food and drink scene is a blend of Lao and ethnic cuisine. Here are some of the must-tries:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Laap: A traditional Laotian dish made with minced meat, herbs, and spices
  • Khao Niao: A popular sticky rice dish that is often an accompaniment in almost all Lao meals
  • Tam Mak Hoong: A spicy papaya salad which is one of the most common and loved dishes of Lao cuisine

Local Beverages

  • Beer Lao: A popular local beer which can be found almost everywhere in the island
  • Lao Lao: This unique rice wine is a specialty of the country and can be found in many bars and restaurants

Where to Stay in Don Khong: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Don Khong offers various accommodation options ranging from budget-friendly hostels to luxurious hotels. Here are some of the best options:

  • Sala Done Khone: An eco-friendly resort located on the banks of the Mekong River, offering traditional bungalows with stunning river views.
  • Fragipani Resort: A luxurious hotel featuring comfortable rooms, an outdoor pool, and an on-site restaurant serving local and international cuisine.
  • Pon’s Guesthouse: A budget-friendly option featuring unique bungalows made of bamboo and wood.

What to Buy in Don Khong: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Don Khong offers plenty of traditional Lao handicrafts and other items that make wonderful souvenirs. Here are some items to consider:

  • Handmade Lao silk textiles: Single-colored according to various provinces
  • Wood carvings: Take home wooden carvings such as traditional fishing boats and animal shapes
  • Silk scarves: Light, airy, and chic, these scarves are perfect for keeping the heat away

Local Culture and Customs of Don Khong

Lao culture and customs are evident in the island’s traditional festivals and religious ceremonies. Here are some tips to help you respect and appreciate their culture:

  • Dress modestly when visiting temples and religious sites
  • Remove shoes before entering any religious building, home or office
  • Respecting elders is highly esteemed in Lao culture

Best Time to Visit Don Khong

The best time to visit Don Khong is between October and March. The weather during this period is dry and cool, with minimal rainfall and comfortable temperatures. It’s the perfect time to enjoy outdoor activities and sightseeing.

Do’s and Don’ts of Don Khong

Here are some important do’s and don’ts during your stay in Don Khong:

Do:

  • Respect the local customs and traditions.
  • Try Lao cuisine and explore the local markets.
  • Wear comfortable clothing and walking shoes while going on hikes or bike rides.

Don’t:

  • Litter in public spaces.
  • Make loud noises or cause disruptions, particularly in sacred areas.
  • Disrespect local authorities.

Safety and Health

Safety in Don Khong

Don Khong is generally a safe place to visit. However, it’s always a good idea to take precautions and stay aware of your surroundings. Here are some safety tips:

  • Avoid walking alone in quiet areas and at night.
  • Keep your personal belongings close to you and be mindful of pickpockets.
  • Avoid drinking tap water and stick to bottled water.

Health in Don Khong

It’s important to take some simple precautions to stay healthy in Don Khong. Here are some tips:

  • Apply insect repellent to avoid mosquito-borne illnesses like dengue fever or malaria.
  • Wear a hat and sunscreen to prevent sunburn.
  • Avoid eating uncooked or undercooked food.

Getting to Don Khong: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Don Khong

The nearest airport to Don Khong is Pakse International Airport, which is about a 90-minute drive away. There are regular flights to Pakse International Airport from the capital Lao and the neighboring countries.

Bus Stations Near Don Khong

The nearest bus station to Don Khong is located in Pakse, where regular bus services from Vientiane and the neighboring countries run regularly.

Other Options

Another interesting way to reach Don Khong is through a boat ride from Si Phan Don, the group of four thousand islands in the Mekong River, which provides a scenic route for travelers.

How to Get Around in Don Khong

The easiest way to get around Don Khong is by renting a bicycle or motorbike to explore the island at your own leisure. Tuk-tuks are also available for hire for short distances.

Where to Go Next from Don Khong: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Here are some recommended nearby destinations and excursions to explore after your visit to Don Khong:

  • Si Phan Don: Take a short boat ride from Don Khong to Si Phan Don, an archipelago of islands, each with its unique identity.
  • Pakse: The capital of Champasak Province, located at the confluence of the Mekong and Xe Don Rivers.
  • Wat Phou: One of the most significant and well-preserved Khmer Empire temple complexes in Southeast Asia.

Conclusion

Don Khong, one of the hidden gems of Laos, is a destination that satisfies the adventurous and cultural spirit of every traveler. From stunning natural wonders to ancient heritage sites, the island, with its charming hospitality, stunning sights, and remarkable cuisines, remains one of the must-visit places in Southeast Asia.
