Introduction to Saravan

Nestled in the southern part of Laos, Saravan is a hidden gem that’s perfect for travelers who want to get off the beaten path. Surrounded by lush greenery and natural beauty, this town boasts of cultural and historical significance, as well as a range of outdoor adventure activities. In this travel guide, we will take you on a journey to explore the best places to visit, things to do, food to try, and culture to experience in Saravan.

List of Places to Visit in Saravan

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Wat Lamanok

One of the most significant temples in Saravan, Wat Lamanok is a must-see for anyone who wants to learn about the local culture and religion. Built over 200 years ago, this beautiful temple is known for its intricate carvings and stunning architecture.

Tadlo Resort

Nestled in the beautiful Bolaven Plateau, Tadlo Resort is a great place to relax and unwind after a day of exploring Saravan. This eco-friendly resort offers a range of activities, including trekking, mountain biking, and kayaking.

Dong Hua Sao National Park

Nature lovers will appreciate the beautiful scenery at Dong Hua Sao National Park. This protected area covers more than 1,000 square kilometers and is home to a range of wildlife, including elephants, tigers, and gibbons.

Natural Attractions of Saravan: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Tad Fane Waterfall

This stunning waterfall is one of the most popular tourist attractions in Saravan. Visitors can hike to the top of the waterfall for an unforgettable view of the surrounding area.

Bolaven Plateau

Covering more than 10,000 square kilometers, the Bolaven Plateau is a paradise for outdoor enthusiasts. Visitors can enjoy trekking, cycling, kayaking, and rock climbing amid the beautiful scenery of this region.

Khone Phapheng Waterfall

Located on the Mekong River, Khone Phapheng is the largest waterfall in Southeast Asia. Visitors can take a boat tour to get up close and personal with this impressive natural wonder.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Saravan

Explore the Bolaven Plateau

The Bolaven Plateau is one of the best places in Saravan for hikers and bikers. There are several routes to choose from, ranging from easy to difficult, and all offer stunning views of the scenery.

Tadlo to Paksong

This 20-kilometer route is ideal for mountain bikers who want a challenging ride. The route can be completed in one day and takes you through some of the most beautiful scenery in the region.

Best Things to Do in Saravan: Activities and Tours

Visit the Tadlo Elephant Sanctuary

One of the best things to do in Saravan is to visit the Tadlo Elephant Sanctuary. This sanctuary provides a safe space for elephants that have been rescued from the logging and tourism industries.

Take a Boat Tour of the Mekong River

Explore the Mekong River on a boat tour and discover the natural beauty of this region. You’ll pass by small villages, lush jungles, and stunning waterfalls.

Week-Long Itinerary for Saravan

Day 1-2

Explore the cultural and historical attractions in Saravan, including Wat Lamanok and the Tadlo Resort

Day 3-5

Head to the Bolaven Plateau for some outdoor adventure activities, including hiking, mountain biking, and kayaking.

Day 6-7

Wrap up your trip with a visit to the Tadlo Elephant Sanctuary and a boat tour of the Mekong River.

Food and Drink Scene of Saravan

Must-Try Dishes

Khao Piak Sen

This traditional Lao dish is a noodle soup made with chicken or beef and flavored with aromatics and herbs.

Laap

Laap is a spicy salad made with meat (usually beef or chicken) and flavored with lime juice, chili, and herbs.

Tam Mak Houng

Also known as green papaya salad, Tam Mak Houng is a dish made with shredded green papaya mixed with chili, lime juice, fish sauce, and peanuts.

Local Beverages

Beer Lao

One of the most famous beers in the region, Beer Lao is a smooth and refreshing beer that’s perfect for hot days.

Coffee

Laos is known for its delicious coffee, which is grown in the Bolaven Plateau. Visitors can enjoy a cup of coffee at one of the many cafes and restaurants in Saravan.

Where to Stay in Saravan: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Tadlo Lodge

This cozy lodge is located in the heart of Tadlo Village and offers a range of accommodation options, including bungalows and guesthouses.

Xe Pian Hotel

Located in the center of Saravan, Xe Pian Hotel offers comfortable rooms and easy access to the town’s attractions.

What to Buy in Saravan: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Handmade textiles

Visitors can purchase handmade textiles, including scarves, bags, and clothing, from local markets and shops.

Coffee

Coffee lovers can bring home fresh coffee beans from the Bolaven Plateau to enjoy later.

Local Culture and Customs of Saravan

The people of Saravan are known for their friendly and welcoming nature. Visitors should be respectful of local customs and traditions, including covering their shoulders and knees when visiting temples and removing their shoes before entering a home or temple.

Best Time to Visit Saravan

The best time to visit Saravan is between November and February, when temperatures are cool and dry. The hot and humid rainy season between May and September can make outdoor activities uncomfortable.

Do’s and Don’ts of Saravan

Do:

  • Respect local customs and traditions
  • Be friendly and polite to locals
  • Dress appropriately when visiting temples and other cultural sites

Don’t:

  • Disrespect Buddhist customs and beliefs
  • Touch someone’s head without permission, as it is considered rude
  • Be loud and disruptive in public places

Safety and Health

Safety in Saravan

Saravan is a safe town for travelers, but visitors should be aware of pickpocketing and petty theft. It is recommended to keep valuable items in a secure location and avoid traveling alone at night.

Health in Saravan

Visitors to Saravan should be up to date on routine vaccinations, including measles, mumps, and rubella (MMR), diphtheria-tetanus-pertussis, varicella (chickenpox), and polio.

Getting to Saravan: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Saravan

There are no airports in Saravan; travelers can fly into Pakse International Airport (PKZ), which is about 100 kilometers away.

Bus Stations Near Saravan

The main bus station in Saravan is located in the town center, and visitors can take buses to nearby towns and cities.

Other Options

There are also several private car services and taxis available for hire in Saravan.

How to Get Around in Saravan

The best way to get around Saravan is on foot or by bicycle. There are several bike rental shops in the town center, and visitors can explore the region at a leisurely pace.

Where to Go Next from Saravan: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Champasak

Located about an hour’s drive from Saravan, Champasak is a charming town known for its ancient temples and beautiful architecture.

Si Phan Don

Also known as the Four Thousand Islands, Si Phan Don is a group of islands located in the Mekong River. Visitors can enjoy activities like kayaking and boat tours.

Conclusion

Saravan is a hidden gem that offers a unique blend of culture, history, and outdoor adventure. From exploring the region’s natural wonders to delving into the local culture, there’s something for everyone in Saravan. So pack your bags and get ready for an unforgettable journey to this beautiful town in southern Laos.
