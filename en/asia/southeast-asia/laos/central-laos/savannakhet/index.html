Introduction to Savannakhet

Located in Central Laos, Savannakhet is a hidden gem waiting to be explored. The city is a perfect blend of cultural and natural experiences, offering an array of attractions to its visitors. From museums that showcase the history and culture of Laos to the stunning natural landscapes, adventurous hiking and biking routes, and delicious food, Savannakhet has something for everyone.

In this travel guide, we will take a closer look at the culturally and naturally significant attractions, local cuisine, accommodations, must-try souvenirs, and health and safety in Savannakhet, among many other things. Let’s explore the city and all it has to offer!

List of Places to Visit in Savannakhet

Here is a list of places you shouldn’t miss while visiting Savannakhet:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Savannakhet Provincial Museum
  • Dinosaur Museum
  • That Inhang Stupa
  • Wat Xayaphoum

Natural Attractions of Savannakhet: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Dong Natad Forest
  • Chomphet Stupa
  • Tham Piew Cave
  • Oum Muong Temple

Hiking/Biking Routes of Savannakhet

Savannakhet is a biking and hiking paradise. You can rent a bike from rental shops in the city or join any of the guided tours. Here are some popular biking and hiking trails in Savannakhet:

  • Ban Nonsavang and Ban Dongpho Kham - ideal for biking.
  • Ban Phot and Ban Taad hiking routes - hiking paradise for those wanting to explore the natural landscapes of Savannakhet.

Best Things to Do in Savannakhet: Activities and Tours

  • Visit Savanxay Market, a popular local market in Savannakhet. The market takes place on the 1st, 6th, and 11th days of the Lao lunar calendar.
  • Take a boat ride along the Mekong River.
  • Attend the Savannakhet Boat Racing Festival which takes place every year during the full moon of the seventh lunar month.
  • Explore the Savannakhet Night Market and Taseng Market that sell a variety of local products.

Week-Long Itinerary for Savannakhet

Here is a suggested week-long itinerary for Savannakhet:

Day Activity
1 Visit the Savannakhet Provincial Museum and explore the night markets.
2 Discover the Dong Natad Forest and Tham Piew Cave.
3 Explore the city’s religious sites – Oum Muong, That Inhang, and Wat Xayaphoum.
4 Take a boat ride along the Mekong River and relax at the Chomphet Stupa.
5 Rent a bike and explore Ban Nonsavang and Ban Dongpho Kham.
6 Attend the Savannakhet Boat Racing Festival and admire the local culture.
7 Farewell Savannakhet and enjoy a lovely meal before departing.

Food and Drink Scene of Savannakhet

Food and drink are an essential part of Lao culture, and Savannakhet is no exception. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages in Savannakhet:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Khao Niew - sticky rice served with for example jaew, a chili sauce, and laab, a minced meat salad.
  • Kua Mee - fried noodles
  • Sai Oua - spicy Lao sausage

Local Beverages

  • Beer Lao, the country’s local beer - the perfect refreshment after a long day.
  • Lao Lao – a popular rice wine

Where to Stay in Savannakhet: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

There are several accommodation options in Savannakhet. Here are some of the most popular options:

  • Inthira Hotel – a boutique hotel in the center of Savannakhet. It features air-conditioned rooms with an elegant design and a swimming pool.
  • Savan Resorts – located on the outskirts of Savannakhet. It offers a relaxing environment with rooms that are spacious, clean, and comfortable.
  • Khammouane Guesthouse – budget-friendly guesthouse that is located in the center of Savannakhet with an on-site restaurant.

What to Buy in Savannakhet: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Take some of Savannakhet’s beauty home by purchasing souvenirs and local products. Here are some of the most popular options:

  • Lao traditional arts and crafts – handmade textiles, woven baskets, and pottery
  • Lamphoun Hin, a local herbal remedy that could ease a range of discomforts, including headaches, exhaustion and stomach concerns.

Local Culture and Customs of Savannakhet

The majority of people in Savannakhet are Buddhists, so it is essential to be mindful when visiting religious sites or attending any cultural events.

Local cultures are full of traditions and customs that may differ from other cultures. Still, there are few universal things to keep in mind like taking off your shoes before entering someone’s home or temple, dressing appropriately when visiting religious sites, and being respectful of the food culture.

Best Time to Visit Savannakhet

The best time to visit Savannakhet is between November to February when the weather is mild and pleasant. It is also advisable to avoid the rainy season (May to October) as it restricts outdoor activities.

Do’s and Don’ts of Savannakhet

Do:

  • Respect the local culture and traditions.
  • Try the local cuisine.
  • Be mindful of the rules when visiting religious sites.

Don’t:

  • Avoid getting involved in drugs. Drug-related offenses in Laos can lead to lengthy prison sentences.
  • Do not criticize the local customs.

Safety and Health

Safety in Savannakhet

Savannakhet is generally safe, and the crime rate is low. However, it is essential to remain vigilant, especially in the evenings.

Health in Savannakhet

The air quality in the city is not great, so it is advisable to wear a mask while exploring the city. Tap water is not safe to drink, so always purchase bottled water or bring some water purification tablets.

Getting to Savannakhet: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Savannakhet

The nearest airport to Savannakhet is Laos’ Wattay International Airport, located around 8 hours away. You can also take a short flight from Vientiane’s international airport to Savannakhet airport.

Bus Stations Near Savannakhet

Savannakhet has a bus station that connects the city to other parts of Laos.

Other Options

Another popular way of exploring Savannakhet is by joining a guided tour.

How to Get Around in Savannakhet

You can rent a bike or scooter to get around the city, navigate the streets using tuk-tuks or join a guided tour group.

Where to Go Next from Savannakhet: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

There are several exciting destinations and excursions that you can visit from Savannakhet. Here are some of the top recommended options:

  • Pakse - located around 4 hours drive from Savannakhet and is known for its stunning landscapes, including the incredible waterfalls of Tad Fane and Tad Yuang.
  • Don Det - relax on the ‘4000 islands of Laos’ amidst Mekong river and explore the beauty of nature.
  • Huei Thamo - is a famous border crossing to Vietnam.

Conclusion

In conclusion, Savannakhet is a city full of hidden gems that offer an authentic experience of what life in Laos is like. From natural wonders to cultural attractions, there is something for everyone in Savannakhet. Plan your trip now and discover the beauty of this hidden gem in Central Laos.
