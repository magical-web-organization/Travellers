Introduction to Central Laos

Located in Southeast Asia, Central Laos is one of the most hidden gems in the region. It’s an ideal destination for travelers who want to see a more authentic and less touristy side of Laos. Central Laos is known for its scenic views, ancient temples, and traditional way of life. The region is divided into three main provinces: Khammouane, Bolikhamxay, and Xieng Khouang.

In this comprehensive tour guide, we’ll take you through all the must-see attractions, recommend the best places to stay and eat, and provide practical information to help you plan your trip.

List of Places to Visit in Central Laos

Central Laos boasts a diverse range of attractions, including culturally important museums and historical places, natural outdoor adventures, and delicious local cuisine. You’ll find something for everyone, whether you’re an adventurous traveler or a history buff.

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Visitors to Central Laos will feel like they’ve stepped back in time with an abundance of historic sites and museums to explore. Here are some of the most culturally important attractions in the region:

Wat Phou

Wat Phou is an ancient Khmer temple located in Champasak, a province in southern Laos. This UNESCO World Heritage site was built in the 5th century and was expanded during the 11th to 13th centuries. The temple is dedicated to the Hindu god Shiva, and its architecture reflects both Hindu and Buddhist styles.

That Inghang Stupa

That Inghang Stupa is a unique Buddhist stupa located in the Bolikhamxay province. The stupa’s unique design makes it one of the most photogenic spots in Central Laos. The stupa is made of stone, with an octagonal base that is decorated with intricate carvings.

Natural Attractions of Central Laos: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Central Laos provides fantastic opportunities for outdoor adventures provided by the unique landscape, with unforgettable scenery and activities for all the adventurers out there. Here are some of the natural attractions in Central Laos:

Kong Lor Cave

Kong Lor Cave is a stunning natural wonder that stretches over 7km length where you can explore the cave on an easy, guided boat ride. It is one of the most exciting and magnificent caves in the world, with stunning formations and breathtaking views.

Tad Xay Waterfall

Tad Xay Waterfall is located in the Khammouane province and is known for its turquoise-colored waters cascading down the limestone cliffs. It is an ideal place for a refreshing swim, and the surrounding jungle offers plenty of opportunities for hiking or relaxing on the pristine white sand beaches.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Central Laos

Central Laos is full of hiking and biking routes in the heart of lush forests and breathtaking mountain scenery. Here are some of the most popular routes:

Luang Prabang Heritage Trail

The Luang Prabang Heritage Trail is a scenic and easy 4 km trek that starts from the city center, leading to the Tad Sae Waterfall. It passes through authentic villages and lush rice fields, providing an excellent insight into the local lifestyle.

Bolikhamxay Loop

The Bolikhamxay Loop is a picturesque 3-day bike tour that passes through small villages, limestone mountains, and dense forests. This route is highly recommended for nature lovers seeking an authentic and off-the-beaten-path experience.

Best Things to Do in Central Laos: Activities and Tours

Central Laos is full of activities and tours for everyone, from cultural sightseeing to outdoor activities. Here are some of the best things you can do in Central Laos:

Take a Cruise to Si Phan Don

Si Phan Don is located in the Mekong River, and it’s an archipelago of over 4,000 islands. Visitors can take a relaxing boat ride to enjoy the scenery, explore the islands, and go kayaking and swimming in the crystal-clear water.

Visit a Local Village

Experience the authentic lifestyle of the Central Laos people by visiting a local village. The traditional way of life can be observed closely on the streets and in the markets where vendors sell traditional products and food.

Food and Drink Scene of Central Laos

Centra Laos’s food scene boasts delicious local cuisine, including traditional Laotian cuisine, such as spicy Lao fish soup, sticky rice, and grilled meats.

Must-Try Dishes

Larb

Larb is a spicy minced meat salad served with sticky rice. It is made with pork or chicken, mixed with ground toasted rice, fresh mint, and chili peppers.

Khao Poon

Khao Poon is a popular Laotian soup made of rice noodles and a spicy red curry broth. It is usually served with chicken or fish and veggies.

Local Beverages

Lao Lao

Laos’ local spirit, Lao Lao, is made from rice and is known for its high alcohol content. You can find Lao Lao in most restaurants and bars in Central Laos.

Beerlao

Beerlao is the most popular beer in Laos. It’s a refreshing and easy-drinking lager that is perfect for cooling down on a hot Central Laos day.

Where to Stay in Central Laos: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Central Laos offers various accommodation options, from luxury hotels to budget-friendly guesthouses. Here are some of the best places to stay in Central Laos:

My Dream Boutique Resort

My Dream Boutique Resort is a beautiful luxury resort located in Luang Prabang. It offers a stunning view, comfortable rooms, and an outdoor pool overlooking the Mekong River.

Saynamkhan River View

Saynamkhan River View is a serene guest house in Luang Prabang, offering a cozy ambiance for a reasonable price. It is located near all the major attractions and provides excellent views of the river.

What to Buy in Central Laos: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Central Laos offers a great variety of local products and souvenirs you can take home to remember your trip. Here are some of the best items to buy in Central Laos:

Traditional Textiles

Laos has a long tradition of weaving and dying traditional textiles, which can be bought in local markets. They make great souvenirs and come in various colors and designs.

Handicrafts

Handicrafts, such as handmade pottery, wooden carvings, and hand-woven baskets, are also popular souvenirs in Central Laos.

Local Culture and Customs of Central Laos

The Laotian culture is a fascinating mix of traditional customs and Buddhist practices. It’s important to respect the local customs when visiting Central Laos, such as dressing modestly and taking off your shoes before entering temples.

Best Time to Visit Central Laos

The best time to visit Central Laos is between November and February when the weather is dry and cool, making it an ideal time for outdoor activities. However, if you’re interested in the magical moment of the Buddhist Lao New year, you should visit in April.

Do’s and Don’ts of Central Laos

To make the most out of your trip to Central Laos, here are some Do’s and Don’ts:

Do:

  • Respect the local culture and customs
  • Take off your shoes before entering temples
  • Bargain in the markets

Don’t:

  • Touch someone’s head
  • Point your feet towards statues of Buddha

Safety and Health

Central Laos is a relatively safe place for tourists, but it’s always best to take precautions to avoid any mishaps.

Safety in Central Laos

  • Watch your belongings in crowded areas
  • Avoid driving at night
  • Follow safe hiking and trekking guidelines

Health in Central Laos

  • Drink filtered or boiled water
  • Avoid eating raw or undercooked food
  • Pack sunscreen and mosquito repellent

Getting to Central Laos: Transportation Options and Logistics

Central Laos is relatively isolated, but there are a few ways to get there.

Airports Near Central Laos

The closest airports to Central Laos are in Vientiane, Pakse, and Luang Prabang.

Bus Stations Near Central Laos

Bus services operate regularly to Central Laos from major cities in Laos, including Vientiane and Pakse.

Other Options

You can also reach Central Laos by taking a boat along the Mekong River or by hiring a private taxi.

How to Get Around in Central Laos

Transportation options in Central Laos include buses, tuk-tuks, taxis, and motorbike rentals.

Where to Go Next from Central Laos: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

After exploring Central Laos, you should take some side trips to nearby attractions such as the Golden Triangle or Vang Vieng.

Conclusion

Central Laos is one of the most fascinating and charming destinations in Southeast Asia, and with so much to offer, you won’t regret visiting this beautiful country.

Plan your trip now and experience the cultural and natural wonders of Central Laos!
