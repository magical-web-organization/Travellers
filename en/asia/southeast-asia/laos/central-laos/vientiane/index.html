Introduction to Vientiane

Vientiane, the capital city of Laos, is a charming place where you can experience the country’s vibrant history, rich culture, and natural beauty. It is situated in the heart of the country, on the banks of the Mekong River, and is home to many temples, museums, and cultural landmarks that will fascinate visitors.

This comprehensive tour guide will provide you with all the information you need to plan your trip to Vientiane, including a list of places to visit, hiking and biking routes, food and drink scene, lodging options, local customs, safety tips, transportation options, and nearby destinations and excursions.

List of Places to Visit in Vientiane

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Patuxai Victory Monument

Located in the heart of Vientiane, the Patuxai Victory Monument is a beautiful landmark that was built to commemorate Laotian soldiers who fought for independence from France. The architecture of the monument is inspired by the Arc de Triomphe in Paris.

That Luang Stupa

Considered as Laos’ national symbol, the That Luang Stupa is an important Buddhist temple that gleams in gold and stands at nearly 45 meters high. Visitors can walk around the temple and admire it from different angles, as well as learn about its rich history and religious significance.

COPE Visitor Centre

The COPE Visitor Centre is an educational museum that raises awareness of the unexploded bombs from the Vietnam War that still litter Laos and cause devastating injuries to civilians. You can learn about the volunteers who are working towards clearing the mines and providing assistance to victims.

Natural Attractions of Vientiane: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Buddha Park

Located 25 kilometers out of the city, the Buddha Park is a popular attraction that features a collection of impressive concrete sculptures, including a giant reclining Buddha and sculptures of Hindu gods and demons. Visitors can explore the park, take photos, and enjoy a picnic while overlooking the Mekong River.

Phou Khao Khouay National Park

Phou Khao Khouay National Park is a natural reserve that is perfect for outdoor adventures such as hiking, camping, and swimming. Visitors can trek through lush jungles and breathe in the fresh air while enjoying the scenic views.

Mekong River Sunset Cruise

A Mekong River sunset cruise is a fantastic way to end your day in Vientiane. You can relax on a boat, sip a drink, and watch the sun dip below the horizon while enjoying the gentle breeze.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Vientiane

Phou Khao Khouay National Park

The Phou Khao Khouay National Park is a natural reserve that is perfect for hiking and biking. The trails offer stunning views of the lush jungle, waterfalls, and rivers. It’s best to hire a guide for a deeper appreciation of the rich flora and fauna.

Nam Ngum Lake Scenic Route

The Nam Ngum Lake Scenic Route is a picturesque route that passes through small Laotian towns, rice fields, and villages along Lake Nam Ngum. It’s a popular area for cycling, with many local shops renting bikes for the day.

Best Things to Do in Vientiane: Activities and Tours

Laotian Cooking Classes

Laotian cooking classes are a unique way to learn about the country’s culinary traditions. You can explore the local markets with a guide, learn to cook traditional dishes, and enjoy the delicious meals afterward.

Vientiane Tuk-Tuk Adventure

A Vientiane tuk-tuk adventure is a fantastic way to explore the city’s landmarks and attractions in a unique way. The tour lets you sit back and enjoy the ride while a guide shows you around.

Food and Drink Scene of Vientiane

Must-Try Dishes

Khao Jee – Baguette Sandwich

Khao Jee is a popular Laotian dish which is essentially a French baguette sandwich. It is stuffed with grilled meat, vegetables, and homemade pate, combined with a spicy chili sauce.

Laap – Spicy Minced Meat Salad

Laap is a spicy minced meat salad that comes with fresh herbs, chili peppers, and lime juice. It is a traditional dish that’s often served with sticky rice.

Local Beverages

Beer Lao

Beer Lao is a popular local beer, brewed with locally-sourced rice which gives it a light and refreshing flavor.

Lao-Lao

Lao-Lao is a strong rice whiskey that has been distilled using traditional methods for generations. It is often served at social occasions such as weddings and housewarming parties.

Where to Stay in Vientiane: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Vientiane has several accommodation options, ranging from budget-friendly guesthouses to luxurious hotels and resorts. Here are some of the best places to stay:

Budget options

Mixay Paradise Hotel

The Mixay Paradise Hotel is an affordable option located in the heart of Vientiane. It offers comfortable rooms with modern amenities and is within walking distance of major landmarks and attractions.

Vientiane Backpackers Hostel

The Vientiane Backpackers Hostel is another budget-friendly option, located near the city center. Rooms are clean, comfortable, and equipped with modern amenities such as air conditioning and Wi-Fi.

Mid-range options

Ansara Hotel

Ansara Hotel is nestled in the city center, offering comfortable guest rooms accented with a contemporary infusion with local motifs. The hotel is within walking distance of the city’s major landmarks and a quick ride to the airport.

Best Western Vientiane Hotel

Best Western is located between the airport and the city center, offering visitors a tranquil retreat in proximity to seamless urban access. The hotel features stylish decor with modern amenities, including Wi-Fi, fitness center, and a restaurant.

What to Buy in Vientiane: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Silk Fabric and Clothing

At the morning market, visitors may purchase several Laotian silk textiles, clothing items, and accessories. Hand-woven by local artisans, the silk textiles are dyed and embroidered with intricate patterns and motifs in vibrant colors.

Handicrafts

Vientiane night markets offer tourists a variety of souvenirs and handicrafts, including leather products, wood-carved items, paper products, and even jewelry.

Local Culture and Customs of Vientiane

In Vientiane, Buddhist temples hold great significance & we implore that visitors are respectful when visiting these places. Dress conservatively and remove footwear before entering any religious space to show respect for Laotian culture & civilization.

It is customary to greet Laotians with the words “Sokdee Pee Mai” which translates to Happy New Year, and it is respectful to bargain politely when buying souvenirs.

Best Time to Visit Vientiane

Vientiane is a year-round destination, but the best time to visit is during the dry season from November to February when the weather is cooler and drier.

Do’s and Don’ts of Vientiane

Do:

  • Dress Respectfully when visiting temples and religious sites.
  • Try the local street food and some traditional laap and Nam Khao dishes.
  • Learn about the Laotian culture while exploring.

Don’t:

  • Criticize the royal family or the political system.
  • Wear your shoes when entering a temple or someone’s home.
  • Point with feet, as they are considered impure.

Safety and Health

Safety in Vientiane

Overall, Vientiane is considered to be a safe city. However, it’s always best to be vigilant, avoid carrying valuables, and be cautious when walking around at night. Tuk-tuk and taxi services are available 24 hours a day, and hotels and restaurants can hand a contact card for reliable transportation services.

Health in Vientiane

It is necessary to carry bug spray to avoid insect bites and stay hydrated. Laos is a dengue-zone, therefore, precautions to avoid mosquito bites is necessary. Travelers should make sure that their vaccines are up to date and carry a well-stocked medical kit.

Getting to Vientiane: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Vientiane

The Wattay International Airport is Vientiane’s primary airport and is about four kilometers from the city center. Some airlines that fly to Wattay International Airport include China Eastern, Thai Airways, and Vietnam airlines.

Bus Stations Near Vientiane

Visitors may use bus services from Vientiane Southern Bus Terminal, from where travelers may take to different cities and provincial towns in Laos. Most buses are operated by local outfits and may be less comfortable than what travelers from western countries are used to.

Other Options

Visitors also may arrive in the capital city by train, but the journey may take longer, and the train stations are outside Vientiane, and passengers must take road transport to reach the city center.

How to Get Around in Vientiane

There are various ways to get around in Vientiane, such as Tuk-tuk, cyclos (bikes with a cart for extra passengers), and taxis. Tuk-tuks and taxis are available for hire, and guests must negotiate the price before starting the journey.

Where to Go Next from Vientiane: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Vientiane is centrally located and provides a base for exploring other nearby destinations in Laos. Some of the best day trip destinations include:

Vang Vieng

The town of Vang Vieng is a beautiful place that is three hours away from Vientiane. It is known for its stunning views, caves, and natural scenery, including the Nam Song River.

Luang Prabang

Luang Prabang is a UNESCO World Heritage Site nestled at the junction of two rivers, about 12 hours from Vientiane and is known for its well-preserved French architecture, historical landmarks, and scenic beauty.

Conclusion

Vientiane is a hidden gem in Southeast Asia and provides visitors with a glimpse into the rich Laotian culture and customs. This comprehensive tour guide provides you with all the information you need to plan an unforgettable trip to Vientiane.
