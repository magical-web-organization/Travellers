Introduction to Plain of Jars

Tucked into the heart of Laos, Plain of Jars is a mesmeric and mysterious historical landscape that continues to fascinate visitors around the world. Despite the name, Plain of Jars is not a single location, but rather a collection of over 90 sites and 1,000 individual stone vessels scattered across the lush countryside. These remarkable jars, each weighing up to several tonnes, are believed to have been created by an ancient civilization over 2,000 years ago. Each site offers unique insights into the customs and rituals of the people who once inhabited the region.

Today, the Plain of Jars boasts a mix of cultural, natural, and recreational attractions, making it an excellent destination for history buffs, adventure seekers, and travelers looking to escape the crowds and relax in tranquil surroundings. Keep reading to discover the best places to visit, things to do, and where to stay in Plain of Jars, Laos.

List of Places to Visit in Plain of Jars

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Plain of Jars Archaeological Site: The main archeological site includes three jar sites (Site 1, Site 2, and Site 3), and features several raised stone platforms and carved stone animal figures. The on-site museum contains a collection of artifacts, photos, and information about the jars’ cultural significance.

  • Xieng Khouang Provincial Museum: Located in central Phonsavan, the museum displays an extensive collection of ancient and contemporary artifacts from the region, including jars, pottery, jewelry, weapons, and clothing. The museum also offers guided tours and educational programs for children.

  • MAG Visitor Center: The Mines Advisory Group (MAG) has been working tirelessly since 1996 to clear unexploded bombs and mines left over from the Vietnam War. The MAG Visitor Center offers educational exhibits, documentaries, and interactive displays on the history of the war and its impact on the local population.

Natural Attractions of Plain of Jars: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Phou Kheng Hill: For stunning panoramic views of the Plain of Jars and the surrounding mountains, take a trek to the summit of Phou Kheng Hill. This moderate hike takes about two hours to complete.

  • Nong Tang Lake: Located about 3km from downtown Phonsavan, Nong Tang Lake is a peaceful oasis surrounded by lush greenery. Visitors can rent kayaks or paddleboats, have a picnic by the shore, or simply relax and take in the scenery.

  • Kang Kham Cave: This massive limestone cave features striking rock formations, underground lakes, and an array of stalactites and stalagmites. Guided tours are available to explore the cave’s wonders.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Plain of Jars

  • Ban Saphai Trail: This scenic 6km trail leads visitors through rural villages, rice paddies, and towering forests. The trail is relatively easy, with a few moderate inclines, and takes about two hours to complete.

  • Nam Gnum Hike: The Nam Gnum Hike is a challenging full-day trek that rewards visitors with unparalleled views of the Plain of Jars and the sunken Xieng Khouang town. Make sure to bring plenty of water and snacks along the way.

  • Bike Tour: For a more leisurely way to explore the countryside, consider renting a bicycle and cycling around the Plain of Jars. The terrain is mostly flat, and there are plenty of scenic stops along the way.

Best Things to Do in Plain of Jars: Activities and Tours

  • Jar Hunting: Join a guided tour to explore some of the lesser-known jar sites and learn about the archeological techniques used to excavate them. Some of these sites still have not been fully explored.

  • Hot-Air Balloon Ride: Take a hot-air balloon ride for a bird’s-eye view of the Plain of Jars and the surrounding mountains. Flights are offered at sunrise and sunset.

  • Laos Baci Ceremony: Baci is an ancient Lao ritual that involves tying sacred blessings (strings) around a person’s wrist to wish them good luck and good health. Most guesthouses and hotels in Plain of Jars offer Baci ceremonies for guests.

Week-Long Itinerary for Plain of Jars

Day 1: Visit the main Jar site and the Plain of Jars Archaeological Site museum

Day 2: Trek Phou Kheng Hill for panoramic views and explore the MAG Visitor center

Day 3: Hike to Ban Saphai Trail or rent a bike and cycle through the countryside

Day 4: Explore Xieng Khouang Provincial Museum and learn about Lao history and culture

Day 5: Take a hot-air balloon ride or go on a jar-hunting tour

Day 6: Discover the incredible Kang Kham Cave and swim in its underground lakes

Day 7: Participate in a traditional Lao Baci ceremony and relax by Nong Tang Lake

Food and Drink Scene of Plain of Jars

Must-Try Dishes

  • Laab: A spicy minced meat salad seasoned with fragrant herbs and citrus flavors.

  • Khaiphaen: Flash-fried riverweed served with chili dip.

  • Kao Soi Gai: A savory coconut chicken noodle soup.

Local Beverages

  • Lao Beer: A light, crisp beer favored by locals and travelers alike.

  • Lao Lao: A potent moonshine made from sticky rice. Drink it neat or mixed with fruit juice.

  • Lao Coffee: Rich, flavorful coffee grown in the highlands of Laos. Try it with condensed milk or iced for a refreshing treat.

Where to Stay in Plain of Jars: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Phouphadeng Hotel: One of the best-rated hotels in Plain of Jars, this elegant hotel boasts a central location, an on-site restaurant, and a stunning mountain view.

-Lha’s Place Homestay: This cozy and friendly homestay welcomes guests with comfortable rooms, home-cooked meals, and a tranquil garden.

-Phonsavan Villas: These picturesque villas are located just outside Phonsavan and offer guests a peaceful retreat with a shared pool, a shuttle service, and a restaurant.

What to Buy in Plain of Jars: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Stone Crafts: Look for hand-carved jars, bowls, and vases made by local artisans.

  • Laos Silk: Bright, colorful silk textiles are a popular souvenir in Plain of Jars. Look for traditional wall hangings or clothing.

  • Ethnic Jewelry: Handmade silver or metal jewelry is a great way to take home a piece of Lao culture.

Local Culture and Customs of Plain of Jars

The local population is primarily Hmong and Khmu ethnic minority groups, famous for their expertise in handicrafts such as weaving, embroidery, and silverwork. Visitors are urged to respect and observe local customs, including covering up when visiting temples, removing shoes when entering homes, and being polite and friendly to locals. Bargaining is expected at markets and street stalls, but it’s important to remain respectful and not haggle too aggressively.

Best Time to Visit Plain of Jars

The best time to visit Plain of Jars is from November to February, when the weather is dry and cool. March to May is the hottest and driest period of the year, with temperatures soaring up to 40°C. June to September is the wet season, with frequent rain showers and occasional flash floods.

Do’s and Don’ts of Plain of Jars

Do:

  • Dress appropriately for temples and other religious sites.

  • Learn a few words in Lao, such as “sabaidee” (hello) and “kop chai” (thank you).

  • Bring plenty of sunscreen and insect repellent.

Don’t:

  • Disrespect local customs or religious traditions.

  • Photograph or touch religious artifacts or statues without permission.

  • Discard trash or waste in public areas or natural sites.

Safety and Health

Safety in Plain of Jars

Phonsavan is a generally safe and peaceful town, but visitors should remain aware of their surroundings and take basic precautions such as keeping valuables secure and using common sense when traveling at night. Avoid unexplored or isolated areas, and be cautious of unexploded landmines in rural areas.

Health in Plain of Jars

Visitors should come prepared with basic medical supplies and carry adequate travel insurance. Tap water is not recommended for drinking, so drink bottled or boiled water to avoid stomach upset. Mosquitoes can be a problem, so use insect repellent and wear long sleeves and pants during peak biting times.

Getting to Plain of Jars: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Plain of Jars

Xam Neua Airport is the closest airport to Phonsavan town, but it offers limited flights from Vientiane and Houayxay.

Bus Stations Near Plain of Jars

Northern Bus Station in Vientiane is the main bus station servicing Phonsavan and other northern towns. Buses run daily from early morning until late afternoon.

Other Options

Private taxis or minivans are available for hire from Vientiane or Vang Vieng. Tuk-tuk, motorbike, and bicycle rentals are also available in Phonsavan.

How to Get Around in Plain of Jars

Phonsavan is a small town that can be navigated on foot or by bicycle. Tuk-tuks are widely available for longer distances, and motorbike rentals are available for adventurous travelers.

Where to Go Next from Plain of Jars: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Luang Prabang: The UNESCO-listed town of Luang Prabang is a three-hour drive from Phonsavan and is famous for its stunning temples, unique architecture, waterfalls, and night market.

  • Nong Khiaw: This charming riverside town is a popular stopover for adventures to Muang Ngoi and other remote northern Lao destinations.

Conclusion

Plain of Jars offers an unforgettable adventure through time and culture, from the ancient jar sites to the natural beauty of the surrounding countryside. Whether you’re interested in history, adventure, or relaxation, Plain of Jars has something special to offer. With this comprehensive guide at your fingertips, you’ll be ready to plan your trip and discover the wonders of this unique destination in Laos.
