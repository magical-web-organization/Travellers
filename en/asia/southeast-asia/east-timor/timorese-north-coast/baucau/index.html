Introduction to Baucau

Baucau is a historic town located in the North Coast of East Timor in Southeast Asia. Known for its unique blend of rich history, cultural heritage, and natural beauty, Baucau is a must-visit destination for anyone interested in exploring the hidden treasures of Southeast Asia.

Once a thriving center of the Portuguese colonial administration, Baucau still carries with it evidence of its past in the form of colonial-era buildings and landmarks. Despite its tumultuous history, Baucau has managed to preserve its cultural identity and traditions, making it a fascinating and rewarding destination for travelers of all interests and ages.

In this comprehensive guide, we’ll take a closer look at Baucau and all that this fascinating town has to offer, from its many natural wonders to its rich cultural heritage, delicious culinary scene, and thriving tourism industry.

List of Places to Visit in Baucau

Here are some of the most must-visit places in Baucau:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Lautem Fort: This Portuguese-era fortification was built in the 18th century and is one of the most impressive landmarks in Baucau. Visitors can explore the fort’s interior, see a collection of period artifacts and learn about the history of the fortification and the region in general.

  • Baucau Cathedral: This impressive church, built in the 1960s, is the largest church in East Timor and is an important symbol of the country’s Catholic faith. Visitors can marvel at the beautiful stained glass windows and ornate architecture, as well as explore the cathedral’s many hidden corners and alcoves.

  • Buka Hatene Museum: This fascinating museum is dedicated to preserving the history and culture of the region, with a particular focus on the traditions of the Timorese people. Visitors can see artifacts, photographs, and other exhibits that offer a unique and insightful glimpse into the past of Baucau and the surrounding area.

Natural Attractions of Baucau: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Mount Matebian: This stunning mountain dominates the skyline of Baucau and is a popular destination for hikers, climbers, and nature enthusiasts. Visitors can enjoy breathtaking views of the surrounding landscape from the summit, which stands at an impressive 1,315 meters above sea level.

  • Lake Iralalaro: This crystal-clear mountain lake is a popular spot for swimming, picnicking, and relaxing, with its beautiful scenery and fresh, clean water. Visitors can also go fishing or take a boat ride to explore the lake’s many hidden corners.

  • Praia Areia Branca: This picturesque beach is located just outside of Baucau and offers visitors a chance to soak up the sun, swim in the crystal-clear waters, and enjoy a delicious seafood meal at one of the many beachfront restaurants and cafes.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Baucau

For those who enjoy outdoor adventure, Baucau offers a range of hiking and biking trails that showcase the region’s diverse landscape and natural beauty. Some of the best routes to explore include:

  • Mount Matebian Hike: This challenging hike takes visitors to the summit of the region’s highest peak, offering stunning views of the surrounding landscape and a chance to explore the flora and fauna of the area.

  • Baucau to Viqueque Bike Ride: This scenic bike ride takes visitors on a journey through some of the most beautiful parts of Baucau and beyond, passing through charming villages and scenic mountain valleys along the way.

Best Things to Do in Baucau: Activities and Tours

From exploring the region’s natural wonders to getting a glimpse into its rich history and culture, there is no shortage of things to do in Baucau. Here are some of the best activities and tours to consider:

  • Baucau City Tour: This comprehensive tour of the town takes visitors to all the must-see landmarks and attractions, including the Lautem Fort, Baucau Cathedral, and Buka Hatene Museum. Along the way, visitors will learn about the history and culture of this fascinating region from knowledgeable guides.

  • Mount Matebian Sunrise Hike: For those who enjoy early morning hikes, this tour is a must. Visitors will set out before dawn to reach the summit of Mount Matebian in time for an unforgettable sunrise over the Timorese countryside.

  • Diving and Snorkeling: Baucau is home to some of the best diving and snorkeling opportunities in Southeast Asia, with a range of pristine coral reefs and underwater caves to explore.

To make the most of your time in Baucau, we recommend the following week-long itinerary:

Day Activities

Food and Drink Scene of Baucau

No visit to Baucau is complete without trying some of the region’s delicious local cuisine and beverages. Here are some of the must-try dishes and drinks:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Ikan Tude: This delicious fish dish is a local specialty, usually served with rice and fresh vegetables.

  • Caldo de Peixe: This hearty fish soup is a popular lunchtime dish in Baucau, typically served with bread and hot chilies.

  • Carne de Porco com Molho de Tamarindo: This tangy pork dish is a fusion of Timorese and Portuguese cuisine, typically served with rice and a side salad.

Local Beverages

  • Cafe Timor: This strong, sweet coffee is a local favorite, made from locally-grown beans and typically served with sweetened condensed milk.

  • Tukir: This refreshing coconut water drink is a popular beverage in Baucau, known for its hydrating properties and delicious flavor.

Where to Stay in Baucau: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Baucau offers a range of accommodation options to suit any budget or preference, from cozy guesthouses to luxury hotels. Here are some of the best places to stay in Baucau:

  • Pousada de Baucau: This charming guesthouse is located in the heart of Baucau and offers comfortable rooms with modern amenities and a cozy courtyard garden.

  • Baucau Beach Resort: This luxurious hotel is located on Praia Areia Branca and offers stunning ocean views, spacious rooms, and a range of amenities including a swimming pool, restaurant, and bar.

  • Timor Plaza Hotel & Apartments: This modern hotel and apartment complex is located in the heart of Baucau and offers a range of spacious, well-appointed rooms and suites, as well as a rooftop pool and fitness center.

What to Buy in Baucau: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

For those looking to bring home a piece of Baucau, there are a range of souvenirs and local products to choose from. Here are some of the best options:

  • Handwoven Textiles: Baucau is known for its colorful, intricately woven textiles, which make for beautiful and unique souvenirs.

  • Coffee: Baucau and the surrounding region are home to some of the best coffee in Southeast Asia, with a range of locally-grown beans to choose from.

  • Timorese Crafts: From wood carvings to traditional pottery, there are a range of beautiful and authentic Timorese crafts to be found in Baucau and beyond.

Local Culture and Customs of Baucau

Baucau has a rich and unique culture, with a blend of Timorese and Portuguese traditions and customs. Visitors to the region should be aware of the following cultural practices and etiquette:

  • Respect the local people and their customs, particularly when visiting religious sites or participating in traditional ceremonies.

  • Dress modestly when visiting religious sites or interacting with local people.

  • Avoid taking photographs of people without their permission, particularly in rural areas.

  • Try to learn a few basic phrases in Tetum or Portugese, the local languages, as a sign of respect.

Best Time to Visit Baucau

The best time to visit Baucau is during the dry season, from May to September, when temperatures are mild and rainfall is minimal. It is worth noting, however, that Baucau can be visited year-round, with a range of indoor and outdoor activities to enjoy no matter the season.

Do’s and Don’ts of Baucau

To ensure a smooth and enjoyable trip to Baucau, be sure to follow these do’s and don’ts:

Do:

  • Respect the local people and their customs.

  • Dress modestly when visiting religious sites or interacting with local people.

  • Try the local cuisine and beverages, including Ikan Tude and Cafe Timor.

  • Learn a few basic phrases in Tetum or Portuguese as a sign of respect.

Don’t:

  • Take photos of people without their permission.

  • Wear revealing clothing or swimwear outside of designated beach areas.

  • Engage in any behavior that could be considered disrespectful or offensive to the local people.

Safety and Health

While Baucau is generally a safe and welcoming destination, visitors should take the necessary precautions to ensure their safety and health during their stay.

Safety in Baucau

  • Keep a close eye on your belongings and avoid carrying large amounts of cash or valuables.

  • Use caution when driving or walking on the roads, particularly outside of built-up areas.

  • Follow local customs and traditions, particularly when visiting religious sites or participating in traditional ceremonies.

Health in Baucau

  • Ensure that all vaccinations are up-to-date before traveling to Baucau.

  • Be sure to use insect repellent when outdoors to avoid mosquito-borne illnesses.

  • Drink only bottled or purified water to avoid water-borne illnesses.

Getting to Baucau: Transportation Options and Logistics

Getting to Baucau is relatively straightforward, with a range of transportation options available from major cities in East Timor.

Airports Near Baucau

The closest airport to Baucau is Baucau Airport, which is serviced by regular flights from Dili, the capital of East Timor.

Bus Stations Near Baucau

Baucau is relatively well-connected by bus, with regular services operating to and from major cities in East Timor.

Other Options

Taxis and private cars are also available for hire, with many local drivers offering competitive rates and flexible itineraries.

How to Get Around in Baucau

Baucau is a relatively small town that can easily be explored on foot or by bicycle. Taxis and private cars are also available for hire, with many local drivers offering competitive rates and flexible itineraries.

Where to Go Next from Baucau: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

For those looking to continue their travels in East Timor, there are many nearby destinations and excursions to consider. Some of the best options include:

  • Dili: The capital of East Timor is just a short drive from Baucau and offers a range of cultural and historical landmarks, as well as a bustling nightlife and dining scene.

  • Atauro Island: This charming island off the coast of East Timor is known for its pristine beaches, crystal-clear waters, and traditional fishing villages.

  • Jaco Island: This remote island off the coast of East Timor is a UNESCO World Heritage Site known for its stunning natural beauty and unique ecosystems.
