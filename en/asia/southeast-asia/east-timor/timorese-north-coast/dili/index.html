Introduction to Dili

Located on the lush Timorese North Coast, Dili is the capital city of Timor-Leste, also known as East Timor. A tropical paradise with turquoise waters, lush forests, and bright green mountains, Dili is one of the most vibrant and culturally-rich destinations in Southeast Asia.

With a fascinating history and a unique blend of cultures, Dili is home to numerous museums and historical sites, as well as natural attractions like stunning beaches, diving spots, and mountain hikes. Whether you’re looking for outdoor adventures or cultural experiences, Dili has it all.

List of Places to Visit in Dili

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Chega! Exhibition – This museum displays the violent history of Timorese struggle for independence from Portugal and Indonesia.

  • Museum and Cultural Center of East Timor – Exhibits artifacts and traditional textiles. The Portuguese-Timorese restaurant here is worth giving a try.

  • Arte Moris – Art gallery featuring the works of local artists of Dili.

  • Santa Cruz Cemetery – A historical burial site, turned into a memorial place for everyone who has given their life for the country’s independence.

Natural Attractions of Dili: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Cristo Rei Statue – This iconic statue sits on a hill and overlooks the city.

  • Tais Market – An outdoor market wrapped in local fabrics that sells local handcrafts to admire and purchase.

  • Atauro Island – A nearby small island known for its gorgeous coral reefs, offering excellent snorkeling, boating, diving and even hiking trails.

  • Tibar Beach – One of the best beaches in Dili, features shallow waters for swimmers, beach huts to relax in the shade, and few delicious seafood spots.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Dili

  • Mount Ramelau – The highest peak in Timor-Leste, providing incredible views from the top that can be accessed via a moderate hike or bike ride.

  • Matebian Mountain – A popular spot for hikers and mountain bikers, offers incredibly stunning views and a chance to meet welcoming locals living at the foothill.

  • Oecusse Trail – The trail covers a distance of 29km within a beautiful forest, featuring old ruins and traditional settlements.

Best Things to Do in Dili: Activities and Tours

  • Snorkeling and scuba diving – Explore the beautiful coral reefs and diverse marine life in the crystal-clear waters around beautiful beaches.

  • City Tours – Explore the rich cultural heritage and numerous local markets with a knowledgeable tour guide.

  • Sunset Yacht Cruise – Enjoy beautiful Timorese sunset views, and experience Timorese life from a different perspective.

Food and Drink Scene of Dili

Must-Try Dishes

  • Ikan Pepes – grilled fish seasoned in turmeric leaves and chili, cooked in banana leaves.

  • Sasate – grilled chicken or beef skewers, marinated in a spice paste.

  • Batar Da’an – corn is a staple food for Timorese, and locals usually serve it mixed with pumpkin and coconut cream.

Local Beverages

  • CaVana – a locally produced rum served at bars and clubs.

  • Haus Albrew – the local beer brewed in Dili.

  • Bir Bintang – Indonesian beer, usually found around Dili.

Where to Stay in Dili: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Novo Turismo Resort and Spa – A four-star hotel, perfectly situated on a beach to enjoy seafood and watersports.

  • Timor Plaza Hotel & Apartments – A unique addition for short or long stays that offers private apartments.

  • Pousada Baucau – A budget-friendly clean guesthouse that provides excellent value and hospitality.

What to Buy in Dili: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Tais – Handwoven sarongs or textiles, wrapped in colorful fabrics that Timorese make and have specific meanings.

  • Wood Carving – intricately carved and stain-colored wooden masks, miniature houses, and even animals.

  • Sandalwood Products – Timor-Leste is rich on sandalwood plantations, locals produce fragrances, candles, and other items from sandalwood.

Local Culture and Customs Dili

Timorese culture functions with mutual respect for parents, elders, and religious leaders. It’s polite to talk softly, dress modestly, and shave properly because cleanliness is one of the timorese people’s core values. Timorese are hospitable and welcoming, and it’s a norm to shake hands and smile broadly regardless of the time of the day.

Best Time to Visit Dili

From April to September, which is considered to be the dry season, is the best time to visit Dili. The average temperature falls around 30°C, making it bearable while enjoying the beach and other outdoor activities. The wet season, from October to March, is prone to monsoons, frequent rains, and occasional floods.

Do’s and Don’ts of Dili

Do:

  • Learn more about Timorese culture, heritage, and history to appreciate and respect the local culture and traditions.

  • Respect local customs and traditions. Avoid walking around in bikinis or indecent clothing, especially in religious or public places.

Don’t:

  • Don’t take photos or video footage of people or locals without permission, especially at religious sites and ceremonies.

  • Don’t engage in any illegal activities.

Safety and Health

Safety in Dili

Dili is generally one of the safest places to visit, but travelers are advised to practice common sense and caution with their belongings, especially in crowded areas. Always lock the doors of the car, practice safe walking habits, and stay aware of your surroundings.

Health in Dili

Check and update the vaccines of Hepatitis, Typhoid, and Tetanus before traveling to Dili. Mosquito-borne diseases, such as dengue fever, remain a concern. It’s essential to use mosquito repellent, wear protective clothing, and avoid being outdoors when the insects are most active.

Getting to Dili: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Dili

  • Presidente Nicolau Lobato International Airport – The country’s international airport, located 6km southeast of Dili.

  • Cakung Airport – Locals use this airport to fly from Dili to Atauro Island.

Bus Stations Near Dili

  • Tutuala – A local bus station near Dili, offering buses to several destinations in Timor-Leste.

  • Soibada – A local bus station near Dili, offering buses to several tourist destinations in Atauro Island.

Other Options

Renting a Car/Motorbike is a good option to cover nearby sites, and taxis are also available.

How to Get Around in Dili

Most local transportation in Dili comprises buses or minibusses that are locally known as Mikrolets which offer services between different suburbs and connects Dili with other major sites. Taxis and motorbikes can also be found and hired around Dili

Where to Go Next from Dili: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Atauro Island – As mentioned above, only a short boat ride from Dili, it’s perfect for those who love water sports, diving, and less commercialized destinations with serene beaches.

  • Aileu – A municipality, around 50km from Dili, with beautiful and unique landscapes.

  • Liquica – A coastal town located 20km to the west of Dili, known for some of the best beaches in Timor-Leste.

Conclusion

Dili is a tropical paradise that is rich in culture and natural beauty. With its fascinating history, unique landscapes, and rich culture, Timor-Leste is a highlight of any Southeast Asia itinerary. If you’re looking for an off-the-beaten-path destination and a chance to explore the road less traveled, make sure to add Dili to your travel list.
