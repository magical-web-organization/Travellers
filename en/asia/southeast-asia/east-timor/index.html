Introduction to East Timor

East Timor is a small island country located in Southeast Asia. It’s known for its rugged terrain, rich history, and stunning beaches. East Timor was once a Portuguese colony, and later became a part of Indonesia until it gained independence in 2002. The country has a mix of Portuguese, Indonesian, and native cultures, making it unique in the region. In this guide, we will explore the best places to visit, things to do, and tips for traveling in East Timor.

List of Places to Visit in East Timor

East Timor has no shortage of beautiful places to explore. Whether you want to visit cultural landmarks or enjoy outdoor adventures, there’s something for everyone. Here are some must-visit places in East Timor:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Chega! Exhibition - A museum that documents the country’s struggle for independence and human rights violations committed by the Indonesian military during the occupation.
  • Cristo Rei - This towering statue of Jesus stands at the foothills overlooking Dili, the capital city. You can climb up to the top for panoramic views of the city and the coast.
  • Tais Market - A colorful market in Dili selling traditional woven textiles, perfect for souvenirs or gifts.
  • Motael Church - The oldest church in Dili, built in 1952, with beautiful Portuguese colonial architecture.

Natural Attractions of East Timor: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Jacó Island - A small, uninhabited island off the coast of East Timor with crystal-clear water, white sandy beaches and home to some exciting marine life like turtles and dolphins.
  • Tutuala Beach - A secluded beach in the far east of the country known for its pristine waters, coral reefs, and dramatic coastal cliffs.
  • Mount Ramelau - The highest peak in the country with an elevation of 2963m. It’s a popular destination for hikers, and the summit offers breathtaking views.
  • Lake Modo - A stunning crater lake known for its emerald green waters and surrounding dense forests, which hold unique biodiversity.

Hiking/Biking Routes of East Timor

Hiking and biking are popular activities on this rugged island. Below are some recommended trails for hikers and bikers:

  • Cristo Rei to Dare - A challenging hike that takes you up the highest peak in the country with stunning views of the surrounding landscapes along the way.
  • Mt. Matebian - Another challenging hike to discover Timorese hidden isolated villages, wildlife, and stunningly beautiful landscapes.
  • Maubisse Loop - A popular biking route starting from the town of Maubisse, cycling among the hills and valleys of coffee plantations, forests, and peaks.

Best Things to Do in East Timor: Activities and Tours

In addition to hiking and biking, there are many other activities and tours to enjoy in East Timor. Here are some of the best:

  • Diving and Snorkeling in Atauro Island - One of the best secluded diving spots in the world, located a short distance from the mainland. The waters are crystal clear, and marine life is abundant, including corals, dolphins, and turtles.
  • Traditional Village Tours - East Timor is famous for its vibrant local culture, and you can experience it all by booking a traditional village tour. You will get to witness local customs and enjoy delicious food.
  • Coffee Farm Tours - East Timor has a rich tradition of producing high-quality coffee. You can go on tours of local farms and plantations to learn about the coffee-making process and sample some delicious blends.

Week-Long Itinerary for East Timor

If you’re planning on spending a week in East Timor, here’s a suggested itinerary:

Day Activity
1 Visit Dili, the capital city. Check out the museums and historic landmarks, and indulge in the unique blend of Timorese and Portuguese cuisine
2 Hike up Mt. Ramelau - Witness stunning panoramas of the coffee regions in the day and beautiful stars in the night from its highest peak.
3 Tour the beautiful island of Atauro. Enjoy superb diving, snorkeling and coral reef watching.
4 Coffee Farm Tour - Discover and indulge in traditional, delicious Timorese coffee
5 Travel to the eastern part of the country and explore the limestone cave systems of Tutuala
6 Visit Baucau, the second-largest city in the country. Explore the historic Portuguese churches and museum, and hike up to Mate Mussulo summit for some exquisite views
7 Head to Jacó Island and chill out on the white sand beaches or go turtle-watching.

Food and Drink Scene of East Timor

East Timor has a diverse and flavorful cuisine influenced by Portuguese and Indonesian cultures. Here are some must-try dishes:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Batar da’an - A traditional Timorese chicken and rice stew, cooked in a bamboo tube over an open flame.
  • Ikan Pari Asap - Grilled stingray fish, marinated in spicy garlic and shallot paste, served with rice.
  • Laksa - Curry noodle soup with chicken or seafood, coconut milk and a variety of spices.

Local Beverages

  • Caffe Timor - A delicious local coffee with sweet and chocolatey notes, grown in the country’s lush highlands.
  • Tutuala Palm Wine – A liquor distilled from the sap of palm trees in Tutuala. It’s potent but delicious.

Where to Stay in East Timor: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

East Timor has a variety of accommodations to fit different budgets and preferences. Here are some options:

  • Hotel Timor - A grand hotel in the heart of Dili with rooms overlooking the sea.
  • Pousada de Baucau - A charming guesthouse in Baucau with beautiful gardens and an outdoor pool.
  • Dive Timor-Lorosae - A popular hostel in Dili, mainly used as a diving center, with access to free basic dive courses.

What to Buy in East Timor: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Want to take a piece of East Timor home with you? Here are some souvenirs and local products to consider:

  • Tais - Bedspreads, shawls, and bags made of woven fabric with intricate designs.
  • Coffee Beans - High-quality coffee beans grown in the country’s fertile volcanic soil, perfect for coffee lovers back home.
  • Indigenous Musical Instruments - Explore local markets and museums to discover indigenous musical instruments like bamboo flutes, drums and lapitra guitars.

Local Culture and Customs of East Timor

East Timor has a unique blend of Portuguese, Indonesian, and native cultures. Traditional ceremonies and customs are an integral part of daily life in the country. Visitors are expected to be respectful of local traditions and dress modestly when visiting attractions and religious sites.

Best Time to Visit East Timor

The best time to visit East Timor is during the dry season, which runs from May to November. This is when the weather is mild and ideal for outdoor activities and sightseeing. The wet season, from December to April, can be quite intense, with heavy rains and the occasional cyclone.

Do’s and Don’ts of East Timor

Do:

  • Respect local culture and customs.
  • Dress modestly when visiting religious sites.
  • Carry cash as most places don’t accept credit cards.

Don’t:

  • Take photos without permission – Always ask before taking photos, especially in traditional villages.
  • Do drugs - possession and consumption of illegal drugs is strictly prohibited and penalized.

Safety and Health

Safety in East Timor

East Timor is generally a safe country to visit, though some precautions still need to be taken. Here are some tips:

  • Avoid traveling at night, unless absolutely necessary.
  • Stay in well-lit areas and avoid unlit places after dark.
  • Be aware of pickpockets and petty thefts and take necessary precautions.

Health in East Timor

East Timor has a low-risk for major diseases, but travelers should still take some precautions. Visitors should:

  • Drink bottled water and avoid tap water.
  • Visit a doctor before traveling to get all necessary vaccinations.
  • Carry mosquito repellents as the country is prone to mosquito-borne diseases.

Getting to East Timor: Transportation Options and Logistics

East Timor can only be accessed by air, as there are no roads connecting it to other countries. Here are ways to travel to East Timor:

Airports Near East Timor

  • Dili International Airport is the main international airport in the country

Bus Stations Near East Timor

  • Timor-Leste shares borders with Indonesia, but there are no common buses connecting between these nations.

Other Options

  • You can also travel to the country by sea from Australia or Indonesia.

How to Get Around in East Timor

Getting around in East Timor can be challenging as there is limited public transportation. The most common ways to travel are:

  • Taxis - The most convenient way to get around in the cities and towns. They are relatively affordable but be sure to negotiate the rate before starting your journey.
  • Buses - Scattered around in major destinations is a cheap option, but services are sporadic.
  • Mai-Talau minibusses - Cheap, crowded and often unsafe option to reach even remote areas.

Where to Go Next from East Timor: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

East Timor is a great base for exploring Southeast Asia in general. Here are some recommended destinations and excursions:

  • Bali, Indonesia - A popular tourist destination, known for its beautiful beaches, culture, and nightlife. You can reach Bali easily by taking a flight from Dili.
  • Komodo Island, Indonesia - Home of the famous Komodo dragons, and stunning beaches. You can take a flight from Dili to Labuan Bajo and start your exploration.
  • Darwin, Australia - A vibrant city in Australia’s Northern Territory, known for its natural beauty, wildlife, and aboriginal culture. You can fly to Darwin from Dili in just under two hours.

Conclusion

East Timor is a hidden gem with a unique blend of cultures, fascinating history, and stunning natural beauty. It’s an off-the-beaten-path destination that offers plenty to explore for adventurous travelers. Follow this comprehensive guide to experience the highlights of the country while staying safe and respecting local customs.
