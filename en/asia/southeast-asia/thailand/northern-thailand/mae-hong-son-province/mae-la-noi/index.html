Introduction to Mae La Noi

Nestled in Mae Hong Son Province in Northern Thailand, Mae La Noi is a lesser-known gem that offers tourists a glimpse into Northern Thai culture and natural beauty. A perfect getaway for eco-tourists, culture enthusiasts, and nature lovers, Mae La Noi’s lush mountains, serene landscape, and rich cultural heritage make it a must-visit destination in Thailand.

List of Places to Visit in Mae La Noi

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

For history buffs and culture enthusiasts, the following attractions offer deep insights into the life, customs, and traditions of the Northern Thai people:

  • Wat Phra Thart Doi Kong Moo: A beautiful Buddhist temple on the top of a hill overlooking the town, with stunning views and intricate architecture.
  • Ta-Pa Waterfall: A stunning waterfall with seven tiers and an ancient Buddhist sanctuary, the ideal spot for a serene and mindful escape.
  • Mae La Noi Cultural Museum: A must-visit for tourists to learn about the history and culture of the Northern Thai people.
  • Mae La Noi Royal Project: One of the country’s most successful agricultural initiatives, the Royal Project experiments with sustainable practices to improve the livelihood and income of local farmers.

Natural Attractions of Mae La Noi: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

For outdoor enthusiasts and nature lovers, Mae La Noi offers a plethora of scenic hiking trails, stunning viewpoints, and picturesque waterfalls to explore:

  • Doi Mae U-Kho Viewpoint: Known for its picturesque views of Mae La Noi, Mae Sariang, and the surrounding mountain ranges.
  • Doi Huai Mae Sak: Boasts stunning Panoramic views of the Chao Mae Thap Thim National Park.
  • Doi Chang: A peaceful mountainous town situated amidst the lush greenery of coffee plantations, best known for its stunning views of Mae La Noi.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Mae La Noi

For those seeking adventure, the following hiking and biking routes offer a perfect opportunity to explore the beauty of Mae La Noi:

  • Mae La Noi-Doi Khun Mae Tamoe route: A beautiful route, taking bikers across some stunning landscapes with picturesque waterfalls and hilltop viewpoints.
  • Mae La Noi-Pai route: A long and challenging route, but the stunning views are definitely worth the effort.
  • Mae La Noi-Pai Loop: A 100km loop with challenging routes and views of the stunning Pai River Basin.

Best Things to Do in Mae La Noi: Activities and Tours

With so much natural beauty and cultural landmarks, there is no shortage of activities and tours to indulge in Mae La Noi. Some of the best things to do in Mae La Noi are:

  • Bathe in hot springs: Mae La Noi is known for its therapeutic hot springs, which are perfect for soaking in, relaxing, and enjoying the natural beauty.
  • Visit the Karen Hill Tribe Villages: Experience the local culture, customs, and traditions of the Karen hill tribe who have lived in the area for centuries.
  • Go trekking: Explore the surrounding mountain ranges, waterfalls, and coffee plantations on foot or by bike.

Week-Long Itinerary for Mae La Noi

With so much to see and do, here’s a week-long itinerary to help you plan the perfect trip to Mae La Noi:

  • Day 1: Visit the Mae La Noi Cultural Museum, Wat Phra Thart Doi Kong Moo, and Mae La Noi Royal Project.
  • Day 2: Take a dip in the Ta-Pa Waterfall and explore Doi Mae U-Kho viewpoint.
  • Day 3: Trek to the Karen Hill Tribe Villages and experience the local culture, customs, and traditions.
  • Day 4: Explore the lush greenery of Doi Chang, best known for its stunning views of Mae La Noi.
  • Day 5: Take in the picturesque views of Doi Huai Mae Sak and learn about the ongoing agricultural research in the Chao Mae Thap Thim National Park.
  • Day 6: Soak in the therapeutic hot springs and relax at the Mae La Noi wellness resorts.
  • Day 7: Take a long and challenging bike ride through Mae La Noi-Pai Loop, with stunning views of the Pai River Basin.

Food and Drink Scene of Mae La Noi

No trip is complete without indulging in the scrumptious local delicacies offered in the region. Experience the rich Northern Thai cuisine at the following must-visit places:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Khao Soi Mae La Noi: A signature dish, served with soft hand-made noodles, a generous amount of curry, and crunchy texture of fried noodles.
  • Stir Fried Crispy Rice Noodle with egg: A crispy and delicious stir-fried noodle dish that is usually served with vegetables and chicken.
  • Nam Prik Mae La Noi: A spicy dish served with local vegetables and crispy pork.

Local Beverages

  • Doi Chang Coffee: Grown on the Doi Chang mountains, the aromatic coffee beans are brewed to perfection and is a must-try beverage in Mae La Noi.
  • Mae La Noi Lemon Tea: A refreshing drink made from locally grown lemons and cane sugar, perfect for a hot summer’s day.

Where to Stay in Mae La Noi: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

The following are some of the best places to stay in Mae La Noi:

  • Bamboo Hut: An eco-friendly resort with a beautiful garden offering a serene ambiance to relax.
  • Wiang Ngern Hotel: A luxurious hotel located amidst the mountains, offering stunning views and a peaceful ambiance.
  • Yotaka Hotel Mae Hong Son: A budget-friendly yet comfortable hotel, located in the heart of the town and close to all major attractions.

What to Buy in Mae La Noi: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Make sure to bring back some of the famous Mae La Noi coffee, locally made handicrafts, and hand-woven fabrics. The following are the best places to shop:

  • Doi Chang: The place to buy the famous Doi Chang coffee.
  • Mae La Noi North Tour: A perfect place to buy locally made handicrafts and woven fabrics.

Local Culture and Customs of Mae La Noi

The Karen hill tribe, the largest ethnic group in Northern Thailand, have their unique customs, dance, music, clothing, and food. It is a beautiful experience to learn and immerse oneself in the rich culture and heritage of the region.

Best Time to Visit Mae La Noi

The best time to visit Mae La Noi is between November to February. The weather is cool, clear, and dry, with daytime temperatures around 20-25 degrees Celsius. March to May is not recommended as the temperature and humidity can be high and results in sticky weather. June to October is the rainy season, and the region tends to experience frequent downpours.

Do’s and Don’ts of Mae La Noi

Respect local customs and traditions by following these ‘Do’s and Don’ts:

Do:

  • Dress modestly when visiting temples and cultural landmarks.
  • Remove your shoes when entering any sacred space.
  • Learn a few simple Thai phrases to show respect to locals.

Don’t:

  • Do not touch or take photographs of the monks or their belongings without their permission.
  • Do not use your feet to point at people or objects, as it is considered rude.
  • Do not speak loudly or raise your voice in public spaces.

Safety and Health

Safety in Mae La Noi

Mae La Noi is known for being a safe region with a low crime rate. However, it is always better to take the necessary precautions to ensure your safety:

  • Avoid carrying valuables when you travel or carry them with you at all times.
  • Stay vigilant while traveling alone late at night.
  • Be conscious of traffic while walking or biking on the roads.

Health in Mae La Noi

It is always better to have travel insurance when traveling in Mae La Noi. In case of any medical emergency, there is a small hospital and a few clinics in the town. However, for any serious medical treatment, it is advisable to travel to Mae Hong Son, which has a better-equipped hospital.

Getting to Mae La Noi: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Mae La Noi

The nearest airport to Mae La Noi is Mae Hong Son Airport, which is approximately 56 kilometers away from the town. Taxis and buses are easily accessible at the airport.

Bus Stations Near Mae La Noi

Buses to Mae La Noi are available from Chiang Mai Arcade Bus Station, which is approximately 190 kilometers away. Buses also run from Mae Hong Son Town.

Other Options

Renting a car or a motorbike is a better option to travel to Mae La Noi as it provides flexibility and freedom to explore the scenic routes and surrounding regions.

How to Get Around in Mae La Noi

The best way to get around Mae La Noi is by renting a car or a motorbike. Taxis and tuk-tuks are also available, but they are not as prevalent as in the more prominent regions of Thailand.

Where to Go Next from Mae La Noi: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

If you have more time to explore, the following nearby destinations are worth visiting:

  • Pai: A mountainous town that offers a laid-back vibe, beautiful scenery, and plenty of outdoor activities.
  • Mae Hong Son: A picturesque and tranquil city with lots of temples, hot springs, and beautiful lakes surrounding the town.

Conclusion

Mae La Noi is a hidden gem that offers something for everyone. Whether you are seeking adventure, culture, or natural beauty, this beautiful town in Northern Thailand is sure to leave you mesmerized. So, plan your visit and explore the beauty of Mae La Noi!
