Introduction to Mae Sariang

Mae Sariang is a scenic and peaceful district located in Mae Hong Son Province, Northern Thailand. The town is situated along the banks of the Yuam River and surrounded by lush hills and mountains covered in dense forests. The location offers a perfect getaway from the hustle and bustle of city life, attracting tourists who seek a relaxing and quiet retreat.

Mae Sariang is a hidden gem, offering visitors a chance to explore the local culture, visit natural attractions, hike in the mountains, and taste delicious local food. The town is also the home of several ethnic groups, including Thai Yai, Karen, and other hill tribes, making it a great place to learn about the diversity of the region.

List of Places to Visit in Mae Sariang

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Wat Chong Kham Temple - A beautiful temple built in the Burmese Shan style, housing a huge golden Buddha statue and intricate wood carvings.
  • City Pillar Shrine - A sacred shrine dedicated to the city’s patron saint and believed to protect the town from harm.
  • Mae Sariang Folk Museum - A museum showcasing the local culture and history of the town and the surrounding area.

Natural Attractions of Mae Sariang: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Mae Usu Cave - A vast and mysterious cave system with unique rock formations, stalactites, and stalagmites.
  • Salawin National Park - A vast national park with lush forests, stunning waterfalls, and rare wildlife such as gibbons, wild cats, and deer.
  • Doi Kiew Lom Scenic Point - A viewpoint located on top of a mountain that provides breathtaking views of the town, river, and surrounding hills.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Mae Sariang

Mae Sariang is a hiker’s paradise with several hiking trails leading through the picturesque landscape. The trails vary in difficulty, offering something for hikers of all abilities. Some of the popular routes are:

  • Doi Pha Hom Pok National Park - A steep climb that rewards hikers with stunning views of the surrounding hills and valleys.
  • Ban Rak Thai - A scenic trail that passes through tea plantations and small hill tribe villages.
  • Mae Sariang - Mae Jaem Loop - A challenging bike route through mountainous terrain, offering stunning views and scenic stops along the way.

Best Things to Do in Mae Sariang: Activities and Tours

  • Visit the local markets and try local street food.
  • Learn about the local culture and take part in ethnic village tours.
  • Relax in the natural hot springs and spas.
  • Take a cooking class and learn how to cook local dishes.
  • Go bird watching and see rare species that can only be found in the region.

Week-Long Itinerary for Mae Sariang

Food and Drink Scene of Mae Sariang

Must-Try Dishes

  • Khao Soi - A northern Thai dish made with crispy egg noodles, coconut milk, curry, and chicken.
  • Gaeng Om - A soup made with vegetables, meat, and spices.
  • Tam Maak Hoong - A spicy papaya salad with tomatoes and peanuts.

Local Beverages

  • Mae Sariang Coffee - A locally grown coffee variety that is robust in flavor.
  • Longan Juice - A refreshing drink made from longan fruit.

Where to Stay in Mae Sariang: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

There are several accommodation options in Mae Sariang, catering to both budget and luxury travelers. Some of the popular choices are:

  • Baan Rom Mai Guest House - A cozy and affordable guest house located in the city center.
  • Riverhouse Resort - A luxurious riverside resort, offering breathtaking views of the Yuam River.
  • Pai Mai Thong Homestay - A homestay that offers an authentic cultural experience, located in a small Karen village.

What to Buy in Mae Sariang: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Hand-woven textiles and clothing made by hill tribe communities.
  • Local coffee and tea.
  • Handicrafts made from bamboo and wood.

Local Culture and Customs of Mae Sariang

Mae Sariang is home to several ethnic communities, and visitors should observe the local customs and traditions. Some of the key aspects of the local culture are:

  • Respect for religion and spirituality.
  • Respect for elders and community leaders.
  • Modest dress when visiting temples and sacred sites.

Best Time to Visit Mae Sariang

The best time to visit Mae Sariang is from November to February, when the weather is cool and dry. The area experiences a monsoon season from May to October, which can cause disruptions in outdoor activities.

Do’s and Don’ts of Mae Sariang

Do:

  • Dress modestly when visiting temples and other sacred sites.
  • Try local street food and learn about local cuisine.
  • Respect the local culture and traditions.

Don’t:

  • Disrespect religious sites or local customs.
  • Take photos without permission.
  • Litter or damage the environment.

Safety and Health

Safety in Mae Sariang

Mae Sariang is a generally safe place to visit, but visitors should take precautions and be aware of their surroundings. Some tips to ensure safety are:

  • Keep valuable belongings in a safe place.
  • Use an authorized tour operator when participating in outdoor activities.
  • Stay on designated trails when hiking or cycling.

Health in Mae Sariang

Visitors should take precautionary measures when traveling to Mae Sariang, as in any other part of the world. Some tips to ensure good health are:

  • Drink bottled or boiled water.
  • Use insect repellent to prevent mosquito bites.
  • Pack a first aid kit that includes necessary medications and supplies.

Getting to Mae Sariang: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Mae Sariang

The nearest airport to Mae Sariang is Chiang Mai International Airport, which offers flights to several international and domestic destinations. From Chiang Mai, visitors can take a bus or a private car to Mae Sariang, which takes around 5 hours.

Bus Stations Near Mae Sariang

Mae Sariang has a bus station that connects it to other parts of the region, including Chiang Mai, Pai, and Mae Hong Son. Buses run several times per day, and the journey times vary depending on the destination.

Other Options

Visitors can also hire a private car or a motorcycle to travel to Mae Sariang. However, it is important to be aware of the local traffic rules and regulations, especially when driving a motorcycle.

How to Get Around in Mae Sariang

Mae Sariang is a small town and can easily be explored on foot. Visitors can also rent bicycles or motorcycles to explore the surrounding areas.

Where to Go Next from Mae Sariang: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Pai

Pai is a scenic town located around 2 hours away from Mae Sariang. The town is known for its laid-back atmosphere, scenic landscapes, and trekking opportunities.

Mae Hong Son

Mae Hong Son is the capital of Mae Hong Son Province and is located around 3 hours away from Mae Sariang. The town is known for its stunning temples, scenic landscapes, and hill tribe communities.

Conclusion

Mae Sariang is a hidden gem in Northern Thailand, offering visitors a perfect getaway from the hustle and bustle of city life. The town is known for its natural beauty, rich culture, and diverse ethnic communities. Visitors can explore the local surroundings, hike in the mountains, and taste delicious local food. Plan your trip and visit Mae Sariang to experience the serene and peaceful side of Thailand.
