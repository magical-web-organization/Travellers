Introduction to Pai

Located in Mae Hong Son Province, in Northern Thailand, the charming town of Pai is a hidden gem that offers a unique combination of natural beauty, outdoor adventures, cultural richness, and laid-back vibes. Despite its small size, Pai has quickly become a popular destination among backpackers, nature lovers, and those looking for a break from the hustle and bustle of city life.

In this ultimate guide to Pai, we will take you on a tour of the best things to do, places to visit, food to eat, and more in this beautiful town. Whether you’re planning a short visit or an extended stay, get ready to fall in love with Pai.

List of Places to Visit in Pai

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Pai Memorial Bridge: A historical landmark that dates back to World War II, the Pai Memorial Bridge was built by the Japanese army with the help of local villagers. The bridge, which is made of teak wood, is a popular spot for taking pictures and admiring the scenic views of the Pai River.

  • Wat Klang: Also known as the “temple in the middle”, Wat Klang is a beautiful Buddhist temple that dates back to the 19th century. Its unique design, which features a Burmese-style chedi and a Thai-style ubosot, is a testament to the town’s multicultural heritage.

  • Pai Night Market: Located in the heart of Pai, the night market is a lively and colorful gathering place that showcases the best of local food, crafts, and entertainment. Open every night from 6pm onwards, it’s a must-visit for those who want to experience the authentic vibe of Pai.

Natural Attractions of Pai: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Pai Canyon: Also known as Kong Lan, Pai Canyon is a natural wonder that offers breathtaking views of the surrounding mountains and valleys. Visitors can hike along the narrow ridges and cliffs of the canyon, and enjoy the sunset from one of the many lookout points.

  • Tha Pai Hot Springs: Located about 8 km from Pai town, Tha Pai Hot Springs is a natural thermal pool that is believed to have healing properties. Visitors can soak in the warm water, surrounded by lush jungle and picturesque scenery.

  • Mor Paeng Waterfall: A popular spot for picnics and swimming, Mor Paeng Waterfall is a series of small cascades that flow into natural pools. The waterfall is located about 6 km from Pai town, and can be reached via a short hike through the forest.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Pai

Pai is a paradise for outdoor enthusiasts, with plenty of hiking and biking trails that offer stunning views of the landscape. Here are some of the best routes:

  • Yun Lai Viewpoint: This 3 km hike takes you to a panoramic viewpoint that offers breathtaking views of Pai and the surrounding mountains. The trail starts at Pai Canyon and takes about 1.5 hours to complete.

  • The Mae Yen Waterfall Hike: This challenging 7 km hike takes you through dense jungle and bamboo forest, and leads to a beautiful waterfall that is almost hidden from view. The hike takes about 3.5 hours to complete.

  • Pai to Mae Hong Son Bike Route: This scenic 120 km route takes you through mountains, valleys, and rice paddies, and offers a unique opportunity to experience the beauty of Northern Thailand. You can rent a bike in Pai town and explore the route at your own pace.

Best Things to Do in Pai: Activities and Tours

  • Cooking Classes: Learn how to cook delicious Thai dishes with the help of local chefs, using fresh ingredients from the local market.

  • Zipline Adventure: Experience the thrill of flying over the jungle canopy on a zipline tour, which offers panoramic views of Pai and the surrounding mountains.

  • Elephant Sanctuary: Visit a local elephant sanctuary and learn about the importance of elephant conservation. You’ll have the opportunity to feed, bathe, and interact with the elephants in a natural setting.

Food and Drink Scene of Pai

Must-Try Dishes

  • Khao Soi: A Northern Thai specialty, Khao Soi is a curry noodle soup that is usually made with chicken, coconut milk, and spices.

  • Moo Krata: This popular BBQ dish involves cooking thin slices of pork, beef, and vegetables on a hot plate at your table, and dipping them in a spicy sauce.

  • Sticky Rice and Mango: This classic dessert involves pairing sweet, ripe mango with glutinous rice and coconut cream.

Local Beverages

  • Cha Yen: A Thai iced tea that is made with black tea, spices, and sweetened condensed milk.

  • Chang Beer: A popular Thai beer that is refreshing and pairs well with spicy food.

  • Fruit Shakes: Made with fresh local fruits and ice, these shakes come in a variety of flavors, from mango to passionfruit.

Where to Stay in Pai: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Pai offers a range of accommodation options, from budget-friendly hostels to luxury resorts. Here are some of the best:

  • Pai Village Boutique Resort & Farm: A luxurious resort that offers panoramic views of the mountains and rice paddies.

  • Common Grounds Pai: A cozy hostel that offers a range of accommodation options, from dorm rooms to private bungalows.

  • Baantawan Guesthouse: A charming guesthouse that is located near the main street of Pai town, and offers comfortable rooms and a relaxing garden.

What to Buy in Pai: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Hill Tribe Crafts: Pai is home to several hill tribe communities, who produce a range of traditional handicrafts, from woven textiles to silver jewelry.

  • Local Snacks: Try some of the local snacks, such as krapao, a fried snack made with taro, and kanom jeen nam ngiew, a rice noodle dish with spicy pork broth.

  • Tea and Coffee: Pai is known for its high-quality tea and coffee, which you can find at local markets and coffee shops.

Local Culture and Customs of Pai

Pai is a multicultural town that is home to several indigenous communities, including the Shan, Lisu, Lahu, and Karen. Visitors are encouraged to respect local customs and dress modestly when visiting temples and other cultural sites.

Best Time to Visit Pai

The best time to visit Pai is during the cool season, from November to February, when the weather is mild and dry. March to May can be hot and humid, while the rainy season from June to October can bring floods and landslides.

Do’s and Don’ts of Pai

Do:

  • Dress modestly when visiting temples and other cultural sites.
  • Respect local customs and traditions.
  • Try the local food and drink.
  • Explore the natural beauty of Pai.

Don’t:

  • Damage the environment or wildlife.
  • Disrespect local customs or beliefs.
  • Engage in any illegal activities.
  • Leave trash behind.

Safety and Health

Safety in Pai

Pai is generally a safe town, but visitors should take precautions against theft and petty crime, especially in crowded areas and at night. Travelers should also be careful when driving or riding bikes on the narrow and winding roads.

Health in Pai

Visitors to Pai should take precautions against mosquito-borne illnesses, such as dengue fever and malaria. It’s also recommended to drink bottled water and avoid street food that may cause stomach upset.

Getting to Pai: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Pai

The nearest airport to Pai is Chiang Mai International Airport, which is about 135 km away. From there, visitors can take a bus or private taxi to Pai town.

Bus Stations Near Pai

Pai has a small bus station that offers services to nearby destinations, including Chiang Mai and Mae Hong Son. Some bus companies also offer direct services to Bangkok and other major cities in Thailand.

Other Options

Visitors to Pai can also hire a private car or taxi from Chiang Mai or other nearby cities. Alternatively, it’s possible to rent a motorbike or bicycle in Pai town and explore the town and surrounding areas at your own pace.

How to Get Around in Pai

The best way to get around in Pai is by motorbike or bicycle, which can be rented from several shops and guesthouses in town. Alternatively, there are also red songthaews (pickup trucks with bench seating) that offer shared transportation to nearby attractions and villages.

Where to Go Next from Pai: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Mae Hong Son: About 3 hours by road from Pai, Mae Hong Son is a picturesque town that is known for its century-old temples, beautiful lake, and stunning scenery.

  • Chiang Mai: A popular destination in Northern Thailand, Chiang Mai offers a mix of culture, history, and nature, and is home to several beautiful temples, markets, and outdoor adventures.

  • Chiang Rai: Known for its stunning mountains, waterfalls, and parks, Chiang Rai is a hidden gem that offers a unique combination of natural beauty and cultural richness.

Conclusion

Pai is a charming town that offers a unique blend of nature, culture, and adventure. From its stunning viewpoints and natural attractions to its delicious food and cultural richness, Pai is a must-visit destination for anyone traveling to Northern Thailand. With this comprehensive guide, we hope you’ll be inspired to plan your own trip to Pai, and experience all that this hidden gem has to offer.
