Introduction to Mae Salong

Nestled in Northern Thailand’s mountainous Chiang Rai province, Mae Salong offers a glimpse of traditional Chinese culture and natural beauty untouched by mass tourism. Formerly a village occupied by Chinese soldiers who fled from the Communist Revolution, Mae Salong is now a treasure trove of vibrant culture and scenic beauty. This town has something for everyone, whether you’re looking for adventure, relaxation, or cultural immersion.

List of Places to Visit in Mae Salong

Here are some must-visit places in Mae Salong:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Mae Salong History Museum: Discover Mae Salong’s history and culture through the museum’s exhibits, including traditional costumes, pottery, and old photographs.
  • Santikhiri Museum: Explore the history, culture, and customs of the Yunnanese, a Chinese ethnic group who resettled in Mae Salong.
  • Doi Mae Salong: A stunning mountain peak with a stunning view of Mae Salong and the surrounding area.

Natural Attractions of Mae Salong: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Doi Tung: A beautiful and peaceful botanical garden filled with stunning orchids.
  • Doi Chang Mub: A scenic spot where you can witness the breathtaking sunrise in Mae Salong.
  • Doi Pha Tang: A magnificent mountaintop that showcases stunning views of the surrounding countryside.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Mae Salong

  • Mae Salong Nature Trail: This trail is perfect for nature lovers. The trail leads you through thick forests, freshwater creeks, and stunning waterfalls.
  • Doi Mae Salong Biking Trail: Mountain bikers will love this exciting and challenging biking trail that offers stunning scenic views and thrills.

Best Things to Do in Mae Salong: Activities and Tours

  • Tea Plantation Tour: Discover how tea is harvested and processed. You can also enjoy a tea tasting session afterward.
  • Visit the Hill Tribe Village: Explore the local hill tribe villages to learn about their traditional customs, daily life, and handicrafts.
  • Chinese Cultural Dance Show: Experience the rich Chinese culture and tradition by watching the Chinese cultural dance show.

Week-Long Itinerary for Mae Salong

Day 1-2: Explore Mae Salong’s history and culture through the museums and historical sites, such as the Mae Salong History Museum and Santikhiri Museum. Day 3-4: Dive into Mae Salong’s stunning natural beauty by visiting Doi Tung and the Mae Salong Nature Trail. Day 5-6: Take a break from your busy schedule and relax while enjoying the scenic views of Doi Mae Salong and Doi Chang Mub. Day 7: Discover the local culture and tradition by visiting the hill tribes and Chinese cultural dance show.

Food and Drink Scene of Mae Salong

Mae Salong offers a variety of traditional Chinese dishes and Thai food. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Khao Soi: A traditional northern Thai soup made with egg noodles, coconut milk, and curry.
  • Barbecued meat: Chinese-styled meat barbecue featuring pork, chicken, and beef skewers.
  • Sweet potato balls: Deep-fried sweet potato balls that are crispy outside and soft inside.

Local Beverages

  • Oolong tea: Fresh tea picked from local tea plantations that tastes earthy and fragrant.
  • Mulberry juice: Sweet and unique in taste juice made from freshly harvested mulberries.

Where to Stay in Mae Salong: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Mae Salong provides a range of stay options that offer a cultural experience with modern amenities. Here are some recommendations:

  • Baan Ben Home Stay: A cultural and homestay experience that immerses you in the hill tribe’s daily lifestyle. It provides private rooms with a shared bathroom.
  • The Sufa Boutique Hotel: A Chinese-inspired boutique hotel featuring stunning mountain views, spacious rooms, and an outdoor pool.
  • Phufa Waree Chiangrai Resort: A luxurious resort that blends modernity with rustic Lanna-inspired decor.

What to Buy in Mae Salong: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Mae Salong offers some traditional handicrafts and products that you can bring home. Here are our recommendations:

  • Tea: Mae Salong’s tea is famous for its quality and fragrance.
  • Handicrafts: A wide range of handicrafts such as traditional hill tribe bags, batik cloths, and Chinese embroidered blankets are available at the local markets.

Local Culture and Customs of Mae Salong

Mae Salong is a blend of Thai, Chinese, and hill tribe cultures. The village celebrates many festivals and traditions, including Chinese New Year and the Songkran Festival. Hill tribe people celebrate their customs and beliefs, including the “rocket festival,” “flower festival,” and various harvest festivals.

Best Time to Visit Mae Salong

The best time to visit Mae Salong is between October and February, when the weather is cooler and drier.

Do’s and Don’ts of Mae Salong

Do:

  • Dress modestly, especially when visiting temples and cultural sites.
  • Be respectful and polite to local people.
  • Learn some Thai and Chinese phrases to help you communicate better.

Don’t:

  • Don’t touch the head of hill tribe people because it is considered impolite.
  • Don’t enter temples and other religious places with shoes and socks.
  • Avoid public displays of affection.

Safety and Health

Safety in Mae Salong

Mae Salong is generally a safe place to visit. However, travelers should take usual precautions such as keeping your belongings safe and not walking alone in remote areas at night.

Health in Mae Salong

Travelers should be updated on routine vaccines such as measles, mumps, and rubella (MMR), diphtheria, tetanus, and pertussis (DTP). Before traveling, it is best to check with a doctor for more information.

Getting to Mae Salong: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Mae Salong

The closest airport to Mae Salong is Chiang Rai International Airport, which is around 40km away. From the airport, travelers can take a taxi or a shuttle bus to Mae Salong.

Bus Stations Near Mae Salong

The nearest bus station is the Chiang Rai Bus Station. Buses from Bangkok’s Morchit Bus Terminal to Chiang Rai Bus Station are available daily.

Other Options

Renting a car or hiring a driver is an excellent option for those who want the flexibility to explore the area in their own time.

How to Get Around in Mae Salong

Renting a motorcycle or bicycle is the most popular way to get around in Mae Salong. Alternatively, you can hire a car or a driver. Tuk-tuks and songthaews are also available.

Where to Go Next from Mae Salong: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Mae Salong is a great place to visit on its own. However, it’s also near other destinations that are worth exploring:

  • Chiang Rai: If you’re into modern art, temples, and lively night markets, spend a day or two in this nearby city.
  • Doi Mae Salong Nature Reserve: Experience the pure nature of Northern Thailand and spend a day hiking, biking, or camping in a serene forest setting.
  • Golden Triangle: A place where the borders of Myanmar, Laos, and Thailand meet, the Golden Triangle is famous for its historic opium trade and unique cultures.

Conclusion

Mae Salong is a small yet charming mountainous village filled with natural beauty and culture. If you’re interested in traditions, tea, and natural beauty, Mae Salong should be on your bucket list. From the hill tribe culture to the Chinese heritage and breathtaking views, Mae Salong is a unique jewel that rewards travelers who step off the beaten track.
