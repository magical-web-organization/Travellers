Phrae is a hidden gem tucked away in the Northern River Valleys of Thailand. This small city is often overlooked by travelers, but it is rich in culture and history with plenty of natural beauty to explore. In this comprehensive travel guide, we will showcase the best things to do and places to visit in Phrae, from cultural attractions to outdoor adventures, and where to stay and eat.

Introduction to Phrae

Phrae is a small city in Northern Thailand, located in the Phrae Province. It is known for its rich history, cultural traditions, and beautiful natural scenery. The city has a population of around 20,000 and is surrounded by rolling hills and lush forests.

List of Places to Visit in Phrae

There are plenty of places to visit and things to do in Phrae. Here are some of our top recommendations.

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Phrae is a city with a fascinating history, and there are many museums and historical places to explore. The City Walls and Gates, dating back to the 13th century, are a must-see. The Vongburi House Museum is another great attraction, showcasing Phrae’s traditional Lanna architecture and the life of a wealthy teak trading family.

Natural Attractions of Phrae: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Phrae is also known for its natural beauty. The Mae Yom National Park is a great place to explore, with jungle trails alongside the Yom River and a stunning waterfall. The Phae Muang Phi Geological Park is another must-see, with unique rock formations that resemble the surface of the moon. The Doi Phu Kha National Park offers incredible hiking and trekking opportunities, from easy walks to challenging multi-day treks.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Phrae

Phrae offers several hiking and biking routes that take you through its beautiful rural landscapes. You can hike along the Mae Yom River or explore the scenic rice paddies on a bicycle tour. The Doi Phu Kha National Park also offers several hiking routes, ranging from easy walks to challenging treks for experienced hikers.

Best Things to Do in Phrae: Activities and Tours

In addition to hiking and biking, there are plenty of other activities to enjoy in Phrae. You can take a cooking class to learn how to make traditional Thai dishes, or visit a local farm to see how rice and other crops are grown. There are also several tours available, such as a walking tour of Phrae to learn about the city’s history and culture or a guided trek in the Doi Phu Kha National Park.

Week-Long Itinerary for Phrae

If you’re planning a week-long trip to Phrae, here’s a sample itinerary:

Day 1: Arrive in Phrae, explore the City Walls and Gates, visit the Vongburi House Museum Day 2: Hike along the Mae Yom River, visit the Mae Yom National Park Day 3: Take a bike tour through the rice paddies, visit the Phae Muang Phi Geological Park Day 4: Take a cooking class, visit a local farm Day 5: Explore the Doi Phu Kha National Park, go on a guided trek Day 6: Visit a local temple, take a walking tour of Phrae Day 7: Relax and enjoy a spa day, taste local cuisine

Food and Drink Scene of Phrae

Phrae’s food scene is full of local flavors and unique dishes. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages to sample.

Must-Try Dishes

Khao Soi is a popular northern Thai dish that consists of egg noodles, coconut milk, curry paste, and vegetables. Nam Ngiao is a spicy noodle soup with pork or beef, tomatoes, chili, and garlic. Sai Oua is a Northern Thai sausage made with pork and herbs.

Local Beverages

Nam Phrae Mae is a refreshing beverage made from boiled rice, which is then fermented with yeast and left to age. Nam Manao is a sweet and sour drink made from local limes and sugar.

Where to Stay in Phrae: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

There are plenty of accommodation options in Phrae, from affordable hostels to luxury hotels. Here are some recommendations:

Budget-friendly: Heyday Guesthouse, Phrae Garden Boutique Hotel

Mid-range: The Chill House, S. Richy Hotel

Luxury: Maeyom Palace Hotel, Huern Na Na Boutique Hotel

What to Buy in Phrae: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Phrae is known for its traditional handicrafts, including pottery, silk weaving, and woodcarving. Some popular souvenirs to bring home include handcrafted ceramics, textiles, and woven baskets.

Local Culture and Customs of Phrae

Phrae has a rich history and culture, and it’s important to respect local customs and traditions. Some customs to keep in mind include dressing modestly when visiting temples, removing your shoes before entering homes or temples, and always showing respect to elders.

Best Time to Visit Phrae

The best time to visit Phrae is from November until February when the weather is cooler and drier. The rainy season is from May until October when the city can experience heavy rainfall and flooding.

Do’s and Don’ts of Phrae

Do:

  • Respect local customs and traditions
  • Dress modestly when visiting temples
  • Try local cuisine and beverages
  • Explore the natural beauty of the area

Don’t:

  • Speak loudly or make unnecessary noise in public areas
  • Disrespect local customs or religious sites
  • Get involved with illegal activities or drugs

Safety and Health

Safety in Phrae

Phrae is generally a safe city to visit, but it’s important to take necessary precautions such as keeping an eye on your belongings and avoiding walking alone at night.

Health in Phrae

Be sure to check with your doctor or travel clinic before visiting Phrae as vaccinations may be required. It’s also important to stay hydrated and protect yourself from mosquitoes to prevent mosquito-borne illnesses like dengue fever and Zika virus.

Getting to Phrae: Transportation Options and Logistics

Phrae can be reached by plane, bus, or car from various major cities in Thailand.

Airports Near Phrae

The nearest airport is Phrae Airport, which has flights to and from Bangkok.

Bus Stations Near Phrae

Buses run from Bangkok’s Northern Bus Terminal to Phrae Bus Terminal. Buses from Chiang Mai also stop at Phrae Bus Terminal.

Other Options

You can also rent a car or hire a private driver to get to Phrae from nearby cities.

How to Get Around in Phrae

Phrae is a small city that can easily be explored on foot or by bicycle. Taxis and tuk-tuks are also available for short distances.

Where to Go Next from Phrae: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

There are several nearby destinations and excursions that can be visited from Phrae, including:

  • Chiang Mai: A bustling city with cultural landmarks and natural beauty
  • Lampang: A charming city with traditional Lanna architecture and a famous elephant conservation center
  • Sukhothai: A UNESCO World Heritage site with ancient ruins and temples

Conclusion

Phrae is a beautiful and unique destination in Northern Thailand that offers a glimpse into the country’s rich history and culture. With its natural beauty, delicious cuisine, and friendly locals, Phrae is a must-visit for any traveler seeking an authentic Thai experience.
