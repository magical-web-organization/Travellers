Introduction to Hang Chat

Tucked away in the Northern River Valleys of Thailand lies the serene town of Hang Chat. With its idyllic surroundings, rich cultural history, and myriad of adventure activities, it’s no wonder this hidden gem has become a popular destination for travelers looking to experience the very best Thailand has to offer.

List of Places to Visit in Hang Chat

Whether you’re a culture vulture, an outdoor enthusiast, or simply looking for some R&R, Hang Chat has something to offer everyone. Here are some must-visit spots in the area:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  1. Phra Ruang Hot Springs: This tranquil nature park, home to an ancient Khmer ruin, is a popular spot among locals and tourists alike to soak in the natural hot springs.
  2. Phra Ruang Museum: For history enthusiasts, a visit to this museum cannot be missed. Exhibits on the history of the local Lanna people and the famous Phra Ruang dynasty provide insight into the rich cultural heritage of the region.
  3. Wat Ban Den: This picturesque temple complex boasts unique architecture, intricate woodwork, and stunning views of the surrounding hills.

Natural Attractions of Hang Chat: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  1. Doi Ti: Located just a short drive from Hang Chat, Doi Ti offers breathtaking views of the local countryside and plenty of walking trails perfect for hiking or birdwatching.
  2. Lamphun Agricultural Research and Training Centre: Set amidst the rolling hills of Lamphun, this stunning botanical garden is a must-visit for nature lovers.
  3. Mae Ping National Park: With its dense forests, roaring waterfalls, and abundant wildlife, this national park is a must-see for anyone looking to get back to nature.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Hang Chat

For the more adventurous travelers, Hang Chat is home to some of the most spectacular hiking and mountain biking trails in Thailand. Here are a few of our favorites:

  1. Mae Pan Waterfall Hike: This picturesque hike takes you through dense jungle and past multiple waterfalls, culminating in a refreshing dip in the stunning Mae Pan Waterfall.
  2. Doi Ti Hike: With its panoramic views of the surrounding countryside, this challenging hike is worth the effort for those looking to get some exercise while taking in some truly breathtaking scenery.
  3. Mae Ping River Trail: Rent a bike and cycle along the Mae Ping River, stopping at quaint villages and hidden temples along the way.

Best Things to Do in Hang Chat: Activities and Tours

In addition to hiking and biking, there are plenty of other activities and tours available in Hang Chat:

  1. Hot Spring Bathing: Soak in the natural hot springs at Phra Ruang Hot Springs for ultimate relaxation.
  2. Thai Cooking Class: Learn how to cook traditional Thai dishes with a local chef, perfect for foodies who want to take home a new skill.
  3. Cycling Tour: Take a guided tour through the local villages and countryside on a scenic cycling route.

Week-Long Itinerary for Hang Chat

Here’s a sample itinerary to make the most of a week visit to Hang Chat:

Food and Drink Scene of Hang Chat

Get ready to tantalize your taste buds in Hang Chat, where the local cuisine is a melange of spices and bold flavors:

Must-Try Dishes

  1. Khao Soi: A Northern Thai specialty made with egg noodles, curry, and coconut milk served with lime and crispy noodles.
  2. Nam Prik Noom: A spicy dip made with grilled green chilies and served with fresh vegetables.
  3. Hang Chat Sausages: These tasty sausages are spiced with lemongrass, ginger, and garlic and served as a popular street food.

Local Beverages

  1. Cha Yen: A sweet iced Thai tea that’s the perfect refreshing drink on a hot day.
  2. Chang Beer: A popular Thai beer that pairs perfectly with spicy dishes.
  3. Lao Khao: A local spirit made from fermented rice and served as a shot or mixed into cocktails.

Where to Stay in Hang Chat: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

For accommodations in Hang Chat, there are options to suit all budgets:

  1. Hang Chat Grand Hotel: A modern, comfortable hotel that’s a great choice for families or those looking for a bit of luxury.
  2. Hang Chat Backpacker Hostel: This cozy, affordable hostel is perfect for backpackers and solo travelers.
  3. Baipai Thai Home: A stylish guesthouse set amid lush gardens, offering a peaceful oasis in the heart of Hang Chat.

What to Buy in Hang Chat: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

For unique souvenirs and gifts, check out the local markets and handicraft shops:

  1. Bronzeware and Silverware: Hang Chat is known for its exquisite brass and silverwork, having been a hub of these industries for over a thousand years.
  2. Weaving: The traditional weaving industry is still very much alive in Hang Chat, and visitors can purchase handmade textiles to take home with them.
  3. Laquerware: Another local art form, the Hang Chat region is known for its high-quality lacquerware, which makes for a beautiful and unique souvenir.

Local Culture and Customs of Hang Chat

Respect for local customs and traditions is essential when visiting Hang Chat:

  • Dress Modestly: When visiting temples or other religious sites, dress conservatively by covering shoulders and knees.
  • Remove Shoes: Always remove shoes when entering homes or temples.
  • Respect for Buddha: Buddha statues are considered sacred, so avoid taking photos with your back to them.

Best Time to Visit Hang Chat

The best time to visit Hang Chat is during the cool, dry season from November to February, when the weather is most comfortable for outdoor activities.

Do’s and Don’ts of Hang Chat

Do:

  • Try local street food and cuisine.
  • Take off your shoes when entering homes or temples.
  • Respect local customs and traditions.

Don’t:

  • Disrespect Buddha statues or other sacred objects.
  • Hike or camp in unauthorized areas.
  • Litter or damage the local environment.

Safety and Health

When traveling to Hang Chat, it’s important to take precautions to ensure your safety and wellbeing:

Safety in Hang Chat

  • Take caution when cycling or driving on the area’s winding roads.
  • Be aware of your surroundings when visiting markets or crowded areas.
  • Avoid hiking or camping in unauthorized areas.

Health in Hang Chat

  • Avoid drinking tap water and be cautious with street food.
  • Pack sunscreen and mosquito repellent to avoid sunburn and insect bites.
  • Stay hydrated and take frequent breaks when hiking or engaging in outdoor activities.

Getting to Hang Chat: Transportation Options and Logistics

There are several transportation options for getting to Hang Chat, including:

Airports Near Hang Chat

  • Chiang Mai International Airport: Located 65 kilometers from Hang Chat, the airport offers domestic and international flights.

Bus Stations Near Hang Chat

  • Lamphun Bus Station: Connects to major cities throughout Thailand.

Other Options

  • Private Car: Hire a driver or rent a car to explore the surrounding area on your own.
  • Train: The nearest train station is in Chiang Mai, approximately 65 kilometers from Hang Chat.

How to Get Around in Hang Chat

To explore Hang Chat and its surroundings, there are several ways to get around:

  • Bicycle: Rent a bike to explore the local countryside and villages.
  • Tuk-Tuk: A popular option for shorter trips around town.
  • Car or Motorbike: Rent a car or motorbike for more convenience and flexibility.

Where to Go Next from Hang Chat: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

If you want to extend your trip and see more of the region, here are some recommendations:

  • Chiang Mai: One of Thailand’s most popular destinations, Chiang Mai offers endless activities and attractions for travelers.
  • Chiang Rai: Known for its stunning temples and natural beauty, Chiang Rai is a must-visit for anyone venturing north from Hang Chat.
  • Doi Suthep-Pui National Park: Located near Chiang Mai, this national park is home to some of Thailand’s most revered temples and scenic hiking trails.

Conclusion

Whether you’re looking to immerse yourself in local culture, explore the great outdoors, or simply take a break from the hustle and bustle of city life, Hang Chat is the perfect destination. With our comprehensive guide, you’ll be able to plan an unforgettable trip to this hidden gem in Northern Thailand.
