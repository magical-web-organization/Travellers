Introduction to Phitsanulok

Phitsanulok is a culturally rich city located in the Lower Northern region of Thailand. It boasts a rich history and is home to several important historical sites. Known as the birthplace of King Naresuan, Phitsanulok is an ideal destination for history buffs and those interested in exploring Thai culture. Additionally, the city is surrounded by stunning natural scenery, making it a great place for outdoor adventures. This comprehensive travel guide will provide you with everything you need to know for a memorable trip to Phitsanulok.

List of Places to Visit in Phitsanulok

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Wat Phra Sri Rattana Mahathat

This temple is considered to be one of the most important temples in Thailand and is home to one of the country’s most revered Buddha images, Phra Buddha Chinnarat. The temple is a stunning example of traditional Thai architecture and is a must-visit for anyone interested in Thai culture.

The Folklore Museum

Located in the heart of the city, this museum is a great place to learn about the history and culture of Phitsanulok. It is home to a wide variety of exhibits that showcase the unique customs and traditions of the region.

Sergeant-Major Thawee Folk Museum

This museum is dedicated to Sergeant-Major Thawee, a local hero who played a significant role in the fight against the Burmese invasion. The museum features several exhibits highlighting his life and achievements.

Natural Attractions of Phitsanulok: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Phu Hin Rong Kla National Park

This national park is located just outside of Phitsanulok and is home to stunning natural scenery. Visitors can enjoy hiking, camping, and exploring the park’s many waterfalls and caves.

Namtok Chat Trakan National Park

This national park is located east of Phitsanulok and is home to several stunning waterfalls and hiking trails. It is also home to a variety of wildlife, including macaques and hornbills.

Thung Salaeng Luang National Park

This national park is located in the nearby province of Phitsanulok and is known for its stunning fields of wildflowers. Visitors can enjoy hiking and exploring the park’s many scenic trails.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Phitsanulok

Phu Hin Rong Kla National Park Trail

This trail is a 6-kilometer hike that takes visitors through stunning natural scenery, including several waterfalls and caves.

Khek River Route

This 40-kilometer biking route takes visitors along the Khek River and through several picturesque villages.

Namtok Chat Trakan National Park Trail

This trail is a 6-kilometer hike that takes visitors through lush forest and past several stunning waterfalls.

Best Things to Do in Phitsanulok: Activities and Tours

Visit the Night Bazaar

The night bazaar is a great place to sample local cuisine and pick up unique souvenirs. Visitors can stroll through the market and enjoy live music and cultural performances.

Take a Thai Cooking Class

Learn how to cook traditional Thai dishes while experiencing Thai culture firsthand. Several cooking schools in Phitsanulok offer classes for visitors.

Explore the Khao Kho Mountains

Located just outside of Phitsanulok, the Khao Kho Mountains offer stunning views and a variety of outdoor activities, including hiking and camping.

Food and Drink Scene of Phitsanulok

Must-Try Dishes

Khaep mu

Khaep mu is a traditional Thai snack made from deep-fried pork rind. It is often served with a spicy dipping sauce.

Khao thom mud

Khao thom mud is a traditional Thai dish made from sticky rice mixed with pork and herbs. It is wrapped in a banana leaf and steamed.

Nam tok

Nam tok is a spicy beef salad made with grilled beef, herbs, and spices. It is a popular dish in Northern Thailand.

Local Beverages

Nam khao

Nam khao is a crispy rice ball salad that is often served with a variety of fresh herbs and vegetables. It is a refreshing dish that pairs well with local beer.

Singha Beer

Singha Beer is a popular brand of beer in Thailand and is a great way to cool down after a long day of exploring the city.

Where to Stay in Phitsanulok: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Top-Rated Hotels

Topland Hotel & Convention Center

This hotel is located in the heart of the city and is within walking distance of several major attractions. It features a variety of amenities, including a swimming pool and fitness center.

Amarin Lagoon Hotel

This hotel is located just outside of the city and offers stunning views of the surrounding landscape. It features a variety of amenities, including a spa and tennis court.

Budget Accommodations

K1 Guesthouse

This guesthouse is located in the city center and offers clean, comfortable rooms at an affordable price.

VIGO Resort

This hostel is located just outside of the city and offers affordable rooms and dormitories for travelers on a budget.

What to Buy in Phitsanulok: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Traditional Thai Handicrafts

Phitsanulok is known for its traditional Thai handicrafts, including silk weaving, pottery, and wood carving. Visitors can find a variety of unique handicrafts at local markets and shops.

Local Food Products

Visitors can also bring home a taste of Thailand with local food products, including dried fruits, spices, and sauces.

Local Culture and Customs of Phitsanulok

Phitsanulok is a deeply religious city and visitors should be respectful of local customs and traditions. It is important to dress appropriately when visiting temples and to remove shoes before entering any religious buildings.

Best Time to Visit Phitsanulok

The best time to visit Phitsanulok is from November to February when the weather is cool and dry. March to June can be hot and humid, while July to October is the rainy season.

Do’s and Don’ts of Phitsanulok

Do:

  • Dress appropriately when visiting temples and other religious sites.
  • Respect local customs and traditions.
  • Try the local cuisine and explore the night bazaar.

Don’t:

  • Touch or climb on any religious statues or buildings.
  • Disrespect the Thai royal family or government.
  • Litter or pollute the environment.

Safety and Health

Safety in Phitsanulok

Phitsanulok is a safe city, but visitors should take common sense precautions, including keeping valuables secure and avoiding isolated areas at night.

Health in Phitsanulok

Visitors to Phitsanulok should ensure they are up to date on all necessary vaccinations. They should also take precautions to avoid mosquito bites, including using insect repellent and wearing long sleeves and pants.

Getting to Phitsanulok: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Phitsanulok

Phitsanulok Airport is located just outside of the city and offers several daily flights to Bangkok.

Bus Stations Near Phitsanulok

Phitsanulok Bus Terminal is located in the city center and offers regular bus service to several destinations throughout Thailand.

Other Options

Visitors can also reach Phitsanulok by train or by renting a car.

How to Get Around in Phitsanulok

Phitsanulok is a small city and visitors can easily get around on foot or by renting a bicycle. Tuk-tuks and taxis are also available for longer trips.

Where to Go Next from Phitsanulok: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Sukhothai

Sukhothai is a UNESCO World Heritage Site located just a short drive from Phitsanulok. It is home to several stunning historical ruins and is a must-visit for anyone interested in Thai history and culture.

Chiang Mai

Chiang Mai is a popular destination for travelers to Thailand and is located several hours north of Phitsanulok. It is known for its stunning temples, vibrant night bazaar, and delicious cuisine.

Conclusion

Phitsanulok is a hidden gem in Northern Thailand, offering a unique blend of history, culture, and natural beauty. Whether you’re interested in exploring historical sites, hiking through stunning natural scenery, or sampling delicious local cuisine, this city has something for everyone. Use this comprehensive guide to plan an unforgettable trip to Phitsanulok.
