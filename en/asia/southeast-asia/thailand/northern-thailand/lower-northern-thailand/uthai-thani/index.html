Introduction to Uthai Thani

Uthai Thani is a small province located in Lower Northern Thailand, about 200 kilometers north of Bangkok. It was once an important city of the Ayutthaya Kingdom and later became a significant trading center during the reign of King Rama V. Today, Uthai Thani is known for its rich history, culture, and natural beauty. The province is surrounded by green mountains, forests, rice fields, and rivers. It is also home to many temples, museums, and historic sites that offer a glimpse into Thailand’s past.

List of Places to Visit in Uthai Thani

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Uthai Thani is home to many cultural attractions, including museums and historical places that showcase the province’s rich history and culture. Some of the most popular ones are:

  • Wat Sangkat Rattana Khiri: This temple is famous for its unique architecture, which blends traditional Thai and European styles. It also features a beautiful garden and a museum dedicated to the life of King Rama V.
  • Ban Rai Phai Ngam Community: This traditional Thai village is known for its pottery and handicrafts. Visitors can learn about the local way of life and try their hand at making pottery.
  • Wat Khao Sakae Krang: This temple is located on top of a hill and offers stunning views of the surrounding area. It is also home to a large statue of Buddha and a museum dedicated to the history of Uthai Thani.
  • Ban Chiang Sang Cultural Center: This museum showcases the cultural heritage of Uthai Thani, including traditional costumes, handicrafts, and agricultural tools.

Natural Attractions of Uthai Thani: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Uthai Thani is blessed with stunning natural beauty and offers a variety of outdoor adventures, including hiking, biking, and camping. Here are some of the best natural attractions to visit:

  • Huai Kha Khaeng Wildlife Sanctuary: This UNESCO World Heritage site is home to many rare and endangered species, including tigers, leopards, elephants, and gibbons. Visitors can take a guided tour to explore the sanctuary and spot wildlife.
  • Thung Yai Naresuan Wildlife Sanctuary: This national park is another excellent place to see wildlife and enjoy nature. It offers hiking trails, camping sites, and stunning mountain views.
  • Chao Pho Thepharak-Chao Mae Thapthim Shrine: This shrine is located on a hill and offers stunning views of Uthai Thani’s countryside. Visitors can also hike to a nearby waterfall and enjoy a picnic.
  • Khao Pluak Wildlife Sanctuary: This sanctuary is located in the western part of Uthai Thani and offers hiking trails, camping sites, and stunning views of the sunset.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Uthai Thani

Uthai Thani is a great destination for outdoor enthusiasts who love hiking and biking. Here are some of the best routes to explore:

  • Mae Wong National Park: This national park is home to many waterfalls, rivers, and hiking trails. The most popular hiking route is the “Khao Hin Daeng” trail, which offers stunning views of the surrounding mountains.
  • Tham Khao Bin: This cave is located in the western part of Uthai Thani and offers a challenging hiking route to the top of the mountain. The views from the top are breathtaking, especially during the sunset.
  • Huai Kha Khaeng Wildlife Sanctuary: This sanctuary offers a variety of hiking trails that lead to waterfalls, hot springs, and wildlife spotting areas.
  • Chao Pho Thepharak-Chao Mae Thapthim Shrine: This shrine offers a hiking trail to a nearby waterfall and picnic area. It is an excellent place for a family outing.

Best Things to Do in Uthai Thani: Activities and Tours

Besides cultural attractions and outdoor adventures, Uthai Thani offers many exciting activities and tours for visitors. Here are some of the best ones:

  • Cycling Tour: Visitors can rent a bike and explore Uthai Thani’s countryside at their own pace. There are many scenic routes to choose from, including rice fields, temples, and small villages.
  • Rafting Tour: Visitors can take a guided tour down the Sakae Krang River and enjoy the stunning views of the surrounding mountains and forests.
  • Elephant Riding Tour: Visitors can ride an elephant and explore the natural beauty of Uthai Thani. The tour includes a visit to a local village and a Thai cooking class.
  • Boat Tour: Visitors can take a boat tour down the Sakae Krang River and enjoy the views of Uthai Thani’s countryside, including rice fields and small villages.

Week-Long Itinerary for Uthai Thani

Here is a suggested itinerary for a week-long trip to Uthai Thani:

  • Day 1-2: Explore the cultural attractions of Uthai Thani, including Wat Sangkat Rattana Khiri, Ban Rai Phai Ngam Community, and Ban Chiang Sang Cultural Center.
  • Day 3-4: Embark on outdoor adventures, including hiking in Mae Wong National Park, cycling in Uthai Thani’s countryside, and rafting down the Sakae Krang River.
  • Day 5-6: Visit the natural attractions of Uthai Thani, including Thung Yai Naresuan Wildlife Sanctuary, Chao Pho Thepharak-Chao Mae Thapthim Shrine, and Khao Pluak Wildlife Sanctuary.
  • Day 7: Relax and enjoy Uthai Thani’s food and drink scene, including local dishes and beverages.

Food and Drink Scene of Uthai Thani

Uthai Thani is home to many delicious dishes and local beverages that visitors must try. Here are some of the best ones:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Khao Yam: This refreshing salad consists of rice, vegetables, herbs, and a spicy sauce. It is perfect for hot days.
  • Nam Prik Maeng Da: This spicy relish is made with ant eggs, chilies, and lime. It is a popular appetizer in Uthai Thani.
  • Pad Phet Gai: This spicy chicken stir-fry is a favorite among locals. It is usually served with rice or noodles.
  • Tom Kha Gai: This spicy coconut soup with chicken is a popular dish in Thailand. Uthai Thani’s version is known for its unique flavor.

Local Beverages

  • Nam Phrik Long Rua: This refreshing beverage is made with tamarind, sugar, and spices.
  • Cha Om: This herb-infused drink is known for its detoxifying properties and is a popular post-meal drink.
  • Lamyai Juice: This sweet juice is made from longan fruit and is a favorite among locals.

Where to Stay in Uthai Thani: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Uthai Thani offers a variety of accommodation options to suit all budgets and preferences. Here are some of the best ones:

  • Banrai Chern Ma Resort: This resort offers modern rooms and bungalows in a peaceful setting. It also features a swimming pool, a restaurant, and a spa.
  • Palm House: This cozy guesthouse is located in the heart of Uthai Thani’s old town and offers comfortable rooms at an affordable price.
  • Baan Suan Resort: This resort is surrounded by lush gardens and offers spacious rooms and villas. It also features a swimming pool, a restaurant, and a fitness center.

What to Buy in Uthai Thani: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Uthai Thani is known for its handicrafts and local products that make perfect souvenirs or gifts for loved ones. Here are some of the best ones to bring home:

  • Pottery: Uthai Thani’s Ban Rai Phai Ngam Community is famous for its pottery. Visitors can buy handmade vases, bowls, and plates.
  • Handicrafts: Uthai Thani’s markets offer a variety of handicrafts, including woven baskets, Thai silk, and wooden carvings.
  • Local Products: Uthai Thani is known for its fruit products, including dried longan, tamarind, and plum.

Local Culture and Customs of Uthai Thani

Uthai Thani is a predominantly Buddhist province, and visitors should be mindful of local customs and traditions. Here are some tips:

  • Dress modestly when visiting temples or other religious sites.
  • Remove shoes before entering temples or homes.
  • Show respect for the Buddha image and other religious symbols.
  • Do not touch the head of a Thai person, as it is considered disrespectful.
  • Do not point your feet at people or religious symbols.

Best Time to Visit Uthai Thani

The best time to visit Uthai Thani is during the cooler months, from November to February. The weather is pleasant, and the natural beauty of the province is at its best. However, Uthai Thani can be visited year-round, and every season offers unique experiences.

Do’s and Don’ts of Uthai Thani

Do:

  • Try local dishes and beverages.
  • Explore the cultural attractions of Uthai Thani.
  • Respect local customs and traditions.
  • Take care of the environment and wildlife.

Don’t:

  • Disrespect religious symbols or traditions.
  • Be rude or disrespectful to locals.
  • Damage or litter natural attractions or wildlife.
  • Engage in illegal activities or drugs.

Safety and Health

Safety in Uthai Thani

Uthai Thani is generally safe, and crime rates are low. However, visitors should take some precautions, including:

  • Keep valuables in a safe place.
  • Use caution when withdrawing money from ATMs.
  • Be aware of your surroundings and avoid suspicious activities.

Health in Uthai Thani

Uthai Thani is a relatively healthy province, and no special vaccinations are required. However, visitors should take some precautions, including:

  • Avoid tap water and drink bottled or boiled water.
  • Use sunscreen and insect repellent.
  • Carry a basic first-aid kit.
  • Seek medical attention if necessary.

Getting to Uthai Thani: Transportation Options and Logistics

Uthai Thani is located about 200 kilometers north of Bangkok and can be reached by various modes of transportation.

Airports Near Uthai Thani

The nearest airport to Uthai Thani is Don Mueang International Airport in Bangkok. From there, visitors can take a bus or taxi to Uthai Thani, which takes around 3-4 hours.

Bus Stations Near Uthai Thani

Uthai Thani has a bus station that connects to other provinces in Thailand, including Bangkok, Chiang Mai, and Phitsanulok.

Other Options

Visitors can also rent a car or hire a private driver from Bangkok to Uthai Thani. The drive takes around 3-4 hours, depending on traffic.

How to Get Around in Uthai Thani

Uthai Thani is a small province, and most attractions can be easily reached by car or motorcycle. Taxis and tuk-tuks are also available for hire.

Where to Go Next from Uthai Thani: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Uthai Thani is located in an excellent location for exploring other nearby destinations and excursions. Here are some of the best ones:

  • Sukhothai: This historic city is located about 150 kilometers north of Uthai Thani and is home to many ancient ruins and temples.
  • Lopburi: This city is located about 150 kilometers south of Uthai Thani and is home to many monkeys and historic sites.
  • Ayutthaya: This historic city is located about 200 kilometers south of Uthai Thani and is a UNESCO World Heritage site.
