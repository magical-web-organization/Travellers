Introduction to Thungyai-Huai Kha Khaeng Wildlife Sanctuaries

Thungyai-Huai Kha Khaeng Wildlife Sanctuaries is a UNESCO World Heritage site that extends across the provinces of Tak, Kanchanaburi, and Uthai Thani, in Lower Northern Thailand. The area is known for its stunning biodiversity, including rare and endangered species such as tigers, wild elephants, and tapirs, making it one of the most important protected areas in Southeast Asia.

Travelers can expect to be mesmerized by its natural beauty and spectacular scenery, which features mountains, wetlands, and lush forests teeming with life. The area is popular with hikers, birdwatchers, and wildlife enthusiasts.

Thungyai-Huai Kha Khaeng Wildlife Sanctuaries are home to the largest concentration of archaeological sites in mainland Southeast Asia.

Natural Attractions of Thungyai-Huai Kha Khaeng Wildlife Sanctuaries: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Thungyai-Huai Kha Khaeng Wildlife Sanctuaries offer a wide range of outdoor activities and natural scenery. Here are some of the highlights every traveler shouldn’t miss:

  • Thi Lor Su Waterfall: Located in the Umphang Wildlife Sanctuary, the waterfall is the highest in Thailand, boasting a 200-metre drop surrounded by lush forests and wildlife.

  • Huay Mae Khamin Waterfall: Made up of 9 cascading tiers, with each level offering a different experience, Huay Mae Khamin Waterfall is a must-visit destination for adventure-seeking travelers.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Thungyai-Huai Kha Khaeng Wildlife Sanctuaries

Thungyai-Huai Kha Khaeng Wildlife Sanctuaries have an extensive network of hiking and biking trails that take visitors through some of the most breathtaking scenery in Thailand. Here are some of the most popular routes:

  • Maekhamin Waterfall Trail: This 8-kilometer route takes visitors through the jungle to the Huay Mae Khamin Waterfall. Along the way, hikers can spot wildlife and birds.

  • The Mae Tuang Loop: With spectacular views of the surrounding mountains and valleys, this 18-kilometer trail is a great option for intermediate hikers looking for a challenge.

  • The National Park Walking Trail: This short, easy route is perfect for families and those looking to take a leisurely stroll. Visitors can expect to spot some of the park’s wildlife.
