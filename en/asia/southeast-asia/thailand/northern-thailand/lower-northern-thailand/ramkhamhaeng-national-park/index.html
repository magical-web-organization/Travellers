Introduction to Ramkhamhaeng National Park

Ramkhamhaeng National Park is a natural wonderland located in Lower Northern Thailand. Covering an area of 300 square kilometers, it is a place where nature lovers can indulge in outdoor adventures and scenic views. The national park is named after King Ramkhamhaeng, who is also known as the “Father of Thai Script”. He ruled the Sukhothai Kingdom during the 13th century and is credited with creating the first written Thai alphabet.

The park is home to a diverse range of flora and fauna, including over 200 species of birds, 40 species of mammals, and various reptiles and insects. With its multiple peaks, waterfalls, and nature trails, Ramkhamhaeng National Park offers a refreshing escape from the urban life and an opportunity to reconnect with nature.

List of Places to Visit in Ramkhamhaeng National Park

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Ramkhamhaeng National Park has a rich history and culture. There are several museums and historical places within the park that are worth visiting, such as:

Ramkhamhaeng National Museum

This museum showcases the history and culture of the Sukhothai Kingdom. It contains various artifacts, including pottery, ceramics, bronze wares, and sculptures. Visitors can also learn about the life and achievements of King Ramkhamhaeng.

Wat Si Sawai

This ancient temple was built during the 12th century and is one of the most important historical sites in the park. It features a Khmer-style architecture and three prang (tower-like structures).

Natural Attractions of Ramkhamhaeng National Park: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Ramkhamhaeng National Park offers a wide range of natural attractions that are perfect for outdoor adventures and scenic views. These include:

Khao Luang Peak

With a height of 1,835 meters, Khao Luang Peak is the highest mountain in the park. It offers breathtaking views of the surrounding landscape and is a popular spot for hiking. There are several trails leading to the peak, ranging from easy to difficult.

Namtok Mae Ya Pa

Namtok Mae Ya Pa is a beautiful waterfall located in the southern part of the park. It has a height of 15 meters and offers a refreshing swimming spot. Visitors can also go hiking along the nature trails that run alongside the waterfall.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Ramkhamhaeng National Park

Ramkhamhaeng National Park has several hiking and biking routes that are perfect for outdoor enthusiasts. These trails offer a chance to explore the natural beauty of the park up close. Some popular trails include:

  • Khao Luang Trail: This trail leads to the summit of Khao Luang Peak and offers stunning views along the way.
  • Chong Yen Trail: This trail takes visitors through the dense forest and offers a chance to see some of the park’s wildlife, such as gibbons and macaques.
  • Mae Ya Pa Trail: This trail leads to the Mae Ya Pa waterfall and offers a refreshing swim as a reward.

Best Things to Do in Ramkhamhaeng National Park: Activities and Tours

Ramkhamhaeng National Park offers a range of activities and tours that cater to different interests and preferences. Some popular things to do include:

  • Trekking: The park has several trekking routes that offer a chance to explore the dense forest and discover hidden waterfalls and streams.
  • Camping: Visitors can camp overnight in designated areas inside the park and enjoy the peaceful sound of nature.
  • Birdwatching: With over 200 species of birds in the park, birdwatching is a popular activity for nature lovers.
  • Rafting: The nearby Mae Nam Huai Dua Rafting Camp offers rafting trips through the scenic river that passes through the park.

Week-Long Itinerary for Ramkhamhaeng National Park

A week-long itinerary for Ramkhamhaeng National Park could consist of the following:

Day 1-2: Explore the historic sites and museums of the park, including the Ramkhamhaeng National Museum and Wat Si Sawai.

Day 3-4: Go hiking along the park’s trails, such as Khao Luang Trail and Chong Yen Trail.

Day 5: Enjoy a leisurely day by the Mae Ya Pa waterfall and go for a refreshing swim.

Day 6: Try out adventure activities such as rafting or mountain biking.

Day 7: Take a tour of the park’s birdwatching spots and enjoy the rich avian diversity of the park.

Food and Drink Scene of Ramkhamhaeng National Park

Must-Try Dishes

Ramkhamhaeng National Park offers a range of local cuisine that is worth trying. Some must-try dishes include:

  • Pad Thai: A popular Thai stir-fried noodle dish that can be found in most local eateries.
  • Tom Yum Soup: A spicy and sour soup made with shrimp or chicken, lemongrass, and lime.
  • Som Tam: A spicy and sour salad made with green papaya, chili, and lime.

Local Beverages

Some local beverages of the park include:

  • Singha Beer: A popular Thai beer that is widely available in local bars and restaurants.
  • Thai Iced Tea: A sweet and creamy tea that is often served with condensed milk and ice.
  • Coconut Water: The park is home to several coconut farms, and visitors can enjoy fresh coconut water while exploring the park.

Where to Stay in Ramkhamhaeng National Park: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Ramkhamhaeng National Park offers a range of lodging options that cater to different budgets and preferences. Some popular options include:

  • Sukhothai Heritage Resort: A luxury resort located near the park’s entrance that offers comfortable rooms with modern amenities.
  • Campground: The park has several designated camping areas where visitors can pitch a tent and enjoy the peaceful surroundings.
  • Guesthouses: There are several guesthouses located near the park’s entrance that offer budget-friendly accommodation options.

What to Buy in Ramkhamhaeng National Park: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Some popular souvenirs and local products to buy in Ramkhamhaeng National Park include:

  • Thai Silk: The nearby city of Sukhothai is famous for its silk products, and visitors can purchase high-quality silk products from local markets.
  • Handmade Pottery: The park has several pottery studios that offer handmade pottery products, such as bowls, plates, and vases.
  • Local Spices and Herbs: Visitors can purchase locally produced spices and herbs that are used in Thai cuisine, such as lemongrass, kaffir lime leaves, and galangal.

Local Culture and Customs of Ramkhamhaeng National Park

The local culture of Ramkhamhaeng National Park is deeply rooted in Thai traditions and customs. Visitors are expected to respect the local culture and follow certain customs, such as:

  • Dress modestly when visiting temples or historical sites.
  • Remove shoes before entering temples or someone’s home.
  • Use the right hand when giving or receiving items.

Best Time to Visit Ramkhamhaeng National Park

The best time to visit Ramkhamhaeng National Park is between November and February when the weather is cool and dry. The rainy season from May to October can make outdoor activities challenging, and the hot season from March to May can be uncomfortably hot.

Do’s and Don’ts of Ramkhamhaeng National Park

Do:

  • Respect the local culture and customs.
  • Carry sufficient water and snacks during outdoor activities.
  • Keep the park clean and dispose of litter responsibly.

Don’t:

  • Harm or disturb the park’s wildlife.
  • Go off-trail without permission.
  • Damage or remove any plants or trees.

Safety and Health

Safety in Ramkhamhaeng National Park

Ramkhamhaeng National Park is generally a safe place to visit. However, visitors should take some precautions, such as:

  • Avoid hiking alone, especially in remote areas.
  • Wear appropriate footwear and clothing for outdoor activities.
  • Be aware of the weather conditions and take appropriate action if there are signs of thunderstorms or flash floods.

Health in Ramkhamhaeng National Park

Visitors to Ramkhamhaeng National Park should be up to date on routine vaccinations, such as measles, mumps, and rubella (MMR) and have malaria prophylaxis if needed. It is also recommended to bring insect repellent to avoid mosquito bites.

Getting to Ramkhamhaeng National Park: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Ramkhamhaeng National Park

The nearest airport to Ramkhamhaeng National Park is Sukhothai Airport, which is about 50 kilometers away. It offers domestic flights to Bangkok and some other major cities in Thailand.

Bus Stations Near Ramkhamhaeng National Park

There are several bus stations in the nearby cities that offer frequent services to the park. The most popular ones include Sukhothai Bus Terminal and Phitsanulok Bus Terminal.

Other Options

Visitors can also hire a car or a taxi from the nearby cities to reach Ramkhamhaeng National Park.

How to Get Around in Ramkhamhaeng National Park

The best way to explore Ramkhamhaeng National Park is on foot or bicycle. Visitors can rent bicycles from the park’s entrance or bring their own. There are several nature trails and hiking routes that offer a chance to explore the park’s natural beauty up close.

Where to Go Next from Ramkhamhaeng National Park: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Some popular nearby destinations and excursions from Ramkhamhaeng National Park include:

  • Sukhothai Historic Park: A UNESCO World Heritage Site that showcases the ancient city of Sukhothai.
  • Sri Satchanalai Historical Park: Another UNESCO World Heritage Site that features the ruins of an ancient 13th-century city.
  • Phitsanulok: A charming city located on the banks of the Nan River with several historical attractions and natural beauty.

Conclusion

Ramkhamhaeng National Park is a nature lover’s paradise that offers a range of natural attractions and outdoor activities. Visitors can explore the park’s rich history and culture, hike along nature trails, enjoy scenic views, and indulge in local cuisine. With its diverse range of flora and fauna, Ramkhamhaeng National Park is a must-visit destination for anyone looking to reconnect with nature.
