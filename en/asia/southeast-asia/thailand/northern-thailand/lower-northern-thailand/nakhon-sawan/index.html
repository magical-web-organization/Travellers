Introduction to Nakhon Sawan

Located in Lower Northern Thailand, Nakhon Sawan is a small province that is rich in cultural and natural beauty. This province is a hidden gem that is still off the tourist’s track, so you can enjoy the beauty of the place without too much crowding.

The province is blessed with beautiful natural attractions such as waterfalls, mountains, and rivers. Its rich cultural heritage is reflected in its museums, temples, historical sites, and charming old town.

List of Places to Visit in Nakhon Sawan

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Nakhon Sawan has a rich cultural history that can be seen in its museums and historical places.

Wat Khao Wongkot

Wat Khao Wongkot is an ancient temple located on a hill 10 kilometers from the city center. The temple complex contains several temples, shrines, and a stunning reclining Buddha statue

Sawan Park

Sawan Park is a large public park located in the heart of the city. The park has a large lake, children’s play area, and a beautiful walkway that takes you around the lake.

Klong Lan National Park

Klong Lan National Park is a beautiful park that is home to Thailand’s third-largest waterfall. The park is also home to a wide variety of plants and animals, making it a great spot for nature lovers.

Natural Attractions of Nakhon Sawan: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Nakhon Sawan’s natural attractions are breathtaking and perfect for an outdoor adventure.

Khao Pathawi

Khao Pathawi is a small mountain located near Nakhon Sawan. It offers beautiful scenic views of the city and its surroundings. The summit of the mountain is a perfect spot for watching the sunset over the city.

Tha Rae Island

Located in the Chao Phraya River, Tha Rae Island is a small island known for its beautiful scenery and calm atmosphere. Visitors can stroll around the island, relax by the river, or go kayaking.

Mae Wong National Park

Mae Wong National Park is a great spot for trekking, hiking, and camping. The park is home to several mountain peaks, waterfalls, and diverse flora and fauna.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Nakhon Sawan

Nakhon Sawan’s scenic beauty makes it a perfect destination for hiking and biking enthusiasts.

Nakhon Sawan River Walk

Nakhon Sawan River Walk is a beautiful riverside trail that stretches for several kilometers. Visitors can walk, bike, or jog along the trail while enjoying the scenic views of the Chao Phraya River.

Khao Wongkot Route

The Khao Wongkot Route is a 20-kilometer cycling route that takes you through beautiful countryside scenery, with views of rivers, mountains, and forests.

Best Things to Do in Nakhon Sawan: Activities and Tours

Bo Sang Umbrella Making

Bo Sang Umbrella Making is a unique experience where visitors can learn about the traditional Thai craft of making umbrellas from bamboo and paper. Visitors can also buy beautiful umbrellas and other souvenirs from the factory.

Sunflower Fields

The Sunflower Fields in Nakhon Sawan are a beautiful sight during the winter season. These flowers bloom from November to January and attract many visitors

Food and Drink Scene of Nakhon Sawan

Must-Try Dishes

Khao Man Gai

Khao Man Gai is a popular Thai dish that consists of boiled chicken served with rice cooked in chicken broth. This dish is often accompanied by a tangy chili-garlic sauce.

Pla Kapong Neung Manao

Pla Kapong Neung Manao is a steamed fish dish that is seasoned with lime juice, garlic, chili, and fish sauce. This dish is served with rice and vegetables.

Local Beverages

Cha Yen

Cha Yen is a popular Thai iced tea that is made with black tea, condensed milk, and sugar. It is often served with ice and a slice of lemon.

Nam Manao

Nam Manao is a refreshing drink made with lime juice, honey, and water.

Where to Stay in Nakhon Sawan: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Nakhon Sawan offers a range of accommodation options for visitors, including hotels, hostels, and guesthouses.

The Riverside Hotel

The Riverside Hotel is a popular 4-star hotel located on the banks of the Chao Phraya River. The hotel offers stunning views of the river and the city.

U-Sabai Park Resort

U-Sabai Park Resort is a cozy and comfortable resort surrounded by lush greenery. It is a perfect spot for those who want to get away from the hustle and bustle of the city.

What to Buy in Nakhon Sawan: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Bo Sang Umbrellas

Bo Sang Umbrellas are a unique souvenir from Nakhon Sawan. These colorful umbrellas are handmade using bamboo and paper and are available in various sizes and designs.

Pottery

Nakhon Sawan is known for its traditional pottery. Visitors can buy beautiful pottery items such as bowls, vases, and figurines.

Local Culture and Customs of Nakhon Sawan

Nakhon Sawan has a rich cultural heritage that is reflected in its temples, festivals, and local customs.

Festivals

Rafting and Rowing Festival

The Rafting and Rowing Festival is a popular festival held annually in October. Visitors can witness various boat races and traditional cultural performances.

Bo Sang Umbrella Festival

The Bo Sang Umbrella Festival is a colorful 3-day festival held in January. Visitors can watch umbrella-making demonstrations and cultural performances.

Best Time to Visit Nakhon Sawan

The best time to visit Nakhon Sawan is between November and February when the weather is cool and dry. Visitors can also witness the beautiful sunflower fields and the traditional Bo Sang Umbrella Festival.

Do’s and Don’ts of Nakhon Sawan

Do:

  • Respect local customs and traditions.
  • Dress appropriately while visiting temples and other religious sites.
  • Try the local cuisine and snacks.
  • Keep your surroundings clean and tidy.

Don’t:

  • Disrespect the royal family or Thai culture.
  • Smoke or drink in public places.
  • Touch or disturb the wildlife.

Safety and Health

Safety in Nakhon Sawan

Nakhon Sawan is a relatively safe place for tourists. However, visitors should keep their valuables safe and be cautious while traveling at night.

Health in Nakhon Sawan

Visitors should take necessary precautions such as using mosquito repellent, drinking bottled water, and getting vaccinated before visiting Nakhon Sawan.

Getting to Nakhon Sawan: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Nakhon Sawan

The nearest airport to Nakhon Sawan is Don Mueang International Airport in Bangkok. From there, visitors can take a bus or train to reach Nakhon Sawan.

Bus Stations Near Nakhon Sawan

The Nakhon Sawan Bus Station is a major transportation hub that connects the city to other parts of the country. Visitors can take a bus from Bangkok to Nakhon Sawan.

Other Options

Visitors can also take a train to Nakhon Sawan from Bangkok. The railway station is located in the city center.

How to Get Around in Nakhon Sawan

Nakhon Sawan is a relatively small province, and the best way to explore the city is by renting a motorcycle or a bicycle. Visitors can also use public transportation such as tuk-tuks and songthaews to get around the city.

Where to Go Next from Nakhon Sawan: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Phitsanulok

Phitsanulok is a neighboring province located 100 km from Nakhon Sawan. The province is home to several stunning temples, such as Wat Phra Si Rattana Mahathat, and historical sites such as the King Naresuan Shrine.

Sukhothai

Sukhothai is a UNESCO World Heritage Site located 130 km from Nakhon Sawan. The ancient city is home to several ruins and temples that reflect the rich cultural history of Thailand.

Conclusion

Nakhon Sawan is a hidden gem that offers a rich cultural and natural experience for travelers. From its beautiful waterfalls and rivers to its traditional crafts and pottery, Nakhon Sawan has a lot to offer. Visitors can also experience the warmth and hospitality of Thai culture while exploring this hidden province.
