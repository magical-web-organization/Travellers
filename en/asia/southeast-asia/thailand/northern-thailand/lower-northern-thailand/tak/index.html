Introduction to Tak

Tak is a hidden gem located in the Lower Northern region of Thailand, away from the hustle and bustle of the popular tourist destinations. It is a tranquil town surrounded by towering mountains and lush greenery, making it a perfect destination for nature lovers. Tak is known for its cultural heritage, old temples, and historical sites. The province is also famous for its local cuisine, including dishes such as “Kradueng Nai”, a type of crispy pancake, and “Moo Yang Chao Wang”, grilled pork. With plenty of activities and attractions, Tak has something for everyone.

List of Places to Visit in Tak

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Tak Museum

This museum is located in the heart of Tak and features a wide array of historical artifacts, including pottery, antiques, and relics from the ancient Lanna Kingdom. It is a great place to learn about the history and culture of Tak.

Wat Phra That Si Chom Thong

Located on top of a hill, Wat Phra That Si Chom Thong offers stunning views of Tak and the surrounding valley. The temple is over 700 years old and is home to a revered Buddha image.

Ban Tak Bat Devo

This village is famous for its pottery and ceramics. Visitors can witness the pottery-making process and even purchase handmade souvenirs.

Natural Attractions of Tak: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Mae Kasa Waterfall

Mae Kasa Waterfall is a stunning 7-tier waterfall surrounded by lush greenery and a serene atmosphere. It is a great place for a picnic and a refreshing swim.

Bhumibol Dam

Bhumibol Dam is one of the largest hydroelectric dams in Thailand, spanning over 8000 hectares. Visitors can take a tour of the dam and learn about its history and significance.

Khun Dan Prakanchon National Park

Khun Dan Prakanchon National Park is a vast natural reserve spanning over 1000 square kilometers. It is home to numerous waterfalls, caves, and hiking trails.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Tak

Lan Sang National Park

This national park features several hiking trails of varying difficulty levels, with stunning scenic views of the surrounding landscape. There are also plenty of opportunities for bird watching and camping.

Mae Wong National Park

Mae Wong National Park is a popular hiking destination, thanks to its challenging and scenic trails. The park is home to several waterfalls and abundant wildlife, including elephants and tigers.

Best Things to Do in Tak: Activities and Tours

Cycle Tour

Explore the stunning natural beauty of Tak on a cycling tour. Cycle through lush green rice fields, quaint villages, and historical sites.

Kayaking

Kayak down the Mae Ping River and enjoy the serene beauty of Tak’s countryside.

Hot Air Balloon Ride

Experience Tak’s breathtaking landscapes from a unique perspective on a hot air balloon ride.

Food and Drink Scene of Tak

Must-Try Dishes

Kradueng Nai

Kradueng Nai is a crispy pancake filled with a variety of savory and sweet fillings, such as minced pork, banana, and taro.

Khao Soi

Khao Soi is a traditional Northern Thai dish made with egg noodles in a coconut milk-based curry soup topped with crispy noodles and lime.

Local Beverages

Nam Ob

Nam Ob is a refreshing drink made with tamarind and ginger, commonly found in local markets and street vendors.

Nam Prik Noom

Nam Prik Noom is a spicy green chili dip served with fresh vegetables, a popular Northern Thai dish.

Where to Stay in Tak: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

B2 Tak Hotel

B2 Tak Hotel is located in the center of Tak and offers modern amenities, including air conditioning, Wi-Fi, and a fitness center.

Baan Rim Nam Resort

Baan Rim Nam Resort offers bungalows with river views, surrounded by tropical gardens. The resort also features an outdoor swimming pool and a restaurant.

What to Buy in Tak: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Handmade Pottery

Tak is known for its traditional pottery, with several villages specializing in various types and styles of ceramics.

Local Textiles

Visitors can purchase handmade textiles made from local fabrics, such as cotton and silk, featuring intricate patterns and designs.

Local Culture and Customs of Tak

Tak has a rich cultural heritage, with several festivals and events celebrated throughout the year. The province is famous for its traditional dance and music performances, including the “Ram Wong” dance and the “Khaploon” music ensemble.

Best Time to Visit Tak

The best time to visit Tak is from November to February when the weather is cool and dry, making outdoor activities and sightseeing more enjoyable.

Do’s and Don’ts of Tak

Do:

  • Dress conservatively when visiting temples and other religious sites
  • Learn some basic Thai phrases
  • Try the local cuisine

Don’t:

  • Touch a person’s head or point your feet towards someone
  • Disrespect or litter at cultural or religious sites
  • Hug or kiss in public

Safety and Health

Safety in Tak

Tak is a safe destination for tourists and has a low crime rate. However, visitors should be cautious and aware of their surroundings, especially at night.

Health in Tak

Tourists should take precautions against mosquito-borne diseases, such as dengue fever and malaria. Drinking bottled water and avoiding undercooked food can also prevent water-borne illnesses.

Getting to Tak: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Tak

The nearest airport to Tak is Chiang Mai International Airport, which is approximately 3 hours away by car or bus.

Bus Stations Near Tak

Tak has a central bus station, which provides regular services to major cities in Thailand, including Bangkok and Chiang Mai.

Other Options

Visitors can also take a train to Tak, with the nearest railway station located in nearby Kamphaeng Phet.

How to Get Around in Tak

Tuk-tuks and motorbikes are the most popular modes of transportation in Tak. Visitors can also rent bicycles or cars to explore the province.

Where to Go Next from Tak: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Chiang Mai

Chiang Mai is located approximately 190 kilometers north of Tak, and is known for its rich cultural heritage, ancient temples, and bustling night markets.

Sukhothai

Sukhothai is located approximately 160 kilometers south of Tak, and is home to the ruins of the former capital of the Sukhothai Kingdom, a UNESCO World Heritage Site.

Conclusion

Tak is a hidden gem in Northern Thailand, with plenty of attractions, outdoor activities, and local cuisine to offer visitors. From history and culture to adventure and relaxation, Tak has something for everyone. Plan your trip today and discover the charm and beauty of Tak.
