Introduction to Uttaradit

Located in Lower Northern Thailand, Uttaradit is a lesser-known province that offers a mix of natural attractions and cultural landmarks. Nestled in a valley surrounded by mountains, the province is an ideal getaway for those who want to experience the rich history and traditions of Thailand without the crowds of the better-known destinations.

List of Places to Visit in Uttaradit

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Phra Buddha Chinnarat - A highly revered 700-year-old Buddha statue that is considered to be one of the most beautiful in Thailand.

  • Lamnam Nan National Park - A natural wonder with a beautiful forest, stunning waterfalls, and sacred caves that are said to have been used by ancient monks.

  • Ban Khao Laem Archaeological Site - Explore the ruins of an ancient city dating back to the 6th century, where you can see the remains of a temple, a fortification wall, and a moat.

  • Wat Tha Thanon - A temple that is famous for its beautifully decorated pagoda and ancient murals depicting the stories of Buddha.

Natural Attractions of Uttaradit: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Phu Lanka National Park - With towering limestone cliffs and natural hot springs, this park offers a variety of hikes for all levels of experience.

  • Nam Pat Forest Park - A peaceful park with clear-blue water ponds, a bubbling creek and tall trees that provide plenty of shade for picnics or relaxation.

  • Pha Tang Cliff - A stunning viewpoint with panoramic views of ancient mountains and lush forests.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Uttaradit

Phu Soi Dao National Park

This park offers some of the most challenging and rewarding hikes in Thailand, including the 2-day trek up to the peak of Phu Soi Dao, which is the second-highest mountain in the province.

Lamnam Nan National Park

If you’re up for a more leisurely hike, you can explore the ancient caves and waterfalls that are hidden throughout this park. These hikes are perfect for families with children, or those who just want to take in the beauty of nature at their own pace.

Best Things to Do in Uttaradit: Activities and Tours

  • Explore the Night Market - Every weekend, the town square transforms into a bustling night market that offers a variety of local food, handicrafts, and live music.

  • Take a Cooking Class - Gain insight into local cuisine by learning to cook traditional Thai dishes with the help of an experienced local chef.

  • Visit a Hill Tribe Village - Take a guided tour to the nearby hill tribe villages and learn more about the rich culture and traditions of the local people.

Food and Drink Scene of Uttaradit

Must-Try Dishes

  • Tom Kha Gai - A popular soup made with coconut milk, chicken, and fragrant herbs and spices.

  • Khao Soi - A Northern Thai specialty made with egg noodles in a rich curry broth, topped with crispy noodles and garnished with fresh herbs.

  • Kao Gaeng - A classic Thai dish that consists of rice served with multiple curries, meats or vegetables and occasionally a soup.

Local Beverages

  • Thai Tea - A sweet, milky tea that is perfect for hot days.

  • Lok Chong - A refreshing drink made with jelly cubes, ice, and sugar syrup.

  • Singha Beer - A popular local beer that pairs well with Thai food.

Where to Stay in Uttaradit: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Luxurious Accommodations

  • The Riverie by Katathani - A five-star hotel that offers panoramic views of the Nan River and the city skyline.

Budget-Friendly Options

  • Uttaradit Park Hotel - A clean and comfortable hotel located in the heart of the city.

  • Kiam Place Hotel - A budget-friendly hotel that offers clean and comfortable rooms with all the basic amenities.

What to Buy in Uttaradit: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Wooden Crafts - The province is famous for its beautiful carved wooden crafts, including Buddha statues, boxes, and furniture.

  • Textiles - Hand-woven fabrics and colorful garments are some of the most popular souvenirs in Uttaradit.

Local Culture and Customs of Uttaradit

  • Sai Dok Lak - A red-and-white flower emblem that is used widely in Uttaradit’s architecture, textiles, and decorative arts.

  • Buddhism and Animism - As with much of Thailand, the local religion is a mixture of Buddhism and Animism.

Best Time to Visit Uttaradit

The best time to visit Uttaradit is from November to January when the temperature is cool and comfortable. The rainy season lasts from May to October, with storms and occasional flooding.

Do’s and Don’ts of Uttaradit

Do:

  • Respect the local customs and traditions.

  • Dress modestly when visiting temples or holy sites.

  • Try the local food and drinks.

Don’t:

  • Disrespect the local religion or culture.

  • Showcase inappropriate behavior in public.

  • Litter or harm the environment.

Safety and Health

Safety in Uttaradit

Uttaradit is generally a safe place to visit, but it’s always important to exercise caution and stay aware of your surroundings while exploring the local town and natural wonders. Don’t leave your valuables unattended when you go out and be careful when driving or motorcycling on local roads.

Health in Uttaradit

As with much of Southeast Asia, it’s important to take appropriate precautions, such as using insect repellent and drinking only bottled water. It’s a good idea to bring your own first aid kit in case of emergencies.

Getting to Uttaradit: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Uttaradit

The nearest airport to Uttaradit is in Phitsanulok, which is approximately 80 km away.

Bus Stations Near Uttaradit

The main bus station in Uttaradit is located in the city center. Buses are available from Bangkok, Chiang Mai, and other cities throughout Thailand.

Other Options

Taxis and tuk-tuk are also available for hire in the city.

How to Get Around in Uttaradit

The most convenient way to get around Uttaradit is by renting a car or motorcycle. Local buses also run throughout the province, and there are plenty of tuk-tuks available in the city center.

Where to Go Next from Uttaradit: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Chiang Mai - A hub for adventure lovers, with spectacular natural beauty, mouth-watering food, and rich cultural heritage.

  • Phitsanulok - A historic town famous for its ancient temples and shrines, as well as its delicious street food.

Conclusion

Uttaradit may not be as well-known as some of Thailand’s other popular destinations, but it’s a hidden gem that offers rich history, beautiful nature, and plenty of opportunities to learn about local culture and traditions. Its affable locals, quiet atmosphere, and stunning scenery make it worth an adventurous traveler’s glimpse, as well as offering a less frenzied view of Thai culture and hospitality.
