Introduction to Sukhothai

Sukhothai, located in Lower Northern Thailand, is a historical city rich in cultural heritage and natural beauty. Known as the first capital of the Kingdom of Siam, Sukhothai has been recognized as a UNESCO World Heritage Site. The city offers a unique experience for travelers with its ancient temples, museums, national parks, and outdoor activities.

List of Places to Visit in Sukhothai

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Sukhothai Historical Park: The park contains the ruins of the ancient capital, including the impressive Wat Mahathat temple.
  • Ramkhamhaeng National Museum: The museum showcases the history and culture of Sukhothai and its surrounding areas.
  • Wat Si Chum: A breathtaking temple with a large, seated Buddha statue.
  • San Ta Pha Daeng Historical Site: An old temple and castle complex that dates back to the 13th century.

Natural Attractions of Sukhothai: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Si Satchanalai National Park: The park is home to the Si Satchanalai Historical Park, cycling trails, and opportunities for bird watching and hiking.
  • Mae Wong National Park: A remote park with stunning views, waterfalls, and hiking trails.
  • Sukhothai Historical Park River Cruise: A relaxing boat ride down the Yom River that provides a unique perspective of the city.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Sukhothai

Si Satchanalai Historical Park Cycling Route

This 10-kilometer cycling route takes you through the stunning Si Satchanalai Historical Park, including temples such as Wat Chang Lom and Wat Phra Si Rattana Mahathat.

Mae Wong National Park Trek

The adventurous can take on a challenging, 3-day trek through Mae Wong National Park’s rugged terrain, lush forests, and glistening waterfalls.

Best Things to Do in Sukhothai: Activities and Tours

  • Elephant Ride through the Jungle: Elephants are a symbol of Thailand, and tourists can take an hour-long ride through the dense forest on the backs of these gentle giants.
  • Thai Cooking Class: Learn how to prepare traditional Thai dishes from experienced chefs and savor your creations.
  • Antique Exploration: Visit Talat Ton Pho, Sukhothai’s renowned antiques market, for unique souvenirs and unexpected treasures.

Week-Long Itinerary for Sukhothai

Day 1: Visit Sukhothai Historical Park and explore the temples and ruins. Day 2: Take a cycling tour of Si Satchanalai Historical Park. Day 3: Trek through Mae Wong National Park. Day 4: Visit Wat Si Chum and Ramkhamhaeng National Museum. Day 5: Take a Thai cooking class and go antique shopping. Day 6: Go on an elephant ride through the jungle. Day 7: Relax and enjoy the scenic views at the Si Satchanalai National Park.

Food and Drink Scene of Sukhothai

Must-Try Dishes

  • Khao Soi: A northern Thai specialty, khao soi is a soup made with curry, coconut milk, and egg noodles.
  • Nam Prik Ong: A spicy tomato and pork dip that’s served with steamed vegetables and sticky rice.
  • Pad Thai Thip Samai: This world-renowned Pad Thai street food has been a Sukhothai staple for over 50 years.

Local Beverages

  • Cha Yen: A sweet and creamy tea made with condensed milk and black tea.
  • Singha Beer: A popular Thai beer, Singha beer is refreshing and perfect for hot days.

Where to Stay in Sukhothai: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Legendha Sukhothai Resort: This resort offers a blend of traditional Thai and contemporary aesthetics, with lush gardens and a saltwater pool.
  • Sila Resort: A cozy guesthouse with large rooms and balconies overlooking rice paddies.
  • At Home Sukhothai: This budget hostel features single and double private rooms, with a shared kitchen, living room, and garden area.

What to Buy in Sukhothai: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Celadon Pottery: Sukhothai is known for its beautiful celadon pottery, which has been produced for over 700 years.
  • Handcrafted Furniture: Skilled craftsmen in Sukhothai create unique wooden furniture, including ornamental carvings, folding screens, and rocking chairs.

Local Culture and Customs of Sukhothai

  • Women should wear clothing that covers their shoulders and knees when visiting temples or other religious sites.
  • Shoes must always be removed before entering a temple or someone’s home.

Best Time to Visit Sukhothai

The best time to visit Sukhothai is between November and February when the weather is cooler and dry.

Do’s and Don’ts of Sukhothai

Do:

  • Respect the culture and customs of the locals.
  • Take off your shoes before going inside temples or someone’s house.
  • Bargain at markets and prepare to save if paying the initial price at stores.

Don’t:

  • Touch someone’s head or point your feet at another person, as it is considered rude in Thai culture.
  • Disrespect the royal family or the Buddhist religion.
  • Forget to wear sunscreen and bring mosquito repellent to outdoor activities.

Safety and Health

Safety in Sukhothai

Sukhothai is a relatively safe destination, but visitors should still be vigilant with their belongings and avoid travelling alone at night.

Health in Sukhothai

Tap water in Sukhothai is not drinkable. It’s best to purchase bottled water and avoid street food that may cause stomach issues. Dengue fever is present in the area, so visitors should take measures to prevent mosquito bites.

Getting to Sukhothai: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Sukhothai

The closest airport to Sukhothai is Sukhothai Airport, which offers domestic flights from Bangkok and Chiang Mai.

Bus Stations Near Sukhothai

Sukhothai Bus Terminal is the primary station that connects to destinations such as Bangkok, Chiang Mai, and Ayutthaya.

Other Options

Trains run from Bangkok to Phitsanulok, located 60 kilometers south of Sukhothai. From there, visitors can take a bus or taxi to Sukhothai.

How to Get Around in Sukhothai

Taxis and tuk-tuks are available in Sukhothai for short distances, but the best way to explore the city and surrounding areas is by renting a bicycle or motorbike.

Where to Go Next from Sukhothai: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Chiang Mai: A city located in Northern Thailand, known for its stunning temples, markets, and coffee shops.
  • Ayutthaya: A UNESCO World Heritage site located near Bangkok, boasting ancient temples and ruins.
  • Mae Hong Son: A picturesque town that rests among the green hills and valleys of North Thailand, and is known for its serene landscape and adventure activities.

Conclusion

Sukhothai is a historical city with an abundance of cultural and natural attractions that offer an unforgettable experience for travelers. With its warm hospitality, delicious cuisine, and scenic views, Sukhothai is a must-visit destination for anyone traveling through Thailand’s north.
