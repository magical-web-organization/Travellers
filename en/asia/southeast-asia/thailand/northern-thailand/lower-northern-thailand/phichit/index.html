Introduction to Phichit

Located in Lower Northern Thailand, Phichit presents unique tourism opportunities for travelers. Situated between the two mighty rivers, the Nan River and the Yom River, the place holds a rich cultural and historical significance in Thailand. The ancient provincial capital houses some of the best temples and natural attractions that are worth exploring. In this guide, we’ll explore the best places to visit, where to eat and stay, and how to plan an unforgettable itinerary for your trip to Phichit.

List of Places to Visit in Phichit

Phichit Province is best known for its cultural and natural attractions:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Wat Tha Luang
  • Wat Chula Manee
  • Wat Nakhon Chum
  • Wat Ruesi Sala
  • The City Pillar Shrine
  • Folklore Museum

Natural Attractions of Phichit: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Khao Bang Pra Wildlife Sanctuary
  • Tung Salaeng Luang National Park
  • Waterfall Route
  • Sam Ngam Hot Springs
  • Bueng Si Fai

Hiking/Biking Routes of Phichit

Phichit is known for its natural beauty that offers great opportunities for outdoor activities like hiking and biking. Here are some of the best routes:

  • Tung Salaeng Luang Nature Trail
  • Khao Kho Wildlife Observation Tower Route
  • Pho Prathap Chang Route

Best Things to Do in Phichit: Activities and Tours

  • Participate in a Wang Chao Ceremony
  • Attend the Phichit Boat Racing Festival
  • Check out the local markets
  • Take a boat tour of the Nan River

Week-Long Itinerary for Phichit

Day 1: Explore the local historical and cultural sites, including Wat Tha Luang and The City Pillar Shrine

Day 2: Visit the Khao Bang Pra Wildlife Sanctuary

Day 3: Hike Tung Salaeng Luang Nature Trail

Day 4: Relax at Sam Ngam Hot Springs

Day 5: Bike Pho Prathap Chang Route

Day 6: Take a boat tour of the Nan River

Day 7: Experience the Wang Chao Ceremony

Local Culture and Customs of Phichit

  • Phichit residents mostly speak Northern Thai dialect.
  • The primary religion is Buddhism.
  • The Wang Chao Ceremony is an important annual event.

Best Time to Visit Phichit

The best time to visit Phichit is from November to February, as the weather is pleasant all day long with clear blue skies and cool breezes.

Do’s and Don’ts of Phichit

Do:

  • Dress modestly while visiting the temples.
  • Always greet locals with the traditional Wai gesture.
  • Participate in local events and festivities.

Don’t:

  • Do not touch the head of any person or statue of Buddha.
  • Do not point with your feet towards any holy site or person.

Safety and Health

Safety in Phichit

Phichit is a safe place to visit, but be cautious while traveling at night or alone. Always keep your belongings with you for safety purposes.

Health in Phichit

Carry insect repellent and sunscreen with you. Also, drink only bottled water to avoid water-borne illnesses.

Getting to Phichit: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Phichit

The nearest airports to Phichit are:

  • Phitsanulok Airport
  • Chiang Mai International Airport

Bus Stations Near Phichit

Phichit Bus Terminal is a significant station serving regular buses to Bangkok, Chiang Mai, and other provinces of Thailand.

Other Options

Apart from buses, taxis and car rentals are also available as alternative transportation options.

How to Get Around in Phichit

Taxis, Tuk-Tuk, and motorbike rentals are the primary means of transportation in Phichit.

Where to Go Next from Phichit: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Chiang Mai: Visit the stunning temples and hills of Chiang Mai.
  • Lampang: Go for an elephant ride and visit the ancient temples.
  • Sukhothai: Explore the historical sites and ruins of the ancient capital of Thailand.

Conclusion

Phichit is an ideal place to experience excellent natural and cultural diversity. The small province is one of the most underrated but promising tourist destinations in Thailand. This guide will help you explore the best of Phichit, from visiting historical sites to enjoying outdoor adventures. Don’t forget to try the local delicacies, shop for some souvenirs, and take away beautiful memories of your trip to Phichit.
