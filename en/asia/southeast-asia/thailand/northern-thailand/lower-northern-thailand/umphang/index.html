Introduction to Umphang

Umphang is a district in the northern part of Tak province, located in Lower Northern Thailand. The district is known for its rugged mountain ranges, stunning waterfalls, and beautiful natural attractions. It is a perfect destination for adventure seekers, nature lovers, and those looking for an off-the-beaten-path experience.

In this travel guide, we will take a closer look at the must-visit places in Umphang, the best things to do, where to eat, where to stay, and much more.

List of Places to Visit in Umphang

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Wat Tham Suea

One of the most popular cultural attractions in Umphang is Wat Tham Suea, also known as the Tiger Cave Temple. The temple is located on a hilltop, surrounded by lush greenery. Visitors can climb up to the hilltop and enjoy the stunning panoramic views of the surrounding landscape.

Umphang Museum

The Umphang Museum is a small but interesting museum that showcases the history and culture of the Umphang district. The museum displays a range of artifacts, photographs, and exhibits related to the local history, culture, and traditions of the area.

Natural Attractions of Umphang: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Thi Lor Su Waterfall

Thi Lor Su Waterfall is one of the most beautiful waterfalls in Thailand and is a must-visit attraction of Umphang. The waterfall is situated in the Thi Lor Su National Park and is surrounded by lush forests and mountains. Visitors can take a dip in the natural pools, enjoy a picnic, or go trekking in the scenic surroundings.

Mae Klong River

Mae Klong River is a popular spot for whitewater rafting and kayaking adventures in Umphang. The river flows through the Umphang Wildlife Sanctuary and provides visitors with a thrilling ride through the scenic wilderness.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Umphang

Umphang Wildlife Sanctuary Trek

The Umphang Wildlife Sanctuary Trek is one of the most popular hiking routes in Umphang and is known for its rugged terrain, dense forests, and exotic wildlife. The trek takes visitors through the heart of the sanctuary and offers a chance to spot some of Thailand’s most iconic wildlife, such as elephants, deer, gibbons, and snakes.

Mae Klong River Cycling Trail

The Mae Klong River Cycling Trail is a scenic and adventurous ride that takes visitors through the beautiful countryside of Umphang. The trail runs alongside the Mae Klong River and offers stunning views of the surrounding mountains, forests, and villages.

Best Things to Do in Umphang: Activities and Tours

Thi Lor Su Waterfall Tour

A Thi Lor Su Waterfall Tour is a must-do activity for visitors to Umphang. The tour takes visitors on a scenic journey through the Thi Lor Su National Park and provides them with an opportunity to witness the stunning beauty of the waterfall up close.

Whitewater Rafting on Mae Klong River

Whitewater rafting on the Mae Klong River is a thrilling adventure that can be enjoyed by both experienced and novice rafters. The rapids range from class II to class IV, providing visitors with a good mix of excitement and fun.

Food and Drink Scene of Umphang

Must-Try Dishes

Gang Pa (Jungle Curry)

Gang Pa is a spicy and savory curry that is popular in the Northern provinces of Thailand, including Umphang. The dish is made with a mix of fresh vegetables, herbs, and spices, and is typically served with steamed rice.

Sai Ua (Northern-Style Sausage)

Sai Ua is a flavorful sausage that is popular in the Northern provinces of Thailand. The sausage is made with pork, herbs, and spices, and is grilled to perfection. It is served with sticky rice and a spicy dipping sauce.

Local Beverages

Nam Manao (Lime Juice)

Nam Manao is a refreshing drink made with fresh lime juice, sugar, and water. It is a popular drink in Thailand and is a perfect beverage to quench your thirst on a hot day in Umphang.

Cha Yen (Thai Iced Tea)

Cha Yen is a sweet and creamy Thai iced tea that is popular all over Thailand. The drink is made with black tea, condensed milk, and sugar, and is served on ice. It is a perfect beverage to enjoy with spicy Thai food.

What to Buy in Umphang: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Hill Tribe Crafts and Textiles

The local villages in Umphang are known for their unique and colorful textiles, handwoven by the hill tribe people. Visitors can purchase a range of products, including bags, blankets, table runners, and clothing items.

Local Spices and Herbs

Thailand is famous for its aromatic and flavorful spices and herbs, and visitors to Umphang can purchase a range of local herbs and spices to bring home. Some of the popular items include lemongrass, galangal, turmeric, and chili paste.

Local Culture and Customs of Umphang

The local community in Umphang is known for its friendly and welcoming attitude towards visitors. Visitors are advised to show respect for local customs and traditions, dress modestly, and avoid overt displays of affection in public.

Best Time to Visit Umphang

The best time to visit Umphang is between November and February when the weather is pleasant and dry. The temperature during this time ranges from 15-30°C and provides visitors with the perfect weather for outdoor activities and sightseeing.

Do’s and Don’ts of Umphang

Do:

  • Respect the local customs and traditions
  • Dress modestly, especially when visiting religious sites
  • Carry a water bottle and sunscreen when exploring the natural attractions

Don’t:

  • Display overt displays of affection in public
  • Wear revealing clothes in public
  • Litter or damage the natural attractions

Safety and Health

Safety in Umphang

Umphang is a relatively safe district, and visitors are advised to take the usual precautions when traveling in any unfamiliar area, such as avoiding unlit and secluded areas at night, staying alert, and keeping valuable possessions safe.

Health in Umphang

It is advisable to carry insect repellent when traveling to Umphang as the district is known for its mosquito population. Visitors should also be up to date on their routine vaccinations and consult their doctor before traveling.

Getting to Umphang: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Umphang

The nearest airport to Umphang is Mae Sot Airport, which is around 250km away. From here, visitors can hire a taxi or take a bus to reach Umphang.

Bus Stations Near Umphang

Umphang Bus Terminal is the main bus station in the district and serves many cities in Thailand, including Bangkok, Chiang Mai, and Mae Sot.

Other Options

Visitors can also hire a private car or join a tour group to reach Umphang.

How to Get Around in Umphang

The best way to get around Umphang is by hiring a motorbike or a bicycle. Taxis and tuk-tuks are also available for short trips around the district.

Where to Go Next from Umphang: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Chiang Mai

Chiang Mai is a vibrant and culturally rich city located around 300km from Umphang. Visitors can explore the city’s ancient temples, bustling night markets, and enjoy a range of adventure activities.

Mae Sot

Mae Sot is a border town located around 250km from Umphang and is known for its rich cultural diversity. Visitors can explore the town’s bustling markets, visit its ancient temples, and enjoy a range of outdoor activities.

Conclusion

Umphang is the perfect destination for visitors looking for an off-the-beaten-path experience and an opportunity to explore Thailand’s natural and cultural beauty. From stunning waterfalls to exotic wildlife, vibrant local culture, and mouth-watering cuisine, Umphang has something for everyone. We hope this travel guide has provided you with all the information you need to plan your next adventure to Umphang.
