Si Satchanalai, a small ancient city in Northern Thailand, is a hidden gem for history buffs and outdoor enthusiasts alike. Situated in the Sukhothai Province, Si Satchanalai was once the capital of the Sukhothai Kingdom and is now a UNESCO World Heritage Site. This travel guide will take you through the top attractions, activities, accommodations, and more in Si Satchanalai.

Introduction to Si Satchanalai

Si Satchanalai, meaning “City of Good People,” was founded in the 13th century and became the second capital of the Sukhothai Kingdom after the royal family moved from Sukhothai. The city flourished during the reign of King Ram Khamhaeng, who is credited with creating the Thai alphabet and unifying Thailand.

Today, Si Satchanalai is known for its well-preserved ruins, ancient temples, and scenic beauty.

List of Places to Visit in Si Satchanalai

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  1. Si Satchanalai Historical Park - Explore the ruins of the ancient city, including the city wall, temples, and palaces.
  2. The Ramkhamhaeng National Museum - Learn about the history of the Sukhothai Kingdom and its eventual decline.
  3. Wat Chang Lom - Admire the 39 elephant statues surrounding the chedi, or Buddhist stupa.

Natural Attractions of Si Satchanalai: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  1. Mae Yom National Park - Hike through the lush forests and explore waterfalls in this beautiful national park.
  2. Saphan Waterfall - Take a refreshing dip in this picturesque waterfall.
  3. The Si Satchanalai Dam - Enjoy scenic views of the mountains and take a boat tour of the dam.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Si Satchanalai

  1. Mae Yom National Park - The park offers various trails, ranging from easy to difficult, and takes you through stunning natural landscapes.
  2. Doi Kaeo Forest Park - This forest park offers a 7-kilometer loop through the forest, with stunning views of the surrounding mountains and rice paddies.

Best Things to Do in Si Satchanalai: Activities and Tours

  1. Biking Tour - Rent a bike and explore the ancient city and nearby villages.
  2. Thai Cooking Class - Learn how to cook traditional Thai cuisine and taste delicious local dishes.
  3. Elephant Experience - Get up close and personal with gentle giants and learn about their conservation.

Food and Drink Scene of Si Satchanalai

Must-Try Dishes

  1. Khao Soi - This Northern Thai dish consists of coconut curry broth and egg noodles topped with crispy fried noodles.
  2. Bua Loy Nam Khing - A sweet and savory dessert made of rice flour balls in ginger syrup.
  3. Lon Pla Kem - A savory spread made from salted fish and fragrant spices.

Local Beverages

  1. Cha Yen - A sweet Thai iced tea made with condensed milk.
  2. Mekong Whiskey - A popular Thai whiskey made from rice.

Where to Stay in Si Satchanalai: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  1. Sriwilai Sukhothai Resort and Spa - A luxurious resort with beautiful views of the mountains and rice paddies.
  2. Le Charme Sukhothai Hotel - A modern hotel with an outdoor pool and restaurant.
  3. Ban Thai Guesthouse - A budget-friendly option with comfortable rooms and a friendly staff.

What to Buy in Si Satchanalai: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  1. Sangkhalok Pottery - Buy handmade pottery that dates back to the Sukhothai period and is still made using traditional techniques.
  2. Khanom Waan - A type of Thai sweet made from coconut and rice flour.
  3. Buddhist Amulets - Si Satchanalai is famous for the creation of Buddhist amulets, which are believed to bring good luck and protection.

Local Culture and Customs of Si Satchanalai

The people of Si Satchanalai are known for their hospitality, and visitors are expected to show respect for local customs. The traditional way of greeting is the wai, which involves placing your palms together in front of your chest and bowing your head.

Best Time to Visit Si Satchanalai

The best time to visit Si Satchanalai is from November to February, during the cool and dry season. The weather is pleasant, and the vegetation is lush and green.

Do’s and Don’ts of Si Satchanalai

Do:

  1. Dress modestly when visiting temples or holy sites.
  2. Be respectful of local customs and traditions.
  3. Try local food and drinks.

Don’t:

  1. Display public affection.
  2. Wear revealing clothing.
  3. Disrespect the national anthem.

Safety and Health

Safety in Si Satchanalai

Si Satchanalai is generally safe, but visitors should be aware of pickpockets and scams. Keep your valuables safe and be cautious when approached by strangers.

Health in Si Satchanalai

The tap water in Si Satchanalai is not safe to drink, so be sure to drink bottled or purified water. Mosquitoes are prevalent in the area, so bring insect repellent and wear protective clothing to avoid mosquito-borne illnesses.

Getting to Si Satchanalai: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Si Satchanalai

The nearest airport to Si Satchanalai is Sukhothai Airport, which is about 30 kilometers away. Flights are limited, and most visitors arrive via other means of transportation.

Bus Stations Near Si Satchanalai

The main bus station in Sukhothai is about 60 kilometers away from Si Satchanalai. Buses from Bangkok and other major cities in Thailand stop at this bus station.

Other Options

The most convenient way to get to Si Satchanalai is by private car or taxi. It takes about 1.5 hours from Sukhothai or 5 hours from Bangkok.

How to Get Around in Si Satchanalai

The best way to get around Si Satchanalai is by renting a bike or motorbike. Tuk-tuks and taxis are also available but may be more expensive.

Where to Go Next from Si Satchanalai: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  1. Sukhothai Historical Park - The old capital of the Sukhothai Kingdom, also a UNESCO World Heritage Site.
  2. Chiang Mai - A vibrant city known for its temples, food, and culture.
  3. Phrae - A town with well-preserved traditional architecture and silver handicrafts.

Conclusion

Si Satchanalai is a beautiful and historic destination that should be on every traveler’s bucket list. With its rich cultural heritage, stunning natural beauty, and friendly people, Si Satchanalai is a destination not to be missed. Plan your trip today and discover the wonders of this ancient city in Northern Thailand.
