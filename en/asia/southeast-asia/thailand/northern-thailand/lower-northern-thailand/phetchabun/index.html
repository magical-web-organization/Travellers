A Comprehensive Travel Guide to Phetchabun

Phetchabun is a hidden gem located in the Lower Northern region of Thailand. This charming province has something for everyone, from scenic views to historical sites to outdoor adventures. Here’s a guide to the best places to visit, things to do, and where to stay in Phetchabun.

Introduction to Phetchabun

Phetchabun is a peaceful province in the northern part of Thailand that is known for its beautiful mountains. It has a long history, and its location between the north and the central regions of Thailand means that it has been a crossroads of trade and culture for centuries. The province is home to three major rivers, which make it an excellent region for agriculture.

List of Places to Visit in Phetchabun

Phetchabun is a diverse province with a range of attractions to suit every taste. Here’s a list of the places you don’t want to miss:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Phetchabun City Pillar Shrine: This is a sacred site where locals come to pay their respects to the city’s guardian spirits. The shrine also features beautiful architecture and intricate carvings.
  • Wat Pha Sorn Kaew: This stunning temple is known for its intricate mosaic design, which covers almost every inch of the building’s exterior. The temple offers breathtaking views of the surrounding mountains.
  • Khao Kho Memorial: This historical site commemorates the battles fought between the Thai army and communist insurgents in the 1970s. The memorial features a museum, a statue of King Rama IX, and a beautiful park.

Natural Attractions of Phetchabun: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Phu Hin Rong Kla National Park: This park is an outdoor enthusiast’s paradise. It features hiking trails, waterfalls, and stunning views of the mountains. Visitors can also camp in the park overnight.
  • Nam Nao National Park: Home to a variety of flora and fauna, this park has something for everyone. It features hiking trails, scenic drives, and wildlife spotting.
  • Kaeng Sopha Waterfall: One of the most beautiful waterfalls in Thailand, Kaeng Sopha is located within the Kaeng Sopha Wildlife Sanctuary. The waterfall is surrounded by lush greenery, making it a perfect spot for a picnic.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Phetchabun

Phetchabun’s beautiful mountains offer plenty of opportunities for hiking and biking. Here are the best routes for those looking to explore the great outdoors:

  • Phu Thap Boek: One of the highest peaks in the province, Phu Thap Boek offers stunning views of the surrounding mountains. The mountain is also home to a number of hiking trails and campsites.
  • Khao Kho: This mountain is another popular spot for hiking and biking. It offers breathtaking views of the valley below.
  • Khao Kho Wind Farm: This area features more than 20 wind turbines and offers stunning views of the surrounding mountains. It’s a popular spot for both hiking and photography.

Best Things to Do in Phetchabun: Activities and Tours

Phetchabun has plenty of things to offer visitors. Here are some of the best activities and tours to add to your itinerary:

  • Hot Air Balloon Ride: Take a spectacular hot air balloon ride over the mountains for a unique and unforgettable experience.
  • Coffee Plantation Tour: Phetchabun is known for its coffee, and a tour of a local coffee plantation is a must for coffee lovers.
  • ATV Adventure: Explore the mountains on an ATV for an adrenaline-filled adventure.

Food and Drink Scene of Phetchabun

Phetchabun has a unique and delicious culinary scene that is not to be missed. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Khao Khluk Kapi: A local dish made with shrimp paste, rice, veggies, and meat.
  • Sai Ua: A spicy Northern Thai sausage made with pork and herbs.
  • Gaeng Om: A spicy soup made with meat and vegetables.

Local Beverages

  • Phetchabun Coffee: This province is known for its delicious coffee, which is grown on the mountainsides.
  • Cha Phetchaburi: A refreshing Thai tea served over ice.
  • Nam Manao: A sweet and sour lime juice.

Where to Stay in Phetchabun: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Phetchabun has plenty of accommodation options to suit any budget. Here are some of the best places to stay:

  • Bualuang Hotel: A mid-range hotel located in the heart of Phetchabun City.
  • Baan Rabiangmai: A charming guesthouse located in a peaceful village.
  • Phumek Sirikun: A budget-friendly hostel located near the mountains.

What to Buy in Phetchabun: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Phetchabun has a number of unique souvenirs and local products to offer visitors. Here are some things to consider bringing home:

  • Phetchabun Coffee: Roasted and packaged beans are widely available throughout the province.
  • Handicrafts: Local artisans produce a variety of handicrafts, including pottery, woven baskets, and woodcarvings.
  • Herbal Products: Phetchabun is known for its herbal remedies, which are thought to have healing properties.

Local Culture and Customs of Phetchabun

Phetchabun has a rich cultural heritage that is worth exploring. Here are some aspects of the local culture and customs to be aware of:

  • Religion: Many locals practice Buddhism, and there are a number of important temples throughout the province.
  • Dress Code: Visitors should dress modestly when visiting temples or other religious sites.
  • Festivals: Phetchabun is home to a number of lively festivals throughout the year, including the Flower Festival and the Rocket Festival.

Best Time to Visit Phetchabun

The best time to visit Phetchabun is between November and February, when the weather is cool and dry. During this time, the mountains are also covered in beautiful pink and white blossoms.

Do’s and Don’ts of Phetchabun

Do:

  • Respect local customs and traditions.
  • Dress modestly when visiting religious sites.
  • Pay attention to the weather, especially during the rainy season.

Don’t:

  • Disrespect the monarchy or other important cultural symbols.
  • Litter or damage the environment.
  • Overstay your visa or break any immigration laws.

Safety and Health

Safety in Phetchabun

Overall, Phetchabun is a safe destination for travelers. However, visitors should take standard safety precautions, such as avoiding walking alone at night and keeping valuables secure.

Health in Phetchabun

Travelers to Phetchabun should be up-to-date on routine vaccinations. Visitors should also take precautions against mosquito-borne illnesses, such as dengue fever and malaria.

Getting to Phetchabun: Transportation Options and Logistics

Phetchabun is easily accessible by a range of transportation options. Here are some tips for getting to Phetchabun:

Airports Near Phetchabun

The nearest airport to Phetchabun is Suvarnabhumi Airport in Bangkok, which is about a four-hour drive from the province.

Bus Stations Near Phetchabun

There are a number of bus stations in Phetchabun Province, including Phetchabun Bus Terminal and Lom Sak Bus Terminal. Buses run regularly to and from other nearby cities and provinces.

Other Options

Visitors can also rent a car or motorbike to explore the province on their own.

How to Get Around in Phetchabun

The easiest way to get around Phetchabun is by car. Visitors can rent a car or use a taxi or tuk-tuk to get around the city. There are also a number of tour companies that offer guided tours of the province.

Where to Go Next from Phetchabun: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

There are a number of nearby destinations that visitors to Phetchabun might want to consider:

  • Sukhothai: The ancient capital of Siam is less than three hours from Phetchabun and is home to a number of UNESCO World Heritage Sites.
  • Chiang Mai: Known as the cultural capital of Northern Thailand, Chiang Mai is about four hours from Phetchabun and offers a range of cultural and outdoor activities.
  • Bangkok: The bustling capital of Thailand is about four hours from Phetchabun and is home to a number of important cultural sites and attractions.

Conclusion

Phetchabun is a charming province that offers a range of attractions for visitors. From its beautiful mountains to its rich cultural heritage, there’s something for everyone in this hidden gem of Thailand. Plan your trip today and discover all that Phetchabun has to offer.
