Introduction to Chiang Mai Province

Chiang Mai Province is a cultural and historical gem located in the Northern region of Thailand. Often referred to as the “Rose of the North,” it’s the second-largest province in Thailand and has become one of the most popular tourist destinations in Southeast Asia. With its rich history and diverse natural landscape, Chiang Mai Province has something for everyone, from foodies and culture buffs to outdoor enthusiasts and adventure seekers.

List of Places to Visit in Chiang Mai Province

If you’re planning a trip to Chiang Mai Province, these are the must-see attractions that should be on your list:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Chiang Mai Old City
  • Wat Phra That Doi Suthep
  • Chiang Mai National Museum
  • Tribal Museum
  • Chiang Mai City Arts and Cultural Center
  • Three Kings Monument
  • Baan Haw Mosque

Natural Attractions of Chiang Mai Province: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Doi Inthanon National Park
  • Mae Sa Waterfall
  • Huay Tung Tao Lake
  • Chiang Mai Night Safari
  • Chiang Mai Zoo
  • Bua Tong Waterfall
  • Mae Sa Elephant Camp

Hiking/Biking Routes of Chiang Mai Province

Chiang Mai Province is a paradise for outdoor enthusiasts. There are plenty of great hiking and biking routes in and around the province. Here are some recommendations:

  • Doi Suthep-Pui National Park
  • Mae Sa Valley Trail
  • Chae Son National Park
  • Ob Luang National Park
  • Mae Ngat Valley

Best Things to Do in Chiang Mai Province: Activities and Tours

Whether you’re interested in exploring cultural sites, adventurous activities, or culinary experiences, Chiang Mai Province has plenty to offer. Here are some of the best things to do:

  • Attend a cooking class and learn how to make traditional Thai dishes
  • Experience a Thai massage or spa treatment
  • Take a hot air balloon tour over the province’s stunning landscape
  • Visit one of the many night markets for some local shopping and street food
  • Take a river rafting tour along the Mae Taeng River

Week-Long Itinerary for Chiang Mai Province

If you’re planning a week-long trip to Chiang Mai Province, here’s a suggested itinerary:

Day Activity
1 Visit Chiang Mai Old City and Three Kings Monument
2 Explore Doi Suthep-Pui National Park and Wat Phra That Doi Suthep
3 Trek in Doi Inthanon National Park
4 Take a cooking class and visit a night market
5 Visit the Mae Sa Elephant Camp and Mae Sa Waterfall
6 Enjoy a hot air balloon tour and a Thai massage
7 Relax at Huay Tung Tao Lake and Chang Mai Zoo

Food and Drink Scene of Chiang Mai Province

Chiang Mai Province has a vibrant food and drink scene with a wide variety of local dishes and beverages. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Khao Soi (curry noodle soup)
  • Sai Oua (northern Thai sausage)
  • Larb Moo (minced pork salad)
  • Som Tam (spicy papaya salad)
  • Mango Sticky Rice

Local Beverages

  • Chang Beer
  • Singha Beer
  • Cha Yen (Thai iced tea)
  • Nam Manao (lime juice)

Where to Stay in Chiang Mai Province: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Chiang Mai Province has accommodation options for every budget and preference. Here are some recommendations in different areas:

  • Old City: Na Nirand Romantic Boutique Resort, De Naga Hotel Chiang Mai
  • Nimman: Akyra Manor Chiang Mai, X2 Vibe Chiang Mai Decem Hotel
  • Riverside: Shangri-La Hotel Chiang Mai, Anantara Chiang Mai Resort

What to Buy in Chiang Mai Province: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Chiang Mai Province is known for its unique handicrafts and local products. Here are some recommendations:

  • Thai silk products
  • Hand-carved wooden items
  • Silver jewelry
  • Hand-painted ceramics
  • Traditional Thai art

Local Culture and Customs of Chiang Mai Province

Chiang Mai Province has a rich cultural heritage and unique customs that visitors should be aware of. Here are some things to keep in mind:

  • Dress modestly when visiting temples and religious sites
  • Remove your shoes before entering homes and some businesses
  • Show respect for the Thai monarchy and national symbols
  • Be mindful of cultural sensitivities

Best Time to Visit Chiang Mai Province

The best time to visit Chiang Mai Province is between November and February when the weather is pleasant and dry. March to May is hot and dry, and June to October is the rainy season.

Do’s and Don’ts of Chiang Mai Province

Here are some do’s and don’ts to keep in mind when visiting Chiang Mai Province:

Do:

  • Respect local culture and customs
  • Try local food and drink
  • Negotiate prices in markets and street vendors

Don’t:

  • Disrespect the Thai monarchy or national symbols
  • Touch someone’s head, as it’s considered rude
  • Show affection in public

Safety and Health

Here are some tips for staying safe and healthy in Chiang Mai Province:

Safety in Chiang Mai Province

  • Avoid unlicensed transportation
  • Be cautious around stray animals
  • Keep valuables safe and secured

Health in Chiang Mai Province

  • Use insect repellent to avoid mosquito-borne diseases
  • Drink bottled water to avoid stomach issues
  • Be wary of street food hygiene

Getting to Chiang Mai Province: Transportation Options and Logistics

Chiang Mai Province is easily accessible from the rest of Thailand and neighboring countries. Here are some transportation options:

Airports Near Chiang Mai Province

  • Chiang Mai International Airport (CNX)

Bus Stations Near Chiang Mai Province

  • Arcade Bus Station
  • Chiang Mai Bus Terminal (also known as Chang Puak Bus Terminal)

Other Options

  • Train: Chiang Mai Railway Station
  • Car: rental options available

How to Get Around in Chiang Mai Province

Here are some ways to get around Chiang Mai Province:

  • Tuk-tuks and songthaews (local shared taxis)
  • Grab (ride-hailing service)
  • Rent a motorbike or bicycle

Where to Go Next from Chiang Mai Province: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Chiang Mai Province is a great hub for exploring Northern Thailand. Here are some ideas for nearby destinations:

  • Chiang Rai Province: home to the famous White Temple
  • Pai: a laid-back town with beautiful natural scenery
  • Mae Hong Son Province: a mountainous region with a unique culture

Conclusion

Chiang Mai Province is a destination that truly offers something for every type of traveler. With its rich culture, stunning landscape, and delicious cuisine, it’s no wonder that it has become a popular spot for tourists from all over the world. Whether you’re interested in exploring history and culture or seeking outdoor adventure, Chiang Mai Province is sure to delight.
