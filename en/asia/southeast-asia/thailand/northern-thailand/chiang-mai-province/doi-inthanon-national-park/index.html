Introduction to Doi Inthanon National Park

Doi Inthanon National Park is a must-visit destination for nature lovers and outdoor enthusiasts who are looking for a relaxing escape into lush forests, scenic waterfalls, and misty mountains in Northern Thailand. Located in Chiang Mai Province, this national park covers an area of 482 square kilometers and is home to diverse flora and fauna, including rare bird species, orchids, and mosses. Doi Inthanon, the highest mountain in Thailand, rises up to 2,565 meters above sea level and offers spectacular views of the surrounding valleys and rice paddies.

In this comprehensive guide, we will provide you with everything you need to know to plan a memorable trip to Doi Inthanon National Park, from popular attractions to local cuisine, from transportation options to safety tips.

Food and Drink Scene of Doi Inthanon National Park

Must-Try Dishes

  1. Khao Soi - Northern Thai noodle soup with chicken or beef, coconut milk, and crispy noodles
  2. Sai Oua - Grilled or fried Northern Thai sausage with chilli, garlic, and lemongrass
  3. Larb Kua - Spicy Northern Thai minced pork salad with herbs and vegetables
  4. Kaeng Hung Lay - Northern Thai curry with pork, ginger, garlic, and tamarind

Local Beverages

  1. Cha Yen - Thai iced tea with sweetened condensed milk
  2. Singha Beer - Local Thai beer
  3. Lao Khao - Rice whisky

Best Time to Visit Doi Inthanon National Park

The best time to visit Doi Inthanon National Park is during the dry season from November to February when the weather is cool and dry, and the views are clear.

Safety and Health

Safety in Doi Inthanon National Park

  1. Always follow the instructions of the guide and park rangers.
  2. Carry a first aid kit and be aware of emergency services.
  3. Avoid hiking or trekking alone.
  4. Check the weather forecast before starting any outdoor activity.

Health in Doi Inthanon National Park

  1. Be aware of the presence of malaria and other tropical diseases.
  2. Wear long-sleeved clothing and use insect repellent.
  3. Drink only bottled or boiled water.
  4. Follow proper hygienic practices and avoid uncooked food.
