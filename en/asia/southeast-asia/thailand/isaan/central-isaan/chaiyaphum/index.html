Chaiyaphum is a serene and charming town located in central Thailand’s Isaan region, known for its abundant natural beauty and rich cultural heritage. The town is surrounded by lush green forests, stunning limestone mountains, and scenic waterfalls, making it a perfect spot for adventure lovers and nature enthusiasts. Furthermore, the town boasts many cultural landmarks steeped in ancient history. Let’s dive into the details of what Chaiyaphum has to offer!

Introduction to Chaiyaphum

Chaiyaphum dates back to the 14th century and has a rich tapestry of history, culture, and natural attractions to explore. The town’s central location makes it easy to get around and offers a unique glimpse into the traditional way of life of the northeastern part of Thailand. You can expect to find authentic Thai food, friendly locals, and unspoiled landscapes that are sure to leave a lasting impression.

List of Places to Visit in Chaiyaphum

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Prang Ku

An impressive ancient shrine built during the Khmer Empire, Prang Ku is an architectural marvel steeped in rich history. The shrine offers visitors a glimpse into the region’s pre-Buddhist era and provides a unique window into Thailand’s cultural heritage.

Ban Khwao

A village located in the southern part of Chaiyaphum, Ban Khwao is famous for its traditional silk weaving. The village features small cottages and scenic rice fields, where you can watch the silk weaving process in action.

Natural Attractions of Chaiyaphum: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Pa Hin Ngam National Park

Pa Hin Ngam National Park is a stunning area of natural beauty that boasts sandstone cliffs, bizarre rock formations, and a plethora of flora and fauna. The park is a perfect spot for hiking, camping, and exploring the beautiful natural attractions.

Tat Ton National Park

Tat Ton National Park offers picturesque waterfalls surrounded by dense forests and limestone cliffs. You can go for a refreshing swim, relax in the serene surroundings, or spot the birdlife in this beautiful park.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Chaiyaphum

Hiking and biking the beautiful trails around Chaiyaphum is an enjoyable way to explore the region’s natural beauty. A popular hiking trail is the stunning Phu Khao Phanom Rung, a mountain located in the northeastern part of the town, which offers scenic views and an insight into the region’s historical importance.

Best Things to Do in Chaiyaphum: Activities and Tours

Explore the Local Markets

Visiting the local markets and street food stalls in Chaiyaphum is an excellent way to discover the local cuisine and traditions. You can try delicious street food, handmade souvenirs, and fresh fruits.

Attend the Phi Ta Khon Festival

The Phi Ta Khon Festival is a colorful and vibrant celebration held annually in Dan Sai district, which is about an hour’s drive from Chaiyaphum. The festival is an excellent opportunity to experience the local culture and traditions.

Week-Long Itinerary for Chaiyaphum

A week-long itinerary for Chaiyaphum could include a visit to Prang Ku, exploring the outdoors at Pa Hin Ngam National Park, a day of hiking at Phu Khao Phanom Rung, attending the Phi Ta Khon Festival in Dan Sai district, visiting the local silk villages and organic farms, and spending a day at Tat Ton National Park.

Food and Drink Scene of Chaiyaphum

Must-Try Dishes

Sai Oua

Sai Oua is a traditional sausage made with pork, herbs and spices. The distinctive taste and aroma of Sai Oua is a testament to the region’s culinary expertise.

Kai Yang (Grilled Chicken)

Kai Yang is a popular grilled chicken dish served with papaya salad. The grilled chicken is marinated with lemongrass, coriander, and garlic, giving the chicken meat its signature taste.

Local Beverages

Lamai Khaow

Lamai Khaow is a local drink made from coconut juice, palm sugar, pandan leaves, and ice. The refreshing taste of Lamai Khaow makes it an excellent drink to cool off on a hot day.

Nam Som

Nam Som is a drink made with fermented rice water and served with ice and sugar. This drink has a unique taste and is an acquired taste for some.

Where to Stay in Chaiyaphum: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Chaiyaphum offers a variety of accommodation options for tourists. The town has several budget-friendly guesthouses, comfortable hotels, and charming boutique B&Bs. Some of the top accommodation options include Lux Garden Hotel and Grand Palace Hotel.

What to Buy in Chaiyaphum: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Chaiyaphum is famous for its silk fabrics, hand-woven baskets, and organic produce. Some of the best places to buy souvenirs include the local markets and boutique stores in the town.

Local Culture and Customs of Chaiyaphum

Chaiyaphum’s culture has deep roots in northeastern Thai traditions. It is common for the locals to practice Buddhism, eat traditional pork dishes, and practice silk weaving. The locals are known for their friendliness and hospitality, and visitors are often welcomed with open arms.

Best Time to Visit Chaiyaphum

The best time to visit Chaiyaphum is between November and March when the weather is mild, and the skies are clear. The region experiences a hot and humid climate, and visitors are advised to carry sunscreen and avoid midday outdoor activities during the summers.

Do’s and Don’ts of Chaiyaphum

Do:

  • Dress modestly when visiting temples and sacred sites
  • Try the local food and drink
  • Respect the local customs and traditions

Don’t:

  • Touch people’s heads, it is considered disrespectful in Thai culture
  • Show public displays of affection
  • Disrespect the Thai monarchy

Safety and Health

Safety in Chaiyaphum

Chaiyaphum is generally considered a safe town, however, visitors are advised to take the usual precautions such as locking your valuables safely and avoiding walking alone at night.

Health in Chaiyaphum

Visitors are advised to carry insect repellents and wear protective clothing to avoid mosquito bites. Chaiyaphum’s hospitals and clinics offer standard medical facilities and are easily accessible.

Getting to Chaiyaphum: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Chaiyaphum

The nearest airport to Chaiyaphum is Khon Kaen Airport, which is located around 150 km from the center of the town.

Bus Stations Near Chaiyaphum

There are several direct bus services from Bangkok’s Mo Chit Bus Terminal to Chaiyaphum, with a travel time of about 5 to 6 hours.

Other Options

Visitors can also hire a cab or book a private transfer to get to Chaiyaphum from neighboring towns or cities.

How to Get Around in Chaiyaphum

Visitors can rent a motorbike or a bicycle to explore the town’s scenic countryside. Taxis and local tuk-tuks are also readily available for visitors’ convenience.

Where to Go Next from Chaiyaphum: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

The nearby destinations and excursions around Chaiyaphum include visiting the Ban Tha Sawan village, Thek Chok Waterfall, and the Khorat Plateau.

Conclusion

Chaiyaphum is a charming little town that offers a perfect blend of natural beauty, rich cultural heritage, and friendly locals. With this comprehensive travel guide, visitors to Chaiyaphum can explore the best places to visit, where to stay, what to eat and explore, making it easy to plan an unforgettable trip to this hidden gem of Thailand.
