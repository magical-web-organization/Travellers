Introduction to Kalasin

Situated in Central Isaan, Kalasin is a small province in the northeastern region of Thailand. A hidden gem that is known for its cultural and natural attractions, Kalasin attracts visitors looking for a unique experience away from the beaten path. With fascinating museums, ancient ruins, stunning waterfalls, and outdoor adventures, Kalasin offers something for everyone. In this tour guide, we will share the top things to do, places to visit, where to stay, what to eat, and local culture of Kalasin.

List of Places to Visit in Kalasin

Kalasin features a range of culturally significant attractions and natural wonders that must be visited. Below are some of the must-visit places in Kalasin.

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Phutthasathan Phu Sing: A seat of Buddhism and the tallest mountain in Kalasin, Phu Sing is home to ancient Buddhist temples. It also offers stunning views of the surrounding areas.

  • Kalasin National Museum: A repository of Kalasin’s rich cultural heritage, the museum displays ancient artifacts, antiques, and exhibits on the history and culture of this charming province.

  • Phrathat Yakhu: A 1,000-year-old Buddhist temple, Phrathat Yakhu is a UNESCO World Heritage site. The temple houses a relic of Buddha and provides breathtaking views of the horizon.

Natural Attractions of Kalasin: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Phu Faek Forest Park: A lush conservation area comprising rainforests, waterfalls, and natural wildlife that offer plenty of opportunities for hiking and outdoor activities.

  • Phu Pha Lek National Park: Famous for its towering limestone cliffs, verdant forests, and deep valleys, Phu Pha Lek National Park is the ultimate destination for thrill-seekers and nature lovers.

  • Lampao Dam: A popular spot for water sports enthusiasts, Lampao Dam is an excellent location for those looking to enjoy a relaxing day of kayaking, fishing, or picnicking.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Kalasin

Kalasin offers a range of hiking and biking options for nature enthusiasts and those who want to explore the province’s scenic terrain. Phu Faek Forest Park and Phu Pha Lek National Park are the two most popular destinations for hiking and biking tours.

Best Things to Do in Kalasin: Activities and Tours

Kalasin is a destination that has something for everyone, whether you’re searching for adventure, relaxation, or cultural immersion. Here are some of the best things to do in Kalasin.

  • Visit Ban Kut Fu: Located in the suburbs of Kalasin, Ban Kut Fu is known for its handwoven silk, which is produced using traditional techniques passed down through generations.

  • Explore the Ruins of Ku Pum: Ku Pum is an ancient Khmer temple complex from the 10th century. Its intricate carvings and stunning architecture offer a glimpse into Thailand’s rich cultural heritage.

  • Participate in a Buffalo Race: Buffalo racing is a traditional spectacle that takes place in Kalasin each October. The races are accompanied by food and cultural events.

Food and Drink Scene of Kalasin

Kalasin boasts a unique culinary scene, with local delicacies that reflect the province’s cultural heritage. Here are some must-try dishes and beverages.

Must-Try Dishes

  • Kaeng Kai Mot Daeng: A spicy red curry made with chicken, coconut milk, and red chillies.

  • Nam Prik Pla Ra: A dip made from fermented fish, green chilli, garlic, and lime juice. It is often served with raw vegetables.

  • Khao Ji: A rice-based snack filled with peanuts, pork, or taro, and deep-fried until crispy.

Local Beverages

  • La Mai Mai: A local alcoholic drink made from distilled rice that has a fruity aroma and slightly sweet taste.

  • Kafae Boran: A traditional Thai-style coffee made with ground coffee beans and brewed with condensed milk.

Where to Stay in Kalasin: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Kalasin offers a variety of accommodation options, from budget-friendly guesthouses to luxury hotels. Some of the top-rated options include:

  • Rai Kayla Hotel: A cozy and comfortable hotel located near the town center, offering a range of amenities, including free Wi-Fi, restaurant, and free parking.

  • Petchlarn Hotel: A stylish hotel offering top-class facilities such as spa services, fitness center, and a swimming pool.

What to Buy in Kalasin: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Kalasin offers a wide range of local products that make great souvenirs. Here are some of the best items to buy in Kalasin:

  • Silk Scarves: Handwoven silk scarves of various colors and patterns.

  • Porcelain: Handcrafted porcelain ware, including tea sets and vases.

  • Hand-Carved Wooden Sculptures and Accessories: Intricately designed wooden products made by skilled local craftsmen.

Local Culture and Customs of Kalasin

Kalasin is a province rich in culture and customs that reflect its history and heritage. The locals are known for their warm hospitality and for preserving their customs despite the wave of modernization. When visiting Kalasin, it is essential to be respectful of these traditions.

Best Time to Visit Kalasin

The best time to visit Kalasin is between November to February, when the weather is cooler and more pleasant.

Do’s and Don’ts of Kalasin

When visiting Kalasin, it is important to be respectful of local traditions and customs. Here are some essential do’s and don’ts:

Do:

  • Respect the local customs and traditions
  • Learn a few basic Thai phrases
  • Dress appropriately when visiting temples or other religious places
  • Take your shoes off when entering someone’s house

Don’t:

  • Touch anyone on the head, as it is considered rude
  • Show any public displays of affection
  • Disrespect the monarchy or Buddhism
  • Raise your voice or become aggressive in public

Safety and Health

Safety in Kalasin

Kalasin is a relatively safe destination for tourists, with low crime rates. However, it is important to take standard safety precautions, such as securing your valuables and being aware of your surroundings.

Health in Kalasin

Visitors to Kalasin should take necessary health precautions, such as getting vaccinated and drinking bottled water.

Getting to Kalasin: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Kalasin

The closest airport to Kalasin is Udon Thani International Airport, which is located 90 km away.

Bus Stations Near Kalasin

Kalasin Bus Terminal is the most convenient option for the arriving by bus. It has regular scheduled services to Bangkok, Udon Thani, and other nearby destinations.

Other Options

There are also local trains that run from Bangkok to Kalasin.

How to Get Around in Kalasin

The most convenient ways to get around in Kalasin are by taxi or songthaew (shared taxi). Motorbike rentals are also available in the city.

Where to Go Next from Kalasin: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Kalasin is located in a region rich in cultural and natural attractions. Some of the nearby destinations and excursions include:

  • Khon Kaen: A nearby city known for its rich culture, historical temples, and bustling night markets.

  • Phu Kao-Phu Phan Kham National Park: A vast national park that covers an area of 570 square kilometers, featuring breathtaking scenery and stunning viewpoints.

  • Nong Khai: A city situated on the Thai-Lao border, offering scenic views of the Mekong River and a range of cultural attractions.

Conclusion

Kalasin is a small but culturally rich destination that offers something for everyone. With its breathtaking natural beauty, ancient ruins, and vibrant local culture, Kalasin is an ideal destination for those looking to explore Thailand off the beaten path. We hope this comprehensive tour guide will help you plan your trip to Kalasin and make the most of your visit.
