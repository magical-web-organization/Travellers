Introduction to Maha Sarakham

Maha Sarakham is a small, charming province located in Central Isaan, Northeastern Thailand. It is known for its rich cultural heritage, fascinating history, and abundant natural beauty. Maha Sarakham is an ideal destination for travelers looking for an authentic Thai experience off the beaten path.

The province is home to several important Buddhist temples, national parks, museums, and ancient ruins. Additionally, Maha Sarakham is known for its delicious local cuisine, bustling night markets, and friendly locals.

Whether you are interested in exploring the local culture, indulging in culinary delights, or embarking on outdoor adventures, there is something for everyone in Maha Sarakham. This guide will provide you with everything you need to know to plan the ultimate trip to this hidden gem in Southeast Asia.

List of Places to Visit in Maha Sarakham

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Wat Phra That Na Dun

Wat Phra That Na Dun is a famous Buddhist temple perched on a hilltop overlooking the city of Maha Sarakham. It is one of the most revered temples in the area and attracts pilgrims from all over the country. The temple features a grand pagoda, intricate murals, and stunning views of the surrounding countryside.

Ban Chiang National Museum

The Ban Chiang National Museum is a must-visit destination for history buffs. It houses a collection of ancient artifacts dating back to the prehistoric period and provides insights into the region’s unique cultural heritage. The museum is located in Nong Han, a small town about 20 kilometers from Maha Sarakham.

Phi Mai Historical Park

Phi Mai Historical Park is an ancient complex comprising several well-preserved Khmer-style temples dating back to the 10th century. The park is located in the neighboring province of Nakhon Ratchasima but is easily accessible from Maha Sarakham. It is a UNESCO World Heritage Site and a popular destination for history lovers.

Natural Attractions of Maha Sarakham: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Phu Pha Thoep National Park

Phu Pha Thoep National Park is a nature lover’s paradise and one of the most beautiful places to visit in Maha Sarakham. It offers stunning views of the surrounding mountains, lush forests, and serene waterfalls. The park is also home to rare wildlife, including the Siamese hare, black bear, and various species of birds.

Huai Yang Reservoir

Huai Yang Reservoir is the largest reservoir in Maha Sarakham and a popular spot for fishing and boating. The reservoir is surrounded by scenic hills and forests and offers a peaceful escape from the hustle and bustle of the city. Visitors can rent a boat and explore the reservoir on their own or hire a local guide for a more in-depth experience.

Namtok Tat Thong National Park

Namtok Tat Thong National Park is a serene natural wonder located a short drive from Maha Sarakham. The park features a towering waterfall, lush forests, and several hiking trails. Visitors can trek through the jungle and enjoy panoramic views of the surrounding countryside.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Maha Sarakham

Maha Sarakham is a haven for outdoor enthusiasts, and there are several excellent hiking and biking routes to explore. The following are some of the most popular trails:

Phu Pha Thoep Trek

The Phu Pha Thoep Trek is a challenging but rewarding hike that takes visitors through the stunning natural beauty of Phu Pha Thoep National Park. The trail is about 9 kilometers long and requires moderate physical fitness.

Maha Sarakham Bike Loop

The Maha Sarakham Bike Loop is a 50-kilometer circuit that takes riders through picturesque countryside and charming villages. The route is perfect for those who want to explore the local culture and nature on two wheels.

Namtok Tat Thong Trail

The Namtok Tat Thong Trail is a moderate hike that takes visitors to the top of Tat Thong Waterfall. The route is about 4 kilometers long and offers stunning views of the surrounding jungle.

Best Things to Do in Maha Sarakham: Activities and Tours

Local Markets and Food Tours

Maha Sarakham is known for its vibrant night markets, street food, and local delicacies. Visitors can take a guided food tour and sample some of the province’s most delicious dishes, including som tam (papaya salad), kai yang (grilled chicken), and khao neow mamuang (mango sticky rice).

Traditional Thai Massage

Traditional Thai massage is a therapeutic and relaxing experience that involves stretching and deep-tissue massage. Visitors can enjoy an authentic Thai massage at one of the many local spas and wellness centers in Maha Sarakham.

Temple Visits and Meditation

Maha Sarakham is home to several beautiful Buddhist temples, and visitors can participate in meditation sessions and learn about the local religion and culture.

Food and Drink Scene of Maha Sarakham

Must-Try Dishes

Maha Sarakham is known for its spicy, flavorful, and diverse cuisine. The following are some of the must-try dishes:

Som Tam

Som tam is a crispy and spicy papaya salad that is popular throughout Thailand. It is a refreshing and delicious dish that is perfect for hot summer days.

Khao Man Gai

Khao man gai is a traditional Thai dish that consists of poached chicken, rice, and chili sauce. It is a simple yet flavorful dish that is popular for breakfast and lunch.

Pad Thai

Pad Thai is a classic Thai dish that features stir-fried rice noodles with vegetables, meat, and peanuts. It is a favorite among locals and tourists alike and can be found in almost every Thai restaurant.

Local Beverages

Thai Iced Tea

Thai iced tea is a refreshing and sweet beverage that consists of black tea, condensed milk, and sugar. It is a popular drink in Thailand and can be found in almost every local restaurant and cafe.

Cha Yen

Cha yen is a cold and creamy drink that is made with black tea, milk, sugar, and spices. It is a popular drink throughout Southeast Asia and is a must-try in Maha Sarakham.

Where to Stay in Maha Sarakham: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Maha Sarakham offers a wide range of accommodation options to suit every budget and preference. The following are some of the best hotels, hostels, and guesthouses in the area:

Avatara Resort

Avatara Resort is a luxurious hotel located in the heart of Maha Sarakham. It features spacious rooms, a swimming pool, a fitness center, and a restaurant serving local and international cuisine.

Rim Nam Baan Suan Resort

Rim Nam Baan Suan Resort is a cozy and peaceful resort located on the banks of the Chi River. It features traditional Thai-style bungalows, a garden, and a restaurant serving delicious local dishes.

Ubonwan Resort

Ubonwan Resort is a family-friendly resort located in the countryside of Maha Sarakham. It features spacious rooms, a swimming pool, a playground, and a restaurant serving authentic Thai cuisine.

What to Buy in Maha Sarakham: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Visitors to Maha Sarakham can shop for unique souvenirs, handicrafts, and local products at several markets and shops throughout the province. Some of the best items to buy include:

Thai Silk

Thai silk is a high-quality and luxurious textile that is known for its vibrant colors and intricate designs. Visitors can buy beautiful silk products, including scarves, shawls, and clothes, at several local shops in Maha Sarakham.

Pottery

Maha Sarakham is known for its colorful and unique pottery products, including bowls, vases, and other decorative items. Visitors can buy authentic and high-quality pottery at local markets and shops.

Local Culture and Customs of Maha Sarakham

Thailand is a country with a rich cultural heritage, and Maha Sarakham is no exception. Visitors should respect the local customs, manners, and beliefs when traveling in the province. Some tips to keep in mind include:

  • Always remove your shoes before entering a temple or a local’s home.
  • Dress modestly and avoid wearing revealing clothing.
  • Learn a few basic phrases in Thai, such as “hello” and “thank you.”
  • Be respectful to monks and avoid touching their robes or asking personal questions.

Best Time to Visit Maha Sarakham

The best time to visit Maha Sarakham is between November and February when the weather is cooler and less humid. The temperatures during this time range from 20 to 30 degrees Celsius, making it a perfect time to explore the natural and cultural attractions in the province.

Do’s and Don’ts of Maha Sarakham

Do:

  • Try the local food and beverages.
  • Learn about the local culture and customs.
  • Respect the local people, temples, and beliefs.
  • Enjoy outdoor activities, such as hiking and biking.

Don’t:

  • Touch or take photos with wild animals.
  • Disrespect the local beliefs or culture.
  • Get involved in illegal activities, such as drugs or gambling.

Safety and Health

Safety in Maha Sarakham

Maha Sarakham is a relatively safe destination for travelers. However, visitors should exercise the same level of caution as they would in any foreign country. Some tips to keep in mind include:

  • Keep an eye on your valuables and avoid carrying large amounts of cash.
  • Use common sense when traveling, especially at night.
  • Be cautious when driving or renting a motorcycle.

Health in Maha Sarakham

Maha Sarakham is a relatively safe destination in terms of health concerns. However, visitors should take some basic precautions, such as drinking bottled water, washing hands frequently, and avoiding street food that looks unhygienic. Visitors should also seek medical attention if they experience any symptoms of illness.

Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Maha Sarakham

The nearest airport to Maha Sarakham is Maha Sarakham Airport. However, this airport currently only serves domestic flights. The nearest international airport is Bangkok’s Suvarnabhumi Airport, located about 400 kilometers away.

Bus Stations Near Maha Sarakham

Maha Sarakham has a central bus station that serves destinations throughout the province and the region. Visitors can also take buses from Bangkok’s Mochit Bus Terminal to Maha Sarakham.

Other Options

Visitors can also reach Maha Sarakham by train or car. There are several train stations in the region, and Maha Sarakham is accessible by several major highways.

How to Get Around in Maha Sarakham

Maha Sarakham is a small province, and many of the attractions are within a short drive from the city center. Visitors can travel around the province by taxi, tuk-tuk, motorcycle taxi, or by renting a car or motorcycle from a local rental agency.
