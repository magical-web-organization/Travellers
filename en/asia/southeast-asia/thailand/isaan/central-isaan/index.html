Introduction to Central Isaan

Central Isaan is a region located in northeast Thailand that offers visitors a unique blend of culture, history, and natural beauty. This area, also known as the Khorat Plateau, is home to a number of unique attractions and is well worth a visit for anyone looking to experience the real Thailand.

List of Places to Visit in Central Isaan

Here are some of the top places to visit in Central Isaan:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Phimai Historical Park: This impressive Khmer temple complex is one of the largest in Thailand and dates back to the 11th century.
  • Ban Prasat Archaeological Site: This site contains ancient ruins and artifacts from an ancient civilization that existed in the area more than 2,000 years ago.
  • Maha Viravong National Museum: This museum showcases a vast collection of artifacts and antiques related to Thailand’s southeast Asian history.
  • Wat Pa Sutthawat: This is a Buddhist temple known for its beautiful architecture and ornate design.

Natural Attractions of Central Isaan: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Phu Wiang National Park: This is a great location for hiking and enjoying the natural beauty of the region.
  • Khao Yai National Park: This park is home to a range of wildlife and is a great spot for camping and hiking.
  • Chet Sao Noi Waterfall: This waterfall is located near Khao Yai National Park and is a popular spot for swimming and picnicking.
  • Nam Nao National Park: Known for its stunning scenery and wildlife, Nam Nao National Park is a great spot for camping and hiking.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Central Isaan

Central Isaan is home to some stunning hiking and biking trails. Some of the best options include:

  • Phu Pha Man National Park: This park offers some great hiking trails that are suitable for both beginners and experienced hikers.
  • Wang Nam Khiao: This is a popular spot for cyclists and offers some stunning views of the surrounding countryside.
  • Phu Kradueng National Park: This park features a range of hiking and trekking routes that are suitable for all levels of fitness.

Best Things to Do in Central Isaan: Activities and Tours

Here are some of the best things to do in Central Isaan:

  • Visit a silk factory and learn about the traditional methods of silk production
  • Take a trip to a local market and experience the vibrant atmosphere of activity and color
  • Visit a vineyard and learn about the local wines produced in the region
  • Attend a traditional dance performance or festival to experience the unique culture of Central Isaan

Week-Long Itinerary for Central Isaan

Day 1: Visit Phimai Historical Park and explore the ancient ruins of the Khmer temple complex.

Day 2: Hike in Khao Yai National Park and spot some of the wildlife in the area.

Day 3: Explore the local markets and sample some of the delicious street food.

Day 4: Take a bike ride through the stunning countryside of Wang Nam Khiao.

Day 5: Visit a vineyard and learn about the production of local wines.

Day 6: Visit the Ban Prasat Archaeological Site and discover the ancient ruins and artifacts of an ancient civilization.

Day 7: Relax and unwind at a local hot spring.

Food and Drink Scene of Central Isaan

Central Isaan is known for its delicious cuisine and local specialties. Here are some of the must-try dishes and beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Som Tam: This is a spicy papaya salad that is a popular dish in the region.
  • Larb: This is a spicy minced meat salad that is a staple of northeastern Thai cuisine.
  • Nam Tok: This is a spicy beef salad that is usually served with sticky rice.
  • Sai Ua: This is a type of sausage made with ground pork and a range of herbs and spices.

Local Beverages

  • Mekhong: This is a popular Thai whiskey that is produced in the region.
  • Singha Beer: This is a locally produced beer that is very popular in Thailand.
  • Thai Iced Tea: This is a sweet and creamy tea that is made with condensed milk and is a popular drink throughout the country.

Where to Stay in Central Isaan: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

There are a range of accommodation options available in Central Isaan, from budget-friendly hostels to luxury resorts. Some popular options include:

  • Phimai Paradise Hotel: This hotel offers comfortable rooms and easy access to Phimai Historical Park.
  • Rimtarninn Hotel: This hotel is located in the heart of Khon Kaen and offers comfortable rooms at an affordable price.
  • Irawadee Resort: This is a luxury resort located in the heart of the countryside and offers beautiful views of the surrounding hills.

What to Buy in Central Isaan: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

There are a range of unique souvenirs and local products that you can buy in Central Isaan. Some popular options include:

  • Silk products: The region is known for its traditional silk production, and you can find a range of silk products, including scarves, bags, and clothing.
  • Local wines: There are a number of vineyards in the area, and you can purchase bottles of locally produced wine to take home with you.
  • Handmade pottery: There are a number of pottery studios in the region, and you can find a range of unique and handmade pottery products to take home.

Local Culture and Customs of Central Isaan

Central Isaan has a rich and unique culture that is influenced by its history and geography. The people of the region are known for their friendly and welcoming nature, and visitors are often struck by the strong sense of community and tradition that permeates the area. Buddhism is the predominant religion in the region, and there are a number of Buddhist temples and monasteries that you can visit to learn more about the local religious practices.

Best Time to Visit Central Isaan

The best time to visit Central Isaan is between November and February, when the weather is cooler and drier. The region can get quite hot and humid during the summer months, so it is best to plan your visit for the cooler months if possible.

Do’s and Don’ts of Central Isaan

Do:

  • Dress modestly when visiting temples or other religious sites.
  • Be respectful of local customs and traditions.
  • Try the local cuisine and sample the street food.
  • Smile and be friendly to locals - they are known for their warm hospitality.

Don’t:

  • Disrespect the local culture or traditions.
  • Engage in public displays of affection.
  • Take photos of people without their permission.
  • Ignore local laws and customs.

Safety and Health

Safety in Central Isaan

Central Isaan is generally a safe and friendly place to visit, but it is still important to take basic safety precautions. Avoid walking alone at night and be aware of your surroundings. It is also a good idea to keep your valuables in a secure location and to be cautious when using ATMs.

Health in Central Isaan

The tap water in Central Isaan is not safe to drink, so it is best to stick to bottled water or other bottled drinks. It is also a good idea to take basic precautions against mosquitoes, especially during the rainy season, to avoid mosquito-borne illnesses like dengue fever.

How to Get Around in Central Isaan

There are a range of transportation options available in Central Isaan, including taxis, tuk-tuks, and songthaews (shared taxis). Motorbikes can also be rented for those who prefer to explore the region independently.

From ancient ruins to stunning natural scenery, there is something for everyone in this unique and interesting part of the country. Whether you are interested in history, culture, or outdoor adventures, Central Isaan is sure to impress.
