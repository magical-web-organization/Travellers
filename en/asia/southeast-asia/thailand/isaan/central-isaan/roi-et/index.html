Introduction to Roi Et

Tucked away in the northeastern region of Thailand, Roi Et offers a taste of authentic Isaan culture. Known as the “Land of One Hundred Rice Fields,” this province is home to picturesque countryside views, ancient temples, and traditional festivals. The name “Roi Et” derives from the seven ancient ponds that were constructed to provide water during dry periods. With its serene atmosphere and warm hospitality, Roi Et is a must-visit for anyone seeking an off-the-beaten-path experience in Thailand.

List of Places to Visit in Roi Et

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Roi Et National Museum

The Roi Et National Museum is a fascinating place that offers a glimpse into the history of the province. The museum houses a collection of artifacts and exhibits that date back to the Dvaravati and Khmer eras. Visitors can see ancient pottery, bronze utensils, traditional clothing, and Buddhist images.

Wat Pho Chai

Wat Pho Chai is one of the most important religious sites in Roi Et. The temple features a stunning 68-meter-high pagoda that is visible from anywhere in the province. Inside the pagoda, visitors will find a Buddha statue and can climb to the top to enjoy sweeping views of the surrounding countryside.

Natural Attractions of Roi Et: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Somdet Phra Srinagarindra Park

Somdet Phra Srinagarindra Park, also known as Bueng Phlan Chai, is a gorgeous public park that surrounds a peaceful lake. Visitors can rent a bicycle or paddleboat to explore the park and take in the scenic views.

Pha Nam Yoi

Pha Nam Yoi is a geological wonder that is located about 40 kilometers from Roi Et town. This cliff boasts a unique shape and offers stunning views of the surrounding landscape. It’s a popular spot for hiking and rock climbing.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Roi Et

Nimmanoradee Cycling Route

The Nimmanoradee cycling route is a 38-kilometer-long trail that weaves through rice fields and small villages. It’s a great way to experience the countryside and get some exercise.

Charoen Sin Waterfall Trail

The Charoen Sin Waterfall Trail is a moderately difficult hiking trail that leads to a beautiful waterfall. Along the way, visitors can admire the lush forest and spot wildlife like monkeys and birds.

Best Things to Do in Roi Et: Activities and Tours

Roi Et Elephant Village

At the Roi Et Elephant Village, visitors can interact with these gentle giants and learn about their daily routines. The tour includes a ride on an elephant’s back through the surrounding countryside.

Ban Wai Tai Silk Weaving Village

Ban Wai Tai Silk Weaving Village is a small community that specializes in traditional silk weaving. Visitors can watch the process of turning silk threads into beautiful fabrics and purchase some souvenirs to take home.

Food and Drink Scene of Roi Et

Must-Try Dishes

Tom Saep Roi Et

Tom Saep Roi Et is a spicy and sour soup that features pork bones, blood, and offal. It’s a traditional dish that is popular among locals.

Pla Ra

Pla Ra is a fermented fish dish that is often eaten with sticky rice. It’s a signature dish of Isaan cuisine and has a distinct flavor.

Local Beverages

Ubon Ratchathani Rice Wine

Ubon Ratchathani Rice Wine is a popular local beverage that is made by fermenting glutinous rice. It’s slightly sweet and has a low alcohol content.

Thai Tea

Thai Tea is a sweet and milky drink that is often served at restaurants and cafes. It’s a refreshing option for a hot day.

Where to Stay in Roi Et: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Ploy Palace Hotel

Ploy Palace Hotel is a luxurious hotel that offers spacious rooms and ample amenities. It’s located in the heart of Roi Et town and is ideal for travelers who want to be close to the action.

Baan Suan Homestay

Baan Suan Homestay is a charming guesthouse that is run by a local family. It offers cozy rooms and a warm atmosphere that will make visitors feel right at home.

What to Buy in Roi Et: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Silk Products

Roi Et is known for its high-quality silk products, including scarves, bags, and clothing. Visitors can purchase these items at local markets or at Ban Wai Tai Silk Weaving Village.

Thai Spices

Isaan cuisine is famous for its bold and spicy flavors, and visitors can take some of those flavors home by purchasing local spices like chili powder and lemongrass.

Local Culture and Customs of Roi Et

Roi Et has a rich cultural heritage that is celebrated through festivals, temples, and traditional customs. Visitors should be respectful of local traditions and dress modestly when visiting religious sites.

Best Time to Visit Roi Et

The best time to visit Roi Et is from November to February, when the weather is cool and dry. March to May can be uncomfortably hot, while June to October is the rainy season.

Do’s and Don’ts of Roi Et

Do:

  • Try local Isaan cuisine
  • Dress modestly when visiting temples
  • Learn some basic Thai phrases

Don’t:

  • Touch the head of a Thai person
  • Show public displays of affection

Safety and Health

Safety in Roi Et

Roi Et is a safe province, and violent crime is rare. However, visitors should watch out for pickpockets in crowded areas and take precautions to prevent theft.

Health in Roi Et

Tap water in Roi Et is not safe for drinking, so visitors should stick to bottled water. The province also has a high incidence of dengue fever, so visitors should use mosquito repellent and wear long sleeves and pants.

Getting to Roi Et: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Roi Et

The nearest airport to Roi Et is in Ubon Ratchathani, which is about 100 kilometers away. From there, visitors can take a bus or taxi to Roi Et town.

Bus Stations Near Roi Et

Roi Et has a bus station that connects to other cities in Isaan and beyond. Buses run regularly to Bangkok, Chiang Mai, and other popular destinations.

Other Options

Visitors can also take a train to Maha Sarakham, which is about 60 kilometers from Roi Et town. From there, they can take a bus or taxi to Roi Et.

How to Get Around in Roi Et

The best way to get around in Roi Et is by motorbike or bicycle. Visitors can rent these modes of transportation from local shops.

Where to Go Next from Roi Et: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Nakhon Phanom

Nakhon Phanom is a province that borders Laos and is known for its stunning Mekong River views and cultural landmarks.

Khon Kaen

Khon Kaen is a bustling city that is home to a variety of attractions, including temples, museums, and parks.

Conclusion

Roi Et is a hidden gem in Thailand that offers a unique perspective on Isaan culture. With its cultural landmarks, natural beauty, and warm hospitality, it’s a destination that shouldn’t be missed. Whether you enjoy outdoor adventures, trying new foods, or learning about local customs, Roi Et has something to offer for everyone.
