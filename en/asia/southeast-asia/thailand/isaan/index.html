Introduction to Isaan

Isaan is a region in northeastern Thailand, comprising of 20 provinces. It is bordered by Laos to the north and east, Cambodia to the southeast, and central Thailand to the south. Isaan is known for its rich cultural heritage, national parks, and fantastic food.

List of Places to Visit in Isaan

If you are planning a trip to Isaan, here’s a list of the top attractions to include in your itinerary:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Phimai Historical Park
  • Phanom Rung Historical Park
  • Ban Chiang Archaeological Site
  • Maha Viravong National Museum
  • Khmer Ruins of Muang Tam and Prasat Hin Phimai
  • Koh Ker and Preah Vihear Temples in Cambodia

Natural Attractions of Isaan: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Phu Kradueng National Park
  • Pha Taem National Park
  • Nam Nao National Park
  • Mekong River and Chiang Khan District
  • Kaeng Song Waterfall and Nong Khai Province
  • Sirindhorn Dam and Ubolratana Dam Scenic Views

Hiking/Biking Routes of Isaan

For adventure enthusiasts, Isaan offers some of the most challenging and scenic hiking and biking routes. Here are some popular trails to consider:

  • Phu Kradung Trekking
  • Nam Nao Limestone Cave Trekking
  • Bueng Khong Long Cycling Trail
  • Chiang Khan Biking Trail
  • Khon Kaen Forest Hiking

Best Things to Do in Isaan: Activities and Tours

Apart from the historical and natural attractions, Isaan offers many cultural tours and activities. Here are a few must-try experiences:

  • Join in a traditional Isaan dance class
  • Take a pottery class in the province of Dan Kwian
  • Visit a silk weaving village and try your hand at silk-making
  • Go rock climbing at Nam Phong National Park
  • Visit a local farm and experience rural farm life

Week-Long Itinerary for Isaan

If you have a week in Isaan, here’s a sample itinerary that covers all the top attractions:

Day Activities
1 Arrive in Khon Kaen, visit the National Museum, and explore the city
2 Drive to Phimai, visit Phimai Historical Park, and enjoy local cuisine
3 Take a trip to Ban Chiang Archaeological Site, and Go hiking in Phu Phra Bat Park
4 Visit temples in Udon Thani, Phu Kradueng National Park for hiking
5 Visit Khong Chiam for scenic views and relax by the beach
6 Enjoy nature at Pha Taem National Park and drive to Chiang Khan District
7 Take a boat ride along the Mekong River in morning and drive back to Khon Kaen

Food and Drink Scene of Isaan

Isaan has an exceptional food scene, and it’s famous for its spicy and flavorful cuisine. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Som Tam Salad (green papaya salad)
  • Larb Moot (spicy minced pork salad)
  • Tom Saep (hot and sour soup)
  • Pla Ra (fermented fish dish)
  • Khao Niao (sticky rice)

Local Beverages

  • Nam Manao (lime juice)
  • Lam Yai (longan fruit juice)
  • Nam Oy (sugar cane juice)
  • Beer Lao (popular Laotian beer)

What to Buy in Isaan: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Isaan has a unique shopping scene, where you’ll find traditional handicrafts, village-made textiles, and fresh produce. Here are some items to consider buying:

  • Silk fabrics and clothing
  • Mudmee tie-dye textiles
  • Pottery and ceramics
  • Handcrafted jewelry and accessories
  • Spices and herbs

Local Culture and Customs of Isaan

Isaanese culture is distinctive and has its traditions, customs, and dialect. Here are some essential things to know:

  • Isaanese people are known for their hospitality and friendliness
  • The local dialect differs from the standard Thai language
  • Buddhism is the predominant religion in the region
  • Mor lam and Luk Thung are popular traditional music and dance forms
  • Family and community are integral to the Isaanese way of life

Best Time to Visit Isaan

Isaan has a tropical climate, and the best time to visit is between November and February. During this time, the weather is pleasant, with cool temperatures and low humidity. Avoid visiting during the monsoon season, from May to October, when rainfall is high and flooding may occur.

Do’s and Don’ts of Isaan

Here are some essential tips to ensure you have a smooth and enjoyable trip to Isaan:

Do:

  • Dress modestly when visiting temples and religious sites
  • Try local food dishes and traditional Isaanese drinks
  • Learn basic phrases in the local language to communicate with the locals

Don’t:

  • Don’t touch anyone’s head, as it’s considered rude in Thai culture
  • Avoid pointing your feet at people or objects, as it’s also a sign of disrespect
  • Don’t litter in public areas

Safety and Health

Isaan is a relatively safe region to travel to, although it’s always a good idea to take some precautions. Here are some essential health and safety tips:

Safety in Isaan

  • Be cautious when driving on rural roads, as authorities may not patrol these areas
  • Keep valuables and important documents secure when traveling
  • Avoid hiring unlicensed taxis or motorcycle drivers

Health in Isaan

  • Drink bottled water or boiled water to avoid stomach illnesses
  • Get your vaccines and prophylaxis before your trip
  • Wear sunscreen and insect repellent

Getting to Isaan: Transportation Options and Logistics

Isaan is well connected to the rest of Thailand and international destinations via various modes of transportation. Here are some transportation options to consider:

Airports Near Isaan

  • Khon Kaen Airport
  • Udon Thani International Airport
  • Nakhon Phanom Airport
  • Roi Et Airport

Bus Stations Near Isaan

  • Bangkok to Khon Kaen Bus Terminal
  • Phuket to Udon Thani Bus Terminal
  • Chiang Mai to Nakhon Phanom Bus Terminal
  • Surat Thani to Roi Et Bus Terminal

Other Options

  • Trains from Bangkok to Nong Khai or Udon Thani
  • Private taxi services
  • Car rental companies

How to Get Around in Isaan

Isaan has an efficient local transportation system, with options ranging from buses and taxis to tuk-tuks and motorcycle taxis. Here’s how to get around:

  • Buses and minibusses are the most popular modes of public transport
  • Tuk-tuks and motorcycle taxis are common in urban areas
  • Car rental services are available for those who prefer self-driving

Where to Go Next from Isaan: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

If you have more time in Thailand, here are some destinations to consider visiting from Isaan:

  • Bangkok
  • Chiang Mai
  • Phuket
  • Krabi
  • Pattaya

Conclusion

Isaan is a fantastic destination for travelers who crave adventure, culture, and good food. With this comprehensive guide, you’ll have all the essential details to plan your trip, explore the top attractions, and make the most of your visit to northeast Thailand.
