Introduction to Sakon Nakhon

Sakon Nakhon is a province located in Northeastern Thailand, also known as Isaan. The capital city, also called Sakon Nakhon, is a small and charming town rich in history, culture, and natural beauty. Its location between the Phu Phan Mountains and the Mekong River gives it a unique setting, making it a great destination for outdoor enthusiasts, history buffs, and cultural travelers. This city is also well-known for its traditional silk production and the Phi Ta Khon Festival, a vivid and colorful mask festival that takes place annually.

List of Places to Visit in Sakon Nakhon

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Phra That Choeng Chum: This is the city’s landmark and the most important temple in Sakon Nakhon, representing the city’s history, culture, and religion.
  • Buddha Footprints Shrine: With a beautiful and peaceful atmosphere, this shrine is located on the foot of Nong Han Lake and is home to Buddha’s footprints, considered a holy site among Buddhists.
  • Ban Na Si Bunrueang Cultural Center: This small museum showcases local Thai arts and crafts, including the famous Sakon Nakhon silk.
  • Phu Phan National Park: With its limestone mountains, waterfalls, and caves, this park is the perfect place for a day trip, camping, hiking, and bird watching.

Natural Attractions of Sakon Nakhon: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Nong Han Lake: This is a large freshwater lake that offers stunning views of the surrounding mountains and is home to many species of fish and birds, a perfect place to enjoy a boat ride and fishing.
  • Phu Phan Mountains: These mountains offer great outdoor adventures including trekking, hiking, and camping. The mountain range covers more than 200 kilometers with a diverse landscape of limestone cliffs, forests, and waterfalls.
  • Phu Pha Lek National Park: This park offers unique landscapes such as sandstone formations, evergreen forests, and rare wildflowers. It is also home to many species of plants and animals, including the endangered Siamese crocodile.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Sakon Nakhon

  • Phu Pha Lek Trail: This is a challenging but beautiful 10 km hike with stunning views of the national park, the Phu Phan Mountains, and the Mekong River.
  • Ban Khok Kong Trail: This is an easy 4.5 km trail perfect for a leisurely bike ride. It follows the Sakon Nakhon canal through the rice paddies and lush countryside.

Best Things to Do in Sakon Nakhon: Activities and Tours

  • Phi Ta Khon Festival: Experience the vibrant and colorful mask festival held annually in June or July, where locals dress up in masks and costumes to ward off evil spirits.
  • Silk Weaving Workshops: Learn about silk production, from growing the worms to the final product, and participate in hands-on workshops.
  • Thai Cooking Classes: Learn how to cook traditional Isaan cuisine with local ingredients, including som tam (papaya salad) and sticky rice.
  • Cycling Tours: Join a local guide and explore the scenic countryside, visit temples, and learn about the city’s rich history and culture.

Food and Drink Scene of Sakon Nakhon

Must-Try Dishes

  • Som Tam: Papaya salad with fresh vegetables, peanuts, and chili. This dish is famous throughout Thailand, but the Isaan version is particularly spicy and served with sticky rice.
  • Gaeng Om: A flavorful soup with mixed vegetables, meat, and herbs, usually made with chicken or pork.
  • Sai Krok Isaan: Grilled fermented pork sausages, a popular street food in Isaan.
  • Kaeng Wai: Slow-cooked beef soup with fresh herbs, served with rice noodles.

Local Beverages

  • Lao Khao: A potent rice wine, usually homemade and served at local festivals and celebrations.
  • Nam Oi: A refreshing juice made from sugar palm tree sap, served with ice and a squeeze of lime.

Where to Stay in Sakon Nakhon: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • The Majestic Sakon Nakhon Hotel: A 4-star hotel located in the city center, with comfortable rooms and great views of the city.
  • Ban Phor Liang Meun Resort: A cozy resort located in the countryside, with beautiful views of the scenery and great amenities.
  • The Loft Hostel: A budget-friendly option located in the city center, with comfortable beds, a shared kitchen, and friendly staff.

What to Buy in Sakon Nakhon: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Sakon Nakhon silk: This traditional silk production is a must-buy souvenir. It is known for its unique patterns and bright colors.
  • Phu Phan Pottery: Beautiful handcrafted pottery made by local artisans, including vases, plates, and bowls.
  • Otop Products: Locally made products featuring high-quality wood carvings, handmade soaps, and herbal products.

Local Culture and Customs of Sakon Nakhon

  • Phi Ta Khon Festival: One of the most important festivals in the region, celebrating the end of Buddhist Lent with lively music, dancing, and colorful masks.
  • Buddhism and Spirit Worship: Sakon Nakhon has a strong Buddhist culture, and spirit worship is also practiced, as the city is home to many animistic groups.
  • Hospitality and Politeness: Like most Thais, the locals in Sakon Nakhon are very friendly and hospitable, often going out of their way to help visitors.

Best Time to Visit Sakon Nakhon

The best time to visit Sakon Nakhon is between November and February when the weather is cool and dry, making it perfect for outdoor activities. The temperature can go up to 40 °C in the summer months from March to May, and the rainy season from June to October can also affect outdoor activities.

Do’s and Don’ts of Sakon Nakhon

Do:

  • Learn some basic Thai phrases. This will go a long way in showing respect to the locals.
  • Try the local food and drinks. Sakon Nakhon has unique and delicious cuisine that you won’t find elsewhere in Thailand.
  • Dress appropriately when visiting temples. Cover your knees and shoulders and remove your shoes before entering.

Don’t:

  • Don’t disrespect the monarchy. Thailand has strict laws when it comes to disrespecting the royal family.
  • Don’t touch someone’s head or point your feet at them. These are considered disrespectful gestures in Thai culture.
  • Don’t haggle too much when buying souvenirs. While bargaining is acceptable, don’t push too hard and take advantage of local vendors.

Safety and Health

Safety in Sakon Nakhon

Overall, Sakon Nakhon is a safe destination for tourists. However, it is always important to take precautions and be aware of your surroundings, especially when traveling at night or in isolated areas.

Health in Sakon Nakhon

Thailand is considered a relatively safe place to travel in terms of infectious diseases. However, it is always recommended to consult with your doctor before traveling and to take precautionary measures, such as using insect repellent and practicing good hygiene.

Getting to Sakon Nakhon: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Sakon Nakhon

Sakon Nakhon Airport (SNO) is the closest airport to the city, with several domestic flights arriving throughout the day. The flight time from Bangkok is around 75 minutes.

Bus Stations Near Sakon Nakhon

There are several bus stations in the city that serve different destinations. The main bus station is located in the city center and offers regular services to Bangkok, Chiang Mai, and other major cities in Thailand.

Other Options

Private taxis and minivans can also be arranged from nearby cities such as Khon Kaen, Udon Thani, and Nakhon Phanom.

How to Get Around in Sakon Nakhon

The most popular way to get around Sakon Nakhon is by car or motorcycle. Several rental companies offer competitive rates, and it is important to always wear a helmet and drive safely. Tuk-tuks and taxis are also available in the city center.

Where to Go Next from Sakon Nakhon: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Nakhon Phanom: Known for its scenic views of the Mekong River and its historical sites, such as the Ho Chi Minh Trail and the Thai-Lao Friendship Bridge.
  • Udon Thani: One of the largest cities in Northeastern Thailand, known for its vibrant nightlife, urban parks, and cultural attractions.
  • Khao Yai National Park: A UNESCO World Heritage Site, located around six hours from Sakon Nakhon, famous for its dense forest, diverse wildlife, and stunning waterfalls.

Conclusion

Sakon Nakhon is a hidden gem in Northeastern Thailand, offering a unique blend of cultural sites, natural beauty, and adventure activities. From the vibrant Phi Ta Khon Festival to the tranquil Nong Han Lake, there is something for everyone in this charming city. With this comprehensive travel guide, you can plan your ideal itinerary and experience the best of Sakon Nakhon.
