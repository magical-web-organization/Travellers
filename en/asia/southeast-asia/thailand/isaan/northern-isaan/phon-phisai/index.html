Introduction to Phon Phisai

Phon Phisai is a district in the northeastern region of Thailand, also known as Isaan. Despite being less popular than its neighboring provinces, it is increasingly becoming a destination worth exploring, with its lush greenery, cultural sites, and local lifestyle.

Phon Phisai is located in Nong Khai Province, bordering Laos. Its name is derived from a local tree species called “Phisai,” which is abundant in the area.

The district is primarily an agricultural hub, with rice paddies, fruit orchards, and vegetable gardens dotting the landscape. The local industry includes weaving and pottery. Visitors can expect to see traditional practices and cultural remnants of the Tai Lao ethnic group.

This guide will dive into what to see, do, eat, and experience in Phon Phisai and how to plan the perfect trip to this hidden gem.

List of Places to Visit in Phon Phisai

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Wat Pho Chai - A Buddhist temple that houses a 400-year old Buddha statue and intricate wall murals.
  • Phu Thai Cultural Museum - A museum that showcases the cultural heritage of the Phu Thai people. The exhibits include traditional costumes, tools, handicrafts, and musical instruments.
  • Sala Keoku - A park that features giant sculptures of Buddhist and Hindu deities, created by Luang Pu Bunleua Surirat, a local artist.
  • Phra That Bang Phuan - A revered chedi (stupa) said to contain relics of the Buddha. It is situated on a hilltop, offering a panoramic view of the surrounding landscape.

Natural Attractions of Phon Phisai: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Phu Pha Thoep National Park - A nature reserve that covers over 80,000 hectares of forested mountains, waterfalls, and wildlife. Popular activities in the park include trekking, camping, bird-watching, and rock climbing.
  • Phu Thok - A scenic hilltop that can be accessed by climbing a steep staircase of 500 steps. Once on top, visitors will be rewarded with a panoramic view of the Mekong River and the surrounding countryside.
  • Phu Kao-Phuparn Forest Park - A protected forest that has several hiking trails leading to waterfalls and viewpoints. It is also home to a large population of native birds.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Phon Phisai

  • Phu Pha Thoep National Park Trek - This trek takes visitors through the lush jungles and ascending to the mountain ridges. The trail goes through many stunning viewpoints and ends at the top of Phu Pha Thoep, providing breathtaking vistas of the area.
  • Phu Thok Staircase Hike - A challenging but rewarding climb that leads to the top of Phu Thok hill.
  • Sala Keoku Park Bike Ride - Bring a bike and take an extra leisurely ride through Sala Keoku Park and get to see all the giant sculptures up close and personal.

Best Things to Do in Phon Phisai: Activities and Tours

  • Boat Ride Along the Mekong River - Take a boat ride along the Mekong River to witness the stunning beauty of the landscape.
  • Pottery Making Workshop - Observe and participate in a pottery-making workshop and learn about the ancient techniques used by local potters.
  • Local Market Experience - Visit a morning market to get the real local experience, and get your hands on some fresh fruits and vegetables, meats, and iconic local dishes of Isaan.
  • Tai Lao Weaving Workshop – Visit a local weaving community and learn how traditional textiles are produced.

Week-Long Itinerary for Phon Phisai

Food and Drink Scene of Phon Phisai

Must-Try Dishes

  • Larb - A spicy minced meat salad with toasted sticky rice, chili, and herbs.
  • Nam Tok - Char-grilled, sliced meat mixed with chilies, roasted rice powder, and herbs.
  • Som Tam - A tangy and spicy papaya salad mixed with tomatoes, green beans and peanuts.
  • Khao Soi - A classic curry noodle soup with chicken or beef, and crispy fried noodles.
  • Moo Ping - Char-grilled pork skewers marinated in a mix of garlic, coriander, and fish sauce.

Local Beverages

  • Lao Khao - A traditional rice spirit distilled and bottled at home. It is potent and usually served with a mixer.
  • Thai Tea - Sweet, milky tea made from Ceylon tea leaves, condensed milk, and spices. It is usually served cold and is found in many street stalls.
  • Sugar Cane Juice - Freshly squeezed juice from sugar canes, often flavored with lime, ginger, and mint.

Where to Stay in Phon Phisai: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Ban Kham Pun Homestay - A cozy homestay with traditional-style wooden houses and a lush garden. It offers various activities such as weaving workshops, cooking classes, and boating excursions.
  • Baan Sabai Rim Nam Resort - A resort-style guesthouse with an outdoor pool and scenic views of the Mekong River.
  • Phumon Restaurant and Apartment - A budget-friendly option located in the town center, with clean rooms and a restaurant offering local cuisine.

What to Buy in Phon Phisai: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Pottery - Handcrafted pottery by local artisans, usually made from black clay and decorated with unique patterns and motifs.
  • Phu Thai Clothing - Traditional Phu Thai dresses and accessories, handmade with colorful fabrics and intricate designs.
  • Rice - High-quality white, brown, and sticky rice, grown locally in the fertile farmland of Isaan.
  • Fruits and Vegetables - Fresh and tasty tropical fruits, such as mangoes, papayas, and durians, as well as chili, garlic, and lemongrass.

Local Culture and Customs of Phon Phisai

  • Language - The main language spoken in Phon Phisai is Thai, but many locals also speak Lao. English is not widely spoken.
  • Religion - The majority of people in Phon Phisai practice Theravada Buddhism, but there are also small communities of Christians and Muslims.
  • Etiquette - It is customary to take off your shoes before entering someone’s home or temple. Dress modestly, especially when visiting religious sites. Avoid pointing your feet at people or sacred objects.

Best Time to Visit Phon Phisai

The best time to visit Phon Phisai is during the cooler and drier months of November to February. At this time, the weather is mild, and the landscape is lush and green. March to May is the hot season, with high temperatures and occasional heatwaves. The rainy season starts from June to October, with occasional floods and landslides.

Do’s and Don’ts of Phon Phisai

Do:

  • Do try the local cuisine and street food
  • Do visit the temples, museums and cultural attractions
  • Do respect the local culture and customs
  • Do bring comfortable shoes for trekking and hiking
  • Do carry cash, as not all establishments accept cards.

Don’t:

  • Don’t disrespect local traditions and beliefs
  • Don’t litter or leave trash in public areas
  • Don’t touch or climb on the Buddha statues
  • Don’t go topless or wear revealing clothes in public areas.

Safety and Health

Safety in Phon Phisai

Phon Phisai is generally a safe destination. However, visitors should take extra precautions when trekking or hiking, as there are wild animals and uneven terrain. It is advisable to hire a local guide or join an organized tour.

Health in Phon Phisai

Visitors should have up-to-date vaccinations and carry adequate health insurance coverage. Dengue fever and malaria are common in the region, and preventive measures, such as using mosquito repellent, are advisable. Tap water is not safe to drink, so travelers should stick to bottled water or purified mineral water.

Getting to Phon Phisai: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Phon Phisai

The nearest airport to Phon Phisai is Udon Thani International Airport, which is about 80 km away. It is serviced by several domestic and international airlines, including Thai Airways, Air Asia, and Nok Air.

Bus Stations Near Phon Phisai

The easiest way to get to Phon Phisai is by bus. Regular buses run several times a day from nearby towns such as Nong Khai, Khon Kaen, and Udon Thani. The bus terminal is located in the town center, making it accessible to many parts of the district.

Other Options

Taxis and private hire vehicles can be arranged from nearby towns or airports for those who prefer more privacy.

How to Get Around in Phon Phisai

The best way to get around Phon Phisai is by renting a motorbike or a bicycle. Motorbike rentals are available from guesthouses and travel agencies for a minimal fee per day. Tuk-tuks and songthaews (pick-up trucks with benches at the back) are also popular among locals, but are comparatively more expensive.

Where to Go Next from Phon Phisai: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Nong Khai - A charming riverside town with a vibrant night market, excellent restaurants, and cultural sites, including the notorious Sala Kaew Ku sculpture park.
  • Loei - A mountainous and hilly province with stunning breathtaking views, lush forests filled with flowers, and ancient Khmer ruins.
  • Vientiane - The capital city of Laos, located across the Mekong River from Nong Khai. The city features ancient Buddhist temples, French colonial architecture, and a vibrant night market and restaurant scene.

Conclusion

Phon Phisai is a hidden gem in Northern Isaan, less crowded but just as enjoyable as its sister provinces.
