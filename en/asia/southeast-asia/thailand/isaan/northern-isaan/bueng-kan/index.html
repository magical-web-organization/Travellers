Introduction to Bueng Kan

Located in Northern Isaan in Thailand, Bueng Kan is one of the country’s newest provinces, having only been established in 2011. Despite its relative youth, this hidden gem offers a wealth of natural and cultural attractions, making it a must-visit destination for any traveler exploring the region. Here’s a comprehensive tour guide to help you plan your trip to Bueng Kan, Thailand.

List of Places to Visit in Bueng Kan

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Bueng Kan may be a relatively new province, but its history goes back millennia, and there are plenty of historical and cultural attractions to explore.

Bueng Khong Long Museum

Located in the city center, the Bueng Khong Long Museum is an excellent place to learn about the region’s natural and cultural history. The interactive exhibits take you on a journey through time, from the geological formation of the area to the first human settlement and on to the present day.

Wat Phu Tok

Nestled on a rocky outcrop just outside the city, Wat Phu Tok is a stunning example of Northern Isaan’s unique brand of Buddhism. The temple is built into the side of a cliff and is accessible via a series of precarious wooden walkways and staircases. The views from the top are absolutely breathtaking, making it well worth the effort.

Phu Singh Historical Park

For history buffs, the Phu Singh Historical Park is a must-visit attraction. The park features ruins from the Khmer Empire, dating back to the 10th century. Explore the ancient temples and learn about the region’s fascinating history.

Natural Attractions of Bueng Kan: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Bueng Kan is also home to some of Thailand’s most spectacular natural wonders, from towering mountains to serene lakes.

Phu Wua Wildlife Reserve

The Phu Wua Wildlife Reserve is a haven for nature lovers. The reserve covers over 90,000 acres and is home to a diverse range of flora and fauna, including elephants, tigers, leopards, deer, and more. Take a guided tour to explore the stunning landscapes of the reserve and spot some of the local wildlife.

Kaeng Khut Khu Rapids

For adventure seekers, the Kaeng Khut Khu Rapids are a must-visit attraction. The rapids offer some of the best white-water rafting in Thailand, with rapids ranging from Class II to Class IV. The stunning scenery and adrenaline-packed adventure make this a once-in-a-lifetime experience.

Sirindhorn Reservoir

The serene Sirindhorn Reservoir is a popular spot for fishing, boating, and relaxing. Take in the stunning views of the surrounding mountains, or rent a boat and explore the crystal-clear waters of the reservoir.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Bueng Kan

With its rugged terrain and stunning natural scenery, Bueng Kan is an excellent destination for hiking and biking enthusiasts.

Phu Thok

Located just outside the city, Phu Thok offers some of the best hiking in the province. The trail to the top is challenging but rewarding, with stunning views of the surrounding landscape at the summit.

Kaeng Khut Khu National Park

For mountain bikers, the Kaeng Khut Khu National Park offers some thrilling trails, with steep inclines and technical descents. The scenery is truly breathtaking, with rocky outcrops and stunning views at every turn.

Best Things to Do in Bueng Kan: Activities and Tours

There are plenty of activities and tours to keep you entertained during your stay in Bueng Kan.

Kayaking on the Mekong River

Take a guided kayak tour down the Mekong River, which flows along the border between Thailand and Laos. Enjoy the stunning views of the river and its surrounding scenery, and stop off at local villages along the way.

Thai Cooking Class

Learn how to cook some of Thailand’s most delicious dishes with a Thai cooking class. Discover the secrets of local cuisine, and create your own unique dishes under the guidance of experienced chefs.

Week-Long Itinerary for Bueng Kan

Here’s a sample itinerary for a week-long trip to Bueng Kan, Thailand:

Day 1: Explore the city center, including the Bueng Khong Long Museum and local markets.

Day 2: Visit Wat Phu Tok in the morning, and spend the afternoon hiking at Phu Thok.

Day 3: Take a guided tour of the Phu Wua Wildlife Reserve, and spot some of the local wildlife.

Day 4: White-water rafting at Kaeng Khut Khu Rapids, followed by a relaxing evening at Sirindhorn Reservoir.

Day 5: Explore the temples and ruins at Phu Singh Historical Park.

Day 6: Mountain biking in the Kaeng Khut Khu National Park, followed by a Thai cooking class.

Day 7: Kayaking on the Mekong River, with stops at local villages along the way.

Food and Drink Scene of Bueng Kan

Must-Try Dishes

No visit to Bueng Kan is complete without sampling some of the local cuisine. Here are some must-try dishes:

Tom Saep

A spicy and sour soup made with pork ribs, lemongrass, and chilies.

Larb

A salad made with minced pork or chicken, mint, onions, and chilies.

Northern Isaan Sausage

A pork sausage flavored with lemongrass, garlic, and chilies.

Local Beverages

Kaoliang

A strong rice-based liquor, popular in Northern Isaan.

Thai Iced Tea

A sweet and creamy iced tea, often served with condensed milk.

Where to Stay in Bueng Kan: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

There are plenty of accommodation options available in Bueng Kan, ranging from budget-friendly hostels to luxurious hotels.

The River House Resort & Spa

A luxurious resort with stunning views of the Mekong River.

Buengkan City Hotel

A modern hotel located in the heart of the city.

Bualuang Hotel

A budget-friendly option with clean and comfortable rooms.

What to Buy in Bueng Kan: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Local Handicrafts

Bueng Kan is known for its traditional crafts, including woven textiles, pottery, and wood carvings.

Kaeng Khut Khu Chili Paste

A spicy and flavorful chili paste, made with locally grown chilies and other ingredients.

Local Snacks

Don’t miss out on the chance to try some of Bueng Kan’s delicious snacks, including krayasat (a sweet and savory snack made with coconut, sugar, and sesame seeds), and khanom khrok (coconut cakes).

Local Culture and Customs of Bueng Kan

The people of Bueng Kan are known for their friendly and welcoming nature. It’s important to be respectful of local customs and traditions during your visit.

Best Time to Visit Bueng Kan

The best time to visit Bueng Kan is between November and February, when the weather is cool and dry. Avoid visiting during the rainy season (June to October), when flooding can occur in some areas.

Do’s and Don’ts of Bueng Kan

Do:

  • Dress modestly when visiting temples and other cultural attractions.
  • Try some of the local cuisine and snacks.
  • Be polite and respectful towards locals.

Don’t:

  • Eat or drink in public during the month of Ramadan.
  • Touch someone on the head (considered disrespectful).
  • Disrespect local customs and traditions.

Safety and Health

Safety in Bueng Kan

Bueng Kan is a safe destination for travelers. However, it’s important to take normal precautions to avoid theft and pickpocketing.

Health in Bueng Kan

No specific health risks are associated with travel to Bueng Kan. However, it’s a good idea to consult with a doctor before traveling and to take the necessary precautions to avoid mosquito bites.

Getting to Bueng Kan: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Bueng Kan

The nearest airport to Bueng Kan is Udon Thani International Airport, located approximately 125 km away from the city.

Bus Stations Near Bueng Kan

Buses are the most common mode of transportation for getting to Bueng Kan. Buses run regularly from Udon Thani and other nearby cities.

Other Options

Taxis and private cars are also available for hire, but they can be more expensive than taking a bus.

How to Get Around in Bueng Kan

The easiest way to get around Bueng Kan is by renting a car or hiring a private driver. However, there are also plenty of tuk-tuks and motorbike taxis available for hire in the city.

Where to Go Next from Bueng Kan: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Nong Khai

Located approximately 200 km away from Bueng Kan, Nong Khai is a charming city known for its stunning temples, markets, and the famous sculpture park Sala Keoku.

Vientiane, Laos

Just across the border from Nong Khai lies Vientiane, the capital city of Laos. Explore the city’s temples, museums, and markets, and sample some of the delicious local cuisine.

Conclusion

Bueng Kan is a hidden gem, offering a wealth of cultural and natural attractions that are waiting to be explored. With its friendly people, delicious cuisine, and stunning scenery, this province is the perfect destination for any traveler looking to escape the crowds and discover a unique corner of Thailand. So what are you waiting for? Plan your trip to Bueng Kan today!
