Introduction to Northern Isaan

Northern Isaan is a region located in the northeastern part of Thailand. It is home to some of the country’s most culturally significant sites, as well as natural wonders that will leave you in awe. If you’re looking for a place to explore off the beaten path, Northern Isaan is the perfect destination. In this travel guide, we’ll take you through the top places to visit, best things to do, and where to stay, eat, and shop in Northern Isaan.

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Northern Isaan is known for its rich cultural heritage, with many historical sites and museums to explore. Here are some of the top culturally significant attractions in Northern Isaan:

Phimai Historical Park

Phimai Historical Park is one of the most important Khmer temples in Thailand. The park is home to a well-preserved temple, which is said to have been built in the 11th century. The temple is dedicated to Shiva, and it is surrounded by a moat and outer walls. Visitors can explore the temple and learn about its history at the on-site museum.

Ban Chiang Archaeological Site

Ban Chiang Archaeological Site is a UNESCO World Heritage Site that dates back to the Bronze Age. It is home to some of the oldest pottery and metal artifacts in the world, as well as evidence of early rice cultivation. Visitors can explore the site and learn about the ancient civilization that once lived here.

Sirindhorn Museum

The Sirindhorn Museum is located in Kalasin and is dedicated to educating visitors about the cultural history and the geology of the region. The museum is named after Princess Maha Chakri Sirindhorn and showcases exhibits on the prehistoric times of Isaan.

Natural Attractions of Northern Isaan: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Northern Isaan is home to some of the most breathtaking natural wonders in Thailand. Here are some of the top natural attractions in Northern Isaan:

Phu Kradueng National Park

Phu Kradueng National Park is a popular destination for nature lovers. The park is home to several waterfalls, hiking trails, and a variety of endemic wildlife. Visitors can hike to the summit of Phu Kradueng, which offers stunning views of the surrounding mountains and valleys.

Khao Yai National Park

Khao Yai National Park is one of the largest national parks in Thailand. It is home to a diverse range of flora and fauna, including elephants, tigers, and gibbons. Visitors can hike through the park’s many trails and enjoy the stunning scenery of waterfalls, forests, and mountains.

Phu Phrabat Historical Park

Phu Phrabat Historical Park is a unique geological site that features unique rock formations and scenic views. The park is home to many ruins, including ancient temples, caves and pagodas. The highlight of the park is the large rock formation known as “Phra Non,” which resembles a reclining Buddha.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Northern Isaan

Northern Isaan offers some of the best hiking and biking routes in Thailand. Here are some of the top routes for outdoor enthusiasts:

Phu Kradueng Hiking Trail

The Phu Kradueng Hiking Trail is a challenging hike that takes visitors to the summit of Phu Kradueng. The trail takes approximately four to six hours to complete and offers breathtaking views of the surrounding landscape.

Loie Dan Sai Biking Trail

The Loie Dan Sai Biking Trail is a scenic and peaceful biking trail that takes visitors through the countryside of Northern Isaan. The trail follows the roads from Loei to Dan Sai, which passes through rice fields, orchards and forests.

Best Things to Do in Northern Isaan: Activities and Tours

Apart from visiting the top attractions and outdoor adventures, there are several other things to do in Northern Isaan. Here are some of the best activities and tours:

Traditional Thai Crafts Tour

Northern Isaan is known for its traditional Thai crafts, such as silk weaving, pottery, and basket weaving. Visitors can take a tour to learn about these crafts and see them being made by local artisans.

Authentic Local Cuisine Tour

Northern Isaan is also known for its unique cuisine, which is influenced by the neighboring countries of Laos and Cambodia. Visitors can join an authentic food tour to sample some of the local dishes such as som tam, larb, and nam tok.

Food and Drink Scene of Northern Isaan

Northern Isaan’s food and drink scene is diverse and delicious. Here’s what you should try:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Som Tam: a spicy green papaya salad
  • Larb: a spicy minced meat salad
  • Nam Tok: a salad made from thinly sliced grilled meat
  • Kaeng Om Gai: a chicken curry with herbs and spices

Local Beverages

  • Khao Ji: a local millet beer
  • Laombong: a fermented tea drink
  • Nam Manao: a refreshing lime juice drink

Local Culture and Customs of Northern Isaan

Northern Isaan’s culture is heavily influenced by Buddhism and the neighboring countries of Laos and Cambodia. Locals are known for their hospitality and warmth towards visitors.

Best Time to Visit Northern Isaan

The best time to visit Northern Isaan is during the cooler months of November to February, when the temperatures are lower. The region experiences a hot, humid climate for the rest of the year, making outdoor adventures less enjoyable.

Do’s and Don’ts of Northern Isaan

Here are some do’s and don’ts to keep in mind when visiting Northern Isaan:

Do:

  • Dress modestly when visiting temples and other religious sites.
  • Try the local cuisine and experience the culture.
  • Be respectful of the locals and their customs.

Don’t:

  • Disrespect the monarchy or Buddhism.
  • Disrespect the culture and beliefs of the locals.
  • Publicly display affection.
