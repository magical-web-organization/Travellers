Introduction to Nong Bua Lamphu

Nong Bua Lamphu is a small province located in the northeastern region of Thailand, also known as Isaan. The province is surrounded by lush green mountains and filled with beautiful scenic views. Its name literally translates to “the land of lotus ponds,” which is a nod to the many lotus ponds found throughout the region. Nong Bua Lamphu is an up-and-coming travel destination that offers a unique blend of culture, nature, and adventure.

List of Places to Visit in Nong Bua Lamphu

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Nong Bua Lamphu National Museum

The Nong Bua Lamphu National Museum provides visitors with a comprehensive insight into the province’s rich history and diverse culture. The museum houses various artefacts, traditional clothing, art, and displays that depict the region’s unique characteristics.

Natural Attractions of Nong Bua Lamphu: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Phu Kao-Phu Phan Kham National Park

Phu Kao-Phu Phan Kham National Park is located in the southern area of Nong Bua Lamphu province. The national park is a paradise for nature lovers, adventure seekers, and outdoor enthusiasts. The park offers stunning views of the mountains, rich and varied flora, and fauna.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Nong Bua Lamphu

Phu Langka National Park

Phu Langka National Park is a hub for hiking and trekking enthusiasts. With varied levels of difficulty, visitors can access multiple routes that offer scenic views and breathtaking waterfalls. The park provides trekkers with a chance to experience the serene and beautiful natural landscapes of Nong Bua Lamphu.

Best Things to Do in Nong Bua Lamphu: Activities and Tours

Cycle Tour

A cycle tour is among the most popular and fascinating ways to explore the province’s beauty. Visitors can cycle around the ponds, lush green fields, and take in top-notch views of the scenic mountains.

Food and Drink Scene of Nong Bua Lamphu

Must-Try Dishes

Sai Krok Isan

Sai Krok Isan is a unique and popular dish in northeastern Thailand. It is a fermented sausage made up of pork with garlic, chili peppers, sticky rice, and herbs, adding depth to its flavor.

Local Beverages

Sangsom

Sangsom is one of the most popular alcoholic beverages in Thailand. It is produced at the Sangsom Distillery in Nong Khai Province.

What to Buy in Nong Bua Lamphu: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Hand-Woven Fabrics

Hand-woven fabrics are among the most in-demand products in Nong Bua Lamphu. Visitors can purchase locally handwoven fabrics like silk woven in vibrant colors and refined patterns as souvenirs.

Local Culture and Customs of Nong Bua Lamphu

The local people of Nong Bua Lamphu still follow Thai traditions such as the “Wai” (a greeting that involves bowing one’s head while holding one’s palms together as a sign of respect).

Best Time to Visit Nong Bua Lamphu

The best time to visit Nong Bua Lamphu is from November to February during the cool and dry season.

Do’s and Don’ts of Nong Bua Lamphu

Do:

  • Dress appropriately when visiting temples.
  • Respect local customs and traditions.

Don’t:

  • Disrespect the royal family.
  • Litter in public places.

Safety and Health

Safety in Nong Bua Lamphu

Nong Bua Lamphu is generally a safe place but be aware of your surroundings and take usual precautions measures in mind. Visitors should carry a copy of their passport when out and about.

Health in Nong Bua Lamphu

Visitors should drink plenty of water and ensure they remain hydrated when exploring the province. Medical facilities are limited, and visitors are advised to carry their necessary medicines.

Getting to Nong Bua Lamphu: Transportation Options and Logistics

Visitors can reach Nong Bua Lamphu through various means of transportation.

Airports Near Nong Bua Lamphu

Khon Kaen Airport is the nearest airport to Nong Bua Lamphu, which is around 140 km.

Bus Stations Near Nong Bua Lamphu

The nearest bus station is the Nong Bua Lamphu Bus Terminal, located in the province’s city.

Other Options

Visitors can also reach Nong Bua Lamphu by hiring a private taxi or renting a car.

How to Get Around in Nong Bua Lamphu

Visitors can hire motorcycles, bicycles or take a taxi or bus.

Where to Go Next from Nong Bua Lamphu: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Udon Thani

Udon Thani is the largest province in northern Thailand and is home to an abundance of cultural attractions, scenic landscape and is approximately 180 km away from Nong Bua Lamphu.

Conclusion

Nong Bua Lamphu offers visitors a rare opportunity to experience Thai culture and explore the scenic beauty of northeast Thailand. With hospitable locals, beautiful culture, serene landscapes, and an abundance of outdoor activities, it has something to offer for everyone.
