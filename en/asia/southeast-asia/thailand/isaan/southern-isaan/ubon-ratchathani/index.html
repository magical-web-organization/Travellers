Introduction to Ubon Ratchathani

Nestled in the heart of Southern Isaan, Ubon Ratchathani is a hidden gem in Thailand that boasts of rich cultural heritage, breathtaking natural landscapes, and diverse local cuisine. Known as the “City of the Lotus,” Ubon Ratchathani has so much to offer to travelers seeking an authentic Thai experience off the beaten path.

Travelers to Ubon Ratchathani can expect to discover a city with a mix of old and new, with a charming blend of Thai and Lao influences. The city is known for its vibrant festivals, numerous temples, and magnificent nature reserves. Regardless of whether you are a foodie, culture vulture, outdoor enthusiast, or history buff, Ubon Ratchathani has something for everyone.

List of Places to Visit in Ubon Ratchathani

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

For travelers interested in history and culture, Ubon Ratchathani offers several museums and historical sites worth visiting.

1. Ubon Ratchathani National Museum

This museum offers an immersive experience to visitors based on the history, culture, and traditions of Ubon Ratchathani. The museum has nine exhibition rooms that showcase the art, archaeology, literature, and religious heritage of the city.

2. Wat Thung Si Muang

Wat Thung Si Muang is a historic Buddhist temple located near the Ubon Ratchathani City Pillar Shrine. The temple features beautiful architecture and artwork, including a huge Buddha statue and several wall murals.

3. Pha Taem National Park

Pha Taem National Park is another top attraction in Ubon Ratchathani. The park is known for its beautiful landscapes, ancient rock art, and stunning views of the Mekong River. There are also several hiking trails in the park that lead visitors to caves and waterfalls.

Natural Attractions of Ubon Ratchathani: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

For those who love to be outdoors and enjoy natural beauty, Ubon Ratchathani offers several breathtaking natural attractions.

1. Sam Pan Bok

Sam Pan Bok is a site where the Mekong River flows across a series of rocks, forming various pools and whirlpools. The site is also home to a range of bird species.

2. Kaeng Tana National Park

Kaeng Tana National Park has a unique ecosystem with a large freshwater swamp that is home to over 100 bird species, as well as several types of aquatic animals. Visitors can take a boat ride to explore the park and visit the nearby Kaeng Tana Waterfall.

3. Pha Taem National Park

In addition to its historical significance, Pha Taem National Park offers stunning views of the Mekong River and Laos. Visitors can take hiking trails that lead to waterfalls and caves in the park.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Ubon Ratchathani

Ubon Ratchathani is home to several hiking and biking trails that offer travelers a chance to explore the natural beauty of the area.

1. Namtok Soi Sawan

Namtok Soi Sawan is a beautiful 9-kilometer long trek through Pha Taem National Park that leads visitors to a stunning waterfall. The trail is not too steep, making it accessible for hikers of all skill levels.

2. Ubon Ratchathani Bike Route

Ubon Ratchathani Bike Route is a flat, 25-kilometer route that winds through local villages, temples, and scenic views. Bikes are available for rent at several locations in the city.

Best Things to Do in Ubon Ratchathani: Activities and Tours

Regardless of your interests, Ubon Ratchathani offers several activities and tours that cater to different types of travelers.

1. Join the Ubon Ratchathani Candle Festival

The Ubon Ratchathani Candle Festival is held annually in July and is the city’s biggest celebration of the year. The festival is a showcase of elaborate wax carvings of Buddhist stories, and visitors can enjoy a colorful parade and other cultural displays.

2. Take a Walking Tour of Ubon Ratchathani

A walking tour provides visitors with a chance to explore the city’s most important landmarks, temples, and markets. Walking tours are available through tour operators, including Meetrip.

3. Try Local Cuisine

Ubon Ratchathani has a unique food culture that blends Thai and Lao flavors. Visitors can try local delicacies such as Kanom Jeen Nam Ya (rice noodles with fish curry), Laap Isan (spicy minced meat salad), and Naem (fermented pork sausage).

Food and Drink Scene of Ubon Ratchathani

Must-Try Dishes

Ubon Ratchathani offers a unique culinary experience that blends Thai and Lao flavors. Here are some must-try dishes:

1. Kanom Jeen Nam Ya

Kanom Jeen Nam Ya is a flavorful dish made with rice noodles and a fish curry sauce. It is typically served with fresh herbs and vegetables.

2. Laap Isan

Laap Isan is a spicy minced meat salad that is a staple of Northeast Thailand. It is typically made with beef or pork and flavored with lime juice, chili, and fresh herbs.

3. Naem

Naem is a fermented pork sausage that is a popular snack in Ubon Ratchathani. It is usually served with rice, peanuts, and fresh herbs.

Local Beverages

Ubon Ratchathani offers several notable and unique beverages that are worth trying:

1. Khao Poon

Khao Poon is a spicy rice noodle soup made with fish or chicken broth and a range of spices. It is often garnished with fresh herbs and crispy fried noodles.

2. Isaan-style Iced Coffee

Isaan-style Iced Coffee is a popular beverage in the region. It is made with strong coffee, condensed milk, and ice.

3. Khao Lam

Khao Lam is a traditional Thai dessert that is made by cooking sticky rice with coconut cream and sugar inside a bamboo tube.

Where to Stay in Ubon Ratchathani: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Ubon Ratchathani offers several accommodation options that cater to different budgets and preferences. Here are some of the best options:

1. Prajaktra Design Hotel

Located in the heart of Ubon Ratchathani, Prajaktra Design Hotel is a chic hotel that offers comfortable rooms and a great location. It is close to several attractions, including Ubon Ratchathani National Museum and Wat Thung Si Muang.

2. Jai Yen Homestay

For travelers on a budget, Jai Yen Homestay is a great option. This guesthouse offers single and double rooms with shared bathrooms, as well as a communal kitchen and living area.

3. Tohsang City Hotel

Tohsang City Hotel is a luxury hotel that offers spacious rooms and a range of amenities, including a swimming pool, fitness center, and spa. It is located in a quiet area but is still within walking distance of several attractions.

What to Buy in Ubon Ratchathani: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Ubon Ratchathani is known for its handcrafted products, including textiles, pottery, and wood carvings. Here are some souvenirs to consider:

1. Pha Khao Ma

Pha Khao Ma is a popular traditional fabric in Ubon Ratchathani. It is made by hand, using traditional weaving techniques, and features intricate patterns and vibrant colors.

2. Pottery

Ubon Ratchathani is well-known for its pottery. Visitors can find a range of pottery products, including vases, bowls, and plates, in local markets.

3. Wood Carvings

Wood carvings are another popular souvenir in Ubon Ratchathani. Visitors can find a range of wood carvings, including statues, figurines, and furniture, in local markets and handicraft shops.

Local Culture and Customs of Ubon Ratchathani

Ubon Ratchathani has a unique culture and customs that visitors should be aware of. Here are some things to keep in mind:

  • Buddhism is the dominant religion in Ubon Ratchathani, and visitors should respect the local customs and traditions.
  • Dress modestly when visiting temples and other religious sites.
  • Remove your shoes before entering a temple or home.
  • Do not touch someone’s head, as it is considered disrespectful in Thai culture.

Best Time to Visit Ubon Ratchathani

The best time to visit Ubon Ratchathani is between November and February, when the weather is cool and dry. Visitors should avoid the rainy season, which falls between May and October.

Do’s and Don’ts of Ubon Ratchathani

Do:

  • Try local cuisine, including Laap Isan and Naem.
  • Experience local festivals, such as the Ubon Ratchathani Candle Festival and Loy Krathong.
  • Visit Pha Taem National Park to witness stunning natural beauty and ancient rock art.

Don’t:

  • Show disrespect to local customs, including removing your shoes before entering a temple or home.
  • Touch someone’s head, as it is considered disrespectful in Thai culture.
  • Take Buddha statues or images out of the country.

Safety and Health

Safety in Ubon Ratchathani

Ubon Ratchathani is generally a safe city for travelers. However, visitors should take precautions to avoid theft and stay safe while exploring the city, including staying in well-lit areas and avoiding carrying large sums of money.

Health in Ubon Ratchathani

Visitors to Ubon Ratchathani should consult their doctor before traveling and ensure that their vaccinations are up-to-date. The city has a range of medical facilities, including hospitals and clinics, but travelers should also invest in travel insurance.

Getting to Ubon Ratchathani: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Ubon Ratchathani

Ubon Ratchathani is served by Ubon Ratchathani Airport, which offers daily flights to Bangkok and other regional cities.

Bus Stations Near Ubon Ratchathani

Ubon Ratchathani is well-connected by bus to other regions in Thailand and Laos. The city has several bus terminals, including the Central Bus Station and the BKS Bus Terminal.

Other Options

Travelers can also reach Ubon Ratchathani by train. The city is the last stop on the Northeastern Line, which connects to Bangkok and other cities in Northeast Thailand.

How to Get Around in Ubon Ratchathani

Ubon Ratchathani is a small city, and most attractions are within walking distance. Tuk-tuks and taxis are also available for short distances, while motorbike rentals are available for visitors who want to explore outside the city.

Where to Go Next from Ubon Ratchathani: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Chong Mek

Chong Mek is a small town located near the Laos border. Visitors can take a short 30-minute drive from Ubon Ratchathani to explore the beautiful rice paddies and visit the local market.

Khong Chiam

Khong Chiam is a popular tourist destination located about an hour’s drive from Ubon Ratchathani. Visitors can explore the Khong Chiam district’s natural beauty, including its many waterfalls and Mekong River views.

Conclusion

Ubon Ratchathani is a city that offers a unique blend of culture, nature, and local cuisine. With so much to see and do, travelers to Ubon Ratchathani can expect an authentic Thai experience and memories that will last a lifetime.
