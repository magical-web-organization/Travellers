Introduction to Buriram

Buriram is a province located in Southern Isaan in Thailand, known for its unique cultural blend of Khmer and Thai traditions. Its central location and excellent transport links make it an ideal base for exploring the region. Buriram is also home to some of Thailand’s finest Khmer ruins, and its rich cultural heritage has turned it into a popular destination for national and international visitors.

List of Places to Visit in Buriram

If you’re planning a trip to Buriram, there are several cultural and natural attractions worth visiting. Below is a list of some of the best places to visit.

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  1. Phanom Rung Historical Park - An incredible Khmer temple complex built between the 10th and 13th century that sits atop an extinct volcano. The temple is dedicated to the god Shiva and is considered one of the best-preserved Khmer temples in Thailand.
  2. Prasat Hin Muang Tam - Another impressive Khmer ruin located south of Buriram that dates back to the 10th century. The temple features a picturesque reflection pond and intricate sandstone carvings.
  3. Khao Kradong Forest Park - The park is an ancient volcano with a Buddhist shrine on top. The climb to the top of the mountain is steep, but the panoramic view of the city is breathtaking. Plus, it is a popular destination for locals and visitors alike.
  4. Buriram Castle - A renovated fort built-in 1593 and famous for its architecture and history. The castle’s rich past makes it one of the most popular spots for history buffs.

Natural Attractions of Buriram: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  1. Huai Talat Reservoir - Located near Khao Kradong Forest Park, Huai Talat is a beautiful lake that covers 20 km2. It’s an excellent spot for boating, fishing, picnicking, and hiking.
  2. Non Dindaeng Waterfall - If you love natural beauty, Non Dindaeng is a must-visit. This stunning waterfall is surrounded by lush greenery that makes it ideal if you want to relax, swim, or hike.
  3. Phutthaisawan Temple - Located south of Buriram, this temple is surrounded by stunning views of the countryside and the large Ubonrat Dam.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Buriram

Buriram has several beautiful hiking trails and bike routes that make it easy to explore the province’s natural beauty. Some of the best trails include:

  1. Khao Kradong Forest Park Trail - Follow the path up the mountain to the stunning view of the city.
  2. Huai Talat Reservoir Trail - A 10km trail suitable for hiking and cycling.
  3. Non Dindaeng Waterfall Trail - A 2km hiking route that leads to the picturesque waterfall.
  4. Ban Kruat Cycling Trail - A beautiful route through farms, rice paddies, and villages.

Best Things to Do in Buriram: Activities and Tours

There are plenty of activities and tours to take part in while in Buriram. Some of the most popular activities include:

  1. Buriram United Football Club - For sports lovers, be sure to catch a game and cheer for the famous Buriram United Football Club.
  2. Thai Massage Therapy - Relax and rejuvenate in one of Buriram’s many massage parlors.
  3. Local Craft Workshops - Learn how to weave, pottery, and silversmithing in the traditional way.
  4. Temple Tours - Take a tour of the various temples and learn about their history and significance.

Food and Drink Scene of Buriram

The food and drink scene in Buriram is diverse and delicious, with plenty of unique and traditional dishes and beverages to try.

Must-Try Dishes

  1. Kai Yang - Grilled chicken marinated and served with sticky rice and spicy sauce.
  2. Som Tum - Spicy papaya salad with tomato, lime, and peanuts.
  3. Khao Soi - Curry noodle soup made with chicken, coconut milk, and spices.
  4. Larb - Spicy, tangy meat salad made with herbs and lime.

Local Beverages

  1. Chang Beer - A popular beer made in Thailand and enjoyed throughout Southeast Asia.
  2. Nam Oy - A sweet and tangy blended berry drink.

Where to Stay in Buriram: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Buriram offers a variety of accommodations, from budget-friendly hostels to luxurious hotels. Some of the best places to stay are:

  1. The Cube Resort - A luxurious resort-style hotel with a modern and relaxed atmosphere.
  2. Victoria Home and Resort - A unique and stylish boutique hotel with a garden and outdoor pool.
  3. PY Hotel - A mid-range hotel with a modern design and excellent location.
  4. Buriram Ratchadamnern - Located in the heart of the city, this hotel offers affordable prices and comfortable rooms.

What to Buy in Buriram: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Some of the best souvenirs to bring back from Buriram include:

  1. Handwoven silk products - silk scarves and shawls made with traditional Thai weaving techniques.
  2. Pottery and ceramics - beautiful and unique pottery made in the area.
  3. Silverware - handcrafted silver jewelry and other silver products.

Local Culture and Customs of Buriram

Buriram’s culture is a unique blend of Thai and Khmer influences, with a rich history and traditions. The locals are friendly and hospitable, and visitors are expected to show respect for the culture and customs. It is customary to remove shoes before entering temples and private homes, dress modestly when visiting religious sites, and to show politeness and respect to the elders.

Best Time to Visit Buriram

The best time to visit Buriram is from November to February when the weather is cool and dry. April is also a great time to visit, especially if you want to experience the Thai New Year or Songkran festival, known for its water fights and street parties.

Do’s and Don’ts of Buriram

Here are some things to keep in mind while visiting Buriram:

Do:

  1. Show respect for the local culture and customs.
  2. Try the local cuisine and beverages.
  3. Protect yourself from the sun and stay hydrated.
  4. Learn basic Thai phrases and greetings

Don’t:’:

  1. Disrespect the local religion and customs.
  2. Wear revealing clothing when visiting religious sites.
  3. Take photos of the locals without their permission.
  4. Hug or kiss in public

Safety and Health

Safety in Buriram

Buriram is a safe province with a low crime rate, but it’s always wise to be aware of your surroundings, especially at night. Always use precautions like locking doors and windows, securing belongings, and avoiding unlit areas.

Health in Buriram

Be sure to protect yourself from mosquito bites when travelling to rural areas. It’s also important to stay hydrated and use sunscreen when spending time outdoors.

Getting to Buriram: Transportation Options and Logistics

Buriram is easily accessible by road, rail, and air.

Airports Near Buriram

  1. Buriram Airport - Domestic flights to Bangkok and Chiang Mai.

Bus Stations Near Buriram

  1. Buriram Bus Station - Direct Bus services from Bangkok and other major cities in Thailand.

Other Options

  1. Car - Driving is also possible and will take approximately 4-5 hours from Bangkok.

How to Get Around in Buriram

Getting around Buriram is easy with a few options like renting a scooter, taking a tuk-tuk, or using a taxi. Visitors can also take bus services to reach their desired destinations.

Where to Go Next from Buriram: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Beyond Buriram, there are several nearby destinations worth exploring, including:

  1. Nakhon Ratchasima - A bustling city with a variety of Thai and international restaurants and cafes.
  2. Khao Yai National Park - One of the most popular national parks in Thailand, with stunning waterfalls, hiking trails, and wildlife.
  3. Phimai - A small town near Buriram famous for its ancient Khmer temple, Phimai Historical Park.

Conclusion

Buriram is a remarkable province rich in history and natural beauty, and there are plenty of things to see and do for all visitors. Be sure to indulge in the local culture and cuisine and take part in the various activities and tours that show off this charming province.
