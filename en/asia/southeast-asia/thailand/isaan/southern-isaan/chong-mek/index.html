Introduction to Chong Mek

Nestled in Southern Isaan in Thailand, Chong Mek is a quaint town that is waiting to be explored. The town is famous for being a gateway to Laos, as it lies on the border that separates Thailand from its neighboring country. This makes it an ideal destination to visit if you want to explore Southeast Asia.

Chong Mek is known for its rich cultural heritage, featuring numerous historical landmarks and ancient temples. This guide will take you through the best places to check out while in Chong Mek. Additionally, we will cover the best things to do, where to stay, where to eat, and essential travel tips.

List of Places to Visit in Chong Mek

The following is a list of must-visit places in Chong Mek:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Phra That Phanom: This is an important religious site and the most famous Buddhist temple in Southern Isaan. The temple hosts an annual festival that sees thousands of visitors flock to the area.
  • Wat Tham Khuha Sawan: This temple is located in a natural cave and is home to an image of Buddha in a reclining position. Visitors will be amazed by the stunning cave surroundings and spectacular views outside of the temple.
  • Wat Sirindhorn Wararam: Built-in 1997, this temple is named after Princess Maha Chakri Sirindhorn. It features stunning architecture, a stunning lake, and a museum that houses artifacts from the region’s history.

Natural Attractions of Chong Mek: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Huai Luang Reservoir: This is the largest reservoir in Thailand and offers impressive scenic views of the area. Visitors can relax and enjoy themselves in the verdant greenspace surrounding the reservoir. Visitors can also fish or rent a boat for an unforgettable boating experience.
  • Pu Kao Laang: This place is a hidden gem for nature lovers, located just 9 km from Chong Mek. Pu Kao Laang is a mountain range that offers great hiking opportunities and stunning views.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Chong Mek

Chong Mek is renowned for offering visitors with exceptional hiking and biking trails. The following are some of the best trails to check out:

  • Huay Krabak Yai Trail: This trail is a favorite among hikers and offers panoramic views of the surrounding area. The trail is quite challenging, but the reward of the phenomenal views is worth the effort.
  • Khao Khat Wildlife Sanctuary: Located just a few kilometers from Chong Mek, Khao Khat Wildlife Sanctuary offers some of the best biking, hiking, and trekking trails in the region. The trails are well paved and offer a great way to explore the area.

Best Things to Do in Chong Mek: Activities and Tours

  • Laos Border Market: Visitors will enjoy exploring the Laotian Market, which offers an extensive array of clothes, handicrafts, and food. Take the opportunity to interact with locals and soak in the beautiful culture.
  • Longboat Tours: Rent a longboat and explore the Mekong River, where visitors can immerse themselves in the beautiful scenery surrounding this area.

Food and Drink Scene of Chong Mek

Must-Try Dishes

Southern Isaan is famous for its spicy and sour dishes that are sure to delight your taste buds. Here are some signature dishes that you must try while in Chong Mek:

  • Pad Thai Laos: A dish famous in Southern Isaan that is similarly like Pad Thai but has a spicy taste, which gives it a whole lot more flavor.
  • Laab Sab: A famous dish made from minced pork or chicken that contains a lot of spices and herbs such as lime juice, fish sauce, and chillies.

Local Beverages

Chang beer and Sangsom Whiskey are the two most common drinks found in Chong Mek. These drinks are readily available in local bars and restaurants.

Where to Stay in Chong Mek: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

There are plenty of accommodations in Chong Mek that vary in size and price range, making it more inclusive for travelers. Here are some of our top recommendations:

  • Mekong Grand Hotel: This is one of the most luxurious accommodations in Chong Mek, and it features a pool, a fitness center, and excellent customer service.
  • Nipha Guesthouse: This guesthouse is more affordable and offers a home-like feeling for guests.

What to Buy in Chong Mek: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Handicrafts: Visitors can find a range of handicrafts in the Laotian Market in Chong Mek. These unique items make great souvenirs and gifts.
  • Refreshing Plants: Chong Mek is known for producing plants and fruits like grapefruits; visitors can buy fresh fruits which they can take back home as fresh fruits.

Local Culture and Customs of Chong Mek

Chong Mek is known for its cultural practices, especially the Laotian influence in the region. Majority of the population in Chong Mek is of Laotian origin, and guests should be respectful and tolerant of their customs and traditions.

Best Time to Visit Chong Mek

The best time to visit Chong Mek is between November and February when the weather is pleasant and mild.

Do’s and Don’ts of Chong Mek

Here are some Do’s and Don’ts for visitors to Chong Mek:

Do:

  • Dress modestly when visiting religious sites
  • Bring insect repellent to protect against mosquitoes in hiking and outdoor areas

Don’t:

  • Don’t take photos during religious ceremonies without permission
  • Don’t visit unauthorized areas along the border checkpoints.

Safety and Health

Safety in Chong Mek

Crime rates in Chong Mek are minimal, but visitors should exercise caution and keep an eye on your belongings.

Health in Chong Mek

Visitors should take precautions against mosquito-borne illnesses by using insect repellent, covering up, and using mosquito nets to sleep under.

Getting to Chong Mek: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Chong Mek

The nearest airport to Chong Mek is Ubon Ratchathani Airport, which is around 200 km away from the town.

Bus Stations Near Chong Mek

There are several bus stations in Chong Mek that offer trips to other popular destinations such as Bangkok.

Other Options

Visitors can also hire taxis, rent a car, or motorbike to get around Chong Mek.

How to Get Around in Chong Mek

Visitors can get around Chong Mek by riding in tuk-tuks, renting motorbikes or bicycles, or going on foot.

Where to Go Next from Chong Mek: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

After exploring Chong Mek, visitors can head to other nearby popular destinations such as Pakse, Laos, or the Phimai Historical Park in Thailand.

Conclusion

Chong Mek offers visitors a unique Southeast Asian experience that is hard to come by. With its rich cultural heritage, natural wonders, scenic vistas, and exciting activities, Chong Mek is a destination that should be on every traveler’s bucket list. This comprehensive guide has provided you with everything you need to plan your trip to Chong Mek successfully. Pack your bags, and have an unforgettable time in this magical town!
