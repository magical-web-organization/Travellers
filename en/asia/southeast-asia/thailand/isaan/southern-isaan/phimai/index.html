Introduction to Phimai

Located in Southern Isaan, Phimai is a small town situated in the Nakhon Ratchasima province of Thailand. Considered as one of the underrated tourist destinations in the country, this beautiful town is known for its historical significance and natural beauty. It is home to the Phimai Historical Park, which is known for its Khmer temple ruins that date back to the 11th-12th century. In this guide, we’ll take a look at all the places to visit and things to do in Phimai.

Phimai is known for its silk products and local handicrafts. You can find them in the local markets and shops.

Local Culture and Customs of Phimai

Phimai’s cultural heritage is influenced by the Khmer empire and Buddhist traditions. Respectful clothing is advised when visiting temples or other religious sites.

Best Time to Visit Phimai

The best time to visit Phimai is between November and February when the weather is cool and dry

Do’s and Don’ts of Phimai

Do:

  • Dress respectfully when visiting religious places
  • Try the local street food
  • Respect the local culture and traditions

Don’t:

  • Disrespect the Thai monarchy
  • Touch or climb on the temples or ruins
  • Litter in public places

Safety and Health

Safety in Phimai

Phimai is generally a safe place for tourists. However, be mindful of pickpocketing in crowded areas. Always carry a copy of your passport and important documents with you.

Health in Phimai

It’s always best to drink bottled water and take precautions against mosquitoes. Dress in lightweight, loose-fitting clothing, and use mosquito repellent.

Phimai is a small town and can be explored on foot or by bike. Renting a bike is a popular mode of transportation among tourists.

Conclusion

Phimai is a hidden gem in Thailand that is definitely worth exploring. From the Khmer temple ruins to the natural beauty of the surrounding area, Phimai has something for everyone. Don’t miss out on this charming and culturally significant town.
