Introduction to Sisaket

Sisaket is a province located in Southern Isaan, known for its fascinating blend of Khmer and Thai cultures. The city boasts a rich history, sacred temples, and breathtaking natural scenery. In this comprehensive travel guide, we’ll explore Sisaket’s top attractions, outdoor adventures, delicious cuisine, and insider travel tips to help you plan your visit.

List of Places to Visit in Sisaket

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Wat Si Mahapo - One of the most revered temples in Sisaket, featuring beautiful Khmer-style architecture and important Buddhist relics.
  • Mueang Fa Daet Song Yang - Ancient ruins of an ancient Khmer city, showcasing intricate carvings and impressive architecture.
  • Sisaket Rajabhat University Fine Arts Museum - Museum featuring exquisite artwork, sculptures, and artifacts that showcase the region’s cultural traditions.
  • Phra Maha Chedi Chai Mongkol - A massive stupa that houses a relic of the Buddha’s bones and offers stunning panoramic views of the surrounding landscape.
  • Phra That Na Dun - A revered Buddhist temple that houses a sacred relic of the Buddha and features a serene meditation garden.

Natural Attractions of Sisaket: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Pha Taem National Park - A beautiful nature reserve featuring towering cliffs, scenic waterfalls, and ancient rock art.
  • Roi Et-Kalasin Cycling Route - A scenic cycling route that takes you through picturesque rice paddies, quaint villages, and stunning countryside.
  • Kaeng Tana National Park - A gorgeous park with impressive rock formations, stunning waterfalls, and an abundance of wildlife.
  • Huai Chan Reservoir - A peaceful reservoir where visitors can enjoy kayaking, fishing, and hiking while basking in the scenic surroundings.
  • Kaeng Lamduan National Park - A gorgeous park that features tranquil forests, pristine waterfalls, and exciting hiking trails.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Sisaket

  • Phra Vihan Hiking Trail - This popular hiking trail offers breathtaking views of Phra Vihan Temple and its surrounding cliffs.
  • Pha Taem National Park Hiking Trail - This trail takes you through one of Thailand’s most scenic national parks, featuring ancient rock art, towering cliffs, and picturesque waterfalls.
  • Roi Et-Kalasin Cycling Route - This scenic cycling route takes you through some of Sisaket’s most picturesque countryside, quaint villages, and rice paddies.

Best Things to Do in Sisaket: Activities and Tours

  • Explore Sisaket’s Traditional Markets - Sample local delicacies and shop for souvenirs at one of the city’s vibrant markets.
  • Take a Cooking Class - Learn to cook traditional Isaan cuisine and enjoy a delicious meal with newfound friends.
  • Visit a Silk Weaving Village - See local artisans at work and purchase handmade silk products from Sisaket’s skilled weavers.
  • Take a Temple Tour - Discover Sisaket’s beautiful Buddhist temples, featuring lavish architecture and intricate carvings.
  • Enjoy a Relaxing Massage - Unwind with a Thai massage at one of Sisaket’s many spas or massage parlors.

Food and Drink Scene of Sisaket

Must-Try Dishes

  • Larb Muang - Spicy minced pork salad with a tangy dressing.
  • Som Tum - A refreshing and spicy papaya salad.
  • Khao Niao - Sticky rice served with savory and sweet dishes.
  • Tom Saeb Nua - A spicy and sour beef soup.
  • Nam Tok - Grilled pork salad seasoned with chili and lime.

Local Beverages

  • Lao Beer - A popular beer made from locally sourced rice.
  • Kafe Boran - A traditional Thai coffee served in a clay pot with condensed milk.
  • Sin Somros - A refreshing and sweet rose-flavored drink.

Where to Stay in Sisaket: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • The Petchvarin Resort and Spa - A luxurious resort nestled in the heart of Sisaket’s lush countryside.
  • The Breeze Resort - A charming resort with comfortable rooms and excellent service.
  • Ban Nawin Resort - A peaceful and budget-friendly resort with beautiful gardens and comfortable rooms.
  • Home and Bike Hostel and Cafe - A trendy hostel featuring comfy dorms and private rooms.

What to Buy in Sisaket: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Silk Products - Sisaket is known for its silk weaving, so don’t forget to bring home some gorgeous silk scarves or fabric.
  • Local Snacks and Spices - Take home some delicious Isaan snacks or buy some locally grown spices to enhance your home cooking.
  • Traditional Clothing - Check out the local markets and shops for traditional Isaan-style clothing.

Local Culture and Customs of Sisaket

  • Sisaket locals are proud of their Khmer heritage and place great importance on Buddhist traditions and practices.
  • Visitors should be respectful and mindful when visiting temples or attending religious ceremonies and events.
  • Isaan people, in general, are known for their friendly and laid-back nature, so expect a warm welcome and plenty of smiles.

Best Time to Visit Sisaket

The best time to visit Sisaket is between November and February when the weather is cool, and the skies are clear. However, bear in mind that this period is also peak tourist season, so expect higher prices and larger crowds. March to May is dry season and can be quite hot. June to October is rainy season, so expect frequent downpours and humid weather.

Do’s and Don’ts of Sisaket

Do:

  • Do dress modestly when visiting temples or other sacred sites.
  • Do try the local food and immerse yourself in Isaan’s rich cultural traditions.
  • Do be respectful and polite when interacting with locals.

Don’t:

  • Don’t disrespect sacred Buddhist relics or places of worship.
  • Don’t touch or take photos with wild animals or plants.
  • Don’t litter or damage the environment.

Safety and Health

Safety in Sisaket

Sisaket is a safe destination with a low crime rate. However, visitors should exercise caution when traveling alone at night and avoid carrying large sums of money.

Health in Sisaket

Sisaket’s tap water is generally safe to drink, but it’s advisable to stick to bottled water to avoid stomach upset. Visitors should also be up-to-date on routine vaccinations and consult their doctor regarding additional vaccines or medications before traveling.

Getting to Sisaket: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Sisaket

  • Ubon Ratchathani International Airport - The nearest airport to Sisaket, located approximately 1 hour and 30 minutes away.

Bus Stations Near Sisaket

  • Sisaket Bus Terminal - The main bus station in Sisaket.
  • Ubon Ratchathani Bus Terminal - Another nearby bus station located approximately 1 hour and 30 minutes away.

Other Options

  • Visitors can also rent a car or motorbike to explore Sisaket’s scenic countryside.

How to Get Around in Sisaket

  • Visitors can hire a taxi, tuk-tuk, or motorbike to get around Sisaket.
  • Cycling enthusiasts can explore the city’s top attractions on two wheels.

Where to Go Next from Sisaket: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Khao Phra Wihan National Park - A scenic park famous for its impressive Khmer architecture and ancient ruins.
  • Ubon Ratchathani - Sisaket’s neighboring province features a rich Buddhist heritage, fascinating historic sites, and delicious food.
  • The Mekong River - Take a boat trip along the mighty Mekong River, which forms the natural border between Thailand and Laos.

Conclusion

Sisaket is an off-the-beaten-path destination that offers visitors a unique glimpse into Isaan’s rich heritage, stunning natural scenery, and delicious cuisine. From ancient ruins and sacred temples to scenic hikes and lush national parks, Sisaket has something for everyone. We hope this comprehensive travel guide has helped you plan your visit and discover the best that Sisaket has to offer.
