Introduction to Pak Chong

Pak Chong is a charming district of Nakhon Ratchasima Province, located in Southern Isaan of Thailand. Known as the gateway to Khao Yai National Park, Pak Chong is a popular destination among nature enthusiasts and adventure seekers. Aside from its natural beauty, the town is also rich in history and culture, with several museums and historical sites to explore. Whether you’re looking for a laid-back vacation or an active outdoor adventure, Pak Chong has something to offer.

List of Places to Visit in Pak Chong

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Prasat Hin Phimai

Prasat Hin Phimai is a Khmer temple complex in Phimai, Nakhon Ratchasima. Built in the 11th and 12th centuries, it is one of the best-preserved Khmer temples in Thailand and is considered a significant historical site.

Thao Suranari Monument

The Thao Suranari Monument is another iconic landmark in Nakhon Ratchasima. The monument honors the brave wife of a local governor who saved the town from invasion during the 1826 war. It is a symbol of female heroism and the enduring spirit of the Thai people.

Natural Attractions of Pak Chong: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Khao Yai National Park

Khao Yai National Park is one of the largest and most popular national parks in Thailand, encompassing over 2,000 square kilometers of forest, grasslands, and waterfalls. The park is home to various wildlife, including elephants, tigers, gibbons, and hornbills. It is also a UNESCO World Heritage site and a must-visit for nature lovers.

Farm Chokchai

Farm Chokchai is an agro-tourism attraction where visitors can learn about the daily life of Thai farmers and experience a range of activities. The farm has a petting zoo, horse riding, ATV riding, milking cow, and cowboy shows that provide a fun-filled day for visitors of all ages.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Pak Chong

Khao Yai National Park Trails

Khao Yai National Park has several hiking trails that offer different levels of difficulty and scenery. The most popular trails are the Haew Suwat Waterfall Trail, Kong Kaeo Waterfall Trail, and Khao Khieo Bird Watching Trail. These trails will take you through the forest, past waterfalls, and observation points where you can see the wildlife and birds.

Pak Chong Bike Tours

For those who prefer cycling over hiking, Pak Chong has several bike tours that traverse the town’s countryside and farmlands. These bike tours offer a unique perspective on the rural life of Thailand and provide opportunities to interact with the friendly locals.

Best Things to Do in Pak Chong: Activities and Tours

Vineyard Tours

Pak Chong is known for its vineyards that produce excellent wines. A vineyard tour is a great way to taste the local wines and learn about the wine-making process. Some of the popular Vineyards include PB Valley, GranMonte, and Village Farm and Winery.

Nong Nooch Tropical Garden

The Nong Nooch Tropical Garden is a picturesque landscape of tropical gardens and beautiful flora. The garden has various activities for visitors, including elephant shows, Thai cultural performances, and traditional Thai massage treatments.

Food and Drink Scene of Pak Chong

Must-Try Dishes

Pak Chong’s cuisine features a blend of Isaan and Central Thai flavors that are delicious and spicy. The must-try dishes in Pak Chong include;;

  • Somtum (Papaya Salad)
  • Khao Soi (Curry Noodles)
  • Laab Moo (Spicy pork salad)
  • Tom Yum (Spicy soup)

Local Beverages

Pak Chong is famous for its wine, and several wineries offer wine-tasting tours to visitors. Pak Chong also has several local beer brands, including Singha and Leo, which are popular among locals.

Where to Stay in Pak Chong: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Pak Chong has various accommodation options for different budgets. From luxury hotels and resorts to budget-friendly guesthouses, there are plenty of options for travelers. Some of the accommodation options include:

  • The Hill Khao Yai Resort
  • Thames Valley Khao Yai
  • The Courtyard Khao Yai
  • Inapan Resort

What to Buy in Pak Chong: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

The local products of Pak Chong reflect the town’s rural and agricultural heritage. Some popular souvenirs to bring back home from Pak Chong are:

  • Pak Chong Wine
  • Handmade knives
  • Krajood (orchid bark fabric)
  • Molam CDs (traditional Thai music)

Local Culture and Customs of Pak Chong

Pak Chong’s culture is a blend of the traditional Thai culture of Central Thailand and the Isaan culture of Northern Thailand. The people of Pak Chong are friendly and hospitable, and visitors are always welcome to participate in local festivals and celebrations.

Best Time to Visit Pak Chong

The best time to visit Pak Chong is during the cool season from November to February when the temperature is pleasant, and the weather is dry. This is also the peak tourist season, so expect higher prices and crowds. The rainy season (June to October) can be a good time for waterfalls, but some national park trails may be inaccessible due to the rain.

Do’s and Don’ts of Pak Chong

Do:

  • Dress appropriately for temples and religious sites.
  • Respect local customs and traditions.
  • Try the local cuisine and interact with locals.

Don’t:

  • Disrespect the Royal family or Thai traditions.
  • Touch or give money to children or monks.
  • Engage in illegal activities such as drug use.

Safety and Health

Safety in Pak Chong

Pak Chong is generally a safe town for travelers, but it’s always advisable to take precautions.

  • Be careful when swimming in natural bodies of water, as they may have strong currents.
  • Lock your valuables in your hotel safe.
  • Use common sense and be aware of your surroundings, especially at night.

Health in Pak Chong

It’s always advisable to carry a basic first aid kit with you when traveling to Pak Chong. The town has several hospitals and clinics in case of emergency.

  • Make sure to drink only bottled water.
  • Wear sunscreen and insect repellent to protect against sunburn and mosquito-borne diseases.
  • Seek medical attention if you experience any symptoms of illness.

Getting to Pak Chong: Transportation Options and Logistics

Pak Chong is easily accessible from Bangkok by various means of transportation, including:

  • Bus: Several buses depart regularly to Pak Chong from Bangkok’s Northern and Northeastern Bus Terminals.
  • Train: Trains also operate scheduled services from Bangkok’s Hualamphong Station to Pak Chong.
  • Private Car or Taxi: You can also hire a private car or taxi to take you to Pak Chong.

Airports Near Pak Chong

The nearest airport to Pak Chong is Don Mueang International Airport in Bangkok, which is approximately 150 kilometers away.

Bus Stations Near Pak Chong

The main bus terminal in Pak Chong is the Pak Chong Bus Terminal, which operates services to and from Bangkok and other major cities in Thailand.

Other Options

Some other options for getting to Pak Chong include renting a car or hiring a private driver.

How to Get Around in Pak Chong

The best way to get around in Pak Chong is by rental car, motorcycle, or bicycle. Taxis and tuk-tuks are also available, but they can be expensive, especially for longer journeys.

Where to Go Next from Pak Chong: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

There are several nearby destinations that you can visit for a day trip from Pak Chong, including:

  • Khao Yai National Park
  • Wang Nam Khiao
  • Korat
  • Chaiyaphum

Conclusion

Pak Chong is a charming destination that offers a mix of adventure, culture, and relaxation. Whether you’re interested in hiking through a national park, sampling local wines, or learning about Thai history, there is something for everyone in Pak Chong. With this comprehensive travel guide, you have all the information you need to plan your trip and make the most out of your visit to this beautiful town in Thailand.
