Introduction to Southern Isaan

Southern Isaan is a region located in Thailand’s northeastern region, known as Isaan. This culturally rich area is where ancient Khmer temples stand, traditional Thai silk is woven, and spicy Thai cuisine is spicier than ever. It offers a unique travel experience that is off the beaten path, but well worth the journey. In this tour guide, you’ll discover everything you need to know to plan a memorable trip to Southern Isaan.

List of Places to Visit in Southern Isaan

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Phimai Historical Park: home to one of the most significant Khmer temples in Thailand, this historical park houses grand structures and stone carvings from the ancient Khmer Empire.
  • Ban Prasat Archaeological Site: This site features multiple Khmer ruins and a museum with artifacts from Khmer and Dvaravati periods.
  • National Museum of Ubon Ratchathani: displays exhibits on Ubon Ratchathani’s culture, history, and handicrafts.

Natural Attractions of Southern Isaan: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Pha Taem National Park: home to stunning views of the Mekong River, ancient rock formations, a nature trail, and prehistoric rock paintings.
  • Sirindhorn Wararam Phu Prao Temple: marked by its stunning views of the Mekong, it’s also the location for the famous, “Golden Light” Buddhist ceremony.
  • Sam Pan Bok: Also known as the “Grand Canyon of Thailand,” this geological site features vast rock formations, and unique sandstone eroded by the Mekong River.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Southern Isaan

  • Ubon Ratchatani - Sirindhorn Dam: A perfect option for nature lovers who like to enjoy scenic views while hiking. This hike covers approximately 9.5 km from the entrance area to the Sirindhorn Dam with a beautiful view of the surrounding environment.
  • Khao Phra Wihan National Park Trail: a two-hour hike from the Prasat Khao Phra Wihan parking area before reaching the temple ruins. It offers picturesque sights while following a challenging path.

Best Things to Do in Southern Isaan: Activities and Tours

  • Silk weaving: This area is well known for its silk, and most silk shops will offer tours to witness the weaving and production process.
  • Explore the night markets: Southern Isaan has some excellent night markets, where you can taste some authentic Thai food.
  • Attend a local festival: there is a wide range of festivals held across Southern Isaan, keeping with the traditions of the region.

Week-Long Itinerary for Southern Isaan

Food and Drink Scene of Southern Isaan

Must-Try Dishes

  • Som Tum: A spicy papaya salad that is a staple of Isaan cuisine
  • Laab: A spicy meat salad that usually combines ground meat, chili, and herbs.
  • Nam Tok: A grilled beef salad similar to Laab, but with charred strips of beef and mint leaves.
  • Tom Yum: A spicy soup made with shrimp and lemongrass.

Local Beverages

  • Thai Coffee: A unique blend of coffee and tea that can be found in cafes throughout Southern Isaan.
  • Thai Iced Tea: Made with black tea, spices, and condensed milk, this tea is sweet and refreshing.
  • Andaman Lime Juice: Lime juice is a refreshing beverage made with sweetened lime water with coconut milk.

Where to Stay in Southern Isaan: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Rai Fah Buriram Hotel: Located in Buriram city with comfortable rooms and a pool.
  • Tohsang Khongjiam Resort: Offers a picturesque option with a riverside view and offers a range of activities.
  • Sunee Grand Hotel and Convention Centre: An upscale option with an on-site golf course and spa facilities.

What to Buy in Southern Isaan: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Silk: Southern Isaan is famous for its silk, and visitors can find a range of silk fabrics, clothing, and accessories in markets and silk shops throughout the region.
  • Handicrafts: From hand-woven baskets to traditional pottery, visitors can purchase an array of authentic handicrafts that represent the culture and customs of Southern Isaan.
  • Spices: The local cuisine is loaded with spice, and visitors can buy packs of spice blends or individual ingredients at local markets.

Local Culture, and Customs of Southern Isaan

The people of Southern Isaan are known for their unique customs and beliefs. One of these practices is the “Baci” ceremony, used for special events such as weddings, births, and graduations. It is believed to focus positive energy on those taking part in the ceremony.

Best Time to Visit Southern Isaan

The peak season to visit Southern Isaan is between November to February, when the weather is drier and cooler. March to April is a hot season, and May to October is the rainy season.

Do’s and Don’ts of Southern Isaan

Do:

  • Dress respectfully when visiting temples and historical landmarks.
  • Enjoy the street food and local cuisine.
  • Respect the local customs and traditions.

Don’t:

  • Wear skimpy clothing in public places.
  • Touch someone’s head as it’s a sign of disrespect.
  • Show public affection, kissing, and hugging in public places is offensive in Thailand.

Safety and Health

Safety in Southern Isaan

Southern Isaan is relatively safe. However, tourists should always be vigilant when traveling in unfamiliar areas, and avoid carrying around large amounts of money.

Health in Southern Isaan

Tourists should have necessary vaccinations while visiting Southern Isaan and travel with adequate health insurance. Mosquito-borne diseases such as dengue fever can be common; mosquito repellent is a must.

Getting to Southern Isaan: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Southern Isaan

The primary airports near Southern Isaan are Ubon Ratchathani Airport and Buriram Airport. International travelers can usually fly into Bangkok’s Suvarnabhumi Airport and then take a connecting flight.

Bus Stations Near Southern Isaan

Buses are the most economical mode of transportation in Southern Isaan, so bus stations in Buriram and Ubon Ratchathani are available.

Other Options

Renting a car or hiring a driver is also an alternate option to explore the region’s highways.

How to Get Around in Southern Isaan

Renting a vehicle is the best option as public transport is scarce and inconsistent around the region. Visitors can also opt for hiring a driver or touring with tours and transportation companies within the region.

Where to Go Next from Southern Isaan: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Chiang Mai: explore the historic old city and visit the stunning temples
  • Bangkok: Get into Thailand’s capital city for a taste of urban culture and excessive cuisine.
  • Siem Reap, Cambodia: access the best temple ruins in the region and soak in Cambodia’s history and unique cuisine.

Conclusion

Southern Isaan is an exciting region with a wide range of cultural heritage, natural wonder, flavorful cuisine, and neighboring historical sites. Exploring it is an experience that should not be missed. We hope this guide has helped plan an unforgettable trip to Southern Isaan.
