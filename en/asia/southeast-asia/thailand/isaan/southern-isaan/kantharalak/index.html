Introduction to Kantharalak

Kantharalak, located in Southern Isaan, Thailand, is a hidden gem waiting to be discovered. Its rich cultural heritage and diverse natural beauty make it an ideal destination for all travelers. From ancient ruins to outdoor adventures, Kantharalak has something for everyone.

List of Places to Visit in Kantharalak

Whether you want to explore the cultural attractions or indulge in outdoor adventures, Kantharalak has a range of places to visit. Some of the top attractions include:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Phanom Rung Historical Park: This ancient Khmer temple complex is one of the best-preserved in Thailand and offers stunning views of the surrounding countryside.
  • Ban Prasat Archaeological Site: This represents the ruins of a prehistoric settlement dating back to the bronze age.
  • Wat Khao Angkhan: A beautiful temple located on a hilltop, offering breathtaking views of Kantharalak and the surrounding area.

Natural Attractions of Kantharalak: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • The Khao Phra Wihan National Park: This park offers rugged mountains, stunning sunsets, and an abundance of wildlife, making it a popular destination for trekking and wildlife spotting.
  • Sirindhorn Dam: Located in the valley of the Mun River, the dam provides scenic views that make it a popular picnic spot.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Kantharalak

Kantharalak has some of the best hiking and biking trails in Thailand. Here are a few routes to check out:

  • Phanom Rung Historical Park: The park offers several hiking trails that lead through the ancient temple ruins and offer stunning views of the surrounding countryside.
  • Khao Phra Wihan National Park: This park has several trekking routes that take you through rugged mountains, dense forests, and offer breathtaking views of the surrounding area.

Best Things to Do in Kantharalak: Activities and Tours

  • Explore Ancient Temples: Kantharalak is home to several ancient temples with intricate designs and unique architecture, including Wat Khao Angkhan and Phanom Rung Historical Park.
  • Trek the Mountains: Kantharalak has several mountain trails that offer stunning views of the surrounding landscape. The Khao Phra Wihan National Park is the perfect destination for hikers and outdoor enthusiasts.
  • Explore the Local Cuisine: Kantharalak is known for its local cuisine. Make sure to try the spicy som tam salad and delicious Thai curries.
  • Attend Local Festivals: Kantharalak is home to several colorful festivals throughout the year. The Bun Bang Fai Festival, celebrated in May, is the biggest and most vibrant.

Week-Long Itinerary for Kantharalak

Day 1: Visit Phanom Rung Historical Park and explore its ancient ruins.

Day 2: Explore the Khao Phra Wihan National Park and take in the stunning views of the mountains and wildlife.

Day 3: Visit Ban Prasat Archaeological Site and learn about the prehistoric settlements in Kantharalak.

Day 4: Take a break and relax by the Sirindhorn Dam.

Day 5: Explore the local markets and try some of the delicious cuisine in Kantharalak.

Day 6: Attend a local festival or participate in outdoor activities.

Day 7: Take a day trip to nearby destinations like Ubon Ratchathani or Surin.

Food and Drink Scene of Kantharalak

Kantharalak is famous for its delicious cuisine. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Som Tam Salad: A spicy papaya salad with tamarind, peanuts, and chilies.
  • Pad Krapow Moo Saap: A stir-fry dish made with pork, basil leaves, and chilies.
  • Tom Yum Goong: A spicy and sour soup made with shrimp, lemongrass, and lime juice.

Local Beverages

  • Thai Iced Tea: A sweet and creamy iced tea flavored with spices and condensed milk.
  • Coconut Water: A refreshing drink made from the clear liquid inside young green coconuts.

Where to Stay in Kantharalak: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Kantharalak has a range of accommodations to suit all budgets. Some popular options include:

  • Pimarnman: A comfortable hotel with modern amenities, located in the heart of Kantharalak.
  • Baan Prasat Homestay: A traditional Thai-style guesthouse, located near Ban Prasat Archaeological Site.
  • Night Market Hostel: A budget-friendly hostel located near the local night market.

What to Buy in Kantharalak: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Kantharalak is known for its unique handicrafts and local products. Some popular souvenirs include:

  • Traditional Thai silk products: Kantharalak is famous for its high-quality silk fabrics and products.
  • Handmade ceramics: The ceramics produced in Kantharalak are unique and colorful, and make excellent souvenirs.
  • Local spices and herbs: Visitors can find a wide variety of spices and herbs used in traditional Thai cuisine.

Local Culture and Customs of Kantharalak

Kantharalak is deeply rooted in Buddhist culture and customs. Visitors are expected to respect local customs, including removing shoes before entering temples and dressing modestly.

Best Time to Visit Kantharalak

The best time to visit Kantharalak is between November and February, when the weather is cool and dry. However, the beauty of the area can be seen in all seasons.

Do’s and Don’ts of Kantharalak

Do:

  • Respect the local culture and customs.
  • Try the local cuisine and beverages.
  • Visit the must-see attractions.

Don’t:

  • Wear revealing clothing when visiting temples or other religious sites.
  • Disrespect the local culture or customs.
  • Litter or damage the natural environment.

Safety and Health

Kantharalak is a safe destination for travelers. However, visitors should take standard precautions to ensure their safety. Here are some things to keep in mind:

Safety in Kantharalak

  • Use common sense when traveling alone at night.
  • Be aware of your surroundings and avoid unsafe areas.
  • Take care when participating in outdoor activities.

Health in Kantharalak

  • Carry insect repellent and sunscreen when participating in outdoor activities.
  • Stay hydrated and avoid drinking tap water.
  • Seek medical attention immediately if you fall ill during your trip.

Getting to Kantharalak: Transportation Options and Logistics

Kantharalak is well-connected to major cities in Thailand. Visitors can reach the city by air, bus, or private car.

Airports Near Kantharalak

  • Ubon Ratchathani Airport: This is the closest airport to Kantharalak, located approximately 90 kilometers away.

Bus Stations Near Kantharalak

  • Ubon Ratchathani Bus Station: Buses run regularly from Bangkok to Ubon Ratchathani, and visitors can take a bus to Kantharalak from there.

Other Options

  • Private Car: Visitors can rent a car or hire a private driver to reach Kantharalak.

How to Get Around in Kantharalak

Visitors can explore Kantharalak by foot, bicycle, or motorcycle. Taxis and tuk-tuks are also available for hire.

Where to Go Next from Kantharalak: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Kantharalak is located near several popular destinations in Thailand, including:

  • Ubon Ratchathani: A bustling city with several cultural attractions, located approximately 90 kilometers away from Kantharalak.
  • Surin: Famous for its annual elephant festival, Surin is located approximately 160 kilometers away from Kantharalak.

Conclusion

Kantharalak is an ideal destination for travelers looking to experience the culture and natural beauty of Thailand. With its fascinating historical sites, unique cuisine, and stunning natural parks, Kantharalak is a must-visit destination for anyone traveling to Southeast Asia.
