Introduction to Nakhon Ratchasima

Nakhon Ratchasima, also known as Khorat, is a northeastern province of Thailand. It is the third-largest province in the country and sits at the gateway to the Isaan region. Nakhon Ratchasima is steeped in history and culture, offering visitors a unique mix of traditional Thai and Khmer influences.

List of Places to Visit in Nakhon Ratchasima

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Prasat Hin Phimai Historical Park

Prasat Hin Phimai is a Khmer temple complex located 60 km from Nakhon Ratchasima. It was built during the 11th-12th century and is considered one of the most significant Khmer historical sites in Thailand.

Thao Suranari Monument

The Thao Suranari Monument is a bronze statue located in the heart of Nakhon Ratchasima city. It depicts Thao Suranari, a local heroine who helped defend the city against the Laotian invasion in the 19th century.

Maha Viravong National Museum

The Maha Viravong National Museum showcases the history of Nakhon Ratchasima and the surrounding region. The museum houses a large collection of Buddhist artifacts, including ancient statues and carvings.

Natural Attractions of Nakhon Ratchasima: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Khao Yai National Park

Khao Yai National Park is a UNESCO World Heritage site known for its lush forests, waterfalls, and diverse wildlife. The park offers a range of activities, including hiking, birdwatching, and wildlife watching.

Lam Takhong Dam

Lam Takhong Dam is a popular spot for locals and tourists alike. The dam offers stunning views of the surrounding mountains and is a great place to relax and enjoy a picnic.

Pak Thong Chai Cultural Centre

The Pak Thong Chai Cultural Centre is a traditional Thai cultural center that showcases the local way of life. Visitors can enjoy traditional Thai dance performances, learn about local crafts, and taste authentic Thai food.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Nakhon Ratchasima

Khao Yai National Park

Khao Yai National Park offers a range of hiking trails, from short nature walks to multi-day treks. Visitors can also rent bicycles to explore the park on two wheels.

Wang Nam Khiao

Wang Nam Khiao is a mountainous district in Nakhon Ratchasima known for its stunning natural scenery. The area offers a range of hiking and biking routes, including the popular Pha Kep Tawan viewpoint.

Best Things to Do in Nakhon Ratchasima: Activities and Tours

Wat Theppitak Punaram

Wat Theppitak Punaram is a unique temple that features a two-story pagoda and a giant reclining Buddha statue. Visitors can also enjoy a boat ride on the temple’s lake and take in the scenic views.

Jim Thompson Farm

The Jim Thompson Farm is a popular destination for families and nature lovers. The farm is known for its beautiful sunflower fields and offers a range of activities, including horse riding and ATV tours.

Food and Drink Scene of Nakhon Ratchasima

Must-Try Dishes

Nam Prik Pla Ra

Nam Prik Pla Ra is a spicy dip made with fermented fish and chili peppers. It is a staple dish in Isaan and is often served with vegetables and sticky rice.

Som Tum

Som Tum is a spicy salad made with green papaya, chili peppers, and peanuts. It is a popular dish in Isaan and can be found at most street food stalls and restaurants.

Local Beverages

Thai Tea

Thai tea is a sweet and creamy tea served with ice. It is a popular drink in Thailand and can be found at most cafes and restaurants.

Chang Beer

Chang Beer is a popular Thai beer known for its crisp and refreshing taste. It is widely available in bars and restaurants throughout Nakhon Ratchasima.

Where to Stay in Nakhon Ratchasima: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Dusit Princess Korat

Dusit Princess Korat is a luxury hotel located in the heart of Nakhon Ratchasima city. The hotel offers comfortable rooms, an outdoor pool, and a range of dining options.

Sripattana Hotel

Sripattana Hotel is a budget-friendly hotel located close to the city center. The hotel offers clean and comfortable rooms, a restaurant, and a swimming pool.

Sakol Hotel

Sakol Hotel is a modern hotel located near Nakhon Ratchasima’s attractions. The hotel offers spacious rooms, a restaurant, and a rooftop swimming pool.

What to Buy in Nakhon Ratchasima: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Khao Yai Wine

Khao Yai is known for its vineyards and wine production. Visitors can purchase local wines to bring home as a souvenir.

Khao Jam Cakes

Khao Jam Cakes are a traditional dessert made with glutinous rice, coconut cream, and sugar. They can be found at local markets and make a great souvenir or gift.

Local Culture and Customs of Nakhon Ratchasima

Nakhon Ratchasima is heavily influenced by both Thai and Khmer cultures. The locals are known for their warm hospitality and traditional customs, including the popular Bun Bang Fai Rocket Festival and Phimai Festival.

Best Time to Visit Nakhon Ratchasima

The best time to visit Nakhon Ratchasima is between November and February when the weather is cool and dry. The rainy season runs from May to October.

Do’s and Don’ts of Nakhon Ratchasima

Do:

  • Respect local customs and traditions
  • Learn a few words of Thai before your visit
  • Bargain at local markets and street food stalls

Don’t:

  • Wear revealing clothing when visiting temples or mosques
  • Disrespect the royal family or the Buddhist religion
  • Drink tap water

Safety and Health

Safety in Nakhon Ratchasima

Nakhon Ratchasima is generally a safe place to visit. However, travelers should take basic precautions, such as keeping valuables secure and avoiding walking alone at night.

Health in Nakhon Ratchasima

Travelers should take precautions to avoid mosquito bites, as there is a risk of dengue fever and other mosquito-borne illnesses in the region. It is also recommended to drink bottled water and avoid eating uncooked food.

Getting to Nakhon Ratchasima: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Nakhon Ratchasima

The nearest airport to Nakhon Ratchasima is Suvarnabhumi Airport in Bangkok, which is approximately 2.5 hours away by car.

Bus Stations Near Nakhon Ratchasima

Nakhon Ratchasima has two bus stations: the old bus station and the new bus station. Both stations offer regular bus services to Bangkok and other destinations in Thailand.

Other Options

Travelers can also reach Nakhon Ratchasima by train. The city has a train station with regular services to Bangkok and other destinations.

How to Get Around in Nakhon Ratchasima

Travelers can get around Nakhon Ratchasima by tuk-tuk, taxi, or rental car. It is also possible to rent bicycles in some areas, such as Khao Yai National Park.

Where to Go Next from Nakhon Ratchasima: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Buriram

Buriram is a province located to the east of Nakhon Ratchasima. It is known for its Khmer ruins and its rich history and culture.

Ayutthaya

Ayutthaya is a UNESCO World Heritage site located north of Bangkok. It was the capital of Thailand from the 14th to the 18th century and is known for its ancient temples and ruins.

Conclusion

Nakhon Ratchasima is a unique and vibrant city that offers visitors a range of activities, including exploring historical sites, hiking in national parks, and trying traditional Thai food. With this comprehensive travel guide, visitors can plan their next trip to Nakhon Ratchasima with ease and confidence.
