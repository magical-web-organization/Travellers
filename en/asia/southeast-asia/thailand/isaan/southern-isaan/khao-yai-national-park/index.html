Introduction to Khao Yai National Park

Khao Yai is one of the largest and most popular national parks in Thailand, located in the Southern Isaan region. Established in 1962, the park covers an area of 2,168 square kilometers and is home to a diverse range of wildlife. Khao Yai is a UNESCO World Heritage site and is known for its stunning landscapes, lush jungles, scenic waterfalls, and abundant wildlife, including elephants, tigers, and gibbons. The park is a must-visit destination for nature lovers and outdoor enthusiasts.

List of Places to Visit in Khao Yai National Park

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Haew Narok Waterfall: A famous waterfall and one of the most popular attractions in the park.
  • Pha Kluaymai Waterfall: A secluded waterfall located deep in the jungle, known for its natural beauty and tranquility.
  • Khao Yai Art Museum: A museum showcasing contemporary Thai artwork and Thai silk fabric.
  • Palio Khao Yai: An Italian-themed shopping village with restaurants, shops, and cafes.

Natural Attractions of Khao Yai National Park: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Khao Rom Viewpoint: A viewpoint offering a panoramic view of the surrounding area, including the park’s lush forests and mountains.
  • Pha Diao Dai: A scenic rock formation with a beautiful view of the park.
  • Heo Narok Waterfall: A gorgeous waterfall surrounded by thick jungle.
  • Khao Yai Winery: A vineyard that offers tours and wine tastings.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Khao Yai National Park

Khao Yai National Park offers several hiking and biking trails of varying difficulty levels. Some of the popular trails include:

  • Khao Yai Nature Trail: A 1.5-kilometer elevated trail through the forest canopy.
  • Kong Kaew Trail: A 6-kilometer trail through the forest.
  • Haew Suwat Waterfall Trail: A 2.5-kilometer trail to Haew Suwat waterfall.

Best Things to Do in Khao Yai National Park: Activities and Tours

  • Elephant Riding: A popular activity for visitors, offering the opportunity to ride on the back of an elephant through the park.
  • Night Safari: A night tour of the park, offering a chance to see wildlife such as elephants, tigers, and deer in their natural habitat.
  • Zip Lining: An adventurous activity that allows visitors to glide through the forest canopy at high speeds.
  • Mountain Biking: A great way to explore the park and its beautiful scenery.

Week-Long Itinerary for Khao Yai National Park

Day 1: Arrive in Khao Yai and check into your accommodation. Day 2: Visit Haew Narok Waterfall, Pha Kluaymai Waterfall, and Khao Yai Art Museum. Day 3: Hike the Khao Yai Nature Trail and explore the park’s forest. Day 4: Take a night safari and spot nocturnal wildlife. Day 5: Go on a bike tour and visit the vineyards and wineries around the park. Day 6: Visit Palio Khao Yai and explore the village. Day 7: Depart Khao Yai and head to your next destination.

Food and Drink Scene of Khao Yai National Park

Must-Try Dishes

  • Som Tam: A classic Thai papaya salad.
  • Tom Yum Goong: A spicy shrimp soup.
  • Pad Thai: A stir-fried noodle dish with peanuts and veggies.
  • Khao Soi: A curry noodle soup.

Local Beverages

  • Thai iced tea: A sweet and creamy iced tea.
  • Cha Yen: Cold milk tea.
  • Singha Beer: A popular Thai beer.

Where to Stay in Khao Yai National Park: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • THA City Loft Hotel: A modern hotel in the heart of town.
  • Greenery Resort Khao Yai: A resort surrounded by lush gardens.
  • Lala Mukha Tented Camp: A glamping experience with luxury tents.
  • The Birder’s Lodge: A lodge offering birdwatching tours and activities.

What to Buy in Khao Yai National Park: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Thai Silk: High-quality silk fabric.
  • Pottery and Ceramics: Handcrafted pottery and ceramics from local artisans.
  • Silverware and Jewelry: Beautiful handmade silverware and jewelry.

Local Culture and Customs of Khao Yai National Park

Khao Yai National Park is a culturally rich region. Visitors should be respectful of local customs and etiquette, including dressing conservatively when entering religious sites.

Best Time to Visit Khao Yai National Park

The best time to visit Khao Yai National Park is between November and February, during the cooler, drier season. The park is also open year-round.

Do’s and Don’ts of Khao Yai National Park

Do:

  • Do carry water and snacks with you on hikes or outdoor activities.
  • Do be respectful of local customs and culture when visiting the park.
  • Do adhere to park regulations and guidelines.

Don’t:

  • Don’t feed the wildlife in the park.
  • Don’t litter or leave trash in the park.
  • Don’t disturb the natural environment or damage trees or plants.

Safety and Health

Safety in Khao Yai National Park

Visitors should stay on designated paths and trails, avoid getting too close to wildlife, and carry a first aid kit in case of emergencies.

Health in Khao Yai National Park

Visitors should bring insect repellent and wear long, protective clothing to prevent mosquito bites. It’s also advised to get vaccinated before your trip.

Getting to Khao Yai National Park: Transportation Options and Logistics

Visitors can reach Khao Yai National Park by car, bus, or taxi. The nearest airports are Don Mueang International Airport in Bangkok and U-Tapao Rayong-Pattaya International Airport.

Airports Near Khao Yai National Park

  • Don Mueang International Airport in Bangkok.
  • U-Tapao Rayong-Pattaya International Airport.

Bus Stations Near Khao Yai National Park

  • Mo Chit Bus Terminal in Bangkok.
  • Pak Chong Bus Station in Khao Yai.

Other Options

  • Taxi or private car service.

How to Get Around in Khao Yai National Park

The best way to get around the park is by car or bike. Visitors can also hire a guide who can show them the best spots and provide information about the park.

Where to Go Next from Khao Yai National Park: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Bangkok: Thailand’s bustling capital city.
  • Nakhon Ratchasima: A charming city with a rich history.
  • Saraburi: A beautiful province known for its natural beauty and ancient temples.

Conclusion

Khao Yai National Park is a must-visit destination for nature lovers and outdoor enthusiasts. With its stunning landscapes, diverse wildlife, and rich cultural heritage, the park offers something for everyone. Use this comprehensive travel guide to plan your trip and make the most of your visit to Khao Yai National Park.
