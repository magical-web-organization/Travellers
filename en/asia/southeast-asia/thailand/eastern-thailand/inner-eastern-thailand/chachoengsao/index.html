Introduction to Chachoengsao

Chachoengsao is a charming province located in Inner Eastern Thailand, about an hour’s drive away from Bangkok. The province is known for its rich cultural heritage, natural attractions, and traditional way of life.

With its fertile farmlands, serene rivers, and ancient temples, Chachoengsao offers a delightful escape from the bustling city life of Bangkok. Whether you’re looking for outdoor adventures, cultural experiences, or simply a relaxing getaway, Chachoengsao has something for everyone. Read on to discover the best places to visit, things to do, and tips for a memorable trip to Chachoengsao.

List of Places to Visit in Chachoengsao

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Chachoengsao is home to several museums and historical sites that offer a glimpse into the province’s rich cultural and religious heritage. Here are some of the must-visit attractions:

  • Wat Sothon Wararam Worawihan: This is one of the most important Buddhist temples in Thailand and is home to a revered statue of Lord Buddha that is believed to have healing powers.

  • Wat Saman Rattanaram: This temple is famous for its giant statue of the Hindu god Ganesha, which is believed to be the largest in the world.

  • Bang Khla Floating Market: This is a traditional Thai market that is situated on a canal and offers a variety of local food, handicrafts, and souvenirs.

Natural Attractions of Chachoengsao: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

For nature lovers and adventure enthusiasts, Chachoengsao offers an array of options. Here are some of the best natural attractions in Chachoengsao:

  • Khao Kitchakut National Park: This is a popular destination for hiking and camping, with several trails leading to scenic waterfalls and hilltop views.

  • Wat Paknam Jolo: This temple is situated on the bank of the Bang Pakong River and offers a stunning view of the river and its surroundings.

  • Khlong Suan Market: This is a local market that sells a variety of fresh produce as well as cooked food, with an emphasis on seafood.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Chachoengsao

Chachoengsao offers several hiking and mountain biking routes that cater to different levels of fitness and experience. Here are some of the best hiking and biking routes in Chachoengsao:

  • Khao Chamao-Khao Wong National Park: This park offers several trails that lead to waterfalls and scenic viewpoints, with varying degree of difficulty.

  • Bang Pakong River Route: This is a cycling route that follows the Bang Pakong River, offering scenic views of the river and its surrounding farms and villages.

Best Things to Do in Chachoengsao: Activities and Tours

Chachoengsao offers a variety of activities and tours that cater to different interests. Here are some of the best things to do in Chachoengsao:

  • River Cruise: Take a leisurely cruise along the Bang Pakong River and enjoy the scenic views of the countryside.

  • Thai Cooking Class: Learn how to prepare traditional Thai dishes using fresh local ingredients.

  • Elephant Sanctuary Tour: Visit an elephant sanctuary and learn about the conservation efforts to protect these gentle giants.

Week-Long Itinerary for Chachoengsao

Here’s a sample itinerary for a week-long trip to Chachoengsao:

  • Day 1: Visit Wat Sothon Wararam Worawihan and explore the nearby markets;
  • Day 2: Go hiking in Khao Chamao-Khao Wong National Park;
  • Day 3: Take a river cruise along the Bang Pakong River;
  • Day 4: Visit Wat Paknam Jolo and the nearby floating market;
  • Day 5: Take a cooking class and enjoy a traditional Thai meal;
  • Day 6: Visit the elephant sanctuary and learn about their conservation efforts;
  • Day 7: Relax and enjoy the local food and culture at your leisure.

Food and Drink Scene of Chachoengsao

Chachoengsao is known for its delicious and diverse cuisine, with an emphasis on seafood, fresh vegetables, and herbs. Here are some of the must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Tom Yum Goong: Spicy and sour soup with shrimp, lemongrass, and galangal.

  • Pla Raad Prik: Fried fish with spicy chili paste and onions.

  • Pad Pak Boong Fai Daeng: Stir-fried morning glory with oyster sauce.

Local Beverages

  • Cha Yen: Thai iced tea with condensed milk.

  • Nam Manao: Lime juice with sugar and ice.

  • Singha Beer: A popular Thai beer brewed in Chachoengsao.

Where to Stay in Chachoengsao: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Chachoengsao offers a range of accommodation options that cater to different budgets and preferences. Here are some of the best places to stay in Chachoengsao:

  • River Breeze Hotel: A luxurious hotel situated on the banks of the Bang Pakong River.

  • Baan Khun Inn: A cozy guesthouse located near the local markets and Wats.

  • Jasmine Resort Hotel: A modern hotel with an outdoor pool and fitness center.

What to Buy in Chachoengsao: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Chachoengsao offers a variety of souvenirs and local products that make for great gifts or keepsakes. Here are some of the best things to buy in Chachoengsao:

  • Chachoengsao Pineapple: A delicious and juicy pineapple that is unique to the province.

  • Benjarong Porcelain: A traditional Thai porcelain with intricate designs and bright colors.

  • Handicrafts: Chachoengsao is known for its woven baskets, bamboo products, and pottery.

Local Culture and Customs of Chachoengsao

Chachoengsao has a rich cultural heritage, and visitors are encouraged to respect local customs and traditions. Here are some things to keep in mind:

  • Dress modestly when visiting temples or other religious sites.

  • Remove shoes before entering someone’s home or a temple.

  • Ask for permission before taking photographs of people or religious statues.

Best Time to Visit Chachoengsao

The best time to visit Chachoengsao is from November to February, when the weather is cool and dry, and the landscape is lush and green. March to May can be hot and humid, while June to October is the rainy season.

Getting to Chachoengsao: Transportation Options and Logistics

Chachoengsao is easily accessible from Bangkok, with several transportation options available.

Airports Near Chachoengsao

The nearest international airport is Suvarnabhumi Airport in Bangkok. From there, you can take a taxi or a bus to Chachoengsao.

Bus Stations Near Chachoengsao

There are several bus stations in Bangkok that offer services to Chachoengsao, including Ekkamai Bus Station, Morchit Bus Terminal, and Mo Chit Bus Station.

Other Options

You can also hire a private car or a taxi from Bangkok to Chachoengsao, which is a convenient and comfortable option.

How to Get Around in Chachoengsao

The best way to get around Chachoengsao is by motorbike, which is convenient and inexpensive. You can also rent a car or take a taxi or a songthaew (local shared taxi).

Where to Go Next from Chachoengsao: Nearby Destinations and Excursions
