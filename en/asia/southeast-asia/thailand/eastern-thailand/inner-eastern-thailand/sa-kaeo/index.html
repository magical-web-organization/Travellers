Sa Kaeo Travel Guide: Exploring the Gems of Inner Eastern Thailand

Sa Kaeo is a hidden jewel in the Inner Eastern part of Thailand that is often overlooked by travelers, but it is quickly gaining popularity as a destination among adventurous souls. Sa Kaeo offers a unique blend of culture, history, and nature, making it an ideal destination for those looking for an offbeat experience. In this comprehensive Sa Kaeo travel guide, we will explore the best places to visit, things to do, where to stay, and what to eat in Sa Kaeo.

List of Places to Visit in Sa Kaeo

Sa Kaeo has a lot to offer for tourists, from culturally significant attractions to natural wonders. Here is a list of places to visit in Sa Kaeo:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Wat Khao Wongkot: This ancient temple complex, located on a hilltop, is a must-visit destination in Sa Kaeo. The temple dates back to the Ayutthaya period and boasts awe-inspiring khmer-style architecture.
  • Sa Kaeo Museum: This museum offers a glimpse into the rich history and culture of Sa Kaeo. It features exhibitions on the province’s role in the Khmer Empire and shows a documentary on the famous War of Fire.
  • Khao Chakan Museum: One of the noteworthy museums of the province that features collections of ancient artifacts and offers insights into the area’s history and cultures.

Natural Attractions of Sa Kaeo: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Khao Ang Rue Nai Wildlife Sanctuary: Situated in the mountainous region of Sa Kaeo, this scenic wildlife sanctuary features an array of flora and fauna and extensive trails for trekking and hiking. Visitors can enjoy a breathtaking panoramic view of the province.
  • Namtok Khao Chamao National Park: This national park is a paradise for nature lovers and thrill-seekers alike, offering waterfalls, hot springs, hiking trails, and abundant wildlife.
  • Wang Somboon Art and Culture Center: This cultural center is an excellent opportunity to explore the local flora and fauna by taking a stroll through its beautiful flower gardens.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Sa Kaeo

Sa Kaeo’s natural landscapes and mountainous terrain are perfect for outdoor adventurers’ hiking and biking routes. These are some of the best trails and routes in Sa Kaeo:

  • Khao Chakan: This 7.6km hiking trail is an ideal option for adventure seekers. This trail offers scenic views of the Sa Kaeo province and its mountain ranges.
  • Khao Phra Pradoo: This 5.4km trail is an ideal option for intermediate hikers with a stunning view of the plain below.
  • Mountain Biking: For mountain biking enthusiasts, the rolling hill teeming with verdant forests can be a great way to get your heart pumping while enjoying nature.

Best Things to Do in Sa Kaeo: Activities and Tours

Sa Kaeo offers plenty of activities and tours for travelers who want to make the most of their visit:

  • Visit the Old Market: The Old Market is a vibrant destination in Sa Kaeo where travelers can experience the daily lives of the locals. The market is a great place to buy souvenirs, local handicrafts, and traditional foods.
  • Visit the Amphibian Temple: This temple is a huge tourist attraction in the province and is known for its frog ornaments surrounding the temple grounds. Visitors also get a chance to learn and appreciate the mystical belief that the temple signifies something divine.
  • Farm Tours: Take a tour of the local farms to get a glimpse into the local agricultural practices.

Food and Drink Scene of Sa Kaeo

Sa Kaeo’s food scene is a testament to the region’s agricultural roots. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Khao Yam Puean: A classic dish of lightly flavored rice salad with herbs and vegetables uniquely Thai in flavors. It is usually served with a scoop of spicy and sour gravy in a separate small bowl.
  • Pad Phed: A local stir-fry dish that features an explosion of multiple taste components, including sour, salty, sweet, and spicy with chili paste. Pad Phed is a hot and spicy alternative to the classic Pad Thai.
  • Kanom Jeen: A popular rice noodle dish in Southern and Eastern Asia that is prepared by pounding the rice into a fine powder and kneading it into strands of noodles.

Local Beverages

  • Lemongrass Tea: A healthy and refreshing drink, Lemongrass tea is a local favorite in Sa Kaeo. Its citrusy aroma and zesty flavor make it a perfect thirst-quencher on a hot day.
  • Nhom Cha: This Thai-style spiced tea is a perfect combination of different herbs and spices such as chrysanthemum, rose, and lemongrass. It is an excellent way to warm up and soothe on a chilly day.

Where to Stay in Sa Kaeo: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Sa Kaeo has a range of accommodation options catering to different budgets and preferences. Here are some options:

  • Sa Kaeo Ruen Thai Resort: One of the best hotels in the province that offers affordable and comfortable lodging with a modern interior. The hotel also features a swimming pool, a garden, and Wi-Fi.
  • Garden Home Sa Kaeo: A budget-friendly option with clean and comfortable rooms. This homestay features a garden, a restaurant, and free Wi-Fi.
  • Baan Rimnam: An excellent option for those who want to experience traditional Thai architectural style. This guesthouse offers a beautiful garden and free Wi-Fi in its rooms.

What to Buy in Sa Kaeo: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Travelers seeking unique and authentic souvenirs can find them in Sa Kaeo’s local markets. Here are some things to buy in Sa Kaeo:

  • Authentic Thai Silk: Thailand is famous for its silk, and Sa Kaeo is home to some of the finest silk products in the country.
  • Handicrafts: Visit the local markets to find unique and beautiful handicrafts made by local artisans.
  • Local Produce: Sa Kaeo is known for its delicious fruit, including its freshly picked jackfruit, mango, and durian.

Local Culture and Customs of Sa Kaeo

Sa Kaeo is a culturally rich province with a unique blend of Thai and Khmer cultures. The locals are known to be very warm and welcoming to visitors. It is important to be respectful of local customs and dress modestly when visiting temples or other religious sites.

Best Time to Visit Sa Kaeo

The best months to visit Sa Kaeo are from November to February when the weather is cool, with temperatures ranging from 20-25°C. The green season, from May to October, is a great time to explore the natural attractions in Sa Kaeo, but it does get hot and humid.

Do’s and Don’ts of Sa Kaeo

Do:

  • Respect the local customs and traditions.
  • Dress modestly when visiting temples or other religious sites.
  • Be open to new experiences and immerse yourself in local culture.

    Don’t:

  • Disrespect religious sites or use inappropriate language or gestures.
  • Be rude to the locals

Safety and Health

Safety in Sa Kaeo

Sa Kaeo is a relatively safe place, and visitors are unlikely to encounter any significant safety concerns. However, it is advisable to use common sense and be vigilant, especially when traveling alone at night.

Health in Sa Kaeo

Travelers are recommended to consult a doctor or travel clinic for vaccinations and health advice before traveling to Sa Kaeo. It is also recommended to drink only bottled water and avoid eating food sold on the streets to prevent food-borne illnesses.

Getting to Sa Kaeo: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Sa Kaeo

The nearest airports to Sa Kaeo are the Suvarnabhumi International Airport in Bangkok, located about 2 hours to the west of Sa Kaeo. From there, visitors can take a private taxi or the minibus shuttle to reach the province.

Bus Stations Near Sa Kaeo

Visitors can take a bus from Bangkok’s Eastern Bus Terminal (Ekkamai) to Sa Kaeo. Buses operate frequently, and the journey takes approximately 3 hours.

Other Options

Private car hire and taxi services are also available to reach Sa Kaeo from different parts of Thailand.

How to Get Around in Sa Kaeo

Visitors can rent a car, hire a taxi, or take a tuk-tuk to explore the province. The local transportation system is also quite good, with buses, songthaews, and motorbikes taxis available for hire.

Where to Go Next from Sa Kaeo: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Sa Kaeo is the gateway to Eastern Thailand, and visitors can explore other nearby destinations. Here are some options:

  • Cambodia: Cross the border into Cambodia and explore its magnificent temples, including Angkor Wat.
  • Chanthaburi: A quaint coastal town located two hours away from Sa Kaeo, Chanthaburi is famous for its fruit markets, ancient temples, and stunning beaches.
  • Koh Chang: Take a ferry ride from Trat to Koh Chang and explore the beautiful beaches, waterfalls, and pristine rainforests.

Conclusion

Sa Kaeo is a hidden gem with a rich cultural heritage and stunning natural attractions. This Sa Kaeo travel guide covers everything from the must-see places to visit, food and drink scene, where to stay, local culture and customs, and the best time to visit. Whether you are looking for adventure or relaxation, Sa Kaeo has something for everyone.
