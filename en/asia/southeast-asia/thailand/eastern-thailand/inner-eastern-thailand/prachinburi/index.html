Introduction to Prachinburi

Situated in Inner Eastern Thailand, Prachinburi is a province rich in cultural heritage and natural beauty. Visitors will fall in love with the welcoming locals, traditional architecture, and delicious cuisine.

List of Places to Visit in Prachinburi

There’s no shortage of things to see and do in Prachinburi. Here are a few of the top places to add to your itinerary:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Wat Kaeo Phichit: A beautiful Buddhist temple known for its intricate murals and architecture.
  • Prachinburi National Museum: Learn about the history of the province and view artifacts from ancient times to the present.
  • Khao I-to Waterfall: A scenic and tranquil spot that’s perfect for picnicking or swimming.

Natural Attractions of Prachinburi: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Khao Yai National Park: This UNESCO World Heritage Site is home to wildlife, waterfalls, and lush forests.
  • Nam Tok Sarika: A stunning waterfall that’s surrounded by mountains and evergreen trees.
  • Khao Chakan: A popular spot for mountain biking, hiking, and birdwatching.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Prachinburi

For adventure seekers, Prachinburi offers an abundance of outdoor activities. Here are a few of the best routes to explore:

  • Khao I-to Waterfall Trail: A steep, 3-kilometer hike that rewards trekkers with breathtaking views of the waterfall.
  • Prachinburi Riverside Bike Trail: This scenic path offers a leisurely way to explore the province’s natural beauty.
  • Khao Chakan Mountain Biking: Challenge yourself with a scenic and technical ride through this mountainous region.

Best Things to Do in Prachinburi: Activities and Tours

From cultural experiences to outdoor adventures, there’s something for everyone in Prachinburi. Here are a few of the must-do activities and tours:

  • Elephant Sanctuary Tour: Visit a local elephant sanctuary and learn about the conservation efforts to protect these magnificent creatures.
  • Thai Cooking Class: Learn how to make traditional Thai dishes with a hands-on cooking class.
  • Night Safari Khao Yai: Experience the wild side of Prachinburi with a night safari that includes sightings of animals like elephants, tigers, and leopards.

Week-Long Itinerary for Prachinburi

If you’re planning a week-long trip to Prachinburi, here’s an itinerary that covers the best the province has to offer:

Day Activities
1 Visit the Prachinburi National Museum, Wat Kaeo Phichit, and Khao I-to Waterfall.
2 Explore Khao Yai National Park with a scenic hike or drive through the park’s many trails.
3 Take a Thai cooking class and learn how to make traditional dishes.
4 Visit the Khao Chakan mountain biking trail or take a day trip to Nam Tok Sarika.
5 Go on a nature walk and learn about the local flora and fauna.
6 Experience the Night Safari Khao Yai and see the park’s nocturnal animals up close.
7 Relax and unwind at a local spa or hot spring.

Food and Drink Scene of Prachinburi

No visit to Prachinburi is complete without trying some of the province’s delicious cuisine. Here are a few must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Kanom Jeen Nam Ya: A spicy noodle dish served with a coconut milk-based sauce and fish.
  • Pad Mee Korat: A stir-fried noodle dish with chicken, pork, or beef, commonly found at street markets.
  • Gaeng Pa: A spicy jungle curry made with meat, vegetables, and bamboo shoots.

Local Beverages

  • Lemongrass Tea: A refreshing and fragrant tea made with lemongrass and sugar.
  • Nam Tan Sod: A sweet and creamy drink made with palm sugar and coconut milk.
  • Thai Iced Tea: A popular drink made with black tea, sugar, and milk.

Where to Stay in Prachinburi: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Prachinburi offers a range of accommodations to suit any budget. Here are a few top options:

  • Tawaravadee Resort: A luxury resort that’s perfect for families or couples seeking a romantic getaway.
  • The Restore Prachinburi: A budget-friendly option with comfortable rooms and a central location.
  • Bann Puen Guesthouse: A charming guesthouse with a homely atmosphere and friendly staff.

What to Buy in Prachinburi: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

If you’re looking for souvenirs or gifts for loved ones, Prachinburi has plenty of unique offerings. Here are a few options:

  • Silk Products: Prachinburi is known for its high-quality silk and textiles, so consider purchasing a scarf or sarong.
  • Ceramics: Local artisans create beautiful ceramic pieces, like vases or bowls, that make for ideal keepsakes.
  • Honey: Prachinburi is a region known for locally sourced honey, which can be both a delicious and healthy souvenir to bring back home.

Local Culture and Customs of Prachinburi

When visiting Prachinburi, it’s important to respect the local culture and customs. Here are a few things to keep in mind:

  • Remember to remove your shoes before entering a temple or someone’s home.
  • Dress modestly when visiting temples or other religious sites.
  • Be polite and respectful towards locals, and try to learn a few basic Thai phrases.

Best Time to Visit Prachinburi

The best time to visit Prachinburi is between November and February when the temperatures are mild and the weather is dry. The rainy season typically runs from May to October, and visitors should expect hot and humid conditions during this time.

Do’s and Don’ts of Prachinburi

When visiting Prachinburi, there are a few things to keep in mind to ensure a pleasant and respectful trip:

Do:

  • Try the local food and drinks.
  • Remove your shoes before entering a temple or someone’s home.
  • Carry a reusable water bottle and refill it at local drinking fountains to reduce plastic waste.

Don’t:

  • Disrespect the local culture, customs, or religion.
  • Leave garbage or litter in public areas.
  • Touch or disturb wildlife in national parks or other natural areas.

Safety and Health

Safety in Prachinburi

Prachinburi is a generally safe province, with low rates of violent crime. However, visitors should exercise caution when travelling alone at night or in isolated areas, and keep an eye on their valuables in crowded areas or when using public transportation.

Health in Prachinburi

Visitors to Prachinburi should take precautions to protect themselves from mosquito-borne illnesses like dengue fever and malaria. It’s also important to drink only bottled or boiled water and to practice good hygiene habits like washing hands regularly.

Getting to Prachinburi: Transportation Options and Logistics

Prachinburi is easily accessible by several transportation methods. Here are a few options:

Airports Near Prachinburi

  • Don Mueang International Airport (DMK): Located in Bangkok and approximately a 2-hour drive to Prachinburi.

Bus Stations Near Prachinburi

  • Morchit Bus Terminal: Located in Bangkok and approximately a 2-hour drive to Prachinburi.
  • Prachinburi Bus Terminal: Located in the city center and offering connections to other nearby provinces.

Other Options

  • Private Car or Taxi: A comfortable and convenient option for those looking to travel from Bangkok or other nearby destinations.
  • Train: The nearest train station is in Prachinburi city center, offering connections to Bangkok and other locations in Thailand.

How to Get Around in Prachinburi

To explore the province, visitors can rent a car, hire a taxi, or take public transportation. Many of the popular tourist attractions are located outside of the city center, so having a private car may be the most convenient option.

Where to Go Next from Prachinburi: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

If you’re extending your trip beyond Prachinburi, consider visiting nearby destinations like:

  • Nakhon Nayok: Home to stunning waterfalls and national parks.
  • Khao Yai National Park: A UNESCO World Heritage Site with pristine wilderness and wildlife.

Conclusion

Prachinburi is an excellent destination for those looking to explore the cultural and natural wonders of Eastern Thailand. From scenic hikes to delicious cuisine, visitors will find plenty of reasons to fall in love with this charming province.
