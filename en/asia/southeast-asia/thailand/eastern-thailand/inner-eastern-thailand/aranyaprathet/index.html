Introduction to Aranyaprathet

Nestled in Inner Eastern Thailand, Aranyaprathet is a town that borders the neighboring country of Cambodia. Known for its lively markets, historical sites, and scenic natural attractions, Aranyaprathet is a destination that offers a unique blend of culture, adventure, and relaxation. Whether you are coming here to explore the ancient temples, indulge in delicious street foods, or simply soak in the local vibe, there is something for everyone in Aranyaprathet.

List of Places to Visit in Aranyaprathet

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Prasat Khao Noi - A beautiful Khmer-style temple dating back to the 9th century, perched atop a small hill offering panoramic views of Aranyaprathet.
  • Wat Anubanpot - A prominent Buddhist temple housing a famous statue of the Buddha. The temple premise is tranquil and serene and one can take some meditation classes here.
  • Khmer Ruins - A few kilometers from the town of Aranyaprathet lies the remains of Khmer temple ruins, which are believed to have been built during the 12th century by King Suryavarman II.

Natural Attractions of Aranyaprathet: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Sa Kaeo Waterfall - Located near the town of Aranyaprathet, Sa Kaeo Waterfall is a popular natural attraction that offers a refreshing dip in the natural pool.
  • Khao Chakan mountain - A beautiful mountain peak, which is ideal for hiking and trekking. The breathtaking views from the summit are worth the effort.
  • O-wun waterfall- Another beautiful waterfall, surrounding by forested hills that offers splendid views of nature

Hiking/Biking Routes of Aranyaprathet

  • Khao Chakan Mountain - The mountain peak is ideal for hiking. Visitors have the option of either trekking from the base or hire a guide.
  • O-wun waterfall - A beautiful scenic spot that offers many trails for hiking and exploring the forest.

Best Things to Do in Aranyaprathet: Activities and Tours

  • Explore the local Marketplaces - Morning markets selling everything from fish and meat to clothes and women’s accessories.
  • Visit the Khmer Ruins - A few kilometers from the town of Aranyaprathet lies the remains of Khmer temple ruins, which are believed to have been built during the 12th century.
  • Take a cycling tour of the countryside - Enjoy a leisurely cycle tour of the town and the lush surrounding countryside.

Food and Drink Scene of Aranyaprathet

Must-Try Dishes

  • Khao Pad Khun Chai - Fried rice with chicken, vegetables, and a sprinkle of fresh basil.
  • Gai Tod - Deep-fried chicken accompanied by a spicy dipping sauce.
  • Som Tam - Spicy papaya salad with a tangy dressing.

Local Beverages

  • Cha Yen - A sweet, refreshing Thai iced tea.
  • Lemongrass tea - A warm, soothing herbal tea made from fresh lemongrass.

Where to Stay in Aranyaprathet: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Aranyaprathet offers a range of accommodations that suit every budget and preference. From budget-friendly hostels to comfortable hotels, there are many options to choose from. Some of the top hotels are:

  • VRP Resort - A luxurious hotel that features an outdoor pool, fitness center, and restaurant serving Thai and European cuisine.
  • River Hill Resort - This eco-friendly resort has a tranquil atmosphere with a restaurant and garden.
  • D-Box Hostel - A budget-friendly hostel that offers clean, comfortable dormitories and a common area.

What to Buy in Aranyaprathet: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Silk Scarves - A perfect souvenir to bring home as a memory of the trip.
  • Handicrafts - The local markets are full of local artisans and craftsmen who make beautiful shared art and crafts.

Local Culture and Customs of Aranyaprathet

Aranyaprathet is a town deeply rooted in Buddhist traditions and simple traditional lifestyles. It is important to dress modestly when visiting temples, removing shoes before entering, and respecting the religious customs.

Best Time to Visit Aranyaprathet

The best time to visit Aranyaprathet is from November to February when the weather is mild and comfortable. Avoid visiting during the monsoon season, which runs from May to October.

Do’s and Don’ts of Aranyaprathet

Do:

  • Respect the local culture and customs.
  • Dress modestly when visiting temples.
  • Try the street foods for an authentic culinary experience.

Don’t:

  • Take pictures of locals without their permission.
  • Disrespect Buddhist temples and holy places.
  • Litter or damage the environment.

Safety and Health

Safety in Aranyaprathet

Aranyaprathet is a relatively safe destination for travelers. However, it’s important to exercise caution and take normal safety precautions such as watching belongings in crowded areas and avoiding walking alone at night.

Health in Aranyaprathet

Travelers should ensure that they are up-to-date with routine vaccinations before visiting Aranyaprathet. The town can be dusty, so be mindful of respiratory issues as well as heatstroke. It is advisable to carry adequate sunscreen and water bottles to prevent dehydration.

Getting to Aranyaprathet: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Aranyaprathet

The nearest airport is the Suvarnabhumi Airport in Bangkok, about 200 km away.

Bus Stations Near Aranyaprathet

The town is served by various private and state-run bus companies. You can take a bus from Bangkok to Aranyaprathet or Siem Reap in Cambodia.

Other Options

One can hire private taxis to travel and explore Aranyaprathet. Self-driving cars and motorbikes are also possible options.

How to Get Around in Aranyaprathet

The best way to get around Aranyaprathet is by renting a motorbike. Tuk-tuks and songthaews are other convenient and budget-friendly options to travel within the town limits.

Where to Go Next from Aranyaprathet: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Located on the border of Cambodia, Aranyaprathet is an ideal starting point for exploring the nearby Siem Reap, famous for the Angkor Wat temple complex, and the beautiful Battambang in Cambodia.

Conclusion

Aranyaprathet is one of the hidden gems of Northeastern Thailand, offering unique cultural experiences, natural beauty, and outdoor adventures. From exploring the local markets to hiking the picturesque mountain peaks, there is something for everyone in this charming town. Experience the local culture and cuisine, connect with the locals and explore the untouched beauty of Aranyaprathet.
