Introduction to Trat Province

Nestled in the easternmost part of Thailand, Trat Province is a hidden gem that boasts an abundance of natural wonders, pristine beaches, cultural landmarks, and idyllic island getaways. Located on the border with Cambodia and facing the Gulf of Thailand, Trat Province is a world away from the hustle and bustle of Bangkok, offering visitors a chance to experience the authentic and laid-back side of Thailand.

List of Places to Visit in Trat Province

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Trat Province has a rich history that dates back to the early Ayutthaya period, with cultural landmarks and museums that showcase the province’s unique heritage. Some of the must-visit places in Trat Province include:

  • The Trat Museum: This museum is home to a vast collection of artifacts, antiques, and exhibits that chronicle the history of Trat Province, from the prehistoric period to the present day.

  • Wat Buppharam: A stunning temple with an imposing golden chedi, intricate murals, and beautiful gardens.

  • Khao Banthat Wildlife Reserve: A protected wildlife area where visitors can observe a wide range of local wildlife, including rare bird species, monkeys, and reptiles.

  • Koh Kood Historical Site: A site featuring ancient ruins of stone structures and sculptures that date back to the Khmer Empire.

Natural Attractions of Trat Province: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Trat Province is one of the most scenic regions in Thailand, offering a plethora of outdoor activities and breathtaking natural wonders. Some of the must-visit places in Trat Province include:

  • Mu Koh Chang National Park: A pristine national park that encompasses several islands, lush rainforests, waterfalls, and beaches.

  • Koh Chang: The second-largest island in Thailand, Koh Chang boasts pristine beaches, picturesque fishing villages, and lush rainforests.

  • Koh Kood: An idyllic island getaway that offers pristine beaches, crystal clear waters, and stunning sunsets.

  • Khlong Phlu Waterfall: A spectacular waterfall that cascades over several tiers of rocks and offers a refreshing swimming spot.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Trat Province

Trat Province is a paradise for outdoor enthusiasts, with a network of well-maintained hiking and biking trails that offer scenic views and the opportunity to explore the region’s natural beauty up close. Some of the best hiking and biking routes in Trat Province include:

  • Khao Lan Mountain Trail: A challenging hike to the top of Khao Lan Mountain that offers panoramic views of the surrounding countryside.

  • Bang Bao Bay Trail: A scenic bike ride that winds along the coastline and through picturesque fishing villages.

  • Klong Plu Waterfall Trail: A moderate hike through lush rainforests that leads to a stunning waterfall with multiple tiers of cascading water.

Best Things to Do in Trat Province: Activities and Tours

Trat Province offers an exciting array of activities and tours that cater to every taste and budget. Whether you’re a nature lover, a history buff, or an adventure seeker, there’s something for everyone in Trat Province. Here are some of the best things to do in Trat Province:

  • Island hopping tours: Explore the idyllic islands of Koh Chang, Koh Mak, and Koh Kood by boat, and enjoy snorkeling, swimming, and sunbathing on secluded beaches.

  • Cooking classes: Learn how to cook authentic Thai dishes using fresh, locally sourced ingredients, and take home some new recipes to impress your friends and family.

  • Scuba diving and snorkeling: Discover the magical underwater world of Trat Province, with its vibrant coral reefs, tropical fish, and other marine creatures.

  • Thai massage: Relax and rejuvenate with a traditional Thai massage, or treat yourself to a spa day at one of Trat Province’s many luxurious spas.

Week-Long Itinerary for Trat Province

Day 1: Explore Trat Town and visit the Trat Museum. Day 2: Take a day trip to Koh Chang and explore the island’s pristine beaches and waterfalls. Day 3: Go island hopping and snorkeling in Koh Mak. Day 4: Visit the ancient ruins and sculptures at Koh Kood Historical Site. Day 5: Hike to the top of Khao Lan Mountain and enjoy panoramic views of the surrounding countryside. Day 6: Relax on the beaches of Koh Kood and watch the sunset over the sea. Day 7: Take a day trip to the Khlong Phlu Waterfall and enjoy a refreshing swim in the cool waters.

Food and Drink Scene of Trat Province

Must-Try Dishes

Trat Province is renowned for its unique blend of Thai, Cambodian, and Malay cuisines, with a range of mouth-watering dishes that will tantalize your taste buds. Some of the must-try dishes in Trat Province include:

  • Kua Kling: A spicy and savory dish made with pork or chicken, chili paste, and a mixture of herbs and spices.

  • Kanom Jeen Nam Ya: A flavorful dish made with rice noodles, spicy curry sauce, and a variety of fresh herbs and vegetables.

  • Pad Cha: A stir-fry dish made with fresh seafood or meat, chili peppers, and aromatic herbs such as kaffir lime leaves and lemongrass.

Local Beverages

Trat Province is also famous for its refreshing and flavorful local beverages, which are a perfect way to beat the heat and cool down after a long day of exploring. Some of the must-try beverages in Trat Province include:

  • Nam Dok Mai: A sweet and fragrant juice made from the locally grown mangoes.

  • Cha Yen: A Thai-style iced tea that’s sweetened with condensed milk and flavored with spices such as star anise and cloves.

  • Chang Beer: A popular local beer that’s refreshing and easy to drink.

Where to Stay in Trat Province: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Trat Province offers a wide range of accommodation options to suit every budget and taste, from luxury resorts and hotels to backpacker hostels and guesthouses. Some of the best places to stay in Trat Province include:

  • Koh Chang Paradise Resort & Spa: A luxurious beachfront resort that offers stunning views of the sea and lush tropical gardens.

  • Banpu Koh Chang: A budget-friendly hotel that’s just a short walk from White Sand Beach and other popular attractions.

  • Johnny Guesthouse: A cozy guesthouse that offers comfortable and affordable accommodations in the heart of Trat Town.

What to Buy in Trat Province: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Trat Province is full of unique and handmade products that make perfect souvenirs and gifts for friends and family back home. Some of the best souvenirs to buy in Trat Province include:

  • Hand-woven silk scarves and textiles
  • Handmade ceramics and pottery
  • Locally grown spices and herbs
  • Hand-carved wooden crafts and figurines

Local Culture and Customs of Trat Province

Trat Province has a rich cultural heritage that is steeped in tradition and local customs. It’s important to show respect for the local culture and customs, which includes dressing modestly and removing shoes before entering temples and other religious sites. English is spoken widely in Trat Province, but it’s always appreciated to learn a few basic phrases in Thai to show respect for the local culture.

Best Time to Visit Trat Province

The best time to visit Trat Province is between November and February, which is the coolest and driest season of the year. However, if you’re looking for lower prices and fewer crowds, consider visiting during the shoulder season (March to May) or the off-season (June to October).

Do’s and Don’ts of Trat Province

Do:

  • Show respect for the local culture and customs
  • Dress modestly, especially when visiting temples and religious sites
  • Learn a few basic phrases in Thai
  • Try the local cuisine and beverages
  • Take plenty of sunscreen and mosquito repellent

Don’t:

  • Disrespect the local culture or religion
  • Litter or damage the natural environment
  • Buy or use illegal drugs
  • Touch or disturb wildlife

Safety and Health

Safety in Trat Province

Trat Province is generally a safe destination for travelers, with low crime rates and a friendly local population. However, it’s always important to take basic precautions, such as keeping your valuables secure and avoiding walking alone in unfamiliar areas at night.

Health in Trat Province

Trat Province has no major health risks, but it’s always a good idea to take basic precautions such as drinking bottled water, wearing sunscreen, and using insect repellent to prevent mosquito-borne diseases such as dengue fever and malaria.

Getting to Trat Province: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Trat Province

Trat Airport is the main airport serving Trat Province, with daily flights from Bangkok and other major cities in Thailand.

Bus Stations Near Trat Province

Trat Bus Terminal is the main transportation hub in Trat Province, with frequent buses to Bangkok and other destinations in Thailand.

Other Options

Private taxis and car rentals are also available for those who prefer more flexibility and convenience.

How to Get Around in Trat Province

Trat Province is easy to navigate, with a network of public transportation options and plenty of rental services available. Some of the best ways to get around in Trat Province include:

  • Songthaews: Shared minibusses that run on specific routes and can be hailed from the side of the road.

  • Motorbikes and scooters: An affordable and flexible way to explore the region’s natural beauty and attractions.

  • Taxis and private cars: Perfect for those who prefer more comfort and convenience.

Where to Go Next from Trat Province: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Trat Province is an excellent base for exploring the neighboring regions of Thailand and Cambodia, with many exciting destinations and excursions within easy reach. Some of the best nearby destinations and excursions from Trat Province include:

  • Siem Reap, Cambodia: Home to the famous Angkor Wat temple complex and other cultural landmarks.

  • Koh Chang National Marine Park: A stunning natural wonderland that boasts a wealth of marine life, coral reefs, and beaches.

  • Chanthaburi Province: A rich and diverse region that’s full of cultural landmarks, natural wonders, and scenic vistas.

Conclusion

Trat Province is a hidden gem that offers a unique and authentic travel experience for those who venture beyond the well-trodden tourist trail. With its stunning natural beauty, rich cultural heritage, and laid-back vibe, Trat Province is a must-visit destination for any traveler looking to explore the less-traveled regions of Thailand.
