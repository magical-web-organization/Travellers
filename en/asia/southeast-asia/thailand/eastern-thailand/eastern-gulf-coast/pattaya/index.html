Introduction to Pattaya

Pattaya is a vibrant coastal city located on the Eastern Gulf Coast of Thailand and is one of the country’s most popular tourist destinations. Known for its beautiful sandy beaches, unique attractions and vibrant nightlife, Pattaya affords its visitors the opportunity to indulge in city life while enjoying the warm weather and stunning natural beauty of the region.

Pattaya is approximately 147 km southeast of Bangkok and is easily accessible by car, bus, or air transportation. Visitors to Pattaya are greeted with an atmosphere of excitement and wonder, with plenty of things to do and see. Whether it be a family vacation or a romantic getaway, Pattaya has plenty of experiences to offer.

List of Places to Visit in Pattaya

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Pattaya has several museums and historical sites that are worth visiting:

  1. Pattaya Floating Market - This attraction allows visitors to experience the local way of life on the water. Try local food, buy souvenirs and take a boat ride around the market.

  2. Sanctuary of Truth - This magnificent and intricately carved temple is made entirely of wood and represents the four major art forms of Thailand: literature, music, sculpture, and architecture.

  3. Nong Nooch Tropical Garden - This sprawling garden is home to some of Asia’s most beautiful landscapes, orchids, outdoor shows, along with rides on elephant backs.

Natural Attractions of Pattaya: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Pattaya has much to offer when it comes to natural attractions:

  1. Koh Larn Island - This island is located just a 30-minute ferry ride from Pattaya and is the perfect escape from the busy city. The island offers activities such as parasailing, jet skiing, and snorkeling.

  2. Pattaya Beach - This is the main beach in Pattaya, and it is perfect for swimming and sunbathing. The oceanfront promenade adds more value to it.

  3. Khao Pattaya Viewpoint - Enjoy stunning panoramic views of Pattaya by heading to this viewpoint, where visitors can feel on top of the world.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Pattaya

If you are a lover of outdoor adventure, Pattaya has many hiking and biking trails, and some of the best ones are:

  1. Pratamnak Hill -Jomtien Park - This park has a crisp and beautiful landscape, with lush green gardens, and panoramic scenes from the walking tracks.

  2. Doi Inthanon National Park - This park is a must-visit if you enjoy scenic landscapes with hiking paths cutting through the beautiful forests

  3. Koh Samet Island - One of Thailand’s most pristine and beautiful islands. The island is famous for its gentle hiking and biking tracks.

Best Things to Do in Pattaya: Activities and Tours

Pattaya has plenty of enticing things to do and see:

  1. Walking Street - Pattaya’s liveliest street that comes alive at night with plenty of restaurants, bars, and nightclubs.

  2. Ripley’s Believe It or Not! - Enjoy the oddities and bizarre facts of this museum that has a collection of the world’s most unusual, odd, and rare displays.

  3. Coral Island - Discover this stunning island with a glass-bottom boat tour, snorkeling, and scuba diving.

Week-Long Itinerary for Pattaya

For a hassle-free travel experience, here’s an itinerary for your weeklong adventure in Pattaya:

  1. Day 1- Relax on Pattaya Beachbar

  2. Day 2- Visit the Sanctuary of Truth and the Pattaya Floating Market

  3. Day 3- Explore the Nong Nooch Tropical Garden and then head to Walking Street’s nightlife for your evening fun

  4. Day 4- Take a day trip to the beautiful Koh Larn Island.

  5. Day 5- Visit the Khao Pattaya Viewpoint and then indulge in water sports at Coral Island.

  6. Day 6- Biking tour to Pratumnak Hill - Jomtien Park.

  7. Day 7- Grab some souvenirs at the local markets before heading to the airport.

Food and Drink Scene of Pattaya

Pattaya offers an extensive variety of local and international cuisine, along with many different dining options for tourists.

Must-Try Dishes

  1. Pad Thai - A famous stir-fry rice noodle dish with meat, eggs, peanuts, and bean sprouts.

  2. Som Tum - a spicy papaya salad made with shredded green papaya, tomatoes, chili peppers, lime juice, sugar, fish sauce, and peanuts.

  3. Gaeng Keow Wan - Green curry made with fresh herbs, coconut milk, and chicken or beef.

Local Beverages

  1. Thai Tea - A famous iced tea made with condensed milk, sugar, and Thai Tea powder

  2. Chang Beer - One of the most popular beers in Thailand.

  3. Singha Beer - Another famous beer in Thailand that is loved for its taste and crisp nature.

Where to Stay in Pattaya: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Pattaya has something for every traveler, with a variety of affordable and lavish accommodation options.

  1. Dusit Thani Pattaya - A 5-star hotel on Pattaya Beach.

  2. Noursabah Pattaya - A quaint hotel with beachside views and an outdoor pool

  3. Ostello - An affordable hostel with clean rooms and central location in Pattaya.

What to Buy in Pattaya: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  1. Thai Silk - Thailand is famous for its silk, and Pattaya has unique silk stores with various clothing and accessories options.

  2. Buddha Statues - You will find numerous shops selling replicas of Lord Buddha’s beautiful statues, which represent peace and tranquility.

  3. Thai Spices and Herbs - An excellent souvenir option for those keen on cooking as you will discover various locally produced herbs and spices.

Local Culture and Customs of Pattaya

The locals in Pattaya revere their culture and traditions, and visitors should respect that when traveling to Pattaya. Dress modestly when visiting religious temples and avoid public displays of affection.

Best Time to Visit Pattaya

The high season for tourism in Pattaya is between November and February when the weather is at its most pleasant. The humidity is low, the skies are blue, and temperatures are relatively mild.

Do’s and Don’ts of Pattaya

Do:

  1. Respect the culture and customs of Pattaya

  2. Carry a copy of your passport at all times.

  3. Wear sunscreen to protect yourself from the hot sun.

Don’t:

  1. Engage in illegal activities like drugs or gambling.

  2. Flirt with the local women as they may be prostitutes.

  3. Take photographs of temples or religious sites without permission.

Safety and Health

Safety in Pattaya

Pattaya has a relatively low crime rate. However, travelers should generally avoid carrying large amounts of cash and valuables.

Health in Pattaya

Visitors should take precautions against mosquito bites and carry insect repellent. Food served at street vendors may not be sanitary, so travelers should stick to eating at established restaurants.

Getting to Pattaya: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Pattaya

The nearest airport to Pattaya is U-Tapao-Rayong Pattaya International Airport, approximately 45 kilometers away.

Bus Stations Near Pattaya

The Pattaya Bus Terminal is located in North Pattaya and operates buses to and from the city.

Other Options

Car rentals and taxis are readily available in Pattaya.

How to Get Around in Pattaya

Taxis, Songthaews (local shared pickup trucks), and motorbike taxis are the best way to explore Pattaya. Renting a car or scooter can also be a great option for those who want more flexibility and privacy.

Where to Go Next from Pattaya: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  1. Bangkok - Thailand’s capital city, located about two hours by car from Pattaya.

  2. Ayutthaya - The ancient capital of the Siam Kingdom, located about three hours by car from Pattaya.

  3. Koh Chang - A picturesque island with pristine beaches located about five hours by car from Pattaya.

Conclusion

Pattaya is a unique and vibrant city with something special to offer every visitor. From scenic views and outdoor adventures to cultural attractions and excellent dining, Pattaya has it all. With this comprehensive guide, you can make the most of your visit to this beautiful city.
