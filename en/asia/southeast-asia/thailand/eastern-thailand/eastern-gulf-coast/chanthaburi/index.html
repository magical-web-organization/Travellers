Introduction to Chanthaburi

Chanthaburi is a charming province located in the Eastern Gulf Coast of Thailand, boasting pristine beaches, idyllic countryside, and sumptuous cuisine. With a population of approximately 545,000, Chanthaburi is a vibrant hub that attracts visitors with its fascinating history, culture, and natural beauty. The province is famous for its fruits, especially durian and mangosteen.

List of Places to Visit in Chanthaburi

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

The Cathedral of the Immaculate Conception

Built in 1909, the Cathedral of Immaculate Conception is considered the largest Catholic Church in Thailand and is the center of religious activities in Chanthaburi.

Chanthaboon Waterfront Community

A riverside community that has preserved its diverse cultural heritage, including the historic wooden houses that showcase the unique blend of Thai, Chinese, and Vietnamese architecture.

Chanthaburi Gem and Jewelry Center

Recognized as one of the world’s leading gemstone trading centers, visitors can observe the locals as they work on the gemstones and even buy them.

Chao Lao Beach

One of the unlimited, tranquil beaches in Chanthaburi, and a haven for anyone who wants to enjoy Chanthaburi’s island life.

Natural Attractions of Chanthaburi: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Khao Kitchakut National Park

A mountainous area that is a prime location for hiking, bird watching, and seeing the famous Thong Prior Forest Temple. Take a peaceful walk to the summit of the meteorological station to enjoy the majestic panoramic view of Chanthaburi.

Namtok Phlio National Park

A forest park established in 1981, Namtok Phlio National Park is renowned for its seven-tiered waterfall and plentiful plant and wildlife.

Chanthaburi City Pillar Shrine

A legendary Shrine located at the centre of the Chanthaburi city which reflects the Thai culture and beliefs.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Chanthaburi

There are many hiking and biking routes in Chanthaburi, and all of them have explorers eager to discover the beautiful landscapes and scenic views of the town. Some of the favourite trails to the mountains are; Laem Sing Forest Conservation Centre, Khao Phloi Waen, and Khao Khitchakut National Park.

Best Things to Do in Chanthaburi: Activities and Tours

The Taste of Chanthaburi

Visitors will enjoy an array of authentic Thai cuisine that offers a variety of local and international dishes, including Chanthaburi’s signature dishes like sweet and sour curry, fried mushroom ball, Niao Dum, and Kaeng Khiao Wan. At Saphan Pla, sip in a fresh papaya salad and relax while admiring the view of the river.

Explore the market

Visiting Chanthaburi’s many markets is an experience in itself and there’s nothing that’s not available when you immerse yourself in this fantastic market.

River rafting

River rafting in Chanthaburi offers an adrenaline-pumping journey that traverses wild waters and spectacular scenery.

Week-Long Itinerary for Chanthaburi

Day 1: Chanthaburi City Tour

  • Explore Chanthaburi city
  • Walk through Chanthaboon Waterfront Community
  • Visit the Cathedral of the Immaculate Conception

Day 2: Khao Kitchakut National Park

  • Hike or mountain bike to the meteorological station
  • Eat at the park’s food court
  • Visit the City Pillar Shrine

Day 3: Namtok Phlio National Park

  • Trek through the forest
  • Enjoy the natural beauty of the waterfall
  • Relax at the park

Day 4: Soothai Wararam Temple

  • Visit Soothai Wararam Temple, one of Thailand’s most preserved cultural heritage
  • Take a bike ride to the monument of King Taksin the Great
  • Visit the Chanthaburi Gem and Jewelry Center

Day 5: Three Fabric and Sericulture Centre

  • Visit Three Fabric and Sericulture Centre
  • Explore Chanthaburi’s markets, including the well-known Talad Nat and Talad Chanthaburi
  • Visit the seaside temple of Sri Prawatsr

Day 6: Chao Lao Beach

  • Visit Chao Lao Beach for a day of sun, sea, and sand
  • Swim, sunbathe, and relax on the beach
  • Experience the local life with authentic seafood cuisine

Day 7: Departure

  • Return home or continue your Thailand adventure to other destinations

Food and Drink Scene of Chanthaburi

Must-Try Dishes

Niao Dum

Niao Dum is a Thai delicacy and a must-try dish when in Chanthaburi. It is a mix of rice, spices, onions, garlic, and chicken which is wrapped in banana leaves and steamed.

Sweet and Sour Curry

A tangy Thai curry with a blend of vegetables and a sour tang that complements the sweetness of the coconut milk.

Fried Mushroom Ball

A crispy mushroom ball, excellent for a quick snack or appetizer.

Local Beverages

Nam Manao

Nam Manao is a refreshing drink with lime juice that is perfect during hot weather.

Cha Manao

Cha Manao is the perfect accompaniment with a flavourful sweet and sour curry made of a blend of tea and lime.

Where to Stay in Chanthaburi: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Bussarakam Place Hotel

A well-located hotel, Bussarakam Place Hotel is known for its friendly staff and affordable rooms.

Pandinthong Apartment

For those on a budget, Pandinthong Apartment offers affordable accommodation while providing a comfortable stay.

K.P. Grand Hotel

Located in the heart of Chanthaburi, K.P. Grand Hotel provides an excellent view of the city skyline.

What to Buy in Chanthaburi: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Durian Chips

Visit Chanthaburi for its durian chips, a popular souvenir for tourists who love Thailand’s famous “King of Fruits.”

Gemstones and Jewelry

Chanthaburi is famous for its gemstones and jewelry, and visitors can purchase them at the Chanthaburi Gem and Jewelry Center.

Local Culture and Customs of Chanthaburi

Chanthaburi has a unique blend of cultures and customs, influenced by Thai, Chinese, and Vietnamese. When visiting the temples, it is traditional to dress appropriately and remove shoes before entering holy sites. Respect the elders, and don’t engage in any activities that might be considered disrespectful to the community.

Best Time to Visit Chanthaburi

The best time to visit Chanthaburi is between November and February, when the weather is dry and cool. The temperature remains between 20 to 30 degrees Celsius.

Do’s and Don’ts of Chanthaburi

Do:

  • Respect local customs and traditions.
  • Dress appropriately when visiting temples.
  • Explore Chanthaburi’s local markets.
  • Try the delicious Thai cuisine.

Don’t:

  • Engage in activities that may be considered disrespectful to the community.
  • Consume illegal drugs.

Safety and Health

Safety in Chanthaburi

Chanthaburi is generally a safe city but always be cautious of pickpockets. Be aware of your surroundings, and take extra care of your valuable items when you are in crowded areas or on public transport.

Health in Chanthaburi

Visitors should be mindful of medical issues in Chanthaburi, especially during the rainy season. Dehydration and insect-borne diseases are common, so it is essential to drink plenty of water and to apply mosquito repellent to help prevent illnesses.

Getting to Chanthaburi: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Chanthaburi

Travellers can fly to Trat Airport or Suvarnabhumi Airport in Bangkok, then take a minibus or taxi to Chanthaburi city.

Bus Stations Near Chanthaburi

Chanthaburi Bus Terminal offers travel options to destinations throughout Thailand, including Bangkok, Pattaya, and Koh Chang.

Other Options

Travellers can reach Chanthaburi by hiring a personal driver or renting a car with a driver.

How to Get Around in Chanthaburi

Taxis or local buses are the best way to get around Chanthaburi. Motorbikes are also available for hire, and bicycles and motorbikes can be rented from various rental shops.

Where to Go Next from Chanthaburi: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Nearby Destinations from Chanthaburi include:

  • Koh Chang National Park
  • Trat
  • Pattaya
  • Rayong

Excursions include:

  • River rafting
  • Hiking in Khao Kitchakut National Park
  • Visiting local museums and historical landmarks.

Conclusion

Chanthaburi is a must-visit destination for those wanting to experience the culture, beauty, and cuisine of Thailand. With its spectacular natural scenery, unique blend of cultures and customs, and its rich history and heritage, Chanthaburi offers a truly unforgettable experience. Visit Chanthaburi for its bustling markets, pristine beaches, and its famous gemstones.
