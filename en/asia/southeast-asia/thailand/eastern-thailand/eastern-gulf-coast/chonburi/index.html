Chonburi, located in the Eastern Gulf Coast of Thailand, is a charming province known for its sandy beaches, lush countryside, mouth-watering seafood, and rich cultural heritage. With a plethora of natural and cultural attractions to explore, Chonburi is perfect for those who want to escape the hustle and bustle of city life and immerse themselves in the local culture. Here’s your comprehensive travel guide to Chonburi, including the top things to do and see, where to stay, and what to eat.

Introduction to Chonburi

Located about 80 km southeast of Bangkok, Chonburi is one of the most popular tourist destinations in Eastern Thailand. The province, which has a rich history dating back to the Ayutthaya period, is famous for its beautiful beaches, ancient temples and shrines, museums and historical sites, and vibrant local culture. Chonburi is also known for its delicious seafood, which is caught fresh daily from the Gulf of Thailand.

List of Places to Visit in Chonburi

Chonburi is a province that is filled with diverse attractions and activities that cater to visitors of all interests. Here are a few must-visit places during your trip to this beautiful province.

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Wat Yansangwararam Woramahawihan: A large temple complex situated on a scenic hilltop overlooking the Gulf of Thailand, Wat Yansangwararam is home to impressive architecture, sacred statues, and beautiful gardens.
  • Sriracha Tiger Zoo: Home to over 200 Bengal tigers and other exotic animals, the Sriracha Tiger Zoo is an exciting destination that is perfect for families.
  • Khao Kheow Open Zoo: Known for being one of the largest zoos in Thailand, the Khao Kheow Open Zoo offers a thrilling experience where visitors can wander through natural habitats that house a wide range of animals including rhinos, giraffes, pandas, and more.
  • Muang Boran: Also known as the Ancient City, Muang Boran is a cultural park that houses replicas of historic Thai landmarks like temples, shrines, and palaces.
  • Bangsaen Beach: A popular holiday spot among locals, Bangsaen Beach offers beautiful sands, clear waters, and delicious seafood.
  • The Flight of the Gibbon: Take your adventure to the next level with a zip-line tour through the lush forest canopy at The Flight of the Gibbon. Experience white-knuckle thrills as you soar through the treetops with breathtaking views of the surrounding countryside.

Natural Attractions of Chonburi: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Nong Nooch Tropical Garden: A vast expanse of beautiful landscaped gardens, Nong Nooch is famous for its beautiful orchids, bonsai trees, and elephant shows.
  • Koh Si Chang: Koh Si Chang is a small island situated off the coast of Chonburi, famed for its pristine beaches, crystal-clear waters, and tranquil atmosphere.
  • Ko Khram Yai: Looking for ultimate seclusion? Head to Ko Khram Yai, where the only places to stay are tents or basic bungalows. The small island is uninhabited apart from a military outpost; explore the stunning coral reefs, take a hike, or simply relax on the quiet sandy beaches.
  • Koh Samed: A small island located near the eastern coast of Thailand, Koh Samed is famous for its clear waters, white sandy beaches, and picturesque scenery. The island is home to several beach resorts, bars, and restaurants.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Chonburi

Chonburi is surrounded by beautiful hills and mountains that provide a great opportunity for hiking and biking enthusiasts. Here are a few great trails in the area:

  • Khao Sam Muk: Located in Bang Saen, Khao Sam Muk is a popular hiking destination for those seeking panoramic views of the surrounding area.
  • Khao Kheow Open Zoo: In addition to housing a diverse range of animal species, the zoo also offers numerous trails that allow visitors to hike through the lush tropical forest.
  • Chaloem Phrakiat Bangsaen Trail: This newly completed seaside trail is perfect for those who want to experience an evening hike while enjoying the ocean breeze.
  • Parichat Trail: Located in Khao Khiao - Khao Chomphu Wildlife Sanctuary, the Parichat Trail is a challenging hike, but the views of nature and wildlife are simply breathtaking.

Best Things to Do in Chonburi: Activities and Tours

Looking for some unique activities to keep you busy in Chonburi? Here are a few fun and engaging options for you:

  • Cooking Classes: Take a Thai cooking class and learn how to make popular local dishes like Pad Thai, Tom Kha Gai, and Green Curry.
  • Scuba Diving: Chonburi is home to some of the best dive sites in Thailand, where you can explore the colorful coral reefs and vibrant marine life.
  • Golfing: There are numerous golf courses in Chonburi that offer world-class facilities and stunning views of the surrounding countryside.
  • Thai Massage: After a long day of sightseeing, treat yourself to a traditional Thai massage that will help you relax and rejuvenate your body and mind.

Week-Long Itinerary for Chonburi

Here’s a sample itinerary that covers some of the best attractions and activities in Chonburi:

Day Activity
1 Visit the Sriracha Tiger Zoo and learn about the conservation of rare and endangered species.
2 Explore the ancient temples and shrines of Chonburi’s Cultural Trail.
3 Take a hike through the lush forest of the Khao Kheow Open Zoo and discover the diverse flora and fauna.
4 Visit the pristine beaches of Koh Si Chang and enjoy a day of sun, sea, and sand.
5 Take a Thai cooking class and learn how to prepare traditional dishes using local ingredients.
6 Visit the picturesque island of Koh Samed and enjoy the stunning views, clear waters, and laid-back vibe.
7 Relax and unwind at one of Chonburi’s top-rated spas, where you can indulge in a traditional Thai massage or other wellness treatments.

Food and Drink Scene of Chonburi

Chonburi is famous for its fiery and flavorful cuisine that is both delicious and affordable. Here are a few dishes and drinks to try during your visit:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Tom Yum Goong: A spicy and sour soup made with shrimp, lemongrass, galangal, and lime leaves.
  • Som Tam: A refreshing papaya salad that combines sweet, sour, and spicy flavors with crunchy peanuts and dried shrimp.
  • Pad Thai: A popular stir-fried rice noodle dish that typically includes shrimp, tofu, bean sprouts, and chives.
  • Laab Mu: A spicy minced pork salad that is flavored with fresh herbs, lime juice, and fish sauce.

Local Beverages

  • Chang: This popular Thai beer has a smooth taste and is perfect for cooling off on a hot day.
  • Cha Yen: Also known as Thai iced tea, this sweet and creamy beverage is made with black tea, condensed milk, and spices.
  • Sang Som: A popular Thai rum made from sugarcane and molasses, Sang Som is often mixed with Thai iced tea for a refreshing cocktail.

Where to Stay in Chonburi: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

From luxury resorts to budget-friendly guesthouses, Chonburi has a wide range of accommodation options to choose from. Here are a few top-rated options:

  • Centara Grand Mirage Beach Resort: A luxurious beachfront property that offers stunning ocean views, a private beach, and world-class amenities.
  • Chill Inn Hostel: Perfect for budget travelers, the Chill Inn Hostel provides clean and comfortable dormitory-style accommodation at an affordable price.
  • Cape Dara Resort: Set on its own breathtaking peninsula that juts out into the sea, Cape Dara Resort offers luxurious rooms, a private beach, and a range of indulgent spa treatments.

What to Buy in Chonburi: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

If you’re looking for unique and authentic souvenirs to take back home, here are a few ideas:

  • Sriracha Sauce: A popular hot sauce that originated in the town of Sriracha in Chonburi.
  • Handmade Crafts: Chonburi is home to many artisanal shops and markets that showcase traditional handicrafts like pottery, woodworking, and weaving.
  • Fresh Seafood: Chonburi is famous for its fresh and delicious seafood that can be bought at local markets and restaurants.
  • Thai Silk: Experience the beauty and elegance of traditional Thai silk, which can be found in a variety of colors and patterns.

Local Culture and Customs of Chonburi

Chonburi is known for its vibrant and colorful festivals that celebrate its rich cultural heritage. These include:

  • Songkran Festival: This three-day festival, which takes place in mid-April, marks the Thai New Year and features lots of splashing water, street parties, and local food.
  • Rocket Festival: Held in May, this lively event involves the launching of handmade rockets into the sky, traditional dancing, and local food stalls.
  • Vegetarian Festival: This week-long festival, which usually takes place in October, involves vegetarian food stalls, parades, and religious ceremonies.

Best Time to Visit Chonburi

The best time to visit Chonburi is between November and February, when the weather is warm and sunny, but cool and comfortable. Avoid visiting during the rainy season between May and October, as heavy rainfall can disrupt your travel plans and make outdoor activities difficult.

Do’s and Don’ts of Chonburi

Do:

  • Respect local customs and traditions, especially when visiting religious sites or interacting with locals.
  • Dress modestly when visiting temples or other religious sites.
  • Try to use a few basic Thai phrases when engaging with locals.
  • Explore the local markets and sample the delicious street food available.

Don’t:

  • Do not touch the head of a Thai person, as this is considered rude.
  • Do not point your feet towards sacred objects or people, as this is considered disrespectful.
  • Do not litter or damage the natural environment.
  • Do not argue or raise your voice in public, as this is considered impolite.

Safety and Health

Safety in Chonburi

Chonburi is a relatively safe province for tourists, but like any other destination, it’s important to keep your wits about you and take basic precautions. Here are a few safety tips:

  • Avoid carrying large amounts of cash or valuables when going out.
  • Exercise caution when crossing the street, as traffic can be chaotic.
  • Be aware of scams and pickpockets in tourist areas, and only use licensed and reputable tour operators.
  • Use sunscreen and insect repellent when spending time outdoors.

Health in Chonburi

Chonburi is not known for any major health risks, but it’s always advisable to take basic precautions to ensure your well-being during your stay. Here are a few health tips:

  • Use bottled or boiled water to avoid stomach upsets.
  • Ensure that you have up-to-date vaccinations before traveling to Thailand.
  • Wear appropriate clothing to protect yourself from mosquito bites.
  • Pack any necessary medications or items in your travel medical kit.

Getting to Chonburi: Transportation Options and Logistics

Chonburi is located about a 90-minute drive from Bangkok, making it easily accessible through various modes of transportation. Here are a few options to consider:

Airports Near Chonburi

  • Suvarnabhumi Airport: Located 70 km away, this is the primary International airport for Bangkok.

Bus Stations Near Chonburi

  • Bangkok Bus Terminal (Eastern): Located in Ekamai, Bangkok, this is the primary bus terminal that connects Bangkok to Chonburi.
  • Pattaya Bus Terminal: Located in Pattaya, this bus terminal offers regular services to Chonburi Province.

Other Options

  • Taxis: You can book a private taxi or a shared taxi from Bangkok to Chonburi.
  • Trains: The nearest train station to Chonburi is in Ban Phlu Ta Luang, which is about a 20-minute drive away.

How to Get Around in Chonburi

Getting around Chonburi is relatively easy, with several options available:

  • Taxis and Private Cars: Taxis and private cars can be hired for a reasonable price. Private cars may be best for those exploring the province on a daily basis.
  • Motorcycle Taxis: Motorcycle taxis are a popular mode of transportation in Chonburi, and they are easy to find on most street corners.
  • Songthaews: Shared taxis or “Songthaews” are common in Chonburi and offer an affordable and convenient way to get around.

Where to Go Next from Chonburi: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Chonburi is situated in a region of Thailand that offers many other amazing destinations to explore. Here are a few top choices:

  • Pattaya: Take a short 30-minute drive south to Pattaya, the world-famous seaside resort town that offers a combination of sandy beaches, lively nightlife, and other fun activities.
  • Rayong: With its relaxed pace, scenic coastlines, and fresh seafood, Rayong is a popular destination for those seeking a less crowded beach experience.
  • Bangkok: Travel north to Bangkok, the vibrant capital city of Thailand, and explore its many palaces, temples, markets, and food scene.

Conclusion

Chonburi offers an ideal location for those seeking outdoor adventures, cultural immersion, and relaxation in a beautiful and affordable destination in Thailand. With its stunning natural scenery, mouth-watering cuisine, and rich cultural heritage, Chonburi is a place that has something for everyone. So, plan your trip to Chonburi today, and get ready for an unforgettable travel experience!
