Introduction to Eastern Gulf Coast

Thailand’s Eastern Gulf Coast is a gem waiting to be explored. This beautiful region boasts natural beauty with white sandy beaches, clear waters, and hidden lagoons. Visitors will have plenty of choices when it comes to adventurous activities and excursions, while history and cultural enthusiasts will enjoy the vast and incredibly well-preserved cultural and historical sites. Whether you are a solo traveler or coming to Eastern Gulf Coast with family and friends, you are guaranteed to have a memorable experience.

Local Culture and Customs of Eastern Gulf Coast

Thailand is a country that values its culture and customs, and visitors should be mindful of this when exploring Eastern Gulf Coast. Here are a few customs to note:

  • Remove shoes before entering a temple or a Thai house.
  • The head is considered sacred while the feet are considered unclean.
  • Thais greet each other with the “wai” - a gesture involving hands pressed together.

Best Time to Visit Eastern Gulf Coast

The best time to visit Eastern Gulf Coast is from November to February, where the weather is dry and mild. But the region is a beautiful destination all year round, with each season offering unique sights and activities.

Do’s and Don’ts of Eastern Gulf Coast

Do:

  • Dress conservatively when visiting temples and other religious sites.
  • Get travel insurance before visiting Thailand.
  • Plan to stay for at least a week to fully explore the region.

Don’t:

  • Disrespect the Thai monarchy or culture.
  • Take advantage of the local customs or traditions.
  • Overstay your visa.

Safety and Health

Safety in Eastern Gulf Coast

Thailand, in general, is generally a safe place to visit, with visitors unlikely to encounter any major safety concerns. However, tourists should always take extra care when traveling late at night or in isolated areas.

Health in Eastern Gulf Coast

Visitors to Eastern Gulf Coast should always ensure they have comprehensive travel insurance, and travelers’ core vaccines should be up-to-date before arriving in the region.

Getting to Eastern Gulf Coast: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Eastern Gulf Coast

The closest airport is U-Tapao Rayong Pattaya International Airport (UTP), roughly a 45 to 60-minute drive from Pattaya.

Other nearby airports include Suvarnabhumi Airport (BKK) and Don Mueang International Airport (DMK).

Bus Stations Near Eastern Gulf Coast

  • Pattaya Bus Terminal
  • Rayong Bus Terminal

Other Options

  • Private cars/taxis
  • Motorbikes

How to Get Around in Eastern Gulf Coast

The best way to explore Eastern Gulf Coast is by renting a car or motorbike. There is little traffic congestion, which makes driving simple. Foreign visitors will need a Thai driving license to rent a car or bike locally.
