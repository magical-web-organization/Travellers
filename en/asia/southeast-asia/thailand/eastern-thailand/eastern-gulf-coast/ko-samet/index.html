Are you planning your next getaway to Thailand and looking for a sunny paradise with pristine white sand beaches, crystal clear waters, and a laid-back lifestyle? Ko Samet might be perfect for you! This charming island is located off the eastern coast of Thailand, and it offers a perfect escape for nature enthusiasts, beach lovers, and those seeking a relaxed vacation. Here is our comprehensive tour guide to help you plan your trip to Ko Samet.

Introduction to Ko Samet

Ko Samet, also known as Koh Samed, is a small island situated around seven kilometers off the mainland’s south coast, and it is part of the Khao Laem Ya–Mu Ko Samet National Park. It covers an area of around 6.5 square kilometers and it is a popular destination for both locals and tourists. The island is named after the cajeput tree, known as “samet” in Thai.

Ko Samet has some of the most beautiful beaches in Thailand with crystal clear turquoise waters and powdery white sand. The island is blessed with a tropical climate, and the warm temperatures range from 28°C to 35°C with sunny days throughout the year. The best time to visit Ko Samet is from December to May when the weather is dry and sunny.

List of Places to Visit in Ko Samet

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Ko Samet has a rich history and culture to explore. Here are some of the must-visit historical sites and museums:

  1. Wat Ko Samet: The island’s most prominent Buddhist temple, with impressive architecture and a peaceful atmosphere.
  2. Khao Laem Ya–Mu Ko Samet National Park: A protected area that comprises several islands and has lush forests, abundant wildlife, and stunning beaches.
  3. King Mongkut’s Institute of Technology Ladkrabang Marine Science Campus Museum: A marine biology museum with many exhibits showcasing local marine life, including fish, coral reefs, and sea turtles.

Natural Attractions of Ko Samet: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Ko Samet is a nature lover’s paradise, with plenty of opportunities for outdoor activities such as snorkeling, swimming, hiking, and more. Here are some of the top natural attractions:

  1. Sai Kaew Beach: The island’s most popular beach and a perfect spot for water sports and sunbathing.
  2. Ao Prao Beach: Surrounded by lush greenery, this tranquil beach offers a relaxing spot to unwind.
  3. Khao Laem Ya: Climb this hill for panoramic views of the sea and the surrounding islands.
  4. Koh Kudee: A horseshoe-shaped beach with clear waters and beautiful sand, perfect for swimming and snorkeling.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Ko Samet

Exploring Ko Samet on foot or by bicycle is an excellent way to discover the island’s natural beauty and cultural heritage. Here are some great routes for hiking and biking:

  1. Koh Samet Village to Phrao Bay: A 3.5-kilometer road with stunning coastal views.
  2. Ao Kiew Na Nai to Ao Kiew Na Nok: A 2.5-kilometer trail through the forest offering beautiful scenery.
  3. Sai Kaew to Ao Prao: A scenic route through the island’s most popular beaches.

Best Things to Do in Ko Samet: Activities and Tours

Ko Samet is not just about sunbathing and swimming, but also offers exciting activities to spice up your trip. Here are some recommended things to do:

  1. Snorkeling and Diving: Ko Samet has several great dive sites to explore its underwater beauty.
  2. Island hopping: Take a boat trip to the nearby islands and discover their tropical paradise.
  3. Kayaking: Rent a kayak and paddle around the island’s coastline for a unique perspective.
  4. Thai cooking classes: Learn how to cook traditional Thai dishes with a cooking class.

Week-Long Itinerary for Ko Samet

Here is a plan for a memorable week-long trip on Ko Samet:

Day Activities
Day 1 Relax on Sai Kaew Beach, take a boat trip to nearby Ko Kudee
Day 2 Explore the Khao Laem Ya–Mu Ko Samet National Park, and hike up Khao Laem Ya hill
Day 3 Snorkel or dive in the clear waters off the island
Day 4 Visit Wat Ko Samet and King Mongkut’s Institute of Technology Ladkrabang Marine Science Campus Museum
Day 5 Kayak around the coastline and enjoy scenic views
Day 6 Take a Thai cooking class and savor delicious local dishes
Day 7 Shop for souvenirs and relax on the beach before departure

Food and Drink Scene of Ko Samet

Thai cuisine is famous for its bold flavors, fresh ingredients, and aromatic spices, and Ko Samet is no exception. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  1. Pad Thai: A classic Thai dish made with stir-fried rice noodles, vegetables, eggs, and your choice of meat or tofu.
  2. Tom Yum Goong: A spicy and sour soup made with shrimp, lemongrass, chili, and lime.
  3. Green Curry: A spicy and creamy curry made with coconut milk, green chili, and your choice of meat or seafood.

Local Beverages

  1. Chang beer: A crisp and refreshing beer, perfect for hot weather.
  2. Thai iced tea: A sweet and creamy iced tea made with black tea, condensed milk, and spices.

Where to Stay in Ko Samet: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Ko Samet has a wide range of accommodation options to suit all budgets and preferences. Here are some of the top choices:

  1. Samed Villa Resort: A luxurious resort on Sai Kaew Beach, with splendid beachfront villas and sea views.
  2. Tubtim Resort: A peaceful and tranquil resort located on the west coast of the island, with stylish bungalows and an outdoor pool.
  3. Samed Pavilion Resort: A budget-friendly resort close to the beach with cozy rooms and an outdoor pool.

What to Buy in Ko Samet: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Ko Samet is a great place to shop for souvenirs and local products. Here are some recommended items to buy:

  1. Cajeput Oil: An aromatic essential oil made from the cajeput tree that is said to have medicinal properties.
  2. Handmade clothing and accessories: From colorful sarongs to woven bags handcrafted by locals, there are many unique items to choose from.
  3. Local snacks and sweets: Ko Samet has a variety of sweet and savory snacks that make great gifts and souvenirs.

Local Culture and Customs of Ko Samet

The local culture of Ko Samet is a unique blend of Thai traditions and island lifestyle. Here are some basic customs to keep in mind:

  1. Dress modestly when visiting temples or entering main streets.
  2. Remove shoes before entering inside of temples and some shops.
  3. Respect local people and their beliefs.

Best Time to Visit Ko Samet

The best time to visit Ko Samet is from December to May when the weather is dry and sunny. However, it is also the peak season, so expect large crowds and higher prices.

Do’s and Don’ts of Ko Samet

Here are some do’s and don’ts to keep in mind when visiting Ko Samet:

Do:

  1. Be respectful to the locals and their culture.
  2. Wear sunscreen and insect repellent while participating in outdoor activities.
  3. Stay hydrated and drink plenty of water.
  4. Try the local cuisine.

Don’t:

  1. Litter on the beaches or the streets.
  2. Touch or pollute the coral reefs while snorkeling or diving.
  3. Disrespect the Thai king and the royal family.

Safety and Health

Ko Samet is a relatively safe place for travelers, but it’s essential to take some precautions. Here are some tips:

Safety in Ko Samet

  1. Keep your valuables secure and be mindful of pickpockets while traveling.
  2. Always wear a helmet while riding a motorbike or bicycle.
  3. Avoid swimming in shallow waters as there are occasional rocks that can be dangerous.

Health in Ko Samet

  1. Use insect repellent to avoid mosquito bites and the dengue virus.
  2. Drink only bottled or boiled water to prevent stomach upset.
  3. Apply sunscreen as the sun can be intense on the island.

Getting to Ko Samet: Transportation Options and Logistics

Ko Samet is easily accessible by a combination of land and sea transportation. Here are some transportation options:

Airports Near Ko Samet

The nearest airports to Ko Samet are U-Tapao International Airport (UTP) and Bangkok’s Suvarnabhumi Airport (BKK).

Bus Stations Near Ko Samet

There are several bus stations that provide services to Ko Samet from Bangkok, Rayong, and Pattaya.

Other Options

One can take either a public van or a private taxi from Ban Phe, the ferry terminal on the mainland, to the island. There are ferries every day from 6 am to 6 pm from Ban Phe to the island, with a travel time of approximately 30 minutes.

How to Get Around in Ko Samet

Getting around Ko Samet is not difficult, and travelers can choose from a variety of options, including:

  1. Motorbike rentals
  2. Bicycle rentals
  3. Walking
  4. Shared taxi rides

Where to Go Next from Ko Samet: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Ko Samet has many beautiful nearby destinations that you can explore:

  1. Pattaya: A vibrant coastal city with a lively nightlife and many recreational activities.
  2. Koh Chang: A bigger and less commercialized island with stunning beaches, waterfalls, and national parks.
  3. Rayong: A city off the coast with beautiful beaches, delicious seafood, and a relaxed atmosphere.

Conclusion

Ko Samet is a hidden gem in Thailand that promises relaxation and sunshine, cultural discovery, and outdoor adventure. Use this guide to plan your perfect getaway to this tropical sanctuary by the sea.
