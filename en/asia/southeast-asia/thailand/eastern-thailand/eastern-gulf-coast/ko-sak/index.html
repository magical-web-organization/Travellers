Introduction to Ko Sak

Ko Sak, a small and breathtaking island, is located in the Eastern Gulf Coast in Eastern Thailand. The island is a popular destination for its peaceful, laid-back atmosphere, crystal clear waters, and coral reefs. If you’re looking for a serene escape from the hustle and bustle of city life, Ko Sak is the perfect place to visit.

List of Places to Visit in Ko Sak

Ko Sak is home to many culturally important attractions, natural wonders, hiking and biking routes, and much more. Let’s explore some of the top attractions to add to your itinerary.

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  1. Wat Phra Kaew: This ancient temple is believed to be the birthplace of the famous Emerald Buddha, a highly revered and sacred symbol of Thailand.
  2. The Sanctuary of Truth: It is a magnificent wooden structure that’s decorated with intricately carved sculptures and carvings based on traditional Thai and Hindu mythology.
  3. Sanctuary of Nong Nooch: Featuring stunning botanical gardens, cultural shows, and a traditional Thai architecture, it’s a top attraction for visitors of all ages.

Natural Attractions of Ko Sak: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  1. Ko Sak Beach: Featuring soft golden sand, crystal clear waters, and palm trees, the beach is a perfect spot for relaxation.
  2. Ko Sak Coral Reef: The underwater world here brings visitors face-to-face with marine life such as corals and multi-colored fishes.
  3. Khao Sam Muk: A small hill which offers a panoramic view of the entire island.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Ko Sak

  1. Ban Amphoe to Ko Sak: This route is ideal for those who want to combine adventure and sightseeing and is best traversed on a mountain bike.
  2. Ko Sak to Ban Kram: This route follows the coastline, offering stunning views of the Gulf of Thailand.

Best Things to Do in Ko Sak: Activities and Tours

  1. Snorkeling and Diving: Experience the crystal-clear water of Ko Sak and explore the underwater world.
  2. Fishing: Traditional fishing methods on Ko Sak include casting nets, traps, and fishing lines.
  3. Kayaking: Take a leisurely paddle and explore the hidden beaches and coves.

Week-Long Itinerary for Ko Sak

Day 1: Arrival and visit to Wat Phra Kaew. Day 2: Snorkeling and scuba diving excursions to Ko Sak Coral Reef. Day 3: Explore the Khao Sam Muk hill and enjoy the panoramic views. Day 4: Visit the Sanctuary of Nong Nooch. Day 5: Walking tour of local markets and Ban Amphoe to Ko Sak on a mountain bike. Day 6: Relaxation on Ko Sak Beach and experience a Thai massage. Day 7: Fishing or kayaking on the island.

Food and Drink Scene of Ko Sak

Must-Try Dishes

  1. Tom Yum Goong: A spicy soup made with shrimp and coconut milk.
  2. Pad Thai: A classic Thai noodle dish that’s stir-fried with eggs, vegetables, and peanuts.
  3. Pineapple Fried Rice: A sweet and savory dish that features chunks of golden pineapple along with shrimp and cashews.

Local Beverages

  1. Thai Iced Tea: A sweet and creamy iced tea that’s made with condensed milk and spiced with star anise.
  2. Chang Beer: A popular beer brewed in Thailand that’s a great way to beat the heat.

Where to Stay in Ko Sak: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  1. Bannammao: A guesthouse located near the Ko Sak beach.
  2. Ark Bar Beach Resort: A beachfront resort that provides easy access to the beach and local shops.
  3. Huay Yai Riverside Resort: This riverside resort is in a serene location, a perfect place for those seeking peace and tranquility.

What to Buy in Ko Sak: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  1. Handmade Wooden Craftsmanship: These are intricately carved wooden crafts including jewelry boxes, photo frames, and home decor items.
  2. Khao Lam: A Thai dessert made up of coconut milk and sticky rice.
  3. Local Fruit: Ko Sak is known for its fresh tropical fruits including mango, durian, and lychee.

Local Culture and Customs of Ko Sak

The local people of Ko Sak are friendly and welcoming. It’s essential to dress conservatively while visiting temples or other religious sites, and it’s crucial to understand and respect the Thai culture and customs.

Best Time to Visit Ko Sak

The best time to visit Ko Sak is during the dry season, from November to April when the weather is pleasant, and humidity is low.

Do’s and Don’ts of Ko Sak

Do:

  1. Be respectful of Thai culture and customs.
  2. Dress conservatively while visiting religious sites.
  3. Learn a few Thai phrases to communicate better with the locals.

Don’t:

  1. Don’t take any drugs as it’s illegal in Thailand.
  2. Don’t touch or displace artifacts in temples and religious sites.
  3. Don’t take off your shoes in any place other than your room.

Safety and Health

Safety in Ko Sak

Ko Sak is a safe place to visit. However, it’s essential to take precautions like locking your valuables in a safe, carrying minimal valuables, and being alert at night. Avoid getting involved with illegal drugs or criminal activities.

Health in Ko Sak

Ko Sak is a mostly safe environment for visitors. However, it’s recommended to drink only bottled water, avoid eating food from roadside stalls and practice good hygiene.

Getting to Ko Sak: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Ko Sak

The nearest airport to Ko Sak is the U-Tapao International Airport, which is approximately 30 kilometers away from the island.

Bus Stations Near Ko Sak

There is no bus station on Ko Sak Island. The Ban Ampore Bus Station, located 12 kilometers away from the Island, is a popular starting point for visitors to the island.

Other Options

Taxi services and rental cars are available at the airport and Ban Ampore Bus Station. Visitors can also get to the island via a ferry from the nearby Chonburi coast.

How to Get Around in Ko Sak

Visitors can rent a bicycle or scooter on the island, go hiking, or hire a local taxi to visit different attractions.

Where to Go Next from Ko Sak: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Some of the nearby destinations that can be visited from Ko Sak are Pattaya, Jomtien Beach, and the island of Koh Larn.

Conclusion

Ko Sak is a beautiful, peaceful island that offers a unique experience for visitors looking for a serene escape from the hustle and bustle of city life. By visiting the top attractions, indulging in the delicious food and drink culture, and following the recommended safety precautions, you’ll surely make the most out of your visit to Ko Sak.
