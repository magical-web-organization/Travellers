Introduction to Rayong

Located in the Eastern Gulf Coast region of Thailand, Rayong is one of the country’s must-visit destinations. The area is well-known for its stunning coastline, with pristine beaches, crystal-clear waters, and diverse marine life. The region balances historic and cultural significance with natural beauty, providing visitors with an unforgettable experience. Rayong is budget-friendly and offers a variety of options for travelers of all backgrounds.

List of Places to Visit in Rayong

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

The Ban Phe Museum

The Ban Phe Museum is located in Ban Phe village, Rayong, and highlights the history of the area. The museum is home to various exhibits that showcase the different aspects of the region’s culture and showcase the lifestyles of the locals. The exhibits include artifacts, ancient pottery, traditional Thai household items, and much more.

Wat Pa Pradu

Wat Pa Pradu is a beautiful temple located in the city center of Rayong. The temple is surrounded by lush greenery and is home to a large standing Buddha statue. The statue is the main attraction, but visitors can also explore the ornate architecture of the temple.

Natural Attractions of Rayong: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Khao Laem Ya - Mu Ko Samet National Park

Khao Laem Ya - Mu Ko Samet National Park is a must-visit destination for nature lovers and outdoor enthusiasts. The park is home to a rich and diverse ecosystem with stunning fauna and flora. The park is an excellent destination for hiking or taking a boat tour to explore the stunning lunar-like landscape.

Mae Ram Phueng Beach

Mae Ram Phueng Beach is a long, white sandy beach that stretches for approximately 11km. The beach is perfect for swimming, relaxing, and sunbathing. The area is relatively untouched and provides visitors with a sense of solitude and serenity.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Rayong

Hiking and biking are excellent activities to explore the natural beauty of Rayong. The Khao Laem Ya - Mu Ko Samet National Park is the ideal destination, with a plethora of hiking trails. The park also offers bike rental services, so visitors can bike through the park to explore the beauty of the Eastern Gulf Coast.

Best Things to Do in Rayong: Activities and Tours

Rayong offers numerous activities and tours, ranging from cultural experiences to outdoor adventures. Some must-see attractions and activities include:

  • Explore the local markets and sample delicious street food in the city center of Rayong.
  • Take a boat tour to Koh Samet island, an idyllic destination perfect for swimming, sunbathing, diving, or snorkeling.
  • Visit the Khao Chamao Waterfall, an impressive waterfall located in the Khao Chamao-Khao Wong National Park.
  • Take a seafood cooking class and learn how to prepare traditional Thai cuisine.
  • Hire a personal guide and explore the natural beauty of the Khao Laem Ya - Mu Ko Samet National Park.

Week-Long Itinerary for Rayong

With so much to see and do in Rayong, a one-week itinerary is ideal to fully explore the area. Here is an example:

  • Day 1-2: Explore the city center of Rayong and visit cultural attractions such as the Ban Phe Museum and Wat Pa Pradu.
  • Day 3: Take a boat or bus tour to Koh Samet island and enjoy the white sandy beaches.
  • Day 4: Visit the Khao Chamao-Khao Wong National Park and hike to the Khao Chamao waterfall.
  • Day 5: Relax and unwind at the Mae Ram Phueng beach.
  • Day 6: Take a seafood cooking class and learn about traditional Thai cuisine.
  • Day 7: Explore the nature and beauty of the Khao Laem Ya - Mu Ko Samet National Park, and take a hike in the morning.

Food and Drink Scene of Rayong

Must-Try Dishes

Rayong is famous for its seafood, and visitors cannot leave without trying the following dishes:

  • Pla Raad Prik (Fried Fish with Chili Paste)
  • Goong Dten Bpan (Grilled Jumbo Prawns)
  • Hoy La (Clams with Spices)
  • Tom Yam Gung (Spicy Shrimp Soup)
  • Tom Kha Gai (Chicken in Coconut Milk Soup)

Local Beverages

  • Cha Manao (Lime Tea)
  • Nam Ta Krai (Lemongrass Juice)
  • Chang Beer (Local Thai Beer)

Local Culture and Customs of Rayong

Rayong is a traditional area of Thailand, so visitors must be respectful of the local customs and traditions. It is customary to take off your shoes when entering someone’s home or a temple, and visitors are encouraged to dress modestly when visiting cultural attractions.

Best Time to Visit Rayong

The best time to visit Rayong is from November to February, as the climate is pleasant, and the sea is calm. The weather is cool, making it an ideal time for outdoor activities such as hiking, biking, and sunbathing.

Do’s and Don’ts of Rayong

Do:

  • Respect the Thai customs and traditions.
  • Dress modestly when visiting a cultural site or entering someone’s home.
  • Remove your shoes before entering a temple or local’s homes.

Don’t:

  • Disrespect the Thai royal family or their customs.
  • Sit with your feet pointed or raised at someone or towards a Buddha image.
  • Touch a Thai monk unless they initiate a conversation with you.

Safety and Health

Safety in Rayong

Rayong is a safe area, and visitors need not worry. However, visitors are advised to be cautious when swimming or engaging in water sports to avoid accidents or incidents.

Health in Rayong

Visitors are advised to check vaccine recommendations before entering Thailand. The area is relatively safe concerning health, but visitors are encouraged to take precautions for food and drink safety.

How to Get Around in Rayong

Rayong is easily accessible via numerous public transportation options such as buses and trains. Visitors can also hire a bike, car, or scooter for transportation. Taxi services are also available for travelers looking for a more comfortable and convenient option.

Where to Go Next from Rayong: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

After exploring Rayong’s beauty, visitors can explore nearby areas such as:

  • Pattaya: A bustling city known for its nightlife and beautiful beaches.
  • Koh Samui: A well-known island located in the Gulf of Thailand, known for its natural beauty.
  • Bangkok: Thailand’s capital city and a must-visit destination for culture, history, and entertainment.

Rayong is a hidden gem in Eastern Thailand, offering a wealth of experiences for travelers looking for an authentic Thai experience.
