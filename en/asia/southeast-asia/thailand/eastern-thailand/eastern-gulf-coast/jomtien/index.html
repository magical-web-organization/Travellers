Jomtien Travel Guide: The Ultimate Tour Guide

Jomtien is a hidden gem located in the eastern Gulf Coast of Thailand. It’s a popular destination for those who want to escape the hustle and bustle of city life. The picturesque town has plenty to offer, including stunning beaches, natural attractions, cultural heritage sites, and mouth-watering cuisine. Our Jomtien travel guide will take you through the best places to visit, food and drink scene, where to stay, and everything in between to help you plan the perfect trip to Jomtien, Thailand.

Introduction to Jomtien

Jomtien is located in Eastern Thailand, just a 10-minute drive from Pattaya. It’s a perfect destination for those who want to relax by the beach or indulge in outdoor activities. The town is popular among tourists who want a quieter alternative to Pattaya. Jomtien has plenty of options for accommodations for all budgets, including hotels, hostels, and guest houses.

List of Places to Visit in Jomtien

When visiting Jomtien, there are plenty of places to explore. Here are some popular attractions you should add to your itinerary.

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • The Sanctuary of Truth - A beautiful temple made entirely out of wood. It’s a must-visit if you are looking to learn more about Thai culture.
  • Nong Nooch Tropical Garden - A vast botanical garden located in the heart of Jomtien. The garden features a large collection of orchids, palms, and exotic plants from around the world.
  • Temple of Truth - A magnificent temple that is entirely made out of teakwood. It’s a unique architectural masterpiece that is worth visiting.

Natural Attractions of Jomtien: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Jomtien Beach - The beach is famous for its clean waters, soft sandy beaches, and a vast array of water sports activities like swimming, jet skiing, parasailing, and more.
  • Koh Larn Island - An excellent place for adventure enthusiasts with activities like snorkeling, parasailing, and scuba diving available.
  • The Pattaya Floating Market – A bustling market located on the outskirts of Jomtien, featuring an extensive selection of food and souvenirs for the visitors to explore.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Jomtien

One of the top activities to do in Jomtien is hiking or biking. Here are some popular routes to consider:

  • Jomtien Beach to Pattaya Beach - A beautiful scenic route that will take you along the coast of Jomtien and Pattaya.
  • The Pratumnak Hill - A moderately challenging path with a breathtaking view of the bay area from the top.
  • Sukhumvit Road Cycling - A central cycling path with plenty of stops and cafes to rest and refuel.

Best Things to Do in Jomtien: Activities and Tours

If you’re looking for some exciting activities to do in Jomtien, here are some popular tours and activities to consider:

  • Elephant Jungle Sanctuary Visit - A unique opportunity to interact with elephants in their natural environment.
  • Tandem Skydiving - A thrilling experience of jumping out of a plane and soaring above Jomtien.
  • Parasailing – Experience the thrill of one of the popular water sports activities, parasailing over Jomtien beach, taking in the amazing views of its surroundings.

Food and Drink Scene of Jomtien

Jomtien is famous for its delectable Thai cuisine with a coastal twist. Here are some of the must-try dishes:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Tom Yum Kung - A spicy, sour, and savory soup made with shrimp, lemongrass, and chili peppers.
  • Pad Thai - A popular street food dish made with rice noodles, tofu, peanuts, and shrimp.
  • Ka Pow Gai - A stir-fried dish made with chicken, basil, chili, and garlic, served with rice.

Local Beverages

  • Thai Iced Tea - A popular drink made with black tea, sugar, and condensed milk, served over ice.
  • Chang Beer - A sweet, light lager beer, brewed locally in Thailand.
  • Mekhong Whiskey - A local coconut-flavored whiskey that is typically served as part of the after-dinner drinks menu.

Where to Stay in Jomtien: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Jomtien has plenty of accommodation options to choose from. Here are some popular options:

  • Z by Zing - A stylish hotel located in the heart of Jomtien. It features a rooftop pool and bar with stunning views of the coastline.
  • The Jomtien Twelve - A cozy guesthouse located near the beach, offering clean rooms and delicious breakfast.
  • Baan Suan Rim Talay - A popular hostel located near the beach, offering affordable dormitory-style accommodation.

What to Buy in Jomtien: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Jomtien is famous for its unique handicrafts and souvenirs. Here are some products that you can buy as souvenirs for yourself or friends and family:

  • Thai silk scarves - A beautiful and unique souvenir that you can wear.
  • Handmade soaps and skincare products - A popular gift item that features natural ingredients like coconut oil and aloe vera.

Local Culture and Customs of Jomtien

Thailand is a country with a rich cultural heritage. Here are some tips on cultural etiquette to keep in mind when visiting Jomtien:

  • Respecting the king - Thailand is a country that reveres its royals. It is essential to be respectful when mentioning the royal family or the monarchy.
  • Removing shoes - It’s customary to take off your shoes before entering temples or private homes.
  • Dress modesty - When visiting religious sites, it’s essential to dress conservatively and cover the shoulders and knees.

Best Time to Visit Jomtien

The best time to visit Jomtien is between November to February. The weather is pleasant with clear skies, and the temperature is mild. It’s the perfect time for outdoor activities such as hiking, cycling, and even skydiving.

Do’s and Don’ts of Jomtien

Do:

  • Respect the locals - It is vital to be respectful towards the locals and their customs and traditions.
  • Drink lots of water – Thailand’s hot climate can be dehydrating; it’s essential to keep yourself hydrated.
  • Bargain at markets - You can bargain for a better price at markets, especially if you are buying souvenirs or street food.

Don’t:

  • Disrespect the King/royal family - Speaking or acting disrespectfully towards the monarchy is considered an offense in Thailand.
  • Touch the Buddha statues - It’s forbidden to touch the Buddha statues or other religious artifacts in Thailand.
  • Disrespectful dress/behavior in religious sites - Dress modesty and avoid any behavior that may offend the religious site’s visitors.

Safety and Health

Jomtien is a safe destination for tourists. Here are some tips to stay safe:

Safety in Jomtien

  • Be aware of pickpockets - Like any tourist destination, be mindful of your belongings and avoid carrying too much cash with you.
  • Use taxis instead of motorbikes - Taxis are a safer mode of transportation than motorbikes.
  • Avoid unlicensed tour guides - To ensure your safety, always choose a licensed guide when booking tours.

Health in Jomtien

  • Stay hydrated - Thailand’s hot climate can be dehydrating; it’s essential to keep yourself hydrated.
  • Prepare for mosquitoes - Jomtien is a coastal area, and mosquitoes are prevalent. It’s essential to be prepared with mosquito repellent or wearing long-sleeved shirts and long pants.
  • Watch what you eat - Be cautious while trying out street food, which may not meet hygiene standards.

Getting to Jomtien: Transportation Options and Logistics

There are several transportation options available to get to Jomtien. Here are some popular options:

Airports Near Jomtien

  • U-Tapao International Airport - Located just 30 kilometers south of Jomtien, it’s the nearest airport to reach Jomtien.

Bus Stations Near Jomtien

  • Eastern Bus Terminal (Ekkamai) - This station has buses going directly to the Pattaya Bus Terminal, which is just 10 minutes drive from Jomtien.

Other Options

  • Taxis - Another option to travel to Jomtien is through taxis. These taxis are comfortable and offer more privacy and flexibility.

How to Get Around in Jomtien

Jomtien is a small town with plenty of transportation options available. Here are some popular choices:

  • Motorbike Taxis - One of the popular forms of transportation in Jomtien, especially for short distances. These drivers typically wear orange vests and are readily available.
  • Songthaews - Jomtien also has shared taxis offering fixed routes. They are a more affordable option, but they have limited schedules.
  • Tuk-Tuks - Tuk-tuks are another mode of transportation in Jomtien and are easily recognizable by their unique style.

Where to Go Next from Jomtien: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

After exploring Jomtien, there are several other nearby destinations that you can visit, such as:

  • Pattaya - Just a 10-minute drive from Jomtien, Pattaya offers a vibrant nightlife and several other attractions such as Tiffany’s Cabaret, Alcazar Show, multiple walking streets and shopping areas.
  • Bangkok - Thailand’s bustling capital city is just two hours away from Jomtien. Bangkok is the cultural, economic, and political center of Thailand.

Conclusion

Jomtien is a beautiful destination in Eastern Thailand that offers plenty of cultural and natural attractions. From pristine beaches to mouth-watering cuisine, Jomtien has something for everyone. With our comprehensive Jomtien travel guide, you can explore the best the town has to offer while staying safe and respecting local customs and traditions.
