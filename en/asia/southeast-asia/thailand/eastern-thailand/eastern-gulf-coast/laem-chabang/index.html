A Comprehensive Travel Guide to Laem Chabang, Thailand

Laem Chabang may not be a well-known destination in Thailand, but it is a hidden gem that is worth discovering. Located in the eastern Gulf coast, in Eastern Thailand, Laem Chabang is an excellent place for culture lovers and adventure seekers alike. In this travel guide, we’ll show you the most important places to visit, the best things to do, where to stay, and how to get there.

Introduction to Laem Chabang

Laem Chabang is a port city in Chonburi province, Eastern Thailand. It has a population of over 60,000 people and is an important center of trade and industry for the region. Laem Chabang Port is one of the busiest ports in Thailand, and it serves as the gateway for Bangkok, which is located about 120 km away.

List of Places to Visit in Laem Chabang

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Khao Sam Muk: This is a hill in Bang Saen that offers stunning views of the Gulf of Thailand. It’s also home to a Chinese shrine and a Buddhist temple.
  • Wat Yansangwararam Woramahawihan: This is a large temple complex that features a replica of the Buddha footprint and a large statue of Buddha.
  • Viharnra Sien: This is a museum that showcases Chinese art and culture, including a large collection of jade figurines and porcelain items.
  • Baan Sukhawadee: This is a palace-like mansion that’s open to the public with impressive buildings and beautiful gardens.

Natural Attractions of Laem Chabang: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Bangsaen Beach: This is a popular beach with golden sand and clear water. It’s a great place to relax and enjoy the sun.
  • Koh Loy Park: This is a small island located in the middle of Laem Chabang Port. It’s connected to the mainland by a bridge, and it has a park, a small temple, and good views over the sea.
  • Khao Kheow Open Zoo: This is a large zoo that features over 8,000 animals and plants from around the world. It’s a great place to learn about wildlife and conservation.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Laem Chabang

Laem Chabang has many parks and nature reserves that are perfect for hiking and biking. Some of the best routes to try are:

  • Khao Kheow Open Zoo: The zoo has several nature trails that offer a chance to see some of the animals up close while trekking through the jungle.
  • Bang Phra Reservoir: This is a large lake located about 8km from Laem Chabang. There is a cycling trail around the lake that offers scenic views and fresh air.

Best Things to Do in Laem Chabang: Activities and Tours

  • Visit a Floating Market: There are several floating markets around Laem Chabang that offer a chance to buy souvenirs, sample local food, and take a boat ride.
  • Explore the City on Foot: Laem Chabang is a small city with some interesting architecture and nice shops and restaurants to discover.
  • Take a Day Trip to Pattaya: Pattaya is a nearby city that’s known for its nightlife and beaches. It’s just a short drive away from Laem Chabang, and it makes for a fun day trip.

Week-Long Itinerary for Laem Chabang

Day 1: Take a city tour and visit Wat Yansangwararam Woramahawihan.

Day 2: Spend some time at Bangsaen Beach and Ban Sukhawadee.

Day 3: Explore Viharnra Sien Museum and go to the Khao Sam Muk viewpoint.

Day 4: Visit the Khao Kheow Open Zoo and explore the hiking trails.

Day 5: Take a day trip to Pattaya and enjoy the nightlife.

Day 6: Spend the day at Koh Loy, visit the park, and have some seafood.

Day 7: Relax and unwind with a spa treatment or massage in one of the many spas in Laem Chabang.

Food and Drink Scene of Laem Chabang

Must-Try Dishes

  • Tom Yam Goong: This is a spicy and sour soup with shrimp and lemongrass. It’s one of Thailand’s most famous dishes and definitely worth trying.
  • Grilled Seafood: Laem Chabang is located near the sea, so there is an abundance of fresh and delicious seafood to eat.
  • Mango Sticky Rice: This is a traditional Thai dessert made with sticky rice, mango, and sweet coconut cream. It’s the perfect way to end a meal.

Local Beverages

  • Thai Iced Tea: This is a sweet and creamy tea made with condensed milk and spices. It’s a refreshing drink on a hot day.
  • Singha Beer: This is one of Thailand’s most well-known beers, and it’s a good choice to pair with grilled seafood.

Where to Stay in Laem Chabang: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Balcony Seaside Sriracha: This hotel is located near the sea and offers rooms with great sea views and modern amenities.
  • The Park Bangpra: This resort-style hotel has a large pool and a peaceful garden. It’s a good choice for those who want to relax and unwind.
  • Kroque Boutique & Bistro: This hotel is located in the city center and has stylish rooms and a great restaurant.

What to Buy in Laem Chabang: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Thai Silk: Thailand is famous for its silk, and there are many shops in Laem Chabang that offer beautiful silk products.
  • Spices and Herbs: Thai cuisine is known for its unique blend of spices and herbs. You can buy local spices and herbs in many markets and shops in Laem Chabang.
  • Handicrafts: There are many craftspeople in Laem Chabang who make beautiful handcrafted items, including pottery, wood carvings, and textiles.

Local Culture and Customs of Laem Chabang

  • Respect the King and the Royal Family: Thai people have deep respect for their monarchy, so it’s important to show proper respect in public.
  • Dress modestly when visiting temples and other religious sites: This means covering your shoulders and legs, and removing your shoes before entering temples.
  • Don’t step on the currency: This is considered disrespectful in Thailand, and people can be fined or punished for doing so.

Best Time to Visit Laem Chabang

The best time to visit Laem Chabang is between November and February, when the weather is dry and cool. The temperatures can get quite hot between March and May, and it can be rainy from June to October.

Do’s and Don’ts of Laem Chabang

Do:

  • Try the local cuisine: Laem Chabang has some excellent seafood and Thai dishes that you won’t find anywhere else.
  • Be respectful to the locals: Thai people are friendly and welcoming, so it’s important to show them the same respect.
  • Carry small change with you: Tipping is not mandatory in Thailand, but it’s common to leave a small amount for good service.

Don’t:

  • Touch someone’s head: In Thai culture, the head is considered sacred, so it’s impolite to touch someone’s head without permission.
  • Raise your voice or show anger in public: Thai people value calmness and harmony, so it’s important to keep a respectful tone in public.
  • Scold or criticize someone in public: This can cause the other person to lose face and feel embarrassed.

Safety and Health

Safety in Laem Chabang

Laem Chabang is a relatively safe city, but it’s important to take the usual precautions.

  • Avoid going out alone at night: As with any city, it’s a good idea to be cautious and stay in well-lit areas at night.
  • Be aware of your surroundings: Pickpocketing and theft can occur in tourist areas, so it’s important to be mindful of your surroundings and keep your valuables close.

Health in Laem Chabang

It’s important to take precautions to avoid illness and disease when traveling to Laem Chabang.

  • Drink bottled water: Tap water in Thailand is not safe for drinking, so make sure to drink only bottled water.
  • Protect yourself from mosquitoes: Dengue fever is a risk in Thailand, so be sure to carry mosquito repellent with you.
  • Get up-to-date on vaccines: Consult with a doctor before traveling to Thailand to discuss which vaccines you need to update or obtain beforehand.

Getting to Laem Chabang: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Laem Chabang

The closest airport to Laem Chabang is U-Tapao International Airport, located about 50 km away. Alternatively, you can fly into Bangkok and take a taxi or public transportation to Laem Chabang.

Bus Stations Near Laem Chabang

There are several bus stations in nearby cities that offer transport to Laem Chabang. The main bus station in Bangkok is Mochit Bus Terminal, which has frequent departures to Laem Chabang.

Other Options

Another option is to take a train from Bangkok to Chonburi train station and then take a taxi or bus to Laem Chabang.

How to Get Around in Laem Chabang

The best way to get around in Laem Chabang is by renting a motorbike or taking a taxi. Walking is also a viable option, as many of the attractions are located within walking distance of each other.

Where to Go Next from Laem Chabang: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Pattaya: Pattaya is a nearby city that’s known for its beaches, nightlife, and entertainment.
  • Bangkok: Bangkok is just a few hours drive from Laem Chabang, and it’s a must-visit destination for anyone traveling to Thailand.
  • Koh Samet: This is a small island located off the coast of Rayong province. It’s known for its beautiful beaches and crystal-clear waters.

Conclusion

Laem Chabang is a hidden gem in Thailand that’s worth visiting for its cultural attractions, natural beauty, and delicious seafood. Whether you’re interested in history and art, outdoor adventures, or just relaxing on the beach, Laem Chabang has something for everyone. So, pack your bags and get ready for an unforgettable trip to Thailand’s eastern coast.
