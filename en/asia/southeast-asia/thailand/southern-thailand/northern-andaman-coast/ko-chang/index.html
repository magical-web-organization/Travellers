If you are looking for a true paradise, look no further than Ko Chang. Located in Southern Thailand, this hidden treasure is a gem that captivates the hearts of visitors. Here is your comprehensive travel guide to Ko Chang, complete with information on the best places to stay, dine, and explore.

Introduction to Ko Chang

Ko Chang is a small island that stretches 30 km long and 14 km wide. It has gradually grown in tourist popularity in recent years, but still maintains its pristine nature and authentic charm. Visitors come to Ko Chang for its unspoiled natural beauty, clear blue waters, and extensive outdoor adventures.

List of Places to Visit in Ko Chang

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Ko Chang also has an interesting history which visitors can explore through museums and historical places. If you are intrigued by Thai history, make sure to visit the following attractions:

  • Koh Chang Naval Battle Memorial
  • Ko Chang Temple
  • Koh Chang National Park

Natural Attractions of Ko Chang: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Ko Chang’s natural beauty is its main attraction, with plenty of outdoor adventures to keep visitors entertained. Whether you are interested in hiking, water sports, or simply taking a stroll along the beach, Ko Chang has it all. Here are some must-see natural attractions:

  • Klong Plu Waterfall
  • Than Mayom Waterfall
  • Kai Bae Beach
  • White Sand Beach
  • Lonely Beach

Hiking/Biking Routes of Ko Chang

Hiking and biking are two of the most popular activities in Ko Chang. The island has plenty of trails for both beginners and experienced hikers and bikers, offering stunning views of the island’s natural wonders. Some of the best hiking and biking routes include:

  • Klong Plu Waterfall Route
  • Salak Phet Route
  • Bailan Bay to Lonely Beach

Best Things to Do in Ko Chang: Activities and Tours

Ko Chang is packed with activities and tours to suit every interest and travel style. Here are some of the best things to do in Ko Chang:

  • Go Island Hopping
  • Visit Local Fishing Villages
  • Enjoy a Sunset Cruise
  • Scuba Diving and Snorkeling
  • Rent Motorbikes or Scooters

Week-Long Itinerary for Ko Chang

If you are planning a week-long trip to Ko Chang, here is a suggested itinerary to help you plan your trip:

Day 1: Arrive and relax on the beach Day 2-3: Go island hopping, snorkeling, and scuba diving Day 4: Rent a bike or take a tour to explore the island’s inland areas; visit a waterfall Day 5-6: Book a sunset cruise or visit local fishing villages Day 7: Departure

Food and Drink Scene of Ko Chang

Ko Chang’s cuisine is a mixture of Thai and seafood dishes, with fresh, locally-sourced ingredients. Here are some of Ko Chang’s must-try dishes:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Pad Thai
  • Tom Yum Soup
  • Massaman Curry
  • Seafood Platter

Local Beverages

  • Chang Beer
  • Mekhong Whiskey

Where to Stay in Ko Chang: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Ko Chang boasts a wide range of accommodation options to suit every budget and travel style, from luxury hotels to budget-friendly guesthouses and hostels. Here are some recommendations:

  • The Emerald Cove Koh Chang - Luxury hotel on the beach
  • Siam Royal View Villas - Private villas with a stunning view
  • Little Sunshine Boutique Beach Resort - Budget-friendly and cozy resort

What to Buy in Ko Chang: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Ko Chang’s markets are filled with hand-crafted souvenirs and unique local products that make for great mementos of your visit. Here are some items to consider bringing home:

  • Handmade soaps and shampoos
  • Thai silk and cotton fabrics
  • Local spices and sauces
  • Jewelry and accessories crafted by local artisans

Local Culture and Customs of Ko Chang

Ko Chang’s culture is heavily influenced by the Buddhist religion. Visitors to the island are expected to dress modestly when visiting temples and other holy sites. Additionally, it is considered rude to touch someone’s head as it is believed to be the most sacred part of the body.

Best Time to Visit Ko Chang

The best time to visit Ko Chang is between November and February when the weather is comfortable, and rainfall and humidity levels are low. The peak season is during December and January, so expect higher prices and bigger crowds during this time.

Do’s and Don’ts of Ko Chang

Do:

  • Respect local culture and customs
  • Enjoy the local cuisine
  • Protect the environment

Don’t:

  • Litter or damage the natural environment
  • Disrespect local culture and customs
  • Engage in illegal activities

Safety and Health

Safety in Ko Chang

Ko Chang is generally a safe and friendly destination for tourists. However, visitors are advised to be careful when swimming in the ocean, particularly in the off-season (June to October) when the currents can be strong.

Health in Ko Chang

Ko Chang has a medical clinic and a hospital, but for more serious medical emergencies, patients may need to be airlifted to the mainland. Visitors are advised to carry travel insurance that covers medical expenses.

Getting to Ko Chang: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Ko Chang

The closest airport to Ko Chang is Trat airport, which can be reached from Bangkok with one flight connection.

Bus Stations Near Ko Chang

From Bangkok, visitors can take a bus to Trat and then take a ferry to Ko Chang.

Other Options

For those who prefer a private transfer, there is the option of taking a minibus to Ko Chang from Bangkok.

How to Get Around in Ko Chang

Visitors can explore Ko Chang’s highlights either by renting a motorbike or a scooter, taking local transportation, or booking a private tour.

Conclusion

Ko Chang truly is a tropical paradise, packed with natural wonders and cultural experiences that will have you coming back for more. With this comprehensive travel guide, you are now equipped with everything you need to make the most out of your trip to this beautiful island.
