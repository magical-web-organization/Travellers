Introduction to Mu Ko Surin National Park

Mu Ko Surin National Park is one of the most breathtaking natural sites that you can explore in Thailand. Located in the northern Andaman Coast, in southern Thailand, Mu Ko Surin National Park comprises five stunning islands and the surrounding sea. The park is home to an amazing range of flora and fauna, making it a paradise for nature lovers and outdoor enthusiasts. In this guide, we’ll cover everything you need to know to plan the perfect trip to Mu Ko Surin National Park.

List of Places to Visit in Mu Ko Surin National Park

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

While Mu Ko Surin is primarily known for its natural beauty, there are also some fascinating cultural attractions to explore. Here are some of the top places to visit:

  • The Moken Village: Also known as the Sea Gypsy Village, this is a unique cultural experience where you can learn about the traditional lifestyle of the Moken people who have lived around these islands for centuries.
  • Queen Sikrit’s Garden: This is a lovely botanical garden created by Queen Sikrit of Thailand in 1965. It’s a peaceful retreat with stunning views and plenty of exotic plant species to discover.

Natural Attractions of Mu Ko Surin National Park: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

The real magic of Mu Ko Surin is in its natural beauty. Here are some of the top outdoor adventures and scenic spots to explore:

  • Snorkeling and diving: The clear turquoise waters around the islands are home to an incredible array of marine life and coral reefs that are perfect for snorkeling or diving.
  • Beaches: The stunning white sand beaches on the islands are some of the best in Thailand and offer the perfect setting for swimming, sunbathing, and relaxing with a book.
  • Hiking: The islands have several hiking trails, including one leading to the viewpoint on the top of the Suthep Hill, which offers breathtaking panoramic views of the surrounding islands and sea.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Mu Ko Surin National Park

If you’re up for some physical activity, there are plenty of trails to explore. Here are some of the best routes to take:

  • Suthep Hill Trail: This is a moderate hike that takes you to the highest point on the Surin Islands. The trail is approximately 4km long and offers stunning views of the surrounding landscape.
  • Biking on the island: You can rent a bike to explore the island on your own. This is a great way to see the stunning sights of the islands at your own pace.

Best Things to Do in Mu Ko Surin National Park: Activities and Tours

Mu Ko Surin offers endless opportunities for outdoor activities and tours. Here are some of the best things to do:

  • Island hopping: You can take a boat tour of the surrounding islands, which allows you to explore different beaches and viewpoints.
  • Snorkeling and Scuba diving: With crystal clear water, the marine life on the islands is amazing, which makes Mu Ko Surin the perfect spot for diving enthusiasts.
  • Kayaking along the shoreline: Kayaking is a great way to explore the many hidden coves and bays of the Surin Islands.

Week-Long Itinerary for Mu Ko Surin National Park

Here’s one suggested itinerary for a week-long trip to Mu Ko Surin National Park:

Day 1: Arrive at Khuraburi Pier, take a boat to the islands, and check into a hotel.

Day 2: Snorkeling or diving excursion and beach time

Day 3: Kayaking tour around the islands and some hiking

Day 4: Visit the Sea Gypsy Village and Queen Sikrit’s Garden

Day 5: More beach time, exploration, and relaxation

Day 6: Island hopping tour and more snorkeling or diving

Day 7: Depart from the islands at your own pace

Food and Drink Scene of Mu Ko Surin National Park

Must-Try Dishes

As an eco-sensitive destination, the island offers sustainable seafood and authentic Thai cuisine. Here are some must-try dishes:

  • Tom Yam Soup: A classic Thai dish that is known to have originated from Surin Islands with fresh seafood like the tom yam talay.
  • Lab Moo (Pork Salad): A salad-like dish made with minced pork, chili, and fresh herbs.
  • Papaya Salad: Som Tam, a refreshing salad made of shredded green papaya, fish sauce, lime juice, and Thai chili is a popular street food in the region.

Local Beverages

  • Cha-yen (Thai Iced Tea): A sweet, creamy, and refreshing iced tea made with condensed milk, tea, and sometimes with spices.
  • Singha Beer: Thailand’s most popular beer brand

Where to Stay in Mu Ko Surin National Park: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

There are several options for accommodation, ranging from high-end eco-lodges to hostels. Luxury: The Cliff & River Jungle Resort, Serenity Resort, Koh Surin Villa. Budget: Surin Bay Inn , Surin National Park Hostel

What to Buy in Mu Ko Surin National Park: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Here are some local goods to bring back home as keepsakes:

  • Handmade crafts: The Moken villagers on the island sell handmade crafts like woven baskets and hand-carved wooden sculptures.
  • Seafood products: Surin is known for its sustainable seafood products. Buy some local fish sauce, dried shrimps, or salted fish.

Local Culture and Customs of Mu Ko Surin National Park

While visitors are welcome at Mu Ko Surin National Park, it’s essential to respect the local customs and cultures. Here are some things to be mindful of:

  • Dress modestly when visiting temples or other cultural sites.
  • Plan to remove shoes before entering cultural sites.
  • Speak softly and avoid playing loud music to respect the peaceful atmosphere of the islands.

Best Time to Visit Mu Ko Surin National Park

The best time to visit the park is from November to April when the weather is dry and sunny, and the water is clear and calm, perfect for water activities. However, the park may be closed from May to October due to the monsoon season.

Do’s and Don’ts of Mu Ko Surin National Park

Here are some do’s and don’ts to keep in mind when visiting Mu Ko Surin National Park:

Do:

  • Respect the natural beauty of the park
  • Follow the Leave No Trace principles
  • Observe the park’s rules and regulations while participating in outdoor activities.

Don’t:

  • Use plastic to avoid spoiling the natural environment
  • Disturb the rare species of flora and fauna in the park
  • Smoke and drink alcohol in non-designated areas.

Safety and Health

Safety in Mu Ko Surin National Park

Visitors are requested to follow the park’s regulations and guidelines strictly to ensure the safety of themselves and the environment. Adhere to the following when traveling around the park:

  • Wear appropriate clothing, footwear, and protective gear before participating in any activity.
  • Listen to the instructions from guides and rangers.
  • Keep yourself protected from ticks and other insects.

Health in Mu Ko Surin National Park

It’s essential to have a basic first aid kit when visiting Mu Ko Surin National Park. Keep a check on your hydration levels and carry know of the nearest clinic or hospital.

Getting to Mu Ko Surin National Park: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Mu Ko Surin National Park

The closest airports to Mu Ko Surin National Park are Phuket, Trang, and Krabi. From these three airports, you can take a taxi or bus to Khuraburi Pier.

Bus Stations Near Mu Ko Surin National Park

The primary bus station that serves the route is Bangkok’s Southern Bus Terminal to Khuraburi Piers.

Other Options

  • Private car transfers are available, but they could be expensive.
  • Local buses to the pier from places like Ranong, Phuket, and Krabi.

How to Get Around in Mu Ko Surin National Park

There is no public transportation on the islands, and you must ask your hotel or resort to arrange boat transfers or book tours with agencies available on the island.

Where to Go Next from Mu Ko Surin National Park: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Khao Sok National Park: A small town with plenty of hiking and outdoor activities located approximately 165km northeast from Surin Island.
  • Phuket Island: The bustling resort island is located around 230km to the south of the Park.
  • Krabi Province: You can explore its stunning beaches and stunning islands just 250 km away from Mu Ko Surin National Park.

Conclusion

Mu Ko Surin National Park is an incredible Thai destination that offers a perfect harmony between untouched natural beauty, outdoor activities, and cultural experiences. By following this comprehensive guide and adhering to the park’s regulations, visitors can preserve the natural beauty of the islands for generations to come.
