Introduction to Similan Islands

Similan Islands is a group of nine small islands located in the Northern Andaman Coast in Southern Thailand. The island group is part of the Mu Koh Similan National Park, which has been designated as a protected marine area. It has quickly become a popular destination for travelers from all around the world, thanks to its crystal-clear waters, stunning beaches, and breathtaking scenery.

List of Places to Visit in Similan Islands

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Although Similan Islands isn’t known for its cultural landmarks, there are a few places that are worth visiting. The most noteworthy is the Chao Leh Museum, which celebrates the cultural heritage of the Chao Leh, the indigenous people who have lived in the area for generations.

Natural Attractions of Similan Islands: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

The main attractions of Similan Islands are its natural wonders, including its untouched beaches, crystal-clear waters, and stunning coral reefs. Here are some of the must-visit natural attractions:

  • Donald Duck Bay: This bay gets its name from the rock formation that looks like the famous cartoon character. It’s a perfect spot for snorkeling and swimming.
  • Koh Payu: This island is known for its beautiful beaches and stunning views. From here, you can also go on a jungle trek and explore the island’s lush vegetation.
  • Similan Island Viewpoint: The viewpoint offers stunning views of the Similan Islands and is an excellent spot for photography enthusiasts.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Similan Islands

Similan Island is a great destination for outdoor enthusiasts, with plenty of hiking and biking trails to explore. Here are some of the best routes:

  • Koh Miang: This island has two hiking trails, one for the northern part of the island and another for the southern part. Both offer incredible views of the surrounding area.
  • Koh Ba Ngu: This island is known for its challenging hiking trail that offers stunning views of the island’s crystal-clear waters.
  • Koh Similan: The island has a 5-kilometer trail that crosses the island’s lush vegetation and beautiful beaches.

Best Things to Do in Similan Islands: Activities and Tours

From snorkeling and diving to kayaking and island hopping, there’s no shortage of activities in Similan Islands. Here are some of the best things to do:

  • Snorkeling: The clear waters of Similan Islands make it an excellent destination for snorkeling and diving. The island’s coral reefs are home to a vast array of marine life, including colorful fish, sea turtles, and tropical corals.
  • Island Hopping: Take a day trip to some of the nearby islands to explore their stunning beaches and breathtaking scenery.
  • Kayaking: Rent a kayak and explore the island’s coastline for a unique perspective.

Week-Long Itinerary for Similan Islands

If you’re planning to spend a week in Similan Islands, here’s a suggested itinerary:

  • Day 1-2: Relax on the beaches of Koh Similan and explore the island’s hiking trails.
  • Day 3-4: Go island hopping and visit some of the nearby islands. Koh Ba Ngu and Koh Miang are two that you don’t want to miss.
  • Day 5-6: Get in the water and go snorkeling or diving. The coral reefs around Similan Islands are some of the best in the world.
  • Day 7: Spend your last day exploring the nearby town of Khao Lak.

Food and Drink Scene of Similan Islands

Must-Try Dishes

While Similan Islands isn’t known for its culinary scene, there are a few dishes that you must try:

  • Tom Yum Goong: A spicy and sour shrimp soup that is a staple of Thai cuisine.
  • Pad Thai: A stir-fried noodle dish that is a ubiquitous street food in Thailand.
  • Som Tam: A spicy salad made with shredded papaya, chili, and lime juice.

Local Beverages

While you’re in Similan Islands, make sure to indulge in some of the local drinks:

  • Singha Beer: One of Thailand’s most popular beers, Singha Beer is a refreshing drink perfect for the island’s tropical climate.
  • Thai Iced Tea: A sweet and creamy iced tea that is popular throughout Thailand.
  • Coconut Water: A refreshing drink that is rich in electrolytes and perfect for rehydrating after a day in the sun.

Where to Stay in Similan Islands: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Similan Islands has some great options for accommodation, ranging from budget-friendly hostels to luxury resorts. Here are some of the most popular places to stay:

  • Similian Seven Sea Club: A budget-friendly hostel that offers basic accommodation and easy access to the beach.
  • The Sarojin: A luxury resort that offers beachfront villas and a range of amenities, including a spa and swimming pool.
  • Baan Khao Lak Resort: An eco-friendly resort that offers a range of accommodations, from bungalows to luxury villas.

What to Buy in Similan Islands: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

If you’re looking for souvenirs to bring back from Similan Islands, here are some options:

  • Handmade Jewelry: Many of the island’s shops sell handmade jewelry featuring local gemstones.
  • Local Handicrafts: There are several local artisans on the island who create beautiful handmade crafts, including pottery and textiles.
  • Salted Fish: Similan Islands is known for its salted fish, which makes for a delicious and unique souvenir.

Local Culture and Customs of Similan Islands

The main cultural heritage of Similan Islands is that of the Chao Leh, the indigenous people who have lived on the islands for generations. Their traditional way of life revolves around fishing and a deep connection to the sea. Visitors are encouraged to respect their local customs and traditions.

Best Time to Visit Similan Islands

The best time to visit Similan Islands is during the dry season, which typically runs from December to April. During this period, the weather is sunny and dry, and the sea is calm. The rainy season, which runs from May to November, is not the best time to visit the island as the seas can be rough, making it difficult to explore fully. During the rainy season, several tour operators may suspend their services due to safety concerns.

Do’s and Don’ts of Similan Islands

Here are some do’s and don’ts to keep in mind when visiting Similan Islands:

Do:

  • Respect the local culture and customs of the Chao Leh.
  • Wear sunscreen and stay hydrated.
  • Book your trip in advance to ensure availability.

Don’t:

  • Litter on the islands or in the water.
  • Touch or damage the coral reefs.
  • Wear beachwear in public places outside of the beach.

Safety and Health

Safety in Similan Islands

Similan Islands are generally safe, although visitors should take precautions to avoid accidents or injuries. Here are some safety tips to keep in mind:

  • Always wear a life jacket when participating in any water-based activities.
  • Be aware of your surroundings, particularly when hiking or exploring the islands’ more remote areas.
  • Be mindful of strong currents and high surf conditions when swimming or snorkeling.

Health in Similan Islands

Similan Islands are a relatively safe destination in terms of health, and no particular health risks are associated with the island. However, visitors should still take precautions to avoid sickness or injury. Here are some health tips to keep in mind:

  • Always drink bottled water and avoid tap water.
  • Wear mosquito repellent to avoid insect bites.
  • Seek medical attention if you experience any symptoms of illness or discomfort.

Getting to Similan Islands: Transportation Options and Logistics

There are several ways to get to Similan Islands. Here are the most popular options:

Airports Near Similan Islands

The nearest airport to Similan Islands is the Phuket International Airport, which is approximately 93 kilometers (58 miles) from the island. From the airport, visitors can take a private taxi or shuttle bus to Khao Lak, from where you can catch a boat to the island.

Bus Stations Near Similan Islands

The closest bus station to Similan Islands is the Khao Lak Bus Station. From there, visitors can take a speedboat or ferry to the island.

Other Options

Visitors can also hire a private car or taxi from Phuket or Khao Lak to take them directly to the pier.

How to Get Around in Similan Islands

The most common way to get around Similan Islands is by boat or foot. Visitors can also rent a bike from some of the island’s guesthouses or resorts.

Where to Go Next from Similan Islands: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

If you’re looking to continue your adventure after leaving Similan Islands, here are some nearby destinations and excursions to consider:

  • Phuket: A bustling city located approximately 93 kilometers (58 miles) from Similan Islands.
  • Koh Phi Phi: An island located in the Andaman Sea, known for its stunning beaches and crystal-clear waters.
  • Khao Lak: A nearby town that serves as a hub for many visitors to Similan Islands.

Conclusion

Similan Islands is a hidden gem that offers visitors stunning natural beauty, world-class snorkeling and diving, and a chance to explore the local culture and traditions of the Chao Leh people. With this comprehensive guide, you’ll have all the information you need to make the most of your trip to this breathtaking destination.
