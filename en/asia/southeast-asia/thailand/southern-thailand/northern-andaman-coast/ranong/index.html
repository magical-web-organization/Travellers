Ranong, a small province tucked in the Northern Andaman Coast of Thailand, is a hidden gem that has yet to be discovered by many tourists. With its lush green forests, pristine beaches, and natural hot springs, Ranong offers visitors a chance to experience Thailand’s natural beauty away from the crowds and tourist traps of more popular destinations.

In this comprehensive travel guide to Ranong, we’ll take you on a journey through its culturally rich attractions, incredible outdoor adventures, delicious food and drink scenes, and much more. Let’s dive in.

Introduction to Ranong

Ranong is located in the southern part of Thailand’s Andaman coast, bordering Myanmar (Burma) to the north and the Andaman Sea to the west. The province is known for its lush tropical forests, rare wildlife, and hot springs. Although small in size, Ranong has something for everyone, from culture enthusiasts to adventure seekers.

List of Places to Visit in Ranong

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Ranong is home to several culturally rich attractions that represent the province’s history and heritage.

One such attraction is the Ranong Provincial Cultural Center, a museum that showcases the province’s culture and history. The museum exhibits an extensive collection of artifacts, including traditional clothing, musical instruments, and ancient tools used by the locals. The center is a must-visit for anyone looking to learn more about Ranong’s rich cultural heritage.

Another cultural attraction worth visiting is the Punyaban Waterfall, a sacred site for the local people who believe the waterfall is home to a powerful spirit. The waterfall’s crystal clear water, lush green forests, and natural pools provide a serene atmosphere for visitors to relax and appreciate the natural beauty of Ranong.

Natural Attractions of Ranong: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Ranong’s lush tropical forests, crystal clear rivers, and pristine beaches make it a paradise for outdoor enthusiasts. Visitors can engage in various outdoor activities, including hiking, biking, and water sports.

One of the most popular natural attractions in Ranong is the Raksawarin Hot Springs. The natural hot springs are located in the midst of a lush forest, providing visitors with an idyllic and picturesque setting to relax and soak in the therapeutic waters. The hot springs are said to offer many health benefits due to their high mineral content.

Another natural attraction worth visiting is the Koh Phayam Island, a remote and tranquil island located off the coast of Ranong. The island’s pristine beaches, crystal clear waters, and lush greenery offer a serene and relaxing atmosphere for visitors to unwind and recharge. Visitors can engage in various water sports, such as snorkeling and diving, or simply relax on the beach and soak up the sun.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Ranong

Ranong offers several scenic hiking and biking routes, providing visitors with breathtaking views of the province’s natural beauty. One such route is the Chumphon - Ranong Road, a winding road that runs through mountains, offering stunning views of the Andaman sea. Visitors can rent a bike and ride along the winding roads, taking in the scenery and fresh air.

Another popular hiking route is the Ngao Waterfall National Park, a park that features several waterfalls, natural pools, and hiking trails. Visitors can hike through the lush forest and enjoy the park’s stunning landscapes.

Best Things to Do in Ranong: Activities and Tours

Aside from the various natural attractions and cultural sites, Ranong offers visitors several activities to enjoy, such as cooking classes, hot springs tours, and river cruises.

One such activity is the Thai Cooking Class, where visitors can learn to cook traditional Thai dishes and sauces, including curry, pad Thai, and Tom Yum soup. The cooking class includes a visit to the local market to buy fresh and authentic ingredients that make the dishes even more flavorful.

Another popular activity is the hot springs tour, where visitors can explore Ranong’s natural hot springs, including Raksawarin, Ngam Kroh, and Ranong Onsen. Visitors can soak in the hot springs and enjoy their therapeutic benefits while taking in the scenic views.

Week-Long Itinerary for Ranong

A week-long itinerary for Ranong would include exploring the cultural attractions and engaging in outdoor activities such as hiking, biking, and water sports. Here’s a seven-day itinerary with some fun activities to make the most of your trip to Ranong:

  • Day 1: Visit the Ranong Provincial Cultural Center and Punyaban Waterfall.

  • Day 2: Explore the Raksawarin Hot Springs and hike through the lush forest.

  • Day 3: Take a boat trip to the Koh Phayam Island and spend the day relaxing on the pristine beaches.

  • Day 4: Rent a bike and ride along the winding roads of Chumphon - Ranong Road while enjoying the stunning views of the Andaman sea.

  • Day 5: Take a cooking class and learn to cook traditional Thai dishes.

  • Day 6: Take a hot springs tour and explore the other hot springs in the area.

  • Day 7: Visit Ngao Waterfall National Park and enjoy the scenic views and hiking trails.

Food and Drink Scene of Ranong

Ranong’s food and drink scene is a perfect reflection of the province’s rich culture and heritage. From local street foods to fine dining restaurants, Ranong has it all. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages that you don’t want to miss:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Khao Soi: A Northern Thai curry noodle soup that’s a must-try dish in Ranong.

  • Gaeng Taypo: A sour soup made with fish and vegetables, a popular dish in Ranong.

  • Hoi Chak Teen: A unique local seafood dish made with fresh blood cockles.

Local Beverages

  • Nam Manao: A refreshing lime juice drink perfect for cooling down on a hot day.

  • Nam Phrae Wan: A sweet drink made from fruit syrup and mixed with ice.

Where to Stay in Ranong: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Ranong offers many accommodation options for visitors, from luxury hotels to budget-friendly guesthouses and hostels. Here are some popular options:

  • Tinidee Hotel @ Ranong: A luxury hotel located in the heart of Ranong. The hotel offers modern amenities, an outdoor pool, and a spa for guests to enjoy.

  • The Hidden Resort and Restaurant: A serene and peaceful resort located in a tranquil forest. The resort offers comfortable rooms and a natural hot spring pool for guests to enjoy.

  • Ranong Garden Hotel: A budget-friendly hotel located in the heart of Ranong. The hotel offers clean and comfortable rooms at an affordable price.

What to Buy in Ranong: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Ranong offers visitors several unique souvenirs and local products to bring back home. Here are some popular items to buy:

  • Herbal ball: A traditional Thai massage ball made with natural herbs and spices.

  • Ranong Tea: A famous tea brand produced in Ranong, made with local tea leaves and herbs for a unique taste.

Local Culture and Customs of Ranong

Ranong’s local culture and customs are shaped by the province’s diverse history and heritage. The locals have a strong connection with nature and believe in the importance of preserving the environment.

Some local customs to keep in mind while visiting Ranong include dressing modestly while visiting temples and removing shoes before entering a home or temple.

Best Time to Visit Ranong

The best time to visit Ranong is between November and April when the weather is dry and mild. The temperatures during this time range from 25 to 32 degrees Celsius, making it perfect for outdoor activities and exploring the natural attractions.

Do’s and Don’ts of Ranong

Do:

  • Respect the local customs and traditions

  • Dress modestly while visiting temples

  • Try the local cuisine and beverages

Don’t:

  • Litter or damage the environment

  • Disrespect local traditions or beliefs

Safety and Health

Safety in Ranong

Ranong is generally safe, with low crime rates. However, visitors should take precautions and be aware of their surroundings, especially when visiting secluded areas.

Health in Ranong

As with any travel destination, visitors should take precautions to stay healthy while visiting Ranong. It’s recommended to bring insect repellent and to stay hydrated. Visitors should also avoid drinking tap water and consume only bottled or boiled water.

Getting to Ranong: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Ranong

The nearest airport to Ranong is Ranong Airport, which offers daily flights to Bangkok and Phuket.

Bus Stations Near Ranong

Ranong’s bus station serves as a transportation hub for several bus companies that provide service to other parts of Thailand.

Other Options

Visitors can also reach Ranong by private car or by hiring a taxi from nearby cities.

How to Get Around in Ranong

Getting around in Ranong is easy, with several transportation options available, including taxis, tuk-tuks, and motorbikes. Visitors can also rent a car or motorcycle to explore the province at their own pace.

Where to Go Next from Ranong: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Ranong’s location makes it easy to explore nearby destinations and excursions. Some popular nearby destinations include Koh Samui, Khaolak, and Chumphon.

Conclusion

We hope this comprehensive travel guide to Ranong has inspired you to plan your trip to this hidden gem of Southern Thailand. Ranong offers visitors a chance to explore the province’s rich cultural heritage and natural beauty while enjoying a relaxing and peaceful atmosphere. Whether you’re an adventure seeker or looking for a tranquil retreat, Ranong has something for everyone. Plan your trip today and experience the beauty of Ranong.
