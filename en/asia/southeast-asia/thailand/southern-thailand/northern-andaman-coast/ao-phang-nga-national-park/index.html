Ao Phang Nga National Park Travel Guide

Ao Phang Nga National Park is located in the Northern Andaman Coast of Southern Thailand. This beautiful park is famous for its towering limestone cliffs, pristine beaches, and lush mangroves. Visitors to the park can enjoy a variety of activities, from exploring caves to hiking through the jungle. In this comprehensive travel guide, we’ll take a closer look at everything you need to know to plan your trip to Ao Phang Nga National Park.

Introduction to Ao Phang Nga National Park

Ao Phang Nga National Park covers an area of 400 square kilometers and is located in Phang Nga Province. The park is home to a diverse range of flora and fauna, including monkeys, deer, and a variety of bird species. The park’s main attraction is the stunning limestone cliffs that rise up from the turquoise waters, forming a dramatic landscape that is unique to this region. Another highlight of the park is the dense mangrove forests that line the coast.

List of Places to Visit in Ao Phang Nga National Park

There are many amazing places to visit within Ao Phang Nga National Park. Here are a few must-see attractions:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Wat Tham Suwan Khuha Temple: This beautiful temple is located inside a cave and is home to a reclining Buddha statue.
  • Koh Panyee Floating Village: This fishing village is built entirely on stilts and is a fascinating place to explore.
  • James Bond Island: This island is famous for its appearance in the James Bond film “The Man with the Golden Gun.”

Natural Attractions of Ao Phang Nga National Park: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Phang Nga Bay: This breathtakingly beautiful bay is dotted with islands and limestone cliffs rising up from the water.
  • Tham Lot Cave: This limestone cave is known for its stunning stalactites and stalagmites.
  • Ton Pariwat Wildlife Sanctuary: This sanctuary is home to a variety of wildlife, including gibbons and macaques.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Ao Phang Nga National Park

Ao Phang Nga National Park is a great place to explore on foot or by bike. There are several trails that offer beautiful views of the park’s natural beauty, including:

  • Khao Chang Viewpoint Trail: This trail offers stunning panoramic views of the park and the surrounding islands.
  • Ton Pariwat Waterfall Trail: This trail leads to a beautiful waterfall and offers the opportunity to spot wildlife along the way.

Best Things to Do in Ao Phang Nga National Park: Activities and Tours

There are many fun and exciting activities to do in Ao Phang Nga National Park. Here are a few of our favorites:

  • Kayaking: Paddle through the park’s mangroves and explore hidden lagoons.
  • Snorkeling: Discover the underwater beauty of Phang Nga Bay.
  • Boat Tours: Take a boat tour of the bay and explore its many hidden coves and beaches.

Week-Long Itinerary for Ao Phang Nga National Park

If you have a week to spend in Ao Phang Nga National Park, here’s a sample itinerary to help you plan your trip:

  • Day 1: Explore Koh Panyee Floating Village.
  • Day 2: Take a boat tour of Phang Nga Bay.
  • Day 3: Visit James Bond Island.
  • Day 4: Hike to Ton Pariwat Waterfall.
  • Day 5: Kayak through the park’s mangroves.
  • Day 6: Snorkel in Phang Nga Bay.
  • Day 7: Relax on one of the park’s beautiful beaches.

Food and Drink Scene of Ao Phang Nga National Park

No trip to Ao Phang Nga National Park is complete without trying some of the local cuisine. Here are a few must-try dishes:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Tom Yum Goong: A spicy and sour soup made with shrimp and lemongrass.
  • Pad Thai: Stir-fried noodles with vegetables, shrimp, and peanuts.
  • Massaman Curry: A mild curry made with beef or chicken, potatoes, and peanuts.

Local Beverages

  • Thai Tea: A sweet and creamy iced tea.
  • Chang Beer: A popular local beer.

Where to Stay in Ao Phang Nga National Park: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

There are many accommodation options in and around Ao Phang Nga National Park. Here are a few that we recommend:

  • Six Senses Yao Noi: A luxurious resort located on a private island.
  • Baan Suan Resort: A budget-friendly option that’s just a short walk from the beach.
  • Phang Nga Resort and Spa: A mid-range option that offers stunning views of the bay.

What to Buy in Ao Phang Nga National Park: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

There are many unique souvenirs and local products to bring home from Ao Phang Nga National Park. Here are a few of our favorites:

  • Batik Clothing: Colorful and intricately designed batik clothing is a popular local product.
  • Handmade Soap: Made with natural ingredients like coconut oil and lemongrass, these soaps make great gifts.
  • Woven Baskets: These beautifully woven baskets are made by local artisans and come in a variety of sizes and designs.

Local Culture and Customs of Ao Phang Nga National Park

The people of Phang Nga Province are friendly and welcoming to visitors. It’s important to be respectful of their culture and customs while visiting the park. Here are a few things to keep in mind:

  • Dress modestly when visiting temples or other cultural sites.
  • Remove your shoes before entering someone’s home or a temple.
  • Learn a few basic Thai phrases before your trip to help you communicate with locals.

Best Time to Visit Ao Phang Nga National Park

The best time to visit Ao Phang Nga National Park is between November and April when the seas are calm and the weather is dry and mild. The park can be visited year-round, but monsoon season (May to October) can bring heavy rainfall and rough seas.

Do’s and Don’ts of Ao Phang Nga National Park

Here are a few do’s and don’ts to keep in mind while visiting Ao Phang Nga National Park:

Do:

  • Respect the natural environment and wildlife.
  • Follow park rules and regulations.
  • Carry out all trash and leave nothing behind.

Don’t:

  • Disturb wildlife or remove any plants, rocks, or other natural objects.
  • Feed the animals.
  • Swim or sunbathe on coral reefs.

Safety and Health

While Ao Phang Nga National Park is generally a safe place to visit, it’s important to take a few precautions to ensure that you have a safe and healthy trip.

Safety in Ao Phang Nga National Park

  • Be aware of your surroundings and keep an eye on your belongings.
  • Only use licensed tour operators for activities like snorkeling or kayaking.

Health in Ao Phang Nga National Park

  • Drink only bottled or filtered water.
  • Wear insect repellent to prevent mosquito bites.
  • Be prepared for the heat and stay hydrated.

Getting to Ao Phang Nga National Park: Transportation Options and Logistics

There are several ways to get to Ao Phang Nga National Park. Here are a few options:

Airports Near Ao Phang Nga National Park

  • Phuket International Airport: Located about 75 kilometers from the park.

Bus Stations Near Ao Phang Nga National Park

  • Phang Nga Bus Terminal: Located in the town of Phang Nga, about 20 kilometers from the park.

Other Options

  • Private Car: You can hire a private car to take you directly to the park.
  • Ferry: You can take a ferry from Phuket to Koh Yao Noi, which is located near the park.

How to Get Around in Ao Phang Nga National Park

The best way to get around Ao Phang Nga National Park is by boat or on foot. There are several licensed tour operators who offer boat tours of the park, or you can rent a kayak and explore on your own. There are also several hiking trails within the park that offer stunning views.

Where to Go Next from Ao Phang Nga National Park: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

There are many other beautiful destinations in Southern Thailand that are worth visiting after your trip to Ao Phang Nga National Park. Here are a few ideas:

  • Krabi: This province is known for its stunning beaches and limestone cliffs.
  • Koh Lanta: This island is home to beautiful beaches and excellent diving spots.
  • Koh Phi Phi: This group of islands is famous for its crystal-clear waters and stunning scenery.

Conclusion

Ao Phang Nga National Park is a must-visit destination in Southern Thailand. With its stunning natural beauty, rich culture, and exciting activities, there’s something for everyone to enjoy. Follow this comprehensive travel guide to plan your trip to this magical place and experience all that it has to offer.
