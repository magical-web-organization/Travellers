Introduction to Ao Nang

Ao Nang is a bustling tourist destination located in Krabi Province, Southern Thailand. The town features a mix of natural beauty, outdoor adventures, cultural attractions, and a lively food and drink scene. Ao Nang is surrounded by towering limestone cliffs, pristine beaches, and crystal clear waters, making it an ideal spot for outdoor activities like rock climbing, kayaking, and snorkeling.

Most visitors use Ao Nang as a base to explore the surrounding islands and national parks, such as Koh Phi Phi, Railay Beach, and Noppharat Thara Beach. With its warm and welcoming atmosphere, laid-back vibe, and diverse offerings, Ao Nang has become a favorite destination for both backpackers and luxury travelers alike.

List of Places to Visit in Ao Nang

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Ao Nang and the surrounding area have several culturally significant attractions that travelers can visit, including the following:

  • Wat Tham Sua: is a beautiful temple located in the heart of Krabi Province. The temple features a complex of shrines, cave networks, and meditation spaces, along with stunning views of the surrounding valley.

  • Khao Khanap Nam: Two towering limestone cliffs that have become the symbol of Krabi Town. Visitors can take a long-tail boat from Krabi pier to visit this small shrine inside the caves under the cliffs.

  • Krabi Contemporary Art Museum: This museum showcases contemporary works of art from local and international artists. The museum exhibition also features interactive installations, sculptures, and paintings.

Natural Attractions of Ao Nang: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Ao Nang is an ideal destination for outdoor enthusiasts looking to explore the surrounding area’s natural beauty. Here are some of the must-visit places in Ao Nang:

  • Noppharat Thara Beach: is a beautiful, long beach that is ideal for swimming and sunbathing. It is also a popular spot for kiteboarding, windsurfing, and snorkeling.

  • Railay Beach: is a famous beach accessible only by boat from Ao Nang or Krabi. The area is known for its white sandy beaches, turquoise waters, and towering limestone cliffs. Railay Beach is a popular destination for rock climbing, kayaking, and cliff jumping.

  • Phi Phi Islands: are two stunning islands located between Krabi and Phuket. The islands are known for their crystal clear waters, colorful coral reefs, and breathtaking views. Visitors can enjoy a variety of water activities like snorkeling, scuba diving, and island hopping.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Ao Nang

Ao Nang and the surrounding area have several hiking and biking trails that provide stunning views of the natural beauty of the region. It is a paradise for those who wish to spend a day in nature while exploring its iconic landscapes. Here are a few of the popular routes:

  • Tab Kak Hang Nak Nature Trail: is a scenic hiking trail that offers stunning views of Krabi’s coastline. The hike offers views of cliffs and limestone formations. The trail is 3.7 km long and is considered moderately challenging.

  • Ao Nang-Klong Muang Cycling Route: is a 20 km route that is popular among cycling enthusiasts. The route passes through small towns, paddy fields, and scenic coastal views.

Best Things to Do in Ao Nang: Activities and Tours

Here are some of the best things to do and most popular tours in Ao Nang:

  • Island hopping: There are countless islands around Ao Nang to explore, and most of them are uninhabited. Take a long-tail boat, speedboat, or day trip to explore them and its stunning beaches.

  • Rock Climbing: Krabi’s towering limestone cliffs have made it one of the world’s premier rock climbing destinations. Railay Beach and Ton Sai Beach are popular spots for rock climbing.

  • Scuba Diving and Snorkeling: Take a day trip to Koh Phi Phi to explore the underwater world of Krabi. The islands are known for their crystal clear waters and colorful coral reefs.

Week-Long Itinerary for Ao Nang

Here is an itinerary for a week-long visit to Ao Nang:

Day Activity
1 Visit the Tiger Cave temple and take a sunset cruise on a long-tail boat
2 Spend the day exploring Railay Beach and trying out rock climbing

Food and Drink Scene of Ao Nang

Must-Try Dishes

Thai cuisine is one of the world’s most popular for a reason. Here are some dishes that you must try when in Ao Nang:

  • Green Curry: Made with coconut milk, green curry paste, chicken, and vegetables, green curry is a delicious and rich Thai specialty.

  • Som Tam (Green Papaya Salad): A sweet and spicy salad made with thinly sliced papayas, dried shrimp, peanuts, and lime juice.

  • Tom Yum Goong (Spicy Shrimp Soup): A spicy soup with prawns, mushrooms, and lemongrass.

Local Beverages

Here are some local drinks to try while in Ao Nang:

  • Thai Iced Tea: A sweet and creamy tea made with condensed milk, sugar, and black tea.

  • Singha Beer: A local beer that goes with almost everything.

  • Lemongrass Juice: A refreshing and healthy drink made with fresh lemongrass, lime juice, and sugar.

Where to Stay in Ao Nang: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Ao Nang offers a wide range of accommodation options to suit any budget. Here are a few popular options:

  • Rayavadee Resort: An award-winning luxury resort located on Railay Beach.

  • Centara Grand Beach Resort & Villas Krabi: A 5-star resort on the shores of Pai Plong Bay.

  • Ao Nang Cliff Beach Resort: A mid-range hotel on the beach with stunning views of the Andaman Sea.

  • Pak Up Hostel: A budget-friendly hostel in the heart of Ao Nang.

What to Buy in Ao Nang: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

If you are looking for some local souvenirs and handicrafts to bring back home, here’s what to look out for:

  • Thai Silk Scarves: Colorful, soft, and luxurious. Thai silk scarves come in various stunning patterns and styles.

  • Coconut Oil: Pure coconut oil is known for its moisturizing properties and is a staple in Thai cuisine.

  • Handmade Jewelry: Ao Nang is known for its intricate handmade jewelry made from brass, silver, gold, and semi-precious gems.

Local Culture and Customs of Ao Nang

Thailand has a unique culture that is rich in tradition and history. Here are a few things to keep in mind while in Ao Nang:

  • Wai: This is the traditional greeting in Thailand. It is a slight bow with hands clasped as if in prayer.

  • Respect: Respect for elders is an essential part of Thai culture. As a visitor, it is essential to show respect and courtesy to locals.

  • Dress Code: When visiting temples or attending a formal event, it is appropriate to dress modestly. Avoid shorts, tank tops, or revealing clothing.

Best Time to Visit Ao Nang

The best time to visit Ao Nang is between November and April when the weather is dry and less humid. However, this is the peak tourist season, and prices can be high. May to October is also a good time to visit Ao Nang, although there might be intermittent monsoon rains.

Do’s and Don’ts of Ao Nang

Here are some do’s and don’ts to keep in mind while in Ao Nang:

Do:

  • Try the local food and drinks.

  • Respect local customs and traditions.

  • Carry cash, as some small shops and vendors do not accept cards.

Don’t:

  • Drink tap water. Stick to bottled water.

  • Touch a Buddhist monk without their permission.

  • Disrespect or speak ill of the King and the Royal family.

Safety and Health

Safety in Ao Nang

Ao Nang is a safe destination for travelers. Most visitors have a safe and enjoyable trip. However, petty crime is common in highly populated tourist areas like Ao Nang, so it is essential to be aware of your surroundings, avoid carrying valuables, and be cautious when traveling at night.

Health in Ao Nang

Ao Nang and the surrounding areas have several health clinics and pharmacies. Travelers are advised to come with their health insurance and visit a doctor in case of emergencies. Tap water is not safe for drinking, so visitors should stick to bottled water.

Getting to Ao Nang: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Ao Nang

Krabi International Airport serves Ao Nang and the surrounding areas. Direct flights to Krabi can be found from several cities in Southeast Asia and Europe.

Bus Stations Near Ao Nang

Krabi Bus Terminal is located about 30 minutes from Ao Nang. Buses run regularly to and from Bangkok, Phuket, and other cities in Thailand.

Other Options

Taxis, shuttle buses, and minivans are available for transportation between Krabi and Ao Nang.

How to Get Around in Ao Nang

Ao Nang is relatively small and is walkable. There are also several transportation options, including:

  • Tuk-tuks: A three-wheeled vehicle that is the most common mode of transport in Ao Nang.

  • Motorbike taxis: Ideal for solo travelers or couples, motorbike taxis offer door-to-door service and are faster than tuk-tuks.

  • Songthaews: These are converted pick-up trucks with benches in the back. They are cheap and ideal for larger groups.

Where to Go Next from Ao Nang: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Here are a few nearby destinations and excursions to explore after your visit to Ao Nang:

  • Koh Phi Phi Islands: Take a boat to these iconic islands for its stunning beaches and turquoise waters.

  • Phuket: Thailand’s largest island is located 3-hours drive from Ao Nang and offers a diverse range of attractions and nightlife.

  • Koh Lanta: A peaceful and laid-back island well known for its secluded beaches.

Conclusion

Ao Nang is a perfect combination of natural beauty, adventure, culture, food, and welcoming locals. Follow this travel guide to enjoy a fun, safe, and unforgettable vacation in Krabi, Thailand.
