Introduction to Ko Poda

Ko Poda is a small island located in the picturesque Krabi Province of southern Thailand. Surrounded by crystal-clear waters and pristine beaches, the island offers stunning views of limestone cliffs and lush jungles. Whether you’re looking to relax on the beach, explore the island’s natural wonders or experience Thai culture, Ko Poda is a must-visit destination.

List of Places to Visit in Ko Poda

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Krabi Town

Krabi Town is located on the mainland and it’s the perfect place to learn more about Thai culture and traditions. You can visit the Khao Khanap Nam Caves, Wat Kaew Temple, and the Krabi Contemporary Art Museum.

Tiger Cave Temple

Also known as Wat Tham Sua, the Tiger Cave Temple is a Buddhist temple located in Krabi. The temple’s main attraction is the giant golden Buddha statue and the stunning views of the surrounding landscape from the top.

Natural Attractions of Ko Poda: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Railay Beach

Railay Beach is a paradise for adventure lovers. You can go rock climbing, kayaking, and snorkelling, or you can simply relax on the beach and soak up the sun.

Princess Lagoon

Princess Lagoon is a hidden gem on Ko Poda island. You have to hike through the jungle to reach it, but the reward is a stunning emerald lagoon surrounded by limestone cliffs.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Ko Poda

Phra Nang Cave Hike

This easy hike takes you to the beautiful Phra Nang Cave, located on the Railay Peninsula. The cave is known for its unique stalactites and stalagmites and its stunning beach.

Cycling around Ko Poda Island

Cycling is a great way to explore Ko Poda’s rugged terrain. The loop around the island is around 10 kilometres long and takes you through coconut groves, mangroves, and small fishing villages.

Best Things to Do in Ko Poda: Activities and Tours

Island Hopping

Ko Poda is part of a group of islands known as the Four Islands. You can take a boat tour of the islands and explore their unique landscapes and beaches.

Snorkelling and Diving

The waters surrounding Ko Poda are teeming with colourful marine life. You can go snorkelling or diving and explore the coral reefs and the different species of fish.

Week-Long Itinerary for Ko Poda

Food and Drink Scene of Ko Poda

Must-Try Dishes

Tom Yum Goong

Tom Yum Goong is a spicy and sour soup made with shrimp, lemongrass, and other herbs. It’s a staple of Thai cuisine.

Pad Thai

Pad Thai is a stir-fried noodle dish that’s a favourite among locals and tourists alike. It’s made with rice noodles, vegetables, and peanuts.

Local Beverages

Singha Beer

Singha Beer is a Thai beer that’s popular all over the country. It’s a refreshing drink to have on a hot day at the beach.

Thai Iced Tea

Thai Iced Tea is a sweet and creamy tea made with condensed milk, sugar, and brewed tea. It’s a perfect drink to cool down after a spicy meal.

Where to Stay in Ko Poda: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Bamboo Heaven

Bamboo Heaven is a budget-friendly guesthouse located on the beachfront. The rooms have sea views, and the staff is friendly and helpful.

Aonang Cliff Beach Resort

Aonang Cliff Beach Resort is a luxury hotel located on a cliff overlooking the Andaman Sea. The hotel has a pool, a spa, and a restaurant that serves delicious Thai cuisine.

What to Buy in Ko Poda: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Handmade Soap

Many shops on Ko Poda sell handmade soap made from local ingredients like coconut oil and aloe vera.

Thailand Silks

Thai silk is a well-known traditional Thai fabric. You can find beautiful silk scarves, dresses, and shirts in many markets and shops on Ko Poda.

Local Culture and Customs of Ko Poda

Buddhist Temples

Thailand is a predominantly Buddhist country, and there are many temples on Ko Poda. When visiting a temple, it’s important to dress modestly and remove your shoes before entering.

Wai

Wai is a traditional Thai greeting that involves putting your palms together in front of your chest and bowing your head. It’s a sign of respect and is used in many social situations.

Best Time to Visit Ko Poda

The best time to visit Ko Poda is during the dry season, which runs from November to April. The weather is warm and sunny, and the water is calm and clear.

Do’s and Don’ts of Ko Poda

Do:

  • Respect Thai culture and customs
  • Dress modestly when visiting temples
  • Try local food and drink

Don’t:

  • Disrespect Thai culture and customs
  • Leave your valuables unattended on the beach
  • Drink too much alcohol and cause a disturbance

Safety and Health

Safety in Ko Poda

Ko Poda is a safe destination for tourists, but it’s always a good idea to take precautions. Be aware of your surroundings, don’t leave your belongings unattended, and be careful when swimming in the sea.

Health in Ko Poda

Make sure you have travel insurance that covers medical emergencies before traveling to Ko Poda. It’s also a good idea to bring any necessary prescription medication with you.

Getting to Ko Poda: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Ko Poda

Krabi International Airport is the closest airport to Ko Poda, located approximately 40 kilometres away. You can take a taxi from the airport to the pier and then catch a ferry to Ko Poda.

Bus Stations Near Ko Poda

The main bus station in Krabi Town is the Krabi Bus Terminal. From there, you can take a songthaew (a shared taxi) to the pier and then catch a ferry to Ko Poda.

Other Options

You can also hire a private taxi from Krabi to take you directly to the pier and then catch a ferry to Ko Poda.

How to Get Around in Ko Poda

The best way to get around Ko Poda is on foot, by bicycle, or by long-tail boat. The island is small enough to explore on foot, and renting a bicycle or a long-tail boat is an affordable option.

Where to Go Next from Ko Poda: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Krabi Town

Krabi Town is a great destination to explore Thai culture and traditions. You can visit temples, museums, and art galleries, or simply wander around the town and soak up the atmosphere.

Phi Phi Islands

The Phi Phi Islands are a group of islands located in the Andaman Sea. The islands are famous for their stunning beaches and crystal-clear waters.

Conclusion

Ko Poda is a hidden gem that shouldn’t be missed on a trip to Thailand. Whether you’re looking for outdoor adventures, cultural experiences, or just a place to relax, Ko Poda has something for everyone. With its stunning natural beauty, friendly locals, and delicious cuisine, Ko Poda is a destination that will leave a lasting impression on you.
