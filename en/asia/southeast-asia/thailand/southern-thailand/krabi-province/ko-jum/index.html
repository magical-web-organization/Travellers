Introduction to Ko Jum

Ko Jum (also known as Koh Pu) is a small island located in Krabi Province, Southern Thailand. It is situated between Phi Phi Islands and Krabi town, and it’s an ideal spot for travelers looking for a quiet, peaceful, and serene place to unwind.

Ko Jum’s isolation and natural beauty make it an ideal destination for travelers looking for an off-the-beaten-track destination. The island is known for its pristine beaches, lush jungle, towering limestone cliffs, and friendly locals. It’s a perfect place to relax, soak up some sun, and experience the authentic Thai way of life.

List of Places to Visit in Ko Jum

Ko Jum is relatively small, and you can easily explore the island in a day or two. Here are some of the places you’ll want to visit:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Ko Jum doesn’t have many historical sites or museums, but you can still learn about the local culture by visiting the following:

  • Wat Ko Jum: This is the island’s main temple and a community center. Visitors are welcome to explore the temple grounds and take part in the various festivities throughout the year.

Natural Attractions of Ko Jum: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Ko Jum is all about natural beauty and outdoor adventures. Here are some of the island’s natural attractions:

  • Ko Jum Beach: This is the island’s main beach, and it’s perfect for swimming, sunbathing, and relaxing. The beach is long and wide, with powdery sand and crystal clear water.

  • Jungle Trekking: There are many hiking trails throughout the island that take you through the dense jungle, past waterfalls, and up to scenic viewpoints.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Ko Jum

If you’re interested in exploring the island on foot or by bike, here are some routes worth checking out:

  • Ao Si Beach Hike: This hike takes you along the rugged coastline and through the jungle to a secluded beach that’s only accessible by foot.

  • Village Bike Tour: Rent a bike and explore the island’s quaint villages, see the local way of life, and stop at some of the island’s beautiful beaches.

Best Things to Do in Ko Jum: Activities and Tours

If you’re looking for more than just sun, sand, and sea, here are some other activities to try:

  • Island Hopping: Take a day trip to neighboring islands, such as Phi Phi Islands, Koh Lanta, or James Bond Island.

  • Snorkeling and Diving: The waters around Ko Jum are home to a diverse array of marine life, including colorful tropical fish, rays, and sea turtles.

Week-Long Itinerary for Ko Jum

Here’s a sample itinerary for a week-long trip to Ko Jum:

Day Activity
1 Arrive in Krabi, transfer to Ko Jum
2 Relax on the beach, explore the island on foot or by bike
3 Take a day trip to nearby islands
4 Go snorkeling or diving
5 Hike to a scenic viewpoint, watch the sunset
6 Learn about local culture and traditions
7 Depart Ko Jum, transfer back to Krabi

Food and Drink Scene of Ko Jum

Ko Jum offers a selection of restaurants and bar options, ranging from street vendors to guesthouse restaurants and beachside bars.

Must-Try Dishes

Here are some traditional Thai dishes that are worth trying:

  • Som Tum (Papaya Salad): A spicy and refreshing salad made with grated papaya, lime, chili, garlic, and fish sauce.

  • Tom Yum Goong (Spicy Shrimp Soup): A flavorful soup made with shrimp, lemongrass, chili, and kaffir lime leaves.

Local Beverages

Here are some local drinks to try:

  • Chang Beer: A popular Thai beer that’s refreshing and light.

  • Singha Beer: Another popular Thai beer that’s slightly sweeter than Chang.

Where to Stay in Ko Jum: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Ko Jum offers a range of accommodation options to suit every budget, including guesthouses, hostels, and hotels.

Guesthouses

Here are some affordable options:

  • Wooden Bungalows at Ko Jum Beach Villas: These basic wooden bungalows are located right on the beach, offering stunning sea views and quick access to the water.

  • Ting Rai Bay Resort: This mid-range resort offers air-conditioned bungalows that are perfect for couples and families.

Hotels

For those looking for more luxurious options:

  • Andaman Beach Resort: This beachfront resort features private bungalows with air conditioning, hot water, and sea views.

  • Koh Jum Lodge: This eco-friendly resort is located in the heart of the jungle and offers private bungalows and a range of activities.

What to Buy in Ko Jum: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Here are some popular souvenirs and local products:

  • Handmade Jewelry: There are several local artisans that offer handmade jewelry made from shells, stones, and silver.

  • Traditional Thai Silk: Buy some beautiful silk scarves that are handwoven and dyed using natural dyes.

Local Culture and Customs of Ko Jum

Ko Jum has a rich cultural heritage, and visitors are advised to be respectful of local customs and traditions.

  • Dress Modestly: When visiting temples or other cultural sites, ensure you dress modestly covering your shoulders and knees.

Best Time to Visit Ko Jum

The best time to visit Ko Jum is during the dry season, which is from November to April. During this time, the weather is sunny and dry, making it perfect for outdoor activities. The monsoon season is from May to October, and the island can be prone to heavy rains and rough seas.

Do’s and Don’ts of Ko Jum

Do:

  • Respect the local culture and customs.

  • Take care of the natural environment and wildlife.

Don’t:

  • Sunbathe topless or nude on the beach.

  • Litter or leave trash on the beach or in the ocean.

Safety and Health

Safety in Ko Jum

Ko Jum is relatively safe, but it’s always recommended to take precautions when traveling:

  • Be aware of your surroundings and avoid walking alone at night.

  • Keep your valuables locked up in your hotel room safe.

Health in Ko Jum

Most of the Thailand is considered safe, but it is always advisable to take certain precautions:

  • Drink only bottled or boiled water.

  • Pack your sunscreen, especially during the dry season.

Getting to Ko Jum: Transportation Options and Logistics

Here are your options:

Airports Near Ko Jum

The nearest airport to Ko Jum is Krabi International Airport. From here, you can take a taxi or private minibus to Laem Kruat Pier, where you can take a ferry to Ko Jum.

Other Options

You can also take a bus from Bangkok to Krabi and then take a ferry to Ko Jum. Alternatively, you can hire a private driver or take a shared taxi from Krabi town.

How to Get Around in Ko Jum

Ko Jum is a relatively small island, and you can easily explore it on foot or by bike. You can rent bikes from guesthouses and hotels on the island. If you need to go to the other side of the island, you can hire a tuk-tuk.

Where to Go Next from Ko Jum: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Here are some nearby destinations you can visit:

  • Phi Phi Islands: A popular island chain that’s known for its stunning beaches, clear waters, and parties.

  • Koh Lanta: This island is known for its laid-back vibe, beautiful beaches, and diving spots.

Conclusion

Ko Jum has something for every traveler, whether you’re looking for a romantic beach getaway or an outdoor adventure. With stunning scenery, friendly locals, and authentic Thai culture, this little island is a must-visit destination for anyone exploring Krabi Province.
