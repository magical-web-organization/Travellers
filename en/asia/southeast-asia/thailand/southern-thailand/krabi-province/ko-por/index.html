Introduction to Ko Por

Ko Por is a hidden gem located in the Krabi Province of Southern Thailand. This small island is filled with natural beauty, stunning beaches, and cultural landmarks that make it a must-visit destination in Southeast Asia. With a slow-paced lifestyle, Ko Por is perfect for travelers looking to escape the hustle and bustle of city life.

List of Places to Visit in Ko Por

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Ko Por has a rich history that’s reflected in its many cultural landmarks. A visit to these museums and historical places will give you a glimpse into the unique culture of this island.

  • The Old Town of Ko Por: Explore the charming Old Town, which is filled with traditional Thai architecture, art galleries, and boutiques. It reflects the culture and heritage of the island.
  • Wat Koh Por: Visit the island’s largest temple, which is located in the heart of the Old Town. Its golden pagoda and ancient Buddha statue are a must-see for any visitor.
  • The Ko Por Museum: This museum displays a wide collection of artifacts and antiques retrieved from the island’s history, which provides a glimpse into life on the island throughout the centuries.

Natural Attractions of Ko Por: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Ko Por boasts some breathtaking natural attractions that should not be missed during your visit. Here are some of the best ones to explore:

  • Ao Klong Hin Beach: one of the most beautiful beaches located in Ko Por. This beach is surrounded by cliffs, limestone formations, and stunning turquoise water.
  • Goh Koh Cave: The cave is nested in the mangrove forest of the island, which offers unique rock formations and forests views. This is a great option for a half-day long adventure.
  • Ko Por Forest Park: For nature lovers, Ko Por forest park is a must-see destination. It’s a vast reserve of more than 200 hectares, which is covered by the tropical rainforest, rolling hills, and various kinds of fauna.
  • Ko Por Hot Spring: At the heart of Ko Por, there are natural hot springs that are renowned to have therapeutic and healing benefits. Don’t miss the opportunity to take a soak or even plan a day spa trip in this naturally beautiful location.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Ko Por

Ko Por is an ideal place to explore by foot or bike. Here are some great routes to consider:

  • The Old Town Walking Trail: stroll through the charming alleyways of the Old Town, stop by the local markets, and take in the breathtaking architecture and decoration of the buildings.
  • Ko Por Nature Trail: An easy and relaxing hike, which provides a different aspect of Ko Por’s natural beauty.
  • Cycling Tour Along the Coastal Route: This is a more challenging route but often cited as the “must-do” cycling experience in Ko Por, with great views of the island, Andaman Sea, and the many karsts jutting out of the water.

Best Things to Do in Ko Por: Activities and Tours

Ko Por has plenty of activities and tours that cater to different interests, from water sports to cultural experiences and everything in between.

  • Island Hopping Tours: Take a speedboat or day tour to neighboring islands such as Koh Phi Phi, Koh Lanta, or Koh Yao Noi, which offer beautiful and unique experiences.
  • Snorkeling and Diving: Ko Por has several glowing beaches and stunning coral reefs, providing some of Thailand’s best snorkeling and diving spots.
  • Cooking Classes: Discover Ko Por’s culinary culture through cooking classes offered by local restaurants and chefs.
  • Krabi National Park Sightseeing: This national park is a popular choice for all travelers to the region due to its scenic beauty, with endless amounts of trails, stunning waterfalls, and stunning landscapes.

Week-Long Itinerary for Ko Por

Here’s a sample itinerary for exploring Ko Por over a week:

Day Activities
Day 1 Explore Old Town and visit museums
Day 2 Visit Wat Koh Por Temple and Hot Springs
Day 3 Go Snorkeling or Scuba Diving at one of the nearby islands
Day 4 Go Cycling Along the Coastal route
Day 5 Take a nature walk through Ko Por Forest Park
Day 6 Visit Goh Koh Caves, or enjoy a day-long excursion to Krabi National Park
Day 7 Spend your day at Ao Klong Hin Beach before enjoying a sunset cruise on the way back

Food and Drink Scene of Ko Por

Must-Try Dishes

Several local foods are one-of-a-kind in Ko Por, and here’s our list of must-try dishes and culinary experiences:

  • Tom Yum Goong: a soup made from coconut milk, shrimp, mushrooms, galangal, lemongrass, and chili.
  • Massaman Curry: an aromatic and mild curry made with chicken or beef, potatoes, peanuts, and coconut milk.
  • Grilled Seafood: a variety of seafood available is grilled in a very unique way and served at many beachside restaurants.

Local Beverages

  • Coconut Milk: Whenever in Ko Por, Keep fresh young coconuts on the list.
  • Thai Drinks: Like many other places around Thailand, cool drinks can be found everywhere, so do not miss out on trying Sugar cane or lime or other smoothies.

Where to Stay in Ko Por: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Ko Por offers plenty of accommodation options, from luxurious to budget-friendly, including beach resorts, tents, guesthouses, and bungalows. Here are the best places to stay on the island:

  • Ko Por Sea Heaven Resort: A beach-side atmosphere, rooms feature amazing views.
  • Ko Por Bungalow: Found within the fisherman’s village, this offers a slice of traditional Thai culture.
  • Evergreen Resort: A bungalow style resort only 2.7 km from the Old Town and offering stunning island views.

What to Buy in Ko Por: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Ko Por offers plenty of souvenirs and local produces which provide a glimpse of their local culture and life. Here are some of the best items that you can consider to bring back home from Ko Por:

  • Handmade Souvenirs: A great souvenir reminiscent of this trip on the island is something handmade, from pottery to woven bags, or bracelets to wooden souvenirs.
  • Whiskey: Visitors looking for something a little stronger may be interested in the unique whiskey distilled on the island.
  • Local Honey: One of Ko Por’s most sought-after local goods is its blend of melon-flavored honey.

Local Culture and Customs of Ko Por

Thai culture has deep-rooted customs and traditions, which can be seen in the life on the island. From traditional dances to long-tail boats, Ko Por captures Thailand’s authentic charm and friendly vibe. Tourists visiting the island to respect their traditions and culture are always welcomed.

Best Time to Visit Ko Por

The peak season in Ko Por lies between November and April, with the season’s best weather being between November and February. The monsoon season from May to October can hurt your chances at outdoor activities, like island-hopping or exploring beaches.

Do’s and Don’ts of Ko Por

Do:

  • Respect local cultural traditions, such as covering shoulders and knees in temples and religious sites.
  • Eat the local cuisine!
  • Try your best to learn some phrases in Thai like “Hello” and “Thank You.”

Don’t:

  • Walk into somebody’s home without an invitation.
  • Avoid taking things with you from the beach, as it is considered illegal.

Safety and Health

Safety in Ko Por

Ko Por is generally a safe destination with low crime rates with friendly locals who are always happy to help. Visitors should follow the usual safety precautions like keeping their belongings safe and being aware of surroundings.

Health in Ko Por

Visitors should keep a few things in mind, like avoiding consuming tap water and bringing enough mosquito repellent. Several pharmacies and clinics are available on the island for minor health issues. In case of any emergency, one can consider getting in touch with the nearest tourism police.

Getting to Ko Por: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Ko Por

The nearest international airport is Krabi International Airport. Many airlines offer flights from various Asian countries. From the airport, visitors can make their way to Ko Por via a combination of buses and ferries.

Bus Stations Near Ko Por

The nearest bus stations to Ko Por are Ao Nang Bus Station and Krabi Bus Terminal. One can use these to travel to Ko Por in the most economical way possible.

Other Options

Ko Por is not accessible by road, so visitors can reach the island via boat services. One can opt for Ao Nang Pier or Thalane pier for a direct speedboat ride or catch a ferry from different island piers being Phuket, Lanta. All ferries generally leave at scheduled times, and the time duration ranges from 30 minutes to 2 hours.

How to Get Around in Ko Por

The best way to get around Ko Por is by walking, biking, or hiring a tuk-tuk (three-wheeler taxi). Bicycle or motorcycle rentals are available at affordable rates. Many of the island’s major attractions are within walking distance, so it’s the best way to explore the stunning scenery.

Where to Go Next from Ko Por: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Travelers who want to explore other areas can consider nearby destinations like Koh Lanta, Koh Phi Phi, Railay Beach, or island-hop around Krabi Province, all within a couple of hours by boat.

Conclusion

Ko Por is a hidden gem that offers the perfect combination of natural beauty, culture, and adventure activities. This small island is full of things to do and see, from stunning beaches and delicious cuisine to cultural landmarks and outdoor adventures. Hopefully you have learned enough about this beautiful island and its many unique offerings - it’s bound to make an unforgettable destination for your next holiday.
