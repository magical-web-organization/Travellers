Introduction to Laem Sing

Laem Sing is a hidden gem located in the north of Phuket, Southern Thailand. This quiet and secluded bay is known for its stunning scenery, turquoise waters, and white sandy beach that stretches for about 150 meters. Laem Sing is a perfect destination for those who seek privacy, relaxation, and outdoor activities.

List of Places to Visit in Laem Sing

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Wat Phra Nang Sang Temple: This ancient temple is located on the cliff overlooking the sea. It is one of the oldest religious sites in Phuket, with a history dating back to the 16th century. The temple is famous for its intricate architecture, stunning views, and peaceful surroundings.
  • Thalang National Museum: Located about 15 minutes from Laem Sing, this museum is a great place to learn about the history and culture of Phuket. It houses a collection of artifacts and exhibits highlighting the island’s past, including the Battle of Thalang, which took place in 1785.
  • Old Phuket Town: Although not exactly in Laem Sing, Old Phuket Town is worth a visit for those interested in architecture and history. It features a fusion of colonial, Thai, and Chinese architecture, with colorful shophouses, cafes, and boutiques.

Natural Attractions of Laem Sing: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Laem Sing Viewpoint: One of the main attractions in Laem Sing, this viewpoint offers a panoramic view of the bay, sea, and surrounding cliffs. It’s a great place to watch the sunset or take stunning pictures.
  • Snorkeling and Diving: The clear waters around Laem Sing make it an ideal spot for snorkeling and diving. You can explore the colorful coral reefs, tropical fish, and other marine life.
  • Trekking and Biking: There are several trekking and biking routes around Laem Sing, allowing you to explore the lush jungle and scenic coastline. Some popular routes include the Khao Phra Thaeo National Park and the Bang Pae Waterfall.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Laem Sing

Laem Sing and its surroundings offer some of the most scenic and adventurous hiking and biking routes in Phuket. These are some of the most popular trails in the area:

  • Khao Phra Thaeo National Park: This protected rainforest area covers about 23 square kilometers and features several hiking trails, waterfalls, and wildlife.
  • Bang Pae Waterfall: This 10-meter high waterfall is located in the Khao Phra Thaeo National Park and is a popular spot for picnics and swimming.
  • Laem Sai cycling route: This 25-kilometer cycling route starts and ends in Laem Sing and passes through some of the most scenic and unspoiled areas in Phuket. It’s relatively easy and suitable for all levels of fitness.

Best Things to Do in Laem Sing: Activities and Tours

  • Private Beach Picnic: Enjoy a private picnic on the beach, with a stunning view of the bay and sea. You can choose from a selection of Thai and international cuisine, such as fresh seafood, salads, fruits, and desserts.
  • Island Tours: Explore the nearby islands and beaches, such as James Bond Island, Phi Phi Islands, and Phang Nga Bay. You can opt for a private or group tour, with activities such as snorkeling, kayaking, and sightseeing.
  • Sailing and Yachting: Experience the ultimate luxury with a private yacht or sailboat. You can customize your itinerary and enjoy activities such as swimming, sunbathing, and dining on board.

Week-Long Itinerary for Laem Sing

Day 1: Arrival and check-in at your preferred accommodation Day 2: Visit Wat Phra Nang Sang Temple and Thalang National Museum Day 3: Trekking and biking in Khao Phra Thaeo National Park Day 4: Island hopping tour to Phi Phi Islands or James Bond Island Day 5: Private beach picnic and relaxation Day 6: Sailing and yachting trip in Phang Nga Bay Day 7: Departure

Food and Drink Scene of Laem Sing

Must-Try Dishes

  • Tom Yam Goong: This spicy and sour soup is a classic Thai dish, made with shrimp, lemongrass, galangal, lime, and chili. It’s perfect for a cold day or after a swim in the sea.
  • Massaman Curry: Another popular Thai dish, this curry is rich and mild, with a combination of potatoes, peanuts, chicken, or beef. It’s served with rice or noodles.
  • Mango Sticky Rice: A sweet dessert made with fresh mangoes, glutinous rice, and coconut milk. It’s a refreshing treat after a spicy meal.

Local Beverages

  • Thai Iced Tea: Also known as Cha Yen, this sweet and creamy drink is made with black tea, condensed milk, and ice. It’s a popular drink in Thailand, especially during hot days.
  • Singha Beer: One of the most famous Thai beers, Singha beer is light and refreshing, with a crisp taste and golden color.
  • Coconut Water: Fresh coconut water is widely available in Laem Sing, and it’s a great way to quench your thirst during hot and humid days.

Where to Stay in Laem Sing: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

There are several accommodation options in Laem Sing, ranging from budget-friendly hostels to luxury villas and resorts. These are some of the most popular options:

  • Le Meridien Phuket Beach Resort: This five-star resort is located on the beach and features several swimming pools, restaurants, and spa facilities.
  • Laem Sing Villa by Tropiclook: A private villa with a stunning view of the sea and private pool. It’s suitable for families and groups.
  • Laem Sing Inn: A budget-friendly guesthouse located near the beach and several restaurants and shops.

What to Buy in Laem Sing: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Thai Silk: Thailand is famous for its silk products, such as scarves, dresses, and bags. You can find several shops selling high-quality silk products in Phuket.
  • Handmade Soaps: Laem Sing is home to several local soap makers, who use natural ingredients such as coconut, honey, and herbs to create unique and fragrant soaps.
  • Pearls: Phuket is a hub for pearl jewelry, and you can find several shops selling high-quality pearls and pearl products. Some of the most popular items include necklaces, earrings, and bracelets.

Local Culture and Customs of Laem Sing

Thailand has several cultural and religious customs, and it’s important to show respect and observe them during your stay in Laem Sing. Here are some tips:

  • Dress modestly: When visiting temples and other religious sites, make sure to dress appropriately, covering your shoulders and knees.
  • Remove your shoes: When entering someone’s home or some temples and shops, you may need to remove your shoes as a sign of respect.
  • Wai: The wai is a traditional Thai greeting, involving a slight bow and hands pressed together as if in prayer. You can use it to say hello, thank you, or goodbye.

Best Time to Visit Laem Sing

The best time to visit Laem Sing is from November to February, which is the dry season in Phuket. During this time, the weather is pleasant and sunny, with mild temperatures and low humidity. The peak season is from December to January, so make sure to book your accommodation and activities in advance.

Do’s and Don’ts of Laem Sing

Do:

  • Respect the local culture and customs.
  • Try the local food and drinks.
  • Explore the surroundings and enjoy the natural beauty of Laem Sing.

Don’t:

  • Disrespect the Thai royal family.
  • Litter or pollute the sea and surroundings.
  • Haggle too much in shops and markets.

Safety and Health

Safety in Laem Sing

Laem Sing is generally a safe and peaceful destination, but it’s always wise to take precautions, such as:

  • Wear sunscreen and protect yourself from the sun.
  • Stay hydrated and drink plenty of water.
  • Be careful when swimming and snorkeling, and follow safety guidelines.

Health in Laem Sing

Phuket is a relatively safe destination in terms of health risks, but it’s recommended to take some precautions, such as:

  • Check if you need any vaccinations or medications before traveling.
  • Use insect repellent when necessary, especially during the rainy season.
  • Wash your hands frequently and avoid drinking tap water.

Getting to Laem Sing: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Laem Sing

The nearest airport to Laem Sing is Phuket International Airport, which is about 30 kilometers south of the bay. You can take a taxi or shuttle bus from the airport to Laem Sing, which usually takes around 45 minutes.

Bus Stations Near Laem Sing

Phuket Bus Terminal 2 and Bus Terminal 3 are the main bus stations in Phuket, serving both local and long-distance routes. From there, you can take a taxi or local bus to Laem Sing.

Other Options

There are several transportation options available in Phuket, such as:

  • Taxi: Taxis are widely available in Phuket, and you can hire one to travel to Laem Sing or other destinations.
  • Car Rental: You can rent a car or motorbike in Phuket and drive to Laem Sing. Make sure to have a valid driver’s license and insurance.
  • Private Transfer: Some hotels and tour operators offer private transfer services to Laem Sing.

How to Get Around in Laem Sing

Laem Sing is a relatively small and secluded bay, and the best way to get around is on foot or bicycle. You can rent a bicycle or scooter from one of the local shops to explore the surroundings.

Where to Go Next from Laem Sing: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Phuket Old Town: Explore the colorful shophouses, cafes, and boutiques of Old Phuket Town, located about 30 kilometers south of Laem Sing.
  • Patong Beach: One of the most famous beaches in Phuket, Patong Beach is known for its lively nightlife, shopping, and watersports. It’s located about 25 kilometers southwest of Laem Sing.
  • Ao Phang Nga National Park: Located about 70 kilometers northeast of Laem Sing, this national park features stunning limestone cliffs, hidden lagoons, and mangrove forests.

Conclusion

Laem Sing is a hidden gem in Phuket, offering a unique combination of natural beauty, cultural attractions, and outdoor activities. Whether you’re looking for a peaceful vacation or an adventurous trip, Laem Sing has something for everyone. Make sure to plan your trip in advance and immerse yourself in the local culture and customs.
