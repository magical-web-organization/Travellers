Introduction to Khao Phra Thaeo Non-Hunting Area

Khao Phra Thaeo Non-Hunting Area is a protected national park located in the north of Phuket Island in Southern Thailand. It is known for its untouched forest, rich biodiversity, and natural beauty. The national park is spread over an area of 22,295 hectares and has a wide range of flora and fauna.

The park also has a cultural significance as it is home to several Buddhist temples and ancient shrines. Khao Phra Thaeo Non-Hunting Area is an ideal place for nature lovers, hikers, bird watchers, and adventure enthusiasts.

Local Culture and Customs of Khao Phra Thaeo Non-Hunting Area

The local culture and customs of Khao Phra Thaeo Non-Hunting Area are based primarily on Buddhism. Visitors are expected to dress modestly while in Buddhist temples and shrines. People in Phuket are generally friendly and hospitable.

Best Time to Visit Khao Phra Thaeo Non-Hunting Area

The best time to visit Khao Phra Thaeo Non-Hunting Area is from December to February as the weather is dry and comfortable for outdoor activities. March to May is also a good option as the weather is still dry, although it can get quite hot.

Safety and Health

Khao Phra Thaeo Non-Hunting Area is generally safe, but visitors should still be aware of their surroundings and take precautions against theft and pickpocketing. Additionally, visitors need to take precautions while exploring nature trails and hiking to avoid injuries.

Health in Khao Phra Thaeo Non-Hunting Area

Visitors to Khao Phra Thaeo Non-Hunting Area should drink only bottled water and take precautions against insect bites to avoid contracting illnesses like dengue fever or malaria.
