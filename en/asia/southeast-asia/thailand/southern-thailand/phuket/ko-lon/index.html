Introduction to Ko Lon

Ko Lon is a small island located in the southern region of Thailand and belongs to the province of Phuket. Spanning about 3km in length and 1km in width, the island is a tropical paradise that offers a peaceful getaway from the hustle and bustle of mainland Thailand. Known for its pristine beaches, rich history, and vibrant culture, Ko Lon is a must-visit destination for travelers looking for an authentic Thai experience.

List of Places to Visit in Ko Lon

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

If you’re interested in learning about the history and culture of Ko Lon, visit the following museums and historical places:

  • Ko Lon Old Town: Ko Lon Old Town boasts well-preserved Sino-Portuguese architecture that offers an authentic representation of the town’s history. Visitors can see the old shophouses, shrines, and temples that have been standing since the late 19th century.
  • Gengyuan Chinese Shrine: One of the oldest shrines in Ko Lon. Its intricate artwork and elaborate roof demonstrate a unique Chinese-Thai blend of cultures and traditions.
  • Wat Koh Kaew: A beautiful Buddhist temple with a vibrant, red and gold exterior. The temple is known for its iconic, golden Chedi, or spire.

Natural Attractions of Ko Lon: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Ko Lon has many outdoor activities that showcase its natural beauty. Here are some must-see locations:

  • Khao Khat Viewpoint: This scenic viewpoint in the island’s southern region allows visitors to enjoy panoramic views of Ko Lon’s coastlines and surrounding islands.
  • Laem Nga Beach: A secluded beach that’s perfect for relaxing or enjoying a picnic with family and friends. Visitors can also rent a kayak or go fishing in the nearby waters.
  • Ko Lon Conservation Centre: This conservation centre is dedicated to protecting the island’s wildlife, including sea turtles, monkeys, and various species of birds and reptiles. Visitors can participate in educational programs and tours.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Ko Lon

Ko Lon’s hilly terrain offers excellent opportunities for hikers and bikers alike. Here are some of the best trails:

  • Ko Lon National Park: Covering a significant portion of the island’s interior, the park is a perfect spot for hikers and nature lovers. Along the way, you’ll discover spectacular waterfalls and scenic views.
  • Ao Sen Beach: Located in the northern region of Ko Lon, the beach offers a stunning coastal trail that’s perfect for bikers.
  • Haad Lang Khae Beach: This quiet beach boasts a hidden trail that links you to tranquil nearby beaches. The trail offers a chance to spot monkeys, birds, and other wildlife.

Best Things to Do in Ko Lon: Activities and Tours

In addition to the hiking and biking options, there are plenty of activities and tours available on Ko Lon:

  • Snorkeling and Diving: There are many snorkeling and diving sites around the island, including Club Med Beach and Ao Sane Beach. Visitors can see coral, colorful fish, and even some marine animals such as sea turtles.
  • Thai Cooking Class: Learn how to make local Thai dishes from professional chefs who can teach you the secrets behind authentic Thai food.
  • Batik Painting Workshop: Ko Lon has a long history of batik painting, a traditional art form. Visitors can learn this process from skilled artisans on the island.

Week-Long Itinerary for Ko Lon

Day 1: Arrival and settle in at hotel or guesthouse Day 2: Visit Ko Lon Old Town and Gengyuan Chinese Shrine. In the afternoon, head to Ao Sen Beach for a coastal trail. Day 3: Go snorkelling or diving and have a picnic lunch on the beach. In the evening, take a cooking class and learn some traditional Thai recipes. Day 4: Visit Wat Koh Kaew and hike in Ko Lon National Park, and enjoy the beautiful waterfalls. Day 5: Rent a bike and ride to Haad Lang Khae Beach to spot some monkeys or enjoy the sunset with scenic views. Day 6: Batik painting workshop and enjoying a sunset boat tour. Day 7: Departure from Ko Lon.

Food and Drink Scene of Ko Lon

Must-Try Dishes

Ko Lon’s cuisine is a delicious mixture of Thai and Chinese influences. Some must-try dishes include:

  • Tom Yam Goong: A spicy and sour soup made with shrimp, lemongrass, and chili pepper.
  • Pad Thai: A stir-fried rice noodle dish with eggs, bean sprouts, and peanuts.
  • Green Curry: A classic Thai curry made with green chili peppers, coconut milk, and chicken or vegetables.

Local Beverages

  • Chang beer: A popular Thai beer brand.
  • Thai Iced Tea: A sweetened tea made with condensed milk and served over ice.
  • Sangsom: A rum-like drink that’s popular with locals.

Where to Stay in Ko Lon: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

There are many options for accommodation on Ko Lon, depending on your preference and budget. Here are some of our top picks:

  • Club Med Phuket: A luxury resort with all-inclusive packages, located on the northern tip of the island.
  • Laem Sai Beach Resort: A peaceful, secluded resort located on the eastern side of Ko Lon.
  • Baiyok Villa: A private villa that boasts panoramic sea views on the western side of Ko Lon.

What to Buy in Ko Lon: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Ko Lon is a great place to buy souvenirs that reflect the island’s unique culture, including:

  • Batik Products: The traditional art of batik painting is famous on the island, and there are many shops selling batik products such as clothes and bags.
  • Phuket Pearls: Ko Lon and its nearby islands are known for producing high-quality pearls. Visitors can purchase jewelry made with locally harvested pearls.
  • Thai Silk: Ko Lon has many shops selling Thai silk products such as scarves and clothing.

Local Culture and Customs of Ko Lon

The local culture of Ko Lon is predominantly Thai-Chinese, and visitors should be aware of some customs and traditions:

  • Dress Appropriately: When visiting temples or other cultural attractions, visitors should dress modestly. This means covering the shoulders and knees.
  • Remove Shoes: It’s customary to remove your shoes before entering someone’s home or a temple.
  • Respect the King: Thailand has a strict lèse-majesté law, which means that it’s illegal to speak ill of the royal family. Visitors should show respect towards any images or statues of the king and queen that they come across.

Best Time to Visit Ko Lon

The best time to visit Ko Lon is from November to February when the weather is cooler and less humid. Avoid traveling to the island during the monsoon season, which occurs from May to October and can cause heavy rains and flooding.

Do’s and Don’ts of Ko Lon

Do:

  • Try local food and drinks
  • Learn some basic Thai words and phrases
  • Take your shoes off when entering temples or homes

Don’t:

  • Disrespect the royal family
  • Touch someone’s head or feet as it’s considered disrespectful
  • Haggle too much when shopping, it’s not customary in Thailand.

Safety and Health

Safety in Ko Lon

Ko Lon is generally a safe destination for tourists, but it’s important to exercise some basic precautions:

  • Stay aware of your surroundings and keep valuables secure
  • Keep an eye out for scams or pickpocketing schemes

Health in Ko Lon

There are no major health risks on Ko Lon, but it’s always a good idea to take some basic precautions:

  • Drink only bottled water or filtered water
  • Apply insect repellent to avoid mosquito-borne diseases such as dengue fever

Getting to Ko Lon: Transportation Options and Logistics

Ko Lon is accessible by boat from Phuket, which is about a 30-minute ride. Here are some transportation options to consider:

  • Ferry: Ferries run regularly throughout the day from Phuket to Ko Lon. Tickets can be purchased online or in person at the pier.
  • Private Boat: Visitors can rent a private boat, which is a convenient option if traveling with a large group.
  • Water Taxi: Water taxis are also available for hire for a more personalized experience. Visitors can negotiate prices with individual operators.

Airports Near Ko Lon

The closest airport to Ko Lon is Phuket International Airport, which is approximately 45 minutes away by car.

Bus Stations Near Ko Lon

The closest bus station to Ko Lon is Phuket Bus Terminal, which is about 30 minutes away by car.

Other Options

Some other options to consider include hiring a taxi or renting a car or motorbike on the island.

How to Get Around in Ko Lon

Ko Lon is a small island, and it’s easy to get around on foot or by bicycle. Visitors can also rent a motorbike or hire a taxi to explore the island.

Where to Go Next from Ko Lon: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Phuket is the most popular nearby destination, with plenty of flights from Phuket International airport leaving for both domestic and international locations. Other nearby destinations include:

  • Phi Phi Islands: Ko Phi Phi is a short ferry ride away from Ko Lon and is known for its stunning beaches and crystal-clear waters.
  • Krabi: Another popular destination nearby, Krabi is known for its stunning beaches, rock formations, and adventurous outdoor activities.

Conclusion

Ko Lon is a hidden gem that offers an authentic Thai experience. With its beautiful beaches, rich history, and vibrant culture, it’s a must-visit destination for travelers who want to experience the best of Thai island life. Follow our guide to discover the best places to visit, eat, and stay and make unforgettable memories on your trip to Ko Lon!
