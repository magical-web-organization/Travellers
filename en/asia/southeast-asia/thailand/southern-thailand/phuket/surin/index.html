Introduction to Surin

Surin, located in Southern Thailand, is a hidden gem of the country. With a rich historical and cultural heritage, breathtaking natural scenery, and a vibrant food and drink scene, Surin is a must-visit destination for anyone traveling to Thailand.

List of Places to Visit in Surin

There is no shortage of attractions for visitors to Surin. Whether you’re interested in history and culture or outdoor adventures, Surin has plenty to offer. Here are some of the top places to visit in Surin:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Surin National Museum: Learn about the history, culture, and traditions of the Surin province at this fascinating museum.
  • Prasat Muang Thi: This ancient Khmer temple dates back to the 11th century and is a must-visit for history buffs.
  • Wat Burapharam: One of the most important Buddhist temples in Surin, this impressive complex features beautiful architecture and fascinating artwork.

Natural Attractions of Surin: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Phu Khao Thong: This hill offers stunning panoramic views of the Surin province and is a popular spot for hiking and exploring.
  • Huay Luang Reservoir: Located about 30 km from Surin, this scenic reservoir is a great place for fishing, boating, and enjoying the outdoors.
  • Phanom Sawai Forest Park: This lush forest park is home to a variety of wildlife and is a popular spot for hiking and birdwatching.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Surin

For those who love to explore the outdoors, Surin has plenty of hiking and biking opportunities. Some popular routes include:

  • Phu Khao Thong Hike: This moderate hike offers breathtaking views of the Surin province.
  • Surin to Si Sa Ket Bike Route: This 90 km bike route takes you through beautiful countryside and past ancient temples.
  • Phu Rua Hike: This challenging hike takes you to the top of Phu Rua hill, offering stunning views of the surrounding landscape.

Best Things to Do in Surin: Activities and Tours

In addition to visiting historical and natural attractions, Surin offers a variety of activities and tours for visitors to enjoy. Some top options include:

  • Elephant shows and rides: Surin is famous for its elephants, and there are several opportunities to see them in action.
  • Thai cooking classes: Learn how to cook authentic Thai dishes and take home some new recipes.
  • Meditation and yoga classes: Surin’s peaceful surroundings make it a great place to unwind and focus on inner peace.

Week-Long Itinerary for Surin

For those planning a longer stay in Surin, here’s a sample week-long itinerary:

Day Activities
1 Visit Surin National Museum and explore the city’s markets and street food scene
2 Hike up Phu Khao Thong and visit Prasat Muang Thi
3 Take a day trip to Huay Luang Reservoir for fishing and boating
4 Visit Wat Burapharam and take an elephant ride
5 Take a bike ride to Si Sa Ket along ancient temple routes
6 Visit Phanom Sawai Forest Park for hiking and birdwatching
7 Relax with a yoga or meditation class, followed by a traditional Thai massage

Food and Drink Scene of Surin

No visit to Surin is complete without trying some of the delicious local cuisine. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Kai Yang: Grilled chicken seasoned with lemongrass and other spices
  • Som Tam: Spicy green papaya salad
  • Khao Niew: Sticky rice

Local Beverages

  • Thai Iced Tea: Sweet and creamy tea served over ice
  • Chang Beer: A popular Thai beer with a light and refreshing taste
  • Mekhong Whiskey: A local whiskey made from rice, wheat, and corn

Where to Stay in Surin: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Surin offers a variety of lodging options for visitors, ranging from budget-friendly hostels to luxurious hotels. Some popular choices include:

  • Thong Tarin Hotel: A beautiful hotel located near Surin National Museum
  • Baan Chang Ton: A cozy guesthouse located near Huay Luang Reservoir
  • Baan Kamtong Homestay: A traditional homestay experience in the heart of the city

What to Buy in Surin: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

If you’re looking for souvenirs or local products to bring home, Surin has plenty of options. Some popular choices include:

  • Surin Silk: Beautiful handcrafted silk products, including clothing and home decor
  • Elephant Statues: Surin is famous for its elephants, so why not bring a reminder of your visit home?
  • Spices and Sauces: Stock up on local spices and sauces to add some Thai flavor to your cooking back home.

Local Culture and Customs of Surin

Surin is a province with a rich cultural heritage, and visitors should be respectful of local customs. Some things to keep in mind include:

  • Dress modestly: When visiting temples or other cultural sites, dress modestly and remove your shoes before entering.
  • Show respect for the monarchy: The Thai monarchy is highly respected, and visitors should be mindful of this when in public.
  • Avoid touching people’s heads: In Thai culture, the head is considered sacred, and it is rude to touch someone’s head without permission.

Best Time to Visit Surin

The best time to visit Surin is between November and March, when the weather is dry and cool.

Do’s and Don’ts of Surin

Here are some do’s and don’ts to keep in mind when visiting Surin:

Do:

  • Try new foods: Surin is known for its unique cuisine, so be adventurous and try something new.
  • Be respectful: Show respect for local customs and traditions.
  • Hire a guide: Hiring a guide can help you navigate the city and learn about its culture and history.

Don’t:

  • Disrespect the monarchy: The Thai monarchy is highly respected, and visitors should be mindful of this when in public.
  • Litter: Keep Surin beautiful by disposing of your trash properly.
  • Disrespect the culture: Don’t make fun of or disrespect local customs and traditions.

Safety and Health

While Surin is a relatively safe destination compared to other parts of Thailand, it’s important to take some precautions to ensure your safety and health.

Safety in Surin

  • Beware of pickpockets: Keep an eye on your belongings, particularly in crowded areas.
  • Be cautious when riding motorcycles: If you’re renting a motorcycle to get around, make sure to wear a helmet and drive carefully.
  • Avoid drinking tap water: Stick to bottled water to avoid getting sick.

Health in Surin

  • Get vaccinated: You should make sure you are up to date on your routine vaccines before traveling to Surin.
  • Protect yourself from insects: Mosquitoes can carry diseases, so make sure to use insect repellent and wear long sleeves and pants.
  • Be careful with street food: While Surin’s street food is delicious, it’s important to be cautious to avoid food poisoning.

Getting to Surin: Transportation Options and Logistics

Surin is located about 450 km from Bangkok and can be reached by several transportation options.

Airports Near Surin

The closest airport to Surin is Ubon Ratchathani Airport, located about 120 km away.

Bus Stations Near Surin

Surin has a bus station with connections to several major cities in Thailand, including Bangkok and Chiang Mai.

Other Options

Some visitors opt to hire a private car or take a taxi from Bangkok to Surin.

How to Get Around in Surin

Surin is a relatively small city, and many of the attractions can be reached on foot. However, visitors can also rent bicycles or motorcycles to get around.

Where to Go Next from Surin: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

If you’re planning to explore more of Thailand after visiting Surin, here are some nearby destinations and excursions to consider:

  • Khao Yai National Park: Located about 250 km from Surin, this national park is home to a variety of wildlife and stunning natural scenery.
  • Siem Reap, Cambodia: Surin is just a few hours’ drive from the Cambodian border, making it a great jumping-off point for a trip to Siem Reap and the famous temples of Angkor Wat.
  • Khong Chiam: This scenic town located about 200 km from Surin offers beautiful views of the Mekong River and a laid-back, relaxed vibe.

Conclusion

Surin may be a lesser-known destination in Thailand, but it offers a wealth of attractions and experiences for visitors. Whether you’re interested in history and culture, outdoor adventures, or delicious local cuisine, Surin is sure to exceed your expectations. Plan your trip today and discover the magic of this hidden gem of Thailand.
