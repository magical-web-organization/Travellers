Introduction to Ko Hae

Ko Hae, also known as Coral Island, is a small island located off the southeastern coast of Phuket, Thailand. It is a popular destination for travelers looking for a relaxing beach vacation amidst crystal-clear waters and scenic landscapes. The island is easily accessible from Phuket and offers several attractions that cater to all types of travelers, from culturally significant museums to exhilarating outdoor adventures.

Whether you are looking for a romantic getaway, family vacation, or a solo adventure, Ko Hae is the perfect destination for you. Join us as we explore this hidden gem of Thailand, discover its natural wonders, and experience its vibrant culture.

List of Places to Visit in Ko Hae

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

1. Phuket Seashell Museum: The Phuket Seashell Museum is a unique museum that showcases an extensive collection of the rarest seashells from around the world. Visitors can discover the history and science behind these beautiful creatures and even buy some as souvenirs.

2. Chalong Temple: Chalong Temple is the largest and most visited Buddhist temple in Phuket. It is home to several pagodas, statues, and historical artifacts that represent the island’s rich culture and traditions. Visitors can learn about Buddhism, make offerings, and soak in the peaceful surroundings.

Natural Attractions of Ko Hae: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

1. Banana Beach: Banana Beach is a secluded and picturesque beach located on the southern end of Ko Hae. It is a popular spot for snorkeling, swimming, and relaxation. The crystal-clear waters and white sandy beach make it an ideal destination for beach lovers.

2. Coral Island Viewpoint: The Coral Island Viewpoint offers stunning panoramic views of the island and the Andaman Sea. It is a great spot for photographers and nature lovers to watch the sunrise or sunset.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Ko Hae

For the adventurous souls, Ko Hae offers several hiking/biking trails that take you through lush green tropical forests and offer breathtaking views of the island.

1. Coral Island Hike: The Coral Island Hike takes visitors through the island’s dense jungle, up to the viewpoint, and back down to the beach. The trail is easy to navigate and offers stunning views of the sea and surrounding islands.

Best Things to Do in Ko Hae: Activities and Tours

1. Snorkeling and Diving: The crystal-clear waters of Ko Hae are home to a wide variety of colorful marine life. Visitors can go snorkeling or diving to explore the coral reefs and spot exotic fish species.

2. Parasailing: For the thrill-seekers, parasailing is a popular activity in Ko Hae. Visitors can get on a parasail and soar high above the islands and admire the breathtaking views from above.

Food and Drink Scene of Ko Hae

Must-Try Dishes

1. Tom Yum Goong: Tom Yum Goong is a popular spicy and sour shrimp soup that is a staple in Thai cuisine. It is a must-try dish for visitors to Ko Hae.

2. Pad Thai: Pad Thai is a stir-fried noodle dish that is typically made with vegetables, meat or seafood, and topped with peanuts and lime.

Local Beverages

1. Thai Iced Tea: Thai Iced Tea is a sweet and creamy tea made from black tea, condensed milk, and sugar. It is the perfect beverage to cool off on a hot day.

2. Chang Beer: Chang Beer is a popular Thai beer that is a perfect accompaniment for spicy Thai dishes.

Where to Stay in Ko Hae: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Ko Hae offers a wide range of accommodation options to suit all budgets and preferences. Here are some of the most popular options:

1. Coral Island Resort: Coral Island Resort is a luxurious beachfront resort that offers spacious rooms, a swimming pool, and several dining options.

2. Coral Island Bungalows: Coral Island Bungalows offer a more affordable and rustic option for visitors. The bungalows are located in a peaceful setting and offer stunning sea views.

What to Buy in Ko Hae: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Ko Hae offers several unique and locally made souvenirs that visitors can take back home as a memento of their trip. Here are some ideas:

1. Seashell Jewelry: The Phuket Seashell Museum has an extensive collection of seashell jewelry that is both beautiful and unique.

2. Thai Spices: Visitors can buy a selection of aromatic Thai spices, such as curry powder, chili paste, and lemongrass, to take back home and add flavor to their dishes.

Local Culture and Customs of Ko Hae

Thai culture is steeped in traditions and customs that are different from Western cultures. Here are some tips to help visitors understand and appreciate Thai culture:

  1. Wearing conservative clothing, such as covering your knees and shoulders, is essential when visiting temples or religious sites.

  2. Be mindful of the Buddhist monks and avoid physical contact or engaging in conversations with them.

Best Time to Visit Ko Hae

The best time to visit Ko Hae is during the dry season, which runs from November to April. The weather is warm and pleasant with clear blue skies, making it the perfect time for outdoor activities and exploring the island.

Do’s and Don’ts of Ko Hae

Do:

  1. Respect locals’ customs and traditions.

  2. Wear appropriate clothing when visiting temples or religious sites.

Don’t:

  1. Touch or disturb marine life while snorkeling or diving.

  2. Disrespect or make fun of Thai culture or customs.

Safety and Health

Safety in Ko Hae

Ko Hae is generally a safe destination for travelers. However, visitors must take the necessary precautions while on the beach or engaging in water sports activities. In case of an emergency, visitors can contact the local authorities or their embassy.

Health in Ko Hae

Visitors to Ko Hae must take the necessary precautions to avoid illness or injury. Drink only bottled water, use sunscreen to protect against the sun’s harmful rays, and be wary of mosquito bites. In case of a medical emergency, visitors can seek medical attention at the local hospital or clinic.

Getting to Ko Hae: Transportation Options and Logistics

Ko Hae is easily accessible from Phuket Island. Visitors can take a speedboat or ferry from Rawai Beach or Phuket Town to reach the island.

Airports Near Ko Hae

Phuket International Airport is the nearest airport to Ko Hae. It is located about 47 km from Rawai Beach, which is the departure point for speedboats and ferries to the island.

Bus Stations Near Ko Hae

There are no bus stations near Ko Hae. Visitors can take a taxi from Phuket Town to Rawai Beach, where they can catch a speedboat or ferry to the island.

Other Options

Visitors can also rent a private boat charter or join a group tour to reach Ko Hae.

How to Get Around in Ko Hae

The best way to get around in Ko Hae is on foot. The island is small and easy to navigate on foot or bicycle. Visitors can also rent a scooter or hire a taxi to explore the island.

Where to Go Next from Ko Hae: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

For those looking to explore further, Ko Hae is a great starting point for several excursions and nearby destinations. Here are some options:

1. Phuket Old Town: Phuket Old Town is a historic district that offers a glimpse into the island’s rich culture and traditions. Visitors can explore the colorful streets, visit the Sino-Portuguese buildings, and sample local cuisine.

2. Phi Phi Islands: The Phi Phi Islands are located approximately 40 km east of Phuket and are a popular destination for day trips or overnight stays. Visitors can explore its stunning beaches, snorkel or dive in its crystal-clear waters, and hike to its viewpoints.

Conclusion

Ko Hae is a hidden gem of Thailand that offers a picturesque and relaxing destination to visitors. With several attractions, outdoor adventures, and cultural experiences, Ko Hae caters to all types of travelers. So, whether you are looking for a romantic getaway, family vacation, or a solo adventure, Ko Hae is the perfect destination for you!
