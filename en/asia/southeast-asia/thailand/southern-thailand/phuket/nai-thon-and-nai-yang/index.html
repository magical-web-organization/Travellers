Introduction to Nai Thon and Nai Yang

Located on the northwest coast of Phuket, Nai Thon and Nai Yang are two beautiful and peaceful beaches that are perfect for those looking for a quieter and less crowded vacation spot. The area, which was once a bustling turtle egg-laying site, is now a protected national park and boasts crystal-clear waters and stunning views.

With easy access to other popular Phuket attractions, Nai Thon and Nai Yang offer amazing natural beauty, outdoor adventures, cultural experiences, and delicious cuisine. In this guide, we will explore the best places to visit, things to do, where to eat, and where to stay in Nai Thon and Nai Yang.

List of Places to Visit in Nai Thon and Nai Yang

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Wat Nai Thon: A beautiful and historic Buddhist temple located in the heart of Nai Thon. The temple features a stunning white building with intricate carvings and gold accents, and visitors can learn about Thai culture and religion while admiring the architecture.

  • Turtle Village: A unique shopping center located in Nai Yang that offers visitors everything from trendy boutiques and restaurants to spa treatments and a local market.

Natural Attractions of Nai Thon and Nai Yang: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Sirinat National Park: A protected area that encompasses Nai Thon and Nai Yang beaches, as well as several other pristine beaches and forests. Visitors can enjoy hiking, birdwatching, and soaking up the natural beauty of the park.

  • Nai Yang Beach: A beautiful and secluded beach that is perfect for swimming, hiking, and sunbathing. The beach is also known for its incredible sunset views.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Nai Thon and Nai Yang

  • Khao Phra Thaeo National Park: A stunning natural area that is home to several waterfalls, hiking trails, and lush forests. Visitors can explore the park on foot or by bike and enjoy the breathtaking views.

Best Things to Do in Nai Thon and Nai Yang: Activities and Tours

  • Scuba Diving and Snorkeling: The waters around Nai Thon and Nai Yang are home to a diverse array of marine life, and several scuba diving and snorkeling tours are available for visitors who want to explore the reefs and see the fish up close.

  • Thai Cooking Classes: Learn how to cook traditional Thai dishes with local and experienced chefs, and bring the tastes of Thailand home with you.

Week-Long Itinerary for Nai Thon and Nai Yang

Food and Drink Scene of Nai Thon and Nai Yang

Must-Try Dishes

  • Tom Yum Goong: This famous and aromatic Thai soup is a must-try dish for anyone visiting Thailand. Made with shrimp, lemongrass, and chili, this soup is both spicy and refreshing.

  • Som Tam: A traditional Thai salad that is made with green papaya, chili, and peanuts. This dish offers a perfect balance of sweet, sour, and spicy flavors.

Local Beverages

  • Thai Iced Tea: A refreshing and sweet tea made with black tea and condensed milk, often served over crushed ice.

  • Chang Beer: A popular Thai beer that is brewed to be extra strong and refreshing.

Where to Stay in Nai Thon and Nai Yang: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Andaman White Beach Resort: A luxurious beachfront resort that offers guests stunning views, comfortable accommodations, and easy access to nearby attractions.

  • The Slate Hotel: A unique and stylish hotel that is inspired by Phuket’s tin mining history and features beautiful rooms and amazing amenities.

What to Buy in Nai Thon and Nai Yang: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Thai Silk Scarves: A beautiful and traditional Thai souvenir that is perfect for anyone looking to bring home a piece of Thai culture.

  • Handmade Soaps and Lotions: Several local shops in Nai Yang offer handmade soaps and lotions made with natural and aromatic ingredients.

Local Culture and Customs of Nai Thon and Nai Yang

Visitors to Nai Thon and Nai Yang should make an effort to respect local customs and traditions. It is also important to dress modestly when visiting temples or other cultural attractions.

Best Time to Visit Nai Thon and Nai Yang

November through February is the best time to visit Nai Thon and Nai Yang, as the weather is cool and dry. Visitors should avoid the rainy season from May to October.

Do’s and Don’ts of Nai Thon and Nai Yang

Do:

  • Respect local customs and traditions.

  • Try the local cuisine and drinks.

Don’t:

  • Wear revealing clothing, especially when visiting temples or other cultural attractions.

  • Engage in disrespectful or harmful behavior towards locals or wildlife.

Safety and Health

Safety in Nai Thon and Nai Yang

Nai Thon and Nai Yang are generally safe for visitors, but it is still important to take basic safety precautions, such as not leaving valuables unattended on the beach. Visitors should also be aware of their surroundings and avoid walking alone at night.

Health in Nai Thon and Nai Yang

Visitors to Nai Thon and Nai Yang should be up to date on routine vaccinations and consider getting vaccinated for Hepatitis A and typhoid. Mosquito-borne illnesses such as dengue fever and Zika are present in Thailand, so visitors should take precautions to avoid mosquito bites.

Getting to Nai Thon and Nai Yang: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Nai Thon and Nai Yang

The closest airport to Nai Thon and Nai Yang is Phuket International Airport, which is approximately 15-20 minutes away by car or taxi.

Bus Stations Near Nai Thon and Nai Yang

Several bus stations are located throughout Phuket, with the nearest one to Nai Thon and Nai Yang being the Phuket Bus Terminal 2.

Other Options

Taxis, tuk-tuks, and motorbike rentals are all available in Phuket and are convenient options for getting around Nai Thon and Nai Yang.

How to Get Around in Nai Thon and Nai Yang

Walking and biking are the best ways to get around Nai Thon and Nai Yang. Taxis and tuk-tuks are available, but they can be expensive. Motorbikes can also be rented, but visitors should be aware of the risks associated with driving a motorbike in Thailand.

Where to Go Next from Nai Thon and Nai Yang: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Patong: A popular beach town known for its nightlife, shopping, and entertainment options.

  • Khao Lak: A beautiful and peaceful area located north of Phuket that is home to stunning beaches and national parks.

Conclusion

Nai Thon and Nai Yang are two hidden gems in Phuket that offer visitors a chance to relax and enjoy Thailand’s natural beauty. From stunning beaches and national parks to cultural attractions and delicious cuisine, the area has something for everyone. So pack your bags and head to Nai Thon and Nai Yang for an unforgettable vacation experience!
