Introduction to Haad Son

Welcome to Haad Son, a peaceful and secluded bay nestled on the western coast of Ko Pha-ngan island, Thailand. With its pristine white sand beach, crystal-clear waters, and lush tropical vegetation, Haad Son offers visitors the perfect retreat from the hustle and bustle of city life. This guide will provide you with everything you need to know about this hidden gem and make the most out of your visit.

Local Culture and Customs of Haad Son

  • Dress modestly when visiting religious sites
  • Remove your shoes before entering a Thai home or temple
  • Do not touch anyone’s head, as it is considered sacred
  • Show respect to the King and Queen of Thailand by standing during the national anthem

Best Time to Visit Haad Son

The best time to visit Haad Son is between November and April, during the dry season when the weather is mild and the sea is calm. However, if you want to avoid the crowds, you can visit during the low season, which runs from May to October.

Do’s and Don’ts of Haad Son

Do:

  • Respect the local culture and customs
  • Negotiate prices with street vendors
  • Stay hydrated by drinking plenty of water
  • Relax and enjoy the beautiful natural surroundings

Don’t:

  • Disrespect the King or Queen of Thailand
  • Touch or disturb the marine life when snorkelling or diving
  • Forget to apply sunscreen before spending time in the sun
  • Engage in illegal activities

Safety and Health

Safety in Haad Son

Haad Son is a safe destination, but visitors should still exercise caution and take basic safety precautions, such as locking valuables in the hotel safe and being aware of their surroundings.

Health in Haad Son

It is recommended to obtain travel health insurance before travelling to Haad Son. Visitors should also take precautions against mosquito-borne illnesses, such as dengue fever, by wearing protective clothing and mosquito repellent.

Other Options

How to Get Around in Haad Son

The easiest way to get around Haad Son is to rent a motorbike or bicycle. Taxis and songthaews (shared trucks) are also available for hire, but may be more expensive than renting your own vehicle.
