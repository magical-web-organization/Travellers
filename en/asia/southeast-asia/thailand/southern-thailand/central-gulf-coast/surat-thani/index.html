Introduction to Surat Thani

Surat Thani, a city in the southern part of Thailand, is the gateway to many popular tourist destinations. Located on the eastern shore of the Gulf of Thailand, it serves as a hub for those heading to the Samui Archipelago, Koh Phangan, and Koh Tao Islands. The city is also known as the “City of Good People” which reflects its welcoming atmosphere and friendly locals. Surat Thani is a melting pot of cultural and natural attractions, making it a well-rounded destination for travelers.

List of Places to Visit in Surat Thani

When visiting Surat Thani, you can explore a range of captivating destinations. We have compiled a list of must-see attractions for your reference.

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Surat Thani City Pillar Shrine: Pay a visit to Surat Thani’s religious sanctuary. This beautiful shrine is a popular site to pray for good luck or make a vow for important ceremonies.
  • Ban Don Floating Market: The Ban Don is a morning market located around five kilometers from Surat Thani. Distributing fresh fruits, vegetables, and seafood daily, this floating market is an important cultural landmark in Surat Thani.
  • Phra Borom That Chaiya: Home to an ancient pagoda, this architectural treasure stands in the heart of town and serves as a symbolic representation of the region’s rich history.

Natural Attractions of Surat Thani: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Khao Sok National Park: Spanning about 739 sq km, Khao Sok is a natural gem that houses an abundance of wildlife. You can experience an array of activities inside the park including trekking, canoeing, and jungle exploration.
  • Chiew Larn Lake: Immerse yourself in the beauty of Chiew Larn, a serene freshwater lake located in Khao Sok National Park. Surrounded by towering limestone cliffs, you can book a boat tour to take you on a memorable journey of the lake’s stunning scenery.
  • Wat Phra Borommathat Chaiya Temple: Overlooking the Gulf of Thailand from a hilltop, this is a sacred shrine to be visited if you are in Surat Thani. This temple complex of stunning architecture is located about 40 kilometers from the city center.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Surat Thani

Surat Thani has plenty of outdoor activities for people who love hiking, trekking, and biking. Here are some of the best trails in the region:

  • Khao Sok National Park: Explore the wild terrain of Khao Sok by taking the Park’s hiking trails. You can choose from a variety of easy to difficult trails, depending on your physical fitness and trekking experience.
  • Koh Nang Yuan Island: If you prefer a less-intense route, head to Koh Nang Yuan Island for some pleasurable walks or biking activities. With paved trails running through the island’s breathtaking scenery, this is one of the most beautiful biking routes you can experience in Thailand.

Best Things to Do in Surat Thani: Activities and Tours

Here are some of the best things you can do in Surat Thani:

  • Experience the Fireflies Cruise: Capture mesmerizing views of the Fireflies Cruise while floating along the beautiful tributary of the Tapi River. This experience is sure to leave you amazed by the creatures’ magical light show on the trees.
  • Visit a Silk Factory: Surat Thani is known for its silk production, and visiting a silk factory is a great idea. Here you can learn about silk production, making your way completely immersed in the local traditions.
  • Scuba Diving: Scuba Diving is a great way to witness the beauty of the Gulf of Thailand. The region is abundant with marine life, making for a fantastic scuba diving experience.

Week-Long Itinerary for Surat Thani

Day 1: Head to the Surat Thani City Pillar Shrine. Enjoy the architectural design and the peaceful atmosphere of the shrine. In the evening, visit the Ban Don Floating Market and savor some of the region’s best street food.

Day 2: Spend this day at the stunning Chiew Larn Lake. Take a boat tour to explore the beauty of the surrounding limestone cliffs.

Day 3-4: Trek through the Khao Sok National Park and spend two days exploring the wildlife and the natural habitat of some of the world’s most endangered species.

Day 5: Take a break from trekking and visit the local silk factory. Here you can witness the art of weaving and silk production.

Day 6: Explore Koh Nang Yuan Island on a bike-ride and experience its beautiful views and landscapes.

Day 7: Conclude your trip with a Fireflies Cruise. Witness thousands of fireflies lighting up trees, leaving you with an awe-inspiring experience.

Food and Drink Scene of Surat Thani

Thailand is known for its impeccable food scene, and Surat Thani is no exception. Here are some of the must-try local dishes:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Tom Yam Kung: A classic Thai dish, Tom Yam Kung is a soup made with shrimps, galangal, lemongrass, chili, mushrooms, and other herbs and spices.
  • Kanom Jeen: Kanom Jeen is a popular dish made with fermented rice noodles served with various vegetable curries and soups.
  • Khao Mok Gai: A local dish of seasoned rice cooked in a combination of spices, with chicken cooked in a Muslim-style curry.
  • Roti: This dessert is made with a thin layer of pastry filled with banana, Nutella, or egg.

Local Beverages

  • Chao Kuai: A refreshing iced drink, consisting of bael fruit and pandan juice.
  • Krating Daeng: From the makers of Red Bull, Krating Daeng is a Thai energy drink.

Where to Stay in Surat Thani: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Surat Thani offers a great selection of accommodations, catering to all budgets. Here are some of our picks:

  • Baan Tai Hotel: Located in a scenic environment and close to the beach, this is a perfect place if you want to be close to nature.
  • Novotel Surat Thani: A modern hotel with all the amenities and comforts of a good hotel stay.
  • 13 Coins Resort: This is a quirky resort that offers a unique experience of staying in cozy and stylish cabanas.

What to Buy in Surat Thani: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Surat Thani is famous for its silk fabrics and oyster sauce. Here are some of the most popular souvenirs you can bring back to your family and friends:

  • Thai Silk: The province of Surat Thani is known for its silk production, making beautiful silk clothing an ideal souvenir
  • Oyster Sauce: One of the popular food products produced in Surat Thani is the oyster sauce, which makes it a perfect product to bring back home.

Local Culture and Customs of Surat Thani

  • When visiting temples, being respectful and taking off your shoes is a must.
  • Dressing modestly is expected while visiting religious sites.
  • The people of Surat Thani are known for their hospitality, welcoming attitude, and friendly smiles.

Best Time to Visit Surat Thani

The best time to visit Surat Thani is between December to May. The weather is cool and dry, making it perfect to explore the natural habitat of the region without sweltering in the heat.

Do’s and Don’ts of Surat Thani

Do:

  • Be respectful of the local culture and customs.
  • Greeting the locals with a smile and saying “sawatdee ka/krup” can go a long way in making friends.

Don’t:

  • Do not disrespect religious sites and cultural monuments.
  • Do not get involved in illegal activity, as it may lead to harsh consequences.

Safety and Health

Safety in Surat Thani

  • Always keep an eye on your belongings and keep them secure.
  • Be cautious while taking a taxi or tuk-tuk and make sure to agree upon a price before starting the journey.

Health in Surat Thani

  • Keep yourself hydrated as the weather can be exceptionally humid.
  • Wear sunscreen and use mosquito repellent.

Getting to Surat Thani: Transportation Options and Logistics

Surat Thani is blessed with various transportation options, making it easily accessible for travelers. Here are some routes to consider:

Airports Near Surat Thani

  • Surat Thani Airport (URT) serves as the primary airport, with daily flights from Bangkok and other major cities in Thailand.

Bus Stations Near Surat Thani

  • Surat Thani Bus Terminal is the central bus station, providing transportation to the nearby islands and other parts of the country.

Other Options

  • You can take a train from Bangkok, which is a great way to witness the scenic route from the city to Surat Thani.

How to Get Around in Surat Thani

You can explore Surat Thani by taking several modes of transportation:

  • Tuk-tuks and taxis are readily available for short distances, and they are available at the bus station and airport.
  • Motorbikes rentals are also available for travelers who want to experience the freedom of the open road.

Where to Go Next from Surat Thani: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

There are many places to visit near Surat Thani:

  • Koh Tao Island: Popular for its snorkeling and scuba diving opportunities.
  • Koh Phangan Island: Known for its full moon parties.
  • Khao Lak: Khao Lak is famous for its serene beaches, diving, and trekking in the nearby Khao Sok National Park.

Conclusion

Surat Thani is an excellent destination for travelers looking to experience both culture and natural beauty. With an abundance of attractions, activities, and local delicacies, it is no wonder why Surat Thani is known as the City of Good People!
