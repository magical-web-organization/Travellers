A Comprehensive Tour Guide for Nakhon Si Thammarat, Thailand

Nakhon Si Thammarat is a culturally-rich province located in the Southern part of Thailand. This region, which has a population of over 1.5 million people, boasts stunning vistas, a vibrant food scene, and historical landmarks that tell the interesting story of Thailand’s past. In this guide, we will explore the most notable attractions, cultural customs, transportation options, and other crucial information to help you experience Nakhon Si Thammarat like a pro.

List of Places to Visit in Nakhon Si Thammarat

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Nakhon Si Thammarat is home to several cultural landmarks that reflect the rich history of the region. Some of these attractions include:

  • Wat Phra Mahathat Woramahawihan: This temple is one of the most significant Buddhist temples in the whole of Thailand, and it is considered the spiritual heart of Nakhon Si Thammarat. The temple boasts impressive architecture, beautiful gardens, and historical relics.
  • Khao Luang National Park: For nature enthusiasts, this park, which is home to stunning waterfalls, dense forests, and exotic wildlife, will be an exciting attraction to explore.
  • Nakhon Si Thammarat National Museum: This museum houses an extensive collection of artifacts and relics from Thailand’s ancient past, including sculptures, pottery, weapons, and ornate jewelry.
  • Chedi Yak: This ancient monument dates back to the Srivijaya period, and it is an essential cultural landmark for Nakhon Si Thammarat residents with its unique architectural design and historical significance.

Natural Attractions of Nakhon Si Thammarat: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

In addition to cultural landmarks, Nakhon Si Thammarat is also home to several natural attractions that guarantee to take your breath away, including:

  • Khiriwong Village: This charming village, situated around a picturesque mountain range, is an eco-tourism hotspot due to its stunning natural features, such as pristine waterfalls and spectacular views of the surrounding nature.
  • Khao Nan National Park: With its incredible limestone formations, lush forests and cascading waterfalls, this park is a haven for adventurous travellers looking to explore the natural beauty of Nakhon Si Thammarat.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Nakhon Si Thammarat

Nakhon Si Thammarat offers fantastic biking routes for adventure enthusiasts. Some of the popular routes include:

  • Khao Luang Road: This route is just over 30 kilometers, and it takes you past the stunning Khao Luang National Park, through charming rural areas, and towards the coast.
  • Phrom Lok Waterfall: This one is an off-road bike route that passes through picturesque villages, limestone caves, and leading you to the stunning Phrom Lok Waterfall.

Best Things to Do in Nakhon Si Thammarat: Activities and Tours

In addition to popular attractions, there are several activities and tours that visitors can enjoy during their visit to Nakhon Si Thammarat, including:

  • Farm-to-Table Experience: This tour is an opportunity to visit local farms and learn about the process of growing fruits and vegetables specific to the area. Visitors also get to taste and experience the farm’s fresh produce, which includes sweet pineapples, honey, and herbs.
  • Temples Tour: Nakhon Si Thammarat is home to several impressive Buddhist temples that are the pride of the region. Taking a guided tour of these ancient buildings offers a chance to explore the intricate architecture, historical relics and learn about Buddhist beliefs.

Week-Long Itinerary for Nakhon Si Thammarat

Here’s a sample itinerary for a week-long trip to Nakhon Si Thammarat:

Day 1: Explore Wat Phra Mahathat Woramahawihan and the surrounding gardens. Day 2: Visit the Nakhon Si Thammarat National Museum, and sample local cuisine in town over lunch. Day 3: Hike through Khao Luang National Park and enjoy stunning views of the surrounding nature. Day 4: Enjoy a farm-to-table experience where you can learn how to cook traditional Thai dishes before sampling your creations. Day 5: Take a bike ride through Phrom Lok Waterfall Route. Day 6: Tour some of the region’s ancient Buddhist temples, including Chedi Yak and Wat Tantayapirom. Day 7: Relax and rejuvenate in one of the local hot spring resorts before departing Nakhon Si Thammarat.

Food and Drink Scene of Nakhon Si Thammarat

Thai cuisine is famous around the world for its rich flavors and spices, and Nakhon Si Thammarat is no exception. Here are some of the must-try dishes and local beverages you should sample while in the province:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Khao Yum: This dish is made of rice, vegetables, and shrimp laced with herbs and spices and topped with a sweet and sour sauce.
  • Kua Kling: This is a spicy, dry curry dish featuring sliced meat and a mix of flavorful spices such as garlic, turmeric, and chili.

Local Beverages

  • Nam-Ngiew Juice This refreshing drink is made from pickled Myrtle leaves and ginger.
  • Narae Prik This is a sweet crisp drink made from young taproots of the kaffir lime tree.

Where to Stay in Nakhon Si Thammarat: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Nakhon Si Thammarat offers visitors a variety of accommodation options to suit every budget, ranging from cozy guesthouses to high-end resorts. Some of the best places to stay in Nakhon Si Thammarat are:

  • The Twin Lotus Hotel: With beautiful rooms, a spa, and several restaurants, The Twin Lotus Hotel offers a luxurious stay in the heart of the city.
  • Banyan Tree Resort: This upscale spa resort offers private villas complete with private gardens and pools, perfect for a romantic getaway.
  • KaliBuri Resort: This eco-resort is situated in the stunning Khiriwong Village, surrounded by lush, tropical nature.

What to Buy in Nakhon Si Thammarat: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Some of the excellent souvenirs to bring back home after your visit to Nakhon Si Thammarat include:

  • Kratib: Woven baskets made from leaves of bamboo shoots, used for storing food and displaying artifacts and souvenirs.
  • Marble products: Nakhon Si Thammarat is famous for its unique marble products, including vases, statues, and jewelry.
  • Banutron fabric: This fabric is made with patterns featuring the region’s rich cultural and historical significance, ranging from traditional garb to intricate designs featuring religious icons.

Local Culture and Customs of Nakhon Si Thammarat

  • Respect the king and the monarchy, a highly esteemed institution in Thailand.
  • Shoes should be removed when entering Thai homes, temples, and schools.
  • Showing respect to Buddhist monks should be observed when coming across them.

Best Time to Visit Nakhon Si Thammarat

The best time to visit Nakhon Si Thammarat would be from November to February, when the temperatures are cooler, and the skies are clear. However, if you are looking for a low-budget option, visiting during the monsoon season between June and October is a great option for discounts and promotions.

Do’s and Don’ts of Nakhon Si Thammarat

Do:

  • Book the accommodation and tours in advance.
  • Wear appropriate clothing when visiting temples, make sure to cover your shoulders and knees.

Don’t:

  • Disrespect the national anthem - it is played every morning at 8:00 AM and 6:00 PM.
  • Show affection in public - public displays of affection are considered inappropriate in Thailand.

Safety and Health

Safety in Nakhon Si Thammarat

Nakhon Si Thammarat is generally a safe destination for visitors. The biggest risks are petty theft and scams, so we recommend being vigilant about your belongings and any solicitations. Remember to lock your valuables in a hotel safe and to keep an eye out for your surroundings when walking around public areas.

Health in Nakhon Si Thammarat

Healthcare in Nakhon Si Thammarat is easily accessible, with several hospitals and medical clinics that you can visit. However, we recommend getting travel insurance in case of any medical emergencies, and remember to bring necessary medication such as insect repellent and sunscreen.

Getting to Nakhon Si Thammarat: Transportation Options and Logistics

Nakhon Si Thammarat is well-connected to several other parts of Thailand, with several available transportation options:

Airports Near Nakhon Si Thammarat

  • Nakhon Si Thammarat International Airport This airport is located seven kilometers from the city center and offers direct flights to various parts of Thailand.

Bus Stations Near Nakhon Si Thammarat

  • Nakhon Si Thammarat Bus Station This central bus station offers various rides and routes to and from Bangkok, Surat Thani, and other Southern destinations.

Other Options

  • Car Rental Services: If you prefer more flexibility during your travels, you could opt to rent a car with a GPS system from reliable car rental providers like Hertz or Avis.

How to Get Around in Nakhon Si Thammarat

Nakhon Si Thammarat offers various ways to get around the region, including tuk-tuks, public buses, motorbike taxis, and car rental services. Some of the bike rental services to consider include Top Bike Kainong Motobike and George’s Motorbike rental.

Where to Go Next from Nakhon Si Thammarat: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Some of the top neighboring destinations from Nakhon Si Thammarat include:

  • Koh Samui: This is a nearby island paradise, home to postcard-perfect beaches and luxurious resorts.
  • Krabi: This destination is famous for its stunning natural formations, including limestone cliffs and towering mountains.
  • Phuket: This iconic beach destination boasts fantastic nightlife, luxury resorts, and crystal-clear waters ideal for snorkeling.

Conclusion

Nakhon Si Thammarat is a fantastic destination for anyone looking to explore rich cultural heritage, stunning nature, and exciting activities. We hope this guide helps you to plan your adventure and gain memorable experiences. Don’t forget to try the local cuisine, immerse yourself in the culture, and make lasting memories that you’ll remember for years to come.
