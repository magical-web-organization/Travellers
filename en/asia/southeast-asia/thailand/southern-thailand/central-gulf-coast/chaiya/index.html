Introduction to Chaiya

Located in the Central Gulf Coast of Southern Thailand, Chaiya is a charming town known for its historical sites, natural attractions, and local cuisines. The town dates back to the 7th century and is believed to have been a flourishing center of trade and culture during the Srivijaya period. Today, Chaiya combines its rich cultural heritage with modern attractions, making it a must-visit destination for travelers in Thailand. In this travel guide, we will explore the best places to visit, things to do, and food to try in Chaiya.

List of Places to Visit in Chaiya

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  1. Wat Phra Borom That Chaiya: This ancient temple complex is home to the Mahayana-style stupa, a seven-tiered pagoda structure that dates back to the 8th century.

  2. Chaiya National Museum: The museum showcases the history and culture of the Chaiya region. It houses a vast collection of Srivijaya artifacts, including ancient pottery, ceramics, statues, and Buddha images.

  3. Wat Suan Mokkhaphalaram: Founded by the famous monk Buddhadasa Bhikkhu, this temple is known for its meditation retreats that attract visitors from around the world.

  4. Phumriang Ceramics Park: This ceramics park showcases the famous Phumriang Ceramics, which have been produced in Chaiya for over 300 years. The park has a showroom, a pottery-making village, and a museum.

Natural Attractions of Chaiya: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  1. Khao Kriap Cave: A natural wonder located in the Phra Jomthong Mountains, this cave system is over 100 meters long and features stalactites and stalagmites.

  2. Tapi River: A beautiful river that runs through Chaiya, the Tapi River is ideal for kayaking, fishing, and sightseeing.

  3. Khao Plai Dam: A scenic viewpoint overlooking the entire Chaiya town, the Khao Plai Dam is perfect for panoramic views and sunset watching.

  4. Khao Chong Krachok: A hilltop temple that offers stunning views of the surrounding valleys and forests.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Chaiya

  1. Tapi River Cycling Route: A 20-kilometer biking route that runs along the picturesque Tapi River.

  2. Khao Kriap Cave Trekking Route: A 2-kilometer hiking trail that takes visitors to the Khao Kriap Cave.

  3. Phra Jomthong Mountain Bike Trail: A 10-kilometer mountain bike trail that runs through the Phra Jomthong Mountains.

Best Things to Do in Chaiya: Activities and Tours

  1. Ceramics Workshop: Visitors can participate in a ceramics workshop at Phumriang Ceramics Park and create their own pottery.

  2. Meditation Retreat: Wat Suan Mokkhaphalaram offers meditation retreats for visitors who wish to learn more about mindfulness and Buddhist teachings.

  3. Kayaking Tour: A guided kayaking tour along the Tapi River for a scenic and peaceful experience.

Week-Long Itinerary for Chaiya

Day Activities
Day 1 Visit Chaiya National Museum and Wat Phra Borom That Chaiya
Day 2 Explore the Phumriang Ceramics Park and take part in a ceramics workshop

Food and Drink Scene of Chaiya

Must-Try Dishes

  1. Khao Yam: A healthy and flavorful salad made with rice, herbs, vegetables, and grilled fish served with a sweet and spicy sauce.

  2. Khanom Chak: A traditional snack made of sticky rice and coconut wrapped in banana leaves.

  3. Tom Yum Goong: A spicy and sour soup made with shrimp, lemongrass, and chili.

  4. Grilled Fish: Freshly grilled fish served with spicy dipping sauce is a must-try in Chaiya.

Local Beverages

  1. Nam Manao: A refreshing lime juice drink that can be found in almost every restaurant and cafe.

  2. Cha Yen: A Thai iced tea made with sweetened condensed milk and tea leaves.

  3. Coconut Water: Fresh coconut water is a popular drink in Chaiya, especially during the hot summer months.

Where to Stay in Chaiya: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  1. Baan Phumriang Resort: A charming resort located near Phumriang Ceramics Park, offering beautiful views and traditional Thai architecture.

  2. Chaiya Garden: A budget-friendly guesthouse located in the heart of Chaiya, offering comfortable accommodation and easy access to the town’s attractions.

  3. The One Boutique Hotel: A modern and stylish hotel located on the outskirts of Chaiya, offering excellent facilities and a peaceful atmosphere.

What to Buy in Chaiya: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  1. Phumriang Ceramics: Handmade ceramics that can be purchased at Phumriang Ceramics Park and local markets around town.

  2. Coconut Sugar: A locally produced sweetener made from coconut sap that is used in many Thai desserts and snacks.

  3. Thai Silk: Beautiful and high-quality silk fabrics that can be found at many shops and markets in Chaiya.

Local Culture and Customs of Chaiya

Chaiya is a predominantly Buddhist town, and visitors are expected to dress conservatively when visiting temples and religious sites. It is also customary to remove shoes before entering temples or someone’s home. Tipping is not customary in Chaiya, but rounding up the bill is appreciated.

Best Time to Visit Chaiya

The best time to visit Chaiya is during the dry season, which runs from November to April. The temperatures are mild, and the weather is perfect for outdoor activities and sightseeing. However, Chaiya can be visited year-round, and even during the rainy season, there are plenty of indoor activities to enjoy.

Do’s and Don’ts of Chaiya

Do:

  1. Dress conservatively when visiting temples.

  2. Be respectful of local customs and traditions.

  3. Take off your shoes before entering someone’s home or a temple.

Don’t:

  1. Leave trash or debris in public spaces.

  2. Disrespect Buddha images, temples, or other religious symbols.

  3. Try to haggle too aggressively when shopping in local markets.

Safety and Health

Safety in Chaiya

Chaiya is a relatively safe destination, and violent crime is rare. However, visitors should still exercise caution and be aware of their surroundings, especially when traveling alone at night or in remote areas.

Health in Chaiya

While there are no major health risks in Chaiya, visitors should still take basic precautions such as drinking bottled water and avoiding eating street food that may be undercooked or unsanitary. It is also a good idea to pack insect repellent, especially during the rainy season.

Getting to Chaiya: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Chaiya

The closest airport to Chaiya is Surat Thani International Airport, which is approximately 50 kilometers away. Visitors can take a taxi or a private car from the airport to Chaiya.

Bus Stations Near Chaiya

Chaiya has a small bus station that is well-connected to other towns and cities in the region. Visitors can take a bus from Surat Thani or other nearby destinations to Chaiya.

Other Options

Visitors can also rent a car or a motorbike to explore Chaiya and the surrounding areas at their own pace.

How to Get Around in Chaiya

The best way to get around Chaiya is by motorbike or bicycle, which can be rented from many shops and guesthouses around town. Taxis and songthaews (shared taxis) are also available for short trips in and around town.

Where to Go Next from Chaiya: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  1. Koh Samui: A popular island destination located in the Gulf of Thailand, known for its beautiful beaches and vibrant nightlife.

  2. Khao Sok National Park: A stunning natural park located in Surat Thani province, home to the famous Cheow Lan Lake and breathtaking limestone mountains.

  3. Surat Thani: The capital city of Surat Thani province, known for its delicious food, night markets, and charming temples.

Conclusion

Chaiya offers a unique blend of culture, history, and natural beauty that makes it one of the most fascinating destinations in Southern Thailand. By following this comprehensive travel guide, visitors can experience the best that Chaiya has to offer, from its ancient temples to its stunning landscapes and delicious local cuisine.
