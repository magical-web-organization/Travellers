Introduction to Khanom

Explore the hidden gems of southern Thailand in Khanom, a secluded town on the east coast of the Thai Gulf. Known for its quiet, unspoiled beaches, towering mountains, and natural landmarks, Khanom is an up-and-coming destination for travelers looking to escape the hustle and bustle of popular Thai hotspots like Phuket and Krabi. This travel guide will provide information and tips to help you plan your visit to Khanom and make the most of your adventure.

List of Places to Visit in Khanom

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Take a step back in time and learn about Khanom’s rich culture and history at one of the town’s museums or historical sites.

  • Wat Phra Mahathat Woramahawihan - This ancient temple, also known as Wat Khanom, is one of the most important religious sites in southern Thailand. Admire the intricate designs of the temple’s pagodas and shrines and witness the locals’ devotion to their faith.
  • Khanom City Pillar Shrine - The shrine is located in the heart of the town and is believed to protect Khanom and its people who come here to make offerings to the gods.
  • Nakhon Si Thammarat National Museum - Although not in Khanom town, the museum is just a short drive away. The museum displays ancient artifacts and provides an interesting glimpse into the region’s history.

Natural Attractions of Khanom: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Discover some of the most stunning natural attractions in southern Thailand through outdoor activities and breathtaking views.

  • Khao Wang Thong Cave - Explore the depths of the underground world in the enormous Khao Wang Thong Cave. The cave features stalactites and stalagmites, which form captivating rock formations.
  • Pha Nam Yoi Waterfall - Trek through the lush green jungles to reach this hidden waterfall, where you can swim in crystal-clear water and relax amidst the serene natural beauty.
  • Dolphin Watching Tour - Situated between the Thai Gulf and the Andaman Sea, Khanom boasts one of the highest concentrations of dolphins in Thailand. Take a boat tour, watch the playful mammals, and snap some memorable pictures of these impressive creatures.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Khanom

Hiking and biking are great ways to explore the natural beauty of Khanom, and there are several trails to choose from.

  • Hin Lad Waterfall Trail - Hike through the lush green forest and witness the tranquility of the waterfall. This trail is suitable for both intermediate and advanced hikers.
  • Khao Plai Dam Viewpoint Trail - With an elevation of 505 meters, the mountain peak offers a panoramic view of the surrounding areas of Khanom and the Gulf of Thailand. The trail is suitable for advanced hikers and mountain bikers.
  • Khanom Beach Cycling Trail - Explore the beautiful coastline of Khanom on two wheels. The trail is 60 kilometers long and is suitable for both beginners and experienced cyclists. Along the way, you’ll have ample opportunities to take pictures of the unique scenery and observe the daily life of the locals.

Best Things to Do in Khanom: Activities and Tours

Whether you’re traveling solo, with friends or family, there is always something to do in Khanom.

  • Snorkeling and Scuba Diving - Take a dive underwater and marvel at the stunning corals and colorful marine life in the Gulf of Thailand.
  • Thai Cooking Classes - Learn how to cook some of the most famous Thai dishes from a professional chef and indulge yourself in the unique flavors of Khanom.
  • Sunset Beach Walk - Watch the mesmerizing sunset and stroll along the picturesque beach hand-in-hand with your loved one.

Food and Drink Scene of Khanom

Must-Try Dishes

Khanom offers a diverse range of cuisine that reflects the town’s unique cultural blend. The town is famous for its seafood, and the food is usually cooked using locally sourced ingredients and spices.

  • Gaeng Som Pla - Spicy and sour fish curry with vegetables like pumpkin, okra and eggplant.
  • Khanom Moo Yang - Delicious grilled pork on skewers served with a tangy Thai dipping sauce.
  • Khanom Chin - A traditional southern Thai dish made with fermented rice noodles in a spicy curry sauce.

Local Beverages

Thailand is famous for its refreshing beverages, and Khanom is no different. Here is a list of some of the most popular local drinks:

  • Coconut Water - Sip on a chilled fresh coconut while lounging on the beach.
  • Thai Tea - A sweet and rich milk tea with a hint of spicy flavor.
  • Fresh Fruit Shake - Enjoy various fruit shakes, from mango to watermelon, as they are specialities in Khanom for the locals and tourists alike.

Where to Stay in Khanom: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Khanom offers a range of accommodations that cater to all budgets and travel needs. Here are some of the best places to stay in Khanom:

  • Aava Resort & Spa - A luxury resort that offers panoramic sea views, pool villas, and spa services.
  • Happy Resort Khanom - A budget-friendly resort with comfortable room and bungalows and a swimming pool. Perfect for families.
  • Ban Thanyanan - This eco-friendly guesthouse is located right near the beach, and offers a peaceful atmosphere and conscientious hosts.

What to Buy in Khanom: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Khanom Salt - The salt is locally harvested and produced without any artificial additives. This salt is rich in minerals and flavors, and has been a staple in southern Thai cooking for centuries.
  • Tapioca Chips - These sliced root chips are a popular snack in Khanom, and are made from cassava root, flour, spices and lime juice.
  • Batik Fabric - This traditional fabric is hand-dyed piece-by-piece by local artisans. Batik fabric is used to create unique clothing, bags, and home décor items.

Local Culture and Customs of Khanom

Thailand is known for its friendly locals, rich culture, and spiritual traditions. Here are some tips to help you navigate the local customs and values in Khanom:

  • Dress Modestly - Dress respectfully when visiting temples and other religious sites. Covering your shoulders and knees is appropriate.
  • Remove Your Shoes - When entering someone’s home or temple, remove your shoes as a sign of respect.
  • Do Not Touch People’s Heads - In Thailand, the head is considered sacred, so avoid touching people’s heads.

Best Time to Visit Khanom

The best time to visit Khanom is between December and May, during the dry season. The temperatures are moderate, and the skies are clear during this season. Avoid the rainy season, which runs from June to November, when the weather can be unpredictable and hinder outdoor activities.

Do’s and Don’ts of Khanom

Do:

  • Dress modestly when visiting religious sites and temples.
  • Try the local cuisine.
  • Respect the local culture and traditions.

Don’t:

  • Disrespect the Thai King, as it’s illegal in Thailand.
  • Touch people’s heads or point your feet at someone.
  • Forget to carry cash, as many smaller businesses might not accept credit cards.

Safety and Health

Safety in Khanom

Thailand is generally a safe country, Khanom included, but it is always advisable to take precautions to keep yourself safe.

  • Don’t travel alone at night, particularly in isolated areas.
  • Keep an eye on your belongings, especially in crowded areas.
  • Wear a helmet when renting a motorbike or bicycle.

Health in Khanom

Before traveling to Thailand, ensure that your vaccinations are up-to-date, and consult your doctor regarding recommended shots.

Malaria is still prevalent in some parts of Thailand, but not so much in Khanom. Take necessary precautions like using repellent, nets and wearing long-sleeved garments.

Getting to Khanom: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Khanom

The closest airport to Khanom is Nakhon Si Thammarat International Airport, approximately 50 kilometers away. Another option is Surat Thani International Airport, which is around 150 kilometers away from Khanom.

Bus Stations Near Khanom

Khanom is accessible via both private and public long-distance buses from Surat Thani, Nakhon Si Thammarat, and Krabi.

Other Options

The train is also an option that terminates at Surat Thani railway station. If you drive your own car or bike, there are public parking lots available throughout the town.

How to Get Around in Khanom

The most common mode of transport in Khanom is by motorbike, which can be rented from local vendors. Alternatively, bicycles and car rentals are also available to move around the small town and nearby attractions.

Where to Go Next from Khanom: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

After spending a few days in Khanom, consider visiting these nearby destinations:

  • Koh Samui - Take a ferry from Khanom to Koh Samui, and enjoy the vibrant nightlife, stunning beaches, and friendly locals of the popular Thai island.
  • Khao Sok National Park - Drive or take a bus to Khao Sok National Park, and indulge yourself in the natural beauty of the jungle, with river canyons, limestone cliffs, and abundant flora and fauna.
  • Koh Tao - Take a ferry from Khanom to Koh Tao, which is around 3 hours away, and explore this popular diving destination.

Conclusion

Khanom offers a unique blend of culture, natural beauty, and adventure, making it a perfect destination for travelers who want to experience a side of Thailand that’s off-the-beaten-path. With this comprehensive guide, you’re now equipped with all the information you’ll need to make the most out of your visit to Khanom and to experience all the town has to offer.
