Introduction to Mae Nam

Mae Nam is a peaceful and laid-back village on the northern coast of the tropical island of Ko Samui in Southern Thailand. Mae Nam offers a great escape from the bustling tourist hotspots of Chaweng and Lamai but still has plenty to offer, including beautiful beaches, lush green hills, outstanding cuisine, plenty of outdoor activities, and cultural attractions.

List of Places to Visit in Mae Nam

Here is a list of the top places to visit in Mae Nam:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Wat Na Phra Lan: This beautiful temple is located in the center of Mae Nam and features intricate carvings, colorful murals, and a stunning golden Buddha statue.
  • Bophut Beach Pier: This historic pier is over 100 years old and offers stunning views of the sunset and the Gulf of Thailand.
  • Fisherman’s Village: This charming village features traditional teak houses, boutique shops, and authentic cuisine.

Natural Attractions of Mae Nam: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Mae Nam Beach: This long sandy beach boasts crystal-clear waters and is less crowded than other beaches on the island.
  • Hin Lad Waterfall: A beautiful and serene jungle waterfall located in the hills of Mae Nam.
  • Mae Nam Viewpoint: Get panoramic views of the surrounding countryside and neighboring islands.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Mae Nam

Hiking and biking enthusiasts will enjoy exploring the Mae Nam hills on various trails that offer a great way to see the island’s natural beauty.

Best Things to Do in Mae Nam: Activities and Tours

  • Island Hopping: Take a day trip to nearby islands to discover remote beaches and hidden coves.
  • Thai Cooking Classes: Learn how to make delicious and authentic Thai dishes with cooking classes.
  • Snorkeling and Diving: Explore the warm waters and colorful marine life of the Gulf of Thailand.

Week-Long Itinerary for Mae Nam

A suggested itinerary for a week in Mae Nam:

Day 1 Relax on Mae Nam Beach
Day 2 Visit the Hin Lad Waterfall
Day 3 Explore the Fisherman’s Village
Day 4 Take a Thai cooking class
Day 5 Island hopping to Koh Phangan
Day 6 Hike to the Mae Nam Viewpoint
Day 7 Relax and enjoy the local cuisine

Food and Drink Scene of Mae Nam

The food scene in Mae Nam is a mix of traditional Thai cuisine and international options.

Must-Try Dishes

  • Pad Thai: A classic Thai noodle dish with vegetables, eggs, and peanuts.
  • Tom Yum Goong: A spicy and sour soup made with shrimp, lemongrass, and chili.

Local Beverages

  • Fresh Coconut Water: A refreshing drink straight from the coconut.
  • Cha Yen: A Thai iced tea made with condensed milk.

Where to Stay in Mae Nam: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Mae Nam offers a variety of accommodations for all budgets, including:

  • Anantara Bophut Koh Samui Resort
  • Paradise Beach Resort
  • The Florist Resort

What to Buy in Mae Nam: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Hand-carved Coconut Bowls: A beautiful and functional souvenir that supports local artisans.
  • Thai Silk Scarves: A colorful and luxurious accessory to wear or give as a gift.

Local Culture and Customs of Mae Nam

Mae Nam residents are predominantly Buddhist, and visitors are expected to dress modestly at temples and show respect for local customs.

Best Time to Visit Mae Nam

The best time to visit Mae Nam is from December to February when the weather is cooler and drier, making it ideal for outdoor activities.

Do’s and Don’ts of Mae Nam

Do:

  • Dress appropriately when visiting temples or other religious sites.
  • Keep your passport and valuables safe at all times.

Don’t:

  • Touch anyone’s head as it is considered rude in Thai culture.
  • Show disrespect to the royal family or national symbols.

Safety and Health

Safety in Mae Nam

Mae Nam and Ko Samui are generally safe, but visitors should be aware of petty theft and pickpocketing in crowded areas.

Health in Mae Nam

Visitors should take precautions against mosquito bites and bring any necessary medications or immunizations.

Getting to Mae Nam: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Mae Nam

The closest airport to Mae Nam is Samui International Airport, which is about a 15-minute drive away.

Bus Stations Near Mae Nam

The Nathon Bus Terminal serves most of Ko Samui, including Mae Nam.

Other Options

Taxis, private drivers, and rental cars are all available for transportation around the island.

How to Get Around in Mae Nam

Motorbikes and scooters are available for rent, but visitors should be aware of traffic laws and safety concerns.

Where to Go Next from Mae Nam: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Chaweng: A bustling and lively town with a wide range of activities and nightlife.
  • Lamai: A relaxed and family-friendly destination with a great selection of restaurants and shops.
  • Ang Thong Marine Park: A stunning archipelago of uninhabited islands with pristine beaches and coral reefs.

Conclusion

Mae Nam is a hidden gem on the island of Ko Samui that offers a peaceful and relaxing vacation while still providing plenty of activities and attractions for visitors to enjoy.
