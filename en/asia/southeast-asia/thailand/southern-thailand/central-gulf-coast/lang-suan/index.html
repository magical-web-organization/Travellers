Introduction to Lang Suan

Nestled in Central Gulf Coast, Southern Thailand, Lang Suan is a serene town that boasts of a rich cultural heritage, pristine natural wonders, and delicious local cuisine. It is a hidden gem that has not yet been overrun by tourists. This makes it a great destination for travelers who are looking for an authentic Thai experience away from the crowds.

List of Places to Visit in Lang Suan

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Chedi Thao Thep Kasattri - This chedi was built in the honor of Thao Thep Kasattri, who helped lead the battle against the Burmese invasion in the 18th century.
  • Wat Tham Suea - This temple is built on a hill, and visitors must climb 289 steps to reach the top. The historic temple features ancient ruins and golden statues.

Natural Attractions of Lang Suan: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Khao Luang National Park - This stunning park features misty mountains, sparkling waterfalls, and lush rainforests.
  • Koh Tha Thong - A small island off the coast of Lang Suan that is home to vibrant coral reefs and crystal-clear waters.
  • Thung Yai Naresuan Wildlife Sanctuary - home to a diverse array of wildlife, including elephants and tigers.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Lang Suan

  • Khao Luang National Park - This park has several hiking trails, including a trek to the summit of Khao Luang mountain and a trail leading to the gorgeous Than Sawan Waterfall.

Best Things to Do in Lang Suan: Activities and Tours

  • Visit a Local Fruit Orchard - Lang Suan is famous for its delicious fruits, including durian, rambutan, and mangosteen. Take a tour of a local fruit orchard to learn about the harvesting process and taste some of the local produce.
  • Explore the Streets of Lang Suan - Take a stroll through the town’s quaint streets and visit its charming cafes and shops.
  • Take a Boat Tour - Explore the mangrove forests that surround Lang Suan by taking a boat tour.

Food and Drink Scene of Lang Suan

Must-Try Dishes

  • Durian - Lang Suan is famous for its delicious and pungent durian.
  • Grilled Fish with Mango Salad - A popular dish that features a grilled fish served with a spicy and tangy mango salad.
  • Tom Yum Kung - A spicy and sour shrimp soup that is a staple of Thai cuisine.

Local Beverages

  • Thai Iced Tea - A sweet and creamy iced tea that is a popular drink in Thailand.
  • Coconut Water - A refreshing and healthy drink that is readily available in Lang Suan.

Where to Stay in Lang Suan: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • X2 Vibe Chumphon Hotel - A luxurious hotel that offers stunning views of the Gulf of Thailand.
  • Prachakorn Inn Hotel - A budget-friendly hotel that is located in the heart of Lang Suan.
  • Baan Saranya - A traditional Thai guesthouse that offers a unique and authentic experience.

What to Buy in Lang Suan: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Fruits - Take home some of the delicious fruits that are grown in Lang Suan, including durian, rambutan, and mangosteen.
  • Hand-Woven Fabrics - Lang Suan is known for its beautiful hand-woven fabrics that are made using traditional techniques.

Local Culture and Customs of Lang Suan

  • Respect for Monks - Monks hold a revered place in Thai culture, and visitors should always show them respect by refraining from loud conversations or taking photos without permission.

Best Time to Visit Lang Suan

The best time to visit Lang Suan is from November to February, when the weather is cool and dry.

Do’s and Don’ts of Lang Suan

Do:

  • Take off Shoes Before Entering Temples - Visitors should remove their shoes before entering any temple or other religious sites.
  • Dress Conservatively - Visitors should dress conservatively when visiting temples or other traditional sites.

Don’t:

  • Touch the Head of a Thai Person - The head is considered a sacred part of the body in Thai culture, and it is disrespectful to touch it.
  • Disrespect the Monarchy - Thailand has strict laws against disrespecting the country’s monarchy, and visitors should be respectful of this cultural norm.

Safety and Health

Safety in Lang Suan

Lang Suan is a relatively safe town, but visitors should take precautions to protect themselves from petty theft and pickpocketing.

Health in Lang Suan

Visitors should take care to avoid drinking tap water in Lang Suan, and should always drink bottled or purified water.

Getting to Lang Suan: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Lang Suan

The closest airport to Lang Suan is Chumphon Airport, which is approximately 40 km away.

Bus Stations Near Lang Suan

Lang Suan Bus Station offers regular bus services to nearby towns and cities.

Other Options

Visitors can also take a train to Chumphon and then take a taxi or bus to Lang Suan.

How to Get Around in Lang Suan

Visitors can explore the town on foot or by renting a bicycle or motorcycle.

Where to Go Next from Lang Suan: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Chumphon - A bustling city that is located approximately 50 km from Lang Suan.
  • Koh Tao - A small island that is popular for its diving and snorkeling opportunities.
  • Surat Thani - This bustling city is located approximately 200 km from Lang Suan and is a hub for travelers heading to the nearby islands.

Conclusion

Lang Suan is a hidden gem that offers visitors a unique and authentic Thai experience. Whether you are exploring its stunning natural wonders or immersing yourself in its rich cultural heritage, Lang Suan is sure to leave a lasting impression.
