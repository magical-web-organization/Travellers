Ko Bulon Leh, a tiny island situated in the Andaman Sea, has recently emerged as a popular tourist destination in Thailand. This beautiful island is known for its pristine beaches, crystal clear waters, marine life, and spectacular sunsets. It’s an ideal destination for travelers seeking peace and tranquility, away from the hustle and bustle of the city. This comprehensive travel guide to Ko Bulon Leh covers everything you need to know to plan your trip.

Introduction to Ko Bulon Leh

Ko Bulon Leh is part of the Koh Libong archipelago and is located in Southern Thailand. The island is famous for its untouched nature, stunning beaches, and crystal clear water. The island is still relatively undiscovered, although tourism is starting to develop in this area.

List of Places to Visit in Ko Bulon Leh

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

There is limited historical and cultural significance on the island, but there are a few sites to visit to learn about the local culture. One is the wooden mosque that was built on the island in the early part of the 20th century. Visitors can also visit the village and interact with the local people to get a glimpse of their way of life.

Natural Attractions of Ko Bulon Leh: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Ko Bulon Leh is an ideal place for outdoor enthusiasts, offering a range of activities such as snorkeling, scuba diving, and kayaking. The beaches on the island are stunning, with crystal clear water and white sandy beaches. Visitors can also enjoy hiking trails that lead to scenic viewpoints on the island, with panoramic views of the stunning natural surroundings.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Ko Bulon Leh

There are plenty of trails on the island. Some of the popular hikes include the trail to Koh Culture Viewpoint, a ten-minute climb from the beach that offers incredible views of the surrounding islands. Another popular hike is the trail to the southern tip of the island, where hikers can enjoy the breathtaking vistas of the Andaman Sea.

Best Things to Do in Ko Bulon Leh: Activities and Tours

Apart from the activities mentioned above, visitors can explore the island on foot, bike or boat. You can rent a boat and explore the surrounding islands, with stunning views of the turquoise waters and wildlife. Visitors can also join a guided tour that includes a visit to the nearby Koh Hin Ngam island, known for its black stones that cover the entire island.

Week-Long Itinerary for Ko Bulon Leh

Day 1: Arrive in Ko Bulon Leh and check into the hotel. Day 2: Enjoy a full day of water activities like snorkeling, diving, and kayaking. Day 3: Take a hike along the nature trails and enjoy scenic views of the island. Day 4: Visit the Wooden Mosque and the village to learn about the local culture. Day 5: Rent a boat and visit the surrounding islands. Day 6: Explore Koh Hin Ngam, known for its black stones. Day 7: Depart from Ko Bulon Leh.

Food and Drink Scene of Ko Bulon Leh

Must-Try Dishes

Ko Bulon Leh is known for its fantastic seafood, especially squid and fish. Other local delicacies that you can try include coconut-based curries, fried rice, and fresh fruits.

Local Beverages

The island is known for its coconut water. You can also indulge in refreshing cocktails and drinks made with local fruits.

Where to Stay in Ko Bulon Leh: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Ko Bulon Leh is home to several budget-friendly accommodations, guesthouses, and hotels. You can also find camping sites on the island for a unique experience.

What to Buy in Ko Bulon Leh: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Some of the popular souvenirs include handmade beachwear, seashell jewelry, handicrafts, and dried fruit from the island.

Local Culture and Customs of Ko Bulon Leh

The local people of Ko Bulon Leh belong to the Chao Leh tribe. These people are known for their unique way of life, which is heavily influenced by the sea. Visitors should dress modestly while visiting the island and respect local customs and traditions.

Best Time to Visit Ko Bulon Leh

The best time to visit Ko Bulon Leh is between November and February, when the weather is dry, and the temperatures are pleasant. Visitors should avoid traveling during the monsoon season (May to October), when rainfall and storms are common.

Do’s and Don’ts of Ko Bulon Leh

Do:

  • Dress modestly and respect local customs and traditions.
  • Carry a map while hiking.
  • Use sunblock to protect your skin from the sun.
  • Try the local cuisine.

Don’t:

  • Litter on the island.
  • Touch or disturb the marine life.
  • Wear shoes inside the mosque.

Safety and Health

Safety in Ko Bulon Leh

Ko Bulon Leh is generally a safe place to visit, with low crime rates. However, visitors should always keep an eye on their belongings and avoid carrying large amounts of cash or valuables.

Health in Ko Bulon Leh

Visitors should avoid drinking tap water and consume only bottled or filtered water. They should also take necessary precautions against mosquito bites and carry mosquito repellent.

Getting to Ko Bulon Leh: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Ko Bulon Leh

The nearest airport to Ko Bulon Leh is Trang Airport. From Trang Airport, visitors can take a taxi to the Pak Meng Pier and then take a ferry to the island.

Bus Stations Near Ko Bulon Leh

Visitors can take a bus from Bangkok to Hat Yai. From Hat Yai, they can take a bus or taxi to the Pak Meng Pier and then take a ferry to the island.

Other Options

Visitors can also take a ferry from the Koh Lanta island to Ko Bulon Leh.

How to Get Around in Ko Bulon Leh

The island is small, and visitors can explore it on foot. Visitors can also rent a bike or a motorbike to explore the island.

Where to Go Next from Ko Bulon Leh: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Some of the nearby attractions include Koh Lipe, Koh Ngai, and Koh Tarutao, all of which offer similar natural beauty and an unspoiled atmosphere.

Conclusion

Ko Bulon Leh is a hidden gem in Southern Thailand, offering visitors a unique travel experience. The island’s stunning natural beauty, abundance of outdoor activities, and local culture make it an ideal destination for travelers seeking adventure and tranquility. With this comprehensive travel guide to Ko Bulon Leh, visitors can plan their trip with ease and make the most of their time on the island.
