Ko Tarutao is a remote and unspoiled island located in Southern Andaman Coast, Thailand. From pristine beaches and crystal-clear waters to cultural and historical attractions, there is something for everyone to see and do on this island paradise. This comprehensive travel guide to Ko Tarutao will help you plan your visit and make the most of your time in this beautiful and fascinating destination.

Introduction to Ko Tarutao

Ko Tarutao is an island located in the Tarutao National Marine Park, which consists of 51 islands close to the Thailand-Malaysia border. The island is situated in Southern Andaman Coast and is known for its stunning natural beauty, including lush rainforests, pristine beaches, and crystal-clear waters. The island has a rich history and was once used as a prison, but today, it is a popular tourist destination for people seeking natural beauty, adventure, and relaxation.

List of Places to Visit in Ko Tarutao

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  1. Tarutao Prison Museum: This museum was once used as a prison and is now a popular attraction, showcasing the history of the island and its role in the past.
  2. Lighthouse: The lighthouse on Ko Tarutao offers spectacular views of the surrounding sea and islands.

Natural Attractions of Ko Tarutao: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  1. Beaches: Ko Tarutao has many beautiful beaches to explore, including Ao Son, Adang Beach, and Sunset Beach.
  2. Snorkeling and Diving: The crystal-clear waters around the island provide excellent opportunities for snorkeling and diving, with many species of marine life to see.
  3. Jungle Trekking: The jungle on Ko Tarutao offers an incredible hiking experience, with challenging trails and stunning views.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Ko Tarutao

  1. Tarutao National Park: Hike through the lush rainforest and explore the beautiful wildlife on the island.
  2. Bike through the island and enjoy the stunning views of the beaches and wildlife.

Best Things to Do in Ko Tarutao: Activities and Tours

  1. Island Hopping Tour: Take a boat tour and explore the surrounding islands and their natural beauty.
  2. Snorkeling and Diving Tour: Take an organized tour and explore the rich underwater world around Ko Tarutao.

Food and Drink Scene of Ko Tarutao

Must-Try Dishes

  1. Spicy Seafood Soup: A spicy and delicious seafood soup popular on the island.
  2. Pad Thai: A classic Thai dish available everywhere in Thailand, served with noodles and various toppings.

Local Beverages

  1. Thai Iced Tea: A popular beverage in Thailand, made from tea, milk, and sugar.
  2. Coconut Water: Fresh coconut water is readily available on the island and provides a refreshing drink during hot days.

Where to Stay in Ko Tarutao: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  1. Tarutao National Park Headquarters: Accommodations within the National Park, spectacular sea views with breakfast facilities and many surrounding activities.
  2. Tarutao Resort: The resort is located in the seaside area of Ko Tarutao, offering a stunning sea view and ample facilities and amenities to the guests.

What to Buy in Ko Tarutao: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  1. Handmade Crafts: Artifacts and mementos are crafted by local artisans and are perfect souvenirs to bring home.
  2. Seafood: The fresh seafood from the island is an excellent gift to take home for friends and family.

Local Culture and Customs of Ko Tarutao

Ko Tarutao has a rich and unique culture that is influenced by its history and location. Visitors should respect local customs and dress modestly when visiting religious sites. It is also customary to remove shoes before entering temples and private homes.

Best Time to Visit Ko Tarutao

The best time to visit Ko Tarutao is during the dry season from November to February. The weather is pleasant and ideal for outdoor activities and exploring the island’s beauty. It is not ideal to visit during July or August, which is the rainy season, as the heavy rain and strong winds can hinder travel to the island.

Do’s and Don’ts of Ko Tarutao

Do:

  • Respect the cultural and religious customs of the local people.
  • Carry cash, as most places on the island do not accept credit cards.

Don’t:

  • Litter or damage the environment while exploring the island.
  • Visit restricted areas in the Tarutao National Park.

Safety and Health

Safety in Ko Tarutao

Ko Tarutao is generally safe for tourists. However, visitors should take standard precautions, such as being aware of their surroundings and not leaving valuables unattended on the beach.

Health in Ko Tarutao

Visitors should take precautions against mosquito bites as Thailand is known for mosquito-borne diseases. It is also advisable to bring sunscreen, as the sun can be intense during the day.

Getting to Ko Tarutao: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Ko Tarutao

The closest airports to Ko Tarutao are Hat Yai International Airport and Langkawi International Airport in Malaysia.

Bus Stations Near Ko Tarutao

The nearest bus station is in Satun, which is about a 45-minute drive from Ko Tarutao.

Other Options

Visitors can also take a ferry from various locations in Thailand or Malaysia to reach Ko Tarutao.

How to Get Around in Ko Tarutao

The island is not large, and visitors can explore it on foot or by renting bicycles. Visitors can also take a boat tour to explore the surrounding islands.

Where to Go Next from Ko Tarutao: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  1. Satun: A laid-back town on the mainland, located 50 kilometers from Ko Tarutao, offering a range of culturally significant attractions.
  2. Langkawi: A nearby Malaysian island popular for its beaches and duty-free shopping.

Conclusion

Ko Tarutao is a hidden gem with stunning natural beauty, cultural landmarks, and exciting adventures. This comprehensive travel guide will help you plan your visit to this unique island and make the most of your time exploring everything it has to offer. Remember to take standard precautions, respect local customs, and enjoy your trip to Ko Tarutao.
