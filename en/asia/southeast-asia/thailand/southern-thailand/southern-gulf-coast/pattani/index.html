A Comprehensive Travel Guide to Pattani, Thailand

Located in Southern Gulf Coast, Pattani is a hidden gem of Thailand. It is a province rich in history, culture, and natural beauty. The town is surrounded by mountains that offer scenic views, and the rivers that flow adds to its beauty. This travel guide will provide all the essential information required for planning a trip to Pattani.

List of Places to Visit in Pattani

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  1. Pattani Central Mosque: It is one of the most significant mosques in Thailand, built-in 1954. The mosque is famous for its intricate designs and is a unique blend of Malaysian and Middle Eastern architecture.
  2. The Pattani Museum: A historical museum with exhibits about the history of the Pattani province, including its Malay roots and the different rulers who once governed the area.
  3. The Khao Nam Khang National Park: A beautiful natural park located at the border of Pattani and Narathiwat province. The park has many hiking trails, waterfalls, and scenic viewpoints.
  4. The Waeng Historical Market: A traditional street market that still retains its charm and authenticity, with stalls selling artisanal foods, desserts, and cultural handicrafts.

Natural Attractions of Pattani: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  1. The Budo-Su-ngai Padi National Park: A beautiful park located in the neighboring Narathiwat province, known for its waterfalls, hot springs, and diverse wildlife.
  2. The Yaring Mangrove Forest: A beautiful natural habitat that is home to many bird species and marine animals.
  3. The Sai Khao Waterfall: A beautiful waterfall located in the Yaring district.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Pattani

Pattani offers many hiking and biking routes for outdoor enthusiasts. The Khao Nam Khang National Park is the most popular destination for hiking. Here are some of the best hiking trails in Pattani:

  1. Khao Nam Khang Mountain Trail: A 7 kilometers trek to the top of the Khao Nam Khang mountain, which takes about 3-4 hours to complete.
  2. Bangpu Nature Trail: A 4 kilometers trek amidst nature that takes about 2 hours to complete.

Best Things to Do in Pattani: Activities and Tours

  1. Visit the Pattani Central Mosque and learn about its rich Islamic heritage.
  2. Take a tour of the Pattani Museum and learn about the history of the province.
  3. Explore the Waeng Historical Market and indulge in some cultural handicrafts and culinary delights.
  4. Go trekking in the Khao Nam Khang National Park and witness the natural beauty up close.

Week-Long Itinerary for Pattani

Here is a week-long itinerary for Pattani covering all essential highlights:

Day 1: Visit the Pattani museum and explore the city center Day 2: Go on a nature trail to the Khao Nam Khang National Park Day 3: Explore the Waeng Historical Market and indulge in some local delicacies Day 4: Take a tour of the Pattani Central Mosque and visit the surrounding temples Day 5: Take a day trip to the nearby Budo-Su-ngai Padi National Park and witness the waterfalls. Day 6: Visit the Yaring Mangrove Forest Reserve and indulge in some bird watching. Day 7: Relax and unwind at Sai Khao Waterfall or take a cycling tour to the nearby villages.

Food and Drink Scene of Pattani

Pattani is famous for its food scene, and it offers a wide range of culinary delights. Here are some of the must-try dishes that you shouldn’t miss:

Must-Try Dishes

  1. Khao Mok Gai: A traditional chicken biryani dish with local spices.
  2. Roti Saimai: A dessert that comprises strands of cotton candy wrapped in crispy crepe-like pastry.
  3. Khanom Jeen: A dish that has thin rice noodles served with a spicy fish or chicken curry sauce.

Local Beverages

  1. Teh Tarik: A tea drink made by pulling the tea between two containers to produce a frothy mixture.
  2. Cha Yen: A sweet and creamy Thai iced tea made with condensed milk and spices.
  3. Nam Manao: A refreshing lemonade that is famous in Thailand.

Where to Stay in Pattani: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Pattani offers a range of accommodation options for every budget. Here are some of the best options:

  1. The Park Hotel Pattani: A luxury hotel located in the city center offering excellent service and amenities.
  2. The Uncle Resort Pattani: A mid-budget hotel with clean and comfortable rooms and excellent service.
  3. The Siam Hotel Pattani: A budget hotel located in the prime location of the Waeng District.

What to Buy in Pattani: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Pattani offers a range of local products and souvenirs that you can bring home. Here are some of the options:

  1. Songket Clothes: Handwoven Malay traditional clothes that are vibrant and colorful.
  2. Batik Cloth: A unique cloth that is made by hand painting designs on cotton or silk.
  3. Bang Pu Honey: The honey produced by the bees in Bang Pu offers a unique flavor that is worth trying.

Local Culture and Customs of Pattani

Pattani has a unique Malay-Muslim culture that is worth exploring. The people in this province are warm and hospitable. It’s advisable to dress conservatively and show respect to the local customs and traditions.

Best Time to Visit Pattani

The best time to visit Pattani is from November to March. During this period, the weather is dry with pleasant temperatures and perfect for outdoor activities.

Do’s and Don’ts of Pattani

Do:

  1. Dress conservatively and show respect to the local customs and traditions.
  2. Learn some basic Malay phrases and greetings.
  3. Try the local cuisine and delicacies.

Don’t:

  1. Criticize the local customs and traditions.
  2. Dress inappropriately, especially when visiting religious places.
  3. Engage in illegal activities.

Safety and Health

Safety in Pattani

Pattani is generally safe for tourists. However, it is advisable to stay away from the southern border area, where there have been safety concerns in the past.

Health in Pattani

Pattani has an excellent healthcare system, and medical facilities are readily available. It’s advisable to carry personal medical insurance in case of any medical emergencies.

Getting to Pattani: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Pattani

The nearest international airports to Pattani are:

  1. Hat Yai International Airport
  2. Narathiwat Airport

Bus Stations Near Pattani

The primary bus station in Pattani is the Pattani Bus Station, which offers frequent services to nearby cities.

Other Options

You can also reach Pattani by train from Bangkok. The nearest railway station to Pattani is the Thepa railway station, from where you can take a local bus to Pattani.

How to Get Around in Pattani

The most convenient way to get around Pattani is by hiring a local taxi or renting a motorbike. Local buses are also available but may not be the most comfortable mode of transportation.

Where to Go Next from Pattani: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Nearby destinations and excursions from Pattani that you shouldn’t miss include:

  1. Narathiwat: Visit the Budo-Su-Ngai Padi park and the beautiful mountains in Narathiwat.
  2. Hat Yai: The bustling city of Hat Yai is just a few hours away and offers many shopping and entertainment options.
  3. Songkhla: Visit the Songkhla Lake and explore the historical sites and temples in Songkhla.

Conclusion

Pattani is a beautiful province that offers something for everyone. From its rich history to its natural beauty, Pattani is a destination worth visiting in Thailand. Use this travel guide to plan your trip efficiently and make the most of your time in Pattani.
