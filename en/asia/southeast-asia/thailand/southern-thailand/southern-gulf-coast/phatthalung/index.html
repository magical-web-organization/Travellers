Introduction to Phatthalung

Phatthalung is a charming small town located in Southern Thailand, known for its natural beauty, rich culture, and historical significance. Situated between the two popular tourist destinations of Songkhla and Nakhon Si Thammarat, Phatthalung is often overlooked by visitors to Thailand. However, it is a hidden gem that deserves to be explored by those who want to experience a tranquil and unspoiled side of Thailand.

List of Places to Visit in Phatthalung

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Phatthalung has a rich history and culture, which is reflected in its numerous temples and historical sites. Some of the top highlights include:

  • Phatthalung National Museum: This museum provides visitors with an overview of the history, culture, and traditions of the Phatthalung region. It displays a collection of artifacts, including ancient pottery, coins, and Buddha images.

  • Wat Khuha Sawan: This temple is built inside a limestone cave, and features a large reclining Buddha statue. Visitors can climb up to the cave to enjoy stunning views of the surrounding area.

  • Khao Chaison Hot Springs: These hot springs are believed to have healing properties, and offer visitors a chance to relax in the natural pools surrounded by lush green forest.

Natural Attractions of Phatthalung: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Phatthalung is blessed with stunning landscapes, including mountains, lakes, and waterfalls. Some of the best outdoor attractions include:

  • Khao Ok Thalu: This iconic limestone mountain is the symbol of Phatthalung, and offers visitors a challenging but rewarding trek with panoramic views of the surrounding landscape.

  • Thale Noi: This scenic lake is part of a protected wildlife reserve, and is home to a variety of bird species, including the rare white-eyed river martin. Visitors can take a boat tour to explore the lake and spot the birds.

  • Ton Nga Chang Waterfall: This picturesque waterfall is located in the Khao Pu-Khao Ya National Park, and is surrounded by lush jungle. Visitors can swim in the waterfall’s cool pools and enjoy the natural beauty.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Phatthalung

Phatthalung is a great destination for outdoor enthusiasts, with plenty of hiking and biking routes to explore. Some of the top trails include:

  • Khao Ok Thalu Trail: This is a challenging trek up the iconic mountain of Phatthalung, and offers stunning views of the surrounding area.

  • Tham Khao Ok Thalu Route: This is an easier trek that leads visitors to the Khao Ok Thalu cave, which is filled with unique rock formations and stalactites.

  • Tham Chomphon Route: This is a scenic biking route that takes visitors through forests, past lakes, and offers breathtaking views of the mountains in the distance.

Best Things to Do in Phatthalung: Activities and Tours

Apart from exploring the natural and cultural attractions, visitors to Phatthalung can also enjoy the following activities:

  • Try the local specialty: Khanom La (steamed rice cakes). These delicious snacks can be found in local markets and are a must-try when visiting Phatthalung.

  • Attend a local festival: Phatthalung is known for its colorful festivals, such as the Phatthalung Red Cross Fair, which takes place every year in March.

  • Take a cooking class: Learn how to prepare traditional Thai dishes with local ingredients in a small group setting.

Week-Long Itinerary for Phatthalung

Day 1: Explore Phatthalung Town and visit the Phatthalung National Museum to learn about the history and culture of the region.

Day 2: Trek up Khao Ok Thalu and enjoy the stunning views from the top.

Day 3: Visit the Thale Noi bird reserve and take a boat tour to spot rare bird species.

Day 4: Relax in Khao Chaison Hot Springs and enjoy the healing properties of the natural pools.

Day 5: Take a cooking class and learn how to make traditional Thai dishes.

Day 6: Cycle around the scenic Tham Chomphon Route and enjoy the natural beauty of Phatthalung.

Day 7: Visit the Ton Nga Chang Waterfall, and enjoy a refreshing swim in the cool pools.

Food and Drink Scene of Phatthalung

Must-Try Dishes

Phatthalung has its own unique cuisine, which includes the following must-try dishes:

  • Khanom La (steamed rice cakes): These delicious snacks are made by mixing rice flour with coconut milk and steaming the mixture in banana leaf cups.

  • Tom Kha Gai: This creamy coconut soup with chicken and fragrant Thai herbs is a staple in Southern Thai cuisine.

  • Gaeng Som Pla Thapthim (Sour Curry with Red Snapper): This tangy and spicy curry is made with fresh red snapper and a sour tamarind soup base.

Local Beverages

When in Phatthalung, be sure to try the following local drinks:

  • Cha Yen (Thai Iced Tea): This sweetened tea is made with black tea, condensed milk, and sugar, and is a popular drink in Thailand.

  • Nam Tan Yen (Iced Brown Sugar Drink): This refreshing beverage is made with brown sugar, pandan leaves, and lime juice, and is a perfect antidote to the heat of the Thai sun.

Where to Stay in Phatthalung: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Phatthalung offers a variety of budget-friendly accommodation options, including hotels, hostels, and guesthouses. Some of the best places to stay include:

  • The Field Home Hostel: This cozy hostel offers affordable private and dormitory rooms, and is a great place to meet other travelers.

  • Na Kon Cha Amnart Hotel: This modern hotel is located in the heart of Phatthalung town, and features comfortable rooms and a rooftop restaurant.

  • Baan Sakthong Resort: This peaceful resort is situated by a lake, and offers guests a chance to relax and enjoy the natural beauty of Phatthalung.

What to Buy in Phatthalung: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

When in Phatthalung, be sure to pick up the following unique souvenirs and local products:

  • Handwoven fabrics and textiles: These beautiful fabrics are made by local artisans, and are known for their intricate designs and bright colors.

  • Khon Khanom La (steamed rice cakes mold): Bring home your very own Khanom La mold to recreate the traditional Thai snack at home.

Local Culture and Customs of Phatthalung

Phatthalung has a rich and vibrant culture, which is influenced by its unique location at the crossroads of different ethnic minorities. Visitors should keep the following cultural customs and etiquette in mind:

  • Remove shoes before entering temples and homes.

  • Show respect for the royal family and Buddhism.

  • Do not touch people’s heads or use your feet to point at objects.

Best Time to Visit Phatthalung

The best time to visit Phatthalung is between November and February, when the weather is cool and dry. The rainy season lasts from May to October, and can make outdoor activities more challenging.

Do’s and Don’ts of Phatthalung

Do:

  • Try the local cuisine and drinks.

  • Respect the local customs and traditions.

  • Explore the natural and cultural attractions.

Don’t:

  • Litter or damage the environment.

  • Disrespect Buddhism or the royal family.

  • Bargain too aggressively in markets.

Safety and Health

Safety in Phatthalung

Phatthalung is generally a safe destination for travelers, with low crime rates and a friendly local population. However, visitors should take the following precautions:

  • Avoid traveling alone at night.

  • Keep valuables in a secure location.

  • Be aware of the risks of dengue fever and take necessary precautions.

Health in Phatthalung

Phatthalung has modern medical facilities and trained medical staff. Visitors should take the following health precautions:

  • Drink only bottled water and avoid ice in drinks.

  • Use insect repellent and wear long clothing to avoid mosquito bites.

  • Get vaccinated for Hepatitis A and B, and Typhoid.

Getting to Phatthalung: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Phatthalung

Phuket International Airport and Hat Yai International Airport are the closest international airports to Phatthalung, both located approximately 120 km away.

Bus Stations Near Phatthalung

Phatthalung Bus Station is located in the town center, and offers regular services to other destinations in Thailand, including Bangkok, Krabi, and Hat Yai.

Other Options

Private taxis, rental cars, and motorbikes are also available for hire in Phatthalung.

How to Get Around in Phatthalung

Phatthalung is a small town that can easily be explored on foot or by bicycle. Taxis and motorbikes are also available for hire for those wishing to explore the surrounding areas and attractions.

Where to Go Next from Phatthalung: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Phatthalung is located in Southern Thailand, and is surrounded by many other popular destinations that are worth exploring, including:

  • Songkhla: This coastal city is known for its beautiful beaches, historic temples, and delicious seafood.

  • Nakhon Si Thammarat: This ancient city is famous for its ornate temples, bustling night markets, and traditional handicrafts.

  • Krabi: This famous beach destination is known for its stunning landscape of limestone cliffs, azure waters, and powdery white sand.

Conclusion

Phatthalung is a hidden gem in Southern Thailand, offering visitors a chance to experience natural beauty, rich culture, and history, without the crowds of other tourist destinations. Whether you’re an adventurous outdoor enthusiast, a culture lover, or just looking for a peaceful escape from the hustle and bustle of city life, Phatthalung is the perfect destination.
