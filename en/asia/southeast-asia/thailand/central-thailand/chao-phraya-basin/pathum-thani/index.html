Introduction to Pathum Thani

Pathum Thani is a peaceful province located in the central region of Thailand. The province is just over 40 kilometers north of Bangkok and is renowned for its rich historical landmarks, cultural heritage, and exciting outdoor activities. Pathum Thani, which means “lotus flower town,” is named after the lotus plant that thrives in the province’s many waterways.

The province is home to friendly locals, picturesque landscapes, and serene temples. With a balance of tradition and modernity, Pathum Thani is a fantastic destination for those seeking a glimpse at authentic Thai culture. In this guide, we will take you on a journey to discover the best places to visit, food to try, and adventures to embark on in Pathum Thani.

List of Places to Visit in Pathum Thani

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Wat Hong Thong

Wat Hong Thong is a beautiful temple located in Pathum Thani’s Bang Kadi district. This ancient temple is one of the oldest in the province, dating back over 600 years. The temple has a unique architectural style and features intricate carvings and decorations. The impressive 28-meter-long reclining Buddha is the main highlight of Wat Hong Thong.

Wat Chedi Thong

Another temple that is worth a visit in Pathum Thani is Wat Chedi Thong. The name of the temple, which means “Golden Stupa,” is given due to the striking golden spire that stands at the center of the temple complex. The temple features a large prayer hall and a beautiful courtyard.

Pathum Thani Cultural Centre

Pathum Thani Cultural Centre is a fantastic place to learn more about the province’s rich cultural heritage. The center showcases traditional Thai dance performances, traditional music, and exhibitions of local handicrafts. Visitors can also attend workshops on handicraft making and traditional cooking.

Ayutthaya Historical Park

Ayutthaya Historical Park is a UNESCO World Heritage Site located just a short distance from Pathum Thani. The park is a fascinating place to explore and discover Thailand’s ancient history. It is home to over 400 temples and ruins that date back to the Ayutthaya period, providing an insight into the country’s past.

Natural Attractions of Pathum Thani: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Khlong Luang Arboretum

Khlong Luang Arboretum is a botanic garden located in Khlong Luang District. The garden is known for its collection of impressive trees, plants, and orchids. Visitors can take a leisurely stroll around the garden’s winding paths, taking in the beautiful landscape, or visit the on-site butterfly garden.

Dream World

Dream World is an amusement park located in Thanyaburi District and is perfect for families visiting Pathum Thani. The park offers a wide range of thrilling rides, from roller coasters to water rides. Visitors can also enjoy a variety of shows, including a high-energy parade and a Hollywood stunt show.

Khao Yai National Park

Khao Yai National Park is located just a few hours’ drive from Pathum Thani and is one of Thailand’s most famous national parks. The park is known for its diverse range of wildlife, including elephants, tigers, and bears, and offers visitors the opportunity to explore the lush jungle on adventurous hikes.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Pathum Thani

Khao Yai National Park Hiking Trail

The hiking trail at Khao Yai National Park is a must-do for adventure lovers visiting Pathum Thani. The trail is approximately six kilometers long and takes about three to four hours to complete. The trail takes visitors through various breathtaking landscapes, including lush jungle, sparkling waterfalls, and rolling hills.

King Rama IX Park Bike Trail

The bike trail at King Rama IX Park is a popular destination for cycling enthusiasts visiting Pathum Thani. The trail is approximately 12 kilometers long and takes visitors through picturesque parks, gardens, and a scenic lake. The park also offers bike rentals for those who do not have their own bike.

Best Things to Do in Pathum Thani: Activities and Tours

Thai Cooking Classes

Pathum Thani is an excellent destination for foodies as it boasts a vibrant culinary scene. One activity that visitors can participate in is a traditional Thai cooking class. Many cooking classes offer hands-on experiences, teaching visitors how to cook popular Thai dishes such as pad Thai, green curry, and mango sticky rice.

Floating Market Tours

Pathum Thani’s markets are renowned for their vibrant colors, smells, and sounds. The best way to experience the markets is by taking a floating market tour. Visitors can explore the market in traditional long-tail boats, taking in the hustle and bustle of vendors selling fresh fruit, street food, and local handicrafts.

Food and Drink Scene of Pathum Thani

Must-Try Dishes

Tom Yum Goong

Tom Yum Goong is one of Thailand’s most famous dishes and a must-try when visiting Pathum Thani. The spicy and sour soup is made with lemongrass, kaffir lime leaves, galangal, chili peppers, and prawns.

Khao Soi

Khao Soi is a rich, aromatic curry-like dish that is made with egg noodles, coconut milk, and various spices such as curry powder, turmeric, and coriander. The dish is often topped with crispy noodles and served with lime and pickled vegetables.

Local Beverages

Cha Yen

Cha Yen, or Thai iced tea, is a sweet, milky tea that is a favorite in Thailand. The tea is made with black tea leaves, condensed milk, and sugar, giving it its characteristic creamy texture and sweet taste.

Singha Beer

Singha Beer is Thailand’s most popular beer and is brewed locally. The beer is light, refreshing, and goes well with Thai food.

Where to Stay in Pathum Thani: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

The Idle Residence

The Idle Residence is a boutique hotel located in the heart of Pathum Thani. The hotel has a contemporary design and features spacious and stylish rooms, a rooftop pool, and a restaurant serving delicious local and international cuisine.

Pinehurst Golf Club and Hotel

Pinehurst Golf Club and Hotel is an excellent accommodation option for golf enthusiasts visiting Pathum Thani. The hotel has an 18-hole golf course, an outdoor pool, and a restaurant serving traditional Thai cuisine.

What to Buy in Pathum Thani: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Handicrafts

Pathum Thani is renowned for its intricate handicrafts, including wooden carvings, woven textiles, and ceramics. Visitors can find a wide range of handicrafts at the local markets and shops, making for memorable souvenirs.

Thai Snacks

Thai snacks are a must-try when visiting Pathum Thani. Some of the most popular Thai snacks include crispy coconut rolls, sweet mango sticky rice, and spicy green papaya salad. Visitors can find a variety of snacks at the local markets and shops.

Local Culture and Customs of Pathum Thani

Pathum Thani is a province steeped in tradition and is home to a friendly and welcoming community. Some of the customs include paying respect to elders, removing one’s shoes before entering a home or temple, and dressing modestly when visiting temples.

Best Time to Visit Pathum Thani

The best time to visit Pathum Thani is between November and February when the weather is mild and dry. The province experiences a hot and humid climate throughout most of the year, making it uncomfortable for outdoor activities.

Do’s and Don’ts of Pathum Thani

Do:

  • Dress modestly when visiting temples
  • Remove shoes when entering a home or temple
  • Learn some basic Thai phrases

Don’t:

  • Disrespect religious customs and traditions
  • Touch someone’s head (considered impolite in Thai culture)
  • Raise one’s voice in public

Safety and Health

Safety in Pathum Thani

Pathum Thani is a safe destination for travelers. However, visitors should take care of their belongings when in crowded areas and avoid walking alone at night.

Health in Pathum Thani

Visitors should drink bottled water and take precautions against mosquito bites to avoid contracting diseases such as dengue fever or malaria.

Getting to Pathum Thani: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Pathum Thani

The nearest airport to Pathum Thani is Suvarnabhumi Airport, which is approximately 30 kilometers away. From the airport, visitors can take a taxi or arrange for private transportation to Pathum Thani.

Bus Stations Near Pathum Thani

There are several bus stations in Bangkok that provide transportation to Pathum Thani, including the Mo Chit Bus Terminal and the Northern Bus Terminal.

Other Options

Visitors can also take a train from Bangkok’s Hualamphong Station to Pathum Thani.

How to Get Around in Pathum Thani

Visitors can travel around Pathum Thani by taking tuk-tuks or taxis. Rental cars and motorcycles are also available for those who prefer to travel independently.

Where to Go Next from Pathum Thani: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Bangkok

Bangkok is just a short distance from Pathum Thani and is a fantastic destination for those seeking vibrant nightlife, colorful markets, and impressive temples.

Ayutthaya

Ayutthaya, located just a few hours’ drive from Pathum Thani, is another UNESCO World Heritage Site and offers visitors a glimpse at ancient Thai culture and history.

Conclusion

Pathum Thani is a charming destination that offers a diverse range of adventure activities, cultural landmarks, and a rich food and drink scene. The province’s laid-back atmosphere and friendly locals make it a fantastic place for travelers seeking an authentic Thai experience. With this comprehensive travel guide, visitors can plan an unforgettable trip filled with adventures and memories to last a lifetime.
