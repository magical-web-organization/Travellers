A Comprehensive Travel Guide to Samut Prakan, Thailand

Located in the Chao Phraya Basin in Central Thailand, Samut Prakan is a hidden gem offering visitors a chance to explore both natural and cultural attractions. If you’re planning a trip to Thailand, make sure Samut Prakan is on your itinerary. Our comprehensive travel guide will help you plan the perfect trip.

Introduction to Samut Prakan

Samut Prakan is a province that borders Bangkok, which makes it perfectly situated for visitors who want to explore Thailand’s capital city but want to experience something different as well. It’s a quiet, laid-back area that is perfect for those who want to escape the hustle and bustle and enjoy a more relaxing trip.

List of Places to Visit in Samut Prakan

When it comes to places to visit in Samut Prakan, you won’t be disappointed. Here are some of the most popular destinations:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Erawan Museum: A three-headed elephant statue that houses antique collection of Thai artifacts and art.
  • Bangpu Recreation Center: A wetland park with boardwalks, green areas and places to sit and enjoy beautiful views of migratory birds.
  • Phra Samut Chedi: A beautiful temple situated on an island, accessible only by boat

Natural Attractions of Samut Prakan: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Bang Namphueng Floating Market: A quiet market situated on a river with traditional local food, crafts and souvenirs.
  • Bang Krachao: Known as the “Green Lung” of Bangkok, this is the place for cycling and sightseeing. Rent a bike and enjoy the peaceful scenery.
  • Muang Boran (Ancient City): A Thai outdoors museum that showcases the traditional architecture of Thailand’s cities, temples, palaces, etc.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Samut Prakan

If you’re looking to get outdoors and explore, Samut Prakan has a lot to offer. Here are some of the best hiking and biking routes in the area:

  • Bang Krachao Island: With its enchanting natural beauty and serene atmosphere, Bang Krachao is a haven for nature lovers.
  • Suvarnabhumi Cycling Route: A 23-kilometer track that takes cyclists on an exploration of Thai architecture and culture.

Best Things to Do in Samut Prakan: Activities and Tours

Samut Prakan has an array of activities and tours on offer:

  • Experience Thai Culture: Learn Thai cooking, Thai dance and music or traditional fighting methods.
  • Explore the River: Take a boat tour of Chao Phraya River and enjoy the scenic beauty along its banks.
  • Shop Local: Shop for local crafts, foods, and souvenirs in the various markets around the province.

Week-Long Itinerary for Samut Prakan

Here’s a suggested itinerary for a week-long trip to Samut Prakan:

Day Activity
Day 1 Visit the Erawan Museum and then go to Bang Namphueng Floating Market
Day 2 Explore Bang Krachao Island on a bike tour
Day 3 Spend a day at Bangpu Recreation Center and then visit Phra Samut Chedi
Day 4 Take a boat tour along the Chao Phraya River
Day 5 Visit Muang Boran (Ancient City)
Day 6 Learn about Thai culture through cooking/dance or fighting lessons
Day 7 Shop for local crafts and souvenirs at various markets in Samut Prakan

Food and Drink Scene of Samut Prakan

When it comes to food and drink in Samut Prakan, you’ll find a mix of traditional Thai cuisine as well as influences from other cultures.

Must-Try Dishes

  • Pad Thai: A classic stir-fried noodle dish that’s a staple in Thai cuisine.
  • Tom Yum Goong: A spicy and sour soup featuring shrimp as the main ingredient.
  • Green Curry: A creamy and spicy curry dish with meat and veggies cooked in coconut milk.

Local Beverages

  • Cha Yen: A Thai iced tea made with condensed milk and black tea.
  • Chao Phraya: A beer brewed in Thailand.
  • Nam Manao: A refreshing lime soda drink that’s popular in Thailand.

Where to Stay in Samut Prakan: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Samut Prakan has a range of accommodation options to choose from, depending on your budget and preferences. Here are some of our top picks:

  • The Idle Residence: A boutique hotel with a modern design and a great location.
  • Samutprakan Hotel: An affordable hotel with basic amenities, perfect for budget travelers.
  • Baan Jantra Place: A guesthouse with a traditional Thai design and warm hospitality.

What to Buy in Samut Prakan: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

If you’re looking for the perfect souvenirs or gifts to bring back from Samut Prakan, we recommend the following local products:

  • Thai Silk: Known for its lustrous texture and vibrant colors, Thai silk is a must-buy.
  • Thai Handicrafts: From traditional masks to statues, Thai handicrafts make for unique gifts.
  • Spices and Herbs: Spice up your cooking with fresh herbs and spices from the local markets.

Local Culture and Customs of Samut Prakan

Samut Prakan has a rich cultural heritage with traditions deeply rooted in Buddhism. Some local customs to be aware of when visiting include:

  • Dress Modestly: Dress modestly when visiting temples and other places of worship.
  • Remove Shoes: When entering homes, temples, and other buildings, remove your shoes before stepping in.
  • Show Respect: Thai people are very respectful of their elders, monarchy and the Buddha. Try to show proper respect (such as not pointing your feet at people or statues).

Best Time to Visit Samut Prakan

The best time to visit Samut Prakan is from November to February, when temperatures are at their lowest, ranging from around 20°C to 30°C. This time of year is also considered the dry season, which means less humidity and fewer mosquitoes.

Do’s and Don’ts of Samut Prakan

When traveling to Samut Prakan, it’s important to keep in mind these do’s and don’ts:

Do:

  • Try local food and drink
  • Engage in cultural activities
  • Respect local customs and traditions

Don’t:

  • Disrespect the Buddha, monarchy, or elders.
  • Take Buddha statues or any other religious items back home as souvenirs.

Safety and Health

When traveling, safety and health are always a priority. Here’s what you need to know when it comes to Samut Prakan:

Safety in Samut Prakan

  • Avoid going out late at night in less-traveled areas.
  • Be aware of pickpockets in crowded areas.
  • Use a reliable taxi or ride-sharing service when traveling.

Health in Samut Prakan

  • Make sure you have all necessary vaccines before traveling.
  • Drink bottled water (or safely filtered and boiled tap water) and avoid eating raw or undercooked food.

Getting to Samut Prakan: Transportation Options and Logistics

There are several ways to get to Samut Prakan from Bangkok and other nearby areas:

Airports Near Samut Prakan

  • Suvarnabhumi Airport in Bangkok is located approximately 16.5 km from Samut Prakan.

Bus Stations Near Samut Prakan

  • Samut Prakan Bus Terminal
  • Eastern Bus Terminal (Ekkamai)

Other Options

  • Taxis and ride-sharing services are available from Bangkok to Samut Prakan.

How to Get Around in Samut Prakan

The best way to get around Samut Prakan is by taxi, renting a motorbike, or taking a bike tour. You can also use public transportation like buses or minivans.

Where to Go Next from Samut Prakan:

If you’re looking to explore more of Thailand, there are several nearby destinations and excursions you can take:

  • Bangkok
  • Ayutthaya
  • Pattaya

Conclusion

Samut Prakan is a destination that should be on every traveler’s list. Whether you’re interested in exploring cultural attractions, enjoying outdoor adventures, or soaking up the local atmosphere, Samut Prakan has something for everyone.
