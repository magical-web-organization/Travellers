Saraburi, located in the Chao Phraya Basin in Central Thailand, is a picturesque province offering an array of natural and cultural attractions. From historical sites and museums to outdoor adventures and scenic views, the province has plenty to offer to visitors. This comprehensive travel guide will help you plan your trip to Saraburi by providing detailed information on the best places to visit, things to do, where to stay, and more.

Introduction to Saraburi

Saraburi is a relatively small province in Thailand, with a population of around 600,000 people. The province is known for its picturesque natural landscapes, including national parks, waterfalls, and mountains.

Saraburi is also rich in history and culture. The province is home to several museums and historical sites, including the Wat Phra Phutthabat Temple and the Saraburi National Museum. The province is also famous for its annual Monkey Buffet Festival, a unique cultural event that attracts visitors from around the world.

List of Places to Visit in Saraburi

Whether you’re interested in history, culture, or nature, Saraburi has something for everyone. Here are some of the top attractions to see during your visit:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Wat Phra Phutthabat Temple: This ancient temple is one of the most important religious sites in Thailand, housing a footprint of the Buddha.
  • Saraburi National Museum: Located in the heart of Saraburi city, this museum houses artifacts and exhibits that showcase the province’s rich history and culture.
  • Phra Buddha Bat Khao Sam Roi Yot National Park: This park is home to a famous Buddha’s footprint, and breathtaking views of the surrounding mountains.

Natural Attractions of Saraburi: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Khao Sam Lan National Park: This park is known for its lush forests, stunning waterfalls, and picturesque mountain trails.
  • Namtok Samlan National Park: This park is home to a series of cascading waterfalls and crystal-clear pools, perfect for swimming and relaxing.
  • Phi Mai Waterfall: This waterfall is the perfect destination for a picnic, with its cool, clear waters and scenic views.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Saraburi

Saraburi is a haven for outdoor enthusiasts, with plenty of hiking and biking trails to explore. Some of the most popular routes include:

  • Khao Yai National Park: This park is home to some of the most beautiful hiking trails in Thailand, with stunning views of the surrounding mountains and forests.
  • Namtok Phlio National Park: This park is located in the eastern part of Saraburi and is ideal for intermediate to advanced hikers.
  • Khao Sam Lan National Park: This park has several trails of varying difficulty levels, perfect for hikers and bikers of all skill levels.

Best Things to Do in Saraburi: Activities and Tours

Aside from the cultural and natural attractions, Saraburi has plenty of activities and tours for visitors, including:

  • The Monkey Buffet Festival: Held every November, this festival is a unique cultural event that attracts visitors from around the world.
  • Hot springs and spas: There are several hot springs and spas in the province where visitors can relax and rejuvenate.
  • Local markets and shopping: Saraburi is home to several local markets, where visitors can find unique souvenirs and local products to bring home.

Week-Long Itinerary for Saraburi

If you’re planning a week-long trip to Saraburi, consider this itinerary:

  • Day 1: Visit Wat Phra Phutthabat Temple and explore Saraburi National Museum.
  • Day 2: Hike in Khao Sam Lan National Park and take in the scenic views.
  • Day 3: Explore the local hot springs and spas for a relaxing day.
  • Day 4: Take a trip to Namtok Phlio National Park for more hiking.
  • Day 5: Enjoy a day of shopping and exploring local markets.
  • Day 6: Visit Phi Mai Waterfall for a picnic and take a dip in the crystal-clear waters.
  • Day 7: Attend the Monkey Buffet Festival and experience the unique local culture.

Food and Drink Scene of Saraburi

Saraburi is known for its delicious food and refreshing beverages. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Khao tom, or rice soup with pork and egg, is a popular breakfast item in Saraburi and is often served with fresh herbs and veggies.
  • Kaeng khiao wan, or green curry with chicken or pork, is a spicy and flavorful dish that’s a staple in Thai cuisine.

Local Beverages

  • Cha yen, or Thai iced tea, is a popular refreshing beverage made from black tea, milk, and sugar.
  • Nam manao, or lime juice, is a refreshing drink that’s perfect for hot summer days.

Where to Stay in Saraburi: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

There are plenty of accommodation options in Saraburi to suit any budget and preference, including hotels, hostels, and guesthouses. Some of the top options include:

  • The Valley Khaoyai by Zen: This luxurious hotel is located in the heart of Khao Yai National Park, surrounded by lush forests and mountains.
  • The Camp Khaoyai: This eco-friendly resort features private glamping tents, offering a unique and comfortable camping experience.
  • Muaklek Health Spa and Resort: This resort is located in the beautiful Muaklek valley and offers a range of treatments and massages.

What to Buy in Saraburi: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

If you’re looking for unique souvenirs and local products to bring home from Saraburi, consider picking up:

  • Handmade pottery and ceramics: Saraburi is known for its beautiful pottery and ceramics, which are sold in local markets and shops.
  • Traditional Thai clothing: You can find traditional Thai clothing, such as sarongs and scarves, in Saraburi’s local markets.
  • Local snacks and sweets: Saraburi is known for its delicious local snacks and sweets, such as roasted peanuts and sugar-coated tamarind.

Local Culture and Customs of Saraburi

When visiting Saraburi, it’s important to respect the local culture and customs. Here are some tips to keep in mind:

  • Dress modestly when visiting temples and other religious sites.
  • Remove your shoes before entering someone’s home or a temple.
  • Be respectful of local customs and traditions.

Best Time to Visit Saraburi

The best time to visit Saraburi is during the cool season, from November to February, when the weather is cool and dry. However, the province can be visited year-round, with monsoon season occurring from May to October.

Do’s and Don’ts of Saraburi

Here are some do’s and don’ts to keep in mind when visiting Saraburi:

Do:

  • Try the local cuisine and beverages.
  • Respect the local culture and traditions.
  • Explore the natural and cultural attractions.

Don’t:

  • Disrespect local customs and traditions.
  • Show up at a temple or religious site inappropriately dressed.
  • Speak loudly or cause a disturbance in public places.

Safety and Health

Saraburi is generally a safe province to visit, with little crime reported against tourists. However, it’s important to take certain precautions, such as:

Safety in Saraburi

  • Be aware when walking at night, and avoid isolated areas.
  • Keep valuables in a safe place, and don’t flash them in public.

Health in Saraburi

  • Drink bottled water, and avoid tap water and ice.
  • Use mosquito repellent to protect against dengue fever and other mosquito-borne illnesses.

Getting to Saraburi: Transportation Options and Logistics

Saraburi is easy to reach from Bangkok, with a variety of transportation options available. Here are some of the best ways to get to Saraburi:

Airports Near Saraburi

  • Don Mueang International Airport: This airport is located in Bangkok and is around a 2-hour drive from Saraburi.
  • Suvarnabhumi Airport: This is the main international airport in Bangkok and is around a 2.5-hour drive from Saraburi.

Bus Stations Near Saraburi

  • Mo Chit Bus Station: This is the main bus station in Bangkok, with buses to Saraburi departing regularly.
  • Saraburi Bus Station: This is the main bus terminal in Saraburi, with buses to other destinations in Thailand.

Other Options

  • Private taxis and car rentals are available from Bangkok to Saraburi.

How to Get Around in Saraburi

It’s easy to get around Saraburi by public transportation, with plenty of options available, including:

  • Tuk-tuks and songthaews: These are the most common forms of public transportation in Saraburi, offering affordable and convenient transportation around the province.
  • Motorbike rentals: Motorbike rentals are available in Saraburi and are a great way to explore the countryside.
  • Private taxis: Private taxis are available for hire, but can be expensive compared to other transportation options.

Where to Go Next from Saraburi: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

If you have extra time in Thailand, consider visiting these nearby destinations and excursions:

  • Khao Yai National Park: This national park is known for its stunning waterfalls, scenic views, and abundant wildlife.
  • Ayutthaya: This ancient city is a UNESCO World Heritage Site and is home to numerous temples and historical sites.
  • Bangkok: The capital city of Thailand is just a few hours’ drive from Saraburi and offers an array of cultural, historical, and entertainment options.

Conclusion

Saraburi, Thailand is a hidden gem that offers a perfect balance of cultural heritage and natural beauty. From visiting ancient temples and museums to exploring national parks and waterfalls, the province has something for visitors of all ages and interests. This comprehensive guide should serve as an excellent resource for those planning a trip to Saraburi, providing all the information needed to make it a memorable and enjoyable experience.
