Introduction to Ko Kret

Ko Kret is a beautiful man-made island in the Chao Phraya Basin, located in the heart of Thailand. The island is home to a fascinating community and unique culture that is unlike any other in Thailand. The island is famous for its peaceful atmosphere, scenic beauty, and rich history. It’s the perfect destination for travelers who want to experience authentic Thailand without the crowds.

List of Places to Visit in Ko Kret

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums, and Historical Places

Ko Kret has a rich cultural heritage that is reflected in the island’s architecture, temples, and museums. Here are some must-visit destinations for history buffs:

  • Wat Poramai Yikawat - A renowned Buddhist temple that is over 200 years old. The temple has a beautiful central pagoda and is home to several Buddha statues.
  • The Mon Pottery Village - Here, you can witness the traditional Mon pottery making process. You can also buy beautiful ceramics and souvenirs to take home.
  • Wat Sao Tong Thong - A charming temple built in a traditional Northern Thai style. The temple has a golden Buddha statue and beautiful, ornate carvings.

Natural Attractions of Ko Kret: Outdoor Adventures, and Scenic Views

Ko Kret is known for its natural beauty, which attracts those who love the outdoors. Here are some of the top attractions:

  • Ko Kret Nature Trail - A picturesque, 3 km hiking trail that provides superb views of the island’s lush greenery and the Chao Phraya River.
  • Sunflower Field - A field filled with beautiful sunflowers, open only from November to February.
  • Cycling along the Chao Phraya River - The northern shores of Ko Kret offer a relaxing, scenic cycling path that lets you enjoy the stunning scenery.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Ko Kret

Hikers and bikers looking for a challenge will love Ko Kret’s countryside, which has plenty of trails that vary from easy to strenuous. Here are two popular routes worth considering:

  1. Ko Kret Nature Trail - A 3km loop trail, which starts at Wat Poramai Yikawat and offers stunning views of the river while taking you through lush greenery.
  2. Chao Phraya River Trail - A 30 km cycling path along the river which will take you through different towns and villages nearby.

Best Things to Do in Ko Kret: Activities and Tours

There are plenty of activities to do in Ko Kret that you can’t find anywhere else in Thailand. Here are some of the top things to do:

  • Discover Mon Pottery – Ratchaburi is famous for its Mon ceramics so you can’t leave Ko Kret without checking them out.
  • Take a Boat Tour – Take a slow boat tour around the island for amazing views and an unforgettable experience.
  • Traditional Weaving Workshop - Learn about traditional weaving techniques by visiting local cloth and silk weaving factories.

Week-Long Itinerary for Ko Kret

If you’re planning to visit Ko Kret for a week, follow this itinerary to make the most of your trip.

Day Activity
1 Arrive in Ko Kret and explore the island.
2 Visit Wat Poramai Yikawat, the Mon Pottery Village, and Wat Sao Tong Thong.
3 Take a slow boat tour around the island.
4 Cycle along the Chao Phraya River and visit local weaving factories.
5 Visit the Sunflower Field and enjoy the natural scenery of the island.
6 Take a trip to the nearby town of Nonthaburi and explore historic sites.
7 Check out local markets and enjoy traditional Thai cuisine before you head home.

Food and Drink Scene of Ko Kret

Ko Kret is famous for its traditional northern-style food and Thai snacks. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Khao Chae - A refreshing Thai dish made of rice soaked in cool jasmine water, served with traditional side dishes like shrimp paste, ginger, and fried shallots.
  • Pla Tod Rad Prik- A fresh fish dish served with sweet and spicy chili sauce.

Local Beverages

  • Cha-manao - A refreshing drink made with lemon juice, sugar, salt, and soda water.
  • Fruit Shakes - Fresh fruit shakes are readily available throughout the island, and there’s nothing more refreshing on a hot day.

Where to Stay in Ko Kret: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

While there is no shortage of accommodation options in Ko Kret, be advised that there are no five-star hotels on the island. However, there are several modest guesthouses and hostels for those on a budget, such as the Bangkrua Riverside Homestay and The OMyHostel. For those looking for a more luxurious stay, you can find a variety of hotels in nearby Bangkok.

What to Buy in Ko Kret: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Ko Kret’s Mon community is known for its traditional crafts, especially its pottery. These make for great souvenirs to take back home. Other local products you should consider include:

  • Hand-woven silks and clothes
  • Fresh fruits like dragon fruit, mango, papaya, and durian
  • Local snacks like Kanom Krok and Kanom Buang (Thailand’s famous desserts)

Local Culture and Customs of Ko Kret

The people of Ko Kret are predominantly Mon, and they have a unique cultural identity that is different from the rest of Thailand. One thing to note is that the Mon community is known for their hospitality, so make sure you greet people with a smile and show respect to their culture.

Best Time to Visit Ko Kret

The best time to visit Ko Kret is during the dry season, which runs from November to February. This season offers mild to cool temperatures and great weather for exploring the island. However, if you’re looking to avoid the crowds, you may want to visit during the low season (April - October).

Do’s and Don’ts of Ko Kret

Do:

  • Be respectful to local customs and traditions.
  • Try the local food and street food stalls.
  • Take part in local activities and workshops.

Don’t:

  • Take photographs of people or holy sites without asking for permission first.
  • Litter or damage the local environment.

Safety and Health

Safety in Ko Kret

Ko Kret is a relatively safe place to visit, but be aware of petty theft in crowded areas like markets and festivals.

Health in Ko Kret

There are no hospitals on the island, so if you need medical attention, you should head to the nearest hospital in Bangkok. Be sure to bring sunscreen, insect repellent, and drink plenty of water to avoid heat exhaustion.

Getting to Ko Kret: Transportation Options and Logistics

Ko Kret is accessible by boat, bus, or car. Here are some transportation options to consider:

Airports Near Ko Kret

Ko Kret is in the Chao Phraya Basin, so the nearest airport is the Don Mueang International Airport in Bangkok, which is approximately 22 km or an hour’s drive away.

Bus Stations Near Ko Kret

Tha Chang Pier, which is near the Grand Palace and Wat Phra Kaew, is the most convenient location to catch the Chao Phraya Express Boat to Ko Kret.

Other Options

Alternatively, you can hire a taxi or use public transportation like buses or the MRT to get to Ko Kret.

How to Get Around in Ko Kret

The best way to get around Ko Kret is on foot or by bicycle. You can also rent motorbikes to explore the island easily.

Where to Go Next from Ko Kret: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

If you want to extend your trip to nearby destinations, consider these popular options:

  • Bangkok – A bustling city with plenty of attractions, including the Grand Palace and Chatuchak Weekend Market.
  • Ayutthaya – A UNESCO World Heritage Site, Ayutthaya is an ancient city that boasts beautiful ruins and majestic temples.

Conclusion

Ko Kret is an off-the-beaten-path destination that is perfect for travelers who want to escape the crowds and experience traditional Thailand. From its scenic beauty to the rich cultural heritage, there’s something to enjoy for every kind of traveler. Plan your trip and get the chance to experience this hidden gem in Central Thailand.
