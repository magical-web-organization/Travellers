Introduction to Sukhumvit

Sukhumvit is a popular district in the bustling city of Bangkok, Thailand. It is known for its wide range of tourist attractions, from cultural sites to natural wonders, and is home to some of the city’s most exciting nightlife scenes. Sukhumvit stretches over 18 kilometers and is considered one of Bangkok’s longest roads. It spans from Bangkok’s central business district to the city’s eastern suburbs. In this guide, we explore some must-see attractions and experiences for visitors to Sukhumvit.

List of Places to Visit in Sukhumvit

Below are some of the most popular places to visit in Sukhumvit along with a brief overview of what to expect at these locations:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  1. Bangkok Art & Culture Centre - This museum is a great place to learn about Thai contemporary art and culture. It features several galleries, exhibition spaces, and performance halls. Visitors can explore art, music, film, and fashion.
  2. Kamthieng House Museum - This traditional, teak wood house was built in the late 19th century and provides visitors with a glimpse of Thailand’s rural past. It showcases traditional tools, household items, and farming implements.
  3. Jim Thompson House Museum - This museum is located in a traditional Thai-style house. It was the home of American silk entrepreneur, Jim Thompson. Visitors can explore the house’s interior and gardens, which showcase traditional Thai architecture and decorative arts.
  4. Erawan Shrine - This shrine is located near the Grand Hyatt Erawan Hotel and is popular among tourists and locals. It is dedicated to the Hindu god, Brahma, and is a great place to witness Thai religious customs and traditions.

Natural Attractions of Sukhumvit: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  1. Benjasiri Park - This beautiful park features a large lake, gardens, and walking paths. It is a great place to stroll, relax or enjoy a picnic.
  2. Lumpini Park - This park is one of Bangkok’s largest and most popular parks. It provides visitors with plenty of green space to explore, as well as a lake, jogging paths, and exercise equipment.
  3. Phra Khanong Canal - This canal weaves through the heart of Sukhumvit and is a great place to take a relaxing boat ride. Visitors can also explore the area’s street food culture from the floating market and eatery boats.
  4. Benchasiri Mangrove Park - This park is known for its unique canopy walk and is an excellent place to see exotic birds, reptiles, insects, and other wildlife.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Sukhumvit

  1. Benjakitti Park Bike Trail - Visitors can rent bikes and explore the park’s 3.5-kilometer bike trail.
  2. Sukhumvit Street Route - This busy and vibrant street is a great place to people-watch and take in the local scenery.

Best Things to Do in Sukhumvit: Activities and Tours

  1. Asiatique The Riverfront - This outdoor shopping center boasts over 1,500 boutiques, restaurants, and shops. Visitors can enjoy live music and performances or take a ride on the famous Asiatique Sky Ferris wheel.
  2. Siam Park City - This amusement park provides visitors with an exciting day of water rides, roller coasters, and other thrilling attractions. It is great for families with children or anyone looking for a fun-filled day.
  3. Cooking Class - Visitors can take a cooking class and learn how to cook traditional Thai dishes. Classes are typically an interactive, hands-on experience.
  4. Muay Thai Lesson - Visitors can take a lesson in the ancient martial art of Muay Thai. Classes are available for all skill levels, and they can provide a great workout.

Week-Long Itinerary for Sukhumvit

Day 1: Visit the Kamthieng House Museum, the Erawan Shrine, and explore the Soi Nana neighborhood. Day 2: Take a biking tour of the Sukhumvit Street Route.
Day 3: Spend the day at Benjasiri Park. Day 4: Visit the Bangkok Art & Culture Centre and explore the Siam Square neighborhood.
Day 5: Take a cooking class and learn how to cook traditional Thai dishes.
Day 6: Explore the Jim Thompson House Museum and take a relaxing boat ride along the Phra Khanong Canal.
Day 7: Spend the day at Siam Park City, one of Thailand’s largest amusement parks.

These are only a few of the countless tourist attractions and activities available in Sukhumvit. Visitors can create their own unique itinerary based on their interests and travel preferences.

Food and Drink Scene of Sukhumvit

Sukhumvit is renowned for its diverse cuisine that is both delicious and inexpensive. Below are some must-try dishes and local beverages to experience in Sukhumvit.

Must-Try Dishes

  1. Pad Thai - This classic Thai dish is made with rice noodles, shrimp, chicken, tofu, bean sprouts, and ground peanuts. It is topped with lime wedges and chili flakes.
  2. Tom Yum Soup - This traditional Thai soup is made from lemongrass, kaffir lime leaves, galangal, fish sauce, chili peppers, and coconut milk. It is typically served with shrimp or chicken.
  3. Som Tam - This spicy papaya salad is made with shredded green papaya, tomatoes, dried shrimp, peanuts, and chili peppers.
  4. Khao Soi - This dish is a Burmese-influenced curry made with egg noodles, meat (usually chicken), and a rich, creamy sauce.

Local Beverages

  1. Thai Iced Tea - This sweet, milky tea is popular in Bangkok and is typically served iced with condensed milk.
  2. Fresh Fruit Shake - Som Choon is a popular shake made with fresh tropical fruits such as mango, papaya, pineapple, and strawberries.
  3. Chang Beer - Chang is a popular Thai beer that can be found all over Sukhumvit.
  4. Sangsom - Since locals prefer rum over whiskey or vodka, Sangsom is popular among locals and tourists.

Where to Stay in Sukhumvit: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Sukhumvit is home to a wide range of accommodations to suit every budget and travel style. Visitors can choose from luxury hotels such as the Marriott and the Hilton, stylish boutique hotels like the Hyatt Place Bangkok Sukhumvit, or hostels and guesthouses that cater to budget travelers.

What to Buy in Sukhumvit: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Sukhumvit is an excellent place to shop for souvenirs and local products to take home with you. Visitors can find everything from fashionable clothing to local handicrafts. Must-buy items include:

  1. Thai Silk Products - Thailand is famous for its high-quality silk products. Visitors can find everything from silk scarves to clothing and bedding.
  2. Thai Spices - The country’s unique spices are a staple of Thai cuisine. Visitors can buy authentic spices and take them home to add to their food.
  3. Thai Handicrafts - Thailand is known for its intricate handicrafts such as hand-woven baskets and pottery.
  4. Thai Jewelry - Visitors can find beautiful handmade jewelry made from precious and semi-precious stones.

Local Culture and Customs of Sukhumvit

Visitors to Sukhumvit should consider learning about the country’s customs and traditions. Thai people are famously friendly and welcoming, and visitors can expect to experience this hospitality. However, it is also essential to be respectful of the country’s customs and traditions. For example:

  1. Dress Modestly - Both men and women should dress modestly when visiting temples or other religious sites.
  2. Remove Your Shoes - Visitors should remove their shoes before entering people’s homes, temples, or other cultural sites.
  3. Greet with Wai - The Thai greeting ‘wai’ is a gesture involving the pressing together of the palms of both hands followed by a slight bow. Visitors should use this greeting when meeting someone for the first time.
  4. Respect the Monarchy - The Thai people greatly revere their king and royal family. Visitors should be respectful when discussing the monarchy.

Best Time to Visit Sukhumvit

The best time to visit Sukhumvit is from November to February. During the winter months, Bangkok’s weather is relatively mild, with daytime temperatures averaging 25 degrees Celsius. March to May is the hottest time of year, with temperatures often exceeding 35 degrees Celsius. The rainy season lasts from June to October, with showers occurring almost daily.

Do’s and Don’ts of Sukhumvit

Do:

  1. Do try the street food.
  2. Do learn some Thai phrases.
  3. Do take your shoes off before entering someone’s home or temple.
  4. Do be respectful of the country’s customs and traditions.

Don’t:

  1. Don’t touch people on the head.
  2. Don’t disrespect the Thai monarchy.
  3. Don’t cause a scene or get angry in public.
  4. Don’t forget to tip.

Safety and Health

Safety in Sukhumvit

Sukhumvit is relatively safe for tourists, but visitors should take the usual safety precautions that they would in any big city. These include:

  1. Using common sense and taking care of your valuables.
  2. Travelling in groups at night.
  3. Staying in well-lit areas.
  4. Being vigilant when using public transport.

Health in Sukhumvit

Visitors to Sukhumvit should take the necessary health precautions, including:

  1. Drinking bottled water and avoiding tap water.
  2. Avoiding ice in drinks.
  3. Using insect repellent to prevent mosquito bites.
  4. Getting vaccinated before traveling to Thailand.

Getting to Sukhumvit: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Sukhumvit

Visitors to Sukhumvit will typically fly into Bangkok’s Suvarnabhumi International Airport. From there, they can take a taxi or the Airport Rail Link to Sukhumvit.

Bus Stations Near Sukhumvit

Bangkok’s main bus terminal is called the Mo Chit Bus Terminal. From there, visitors can take a taxi or bus to Sukhumvit.

Other Options

Visitors can also take the BTS Skytrain, a comprehensive system of trains and monorails that run throughout Bangkok. Sukhumvit is covered by the Sukhumvit Line.

How to Get Around in Sukhumvit

Sukhumvit is well-connected and easy to get around by public transport. The BTS Skytrain, buses, and taxis are popular modes of transport. Visitors can also rent a bike and explore the area’s bike trails.

Where to Go Next from Sukhumvit: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Sukhumvit is an excellent base from which to explore Thailand’s neighboring areas. Some nearby excursions that visitors might enjoy include:

  1. Visit the ancient city of Ayutthaya.
  2. Explore the famous floating markets of Damnoen Saduak.
  3. Experience the natural beauty of Khao Yai National Park.
  4. Visit the ancient city of Lopburi.

Conclusion

Sukhumvit is a vibrant and exciting district of Bangkok with something to offer visitors of all travel styles. Whether you’re a history buff, a foodie or an adventure-seeker, Sukhumvit has something that will appeal to you. From its delicious street food to its natural wonders, there’s never a dull moment in Sukhumvit.
