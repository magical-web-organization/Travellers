Introduction to Ramkhamhaeng

Located in the Chao Phraya Basin of Bangkok, Ramkhamhaeng is a beautiful neighborhood that is ideal for tourists seeking to explore and experience the city’s many incredible attractions. This travel guide provides an in-depth exploration of the best things to do, see, and eat in Ramkhamhaeng, making it the perfect resource for planning your next travel adventure.

List of Places to Visit in Ramkhamhaeng

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • The Ramkhamhaeng National Museum: This museum provides an excellent opportunity for tourists to learn about the rich history of Bangkok. Visitors can explore exhibits showcasing ancient artifacts and learn about how Bangkok has evolved over time.
  • The Jim Thompson House: This is a beautiful museum that showcases the life and works of Jim Thompson, a prominent American businessman, and art collector who played a key role in revitalizing the Thai silk industry.
  • The Golden Mount Temple: This is a beautiful Buddhist temple that is located on top of a hill at the heart of the neighborhood. Visitors can climb the steep steps to enjoy panoramic views of the city and explore the ancient structures and artifacts of the temple.

Natural Attractions of Ramkhamhaeng: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Ramkhamhaeng University: This is a beautiful university campus that features green spaces, walking paths, and hidden gardens. Visitors can take a relaxing stroll around the campus and explore the different serene locations hidden throughout the area.
  • The Bang Krajao Forest Park: This park offers a unique experience for visitors to explore on foot or by bike. Known as Bangkok’s “green lung,” the expansive park is home to a variety of flora and fauna species.
  • The Khlong Saen Saeb Canal: This canal is a popular destination for those who want to experience the canal culture of Bangkok. Tourists can take a water taxi to explore the various landmarks and attractions located along the canal.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Ramkhamhaeng

Ramkhamhaeng has plenty of options for hiking and biking routes for outdoor enthusiasts. Some of the top routes include:

  • The Ramkhamhaeng University Trail: This trail offers a peaceful walk along the riverside as it passes through the university’s beautiful landscaped gardens and expansive botanical collection.
  • The Khlong Saen Saeb Canal Trail: Tourists can cycle next to the canal and enjoy the cool breeze while taking in the captivating scenery of shopping centers, temples, and historic landmarks.
  • The Bang Kapi Park Trail: Bang Kapi Park is perfect for those that want to go for a bike ride against a natural backdrop. The park is home to a dense forest that is rich in fauna and flora and is located away from the chaos and noise of the city.

Best Things to Do in Ramkhamhaeng: Activities and Tours

Ramkhamhaeng has something special for everyone, offering a range of activities and tours to choose from, including:

  • Thai Cooking classes: Learn to cook like Thai at Yodpiman by Sa-nguan Sri School of Cuisine in Hua Takhe Market.
  • Nightlife: Ramkhamhaeng has a vibrant nightlife scene, with plenty of bars and clubs to enjoy after dark.
  • Shopping: Ramkhamhaeng is home to several shopping centers and markets, including The Mall Bangkapi, which is popular for its various shops and movie theater.

Food and Drink Scene of Ramkhamhaeng

Ramkhamhaeng is renowned for its delectable cuisine and rich culinary traditions. Visitors should try out the following must-try dishes and beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Pad Thai: Known as one of Thailand’s most treasured dishes, Pad Thai is a stir-fried noodle dish that comes with a variety of fish, meat, and vegetable options.
  • Tom Yum Goong: This is a famous Thai spicy shrimp soup that is flavored with lemongrass, chilies, and galangal.
  • Som Tam: This is a spicy Thai green papaya salad that can be eaten with sticky rice and grilled meat.

Local Beverages

  • Thai Iced Tea: This is a popular Thai tea that is served with milk and tastes exceptionally refreshing when served chilled.
  • Chao Phraya River Water: This comes directly from the river and is considered a healthy drink that can rejuvenate the body.
  • Singha Beer: This is a popular Thai beer that is made from various malts and hops, and it is frequently enjoyed by both tourists and locals alike.

Where to Stay in Ramkhamhaeng: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Ramkhamhaeng offers an array of accommodation options for tourists, including budget hostels, high-end hotels, and traditional guesthouses. Some of the highly recommended options include:

  • A-One Boutique Hotel: This is a modern hotel located within walking distance of The Mall Bangkapi. It offers luxury accommodations and elegant amenities in a beautiful setting.
  • Floral House: This is a budget-friendly guesthouse known for its comfortable rooms and friendly staff. It offers great value for money and is conveniently located near Khlong Saen Saeb Canal.
  • Bangkok Garden Inn: This is a popular hostel that offers a range of dormitory-style rooms and private accommodations. The hostel is revered for its welcoming environment and fantastic social atmosphere.

What to Buy in Ramkhamhaeng: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Tourists to Ramkhamhaeng will undoubtedly find a variety of souvenirs and local products to bring back home, including:

  • Thai Silk: Considered some of the best in the world, Thai silk is a fine, lustrous fabric that comes in various unique designs and patterns.
  • Thai Handicrafts: From carved wooden objects and woven bamboo baskets to elaborate pottery and decorative figurines, there are an abundance of stunning Thai handicrafts to explore and buy.
  • Thai Snacks: Thailand produces a range of unique snacks that make for fantastic souvenirs, such as dried squid, spicy rice crackers, and toasted coconut chips.

Local Culture and Customs of Ramkhamhaeng

Ramkhamhaeng embraces the traditional Thai culture, which is manifested in the district’s dance, music, and cuisine. The friendly and welcoming locals are another representation of the beautiful Thai culture. It’s important tourists respect the culture and customs of Thailand, dress moderately, and follow basic Thai customs when interacting with the local population.

Best Time to Visit Ramkhamhaeng

The best time to visit Ramkhamhaeng is between November and February when the weather is mild and pleasant. The temperatures range between 29-32℃, with more moderate humidity levels compared to other seasons, making it ideal for exploring the city on foot or indulging in outdoor activities and tours.

Do’s and Don’ts of Ramkhamhaeng

Do:

  • Dress appropriately when visiting temples or other cultural sites
  • Be respectful of Thai culture and customs, such as removing shoes when entering temples and speaking softly
  • Sample the local Thai cuisine

Don’t:

  • Disrespect Thai culture or traditions
  • Touch people’s heads or point at things with your feet
  • Engage in illegal activities like drugs

Safety and Health

Safety in Ramkhamhaeng

Ramkhamhaeng is generally a safe neighborhood, but tourists must use common sense and stay alert when exploring the area. While incidences of petty theft and pickpocketing can occur, visitors can avoid becoming a victim by not carrying large amounts of cash or valuable items, especially when traveling alone at night.

Health in Ramkhamhaeng

Tourists to Ramkhamhaeng should aim to stay hydrated and protect themselves against the sun to avoid health issues. The water in Bangkok is not considered potable, so tourists must drink bottled water.

Getting to Ramkhamhaeng: Transportation Options and Logistics

Tourists to Ramkhamhaeng can get there via various transportation options, including:

Airports Near Ramkhamhaeng

  • Suvarnabhumi International Airport: This is Bangkok’s primary international airport and is located approximately eight kilometers from Ramkhamhaeng.
  • Don Mueang International Airport: This is Bangkok’s secondary international airport located approximately 22 kilometers from Ramkhamhaeng.

Bus Stations Near Ramkhamhaeng

  • Bangkok Bus Terminal (Eastern): This bus station serves long-distance and regional bus services to the eastern part of Thailand. It is located approximately five kilometers from Ramkhamhaeng.
  • Mochit Bus Terminal: This is the northernmost bus station in Bangkok and serves buses traveling to different regions within Thailand. It is located approximately 26 kilometers from Ramkhamhaeng.

Other Options

Tourists can also travel to Ramkhamhaeng by train, taxi, or bus. Bangkok’s public transportation system, including the skytrain, metro, and buses, is comprehensive, and tourists can travel to all parts of the city conveniently.

How to Get Around in Ramkhamhaeng

Tourists can travel around Ramkhamhaeng via various modes of transportation, including taxis, buses, or the Bangkok’s Mass Rapid Transit (MRT) routes. Tuk-tuks or motorcycle taxis are available for shorter journeys.

Where to Go Next from Ramkhamhaeng: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Visitors can consider exploring the following nearby destinations and excursions from Ramkhamhaeng:

  • Chatuchak Weekend Market: This is a popular weekend market in Bangkok that features over 15,000 stalls that sell everything from clothing to antiques.
  • Wat Arun: This is one of the most famous temples in Bangkok, located on the bank of the Chao Phraya River. The temple is known for its beautiful Khmer-style spire and ornate decorations.
  • The Grand Palace: This is a must-see attraction in Bangkok, featuring multiple structures such as temples, halls, and courtyards surrounded by beautiful gardens.

Conclusion

Ramkhamhaeng is an excellent destination for tourists seeking to explore and experience the rich history and culture of Bangkok. With its unique blend of scenic landscapes, cultural attractions and delicious cuisine, this neighborhood has something to offer everyone. This comprehensive guide should help you plan your trip to Ramkhamhaeng and enjoy the diverse experiences that the neighborhood has to offer.
