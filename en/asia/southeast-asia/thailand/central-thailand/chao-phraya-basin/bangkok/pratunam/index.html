Introduction to Pratunam

Pratunam is a vibrant and bustling district located in the heart of Bangkok, Thailand, known for its bustling markets, shopping malls, and street food scene. Located in the Chao Phraya Basin in Central Thailand, Pratunam has become a popular destination for tourists seeking an authentic Thai experience.

List of Places to Visit in Pratunam

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Bangkok is home to a rich cultural heritage, and Pratunam is no exception. There are several museums and historical places to visit in Pratunam, each offering a glimpse into Thailand’s past. Some of the must-see cultural attractions in Pratunam include:

  • The Bangkok Art and Culture Centre - a contemporary art museum that showcases the works of local and international artists.

  • The Ananta Samakhom Throne Hall - a stunning European-inspired palace that was once used as a royal reception hall and is now a museum.

  • The Golden Mount Temple (Wat Saket) - a picturesque temple that offers panoramic views of Bangkok from the top of a hill.

Natural Attractions of Pratunam: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Pratunam may be located in the heart of Bangkok, but there are plenty of outdoor activities and natural attractions to enjoy in the surrounding area. Some of the best natural attractions in Pratunam include:

  • Lumpini Park - a tranquil oasis in the heart of Bangkok that’s perfect for an early morning run, yoga class, or picnic.

  • Chatuchak Park - one of the largest public parks in Bangkok, featuring a jogging track, bike rental, and botanical gardens.

  • Suan Pakkad Palace Museum - a serene garden oasis that features traditional Thai architecture and ancient artifacts.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Pratunam

For those looking to explore the great outdoors, there are a number of hiking and biking routes in the area that offer stunning scenic views and a chance to connect with nature. Some of the best hiking and biking routes in Pratunam include:

  • The Green Lung of Bangkok - a series of narrow canals, vegetable farms, and orchards that can be explored by bike or foot.

  • The Khlong Toei Market Loop - a cycling route that takes you through one of Bangkok’s largest and busiest fresh food markets.

Best Things to Do in Pratunam: Activities and Tours

There are plenty of must-see attractions and experiences in Pratunam, from exploring traditional floating markets to taking a cooking class to sampling street food. Some of the best things to do in Pratunam include:

  • The Pratunam Market - Known as one of the best and most affordable shopping destinations in Bangkok, Pratunam market features a wide range of local and international brands.

  • The Baiyoke Sky Tower - The tallest tower in Bangkok. It offers an amazing view of the city from its 77th floor.

  • Thai Cooking Class - Several cooking classes offer hands-on experience of Thai cuisine culture.

Week-Long Itinerary for Pratunam

Day 1: Visit the Golden Mount Temple and the Ananta Samakhom Throne Hall, followed by a walk in Lumpini Park, an oasis in the heart of Bangkok.

Day 2: Take a bike ride along the Khlong Toei Market Loop, followed by lunch at one of the local street food vendors.

Day 3: Explore the Pratunam market, and sample some of the delicious street food in the area.

Day 4: Take a Thai cooking class to learn about the local cuisine culture, followed by a night out at one of the local rooftop bars.

Day 5: Relax and unwind at one of the local spas, where you can enjoy a wide range of traditional Thai massages and treatments.

Day 6: Visit the Bangkok Art and Culture Centre to experience contemporary Thai art, followed by a night out at one of the local jazz clubs.

Day 7: Take a day trip to the nearby ancient city of Ayutthaya, a UNESCO World Heritage Site and a must-see attraction in Thailand.

Food and Drink Scene of Pratunam

Must-Try Dishes

Pratunam is a food lover’s paradise, with a wide range of local and international cuisine options available in the area. Some of the must-try dishes in Pratunam include:

  • Pad Thai - a stir-fried noodle dish that’s a staple of Thai cuisine.

  • Som Tum - a spicy green papaya salad.

  • Tom Yum Goong - a hot and sour soup with shrimp and traditional Thai herbs.

Local Beverages

Thailand is known for its unique and delicious drinks, and Pratunam is no exception. Some of the local beverages to try in Pratunam include:

  • Thai Iced Tea - a sweet and refreshing drink made with black tea, sugar, and milk.

  • Chai Yen - a cold drink made with black tea, spices, and condensed milk.

  • Fresh Coconut Water - a hydrating and healthy drink that’s a staple of Southeast Asian cuisine.

Where to Stay in Pratunam: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Pratunam offers an extensive range of accommodation options, from budget hostels to luxury hotels. Some of the best places to stay in Pratunam include:

  • The Amari Watergate Hotel Bangkok - a luxury hotel located in the heart of Pratunam, featuring world-class amenities and stunning views of the city.

  • The Baiyoke Sky Hotel - the tallest hotel in Thailand, offering unbeatable views of Bangkok and easy access to the Pratunam market.

  • The At Mind Executive Suites - a budget-friendly option that offers modern amenities and a central location in Pratunam.

What to Buy in Pratunam: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Pratunam is a shopper’s paradise, offering a wide range of local handicrafts, clothing, and souvenirs. Some of the best things to buy in Pratunam include:

  • Thai Silk Scarves - a traditional Thai item, available in a wide range of beautiful colors and patterns.

  • Thai Spices and Herbs - a must-try souvenir for any food lover, Thai spices and herbs are available at local markets throughout Pratunam.

  • Handmade Jewelry - Pratunam is home to several local artisans who create beautiful handmade jewelry and accessories.

Local Culture and Customs of Pratunam

Thailand has a rich cultural heritage, and it’s important to respect local customs and traditions while visiting Pratunam. Some of the key customs and traditions to be aware of include:

  • Buddhism - Thailand is a predominantly Buddhist country, and there are several temples and religious sites in Pratunam that should be visited with respect.

  • Dress Code - When visiting religious sites, it’s important to dress appropriately, covering your shoulders and knees.

  • Respect for the Monarchy - The Thai monarchy is highly respected in Thai culture. It’s important to show respect for the monarchy while visiting Pratunam.

Best Time to Visit Pratunam

The best time to visit Pratunam is during the cooler, drier months of November to February, when temperatures are most comfortable, and the humidity is at its lowest. Avoid visiting during the rainy season from June to October, as flooding can occur in some parts of the city.

Do’s and Don’ts of Pratunam

Do:

  • Try the Street Food - Pratunam is famous for its delicious street food, so make sure you take advantage of the variety of dishes available.

  • Be Polite and Respectful - Thai people are incredibly friendly and welcoming, but it’s important to be polite and respectful to locals while traveling in Pratunam.

Don’t:

  • Disrespect the Monarchy - Thailand’s monarchy is highly respected, and it’s important to show respect for the monarchy while traveling in Pratunam.

  • Touch People’s Heads - In Thai culture, the head is considered the most sacred part of the body, so it’s important not to touch people’s heads without permission.

Safety and Health

Safety in Pratunam

Bangkok, and Pratunam specifically, is a relatively safe place for tourists, but it’s important to take basic safety precautions while visiting, such as:

  • Avoiding Non-Tourist Areas at Night - While Pratunam is generally considered safe, it’s important to avoid traveling alone in non-tourist areas at night.

  • Beware of Pickpockets - Pratunam is a crowded area, so be aware of your surroundings and keep an eye on your belongings to avoid pickpocketing.

Health in Pratunam

Travelers to Pratunam should be up-to-date on routine vaccinations, such as measles, mumps, and rubella (MMR), tetanus, and influenza. It’s also important to take extra precautions to avoid foodborne illnesses, such as practicing good hand hygiene and avoiding raw foods and tap water.

Getting to Pratunam: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Pratunam

Bangkok is served by two airports, Suvarnabhumi International Airport, and Bangkok Don Muang Airport. Suvarnabhumi is the main airport and is located about 30 minutes from Pratunam.

Bus Stations Near Pratunam

Bangkok has several bus stations, with routes to cities throughout Thailand. The closest bus station to Pratunam is the Mo Chit Bus Station, which is the main hub for buses to northern Thailand.

Other Options

There are several other transportation options available in Bangkok, including:

  • Tuk-tuks - motorized three-wheel vehicles that are a popular mode of transportation in Bangkok.

  • Taxi - metered taxis are available throughout Bangkok and are a convenient way to get around.

  • BTS and MRT - Bangkok’s elevated and underground train systems offer easy and low-cost transportation around the city.

How to Get Around in Pratunam

One of the best ways to get around Pratunam is by walking, as several attractions are within easy walking distance. Other options include taking a tuk-tuk or taxi, or renting a bike for a more leisurely exploration of the area.

Where to Go Next from Pratunam: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

There are several nearby destinations that are easily accessible from Pratunam, including:

  • Ayutthaya - a UNESCO World Heritage Site located about an hour and a half from Bangkok, featuring ancient temples, palaces, and ruins.

  • Koh Samet - a beautiful island located about three hours from Bangkok, known for its stunning beaches and crystal-clear waters.

Conclusion

Pratunam is a vibrant and exciting destination that offers a taste of authentic Thai culture and the perfect home base for any Bangkok adventure. Whether you’re interested in exploring ancient temples, trying new foods, or simply soaking up the local culture, Pratunam has something for everyone.
