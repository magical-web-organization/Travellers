Introduction to Thonburi

Located on the west bank of the Chao Phraya River, Thonburi is a former capital of Thailand and home to several cultural and natural attractions. This riverside district features a rich historical and cultural heritage that attracts travelers from around the world.

From exploring ancient temples and museums to hiking in lush green parks and enjoying scenic river views, Thonburi has plenty of options for all kinds of travelers. In this comprehensive travel guide, we’ll take you through the must-see attractions, the best hiking and biking routes, the top things to do and eat, and everything else you need to know to plan your trip to Thonburi.

List of Places to Visit in Thonburi

Thonburi has a diverse range of attractions, ranging from historical and cultural landmarks to natural parks and outdoor adventures. Here’s a list of the top places to see in Thonburi.

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Wat Arun: Also known as the Temple of Dawn, this iconic Buddhist temple features impressive Khmer-style spires and intricate details that glisten in the sunlight.
  • Royal Barge Museum: Housing a collection of ornate royal barges, this museum is a must-visit for history buffs and watercraft enthusiasts.
  • Bangkok Noi Museum: Explore the rich heritage, culture, and traditions of Thailand’s riverine communities and their unique way of life.
  • Phra Pathom Chedi: Often regarded as the tallest stupa in the world, this revered Buddhist temple features stunning architecture and intricate artwork.
  • Phra Pin Klao Bridge: Spanning over the Chao Phraya River, this majestic bridge offers panoramic views of the river and the city skyline.

Natural Attractions of Thonburi: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Suan Rot Fai: Located in Chatuchak District, this sprawling park offers a wide range of activities and attractions, including a cycling track, a botanical garden, a mini-zoo, and a scenic lake.
  • Bang Krachao: Known as the “Green Lung” of Bangkok, this lush area boasts mangrove forests, birdwatching trails, and traditional markets.
  • Khlong Lat Mayom Floating Market: This authentic floating market offers a wide selection of traditional Thai food, drinks, and handicrafts, all sold from boats along the canal.
  • Taling Chan Floating Market: A popular destination for locals and tourists alike, this bustling market offers delicious street food, live music, and traditional massages.
  • Talad Nam Bang Nam Phueng: Known as the “Klong Bang Luang Artist Village,” this market is famous for its locally-made handicrafts and artworks.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Thonburi

Thonburi has a few excellent options for biking and hiking enthusiasts.

  • Bang Krachao Bike and Kayak Tour: Explore the lush green forests of Bang Krachao by bike and kayak, and discover hidden gems along the way.
  • Taling Chan Bike and Boat Tour: Cycle through the historic alleys of Taling Chan, then take a boat ride along the canal to see the traditional way of life along the river.
  • The Green Lung of Bangkok Bike Tour: Discover the beauty of Bang Kachao on a half-day bike ride and visit the city’s most prominent landmarks.

Best Things to Do in Thonburi: Activities and Tours

Thonburi has a diverse range of activities and tours for all kinds of travelers. Here are some of the best things to do in Thonburi.

  • Take a Chao Phraya River Cruise: Enjoy the stunning views of the river and the city skyline on a luxurious boat ride along the Chao Phraya River.
  • Visit the Grand Palace and the Emerald Buddha: Marvel at the intricate architectural designs and the rich cultural heritage of Bangkok’s most famous landmark.
  • Take a Traditional Thai Cooking Class: Learn the art of Thai cuisine and prepare your favorite Thai dishes with the help of an expert chef.
  • Visit the Wat Pho and the Reclining Buddha: Explore one of the oldest temples in Bangkok and admire the majestic golden statue of the Reclining Buddha.
  • Try Street Food in Thonburi: Sample the delicious local eats, including Pad Thai, Tom Yum Soup, and Mango Sticky Rice at the street vendors and restaurants scattered around the city.
  • Shop at the Floating Markets: Experience the vibrant atmosphere and the unique vibe of Thonburi’s floating markets, and grab some souvenirs to take back home.

Week-Long Itinerary for Thonburi

Day 1: Visit the Grand Palace, the Emerald Buddha, and Wat Pho. Sample some street food around the area.

Day 2: Take a Thonburi Walking Tour to explore the historic temples and landmarks of the district. Go to the Bangkok Noi Museum or The Royal Barge Museum.

Day 3: Cycle through Bang Krachao, the Green Lung of Bangkok. Visit Siamese Fighting Fish Gallery and Sri Nakhon Khuean Khan Park.

Day 4: Take a Chao Phraya River Cruise to explore the city and enjoy the stunning views of the river. Visit Wat Arun, the Temple of Dawn, and enjoy the sunset around there.

Day 5: Visit Taling Chan Floating Market and Khlong Lat Mayom Floating Market to experience the local culture and try various street food.

Day 6: Take a Thai Cooking Class, and prepare famous Thai dishes. Enjoy your food with the scenic views around the class.

Day 7: Visit Chao Phraya River Bank Park and enjoy the nature of Bangkok. Take some photographs around there.

Food and Drink Scene of Thonburi

Thonburi is renowned for its vibrant street food scene that combines traditional Thai flavors and contemporary culinary styles. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages to try in Thonburi.

Must-Try Dishes

  • Pad Thai: A classic Thai dish that features stir-fried rice noodles, shrimp, bean sprouts, and peanuts.
  • Tom Yum Goong: A popular spicy and sour soup made with prawns, lemongrass, kaffir lime leaves, and chilies.
  • Khao Pad: A fried rice dish cooked with onions, garlic, and an assortment of vegetables.
  • Som Tum: A fresh and spicy green papaya salad that’s typically served with grilled meat or seafood.
  • Gai Yang: Grilled chicken marinated in a blend of Thai herbs and spices.

Local Beverages

  • Thai Iced Tea: A classic drink made with black tea, condensed milk, and sugar, served over ice.
  • Cha Yen: Another popular iced tea that features a blend of black tea, spices, and evaporated milk.
  • Coconut Water: A refreshing and hydrating drink made from fresh coconuts.

Where to Stay in Thonburi: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Thonburi offers a wide range of accommodation options for all budgets and preferences. Here are some of our top picks.

  • The River Hotel: A luxurious hotel with stunning views of the Chao Phraya River, featuring a pool, a spa, and a fitness center.
  • The Unforgotten Bed and Breakfast: A charming guesthouse with vintage-style rooms and a cozy atmosphere.
  • Sukhumvit River View Hotel: A budget-friendly hotel with river views and easy access to public transportation.
  • Chatrium Hotel Riverside Bangkok: A five-star hotel with spacious rooms, stunning river views, and a rooftop bar.

What to Buy in Thonburi: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Thonburi has many unique and locally-made souvenirs and products that make perfect gifts or keepsakes. Here are some suggestions.

  • Thai Silk: A luxurious and vibrant fabric made from fine threads of silk, available in an array of colors and patterns.
  • Hand-Woven Baskets: Beautifully crafted and durable baskets made from locally-sourced natural fibers.
  • Handicrafts: From carved wooden souvenirs to handmade pottery, Thonburi has many unique and traditional handicrafts to choose from.
  • Local Snacks: From crispy fried insects to sweet and savory treats, Thonburi’s street markets offer an array of tasty snacks and edibles to bring home.

Local Culture and Customs of Thonburi

Thonburi’s culture and customs reflect the rich heritage and history of Thailand’s riverine communities. Here are some things to keep in mind.

  • Dress modestly when visiting temples and other religious sites.
  • Remove your shoes before entering homes, temples, and some shops.
  • Show respect to elders and authority figures.
  • Don’t forget to try the local food and participate in local traditions and festivals.

Best Time to Visit Thonburi

Thonburi has a tropical climate and experiences hot and humid weather year-round. The ideal time to visit Thonburi is during the dry season, which runs from November to February, when the weather is cooler and less humid.

Do’s and Don’ts of Thonburi

Do:

  • Try the local street food and traditional dishes.
  • Visit the temples and landmarks to get a glimpse into Thai culture and history.
  • Hire a guide or take a tour to get an in-depth understanding of the district’s history and culture.
  • Be respectful towards the locals and their customs and traditions.

Don’t:

  • Take or buy drugs.
  • Disrespect or damage any religious artifacts or symbols.
  • Haggle excessively with street vendors or market sellers.
  • Wear revealing clothing in religious places.

Safety and Health

Safety in Thonburi

Thonburi is a safe district with a low crime rate. However, tourists should take precautions to avoid petty theft and scams, especially in crowded areas and markets. It’s always a good idea to keep valuables safe and be aware of your surroundings while exploring the district.

Health in Thonburi

Thonburi is a relatively healthy district, with few serious health risks. However, tourists should take precautions to avoid mosquito bites, as they can transmit dengue fever and other diseases. It’s also important to stay hydrated in the hot and humid weather and to practice good hand hygiene and food safety to prevent food-borne illnesses.

Getting to Thonburi: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Thonburi

Thonburi is accessible via two airports:

  • Suvarnabhumi International Airport, located about 30 km from Thonburi
  • Don Muang International Airport, located about 35 km from Thonburi

From both airports, travelers can take taxis, buses, or trains to get to Thonburi.

Bus Stations Near Thonburi

  • Southern Bus Terminal: Located in the Thonburi district, this bus station has regular services to various destinations in southern Thailand.
  • Bangkok Bus Terminal (Mo Chit 2): Located in Chatuchak District, this bus station has regular services to various destinations in northern and northeastern Thailand.

Other Options

  • Taxi: Taxis are readily available in Bangkok and can be hailed from anywhere in the city.
  • Tuk-Tuk: A fun and affordable way to travel around Bangkok, Tuk-tuks are three-wheeled taxis that can be hailed from the side of the street.
  • Boat: There are several boat services that operate along the Chao Phraya River, providing a scenic and convenient way to travel around Thonburi and other parts of Bangkok.

How to Get Around in Thonburi

Thonburi has an extensive public transportation system that includes buses, taxis, tuk-tuks, and boats. However, the best way to get around Thonburi is by cycling or walking, especially around the narrow alleys and historic neighborhoods.

Where to Go Next from Thonburi: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Thonburi is conveniently located near several other popular destinations and attractions in Thailand. Here are some suggestions:

  • Ayutthaya: The former capital of Siam and now a UNESCO World Heritage Site, Ayutthaya is just 80 kilometers north of Bangkok.
  • Kanchanaburi: Known for its beautiful natural scenery and rich history, Kanchanaburi is about 130 kilometers west of Bangkok.
  • Pattaya: A beautiful beach town known for its vibrant nightlife and water sports activities.
  • Koh Chang: A stunning island paradise located just off the coast of Trat Province, Koh Chang is known for its pristine beaches and lush rainforests.

Conclusion

Thonburi is a wonderful place to explore the rich cultural and natural heritage of Thailand. From exploring ancient temples and museums to hiking in lush green parks and enjoying scenic river views, Thonburi has something to offer for everyone. We hope this comprehensive travel guide has helped you plan your trip and discover the best that Thonburi has to offer.
