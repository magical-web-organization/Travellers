Introduction to Silom

Silom is one of the most popular neighborhoods in Bangkok, Thailand. Known as the city’s financial district, Silom plays host to numerous banks, offices, and financial institutions. It’s a lively neighborhood, with a mix of modern buildings and traditional homes. This bustling district is where you can find a rich culture, shopping, dining, entertainment, and historical sites all in one place.

List of Places to Visit in Silom

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

One of the must-visit museums is the Bangkok Folk Museum in Bangrak, which showcases the traditional way of life in Bangkok. Another fascinating museum is the Bangkokian Museum, which is a beautifully maintained old mansion that offers a glimpse into life in the early 20th century. You can also visit the famous Sri Maha Mariamman Hindu Temple, located in the heart of Silom.

Natural Attractions of Silom: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Silom is home to many green spaces perfect for a peaceful getaway, such as Lumpini Park, one of the largest parks in Bangkok. The Silom Forest Park is another great option, located on the rooftop of the Bangkok Christian College. Visitors can enjoy a hike, cycle around the park, or take in panoramic views of the city skyline.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Silom

Silom provides plenty of hiking and biking routes that allow travelers to immerse themselves in the natural beauty of the area. The Chao Phraya River is a must-visit spot, with cycling tracks and boat tours available. Another exciting route to explore is the Bangkok Green Route, a network of connecting parks, cycle-ways, and bike trails throughout the city.

Best Things to Do in Silom: Activities and Tours

While Silom may be known for its financial institutions, there’s also plenty of shopping and entertainment options available. You can explore the Patpong Night Market, one of the most popular tourist destinations in Bangkok, or enjoy a spa day at one of the luxury hotels in the area. You can also take a riverboat ride along the Chao Phraya River to explore Bangkok from a different perspective.

Week-Long Itinerary for Silom

For travelers who have a week to spend in Silom, here’s an itinerary that will take you through the best this lively neighborhood has to offer:

Day 1 Visit the Bangkok Folk Museum and the Sri Maha Mariamman Hindu Temple
Day 2 Enjoy a spa day at one of the luxury hotels

Food and Drink Scene of Silom

Must-Try Dishes

Silom is known for its delicious street food options, including the famous Pad Thai, which can be found at any of the street stalls. Some other must-try dishes include Tom Yum Soup, Pad Kra Pao, and Massaman Curry. For a more upscale dining experience, head to the Blue Elephant restaurant, which serves classic Thai dishes with a modern twist.

Local Beverages

One of the most popular drinks in Silom is Thai Iced Tea, a sweet and creamy brew that’s perfect for hot Bangkok days. You can also try Chang Beer, a popular Thai beer that pairs well with spicy foods.

Where to Stay in Silom: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Silom offers a variety of accommodation options for any budget. For a luxury experience, the Shangri-La Bangkok is a top choice, with stunning river views and a range of amenities. For a more budget-friendly option, try the Saphaipae Hostel, which offers clean and comfy bunk beds in a central location.

What to Buy in Silom: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

A trip to Silom isn’t complete without picking up a few souvenirs to take home. Look for Thai silk products, including scarves and bags, which can be found at Jim Thompson’s House. You can also find unique items at the Patpong Night Market, including traditional Thai handicrafts and toys.

Local Culture and Customs of Silom

Thai culture is very respectful, and travelers should be aware of certain customs and manners, such as removing your shoes when entering someone’s home or temple. It’s also important to dress appropriately when visiting temples or museums, with shoulders and knees covered.

Best Time to Visit Silom

November through February is the best time to visit Silom due to the mild weather and low humidity. The rainy season sets in during May and lasts until October, while the hot and humid season starts in March and continues until May.

Do’s and Don’ts of Silom

Do:

  • Be respectful of local customs and manners
  • Try the street food stalls
  • Take a riverboat ride along the Chao Phraya River

Don’t:

  • Disrespect the King or the Royal Family
  • Litter or damage historical sites
  • Wear inappropriate clothing when visiting temples or museums

Safety and Health

Safety in Silom

Silom is generally a safe neighborhood, but travelers should still take standard precautions. Keep your valuables secure, especially when visiting crowded areas. It’s also important to be careful when crossing the street, as traffic can be chaotic in Bangkok.

Health in Silom

It’s important to drink bottled water and avoid tap water in Thailand. Make sure to use insect repellent to avoid mosquito-borne diseases such as dengue fever and malaria. Also, travelers should be aware of the high level of air pollution in Bangkok, particularly during the dry season.

Getting to Silom: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Silom

The Suvarnabhumi International Airport is located about 25 kilometers east of Silom, approximately a 30-minute drive from the neighborhood. Don Mueang International Airport is about 29 kilometers away and is also easily accessible.

Bus Stations Near Silom

Silom is serviced by the Bangkok Bus Terminal and can easily be reached by local buses running all across Bangkok.

Other Options

The BTS Skytrain, as well as the MRT subway system, offer hassle-free and efficient daily transport around Bangkok. Taxis are easily available, and motorbike taxis and tuk-tuks are also an option.

How to Get Around in Silom

Walking is the easiest way to explore Silom, especially during off-peak hours. Taxis and motorbike taxis are widely available, but keep in mind that traffic can be congested during peak hours.

Where to Go Next from Silom: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Silom is a great base to explore other parts of Bangkok, such as the Grand Palace, Wat Arun, and Wat Phra Kaew. It’s also close to the Chao Phraya River, which allows visitors to easily access other parts of Thailand.

Conclusion

Silom offers a mix of modern and traditional experiences within an energetic neighborhood. From dining to history, there’s something here for everyone. Visitors can find a rich cultural experience, adventure, and much more. It’s a must-visit destination when traveling to Thailand.
