If you’re planning a trip to Thailand, make sure to include Nakhon Nayok in your itinerary. Located in the Chao Phraya Basin in Central Thailand, this hidden gem is known for its natural beauty, fascinating history, and vibrant culture. In this comprehensive guide, we’ll take a closer look at what makes Nakhon Nayok special and share some tips for planning an unforgettable visit.

Introduction to Nakhon Nayok

Nakhon Nayok is a province in Thailand, located approximately 100 kilometers northeast of Bangkok. It is known for its rugged mountains, lush forests, and scenic waterfalls, as well as its rich cultural heritage and friendly locals. The province is home to a number of ethnic groups, including the Mon, Lao, and Khmer people, adding to its diversity and unique character.

List of Places to Visit in Nakhon Nayok

Whether you’re a nature lover, history buff, or adventure seeker, there’s plenty to see and do in Nakhon Nayok. Here are some of the top attractions to add to your itinerary:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Khao Nang Buat: This ancient temple, located on a hilltop, offers stunning views of the surrounding countryside and is known for its fascinating history and elegant architecture.
  • Nakhon Nayok Historical Museum: Explore the province’s rich history through exhibits on local culture, industry, and art.
  • Prachin Buri National Museum: This museum showcases the archaeological and historical artifacts from the ancient city of Prachin Buri and nearby areas.

Natural Attractions of Nakhon Nayok: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Khlong Tha Dan Dam: Take in the stunning views of the largest dam in Thailand, surrounded by lush forests and a popular spot for fishing and boating.
  • Khao Yai National Park: Home to tropical forests, waterfalls, and diverse wildlife, this park is perfect for hiking, bird-watching, and wildlife spotting.
  • Sarika Waterfall: A popular spot for swimming and picnicking, this beautiful waterfall can be reached by a short hike through the forest.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Nakhon Nayok

For outdoor enthusiasts, Nakhon Nayok offers a variety of hiking and biking routes through the lush countryside and mountains. Here are some popular trails:

  • Khao Yai National Park hiking trails: The park offers various hiking trails, ranging from easy to difficult, passing through waterfalls, streams, and dense forests.
  • Pang Sida National Park hiking and biking trails: This park is known for its scenic beauty, with several biking and hiking trails running through its lush jungle and mountains.
  • Namtok Sarika – Wang Takrai Park: This park has a beautiful waterfall and a network of trails that circle around the waterfall, leading you deeper into the jungle.

Best Things to Do in Nakhon Nayok: Activities and Tours

To get the most out of your trip to Nakhon Nayok, consider trying some of these popular activities and tours:

  • White-water Rafting: The Nakhon Nayok River is a popular destination for adrenaline junkies and outdoor enthusiasts, who come to experience the thrilling rapids.
  • ATV Rides: Explore remote areas of the jungle and plantations by ATV, which is a perfect way of an exhilarating adventure.
  • Night Safari Tour: Explore the wildlife of Khao Yai National Park on a night safari tour. Spot the local wildlife such as the asiatic bear, clouded leopard, and elephants in their natural habitat.

To make the most of your holiday in Nakhon Nayok, here is our suggested week-long itinerary:

Food and Drink Scene of Nakhon Nayok

One of the joys of visiting Nakhon Nayok is sampling its delicious local cuisine and drinks. Here are some must-try dishes and beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Tom Yum Goong: This spicy shrimp soup is a staple of Thai cuisine and is made with a blend of herbs and spices, fish sauce, and chili paste.
  • Pad Thai: A classic stir-fried noodle dish, Pad Thai features a variety of fresh vegetables, herbs, and spices, along with a protein of your choice.
  • Tom Kha Gai: Another classic Thai soup, Tom Kha Gai is made with coconut milk, chicken, lemongrass, galangal and a variety of herbs and spices.

Local Beverages

  • Thai Iced Tea: A delicious blend of black tea, spices, and sweetened condensed milk, Thai iced tea is a refreshing way to beat the heat.
  • Chang Beer: One of Thailand’s most popular beers, Chang Beer is refreshing and light, making it the ideal choice for a hot day.

Where to Stay in Nakhon Nayok: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

There are plenty of accommodation options in Nakhon Nayok, ranging from budget guesthouses to luxurious resorts:

  • OYO 218 Lertnimitra Hotel: This modern hotel offers clean and comfortable rooms, with easy access to the city center and local attractions.
  • Baan Suan Khun Yaiy: A charming guesthouse located in a peaceful garden setting, offering a relaxed and homely atmosphere.
  • KaoYai Nimman: This resort offers luxurious rooms and suites, with breathtaking views of the mountains and countryside.

What to Buy in Nakhon Nayok: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

If you’re looking for a unique souvenir to bring home from Nakhon Nayok, why not try one of these local products:

  • Handicrafts: Choose from a variety of handmade crafts, including pottery, wood carvings, and textiles.
  • Farm-fresh Produce: Nakhon Nayok is known for its fresh fruits, vegetables, and herbs. Take your pick of the best local produce to bring back home.
  • Orchids and Flowers: As a major producer of orchids in Thailand, Nakhon Nayok is the perfect place to buy beautiful blooms for yourself or as a gift.

Local Culture and Customs of Nakhon Nayok

To truly immerse yourself in the local culture of Nakhon Nayok, there are a few customs and practices you should be aware of:

  • Buddhism: The majority of people in Thailand practice Buddhism, so it’s important to be respectful when visiting temples and other religious sites.
  • Clothing: As with other parts of Thailand, it is important to dress conservatively when visiting Nakhon Nayok, especially when visiting religious sites.
  • Greeting: It’s customary to greet people with a “wai”, which is a slight bow with the hands pressed together in front of the chest.

Best Time to Visit Nakhon Nayok

The best time to visit Nakhon Nayok is between November to February when the temperature is cooler and perfect for exploring outdoor attractions and enjoy many seasonal festivals in the area.

Do’s and Don’ts of Nakhon Nayok

Do:

  • Try local food and drinks to experience the flavors of the province.
  • Respect the local customs and culture, including dress codes and religious practices.
  • Explore the outdoors and enjoy the natural beauty of the province.

Don’t:

  • Disrespect the local customs and beliefs.
  • Litter or damage the environment.
  • Take advantage of local people or businesses.

Safety and Health

Safety in Nakhon Nayok

Nakhon Nayok is generally a safe place to visit, but it’s always a good idea to take precautions wherever you go. Here are some tips:

  • Be aware of your surroundings when traveling, especially at night or in unfamiliar areas.
  • Avoid carrying large amounts of cash or valuables around with you.
  • Stay hydrated and wear a hat during hot days.

Health in Nakhon Nayok

There are no major health concerns in Nakhon Nayok, but it’s always wise to take precautions:

  • Drink bottled water or treated water to avoid stomach illness.
  • Wear mosquito repellent to avoid mosquito-borne diseases such as dengue fever and malaria.
  • Seek medical attention if you experience any unusual symptoms.

Getting to Nakhon Nayok: Transportation Options and Logistics

Nakhon Nayok is easily accessible from Bangkok and other major cities in Thailand. Here are some transportation options to consider:

Airports Near Nakhon Nayok

The nearest international airport to Nakhon Nayok is the Suvarnabhumi Airport in Bangkok, approximately 120 kilometers away.

Bus Stations Near Nakhon Nayok

There are several bus stations in Bangkok that offer services to Nakhon Nayok. The journey takes approximately 2 hours.

Other Options

Private cars and taxis can also be arranged to bring you directly to Nakhon Nayok from Bangkok or other nearby destinations.

How to Get Around in Nakhon Nayok

To explore the province, consider these transportation options:

  • Songthaews: These shared taxis are the most common form of public transport in Nakhon Nayok and are affordable and convenient.
  • Tuk-tuks: Similar to a motorized rickshaw, tuk-tuks are another common form of transport but are a bit more expensive.
  • Rental car or bike: This is a good option if you prefer the flexibility of having your own transportation, but it is important to check your insurance policy carefully before renting a vehicle.

Where to Go Next from Nakhon Nayok: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Nakhon Nayok is surrounded by other interesting destinations worth exploring. Here are some suggestions:

  • Khao Yai National Park: A UNESCO World Heritage Site, this park is known for its diverse wildlife and stunning scenery.
  • Prachin Buri: Located near Nakhon Nayok, this province is home to several historical and cultural sites, as well as scenic countryside.
  • Bangkok: The capital city of Thailand is just a short drive away and offers plenty of attractions, food, and shopping options.

Conclusion

Nakhon Nayok is a hidden gem in Central Thailand, offering a fascinating blend of culture, history, and natural beauty. With this comprehensive guide, you can plan your trip with ease, knowing where to go, what to see, what to eat, and how to get there. Whether you’re seeking outdoor adventure, cultural immersion, or just a relaxing holiday, Nakhon Nayok has it all.
