Sing Buri Travel Guide - A Comprehensive Tour Guide for Sing Buri, Thailand

Welcome to Sing Buri, a district in Chao Phraya Basin, Central Thailand. Known for its abundance of historical and cultural sites, Sing Buri is a must-visit destination on any travel itinerary to Thailand. In this comprehensive travel guide, we’ll take you on a journey through the best places to visit, things to do, food and drink scene, where to stay, and more in Sing Buri. Let’s get started!

List of Places to Visit in Sing Buri

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Sing Buri is home to a wealth of historical and cultural attractions that offer a glimpse into Thailand’s rich past. Here are some of our top picks:

  • Wat Phra Non Chakkrasi Worawihan - a 17th-century temple that houses a giant reclining Buddha statue
  • City Pillar Shrine - a sacred site that houses the city’s guardian spirit
  • Sing Buri National Museum - a museum that showcases Sing Buri’s local history and culture
  • Phra Buddha Chinnarat - a beautiful golden Buddha statue that is a must-visit for any Buddhist traveler
  • Wat Sawang Arom - a 200-year-old temple that is known for its unique architecture and serene atmosphere
  • Wat Sing - a temple that features intricate murals and wall paintings

Natural Attractions of Sing Buri: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

If you’re seeking adventure and breathtaking scenery, Sing Buri won’t disappoint. Here are some of the best natural attractions to visit:

  • Chao Phraya Dam - a picturesque dam that offers stunning views of the river and surrounding landscape
  • Kraison Siharat Hall - a beautiful park that’s perfect for picnics and relaxation
  • Khao Chan Ngam Cave - an awe-inspiring cave system that’s perfect for adventurous travelers
  • Bueng Chawak Chaloem Phrakiat - a stunning freshwater lake that’s perfect for swimming, fishing, and boating
  • Bang Rachan Memorial Park - a park that narrates the story of a historical battle
  • Wat Phra Si Rattana Mahathat - a temple that features a beautiful garden and lake

Hiking/Biking Routes of Sing Buri

Sing Buri’s gorgeous natural scenery makes it the perfect destination for hiking and biking enthusiasts. Check out these top hiking and biking routes:

  • Bike Route 3116 - a 20-kilometer route that takes you through the countryside and beautiful scenery
  • Khao Wongkot Mountain - a challenging hike that takes you to the top of a mountain for stunning views of the area
  • Phra Phuttha Pha Daeng - a beautiful hike that leads you to an impressive Buddha statue

Best Things to Do in Sing Buri: Activities and Tours

Whether you’re traveling solo, with family, or friends, Sing Buri offers a wealth of activities and tours to keep you entertained. Here are some of our top picks:

  • Phra Ruang Hot Spring - a popular spot for enjoying hot springs and relaxation
  • Bike Tour of Sing Buri - a fun and informative way to see the city’s highlights on two wheels
  • Homestay Experience - an immersive experience that lets you live with a local family and learn more about their daily lives
  • Rice Farming Experience - a unique opportunity to try your hand at one of Thailand’s primary industries
  • Thai Cooking Class - a fun and interactive way to learn how to cook delicious Thai food

Week-Long Itinerary for Sing Buri

To help you plan your trip, here’s a week-long itinerary for exploring the best of Sing Buri:

Day 1: Explore the city center and visit Wat Phra Non Chakkrasi Worawihan and the City Pillar Shrine.

Day 2: Visit the Sing Buri National Museum and Phra Buddha Chinnarat.

Day 3: Head out to Chao Phraya Dam and Khao Chan Ngam Cave.

Day 4: Experience homestay and rice farming activities.

Day 5: Visit Bueng Chawak Chaloem Phrakiat and Bang Rachan Memorial Park.

Day 6: Take a Thai cooking class and enjoy the local food scene.

Day 7: Relax at Phra Ruang Hot Spring before departing Sing Buri.

Food and Drink Scene of Sing Buri

Must-Try Dishes

Sing Buri’s food scene is a mix of traditional Thai and local specialties. Some must-try dishes include:

  • Khao Soy Sing Buri - a local version of the popular northern Thai dish
  • Pla Too Yat Sai - a deep-fried stuffed fish that’s delicious and filling
  • Larb Mhoo Sing Buri - a spicy and tangy pork salad that’s full of flavor
  • Kanom Jeen - a traditional Thai noodle dish that’s popular in Sing Buri
  • Kao Pad Sapparot - a sweet and savory pineapple fried rice dish

Local Beverages

Thai people love their drinks, and Sing Buri is no exception. Here are some local beverages to try:

  • Thai Iced Tea - a sweet and creamy tea that’s popular throughout Thailand
  • Lime Juice - a refreshing drink that’s perfect for hot days
  • Coconut Water - a must-try when visiting any Thai destination
  • Sing Buri Coffee - a local coffee drink that’s perfect for coffee lovers

Where to Stay in Sing Buri: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Sing Buri offers a range of accommodation options to suit all budgets and preferences. Here are some top picks:

  • Sing Buri River Kwai Resort - a beautiful resort that offers stunning views of the river and mountains
  • Baantorfun Resort - a relaxing and peaceful resort that’s perfect for couples and families
  • Phumayon Boutique Resort - a stylish and modern resort that offers a pool and restaurant
  • Sri Wilai Suk Guesthouse - a budget-friendly guesthouse that offers comfortable rooms and friendly staff

What to Buy in Sing Buri: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

No trip to Sing Buri is complete without bringing back a few souvenirs. Here are some unique and local products to consider:

  • Pottery - Sing Buri is known for its beautiful pottery, and you can find unique pieces at local shops and markets
  • Handmade Bags - local artisans create unique bags that make for great gifts or personal use
  • Local Food Products - stock up on local spices, sauces, and snacks to bring a taste of Sing Buri back home with you

Local Culture and Customs of Sing Buri

Thai culture is steeped in tradition, and Sing Buri is no exception. Here are some local customs and traditions to keep in mind when visiting Sing Buri:

  • Dress Modestly - Thai people value modesty, so remember to dress appropriately when visiting temples and other sacred sites
  • Remove Shoes - when entering temples or homes, it’s customary to remove your shoes as a sign of respect
  • Wai - a small bow or gesture with hands pressed together is customary when greeting people or showing respect
  • Respect Local Customs - when in doubt, always observe and follow local customs and traditions

Best Time to Visit Sing Buri

Sing Buri can be visited year-round, but the best time to visit is between November and February when the weather is cooler and drier. Avoid visiting in March and April as it can be incredibly hot and humid.

Do’s and Don’ts of Sing Buri

Do:

  • Respect local culture and customs
  • Tip service workers
  • Learn some basic Thai phrases
  • Try local cuisine and drinks
  • Bargain when shopping at local markets

Don’t:

  • Disrespect sacred sites or religious customs
  • Take photos without permission
  • Touch someone’s head as it is considered rude
  • Show too much affection in public

Safety and Health

Safety in Sing Buri

Sing Buri is generally safe for travelers, but it’s always a good idea to take standard safety precautions like locking up valuables and being aware of your surroundings. Avoid carrying too much cash on you, especially when traveling alone. Additionally, be cautious when driving or renting a motorcycle as the roads can be dangerous.

Health in Sing Buri

Sing Buri is not known for any unique health risks, but make sure to use common sense and stay hydrated when traveling during hot and humid weather. Wear sunscreen and mosquito repellent when needed, especially during the rainy season.

Getting to Sing Buri: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Sing Buri

The nearest airport to Sing Buri is Suvarnabhumi Airport in Bangkok, which is approximately 125 kilometers from Sing Buri. From there, you can take a taxi or bus to reach Sing Buri.

Bus Stations Near Sing Buri

Sing Buri has a central bus station that offers connections to various cities throughout Thailand, including Bangkok and Chiang Mai.

Other Options

Travel by train is also an option to reach Sing Buri from Bangkok and other major cities in Thailand. Private taxis and car rentals are also available if you prefer more flexibility in your travel plans.

How to Get Around in Sing Buri

The best way to get around in Sing Buri is by tuk-tuk or motorcycle taxi, especially if you want to explore the city center and local attractions. For longer distances, you can also rent a car or hire a private driver.

Where to Go Next from Sing Buri: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Sing Buri is surrounded by various cities and attractions that can be easily reached within a few hours’ drive, including Ayutthaya, Lopburi, and Khao Yai National Park.

Conclusion

Sing Buri is a hidden gem in Thailand that’s just waiting to be discovered. With its wealth of historical and cultural sites, beautiful scenery, and delicious local cuisine, it’s a must-visit destination for any traveler to Thailand. We hope this comprehensive travel guide has inspired you to plan your next trip to Sing Buri and experience all it has to offer. Happy travels!
