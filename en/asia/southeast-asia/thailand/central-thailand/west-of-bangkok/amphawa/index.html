Introduction to Amphawa

Located in the west of Bangkok, Amphawa is a charming small town in Central Thailand that offers visitors an authentic taste of local life. The town is famous for its floating market, which is open on weekends and draws both locals and tourists. Aside from the market, Amphawa boasts a rich cultural heritage, natural beauty, and delicious local food.

List of Places to Visit in Amphawa

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Wat Bang Kung

One of the most important historical sites in Amphawa is Wat Bang Kung, a temple that dates back to the Ayutthaya period. The temple is famous for its striking architecture, which features a chapel that is enclosed inside the roots of a banyan tree.

King Rama II Memorial Park

The King Rama II Memorial Park is a tribute to the second king of the Chakri Dynasty, who was born in Amphawa. The park features a museum with exhibits about the king’s life and the history of the town. Visitors can also enjoy beautiful gardens and a statue of the king.

Natural Attractions of Amphawa: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Amphawa Canal

The Amphawa Canal is a picturesque waterway that runs through the town and is lined with traditional Thai houses and shops. Visitors can take a boat tour of the canal or stroll along the pedestrian bridge to enjoy the views.

Bang Kaeo Mangrove Forest

Nature lovers should not miss the Bang Kaeo Mangrove Forest, which is a protected area near Amphawa. The forest features a network of boardwalks that allow visitors to explore the mangroves on foot.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Amphawa

Amphawa Walking Street

The Amphawa Walking Street is a popular place for visitors to enjoy a leisurely walk while browsing the local market stalls. The street is closed to traffic on weekends and features a variety of food, crafts, and souvenirs.

Tha Kha Floating Market

Tha Kha Floating Market is another famous floating market near Amphawa, which is less touristy than the Amphawa Floating Market and offers a more authentic experience. Visitors can take a boat tour of the market and enjoy local food and drink.

Best Things to Do in Amphawa: Activities and Tours

Thai Cooking Class

Visitors who are interested in learning about Thai cuisine can take a cooking class in Amphawa, where they will learn how to prepare traditional dishes such as Pad Thai, Tom Yum Soup, and Som Tam.

Firefly Tour

One of the highlights of visiting Amphawa is the chance to see thousands of fireflies lighting up the night sky. Visitors can take a boat tour of the river to see the fireflies in action.

Week-Long Itinerary for Amphawa

Food and Drink Scene of Amphawa

Must-Try Dishes

Amphawa Grilled Seafood

Amphawa is famous for its grilled seafood, which is cooked fresh by local vendors at the floating market.

Khanom Bueang

Khanom Bueang is a popular Thai dessert that is made with crispy crepes filled with sweet or savory ingredients such as coconut, egg, or shrimp.

Local Beverages

Nam Kang Sai

Nam Kang Sai is a refreshing drink made with shaved ice, sweet syrup, and various toppings such as jelly, beans, and fruit.

Cha Yen

Cha Yen is a sweet Thai iced tea that is made with black tea, milk, and sugar.

Where to Stay in Amphawa: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Amphawa Na Non Hotel & Spa

Amphawa Na Non Hotel & Spa is a luxury hotel located near the Amphawa Floating Market. The hotel features a spa, outdoor pool, and restaurant serving traditional Thai cuisine.

Baan Amphawa Resort & Spa

Baan Amphawa Resort & Spa is a beautiful resort located on the banks of the Amphawa Canal. The resort features traditional Thai-style villas, a spa, and a restaurant.

What to Buy in Amphawa: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Handmade Crafts

Amphawa is known for its handmade crafts, including ceramics, textiles, and jewelry. Visitors can purchase these items at the local markets and shops.

Local Food Products

Visitors should not miss the chance to try local food products such as coconut sugar, palm sugar, and fish sauce, which are made by local producers and sold at the markets.

Local Culture and Customs of Amphawa

Amphawa is a predominantly Buddhist town, and visitors should respect local customs and traditions. Visitors should dress conservatively when visiting temples, remove their shoes before entering buildings, and show respect to monks and elders.

Best Time to Visit Amphawa

The best time to visit Amphawa is between November and February when the weather is dry and cool. However, visitors should be aware that this is also the peak tourist season, and crowds can be an issue.

Do’s and Don’ts of Amphawa

Do:

  • Dress conservatively when visiting temples and other religious sites.
  • Remove your shoes before entering buildings.
  • Show respect to monks and elders.

Don’t:

  • Touch or disrespect Buddhist statues or shrines.
  • Litter or damage the natural environment.
  • Use drugs or engage in illegal activities.

Safety and Health

Safety in Amphawa

Amphawa is generally a safe town for visitors. However, visitors should be aware of pickpockets and keep an eye on their belongings in crowded places.

Health in Amphawa

Visitors should take precautions to avoid mosquito bites and protect themselves from the sun. Visitors should also drink only bottled or boiled water and avoid street food that is not cooked thoroughly.

Getting to Amphawa: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Amphawa

The nearest airport to Amphawa is Suvarnabhumi International Airport in Bangkok, which is approximately 70 kilometers away. Visitors can hire a taxi or take a bus from the airport to Amphawa.

Bus Stations Near Amphawa

Visitors can also reach Amphawa by bus from Bangkok’s Southern Bus Terminal. Buses run regularly throughout the day, and the journey takes approximately two hours.

Other Options

Visitors can also hire a private car or take a taxi from Bangkok to Amphawa. This option offers more flexibility and allows visitors to stop at other attractions along the way.

How to Get Around in Amphawa

The best way to get around Amphawa is on foot or by bicycle. Visitors can also hire a tuk-tuk or take a boat tour of the river to explore the town and nearby attractions.

Where to Go Next from Amphawa: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Bangkok

Bangkok is just a two-hour bus ride from Amphawa and offers visitors endless shopping, dining, and sightseeing options, including the Grand Palace, Wat Arun, and Chatuchak Weekend Market.

Kanchanaburi

Kanchanaburi is a beautiful province located in western Thailand and is famous for its waterfalls, national parks, and historical sites such as the Bridge over the River Kwai.

Conclusion

Amphawa is a charming, off-the-beaten-path destination in Thailand that offers visitors an authentic taste of local life. From its floating market and cultural attractions to its natural beauty and delicious food, there is something for everyone in Amphawa. With this comprehensive travel guide, visitors can plan their trip with ease and make the most of their time in this beautiful town.
