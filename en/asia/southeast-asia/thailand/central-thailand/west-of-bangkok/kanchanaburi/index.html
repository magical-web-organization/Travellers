Introduction to Kanchanaburi

Kanchanaburi is a charming town located in central Thailand’s Kanchanaburi Province. Known for its beautiful natural scenery and rich historical significance, Kanchanaburi has become a favorite destination of both locals and tourists alike. The town is easily accessible from Bangkok, just 130 km to the west at the confluence of the Kwai Noi and Kwai Yai rivers.

Kanchanaburi was an important hub during World War II, and the infamous Bridge over the River Kwai is one of its most popular attractions. However, the town has much more to offer beyond its historical significance, as it is surrounded by mountains, waterfalls, and national parks.

This travel guide to Kanchanaburi will provide you with a list of must-see attractions, activities, food, and accommodations. Read on to plan your perfect trip to Kanchanaburi.

List of Places to Visit in Kanchanaburi

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Bridge over the River Kwai: This iconic steel bridge is a must-visit attraction for anyone in Kanchanaburi. It is the site of one of the most tragic stories of World War II and was immortalized in the famous novel and movie. Today, it is a symbol of hope and reconciliation between nations.
  • Hellfire Pass: This historic site is where Allied POWs and Asian conscripts were forced to dig a railway through a mountain during World War II. Visitors can walk through the museum and the nearby trails to learn about the harrowing conditions under which the railway was constructed.
  • War Cemetery: Located near the River Kwai, the cemetery contains the graves of thousands of Allied prisoners of war who died while building the railway. It is a solemn yet peaceful place to pay respects to those who lost their lives during the construction of the infamous railway.

Natural Attractions of Kanchanaburi: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Erawan National Park: A popular attraction for hikers, Erawan National Park is a beautiful natural reserve known for its stunning waterfalls and emerald green pools. The park is home to a wide variety of flora and fauna, making it a paradise for nature lovers.
  • Sai Yok Noi Waterfall: Located in the Sai Yok National Park, Sai Yok Noi waterfall is one of the most picturesque waterfalls in all of Thailand. Visitors can swim in the crystal-clear waters or take a boat ride along the river.
  • Elephant’s World: This sanctuary for elephants offers visitors the chance to interact with these gentle giants in a responsible and sustainable way. You can feed, bathe, and even help with the daily care of the elephants.

Hiking/Biking Routes

  • Mueang Sing Historical Park: This historic park is home to the ruins of an ancient city that dates back to the 13th century. Visitors can bike or hike through the park and admire the beautifully preserved ruins.
  • Srinakarin Dam: This impressive dam is not only a feat of engineering but also provides a beautiful scenic route for bikers and hikers. Visitors can enjoy the stunning views of the dam and its surrounding landscape.

Best Things to Do in Kanchanaburi: Activities and Tours

  • Death Railway Train Ride: One of the most popular activities in Kanchanaburi is taking a ride on the Death Railway. The train ride offers stunning views of the surrounding mountain ranges and the River Kwai, and is particularly popular for its panoramic view across the bridge.
  • River Rafting: The River Kwai is a great spot for adventure seekers who want to experience the thrill of white-water rafting. Visitors can choose from several different courses, depending on their skill level and experience.
  • Night Markets: Kanchanaburi is home to several night markets where visitors can find a variety of local products and souvenirs. The 13 Market and Kanchanaburi Night Market are popular market places among visitors.

Food and Drink Scene of Kanchanaburi

Must-Try Dishes

  • Khao Soi: A popular dish in northern Thailand, Khao Soi is a coconut curry soup served with egg noodles and chicken. In Kanchanaburi, it is made with a unique twist.
  • Som Tam: A classic of Thai cuisine, Som Tam is a spicy salad made with shredded papaya, tomatoes, chilies, and fish sauce. It is a great dish to cool off on a hot day.
  • Kanom Jeen: Another northern Thai specialty, Kanom Jeen is a dish made of fermented rice noodles served with a spicy curry.

Local Beverages

  • Cha Yen: Also known as Thai iced tea, Cha Yen is a sweet, creamy, and refreshing drink made of black tea, condensed milk, and spices.
  • Chang Beer: Chang Beer is one of the most popular beers in Thailand, and it is the perfect drink to pair with spicy Thai food.
  • Lemongrass Tea: Slightly sweet and refreshing, this tea is made from lemongrass and sweetened with honey. It is also believed to have certain health benefits.

Where to Stay in Kanchanaburi: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Kanchanaburi has a variety of accommodations to choose from, including hotels, hostels, and guesthouses. Here are some recommendations:

  • The Felix River Kwai Resort: A luxurious resort with stunning views of the River Kwai, The Felix River Kwai Resort features an outdoor infinity pool and beautifully landscaped gardens.
  • Jolly Frog Backpackers: A budget-friendly hostel that offers clean and comfortable rooms, Jolly Frog Backpackers is a great choice for backpackers looking for an affordable place to stay.
  • Hobo Hostel: This quirky and cozy hostel offers both dormitories and private rooms. The social area is a perfect place to lounge and meet fellow travellers.

What to Buy in Kanchanaburi: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Maeklong Railway Market: This market is famous for its unique location on a railway line. Visitors can buy a variety of local products such as fresh fruits, vegetables, and souvenirs.
  • Candle Light Cultural Centre: The centre features a unique candle-making workshop where visitors can learn to make candles with traditional methods. Visitors can also purchase candles as souvenirs.
  • Jewelry Stones: Kanchanaburi is known for its emeralds, rubies and sapphires. The jewelry stones are authentic and locally sourced, making them a great souvenir for those who appreciate natural gemstones.

Local Culture and Customs of Kanchanaburi

Thailand is a predominantly Buddhist country, and Kanchanaburi is no exception. Visitors should dress appropriately when visiting temples and other religious sites, covering their shoulders and knees. It is also important to remove your shoes before entering temples or someone’s home.

Best Time to Visit Kanchanaburi

The best time to visit Kanchanaburi is between November and February, when the weather is cooler and drier. The rainy season begins in March and lasts until November, so it is best to avoid visiting during these months.

Do’s and Don’ts of Kanchanaburi

Do:

  • Dress appropriately when visiting religious sites
  • Respect the local culture and customs
  • Bargain when shopping at markets

Don’t:

  • Touch someone’s head, as it is considered impolite
  • Use your feet to point at someone or something, as it is a sign of disrespect
  • Raise your voice or show anger in public, as it is considered inappropriate

Safety and Health

Safety in Kanchanaburi

Kanchanaburi is generally a safe destination for tourists, but visitors should take precautions to stay safe, such as:

  • Avoiding walking alone at night
  • Keeping an eye on your valuables in crowded areas
  • Using licensed taxis or Grab for transportation

Health in Kanchanaburi

While Kanchanaburi does not have any major health risks, visitors should take precautions to avoid common illnesses such as food poisoning or mosquito-borne diseases. It is important to drink only bottled or boiled water, avoid uncooked or undercooked food, and wear mosquito repellent when outdoors.

Getting to Kanchanaburi: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Kanchanaburi

The nearest airport to Kanchanaburi is Don Mueang International Airport in Bangkok, which is approximately 150 km away.

Bus Stations Near Kanchanaburi

The most convenient way to get to Kanchanaburi is by bus from Bangkok or other major cities in Thailand. The main bus station in Kanchanaburi is Kanchanaburi Bus Station.

Other Options

Visitors can also choose to take a train or taxi from Bangkok to Kanchanaburi.

How to Get Around in Kanchanaburi

The most convenient way to get around Kanchanaburi is by hiring a tuk-tuk or a taxi. Visitors who want to explore the town on their own can also rent a bicycle or a motorbike.

Where to Go Next from Kanchanaburi: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Ayutthaya: The ancient city of Ayutthaya is located just a few hours north of Kanchanaburi, making it a popular day trip destination for visitors. Ayutthaya is known for its ancient temples and ruins, making it a must-visit destination for history buffs.
  • Bangkok: The vibrant and bustling city of Bangkok is just a few hours’ drive from Kanchanaburi. Visitors can explore the city’s temples, markets, and famous nightlife.
  • Damnoen Saduak Floating Market: Located just a short distance from Kanchanaburi, Damnoen Saduak Floating Market is a bustling and colorful market where visitors can buy local products and souvenirs.

Conclusion

Kanchanaburi is a remarkable town that offers a diverse array of attractions for visitors. From its historical significance to its beautiful natural scenery, Kanchanaburi is a destination that has something for everyone.
