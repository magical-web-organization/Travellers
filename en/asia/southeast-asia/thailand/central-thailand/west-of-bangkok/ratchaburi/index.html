Introduction to Ratchaburi

Located in central Thailand, Ratchaburi is a province that is known for its rich history, stunning natural beauty, and vibrant culture. This region, located just west of Bangkok, offers a wide range of attractions and activities for visitors of all ages and interests.

From exploring ancient temples to hiking through lush forests, there is something for everyone in Ratchaburi. This travel guide will take you through the best things to see and do, as well as provide practical information to help you plan your trip to this charming province.

List of Places to Visit in Ratchaburi

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Ratchaburi is home to several cultural and historical landmarks, which are must-visit attractions for history buffs and architecture enthusiasts.

Wat Phra Si Ratana Mahathat

One of the most impressive Buddhist temples in the country and the most famous temple in Ratchaburi, Wat Phra Si Ratana Mahathat is a symbol of the province with its large golden chedi and pagoda-style roofs. Here, visitors can view the relic of Lord Buddha and take in the breathtaking views of the surrounding landscapes.

Khao Ngu Stone Park

Another highlight in Ratchaburi is the Khao Ngu Stone Park. It is a unique geological formation made of limestone caves and features beautiful rock formations, stalactites, and stalagmites. Visitors can explore the caves and admire several Buddha images carved out of the stone.

Chompol Cave

Chompol Cave, also known as the “Prison Cave,” is an important historical site in Rachaburi. The cave was used as a political prison during World War II by the Japanese army. The cave has many rock carvings and paintings which tell the story of Thailand’s past.

Natural Attractions of Ratchaburi: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Ratchaburi’s natural scenery is characterized by lush forests, rolling hills, and winding rivers. Visitors can enjoy several outdoor activities such as hiking, cycling, and boating.

Damnoen Saduak Floating Market

Located about 100 km from Bangkok, the Damnoen Saduak Floating Market is a popular tourist destination. Here, visitors can take a boat ride through the canal, check out the local crafts and taste the delicious local food.

Khao Bin Cave

One of the most significant limestone caves in Ratchaburi is Khao Bin cave. With its stunning stalactites, stalagmites, and rock formations, this cave is a mesmerizing sight to see. Visitors can go for a walk around the cave, look for the hidden cave passages, and relish breathtaking views of the surrounding mountains.

Kaeng Khoi Reservoir

The Kaeng Khoi Reservoir is a popular spot for boating, fishing, and cycling. Visitors can enjoy a peaceful stroll around the reservoir, take a boat ride to view the scenic views of the surrounding hills, and try their luck catching some fish.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Ratchaburi

There are several hiking and biking routes in Ratchaburi that are perfect for adventure enthusiasts.

Chet Khot Waterfall

Chet Khot waterfall is a famous natural attraction in Ratchaburi province. It has a height of over 40 meters and is surrounded by a lush forest. Visitors can go for a hike through the park to reach the waterfall, take a dip in the natural pools or have a picnic near the river.

Khao Chon Waterfall

Another popular waterfall that is worth a visit is the Khao Chon Waterfall. It is a multi-tiered waterfall and is surrounded by tropical greenery. Visitors can enjoy the stunning views of the waterfall while trekking in the natural surroundings.

Best Things to Do in Ratchaburi: Activities and Tours

Ratchaburi offers a wide range of activities and tours, suitable for visitors of all ages and interests.

Explore the Night Markets

Ratchaburi’s night markets rank among the best in Thailand. Here, visitors can try local delicacies, shop for souvenirs, and experience the local vibe. The most popular night market is the Ratchaburi Walking Street, which takes place every Saturday.

Visit the Khao Sam Roi Yot National Park

Khao Sam Roi Yot National Park is the largest park in Thailand. This natural reserve is home to many rare wildlife species and features several natural attractions such as caves, beaches, and viewpoints.

Take a Relaxed Bike Tour

Explore the Ratchaburi countryside on a leisurely bike tour. Visitors can experience the peace and tranquility of the rural province and take in the breathtaking views of the fields, orchards, and mountains.

Week-Long Itinerary for Ratchaburi

Here is a week-long itinerary for visitors looking to explore Ratchaburi’s best attractions.

Day Activities
1 Explore Wat Phra Si Ratana Mahathat, Khao Ngu Stone Park, and the Chompol Cave.
2 Take a boat ride through the Damnoen Saduak Floating Market and visit the Khao Bin cave.
3 Go cycling around Kaeng Khoi Reservoir and visit the Suan Phueng district.
4 Trek to the Chet Khot Waterfall and visit the Ratchaburi Walking Street night market.
5 Check out the Khao Sam Roi Yot National Park.
6 Visit the Khao Chon Waterfall and take a bike tour around the rural areas.
7 Relax and unwind at your hotel or guesthouse.

Food and Drink Scene of Ratchaburi

Ratchaburi’s food and drink scene is dominated by Thai cuisine, with many local specialties and street food options.

Must-Try Dishes

Jok krathiam

Jok krathiam is a popular breakfast dish in Ratchaburi. It is a type of rice porridge that is cooked with garlic and served with pork or chicken.

Kao Chae

Kao Chae is a traditional Thai dish served in Ratchaburi during the summer season. The dish consists of a mixture of rice, iced jasmine-scented water, and topped with a variety of side dishes such as fried sweet peppers, crispy pork, and pickled carrots.

Kanom Jeen Ratchaburi

Kanom Jeen is a spicy noodle dish that originated in Ratchaburi. The noodles are made from fermented rice and are served with a spicy curry sauce, bean sprouts, and a variety of vegetables.

Local Beverages

Cha Yen

Cha Yen is the famous local drink of Thailand. It is a sweet milk tea that is served over ice and is perfect for cooling off on a hot day in Ratchaburi.

Nam Taohoo

Nam Taohoo is a refreshing drink made from fresh soy milk, sugar, and ice. It is a perfect beverage to quench thirst on a hot summer day in Ratchaburi.

Where to Stay in Ratchaburi: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Ratchaburi has many accommodation options suitable for all budgets. Visitors can choose from luxurious resorts, budget hotels, guesthouses, homestays, and hostels.

Villa Maroc Resort

Villa Maroc Resort is a luxurious resort located in the scenic Suan Phueng district. This Moroccan-inspired resort features stunning views of the surrounding mountains and offers guests a serene and peaceful retreat.

The Harmony Resort

The Harmony Resort is an eco-friendly resort located near the Damnoen Saduak Floating Market. This tranquil retreat features comfortable accommodations, gardens, and a pool for guests to relax and unwind.

Reindeer Park Resort

Reindeer Park Resort is a unique resort located in the rural area of Ratchaburi. It is ideal for families and couples looking to stay in a peaceful and serene environment.

What to Buy in Ratchaburi: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Visitors to Ratchaburi can bring home several unique souvenirs and local products.

Pottery and Ceramics

Ratchaburi has a long history of pottery and ceramic production. Visitors can buy beautiful handcrafted pottery and ceramics from the local shops and markets.

Khao Lam

Khao Lam is a popular local snack made from sticky rice cooked in bamboo tubes with coconut milk, sugar, and salt. Visitors can buy this delicious snack from the local markets as a perfect souvenir.

Local Culture and Customs of Ratchaburi

Ratchaburi’s local culture and customs are heavily influenced by Buddhism. Visitors should dress appropriately when visiting temples and other cultural attractions, covering their shoulders and legs. It is also important to respect local customs and traditions.

Best Time to Visit Ratchaburi

The best time to visit Ratchaburi is during the dry season, from November to February, when the weather is cool and dry. Visitors can enjoy outdoor activities and comfortably explore the local attractions without worrying about heavy rainfall or humidity.

Do’s and Don’ts of Ratchaburi

Do:

  • Dress appropriately when visiting temples and cultural attractions.
  • Try the local food and drink.
  • Learn about the local culture and customs.

Don’t:

  • behave disrespectfully towards the local customs and traditions.
  • Litter or harm the environment.
  • Ignore the advice of locals regarding safety and security.

Safety and Health

Safety in Ratchaburi

Like in other parts of Thailand, visitors to Ratchaburi should be aware of their surroundings and take necessary precautions to ensure their safety. Travelers should secure their valuables and belongings, particularly in crowded areas and tourist attractions.

Health in Ratchaburi

Ratchaburi is generally a safe place for visitors. However, visitors should be aware of the potential for mosquito-borne illnesses such as dengue fever and take precautions to prevent mosquito bites. Visitors should also check for local advisories regarding respiratory illnesses and take appropriate measures to avoid them.

Getting to Ratchaburi: Transportation Options and Logistics

Ratchaburi is well-connected by road and air from Bangkok and other major cities in Thailand.

Airports Near Ratchaburi

The nearest airport to Ratchaburi is Don Mueang International Airport in Bangkok, which is approximately 100 km away. Visitors can also fly into Suvarnabhumi Airport in Bangkok and take a connecting taxi or bus to reach Ratchaburi.

Bus Stations Near Ratchaburi

There are several bus stations in Bangkok offering services to Ratchaburi. Visitors can choose to take a bus from the Southern Bus Terminal, the Northern Bus Terminal, or the Eastern Bus Terminal.

Other Options

Visitors can also hire a private taxi or rent a car to reach Ratchaburi. The journey takes about two hours from Bangkok and offers visitors stunning views of the countryside along the way.

How to Get Around in Ratchaburi

Visitors can use several modes of transport to get around Ratchaburi, including tuk-tuks, taxis, motorcycles, and bicycles. Taxis and tuk-tuks can be hailed on the street, while bicycles and motorcycles are available for rent at local shops and guesthouses.

Where to Go Next from Ratchaburi: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Ratchaburi is one of the most accessible provinces from Bangkok and is a great base for exploring the nearby regions.

Hua Hin

Hua Hin is a popular beach resort town located about 100 km south of Ratchaburi. It is famous for its sandy beaches, luxury resorts, and its international golf courses.

Kanchanaburi

Kanchanaburi is a province in western Thailand, known for its beautiful waterfalls, historical landmarks, and natural attractions. Visitors can witness the impressive Bridge over the River Kwai, visit waterfalls, and tour the natural parks.

Conclusion

Ratchaburi is a charming province in Thailand that offers something for every kind of visitor. With its rich history, stunning natural beauty, and vibrant culture, this province is a must-visit destination for travelers looking for an authentic Thai experience. Visitors can explore ancient temples, go hiking and cycling, try local food and drink, and enjoy a peaceful retreat at one of the many accommodations. Plan your trip to Ratchaburi today, and experience all that this beautiful province has to offer.
