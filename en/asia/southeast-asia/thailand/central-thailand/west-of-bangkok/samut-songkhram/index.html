Introduction to Samut Songkhram

Nestled in the west of Bangkok, Samut Songkhram is a small province in Central Thailand. Also known as Mae Klong, the province is renowned for its picturesque scenery, abundant cultural and historical sites, and rich culinary heritage. Despite its close proximity to Bangkok, Samut Songkhram remains relatively undiscovered by tourists, making it an ideal destination for those looking to explore off-the-beaten-track Thailand.

List of Places to Visit in Samut Songkhram

If you’re planning a trip to Samut Songkhram, these must-visit attractions should be on your list:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Amphawa Floating Market - One of Thailand’s most famous floating markets, Amphawa is home to a plethora of food stalls and souvenir shops.
  • Wat Bang Kung - A small temple which has become famous for being overtaken by a banyan tree, creating a unique blend of nature and architecture.
  • King Rama II Memorial Park - A vast park with a stunning palace, a museum showcasing Thai arts and crafts, and beautiful gardens.
  • Don Hoi Lot - A beach with unusual horseshoe crab shapes imprinted on the sand.

Natural Attractions of Samut Songkhram: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Mangrove Forest Conservation Center - A beautiful boardwalk trail through the mangrove forests where you can spot various species of birds.
  • Bang Ka Chao Green Lung - A serene eco-tourism spot that offers a peaceful respite from the hustle and bustle of Bangkok.
  • Tha Kha Floating Market - A lesser-known floating market, where you can savor Thai dishes cooked by local vendors.
  • Wat Tham Khao Wong - A unique temple complex hidden inside a cave which offers fantastic views of the surrounding landscape.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Samut Songkhram

Samut Songkhram offers some excellent hiking and biking routes, including:

  • Bang Kung Camp Bike Trail - A 20-km trail that takes you through historic sites and offers stunning river views.
  • Samut Songkhram Bike Route - A shorter trail that’s great for beginners and takes you through the heart of the province’s rural areas.

Best Things to Do in Samut Songkhram: Activities and Tours

  • Boat tours - One of the most popular activities in Samut Songkhram, boat tours take you through the province’s intricate canal system, allowing you to see local life in action.
  • Cooking classes - Samut Songkhram has a rich culinary heritage, and you can learn how to cook some of its most famous dishes in classes offered by locals.
  • Cycling tours - A great way to explore the province’s picturesque countryside, cycling tours can be arranged through local tour operators.
  • Temple visits - Samut Songkhram is home to some of Thailand’s most unique and impressive temples, including those built within caves and overgrown with trees.

Week-Long Itinerary for Samut Songkhram

With so many things to see and do, planning a week-long itinerary for Samut Songkhram is easy. Here’s one possible plan:

  • Day 1: Explore Amphawa Floating Market and visit Wat Bang Kung.
  • Day 2: Learn about Thai culture and history at King Rama II Memorial Park and Tha Kha Floating Market.
  • Day 3: Experience the province’s natural beauty with a visit to Mangrove Forest Conservation Center and Bang Ka Chao Green Lung.
  • Day 4: Take a cooking class and sample some traditional Thai dishes.
  • Day 5: Go on a cycling tour of the countryside and visit the Wat Tham Khao Wong temple complex.
  • Day 6: Take a boat tour along the canals and visit more temples.
  • Day 7: Relax on the beach at Don Hoi Lot and shop for souvenirs at local markets.

Food and Drink Scene of Samut Songkhram

Samut Songkhram is known for its delicious and unique dishes, which incorporate local ingredients like shrimp paste, salted fish, and rice. Some of the must-try dishes include:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Ta Champa - A creamy and tangy curry made with shrimp paste, coconut cream, and vegetables.
  • Khanom Chin - A fermented rice noodle dish served with various curries and sauces.
  • Hoi Chak Teen - A savory shrimp and vegetable soup made with the leaves of the Pong Kan tree.

Local Beverages

  • Nam Chuet - A refreshing drink made with a blend of fruits and herbs.
  • Cha Suan - A sweet and savory tea made with Chinese herbs and sweetened with sugar.
  • Cha Manao - A lime juice drink that’s perfect for hot days.

Where to Stay in Samut Songkhram: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Samut Songkhram has a range of accommodation options to suit every budget. Some popular choices include:

  • Ruen Nam Yen Resort - A beautiful resort with a pool and stunning river views.
  • Baan Amphawa - A boutique hotel located in a restored 100-year-old house.
  • Thai Cat Hostel - A clean and cozy hostel with a charming atmosphere.
  • Baan Kornnara - A traditional Thai guesthouse with beautifully decorated rooms.

What to Buy in Samut Songkhram: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Samut Songkhram offers a variety of unique souvenirs and local products. Some popular choices include:

  • Salted Fish - A specialty of this province, salted fish is a perfect gift for friends and family.
  • Coconut Sugar - Made from pure coconut sap, coconut sugar is a healthier alternative to regular sugar.
  • Handmade Crafts - From traditional Thai textiles to intricate wood carvings, Samut Songkhram has a range of handmade crafts that make for unique souvenirs.

Local Culture and Customs of Samut Songkhram

Like the rest of Thailand, Samut Songkhram has a rich cultural heritage that’s worth learning about. Some customs to keep in mind include:

  • Removing your shoes - It’s customary to remove your shoes before entering someone’s home or a temple.
  • Respecting Buddhism - Many of the attractions in Samut Songkhram are temples and religious sites, so it’s important to dress modestly and behave respectfully.
  • Greeting with a wai - The traditional Thai greeting is a wai, which involves placing your hands together in a prayer position and bowing your head.

Best Time to Visit Samut Songkhram

The best time to visit Samut Songkhram is during the dry season, which runs from November to February. The weather is cooler and less humid during this time, making it ideal for outdoor activities. However, if you’re looking to see the floating markets in action, it’s best to visit during the weekends or public holidays.

Do’s and Don’ts of Samut Songkhram

Do:

  • Try the local cuisine - Samut Songkhram is known for its unique and delicious dishes, so don’t be afraid to try something new.
  • Explore the countryside - Some of the province’s best attractions can be found off the beaten track, so rent a bike or take a hike to discover hidden gems.
  • Engage with locals - Thais are known for their friendliness and hospitality, so don’t be shy to strike up a conversation with locals.

Don’t:

  • Disrespect Buddhism - Thailand is a deeply Buddhist country, so it’s important to show respect to religious sites and practices.
  • Dress inappropriately - It’s important to dress modestly when visiting temples and religious sites. Men should avoid wearing shorts, while women should cover their shoulders and knees.
  • Engage in illegal activities - While Thailand is known for its vibrant nightlife, it’s important to remember that many activities, particularly those involving drugs or prostitution, are illegal and can result in serious consequences.

Safety and Health

Safety in Samut Songkhram

Samut Songkhram is generally a safe and peaceful province, but it’s important to take common sense precautions like not leaving valuables unattended.

Health in Samut Songkhram

Thailand is a developing country, so it’s important to take precautions when it comes to food and drink. Stick to bottled water and avoid street food that looks unsafe or unclean.

Getting to Samut Songkhram: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Samut Songkhram

The closest airport to Samut Songkhram is Suvarnabhumi International Airport in Bangkok, which is approximately 70 kilometers away.

Bus Stations Near Samut Songkhram

Buses from Bangkok to Samut Songkhram depart from the Southern Bus Terminal. They take roughly 2 hours and cost around 50-100 baht.

Other Options

Private taxis and minivans can be hired from Bangkok to Samut Songkhram, which is a convenient option if you’re traveling in a group or with lots of luggage.

How to Get Around in Samut Songkhram

Exploring Samut Songkhram by bike or motorbike is a popular option for visitors. Taxis and tuk-tuks are also widely available, although negotiating a price can be tricky. Many of the main attractions are within walking distance of each other.

Where to Go Next from Samut Songkhram: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Samut Songkhram is located in the heart of Central Thailand, making it easy to explore neighboring provinces like Ratchaburi and Petchaburi. Some other popular destinations include:

  • Kanchanaburi - Famous for the Bridge Over the River Kwai and its World War II history, Kanchanaburi is located around 2 hours from Samut Songkhram.
  • Cha Am and Hua Hin - Popular beach destinations located around 2-3 hours from Samut Songkhram.
  • Bangkok - Thailand’s bustling capital city, Bangkok is located around 1 hour from Samut Songkhram and is popular for shopping, dining, and nightlife.

Conclusion

With its rich cultural heritage, stunning natural scenery, and mouth-watering cuisine, Samut Songkhram is a hidden gem waiting to be discovered. Whether you’re looking for outdoor adventures, indulging in gourmet food, or learning about Thailand’s history and culture, Samut Songkhram has something to offer everyone.
