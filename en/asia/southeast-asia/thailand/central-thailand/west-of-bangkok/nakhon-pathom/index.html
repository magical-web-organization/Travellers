Introduction to Nakhon Pathom

Nakhon Pathom is a historic city in Central Thailand, located just 56 km west of Bangkok. It is known for its ancient temples, historical sites, and beautiful landscapes. The city is also famous for being the birthplace of Buddhism in Thailand. Travelers who are interested in exploring the cultural and natural heritage of Thailand should definitely include Nakhon Pathom in their itinerary.

List of Places to Visit in Nakhon Pathom

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Phra Pathom Chedi: Phra Pathom Chedi is a 127-metre tall stupa, one of the tallest in the world and the largest in Thailand. It is located in the heart of Nakhon Pathom and considered as one of the most important Buddhist sites in the country.
  • Sanam Chan Palace: Sanam Chan Palace is a beautiful palace constructed in 1907 and used as a royal residence of King Rama VI during his visits to Nakhon Pathom. The palace has a museum that showcases the history of the palace and its royal inhabitants.
  • Wat Bang Phra: Wat Bang Phra is a Buddhist temple well-known for its Sak Yant tattooing tradition. Visitors can get traditional Thai tattoos while receiving blessings from the monks.

Natural Attractions of Nakhon Pathom: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Don Wai Market: Don Wai Market is a popular floating market in Nakhon Pathom. It is located on the river and offers a wide variety of Thai foods, including seafood, grilled meats, and sweet desserts.
  • Don Hoi Lot: Don Hoi Lot is a famous seafood market located on the Gulf of Thailand. Visitors can watch the local fishermen casting their nets for crabs and shellfish.
  • Nakhon Pathom National Forest: Nakhon Pathom National Forest is a beautiful natural park located just outside the city. It features a variety of trails for hiking and bike riding.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Nakhon Pathom

  • Nakhon Pathom to Kanchanaburi Bike Trail: This 140 km bike trail is one of the most scenic routes in Thailand. It starts in Nakhon Pathom and takes you through the scenic countryside all the way to Kanchanaburi.
  • Nakhon Pathom Loop Bike Trail: This 30 km bike trail is perfect for an easy afternoon ride. It takes you through the local villages, rice fields, and orchards surrounding Nakhon Pathom.

Best Things to Do in Nakhon Pathom: Activities and Tours

  • Thai Cooking Class: Learn how to cook traditional Thai dishes with a local chef.
  • Bike Tour: Take a bicycle tour of the city to explore its hidden gems.
  • Thai Massage: Unwind with a relaxing Thai massage at one of the local spas.

Food and Drink Scene of Nakhon Pathom

Must-Try Dishes

  • Kuay Tiew Kua Gai: Stir-fried noodle with chicken and vegetables.
  • Som Tam Pu Ma: Spicy papaya salad made with crab.
  • Tom Yum Kung: A hot and sour seafood soup made with shrimp.

Local Beverages

  • Cha Yen: A sweet and creamy Thai iced tea.
  • Nam Manao: A refreshing lime juice.
  • Sato: A local wine made from fermented rice.

Where to Stay in Nakhon Pathom: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Baan Jantra Place: A comfortable and affordable hotel located in the heart of the city.
  • Plearn Hostel: A cozy and clean hostel perfect for backpackers.
  • Baan Jumpa Residence: A charming guesthouse surrounded by lush gardens.

What to Buy in Nakhon Pathom: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Thai Silk: Colorful and beautiful Thai silk products make perfect souvenirs.
  • Handicrafts: Nakhon Pathom is known for its handicrafts, including basketry and ceramics.
  • Thai Snacks and Spices: Buy some local snacks and spices to take home and experience Thai flavors.

Local Culture and Customs of Nakhon Pathom

Nakhon Pathom is a predominantly Buddhist city, so visitors should be respectful when visiting temples and other religious sites. Dress conservatively and remove your shoes when entering temples. It is also customary to bow before a Buddha statue as a sign of respect.

Best Time to Visit Nakhon Pathom

The best time to visit Nakhon Pathom is from November to February when the weather is cool and dry. Avoid visiting during the rainy season from June to October.

Do’s and Don’ts of Nakhon Pathom

Do:

  • Respect the local culture and customs.
  • Try the local food and drinks.
  • Bargain at local markets.

Don’t:

  • Disrespect religious sites or customs.
  • Engage in illegal activities.
  • Touch or feed wild animals.

Safety and Health

Safety in Nakhon Pathom

Nakhon Pathom is a relatively safe city, but visitors should take precautions and avoid travelling alone at night. Be aware of your surroundings and keep your valuables safe.

Health in Nakhon Pathom

Make sure to stay hydrated and protect yourself from the sun. Use mosquito repellent and take precautions to avoid mosquito-borne diseases such as Dengue fever.

Getting to Nakhon Pathom: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Nakhon Pathom

The nearest airport to Nakhon Pathom is Suvarnabhumi Airport in Bangkok, which is about an hour’s drive away.

Bus Stations Near Nakhon Pathom

Nakhon Pathom Bus Station is located in the city center and offers regular services to Bangkok and other cities in Thailand.

Other Options

Taxis and private cars are also available for hire.

How to Get Around in Nakhon Pathom

The best way to get around Nakhon Pathom is by bicycle or motorcycle. Taxis and tuk-tuks are also readily available.

Where to Go Next from Nakhon Pathom: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Kanchanaburi: A scenic city located three hours northwest of Nakhon Pathom known for its historical sites and natural attractions.
  • Ayutthaya: An ancient city located two hours northeast of Nakhon Pathom known for its temples and ruins.

Conclusion

Nakhon Pathom is a beautiful city worth visiting for its cultural and natural heritage and delicious food. With this comprehensive travel guide, planning your perfect trip to Nakhon Pathom should be easy and stress-free.
