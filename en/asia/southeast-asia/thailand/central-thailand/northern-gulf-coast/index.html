Northern Gulf Coast, located in Central Thailand, is a stunning region that boasts a mix of natural beauty, rich cultural heritage, and modern developments. From pristine beaches to ancient temples and bustling cities, Northern Gulf Coast has something for every type of traveler. In this comprehensive travel guide, we will cover everything you need to know before planning your trip to Northern Gulf Coast, Thailand.

Introduction to Northern Gulf Coast

Northern Gulf Coast is an expansive region that comprises several provinces, including Prachuap Khiri Khan, Chumphon, Surat Thani, and Nakhon Si Thammarat. The region features a diverse landscape, ranging from lush forests to rolling hills and stunning beaches. Northern Gulf Coast is also home to several important historical and cultural landmarks, including ancient temples and museums.

List of Places to Visit in Northern Gulf Coast

If you plan to visit Northern Gulf Coast, there are several places you should add to your itinerary. Here are some of the top attractions to visit in Northern Gulf Coast:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Phra Pathom Chedi, Nakhon Pathom
  • The National Museum of Phra Nakhon Si Ayutthaya
  • Wat Phra Mahathat Woramahawihan, Nakhon Si Thammarat
  • Chumphon National Museum, Chumphon

Natural Attractions of Northern Gulf Coast: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Khao Sam Roi Yot National Park, Prachuap Khiri Khan
  • Koh Tao, Surat Thani
  • Thale Noi Waterbird Sanctuary, Phatthalung
  • Hat Khanom - Mu Ko Thale Tai National Park, Nakhon Si Thammarat

Northern Gulf Coast offers several natural trails that are ideal for hiking and biking. Discover some of the best trails and scenic routes in the region that will give you a sense of exploration and adventure during your visit.

  • Ban Krut - Saphli Forest Park, Chumphon

  • The Queen’s Park, Nakhon Si Thammarat

Best Things to Do in Northern Gulf Coast: Activities and Tours

In addition to sightseeing, Northern Gulf Coast offers several activities and tours that you can enjoy during your stay. Here are some top things to do in Northern Gulf Coast:

  • Surfing and beach activities in Hua Hin, Prachuap Khiri Khan
  • Snorkeling and scuba diving in Koh Tao, Surat Thani
  • Rafting and kayaking in Khao Sok National Park, Surat Thani
  • Visiting the floating markets of Damnoen Saduak, Ratchaburi

Week-Long Itinerary for Northern Gulf Coast

If you plan on visiting Northern Gulf Coast for a week, here’s a sample itinerary to help you maximize your trip:

Day 1: Arrive in Bangkok and travel to Phra Nakhon Si Ayutthaya for a tour of the historic city.

Day 2: Visit the National Museum of Phra Nakhon Si Ayutthaya and explore the Wat Phra Sri Sanphet to witness ancient architecture.

Day 3: Spend a day at Khao Sam Roi Yot National Park for hiking, exploring caves, and other outdoor activities.

Day 4: Visit Koh Tao and enjoy a snorkeling or scuba diving trip at Koh Nang Yuan.

Day 5: Explore Koh Samui and visit the famous Big Buddha Temple.

Day 6: Take a trip to Surat Thani and explore Khao Sok National Park for camping, kayaking, and trekking.

Day 7: End the trip with a visit to Nakhon Si Thammarat to explore the ancient temples.

Food and Drink Scene of Northern Gulf Coast

Northern Gulf Coast offers several unique culinary experiences, from street food to traditional dishes. Here are some must-try dishes and beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Pad Thai, a stir-fry noodle dish that is synonymous with Thai cuisine
  • Tom Yum Soup, a spicy and sour soup with shrimp or chicken
  • Massaman Curry, a Muslim-influenced curry made from beef, potatoes, and peanuts
  • Khao Niao Mamuang, a sweet sticky rice dessert with mangoes

Local Beverages

  • Cha yen, Thai iced tea made from black tea, condensed milk, and sugar
  • Chang Beer, a popular local beer in Thailand
  • Nam Manao, a refreshing lime juice drink with salt and sugar

Local Culture and Customs of Northern Gulf Coast

The local culture of Northern Gulf Coast is heavily influenced by Buddhism, and visitors should be mindful of customs and dress codes at temples and other religious sites. It is also customary to remove shoes before entering someone’s house.

Best Time to Visit Northern Gulf Coast

Northern Gulf Coast experiences a tropical climate, with temperatures averaging around 30°C (86°F) throughout the year. The best time to visit Northern Gulf Coast is between November and May when the weather is cooler and drier.

Do’s and Don’ts of Northern Gulf Coast

Do:

  • Dress modestly when visiting temples and other religious sites.
  • Be respectful of local customs and culture.
  • Try to learn some basic Thai phrases to communicate with locals.

Don’t:

  • Touch someone’s head in Northern Gulf Coast, as it is considered disrespectful.
  • Raise your voice or engage in arguments in public.

Safety and Health

Safety in Northern Gulf Coast

Northern Gulf Coast is generally safe for travelers, but visitors should exercise caution when traveling to remote areas or traveling at night. Pickpocketing and theft are the most common crimes in Thailand, so be mindful of your belongings at all times.

Health in Northern Gulf Coast

Health facilities in Northern Gulf Coast are generally good, but visitors should take precautions against common illnesses such as dengue fever and Zika virus. It is also recommended to drink bottled water and to use insect repellent when outdoors.
