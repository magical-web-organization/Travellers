A Comprehensive Guide to Ban Krut, Thailand

Located in Central Thailand’s Northern Gulf Coast, Ban Krut is a hidden gem within the country’s many destinations. For years, it has been a lesser-known destination as compared to its flashier, more popular counterparts such as Phuket or Pattaya; however, Ban Krut’s pristine beaches, lush greenery, and rich cultural history make it a must-visit for travelers looking for a more off-the-beaten-path experience. In this comprehensive guide, we’ll explore the best things to do, how to get around, where to stay, what to eat, and much more to help you plan a perfect trip.

List of Places to Visit in Ban Krut

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  1. Ban Krut Temple: Explore the local temple, which is over 400 years old and is a religious and cultural center for the community.
  2. Ban Krut Museum: Learn about the area’s history, culture, and traditions, with exhibits dating all the way back to the 17th century.
  3. Ban Krut Art Gallery: Take a look at some of the incredibly talented local artists who showcase their work in the gallery.

Natural Attractions of Ban Krut: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  1. Thalay Ban Krut Beach: Lounge on the clean, quiet, and tranquil beach where you can unwind and soak up the sun. You can also swim or go snorkeling in the clear waters.
  2. Khao Kalok: Enjoy a scenic view of the coast by hiking or biking up Khao Kalok Mountain, which takes about 30 minutes to reach the peak.
  3. Ban Krud Mangrove Forest Conservation: Take a tour along the mangrove forest trails, which is a popular spot for bird watching.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Ban Krut

You can explore the beautiful environment of Ban Krut by following the trails along Khao Kalok mountain. The trail offers a fantastic view of the coastline, and there are many stops to experience the scenic views or take a break. If you prefer biking, you can rent a bike from any of the rental shops nearby and cycle through the mountain trails.

Best Things to Do in Ban Krut: Activities and Tours

  1. Snorkeling and Diving: Experience the underwater beauty of the area by trying snorkeling or diving.
  2. Thai Cooking Lessons: Learn how to cook delicious Thai meals from local chefs.
  3. Boat Tours: Take a boat tour to explore the scenic beauty of the area from a different perspective.
  4. Fishing Trips: Enjoy a day of fishing, and catch various varieties of fish such as barracuda or tuna.

Food and Drink Scene of Ban Krut

Must-Try Dishes

  1. Tom Yum Goong: A spicy Thai soup with prawns, lemongrass, chili, and lime leaves.
  2. Pad Thai: A classic Thai dish made with stir-fried rice noodles, tofu, shrimp, egg, bean sprouts, and peanuts.
  3. Som Tum: A spicy raw papaya salad made with chili, garlic, green beans, peanuts, tomatoes, and lime juice.

Local Beverages

  1. Thai Iced Tea: A sweet tea made with condensed milk and sugar.
  2. Singha Beer: A popular Thai beer that pairs perfectly with spicy food.

Where to Stay in Ban Krut: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  1. Baan Talay Dao Resort: A luxury resort located near Ban Krut Beach, perfect for a peaceful and relaxing getaway.
  2. Ban Krut Resort: A great option for those looking for an affordable yet comfortable place to stay.
  3. Baan Krut Villa: A private villa with a beautiful garden view, perfect for a family or group of travelers.

What to Buy in Ban Krut: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  1. Beachwear and Accessories: Purchase a souvenir of your visit to Ban Krut by buying beachwear such as swimsuits, hats, and towels.
  2. Banana Leaf Wraps: Try local snacks like khanom chaw muang, which are banana leaf wraps filled with vegetables.
  3. Handmade Art and Crafts: Ban Krut’s artisan community creates unique handmade art, pottery, and crafts that you won’t find anywhere else.

Local Culture and Customs of Ban Krut

Like most Thai communities, Ban Krut is known for its hospitality, kindness, and respect towards the elderly and monks. Buddhism is the prevalent religion, visible in the number of temples throughout the area. If you plan to visit a religious site in Ban Krut, make sure to dress appropriately, covering your shoulders and knees. It’s also customary to take off shoes before entering a household or temple.

Best Time to Visit Ban Krut

The best time to visit Ban Krut is between November and February, when the climate is pleasant and mild. However, if you’re looking for a beach holiday, visiting between March and May would be ideal for swimming and sunbathing. Additionally, visiting Ban Krut during the off-season (June to October) can offer budget-friendly prices, but make sure to check the weather forecast beforehand as it’s highly likely to rain.

Do’s and Don’ts of Ban Krut

Do:

  1. Respect the local culture and customs.
  2. Dress appropriately when visiting religious sites.
  3. Take your shoes off before entering a household or temple.

Don’t:

  1. Litter or damage the environment.
  2. Criticize the Thai Royal Family or insult Buddhism.
  3. Touch a monk or a statue of Buddha.

Safety and Health

Safety in Ban Krut

Ban Krut is generally a safe place to visit, but like any tourist destination, petty crime can occur. Ensure that you keep your valuables safe and avoid wandering alone in deserted areas, especially at night.

Health in Ban Krut

Although it’s rare, the risk of contaminated food and drink are present. Drink bottled water or boil tap water before consuming it. Insect-borne diseases such as Malaria or Dengue Fever are present, so it is advisable to take necessary precautions.

Getting to Ban Krut: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Ban Krut

The nearest airport to Ban Krut is Surat Thani Airport (URT), located 180 km away. It’s not feasible to fly directly to Ban Krut, but there are shuttle services available from the airport to Ban Krut.

Bus Stations Near Ban Krut

The nearest bus station to Ban Krut is Bang Saphan Noi bus station, which is around 20 kilometers away. From there, you can take a taxi or a songthaew (shared public transport) to Ban Krut.

Other Options

You can reach Ban Krut via private taxi or hire a car, which is an ideal mode of transport for those who prefer flexibility and independence while traveling.

How to Get Around in Ban Krut

Renting a bike or a scooter is the ideal way to explore Ban Krut. You can also opt for local public transport such as songthaews or tuk-tuks. The best way to get around comfortably is to hire a private taxi, which is readily available throughout the town.

Where to Go Next from Ban Krut: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  1. Hua Hin: Approximately two hours away from Ban Krut, Hua Hin is popular for its colorful markets and happening nightlife.
  2. Phuket: Known for its stunning beaches and lively atmosphere, Phuket is around seven hours away from Ban Krut and is best reached via a domestic flight.
  3. Krabi: Home to magnificent limestone cliffs and turquoise waters, Krabi is an excellent destination for adventure seekers and those who love watersports.

Conclusion

Ban Krut may not be the most famous destination in Thailand, but it offers travelers a unique and authentic experience. From exploring rich cultural and historical sites to relaxing on pristine beaches, Ban Krut has something for everyone. We hope this comprehensive guide has helped you plan a memorable trip to Ban Krut, Thailand.
