Introduction to Mary Province

Located in central Turkmenistan, Mary Province is an ideal destination for adventure seekers and history buffs alike. The region is rich in cultural and historical landmarks, natural beauty and offers a unique insight into Turkmenistan’s culture and traditions.

List of Places to Visit in Mary Province

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Ancient Merv: Located on the ancient Silk Road, Ancient Merv is a UNESCO World Heritage site and one of the most important archaeological sites in Central Asia. The site contains the remains of a series of cities and fortresses, all of which played a crucial role in the Silk Road trade.
  • Gonur Depe: Another archaeological site, Gonur Depe, reveals the ancient civilization that once settled in the area around 2000 BC.
  • Mary Museum: A must-visit for history buffs, the Mary Museum showcases the region’s archaeological discoveries, historical artifacts, and modern art.

Natural Attractions of Mary Province: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Kugitang Nature Reserve: Perfect for nature lovers, the Kugitang Nature Reserve is home to stunning mountain scenery, underground caves, and ancient petroglyphs.
  • Baidaly Nature Reserve: The Baidaly Nature Reserve is another scenic spot, home to abundant flora and fauna. The area is also a habitat for wild goats, which make for an incredible sight.
  • Kopet Dag Mountains: The Kopet Dag Mountains are a great place to explore, featuring hiking trails with panoramic views of the surrounding landscape.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Mary Province

Mary Province offers several scenic hiking and biking routes for those looking for a bit of adventure. Popular routes include:

  • Kopet Dag Ridge Hike: A challenging hike up the Kopet Dag Ridge, offering incredible views of the landscape.
  • Kopet Dag-Alty Karli Hike: A multi-day hike that takes you through several scenic spots, including the Alty Karli canyon and the scenic Kopet Dag Mountains.
  • Merv Bike Tour: A bike tour that takes you through the fascinating ancient city of Merv, passing through its many monuments and ruins.

Best Things to Do in Mary Province: Activities and Tours

  • Camel Trekking: Discover Mary Province on a traditional camel trek with a local guide.
  • Traditional Cuisine Cooking Class: Experience Turkmenistan’s culture and gastronomy by taking a cooking class and learn to prepare traditional local dishes.
  • Horseback Riding: Explore the scenic Kopet Dag Mountains on horseback, and witness the beautiful landscapes of Mary Province from a different perspective.

Week-Long Itinerary for Mary Province

Day 1: Visit Ancient Merv, explore the fortresses and monuments, and gain a deeper understanding of the region’s rich history. Stay overnight in Mary city.

Day 2: Take a day trip to Gonur Depe, one of the largest Bronze Age archaeological sites in the world. Return to Mary city for the night.

Day 3: Explore the Mary Museum, showcasing the region’s history and culture, and visit the nearby park for a picnic. Stay overnight in Mary city.

Day 4-5: Embark on a multi-day hike to Alty Karli canyon and the Kopet Dag Mountains. Take in the breathtaking views and witness the region’s natural beauty.

Day 6: Enjoy a cooking class and learn to make traditional Turkmen cuisine. Stay overnight in Mary city.

Day 7: Take a day trip to the Kugitang Nature Reserve and explore the underground caves and petroglyph sites. Return to Mary city for the night.

Food and Drink Scene of Mary Province

Must-Try Dishes

  • Plov: This traditional Uzbek dish is also extremely popular in Turkmenistan. It consists of rice, meat, onions, and carrots, and is often flavored with spices like cumin and coriander.
  • Manty: Similar to dumplings, manty are a staple food in Central Asia, and often filled with meat or vegetables. They are served with a side of sour cream or yogurt.
  • Shashlik: A barbeque dish of skewered meat - lamb, beef, or chicken - served with a side of vegetables.

Local Beverages

  • Chal: A refreshing sour milk drink enjoyed by locals and usually served after meals.
  • Shisha: A traditional Turkmen tea served in small bowls, and often shared among friends or family.

Where to Stay in Mary Province: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Mary city offers a range of accommodation options for all budgets, from hostels and guesthouses to luxury hotels. Some recommendations include:

  • Mary Hotel: Located in the heart of the city, this hotel offers comfortable rooms, excellent dining options, and a central location.
  • Oguzkent Hotel: A luxurious hotel with great amenities, including a spa, fitness center, and multiple dining options.
  • Ak Altyn Hotel: This budget-friendly hotel offers clean rooms, friendly service, and a great location.

What to Buy in Mary Province: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Traditional Decorative Items: Handmade carpets, traditional embroidery, and other decorative items are popular souvenirs among visitors. These can be found in most markets and bazaars.
  • Handicrafts: The region is known for its traditional handicrafts, including pottery, embroidery, and weaving. Visitors can purchase these at local markets or directly from the artisans themselves.

Local Culture and Customs of Mary Province

Turkmenistan has a rich and unique culture, and it’s essential to respect local customs while visiting. Some tips to keep in mind include:

  • Dress Modestly: Turkmenistan is a conservative country, and visitors should dress modestly, covering their shoulders and knees.
  • Be Respectful of Religious Sites: Visitors to religious sites should dress conservatively and follow the rules and customs of the particular site.
  • Respect Elders: Elders are highly respected in Turkmen culture, and visitors should show them due respect.

Best Time to Visit Mary Province

The best time to visit Mary Province is between April and October when the weather is mild and perfect for outdoor activities. However, keep in mind that summers can be hot and dry, so it’s important to stay hydrated.

Do’s and Don’ts of Mary Province

Do:

  • Try Local Cuisine: Turkmenistan has a unique and delicious cuisine, and visitors should try local dishes and drinks.
  • Respect Local Customs: Visitors should respect local customs and follow any rules and regulations.
  • Engage with Locals: Turkmen people are welcoming and friendly, and engaging with them is a great way to learn more about the culture.

Don’t:

  • Take Photos of Government Buildings: Photography is restricted in some areas, including government buildings and military installations.
  • Public Displays of Affection: Public displays of affection are frowned upon in Turkmenistan and should be avoided.
  • Disrespect Local Customs: Visitors should not disrespect local customs, such as not removing their shoes when entering a mosque or religious site.

Safety and Health

Safety in Mary Province

Turkmenistan is a very safe country, and Mary Province is no exception. However, visitors should take usual precautions, such as not walking alone at night and keeping track of their belongings.

Health in Mary Province

Visitors should drink only bottled water and follow basic hygiene practices. The nearest hospitals are located in Ashgabat, so it’s best to have medical insurance when travelling to Mary Province.

Getting to Mary Province: Transportation Options and Logistics

Mary Province is accessible by air, train, or road.

Airports Near Mary Province

  • Mary International Airport: Serves direct flights from Ashgabat and regional cities.

Bus Stations Near Mary Province

  • Mary Bus Station: Provides regular buses to Ashgabat, and other regions in Turkmenistan.

Other Options

  • Train: There are daily trains connecting Mary Province with Ashgabat, Turkmenistan’s capital city.
  • Private Taxis: Private taxis are also available, but visitors should negotiate the rates before starting their journey.

How to Get Around in Mary Province

While there is public transportation in Mary Province, it’s best to hire a private taxi or rent a car for maximum flexibility. Visitors can also opt to explore the charming city on foot or by bike.

Where to Go Next from Mary Province: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Ashgabat: The capital city of Turkmenistan, Ashgabat has many attractions, including stunning mosques, museums, and parks.
  • Dashoguz: A city located in northern Turkmenistan near the Uzbek border, featuring several cultural landmarks and ancient ruins.

Conclusion

Mary Province is a hidden gem in the heart of Turkmenistan, with a rich history, stunning natural scenery, and unique cultural traditions. From exploring ancient monuments to sampling local cuisine, there’s something for everyone in this charming region.
