Introduction to Merv

Merv, located in Mary Province, Turkmenistan, is an ancient city on the Silk Road and was once one of the world’s largest and most prosperous towns. Today, it is a UNESCO World Heritage site and a fascinating destination for history buffs, culture enthusiasts, and adventurers alike.

The city’s rich history spans more than 2,500 years, from the time of the Persian Empire to the arrival of the Turkish Seljuks and Mongol conquerors. Merv’s strategic location on the trade routes between China and Europe made it a center of commerce, religion, and scholarship.

List of Places to Visit in Merv

Merv has a wealth of cultural and natural attractions that showcase its vast history and stunning landscapes. Here are some must-visit spots in Merv:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Sultan Sanjar Mausoleum – This 12th-century mausoleum is the most significant monument of Merv and hailed as one of the most elegant Islamic buildings in Central Asia.

  • Gyz Gala – This fortress complex was the residence of the Sasanian rulers of the city and features remnants and reconstructions of several Zoroastrian and Christian buildings.

  • Great Kyz Kala – One of the most impressive of Merv’s two-story castles, Great Kyz Kala, was built between the 7th and 9th centuries and was used as a palace.

  • Merv Archaeological Museum – A museum located adjacent to the ruins of Sultan Sanjar Mausoleum exhibit Turkmenistan archeological finds.

Natural Attractions of Merv: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Kopetdag Mountains – A short distance from Merv lies Kopetdag Mountains, a picturesque mountain range home to wildlife and rare plant species. Visitors can go hiking, rock climbing, and horse riding in the area.

  • Satlyk Canyon – This dramatic canyon is a favorite spot for nature lovers, with its red sandstone cliffs, geologic formations, and diverse flora and fauna.

  • Sarykamysh Lake – Trekking to Sarykamysh Lake offers an opportunity to appreciate the natural beauty and peaceful scenery of the Karakum Desert that surrounds it.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Merv

Merv offers several hiking and biking trails, perfect for exploring the city’s historic sites and natural splendor. Some of the top routes include:

  • Merv-Gonur Depe Bike Tour – A unique trail that takes visitors to the ancient Gonur Depe settlement and passes through villages and serene landscapes.

  • Archeological Sites Hiking Tour – A popular route that takes hikers through the heart of Merv’s most significant archaeological sites, such as the Mausoleum of Sultan Sanjar, Bayramaly Mosque, and fortress of Gyz Gala.

Best Things to Do in Merv: Activities and Tours

  • Silk Road Tours – Those who are interested in discovering the historical Silk Road will enjoy this cultural tour that takes visitors from the ancient ruins of Merv to other cities in the region.

  • Merv by Night Tour – This tour takes visitors to explore Sultan Sanjar’s Mausoleum under the moonlight, accompanied by a guide who provides details about the history of Merv.

  • Jeep Tours – Visitors can rent a jeep and drive through the Karakum Desert, marveling at the starkly beautiful scenery and getting a feel for the region’s nomadic lifestyle at outlying villages.

Week-Long Itinerary for Merv

Food and Drink Scene of Merv

Turkmen cuisine is known for its flavorful and hearty dishes, and Merv has many options to satisfy hungry travelers. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Plov – A national dish of Turkmenistan, plov is rice cooked with lamb, carrots, and onions.

  • Manty – Dumplings filled with spiced meat and served with sour cream.

  • Kebab – Deliciously grilled meat, usually lamb or beef.

Local Beverages

  • Chal – A traditional Turkmen drink made from fermented camel’s milk.

  • Shorba – A salty broth made from sheep’s head and served with bread.

Where to Stay in Merv: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

There are several accommodations available in Merv, offering varying levels of comfort.

  • Hotel Margush – A modern, comfortable hotel located near the ruins of Merv, featuring a restaurant and souvenir shop.

  • Mary Hotel – A hotel located in the nearby city of Mary that provides clean, spacious rooms and friendly service.

  • Yurt Camps – Offer an authentic nomadic experience for travelers, where they can sleep in the traditional felt tents of the region.

What to Buy in Merv: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Turkmen Carpets – Colorful, intricately woven designs that have cultural significance in Turkmenistan.

  • Handwoven Fabrics – Visit the local bazaars, where one can find delicate fabrics made by local artisans.

  • Jewelry – Traditional, handmade Turkmen jewelry is a popular souvenir to bring home, featuring intricate patterns and bright colors.

Local Culture and Customs of Merv

Turkmenistan is a predominantly Muslim country, and visitors should be respectful of Muslim culture and customs when visiting Merv. Locals are generally friendly to visitors, but it is essential to dress modestly and ask for permission before taking photos of people or religious buildings.

Best Time to Visit Merv

The best time to visit Merv is from September to November or March to May when the weather is mild, and the crowds are smaller. Temperatures can reach up to 40 degrees Celsius in summer, making it challenging to explore the ancient ruins and natural sites.

Do’s and Don’ts of Merv

Do:

  • Respect local customs and dress modestly when visiting religious sites.

  • Try out the local food and drinks and immerse yourself in Turkmen culture.

  • Learn some basic Russian or Turkmen phrases to communicate with locals better.

Don’t:

  • Take photos or videos of people without their permission.

  • Bring or use drugs, as Turkmenistan has strict drug laws.

Safety and Health

Safety in Merv

Merv is a safe destination, with very little crime occurring. However, visitors should always keep an eye on their belongings, especially in crowded tourist areas.

Health in Merv

The tap water in Turkmenistan is not safe to drink, so visitors should always drink bottled water. Make sure to bring mosquito repellent for outdoor activities as there can be a lot of mosquitos during the summer months.

Getting to Merv: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Merv

The nearest airport to Merv is the Mary International Airport, located approximately 30 kilometers from the city center.

Bus Stations Near Merv

Merv bus station is located in the center of the city, connecting Merv with other Turkmen cities.

Other Options

Visitors can also take the train to Mary from Ashgabat, Turkmenistan’s capital, and then take a taxi or bus to Merv.

How to Get Around in Merv

Getting around Merv can be done on foot or by taxi. Taxis can be found near hotels and tourist sites, and negotiating the price before getting in is recommended.

Where to Go Next from Merv: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

There are several nearby destinations worth visiting after exploring Merv.

  • Ashgabat – Turkmenistan’s capital and one of the few cities in the world built entirely of white marble.

  • Darvaza Gas Crater – Known as the “Door to Hell,” this fiery gas crater in the Karakum Desert is a unique tourist attraction.

  • Konye-Urgench – A former Islamic capital of Khorezmian empire, and another UNESCO-listed ancient city worth visiting.

Conclusion

Merv is an amazing destination that offers a unique blend of history, culture, and natural beauty. Whether you’re interested in ancient ruins, outdoor adventures, unusual experiences, or just want to immerse yourself in Turkmen culture, Merv provides a wealth of possibilities. Plan your trip with this comprehensive guide and get ready to experience the wonders of Merv.
