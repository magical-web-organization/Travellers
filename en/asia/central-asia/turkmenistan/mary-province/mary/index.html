Introduction to Mary

Mary is a city located in Mary Province, in Turkmenistan. This Central Asian city is known for its rich culture, ancient history, and natural beauty. It is a must-see destination for anyone who wants to experience the heart of Turkmenistan.

Food and Drink Scene of Mary

Must-Try Dishes

  • Plov (rice with meat, carrots, and onions)
  • Shashlik (grilled meat)
  • Lagman (noodle soup with vegetables and meat)
  • Somsa (meat or vegetable-filled pastry)

Local Beverages

  • Chal (fermented camel milk)
  • Shirin (sweet drink made from grapes)
  • Tea

Local Culture and Customs of Mary

The people of Mary are known for their hospitality and friendliness. Visitors are expected to show respect for local customs and traditions, such as taking off their shoes before entering a mosque or wearing appropriate clothing when visiting religious sites.

Best Time to Visit Mary

The best time to visit Mary is during the spring from March to May or during the fall from September to November. During these months, temperatures are mild and ideal for outdoor activities.

Do’s and Don’ts of Mary

Do:

  • Explore local markets and bazaars.
  • Learn about traditional arts and crafts.
  • Try local cuisine and beverages.
  • Take photos of the beautiful landscapes and historical sites.

Don’t:

  • Disrespect local customs and beliefs.
  • Photograph military or government buildings.
  • Wear revealing clothing in conservative areas.
  • Leave trash or litter in public areas.

Safety and Health

Safety in Mary

Mary is generally safe for visitors. However, it is important to take precautions such as avoiding public demonstrations or protests and taking care of personal belongings.

Health in Mary

Travelers should be up to date on routine vaccinations before visiting Mary. It is recommended to drink bottled water and avoid consuming street food to prevent food-borne illnesses.

The best way to get around Mary is by taxi or private car. Buses are available, but they can be crowded and uncomfortable.
