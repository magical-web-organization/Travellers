Introduction to Kandahar

Kandahar is a historic city in South Afghanistan, situated in the province of the same name. It is known for its rich cultural heritage, historical landmarks, scenic mountains, and authentic local cuisine. Despite its turbulent past, Kandahar has emerged as a popular tourist destination for its unique blend of traditional and modern elements.

This travel guide will take you through the best things to do and see in Kandahar, along with tips on local culture, customs, safety, and transportation. So, pack your bags and get ready for an exciting adventure!

List of Places to Visit in Kandahar

Kandahar has a lot to offer for tourists, whether you are interested in history, outdoor adventures, or cultural experiences. Let’s take a look at some of the top places to visit in Kandahar.

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Citadel of Alexander the Great: Built in the 4th century, this ancient fortress is located in the heart of the city and is one of the most significant historical landmarks in Kandahar.
  • Kandahar Museum: Featuring a vast collection of artifacts from the region’s rich history, this museum is a must-visit for anyone interested in learning about Kandahar’s cultural heritage.
  • Shrine of Baba Wali: This Sufi shrine is considered one of the holiest places in Afghanistan and is a popular destination for pilgrims and visitors.
  • Arghandab National Park: This stunning national park features a vast array of flora and fauna, including wild olive trees and mountain goats.

Natural Attractions of Kandahar: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Spin Ghar Mountains: These majestic mountains offer breathtaking vistas of Kandahar and are perfect for hiking and trekking.
  • Band-e-Amir National Park: Home to stunning blue lakes, lush valleys, and towering cliffs, Band-e-Amir is a must-visit destination for nature lovers.
  • Tarnak River: This beautiful river flows through Kandahar and is an excellent spot for a picnic or a leisurely stroll.
  • Shah Suri Park: Spanning over 1,000 acres, this park is perfect for nature walks or a relaxing picnic with family and friends.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Kandahar

Kandahar is a haven for outdoor enthusiasts, and there are several hiking and biking trails that visitors can explore.

Some of the most popular hiking trails include the Spin Ghar Mountains, Arghandab National Park, and Tarnak River. For those interested in biking, the Spin Ghar Mountains, Arghandab National Park, and Shah Suri Park offer excellent opportunities for cycling.

Best Things to Do in Kandahar: Activities and Tours

Besides hiking and biking, Kandahar offers plenty of other activities and tours that tourists can enjoy. Some of the top attractions include:

  • Shopping at the Kandahar Bazaar: This marketplace is a bustling center for buying authentic Afghan goods, including carpets, crafts, and more.
  • Kandahar International Airport: Take a tour of the airport, which has become a key transportation hub for Western forces in Afghanistan.
  • Kandahar Airfield: Visit the airfield and see the static displays of different aircraft that are used in Afghanistan.
  • Photography Tours: Discover hidden gems and capture stunning landscapes of Kandahar through photo tours.

Week-Long Itinerary for Kandahar

If you’re planning a week-long trip to Kandahar, here’s a suggested itinerary:

  • Day 1: Citadel of Alexander the Great and Kandahar Museum
  • Day 2: Shrine of Baba Wali and Band-e-Amir National Park
  • Day 3: Spin Ghar Mountains and Arghandab National Park
  • Day 4: Tarnak River and Shah Suri Park
  • Day 5: Shopping at the Kandahar Bazaar and photography tour
  • Day 6: Kandahar International Airport and Kandahar Airfield
  • Day 7: Relax and enjoy local cuisine

Food and Drink Scene of Kandahar

Food is an essential part of Kandahar’s culture, and the city is renowned for its delicious cuisine. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Kabuli Pulao: This traditional dish consists of rice cooked with carrots, raisins, and lamb or chicken.
  • Mantu: These steamed dumplings are stuffed with minced beef or lamb and served with a tomato and yogurt sauce.
  • Kofta Challow: Meatballs made from ground beef or lamb, served with tomato sauce and rice.
  • Narenj Palaw: This dish consists of cooked chicken served with orange peel and saffron.

Local Beverages

  • Green Tea: Green tea is a ubiquitous beverage in Kandahar and is usually served with sugar and nuts.
  • Mango Juice: During the summer season, fresh mango juice is a popular and refreshing drink.

Where to Stay in Kandahar: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Kandahar offers a variety of accommodation options for tourists. Here are some of the top choices:

  • Serena Hotel: A luxurious five-star hotel located in the heart of Kandahar city.
  • Continental Guest House: A budget-friendly guest house located near the Kandahar Bazaar.
  • Kandahar Lodge: A mid-range hotel located near the Kandahar International Airport.

What to Buy in Kandahar: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

If you’re looking for unique souvenirs and local products to bring home, Kandahar has plenty to offer. Here are some top choices:

  • Carpets: Afghan carpets are famous worldwide for their exquisite designs and quality.
  • Kandahar Knife: These traditional Afghan knives are handmade and are a popular souvenir among tourists.
  • Pashmina Shawls: These soft and warm shawls are made from the wool of the Kashmiri goat and are perfect for colder weather.
  • Afghan Jewelry: Afghan jewelry is known for its intricate designs and craftsmanship.

Local Culture and Customs of Kandahar

Kandahar is a conservative city, and visitors are expected to respect local customs and culture. Here are some essential tips:

  • Dress Conservatively: Dress modestly and cover your head while visiting mosques and shrines.
  • Remove Shoes: It’s customary to remove shoes before entering someone’s home or a mosque.
  • Respect Religious Beliefs: Do not criticize Islamic customs or beliefs.
  • Learn Some Phrases: Try to learn some basic Pashto or Dari phrases to communicate with locals.

Best Time to Visit Kandahar

The best time to visit Kandahar is during the spring and autumn season, from March to May and September to November. The weather during these months is pleasant, and the mountains are at their most scenic.

Do’s and Don’ts of Kandahar

Do:

  • Dress modestly and respect religious beliefs.
  • Remove shoes before entering places of worship or homes.
  • Learn some basic Pashto or Dari phrases to communicate with locals.
  • Try the local cuisine and explore the unique culture of Kandahar.

Don’t:

  • Criticize Islamic customs or beliefs.
  • Take photographs of people without permission.
  • Drink alcohol in public places.
  • Be disrespectful to locals or their customs.

Safety and Health

Safety in Kandahar

Kandahar has had a turbulent history, and it’s essential to take precautions while traveling in the city. Here are some safety tips:

  • Avoid traveling alone at night.
  • Stay away from areas with a high crime rate or military activity.
  • Be cautious while traveling by public transport or taxis.

Health in Kandahar

It’s essential to take the necessary precautions to stay healthy while traveling to Kandahar. Here are some tips:

  • Drink bottled water and avoid tap water.
  • Be cautious while eating street food.
  • Carry mosquito repellent and other necessary medications.

Getting to Kandahar: Transportation Options and Logistics

Kandahar can be reached by both air and road. Here are some transportation options:

Airports Near Kandahar

  • Kandahar International Airport: The main international airport in Kandahar, offering regular flights to regional and international destinations.

Bus Stations Near Kandahar

  • Kandahar Bus Terminal: A central bus station in Kandahar, offering bus services to various parts of the country.

Other Options

  • Private Car: Private cars can be hired from major cities in Afghanistan to reach Kandahar.

How to Get Around in Kandahar

Local transport options in Kandahar include taxis, buses, and private cars. Taxis are the most common mode of transport for tourists and can be found easily on the streets of Kandahar.

Where to Go Next from Kandahar: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Kandahar is located in a strategic location, and there are several nearby destinations that tourists can explore. Here are some top choices:

  • Herat: Located in western Afghanistan, Herat is known for its rich cultural heritage and stunning architecture.
  • Kabul: The capital city of Afghanistan is a journey back in time with its historic landmarks, museums, and vibrant culture.
  • Mazar-e-Sharif: Known as the “blue city,” Mazar-e-Sharif is famous for its beautiful architecture and important religious shrines.

Conclusion

Kandahar is a beautiful and historic city in Afghanistan, offering tourists a unique blend of traditional and modern elements. From exploring historical landmarks to hiking scenic mountains, Kandahar has something to offer everyone. With this comprehensive travel guide, you’re ready to start planning your next adventure to Kandahar, Afghanistan.
