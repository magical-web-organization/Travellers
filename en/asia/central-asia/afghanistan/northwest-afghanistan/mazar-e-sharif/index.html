Introduction to Mazar-e Sharif

Mazar-e Sharif is a historically rich city situated in Northern Afghanistan, and is famous for its ancient shrines, mosques, museums, and beautiful natural surroundings. Named after the tomb of Ali the Great, a Muslim caliph, it is the fourth-largest city in Afghanistan with a population of around 400,000 people. Mazar-e Sharif is considered one of the most culturally and ethnically diverse provinces of Afghanistan, with Pashtuns, Tajiks, Uzbeks, and Hazaras coexisting peacefully. The city offers unique insights into the multi-ethnic and multi-religious culture of Afghanistan.

List of Places to Visit in Mazar-e Sharif

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Mazar-e Sharif is home to many culturally significant landmarks. Visitors can explore the history of the city at the National Museum of Afghanistan, which has over 1000 artifacts on display, including rare Greco-Bactrian and Islamic coins, ceramics, stone sculptures, and bronze items. Another attraction is the Blue Mosque. Built in the 15th century, the shrine houses the tomb of Imam Ali, the cousin of Prophet Muhammad. The mosque is adorned with intricate blue tiles, and the dome sits on carved marble columns. The Shrine of Hazrat Ali is close to the Blue Mosque, which is a significant site for Shiite Muslims. The large mosque complex includes tombs of notable religious figures such as the martyrs of Mazari Sharif.

Natural Attractions of Mazar-e Sharif: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Apart from the culturally rich attractions, Mazar-e Sharif boasts several natural landscapes to explore. The Band-e Amir National Park is located 125 km southeast of Mazar-e Sharif and is famous for its turquoise blue lakes and waterfalls. The area is great for hiking, swimming, boating, and picnicking. Another attraction is the three sacred lakes in Takht-e Rustam, which is believed to have therapeutic properties. In addition, the city has many parks, including the famous Bibi Mahro Park, which is an ideal place to relax and enjoy the beauty of nature.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Mazar-e Sharif

Mazar-e Sharif offers great hiking and biking routes. A popular hiking trail is the Sher-i-Nasrya Valley, located in Balkh province, which offers breathtaking views of the Hindu Kush Mountains. Another option is the Sultan Omid Khan Mountain, which is a three-day trek located on the outskirts of Mazar-e Sharif. Adventurous travelers can book a hiking or biking tour with a local guide or explore the routes on their own.

Best Things to Do in Mazar-e Sharif: Activities and Tours

Apart from the above-mentioned activities, Mazar-e Sharif has several other activities to offer. Visitors can take a City Tour to explore the historical sites, visit the city’s bustling bazaars, taste local cuisine, and interact with the vibrant locals. For a unique experience, travelers can attend a traditional Afghan wedding or visit a local family’s home for dinner. Moreover, tourists can enjoy off-road adventures, such as sandboarding in the nearby deserts or horseback riding through the scenic countryside.

Week-Long Itinerary for Mazar-e Sharif

  • Day 1: Visit the Blue Mosque and the Shrine of Hazrat Ali. Enjoy lunch at a local restaurant.
  • Day 2: Tour the National Museum of Afghanistan and visit the Citadel of Alexander the Great. Take a walk in the Bibi Mahro Park.
  • Day 3: Explore the nearby town of Balkh, visit the historical sites such as the Shrine of Khwaja Abu Nasr Parsa, Takht-i-Rustam, and the Ancient City of Balkh.
  • Day 4: Head to Band-e Amir National Park for activities such as swimming, hiking, and picnicking.
  • Day 5: Go on an off-road adventure or a horseback riding tour.
  • Day 6: Visit the local bazaars and shop for souvenirs and local products.
  • Day 7: Attend a traditional Afghan wedding or visit a local family’s home for dinner.

Food and Drink Scene of Mazar-e Sharif

Must-Try Dishes

Mazar-e Sharif offers delicious, traditional Afghan cuisine. Some must-try dishes include:

  • Qabili Palao: A staple dish made with Basmati rice, lamb, raisins, carrots, and spices.
  • Mantu: Similar to dumplings, filled with spiced meat and vegetables, and topped with tomato sauce and yogurt.
  • Ashak: Dumpling-like pasta filled with diced leek and served with yogurt, meat sauce, and dried mint.
  • Bolani: Flatbread stuffed with potatoes or various vegetables, and fried or baked.

Local Beverages

Traditional Afghan tea, known as Chai, is a popular beverage served throughout the day. It is typically sweetened with sugar and served with cookies or sweets. Locally sourced juices made from fresh fruits are popular. For a more adventurous drink, try the famous Doogh, a fermented yogurt drink flavored with mint and served cold.

Where to Stay in Mazar-e Sharif: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Mazar-e Sharif offers several accommodation options for visitors. Hotels such as the Balkh Heritage Hotel, the Residency Hotel, and the Serena Hotel offer luxury services, including swimming pools, spas, and fine dining restaurants. Budget options such as hostels and guesthouses, including the Central Backpacker’s Hostel and Moonlight Guesthouse, provide affordable and comfortable accommodation with friendly staff and facilities such as free Wi-Fi and laundry.

What to Buy in Mazar-e Sharif: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Mazar-e Sharif is famous for its rich cultural heritage, and visitors can take home souvenirs such as traditional Afghan clothing, rugs, and silk products, including scarves and shawls. Handmade pottery, silver jewelry, and woodwork are also great options.

Local Culture and Customs of Mazar-e Sharif

Mazar-e Sharif is a conservative city with a strong Islamic culture. Visitors should dress modestly, with women covering their heads and wearing non-revealing clothing. It is essential to respect the local customs and religious practices, especially in the religious landmarks. It is customary to take off shoes when entering mosques and shrines.

Best Time to Visit Mazar-e Sharif

Mazar-e Sharif experiences a semi-arid climate, with summers being hot and dry and winters being cool and wet. The best time to visit the city is during the spring and autumn months (March to May and September to November), when the weather is mild, and rainfall is minimal.

Do’s and Don’ts of Mazar-e Sharif

Do:

  • Dress modestly and respect local customs.
  • Take off shoes when entering mosques and shrines.
  • Try the local cuisine and interact with the friendly locals.
  • Hire a local guide for better exploration.

Don’t:

  • Take photographs of women or children without permission.
  • Drink alcohol in public places.
  • Smoke in public places.

Safety and Health

Safety in Mazar-e Sharif

Mazar-e Sharif is considered safe for tourists. Visitors should avoid traveling alone at night and should always keep their belongings close while walking in public places.

Health in Mazar-e Sharif

Visitors to Mazar-e Sharif are advised to take necessary precautions while consuming food and water. It’s recommended to drink only bottled water, and food should be well-cooked and served hot. It’s also essential to carry a first-aid kit and any necessary medications.

Getting to Mazar-e Sharif: Transportation Options and Logistics

Mazar-e Sharif is easily accessible by air and road. The Mawlana Jalaluddin Balkhi International Airport is the main airport in the city and is served by several airlines, including Ariana Afghan Airlines, Flydubai, and Turkish Airlines. There are also bus services that connect the city with other major cities in the region, including Kabul, Herat, and Kandahar.

Airports Near Mazar-e Sharif

  • Mawlana Jalaluddin Balkhi International Airport (MZR)

Bus Stations Near Mazar-e Sharif

  • Balkh Bus Station
  • Ahmad Shah Baba Bus Station
  • Aliabad Bus Station

Other Options

Private taxis are readily available but can be expensive. It’s recommended to hire a driver or a guide for a better and more comfortable experience.

How to Get Around in Mazar-e Sharif

Mazar-e Sharif is a relatively small city, and visitors can walk or hire a taxi to go around. Local buses and mini-buses are also available, but route information can be difficult to find.

Conclusion

Mazar-e Sharif offers a unique experience, combining a rich cultural heritage, beautiful natural surroundings, delicious cuisine, and welcoming locals. With proper planning and precautions, travelers can explore the beautiful gems the city has to offer.
