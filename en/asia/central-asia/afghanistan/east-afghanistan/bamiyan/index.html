Introduction to Bamiyan

Bamiyan is a hidden gem located in East Afghanistan. It is known for its rich cultural heritage and stunning natural beauty, characterized by soaring cliffs, tranquil valleys, and historic landmarks. The city is a must-visit for those who seek a unique travel experience. The city is also famous for its ancient Buddha statues, which were destroyed by the Taliban in 2001. But, despite the tragedy, Bamiyan is still a breathtaking destination that offers a glimpse into the past and present of Afghanistan.

List of Places to Visit in Bamiyan

Here are some of the best places to visit in Bamiyan.

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • The Bamiyan Museum: This museum houses a grand collection of artifacts and relics from the ancient Gandhara civilization.
  • Shahr-e Gholghola: This historic site was the former capital of the ancient kingdom of Bamiyan.
  • The Cultural Center of Bamiyan: You can experience the vibrant culture and traditions of the Hazarah people of the region.

Natural Attractions of Bamiyan: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • The Band-e Amir National Park: This park is a stunning collection of six deep blue lakes that offer a picturesque location for picnics and swimming.
  • The Buddha niches and caves: You can still admire the stunning niches where the ancient Buddha statues used to be and explore the natural caves in the cliff.
  • The Lalander Buddhist Monastery: This monastery is known for its stunning location, nestled in the mountains above the valley.

Hiking/Biking Routes in Bamiyan

Hiking and biking enthusiasts will find numerous trails and paths to explore, with stunning scenic views of the mountains and valleys.

Here are a few popular routes:

  • Band-e-Amir Cliffs Trail: This trail takes you across the Band-e Amir National Park, offering stunning views of the six blue lakes and surrounding cliffs.
  • Shahr-e Gholghola Trail: This trail is perfect for history buffs, taking you through the ancient ruins of the former capital of the Bamiyan Kingdom.
  • The Valley Trail: This trail is a leisurely hike that takes you through the tranquil valleys surrounded by towering cliffs offering breathtaking views of the region.

Best Things to Do in Bamiyan: Activities and Tours

From exploring ancient ruins to discovering natural wonders, there are many things to do in Bamiyan. Here are some activities that you shouldn’t miss:

  • Take a guided tour of the ancient Buddha niches and caves to learn about the culture and history of Bamiyan.
  • Attend a traditional dance and music performance at the Cultural Center.
  • Explore the beautiful national parks and valleys by hiking, biking, or even horseback riding!

Week-Long Itinerary for Bamiyan

Day 1: Arrival in Bamiyan, check-in at your hotel, and explore the town center.

Day 2: Take a guided tour of the Buddha niches and caves and visit the Bamiyan Museum. In the afternoon, explore the Lalander Buddhist Monastery.

Day 3: Take a hike up to the Band-e Amir National Park, and discover the beautiful blue lakes and the surrounding cliffs.

Day 4: Visit the Shahr-e-Gholghola, the former capital of the Bamiyan Kingdom, and learn about its history and significance.

Day 5: Enjoy a day of leisure, explore the local markets, and find some souvenirs to take home.

Day 6: Take a horseback riding tour of the valleys surrounding Bamiyan.

Day 7: Departure from Bamiyan.

Food and Drink Scene of Bamiyan

Bamiyan is known for its traditional local cuisine that reflects the region’s distinct cultural heritage.

Must-Try Dishes

  • Mantu: This is a traditional Afghan dumpling filled with minced meat and onions, served with yogurt sauce.
  • Kabuli Palaw: This is a national dish of Afghanistan made with rice, lentils, and lamb or chicken, flavored with aromatic spices.
  • Ashak: This is another type of dumpling filled with leeks and topped with a tomato-based sauce.

Local Beverages

  • Chai: This is a strong and flavorful tea that is typically served in small glasses with sugar and milk.
  • Doodh Soda: This is a unique drink that is made by mixing carbonated water with milk, sugar, and rosewater.

Where to Stay in Bamiyan: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Bamiyan offers a variety of accommodation options for travelers on any budget, from luxury hotels to budget-friendly hostels.

Here are some popular options:

  • The Safi Landmark Hotel & Suites: This hotel is centrally located in Bamiyan, offering luxury rooms with stunning views of the valley.
  • The Silk Road guesthouse: This guesthouse is situated in the Old City of Bamiyan, offering comfortable rooms and a laid-back atmosphere.
  • The Pamir Hotel: This family-run hotel offers clean and comfortable rooms at an affordable price.

What to Buy in Bamiyan: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Bamiyan offers a range of unique souvenirs and local products that you can take home with you.

Here are some popular items:

  • Handcrafted rugs and carpets: Bamiyan is known for its traditional weaving techniques, and you can find a wide range of handcrafted rugs and carpets in the local markets.
  • Local jewelry: You can find unique pieces of jewelry in Bamiyan that are made from silver, turquoise, and other locally sourced materials.
  • Pottery: Bamiyan is known for its unique pottery, and you can find a variety of handcrafted ceramics that make for beautiful souvenirs.

Local Culture and Customs of Bamiyan

Bamiyan is home to the Hazara people, who are known for their distinctive culture and traditions. The people of Bamiyan are warm and welcoming, and you can experience this hospitality while exploring the local markets and sampling the traditional cuisine.

Best Time to Visit Bamiyan

The best time to visit Bamiyan is between May and September when the weather is mild and pleasant. These months offer the perfect opportunity for outdoor activities and exploration. During winter, the region experiences heavy snowfall, which can make it inaccessible.

Do’s and Don’ts of Bamiyan

Here are some do’s and don’ts to keep in mind when visiting Bamiyan:

Do:

  • Respect local customs and dress modestly when visiting religious sites.
  • Try the local cuisine and interact with the locals to get a better understanding of their culture and traditions.
  • Carry appropriate clothing and gear for outdoor activities such as hiking and biking.

Don’t:

  • Photograph locals or religious sites without asking for permission first.
  • Drink or eat in public during the holy month of Ramadan.
  • Wear revealing clothing or overly tight clothing in public.

Safety and Health

Safety in Bamiyan

Bamiyan is generally considered a safe destination for travelers, but it is still advisable to take precautions such as avoiding traveling alone at night and avoiding areas that are known to be dangerous.

Health in Bamiyan

It is advisable to get vaccinated before visiting Bamiyan and carry a sufficient supply of any necessary medications. It is also recommended to drink only bottled water and avoid street food.

Getting to Bamiyan: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Bamiyan

The closest airport to Bamiyan is the Bamyan Airport, which is served by domestic flights from Kabul.

Bus Stations Near Bamiyan

There are no bus stations in Bamiyan, but you can reach the city by bus from Kabul or other nearby cities.

Other Options

You can also reach Bamiyan by taking a shared taxi or hiring a private car from Kabul or other nearby cities.

How to Get Around in Bamiyan

You can explore Bamiyan on foot or by hiring a taxi or renting a bike or motorcycle to get around.

Where to Go Next from Bamiyan: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Here are some nearby destinations that you can consider visiting after your trip to Bamiyan:

  • Kabul: This capital of Afghanistan is known for its vibrant culture, stunning architecture, and bustling markets.
  • Herat: This ancient city is located in western Afghanistan, known for its rich history and stunning Islamic architecture.
  • Mazar-e-Sharif: This city is home to the stunning Blue Mosque and is known for its rich cultural heritage.

Conclusion

Bamiyan offers a unique and unforgettable experience for travelers looking for something off the beaten path. From ancient ruins to natural beauty, there is something for everyone in this stunning region of Afghanistan. With proper planning and precautions, Bamiyan is a safe and rewarding destination to explore.
