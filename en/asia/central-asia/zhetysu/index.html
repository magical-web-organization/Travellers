Introduction to Zhetysu

Zhetysu is a region located in the southeastern part of Kazakhstan. The region’s name, which means “Seven Rivers,” is derived from the seven main rivers which pass through the region - the Ili, Karatal, Karabulak, Lepsy, Sharyn, Ayaguz, and Sogety. Zhetysu is a culturally and scenically rich region, with a mix of natural attractions, outdoor adventures, historical landmarks, and museums. With its mild climate, friendly locals, and tranquil charm, Zhetysu is an ideal destination for travelers seeking an off-the-beaten-path experience.

List of Places to Visit in Zhetysu

Here are some of the top places to visit in Zhetysu:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Aksu-Zhabagly Nature Reserve

Located in the western part of the Tien Shan Mountains, the Aksu-Zhabagly Nature Reserve is one of the most scenic and biodiverse regions in Kazakhstan. The reserve is home to rare plant and animal species, including the snow leopard, ibex, and lynx. Visitors can explore the reserve’s hiking trails and take in the stunning views of the mountains and valleys.

Mausoleum of Khoja Ahmed Yasawi

This 14th-century mausoleum is a UNESCO World Heritage Site and is considered one of the most important Islamic architectural monuments in Central Asia. The mausoleum was built to honor the famous Sufi poet and philosopher Khoja Ahmed Yasawi.

Turkestan

Turkestan, located in southern Kazakhstan, is one of the oldest cities in Central Asia. The city’s main attraction is the 14th-century mausoleum of the great Sufi philosopher Khoja Ahmed Yasawi, which is also a World Heritage Site.

Natural Attractions of Zhetysu: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Charyn Canyon

Charyn Canyon is a natural wonder located in the eastern part of Zhetysu. The canyon is known for its colorful rock formations and unique geological formations. Visitors can hike along the canyon trails, go rock climbing, or simply take in the stunning views of the surrounding landscape.

Kolsai Lakes

The Kolsai Lakes are a series of three alpine lakes located in the northern part of Zhetysu. These crystal clear lakes are surrounded by pine forests and snow-capped mountains, making them a popular destination for hiking, picnicking, and camping.

Altyn-Emel National Park

Altyn-Emel National Park is another must-visit destination in Zhetysu. The park is home to a wide variety of flora and fauna, including the rare argali sheep and the goitered gazelle. Visitors can explore the park’s hiking trails, take a jeep tour to the Singing Dunes or visit the ancient rock drawings at Tamgaly-Tas.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Zhetysu

Zhetysu is a paradise for hikers and bikers, thanks to its vast network of trails and stunning natural landscapes. Here are some of the best routes in the region:

  • Charyn Canyon hiking trail
  • Kolsai Lakes hiking trail
  • Turgen Gorge hiking and biking trail
  • Altyn-Emel National Park jeep tour

Best Things to Do in Zhetysu: Activities and Tours

Here are some of the best things to do in Zhetysu:

  • Go hiking or biking in Charyn Canyon or Kolsai Lakes
  • Explore the ancient rock art at Tamgaly-Tas in Altyn-Emel National Park
  • Take a jeep tour to see the Singing Dunes in Altyn-Emel National Park
  • Visit the Mausoleum of Khoja Ahmed Yasawi in Turkestan
  • Visit the Aksu-Zhabagly Nature Reserve
  • Explore the local bazaars and shops to buy souvenirs and local products
  • Try the local cuisine, including beshbarmak, a traditional dish made with boiled meat and noodles, and shubat, a fermented camel milk drink.

Food and Drink Scene of Zhetysu

Zhetysu’s cuisine is a mix of traditional Kazakh and Central Asian dishes, with influences from China and Russia. Here are some must-try dishes and beverages in the region:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Beshbarmak: boiled meat and noodles, served with onion and broth
  • Lagman: a noodle dish with meat and vegetables
  • Manti: steamed dumplings with meat and onions

Local Beverages

  • Shubat: fermented camel milk drink that is often served with meals
  • Kumys: fermented mare’s milk, which is a popular beverage in Kazakhstan and other Central Asian countries.

What to Buy in Zhetysu: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Zhetysu is known for its beautiful handicrafts, including rugs, tapestries, and embroidery. Visitors can also find unique souvenirs and gifts made from camel hair, such as scarves, socks, and hats. Other popular souvenirs include traditional Kazakh instruments, such as the dombra (a stringed instrument) and the kobyz (a bowed instrument).

Local Culture and Customs of Zhetysu

Kazakh culture is deeply rooted in hospitality, family values, and respect for elders. Visitors to Zhetysu can expect to be welcomed with open arms and treated with kindness and generosity. It’s important to dress modestly when visiting religious sites, and to remove your shoes before entering homes or mosques. Visitors should also refrain from drinking alcohol in public places.

Best Time to Visit Zhetysu

The best time to visit Zhetysu is between May and September, when the weather is warm and dry, and the hiking trails and natural attractions are open.

Do’s and Don’ts of Zhetysu

Do:

  • Dress modestly when visiting religious sites.
  • Remove your shoes before entering homes or mosques.
  • Respect local customs and traditions.
  • Try the local cuisine.

Don’t:

  • Drink alcohol in public places.
  • Hike or explore natural attractions alone.
  • Take pictures of military installations or personnel.

Safety and Health

Safety in Zhetysu

Zhetysu is a generally safe region with low crime rates. However, visitors should be aware of the risk of petty theft in crowded tourist areas. It’s also important to take precautions when hiking or exploring remote areas, as the terrain can be rugged and unforgiving.

Health in Zhetysu

Visitors should be up-to-date on routine vaccinations, including measles-mumps-rubella (MMR), diphtheria-tetanus-pertussis, varicella (chickenpox), polio, and the flu. Visitors should also take precautions to avoid tick bites, as ticks in this region can carry diseases such as Lyme disease and tick-borne encephalitis.

Getting to Zhetysu: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Zhetysu

The nearest international airports to Zhetysu are Almaty International Airport and Nursultan Nazarbayev International Airport in Nur-Sultan, the capital city of Kazakhstan. From there, visitors can rent a car or take a taxi to Zhetysu.

Bus Stations Near Zhetysu

The nearest major bus station to Zhetysu is the Almaty Bus Station, which offers bus services to various cities and towns in Kazakhstan.

Other Options

Visitors can also travel to Zhetysu by train, as the region is served by several train stations, including the Almaty-1 and Almaty-2 stations in Almaty.

How to Get Around in Zhetysu

The best way to get around Zhetysu is by car, as public transportation is limited and unreliable. Visitors can rent a car from the airport or from various rental agencies in Almaty. Alternatively, visitors can hire a driver or join a guided tour.

Where to Go Next from Zhetysu: Nearby Destinations and Excursions
