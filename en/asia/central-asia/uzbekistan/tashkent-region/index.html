Introduction to Tashkent Region

Uzbekistan’s Tashkent Region is a hidden gem located in Central Asia. Offering a perfect blend of cultural heritage, natural beauty, and adventure, it remains largely undiscovered by tourists despite being the most populous region of Uzbekistan. The region’s capital, Tashkent, is the country’s primary entry point and transportation hub.

If you’re planning on visiting Tashkent Region, our comprehensive travel guide will help you create an unforgettable experience.

Best Things to Do in Tashkent Region: Activities and Tours

  • Explore Tashkent’s Old City and bazaars.
  • Visit the architectural marvels and historical sites of Samarkand and Bukhara.
  • Take a hot air balloon ride over the Chimgan Mountains.
  • Go skiing or snowboarding in Chimgan ski resorts.
  • Go rafting or kayaking in the Charvak Reservoir.
  • Try your hand at horseback riding in the Tashkent outskirts.
  • Learn Uzbek cooking with a local chef.

Local Culture and Customs of Tashkent Region

Uzbekistan is a conservative Muslim country, and it’s important to respect local customs and traditions, especially when visiting mosques or other places of worship. Dress modestly, remove your shoes before entering homes, and avoid public displays of affection.

Best Time to Visit Tashkent Region

The best time to visit Tashkent Region is from March to May or from September to November when the weather is mild and comfortable. Summers can be very hot, while winters can be bitterly cold.

Do’s and Don’ts of Tashkent Region

Do:

  • Dress appropriately and conservatively when visiting places of worship.
  • Bargain at the bazaars.
  • Respect local customs and traditions.
  • Try the local cuisine and beverages.

Don’t:

  • Drink tap water.
  • Take photographs of military or police installations.
  • Offend locals by criticizing their religion or culture.

Safety and Health

Safety in Tashkent Region

Tashkent Region, like the rest of Uzbekistan, is a safe destination for tourists. Petty theft can happen, so it’s recommended to keep your valuables safe and secure.

Health in Tashkent Region

It’s important to stay hydrated and use sunscreen to avoid heatstroke and sunburn during the hot summer months. Also, Hepatitis A and typhoid vaccines are recommended before visiting Uzbekistan.
