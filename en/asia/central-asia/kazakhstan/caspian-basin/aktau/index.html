Introduction to Aktau

Aktau is a beautiful city located in the Caspian Basin, in the western part of Kazakhstan. With a population of approximately 200,000 people, it is a hub for tourists wanting to explore the surrounding area’s beautiful natural scenery, cultural landmarks, and historical sites. The city is renowned for its beautiful beaches, turquoise waters, and breathtaking sunsets that will leave you awe-inspired.

List of Places to Visit in Aktau

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Aq Kuyruk Cave: This cave is believed to be over 40,000 years old and offers an insight into the region’s history and geology.
  • The Museum of History and Local Lore: Learn about the history, traditions, and culture of the Mangystau region in this beautiful museum.
  • The Aktau City Mosque: One of the most beautiful and large mosques in the country, featuring an impressive capacity of 1,500 people.

Natural Attractions of Aktau: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Mangystau National Park: A stunning national park that is home to unique flora and fauna, including camels, gazelles, and rare bird species. It is an ideal location for wildlife lovers and hikers.
  • The Caspian Sea: With clear turquoise water and sandy beaches, the Caspian Sea is an ideal location for swimming, fishing, and other water-based activities.
  • Shalkar Lake: Located in the Mangystau region, this freshwater lake offers beautiful scenery and is a perfect spot for fishing and picnicking.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Aktau

  • Shetpe-Karakol: A beautiful and challenging trek that takes approximately two days to complete. It offers stunning views of the surrounding desert, mountains, and lake.
  • Mangystau Desert Trek: A challenging trek that takes you through the desert region of Mangystau. It is best to do this trek with a local guide to ensure safety and to learn about the flora and fauna of the region.

Best Things to Do in Aktau: Activities and Tours

  • Nomad Tour: A cultural tour that takes you through the life of nomads and their traditions in the Mangystau region.
  • Jeep Tour: A fun-packed jeep tour that takes you through the rugged terrain of the Kazakh desert region, offering stunning views of the surrounding area.
  • Caspian Sea Boat Tour: Enjoy a boat tour of the Caspian Sea, featuring fishing, swimming, and beautiful views of the city and its surroundings.

Food and Drink Scene of Aktau

Must-Try Dishes

  • Beshbarmak: A dish of boiled meat and noodles that is a staple food in Kazakhstan.
  • Kazy: A traditional sausage made from horsemeat.
  • Baursaki: A Kazakh version of doughnuts that are usually served with jam or honey.

Local Beverages

  • Kumys: A fermented drink made from mare’s milk that is believed to have medicinal qualities.
  • Shubat: A sour milk drink that is made from camel’s milk. It is believed to be helpful in treating digestive problems.

Where to Stay in Aktau: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Renaissance Aktau Hotel: A luxury hotel that offers stunning sea views and is located in the heart of the city.
  • Golden Palace Hotel: An affordable hotel that is located near the Aktau City Mosque and Mangystau National Park.
  • Marin Inn: A guesthouse that offers comfortable rooms and excellent service, located near the Aktau beach.

What to Buy in Aktau: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Rugs and Carpets: Local rugs and carpets that feature traditional Kazakh designs.
  • Traditional Clothing: Traditional Kazakh clothing, including shapan, chapan, and kalpak.
  • Jewelry: Local jewelry made from gold, silver, and gemstones.

Local Culture and Customs of Aktau

Kazakh culture is rich and diverse, with a mix of Asian and European influences. The hospitality of the Kazakh people is world-renowned, and the locals will welcome you with open arms. The traditional Kazakh dress is still worn by many locals, and you will see this at weddings and cultural events.

Best Time to Visit Aktau

The best time to visit Aktau is from May to September when the weather is warm and ideal for outdoor activities. The average temperature during these months ranges from 20°C to 35°C. Winter months can be cold, with temperatures dropping below zero.

Do’s and Don’ts of Aktau

Do:

  • Respect the local customs and traditions.
  • Try the local food and drinks.
  • Wear modest clothing when visiting mosques and other religious places.

Don’t:

  • Take pictures of locals without their permission.
  • Show public display of affection.
  • Wear shoes inside the mosque.

Safety and Health

Safety in Aktau

Aktau is generally a safe city, with low levels of crime. However, it is important to take precautions when traveling alone, especially at night. It is also essential to be aware of the weather conditions, as the area can experience severe storms and strong winds.

Health in Aktau

It is recommended that you receive all necessary vaccinations before traveling to Aktau. It is also important to take precautions against mosquito bites, as malaria and other diseases are prevalent in the region.

Getting to Aktau: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Aktau

  • Aktau International Airport: The main airport in the region, with international and domestic flights available.

Bus Stations Near Aktau

  • Aktau Bus Station: The central bus station in the city, with buses available to nearby cities and villages.

Other Options

  • Taxi: Taxis are readily available in the city and are relatively inexpensive.
  • Car Rental: Several car rental companies operate in the city, offering a wide range of vehicles at competitive prices.

How to Get Around in Aktau

The best way to get around Aktau is by taxi, as they are readily available and relatively inexpensive. However, it is important to negotiate the fare before getting into the car. You can also rent a car, but it is recommended that you have a local driver due to the challenging driving conditions in the region.

Where to Go Next from Aktau: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Zhemchuzhina Hotel: Located on the Caspian Sea, featuring outdoor activities and breathtaking scenery.
  • Kenderli Beach: A beautiful beach located in the Mangystau region, featuring blue waters and fine white sand.
  • Beyneu: A small town located on the border between Kazakhstan and Uzbekistan, featuring stunning natural scenery and traditional Kazakh culture.

Conclusion

Aktau is a beautiful city that offers visitors a unique and unforgettable experience. With its stunning scenery, rich culture, and welcoming locals, there is no shortage of things to do and see. Whether you are interested in outdoor activities, historical landmarks, or cultural experiences, Aktau is the perfect destination for you.
