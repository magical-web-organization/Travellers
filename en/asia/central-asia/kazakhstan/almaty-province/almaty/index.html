Introduction to Almaty

Almaty is the largest city in Kazakhstan and is situated in the southeastern part of the country. It was the former capital of Kazakhstan and remains the cultural and commercial center of the country. The city is surrounded by mountains and is known for its stunning natural beauty, making it a popular destination for outdoor enthusiasts. With its rich history, delicious food, and friendly people, Almaty is a must-visit destination in Central Asia.

List of Places to Visit in Almaty

Here are some of the top places to visit in Almaty:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Kazakhstan Museum of Arts: This museum houses a large collection of Kazakhstani art, including paintings, sculptures, and contemporary art.

  • Central State Museum: This museum showcases the culture and history of Kazakhstan, including exhibits on archaeology and ethnography.

  • Zelyony (Green) Bazaar: This bustling market offers a glimpse into the daily life of locals and is a great place to sample traditional Kazakhstani dishes and buy souvenirs.

  • Ascension Cathedral: This beautiful Russian Orthodox church is one of the oldest buildings in the city and offers stunning views of Almaty.

  • Kok-Tobe: This mountain offers breathtaking views of Almaty and has a variety of attractions, including a souvenir market, amusement park, and a cable car ride.

Natural Attractions of Almaty: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Big Almaty Lake: This scenic lake is located high in the mountains and offers stunning views of the Almaty skyline.

  • Medeu: This outdoor ice skating rink is one of the highest in the world and offers beautiful views of the surrounding mountains.

  • Charyn Canyon: This natural wonder is located a few hours outside of Almaty and features unique rock formations and scenic views.

  • Shymbulak Ski Resort: This ski resort is located in the mountains above Almaty and offers world-class skiing and snowboarding conditions during the winter months.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Almaty

Almaty is a hiker and biker’s paradise, with numerous trails offering stunning views of the surrounding mountains and nature. Some popular hiking and biking trails include:

  • Kok-Zhailau: This trail is located in the mountains and passes through beautiful meadows and forests.

  • First President’s Park: This park is located in the city and features a variety of walking and biking paths.

  • Chimbulak to Talgar Pass: This challenging hiking trail offers breathtaking views of the surrounding mountains and valleys.

Best Things to Do in Almaty: Activities and Tours

  • Almaty City Tour: This tour offers a glimpse into the history and culture of the city and includes stops at the Central State Museum, Ascension Cathedral, and Zelyony Bazaar.

  • Almaty Food Tour: This tour allows visitors to sample traditional Kazakhstani dishes and learn about the country’s cuisine and culture.

  • Charyn Canyon Tour: This tour takes visitors to one of the most stunning natural wonders in Kazakhstan and includes a guided hike through the canyon.

  • Shymbulak Ski Tour: This tour takes visitors to one of the best ski resorts in Kazakhstan and includes ski rentals and lift tickets.

Week-Long Itinerary for Almaty

Day 1: Visit the Central State Museum and the Ascension Cathedral.

Day 2: Take a food tour and visit the Zelyony Bazaar.

Day 3: Hike to Big Almaty Lake and enjoy the scenic views.

Day 4: Visit the Kazakhstan Museum of Arts and take a walk in First President’s Park.

Day 5: Take a day trip to Charyn Canyon.

Day 6: Ski and snowboard at Shymbulak Ski Resort.

Day 7: Relax and enjoy the beauty of Almaty.

Food and Drink Scene of Almaty

Must-Try Dishes

  • Beshbarmak: A traditional dish made with boiled meat and noodles.

  • Plov: A rice dish with meat and vegetables.

  • Laghman: A noodle dish with vegetables and meat.

  • Samsa: A baked pastry filled with meat and vegetables.

Local Beverages

  • Kumys: A fermented horse milk beverage.

  • Shubat: A fermented camel milk beverage.

  • Chalap: A fermented cow milk beverage.

  • Kefir: A thin yogurt-like drink.

Where to Stay in Almaty: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Almaty offers a variety of accommodations, from luxury hotels to budget-friendly hostels. Some popular options include:

  • Rixos Almaty Hotel: A luxury hotel located in the heart of the city.

  • Renion Residence Hotel: A mid-range hotel located near the Central State Museum and other attractions.

  • Almaty Backpackers Hostel: A budget-friendly hostel located in the city center.

What to Buy in Almaty: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Shapan (Traditional Robe): A beautifully embroidered robe worn by Kazakhstani women.

  • Shashka (Traditional Sword): A curved sword traditionally used by Kazakhstani warriors.

  • Dombra (Traditional Musical Instrument): A two-stringed instrument used in traditional Kazakhstani music.

  • Kazakhstani Rug: A handcrafted rug featuring traditional Kazakhstani designs and colors.

Local Culture and Customs of Almaty

Kazakhstani culture is a blend of various ethnic groups, including Kazakhs, Russians, Uzbeks, and Uighurs. It is customary to remove your shoes before entering someone’s home and to avoid wearing revealing clothing. Visitors should also be respectful of local customs and traditions, including the importance of hospitality and respect for elders.

Best Time to Visit Almaty

The best time to visit Almaty is from May to September when the weather is mild and perfect for exploring the city and the surrounding mountains. The winter months can be bitterly cold, but offer the opportunity for skiing and snowboarding.

Do’s and Don’ts of Almaty

Do:

  • Be respectful of local customs and traditions.

  • Try the delicious Kazakhstani cuisine.

  • Explore the stunning natural beauty of the surrounding mountains.

Don’t:

  • Wear revealing clothing in public.

  • Smoke in public places.

  • Be loud and disruptive in public areas.

Safety and Health

Safety in Almaty

Almaty is generally a safe city, but visitors should be cautious and aware of their surroundings. Petty theft can occur in crowded areas, so it is important to keep an eye on your belongings.

Health in Almaty

Visitors should be up to date on routine vaccinations and may need additional vaccines depending on the length and purpose of their trip. The tap water in Almaty is not suitable for drinking, so visitors should stick to bottled water.

Getting to Almaty: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Almaty

  • Almaty International Airport: The main airport serving Almaty and the surrounding area.

Bus Stations Near Almaty

  • Sayran Bus Station: The main intercity bus station in Almaty.

  • Almaty-1 Bus Station: Another major intercity bus station in Almaty.

Other Options

  • Train: The Almaty-1 Train Station provides rail service to destinations throughout Kazakhstan and Russia.

  • Car rental: Several car rental companies operate in Almaty, making it easy to explore the surrounding area at your own pace.

How to Get Around in Almaty

The best way to get around Almaty is by taxi or public transportation. Taxis are plentiful and relatively inexpensive, and there are several bus and metro routes that connect the city’s main attractions.

Where to Go Next from Almaty: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Astana: The capital city of Kazakhstan is located to the north of Almaty and features a unique blend of modern architecture and traditional Kazakhstani culture.

  • Lake Issyk-Kul: This stunning lake is located in neighboring Kyrgyzstan and is a popular destination for swimming and boating.

  • Turkestan: This historic city is located to the south of Almaty and features a famous mausoleum and other important religious sites.

Conclusion

Almaty is a beautiful and fascinating city that offers something for every traveler. From stunning natural beauty and outdoor adventures to rich history and culture, Almaty is a must-visit destination in Central Asia.
