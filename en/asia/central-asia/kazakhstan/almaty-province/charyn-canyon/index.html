Introduction to Charyn Canyon

Charyn Canyon is one of the most spectacular natural wonders of Kazakhstan. Located in Almaty Province, in Central Asia, this natural landmark is famous for its colorful rock formations, deep gorges, and unique geological features. The canyon stretches for around 80 kilometers and is part of the Charyn National Park, which was established in 2004 to protect the unique flora and fauna of the region. Whether you are an adventure seeker, a nature lover, or a culture enthusiast, Charyn Canyon has something to offer for everyone.

List of Places to Visit in Charyn Canyon

If you are planning a trip to Charyn Canyon, here’s a list of must-visit places that you shouldn’t miss:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Charyn State Historical and Cultural Museum-Reserve: This museum is located in the town of Kegen, near the entrance to the canyon, and showcases the history and culture of the local Kazakh people. The exhibits include traditional costumes, jewelry, crafts, and artifacts related to the nomadic lifestyle of the region.
  • Tamgaly Tas: A UNESCO World Heritage Site located about 120 km from Charyn Canyon, Tamgaly Tas is a collection of petroglyphs dating back to the Bronze Age. The carvings depict hunting scenes, shamanistic rituals, and other aspects of ancient Kazakh culture.

Natural Attractions of Charyn Canyon: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Valley of Castles: This is one of the most popular viewpoints in Charyn Canyon, known for its unique rock formations that resemble medieval castles. The valley is best visited during sunset or sunrise to witness the play of light and shadow on the rocks.
  • Moon Valley: This is another stunning viewpoint that offers panoramic views of the canyon. The name comes from the lunar landscape of the area, with eroded cliffs and gorges that resemble craters on the moon.
  • Canyon Rafting: For adventure seekers, Charyn Canyon offers the opportunity of white-water rafting through the fast-flowing Charyn River. The rafting trip takes around 3 hours and the rapids range from Class I to Class IV, making it suitable for both beginners and experienced rafters.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Charyn Canyon

Charyn Canyon offers several hiking and biking routes that allow visitors to explore the natural and cultural attractions of the region. Some of the popular trails are:

  • Devil’s Gorge Hike: This is a challenging 5-km hike that takes you through the heart of Charyn Canyon, passing through narrow gorges, towering cliffs, and lush forests. The highlight of the hike is the Devil’s Gorge, a narrow ravine with towering walls that resemble the mouth of a demon.
  • Canyon Rim Hike: A moderate 7-km hike that takes you along the edge of the canyon, offering stunning views of the valley below. The trail passes through several scenic viewpoints, including the Valley of Castles and the Moon Valley.
  • Mountain Biking: For cycling enthusiasts, Charyn Canyon offers several off-road tracks and trails that take you through the scenic countryside and the rugged terrain of the canyon. One of the popular routes is the 18-km track from Kegen to the Canyon entrance, which offers a mix of gravel roads and dirt paths.

Best Things to Do in Charyn Canyon: Activities and Tours

Besides hiking, biking, and rafting, Charyn Canyon offers several other activities and tours that cater to different interests and preferences. Some of the best things to do in Charyn Canyon are:

  • Horseback Riding: Horseback riding is a popular activity in Charyn Canyon, allowing visitors to explore the scenic countryside and the rocky terrain of the canyon. The tours range from short rides to multi-day camping trips, depending on your level of expertise.
  • Photo Safari: If you’re a photography buff, Charyn Canyon offers several photo safari tours that take you to the best viewpoints and vantage points of the canyon, where you can capture the stunning landscape and the local culture.
  • Camping and Stargazing: Charyn Canyon offers some of the most unique and secluded camping spots in Kazakhstan. The campsites are located in scenic locations, with stunning views of the canyon and the night sky.

Food and Drink Scene of Charyn Canyon

Kazakh cuisine has a rich culinary heritage, with a mix of meat dishes, dairy products, and bread that reflect the nomadic lifestyle of the region. In Charyn Canyon, you can try some of the traditional dishes and local beverages, including:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Beshbarmak: A popular Kazakh dish made with boiled meat (usually lamb), served on a bed of flat noodles and topped with onions and sour cream.
  • Kuyrdak: A hearty dish made with diced meat (usually beef), potatoes, onions, and spices.
  • Baursak: A deep-fried bread that is a staple food in Kazakh households, served with butter or honey.

Local Beverages

  • Kumys: A fermented drink made from mare’s milk, with a slightly sour taste and a low alcohol content.
  • Shubat: Another fermented milk drink, made from camel’s milk, which is popular among the nomads of the region.

Where to Stay in Charyn Canyon: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Charyn Canyon offers several accommodation options for visitors, ranging from budget hostels to luxury lodges. Some of the popular places to stay in Charyn Canyon are:

  • Charyn Canyon Eco-Camp: This is a budget-friendly campsite located near the entrance of the canyon, offering tented accommodation and basic amenities like toilets and showers.
  • Kainar Hotel: A mid-range hotel located in Kegen town, offering comfortable rooms and a restaurant serving Kazakh and international cuisine.
  • Rakhman Guesthouse: A family-run guesthouse located in Saty village, offering cozy rooms, free Wi-Fi, and a garden.

What to Buy in Charyn Canyon: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Charyn Canyon offers several local products and souvenirs that you can buy as a memento of your trip, including:

  • Handmade Crafts: The local Kazakh artisans make a variety of handmade crafts, including embroidered textiles, felt hats and slippers, and decorative items made from animal horns and bones.
  • Horsehair Products: The Kazakh people have a long tradition of making various products from horsehair, including ropes, bracelets, and belts. These items make for unique and authentic souvenirs that reflect the local culture.
  • Traditional Musical Instruments: The Kazakh music is an essential part of the local culture, and you can buy traditional musical instruments like the Dombra (a two-stringed lute) or the Kobyz (a horsehair instrument).

Local Culture and Customs of Charyn Canyon

The Kazakh people have a rich cultural heritage that reflects their nomadic lifestyle and their Islamic faith. Here are some tips to keep in mind when visiting Charyn Canyon:

  • Dress modestly and respect local customs, especially when visiting mosques or other religious sites.
  • Always ask for permission before taking someone’s photograph, especially when it comes to the local people or their homes.
  • Don’t litter or damage the natural or cultural heritage of the region.

Best Time to Visit Charyn Canyon

The best time to visit Charyn Canyon is during the spring and autumn seasons, when the weather is mild and pleasant, and the landscape is lush and green. During the summer months, the temperatures can get hot and dry, reaching up to 40°C, while the winter months can be cold and snowy, making outdoor activities challenging.

Do’s and Don’ts of Charyn Canyon

Here are some do’s and don’ts to keep in mind when exploring Charyn Canyon:

Do:

  • Plan your trip in advance, and book your accommodation and activities in advance.
  • Follow the safety guidelines for hiking, biking, and rafting, and hire a guide if necessary.
  • Respect the local customs and traditions, and try to learn some basic Kazakh phrases.

Don’t:

  • Litter or damage the natural or cultural heritage of the region, and avoid disturbing the local wildlife.
  • Wander off the designated trails or venture into restricted areas to avoid accidents or injuries.
  • Buy or drink unfiltered water, which can cause digestive issues.

Safety and Health

Safety in Charyn Canyon

Charyn Canyon is a relatively safe destination in terms of crime, but visitors should be aware of the potential hazards of natural and outdoor activities. Hiking, biking, and rafting can involve risks like falling, dehydration, or exposure to extreme temperatures, so it’s crucial to follow the safety guidelines and hire a guide if necessary.

Health in Charyn Canyon

The health risks in Charyn Canyon are similar to those in other remote and rural areas, including altitude sickness, sunburn, and gastrointestinal illnesses. Visitors should take necessary precautions like wearing sunscreen, drinking plenty of water, and avoiding raw or uncooked foods. It’s also a good idea to bring a first-aid kit and any necessary medication, as medical facilities in the region can be limited.

Getting to Charyn Canyon: Transportation Options and Logistics

Charyn Canyon is located about 200 km east of Almaty, the largest city in Kazakhstan, and can be accessed by road, air, or rail. Here are the main transportation options available:

Airports Near Charyn Canyon

The closest airport to Charyn Canyon is the Almaty International Airport, which is served by several domestic and international airlines. From Almaty, you can hire a taxi or rent a car to drive to Charyn Canyon.

Bus Stations Near Charyn Canyon

Several bus companies operate regular services from Almaty to the nearby towns of Kegen and Shonzhy, which are located at the entrance of Charyn Canyon. From there, you can hire a taxi or join a local tour to explore the canyon.

Other Options

You can also join a guided tour from Almaty or Saty village, which offers transportation, accommodation, and activities in Charyn Canyon as part of the package. Alternatively, you can rent a car and drive yourself to the canyon, although the roads can be challenging and rugged in some parts.

How to Get Around in Charyn Canyon

Charyn Canyon is a relatively small and accessible region, and the best way to explore it is on foot or by bike. You can also hire a local guide or join a tour to visit the main attractions and viewpoints of the canyon.

Where to Go Next from Charyn Canyon: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

If you have more time to explore the region, Charyn Canyon offers several nearby destinations and excursions that are worth a visit, including:

  • Kolsai Lakes National Park: Located about 60 km from Saty village, Kolsai Lakes is a scenic natural reserve that offers hiking, horseback riding, and camping opportunities in the mountains.
  • Altyn Emel National Park: Located about 200 km from Charyn Canyon, Altyn Emel is a vast wilderness area that is home to diverse wildlife, ancient burial sites, and unique natural landmarks like the Singing Dunes.
  • Almaty City: Known as the “cultural capital” of Kazakhstan, Almaty offers a mix of modern and traditional attractions, including museums, art galleries, and parks.

Conclusion

Charyn Canyon is a stunning natural wonder that offers a unique mix of natural and cultural attractions for visitors. Whether you’re seeking adventure, relaxation, or an immersion in local culture, Charyn Canyon has something to offer for everyone. With our comprehensive travel guide, you can plan your trip to Charyn Canyon with ease and confidence, and experience one of the most spectacular landscapes in Kazakhstan.
