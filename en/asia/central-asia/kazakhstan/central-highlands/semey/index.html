Introduction to Semey

Semey, located in the Central Highlands of Kazakhstan, is a city rich in history and culture. Formerly known as Semipalatinsk, this city was once an important center for the Soviet Union’s nuclear program, but has since become a peaceful community with plenty of attractions for tourists.

With its vibrant food scene, stunning natural scenery, and diverse cultures, there is something for everyone in this city. Whether you want to spend your time exploring historical sites, experiencing outdoor adventures or trying the local cuisine, Semey won’t disappoint you.

This comprehensive guide will cover all the must-visit places, what to do, where to stay, and how to get around Semey during your visit.

List of Places to Visit in Semey

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Abay Museum

The Abay Museum is dedicated to the remarkable life of Abay Qunanbayuli, who was a poet, thinker, and philosopher. The museum displays a collection of his books, manuscripts, and personal belongings.

Semipalatinsk Test Site Museum

Visit this museum to see the history of the Soviet Union’s nuclear tests conducted in Semey. It contains photographs, documents, as well as preserved nearby nuclear explosions’ tarnished landscapes.

Semey Regional Local Lore Museum

Discover Semey’s ancient and modern history with a visit to the regional local lore museum. This museum houses a collection of exhibits on local flora and fauna, the city’s history, and cultural heritage.

Natural Attractions of Semey: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Bektau-Ata

Bektau-Ata is a stunning group of granite mountains located 70 km from Semey. The place is popular for its surreal rock formations, stunning valleys, and unique landscapes.

Semey Regional Landscape Park

This park offers some of the best views of Semey’s natural beauty. Hike through the park’s trails, see the amazing flora and fauna of the area– perfect for a picnic, too!

Shulbinsk Reservoir

The Shulbinsk Reservoir, also known as the Irtysh Reservoir, is a famous tourist spot in Semey. The area offers outdoor activities such as boating, fishing, and camping in its scenic shores.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Semey

Zhuravlevka Reserve

Zhuravlevka is a nature reserve located about an hour’s drive from Semey. Trek through the nature reserve’s trails surrounded by untouched wildlife and natural beauty.

Bektau-Ata

Aside from its surreal rock formations, Bektau-Ata is also a popular spot for hiking and mountain climbing. Its natural panorama provides an extraordinary view of the surrounding mountain range.

Karakaraly National Park

Karakaraly National Park, situated at the western end of the Rudnyi Altay, is ideal for hiking, bird watching and wildlife sightseeing.

Best Things to Do in Semey: Activities and Tours

Visit Lake Markakol

Lake Markakol is one of the most unique and striking lakes in Kazakhstan. The lake is known for its turquoise color and is also a habitat for local fish species, including unique wild land-locked salmon.

Explore Semey’s Nightlife

Experience Semey’s nightlife with an array of bars, restaurants, and cafes to choose from. Known for their vibrant atmosphere, they serve unique and delicious local dishes.

Participate in Semey’s Festivals and Events

Semey hosts several festivals and events throughout the year. The most noteworthy among them are the Independence Day celebrations on December 16, National Unity Day on May 1, and Nauryz (New Year’s Day) festival in March.

Food and Drink Scene of Semey

Must-Try Dishes

Beshbarmak

Beshbarmak is a traditional dish consisting of boiled meat (lamb, beef, or horse), served over boiled pasta-like dough, and onions.

Kuyrdak

Kuyrdak is a slow-cooked meat and vegetable stew— it’s a common meal in Kazakhstan. This dish is made with lamb or beef, the star ingredient complemented with other vegetables and spices.

Kylmai

Kylmai, made of raw mincemeat, traditional spices, and pasta-like dumplings, is a unique dish that is usually eaten as an appetizer or a snack in Semey.

Local Beverages

Kymyz

Kymyzy is a traditional Kazakh drink made from fermented mare’s milk. One can usually find it in the nomadic communities of Kazakhstan.

Shubat

Shubat, made from camel milk, is another traditional Kazakh drink. It is sour in taste but is known for its medicinal properties.

Where to Stay in Semey: Hotels, Hostels, and Guest Houses

Semey has a rich selection of hotels, hostels, and guest houses one can choose from, providing the perfect choices for every budget.

Hotel Ambassador

This hotel is an excellent option for mid-range travelers. The rooms are comfy and well-furnished, while the staff are polite and offer excellent service.

Guest House Bukhara

For budget travelers, Guest House Bukhara offers affordable, clean, and comfortable rooms.

Hotel Irtysh

The luxury Hotel Irtysh provides elegant rooms equipped with excellent amenities (like sauna bath, swimming pool, and gym facility.)

What to Buy in Semey: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Handicrafts

Semey is known for its unique handmade items including carpets, embroidery, and fur products. Bargain at the Semey flea market for unique finds.

Local Food Products

Bring home some traditional Semey food products, including honey, jam, and dried fruits, typically made by locals from neighboring areas.

Local Culture and Customs of Semey

Kazakhstan is a multi-cultural country filled with different customs and traditions. The Kazakh culture is known to have a vibrant display of dance, music, fashion, and food.

Best Time to Visit Semey

The best time to visit Semey is during the summer months (June to August), where the weather is warm, and the natural scenery is at its prime.

Do’s and Don’ts of Semey

Do:

  • Try traditional Kazakh cuisine like beshbarmak and kuyrdak.
  • Respect the local customs and traditions.
  • Take off your shoes when entering mosques and other religious places.

Don’t:

  • Drink alcohol in public (especially on religious festivals).
  • Dress scantily on sacred sites.
  • Take photographs of people without their permission.

Safety and Health

Safety in Semey

In general, Semey is a safe city for tourists. However, vigilance is necessary when venturing in the city. Always be cautious of your belongings and avoid dark and isolated areas of Semey.

Health in Semey

Proof of vaccination may be required when entering Semey. Travelers should take necessary health precautions like checking their vaccinations before traveling to Kazakhstan.

Getting to Semey: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Semey

Semey International Airport, also known as Semipalatinsk Airport, is the nearest airport with direct international and local flights.

Bus Stations Near Semey

Semey Bus Station is the major bus station and is well-connected to neighboring locations.

Other Options

Semey is several miles from the Trans-Siberian Railway, which can provide an extraordinary scenic route to the town.

How to Get Around in Semey

Taxis and buses are the most affordable modes of transportation to navigate through Semey. Taxis are preferable, but can be very expensive for the inexperienced traveler. Boarding a bus is an excellent option for those who want to save money.

Where to Go Next from Semey: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Ust-Kamenogorsk

Ust-Kamenogorsk is a city located North-West of Semey and is known for its natural beauty, picturesque views, and traditional Kazakhstan art.

Karaganda

Located in the Central part of Kazakhstan, Karaganda is famous for having the best preserved architecture of the former Soviet Union.

Conclusion

Semey, Kazakhstan is a must-see destination for adventure and history lovers. This travel guide can help you experience the best of what Semey has to offer, from stunning natural views to delicious traditional delicacies combined with unique local culture, and so much more.
