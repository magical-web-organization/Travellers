Introduction to Central Highlands

Central Highlands is a region located in the central part of Kazakhstan, in Central Asia. It is known for its natural attractions, such as lakes, mountains, and valleys, as well as its rich history and culture. Central Highlands is a must-visit destination for travelers who want to explore the diverse landscapes and immerse themselves in the local heritage of Kazakhstan.

Local Culture and Customs of Central Highlands

  • Kazakh people are known for their hospitality and warmth towards visitors.
  • It is customary to remove shoes before entering someone’s home.
  • It is polite to sample some of the offered food and drinks during a meal or celebration.
  • Kazakh culture values respect for elders and guests.

Best Time to Visit Central Highlands

The best time to visit Central Highlands is from May to September when the weather is mild and pleasant, and the natural attractions are at their best.

Do’s and Don’ts of Central Highlands

Do:

  • Respect local customs and traditions.
  • Try the local food and drinks.
  • Wear conservative clothing when visiting religious sites.
  • Learn a few phrases in Kazakh language.

Don’t:

  • Smoke in public places.
  • Take photographs of military buildings or personnel.
  • Touch or remove anything from historical sites or museums.
  • Be disrespectful to elders or locals.

Safety and Health

Safety in Central Highlands

Central Highlands is generally a safe place to visit; however, visitors should take common sense precautions to avoid theft or robbery. It is also advisable to stick to marked trails when hiking or camping in the natural areas.

Health in Central Highlands

Visitors should be up-to-date on their routine vaccinations before traveling to Central Highlands. It is also recommended to get vaccinated for hepatitis A and B, typhoid, and rabies. The region is also prone to tick-borne diseases, so visitors should take measures to prevent tick bites.
