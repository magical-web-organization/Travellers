Introduction to Kazakhstani North

Kazakhstani North is a region in the north of Kazakhstan, located in Central Asia. The region is filled with natural beauty, history, and culture. From the rugged Altai Mountains to the stunning Korgalzhyn Nature Reserve, there is plenty to explore in this fascinating part of the world.

List of Places to Visit in Kazakhstani North

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  1. National Museum of Kazakhstan: This museum houses a vast collection of artifacts and exhibits that showcase the country’s history and culture.
  2. Akmolinskiy Regional Museum: Located in the city of Kokshetau, this museum features displays on regional history and traditions.
  3. Bayanaul State National Park: Home to the stunning Bayanaul Mountains, this park is a perfect place to explore Kazakhstani’s natural and cultural beauty.
  4. Palace of Peace and Reconciliation: This striking pyramid-shaped building in the city of Astana is a modern symbol of peace and harmony.

Natural Attractions of Kazakhstani North: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  1. Altai Mountains: These rugged mountains cover an impressive area of the region and offer some of the most beautiful scenery in Kazakhstan.
  2. Korgalzhyn Nature Reserve: Located near the city of Astana, this nature reserve is home to an incredible variety of bird species, including pink flamingos.
  3. Lake Balkhash: This enormous lake is located in southeastern Kazakhstan and is one of the largest lakes in Asia.
  4. Charyn Canyon: Often called the Grand Canyon of Central Asia, this stunning natural wonder is a must-visit for nature lovers.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Kazakhstani North

  1. Altai Trek: This 14-day trek takes hikers through the stunning Altai Mountains, passing through remote villages and offering breathtaking views of the region’s natural beauty.
  2. Bayanaul National Park Trek: A popular trek in the Bayanaul State National Park, this trail takes hikers through pine forests, rocky landscapes and past waterfalls and mountain lakes.
  3. East Kazakhstan Bike Tour: This 13-day biking tour takes cyclists through the extraordinary landscape of East Kazakhstan, offering a unique opportunity to explore the region on two wheels.

Best Things to Do in Kazakhstani North: Activities and Tours

  1. Horseback Riding Tours: Explore the rugged mountains and vast steppes of Kazakhstan in traditional Kazakh style – on horseback.
  2. Balloon Tours: Get a bird’s eye view of the magnificent Kazakhstani landscape with a hot air balloon ride over the Altai Mountains, Korgalzhyn Nature Reserve, or Lake Balkhash.
  3. Skiing and Snowboarding: The Khan Tengri and Chimbulak ski resorts offer some of the best skiing and snowboarding in Central Asia.
  4. City Tours: Take a tour of Kazakhstan’s modern capital city Astana, and get a glimpse of the country’s unique blend of traditional and modern culture.

Food and Drink Scene of Kazakhstani North

Must-Try Dishes

  1. Beshbarmak: A traditional Kazakh dish made from boiled or steamed meat, usually lamb or beef, served with boiled dough called “kazy” and onions.
  2. Lagman: A hearty soup made from noodles, meat, and vegetables, typically seasoned with spices.
  3. Shubat: A refreshing fermented milk drink made from camel milk.

Local Beverages

  1. Kymyz: A traditional Kazakh drink made from fermented mare’s milk.
  2. Shubat: A refreshing fermented milk drink made from camel milk.

Where to Stay in Kazakhstani North: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  1. Hilton Astana: Located in the heart of Astana, this luxury hotel offers stunning views of the city skyline.
  2. Kokshetau Hotel: A comfortable and affordable hotel located in the central city of Kokshetau.
  3. Altyn Emel Guesthouse: A charming guesthouse located near the Altyn Emel National Park, offering easy access to the park’s scenic beauty.

What to Buy in Kazakhstani North: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  1. Kazakh Hats: Traditional felt hats are a staple of Kazakh culture and make for a unique souvenir.
  2. Camel Wool Carpets: These handmade carpets are often sold at local markets and are a testament to the region’s nomadic heritage.
  3. Traditional Instruments: Beautiful stringed instruments such as the dombra and kobyz are an essential part of Kazakh music and make for a unique and authentic souvenir.

Local Culture and Customs of Kazakhstani North

Kazakhs are known for their hospitality, and visitors should expect to be welcomed with open arms into homes and bazaars. The traditional yurt dwelling is still in use in some rural areas. Guests are typically served tea and snacks upon arrival, and it is considered rude to decline their offer. Kazakh traditional music and dances are an essential part of the country’s culture and are often performed at festivals and weddings. Kazakh society is patriarchal, and women are often expected to take on traditional roles in the family and community.

Best Time to Visit Kazakhstani North

The best time to visit Kazakhstani North is between April and October when temperatures are mild, and outdoor activities are accessible. Winters can be bitterly cold and are best avoided if possible.

Do’s and Don’ts of Kazakhstani North

Do:

  • Respect local customs and culture.
  • Dress appropriately, especially when visiting religious sites.
  • Learn a few basic phrases of the Kazakh language.

Don’t:

  • Discuss politics or religion unless invited to do so.
  • Get involved in drugs or other illegal activities.
  • Take photographs of people without permission.

Safety and Health

Safety in Kazakhstani North

Kazakhstan is a relatively safe country for travelers, although there have been some cases of petty theft and pickpocketing in crowded areas. Visitors should exercise caution when traveling independently, especially in isolated areas. It is also advisable to be aware of your surroundings when using public transportation, especially at night.

Health in Kazakhstani North

There is a low risk of infectious disease in Kazakhstan, and no vaccines are required to enter the country. It is important to ensure that you have the right travel insurance that covers medical treatment, including emergency evacuation if necessary.

Where to Go Next from Kazakhstani North: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Lake Issyk-Kul: Located just across the border in Kyrgyzstan, this stunning lake is one of the largest alpine lakes in the world.
