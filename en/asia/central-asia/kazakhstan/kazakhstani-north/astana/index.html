Introduction to Astana

Astana, the capital city of Kazakhstan, is a modern urban center with futuristic skyscrapers, contemporary landmarks, and world-class attractions. The city boasts a rich history, diverse culture, and unique blend of Eastern and Western influences. The best time to visit Astana is during the summer months, from June to September, when the temperature is warm and sunny.

List of Places to Visit in Astana

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Astana has a rich cultural heritage, and its museums and historical landmarks reflect this history. Some of the must-see attractions in this category include:

  • National Museum of Kazakhstan: showcasing the country’s history, culture, art, and natural resources.
  • Palace of Peace and Reconciliation: a unique pyramid-shaped building containing exhibition centers, galleries, and an opera hall.
  • Bayterek Tower: a famous symbol of Astana, with a panoramic observation deck offering views of the cityscape.
  • Hazret Sultan Mosque: a grand, blue-domed mosque with intricate interior design.

Natural Attractions of Astana: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

For nature enthusiasts and adventure seekers, Astana has plenty to offer. Some of the best natural attractions include:

  • Astana Water Park: a family-friendly water park with exciting water rides and swimming pools.
  • Ishim River Embankment: a scenic riverfront walkway with picturesque views and recreation facilities.
  • Khan Shatyr Entertainment Center: the world’s largest tented structure, with an indoor park, shopping complex, and restaurants.
  • Nur-Astana Mosque: a magnificent mosque with beautiful gardens and fountains.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Astana

Astana’s beautiful landscape is perfect for hiking and biking enthusiasts. Some of the best routes for outdoor adventures include:

  • Astana Park of Culture and Leisure: a green oasis with walking trails, playgrounds, and a lake for recreational activities.
  • Akorda Presidential Palace: an architectural gem surrounded by well-manicured gardens, ideal for a sunset stroll.
  • EXPO Boulevard: a scenic route connecting the city’s main attractions, with beautiful views of Nurzhol Boulevard and the Nazarbayev Center.

Best Things to Do in Astana: Activities and Tours

There are numerous activities and tours that cater to different interests and preferences. Some of the most popular activities include:

  • City Segway Tour: a guided tour of Astana’s modern architecture, historical landmarks, and cultural gems on a two-wheeled vehicle.
  • Evening Boat Ride on Ishim River: a relaxing boat tour along the river to admire the city’s skyline and waterfront attractions.
  • National Horse Games: a traditional Kazakh sport, held between July and September, featuring horse racing, wrestling, and archery.
  • Alzhir Gulag Museum: a moving tribute to the victims of Stalinist repression during the Soviet era.

Food and Drink Scene of Astana

Must-Try Dishes

Kazakh cuisine is a unique blend of Eastern and Western flavors, with a strong emphasis on meat dishes, dairy products, vegetables, and bread. Here are some of the must-try dishes in Astana:

  • Beshbarmak: boiled meat (usually lamb or beef) served with homemade noodles and onion sauce.
  • Kuyrdak: fried sheep’s liver, heart, and kidneys with onions and potatoes.
  • Plov: rice cooked with meat, vegetables, and spices.
  • Manty: steamed dumplings filled with minced meat and herbs.

Local Beverages

Kazakhstan is known for its tea culture, and tea-drinking is widely popular in Astana. Some of the local beverages include:

  • Shubat: a fermented camel milk drink, rich in probiotics and vitamins.
  • Baursak: fried dough balls, often served with tea or kumys.
  • Kymys: a traditional Kazakh drink made from fermented mare’s milk.

What to Buy in Astana: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Astana offers a range of souvenirs and local products that are perfect for bringing back home as a memento of your visit. Some of the top items to buy in Astana include:

  • Kazakh jewelry: intricate handcrafted silver necklaces, bracelets, and earrings.
  • Kazakh rugs: colorful and ornate handwoven carpets, mats, and wall hangings.
  • Kazakh hats: traditional fur hats adorned with silk embroidery.
  • Kazakh chocolate: locally produced artisan chocolates made from Kazakh cocoa beans.

Local Culture and Customs of Astana

Kazakhstan has a rich cultural heritage, and visitors are expected to respect the country’s customs and traditions. Some of the key cultural customs in Astana are:

  • Dress modestly: Kazakh culture values modesty and respectability, and visitors should avoid short skirts, shorts, or revealing clothing.
  • Remove shoes indoors: it is customary to remove shoes when entering homes, mosques, and other religious or cultural sites.
  • Offer tea to guests: tea-drinking is an important part of Kazakh hospitality, and guests are often offered tea or other drinks to show warm hospitality.

Best Time to Visit Astana

The best time to visit Astana is during the summer months, from June to September, when the weather is warm and sunny, and most outdoor attractions and activities are available. The winter months, from November to March, can be harsh and snowy, with temperatures dropping below freezing, making outdoor activities less enjoyable.

Do’s and Don’ts of Astana

Do:

  • Try local cuisine and beverages.
  • Dress appropriately, especially when visiting religious or cultural sites.
  • Respect local customs and traditions.

Don’t:

  • Drink tap water.
  • Take photographs of people without their permission.
  • Smoke in public places.

Safety and Health

Safety in Astana

Astana is generally a safe city, with low crime rates and strict law enforcement. However, visitors should take normal precautions, such as avoiding isolated areas at night, not leaving valuables in plain sight, and using licensed taxi services.

Health in Astana

Visitors to Astana should take care to drink bottled water and avoid eating uncooked or undercooked foods. It is also advisable to have up-to-date vaccinations for common diseases such as hepatitis A and B, typhoid, and measles, mumps, and rubella (MMR).

Getting to Astana: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Astana

Nursultan Nazarbayev International Airport is the main airport serving Astana, located about 10 miles southeast of the city center.

Bus Stations Near Astana

The Astana Bus Station is located on the outskirts of the city, with connections to neighboring cities and towns.

Other Options

There are also train connections to Astana from major cities in Kazakhstan, and long-distance buses from neighboring countries, including Russia, China, and Kyrgyzstan.

How to Get Around in Astana

In Astana, visitors can use public transportation, including buses, trams, and trolleybuses, as well as ride-hailing services, such as Uber and Yandex.Taxi. Bicycles and electric scooter rentals are also available for short trips, and walking is a great way to explore the city’s major attractions, as many are located close to each other.

Where to Go Next from Astana: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Astana serves as a base for exploring the many beautiful destinations in Kazakhstan, including:

  • Shymkent: a city renowned for its historic sites, beautiful parks, and diverse cuisine.
  • Almaty: the cultural heart of Kazakhstan, with a vibrant arts scene, beautiful mountains, and hiking trails.
  • Karaganda: a city famous for its coal mines, Soviet-era architecture, and museums.

Conclusion

Astana is a beautiful and modern city, with something to offer for every type of traveler. Visitors can indulge in the best local cuisine, shop for traditional souvenirs, explore the city’s cultural landmarks, and experience its natural wonders.
