Introduction to Naurzum State Nature Reserve

Nestled in the Kazakhstani North, Naurzum State Nature Reserve is a natural treasure that boasts a diverse range of wildlife, flora, and landscapes. Covering an area of over 200,000 hectares, the reserve is home to over 30 species of mammals, 200 species of birds, and more than 600 species of plants. With its scenic landscapes and abundant wildlife, Naurzum State Nature Reserve is a paradise for nature lovers, birdwatchers, and outdoor enthusiasts.

List of Places to Visit in Naurzum State Nature Reserve

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

If you’re interested in learning more about the history and culture of this region, there are several museums and historical sites to explore in and around the reserve.

  • Naurzum Museum of Local Lore: Located in the nearby town of Naurzum, this museum is dedicated to the history and culture of the region. Here, you’ll find exhibits on the traditional lifestyles of the Kazakh nomads, as well as displays of folklore and handicrafts.

  • Ibrahim Altynsarin Museum: This museum is located in the village of Altynsarin and is dedicated to the life and work of Ibrahim Altynsarin, a famous Kazakh writer, educator, and public figure.

  • Petroglyphs of Tamgaly: These ancient rock carvings are located about 120 km from the reserve and provide a fascinating glimpse into the history and culture of the region.

Natural Attractions of Naurzum State Nature Reserve: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

From hiking and birdwatching to horseback riding and camping, there are endless outdoor adventures to be had at Naurzum State Nature Reserve. Here are some of the top natural attractions to explore:

  • Lake Tengiz: This saline lake, located in the heart of the reserve, is a popular spot for birdwatching. Here, you can see a variety of waterbirds, including flamingos, pelicans, swans, and geese.

  • Birch Grove: This picturesque grove of birch trees is located next to Lake Tengiz and offers a peaceful spot for a picnic or a leisurely stroll.

  • Zhabaykalsk Pine Forests: These forests, located on the southern edge of the reserve, are home to old-growth pine trees and a variety of wildlife, including deer, moose, and lynx.

  • Kazakh Steppe: This vast grassland covers almost 80% of the reserve and is home to wild horses, saiga antelopes, and many other animals.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Naurzum State Nature Reserve

One of the best ways to explore the natural beauty of Naurzum State Nature Reserve is by hiking or biking through its scenic landscapes. Here are some of the top hiking and biking routes to check out:

  • Tengiz-Korgalzhyn Hiking Trail: This trail covers a distance of 42 km and takes you through some of the most scenic parts of the reserve, including Lake Tengiz and the Kazakh Steppe.

  • Bicycle Trail in Naurzum: This 25 km bike trail takes you through the reserve’s birch groves and pine forests, offering stunning views of the surrounding landscapes.

  • Izim-Hamir Hiking Trail: This 17 km trail takes you through the heart of the reserve and offers a chance to see a variety of wildlife, including saiga antelopes and wild horses.

Best Things to Do in Naurzum State Nature Reserve: Activities and Tours

Whether you’re looking to explore the natural beauty of the reserve or learn more about the local culture and customs, there are plenty of activities and tours to choose from. Here are some of the top things to do in Naurzum State Nature Reserve:

  • Birdwatching Tour: Take a guided tour of Lake Tengiz and the surrounding wetlands and see a variety of waterbirds, including flamingos, swans, and geese.

  • Horseback Riding Tour: Explore the Kazakh Steppe on horseback and see wild horses, saiga antelopes, and other animals up close.

  • Nomadic Culture Tour: Learn about the traditional Kazakh way of life and take part in traditional activities such as yurt-building, horse-riding, and traditional archery.

Food and Drink Scene of Naurzum State Nature Reserve

While the food scene in Naurzum State Nature Reserve may be simple, it’s rich in flavor and tradition. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Beshbarmak - a traditional dish of boiled meat and noodles, served with onions and potatoes.

  • Kazy - a type of sausage made from horse meat, usually served with bread and tea.

  • Baursak - a type of fried dough typically eaten as a snack or dessert.

Local Beverages

  • Kumys - a traditional Kazakh beverage made from fermented mare’s milk, often touted for its health benefits.

  • Shubat - a sour dairy drink made from fermented camel’s milk.

  • Tea - a staple beverage in the Kazakh culture, typically served with sugar and milk.
