Introduction to Taraz

Taraz, one of the oldest cities in Kazakhstan, is located in the southern part of the country. It is considered the cultural and historical hub of Kazakhstan and is known for its ancient landmarks, beautiful sceneries, and mouth-watering food. Whether you are a history buff, outdoor enthusiast, or just looking for an escape in a new, exciting location, Taraz is the perfect destination for you.

List of Places to Visit in Taraz

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Aisha Bibi Mausoleum

Aisha Bibi Mausoleum is an ancient mausoleum located 18 km outside of Taraz. It was built in the 11th century and is known for its unique architecture and stunning carvings. The mausoleum is dedicated to Aisha Bibi, the granddaughter of Prophet Muhammad.

Karakhan Mausoleum

Karakhan Mausoleum is another ancient mausoleum located in the center of Taraz. It was built in the 11th century and is believed to be the final resting place of Karakhan, a local ruler. The mausoleum is known for its intricate carvings and detailed motifs.

Taraz Historical Museum

The Taraz Historical Museum is located in the center of Taraz and is dedicated to preserving the city’s rich history and cultural heritage. The museum houses a collection of ancient artifacts, including pottery, jewelry, and historical documents.

Natural Attractions of Taraz: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Karatau Mountains

The Karatau Mountains are located to the west of Taraz and are a popular destination for hiking and scenic drives. The mountains are home to diverse wildlife and offer breathtaking views of the surrounding region.

Shu Valley

The Shu Valley is a long, narrow valley located to the north of Taraz. It is known for its stunning views and serene environment. Visitors can enjoy hiking, camping, and horseback riding in the valley.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Taraz

Taraz offers a range of hiking and biking routes for adventurers. The Karatau Mountains offer some of the most challenging trails, while the Shu Valley offers more moderate routes. Visitors can also explore the Aksu Canyon, which offers stunning views and unique rock formations.

Best Things to Do in Taraz: Activities and Tours

Visit the Local Bazaar

Taraz is known for its bustling bazaar, which offers a wide range of fresh produce, souvenirs, and local products. Visitors can explore the bazaar, try local delicacies, and haggle for deals.

Attend a Traditional Kazakh Wedding

Traditional Kazakh weddings are famous for their vibrant ceremonies, delicious food, and stunning traditional dress. Visitors can experience the unique culture and hospitality of Kazakhstan by attending a traditional Kazakh wedding.

Food and Drink Scene of Taraz

Must-Try Dishes

Beshbarmak

Beshbarmak is the national dish of Kazakhstan and consists of boiled meat served on a bed of noodles with onions and potatoes. It is often served with broth and is a popular dish to share with friends and family.

Kuurdak

Kuurdak is a traditional Kazakh dish made from mutton and potatoes. The meat is fried until tender and served with onions and herbs.

Local Beverages

Kumis

Kumis is a fermented drink made from mare’s milk. It is a popular drink in Kazakhstan and is said to have many health benefits.

Shubat

Shubat is a sour milk drink made from camel’s milk. It is a popular drink in the Central Asian region and is often served as a refreshing drink during hot summer months.

Where to Stay in Taraz: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Taraz offers a range of accommodation options, including hotels, hostels, and guesthouses. Some popular options include the Dostyk Hotel, the Taraz Hotel, and the Ak-Maral Guesthouse.

What to Buy in Taraz: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Visitors can find a range of unique souvenirs and local products in Taraz’s bustling bazaar. Handcrafted pottery, traditional Kazakh clothing, and local food products are some popular items.

Local Culture and Customs of Taraz

Kazakhstan has a unique culture and is known for its rich traditions and hospitality. Visitors should respect local customs and show politeness and courtesy to locals.

Best Time to Visit Taraz

The best time to visit Taraz is during the spring and fall months when the weather is mild and comfortable. Summer months can be hot, and winter months can be cold and snowy.

Do’s and Don’ts of Taraz

Do:

  • Dress appropriately when visiting religious sites.
  • Show respect to elders and authority figures.
  • Try local delicacies and traditional Kazakh food.

Don’t:

  • Take pictures of military installations or government buildings.
  • Raise your voice in public spaces.
  • Insult or disrespect the Kazakh culture or traditions.

Safety and Health

Safety in Taraz

Taraz is generally safe for visitors, but tourists should take basic precautions, such as avoiding dark and deserted streets at night.

Health in Taraz

Visitors should ensure they are up to date with their routine vaccinations and consider hepatitis A and typhoid vaccinations. It is also essential to drink bottled or filtered water to avoid gastrointestinal problems.

Getting to Taraz: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Taraz

The closest airport to Taraz is Shymkent International Airport, located approximately 155 km away. Visitors can also fly into Almaty International Airport, located approximately 600 km away.

Bus Stations Near Taraz

Taraz has several bus stations that offer transportation to nearby cities and towns. The Taraz bus station is located in the center of the city and offers both domestic and international connections.

Other Options

Taxis and private drivers are also available for transportation in Taraz and the surrounding region.

How to Get Around in Taraz

Walking and cycling are popular ways to explore Taraz. Taxis and private drivers are also available for transportation.

Where to Go Next from Taraz: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Shymkent

Shymkent, the capital city of the South Kazakhstan region, is located approximately 155 km from Taraz. It is known for its vibrant nightlife, beautiful parks, and museums.

Aksu-Zhabagly Nature Reserve

Aksu-Zhabagly Nature Reserve is located approximately 180 km from Taraz and is known for its diverse wildlife, stunning landscapes, and unique flora and fauna.

Conclusion

Taraz is an excellent destination for visitors looking to experience the rich history, culture, and natural beauty of Kazakhstan. With its range of attractions, stunning landscapes, and delicious food, Taraz has something for everyone.
