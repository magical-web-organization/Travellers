Introduction to Issyk Kul

Issyk Kul is a beautiful alpine lake nestled in the Tian Shan Mountains of Kyrgyzstan, in central Asia. With a surface area of over 6,200 square kilometers and a maximum depth of 668 meters, Issyk Kul is the second-largest alpine lake in the world. The name Issyk Kul translates to “warm lake” in the local language, and it’s a popular tourist destination in Kyrgyzstan.

List of Places to Visit in Issyk Kul

Here is a list of must-visit places in Issyk Kul:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • The Regional Museum in Karakol: This museum showcases the history and culture of the Issyk Kul region, including exhibits on local flora and fauna, traditional Kyrgyz crafts, and archaeological finds from the surrounding mountains.
  • The Djety Oguz Valley: This picturesque valley is home to several unique rock formations, as well as the Djety Oguz waterfall, and is steeped in local folklore and legend.
  • The Silk Road Burial Mounds: These ancient burial mounds are thought to date back to the 5th century BC and offer insight into the customs and traditions of the region’s former inhabitants.

Natural Attractions of Issyk Kul: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Altyn Arashan: This stunning mountain valley is home to natural hot springs, a Uighur mosque, and breathtaking views of the surrounding peaks.
  • Skazka Canyon: This fantastical canyon is filled with otherworldly shapes and formations, carved out of the red sandstone by the wind and water over millions of years.
  • Fairy Tale Canyon: This canyon is famous for its colorful sandstone cliffs, which have been eroded into a variety of striking shapes and sizes that resemble creatures and objects from children’s stories.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Issyk Kul

Issyk Kul is a paradise for hikers and bikers alike, with numerous trails and routes winding through its rugged, scenic terrain. Some popular hiking and biking destinations include:

  • The Jeti Oguz valley and waterfall trail.
  • The Skazka Canyon and surrounding trails.
  • Terskey Ala-Too mountains.

Best Things to Do in Issyk Kul: Activities and Tours

Some of the best things to do in Issyk Kul include:

  • Horseback riding: A popular local activity, with many opportunities to explore the surrounding mountains and valleys on horseback.
  • Boat tours: Take a leisurely cruise on the lake and enjoy the stunning scenery.
  • Cultural tours: Experience local traditions and customs, such as felt-making and eagle-hunting.

Week-Long Itinerary for Issyk Kul

Here is a sample itinerary for a week-long trip to Issyk Kul:

Day Activity
1 Explore the Regional Museum in Karakol and take a dip in the hot springs at Altyn Arashan.
2 Hike to the top of the Terskey Ala-Too mountains for panoramic views of the surrounding landscape.
3 Take a leisurely boat tour of Issyk Kul and visit the Silk Road Burial Mounds.
4 Horseback ride to the Djety Oguz Valley and waterfall.
5 Enjoy a cultural tour, including visits to traditional Kyrgyz workshops and a yurt camp experience.
6 Bike through Skazka Canyon and enjoy a picnic lunch at the top.
7 Relax on the beach and soak up some sun before departing for your next destination.

Food and Drink Scene of Issyk Kul

Kyrgyz cuisine is heavily influenced by the country’s nomadic past, with many dishes featuring meat, dairy, and bread. Some local favorites include:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Beshbarmak: A hearty dish made with boiled meat and noodles, typically served with a generous dollop of sour cream.
  • Manty: Steamed dumplings filled with ground meat and onions.
  • Laghman: A spicy noodle soup typically made with mutton or beef.

Local Beverages

  • Kymyz: A traditional Kyrgyz drink made from fermented mare’s milk.
  • Chalap: A salty yogurt drink similar to buttermilk.

Where to Stay in Issyk Kul: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

There are plenty of accommodations to choose from in Issyk Kul, ranging from luxury hotels to budget-friendly guesthouses. Here are some options:

  • Karven Four Seasons Resort: A 5-star resort located on the shore of Issyk Kul, offering luxurious rooms, a private beach, and a range of activities.
  • Issyk Kul Lodge: A rustic guesthouse situated in the mountains, offering cozy accommodations and stunning views.
  • Green Yard Guesthouse: A budget-friendly option located in the town of Cholpon-Ata, offering comfortable rooms and a friendly atmosphere.

What to Buy in Issyk Kul: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Some of the best souvenirs to bring home from Issyk Kul include:

  • Shyrdak felt rugs: A traditional Kyrgyz handicraft, featuring intricate designs and bright colors.
  • Yurts: Small yurts make great souvenirs and can be used as decorations or mini-gazebos.
  • Kyrgyz honey: Known for its flavor and medicinal properties, Kyrgyz honey is a popular local product.

Local Culture and Customs of Issyk Kul

Kyrgyz culture is steeped in tradition and hospitality. Visitors to Issyk Kul can expect to be welcomed with warmth and kindness, and are encouraged to learn about and respect local customs and practices. Some general etiquette tips include:

  • Remove your shoes before entering a home or yurt.
  • Always offer to share food or drink with your host.
  • Dress modestly and conservatively, especially in rural areas.

Best Time to Visit Issyk Kul

The best time to visit Issyk Kul is between June and September, when the weather is warm and sunny. The area receives a lot of snowfall in the winter, making travel difficult, if not impossible.

Do’s and Don’ts of Issyk Kul

Do:

  • Respect local customs and traditions.
  • Sample the local cuisine and beverages.
  • Try to learn a few words of Kyrgyz, such as “salam” (hello) and “rahmat” (thank you).

Don’t:

  • Disrespect religious or cultural sites.
  • Engage in illegal activities, such as drug use or theft.
  • Take photos of people without their permission.

Safety and Health

Safety in Issyk Kul

Issyk Kul is generally a safe place to visit, with low levels of crime and violence. However, visitors should still exercise caution and be aware of their surroundings, especially when traveling in rural areas.

Health in Issyk Kul

Visitors to Issyk Kul should be up-to-date on routine vaccinations and consult their doctor regarding any necessary additional vaccinations or preventive medications. The high altitude and rugged terrain can also pose a risk to visitors with certain health conditions, such as heart or respiratory problems.

Getting to Issyk Kul: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Issyk Kul

The closest airport to Issyk Kul is Manas International Airport in Bishkek, which is about 400 kilometers away.

Bus Stations Near Issyk Kul

Buses run regularly between Bishkek and the town of Cholpon-Ata, on the northern shore of Issyk Kul.

Other Options

Taxis and private cars are also available for hire, either in Bishkek or locally in Issyk Kul.

How to Get Around in Issyk Kul

The best way to get around Issyk Kul is by car or taxi. Many of the main attractions are located along the shore, and there is little public transportation available.

Where to Go Next from Issyk Kul: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

If you’re looking to continue your adventure in Kyrgyzstan, here are some nearby destinations worth exploring:

  • Bishkek: The capital city of Kyrgyzstan, with a vibrant food and arts scene.
  • Osh: A historic city in southern Kyrgyzstan, known for its bustling bazaars and ancient architecture.
  • Sary-Chelek: A beautiful lake and nature reserve located in the neighboring Jalal-Abad region.

Conclusion

Issyk Kul is a stunning destination with something for everyone, whether you’re looking to explore the great outdoors, experience local culture and traditions, or simply relax and soak up the scenery. With its friendly locals, delicious food, and breathtaking natural beauty, Issyk Kul is a must-visit destination in Kyrgyzstan.
