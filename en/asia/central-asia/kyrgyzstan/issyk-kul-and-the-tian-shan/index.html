Introduction to Issyk Kul and the Tian Shan

Nestled in the heart of Central Asia, Kyrgyzstan offers breathtaking natural wonders to explore. Issyk Kul, the second-largest alpine lake in the world, and the nearby Tian Shan mountain range are two of the top destinations that should not be missed.

Issyk Kul, which is also known as the “Hot Lake,” has a surface area of over 6,200 square kilometers and a maximum depth of 668 meters. It is surrounded by the Tian Shan mountain range, which has peaks over 7,000 meters high.

This comprehensive guide will take you through the best places to visit, things to do, where to stay, and more in Issyk Kul and the Tian Shan.

List of Places to Visit in Issyk Kul and the Tian Shan

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Issyk Kul State Historical and Cultural Museum-Reserve: This museum exhibits the history, culture, and traditions of the Issyk Kul region.
  • Burana Tower: An ancient minaret located in the Chuy Valley, which dates back to the 10th century.
  • Tash Rabat: This historical site is a 15th-century stone caravanserai located in the Tian Shan mountains. It is now a museum and an important stop on the Silk Road.
  • Karakol Museum of Local Lore: This museum showcases exhibits of local flora and fauna, nomadic culture, and regional history.
  • Dungan Mosque: Constructed in 1907 by Chinese Muslims, it is a unique Muslim mosque with a distinctive style not found in any other part of the world.

Natural Attractions of Issyk Kul and the Tian Shan: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Lake Issyk Kul: Enjoy the crystal clear waters of the lake and experience local village life along the shore.
  • Jeti Oguz: This beautiful valley in the Tian Shan mountains features seven red rock formations shaped like bulls.
  • Altyn Arashan: A beautiful hot spring located in a picturesque valley surrounded by mountains. Visitors can relax in the hot springs while enjoying scenic views of the surrounding area.
  • Fairy Tale Canyon: A stunning sandstone canyon featuring various rock formations that resemble animals, objects, and different figures.
  • Karakol National Park: This park covers an area of more than 1500 square kilometers and features beautiful scenic landscapes, diverse flora and fauna, and a range of outdoor activities such as hiking, skiing, and horseback riding.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Issyk Kul and the Tian Shan

Kyrgyzstan, known as the land of the horseback rider, offers some of the best hiking and biking routes in the world. Here are some of the best trails that you can explore:

  • Issyk Kul Hiking Trails: There are several hiking trails in the Issyk Kul region that are popular among both locals and tourists. Among the most popular are the Jeti Oguz and the Skazka Canyons.
  • Tian Shan Mountains: The Tian Shan mountain range is a paradise for hikers and bikers. There are numerous trails to be explored, ranging from easy walks to challenging multi-day treks. Some of the most scenic routes include the Ala Archa, Altyn Arashan, and the Karakol Valley.
  • Eagle Hunting Trails: If you’re looking for something unique, take a guided tour with an eagle hunter and explore the beautiful mountains in the Issyk Kul region.

Best Things to Do in Issyk Kul and the Tian Shan: Activities and Tours

Here are some of the best activities and tours that you can do in Issyk Kul and the Tian Shan:

  • Horseback Riding: Kyrgyzstan is known as the land of the horseback rider, and there’s no better way to explore the beautiful landscapes than on horseback. You can take short day tours or multi-day tours that include horseback riding, hiking, and camping.
  • Nomadic Homestays: Experience the local nomadic culture by staying with a local family. You can learn about their traditions, daily life and enjoy homemade food.
  • Skiing: The Karakol ski resort is one of the best places to experience skiing in Kyrgyzstan, offering a variety of runs for all levels of skiers.
  • Visit Local Markets: If you’re interested in local culture, visiting local markets is a must. You’ll find a variety of fresh produce, dry fruits, souvenirs, and local handicrafts.
  • Attend a Yurt Building Workshop: Kyrgyzstan is famous for its yurt structures used by the nomadic communities for centuries, and attending a yurt building workshop is a unique experience that will allow you to learn about the process, experience the craft and build a traditional yurt.

Week-Long Itinerary for Issyk Kul and the Tian Shan

If you have a week to explore Issyk Kul and the Tian Shan, here’s a suggested itinerary:

  • Day 1-2: Explore Bishkek and surroundings
  • Day 3: Visit Burana Tower and Tash Rabat historical sites
  • Day 4: Visit Jeti Oguz, Fairy Tale Canyon and enjoy a horseback ride
  • Day 5: Take a relaxing day at Altyn Arashan hot springs
  • Day 6: Explore Karakol National Park and hike to the Karakol Valley
  • Day 7: Enjoy a scenic drive around Issyk Kul, visit local markets, and relax on the beach.

Food and Drink Scene of Issyk Kul and the Tian Shan

The food in Kyrgyzstan is heavily influenced by its nomadic past and its location on the Silk Road. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Beshbarmak: A traditional dish made from boiled noodles, meat, and onions.
  • Laghman: Noodle soup with meat and vegetables
  • Manti: Steamed dumplings with meat and onions
  • Plov: Rice pilaf with carrots, onions, and meat

Local Beverages

  • Kymyz: A traditional drink made from fermented mare’s milk
  • Bozo: A drink made from fermented millet
  • Chalap: A sour yogurt drink

Where to Stay in Issyk Kul and the Tian Shan: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

There are various options for accommodation in Issyk Kul and the Tian Shan region, ranging from budget hostels to luxury hotels. Some of the popular options include:

  • Rakhat Hotel: A modern hotel located in the center of Bishkek city
  • Eco hostel: An eco-friendly guesthouse located in Karakol with a beautiful garden and mountain views
  • Ashu Guesthouse: A lovely guesthouse located in Tamga village, overlooking Issyk Kul.
  • Moon Hill Hostel: A charming hostel located in Jeti Oguz, offering fantastic views of the red rock formations.

What to Buy in Issyk Kul and the Tian Shan: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Kyrgyzstan offers a variety of souvenirs and local products that you can bring back home to remember your trip.

  • Felt Products: Kyrgyzstan is known for its handmade felt products, including slippers, hats, and bags.
  • Nomadic Jewelry: Handmade traditional Kyrgyz jewelry, including earrings, necklaces, and bracelets.
  • Juniper Products: Kyrgyzstan is famous for its juniper trees, and you can buy juniper products such as perfumes, oils, and incense.
  • Dry Fruits and Nuts: Kyrgyzstan is known for its high-quality dry fruits and nuts, including almonds, pistachios, and apricots.

Local Culture and Customs of Issyk Kul and the Tian Shan

Kyrgyzstan’s cultural heritage is influenced by its nomadic past and its location on the Silk Road. The people are hospitable, and the culture is steeped in traditions and rituals. Here are some things to keep in mind:

  • Respect Local Customs: Be respectful of the local customs and etiquette, dress modestly and ask for permission before taking photographs of people.
  • Guest Etiquette: In Kyrgyz culture, hosts are top priority, always take off your shoes, and bring a small gift when visiting a local family or staying in a homestay.
  • Horseback Riding: Horses are an important aspect of Kyrgyz culture and are seen as a symbol of freedom and independence. Be respectful when riding horses, be careful not to hurt them, and listen to your horseman’s instructions.

Best Time to Visit Issyk Kul and the Tian Shan

The best time to visit Issyk Kul and the Tian Shan is from May to September. During this time, the weather is mild and pleasant, and the landscapes are green and lush. The temperature can vary from 20 to 30 degrees Celsius, making it an ideal time for outdoor activities.

Do’s and Don’ts of Issyk Kul and the Tian Shan

Do:

  • Learn some basic Kyrgyz phrases and customs: Showing respect to the local culture and language is appreciated by locals.
  • Try Local Food: Kyrgyz cuisine is delicious and offers a variety of unique dishes inspired by a nomadic lifestyle.
  • Pack and dress appropriately: Weather can be unpredictable, so be sure to pack layers for different temperatures and dress modestly when visiting rural areas.

Don’t:

  • Take photos of locals without permission: Always ask before taking photos of locals or children.
  • Disrespect the environment: Kyrgyzstan’s natural environment is unique, and it is essential to keep it clean and tidy.
  • Shoot guns: Guns are seen as a symbol of power in Kyrgyz culture, and shooting them recreationally is frowned upon.

Safety and Health

Safety in Issyk Kul and the Tian Shan

Kyrgyzstan is generally a safe place to travel. However, it is essential to take some safety precautions to ensure a hassle-free trip.

  • Avoid political demonstrations: Political demonstrations are rare, but when they occur, it is best to avoid them to ensure your safety.
  • Use registered taxis: Only use taxis that are registered with official taxi companies, as unregistered taxis may be risky.
  • Be cautious when hiking: Hiking in the mountains can be challenging and dangerous, a guide is recommended, and to follow warnings such as weather alerts.
  • Stay informed: Check the local news and consult with locals or tourism offices about the security of the area you plan to visit.

Health in Issyk Kul and the Tian Shan

It’s essential to be prepared for possible health issues before traveling to Kyrgyzstan. Here are some tips to keep in mind:

  • Get Vaccinated: Hepatitis A, Hepatitis B and Typhoid vaccines are recommended before traveling to Kyrgyzstan.
  • Stay Hydrated: Drink plenty of water, avoid tap water and only drink bottled water, and use sunscreen to protect against the sun.
  • Pack a First Aid Kit: Make sure you have a well-stocked first aid kit, including medicines for common illnesses like headaches or stomach bugs.

Getting to Issyk Kul and the Tian Shan: Transportation Options and Logistics

Kyrgyzstan is still developing its transportation infrastructure. Here are some of the best ways to get to Issyk Kul and the Tian Shan:

Airports Near Issyk Kul and the Tian Shan

  • Manas International Airport: The international airport serving the Kyrgyz capital of Bishkek is Manas International Airport, which provides regular flights to major European and Asian cities.
  • Karakol Airport: There is also a small regional airport in Karakol, which offers limited domestic flights to Bishkek.

Bus Stations Near Issyk Kul and the Tian Shan

  • Bishkek Western Bus Station: Most buses to Issyk Kul and the surrounding areas depart from the Bishkek western bus station, which offers affordable transport options.
  • Karakol Bus Station: The Karakol bus station offers excellent services and provides transport to Bishkek and nearby regions.

Other Options

  • Taxis: Taxis are available in most cities, and you can negotiate rates with the driver. Agree on a price before starting to avoid misunderstandings.
  • Car Rentals: Car rental companies are available in Bishkek, Karakol, and Issyk Kul Region, and are a great option for independent travel.

How to Get Around in Issyk Kul and the Tian Shan

Public transportation in Kyrgyzstan is composed of buses, marshrutkas (mini-buses), taxis, and shared taxis. Public transportation can be crowded and uncomfortable, so you may want to consider renting a car or taking a private transfer instead.

Where to Go Next from Issyk Kul and the Tian Shan: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Kyrgyzstan is located in Central Asia, and there is no shortage of exciting destinations to explore after your trip to Issyk Kul and the Tian Shan. Here are some options:

  • Almaty, Kazakhstan: A modern city just over the border from Kyrgyzstan, is known for its serene parks and gardens, nightlife, and winter ski resorts.
  • Samarkand, Uzbekistan: One of the most historically important cities in the world, Samarkand is famous for its stunning mosques, amazing gardens, and traditional bazaars.
  • Tashkent, Uzbekistan: Uzbekistan’s capital city, Tashkent, is a vibrant, modern city that combines Soviet-era architecture with traditional Islamic designs. It has a rich cultural heritage that is well worth exploring.
