Introduction to Kadji Say

Kadji Say is a hidden gem located in Issyk Kul and the Tian Shan mountains of Kyrgyzstan, Central Asia. This small village offers an escape into the serene and magnificent mountains, with plenty of scenic tracks and trails where you can hike or bike your way through. Apart from its natural beauty, Kadji Say is home to various historical and cultural landmarks, which offer a glimpse into the village’s rich heritage.

List of Places to Visit in Kadji Say

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Kadji Say has various historical landmarks that visitors can explore, each of which offers unique insights into the village’s culture and history. Some of these landmarks include:

  • The Burana Tower: The Burana Tower is a minaret built in the 11th century and is one of the unique structures of its time. The tower offers panoramic views of Kyrgyzstan’s landscape and is a perfect spot for photography.

  • The Petroglyphs of Cholpon Ata: The Petroglyphs of Cholpon Ata has a large collection of petroglyphs dating back to the Bronze Age. These petroglyphs depict the lifestyle of early nomads, and visitors get to learn about their social and cultural practices.

  • The Russian Orthodox Holy Trinity Cathedral: The Russian Orthodox Holy Trinity Cathedral is a beautiful orthodox church that visitors can explore. It displays magnificent wooden carvings and is home to a collection of rare religious paintings.

Natural Attractions of Kadji Say: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Apart from its cultural and historical sites, Kadji Say is famous for its outdoor adventure and scenic views. The village offers plenty of hiking and biking tracks that allow visitors to roam around the countryside and enjoy nature at its best. Some of the natural landmarks you can visit while in Kadji Say include:

  • Lake Issyk-Kul: Lake Issyk-Kul is one of Kyrgyzstan’s natural wonders, and Kadji Say is located only a stone’s throw away. The lake is the second-largest inland body of water in the world and is surrounded by snow-capped mountains. Visitors can enjoy swimming or have a relaxing picnic on the beach.

  • The Tian Shan Mountains: The Tian Shan Mountains are a collection of snow-capped mountains located near Kadji Say. Visitors can hike and bike on the several tracks, and the route to the Ala-Kul pass is a must-try for adventure seekers.

  • The Sary-Chelek Biosphere Reserve: The Sary-Chelek Biosphere Reserve is a large nature reserve located in the Tian Shan mountains. It has a diverse variety of flora and fauna, and visitors can enjoy a guided wildlife tour.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Kadji Say

Kadji Say has several hiking and biking tracks that visitors can use to explore the countryside and enjoy nature’s beauty. Some of the popular routes include:

  • The Keskenkija Loop: This route is a four-day hiking track that covers 54 km, and visitors get to enjoy views of Lake Issyk-Kul, the Tian Shan Mountains, and Keskenkija gorge.

  • The Altyn Arashan: This route is a two-day hiking experience that covers 31 km. Visitors can enjoy the magical hot springs in the Altyn Arashan valley and take in the stunning views of the Tian Shan mountains.

  • The Kok-Jayik Loop: This route is a three-day bike track covering 85 km. Visitors get to experience the Kyrgyz lifestyle by staying with nomads, grazing livestock, and riding horses.

Best Things to Do in Kadji Say: Activities and Tours

Apart from hiking, biking, and visiting landmarks, there are several other activities visitors can engage in when in Kadji Say. Some popular options include:

  • Horseback riding: Visitors can ride horses and experience the Kyrgyz nomadic lifestyle. Guides can lead visitors through scenic farms, mountain passes, and scenic forests while on horseback.

  • Skiing and snowboarding: During winter, visitors enjoy skiing and snowboarding on the snow-covered mountains of Kadji Say. Ski resorts such as Karakol, Too-Ashu, and Orlovka are nearby.

  • Paragliding: The mountainous terrain and stable winds in Kadji Say offer the perfect conditions for paragliding enthusiasts. Visitors can take off from the top of the Tian Shan Mountain range and glide down for a bird’s eye view of the Kyrgyz countryside.

Week-Long Itinerary for Kadji Say

If you’re planning to spend a week in Kadji Say, here’s a sample itinerary to guide you:

Day 1-2: Explore the Burana Tower and Cholpon Ata Petroglyphs

Spend your first two days exploring the cultural landmarks in Kadji Say. Visit the Burana Tower and the Cholpon Ata Petroglyphs to learn about the region’s rich history and culture.

Day 3-4: Hiking the Keskenkija Loop

Spend the next two days hiking through the Keskenkija Loop, covering 54km in the process. Enjoy views of Lake Issyk-Kul, the Tian Shan Mountains, and Keskenkija gorge.

Day 5-6: Horseback Riding and Skiing

Day five is reserved for horseback riding, while day six is skiing and snowboarding on the snow-covered mountains of Kadji Say.

Day 7: Visiting the Lake Issyk-Kul

On the final day, visit Lake Issyk-Kul and have some time swimming or have a relaxing picnic on the beach.

Food and Drink Scene of Kadji Say

Kyrgyzstan has unique and tasty cuisine, and visitors can enjoy an array of local dishes and beverages when in Kadji Say.

Must-Try Dishes

  • Beshbarmak: Beshbarmak is a traditional Kyrgyz food made of boiled lamb and noodles. It’s the national dish of Kyrgyzstan and is a must-try when in Kadji Say.

  • Plov: Plov is a rice-based dish that incorporates vegetables and meat. It’s a popular meal among locals and visitors alike.

  • Samsa: Samsa is a baked pastry stuffed with minced meat or potatoes.

Local Beverages

  • Kymyz: Kymyz is a fermented drink made from mare’s milk. It’s a favorite among locals and visitors and considered a delicacy.

  • Bozo: Bozo is another traditional Kyrgyz drink made from millet.

Where to Stay in Kadji Say: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

There are several accommodation options in Kadji Say, depending on your budget and preference. Some popular options include:

  • Usch Konyr Guest House: The Usch Konyr Guest House is located near the Keskenkija Gorge and offers cozy accommodation with breathtaking mountain views.

  • Royal Beach Hotel Issyk-Kul: The Royal Beach Hotel is an affordable beachfront hotel that provides excellent services and has comfortable rooms with stunning views.

  • Karakol Ski Resort: For those looking for a ski resort, Karakol offers excellent accommodation options that meet different budgets.

What to Buy in Kadji Say: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

When in Kadji Say, visitors can bring home some of the local souvenirs and products to remind them of their visit. Some of the popular souvenirs include:

  • Kyrgyz hats: Kyrgyz hats are a traditional garment made from felt and adorned with embroidered designs.

  • Kyrgyz jewelry: The Kyrgyz people are skilled craftsmen, and visitors can buy locally made jewelry pieces with intricate designs.

  • Kyrgyz carpets: Kyrgyz carpets are famous in the region and are made from wool and have distinct designs.

Local Culture and Customs of Kadji Say

Kyrgyz people are hospitable and friendly, and visitors should expect to be treated with respect and kindness. The national language is Kyrgyz, but Russian and English are widely spoken, especially in tourist areas. Kyrgyz people follow Islamic traditions and dress modestly. Visitors should dress respectfully when visiting religious locations.

Best Time to Visit Kadji Say

The best time to visit Kadji Say is during summer (June to August) when the weather is suitable for outdoor activities such as hiking, biking, and swimming. Winter is an excellent time for skiing and snowboarding.

Do’s and Don’ts of Kadji Say

Do:

  • Respect the local culture and customs and dress modestly when visiting religious sites.
  • Ask for permission before taking photographs of people.
  • Be open to trying new foods and drinks.

Don’t:

  • Refuse offers of hospitality, such as food and drink, as it is considered impolite.
  • Criticize the local customs, religion, or culture in public.

Safety and Health

Safety in Kadji Say

Kadji Say is relatively safe, and visitors should take the same precautions as they would in any other tourist destination. Take care of your belongings and avoid walking alone at night.

Health in Kadji Say

Visitors should take health precautions, such as drinking bottled water and avoiding street food. When hiking, carry enough water and wear appropriate hiking gear to avoid injury.

Getting to Kadji Say: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Kadji Say

The nearest airport to Kadji Say is the Manas International Airport in Bishkek. From the airport, visitors can take a taxi or bus to Kadji Say.

Bus Stations Near Kadji Say

The Bishkek Bus Station is the closest bus station to Kadji Say. From Bishkek, visitors can take a bus to Karakol, which is a two-hour drive from Kadji Say.

Other Options

Visitors can also choose to rent a car or take a private transfer to Kadji Say.

How to Get Around in Kadji Say

Getting around Kadji Say is relatively easy, and visitors can use the following transportation options:

  • Taxi: Taxis are available in Kadji Say, and visitors can negotiate fares with the drivers.

  • Buses: There are public buses that operate within Kadji Say and other parts of Kyrgyzstan.

Where to Go Next from Kadji Say: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Kyrgyzstan has several exciting regions and landmarks for visitors to explore. Some nearby destinations and excursions include:

  • Bishkek: Bishkek is the capital city of Kyrgyzstan and is located three hours from Kadji Say. It’s a bustling metropolis with a mix of Soviet-era buildings and modern structures.

  • Tash-Rabat: The Tash-Rabat is an ancient caravanserai that served as a stopover for travelers on the Silk Road. It’s located four hours from Kadji Say and offers an exciting excursion into Kyrgyzstan’s history.

Conclusion

Kadji Say offers visitors a unique opportunity to experience Kyrgyzstan’s culture, history, and natural beauty. Visitors can explore picturesque landscapes, immerse themselves in local customs, and try out the delicious local cuisine. Whether you’re looking for an adventure in the mountains or a relaxing beach holiday, Kadji Say has something for everyone.
