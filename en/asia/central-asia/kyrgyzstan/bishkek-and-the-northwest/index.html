Introduction to Bishkek and the Northwest

Bishkek is the capital city of Kyrgyzstan, located in the Northwest region of Central Asia. The city is situated at the foot of the Ala-Too Mountains and is known for its wide boulevards, Soviet-era architecture, and bustling bazaars. The Northwest region is home to many natural attractions, including mountain ranges, valleys, and lakes. This travel guide will provide you with an overview of the best places to visit, things to do, where to stay, and what to buy in Bishkek and the Northwest of Kyrgyzstan.

Food and Drink Scene of Bishkek and the Northwest

Must-Try Dishes

  • Beshbarmak: A traditional Kyrgyz dish made with boiled meat, cooked noodles, and onion sauce.
  • Lagman: A noodle soup dish with vegetables and meat.
  • Plov: A rice dish typically served with meat or vegetables.
  • Manty: A steamed dumpling filled with meat or vegetables.

Local Beverages

  • Kumys: A fermented drink made from mare’s milk.
  • Shoro: A cold beverage made from wheat.
  • Bozo: A slightly alcoholic drink made from fermented millet.

Local Culture and Customs of Bishkek and the Northwest

Kyrgyzstan is known for its nomadic culture and hospitality towards guests. Visitors are encouraged to dress modestly, remove shoes when entering homes or mosques, and avoid pointing with their fingers. It is customary to drink tea and eat snacks as a sign of hospitality when visiting someone’s home.

Best Time to Visit Bishkek and the Northwest

The best time to visit Bishkek and the Northwest is from late June to early September when the weather is warm and dry, making it the perfect time for outdoor activities. Winter sports enthusiasts should visit from December to March when skiing, snowboarding, and ice skating are popular.

Do’s and Don’ts of Bishkek and the Northwest

Do:

  • Dress modestly, especially when visiting religious or cultural places.
  • Always ask for permission before taking photos of people or their property.
  • Bring cash when visiting markets and bazaars.

Don’t:

  • Don’t drink tap water or eat unwashed fruits and vegetables, as waterborne diseases are common.
  • Don’t wear shoes inside a mosque or someone’s home.
  • Don’t disrespect local customs and traditions.

Safety and Health

Safety in Bishkek and the Northwest

Bishkek and the Northwest region are considered relatively safe for tourists. However, it is recommended to be cautious when traveling alone at night and to avoid displaying valuables in public places.

Health in Bishkek and the Northwest

Visitors are advised to bring their own medical kits and be up to date with standard vaccinations, including hepatitis A and B, typhoid, and tetanus. It is also recommended to purchase travel insurance before departing.

Taxis, buses, and shared taxis are readily available in Bishkek and the Northwest region. Renting a car is also an option, but drivers should be cautious of the country’s poorly maintained road infrastructure.
