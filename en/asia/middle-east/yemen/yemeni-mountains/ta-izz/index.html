Introduction to Ta’izz

Ta’izz is a city located in the Yemeni Mountains of Yemen, in the Middle East of Asia. The city has a rich history and culture that dates back to the 6th century BC. Ta’izz offers a variety of attractions for tourists to explore, including historical sites, natural wonders, outdoor adventures, and local food and drink. In this comprehensive tour guide, we will cover all the essential information you need to know to plan your visit to Ta’izz.

List of Places to Visit in Ta’izz

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Al-Qahira Castle: A 12th-century castle built during the Ayyubid dynasty, located on a hilltop overlooking the city.
  • Great Mosque of Ta’izz: A large mosque dating back to the 12th century, with an impressive facade and intricate designs.
  • National Museum of Ta’izz: A comprehensive museum dedicated to showcasing the history and culture of Ta’izz, with exhibits ranging from ancient artifacts to more modern arts and crafts.

Natural Attractions of Ta’izz: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Jabal Sabr: A mountain that overlooks the city with breathtaking panoramic views of Ta’izz and its surroundings.
  • Wadi Dahr: A scenic valley with a series of cascading waterfalls, perfect for a refreshing dip on a hot day.
  • Al-Hoban: A limestone cave system located near the city with stunning stalactites and stalagmites.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Ta’izz

For those who enjoy outdoor activities, there are many options for hiking and biking in Ta’izz. Some popular routes include:

  • Jabal Sabr Hike: A challenging hike that takes you to the highest point of Ta’izz, where you can enjoy panoramic views of the city and the surrounding mountains.
  • Wadi Dahr Trail: A moderate hike that takes you through the scenic valley of Wadi Dahr, where you can enjoy the waterfalls and lush vegetation.
  • Al-Hoban Cave Trail: A moderate hike that takes you through the limestone cave system of Al-Hoban, where you can marvel at the stunning rock formations.

Best Things to Do in Ta’izz: Activities and Tours

  • Old City Walking Tour: Take a guided tour of the historic Old City of Ta’izz, with its narrow alleys and traditional architecture.
  • Market Tour: Explore the vibrant local markets of Ta’izz, where you can find anything from spices and textiles to handicrafts and souvenirs.
  • Traditional Cooking Class: Learn how to cook traditional Yemeni dishes with a local chef, and enjoy the fruits of your labor in a cozy setting.

Food and Drink Scene of Ta’izz

Must-Try Dishes

  • Salta: A hearty stew made with beans, meat, and spices, served with bread and a side of chili paste.
  • Bint al-Sahn: A sweet and buttery pastry made with layers of dough, honey, and clarified butter.
  • Zarb: A traditional Yemeni barbecue consisting of meat, vegetables, and rice cooked in an underground oven.

Local Beverages

  • Qishr: A warm and spicy coffee-like drink made with ginger, cinnamon, and coffee husks.
  • Shahi: A refreshing and sweet tea made with milk, sugar, cinnamon, and cardamom.

Where to Stay in Ta’izz: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Sheba Hotel: A historic hotel located in the heart of the Old City, with traditional architecture and comfortable rooms.
  • Al Saeed Hotel Taiz: A modern hotel with excellent facilities, including a rooftop pool and panoramic views of the city.
  • Casa Arabia Hotel: A cozy guesthouse with a homely ambiance and personalized service.

What to Buy in Ta’izz: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Handicrafts: Ta’izz is famous for its handmade pottery, jewelry, textiles, and carpets, which make excellent souvenirs.
  • Spices: Yemen is known for its aromatic spices, such as cumin, coriander, and cardamom, which can be bought in local markets.
  • Frankincense: Yemen is one of the few countries in the world where frankincense trees grow naturally, and its resin is highly valued for its medicinal and aromatic properties.

Local Culture and Customs of Ta’izz

  • Dress modestly, especially when visiting religious sites or attending events.
  • Yemen is a Muslim country, so respect local customs and traditions, such as not eating during daylight hours during the month of Ramadan.
  • Hospitality is highly valued in Yemeni culture, and it is common to be invited to people’s homes for meals or drinks.

Best Time to Visit Ta’izz

The best time to visit Ta’izz is between October and April when the weather is mild and pleasant. The summer months (June to September) can be hot and humid, making it challenging to explore the city’s outdoor attractions.

Do’s and Don’ts of Ta’izz

Do:

  • Try local food and drink, especially traditional Yemeni dishes.
  • Learn basic Arabic phrases, which can go a long way in communicating with locals.
  • Be respectful of local customs and dress modestly, especially in religious sites.

Don’t:

  • Don’t take photographs of people without their permission.
  • Don’t drink tap water or ice cubes made from tap water.
  • Don’t bring drugs or alcohol into the country.

Safety and Health

Safety in Ta’izz

Yemen has been suffering from instability and conflict in recent years, so it is essential to stay up to date with the latest travel advisories before planning a trip. It is generally safe to visit Ta’izz if you follow basic precautions, such as avoiding large crowds, staying aware of your surroundings, and not venturing out after dark.

Health in Ta’izz

It is advisable to take the necessary vaccinations before traveling to Yemen, such as hepatitis A and B, typhoid, and polio. It is also recommended to bring mosquito repellent, as there is a risk of malaria and dengue fever in some areas of the country.

Getting to Ta’izz: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Ta’izz

The closest airport to Ta’izz is Sana’a International Airport, which is about a 3-hour drive from the city. However, due to the ongoing conflict in Yemen, international flights to Sana’a have been suspended, so the only way to get to Ta’izz is through neighboring countries such as Oman or Saudi Arabia.

Bus Stations Near Ta’izz

There are several bus stations in Ta’izz that offer services to neighboring cities and towns, such as Aden, Sana’a, and Ibb. However, it is essential to check the latest travel advisories before using public transportation in Yemen due to security concerns.

Other Options

Taxis and private cars are widely available in Ta’izz, but it is advisable to arrange for transportation through a reputable company or hotel to ensure safety.

How to Get Around in Ta’izz

The best way to get around Ta’izz is on foot, especially in the historic Old City, which has narrow alleys and limited vehicle access. Taxis and private cars are also available, but it is advisable to negotiate the fare before getting in the vehicle.

Where to Go Next from Ta’izz: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Aden: A port city located on the southern coast of Yemen, with beautiful beaches and a rich history of trade and commerce.
  • Sana’a: The capital city of Yemen, known for its UNESCO World Heritage-listed Old City and traditional architecture.
  • Ibb: A charming town located in the Yemeni mountains, known for its lush green landscapes and traditional crafts.

Conclusion

Ta’izz is a fascinating city with a rich history, culture, and natural beauty. Though Yemen has suffered from instability and conflict in recent years, with proper planning and basic precautions, visiting Ta’izz can be an enriching and rewarding experience.
