Yemeni Highlands Travel Guide

Located in Yemen, in the Middle East, the Yemeni Highlands is a breathtaking region that offers a glimpse into the country’s rich history, culture, and natural beauty. This comprehensive travel guide will help you explore the best of Yemeni Highlands, including its culturally important attractions, natural attractions, hiking and biking routes, best things to do, food and drink scene, where to stay, local culture and customs, safety and health, transportation options, and nearby destinations.

Introduction to Yemeni Highlands

The Yemeni Highlands is a mountainous region that is situated in the western and central part of Yemen. The region is home to several mountain ranges, including the Haraz, Sarawat, and Bani Hareth, with elevations ranging between 2,000 and 3,000 meters above sea level. Yemeni Highlands is known for its picturesque landscape, traditional architecture, and ancient ruins that date back to the pre-Islamic period.

  • Mandi

Local Beverages

What to Buy in Yemeni Highlands: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Yemeni Highlands is known for its traditional handicrafts, including woven baskets, pottery, and rugs. Here are some of the best souvenirs and local products to bring home:

  • Yemeni honey
  • Zabibah - a traditional dessert made with raisins
  • Yemeni coffee
  • Khanjar - a curved dagger that is worn as a symbol of traditional masculinity

Local Culture and Customs of Yemeni Highlands

Yemeni culture is deeply rooted in its Islamic heritage, and locals take pride in their customs and traditions. Some of the customs and traditions that you might encounter in Yemeni Highlands include:

  • Dressing modestly
  • Removing shoes before entering mosques and homes
  • Using the right hand for eating and accepting gifts

Best Time to Visit Yemeni Highlands

The best time to visit Yemeni Highlands is between October and April when the weather is mild and dry. During the summer months, the region experiences intense heat and occasional rainstorms.

Do’s and Don’ts of Yemeni Highlands

To ensure a smooth and enjoyable trip to Yemeni Highlands, here are some do’s and don’ts:

Do:

  • Dress modestly
  • Learn a few Arabic phrases
  • Respect local customs and traditions

Don’t:

  • Take photographs of people without their permission
  • Consume alcohol in public
  • Disrespect Islam or its symbols

Safety and Health

Yemeni Highlands, like the rest of Yemen, is prone to political unrest and violence, making it potentially dangerous for tourists. It’s essential to stay updated with the security situation and take necessary precautions when traveling.

Safety in Yemeni Highlands

  • Avoid traveling after dark and in areas with known security risks
  • Carry a copy of your passport with you at all times
  • Register with your embassy or consulate before traveling to Yemen

Health in Yemeni Highlands

  • Make sure you receive all necessary vaccinations before traveling to Yemen
  • Avoid drinking tap water or eating street food that may be contaminated
  • Carry a first aid kit and any necessary medication with you at all times

Getting to Yemeni Highlands: Transportation Options and Logistics

The easiest way to get to Yemeni Highlands is by flying into Sana’a International Airport, which is the main airport in Yemen. From the airport, you can hire a taxi to get to your accommodation. There are also several bus stations in Sana’a that connect to other cities in Yemen.

Airports Near Yemeni Highlands

  • Sana’a International Airport

Bus Stations Near Yemeni Highlands

  • Al-Thawra Hospital Bus Station
  • Al-Hudaydah Bus Station
  • Taiz Bus Station

Other Options

Due to the ongoing political instability in Yemen, it’s best to avoid travel to the country unless absolutely necessary. If you do decide to travel to Yemen, be sure to check the latest travel advisories from your country’s foreign office.

How to Get Around in Yemeni Highlands

The best way to get around Yemeni Highlands is by hiring a local car and driver or by using public buses. Taxis and shared vans are also available but can be unreliable.
