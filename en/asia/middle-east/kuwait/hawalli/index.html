Introduction to Hawalli

Located in the heart of Kuwait, the vibrant district of Hawalli offers a unique blend of traditional and modern attractions. From world-class museums and historical landmarks to outdoor adventures and top-notch restaurants, Hawalli has something for everyone. Whether you’re a history buff, a nature lover, or a foodie, this guide will help you plan your ultimate Hawalli itinerary.

List of Places to Visit in Hawalli

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

If you want to immerse yourself in the rich culture and history of Hawalli, start with these must-visit attractions:

  • Kuwait National Museum: This impressive museum showcases Kuwait’s history and culture, with a fascinating collection of artifacts, artwork, and interactive exhibits.

  • Tareq Rajab Museum: Home to one of the largest collections of Islamic art in the world, this museum offers a rare glimpse into the region’s cultural heritage.

  • Al-Qurain Martyr’s Museum: Dedicated to the memory of the Kuwaiti martyrs who lost their lives in the Gulf War, this museum is a moving tribute to Kuwait’s resilience and strength.

Natural Attractions of Hawalli: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

If you’re seeking adventure and natural beauty, check out these top attractions:

  • Al Shaheed Park: Located in central Kuwait, this impressive urban park is a must-visit for nature lovers, with over 200,000 square meters of lush greenery, tranquil lakes, and jogging trails.

  • Messilah Beach: With its crystal-clear waters and soft white sand, Messilah Beach is a popular spot for swimming, sunbathing, and watersports like jet skiing and parasailing.

  • Jahra Pools Reserve: If you’re a birdwatcher or wildlife enthusiast, don’t miss Jahra Pools Reserve, a protected area with over 230 bird species, desert mammals, and reptiles.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Hawalli

For outdoor enthusiasts, Hawalli offers a wealth of hiking and biking routes, including:

  • Al Qurain Martyrs Trail: This 3 km trail is perfect for beginners, with stunning views of the surrounding desert and plenty of historical landmarks along the way.

  • Bayan Biking Trail: Located in the heart of Hawalli, this scenic 8 km trail offers a mix of paved and dirt paths, with breathtaking views of the Gulf and the city skyline.

  • Mutlaa Ridge Trail: For experienced hikers, the Mutlaa Ridge Trail is a challenging 9 km climb through rugged terrain and rocky peaks, with stunning panoramic views from the summit.

Best Things to Do in Hawalli: Activities and Tours

To make the most of your Hawalli trip, consider these top activities and tours:

  • Kuwait City Tour: Discover the hidden gems of Kuwait City, including the Grand Mosque, the Kuwait Towers, and the historic Souq Al Mubarakiya, with a knowledgeable guide.

  • Desert Safari: For a true taste of Kuwaiti life and culture, hop on a 4x4 jeep and explore the vast sand dunes and Bedouin camps of the desert.

  • Boat Tours: Take a leisurely boat tour along Kuwait Bay and enjoy the stunning views of the city skyline and the Persian Gulf, while sipping on traditional Arabic coffee and tea.

Week-Long Itinerary for Hawalli

If you have a week to explore Hawalli, here’s a sample itinerary to get you started:

Day 1: Kuwait National Museum, Kuwait Towers, and Grand Mosque

Day 2: Tareq Rajab Museum, Al-Qurain Martyr’s Museum, and Souq Al Mubarakiya

Day 3: Al Shaheed Park, Jahra Pools Reserve, and Messilah Beach

Day 4: Bayan Biking Trail, Al Kout Mall, and Al Fanar Cultural Center

Day 5: Desert Safari, Dhow Harbor, and Sharq Mall

Day 6: Mutlaa Ridge Trail, Scientific Center, and Aqua Park

Day 7: Boat Tour, Marina World, and The Avenues

Food and Drink Scene of Hawalli

No trip to Hawalli is complete without trying the local cuisine and beverages. Here are some must-try dishes and drinks:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Machboos: A traditional rice dish cooked with meat or fish, spices, and herbs, served with a side of salad and lime.

  • Balaleet: A sweet and savory breakfast dish made with vermicelli noodles, eggs, sugar, and cinnamon.

  • Ghabga: A festive feast served during Ramadan, consisting of lamb, rice, dates, and Arabic coffee.

Local Beverages

  • Karak Tea: A popular tea blend of black tea and cardamom, served hot and sweet, usually with a side of biscuits or dates.

  • Laban: A refreshing yogurt drink, often infused with mint, cucumber, and garlic.

  • Vimto: A fruity and sparkling beverage, popular during Ramadan and other festive occasions.

Where to Stay in Hawalli: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

If you’re looking for accommodation in Hawalli, these options should be on your radar:

  • The Regency Hotel Kuwait: A luxury hotel with a private beach, indoor and outdoor pools, and world-class restaurants.

  • Kuwait Continental Hotel: A budget-friendly option, located in the heart of Hawalli, with cozy rooms and free Wi-Fi.

  • Al Muhanna Plaza Hotel: A mid-range hotel with spacious suites, a fitness center, and an on-site restaurant.

What to Buy in Hawalli: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

If you want to bring a piece of Hawalli back home with you, consider these souvenirs and local products:

  • Arabian Oud: High-quality perfumes and scents made with traditional Arabic ingredients, like oud and amber.

  • Dates: Fresh and dried dates are a popular snack in Kuwait, with a wide variety of flavors and textures.

  • Jewelry: From gold to silver to precious gems, Kuwait is known for its beautiful and unique jewelry designs.

Local Culture and Customs of Hawalli

To be respectful and informed during your trip to Hawalli, it’s important to understand the local culture and customs. Here are some key points:

  • Religion: The majority of Kuwait’s population is Muslim, so it’s important to be mindful of Islamic customs and practices, especially during the holy month of Ramadan.

  • Dress Code: While there’s no official dress code in Hawalli, it’s recommended to dress modestly and avoid revealing clothing, especially in public places.

  • Greetings: In Kuwait, it’s common to greet people with “As-salamu alaykum” (peace be upon you) and respond with “Wa ‘alaykum as-salam” (and upon you, peace).

Best Time to Visit Hawalli

The best time to visit Hawalli is during the winter months (November to March), when temperatures are mild and pleasant, and outdoor activities are more enjoyable. It’s also a good time to experience traditional festivals and events, like the National Day celebration in February.

Do’s and Don’ts of Hawalli

To stay respectful and safe during your trip to Hawalli, keep in mind these do’s and don’ts:

Do:

  • Try the local food and beverages, and immerse yourself in the culture of Hawalli.

  • Dress modestly and respectfully, especially in religious and conservative areas.

  • Learn a few Arabic phrases, like greetings and basic conversations, to communicate with locals.

Don’t:

  • Drink alcohol in public places or during Ramadan, as it’s considered a violation of Islamic law.

  • Take pictures of people without their consent, especially women in traditional dress.

  • Engage in public displays of affection or offensive behavior, as it’s against Kuwaiti law and culture.

Safety and Health

To ensure a safe and healthy trip to Hawalli, consider these tips:

Safety in Hawalli

  • Avoid traveling alone at night, especially in secluded or unfamiliar areas.

  • Be aware of your surroundings and belongings, and keep your valuables safe and secure.

  • Follow traffic and pedestrian rules, and use caution when crossing streets or driving.

Health in Hawalli

  • Check with your doctor about necessary vaccinations and medications for Kuwait.

  • Avoid drinking tap water or consuming raw or undercooked food.

  • Protect yourself from the sun and heat, and stay hydrated throughout the day.

Getting to Hawalli: Transportation Options and Logistics

To get to and around Hawalli, consider these transportation options:

Airports Near Hawalli

The closest airport to Hawalli is the Kuwait International Airport, located about 20 km south of the city. From there, you can take a taxi or a rental car to your hotel or destination.

Bus Stations Near Hawalli

The local bus system in Hawalli is limited, but there are some bus stations and routes available, like the City Bus and the Kuwait Public Transport Company.

Other Options

  • Taxis: Taxis are widely available in Hawalli, and they’re a convenient and affordable way to get around the city.

  • Rental Cars: If you prefer to drive, you can rent a car from one of the many rental companies in Hawalli or at the airport.

How to Get Around in Hawalli

To get around Hawalli, here are some options:

  • Taxis: Taxis are a popular and convenient way to get around the city, with many reputable companies and drivers available.

  • Rental Cars: If you prefer to drive, you can rent a car from one of the many rental companies in Hawalli, and use apps like Google Maps to navigate the city.

  • Public Transportation: While the local bus system is limited in Hawalli, there are some routes and stations available, like the City Bus and the Kuwait Public Transport Company.

Where to Go Next from Hawalli: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

If you want to explore more of Kuwait and the Middle East, consider these nearby destinations and excursions:

  • Kuwait City: A vibrant and cosmopolitan city with a rich history and culture, including the iconic Kuwait Towers, the Kuwait National Museum, and the historic Souq Al Mubarakiya.

  • Failaka Island: A beautiful and serene island located about 20 km off the coast of Kuwait, with stunning beaches, ancient ruins, and traditional Kuwaiti heritage.

  • Dubai: A popular and world-famous destination, known for its skyscrapers, shopping malls, and luxurious resorts, located about 2 hours by flight from Kuwait.

Conclusion

From breathtaking natural beauty to rich cultural heritage, Hawalli is a unique and exciting destination for travelers of all backgrounds and interests. With this comprehensive guide, you can plan your ultimate Hawalli itinerary, and make the most of your time in this special part of the world.
