Are you planning a trip to Kuwait? This small Middle Eastern country boast some of the most fascinating cultural attractions, natural wonders, and outdoor adventures that you can experience. From vibrant museums to scenic hiking routes, there’s something for everyone in Kuwait. This comprehensive travel guide covers everything you need to know to make the most out of your visit to Kuwait.

Introduction to Kuwait

Kuwait is a small country located in the Middle East, bordering Iraq, Saudi Arabia, and the Persian Gulf. It has a rich history and culture that has been shaped by its unique geographic location and its many conquerors and traders over the centuries. The official language of Kuwait is Arabic, and the country’s currency is the Kuwaiti Dinar.

List of Places to Visit in Kuwait

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Kuwait has preserved its history and heritage through a number of museums and historical places, including:

  • Kuwait National Museum: This museum showcases the history, culture, and traditions of Kuwait through various exhibits and artifacts.
  • Tareq Rajab Museum: Located in Kuwait City, this museum houses a wide range of Islamic art, jewelry, calligraphy, and other antiques.
  • Kuwait House of National Works: This museum, located in Jahra, displays the cultural and social history of Kuwait.
  • Failaka Island: This small island offers a unique chance to explore the ruins of ancient Mesopotamia.

Natural Attractions of Kuwait: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

If you’re a nature lover or just seeking some adventure, Kuwait won’t disappoint you. Here are some of the must-visit natural attractions in Kuwait:

  • Al Kout Beach: This beautiful beach offers a stunning view of the Persian Gulf and is ideal for swimming and sunbathing.
  • Al Shaheed Park: Located in Kuwait City, Al Shaheed Park features beautiful gardens and a lake perfect for a picnic or a walk.
  • The Kuwait Zoo: The Kuwait Zoo is home to numerous exotic animals, such as elephants, lions, tigers, and zebras.
  • Wafra Farms: This agricultural area located in southern Kuwait offers trails for hiking and biking, as well as a chance to explore the traditional way of life in the country.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Kuwait

Kuwait offers several hiking and biking routes where visitors can enjoy the beautiful scenery and natural surroundings. Here are some of the popular routes:

  • Al Kout Trail: A scenic coastal trail that offers beautiful views of the Persian Gulf.
  • Jahra Pools Reserve: A nature reserve located in northern Kuwait that offers hiking trails and bird watching opportunities.
  • Al-Shaqaya Trail: A challenging hiking trail that traverses a rocky terrain offering stunning views of the desert landscape.

Best Things to Do in Kuwait: Activities and Tours

Kuwait offers a wide range of activities and tours that cater to different interests and preferences, such as:

  • Dhow Cruise: A traditional boat cruise to explore the Persian Gulf and watch the sunset.
  • Kuwait City Tour: A guided tour of Kuwait City to explore the architectural landmarks and cultural attractions of the city.
  • Desert Safari: An off-road expedition to explore the desert landscape of Kuwait in a 4x4 vehicle.

Week-Long Itinerary for Kuwait

Here is a suggested week-long itinerary that incorporates the most popular attractions and activities in Kuwait:

Day Activity
1 Visit the Kuwait National Museum and explore the cultural heritage of the country.
2 Go on a Dhow cruise and enjoy the breathtaking views of the Persian Gulf.
3 Visit Al Shaheed Park and the Kuwait Tower, two of the most iconic landmarks of the country.
4 Take a desert safari to explore the natural wonders of Kuwait.
5 Discover the wildlife and nature of Jahra Pools Reserve.
6 Explore the ruins of ancient Mesopotamia and spend the day in Failaka Island.
7 Relax and enjoy the beautiful Al Kout Beach, one of the most popular beaches in Kuwait.

Food and Drink Scene of Kuwait

A trip to Kuwait would be incomplete without tasting its delicious local cuisine and drinks. Here are some of the most popular dishes and beverages to try:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Machboos: A traditional rice dish cooked with saffron and different types of meat or seafood;
  • Harees: A popular wheat dish that is similar to porridge and often served with lamb or chicken;
  • Gers ogaily: A sticky, puffy pastry made with dates, sugar, and cardamom.

Local Beverages

  • Tea: Kuwaitis love tea, and it’s an essential part of their daily routine.
  • Qahwa: A traditional Arabian coffee made with cardamom and saffron.

Where to Stay in Kuwait: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Kuwait offers a variety of accommodation options, including luxury hotels, budget-friendly guesthouses, and hostels. Some of the most recommended hotels include:

  • Jumeirah Messilah Beach Hotel and Spa: A luxurious beachfront resort that boasts excellent facilities, stunning views, and a private beach.
  • The Regency Hotel Kuwait: A five-star hotel that offers excellent customer service and top-notch amenities, such as a rooftop pool and spa.
  • Gulf Rose Hotel: A budget-friendly hotel that offers comfortable rooms and a central location in Kuwait City.

What to Buy in Kuwait: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Kuwait offers a wide range of local products that make perfect souvenirs, such as:

  • Dates: Dates are a staple food in Kuwait and come in different varieties, so make sure to try and bring some back home.
  • Perfumes: Kuwait is known for producing high-end perfumes that make excellent gifts.
  • Gold: Gold is a popular product in Kuwait, and you can find a diverse range of designs and styles in the city’s many gold markets.

Local Culture and Customs of Kuwait

It’s essential to understand the cultural customs and practices of Kuwait before visiting. Some of the most essential customs include:

  • Dress code: Kuwait is a Muslim country, and visitors are expected to dress modestly in public places.
  • Greetings: Kuwaitis are known for their hospitality, and it’s customary to greet people with a smile and a handshake.
  • Religious Respect: Visitors should respect local religious customs and pray before entering a mosque.

Best Time to Visit Kuwait

The best time to visit Kuwait is during the winter months from October to February when the weather is mild and pleasant for outdoor activities. Summers in Kuwait are incredibly hot and humid, and it’s best to avoid visiting during this time of the year.

Do’s and Don’ts of Kuwait

Do:

  • Dress appropriately for public places.
  • Learn some basic Arabic phrases and greetings.
  • Show respect for local customs and traditions.

Don’t:

  • Consume alcohol in public places.
  • Show public displays of affection.
  • Eat, drink, or smoke in public places during Ramadan.

Safety and Health

Safety in Kuwait

Kuwait is a relatively safe country to visit, with little to no serious incidents of crime targeting tourists. However, visitors should stay vigilant and avoid isolated areas at night.

Health in Kuwait

Kuwait has an excellent healthcare system, and visitors can access quality healthcare services throughout the country. It’s always recommended to have travel health insurance before traveling, and visitors should carry a copy of their passport and medical records with them.

Getting to Kuwait: Transportation Options and Logistics

Kuwait has an international airport, Kuwait International Airport, that serves numerous airlines from different parts of the world. Visitors can also enter Kuwait by land via Saudi Arabia or Iraq. Several bus stations in Kuwait connect the city and other towns in the country.

How to Get Around in Kuwait

Kuwait offers many transportation options for tourists, including taxis, buses and rentable cars. Taxis and buses are widely available and affordable, while renting a car is ideal for those who want to explore the country independently.

Where to Go Next from Kuwait: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Kuwait is truly an ideal base for exploring the greater Middle East region. Here are some of the most popular destinations near Kuwait:

  • Bahrain: A small island country located east of Kuwait that offers cultural attractions and excellent shopping opportunities.
  • Qatar: A neighboring country known for its modern architecture, luxury shopping, and cultural events.
  • Dubai: A popular tourist destination known for its skyscraper buildings, desert safaris, and vibrant nightlife.

Conclusion

Kuwait might be small, but it offers a wealth of attractions, activities and experiences that make it a must-visit destination for any traveler. Whether you’re interested in exploring the country’s history and culture or seeking outdoor adventures, you’re sure to find something that suits your preferences in this fascinating country. With this comprehensive travel guide, you have all the information you need to plan your trip to Kuwait and make unforgettable memories.
