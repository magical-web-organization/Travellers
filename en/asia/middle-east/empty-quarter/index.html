Introduction to Empty Quarter

Empty Quarter, also known as Rub’ al Khali, is the largest sand desert in the world, covering an area of approximately 250,000 square miles. The desert is located in the south of the Arabian Peninsula, spanning several countries including Saudi Arabia, Oman, the United Arab Emirates, and Yemen. Empty Quarter is known for its scenic landscapes, breathtaking sunsets, and cultural importance. It is a must-visit destination for adventure seekers, nature enthusiasts, and history buffs.

List of Places to Visit in Empty Quarter

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Al Ula: A historical site in Saudi Arabia, offering visitors the opportunity to explore ancient rock art from the Nabataean period, tombs, and other historical monuments.
  • Qasr Al Sarab: A luxury resort that provides visitors with the opportunity to experience traditional Arabian hospitality and explore the nearby Liwa Desert.
  • Ubar: An ancient lost city in Oman, uncovering secrets about the region’s past.
  • Falaj Al-Mudhail: An irrigation system in Oman that is part of the UNESCO World Heritage Sites, reflecting the ingenious water management system of the ancient Arabians.

Natural Attractions of Empty Quarter: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Rub’ al Khali desert: The vast desert is famous for its stunning sand dunes, endless vistas, and breathtaking sunsets.
  • Jebel Hafit: A mountain range in the United Arab Emirates with various trekking and hiking trails that offer scenic views of the landscape.
  • Wadi Rum: A valley located in Jordan that is also popular for hiking and off-road adventures.
  • Dhofar Coast: The southern region of Oman boasts stunning beaches, sparkling waters, and scenic coastlines.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Empty Quarter

  • Liwa Oasis: Located in the United Arab Emirates, offering visitors the opportunity to explore the desert, dunes, and nearby oases.
  • Jabal Bani Hammad Trekking Route: A trail in Oman for hiking enthusiasts, providing them with the chance to explore the region’s natural beauty.
  • Wadi Dawqah: This valley in Oman is a popular destination for trekking where you can explore local flora and fauna and enjoy the stunning mountain views.

Best Things to Do in Empty Quarter: Activities and Tours

  • Camel Trekking: A traditional way to explore the desert and enjoy stunning views of Empty Quarter.
  • Quad Biking: An exciting way to explore the desert and dunes of Empty Quarter.
  • Hot Air Balloon Rides: Offering visitors an unforgettable experience of observing the desert and its surroundings from high above.
  • Stargazing: The vast landscape of Empty Quarter offers an unobstructed view of the stars, making it an ideal destination for stargazing enthusiasts.

Week-Long Itinerary for Empty Quarter

Day Activity
Day 1 Arrive in Liwa oasis, United Arab Emirates

Day 5 Travel to Wadi Rum, Jordan, and enjoy an off-road adventure in the desert
Day 6 Visit Al Ula, Saudi Arabia, to explore the ancient monuments and rock art

Food and Drink Scene of Empty Quarter

Must-Try Dishes

  • Mandi: A traditional Yemeni dish consisting of meat and rice cooked underground, making it succulent and flavorful.
  • Shuwa: A slow-roasted lamb dish, which is a traditional dish served on special occasions in Oman.
  • Khobz: A type of bread, which is a staple food item in most Middle Eastern countries.
  • Mahalabiya: A milk pudding, popular in Oman, that is usually flavored with dates or cardamom.

Local Beverages

  • Karak Chai: A popular spicy tea that is a staple beverage in most countries of the Middle East.
  • Arabic Coffee: A traditional coffee served in a small cup, with a strong taste and a hint of cardamom.
  • Laban: A refreshing drink made from yogurt and a hint of salt, commonly consumed in Oman and Saudi Arabia.

Where to Stay in Empty Quarter: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Qasr Al Sarab Desert Resort by Anantara: A luxury resort in the United Arab Emirates, which is perfect for those looking for 5-star amenities and a taste of traditional Arabian hospitality.
  • The View: A boutique hotel in Oman, which offers stunning panoramic views of the Hajar Mountains and a chance to experience Omani culture.
  • Sunset Camp: A luxury Bedouin camp in Jordan that offers a unique experience of experiencing Bedouin culture and stunning stargazing views.

What to Buy in Empty Quarter: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Incense: Incense is an aromatic substance that has been used for thousands of years in the Arabian Peninsula. You can buy local incense from local markets in Oman and Saudi Arabia.
  • Dates: Dates are a staple food item in most Middle Eastern countries and have been cultivated in this region for centuries. You can buy fresh or dried dates from local markets in Saudi Arabia, Oman, and the United Arab Emirates.
  • Omani Halwa: A dessert made from sugar, saffron, and rosewater, Omani Halwa is a favorite sweet dish in Oman and can be bought from local markets and sweet shops throughout the country.

Local Culture and Customs of Empty Quarter

  • Clothing: It is advisable to dress conservatively and modestly, especially when visiting cultural or religious sites. Women should cover their shoulders and knees, and men should avoid wearing shorts.
  • Ramadan: Ramadan is a holy month in the Muslim calendar, and it is essential to respect the customs during this time. Visitors should avoid eating or drinking in public during daylight hours.
  • Hospitality: Arabian culture values hospitality, and it is not uncommon for visitors to be offered tea or coffee in local homes or businesses.

Best Time to Visit Empty Quarter

The best time to visit Empty Quarter is from October to May, when the temperatures are mild and pleasant for outdoor activities. The summer months from June to September are swelteringly hot, and the temperatures can rise to over 50°C.

Do’s and Don’ts of Empty Quarter

Do:

  • Dress appropriately.
  • Research local customs and traditions before visiting.
  • Experience traditional Arabian hospitality by accepting tea or coffee when offered.

Don’t:

  • Consume alcohol in public.
  • Display affection in public.
  • Photograph people without their consent.

Safety and Health

Safety in Empty Quarter

Empty Quarter is generally a safe destination for tourists, but there are a few things to be aware of. Petty theft can occur in markets and public transportation areas, so it is advisable to keep a close eye on your belongings. Visitors should also respect the conservative dress code in public and avoid attracting unwanted attention.

Health in Empty Quarter

Visitors to Empty Quarter should be up to date with their routine vaccinations and consider vaccinations for hepatitis A and B, typhoid, and rabies depending on their travel plans. Visitors should also take precautions against the heat and dehydration by drinking plenty of water and carrying a sufficient amount of sunscreen.

Getting to Empty Quarter: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Empty Quarter

The nearest airports to Empty Quarter are:

  • Riyadh International Airport in Saudi Arabia
  • Muscat International Airport in Oman
  • Dubai International Airport in the United Arab Emirates

Bus Stations Near Empty Quarter

There are several bus stations in each country that connects to Empty Quarter. Some of the major ones are:

  • Abu Dhabi Central Bus Station in the United Arab Emirates
  • Al-Mitla Pass Bus Station in Saudi Arabia
  • Muscat Bus Station in Oman

Other Options

Private cars or taxis are also available, but it is advisable to hire a local guide or driver to ensure a safe and comfortable journey.

How to Get Around in Empty Quarter

The best way to explore the vast desert of Empty Quarter is by hiring a 4x4 vehicle or joining a guided tour. Hiking and biking tours are also available for those interested in exploring the landscape on foot or on two wheels.

Where to Go Next from Empty Quarter: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Petra, Jordan: A historical site and UNESCO World Heritage Site famous for its rock-carved architecture.
  • Dubai, United Arab Emirates: A bustling metropolis that is famous for its skyscrapers, beaches, and shopping.
  • Muscat, Oman: The capital city of Oman, known for its stunning architecture, beaches, and cultural sites.

Conclusion

Empty Quarter is a unique and fascinating destination that offers visitors an opportunity to explore the natural beauty, traditional culture, and historical sites of the Arabian Peninsula. Adventure seekers, nature enthusiasts, and history buffs alike will find plenty of exciting activities and experiences in this vast desert region.
