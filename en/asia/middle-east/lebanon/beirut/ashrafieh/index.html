Introduction to Ashrafieh

Welcome to Ashrafieh, Beirut’s most cosmopolitan and lively neighborhood. Located in the heart of the city, Ashrafieh offers a fascinating mix of history, culture, and modernity. This vibrant district is home to some of the best restaurants, shops, museums, and architectural wonders in Lebanon.

Here, on this travel guide, we will provide you with all the information you need to plan your perfect trip to Ashrafieh. From the best things to see, do, and eat, to where to stay and how to get around, we got you covered.

List of Places to Visit in Ashrafieh

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Sursock Museum: A historic mansion turned art museum, showcasing contemporary and modern art from Lebanon and the wider region.
  • National Museum of Beirut: A must-visit destination for history lovers. Featuring a vast collection of ancient artifacts, including Phoenician, Roman, Byzantine, and Ottoman art and archaeology.
  • MIM Mineral Museum: Explore the mineral world in this unique museum, showcasing over 2000 minerals and providing information on their geological origins.

Natural Attractions of Ashrafieh: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Horsh Beirut: A majestic urban park located in the heart of Ashrafieh. Perfect for picnics, jogging, and enjoying nature in the middle of the city.
  • Sanayeh Garden: Another beautiful public green space located near Ashrafieh. Sanayeh features an array of flowers, fountains, and a large pond.
  • Verdan Park: A pristine multi-use park, featuring soccer fields, basketball courts, and jogging tracks.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Ashrafieh

  • Beirut Waterfront Trail: A 4.5 km seaside walk or bike ride, stretching from the Beirut city center to the Corniche. Enjoy beautiful views of the Mediterranean sea, Beirut’s landmarks, and bustling seaside neighborhoods.
  • Beirut River Trail: A 5 km walk or bike ride that goes along the Beirut River. Trailheads are located in Horsh Beirut forest and Beirut Hippodrome.
  • Beirut Pine Forest Trail: A 7 km trail that goes through pine forests and hills around Beirut.

Best Things to Do in Ashrafieh: Activities and Tours

  • Beirut Art Tour: A curated art and culture tour of the city’s best museums, galleries, and art spaces. Led by expert guides with in-depth knowledge of Beirut’s contemporary art scene.
  • Street Food Tour: Discover the vibrant and distinct flavors of Lebanon’s food scene on this street food tour. Sample Lebanon’s best street foods and get to know the vendors behind the delicious dishes.
  • Nightlife Tour: Dive into Beirut’s nightlife scene, known for its unique post-war spirit and nonstop party atmosphere.

Week-Long Itinerary for Ashrafieh

Day 1: Visit Sursock Museum and explore the surrounding Achrafieh neighborhood.

Day 2: Take a walk along Beirut Waterfront Trail and explore nearby districts such as Gemmayzeh and Mar Mikhael.

Day 3: Visit the National Museum of Beirut and have lunch at one of the many restaurants in the Hamra district.

Day 4: Go for a hike along the Beirut River Trail and end your day with a drink at one of Mar Mikhael’s rooftop bars.

Day 5: Take a day trip to Byblos, one of Lebanon’s most picturesque towns, located just a short drive from Ashrafieh.

Day 6: Explore the edgy and artsy district of Karantina, located right next to Ashrafieh.

Day 7: Take a cooking class to learn how to make traditional Lebanese dishes and shop for souvenirs at nearby boutiques.

Food and Drink Scene of Ashrafieh

Ashrafieh is a food lover’s paradise, with countless restaurants and cafes offering everything from traditional Lebanese cuisine to modern fusion dishes. Here are some must-try dishes and beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Tabbouleh: A refreshing salad made with parsley, tomatoes, mint, onions, and bulgur.
  • Kebab: Grilled meat (chicken, beef or lamb) skewered and marinated with spices.
  • Manoushe: A traditional Lebanese flatbread topped with cheese, herbs, and meat.

Local Beverages

  • Arak: A traditional Levantine alcoholic drink made from grapes and flavored with anise.
  • Lebanese Wine: Lebanon is famous for its exquisite wines. Try a bottle of Chateau Musar or Ksara to experience Lebanese wine culture.

Where to Stay in Ashrafieh: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Hotel Albergo: A luxurious boutique hotel, located in an elegant mansion, featuring a rooftop pool and stunning views of Beirut.
  • Saifi Suites: A contemporary hotel, offering spacious and modern apartments with kitchenettes and balconies.
  • Urban Central Suites: A budget-friendly option, offering comfortable rooms and a great location in the middle of Ashrafieh.

What to Buy in Ashrafieh: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Lebanese Olive Oil: Lebanon is known for its high-quality olive oil. Buy a bottle of local olive oil to use in your cooking at home.
  • Lebanese Sweets: Satisfy your sweet tooth with delicious Lebanese sweets such as baklava, maamoul, and halawa.
  • Handicrafts and Souvenirs: Shop for local handicrafts and souvenirs in the trendy boutiques of Achrafieh and surrounding areas.

Local Culture and Customs of Ashrafieh

Lebanon has a rich cultural heritage, and Ashrafieh is no exception. Visitors should respect local customs and dress modestly when visiting religious sites.

Best Time to Visit Ashrafieh

The best time to visit Ashrafieh is during the spring and fall months (March to May and September to November). The weather is mild, and the city’s outdoor space is perfect for hiking, biking, and sightseeing.

Do’s and Don’ts of Ashrafieh

Do:

  • Learn basic Arabic phrases and greetings.
  • Dress appropriately when visiting religious sites.
  • Try the local food and drinks.

Don’t:

  • Discard trash on the street.
  • Take photos of local people without their permission.
  • Show public displays of affection.

Safety and Health

Lebanon is generally a safe country, but visitors should be aware of their surroundings and take common safety measures such as avoiding walking alone at night and not carrying large amounts of cash.

Safety in Ashrafieh

Ashrafieh is a safe and well-policed neighborhood, but visitors should stay vigilant and keep their belongings close.

Health in Ashrafieh

Lebanon has a good healthcare system, but visitors should have adequate travel insurance in case of emergencies.

Getting to Ashrafieh: Transportation Options and Logistics

Ashrafieh is located in the heart of Beirut and can be easily reached by various means of transportation.

Airports Near Ashrafieh

  • Beirut-Rafic Hariri International Airport, located just 10 km from Ashrafieh.

Bus Stations Near Ashrafieh

  • Cola Bus Station: a major bus station located 5 km from Ashrafieh.
  • Charles Helou Bus Station: another bus station, located 3 km from Ashrafieh.

Other Transportation Options

  • Taxi: readily available in most parts of Beirut.
  • Metro: Beirut’s new metro system is under construction and will soon provide another means of transportation for visitors.

How to Get Around in Ashrafieh

Ashrafieh is a pedestrian-friendly neighborhood, and visitors can easily walk to most attractions. Taxis and buses are also available for longer distances.

Where to Go Next from Ashrafieh: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Jeita Grotto: A must-visit attraction, located just 20 km from Ashrafieh. Featuring beautiful limestone caves and waterfalls.
  • Byblos: A historic seaside town, located 35 km from Ashrafieh. With a beautiful old souq and many historic landmarks.
  • Harissa: A pilgrimage site located on the hills above Beirut. With a large statue of the Virgin Mary and stunning views of the city.

Conclusion

Ashrafieh is a dynamic and fascinating neighborhood, offering visitors an array of cultural, natural, and culinary experiences. From hiking and biking to exploring museums and savoring Lebanese cuisine, Ashrafieh has it all. We hope this travel guide has helped you plan your perfect trip to Ashrafieh, and we wish you a memorable stay in Lebanon.
