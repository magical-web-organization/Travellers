Introduction to El Mina

El Mina, also known as Tripoli Port, is a beautiful coastal city located in the North Governorate of Lebanon. El Mina has a long history of maritime trade, and it is said that the Phoenicians named it “Sidon of the North.” Today, El Mina is a thriving city with a rich cultural heritage and a vibrant food and drink scene.

List of Places to Visit in El Mina

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

El Mina is a city with a rich history, and there are several museums and historical places worth visiting. The Tripoli Crusader Castle is a must-visit, as it is one of the most well-preserved and impressive castles in Lebanon, dating back to the 12th century. The Saint-Gilles Citadel is another historical attraction in El Mina, built in the early 13th century by the Crusaders. The El Mina Municipal Museum is a small museum showcasing the city’s history and culture, while the Rachid Karameh Exhibition Center is a contemporary art venue located in the city center.

Natural Attractions of El Mina: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

El Mina is also known for its natural beauty and outdoor adventure activities. The Palm Islands Nature Reserve is a beautiful protected area in the Mediterranean Sea, home to various endangered species of birds and marine life. The Reserve is accessible by boat from El Mina port. The Jabal Moussa Biosphere Reserve is a nearby mountainous area with hiking trails and beautiful scenic views of the countryside. For those seeking a beach vacation, El Mina has several beautiful beaches like Ramlet el Baida, Al Mina Aquarium Beach, and Menjez.

Hiking/Biking Routes of El Mina

Jabal Moussa Biosphere Reserve is a perfect El Mina destination for hiking enthusiasts. There are several hiking trails around the Reserve, varying in difficulty levels from easy to challenging. There are also biking trails that offer scenic views of the countryside for mountain biking enthusiasts.

Best Things to Do in El Mina: Activities and Tours

El Mina has several activities and tours to offer. Boat tours are perfect for exploring the city from a new perspective, allowing you to take in the beautiful coastline and architecture. El Mina also has several old markets, like Tabbaneh and Haraj Al Sagha, where you can find a variety of goods and souvenirs. Traditional Hammams (Turkish Baths) that allow visitors to experience the centuries-old Turkish bathing ritual are highly recommended.

Week-Long Itinerary for El Mina

Day 1: Explore El Mina city center, including the Municipal Museum and Saint-Gilles Citadel.

Day 2: Visit the Jabal Moussa Biosphere Reserve and hike one of the trails.

Day 3: Take a boat tour to the Palm Island Nature Reserve.

Day 4: Visit the Tripoli Crusader Castle and Haraj Al Sagha market.

Day 5: Enjoy a day at one of El Mina’s beautiful beaches, like Ramlet el Baida.

Day 6: Visit the El Mina Old Souk, experience Turkish baths in Hammams, and dine at one of the city’s seafood restaurants.

Day 7: Take a day trip to the nearby Cedars of God and the city of Byblos.

Food and Drink Scene of El Mina

Must-Try Dishes

El Mina boasts a unique cuisine influenced by its history and location. As a coastal city, seafood is a specialty, and the city has several seafood spots like Samir & Gilgamesh, Al Sayad, and Al Karaz, where you can try traditional dishes like Fish Sayadieh, Raw Kibbeh, and Grilled Calamari. Manakish is a Lebanese traditional food, a round thin dough topped with Zaatar or cheese or mixed, is a must-try.

Local Beverages

There is no shortage of beverages to try in El Mina. Ayran is a famous local drink made from yogurt, salt, and water, while traditional Arak is Lebanon’s national drink, made from grape spirits, aniseed, and water.

Where to Stay in El Mina: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

El Mina has several accommodation options to suit all budgets. The luxurious quality-built hotels in El Mina include The Crystal Blue Hotel, Al Ahlam Hotel Apartments, and Dolce Vita Hotel. Other lodging options include hostels, guesthouses, and rental apartments, with affordable options being the Saint Family Guesthouse and Hostel, as well as several cozy guesthouses that offer a trendier, authentic feel.

What to Buy in El Mina: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

El Mina has several markets and stores where visitors can buy souvenirs and local products that are unique to the city. Olive oil and local dried herbs, Arak and locally grown fruits, like cherries and apples, can be found in El Mina’s markets.

Local Culture and Customs of El Mina

El Mina is a city with a rich cultural heritage and history, and visitors should respect the local culture and customs. Dress modestly while visiting religious sites and avoid public displays of affection.

Best Time to Visit El Mina

The best time to visit El Mina is from May to October when the weather is warm, and the beaches are open. Winters can be cold, rainy, and sometimes snowy.

Do’s and Don’ts of El Mina

Do:

  • Respect local customs and dress modestly while visiting religious sites.
  • Try to learn basic Lebanese Arabic before visiting El Mina.

Don’t:

  • Have public displays of affection.
  • Take photos of individuals and landmarks without permission.

Safety and Health

Safety in El Mina

El Mina is relatively safe, but visitors should still exercise caution, especially when traveling alone.

Health in El Mina

There are various hospitals and clinics in the city in case of medical emergencies, and visitors are advised to have travel insurance that covers medical emergencies.

Getting to El Mina: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near El Mina

The nearest airport to El Mina is Beirut–Rafic Hariri International Airport, located around 86 kilometers from the city.

Bus Stations Near El Mina

There are several bus stations in Beirut that serve El Mina, and visitors can take a bus from the Charles Helou Bus Station or the Cola Intersection.

Other Options

Taxis are readily available and can be hired to reach El Mina from Beirut or other neighboring cities.

How to Get Around in El Mina

Walking is the best way to get around El Mina’s city center, while taxis can help you get to the outskirts and nearby attractions.

Where to Go Next from El Mina: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

El Mina offers several nearby destinations and excursions, such as the Cedars of God, a UNESCO World Heritage site for its precious cedar forests, and the city of Byblos, another Phoenician ancient port that houses historical attractions and natural beauty.

Conclusion

El Mina offers a perfect blend of history, nature and culture. From boasting picturesque scenery to offering rugged natural beauty and stunning beaches, El Mina will leave you spoiled for choice. You will find no shortage of things to see and do in the Lebanese city, making it a must-visit destination for all kinds of travelers.
