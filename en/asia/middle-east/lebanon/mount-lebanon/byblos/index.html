Introduction to Byblos

Byblos is a historic yet vibrant city located in Mount Lebanon, Lebanon. Being one of the world’s oldest continuously inhabited cities, Byblos boasts rich cultural heritage, charming cobblestone streets, and stunning Mediterranean views.

List of Places to Visit in Byblos

Here are the top places to visit in Byblos:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Byblos Castle: Built by the Crusaders in the 12th century, the castle is a prominent landmark and a great place to explore the city’s history through artifacts and exhibitions.
  • Byblos Archeological Site: A UNESCO World Heritage site next to the harbor, where historic ruins from different eras can be viewed.
  • Wax Museum: A unique museum where visitors can browse lifelike wax figures of prominent Lebanese figures like the famous writer Gebran Khalil Gebran and the singer Fairouz.

Natural Attractions of Byblos: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Jeita Grotto: An enchanting system of caves and rock formations located 18km from Byblos. Visitors can take a boat ride through the Lower Cave and explore the Upper Cave with walking paths.
  • Adonis Valley: A picturesque valley located in the mountains between Byblos and Beirut. The valley is famous for its spectacular views, flora, and fauna, and outdoor activities like hiking, camping, and exploring.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Byblos

For hiking and biking enthusiasts, Byblos is the perfect destination with numerous trails and routes in the surrounding area. The Mount Lebanon massif has breathtaking landscapes and nature reserves, including Al Shouf Cedar Nature Reserve, Horsh Ehden Nature Reserve, and Tannourine Cedars Reserve.

Best Things to Do in Byblos: Activities and Tours

Byblos is the perfect destination for relaxation, exploration, and entertainment. Here are some of the best things to do in Byblos:

Byblos Boat Trip

A delightful boat trip from the harbor of Byblos takes visitors on a relaxing and guided cruise along the coastline, and it’s the ideal way to get some great views of Byblos.

Shopping Souks and Old Market

Byblos’ old market is one of the best places to stroll around, admire the traditional buildings, and shop for local handicrafts, fresh produce, and souvenirs.

Week-Long Itinerary for Byblos

For an ideal week-long trip to Byblos, here’s the recommended itinerary:

Day Activity
1 Visit the castle and the Wax Museum
2 Explore the Jeita Grotto and Adonis Valley

Food and Drink Scene of Byblos

The cuisine of Byblos is world-renowned with its rich flavors and outstanding dishes. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Fish tajine: a traditional dish of eastern Mediterranean cuisine, Tajine consists of fish roasted with vegetables and aromatic herbs.
  • Fattoush: a salad made of fresh vegetables, dried mint, and toasted bread.
  • Zaatar Manouche: Manouche is the nation’s staple bread, and when sprinkled with Zaatar, it is a delicious snack anytime.

Local Beverages

  • Arak: A traditional Lebanese liquor, made from distilled grapes, is usually mixed with water and ice and served as a refreshing aperitif with meals.
  • Wine: Lebanon has a long tradition of winemaking and is home to various vineyards that offer wine tasting experiences.

Where to Stay in Byblos: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Byblos offers numerous accommodation options ranging from luxury hotels to affordable guesthouses. Here are some top choices:

  • Byblos Sur Mer: A luxury hotel located right by the coastline with 30 rooms and amazing Mediterranean views.
  • Aleph Boutique Hotel: A mid-range hotel with a relaxing pool area and a rooftop terrace with stunning views of Byblos.
  • Ahiram Guest house: A charming guesthouse in the heart of Byblos’ Old Town with quaint rooms and breakfast included.

What to Buy in Byblos: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Byblos offers visitors an excellent opportunity to shop for local handicrafts, arts, and souvenirs. Here are some things to consider bringing back home:

  • Olive oil soaps: From traditional artisan brands to luxurious CBD-infused varieties, you can find an incredible range of soaps in Byblos.
  • Brass handicrafts: Handcrafted items from brass, such as candle holders, vases, and trays that are unique to Byblos.
  • Lebanese wines: Bring a bottle of wine from the famous Lebanese wine regions to share with friends back home.

Local Culture and Customs of Byblos

Byblos is a conservative, family-oriented society with a long history and unique culture. Here are some local customs and etiquettes to consider:

  • Dress modestly, especially when visiting religious sites.
  • Respect elders and authority figures.
  • Avoid public displays of affection.

Best Time to Visit Byblos

The climate in Byblos is mild in general, with warm summers and mild, rainy winters. The best time to visit Byblos is during the sunny seasons of spring and summer, from April to September, when visitors can enjoy outdoor activities and events.

Do’s and Don’ts of Byblos

Here are some essential do’s and don’ts to observe in Byblos:

Do:

  • Try to learn a few Arabic and French words to get around better.
  • Try to experience the traditional Lebanese lifestyle to make the most of your trip.
  • Be polite and courteous to the locals.

Don’t:

  • Don’t use your left hand to eat or pass things to people.
  • Avoid consuming skimpy clothing or beachwear while visiting sacred destinations.
  • Don’t overstep boundaries with locals in terms of religion or politics.

Safety and Health

Safety in Byblos

Byblos is a safe and peaceful city with low crime rates. Visitors should exercise caution in a few areas like the souks, especially when carrying large sums of cash. Tourists should also avoid being out on the roads during rush hours.

Health in Byblos

Byblos’s medical services significantly adhere to the international norms, and it has several hospitals and clinics. However, you should take standard safety precautions like drinking bottled water to avoid tap water and frequent hand washing.

Getting to Byblos: Transportation Options and Logistics

Byblos is accessible via various transportation methods, including:

Airports Near Byblos

  • Beirut Rafic Hariri International Airport: Located just 40 km from the city center of Byblos, the primary international airport in Lebanon.

Bus Stations Near Byblos

  • Charles Halawi Bus Station: Located 37 km from Byblos, the bus station is easily accessible by bus or cab.

Other Options

Cabs and Uber are also readily available to get to Byblos from nearby locations.

How to Get Around in Byblos

Byblos is a small town, and the best way to explore it is on foot. Taxis are also readily available for visitors to explore areas beyond the town center, and there are car rental options for those looking for greater flexibility in their travel itinerary.

Where to Go Next from Byblos: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Beirut

Beirut, the capital of Lebanon, is only a 40-minute drive away from Byblos. Widely regarded as the most vibrant city in the Middle East, it is famous for its nightlife, shopping, and unique cultural experiences.

Conclusion

Byblos’s mix of natural beauty, historic sites, and modern flair makes it an incredible destination for every kind of traveler. Whether you’re interested in exploring the history and culture, relaxing at the beach or shopping for local crafts, Byblos has something for everyone.
