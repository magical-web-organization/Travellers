Introduction to Deir el Qamar

Deir el Qamar is a charming village nestled in the heart of Mount Lebanon and filled with history, culture, and picturesque scenery. Its name translates to “Monastery of the Moon”, which it got from the 16th-century structures that once dominated the town center. Today, Deir el Qamar is a popular tourist destination and is listed as a UNESCO World Heritage Site for its outstanding architecture and cultural significance.

List of Places to Visit in Deir el Qamar

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Palace of Emir Fakhr-al-Din al-Maani: This 17th-century palace was the home of Emir Fakhr-al-Din al-Maani, a ruler of Lebanon during the Ottoman Empire. The palace has been restored and turned into a museum where visitors can learn about the history of the town and its importance in the region.
  • Deir el Qamar Church: This church is one of the largest and most beautiful in Deir el Qamar, featuring stunning frescoes, marble columns, and intricate carvings. Visitors can also climb to the top of the bell tower for panoramic views of the village.
  • Armenian Genocide Monument: Located on the main square of Deir el Qamar, this monument honors the Armenian victims of the 1915 genocide.
  • Monument of Emir Fakhr-al-Din: This statue pays tribute to the Emir who played a significant role in the development and protection of Lebanon during his reign.

Natural Attractions of Deir el Qamar: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Beiteddine Palace: Located just a few miles from Deir el Qamar, Beiteddine Palace is a stunning example of Lebanese architecture and features beautifully manicured gardens.
  • Chouf Cedar Nature Reserve: A short drive away from Deir el Qamar, this reserve is home to ancient cedar trees and a diverse array of flora and fauna.
  • Mount Lebanon: This mountain range offers stunning scenic views, hiking trails, and outdoor sports such as mountain biking.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Deir el Qamar

Deir el Qamar offers excellent opportunities for hiking and mountain biking, taking visitors through stunning landscapes and natural settings. Some of the best routes to take include:

  • Belou3 Bal3a Trail: This trail takes visitors through lush forests and rocky mountains, offering stunning views of the surrounding scenery.
  • Sahalet el Baas Trail: This trail offers a unique experience for hikers and mountain bikers, taking visitors through the traditional Lebanese village of Sahalet el Baas and past olive groves and vineyards.
  • Masa7at el Mir Trail: This hike is perfect for those looking for a more challenging climb, with steep inclines and rocky terrain. The reward is an impressive panoramic view of Deir el Qamar and the surrounding mountains.

Best Things to Do in Deir el Qamar: Activities and Tours

  • Cooking Classes and Food Tours: Learn about Lebanese cuisine by taking a cooking class or going on a food tour through the winding streets of Deir el Qamar.
  • Wine Tasting: Lebanon is renowned for its wine production, and Deir el Qamar offers excellent opportunities to taste the wines of the region.
  • Shopping: Check out the local markets and shops to buy handmade crafts, souvenirs, and traditional Lebanese products.

Week-Long Itinerary for Deir el Qamar

Day 1: Arrival and check-in at your hotel, followed by a tour of Deir el Qamar’s Old Town and a visit to the Palace of Emir Fakhr-al-Din al-Maani.

Day 2: Hiking or biking along the Belou3 Bal3a Trail, followed by a visit to the Sahalet el Baas Village.

Day 3: Taking a cooking class to learn about Lebanese cuisine or going on a food tour through the winding streets of Deir el Qamar.

Day 4: A day trip to the Beiteddine Palace and its beautifully manicured gardens.

Day 5: Wine tasting at a local vineyard or a visit to Chouf Cedar Nature Reserve.

Day 6: Shopping in local markets and checking out the traditional Lebanese crafts and souvenirs.

Day 7: Departure from Deir el Qamar.

Food and Drink Scene of Deir el Qamar

Must-Try Dishes

  • Man’oushe: A classic Lebanese pastry made with thin dough, olive oil, and toppings such as za’atar, cheese, or minced meat.
  • Kibbeh: A dish made of minced meat and crushed wheat, usually served raw or cooked with various spices.
  • Tabbouleh: A salad made of finely chopped parsley, onions, tomatoes, and bulgur, dressed with lemon juice and olive oil.

Local Beverages

  • Arak: A traditional Lebanese alcoholic drink made from grapes and anise.
  • Ayran: A refreshing non-alcoholic beverage made of yogurt, water, and salt.

Where to Stay in Deir el Qamar: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Le Chateau Deau Hotel: A luxurious hotel located in the heart of the city, offering stunning views of the mountains and a heated pool.
  • Beit Trad Guesthouse: A traditional Lebanese house converted into a guesthouse, offering guests an authentic experience of Lebanese culture and hospitality.
  • La Maison De La Foret: A boutique hotel located in the Chouf Cedar Nature Reserve, offering accommodations in a serene and natural setting.

What to Buy in Deir el Qamar: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Olive Oil: Lebanon is famous for its excellent quality olive oil, and Deir el Qamar is home to several olive groves and mills.
  • Traditional Handicrafts: Check out the local markets for traditional Lebanese crafts such as pottery, woodwork, and textiles.

Local Culture and Customs of Deir el Qamar

Lebanon is a country with rich culture and customs, and Deir el Qamar is no different. Visitors should respect local traditions and customs, such as dressing modestly when visiting religious sites and not drinking alcohol in public places during Ramadan.

Best Time to Visit Deir el Qamar

The best time to visit Deir el Qamar is during the spring and fall months when the weather is mild and perfect for outdoor activities. Summer can be hot and crowded, while winter months may bring snow and freezing temperatures.

Do’s and Don’ts of Deir el Qamar

Do:

  • Respect local cultures and customs.
  • Dress modestly when visiting religious sites.
  • Try out the local cuisine and traditional products.

Don’t:

  • Drink alcohol in public places during Ramadan.
  • Disrespect local traditions and customs.
  • Take photographs of locals without their permission.

Safety and Health

Safety in Deir el Qamar

Deir el Qamar is a relatively safe destination for tourists. However, travelers should always take precautions to keep their belongings safe and avoid unlit areas at night.

Health in Deir el Qamar

Lebanon has a relatively high level of healthcare services, and Deir el Qamar has a few private clinics and pharmacies. Visitors should have travel insurance that covers any potential medical emergencies.

Getting to Deir el Qamar: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Deir el Qamar

  • Beirut Rafic Hariri International Airport: Located approximately 34 kilometers from Deir el Qamar, this is the main airport for international flights to Lebanon.

Bus Stations Near Deir el Qamar

  • Cola Junction Bus Station: Located in Beirut, this is the main bus station for buses heading to Deir el Qamar.

Other Options

  • Taxis are readily available in Beirut and other major cities, and many drivers will take passengers directly to Deir el Qamar.

How to Get Around in Deir el Qamar

The best way to get around Deir el Qamar and explore the surrounding attractions is by hiring a car or taxi. Buses are also available but may not be reliable for schedules or destinations.

Where to Go Next from Deir el Qamar: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Beirut: Lebanon’s vibrant capital city is just a short drive away from Deir el Qamar, offering excellent shopping, dining, and cultural experiences.
  • Jeita Grotto: A stunning limestone cave located approximately 40 kilometers north of Beirut, featuring impressive stalactites and stalagmites.
  • Byblos: One of the oldest continuously inhabited cities in the world and a UNESCO World Heritage Site, Byblos is located approximately 30 kilometers north of Beirut.

Conclusion

Deir el Qamar is a captivating destination for anyone looking to experience Lebanese culture, history, and nature. With plenty of outdoor adventures, cultural and natural attractions, and opportunities for food and drink, Deir el Qamar is a must-visit destination for your next trip to Lebanon.
