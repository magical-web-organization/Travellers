A Comprehensive Travel Guide to Jezzine, Lebanon

Jezzine is a small town located in south Lebanon, amidst the stunning beauty of the Lebanon Mountains. It is known for its natural wonders, cultural richness, outdoor adventures, and serene ambiance. Whether you are a history buff, an outdoor enthusiast, or a food lover, Jezzine has something for everyone. In this comprehensive travel guide, we will take you on a virtual tour of Jezzine and help you plan your visit to this wonderful town.

Introduction to Jezzine

Jezzine is a town situated in the Governorate of South Lebanon, about 22 km southeast of Sidon. It is located at an altitude of 950 meters above sea level, surrounded by the Lebanon Mountains. The population of the town is around 8,000, and the majority of the inhabitants belong to the Druze community.

Jezzine is known for its olive groves, apricots, cherries, and pine trees. The town has a long history dating back to the Phoenician era, and it witnessed several invasions throughout history, including the Crusaders, Ottomans, and French. This has left a cultural impact on Jezzine, with historical monuments and museums scattered all over the town.

List of Places to Visit in Jezzine

Jezzine is a town with an abundance of natural wonders and cultural landmarks. Here is a list of places you should not miss during your visit:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Jezzine Municipal Museum: An impressive museum showcasing local traditions, culture, and history of Jezzine.
  • Our Lady of the Mountains Shrine: A beautiful church built on top of a cliff. It offers a magnificent view of Jezzine and the surrounding mountains.
  • Beit el Din Palace: A magnificent palace built by Emir Bechir II in the 19th century. It is now open to the public as a museum.

Natural Attractions of Jezzine: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Jezzine Waterfall: A breathtaking waterfall cascading down from a height of 40 meters. It is one of the most popular tourist attractions in Jezzine.
  • Bkassine Pine Forest: A vast pine forest covering an area of 400 hectares. It is a perfect spot for a picnic or a leisurely walk.
  • Moukhtara Cedars Reserve: A nature reserve famous for its centuries-old cedar trees. It offers hiking and wildlife watching opportunities.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Jezzine

Jezzine is a paradise for hikers and bikers, with several well-marked trails and routes. Here are some of the best routes for outdoor enthusiasts:

  • Jezzine to Niha Trail: This trail takes you on a scenic journey through olive groves and pine forests, passing by ancient ruins and historical landmarks.
  • Jezzine to Bkassine Trail: A challenging hike that leads you through the Bkassine Pine Forest. You will experience breathtaking views of the mountains and valleys.
  • Moukhtara to Barouk Trail: This trail takes you through the cedar forests of the Moukhtara Cedars Reserve to the Barouk Natural Reserve.

Best Things to Do in Jezzine: Activities and Tours

Apart from hiking and sightseeing, Jezzine offers several exciting activities and tours. Here are some of the best things to do in Jezzine:

  • Jezzine Eco-Tour: An eco-tour of Jezzine that takes you through the town’s natural wonders, cultural landmarks, and traditional villages.
  • Jezzine Paragliding: A thrilling paragliding experience that lets you soar over the Lebanon Mountains and enjoy the breathtaking views from above.
  • Jezzine Olive Picking: A unique experience that lets you pick olives from the town’s olive groves and learn about the traditional process of olive oil production.

Week-Long Itinerary for Jezzine

Jezzine is a small town, but it has enough attractions to keep you busy for a week. Here is a week-long itinerary that covers the best of Jezzine:

Food and Drink Scene of Jezzine

Jezzine’s food and drink scene is a combination of Lebanese and Druze cuisine. The town is known for its fresh produce, including olives, apricots, cherries, and herbs. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages in Jezzine:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Makdous: A popular appetizer made with small eggplant stuffed with walnuts, garlic, and red pepper.
  • Moussaka: A traditional Lebanese dish made with eggplant, minced meat, and tomato sauce.
  • Kubbeh Soup: A hearty soup made with bulgur wheat, ground meat, and spices.

Local Beverages

  • Arak: A traditional Lebanese spirit made from distilled grapes and flavored with aniseed. It is usually served with mezze.
  • Ayran: A refreshing drink made from yogurt, water, and salt. It is a perfect cooling drink on a hot day.
  • Jallab: A sweet drink made from grape molasses, rose water, and pine nuts. It is usually served during Ramadan.

Where to Stay in Jezzine: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Jezzine offers several accommodation options, from budget-friendly hostels to luxurious hotels. Here are some of the best places to stay in Jezzine:

  • Al-Qasmieh Hotel: A three-star hotel located in the heart of Jezzine. It offers comfortable rooms with mountain views.
  • Jezzine Guest House: A budget-friendly guest house located near Jezzine’s attractions. It offers clean rooms and a friendly ambiance.
  • La Maison des Sources: A luxurious guesthouse located in the Bkassine Pine Forest. It offers spacious rooms and a serene atmosphere.

What to Buy in Jezzine: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Jezzine is known for its handicrafts, including pottery, embroidery, and weaving. You can also find several local products to bring back home. Here are some of the best souvenirs and local products to buy in Jezzine:

  • Olive Oil: Jezzine is known for its high-quality olive oil. You can buy it from the local markets or from the olive oil producers.
  • Pottery: Jezzine’s pottery is famous for its intricate designs and vibrant colors. You can buy it from the pottery workshops in town.
  • Herbs and Spices: Jezzine’s fresh herbs and spices are a must-try. You can buy them from the local markets or from the herb farmers.

Local Culture and Customs of Jezzine

Jezzine’s culture is a blend of Lebanese and Druze traditions. The town is known for its hospitality, respect for elders, and love for nature. The locals are friendly and welcoming to visitors, and they take pride in their heritage and traditions.

Best Time to Visit Jezzine

The best time to visit Jezzine is from March to November, during the spring and summer months. The weather is mild and pleasant, and the town’s natural wonders are at their best. However, if you want to experience the town’s winter charm, you can visit during December and January, when the town is covered in snow.

Do’s and Don’ts of Jezzine

Do:

  • Respect the local customs and traditions.
  • Dress modestly when visiting religious sites.
  • Try the local cuisine and beverages.
  • Learn some Arabic phrases to communicate with the locals.

Don’t:

  • Drink alcohol in public places, especially during Ramadan.
  • Take photos of people without their permission.
  • Smoke in public places, except in designated areas.

Safety and Health

Safety in Jezzine

Jezzine is a safe town, and the crime rate is low. However, it is always advisable to take precautions, especially when traveling alone. Avoid walking alone at night and always keep your valuables safe.

Health in Jezzine

Jezzine has a good healthcare system, and there are several hospitals and clinics in the town. However, it is always advisable to carry your own medication and insurance documents.

Getting to Jezzine: Transportation Options and Logistics

Jezzine is easily accessible by road and air. Here are some transportation options to get to Jezzine:

Airports Near Jezzine

The nearest airport to Jezzine is Rafic Hariri International Airport in Beirut, which is about 50 kilometers away. You can take a taxi or a bus from the airport to Jezzine.

Bus Stations Near Jezzine

There are several bus stations near Jezzine, including Sidon, Tyre, and Jounieh. From these stations, you can take a taxi or a bus to Jezzine.

Other Options

You can also rent a car to travel to Jezzine. However, it is advisable to take a driver who is familiar with the roads and terrain.

How to Get Around in Jezzine

Jezzine is a small town, and you can easily explore it on foot or by bicycle. You can also take a taxi or a bus to reach the attractions.

Where to Go Next from Jezzine: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Jezzine is located in the heart of south Lebanon, and there are several nearby destinations and excursions you can explore, including:

  • Sidon: A coastal city famous for its historical landmarks and seafood cuisine.
  • Tyre: An ancient Phoenician city known for its archaeological sites and sandy beaches.
  • Beirut: The capital city of Lebanon, known for its vibrant nightlife, culture, and shopping.

Conclusion

Jezzine is a town with a rich cultural heritage and natural wonders. It offers several attractions, activities, and experiences for visitors to explore. Whether you want to hike the mountains, taste the local cuisine, or learn about the town’s history and traditions, Jezzine has something for everyone. We hope this comprehensive travel guide has helped you plan your visit to Jezzine and make the most of your trip.
