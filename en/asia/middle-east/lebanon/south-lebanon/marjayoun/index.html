A Comprehensive Travel Guide to Marjayoun

Marjayoun is a stunning city located in the South of Lebanon, Middle East. This city boasts cultural and natural attractions, including museums, historical places, outdoor adventures, and scenic views. In this comprehensive travel guide, we’ll take you on a journey to Marjayoun and explore the best places to visit, where to stay, what to eat and drink, and local culture and customs.

Introduction to Marjayoun

Marjayoun is a picturesque city that sits at the foot of Mount Hermon. It was founded in the 16th century and has a rich history. Marjayoun boasts an abundance of natural and cultural attractions that make it an ideal destination for travelers who love history and outdoor adventures. This city has been a crossroads of civilizations, which in turn have left their cultural imprints forever embedded in its history.

List of Places to Visit in Marjayoun

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Marjayoun is a city rich in history and culture, and its museums and historical places are well worth visiting. If you’re interested in learning more about the city’s past, you should include these places in your itinerary:

  1. The Crusaders Castle
  2. Deir El Qamar
  3. Khiam Detention Center Museum

Natural Attractions of Marjayoun: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

If you’re an adventure enthusiast or nature lover, Marjayoun is the perfect destination for you. You can explore its natural attractions and enjoy outdoor activities that include:

  1. Mount Hermon Hiking Trail
  2. Roueiss Cave
  3. Al Shihek Wetland Reserve

Hiking/Biking Routes of Marjayoun

Hiking and biking are popular activities to enjoy in Marjayoun. The city offers a range of trails that vary in difficulty level. Some of the best routes to explore include:

  1. The Trail of Assi River
  2. Hima Karakoun Wetland Trail
  3. Wadi Al Kouafi Trail

Best Things to Do in Marjayoun: Activities and Tours

Marjayoun offers plenty of fun activities and tours for travelers to enjoy. From cultural tours to outdoor adventures, there’s something for everyone. Some of the best things to do in Marjayoun are:

  1. Wine tasting at Chateau Oumsiyat
  2. Clay Modeling with Aida Sakkab
  3. Culinary Tour of Marjayoun

Week-Long Itinerary for Marjayoun

A week-long itinerary is an excellent way to experience the best that Marjayoun has to offer. Here’s a sample one-week itinerary:

Day Activities
1 Arrival in Marjayoun
2 Visit to Deir El Qamar and Crusaders Castle
3 Hiking tour to Wadi Al Kouafi Trail

5 Clay Modeling with Aida Sakkab

7 Departure from Marjayoun

Food and Drink Scene of Marjayoun

In Marjayoun, you’ll find an abundance of mouth-watering dishes that are unique to the city, and it’s a must-try for food lovers. The local cuisine is influenced by Palestinian, Jordanian, and Syrian foods, and it’s a combination of fresh herbs, Middle Eastern spices, and local ingredients. Here are some must-try dishes:

Must-Try Dishes

  1. Shawarma
  2. Kebbeh
  3. Man’ousheh

Local Beverages

  1. Arak
  2. Tea
  3. Coffee

Where to Stay in Marjayoun: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Marjayoun has plenty of accommodation options for travelers. Whether you’re looking for a luxurious hotel or a charming guesthouse, you’ll find something that fits your budget and preferences. Some of the best places to stay include:

  1. Maisonette Ayni
  2. Bouyouti
  3. Beit Wadih Hotel and Restaurant

What to Buy in Marjayoun: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Marjayoun is known for its handmade crafts, which make excellent souvenirs. Some of the best souvenirs to bring back home from Marjayoun are:

  1. Ceramics
  2. Mosaic items
  3. Paintings

Local Culture and Customs of Marjayoun

Marjayoun is a culturally rich city, and it’s essential to be respectful of local customs and practices. The hospitality of people in Marjayoun is worth noticing. They love accommodating travelers who express an interest in their culture and customs. Here are some customs to keep in mind:

  1. Dressing modestly is a sign of respect.
  2. Tipping is customary in many places.
  3. Learning and using basic phrases in Arabic is appreciated.

Best Time to Visit Marjayoun

The best time to visit Marjayoun is from April to June and from September to November. During these months, the weather is comfortable, and there’s plenty to do and see.

Do’s and Don’ts of Marjayoun

Do:

  1. Respect local customs and culture.
  2. Plan to stay for at least a week.
  3. Try the local cuisine and wine.

Don’t:

  1. Disrespect local customs and culture.
  2. Dress inappropriately in public places
  3. Engage in public displays of affection.

Safety and Health

Safety in Marjayoun

Marjayoun is generally safe, and visitors should not have to worry about safety issues. However, visitors are advised to be cautious when travelling alone after dark and avoid unknown and unlit areas.

Health in Marjayoun

Marjayoun has adequate medical facilities, and travelers should not be worried about health concerns. Travelers are advised to bring their prescription medications as some might not be readily available in local pharmacies.

Getting to Marjayoun: Transportation Options and Logistics

Marjayoun is accessible by different modes of transport, and the city is about 90 km south of Beirut.

Airports Near Marjayoun

  1. Beirut-Rafic Hariri International Airport is the closest airport to Marjayoun, located around 85km from the city.

Bus Stations Near Marjayoun

  1. Travelers can use public buses operated by the Lebanese Commuting Company. The buses operate from Beirut to Nabatieh and from there, taxis can take you to Marjayoun.

Other Options

  1. Taxis are available throughout Lebanon but can be expensive.

How to Get Around in Marjayoun

Once you’re in Marjayoun, the easiest way to get around is by taxi, which is readily available. However, you can also hire a car or a motorbike, which will offer you more flexibility.

Where to Go Next from Marjayoun: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Marjayoun is an excellent base to explore the Southern part of Lebanon. Here are some of the best destinations to visit from Marjayoun:

  1. Sidon
  2. Tyre
  3. Hasbaya

Conclusion

Marjayoun is a beautiful city that offers a blend of both culture and nature. Visitors can enjoy delicious local cuisine, explore the city’s historic sites, and enjoy outdoor adventures. We hope that this comprehensive guide has provided you with all the information you need to plan your visit and explore the best that Marjayoun has to offer.
