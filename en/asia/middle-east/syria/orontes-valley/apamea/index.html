Introduction to Apamea

Apamea, situated in the Orontes Valley, is one of the ancient cities in Syria known for its archaeological and cultural significance. It has been a center of trade, commerce, and power for the Hellenistic, Roman, and Byzantine periods. Visitors who venture to Apamea will be able to experience the beauty and history of this ancient city that still boasts well-preserved ruins of its past.

List of Places to Visit in Apamea

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

One of the prime attractions in Apamea is its extensive Greco-Roman archaeological ruins, renowned as one of the top ten historical sites in Syria. The site features a spacious main column avenue, a 1.85 km-long street lined with Corinthian columns, Roman baths, a theatre, a well-preserved colonnaded street, and the Sphinxes Gate. The Apamea Museum showcases artefacts from the Hellenistic, Roman, and Byzantine periods, like Greco-Roman sculptures, inscriptions, mosaics, and ceramics.

Natural Attractions of Apamea: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

For nature enthusiasts, the Al-Tell Waterfall ranks high in the list of attractions. The waterfall is located on the Al-Tell River, less than 8 km from Apamea, and it features cascades of crystal-clear water amidst a lush green landscape. The Al-Ghab Plain, located close to the city, is a massive natural plain surrounded by the mountains of the Syrian Coastal Range, boasting scenic views that attract many visitors.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Apamea

The Apamea Valley is an ideal spot for hiking and biking, with its rugged terrain, wildflowers, and abundant wildlife. The Apamea Trail, which is a 13.5 km circuit, encompasses the Sphinxes Gate, the colonnaded street, the southern gates, Hellenistic walls, and the fortress overlooking the city. It is highly recommended to hire a guide and get proper equipment before setting out on the trails.

Best Things to Do in Apamea: Activities and Tours

One of the best things to do while in Apamea is taking a guided tour to appreciate the city’s historical and cultural significance. The tour, which usually takes around four hours, includes visits to the theatre, the St. Simeon church and monastery, and the local market, as well as other notable locations around the city.

Food and Drink Scene of Apamea

Must-Try Dishes

The Apamean cuisine is a blend of Mediterranean and Arabic flavors. One of the must-try dishes is the grape leaves stuffed with rice and minced meat, which is usually served with yogurt. Another popular dish is the kibbeh, a mixture of bulgur, minced meat, and spices, which is rolled into balls and fried until golden brown. The local flatbread, Khobz, is also worth trying.

Local Beverages

The traditional Syrian drink, Arak, is a licorice-flavored spirit that is typically mixed with water and ice. Tea, on the other hand, is ubiquitous and is often served with sugar and mint.

Where to Stay in Apamea: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

For budget-conscious travelers, budget hotels and guesthouses are available in the city center. One of the highly recommended budget hotels is the Apamea Rest House. For more luxurious stays, hotels like the Hama Cham Palace and the Safir Hotel Homs are also available nearby.

What to Buy in Apamea: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Tourists may find a range of hand-crafted souvenirs, including pottery, traditional clothing, baskets, and rugs, in the local markets of Apamea. The city center is where most shops can be found.

Local Culture and Customs of Apamea

The language spoken in Apamea is Arabic, but English is also widely spoken. It is essential to dress modestly and respect local customs, especially when visiting religious places like the St. Simeon Monastery.

Best Time to Visit Apamea

The best time to visit Apamea is during the spring or autumn months (April and October). The weather is mild, and the crowds are fewer.

Do’s and Don’ts of Apamea

Do:

  • Dress appropriately, covering knees and shoulders when in public.
  • Respect local customs and traditions.

Don’t:

  • Take photographs of people without permission.
  • Display public affection.

Safety and Health

Safety in Apamea

Apamea is considered a safe place to visit. However, visitors must keep themselves informed of any travel advisories issued by their government.

Health in Apamea

Visitors should avoid drinking tap water and stick to drinking bottled or boiled water. It is advisable to carry a medical kit and check with their healthcare provider for any necessary vaccinations

Getting to Apamea: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Apamea

The nearest airport to Apamea is Aleppo International Airport, which is around 140 km from the city center.

Bus Stations Near Apamea

Homs Bus Station is the nearest bus station to Apamea, around 93 km from the city center. Regular services are available from Homs to Apamea.

Other Options

Taxis and rental cars are available from all major Syrian cities to Apamea.

How to Get Around in Apamea

Getting around in Apamea on foot or by hiring a taxi or rental car is the best way to explore the city and its attractions.

Where to Go Next from Apamea: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Hama, located just 58 km away from Apamea, is one of the popular destinations among tourists. It is renowned for its giant norias, traditional water wheels that are considered a symbol of the city.

Conclusion

Apamea is a city steeped in history and culture that is waiting to be explored. With its rich heritage and adventure opportunities, Apamea is a destination that is sure to appeal to travelers seeking authenticity in the Middle East. From the ancient city ruins to the Al-Tell Waterfall, it offers a diversity of attractions that caters to all. Plan accordingly and get ready to immerse yourself in the wonders of this ancient land.
