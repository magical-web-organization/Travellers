Introduction to Hama

Hama is a city located in the Orontes Valley, in central Syria. Known for its many impressive archeological and historical sites, the city has a rich cultural heritage that dates back many centuries. Hama is also famous for its imposing Norias, or water wheels, which have provided irrigation to the surrounding area for centuries.

List of Places to Visit in Hama

If you’re planning a trip to Hama, there are many attractions that you won’t want to miss. Here’s a list of the top sights to see:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • The Citadel of Hama: Built in the 12th century, the Citadel is a large structure that now houses a museum featuring artifacts from the area’s rich history.
  • The Great Mosque of Hama: This historic mosque dates back to the 8th century and is one of the oldest in Syria.
  • The Museum of the Revolution: This museum documents the history of the Syrian Civil War, providing insight into the ongoing conflict.

Natural Attractions of Hama: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Al-Bassel Dam: This sprawling dam provides ample opportunities for fishing, boating, and other water-based activities. It’s also a popular spot for picnicking and hiking.
  • Orontes River: This picturesque river runs through the heart of Hama, offering scenic views and opportunities for kayaking and other outdoor adventures.
  • The Norias: These ancient water wheels are located on the Orontes River and are some of Hama’s most iconic landmarks.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Hama

For those who love the great outdoors, there are plenty of hiking and biking trails to explore in Hama. One of the most popular routes is the Orontes Trail, which runs along the Orontes River and offers stunning views of the surrounding countryside.

Best Things to Do in Hama: Activities and Tours

In addition to hiking and biking, there are plenty of other great activities to check out in Hama. Here are a few:

  • Take a guided tour of the fantastic Archeological Museum in Hama.
  • Visit the Thermal Baths of Hama that have been used since the Roman times.
  • Pay a visit to the famous Hama water wheels, a symbol of the ancient building construction.
  • Go on a shopping excursion to the local markets and souks, where you’ll find everything from spices and textiles to handmade pottery and jewelry.

Week-Long Itinerary for Hama

If you’re planning to spend a week in Hama, here’s a sample itinerary to help you make the most of your trip:

Day 1 Arrive in Hama, check into your hotel
Day 2 Take a guided tour of the Great Mosque of Hama and the Citadel
Day 3 Hike the Orontes Trail and visit the Thermal Baths
Day 4 Visit the Museum of the Revolution and learn about the Syrian Civil War
Day 5 Explore the Norias and go on a shopping trip to the local markets
Day 6 Take a day trip to nearby Apamea, an ancient Roman city
Day 7 Enjoy some leisure time in Hama before departing for your next destination

Food and Drink Scene of Hama

Syrian food is famous for its delicious and flavorful dishes, and Hama is no exception. Here are some must-try meals and local beverages to enjoy during your visit:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Hummus: This chickpea-based dip is a staple of Syrian cuisine and can be found at most restaurants in Hama.
  • Kebabs: Whether you prefer lamb, beef, or chicken, you’ll find plenty of delicious kebab options in Hama.
  • Fattoush: This fresh and tangy salad is made with tomatoes, cucumbers, onions, and a lemony dressing.

Local Beverages

  • Saltah: A traditional hot drink made from boiled chicory root, saltah is the perfect way to warm up on a chilly day.
  • Arak: This anise-flavored liquor is popular throughout the Middle East and is often enjoyed with meze or other small dishes.

Where to Stay in Hama: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

There are many great accommodation options in Hama, from luxury hotels to budget-friendly guesthouses. Here are a few recommendations:

  • Red Palace Hotel: This elegant hotel is located in the heart of Hama and offers luxurious rooms and suites.
  • Zzaman Boutique Hotel: This charming guesthouse features beautifully decorated rooms and a lovely courtyard.
  • Al Jouri Hotel: A budget-friendly option, this hotel offers clean and comfortable rooms at affordable prices.

What to Buy in Hama: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

If you’re looking to bring home some souvenirs from your trip to Hama, there are plenty of options to choose from. Here are a few ideas:

  • Handmade pottery: Hama is known for its beautiful pottery, and you’ll find plenty of shops and markets selling ceramics and other handmade goods.
  • Spices and herbs: Syrian cuisine is famous for its use of fragrant spices, and you’ll find plenty of shops selling high-quality spices and herbs that make great souvenirs.
  • Textiles: From handmade carpets and rugs to colorful scarves and shawls, there are plenty of opportunities to bring home some beautiful textiles from Hama.

Local Culture and Customs of Hama

While Hama is a modern and cosmopolitan city, it’s also steeped in tradition and culture. Visitors should be respectful of local customs, including dress codes in certain areas and gender segregation in some social situations.

Best Time to Visit Hama

The best time to visit Hama is during the spring and fall, when the weather is mild and comfortable. Summers can be quite hot, with temperatures often exceeding 100 degrees Fahrenheit, so it’s best to avoid visiting during this time if possible. Winters can be cool and rainy, but still pleasant for exploring the city’s many attractions.

Do’s and Don’ts of Hama

Here are a few things to keep in mind when visiting Hama:

Do:

  • Dress modestly in certain areas of the city, including mosques and other religious sites.
  • Be respectful of local customs and traditions, including gender segregation in some social situations.
  • Ask permission before taking photos of local people, and be aware that some may not wish to be photographed.

Don’t:

  • Drink tap water, which is not considered safe for tourists.
  • Engage in political discussions, which can be sensitive and controversial in Syria.
  • Show disrespect for religious or cultural practices, which can be seen as offensive.

Safety and Health

While Syria is not without its challenges, Hama is generally considered to be a safe and welcoming city for visitors. Here are a few things to keep in mind:

Safety in Hama

  • Avoid traveling in areas that are known to be unsafe or where the Syrian government has imposed travel restrictions.
  • Be aware of your surroundings at all times, particularly in crowded areas or public transportation.
  • Follow local customs and traditions, including dress codes and gender segregation in some situations.

Health in Hama

  • Drink only bottled water or other sealed beverages, as tap water is not safe for tourists.
  • Be aware of food hygiene and avoid eating uncooked or undercooked foods, particularly from street vendors or other informal sources.
  • Be prepared for the heat and sun, particularly during the summer months, by wearing loose-fitting clothing, a hat, and sunscreen.

Getting to Hama: Transportation Options and Logistics

Hama is well-connected to other cities in Syria and neighboring countries by a variety of transportation options. Here’s what you need to know:

Airports Near Hama

  • The closest airport to Hama is Aleppo International Airport.
  • Other airports in the region include Damascus International Airport and Lattakia Airport.

Bus Stations Near Hama

  • Hama has a central bus station that provides service to other cities throughout Syria, including Aleppo and Damascus.
  • International bus service is also available to and from neighboring countries such as Turkey and Lebanon.

Other Options

  • Taxis and private car hires are widely available in Hama, although prices vary widely depending on the provider.
  • Many visitors to Hama also choose to rent a car, which can provide greater flexibility and ease of travel within the city and surrounding areas.

How to Get Around in Hama

While Hama is a relatively small city, it’s still easiest to get around by car or taxi. However, public transportation is also available, including buses and shared taxis.

where to Go Next from Hama: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

If you’d like to explore more of the surrounding area, there are plenty of great destinations to check out from Hama. Here are a few recommendations:

  • Maarat al-Numan: This nearby city is home to a stunning medieval Great Mosque and is a popular destination for history buffs.
  • Krak des Chevaliers: One of the most impressive ancient castles in the world, the Krak des Chevaliers is located just a short drive from Hama.
  • Mount Qasioun: Located just outside of Damascus, this mountain offers stunning views of the city and surrounding area.

Conclusion

Whether you’re interested in history, culture, or outdoor adventure, Hama offers something for everyone. With its impressive archeological and historical sites, natural beauty, and vibrant culture, this city is a must-see destination for anyone traveling to Syria.
