Introduction to Tartous

Nestled in the heart of the Syrian Coast and Mountains, Tartous is a picturesque city that boasts a rich array of cultural, historical, and natural attractions. This bustling seaport town, located in the northwest of Syria, is steeped in history, and the evidence of its past can be seen in the architecture of its buildings, the stories of its people, and the culture that emanates from it. Visiting Tartous is like taking a journey through time, as the city has been inhabited since the 2nd century BC and has been shaped by various empires over the centuries. In this comprehensive travel guide, we will explore the top attractions, activities, and experiences that Tartous offers, helping you plan the perfect trip to this enchanting destination.

List of Places to Visit in Tartous

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

When it comes to cultural attractions, Tartous is certainly not lacking in options. For history buffs and archeology enthusiasts, there are a plethora of museums and historical sites to explore:

  1. National Museum of Tartous: This museum offers a collection of archeological and historical artifacts, including pottery, jewelry, statues, and other objects that shed light on the rich history of Tartous and its surrounding areas.
  2. Citadel of Tartous: Built by the Crusaders in the 12th century, this castle is an impressive sight and offers magnificent views of the city and the Mediterranean Sea.
  3. Old City of Tartous: Stroll along the narrow, winding streets of the old city, which date back to the Ottoman era, and admire the traditional Middle Eastern architecture of the buildings, the souks, and the bustling markets.

Natural Attractions of Tartous: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Tartous is a nature lover’s paradise, with plenty of opportunities for outdoor activities and breathtaking views:

  1. Al-Azim Lake: A man-made lake located just outside the city, Al-Azim Lake offers opportunities for boating, fishing, and picnics, and is surrounded by lush greenery and stunning views.
  2. Qalaat Marqab: This stunning fortress overlooks the Mediterranean Sea and offers panoramic views of the surrounding countryside. It is a popular spot for hiking and exploring the rugged terrain.
  3. Krak des Chevaliers: One of the best-preserved medieval castles in the world, Krak des Chevaliers is a UNESCO World Heritage Site and offers an unforgettable glimpse into the past.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Tartous

For those who love to hike and bike, Tartous has an abundance of trails and routes to explore:

  1. Alzabadani Trail: This scenic trail starts at the Alzabadani dam and winds its way through the surrounding hills and mountains, offering breathtaking views of the Alzabadani Valley.
  2. Qalaat Marqab Trail: This moderately difficult trail starts at the foot of the Qalaat Marqab fortress and takes hikers through forests, valleys, and rugged terrain before reaching the summit, which offers stunning views of the Mediterranean Sea.
  3. Krak des Chevaliers Trail: This challenging trail leads hikers through the rugged terrain surrounding the Krak des Chevaliers fortress, offering sweeping views of the surrounding countryside.

Best Things to Do in Tartous: Activities and Tours

If you want to get the most out of your visit to Tartous, consider taking a tour or participating in one of the many activities the city has to offer:

  1. Boat Tours: Take a boat tour of the Tartous harbor and surrounding areas, and get a unique perspective of the city from the water.
  2. Cultural Tours: Tour the old city, visit the museums and historical sites, and immerse yourself in Tartous’s rich cultural heritage.
  3. Food Tours: Sample the local cuisine and learn about the traditions and ingredients that make Syrian food so unique and flavorful.

Food and Drink Scene of Tartous

Sampling the local cuisine is a must-do when visiting Tartous. Here are some of the best dishes and local beverages to try:

Must-Try Dishes

  1. Meze: A selection of small dishes, typically including hummus, baba ganoush, stuffed grape leaves, and falafel.
  2. Kebabs: Grilled meat skewers, typically made with lamb or chicken.
  3. Sfiha: A type of pizza made with meat or vegetables.

Local Beverages

  1. Arak: An anise-flavored alcoholic drink, often served with water and ice.
  2. Tea: Strong black tea served in small glasses, often sweetened with sugar or honey.
  3. Ayran: A salty yogurt drink, often served with a meal.

Where to Stay in Tartous: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Tartous offers a range of accommodation options to suit all budgets and preferences:

  1. Grand Hotel Tartous: A luxurious hotel located in the heart of the city, with stunning views of the sea.
  2. Al Salam Hotel: A mid-range hotel located in a quiet neighborhood, with comfortable rooms and a friendly staff.
  3. Al Najem Guesthouse: A budget-friendly guesthouse located in the old city, with basic but clean accommodations.

What to Buy in Tartous: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Looking for the perfect souvenir to bring home from Tartous? Consider purchasing one of these local products:

  1. Spices: Syrian spices are renowned for their unique blends and bold flavors.
  2. Handmade Crafts: From pottery to textiles, there are plenty of locally-made handicrafts to choose from.
  3. Olive Oil: Tartous is known for its high-quality olive oil, which can be purchased at local markets.

Local Culture and Customs of Tartous

When visiting Tartous, it’s important to be aware of local customs and traditions:

  1. Dress Conservatively: Syrian culture is conservative, and visitors should dress appropriately, covering their shoulders, chest, and knees.
  2. Respect Religious Sites: Mosques and churches are sacred places, and visitors should remove their shoes before entering and refrain from taking photos inside.
  3. Greet Locals Politely: It is customary to greet locals with a handshake and a polite greeting, such as “Salam alaykum” (peace be upon you).

Best Time to Visit Tartous

The best time to visit Tartous is from April to June and from September to November, when the weather is mild and pleasant.

Do’s and Don’ts of Tartous

To help you navigate the local customs and traditions of Tartous, here are some do’s and don’ts to keep in mind:

Do:

  1. Try the Local Cuisine: Syrian food is delicious and unique, and trying it is a must-do on any visit to Tartous.
  2. Respect Local Customs: Be mindful of local customs and traditions, and dress and behave appropriately.
  3. Take a Tour: Guided tours offer a great way to learn about the city’s history and culture.

Don’t:

  1. Take Photos of Women Without Permission: Women’s photographs should not be taken without their permission.
  2. Drink Alcohol in Public: Public drinking is frowned upon in Syria, and it is illegal to drink alcohol in public places.
  3. Be Disrespectful of Local Traditions: Syrian culture is steeped in tradition and respect for elders, and visitors should be mindful of this when interacting with locals.

Safety and Health

Safety in Tartous

Tartous is generally a safe city, but visitors should take reasonable precautions to ensure their safety:

  1. Avoid Demonstrations: Political demonstrations can occur, and it is best to avoid them to ensure your safety.
  2. Be Cautious at Night: As in any city, it is best to exercise caution when walking around at night, particularly in poorly lit areas.

Health in Tartous

Visitors to Tartous should ensure they are up to date on routine vaccinations and take precautions to avoid food and water-borne illness.

Getting to Tartous: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Tartous

Bassel Al-Assad International Airport in Latakia is the closest airport to Tartous, and it is approximately a 45-minute drive from the city.

Bus Stations Near Tartous

Tartous Bus Station is the main bus station serving the city, with buses departing to and from various destinations throughout Syria.

Other Options

Many visitors opt to rent a car or hire a private driver to get around Tartous and the surrounding areas.

How to Get Around in Tartous

The best way to get around Tartous is on foot or by taxi. Taxis are widely available and affordable, and they offer a convenient way to get around the city.

Where to Go Next from Tartous: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

If you have time to explore further afield, there are plenty of nearby destinations and excursions worth considering:

  1. Krak des Chevaliers: This stunning medieval castle is located approximately 40km to the east of Tartous and is well worth a visit.
  2. Latakia: Located approximately 70km north of Tartous, Latakia is a bustling seaport town with a rich history and impressive architecture.
  3. Hama: Approximately 150km south of Tartous, Hama is famous for its ancient waterwheels and beautiful parks and gardens.

Conclusion

Tartous is a fascinating city with something to offer every type of traveler. From the rich cultural heritage and stunning natural scenery to the delicious food and friendly locals, Tartous is an enchanting destination that is well worth a visit. Whether you’re a history buff, nature lover, or simply looking for a relaxing beach vacation, Tartous has it all. We hope this comprehensive travel guide has inspired you to plan your visit to Tartous and explore all that this remarkable city has to offer.
