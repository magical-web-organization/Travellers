Palmyra, located in the heart of the Syrian Desert, is one of the world’s most remarkable historical destinations. An ancient city that dates back to the Neolithic period, Palmyra is a UNESCO World Heritage Site revered for its stunning architecture, rich cultural heritage, and incredible natural wonders. Whether you’re a history buff, an adventurer seeking outdoor excitement, or a foodie looking to indulge your taste buds in Middle Eastern cuisine, Palmyra is a destination like no other.

In this comprehensive travel guide to Palmyra, we’ll take you on a journey through the city’s rich history, culture, and cuisine. From must-see attractions to hiking and biking routes, food and drink recommendations to local customs and traditions, we’ve got everything you need to know to plan the ultimate trip to Palmyra.

Introduction to Palmyra

Palmyra is a city located in the central part of Syria, in the Homs Governorate. It was once an important trading hub and a meeting point between the East and the West. The city is located in the Syrian Desert, providing a stunningly scenic backdrop to its many attractions. Palmyra boasts a rich cultural heritage and has played an influential role in shaping the history of the Middle East.

List of Places to Visit in Palmyra

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Palmyra is home to a range of museums, historical sites, and other culturally important attractions. Here are some of the must-visit places in the city:

  • The Temple of Baal: The stunning Temple of Baal is one of the most well-known landmarks in Palmyra. Built in the first century AD, the temple was dedicated to the Mesopotamian god of the same name, and was considered to be one of the most important religious sites in the ancient world.

  • The Great Colonnade: The Great Colonnade is a monumental avenue lined with columns that stretches for over a kilometer through the heart of the city. Built in the second century AD, it was once the major thoroughfare of Palmyra and is a testament to the city’s incredible architectural achievements.

  • The Palmyra Museum: The Palmyra Museum houses a wide range of artifacts and exhibits that showcase the city’s rich cultural heritage. Visitors can see everything from ancient glassware and pottery to sculptures and jewelry.

Natural Attractions of Palmyra: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

In addition to its cultural attractions, Palmyra is a great destination for outdoor enthusiasts. Here are some of the top natural attractions in the city:

  • The Valley of the Tombs: The Valley of the Tombs is a stunning canyon located just outside of Palmyra, and contains a number of ancient tombs and burial sites. Visitors can hike through the valley and enjoy the breathtaking views of the surrounding desert landscape.

  • Jebel al-Musaytir: Jebel al-Musaytir is a mountain located just north of Palmyra, and is a popular spot for hiking and trekking. Visitors can climb to the summit and enjoy stunning views of the surrounding desert and Palmyra itself.

  • The Palmyra Oasis: The Palmyra Oasis is a lush green oasis located just outside the city, and is a great spot to relax and unwind. Visitors can take a dip in the cool waters, enjoy a picnic in the shade of the palm trees, or simply take in the stunning views of the desert landscape.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Palmyra

Palmyra is a great destination for outdoor activities like hiking and biking. Here are some of the top routes to explore:

  • The Palmyra Desert Trek: This breathtaking desert trek takes hikers through the heart of the Syrian Desert, passing by ancient ruins, tombs, and other sites of historical importance.

  • The Palmyra Mountain Bike Adventure: This challenging mountain bike route takes riders through rugged terrain and stunning mountain scenery, providing a thrilling and unforgettable experience.

Best Things to Do in Palmyra: Activities and Tours

Palmyra offers a range of activities and tours that are sure to please any traveler. Here are some of the top things to do in Palmyra:

  • Sunset Camel Trek: Take a relaxing camel trek through the desert as the sun sets over the ancient ruins of Palmyra.

  • Hot Air Balloon Ride: Take to the skies and take in the stunning views of the surrounding desert landscape from above.

  • City Tour: Explore the city’s vibrant culture and ancient history on a guided tour of Palmyra’s top attractions.

Week-Long Itinerary for Palmyra

For travelers looking to experience all that Palmyra has to offer, a week-long itinerary is the perfect way to do so. Here’s a suggested itinerary for a week in Palmyra:

Day 1: Visit the Temple of Baal and the Great Colonnade

Day 2: Explore the Palmyra Museum and the Valley of the Tombs

Day 3: Hike to the summit of Jebel al-Musaytir

Day 4: Relax at the Palmyra Oasis and take a dip in the cool waters

Day 5: Experience a hot air balloon ride over the city

Day 6: Take a sunset camel trek through the desert

Day 7: Visit the local souks and markets and pick up some unique souvenirs to take home

Food and Drink Scene of Palmyra

Palmyra’s cuisine is renowned for its unique flavors and local specialties. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Kebab Halabi: A delicious meat dish served with a range of fresh vegetables and spices.

  • Fattoush: A fresh, zesty salad made with tomatoes, cucumber, and herbs, topped with crispy pita chips.

  • Baklava: A sweet pastry made with layers of filo dough, honey, and pistachios.

Local Beverages

  • Carob Juice: Made from the fruit of the carob tree, this refreshing drink is a popular local specialty.

  • Syrian Coffee: A strong, rich coffee served with cardamom and other spices.

Where to Stay in Palmyra: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Palmyra offers a range of accommodation options to suit any budget. Here are some top picks for hotels, hostels, and guesthouses:

  • Zenobia Hotel: Situated in the heart of Palmyra, Zenobia Hotel offers guests a comfortable and convenient base to explore the city.

  • Beit Al-Wali Guesthouse: This charming guesthouse offers guests a traditional Syrian experience, with beautiful rooms and a warm, welcoming atmosphere.

  • Palmyra Hotel: With stunning views of the ancient ruins, Palmyra Hotel is a great choice for travelers looking for a unique and memorable experience.

What to Buy in Palmyra: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Palmyra’s local markets and souks offer a range of unique souvenirs and local products. Here are some top picks:

  • Spices and Herbs: Take home some of Palmyra’s vibrant spices and herbs to add flavor to your own cooking.

  • Handicrafts: From pottery to textiles, Palmyra’s local handicrafts are a great way to bring home a piece of the city’s rich cultural heritage.

Local Culture and Customs of Palmyra

Palmyra is a city steeped in tradition and custom. Here are some tips to help you navigate the local culture and customs:

  • Dress modestly and respectfully when visiting religious sites and public places.

  • Always remove your shoes when entering someone’s home or a mosque.

  • Respect local customs and traditions, and be open to learning about different ways of life.

Best Time to Visit Palmyra

The best time to visit Palmyra is during the spring (March-May) and fall (September-November) seasons when the weather is mild and comfortable. Summers can be extremely hot, while winters can be cold and dry.

Do’s and Don’ts of Palmyra

Do:

  • Do dress modestly and respectfully when visiting religious sites and public places.

  • Do try local specialties like kebab halabi and carob juice.

  • Do be respectful of local customs and traditions.

Don’t:

  • Don’t take photographs without permission, especially of people.

  • Don’t touch or take anything from historical sites or tombs.

  • Don’t engage in public displays of affection.

Safety and Health

Safety in Palmyra

While Palmyra has been affected by the conflict in Syria, the city is currently under the control of the Syrian government and is considered safe for travelers.

Health in Palmyra

Recommended vaccinations for Palmyra include hepatitis A and B, typhoid, and tetanus. Visitors should also take precautions to prevent dehydration and heat exhaustion in the hot desert climate.

Getting to Palmyra: Transportation Options and Logistics

Palmyra is located approximately 210 km northeast of Damascus, and transportation options include:

Airports Near Palmyra

The nearest airport to Palmyra is the Damascus International Airport, located approximately 220 km south of the city.

Bus Stations Near Palmyra

Buses run regularly between Palmyra and Damascus, Homs, and other nearby cities. Most buses depart from the Palmyra Bus Station, located in the city center.

Other Options

Taxis and private car hires are available for those who prefer to travel independently.

How to Get Around in Palmyra

Palmyra is a small city that can be easily navigated on foot. Taxis and public buses are also available for longer journeys.

Where to Go Next from Palmyra: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Palmyra is surrounded by a range of nearby destinations and excursions. Here are some top picks:

  • Krak des Chevaliers: This stunning medieval castle is located approximately 50 km west of Palmyra, and is a UNESCO World Heritage Site.

  • Bosra: Located approximately 140 km south of Palmyra, Bosra is an ancient city that dates back to the Roman period.

  • Dead Cities: Located to the northwest of Palmyra, the Dead Cities are a group of abandoned Roman cities that offer stunning insights into ancient life in Syria.

Conclusion

Palmyra is a truly remarkable destination, offering visitors an unforgettable experience that combines history, culture, and natural beauty. With our comprehensive travel guide, you’ll be fully prepared to plan the ultimate trip to this hidden gem in the heart of the Syrian Desert.
