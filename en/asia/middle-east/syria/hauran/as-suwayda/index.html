Introduction to As-Suwayda

Located in the Hauran region of Syria, As-Suwayda is an ancient city that has preserved its traditional architecture, customs, and culture. The city is known for its rich cultural and historical heritage, natural wonders, and outdoor adventures. As-Suwayda is also famous for its delicious cuisine and warm hospitality.

List of Places to Visit in As-Suwayda

There are several exciting places to explore in As-Suwayda, ranging from cultural and historical attractions to natural wonders and outdoor adventures.

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

As-Suwayda has a rich history that dates back to biblical times. Some of the top museums and historical sites worth visiting include:

  • National Museum of As-Suwayda: This museum features a collection of artifacts and exhibits that showcase the city’s cultural and historical heritage.
  • As-Suwayda Citadel: This medieval fortress is a popular attraction and offers a panoramic view of the city.
  • Roman Amphitheater: This well-preserved amphitheater is believed to be over 2,000 years old and is an architectural marvel.

Natural Attractions of As-Suwayda: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

As-Suwayda is also home to several natural wonders and outdoor adventures that are a must-visit for travelers. Some of the top natural attractions include:

  • Shahba National Park: This vast park encompasses an area of over 25,000 acres and is home to a variety of wildlife, including gazelles and wolves. The park also features several hiking and biking trails.
  • Al-Nawaimeer Aculpulco Lake: This pristine lake is a popular spot for swimming, fishing, and picnics. There are several cafes and restaurants near the lake that offer delicious local cuisine.
  • Wadi Al-Arab: This scenic valley is a popular destination for hiking, camping, and enjoying the natural beauty of As-Suwayda.

Hiking/Biking Routes of As-Suwayda

As-Suwayda is known for its rugged terrain and mountainous landscape, making it an ideal destination for hiking and biking enthusiasts. Some of the top hiking and biking routes include:

  • Wadi Al-Arab Hike: This moderate hike takes you through the stunning Wadi Al-Arab valley and offers breathtaking views of the surrounding mountains.
  • Shahba National Park Bike Trail: This challenging trail takes you through the rugged terrain of Shahba National Park and is ideal for experienced bikers.
  • Al-Sukhna Hike: This scenic hike takes you through almond and olive groves and offers stunning views of the surrounding countryside.

Best Things to Do in As-Suwayda: Activities and Tours

There are several exciting things to do and tours to take in As-Suwayda, including:

  • Visit a Local Olive Oil Farm: As-Suwayda is known for its delicious olive oil, and there are several local farms that offer tours and tastings.
  • Take a Cultural Tour: Explore the rich cultural heritage of As-Suwayda by taking a tour of the city’s historical and cultural landmarks.
  • Enjoy Outdoor Adventures: Hike, bike, camp, or swim in the pristine natural wonders of As-Suwayda.

Week-Long Itinerary for As-Suwayda

Day 1: Visit National Museum of As-Suwayda and As-Suwayda Citadel. Day 2: Explore the Roman Amphitheater and Wadi Al-Arab. Day 3: Hike the Al-Sukhna Trail and visit a local olive oil farm. Day 4: Bike the Shahba National Park trail. Day 5: Relax at Al-Nawaimeer Aculpulco Lake and enjoy local cuisine. Day 6: Take a cultural tour of the city and try traditional crafts. Day 7: Enjoy a day of outdoor adventures at Shahba National Park.

Food and Drink Scene of As-Suwayda

As-Suwayda is famous for its delicious cuisine, which features local produce like olives, almonds, and lamb. Some of the must-try dishes include:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Mahashi: This dish consists of stuffed vegetables like eggplant and peppers, filled with spiced rice and minced lamb.
  • Arabic Shawarma: This popular street food is made with marinated and grilled meat, usually lamb or chicken, wrapped in pita bread and served with sauce and veggies.
  • Moutabal: This roasted eggplant dip is a staple of As-Suwayda cuisine, and it’s often served with flatbread.

Local Beverages

  • Arabic Tea: This sweet and fragrant tea is a popular beverage in As-Suwayda and is usually served with fresh mint leaves and sugar.
  • Arak: This anise-flavored spirit is a popular alcoholic beverage in the Middle East.

Where to Stay in As-Suwayda: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

There are several affordable and comfortable lodging options in As-Suwayda, including hotels, hostels, and guesthouses. Some of the top places to stay include:

  • Al Diwan Hotel: This traditional-style hotel is located in the heart of As-Suwayda and offers comfortable rooms and a restaurant.
  • Al-Muzafar Hotel: This budget-friendly hotel is ideal for travelers and offers modern amenities, comfortable rooms, and a friendly staff.
  • Al Iwan Guest House: This cozy guesthouse is located in the Old City and offers traditional-style accommodations and a peaceful courtyard.

What to Buy in As-Suwayda: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

As-Suwayda is famous for its traditional crafts and cultural products. Some of the top souvenirs to buy include:

  • Olive Oil: As-Suwayda is famous for its delicious olive oil, and there are several local farms that offer high-quality oil for sale.
  • Hand-woven Carpets and Rugs: These colorful and intricate carpets and rugs are made by local artisans and are a beautiful way to bring a piece of As-Suwayda culture home.
  • Traditional Jewelry: As-Suwayda is known for its intricate and unique jewelry designs, made using precious metals and stones.

Local Culture and Customs of As-Suwayda

As-Suwayda has a rich cultural heritage that reflects both its Christian and Muslim roots. Visitors should be respectful of local customs and beliefs, dress modestly, and refrain from alcohol and pork consumption in public places.

Best Time to Visit As-Suwayda

The best time to visit As-Suwayda is from March to May and from September to November. The weather during these months is mild and pleasant, making it ideal for outdoor activities and sightseeing.

Do’s and Don’ts of As-Suwayda

Do:

  • Respect local customs and traditions.
  • Dress modestly when visiting religious sites.
  • Try the local cuisine and sample traditional products.

Don’t:

  • Drink alcohol or consume pork in public places.
  • Disrespect local customs or beliefs.
  • Take photographs of people without their permission.

Safety and Health

As-Suwayda is generally a safe city for travelers; however, visitors should be aware of the current political situation in Syria and take precautions while traveling. Visitors should also ensure that they have adequate medical insurance and take necessary vaccinations before traveling to Syria.

Safety in As-Suwayda

  • Avoid large public gatherings and demonstrations.
  • Be cautious when traveling alone or at night.
  • Stick to well-lit and well-traveled areas.

Health in As-Suwayda

  • Check with your doctor regarding necessary vaccinations and medications before traveling.
  • Drink bottled or boiled water only.
  • Wash hands frequently and carry sanitizers.

How to Get Around in As-Suwayda

Taxis and rental cars are the most convenient forms of transportation in As-Suwayda. Visitors can also take local buses and minibusses to navigate the city.

Where to Go Next from As-Suwayda: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

As-Suwayda is located in the Hauran region and is surrounded by several other exciting destinations worth exploring, including:

  • Bosra: This ancient city is known for its well-preserved Roman theater and iconic arch.
  • Jabal Al-Arab: This mountainous region is a popular hiking and camping destination for outdoor enthusiasts.
  • Damascus: The capital city of Syria is just over an hour’s drive from As-Suwayda and is known for its rich cultural heritage and historical landmarks.

Conclusion

As-Suwayda is an exciting destination for cultural and outdoor enthusiasts alike. With its rich history, natural wonders, unique cuisine, and warm hospitality, As-Suwayda is an unforgettable travel experience.
