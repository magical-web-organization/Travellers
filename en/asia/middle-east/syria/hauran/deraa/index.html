Introduction to Deraa

Deraa is a city located in the Hauran plain, situated in southwestern Syria. The city is known for being one of the oldest continuously inhabited cities in the world, with a history dating back to the 7th century BC. Deraa is also known for being the birthplace of the Syriac civilization and the location of the famous Battle of Deraa during the Arab Spring.

List of Places to Visit in Deraa

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Deraa is home to a number of museums and historical places that are worth visiting. The National Museum of Deraa is a must-see for those interested in Syriac history, as it houses a large collection of artifacts and relics from the ancient Syriac civilization. The Omari Mosque is another important cultural attraction, as it is one of the oldest mosques in Syria, dating back to the 7th century AD. For those interested in military history, the Battle of Deraa Memorial and Museum is also worth a visit.

Natural Attractions of Deraa: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Deraa is surrounded by beautiful landscapes that are a delight for nature lovers. The Al-Rashid Garden is one such attraction, with its beautiful flora and fauna, making it a haven for bird-watchers. Another must-see is the Al Riyadiyah Park, which is located in the heart of Deraa and is perfect for a relaxing stroll. Additionally, the rock formations in the nearby mountains are perfect for rock climbing and provide a scenic view of the surrounding area.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Deraa

The beautiful scenery in and around Deraa is perfect for hiking and biking enthusiasts. The Darb Al-Arba’in (The Forty Road) is one such route and is popular among hikers and bikers. The route is a 40-kilometer-long road that passes through scenic landscapes and beautiful countryside, making it an ideal adventure. The Al-Mahatta Park is another popular spot for hiking and is ideal for those looking for a shorter trail.

Best Things to Do in Deraa: Activities and Tours

Deraa has plenty of activities to offer visitors. One such activity is a tour of the city’s ancient ruins and archaeological sites. Another popular activity is a visit to the local bazaars, where you can find traditional Syrian crafts, souvenirs, and spices. For those interested in nature, a trip to the nearby Al-Rashid Garden is a must. Additionally, a nighttime visit to the Omari Mosque is an experience not to be missed.

Week-Long Itinerary for Deraa

Day 1: Visit the National Museum of Deraa and the Omari Mosque Day 2: Go rock climbing in the local mountains Day 3: Take a tour of the city’s ancient ruins and archaeological sites Day 4: Visit the Al-Rashid Garden for birdwatching Day 5: Explore the local craft bazaars for souvenirs Day 6: Take a hike along the Darb Al-Arba’in (The Forty Road) Day 7: Relax at the Al Riyadiyah Park

Food and Drink Scene of Deraa

Must-Try Dishes

Deraa offers a unique blend of Middle Eastern cuisine that is sure to delight visitors. One must-try dish is the famous Fattet Batinjan, a dish made from eggplant, yogurt, and bread. Another popular dish is the Kibbeh, a meat dish made with bulgur wheat and spices. For dessert, try the Halawet El-Jibn, a sweet cheese pastry soaked in sugar syrup.

Local Beverages

One of the most famous local beverages in Deraa is the Jallab drink, which is made from a mixture of molasses, rosewater, and sugar, and topped with pine nuts. Another popular drink is the Tamarind drink, which is made from the tamarind fruit and is known for its refreshing and sweet taste.

Where to Stay in Deraa: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Deraa offers a range of accommodation options for visitors, with hotels, hostels, and guesthouses available to suit all budgets. Some of the most popular options include the Al-Rabie Hotel, the Al-Amar Hotel, and the Al-Bustan Hotel. For those on a budget, the Deraa Youth Hostel is a great option.

What to Buy in Deraa: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Visitors to Deraa will find a variety of souvenirs and local products to take home. Some of the most popular options include traditional Syrian crafts, such as pottery and glassware. Additionally, spices and herbs are also popular, with many visitors picking up local blends to take home.

Local Culture and Customs of Deraa

Deraa is a city that takes pride in its rich cultural heritage, and visitors are encouraged to respect local customs and traditions. Women are expected to dress conservatively, and it is recommended to dress modestly when visiting religious sites.

Best Time to Visit Deraa

The best time to visit Deraa is between March and May, when the weather is mild, and the landscapes are at their most beautiful.

Do’s and Don’ts of Deraa

Do:

  • Dress modestly
  • Respect local traditions and customs
  • Try local cuisine and beverages
  • Explore the ancient ruins and archaeological sites

Don’t:

  • Disrespect religious sites
  • Wear revealing clothing
  • Photograph people without their permission
  • Visit during the summer months, when the weather can be uncomfortably hot.

Safety and Health

Safety in Deraa

While the security situation in Syria remains challenging and unpredictable, Deraa is considered safe to visit for travelers who take appropriate precautions. It is important to avoid any political gatherings or protests, and visitors should be aware of local sensitivities and cultural norms.

Health in Deraa

Travelers to Deraa should get up-to-date on routine vaccinations before traveling, including measles-mumps-rubella (MMR), diphtheria-tetanus-pertussis, varicella (chickenpox), polio, and flu. Additionally, visitors to Syria should be vigilant about food and water hygiene to avoid stomach illnesses.

Getting to Deraa: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Deraa

The nearest international airport to Deraa is Damascus International Airport. From there, visitors can take a taxi, hire a car, or take a bus to reach Deraa.

Bus Stations Near Deraa

Deraa has a central bus station that services buses to and from all major Syrian cities.

Other Options

Alternatively, visitors can also take a taxi or hire a car from nearby cities like Damascus or Amman in Jordan.

How to Get Around in Deraa

While walking is the best option for exploring the city center of Deraa, visitors can also use taxis or private cars to get around. Biking and hiking are also popular options for exploring the surrounding countryside.

Where to Go Next from Deraa: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Nearby destinations to Deraa include the ancient Roman city of Bosra and the city of Sweida, known for its famous traditional sweets. Visitors can also take a day trip to the nearby city of Damascus, known for its stunning architecture and vibrant markets.

Conclusion

Deraa is a city that offers visitors a wealth of cultural and natural wonders, with plenty of activities to suit all interests. From ancient ruins and archaeological sites to scenic hikes and delicious local cuisine, Deraa is a must-visit destination for anyone traveling in Syria. Visitors are encouraged to take appropriate precautions while traveling and to respect local customs and traditions.
