Introduction to Hauran

Located in southern Syria, Hauran is a region known for its rich history, stunning natural landscapes, and delicious cuisine. This area boasts a number of ancient ruins, fascinating museums, hiking and biking trails, and local products that are sure to leave visitors captivated.

List of Places to Visit in Hauran

Here is a list of the top attractions to see in Hauran:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Bosra Amphitheatre
  • Qanawat Archaeological Site
  • Suweida Museum
  • Shahba Archaeological Site
  • Kanaker Village

Natural Attractions of Hauran: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Mount Hermon
  • Al-Musayfirah Park
  • Jabal al-Arab Forest Reserve
  • Wadi al-Rummah

Hiking/Biking Routes of Hauran

Hauran offers some of the most beautiful hiking and biking trails in the region. Here are some of the best routes for outdoor enthusiasts:

  • Al-Fowara Trail
  • Shabraq Trail
  • Jabal Al-Arab Trail

Best Things to Do in Hauran: Activities and Tours

Apart from hiking and biking, here are some recommended activities to do:

  • Visit local markets
  • Attend a traditional wedding
  • Join a cooking class
  • Take a guided tour of the archaeological sites

Week-Long Itinerary for Hauran

For a week-long adventure in Hauran, here is a suggested itinerary:

Local Culture and Customs of Hauran

Hauran’s culture is deeply rooted in Arab traditions and customs. When visiting, be aware of the following cultural considerations:

  • Respect local dress codes
  • Remove shoes before entering someone’s home
  • Always use your right hand for eating or shaking hands

Best Time to Visit Hauran

The best time to visit Hauran is during the spring (March to May) or autumn (September to November). The weather is mild during these seasons, making it ideal for outdoor activities.

Do’s and Don’ts of Hauran

Here are some general do’s and don’ts to keep in mind during your visit to Hauran:

Do:

  • Dress conservatively
  • Try the local cuisine
  • Respect local customs

Don’t:

  • Take pictures of people without their permission
  • Drink tap water
  • Show public displays of affection

Safety and Health

Safety in Hauran

Although Hauran is generally considered safe, it is advisable to practice caution and stay informed about any political changes in Syria. Travelers should avoid political rallies and demonstrations, and be aware of their surroundings.

Health in Hauran

Visitors should be vaccinated against common diseases such as Hepatitis A and B, Tetanus, and Typhoid. It’s also important to drink bottled water and use insect repellent to prevent mosquito-borne illnesses such as malaria and dengue fever.

Getting to Hauran: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Hauran

The closest airport to Hauran is Damascus International Airport (DAM), which is about a 90-minute drive from the city.

Bus Stations Near Hauran

There are two major bus stations in Hauran: Suweida Bus Station and Shahba Bus Station. These stations offer regular buses to other cities in Syria.

Other Options

Taxis and private cars are also available for hire in Hauran.

How to Get Around in Hauran

The best way to get around Hauran is by private car or taxi. Buses and minibusses are also available but can be crowded and uncomfortable.

Where to Go Next from Hauran: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Some of the nearby destinations and excursions from Hauran include:

  • Damascus
  • Palmyra
  • Krak des Chevaliers
  • Aleppo

Conclusion

Hauran is a stunning region filled with natural beauty, fascinating history, and warm hospitality. Exploring this area is an unforgettable experience where you can immerse yourself in Arabic culture and cuisine. From hiking and biking to visiting ancient ruins and sampling local dishes, Hauran has something for everyone.
