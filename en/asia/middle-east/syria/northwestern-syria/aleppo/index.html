Introduction to Aleppo

Aleppo is a historic city in northwestern Syria, renowned for its rich cultural heritage and architecture. The city was once a vital center of commerce on the Silk Road and has been a melting pot of cultures for centuries, with influences from the Greeks, Romans, Byzantines, and Ottomans. It is an ideal destination for those who want to explore the beauty of the Middle East.

List of Places to Visit in Aleppo

Aleppo has a plethora of attractions that are worth visiting. Here are some of the must-visit places in Aleppo.

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • The Citadel of Aleppo: A magnificent fortress that dates back to the 13th century, the Citadel of Aleppo is a must-visit destination in the city. The fortress has been damaged in the Syrian Civil War but is still a remarkable sight.

  • Aleppo Souk: The city’s largest covered market, Aleppo Souk is a vibrant marketplace that dates back to the 14th century. The market offers a wide range of goods, including textiles, jewelry, spices, and more.

  • The Great Mosque of Aleppo: Also known as the Umayyad Mosque, the Great Mosque of Aleppo is one of the oldest and most significant mosques in the city. The mosque has been destroyed in the Syrian Civil War but is being restored.

Natural Attractions of Aleppo: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Al-Shahbaa Park: A beautiful public park in Aleppo, Al-Shahbaa Park is an ideal destination for a leisurely afternoon walk. The park offers well-manicured gardens, fountains, and a small lake.

  • Mount Simeon: A stunning mountain located just outside of Aleppo, Mount Simeon offers breathtaking views of the city and surrounding wilderness.

  • Queiq River: A prominent river in Aleppo, the Queiq River is a popular destination for those who want to go fishing or enjoy a picnic by the river banks.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Aleppo

Aleppo has many hiking and biking routes that offer breathtaking views of the city and the surrounding countryside. Here are some of the best routes:

  • Queiq Trail: A scenic trail that follows the Queiq River, the Queiq Trail is an ideal destination for hikers and nature lovers.

  • Mount Simeon Trail: A challenging but rewarding hike, the Mount Simeon Trail offers stunning views of the city and surrounding mountains.

  • Countryside Biking Trail: A leisurely biking route that takes you through the countryside surrounding Aleppo, the Countryside Biking Trail is an ideal way to explore the rural beauty of the region.

Best Things to Do in Aleppo: Activities and Tours

Apart from the places mentioned above, Aleppo offers visitors a wide range of activities and tours. Here are some of the best things to do in Aleppo:

  • Take a walking tour of the city: A guided walking tour of Aleppo is the perfect way to explore the city’s hidden gems and learn about its rich history and culture.

  • Visit a hammam: A traditional Turkish bath, a hammam is a must-visit destination for those seeking relaxation and rejuvenation.

  • Sample local cuisine: Aleppo is renowned for its food, so make sure you sample some of the local dishes, such as kebabs, kibbeh, and fattoush.

Food and Drink Scene of Aleppo

Aleppo is renowned for its food, which is a fusion of Turkish and Mediterranean cuisine. Here are some must-try dishes and beverages in Aleppo:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Kebabs: Grilled meat skewers, usually served with bread and vegetables.

  • Kibbeh: A popular Levantine dish made of bulgur and minced meat.

  • Fattoush: A refreshing salad made with fresh vegetables and herbs.

  • Ma’amoul: A sweet pastry filled with dates or nuts.

Local Beverages

  • Arak: An anise-flavored alcoholic drink that is popular in the Middle East.

  • Sahlab: A warm and soothing milk-based drink flavored with orchid root and cinnamon.

  • Karak tea: A popular tea in the Middle East, karak tea is a strong tea that is often served with spices and condensed milk.

Where to Stay in Aleppo: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Aleppo has a wide range of accommodation options that cater to all budgets. Here are some of the best options:

  • Beit Wakil: A luxurious guesthouse located in the heart of Aleppo’s historic district.

  • Al-Jdeide Inn: A budget-friendly hotel that offers clean and comfortable rooms.

  • Sheraton Aleppo: A five-star hotel that offers modern amenities and stunning views of the city.

What to Buy in Aleppo: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Aleppo is known for its textiles, ceramics, and handicrafts. Here are some of the best things to buy in Aleppo:

  • Damascus Steel: A type of steel that is known for its strength and durability, Damascus steel is a popular souvenir from Aleppo.

  • Aleppo Soap: A luxurious soap that is made using olive oil and laurel oil, Aleppo soap is renowned for its healing properties.

  • Handicrafts: Aleppo is renowned for its handicrafts, such as pottery, textiles, and glassware.

Local Culture and Customs of Aleppo

Aleppo has a rich cultural heritage, and visitors must be respectful of local customs and traditions. Here are some important things to keep in mind:

  • Dress Modestly: Most Syrians dress conservatively, so visitors should avoid wearing revealing outfits.

  • Show Respect in Religious Places: Visitors should show respect when visiting religious places, such as mosques and churches, by covering up and removing their shoes.

  • Greet People Respectfully: Visitors should greet people respectfully, using phrases such as “salam alaykum” (peace be upon you).

Best Time to Visit Aleppo

The best time to visit Aleppo is during the spring (March to May) and fall (September to November) months, when the weather is mild, and the crowds are relatively low.

Do’s and Don’ts of Aleppo

Here are some important do’s and don’ts when visiting Aleppo:

Do:

  • Try the Local Cuisine: Aleppo is renowned for its food, so make sure you sample some of the local dishes.

  • Be Respectful: Respect local customs and traditions.

  • Learn Some Arabic: Learning some basic Arabic phrases can go a long way in connecting with locals.

Don’t:

  • Photograph Military or Government Buildings: It is illegal to take photographs of military or government buildings in Syria.

  • Drink Tap Water: Visitors should avoid drinking tap water and opt for bottled water instead.

  • Ignore Local Customs: Visitors should make an effort to learn about local customs and traditions and show respect for them.

Safety and Health

Safety in Aleppo

While the security situation in Aleppo has improved in recent years, visitors should still exercise caution and avoid the eastern districts of the city. Visitors should also be aware of the risk of unexploded landmines in certain areas of the city.

Health in Aleppo

Visitors should ensure that they are up to date on their vaccinations before traveling to Aleppo. It is also essential to bring any necessary medication, such as anti-malarial tablets or prescription medication.

Getting to Aleppo: Transportation Options and Logistics

Aleppo is well-connected by air and road.

Airports Near Aleppo

The closest airport to Aleppo is the Aleppo International Airport. However, due to the Syrian Civil War, the airport is currently closed.

The nearest airport to Aleppo is the Adana Sakirpasa Airport in Turkey, which is approximately 180 kilometers away.

Bus Stations Near Aleppo

There are daily buses to and from Aleppo from major cities in Syria, including Damascus and Homs.

Other Options

Visitors can also travel to Aleppo by train from Damascus.

How to Get Around in Aleppo

The most convenient way to get around Aleppo is by taxi. However, visitors should agree on a price before starting their journey, as taxis in Aleppo do not have meters. Visitors can also opt for public buses or hire a car.

Where to Go Next from Aleppo: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Here are some nearby destinations that are worth visiting from Aleppo:

  • Hama: A city renowned for its water wheels and ancient mosques.

  • Latakia: A coastal city that offers stunning beaches and seaside resorts.

  • Crac des Chevaliers: A medieval castle that is one of the most well-preserved in the world.

Conclusion

Aleppo is a beautiful city that offers visitors a unique blend of history, culture, and natural beauty. With this comprehensive guide, you can plan your itinerary and ensure that you don’t miss out on any of the city’s hidden gems. So, pack your bags and get ready to explore this enchanting city!
