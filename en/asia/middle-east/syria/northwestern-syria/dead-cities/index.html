Dead Cities - A Comprehensive Travel Guide

Dead Cities, located in Northwestern Syria, is an ancient archaeological site that dates back to the Byzantine era. The site comprises 700 abandoned settlements and is a testament to the area’s rich history and culture. The Dead Cities site is truly one of the wonders of the world and must be on every traveler’s bucket list. In this comprehensive travel guide, we will explore the best places to visit, things to do, where to stay, and how to get around Dead Cities.

List of Places to Visit in Dead Cities

Dead Cities is a vast area with numerous sites to explore. Here are some must-visit places in Dead Cities:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Cyrrhus: An ancient city that dates back to the Hellenistic period
  • Apamea: A vast ancient city that was once conquered by Alexander the Great
  • Maarat al-Numan: A city that dates back to the Byzantine era and was once an important commercial center
  • Serjilla: A former Roman city that was later inhabited by the Byzantines

Natural Attractions of Dead Cities: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Aleppo Citadel: A massive fortress that offers stunning views of the city
  • Mount Simeon: A mountain that offers a breathtaking view of the surrounding area
  • Lake Assad: A massive lake that offers numerous opportunities for boating and other outdoor activities

Hiking/Biking Routes of Dead Cities

Dead Cities is a popular destination for hikers and bikers. There are plenty of scenic routes that offer breathtaking views of the surrounding area. Some popular routes include:

  • The Apamea Trail: A 15-kilometer trail that offers stunning views of the ancient city of Apamea
  • The Aleppo Trail: A 20-kilometer trail that takes you through the Aleppo countryside
  • The Serjilla Trail: A 10-kilometer trail that offers stunning views of the Serjilla ruins.

Best Things to Do in Dead Cities: Activities and Tours

There are plenty of things to do in Dead Cities, from exploring the ancient ruins to enjoying the local cuisine. Here are some of the best things to do:

  • Visit the archaeological sites of Apamea and Cyrrhus
  • Take a hot-air balloon ride over Mount Simeon for an unforgettable experience
  • Go hiking or biking on one of the trails mentioned above
  • Take a tour of the Dead Cities site to learn more about its history and culture.

Food and Drink Scene of Dead Cities

Dead Cities is known for its delicious cuisine, which combines traditional Middle Eastern and Syrian flavors. Here are some must-try dishes:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Mujadara: A dish made of lentils, rice, and onions
  • Kebab: Grilled meat on skewers
  • Fatayer: A pastry filled with spinach or meat

Local Beverages

  • Arabic coffee: A strong black coffee flavored with cardamom
  • Arak: An anise-flavored alcoholic beverage

Where to Stay in Dead Cities

There are several options for accommodation in Dead Cities, ranging from hotels to guesthouses. Here are some popular options:

  • Dar Talama: A guesthouse located in Maarat al-Numan
  • Emir Palace Hotel: A luxurious hotel in the city of Aleppo
  • Serjilla Guest House: A guesthouse in the ancient city of Serjilla

What to Buy in Dead Cities: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Dead Cities is known for its handmade crafts and souvenirs. Here are some popular items to bring home:

  • Handmade ceramics
  • Handmade silk scarves
  • Spices and herbs

Local Culture and Customs of Dead Cities

Dead Cities is a conservative area, and visitors are advised to dress modestly and respect local customs. It is also important to note that alcohol is not widely available and should not be consumed in public.

Best Time to Visit Dead Cities

The best time to visit Dead Cities is during the spring and fall, when the weather is mild and pleasant. Summer can be very hot, while winter can be cold and rainy.

Do’s and Don’ts of Dead Cities

Do:

  • Dress modestly to respect local customs.
  • Learn some basic Arabic phrases to communicate with the locals.
  • Take a guided tour of the Dead Cities site to learn more about its history and culture.

Don’t:

  • Drink alcohol in public.
  • Take photographs of people without their permission.
  • Show disrespect towards local customs and traditions.

Safety and Health

Safety in Dead Cities

Syria is currently experiencing political instability, and visitors are advised to check travel advisories before planning a trip. It is also important to be aware of your surroundings and take appropriate safety precautions.

Health in Dead Cities

Visitors are advised to take necessary health precautions before traveling to Dead Cities. It is recommended to get vaccinated and bring a basic first aid kit. It is also important to stay hydrated and avoid tap water.

Visitors can get around Dead Cities by using public transportation, pre-booked tours, or hiring a private car or taxi.
