Introduction to Khasab

Khasab is a picturesque city located in the Musandam Peninsula, in Northern Oman. It is known for its towering cliffs, crystal-clear waters, and traditional dhow cruises that offer stunning views of nearby fjords. Khasab is also a great place to experience Omani culture, with numerous museums and traditional markets.

List of Places to Visit in Khasab

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Khasab Castle: Built in the 17th century, this fort served as a military stronghold and is now home to a museum showcasing traditional Omani artefacts.
  • Musandam Peninsula: Explore the traditional architecture, stunning views of the fjords, and blend of ancient and modern culture in this region.
  • Khasab Friday Market: This traditional market is located on the outskirts of the city and features local produce, handmade items, and more.

Natural Attractions of Khasab: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Jebel Harim: This mountain is the highest peak in Musandam and offers panoramic views of the surrounding region.
  • Khawr Sham: This stunning deep-water bay is a great place to spot dolphins and explore the local marine life.
  • Telegraph Island: This small rocky island was used for sending telegraph messages between India and London in the late 19th century.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Khasab

  • Wadi Khasab: This hiking trail offers stunning views of the surrounding valley and is known for its lush greenery and freshwater pools.
  • Jebel Harim: This popular hiking route offers scenic views of the Musandam Peninsula.

Best Things to Do in Khasab: Activities and Tours

  • Dhow Cruises: Explore the pristine waters and stunning fjords of Khasab by taking a traditional Omani dhow cruise.
  • Snorkelling and Diving: Explore the colourful marine life of Khasab’s waters.
  • Camping: Experience the natural beauty of the Musandam Peninsula by camping under the stars.

Week-Long Itinerary for Khasab

Day Activities
1 Explore Khasab Castle and the surrounding area

Food and Drink Scene of Khasab

Must-Try Dishes

  • Machboos: A traditional rice dish cooked with lamb or chicken, tomatoes, onions, and an array of spices.
  • Shuwa: A tender meat dish that is marinated in spices and cooked in an underground oven.

Local Beverages

  • Kahwa: A traditional Omani coffee made with cardamom and served with dates.
  • Laban: A refreshing yogurt drink that is commonly served with meals.

Where to Stay in Khasab: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Atana Khasab: A modern 4-star hotel that offers stunning views of the surrounding mountains and waters.
  • Khasab Hotel: A traditional 3-star hotel located in the heart of Khasab city.
  • Khasab Guest House: A great option for budget travellers, this guesthouse offers comfortable rooms and a friendly atmosphere.

What to Buy in Khasab: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Dates: Oman is known for its high-quality dates, which are available in a variety of flavours.
  • Silver Jewelry: Traditional Omani silver jewelry is a great souvenir to bring home.

Local Culture and Customs of Khasab

  • Dress modestly when visiting religious sites and mosques.
  • Avoid consuming alcohol in public.
  • Respect local customs and traditions.

Best Time to Visit Khasab

The best time to visit Khasab is from October to March when the weather is mild and comfortable for exploring the outdoors.

Do’s and Don’ts of Khasab

Do:

  • Respect local customs and traditions.
  • Drink plenty of water to stay hydrated in the desert heat.
  • Be prepared for moderate to high UV rays.

Don’t:

  • Photograph people without their permission.
  • Consume alcohol in public.
  • Wear revealing clothing that may be inappropriate for the local culture.

Safety and Health

Safety in Khasab

Khasab is generally a safe destination for tourists. However, travellers should always be aware of their surroundings and take precautions to prevent theft and scams.

Health in Khasab

Travellers should be up to date on routine vaccinations and consider getting a Hepatitis A and Typhoid vaccine. It is also important to carry any necessary prescription medication.

How to Get Around in Khasab

Taxis are readily available in Khasab, and many tourist attractions are located within walking distance of each other.

Where to Go Next from Khasab: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Muscat: Oman’s capital city is just a short flight from Khasab and offers a wealth of historical and cultural attractions.
  • Dubai: Just a few hours’ drive from Khasab, Dubai is known for its shopping, luxury resorts, and modern architecture.

Conclusion

With its stunning natural beauty, rich cultural heritage, and variety of outdoor activities, Khasab is a must-visit destination for travellers looking to explore Oman’s hidden gems.
