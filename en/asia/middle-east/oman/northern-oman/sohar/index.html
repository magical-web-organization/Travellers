Introduction to Sohar

Located in the Northern region of Oman, Sohar is a charming and historic city that is quickly becoming a popular destination for travelers seeking a quieter retreat in the Middle East. Sohar is known for its rich history, cultural significance, natural beauty, and modern developments.

With this comprehensive travel guide, you can explore the hidden gems of Sohar and plan your perfect vacation in this enchanting city.

List of Places to Visit in Sohar

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Sohar is steeped in history and culture, with many museums and historical sites worth visiting.

  1. Sohar Fort - one of the top tourist attractions in the city, the Sohar Fort dates back to the 13th century and was once an important trading center.
  2. Maritime Museum - learn about Oman’s seafaring history and the importance of the sea to the country’s economy and culture.
  3. Falaj al-Qabail - one of the oldest and largest falaj systems in Sohar, this water distribution channel was built in 2500 BC and is still utilized to irrigate the surrounding farms and plantations.
  4. Sohar Handicrafts Center - showcases the traditional arts and crafts of the region including pottery, weaving, and silverwork.

Natural Attractions of Sohar: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Sohar is also home to breathtaking natural attractions that are perfect for outdoor adventures and scenic views.

  1. Sohar Beach - a pristine beach with clear waters that is great for swimming, sunbathing, and picnicking.
  2. Jebel Akhdar Mountains - a mountain range that’s known for its rugged terrain and stunning views, and popular for hiking and trekking.
  3. Al Hajir Mountains - consists of rugged peaks, deep canyons, and picturesque valleys that offer a range of outdoor activities including camping, off-roading, and mountain biking.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Sohar

Sohar is a great destination for hiking and biking enthusiasts, with several routes that cater to different levels and preferences.

  1. Sohar Corniche - a 7-kilometer paved multi-use trail that is perfect for cyclists and walkers. The trail provides scenic views of the city’s coastline.
  2. Wadi Al Jizi - a natural reserve that offers some of the best hiking trails in Sohar. The trails lead to a stunning waterfall and freshwater pools where you can take a refreshing swim.
  3. Jebel Akhdar Trails - the mountain range provides a range of hiking trails that offer breathtaking views and a glimpse into Oman’s natural beauty.

Best Things to Do in Sohar: Activities and Tours

Sohar has plenty of activities and tours that cater to different interests and preferences.

  1. Dolphin watching - embark on a boat trip to observe the playful dolphins on Oman’s coast.
  2. Dhow Cruise - an evening boat ride on a traditional Omani dhow that offers scenic views of the city skyline.
  3. Omani cooking class - learn how to cook traditional Omani dishes with a local chef.
  4. Stargazing - head out to Al Hajir Mountains for a tour that offers an unmissable stargazing experience.

Week-Long Itinerary for Sohar

If you’re planning a week-long vacation in Sohar, here’s a recommended itinerary:

Day 1-2: Explore Sohar city and its cultural and historical attractions Day 3: Hiking and biking in the Wadi Al Jizi natural reserve Day 4: Visit Al Hajir Mountains and experience outdoor activities like camping or off-roading Day 5: Relax on Sohar Beach and enjoy the scenic views Day 6: Take a boat tour or go dolphin watching Day 7: Head out to Jebel Akhdar Mountains for hiking trails and scenic views

Food and Drink Scene of Sohar

Sohar offers a wide range of local and international cuisine, with seafood as the main highlight.

Must-Try Dishes

  1. Shuwa - a slow-cooked lamb or goat meat that is seasoned with spices and served with rice and vegetables.
  2. Harees - a traditional Omani stew made with wheat, meat, and spices
  3. Machboos - a rice dish with meat or fish, seasoned with spices and saffron.

Local Beverages

  1. Kahwa - a traditional Omani coffee that is flavored with cardamom and dates.
  2. Laban - a refreshing yogurt drink that is a popular choice to beat the Omani heat.

Where to Stay in Sohar: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Sohar has a range of accommodation options that cater to different budgets and preferences.

  1. Crowne Plaza Sohar - a 5-star hotel with luxurious amenities and excellent customer service.
  2. Al Wadi Hotel Sohar - a mid-range hotel that offers comfortable rooms and an outdoor pool.
  3. Sohar Beach Hotel - a beachfront hotel that offers spacious rooms with sea views.

What to Buy in Sohar: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Sohar is known for its exquisite handicrafts and artifacts that make great souvenirs.

  1. Silverwork - Sohar has a rich history of silverwork, and you can find traditional silver jewelry and decorative items at the Sohar Handicrafts Center.
  2. Pottery - Sohar has a thriving pottery industry that produces traditional and modern designs crafted by local artisans.

Local Culture and Customs of Sohar

Oman is a conservative Muslim country, and visitors should respect the local culture and customs. Modest dressing is recommended, and alcohol consumption is only permitted at designated places.

Best Time to Visit Sohar

The best time to visit Sohar is from November to April, as the weather is mild and pleasant during this time. Summers in Sohar can be extremely hot, with temperatures reaching up to 40 degrees Celsius.

Do’s and Don’ts of Sohar

Do:

  1. Dress modestly.
  2. Respect local customs and practices.
  3. Try traditional Omani food.

Don’t:

  1. Engage in public displays of affection
  2. Drink alcohol in public
  3. Disrespect the local religion and traditions

Safety and Health

Safety in Sohar

Sohar is generally a safe city for tourists. Petty crime, such as pickpocketing and theft, can occur in crowded tourist areas, so it is recommended to take precautions such as not carrying large sums of cash and keeping valuable items secure.

Health in Sohar

Oman has excellent health care facilities, and there are several hospitals and clinics in Sohar that provide medical care to locals and tourists.

Getting to Sohar: Transportation Options and Logistics

Sohar is easily accessible by air, road, and sea.

Airports Near Sohar

  1. Sohar International Airport - located in the city center and connects to several domestic and international destinations.

Bus Stations Near Sohar

  1. Sohar Bus Station - serves local and regional destinations.
  2. Al Buraimi Bus Station - located near the Oman-UAE border and connects Sohar to other cities in Oman and the UAE.

Other Options

  1. Car Rental - Several car rentals are available in Sohar city.
  2. Taxis - Taxis are readily available in the city.

How to Get Around in Sohar

The best way to get around Sohar is by car or taxi. You can also use public transportation like buses or hire a private driver.

Where to Go Next from Sohar: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

If you have time, explore these nearby destinations from Sohar.

  1. Al Rustaq - a charming city known for its hot springs and old forts.
  2. Muscat - Oman’s capital city and a vibrant cultural hub.
  3. Al-Hoota Cave - a stunning underground cave that offers a unique geological experience.

Conclusion

Sohar is a hidden gem in Oman that offers visitors a chance to experience the country’s rich culture, history, and natural beauty.
