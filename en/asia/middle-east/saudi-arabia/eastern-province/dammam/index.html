Introduction to Dammam

Dammam is the capital of the Eastern Province and one of Saudi Arabia’s largest cities, located in the heart of the Arabian Gulf. It is a rapidly growing industrial and commercial hub, with a rich history and culture that dates back to ancient times.

The city is renowned for its beautiful beaches, vibrant nightlife, and bustling markets. Dammam is also an excellent base for exploring the region’s natural beauty and cultural attractions.

Whether you’re interested in history, adventure, or shopping, Dammam has something to offer everyone. In this comprehensive travel guide, we’ll show you the best places to visit, things to do, and where to stay in Dammam.

List of Places to Visit in Dammam

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Dammam National Museum

The Dammam National Museum is one of the city’s most popular cultural attractions, located in the heart of the city. It is home to several permanent exhibitions showcasing the region’s history and culture, including traditional costumes, artifacts, and old photographs. The museum is a must-visit for anyone interested in Saudi Arabia’s rich heritage.

Tarout Castle

Tarout Castle is a historical fortress that dates back to the 16th century. Located on the island of Tarout, just a short boat ride from Dammam, the castle offers stunning views of the Arabian Gulf and the Eastern Province. It is a popular spot for photography enthusiasts and history buffs.

Natural Attractions of Dammam: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Half Moon Beach

Half Moon Beach is one of Dammam’s most stunning natural attractions. It is a crescent-shaped bay that stretches for more than 2.5 kilometers and is surrounded by crystal-clear waters. Visitors can swim, sunbathe, or indulge in water sports such as jet-skiing or parasailing.

King Fahd Park

King Fahd Park is a sprawling green space in the heart of Dammam, covering more than 3 million square meters. It is home to several man-made lakes, colorful gardens, and a range of recreational facilities. Visitors can have picnics, go boating, or enjoy a leisurely walk around the park.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Dammam

Al Danah Hiking Trail

Al Danah Hiking Trail is a scenic route through the majestic Al Danah Plateau, located just outside of Dammam. The trail takes hikers through stunning natural scenery, including hills, valleys, and rocky outcrops. It is suitable for experienced hikers and beginners alike.

Al Khobar Corniche

The Al Khobar Corniche is a scenic coastal park in the city of Al Khobar, just a short drive from Dammam. It is a popular spot for biking and jogging, with several designated paths that take visitors along the picturesque Gulf shoreline.

Best Things to Do in Dammam: Activities and Tours

Desert Safari

A desert safari is a must-do activity for any adventure-seeker in Dammam. Visitors can take a guided tour of the desert landscape, including sand dunes, oases, and traditional Bedouin villages. The tours usually include a stopover for a traditional Arabic dinner and entertainment.

Shopping at Al Danah Mall

Al Danah Mall is one of Dammam’s most popular shopping destinations, with more than 400 stores selling everything from luxury fashion brands to electronics and home goods. The mall also features several restaurants, cafes, and entertainment options, including a cinema complex.

Week-Long Itinerary for Dammam

Day 1: Visit the Dammam National Museum and explore the local souks.

Day 2: Take a desert safari tour and enjoy a traditional Arabic dinner in the desert.

Day 3: Spend the day exploring Half Moon Beach and indulge in water sports activities.

Day 4: Discover the historical sites of Tarout Castle on the island of Tarout.

Day 5: Enjoy the greenery of King Fahd Park and take a leisurely walk around the park.

Day 6: Go shopping at Al Danah Mall and treat yourself to some luxury goods.

Day 7: Take a tour of the Al Danah Plateau and its surrounding natural attractions.

Food and Drink Scene of Dammam

Must-Try Dishes

Kabsa

Kabsa is the national dish of Saudi Arabia and is widely popular in Dammam. It is a spicy rice dish flavored with saffron and served with chicken, beef, or lamb.

Shawarma

Shawarma is a popular Middle Eastern sandwich filled with grilled meat, vegetables, and spices. It is a staple street food in Dammam and is available everywhere.

Local Beverages

Arabic Coffee

Arabic coffee is a traditional beverage in Saudi Arabia, made by roasting green coffee beans and brewing them with spices and cardamom. It is usually served with dates and is a symbol of hospitality in Saudi culture.

Laban

Laban is a popular yogurt-based drink in the region, flavored with mint or cardamom. It is a refreshing drink that is ideal for hot summer days.

Where to Stay in Dammam: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Luxury Hotels

Sheraton Dammam Hotel and Convention Centre

The Sheraton Dammam Hotel and Convention Centre is a five-star hotel located in the heart of the city. It features luxurious rooms, world-class dining options, and a range of leisure facilities, including a spa, fitness center, and an outdoor swimming pool.

Golden Tulip Dammam Corniche Hotel

The Golden Tulip Dammam Corniche Hotel is a four-star hotel that offers stunning views of the Arabian Gulf. It features spacious rooms, a rooftop restaurant, and a range of business facilities.

Budget Hotels

Casablanca Hotel

Casablanca Hotel is a budget-friendly hotel located in the heart of Dammam. It features cozy rooms, free Wi-Fi, and a range of dining options within walking distance.

Al Gosaibi Hotel

Al Gosaibi Hotel is a budget-friendly hotel located in the city’s commercial district. It features comfortable rooms, free Wi-Fi, and a restaurant serving local and international cuisine.

What to Buy in Dammam: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Dates

Dates are a popular souvenir in Saudi Arabia, and Dammam is no exception. The city is renowned for its high-quality dates, which are available in local markets and souks.

Traditional Clothing

Traditional clothing, such as thobes and abayas, are also popular souvenirs in Dammam. They are available in a range of styles and designs and can be found in local souks and markets.

Local Culture and Customs of Dammam

Saudi Arabia is a conservative country with a strong Islamic culture. Visitors to Dammam should dress modestly and observe local customs and traditions, such as avoiding public displays of affection and respecting prayer times.

Best Time to Visit Dammam

The best time to visit Dammam is during the winter months, from December to February, when temperatures are mild and pleasant. Summer temperatures can be unbearably hot, reaching over 40 degrees Celsius, making outdoor activities challenging.

Do’s and Don’ts of Dammam

Do:

  • Dress modestly
  • Respect local customs and traditions
  • Be polite and courteous

Don’t:

  • Engage in public displays of affection
  • Consume alcohol in public places
  • Take photographs of local people without their permission

Safety and Health

Safety in Dammam

Dammam is a relatively safe city, but visitors should take basic precautions, such as avoiding deserted areas at night and keeping valuables out of sight.

Health in Dammam

Visitors should take standard health precautions, such as drinking bottled water, avoiding uncooked food, and ensuring they have up-to-date vaccinations.

Getting to Dammam: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Dammam

Dammam is served by the King Fahd International Airport, which is located approximately 20 kilometers northwest of the city center. It is the largest airport in Saudi Arabia and is served by several international airlines.

Bus Stations Near Dammam

The Dammam Bus Station is located in the city center and is the main hub for public transportation. It offers services to several destinations across Saudi Arabia, including Riyadh and Jeddah.

Other Options

Taxis and car rentals are readily available in Dammam, and visitors can use ride-sharing applications such as Uber or Careem to get around the city.

How to Get Around in Dammam

The best way to get around Dammam is by car, as public transportation can be limited, especially at night. Taxis are readily available but can be expensive, and ride-sharing applications such as Uber or Careem are also available.

Where to Go Next from Dammam: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Al Khobar

Al Khobar is a neighboring city of Dammam, located just a short drive away. It is home to several shopping areas, as well as the picturesque Al Khobar Corniche.

Jubail

Jubail is a coastal town located approximately 100 kilometers north of Dammam. It is renowned for its pristine beaches and is an excellent destination for a day trip or a weekend getaway.

Conclusion

Dammam is a fascinating city with a rich cultural heritage and stunning natural scenery. Whether you’re interested in history, adventure, or shopping, Dammam has something to offer everyone. We hope this comprehensive travel guide has inspired you to visit this exciting destination and explore all that it has to offer.
