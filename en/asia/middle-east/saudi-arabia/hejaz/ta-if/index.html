Introduction to Ta’if

Ta’if is a city located in the Hejaz region of Saudi Arabia, which is famous for its natural beauty, cultural heritage, and outdoor activities. It lies at an altitude of 1700 meters above sea level, and is surrounded by mountains, valleys, and orchards. Ta’if is known as the city of roses, as the region produces the rarest types of fragrant roses, which are used to make perfumes, rose water, and other products.

List of Places to Visit in Ta’if

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • The Shubra Palace: This palace was built in the 20th century and was the summer residence of the ruling family of Ta’if for many years. It features beautiful architecture and is surrounded by gardens and fountains.

  • Ta’if Regional Museum: This museum contains a collection of artifacts and displays that showcase the rich history and culture of the Hejaz region, including the ancient Lihyanite Kingdom.

  • Al Misan Museum: This museum houses a vast collection of vintage cars, traditional arts, and crafts, including ceramics, pottery, and textiles.

Natural Attractions of Ta’if: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Al Rudaf Park: This park is located on the outskirts of Ta’if and offers stunning views of the surrounding mountains and valleys. Visitors can enjoy picnics, camping, hiking, and outdoor activities.

  • Al Hada Mountain: This mountain is located to the southwest of Ta’if and is a popular destination for hiking, biking, and camping. It offers breathtaking views of the city and the surrounding areas.

  • Wadi Mitna: This is a beautiful valley located in the west of Ta’if and is known for its natural beauty and scenic views. Visitors can hike, bike, or simply relax and enjoy the peaceful surroundings.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Ta’if

Ta’if offers a wide range of hiking and biking trails that cater to all levels of experience. Some of the popular routes include:

  • Al Hada Mountain Trail: This trail is 12 km long and is located on the Al Hada Mountain. It offers scenic views and is suitable for experienced hikers.

  • Wadi Qarn Trail: This trail is 6 km long and starts from the main road of Al Hada Mountain. It leads to Wadi Qarn and offers beautiful scenery and an opportunity to see rare plants and animals.

  • Shafa Route: This route is located in the Al Shafa area and is 10 km long. It is suitable for experienced hikers and offers stunning views of the city and the mountains.

Best Things to Do in Ta’if: Activities and Tours

There are many exciting activities and tours to do in Ta’if, including:

  • Roses Farm Tour: Visitors can take a tour of the rose farms, learn about the process of making rose water and other products, and shop for unique souvenirs.

  • Paragliding: Visitors can go paragliding and enjoy the beautiful views of Ta’if from above.

  • Off-Road Tours: Visitors can take an off-road tour of the desert, explore the sand dunes, and experience the thrill of dune bashing.

Week-Long Itinerary for Ta’if

Day 1: Visit the Ta’if Regional Museum and Al Misan Museum

Day 2: Go on a hiking tour of Al Hada Mountain

Day 3: Take a tour of the rose farms and buy unique souvenirs

Day 4: Explore the Shubra Palace and enjoy a picnic in Al Rudaf Park

Day 5: Take a paragliding tour and enjoy the stunning views of Ta’if from above

Day 6: Go on an off-road tour of the desert and experience dune bashing

Day 7: Relax in a local hammam and enjoy traditional Arabic spa treatments

Food and Drink Scene of Ta’if

Must-Try Dishes

  • Albaik: This is a local fast-food chain that serves delicious fried chicken and seafood.

  • Kabssa: This is a traditional Saudi Arabian dish made with rice, meat, and spices.

  • Harees: This is a porridge-like dish made with wheat and meat, and is usually served during Ramadan.

Local Beverages

  • Qahwa: This is a traditional Arabic coffee that is served with dates and is popular throughout Saudi Arabia.

  • Sahlab: This is a sweet and creamy drink made with milk, sugar, and orchid root.

  • Jallab: This is a refreshing drink made with grape molasses, rose water, and pine nuts.

Where to Stay in Ta’if: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Ta’if offers a range of accommodation options, including luxurious hotels, budget-friendly hostels, and cozy guesthouses. Some popular options include:

  • Le Meridien Al Hada: This is a luxurious hotel located on the Al Hada Mountain, offering stunning views and world-class amenities.

  • Boudl Taif: This is a budget-friendly hotel located in the heart of Ta’if, offering comfortable rooms and excellent services.

  • Al Ward Al Maki Hotel: This is a guesthouse located in the Al Ward Village, offering cozy rooms and warm hospitality.

What to Buy in Ta’if: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Fragrant Roses: Ta’if is famous for its fragrant roses, which are used to make perfumes, rose water, and other products.

  • Traditional Arabic Attire: Visitors can buy traditional Arabic clothes, including abayas, thobes, and hijabs, as souvenirs.

  • Local Souvenirs: Visitors can buy unique souvenirs, including handmade pottery, rugs, and carpets, as well as local spices and dates.

Local Culture and Customs of Ta’if

  • Attire: Visitors are expected to dress modestly and respect local customs and traditions.

  • Prayer Times: Visitors should be aware of the local prayer times and avoid visiting mosques during prayer times.

  • Social Etiquette: Visitors should be respectful of local customs, including the use of the right hand for eating and greeting, and avoiding physical contact with members of the opposite sex in public places.

Best Time to Visit Ta’if

The best time to visit Ta’if is during the months of March to May, when the weather is mild and comfortable. The rose festival also takes place during this time, offering visitors a chance to experience the fragrant beauty of the region.

Do’s and Don’ts of Ta’if

Do:

  • Respect local customs and traditions.

  • Dress modestly and appropriately.

  • Try local food and drinks.

Don’t:

  • Public displays of affection are not allowed.

  • Photography of local women is not allowed without permission.

  • Alcohol is strictly prohibited in public places.

Safety and Health

Safety in Ta’if

Ta’if is a safe city to visit, but visitors should take necessary precautions, including:

  • Avoid deserted areas at night.

  • Beware of pickpocketing and scams.

  • Follow the local traffic rules and avoid reckless driving.

Health in Ta’if

Visitors should be aware of the extreme weather conditions in Ta’if, including the hot and dry summers and the cold winters. Visitors should also take necessary precautions against heatstroke and dehydration during the summer months.

Getting to Ta’if: Transportation Options and Logistics

Ta’if is easily accessible by air, bus, or car. Some popular options include:

Airports Near Ta’if

  • Ta’if International Airport: This is the main airport in Ta’if and is located 30 km from the city center.

Bus Stations Near Ta’if

  • Ta’if Central Bus Station: This is the main bus station in Ta’if and offers bus services to various destinations in Saudi Arabia.

Other Options

  • Visitors can also rent a car or hire a taxi to get to Ta’if from other destinations in Saudi Arabia.

How to Get Around in Ta’if

The best way to get around in Ta’if is by taxi or car. Visitors can also use public transportation, including buses and mini-buses, to get around the city.

Where to Go Next from Ta’if: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Some popular destinations to visit near Ta’if include:

  • Mecca: This is a holy city for Muslims and is located about 80 km from Ta’if.

  • Jeddah: This is a coastal city located about 170 km from Ta’if, and offers a range of attractions, including beaches, museums, and shopping malls.

  • Al Wahba Crater: This is a stunning natural attraction located near the city of Makkah, and is a popular destination for hiking and camping.

Conclusion

Ta’if is a culturally rich and naturally diverse city that offers a range of attractions and activities for visitors. It is a perfect destination for those who want to experience the beauty of Saudi Arabia and learn about its rich history and traditions.
