Introduction to Jeddah

Jeddah, located in the Hejaz region of Saudi Arabia, is one of the largest and most cosmopolitan cities in the country. Known for its stunning beaches, rich culture, and historic landmarks, Jeddah attracts travelers from all over the world.

List of Places to Visit in Jeddah

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Al Tayebat International City: A museum that showcases the history and culture of Saudi Arabia.
  • Jeddah Corniche: A popular spot for locals and tourists alike, the Corniche is a beautiful waterfront promenade that stretches for miles.
  • Al Balad: Also known as Old Jeddah, this area is home to numerous historic buildings and traditional souks.

Natural Attractions of Jeddah: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Red Sea Mall: One of the largest shopping centers in Jeddah, featuring numerous designer stores and restaurants.
  • Al Shallal Theme Park: A popular amusement park offering a variety of rides and attractions.
  • Fakieh Aquarium: Home to over 200 species of marine life and the largest aquarium in Saudi Arabia.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Jeddah

  • Al Murjan Island Trail: A scenic coastal route that starts at the Sheraton Hotel and ends at the Al Murjan Island bridge.
  • Al Andalus Trail: A popular hiking and biking trail that offers stunning views of the Red Sea and the Jeddah skyline.
  • Al Tayebat Trail: A beautiful route that takes you through the heart of Old Jeddah, offering a glimpse into the city’s rich history and culture.

Best Things to Do in Jeddah: Activities and Tours

  • Scuba Diving and Snorkeling: Explore the beautiful coral reefs and marine life of the Red Sea.
  • Desert Safari: Experience the Arabian desert on a thrilling Jeep or camel ride.
  • City Tour: Learn about the history and culture of Jeddah on a guided tour that takes you to the city’s top attractions.

Week-Long Itinerary for Jeddah

Day Itinerary
1 Visit Al Balad and explore the traditional souks
2 Spend a day at the beach and enjoy some water sports
3 Take a guided city tour and visit the Jeddah Corniche
4 Explore the Fakieh Aquarium and attend a dolphin show
5 Hike the Al Tayebat Trail and visit Al Tayebat International City
6 Go on a desert safari and experience the Arabian desert
7 Relax at a spa or enjoy some shopping at the Red Sea Mall

Food and Drink Scene of Jeddah

Must-Try Dishes

  • Kabsa: A traditional Saudi dish made with rice, spices, and meat or chicken.
  • Shawarma: A popular Middle Eastern dish consisting of meat, vegetables, and sauces wrapped in a pita bread.
  • Falafel: A vegetarian dish made from ground chickpeas, spices, and herbs.

Local Beverages

  • Qahwa: A traditional Arabic coffee made with cardamom and served with dates.
  • Jallab: A refreshing drink made with grape molasses, rose water, and pine nuts.
  • Laban: A salty yogurt drink that is popular throughout the Middle East.

Where to Stay in Jeddah: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Jeddah offers a range of accommodation options to suit all budgets and preferences. Some of the most popular options include:

  • Jeddah Hilton: A luxurious hotel located on the Corniche, with stunning views of the Red Sea.
  • Red Sea Palace: A mid-range hotel located in the heart of Jeddah’s business district.
  • Al Balad Inn: A budget-friendly guesthouse located in Old Jeddah, offering traditional Arabian hospitality.

What to Buy in Jeddah: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Gold Jewelry: Jeddah is known for its gold souks, which offer a huge variety of gold jewelry at competitive prices.
  • Perfumes and Oud: Jeddah is famous for its Arabian perfumes and oud-based fragrances.
  • Spices and Dates: Jeddah is a hub for the spice trade in Saudi Arabia, with numerous souks selling a wide variety of spices and dates.

Local Culture and Customs of Jeddah

Saudi Arabia is a conservative country, and visitors are expected to dress modestly and adhere to local customs and traditions. Some key things to keep in mind when visiting Jeddah include:

  • Dress conservatively, especially in public places.
  • Avoid public displays of affection.
  • Respect the Islamic call to prayer and avoid making noise during prayer times.

Best Time to Visit Jeddah

The best time to visit Jeddah is during the winter months (November to February), when temperatures are mild and the weather is pleasant for outdoor activities.

Do’s and Don’ts of Jeddah

Do:

  • Learn some basic Arabic phrases to communicate with locals.
  • Respect local customs and traditions.
  • Try the local food and drinks.

Don’t:

  • Consume alcohol in public.
  • Take photographs of people without their permission.
  • Display affection in public.

Safety and Health

Safety in Jeddah

Jeddah is a safe city for travelers, with low levels of crime and violence. However, visitors should take precautions, such as avoiding walking alone at night and not carrying large amounts of cash.

Health in Jeddah

Visitors to Jeddah should be up to date on routine vaccinations, including measles, mumps, and rubella (MMR), and hepatitis A and B. Travelers should also take precautions to avoid mosquito-borne illnesses, such as dengue fever and malaria.

Getting to Jeddah: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Jeddah

  • King Abdulaziz International Airport: Located about 20 kilometers north of Jeddah, this airport is served by numerous international airlines.

Bus Stations Near Jeddah

  • Jeddah Central Bus Station: Located in the center of Jeddah, this bus station offers services to various destinations within Saudi Arabia.

Other Options

  • Train: The Haramain High-Speed Railway connects Jeddah with Mecca and Medina, providing a fast and convenient way to travel between these cities.
  • Car Rental: Visitors to Jeddah can rent a car from numerous international car rental companies, such as Hertz and Avis.

How to Get Around in Jeddah

  • Taxi: Taxis are abundant in Jeddah and can be hailed easily from the street or booked through a ride-hailing app.
  • Bus: Jeddah has an extensive network of public buses that connect various parts of the city.
  • Private Car: Car rental is a popular option for visitors who wish to explore Jeddah and the surrounding areas at their own pace.

Where to Go Next from Jeddah: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Mecca: The holiest city in Islam, located just 70 kilometers from Jeddah.
  • Medina: The second holiest city in Islam, located about 420 kilometers from Jeddah.
  • Taif: A beautiful mountain town located about 170 kilometers from Jeddah, known for its rose farms and mountain scenery.

Conclusion

Jeddah is a fascinating and diverse city, offering something for everyone. Whether you’re interested in history and culture, outdoor adventures, or shopping and dining, Jeddah has it all. With this comprehensive guide, you’ll be able to plan the perfect trip to Jeddah and make the most of your visit to this vibrant city.
