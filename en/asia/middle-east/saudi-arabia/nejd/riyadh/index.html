Introduction to Riyadh

Riyadh, located in the Nejd region of Saudi Arabia, is the capital and largest city of the country. The city is known for its rich history, Islamic architecture, and traditional Arabian culture. Riyadh attracts visitors from all over the world with its fascinating blend of old and new.

  3. Riyadh Gallery

Food and Drink Scene of Riyadh

Riyadh boasts a vibrant food and drink scene that offers a range of traditional Arabian and international cuisines. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages in Riyadh:

Must-Try Dishes

  1. Kabsa - a traditional Saudi Arabian rice dish with meat or chicken
  2. Shawarma - an Arabic wrap filled with roasted meat, vegetables, and sauces
  3. Mutabbaq - a popular street food made with thin dough and filled with meat or vegetables
  4. Thareed - a tomato-based stew served with bread
  5. Machboos - a spicy rice dish made with meat or fish

Local Beverages

  1. Arabic Coffee
  2. Jallab - a refreshing drink made with dates, grape molasses, and rose water
  3. Vimto - a popular fruit drink

Local Culture and Customs of Riyadh

Riyadh’s culture is deeply influenced by Islamic traditions, and visitors should be mindful of local customs and practices. Here are some tips on local culture and customs in Riyadh:

  • Dress conservatively, keeping your arms and legs covered while in public places.
  • Be respectful while visiting religious sites and mosques.
  • Avoid public displays of affection.
  • Ramadan is a holy month for Muslims, and visitors are expected to respect the customs and practices of the month.

Best Time to Visit Riyadh

The best time to visit Riyadh is during the winter months between November to February when the weather is pleasant, with temperatures ranging between 20 to 25 degrees Celsius. The summer months can be scorching and humid, with temperatures rising above 40 degrees Celsius.

Do’s and Don’ts in Riyadh

Here are some do’s and don’ts to keep in mind while visiting Riyadh:

Do:

  • Greet locals with “As-salamu alaykum” or “hello.”
  • Respect local customs and traditions.
  • Carry enough cash as card payments may not be accepted everywhere.
  • Haggle while shopping in local markets.

Don’t:

  • Drink alcohol in public places.
  • Smoke in public places.
  • Show disrespect towards the royal family or Islamic faith.
  • Photograph locals without their consent.

Safety and Health

While Riyadh is a safe city, visitors should be mindful of certain safety and health concerns. Here is some information on safety and health in Riyadh:

Safety in Riyadh

  • Avoid public demonstrations or political rallies.
  • Travel in groups, especially after sunset.
  • Stay aware of your surroundings and keep your belongings safe.

Health in Riyadh

  • Drink bottled water and avoid tap water.
  • Carry sufficient medication for any pre-existing medical conditions.
  • Apply sunscreen and wear protective clothing during outdoor activities to avoid heatstroke.
