Introduction to Khamis Mushayt

Located in Asir, Saudi Arabia, Khamis Mushayt is a city surrounded by mountains with marvelous scenic views, pleasant weather, and a rich cultural heritage. It is known as one of the oldest and most beautiful regions in the country, with a unique culture and a blend of hospitality that is unmatched anywhere else in the kingdom. In this travel guide, we will explore the top attractions, the food and drink, culture, and customs, transportation options, health and safety, and more in Khamis Mushayt.

List of Places to Visit in Khamis Mushayt

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Al Okhdood Archeological Site: Located 30km north of Khamis Mushayt, this UNESCO heritage site features the ruins of the ancient city of Dedan. It has beautifully preserved walls, gates, and tombs – a must-visit for history buffs.

  • Abu Mattar Almoothanna Palace: This castle is a gem of Asir architectural design, named after the leader of the Al-Muttahanna tribe. It is an excellent example of an authentic Asiri Castle, which is located at the highest point of Al-Masif, providing visitors with stunning panoramic views of the city.

  • Asir National Museum: This museum is the perfect place to explore the rich heritage and culture of Asir. It features over 15 galleries showcasing a range of artifacts, antiques, and traditional items that are both intriguing and informative.

Natural Attractions of Khamis Mushayt: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Jebel Aneiza: A majestic mountain range, home to numerous caves, valleys, and waterfalls. Aneiza is an ideal destination for nature lovers who want to experience the beauty of the Saudi Arabian countryside.

  • Asir National Park: Located on the outskirts of Khamis Mushayt, this park is rich in flora and fauna. It offers a chance to get up close with nature and a wide range of activities like hiking, camping, and bird watching.

  • Al Namas Dam: A man-made wonder located on the outskirts of Khamis Mushayt. Al Namas Dam offers scenic views, perfect for a picnic, and sightseeing. Visitors can also enjoy boating and fishing.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Khamis Mushayt

Khamis Mushayt is a hiker’s paradise, with breathtaking scenery, and rugged terrains. It provides numerous hiking and biking trails for adventure enthusiasts. The Al Ittihad Park is a famous park in the heart of the city, offering over five kilometers of well-maintained tracks that are great for walking, jogging, and cycling.

For more experienced hikers, there are more challenging routes, including the Aseer summit trail, providing the hiker with unparalleled views from the mountain’s peak at 3014 m above sea level. Visitors should have proper hiking gear when tackling these trails.

Best Things to Do in Khamis Mushayt: Activities and Tours

Here are the top things to do in Khamis Mushayt:

  • Visit the Al Okhdood Archeological Site.

  • Explore The Asir National Museum.

  • Take in the stunning views from the Asiri Palace of Abu Mattar Almoothanna.

  • Discover the beauty of Al Reservoir Park, located near the stunning Al Namas Dam.

  • Take a walk in the Al Ittihad Park.

  • Go on a bird-watching trip to the Al Soudah mountains.

Week-Long Itinerary for Khamis Mushayt

Here’s a week-long itinerary for Khamis Mushayt:

Day 1: Visit Al Okhdood Archeological Site, and Al Reservoir Park.

Day 2: Go to Asir National Park, the biggest national park in the region.

Day 3: Explore the Asir National Museum and Abu Mattar Almoothanna Palace.

Day 4: Take a walk in the Al Ittihad Park and visit the nearby Al Namas Dam.

Day 5: Drive to Jebel Aneiza, known for its stunning natural beauty.

Day 6: Hike up the Aseer Summit trail, which is the highest peak in the Asir region.

Day 7: Take a bird-watching trip to Al Soudah, Saudi Arabian mountains.

Food and Drink Scene of Khamis Mushayt

Must-Try Dishes

  • Foul: A traditional Saudi breakfast dish consists of fava beans cooked with spices and topped with olive oil, lemon juice, and parsley.

  • Jareesh: A savory dish made of cracked wheat cooked with either chicken meat or lamb.

  • Shakshouka: This flavorful dish features eggs poached in a sauce of tomatoes, onions, and peppers.

Local Beverages

  • Gahwa: A traditional Arabic coffee made of freshly roasted beans, served in a small cup with cardamom.

  • Jellab: A refreshing drink made of dates, raisins, and grape molasses, served cold with crushed ice.

What to Buy in Khamis Mushayt: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Khamis Mushayt has a rich culture with a wide range of unique products perfect for souvenir shopping. Here are some must-buy items:

  • Asir Honey: The Asir region has a long tradition of beekeeping known for its unique floral aroma.

  • Asir Handicrafts: Asir is regarded as the hub of traditional Saudi handicrafts like pottery, basket weaving, and weaving.

Local Culture and Customs of Khamis Mushayt

The Asiri people believe in simplicity and hospitality. They have a rich culture and are proud of their heritage, as displayed in their folklore, music, and dance. Visitors should also dress modestly in public places, and it is respectful to remove their shoes when entering a mosque or a private home.

Best Time to Visit Khamis Mushayt

The best time to visit Khamis Mushayt is from October to May when the weather is pleasant and mild. The summer months are hot, with temperatures reaching up to 45°C, and humidity is high.

Do’s and Don’ts of Khamis Mushayt

Do:

  • Respect local culture and customs.
  • Take off shoes when entering a mosque or someone’s home.
  • Try local cuisine and experience the Asiri hospitality.

Don’t:

  • Do not take pictures of people without their permission.
  • Do not engage in public display of affection.
  • Do not insult the local culture and customs.

Safety and Health

Safety in Khamis Mushayt

Khamis Mushayt is a relatively safe city; however, visitors should take basic precautions like avoiding unlit streets at night and keeping away from wild animals in remote areas. It is also important to keep track of valuables and keep them safe.

Health in Khamis Mushayt

Visitors should avoid drinking tap water and stick to bottled water, which is readily available. It is also wise to carry sun protection, and visitors should take into consideration the elevation changes when traveling to Asir. Medical facilities are available, and visitors should have health insurance when traveling to Saudi Arabia.

Getting to Khamis Mushayt: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Khamis Mushayt

Abha International Airport is the nearest airport, located approximately 80km away from Khamis Mushayt. King Khalid International Airport in Riyadh is also accessible, with international flights available.

Bus Stations Near Khamis Mushayt

There are no bus stations in Khamis Mushayt. Still, there are public buses and private taxis running frequently from Abha to Khamis Mushayt, which takes roughly an hour.

Other Options

Visitors can also reach Khamis Mushayt via train to Abha and private taxis for convenience and comfort.

How to Get Around in Khamis Mushayt

Getting around Khamis Mushayt is easy, and visitors have numerous options to choose from, including buses, private taxis, rental cars, and trains.

Where to Go Next from Khamis Mushayt: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Here are the top destinations to visit after Khamis Mushayt:

  • Abha: Known for its lush greenery and the historical city of Al Habalah.

  • Jazan: A city located in the far south, home to stunning coastal scenery and pristine beaches.

Conclusion

Khamis Mushayt is a hidden gem in Saudi Arabia, waiting to be discovered. With the majestic mountains, unique culture, and culinary delights, it is a perfect destination for travelers seeking a relaxing and authentic Arabian experience. Khamis Mushayt has something for everyone, from nature lovers to history buffs to adventure seekers, and it promises to be a trip never to be forgotten.
