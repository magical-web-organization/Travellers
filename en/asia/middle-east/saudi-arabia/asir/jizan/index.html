Comprehensive Tour Guide to Jizan, Saudi Arabia

Located in the Asir region, in southwestern Saudi Arabia, Jizan is a city with a rich history and a stunning location filled with natural wonders. From imposing mountain ranges and lush rainforests to pristine beaches and archaeological sites, Jizan has a lot to offer to its visitors. In this comprehensive tour guide, we will cover the most important places to visit, the best hikes and biking routes, food and drink scene, where to stay and much more. Keep reading to discover everything you need to know to plan your next trip to Jizan.

List of Places to Visit in Jizan

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Jizan is a city with a rich cultural background. One of the most popular museums to visit is the Jizan Museum, home to several exhibits about the history, traditions and culture of the Asir people. Another must-visit place is the Jizan Castle, an impressive structure that dates back to the 17th century and has been restored to its former glory. Another popular historical site is the Al Khoubah Castle, located on a hilltop, provides a panoramic view of the surrounding region.

Natural attractions of Jizan: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Jizan is blessed with a diverse and unique natural beauty that is unlike any other in the country. One of the most popular natural attractions is the Farasan Islands, a group of coral islands with crystal-clear waters that are perfect for snorkeling and diving excursions. The Al-Aridhah Nature Park is another popular spot, with its diverse range of flora and fauna, and walking trails to explore the natural beauty of Jizan.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Jizan

For those who love outdoor activities, Jizan has plenty to offer, including hiking and biking trails that will take you through breathtaking terrain. One of the most popular trails is the Jizan Island Trail, which takes you through a dense forest filled with a variety of birdlife. Another popular route is the Al Mahala Trail which meanders its way through mountains, wadis, and lush countryside.

Best Things to Do in Jizan: Activities and Tours

There are plenty of things to do and see in Jizan, and no trip would be complete without experiencing them. Try embarking on a 4x4 desert tour or diving under the Farasan Island Marine Wildlife Sanctuaries to explore the stunning coral reefs. Another popular activity is the Sandboarding Adventure Tour, which takes you across the sand dunes on a sandboard. Finally, immerse yourself in Saudi Arabian culture by visiting a traditional Souq market or watching a traditional dance show.

Food and Drink Scene of Jizan

Must-Try Dishes

Jizan is renowned for its delicious traditional cuisine with a few unique dishes you must try. One popular dish is the Al Kabsa, which is considered to be the national dish of Saudi Arabia. It is a rice-based dish with meat served with vegetables and spices. Another popular dish is the Al Mandy or the Mandi, a tasty meal of meat and rice mixed with various spices, cooked in a traditional clay oven. For the sweet lovers, try out the Borek, a pastry or pie made from phyllo, cheese, and spinach.

Local Beverages

The perfect drink to have when you are in Jizan is Maheeb, a traditional Arabic drink made from fermented dates. Another popular drink among locals is Qahwa, a traditional Arabic coffee served with dates, a symbol of hospitality in the Middle East.

Where to Stay in Jizan: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Jizan has an excellent range of accommodations to suit every budget and preference. If you are looking for luxury, the Juman Hotel Jizan is one of the top-rated hotels in the city. You could also opt for a mid-range stay at the Radisson Blu Resort Jizan or the Mercure Jizan Hotel. Budget travelers can choose from a range of guesthouses and hostels, including the Alba Residency, Dar Al Hamra Suites, and Al Ayoon Hotel.

What to Buy in Jizan: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

For souvenirs, Jizan offers a range of traditional items to bring back home, including the Arabic Oud, a stringed musical instrument, perfumes, and Arabian dates, and sweets. Head to the Souq market in Jizan city to find souvenirs that capture the essence of Jizan culture.

Local Culture and Customs of Jizan

In Jizan, it is important to dress conservatively and respect local customs and religious beliefs. Avoid consuming alcohol and refrain from public displays of affection. Also, be aware that during the month of Ramadan, it is customary for Muslims to fast during the day, so restaurants may be closed during this time.

Best Time to Visit Jizan

The best time to visit Jizan is during the winter months, from December to March, when the weather is mild, and outdoor activities are at their peak. However, if you want to experience the city’s monsoon season, plan your trip between the months of July and September.

Do’s and Don’ts of Jizan

Do:

  • Dress conservative
  • Respect local culture and customs
  • Try traditional Arabic dishes
  • Visit a traditional Souq market

Don’t:

  • Drink alcohol in public
  • Engage in public displays of affection
  • Be loud in public areas
  • Ignore local traditions and customs

Safety and Health

Safety in Jizan

Jizan is a safe city to visit, with low instances of crime. However, it is essential to keep an eye on your belongings and avoid walking alone at night.

Health in Jizan

Travelers must take precautions when it comes to their health in Jizan, including getting vaccinated for hepatitis A and B and being up to date with routine vaccinations. Mosquito-borne diseases such as malaria and dengue fever are prevalent in Jizan, so travelers should take measures to avoid mosquito bites.

Getting to Jizan: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Jizan

Jizan has its international airport, the King Abdullah Bin Abdulaziz Airport, with flights running from and to several domestic and international destinations.

Bus Stations Near Jizan

The Saudi Arabian Public Transport Company (SAPTCO) is the primary bus operator in Jizan that runs services across the region and neighboring cities, including Jeddah and Riyadh.

Other Options

Taxis and rental cars are the best options for travelers who want to explore the city at their own pace.

How to Get Around in Jizan

There are plenty of transportation options to get around Jizan, including taxis, buses, and rental cars. Taxis are the most convenient way to travel, especially for short distances. You could also consider renting a car if you want to explore the city and its surroundings.

Where to Go Next from Jizan: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

If you have more time to explore after Jizan, consider visiting the city of Abha, located in the Asir region. Another popular destination is Taif, home to the famous Taif Rose Festival.

Conclusion

Jizan is a unique destination in the Kingdom of Saudi Arabia, with a rich cultural and historical background and stunning natural beauty. From museums and historical sites to outdoor adventures and delicious traditional cuisine, Jizan has a lot to offer to its visitors. We hope our comprehensive tour guide has provided you with all the information you need to plan your next trip to this beautiful city.
