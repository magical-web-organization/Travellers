Introduction to Tabuk

Located in the north of Saudi Arabia, Tabuk is a hidden gem waiting to be discovered. Boasting a rich history and natural wonders, this city is a must-visit destination for travelers who want to explore the lesser-known side of the Middle East. In this comprehensive travel guide, we’ll cover all you need to know, including the top attractions, hiking routes, food and drink scene, and cultural customs, to help you make the most of your trip to Tabuk.

List of Places to Visit in Tabuk

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

A visit to Tabuk’s museums and historical places is a must for those interested in Saudi Arabia’s rich history and culture. The Tabuk Castle and Museum is a 15th-century fort with exhibits on the region’s history, while the Al Rajajil standing stones are an impressive collection of prehistoric pillars. The Tayma Oasis, a UNESCO World Heritage Site, is another popular destination, featuring well-preserved ruins of palaces, temples, and forts.

Natural Attractions of Tabuk: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Tabuk is home to some of the most stunning natural landscapes in Saudi Arabia. The Al Lithan Mountain Range offers scenic views of the colorful valleys and canyons, while the Al-Bida Park boasts a picturesque lake and trees. Outdoor enthusiasts can explore the Wadi Al Disah, a stunning valley with natural springs, while thrill-seekers can try their hand at rock climbing in the Harrat Lunayyir Geopark.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Tabuk

Tabuk is a paradise for hikers and bikers, with trails of varying difficulty available for all levels. The As Sawda Mountain, the highest peak in Saudi Arabia, is a popular hiking spot, offering breathtaking sunrise views at the top. The Al Hijaz Railway Trail is a historical path built by Ottoman Turks in 1908, with restored railway stations along the way. Bikers can explore the desert dunes on the Badayer Trail, or the challenging rocky terrain on the Al Ula-Ad Diriyah Trail.

Best Things to Do in Tabuk: Activities and Tours

There’s always something to do in Tabuk, whether you want to explore the outdoors, experience local culture, or just relax. Visitors can take a guided tour of the city’s historical sites, sample traditional dishes at local restaurants, or attend a cultural festival. For adventure seekers, there are hot air balloon rides, sandboarding, and quad biking tours.

Week-Long Itinerary for Tabuk

If you’re planning a week-long trip to Tabuk, here’s a sample itinerary to help you plan your days:

1 Visit the Tabuk Castle and Museum, and see the Al Rajajil standing stones

Food and Drink Scene of Tabuk

Must-Try Dishes

Tabuk is known for its rich culinary traditions, with a mix of Arabic, African, and Turkish influences. Dishes to try include the famous kabsa, a rice dish with meat or chicken, and the spiced bread, khobz al-tawa. Other traditional dishes include the tikka, a grilled chicken dish, and the mishkak, tender lamb grilled on skewers.

Local Beverages

Tabuk is a dry city, so alcohol is not served. However, there are plenty of non-alcoholic refreshing drinks to try, including the laban, a creamy yogurt drink, and the sahlab, a creamy hot milk drink with a dash of cinnamon. For a more indulgent experience, try the thick Arabic coffee or the fresh mint tea.

Where to Stay in Tabuk: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Tabuk offers a range of comfortable and affordable accommodations, with options to suit every budget. Luxury hotels include the Hilton Garden Inn and the Coral Tabuk Hotel, while mid-range options include the Raoum Inn Tabuk and the Tabuk Plaza Furnished Apartments. For budget travelers, there are hostels and guesthouses, such as the Al Raqi Resort and the Al Amirah Palace.

What to Buy in Tabuk: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

For unique souvenirs and gifts, head to the Tabuk Souq, a traditional marketplace selling handmade goods and local products. Popular items include the oud, a traditional Arabian perfume, and the Bedouin jewelry, handcrafted from silver and turquoise. For those with a sweet tooth, the dates, a local delicacy, and the halwa, a sweet dessert made from sesame paste, are a must-try.

Local Culture and Customs of Tabuk

Tabuk is a conservative city, and visitors are expected to respect local customs and traditions. Women are expected to dress modestly, covering their hair and wearing loose clothing, while men should avoid wearing shorts in public areas. Public displays of affection are not tolerated, and alcohol should not be brought or consumed in public.

Best Time to Visit Tabuk

The best time to visit Tabuk is during the winter months, from October to March, when temperatures are mild and comfortable for outdoor activities. During the summer months, from April to September, temperatures can soar to over 40 degrees Celsius, making it challenging to explore the city.

Do’s and Don’ts of Tabuk

Do:

  • Respect local customs and traditions
  • Dress modestly, covering your hair and wearing loose clothing
  • Try traditional dishes and drinks
  • Explore the natural wonders of Tabuk

Don’t:

  • Drink alcohol in public areas
  • Engage in public displays of affection
  • Wear revealing clothing in public areas

Safety and Health

Safety in Tabuk

Tabuk is a safe city with a low crime rate. However, visitors should take standard safety precautions, such as avoiding walking alone at night, keeping valuables and documents safe, and being aware of their surroundings. Visitors should also be cautious when driving, as the roads can be busy and chaotic.

Health in Tabuk

Visitors should take standard health precautions, such as staying hydrated, wearing sunscreen and insect repellent, and seeking medical assistance if needed. It is essential to carry necessary medications with you, as some prescriptions may not be available locally.

Getting to Tabuk: Transportation Options and Logistics

Tabuk is easily accessible via various transportation modes.

Airports Near Tabuk

Tabuk Regional Airport is the primary airport servicing the city, with direct flights to major cities in Saudi Arabia, including Riyadh and Jeddah.

Bus Stations Near Tabuk

The Tabuk Bus Station offers regular bus services to cities across Saudi Arabia, including Madinah and Jeddah.

Other Options

Taxis and rental cars are also available, with numerous car rental agencies located near the airport and in the city center.

How to Get Around in Tabuk

Tabuk is a relatively small city, and most of the attractions are within walking distance or a short drive away. Taxis and rental cars are readily available for those who prefer not to walk.

Where to Go Next from Tabuk: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Located near the border with Jordan, Tabuk is an ideal starting point for those looking to explore more of Saudi Arabia and its neighboring countries. Visitors can travel to the Red Sea resort of Aqaba, Jordan, or visit the ancient city of Petra, one of the Seven Wonders of the World. For a more traditional Arabian experience, head to the holy city of Madinah, home to the Prophet’s Mosque.

Conclusion

Tabuk is an exciting and underrated destination waiting to be explored. From natural wonders and historical landmarks to fantastic cuisine and cultural traditions, this city offers something for everyone. We hope this comprehensive travel guide has inspired you to visit Tabuk and enjoy all that this hidden gem has to offer.
