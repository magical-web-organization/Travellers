Introduction to Sürmene

Sürmene is a beautiful district located in the Trabzon province of Turkey. It sits on the eastern side of the Karadeniz region, bordering the Black Sea. The district boasts natural sceneries, cultural heritage and a unique local cuisine that is worth exploring. This charming town has a plethora of cultural and historical artifacts that attracts visitors to the area to learn about Sürmene’s rich history.

List of Places to Visit in Sürmene

If you’re planning on visiting Sürmene and you’re wondering what places to explore, here’s a list of some of the top destinations that you must visit:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Sürmene Castle: A historical castle in the heart of the city built during the Ottoman period.
  • Atatürk Pavillion: Historically significant monument dedicated to the founder of the Turkish Republic, Mustafa Kemal Atatürk.
  • Sürmene Carpet Museum: An interesting and informative museum showcasing the unique designs and techniques of traditional Anatolian carpet making.
  • Boztepe Mosque: A historic mosque with Ottoman architecture that is still in use today.

Natural Attractions of Sürmene: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Karaagac Nature Park: A lovely park with beautiful walking trails, picnic areas and scenic views of the surrounding mountains and the Black Sea.
  • Plateau of Dooruzor: Visit one of Sürmene’s beautiful highland plateau regions that offer breathtaking views of the surrounding mountains and valleys.
  • Haldizen Highland: A beautiful highland pasture with green meadows, wildflowers and creeks that is perfect for summer picnics and relaxation.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Sürmene

Sürmene is also a perfect place for outdoor enthusiasts, with many hiking and biking trails available in the surrounding mountains and natural parks.

  • Derecik Trail: A 5-km long woodland trail near the picturesque village of Derecik, offering stunning views of the scenic natural surroundings.
  • Çiçek Plateau Trail: A 7-km trail leading from Sürmene’s center to Çiçek Plateau, with beautiful views of the Black Sea coast and surrounding Karadeniz mountains.
  • Haros T. Şaban Trail: A scenic 9-km trail, starting from the village of Pazarköy and ending at Haros T. Şaban Plateau, offering amazing views of the surrounding mountains and valleys.

Best Things to Do in Sürmene: Activities and Tours

Sürmene is a place full of adventures and experiences. Here are some of the best things to do during your visit:

  • Join a traditional Turkish cooking class and learn the art of preparing Sürmene’s traditional dishes.
  • Visit Sürmene’s local bazaars and markets to buy traditional handicrafts, carpets and locally made pottery.
  • Take a boat tour of the Black Sea and witness the magnificent coastline from the sea.
  • Enjoy the unique local cuisine at one of the many local restaurants and cafes.

Food and Drink Scene of Sürmene

Sürmene’s cuisine is heavily influenced by the local Black Sea flora and fauna, resulting in a unique taste that you won’t find anywhere else. The main ingredients of Sürmene’s cuisine include fish, local herbs, and vegetables. Here are a few dishes and beverages that you must try while in Sürmene:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Mıhlama: A specialty cheesy cornmeal dish that is a local favorite.
  • Hamsiköy Pide: A tasty Turkish pizza with butter, cheese and minced meat toppings.
  • Kuymak: A delicious cheese fondue-like dish made with grated cornmeal and served with fried trout.

Local Beverages

  • Rize Tea: Deliciously sweet traditional black tea that is one of Turkey’s most famous teas.
  • Raki: A traditional Turkish anise-flavored liqueur that is often consumed at dinner parties and special occasions.
  • Sürmene Vodka: A potent alcoholic drink made from local potatoes blended with different flavors and spices.

Where to Stay in Sürmene: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Sürmene has an extensive variety of accommodation options available, ranging from luxurious hotels to budget-friendly guesthouses.

  • Royal Life Villas & Hotel: A luxurious hotel with breathtaking views of the Black Sea and the surrounding mountains.
  • Celal’inn Lodge: A charming and cozy wooden home-style lodge located in the heart of the city.
  • Tahsin Bey Konağı: A traditional Ottoman-style mansion turned guest house with authentic wooden furniture and amenities.

What to Buy in Sürmene: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Sürmene is famous for its traditional handicrafts, pottery, textiles and locally produced specialty food items.

  • Sürmene Knives: Traditional handmade knives, famous for their craftsmanship, sharp blades and unique designs.
  • Sürmene Carpets: Beautiful hand-knotted carpets made using local wool and dyes, known for their intricate designs and quality materials.
  • Cornelian Cherry: a sour cherry native to the Black Sea region, often used in making jams and compotes, this fruit is native to the region and makes an excellent souvenir.

Local Culture and Customs of Sürmene

Sürmene has a rich cultural heritage and a unique local lifestyle, heavily influenced by its location in the Black Sea region.

One of the most significant cultural events in Sürmene is Kudiluluk, a four-day period during which the locals celebrate the end of the agricultural season with traditional music, dance, and feast. The Kudiluluk ceremony offers a unique opportunity to immerse yourself in Sürmenen culture and heritage.

Best Time to Visit Sürmene

The best time to visit Sürmene is during the summer months between the end of June and the beginning of September. During this time, the weather is pleasant, and you’ll have the opportunity to explore the natural beauty of the region without being hampered by the winter weather.

Do’s and Don’ts of Sürmene

Do:

  • Respect the local culture and customs.
  • Try the local cuisine and taste the locally made beverages.
  • Tip service people generously.

Don’t:

  • Wear revealing clothing while in public places.
  • Drink alcohol openly in public.
  • Engage in disrespectful behavior towards locals.

Safety and Health

Safety in Sürmene

Sürmene is a safe place to visit, though it’s still advisable to keep your valuables close and avoid isolated areas at night. As a tourist, be aware of your surroundings, particularly in crowded areas such as markets and public transportation.

Health in Sürmene

Sürmene has a well-maintained healthcare system, and there are several medical facilities available, including hospitals and medical centers. It is essential to ensure that you have adequate health insurance coverage before coming to Turkey.

Getting to Sürmene: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Sürmene

Trabzon International Airport (TZX) is the nearest airport to Sürmene. It lies about 42km to the west of the city, and it provides easy access to the rest of the country and international destinations.

Bus Stations Near Sürmene

Sürmene has a well-connected network of bus stations. Havaş, Bayburt Turizm, and Batum Express all have daily buses to and from the other cities in the region. Additionally, Minibus services are available on-demand throughout the city.

Other Options

Taxis are a common transportation option in Sürmene. Renting a car is also an option; car rentals are available from the Trabzon International Airport.

How to Get Around in Sürmene

The best way to get around in Sürmene is to use a taxi or to rent a car. Minibusses offer convenient services within the town and nearby areas.
