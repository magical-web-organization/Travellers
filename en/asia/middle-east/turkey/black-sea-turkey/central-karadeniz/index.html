Introduction to Central Karadeniz

Located in the Black Sea region of Turkey, Central Karadeniz is an enchanting destination that offers an abundance of natural beauty, rich culture, and intriguing history. Known for its lush green forests, misty mountains, picturesque valleys, and breath-taking coastline, Central Karadeniz ticks all the boxes for a perfect getaway. This comprehensive travel guide will help you discover the hidden gems of Central Karadeniz, from its must-visit attractions to its hiking and biking routes.

List of Places to Visit in Central Karadeniz

Visitors to Central Karadeniz are spoilt for choice when it comes to places to visit. Here are some must-visit places that should not be missed:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Trabzon Museum: Home to an extensive collection of archaeological artefacts, including pottery, coins, and jewellery.
  • Hagia Sophia: A magnificent Byzantine church that has been converted into a mosque and a museum.
  • Sumela Monastery: A stunning Greek Orthodox monastery that dates back to the 4th century and is perched on the cliffs of the Altindere Valley.
  • Ataturk Mansion: A beautiful mansion that was once the residence of Mustafa Kemal Ataturk, the founder of modern Turkey.

Natural Attractions of Central Karadeniz: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Uzungol: A serene lake surrounded by verdant hills and forests, offering visitors a tranquil retreat from the hustle and bustle of city life.
  • Firtina Valley: An enchanting valley with picturesque villages, gushing waterfalls and verdant meadows. Perfect for hiking and picnicking.
  • Ayder Plateau: A high-altitude mountain plateau surrounded by lush green hills and forests, renowned for its thermal baths, local cuisine, and outdoor activities.
  • Karagol Sahil National Park: A scenic national park that boasts turquoise lakes, pine forests and stunning views of the Black Sea coastline.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Central Karadeniz

Central Karadeniz is a paradise for outdoor enthusiasts, with its numerous hiking and biking trails. Here are some popular routes that offer stunning views of the region:

  • Kavrun Yayla Yolu: A 10-kilometre trail that winds through stunning meadows and pine forests.
  • Haldizen Yayla Yolu: A 7-kilometre trail that takes visitors through verdant forests, gurgling streams, and scenic valleys.
  • Uzungol Yayla Yolu: A 6-kilometre trail that offers stunning views of the Uzungol Lake and the surrounding mountains.
  • Soganli Yayla Yolu: A 12-kilometre trail that leads visitors through breathtaking valleys, with views of the Firtina River and the Black Sea coastline.

Best Things to Do in Central Karadeniz: Activities and Tours

Central Karadeniz offers an abundance of activities and tours that cater to everyone’s interests. Here are some of the best things to do:

  • Take a boat tour of the Uzungol Lake and enjoy the stunning scenery.
  • Go rafting on the Firtina River and experience an adrenaline rush.
  • Enjoy a traditional Turkish hammam experience in Trabzon and rejuvenate yourself.
  • Explore the culinary scene of Central Karadeniz and savour its delicious local cuisine.

Week-Long Itinerary for Central Karadeniz

Here’s a comprehensive itinerary for a week-long trip to Central Karadeniz:

Day Activities
Day 1 Visit the Trabzon Museum and the Hagia Sophia. Enjoy a traditional lunch at one of the local eateries.
Day 2 Take a boat tour of the Uzungol Lake. Explore the nearby village and its local markets.
Day 3 Go hiking in the Ayder Plateau and soak in the thermal baths. Taste local delicacies for dinner.
Day 4 Explore the Sumela Monastery and its stunning views. Go shopping for souvenirs in Trabzon.
Day 5 Go rafting on the Firtina River. Enjoy a traditional Turkish hammam experience in the evening.
Day 6 Take a cable car to the Boztepe Hill and enjoy panoramic views of the Black Sea coast.
Day 7 Explore the Karagol Sahil National Park and its breathtaking scenery. Enjoy a final traditional dinner before departure.

Food and Drink Scene of Central Karadeniz

Central Karadeniz offers a mouth-watering culinary scene, that is influenced by Turkish, Greek, and Russian cuisines. Here are some must-try dishes and beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Hamsiköy pide: A local dish that features a crispy flatbread, topped with cheese, eggs, and butter.
  • Kuymak: A comforting dish that consists of melted cheese, cornmeal, and butter.
  • Mihlama: A rich cornmeal porridge with butter and cheese, served hot.
  • Karalahana Soup: A healthy soup made with kale, rice, and meat.

Local Beverages

  • Rize Tea: A rich and robust black tea that is grown in the Rize region of Central Karadeniz.
  • Cherry Juice: A refreshing summer beverage made with fresh cherries.
  • Raki: A potent anise-flavoured alcoholic beverage that is popular in Central Karadeniz.
  • Black Sea wine: A unique wine that is made from local grapes and offers complex and fruity flavours.

Where to Stay in Central Karadeniz: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Central Karadeniz offers a range of accommodations that cater to different budgets and preferences. Here are some top picks:

  • Zorlu Grand Hotel: A luxurious hotel in Trabzon that offers modern amenities and stunning views of the Black Sea coastline.
  • Ayder Doga Resort: A charming resort in the Ayder Plateau that offers thermal baths, outdoor activities, and cosy rooms.
  • Iris Hotel: A budget-friendly hotel in the heart of Trabzon that offers clean rooms and basic amenities.
  • Uzungol Elgun Hotel: A family-friendly hotel in Uzungol, that offers stunning views of the lake and the surrounding mountains.

What to Buy in Central Karadeniz: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Central Karadeniz offers an array of unique souvenirs and local products that are perfect to take home. Here are some recommendations:

  • Hazelnuts: Central Karadeniz is renowned for its hazelnuts, which are used in the production of Nutella.
  • Tea and tea sets: Rize tea and tea sets make for perfect souvenirs.
  • Handmade silver jewellery: Trabzon is renowned for its silver jewellery that is handmade by local artisans.
  • Local spices: Karadeniz cuisine makes use of distinctive local spices like black cumin and Sumac.

Local Culture and Customs of Central Karadeniz

Central Karadeniz has a unique culture that is shaped by its Greek, Russian, and Turkish influences. Here are some customs and traditions that are worth knowing:

  • Hospitality: Locals are known for their warm hospitality and welcoming nature.
  • Music and dance: Central Karadeniz has a vibrant music and dance scene, with the horon dance being a popular folk dance.
  • Religion: The majority of the population is Muslim, and prayer times are observed strictly.
  • Traditional clothing: Women in rural areas can be seen wearing traditional dresses with colourful embroidery.

Best Time to Visit Central Karadeniz

Central Karadeniz can be visited all year round, but the best time to visit is from May to September when the weather is mild and pleasant. September is a good time to visit if you are interested in attending the Karadeniz Culture and Tourism Festival.

Do’s and Don’ts of Central Karadeniz

Do:

  • Greet locals with a smile and “Merhaba” (Hello).
  • Try the local cuisine and delicacies.
  • Respect local customs and traditions.
  • Take off your shoes while entering a mosque or a traditional house.

Don’t:

  • Criticise or mock local customs or religion.
  • Dress inappropriately in public places.
  • Be intrusive while taking photographs of people.

Safety and Health

Safety in Central Karadeniz

Central Karadeniz is a safe destination to visit, but it is best to exercise caution while travelling. Avoid visiting remote areas at night and take precautionary measures while hiking or rafting.

Health in Central Karadeniz

Central Karadeniz is a low-risk area for communicable diseases, but it is important to take precautions against food and water-borne illnesses. It is advisable to drink bottled water and avoid consuming uncooked or undercooked meat.

Getting to Central Karadeniz: Transportation Options and Logistics

Central Karadeniz is well-connected by air, road, and sea.

Airports Near Central Karadeniz

  • Trabzon Airport: The main airport in the region, with flights from major domestic and international cities.
  • Ordu-Giresun Airport: A smaller airport that offers flights to major Turkish cities.

Bus Stations Near Central Karadeniz

  • Trabzon Bus Terminal: The main bus terminal that offers frequent buses to major Turkish cities.
  • Rize Bus Terminal: A smaller bus terminal that offers buses to major cities in the region.

Other Options

  • Ferry: There are regular ferries from Trabzon to the nearby cities of Sochi and Batumi.
  • Car: Central Karadeniz can be reached by car via the D010 highway (also known as the Black Sea Coastal Road).

How to Get Around in Central Karadeniz

The best way to explore Central Karadeniz is by car or bus, as public transportation can be infrequent or unreliable. Taxis, shared taxis, and minibuses are also available.

Where to Go Next from Central Karadeniz: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Central Karadeniz is surrounded by numerous stunning destinations that are worth visiting. Here are some nearby destinations and excursions:

  • Amasya: A picturesque town known for its historic houses, green forests, and scenic views.
  • Samsun: A vibrant city that boasts stunning architecture, beautiful parks, and a bustling port.
  • Mount Ararat: A majestic mountain that is located just outside Central Karadeniz and is a popular destination for hikers.

Conclusion

Central Karadeniz is a hidden gem that is waiting to be discovered. With its rich culture, natural wonders, outdoor adventures, and delectable cuisine, it offers an unparalleled travel experience. This comprehensive travel guide will help you plan your trip to Central Karadeniz and make the most of your visit.
