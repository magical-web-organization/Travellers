Introduction to Amasya

Amasya, a city located in the Central Karadeniz region of Turkey, is a hidden gem that attracts visitors from all over the world. This small city is situated on the banks of the Yeşilırmak River surrounded by mountains covered with lush green forests. The city has a rich history dating back to 3000 BC and was the capital of the Pontus Kingdom.

With its unique landscape, historical landmarks, and welcoming locals, Amasya provides a perfect retreat to escape the hustle and bustle of city life. This comprehensive travel guide will help you plan your itinerary to explore the best that Amasya has to offer.

List of Places to Visit in Amasya

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Amasya Castle: Located on a hilltop, Amasya Castle, also known as the Harşena Castle, offers stunning views of the city. The castle houses a small museum that exhibits artifacts and relics from the Roman, Byzantine, and Ottoman periods.
  • The Archaeological Museum of Amasya: The museum displays a vast collection of archaeological finds from Amasya and its surrounding areas, including mosaics, coins, and ancient tombs.
  • Sultan Beyazıt II Mosque Complex: Built-in the 15th century, the mosque complex is an excellent example of Ottoman architecture with its intricate decorations and tilework.
  • The Hazeranlar Mansion: Located on the Yeşilırmak riverside, the mansion serves as a museum today, showcasing the luxurious lifestyle of the Ottoman elite.

Natural Attractions of Amasya: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Küçük Ağri Mountain: Popular for hiking and trekking, Küçük Ağri Mountain offers stunning views of the valley and the city below.
  • Gözlek Thermal Springs: Known for its therapeutic benefits, Gözlek Thermal Springs is an ideal spot to relax and rejuvenate.
  • Gümüşköy Canyon: The canyon provides an opportunity for rafting and canoeing in the river.
  • The Rock Tombs: The ancient rock tombs situated on the Yeşilırmak riverbank offer a glimpse into the history of Amasya.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Amasya

The city offers several scenic routes for hiking and biking, including:

  • The Sultan II. Beyazıt Hiking Trail: A 15 km trail that takes you to the summit of Küçük Ağri Mountain.
  • The Yeşilırmak River Trail: A 10 km trail that runs along the Yeşilırmak riverbank and provides stunning views of the city and surrounding mountains.
  • The Amasya Ring Road: A 30 km route that loops around the city providing a panoramic view of Amasya.

Best Things to Do in Amasya: Activities and Tours

  • Take a boat ride along the Yeşilırmak River.
  • Visit the Amasya Bazaar and shop for local handicrafts, textiles, and ceramics.
  • Attend the Amasya International Film Festival (held in June).
  • Explore the traditional Ottoman houses and mansions, now converted into hotels and restaurants.

Week-Long Itinerary for Amasya

Day 1: Explore the city center, including the Sultan Beyazıt II Mosque Complex and the Archaeological Museum of Amasya.

Day 2: Visit the Harşena Castle and the İç Kale (inner castle).

Day 3: Take a boat ride on the Yeşilırmak River and visit the Hazeranlar Mansion.

Day 4: Hike the Sultan II. Beyazıt Hiking Trail and enjoy the panoramic view of the city.

Day 5: Visit the Gümüşköy Canyon for water sports or relax at the Gözlek Thermal Springs.

Day 6: Visit the ancient rock tombs and enjoy a traditional Turkish breakfast at a riverside restaurant.

Day 7: Shop for local crafts and souvenirs at the Amasya Bazaar and enjoy the local cuisine at one of the traditional Ottoman houses.

Food and Drink Scene of Amasya

Must-Try Dishes

  • Mantı: A classic Turkish dish of small dumplings with meat and spices, served with a yogurt sauce.
  • Pide: Turkish-style pizza with various toppings.
  • Sulu Köfte: Meatballs cooked in a tomato-based broth with vegetables and spices.
  • Alabalık: Trout fish served grilled or fried and garnished with herbs.

Local Beverages

  • Ayran: A refreshing yogurt-based drink.
  • Boza: A sweet and tangy fermented drink made from corn.
  • Turkish Tea: A black tea brewed and served strong, typically accompanied by a cube of sugar.

Where to Stay in Amasya: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Amasya offers a range of accommodations, including traditional Ottoman houses, hotels, hostels, and guesthouses. The best places to stay are:

  • The Kaplica Hotel: A luxurious hotel with thermal baths.
  • The Safran Konak: A boutique hotel with traditional Turkish decor and a stunning view of the city.
  • The Tefta Tashan Hotel: A historic building converted into a guesthouse with a rustic atmosphere.
  • The Amasya Ferahi Evleri: A traditional Ottoman mansion turned into a hotel.

What to Buy in Amasya: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Amasya Ceramics and Pottery: Local artisans produce exquisite examples of traditional Turkish ceramics.
  • Amasya Silk: The city is known for its superior quality silk, popular with tourists as a souvenir.
  • Pinecone Tea: Dried pinecones infused in water produce a caffeine-free tea endowed with taste and scent.

Local Culture and Customs of Amasya

Amasya has a rich and diverse cultural heritage, which is reflected in its architecture, traditions, and lifestyle. Some cultural highlights of Amasya are:

  • Turkish Baths: A Turkish bath or hammam is an essential element of the local culture, offering visitors a rejuvenating experience.
  • Music: Turkish traditional music is an integral part of the culture, and you can enjoy local musicians playing traditional instruments in many venues.
  • Local Festivals: The city hosts several festivals throughout the year, including the Amasya International Film Festival and the Amasya Ceviz Festival.

Best Time to Visit Amasya

The best time to visit Amasya is during the spring (April to June) and autumn (September to November) months when the weather is mild and pleasant. The summer months of July and August can be sweltering and uncomfortably hot, while the winter months can be cold and snowy.

Do’s and Don’ts of Amasya

Do:

  • Respect local customs and traditions.
  • Wear modest clothing while visiting mosques and religious places.
  • Try out the local cuisine and delicacies.

Don’t:

  • Drink tap water.
  • Be disrespectful or offensive towards the local culture and customs.
  • Take photos without permission in religious places and mosques.

Safety and Health

Safety in Amasya

Amasya is a relatively safe city compared to other cities in Turkey. However, travelers should take appropriate safety measures, such as keeping their valuables safe and being mindful of their surroundings.

Health in Amasya

The local hospitals and health facilities in Amasya offer high-quality medical services. Travelers must take the necessary precautions such as getting vaccinated against hepatitis A and B, typhoid, and tetanus.

Getting to Amasya: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Amasya

The nearest airport is the Merzifon Airport, which is approximately 50 km from Amasya. Direct flights from Istanbul are available.

Bus Stations Near Amasya

Amasya has a well-connected network of bus services, with regular buses from Istanbul, Ankara, and other major cities in Turkey. The Amasya Bus Terminal is located on the outskirts of the city.

Other Options

Visitors can also travel by train or private car to Amasya.

How to Get Around in Amasya

Visitors can explore the city on foot or by renting a bike or car. Taxis and buses are also available for transportation within the city.

Where to Go Next from Amasya: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Visitors can plan day trips to nearby destinations such as Samsun, Ordu, and Tokat. These cities offer beautiful landscapes, historical landmarks, and cultural attractions.

Conclusion

Amasya, a beautiful, historic, and culturally rich city providing an excellent blend of natural and historical landmarks. The city offers a perfect escape from the urban life into the lap of nature with its tranquil resorts, beautiful scenery, and outdoor activities. This comprehensive guide will help you plan your itinerary and explore the best that Amasya has to offer.
