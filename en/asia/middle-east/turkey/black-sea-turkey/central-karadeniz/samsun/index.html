Introduction to Samsun

Located in the Central Karadeniz region of Black Sea Turkey, Samsun is a bustling port city that has a rich history and culture. It is the largest city in the region and serves as the gateway to the Black Sea coast. The city has a perfect blend of old-world charm and modern amenities, making it an ideal destination for both history buffs and adventure seekers. This comprehensive travel guide will give you an insight into the top things to see and do in Samsun.

List of Places to Visit in Samsun

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Samsun has a rich history, and there are plenty of cultural attractions to explore. Here are some of the top museums and historical places to visit in the city:

  • Samsun Archeology and Ethnography Museum: This museum is home to over 35,000 artifacts that showcase the region’s history and culture.

  • Bandırma Ferry Museum: This museum features the ferry that carried Mustafa Kemal Atatürk, the founder of modern Turkey, from Istanbul to Samsun in 1919.

  • Atatürk Monument: This monument is dedicated to Mustafa Kemal Atatürk, and it is a significant landmark in the city.

  • Samsun Grand Mosque: With its impressive architecture and ornate decoration, this mosque is one of the most visited religious sites in the city.

Natural Attractions of Samsun: Outdoor Adventures, and Scenic Views

Samsun is also home to some breathtaking natural attractions, which are perfect for outdoor enthusiasts. Here are some top natural attractions in Samsun:

  • Pınarbaşı Canyon: This is an excellent place for hiking and rock climbing. It features stunning waterfalls, springs, and natural hot springs.

  • Bafra Beach: This charming beach is the perfect place to relax and sunbathe. It’s clean, tranquil, and has clear waters.

  • Kızılırmak Delta Bird Sanctuary: This delta covers an area of almost 15,000 hectares and is home to a diverse range of birds and other wildlife.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Samsun

The best way to explore the natural beauty of Samsun is on foot or by bike. Here are some of the best hiking and biking routes in the city:

  • Pınarbaşı Trekking Trail: This trail starts from the Pınarbaşı Canyon and takes you through some of the most spectacular landscapes in the region.

  • Tekkekaya Village Cycle Route: This route starts from the Tekkekaya village and takes you through the forested hills and valleys of Samsun.

Best Things to Do in Samsun: Activities and Tours

Apart from sightseeing, there are also many other things to do in Samsun. Here are some top activities and tours to consider:

  • Turkish Bath Experience: Indulge in a traditional Turkish bath experience and relax your body and mind.

  • Local Market Tour: Discover the local food scene and shop for local products at the vibrant markets of Samsun.

  • Black Sea Fishing Tour: For a unique experience, go on a Black Sea fishing tour to catch some of the freshest fish in the region.

Week-Long Itinerary for Samsun

Samsun has plenty to offer, and you may need more than a day or two to explore the city properly. Here’s a sample week-long itinerary to get the most out of your visit:

Day 1-2: Visit the cultural attractions, such as the Samsun Archeology and Ethnography Museum and the Bandırma Ferry Museum.

Day 3-4: Explore the natural attractions of Samsun, including the Pınarbaşı Canyon and Bafra Beach.

Day 5: Indulge in a traditional Turkish bath experience or visit the local markets to shop for souvenirs.

Day 6: Go on a Black Sea fishing tour and end your day with a seafood feast in one of the local restaurants.

Day 7: Visit nearby destinations such as Ordu or Giresun to explore more of the Black Sea region’s beauty.

Food and Drink Scene of Samsun

Samsun has a diverse food and drink scene that reflects the region’s cultural and historical influences. Here are some of the must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Mısır Çorbası: This is a hearty corn soup that is a favorite among locals.

  • Karalahana Çorbası: This soup is made with black cabbage and is a staple in Samsun’s cuisine.

  • Hamsi Tava: This dish features fried anchovies with a crispy exterior and tender, flavorful meat.

Local Beverages

  • Çay: Tea is the most popular beverage in Turkey, and you’ll find it everywhere in Samsun.

  • Rakı: If you prefer alcoholic beverages, try Rakı, Turkey’s famous anise-flavored drink.

Where to Stay in Samsun: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Samsun offers plenty of accommodation options to suit all budgets and preferences. Here are some top choices:

  • Anemon Samsun Hotel: This is a luxurious hotel located in the city center, offering stunning views of the Black Sea.

  • North Point Hotel: This hotel is a budget-friendly option that offers clean and cozy rooms with modern amenities.

  • Samsun Konaks: This is a traditional guesthouse that offers a unique experience in a historic building in the heart of the city.

What to Buy in Samsun: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Here are some of the best souvenirs and local products to buy in Samsun:

  • Lacework: Samsun is famous for its intricate lacework, which makes for a beautiful and unique souvenir.

  • Hamsi: Samsun is known for its delicious anchovies, which you can buy fresh or canned to take home.

Local Culture and Customs of Samsun

Samsun has a rich cultural heritage, and locals take pride in preserving their traditions. Here are a few customs and cultural practices to keep in mind:

  • Respectful Dressing: It’s best to dress modestly, especially when visiting mosques or other religious sites.

  • Hospitality: Samsun locals are known for their kindness and hospitality, so expect a warm welcome wherever you go.

Best Time to Visit Samsun

The best time to visit Samsun is from May to October when the weather is pleasant and suitable for outdoor activities. The summer months can be hot, but the cool sea breeze offers a refreshing escape.

Do’s and Don’ts of Samsun

Here are some dos and don’ts to follow when visiting Samsun:

Do:

  • Try Local Food: Samsun has a diverse culinary scene, and you’ll find plenty of delicious dishes to try.

  • Learn Some Turkish: Even a few words of Turkish will go a long way in showing your respect and appreciation for the local culture.

Don’t:

  • Negotiate Aggressively: Haggling is common in Turkey, but avoid negotiating aggressively as it can be seen as disrespectful.

  • Show Public Displays of Affection: Public displays of affection are not common in Turkey, so it’s best to avoid them.

Safety and Health

Safety in Samsun

Samsun is generally a safe destination for travelers, but it’s always best to take necessary precautions like you would in any other city.

  • Avoid Exchanging Money on the Street: Only exchange money at authorized currency exchange offices or banks to avoid being scammed.

  • Be Careful with Your Belongings: Keep your valuables secure to avoid theft, especially in crowded areas.

Health in Samsun

It’s always advisable to check the latest travel health advice from the World Health Organization and take necessary vaccinations or medications if required.

Getting to Samsun: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Samsun

Samsun is served by Samsun-Çarşamba Airport, which has regular flights to Istanbul and Ankara.

Bus Stations Near Samsun

Samsun has several bus stations that serve various destinations in Turkey. The most popular one is the Samsun Bus Station.

Other Options

Samsun is also accessible by train, which connects the city to destinations like Ankara, Istanbul, and Kars.

How to Get Around in Samsun

Samsun has an efficient public transportation system, including buses and trams. Taxis are also readily available and reasonable. You can also rent a car or a bike to explore the city at your own pace.

Where to Go Next from Samsun: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Samsun is an excellent base to explore the beauty of the Black Sea region. Here are some nearby destinations worth exploring:

  • Ordu: Known for its beautiful beaches and lush greenery, Ordu is a great place for nature lovers.

  • Giresun: This picturesque city is home to stunning Ottoman architecture and pristine beaches.

Conclusion

Samsun is a fascinating part of Turkey, with a rich history, beautiful natural attractions, and a vibrant culture. With this comprehensive travel guide, you’ll be able to make the most of your visit and experience all that Samsun has to offer.
