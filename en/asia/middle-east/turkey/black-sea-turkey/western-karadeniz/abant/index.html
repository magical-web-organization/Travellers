Introduction to Abant

Abant is a small town located in the western Karadeniz region in Black Sea Turkey. This picturesque destination is renowned for its scenic views, natural surrounds and peaceful environment. Abant is a great place for visitors who are looking for a relaxing and refreshing experience, away from city life.

List of Places to Visit in Abant

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

If you’re interested in learning about the culture and history of Abant, you’ll find many fascinating and enriching places to visit. Here are a few of the top cultural attractions in Abant:

  • Village of Göynük: The charming village of Göynük is one of the most popular places in Abant. This traditional village is home to a historic mosque and a few well-preserved old houses.
  • Akkaya Travertines: Discover the stunning natural beauty of Akkaya Travertines, a popular tourist spot located near Abant.
  • Kursunlu Mosque: Explore the Kursunlu Mosque, a historical mosque that dates back several centuries.

Natural Attractions of Abant: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Abant is renowned for its breath-taking natural beauty, and visitors can enjoy a range of outdoor adventures and scenic views:

  • Abant Lake: The stunning Abant Lake is the main attraction in the area, with its serene blue-green waters and lush surroundings.
  • Samandere Waterfall: Hike to the Samandere Waterfall to view the cascading waters as they tumble into a pool below.
  • Kokpinar Livadasi: Kokpinar Livadasi is a beautiful plateau located in the Karadeniz region of Turkey. The area has a wide variety of plants and animals and is a popular spot for hiking and picnicking.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Abant

Abant is a great destination for hiking and biking enthusiasts, with a range of trails and routes to choose from. Here are a few popular routes to consider:

  • Abant Trail: This 10-km trail is perfect for experienced hikers who enjoy a challenge. The trail is well-maintained and provides stunning views of Abant Lake and the surrounding landscape.
  • Göynük Trail: This trail winds through the picturesque village of Göynük and passes historical sites and natural attractions along the way.
  • Sultan Demir Baba Trail: Located near the village of Akçasu, this trail is a favourite among mountain bikers and hikers, offering stunning views and challenging terrain.

Best Things to Do in Abant: Activities and Tours

While Abant is primarily known as a relaxing destination, there are still plenty of activities to keep visitors engaged during their stay. Here are a few of our top picks:

  • Horseback Riding: Experience the beauty of Abant on horseback. The trails provide stunning views and are suitable for both beginners and experienced riders alike.
  • Hot Air Balloon Rides: Soar above the trees and enjoy a bird’s eye view of the stunning natural beauty of Abant from a unique perspective.
  • ATV Tours: Get your adrenaline pumping with an ATV tour through the forests and trails of Abant.

Food and Drink Scene of Abant

Must-Try Dishes

Turkish cuisine is famous all over the world, and visitors to Abant can look forward to trying out some of the local delicacies. Here are a few must-try dishes:

  • Manti: A Turkish version of dumplings, manti can be found in many restaurants in Abant.
  • Kebab: Kebabs are a staple of Turkish cuisine, and Abant has numerous restaurants that serve the dish.
  • Baklava: This sweet, pastry dessert is a favourite among locals and tourists alike.

Local Beverages

Tea and coffee are the most popular beverages in Abant, and you can find many cafes and teahouses in the area. Ayran, a refreshing yogurt-based drink, is also a local favourite.

Where to Stay in Abant: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Abant has a range of accommodation options available including hotels, hostels, and guesthouses. Here are some of our top recommendations:

  • Buyuk Abant Oteli: This is the most popular hotel in Abant, located right on the edge of the lake. Guests can enjoy stunning lake views and modern amenities.
  • Abant Gol Evleri: These cosy and charming cabins are located in the forest surrounding the lake and offer a peaceful, secluded stay.
  • Koru Otel: This guesthouse has a traditional Turkish feel and is located just a short walk from the village of Göynük.

What to Buy in Abant: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Abant has a range of local products and souvenirs to bring home as souvenirs or gifts. Here are just a few ideas:

  • Handmade Carpets and Rugs: Abant is known for its skilled carpet makers, and visitors can purchase beautiful handmade rugs and carpets.
  • Honey: Honey is a local specialty in Abant and is sold by many vendors in the area.
  • Turkish Delight: Locally made Turkish delights are a favourite souvenir with tourists.

Local Culture and Customs of Abant

Visitors to Abant should be aware of local customs and etiquette. Shaking hands and using basic Turkish expressions, such as “Merhaba” (Hello) and “Tesekkür ederim” (Thank you), are greatly appreciated in social situations. Modest dress is also recommended during visits to historical or religious sites.

Best Time to Visit Abant

Abant is beautiful all year round, but the best time to visit depends on your personal preferences. The summer months (June-August) are ideal for hiking and outdoor activities, while winter (December-February) is perfect for skiing and snowboarding.

Do’s and Don’ts of Abant

Do:

  • Dress modestly while visiting mosques or other religious sites.
  • Say “Merhaba” and “Tesekkür ederim” when interacting with locals.
  • Try the local dishes and drinks.
  • Take time to appreciate the natural beauty of Abant.

Don’t:

  • Disrespect local customs and traditions.
  • Litter or damage the environment.
  • Wear inappropriate clothing when visiting mosques or religious sites.
  • Be too loud or disruptive in public places.

Safety and Health

Safety in Abant

Abant is generally a safe destination for tourists. However, visitors should still take precautions, such as keeping valuables secure and being aware of their surroundings. It is also important to follow local customs and etiquette to ensure a safe and respectful stay.

Health in Abant

Abant has no major health concerns, and visitors do not require any vaccinations to travel to the area. However, it is recommended that visitors carry basic first aid supplies and take precautions against mosquito bites to prevent mosquito-borne illnesses.

Getting to Abant: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Abant

The nearest airport to Abant is Istanbul International Airport, which is approximately a 3.5-hour drive away.

Bus Stations Near Abant

Buses run regularly from Istanbul to Bolu, which is the nearest town to Abant. It is recommended to take a taxi or hire a car to travel the final distance to Abant.

Other Options

Visitors can also opt to hire a taxi or rent a car to get to Abant from Istanbul or other nearby towns.

How to Get Around in Abant

Abant is a small town, and visitors can easily get around on foot or by bicycle. Taxis are also available for those who prefer not to walk or cycle.

Where to Go Next from Abant: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Abant is located in the Karadeniz region of Turkey, and visitors can easily explore nearby destinations, including:

  • Bolu: This charming town is located just a short distance from Abant and offers additional natural and cultural attractions.
  • Amasra: Amasra is a historic town located on the Black Sea coast, known for its beautiful beaches and winding streets.
  • Safranbolu: This UNESCO World Heritage site is a well-preserved Ottoman town with beautiful architecture and a rich history.

Conclusion

Abant is a beautiful and serene destination that offers visitors a chance to unwind and appreciate the natural beauty of Turkey. With a range of cultural attractions, outdoor activities, and delicious local cuisine, there is something for everyone in this picturesque town.
