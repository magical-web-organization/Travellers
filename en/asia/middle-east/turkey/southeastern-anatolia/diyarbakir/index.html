Introduction to Diyarbakır

Diyarbakır is a historic city located in the southeast region of Turkey, near the Syrian border. Known for its ancient walls and rich cultural heritage, Diyarbakır is a popular destination for travelers looking to experience the unique blend of Turkish, Kurdish, and Arabic cultures found in this part of the world.

List of Places to Visit in Diyarbakır

Whether you’re interested in exploring ancient ruins or soaking up the local culture, there’s something for everyone in Diyarbakır. Here are some of the must-visit destinations:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • The Diyarbakır Fortress and Hevsel Gardens Cultural Landscape: This UNESCO World Heritage Site is the main attraction of the city. The walls and fortifications that surround the city date back to the Roman era, and the Hevsel Gardens are an oasis in the desert, featuring ancient aqueducts that still bring water into the city.

  • The Great Mosque of Diyarbakır: Built in the 11th century, this is one of the oldest and largest mosques in Turkey.

  • Cahit Sıtkı Tarancı Museum: A museum dedicated to the life and works of Cahit Sıtkı Tarancı, one of Turkey’s most famous poets.

Natural Attractions of Diyarbakır: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Tigris River: The Tigris River runs past Diyarbakır, and a boat ride or stroll along the river walk is a great way to enjoy the city’s natural beauty.

  • Hazar Lake: Just an hour outside the city, this beautiful lake is a popular spot for fishing, boating, and bird-watching.

  • Mount Nemrut: This mountain is home to some of the world’s most interesting ancient ruins, including the tomb of King Antiochus I.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Diyarbakır

There are a number of scenic hiking and biking routes in and around Diyarbakır, including:

  • The Diyarbakır City Walls: You can walk along the ancient walls that surround the city for incredible views of the city and surrounding landscape.

  • Hevsel Gardens: Take a leisurely stroll through the gardens for a glimpse into the region’s agricultural heritage.

  • Mount Nemrut: There are several hiking trails that lead to the ancient ruins on this beautiful mountain.

Best Things to Do in Diyarbakır: Activities and Tours

  • Visit the historic bazaar: Diyarbakır’s bazaar is a great place to shop for souvenirs and experience the local culture.

  • Try the local food: Diyarbakır is famous for its kebabs, baklava, and other delicious Turkish foods.

  • Take a guided tour: The city has a number of guided tours that will take you to all the top attractions and give you a deeper understanding of the city’s rich history.

Week-Long Itinerary for Diyarbakır

Day 1: Explore the historic city center and the Great Mosque of Diyarbakır. Day 2: Take a boat ride along the Tigris River and enjoy the beautiful views. Day 3: Visit the Cahit Sıtkı Tarancı Museum and other cultural attractions. Day 4: Take a day trip to Mount Nemrut and explore the ancient ruins. Day 5: Visit Hazar Lake and enjoy outdoor activities like fishing and boating. Day 6: Take a guided tour of the city’s bazaar and try some of the local food. Day 7: Spend the day exploring the Diyarbakır Fortress and Hevsel Gardens Cultural Landscape.

Food and Drink Scene of Diyarbakır

Must-Try Dishes

  • Diyarbakır-style kebabs: Made with marinated lamb or beef, these kebabs are grilled over an open flame and served with rice or bulgur.

  • Baklava: This sweet pastry is made with layers of phyllo dough, chopped nuts, and honey syrup.

  • Ayran: This refreshing yogurt-based beverage is a popular drink in Turkey.

Local Beverages

  • Şıra: This non-alcoholic grape juice is a popular drink in the region.

  • Rakı: A traditional anise-flavored liquor, often served with mezes or small appetizers.

Where to Stay in Diyarbakır: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Diyarbakır has a range of accommodation options to suit all budgets, including traditional guesthouses, modern hotels, and cozy hostels.

  • Hilton Garden Inn Diyarbakır: A modern and stylish hotel located in the heart of the city.

  • Cavusoglu Oteli: A traditional guesthouse located in the historic city center.

  • Figen Han Konagi: A cozy and welcoming hostel located in a traditional Ottoman mansion.

What to Buy in Diyarbakır: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Carpets and Kilims: Diyarbakır is known for its hand-woven carpets and kilims, which make great souvenirs and gifts.

  • Honey: The region produces some of the best honey in Turkey, which is often sold in traditional markets and bazaars.

  • Spices: Diyarbakır is a great place to stock up on spices and teas, including saffron, sumac, and black cumin.

Local Culture and Customs of Diyarbakır

Diyarbakır is a city with a rich cultural heritage, and visitors are encouraged to respect local customs and traditions. It is important to dress modestly and remove shoes before entering mosques or other religious sites.

Best Time to Visit Diyarbakır

The best time to visit Diyarbakır is during the spring and fall months, when the weather is mild and pleasant. Summers can be extremely hot, with temperatures reaching over 100°F, while winters are cold and sometimes snowy.

Do’s and Don’ts of Diyarbakır

Do:

  • Respect local customs and traditions.
  • Try the local food and drink.
  • Dress modestly when visiting mosques or other religious sites.

Don’t:

  • Take photographs of people without their permission.
  • Drink alcohol in public.
  • Visit political rallies or protests.

Safety and Health

Safety in Diyarbakır

Diyarbakır is a safe city for travelers, but it is important to be aware of your surroundings and take necessary precautions. It is also important to be aware of travel advisories and restrictions from your home country and follow all local laws and regulations.

Health in Diyarbakır

Visitors to Diyarbakır should ensure that they have up-to-date vaccinations and take necessary precautions to avoid food-borne illnesses. It is also important to have adequate travel insurance and access to medical care in case of emergency.

Getting to Diyarbakır: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Diyarbakır

Diyarbakır Airport is the primary airport serving the city, with domestic and international flights available.

Bus Stations Near Diyarbakır

There are several bus stations in and around Diyarbakır, with regular services to other cities in Turkey and the surrounding region.

Other Options

Taxis and rental cars are also available for travelers who prefer to drive themselves.

How to Get Around in Diyarbakır

Walking is the best way to experience the city’s culture and history, but there are also local buses and taxis available for visitors who need to travel further.

Where to Go Next from Diyarbakır: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Diyarbakır is a great starting point for travelers looking to explore the wider region of southeastern Turkey. Nearby destinations include:

  • Mardin: A historic city with beautiful architecture and incredible views of the Mesopotamian plain.

  • Hasankeyf: An ancient city built into the cliffs overlooking the Tigris River, with a rich history dating back to the Roman era.

  • Urfa: A holy city with important religious sites for both Christians and Muslims.

Conclusion

Diyarbakır is a truly unique destination that offers travelers the chance to experience the rich culture and history of southeastern Turkey. Whether you’re interested in exploring ancient ruins, sampling local cuisine, or simply soaking up the local culture, a trip to Diyarbakır is sure to be an unforgettable experience.
