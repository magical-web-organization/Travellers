Introduction to Kilis

Kilis is a small city located in Southeastern Anatolia, in Turkey, bordering Syria to the south. It is considered one of the country’s most peaceful cities, despite its proximity to conflict zones. Kilis is a place of contrast, where ancient traditions and modern conveniences harmoniously coexist. Here, you can find a mix of Turkish and Arabic culture, creating a unique destination for travelers seeking a true cultural experience.

List of Places to Visit in Kilis

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Kilis is steeped in history, and there’s no better way to learn about it than by visiting its museums and historical places. Kilis Museum of Archaeology is a great first stop for those interested in history. The museum houses a vast collection of artifacts from the region’s Neolithic, Hellenistic, and Roman periods. Another important cultural attraction is the Ulu Mosque, which was built during the Ottoman Empire and is still in use today. Visitors are welcome, but should respect the mosque’s dress code.

Natural Attractions of Kilis: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Kilis is not only rich in history and culture but also offers a wide range of scenic views and outdoor activities. The Azaplı Forest is a great place to explore the local flora and fauna. It offers hiking and biking routes that will lead you to a beautiful waterfall surrounded by pine and oak trees. The Sarıyer Dam is another natural wonder that offers scenic views for visitors. This man-made lake is a perfect spot for a picnic or birdwatching.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Kilis

Kilis offers various hiking and biking routes for outdoor enthusiasts. One of the popular routes is the Azaplı-Yuvacık trail, which is a 12-km trail that starts from the Azaplı Forest and leads to a small village called Yuvacık. There are also bike routes that offer stunning views of the city and surrounding landscapes.

Best Things to Do in Kilis: Activities and Tours

Kilis offers a range of activities that cater to different interests. You can take a cooking class and learn how to make local dishes such as Kilis dolma or baklava. You can also take a cultural tour, which will take you to historical places and introduce you to local customs and traditions. The city also offers horseback riding tours that will take you through scenic routes and offer a unique perspective on the city’s natural beauty.

Week-Long Itinerary for Kilis

Day 1: Visit Kilis Museum of Archaeology, then take a walk around the city’s old quarters.

Day 2: Take a cultural tour and visit Ulu Mosque, Gazi Ahmed Bey Mosque, and Topraklık Hill.

Day 3: Visit the Azaplı Forest and take a hiking/biking route.

Day 4: Relax by the Sarıyer Dam and have a picnic.

Day 5: Take a cooking class and learn how to make local dishes.

Day 6: Take a horseback riding tour.

Day 7: Try some local street food and shop for souvenirs.

Food and Drink Scene of Kilis

Must-Try Dishes

Kilis cuisine is a fusion of Turkish and Arabic influences, resulting in a variety of savory dishes. Kilis dolma, made with minced meat, rice, and spices, and wrapped in vine leaves, is a must-try dish. Another famous dish is lathyrus soup, made with an edible legume that is only grown in the region.

Local Beverages

While in Kilis, make sure to try şıllık, a local drink made with yogurt, water, salt, and mint. It is a refreshing beverage and a popular way to beat the heat during the summer months.

Where to Stay in Kilis: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Kilis offers numerous accommodation options, ranging from budget-friendly hostels to luxurious hotels. The best area to stay in is the city center, as it is close to major attractions and offers easy access to transportation. Here are some of our top picks:

  • Kilis Otel: A budget-friendly option that offers clean and comfortable rooms and is located in the city center.
  • Grand Hotel Kilis: A mid-range hotel that offers modern amenities and is close to the city’s major attractions.
  • Dedeman Gaziantep: A luxurious hotel located in nearby Gaziantep, which offers a range of amenities, including a spa and fitness center.

What to Buy in Kilis: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

If you’re looking to take home something to remind you of your stay in Kilis, there are plenty of options. Kilis soaps, made from olive oil, are popular among tourists. You can also buy regional spices and traditional Kilis dolls.

Local Culture and Customs of Kilis

Kilis has a unique culture that blends Turkish and Arabic elements. The city is known for its hospitality, and visitors are often welcomed warmly. As in many other Middle Eastern countries, modesty is highly valued in Kilis, and it is recommended to dress conservatively.

Best Time to Visit Kilis

The best time to visit Kilis is during spring and fall when the temperatures are mild, and the crowds are fewer. Summer can be hot and humid, while winters can be chilly and rainy.

Do’s and Don’ts of Kilis

Do:

  • Respect local customs and traditions.
  • Dress modestly in public places.
  • Try local dishes and drinks.

Don’t:

  • Take pictures of individuals without their permission.
  • Visit Syria or other conflict zones near Kilis.

Safety and Health

Safety in Kilis

Kilis is a safe city to travel to, despite its proximity to conflict zones. However, it is recommended to stay informed about the current situation in the region and to follow the advice of the local authorities.

Health in Kilis

Kilis has a modern healthcare system, and travelers can receive medical care at local hospitals. However, it is recommended to have travel insurance that covers medical emergencies.

Getting to Kilis: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Kilis

The nearest airport to Kilis is Gaziantep International Airport, which is 50 km away from the city center. From the airport, you can take a taxi or bus to reach Kilis.

Bus Stations Near Kilis

Kilis has a bus station that offers daily services to major cities in Turkey, such as Gaziantep, Adana, and Istanbul.

Other Options

Travelers can also rent a car or hire a private driver to reach Kilis.

How to Get Around in Kilis

Kilis is a small city, and the easiest way to get around is by walking. However, taxis and buses are also available for those who need them.

Where to Go Next from Kilis: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Kilis is located in the southeastern part of Turkey, near the border with Syria. Aleppo, the largest city in Syria, is just 60 km away from Kilis, but it is not recommended to visit due to the ongoing conflict in the area. However, there are several nearby destinations that are worth visiting, such as Gaziantep, one of the oldest cities in the region.

Conclusion

Kilis is a hidden gem that offers a unique cultural experience for travelers seeking an off-the-beaten-path destination. With its rich history, delicious cuisine, and stunning natural beauty, Kilis is a destination worth visiting. So, pack your bags, and get ready to explore this fascinating city.
