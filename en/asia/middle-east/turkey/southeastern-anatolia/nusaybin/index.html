The Ultimate Travel Guide to Nusaybin, Turkey

Located in Southeastern Anatolia, in Middle East Turkey, Nusaybin is a hidden gem that awaits discovery. It boasts a rich cultural heritage, historical significance, outdoor adventures, scenic landscapes, and a warm, welcoming community. This comprehensive travel guide will help you explore the best of Nusaybin, from must-visit attractions to hiking routes, and local cuisines to cultural customs.

Introduction to Nusaybin

Nusaybin, also known as Nisibis, is an ancient city that has been inhabited since the 13th century BC. The city has been ruled by various empires, including the Assyrians, Medes, Persians, and Romans. It played a crucial role in the Roman-Persian Wars and was a significant stop on the Silk Road.

Today, Nusaybin is a bustling city that attracts visitors with its friendly locals, unique culinary offerings, and cultural attractions.

List of Places to Visit in Nusaybin

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • The Nusaybin Walls: Built during the Roman era, these walls contain various structures such as the water tank, watchtowers, and city gates.
  • Hasankeyf: This ancient city was buried under water after a dam was built. However, the remains of the historic city can still be explored, such as the castle, mosque, and caves.
  • The Grand Mosque or Ulu Camii: This 15th-century mosque boasts intricate stone carvings and a towering minaret. It is still active and open to the public.
  • The Nusaybin Museum: This museum displays ancient artifacts, including pottery, coins, sculptures, and weaponry from the Byzantine, Roman, and Ottoman eras.

Natural Attractions of Nusaybin: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Dara: This ancient city is situated on the Mesopotamian Plateau and has ruins and a Byzantine fortress to explore.
  • Kalecik Castle: This medieval fortress is located on a hilltop and offers breathtaking views of the Tigris River and the surrounding mountains.
  • Gorgan Waterfall: This 15-meter high waterfall is surrounded by lush greenery and is a perfect spot for a picnic.
  • Umutlu River: This river provides an excellent opportunity for canoeing, kayaking and rafting.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Nusaybin

Nusaybin offers a variety of hiking and biking routes for outdoor enthusiasts. Some popular routes include:

  • Dara Hiking Trail: This trail starts from the Dara ruins and leads to the ancient Roman bridge, which is still functional.
  • Kalecik Castle Trail: This 12-kilometer trail takes you through the mountains and ends at the stunning Kalecik Fortress.
  • Umutlu River Trail: This trail is a 15-kilometer route along the Umutlu River and provides an opportunity to explore the natural beauty of the region.

Best Things to Do in Nusaybin: Activities and Tours

  • Join a guided tour to explore the ancient city of Dara and the surrounding natural beauty of Nusaybin.
  • Take a hot-air balloon ride over Nusaybin for a one-of-a-kind view of the city and the Tigris River.
  • Visit the local bazaars and markets to explore the cultural heritage of Nusaybin and shop for unique souvenirs such as handmade carpets, leather goods, and jewelry.
  • Head to the Umutlu River for some rafting or kayaking adventure.
  • Experience the traditional Turkish bath, also known as Hamam.

Week-Long Itinerary for Nusaybin

Day 1 - Arrive in Nusaybin and explore the city’s cultural heritage, including the Nusaybin Walls and Grand Mosque.

Day 2 - Take a guided tour to Dara and the Kalecik Castle, and enjoy a scenic hike.

Day 3 - Visit the Nusaybin Museum to explore the ancient history of the region, followed by souvenir shopping at the local bazaars and markets.

Day 4 - Take a hot-air balloon ride over Nusaybin for breathtaking views of the city and the Tigris River.

Day 5 - Enjoy a day of adventure by rafting or kayaking on the Umutlu River.

Day 6 - Take a break and relax with a traditional Turkish bath or Hamam.

Day 7 - Depart Nusaybin and head to nearby destinations such as the ancient city of Mardin or explore the historic city of Diyarbakir.

Food and Drink Scene of Nusaybin

Nusaybin offers a unique culinary experience, with its blend of Turkish and Middle Eastern flavors. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages.

Must-Try Dishes

  • Mardin Kebab - A juicy lamb or chicken kebab served with traditional pita bread.
  • Ezogelin Soup - A traditional soup made with red lentils, bulgur, onion, and tomato paste.
  • Baklava - A sweet pastry made of layers of phyllo dough, nuts, honey, and butter.

Local Beverages

  • Ayran - A refreshing drink made of yogurt, water, and salt.
  • Salep - A hot, sweet milky drink made with orchid roots and cinnamon.
  • Turkish coffee - A strong and flavorful coffee served hot.

Where to Stay in Nusaybin: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Nusaybin offers a range of accommodation options to suit all budgets, from affordable guesthouses to luxurious hotels. Some popular options include:

  • Evim Butik Otel: A budget-friendly yet comfortable guesthouse located in the heart of Nusaybin.
  • Talay Hotel Nusaybin: A mid-range hotel with amenities such as a restaurant, bar, and free Wi-Fi.
  • Rabis Hotel: A luxurious hotel with an outdoor pool, spa, and fitness center.

What to Buy in Nusaybin: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Nusaybin is known for its unique handicrafts, textiles, and spices. Here are some popular items to buy as souvenirs:

  • Handmade carpets and rugs: Nusaybin is renowned for its colorful and intricate carpets and rugs.
  • Leather Goods: Handcrafted leather products such as bags, belts, and jackets.
  • Spices: Discover unique Middle Eastern flavors, such as sumac and za’atar.

Local Culture and Customs of Nusaybin

Nusaybin is a multicultural city that is home to various ethnicities and tribes. While the locals are welcoming and friendly, it’s important to be aware of the following customs:

  • Dress modestly when visiting historical places, religious sites, or places of worship.
  • Remove your shoes before entering a mosque or an individual’s home.
  • Be respectful of local customs and traditions.

Best Time to Visit Nusaybin

The best time to visit Nusaybin is between May and September when the weather is pleasant and ideal for outdoor activities.

Do’s and Don’ts of Nusaybin

Do:

  • Explore the cultural heritage of Nusaybin by visiting its museums, historical sites, and bazaars.
  • Experience the local cuisine and try regional dishes such as Mardin Kebab and Baklava.
  • Engage with the locals and learn about their traditions and customs.

Don’t:

  • Disrespect local customs and traditions.
  • Take photographs of people without their permission.
  • Litter in the public places.

Safety and Health

Safety in Nusaybin

Nusaybin is a safe city for travelers to visit. However, it’s advisable to take the following precautions:

  • Avoid any political demonstrations or gatherings.
  • Stay aware of your surroundings and keep an eye on your belongings.
  • Take a registered taxi or public transportation when traveling late at night.

Health in Nusaybin

While Nusaybin has a reliable healthcare system, it’s advisable to carry health insurance and take necessary precautions before traveling. Don’t forget to carry a first-aid kit with essential medicines.

Getting to Nusaybin: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Nusaybin

The nearest international airport to Nusaybin is the Gaziantep Airport (GZT), which is approximately 120 kilometers away.

Bus Stations Near Nusaybin

Nusaybin can be reached via intercity buses from Istanbul, Ankara, and other major cities in Turkey. The nearest bus station is located in Midyat, which is approximately 35 kilometers away.

Other Options

  • Take a taxi or private shuttle from nearby cities such as Mardin or Midyat.
  • Rent a car and drive to Nusaybin, taking in the scenic views along the way.

How to Get Around in Nusaybin

Nusaybin is a small city that can be easily explored on foot. However, if you prefer to use public transportation, there are frequent buses and taxis available.

Where to Go Next from Nusaybin: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Mardin: Visit this historic city that boasts unique architecture and museums.
  • Midyat: Explore the picturesque town that is renowned for its silversmiths and traditional architecture.
  • Diyarbakir: Visit the ancient city that has been inhabited since the Hellenistic era and is home to many historical sites and museums.

Conclusion

Nusaybin is a hidden cultural treasure that is waiting to be discovered. This comprehensive travel guide has provided you with important information about the city, including must-visit attractions, outdoor adventures, local cuisine, and culture, among others. Explore Nusaybin and discover its unique charm, culture, and traditions, and you’ll fall in love with this city’s warm and welcoming community.
