Burdur, located in the Lakes District of Mediterranean Turkey, is a charming city that boasts rich historical and cultural heritage, stunning natural landscapes, and a vibrant food and drink scene. Whether you’re looking for outdoor adventures, cultural experiences, or just a relaxing holiday, Burdur has something to offer for everyone. In this comprehensive travel guide, we’ll explore the must-visit places, hiking/biking routes, activities, food, and customs of Burdur to help you plan the perfect trip.

Introduction to Burdur

Burdur is a city in the Burdur Province of Turkey, nestled at the foot of the Taurus Mountains, surrounded by a number of freshwater lakes. It is known for its natural beauty, ancient history, and cultural heritage. The city dates back to ancient times, and its rich history is reflected in its many historic sites and ruins.

List of Places to Visit in Burdur

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  1. Burdur Museum: The Burdur Museum houses a fine collection of ancient artifacts, including pottery, statues, coins, and weapons.
  2. Hacılar Höyük: This ancient settlement dates back to the Neolithic period and is home to some of the world’s oldest houses.
  3. Sagalassos: This ancient city is located on the slopes of Mount Akdağ, and is home to a number of well-preserved historical sites, including the Temple of Hadrian and the Nymphaeum.

Natural Attractions of Burdur: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  1. Salda Lake: This stunning lake boasts crystal-clear waters, white sandy beaches, and breathtaking mountain views. It’s a popular spot for swimming, sunbathing, and water sports.
  2. Gölcük Lake: This small crater lake is surrounded by lush greenery and stunning views of the nearby mountains. It’s a popular spot for hiking and picnicking.
  3. Taurus Mountains: These majestic mountains offer some of the best hiking and climbing opportunities in Turkey, with stunning views of the surrounding landscapes.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Burdur

  1. Akdağ Nature Park: This park is located on the slopes of Mount Akdağ and offers a number of hiking trails that wind through the forest and offer stunning views of the surrounding mountains.
  2. Salda Lake Hiking Trail: This trail follows the shores of Salda Lake and offers stunning views of the turquoise waters and white sandy beaches.
  3. Çubuk Gorge Hiking Trail: This trail winds through the scenic Çubuk Gorge and offers stunning views of the surrounding mountains and waterfalls.

Best Things to Do in Burdur: Activities and Tours

  1. Salda Lake Boat Tour: Take a scenic boat tour of the crystal-clear waters of Salda Lake and soak up the stunning views of the surrounding mountains and beaches.
  2. Sagalassos Tour: Explore the ancient ruins of Sagalassos and learn about the city’s rich history and culture.
  3. Taurus Mountains Jeep Safari: Take a thrilling jeep safari through the rugged terrain of the Taurus Mountains and soak up the stunning views of the surrounding landscapes.

Food and Drink Scene of Burdur

Must-Try Dishes

  1. Tandır kebabı: This succulent lamb dish is slow-roasted in a traditional clay oven, giving it a rich and smoky flavor.
  2. Pide: This flatbread is often filled with cheese, meat, or vegetables, and is a popular street food in Turkey.
  3. Köfte: These spiced meatballs are often served with a creamy yogurt sauce and fresh herbs.

Local Beverages

  1. Ayran: This refreshing yogurt drink is popular throughout Turkey and is often served as a side dish with meals.
  2. Şalgam suyu: This spicy drink is made from pickled turnips and is often served as a refreshing drink with kebab or other spicy dishes.

Where to Stay in Burdur: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  1. Mövenpick Hotel Burdur: This luxury hotel is located in the city center and offers stunning views of the nearby mountains and lakes.
  2. Otel Alanur: This budget-friendly hotel is located near Salda Lake and offers comfortable rooms and easy access to the lake’s beaches.
  3. Lakeside Country Club: This charming guesthouse is nestled in the scenic countryside near Gölcük Lake and offers a peaceful and relaxing atmosphere.

What to Buy in Burdur: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  1. Kilim rugs: These colorful and intricately woven rugs are a beloved tradition of Turkey’s craft heritage.
  2. Hand-painted ceramics: Burdur is known for its vibrant and intricately decorated ceramics, which make for unique and beautiful souvenirs.
  3. Olive oil soap: This high-quality soap is made from locally grown olives and is a popular item among tourists looking for natural and eco-friendly products.

Local Culture and Customs of Burdur

Burdur is a city rich in cultural heritage, with a unique blend of Turkish, Greek, and Roman influences. The locals are friendly and hospitable, and the city is known for its warm and welcoming atmosphere. It’s important to respect their customs and traditions, such as removing your shoes before entering someone’s home or mosque, and covering your head and shoulders when visiting religious sites.

Best Time to Visit Burdur

The best time to visit Burdur is from April to October, when the weather is warm and sunny, and the outdoor activities are in full swing. However, the city is beautiful year-round, with mild winters and plenty of indoor activities and cultural events to enjoy.

Do’s and Don’ts of Burdur

Do:

  1. Respect the local customs and traditions.
  2. Take off your shoes before entering someone’s home or mosque.
  3. Try the local cuisine and drinks.

Don’t:

  1. Wear revealing clothing or swimwear in public places.
  2. Take photos of people or religious sites without permission.
  3. Haggle too aggressively in the local markets.

Safety and Health

Safety in Burdur

Burdur is a relatively safe city for tourists, with a low crime rate and friendly locals. However, it’s important to take basic safety precautions such as keeping an eye on your belongings and avoiding isolated areas at night.

Health in Burdur

There are no major health risks in Burdur, but it’s always a good idea to carry a basic first-aid kit and take precautions against insect bites and sunburn.

Getting to Burdur: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Burdur

  1. Antalya Airport: This is the nearest international airport to Burdur, located around 200 kilometers away.
  2. Dalaman Airport: This airport is located around 250 kilometers away from Burdur and is another popular option for international travelers.

Bus Stations Near Burdur

  1. Burdur Bus Station: This is the main bus station in Burdur, with regular services to all major cities in Turkey.
  2. Antalya Bus Station: This is the major bus station in Antalya and offers a number of services to Burdur and other nearby cities.

Other Options

It’s also possible to reach Burdur by car or taxi, although this is generally more expensive and time-consuming than taking public transportation.

How to Get Around in Burdur

The best way to get around Burdur is by car or taxi, as many of the major attractions are located outside of the city center. However, it’s also possible to explore the city by foot or bicycle, and many of the main sites and attractions are within walking distance of each other.

Where to Go Next from Burdur: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  1. Antalya: This vibrant coastal city is located around 200 kilometers from Burdur and is known for its ancient ruins, picturesque harbor, and vibrant nightlife.
  2. Köyceğiz: This serene lakeside town is located around 150 kilometers from Burdur and is known for its natural hot springs and peaceful atmosphere.
  3. Kalkan: This quaint seaside town is located around 220 kilometers from Burdur and is known for its ancient ruins, charming harbor, and excellent seafood restaurants.

Conclusion

Burdur is a hidden gem, offering stunning natural and cultural attractions that are sure to leave a lasting impression on visitors. Whether you’re interested in history, outdoor adventure, or just relaxing in the sun, Burdur has it all. From hiking through the Taurus Mountains to exploring the ancient ruins of Sagalassos, this charming city has something to offer for everyone, making it the perfect destination for a unique and unforgettable holiday.
