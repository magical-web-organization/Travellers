Introduction to Lakes District

Lakes District is one of the hidden gems of Turkey, located in the Mediterranean region, surrounded by scenic lakes, waterfalls, and mountains. Known as ‘Göller Bölgesi’ in Turkish, it is a popular destination among nature lovers and adventure-seekers.

The region is home to seven freshwater lakes, including Lake Eğirdir, Lake Beyşehir, and Lake Burdur. Each lake has its unique charm, offering excellent opportunities for swimming, boating, and fishing.

But Lakes District is not just about nature, it also boasts a rich cultural heritage with its fascinating museums, archaeological sites, and ancient ruins.

This travel guide will take you through the best places to visit in Lakes District, including its cultural and natural highlights, top things to do, and local cuisine.

List of Places to Visit in Lakes District

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Antioch of Pisidia Archaeological Site: A well-preserved ancient city that dates back to the Roman era, located in Yalvaç.

  • Isparta Museum: A museum that showcases the cultural heritage of Lakes District, including pottery, textiles, and ethnographic items.

  • Termessos Ancient City: A ruined city located on a mountaintop near Antalya. The city is known for its remarkable ancient architecture, including the Theater and the Agora.

  • The Mevlana Museum: A museum that honors the teachings of Rumi, a 13th-century poet and philosopher, located in Konya.

Natural Attractions of Lakes District: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Lake Eğirdir: A serene lake nestled in the mountains, offering opportunities for swimming, boating, and fishing.

  • Davraz Ski Resort: A ski resort located on the Davraz Mountain, offering fantastic ski slopes and views of the surrounding mountains.

  • Sagalassos Archaeological Site: A well-preserved ancient city that is located high in the mountains, offering breathtaking views of the surrounding landscapes.

  • Köprülü Canyon National Park: A stunning natural reserve with a river, canyons, and waterfalls, offering exciting white-water rafting and canyon trekking activities.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Lakes District

Lakes District has numerous trails for hikers and mountain bikers of all levels:

  • The Kibyra Antique City Hiking Trail: A trail that passes through the dense forests and ancient ruins of Kibyra Antique City.

  • The Gonen Pond Trail: A scenic trail that passes through the lush vegetation of Gonen Pond and offers opportunities for bird watching.

  • The Sultan Sazlığı Hiking Trail: A trail that passes through the charming Sultan Sazlığı wetlands, offering excellent bird watching opportunities.

  • The Davraz Mountain Biking Trail: A challenging trail that offers stunning views of the surrounding mountains and forests.

Best Things to Do in Lakes District: Activities and Tours

  • Take a boat tour on Lake Eğirdir: Enjoy the serene waters of Lake Eğirdir while taking in stunning mountain views.

  • Join a white-water rafting tour on Köprülü Canyon: Experience the adrenaline rush while exploring the stunning canyons and waterfalls of Köprülü Canyon National Park.

  • Explore the ancient ruins of Sagalassos: Take a guided walking tour of the well-preserved ancient city of Sagalassos, located in the mountains near Burdur.

  • Visit the traditional carpet weaving workshops: Observe local artisans creating beautiful rugs in traditional workshops.

Week-Long Itinerary for Lakes District

  • Day 1: Visit Antioch of Pisidia Archaeological Site and explore the historical ruins.

  • Day 2: Take a boat tour on Lake Eğirdir and enjoy the scenic views of the surrounding mountains.

  • Day 3: Go white-water rafting on Köprülü Canyon National Park and explore the stunning canyons.

  • Day 4: Explore the ancient ruins of Sagalassos and enjoy the panoramic views of the surrounding mountains.

  • Day 5: Visit the Mevlana Museum in Konya and discover the teachings of Rumi.

  • Day 6: Hike the Sultan Sazlığı Hiking Trail and enjoy the bird watching opportunities in the Sultan Sazlığı wetlands.

  • Day 7: Shop for traditional rugs and tapestries in one of the many carpet weaving workshops in the region.

Food and Drink Scene of Lakes District

Must-Try Dishes

  • Burrito: A savory pastry made with stuffed minced meat, potatoes, and vegetables.

  • Muhlama: A cheesy dish made with cornmeal, butter, and melted kasar cheese.

  • Kebap: A well-known Turkish dish made with marinated meat grilled over an open flame.

  • Baklava: A famous Turkish pastry made with layers of filo pastry filled with chopped nuts and syrup.

Local Beverages

  • Çay: The traditional Turkish tea, brewed using two stacked kettles.

  • Salep: A warm drink made from orchid root flour, sugar, milk, and cinnamon.

  • Boza: A fermented drink made from bulgur, sugar, and water. It is usually served with cinnamon and roasted chickpeas.

Where to Stay in Lakes District: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Lakes District has a wide range of accommodations, ranging from luxurious hotels to budget-friendly guesthouses:

  • Eğirdir Zeybek Hotel: A lakefront hotel with a private beach and a picturesque garden.

  • Köroğlu Otel: A historic hotel located in the town center of Isparta, featuring spacious rooms and traditional decor.

  • Lale Hostel: A cozy and affordable hostel located in the heart of Eğirdir.

  • Somer Pansiyon: A cozy guesthouse with a garden and a shared lounge, located within walking distance of Lake Eğirdir.

What to Buy in Lakes District: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Traditional Rugs: Lakes District is famous for its high-quality, hand-woven rugs and tapestries.

  • Rose Oil: Isparta is known for its production of rose oil, which is used in various cosmetic and medicinal products.

  • Leather Goods: Lakes District is home to many leather workshops producing high-quality leather jackets, bags, and shoes.

Local Culture and Customs of Lakes District

Lakes District has a rich and diverse cultural heritage, shaped by its history and traditions. The locals are known for their hospitality and warmth, and visitors are often greeted with a cup of Turkish tea.

When visiting Lakes District, it is important to respect the local customs and traditions. Dress modestly when visiting religious sites, and remove your shoes before entering mosques.

Best Time to Visit Lakes District

The best time to visit Lakes District is from April to October when the weather is sunny and mild. During the winter months, the region experiences heavy snowfall, making it an excellent time to visit for skiing and winter sports enthusiasts.

Do’s and Don’ts of Lakes District

Do:

  • Try the local cuisine: Lakes District is known for its delicious and unique local dishes, make sure to try them.

  • Explore the natural beauty of the region: Lakes District is renowned for its scenic lakes, waterfalls, and mountain landscapes, so make sure to explore them.

  • Be respectful towards the locals: The locals are known for their hospitality and warmth, so make sure to treat them with respect and kindness.

Don’t:

  • Waste water: Lakes District is known for its freshwater lakes, make sure to use water responsibly.

  • Litter: The region is home to many natural reserves and parks, make sure to dispose of your garbage properly.

  • Disrespect local customs: Lakes District has a rich cultural heritage, be respectful towards local customs and traditions.

Safety and Health

  • Be aware of your surroundings: Like any other city or region, make sure to be aware of your surroundings to avoid any potential threats.

  • Use a good travel guide: A good travel guide will provide you with essential safety tips and information.

Lakes District has well-equipped hospitals and medical clinics. Make sure to bring a copy of your prescription medicines, and get any necessary vaccinations before your trip.

Getting to Lakes District: Transportation Options and Logistics

Lakes District is easily accessible by air, road, and bus.

Airports Near Lakes District

  • Antalya Airport: The closest international airport, located 260 km away from Lakes District.

  • Isparta Suleyman Demirel Airport: A regional airport, located 50 km away from Lakes District.

Bus Stations Near Lakes District

  • Isparta Bus Station: A busy bus station located in the heart of the city, providing transportation to Antalya, Konya, and other destinations.

  • Eğirdir Bus Station: A small bus station providing transportation to nearby cities such as Burdur and Afyon.

Other Options

  • Taxi: Taxis are readily available in Lakes District, make sure to negotiate the fare before getting in.

  • Car Rental: Car rental services are available at the airports and bus stations.

How to Get Around in Lakes District

The best way to explore Lakes District is by renting a car or hiring a taxi. Public transportation, such as buses and dolmuşes, is also available.

Where to Go Next from Lakes District: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Antalya: A popular coastal city located 200 km away from Lakes District, known for its beautiful beaches and historical sites.

  • Cappadocia: A unique region known for its rock formations and underground cities, located 450 km away from Lakes District.

  • Pamukkale: A stunning natural site known for its terraced hot springs and white travertine terraces, located 210 km away from Lakes District.
