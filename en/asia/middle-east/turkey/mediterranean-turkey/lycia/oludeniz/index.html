Introduction to Ölüdeniz

Ölüdeniz is a resort village in the southwestern coast of Turkey in the province of Muğla. It is situated in the historical region of Lycia, which is known for its ancient ruins, scenic coastline, and turquoise waters. Ölüdeniz boasts one of the most photographed beaches in the world, the Blue Lagoon, which is a sheltered bay of calm and crystal-clear water. This guide will help you explore the must-visit places, local cuisine, outdoor adventures, and cultural experiences of Ölüdeniz.

List of Places to Visit in Ölüdeniz

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Lycian Way: The Lycian Way is a 509-kilometer long-distance trail that passes through stunning landscapes, ancient ruins, and hillside villages. It is one of the best hiking routes in the world and was named one of the top ten trails by The Sunday Times.

  • Kayaköy: Kayaköy is a ghost town that was abandoned after the population exchange between Greece and Turkey in 1923. The village consists of hundreds of stone houses, churches, and chapels that are now in ruins.

  • Butterfly Valley: Butterfly Valley is home to over 80 species of butterflies and is a perfect spot for nature lovers. It is accessible by boat or by hiking from Ölüdeniz.

Natural Attractions of Ölüdeniz: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Blue Lagoon: Blue Lagoon is a stunning sheltered bay with calm, turquoise waters. It is an ideal place for swimming, snorkeling, and sunbathing.

  • Babadag Mountain: Babadag Mountain is a paragliding spot that offers panoramic views of Ölüdeniz and its surroundings. It is one of the top paragliding destinations in the world.

  • Oludeniz Beach: Oludeniz Beach is a long strip of sandy shoreline where you can enjoy loads of fun activities like kayaking and paddleboarding in the shimmering blue waters.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Ölüdeniz

The Lycian Way is the most famous hiking trail in Ölüdeniz, stretching over 509 kilometers and passing through gorgeous scenery, ancient ruins, and charming villages. The coastal path can take up to 40 days to complete, but you can choose individual sections for shorter hikes. A popular hiking trail is the Sunset Trail, which takes you to the top of Babadag Mountain at sunset for spectacular views. Biking enthusiasts can explore the countryside on designated biking paths and discover Ölüdeniz’s authentic culture.

Best Things to Do in Ölüdeniz: Activities and Tours

  • Scuba Diving: Ölüdeniz’s pristine waters and abundant marine life make it an ideal destination for scuba diving. You can explore caves, reefs, and shipwrecks with the help of professional diving instructors.

  • Boat Trips: You can take a boat tour around Ölüdeniz and explore its beautiful bays, islands, and secluded coves. You can also try fishing or have a game of beach volleyball.

  • Turkish Bath: Turkish Bath is an authentic Ottoman-era experience where you can relax and unwind in a steamy room and enjoy a massage and scrub.

Week-Long Itinerary for Ölüdeniz

Day 1: Arrival and settling in at the hotel. Day 2: Hiking the Lycian Way’s scenic section from the Blue Lagoon to Faralya. Day 3: Paragliding from the summit of Babadag Mountain and enjoying the view from Butterfly Valley. Day 4: A day at sea, enjoying the turquoise water, sun, and boat trips. Day 5: Day trip to the ancient ruins of Patara, an important Lycian city. Day 6: Exploring the ancient city of Xanthos, the capital of Lycia, and its UNESCO World Heritage sites. Day 7: Relaxing at the beach, trying local cuisine, and souvenir shopping.

Food and Drink Scene of Ölüdeniz

Must-Try Dishes

  • Meze: Meze is a traditional Turkish appetizer made of various small dishes, such as hummus, eggplant salad, and stuffed grape leaves.

  • Pide: Pide is a Turkish-style pizza made with cheese, meat, and vegetables.

  • Şakşuka: Şakşuka is a vegetarian dish made of fried eggplant, tomato, and green pepper.

Local Beverages

  • Ayran: Ayran is a Turkish yogurt drink mixed with salt and water.

  • Sahlep: Sahlep is a hot beverage made of orchid-root powder, milk, and sugar.

  • Raki: Raki is an anise-flavored alcoholic beverage that is usually served with meze.

Where to Stay in Ölüdeniz: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Ölüdeniz has plenty of accommodation options to choose from, including luxury hotels, family-run guesthouses, and backpacker-party hostels. Here are some recommendations:

  • Liberty Hotels Lykia: A luxury hotel with all-inclusive options and direct access to the beach.

  • Shaka Camping: A budget-friendly hostel with a lively atmosphere, a pool, and entertainment activities.

  • Montenegro Motel: A family-owned and run guest house offering comfy rooms with a view and balcony.

What to Buy in Ölüdeniz: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Turkish Delight: Turkish Delight is a sweet and chewy dessert made with starch and sugar.

  • Ceramics and Pottery: Ölüdeniz has many shops selling handmade pottery, plates, and ceramic souvenirs.

  • Carpet and Rugs: Ölüdeniz has a rich carpet weaving history and is known for its high-quality rugs and carpets.

Local Culture and Customs of Ölüdeniz

Ölüdeniz has a rich cultural heritage and tradition, and its people are proud of their customs and hospitality. Here are some cultural tips to bear in mind:

  • Respectful Dressing: Although Ölüdeniz is a tourist destination, it is still a conservative Muslim country, and modest dressing is highly appreciated.

  • Tipping: Tipping is not necessary in Turkey, but it is customary to round up the bill in restaurants and cafes and to tip hotel staff and tour guides if you are satisfied with their service.

Best Time to Visit Ölüdeniz

The best time to visit Ölüdeniz is from May to October, when the weather is sunny and warm, and the sea is calm and clear. However, the peak season is July and August, and prices can be higher.

Do’s and Don’ts of Ölüdeniz

Do:

  • Try Turkish Shave: Turkish shave is a traditional grooming experience where you can get a close and refreshing shave and a facial massage.

  • Learn Some Turkish Phrases: Learning some Turkish phrases can make your trip more enjoyable and help you connect with locals.

Don’t:

  • Drink Tap Water: It is not advisable to drink tap water in Turkey, so always opt for bottled water.

  • Take Photos Without Permission: Always ask for permission before taking pictures of locals or religious sites.

Safety and Health

Safety in Ölüdeniz

Ölüdeniz is a safe destination for tourists, but as with any other place, it is important to take precautions and be aware of pickpocketing and scams.

Health in Ölüdeniz

Make sure you have comprehensive travel insurance and bring necessary medications, sunscreen, and insect repellents. Drink plenty of bottled water, wear sunscreen, and avoid food and drinks from unhygienic places.

Getting to Ölüdeniz: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Ölüdeniz

The nearest airport to Ölüdeniz is Dalaman International Airport, which is about 60 kilometers away. Dalaman Airport has regular flights to and from various European destinations, and domestic flights are available from major Turkish cities.

Bus Stations Near Ölüdeniz

Fethiye Bus Station is the main bus station serving Ölüdeniz, and it is situated about 14 kilometers from the resort. Regular buses run between Fethiye and Ölüdeniz.

Other Options

Taxis and private transfers are available from Dalaman Airport, Fethiye town, and other parts of the region.

How to Get Around in Ölüdeniz

Ölüdeniz is a small village, so you can easily explore it on foot. Bicycles and scooters are available for rent, and taxis are available too. Many tourists also rent a car to explore the surrounding areas.

Where to Go Next from Ölüdeniz: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Ölüdeniz is surrounded by many beautiful places that are worth exploring:

  • Fethiye: Fethiye is a charming seaside town with a beautiful marina, traditional markets, and ancient ruins.

  • Saklıkent Canyon: Saklıkent Canyon is a stunning natural wonder that is perfect for hiking, swimming, and photo-taking.

  • Dalyan: Dalyan is a small town that is situated alongside a river, with a variety of outdoor activities, turtle conservation centres, and the architectural ruins of the ancient Lycian city of Kaunos.

Conclusion

Ölüdeniz is a dazzling destination that offers a unique blend of natural wonders, cultural richness, and outdoor adventures. With this comprehensive guide, you can plan your trip to this paradise and create lasting memories. Exploring Ölüdeniz’s crystal waters, immersing yourself in the historical and cultural landmarks, savouring its authentic cuisine, and enjoying the Turkish hospitality will undoubtedly make your trip unforgettable.
