Kayaköy is a charming and historical village situated in the Lycian region of Mediterranean Turkey. Set amidst the lush green hillside, the village boasts a rich history, natural landscapes, and a laid-back atmosphere that attracts both adventure seekers and cultural enthusiasts alike. In this comprehensive travel guide, we’ll provide you with all the information you need to plan your ultimate trip to Kayaköy.

Introduction to Kayaköy

Kayaköy, also known as Karmylassos, is a picturesque village situated near Fethiye, in the Muğla province of Turkey. The village was originally inhabited by Greeks until 1923, when the population was exchanged with Turkish citizens from Greece. The village has remained relatively untouched since then and has become a popular destination for tourists seeking a glimpse into Turkey’s rich history and culture.

List of Places to Visit in Kayaköy

There are plenty of things to see and do in Kayaköy, from exploring its historical attractions to enjoying scenic views of the surrounding landscapes. Here are some of the top places to visit in the village.

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Kayaköy Ghost Town: This abandoned town is a must-see when visiting Kayaköy. The town features Greek-style houses and is an eerie reminder of the past.

  • Church of St. Mary: This Orthodox church is built into a cliff and offers stunning views of the surrounding area. It has been well-preserved and features ornate walls and detailed frescoes.

  • Museum of Kayaköy: The museum offers an insight into the history of the village, with exhibits on the exchange of populations and the forced migration of the Greek community from Kayaköy to Greece.

Natural Attractions of Kayaköy: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Lycian Way Trail: This trail offers a challenging hike through the rugged terrain of the Taurus Mountains. The trail offers stunning views of the surrounding landscapes.

  • Butterfly Valley: Located at the foot of Mount Babadağ, this hidden valley features a waterfall, beach, and plenty of opportunities for hiking and birdwatching.

  • Gemile Beach: Tucked away in a secluded cove, this beach offers crystal-clear waters and stunning views of the Mediterranean Sea.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Kayaköy

Kayaköy, situated in the Taurus Mountains, offers plenty of opportunities for hiking and biking enthusiasts. Here are some of the top routes to explore.

  • Lycian Way: This 540-kilometer trail starts in Fethiye and passes through Kayaköy. The trail offers challenging terrain and stunning views of the surrounding landscapes.

  • Babadag Mountain: The mountain is a popular spot for paragliding but also offers hiking routes with stunning views over the coastline.

Best Things to Do in Kayaköy: Activities and Tours

There are plenty of things to do in Kayaköy that cater to different interests, ages, and budgets. Here are some of the best activities and tours to experience.

  • Kayaköy Village Tour: Take a guided tour of the village and get an insight into its history and culture.

  • Sea Kayaking: Explore the beautiful bays, beaches, and caves in the region by sea kayak.

  • Jeep Safari: Take a bumpy ride through the rugged terrain of the Taurus Mountains and explore the hidden gems of the region.

Food and Drink Scene of Kayaköy

The food scene in Kayaköy is heavily influenced by its Greek heritage, with fresh ingredients, seafood, and olive oil playing starring roles in the local cuisine.

Must-Try Dishes

  • Moussaka: A traditional baked dish made with eggplant, minced meat, and béchamel sauce.

  • Dolma: Stuffed vegetables such as peppers, tomatoes, or grape leaves with a mixture of rice, onions, herbs, and spices.

  • Baklava: A sweet pastry made with layers of filo dough, filled with chopped nuts and sweetened with syrup or honey.

Local Beverages

  • Raki: A traditional alcoholic drink made with anise and distilled grape juice. It is usually enjoyed with meze dishes.

  • Turkish tea: A strong, black tea that is a staple of Turkish culture.

What to Buy in Kayaköy: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Kayaköy offers many unique souvenirs and local products that you can take home as a reminder of your trip. Here are some suggestions.

  • Olive oil: Kayaköy produces high-quality olive oil, which makes an excellent souvenir or a gift.

  • Handwoven carpets and rugs: Kayaköy is well-known for its traditional handwoven carpets and rugs made by local artisans.

  • Ceramic pottery: The village boasts several pottery shops selling handcrafted pottery, including plates, vases, and bowls.

Local Culture and Customs of Kayaköy

Kayaköy has a rich cultural heritage, with a mix of Greek and Turkish traditions. The locals are welcoming and hospitable, and it’s essential to respect their customs and traditions. Here are some things to keep in mind.

  • Dress modestly: When visiting mosques or other religious sites, dress modestly and cover your head, knees, and shoulders.

  • Remove your shoes: It’s customary to remove your shoes when entering someone’s home or a mosque.

  • Tipping: Tipping is not mandatory, but it’s appreciated as a gesture of thanks for excellent service.

Best Time to Visit Kayaköy

The best time to visit Kayaköy is during the shoulder seasons of spring (April-May) and autumn (September-November), when the temperatures are mild, and the crowds are fewer. However, the summer months of June-August offer plenty of outdoor activities and festivals. It’s best to avoid visiting Kayaköy during the winter months, as it can get cold and rainy.

Do’s and Don’ts of Kayaköy

Here are some do’s and don’ts to keep in mind when visiting Kayaköy.

Do:

  • Learn some Turkish: The locals appreciate it when visitors try to speak some Turkish, even if it’s a simple greeting.

  • Respect the environment: Kayaköy is a natural paradise, and it’s crucial to respect the environment by not littering or damaging the flora and fauna.

  • Try the local cuisine: The food in Kayaköy is delicious and diverse, and it’s worth trying different dishes to get a taste of the local cuisine.

Don’t:

  • Take photos without permission: Some locals may not want their photo taken, so always ask for permission before taking pictures.

  • Haggle excessively: It’s customary to haggle at markets, but don’t do it excessively, as it can be disrespectful.

  • Disrespect religious sites: It’s essential to respect mosques and other religious sites, dress appropriately and avoid eating or drinking inside.

Safety and Health

Kayaköy is a safe destination for tourists, but it’s important to take some precautions to stay safe and healthy.

Safety in Kayaköy

  • Be careful of uneven terrain: The hiking and biking routes in Kayaköy can be challenging and have uneven terrain. Wear proper footwear and take necessary safety measures.

  • Avoid isolated areas at night: It’s best to avoid isolated areas at night, especially if you’re alone.

  • Use reputable tour operators: If you’re taking tours, make sure to choose a reputable tour operator with excellent safety records.

Health in Kayaköy

  • Stay hydrated: The temperatures can get hot in Kayaköy during the summer months, so make sure to stay hydrated.

  • Be cautious of food and water: Make sure to only eat and drink from reputable sources to avoid food poisoning.

  • Pack necessary medication: If you have any pre-existing medical conditions, make sure to pack all necessary medications.

Getting to Kayaköy: Transportation Options and Logistics

Getting to Kayaköy is relatively easy, with several transport options available. Here are some of the most common options.

Airports Near Kayaköy

  • Dalaman International Airport: The airport is located approximately 58 km from Kayaköy and offers both international and domestic flights.

Bus Stations Near Kayaköy

  • Fethiye Bus Station: The bus station is located approximately 8 km from Kayaköy and offers regular bus services to several Turkish cities.

Other Options

  • Taxis: Taxis are readily available but can be expensive.

  • Renting a car: Renting a car is a popular option for tourists who want to explore the region independently.

How to Get Around in Kayaköy

Kayaköy is a small village that is best explored on foot. However, if you want to explore the surrounding areas, renting a car or taking a taxi is your best bet.

Where to Go Next from Kayaköy: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Kayaköy is situated in an area that offers plenty of nearby destinations and excursions. Here are some top options to consider.

  • Fethiye: The bustling harbor town is just a few kilometers away from Kayaköy and offers a range of activities, including boat tours, markets, and nightlife.

  • Oludeniz: The stunning coastal town is known for its clear blue waters and offers plenty of opportunities for water sports, including paragliding and kiteboarding.

  • Saklikent National Park: The park is a popular destination for hiking and offers stunning views of the canyon and waterfalls.

Conclusion

Kayaköy is a unique destination that offers a mix of cultural and natural attractions. The village’s rich history, pristine landscapes, and friendly locals make it a must-visit for anyone exploring the Lycian region of Turkey.
