Introduction to Kaş

Kaş is a charming town located on the Mediterranean coast in southwestern Turkey. It is situated in the region of Lycia, offering deep-blue water, ancient ruins, and natural beauty. Kaş offers an astonishing collection of archaeological legacies and stunning scenery, making it a dream destination for travelers.

List of Places to Visit in Kaş

There are plenty of things that Kaş has to offer. Here is a curated list of places to visit while exploring Kaş:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Antiphellos Ancient City, situated on the hills close to the center of Kaş, showcasing a theatre, a necropolis, and a Roman bath.
  • Kaputaş Beach, with the gorgeous view of crystal-clear turquoise water, white sand and rocky cliffs on both sides
  • The Hellenistic theatre of Xanthos, located 45 km away from Kaş
  • The sunken city of Kekova, a Lycian city that was submerged underwater due to an earthquake
  • The Patara archeological site, the birthplace of Apollonius of Rhodes, showcasing historic tombs and ruins from Lycian, Roman, Byzantine, and Ottoman periods

Natural Attractions of Kaş: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Paradise Bay, located on the Kalkan road, sand and pebble beach, a great place for snorkeling and sunbathing
  • Butterfly Valley, situated between Fethiye and Kaş, a unique spot that is dominated by more than 80 species of butterflies
  • Akyaka, the most stunning bay on the Turkish Riviera, located within the borders of Muğla-Akyaka, featuring mesmerizing oxygen-rich atmosphere
  • Patara Beach, a 12km white sand beach, perfect for swimming and sunbathing while enjoying the incredible nature around
  • Saklikent Canyon, a great place to take a break from the hot and humid weather during the summer season.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Kaş

Whether you are into outdoor excursions, solo traveling, or adrenaline adventures, there is plenty of hiking and biking routes to explore in Kaş. You can observe lush valleys, mountainous terrains, and amazingly colorful nature while hiking through places like The Lycian Way, The Carian Trail, and The Saint Nicholas Way.

Best Things to Do in Kaş: Activities and Tours

Kaş offers plenty of activities and tours, have fun while exploring the ancient ruins, or try some adventure sports like diving, paragliding, and kayaking. Here are some of the best things to do in Kaş:

  • Take a traditional Turkish bath, and get your skin exfoliated in a hot steam room while sipping delicious Turkish tea.
  • Go paragliding, and take breathtaking aerial shoots over the coastline and sunken city of Kekova.
  • Take a sunset boat trip to ask your loved one to marry you
  • Learn scuba diving from certified guides and experience admiring the underwater beauty at Kaş diving sites
  • Visit a traditional Turkish Carpet shop, witness handmade carpets and rugs, and shop for souvenirs for your loved ones.

Week-Long Itinerary for Kaş

  • Day 1: Visit the ancient tombs of Antiphellos Ancient City and then relax on the beach at Kaputaş
  • Day 2: Go diving or snorkeling and explore the underwater world of Kaş
  • Day 3: Take a trekking route at the Saklikent Canyon
  • Day 4: Go on a day trip to the sunken city of Kekova
  • Day 5: Visit the Patara Archeological Site and spend some time lounging on Patara Beach
  • Day 6: Take a day trip to nearby historical places like The Hellenistic theatre of Xanthos and The Patara Archaeological Site
  • Day 7: Shop for some souvenirs, and enjoy the nightlife of Kaş.

Food and Drink Scene of Kaş

Kaş is a foodie’s paradise, offering some of the finest local dishes, snacks, and drinks in the Mediterranean. Local restaurants and markets offer traditional dishes like mezze plates, grilled octopus, and stuffed grape leaves. Here are a few local delicacies to try:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Çiğ Kofte (raw meatballs made with bulgur)
  • Tandır (slow-cooked meat)
  • Şiş Kebab (marinated chunks of meat grilled on skewers)
  • Manti (Turkish dumplings filled with ground meat)

Local Beverages

  • Raki (anise-flavored alcoholic drink)
  • Ayran (a refreshing yogurt beverage)
  • Türk Kahvesi (Turkish coffee)

Local Culture and Customs of Kaş

Kaş has a distinctive culture and customs that you should be aware of when visiting so as not to offend anyone. One should dress appropriately, and women should cover their shoulders before entering a mosque. Tipping is customary in restaurants and cafes, and bargaining in shops and markets is acceptable.

Best Time to Visit Kaş

The best time to visit Kaş is from April until October when there is more pleasant weather and less rainfall. Visiting during the mild spring and autumn season can be a good option if you want to avoid the crowd during peak summers.

Do’s and Don’ts of Kaş

Do:

  • Do hire a professional licensed tour guide to explore ancient places and understand the history behind them.
  • Do respect local customs and traditions while visiting mosques and heritage places.

Don’t:

  • Don’t leave your belongings unattended while visiting crowded tourist spots.
  • Don’t take the Lycian ruins which have been preserved for thousands of years.

Safety and Health

Safety in Kaş

Kaş is a relatively safe place compared to other parts of Turkey, and tourists can enjoy this area without fear. However, visitors should be alert to pickpockets and purse snatchers in busy tourist areas.

Health in Kaş

Kaş is a safe place for visitors from health hazards like food poisoning or waterborne infections. Travelers should be careful with swimming in the sea and avoid diving and snorkeling activities altogether if they have any medical issues.

Getting to Kaş: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Kaş

The nearest airport to Kaş is the Dalaman Airport, approximately 140 km away. Visitors can hire a private car or catch a scheduled transfer bus to reach Kaş from the airport.

Bus Stations Near Kaş

The destination for intercity buses traveling to Kaş is the main bus station, which is located near the center of the town. From there, visitors can choose from a variety of transportation options to reach hotels or other parts of the town.

Other Options

Visitors can also book a rental car service or private transfer to reach Kaş, making it more convenient to travel.

How to Get Around in Kaş

Kaş is a small town, and visitors can easily explore its charms on foot or by bike. The town also offers an affordable local minibus service connecting tourists to the nearby popular tourist destination.
