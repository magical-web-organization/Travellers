Introduction to Demre

Nestled on the Mediterranean coast of Turkey, Demre is a hidden gem that boasts a rich cultural heritage, stunning natural scenery, and warm hospitality. Formerly known as Myra, this ancient city in the Lycia region dates back to the 5th century BC and was an important stop on the trade route between Rome and Byzantine.

Until the present day, Demre retains its position as a popular Turkish destination. With stunning beaches, archaeological sites, and a rich history, this small town is a perfect place for a relaxing holiday.

List of Places to Visit in Demre

Discover the cultural and natural gems of Demre through these must-visit attractions:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Church of St. Nicholas: One of the most popular tourist destinations in Demre is the Church of St. Nicholas. This ancient church, built-in 520 AD, is home to the tomb of Saint Nicholas, the inspiration for Santa Claus.
  • Andriake Ancient City: This ancient port city located in Demre is a testament to Lycian civilization. Visitors can explore the ruins of Agora, granaries, public baths, and numerous shops that once stood in this city.
  • Myra Ancient City: This ancient city is known for its impressive architecture, including the impressive Lycian tombs carved in the rock faces, which date back to the 4th century BC.

Natural Attractions of Demre: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Kekova Island: This picturesque island located offshore Demre is home to the well-preserved ruins of a sunken city, best explored by boat, and revealing an underwater world.
  • Simena Castle: Located in the quaint fishing village of Kaleköy, this castle offers stunning views of the Mediterranean coast and a glimpse at Lycian history, including ancient tombs and a 4th-century BC theater.
  • Yanartaş: This natural phenomenon, also known as the “Chimera” fires, is a series of vents that spew flames from the mountain. The site is shrouded in mythology, and the unique setting makes for an unforgettable sightseeing experience.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Demre

Demre has plenty of hiking and biking trails, and these are some of the most popular routes:

  • Lycian Way: This ancient trail covers over 500 kilometers and is one of the most popular hiking trails in Turkey. It runs along the Mediterranean coastline of Turkey, offering breathtaking sea views and encounters with Lycian ruins.
  • Gökgemile Beach Trail: This trail starts at Demre Castle and meanders through pine forests before leading to one of the most secluded beaches in the area.
  • Myra River Canyon Trail: This trail offers an unforgettable journey through the Myra Canyon, exposing the natural beauty of this region.

Best Things to Do in Demre: Activities and Tours

  • Boat Tour: Take a leisurely boat ride along Kekova Island and discover the sunken ruins of an ancient city.
  • Paragliding: Experience the thrill of soaring through the air while enjoying breathtaking views of the Mediterranean coastline.
  • Jeep Safari: Enjoy a fun and adventurous tour of Demre’s rugged terrain in a Jeep.

Food and Drink Scene of Demre

Demre’s food and drink scene offers a blend of traditional Turkish and Mediterranean flavors:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Yaprak Sarma / Dolma: This traditional Turkish dish is a wrap made of vinegar-soaked grape leaves and filled with a flavorful mixture of rice, herbs, and pine nuts.
  • Karniyarik: This dish consists of stuffed eggplant with seasoned ground meat and onions baked in olive oil and tomato sauce.
  • Lahmacun: Lahmacun is a thin, crunchy Turkish pizza with a topping made of minced meat, tomatoes, onions, and spices.

Local Beverages

  • Raki: This popular alcoholic beverage is anise flavored and is commonly served with mezze, a selection of small dishes.
  • Ayran: Ayran is a refreshing non-alcoholic drink made of yogurt, salt, and water.

Where to Stay in Demre: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Demre offers a range of accommodations from budget-friendly hostels to luxurious beachfront resorts. Some of the best accommodations include:

  • Demre Inn: This budget-friendly inn offers a comfortable stay in a central location that is ideal for exploring the city. The staff is friendly, and the breakfast is fantastic.
  • Gül Konakları: This historic house has been converted into a charming boutique hotel that offers a unique experience of Demre’s cultural heritage.
  • The Lykia Edrassa Hotels: This hotel offers upscale accommodation with stunning sea views, featuring a private beach, swimming pools, and spa facilities.

What to Buy in Demre: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

When visiting Demre, look out for these souvenirs:

  • Lycian Artifacts: Demre is rich in Lycian history, and visitors can purchase miniature versions of the ancient Lycian tombs as souvenirs.
  • Olive Wood Carvings: Demre is surrounded by olive groves, and local artisans craft unique pieces of art from olive wood.
  • Handwoven Rugs: The region around Demre is famous for its intricate handwoven rugs, and visitors can purchase unique pieces to take home as souvenirs.

Local Culture and Customs of Demre

As a predominantly Muslim society, it is essential to dress conservatively when visiting Demre. Visitors should be respectful of the local culture and customs and dress modestly when entering religious sites.

Best Time to Visit Demre

The best time to visit Demre is from April to June or September to November. During these periods, the weather is mild, and tourist crowds are few, allowing for a more enjoyable experience.

Do’s and Don’ts of Demre

Do:

  • Respect the local culture and customs.
  • Try the traditional Turkish food and delicacies.
  • Bargain while shopping at local bazaars.

Don’t:

  • Dress inappropriately when entering religious sites.
  • Argue with taxi drivers or tour operators.
  • Drink tap water, it is not recommended, they have bottled water instead.

Safety and Health

Safety in Demre

Demre is generally a safe destination, but visitors should exercise caution and be aware of their surroundings, especially when traveling alone at night or in unfamiliar areas.

Health in Demre

There are no specific health concerns when traveling to Demre. However, visitors should ensure that they are up-to-date with routine vaccinations before traveling and take precautions while swimming in the Mediterranean waters, especially avoiding sea urchins.

Getting to Demre: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Demre

The nearest airport to Demre is the Antalya International Airport, located approximately 130 kilometers from Demre.

Bus Stations Near Demre

The nearest bus station is located in the neighboring city of Antalya. Daily bus services connect Antalya and Demre.

Other Options

Taxis, cars, and bikes are available for hire, and they provide a more flexible mode of transportation.

How to Get Around in Demre

The best way to explore Demre is on foot, especially when exploring the historic city center. Alternatively, visitors can hire a bicycle, scooter, or car to access some more remote areas like Kekova Island or Myra Canyon.

Where to Go Next from Demre: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Antalya: This bustling city, 130 kilometers away from Demre, is a hub for culture, history, and nightlife.
  • Olympos: Located 30 kilometers away from Demre, Olympos is known for its sprawling ruins and stunning beaches.
  • Kaş: This picturesque coastal town is located 50 kilometers away from Demre and is known for its tranquil atmosphere, colorful cobbled streets, and stunning beaches.

Conclusion

Demre is a perfect destination for travelers looking to explore the hidden gems of Turkey. From stunning ruins, outdoor activities, to delicious local cuisine, Demre’s cultural and natural beauty makes it a must-visit place. Be sure to explore museums, enjoy outdoor activities, and taste traditional Turkish food while in Demre.
