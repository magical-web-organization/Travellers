Introduction to Finike

Nestled on the Mediterranean coast of Turkey, Finike is a small yet picturesque town that promises a memorable vacation to its visitors. Known for its unspoiled natural beauty and rich history, this town has something to offer for everyone.

List of Places to Visit in Finike

Whether you’re a history buff, nature lover, adventure seeker, or simply want to relax and soak in the sun, Finike has plenty of options to explore. Here are some of the top spots to visit:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Finike Archeological Museum: This museum houses a vast collection of artifacts that showcase the town’s ancient history.
  • Phaselis Ancient City: Located 15 km from Finike, this well-preserved ancient city is a testament to the town’s rich cultural heritage.
  • St. Paul’s Trail: A historical trail that takes you through scenic forests and ancient Lycian ruins.

Natural Attractions of Finike: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Kemer National Park: This national park boasts a stunning coastline, crystal-clear waters, and an array of marine life
  • Olympos Coastal National Park: A perfect spot for nature enthusiasts, with its combination of serene beaches, hidden coves, and lush greenery
  • Göynük Canyon: An ideal hiking spot that rewards you with mesmerizing waterfalls and natural pools.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Finike

Finike is a haven for hikers and bikers, with several scenic routes that take you through picturesque landscapes. Some of the best trails include:

  • Lycian Way: A 500-km trail that spans the entire length of the coastline, providing some of the best views of the Mediterranean.
  • Olympos Beach to Adrasan: A scenic trail that takes you through stunning beaches and rolling hills.
  • Phaselis Ancient City to Beycik Village: A historic trail that passes through ancient ruins, rocky terrain, and lush forests.

Best Things to Do in Finike: Activities and Tours

From exploring the town’s rich history to indulging in outdoor adventures, Finike offers a plethora of exciting activities to choose from. Here are some of the best things to do:

  • Boat Tour: Explore the town’s coastline on a boat tour that takes you through hidden coves, turquoise waters, and charming beaches.
  • Paragliding: Soar above the Mediterranean coastline and take in breathtaking views of the town from above.
  • Scuba Diving: Discover the vibrant marine life that lies beneath the azure waters of the Mediterranean Sea.

Week-Long Itinerary for Finike

To make the most of your vacation in Finike, here is a recommended itinerary for a week-long trip:

Day Activity
1 Visit Phaselis Ancient City and the Finike Archeological Museum

Food and Drink Scene of Finike

Finike’s culinary scene is a blend of traditional Turkish and Mediterranean flavors that tantalize your taste buds. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Manti: A type of Turkish dumpling, filled with spicy meat and served with yoghurt and tomato sauce
  • Kebabs: A popular Turkish dish, consisting of grilled meat, and vegetables on skewers
  • Baklava: A rich Turkish dessert made of layers of phyllo pastry, chopped nuts, and honey syrup

Local Beverages

  • Turkish Tea: A classic black tea that is a staple in Turkish households
  • Ayran: A refreshing drink made with yoghurt and water
  • Sahlep: A traditional hot drink made with milk, sugar, and orchid root powder

Where to Stay in Finike: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Finike offers a variety of accommodation options for every budget and preference. From luxurious hotels to cozy guesthouses, here are some top picks:

  • Kale Hotel: A family-run hotel in the heart of the town, offering comfortable rooms with stunning sea views.
  • Simena Pension: A charming guesthouse with a traditional Turkish décor, located close to the beach.
  • Botanik Camping: An idyllic camping spot that offers tranquility, nature, and outdoor activities.

What to Buy in Finike: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

When in Finike, don’t miss out on bringing some of the town’s authentic souvenirs and local products back home. Here are some of the best things to buy:

  • Lycian Ceramic Ware: Beautifully crafted ceramics that reflect the ancient Lycian culture.
  • Finike Olive Oil: A local specialty that is known for its unique flavor.
  • Turkish Delight: A popular Turkish treat made of sugar and nuts, available in a variety of flavors.

Local Culture and Customs of Finike

Finike’s culture is an amalgamation of diverse influences from different civilizations that have lived here. The locals are warm, welcoming, and proud of their heritage. It is customary to remove your shoes before entering someone’s house or a mosque.

Best Time to Visit Finike

The best time to visit Finike is during the spring and autumn season, from April to May and September to November. These months offer pleasant weather, fewer crowds, and reasonable prices. Summers can be hot and humid, while winters are mild and rainy.

Do’s and Don’ts of Finike

Do:

  • Dress modestly when visiting religious sites
  • Greet people with “Merhaba,” which means hello in Turkish
  • Sample the local cuisine and enjoy its diverse flavors

Don’t:

  • Show public displays of affection
  • Enter a mosque without appropriate clothing
  • Refuse an offered cup of tea, as it is a gesture of hospitality and friendship

Safety and Health

Safety in Finike

Finike is a safe town with low crime rates. However, it is always advisable to take necessary precautions and be aware of your surroundings. Keep your valuables safe and avoid wandering alone late at night.

Health in Finike

The standard of healthcare facilities in Finike is good, with both public and private hospitals available. It is mandatory to have travel insurance before visiting Turkey to ensure medical coverage.

Getting to Finike: Transportation Options and Logistics

Finike is well-connected to major cities in Turkey, with several transportation options available.

Airports Near Finike

  • Antalya Airport: Located 100 km from Finike, it is the closest airport to the town.

Bus Stations Near Finike

  • Antalya Bus Station (Otogar): A major bus station that connects Finike to all major cities in Turkey.

Other Options

  • Car Rental: A good option for those who prefer to travel at their own pace and explore the town’s surrounding areas.
  • Taxi: Taxis are easily available, but make sure to negotiate the price beforehand.

How to Get Around in Finike

Finike is a small town that is easy to navigate on foot. However, you can also rent a bike or a scooter to explore the town’s surrounding areas.

Where to Go Next from Finike: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Finike’s strategic location on the Mediterranean coast makes it an ideal starting point for exploring nearby attractions. Some of the top destinations to visit include:

  • Olympos: A charming beach town with a rich history and laid-back vibe.
  • Demre: Home to the ancient city of Myra and the famous St. Nicholas Church.
  • Kaş: A scenic coastal town that is perfect for diving, snorkeling, and soaking up the Mediterranean sun.

Conclusion

Finike is an off-the-beaten-path gem that has something for everyone. With its rich history, scenic landscapes, and exciting activities, it promises a memorable vacation that you will cherish for a lifetime. Plan your trip in advance, explore the town’s hidden corners, and immerse yourself in its unique culture and charm.
