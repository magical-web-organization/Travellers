Introduction to Kemer

Kemer is a small town located in the region of Lycia, on the Mediterranean coast of Turkey. It is one of the most popular vacation spots in the country, with crystal-clear water, breathtaking scenery, and a warm climate. Kemer offers a unique blend of natural beauty and cultural significance that makes it an ideal destination for travelers seeking adventure, history, and relaxation.

List of Places to Visit in Kemer

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Phaselis Antique City: This ancient city has a rich history dating back to the 7th century BC. It offers visitors a glimpse of the Roman, Byzantine, and Ottoman eras.
  • Olympos Ruins: Another historical site worth exploring; it is located in a valley surrounded by natural beauty.
  • Chimera: This natural flame has been burning continuously for over 2000 years and is a must-visit spot in the region.

Natural Attractions of Kemer: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Tahtali Mountain: The highest peak in the region offers a stunning view of the coastline.
  • Goynuk Canyon: Enjoy a scenic hike, swim in crystal-clear water, and experience beautiful waterfalls.
  • Moonlight Beach: A hidden gem in Kemer; this stunning beach offers white sands, crystal blue waters, and beautiful views.
  • Adrasan Beach: This secluded beach provides a peaceful atmosphere and beautiful scenery.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Kemer

  • Lycian Way: This 500 km route is ideal for long-distance hikers, and it takes visitors through beautiful coastal trails and forests.
  • Olympos-Cirali Route: This beautiful hiking trail offers beautiful views of the surrounding beaches, forests, and ridges.
  • Beydaglari Coastal National Park: This park offers various hiking options, including Lycian Way, Phaselis Way, and Yanartas trails with stunning views of mountains and coastline.

Best Things to Do in Kemer: Activities and Tours

  • Water sports: Kemer is known for its water sports, including parasailing, jet skiing, and scuba diving.
  • ATV rides: Explore the beautiful scenery of the region with ATV tours designed for adrenaline junkies.
  • Boat tours: Discover the hidden gems of the coast and enjoy great views of the Mediterranean.
  • Turkish bath: Experience the Turkish bath, a unique traditional bath with steaming hot water and a relaxing atmosphere.

Week-Long Itinerary for Kemer

Day 1-3: Explore the beaches of Kemer, including Moonlight Beach and Adrasan Beach, and enjoy some water sports. Day 4: Visit the ancient city of Phaselis and explore the ruins. Day 5: Take a boat tour and visit secluded beaches and hidden coves. Day 6: Go hiking in Tahtali Mountain, the highest peak in the region. Day 7: Experience the Turkish bath and relax before departing.

Food and Drink Scene of Kemer

Must-Try Dishes

  • Pide: A traditional Turkish dish, similar to pizza, commonly served with meat, cheese, or vegetables.
  • Kebab: A popular dish made with grilled meat, served with rice or vegetables.
  • Borek: This is a savory pastry filled with spinach, cheese, or meat.

Local Beverages

  • Raki: A traditional alcoholic beverage, often served with meze, a type of tapas in Turkey.
  • Ayran: A salty yogurt drink, commonly served with meat dishes as a refreshing drink.

Where to Stay in Kemer: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Kemer has many options for visitors who want to stay in hotels, hostels, or guesthouses. Some of the most popular options include:

  • Rixos Premium Tekirova: This five-star hotel is located in the heart of Kemer and offers breathtaking views of the sea.
  • Fame Residence: This four-star hotel is located near the beach and is a great option for those on a budget.
  • Kemer Lodge Hotel: A cozy guesthouse located near popular attractions, offering comfortable accommodation options.

What to Buy in Kemer: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Turkish Delight: A popular Turkish dessert, made with confectionery sugar, corn starch, and a variety of flavors.
  • Turkish Tea: A staple beverage in Turkey, tea is widely available in Kemer and comes in a variety of flavors.
  • Kilim Rugs: Handwoven rugs, blankets, and tapestries produced with traditional methods.

Local Culture and Customs of Kemer

Kemer is a conservative region of Turkey, and visitors should always be respectful of the local customs and culture. Visitors should dress modestly and learn some basic Turkish phrases to communicate with locals respectfully.

Best Time to Visit Kemer

The best time to visit Kemer is during the summer months, from June to September. The weather during these months is warm and sunny, and the sea temperature is ideal for swimming and water sports.

Do’s and Don’ts of Kemer

Do:

  • Respect local customs and culture.
  • Dress modestly in public areas.
  • Learn some basic Turkish phrases for better communication with locals.

Don’t:

  • Drink tap water; stick to bottled water.
  • Allow street vendors to pressure you into buying things.
  • Wear revealing clothing in public areas.

Safety and Health

Safety in Kemer

Kemer is a safe destination for tourists, with low crime rates. However, it is always advisable to take precautions, such as staying in well-lit areas at night and hiding valuables.

Health in Kemer

Visitors should always drink bottled water, and sun protection is important, especially during the summer months. For emergencies, visitors can visit Kemer State Hospital located in the center.

Getting to Kemer: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Kemer

The closest airports to Kemer are Antalya International Airport and Gazipasa-Alanya Airport, both offering regular flights from major cities in Turkey.

Bus Stations Near Kemer

Kemer has a bus station located in the center, where visitors can take a bus to major cities in Turkey, including Istanbul and Ankara.

Other Options

Visitors can rent a car to explore the region on their own or take a taxi. The most popular transportation method, however, is taking a Dolmus, a shared minibus that runs between cities.

How to Get Around in Kemer

Dolmus is the most common mode of transportation in Kemer, running between Kemer’s center and neighboring cities. Visitors can also rent bicycles or motorcycles to explore the beautiful scenery of the region.

Where to Go Next from Kemer: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Visitors can take day trips to Antalya and explore its famous Old Town and beaches or visit the nearby city of Olympos for historical and natural beauty.

Conclusion

Kemer offers the perfect blend of adventure, relaxation, and natural beauty, making it an ideal destination for tourists from all over the world. From historical landmarks to natural wonders, Kemer has something for everyone. Use this guide to plan your next trip to Kemer and create unforgettable memories!
