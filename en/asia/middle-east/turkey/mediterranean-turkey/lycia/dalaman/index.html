Introduction to Dalaman

Located in the Lycian region of Mediterranean Turkey, Dalaman is a picturesque town that has become a popular tourist destination for its stunning natural beauty and rich historical heritage. This town is steeped in history, from ancient ruins and centuries-old buildings to traditional markets and stunningly beautiful beaches. If you’re planning a trip to Dalaman, here is a comprehensive guide to the best places to visit, things to do, and how to make the most out of your stay.

List of Places to Visit in Dalaman

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

One of the main draws of Dalaman is its rich cultural history, with a plethora of museums and historical attractions that are waiting to be explored.

Kayakoy Ghost Town

Kayakoy Ghost Town is a deserted village that was abandoned by its inhabitants during the population exchange between Greece and Turkey in the early 20th century. Today, the village remains an eerie reminder of a bygone era, and the ruins of its stone houses, churches, and public buildings are a fascinating sight to behold.

Likya Tombs of Dalyan

The Lycian Tombs at Dalyan are a remarkable example of ancient Lycian architecture. Cut into the cliffs overlooking the Dalyan River, these tombs date back to the 4th century BC and are a must-visit for those interested in ancient history.

Fethiye Castle

Fethiye Castle, also known as the Castle of Telmessos, is a prominent historical landmark that dates back to the 15th century. The castle was built by the Knights of Rhodes to protect the city from pirate attacks, and today, it houses a museum that showcases the town’s fascinating history.

Natural Attractions of Dalaman: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Dalaman is replete with natural wonders that are perfect for nature lovers and outdoor enthusiasts.

Kursunlu Waterfall

The Kursunlu Waterfall is one of the most picturesque waterfalls in the region, surrounded by lush forests, waterfalls, and walking trails that allow visitors to explore the natural beauty of the area.

Kapikargin Sulfur Springs

Located in the nearby town of Koycegiz, Kapikargin Sulfur Springs is a natural wonder with a high sulfur content that has therapeutic properties. Visitors come here to soak in the mineral-rich waters that are renowned for their healing properties.

Iztuzu Beach

Iztuzu Beach, also known as Turtle Beach, is a protected natural reserve that is home to many species of rare birds and loggerhead sea turtles. The beach is a popular place for swimming and sunbathing, and the crystal-clear waters make it an ideal spot for snorkeling and diving.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Dalaman

The beautiful landscape of Dalaman makes it a great destination for hiking and biking enthusiasts, with several trails scattered throughout the region.

The Carian Trail

The Carian Trail is a long-distance hiking trail that runs across the lycian region of Turkey. The trail is around 820 km long and passes through Dalaman, allowing hikers to explore the beautiful countryside.

Gocek Nature Trail

The Gocek Nature Trail is a 3.5km trail that is perfect for nature lovers who want to explore the serene countryside of the region. The trail starts from the Gocek Marina, and beautiful views of the bay can be enjoyed along the way.

Best Things to Do in Dalaman: Activities and Tours

Dalaman has a variety of activities and tours to choose from, whether you want to explore the natural wonders of the region or uncover its rich cultural history.

Paragliding

Paragliding is a popular activity in Oludeniz, a resort town close to Dalaman. The stunning sea views and clear blue skies make it one of the best spots in the world for paragliding.

Boat Tours

Boat tours are a great way to explore the archipelago of small islands and hidden bays surrounding Dalaman. Visitors can charter a private boat or join a group tour that includes snorkeling and swimming stops.

Week-Long Itinerary for Dalaman

If you’re planning a week-long trip to Dalaman, here is an itinerary that will allow you to make the most of your stay:

  • Day 1: Visit Fethiye Castle and explore the town’s markets
  • Day 2: Take a boat tour of the surrounding islands and bays
  • Day 3: Hike the Carian Trail or the Gocek Nature Trail
  • Day 4: Visit the ancient city of Kaunos and the Lycian Tombs
  • Day 5: Soak in the therapeutic waters of Kapikargin Sulfur Springs
  • Day 6: Go paragliding in Oludeniz, known as one of the top paragliding spots in the world.
  • Day 7: Relax on the stunning Iztuzu Beach

Food and Drink Scene of Dalaman

Turkish cuisine is known for its rich flavors and diverse regional culinary traditions, and Dalaman is no exception. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

Pide

Pide is a traditional Turkish flatbread that is popular in Dalaman, usually filled with cheese or ground meat.

Lahmacun

Lahmacun is a thin, crispy Turkish-style pizza topped with spiced minced meat, onion, and parsley.

Local Beverages

Raki

Raki is the traditional Turkish alcoholic beverage, a clear, anise-flavored drink that is often consumed with mezze dishes.

Salep

Salep is a hot, milky beverage made from orchid roots that is popular in winter months.

Where to Stay in Dalaman: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Dalaman has a range of options for those looking for a place to stay, from luxurious resorts to budget-friendly guesthouses.

Hilton Dalaman Sarigerme Resort & Spa

One of the most luxurious hotels in the area is the Hilton Dalaman Sarigerme Resort & Spa, boasting a beachfront location, several pools, and numerous restaurants and bars.

Dalaman Airport Lykia Resort Hotel

The Dalaman Airport Lykia Resort Hotel is a 5-star resort located near the airport, making it an excellent choice for those who have an early-morning flight.

What to Buy in Dalaman: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Visitors to Dalaman can find many unique local products and souvenirs to bring back home. Some of the best buys include:

Turkish Delight

Turkish delight is a type of candy made from gelatin and flavored with rosewater or other delicate flavors. The candy comes in various shapes, sizes, and flavors.

Kilim Rugs

Kilim rugs are handmade, flat-woven rugs made from wool or cotton and dyed with natural pigments. Visitors to Dalaman can purchase kilim rugs and other handmade textiles at local markets.

Local Culture and Customs of Dalaman

Hospitality is a cornerstone of Turkish culture, and visitors to Dalaman should expect a warm welcome from the locals. As in all of Turkey, it’s best to dress conservatively when entering mosques and other religious sites, and visitors should remove their shoes before entering homes or other places of worship.

Best Time to Visit Dalaman

The best time to visit Dalaman is between April and October when the weather is warm, and many outdoor activities are available. However, July and August can be very hot and crowded, so it’s best to avoid these months if possible.

Do’s and Don’ts of Dalaman

Do:

  • Learn a few Turkish phrases and use them whenever possible to show respect to the locals.
  • Try the local cuisine and traditional beverages.
  • Dress conservatively when visiting religious sites.

Don’t:

  • Drink tap water, stick to bottled water instead.
  • Visit or take pictures of military zones without permission, as it is strictly forbidden.

Safety and Health

Safety in Dalaman

Dalaman is generally a very safe town, with low crime rates, and visitors can feel comfortable walking around in most areas. However, tourists should still exercise caution and be vigilant, especially when carrying valuables.

Health in Dalaman

Visitors to Dalaman should make sure they are up to date on routine vaccines before traveling. The tap water is not safe to drink, so stick to bottled water. Mosquitoes can be a problem during the summer months, so it’s a good idea to bring insect repellent.

Getting to Dalaman: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Dalaman

Dalaman International Airport is the closest airport to the town, and it receives flights from various destinations in Europe and Asia. From there, visitors can take taxis or buses to reach their accommodation.

Bus Stations Near Dalaman

Several bus companies operate services to and from Dalaman, connecting it to major cities and tourist destinations within Turkey.

Other Options

Visitors can also take a ferry from nearby Fethiye to several Greek islands.

How to Get Around in Dalaman

Visitors to Dalaman can use local buses to get around the town and the wider region. There are also several car rental companies in the town and the airport, making it easy to explore the region on your terms.

Where to Go Next from Dalaman: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Oludeniz

Oludeniz is a resort town known for its beautiful lagoon and stunning turquoise waters, making it a popular choice for beach enthusiasts and paragliding enthusiasts.

Kas

The town of Kas is located further east along the coast and is known for its beautiful harbor and quaint, white-washed houses.

Conclusion

Whether you’re looking to explore the region’s history, relax on pristine beaches, or indulge in delicious Turkish cuisine, Dalaman has something to offer every traveler. With this comprehensive guide, you’re sure to get the most out of your trip to this beautiful corner of Turkey.
