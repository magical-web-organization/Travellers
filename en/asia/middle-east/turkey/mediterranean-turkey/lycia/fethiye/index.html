Fethiye Travel Guide - A Comprehensive Tour Guide

Fethiye is a charming city located in Turkey’s Mediterranean coast in the region of Lycia. Its beautiful beaches, scenic landscapes, and rich history attract visitors from all over the world. In this comprehensive guide, we’ll cover everything you need to know to plan your trip to Fethiye.

Introduction to Fethiye

Fethiye is an ancient city that has a history dating back to 400 BC. It was once a part of the Lycian Kingdom and later became a part of the Ottoman Empire. Today, it’s a bustling city that attracts visitors with its beautiful beaches, turquoise waters, and ancient ruins.

List of Places to Visit in Fethiye

Visitors to Fethiye have plenty of things to see and do. Here’s a list of some of the top places to visit in Fethiye:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Fethiye Museum: The museum is home to many artifacts and exhibits that showcase Fethiye’s rich history and culture.
  • Lycian Tombs: The ancient tombs were carved in the rock face and overlook Fethiye Bay, offering stunning views.
  • Kayaköy: The abandoned Greek village is a hauntingly beautiful place that gives visitors an insight into the region’s turbulent past.

Natural Attractions of Fethiye: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Ölüdeniz Beach: The famous Blue Lagoon is a beautiful beach surrounded by dramatic mountains.
  • Butterfly Valley: A beautiful natural reserve that is home to hundreds of butterfly species.
  • Saklikent Gorge: A beautiful canyon that is popular with hikers and nature lovers.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Fethiye

Fethiye is not only beautiful but also offers great hiking and biking trails. Here are some routes worth exploring:

  • Lycian Way: The popular hiking trail that runs along the coast of Turkey.
  • Saklikent Gorge: The canyon provides a scenic and shaded hike that is a great activity in summers.
  • Kabak Bay to Faralya: A great hiking trail that takes you through beautiful forests, rugged coastline, and stunning vistas.

Best Things to Do in Fethiye: Activities and Tours

Fethiye provides a range of activities and tours for all types of travelers. Here are some of the best things to do in Fethiye:

  • Paragliding: Soar above the picturesque Blue Lagoon with one of the world’s best paragliding spots.
  • Boat Trips: Explore the pristine coastline with a relaxing boat tour.
  • Turkish Bath: Refresh your body with a traditional Turkish Bath.

Week-Long Itinerary for Fethiye

If you’re planning to spend a week in Fethiye, here’s a good itinerary:

Food and Drink Scene of Fethiye

Fethiye has a vibrant food and drink scene that is worth exploring. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Gozleme: A popular street food that is a type of Turkish filled pancake.
  • Pide: A Turkish-style pizza that is made of boat-shaped dough, topped with cheese, vegetables, and meat.
  • Lahmacun: A thin, crispy Turkish pizza that is topped with minced meat and various spices.

Local Beverages

  • Raki: A traditional Turkish beverage that is anise-flavored and sometimes referred to as “lion’s milk.”
  • Tea: A ubiquitous beverage that is served in cafes, restaurants, and homes across Turkey.
  • Ayran: A refreshing yogurt drink that pairs perfectly with traditional Turkish cuisine.

Where to Stay in Fethiye: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Fethiye has a range of accommodation options to suit all budgets. Here are some of the best places to stay in Fethiye:

  • Hillside Beach Club: A luxury resort set above the stunning Blue Lagoon.
  • Yacht Classic Hotel: A stylish boutique hotel located in the heart of Fethiye.
  • Friends Pansiyon: A friendly hostel that offers comfortable rooms at an affordable price.

What to Buy in Fethiye: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

There are many local products and souvenirs worth checking out in Fethiye. Here are some of them:

  • Turkish Carpets: Handmade carpets sourced from traditional Anatolian villages.
  • Olive Oil: Made from olives that are grown in the Mediterranean region around Fethiye.
  • Turkish Delight: A classic sweet that is made of sugar, cornstarch, and a range of flavors like rose, pistachio or hazelnut.

Local Culture and Customs of Fethiye

There are a few local customs and practices that visitors should be aware of when visiting Fethiye.

  • Dress modestly when visiting religious sites, such as mosques and mausoleums.
  • Always remove your shoes before entering someone’s home or a mosque.
  • Turkish people typically greet with a handshake and a smile.

Best Time to Visit Fethiye

The best time to visit Fethiye is between April and October when the weather is warm and sunny, and the crowds are thin. However, peak season is in July and August, which can be crowded and hot.

Do’s and Don’ts of Fethiye

Here are some do’s and don’ts to follow while traveling in Fethiye:

Do:

  • Try Turkish dishes and drinks.
  • Explore the beautiful nature reserves and landscapes.
  • Respect the local culture and customs.

Don’t:

  • Drink tap water.
  • Visit unregistered tour agencies.
  • Take illegal drugs.

Safety and Health

Safety in Fethiye

Fethiye is a generally safe place to travel, but visitors should take standard safety precautions to avoid any inconvenience or danger.

  • Beware of pickpockets and keep your belongings safe.
  • Only use licensed taxis or transportation options.
  • Be cautious while hiking or swimming and avoid deserted or isolated places during nighttime.

Health in Fethiye

Turkey’s medical system is generally advanced in most major cities and tourist destinations. In case of an emergency, it’s advisable to call the Universal Emergency Number 112 or head to the nearest hospital.

Getting to Fethiye: Transportation Options and Logistics

There are several transportation options available for visiting Fethiye:

Airports Near Fethiye

  • Dalaman Airport is the closest airport to Fethiye and is located about 55km away.
  • Antalya Airport is another option and is about 200km away from Fethiye.

Bus Stations Near Fethiye

  • Fethiye Bus Station is the main bus station and operates daily bus services to most cities in Turkey.

Other Options

  • Visitors can rent a car and drive to Fethiye. However, driving in Turkey requires nerves of steel and can be chaotic and stressful for drivers who are not used to the local driving style.

How to Get Around in Fethiye

There are several transportation options available for getting around in Fethiye:

  • Dolmuş: Shared taxis that run frequently along most major routes in Fethiye.
  • Taxi: Taxis are ubiquitous and charge based on the meter.
  • Rent a car: Renting a car is one of the best ways to explore Fethiye’s many attractions.

Where to Go Next from Fethiye: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Fethiye is a great starting point for exploring the surrounding area. Here are some of the best places to visit near Fethiye:

  • Dalyan: A small town that is home to the ancient Lycian rock tombs of Kaunos.
  • Pamukkale: A desert-like spot with cascading natural hot springs and mineral terraces.
  • Antalya: A beautiful coastal city that boasts a vibrant food and drink scene.

Conclusion

Fethiye is a charming city that offers visitors a mix of historical sites, beautiful nature, and outdoor activities. We hope this guide helps you plan your trip to Fethiye and make the most of your time in this beautiful location.
