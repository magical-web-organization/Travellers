Introduction to Tarsus

Located in the Cilician Plains of the Mediterranean region of Turkey, Tarsus has a rich history and is believed to be one of the oldest cities in the world. It is said that Paul the Apostle was born in Tarsus and it was also an important city during the Roman Empire. Today, Tarsus is a popular tourist destination for its historical landmarks, natural beauty, and delicious cuisine. In this comprehensive travel guide, we’ll cover everything you need to know to plan a trip to Tarsus.

List of Places to Visit in Tarsus

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Saint Paul’s Church

Saint Paul’s Church is a significant religious site located in the center of Tarsus. It is believed to be the birthplace of St. Paul and there is a plaque that marks the spot where he was born.

Cleopatra’s Gate

Cleopatra’s Gate, also known as the Roman Gate, is another historical landmark in Tarsus. It is an impressive structure with two towers and was constructed during the Roman era to mark the entrance to the city.

Tarsus Museum

The Tarsus Museum is an excellent place to learn about the history and culture of Tarsus. The museum has a collection of archeological artifacts dating back to the Bronze Age and has exhibits on the city’s famous residents such as St. Paul and Cleopatra.

Natural Attractions of Tarsus: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Tarsus Waterfall

Tarsus Waterfall is a beautiful natural wonder located on the outskirts of the city. It is an ideal spot for nature lovers and adventure seekers due to the hiking trails and scenic views.

Goksu Delta

Goksu Delta is the meeting point of the Goksu River and the Mediterranean Sea. It is an important area for nature conservation and is home to a wide range of wildlife such as flamingos, pelicans, and turtles.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Tarsus

Tarsus is a great destination for those who love to explore the outdoors. Here are some of the best hiking and biking routes in and around Tarsus:

  • Tarsus Waterfall Trail
  • Goksu Delta Bird Watching Trail
  • Cukurova Trail

Best Things to Do in Tarsus: Activities and Tours

In addition to exploring the historical and natural attractions, Tarsus offers a variety of activities and tours for tourists. Here are some of the best things to do in Tarsus:

  • Taste traditional Turkish dishes and drinks
  • Take a boat tour along the Goksu Delta
  • Kayak on the Goksu River

Week-Long Itinerary for Tarsus

To help you plan your trip to Tarsus, here is a week-long itinerary:

  • Day 1: Explore the historical landmarks in the city center, including Saint Paul’s Church and Cleopatra’s Gate.
  • Day 2: Visit the Tarsus Museum and learn about the city’s history and culture.
  • Day 3: Hike to the Tarsus Waterfall and enjoy the scenic views.
  • Day 4: Take a boat tour along the Goksu Delta and spot the local wildlife.
  • Day 5: Kayak on the Goksu River and enjoy the outdoor adventure.
  • Day 6: Take a day trip to nearby Adana and explore the city’s marketplaces and mosques.
  • Day 7: Relax and enjoy some traditional Turkish food and drinks.

Food and Drink Scene of Tarsus

Tarsus offers a variety of delicious Turkish dishes and local beverages for visitors to sample. Here are some of the must-try dishes and drinks:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Adana Kebab: A popular Turkish dish made with spicy minced meat and cooked on a skewer.
  • Lahmacun: A thin, crispy Turkish pizza topped with spiced ground meat and vegetables.
  • Baklava: A sweet pastry made with layers of phyllo dough and honey.

Local Beverages

  • Turkish tea: A popular drink in Turkey, served hot in small glasses.
  • Ayran: A refreshing yogurt drink that complements spicy Turkish dishes.
  • Turkish coffee: A strong, flavorful coffee that is brewed in a traditional style.

What to Buy in Tarsus: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

When visiting Tarsus, it’s a good idea to bring home some souvenirs to remember your trip. Here are some of the best things to buy in Tarsus:

  • Turkish Delight: A popular sweet treat made with sugar and starch.
  • Carpet and rugs: Tarsus is famous for its beautifully woven carpets and rugs.
  • Olive oil: Tarsus produces high-quality olive oil that makes a great gift.

Local Culture and Customs of Tarsus

When visiting Tarsus, it’s important to respect the local customs and culture. Here are some things to keep in mind:

  • Dress modestly when visiting mosques and other religious sites.
  • Remove shoes before entering someone’s home.
  • Tipping is not expected but appreciated.

Best Time to Visit Tarsus

The best time to visit Tarsus is between March and May and from September to November when the weather is mild and pleasant.

Do’s and Don’ts of Tarsus

Do:

  • Try the local cuisine.
  • Visit the historical landmarks and museums.
  • Enjoy outdoor activities and adventures.

Don’t:

  • Speak disrespectfully about religion or politics.
  • Eat or drink during Ramadan in public places.
  • Expect locals to speak English fluently.

Safety and Health

Safety in Tarsus

Tarsus is a safe city for tourists, but it’s always a good idea to be aware of your surroundings and take necessary precautions to avoid any incidents.

Health in Tarsus

Visitors to Tarsus should take necessary immunizations before traveling and drink bottled water to avoid any health issues.

Getting to Tarsus: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Tarsus

Adana Sakirpasa Airport is the closest airport to Tarsus, located just 30 kilometers away.

Bus Stations Near Tarsus

Tarsus Otogar is the main bus station in Tarsus, with frequent buses to nearby cities and towns.

Other Options

Tarsus is also accessible by train and private car/taxi.

How to Get Around in Tarsus

The best way to get around Tarsus is by taxi or by renting a car. Walking and biking are also popular options within the city center.

Where to Go Next from Tarsus: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Tarsus is a great base for exploring the surrounding areas. Here are some of the best nearby destinations and excursions:

  • Adana: The largest city in the region, famous for its kebabs and bazaars.
  • Cappadocia: Known for its unique geological formations and hot air balloon rides.
  • Antalya: A popular vacation spot on the Mediterranean coast.

Conclusion

Tarsus is a city steeped in history and natural beauty, making it an ideal place to explore for tourists.
