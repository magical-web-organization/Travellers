Introduction to Silifke

Silifke, located in the Mediterranean region of Turkey, is a city that offers a unique mix of ancient history and natural beauty. The city is situated on the foothills of the Cilician Mountains, which makes it an ideal destination for outdoor enthusiasts.

Silifke, also known as Selefke, has a rich cultural and historical heritage that dates back to the ancient times. The city was once an important center of the Cilician Kingdom and was ruled by various empires throughout history. It is now a popular tourist destination that attracts visitors with its fascinating historical sites, scenic landscapes, and warm hospitality.

In this travel guide, we will take a closer look at the best things to do and see in Silifke, where to stay, what to eat, and much more.

List of Places to Visit in Silifke

Silifke has a lot to offer to its visitors, from ancient ruins to natural wonders. Here’s our list of top attractions to visit in Silifke.

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Silifke Castle is a stunning fortress that offers breathtaking views of the city and the surrounding landscape. The castle dates back to the Roman and Byzantine eras and is a must-visit attraction for history buffs.
  • Kiz Castle is another impressive castle that overlooks the city. It was built during the Crusader period and played a crucial role in the region’s history.
  • Uzuncaburç is an ancient city that was once a part of the Kingdom of Cilicia. Visitors can explore the remains of the city’s ancient buildings, such as the city walls, baths, and the Temple of Zeus.
  • Olba Ancient City is a Roman-era city that is famous for its beautiful mosaics, thermal baths, and a well-preserved amphitheater.
  • Silifke Museum is an excellent place to learn about the city’s rich history and heritage. The museum houses a vast collection of artifacts, including sculptures, pottery, and coins from the ancient periods.

Natural Attractions of Silifke: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Alahan Monastery is a beautiful ancient monastery that is set in a stunning location. Visitors can enjoy the breathtaking views of the mountains and the countryside while exploring the monastery’s impressive architecture.
  • The Narlikuyu Caves are a series of underground caves that are famous for their stunning rock formations and natural pools.
  • Manastır Canyon is a picturesque canyon that is surrounded by scenic views and breathtaking landscapes. Visitors can take a hiking trail through the canyon and enjoy the natural beauty of the region.
  • Goksu Delta is a beautiful wetland that is home to a wide range of bird species, including pelicans and flamingos.
  • The Cennet-Cehennem (Heaven and Hell) Canyon is a beautiful natural attraction that is known for its stunning waterfalls, deep pools, and lush vegetation. Visitors can enjoy various water activities, such as swimming, rafting, and canoeing, in this beautiful location.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Silifke

Silifke is a paradise for outdoor enthusiasts, with many hiking and biking trails that offer stunning views of the region’s natural beauty. Some of the popular hiking and biking routes in Silifke are:

  • The Cennet-Cehennem (Heaven and Hell) Canyon Trail is a beautiful hiking trail that takes visitors through the stunning natural beauty of the canyon. The trail is around 6 kilometers long and takes around 2-3 hours to complete.
  • The Alahan Monastery Trail is a popular hiking route that takes visitors through the ancient ruins of the monastery and offers breathtaking views of the surrounding landscape.
  • The Goksu Delta Trail is a scenic trail that takes visitors through the wetlands of the Goksu Delta, where they can observe a wide range of bird species.
  • The Manastır Canyon Trail is a challenging trail that takes visitors through the picturesque canyon and offers stunning views of the region’s natural landscapes.

Best Things to Do in Silifke: Activities and Tours

Silifke is a city that offers a wide range of exciting activities and tours for its visitors. Here are some of the best things to do in Silifke:

  • Go on a Jeep Safari to explore the rugged terrain, scenic views and landscapes, and stunning natural beauty of the region.
  • Take a Boat Tour to explore the beautiful coastline, hidden coves, and pristine beaches of Silifke.
  • Go Rafting on the Goksu River for an exhilarating experience through the beautiful landscape of Silifke.
  • Visit a Traditional Turkish Bath to rejuvenate, relax, and refresh yourself. Silifke has many traditional Turkish baths that offer the ultimate spa experience.
  • Enjoy a Scuba Diving Experience to explore the beautiful underwater world of the Mediterranean. Silifke has some of the best diving spots in the region.

Week-Long Itinerary for Silifke

Here’s a week-long itinerary for those who want to explore Silifke and its surroundings:

Day 1: Arrival in Silifke

Explore the city and its surroundings on your first day. Start with a visit to Silifke Castle, followed by a visit to Kiz Castle. In the afternoon, take a stroll through the city’s charming streets and enjoy the local cuisine.

Day 2: Uzuncaburç

Visit the ancient city of Uzuncaburç and explore its remains, including the city walls, baths, and the Temple of Zeus.

Day 3: The Narlikuyu Caves

Explore the stunning natural beauty of the Narlikuyu Caves and admire their beautiful rock formations and natural pools.

Day 4: Alahan Monastery and Goksu Delta

Visit the beautiful Alahan Monastery and enjoy the hiking trail through the stunning landscape. In the afternoon, head to the Goksu Delta and explore the wetlands that are home to various bird species.

Day 5: The Cennet-Cehennem (Heaven and Hell) Canyon

Take a tour through the Cennet-Cehennem Canyon, enjoy stunning waterfalls, and participate in various water sports.

Day 6: Free Day

Take some time to relax and explore the city’s local shops and markets. Enjoy the local cuisine and immerse yourself in Silifke’s culture and heritage.

Day 7: Departure

Say goodbye to Silifke and head to your next destination.

Food and Drink Scene of Silifke

Silifke has a rich culinary heritage that reflects the city’s history and culture. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages in Silifke:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Biber dolması stuffed bell peppers
  • Yaprak sarması grape leaves stuffed with rice and herbs
  • Tandır kebabı roasted lamb or beef cooked in a traditional clay oven
  • Mıhlama a savory dish made with cornmeal, cheese, and butter
  • Keme mantısı a type of pasta filled with lamb, onion, and spices

Local Beverages

  • Şalgam suyu a popular beverage made from fermented turnip juice and spices.
  • Cay or Turkish tea is a ubiquitous beverage that is served in small glasses.
  • Raki a traditional alcoholic beverage that is especially popular during mealtimes.

Local Culture and Customs of Silifke

Silifke is a friendly and welcoming city that has a rich cultural and historic heritage. The locals are known for their hospitality, and visitors are always welcomed warmly. The city has a mixed population of ethnic Turks and Arabic-speaking Alawi people, and it is fascinating to observe the practices and traditions of both communities.

Best Time to Visit Silifke

The best time to visit Silifke is during the spring and autumn seasons when the temperature is mild and the landscape is at its most beautiful. Summer months can be hot and humid, while winter can be rainy, so it is best to avoid these periods if possible.

Do’s and Don’ts of Silifke

Here are some general do’s and don’ts to keep in mind when visiting Silifke:

Do:

  • Dress appropriately when visiting religious sites or mosques.
  • Enjoy local cuisine and try traditional dishes.
  • Respect local traditions and customs.
  • Bargain at markets and shops.
  • Try to learn some basic Turkish phrases before your visit.

Don’t:

  • Disrespect local customs and traditions.
  • Take pictures of people without their consent.
  • Overconsume alcohol in public places.
  • Be too loud in residential areas.
  • Venture into deserted areas alone at night.

Safety and Health

Silifke is a relatively safe city, with a low crime rate. Visitors should take standard precautions when traveling and keep their valuables safe. As with any foreign country, it is advisable to have travel insurance to be prepared for any unforeseen medical emergencies.

Getting to Silifke: Transportation Options and Logistics

Silifke is accessible by multiple modes of transportation. Here is an overview of transportation options to get to Silifke:

Airports Near Silifke

The closest airport to Silifke is Adana Airport, approximately an hour away by car or bus.

Bus Stations Near Silifke

Silifke has a well-connected bus terminal that connects it with other cities in Turkey.

Other Options

Car rentals and private transfers can be arranged with local tour operators, making it a convenient mode of transportation to explore the city and its surroundings.

How to Get Around in Silifke

Silifke is a walkable city, and visitors can explore most places on foot. Taxis and buses are also available for more extended journeys, and car rentals can be arranged to explore the city and its surroundings.

Where to Go Next from Silifke: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Silifke is located in the Mediterranean region of Turkey and is ideal for combining with other destinations in the area. Here are some nearby destinations and excursions that visitors can take from Silifke:

  • Mersin: A vibrant coastal city that offers stunning beaches and a vibrant culinary scene.
  • Antalya: A popular tourist destination that is known for its stunning coastlines, ancient ruins, and beautiful resorts.
  • Cappadocia: A unique region that offers stunning natural landscapes, historical sites, and hot air balloon rides.

Conclusion

Silifke is a charming city in the Mediterranean region of Turkey that offers a unique mix of cultural and natural attractions. From ancient ruins to natural wonders, Silifke has something to offer to every traveler. Visitors can enjoy stunning landscapes, exciting outdoor activities, rich history, and warm hospitality that makes it an ideal destination for a relaxing vacation. Use this guide to plan your visit to Silifke, and immerse yourself in its rich culture, history, and beauty.
