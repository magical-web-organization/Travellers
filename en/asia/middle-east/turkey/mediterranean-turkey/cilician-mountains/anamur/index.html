Located in the beautiful region of Cilician Mountains in Mediterranean Turkey, Anamur is an off-the-beaten-path destination that is perfect for travelers seeking a unique cultural experience. With its rich history, beautiful natural attractions, great food, and welcoming locals, Anamur has something to offer for everyone.

In this comprehensive guide, we’ll provide you with everything you need to know about Anamur, including the best places to visit, things to do, where to eat and stay, and more.

Introduction to Anamur

Anamur is a coastal town located in the province of Mersin in Turkey, and is situated between the Taurus Mountains and the Mediterranean Sea. The town is known for its beautiful beaches, stunning natural scenery, and rich cultural heritage.

Anamur was once an important center of the ancient Cilician civilization and was also occupied by many different empires throughout history, including the Romans, Byzantines, and Ottomans.

Despite being a small town, Anamur has a lot to offer visitors. From exploring its historical landmarks to hiking in the beautiful mountains and indulging in delicious local cuisine, there’s always something to keep you busy in Anamur.

List of Places to Visit in Anamur

If you’re planning a trip to Anamur, here are some of the top places to visit:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  1. Anemurium: A historical site that houses ancient ruins dating back to the Roman and Byzantine periods.
  2. Alaköprü: A beautifully preserved bridge from the Ottoman era that spans over the Dragon River.
  3. Anamur Castle: A medieval castle built by the Armenians in the 11th century.
  4. Anamur Museum: A small museum that showcases the history and culture of Anamur and the surrounding region.

Natural Attractions of Anamur: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  1. Mamure Castle: A stunning seaside castle that dates back to the 12th century.
  2. Bozyazı Karaburun Nature Park: A beautiful nature reserve that boasts unspoiled beaches, hiking trails, and stunning views of the Mediterranean.
  3. Anamurium Beach: One of the most popular beaches in Anamur, known for its crystal-clear waters and beautiful surroundings.
  4. Kaledran Valley: A scenic valley located near Anamur Castle that is perfect for hiking and exploring.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Anamur

For outdoor enthusiasts, Anamur offers some of the best hiking and biking routes in Turkey. Some of the most popular routes include:

  1. Cennet-Cehennem Canyon: A beautiful canyon that offers stunning views of the surrounding mountains and valleys.
  2. Bozyazı Karaburun Nature Park: A beautiful park that boasts several hiking trails of varying degrees of difficulty.
  3. Kaledran Valley: A scenic valley that is perfect for hiking and exploring.
  4. Taurus Mountains: A range of mountains that offer a variety of hiking and biking routes for all skill levels.

Best Things to Do in Anamur: Activities and Tours

If you’re looking for things to do in Anamur, here are some of the best activities and tours:

  1. Mamure Castle Tour: A guided tour of the castle that includes a visit to the castle walls, towers, and underground tunnels.
  2. Boat Tours: Explore the beautiful coastline of Anamur and discover hidden coves and secluded beaches.
  3. Turkish Bath: Relax and unwind at one of the traditional Turkish baths in Anamur.
  4. Local Market Tours: Experience the local culture and taste the delicious local cuisine at one of the many markets in Anamur.

Week-Long Itinerary for Anamur

If you’re planning a week-long trip to Anamur, here’s a sample itinerary:

Day 1 - Arrival and check-in at your hotel Day 2 - Visit Anemurium and Alaköprü Day 3 - Explore Anamur Castle and Anamur Museum Day 4 - Hike in Kaledran Valley and enjoy a traditional Turkish lunch Day 5 - Relax at Anamurium Beach and take a boat tour of the coastline Day 6 - Visit Mamure Castle and explore the ancient ruins Day 7 - Visit Bozyazı Karaburun Nature Park and enjoy the beautiful scenery

Food and Drink Scene of Anamur

Anamur is known for its delicious local cuisine, which features fresh seafood, herbs, and spices. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  1. Mersin Tantuni: Grilled meat served in a wrap with mixed greens and sauce.
  2. Kuzu tandır: Slow-cooked lamb served with rice, salad, and yogurt.
  3. Balık ekmek: Grilled fish served on a bun with lettuce, tomato, and onion.
  4. Kabak tatlısı: A sweet dessert made with pumpkin, nuts, and syrup.

Local Beverages

  1. Ayran: A refreshing drink made with yogurt, water, and salt.
  2. Boza: A traditional Turkish drink made from fermented wheat or millet.
  3. Salep: A warm, creamy drink made from ground orchids, milk, and sugar.
  4. Turkish Coffee: Strong, rich coffee served in a small cup with grounds at the bottom.

Where to Stay in Anamur: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Anamur offers a variety of accommodation options, including hotels, hostels, and guesthouses. Here are some of the best places to stay:

  1. Anemurion Hotel: A modern hotel located in the heart of Anamur.
  2. Mamure Kalesi Pansiyon: A traditional guesthouse located within walking distance of Mamure Castle.
  3. Klados Hotel: A boutique hotel located near Anamurium Beach.
  4. Anorma Hotel: A family-friendly hotel located in the center of Anamur.

What to Buy in Anamur: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

If you’re looking for souvenirs and local products to bring back home, here are some of the best options:

  1. Handwoven Carpets: Anamur is famous for its handwoven carpets and rugs.
  2. Olive Oil: The area around Anamur is known for its high-quality olive oil.
  3. Almonds: Anamur is famous for its delicious almonds.
  4. Bronze Jewelry: The region has a long tradition of bronze jewelry making.

Local Culture and Customs of Anamur

Anamur has a rich cultural heritage that is deeply rooted in its history and traditions. The locals are friendly and welcoming to visitors, and are proud of their heritage and customs.

As a visitor to Anamur, it’s important to respect the local culture and customs. Some of the things to keep in mind include dressing modestly when visiting religious sites, avoiding public displays of affection, and removing your shoes when entering a mosque or someone’s home.

Best Time to Visit Anamur

The best time to visit Anamur is between May and September when the weather is warm and sunny. However, if you prefer cooler temperatures and fewer crowds, you can also visit during the winter months.

Do’s and Don’ts of Anamur

When visiting Anamur, here are some things to do and avoid:

Do:

  1. Try the local cuisine and beverages
  2. Explore the historical landmarks and natural attractions
  3. Respect the local culture and customs
  4. Learn a few basic Turkish phrases

Don’t:

  1. Disrespect the local culture and customs
  2. Leave litter or damage natural sites
  3. Stay out too late in unpopulated areas
  4. Take photos of locals without their permission

Safety and Health

Anamur is a relatively safe destination for travelers, and violent crimes are rare. However, visitors should take basic precautions to avoid theft and pickpocketing, especially in crowded areas.

As for health, it’s always a good idea to check any travel advisories and make sure that your vaccinations are up to date before visiting Anamur. Bring any necessary prescription medications with you, and make sure to drink plenty of water to stay hydrated.

Getting to Anamur: Transportation Options and Logistics

Anamur is easily accessible by car, bus, or plane. Here are some of the transportation options available:

Airports Near Anamur

  1. Adana Şakirpaşa Airport: The closest airport to Anamur, located around 190 km away.
  2. Antalya Airport: A larger airport located around 270 km away from Anamur.

Bus Stations Near Anamur

  1. Anamur Otogar: The main bus station in Anamur with connections to most major cities in Turkey.
  2. Bozyazı Otogar: A smaller bus station located around 15 km from Anamur.

Other Options

  1. Car Rentals: You can easily rent a car from Adana Airport or Antalya Airport.
  2. Private Transfers: You can book a private transfer from the airport directly to your hotel.

How to Get Around in Anamur

The best way to get around in Anamur is by car or taxi, but you can also rent a bike or take public transportation. Taxis are relatively cheap and easy to find, whereas car rentals are more expensive but offer more flexibility.

Where to Go Next from Anamur: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Anamur is located in the beautiful region of Cilician Mountains, and there are several nearby destinations and excursions that you can visit:

  1. Mersin: A bustling port city located around 250 km from Anamur.
  2. Silifke: A charming town located around 60 km from Anamur, famous for its Roman ruins.
  3. Gazipaşa: A seaside town located around 90 km from Anamur, famous for its beautiful beaches.

Conclusion

Anamur is a hidden gem in Turkey that is waiting to be explored. With its breathtaking natural scenery, rich cultural heritage, delicious cuisine, and warm locals, Anamur is a destination that is not to be missed. We hope that this guide has provided you with all the information you need to plan your trip to Anamur and make the most of your visit.
