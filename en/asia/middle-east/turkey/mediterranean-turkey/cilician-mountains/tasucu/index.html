If you are planning on vacationing in Turkey, and looking for an off-the-beaten-path destination, then Tasucu should be your top pick. Located in Cilician Mountains, in Mediterranean Turkey, Tasucu boasts a stunning natural environment, delicious local cuisine, and rich history and culture.

Introduction to Tasucu

Tasucu is a small town, with a population of around 10,000, situated in the Mersin Province of Turkey. Known for its sandy beaches, pine forests, and spectacular mountain views, Tasucu is the perfect getaway for those looking for a peaceful, relaxed vacation. The town is a favorite destination for locals, but it is yet to be discovered by international tourists. So, if you are looking for an authentic Turkish experience, Tasucu is the place for you.

List of Places to Visit in Tasucu

There are several attractions in and around Tasucu that are worth visiting. From culturally significant museums and historical places to tranquil natural settings, Tasucu has a lot to offer to visitors.

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Tasucu Castle: Located on a hill overlooking the town, Tasucu Castle is a must-visit for history buffs. It dates back to the Byzantine era and was later expanded by the Knights of St. John.
  • Tasucu Ataturk Museum: This museum houses collections of photos and personal items of Ataturk, the founder of the Turkish Republic. The museum is located in a beautiful old mansion, and the gardens alone are worth the visit.

Natural Attractions of Tasucu: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Cennet-Cehennem (Heaven-Hell) Canyon: This canyon makes for a perfect day trip. The canyon is a magical place with its crystal-clear waters, lush flora, and fauna.
  • Narlikuyu Cave: Narlikuyu Cave is a stunning natural creation offering visitors a chance to see stalactites and stalagmites, and underground waterfalls. It is located on the outskirts of Tasucu town, and it is one of the most beautiful caves in Turkey.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Tasucu

Tasucu is a paradise for those who love hiking or biking. The town has a lot of trails that offer different levels of difficulty, depending on your preference. The most famous routes are in the Cennet-Cennem canyon, offering stunning views of the mountains and the town.

Best Things to Do in Tasucu: Activities and Tours

Tasucu offers a range of activities and tours for travelers to enjoy. From kayaking to fishing, cultural walking tours to Turkish cooking classes, there’s something for everyone in Tasucu.

Week-Long Itinerary for Tasucu

If you’re planning a week-long stay in Tasucu, consider the following itinerary:

Day 1: Arrive in Tasucu and explore the town center. Day 2-3: Explore the natural attractions of Tasucu, including the Cennet-Cehennem canyon and Narlikuyu Cave. Day 4: Take a guided walking tour of Tasucu’s cultural and historical sites, including Tasucu Castle and Ataturk Museum. Day 5-6: Go on a river kayaking tour or spend a day fishing in the Mediterranean sea. Day 7: Spend the day relaxing on the beach and taking in the sea views.

Food and Drink Scene of Tasucu

Tasucu has a rich culinary scene, with many delicious local dishes to try. Here are some must-try dishes:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Tasucu Kebab: A local specialty made with grilled lamb or beef, onions, and peppers wrapped in lavash.
  • Kisir: A salad made with bulgur wheat, tomatoes, onions, parsley, and spices.
  • Piyaz: A salad made with white beans, onions, tomatoes, and parsley.

Local Beverages

  • Turkish tea: A staple drink in Turkey, this sweet and strong tea is served everywhere in Tasucu.
  • Ayran: A traditional Turkish drink made with yogurt, water, and salt. It is the perfect refreshing drink on a hot day.

Where to Stay in Tasucu: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Tasucu has a range of accommodation options available, from budget-friendly hostels to luxury hotels. Here are some recommended options:

  • Tasucu Hotel: Located in the town center, this comfortable hotel offers clean and cozy rooms, a restaurant serving local cuisine, and free Wi-Fi.
  • Tasucu Backpackers Hostel: This budget-friendly hostel offers dormitory-style rooms and private rooms. Guests have access to a shared kitchen, a terrace, and free Wi-Fi.

What to Buy in Tasucu: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Tasucu is famous for its handicrafts, including handmade pottery, carpets and rugs, and traditional Turkish lamps. You can find some unique souvenirs to take back home at the local markets.

Local Culture and Customs of Tasucu

Tasucu has a rich cultural heritage, known for its traditional music, dance and folklore. The town’s people are warm and welcoming, and it is customary to greet each other with a handshake or a kiss on the cheek. When visiting mosques, it’s important to dress conservatively, covering your head, shoulders, and knees.

Best Time to Visit Tasucu

The best time to visit Tasucu is between May and September when the weather is warm and sunny, and the sea is perfect for swimming. The town is also less crowded during these months.

Do’s and Don’ts of Tasucu

Do:

  • Respect the local culture and customs.
  • Try the local cuisine and drinks.
  • Spend some time exploring the natural beauty of Tasucu.

Don’t:

  • Wear revealing clothing when visiting mosques or religious sites.
  • Ignore the signs of strong ocean currents and waves.

Safety and Health

Safety in Tasucu

Tasucu is a safe town, but like any other place, it’s important to take basic precautions to ensure your safety. Avoid walking alone at night, and keep an eye on your belongings at all times.

Health in Tasucu

Tap water in Tasucu is safe to drink, but it’s always safer to drink bottled water. It’s also advisable to carry insect repellent, as mosquitos can be quite common in the summer months.

Getting to Tasucu: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Tasucu

The nearest airport to Tasucu is Adana Airport, which is approximately 100 kilometers away. From there, you can take a taxi or a bus to Tasucu.

Bus Stations Near Tasucu

Tasucu has its own bus station, and it’s well connected to other cities in Turkey. Buses run frequently from Mersin to Tasucu.

Other Options

If you prefer to drive, there are car rental companies available at Adana Airport, and the drive from there to Tasucu is approximately 1 hour and 30 minutes.

How to Get Around in Tasucu

The best way to get around Tasucu is by car or taxi. You can rent a car from one of the rental companies at Adana Airport, or take a taxi from the town center.

Where to Go Next from Tasucu: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Tasucu is close to some great destinations in Turkey. Here are some nearby destinations you can visit:

  • Mersin: A port city with a rich cultural heritage and an impressive coastline.
  • Kizkalesi: A charming town with a castle dating back to the Roman era.

Conclusion

Tasucu is an unforgettable destination that combines natural beauty, rich culture, and delicious cuisine. With its friendly people, tranquil atmosphere, and range of activities and tours, it’s the perfect off-the-beaten-path destination for any traveler. So, pack your bags and get ready to explore Tasucu!
