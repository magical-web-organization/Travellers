Introduction to Mediterranean Turkey

Mediterranean Turkey is a region in southern Turkey that stretches from the city of Antalya to the border of Syria. This region is rich in history, culture, and natural beauty. From ancient ruins to pristine beaches, Mediterranean Turkey is a destination that offers something for every traveler. So if you’re planning a trip to Turkey, make sure to include Mediterranean Turkey in your itinerary.

List of Places to Visit in Mediterranean Turkey

Mediterranean Turkey has plenty of destinations to explore. Here are some of the most popular:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Ephesus: This ancient city is one of the best-preserved examples of Roman architecture in the world.
  • Perge: Located near Antalya, this ancient city features a well-preserved stadium and theater.
  • Aspendos Theater: This well-preserved Roman theater is still used for concerts and shows today.
  • Museum of Antalya: This museum showcases artifacts and exhibits from the region’s ancient civilizations.

Natural Attractions of Mediterranean Turkey: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Olympos National Park: This park is home to the Chimera, a natural phenomenon where flames come out of the ground.
  • Saklikent Canyon: This canyon offers hiking trails and a refreshing stream to cool off in.
  • Duden Waterfalls: These waterfalls are located in Antalya and offer beautiful views of the Mediterranean Sea.
  • Ölüdeniz Beach: This stunning beach features crystal-clear waters and a famous paragliding spot.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Mediterranean Turkey

Mediterranean Turkey is a perfect destination for outdoor enthusiasts. Here are some of the best hiking and biking routes in the region:

  • Lycian Way: This 540-kilometer route is one of the world’s most beautiful hiking trails, stretching from Fethiye to Antalya.
  • St. Paul Trail: This trail follows the route of St. Paul’s first journey through southern Turkey.
  • Cilician Way: This trail takes you through the Taurus Mountains and offers stunning views of the Mediterranean Sea.
  • Bike Route Along the Coast: If you prefer biking, this route along the coast is perfect for you.

Best Things to Do in Mediterranean Turkey: Activities and Tours

Mediterranean Turkey offers a variety of activities and tours for travelers. Here are some of the best things to do in the region:

  • Hot Air Balloon Ride in Cappadocia: Take a hot air balloon over the stunning landscape of Cappadocia.
  • Turkish Bath Experience: Enjoy a traditional Turkish bath experience to relax your body and mind.
  • Jeep Safari in Taurus Mountains: Explore the beautiful Taurus Mountains on a thrilling jeep safari.
  • Whitewater Rafting on the Köprüçay River: Experience some adrenaline rush by rafting on the Köprüçay River.

Food and Drink Scene of Mediterranean Turkey

The food and drink scene of Mediterranean Turkey offers a variety of local flavors and dishes. Here are some must-try dishes and locally produced beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Meze: This refers to a variety of small dishes served as appetizers, such as stuffed grape leaves and hummus.
  • Adana Kebab: This spicy kebab is made with ground lamb and served with bulgur or bread.
  • Manti: Small dumplings filled with meat, served with yogurt and spices.

Local Beverages

  • Raki: A strong anise-flavored alcoholic drink that’s typically consumed with meze.
  • Turkish Tea: Served in tulip-shaped glasses, it is a staple of Turkish culture.
  • Ayran: A refreshing yogurt-based drink that’s good for combating the heat.

Where to Stay in Mediterranean Turkey: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Mediterranean Turkey has a range of accommodation options, from luxury hotels to budget-friendly hostels. Here are some of the best places to stay:

  • Rixos Premium Belek: This luxury resort in Antalya offers all-inclusive amenities and multiple swimming pools.
  • Old Town Kaleici in Antalya: Stay in a traditional guesthouse located in the historic Old Town of Antalya.
  • The North Shields in Fethiye: This budget-friendly hostel offers a cozy atmosphere and stunning views.
  • MDF Apart Hotel in Cirali: These family-run apartments offer a peaceful atmosphere and easy access to the beach.

What to Buy in Mediterranean Turkey: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Before you leave Mediterranean Turkey, make sure to pick up some locally-made souvenirs and products. Here are some of the best things to buy:

  • Kilim Rugs: These handmade, colorful rugs are a staple of Turkish decor.
  • Turkish Delight: A traditional sweet treat with many flavors.
  • Olive Oil Soap: This soap is made from locally-produced olive oil and is known for its health benefits.

Local Culture and Customs of Mediterranean Turkey

Turkey is a culturally rich country with a unique blend of East and West. Here are some local customs and traditions to be aware of:

  • Hospitality: Turks are known for their hospitality and warm welcomes to visitors.
  • Tea Culture: Tea is a staple of Turkish culture, and it is often offered as a gesture of friendship.
  • Religion: Turkey is predominantly Muslim, and travelers should be respectful of local customs and dress modestly when visiting religious sites.

Best Time to Visit Mediterranean Turkey

Mediterranean Turkey has a mild Mediterranean climate, making it a desirable destination all year round. However, the best time to visit is from April to May and from September to October when the weather is cooler and less crowded.

Do’s and Don’ts of Mediterranean Turkey

Here are some do’s and don’ts to keep in mind when visiting Mediterranean Turkey:

Do:

  • Dress modestly, especially when visiting religious sites.
  • Haggle when shopping in markets, it’s expected.
  • Try local cuisine and beverages.

Don’t:

  • Expect people to speak English everywhere.
  • Neglect taking off your shoes when entering a mosque or someone’s house.
  • Drink tap water. Always opt for bottled water.

Safety and Health

Mediterranean Turkey is overall a safe destination for travelers, but here are some safety and health tips to keep in mind:

Safety in Mediterranean Turkey

  • Beware of scams in touristy areas.
  • Avoid walking alone at night in unlit areas.
  • Check travel advisories before visiting areas close to the Syrian border.

Health in Mediterranean Turkey

  • Bring sunscreen and a hat to protect yourself from the sun.
  • Make sure to hydrate and drink enough water especially when doing outdoor activities.
  • Always carry your medication and necessary prescriptions.

Getting to Mediterranean Turkey: Transportation Options and Logistics

There are several ways to get to Mediterranean Turkey, including:

Airports Near Mediterranean Turkey

  • Antalya Airport: The main airport in the region, served by numerous international and domestic flights.
  • Dalaman Airport: Serves tourists going to Fethiye and Marmaris.
  • Gazipasa Airport: The nearest airport to Alanya.

Bus Stations Near Mediterranean Turkey

  • Antalya Bus Terminal: The main bus terminal in the region, serving both local and intercity buses.
  • Fethiye Bus Terminal: The terminal that serves tourists going to coastal destinations like Olu Deniz and Kas.

Other Options

  • Renting a car: This is a convenient option for those who want to explore the region independently.
  • Taking a train: There are numerous train routes connecting different cities within Turkey.

How to Get Around in Mediterranean Turkey

Getting around Mediterranean Turkey is relatively easy, with various options including:

  • Local buses
  • Dolmus (shared taxi)
  • Metro and tram in Antalya
  • Taxis and ride-hailing apps like Uber and BiTaksi

Where to Go Next from Mediterranean Turkey: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Mediterranean Turkey is well-connected to other regions of Turkey, making it easy to continue your travels. Here are some nearby destinations and excursions to consider:

  • Cappadocia: Experience the unique landscape and underground cities of Cappadocia.
  • Pamukkale: Explore the famous white terraces and ancient city of Hierapolis.
  • Istanbul: Turkey’s vibrant and historically rich capital city.

Conclusion

Mediterranean Turkey is a destination that offers something for everyone, from ancient ruins to stunning beaches and natural wonders. Plan your next trip to this region of Turkey and experience the blend of cultures and flavors that make it a unique destination.
