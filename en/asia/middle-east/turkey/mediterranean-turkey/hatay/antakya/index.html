Introduction to Antakya

Antakya is a city located in Hatay, in southwestern Turkey, near Syria’s border. Known for its rich history, Antakya is a city of unique culture with diverse culinary delights, ancient ruins, and natural scenery. The city provides access to numerous attractions, makes it a popular tourist destination.

List of Places to Visit in Antakya

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Hatay Archaeology Museum

The Hatay Archaeology Museum is a historical museum which exhibits artifacts dating back to the Hellenistic and Roman period. It showcases a collection of mosaics, sculptures, and ancient building parts.

St. Peter’s Church

St. Peter’s Church is famous for being one of the earliest churches in the world, and it is believed to have been founded by the Apostle Peter himself. The church is a fascinating place to visit for its rich history and beautiful architecture.

Hatay Archaeological Museum

Hatay Archaeological Museum is home to an extensive collection of ancient artifacts, including pottery, jewelry, and coins, all dating back to the region’s time under Roman and Byzantine rule. The museum also has a spectacular mosaic collection.

Antakya Bazaar (Old Market)

Located in the city center, the Antakya Bazaar is a hopping marketplace, offering visitors an authentic opportunity to soak up the local culture. You can buy fresh fruits, spices, honey, handcrafted textiles, and Turkish delights.

Natural Attractions of Antakya: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Gözlükule Hill

Gözlükule Hill, situated on the banks of the Orontes River, offers a breathtaking view of Antakya. You can hike to the top of the hill, take in the beautiful scenery, and later relax in one of the traditional Turkish cafes.

Harbiye Waterfalls

Harbiye Waterfalls are natural falls located about 7 kilometers from the city center. You can relax and enjoy the view while having a picnic in the beautiful park, or you can take a swim at the bottom of the falls.

Vakıflı Village

Vakıflı Village, located on the slopes of the Nur Mountains, is a small Armenian village. The village’s vineyards and olive groves offer beautiful sceneries, and the locals produce organic wine and olive oil. You can explore the village’s traditional houses, churches, and temples.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Antakya

For an exhilarating outdoor adventure, you can explore the city on bike or hiking routes.

Hiking Trails

The Nur Mountains and Orontes River Valley offer many attractive trails for hikers, including Harbiye Falls Trail and St. Peter’s Mountain Trail. Visitors can hike up to the mountains and hills around Antakya and enjoy beautiful views of the city and its landscapes.

Cycling Routes

The cycling routes around Antakya range from easy to strenuous, allowing tourists to explore the scenic routes and natural surroundings at their own pace. The route from the city to Harbiye Waterfall is a recommendable route for cycling.

Best Things to Do in Antakya: Activities and Tours

Traditional Turkish Bath

Visiting a traditional Turkish bath, known as Hammam, is an excellent way to relax and unwind while indulging in one of the oldest and most revered Turkish customs. The historical Kızıldağ Hamamı in Antakya is a recommendable spot to experience this ancient ritual.

Food and Culture Tour

The best way to find out about local culture is through the taste of the food! The food and culture tours in Antakya provide insight into the region’s culinary traditions and history, taking visitors on a tasting journey through the city’s food markets and venues.

Nightlife in Antakya

The nightlife scene in Antakya is vibrant with several pubs and clubs around the city center. You can also enjoy a night at the open-air cinema or attend one of the local art & cultural festivals that take place in the city.

Week-Long Itinerary for Antakya

Day 1: Visit Hatay Archaeology Museum and St. Peter’s Church.

Day 2: Take a visit to Harbiye Waterfalls to swim or have a picnic, try the legendary dish “Oruk”.

Day 3: Spend half-day in the market, go for a local food tour and relax in one of the classic Turkish baths.

Day 4: Explore the Nur Mountains’ scenic views; visit the historic Vakıflı village and sip on their organic wines.

Day 5: Visit Antakya’s fortress and archaeological sites.

Day 6: Take a cycling route from Antakya to the Harbiye Waterfall and relax with a drink.

Day 7: Visit the Church of St. George and the Habib-i-Neccar Mosque, and later head towards the open-air cinema.

Food and Drink Scene of Antakya

Must-Try Dishes

Künefe

Künefe is a sweet dessert made with cheese and syrup, which is famous in Antakya. Local restaurants such as Uzun Caddesi, a famous street in Antakya, are well known for serving the best künefe in the city.

Oruk

Oruk is a local specialty made with minced meat, bulgur, tomatoes, onions, and spices. This dish is often paired with freshly baked bread.

Kebab

Kebab is a popular dish in Antakya, which is grilled or roasted meat in a spiced-up sauce, served with rice or bulgur.

Local Beverages

Pomegranate juice

Pomegranate juice is famous in Antakya, and it’s considered a top choice to beat the midday heat of the city.

Şerbet

Şerbet is a sweet and refreshing syrup-made drink, which is also popular in Antakya. Local restaurants and taverns serve many types of şerbet, and it is available year-round.

Where to Stay in Antakya: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Antakya offers a range of accommodation options to its visitors, ranging from luxurious five-star hotels to budget hostels.

Some of the recommended hotels are Ottoman Palace Hotel, Savon Hotel, and Antakya Beyaz Otel.

If you’re looking for budget-friendly accommodations, you can opt for a guesthouse or hostel, like Grand Mira Hotel, Güler Park Hotel or Harbiye Turizm.

What to Buy in Antakya: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Antakya has plenty of souvenir and handicraft shops, selling traditional textiles, ceramics, and decorative items. Some of the must-buy local products in Antakya are:

  • Baklava
  • Spices and herbs
  • Handwoven Kilims and Carpets
  • Syrian Soap
  • Copper and bronze goods

Local Culture and Customs of Antakya

Antakya is a culturally rich city, where locals are friendly and hospitable to visitors. Here are a few customs and traditions that may help visitors to appreciate the city’s culture better:

  • It’s respectful to dress modestly and cover your head when visiting a Mosque or religious site.
  • Turkish hospitality is a big part of Antakya’s culture. Locals often welcome visitors warmly and offer them a cup of tea or coffee, which is a sign of respect for the guests.
  • Arabic and Kurdish are widely spoken alongside Turkish in Antakya.

Best Time to Visit Antakya

The best time to visit Antakya is from September to November and from March to May, when the weather is pleasant and the tourist activity is low. It’s wise to avoid the mid-summer months, June to August, because of the extreme heat in the city.

Do’s and Don’ts of Antakya

Do:

  • Try the local food and beverages
  • Respect local customs and traditions
  • Dress modestly when visiting religious sites

Don’t:

  • Drink alcohol in public places
  • Carry a lot of cash in the crowded markets
  • Photograph locals without their permission

Safety and Health

Safety in Antakya

Antakya is generally a safe city to visit, but visitors should keep an eye on their belongings when visiting the crowded bazaars or public spaces. It’s also recommended to take taxis at night instead of walking, especially for solo travelers.

Health in Antakya

Antakya is not a high-risk area for communicable diseases, but visitors should always take precautions when traveling. It’s recommended to drink bottled water and avoid drinking tap water. Visitors should also practice good hygiene to avoid food poisoning, wash their hands before meals, and maintain a healthy diet.

Getting to Antakya: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Antakya

The nearest airport to Antakya is Hatay Airport, which is only 25 kilometers from the city’s center. The airport provides direct flights to numerous cities in Turkey such as Istanbul, Ankara, and Izmir.

Bus Stations Near Antakya

Antakya Intercity Bus Terminal is the main transportation hub for intercity travel. It provides a network of bus services to several Turkish cities, including Istanbul, Izmir, Ankara, and Bursa.

Other Options

Antakya is well-connected to its neighboring cities through the train service from the Antakya Terminal to Adana, Mersin, and Konya.

How to Get Around in Antakya

Antakya’s city center is small and walkable, making it easy to explore on foot. Taxis are a convenient and affordable option for longer distances. Additionally, bikes and scooters are available for rent across the city.

Where to Go Next from Antakya: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Antakya is located in the Hatay province, densely surrounded by both historical and natural places.

  • Samandağ, where the Turkmen village and Güzelyayla National Park offer splendid beach, history, culture, and nature.
  • The old town of Dörtyol, which has a stunning view of the Mediterranean seashore.
  • The city of Iskenderun, which is located on the eastern coast of Mediterranean, has a beautiful beach and promenade, city squares and parks, a charming market, and tourist hotspots.

Conclusion

Antakya is an amazing city with a fascinating culture, history, and natural beauty. It offers many attractions and activities suitable for all tastes and interests, from museums and historical sites to natural scenery and outdoor adventures. Visitors can also indulge in the city’s culinary delights and handicrafts while taking in the authentic Turkish culture.
