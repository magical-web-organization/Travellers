Introduction to Yalova

Located in the Eastern Marmara region, Yalova is a hidden gem that offers a perfect blend of rich history, cultural diversity, and natural beauty. It’s a small seaside town in Turkey that’s blessed with hot springs, beautiful beaches, dense green forests, and amazing landscapes that will leave you in awe. Yalova is a popular weekend getaway destination for Istanbul residents who seek a peaceful and relaxing escape from the city bustle.

List of Places to Visit in Yalova

Whether you’re a history buff, a nature lover, or an adventure seeker, Yalova has something to offer to everyone. Here is a list of must-see places in Yalova:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Yalova Ataturk Mansion Museum: It’s a historical building that served as a summer house for the first president of Turkey, Ataturk.
  • Sudusen Waterfall: A natural beauty that’s located at the end of Yuruyen Kosk (Walking Pavilion) in the center of town.
  • Yuruyen Kosk (Walking Pavilion): A century-old pavilion that served as a resting spot for Ottoman Sultans while they travelled to Bursa.
  • Koyunbaba Bath: One of the oldest public bathhouses in Yalova, which was built during the Ottoman era.

Natural Attractions of Yalova: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Karaca Arboretum: A nature lover’s paradise that’s home to over 2000 plant species, including endangered and rarely seen ones.
  • Thermal Spas: Yalova is famous for its thermal springs, which are believed to have healing properties. Some of the popular ones include Termal, Hammamli, and Yayla.
  • Cinarcik Beach: One of the most beautiful and scenic beaches in Yalova, which offers crystal clear water and breath-taking sunsets.
  • Karatas Merkez Cami: A 14th-century mosque that’s famous for its remarkable Ottoman architecture and intricate detailing.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Yalova

Yalova offers several hiking and biking trails that run through its lush green forests, tranquil valleys, and magnificent hills. Some of the most popular routes include:

  • Yalova – Termal (35 km): A scenic route that passes through Yuruyen Kosk, Karaca Arboretum, and Termal hot springs.
  • Yalova – Kartalabani (25 km): A challenging route that offers stunning views of the Marmara Sea and the surrounding hills.
  • Yalova – Kocadere Valley (20 km): A moderate route that takes you through Kocadere Valley, which is famous for its wildflowers and waterfalls.

Best Things to Do in Yalova: Activities and Tours

There are plenty of things to do in Yalova, whether you want to relax and rejuvenate in the hot springs or explore the scenic beauty of the region. Here are some of the best things to do in Yalova:

  • Take a dip in one of the hot springs and enjoy a relaxing spa treatment.
  • Visit the Karaca Arboretum and be amazed by the impressive collection of plants and trees from around the world.
  • Take a boat tour of Cinarcik Beach and enjoy the breath-taking view of the sea.
  • Enjoy panoramic views of the city and the sea from the Yuruyen Kosk.
  • Participate in a hiking or biking tour to enjoy the scenic beauty of the hills and mountains.

Here is a suggested week-long itinerary for Yalova:

Food and Drink Scene of Yalova

Yalova’s food and drink scene is a mix of Turkish and Balkan cuisines that promises to tantalize your taste buds. Here are some of the must-try dishes and local beverages in Yalova:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Hamsili Ekmek: A tasty dish made with anchovies, bread, and vegetables.
  • Iskembe Soup: A hearty soup made with tripe and vegetables.
  • Pide: A Turkish flatbread that’s typically stuffed with cheese, minced meat, and vegetables.

Local Beverages

  • Ayran: A refreshing drink made with yogurt, water, and salt.
  • Raki: An anise-flavored alcoholic drink that is often paired with meze.
  • Salep: A popular winter drink made with milk, orchid roots, and cinnamon.

Where to Stay in Yalova: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Yalova offers a wide range of accommodation options that suit all budgets and preferences. Here are some of the best places to stay in Yalova:

  • Green Thermal Hotel: A luxurious hotel that’s located in the Termal region and offers excellent spa facilities.
  • Limak Thermal Boutique Hotel: Another great option for those who want to indulge in spa treatments and enjoy a relaxing stay.
  • Sirinler Pension: A budget-friendly guesthouse that’s located in the center of Yalova and offers clean and comfortable rooms.

What to Buy in Yalova: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Yalova is famous for its natural beauty and rich history, which is reflected in its local products and souvenirs. Here are some of the best things to buy in Yalova:

  • Organic honey and jams
  • Hand-woven carpets and kilims
  • Local olive oil and olives
  • Traditional Turkish pottery and ceramics

Local Culture and Customs of Yalova

Yalova is a diverse and tolerant city that has a rich cultural heritage. Turkish is the official language spoken in Yalova, but English is widely spoken in tourist areas. Yalova residents are known for their hospitality and friendliness, which makes the city a welcoming destination for visitors from around the world.

Best Time to Visit Yalova

The best time to visit Yalova is from April to November when the climate is mild, and the days are warm and sunny. The peak season is from June to August when the temperatures are high, and the crowds are larger.

Do’s and Don’ts of Yalova

Do:

  • Explore the natural beauty of the region, including its hot springs, waterfalls, and forests.
  • Try the local dishes and beverages to experience the taste of the Turkish and Balkan cuisine.
  • Respect the local customs and dress modestly in public spaces.

Don’t:

  • Be disrespectful to the local culture and customs.
  • Drink tap water without boiling it first.
  • Leave your valuables unattended in public spaces.

Safety and Health

Safety in Yalova

Yalova is a safe city for tourists, but it’s recommended to be vigilant and aware of your surroundings, especially in tourist areas. Petty theft and pickpocketing are common, so it’s essential to keep your valuables safe and secure.

Health in Yalova

Yalova offers excellent health facilities, and there are several hospitals and clinics that provide medical care to locals and tourists. However, it’s recommended to have comprehensive travel insurance that covers medical expenses and emergencies.

Getting to Yalova: Transportation Options and Logistics

Yalova is easily accessible by various modes of transportation, including air, land, and sea.

Airports Near Yalova

The nearest airport is Sabiha Gokcen International Airport, which is located about 66 km from Yalova. Istanbul Airport is another option, which is about 180 km away.

Bus Stations Near Yalova

There are frequent buses to Yalova from Istanbul, Bursa, and Ankara. The central bus station in Yalova is located in the city centre, which is easily accessible by public transportation or taxi.

Other Options

You can also take a ferry from Istanbul to Yalova, which is a scenic and convenient option. The ferry ride takes about an hour and offers breath-taking views of the Marmara Sea.

How to Get Around in Yalova

Yalova is a small town, and most of the attractions are located in the city centre and can be easily reached by walking or public transportation. However, if you want to explore the surrounding areas, it’s recommended to rent a car or hire a taxi.

Where to Go Next from Yalova: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Yalova is located close to several popular destinations and excursion options, including:

  • Bursa: A beautiful city that’s famous for its Ottoman architecture, historic landmarks, and ski resorts.
  • Sapanca Lake: A beautiful lake that’s surrounded by lush green forests and beautiful landscapes.
  • Istanbul: The largest and most populous city in Turkey, which is known for its rich history, culture, and beautiful landmarks.

Conclusion

Yalova is a beautiful and charming city that offers a perfect blend of natural beauty, rich history, and cultural diversity. Whether you want to explore the hot springs, enjoy outdoor activities, or indulge in local cuisine, there’s something for everyone in Yalova. It’s a perfect destination for a weekend getaway, a family vacation, or a romantic escape.
