Introduction to Izmit

Located in Eastern Marmara, Turkey, Izmit is a city that boasts a rich history, beautiful natural scenery, and a lively cultural scene. While many travelers may overlook Izmit when planning a trip to Turkey, it’s a hidden gem that’s worth exploring. With its stunning coastal location, excellent food and drink, and fascinating cultural attractions, Izmit has something to offer for every type of traveler.

List of Places to Visit in Izmit

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Izmit is home to several museums and historical sites that provide a glimpse into the city’s fascinating past. One must-visit destination is the Kocaeli Museum, which showcases the region’s rich cultural and archaeological heritage. The museum’s impressive collection includes artifacts and exhibits that span the Paleolithic era through the Ottoman empire, and is a must-visit for history buffs.

Another top attraction is the Izmit Clock Tower, which dates back to the early 20th century and is a symbol of the city’s industrial past. Visitors can climb to the top of the tower for panoramic views of the surrounding area.

Natural Attractions of Izmit: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

There’s no shortage of natural beauty to discover in Izmit. One popular destination is the Sapanca Lake, which is known for its pristine waters and picturesque views. Visitors can partake in a variety of outdoor activities, including boating, fishing, and hiking.

Another must-visit location is the Kartepe Ski Center, which is a popular winter sports destination. With its breathtaking mountain views and well-maintained slopes, Kartepe is the perfect destination for skiing, snowboarding, and other winter sports.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Izmit

For travelers looking to explore the great outdoors on foot or bike, Izmit offers a variety of scenic trails and paths. One top location is the Ballikayalar Nature Park, which features scenic hiking trails and breathtaking views of the surrounding hillsides.

Another popular destination is the Izmit-Pangalti cycling route, which winds its way through beautiful countryside and passes by many historical landmarks and local villages.

Best Things to Do in Izmit: Activities and Tours

There are plenty of fun and exciting activities and tours to discover in Izmit. One top destination is the Izmit Bazaar, which is a bustling marketplace that’s perfect for bargain hunting and souvenir shopping. Visitors can browse a wide variety of goods, including traditional Turkish rugs, ceramics, and spices.

Another popular option is to take a day trip to the beautiful Princes’ Islands, which are located just a short ferry ride from Izmit. Visitors can explore the islands’ picturesque fishing villages, take a scenic bike ride, or simply relax on one of the many beautiful beaches.

Week-Long Itinerary for Izmit

For travelers looking to experience the best of Izmit in one week, here is a suggested itinerary:

  • Day 1: Explore the museums and historical landmarks of the city, including the Kocaeli Museum and Izmit Clock Tower.
  • Day 2: Take a guided tour of the Ballikayalar Nature Park, and be sure to bring a picnic lunch to enjoy in one of the park’s many scenic picnic areas.
  • Day 3: Spend the day exploring the Sapanca Lake, with activities like fishing, kayaking, or simply soaking up the sun on the beach.
  • Day 4: Take a day trip to the nearby Princes’ Islands, and explore the islands’ many beautiful beaches, bike paths, and historic sites.
  • Day 5: Explore the local food and drink scene by sampling traditional dishes like kebab, baklava, and Turkish tea.
  • Day 6: Take a scenic drive to Kartepe Ski Center for a day of winter sports and mountain views.
  • Day 7: Spend the day at the Izmit Bazaar, shopping for unique souvenirs and exploring the many fascinating stalls and shops.

Food and Drink Scene of Izmit

Izmit is home to a variety of delicious dishes and refreshing drinks that are worth trying during your visit. Here are a few must-try items:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Kebab: Izmit is known for its delicious kebab, which is grilled over an open flame and seasoned with a blend of spices and herbs.
  • Baklava: This sweet pastry is a staple of Turkish cuisine, and it’s a must-try in Izmit. Layers of phyllo dough are filled with honey and chopped nuts for a delicious treat.
  • Manti: This Turkish dumpling is similar to Italian ravioli, but with a different filling. Manti typically contains ground beef or lamb, onions, and spices, and is served with a yogurt sauce.

Local Beverages

  • Turkish tea: Served hot and usually accompanied by sugar cubes or honey, Turkish tea is a staple of Turkish culture and a must-try during your visit to Izmit.
  • Raki: This anise-flavored alcoholic drink is popular in Turkey, and is often enjoyed with a traditional Turkish meal.

Where to Stay in Izmit: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Izmit offers a wide variety of accommodation options, from luxurious hotels to budget-friendly hostels and guesthouses. Some top locations include the Hilton Garden Inn, the Wes Hotel, and the Erenler Hotel.

What to Buy in Izmit: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

There are plenty of unique and interesting souvenirs to be found in Izmit, including traditional Turkish rugs, ceramics, and spices. Visitors can also find handmade jewelry, clothing, and other crafts at local markets and bazaars.

Local Culture and Customs of Izmit

Izmit has a rich and fascinating culture that’s worth exploring during your visit. Some popular customs and traditions include the art of Turkish coffee making, traditional music and dance performances, and the celebration of national holidays and festivals throughout the year.

Best Time to Visit Izmit

The best time to visit Izmit is during the spring or fall, when the temperatures are mild and the weather is pleasant. During the summer, temperatures can be quite hot and humid, while the winter months can be cold and snowy.

Do’s and Don’ts of Izmit

Here are a few important do’s and don’ts to keep in mind during your visit to Izmit:

Do:

  • Learn a few basic Turkish phrases to show respect for the local culture.
  • Dress modestly when visiting religious landmarks and areas.
  • Try local food and drink specialties during your stay.

Don’t:

  • Touch or photograph religious artifacts without permission.
  • Make assumptions about the local culture or customs without first asking questions or doing research.
  • Overlook some of the smaller, lesser-known attractions and landmarks in favor of only visiting the most well-known destinations.

Safety and Health

Safety in Izmit

Izmit is a relatively safe city, but visitors should still exercise caution when traveling through the city. Be aware of pickpockets and other common scams, and avoid walking alone at night in unfamiliar areas.

Health in Izmit

Izmit has a high standard of medical care, and visitors can access quality hospitals and emergency care facilities throughout the city. It’s always a good idea to make sure you have adequate travel insurance before departing for your trip, in case of any unexpected medical emergencies.

Getting to Izmit: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Izmit

The nearest airport to Izmit is the Istanbul Sabiha Gokcen International Airport, which is located approximately 70 kilometers away. Visitors can take a taxi, bus, or shuttle to reach Izmit from the airport.

Bus Stations Near Izmit

There are several bus companies that offer service to and from Izmit, including Kamil Koc and Ulusoy. Bus travel is a popular and affordable option for travelers within Turkey.

Other Options

Visitors can also travel to Izmit by train, with service available from Istanbul and other major Turkish cities. Another option is to rent a car and drive to Izmit, although visitors should be aware of potential traffic congestion and road conditions.

How to Get Around in Izmit

The best way to get around Izmit is by taxi, which is a relatively affordable and convenient option. Visitors can also take the local bus or dolmus (shared taxi vans) to get around the city.

Where to Go Next from Izmit: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Izmit is located in the heart of Eastern Marmara, with a variety of nearby destinations and excursions to explore. Some popular options include the city of Istanbul, the historic town of Bursa, and the seaside town of Yalova.

Conclusion

Izmit is a hidden gem that’s worth exploring for travelers looking to experience a lesser-known side of Turkey. With its rich cultural heritage, stunning natural beauty, and lively food and drink scene, there’s truly something for everyone to enjoy in Izmit. Whether you’re seeking outdoor adventures, cultural attractions, or simply a chance to relax and unwind by the sea, Izmit is a destination that won’t disappoint.
