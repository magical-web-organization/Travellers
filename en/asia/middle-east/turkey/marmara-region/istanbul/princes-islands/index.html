Introduction to Princes’ Islands

Princes’ Islands is a group of nine small islands located off the coast of Istanbul in the Marmara Sea. Originally, the islands were used as a place of exile for Byzantine rulers, and later for the Ottoman princes, hence the name Princes’ Islands. Today, they are known for their tranquility and natural beauty, and offer visitors a great escape from the bustling city. The islands are car-free, making them the perfect place for a leisurely bike ride or tranquil walk. In this comprehensive guide, we will take you through the must-visit places, hiking and biking routes, food and drink scene, where to stay, and more.

List of Places to Visit in Princes’ Islands

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

The Princes’ Islands are home to several museums and historical landmarks that are worth a visit. Here are some of the most culturally important places to see:

  • Aya Yorgi Church: One of the oldest churches on the islands, Aya Yorgi provides visitors with views of Istanbul, the Sea of Marmara, and the surrounding islands.

  • Museum of the Princes’ Islands: This museum showcases the history and culture of the islands, including the architecture and flora and fauna of the region.

  • Hamidiye Mosque: The mosque was built in honor of Sultan Abdul Hamid II, and its location on a hill provides stunning views of the islands.

  • Monastery of the Transfiguration: The monastery dates back to the Byzantine era and is believed to have been built in honor of the Transfiguration of Jesus Christ.

Natural Attractions of Princes’ Islands: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

The stunning natural beauty of the Princes’ Islands are a sight to behold. Here are some of the best spots to explore:

  • Buyukada Beach: This is the largest beach on the islands, offering visitors a chance to relax by the sea after exploring the sites.

  • Aya Nikola Monastery: Located on the highest peak of Heybeliada, this monastery provides visitors with breathtaking views of the island and surrounding sea.

  • Phosphorus Cave: This sea cave is located on the northern tip of Burgazada and is known for its bright blue phosphorescent waters.

  • Halki Palace: This palace was built by the Greek Orthodox Patriarch in the 1800s, and its traditional Ottoman-style architecture is a must-see.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Princes’ Islands

The islands are car-free and the best way to explore them is on foot, on a bike, or on a horse carriage. Here are some of the best hiking/biking routes to take:

  • The Grand Tour: This 20-kilometer long route takes visitors on a scenic journey around the islands, including stops at some of the most popular sites.

  • Heybeliada Circular Route: This 8-kilometer route takes visitors through the center of Heybeliada and provides stunning views of the sea.

  • Maden Bay to Yüce Tepe: This challenging 12-kilometer hike takes visitors through forests and up to the highest peak on the islands, Yüce Tepe.

Best Things to Do in Princes’ Islands: Activities and Tours

In addition to visiting the museums and landmarks, the islands offer plenty of activities and tours. Here are some of the best things to do on the islands:

  • Horse-drawn carriage tour: This is a traditional way to explore the islands, and visitors can choose from several routes that take them to the most popular sites.

  • Kayaking tour: Visitors can rent kayaks and explore the different bays and coves on the islands.

  • Swimming: There are several public beaches on the islands that are perfect for swimming and sunbathing on a hot summer day.

Week-Long Itinerary for Princes’ Islands

Here’s our suggested week-long itinerary for the Princes’ Islands:

Day 1: Arrive at Istanbul and take a ferry to the Princes’ Islands Day 2: Explore Burgazada and visit the Phosphorus Cave Day 3: Take a horse-drawn carriage tour of Buyukada and visit Aya Yorgi Church and the Museum of the Princes’ Islands Day 4: Go for a hike to Yüce Tepe and visit the Monastery of the Transfiguration Day 5: Rent a kayak and explore the different bays and coves of the islands Day 6: Visit Heybeliada and go for a swim at Heybeliada Beach Day 7: Relax and enjoy the serenity of the islands

Food and Drink Scene of Princes’ Islands

Must-Try Dishes

The Princes’ Islands offer visitors a unique culinary experience, with a mix of Ottoman and Greek cuisine. Here are some of the must-try dishes:

  • Mussels Stuffed with Rice: This popular street food is a mix of rice, spices, and mussels, and is a favorite among the locals.

  • Fish Sandwich: Freshly caught fish is served on a piece of bread with salad and lemon, making it a perfect snack or lunch option.

  • Greek-style Meatballs: These meatballs are made with a mixture of beef and lamb, and are served with a side salad and yogurt sauce.

Local Beverages

The islands are known for their traditional Turkish tea, which is served in small glasses with sugar cubes. Another local favorite is Salgam, a popular fermented turnip juice that is a staple on the islands.

Where to Stay in Princes’ Islands: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

There are several accommodation options on the islands, ranging from budget-friendly hostels to luxurious hotels.

  • Splendid Palace Hotel: This hotel is located on Buyukada and offers stunning views of the sea and surrounding islands.

  • Studio Apartments of Princes’ Islands: This hostel is located in Heybeliada and offers affordable accommodation for backpackers and budget-conscious travelers.

  • Ada Palas Hotel: This hotel is located on Burgazada and is a great option for those looking for a luxurious stay on the islands.

What to Buy in Princes’ Islands: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

The islands offer several unique products that make great souvenirs to bring back home. Some of the best things to buy include:

  • Olive Oil Soap: This soap is made from local olive oil and is a popular souvenir among tourists.

  • Handmade Ceramics: Visitors can find a variety of beautifully crafted ceramic products, ranging from cups and plates to vases and decorative items.

  • Sea Sponges: The islands are surrounded by crystal-clear waters, which makes for an abundance of sea sponges that are sold in various shops around the islands.

Local Culture and Customs of Princes’ Islands

The Princes’ Islands have a rich history and culture, which is evident in the architecture and food of the region. The islands have several religious and cultural events throughout the year, such as the Feast of St. George in April and the Harvest Festival in September.

Best Time to Visit Princes’ Islands

The best time to visit the Princes’ Islands is from May to September when the weather is warm and sunny, and the sea is perfect for swimming. The islands can get crowded during the peak summer months, so visitors should plan their trip accordingly.

Do’s and Don’ts of Princes’ Islands

Do:

  • Respect local customs and traditions, especially when visiting religious landmarks

  • Explore the islands on foot, by bike, or on a horse-drawn carriage

  • Try traditional Turkish and Greek cuisine

Don’t:

  • Litter or damage the natural surroundings

  • Swim in prohibited areas

  • Wear revealing clothing when visiting religious sites

Safety and Health

Safety in Princes’ Islands

The Princes’ Islands are generally considered safe for tourists, though visitors should be cautious of pickpockets in crowded areas. The islands are also prone to forest fires during the summer months, so visitors should take appropriate precautions.

Health in Princes’ Islands

The Princes’ Islands have several medical facilities that can provide basic healthcare services. Visitors should also carry their own medicine and consult their doctor before traveling.

Getting to Princes’ Islands: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Princes’ Islands

Visitors can reach the Princes’ Islands by flying into Istanbul’s Ataturk or Sabiha Gokcen airports, and then taking a ferry from one of the ports around the city.

Bus Stations Near Princes’ Islands

Visitors can also reach Istanbul by bus and then take a ferry to the Princes’ Islands from one of the city’s ports.

Other Options

Visitors can also take a private boat or a water taxi to the Princes’ Islands from Istanbul’s European or Asian side.

How to Get Around in Princes’ Islands

The islands are car-free, and the best way to get around is on foot, by bike, or on a horse-drawn carriage. Visitors can rent bikes or carriages at various points around the islands.

Where to Go Next from Princes’ Islands: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Visitors can explore the nearby destinations such as Istanbul, Bursa, and Yalova. They can take a ferry or a bus to reach these places from Princes’ Islands.

Conclusion

The Princes’ Islands are a hidden gem that offers visitors a unique cultural and natural experience, away from the hustle and bustle of Istanbul. Whether visitors are interested in the history and culture of the region, or just want to relax and soak up the sun, the islands have something to offer for everyone.
