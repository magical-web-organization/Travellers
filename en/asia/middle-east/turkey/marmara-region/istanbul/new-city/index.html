Introduction to New City

Nestled in the heart of Istanbul, New City (or Beyoglu) is a lively cosmopolitan district that offers visitors a perfect blend of history and modernity. Known for its vibrant shopping scene, gastronomic delights and cultural attractions, this neighborhood is a hotspot for tourists looking to explore the best of Istanbul. New City is easily accessible by public transport, making it a convenient location to base your travels from.

List of Places to Visit in New City

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Istanbul Modern: This contemporary art museum showcases Turkey’s modern artistic talent and is one of the country’s most frequented attractions.
  • Pera Museum: The Pera Museum showcases Ottoman-era paintings, ceramics, and other artifacts from Turkey’s cultural history.
  • Galata Tower: One of Istanbul’s most famous landmarks, Galata Tower was built in the 14th century and offers spectacular views of the city from its viewing platform.
  • Istiklal Street: A bustling pedestrian street filled with shops, restaurants, and cafes. It’s an excellent spot to pick up souvenirs or to enjoy some traditional Istanbul cuisine.
  • Taksim Square: The pulse of New City, Taksim Square is a popular spot for locals and tourists alike. The square is known for its lively atmosphere, street performances, and nightlife.

Natural Attractions of New City: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Gezi Park: A tranquil oasis in the heart of New City, Gezi Park is the perfect place to take a break from the hustle and bustle of the city.
  • Bosphorus Cruise: Take a boat trip along Istanbul’s famous Bosphorus Strait and enjoy stunning views of the city’s iconic skyline from the water.
  • Taksim Gezi Park Cable Car Ride: The cable car ride from Taksim Square to Gezi Park offers breathtaking views of the city.

Hiking/Biking Routes of New City

  • Galata Bridge to Eminonu: This scenic route takes you across the Galata Bridge and along the Bosphorus Strait. You’ll pass by historic landmarks and charming neighborhoods along the way.
  • Istiklal Caddesi: This pedestrian street is a popular spot for walking and cycling. It runs the length of New City and passes by many of the area’s main attractions.

Best Things to Do in New City: Activities and Tours

  • Cooking Classes: Join a cooking class and learn how to make traditional Turkish dishes like kebabs and baklava.
  • Turkish Hamam Experience: Relax like a local and experience an authentic Turkish bath in a traditional hamam.
  • Pub Crawl: New City is known for its lively nightlife, and a pub crawl is a fantastic way to experience the area’s party scene.

Week-Long Itinerary for New City

Day Activity
Day 1 Explore the historic Galata Tower and catch the sunset from its viewing platform.
Day 2 Visit the Pera Museum to see stunning Ottoman-era art and enjoy lunch in the charming neighborhood surrounding the museum.

Food and Drink Scene of New City

Must-Try Dishes

  • Doner Kebab: A popular street food in Istanbul, doner kebab is a Turkish-style shawarma made with seasoned meat and grilled over an open flame.
  • Baklava: A sweet pastry made with layers of phyllo dough and filled with chopped nuts and honey syrup.
  • Turkish Delight: A chewy sweet flavored with rose water, pistachio or lemon.

Local Beverages

  • Turkish Tea: A staple of Turkish culture, this warm and fragrant tea is enjoyed throughout the day.
  • Raki: A traditional Turkish anise-flavored alcoholic drink that is often served with meze dishes.

Where to Stay in New City: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Pera Palace Hotel: This historic hotel was built in the late 19th century and is famous for hosting many famous guests including Ernest Hemingway and Agatha Christie.
  • Witt Istanbul Suites: Located in the heart of New City, this trendy boutique hotel offers modern design and gorgeous city views.
  • Cheya Residence Nisantasi: A stylish apartment hotel in Istanbul’s chic Nisantasi neighborhood, just a short walk from New City.

What to Buy in New City: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Turkish Coffee and Tea: You can find bags of Turkish coffee and tea in many shops throughout New City.
  • Turkish Ceramics: From colorful bowls to intricate tiled murals, Turkish ceramics are beautiful and unique souvenirs.
  • Turkish Delight: Take a box of this tasty treat home with you!

Local Culture and Customs of New City

  • Dress Code: While there are no strict rules for dress in New City, it is still respectful to dress modestly and cover your shoulders and knees when entering religious sites.
  • Tipping: Tipping is expected in restaurants and cafes, but it is not customary to tip taxi drivers or barbershops.

Best Time to Visit New City

The best time to visit New City is between May and September when the weather is warm and sunny. However, Istanbul can be very crowded during peak season, so be prepared for crowds and long lines at popular attractions.

Do’s and Don’ts of New City

Do:

  • Do try the local cuisine and sample as much street food as possible.
  • Do learn a few Turkish phrases, such as “Merhaba” (hello) and “Tesekkurler” (thank you).

Don’t:

  • Don’t drink the tap water. Stick to bottled water to avoid getting sick.
  • Don’t underestimate the time it takes to get to attractions. Plan accordingly to avoid missing out on any must-see attractions.

Safety and Health

Safety in New City

New City is a safe neighborhood, but it’s always a good idea to stay aware of your surroundings and take precautions to keep yourself and your belongings safe.

Health in New City

You don’t need any specific vaccinations to travel to New City, but it’s always smart to keep your vaccinations up to date and to take any necessary medication with you.

Getting to New City: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near New City

  • Istanbul New Airport: Located about 68 kilometers away from New City, this airport is the largest in Turkey and has direct flights from many international destinations.
  • Sabiha Gokcen Airport: Located on the Asian side of Istanbul, this airport is approximately 45 kilometers away from New City and has flights from many European destinations.

Bus Stations Near New City

  • Taksim Square Bus Terminal: This bus terminal is located right in the heart of New City, making it a convenient location to travel to and from.

Other Options

  • Taxi: Taxis are a convenient and affordable way to get around Istanbul. Make sure to agree on a price before getting into the taxi to avoid being overcharged.
  • Public Transport: Istanbul has a comprehensive public transport system including buses, trains, and ferries. The Istanbulkart is a smart card that allows you to easily use all forms of public transport.

How to Get Around in New City

Walking and cycling are the best ways to get around New City. The area is also well-served by public transport, including buses and metro lines. Taxis are also readily available, but be sure to agree on a price before getting into the taxi to avoid being overcharged.

Where to Go Next from New City: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Topkapi Palace: The former residence of Ottoman sultans, Topkapi Palace is a stunning palace complex that is a must-see for any visitor to Istanbul.
  • Hagia Sophia: A masterpiece of Byzantine architecture, Hagia Sophia is a UNESCO World Heritage Site and a must-visit for any history buff.
  • Istanbul Archaeology Museums: One of the world’s greatest museum complexes, the Istanbul Archaeology Museums is home to some of Turkey’s most important artifacts and historical treasures.

Conclusion

New City in Istanbul is a vibrant and exciting neighborhood that offers visitors a perfect blend of culture, history, and modernity. From must-visit museums to outdoor adventures and fantastic cuisine, there’s something for everyone in New City. Whether you’re planning a week-long trip or just a short stay, this travel guide has all the information you need to make the most of your time in this fascinating district.
