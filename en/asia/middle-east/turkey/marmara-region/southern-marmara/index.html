Introduction to Southern Marmara

Located in the Marmara region of Turkey, Southern Marmara is an often-overlooked travel destination that offers breathtaking natural beauty, ancient history, and a unique cultural experience. Though it is a relatively small region, Southern Marmara has much to offer, including world-renowned thermal spas, scenic hiking trails, and historic Ottoman landmarks.

List of Places to Visit in Southern Marmara

Southern Marmara is a destination that offers something for everyone, whether you are interested in history, outdoor activities, or simply soaking in the local culture. Here is a list of some of the must-visit places in the region:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Bursa City Museum: Explore the rich history of the region at the Bursa City Museum, which showcases artifacts and exhibits from the Ottoman period and beyond.
  • Green Mosque: Admire the intricate tile work and stunning architecture of this 15th-century mosque, which is a UNESCO World Heritage Site.
  • Cumalikizik Village: Visit this charming Ottoman-era village, which is renowned for its traditional houses and authentic local cuisine.
  • Mudanya Armistice House: Learn about the end of World War I and the establishment of the Turkish Republic at this historic landmark.

Natural Attractions of Southern Marmara: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Uludag National Park: Hike the stunning trails of Turkey’s oldest national park and take in the breathtaking views of the surrounding mountains and forests.
  • Lake Iznik: Enjoy a peaceful day of fishing or kayaking on the crystal-clear waters of this tranquil lake.
  • Yenisehir Botanic Garden: Explore the lush gardens and exotic plant life of this stunning botanical garden.
  • Sakarya River: Go rafting, kayaking, or fishing on this picturesque river, which is home to many species of fish and birds.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Southern Marmara

Southern Marmara is a hiker’s paradise, with dozens of scenic hiking trails and nature walks to explore. Here are some of the best hiking/biking routes in the region:

  • Kutuk Yaylasi: Hike the rugged trails of this stunning mountain valley, which offers incredible views of the surrounding peaks and valleys.
  • Oylat Caves and Thermal Springs: Explore the stunning limestone caves and soak in the therapeutic hot springs at this popular hiking and camping spot.
  • Uluabat Lake: Bike or hike along the tranquil shores of Turkey’s second-largest freshwater lake, which is home to a wide variety of wildlife and plant life.
  • Karacabey Hiking Trail: Hike through ancient forests and pastoral landscapes on this popular hiking trail, which is known for its stunning views and abundant wildlife.

Best Things to Do in Southern Marmara: Activities and Tours

In addition to hiking, biking, and exploring the cultural landmarks of Southern Marmara, there are plenty of other activities and guided tours to enjoy in the region. Here are some of the best:

  • Hot air balloon ride: Take a breathtaking hot air balloon ride over the stunning valleys and mountains of Southern Marmara.
  • Wine tours: Explore the vineyards and taste the local wine at one of the many wineries in the region.
  • Skiing on Uludag: During winter months, Uludag is transformed into a winter paradise, offering skiing opportunities for locals and visitors.
  • Pamukkale Thermal Springs: Take a day trip to the nearby Pamukkale Thermal Springs to experience the healing waters and stunning beauty of this natural wonder.

Week-Long Itinerary for Southern Marmara

To help plan your next trip to Southern Marmara, we have put together a week-long itinerary that covers all the must-visit sights and activities in the region.

Day 1: Explore the historic city of Bursa, including the Green Mosque, Bursa Castle and the Ottoman Tombs.

Day 2: Spend the day hiking or biking in Uludag National Park, taking in the stunning mountain views and lush forests.

Day 3: Take a day trip to the charming Cumalikizik Village, where you can admire the traditional Ottoman architecture and enjoy local cuisine.

Day 4: Soak in the healing waters of the Oylat Thermal Springs and explore the stunning limestone caves in the area.

Day 5: Go on a guided winery tour, tasting the local wines and learning about the history of winemaking in the region.

Day 6: Take a day trip to the nearby Pamukkale Thermal Springs, where you can experience the healing properties of the natural hot springs.

Day 7: Spend your last day exploring the local markets and bazaars, purchasing souvenirs and immersing yourself in the local culture.

Food and Drink Scene of Southern Marmara

No trip to Southern Marmara would be complete without sampling the local cuisine and beverages. Here are some of the must-try dishes and drinks in the region:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Iskender Kebap: A traditional Turkish dish from Bursa that consists of grilled meat and tomato sauce over toasted bread.
  • Balik Ekmek: A popular seafood sandwich served with fresh vegetables and herbs.
  • Manti: A type of dumpling filled with ground meat and served with yogurt and tomato sauce.
  • Baklava: A sweet pastry made with layers of phyllo dough, nuts, and honey syrup.

Local Beverages

  • Turkish Tea: A staple of Turkish culture, enjoy a cup of strong, black tea served in traditional tulip-shaped glasses.
  • Raki: A popular anise-flavored alcoholic beverage that is often served with meze dishes or enjoyed as an after-dinner drink.
  • Ayran: A refreshing yogurt-based drink that is often served alongside meals or as a digestive aid.

Where to Stay in Southern Marmara: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Southern Marmara has a wide variety of accommodation options to suit every budget and style of travel. Here are some of the top options:

  • Karakoy Butik Otel: A boutique hotel located in the heart of Bursa, offering elegantly decorated rooms and a rooftop terrace with stunning city views.

  • Uludag Ski Resort Hotel: A spacious hotel located in the heart of Uludag National Park, offering stunning mountain views and easy access to the ski slopes.

  • Mr. Brown Hotel: A budget-friendly guesthouse located in the charming village of Cumalikizik, offering cozy rooms and a communal kitchen.

What to Buy in Southern Marmara: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Southern Marmara has a rich history and cultural heritage, making it the perfect destination to pick up unique souvenirs and local products to bring home. Here are some of the top items to purchase in the region:

  • Iznik Pottery: Iznik is known for its beautiful ceramics, which depict intricate patterns and designs inspired by Ottoman-era art and architecture.
  • Silk Scarves: Bursa is renowned for its high-quality silk production, and you can find beautiful silk scarves and textiles in the local markets and bazaars.
  • Spices: Turkey is famous for its aromatic spices, and Southern Marmara is no exception. Stock up on fragrant spices such as cumin, sumac, and za’atar at the local markets and spice shops.

Local Culture and Customs of Southern Marmara

Southern Marmara has a unique culture and customs that are shaped by its historic Ottoman heritage and the diverse ethnic groups that call the region home. Some tips for respecting and embracing the local culture include dressing modestly when visiting religious landmarks, removing your shoes before entering homes or mosques, and greeting locals with a friendly “Merhaba” or “Hello.”

Best Time to Visit Southern Marmara

The best time to visit Southern Marmara is during the spring and fall months, when the weather is mild and pleasant, and the crowds are smaller. However, the region is also beautiful in the winter months when the mountains are covered in snow and the ski slopes are open.

Do’s and Don’ts of Southern Marmara

Do:

  • Do try the local cuisine and beverages, such as Iskender Kebap and Raki.

  • Do respect the local culture and dress modestly when visiting religious landmarks.

  • Do explore the natural beauty of the region, by hiking, biking, or skiing.

Don’t:

  • Don’t drink tap water, stick to bottled water instead.

  • Don’t forget to remove your shoes before entering homes or mosques.

  • Don’t litter or disrespect the local environment.

Safety and Health

Safety in Southern Marmara

Southern Marmara is generally a safe travel destination, with low levels of crime and a welcoming local population. However, it is advisable to take standard precautions and keep your belongings secured and valuables out of sight.

Health in Southern Marmara

Southern Marmara has a good standard of healthcare, with many hospitals and medical facilities available throughout the region. However, it is advisable to buy comprehensive travel insurance and carry any necessary medication with you.

Getting to Southern Marmara: Transportation Options and Logistics

Southern Marmara is easily accessible by air, bus, or car. Here are some transportation options to consider:

Airports Near Southern Marmara

  • Sabiha Gokcen International Airport in Istanbul: This airport is located on the Asian side of Istanbul and is approximately 2.5 hours from Southern Marmara by car.

  • Bursa Yenisehir Airport: This regional airport is located approximately 50 kilometers from Bursa city center and offers flights to and from various domestic destinations in Turkey.

Bus Stations Near Southern Marmara

  • Bursa Bus Terminal: This large bus terminal is located in the heart of Bursa and offers connections to other cities throughout Turkey and beyond.

Other Options

  • Car Rental: Southern Marmara is easily accessible by car, with major highways connecting the region to other parts of Turkey.

How to Get Around in Southern Marmara

Southern Marmara is best explored by car or public transportation. Local buses and dolmus (mini-buses) are inexpensive and reliable, offering connections between cities and towns throughout the region. Taxis are also available in most cities and can be hailed from the street or booked in advance.

Where to Go Next from Southern Marmara: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

If you have extra time to spare, there are plenty of nearby destinations and day-trips you can take from Southern Marmara. Here are some ideas:

  • Istanbul: Turkey’s largest city and cultural capital, Istanbul is just a short drive or bus ride away from Southern Marmara.

  • Gallipoli Peninsula: Learn about the history of the First World War and pay your respects to the fallen soldiers at the historic battlefields of Gallipoli.

  • Pergamon: Explore the ruins of this ancient city, which was once an important cultural and intellectual center in the Hellenistic period.

Conclusion

Southern Marmara is a hidden gem of Turkey, offering stunning natural beauty, rich cultural landmarks, and a unique travel experience. Whether you are interested in hiking through the mountains, soaking in therapeutic hot springs, or exploring Ottoman-era architecture, Southern Marmara is a destination that promises to dazzle and delight. So pack your bags and get ready for an adventure in this beautiful region of Turkey!
