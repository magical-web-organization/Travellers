Bursa is the fourth-largest city in Turkey and is situated in Southern Marmara province, in the Marmara region, Middle East, Asia. This beautiful city is known for its stunning natural scenery, ancient history, and traditional Turkish culture. From outdoor adventures to historical landmarks, Bursa has something to offer for travelers of all interests. In this comprehensive travel guide, we will explore the best places to visit, things to do, the local culture and customs of Bursa, and much more.

Introduction to Bursa

The city of Bursa is surrounded by beautiful mountains and is situated on the shores of the Sea of Marmara. It was the first capital city of the Ottoman Empire and is known for its thermal springs, beautiful parks, and museums. With its rich history, fascinating culture, and beautiful landscape, Bursa is a must-visit destination in Turkey.

List of Places to Visit in Bursa

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Bursa City Museum: This museum showcases the history and culture of Bursa with exhibitions on traditional handicrafts, clothing, and Ottoman artifacts.
  • Green Mosque and Tomb: This mosque is known for its intricate tilework, decoration, and beautiful interiors.
  • Bursa Castle: The castle dates back to the Ottoman period and offers spectacular views of the city.
  • Cumalikizik Village: This historic Ottoman village located on the slopes of Uludag Mountain is famous for its narrow streets, old houses and traditional Turkish architecture.
  • Grand Mosque: This mosque is one of the largest in Turkey and has beautiful interiors, a large courtyard, and an imposing minaret.
  • Historic Cumalikizik Market: This market is known for its famous chestnut candies and traditional foods.

Natural Attractions of Bursa: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Uludag National Park: Home to diverse plant and animal life, this national park offers great outdoor adventures like hiking, camping, skiing, and mountaineering while offering breathtaking views.
  • Hot Springs: This region has numerous hot springs that are great for relaxing and taking a dip.
  • Lake Iznik: An ancient city that is now submerged beneath the lake, visitors can take a tour of the area and enjoy swimming in the pristine waters of the lake.
  • Kestel Plateau and Waterfalls: This area has beautiful secluded spots surrounded by waterfalls and greenery for some peaceful family time.
  • Saitabat Waterfall: A stunning waterfall that is a popular picnic spot and popular for lovers of adventure activities like rafting.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Bursa

The following areas provide great hiking and biking experiences in Bursa:

  • Uludag National Park
  • Kestel Plateau
  • Cumalikizik Village

Best Things to Do in Bursa: Activities and Tours

  • Exploring the historic city center: With its narrow streets and traditional architecture, wandering around the city on foot is an enjoyable experience and a way to experience the local lifestyle!
  • Taking a Dip at the hot springs: Bursa is known for its thermal springs, and a visit to one of the many hot springs in the region is a must-do activity.
  • Going up to Uludag mountain: Take a relaxing journey on a cable car or the gondola to Uludag, the best skiing resort in the region.
  • Having a taste of local cuisine: Bursa’s local cuisine is famous throughout Turkey and features delicious dishes such as Iskender Kebap, Kofte, and Pide. A food tour is an excellent way to try out the local cuisine and gain insight on local food culture.
  • Visiting local markets: For those interested in buying souvenirs or locally made handicrafts, the historic Cumalikizik Market and the Silk Market would be a great place to start.

Week-Long Itinerary for Bursa

  • Day 1: Explore the city center and visit the Grand Mosque, Bursa City Museum, and the Green Mosque and Tomb.
  • Day 2: A day trip to Lake Iznik visiting the ancient city of Nicea.
  • Day 3: Enjoy a relaxing day at one of the famous Bursa hot springs.
  • Day 4: Take a tour of Cumalikizik Village and the historic market place.
  • Day 5: Hike or bike through the Uludag National Park.
  • Day 6: Visit the Kestel Plateau and enjoy the natural scenery.
  • Day 7: Visit the city’s Castle and enjoy the panoramic views of the city.

Food and Drink Scene of Bursa

Must-Try Dishes

  • Iskender Kebap: Thinly cut lamb on top of peppered bread, covered in tomato sauce and yoghurt.
  • Kofte: A delicious meatball made with bulgur, spices, and minced meat.
  • Pide: A traditional Turkish bread, great with meat or vegetables.

Local Beverages

Bursa is famous for its sweet Turkish coffee and tea offered in glasses.

Where to Stay in Bursa: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Bursa offers a wide range of accommodation options for travelers, including hotels, hostels, and guesthouses. Some of the top accommodation options include:

  • Hilton Bursa Convention Center & Spa
  • Kervansaray Thermal Hotel
  • Bursa Palas Hotel

What to Buy in Bursa: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Bursa is known for its locally made silk products and handicrafts, such as ceramics, handwoven carpets, and silk scarves. Chestnut candy is also a popular souvenir among tourists.

Local Culture and Customs of Bursa

Bursa is known for its traditional Turkish culture, and the locals are very hospitable and welcoming to visitors. It is essential to respect local customs, such as removing shoes while entering mosques and covering heads while visiting religious sites.

Best Time to Visit Bursa

The best time to visit Bursa is during the spring and autumn months (March to May and September to November), when the temperatures are mild and the area is less crowded.

Do’s and Don’ts of Bursa

Do:

  • Dress Appropriately when visiting religious sites
  • Try local dishes and drinks
  • Take advantage of hiking and outdoor activities

Don’t:

  • Be disrespectful towards local customs and culture
  • Leave trash in public areas
  • Participate in street fights.

Safety and Health

Safety in Bursa

Bursa is a relatively safe city, and visitors should take standard precautions to ensure they have a secure trip.

Health in Bursa

Bursa has excellent medical facilities, and visitors should take standard health precautions like vaccinations and staying hydrated.

Getting to Bursa: Transportation Options and Logistics

Bursa can be reached by air, bus, and train. Some of the nearest airports are the Istanbul Airport, located approximately 156 km away from Bursa, and the Yenisehir Bursa Airport located 50 km away from the city center. The city is well connected with the rest of Turkey, and taking a bus or a train ride is a convenient option too.

Airports Near Bursa

  • Istanbul Airport
  • Yenisehir Bursa Airport

Bus Stations Near Bursa

  • Terminal Bus Station
  • Mudanya Bus Terminal

Other Options

  • Ferry: If coming from Istanbul, visitors may take a ferry to Yalova and then a bus to Bursa.
  • Rent a Car: Renting a car is a good option if visitors want to visit other parts of Turkey.

How to Get Around in Bursa

Bursa has an excellent public transportation system made up of buses, trams, and a cable car that can take visitors up to Uludag National Park. Taxis, car rentals, and bikes are also available.

Where to Go Next from Bursa: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Nearby destinations to explore include Istanbul, Canakkale or Ayvalik. These are popular tourist spots that offer a wide range of activities and attractions.

Conclusion

Bursa is a unique destination with natural beauty, rich culture, and historical landmarks. This comprehensive guide will help visitors plan their trip and make the most of their stay in the city. From exploring the city center to relaxing at the hot springs or hiking through the national parks, Bursa has something for everyone!
