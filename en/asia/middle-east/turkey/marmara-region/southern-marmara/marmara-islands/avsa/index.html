If you’re looking for a sun-soaked, off-the-beaten-track getaway, Avşa Island, situated in Southern Marmara, Turkey, is the perfect destination for you. From pristine beaches to fascinating historical sites, this hidden gem offers something for every kind of traveler. In this ultimate guide, we’ll take you through the various aspects of Avşa, including where to go, what to do, and where to eat, stay and shop. So, let’s start exploring this traveler’s paradise.

Introduction to Avşa

Located in the Marmara Islands, Avşa is a tiny island that spans just 2.5 square miles in southern Marmara, Turkey. It’s one of the lesser-known travel destinations in Turkey, which makes it perfect for travelers who want to explore less-crowded and unspoiled natural sites. Avşa is known for its azure-blue waters, white-sand beaches, and cool Mediterranean climate.

List of Places to Visit in Avşa

Avşa Island is famous for its diverse array of natural and cultural attractions. Let’s take a look at some of the top places to visit in Avşa.

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Avşa Island Museum

The Avşa Island Museum is one of the main cultural attractions of the island, located in the center of the island near the harbor. Visitors can explore the exhibits that showcase the history of the island and the Marmara region, including artifacts, traditional costumes, and tools used by local people.

The Old Mosque of Avşa Island

The Old Mosque, located near the harbor in the center of the island, is a historical site dating back to the Ottoman era. It’s a great spot to learn about Islamic architecture and culture.

Natural Attractions of Avşa: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Aquapark

For outdoor enthusiasts, Aquapark offers a range of water-based activities and sports for adults and kids. It’s the perfect place to cool off on a hot summer day and have some fun with friends and family.

Hiking Trails of Avşa

Hiking lovers will adore the scenic trails that span the island, offering panoramic views of the sea and the surrounding mountains. The trails are well-marked and range in difficulty, making them suitable for both novice and experienced hikers.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Avşa

Avşa Island is a hiker’s paradise, with several exciting trails and routes to explore. Some of the most popular routes are:

  1. Kumluk Cove Trail: This trail offers a moderately challenging hike spanning over two miles, with impressive views of the island’s coastline, the sea, and the mountains.

  2. Cinarli Trail: This gentle hike offers a one-mile loop through pine trees and rocky outcrops, with scenic views of the sea.

Best Things to Do in Avşa: Activities and Tours

Avşa Island has a diverse range of activities and tours that will keep you entertained throughout your stay. Some of the best things to do on the island are:

Horse Riding

Horse riding enthusiasts can explore the island on horseback, with tour operators offering trail rides to the island’s most scenic spots.

Boat Tours

Take a boat tour around the island and get a view of the stunning coastline from the water. Spot dolphins and other marine life as you sail around the island.

Week-Long Itinerary for Avşa

If you’re planning a week-long trip to Avşa Island, here’s an itinerary that covers the must-see attractions and experiences on the island:

Day 1 Arrival and check-in at the hotel
Day 2 Visit Avşa Island Museums and explore the Old Mosque
Day 3 Spend the day at the beach and take a boat tour around the island
Day 4 Go hiking in the morning, and then take a horse ride in the afternoon
Day 5 Visit the nearby town of Marmara Island and explore its historical sites
Day 6 Take a day trip to the famous Trojan Horse in nearby Çanakkale
Day 7 Relax at the beach and enjoy the local cuisine before departure

Food and Drink Scene of Avşa

No trip to Avşa Island is complete without exploring the local cuisine. From fresh seafood to traditional Turkish dishes, the island has a diverse array of food options.

Must-Try Dishes

Some of the must-try dishes include:

  • Seafood platter: A delicious assortment of locally caught seafood, including grilled fish, calamari, and prawns.
  • Pide: A Turkish-style pizza made with fresh vegetables and meat.
  • Tahini helva: A traditional Turkish dessert made with sesame paste and sugar.

Local Beverages

The island is known for its refreshing local beverages, such as:

  • Rakı: A popular Turkish liquor made from grapes and anise.
  • Aryan: A refreshing yogurt-based drink, perfect for hot summer days.
  • Turkish Tea: A must-try beverage for any tea lover.

Where to Stay in Avşa: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Avşa Island has a good range of accommodation options, including hotels, hostels, and guesthouses. Some of the best options are:

  • Saruhan Hotel: A great mid-range hotel located close to the beach and harbor.
  • Aysem Hotel: A budget-friendly hotel with clean and comfortable rooms.
  • Guest House 34: A cozy and welcoming guesthouse that offers a personalized touch to your stay.

What to Buy in Avşa: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

When in Avşa Island, you can bring home some souvenirs and local products to cherish your memories. Some of the popular items to shop for are:

  • Turkish Delight: A sweet dessert made of starch, sugar, and flavorings, a perfect gift for someone with a sweet tooth.
  • Olive oil soap: Locally produced olive oil soap makes an ideal gift for those who love natural skincare products.
  • Hand-made ceramics: These are unique souvenirs made by local artisans, with intricate designs and patterns.

Local Culture and Customs of Avşa

The locals of Avşa Island are amicable and welcoming. Still, it’s important to respect their traditions and customs. Here are a few things to keep in mind:

  • Dress modestly when visiting historical sites or mosques.
  • Remove shoes before entering someone’s home or sacred places.
  • It’s customary to greet elders and use formal titles when addressing them.

Best Time to Visit Avşa

The best time to visit Avşa Island is between May and October when the weather is mild, and the sea is warm and perfect for swimming.

Do’s and Don’ts of Avşa

Do:

  • Respect the local customs and traditions.
  • Carry enough cash, as some shops may not accept credit cards.
  • Try local cuisine and beverages.

Don’t:

  • Litter in public places.
  • Use drugs or engage in other illegal activities.
  • Disrespect the local beliefs and practices.

Safety and Health

Safety in Avşa

Avşa Island is relatively safe, but it’s advisable to be cautious while traveling. Here are a few safety tips:

  • Don’t leave your valuables unattended.
  • Be vigilant while traveling at night.
  • Don’t take drinks or food from strangers.

Health in Avşa

Pack your essentials medications, as you may not find them readily available on the island. If you need medical assistance, head to the Avşa Island State Hospital, located in the south of the island.

Getting to Avşa: Transportation Options and Logistics

To reach Avşa Island, you can take a ferry from Istanbul to Marmara Island, and then take a smaller ferry to Avşa Island. Here are some transportation options:

Airports Near Avşa

The nearest airport is Istanbul Airport, located about a hundred miles from Marmara Island.

Bus Stations Near Avşa

Various bus services connect Istanbul to Marmara Island. From there, you can take a ferry to Avşa Island.

Other Options

You can also rent a car and drive to Marmara island, park your car on the mainland, and take a ferry to Avşa Island.

How to Get Around in Avşa

Getting around Avşa Island is relatively easy. You can walk, rent a bike, hire a taxi, or even rent a scooter to explore the island at your own pace.

Where to Go Next from Avşa: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

If you have additional time, there are several exciting destinations to explore around Avşa. Here are some popular choices:

  • Marmara Island
  • Çanakkale - famous for the Trojan Horse statue
  • Bozcaada Island - known for its beautiful beaches and local wines

Conclusion

Avşa Island is an idyllic spot for those seeking a relaxed holiday away from the crowds. The beaches, the hiking trails, the delicious food, and friendly locals will make you fall in love with this hidden gem of Turkey. Use this guide to plan your trip to Avşa Island and explore all the island has to offer. Happy travels!
