Introduction to Uzunköprü

Uzunköprü is a small town located in the Edirne province of the Western Thrace region of Turkey, just 25 kilometers away from Edirne. The town got its name from its “long bridge,” a historical landmark that crosses over the Ergene River.

The town is best known for its rich history and culture, stunning natural beauty and lush countryside, and warm hospitality. Exploring Uzunköprü is like taking a journey back in time, with its great historical sites, traditional crafts, hilly terrain, and unspoiled rural landscapes. As a lesser-known destination, it offers an off-the-beaten-path experience to travelers looking for a unique and authentic Turkish adventure.

List of Places to Visit in Uzunköprü

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Uzunköprü Long Bridge - This historical Ottoman-era bridge spans across the Ergene River and is the town’s most iconic landmark. It was built in 1443 by Grand Vizier Hacı İvaz Pasha and is listed as a UNESCO World Heritage Site.
  • Uzunköprü Mosque - This mosque, also referred to as the Grand Mosque, was built by Mimar Sinan, the great Ottoman architect in the 16th century. It is a masterpiece of Ottoman architecture, with its elegant and unique design, decorative tiles, and fine artwork on the ceilings.
  • Museum of Ethnography - This museum showcases the local culture, customs, and crafts of the Edirne region. Visitors can learn about traditional weaving, pottery, and other crafts, as well as see a collection of local ethnographic items.
  • Karaağaç Train Station - Built-in 1874 and located in a picturesque setting, the Karaağaç train station was the starting point of the Orient Express. The station is still in use today and offers a glimpse into Turkey’s railway history.

Natural Attractions of Uzunköprü: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Kirmasti Creek - This picturesque natural attraction boasts crystal clear waters, verdant forest land, and a waterfall. It is a popular spot for swimming, hiking, and picnics.
  • Pythian Temple - Located on a hilltop, this ancient temple offers panoramic views of the surrounding countryside. It was dedicated to the Greek god Apollo and is believed to have been constructed during the Hellenistic period.
  • Kayacik Forest - This beautiful forest is a great spot for camping and hiking, with its scenic trails and picnic areas. It is also home to a variety of wildlife, including deer, foxes, and rabbits.
  • Sazlidere Dam - This man-made lake, located just outside Uzunköprü, is a popular spot for fishing, boating, and picnicking. The stunning views of the surrounding hills and forests make it an ideal place for a leisurely stroll.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Uzunköprü

  • Long Bridge Trail - This 13 km trail takes hikers and bikers on a journey along the Ergene River, across the iconic Long Bridge, and through the peaceful countryside.
  • Hilltop Trail - This 7.5 km trail leads to the top of a scenic hill, offering stunning views of the surrounding countryside.

Best Things to Do in Uzunköprü: Activities and Tours

  • Visit the Long Bridge at Sunset - Take a leisurely stroll across the Long Bridge at sunset and watch as the sky turns pink and orange over the Ergene River.
  • Shop at the Local Bazaar - Browse the stalls and shops in the town’s bustling bazaar, which sells everything from spices and textiles to fresh produce and handmade goods.
  • Explore the Countryside - Take a scenic drive or a leisurely walk through the picturesque countryside, stopping to admire the stunning scenery and to chat with friendly locals.
  • Attend a Local Festival - Uzunköprü hosts several festivals throughout the year, including the Cherry Festival, the Wheat Harvest Festival, and the International Folk Music Festival.

Food and Drink Scene of Uzunköprü

Must-Try Dishes

  • Etli Ekmek - Turkish flatbread loaded with minced meat, vegetables, and lots of spices, baked in a wood-fired oven.
  • Karniyarik - Baked eggplant stuffed with minced meat, parsley, garlic, and tomatoes.
  • Paçanga Börek - A savory pastry filled with feta cheese, spinach, and parsley.

Local Beverages

  • Ayran - A refreshing yogurt-based drink, often served with spicy or heavy meals.
  • Turkish Tea - A staple of Turkish culture, tea is served throughout the day in small glass cups.
  • Raki - A strong anise-flavored spirit typically consumed as an aperitif before a meal.

Where to Stay in Uzunköprü: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Grand Ontario Hotel - This hotel offers comfortable rooms and great views of the Long Bridge.
  • Skylife Otel - Located in the town center, this hotel offers spacious rooms and a great breakfast buffet.
  • Neslihan Hanım Konağı - A charming guesthouse with clean, comfortable rooms and a welcoming host.

What to Buy in Uzunköprü: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Woven Rugs and Carpets - Uzunköprü is known for its traditional weaving, and visitors can purchase handmade carpets, kilims, and rugs from local shops and bazaars.
  • Pottery - Made from local clay, Uzunköprü’s pottery features distinctive designs and colors.
  • Edirne Pepper - A spicy and flavorful dried pepper that is a staple of Turkish cuisine.

Local Culture and Customs of Uzunköprü

  • Hospitality is a core value in Uzunköprü, and visitors are welcomed with warmth and generosity.
  • The town’s traditional crafts, including weaving, pottery, and woodworking, have been maintained for generations.
  • Uzunköprü is a predominantly Muslim town, and visitors should dress modestly and respect local customs.

Best Time to Visit Uzunköprü

The best time to visit Uzunköprü is during the spring (April to June) or fall (September to November), when the weather is mild and pleasant.

Do’s and Don’ts of Uzunköprü

Do:

  • Dress modestly and respectfully, especially in religious sites.
  • Try the local cuisine and beverages, including Turkish tea and Raki.
  • Engage with locals and learn about their culture and customs.

Don’t:

  • Photograph people without their permission.
  • Disrespect religious sites and customs.
  • Bargain too aggressively in shops and bazaars.

Safety and Health

Safety in Uzunköprü

Uzunköprü is generally safe for travelers. Visitors should take normal precautions, such as avoiding isolated areas and keeping valuables out of sight.

Health in Uzunköprü

There are no major health concerns in Uzunköprü. However, visitors should carry necessary medications with them and ensure that they have travel insurance.

Getting to Uzunköprü: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Uzunköprü

The nearest airport to Uzunköprü is the Edirne Airport, which is approximately 25 km away.

Bus Stations Near Uzunköprü

Uzunköprü has a small bus station that serves local and regional routes. The Edirne Bus Terminal, located in the nearby city of Edirne, offers connections to major cities, including Istanbul and Ankara.

Other Options

Visitors can arrive in Uzunköprü by taking a train or hiring a car.

How to Get Around in Uzunköprü

The town center of Uzunköprü can be easily explored on foot. Bicycles and scooters are also available for rent.

Where to Go Next from Uzunköprü: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Edirne - Just 25 km away, Edirne is a charming city with a rich history and culture. The city is home to beautiful mosques, bustling bazaars, and a vibrant food scene.
  • Gallipoli Peninsula National Park - This beautiful nature reserve is a popular spot for camping, hiking, and swimming, with its stunning beaches and lush forests.
  • Alexandroupolis - This Greek city is located across the border from Uzunköprü and is home to beautiful beaches, ancient sites, and a lively nightlife.

Conclusion

Uzunköprü is a hidden gem in Turkey that offers a unique and authentic travel experience. Visitors can explore the town’s rich history and culture, hike the scenic trails, and sample the delicious local cuisine. With its warm hospitality and stunning natural beauty, Uzunköprü is a destination that should be on every traveler’s list.
