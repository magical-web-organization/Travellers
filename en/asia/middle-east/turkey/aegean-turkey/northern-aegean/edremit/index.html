Introduction to Edremit

Located in the Aegean region of Turkey, Edremit is a small town that is often overlooked by tourists. However, those who do make the journey are rewarded with a rich history, beautiful natural surroundings, and a welcoming local culture.

List of Places to Visit in Edremit

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Kazdağı National Park: Surrounded by the Kaz Mountains, or Kazdağı in Turkish, this national park is home to a variety of flora and fauna, as well as ancient ruins.
  • Assos: Perched on a hill overlooking the Aegean Sea, Assos features ancient ruins, including the remains of a temple constructed by Aristotle’s student, Aristotle.
  • Antandros Ancient City: This ancient city features ruins dating back to the 4th century BC, including a well-preserved theater.

Natural Attractions of Edremit: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Cunda Island: Just off the coast of Edremit, Cunda Island offers picturesque beaches and stunning sunsets.
  • Ayvalık Islands: A group of islands just off the coast of Edremit, Ayvalık Islands offer secluded beaches and crystal-clear waters.
  • Beydağları Coastal National Park: This national park features scenic views of the Mediterranean coastline and a variety of hiking trails.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Edremit

  • Sutculer Yaylası: A popular hiking spot, Sutculer Yaylası is located in the Kaz Mountains and offers stunning views of the surrounding landscape.
  • Kozak Yaylası: Located near the village of Kozak, this area provides a challenging bike ride through the mountains and beautiful scenery.

Best Things to Do in Edremit: Activities and Tours

  • Traditional Turkish Bath: Experience traditional Turkish culture with a relaxing bath and massage at one of Edremit’s historic hammams.
  • Wine Tasting Tours: Take a tour of the local vineyards and sample some of Turkey’s finest wines.
  • Jeep Safari Tours: Explore the beautiful countryside of Edremit on a Jeep safari tour.

Food and Drink Scene of Edremit

Must-Try Dishes

  • Zeytinyağlı Yaprak Sarma: A traditional vegetarian dish made of vine leaves stuffed with rice and herbs and cooked in olive oil.
  • Balık Dolması: Stuffed with onion, tomato, garlic, and herbs, this dish features a whole fish cooked in the oven.
  • Ayvalık Toast: A popular Turkish snack, Ayvalık toast consists of toasted bread topped with melted cheese, tomato sauce, and spices.

Local Beverages

  • Turkish Tea: Served in small glasses, Turkish tea is a staple of daily life in Turkey.
  • Raki: A strong anise-flavored liquor, raki is commonly consumed with meals.

Where to Stay in Edremit: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Istanbul Hotel: This historic hotel located in the city center offers traditional Turkish hospitality and easy access to local attractions.
  • Limon Pansiyon: A cozy guesthouse located on a quiet street in the old town, Limon Pansiyon offers comfortable accommodations and a welcoming atmosphere.
  • Cunda Sade Hotel: Located on Cunda Island, this modern hotel features stylish rooms and a rooftop bar with stunning views of the Aegean Sea.

What to Buy in Edremit: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Olive Oil: Known for its high-quality olive oil production, Edremit offers a variety of olive oil products that make great gifts or souvenirs.
  • Turkish Delight: A sweet confection made of sugar, cornstarch, and flavorings, Turkish Delight is a popular treat in Turkey.
  • Ceramic Bowls: Made in local workshops, these handmade ceramic bowls are a beautiful and functional souvenir to bring home.

Local Culture and Customs of Edremit

  • Hospitality: Turkish culture is known for its hospitality, and visitors to Edremit can expect warm welcomes and generous gestures from the locals.
  • Ramadan: A Muslim holiday that occurs during the ninth month of the lunar calendar, Ramadan is an important celebration for many locals in Edremit.

Best Time to Visit Edremit

The best time to visit Edremit is between April and June or September and November, when the weather is mild and crowds are smaller.

Do’s and Don’ts of Edremit

Do:

  • Dress modestly when visiting religious sites.
  • Remove your shoes before entering someone’s home or a mosque.
  • Learn a few Turkish phrases before arriving.

Don’t:

  • Public displays of affection are considered rude in Turkish culture.
  • Discuss politics or religion with locals.

Safety and Health

Safety in Edremit

Edremit is a relatively safe town, but visitors should still take precautions to protect themselves from pickpockets and scams. It is also important to be aware of your surroundings and avoid walking alone at night in isolated areas.

Health in Edremit

Visitors to Edremit should ensure that they are up to date on their routine vaccinations and consider receiving vaccinations for Hepatitis A and B. It is also important to drink bottled water and take measures to prevent mosquito bites.

Getting to Edremit: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Edremit

  • Balikesir Koca Seyit Airport: Located just 8 km from Edremit, this airport serves flights from Istanbul and Ankara.
  • Izmir Adnan Menderes Airport: Located 155 km from Edremit, this airport serves both domestic and international flights.

Bus Stations Near Edremit

  • Edremit Bus Station: Located in the city center, this bus station serves buses from various locations throughout Turkey.
  • Balıkesir Bus Station: Located just 36 km from Edremit, this bus station offers frequent connections to other cities throughout Turkey.

Other Options

  • Car rental: Visitors can rent cars from various locations in Edremit to explore the surrounding area at their own pace.
  • Taxi: Taxis are readily available in Edremit for both short and long-distance trips.

How to Get Around in Edremit

The best way to get around Edremit is by taxi or on foot. Visitors can also rent bicycles or scooters for a fun and easy way to explore the town and surrounding areas.

Where to Go Next from Edremit: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Troy: Located 200 km from Edremit, the ancient ruins of Troy are a must-see for history buffs.
  • Ephesus: One of the best preserved ancient cities in the world, Ephesus is located 275 km from Edremit.
  • Pamukkale: Famous for its natural hot springs and calcium travertine terraces, Pamukkale is located 320 km from Edremit.

Conclusion

Whether you are drawn by the rich history, beautiful nature, or warm Turkish hospitality, Edremit has something to offer everyone. Use this comprehensive tour guide to plan your perfect trip and discover all that this hidden gem has to offer.
