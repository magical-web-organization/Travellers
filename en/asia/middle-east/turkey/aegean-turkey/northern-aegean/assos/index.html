Introduction to Assos

Assos is a charming small town located in the northern part of the Aegean region in Turkey, known for its stunning beaches, crystal clear waters, ancient ruins, and delicious Mediterranean cuisine. The town is situated in a picturesque location surrounded by green hills overlooking the Gulf of Edremit. The town’s historic landmarks and nature make it a popular tourist destination that attracts visitors worldwide.

List of Places to Visit in Assos

Assos is a stunning destination that appeals to both nature enthusiasts and culture lovers. Here are some of the most visited tourist attractions in the area:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Assos Ancient Theater

The Assos ancient theater is one of the most significant archeological sites in Turkey, dating back to the Hellenistic period. The theater is situated on the hill overlooking the sea, which offers fantastic views of the Gulf of Edremit.

Assos Acropolis

Assos Acropolis is a fascinating landmark located on the hilltop, home to the Athena Temple, offering a glimpse into the common artistic practices of ancient civilizations.

Hamam of Hudavendigar

The Hamam of Hudavendigar is an Ottoman era Turkish bath complex that is still operational today. It offers a unique experience for the visitors to enjoying a traditional Turkish bath.

Natural Attractions of Assos: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Assos Beaches

Assos is home to some of the most stunning beaches in Turkey, where visitors can enjoy swimming, sunbathing, and water sports. Some of the top beaches in Assos include Kadırga Bay, Sivrice Bay, and Assos Beach.

Behramkale Village

Behramkale village is a traditional Turkish village located close to Assos. It offers a glimpse into the traditional Turkish lifestyle and is great for hiking or strolling around to soak up the charm and culture of the town.

Assos National Park

Assos National Park is a protected area with great trekking and hiking routes that provide breathtaking views of the Aegean Sea and the surrounding hills.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Assos

Assos is well known for its stunning natural beauty and offers various hiking and biking routes to explore its scenery. Some of the most popular routes include trekking routes of Sivrice Bay and Assos, famous for its lush greenery and spectacular panoramic sea views. Visitors can experience a range of outdoor activities such as hiking, cycling and horse riding.

Best Things to Do in Assos: Activities and Tours

Assos has a vast array of tourist activities that visitors can enjoy to make the most of their stay. Below are some of the best things to do in Assos:

Visit a Traditional Turkish Market

If you’re a fan of shopping, Assos has unique colorful traditional Turkish markets where visitors can shop for local souvenirs, traditional clothes, and accessories.

Boat Tours of Assos

Assos is famous for its boat tours, which provide a unique perspective of the rugged coastline and explore the hidden coves and bays of the area.

Stand-Up Paddleboarding

Stand-up paddleboarding is an excellent activity for those looking for a fun and challenging water sport. Assos provides great opportunities for this activity due to its clear blue waters.

Week-Long Itinerary for Assos

A week in Assos provides plenty of time to explore its natural beauty, ancient ruins, and culture. Below is a suggested itinerary for a week in Assos:

Food and Drink Scene of Assos

Assos is known for its traditional Mediterranean cuisine, and here are some of the must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Köfte: Turkish meatballs made with beef, parsley and spices.
  • Midye Dolma: Stuffed mussels with rice, herbs, and currants.
  • Kabak Çiçeği Dolma: Fried stuffed zucchini flowers with cheese and herbs.

Local Beverages

  • Raki: An alcoholic drink similar to ouzo, made with aniseed.
  • Çay: Turkish tea served black in small glasses
  • Ayran: A refreshing yogurt-based drink often served as a side dish to food.

Where to Stay in Assos: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Assos offers various options for accommodations according to one’s budget and travel style. Some of the best places to stay in Assos are:

  • Assos Alarga Guesthouse
  • Assos Eden Beach Resort
  • Assos Dove Hotel

What to Buy in Assos: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Assos has many great souvenirs and local products to take home as a souvenir, for instance, traditional Turkish handmade products, spices, and olive oil. The best places to get these items are the traditional Turkish markets in the area.

Local Culture and Customs of Assos

Assos people are mostly friendly and hospitable but appreciate respectful behavior towards their local customs and traditions. Visitors are expected to dress modestly and remove their shoes in the homes and mosques.

Best Time to Visit Assos

The best time to visit Assos is between April and October when the weather is mild, making it the perfect time for outdoor activities, and the town is buzzing with tourists and events.

Do’s and Don’ts of Assos

Do:

  • Respect the local customs and traditions.
  • Try traditional Turkish cuisine and drinks.
  • Enjoy the stunning natural beauty of the area.

Don’t:

  • Wear revealing clothes when visiting mosques, churches or public places.
  • Touch artifacts at the historical sites.
  • Drink tap water.

Safety and Health

Safety in Assos

Assos is a safe town to visit, but it’s important to take precautions such as avoiding leaving valuables in an unattended car or unsupervised areas.

Health in Assos

Turkey has a public and private healthcare system, with doctors and nurses available 24/7. Visitors should bring their travel insurance with them to make sure they have adequate coverage.

Getting to Assos: Transportation Options and Logistics

It’s easy to reach Assos with several transportation options available:

Airports Near Assos

The nearest airport to Assos is Edremit Airport located about an hour’s drive away. Alternatively, visitors can fly to İzmir Adnan Menderes Airport or İstanbul New Airport, which is also within a few hours’ drive distance.

Bus Stations Near Assos

Buses to Assos are available from major Turkish cities such as Ankara, Izmir, and Istanbul. Visitors can buy their tickets through online or at the central bus stations throughout Turkey.

Other Options

Visitors can also choose to drive or rent a car, although they need to be aware of the tolls and traffic regulations in Turkey.

How to Get Around in Assos

Assos is a small town and easily accessible on foot. However, visitors can also rent a car, bike, or scooter to explore its surroundings.

Where to Go Next from Assos: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Assos is a great base to explore the surrounding areas. Some of the popular destinations for day trips are:

  • Troy Ancient City
  • Gallipoli Peninsula Historical National Park
  • Mount Ida (Kazdağı) National Park

Conclusion

Assos is a beautiful destination in Turkey that appeals to both culture lovers and outdoor enthusiasts. With its stunning beaches, ancient ruins, delicious food, and welcoming locals, Assos is undoubtedly an unforgettable Turkish experience.
