Introduction to Babakale

Babakale is nestled on a hill at the edge of the Aegean Sea in Turkey’s Balikesir Province. This charming town is surrounded by pristine countryside, rolling hills, and crystal-clear waters, making it an ideal destination for nature lovers and outdoor enthusiasts. Babakale’s streets are lined with antique buildings, small inns, cozy restaurants, and local shops. Its rich culture, history, and breathtaking vistas make it worthy of a spot in every traveler’s bucket list.

List of Places to Visit in Babakale

Babakale offers an array of cultural and natural attractions for travelers to explore. Here are some of the must-visit places in Babakale:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Babakale Kalesi - The Babakale Castle is the town’s main attraction, built by the Venetians in the 18th century. From the castle’s high walls, visitors can spot magnificent views of the town and the sea. The castle has a small museum and a teahouse where visitors can relax.
  • Assos, Behramkale - Just 30 minutes from Babakale, Assos is a well-preserved ancient city founded in the 7th century BC by the Aeolians. It features a monumental gate, a theater, and ruins of numerous temples.

Natural Attractions of Babakale: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Zeus Altari National Park - This remarkable national park is home to many endemic plant and animal species, including the rare Caucasian fir tree, and a unique species of wild goat named Bezoar ibex. Visitors can enjoy hiking and picnicking in the park with its stunning views of the Aegean.
  • Babakale Beach - This stunning beach with crystal-clear turquoise waters is an ideal spot for a relaxing swim, sunbathing, or snorkeling.
  • Geyikli Kazdaglari Natural Park - A mountain sanctuary in Kaz valley, visitors can discover illuminated underground caves, view endangered species, and discover the natural environment via hiking trails.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Babakale

Babakale is a fantastic destination for hiking and biking enthusiasts, with plenty of trails for every skill level.

  • Dede Mountain Trail: A moderate trail for explorers that leads to the top of Dede Mountain to admire the scenic views; this hike is perfect for seeing the rare flowers and plants that are native to Babakale.
  • Thermal Springs Trail: A relatively easy hike of around 6 km, this trail takes in the stunning views of the thermal springs as they rise from the earth, and wildflowers and wildlife that seem to be dancing along the way.

Best Things to Do in Babakale: Activities and Tours

  • Kayaking and Canoeing: Visitors can explore the rugged coastline and hidden coves on a guided Kayak or Canoeing tour
  • Cooking Classes: Visitors interested in Turkish cuisine can learn how to cook traditional Turkish dishes; from fresh fish to lamb kebabs and more.
  • Sailing and boat tours: Discover the beauty of the coast via a catamaran tour or full-day sailing trip with an experienced captain.

Week-Long Itinerary for Babakale

Here is a suggested week-long itinerary for visitors to enjoy the best of what Babakale has to offer:

Day Activity
1 Visit Babakale Castle and enjoy stunning sea views
2 Take a guided tour of Assos, Behramkale, and explore the ancient ruins
3 Hike the Dede Mountain Trail and enjoy scenic views
4 Go for a swim and snorkel at Babakale Beach
5 Take a day trip to the Zeus Altari National Park and discover natural beauty
6 Sign up for a kayaking tour or a cooking class
7 Go sailing for a day or go souvenir shopping in town

Food and Drink Scene of Babakale

Babakale has a vibrant food and drink scene with plenty of local dishes and beverages that visitors must try.

Must-Try Dishes

  • Pide: A Turkish-style pizza that comes topped with a variety of meats and vegetables
  • Dolma: Stuffed grape leaves, a popular Turkish appetizer
  • Fresh Seafood: With Babakale’s close proximity to the Aegean Sea, visitors can enjoy fresh seafood cooked to perfection.

Local Beverages

  • Raki: A popular anise-flavored spirit that locals enjoy with mezze, the Turkish version of appetizers
  • Turkish Coffee: Served in traditional copper pots, Turkish coffee is known for its strong, rich flavor, and is a staple of Turkish culture.

Where to Stay in Babakale: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Babakale has a range of accommodations, including hotels, hostels, and guesthouses suitable for all budgets:

  • Babakale Göl Pansiyon: A comfortable and cozy guesthouse located near the town center, accessible by the beach and the castle walls.
  • Babakale Pension: A traditional-style hotel with charming rooms and out of this world breakfast

What to Buy in Babakale: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Babakale offers an array of souvenirs and local products to bring home, including:

  • Olive oil: Babakale olive oil is highly valued locally, known for its excellent quality.
  • Ceramics: Visitors can find charming ceramics like bowls, plates, and vases handmade by local artisans in the town’s shops

Local Culture and Customs of Babakale

The local culture of Babakale is a mix of Turkish and Greek influences. Some cultural norms visitors should respect include dress codes in mosques, haggling in local markets, and not smoking in public places where signs indicate no smoking.

Best Time to Visit Babakale

The best time to visit Babakale is from April to November when the weather is mild and pleasant. In summer, the town gets crowded with tourists, which can make it more challenging to find affordable accommodation.

Do’s and Don’ts of Babakale

Do:

  • Respect the local customs and traditions and dress modestly when visiting mosques or religious sites.
  • Taste the local cuisine and try traditional Turkish dishes.
  • Do try to learn some basic Turkish before you travel. It will help you communicate better with the locals.

Don’t:

  • Don’t litter or disrespect the local environment.
  • Don’t impose your cultural beliefs on the locals.
  • Don’t forget to tip your waiters, servers, or tour guides if they offer outstanding service.

Safety and Health

Safety in Babakale

Babakale is a safe destination for travelers. Tourists should avoid wandering around unfamiliar areas at night or avoid walking alone in remote areas. They should also keep an eye on their belongings, especially in crowded areas.

Health in Babakale

Babakale is an environmentally conscious town with modern health facilities. However, visitors are advised to consult their doctors regarding health precautions and any relevant vaccinations before traveling.

Getting to Babakale: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Babakale

The nearest airports to Babakale are:

  • Bandirma (BDM) - 103 km
  • Balikesir (BZI) - 128 km
  • Edremit (EDO) - 140 km

Bus Stations Near Babakale

  • The nearest bus station to Babakale is Ayvacik town; travelers must take a taxi to Babakale afterward.

Other Options

  • Tourists can arrive by ferry via the nearby Geyikli pier.

How to Get Around in Babakale

  • Babakale is a small town, and visitors can easily get around on foot, bicycle or scooter. Bus service is also available.

Where to Go Next from Babakale: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Troy: A legendary city, and a UNESCO World Heritage site that is approximately two hours away from Babakale.
  • Tenedos: A small island located near the town of Geyikli that offers scenic natural beauty and sandy beaches.
  • Pergamon: The ancient city of Pergamon offers a glimpse into Greco-Roman history over an hour from Babakale.

Conclusion

Babakale is a gem of a town, with its captivating beauty, intriguing culture, and stunning natural surroundings. Whether you’re into exploring ancient ruins, discovering local folklore, or finding the best viewpoints, Babakale offers something for every traveler. Happy exploring!
