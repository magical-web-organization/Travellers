Introduction to Bergama

Nestled on the west coast of Turkey, Bergama is a small but culturally rich city in the Izmir Province. Known as Pergamon in ancient times, it was once a major center of learning and home to the impressive Pergamon Altar. Nowadays, Bergama is an excellent tourist destination for those interested in history, outdoor adventures, and local Turkish culture.

In this comprehensive travel guide, we’ll explore the best things to do in Bergama, from visiting its historical places and natural attractions, to hiking/biking routes, local food and drink scene, where to stay, transportation and logistics, and nearby destinations to visit.

List of Places to Visit in Bergama

Bergama is a small city, but it has a wealth of tourist attractions. To make the most of your trip, consider visiting the following places.

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Pergamon Acropolis and Theater: One of the most important historical sites in Bergama, the Pergamon Acropolis is a complex of temples, theaters, and courtyards, and the Pergamon Theater is the steepest ancient theater in the world.
  • Bergama Archeological Museum: Housed in a 19th-century mosque, the Bergama Museum houses a collection of artifacts and treasures from the ancient city of Pergamon.
  • The Red Basilica: This large, unusual brick building was originally a temple dedicated to the Egyptian god, Serapis.
  • Asklepion: This ancient medical center was one of the most significant in the Hellenistic world.
  • Kizil Avlu (Red Courtyard): Once home to the Red Basilica marketplace, this large courtyard now serves as a popular spot for locals to gather and relax.

Natural Attractions of Bergama: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Dikili Beach: Located just a few kilometers north of Bergama, Dikili Beach is a popular spot for sunbathing and swimming.
  • Kestane Stream: This winding stream runs through the hills above Bergama and is a popular spot for picnicking and hiking.
  • Mount Ida: Located about an hour’s drive from Bergama, Mount Ida is a breathtaking natural attraction and a popular spot for hiking and picnicking.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Bergama

Bergama is surrounded by rolling hills and stunning natural scenery, making it a paradise for outdoor enthusiasts. Some popular hiking and biking routes include:

  • The Selcuk Trail: This 17-kilometer trail runs through forests and hills and offers stunning views of the surrounding landscape.
  • The Bergama-Edremit Route: This 28-kilometer route is a favorite among local mountain bikers and stretches from the coast to the mountains.

Best Things to Do in Bergama: Activities and Tours

In addition to its historical and natural attractions, Bergama offers plenty of activities and tours. Here are some of the best:

  • Turkish Bath (Hamami): Experience the Turkish tradition of bathing at one of Bergama’s many hamams.
  • Olive Oil Tasting: Bergama is known for its high-quality olive oil, and many local producers offer tasting tours.
  • Hot Air Balloon Ride: Take in the beauty of Bergama from above with a hot air balloon ride.
  • Walking Tour: A walking tour is an excellent way to explore the historic sites and local culture of Bergama.
  • Cooking Class: Learn how to cook traditional Turkish dishes at one of Bergama’s many cooking schools.

Week-Long Itinerary for Bergama

To help you make the most of your trip, we’ve put together a week-long itinerary for Bergama:

Food and Drink Scene of Bergama

Bergama is known for its traditional Turkish cuisine and delicious local dishes. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Kofte: A spiced meatball dish made with lamb or beef.
  • Pide: A type of Turkish pizza made with cheese, meat, or vegetables.
  • Baklava: A sweet pastry made with layers of phyllo dough, nuts, and honey.

Local Beverages

  • Raki: A traditional anise-flavored liquor often served with meze (appetizers).
  • Ayran: A refreshing yogurt-based drink mixed with water and salt.

Where to Stay in Bergama: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Bergama offers a range of accommodations to suit all budgets and needs. Here are some popular options:

  • Anil Hotel: A mid-range option located in the heart of Bergama.
  • Bergama Hotel: A budget-friendly option located just a short walk from Bergama’s historic sites.
  • Sabri Bey Konagi: A charming guesthouse located in a restored Ottoman mansion.

What to Buy in Bergama: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Bergama is known for its handmade crafts and artisanal products. Some popular souvenirs and local products include:

  • Carpets and kilims: Hand-woven rugs and carpets made in Turkey.
  • Ceramic bowls and plates: Beautifully crafted ceramic bowls and plates.
  • Olive oil: High-quality olive oil from local producers.

Local Culture and Customs of Bergama

Bergama is a conservative but welcoming city. When visiting, be respectful of local customs and dress modestly, particularly when visiting mosques or other religious sites. Many locals speak Turkish, but English is also widely spoken.

Best Time to Visit Bergama

The best time to visit Bergama is during the spring (April to June) or fall (September to November) when temperatures are mild and the crowds are thinner. Temperatures can soar during the summer months, making it less pleasant to explore the city’s outdoor attractions.

Do’s and Don’ts of Bergama

Do:

  • Dress modestly when visiting religious sites.
  • Try traditional Turkish dishes and local specialties.
  • Bargain when shopping at local markets.

Don’t:

  • Take pictures of people without asking for their permission first.
  • Drink tap water (stick to bottled water).
  • Ignore local customs and traditions.

Safety and Health

Safety in Bergama

Bergama is a safe city for tourists to visit. However, be aware of pickpocketing in crowded areas and use caution when traveling alone at night.

Health in Bergama

Pack a first aid kit when traveling to Bergama and make sure you have health insurance that covers medical emergencies. Stick to bottled water and avoid eating raw or uncooked foods.

Getting to Bergama: Transportation Options and Logistics

Bergama is accessible by car, bus, or plane. Here are some options:

Airports Near Bergama

The nearest airport to Bergama is Izmir Adnan Menderes Airport, which is a two-hour drive away.

Bus Stations Near Bergama

Bergama Bus Station (Otogar) is located on the outskirts of the city and offers frequent services to Izmir and other cities in Turkey.

Other Options

You can also reach Bergama by car (about 2.5 hours from Izmir) or by hiring a private driver.

How to Get Around in Bergama

Bergama is a small city that is easily explored on foot. Taxis and local buses are also available for transport within the city.

Where to Go Next from Bergama: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Bergama is located near several other popular destinations in Turkey. Here are some nearby places to visit:

  • Izmir: Turkey’s third-largest city is just a two-hour drive from Bergama and offers plenty of attractions, including the historic Kemeralti Bazaar and the ancient Agora Open Air Museum.
  • Cesme: This popular coastal resort town is a popular spot for sunbathing and water sports and is located about 90 minutes from Bergama.
  • Ayvalik: Located about an hour from Bergama, Ayvalik is a charming seaside town with a historic Ottoman quarter and beautiful sea views.

Conclusion

Whether you’re interested in history, culture, outdoor adventures or local cuisine, Bergama has something to offer every traveler. Use this comprehensive travel guide to plan your trip to this fascinating Turkish city and create unforgettable memories.
