Introduction to Sandıklı

Sandıklı is a small yet picturesque town located in the Afyonkarahisar province of Central Anatolia, Turkey. It has a rich history that dates back to the Phrygian period and is surrounded by stunning natural wonders. This travel guide will provide you with a comprehensive tour of Sandıklı, including the best places to visit, hiking and biking trails, where to stay, and what to eat.

List of Places to Visit in Sandıklı

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

1. Phrygian Valley

Located about 10 minutes from Sandıklı, the Phrygian Valley offers a glimpse into the ancient Phrygian civilization, known for their distinctive rock-cut tombs and monuments.

2. Sandıklı Museum

The Sandıklı Museum houses artefacts from the Classical, Hellenistic, Roman and Byzantine periods.

3. Ulu Mosque

The Ulu Mosque is a beautiful example of Ottoman architecture and features intricate tile work and calligraphy.

4. Afyonkarahisar Castle

Located just outside Sandıklı, Afyonkarahisar Castle dates back to the Hellenistic period and offers stunning views of the surrounding area.

Natural Attractions of Sandıklı: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

1. Dumlupınar Canyon

The Dumlupınar Canyon is a beautiful natural wonder located just a short drive from Sandıklı. Visitors can hike, bike or even take a guided tour to explore its breathtaking scenery.

2. Akdağ Mountain National Park

The Akdağ Mountain National Park is home to beautiful scenery, lush forestry, and panoramic views of the surrounding area.

3. Çakıtır Lake

Located on the outskirts of Sandıklı, Çakıtır Lake is a tranquil spot with beautiful views of the lake and mountains. Visitors can enjoy fishing, kayaking or simply take a relaxing stroll.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Sandıklı

Sandıklı is a hiker’s paradise with a range of trails for all experience levels. Some popular routes include the Dumlupınar Canyon trail, the Akdağ Mountain trail, and the Phrygian Valley trail. Visitors can also take guided tours or rent bikes to explore the area on two wheels.

Best Things to Do in Sandıklı: Activities and Tours

1. Turkish Bath

Visitors can indulge in the traditional Turkish bath experience at one of Sandıklı’s many bathhouses. Experience a relaxing massage, hot bath, and steam room.

2. Hot Springs

Sandıklı is famous for its hot springs, offering visitors a chance to relieve stress and relax in the thermal waters. Try the thermal baths at Sandıklı Thermal Spa or Omer Thermal Hotel.

3. Local Market

Head to Sandıklı’s local market, known for its fresh produce, clothing and traditional handicrafts.

Food and Drink Scene of Sandıklı

Must-Try Dishes

1. Börek

A Turkish savory pastry made with phyllo dough and filled with spinach and feta cheese or meat.

2. Mantı

A Turkish version of ravioli, Mantı are small dumplings stuffed with seasoned ground meat and served with yogurt and tomato sauce.

3. Kebabs

Turkish cuisine is known for its kebabs, which are grilled skewers of marinated meat, vegetables or seafood.

Local Beverages

1. Turkish Tea

No trip to Turkey is complete without trying the country’s famous “chai”.

2. Ayran

A popular summer drink made of yogurt, water, and salt, Ayran is a refreshing and healthy beverage.

Where to Stay in Sandıklı: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Sandıklı offers a range of accommodations to suit all budgets, from cozy guesthouses to luxury hotels. Some popular choices include the Sandıklı Thermal Spa Hotel, Omer Thermal Hotel and Sandıklı Dört Mevsim Otel.

What to Buy in Sandıklı: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Sandıklı is known for its traditional handicrafts, including carpets, woven textiles, pottery, and jewelry. Be sure to check out the local markets for unique souvenirs to bring back home.

Local Culture and Customs of Sandıklı

Turkey is a conservative country where modesty is expected. Visitors should dress modestly and be respectful of local customs. Sandıklı is known for its hospitality and friendly locals, so be sure to embrace the local culture and learn a few phrases in Turkish.

Best Time to Visit Sandıklı

The best time to visit Sandıklı is during the spring and fall when temperatures are mild and pleasant.

Do’s and Don’ts of Sandıklı

Do:

  • Respect local customs and traditions.
  • Dress modestly.
  • Try traditional Turkish cuisine.
  • Be polite and respectful to the locals.

Don’t:

  • Do not disrespect local customs and traditions.
  • Do not wear revealing clothing.
  • Do not leave trash behind when hiking.

Safety and Health

Safety in Sandıklı

Sandıklı is a safe destination for tourists. Visitors are advised to take the usual precautions when traveling, such as keeping valuables safe and being aware of pickpockets in crowded areas.

Health in Sandıklı

Sandıklı has several hospitals and medical centers that are well-equipped to handle emergencies. Visitors are advised to purchase travel insurance before their trip for added peace of mind.

Getting to Sandıklı: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Sandıklı

The nearest major airports are the Afyon Airport and the Esenboga Airport in Ankara.

Bus Stations Near Sandıklı

Sandıklı has a bus station that services both domestic and international destinations.

Other Options

Visitors can also rent a car or hire a private driver to reach Sandıklı.

How to Get Around in Sandıklı

Sandıklı is a small town that can easily be explored on foot. Visitors can also rent a car or take the local minibuses (dolmuş) to get around.

Where to Go Next from Sandıklı: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Sandıklı is conveniently located near several other popular destinations in Turkey. Some popular excursions include:

  • Afyonkarahisar, known for its castle and hot springs.
  • Konya, the spiritual center of Turkey known for its whirling dervishes.
  • Pamukkale, a UNESCO World Heritage Site famous for its natural hot springs.

Conclusion

Sandıklı is a hidden gem in Central Anatolia, offering visitors a chance to explore Turkey’s rich cultural and natural heritage. With its stunning scenery, warm hospitality, and delicious cuisine, it’s a destination not to be missed.
