Introduction to Sivas

Sivas is a city located in the Central Anatolia region of Turkey, one of the most traditional and historically rich regions in the country. Sivas is considered the fourth largest city of the region, and it is famous for its significant contributions to the Turkish culture and history. The city represents the perfect blend of ancient and modern elements. It is surrounded by high mountains and vast plains, providing a diverse range of activities to explore.

List of Places to Visit in Sivas

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Sivas Museum: This museum has a vast collection of artifacts, ceramics, and textiles from Hittite and Ottoman times. It is an excellent place to learn about the history of the city.

  • Gök Medrese: Built-in the 13th century, this historical center is one of the best examples of Seljuk architecture in Turkey.

  • Köprülü Mehmet Pasha Mosque: A stunning Ottoman mosque built in the early 17th century, made even more captivating by the garden surrounding it.

  • Sultan Sahabuddin Turbesi: The most important historic tomb in Sivas, considered a sacred place for visitors.

Natural Attractions of Sivas: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Susehri Plateau: A beautiful plateau on the outskirts of Sivas known for its magnificent scenery and alpine flora.

  • Zara Pınarbaşı: Known for its natural spas, Sivas has a long-established spa culture, and Zara Pınarbaşı is the most famous location for thermal waters.

  • Eskigümüş Monastery: A beautiful, isolated monastery from the Byzantine era, perfect for those who love picturesque landscapes and history.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Sivas

Sivas is a beautiful city with various terrains that are perfect for cycling and trekking. Here are the top routes to check out—

  • Sivas City Center: A great way to explore Sivas’ city center, seeing the landmarks of museums, mosques, and historical places.

  • Sivas to Demirkent: This route covers through the vast and beautiful countryside, passing by traditional Turkish houses and small villages.

  • Sivas to Kalintepe: The route starts in the city center, leading the way to the top of Kalintepe Mountain for a breathtaking view of the city.

Best Things to Do in Sivas: Activities and Tours

  • Hot Air Balloon Ride: Enjoy the pristine and unspoiled beauty of Sivas in a hot air balloon. See the city from a unique perspective and enjoy magnificent sightseeing.

  • Horse and Camel Riding: Explore the beautiful views in and around the city on the back of a horse or camel.

  • Rafting: Experience the thrill of white water rafting in Sivas, surrounded by beautiful scenery and rushing water.

Week-Long Itinerary for Sivas

Day 1: Explore Sivas city center, visiting museums and historical places.

Day 2: Trekking to Kösedağ National Park, which is located 65 km from the city.

Day 3: Check out the Zara Pınarbaşı thermal baths for rest and relaxation.

Day 4: Visit historical sites on the outskirts of the city, including Gök Medrese and the Köprülü Mehmet Pasha Mosque.

Day 5: Go on a hot air balloon ride over the city for an incredible view.

Day 6: Enjoy horse-riding in Kızılırmak Delta National Park, located 75 km from the city.

Day 7: Take a rest and enjoy local restaurants and markets while checking out souvenirs to bring home.

Food and Drink Scene of Sivas

Must-Try Dishes

  • Kesme Çorbası: A hearty soup made with diced lamb, herbs, and onions.

  • Mumbar Dolması: Stuffed sheep intestines, prepared with rice, onions, and spices.

  • Bulgur Pilavı: Coarse bulgur cooked with lamb or chicken broth.

  • Testi Kebabı: A meat and vegetable dish cooked in a clay pot, known for its unique taste and presentation.

Local Beverages

  • Ayran: A yogurt drink commonly consumed in Turkey.

  • Boza: A traditional Turkish fermented drink with a sweet-sour taste, made with millet or wheat.

Where to Stay in Sivas: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Sivas Kaya Hotel: A beautiful and traditional hotel located in the city center, with excellent reviews and affordable rates.

  • Sivas Wellborn Hotel: A modern and chic hotel with high-end facilities and a beautiful spa center.

  • Madi Hotel Sivas: An affordable and comfortable hotel, great for backpackers and budget travelers.

What to Buy in Sivas: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Melikgazi Kilim: A beautiful type of rug woven by hand in Sivas.

  • Coppersmith Art: Sivas has a rich history of coppersmithing, so you can find beautiful copper pots, pans, decorative ornaments, and other products.

  • Handmade Knives: Sivas has a culture of knife-making, so you can find incredible handmade knives here.

Local Culture and Customs of Sivas

Sivas has a strong cultural heritage with distinctive customs and traditions. The residents of Sivas are known for their hospitality, religious affiliation, and conservative lifestyle. Visitors should dress modestly and respectfully when visiting mosques and conservative areas.

Best Time to Visit Sivas

The best time to visit Sivas is during the late spring to early autumn seasons (April to October). The weather during this time is mild, and there is plenty of sunshine. Winters in Sivas can be incredibly harsh, and snow tends to be heavy, making outdoor activities difficult.

Do’s and Don’ts of Sivas

Do:

  • Respect local customs and traditions.

  • Wear modest clothing when visiting Mosques and Muslim areas.

  • Learn a few Turkish phrases to communicate with the locals.

Don’t:

  • Drink alcohol in public areas, as it is forbidden in the Muslim religion.

  • Get angry, show aggression, or lose your temper with locals, as it is considered hugely disrespectful.

  • Disrespect holy sites and religious practices.

Safety and Health

Safety in Sivas

Sivas is a safe city, and crime rates are low. However, visitors should still exercise caution and practice common sense when traveling to less populated or obscure areas. Also, it is advisable to avoid traveling outside during the night time.

Health in Sivas

Sivas is a city with modern health facilities and well-equipped hospitals. However, visitors should still take precautions, such as drinking bottled water and being conscious of personal hygiene.

Getting to Sivas: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Sivas

  • Sivas Nuri Demirag Airport: Located around 20km from the city, it is the principal airport in the area.

Bus Stations Near Sivas

  • Sivas Intercity Bus Station: Located in the city center and offers bus connections to all major cities in Turkey.

Other Options

  • Renting a Car: Renting a car is an excellent option for those who want to explore the city on their own, and several car rental businesses services in the city.

How to Get Around in Sivas

Sivas has a range of transportation options, including buses, taxis, and car rentals. There is also a well-organized public transport system, with buses running throughout the city.

Where to Go Next from Sivas: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Amasya: A beautiful city located 200 km to the north of Sivas with a rich history and picturesque Ottoman buildings.

  • Kayseri: A city located 200 km to the south of Sivas and considered the heart of Turkey, Kayseri has attractions like Erciyes Mountain and key historical buildings.

  • Çorum: A city located 180 km to the west of Sivas famous for its cuisine, beauty, and rich history.

Conclusion

Sivas is a city with a rich cultural heritage, beautiful scenery, and well-preserved Ottoman architecture. It is an excellent destination for those who want to explore Turkey’s interior and immerse themselves in Turkish culture. With plenty of activities, sights, and natural beauty, Sivas promises a unique experience for all its visitors.
