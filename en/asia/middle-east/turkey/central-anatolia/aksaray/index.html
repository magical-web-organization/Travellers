Introduction to Aksaray

Aksaray, located in the Central Anatolia region of Turkey, is a city that has been inhabited since ancient times. It has a rich history with many historical sites and natural wonders. The city is also a popular stopover for those who are travelling between Istanbul and Cappadocia. Aksaray is a very hospitable and welcoming city, where visitors can witness the local customs and traditions of the people. In this comprehensive travel guide, we’ll cover everything you need to know to plan a trip to Aksaray.

List of Places to Visit in Aksaray

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Aksaray Museum: The museum has a collection of artifacts from the Roman, Byzantine, and Ottoman periods.
  • Ulu Mosque: Built in the Seljuk era, Ulu Mosque has a unique wooden roof and interior decorations.
  • Sarihan Caravanserai: Sarihan Caravanserai was built in the 13th century and served as a stopover for traders during the Seljuk period.

Natural Attractions of Aksaray: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Ihlara Valley: Ihlara Valley is a natural wonder of Aksaray, where you can hike and explore the natural beauty of the canyon.
  • Salt Lake: Salt Lake is a vast saltwater lake with a unique ecosystem and a popular spot for bird watching.
  • Fantasy Valley: Fantasy Valley is a surreal landscape that is shaped by the erosion of volcanic rock formations.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Aksaray

  • Ihlara Valley Hiking Trail: A 14-kilometer trek through the Ihlara Valley, where you can discover hidden cave churches and experience the natural beauty of the valley.
  • Kiliclar Valley Hiking Trail: A 5-kilometer hiking trail through the Kiliclar Valley, which takes you to the top of the valley for a panoramic view of the landscape.
  • Sultan Sazlığı Nature Trail: A one-kilometer boardwalk trail through the reeds and marshes of the Sultan Sazlığı Nature Reserve.

Best Things to Do in Aksaray: Activities and Tours

  • Hot Air Balloon Ride: A hot air balloon ride is a must-do activity in Aksaray, especially for visitors who want to witness the otherworldly beauty of Cappadocia from above.
  • Turkish Bath: A traditional Turkish bath or “hammam” is a great way to relax and unwind after a day of hiking and exploring.
  • Cappadocia Tours: Aksaray is a great starting point to explore the beautiful landscape of Cappadocia. There are many tours and packages available for visitors to explore the area.

Week-Long Itinerary for Aksaray

Day 1: Arrive in Aksaray and explore the local markets and bazaars.

Day 2: Visit the Aksaray Museum and Ulu Mosque.

Day 3: Explore the Ihlara Valley and hike the Ihlara Valley Hiking Trail.

Day 4: Visit Sarihan Caravanserai and Salt Lake.

Day 5: Take a hot air balloon ride over Cappadocia and visit the underground cities.

Day 6: Experience a traditional Turkish bath and visit the Fantasy Valley.

Day 7: Depart from Aksaray and head to your next destination.

Food and Drink Scene of Aksaray

Must-Try Dishes

  • Aksaray Tandir: Oven-roasted lamb or beef that is marinated with local spices and served with Turkish bread.
  • Manti: Turkish dumplings that are usually stuffed with seasoned ground beef or lamb and served with yogurt and tomato sauce.
  • Katmer: A pastry made with layers of dough, butter, and sugar.

Local Beverages

  • Ayran: A refreshing yogurt-based drink that is served chilled.
  • Turkish Tea: A popular hot tea that is usually served with tiny cups in local cafés.
  • Turkish Coffee: A strong and sweet coffee that is brewed with fine coffee grounds and served in small cups.

Where to Stay in Aksaray: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Grand Altuntas Hotel: A four-star hotel that is located in the city center and offers modern amenities.
  • Bareve Hotel: A budget-friendly hotel that is located near the local bazaar and offers clean and comfortable accommodation.
  • Sude Konak Hotel: A traditional guesthouse that is located in a restored Ottoman mansion and offers a unique and authentic experience.

What to Buy in Aksaray: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Kilim: A type of Turkish carpet that is handwoven with natural fibers and dyes.
  • Ceramic Plates and Pottery: Aksaray is well-known for its ceramic plates and pottery that are decorated with traditional motifs and patterns.
  • Spices and Herbs: Aksaray is a great place to buy locally produced spices and herbs that are used in Turkish cuisine.

Local Culture and Customs of Aksaray

The people of Aksaray are known for their hospitality and warm welcomes to visitors. It is important to dress modestly and respect the local customs and traditions. When visiting mosques or other religious sites, shoes should be removed before entering.

Best Time to Visit Aksaray

The best time to visit Aksaray is from April to June or September to November. These months offer milder temperatures and less crowded tourist destinations. Summers can be very hot, and winters can be cold with occasional snow.

Do’s and Don’ts of Aksaray

Do:

  • Dress modestly and respect the local customs and traditions.
  • Learn a few Turkish phrases and words to interact with locals.
  • Explore the natural wonders of Aksaray and surrounding areas.

Don’t:

  • Take pictures of people without their permission.
  • Drink tap water, always drink bottled water instead.
  • Leave garbage or litter in the natural areas.

Safety and Health

Safety in Aksaray

Aksaray is a relatively safe city, but like any other city, travelers should be aware of their surroundings and take basic safety precautions. It is recommended to avoid walking alone in quiet or poorly lit areas at night.

Health in Aksaray

Visitors do not need any vaccinations to visit Aksaray. The city has a few hospitals and clinics that provide basic healthcare services. Visitors should pack basic medications with them and purchase travel insurance before arriving.

Getting to Aksaray: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Aksaray

The nearest airport to Aksaray is the Nevsehir Kapadokya Airport, which is approximately 85 kilometers away. There are daily flights to and from Istanbul and other major cities in Turkey.

Bus Stations Near Aksaray

Aksaray has two central bus stations, both of which offer regular services to major cities in Turkey, including Istanbul, Ankara, and Kayseri.

Other Options

Visitors can also arrive in Aksaray by renting a car or a private transfer from major cities in Turkey.

How to Get Around in Aksaray

The most convenient and cost-effective way of getting around in Aksaray is by using public transportation, such as buses and minibuses. Taxis are also available but can be more expensive. Visitors can rent a car to explore the surrounding areas of Aksaray.

Where to Go Next from Aksaray: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Cappadocia: One of Turkey’s most popular tourist destinations, Cappadocia is known for its unique landscape, underground cities, and hot air balloon rides.
  • Konya: The birthplace of the Sufi philosopher Rumi, Konya is a city rich in Turkish-Islamic history and culture.
  • Nigde: Nigde is a charming city that is often overlooked by tourists, but it is a great place to explore the local culture and taste delicious Turkish cuisine.

Conclusion

Aksaray is a city that offers a unique blend of history, nature, and Turkish culture. With a variety of places to visit, things to do, and local products to buy, visitors can immerse themselves in an authentic and unforgettable Turkish experience.
