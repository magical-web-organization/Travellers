Introduction to Çatalhöyük

Nestled in the heart of Central Anatolia, Çatalhöyük is a breathtaking ancient site that is considered one of the most significant archaeological discoveries of the past century. Composed of two adjacent mounds, it is believed to have been inhabited continuously for over 2,000 years, making it one of the oldest and most well-preserved Neolithic settlements in the world. Today, visitors to Çatalhöyük can explore the remnants of this fascinating civilization and immerse themselves in the local culture of modern-day Turkey.

Local Culture and Customs of Çatalhöyük

To fully immerse yourself in the local culture, it is important to be aware of customs and traditions. Some key things to know include:

  • It is customary to remove shoes before entering a mosque or someone’s home.
  • Turkish hospitality is renowned, and it is common to be offered tea, coffee, or a meal when visiting someone’s home.
  • Clothing should be modest, particularly when visiting religious sites.

Best Time to Visit Çatalhöyük

The best time to visit Çatalhöyük is in the spring or fall when temperatures are mild and the crowds are thinner. Summers can be hot and crowded, while winters can be cold and snowy.
