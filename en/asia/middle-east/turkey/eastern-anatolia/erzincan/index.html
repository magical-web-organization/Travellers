Introduction to Erzincan

Erzincan is a city located in Eastern Anatolia, Turkey. It is situated in the middle of the country, close to Armenia and Georgia, making it a great stop-over destination for travelers exploring the region. Erzincan offers a variety of cultural and natural attractions, as well as outdoor activities, making it a perfect destination for adventure seekers and culture enthusiasts alike.

List of Places to Visit in Erzincan

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Erzincan has a rich history dating back to the Hellenistic period, and it is home to many historical and cultural sites. The city’s most famous attraction is the Erzincan Castle, which served as a military post for centuries. Another popular site is the Çobandede Bridge, which dates back to the 13th century and is a great example of medieval Seljuk architecture. If you’re interested in history, don’t miss out on the Erzincan Museum, which showcases the region’s cultural heritage through a collection of artifacts from ancient times to the present.

Natural Attractions of Erzincan: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Erzincan is known for its natural beauty, boasting stunning landscapes and outdoor adventures. Take a hike to the Munzur Valley National Park, where you can explore the Munzur Mountains and enjoy breathtaking views of the area’s flora and fauna. The Karanlık Canyon, located north of Erzincan, is another must-visit attraction, with its unique geological formations and crystal-clear water that invites visitors for a refreshing swim.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Erzincan

Erzincan is the perfect destination for hiking and biking enthusiasts. The Munzur Valley National Park, with its magnificent mountains and crystal-clear waters, offers many trails for both beginners and experienced hikers. The Cimera Lake is another great destination for hiking, with a 10 km trail that circles the lake and boasts spectacular views of the surrounding area.

Best Things to Do in Erzincan: Activities and Tours

Erzincan offers a variety of activities and tours for visitors of all ages. If you’re looking for a unique experience, take a hot air balloon ride over the Munzur Mountains and enjoy panoramic views of the region. For those interested in history and culture, take a guided tour of the city’s historical sites, such as the Erzincan Castle and the Çobandede Bridge. Outdoor adventure seekers can try their hand at river-rafting in the impressive Karasu River.

Week-Long Itinerary for Erzincan

Day 1: Explore Erzincan Castle and Çobandede Bridge Day 2: Take a tour of the Erzincan Museum and learn about the region’s cultural heritage Day 3: Hike to the Munzur Valley National Park and enjoy the stunning landscapes Day 4: Go river-rafting in the Karasu River Day 5: Take a hot air balloon ride to see the Munzur Mountains from above Day 6: Visit the Cimera Lake and hike around the lake Day 7: Relax and enjoy local food and beverages in Erzincan’s restaurants and bars

Food and Drink Scene of Erzincan

Must-Try Dishes

Erzincan is known for its delicious cuisine, which is heavily influenced by the region’s Kurdish and Armenian heritage. Don’t miss out on trying the local specialty, ‘kemikli pursaka’, a soup made with meat and vegetables. ‘Kebab’ is another popular dish, with many varieties to choose from, such as lamb or chicken skewers. For a sweet treat, try ‘kadayif’, a pastry made with shredded phyllo dough, sugar syrup, and pistachio.

Local Beverages

Erzincan has a rich tea and coffee culture and is known for its potent ‘keyif’ tea, served in small glasses accompanied by traditional sweet treats. ‘Bozaba’, a non-alcoholic drink made from cornelian cherries, is also popular among locals and visitors alike.

Where to Stay in Erzincan: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Erzincan offers a range of accommodation options, from budget-friendly hostels to luxury hotels. For a charming and authentic experience, stay in one of the city’s guesthouses and enjoy a more personal touch. For those looking for a luxury stay, the Ramada Plaza by Wyndham is a great option, while the Sim-Er Hotel is ideal for budget travelers.

What to Buy in Erzincan: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Erzincan is known for its handcrafted copper goods and traditional textiles, such as kilims, carpets, and rugs. Don’t miss out on buying some of the region’s renowned honey, which is produced using traditional methods and is considered one of the best in the country.

Local Culture and Customs of Erzincan

Erzincan is a multicultural city, with a rich history and heritage that is reflected in its customs and traditions. It is considered polite to remove your shoes when entering someone’s home, and it is customary to bring a small gift for your host. Traditional folk dance and music are an important part of the local culture, and you may have the chance to witness some performances during your visit.

Best Time to Visit Erzincan

The best time to visit Erzincan is during the summer months, from June to September when the weather is warm and sunny. During this time, you can take advantage of the many outdoor activities and enjoy the stunning landscape to the fullest.

Do’s and Don’ts of Erzincan

Do:

  • Respect local customs and traditions
  • Try the local cuisine and beverages
  • Take advantage of the outdoor activities and stunning landscape
  • Bring appropriate clothing for the weather and outdoor adventures

Don’t:

  • Wear revealing clothing in public
  • Take photos of people without their permission
  • Disturb public order or litter the natural environment

Safety and Health

Safety

Erzincan is generally a safe city, but visitors are advised to take precautions and be aware of their surroundings, especially at night. It is recommended to keep your valuables safe and avoid carrying large sums of cash on you.

Health

Visitors are advised to drink bottled water and avoid consuming tap water to prevent any potential health risks. It is also recommended to bring any necessary medication and consult with a medical professional before traveling to Erzincan.

Getting to Erzincan: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Erzincan

The nearest airport to Erzincan is the Erzincan Airport, which is located only 8 km from the city center. The airport serves both domestic and international flights.

Bus Stations Near Erzincan

Erzincan is well-connected to other major cities in Turkey via bus. The city has two bus stations, the Erzincan İlçe Otogarı and the Erzincan Şehirlerarası Otogarı, both of which offer daily services to destinations across the country.

Other Options

For those interested in a scenic journey, the Eastern Express Train runs from Ankara to Kars and stops at Erzincan. The journey offers breathtaking views of the countryside and is an unforgettable experience.

How to Get Around in Erzincan

Erzincan is a relatively small city, and most attractions are within walking distance. Local transport options include taxis and public buses, both of which are cheap and convenient. For those interested in renting a car, several rental agencies are available in the city center.

Where to Go Next from Erzincan: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Erzincan is a great starting point for exploring other destinations in Eastern Turkey, such as Kars or Van. The nearby Oltu district, famous for its black stone jewelry, is also worth a visit.

Conclusion

Erzincan offers a unique blend of history, culture, and outdoor adventure, making it a must-visit destination for any traveler. With its stunning natural landscapes, rich cultural heritage, and delicious cuisine, Erzincan is sure to leave a lasting impression on visitors.
