Introduction to Eastern Anatolia

Eastern Anatolia is a captivating region in Turkey that boasts natural beauty, rich cultural heritage, and warm hospitality. It is located in the eastern part of Turkey and bordered by Armenia, Azerbaijan, Georgia, Iran, and Syria. The region is a perfect destination for travelers who are looking for a unique experience and want to get off the beaten path.

Food and Drink Scene of Eastern Anatolia

The cuisine of Eastern Anatolia is a fusion of Turkish, Armenian, Kurdish, and Persian influences. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  1. Kebabs and grilled meats
  2. Pide (Turkish pizza)
  3. Mantı (Turkish ravioli)
  4. Lahmacun (Turkish flatbread)
  5. Şiş köfte (Spicy meatballs)

Local Beverages

  1. Ayran (Yoghurt drink)
  2. Boza (Fermented drink made from maize)
  3. Serbet (Traditional Ottoman drink)
  4. Raki (Anise-flavored alcoholic drink)
  5. Turkish tea

Local Culture and Customs of Eastern Anatolia

Eastern Anatolia is a culturally significant region with a diverse population. Here are some customs and traditions to keep in mind while visiting:

  1. Dress modestly while visiting mosques and historical places
  2. Remove shoes before entering a mosque
  3. Respect local customs and traditions
  4. Leave tips if you are satisfied with the service
  5. Generally, the people are conservative, so avoid public display of affection

Best Time to Visit Eastern Anatolia

The best time to visit Eastern Anatolia is from May to October when the weather is dry and pleasant. In winter, the region experiences heavy snowfall, making it suitable for winter sports enthusiasts.

Do’s and Don’ts of Eastern Anatolia

Here are some Do’s and Don’ts when traveling to Eastern Anatolia:

Do:

  1. Respect the local culture and customs
  2. Try the local cuisine
  3. Learn some Turkish words and phrases
  4. Take off shoes before entering a mosque or a house
  5. Bargain at the markets and bazaars

Don’t:

  1. Do not take pictures of people unless you ask for permission
  2. Do not use your left hand while eating or greeting
  3. Do not make negative comments about Turkish politics or religion
  4. Do not use offensive language
  5. Do not leave a mosque or religious gathering early

Safety and Health

Safety in Eastern Anatolia

Eastern Anatolia is a generally safe region of Turkey. However, travelers should take the usual precautions such as not carrying large amounts of cash, not walking alone at night, and keeping an eye on their belongings at all times.

Health in Eastern Anatolia

Water in the region is usually safe to drink if boiled or treated. However, it is advisable to stick to bottled water. Travelers should also be up to date on routine vaccinations and consider getting Hepatitis A and B vaccinations before visiting.

How to Get Around in Eastern Anatolia

The best way to explore Eastern Anatolia is by renting a car or hiring a driver as public transportation can be limited. Private day tours with a local guide are also an option.
