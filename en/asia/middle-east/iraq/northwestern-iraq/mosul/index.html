Introduction to Mosul

Mosul is a city in northwestern Iraq that is known for its rich history, archeological sites, and natural beauty. The city is located in the Nineveh Governorate on the west bank of the Tigris River, and is the third-largest city in Iraq. Mosul has a diverse population, with Arabs, Kurds, Assyrians, and Turkmen living together in harmony.

List of Places to Visit in Mosul

Mosul has a variety of attractions to offer to visitors, including:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Mosul Museum: This museum features the history and culture of the ancient city of Nineveh and its surrounding areas. The museum is home to a collection of artifacts dating back to the Assyrian and Babylonian periods.

  • Al-Nabi Yunus Mosque: This mosque is located on the east bank of the Tigris River and is named after the prophet Jonah.

  • Al Tahira Church: This church is a Syriac Catholic church that dates back to the 16th century. It features stunning architecture and is a unique example of the religious diversity of Mosul.

Natural Attractions of Mosul: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Makhoul Mountains: Located about 60 kilometers north of Mosul, these mountains offer breathtaking scenery and some of the best hiking trails in the region.

  • Al-Shallalat Park: This park is located on the east bank of the Tigris River and is a perfect spot for picnics and relaxation.

  • Mosul Dam: The Mosul Dam is one of the largest dams in the Middle East and provides irrigation and electricity to the region.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Mosul

Mosul has some of the best hiking and biking routes in Iraq. Some of the most popular routes include:

  • Makhoul Mountain Hike: This hike takes you through the stunning Makhoul Mountains and provides amazing views of the surrounding landscape.

  • Tigris River Biking: The Tigris River runs through Mosul and offers a scenic route for biking.

Best Things to Do in Mosul: Activities and Tours

  • Archeological Tours: You can take a tour of the ancient city of Nineveh and explore its historical sites.

  • Food Tours: Mosul has a vibrant food scene, and you can take a food tour to sample some of its delicious dishes.

  • Photography Tours: The city of Mosul is filled with stunning views and archeological sites, making it a perfect destination for photography enthusiasts.

Week-Long Itinerary for Mosul

Day 1: Visit the Mosul Museum and Al-Nabi Yunus Mosque. Day 2: Take a hike in the Makhoul Mountains. Day 3: Explore Nineveh and its archeological sites. Day 4: Take a food tour of Mosul and sample its delicious dishes. Day 5: Visit the Mosul Dam and enjoy the scenic views. Day 6: Take a photography tour of Mosul. Day 7: Relax in Al-Shallalat Park.

Food and Drink Scene of Mosul

Must-Try Dishes

  • Kubba Mosul: A traditional dish made of meat, rice, and spices, and is a popular snack in the city.

  • Masgouf: A roasted fish dish that is a popular meal in Mosul.

  • Pacha: A dish made of sheep’s head and feet and is a popular winter dish in Mosul.

Local Beverages

  • Iraqi Tea: A black tea, flavored with cardamom, that is a perfect way to start your day in Mosul.

  • Sharab al-Rumman: A delicious pomegranate juice that is a popular drink in the city.

Where to Stay in Mosul: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Ninawa Hotel: This hotel is located in the heart of the city and offers modern amenities and comfortable rooms.

  • Al-Farouk Hotel: Situated on the east bank of the Tigris River, this hotel offers stunning views of the city and the river.

  • Ezzideen Guesthouse: This guesthouse is located in the historic district of Mosul and offers comfortable rooms with traditional decorations.

What to Buy in Mosul: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Mesopotamian Artifacts: Mosul is known for its rich history and archeological sites, and you can find various artifacts that make perfect souvenirs.

  • Traditional Clothing: Mosul has a unique textile industry, and you can find traditional clothing that makes for an authentic souvenir.

Local Culture and Customs of Mosul

Mosul is a city of diverse cultures and communities, and visitors should respect local customs and traditions. Proper dress code and conservative behavior are expected when visiting religious sites.

Best Time to Visit Mosul

The best time to visit Mosul is from March to May and from September to November when the weather is mild and pleasant.

Do’s and Don’ts of Mosul

Do:

  • Respect local customs and traditions.
  • Dress modestly when visiting religious sites.
  • Try the delicious food and drink.

Don’t:

  • Take pictures of locals without their permission.
  • Criticize Iraqi politics and culture.

Safety and Health

Safety in Mosul

It is recommended that visitors avoid traveling to Mosul due to the ongoing security situation.

Health in Mosul

Visitors should ensure that they have all necessary vaccinations before traveling to Mosul.

Getting to Mosul: Transportation Options and Logistics

Bus Stations Near Mosul

Other Options

  • Taxis and private cars can be hired from Baghdad to Mosul.

How to Get Around in Mosul

Taxis and buses are the most common modes of transportation in Mosul.

Where to Go Next from Mosul: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Duhok: Located about 100 kilometers north of Mosul, this city is known for its historic sites and scenic landscapes.

  • Erbil: Located about 80 kilometers east of Mosul, Erbil is the capital of the Kurdistan Region and is known for its ancient Citadel and vibrant bazaar.

Conclusion

Mosul is a city with a rich history, archeological sites, and natural beauty. Visitors can enjoy a variety of activities such as hiking, biking, and food tours. While safety is a concern, the city offers a unique cultural experience that is worth exploring for those brave enough to venture into the region.
