Alqosh, located in the northern part of Iraqi Kurdistan, is a small town that is renowned for its historical importance, scenic beauty, and cultural significance. This ancient town has a unique blend of Kurdish, Assyrian, and Arabic culture, and offers visitors a chance to explore its fascinating history, breathtaking landscapes, and lively local culture.

This comprehensive travel guide will provide you with all the essential information you need to plan your trip to Alqosh, including a list of must-see attractions, where to stay, what to eat, and how to get around.

List of Places to Visit in Alqosh

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Alqosh has a rich history and a deep cultural heritage, with several significant landmarks and attractions that attest to its past. Some of the top cultural sites to visit in Alqosh include:

  • Alqosh Monastery: Also known as the Rabban Hormizd Monastery, this ancient monastery is one of the most significant historical sites in Alqosh. It was founded in the 7th century and is located on top of a granite hill overlooking the town. The monastery includes a museum that houses a large collection of religious artifacts, manuscripts, and sculptures.
  • The Tomb of Nahum: The burial site of Nahum the prophet, whose prophecy is recorded in the Old Testament book of Nahum. The tomb is located in a small cave on the outskirts of Alqosh and is considered a significant pilgrimage site.
  • Alqosh Museum: A small museum that showcases the local history and culture through exhibits of traditional costumes, crafts, and folk art.
  • Mar Yohanna Church: A beautiful ancient church built in the 15th century, adorned with stunning frescoes and carvings.

Natural Attractions of Alqosh: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Apart from its significant cultural landmarks, Alqosh is also known for its natural beauty and stunning landscapes. The town is surrounded by rolling hills, vast plains, and beautiful valleys, offering visitors a chance to unwind and connect with nature. Some of the top natural attractions to visit in Alqosh include:

  • Hazar Merd Cave: One of the most beautiful natural wonders in Alqosh, the cave is located about 10 kilometers from the town center and is renowned for its stunning stalactites and stalagmites.
  • Mount Maqloub: The highest peak in the area, with an elevation of around 3,000 meters, offers breathtaking views of the town and the surrounding landscape.
  • Alqosh Dam: A beautiful reservoir located near the town, which provides a scenic spot for picnics and outdoor recreation.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Alqosh

For outdoor enthusiasts, Alqosh offers excellent hiking and biking routes that can be enjoyed throughout the year. Some of the popular routes include:

  • Mount Maqloub Trail: This trail starts from the town center and takes you to the summit of Mount Maqloub, offering stunning views of the surrounding landscape.
  • Ain Sifni Trail: A beautiful hiking trail that takes you through the Ain Sifni valley, offering stunning views of the mountains and the valley.
  • Alqosh Black Road: A scenic mountain bike trail that starts from the town and takes you through the scenic countryside of Alqosh.

Best Things to Do in Alqosh: Activities and Tours

Apart from hiking, biking, and sightseeing, Alqosh offers several other exciting activities and tours for visitors. Some of the must-try experiences include:

  • Cultural Tours: Guided tours of the Alqosh Monastery, the Tomb of Nahum, the Alqosh Museum, and other cultural sites.
  • Culinary Tours: A food tour of Alqosh, where you get to taste various local dishes and specialties, including dolma, kebab, and kibbeh.
  • Handcrafts Workshops: Attend workshops to learn about traditional Kurdish, Assyrian, and Arabic handicrafts like weaving, embroidery, and pottery-making.

For visitors who have a week to spare, Alqosh offers a comprehensive itinerary that covers all the top attractions and activities in the town.

Food and Drink Scene of Alqosh

Kurdish cuisine is known for its mouth-watering dishes and unique spices, and Alqosh is no exception. The town offers visitors a chance to taste the delicious local cuisine, with several restaurants and cafes that serve traditional Kurdish, Arab, and Assyrian dishes. Some of the must-try dishes include:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Dolma: Stuffed grape leaves filled with rice, herbs, and minced meat.
  • Kebab: Skewered meat cooked over fire, usually served with rice and vegetables.
  • Kibbeh: A meat-stuffed bulgur shell that is deep-fried or baked.

Local Beverages

  • Ayran: A traditional yogurt-based beverage that is refreshing and healthy.
  • Kurdish Tea: A strong, sweet black tea that is served in small glasses and is a part of the Kurdish hospitality tradition.

Where to Stay in Alqosh: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Alqosh has several hotels, guesthouses, and hostels that cater to different budgets and preferences. Some of the top options include:

  • The Hideout Hostel: Located in the town center, this hostel offers clean and comfortable rooms and a friendly atmosphere.
  • The Chaldean Cultural Center Guesthouse: A guesthouse located in the heart of Alqosh, offering beautiful views of the town and the surrounding landscape.
  • The Grand Millennium Hotel: A luxurious hotel located on the outskirts of Alqosh, offering stunning views of the mountains and the valley.

What to Buy in Alqosh: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Alqosh is renowned for its traditional handicrafts, which include handwoven rugs, embroidery, pottery, and woodwork. Some of the top souvenirs to buy in Alqosh include:

  • A Handwoven Rug: Traditional Kurdish rugs are known for their intricate designs and stunning colors.
  • A Handmade Pottery: Alqosh has a long history of pottery making, and you can buy beautiful pots, plates, and bowls from the local markets.
  • A Wooden Handicraft: You can buy unique wood carvings, furniture, and toys made by local artisans in Alqosh.

Local Culture and Customs of Alqosh

Alqosh has a unique blend of Kurdish, Assyrian, and Arabic culture, and visitors are advised to respect local customs and traditions. Some of the customs to keep in mind include:

  • Dress modestly when visiting religious sites and conservative areas.
  • Remove shoes when entering a mosque or a private house.
  • Always ask for permission before taking photos of people.

Best Time to Visit Alqosh

The best time to visit Alqosh is during the spring (March to May) and autumn (September to November) seasons, when the weather is mild and pleasant, and the valleys and hills are lush green.

Do’s and Don’ts of Alqosh

Do:

  • Try the local cuisine.
  • Engage with the friendly locals.
  • Respect the local customs and traditions.

Don’t:

  • Drink alcohol in public places.
  • Wear revealing clothing in conservative areas.
  • Touch or remove ancient artifacts and relics from historical sites.

Safety and Health

Safety in Alqosh

Alqosh is generally a safe town, with friendly locals and little crime. However, visitors are advised to take basic precautions to stay safe, such as:

  • Avoid traveling alone at night.
  • Keep valuables in a secure place.
  • Respect cultural differences.

Health in Alqosh

Alqosh has a basic healthcare system, with several clinics and hospitals located in the town and nearby cities. However, it is recommended to have travel insurance and consult your doctor before traveling to Alqosh.

Getting to Alqosh: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Alqosh

The nearest airport to Alqosh is the Erbil International Airport, which is around 80 kilometers away from the town center. Taxis and shared minivans are available from the airport to Alqosh.

Bus Stations Near Alqosh

The main bus station in Alqosh is located in the town center, and several buses are available from nearby cities and towns, including Erbil, Mosul, and Dohuk.

Other Options

Several private car rental companies and tour operators offer transportation services and guided tours to Alqosh from nearby cities and tourist attractions.

How to Get Around in Alqosh

Alqosh is a small town, and most of the tourist attractions are located within walking distance from the town center. However, taxis and shared minivans are available for visitors who want to explore the surrounding areas.

Where to Go Next from Alqosh: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Alqosh is located in a beautiful region of Iraqi Kurdistan, and several nearby destinations offer excellent opportunities for tourism and exploration. Some of the top destinations to visit from Alqosh include:

  • Erbil: The vibrant capital of Iraqi Kurdistan, with several historical and cultural landmarks and attractions.
  • Dohuk: A beautiful city located near the Turkish border, with several natural and cultural sites to visit.
  • Lalish: A holy town located in the foothills of the Sinjar Mountains, which is the spiritual center of the Yezidi religion.

Conclusion
