Introduction to Zakho

Located in the Dohuk Governorate of Iraqi Kurdistan, Zakho is a historical city with a rich cultural and natural heritage. This vibrant city is a popular tourist destination for its scenic landscapes, cultural landmarks, and warm hospitality. It is easily accessible from a number of airports and bus stations, making it an ideal destination for travelers to explore. In this comprehensive guide, we will introduce you to the top places to visit, things to do, where to stay, what to eat, and more in Zakho.

List of Places to Visit in Zakho

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Zakho is a city steeped in history with a rich cultural heritage. There are several important cultural landmarks that visitors should not miss, including:

  • Zakho Citadel: This ancient fortress dates back to the 7th century and was once an important strategic location for trade and commerce. Today, it is a popular tourist attraction with stunning views of the city and the surrounding areas.
  • Zakho Bridge: This historical bridge is located in the heart of the city and has been standing for over 500 years. It is a popular spot for locals and tourists alike to relax and take in the local scenery.
  • Zakho Museum: The museum is located in the city center and provides a glimpse into the history and culture of the area. It houses artifacts and exhibits that span thousands of years, including artifacts from the Assyrian and Babylonian empires.

Natural Attractions of Zakho: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Zakho boasts some of the most breathtaking scenic views in the region. The region is known for its stunning mountain ranges, crystal-clear rivers, and verdant valleys. Here are some of the top places to visit for outdoor adventures and stunning views:

  • Geli Ali Bag: Gali Ali Bag is a picturesque valley located on the outskirts of Zakho. Tourists can enjoy scenic views of the valley, swim in the river, and hike in the surrounding mountains.
  • Chami Rizan: Chami Rizan is a popular park located on the outskirts of the city. It offers visitors a variety of activities such as hiking, picnicking, and horseback riding. The park is known for its stunning views of the surrounding mountains and valleys.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Zakho

For adventure seekers, Zakho offers several hiking and biking trails that offer breathtaking views of the mountains and valleys. Here are some of the top trails that visitors should explore:

  • Geli Ali Bag Trail: This trail runs through the Gali Ali Bag valley and is known for its stunning views of the surrounding mountains and valleys. It is a moderate-level trail that takes around 2-3 hours to complete.
  • Chami Rizan Trail: This trail runs through the Chami Rizan Park and is perfect for beginner-level hikers. It offers stunning views of the park and the surrounding mountains and takes around 1-2 hours to complete.

Best Things to Do in Zakho: Activities and Tours

If you’re looking for unique experiences and activities in Zakho, here are some of the top things to do:

  • Visit the Bazaar: The Zakho bazaar is a must-visit for anyone looking to explore the culture and history of the city. It is a bustling market filled with vendors selling a variety of goods such as traditional textiles, spices, and handcrafted souvenirs.
  • Visit the Waterfall: The Anish Waterfall is located in the heart of Zakho and is a popular spot for tourists and locals alike. The waterfall offers stunning views and a refreshing escape from the city heat.
  • Take a Guided Tour: Guided tours are a great way to explore the city’s rich history and culture. There are several tour operators in the city that offer guided tours of the city’s landmarks and cultural highlights.

Food and Drink Scene of Zakho

Zakho is known for its delicious cuisine and diverse food scene. Here are some of the must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Kebab: The kebab is a staple meat dish in the region, cooked over an open flame and served with rice and a variety of spices.
  • Zerevani: Zerevani is a traditional vegetarian dish made with lentils, rice, and a variety of spices.

Local Beverages

  • Tea: Tea is the most popular beverage in Zakho, served hot and sweetened with sugar.
  • Sharbat: Sharbat is a refreshing drink made with fruit juices and sugar, perfect for cooling off on a hot day.

Where to Stay in Zakho: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Zakho has several options for accommodations, ranging from budget-friendly hostels to high-end hotels. Here are some of the top picks:

  • Sira Hotel and Suites: This luxurious hotel is located in the heart of Zakho and offers stunning views of the city and surrounding mountains.
  • Sunset Guesthouse: Sunset Guesthouse is a budget-friendly option located on the outskirts of the city. It offers comfortable rooms and a relaxed atmosphere.

What to Buy in Zakho: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Zakho is known for its traditional handicrafts and unique souvenirs. Here are some of the best gifts to bring home from your trip:

  • Traditional Textiles: Zakho is known for its beautiful handwoven textiles, including kilims, rugs, and carpets.
  • Spices: The bazaars of Zakho are filled with a variety of aromatic spices, perfect for cooking delicious dishes at home.
  • Handmade Pottery: Handmade pottery is a popular souvenir in Zakho, with a variety of unique designs and styles available.

Local Culture and Customs of Zakho

Zakho is a conservative city, and visitors are expected to dress modestly and respect local customs and traditions. Here are some of the local customs and traditions to keep in mind:

  • Dress Code: Women are expected to dress modestly, covering their heads and arms, especially when visiting mosques and other religious sites.
  • Greeting Customs: It is customary to greet people warmly with a handshake and a smile.
  • Manners: Visitors are expected to show respect and courtesy towards locals, avoiding rude or offensive behavior.

Best Time to Visit Zakho

The best time to visit Zakho is from April to October when the weather is warm and dry. The peak tourist season is from July to September, so expect larger crowds during this time.

Do’s and Don’ts of Zakho

Here are some of the do’s and don’ts to keep in mind when visiting Zakho:

Do:

  • Dress modestly and respect local customs and traditions.
  • Try the local cuisine and explore the city’s bazaars.
  • Treat locals with respect and courtesy.

Don’t:

  • Engage in public displays of affection.
  • Disrespect local customs and traditions.
  • Take photographs of people without their permission.

Safety and Health

Zakho is a safe city for tourists, but visitors should still take appropriate safety precautions. Here are some tips for staying safe and healthy in Zakho:

Safety in Zakho

  • Be aware of your surroundings and keep an eye on your belongings in crowded areas.
  • Avoid walking alone at night, especially in isolated areas.
  • Follow local laws and customs and avoid engaging in risky behavior.

Health in Zakho

  • Drink only bottled water and avoid drinking tap water.
  • Wear sunscreen and carry plenty of water when exploring the outdoors.
  • Make sure your vaccinations are up to date before traveling to Zakho.

Getting to Zakho: Transportation Options and Logistics

Zakho is easily accessible from a number of airports and bus stations in the region. Here are some of the top transportation options:

Airports Near Zakho

  • Erbil International Airport (EBL): This airport is located about 3 hours away from Zakho and offers daily flights to major cities in the Middle East and Europe.
  • Dohuk International Airport (DHK): This airport is located about 45 minutes away from Zakho and offers flights to major cities in Iraq and the Middle East.

Bus Stations Near Zakho

  • Zakho Bus Station: This is the main bus station in Zakho and offers regular bus services to major cities in Iraq and the Middle East.

Other Options

  • Private Transfer: Private transfer services are available from Erbil and Dohuk airports and are a convenient way to travel directly to Zakho.
  • Car Rental: Car rental services are available in Erbil and Dohuk and offer a great way to explore the region at your own pace.

How to Get Around in Zakho

The best way to get around Zakho is by taxi or by car. Taxis are readily available and are the most convenient way to get around the city. Rental cars are also available but are recommended for experienced drivers only.

Where to Go Next from Zakho: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Zakho is located in the heart of the Kurdistan region and offers easy access to several nearby destinations, including:

  • Erbil: The capital of the Kurdistan region, Erbil is known for its rich cultural heritage, local cuisine, and fascinating landmarks.
  • Dohuk: Located just 45 minutes away from Zakho, Dohuk is a bustling city with a vibrant culture and stunning mountain views.
  • Sulaymaniyah: Sulaymaniyah is a popular tourist destination known for its historic landmarks, scenic views, and unique culture.

Conclusion
