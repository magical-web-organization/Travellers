Introduction to Hit

Hit is a small town located in the western region of Iraq, near the Syrian border. It is situated in the middle of the desert, surrounded by sand dunes and rocky formations. It is a perfect destination for adventurers looking for some off-the-beaten-path experiences. Hit is rich in history and culture, and offers some of the most unique sights and experiences in the Middle East. The town has a small population of around 30,000 people, and offers a glimpse into the life and culture of the people living in the desert.

List of Places to Visit in Hit

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Hit Museum: The Hit Museum is a great place to learn about the history and culture of Hit. The museum showcases artifacts and exhibits from the ancient civilizations that once lived in the area. Visitors can see pottery, jewelry, and clothing from different eras.
  • Al-Madrasa Al-Ukhra Mosque: This mosque is one of the most important historical sites in Iraq. It was built in the 13th century and is a great example of Islamic architecture. Visitors can admire the intricate designs and beautiful calligraphy on the walls.
  • Hit Castle: The Hit Castle is a medieval fortification that was built in the 12th century. It is located on a hilltop and provides stunning views of the town and the surrounding desert. Visitors can explore the castle and enjoy the panoramic views.

Natural Attractions of Hit: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Hit River: The Hit River is a great place to cool off during the hot summer months. Visitors can swim, fish or take a relaxing boat ride along the river.
  • Al-Tar River: This river runs through the desert and is a popular spot for birdwatching. Visitors can spot a variety of species, including eagles, vultures, and falcons.
  • Ramadi Lake: This large lake is located just a few kilometers from Hit. It is a popular spot for camping, picnicking and watersports. Visitors can rent boats, canoes, or kayaks and explore the lake.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Hit

Hit is a great place for hiking and biking. There are several routes for both activities, offering stunning views of the desert and the town. Some popular trails include:

  • Hit Sand Dunes Trail: This trail will take you through the desert and over the sand dunes. Enjoy the breathtaking views of the landscape.
  • Jahannam Hills Trail: This trail is the perfect combination of natural beauty and adventure. Enjoy the views of the hills and valleys as you trek through the desert.
  • Hit City Tour by Bike: This tour will take you through the streets of Hit and offer a glimpse into the life of the locals. You will get to see the traditional houses, the marketplaces, and the historic landmarks.

Best Things to Do in Hit: Activities and Tours

  • Desert Safari: Take a tour of the desert in a 4x4 and experience the thrill of dune bashing. You will also get to see camel farms, nomadic villages, and ancient ruins.
  • Hot Air Balloon Ride: See Hit from a different perspective by taking a hot air balloon ride. Enjoy the stunning views of the desert and the town from above.
  • Cooking Classes: Learn how to cook traditional Iraqi dishes from local chefs. You will get to taste your creations and take home the recipes.

Food and Drink Scene of Hit

Must-Try Dishes

  • Masgouf: A grilled fish dish that is marinated in spices and lemon juice. It is served with rice and vegetables.
  • Kebab: A popular Middle Eastern dish made with grilled meat and vegetables, served with pita bread.
  • Quzi: A traditional Iraqi dish made with roasted lamb, rice, and spices.

Local Beverages

  • Tea: Arabic tea is a staple in Hit. It is usually served with dates and has a strong and aromatic taste.
  • Arak: A local alcoholic drink made with anise and distilled grapes.

Where to Stay in Hit: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

There are several options for accommodations in Hit. Most hotels offer basic amenities and are suitable for budget travellers. Some popular options include:

  • Ramadi Hit Hotel: This hotel is located in the heart of the town and offers basic rooms and services.
  • Hit Guesthouse: This guesthouse is a good option for those who want more privacy and comfort. It offers private rooms and some common areas for socializing.
  • Desert Rose Hotel: This hotel is located on the outskirts of Hit and offers stunning views of the desert. It has well-appointed rooms and services.

What to Buy in Hit: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Turquoise Jewelry: Hit is famous for its beautiful turquoise stones. Visitors can purchase bracelets, necklaces, and other jewelry made with these stones.
  • Spices: Iraq is known for its aromatic spices, such as cinnamon, cardamom, and saffron. Visitors can purchase these spices in local markets and shops.
  • Dates: Iraq is a major producer of dates, and visitors can purchase sweet and juicy dates in local markets.

Local Culture and Customs of Hit

  • Dress Code: Hit is a conservative town, and visitors are expected to dress modestly. Women should cover their heads and wear long skirts or pants.
  • Religion: Islam is the predominant religion in Hit, and visitors are expected to respect the local customs and practices.
  • Hospitality: Iraqi culture values hospitality, and visitors can expect warm and welcoming treatment from the locals.

Best Time to Visit Hit

The best time to visit Hit is between October and April, when the weather is mild and comfortable. Summers can be extremely hot, and temperatures can reach up to 50 degrees Celsius.

Do’s and Don’ts of Hit

Do:

  • Dress modestly.
  • Respect local traditions and customs.
  • Learn a few Arabic phrases.

Don’t:

  • Drink alcohol in public.
  • Display public affection.
  • Photograph people without their permission.

Safety and Health

Safety in Hit

Iraq is known for its political instability and security issues. Visitors are advised to check for travel advisories before planning a trip to the country. While Hit is generally safe, visitors are advised to take precautions while travelling in the town and the surrounding areas.

Health in Hit

Visitors are advised to get vaccinated before travelling to Iraq. They should also take precautions while eating street food and drinking water.

Getting to Hit: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Hit

  • Al Asad Airbase: Located about 60 km from Hit, this is the closest airport to the town.

Bus Stations Near Hit

  • Hit Bus Station: Located in the town centre, this station offers services to nearby towns and cities.

Other Options

  • Taxi: Visitors can hire a taxi from nearby towns and cities to get to Hit.

How to Get Around in Hit

The best way to get around in Hit is by walking or biking. Taxis are also available for hire to explore the areas outside of the town.

Where to Go Next from Hit: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Baghdad: The capital city of Iraq is located about 170 km from Hit. Visitors can explore the historic landmarks, museums, and markets of the city.
  • Fallujah: This historic city is about 50 km from Hit and offers a glimpse into the ancient civilizations that once lived in the area.
  • Al-Qaim: This border town is located about 60 km from Hit and is a popular spot for trade and commerce.

Conclusion

Hit is a hidden gem in the Middle East, offering a unique blend of history, culture, and natural beauty. Visitors can explore the ancient ruins, go hiking or biking in the desert, and enjoy the local food and drinks. While Iraq can be a challenging destination, Hit offers a safe and comfortable experience for those who are willing to explore this off-the-beaten-path destination.
