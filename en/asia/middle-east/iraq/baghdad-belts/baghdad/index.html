Introduction to Baghdad

Baghdad, the capital city of Iraq, is located in the Baghdad Belts and is situated on the Tigris River. The city has seen its share of turmoil over the years, but it has managed to bounce back and is now an up-and-coming tourist destination. The city is filled with attractions, landmarks and historical sites that are sure to entice any traveler.

List of Places to Visit in Baghdad

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Baghdad, being the former capital of the Abbasid Caliphate, is home to many historical sites that date back to the Islamic golden age. Some of the popular museums and historical places to visit in Baghdad are:

  1. National Museum of Iraq
  2. Baghdad Museum of Modern Art
  3. Al-Mutanabbi Street - The Literary Quarter
  4. The Abbasid Palace
  5. The Great Mosque of al-Nuri
  6. The Al-Kadhimiya Mosque
  7. The Saddam Hussein Presidential Center

Natural Attractions of Baghdad: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

For outdoor enthusiasts, Baghdad has a lot to offer, including a range of parks, gardens and scenic spots. Some of the popular natural attractions of Baghdad are:

  1. Zawraa Park
  2. Al-Nawabir Garden
  3. Al-Zawrak Park
  4. Al-Rasheed Park
  5. The Tigris River
  6. The Abu Hanifa Mosque

Hiking/Biking Routes of Baghdad

Even though Baghdad is a bustling city, it also has several opportunities for hiking and biking, especially in the city outskirts. Some of the popular hiking and biking routes of Baghdad are:

  1. Al-Zawrak Park to Al-Warood Park - 12 km
  2. Zawraa Park to Abu Nuwas Street - 8 km
  3. Al Mansour Park to The Faculty Club - 10 km
  4. The Tigris River Trail
  5. The Abu Hanifa Trail

Best Things to Do in Baghdad: Activities and Tours

Baghdad has something to offer for everyone, be it a cultural or adventurous experience. Some of the popular things to do in Baghdad are:

  1. Visit the National Museum of Iraq
  2. Explore the Abbasid Palace
  3. Walk down the Literary Quarter of Al-Mutanabbi Street
  4. Take a boat ride on the Tigris River
  5. Explore the historical sites of the city with a professional tour guide

Week-Long Itinerary for Baghdad

To make the most of your visit to Baghdad, here is a suggested week-long itinerary:

Day Activity
Day 1 Visit the National Museum of Iraq and the Baghdad Museum of Modern Art
Day 2 Explore the Al-Mutanabbi Street - The Literary Quarter, and the Abbasid Palace
Day 3 Take a boat ride on the Tigris River and explore the Al-Kadhimiya Mosque
Day 4 Visit the Great Mosque of Al-Nuri and explore the Zawraa Park
Day 5 Hike or bike the Abu Hanifa Trail and visit the Al Mansour Park
Day 6 Explore the Saddam Hussein Presidential Center and the Al-Zawrak Park
Day 7 Relax at Al-Nawabir Garden and visit the Faculty Club

Food and Drink Scene of Baghdad

Must-Try Dishes

Iraqi cuisine is a mix of Arab, Persian, and Turkish flavors. Some of the must-try dishes in Baghdad are:

  1. Masgouf - grilled fish
  2. Kebab - grilled meat
  3. Dolma - stuffed vegetables
  4. Kubba - meat-stuffed balls
  5. Tashreeb - lamb or chicken broth with bread

Local Beverages

Some of the popular local beverages of Baghdad are:

  1. Tea - black tea with sugar and mint
  2. Coffee - strong and usually served with cardamom
  3. Sharaba - fruit juice
  4. Ayran - yogurt drink

Local Culture and Customs of Baghdad

Some of the local culture and customs of Baghdad are:

  1. Respect for elders is highly valued in Iraq, and people show a lot of hospitality towards visitors.
  2. Dress modestly while visiting any religious sites or public places.
  3. Cover your head while visiting mosques.
  4. Be polite while talking to locals and avoid any sensitive topics like politics.
  5. Tipping is not expected, but appreciated.

Best Time to Visit Baghdad

The best time to visit Baghdad is from September to November and February to April when the weather is mild and pleasant.

Do’s and Don’ts of Baghdad

Do:

  1. Dress modestly
  2. Respect the local customs and traditions
  3. Be polite to locals

Don’t:

  1. Speak about politics or religion with strangers
  2. Take photographs of military personnel or sensitive sites without their permission
  3. Visit any area that is marked as a no-go zone

Safety and Health

Safety in Baghdad

Baghdad is generally safe for visitors, but it is important to take the necessary precautions while traveling, such as:

  1. Avoid visiting areas that are marked as a no-go zone
  2. Arrange for a professional local guide for your visits in the city
  3. Respect the local customs and traditions

Health in Baghdad

It is advisable to get vaccinated for basic diseases like mumps, measles, rubella, polio and hepatitis A and B before visiting Baghdad. Tap water is not safe to drink, so it’s recommended that visitors drink bottled water or use a water filter.

Getting to Baghdad: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Baghdad

Baghdad International Airport (BGW) is the main airport that serves the city, and it is located approximately 20 kilometers west of downtown Baghdad.

Bus Stations Near Baghdad

Al-Kut bus station, Al-Musayyib bus station and Al-Najaf bus station are the main bus stations that serve Baghdad.

Other Options

Taxis and rental cars are readily available for hire in Baghdad.

How to Get Around in Baghdad

The best way to get around in Baghdad is by taxi, which is relatively inexpensive. It’s recommended that visitors only ride authorized taxis to maintain safety.

Where to Go Next from Baghdad: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Some of the popular nearby destinations and excursions from Baghdad are:

  1. Karbala - a religious destination approximately 100 km from Baghdad.
  2. Najaf - a historical and religious destination approximately 180 km from Baghdad.
  3. The ancient city of Babylon and the ruins of Ctesiphon.
  4. The Marshes of Iraq situated near the Persian Gulf.

Conclusion

Baghdad is a unique blend of ancient history and modern-day wonder. Its rich culture, history, and hospitality have made it a must-visit destination for any traveler. We hope this guide has given you all the information you need to plan your visit to this beautiful city.
