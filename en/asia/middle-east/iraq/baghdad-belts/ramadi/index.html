Ramadi, a city located in the Baghdad Belts of Iraq, is renowned for its natural landscapes, cultural heritage, and warm hospitality of its people. Due to its strategic location, Ramadi has played a crucial role in the history of Iraq, making it a fascinating destination for both history buffs and outdoor enthusiasts alike.

Here’s a comprehensive guide to the best things to do, see, and experience in Ramadi:

Introduction to Ramadi

Ramadi is the capital of the Al Anbar province and is located in the western part of Iraq, approximately 110 km west of Baghdad. The city has a rich cultural heritage and is renowned for its recreational spots and historical sites. It is also known for its strategic significance in the Iraq War, during which it suffered considerable damage, but has since seen a tremendous transformation.

List of Places to Visit in Ramadi

Here is a list of the best places to visit in Ramadi:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Al-Anbar Museum: An impressive museum that houses a remarkable collection of traditional artefacts and heritage items, including old manuscripts and Islamic art.

  • Sheikh Hamed Al Dulaimi Shrine: A famous religious site with historical importance, surrounded by stunning gardens and beautiful architecture.

  • Anbar University: Explore the educational hub of Ramadi, boasting beautiful museums and galleries, and a remarkable library.

Natural Attractions of Ramadi: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Euphrates River: Take a scenic boat ride on this timeless river, wrapped in a beautiful landscape of lush vegetation and stunning cloud formations.

  • Lake Habbaniyah: A freshwater lake and a popular spot for watersports and recreational activities, surrounded by mountains and rocky terrain.

  • Ramadi Farm: Witness the beautiful rural countryside of Ramadi, with its vast fertile land, and vibrant produce of herbs, vegetables, and fruits.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Ramadi

Are you an outdoor enthusiast seeking adventure and exploring the natural wonders of Ramadi? Here are some of the best hiking and biking routes to explore:

  • Albu Hishma: Enjoy a scenic hike through the calm virgin landscapes of Albu Hishma, absorbing the freshness of the air, and witness the beauty of nature.

  • Euphrates Valley: Take a challenging mountain biking expedition, as you navigate through the valleys and ridges of the majestic Euphrates River and enjoy the stunning views of the surrounding countryside.

Best Things to Do in Ramadi: Activities and Tours

Here are some of the must-do activities and tours in Ramadi:

  • Hop-On Hop-Off Bus Tour: The perfect way to get around and explore Ramadi’s most popular attractions at your own pace.

  • Camel Riding: Experience the desert vibes and take a memorable camel ride, enjoying the magical scenery and capturing exceptional views of the sunset.

  • Culture Tour: Take a tour of Ramadi’s cultural and historical sites, learning about its rich heritage and traditions from a knowledgeable guide.

Week-Long Itinerary for Ramadi

Here is a suggested itinerary for a week-long visit to Ramadi:

  • Day 1: Visit Al-Anbar Museum and Sheikh Hamed Al Dulaimi Shrine.
  • Day 2: Explore Anbar University, and take in the beautiful museums and galleries.
  • Day 3: Take a scenic boat ride on Euphrates River, and enjoy the stunning landscape.
  • Day 4: Visit Lake Habbaniyah, and enjoy watersports and other recreational activities.
  • Day 5: Visit Ramadi Farm and get a glimpse of Ramadi’s rural landscape and agricultural production.
  • Day 6: Go hiking or mountain biking through the majestic Euphrates Valley.
  • Day 7: Take a culture tour and capture the essence of Ramadi’s cultural heritage and traditions.

Food and Drink Scene of Ramadi

Ramadi offers a unique blend of traditional and modern cuisine, with a focus on healthy ingredients and fresh produce. Here are some of the must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Madgooga: A delicious dish made from onions, tomatoes, okra, and fish.

  • Masgouf: A traditional fish dish, grilled and marinated with spices and served with rice.

  • Dolma: A famous vegetarian dish made of rice, herbs, and stuffed vegetables.

Local Beverages

  • Tea: A popular drink in Ramadi, typically steeped with mint, cardamom, and cinnamon.

  • Sharbat: A sweet and refreshing drink made of fruit syrups and rose water.

Where to Stay in Ramadi: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Ramadi has an excellent selection of accommodations, catering to all budgets and preferences. Here are some of the best options:

  • Tower House Hotel: Enjoy the luxury of the Tower House Hotel, featuring luxurious rooms and suites, delicious dining, and exceptional spa services.

  • Al Masar Hotel: A budget-friendly choice with comfortable accommodation, well-equipped rooms and reviews boasting excellent customer service.

  • Crystal Hotel: Elegant decor, excellent location, and top-notch customer service can make Crystal a top choice for travellers on a budget.

What to Buy in Ramadi: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Ramadi is famous for its handicrafts, unique souvenirs, and local products. Here are some of the best items to take home:

  • Pottery: You can find expertly crafted traditional pottery items in Ramadi’s markets, including decorative plates, bowls, and vases.

  • Authentic textiles: Purchase hand-woven fabrics, rugs, cloths and blankets, known for their vibrant colours and intricate patterns.

Local Culture and Customs of Ramadi

Ramadi is rich in culture and traditions, and visitors are encouraged to show respect and follow the local customs. Dress conservatively, pay attention to behaviour and seek some guidance from the locals.

Best Time to Visit Ramadi

The best time to visit Ramadi is during its mild winter season, between October to April. The weather is cooler and more pleasant, ideal for outdoor activities such as hiking, biking and sightseeing.

Do’s and Don’ts of Ramadi

Here are some tips to ensure that your visit to Ramadi is enjoyable and safe:

Do:

  • Respect the local customs and traditions.

  • Take precautions when using public transport.

  • Dress conservatively, covering your arms and legs.

Don’t:

  • Do not travel during the hot summer months, which is considered the off-season.

  • Do not take photographs of sensitive areas or people without permission.

Safety and Health

Safety in Ramadi

While Ramadi has undergone significant transformation in terms of safety, the city is still a conflict zone that requires vigilance and awareness. Talk to people who are knowledgable to your destination before your trip.

Health in Ramadi

Carry a sufficient stock of personal medicines as the healthcare system in Ramadi is yet to be optimally developed, and medical services may not be readily available in all areas.

Getting to Ramadi: Transportation Options and Logistics

Here are the transportation options to travel to Ramadi:

Airports Near Ramadi

The closest airport is Baghdad International Airport, which is approximately 108 km away from Ramadi. You can find connecting flights to other major cities in Iraq and other countries in the Middle East.

Bus Stations Near Ramadi

There are frequent buses that operate from Baghdad to Ramadi.

Other Options

Taking a taxi from Al Bakriya would be another way to reach Ramadi.

How to Get Around in Ramadi

Ramadi has an efficient public transport system with buses, taxis and shared autos. However, it’s always best to book a taxi or a private car for safety reasons.

Where to Go Next from Ramadi: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Here are some of the destinations that are worth visiting close to Ramadi:

  • Baghdad: The Iraqi capital, located only 110 km away from Ramadi.

  • Fallujah: A historic city, located approximately 70 km away from Ramadi.

Conclusion

Ramadi is a place that offers a unique blend of cultural heritage, natural beauty, adventure, and tranquillity. Plan your itinerary correctly and enjoy the best of what this remarkable city has to offer. Just be sure to follow the customs and traditions and take care to respect the city’s residents to have an enjoyable trip.
