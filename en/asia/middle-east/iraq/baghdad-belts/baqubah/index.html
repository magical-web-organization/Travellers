Introduction to Baqubah

Are you planning a visit to Iraq anytime soon? If so, you must consider adding Baqubah to your itinerary. Baqubah is a beautiful city located in the Baghdad Belts, which is a region of Iraq surrounded by the Tigris and Diyala rivers. This off-beat travel destination is blessed with natural beauty, cultural richness, and historical significance. In this travel guide, we will take you on a virtual journey to Baqubah and help you explore some of its best attractions, food, culture, and more.

List of Places to Visit in Baqubah

Here are the top places to visit in Baqubah:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Baqubah Museum: This museum showcases the history, culture, and tradition of the Baqubah region through various artifacts, relics, and photographs.

  • Imam Ali Mosque: This beautiful mosque is a must-visit attraction for anyone interested in Islamic architecture and history.

  • Baqubah Castle: This historical castle is an architectural marvel that served as a residence for the governor of Baqubah during the Abbasid and Ottoman empires.

Natural Attractions of Baqubah: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Lake Hamrin: This stunning lake is surrounded by picturesque mountains and offers various water activities like boating, fishing, and swimming.

  • Hamrin Mountain: This scenic mountain range is a nature lover’s paradise, with hiking and trekking trails leading to breathtaking views.

  • Buhayrat Sadiyah: This lake is another gem of Baqubah, surrounded by lush greenery and offering a peaceful retreat for visitors.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Baqubah

Baqubah is a perfect destination for outdoor enthusiasts, and the city offers many hiking and biking routes. Here are some popular routes worth exploring:

  • Hamrin Mountains: The trek to the top of Hamrin Mountains is a challenging but rewarding journey that offers stunning views.

  • Tarin Hills: This hiking trail passes through Tarin Hills, offering visitors the chance to admire the scenic beauty of the region and indulge in some recreational activities.

  • Diyala River: The banks of the Diyala River offer excellent opportunities for cycling and exploring the breathtaking scenery.

Best Things to Do in Baqubah: Activities and Tours

Here are some of the best things to do in Baqubah:

  • Visit a local market: Baqubah’s local markets offer a vibrant and exciting experience to visitors, with shops selling handicrafts, fabrics, and souvenirs.

  • Take a city tour: A city tour is an excellent way to explore Baqubah’s historical and cultural heritage, with experienced guides providing insights into the city’s past and present.

  • Attend a cultural event: Baqubah hosts several cultural events throughout the year, giving visitors the opportunity to experience the local culture and traditions.

Food and Drink Scene of Baqubah

Baqubah’s food and drink scene are known for their unique flavors and traditional cooking styles. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Tashreeb: A traditional dish made with bread, broth, and meat, served as a popular breakfast option in Baqubah.

  • Kubba: A hearty dish made with ground meat, rice, and spices, stuffed in a dumpling, and fried or baked.

  • Masgouf: A freshwater fish dish grilled over an open flame and served with traditional Iraqi spices and sauces.

Local Beverages

  • Tea: Tea is a popular beverage in Iraq, and Baqubah’s cafes serve different varieties of tea, including black tea, green tea, and mint tea.

  • Sherbet: A refreshing drink made with fruit syrup, water, and ice, commonly served during summer months in Baqubah.

Where to Stay in Baqubah: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Baqubah offers several places to stay for visitors, ranging from budget-friendly hostels to luxury hotels. Here are some of the top options:

  • Diyala Grand Hotel: This hotel is a great option for budget travelers, offering comfortable rooms, excellent amenities, and central location.

  • Baqubah International Hotel: This hotel offers luxurious rooms and suites, a swimming pool, spa, and fitness center to make your stay in Baqubah memorable.

  • Al-Saady Guest House: A family-run guest house, offering cozy rooms and a warm, friendly environment to visitors, perfect for a personalized experience.

What to Buy in Baqubah: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Baqubah offers several unique souvenirs and local products, perfect as gifts or memories of your trip. Here are some popular options:

  • Handicrafts: Baqubah is known for its exquisite handicrafts, including carpets, pottery, and jewelry.

  • Spices: The markets of Baqubah offer various traditional Iraq spices, including saffron, cumin, and coriander.

  • Dates: Iraq is famous for its dates, and Baqubah offers various types of dates, including Medjool and Deglet Noor.

Local Culture and Customs of Baqubah

Iraq is a Muslim country, and visitors must respect the local culture and customs. Here are some essential things to keep in mind:

  • Dress modestly when visiting mosques or religious sites.

  • Avoid public displays of affection.

  • Ask permission before taking photographs of locals or their property.

Best Time to Visit Baqubah

The best time to visit Baqubah is during the spring and autumn months, from March to May and September to November. The weather during these months is mild, with occasional rainfall and pleasant temperatures, making it ideal for outdoor activities.

Do’s and Don’ts of Baqubah

Here are some essential do’s and don’ts to keep in mind while visiting Baqubah:

Do:

  • Respect the local culture and customs.

  • Try the local cuisine and interact with the locals.

  • Bargain while shopping in the local markets.

Don’t:

  • Take photographs of sensitive areas or military installations.

  • Disrespect the local religion or customs.

  • Smoke or drink in public places.

Safety and Health

Safety and health are essential considerations when traveling to Baqubah. Here’s what you need to know:

Safety in Baqubah

Iraq has experienced some political turbulence over the years, but the situation has relatively stabilized in recent years. Visitors should take the necessary precautions, like traveling with a guide, avoiding sensitive areas, and keeping updated with the latest developments.

Health in Baqubah

Visitors should ensure that they are up-to-date with their vaccinations, especially for Hepatitis A and B. It’s also advisable to carry personal medication along and to avoid drinking tap water.

Getting to Baqubah: Transportation Options and Logistics

Baqubah is well-connected to major cities in Iraq, and visitors can choose from various transportation options like buses, taxis, and private cabs. Here’s what you need to know:

Airports Near Baqubah

  • Baghdad International Airport: This airport is the most common point of entry for international visitors to Iraq, located about 60 km from Baqubah.

  • Erbil International Airport: This airport is another option for international visitors, located about 200 km from Baqubah.

Bus Stations Near Baqubah

  • Baghdad Bus Station: Baghdad Bus Station is the primary hub for long-distance buses, offering connections to various major cities in Iraq and nearby countries.

  • Baqubah Bus Station: Baqubah Bus Station offers local and regional bus connections to nearby cities and towns.

Other Options

Visitors can also hire private cars or taxis, easily available in the city, for a more personalized experience.

How to Get Around in Baqubah

The best way to explore Baqubah is on foot or by taxi. Visitors can also rent a bicycle or motorbike to venture out of the city into the countryside.

Where to Go Next from Baqubah: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Baqubah is located in the heart of Iraq’s countryside, surrounded by several nearby destinations and landmarks. Here are some of the top options:

  • Baghdad: Iraq’s capital city, known for its rich history, culture, and architecture.

  • Erbil: Erbil is the capital of the Kurdistan region of Iraq, known for its vibrant culture, cuisine, and natural beauty.

  • Samarra: Samarra is a historical city in Iraq, known for its stunning landmarks and Islamic architecture.

Conclusion

Baqubah is a hidden gem of Iraq, blessed with natural beauty, cultural richness, and historical significance.
