Hawar Islands Travel Guide: A Paradise in the Middle East

Are you looking for a hidden paradise in the Middle East? Look no further than the Hawar Islands! Located in the Kingdom of Bahrain, the Hawar Islands are a peaceful and picturesque archipelago that offers plenty of natural wonders, cultural attractions, and outdoor activities. In this comprehensive travel guide, we’ll take you on a journey to this beautiful island paradise.

List of Places to Visit in Hawar Islands

The Hawar Islands offer a variety of attractions that cater to different interests. Here are some of the must-see places to visit:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Hawar Island Fort: Built during the Portuguese occupation of Bahrain, this well-preserved fortification is located on the northwestern coast of the main island. It offers a glimpse of Bahrain’s rich history and culture.
  • Hawar Cultural and Heritage Center: This museum showcases the traditional life and culture of the Hawar Islanders. It houses several artifacts, photographs, and exhibits that depict the history, customs, and traditions of the local community.

Natural Attractions of Hawar Islands: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Jarada Island: Located on the eastern edge of the Hawar Islands, Jarada Island is a small, uninhabited island that features a pristine white sand beach and clear blue waters. It’s a great place for swimming and snorkeling.
  • Hawar Islands Marine Protected Area: Spanning over 786 square kilometers, this marine reserve is a haven for biodiversity. It offers visitors the chance to see a wide variety of marine creatures, including dolphins, sea turtles, and whale sharks.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Hawar Islands

Exploring the Hawar Islands on foot or by bike is a great way to experience the natural beauty of the archipelago. Here are some of the most popular routes:

  • Hawar Island Coastal Trail: This 24-kilometer trail takes hikers and bikers along the rocky coastlines of Hawar Island. It offers stunning views of the sea and the surrounding islands.
  • Jarada Island Trail: This short and easy trail takes visitors across Jarada Island’s scenic landscape, including the island’s beach and rocky formations.

Best Things to Do in Hawar Islands: Activities and Tours

In addition to outdoor adventures, there are plenty of activities and tours to enjoy in the Hawar Islands:

  • Island Hopping: Explore the nearby islands by boat to discover hidden coves, beaches, and wildlife.
  • Dolphin Watching: Join a guided tour to spot playful dolphins in their natural habitat.
  • Fishing Trips: Hawar Islands offer some of the best fishing in Bahrain, giving visitors the chance to catch a variety of fish species.

Food and Drink Scene of Hawar Islands

The Hawar Islands’ food and drink reflect the traditional Bahraini cuisine, with influences from other Middle Eastern countries. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Machboos: A traditional Bahraini rice dish, usually made with chicken or lamb and seasoned with a blend of spices.
  • Harees: A porridge-like dish made from boiled wheat and meat, originally an Arabic dish that’s also popular in the Gulf region.

Local Beverages

  • Gahwa: A traditional Arabic coffee, brewed with cardamom and served in small cups.
  • Sharbat: A cool and refreshing drink made with syrups, fruit juices, and water.

Where to Stay in Hawar Islands: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

The Hawar Islands offer a range of accommodation options, from luxurious hotels to budget-friendly guesthouses:

  • Arman Hotel: A luxurious hotel located on the main island, offering stunning views of the sea and comfortable rooms.
  • Hawar Resort: A family-friendly resort located on the southern tip of the main island, featuring a private beach and several restaurants.
  • Hawar Island Guest House: A budget-friendly guesthouse located on the main island, featuring clean and comfortable rooms.

What to Buy in Hawar Islands: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

The Hawar Islands offer a range of souvenirs and local products that make great gifts or keepsakes:

  • Pearls: Bahrain has a long history of pearl diving, and the Hawar Islands offer the chance to buy authentic and unique pearl jewelry.
  • Handicrafts: The Hawar Cultural and Heritage Center gift shop offers a range of traditional handicrafts made by local artisans.
  • Spices: Bahrain is also known for its aromatic spices, including saffron, cinnamon, and cardamom. They make great souvenirs and can be found in local markets and shops.

Local Culture and Customs of Hawar Islands

The Hawar Islands have a rich cultural heritage that’s deeply rooted in Bahraini traditions and customs. Visitors should be aware of and respectful of the local culture, including dress codes and religious observances.

Best Time to Visit Hawar Islands

The best time to visit the Hawar Islands is during the winter months (December to February), when the weather is mild and pleasant. Summer months (June to August) can be extremely hot and humid, making outdoor activities less enjoyable.

Do’s and Don’ts of Hawar Islands

Visitors should be aware of the following do’s and don’ts when visiting the Hawar Islands:

Do:

  • Dress modestly, particularly when visiting religious sites.
  • Respect local customs and traditions.
  • Carry enough drinking water and wear sunscreen when going outdoors.

Don’t:

  • Photograph people without their permission.
  • Drink alcohol or smoke in public places.
  • Eat or drink during daylight hours during the month of Ramadan.

Safety and Health

The Hawar Islands are generally safe for visitors, with a low crime rate. However, visitors should take precautions when swimming or participating in water-based activities. Here are some tips for staying safe:

Safety in Hawar Islands

  • Stay within designated swimming areas and avoid swimming alone.
  • Wear life jackets when participating in water-based activities.
  • Be aware of the tides and strong currents when swimming or snorkeling.

Health in Hawar Islands

  • Visitors should have updated vaccinations for hepatitis A and typhoid.
  • Be aware of sunstroke and heat exhaustion during hot weather conditions.

Getting to Hawar Islands: Transportation Options and Logistics

The Hawar Islands are accessible by air and sea. Here are the ways to get there:

Airports Near Hawar Islands

The nearest airport to the Hawar Islands is Bahrain International Airport, which is located on the mainland. From there, visitors can take a taxi or rent a car to reach the ferry terminal.

Bus Stations Near Hawar Islands

There are no bus stations on the Hawar Islands. However, visitors can take a bus from Manama to the ferry terminal.

Other Options

Visitors can also choose to rent a car or take a taxi to reach the ferry terminal.

How to Get Around in Hawar Islands

The Hawar Islands are small and easily navigable on foot or by bike. Visitors can also hire a taxi or rent a car to explore the island further.

Where to Go Next from Hawar Islands: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

After exploring the Hawar Islands, visitors can consider visiting other nearby destinations in Bahrain, including:

  • Manama: Bahrain’s capital city, known for its modern architecture, historical sites, and shopping.
  • Al Jasra: A traditional village located on the west coast of Bahrain, offering a glimpse of Bahraini culture and heritage.
  • Muharraq: A former capital of Bahrain, known for its historic architecture and pearling heritage.

Conclusion

The Hawar Islands offer an ideal destination for visitors seeking a tranquil paradise, rich cultural heritage, and outdoor adventures.
