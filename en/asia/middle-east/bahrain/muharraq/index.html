Introduction

Muharraq, situated in Bahrain, is a culturally rich and historically significant city. Known for its traditional architecture, Muharraq boasts of museums, historical landmarks, natural attractions, and exciting outdoor adventures. With a vibrant food scene, visitors can relish the local cuisine and buy souvenirs from the local markets. In this comprehensive tour guide, we will explore the must-visit places, hiking/biking routes, best things to do, food scene, where to stay, and do’s and don’ts to ensure a safe and fulfilling trip to Muharraq.

List of Places to Visit in Muharraq

Muharraq is packed with several attractions that are a mix of historical landmarks, museums, natural attractions, and spectacular views.

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Shaikh Ebrahim Center for Culture & Research – A heritage center that displays the local arts, crafts, and culture.
  • Al Muharraq Stadium – A famous sports destination.
  • Arad Fort – One of Bahrain’s most ancient areas, featuring traditional Arabian architecture, a central Islamic garden, and stunning views of the sea.
  • Bin Matar House – A restored traditional house that showcases the historical and cultural aspects of Muharraq.
  • Siyadi House – A museum that represents the rich history of Muharraq and its importance in pearl diving.

Natural Attractions of Muharraq: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Prince Khalifa bin Salman Park - A large park with a walking track that overlooks the sea.
  • Hala Bahrain – An outdoor adventure park with activities such as zip lining, obstacle courses, and bungee jumping.
  • Grain Silos Park – An open space that offers stunning views of the sea and sunset.
  • The Tree of Life – A 400-year-old tree that stands alone in the desert with no apparent source of water.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Muharraq

Muharraq offers some great hiking and biking routes that are perfect for outdoor enthusiasts.

  • Muharraq Heritage Trail – A guided tour that takes you through the historical and cultural aspects of Muharraq.
  • Busaiteen Beach Track – A 6-kilometer cycling and walking track that offers beautiful views of the sea.
  • Al Ghous Corniche Walkway – A scenic walking path along the beach with magnificent views of the sea.
  • Al Jasra Trail – A 2-kilometer trail that runs through Al Jasra, offering some of the most beautiful views in Muharraq.

Best Things to Do in Muharraq: Activities and Tours

Muharraq offers several exciting activities and tours that cater to all ages and interests.

  • Boat Tour – Take a boat tour that explores some of the most beautiful islands and beaches around Muharraq.
  • Scuba Diving – Discover the underwater world by going scuba diving around Muharraq.
  • Souq Shopping – Visit the local markets and buy souvenirs, traditional Arabic textiles, handicrafts, and spices.
  • Sunset Horseback Riding – Experience the scenic views of Muharraq on horseback while watching the sunset.

Week-Long Itinerary for Muharraq

To make the most of your trip to Muharraq, consider the following itinerary:

Food and Drink Scene of Muharraq

Muharraq has a diverse food scene with a mix of traditional Arabian cuisine and international flavors.

Must-Try Dishes

  • Machboos – A traditional rice dish with fish or meat, served with salad or yogurt.
  • Ghouzi – A roasted lamb or chicken served on a bed of rice with nuts and spices.
  • Samboosa – Crispy pastry filled with meat, cheese, or vegetables.
  • Halwa – A dessert made from cornstarch, sugar, and nuts.

Local Beverages

  • Arabic Coffee – A fragrant and spiced coffee made with cardamom and served with dates.
  • Tea – A black tea served with mint and sugar.
  • Laban – A yogurt drink with a hint of salt and mint.

Where to Stay in Muharraq: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Muharraq has a range of accommodation options that cater to all budgets and preferences.

  • The Merchant House – A luxury hotel with modern amenities and a rooftop pool.
  • Hala Bahrain Hotel – A budget-friendly hotel with comfortable rooms and a central location.
  • Al Bander Hotel & Resort – A resort-style hotel with spacious rooms, an outdoor pool and a private beach.

What to Buy in Muharraq: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Muharraq has several local markets where you can buy souvenirs and traditional Arabic products. Some of the must-buy items include:

  • Arabic textiles
  • Handicrafts
  • Spices
  • Perfumes

Local Culture and Customs of Muharraq

Muharraq is a Muslim city, and visitors are expected to respect the local culture and customs.

  • Dress Modestly – When visiting mosques or public spaces, ensure that you are appropriately dressed, covering your shoulders and knees.
  • Respect Ramadan – During the holy month of Ramadan, visitors are expected to refrain from eating, drinking, and smoking in public places during the daylight hours.
  • Remove Shoes – When entering mosques or traditional houses, always remove your shoes.
  • Avoid Public Displays of Affection – Public displays of affection, such as hugging or kissing, are not acceptable in Arabian culture.

Best Time to Visit Muharraq

The best time to visit Muharraq is in the cooler months of November to April when the weather is milder and pleasant for outdoor activities.

Do’s and Don’ts of Muharraq

Do:

  • Carry cash as not all shops or restaurants accept credit cards.
  • Respect the local culture and customs.
  • Be punctual for meetings or appointments.
  • Bargain when shopping in the souqs.

Don’t:

  • Drink alcohol in public places.
  • Wear revealing clothing.
  • Eat in public during Ramadan.
  • Disrespect local customs.

Safety and Health

Safety in Muharraq

Muharraq is a safe city, with low crime rates. However, visitors should take standard precautions and ensure they do not travel alone at night.

Health in Muharraq

Muharraq has modern medical facilities and quality medical care. Visitors should ensure they have adequate health insurance that covers them for medical treatment abroad.

Getting to Muharraq: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Muharraq

Bahrain International Airport is the nearest airport, located approximately 5 km from Muharraq. Taxis are readily available at the airport.

Bus Stations Near Muharraq

Public buses run throughout Muharraq, providing an affordable and convenient way to explore the city.

Other Options

Taxis and private cars are readily available and provide an efficient way to travel around Muharraq.

How to Get Around in Muharraq

Walking or cycling is the best way to explore Muharraq, especially the historic district. Taxis and buses are readily available for visiting areas that are further away.

Where to Go Next from Muharraq: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Muharraq is close to many attractions and destinations in Bahrain, making it a great base for exploring the surrounding areas. Some of the nearby destinations and excursions include:

  • Manama – The capital city of Bahrain, located 7 km from Muharraq.
  • Bahrain World Trade Center – A famous landmark that features wind turbines to power the building.
  • Al Fateh Grand Mosque – A magnificent mosque that can accommodate thousands of worshippers.
  • National Museum of Bahrain – A world-famous museum that showcases the history and culture of Bahrain.

Conclusion

Muharraq is a city steeped in culture and history, with an exciting food scene, natural attractions, outdoor adventures, and activities that cater to all ages and interests. With this comprehensive tour guide, visitors can explore the city and discover all that it has to offer while ensuring a safe and enjoyable trip.
