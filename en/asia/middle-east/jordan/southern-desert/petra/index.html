Petra, one of the world’s most iconic archaeological sites, is nestled deep within the Southern Desert of Jordan, in the Middle East of Asia. It is often referred to as the “Rose City” due to the reddish hues of its sandstone cliffs. This ancient city once served as the capital of the Nabataean Kingdom and was an important stop on the trade routes between the Arabian Peninsula, Egypt, and Syria. Today, Petra remains a popular tourist destination, attracting millions of visitors each year. In this comprehensive travel guide to Petra, we’ll cover everything you need to know before planning your visit.

Introduction to Petra

The lost city of Petra is an astonishing archaeological and geological marvel located in southwestern Jordan, about 2.5 hours away from Amman. It was carved directly into the sandstone cliffs by the Nabateans, an Arab tribe, around 312 BC. The city remained a Byzantine regional center up until an earthquake in the 7th century caused significant damage, forcing the inhabitants to abandon the city. Petra was rediscovered by a European traveler in 1812 and later became a UNESCO World Heritage site in 1985.

Local Culture and Customs of Petra

It’s important to remember that Petra is a conservative Muslim country, and visitors should respect local customs and traditions. Here are some important cultural customs to keep in mind:

  • Dress modestly in public
  • Remove your shoes before entering a mosque or home
  • Avoid public displays of affection
  • Respect local customs and traditions
  • Ask for permission before photographing locals

Best Time to Visit Petra

The best time to visit Petra is from March to May (spring) or from September to November (fall) to avoid the extreme heat of the summer months. The temperature during these periods is pleasant and perfect for exploring the ancient city.

Do’s and Don’ts of Petra

Do:

  • Use sunscreen and bring a hat and sunglasses
  • Carry enough water and snacks with you
  • Wear comfortable walking shoes
  • Respect local customs and traditions
  • Follow the designated hiking and biking trails

Don’t:

  • Litter in the protected areas or on hiking trails
  • Enter any prohibited areas
  • Engage in any acts of animal cruelty
  • Touch any statues, carvings, or ancient structures
  • Take any natural resources or artifacts

Safety and Health

Safety in Petra

Petra is a relatively safe tourist destination, but visitors should still take necessary precautions to stay safe. Here are some tips to keep in mind:

  • Avoid unsafe areas and neighborhoods
  • Be aware of your surroundings at all times, especially at night
  • Keep valuables out of sight and in a safe place
  • Use only licensed taxis and official transportation services

Health in Petra

Travelers to Petra should be up to date on routine vaccinations such as measles/mumps/rubella and hepatitis B. It’s also advised to get a hepatitis A, typhoid, and cholera vaccinations before traveling. Visitors should also ensure all food is thoroughly cooked and avoid tap water unless boiled or treated.
