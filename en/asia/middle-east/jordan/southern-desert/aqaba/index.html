Introduction to Aqaba

Nestled in the Southern Desert of Jordan, Aqaba is a coastal city famed for its picturesque beaches, rich cultural heritage, and natural wonders. As the only port in Jordan, Aqaba has a long and illustrious history dating back thousands of years, making it an ideal destination for those looking to explore the Middle East.

List of Places to Visit in Aqaba

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Aqaba Castle: Also known as Mamluk Castle, this medieval fortress dates back to the 14th century and serves as an impressive example of Islamic art and architecture.
  • Aqaba Museum: Located in the ancient ruins of Ayla, this museum showcases the history of Aqaba with exhibitions that date back over 7,000 years.
  • Aqaba Archaeological Museum: Home to a vast collection of ancient artifacts, this museum is a must-see for history buffs and enthusiasts.

Natural Attractions of Aqaba: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Aqaba Marine Park: With over 25 dive sites, the Aqaba Marine Park is a hub for aquatic adventures and underwater exploration.
  • Wadi Rum: Known for its striking red sandstone mountains and vast desert landscapes, Wadi Rum is a bucket-list destination for outdoor enthusiasts.
  • Aqaba Bird Observatory: Nestled on the eastern shore of the Red Sea, this observatory is home to a plethora of migratory bird species and provides an opportunity for up-close and personal bird watching experiences.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Aqaba

  • Wadi Rum Jeep Tours: Available for day trips or overnight adventures, the Wadi Rum jeep tour is a chance to explore the vast desert landscapes and experience the traditional Bedouin lifestyle.
  • Aqaba Mountain Trail: A challenging but rewarding hike, the Aqaba Mountain Trail takes you on a journey through the stunning landscapes of the Aqaba Mountains.

Best Things to Do in Aqaba: Activities and Tours

  • Aqaba Street Food Tour: Taste your way through the city with a street food tour that features a variety of local bites and beverages.
  • Snorkeling and Diving: With crystal-clear waters and abundant marine life, Aqaba is a prime location for snorkeling and diving.
  • Sunset Cruise: Take in the breathtaking views of the Red Sea at sunset with a romantic cruise along the coastline.

Food and Drink Scene of Aqaba

Must-Try Dishes

  • Mansaf: Jordan’s national dish, mansaf is a bed of rice smothered in lamb broth and topped with tender chunks of lamb and yogurt sauce.
  • Kabsa: Aqaba’s take on the popular Middle Eastern rice dish, kabsa is made with fragrant rice, grilled chicken, and a variety of aromatic spices.
  • Falafel: A vegetarian staple, this fried dough made from chickpeas is served with a variety of sauces and is a popular street food in Aqaba.

Local Beverages

  • Mint Tea: Served hot or cold, mint tea is a refreshing beverage with a subtle sweetness and minty flavor.
  • Arak: A strong anise-flavored liquor, Arak is a popular drink in Jordan and is often served alongside meze.

Where to Stay in Aqaba: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Kempinski Hotel Aqaba Red Sea: A luxurious hotel with stunning sea views, the Kempinski offers a range of amenities, including a spa, private beach, and several on-site dining options.
  • Bedouin Garden Village: Located just outside of the city, the Bedouin Garden Village offers a peaceful retreat with comfortable lodgings, an on-site restaurant, and an outdoor pool.
  • Al-Qidra Hotel Aqaba: A budget-friendly option located in the heart of the city, the Al-Qidra offers clean and comfortable rooms and easy access to many of Aqaba’s top attractions.

What to Buy in Aqaba: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Dead Sea Products: Aqaba is located just a few hours from the Dead Sea, known for its mineral-rich mud and salts that are said to have numerous health benefits.
  • Jordanian Handicrafts: From woven rugs to intricate jewelry, Jordan is known for its traditional handicrafts that make for unique and beautiful souvenirs.
  • Arabic Spices: Pick up a medley of Arabic spices to bring home and add to your own culinary creations.

Local Culture and Customs of Aqaba

Jordanians are known for their warm hospitality and kindness towards travelers. It is common to remove one’s shoes before entering a home or mosque, and modest dress is appreciated, especially in more traditional areas.

Best Time to Visit Aqaba

The best time to visit Aqaba is during the shoulder seasons of spring (March to May) and autumn (September to November) when temperatures are mild and the crowds are thinner.

Do’s and Don’ts of Aqaba

Do:

  • Dress modestly, especially when visiting religious sites.
  • Try the local cuisine and street food.
  • Respect local customs and traditions.

Don’t:

  • Drink alcohol in public places.
  • Take photographs of people without their consent.
  • Disrespect the local culture and traditions.

Safety and Health

Safety in Aqaba

Jordan is considered a safe and stable country, and Aqaba is no exception. However, it’s always important to remain aware of your surroundings and take necessary precautions, such as not leaving valuables unattended.

Health in Aqaba

It is recommended to drink bottled water in Aqaba, as tap water is not always safe. Sunscreen and insect repellent are also important to bring, especially for outdoor activities and excursions.

Getting to Aqaba: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Aqaba

Aqaba has its own airport, King Hussein International Airport (AQJ), which offers flights from major Middle Eastern cities and Europe.

Bus Stations Near Aqaba

JETT buses operate daily services from Amman to Aqaba, with several stops along the way.

Other Options

Taxis and rental cars are also available, but it’s important to negotiate prices beforehand and avoid taxis without a functioning meter.

How to Get Around in Aqaba

Taxis are readily available in Aqaba and can be hailed down on the street or at designated taxi stands. Rental cars and scooters are also available, but be aware that traffic can be congested and chaotic.

Where to Go Next from Aqaba: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Petra: One of Jordan’s most famous and iconic destinations, Petra is a three-hour drive from Aqaba and is renowned for its stunning rock-cut architecture and ancient history.
  • Wadi Rum: The vast desert landscapes of Wadi Rum are located just a short drive from Aqaba and are a popular destination for visitors looking to explore the great outdoors.
  • Amman: Jordan’s capital city is a bustling metropolis that boasts a rich history, unique culture, and an exciting dining and nightlife scene.

Conclusion

Whether you’re looking to explore rich cultural heritage, indulge in delicious cuisine, or embark on thrilling outdoor adventures, Aqaba has something for everyone. Start planning your dream trip to Aqaba today and prepare to be enchanted by this stunning seaside destination in the heart of the Middle East.
