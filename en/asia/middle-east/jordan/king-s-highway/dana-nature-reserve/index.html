Introduction to Dana Nature Reserve

Nestled in the heart of Jordan’s King’s Highway, Dana Nature Reserve is a hidden gem waiting to be discovered. Spanning over 300 square kilometers, this spectacular nature reserve is home to a diverse range of wildlife, including the Nubian ibex, Syrian serin, and various species of raptors.

With its breathtaking natural beauty and rich cultural heritage, Dana Nature Reserve is a must-visit destination for any nature lover or adventure enthusiast. Whether you’re looking to unwind and soak up the serene atmosphere, or seeking an adrenaline-fueled thrill, this reserve has something for everyone.

List of Places to Visit in Dana Nature Reserve

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Dana Nature Reserve is steeped in history and culture, with numerous museums and historical sites worth exploring. Some of the most notable include:

  • Dana Village: This charming traditional village is a living museum that showcases the way of life of the local people. Visitors can explore the narrow streets and alleyways, admire the breathtaking views, and experience authentic Jordanian hospitality.
  • Feynan Ecolodge: This award-winning eco-tourism lodge is a prime example of sustainable development in the region. It offers an excellent opportunity to learn about the Bedouin way of life and immerse oneself in the local culture.
  • Qaser Al-Azraq: This historic fortress dates back to the 13th century and played a crucial role in the Arab Revolt against the Ottomans. Visitors can explore the castle’s impressive architecture, learn about its fascinating history, and enjoy the spectacular views from the top of the watchtower.

Natural Attractions of Dana Nature Reserve: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Dana Nature Reserve is a paradise for outdoor enthusiasts, with plenty of opportunities for hiking, biking, wildlife spotting, and more. Some of the most popular activities include:

  • Dana Biosphere Reserve: This protected area is home to over 600 plant species, 45 mammal species, and 190 bird species. Visitors can explore the reserve on foot, horseback, or bike, and discover its diverse landscapes, from rugged mountains to deep canyons.
  • Wadi Dana Trail: This 14-kilometer trail offers a challenging but rewarding hike through the heart of the reserve. Along the way, visitors can admire the stunning views, spot wildlife, and learn about the local flora and fauna.
  • Dana Viewpoint: This scenic overlook offers panoramic views of the Dana Reserve and the surrounding mountains. It’s a popular spot for sunset and sunrise viewing and offers a perfect backdrop for some stunning photographs.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Dana Nature Reserve

Dana Nature Reserve is a dream destination for hiking and biking enthusiasts, with numerous trails to suit all levels of ability. Some of the most popular routes include:

  • The Dana to Petra Trek: This four to five-day trek takes visitors through some of the most spectacular landscapes in Jordan, starting from the village of Dana and ending in Petra. Along the way, visitors can explore ancient ruins, marvel at the stunning scenery, and experience the rich culture and hospitality of the local people.
  • The Abu Qudaira Trail: This 12-kilometer hike is a challenging but rewarding route that offers some of the best views of the Dana Reserve. Visitors can explore the rugged terrain, spot wildlife, and enjoy the stunning scenery.
  • The Wadi Feinan Trail: This 11-kilometer circular route takes visitors through the heart of the Feinan eco-tourism project, offering a unique opportunity to learn about sustainable development in the region. Along the way, visitors can enjoy the stunning views, explore ancient ruins, and spot wildlife.

Best Things to Do in Dana Nature Reserve: Activities and Tours

Dana Nature Reserve offers a wide range of activities and tours to suit every taste and budget. Some of the best things to do include:

  • Wildlife Watching: Whether you’re an avid birdwatcher or a nature lover, Dana Nature Reserve offers plenty of opportunities to see some of Jordan’s most fascinating wildlife up close. Some of the most popular species to look out for include the Nubian ibex, Syrian serin, and various species of raptors.
  • Rock Climbing: With its rugged terrain and sheer cliffs, Dana Nature Reserve is a popular destination for rock climbers of all levels of ability. There are numerous routes to choose from, ranging from easy scrambles to challenging multi-pitch climbs.
  • Bedouin Experience: For a truly authentic Jordanian experience, consider booking a Bedouin experience tour. This offers an excellent opportunity to learn about the Bedouin way of life, try traditional food and drink, and experience genuine Jordanian hospitality.

Week-Long Itinerary for Dana Nature Reserve

For those looking to spend a week exploring the best of Dana Nature Reserve, consider the following itinerary:

Day 1: Arrive in Amman and drive to Dana Nature Reserve. Day 2: Explore Dana Village and Feynan Ecolodge. Day 3: Hike the Abu Qudaira Trail. Day 4: Visit Qaser Al-Azraq and enjoy a Bedouin Experience. Day 5: Wildlife watching and bird watching. Day 6: Visit Petra. Day 7: Depart from Dana Nature Reserve.

Food and Drink Scene of Dana Nature Reserve

Jordan’s rich culinary heritage is a highlight of any trip, and Dana Nature Reserve is no exception. From traditional Bedouin dishes to modern fusion cuisine, there’s something to suit every palate. Some must-try dishes include:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Mansaf: This national dish consists of lamb cooked in a yogurt sauce, served over rice and topped with pine nuts.
  • Zarb: This Bedouin specialty is a slow-cooked barbecue feast, cooked in an underground pit and served with a variety of sides.
  • Falafel: This classic Middle Eastern dish consists of deep-fried balls made from chickpeas and spices, served with tahini sauce and pita bread.

Local Beverages

  • Arabic Coffee: This strong and aromatic coffee is a staple of Jordanian hospitality and is served at all formal occasions.
  • Tea: Jordanians take their tea seriously, with a variety of blends and brewing methods to suit every taste.
  • Lemon Mint: This refreshing drink is a popular choice on hot summer days, made with fresh lemon juice and mint leaves.

Where to Stay in Dana Nature Reserve: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Dana Nature Reserve offers a variety of accommodation options to suit every budget and taste. Whether you’re looking for a cozy guesthouse or a luxury eco-resort, there’s something for everyone. Some of the best places to stay include:

  • Feynan Ecolodge: This award-winning eco-tourism lodge offers a range of rooms and cabins, all designed with sustainability in mind. It’s the perfect base for exploring the reserve and learning about sustainable development.
  • Dana Guesthouse: This charming guesthouse is located in the heart of Dana Village and offers traditional Bedouin hospitality. It’s an excellent choice for those looking for an authentic cultural experience.
  • Rummana Campsite: For those looking to get closer to nature, this campsite offers a range of tent and cabin options, as well as stunning views of the reserve.

What to Buy in Dana Nature Reserve: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Jordan is renowned for its handicrafts, and Dana Nature Reserve is an excellent place to shop for locally made souvenirs and products. Some of the best items to bring home include:

  • Hand-woven rugs and carpets.
  • Pottery and ceramics.
  • Handmade soaps and skincare products.
  • Local herbs and spices.

Local Culture and Customs of Dana Nature Reserve

Jordan is a deeply religious and conservative country, and visitors are expected to respect local customs and etiquette. Some tips to keep in mind include:

  • Dress modestly, covering your shoulders and knees.
  • Remove your shoes before entering someone’s home.
  • Avoid public displays of affection.
  • Ask for permission before taking someone’s photo.

Best Time to Visit Dana Nature Reserve

The best time to visit Dana Nature Reserve is from March to May and from September to November. During these months, the weather is mild, and the reserve is awash with colorful wildflowers and lush vegetation. Summer can be scorching, with temperatures soaring over 40 degrees Celsius, while winter can be chilly, with snowfall in some areas.

Do’s and Don’ts of Dana Nature Reserve

Do:

  • Respect local customs and traditions.
  • Dress modestly.
  • Hire a local guide for hiking and camping trips.
  • Try the local food and drink.

Don’t:

  • Drink tap water without filtering it.
  • Leave trash behind.
  • Take photos of people without asking for permission.
  • Be disrespectful towards the local culture and way of life.

Safety and Health

Safety in Dana Nature Reserve

Overall, Dana Nature Reserve is a safe destination for travelers. However, visitors should take normal precautions to ensure their safety, such as keeping valuables secure and avoiding isolated areas at night. It’s also recommended to hire a local guide for hiking and camping trips.

Health in Dana Nature Reserve

As with any destination, travelers should take precautions to ensure good health during their visit to Dana Nature Reserve. It’s recommended to drink bottled water, avoid raw or undercooked food, and use insect repellent to prevent mosquito bites.

Getting to Dana Nature Reserve: Transportation Options and Logistics

Dana Nature Reserve is located in southern Jordan, approximately 250 kilometers from Amman. The most convenient way to get there is by private car or taxi, although there are also public buses available. Some options include:

Airports Near Dana Nature Reserve

  • Queen Alia International Airport, Amman.

Bus Stations Near Dana Nature Reserve

  • Petra Bus Station, located approximately 45 minutes from Dana Nature Reserve.
  • Aqaba Bus Station, located approximately two hours from Dana Nature Reserve.

Other Options

  • Private car or taxi.

How to Get Around in Dana Nature Reserve

The best way to get around Dana Nature Reserve is on foot or by bike. There are numerous trails and paths to explore, and bikes can be rented from local vendors. For longer distances or those with limited mobility, taxis are also available.

Where to Go Next from Dana Nature Reserve: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Jordan is a small country packed with fascinating landmarks and attractions, and there are numerous destinations worth exploring beyond Dana Nature Reserve. Some popular excursions and nearby destinations include:

  • Petra: This world-famous archaeological site is a must-visit destination for any traveler to Jordan, offering glimpses into the ancient kingdom of the Nabateans.
  • Wadi Rum: This stunning desert landscape has been featured in numerous movies and provides a magical backdrop for camping, hiking, and stargazing.
  • Dead Sea: This famous salt lake is the lowest point on earth and offers visitors the chance to float effortlessly in its salty waters.

Conclusion

Dana Nature Reserve is a truly enchanting destination that offers visitors an authentic taste of Jordanian culture and hospitality. Whether you’re seeking outdoor adventure, cultural immersion, or just some R&R in a beautiful natural setting, this reserve has something for everyone. From the stunning landscapes and diverse wildlife to the charming villages and delicious cuisine, get ready to be enchanted by the magic of Dana Nature Reserve.
