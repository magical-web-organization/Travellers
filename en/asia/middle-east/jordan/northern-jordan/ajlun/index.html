Introduction to Ajlun

Ajlun is a small town located in the northern region of Jordan, known for its historical landmarks and beautiful landscapes. The town is situated on a hilltop overlooking the Jordan Valley, making it an ideal destination for nature lovers and history buffs alike.

With its fascinating history, mesmerizing scenery, and friendly locals, Ajlun attracts visitors from all around the world. In this comprehensive travel guide, we’ll take a closer look at the top things to do, where to eat and stay, and how to get around during your visit to Ajlun.

List of Places to Visit in Ajlun

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Ajlun is known for its wealth of historical landmarks, each with its own fascinating story to tell. One of the most popular attractions amongst visitors is the Ajlun Castle, also known as the Qala’at Ar-Rabad. Constructed in 1184 AD, the castle was initially built to protect the area from Crusader attacks.

Another historical site worth visiting is the Al-Rabadh Castle, which dates back to the 13th century. The castle is a fusion of Islamic and Crusader architecture and was used as a military barracks during the Ottoman period.

Natural Attractions of Ajlun: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Ajlun is also popular for its natural beauty, which is why visitors are encouraged to wander around the town to enjoy the stunning views of the Jordan Valley.

If you’re up for some hiking or trekking, consider visiting the Ajlun Mountains Reserve, where you can explore the natural beauty of the region on foot. The reserve boasts several trails and picnic areas, making it a perfect spot for a day trip with family and friends.

Another natural wonder worth visiting is the Wadi Al-Rayan, which is a stunning canyon located south of Ajlun. Visitors can take a hike to the canyon to witness the spectacular waterfalls and natural pools.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Ajlun

Ajlun is full of outdoor activities, including hiking and biking. If you’re an adventure-seeker, Ajlun Mountains Reserve is the perfect place for you. The reserve offers various challenging trails, ranging from moderate to difficult, allowing visitors to test their physical endurance while enjoying the beautiful scenery.

If you’re more into biking, we recommend heading to the Wadi Al-Rayan area, where you can rent a bike and ride through the stunning scenery of the canyon. It’s also a great way to experience the local culture and engage with the residents of the town.

Best Things to Do in Ajlun: Activities and Tours

There’s no shortage of things to do in Ajlun, whether you’re an adventure-seeker or a history buff. For a unique experience, we recommend taking a cooking class to learn how to prepare authentic Jordanian dishes.

For a dose of culture and history, consider taking a guided tour of the Ajlun Castle to learn about its fascinating history and to witness the stunning views of the Jordan Valley from the top of the castle.

Alternatively, you can take a visit to the Soap House in the town and learn how to make traditional soap using natural ingredients such as olive oil and laurel leaves. It’s a great activity to do with your family or friends, and you even get to take your handmade soap home with you.

Week-Long Itinerary for Ajlun

If you’re planning a week-long trip to Ajlun, we’ve put together an itinerary that will help you make the most of your time in the town.

Day 1: Visit the Ajlun Castle and take a guided tour to learn about its history. Day 2: Take a hiking tour of the Ajlun Mountains Reserve and take in the stunning views of the region. Day 3: Explore the Wadi Al-Rayan canyon and relax by the natural pools and waterfalls. Day 4: Visit the Soap House and learn how to make traditional soap. Day 5: Take a cooking class and learn how to prepare authentic Jordanian dishes. Day 6: Visit the Al-Rabadh Castle and marvel at its impressive architecture. Day 7: Spend the day exploring the town on foot or relax at a local cafe and soak up the authentic Jordanian culture.

Food and Drink Scene of Ajlun

Must-Try Dishes

Jordanian cuisine is renowned for its unique blend of flavors, and Ajlun offers a range of culinary delights worth trying during your trip. One of the most popular dishes is Mansaf, which is considered the national dish of Jordan. It’s made with lamb, rice, and a tangy yogurt sauce and is typically served with flatbread.

Another must-try dish is the falafel sandwich, which is made with fresh veggies, hummus, and of course, the tasty fried falafel balls. We also recommend trying Kunafa, a dessert made with shredded pastry and cheese, soaked in a sweet sugar syrup.

Local Beverages

Tea is a staple in Jordan, and locals often drink a type of tea called “sage tea.” It’s made with fresh herbs and is believed to have medicinal properties. Coffee is also a popular beverage in Jordan, and you can find cafes in the town serving it.

If you’re looking for something stronger, try the traditional anise-flavored arak, a popular alcoholic drink in the Middle East.

Where to Stay in Ajlun: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Ajlun offers a range of accommodation options, from budget-friendly hostels to luxury hotels. If you’re looking for an authentic experience, consider staying in a local guesthouse, where you can interact with the residents and learn more about the local culture.

One popular guesthouse in the town is the Ajloun Forest Reserve, which offers rustic but comfortable accommodation options in the heart of the Ajlun Mountains Reserve. For a more luxurious experience, consider staying at the Ajloun Hotel, which offers stunning views of the Jordan Valley and contemporary amenities like a pool and gym.

What to Buy in Ajlun: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Ajlun offers several unique souvenirs and local products to bring home to remind you of your trip. One popular item is the locally produced olive oil, which is known for its rich flavor and health benefits.

Another popular souvenir is the locally made handicrafts, such as ceramics, textiles, and even traditional musical instruments. You can find these items at the local markets and bazaars in the town.

Local Culture and Customs of Ajlun

Jordan is a predominantly Muslim country, and while Ajlun is more liberal in its customs than other parts of Jordan, visitors should still be respectful of the local culture. It’s recommended to dress modestly, especially when visiting religious sites or in public places.

Jordanians are known for their hospitality, so it’s common to be invited into locals’ homes, especially during celebrations or festivals. It’s always a good idea to bring a small gift, such as sweets or pastries, to show your appreciation.

Best Time to Visit Ajlun

The best time to visit Ajlun is during the spring months (March to May), when the weather is mild, and the hills and valleys are covered in colorful flowers. The fall months (September to November) are also pleasant and offer cooler temperatures.

The summer months (June to August) can be extremely hot, with temperatures often reaching above 35°C. The winter months (December to February) can be cold and rainy, so it’s recommended to bring warm clothing and rain gear.

Do’s and Don’ts of Ajlun

Do:

  • Dress modestly when visiting religious sites or public places.
  • Be respectful of the local culture and customs.
  • Try the local cuisine and engage with the residents.

Don’t:

  • Drink alcohol in public places.
  • Take photographs of people without their consent.
  • Show public displays of affection.

Safety and Health

Safety in Ajlun

Jordan is generally a safe country for visitors, with low crime rates and a friendly population. However, visitors should still exercise caution and be vigilant, especially when traveling alone.

Avoid carrying large sums of cash or valuable items in public places, and always lock your hotel room or rental car. In case of an emergency, call the local authorities using the emergency numbers.

Health in Ajlun

Jordan has a good standard of healthcare, with government-run hospitals and clinics available in most cities and towns, including Ajlun. Visitors are advised to carry their medical insurance and to bring any prescription medicines they may need.

It’s recommended to drink bottled water instead of tap water and to use insect repellent, especially during the summer months when mosquitoes are prevalent.

Getting to Ajlun: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Ajlun

The closest airport to Ajlun is the Queen Alia International Airport, which is located in the capital city, Amman – approximately 70 km from Ajlun. From there, visitors can hire a taxi or rent a car to reach the town.

Bus Stations Near Ajlun

There are no direct buses to Ajlun, but buses run regularly from Amman’s North Bus Station to Jerash, which is approximately 25 km from Ajlun. From there, visitors can take a taxi or rent a car to reach the town.

Other Options

Visitors can book a private transfer or taxi from the airport or Amman city center to reach Ajlun. Alternatively, you can rent a car and drive to the town, which is recommended for those planning to explore the surrounding areas independently.

How to Get Around in Ajlun

The best way to get around Ajlun is on foot or by taxi, as the town is compact and easy to navigate. Taxis are readily available throughout the town, and you can negotiate the fare with the driver before starting your trip.

If you’re planning to explore the surrounding areas independently, it’s recommended to rent a car. There are several car rental companies in Amman, and you can easily book a car online or on arrival at the airport.

Where to Go Next from Ajlun: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

If you’re planning to extend your trip to Jordan, there are several nearby destinations and excursions worth considering. The ancient city of Jerash, which is famous for its well-preserved ruins, is located just a short drive from Ajlun.

Other popular destinations within easy reach include the Dead Sea, Wadi Rum, Petra, and Aqaba, each with its own unique charm and attractions.

Conclusion

With its rich history, stunning landscapes, and friendly locals, Ajlun is an ideal destination for anyone seeking an authentic Jordanian experience. From exploring the historical landmarks to trekking through the beautiful mountains, there’s something for everyone in this charming town.
