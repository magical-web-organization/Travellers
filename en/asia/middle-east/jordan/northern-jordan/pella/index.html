Introduction to Pella

Pella is a historically rich city located in Northern Jordan, in the Middle East region of Asia. Known for its ancient ruins, archaeological sites, and stunning natural beauty, Pella is a must-visit destination for any traveler interested in history and culture.

The city has served as an important hub for trade and commerce for centuries, and its prime location among the hills and valleys of the Jordan Valley makes it a popular spot for outdoor enthusiasts and adventure seekers.

In this comprehensive travel guide, we’ll cover everything from the top attractions to the best hiking and biking routes, local culture and customs, and much more. So get ready to explore the wonders of Pella - you won’t be disappointed!

List of Places to Visit in Pella

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

1. Pella Archaeological Site

The Pella Archaeological Site is a must-visit destination for history buffs and archaeology enthusiasts. The site features the remains of an ancient city dating back to the Bronze Age, with ruins from the Roman, Byzantine, and Islamic periods. Highlights include the Columned Street, the Roman Theater, and the Great Church.

2. Pella Museum

Located near the archaeological site, the Pella Museum is a great place to learn more about the city’s history and culture. The museum features a wide range of artifacts, statues, and relics from the ancient city, providing insight into the daily life and customs of the people who once called Pella home.

Natural Attractions of Pella: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

1. Ajloun Forest Reserve

If you’re looking for a taste of Jordan’s stunning natural beauty, a visit to Ajloun Forest Reserve is a must. The reserve is home to a variety of hiking and biking trails that wind their way through lush forests, scenic valleys, and rocky ridges. Highlights include the Ajloun Castle, Soap House, and Ibn Battuta Trail.

2. Hammamat Ma’in Hot Springs

Located just a short drive from Pella, Hammamat Ma’in Hot Springs is a popular spot for relaxation and rejuvenation. The springs feature naturally heated mineral water that is said to have healing properties, making it a great destination for anyone looking to unwind after a long day of sightseeing.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Pella

Ajloun Castle Hike

This scenic hike takes you through the stunning Ajloun Forest Reserve and up to the historic Ajloun Castle, offering breathtaking views of the surrounding hills and valleys. The trail is well-marked and takes approximately 3-4 hours to complete round-trip.

Ibn Battuta Trail Bike Ride

This challenging bike ride takes you through remote Jordanian countryside, past ancient ruins and stunning natural features. The trail is approximately 40 kilometers long and includes both paved and unpaved sections, making it a great option for experienced cyclists.

Best Things to Do in Pella: Activities and Tours

Pella and Jerash Full-Day Tour

This full-day tour takes you through two of Jordan’s most historic and culturally rich cities, Pella and Jerash. Highlights include a visit to the Pella Archaeological Site, the Roman Theater in Jerash, and the Temple of Artemis.

Ajloun Forest Reserve Adventure

This half-day adventure takes you deep into the heart of the Ajloun Forest Reserve, where you can hike, bike, and explore to your heart’s content. Highlights include a visit to the Soap House and Hammamat Ma’in Hot Springs.

Food and Drink Scene of Pella

Must-Try Dishes

1. Mansaf

Mansaf is a traditional Jordanian dish made with lamb or goat meat, rice, and yogurt sauce. It is often served on special occasions and is a staple of local cuisine.

2. Falafel

A popular street food in Jordan, falafel is a deep-fried ball made from ground chickpeas, herbs, and spices. It is usually served in a pita bread with salad and tahini sauce.

Local Beverages

1. Tea

Tea is a staple beverage in Jordan and is often served with mint and sugar. It is a great way to warm up on a chilly day or to relax after a long day of sightseeing.

2. Arak

Arak is an anise-flavored liquor that is popular throughout the Middle East. It is usually served with water and ice, and is a great way to toast to new friends and experiences.

Where to Stay in Pella: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Guest House Um Khalil Al Baqaa

Located just a short walk from the Pella Archaeological Site, Guest House Um Khalil Al Baqaa is a cozy, family-run guest house that offers traditional Jordanian hospitality and comfortable accommodations. Rooms are air-conditioned and feature en-suite bathrooms, and the guest house also offers a traditional Jordanian breakfast.

Ajloun Hotel

The Ajloun Hotel is a modern and comfortable hotel located in the heart of Ajloun city. Rooms are spacious and feature air conditioning, en-suite bathrooms, and free Wi-Fi. The hotel also offers a restaurant and bar, as well as a 24-hour front desk.

What to Buy in Pella: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Olive Oil

Jordan is known for its high-quality olive oil, which is produced in the countryside surrounding Pella. Pick up a bottle or two to bring home and enjoy the flavors of Jordan even after you’ve returned home.

Handmade Crafts

Local artisans in Pella create a variety of handmade crafts, including jewelry, pottery, and textiles. These unique and beautiful items make great souvenirs and are a great way to support the local economy.

Local Culture and Customs of Pella

Jordan is a predominantly Muslim country, and visitors to Pella should dress modestly when out in public. It is also customary to remove your shoes before entering someone’s home or a mosque.

When dining in restaurants or cafes, be sure to ask for a table that is designated as non-smoking if you do not wish to be exposed to cigarette smoke. It is also customary to tip between 10-15% for good service.

Best Time to Visit Pella

The best time to visit Pella is during the spring (March to May) or fall (September to November) when temperatures are mild and the weather is pleasant for outdoor activities. Summers in Jordan can be quite hot, with temperatures reaching up to 40°C, so be sure to pack sunscreen and stay hydrated if visiting during this time.

Do’s and Don’ts of Pella

Do:

  • Dress modestly when out in public
  • Remove shoes before entering mosques or someone’s home
  • Try local dishes and beverages
  • Haggle when shopping in markets or souks

Don’t:

  • Public displays of affection are not common or accepted
  • Drink tap water without boiling it first
  • Photograph people without their permission
  • Overstay your visa

Safety and Health

Safety in Pella

Pella is a relatively safe city with low crime rates. However, as with any travel destination, visitors should take precautions to ensure their safety. This includes being aware of your surroundings, especially at night, and avoiding any areas that are known to be unsafe.

Health in Pella

Visitors to Pella should be up to date on routine vaccinations such as measles, mumps, and rubella, as well as hepatitis A and B. Drinking tap water in Jordan is not recommended, so be sure to stick to bottled water, especially during the summer months when the risk of dehydration is higher.

Getting to Pella: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Pella

The closest airport to Pella is Queen Alia International Airport in Amman, which is approximately 70 kilometers away. From there, visitors can take a taxi or rent a car to reach Pella.

Bus Stations Near Pella

The nearest bus station to Pella is in Ajloun city, which is approximately 15 kilometers away. From there, visitors can take a taxi or hire a car to reach Pella.

Other Options

Private car transfers and taxis are readily available in Jordan, and many visitors opt to hire a car for the duration of their trip. This provides flexibility and freedom to explore at your own pace.

How to Get Around in Pella

The best way to get around Pella is by car or taxi, as public transportation options are limited. Taxis are readily available and can be hailed on the street or booked in advance through a hotel or guesthouse.

Bicycle rental is also available in some areas, although the hilly terrain can be challenging for novice cyclists.

Where to Go Next from Pella: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Amman

Jordan’s vibrant and bustling capital city is a must-visit destination for any traveler. Highlights include the Citadel, Roman Theater, and Rainbow Street.

Dead Sea

The Dead Sea is a natural wonder located along the border between Jordan and Israel. Visitors can float in its salty waters, cover themselves in mineral-rich mud, and enjoy the stunning desert scenery that surrounds it.

Conclusion

Pella is a truly unique and special destination that offers a wealth of history, culture, and natural beauty. Whether you’re an avid history buff, outdoor adventurer, or simply looking for a relaxing getaway, Pella has something for everyone. So pack your bags, book your ticket, and get ready to explore the wonders of Pella!
