Introduction to Amman

Amman is the capital city of Jordan and is located in the northern part of the country. It’s a bustling city filled with rich culture, modern amenities, and historical sites. Amman is often used as a starting point for travelers visiting Jordan, due to its convenient location and extensive transport connections.

This city is also a great destination for tourists interested in learning about Middle Eastern culture and history, with many cultural attractions and historic landmarks. Whether you’re interested in exploring the city’s vibrant food scene, visiting ancient ruins, or simply soaking up the local culture, there’s something for everyone in Amman.

List of Places to Visit in Amman

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Amman has an extensive collection of museums and historical sites that showcase the country’s rich history and culture. Here are a few notable places to visit:

  • The Citadel: This historic site is considered to be one of the oldest continually inhabited places on earth. Located on top of a hill, The Citadel offers panoramic views of the city and a glimpse into the city’s history.

  • The Jordan Museum: This museum showcases Jordan’s history and cultural heritage through collections from the Paleolithic, the Bronze Age, and the Islamic period. There are also exhibits on Jordan’s flora and fauna, traditional costumes, and modern art.

  • The Roman Amphitheater: This well-preserved amphitheater is a great example of Roman architecture and was built during the 2nd century. The amphitheater could seat up to 6,000 people and is still used for cultural events and concerts.

Natural Attractions of Amman: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

For those who love the outdoors, Amman offers plenty of opportunities for adventures and scenic views. Don’t miss these natural attractions:

  • The Dead Sea: Located just outside of Amman, the Dead Sea is known for its high salt content, which makes it nearly impossible to sink. Visitors can float in its buoyant waters and experience its therapeutic qualities.

  • Wadi Rum: This desert valley is known for its stunning red sandstone formations that rise out of the desert floor. Visitors can go hiking, camping or take a tour through the desert in a jeep or camel.

  • Royal Botanic Garden: Situated around 10 kilometers outside of Amman, the Royal Botanic Garden contains miles of walking trails, stunning plant life, and beautiful water features. It’s an excellent place to escape the bustle of the city and experience nature.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Amman

Amman has some excellent hiking and biking routes that offer breathtaking views of the city and beyond. Here are a couple of the most popular routes:

  • The Jordan Trail: This 650-kilometer trail traverses through the length of the country and provides hikers with an excellent way to explore Amman’s surroundings. Visitors can choose to hike shorter sections of the trail if they don’t want to take on the whole route.

  • Mount Nebo: Located around 30 kilometers away from Amman, Mount Nebo is a popular destination for hikers and bikers. It’s the site where Moses is said to have seen the Promised Land and offers stunning views of the surrounding countryside.

Best Things to Do in Amman: Activities and Tours

Amman is full of interesting activities and tours to help you explore the city and its surroundings. Here are a few you shouldn’t miss:

  • Cooking Classes: Amman is famous for its cuisine, which blends local Middle Eastern flavors with international ingredients. Visitors can take cooking classes to learn how to make traditional Jordanian dishes like mansaf, a lamb and rice dish, and fattoush, a popular Middle Eastern salad.

  • Walking Tours: Joining a walking tour is a great way to explore the city’s impressive architecture and historic sites. Some notable tours include the downtown Amman street art tour and the Rainbow Street walking tour.

  • Day Trips: Amman is a great base for day trips to nearby destinations like Petra, one of the world’s most famous archaeological sites. The Dead Sea, Bethany Beyond the Jordan, and Jerash are all located within a day trip’s distance of Amman.

Week-Long Itinerary for Amman

Here is an itinerary for a week-long trip to Amman to help you plan your visit:

Day Activity
1 Visit the Citadel and the Jordan Museum
2 Take a cooking class or go on a walking tour
3 Explore the Roman Amphitheater
4 Spend the day hiking or biking in Wadi Rum
5 Relax and float in the Dead Sea
6 Take a day trip to Petra, Jerash or Bethany Beyond the Jordan
7 Visit the Royal Botanic Garden and enjoy the scenic views of Amman.

Food and Drink Scene of Amman

Jordanian cuisine is unique and full of flavor. Some of the must-try dishes include:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Mansaf: This traditional Jordanian dish consists of lamb cooked in fermented yogurt sauce and served with rice.

  • Hummus: Hummus is a smooth, creamy dip made with chickpeas, tahini, garlic, and lemon juice.

  • Falafel: A popular street food made from ground chickpeas or fava beans that are deep-fried to a crispy texture and served with tahini or spicy sauce.

Local Beverages

  • Arabic Coffee: This type of coffee is brewed using finely ground roasted coffee beans and flavored with cardamom or other spices.

  • Tea with Mint: A common drink in Amman is tea flavored with fresh mint leaves served hot or cold.

Where to Stay in Amman: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Amman offers a wide range of accommodation options to suit every budget. Here are a few notable places to stay:

  • InterContinental Amman: This luxurious five-star hotel is located in the heart of the city and offers a spa, multiple restaurants, and scenic views of Amman.

  • Sydney Hostel: A budget-friendly option in downtown Amman that offers air-conditioned rooms, free Wi-Fi, and a rooftop terrace.

  • Jordan Tower Hotel: This popular hostel provides basic amenities but has a stunning view of the Roman Amphitheater and is centrally located in Amman.

What to Buy in Amman: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Amman’s markets and bazaars are full of unique souvenirs and local products. Here are a few things to look out for:

  • Spices: The country’s proximity to the Middle East ensures you can buy some of the best and freshest spices in the world, such as sumac, saffron or za’atar.

  • Olive Oil: Jordan produces high-quality olive oil, which makes it a perfect gift for food lovers.

  • Handmade Souvenirs: It’s common to find handmade pottery, jewelry, and textiles sold by local artisans in Amman’s markets.

Local Culture and Customs of Amman

Jordan is a Muslim country, and it’s essential to be respectful of local customs and culture. Here are a few things to keep in mind:

  • Dress Modestly: Western tourists are expected to dress modestly when visiting Jordan. Females are required to cover their shoulders and knees in public.

  • Greet With Salaam: The locals greet visitors with a warm “salaam alaykum,” meaning “peace be upon you.” Respond with “wa alaykum salaam,” meaning “and peace be upon you.”

Best Time to Visit Amman

The best time to visit Amman is in the spring or autumn, with temperatures ranging between 15 and 25 degrees Celsius. Summer in Amman can be extremely hot, with temperatures reaching up to 40 degrees Celsius, while winter can be cold and rainy.

Do’s and Don’ts of Amman

Do:

  • Respect Local Customs: It’s essential to respect the local culture and customs, cover your shoulders and knees, and avoid public displays of affection.

  • Drink Plenty of Water: Amman can be hot and dry, so always keep yourself hydrated.

Don’t:

  • Carry Weapons: Jordan has strict gun laws, and it’s illegal to carry weapons without proper permits.

  • Drink Alcohol in Public: While alcohol is legal in Jordan, it’s not common to drink in public places. Stick to bars and restaurants when drinking.

Safety and Health

Safety in Amman

Overall, Amman is a safe city for tourists. The crime rate is low, and the city is well-policed. However, it’s always a good idea to take precautions, such as not walking alone late at night and keeping valuables secured.

Health in Amman

Tap water in Amman is generally safe to drink, but bottled water is widely available and recommended for visitors. It’s essential to protect yourself from the sun and stay hydrated during hot weather.

Getting to Amman: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Amman

The Queen Alia International Airport (AMM) is located around 30 kilometers south of Amman and serves as the country’s main airport. There are international flights connections to many countries worldwide and transporting to and from the airport can be arranged using taxis or the airport bus services.

Bus Stations Near Amman

The Abdali Bus Station is located at the heart of Amman, serving as the main bus station for intercity and international connections. There are regular bus routes to other major cities in Jordan, including Aqaba, Petra, and Jerash.

Other Options

Taxis are widely available and can be requested through hotels or picked up on the street. It’s essential to negotiate the fare with the taxi driver in advance. Alternatively, travelers can consider taking a private car rental for more flexibility.

How to Get Around in Amman

Getting around Amman is relatively easy, with many options available:

  • Taxis: Taxi cabs are widely available and can be flagged on the street or booked through your hotel. It’s essential to negotiate the fare in advance.

  • Buses: Public buses are available but can be crowded and confusing for visitors. The best option would be relying on Uber or Careem (Middle Eastern ride-hailing service)

  • Walking: Amman is a great city for walking, and many of the main attractions are located within walking distance from each other. Just take note that crossing streets in Amman can be hazardous due to aggressive driving habits.

Where to Go Next from Amman: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Jordan is a small country with many nearby destinations to explore. Here are a few other places to add to your itinerary:

  • Petra: Petra is a world-renowned archaeology site and is one of Jordan’s most famous destinations.

  • Dead Sea: The famous Dead Sea is a popular destination for relaxation and wellness, located just a short journey from Amman.

  • Jerash: This ancient city is located around 50 kilometers from Amman and offers visitors some of the most extensive and best-preserved Roman ruins in the world.

Conclusion

Amman is a rich and cultural city, a popular destination for travelers, offering lots of things to see and do. From historic landmarks to stunning natural parks, visitors of all ages will find something to enjoy in Amman. Use this guide to plan your trip and experience the best of this vibrant Middle Eastern city.
