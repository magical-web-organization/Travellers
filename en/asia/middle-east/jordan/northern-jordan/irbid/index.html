Introduction to Irbid

Irbid is a vibrant city located in the northwestern part of Jordan, known for its rich history, beautiful landscapes, and friendly inhabitants. The city, which was founded in the third century BC, has played a significant role in the region’s economic, political, and cultural life. Nowadays, Irbid is a thriving university town, home to several higher education institutions.

Irbid is often overlooked by travelers who are more interested in exploring Jordan’s iconic landmarks like Petra and the Dead Sea. However, Irbid has a lot to offer visitors, from culturally important attractions and natural wonders to exciting outdoor adventures and delicious local cuisine. This comprehensive tour guide will help you discover the best things to see and do in Irbid.

List of Places to Visit in Irbid

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

When it comes to cultural attractions in Irbid, there is no shortage of amazing places to explore. Start by visiting the following:

Jordan Museum of Natural History

Jordan Museum of Natural History is a remarkable museum that features an extensive collection of fossils, plants, and animals that are native to Jordan. The museum educates visitors about the region’s natural history and biodiversity.

Yarmouk University Museum

Yarmouk University Museum is located on the campus of Yarmouk University and is home to a vast collection of archeological artifacts, including mosaics, coins, and pottery.

Ajloun Castle

Ajloun Castle is an ancient fortress that was built in the 12th century by the Muslims to protect the region from the Crusaders. The castle is perched on top of a hill and offers breathtaking views of the surrounding countryside.

Natural Attractions of Irbid: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Irbid is blessed with stunning natural landscapes that attract outdoor enthusiasts from all over the world. Here are some of the best natural attractions to visit:

Irbid Nature Reserve

Irbid Nature Reserve is a beautiful nature reserve that covers over 80 square kilometers of land. The reserve is home to a variety of flora and fauna that can be seen by hiking or biking its many trails.

Ajloun Forest Reserve

Ajloun Forest Reserve is a vast forested area that covers over 13 square kilometers. The reserve is home to different tree species, including oak and pine. Visitors can enjoy hiking or mountain biking through the scenic forest trails.

Wadi Al-Yabis

Wadi Al-Yabis is a stunning valley that is perfect for hiking or enjoying a picnic while taking in the beautiful views. The valley is located about 20 kilometers from Irbid.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Irbid

If you enjoy hiking and biking, Irbid is the perfect destination. Here are some of the best hiking and biking routes:

Irbid Nature Reserve Loop

The Irbid Nature Reserve Loop is a 6-kilometer trail that takes you through the heart of the reserve. The trail offers spectacular views of the reserve’s diverse flora and fauna.

Ajloun Forest Reserve Trek

The Ajloun Forest Reserve Trek is a 10-kilometer trail that takes you through the beautiful Ajloun Forest Reserve. The trail offers stunning views of the forested landscape and is a must-do activity for nature lovers.

Jordan Bike Trail

For experienced mountain bikers, the Jordan Bike Trail is a challenging but rewarding adventure that takes you through some of Jordan’s most beautiful landmarks, including Irbid. The trail covers over 730 kilometers and can take up to 14 days to complete.

Best Things to Do in Irbid: Activities and Tours

In addition to the cultural and natural attractions, Irbid offers visitors a variety of fun activities and tours. Here are some of the best things to do in Irbid:

Visit the Local Markets

The local markets in Irbid are a must-visit for any traveler who wants to experience the city’s vibrant atmosphere. The markets offer a variety of goods, including traditional handicrafts, spices, and fresh produce.

Take a Cooking Class

Jordanian cuisine is flavorful and delicious, and taking a cooking class is an excellent way to learn how to make some of the country’s most popular dishes. Many local restaurants offer cooking classes that teach visitors how to make traditional Jordanian dishes like mansaf and maqluba.

Join a Guided Tour

Joining a guided tour is an excellent way to explore Irbid and its surroundings while learning about the region’s history, culture, and unique characteristics. There are many local tour operators that offer tours of the city and its surroundings, from outdoor adventures to cultural experiences.

Food and Drink Scene of Irbid

Jordanian cuisine is famous for its delicious and flavorful dishes, and Irbid is no exception. Here are some of the must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

Mansaf

Mansaf is Jordan’s national dish and is a must-try for any foodie visiting the country. The dish consists of lamb cooked in a fermented yogurt sauce and served with rice and almonds.

Falafel

Falafel is a popular street food in Jordan and is made from chickpeas that are ground into a paste, shaped into balls, and deep-fried. The balls are usually served in a pita bread with tahini sauce, vegetables, and pickles.

Maqluba

Maqluba is a delicious one-pot meal that consists of chicken or lamb, rice, and vegetables that are layered and cooked together. The dish is flipped over onto a serving platter, hence the name, which means “upside-down” in Arabic.

Local Beverages

Ayran

Ayran is a refreshing beverage that is made from yogurt, water, and salt. The drink is popular in Jordan, Turkey, and other parts of the Middle East.

Mint Tea

Mint tea is a traditional Jordanian beverage that is served hot or cold. The tea is made from fresh mint leaves, sugar, and black tea.

Where to Stay in Irbid: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Irbid offers a variety of accommodation options for visitors, from budget-friendly hostels to luxury hotels. Here are some of the best places to stay:

Yarmouk University Guesthouse

The Yarmouk University Guesthouse is located on the university campus and offers affordable accommodation for visitors. The guesthouse features comfortable rooms and modern amenities, including free Wi-Fi.

Beit Al Fannan

Beit Al Fannan is a boutique hotel that is located in the heart of Irbid. The hotel features elegant rooms and suites that are decorated with traditional Jordanian furnishings.

Zaki Suites

Zaki Suites is a modern hotel that offers spacious suites that are ideal for families or groups of travelers. The hotel features a rooftop terrace and a fitness center.

What to Buy in Irbid: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

When visiting Irbid, there are several local products and souvenirs that you can bring home as a reminder of your trip. Here are some of the best options:

Olive Oil Soap

Olive oil soap is a popular souvenir in Jordan, and Irbid is no exception. The soap is made from locally produced olive oil and is known for its moisturizing properties.

Handicrafts

Irbid is home to several skilled artisans who produce a variety of handicrafts, including pottery, rugs, and embroidery. These items make excellent souvenirs and are a great way to support the local economy.

Local Culture and Customs of Irbid

Jordan is a conservative country, and visitors should be aware of local customs and practices. Here are some of the most important things to keep in mind when visiting Irbid:

  • Dress conservatively when visiting religious sites or public places.
  • Avoid public displays of affection.
  • Respect local customs and traditions.
  • Ask for permission before taking photos of locals.

Best Time to Visit Irbid

The best time to visit Irbid is between April and May or September and November. During these months, the weather is mild, and the crowds are smaller than in the peak tourist season. Jordan can get scorching hot during the summer months, making outdoor activities unbearable.

Do’s and Don’ts of Irbid

Do:

  • Do try the local cuisine.
  • Do respect local customs and traditions.
  • Do dress modestly.
  • Do be aware of your surroundings at all times.

Don’t:

  • Don’t drink tap water, always drink bottled water.
  • Don’t take photos of military installations or government buildings.
  • Don’t offend locals with strong opinions or religious topics.

Safety and Health

Safety in Irbid

Irbid is a relatively safe city, and violent crime is uncommon. However, visitors should keep their belongings close to them and avoid walking alone at night in poorly lit areas.

Health in Irbid

Visitors to Jordan should have up-to-date vaccinations for Hepatitis A and B, typhoid, and tetanus. Travelers should also take precautions to avoid illness, such as drinking bottled water, avoiding street food, and taking insect repellent.

Getting to Irbid: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Irbid

The Queen Alia International Airport in Amman is the closest airport to Irbid. From there, visitors can take a taxi or bus to Irbid.

Bus Stations Near Irbid

Irbid has two bus stations, North and South. Buses run regularly from Amman to Irbid, and other parts of the country.

Other Options

Visitors can also rent a car to travel to Irbid, or hire a private driver.

How to Get Around in Irbid

Irbid has a public transportation system, including buses and taxis. Taxis are the most convenient way to get around the city.

Where to Go Next from Irbid: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Irbid is a great base for exploring other parts of Jordan, here are some nearby destinations:

Jerash

Jerash is located about 30 kilometers south of Irbid and is home to some of the best-preserved Roman ruins in the world.

Ajloun

Ajloun is a picturesque town that is located about 10 kilometers from Irbid. The town is home to the stunning Ajloun Castle and the Ajloun Forest Reserve.

Conclusion

Irbid is a hidden gem in Jordan that offers visitors a unique glimpse into the country’s history, culture, and natural beauty. Whether you’re interested in exploring ancient ruins, hiking through nature reserves, or experiencing traditional Jordanian cuisine, Irbid has something for everyone.
