Introduction to Umm Qais

Umm Qais is a small town located in northern Jordan, approximately 110km north of Amman. It is situated on a hill overlooking the Golan Heights and Lake Tiberias. The town is well-known for its ancient ruins, including the Roman city of Gadara, which was once one of the ten cities of the Decapolis. Umm Qais is also known for its stunning landscapes, traditional culture, and delicious food.

List of Places to Visit in Umm Qais

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

There are several culturally and historically significant attractions in Umm Qais that visitors shouldn’t miss:

  • Gadara: One of the ten cities of the Decapolis, Gadara was a well-known cultural and intellectual center during the Greco-Roman era. Visitors can explore the remains of this ancient city, including the theater, the Nymphaeum, the Street of Columns, and the basilica.
  • Umm Qais Museum: The Umm Qais Museum is located near Gadara and features a collection of artifacts from the region’s Greco-Roman and Ottoman periods.
  • The Ottoman Village: Umm Qais also has a well-preserved Ottoman village that provides a glimpse into the town’s recent history. Visitors can explore traditional houses, a mosque, and a school from this era.

Natural Attractions of Umm Qais: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Umm Qais is surrounded by beautiful landscapes and nature reserves. Some of the most popular natural attractions include:

  • Amra Reserve: Amra Reserve is a beautiful nature reserve with several hiking trails, wildlife, and stunning scenery.
  • Yarmouk River: The Yarmouk River is one of the most significant natural features in Umm Qais. Visitors can take a boat ride or simply relax by the river on a warm summer day.
  • Qais Mountain: Qais Mountain provides visitors with stunning views of the town and the surrounding landscapes. It is a great place to watch the sunset or sunrise.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Umm Qais

There are several hiking and biking routes in Umm Qais that visitors can explore. Some of the most popular ones include:

  • Gadara Trail: This is a moderate hiking route that takes visitors through the ruins of Gadara and offers stunning views of the surrounding landscapes.
  • Amra Reserve Trail: The Amra Reserve Trail is a more challenging hiking route that takes visitors through the beautiful Amra Reserve.
  • Pedal Power Route: The Pedal Power Route is a bike trail that takes visitors through the beautiful mountains surrounding Umm Qais. The trail is suitable for all levels of bikers and provides breathtaking views of the region.

Best Things to Do in Umm Qais: Activities and Tours

There are several activities and tours that visitors can enjoy in Umm Qais. Some of the best ones include:

  • Gadara Tour: A guided tour of the Gadara ruins is a must-do activity for any visitor. The tour provides visitors with a detailed insight into the town’s ancient history.
  • Wine Tasting: Umm Qais is known for its locally-produced wine, and visitors can take part in wine-tasting tours to sample some of the best wines in the region.
  • Cooking Classes: Visitors can also take part in traditional cooking classes to learn how to prepare local dishes such as mansaf and makloubeh.

Week-Long Itinerary for Umm Qais

Day 1: Visit the Gadara ruins and Umm Qais Museum. Day 2: Explore the Amra Reserve and go hiking. Day 3: Take a bike ride on the Pedal Power Route. Day 4: Relax by the Yarmouk River. Day 5: Visit the Ottoman village and learn about the town’s recent history. Day 6: Take part in a wine-tasting or cooking class. Day 7: Watch the sunrise or sunset from Qais Mountain.

Food and Drink Scene of Umm Qais

Must-Try Dishes

Umm Qais is famous for its traditional dishes, which are predominantly based on lamb, chicken, and rice. Some of the must-try dishes include:

  • Mansaf: Mansaf is considered the national dish of Jordan and is a staple of any Jordanian cuisine. It is made with lamb, rice, and a tangy yogurt sauce.
  • Makloubeh: Makloubeh is a popular Jordanian dish made with chicken, rice, and vegetables. It is cooked in a pot and then flipped over before serving.
  • Knafeh: Knafeh is a sweet pastry made with cheese, semolina, and syrup. It is a popular dessert in the Middle East and is often served during special occasions.

Local Beverages

Umm Qais is also known for its locally-produced wine, which is made from grapes grown in the surrounding hills. Visitors can try some of the best wines in the region by taking part in a wine-tasting tour. Other popular beverages include mint tea and Turkish coffee.

Where to Stay in Umm Qais: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Umm Qais has several accommodation options that cater to all budgets and preferences. Some of the best places to stay in the town include:

  • Umm Qais Hotel: Umm Qais Hotel is located near the ruins of Gadara and offers comfortable rooms with beautiful views of the surrounding landscapes.
  • Sama Umm Qais: Sama Umm Qais is a guesthouse located in the Ottoman village of Umm Qais. It provides guests with traditional accommodations and an authentic experience of local culture.
  • Ajloun Forest Reserve: The Ajloun Forest Reserve is located near Umm Qais and offers visitors a unique experience of nature and wildlife. It has several eco-lodges and cabins that provide all the necessary amenities for a comfortable stay.

What to Buy in Umm Qais: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

There are several souvenirs and local products that visitors can buy in Umm Qais, including:

  • Olive oil: Olive oil is produced in large quantities in Jordan, and visitors can buy some of the best olive oil in the region in Umm Qais.
  • Handmade pottery: Visitors can also buy traditional handmade pottery, which is made in the nearby village of Beit Raas.
  • Local honey: Jordan is famous for its high-quality honey, and visitors can buy some of the best honey in the region in Umm Qais.

Local Culture and Customs of Umm Qais

Umm Qais has a rich cultural heritage, and visitors should be aware of local customs and traditions. Some of the most important customs include:

  • Dress code: Visitors should dress modestly when visiting religious sites and in conservative areas.
  • Greetings: It is customary to greet people with the traditional Arabic greeting of “assalamu alaikum” (peace be upon you).
  • Hospitality: Jordanians are known for their hospitality, and visitors are often invited to share a meal or drink with locals.

Best Time to Visit Umm Qais

The best time to visit Umm Qais is during the spring and autumn seasons (March to May and September to November), when the weather is mild and pleasant. The summer months (June to August) can be quite hot, while the winter months (December to February) can be chilly and rainy.

Do’s and Don’ts of Umm Qais

Do:

  • Respect local customs and traditions.
  • Dress modestly when visiting religious sites.
  • Try the local cuisine and wine.
  • Take part in cultural activities and tours.

Don’t:

  • Show affection in public.
  • Wear revealing clothing.
  • Take photographs of people without their permission.
  • Hike or explore the region alone at night.

Safety and Health

Safety in Umm Qais

Umm Qais is generally a safe place to visit, and violent crime is rare. Visitors should take the usual precautions when traveling, such as keeping valuables out of sight and not walking alone at night.

Health in Umm Qais

The water in Umm Qais is safe to drink, but visitors should be aware that the town is located at high altitude, which may cause altitude sickness. Visitors with pre-existing medical conditions should consult their doctor before traveling.

Getting to Umm Qais: Transportation Options and Logistics

Umm Qais is located approximately 110km north of Amman and can be reached by several means of transportation:

Airports Near Umm Qais

The closest airport to Umm Qais is Queen Alia International Airport in Amman. From the airport, visitors can hire a taxi or rent a car to get to Umm Qais. The journey takes approximately three hours.

Bus Stations Near Umm Qais

There are several bus stations in Amman that offer services to Umm Qais. Visitors can take a Jett bus or a public minibus to get to the town. The journey takes approximately three hours.

Other Options

Visitors can also hire a private car or take a taxi to get to Umm Qais from Amman. The journey takes approximately two and a half hours.

How to Get Around in Umm Qais

The best way to get around Umm Qais is by hiring a taxi or a car. Visitors can also explore the town on foot or by bike.

Where to Go Next from Umm Qais: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

There are several nearby destinations and excursions that visitors can take from Umm Qais, including:

  • Irbid: Irbid is the third-largest city in Jordan and is located approximately 30km east of Umm Qais. It has several historical and cultural attractions, including the Islamic Museum and the Irbid Archaeological Museum.
  • Jerash: Jerash is located approximately 45km south of Umm Qais and is famous for its well-preserved Roman ruins, including the Temple of Artemis, the Hadrian’s Gate, and the Hippodrome.
  • Ajloun Castle: Ajloun Castle is a medieval Islamic fortress located approximately 35km southeast of Umm Qais. It was built in the 12th century and provides visitors with stunning views of the surrounding landscapes.

Conclusion

Umm Qais is a beautiful town in Jordan that offers visitors a unique experience of history, culture, and nature. Visitors can explore ancient ruins, hike through stunning landscapes, taste traditional dishes and wine, and learn about local customs and traditions. With this comprehensive tour guide, visitors will be able to plan their trip to Umm Qais and make the most of their time in this beautiful region of Jordan.
