Introduction to Salt

Welcome to Salt, a hidden gem located in the heart of Jordan! Salt is steeped in history and culture, with a rich heritage dating back to the 1800s. This vibrant city is situated just a short drive away from Amman and offers visitors a unique blend of old-world charm and modern attractions.

If you’re looking for a relaxing escape from the hustle and bustle of Amman, Salt is the perfect destination. This guide will provide everything you need to know about Salt, from the best things to do and see, to the best places to eat and stay.

List of Places to Visit in Salt

Salt boasts plenty of attractions and places to visit, ranging from culturally important historical sites to natural wonders and outdoor adventures.

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Salt is home to some of the most fascinating landmarks and historical sites in Jordan. Here are a few must-visit attractions:

  • Salt Archaeological & Folklore Museum: The museum showcases archaeological finds and traditional crafts and costumes from the region.
  • Abu Jaber Museum: This museum displays an impressive collection of traditional Bedouin clothing, jewelry, and artifacts.
  • Wadi Al-Seer Cultural Center: This center offers a glimpse into traditional Jordanian life, with exhibits featuring local handicrafts, pottery, and paintings.

Natural Attractions of Salt: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Salt is widely regarded as an excellent destination for outdoor activities and offers plenty of natural attractions and scenic views. Below are some of the best natural attractions to explore:

  • Wadi Al-Yabis: A scenic nature reserve located on the outskirts of the city, this is an ideal spot for hiking, picnicking, and birdwatching.
  • Salt Hill Nature Reserve: A popular destination for nature lovers, Salt Hill offers stunning views of the Jordan Valley and the Dead Sea.
  • Al-Athar Park: This beautiful park is home to a variety of plants and wildlife, and offers hiking trails, picnic areas, and stunning views of Salt and the surrounding area.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Salt

Salt is renowned for its phenomenal hiking and biking routes which offer scenic views of the surrounding areas. Below are some of the best hiking and biking trails to explore:

  • Wadi Al-Yabis hiking trail: This trail takes visitors past some of the most stunning natural sites in Salt, including cliffs, rocky landscapes, and lush vegetation.
  • Salt Hill biking trail: This trail offers a thrilling biking adventure, through rugged terrain and up steep hills, but rewards riders with breathtaking views of the city and surrounding area.

Best Things to Do in Salt: Activities and Tours

Salt offers visitors an array of activities and tours, catering to a variety of tastes and preferences.

  • City tour of Salt: Take a guided tour of the city’s historic sites and attractions, and learn about the city’s history, culture, and heritage.
  • Cooking class: Learn how to prepare traditional Jordanian dishes, such as Mansaf, from the local chefs.
  • Hiking trip to Salt Hill: Take a guided hiking trip to Salt Hill and be rewarded with beautiful views of the Jordan Valley and the Dead Sea.

Food and Drink Scene of Salt

Jordanian cuisine is renowned for its rich flavors, and Salt is no exception. Here are some of the must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Mansaf: The national dish of Jordan, traditionally served with lamb, rice, and yogurt sauce, and garnished with almonds and herbs.
  • Makloubeh: A traditional meat and rice dish, typically served with eggplant, cauliflower, and potatoes.
  • Kanafeh: A sweet pastry layered with cheese, topped with syrup, and garnished with pistachios.

Local Beverages

  • Arabic coffee: A strong, aromatic coffee typically served with dates or other sweet treats.
  • Tea with mint: A refreshing tea made with fresh mint leaves, typically enjoyed after a meal.

Where to Stay in Salt: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Salt offers visitors plenty of lodging options, ranging from luxury hotels to budget-friendly hostels and guesthouses. Here are some of the best places to stay in Salt:

  • Al Saleh Hotel: A modern hotel with comfortable rooms and excellent amenities, located in the heart of the city.
  • Coral Hotel: A boutique hotel with a beautiful rooftop terrace overlooking the city.
  • Orient Guesthouse: A traditional guesthouse that offers a unique cultural experience, with rooms decorated in the traditional Jordanian style.

What to Buy in Salt: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Salt is home to many local shops and markets where visitors can find artisanal products and unique souvenirs. Here are some of the best things to buy in Salt:

  • Traditional Jordanian clothing and jewelry, such as thobes and Bedouin-style earrings.
  • Handmade soap and skincare products made from Dead Sea minerals and natural ingredients.
  • Handwoven rugs and textiles, crafted using traditional weaving techniques.

Local Culture and Customs of Salt

Jordanian culture is heavily influenced by its Islamic heritage, and visitors should be mindful of local customs and practices. Here are some things to keep in mind:

  • Dress conservatively in public places, covering your arms and legs.
  • Take your shoes off when entering mosques, homes, and other cultural sites.
  • Refrain from public displays of affection.

Best Time to Visit Salt

The best time to visit Salt is during the shoulder seasons of spring (March-May) and fall (September-November), when the weather is mild, and the crowds are thinner. However, Salt can be visited year-round, with summer being the busiest season.

Do’s and Don’ts of Salt

Here are some important do’s and don’ts to keep in mind while visiting Salt:

Do:

  • Respect local customs and traditions.
  • Bargain at markets and shops, but do so politely and respectfully.
  • Try authentic Jordanian cuisine and local products.

Don’t:

  • Drink alcohol in public places, as it is prohibited by Islamic law.
  • Take photographs of people without their permission.
  • Disrespect local mosques and other cultural sites.

Safety and Health

Safety in Salt

Jordan is generally a safe country to visit, with low levels of crime and violence. However, visitors should take precautions such as:

  • Avoid political demonstrations and rallies.
  • Be mindful of your surroundings, particularly in crowded areas.
  • Beware of pickpocketing if you’re carrying valuables with you.

Health in Salt

Jordan has a modern healthcare system, with many hospitals and clinics available throughout the country, including Salt. Visitors should ensure that they have adequate travel health insurance, and be up-to-date on their vaccinations before traveling.

Getting to Salt: Transportation Options and Logistics

Salt is conveniently located near Amman, Jordan’s capital city, and can be easily accessed by several modes of transportation, including:

  • By car: Salt is located about 30 minutes’ drive from Amman, and visitors can easily rent a car or take a taxi to get there.
  • By bus: The JETT bus company operates a regular service between Amman and Salt, with buses departing from Abdali bus station.
  • By taxi: Visitors can also take a taxi from Amman to Salt, although it’s important to negotiate the fare beforehand.

Airports Near Salt

  • Queen Alia International Airport is the closest international airport to Salt, located about 45 kilometers (28 miles) from the city center.

Bus Stations Near Salt

  • Abdali bus station is a major bus station in Amman and offers regular bus services to Salt.

Other Options

  • Private transfers and tours can be arranged through travel agencies, hotels, or private car rental companies.

How to Get Around in Salt

Salt is a small and walkable city, and most of the attractions and sites can be easily explored on foot. Alternatively, visitors can rent a car or take a taxi to get around. Public transportation options in Salt are limited.

Where to Go Next from Salt: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Jordan offers visitors plenty of exciting destinations and attractions, located within easy reach of Salt. Here are some of the best places to visit next:

  • Amman: Jordan’s vibrant capital city is a must-visit destination, and is home to plenty of cultural and historical sites, as well as excellent restaurants and shopping.
  • The Dead Sea: This famous natural wonder is located just an hour’s drive from Salt, and offers visitors the chance to float in the mineral-rich waters and enjoy spa treatments.
  • Jerash: A fascinating ancient city located about an hour’s drive from Salt, Jerash boasts some of the best-preserved Roman ruins in the world.

Conclusion

Salt offers visitors a unique blend of history, culture, and outdoor adventure, making it a must-visit destination for anyone traveling to Jordan. With so much to see and do in Salt, visitors are sure to have an unforgettable experience!
