Introduction to Jerash

Located in Northern Jordan, Jerash is home to some of the most outstanding ruins from Roman times in the Middle East. Nicknamed the “Pompeii of the East,” Jerash is a must-visit destination for history and culture enthusiasts.

Jerash’s history is steeped in the Roman Empire, evident from the many historical remnants scattered across the city, including ancient columns, pillars, and temples that are well preserved in the city’s archeological site.

In addition to historical places, Jerash is renowned for its natural beauty, scenic vistas, and high-quality outdoor recreational activities, including hiking and biking trails that are suitable for all ages and fitness levels.

List of Places to Visit in Jerash

Visitors often come to Jerash for the archaeological site, but there’s much more to explore in this city. Here are some must-visit places to add to your itinerary:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  1. Jerash Archaeological Museum: This museum houses a large collection of artifacts and historical relics that date back to the Bronze Age and the Byzantine period.
  2. Hadrian’s Arch: Built-in 129 AD, this arch is one of the best-preserved Roman-era structures in Jerash.
  3. Temple of Artemis: Dedicated to Artemis, the goddess of fertility, this temple is still in fantastic condition because of its sturdy foundation of solid limestone blocks.
  4. Forum: The Forum was the center of the public life in Jerash. It was a market and meeting place, and several public events were held here.

Natural Attractions of Jerash: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  1. Ajloun Forest Reserve: This nature reserve is a great place to escape the hustle and bustle of the city and explore the nearby forest, as well as many hiking trails.
  2. Mount Nebo: Looking for scenic views? Mount Nebo is a perfect place to capture panoramic views of the Jordan Valley.
  3. Dibeen Forest Reserve: This reserve is characterized by its natural beauty, with its lush trees and green fields. It’s an ideal location for hikers and nature lovers.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Jerash

Jerash has plenty of opportunities for hikers and bikers. Here are some of the best trails to take:

  1. The Jordan Trail: The Jordan Trail is a 650 km alternative to explore Jordan’s diverse terrain, and it passes through through some fascinating sights in Jerash. This trail takes around 4-7 days and is considered difficult yet rewarding.
  2. The Olive Grove Trail: An easy and enjoyable route passing through olive groves.
  3. The Siq Trail: This trail takes hikers through stunning landscapes, including rock formations and wildflowers.

Best Things to Do in Jerash: Activities and Tours

  1. Tour of the ruins of Jerash: Take a leisurely stroll through the ancient city of Jerash and learn about its rich history and culture.
  2. Hot air balloon rides: For a breathtaking bird’s-eye view of the city, take an aerial tour of the traditional hot air balloons.
  3. Horseback riding: Explore the trails around Jerash on horseback to experience the area in a unique way.

Week-Long Itinerary for Jerash

Day 1: Arrive in Jerash and spend your first day exploring the ancient ruins of Jerash. Day 2: Take a guided tour of the Jerash Archaeological Museum and Hadrian’s Arch. Day 3: Visit the Temple of Artemis and have lunch at a local restaurant. Day 4: Take a hike in the Ajloun Forest Reserve and enjoy nature’s scenery. Day 5: Visit the Roman Theater and Forum, and then go shopping in Jerash’s markets. Day 6: Explore the Mount Nevo and the Dibeen Forest Reserve. Day 7: Take your pick of activities from hot air balloon rides to horseback riding.

Food and Drink Scene of Jerash

Must-Try Dishes

  1. Mansaf: A traditional Jordanian dish made of lamb, rice, and yogurt sauce.
  2. Musakhan: A savory chicken dish wrapped in pita bread and seasoned with onions, sumac, and pine nuts.
  3. Kibbeh: A deep fried croquette made of minced meat and bulgur.

Local Beverages

  1. Arak: An anise-flavored liquor that’s typically served with mezze.
  2. Sahlab: A warm milk-based drink that’s flavored with vanilla, sugar, and cinnamon.

Where to Stay in Jerash: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  1. The Olive Branch Hotel: A boutique hotel in the heart of Jerash, famous for its great food and warm hospitality.
  2. Tilal Al Rih Hotel: A five-star hotel in the Ajloun countryside with unique hilltop views.
  3. Hadrian’s Wall Guesthouse: A guesthouse near the ruins, with a rooftop terrace offering panoramic views.

What to Buy in Jerash: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  1. Mosaic artwork: Take home an attractive mosaic from one of the many shops found in the Souq.
  2. Dead Sea products: Purchase skincare products made of natural Dead Sea salts and minerals from the Souq.
  3. Jordanian sweets: From the famous traditional desserts such as the Baklava or Kunafa, this is a perfect souvenir for those with a sweet tooth.

Local Culture and Customs of Jerash

Jordan is predominately Muslim and takes religion sincerely. In Jerash and all of Jordan, it is advisable to dress modestly as a form of respect for their culture and religion.

When entering holy sites such as mosques and churches, one is expected to remove their shoes before entering, as a form of respect.

Best Time to Visit Jerash

The best time to visit Jerash is during the spring and autumn (April to May and September to November) when the weather is mild and dry.

Do’s and Don’ts of Jerash

Do:

  1. Dress modestly, especially when visiting religious sites to show respect for local customs.
  2. Bargain at the local markets, as it’s part of the culture.
  3. Taste the traditional food and explore local culture.

Don’t:

  1. Take photos of people without their permission.
  2. Drink tap water, stick to bottled water to avoid any waterborne diseases.

Safety and Health

Safety in Jerash

Jordan, in general, is a safe country, However, it’s always recommended to be mindful of surroundings and belongings, particularly in crowded markets.

Health in Jerash

Ensure to fill prescription medications before traveling to Jordan as some medication may be restricted. It’s also a good idea to bring a basic first-aid kit and take precautions to prevent mosquito bites.

Getting to Jerash: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Jerash

The closest international airport to Jerash is Queen Alia International Airport in Amman, the capital city of Jordan. Once arrived, it’s a 40-minute drive to Jerash.

Bus Stations Near Jerash

There are regular minibusses that run between Jerash and Amman, making it easy to travel to Amman or to other parts of Jordan.

Other Options

Taxis and private drivers are available but be sure to agree on a price before hiring.

How to Get Around in Jerash

It’s easy to get around Jerash’s attractions by walking, Taxi rides are also easy to use and readily available.

Where to Go Next from Jerash: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  1. Amman: the capital of Jordan is just an hour’s drive from Jerash, and there’s plenty to see and do, including visiting the Citadel and seeing the Roman Amphitheatre.
  2. Dead Sea: The Dead Sea is about an hour and a half drive from Jerash, that’s home to the world’s lowest point on land, and it’s famous for its mineral-rich water and mud.
  3. Madaba: Known as the city of mosaics, Madaba is a city 30km from Jerash that’s home to the famous Madaba Map.

Conclusion

With its captivating history, beautiful landscapes, and rich culture, Jerash is a city worth exploring. Whether you’re interested in hiking, shopping, or trying new foods, there’s something for everyone in this northern Jordanian gem. So why not plan your next trip to Jerash today and explore this beautiful city?
