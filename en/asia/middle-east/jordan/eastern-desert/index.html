Introduction to Eastern Desert

Eastern Desert is located in Jordan, in the Middle East, in Asia. It is a vast desert that covers over 75% of the country and runs from the southern border of Jordan eastward to the Iraqi desert. It is known for its stark and beautiful landscapes, unique wildlife, and fascinating historical and cultural sites. Tourists are attracted to its rugged terrain, picturesque vistas, and the chance to experience ancient cultures and civilizations that have shaped the region.

List of Places to Visit in Eastern Desert

Here are some of the top places to visit in Eastern Desert:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Petra: One of the most iconic and famous attractions in Jordan, Petra is an ancient city that was carved into rock over 2,000 years ago. It was once a thriving hub of trade, religion, and culture.
  • Qasr Kharana: An impressive Qusayr Amra-style desert castle, which believed to have been built in the early 8th century.
  • Wadi Rum: A beautiful and expansive desert valley with towering red sandstone cliffs that offer plenty of outdoor adventure possibilities.

Natural Attractions of Eastern Desert: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Dana Nature Reserve: A beautiful nature reserve that encompasses some of the most stunning landscapes in Jordan. It boasts a diverse range of flora and fauna, beautiful hiking trails and stunning panoramic views.
  • Amman: The bustling capital city of Jordan is a haven for nature and adventure lovers. Here, you can explore beautiful trails, discover scenic vistas, and indulge in a range of exciting outdoor activities.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Eastern Desert

Hikers and bikers will be spoilt for choice when it comes to exploring the vast wilderness of Eastern Desert. Here are some of the top routes to explore:

  • The Jordan Trail: The Jordan Trail is a lengthy route covering 700 km that passes through the most beautiful landscapes of Jordan, including Eastern Desert.
  • Wadi Mujib: Stroll through the beautiful Wadi Mujib as you explore the stunning waterfalls, hot springs and canyons.

Best Things to Do in Eastern Desert: Activities and Tours

If you’re looking for things to do in Eastern Desert, you won’t be disappointed. Here are some must-try activities to add to your itinerary:

  • Bedouin Camping: Experience a night in the desert, eat traditional Bedouin food, and take part in various cultural activities such as camel rides, shisha smoking and Arabian henna art.
  • Hot Air Balloon Ride: Take a hot air balloon ride and witness the stunning panoramic views of Eastern Desert from a bird’s eye angle.

Week-Long Itinerary for Eastern Desert

Here’s a week-long itinerary for exploring Eastern Desert:

  • Day 1: Visit Petra and explore the ancient ruins and temples
  • Day 2: Hiking and exploring the Wadi Rum Desert
  • Day 3: Visit Amman and, choose from the various activities
  • Day 4: Take a hot air balloon ride and travel to the King’s Highway
  • Day 5: Explore the Dana Nature Reserve and cycle through it
  • Day 6: Head toward Wadi Mujib and take a tour of the hot springs, canyons and waterfalls
  • Day 7: Return to Amman, roam around the city and relax.

Food and Drink Scene of Eastern Desert

Here are some must-try dishes and drinks to experience during your stay in Eastern Desert:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Mansaf: A traditional Jordanian dish made of lamb, rice and yogurt sauce.
  • Tabbouleh: A popular salad made of parsley, tomato and bulgur wheat.

Local Beverages

  • Arabic coffee: A traditional beverage served during occasions and events.
  • Tea with mint: A refreshing beverage that is widely consumed in Eastern Desert.

Local Culture and Customs of Eastern Desert

Jordanians are friendly and hospitable people. Some cultural protocols to take note of:

  • Dress modestly in public places
  • Remove your shoes before entering a mosque, religious or personal premises
  • Always greet someone with “Salam Alaikum” which means peace be upon you

Best Time to Visit Eastern Desert

The best time to visit Eastern Desert is from March to May as the temperature is mild, and the nature is at its most colorful, of wildflowers and blossoming trees.

Do’s and Don’ts of Eastern Desert

Do:

  • Be respectful of the local people’s beliefs and customs
  • Haggle, but do it politely

Don’t:

  • Do not drink or eat something in public during Ramadan
  • Do not walk into the mosque with shoes or dress revealing skin

Safety and Health

Safety in Eastern Desert

Eastern Desert is generally a safe place to visit. However, be aware of your belongings in markets and touristy areas.

Health in Eastern Desert

Visitor’s health in Eastern Desert are affected by the climate, hot and dry environment. Ensure staying hydrated and using sunscreen with high SPF.
