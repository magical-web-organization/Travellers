Qasr Amra, located in the Eastern Desert of Jordan, is a UNESCO World Heritage Site that is known for its historical significance and natural beauty. It is situated in the Middle East region of Asia and is a popular destination for tourists who want to explore the Middle Eastern culture and history. In this comprehensive travel guide, we will take you through an unforgettable journey of Qasr Amra, providing you with all the essential information you need to plan and make the most of your trip.

Introduction to Qasr Amra

Qasr Amra is a pre-Islamic castle that was built sometime during the early 8th century A.D. It is considered to be one of the best-preserved examples of early Islamic civilization and was once used as a resting place for travelers along the trade routes. The castle contains some of the best examples of ancient Islamic architecture and artwork, including frescoes and carvings, which can be viewed today.

List of Places to Visit in Qasr Amra

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Start your journey by exploring the ruins of the ancient castle, which are divided into three main sections: the courtyard, the baths, and the audience hall. The courtyard contains the remains of several storerooms and was once used as a market for traders. The baths were used by the castle’s inhabitants for relaxation and cleansing, and contain some of the most beautiful frescoes in the castle. The audience hall was used for governmental functions and is decorated with exquisite carvings and paintings.

The castle’s museum is another must-visit attraction, which showcases the history of the castle and its significance in early Islamic civilization. It contains several artifacts, including pottery, jewelry, and metalwork.

Natural Attractions of Qasr Amra: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

The Eastern Desert, where Qasr Amra is located, is known for its stunning natural beauty. Visitors can enjoy scenic views of the desert landscape and explore the numerous hiking trails in the surrounding area. For those seeking more adventure, camel rides are popular in the area, and you can experience the desert’s vastness and beauty while riding on one of these majestic animals.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Qasr Amra

Hiking and biking are the best ways to explore the natural beauty of the Eastern Desert. There are several hiking trails around the castle that take you through the desert’s pristine landscapes. Some of the popular hiking and biking routes include:

  • Umm al Amad trail
  • Umm Al-Jamal trail
  • Petra trail

Best Things to Do in Qasr Amra: Activities and Tours

In addition to hiking and biking, visitors can indulge in several other activities and tours, including:

  • Camel rides
  • Horseback riding

These activities allow visitors to experience the desert’s beauty and quench their thirst for adventure.

Food and Drink Scene of Qasr Amra

Jordan is known for its delicious cuisine, and Qasr Amra is no exception. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages to indulge in during your trip:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Mansaf (Jordan’s national dish)
  • Sfiha (meat pies)
  • Maqluba (upside-down chicken and rice)

Local Beverages

  • Ayran (yogurt drink)
  • Sahlab (a hot drink made from orchid roots)
  • Tea with mint

Local Culture and Customs of Qasr Amra

Jordan is a predominantly Muslim country, and it is important to respect the local culture and customs. Visitors should dress modestly and remove their shoes before entering mosques or homes.

Best Time to Visit Qasr Amra

The best time to visit Qasr Amra is during the spring and fall seasons (March to May and September to November). During these times, the weather is mild, and the crowds are fewer. The summer months (June to August) can be scorching, and the winter months (December to February) can be chilly, especially at night.

Do’s and Don’ts of Qasr Amra

Do:

  • Respect the local culture and customs
  • Dress modestly
  • Ask for permission before taking photographs of people
  • Bargain while shopping

Don’t:

  • Drink alcohol in public places
  • Show affection in public places
  • Enter mosques or homes in shoes

Safety and Health

Safety in Qasr Amra

Jordan is a safe country to travel, and the crime rate is low. However, it is always recommended to take common-sense safety precautions like do not leave valuable items unattended, avoid walking alone at night, and take a licensed taxi rather than walking in quiet areas.

Health in Qasr Amra

Travelers should ensure that they are up to date with routine vaccinations before visiting Jordan. It is also recommended to take precautions against mosquito bites and to carry a basic first aid kit.

Getting to Qasr Amra: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Qasr Amra

Queen Alia International Airport in Amman is the nearest airport to Qasr Amra.

Bus Stations Near Qasr Amra

The JETT bus company runs regular services between Amman and Qasr Amra.

Other Options

Taxis are also available, but the prices can vary, and it is always recommended to negotiate the price before starting the journey.

How to Get Around in Qasr Amra

The best way to get around Qasr Amra and its surrounding areas is to hire a car or take a taxi. Alternatively, bicycles are available for rent in some areas, and guided tours are also popular.

Where to Go Next from Qasr Amra: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Petra (one of the New Seven Wonders of the World)
  • Wadi Rum (a vast desert landscape)
  • Amman (the capital of Jordan)

Conclusion

Qasr Amra is a hidden gem in the Eastern Desert of Jordan, offering visitors a unique blend of culture, history, and natural beauty.
