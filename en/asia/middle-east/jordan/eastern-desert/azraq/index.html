Introduction to Azraq

Nestled in the heart of Eastern Desert in Jordan, Azraq is a city steeped in history, culture and natural beauty. Renowned for its serene oasis, this tranquil town is an ideal destination for history buffs, naturalists and adventure enthusiasts alike. From historic forts and museums to outdoor adventures and scenic nature trails, Azraq has something to offer for everyone.

Food and Drink Scene of Azraq

Must-Try Dishes

  • Mansaf: a traditional Jordanian dish made of lamb or chicken, rice, and Jameed (dried yogurt)
  • Zarb: a Bedouin-style meat and vegetable dish cooked underground
  • Kebbeh: a dish made of cracked wheat, minced onions, and ground meat

Local Beverages

  • Tea: is the most popular drink in Jordan
  • Arak: an alcoholic anise-flavored liquor
  • Sadeeq: a cool refreshing drink made with lemon and mint

Local Culture and Customs of Azraq

In Azraq, the locals are very hospitable and will welcome visitors with open arms. Modest clothing is appreciated, especially when visiting religious sites. It is also customary to remove shoes before entering a home.

Best Time to Visit Azraq

The best time to visit Azraq is from March to May and September to November when the weather is mild and pleasant.

Do’s and Don’ts of Azraq

Do:

  • Dress modestly especially at religious sites
  • Try the traditional cuisine
  • Respect the local culture and customs

Don’t:

  • Drink alcohol in public places
  • Be disrespectful to the locals
  • Leave trash or litter in public places

Safety and Health

Safety in Azraq

Azraq is generally a safe place to visit, but it is always important to be cautious of your surroundings and belongings.

Health in Azraq

It is recommended to take necessary vaccinations before traveling to Jordan. It is also important to carry sunscreen and bug repellent while in Azraq.
