Introduction to Bur Dubai

Bur Dubai is a historic district situated in the western part of Dubai in the United Arab Emirates. It is a hub of culture, history, and entertainment, with its diverse range of attractions, such as museums, souks, and ancient buildings. This travel guide will provide you with a comprehensive overview of everything that Bur Dubai has to offer.

List of Places to Visit in Bur Dubai

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Dubai Museum: Dubai museum traces the history of Dubai from the pre-oil era to the present day. It is located in the Al Fahidi Fort, which dates back to the 18th century.
  • Sheikh Saeed Al Maktoum House: This is the former residence of the former ruler of Dubai, Sheikh Saeed Al Maktoum. The building has now been turned into a museum showcasing cultural and historical exhibits.
  • Al Bastakiya: This is a historic district in Bur Dubai with traditional Arabic-style houses, narrow lanes, and old wind towers. It is a great place to experience the traditional culture of Dubai.

Natural Attractions of Bur Dubai: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Dubai Creek: This is a natural saltwater creek that separates the city into two parts, Bur Dubai and Deira. You can take a traditional Abra boat ride across the creek and enjoy the beautiful views of the city skyline.
  • Dubai Miracle Garden: Dubai Miracle Garden is the largest flower garden in the world, displaying over 45 million flowers. It is a great place to visit for nature lovers.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Bur Dubai

  • Al Mamzar Beach Park: This is a huge park with a beautiful beach, where you can rent bicycles and ride along the designated bike paths.

Best Things to Do in Bur Dubai: Activities and Tours

  • Desert Safari: A desert safari is a must-do activity in Dubai. You can book a tour to explore the sand dunes, ride camels, and try sandboarding.
  • Dubai Mall: Dubai Mall is the largest mall in the world with over 1200 stores, an aquarium, an ice rink, and a cinema. It is a great place to shop, dine and entertain yourself.

Food and Drink Scene of Bur Dubai

Must-Try Dishes

  • Shawarma: A wrap stuffed with chicken, meat, or veggies with tahini sauce.
  • Falafel: A deep-fried ball made of chickpeas, herbs, and spices.
  • Harees: A traditional porridge made of wheat and meat.

Local Beverages

  • Arabic coffee: A strong, bitter coffee flavored with cardamom.
  • Tea: A sweet and spicy tea made with cinnamon, cardamom, and other spices.

Where to Stay in Bur Dubai: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • XVA Art Hotel: A boutique hotel located in the heart of Al Bastakiya with an art gallery and a rooftop terrace.
  • Arabian Courtyard Hotel & Spa: A popular hotel with traditional Arabic-style rooms, a rooftop pool, and a spa.
  • Backpacker 16 Hostel: A budget-friendly hostel with comfortable dorms and private rooms.

What to Buy in Bur Dubai: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Dates: A staple food of the Middle East.
  • Spices: A vast selection of spices, including saffron, sumac, and za’atar, are available in the souks.
  • Textiles and clothing: Traditional Arabic clothing and textiles, including silk scarves and woven rugs, can be found in the souks.

Local Culture and Customs of Bur Dubai

The United Arab Emirates is a Muslim country, and visitors should be aware of the cultural customs and practices. Dress modestly, and refrain from public displays of affection. Additionally, alcohol is only available in licensed establishments.

Best Time to Visit Bur Dubai

The best time to visit Bur Dubai is from November to April as the weather is mild and pleasant.

Do’s and Don’ts of Bur Dubai

Do:

  • Respect local customs and Islamic culture.
  • Dress modestly.
  • Carry a scarf or shawl to cover your head or shoulders when visiting mosques.

Don’t:

  • Drink or eat in public during Ramadan.
  • Publicly display affection.

Safety and Health

Safety in Bur Dubai

Bur Dubai is a safe district, and crime rates are low. However, travelers should always be aware of their surroundings and take precautions in crowded areas.

Health in Bur Dubai

The UAE has excellent health facilities, and visitors can receive quality medical care. Additionally, travelers should take some precautions, such as staying hydrated and protecting themselves from the sun.

Getting to Bur Dubai: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Bur Dubai

  • Dubai International Airport: Located in Garhoud, it is a 15-minute drive from Bur Dubai.

Bus Stations Near Bur Dubai

  • Al Ghubaiba Bus Station: Located in Bur Dubai, it is the main bus station for the district, connecting it to other parts of the city.

Other Transportation Options

  • Dubai Metro: The Dubai Metro is a fast and efficient way to get around the city. There are two metro stations in Bur Dubai.
  • Taxis: Taxis are widely available and reasonably priced.

How to Get Around in Bur Dubai

The best way to get around Bur Dubai is on foot or by taking an abra boat across Dubai Creek. Taxis and public buses are also available.

Where to Go Next from Bur Dubai: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Deira: Deira is another historic district in Dubai, located on the eastern side of Dubai Creek.
  • Abu Dhabi: Abu Dhabi, the capital of the UAE, is only a two-hour drive from Dubai and is home to iconic landmarks such as the Sheikh Zayed Grand Mosque and Ferrari World.

Conclusion

From its rich cultural heritage to its scenic beauty, Bur Dubai has something to offer for everyone. Whether you are a history buff or an adventure seeker, this district is worth exploring. With this travel guide, we hope you can plan your trip to Bur Dubai and make the most of your visit.
