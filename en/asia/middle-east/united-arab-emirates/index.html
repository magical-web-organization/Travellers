United Arab Emirates (UAE) is a vibrant country located in the Middle East, in Asia, known for its world-class infrastructure, rich history, and modern lifestyle. From the towering skyscrapers of Dubai’s cityscape to the serene environment of Abu Dhabi, the country offers a wide range of attractions for all visitors. Whether you’re interested in learning about the local culture, outdoor adventures, or simply relaxing on the beach, UAE has something for you. In this comprehensive guide, we’ll explore the best places to visit, things to do, where to eat, and how to travel to and around the country.

Introduction to United Arab Emirates

United Arab Emirates is a country located in the south-eastern part of the Arabian Peninsula. It consists of seven emirates: Abu Dhabi, Dubai, Sharjah, Ajman, Fujairah, Ras Al Khaimah, and Umm Al Quwain. The country has a rich history, dating back to over 7,000 years, with cultural influences from Arabic, Persian, and Islamic traditions. The official language spoken is Arabic, but English is widely spoken as well. UAE is famous for its modern architecture, luxury hotels, high-end shopping, and vibrant nightlife.

List of Places to Visit in United Arab Emirates

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

UAE has a rich cultural history, and visiting the museums and historical places provides an excellent opportunity to learn about it.

  • Sheikh Zayed Grand Mosque: An iconic landmark, the mosque is located in Abu Dhabi and is one of the largest mosques in the world. Visitors can tour the mosque and learn about Islamic culture and history.
  • Al Ain Oasis: A UNESCO World Heritage site, Al Ain Oasis is a beautiful palm grove that highlights UAE’s traditional agricultural practices. Visitors can take a guided tour and explore the oasis.
  • Dubai Museum: Located in the Al Fahidi Fort, Dubai Museum showcases the history and development of Dubai. Visitors can explore the various exhibits and get a glimpse of the past.
  • Al Fahidi Historical District: Located in Dubai, the Al Fahidi Historical District is a preserved historical neighborhood that provides a glimpse of traditional Arabic architecture and culture.

Natural Attractions of United Arab Emirates: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

UAE has diverse natural landscapes, ranging from the golden sand dunes to the tranquil waters of the Arabian Gulf.

  • Dubai Miracle Garden: A masterpiece of design and creativity, Dubai Miracle Garden boasts a vibrant display of over 45 million flowers. Visitors can take a leisurely walk and enjoy the mesmerizing floral arrangements.
  • Hajar Mountains: The Hajar Mountains are a popular spot for hiking and are located in the eastern part of the UAE. Visitors can take a guided hiking tour and experience the scenic views of the mountains.
  • Desert Safari: A visit to UAE would be incomplete without experiencing the thrill of a Desert Safari. Visitors can take a guided tour and enjoy activities such as dune bashing, camel riding, and sandboarding.
  • Jebel Jais: Located in Ras Al Khaimah, Jebel Jais is the highest peak in UAE, standing at 1,934 meters. Visitors can enjoy activities such as hiking, zip-lining, and camping.

Hiking/Biking Routes of United Arab Emirates

UAE has several scenic hiking and biking trails that offer visitors an opportunity to explore the natural beauty of the country.

  • Dubai Creek Trail: A 10-kilometer trail that winds along Dubai Creek, the trail offers visitors a chance to explore the city’s history and culture.
  • Hatta Mountain Biking Trail: Located in Hatta, the trail is a challenging 52-kilometer dirt track that provides breathtaking views of the Hajar Mountains.
  • Wadi Shawka Trail: Located in Ras Al Khaimah, the trail is an 8-kilometer hike that offers scenic views of the Wadi Shawka valley.

Best Things to Do in United Arab Emirates: Activities and Tours

There are many activities and tours to choose from in UAE, from adventure sports to luxury experiences.

  • Burj Khalifa Observation Deck: Visit the world’s tallest building and take in the stunning views of Dubai. Visitors can choose from various ticket options.
  • Luxury Yacht Cruise: Relax and unwind on a luxury yacht cruise, where visitors can enjoy stunning views of Dubai’s skyline and indulge in gourmet food and drinks.
  • Ski Dubai: Located in the Mall of Emirates, Ski Dubai is an indoor ski resort that offers year-round skiing and snowboarding. Visitors can also enjoy snow parks and Toboggan runs.
  • Ferrari World Abu Dhabi: A unique amusement park, Ferrari World Abu Dhabi is dedicated to the Ferrari brand and its history. Visitors can enjoy the thrilling rides and attractions.

Week-Long Itinerary for United Arab Emirates

Here is a sample week-long itinerary that covers the top attractions in UAE:

Day 1: Visit Sheikh Zayed Grand Mosque in Abu Dhabi.

Day 2: Explore Dubai Mall and take in the views from Burj Khalifa Observation Deck.

Day 3: Take a Desert Safari and experience the thrill of dune bashing.

Day 4: Visit Al Ain Oasis and take a guided tour.

Day 5: Explore the cultural district in Sharjah.

Day 6: Relax on the beach in Fujairah.

Day 7: Enjoy the outdoor activities in Jebel Jais.

Food and Drink Scene of United Arab Emirates

UAE has a diverse food culture, with influences from Arabia, Persia, and India. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Machboos: A traditional Emirati dish made with spiced rice, meat, and vegetables.
  • Shawarma: A popular Middle Eastern dish made with meat that is shaved from a spit, served in a wrap or pita bread with vegetables and sauces.
  • Falafel: A deep-fried patty made from ground chickpeas, served in a wrap or pita bread with vegetables and sauces.

Local Beverages

  • Karak tea: A popular tea made with black tea, sugar, and spices, originally from India.
  • Laban: A traditional drink made from yogurt and water, sometimes flavored with mint, salt, or spices.

Where to Stay in United Arab Emirates: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

UAE has a wide range of accommodations, from luxury hotels to budget-friendly options.

  • Luxury Hotels: Burj Al Arab in Dubai, Emirates Palace in Abu Dhabi, and Waldorf Astoria Ras Al Khaimah.
  • Budget-Friendly Options: Rove Downtown Dubai, Queen Elizabeth 2 in Dubai, and The Old Quarter Hostel in Sharjah.

What to Buy in United Arab Emirates: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Here are some souvenirs and local products to bring home as a reminder of your trip to UAE:

  • Dates: UAE is famous for its fresh and dried dates, which can be bought at any local market.
  • Arabic Perfume: UAE has a long history of producing traditional Arabic perfumes.
  • Carpet and Textiles: UAE has a rich textile heritage, and visitors can buy handmade carpets and textiles as souvenirs.

Local Culture and Customs of United Arab Emirates

UAE has a traditional Islamic culture, and visitors are expected to respect the customs and traditions of the country. Here are some tips to keep in mind:

  • Clothing: Visitors should dress modestly, covering their shoulders and knees, especially when entering mosques or other religious sites.
  • Ramadan: Ramadan is a holy month in Islam, and during this time, visitors should refrain from eating, drinking, or smoking in public areas.
  • Public Displays of Affection: Public displays of affection are frowned upon in UAE and should be avoided.

Best Time to Visit United Arab Emirates

The best time to visit UAE is between November and April when the weather is pleasant and cooler. During the summer months, the temperature can reach up to 50 degrees Celsius, making it unbearable for most visitors.

Do’s and Don’ts of United Arab Emirates

Do:

  • Respect local culture and customs.
  • Carry cash, as small markets and restaurants might not accept credit cards.
  • Take off shoes when entering mosques or homes.

Don’t:

  • Drink alcohol in public places.
  • Wear revealing clothing in public areas.
  • Show public displays of affection.

Safety and Health

Safety in United Arab Emirates

UAE is a safe country for visitors, with low crime rates. However, visitors should be aware of the local laws and customs and avoid any controversial topics in public.

Health in United Arab Emirates

UAE has a high standard of healthcare, with both public and private hospitals. Visitors should have comprehensive health insurance and take necessary vaccinations and precautions.

Getting to United Arab Emirates: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near United Arab Emirates

  • Dubai International Airport
  • Abu Dhabi International Airport
  • Sharjah International Airport

Bus Stations Near United Arab Emirates

  • Al Ghubaiba Bus Station in Dubai
  • Abu Dhabi Central Bus Station
  • Fujairah Central Bus Station

Other Options

  • Taxis: Taxis are readily available in UAE at a reasonable fare.
  • Car Rental: Visitors can also rent a car to explore the country.

How to Get Around in United Arab Emirates

UAE has a well-developed transportation system, and visitors can choose from various options:

  • Metro: Dubai and Abu Dhabi have a metro system that covers major tourist destinations.
  • Bus: Buses are a cheaper option for visitors and cover most of the major routes.
  • Taxi: Taxis are readily available in UAE at a reasonable fare.

Where to Go Next from United Arab Emirates: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

UAE is a great starting point for exploring other destinations in the Middle East. Here are some nearby destinations to consider:

  • Oman: Located on the southeast coast of the Arabian Peninsula, Oman offers a unique blend of history, culture, and natural beauty.
  • Qatar: Qatar is known for its modern architecture and rich culture, with Doha being a must-visit destination.
  • Jordan: Jordan is famous for its ancient ruins, including the lost city of Petra, and the stunning desert landscapes of Wadi Rum.

Conclusion

United Arab Emirates is an exciting destination that offers a unique blend of ancient culture and modern city living. With so much to see and do, visitors can be assured of an unforgettable experience. Plan your trip carefully, respect local customs and traditions, and enjoy all that UAE has to offer.
