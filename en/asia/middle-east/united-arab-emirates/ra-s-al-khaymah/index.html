Introduction to Ra’s al Khaymah

Ra’s al Khaymah, often shortened to RAK, is one of the seven emirates of the United Arab Emirates, located in the northern part of the country. Although it is the fourth-largest emirate, it is the least populous, making it a great destination for travelers who prefer a more laid-back and tranquil atmosphere compared to the bustling cities of Dubai and Abu Dhabi.

RAK is known for its stunning natural beauty, rich cultural heritage, and adventure opportunities. It’s a must-visit destination for anyone looking to experience the authentic side of the UAE.

List of Places to Visit in Ra’s al Khaymah

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • National Museum of Ras Al Khaimah - showcases the history and culture of RAK, featuring exhibits on the pearl trade, traditional handicrafts, and archaeological discoveries.
  • Dhayah Fort - a 16th-century fort that is perched on top of a hill, offering panoramic views of the surrounding mountains and the sea.
  • Jebel Jais - the highest mountain peak in the UAE, offering plenty of hiking trails, scenic viewpoints, and adventure activities.

Natural Attractions of Ra’s al Khaymah: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Al Wadi Equestrian Adventure Centre - a luxury horse-riding resort that offers guided tours of the desert, mountain trails, and beach.
  • Wadi Shawka - a beautiful canyon surrounded by rugged mountains, offering hiking trails, swimming pools, and picnic spots.
  • Jebel Jais Flight - the world’s longest zipline, stretching over 2.5 km across the Jebel Jais mountains.

Best Things to Do in Ra’s al Khaymah: Activities and Tours

  • Desert Safaris - experience the traditional Bedouin lifestyle by going on a desert safari, which includes dune bashing, camel riding, belly dancing performances, and a buffet dinner.
  • Pearl Farm Tour - learn about the pearl trade that once flourished in the UAE through a guided tour of a traditional pearl farm.
  • Dhow Cruise - sail on a traditional wooden boat along the Ras Al Khaimah coastline, enjoying a barbecue dinner and live entertainment.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Ra’s al Khaymah

  • Jebel Jais Via Ferrata - a challenging hiking trail that includes rock climbing, zip-lining, and abseiling, offering breathtaking views of the mountains and valleys.
  • Jebel Jais Hike - a moderate hiking trail that takes you to the peak of Jebel Jais, where you can enjoy the stunning sunset views.
  • Ras Al Khaimah Corniche - a scenic coastal walkway that stretches for 4 km, featuring stunning views of the Arabian Gulf and the mangrove forests.

Week-Long Itinerary for Ra’s al Khaymah

Day 1: National Museum of Ras Al Khaimah and Dhayah Fort Day 2: Al Wadi Equestrian Adventure Centre and Wadi Shawka Day 3: Jebel Jais Flight and Jebel Jais Hike Day 4: Pearl Farm Tour and Dhow Cruise Day 5: Desert Safari and Jebel Jais Via Ferrata Day 6: Ras Al Khaimah Corniche and Beach Day Day 7: Relaxation Day or Visit nearby destinations like Fujairah or Umm al Quwain

Food and Drink Scene of Ra’s al Khaymah

Must-Try Dishes

  • Machboos - a traditional Emirati rice dish that is cooked with chicken or fish, tomatoes, and spices.
  • Shawarma - a Middle Eastern wrap that is filled with marinated chicken or beef, vegetables, and tahini sauce.
  • Luqaimat - a sweet Emirati dessert that is made of fried dough balls, drizzled with date syrup.

Local Beverages

  • Ayran - a yogurt drink that is tangy and refreshing, perfect for hot weather.
  • Jallab - a sweet drink made of rose water, dates, and grape molasses.

Where to Stay in Ra’s al Khaymah

RAK offers a wide range of accommodation options, from luxury resorts and hotels to budget guesthouses and hostels. Some popular choices include:

  • Waldorf Astoria Ras Al Khaimah - a luxury resort that offers stunning views and upscale amenities.
  • Hilton Al Hamra Beach & Golf Resort - a family-friendly resort that features a private beach, golf course, and water sports activities.
  • Camping at Jebel Jais - an adventurous option that allows you to camp on the highest mountain peak in the UAE, enjoying the stunning sunrise and sunset views.

What to Buy in Ra’s al Khaymah

Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Pearls - RAK was once a major hub for the pearl trade, and you can still find high-quality pearls at local shops.
  • Spices - the UAE is famous for its spices, and RAK offers a wide range of aromatic spices that are perfect for cooking or gifting.
  • Handicrafts - the traditional handicrafts of RAK, such as pottery, embroidery, and weaving, make for unique souvenirs.

Local Culture and Customs of Ra’s al Khaymah

  • Islam is the official religion of the UAE, and most locals are practicing Muslims. Therefore, visitors are expected to dress modestly and respect local customs.
  • Arabic is the official language, but English is widely spoken and understood.
  • Fridays are considered a holy day, and many businesses and attractions may be closed, so plan accordingly.

Best Time to Visit Ra’s al Khaymah

The best time to visit RAK is between November and April, when the weather is mild and pleasant. During the summer months, the temperature can soar to over 40°C, making outdoor activities uncomfortable.

Do’s and Don’ts of Ra’s al Khaymah

Do:

  • Dress modestly, particularly in more conservative areas.
  • Learn a few Arabic phrases and greetings.
  • Respect local customs, such as removing shoes before entering homes or mosques.

Don’t:

  • Show public displays of affection.
  • Drink alcohol in public places.
  • Take photographs of locals without their permission.

Safety and Health

Safety in Ra’s al Khaymah

RAK is a relatively safe destination, with low crime rates and a stable political climate. However, visitors should still exercise caution and take basic safety measures, such as keeping valuable belongings secure and being aware of their surroundings.

Health in Ra’s al Khaymah

The healthcare facilities in RAK are modern and well-equipped, but medical treatment can be expensive for visitors without travel insurance. Visitors should also be aware of the strong sun and take measures to protect themselves from heatstroke and dehydration.

Getting to Ra’s al Khaymah: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Ra’s al Khaymah

  • Ras Al Khaimah International Airport - the main airport in RAK, serving domestic and international flights.
  • Dubai International Airport - located around 90 minutes’ drive from RAK, serving flights from major airlines worldwide.

Bus Stations Near Ra’s al Khaymah

  • RAK Bus Station - the main bus terminal in RAK, offering services to Dubai and other emirates.
  • Al Hamra Bus Station - a smaller bus station located near the coast, offering services to other parts of RAK.

Other Options

  • Private Transfer - travelers can book a private transfer from Dubai or Abu Dhabi to RAK, which offers greater convenience and flexibility.
  • Car Rental - rental cars are widely available in RAK, and offer a convenient way to explore the emirate and nearby destinations independently.

How to Get Around in Ra’s al Khaymah

Public transportation options in RAK are limited, and visitors may find it more convenient to rent a car or take taxis. Alternatively, visitors can book tour packages that include transportation and guided tours.

Where to Go Next from Ra’s al Khaymah: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Fujairah - a neighboring emirate that is known for its beaches, historic forts, and cultural attractions.
  • Umm Al Quwain - one of the smallest emirates in the UAE, offering wildlife parks, water parks, and traditional fishing villages.
  • Musandam - an exclave of Oman that is known for its stunning fjords and aquatic activities, such as snorkeling and diving.

Conclusion

RAK may be a lesser-known emirate of the UAE, but it’s an underrated gem that offers a unique blend of nature, culture, and adventure. Whether you’re interested in exploring the rugged mountains, learning about the local history and customs, or simply relaxing on the beach, RAK has something for everyone. With this comprehensive travel guide, you’re well-equipped to plan an unforgettable trip to RAK.
