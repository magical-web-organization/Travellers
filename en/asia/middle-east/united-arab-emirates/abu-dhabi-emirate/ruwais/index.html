Introduction to Ruwais

Nestled in the heart of the Abu Dhabi emirate in the United Arab Emirates, Ruwais is a small coastal town that offers a unique travel experience to tourists from around the world. Ruwais has a rich culture that is steeped in tradition and history, making it an ideal destination for those seeking to experience the local Emirati way of life.

List of Places to Visit in Ruwais

Whether you’re interested in exploring the town’s rich cultural heritage or taking in the stunning natural scenery, Ruwais has something for everyone. Here are some of the top places to visit in Ruwais:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Ruwais Museum: This museum showcases the town’s history and heritage, including the oil and gas industry that has played a vital role in the town’s development.

  • Jebel Dhanna Fort: This restored fort gives visitors a glimpse into the town’s historical past and the role that forts played in protecting it from invaders.

  • Zayed Library: Named after the founding father of the UAE, Sheikh Zayed, this library houses a collection of books and manuscripts related to the country’s history and culture.

Natural Attractions of Ruwais: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Jebel Hafit: Located about an hour and a half away from Ruwais, Jebel Hafit is one of the highest peaks in the UAE and offers breathtaking views of the surrounding desertscape.

  • Marawah Marine Biosphere Reserve: This protected area is home to a diverse range of marine life, including sea turtles and dugongs. Visitors can take a boat tour to explore the area and learn about the local ecosystem.

  • Habshan-Ruwais Recreational Park: This park is the perfect spot for a picnic or outdoor adventure. Visitors can enjoy a variety of activities, including hiking, biking, and camping.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Ruwais

Ruwais’s picturesque landscape and natural beauty make it a popular destination for hiking and biking enthusiasts. Here are some of the best routes to explore:

  • Jebel Hafit Hiking Trail: This 12-kilometer trail takes visitors up to the summit of Jebel Hafit and offers stunning panoramic views of the surrounding landscape.

  • Al Gharbia Cycling Track: This 34-kilometer track takes visitors through some of the most beautiful areas of Ruwais, including the coastal regions and natural parks.

Best Things to Do in Ruwais: Activities and Tours

From cultural experiences to outdoor adventures, there are plenty of things to do in Ruwais. Here are some of the top activities to consider on your trip:

  • Visit a Camel Farm: Take a tour of a traditional Emirati camel farm and learn about the history and culture of camel rearing in the UAE.

  • Dune Bashing: Take an exhilarating ride over the sand dunes in a 4x4 vehicle and experience the thrill of off-roading.

  • Traditional Emirati Cooking Class: Learn how to prepare traditional Emirati dishes, such as machboos and luqaimat, and enjoy a delicious meal with local hosts.

Week-Long Itinerary for Ruwais

If you’re planning to spend a week in Ruwais, here’s a sample itinerary to help you make the most of your trip:

Day 1: Visit the Ruwais Museum and explore the surrounding areas on foot.

Day 2: Take a boat tour of the Marawah Marine Biosphere Reserve and enjoy a picnic lunch in the park.

Day 3: Explore Jebel Hafit and take the hiking trail to the summit for stunning panoramic views.

Day 4: Participate in a traditional Emirati cooking class and enjoy a delicious meal with local hosts.

Day 5: Visit the Jebel Dhanna Fort and learn about the town’s rich history and heritage.

Day 6: Go dune bashing in the nearby desert and enjoy a traditional Bedouin dinner under the stars.

Day 7: Relax and unwind at the Habshan-Ruwais Recreational Park and enjoy the beautiful natural scenery.

Food and Drink Scene of Ruwais

No trip to Ruwais is complete without indulging in the local cuisine. Here are some of the must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Machboos: This spicy rice dish is made with meat or fish and is flavored with a blend of traditional Emirati spices.

  • Luqaimat: These delicious little dumplings are fried and then drizzled with honey syrup.

Local Beverages

  • Karak Chai: This sweet, milky tea is a staple in Emirati cuisine and is usually served with a side of dates.

  • Jellab: This refreshing drink is made from a blend of grape molasses, rose water, and other spices.

Where to Stay in Ruwais: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

There are a variety of accommodation options in Ruwais to suit every budget and preference. Here are some of the top options:

  • Ruwais Housing Complex: This complex is operated by the Abu Dhabi National Oil Company and offers affordable accommodation for workers and their families.

  • Anantara Qasr al Sarab Desert Resort: This luxury resort is located a short distance from Ruwais and offers stunning views of the surrounding desert landscape.

  • Dhayah Fort Guest House: This historic fort has been restored and converted into a guest house, offering guests a unique and authentic Emirati experience.

What to Buy in Ruwais: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

If you’re looking for the perfect souvenir or gift to bring home, Ruwais has a variety of options to choose from. Here are some ideas:

  • Dates: The UAE is famous for its high-quality dates, which make a perfect gift to bring home.

  • Traditional Clothing and Accessories: Visit one of the local markets to purchase traditional Emirati clothing or jewelry.

  • Spices and Sweets: Bring home a taste of Emirati cuisine with a selection of spices or traditional sweets.

Local Culture and Customs of Ruwais

Emirati culture is steeped in tradition and is characterized by hospitality, family values, and respect for elders. Visitors should be aware of some of the customs and etiquette practices when visiting Ruwais, such as dressing modestly and removing shoes before entering a home or mosque.

Best Time to Visit Ruwais

The best time to visit Ruwais is during the winter months (November to March), when temperatures are cooler and more pleasant for outdoor activities. Summers can be extremely hot and humid, with temperatures soaring to over 40 degrees Celsius.

Do’s and Don’ts of Ruwais

When visiting Ruwais, it’s important to be aware of some of the customs and etiquette practices. Here are some do’s and don’ts to keep in mind:

Do:

  • Dress modestly, especially when visiting religious sites or public places.

  • Practice proper hygiene, especially during the COVID-19 pandemic.

  • Greet locals with a polite “As-salamu alaykum” (peace be upon you).

Don’t:

  • Drink alcohol in public or in places that do not have an alcohol license.

  • Display public affection, such as hugging or kissing, in public places.

  • Photograph locals without their permission.

Safety and Health

Ruwais is a relatively safe place to visit, with low crime rates and a friendly community. However, visitors should still take precautions and practice common sense safety measures.

Safety in Ruwais

  • Familiarize yourself with the local emergency services and contact numbers.

  • Be aware of your surroundings, especially in tourist areas or crowded places.

  • Avoid traveling alone at night or in isolated areas.

Health in Ruwais

  • Check with your doctor for any necessary vaccinations or health precautions before traveling.

  • Practice proper hygiene, such as washing your hands frequently and maintaining social distancing.

  • Drink bottled water or use a water purifier to avoid getting sick from tap water.

Getting to Ruwais: Transportation Options and Logistics

Ruwais is relatively remote, but still accessible via a variety of transportation options. Here are some of the ways to reach Ruwais:

Airports Near Ruwais

  • Abu Dhabi International Airport: This is the closest major airport to Ruwais, located around 240 kilometers away.

  • Dubai International Airport: This airport is located around 280 kilometers away from Ruwais.

Bus Stations Near Ruwais

  • Al Ghubaiba Bus Station: This station operates buses to and from Ruwais regularly.

  • Abu Dhabi Central Bus Station: This station also offers bus services to and from Ruwais.

Other Options

  • Private Car Rental: Renting a car is a great option if you want to explore the surrounding areas and have more flexibility in your travel plans.

  • Taxi Services: Taxis are widely available in the UAE and can be hired for a specific journey or for a whole day.

How to Get Around in Ruwais

The easiest way to get around Ruwais is by car or taxi. There are also several bike and car rental companies available in the town for those who prefer to explore on their own.

Where to Go Next from Ruwais: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

If you have some extra time to explore the region, there are several nearby destinations and excursions worth considering, including:

  • Abu Dhabi City: The capital city of the UAE is around a two-hour drive from Ruwais and offers a range of cultural and tourist attractions.

  • Al Ain: Known as the “Garden City” for its lush greenery, Al Ain is located around 180 kilometers from Ruwais and features a variety of historical and natural tourist attractions.

Conclusion

Ruwais is a hidden gem in the UAE, offering visitors a unique cultural experience and stunning natural scenery. Whether you’re interested in hiking and biking, exploring the local history and heritage, or simply relaxing in beautiful surroundings, Ruwais has something for everyone. Plan your trip today and discover the charm and beauty of this special place.
