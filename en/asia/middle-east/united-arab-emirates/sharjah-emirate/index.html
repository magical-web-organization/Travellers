Introduction to Sharjah (Emirate)

Sharjah is one of the seven emirates of the United Arab Emirates and is located on the east coast of the Arabian Peninsula. Often overshadowed by its neighboring emirates, Sharjah has been working hard to establish itself as a tourist destination for those seeking a more traditional experience of the UAE. Sharjah is known for its rich history, culture, and beautiful coastline offering a variety of activities and attractions for visitors to explore. In this travel guide, we will take you through the best places to visit, things to do, and where to stay in Sharjah.

List of Places to Visit in Sharjah (Emirate)

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Sharjah is known for being the cultural capital of the UAE, with an abundance of museums and historical sites to visit:

Sharjah Museum of Islamic Civilization

This museum houses over 5,000 artifacts from around the Islamic world, ranging from intricate ceramics, textiles, and coins to mosaics and manuscripts. The museum provides insight into the Islamic faith, culture, and history and is an excellent place to understand Sharjah’s past and present.

Sharjah Archaeology Museum

This museum showcases Sharjah’s rich archaeological heritage, including relics from the Bronze and Iron Ages. A vast collection of pottery from the 3rd millennium BC can be seen, and the museum also has a 4,000-year-old scroll of linen.

Sharjah Art Museum

The Sharjah Art Museum displays an extensive collection of contemporary and traditional art from renowned regional and international artists. The museum hosts various exhibitions and programs throughout the year and is an excellent visit for art aficionados.

Sharjah Heritage Area

The Heritage Area takes visitors back in time to the early 1950s, where they can witness how Sharjah’s inhabitants lived at that time. The area has several historic buildings, including the Sharjah Fort, which houses a museum exhibiting local artifacts.

Natural Attractions of Sharjah (Emirate): Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Sharjah has scenic views of the Gulf of Oman and the stunning Hajar Mountains, offering numerous outdoor activities:

Al Majaz Waterfront

Al Majaz Waterfront is a beautiful landscaped park that includes several restaurants, playgrounds, and a fountain display. From here, visitors can take a boat ride around the lagoon and enjoy the stunning view of Sharjah’s skyline.

Mleiha Archaeological Center

Mleiha Archaeological Center is a must-visit attraction for outdoor enthusiasts who want to experience the emirate’s fascinating natural history. Visitors can explore the archaeological sites of Mleiha and witness the remnants of ancient civilizations, including pre-Islamic tombs and settlements.

Kalba

Kalba’s nature reserve is located at the mouth of the wadi Khor Kalba, near the border with Oman. The area is home to several rare species, such as the Arabian leopard and sea turtles, and also has fantastic hiking trails and camping sites.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Sharjah (Emirate)

Sharjah’s Hajar Mountains are an adventurer’s paradise, offering some of the best hiking and biking options:

Jebel Al Jais

Jebel Al Jais is UAE’s highest peak, standing at 1,934 meters height. The hiking trail is challenging, but it rewards hikers with a scenic view of the Arabian Gulf and the surrounding mountains.

Wadi Shees

Wadi Shees is a famous hiking and mountain-biking location in Sharjah, offering breathtaking views of the Hajar Mountains. The wadi has several trails, each with varying difficulties, making it accessible for people of all levels.

Best Things to Do in Sharjah (Emirate): Activities and Tours

Sharjah has plenty of activities and tours to keep visitors busy:

Visit Souks in Sharjah

The Central Souk is one of the city’s most popular markets, filled with traditional handicrafts, textiles, and jewellery, perfect for souvenir shopping. For the spice lovers, The Blue Souk has a massive array of spices, herbs, and fragrances on display.

Participate in a Desert Safari

Desert Safaris are an immensely popular activity when visiting Sharjah. Visitors can experience the undulating dunes while dune bashing or embracing the desert’s tranquillity on a camel ride under the open sky.

Week-Long Itinerary for Sharjah (Emirate)

Day 1-2: Explore Sharjah’s cultural side, visiting Sharjah Museum of Islamic Civilization, Sharjah Art Museum, and Sharjah Heritage Area.

Day 3: Head to the Al Majaz Waterfront and take a boat ride to enjoy the stunning view of Sharjah’s skyline.

Day 4-5: Experience nature at its best with a visit to the Kalba nature reserve and Mleiha Archaeological Center.

Day 6-7: Try hiking in one of the mountains, like Jebel Al Jais, and participate in a Desert Safari adventure.

Food and Drink Scene of Sharjah (Emirate)

Must-Try Dishes

Machboos

Machboos is a dish cooked with saffron-infused rice, vegetables, and meat, topped with fried onions and cashews. It’s a delicacy that has its roots in Bedouin-style cooking.

Harees

Harees is a traditional Emirati dish made with meat and wheat. It’s cooked overnight until the mixture becomes smooth and creamy.

Luqaimat

Luqaimat is a dessert made of dough balls, fried and then drizzled with sugar syrup or honey. It’s an excellent way to end a feast.

Local Beverages

Karak Tea

Karak tea is a popular tea beverage made with black tea, milk, and saffron. It’s a staple drink in the UAE and perfect for a refreshing break during sightseeing.

Laban

Laban is a refreshing drink made with buttermilk and salt. It’s a refreshing drink to beat the summer heat.

Where to Stay in Sharjah (Emirate): Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Sharjah offers various options to its visitors, ranging from luxury hotels to budget-friendly guesthouses:

Emirates Palace Hotel

Emirates Palace is a luxury hotel located in Sharjah’s Gulf of Oman. The hotel offers world-class amenities and boasts a private beach, making it perfect for a relaxing stay.

Aldar Hotel

Aldar Hotel is a budget-friendly option for those visiting Sharjah. The hotel is located in the heart of the city and offers comfortable and clean rooms for an affordable stay.

Sharjah Heritage Youth Hostel

Sharjah Heritage Youth Hostel is a budget-friendly option for backpackers traveling to Sharjah. The hostel is located in the heart of the city and offers clean and comfortable dormitory rooms.

What to Buy in Sharjah (Emirate): Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Gold and Jewelry

The Gold Souk in Sharjah has abundant displays of gold and other precious stones. It’s an excellent place to purchase some traditional jewelry to take back home.

Spices and Dried Fruits

The local souks in Sharjah offer a vast variety of spices and dried fruits. These are great to bring back home for culinary enthusiasts to enjoy cooking with them.

Local Culture and Customs of Sharjah (Emirate)

Sharjah is a conservative city, and visitors are expected to dress appropriately, especially when visiting religious sites. Visitors should avoid wearing shorts, short skirts, or sleeveless tops. Alcohol is banned in Sharjah, and visitors should refrain from drinking in public places. Additionally, visitors should maintain decorum when interacting with locals, respecting their customs and traditions.

Best Time to Visit Sharjah (Emirate)

The best time to visit Sharjah is during the winter months from December to February, when the weather is mild. Summers in Sharjah are scorching, and visitors should avoid visiting during this time.

Do’s and Don’ts of Sharjah (Emirate)

Do:

  • Dress conservatively, especially when visiting religious sites.
  • Respect local customs and traditions.

Don’t:

  • Drink alcohol in public places.
  • Wear revealing clothing.

Safety and Health

Safety in Sharjah (Emirate)

Sharjah is a safe city compared to other cities in the region. However, visitors should be vigilant, especially when traveling alone at night.

Health in Sharjah (Emirate)

Sharjah has excellent healthcare facilities, and visitors can find clinics and hospitals with English-speaking medical professionals.

Getting to Sharjah (Emirate): Transportation Options and Logistics

Sharjah is a bustling city and has various transportation options:

Airports Near Sharjah (Emirate)

Sharjah International Airport is the nearest airport located a few kilometers from the city center. There are several direct flights from major cities around the world to Sharjah.

Bus Stations Near Sharjah (Emirate)

Sharjah has several bus stations that connect it to other emirates and cities around the UAE. The Central Bus Station is the primary bus station in Sharjah.

Other Options

Visitors can also hire a car or take a taxi to explore the city at their leisure.

How to Get Around in Sharjah (Emirate)

Sharjah has various transportation options to get around the city, including taxis, buses, and car rentals. However, the best way to explore Sharjah is on foot, especially when visiting the cultural landmarks and souks.

Where to Go Next from Sharjah (Emirate): Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Sharjah is well connected to its neighboring emirates, and visitors can explore other nearby destinations like Dubai and Abu Dhabi.

Conclusion

Sharjah is a gem waiting to be discovered by visitors to the United Arab Emirates. It boasts a rich cultural heritage and natural beauty, making it an excellent destination for those seeking an authentic experience of the UAE. We hope this comprehensive travel guide has helped you plan your trip to Sharjah, and we wish you an enjoyable visit.
