Introduction to Yazd (Province)

Located in Central Iran, Yazd province is a desert paradise, with a rich history and culture that dates back thousands of years. Yazd city, the provincial capital, is known for its stunning architecture, unique wind-catchers, and ancient Zoroastrian heritage.

List of Places to Visit in Yazd (Province)

There are plenty of cultural, natural, and historical attractions to discover in Yazd province. Here are some of the top places to visit:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  1. The Jame Mosque of Yazd: One of the most important Islamic structures in Iran, the Jame Mosque of Yazd is built in the traditional four-iwan style and features intricate tile work and a stunning blue-tiled dome.

  2. The Yazd Atash Behram: This Zoroastrian temple houses one of the nine Atash Behrams (sacred fires) in Iran and is the most important Zoroastrian temple in Yazd.

  3. The Dowlat Abad Garden: One of the most beautiful traditional Persian gardens in Iran, the Dowlat Abad Garden features a gorgeous pavilion, a central fountain, and tall cypress trees.

  4. The Water Museum: Learn about the ancient qanats (underground aqueducts) of Yazd and how they have helped sustain life in the desert for millennia.

Natural Attractions of Yazd (Province): Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  1. The Kharanagh Village: This abandoned village with its ancient mud-brick buildings and winding alleys makes for a unique day trip and a chance to see the beauty and harshness of desert life.

  2. The Varzaneh sand dunes: These stunning sand dunes offer a surreal and breathtaking scenery, perfect for adventurous travelers who want to sandboard or take in the sunset views.

  3. The Meybod Caravanserai: The historic caravanserai located on the old Silk Road trade route between Yazd and Isfahan, showcases the rich history of Iran and the famous trade routes that once crisscrossed the region.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Yazd (Province)

Yazd province is also popular for its stunning natural landscapes and outdoor activities. Here are some of the top hiking/biking routes:

  1. The Safaieh Mountain: This scenic route is an arduous but rewarding hike that rewards hikers with stunning panoramic views of Yazd’s desert and mountains.

  2. The Taft River Trail: This easy and peaceful trail passes by a number of historic sites and landmarks, including the Kharanaq Castle and the shrine of Khizr.

Best Things to Do in Yazd (Province): Activities and Tours

  1. Take a traditional cooking class and learn how to prepare authentic Persian dishes like qorme sabzi and ash-e reshteh.

  2. Visit the local bazaars and souks to buy traditional handicrafts and textiles, including handmade carpets, kilims, and silk.

  3. Join a desert safari and explore the remote corners of the desert, discovering hidden oases and wildlife along the way.

Week-Long Itinerary for Yazd (Province)

Day 1: Explore the historic sites, museums, and ancient buildings of Yazd city.

Day 2: Take a day trip to the nearby Kharanaq village and explore its ancient alleys and abandoned buildings.

Day 3: Learn about the rich history of the Zoroastrian community in Iran by visiting the Atash Behram, the Towers of Silence, and the Fire Temple.

Day 4: Visit the Varzaneh sand dunes and enjoy an unforgettable sunset experience in the desert.

Day 5: Take a cooking class and learn how to make traditional Persian dishes.

Day 6: Join a desert safari and explore the remote corners of the desert, discovering hidden oases and wildlife along the way.

Day 7: Relax and unwind at one of the many traditional Persian gardens in Yazd, such as the dowlat abad garden.

Food and Drink Scene of Yazd (Province)

The food in Yazd province is as unique and exotic as its culture and history. Here are some must-try dishes:

Must-Try Dishes

  1. Qorme Sabzi: A flavorful stew made with fresh herbs (parsley, cilantro, spinach, and chives), lamb, and kidney beans.

  2. Ash-e Reshteh: A hearty soup made with lentils, beans, chickpeas, herbs, and noodles.

  3. Fesenjan: A sweet and savory stew made with pomegranate molasses, chicken, and walnuts.

Local Beverages

  1. Doogh: A refreshing and healthy yogurt drink, flavored with mint and cucumber.

  2. Sharbat: A sweet and sour fruit juice, often made with sour cherry or pomegranate.

What to Buy in Yazd (Province): Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Yazd is famous for its unique handicrafts and textiles, such as:

  1. Handmade carpets and kilims

  2. Traditional Persian textiles, such as silk and brocade

  3. Pashmak (Persian fairy floss)

  4. Termeh (handwoven Persian fabric)

Local Culture and Customs of Yazd (Province)

Yazd has a rich and unique culture, with a long history of intermingling of various groups, including Arab, Persian, and Zoroastrian communities. The Zoroastrian heritage of Yazd is particularly significant, with many ancient traditions and customs still preserved in the region.

Best Time to Visit Yazd (Province)

The best time to visit Yazd province is from October to March when temperatures are milder and more pleasant.

Do’s and Don’ts of Yazd (Province)

Do:

  1. Respect the rich traditions and customs of the Zoroastrian community.

  2. Dress conservatively, particularly when visiting religious shrines and monuments.

  3. Try the local cuisine and traditional food of Yazd province.

Don’t:

  1. Take photographs of people without their permission.

  2. Visit religious sites during prayer times without permission.

  3. Engage in public displays of affection.

Safety and Health

Safety in Yazd (Province)

Yazd province is a relatively safe place for travelers, with low crime rates and a friendly local population.

Health in Yazd (Province)

Visitors are recommended to drink bottled water and take precautions to avoid heatstroke and dehydration, particularly during the summer months.

Where to Go Next from Yazd (Province): Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  1. Isfahan: A historic city famous for its stunning architecture and beautiful Persian gardens.

  2. Shiraz: A cultural and historical center of Iran, with many famous landmarks, such as the ancient city of Persepolis.

  3. Kerman: A beautiful desert city with a rich history and many cultural attractions, such as the historic Ganjali Khan Complex and the Shah Nematollah Vali Shrine.

Conclusion

Yazd province is a unique and beautiful desert destination that offers a rich history, culture, and natural beauty to travelers from all over the world. From exploring historic sites and museums to hiking in the desert and sampling local cuisine, there is something for everyone in Yazd.
