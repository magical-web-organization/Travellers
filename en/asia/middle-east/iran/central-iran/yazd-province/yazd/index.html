If you’re planning a trip to Iran, make sure to include the city of Yazd in your itinerary. Located in the heart of the country and surrounded by desert, Yazd is a unique and historically rich city with plenty to see and do. In this travel guide, we’ll take a closer look at the best places to visit, activities to do, and where to eat and stay during your trip to Yazd.

Introduction to Yazd

Yazd is often referred to as the “City of Windcatchers” due to its traditional buildings with innovative wind towers that naturally ventilate the buildings. Yazd is also a UNESCO World Heritage site and home to many Zoroastrian temples – Iran’s pre-Islamic religion. Yazd has an arid climate and temperatures can be extreme during the summer months, so it’s best to visit between October and April.

List of Places to Visit in Yazd

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Amir Chakhmaq Complex: A beautiful complex that houses a mosque, bazaar, caravanserai, and bathhouse. It is known for its stunning facade and impressive night views.
  • Dolat Abad Garden: A UNESCO World Heritage site and one of the oldest buildings in Yazd. The central mansion has a beautiful wind tower and a picturesque garden.
  • Zoroastrian Fire Temple: A Zoroastrian temple where a sacred fire has been burning for over 1,500 years.

Natural Attractions of Yazd: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Kharanaq and Meybod: Two small historic towns on the outskirts of Yazd with many old buildings and abandoned structures, including a caravanserai, a mosque, a pigeon tower, an icehouse, and a mudbrick tower.
  • Chak Chak: A Zoroastrian shrine located in a mountain near Yazd. The shrine is believed to be the site where the daughter of the last Zoroastrian ruler sought refuge from Arab invaders.
  • Zeinoddin Caravanserai: A restored caravanserai between Yazd and Kerman. It dates back to the 16th century and has been turned into a hotel for visitors.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Yazd

  • Towers of Silence: Old Zoroastrian burial sites located on top of hills. A great spot for a sunrise or sunset hike or bike ride.
  • Garmeh Oasis: A desert oasis located about 90 km south of Yazd. Surrounded by palm trees, the village also has hot springs and mud volcanoes.

Best Things to Do in Yazd: Activities and Tours

  • Explore the old city: Get lost through the winding alleys and enjoy the architecture.
  • Take a cooking class: Learn how to cook traditional Persian dishes such as khoresh bademjan and ghormeh sabzi.
  • Visit a Zoroastrian temple: Learn about the history and traditions of this ancient religion.
  • Befriend the locals: Iranians are known for their hospitality, so don’t be afraid to strike up a conversation with locals in a teahouse, park or bazaar.

Week-Long Itinerary for Yazd

  • Day 1: Visit the Amir Chakhmaq Complex and explore the old town.
  • Day 2: Visit the Dolat Abad Garden and the Zoroastrian Fire Temple in the morning. In the afternoon, go to Kharanaq and Meybod.
  • Day 3: Take a day trip to Chak Chak and explore the surrounding mountains.
  • Day 4: Go to the Zeinoddin Caravanserai and spend the night there.
  • Day 5: Visit the Towers of Silence in the morning and then Garmeh Oasis.
  • Day 6: Take a cooking class or visit a Zoroastrian temple.
  • Day 7: Soak up the atmosphere and enjoy your last day in Yazd.

Food and Drink Scene of Yazd

Must-Try Dishes

  • Ash-e reshteh: A thick soup made with noodles, beans, herbs, and spices.
  • Kebab: Grilled meat, either beef or lamb, served with rice.
  • Faloodeh: A frozen dessert made with rice noodles and rosewater syrup.

Local Beverages

  • Doogh: A refreshing drink made with yoghurt, mint, and salt.
  • Sekanjabin: A sweet and sour drink made with honey and vinegar.

Where to Stay in Yazd: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Kohan Hotel: A beautiful traditional hotel located in the heart of the old town.
  • Laleh Hostel: A cozy hostel with a rooftop terrace and stunning views of the city.
  • Moshir Garden Hotel: A former royal mansion turned hotel with a beautiful garden and pool.

What to Buy in Yazd: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Termeh: A type of Persian silk brocade with intricate designs.
  • Qotab: A sweet pastry filled with walnuts or almonds.
  • Pistachios: Iran is famous for its high-quality pistachios, and you can find them everywhere in Yazd.

Local Culture and Customs of Yazd

Iranians are generally friendly and welcoming, but there are a few customs and behaviors to keep in mind. Dress modestly and cover your head when visiting religious sites. Always ask for permission before taking someone’s photo, especially women. Avoid political discussions and respect local customs and traditions.

Best Time to Visit Yazd

The best time to visit Yazd is between October and April when temperatures are cooler and more comfortable for outdoor activities.

Do’s and Don’ts of Yazd

Do:

  • Respect local customs and traditions.
  • Learn a few Persian phrases.
  • Try the local food and drinks.

Don’t:

  • Eat or drink in public during Ramadan.
  • Display affection in public.
  • Take photos of people without their permission.

Safety and Health

Safety in Yazd

Yazd is a safe place to visit, and crime rates are very low. However, travelers should take basic precautions, such as not leaving valuables unattended and being aware of their surroundings, especially after dark.

Health in Yazd

Iran has a good healthcare system, and most medications are available at local pharmacies. However, it’s always a good idea to travel with a basic first-aid kit, including any personal prescriptions.

Getting to Yazd: Transportation Options and Logistics

Yazd is located in central Iran and is easily accessible by air, bus, and train.

Airports Near Yazd

  • Shahid Sadooghi Airport: Located about 10 km from the city center.

Bus Stations Near Yazd

  • Yazd Bus Terminal: Located about 7 km from the city center.

Other Options

  • Train: The Yazd Railway Station is located about 7 km from the city center.

How to Get Around in Yazd

Yazd is a relatively small city, and most of the main attractions are within walking distance of each other. However, taxis are readily available, and there are also bicycles and motorbikes for rent.

Where to Go Next from Yazd: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Kerman: A major city in southern Iran known for its historic bazaar and the Shahdad Desert.
  • Esfahan: A beautiful city famous for its stunning Islamic architecture, including the Naqsh-e Jahan Square and the Imam Mosque.
  • Shiraz: A city known for its gardens, poetry, and wine. Shiraz is home to many important historical sites, including the Tomb of Hafez and the Pink Mosque.

Conclusion

Yazd is a must-visit destination in Iran, offering a unique blend of traditional architecture, history, and culture. From exploring the winding alleys of the old town to hiking in the desert, there is something for everyone in Yazd. Book your trip today and experience the magic of this beautiful city for yourself.
