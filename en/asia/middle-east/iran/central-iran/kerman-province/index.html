Introduction to Kerman (Province)

Kerman is a province located in the southeastern part of Iran. It is known for its unique cultural heritage, magnificent architecture, and natural wonders. Its capital city, also named Kerman, is a bustling city that serves as the gateway to the province’s many charms. The province is divided into fourteen counties and is home to many ethnic groups, each with its own distinct culture and traditions.

List of Places to Visit in Kerman (Province)

Here is a list of some of the top attractions in Kerman province:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Bam Citadel: This ancient fortress dating back to the Sassanian era is located near the city of Bam and is a UNESCO World Heritage Site.
  • Zoroastrian Fire Temple: This temple is located in the city of Yazd and is one of the most important Zoroastrian places of worship in the world.
  • Shahdad Desert: This beautiful desert is home to ancient ruins and a unique landscape, making it a popular destination for tourists.
  • Jameh Mosque of Kerman: This historic mosque was built during the Seljuk era and features beautiful tilework and stucco decoration.

Natural Attractions of Kerman (Province): Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Lut Desert: This vast desert is located in the southeast of the province and is known for its interesting landforms and extreme temperatures.
  • Mahan: This small town is known for its beautiful gardens and the mausoleum of the famous Sufi poet, Shah Nematollah Vali.
  • Rayen Castle: This mud-brick castle is located near the city of Rayen and is similar in structure to the Arg-e Bam.
  • Shahdad Kalouts: These unique geological formations are located in the Kavir National Park and are a popular destination for tourists.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Kerman (Province)

Kerman province offers many opportunities for hiking and biking enthusiasts. Some popular routes include:

  • The Alamut Trail: This 50km trail leads through the beautiful mountains and valleys surrounding the city of Kerman.
  • The Kavir Desert Trail: This 120km trail leads through the heart of the Lut Desert and offers breathtaking views of the landscape.
  • The Shahdad Desert Trail: This 80km trail leads through the stunning landscapes of the Shahdad Desert and includes visits to the many historical sites in the area.

Best Things to Do in Kerman (Province): Activities and Tours

Here are some of the best things to do in Kerman province:

  • Explore the ancient ruins at the Bam Citadel or Rayen Castle.
  • Take a tour of the Zoroastrian Fire Temple or the Jameh Mosque of Kerman.
  • Visit the beautiful gardens in Mahan or take a trip to the Shahdad Kalouts.
  • Go hiking or biking along one of the many trails in the province.
  • Take a desert safari or a hot air balloon ride over the Lut Desert.

Food and Drink Scene of Kerman (Province)

Kerman province is known for its unique cuisine and local beverages. Here are some must-try dishes and drinks:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Fesenjan: a rich stew made with walnuts and pomegranate paste, usually served with rice.
  • Qavoot: a traditional dessert made from walnuts and sugar, often served during weddings and other special occasions.
  • Shole Zard: a sweet saffron rice pudding, usually served cold.

Local Beverages

  • Doogh: a refreshing yogurt-based drink flavored with mint and served cold.
  • Sharbat: a sweet fruit juice, often served during the summer months.
  • Saffron Tea: a traditional tea made with saffron and other herbs and spices.

Where to Stay in Kerman (Province): Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

There are many accommodation options available in Kerman province, ranging from luxury hotels to budget-friendly guesthouses. Some popular options include:

  • Pars Hotel Kerman: One of the best luxury hotels in Kerman, located in the heart of the city.
  • Kerman Tourist Hotel: A budget-friendly option that offers comfortable rooms and a convenient location.
  • Kerman Guesthouse: A traditional guesthouse that offers a unique cultural experience for travelers.

What to Buy in Kerman (Province): Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Kerman province is known for its unique handicrafts and local products. Some popular souvenirs and items to bring home include:

  • Persian carpets: Kerman is known for its high-quality handmade carpets, which make a perfect souvenir or gift to bring home.
  • Pistachios: Kerman province is one of the largest producers of pistachios in Iran, and they make a tasty and nutritious snack to bring home.
  • Nay Persian Flute: This traditional Iranian instrument is made in the city of Kerman and is a great souvenir for music lovers.

Local Culture and Customs of Kerman (Province)

Kerman province has a rich cultural heritage, and visitors are encouraged to be respectful of local customs and traditions. Here are some important cultural practices to be aware of:

  • Hospitality is a core value in Iranian culture, so visitors can expect to be welcomed warmly.
  • It is customary to remove shoes before entering someone’s home or a mosque.
  • Men and women may be asked to dress modestly, covering their arms and legs, especially when visiting religious sites.

Best Time to Visit Kerman (Province)

The best time to visit Kerman province is from March to May and from September to November when the weather is mild and the crowds are smaller. The summers in Kerman can be extremely hot, with temperatures reaching into the high 40s (Celsius), whereas the winters can be quite cold, with temperatures dipping below freezing.

Do’s and Don’ts of Kerman (Province)

Here are some important do’s and don’ts to keep in mind when visiting Kerman province:

Do:

  • Dress modestly when visiting mosques or religious sites.
  • Observe local customs and traditions.
  • Be respectful of cultural differences.

Don’t:

  • Take photographs of people without their permission.
  • Drink alcohol in public.
  • Play loud music or be disruptive in public spaces.

Safety and Health

Safety in Kerman (Province)

Kerman province is a generally safe destination for travelers, but visitors should be aware of the risks associated with traveling to the region. Petty crime such as pickpocketing can sometimes be an issue in crowded areas, so visitors should take precautions to protect their belongings.

Health in Kerman (Province)

Visitors to Kerman province should ensure they have up-to-date vaccinations and take precautions to protect themselves against illnesses such as heatstroke and dehydration. The tap water is not safe to drink, so visitors should only drink bottled water or boiled water.

Getting to Kerman (Province): Transportation Options and Logistics

Kerman province is easily accessible by both air and land. Here are some options for getting there:

Airports Near Kerman (Province)

  • Kerman International Airport: This is the main airport in the province and is located approximately 10km from the city center.

Bus Stations Near Kerman (Province)

  • Kerman Bus Terminal: Located in the city center, this bus terminal offers connections to most major cities in Iran.

Other Options

  • Train: There are daily train connections between Kerman and other major cities in Iran.
  • Taxi: Taxis are a convenient and affordable way to get around Kerman province, with many taxis offering long-distance services.

How to Get Around in Kerman (Province)

Getting around Kerman province is relatively easy, with many transportation options available. Here are some popular options:

  • Private taxi: Private taxis are a popular option for getting around in the cities and towns of Kerman province.
  • Rental car: Rental cars are readily available in Kerman and offer travelers the flexibility to explore the province at their own pace.
  • Public transportation: Buses and taxis are widely available in Kerman and offer an affordable way to get around.

Where to Go Next from Kerman (Province): Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Kerman province is located in a region of Iran that is rich in cultural and natural wonders. Here are some nearby destinations that are worth visiting:

  • Yazd: This historic city is located just north of Kerman and is known for its unique architecture and multicultural heritage.
  • Shiraz: Known as the city of poets, Shiraz is located to the west of Kerman and is home to many famous historic sites and museums.
  • Isfahan: This historic and culturally rich city is located to the northwest of Kerman and is considered one of the most beautiful cities in Iran.

Conclusion

Kerman province is a fascinating region of Iran that offers visitors a unique blend of culture, history, and natural beauty. From exploring ancient ruins to hiking through the scenic Lut Desert, there is something for everyone in Kerman.
