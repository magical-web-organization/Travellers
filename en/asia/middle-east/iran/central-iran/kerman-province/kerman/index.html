Introduction to Kerman

Nestled in the southeastern part of Iran, Kerman is an enchanting city that offers a unique blend of natural beauty, historical landmarks, and cultural experiences. Known for its exquisite carpets and cultural heritage, Kerman has been an important trade center since ancient times. Visitors to Kerman can explore ancient citadels, tour museums and bazaars, hike in the mountains or desert, and indulge in delicious local cuisine. Join us on a journey to explore the best of Kerman, Iran.

List of Places to Visit in Kerman

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Kerman is home to numerous historically significant structures and museums, including the following:

  • Arg-e Bam Citadel: Located just outside the city limits of Kerman, this ancient citadel was designated a UNESCO World Heritage site for its exceptional value as an archaeological site.
  • Ganj-Ali Khan Complex: This historical monument includes a bathhouse, bazaar, caravanserai, and mosque. Built in the late 16th century, it reflects the glory of Safavid-era architecture.
  • Zoroastrian Fire Temple: This ancient temple is one of the city’s most important landmarks, and it is believed to be one of the world’s oldest continuously burning fire temples.
  • Kerman Bazaar: This sprawling market offers a glimpse of local life, and is a great place to shop for souvenirs.

Natural Attractions of Kerman: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Kerman is surrounded by jaw-dropping natural landscapes, and there are several nearby attractions to explore, including:

  • Lut Desert: A magnificent and desolate salt desert that is perfect for trekking and observing the stars.
  • Shahdad Desert: Home to the famous Kaluts, these majestic natural monuments of sand and stone are one of the most striking landscapes on earth.
  • Jabalieh Mountain: This gorgeous mountain range is perfect for hiking and rock climbing.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Kerman

Kerman’s mountains and deserts offer a plethora of options to outdoor enthusiasts. Some of the best hiking and biking routes include:

  • Shahdad to Shahdad Kalout: This trek takes you through the heart of the Kaluts and offers breathtaking desert views.
  • Mahan to Rayen: This hiking route is known for its scenic beauty and historical landmarks, including the Rayen Castle.
  • Kuh-e Gandoman Mountain: This route has several trekking options suitable for different skill levels, and provides stunning views of the surrounding landscapes.

Best Things to Do in Kerman: Activities and Tours

Kerman has plenty of exciting activities and tours to keep visitors entertained, including:

  • Carpet Weaving Workshops: Kerman is one of the best places in Iran to experience the art of carpet weaving. Visitors can take workshops where they will learn the basics of rug weaving and make their own souvenir to take home.
  • Camel Riding: A must-do activity for visitors to Kerman is camel riding in the nearby deserts.
  • Kerman City Tour: A guided tour around the city’s cultural and historical sites will give visitors a deeper understanding of the city’s rich past.

Week-Long Itinerary for Kerman

Day 1: Ganj-Ali Khan Complex, Kerman Bazaar

Day 2: Zoroastrian Fire Temple, Shazdeh Garden in Mahan

Day 3: Bam Citadel, Mahan Palace

Food and Drink Scene of Kerman

Must-Try Dishes

  • Kebab Koobideh: A Kerman specialty made of minced meat, onion, and spices mixed together and grilled on skewers.
  • Saffron Ice Cream: Made with saffron and pistachio, this delicious traditional Iranian dessert is served in many places around Kerman.
  • Shole Zard: A traditional Persian rice pudding flavored with saffron, cinnamon, and rosewater.

Local Beverages

  • Dugh: This refreshing drink is made of yogurt, water, and salt.
  • Tea: Tea drinking is a popular pastime in Iran, and Kerman is no exception. Locals drink it throughout the day, usually with sugar or rock candy.

Where to Stay in Kerman: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Kerman has a good range of accommodation options, catering to all travel budgets. Some popular choices are:

  • Pars Hotel: Located in the city center, this hotel provides a comfortable and luxurious stay for travelers.
  • Mahan Garden Hotel: This hotel situated in the Mahan district is a restored centuries-old caravanserai with beautiful gardens.
  • Koohi Guesthouse: A great budget option for backpackers, nestled in the heart of Kerman’s traditional bazaar.

What to Buy in Kerman: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Handwoven Carpets: Kerman is famous for its carpet weaving, and picking up a beautiful handwoven carpet makes for a great way to remember your trip.
  • Pistachios: Iran is one of the largest producers of pistachios in the world, and the pistachios from Kerman are considered some of the best.
  • Copperware: Kerman is famous for its exquisite copper work, including plates, teapots, and candle holders.

Local Culture and Customs of Kerman

The people of Kerman are known for their warm hospitality and friendly nature. Visitors should dress modestly, covering their arms and legs, to respect local customs.

Best Time to Visit Kerman

The best time to visit Kerman is in Spring and Fall, from March to May and September to November, respectively. The average temperatures during these seasons vary between 16°C to 28°C, making it comfortable for outdoor activities.

Do’s and Don’ts of Kerman

Do:

  • Respect local customs and culture.
  • Dress modestly.
  • Explore roadside villages and towns.

Don’t:

  • Take photographs of women without their permission.
  • Drink in public.
  • Ignore local customs and traditions.

Safety and Health

Safety in Kerman

Kerman is generally a safe city to visit, with low crime rates and a friendly local population. However, visitors should take precautions and be aware of their surroundings when exploring remote areas of the desert and mountains.

Health in Kerman

It is essential to check with your healthcare provider about any required immunizations before traveling to Iran. Visitors should also carry appropriate medication for common illnesses like diarrhea or stomach upsets.

Getting to Kerman: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Kerman

Kerman has its international airport, Kerman Airport, which is served by major airlines of the middle east.

Bus Stations Near Kerman

Several long-distance buses serve Kerman. Most of these buses run overnight between the two cities, with varying degrees of comfort.

Other Options

Visitors can also opt for hired vehicles or taxis for transportation to Kerman from neighboring cities like Yazd, Shiraz, and Tehran.

How to Get Around in Kerman

Kerman’s city center is best experienced on foot, but visitors can use local taxis and buses to explore more distant parts of the city. Renting a bike or a motorbike is also a great way to explore the desert landscapes surrounding Kerman.

Where to Go Next from Kerman: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Yazd: Famous for its Windcatchers, the ancient city of Yazd is just a four-hour drive from Kerman and worth visiting.
  • Bam: The historic ruined citadel near Kerman is just an hour’s drive away and is a fascinating place to explore.
  • Shahr-e-Babak: This Zoroastrian heritage site is accessible via a three-hour drive from Kerman.

Conclusion

Kerman city is a hidden gem of Iran with cultural, historical, and natural wonders. Its friendly locals, rich heritage, and stunning landscapes make Kerman a must-visit destination for anyone traveling to Iran. We hope this guide has helped you plan the perfect trip to Kerman. Happy traveling!
