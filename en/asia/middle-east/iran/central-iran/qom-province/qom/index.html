Introduction to Qom

Qom, located in Central Iran, is a city that is often overshadowed by its larger and more well-known neighbors. However, Qom offers its visitors a glimpse into the traditional and culturally rich side of Iran. Known for its Shia Islam religious center and theological schools, Qom is not only a pilgrimage site but also has stunning natural surroundings that offer scenic views and outdoor adventures. In this comprehensive guide, we will walk you through the must-visit places, activities, cultural tips, and safety and health measures for your visit to Qom.

List of Places to Visit in Qom

There are numerous places to visit in Qom, ranging from cultural landmarks to natural attractions. Here is a list of some must-visit sites:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Masjid al-Qadir: One of the oldest mosques in Qom, built in 849 AD, is a symbol of Qom’s Islamic heritage.
  • Museum of Fine Arts: Established in 2001, the museum showcases numerous Persian art collections, calligraphy, and paintings, making it a must-visit for art enthusiasts.
  • Hazrat-e Fatemeh Masoumeh Shrine: The holy shrine of Bibi Fatemeh Masoumeh is one of the most visited landmarks in Qom, making it a must-visit for all. The shrine is known for its intricate Persian architecture, golden tiles, and stunning courtyard.

Natural Attractions of Qom: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Namak Lake: Located 27km south of Qom, is a serene and breathtaking salt lake, perfect for a day trip.
  • Maranjab Desert: A vast desert region located near Qom, providing a surreal landscape of sand dunes, salt pans, and rocky outcrops.
  • Mount Khwajeh: Standing 3383m above sea level, Mount Khwajeh offers hikers and adventure enthusiasts scenic views.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Qom

Qom offers various hiking and biking routes that cater to individuals of different skill levels. Here are some popular routes:

  • Maranjab to Rig-e-Jenn: A 14 km flat route that follows the sandy paths of the desert, providing scenic views of the surrounding landscape.
  • Qom to Naraq: A 25 km route that guides bikers to Naraq, a quaint village known for its grape cultivation and traditional architecture.
  • Mount Khwajeh Trail: A strenuous yet adventurous trek that takes you to the top of Mount Khwajeh, featuring scenic views of the surrounding valleys and mountains.

Best Things to Do in Qom: Activities and Tours

Apart from hiking, biking, and visiting landmarks, Qom also offers a range of activities and tours for its visitors. Here are some must-do activities:

  • Visit the Grand Bazar of Qom: The bustling bazar in the heart of Qom is famous for its handicrafts, spices, and souvenirs.
  • Take a culinary tour: Experience the local food scene of Qom with a culinary tour of the city, which includes tastings of traditional Persian dishes like Ghormeh-sabzi, Kebab, and Falafel.
  • Aerial view of Qom city: Take a hot air balloon ride and view the breathtaking aerial views of the Islamic center of Iran.

Food and Drink Scene of Qom

The food and drink scene in Qom is rich in Persian traditions and flavors. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Ghormeh-sabzi: a traditional Persian dish made from lamb, kidney beans, and a blend of herbs.
  • Kebab: a popular meat dish cooked over an open flame or grill and served with rice and grilled vegetables.
  • Falafel: a signature street food of Qom made from a blend of chickpeas and spices, fried and served with pita bread.

Local Beverages

  • Sharbat: a refreshing drink made from fruits, flowers, and herbs with a mix of sugar and water.
  • Doogh: a popular drink in Iran made from yoghurt and water, often served during meals as a digestive drink.

Where to Stay in Qom: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Qom provides various options for accommodations, ranging from high-end hotels to cozy guesthouses. Here are some of the best places to stay in Qom:

  • Qom Eram Grand Hotel: A modern and luxurious hotel situated in the heart of Qom.
  • Parsian Lotus Hotel: A 5-star hotel known for its excellent service and location, proximate to the shrine of Bibi Fatemeh Masoumeh.
  • Nimvar Traditional Guesthouse: A cozy and traditional guesthouse that provides visitors with an authentic Persian experience.

What to Buy in Qom: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

No trip is complete without some souvenirs to remember the visit by. Here are some popular items to buy in Qom:

  • Traditional Persian handicrafts: Choose from a range of handicrafts, including pottery, textiles, and glassware.
  • Saffron: Qom is known for its high-quality saffron, which is considered as one of the best in the world.
  • Silk rugs: Take home a Persian-style rug, which is hand-woven with intricate designs and vibrant colors.

Local Culture and Customs of Qom

As a pilgrimage site with strong religious roots, Qom follows traditional Islamic customs and practices. It is recommended to dress modestly and follow local customs, including taking off shoes when entering homes or mosques, and not shaking hands with the opposite gender.

Best Time to Visit Qom

The best time to visit Qom is during the spring and autumn (March to April and September to November). During these months, the weather is mild, and the outdoor activities are enjoyable.

Do’s and Don’ts of Qom

Do:

  • Be respectful towards the religious and cultural customs of the city.
  • Try the local dishes and immerse yourself in the Persian food scene.
  • Visit the surrounding natural attractions like Namak Lake and Maranjab desert.

Don’t:

  • Do not take photographs inside the religious sites without permission.
  • Avoid visiting during local religious festivals, as the crowds can be overwhelming.
  • Do not drink alcohol in public places as it is prohibited by law.

Safety and Health

Safety in Qom

Qom is a relatively safe city, but it is advised to take precautions like carrying important documents like passports and avoiding carrying large amounts of cash. Always keep an eye on your personal belongings and avoid walking alone at night.

Health in Qom

Visitors are advised to carry essential medicines and keep themselves hydrated at all times. It is recommended to drink bottled water and avoid street food.

Getting to Qom: Transportation Options and Logistics

Qom can be reached by air, bus, and train. Here are the details:

Airports Near Qom

The nearest airport to Qom is the Imam Khomeini International Airport, located in Tehran, which is about 1.5 hours away.

Bus Stations Near Qom

Many buses operate from Tehran to Qom, and there are multiple bus stations in Tehran that serve buses to Qom.

Other Options

Visitors can also opt for taxis or hire a private car for their commute within the city.

How to Get Around in Qom

The city is relatively small, and visitors can explore most of the landmarks and attractions on foot or by hiring a taxi.

Where to Go Next from Qom: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Qom is well-located for excursions to nearby cities like Tehran, Kashan, and Isfahan. Here are some recommended nearby destinations:

  • Tehran: The capital city of Iran. Tehran is a bustling metropolis famous for its art, architecture, and culture.
  • Kashan: A desert oasis city, known for its Persian architecture and stunning traditional houses.
  • Isfahan: One of the most iconic cities in Iran, known for its Persian art, intricate tilework at mosques, and grand palaces.

Conclusion

Qom may be small, but it features some of the fascinating and beautiful attractions in Iran. The city’s rich culture, natural surroundings, and warm hospitality will ensure that your visit is memorable. With this comprehensive guide, you can plan your trip to Qom, explore its hidden gems, and immerse yourself in the Persian way of life.
