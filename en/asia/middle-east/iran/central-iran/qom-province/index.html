Introduction to Qom Province

Qom Province is situated in the central region of Iran and is one of the country’s smallest provinces. One of the primary highlights of Qom is its status as the center of Shia Islamic scholarship in Iran. The province boasts a rich cultural heritage with numerous historical and religious sites that attract visitors from around the world.

List of Places to Visit in Qom Province

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

The top places to visit in Qom Province are culturally significant attractions that offer a glimpse into the region’s rich history and religion. Some of the top places to visit include:

  • Masjid e Jamkaran: One of the most revered mosques in Shia Islam, Masjid e Jamkaran is a popular pilgrimage site located just outside the city of Qom.
  • Hazar Jarib cave: Located in the Alborz Mountain range, this cave is believed to be one of the oldest caves used for human habitation in the world, with evidence of continuous use for over 70,000 years!
  • Qom Bazaar: This traditional marketplace offers visitors a window into the daily life of locals. Here, visitors can find a range of goods, including spices, Persian carpets, and traditional handicrafts.

Natural Attractions of Qom Province: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

If you are an outdoors enthusiast, Qom Province has some of the most breathtaking natural attractions such as:

  • Salt Lake: Also known as Daryacheh Namak, it is one of the most extensive salt lakes in the world. The lake provides beautiful views and the option to take a dip in the salty waters.
  • Mount Khajeh: Offering stunning views of the surrounding countryside, Mount Khajeh is a popular destination for hikers and climbers.
  • Qomar Bala and Zomorrod Waterfall: The waterfall is situated at the foot of Mount Qomar Bala and is one of the most beautiful natural attractions in the region.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Qom Province

Qom Province is home to numerous hiking and biking trails that offer a great way to explore the region. Some of the top trails include:

  • Zomorrod Waterfall Hike: This trek offers visitors a chance to see Qom Province’s stunning natural beauty, with breathtaking views of the waterfall and surrounding countryside.
  • Khajeh Mountain Bike Trail: This mountain biking route is an adrenaline-fueled adventure that offers stunning views and takes you through some of the region’s most beautiful landscapes.

Best Things to Do in Qom Province: Activities and Tours

Qom Province offers visitors a range of activities and tours that enable them to experience the region’s culture, history, and natural beauty. Some of the top things to do include:

  • Visit the Shrine of Fatima: The Shrine of Fatima is a must-visit destination for Shia Muslims, and it is the ultimate place to learn about the region’s religious history.
  • Take a Cooking Class: Food is an essential part of Iranian culture, and Qom Province is no exception. Take a cooking class to learn how to prepare traditional Iranian dishes and master the art of Persian cuisine.
  • Explore the Alborz Mountains: The Alborz Mountains offer some of the region’s most beautiful landscapes, and there are numerous hiking and biking trails to explore.

Week-Long Itinerary for Qom Province

Day 1: Arrival and tour of the Masjid e Jamkaran

Day 2: Visit Alborz Mountains for a hike/ bike ride

Day 3: Visit Fatima Masumeh Shrine

Day 4: Take a guided tour of the historic city of Qom

Day 5: Visit Hazar Jarib Cave

Day 6: Explore Qom Bazaar

Day 7: Visit Salt Lake and Zomorrod waterfall

Food and Drink Scene of Qom Province

Must-Try Dishes

  • Kebab-e-Barg: This traditional Iranian dish is made with marinated lamb or beef meat grilled to perfection and served with saffron rice.
  • Ghormeh Sabzi: A Persian stew made with herbs, kidney beans, and beef or lamb meat, served with rice.
  • Faloodeh: A local dessert made with cornstarch noodles, rose water, and lime juice, served with syrup and crushed ice.

Local Beverages

  • Doogh: A popular yogurt drink made with mint and salt.
  • Sharbat: A sweet syrup made from rose petals, saffron, or other herbs mixed with water and served cold.

Where to Stay in Qom Province: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Qom Province offers a range of accommodation options to suit all budgets, including hotels, guesthouses, and hostels. Some of the top options include:

  • Panjdari Traditional House: A beautiful traditional guesthouse located in the heart of the city of Qom.
  • Ghasr Hotel: A five-star hotel located near Masjid e Jamkaran.
  • Marjan Guesthouse: A cozy and affordable guesthouse located in the heart of Qom Bazaar.

What to Buy in Qom Province: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Some of the top souvenirs and local products to bring home from Qom Province include:

  • Persian Carpets: Qom Province is famous for its traditional Persian carpets, which are hand-woven and feature intricate designs and patterns.
  • Traditional Handicrafts: Qom Bazaar is a great place to find traditional handicrafts such as ceramics, textiles, and woodwork.
  • Saffron: Known as the “red gold” of Iran, Qom Province is one of the world’s top producers of saffron, which is available to purchase at local markets and shops.

Local Culture and Customs of Qom Province

Qom Province is a religious and conservative region, and visitors should be respectful of local customs and traditions. Women should dress conservatively, covering their heads and wearing long sleeves and pants. It is also considered impolite to shake hands with someone of the opposite sex.

Best Time to Visit Qom Province

The best time to visit Qom Province is during the spring and fall, when the weather is mild, and the natural scenery is at its most beautiful.

Do’s and Don’ts of Qom Province

Do:

  • Dress conservatively, covering your head and wearing long sleeves and pants.
  • Respect local customs and traditions.
  • Visit religious and historic sites with respect.

Don’t:

  • Engage in public displays of affection.
  • Drink alcohol in public.

Safety and Health

Safety in Qom Province

Qom Province is a safe region for visitors, with low levels of crime and terrorism. However, visitors should still take general safety precautions such as keeping valuables secure and avoiding isolated areas at night.

Health in Qom Province

Visitors should ensure they have the necessary vaccinations before traveling to Iran, and it is also recommended to bring medication for common ailments such as diarrhea.

Getting to Qom Province: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Qom Province

The nearest international airport to Qom Province is Tehran Imam Khomeini International Airport, located approximately 100 kilometers away.

Bus Stations Near Qom Province

Qom Province is well-connected to the rest of Iran via a network of bus routes, and there are several bus stations in the region, including the central bus station in the city of Qom.

Other Options

Visitors can also travel to Qom Province by car or taxi from Tehran or other nearby cities.

How to Get Around in Qom Province

Qom Province is a small region that is easy to navigate around by car or taxi. Visitors can also use public buses or Hire a Driver to get around the region.

Where to Go Next from Qom Province: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Tehran: The capital of Iran is located just a few hours’ drive from Qom Province and offers a wide range of cultural and tourist attractions.
  • Isfahan: One of Iran’s most beautiful cities, Isfahan is home to stunning Islamic architecture, including the famous Naqsh-e Jahan Square.

Conclusion

Qom Province is a unique and fascinating destination that offers visitors a glimpse into Iran’s rich history and culture. Whether you are interested in religion, history, nature, or adventure, Qom Province has something to offer everyone!
