Looking for an escape to nature in Iran? Look no further than Margoon Waterfall! Located in the Fars province of central Iran, this incredible natural wonder boasts cascading waterfalls, stunning vistas, and outdoor adventures. Whether you’re a nature lover, adventure enthusiast or history buff, there’s something for everyone in Margoon Waterfall. Discover everything you need to know for planning your trip in this ultimate travel guide.

Introduction to Margoon Waterfall

Margoon Waterfall is a hidden gem in Iran, with a height of 70 meters, and surrounded by lush forests and mountains. The waterfall is located in Margoon Village, which is part of the Sepidan County in Fars province. With a cool climate all year round, Margoon Waterfall is an ideal getaway from the hustle and bustle of city life.

List of Places to Visit in Margoon Waterfall

There are plenty of cultural and natural attractions in Margoon Waterfall. Here are some must-visit places:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  1. Margoon Village Historical Houses Museum: This museum is a must-visit destination for history buffs. You’ll get to know the history of Margoon Village through ancient and traditional tools, utensils, and cultural relics displayed in the museum.

Natural Attractions of Margoon Waterfall: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  1. Margoon Waterfall: The main attraction of Margoon waterfall is, of course, the breathtaking waterfall itself. Visitors can enjoy a mesmerizing view of the cascading water from different sides of the valley.
  2. Sepidan Valley: Don’t forget to visit Sepidan Valley, a natural wonder that’s adjacent to Margoon Waterfall. A 45-minute drive takes you to this beautiful valley, where you can walk and hike along the trails, and take in magnificent views of mountains and forests in the distance.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Margoon Waterfall

Margoon Waterfall is surrounded by mountains and hiking trails. While most of the hiking routes require a good level of fitness, there are some easy options as well. Here are some popular hiking/biking routes:

  1. Margoon Village To Margoon Waterfall Trek: This is by far the most popular trekking route in Margoon. The trek starts at Margoon Village and takes around two to three hours to reach the waterfall. This hiking trail offers incredible views of the surrounding mountains and forests.
  2. Margoon Village to the Sepidan Valley Hike: This hike takes visitors to the Sepidan Valley via the Sijesang Mountain. The trail is a bit challenging but offers amazing views of the valley from the top.

Best Things to Do in Margoon Waterfall: Activities and Tours

Margoon Waterfall offers plenty of activities for adventure enthusiasts. Here are some of the best things to do in Margoon Waterfall:

  1. Rafting: Visitors can enjoy a thrilling rafting experience in the Zohreh River, which flows through the waterfall.
  2. Camping: There are several camping sites available in Margoon waterfall. Camping overnight offers the opportunity to fully immerse yourself in the beauty of nature.
  3. Picnicking: Visitors can enjoy a lovely picnic while taking in the view of the waterfall.

Week-Long Itinerary for Margoon Waterfall

To explore all that Margoon Waterfall has to offer, consider following this week-long itinerary:

Day 1: Arrive and spend the day exploring Margoon Village.

Day 2: Visit the Margoon Village Historical Houses Museum and then trek to Margoon Waterfall.

Day 3: Take a hiking tour of the Sijesang Mountain with a local guide, and then visit Sepidan Valley.

Day 4-7: Explore other natural wonders in Fars province, such as the Bakhtegan Lake, the Dasht-e Arzhan Salt Lake, and the Shapur Cave. Accommodation can be arranged in Shiraz.

Food and Drink Scene of Margoon Waterfall

Margoon Waterfall offers a diverse food and beverage scene, featuring some unique and tasty local dishes and drinks. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  1. Ghalyeh Mahi: This spicy fish curry is a regional specialty that’s a must-try for seafood lovers.
  2. Ash-e Foul Maki: This lentil soup with beans and herbs is a delicious vegetarian option.

Local Beverages

  1. Doogh: This refreshing yogurt drink is a perfect thirst-quencher in the summer heat.
  2. Shirazi Faloodeh: Faloodeh is a frozen dessert that originates from Shiraz. It’s a sweet and tangy noodle-like dessert that’s great for hot summer days.

Where to Stay in Margoon Waterfall: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Margoon Waterfall offers many accommodation options from luxury hotels to budget-friendly guesthouses. Here are some of the best options:

  1. Margoon Waterfall Hotel: A four-star hotel located close to Margoon Waterfall, which offers great views of the waterfall.
  2. Sepidan Ecolodge: A unique guesthouse that’s made entirely of wood and features a lovely garden, night campfire with music, and BBQ facilities.

What to Buy in Margoon Waterfall: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

There are several crafts and products that are unique to Fars province and make great souvenirs or gifts. Here are some of the best items to purchase:

  1. Gabbeh Rugs: These handwoven wool rugs are a regional specialty and are known for their bright colors and distinct patterns.
  2. Persian Pottery: Fars province is famous for its unique pottery, which is made with a rust-colored clay.

Local Culture and Customs of Margoon Waterfall

While visiting Margoon Waterfall, it’s important to respect the local culture and customs. Here are some tips to keep in mind:

  1. Dress modestly, especially when visiting religious sites.
  2. Greet locals with a simple “salam” (hello in Persian) and don’t be afraid to engage in conversation.
  3. Remove your shoes when entering someone’s house.

Best Time to Visit Margoon Waterfall

The best time to visit Margoon Waterfall is from April to June and from September to November. The weather is mild during this time, and visitors can enjoy the outdoor activities without extreme heat or cold.

Do’s and Don’ts of Margoon Waterfall

Do:

  1. Respect the environment and keep it clean.
  2. Learn some basic Persian phrases.
  3. Try local food and drink.

Don’t:

  1. Take photos of people without their permission.
  2. Show affection in public.

Safety and Health

While Margoon Waterfall is a relatively safe destination, it’s important to be aware of the following:

Safety in Margoon Waterfall

  1. Carry plenty of water when hiking and wear proper hiking shoes.
  2. Be cautious when near the waterfall and follow the designated paths and rails.
  3. Avoid carrying valuables and large amounts of cash.

Health in Margoon Waterfall

  1. The area around the waterfall can be cool, so bring warm clothes.
  2. Wear sunscreen to protect your skin from the sun’s harmful rays.
  3. Drink bottled water and avoid consuming raw or uncooked foods.

Getting to Margoon Waterfall: Transportation Options and Logistics

There are several transportation options to get to Margoon Waterfall:

Airports Near Margoon Waterfall

  1. Shiraz International Airport: located about 100 km from Margoon Waterfall.

Bus Stations Near Margoon Waterfall

  1. Sepidan County Bus Terminal: located in the main town of Sepidan.
  2. Shiraz Bus Terminal: located in the city center of Shiraz.

Other Options

  1. Taxis: Taxis are available in Shiraz and Sepidan County and can be hired for the day.

How to Get Around in Margoon Waterfall

The best way to explore Margoon Waterfall is on foot. However, if you plan on exploring further out, hiring a taxi or touring car is recommended.

Where to Go Next from Margoon Waterfall: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

There are several exciting destinations and excursions to experience near Margoon waterfall such as:

  1. Head to Shiraz for a city break, which is known for its historical sites, gardens, and delicious food.
  2. Visit other natural wonders in the region, such as the Dasht-e Arzhan Salt Lake or the nearby Bakhtegan Lake.

Conclusion

Margoon Waterfall is a wonderful destination in Iran, offering stunning natural beauty, outdoor adventures, and cultural attractions. Whether you’re interested in hiking, history, or cuisine, you’re sure to find something to marvel at in Margoon Waterfall. With this comprehensive guide, you’ll be ready to plan your trip and make the most out of your stay.
