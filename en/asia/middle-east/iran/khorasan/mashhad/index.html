Introduction to Mashhad

Mashhad, located in the northeastern region of Iran, is the second-largest city in Iran and the capital of the Khorasan Province. The city is renowned worldwide as a spiritual destination for Shia Muslims, as it is home to the holy shrine of Imam Reza, the eighth Shia Imam. Millions of pilgrims visit Mashhad every year due to the religious significance attached to it. Apart from the religious aspect, Mashhad is an aesthetically pleasing city with scenic views, a rich history, and fascinating culture.

List of Places to Visit in Mashhad

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Imam Reza Holy Shrine: The shrine of Imam Reza is the most visited attraction in Mashhad, a vast complex that offers insight into the Persian Islamic architecture and the Shia beliefs.
  • Mashhad Museum: Located within the Iranian Intellectual Development Center in the city center, the museum houses Persian artifacts that showcase the cultural significance of Khorasan.
  • Nadir Shah Afshar Tomb: A famous fort of the Afsharid Empire, it portrays the richness of Persian history and architecture.
  • Haruniyeh Dome: Considered among the most beautiful monuments in Mashhad, the Haruniyeh Dome resembles the minarets of Esfahan.

Natural Attractions of Mashhad: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Torghabeh Mountains: A series of high mountains that offer spectacular views of Mashhad, it is an ideal place for adventurers and nature enthusiasts.
  • Pardisan Park: One of Mashhad’s largest parks, which boasts lush green lawns, an artificial lake, an amusement park, and a zoo.
  • Shandiz: A small town situated 25 km from Mashhad, famous for its cool climate, scenic views, and apple gardens. An ideal place to escape the hustle and bustle of the city.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Mashhad

  • Khorshid Dam: A stunningly beautiful dam that offers a relaxing atmosphere and the chance to appreciate nature’s essence.
  • Sabzeh Meadow: A meadow situated in the Torghabeh mountains that offers a challenging hike and beautiful scenic views.

Best Things to Do in Mashhad: Activities and Tours

  • Attending Religious Ceremonies: Discover the Shia Muslim culture by participating in religious ceremonies at the Holy Shrine of Imam Reza.
  • Try Ceremonial Food: One of the highlights of the pilgrimage is the food that is handed out at the shrine as a symbol of gratitude.
  • Horse Riding: Explore Mashhad’s beautiful countryside by horseback, taking in the stunning views while basking in a sense of tranquility.

Week-Long Itinerary for Mashhad

Day 1-2: Visit the Holy Shrine of Imam Reza along with all its famous landmarks. Day 3: Enjoy the beautiful views of the Torghabeh Mountains and Pardisan Park. Day 4: Explore the Mashhad Museum and Nadir Shah Afshar Tomb. Day 5: Visit Shandiz and experience the local foods and culture. Day 6-7: Hike and bike in the Khorshid Dam and Sabzeh Meadow.

Food and Drink Scene of Mashhad

Must-Try Dishes

  • Shami: A savory dish made with ground meat, chickpeas, and herbs that is served with bread.
  • Abgoosht: A thick soup made with lamb, potatoes, beans, and tomatoes, served with bread.
  • Gheymeh: A tomato-based stew made with meat, lentils, and potatoes, usually served with rice.
  • Halim: A delicious porridge-style dish made with wheat, meat, and spices.

Local Beverages

  • Doogh: A refreshing, savory yogurt drink mixed with mint, cucumber, and salt.
  • Sharbat: A refreshing and sweet drink made with rosewater, sugar, and ice.

Where to Stay in Mashhad: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Pars International Hotel: A luxurious five-star hotel that offers excellent facilities, including a swimming pool and sauna.
  • Saraane Hotel: A modern boutique hotel situated within the city center, providing a cozy and comfortable stay.
  • Eram Hotel: A cozy, welcoming hotel located close to the Holy Shrine of Imam Reza, making it an ideal option for pilgrims.

What to Buy in Mashhad: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Turquoise and Jewelry: Mashhad is famous for its exceptional turquoise, which is found in the mountains of Khorasan province.
  • Saffron and Spices: Some of the best saffron in the world comes from Iran, and Mashhad is no exception.
  • Paintings and Calligraphy: Iran has a rich tradition of art, particularly in painting and calligraphy. Mashhad provides a lot of options to explore and take back home as souvenirs.

Local Culture and Customs of Mashhad

Mashhad is a religious city, with unique customs and traditions related to the Shia Muslim religion. Therefore, it is necessary to respect Islamic rules, such as being covered from head to toes when visiting the Holy Shrine of Imam Reza.

Best Time to Visit Mashhad

April to June and September to November is the best time to visit Mashhad, as the weather is pleasant, and the tours/hikes are much more enjoyable.

Do’s and Don’ts of Mashhad

Do:

  • Wear appropriate clothing.
  • Respect the local customs and traditions, particularly when visiting the Holy Shrine of Imam Reza.
  • Try the local food and drinks.

Don’t:

  • Do not speak loudly while visiting the Holy Shrine.
  • Do not take pictures without permission, particularly with people of both sexes.

Safety and Health

Safety in Mashhad

Mashhad is a safe city to visit. However, standard safety measures should be observed such as being cautious with valuables and taking general safety precautions.

Health in Mashhad

Travelers should be cautious with drinking tap water and should use bottled water. It is recommended to carry prescribe medications as per the medical conditions.

Getting to Mashhad: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Mashhad

Mashhad International Airport is the primary airport that serves the city with direct flights from within Iran and international destinations.

Bus Stations Near Mashhad

Mashhad has several bus stations that connect to other cities in Iran.

Other Options

Mashhad has several railway stations that provide train services to other cities of Iran.

How to Get Around in Mashhad

Tourists can hire a taxi, use the public transportation systems, or rent a car.

Where to Go Next from Mashhad: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Tus: Tus is a city near Mashhad, which was the birthplace of Ferdowsi, the famous Persian poet.
  • Nishapur: A city famous for its pottery, turquoise, and good eats, and it is located on the Silk Road.
  • Kalat: A mountainous region with fresh air and water springs located close to Mashhad.

Conclusion

Mashhad is a fantastic destination for those interested in exploring Iranian culture and Shia Muslim religion. With its natural sites and historical landmarks, Mashhad is a city that provides a lot to explore and experience. Make sure to respect the cultural and religious customs while enjoying the natural and historical beauty of Mashhad.
