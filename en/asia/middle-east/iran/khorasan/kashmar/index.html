Introduction to Kashmar

Nestled in the heart of Khorasan, in Iran, Kashmar is a beautiful city surrounded by mountains and valleys. The city is home to stunning natural attractions, rich cultural heritage, and warm hospitality of its people. Kashmar is an excellent destination for those who are looking for an off-the-beaten-path experience and want to explore the lesser-known parts of the Middle East.

List of Places to Visit in Kashmar

Kashmar offers a range of attractions that cater to the interests of different types of travelers. Here’s a list of the must-visit places in Kashmar:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Kashmar Museum: A treasure trove of artifacts and relics from the Sassanid and Safavid periods, the museum is a must-visit for history enthusiasts.
  • Gonabad Qanat: An ancient underground aqueduct system that dates back to the Iranian plateau 3000 years ago.
  • Haruniyeh mausoleum: The mausoleum is dedicated to Harun al-Rashid, the fifth Abbasid caliph and a celebrated figure in Islamic history.

Natural Attractions of Kashmar: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Mardeh waterfall: A majestic waterfall nestled in the heart of the mountains, Mardeh waterfall is perfect for hiking and picnicking.
  • Eshghabad valley: Known for its beautiful gardens, Eshghabad valley is a perfect place to relax and enjoy the views.
  • Khorshid cave: A natural marvel that has been formed over thousands of years.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Kashmar

Kashmar’s natural beauty provides plenty of opportunities for hiking and biking enthusiasts. Some of the best trails include:

  • Mardeh waterfall trail: A moderate hike that takes you through the mountains and ends at the beautiful Mardeh waterfall.
  • Golshan village trail: A scenic route that passes through traditional villages and stunning landscapes.

Best Things to Do in Kashmar: Activities and Tours

Kashmar offers a range of activities and tours that cater to the interests of different types of travelers. Here are a few:

  • Explore the underground aqueduct system with a guided tour.
  • Take a cooking class and learn how to make traditional Iranian dishes.
  • Participate in a traditional Iranian tea ceremony.

Week-Long Itinerary for Kashmar

Day 1: Arrive in Kashmar and check into your hotel. Day 2: Visit the Kashmar Museum and the Gonabad Qanat. Day 3: Hike the Mardeh waterfall trail. Day 4: Take a cooking class and learn how to make traditional Iranian dishes. Day 5: Explore the Khorshid cave. Day 6: Participate in a traditional Iranian tea ceremony. Day 7: Depart from Kashmar.

Food and Drink Scene of Kashmar

Iranian cuisine is known for its unique flavors and spices. Kashmar has its own distinct variety of dishes that you must try.

Must-Try Dishes

  • Abgoosht: A hearty stew made with lamb, chickpeas, and potatoes.
  • Khorasan bread: A type of bread that is popular in Khorasan region.
  • Kashk-e-Bademjan: A dip made with eggplants and whey.

Local Beverages

  • Persian tea: A staple drink in Iran, Persian tea is typically served with sugar cubes.
  • Doogh: A refreshing yogurt drink that is perfect for hot days.

Where to Stay in Kashmar: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Kashmar offers a range of accommodation options that cater to different budgets and preferences. Here are a few:

  • Parsian hotel: A luxury hotel that offers stunning views of the surrounding mountains.
  • Golbahar hostel: A budget-friendly option that is perfect for backpackers.
  • Yadegar guesthouse: A charming guesthouse that offers traditional Iranian hospitality.

What to Buy in Kashmar: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Kashmar is known for its handicrafts and traditional products. Here are a few things that you must consider buying:

  • Khorasan carpet: A hand-woven carpet that is known for its intricate designs and vibrant colors.
  • Pistachios: Khorasan is known for its high-quality pistachios.
  • Saffron: A high-value spice that is grown in the Khorasan region.

Local Culture and Customs of Kashmar

Iran is known for its rich cultural heritage and hospitality. Iranians are warm and welcoming people, and you will have no trouble finding locals who are eager to learn about your culture and share their own. It is essential to respect the local customs and dress modestly.

Best Time to Visit Kashmar

The best time to visit Kashmar is from March to November when the weather is mild and dry. Kashmar experiences extreme temperatures in the winter months, so it is best to avoid visiting during that time.

Do’s and Don’ts of Kashmar

Do:

  • Dress modestly.
  • Respect local customs and traditions.
  • Learn a few Farsi phrases.

Don’t:

  • Drink alcohol in public.
  • Show public displays of affection.
  • Take pictures of people without their permission.

Safety and Health

Safety in Kashmar

Kashmar is a safe city, and violent crimes are rare. However, it is essential to be cautious about pickpocketing and petty theft, especially in crowded places.

Health in Kashmar

Iran has a good healthcare system, and medical facilities are available in Kashmar. It is advisable to carry your medication and travel insurance.

Getting to Kashmar: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Kashmar

Mashhad international airport is the nearest airport to Kashmar. From the airport, you can take a taxi or hire a private car to reach Kashmar.

Bus Stations Near Kashmar

Kashmar has a bus station that connects it to other cities in Iran.

Other Options

You can also hire a car or take a train to reach Kashmar from other cities in Iran.

How to Get Around in Kashmar

Taxis and private cars are the best way to get around Kashmar. Buses are also available, but they can be crowded and uncomfortable.

Where to Go Next from Kashmar: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Khorasan is a vast region with a range of attractions that are worth visiting. Some of the nearby destinations include:

  • Mashhad: A holy city that is home to Imam Reza shrine, one of the most important pilgrimage sites in Islam.
  • Sabzevar: A city that has a rich history and cultural heritage.
  • Neyshabur: A city that is known for its turquoise and ceramic products.

Conclusion

Kashmar is a hidden gem in Iran that offers a range of attractions for travelers. From natural wonders to historical and cultural attractions, Kashmar has something to offer for everyone. With its warm hospitality, delicious cuisine, and stunning landscapes, Kashmar is an excellent destination for your next trip to the Middle East.
