Located in the Alborz province of western Iran, Eshtehard is a hidden gem that boasts a plethora of natural beauty and cultural wonders. From hiking and biking routes to stunning scenic views, and museums to historical sites, this city is perfect for travelers looking to experience Iran’s rich culture and history. Our comprehensive travel guide will help you explore the best of Eshtehard and make your trip unforgettable.

Introduction to Eshtehard

Eshtehard is a city in the Alborz province of western Iran, surrounded by mountains and beautiful green landscapes. The city has a rich cultural history, with many historical and cultural attractions to explore. Eshtehard is also known for its delicious traditional dishes and is an ideal destination for outdoor enthusiasts.

List of Places to Visit in Eshtehard

There are plenty of places to visit in Eshtehard, ranging from culturally significant attractions, natural beauty, and outdoor adventures. Here’s a quick rundown of all the must-visit spots:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Kavoshgaran Museum: This museum located in Eshtehard is dedicated to the history of space exploration in Iran, with a focus on the country’s first successful satellite launch.
  • Jameh Mosque of Gilavand: This mosque is a historical attraction in Eshtehard and dates back to the Seljuk period. With its traditional Iranian architecture and intricate decorations, this mosque is a must-visit for history buffs.
  • Shams Palace: This historical palace complex belonged to Shams al-Molk, the governor of Eshtehard during the Qajar period. The palace has impressive architecture and a fascinating history.

Natural Attractions of Eshtehard: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Mount Damavand: Located in the south of Eshtehard, Mount Damavand is the highest mountain in Iran and the highest volcano in Asia. Mount Damavand is an exciting hiking and mountaineering destination that offers stunning scenic views along the way.
  • Lar Protected Area: This protected area is located in the northeast of Eshtehard and is home to diverse flora and fauna. The protected area is ideal for hikers and nature lovers, with several hiking trails and scenic views.
  • Chalus Road: This scenic road is one of the most popular driving routes in Iran and is located near Eshtehard. The road runs along the Alborz mountain range and offers spectacular views of the surrounding landscapes.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Eshtehard

Eshtehard is a popular destination for hiking and biking enthusiasts with several trails and routes available for adventure seekers. Here are some of the best hiking and biking routes in Eshtehard:

  • Lar National Park Trail: This trail is perfect for hikers and leads to the top of Mount Damavand. It is a challenging hike that offers breathtaking views of the surrounding landscapes.
  • Green Lane Trail: This biking route runs through the forest and is great for beginner bikers. The trail is 10 km long and offers scenic views of the Eshtehard countryside.
  • Pulur-Dizin Trail: This hiking trail is located in the heart of central Alborz and offers stunning natural beauty. The trail is 23 km long and is a challenging but rewarding hike.

Best Things to Do in Eshtehard: Activities and Tours

Apart from hiking, biking, and exploring cultural attractions, Eshtehard has several fun activities and tours available for tourists:

  • Eshtehard Hot Springs: Eshtehard is home to natural hot springs that are perfect for relaxing and rejuvenating.
  • Eshtehard Bazaar: This bustling bazaar is a great place to explore traditional Persian products, from handicrafts to spices and traditional clothes.
  • Tour of Damavand Mountain: There are several companies that offer tours of Mount Damavand, ranging from guided hikes to luxury helicopter tours.

Food and Drink Scene of Eshtehard

When it comes to food, Eshtehard has a lot to offer. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages to taste during your trip:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Kebab: This grilled meat dish is a staple in Iranian cuisine and comes in different variations such as lamb, chicken, and beef.
  • Ash Reshteh: This hearty soup is made with herbs, lentils, beans, and noodles, and is typically served with fried onions and thick whey.
  • Baghlava: This sweet pastry is made with layers of phyllo pastry and filled with chopped nuts and syrup.

Local Beverages

  • Doogh: This refreshing drink is made from yogurt, mint, water, and salt and is perfect for hot days.
  • Sekanjabin: This sweet-sour drink is made from honey, vinegar, and mint, and is ideal for summer afternoons.

Where to Stay in Eshtehard: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Eshtehard has several accommodation options for all budgets, from budget-friendly hostels and guesthouses to luxury hotels. Here are some of the best options:

  • Iran Khodro Hotel: This 5-star hotel is located in the heart of Eshtehard and offers stunning scenic views of the surrounding landscapes. The hotel has a range of amenities, including a restaurant, swimming pool, and gym.
  • Atrin Guesthouse: This budget guesthouse is located in the old city center and offers comfortable rooms and a cozy atmosphere.
  • Dar Zarrin Guesthouse: This charming guesthouse is located in the countryside and offers stunning views of the Alborz mountains. The guesthouse has a restaurant and offers trekking and hiking tours.

What to Buy in Eshtehard: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Eshtehard offers several local products and handicrafts that make perfect souvenirs or gifts for loved ones. Some popular items include:

  • Handmade wooden handicrafts: Eshtehard is known for its skilled woodworkers who create beautiful handmade products such as decorative boxes and vases.
  • Traditional Persian rugs: These handmade rugs are intricately woven and come in different sizes and designs.
  • Spices and herbs: Eshtehard is home to an array of spices and herbs that are commonly used in Persian cuisine, such as saffron, sumac, and turmeric.

Local Culture and Customs of Eshtehard

Eshtehard is a city with a rich cultural heritage and customs. Iranians are known for their hospitality and friendliness, and it is common for people to invite guests over for dinner or tea. When visiting cultural or religious sites, it’s important to dress appropriately and remove shoes before entering mosques or holy places.

Best Time to Visit Eshtehard

The best time to visit Eshtehard is from April to October when the weather is mild and ideal for outdoor activities. Summers can be hot, but the nights are cool and pleasant.

Do’s and Don’ts of Eshtehard

Do:

  • Dress conservatively, especially when visiting cultural or religious sites.
  • Learn a few basic Persian phrases.
  • Try the local delicacies.

Don’t:

  • Bring alcohol or consume it publicly.
  • Take pictures of people without their permission.
  • Show public displays of affection.

Safety and Health

Safety in Eshtehard

Eshtehard is a safe city, with low crime rates. However, it’s always a good idea to be vigilant and take necessary precautions, such as not leaving valuables unattended.

Health in Eshtehard

Visitors should ensure they have adequate travel insurance and consider getting vaccinations before traveling to Iran. The city has several hospitals and medical facilities that provide quality healthcare services.

Getting to Eshtehard: Transportation Options and Logistics

Eshtehard is located about 120 km from Tehran, the capital city of Iran. Here are some transportation options to get to Eshtehard:

Airports Near Eshtehard

  • Tehran Imam Khomeini International Airport: This is the nearest international airport to Eshtehard, located about 120 km away from the city center.

Bus Stations Near Eshtehard

  • Karaj Bus Terminal: This bus terminal is located in the city of Karaj, about 25 km away from Eshtehard.
  • Tehran Bus Terminal: This bus terminal is located in the capital city of Tehran and is about 100 km away from Eshtehard.

Other Options

  • Private car: Visitors can also rent a car or hire a private driver to get to Eshtehard.

How to Get Around in Eshtehard

The best way to get around Eshtehard is by using taxis or private drivers. Visitors can also rent a car, but it is not recommended due to the narrow and winding roads.

Where to Go Next from Eshtehard: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Eshtehard is a great base for exploring other nearby destinations and attractions, such as:

  • Tehran: The capital city of Iran has several cultural and historical attractions, such as Golestan Palace, the National Museum of Iran, and the Grand Bazaar.
  • Sari: Located about 250 km northeast of Eshtehard, Sari is a beautiful city with natural attractions such as the Dasht-e Naz Wildlife Refuge and the Mazandaran Sea.
  • Shahrestanak: This small village is located about 30 km south of Eshtehard and is known for its stunning natural beauty and relaxing atmosphere.

Conclusion

Eshtehard is a hidden gem in Iran, with a lot to offer in terms of culture, natural beauty, and outdoor adventures. Whether you’re a history buff or an adventure seeker, Eshtehard has something for everyone. Our comprehensive guide will help you plan your trip and make the most out of your visit to this beautiful city.
