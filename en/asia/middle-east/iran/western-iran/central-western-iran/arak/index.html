Introduction to Arak

Arak is a hidden gem located in the central-western part of Iran. It is known for its rich history, beautiful landscapes, and delicious cuisine. The city is a great base for tourists looking to explore the region’s natural attractions and unique culture.

List of Places to Visit in Arak

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Soltan Abad Museum

Soltan Abad Museum is a must-see attraction, which displays the history and culture of Arak and its surrounding areas. The museum provides an insight into the lives of the people, as well as exhibiting old handicrafts, pottery, and weaving.

Ali Sadr Cave

Ali Sadr Cave is a unique natural attraction located near Arak. It is the biggest water cave in the world and offers an unforgettable experience to visitors. The cave has several waterways and extraordinary limestone formations, making it a fascinating place to explore.

Khomein Jame Mosque

Khomein Jame Mosque is a beautiful mosque in the city of Khomein, located near Arak. It is one of the oldest and most significant cultural attractions of the region.

Natural Attractions of Arak: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Mount Taq-e-Bostan

Mount Taq-e-Bostan is a popular destination for hikers and nature lovers. It offers stunning views of the city and its surroundings. The mountain is also famous for its rock carvings from the Sassanid era.

Lake Tashk

Lake Tashk is a natural freshwater lake surrounded by mountains and greenery. The lake is an ideal spot for fishing, picnicking, and enjoying the natural beauty of the area.

Salt Lake Aran

Salt Lake Aran is breathtakingly beautiful and one of a kind natural attraction. The contrast between the white salt and the blue sky creates stunning photo opportunities.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Arak

Arak has several hiking and biking trails that offer a great outdoor adventure experience. Some of the best trails are Mount Taq-e-Bostan, Zagros Mountains, and Salt Lake Aran.

Best Things to Do in Arak: Activities and Tours

Tour the City

A city tour is an excellent way to explore the culture and history of Arak. The tour includes a visit to the city’s museums, historical sites, and traditional bazaars.

Ali Sadr Cave Tour

Ali Sadr Cave Tour is a fascinating experience that takes visitors on a boat ride through the cave’s waterways. The tour is an immersive experience that allows visitors to explore the cave’s unique features and history.

Attend the Arak Festival

The Arak Festival is held in August and is a celebration of the city’s history and culture. It features traditional music, dance performances, and local cuisine.

Week-Long Itinerary for Arak

Day 1: Visit Soltan Abad Museum and Khomein Jame Mosque.

Day 2: Tour Ali Sadr Cave and Lake Tashk.

Day 3: Explore Mount Taq-e-Bostan and Zagros Mountains.

Day 4: Go on a city tour and visit Arak’s traditional bazaars.

Day 5: Attend the Arak Festival.

Day 6: Enjoy outdoor adventures at Salt Lake Aran.

Day 7: Relax and enjoy local cuisine and beverages.

Food and Drink Scene of Arak

Must-Try Dishes

Ghormeh Sabzi

Ghormeh Sabzi is a traditional Iranian stew made with herbs, meat, and red beans. It is a flavorful and filling dish that is served with rice.

Joojeh Kabab

Joojeh Kabab is a delicious grilled chicken dish that is marinated in yogurt, saffron, and lemon juice. It is typically served with rice or bread.

Local Beverages

Aragh-e-Saqi

Aragh-e-Saqi is a locally-made alcoholic drink that is a specialty of Arak. It is made from grapes and is similar to the Italian brandy, grappa.

Doogh

Doogh is a refreshing yogurt drink that is flavored with mint and salt. It is a popular drink in Iran and is often served with meals.

Where to Stay in Arak: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Parsian Mehr Hotel

Parsian Mehr Hotel is a luxurious hotel located in the city center. It offers comfortable rooms, a restaurant, and a swimming pool.

Sabalan Hotel

Sabalan Hotel is a budget-friendly option for travelers. It is located near the city center and offers clean and comfortable rooms.

What to Buy in Arak: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Handicrafts

Arak is known for its unique handicrafts, such as pottery, weaving, and woodcarvings. These items make great gifts and souvenirs to bring back home.

Saffron

Saffron is one of Iran’s most famous products, and Arak is a great place to purchase high-quality saffron at affordable prices.

Local Culture and Customs of Arak

Arak is a conservative city, and visitors are expected to dress modestly, particularly when visiting mosques and other religious sites. Modest dress for women includes covering the head with a scarf and wearing clothes that cover the arms and legs.

Best Time to Visit Arak

The best time to visit Arak is in the spring and fall months when the weather is mild and pleasant.

Do’s and Don’ts of Arak

Do:

  • Dress modestly when visiting religious sites and public areas.
  • Try the local cuisine and beverages.
  • Explore the outdoor attractions.

Don’t:

  • Be disrespectful to the local culture and customs.
  • Drink in public areas.

Safety and Health

Safety in Arak

Arak is a generally safe city, but it is advisable to avoid any political demonstrations or rallies that can occur sporadically.

Health in Arak

Visitors should be aware of the extreme heat in Arak during the summer months. It is essential to drink plenty of water and wear sunscreen and hats to avoid dehydration and heat exhaustion.

Getting to Arak: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Arak

The nearest airport to Arak is the Imam Khomeini Airport in Tehran, approximately two and a half hours away by car.

Bus Stations Near Arak

Arak has a bus station located in the city center that offers daily connections to major cities in Iran.

Other Options

The most popular option for visitors to Iran is to take a guided tour that includes Arak and other major cities in Iran.

How to Get Around in Arak

The best way to get around in Arak is by taxi or private car. Taxis are available throughout the city and are an affordable option for short distances.

Where to Go Next from Arak: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Kashan

Kashan is a beautiful desert town located about two hours south of Arak. It is known for its historical sites, traditional houses, and beautiful gardens.

Isfahan

Isfahan is a popular tourist destination located about three hours south of Arak. It offers several stunning Islamic historical sites, such as the Imam Square and the Ali Qapu Palace.

Conclusion

Arak is a city that offers a unique and unforgettable travel experience. With its rich history, beautiful landscapes, and delicious cuisine, visitors to Arak will be sure to have an unforgettable trip.
