Introduction to Qazvin

Qazvin is a historical city located in the Central-Western part of Iran. It is considered the gateway to the Iranian capital, Tehran, and is often referred to as the “Pleasant City” due to its mild climate and stunning scenery. Qazvin has a rich history that dates back thousands of years and it was once the capital of the Persian Empire. Today, it is known for its breathtaking architecture, vibrant culture, and delicious cuisine.

List of Places to Visit in Qazvin

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Qazvin is home to some of the most culturally significant attractions in Iran. Here are some of the top places you should visit in the city:

1. Alamut Castle

Alamut Castle is located in the towering Alborz Mountains and is a must-visit for history buffs. The castle was originally built during the 9th century and was used as a stronghold during the Ismaili sect of Shia Islam.

2. Chehel Sotoun

Chehel Sotoun is a historical palace that was constructed during the Safavid dynasty. It is home to stunning architecture and is one of the most iconic buildings in Qazvin.

3. Qazvin Bazaar

The Qazvin Bazaar is the heart of the city’s commerce and has been around since the 11th century. It is known for its architecture, traditional handicrafts, and shops that sell everything from spices and teas to textiles and jewelry.

4. Jameh Mosque of Qazvin

The Jameh Mosque of Qazvin is one of the oldest and largest mosques in Iran. It was built during the 11th century and is known for its impressive architecture and striking tilework.

Natural Attractions of Qazvin: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

If you love the great outdoors, Qazvin is the perfect place to explore. Here are some natural attractions that you should definitely visit:

1. Alamut Valley

Alamut Valley is located on the foothills of the Alborz Mountains and is one of the most scenic places in Iran. It is known for its breathtaking views, lush forests, and pristine rivers.

2. Kavir National Park

Kavir National Park is a desert biome located in Qazvin that stretches across 59,000 hectares. It is home to various species of desert flora and fauna and is one of Iran’s most important natural habitats.

3. Rudkhan Castle

Rudkhan Castle is a medieval castle located in the green forests of Qazvin. It is known for its stunning architecture and offers breathtaking views of the surrounding hillsides.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Qazvin

If you are an adventurer at heart, Qazvin offers some of the most stunning hiking and biking trails in Iran. Here are some routes that you should definitely consider:

1. Alborz Mountain Range Hike

The Alborz Mountain Range Hike is one of the most popular hikes in Qazvin. It takes you through lush forests, past pristine rivers, and offers breathtaking views of the surrounding valleys.

2. Kavir National Park Hike

The Kavir National Park Hike takes you through a desert biome and offers an unforgettable experience. The trail offers stunning views of the desert landscape and is home to various species of desert flora and fauna.

3. Rudkhan Castle Hike

The Rudkhan Castle Hike is a moderately difficult hike that takes you through the lush forests of Qazvin. The route passes through stunning natural scenery and ends at the ruins of the medieval Rudkhan Castle.

Qazvin offers a wealth of activities and tours that will make your visit unforgettable. Here are some things that you should consider doing:

1. Visit the Chehel Sotoun Palace

The Chehel Sotoun Palace is a historical palace that is located in the heart of Qazvin. It is home to stunning architecture, breathtaking gardens, and is one of the most iconic buildings in the city.

3. Take a Walking Tour of Qazvin

Qazvin is a city with rich history and culture, and taking a walking tour is the best way to explore it. You can visit various historical and cultural sites while learning about the city’s past and present.

Food and Drink Scene of Qazvin

When it comes to food and drink, Qazvin has a lot to offer. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

1. Baghali Polo

Baghali Polo is a traditional Iranian dish made with rice, butter, and fava beans. It is usually served with lamb or chicken and is a popular meal in Qazvin.

2. Torshi Tareh

Torshi Tareh is a savory vegetable stew made with sour grapes, chicken, and herbs. It has a distinct flavor that sets it apart from other Iranian stews.

Local Beverages

1. Doogh

Doogh is a traditional Iranian drink made with yoghurt, water, and mint. It is a refreshing drink that is perfect for quenching your thirst on a hot day.

2. Sharbat

Sharbat is a sweet drink made with fruit juice and sugar syrup. It is a popular drink in Qazvin and is often served during the hot summer months.

Local Culture and Customs of Qazvin

Qazvin has a rich culture and customs that are worth exploring. Here are some things that you should know about the city’s local culture:

1. Religious Customs

Qazvin is a predominantly Muslim city, and religious customs and practices are an important part of daily life. Visitors should respect local customs, including dress codes and prayer times.

2. Traditional Festivals

Qazvin is home to several traditional festivals throughout the year, including the Takbir Festival, Norooz, and Ashura. These festivals are a great way to experience the local culture and traditions.

Best Time to Visit Qazvin

The best time to visit Qazvin is during the spring and fall months (March to May and September to November) when the weather is mild and the scenery is at its best.
