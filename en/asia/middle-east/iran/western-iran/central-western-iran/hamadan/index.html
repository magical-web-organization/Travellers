Introduction to Hamadan

Located in Central Western Iran, Hamadan is a city with a rich history and cultural significance. It has been a capital city for various empires throughout history, including the Medes and Achaemenids. The city is known for its historical and cultural landmarks, including the famous Avicenna Mausoleum and the ancient Ganjnameh cuneiform inscriptions. Apart from its cultural heritage, Hamadan also boasts of scenic natural surroundings that make it a popular tourist destination in Iran.

List of Places to Visit in Hamadan

Hamadan is a city with many historical landmarks and natural attractions. Here are the top places to visit in Hamadan:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Avicenna Mausoleum: The final resting place of the renowned Persian philosopher and physician, Avicenna.
  • Ganjnameh Inscriptions: A series of cuneiform inscriptions dating back to the Achaemenid era.
  • Hegmataneh Hill: An ancient city with ruins of palace buildings and temples.
  • Bu Ali Sina Museum: A museum showcasing the life and work of the Persian polymath, Avicenna.

Natural Attractions of Hamadan: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Alisadr Cave: The world’s largest water cave offering boat tours through its underground lakes.
  • Abbasabad Hills: A range of mountains with hiking and trekking routes that offer stunning views of the city.
  • Alvand Mountains: A popular skiing destination during winter and a good spot for summer hikes and camping.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Hamadan

Hamadan boasts of scenic hiking and biking routes that run through the city’s natural surroundings. Some popular routes include:

  • Alvand Mountains
  • Abdul Rahman Park
  • Abbasabad Hills
  • Hamadan-Paskooh road

Best Things to Do in Hamadan: Activities and Tours

Hamadan offers a range of activities and tours for visitors to experience the city’s culture and natural beauty. Here are some of the best things to do in Hamadan:

  • Explore the historical landmarks and museums in the city center.
  • Trek through the Alvand Mountains or Abbasabad Hills for scenic views.
  • Take a boat ride through the Alisadr Cave.
  • Try local delicacies like Bamieh, Kashk-e Bademjan, and Hamadan’s traditional sweets.

Week-Long Itinerary for Hamadan

Day 1: Visit the Avicenna Mausoleum and Bu Ali Sina Museum. Explore the city center and try local food.

Day 2: Trek through the Alvand Mountains and enjoy the scenic views. In the evening, visit Abbasabad Hills and watch the sunset.

Day 3: Take a day trip to Mount Alvand and camp overnight.

Day 4: Explore the Hegmataneh Hill and Ganjnameh Inscriptions. Take a break at a local cafe or restaurant.

Day 5: Visit Alisadr Cave, the world’s largest water cave. Take a boat tour through the underground lakes.

Day 6: Take a bike ride through Abdul Rahman Park and admire its natural beauty.

Day 7: Take a leisurely walk through the city center and shop for souvenirs and local products.

Food and Drink Scene of Hamadan

Hamadan offers a rich culinary experience, with a variety of traditional dishes and sweets. Here are some must-try dishes:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Bamieh: Fried dough made with flour, sugar, and sesame seeds.
  • Kashk-e Bademjan: A dish made with mashed eggplant, onion, garlic, and whey sauce.
  • Ash-e reshteh: A vegetable and noodle soup with beans and herbs.
  • Gheymeh Nesar: A meat dish made with lentils and saffron sauce.

Local Beverages

  • Doogh: A yogurt-based drink with mint and salt.
  • Sharbat: A sweet, flavored syrup that is mixed with water or seltzer.

What to Buy in Hamadan: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Hamadan is known for its local handicrafts and traditional products. Here are some items you can bring back as souvenirs:

  • Hand-woven carpets and rugs
  • Engraved and painted pottery
  • Traditional hand-carved wooden crafts
  • Sweets and local delicacies

Local Culture and Customs of Hamadan

Hamadan is a city with a deep cultural heritage, where traditions and customs are still deeply rooted in everyday life. Visitors should respect local customs and dress modestly when visiting religious sites.

Best Time to Visit Hamadan

The best time to visit Hamadan is during the spring and autumn months (April-May and September-November) when the weather is mild and comfortable for outdoor activities.

Do’s and Don’ts of Hamadan

Do:

  • Respect local customs and traditions.
  • Dress modestly when visiting religious sites.
  • Try local delicacies and shop for traditional products.

Don’t:

  • Take photographs of women without their permission.
  • Drink alcohol in public places as it is prohibited in Iran.
  • Smoke in public places.

Safety and Health

Safety in Hamadan

Overall, Hamadan is a safe place to visit. However, visitors should take precautions and be aware of their surroundings, especially when traveling alone or at night.

Health in Hamadan

Visitors should ensure they have medical insurance before traveling to Hamadan. They should also carry a medical kit that includes basic first aid supplies and any necessary medication.

The easiest way to get around Hamadan is by taxi or private car. There is also a public transportation system, including buses and shared taxis.

Where to Go Next from Hamadan: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Kermanshah: A city with historical landmarks and natural attractions, including Mount Bisotun and Taq-e Bostan.
  • Isfahan: A city with UNESCO World Heritage Sites and cultural landmarks, including the Naqsh-e Jahan Square and the Sheikh Lotfollah Mosque.
  • Tehran: The capital city of Iran, known for its museums, palaces, and modern amenities.

Conclusion

Hamadan is a city with a rich cultural heritage and natural beauty, making it a popular tourist destination in Iran. Visitors can experience the city’s history and culture through its landmarks and museums, enjoy its natural surroundings through hiking and outdoor activities, and try local delicacies and bring back traditional souvenirs. With various accommodation and transportation options, Hamadan is a must-visit destination in Iran.
