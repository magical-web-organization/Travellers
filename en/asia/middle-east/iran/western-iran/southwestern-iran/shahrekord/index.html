Introduction to Shahrekord

Located in southwestern Iran, Shahrekord is the capital city of the Chaharmahal and Bakhtiari Province. The city is surrounded by the Zagros Mountains and divided by the Zayandehrood River, making it an ideal destination for outdoor adventure enthusiasts. Shahrekord is also known for its rich cultural heritage and stunning natural beauty. In this comprehensive travel guide, you will discover the best places to visit, things to do and see, where to stay, what to eat, and the local culture and customs of Shahrekord, Iran.

List of Places to Visit in Shahrekord

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Taq-e-Bostan: A series of large rock reliefs from the Sassanid era, depicting hunting scenes, battles, and investitures.
  • Falak-ol-Aflak Castle: A restored medieval fortress that dates back to the Sassanid era, located in the center of Khorramabad city.
  • Chogha Zanbil Ziggurat: A UNESCO World Heritage site that dates back to 1250 BC, built by the Elamite king Untash-Napirisha to honor the god Inshushinak.

Natural Attractions of Shahrekord: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Koohrang Ski Resort: The only ski resort in Chaharmahal and Bakhtiari Province, located 85 km northeast of Shahrekord.
  • Alvand Mountain: A popular hiking destination with breathtaking views of Shahrekord city and the surrounding mountains.
  • Tange Chahar Fasl Waterfall: A stunning waterfall located near the village of Bazoft, about 45 km west of Shahrekord.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Shahrekord

  • Alvand Mountain Loop Trail: A moderate 8 km loop trail that offers stunning views of Shahrekord city and the surrounding mountains.
  • Dimeh to Gandom-e Beryan Trail: A challenging 20 km one-way trail that leads to the base of Gandom-e Beryan, the highest peak in Chaharmahal and Bakhtiari Province.
  • Chelgerd to Ravar Trail: A scenic 15 km one-way trail that passes through mountain villages and ends at the scenic Ravar canyon.

Best Things to Do in Shahrekord: Activities and Tours

  • Traditional Handicrafts Workshop: Learn about traditional Persian handicrafts such as rug weaving, pottery, and metalwork at local workshops.
  • Nomad Village Tour: Experience the nomadic lifestyle by visiting a traditional nomad village and learning about their culture and customs.
  • Persian Cooking Class: Learn how to cook traditional Persian dishes using locally sourced ingredients.

Food and Drink Scene of Shahrekord

Must-Try Dishes

  • Gheimeh Nesar: A traditional meat and bean stew with cinnamon and saffron.
  • Ashe Sholi: A hearty soup made with lentils, beans, and vegetables.
  • Beryani: A type of kebab made with lamb or beef marinated in saffron and other spices.

Local Beverages

  • Sekanjabin: A traditional Persian drink made with vinegar, honey, and mint.
  • Doogh: A refreshing yogurt drink flavored with mint and dried rose petals.
  • Sharbat-e Albaloo: A sweet and sour drink made with sour cherries.

Where to Stay in Shahrekord: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Tusi Hotel: A modern hotel located in the heart of Shahrekord city.
  • Dimeh Guesthouse: A traditional guesthouse located near the Dimeh village and the start of the Dimeh to Gandom-e Beryan hiking trail.
  • Hanza Hostel: A cozy hostel located in the village of Chelgerd, popular among outdoor adventure enthusiasts.

What to Buy in Shahrekord: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Bakhtiari Rugs: Hand-woven rugs made by the Bakhtiari nomads, famous for their intricate designs and bright colors.
  • Gaz: A type of Persian nougat made with pistachios or almonds.
  • Saffron: One of the highest quality saffrons in the world is produced in Iran, and can be found in local markets in Shahrekord.

Local Culture and Customs of Shahrekord

  • Hospitality: Iranians are known for their hospitality and generosity towards guests. Visitors should expect to be invited into homes and offered food and drinks.
  • Dress Code: Iran has a strict dress code for both men and women. Women should wear a headscarf and loose-fitting clothes that cover their arms and legs, while men should avoid shorts and sleeveless shirts.
  • Respect for Religion: Iran is an Islamic country, and visitors should respect local customs such as avoiding eating or drinking in public during Ramadan.

Best Time to Visit Shahrekord

The best time to visit Shahrekord is in the spring (March to May) or fall (September to November), when the weather is pleasant and the outdoor scenery is at its best. The summer months (June to August) can be quite hot, while the winter months (December to February) can be cold and snowy, especially in the mountainous areas.

Do’s and Don’ts of Shahrekord

Do:

  • Learn a few Persian phrases to communicate with locals.
  • Try the local cuisine and tea.
  • Respect local customs and traditions.

Don’t:

  • Engage in public displays of affection.
  • Drink alcohol in public.
  • Take photos of military or government buildings without permission.

Safety and Health

Safety in Shahrekord

Shahrekord is a relatively safe city, but visitors should take basic precautions such as avoiding walking alone at night, taking licensed taxis instead of unmarked ones, and staying aware of their surroundings.

Health in Shahrekord

Visitors to Shahrekord should have up-to-date vaccinations for hepatitis A and B, and typhoid. It is also recommended to bring mosquito repellent and sunscreen when visiting outdoor areas.

Getting to Shahrekord: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Shahrekord

The nearest airport to Shahrekord is Shahrekord Airport, which has flights to major cities in Iran.

Bus Stations Near Shahrekord

Shahrekord has a central bus station that connects it to major cities in Iran, including Tehran and Isfahan.

Other Options

Taxis are readily available in Shahrekord and can be arranged through hotels or hired directly from the street.

How to Get Around in Shahrekord

The best way to get around Shahrekord is by taxi or private car. Walking is also a good option for exploring the city center.

Where to Go Next from Shahrekord: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Isfahan: A cultural and historical city with stunning architecture and UNESCO World Heritage sites.
  • Tehran: The capital city of Iran, known for its museums, art galleries, and bazaars.
  • Shiraz: A city with a rich cultural and artistic heritage, famous for its Persian gardens and poetry.

Conclusion

Shahrekord is a hidden gem in Iran, with stunning natural beauty, rich cultural heritage, and warm hospitality. With this comprehensive travel guide, you now have all the information you need to plan your trip and make the most of your visit to Shahrekord.
