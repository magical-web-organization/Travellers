Introduction to Aqqala

Aqqala is a small city located in Caspian Iran, in the province of Golestan. Often overlooked by tourists, this charming destination boasts a rich history, stunning natural landscapes, and a unique culture that is sure to leave a lasting impression. In this guide, we’ll take a closer look at some of the best places to visit, things to do, and where to stay in Aqqala.

List of Places to Visit in Aqqala

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  1. Gonbad-e Qabus Tower: This UNESCO World Heritage Site is a stunning tower that dates back to the 11th century. It’s the tallest brick tower in the world and offers a breathtaking panoramic view from the top.
  2. Miankaleh Wildlife Refuge: Located on the southern coast of the Caspian Sea, this wildlife refuge is home to a diverse range of bird species and other wildlife. Visitors can take a guided tour or hike through the park on their own.
  3. Qorveh Castle: The Qorveh Castle is a historic fortress that dates back to the Sassanian period. The castle is located on a mountain and offers stunning views of the surrounding landscape.

Natural Attractions of Aqqala: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  1. Caspian Sea Beaches: Spend a day lounging on the sandy beaches and swimming in the crystal-clear waters of the Caspian Sea. Some of the most popular beaches are located in Ramsar, about an hour’s drive from Aqqala.
  2. Alborz Mountains: Take in the breathtaking scenery of the Alborz Mountains, located just a short drive from Aqqala. Visitors can go hiking or mountain biking on one of the many trails.
  3. Turkmen Sahra Desert: A visit to the nearby Turkmen Sahra desert is a must for any adventure seeker. Here, visitors can go on a camelback ride, camp under the stars, or take a guided tour of the desert.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Aqqala

Aqqala and its surrounding areas are a paradise for outdoor enthusiasts. The city and its surroundings offer plenty of scenic hiking and biking routes. Here are some of the best:

  1. Golestan National Park: This national park offers several hiking trails of varying lengths through lush forests and mountains.
  2. Alborz Mountains: Take on the challenging climb to the summit of Mount Damavand or explore one of the many other trails surrounding the mountains.
  3. Maranjab Desert: The Maranjab Desert is located near the city of Kashan and is a popular destination for hiking and camelback riding tours.

Best Things to Do in Aqqala: Activities and Tours

Aqqala offers a wide range of activities to cater to different interests. Here are some of the best things to do in Aqqala:

  1. Visit the local bazaars: A visit to the Aqqala bazaar is a must for any traveler looking to buy local handicrafts or sample traditional Iranian cuisine.
  2. Local Wine and Traditional Food Tour: Take a guided tour of the local vineyards and taste some of the best wines in the region, paired with Iranian cuisine.
  3. Visit the local villages: Take a day trip to the nearby villages of Kalaleh and Galikesh to learn more about the local culture and customs.

Week-Long Itinerary for Aqqala

Here is a suggested week-long itinerary for a trip to Aqqala:

Day 1: Arrival in Aqqala and check-in at your hotel. Day 2: Gonbad-e Qabus Tower and Miankaleh Wildlife Refuge. Day 3: Qorveh Castle and hiking in the Alborz Mountains. Day 4: Visit the local bazaars and wine tasting tour. Day 5: Day trip to Kalaleh village and Qarahsu River. Day 6: Day trip to Galikesh village and the Maranjab Desert. Day 7: Departure from Aqqala.

Food and Drink Scene of Aqqala

Must-Try Dishes

Aqqala is known for its delicious local cuisine. Here are some must-try dishes:

  1. Baghali Ghatogh: A hearty vegetarian dish made with fava beans, garlic, and dill.
  2. Mirza Ghasemi: A grilled eggplant dish made of tomatoes, garlic, and egg.
  3. Ash Reshteh: A popular soup made with lentils, vegetables, and noodles.

Local Beverages

  1. Faloodeh: This sweet, iced dessert is made with rice noodles and flavored with rose water and lime juice.
  2. Doogh: A refreshing, yogurt-based drink that is sour and slightly carbonated.
  3. Tea: Iranians love tea and it’s an essential part of the culture. Tea is often served with sugar and a side of sweets.

Where to Stay in Aqqala: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Aqqala has a range of accommodation options to suit all preferences and budgets. Here are some recommendations:

  1. Aqqala Grand Hotel: A modern, centrally located hotel with comfortable rooms and excellent amenities.
  2. Kabudwal Hostel: A budget-friendly, family-owned hostel that offers comfortable rooms and a friendly atmosphere.
  3. Simorgh Guesthouse: This stylish guesthouse is located in a quiet part of town and offers clean, comfortable rooms, and excellent hospitality.

What to Buy in Aqqala: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Aqqala is the perfect destination for souvenir shopping. Here are some popular items to bring home:

  1. Persian Rugs: One of the most famous and valuable Iranian handicrafts are Persian rugs. Handmade, high-quality Persian rugs are always worth buying.
  2. Pottery: Iranian pottery is known for its intricate designs and stunning colors, making it a great souvenir to bring home.
  3. Spices and Saffron: Iranian saffron is considered to be some of the best in the world, and is widely used in Persian cuisine. Other popular spices include turmeric, sumac, and za’atar.

Local Culture and Customs of Aqqala

Iranian culture is rich and steeped in tradition, and Aqqala is no exception. Visitors should be aware of the following customs:

  1. Modest Dress: Iran is an Islamic country, and visitors should dress modestly to respect local customs.
  2. Remove shoes before entering homes: Iranians consider it rude to enter a home with shoes on.
  3. Respect for elders: Iranians place a lot of value on respect for elders, so visitors should be aware of this in their interactions.

Best Time to Visit Aqqala

The best time to visit Aqqala is between May and October when the weather is mild and pleasant. During these months, visitors can enjoy outdoor activities and festivals without worrying about the weather being too hot or rainy.

Do’s and Don’ts of Aqqala

Do:

  1. Respect Local Customs: Iranians are welcoming and friendly, but visitors should always respect local customs and traditions.
  2. Try local cuisine and drinks: Iranian cuisine is delicious and offers a wide range of flavors to suit all preferences.
  3. Learn a few words of Farsi: Learning a few words of the local language can help you connect with the locals and make your visit even more enjoyable.

Don’t:

  1. Drink Alcohol in Public: The consumption of alcohol is strictly prohibited in Iran.
  2. Show Public Affection: Public displays of affection are not acceptable in Iran.
  3. Take photos of government buildings: It is strictly prohibited to take photos of government buildings or military installations.

Safety and Health

Safety in Aqqala

Aqqala is generally a safe destination, but visitors should always take common sense precautions, such as keeping their valuables secure and being aware of their surroundings.

Health in Aqqala

Visitors should ensure that their routine vaccinations are up to date before traveling to Iran. It’s also recommended to purchase health insurance and speak with your doctor about any specific health concerns before traveling.

Getting to Aqqala: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Aqqala

The closest airport to Aqqala is the Gorgan Airport, which is approximately 60 kilometers away from the city.

Bus Stations Near Aqqala

There are several bus stations in Aqqala that offer long-distance bus services to other cities in Iran.

Other Options

Visitors can also take a taxi, hire a car or join a guided tour to get to Aqqala.

How to Get Around in Aqqala

The best way to get around Aqqala is by taxi or hiring a private car. Alternatively, visitors can explore the city on foot or by bike.

Where to Go Next from Aqqala: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

There are several nearby destinations that are worth visiting after Aqqala:

  1. Tehran: Visit the bustling capital city of Iran to explore its rich history and culture.
  2. Mashhad: A pilgrimage city with a rich history and stunning architecture.
  3. Shiraz: Known for its beautiful gardens, museums, and ancient history.

Conclusion

Aqqala is a hidden gem in the heart of Iran, offering a unique combination of rich cultural traditions, stunning natural landscapes, and endless opportunities for outdoor adventures. Whether you’re a history buff, an adrenaline junkie, or a foodie, Aqqala has something to offer everyone. By following this guide, visitors are sure to have an unforgettable experience in this beautiful city.
