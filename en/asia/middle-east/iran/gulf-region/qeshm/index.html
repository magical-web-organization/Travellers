Introduction to Qeshm

Qeshm is the largest island in the Persian Gulf, located in the south of Iran. It is known for its stunning natural landscapes, breathtaking seascapes, and unique cultural heritage. Visitors to Qeshm can explore pristine beaches, ancient ruins, and bustling markets, making it a paradise for nature lovers, history enthusiasts, and adventure seekers alike.

List of Places to Visit in Qeshm

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • The Museum of Traditional Arts and Handicrafts houses a diverse collection of traditional Iranian handicrafts, including pottery, textiles, and rugs.

  • Harireh Ancient City is one of the most significant historical sites in Qeshm, dating back to 2000 BC, and is believed to be the former capital of Hormuz Island.

  • Chahkooh Canyon is another must-see ancient attraction that offers a glimpse of the natural and historical beauty of Qeshm.

Natural Attractions of Qeshm: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Mangrove Forests is a unique and globally significant ecosystem, home to a diverse range of plants and animals.

  • Valley of the Stars offers an otherworldly landscape of sandstone formations, created over millions of years by the erosion of the surrounding mountains.

  • Hara Forests is situated on the coast of the island and is home to 1.5% of the world’s mangrove trees.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Qeshm

  • Salt Cave hike is considered one of the best hikes on the island, leading to an underground salt cave.

  • Laft Port Ride offers visitors an opportunity to explore Laft village, the largest traditional port town on the island.

  • Khorbas Cave Trek takes hikers through a scenic countryside of mountains, villages, and a salt lake.

Best Things to Do in Qeshm: Activities and Tours

  • Take a boat tour of the Hengam Islands to see dolphins and explore the island’s natural wonders.

  • Visit the Portuguese Castle of Qeshm to learn more about the region’s history and enjoy stunning views of the island and the Persian Gulf.

  • Experience traditional Gulf cuisine in local restaurants and markets.

Food and Drink Scene of Qeshm

Must-Try Dishes

  • Ghalieh Mahi is fish cooked in a spicy tomato sauce, served with a side of rice.

  • Kaboos is the Qeshm version of bread, which is served with a variety of local dishes like fish and stews.

  • Saadi Soup is made with lentils, herbs, and spices and is a staple in Qeshm cuisine.

Local Beverages

  • Doogh is a yogurt-based drink flavored with mint and sometimes cucumber juice.

  • Sharbat-e-Arghavan is a rose-flavored syrup mixed with water and ice, a refreshing drink on hot Qeshm days.

Where to Stay in Qeshm: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Hara Hotel is a modern hotel centrally located in the city of Qeshm, offering comfortable rooms and a restaurant serving local cuisine.

  • Tolou Ecolodge is located in the middle of nature and offers a unique experience in traditional Iranian architecture and hospitality.

  • Khalij-e-Fars Guesthouse is a family-run guesthouse located in Laft village, offering an authentic experience of local life and culture.

What to Buy in Qeshm: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Turquoise Jewelry is a must-buy for visitors, as Qeshm is known for its high-quality turquoise gemstones.

  • Salt Lamps are made with salt extracted from the Qeshm caves and are known for their healing properties.

  • Gelibaff Textiles is a traditional weaving technique used by local women in Qeshm to make handmade textiles.

Local Culture and Customs of Qeshm

The people of Qeshm have a rich cultural heritage and are known for their warm hospitality. Visitors are expected to respect local customs, such as dressing modestly, removing shoes before entering homes or mosques, and avoiding public displays of affection.

Best Time to Visit Qeshm

The best time to visit Qeshm is between November and May when the weather is mild, and the island is less crowded. The summer months of July and August can be quite hot and humid.

Do’s and Don’ts of Qeshm

Do:

  • Dress modestly and respect local customs.

  • Carry enough cash, as some businesses may not accept credit cards.

  • Try the local cuisine and interact with the locals to learn more about their culture.

Don’t:

  • Take photographs without permission, especially of women.

  • Visit the island during the hot and humid summer months.

  • Drink tap water, which is not safe for consumption.

Safety and Health

Safety in Qeshm

Qeshm is a relatively safe and peaceful island, with low crime rates. However, visitors should take general safety precautions like keeping their valuables safe and being aware of their surroundings, especially at night.

Health in Qeshm

Visitors should be careful when consuming food and beverages, particularly tap water, and ensure they are properly vaccinated before traveling to Qeshm.

Getting to Qeshm: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Qeshm

  • Qeshm International Airport is located on the island and offers direct flights from Tehran, Dubai, and other regional cities.

Bus Stations Near Qeshm

  • Bandar Abbas is the closest bus station to Qeshm, with frequent bus services from major Iranian cities.

Other Options

  • Ferries operate from Bandar Abbas to Qeshm, with daily services.

  • Visitors can also rent a car or a motorbike to explore the island.

How to Get Around in Qeshm

Visitors can travel around the island by renting a car, a motorbike, or using public transport like buses and shared taxis.

Where to Go Next from Qeshm: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Bandar Abbas is a popular destination for tourists, only a two-hour drive from Qeshm and offers a fascinating mix of history and modernity.

  • Hormuz Island is another island in the Persian Gulf, only an hour’s boat ride from Qeshm, and is renowned for its vibrant natural surroundings and colorful sands.

Conclusion

Qeshm is an incredible destination, offering a unique blend of natural beauty and cultural heritage. Visitors to the island can explore ancient ruins, pristine beaches, and bustling markets, making it an ideal destination for nature lovers, history enthusiasts, and adventure seekers. By following this comprehensive travel guide, you can maximize your time on the island and make unforgettable memories.
