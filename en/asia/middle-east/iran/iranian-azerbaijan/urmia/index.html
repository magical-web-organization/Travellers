Introduction to Urmia

Urmia is a beautiful city located in Iranian Azerbaijan, in the northwest of Iran. It is known for its natural wonders, historic sites, and cultural diversity. As one of the largest cities in the area, it offers visitors a unique blend of old and new, modern and traditional.

From stunning natural scenery to historical sites, Urmia has something for everyone. In this comprehensive travel guide, we will explore the best places to visit, cultural and natural attractions, food and drink scene, transportation options, and much more. Let’s get started!

List of Places to Visit in Urmia

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Urmia is rich in cultural heritage, with numerous museums and historical places that offer a glimpse into the city’s past. Here are some of the must-see cultural attractions in Urmia:

  • Azerbaijan Museum: This museum showcases the history and culture of the Azerbaijan region, including various artifacts, manuscripts, and traditional costumes.

  • Urmia Castle: Located in the heart of the city, Urmia Castle dates back to the Seljuk era (11th-14th centuries).

  • Jameh Mosque of Urmia: The Jameh mosque is one of the oldest mosques in Iran, dating back to the 10th century. It features unique architecture and stunning tile work.

  • Armenian Church of Urmia: This beautiful church dates back to the 19th century and is one of the few remaining Armenian churches in the city.

Natural Attractions of Urmia: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Urmia is also known for its beautiful natural attractions, from lakes to mountains, offering visitors a chance to experience the great outdoors. Here are some of the best outdoor adventures and scenic views in Urmia:

  • Lake Urmia: This massive salt lake is one of the largest lakes in the world and a major tourist attraction. Visitors can enjoy boating, swimming, and fishing in its serene waters.

  • Takht-e Sulaiman Mountain: Located just outside the city, this scenic mountain is a popular hiking destination. Visitors can enjoy panoramic views of the city and the surrounding mountains from its peak.

  • Geopark: This UNESCO-recognized geopark is located on the western side of Lake Urmia and features unique geological formations, hot springs, and waterfalls.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Urmia

For those who love to explore the outdoors, Urmia offers numerous hiking and biking trails that provide a great way to experience the natural beauty of the region. Here are some of the best routes:

  • Urmia Lake Walkway: This 150 km trail around Lake Urmia offers stunning views of the lake and the surrounding mountains.

  • Kaboodan Island Hiking Trail: This challenging trail leads visitors to the top of Kaboodan Island, offering panoramic views of the lake.

  • Sahalan Mountain Biking Trail: This 30 km trail offers a great biking experience through the Sahalan Mountains.

Best Things to Do in Urmia: Activities and Tours

Aside from its natural and cultural attractions, Urmia also offers visitors numerous activities and tours that provide a unique experience of the city. Here are some of the best things to do in Urmia:

  • Boat Tour of Lake Urmia: Visitors can enjoy a guided boat tour of the lake, exploring its unique ecosystem and observing the various bird species that inhabit the lake.

  • Bazaar Tour: The bazaar in Urmia is one of the largest in the region and a vibrant hub of activity. Visitors can enjoy a guided tour of the bazaar, learning about the history and culture of the city.

  • Traditional Carpet Weaving Workshop: Urmia is known for its unique carpet weaving techniques, and visitors can enjoy a workshop to learn about the art form and try their hand at weaving.

Week-Long Itinerary for Urmia

To make the most of your trip to Urmia, we recommend a week-long itinerary that includes exploring the city’s cultural and natural attractions while also enjoying activities and tours. Here is a sample itinerary:

  • Day 1: Explore the historical sites of Urmia, including Urmia Castle, the Jameh Mosque, and the Armenian Church.

  • Day 2: Take a guided tour of the bazaar and enjoy some traditional Persian cuisine.

  • Day 3: Visit the Azerbaijan Museum and the Geopark.

  • Day 4: Hike up Takht-e Sulaiman Mountain and enjoy the scenic views.

  • Day 5: Take a boat tour of Lake Urmia and explore the nearby islands.

  • Day 6: Enjoy a traditional carpet weaving workshop and visit the Kaboodan Island.

  • Day 7: Relax and unwind in one of Urmia’s hot springs or enjoy a local spa treatment.

Food and Drink Scene of Urmia

No trip to Urmia would be complete without tasting some of the delicious local cuisine and beverages. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages to try while in Urmia:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Ash-e Doogh: A traditional Persian soup made with yogurt, herbs, and chickpeas.

  • Koofteh Tabrizi: A traditional meatball dish made with minced lamb, rice, and spices.

  • Kebab: A popular grilled meat dish served with rice and grilled vegetables.

Local Beverages

  • Sharbat-e Sekanjebin: A refreshing drink made with mint, vinegar, and sugar.

  • Ayran: A salty drink made with yogurt and water.

  • Tea: Tea is a staple drink in Iran and is often served with sugar cubes or dried fruit.

Where to Stay in Urmia: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Urmia offers a wide range of accommodation options for visitors, from luxury hotels to budget-friendly hostels and guesthouses. Here are some of the best options:

  • Hotel Ghasr: A luxury hotel located in the heart of the city, offering stunning views of Lake Urmia.

  • Hostel Mikhchi: A budget-friendly hostel located near the bazaar, offering clean and comfortable rooms.

  • Maryam Guesthouse: A traditional guesthouse located in a quiet neighborhood, offering a unique cultural experience.

What to Buy in Urmia: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Urmia offers visitors a wide range of unique souvenirs and local products to take home as a memento of their trip. Here are some of the best products to buy:

  • Carpets: Urmia is known for its unique carpet weaving techniques, and visitors can purchase a beautiful, one-of-a-kind carpet as a souvenir.

  • Spices and Herbs: Urmia is known for its delicious spices and herbs, which can be purchased in the bazaar.

  • Handicrafts: Visitors can purchase a wide range of handmade handicrafts, including pottery, textiles, and silver jewelry.

Local Culture and Customs of Urmia

Urmia is a city rich in cultural diversity, with a mix of Persian, Azerbaijani, and Kurdish cultures. Visitors should be respectful of local customs and traditions, including dressing modestly and covering your head in mosques.

Best Time to Visit Urmia

The best time to visit Urmia is during the spring and fall months when the weather is mild, and the natural scenery is at its best.

Do’s and Don’ts of Urmia

Do:

  • Dress modestly and cover your head in mosques.

  • Respect local customs and traditions.

  • Try the local cuisine and beverages.

Don’t:

  • Take photographs of people without their permission.

  • Show affection in public.

  • Drink alcohol in public.

Safety and Health

Safety in Urmia

Urmia is generally a safe city for visitors, although it is always important to take precautions such as avoiding walking alone at night and being aware of your surroundings. Visitors should also be cautious when crossing the busy streets in the city center.

Health in Urmia

Visitors should make sure they have all necessary vaccinations for Iran and bring any necessary medications with them. It is also important to drink only bottled water and avoid eating street food.

Getting to Urmia: Transportation Options and Logistics

Urmia is easily accessible by air, bus, and car. Here are some transportation options and logistics to consider:

Airports Near Urmia

Urmia International Airport is located just 10 km outside the city center and serves both domestic and international flights.

Bus Stations Near Urmia

Urmia Bus Station is located in the city center and serves both local and long-distance buses.

Other Options

Visitors can also travel to Urmia by car or train, although train travel is not as efficient as other options.

How to Get Around in Urmia

Urmia is a walkable city, and the best way to explore its narrow streets and alleys is on foot. Taxis are also available for longer distances, and many hotels and hostels offer bike rentals.

Where to Go Next from Urmia: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

There are a number of nearby destinations and excursions that visitors to Urmia can explore, including:

  • Tabriz: The historic city of Tabriz is just a few hours’ drive from Urmia and offers visitors a chance to explore the traditional bazaar, the Blue Mosque, and the Tabriz Historic Bazaar Complex (a UNESCO World Heritage site).

  • Anzali Lagoon: This beautiful lagoon is located about 90 minutes’ drive from Urmia and offers stunning views of the Caspian Sea.

Conclusion

Urmia is a beautiful city that offers visitors a unique blend of cultural and natural attractions. From exploring its historical sites to hiking its mountains, there is something for everyone in Urmia. We hope this comprehensive travel guide has inspired you to visit this gorgeous city and explore all it has to offer!
