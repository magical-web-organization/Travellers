Introduction to Ariel

Ariel is a picturesque city situated in the West Bank, Palestinian territories. It is a bustling city with a rich cultural heritage and offers breathtaking landscapes. This city is home to numerous museums, historical places, and natural attractions, making it a perfect destination for everyone.

List of Places to Visit in Ariel

To help you plan your trip to Ariel, we have compiled a list of must-visit attractions in the city.

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Ariel University Museum of Art: This museum houses an impressive collection of art created by both Israeli and Arab artists.
  • Ariel Municipal Heritage Museum: Learn about the city’s history and culture through its exhibitions of traditional Palestinian clothing, pottery, and other artifacts.
  • Shiloh Biblical Park: Explore the ancient city of Shiloh, where the Ark of the Covenant was once kept.

Natural Attractions of Ariel: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Ariel National Park: This park boasts a rich biodiversity and offers various hiking trails, viewpoints, and picnic areas.
  • M’kholon Stream: Take a dip in the refreshing water of M’kholon Stream, which runs through Ariel.
  • Jabal-Petrof: Hike to the top of the mountain and enjoy stunning panoramic views of the surrounding landscape.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Ariel

Ariel has numerous hiking and biking trails that offer incredible views of the city and surrounding areas. Some popular routes include:

  • Jabal-Petrof Trail: This is a challenging trail that culminates in stunning panoramic views of the surrounding areas.
  • Ariel National Park Trail: A moderate hiking trail that takes you through the park’s beautiful flora and fauna, providing a peaceful escape from the city.
  • Judean Desert Trail: A scenic and challenging route that takes you through the stunning Judean Desert landscape.

Best Things to Do in Ariel: Activities and Tours

Ariel offers a range of activities and tours that cater to varied interests. Some must-try experiences include:

  • Cultural Tours: Take a tour of the city and learn about its rich history and cultural heritage while exploring its museums, galleries, and landmarks.
  • Adventure Activities: Try exhilarating activities like rock climbing, rappelling, and zip-lining in Ariel’s natural landscapes.
  • Food Tours: Indulge in the local cuisine by taking a food tour through the city’s markets and restaurants.

Food and Drink Scene of Ariel

Must-Try Dishes

  • Musakhan: This traditional Palestinian dish consists of roasted chicken combined with caramelized onions and served on flatbread.
  • Mansaf: A classic Jordanian dish consisting of lamb cooked in a yogurt sauce and served with rice.
  • Falafel: A popular Middle Eastern dish made of fried chickpea balls served in pita bread with vegetables and sauces.

Local Beverages

  • Arak: A traditional anise-flavored spirit found in many Middle Eastern countries, including Palestine.
  • Palestinian Wine: Though not well-known, Palestine is home to a small but growing wine industry, producing wines made from local grapes.

Where to Stay in Ariel: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Ariel offers a range of accommodation options suiting all budgets, ranging from hotels to guesthouses. Some highly recommended options include:

  • Dan Gardens Hotel: A luxurious hotel that boasts panoramic views of Ariel and offers modern amenities.
  • King Solomon Hotel: A highly rated hotel offering affordable prices, comfortable rooms, and on-site dining options.
  • Rafael Hostel Ariel: Offering basic but comfortable accommodation for budget-conscious travelers.

What to Buy in Ariel: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Some popular souvenirs and local products to buy in Ariel include:

  • Olive Oil: Palestine is home to some of the world’s finest olive groves, and Ariel is no exception. You can buy high-quality olive oil from local shops as a souvenir.
  • Handmade Pottery: Check out the local markets for unique and beautifully crafted pottery made by Palestinian artisans.
  • Traditional Clothing and Jewelry: Palestinian traditional clothing like kuffiyeh and handmade jewelry are also excellent souvenirs to bring home.

Local Culture and Customs of Ariel

  • Palestinians are very hospitable and welcoming towards visitors. It is customary to greet people with “Salaam,” which means peace.
  • Modest dress is expected in religious places, so ensure to dress appropriately while visiting mosques and other religious sites.
  • Friday and Saturday are considered significant religious days in Palestine, and shops may be closed, so plan your itinerary accordingly.

Best Time to Visit Ariel

The best time to visit Ariel is from March to May and September to November when the weather is mild, and crowds are thinner. The summers can be extremely hot, and winters can be chilly.

Do’s and Don’ts of Ariel

Do:

  • Respect Palestinian culture and customs while visiting Ariel.
  • Try local cuisines and engage in cultural experiences.
  • Plan and book tours in advance for a seamless experience.

Don’t:

  • Engage in political discussions or debates, especially with strangers.
  • Photograph military personnel or structures without permission.
  • Be disrespectful towards traditional customs and practices.

Safety and Health

Safety in Ariel

Ariel offers a safe environment for tourists, but it’s advised to keep your belongings close and be cautious while traveling alone at night. Avoid walking in isolated or unknown areas.

Health in Ariel

Palestine is prone to gastrointestinal diseases, so avoid drinking tap water and ensure to carry-over a prescription of medications. The city has several hospitals and clinics for immediate medical assistance.

Getting to Ariel: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Ariel

  • Ben Gurion Airport: Israel’s primary international airport, which is located about an hour’s drive from Ariel.

Bus Stations Near Ariel

  • Jerusalem Central Bus Station: An efficient and well-connected bus station located in Jerusalem.

Other Options

  • Private taxi and rental cars services are also available, providing comfort, but they are expensive than public transport.

How to Get Around in Ariel

Ariel’s city center is small and easily walkable, but to explore the neighboring areas, public transport is the best option. You can also rent bicycles, motorcycles, and cars to explore the city at your own pace.

Where to Go Next from Ariel: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Ramallah: A vibrant city that offers street art, local food, and cultural experiences. Located just 30 kilometers from Ariel, it’s an incredible day trip from Ariel.
  • Jerusalem: The holy city of Jerusalem is located about an hour’s drive from Ariel and offers iconic landmarks, historical sites, and religious monuments.
  • Bethlehem: This Palestinian city is located about 50 kilometers from Ariel and is home to one of the world’s oldest churches, Church of Nativity.

Conclusion

Ariel offers a unique and enriching travel experience for everyone. With its rich cultural heritage, stunning natural landscapes, and hospitality of the locals, there is something for everyone in Ariel. We hope this travel guide will help you plan your trip and make it a memorable one!
