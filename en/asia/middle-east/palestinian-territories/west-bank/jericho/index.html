Jericho is a city in the Palestinian territories, located in the West Bank region of the Middle East, in Asia. Jericho is considered one of the oldest cities in the world and is rich in history, culture, and natural beauty. In this comprehensive tour guide, we will provide you with all the information you need to plan your trip to Jericho, from the best places to visit to the local culture and customs.

Introduction to Jericho

Located in the Jordan Valley, Jericho is around 10 km (6.2 mi) away from the Dead Sea, and it is known for its mild weather and tropical climate. Jericho is a popular destination for travelers who are interested in exploring the city’s ancient ruins, historical landmarks, and natural attractions. The city is also famous for its delicious cuisine and warm hospitality, making it an ideal getaway for tourists who want to experience the unique Palestinian culture firsthand.

List of Places to Visit in Jericho

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Hisham’s Palace: Built during the Umayyad period in the 8th century, Hisham’s Palace is one of the most well-preserved ancient palaces in the world. The palace features intricate mosaics, beautifully designed arches, and ancient Roman baths.
  • Tell es-Sultan: Also known as the ancient city of Jericho, Tell es-Sultan is an archaeological site that features ruins from the Bronze Age. The site is home to several historical landmarks, including the ancient walls of Jericho, the ruins of a fortified tower, and the remains of a Biblical-era city gate.
  • Jericho Cable Car: Take a ride on the Jericho Cable Car, which offers panoramic views of the city and its surrounding landscape. The cable car runs from the western part of Jericho to the Mount of Temptation, where you can find the Monastery of the Temptation, a Greek Orthodox monastery.

Natural Attractions of Jericho: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Dead Sea: Located around 10 km (6.2 mi) from Jericho, the Dead Sea is the lowest point on Earth and is a must-visit destination for anyone traveling to the region. The sea boasts crystal-clear turquoise water, mineral-rich mud, and therapeutic saltwater, making it an ideal spot for relaxation and rejuvenation.
  • Wadi Qelt: A stunning oasis located on the edge of the Judean Desert, Wadi Qelt features a beautiful valley with a unique landscape, natural springs, and a series of canyons. Visitors can enjoy hiking and mountaineering in this majestic valley.
  • Ein al-Sultan: This spring is one of the most popular natural wonders of Jericho. The spring is believed to have healing properties and is surrounded by lush greenery and ancient ruins. It’s a perfect spot for picnics and relaxation.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Jericho

Jericho is an excellent destination for outdoor enthusiasts who love hiking and biking. The city offers several scenic routes that take visitors through its beautiful landscape, including:

  • Qumran Trail: A 12.4 km (7.7 mi) trail that starts from the ancient ruins of Qumran and takes you to the beautiful Wadi Qelt.
  • Mount of Temptation Trail: A 3.7 km (2.3 mi) trail that takes you from the cable car station at the base of the mountain to the Monastery of the Temptation.
  • Herod’s Palace Trail: A 6 km (3.7 mi) trail that takes you through the ruins of Herod’s Palace, the ancient fortress of Cypros, and the Hasmonean Palace.

Best Things to Do in Jericho: Activities and Tours

  • Desert Safari Tour: Take a guided tour of the Judean Desert, where you can explore the beautiful landscape, visit ancient ruins and hike through canyons.
  • Oasis Tour: Take a tour of the city’s oases, where you can experience the beauty of natural springs and lush greenery of Jericho.
  • Cooking Class: Learn how to cook delicious Palestinian cuisine and experience the city’s food culture with a local chef.

Food and Drink Scene of Jericho

Jericho boasts a vibrant and delicious food culture that any foodie will appreciate. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Musakhan: A traditional Palestinian dish made with chicken, onions, and sumac, served on a bed of bread.
  • Maqluba: A layered dish made with rice, meat, and vegetables.
  • Kunafa: A sweet pastry that is made with cheese and a sugary syrup.

Local Beverages

  • Arak: A popular anise-flavored spirit that is served in many local bars and restaurants.
  • Tamarind Juice: A sweet and refreshing drink made with tamarind pods.

Where to Stay in Jericho: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Jericho offers a range of accommodation options that can cater to all budgets and styles. Here are some recommendations:

  • InterContinental Jericho: A luxurious five-star hotel that offers excellent service and amenities, including a spa, swimming pool, and on-site restaurant.
  • Jericho Resort Village: A mid-range resort that offers comfortable rooms, a swimming pool, and a restaurant.
  • Al-Qasr Metropole Hotel: A budget-friendly hotel that offers clean and comfortable rooms, a restaurant, and a bar.

What to Buy in Jericho: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Olive Oil Soap: A popular souvenir that is made from locally sourced olives and is perfect for skincare.
  • Dead Sea Salt Products: A range of products from Dead Sea Salt that include bath salts, scrubs, and creams.
  • Keffiyeh: A traditional Palestinian scarf that is a symbol of Palestinian culture and identity.

Local Culture and Customs of Jericho

Jericho’s culture is a blend of Palestinian, Bedouin, and Jordanian traditions. Visitors to Jericho should respect the local customs and traditions, which include:

  • Dress modestly when visiting religious sites.
  • Remove your shoes before entering someone’s home.
  • Greet locals with the traditional Arabic greeting of “As-salamu alaykum.”

Best Time to Visit Jericho

The best time to visit Jericho is between September and May when the weather is mild and perfect for outdoor activities. Summers in Jericho can be extremely hot, with temperatures reaching over 40°C (104°F).

Do’s and Don’ts of Jericho

Do:

  • Learn a few Arabic phrases. It will help you communicate better with locals and make your trip more enjoyable.
  • Respect local customs and traditions.
  • Try Palestinian cuisine and local beverages.

Don’t:

  • Take photographs of people without their permission.
  • Disrespect the local culture and traditions.
  • Get involved in political discussions or debates.

Safety and Health

Safety in Jericho

Jericho is generally safe for tourists. However, be cautious when traveling during the night and avoid displaying valuable items. It is best to use official taxis for transportation.

Health in Jericho

Visitors to Jericho may experience some health challenges due to the extreme heat and dry weather. It is essential to drink plenty of fluids and wear sunscreen and protective clothing to avoid dehydration and heatstroke. Visitors should also ensure that their vaccinations are up to date before visiting Jericho.

Getting to Jericho: Transportation Options and Logistics

Jericho is easily accessible, and travelers can arrive by land, air or sea.

Airports Near Jericho

The nearest airport to Jericho is the Queen Alia International Airport in Amman, Jordan, which is around 75 km (46 mi) away.

Bus Stations Near Jericho

The Central Bus Station in Jerusalem offers regular bus services to Jericho.

Other Options

Private taxis and car rentals are also available for transportation to Jericho from nearby cities.

How to Get Around in Jericho

There are plenty of transportation options in Jericho, including:

  • Taxis: Taxis are readily available and are a convenient way to get around the city.
  • Buses: Buses run frequently around the city, and tickets are affordable.
  • Walking: Exploring Jericho on foot is a great way to soak in the city’s atmosphere and enjoy its natural beauty.

Where to Go Next from Jericho: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Bethlehem: A short 25 km (15.5 mi) drive away from Jericho, Bethlehem is considered the birthplace of Jesus and is home to several religious landmarks, including the Church of the Nativity and the Milk Grotto.
  • Dead Sea: Located only 10 km (6.2 mi) from Jericho, the Dead Sea is one of the most popular tourist destinations in the region.
  • Ramallah: A bustling city located 60 km (37.2 mi) from Jericho and is considered one of the most modern cities in the region.

Conclusion

Jericho is a destination that offers visitors a unique cultural experience, amazing cuisine, and stunning natural beauty. With this comprehensive tour guide, you can plan your trip to Jericho with ease and make the most out of your visit. Enjoy exploring the ancient ruins, hiking the scenic trails, and immersing yourself in the Palestinian culture.
