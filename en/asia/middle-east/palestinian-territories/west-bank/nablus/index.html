Introduction to Nablus

Nablus is a historical city located in the West Bank region of Palestine. The city has a vibrant culture, rich history, and stunning natural beauty which attract visitors from all over the world.

The city was first inhabited in the 6th century BC and has been a significant trade center since then. Today, the city is famous for its impressive archaeological sites, historical landmarks, and local cuisine.

In this Nablus travel guide, we’ll provide you with a comprehensive list of places to visit, things to do, and local customs to follow to make the most out of your visit.

List of Places to Visit in Nablus

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Samaritan Museum

    The Samaritan Museum is a must-visit attraction for history buffs. The museum showcases a collection of artifacts and displays that offer an insight into the Samaritan culture and history.

  • An-Nasr Mosque

    The An-Nasr Mosque is the largest mosque in Nablus and one of the largest in the West Bank. It’s renowned for its stunning architecture, intricate designs, and unique dome.

  • Al-Khadra Mosque

    Al-Khadra Mosque is one of the oldest mosques in Nablus, dating back to the Mamluk era. The mosque boasts an impressive courtyard, beautifully carved columns, and a unique mihrab.

  • Soap Factory

    The Soap Factory is one of the oldest and most visited factories in Nablus. Visitors can take a guided tour through the factory and learn about the traditional soap-making process, which has been passed down from generation to generation.

Natural Attractions of Nablus: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Mount Gerizim

    Mount Gerizim is the highest mountain in the region and offers breathtaking views of the surrounding landscapes. Visitors can hike the mountain or take a cable car to the top.

  • Sebastia National Park

    Sebastia National Park is a historical site located just outside of Nablus. The park comprises an ancient Roman Theater, Crusader Fortress, and other structures dating back thousands of years.

  • Ein Samia Spring

    Ein Samia Spring is an idyllic natural spot to relax and take in the stunning views. The spring is surrounded by lush greenery, and visitors are welcome to swim or take a dip in the cool waters.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Nablus

  • Wadi Qana

    Wadi Qana is a nature reserve that offers a perfect setting for nature lovers. The reserve boasts several hiking trails, stunning views, and a diverse range of flora and fauna.

  • Jabal Haris

    Jabal Haris is one of the most popular hiking spots in Nablus. The mountain boasts several scenic trails that pass through lush forests, waterfalls, and stunning viewpoints.

Best Things to Do in Nablus: Activities and Tours

  • Take a Cooking Class

    Nablus is famous for its mouth-watering cuisine, and visitors can take advantage of the opportunity to learn the local cooking techniques by taking a cooking class.

  • Explore the Old City

    The old city of Nablus offers a glimpse into the history and culture of the region. Visitors can take a guided tour through the narrow alleys, ancient houses, and bustling markets.

Food and Drink Scene of Nablus

Must-Try Dishes

  • Musakhan

    Musakhan is a traditional Palestinian dish that consists of bread, chicken, onions, and sumac. It’s a hearty and flavorful dish that represents the rich culinary heritage of the region.

  • Kanafeh

    Kanafeh is a traditional Palestinian dessert made of shredded pastry, cheese, and syrup. It’s a sweet and savory dish that’s sure to satisfy your taste buds.

Local Beverages

  • Arabic Coffee

    Arabic coffee is a staple in Nablus and is a must-try for coffee lovers. The coffee is brewed with cardamom and served with dates or sweets.

  • Sharia

    Sharia is a refreshing drink made of rosewater, sugar, and water. It’s a popular drink during the hot summer months and is perfect for quenching your thirst.

Where to Stay in Nablus: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Al Yasmina Hotel

    Al Yasmina Hotel is a budget-friendly hotel located in the heart of Nablus. The hotel offers clean and comfortable rooms and is within walking distance to many attractions.

  • Yasmeen Guesthouse

    Yasmeen Guesthouse is a charming guesthouse that offers a cozy and homely atmosphere. The guesthouse is located in the old city and is perfect for those looking for an authentic experience.

What to Buy in Nablus: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Olive Oil Soap

    Nablus is famous for its traditional olive oil soap. Visitors can find shops selling handmade soaps made with local ingredients, including olive oil, laurel, and herbs.

  • Handmade Pottery

    Palestinian pottery is a testament to the rich cultural heritage of the region. Visitors can purchase handmade pottery items, including vases, bowls, and plates, as souvenirs.

Local Culture and Customs of Nablus

Nablus is a conservative city, and visitors are expected to respect the local customs and culture. It’s recommended to dress modestly and avoid public displays of affection.

Best Time to Visit Nablus

The best time to visit Nablus is between March and May and between September and November when the weather is mild, and the crowds are thin.

Do’s and Don’ts of Nablus

Do:

  • Respect the local customs and culture.
  • Try the local cuisine.
  • Learn a few Arabic phrases.

Don’t:

  • Drink alcohol in public.
  • Eat with your left hand.
  • Wear revealing clothing.

Safety and Health

Safety in Nablus

Nablus can be safe for visitors as long as they take precautions, such as avoiding large crowds, staying in well-lit areas at night, and following local customs.

Health in Nablus

Visitors are advised to drink bottled water and avoid eating street food to prevent stomach issues. It’s recommended to pack a basic first aid kit with medications for common ailments.

Getting to Nablus: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Nablus

  • Ben Gurion International Airport

    Ben Gurion International Airport is the closest major airport to Nablus, located approximately 70 kilometers away.

  • Queen Alia International Airport

    Queen Alia International Airport is another major airport, located approximately 180 kilometers from Nablus.

Bus Stations Near Nablus

  • Ramallah Bus Station

    Ramallah Bus Station is the closest bus station to Nablus, and visitors can find frequent connections to and from Nablus.

  • Jerusalem Bus Station

    Jerusalem Bus Station is also another option for those traveling to Nablus.

Other Options

Visitors can also hire a private car or take a taxi from nearby cities.

How to Get Around in Nablus

Visitors can get around Nablus using local buses, taxis, or by foot. The city is relatively small, and many attractions are within walking distance of each other.

Where to Go Next from Nablus: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Ramallah

    Ramallah is a vibrant city located approximately 20 kilometers from Nablus. The city boasts a lively nightlife, bustling markets, and cultural attractions.

  • Jerusalem

    Jerusalem is a must-visit destination and is located approximately 63 kilometers from Nablus. The city is home to several significant religious and historical sites, including the Western Wall and the Dome of the Rock.

Conclusion

Nablus is a unique and fascinating destination that offers a perfect mix of culture, history, and natural beauty. With the help of this Nablus travel guide, visitors can make the most of their trip and experience the best of what the city has to offer.
