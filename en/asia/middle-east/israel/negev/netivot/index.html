Introduction to Netivot

Netivot, a small city in the southern part of Israel, is known for its rich culture and history. Located in the Negev desert region, Netivot offers a unique blend of natural beauty and man-made wonders. The city is known for its strong religious traditions, which are reflected in the many historical sites and museums. In this travel guide, we will explore some of the best attractions and activities that Netivot has to offer.

List of Places to Visit in Netivot

Before we dive in, here is a quick list of the best places to visit in Netivot:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Ohel David Synagogue: One of the most important synagogues in the city, it is named after the founder of the Hasidic movement.
  • Baba Sali Museum: A museum dedicated to Rabbi Israel Abuhatzeira, a revered Moroccan-Jewish rabbi who was said to have miraculous healing powers.
  • Museum of Jewish Heritage in Negev: A museum dedicated to preserving the history and culture of the Jewish community in the Negev region.

Natural Attractions of Netivot: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • HaBesor National Park: A nature reserve with a river running through it, offering beautiful views and opportunities for hiking and biking.
  • Netivot Bike Park: A park designed specifically for biking enthusiasts, with various trails for different skill levels.
  • Gan HaGla’ot: A beautiful garden with numerous plant species and a tranquil atmosphere.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Netivot

Netivot is a great destination for outdoor enthusiasts. Here are some of the best hiking/biking routes:

  • HaBesor National Park Trail: A 13-kilometer trail that runs through HaBesor National Park and offers beautiful views of the park’s river and surrounding landscapes.
  • Netivot Bike Park Trail: A 5-kilometer trail that runs through Netivot Bike Park and is suitable for all skill levels.
  • Gan HaGla’ot Trail: An easy 1.5-kilometer trail that runs through Gan HaGla’ot and offers a relaxing walk in nature.

Best Things to Do in Netivot: Activities and Tours

If you’re looking for things to do in Netivot, here are some of the best activities and tours:

  • Visit Ohel David Synagogue and Baba Sali Museum: Learn about the strong religious traditions of the city at these two important sites.
  • Take a tour of HaBesor National Park: Explore the natural beauty of the park and learn about the flora and fauna of the region.
  • Join a bike tour of Netivot: Explore the city and its surroundings on a bike tour.

Food and Drink Scene of Netivot

Netivot offers a great dining experience with a mix of traditional and modern cuisine. Here are some must-try dishes:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Shakshuka: A classic Israeli breakfast dish made with eggs, tomatoes, and spices.
  • Hummus: A popular Middle Eastern dip made from chickpeas, tahini, and lemon juice.
  • Falafel: Deep-fried balls made from ground chickpeas or fava beans, typically served with hummus and pita.

Local Beverages

  • Arak: A Middle Eastern anise-flavored alcoholic beverage.
  • Wine: Israel has a thriving wine industry, and many local wineries offer tastings and tours.

Where to Stay in Netivot: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Netivot offers a range of accommodation options to suit all budgets. Here are some of the best places to stay:

  • Negev Camel Ranch: A unique accommodation option that offers the chance to stay in a traditional Bedouin-style tent.
  • Merhav Am: A budget-friendly hostel located in the heart of Netivot.
  • Netivot Guest House: A comfortable guest house with a homey atmosphere.

What to Buy in Netivot: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

No trip to Netivot would be complete without buying some souvenirs. Here are some local products to consider:

  • Jewelry: The city is known for its beautiful jewelry, especially silver pieces.
  • Spices: The markets in Netivot offer a wide range of spices, including za’atar and sumac.
  • Glassware: Many local artisans create beautiful glassware, from wine glasses to decorative pieces.

Local Culture and Customs of Netivot

Netivot has a strong religious culture, and visitors are expected to be respectful of local customs. Here are some things to keep in mind:

  • Dress modestly: When visiting religious sites, dress modestly, covering your shoulders and knees.
  • Respect Sabbath: Netivot observes a strict Sabbath, from sundown on Friday until after dark on Saturday. During this time, many businesses and attractions are closed.
  • Kosher food: The city has a strong kosher food culture, and visitors are expected to respect this.

Best Time to Visit Netivot

The best time to visit Netivot is during the spring (March to May) or fall (September to November), when the weather is mild and pleasant. Summer (June to August) can be very hot, while winter (December to February) can be rainy and chilly.

Do’s and Don’ts of Netivot

Here are some important do’s and don’ts to keep in mind while visiting Netivot:

Do:

  • Respect local customs and religious traditions.
  • Dress modestly when visiting religious sites.
  • Try the local cuisine.

Don’t:

  • Visit religious sites during Sabbath without checking beforehand if it’s open.
  • Be disrespectful towards locals or their religious beliefs.
  • Wear revealing clothing in public places.

Safety and Health

Netivot is a relatively safe city, with low crime rates. Here are some safety and health tips to keep in mind:

Safety in Netivot

  • Be aware of your surroundings, especially in crowded areas.
  • Keep your belongings secure and avoid carrying large amounts of cash.
  • Take normal safety precautions at night.

Health in Netivot

  • Make sure to drink lots of water, especially during the summer months.
  • Wear sunscreen and a hat to protect yourself from the sun.
  • If you’re hiking or biking, make sure to have appropriate gear and take proper precautions.

Getting to Netivot: Transportation Options and Logistics

Netivot is located in the southern part of Israel, with good access to major transportation hubs. Here are your transportation options:

Airports Near Netivot

  • Ben Gurion Airport: Israel’s main international airport, located about 120 kilometers from Netivot.

Bus Stations Near Netivot

  • Be’er Sheva Central Bus Station: The main bus station in the area, located in Be’er Sheva about 20 kilometers from Netivot.

Other Options

  • Car rental: Renting a car is a good option if you want to explore the region at your own pace.

How to Get Around in Netivot

The best way to get around Netivot is by car or bike. Taxis and public transportation are also available, but may be less convenient for exploring the region.

Where to Go Next from Netivot: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Netivot is located in a beautiful area with many nearby destinations to explore. Here are some options:

  • Be’er Sheva: The largest city in the Negev region, Be’er Sheva offers a range of galleries, museums, and cultural attractions.
  • Masada National Park: A UNESCO World Heritage Site, this historical fortress offers beautiful views of the Dead Sea and the surrounding region.
  • Mitzpe Ramon: A small town located on the edge of the Ramon Crater, with beautiful views and a thriving arts scene.

Conclusion

Netivot offers a unique blend of natural beauty, cultural traditions, and outdoor adventures. Whether you’re interested in history, religion, or outdoor activities, there is something for everyone in this wonderful city. Plan your trip today and discover all that Netivot has to offer.
