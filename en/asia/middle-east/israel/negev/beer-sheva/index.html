Introduction to Beer Sheva

Welcome to Beer Sheva! Located in the Negev region of Israel, Beer Sheva is a modern city that has a rich history dating back thousands of years. Known as the “Capital of the Negev,” this city is home to a diverse mix of cultures and religions. It’s a fascinating place to visit if you’re interested in history, culture, and outdoor adventure.

List of Places to Visit in Beer Sheva

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

If you’re interested in history, there’s no shortage of museums and historical places in Beer Sheva. One of the must-visit places is the Tel Beer Sheva National Park, a UNESCO World Heritage Site that features ancient ruins and a museum.

The Old City of Be’er Sheva is also a popular attraction where you can see the restored Turkish railway station, the mosque and church, and the Abraham well.

Natural Attractions of Beer Sheva: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

For nature lovers, Beer Sheva has a lot to offer. The Negev Desert is a must-see, and there are many hiking and biking trails available, such as the Israel National Trail or the Negev Highland Trail.

You should also visit the Ein Avdat National Park, an oasis in the middle of the desert that has waterfalls and canyons. The Sapir Observatory is also a must-visit location where you can see stunning views of the cosmos.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Beer Sheva

There are many hiking and biking routes available in and around Beer Sheva. One of the most popular routes is the Israel National Trail, which includes the Negev Highlands Trail.

Another popular route is the Makhtesh Ramon, a unique geological crater located in the heart of the Negev. There are also many guided tours available for those who want to learn more about the geology and ecology of the area.

Best Things to Do in Beer Sheva: Activities and Tours

In addition to the historical and natural attractions, there are many activities and tours available in Beer Sheva. For example, you can take a Bedouin tour to experience the local Bedouin culture, or visit a winery if you’re interested in the local wine production.

Another fun activity is visiting the mosaics at nearby Beit Guvrin National Park. For adventure seekers, there’s also the option of taking a Jeep tour in the desert, or experiencing the thrill of skydiving.

Week-Long Itinerary for Beer Sheva

Day 1: Start your trip by visiting the Tel Be’er Sheva National Park and the Old City of Be’er Sheva.

Day 2: Visit the Ein Avdat National Park and go hiking or biking.

Day 3: Take a Bedouin tour and learn about the local culture.

Day 4: Visit the Sapir Observatory and see the cosmos at night.

Day 5: Take a tour of a local winery and sample the wine.

Day 6: Go hiking or Jeep riding in the Makhtesh Ramon.

Day 7: Visit the mosaics at Beit Guvrin National Park.

Food and Drink Scene of Beer Sheva

Must-Try Dishes

Beer Sheva has a unique culinary scene that includes dishes influenced by Jewish, Bedouin, and Israeli cuisine. Some must-try dishes are:

  • Sabich: A pita stuffed with eggplant, hard-boiled eggs, tahini sauce, and Israeli salad.

  • Shawarma: Thinly sliced meat (chicken, lamb or beef) served in a pita with hummus and salad.

  • Baklava: A sweet pastry made with layers of filo dough, honey, and nuts.

Local Beverages

Beer Sheva is known for its local beer, and there are several microbreweries in the city producing high-quality craft beer. There is also the option of trying Arak, an anise-flavored liquor that is popular in the Middle East.

Where to Stay in Beer Sheva: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

There are several options for accommodation in Beer Sheva, including hotels, hostels, and guesthouses. Some recommendations are:

  • The Leonardo Hotel Beer Sheva: A modern hotel located in the city center.
  • HabaitBe’beer: A guesthouse located in a quiet residential neighborhood.
  • The ZimmerBe’er Sheva: A cozy guesthouse with a garden and outdoor pool.

What to Buy in Beer Sheva: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

If you want to bring home some souvenirs or local products from Beer Sheva, consider buying:

  • Desert honey: made from the nectar of desert plants and flowers.
  • Bedouin jewelry: made with silver and semi-precious stones, often featuring traditional Bedouin designs.
  • Spices and herbs: found in local markets, such as za’atar, sumac, and cumin.

Local Culture and Customs of Beer Sheva

Beer Sheva has a diverse mix of cultures, and it’s important to be respectful of all of them. Visitors should dress modestly and cover their heads when visiting religious sites, such as the mosque and church in the Old City.

It’s also important to be respectful of the Bedouin culture when visiting, and asking for permission before taking photos is recommended.

Best Time to Visit Beer Sheva

The best time to visit Beer Sheva is in the spring and fall when the weather is mild, and the landscapes are at their best. Summer can be very hot, with temperatures often reaching over 100 degrees Fahrenheit.

Do’s and Don’ts of Beer Sheva

Do:

  • Dress modestly when visiting religious sites.
  • Be respectful of the Bedouin culture.
  • Try the local food and drinks.

Don’t:

  • Take photos without permission, especially of the Bedouin people.
  • Disrespect local customs and traditions.
  • Visit during the summer without being prepared for extreme heat.

Safety and Health

Safety in Beer Sheva

Beer Sheva is generally considered a very safe city for tourists. However, as with any city, it’s important to be aware of your surroundings and keep an eye on your belongings.

Health in Beer Sheva

Tap water in Beer Sheva is considered safe to drink. However, it’s always a good idea to carry bottled water with you and stay hydrated, especially when hiking or biking.

Getting to Beer Sheva: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Beer Sheva

The nearest airport to Beer Sheva is Ben Gurion Airport, located about two hours away. There are several transportation options available from the airport to the city, including buses, taxis, and private transfers.

Bus Stations Near Beer Sheva

The main bus station in Beer Sheva is the central bus station, located in the city center. There are frequent bus connections to other cities in Israel, including Tel Aviv and Jerusalem.

Other Options

Another option is traveling to Beer Sheva by train. The city is located on the main train line that connects Tel Aviv and Be’er Sheva, and the train journey takes about an hour.

How to Get Around in Beer Sheva

The best way to get around Beer Sheva is by car or taxi. There are also public transportation options available, including buses and trains.

Where to Go Next from Beer Sheva: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

If you have more time, there are many nearby destinations that are worth visiting, including:

  • Masada National Park: An ancient fortress located on top of a mountain with stunning views of the Dead Sea.

  • Eilat: A resort town located on the Red Sea with coral reefs and beaches.

  • Jerusalem: A city with a rich cultural and religious history, located about an hour away.

Conclusion

Beer Sheva is a fascinating city with a rich culture, history, and outdoor adventure opportunities. There’s something for everyone in this city, whether you’re interested in hiking, history, or local cuisine. We hope this comprehensive guide has helped you plan your trip to Beer Sheva, and that you have an unforgettable experience.
