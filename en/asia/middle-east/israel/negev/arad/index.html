Arad is a small city situated in the Negev region of Israel in the Middle East. The city, with a rich cultural heritage and stunning landscapes, invites travelers to explore its various attractions from museums to natural wonders.

Introduction to Arad

Arad, founded in 1962, is a popular tourist destination in Israel. It’s located in the Judean Desert and is a popular stop for those traveling between the Dead Sea and Masada. It’s popular for its outdoorsy vibe, which includes hiking, biking, and exploring natural wonders.

List of Places to Visit in Arad

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Tel Arad National Park: This archaeological site showcases the ruins from 4000 BC with a Canaanite temple and fortress.
  • Beit HaMusica: A music center with concerts, events, and exhibitions held regularly.
  • Arad Center for Jewish Heritage: A museum that showcases more than 2,000 artifacts of Jewish Culture and history in the Judean Desert.

Natural Attractions of Arad: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Ein Gedi Nature Reserve: Israel’s largest oasis in the middle of the desert. The reserve features several hiking trails and waterfalls.
  • Makhtesh Ramon: A giant 40-kilometer wide crater that is perfect for hiking, stargazing, and rock climbing.
  • Dead Sea: Known for its high salt content, this is the lowest point on Earth and is perfect for floating in its warm water.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Arad

  • Israel National Trail: The trail passes through the city of Arad, and it’s a popular go-to for hikers and trekkers.
  • Colored Sand Path: The trail is 7.5 km long and will take you through Makhtesh Ramon.
  • Amram’s Pillars Trail: A small hike trail in Ein Gedi.

Best Things to Do in Arad: Activities and Tours

  • Stargazing in Makhtesh Ramon: due to the clear skies, stargazing is famous.
  • Masada Sunrise Tour: experience a unique sunrise experience.
  • Canyoning in the Arugot Stream: A fun-filled adventure will offer hours of hiking, slide down waterfalls, and swim through thrilling obstacles.

Food and Drink Scene of Arad

Must-Try Dishes

  • Sabih: An Israeli eggplant dish consisting of fried eggplants, tahini, and salad, served in a pita pocket.
  • Falafel: A Middle Eastern dish made of deep-fried chickpeas or fava beans.
  • Vegetable Couscous / Mograbiah: A dish made with fluffy semolina and vegetables.

Local Beverages

  • Arak: A liquor including dates, tea, and other signature ingredients that are popular in Israel for New Year’s Eve celebrations.
  • Beer: Arad has its microbreweries with a vast array of craft beers such as Rish Lakish, Red Heifer, and Negev Brewery.
  • Tea: Tea is also a part of Israeli culture, and favorite blends include peppermint, green tea, and black tea.

Where to Stay in Arad: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Magic Sunrise Club: A luxury resort with a swimming pool and stunning views of the desert.
  • HI Arad Hostel: A young and vibrant environment and is located in the city center.
  • Etnahta Bedouin: Guests sleep in traditional Bedouin-style tents and offer camel rides and shisha activities.

What to Buy in Arad: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Dead Sea Products: Face creams, body lotions, and soaps made with local minerals and dead sea mud.
  • Handmade Jewelry: A range of unique silver jewelry crafted by Israeli artisans.
  • pomegranate wine: A fruity Israeli wine with a refreshing taste.

Local Culture and Customs of Arad

Arad, much like the rest of Israel, is a melting pot of diverse cultures and influences. The city has an active local theatre and music scene that celebrates Jewish traditions and culture. The residents of Arad, both Jewish and Bedouin, live in harmony.

Best Time to Visit Arad

The best time to visit Arad is in the fall, winter or spring, from October to April, when the weather is mild and pleasant. Summer months between May to September, the temperature can be hot up to 45 °C and these months are not ideal for outdoor activities.

Do’s and Don’ts of Arad

Do:

  • Dress modestly.
  • Be respectful of local customs and traditions.
  • Carry plenty of drinking water.
  • Prepare sun protection gear like hats, sunglasses, and sunblock.
  • Take out travel insurance.

Don’t:

  • Don’t travel in the middle of the day during summer as that can be extremely hot and tiring.
  • Don’t wander off hiking trails.
  • Don’t visit religious sites on Saturdays (Sabbath) when they are closed.
  • Don’t discuss politics or religion without being asked first.

Safety and Health

Safety in Arad

Arad is relatively safe for travelers, but it is essential to take standard precautions, such as being aware of your surroundings and staying away from unlit areas at night. Travelers should keep an eye out for pickpockets in tourist areas.

Health in Arad

The water in Arad is perfectly safe to drink, and tap water regularly goes through regular testing. However, visitors are encouraged to drink plenty of water and carry sunscreen and hats to protect themselves from the sun.

Getting to Arad: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Arad

The nearest airport is Ben Gurion Airport in Tel Aviv, which is approximately a two hours’ drive away. Visitors can access Arad by renting a car, taking a taxi, or by bus.

Bus Stations Near Arad

Arad Bus Station is located just a few minutes walk from the city’s downtown. There are several bus services connecting Arad to other cities in Israel.

Other Options

You can drive yourself or take a private tour that can take you from Tel Aviv or Jerusalem or any surrounding city. This will allow you to enjoy the scenic views of the Judean Desert.

How to Get Around in Arad

Getting around in Arad is easy, and visitors can opt to use private taxis or public transportation such as buses.

Where to Go Next from Arad: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • The Dead Sea: Swimmers can float easily in the mineral-rich waters of the Dead Sea.
  • Masada National Park: Visit the ancient fortress ruins of King Herod’s palace.
  • Ein Gedi Nature Reserve: Hiking, wildlife viewing and waterfall experience.

Conclusion

Arad, a hidden gem in Israel, offers visitors a chance to explore its cultural heritage and stunning natural landscapes. Visitors can expect a mix of recreational and historic attractions, making it a perfect travel destination for all types of travelers. Plan your trip today to fully experience what Arad has to offer.
