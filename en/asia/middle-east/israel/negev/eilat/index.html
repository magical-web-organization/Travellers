Introduction to Eilat

Nestled on the southernmost tip of Israel, Eilat is a popular resort town with a unique location. With the Red Sea to its west, the mountains of the Negev Desert to its east, and the borders of Egypt and Jordan just a short distance away, its position on the map makes it a popular year-round destination.

Here’s what you need to know before planning your trip to Eilat.

List of Places to Visit in Eilat

Eilat boasts a wealth of exciting attractions to suit all interests. Here’s a list of the must-visit places:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • The Underwater Observatory Marine Park: An excellent chance for tourists to explore the marine life in the Red Sea. The underwater observatory is a unique feature where visitors can view marine life even without wearing diving suits.
  • Dolphin Reef Eilat: A unique attraction where visitors get to interact with Dolphins up-close. One can watch the playful creatures swishing their tales and performing acrobatic tricks in the natural setting.
  • Eilat Museum: The museum provides fascinating information about the underwater world and local history of Eilat. Visitors get to see the life of fishermen and historic events that transformed the town into a popular resort destination.

Natural Attractions of Eilat: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Coral Beach Nature Reserve: The beach is an excellent option for tours as it is a protected conservation area. One can stroll along the gorgeous beaches, snorkel in crystal clear water, and even swim through the coral reef.
  • Timna Park: The park is a treat for nature enthusiasts where they can find unique rock formations, hiking trails, and desert landscapes. The park is popular for camping, exploring, and sightseeing.
  • Red Canyon: Located in the heart of the Eilat Mountains, the Red Canyon is a magnificent gorge with red sandstone walls. Visitors can enjoy a variety of activities like rock climbing, hiking, and even rappelling down its steep walls.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Eilat

Eilat offers incredible hiking and biking routes. Here are some of the best ones:

  • The Eilat Red Mountains Tail: An exciting trail with amazing views of the Red Mountains. It is perfect for hiking, and visitors can watch spectacular sunset views from the mountaintops.
  • Arches National Park: The park provides an excellent biking trail suited for all levels of riders. The trail has several skill sections with twists and turns and rewards visitors with impressive views along the way.
  • The Negev Desert: A visit to Eilat will be incomplete without a guided tour of the Negev Desert. Explore the stunning desert landscapes and experience the Bedouin hospitality along the way.

Best Things to Do in Eilat: Activities and Tours

  • Scuba Diving and Snorkeling: The Red Sea offers a unique experience for scuba divers with its rich marine life and coral reefs. For non-swimmers, several tours provide exciting snorkeling experiences.
  • Sea Kayaking: A perfect way to explore the coastline of Eilat. Visitors can opt for guided tours or rent kayaks and observe the marine life.
  • Parasailing: A fun and thrilling experience where visitors glide over the sea with a parasail attached to a speedboat.

Week-Long Itinerary for Eilat

Here’s an ideal week-long itinerary for Eilat:

Day 1 – Coral Beach Nature Reserve & Dolphin Reef Eilat

Day 2 – The Underwater Observatory Marine Park & Eilat Museum

Day 3 – Timna Park

Day 4 – The Negev Desert

Day 5 – Red Canyon

Day 6 – Arches National Park

Day 7 – Free Day / Choose any activity or attraction according to interest.

Food and Drink Scene of Eilat

Eilat offers an exciting food scene with impressive bars, cafes, and restaurants serving international cuisine.

Must-Try Dishes

  • Shakshuka: A popular breakfast dish made of eggs poached in tomato sauce, chili peppers, and onions.
  • Sabich Sandwich: A pita bread sandwich that usually contains fried aubergine, hard-boiled eggs, hummus, tahini sauce, Israeli salad, and pickles.
  • Falafel: Deep-fried chickpea balls served with hummus, tahini sauce, and salads.

Local Beverages

  • Arak: An anise-flavored liquor made from distilled grapes and anise seed. It is often enjoyed as an aperitif or used as a mixer in cocktails.
  • Turkish Coffee: A strong coffee roasted and brewed in a traditional copper cezve.

What to Buy in Eilat: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Israel Shuk: A market selling handicrafts, textiles, and hand-made items.
  • Dead Sea Cosmetics: A popular item to bring back, with companies manufacturing lotions, soaps, and creams made from minerals from the Dead Sea.
  • Jewelry and Gemstones: Eilat is the sole source of Eilat Stone, which is popular as jewelry.

Local Culture and Customs of Eilat

Israel is a diverse nation with people of all religions and cultures staying together. Visitors should expect to witness of a blend of cultures and lifestyles, with people being friendly and welcoming.

Best Time to Visit Eilat

The best time to visit Eilat is between March and May and September and November, with comfortable temperatures for outdoor activities.

Do’s and Don’ts of Eilat

Do:

  • Dress modestly in public places.
  • Respect religious and cultural customs.
  • Carry sunscreen, caps/hats, and water bottles when going outside.
  • Try local food and interact with locals.

Don’t:

  • Do not visit religious areas in improper attire.
  • Do not chew gum in public during the Jewish holiday.
  • Do not take photos of locals without permission.
  • Do not engage in political discussions in public.

Safety and Health

Safety in Eilat

Eilat is a safe destination for tourists, and visitors should feel comfortable walking around during the day and night.

Health in Eilat

Tourists are advised to stay hydrated and carry sunscreen, as it can get extremely hot during the summer days. Visitors can access medical assistance at the Bnai Zion Medical Center located in the town.

Getting to Eilat: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Eilat

  • Eilat Ramon Airport: The main airport serving the city, it is located 19 km north of the city center.

Bus Stations Near Eilat

  • Eilat Central and North Station: The central bus station serving Egged and Metropoline lines.
  • Eilat Airport Park Station: A bus stop en-route to the airport.

Other Options

  • Car Rental: Several car rental companies operate in Eilat.

How to Get Around in Eilat

The best way to explore Eilat is by foot or by renting a taxi, which offers convenient and affordable transportation around town.

Where to Go Next from Eilat: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Petra, Jordan

A UNESCO world heritage site, Petra offers visitors stunning architecture and ancient ruins.

The Dead Sea

The Dead Sea is famous for its unique health benefits and is an excellent way to unwind and rejuvenate.

Conclusion

Eilat is a unique destination with a blend of culture and nature, offering exciting outdoor adventures and immersive cultural experiences. With this comprehensive guide, visitors can plan their itinerary with ease, ensuring they get the most out of their visit.
