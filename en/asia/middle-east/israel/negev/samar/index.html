Introduction to Samar

Nestled in the Negev region of Israel, Samar is a hidden gem waiting to be explored. This beautiful city holds a rich history and heritage, offering visitors a glimpse into the local culture and traditions. With a plethora of historical sites, natural attractions, and adventurous activities, Samar is the perfect destination for any type of traveler.

List of Places to Visit in Samar

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Samar is home to several museums and historical sites that showcase the fascinating history and culture of the city. Some of the must-visit spots include:

  • Negev Museum of Art: Showcasing contemporary Israeli art, the museum features unique exhibitions and stunning artwork from local artists.
  • Bedouin Market: Located in the heart of the city, the Bedouin Market is a must-visit spot for any visitor looking for a unique shopping experience. From intricate handicrafts to traditional clothing and spices, the market has something for everyone.
  • Nabataean City: Also known as Avdat, the Nabataean City is a UNESCO World Heritage Site that offers visitors a glimpse into the impressive engineering and architectural skills of the Nabataean people.

Natural Attractions of Samar: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Samar boasts spectacular natural beauty that is sure to take your breath away. From hiking and biking to scenic vistas, here are some of the best outdoor adventures and natural attractions to explore in Samar:

  • Makhtesh Ramon: A stunning geological formation, Makhtesh Ramon offers visitors an otherworldly experience with its vast canyons, rock formations, and natural beauty.
  • Ein Avdat National Park: This picturesque park offers an oasis in the desert with its sparkling waterfalls and lush vegetation.
  • Zin Valley: The Zin Valley is a hiker’s paradise, with several trails that take you through stunning landscapes with breathtaking views of the desert.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Samar

Samar offers some of the best hiking and biking routes in Israel. From challenging trails to easy ones, there is something for every level of hiker or cyclist. Here are some of the most scenic routes in Samar:

  • The Black Canyon (HaArava HaShahor): The Black Canyon trail is one of the most challenging hiking routes in Samar, offering a unique and unforgettable experience for avid hikers.
  • The Ein Avdat Trail: This scenic trail takes you through the Ein Avdat National Park, showcasing the stunning waterfalls and lush vegetation of the oasis.
  • The Ramon Crater Trail: This popular hiking trail takes you on a journey through the stunning landscape of the Makhtesh Ramon, with breathtaking views of the desert and canyons.

Best Things to Do in Samar: Activities and Tours

Samar offers a wide variety of activities and tours that promise to be fun, exciting, and informative. From wine tasting to camel riding, here are some of the best things to do in Samar:

  • Jeep tours: A jeep tour is a great way to explore the rugged terrain of Samar and take in the stunning views of the desert and canyons.
  • Wine tasting: Samar is known for its excellent wines, with the local vineyards producing some of the finest wines in Israel. A wine tasting tour is a must-do activity for any wine enthusiast.
  • Camel riding: A camel ride is an authentic way to explore the desert landscape and experience the local Bedouin culture.

Week-Long Itinerary for Samar

If you’re planning a week-long trip to Samar, here is a suggested itinerary:

Day 1: Arrive in Samar and explore the local markets and historical sites. Day 2: Take a jeep tour around the surrounding desert and canyons. Day 3: Hike the Ein Avdat National Park trail and learn about the local flora and fauna. Day 4: Visit the Nabataean City and learn about the impressive engineering and architectural skills of the Nabataean people. Day 5: Go wine tasting at the local vineyards and sample some of the finest wines in Israel. Day 6: Take a camel ride through the desert and experience the local Bedouin culture. Day 7: Relax and unwind at the hotel, and reflect on the amazing experiences in Samar.

Food and Drink Scene of Samar

Must-Try Dishes

Samar has a diverse and delicious culinary scene, with traditional dishes that are sure to whet your appetite. Here are some of the must-try dishes in Samar:

  • Mansaf: A traditional Bedouin dish made with lamb, rice, and yogurt sauce.
  • Saluf: A crispy, fried dough filled with cheese or meat.
  • Shakshuka: A savory breakfast dish made with eggs, tomatoes, and spices.

Local Beverages

Samar is known for its delicious wines and coffees. Here are some of the must-try beverages in Samar:

  • Samar Wine: Samar produces some of the finest wines in Israel, with local vineyards featuring a unique blend of flavors and aromas.
  • Cardamom Coffee: A local specialty, cardamom coffee is made by grinding fresh coffee beans with cardamom pods, creating a fragrant and aromatic blend.

Where to Stay in Samar: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Samar offers a wide range of accommodations to suit every budget and preference. From luxurious hotels to budget-friendly hostels, here are some of the best places to stay in Samar:

  • Bereshit Hotel: A luxury hotel located on the edge of the Ramon Crater, offering stunning views of the surrounding landscape.
  • Desert Shade Eco Lodge: A unique and eco-friendly lodging, offering guests a comfortable and sustainable stay in the heart of the desert.
  • Samar Guest House: A cozy and affordable guesthouse, offering a charming and authentic Bedouin experience.

What to Buy in Samar: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Samar is famous for its handicrafts and local products. Here are some of the must-buy souvenirs and local products to bring home:

  • Bedouin Weaving: The local Bedouin women create intricate and beautiful weavings, such as rugs and blankets, that are a perfect souvenir to bring home.
  • Spices: The local markets in Samar offer a wide variety of spices and herbs, such as sumac and za’atar, that are essential ingredients in Middle Eastern cuisine.
  • Arak: A local liquor made with anise, arak is a popular drink in Israel and a great souvenir to bring home.

Local Culture and Customs of Samar

Samar has a rich and unique culture, shaped by its history and geography. Here are some of the local customs and traditions to keep in mind when visiting Samar:

  • Modest Clothing: Samar is a conservative region, and it is recommended to dress modestly when visiting.
  • Respect for Bedouin Culture: Samar has a strong Bedouin culture, and visitors should be respectful of their traditions and way of life.
  • Hospitality: Samar is known for its warm and welcoming hospitality, and visitors can expect to be welcomed with open arms by the locals.

Best Time to Visit Samar

The best time to visit Samar is during the spring (March to May) or fall (September to November) seasons when the weather is mild and pleasant. It is not recommended to visit during the summer months (June to August), as temperatures can get extremely hot and uncomfortable.

Do’s and Don’ts of Samar

Do:

  • Dress modestly.
  • Be respectful of the local customs and traditions.
  • Try the local cuisine and wines.

Don’t:

  • Disrespect the local culture or traditions.
  • Travel alone in remote areas.
  • Forget to bring sunscreen and plenty of water.

Safety and Health

Safety in Samar

Samar is generally a safe destination for travelers. However, it is recommended to take standard precautions, such as avoiding traveling alone in remote areas and staying in well-lit areas at night.

Health in Samar

Travelers should take precautions against the harsh desert environment, such as staying hydrated and protecting themselves from the sun with sunscreen and appropriate clothing.

Getting to Samar: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Samar

The nearest airport to Samar is the Ovda Airport, located about 20km from the city center. Flights to Ovda Airport are available from Tel Aviv, Eilat, and several European cities.

Bus Stations Near Samar

Several bus lines operate to and from Samar, including the Egged bus company and the local bus service. The nearest major bus station is located in Be’er Sheva, about 100km from Samar.

Other Options

Taxis and private car hires are also available as transportation options to Samar.

How to Get Around in Samar

The best way to get around Samar is by car, especially if you want to explore the surrounding wilderness and natural attractions. However, the city center is easily walkable and bike rentals are also available.

Where to Go Next from Samar: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Samar is located in the heart of the desert, making it a great starting point for exploring the surrounding region. Here are some of the best nearby destinations and excursions:

  • Eilat: A popular resort town located on the Red Sea, offering beaches, water activities, and an array of entertainment options.
  • Masada: A UNESCO World Heritage Site, famous for its ancient fortress and dramatic history.
  • Jerusalem: One of the most famous cities in the world, Jerusalem is located about 2 hours from Samar and offers a wealth of history, culture and religious sites to explore.

Conclusion

Samar is a unique and fascinating destination that offers visitors a wealth of history, culture, and natural beauty to explore. Whether you’re interested in hiking and biking, exploring local culture and customs or simply relaxing amidst stunning scenery, Samar has something for everyone. With this comprehensive travel guide, you’re all set for an unforgettable adventure in this hidden gem of the Negev.
