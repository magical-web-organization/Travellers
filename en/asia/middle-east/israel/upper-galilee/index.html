Introduction to Upper Galilee

Upper Galilee is a mountainous region in Northern Israel, bordered by Lebanon to the north and the Hula Valley to the south. It is known for its scenic landscapes, cultural attractions, and outdoor adventures. The region is home to several small towns and villages, each with its unique charm and history.

List of Places to Visit in Upper Galilee

Upper Galilee has plenty to offer to travelers of all kinds. Here are the best places to visit in the region:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  1. The Old City of Safed: Also known as Tzfat, the Old City of Safed is a charming labyrinth of narrow alleys and ancient buildings. It is known for its rich history and vibrant art scene, with several galleries and studios scattered throughout the area.
  2. Rosh Hanikra: A stunning natural wonder on the Mediterranean Sea, Rosh Hanikra features white cliffs and turquoise waters. The site is also home to an enchanting grotto accessible via cable car.
  3. Montfort Castle: An impressive crusader fortress dating back to the 13th century, Montfort Castle is a must-visit site for history buffs.
  4. The Nimrod Fortress: An enormous fortress built in the 13th century by the Muslim ruler Salah ad-Din, the Nimrod Fortress offers awe-inspiring views of the surrounding mountains.

Natural Attractions of Upper Galilee: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  1. Mount Meron: The highest peak in Upper Galilee, Mount Meron offers incredible views of the surrounding countryside. The area is also popular for hiking and camping.
  2. Banias Nature Reserve: A picturesque nature reserve home to a stunning waterfall and ancient ruins. It’s also popular for hiking and birdwatching.
  3. The Sea of Galilee: A freshwater lake in the Jordan Rift Valley, the Sea of Galilee is a popular spot for water sports and fishing. The area is also home to many religious sites, including the Mount of Beatitudes.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Upper Galilee

Upper Galilee is a hiker’s paradise, with miles of well-marked trails winding through forests and valleys. Here are some of the best hiking/biking routes in the region:

  1. The Israel National Trail: A 683-mile trail that begins in the northern Galilee region and winds its way south to the border with Egypt. The trail passes through some of the most stunning landscapes in Israel, including Upper Galilee.
  2. Nahal Kziv: A scenic river trail that winds through a lush valley, Nahal Kziv is famous for its stunning waterfalls and natural pools.
  3. Mount Meron: The peak of Mount Meron is reachable by foot and offers panoramic views of the Upper Galilee.

Best Things to Do in Upper Galilee: Activities and Tours

  1. Kayaking on the Jordan River: Rent a kayak or join a guided tour and paddle down the Jordan River. The river is surrounded by lush vegetation and offers stunning views of the surrounding mountains.
  2. Wine Tasting Tours: Wine lovers should check out the Upper Galilee’s vineyards and wineries. Many offer tasting tours and cooking classes to visitors.
  3. Jeep Tours: Explore the rugged landscape of Upper Galilee on a 4x4 jeep tour. The tours take visitors to scenic viewpoints and hidden natural wonders.

Week-Long Itinerary for Upper Galilee

If you’re planning a week-long trip to Upper Galilee, here is a sample itinerary:

Day 1: Explore the Old City of Safed and its art galleries and studios.

Day 2: Visit Rosh Hanikra and explore the enchanting grotto.

Day 3: Hike up Mount Meron, watch the sunrise, and enjoy a picnic breakfast at the summit.

Day 4: Explore the Banias Nature Reserve and its picturesque waterfall and ancient ruins.

Day 5: Experience kayaking on the Jordan River.

Day 6: Visit a winery in the Upper Galilee and taste some of the region’s best wines.

Day 7: Take a jeep tour of the rugged landscape of Upper Galilee.

Food and Drink Scene of Upper Galilee

Upper Galilee has a rich culinary tradition, with delicious dishes made from fresh local ingredients. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  1. Shakshuka: A popular Middle Eastern breakfast dish made of eggs cooked in a spicy tomato sauce.
  2. Mejadra: A rice and lentil dish served with caramelized onions and spices.
  3. Shawarma: Thinly sliced meat cooked on a spit and served in a pita with hummus, veggies, and tahini sauce.

Local Beverages

  1. Wine: The Upper Galilee is famous for its wineries and delicious wines. Try some Cabernet Sauvignon or Chardonnay to get a taste of the region.
  2. Arak: A traditional Middle Eastern spirit made from anis seeds and is often mixed with water and ice during summer months.

Where to Stay in Upper Galilee: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Upper Galilee offers a variety of accommodation options, each with its unique charm and amenities. Here are some of the best places to stay in the region:

  1. Hotel Mizpe Hayamim: A luxurious hotel nestled in the rolling hills of Upper Galilee, the Mizpe Hayamim offers stunning views and an indulgent spa.
  2. Kibbutz Moran Guesthouse: A quaint guesthouse on a kibbutz, the Moran Guesthouse offers a peaceful escape in the heart of Upper Galilee.
  3. Villa Galilee: A boutique hotel with stunning views of the Sea of Galilee, Villa Galilee is an excellent choice for travelers looking for a relaxing getaway.

What to Buy in Upper Galilee: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Upper Galilee is known for its locally made products, including handcrafted pottery, olive oil, and wine. Here are some of the best souvenirs to bring home:

  1. Olive oil: Upper Galilee is home to several olive groves that produce some of the best olive oil in Israel. Look for local brands in specialty shops or at farmers’ markets.
  2. Handcrafted Pottery: The region has a rich tradition of pottery-making, with many local artists producing beautiful pieces.
  3. Wine: Upper Galilee’s vineyards produce some of the best wine in Israel, making it an excellent gift to bring back home.

Local Culture and Customs of Upper Galilee

Upper Galilee has a rich cultural heritage shaped by its diverse population of Jews, Arabs, and Druze. To experience the local culture, visitors can attend festivals and events, try local food, and learn about the region’s history.

Best Time to Visit Upper Galilee

The best time to visit Upper Galilee is during the spring and fall, when temperatures are mild, and the landscape is lush and green. The summer months can be sweltering, while the winter can be cold and rainy. Overall, Upper Galilee is a year-round destination with plenty to offer visitors no matter the season.

Do’s and Don’ts of Upper Galilee

Here are some useful tips for travelers to Upper Galilee:

Do:

  1. Respect local customs and dress modestly when visiting religious sites.
  2. Explore the natural beauty of Upper Galilee by hiking or biking.
  3. Taste the local cuisine and wine.

Don’t:

  1. Wear revealing clothing in public areas.
  2. Enter religious sites without proper attire.
  3. Leave valuables in your car or unattended in public areas.

Safety and Health

Safety in Upper Galilee

Upper Galilee is generally safe for visitors, with a low crime rate. However, travelers should always exercise caution and take necessary precautions to protect themselves and their belongings.

Health in Upper Galilee

Tap water in Upper Galilee is safe for drinking, but travelers with sensitive stomachs should use bottled water. It’s also recommended to get up-to-date on vaccinations before traveling to Israel.

Getting to Upper Galilee: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Upper Galilee

The nearest airport to Upper Galilee is Ben Gurion Airport (TLV) in Tel Aviv, about a two and a half-hour drive away.

Bus Stations Near Upper Galilee

There are several bus stations in Upper Galilee, including the Central Bus Station in Tzfat and the Safed Bus Station, which offer bus services to other cities in Israel.

Other Options

Rental cars are available at the Ben Gurion Airport or in the larger cities in Israel, making it easy to travel to Upper Galilee.

How to Get Around in Upper Galilee

The best way to explore Upper Galilee is by car, although public transportation options are also available. Taxis, buses, and shared taxis (sherut) are widely available, but schedules may be limited, especially on weekends.

Where to Go Next from Upper Galilee: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

If you have extra time after exploring Upper Galilee, here are some nearby destinations worth visiting:

  1. Tiberias: A bustling city on the shore of the Sea of Galilee, Tiberias offers an array of cultural and historical attractions.
  2. The Golan Heights: Positioned on the Syrian border, the Golan Heights offer scenic landscapes and outdoor adventures, including hiking, skiing, and swimming in natural hot springs.

Conclusion

Upper Galilee is a beautiful and diverse region with something for every traveler. With its scenic landscapes, cultural attractions, and outdoor adventures, it’s a perfect destination for a unique and unforgettable trip to Israel.
