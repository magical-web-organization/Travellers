Introduction to Haifa

If you’re planning a visit to Israel, make sure you add Haifa to your itinerary! The city is located in the Carmel Range, in the northern part of Israel. Haifa is the third-largest city in Israel and a beautiful place to visit with its mix of religions, cultures, and beautiful landscapes.

List of Places to Visit in Haifa

Haifa is a beautiful and vibrant city that has much to offer tourists. Here are some of the top places to visit in Haifa:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Haifa has a rich cultural heritage that visitors can explore through their museums and historical sites:

  • Baha’i Gardens - One of the most popular tourist destinations in Haifa, these beautiful terraced gardens are a UNESCO World Heritage Site.
  • Elijah’s Cave - A significant religious site for Jews, Christians, and Muslims, as it’s where the prophet Elijah is said to have prayed.
  • Haifa Museum of Art - A cultural hotspot of Haifa, it features modern and contemporary Israeli and international art exhibitions.
  • National Museum of Science, Technology, and Space - An interactive museum displaying exhibits on various scientific topics and the history of the Israeli space program.
  • Tikotin Museum of Japanese Art - This museum showcases a remarkable collection of Japanese art curated from various periods of Japanese history.

Natural Attractions of Haifa: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Haifa’s natural attractions are as impressive as its cultural heritage:

  • Carmel National Park - A nature park located on Mount Carmel, offering scenic views and beautiful hiking trails.
  • Beaches of Haifa - Haifa has several beautiful beaches like Dado Beach and Haifa Beach, which are wonderful for swimming and sunbathing.
  • Stella Maris Monastery - This monastery on Mount Carmel offers breathtaking panoramic views of Haifa Bay and the surrounding areas.
  • Louis Promenade - A popular place for sightseeing, from which tourists can see Haifa Bay and admire the famous Golden Dome of the Baha’i Gardens from above.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Haifa

With the natural beauty of Haifa, it’s not surprising that there are several popular hiking and biking spots in and around the city:

  • Mount Carmel Hiking Trails - Offering stunning views of the Mediterranean Sea and the surrounding areas.
  • Ein Hod Trails - Located in the artists’ colony of Ein Hod, these trails showcase the beauty of the Carmel Mountains with its olive groves and vineyards.

Best Things to Do in Haifa: Activities and Tours

Haifa offers a range of activities and tours that will keep visitors fully occupied:

  • Haifa Street Food Tours - Discover the local food culture by tasting a variety of local dishes, including Shawarma, Falafel, and Hummus.
  • Wine Tasting Tours - Visit a local vineyard and taste some of the most delicious Israeli wines.
  • Night Tour of the Baha’i Gardens - Experience these stunning gardens in a different light and appreciate the illuminated beauty of the terraces and fountains.

Week-Long Itinerary for Haifa

If you have one week in Haifa, consider the following suggestions for your itinerary:

Day Itinerary
Day 1 Explore the Baha’i Gardens in the morning, have lunch at Wadi Nisnas, explore the Louis Promenade in the evening
Day 2 Check out the Haifa Museum of Art and visit the Stella Maris Monastery
Day 3 Hike the Mount Carmel trails, visit the Cave of Elijah in the afternoon
Day 4 Relax on the beaches of Haifa
Day 5 Visit the National Museum of Science, Technology, and Space in the morning and have lunch at the German Colony
Day 6 Take a street food tour and try some of the famous local fare
Day 7 Spend the day at the Carmel National Park and take in the beautiful scenery

Food and Drink Scene of Haifa

Haifa has a diverse food and drink scene that you should not miss out on during your stay:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Shawarma - A popular Middle Eastern dish consisting of grilled meat sliced and wrapped inside pita bread with salad and sauces.
  • Hummus - A tasty dip made with chickpeas, tahini, and olive oil eaten with bread or vegetables.
  • Falafel - A deep-fried vegetarian patty made from ground chickpeas, served in a pita and topped with salad or tahini.

Local Beverages

  • Turkish Coffee - Enjoy this strong brew coffee drink served with sugar and served unfiltered.
  • Lemonana - A refreshing drink made from lemonade and fresh mint leaves.
  • Arak - An anise-flavored spirit popular in the Middle East.

Where to Stay in Haifa: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Haifa offers several accommodation options that fit any budget type:

  • Dan Panorama Haifa Hotel - A 5-star hotel with views of Haifa’s port and bay.
  • Maris Hotel - A budget-friendly option located near the Baha’i gardens and Stella Maris Monastery.
  • Port Inn - A hostel located in the German Colony that offers affordable rates.

What to Buy in Haifa: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

To remember your trip to Haifa, make sure you purchase some of its souvenirs and unique products:

  • Olive oil bars and shops - Local olive oil is a famous product in Haifa and provides a unique souvenir for your trip.
  • Handmade ceramics and pottery - Unique and authentic souvenirs that you can bring home as gifts for friends and family members.
  • Spices and exotic tea mixes - Traditional Middle Eastern spices and teas like Za’atar or baglama are popular street souvenirs.

Local Culture and Customs of Haifa

Haifa is a melting pot of cultures and religions, with Arabic and Jewish influences. Visitors should respect local customs and be aware of the traditional dress codes when visiting local religious sites.

Best Time to Visit Haifa

The best time to visit Haifa is during the spring months, from March to May, when the weather is mild and pleasant, and the flowers are in full bloom.

Do’s and Don’ts of Haifa

Here are some of the do’s and don’ts in Haifa:

Do:

  • Do try the local delicacies as they are an essential part of the Haifa experience.
  • Do respect local customs and be modestly dressed when visiting religious sites.

Don’t:

  • Don’t visit religious sites during prayer times, as they are closed to tourists.
  • Don’t try to explore on your own without being properly informed.

Safety and Health

Haifa is a safe destination with low crime rates.

Safety in Haifa

  • Keep an eye on your belongings and be cautious of pickpockets in public places.

Health in Haifa

  • There are no specific health risks in Haifa. Its medical facilities offer high-quality standards for tourists in case of any emergencies.

Getting to Haifa: Transportation Options and Logistics

Haifa is a well-connected city and can be accessed through several transportation options:

Airports Near Haifa

  • Ben Gurion Airport - Israel’s main international airport, situated 120km south of Haifa.
  • Haifa Airport - Just 8km eastward of Haifa, serving regional flights.
  • Ovda Airport - Located 275km south of Haifa, serving charters and seasonal routes for major airlines.

Bus Stations Near Haifa

  • Central Bus Station - Located in the eastern part of Haifa, providing transportation to other cities and destinations.

Other Options

  • Train Services - Connecting Haifa to other major cities in Israel.
  • Taxis and Private Transfers - A convenient and flexible alternative to public transportation.

How to Get Around in Haifa

Haifa provides reasonable transportation options for tourists to navigate around the city. Buses, taxis, and trains are available and affordable in the city.

Where to Go Next from Haifa: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Akko - An ancient fortified city located in the North of Israel and a UNESCO World Heritage Site.
  • Nazareth - Of religious significance to Christians, it is the largest Arab city in Israel.
  • Tiberias - A city located on the shores of the Sea of Galilee, that has stunning views and many fun outdoor activities.

Conclusion

Haifa offers a fantastic blend of natural beauty, history and religion, culture, and contemporary experiences. So, when you visit Haifa, make sure you arrange all your days’ itineraries to fully appreciate the beauty of this cosmopolitan city.
