Abu Ghosh is a tranquil village nestled in the heart of the Jerusalem Hills, just a short drive away from Jerusalem. Known for its historical and cultural significance, Abu Ghosh attracts visitors from all over the world with its stunning scenery, outdoor adventures, and delicious food. In this comprehensive travel guide, we’ll give you an in-depth look at the best places to visit, things to do, where to stay, and much more.

Introduction to Abu Ghosh

Abu Ghosh is a small, predominantly Arab village in central Israel that has managed to preserve its rich history and customs. Its name is derived from the biblical town of Gibeah, which was located in the area. With a population of just over 7,000, Abu Ghosh is a laid-back community that takes pride in its heritage.

List of Places to Visit in Abu Ghosh

Abu Ghosh is home to several culturally significant museums and historical landmarks, making it an excellent destination for history buffs and culture enthusiasts. Here are a few places you should visit:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Church of the Resurrection: This Byzantine-era church is thought to be one of the oldest in the world.
  • Ein Hemed National Park: This beautiful oasis is home to a Crusader fortress dating back to the 13th century.
  • Abu Ghosh Crusader Fortress: This 12th-century fortress is a testament to the village’s rich history.
  • Yad La-Shiryon: Israel’s official memorial site for fallen soldiers houses a museum dedicated to the armored corps.

Natural Attractions of Abu Ghosh: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Mount Eitan: This scenic mountain offers breathtaking views of the village and the surrounding hills.
  • Tel Azeka: This hill offers an impressive view of the Judean lowlands.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Abu Ghosh

Abu Ghosh offers several hiking and biking trails that wind through the hills and valleys of the region. Some of the most popular routes include:

  • Israel Trail: This 1,000-kilometer trail passes through Abu Ghosh on its way from the northern Galilee to the southern tip of Israel.
  • Ein Hemed National Park Trail: This easy 2.5-kilometer trail takes you through the park’s lush vegetation and crystal-clear pools.
  • Wadi Qelt Trail: This challenging 15-kilometer trail takes you through the stunning Wadi Qelt ravine.

Best Things to Do in Abu Ghosh: Activities and Tours

Abu Ghosh offers a wide range of activities and tours for visitors to enjoy. Here are a few of our top picks:

  • Food Tours: One of the highlights of any trip to Abu Ghosh is the opportunity to sample the local cuisine. Take a food tour to taste some of the village’s delicious dishes.
  • Wine Tasting: The hills around Abu Ghosh are home to several top-notch wineries. Take a wine tour to learn about the region’s wine-making traditions.
  • Horseback Riding: Explore the hills and valleys of Abu Ghosh on horseback. Several stables in the area offer guided tours.

Food and Drink Scene of Abu Ghosh

Abu Ghosh is known for its delicious food, and there are plenty of fantastic restaurants and cafes to explore. Here are a few must-try dishes:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Hummus: This creamy chickpea dip is a staple of Israeli cuisine.
  • Machane Yehuda Salad: This refreshing salad is made from fresh vegetables and herbs and is typically served with tahini sauce.
  • Knafeh: This sweet pastry is made from shredded phyllo dough, cheese, and syrup.

Local Beverages

  • Wine: The Jerusalem Hills are home to several top-notch wineries, and Abu Ghosh is the perfect place to sample some of the region’s best wines.
  • Arak: This anise-flavored liquor is a popular drink throughout the Middle Eastern region.

Where to Stay in Abu Ghosh: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Abu Ghosh offers a variety of accommodations to suit every budget and taste. Here are a few of our top picks:

  • Ein Kerem Hotel: This charming boutique hotel is located just a short drive away from Abu Ghosh in the picturesque village of Ein Kerem.
  • Beit Ben Yamin: This hostel is located in the heart of Abu Ghosh and offers budget-friendly accommodations in a cozy and friendly atmosphere.
  • Bayit Bagalil: This luxurious hotel is located in the hills above Galilee and offers incredible views and top-notch amenities.

What to Buy in Abu Ghosh: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Abu Ghosh is famous for its traditional Arab crafts and local products. Here are a few things you should consider buying:

  • Handmade Ceramics: Whether you’re looking for a traditional soap dish or a modern coffee mug, you’re sure to find something unique and beautiful in one of the village’s many ceramics shops.
  • Olive Oil: Abu Ghosh is located in the Judean Hills, which is known for its high-quality olive oil.
  • Spices: The Middle East is famous for its exotic spices, and Abu Ghosh is no exception. Be sure to stock up on some unique blends to take home with you.

Local Culture and Customs of Abu Ghosh

Abu Ghosh is a predominantly Arab village, and it’s essential to respect the local customs and traditions. Here are a few tips to keep in mind:

  • Dress modestly, especially when visiting religious sites.
  • Ask permission before taking someone’s picture.
  • Be respectful of local customs and traditions, such as Ramadan and other religious holidays.

Best Time to Visit Abu Ghosh

The best time to visit Abu Ghosh is in the spring or fall when the weather is cooler and more pleasant. Summers can be scorching, with temperatures often exceeding 100 degrees Fahrenheit. However, if you’re looking to take advantage of the fantastic hiking and outdoor activities available in the region, then summer might be the best time for you.

Do’s and Don’ts of Abu Ghosh

Here are a few do’s and don’ts to keep in mind when visiting Abu Ghosh:

Do:

  • Try the local cuisine.
  • Respect local customs and traditions.
  • Learn a few Arabic phrases.

Don’t:

  • Wear revealing clothing.
  • Take photos without permission.
  • Be disrespectful of local culture.

Safety and Health

Abu Ghosh is a relatively safe destination, but it’s always best to take precautions when traveling. Here are a few things to keep in mind:

Safety in Abu Ghosh

  • Stay away from the West Bank and Gaza Strip.
  • Avoid political demonstrations or protests.
  • Keep your valuables in a safe place and be aware of your surroundings.

Health in Abu Ghosh

  • Make sure you’re up to date on all vaccinations.
  • Be careful about what you eat and drink.
  • Wear sunscreen and a hat to protect yourself from the sun’s rays.

Getting to Abu Ghosh: Transportation Options and Logistics

Abu Ghosh is located just a short drive away from Jerusalem, making it easily accessible by car or bus. Here are some transportation options:

Airports Near Abu Ghosh

  • Ben Gurion International Airport: This is Israel’s largest airport and is located about 30 minutes away from Abu Ghosh by car.

Bus Stations Near Abu Ghosh

  • Jerusalem Central Bus Station: This is the largest bus station in Israel and provides transportation to Abu Ghosh.

Other Options

  • Car Rental: Renting a car is an excellent option if you want to explore the region on your own.
  • Taxi: Taxis are readily available, but they can be expensive.

How to Get Around in Abu Ghosh

Abu Ghosh is a small village, and it’s easy to get around on foot. However, if you want to explore the surrounding hills and valleys, then renting a car or bike is your best bet.

Where to Go Next from Abu Ghosh: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Abu Ghosh is located in the heart of the Jerusalem Hills, making it an excellent base for exploring the region. Here are a few nearby destinations and excursions you might want to consider:

  • Jerusalem: This ancient city is home to some of the world’s most significant religious landmarks, making it a must-visit destination for anyone traveling to Israel.
  • Masada: This mountaintop fortress is a UNESCO World Heritage Site and offers unparalleled views of the Dead Sea and the surrounding desert.
  • Dead Sea: The lowest point on Earth, the Dead Sea is known for its unique mineral properties and is a popular destination for travelers looking to relax and rejuvenate.

Conclusion

Abu Ghosh is a hidden gem in the heart of the Jerusalem Hills, offering a perfect combination of natural beauty and cultural significance. With its stunning scenery, delicious food, and rich history, Abu Ghosh is the perfect destination for anyone looking to experience the best of Israel.
