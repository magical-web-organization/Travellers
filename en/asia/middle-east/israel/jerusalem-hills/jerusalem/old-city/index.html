Introduction to Old City

Located in the Jerusalem Hills of Israel in the Middle East, Old City is a captivating destination steeped in history and culture. This ancient city is divided into four quarters - Jewish, Armenian, Christian, and Muslim- each exuding its own charm, making it one of the most unusual places to visit in the world.

In this comprehensive travel guide, we will explore the best places to visit, things to do, and delicacies to taste in Old City. We’ll also share tips on where to stay, how to get around, local customs, and safety precautions.

List of Places to Visit in Old City

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

The Western Wall: Known as the Wailing Wall, this holy site is the most important for Jewish people worldwide.

Tower of David Museum: This fascinating museum explores Jerusalem’s history and culture through interactive, high-tech exhibits.

The Dome of the Rock: Admire the stunning gilded dome of this Islamic shrine - one of the most impressive works of architecture in the city.

The Church of the Holy Sepulchre: This holy church is believed to be the burial site of Jesus.

Natural Attractions of Old City: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

The Garden Tomb: Escape the bustle of the Old City and relax in the tranquil surroundings of the Garden Tomb.

Mount Zion: Watch the sunset over Old City from this peaceful spot.

Via Dolorosa: Stroll along the famous pilgrimage route that Jesus is believed to have walked on the way to his crucifixion.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Old City

Jerusalem Bike Tour: Tour the city’s historic sites and modern-day marvels by bicycle.

Ramparts Walk: Take a scenic stroll along the ancient walls of Old City.

Mount of Olives Trek: Trek along the slopes of the Mount of Olives to enjoy spectacular views of the city.

Best Things to Do in Old City: Activities and Tours

Food Tours: Savor traditional dishes of Middle Eastern cuisine while exploring the historic markets of Old City.

Night Tours: Witness the city’s history come alive by taking a tour after dark.

Horse-Drawn Carriage Rides: Take a charming ride through Old City’s ancient streets in a traditional horse-drawn carriage.

Food and Drink Scene of Old City

Must-Try Dishes

Falafel: These crunchy balls of chickpeas and spices are the perfect snack to keep you going while exploring the Old City.

Hummus: Try some of the best hummus in the city at Abu Shukri.

Shawarma: Relish the mouth-watering taste of meat, vegetables, and hummus rolled into a wrap.

Local Beverages

Mint Tea: Refresh yourself with a refreshing glass of mint tea after a day exploring Old City.

Arak: Try the local spirit with a distinct aniseed flavor, arak, while enjoying the vibrant nightlife of Jerusalem.

Where to Stay in Old City: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

The Sephardic House: Located in the Jewish Quarter, this restored 19th-century building offers spacious rooms and brilliant views of the Old City.

Christ Church Guest House: A pleasant guest house in the Christian Quarter offers traditional Middle Eastern cuisine, garden views, and proximity to local attractions.

What to Buy in Old City: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Olive Oil Soap: These natural soaps not only smell fantastic, but they also make great gifts.

Kippots: A traditional head-covering worn by Jewish people is easily available throughout the Old City market.

Local Culture and Customs of Old City

It’s worth noting that Old City is a religious and conservative city, you should dress modestly and respectfully throughout the city. As Old City is predominantly Muslim, it’s important to avoid eating or drinking on the streets during Ramadan out of respect.

Best Time to Visit Old City

The shoulder seasons, March to May and September to November, are the ideal times to explore Old City. These months are cooler, and the crowds are thinner, allowing you to enjoy Jerusalem’s attractions at a leisurely pace.

Do’s and Don’ts of Old City

Do:

  • Respect religious customs and traditions.
  • Learn a few Hebrew or Arabic phrases to help you communicate with locals.
  • Dress appropriately when visiting religious sites.

Don’t:

  • Take pictures of strangers or people without their consent.
  • Touch religious artifacts or monuments.
  • Lose your temper or get into arguments with locals.

Safety and Health

Safety in Old City

While Old City is generally safe, it’s essential to keep an eye on your belongings and stay aware of your surroundings, particularly if you’re traveling alone.

Health in Old City

Tap water in Old City is generally safe to drink, but it’s better to stick with bottled water to be on the safe side. Moreover, it is advisable to carry sunscreen and always stay hydrated as the desert climate can be harsh.

Getting to Old City: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Old City

Ben Gurion Airport: Tel Aviv’s Ben Gurion Airport is the nearest international airport, located about 50 kilometers northwest of Old City.

Bus Stations Near Old City

There are several bus stops within Old City, which means that all the city’s major attractions are within walking distance.

Other Options

The light railway system in Jerusalem is a convenient and affordable way to move around the city.

How to Get Around in Old City

Walking Old City is an attractive way to get to know this ancient city. Bicycles and horse-drawn carriages are also available for hire.

Where to Go Next from Old City: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

The Dead Sea: Visit the lowest point on earth and float in the mineral-rich waters of the Dead Sea, just an hour’s drive from Old City.

Bethlehem: Visit the charming and historical town of Bethlehem, believed to be the birthplace of Jesus Christ, a mere 30-minute drive from Old City.

Conclusion

Exploring the Old City of Jerusalem is a unique experience, offering a nostalgic trip back in history. With various ancient sites, rich culture, and natural landscapes, it leaves visitors captivated and audiences spellbound. We hope this guide will provide you with a plethora of information and make your visit to Old City the trip of a lifetime.
