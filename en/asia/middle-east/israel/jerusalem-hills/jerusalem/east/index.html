Introduction to East

East, located in Jerusalem Hills in Israel, is a culturally rich and naturally stunning region that attracts thousands of tourists every year. This region is known for its incredible blend of historical, cultural, and religious sites, as well as its beautiful natural landscapes.

Whether you’re a history buff or an outdoor enthusiast, East has something to offer for everyone. This travel guide will provide you with all the necessary information you need to plan your trip to East, including a list of places to visit, hiking/biking routes, food and drink scene, where to stay, and more.

List of Places to Visit in East

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

If you’re a history buff or interested in cultural sites, East has several historical places and museums to visit:

  • Old City of Jerusalem: Located in the heart of East, the Old City of Jerusalem is a UNESCO World Heritage Site and home to several holy sites, including the Western Wall, the Church of the Holy Sepulchre, and the Dome of the Rock.
  • Yad Vashem: Israel’s official memorial to the victims of the Holocaust is located in Mount Herzl in Jerusalem. It’s a touching and informative museum that takes visitors on a journey through the atrocities of Nazi Germany.
  • Israel Museum: This museum located in West Jerusalem has a vast collection of ancient and modern Israeli art, as well as the world’s largest collection of Dead Sea Scrolls.

Natural Attractions of East: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

For outdoor enthusiasts, East offers a range of activities and scenic views:

  • Mount of Olives: This mountain offers a stunning panoramic view of the Old City of Jerusalem and is one of the most significant sites in Judaism.
  • Jerusalem Hills: This region offers several hiking and biking trails where you can enjoy breathtaking views of the surrounding landscapes.
  • Ein Prat Nature Reserve: Located in the heart of Jerusalem Hills, this nature reserve offers a unique blend of natural beauty and historical sites, including the remains of ancient Roman aqueducts.

Hiking/Biking Routes of East

East offers several hiking/biking routes for outdoor enthusiasts:

  • Jerusalem Trail: This trail is a 42-kilometer route that passes through several historical and cultural sites in East and West Jerusalem.
  • National Parks Trail: This trail is a 20-kilometer route that passes through the beautiful natural landscapes of East, including Ein Prat Nature Reserve and Sataf Springs.

Best Things to Do in East: Activities and Tours

Apart from historical sites and outdoor adventures, East offers several activities and tours:

  • Food Tour: Take a food tour in East and explore the local dining scene, including a taste of authentic Middle Eastern cuisine.
  • City Tour: Take a city tour of East and explore the Old City of Jerusalem and other cultural and historical sites.
  • Religious Tour: If you’re interested in religion, take a religious tour of East and visit sites significant to Judaism, Islam, and Christianity.

Food and Drink Scene of East

Must-Try Dishes

East is known for its authentic Middle Eastern cuisine, and you must try some local dishes:

  • Hummus and Pita Bread: A spread made with chickpeas, tahini, lemon, and garlic, served with fresh pita bread.
  • Shakshuka: Eggs cooked in a flavorful tomato sauce with vegetables and spices.
  • Falafel: Deep-fried chickpea balls served in a pita or as a snack.

Local Beverages

Apart from food, East has a strong tradition of making local beverages:

  • Arak: An anise-flavored alcoholic drink that’s considered the national drink of Israel.
  • Israeli Wine: East is home to several vineyards, and the region is known for producing excellent quality wine. Try some local varieties during your trip.
  • Mint Tea: A traditional Middle Eastern tea made with fresh mint leaves and sugar, served hot.

Where to Stay in East: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

East has several options for accommodation, ranging from budget-friendly hostels to luxurious hotels:

  • Abraham Hostel: A budget-friendly hostel located in the heart of East, known for its comfortable rooms and lively atmosphere.
  • Waldorf Astoria Jerusalem: A luxurious hotel located in West Jerusalem, known for its elegant and spacious rooms, excellent services, and central location.
  • Herod’s Palace: A unique guesthouse that’s located near historical sites in East, featuring authentic decor and a friendly staff.

What to Buy in East: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

If you want to bring back some souvenirs and local products from East, consider these options:

  • Olive Oil: East is known for producing high-quality olive oil, and you can buy some during your trip.
  • Jewelry and Handicrafts: East is also known for its unique jewelry and handicrafts, including pottery, glassware, and textiles.
  • Spices and Condiments: You can also buy Middle Eastern spices and condiments, such as za’atar, sumac, and tahini, from local markets.

Local Culture and Customs of East

East has a rich cultural heritage with a mix of Jewish, Muslim, and Christian traditions:

  • Religion: East is home to several significant religious sites, and visitors should respect the traditions and practices of these faiths.
  • Attire: Dress modestly, especially when visiting religious sites, and avoid revealing clothing.
  • Language: Arabic and Hebrew are the official languages of Israel, but English is widely spoken.

Best Time to Visit East

The best time to visit East is during the spring (March to May) or fall (September to November) when the weather is mild, and crowds are fewer. Summers (June to August) are hot and dry, and winters (December to February) can be cold and rainy.

Do’s and Don’ts of East

Do:

  • Respect the cultural traditions and practices of the region.
  • Dress modestly when visiting religious sites.
  • Try the local cuisine and immerse yourself in the local culture.

Don’t:

  • Display public affection.
  • Use offensive language or gestures.
  • Disrespect religious sites or practices.

Safety and Health

Safety in East

East is a safe destination for tourists. However, visitors should be aware of pickpocketing and theft in crowded areas, especially in the Old City of Jerusalem. It’s also advisable to avoid political demonstrations and gatherings.

Health in East

East has a good healthcare system, and visitors can access medical facilities easily. However, travelers should have travel insurance to cover any medical emergencies. It’s also advisable to drink bottled water to prevent any water-borne illnesses.

Getting to East: Transportation Options and Logistics

East is accessible by several modes of transportation:

Airports Near East

  • Ben Gurion Airport: Located in Tel Aviv, approximately 50 km west of East, is Israel’s primary international airport.

Bus Stations Near East

  • Central Bus Station Jerusalem: Located in West Jerusalem, is the main bus station serving the region.

Other Options

  • Train: Israel has a well-connected railway system, and you can take a train to Jerusalem from various other cities.
  • Car Rental: Renting a car is also an option, and visitors can book a car at Ben Gurion Airport.

How to Get Around in East

East is a small region, and visitors can easily explore the sites on foot. Buses and taxis are also available and can be used to travel within the region.

Where to Go Next from East: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

East is located near several other destinations that are worth exploring:

  • Dead Sea: Located approximately 60 km southeast of East, is famous for its unique and therapeutic waters.
  • Masada: Located approximately 70 km southeast of East, is a UNESCO World Heritage Site and an important historical site in Judaism.
  • Tel Aviv: Located approximately 70 km west of East, is a vibrant city known for its nightlife, beaches, and culture.

Conclusion

East is a fascinating region, and visitors can explore its rich cultural heritage, breathtaking natural landscapes, and unique culinary scene. This travel guide provides all the necessary information to plan your trip to East, whether you’re a history buff, outdoor enthusiast, or just looking to relax and unwind.
