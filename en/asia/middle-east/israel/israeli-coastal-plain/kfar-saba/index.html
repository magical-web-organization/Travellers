Introduction to Kfar Saba

Located in the Israeli Coastal Plain region, Kfar Saba is a charming city that is rapidly growing in popularity among travellers. With a rich history that dates back to the Canaanite period, Kfar Saba offers visitors plenty of ancient ruins, museums, and historical attractions. The bustling city is also known for its outdoor adventures, scenic views, and vibrant food scene.

List of Places to Visit in Kfar Saba

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Gezer Regional Museum: A fascinating museum that showcases the history of Gezer and surrounding regions.

  • Beit Hapalmach Museum: A museum dedicated to the history of the Jewish underground movement during the British Mandate.

  • Sarona Compound: A restored Templar colony that now houses trendy restaurants, bars, and shops.

  • Tel Afek National Park: A beautiful park that contains the remains of biblical-period fortifications, a Byzantine church, and a Crusader fortress.

Natural Attractions of Kfar Saba: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Park Hamesilla: A long park that stretches from Ra’anana to Hod Hasharon, perfect for biking, jogging or leisurely strolls.

  • Taninim Stream Nature Reserve: Ideal for nature lovers, this stunning reserve has scenic walking trails, breathtaking views and a variety of flora and fauna.

  • Ya’ar Hakdumim: A natural forest reserve, home to rare species of flora and fauna, and several hiking trails.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Kfar Saba

  • Israel National Trail: This is a 1000km trail spanning the length of Israel, crossing Kfar Saba. It is perfect for hikers, cyclists and those who prefer long walks in nature.

  • Park Hamesilla: As previously mentioned, it is a perfect park for biking, jogging or leisurely strolls.

  • Nachal Kana: A beautiful reserve that has a variety of scenic hiking trails that are perfect for beginners and easy to navigate.

Best Things to Do in Kfar Saba: Activities and Tours

  • Cooking Classes: Learn the art of Israeli cuisine by joining one of many cooking classes in Kfar Saba.

  • Art Courses: The city is host to several art schools, offering courses that cater to a wide range of interests.

  • City Tour: A guided tour of Kfar Saba’s attractions is an ideal way to explore the city and learn more about its history and culture.

Food and Drink Scene of Kfar Saba

Must-Try Dishes

Kfar Saba is famous for its diverse and delicious cuisine. Here are some must-try dishes

  • Hummus: A Middle Eastern staple, variations of hummus can be found across the city

  • Shakshuka: A dish of eggs poached in tomato sauce with spices and vegetables

  • Falafel: Deep-fried chickpea balls that are a favourite street food in Israel

Local Beverages

  • Arak: A locally made anise-flavoured spirit, best served with water, ice, and fresh mint.

  • Wine: Israel is known for its superb wineries, and Kfar Saba has many to offer, including Tishbi and Margalit.

Where to Stay in Kfar Saba: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Sharon Hotel: A small, but stylish hotel located in the city centre.

  • Sofia Suites: These apartments offer great value and are ideal if you’re planning a longer stay.

  • Kfar Saba Home Hospitality: An excellent choice if you’re looking for an authentic experience.

What to Buy in Kfar Saba: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Jewellery: Kfar Saba has many skilled jewellers who create unique pieces that reflect the city’s culture and history.

  • Olive Oil: locally produced olive oil is a must-try

Local Culture and Customs of Kfar Saba

  • Language: Hebrew and Arabic are the official languages, but you’ll find many locals speak English.

  • Dress Code: Modest dress is expected when visiting religious sites.

Best Time to Visit Kfar Saba

The perfect time to visit Kfar Saba is between March and May when the weather is mild and pleasant.

Do’s and Don’ts of Kfar Saba

Do:

  • Try traditional dishes: Kfar Saba is known for its delicious cuisine, so make sure to try traditional dishes during your visit.

  • Be respectful: Israel is a religious country, and cultural customs should be respected.

Don’t:

  • Visit religious sites on Saturdays: Saturday is the Jewish Sabbath, so it’s best to avoid visiting religious sites on this day.

  • Smoke in public spaces: Smoking is not allowed in public spaces, including restaurants and cafes.

Safety and Health

Safety in Kfar Saba

Kfar Saba is generally safe for travellers. However, it’s always a good idea to stay vigilant and avoid walking alone at night.

Health in Kfar Saba

The tap water is safe to drink, and the city has adequate medical facilities.

Getting to Kfar Saba: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Kfar Saba

  • Ben Gurion Airport (TLV): Israel’s largest airport is 31 km south of Kfar Saba.

Bus Stations Near Kfar Saba

  • Central Bus Station Tel Aviv: The largest bus station in the country, it’s located 40 minutes away from Kfar Saba.

Other Options

  • Train: Kfar Saba is serviced by the train station at Ra’anana-West.

How to Get Around in Kfar Saba

Kfar Saba has an excellent public transport system, including buses, trains and taxis.

Where to Go Next from Kfar Saba: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Herzliya: A coastal city 17 km south-west of Kfar Saba, known for its beaches and marina.

  • Netanya: A coastal city 13 km north of Kfar Saba, known for its beaches, food and entertainment.

Conclusion

Kfar Saba offers a unique blend of natural beauty, cultural experiences, and adventure activities. With its friendly locals and delicious food, there’s no shortage of things to see and do. A visit to Kfar Saba promises to be a memorable trip for anyone who decides to explore this charming city.
