Introduction to Ramla

Ramla has been an essential city in Israel for centuries, as it stands at the crossroads of ancient trade routes. Ramla’s old quarter is full of gems that include well-preserved, ancient Islamic and Christian landmarks. Many interested and curious visitors come to Ramla to explore the city’s historic gates and visit the Tomb of Gebara.

List of Places to Visit in Ramla

Whether you are interested in outdoor adventures, exploring historical landmarks, or enjoying delicious food, Ramla has something to offer for everyone. Here’s a list of top places to visit in Ramla.

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • White Mosque (Great Mosque): One of Israel’s most significant mosques from the early Muslim period.
  • Pool of Arches: It is believed to have been part of a bathhouse during the reign of the Umayyad dynasty in the 7th century.
  • Tower of Ramla: It was built in the 13th century by the Christians and was used as a prison by the Ottomans.
  • Church of Saint Nicodemus: An ancient church that dates back to the Roman period.

Natural Attractions of Ramla: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Kassem Trail: A six-kilometer-long urban trail that runs through Ramla and is perfect for walking, biking, and jogging.
  • Manara cliff: A scenic viewpoint at the cliff’s edge overlooking the coastal plain of Ramla, with the Mediterranean Sea in the distance.
  • Ramat Hanadiv: A nature reserve that stretches over 4,000 acres, with several walking and biking trails.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Ramla

Ramla has several hiking and biking routes that will take you through scenic vistas, offering breathtaking views of the surrounding landscapes. Some of the must-try routes include:

  • Ayalon-Canada Park trail: A popular destination for hikers and bikers that offers an exceptional natural experience.
  • HaBesor Stream: This route runs from the Beit Guvrin National Park to the coastal plain of Ashkelon.

Best Things to Do in Ramla: Activities and Tours

Whether you are traveling alone or with your family, Ramla has a lot to offer in terms of activities and tours. Some of the best things to do in Ramla include:

  • Ramla Market: A bustling market where you can buy everything from fresh produce to antiques.
  • Cooking classes: Take cooking classes and learn to make some of the local delicacies.
  • Walking tours: Explore the city’s history and discover details that you would miss on your own.
  • Private tours: Take a day trip to Jerusalem, Tel-Aviv, or other nearby cities.

Week-Long Itinerary for Ramla

If you are planning a week-long trip to Ramla, here’s a suggested itinerary for the perfect vacation experience.

Food and Drink Scene of Ramla

Israeli cuisine is an essential part of the cultural identity, and Ramla has a bustling and vibrant food scene. Here are some of the must-try dishes and local beverages in Ramla.

Must-Try Dishes

  • Schnitzel: It is a fried or breaded meat dish, and there are several variations.
  • Shakshuka: A dish with eggs poached in a tomato and pepper sauce.
  • Falafel: A dish made of mashed chickpeas and deep-fried into small balls.
  • Hummus: A dip made of chickpeas, tahini, and lemon juice.

Local Beverages

  • Arak: A Middle Eastern alcoholic drink made from aniseed.
  • Israeli wine: Israel’s wine industry is thriving, and you can find a range of local wines to try.

Where to Stay in Ramla: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Ramla has several options when it comes to accommodation, from budget-friendly hostels to luxurious five-star hotels. Some of the best options include:

  • Leonardo Hotel: A four-star hotel with a pool and a sauna.
  • Margoa Hotel: A five-star hotel with exceptional service and rooms.
  • Casa Nova Guesthouse: A cozy guesthouse with a homey atmosphere.

What to Buy in Ramla: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Bring a piece of Ramla’s culture back home with you by buying these souvenirs and local products:

  • Olive Oil: Israel’s olive oil is known for its quality and taste.
  • Handmade Judaica: Ramla’s markets are the ideal place to find handmade Judaica.
  • Arabic Coffee Cups: A set of Arabic coffee cups with traditional patterns makes for a perfect souvenir.

Local Culture and Customs of Ramla

Ramla is a mix of two worlds, with its Jewish and Islamic culture coming together to create a unique atmosphere. Visitors are required to dress modestly when visiting the mosques and holy sites. It is also customary to remove shoes before entering religious sites.

Best Time to Visit Ramla

The best time to visit Ramla is from March to May and from September to November when the weather is mild, and the crowds are thinner. The summers can be hot and humid, making walking and exploring the city’s outdoor attractions challenging.

Do’s and Don’ts of Ramla

Do:

  • Cover your shoulders and knees when visiting mosques and other religious sites.
  • Try the local cuisine and local wines.
  • Explore the old city at night for a breathtaking view.

Don’t:

  • Visit religious sites during prayer times.
  • Wear revealing clothing when visiting religious sites.
  • Ignore the dress code at holy sites.

Safety and Health

Safety in Ramla

Ramla is a relatively safe city, and tourists rarely face any problems. However, it is always recommended to take precautions when traveling to foreign cities.

Health in Ramla

There are no critical health concerns in Ramla, and tourists do not require any vaccines before traveling to Israel.

Getting to Ramla: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Ramla

The closest airport to Ramla is Ben Gurion International Airport, which is about 20 kilometers away. The airport has several transportation options, including taxis and buses.

Bus Stations Near Ramla

There are several bus stations in Ramla that connect the city to other parts of Israel.

Other Options

  • Train: Ramla also has a train station connected to a network of trains throughout Israel.

How to Get Around in Ramla

Ramla is a small city and can be explored on foot. Bikes and rental cars are also available.

Where to Go Next from Ramla: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Whether you want to explore Israel’s history or spend some time on the beach, Ramla is the perfect base to explore the following nearby destinations:

  • Jerusalem: One of the holiest cities in the world, with several historical and religious sites.
  • Tel Aviv: A vibrant and energetic city known for its beaches, nightlife, and modern architecture.
  • Dead Sea: A unique experience that can’t be missed, with its mineral-rich waters and therapeutic mud.

Conclusion

Ramla is much more than just a stop on a tour of Israel. It has a unique combination of diverse cultures, rich history, and tantalizing food. Whether you’re looking to step back in time and learn about the city’s past or explore the hiking trails and outdoor adventures, Ramla has something for every traveler.
