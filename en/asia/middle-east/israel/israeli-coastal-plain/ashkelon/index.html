Introduction to Ashkelon

Located on the Israeli Coastal Plain, Ashkelon is one of Israel’s oldest cities and a hub for history, culture, and outdoor adventure. Just a short drive from Tel Aviv, Ashkelon is a popular destination for travelers looking to experience the country’s diverse landscape and rich heritage. From ancient ruins to sandy beaches and delicious cuisine, there’s something for everyone in Ashkelon.

List of Places to Visit in Ashkelon

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

One of the best things to do in Ashkelon is to explore the city’s rich history and cultural heritage. There are a number of museums and historical sites that are well worth a visit, including:

  • Ashkelon National Park: A sprawling archaeological site that boasts the remains of an ancient city, a Crusader fortress, and a Roman amphitheater. The park is perfect for history buffs and features informative exhibits that detail the city’s fascinating past.

  • The Ashkelon Museum of Archaeology: Housed in a restored Ottoman-era building, this museum features a collection of ancient artifacts and exhibits that showcase the city’s history and culture.

  • The Barzilai Medical Center: A state-of-the-art medical facility that offers a unique look at Israel’s healthcare system.

Natural Attractions of Ashkelon: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Ashkelon is also known for its stunning natural beauty, including beautiful beaches and serene parks. Here are a few of the city’s top natural attractions:

  • Ancient Ashkelon City Walls: A scenic park that offers sweeping views of the Mediterranean Sea and the city’s historic walls.

  • Ashkelon Beach: A beautiful stretch of coastline that is perfect for swimming, sunbathing, and water sports. The beach is lined with cafes, restaurants, and beachside bars.

  • The Ashkelon National Park: In addition to its archaeological sites, the park also features beautiful gardens and nature trails. Visitors can explore the park on foot, by bike, or on a guided tour.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Ashkelon

Ashkelon is a great destination for outdoor enthusiasts, thanks to its incredible network of hiking and biking trails. Some of the city’s top routes include:

  • The Ashkelon Coastal Trail: A popular 12-kilometer trail that follows the coast and offers stunning views of the Mediterranean.

  • The Ashkelon City Trail: A scenic route that winds through the city’s historic neighborhoods and passes by many of its main attractions.

  • The Ashkelon Birding Trail: A unique trail that takes visitors on a tour of the city’s top birding spots, including the nearby Rotem Plain.

Best Things to Do in Ashkelon: Activities and Tours

There are plenty of other things to do in Ashkelon, too. Here are a few more activity and tour ideas to consider:

  • Ashkelon Beer Tour: Visit some of the city’s top microbreweries and sample some of the best local beers.

  • Ashkelon Cooking Classes: Learn how to make traditional Israeli dishes with a local chef.

  • Day trip to nearby cities: Take a day trip to Tel Aviv, Jerusalem, or Be’er Sheva and explore one of Israel’s other exciting destinations.

Week-Long Itinerary for Ashkelon

For visitors who want to experience everything that Ashkelon has to offer, we recommend the following week-long itinerary:

Day 1: Explore Ashkelon National Park, visit the Barzilai Medical Center, and relax on Ashkelon Beach.

Day 2: Visit the Ashkelon Museum of Archaeology, then take a bike tour of Ashkelon’s historic neighborhoods.

Day 3: Take a day trip to Tel Aviv and explore the city’s famous markets, galleries, and museums.

Day 4: Explore the scenic Ashkelon Coastal Trail and enjoy the stunning views of the Mediterranean.

Day 5: Take a cooking class and learn how to make traditional Israeli dishes.

Day 6: Visit nearby Be’er Sheva and explore the city’s rich history and cultural heritage.

Day 7: Relax on the beach or take a birding tour of the nearby Rotem Plain.

Food and Drink Scene of Ashkelon

The food and drink scene in Ashkelon is a reflection of the city’s diverse cultural heritage. Visitors can sample everything from traditional Israeli cuisine to modern fusion dishes. Here are a few must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Shakshuka: A breakfast dish made with eggs, tomatoes, and spices.

  • Hummus: A chickpea dip that is a staple of Middle Eastern cuisine.

  • Falafel: Fried balls made of chickpeas or fava beans, typically served in a pita.

  • Sabich: An Israeli sandwich made with eggplant, hummus, and hard-boiled eggs.

Local Beverages

  • Wine: Israel is home to a growing wine industry and Ashkelon is no exception.

  • Arak: A traditional Middle Eastern liquor made from anise seeds.

  • Craft Beer: Ashkelon is home to several microbreweries that produce top-quality beer.

Where to Stay in Ashkelon: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

There are plenty of accommodation options in Ashkelon, from high-end hotels to budget-friendly hostels. Here are a few of our top picks:

  • Dan Gardens Ashkelon: A beachfront hotel with stunning views of the Mediterranean Sea.

  • Ashkelon Marina Guesthouse: A cozy guesthouse located in the heart of the marina.

  • HI Ashkelon Hostel: A budget-friendly option that offers clean and comfortable accommodations.

What to Buy in Ashkelon: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Visitors to Ashkelon will find plenty of unique souvenirs and locally-made products to take home. Here are a few ideas:

  • Handmade jewelry: Visit one of the city’s many jewelry shops and pick up a one-of-a-kind piece.

  • Spices: The Middle East is known for its incredible spice blends, so be sure to stock up on local favorites.

  • Pottery: Ashkelon is home to several ceramics studios that produce beautiful, handcrafted pieces.

Local Culture and Customs of Ashkelon

Ashkelon is a city that is full of cultural diversity and rich history. Visitors should be respectful of local customs and take the time to learn about the city’s unique traditions. Here are a few customs to keep in mind:

  • Shabbat: Friday evening through Saturday evening is considered the Sabbath in Israel. Many businesses and attractions will be closed during this time, so plan your itinerary accordingly.

  • Dress Code: Israel is a conservative country and visitors should dress appropriately, especially when visiting religious sites.

  • Photography: Always ask permission before taking photos of local people or in sacred sites.

Best Time to Visit Ashkelon

The best time to visit Ashkelon is during the spring or fall, when temperatures are mild and the crowds are smaller. Summer can be hot and crowded, while winter can be rainy and chilly. Visitors should also keep in mind Israel’s religious holidays, as many businesses and attractions may be closed during these times.

Do’s and Don’ts of Ashkelon

Here are a few other do’s and don’ts to keep in mind when visiting Ashkelon:

Do:

  • Respect local customs and traditions.

  • Be aware of your surroundings, especially when traveling alone at night.

  • Try local cuisine and beverages.

Don’t:

  • Play loud music or be disruptive to your neighbors.

  • Take photos of military installations, government buildings, or other sensitive areas.

  • Discriminate against anyone based on their religion, gender, or sexual orientation.

Safety and Health

Israel is a safe and modern country, but visitors should still take precautions to ensure their safety and health while traveling. Here are a few tips to keep in mind:

Safety in Ashkelon

  • Be aware of your surroundings and stay alert at all times, especially when visiting crowded tourist areas.

  • Avoid taking unlicensed taxis or walking alone in isolated areas.

  • Make copies of your passport and other important documents and store them in a safe place.

Health in Ashkelon

  • Make sure your vaccinations are up-to-date before traveling to Israel.

  • Drink bottled water and avoid tap water, especially in rural areas.

  • Wear sunscreen and protect yourself from the sun to avoid heatstroke or sunburn.

Getting to Ashkelon: Transportation Options and Logistics

Ashkelon is easily accessible by several means of transportation, including:

Airports Near Ashkelon

  • Ben Gurion International Airport: The main airport in Israel, located in Tel Aviv.

  • Sde-Dov Airport: A small airport that serves mainly domestic flights.

Bus Stations Near Ashkelon

  • Ashkelon Central Bus Station: Located in the city center, the station offers regular bus services to other cities throughout Israel.

  • Ashkelon Train Station: Train services run from Ashkelon to Tel Aviv and other major cities throughout Israel.

Other Options

  • Taxi: Taxis are widely available throughout Ashkelon and can be hailed on the street or booked through a mobile app.

  • Car rental: Visitors can rent a car at Ben Gurion Airport or at several locations throughout Ashkelon.

How to Get Around in Ashkelon

Getting around Ashkelon is relatively easy, thanks to the city’s well-developed transportation network. Visitors can use public transportation, take taxis, or rent a car to get around. Biking and walking are also popular options, especially for visitors who want to explore the historic neighborhoods.

Where to Go Next from Ashkelon: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Israel is a small but diverse country, and there are plenty of nearby destinations and excursions that make for great day trips from Ashkelon. Here are a few ideas:

  • Tel Aviv: Israel’s cultural capital, Tel Aviv is just a short drive from Ashkelon and offers plenty of attractions, including museums, galleries, and restaurants.

  • Jerusalem: One of the world’s most important religious cities, Jerusalem is a hub for history and culture.

  • Eilat: Located at the southern tip of Israel, Eilat is a bustling resort town that offers incredible snorkeling and diving opportunities.

Conclusion

Ashkelon is a city that is bursting with history, culture, and natural beauty. Whether you’re a history buff, an outdoor enthusiast, or a food lover, there’s something for everyone in this vibrant city. From ancient ruins to sandy beaches and delicious cuisine, Ashkelon is a destination that should not be missed.
