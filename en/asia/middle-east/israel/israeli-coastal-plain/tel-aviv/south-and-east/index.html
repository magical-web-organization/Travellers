Introduction to South and East

South and East are two districts in Tel Aviv that offer travelers a wide variety of cultural, historical, and natural attractions. Located in the Israeli Coastal Plain, one of the most beautiful areas in Israel, South and East fascinate visitors with their stunning landscapes and rich history.

List of Places to Visit in South and East

  • Neve Tzedek: A picturesque neighborhood known for its charming architecture, boutique shops, and trendy cafes.
  • Tel Aviv Port: A bustling waterfront area with a variety of restaurants, bars, and shops.
  • Jaffa: An ancient port city with a rich history, including a famous flea market, an old port, and historical sites.
  • The Carmel Market: A bustling market with a wide variety of food stalls, spices, and souvenirs.
  • HaTachana: A restored train station turned into a complex with cafes, restaurants, and shops.
  • Yemenite Quarter: Known for its authentic Yemenite cuisine and architecture.
  • Hatachanah Beach: A picturesque beach located near HaTachana.
  • Sarona Market: A modern indoor market with a variety of food stalls, shops, and events.
  • Rothschild Boulevard: A historic street with beautiful Bauhaus buildings, great cafes, and restaurants.

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Palmach Museum: A museum dedicated to the Palmach, the elite Jewish fighting force that played a vital role in the establishment of Israel.
  • Independence Hall: The site where David Ben Gurion declared Israel’s independence in 1948.
  • Bauhaus Center: A museum dedicated to Tel Aviv’s famous Bauhaus architecture.
  • Eretz Israel Museum: A museum that showcases Israel’s history and culture through an extensive collection of artifacts, art, and archaeology.
  • Old Jaffa: A historic district filled with ancient buildings, galleries, and museums.

Natural Attractions of South and East: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Charles Clore Park: A beautiful park located on the beachfront with a children’s playground, sports facilities, and BBQ areas.
  • Hayarkon Park: A large park with walking trails, sports facilities, picnic areas, and a lake.
  • Tel Aviv Beaches: Some of the most beautiful beaches in Israel, including Hilton Beach, Banana Beach, and Gordon Beach.
  • Sde Dov Beach: A stunning beach located near Tel Aviv Port.
  • Jaffa Port: A scenic port with its rocky dwellings and amazing views.
  • HaYarkon River: A lovely river that flows through Tel Aviv and is a great spot for kayaking or paddleboarding.

Hiking/Biking Routes of South and East

  • The Tzuk Beach Cliff Walk: A scenic hiking trail that starts at Tzuk Beach and winds along the cliffside overlooking the beach.
  • The Napa Trail: A beautiful hiking trail that loops through the Carmel Forest.
  • The Yarkon Trail: A scenic biking path that runs along the Hayarkon River.
  • The Neve Tzedek Trail: A walking trail that passes through the charming streets of Neve Tzedek.

Best Things to Do in South and East: Activities and Tours

  • Tel Aviv Graffiti Tour: Discover the city’s vibrant street art scene with a walking tour.
  • Tel Aviv Food Tour: Sample the city’s delicious cuisine with a guided food tour.
  • Tel Aviv Bike Tour: Explore the city’s famous sites and attractions with a bike tour.
  • Jaffa Old City Tour: A guided walking tour of Jaffa’s ancient streets.
  • Israeli Wine Tour: Visit the country’s famous vineyards and wineries with a guided tour.

Food and Drink Scene of South and East

Must-Try Dishes

  • Hummus: A classic Middle Eastern dish made from chickpeas, tahini, and olive oil.
  • Shakshuka: A popular breakfast dish of poached eggs in a tomato and pepper sauce.
  • Falafel: Deep-fried balls made of chickpeas, herbs, and spices.
  • Sabich: A vegetarian sandwich made with eggplants, hard-boiled eggs, and tahini.
  • Malabi: A creamy Middle Eastern dessert made from milk, cornstarch, and rosewater.

Local Beverages

  • Arak: A traditional Middle Eastern alcohol made from fermented grapes and flavored with anise.
  • Israeli Wine: Israel produces some of the finest wines in the world, particularly those from the Golan Heights and the Judean Hills.
  • Goldstar Beer: A popular Israeli beer, brewed since 1950.

Where to Stay in South and East: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Abraham Hostel Tel Aviv: A centrally located hostel with a bar, lounge, and rooftop terrace.
  • Shenkin Hotel: A trendy boutique hotel located in the heart of Tel Aviv’s artsy Shenkin Street.
  • Brown TLV Urban Hotel: A hip boutique hotel located near Rothschild Boulevard.
  • Hotel Montefiore: An elegant boutique hotel in a restored historic building.
  • Airbnb: A popular option for travelers looking for a home-away-from-home experience.

What to Buy in South and East: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Spices and Herbs: From za’atar to sumac, Israeli spice markets are a must-visit for any foodie.
  • Dead Sea Products: Israel’s Dead Sea is famous for its healing properties, and you’ll find a wide range of skincare products, including mud masks and salt scrubs.
  • Judaica: Beautiful religious artifacts like menorahs, mezuzahs, and hamsas make for unique souvenirs.
  • Jewelry: Traditional Middle Eastern jewelry, like Bedouin silver bracelets, is a beautiful and unique gift.
  • Olive oil: Israel produces some of the finest olive oil in the world, so stock up on this healthy and flavorful ingredient.

Local Culture and Customs of South and East

Israel is a modern, diverse country, but some cultural customs remain. Respectful dress is appreciated when visiting religious sites and covering the head -with a hat, scarf or shawl- for men and women is a sign of respect. People usually dress up more for evening outings. And above all, everyone in Israel is generally very friendly and welcoming. Israelis are often described as direct, honest, informal and blunt so it’s important to remember that as well.

Best Time to Visit South and East

The best time to visit South and East is from April to June and September to November. This time of year offers the most pleasant weather, with temperatures around 20-25°C.

Do’s and Don’ts of South and East

Do:

  • Try the local cuisine and don’t be afraid to ask for recommendations.
  • Dress respectfully when visiting religious sites.
  • Learn a few Hebrew or Arabic phrases.
  • Try bargaining at the markets.

Don’t:

  • Be disrespectful of local customs and traditions.
  • Take pictures of people without permission.
  • Walk alone in some areas at night.

Safety and Health

Safety in South and East

Tel Aviv is a safe city, but like any other city, it has its share of petty crimes. Always be aware of your surroundings and keep an eye on your belongings. It is advisable not to leave valuables unattended on the beach. However, it is important to understand that terrorism remains a threat in Israel.

Health in South and East

Israel has a high standard of healthcare and medical facilities are modern and well-equipped. Tap water is safe to drink throughout the country. However, it is advisable to take precautions against the hot sun and drink plenty of water during the summer months.

Getting to South and East: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near South and East

The two main airports near Tel Aviv are Ben Gurion International Airport (TLV), located approximately 15 km southeast of the city, and Sde Dov Airport (SDV), which serves domestic flights in Israel.

Bus Stations Near South and East

The main bus station in Tel Aviv is the Central Bus Station (Tahana Merkazit), which connects Tel Aviv to other cities in Israel.

Other Options

Tel Aviv is a small city that can easily be explored on foot or by bike. Taxis and car rentals are also readily available.

How to Get Around in South and East

Public transportation is a convenient and affordable way to get around Tel Aviv. Buses, taxis, and the train system (particularly the light rail) connect the city’s major sites and attractions. Bike rental stations are also available at key locations in the city.

Where to Go Next from South and East: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Jerusalem: The historic and religious center of Israel, located approximately 1 hour’s drive from Tel Aviv.
  • The Dead Sea: The lowest point on earth and a popular destination for spa treatments.
  • Nazareth: A beautiful and historic city in the Galilee region.
  • Haifa: A port city with stunning views of the Mediterranean Sea.
  • Eilat: A resort town located on the Red Sea, famous for its beaches and water sports.

Conclusion

South and East offer travelers a unique blend of history, culture, and natural beauty. With its stunning beaches, vibrant food and drink scene, and rich cultural heritage, South and East is a must-visit destination for anyone traveling to Tel Aviv. So pack your bags, prepare your itinerary, and get ready to experience the best of what this amazing region has to offer.
