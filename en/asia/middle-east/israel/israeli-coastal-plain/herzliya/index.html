Herzliya is a coastal city located in the Israeli Coastal Plain, in the Middle East region of Asia. Situated just north of Tel Aviv, Herzliya offers a vibrant mix of beach culture, nature, history, and modernity. Whether you’re a first-time visitor or a seasoned traveler, this comprehensive tour guide will help you make the most out of your trip to Herzliya. From cultural attractions and natural sights to delicious food and unique souvenirs, there’s something for everyone in this charming city by the sea.

Introduction to Herzliya

Named after Theodor Herzl, the founder of modern Zionism, Herzliya is a city steeped in history and culture. Founded in 1924, Herzliya has grown into one of the most sought-after destinations in Israel, boasting a long stretch of beautiful white-sand beaches, chic boutiques and restaurants, lush parks, museums, and many other attractions.

List of Places to Visit in Herzliya

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Herzliya has a rich cultural heritage, and there are plenty of museums and historical sites that showcase its history and heritage. These include:

  • Herzliya Museum of Art: This museum features contemporary and modern art, as well as temporary exhibitions, cultural events, and educational programs.
  • The Apollonia National Park: A beautiful archaeological site that dates back to the Hellenistic, Roman, Byzantine, and Crusader periods. The park features ancient ruins such as a fortress, port, and acropolis, as well as a beautiful beach.
  • The Herzliya Historical Museum: Located in a restored house from the 19th century, this museum showcases the history of Herzliya and the surrounding area, including the pioneer settlers who founded the city after the First World War.
  • The Museum of Herzliya Hebrew Authors: This museum is dedicated to the history and culture of the Hebrew language and literature, showcasing works by famous Israeli authors such as Shmuel Yosef Agnon and Leah Goldberg.

Natural Attractions of Herzliya: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Herzliya is known for its natural beauty and outdoor adventures. Here are some of the must-visit natural attractions:

  • Herzliya Beach: One of the most beautiful beaches in Israel, Herzliya Beach features crystal-clear waters, soft white-sand, and stunning views of the Mediterranean Sea. Lifeguarded during the summer season, the beach is perfect for swimming, sunbathing, and water sports.
  • Herzliya Park: Located in the center of the city, Herzliya Park features jogging paths, a lake, a fountain, children’s playgrounds, and a picnic area.
  • Apollonia Beach: Adjacent to the Apollonia National Park, this uncrowded and serene beach is a paradise for sunbathers, swimmers, and snorkelers.
  • Shvil HaMaayanot Park: Offering a scenic hiking and biking trail, this park is located in the Yarkon River basin, and features natural springs, waterfalls, and lush vegetation.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Herzliya

Herzliya is home to many scenic outdoor trails that are ideal for hiking and biking. Whether you’re an experienced hiker or a casual cyclist, Herzliya has something for everyone. Some of the most popular trails include:

  • The Herzliya Marina and Beach Trail: This easy 7 km trail runs along the Herzliya beach promenade and marina. The trail is perfect for a leisurely bike ride, jog, or walk, and offers stunning views of the Mediterranean Sea.
  • The Yarkon River Trail: This 27 km trail follows the winding path of the Yarkon River through the heart of Tel Aviv and Herzliya. The trail is perfect for hiking and biking, and offers plenty of opportunities for scenic views, picnics, and nature watching.
  • The Carmel Hill Trail: Located just outside Herzliya, this 10 km trail takes you through the lush Carmel Forest, featuring scenic views of the Mediterranean Sea, and abundant wildlife.

Best Things to Do in Herzliya: Activities and Tours

Herzliya offers a wide range of activities and tours. Here are some of the best ones:

  • Surfing Lessons: With its ideal sea conditions, Herzliya is a great place to learn how to surf. There are many surf schools and rental shops along the beachfront that cater to beginners and experienced surfers alike.
  • Sailing: Take a sailing lesson, charter a yacht, or simply enjoy a sunset cruise along the Herzliya Marina.
  • Wine Tasting: Visit one of the many boutique wineries located in the nearby Shfela region for a wine tasting tour, and sample some of Israel’s finest wines.
  • Food Tours: Explore the local culinary scene by taking a food tour of Herzliya’s restaurants, cafes, and markets.

Week-Long Itinerary for Herzliya

Day 1: Visit the Herzliya Marina and Herzliya Beach, and take a stroll along the beach promenade.

Day 2: Explore the Apollonia National Park and Apollonia Beach, and take a dip in the clear Mediterranean Sea.

Day 3: Take a tour of the Herzliya Museum of Art, followed by a visit to the Herzliya Park and Lake.

Day 4: Take a hiking or biking tour of the Yarkon River Trail, and enjoy a picnic in the park.

Day 5: Join a food tour of Herzliya’s best restaurants, and taste some of the local delicacies.

Day 6: Take a wine tasting tour of the nearby Shfela region, and sample some of Israel’s finest wines.

Day 7: Take a day trip to Tel Aviv, only 10 km away, and explore the city’s many attractions, including the Tel Aviv Museum of Art, the Carmel Market, and Jaffa Port.

Food and Drink Scene of Herzliya

Must-Try Dishes

Herzliya offers a unique blend of Mediterranean, Middle Eastern, and modern cuisine. Here are some of the must-try dishes:

  • Hummus: A classic Middle Eastern dish made with mashed chickpeas, tahini, and olive oil. Herzliya is home to some of the best hummus joints in Israel.
  • Falafel: Deep-fried balls of chickpeas or fava beans, served in a pita bread, with salad, and tahini sauce. Herzliya offers a variety of falafel joints, both traditional and modern.
  • Sabich: A popular Iraqi-Israeli dish made with fried eggplant, hard-boiled egg, Israeli salad, hummus, and tahini, served in a pita bread.
  • Shakshuka: A breakfast dish made with eggs, tomato sauce, peppers, and spices, often served with fresh bread.

Local Beverages

Herzliya offers a variety of local beverages, each with its own unique flavor and history. Some of the most popular ones include:

  • Arak: A clear anise-flavored drink that is often served as an aperitif or with meze dishes.
  • Wine: Israel is known for producing high-quality wines, and Herzliya is home to many boutique wineries that offer tours and tastings.
  • Coffee: Herzliya is home to many trendy cafes that serve excellent coffee, including espresso, cappuccino, and latte.
  • Tea: Herbal tea is a popular beverage in Israel, and Herzliya offers a variety of tea houses that serve a wide range of blends and flavors.

Where to Stay in Herzliya: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Herzliya offers a variety of accommodation options to suit all budgets and preferences. From luxurious hotels to cozy guesthouses, there’s something for everyone. Some of the best options include:

  • The Ritz-Carlton Herzliya: A luxurious hotel located on the beachfront, featuring stylish rooms, a rooftop pool, and a spa.
  • Herods Herzliya: A 5-star hotel located in the heart of Herzliya, featuring spacious rooms, a pool, and a restaurant.
  • Sea Executive Suites: A boutique hotel located on the marina, offering spacious suites with sea views, a rooftop pool, and a fitness center.
  • Herzliya Hostel: A budget-friendly hostel located near the beach, offering dormitory-style rooms and private rooms, as well as a communal kitchen, lounge, and terrace.

What to Buy in Herzliya: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Herzliya offers plenty of unique souvenirs and local products that make perfect gifts for your loved ones. Here are some of the best ones:

  • Olive oil: Israel is known for producing high-quality olive oil, and Herzliya offers many boutique shops that sell locally sourced and produced olive oil.
  • Dead Sea Products: Israel is home to the famous Dead Sea, which is known for its healing properties. You can buy Dead Sea products such as salts, mud, and skincare products in many shops in Herzliya.
  • Judaica: Israel is the birthplace of Judaism, and Herzliya offers many shops that sell Judaica items such as mezuzahs, menorahs, and kippahs.
  • Israeli Wine: Herzliya is located near some of Israel’s best wineries, and you can find a variety of award-winning Israeli wines in many wine shops and markets in the city.

Local Culture and Customs of Herzliya

Herzliya is known for its Mediterranean hospitality, friendliness, and openness. Israeli culture values directness and informality, so don’t be surprised if people approach you with direct questions or comments. Tipping is customary in restaurants and cafes, and it is polite to add an extra 10% to your bill. Shabbat, the Jewish Sabbath, starts on Friday evening and ends on Saturday night, and many shops and restaurants may be closed during this time.

Best Time to Visit Herzliya

The best time to visit Herzliya is during the shoulder seasons of spring (April-May) and fall (October-November), when the weather is mild and sunny. The summer months (June-August) are hot and humid, and the beaches can get crowded. The winter months (December-March) are mild and rainy, and the parks and museums are less crowded.

Do’s and Don’ts of Herzliya

Do:

  • Visit the beaches early in the morning to avoid crowds.
  • Practice Israel’s “water culture,” which means keeping your surroundings clean and tidy.
  • Try the local cuisine, including falafel, hummus, and shakshuka.
  • Learn some basic Hebrew phrases, such as “shalom” (hello/goodbye) and “todah” (thank you).

Don’t:

  • Wear revealing clothing in public areas.
  • Smoke in non-smoking areas, it is against the law.
  • Take photos of sensitive military sites or installations.
  • Touch religious artifacts or items in synagogues and holy places.

Safety and Health

Safety in Herzliya

Herzliya is a safe city for travelers. It is advisable to take the usual precautions when traveling abroad, such as keeping an eye on your belongings and avoiding deserted areas at night. The emergency number in Israel is 100 for police or 101 for medical emergency services.

Health in Herzliya

Herzliya offers modern medical facilities and services. There are many pharmacies and clinics in the city, and hospitals are located in nearby Tel Aviv. The tap water in Herzliya is safe to drink.

Getting to Herzliya: Transportation Options and Logistics

The easiest way to get to Herzliya is by plane. Herzliya is conveniently located just 25 km north of Ben Gurion International Airport, which offers many international and domestic flights. Some of the other transportation options include:

Airports Near Herzliya

  • Ben Gurion International Airport: Located 25 km south of Herzliya, this is the main airport in Israel.
  • Sde Dov Airport: Located just 10 km south of Herzliya, this airport serves mainly domestic flights.

Bus Stations Near Herzliya

  • Herzliya Central Bus Station: Located in the city center, the bus station serves many domestic routes.

Other Options

  • Train: Herzliya is connected to Tel Aviv and other cities in Israel via a modern rail network.
  • Taxi: Taxis are widely available in Herzliya, and it is customary to negotiate the price before starting the ride.
  • Car Rental: There are several car rental companies located in Herzliya that offer affordable rates.

How to Get Around in Herzliya

Herzliya offers several transportation options for getting around the city, including:

  • Bus: Herzliya has an extensive bus network that covers the city and surrounding areas.
  • Taxi: Taxis are plentiful in Herzliya, and it is advisable to negotiate the price before starting the ride.
  • Bicycle: Many hotels in Herzliya offer rental bikes, and there are several bike rental shops located in the city.
  • Car Rental: There are many car rental companies located in Herzliya, and it is easy to rent a car for exploring the city and nearby areas.

Where to Go Next from Herzliya: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Herzliya is located close to many other popular destinations in Israel, including:

  • Tel Aviv: Just 10 km south of Herzliya, Tel Aviv is a vibrant and cosmopolitan city, known for its beaches, nightlife, and culture.
  • Haifa: Located 85 km north of Herzliya, Haifa is a beautiful coastal city, known for its stunning views, gardens, and cultural landmarks.
  • Jerusalem: Located 80 km east of Herzliya, Jerusalem is one of the most important cities in the world, revered for its ancient history, religious landmarks, and cultural heritage.
  • Dead Sea: Located 170 km south of Herzliya, the Dead Sea is one of the world’s most unique natural wonders, known for its medicinal properties and stunning landscapes.

Conclusion

Herzliya is a fascinating and beautiful city that offers a unique blend of culture, nature, and modernity. From its stunning beaches and natural attractions to its rich history and culinary scene, Herzliya has something for every traveler. Plan your trip today and discover this hidden gem in the heart of Israel.
