Introduction to Modi’in

Located in the Shefelah region and known as one of the newest cities in Israel, Modi’in has gained popularity as a perfect destination for travelers seeking an ideal blend of ancient history and modern lifestyle. It is situated between Jerusalem and Tel Aviv and has plenty of attractions, outdoor activities, and local cuisines to offer.

List of Places to Visit in Modi’in

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • The Museum of Antiquities - This museum is home to several antiquities and exhibits dating back to the Iron Age, depicting the rich history of the region.
  • The Maccabean Graves - Located atop the city hill, this place represents the tombs of the ancient Jewish kings and warriors, considered as one of the significant historical sites in Modi’in.
  • Anava Park Reserve - Home to a unique natural reserve with ancient ruins showcasing the Hasmonean and Herodian periods.

Natural Attractions of Modi’in: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Ben Shemen Forest - This forest reserve is a perfect getaway for visitors seeking serene surroundings and outdoor activities like trekking, biking, and camping.
  • Park Anava - A beautiful park known for family picnics, biking trails, and stunning views of the surrounding valleys.
  • Sataf Nature Reserve - Named after an ancient agricultural village, this nature reserve is a perfect place for hikers and outdoor enthusiasts.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Modi’in

The city boasts several exciting hiking, biking, and trekking trails like the Anava Loop Trail, the Modi’in-Maccabim-Reut Bike Path, and Nachal Ktziot.

Best Things to Do in Modi’in: Activities and Tours

  • Experience Shabbat in Modi’in, Israel’s first planned city.
  • Visit the popular Wings Sculpture Garden, a park with unique sculptures, an urban oasis, and a built-in children’s playground.
  • Go shopping and experience local cuisine at the Modi’in Mall.

Food and Drink Scene of Modi’in

Must-Try Dishes

  • Shakshuka - A dish of eggs Poached in tomato sauce, originally from North Africa
  • Falafel - A deep-fried ball made from chickpeas, herbs, and spices, served in pita bread, and topped with salad and sauces
  • Humus - A dip made from chickpeas, tahini, and olive oil, famously served with fresh pita bread

Local Beverages

  • Arak - A traditional alcoholic drink made from anise essence and distilled grapes.
  • Sabra Liqueur - A sweet and sour drink made from Sabra Cactus plant, which is a symbol of Israel.

Where to Stay in Modi’in: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Lighthouse Boutique Guest House - Located in the heart of the city, this guest house is an ideal pick for those who enjoy boutique-interiors and budget-friendly accommodation.
  • Alexander Suites Hotel - Positioned near the Modi’in-Maccabim-Reut Railway Station, this hotel offers comfortable and luxurious stays for families and leisure travelers.
  • Gordonia Hotel Modiin - This hotel is the perfect pick for travelers seeking a view of the city’s magnificent hills and natural beauty.

What to Buy in Modi’in: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Dead Sea Products - A local speciality, which includes skin-care products and cosmetic items made using minerals of the Dead Sea.
  • Jewelry - Beautifully designed Israeli-inspired jewelry, typically made of sterling silver or gold, is a perfect souvenir to take home.
  • Olive Oil - A local specialty, pressed from the olives grown in the surrounding villages, is pure and delicious to taste.

Local Culture and Customs of Modi’in

The city is known for its active and vibrant lifestyle, with most of the locals being young and ambitious professionals. As per the Israeli custom, it is considered inappropriate to dress in revealing clothes, so it is recommended to wear modest attire during your stay in Modi’in.

Best Time to Visit Modi’in

The best time to visit Modi’in is from November to March, when the weather is pleasant and cool, with occasional rain showers. The summers in Modi’in (April to October) are hot and humid, with the temperature soaring high, making outdoor activities challenging.

Do’s and Don’ts of Modi’in

Do:

  • Dress modestly and respect the Israeli customs and traditions.
  • Try the local cuisine, especially the humus and falafel, which are a must-try.
  • Interact with the locals and learn about their lifestyle and customs.

Don’t:

  • Do not visit religious sites during the weekend (Friday evening to Saturday evening), as they are closed for worship.
  • Do not engage in public display of affection or consume alcohol in public areas.

Safety and Health

Safety in Modi’in

Modi’in is considered a safe destination for travelers to visit. The city’s law and order situation is well-maintained, and the police are efficient and well-equipped to handle any emergency.

Health in Modi’in

Israel’s healthcare system is highly advanced and has high-quality facilities, which means the medical facilities in Modi’in is no exception.

Getting to Modi’in: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Modi’in

  • Ben Gurion International Airport, Tel Aviv - Located only 35 km from Modi’in, is the nearest airport, offering both international and domestic flights.

Bus Stations Near Modi’in

  • Modi’in Central Bus Station - This is the main bus station in the city and has regular bus connections to major Israeli cities like Jerusalem and Tel Aviv.

Other Options

  • Train - Modi’in-Maccabim-Reut Railway Station connects the city with the major cities of Jerusalem and Tel Aviv.
  • Taxi Services - Several taxi services are available at the airport and within the city.

How to Get Around Modi’in

The city is well-connected by public transportation services, including buses and trains. The transportation system is efficient and affordable, and taxis are also readily available for short-distance travel.

Where to Go Next from Modi’in: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Tel Aviv - A city with a bustling vibe and stunning beaches, is only an hour drive away from Modi’in.
  • Jerusalem - One of the oldest cities in the world, and the Holy Land’s religious center, is only 35 km away from Modi’in.
  • Dead Sea - Known for its health benefits and unique landscape, is approximately 60 km away from Modi’in.

Conclusion

Modi’in is a destination that offers something for every traveler, from its rich history and heritage to its vibrant streets and local cuisine. This comprehensive guide will help you plan your trip to Modi’in, so you can enjoy your stay and explore the city’s beauty to the fullest. Whether you are an outdoor adventurer or a culture seeker, Modi’in is a perfect destination to unwind and experience Israel’s magnificence.
