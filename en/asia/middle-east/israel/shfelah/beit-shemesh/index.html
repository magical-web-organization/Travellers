Introduction to Beit Shemesh

Located in the Shfelah region of Israel, Beit Shemesh is a small city in the Middle East that offers a perfect blend of rich history, nature, and modern-day amenities to its visitors. With a population of around 120,000, the city offers a plethora of things to see and do for all types of travelers. The city has been inhabited for centuries and boasts a rich cultural heritage that is reflected in its landmarks and attractions.

In this comprehensive travel guide to Beit Shemesh, we’ll take a closer look at the places to visit, accommodations, local customs, and much more to help you plan your trip to this amazing city.

List of Places to Visit in Beit Shemesh

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums, and Historical Places

Beit Shemesh History Museum

Beit Shemesh History Museum is one of the most popular tourist attractions in the city that depicts the historical transformation of Beit Shemesh. This museum has a vast collection of ancient artifacts and exhibits that give an insight into the history of the city.

Tel Maresha

Located near Beit Guvrin National Park, Tel Maresha is an archeological site that offers great insights into ancient Jewish society during the Hellenistic and Roman periods. Visitors can explore the underground tunnels, chambers, and tombs excavated from the soft chalk rock and mosaic floors.

Beit Jamal Monastery

Beit Jamal Monastery is one of the most fascinating tourist destinations in the city. The monastery is built in the tradition of the Crusaders and boasts of stunning frescoes and valuable archaeological findings. The impressive view of the Judean Hills and the surrounding vineyards adds to the serenity of the place.

Natural Attractions of Beit Shemesh: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Nahal Sorek Nature Reserve

Nahal Sorek Nature Reserve is a perfect place for nature lovers and outdoor enthusiasts. The reserve has several hiking and biking trails that offer breathtaking views of the surrounding mountains and valleys. Visitors can also explore the freshwater springs and streams, and the wildlife reserves.

Park HaMaayanot

Located on the foothills of Beit Shemesh, Park HaMaayanot is a popular picnic spot among locals and tourists alike. The park features hiking paths, picnic areas, a small lake, and breathtaking views of the city and its surroundings.

Beit Guvrin National Park

Beit Guvrin National Park is another popular tourist attraction in Beit Shemesh that offers a glimpse into the ancient Jewish culture, particularly the late Roman and Byzantine periods. The park features impressive cave systems and underground tunnels, several hiking trails, and numerous archeological sites.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Beit Shemesh

Beit Shemesh is a paradise for hikers and bikers. The city has some of the best hiking and biking trails that offer magnificent views of the city’s landscape. Some of the most popular hiking and biking trails include Tel Azeka Trail, Nachal Refaim Trail, Jishai Trail, and the Beit Guvrin Trail.

Best Things to Do in Beit Shemesh: Activities and Tours

Wine Tasting Tours

Beit Shemesh is known for its wine production, and it offers some of the best wine tasting tours in the region. Visitors can tour several wineries, taste local wines, and explore the winemaking process in detail.

Jeep Tours

Jeep tours are very popular among tourists in Beit Shemesh. These tours take visitors to the rugged terrain, including off-road areas, vineyards, olive groves, and ancient sites.

Week-Long Itinerary for Beit Shemesh

Day 1 - Visit Beit Shemesh History Museum, explore the nearby Judaean Mountains, and enjoy a wine tasting tour.

Day 2 - Visit Tel Maresha, Nahal Sorek Nature Reserve, and Beit Guvrin National Park.

Day 3 - Visit Park HaMaayanot for a relaxed day filled with picnics, hikes, and fresh air.

Day 4 - Take a jeep tour of the city and its surroundings, visit Beit Jamal Monastery, and enjoy an authentic Israeli lunch.

Day 5 - Visit the ancient sites including Tel Lachish and Avdat and explore the city’s archeological sites.

Day 6 - Take a guided walking tour of Beit Shemesh’s Old City, and visit the local bazaars and markets.

Day 7 - Take a hot air balloon ride over the city for a memorable experience.

Food and Drink Scene of Beit Shemesh

Must-Try Dishes

Beit Shemesh is known for its culinary delights, with a range of unique dishes to try. Some of the must-try dishes include Falafel, Hummus, Shakshuka, Challah (braided bread), and Jerusalem Bagels.

Local Beverages

When it comes to beverages, Beit Shemesh offers a range of options, including Arak, a traditional spirit made from grapes, Cardamom coffee, Tehina shakes, and various types of local wines.

Where to Stay in Beit Shemesh: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Beit Shemesh offers various accommodation options to suit all budgets and preferences. Some of the popular choices include Dan Gardens, Neve Sharet, and Rimonim Hotel Beit Shemesh.

What to Buy in Beit Shemesh: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Some of the popular souvenirs to take back from Beit Shemesh include Honey, Olive Oil, Pottery, Jewelry, and Ceramic Tiles.

Local Culture and Customs of Beit Shemesh

The people of Beit Shemesh are friendly and welcoming, with a rich cultural heritage. Respect for tradition and religion is widespread, and visitors are always welcome to join in community celebrations and events.

Best Time to Visit Beit Shemesh

The best time to visit Beit Shemesh is during the spring and early summer months, between April and June, and in September and October, when the weather is temperate, and the crowds are less.

Do’s and Don’ts of Beit Shemesh

Do:

  • Respect the local customs and traditions.

  • Dress appropriately when visiting religious and historical sites.

  • Try the local cuisine.

Don’t:

  • Take photos of people without their permission.

  • Disrespect their traditions and religion.

  • Ignore the local customs.

Safety and Health

Safety in Beit Shemesh

Beit Shemesh is a safe city with a low crime rate. However, it is advisable to take precautions and avoid wandering alone at night or in unfamiliar areas.

Health in Beit Shemesh

Beit Shemesh has excellent medical facilities, including hospitals and clinics, that offer high-quality care to travelers in need.

Getting to Beit Shemesh: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Beit Shemesh

The Ben Gurion International Airport in Tel Aviv is the nearest airport to Beit Shemesh, which is about 60 kilometers away.

Bus Stations Near Beit Shemesh

The Egged Bus Company operates regular bus services to Beit Shemesh from major cities across Israel, including Jerusalem and Tel Aviv.

Other Options

Train services also operate from Jerusalem and Tel Aviv to Beit Shemesh.

How to Get Around in Beit Shemesh

Beit Shemesh is a small city that is easy to explore on foot or by bike. Local buses and taxis are also available for getting around the city.

Conclusion
