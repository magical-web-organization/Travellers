Introduction to Kyrenia District

Kyrenia district is located in Northern Cyprus, a country in Europe that is only recognized by Turkey. Despite the political situation, Northern Cyprus is a stunning destination with beautiful beaches, hiking trails, and a rich culture that reflects its unique history. Kyrenia, also known as Girne, is the main town of the district, located on the northern coast of Cyprus. This comprehensive travel guide will cover everything you need to know to plan a trip to Kyrenia district.

List of Places to Visit in Kyrenia District

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Kyrenia district is home to many culturally important attractions, including museums and historical places. The Kyrenia Castle is one of the most iconic attractions in the district, and it dates back to the 16th century. It is located on the Kyrenia harbor and houses a museum that displays ancient artifacts and weapons. Another historically significant place to visit is St. Hilarion Castle, a medieval castle that was built in the 11th century and was used as a summer residence for the royal family. The Icon Museum is another attraction that is worth visiting, with its collection of 12th and 15th-century icons that depict religious events.

Natural Attractions of Kyrenia District: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Kyrenia district is also known for its natural attractions, including outdoor adventures and scenic views. The Five Finger Mountains offer stunning views of the district and are great for hiking and biking. The Bellapais Abbey is another must-visit attraction, located on the mountains’ slopes and providing a breathtaking view of the coast. Karpaz National Park is another natural attraction that offers a unique experience, with its untouched beaches, wild donkeys, and historic sites.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Kyrenia District

Kyrenia district offers plenty of hiking and biking routes that are perfect for adventure seekers. The Five Finger Mountains are the most popular hiking destination, with trails that vary in difficulty and length. For cyclists, there are several routes to choose from, including the coastal road from Kyrenia to Karpaz and the mountain trails that offer challenging rides with stunning views. For hiking enthusiasts, the Kyrenia Mountain Trail is a must-try, offering a unique experience of hiking through the district’s natural beauty.

Best Things to Do in Kyrenia District: Activities and Tours

Kyrenia district offers many activities and tours that cater to different interests and preferences. From boat tours that explore the coast to scuba diving experiences that allow you to explore the underwater treasures, there is something for everyone. The district is also home to several golf courses and offers horseback riding tours that take you through the hills and valleys.

Week-Long Itinerary for Kyrenia District

Kyrenia district is vast and offers a lot to see and do. It’s best to plan your trip ahead to make the most of your time there. Here’s a sample week-long itinerary to help you plan your trip:

  • Day 1: Visit Kyrenia Castle and explore the harbor area
  • Day 2: Hike the Five Finger Mountains and enjoy the scenic views
  • Day 3: Visit St. Hilarion Castle and Bellapais Abbey
  • Day 4: Take a boat tour and enjoy the beautiful beaches and crystal-clear waters
  • Day 5: Explore the historic town center of Kyrenia and visit the Icon Museum
  • Day 6: Try horseback riding or golfing in the district’s hills and valleys
  • Day 7: Explore Karpaz National Park and its untouched beaches

Food and Drink Scene of Kyrenia District

The food and drink scene in Kyrenia District is diverse and reflects the district’s unique history and culture. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Meze platter: a combination of small dishes that include hummus, tzatziki, stuffed vine leaves, and more
  • Kleftiko: a slow-cooked lamb dish that is marinated with herbs and spices
  • Souvla: a traditional barbecue dish that is made with meat skewers
  • Pilavuna: a bread dish that is stuffed with vegetables, cheese, or meats

Local Beverages

  • Turkish Coffee: a flavorful coffee that is served in small cups
  • Raki: an anise-flavored alcoholic drink that is often enjoyed with meze
  • Ayran: a refreshing yogurt-based drink that is perfect for the hot summer days
  • Cypriot Wine: the district has several vineyards that produce quality wines that are worth trying

Where to Stay in Kyrenia District: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Kyrenia district offers a range of accommodation options that cater to different budgets and interests. For those looking for a luxury experience, there are several hotels that offer stunning views and top-notch facilities. The district also has several guesthouses and hostels that provide a comfortable stay at a more affordable price. Here are some recommendations:

  • Merit Park Hotel & Casino: a luxurious hotel located near the Five Finger Mountains that offers stunning sea views and exceptional facilities
  • Bella View Hotel: a hotel located on the slopes of Bellapais that offers a peaceful and relaxing experience
  • Hadjios Valley: a guesthouse located in the heart of the village that offers a traditional Cypriot experience

What to Buy in Kyrenia District: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Kyrenia district offers several souvenirs and local products that reflect the district’s unique culture and history. Here are some things that you should consider bringing home:

  • Copperware: the district is famous for its copperware, including plates, cups, and pots
  • Olive Oil: the district has several olive groves that produce high-quality olive oil that is worth trying
  • Turkish Delight: a sweet treat that comes in different flavors, including rose, pistachio, and pomegranate
  • Handwoven Rugs: the district has a long tradition of weaving, and handwoven rugs are a popular souvenir item

Local Culture and Customs of Kyrenia District

Kyrenia district has a rich culture and history that is reflected in its customs and traditions. Here are some things to keep in mind when visiting:

  • Hospitality is a significant aspect of Cypriot culture, and locals are warm and welcoming
  • The district has a strong influence from Turkish tradition and Islam
  • Dress modestly when visiting religious sites
  • It’s customary to remove your shoes when entering a Cypriot home

Best Time to Visit Kyrenia District

The best time to visit Kyrenia district is between May and October, when the weather is warm and sunny. The summer months can be very hot, so it’s best to plan outdoor activities for early morning or late afternoon. The district’s natural beauty is at its peak during spring when the wildflowers are in full bloom.

Do’s and Don’ts of Kyrenia District

Do:

  • Try local food and drinks
  • Visit the historical sites and museums
  • Explore the natural beauty of the Five Finger Mountains and the beaches
  • Shop for souvenirs and local products

Don’t:

  • Do not take photographs of military installations or equipment
  • Avoid discussing the political situation with the locals
  • Do not litter or damage the natural attractions

Safety and Health

Safety in Kyrenia District

Kyrenia district is generally safe for tourists, but like any other destination, it’s essential to take precautions to stay safe. Here are some safety tips:

  • Be aware of your surroundings and avoid walking alone at night
  • Keep your belongings close to you, especially in crowded areas
  • Drink bottled water to avoid stomach problems
  • Take necessary precautions during summer months, such as wearing sunblock and staying hydrated

Health in Kyrenia District

The district has several hospitals and clinics that provide medical care, but it’s still important to have travel insurance that covers emergencies. It’s recommended to get a vaccine for tetanus and hepatitis A before traveling to Northern Cyprus.

Getting to Kyrenia District: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Kyrenia District

The closest airport to Kyrenia district is Ercan International Airport, located in the capital city of Nicosia. It’s important to note that flights to Northern Cyprus often have layovers, and passengers are required to have a visa to enter the country. Another option is to fly to Larnaca International Airport, located in the Republic of Cyprus, and then cross the border to Northern Cyprus.

Bus Stations Near Kyrenia District

The Kyrenia Bus Station is located in the town center, and buses run regularly to other parts of the district and the nearby cities.

Other Options

Renting a car or a taxi is also an option for those who prefer more flexibility and convenience. It’s recommended to book in advance, especially during the high season.

How to Get Around in Kyrenia District

The most common way to get around Kyrenia district is by bus, and the Kyrenia Bus Station is the main hub for buses that run to other parts of the district and nearby cities. There are also several taxi companies that provide services in the district. For those who prefer more flexibility and convenience, renting a car is a viable option.

Where to Go Next from Kyrenia District: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Kyrenia district is located in a beautiful part of Northern Cyprus and provides a great base to explore the nearby destinations and excursions. The capital city of Nicosia is only a short drive away, and it offers a unique blend of Greek and Turkish culture. Famagusta is another popular destination, located on the east coast of Cyprus and offering stunning beaches and historic sites. Karpaz peninsula is also worth visiting, with its untouched beaches and natural beauty.

Conclusion

Kyrenia district is a beautiful destination in Northern Cyprus that offers a unique blend of history, culture, and natural beauty. Whether you’re interested in exploring the historical sites, hiking the Five Finger Mountains, or indulging in local cuisine, there’s something for everyone in Kyrenia district. Use this comprehensive travel guide to plan your trip and make the most of your time in this stunning destination.
