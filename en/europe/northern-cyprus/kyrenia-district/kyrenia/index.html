Introduction to Kyrenia

Kyrenia, also known as Girne, is a charming coastal town located in the Kyrenia District of Northern Cyprus in Europe. The town boasts of a rich history dating back to the 10th century BC and is home to many historical sites and cultural attractions. Visitors to Kyrenia can enjoy stunning views of the Mediterranean Sea, explore ancient castles and fortresses, hike on scenic trails, indulge in water sports, and much more.

This comprehensive travel guide will take you through everything that Kyrenia has to offer. From the best sights to visit to the region’s rich culture, food, and customs, we’ve got you covered. Let’s get started!

Local Culture and Customs of Kyrenia

Kyrenia is a town deeply steeped in culture and tradition. Visitors can learn more about the town’s history, language, and customs by visiting local museums and engaging with the locals.

Best Time to Visit Kyrenia

The best time to visit Kyrenia is during the shoulder seasons in spring and fall (March to May and September to November), as the weather is pleasant and the crowd is sparser. Summers in Kyrenia can be hot and humid, with temperatures above 30°C.

Do’s and Don’ts of Kyrenia

Do:

  • Respect local customs and cultures
  • Dress appropriately while visiting religious sites
  • Learn a few basic Turkish words to communicate with locals
  • Carry cash as many small shops and restaurants do not accept cards
  • Try the local cuisine

Don’t:

  • Don’t photograph military installations and personnel
  • Avoid discussing politics and religion
  • Don’t litter and keep the environment clean
  • Don’t drink tap water

Safety and Health

Safety in Kyrenia

Kyrenia is a safe town for tourists, with low crime rates. Visitors should, however, take basic precautions like not leaving valuables unattended and being alert in crowded areas.

Health in Kyrenia

There are no major health concerns in Kyrenia. Visitors should carry mosquito repellent as there might be mosquitos during summer.

How to Get Around in Kyrenia

Kyrenia is a pedestrian-friendly town, and most of the attractions are within walking distance. Visitors can also take taxis, rent a car or bike, or use the local bus service to get around.
