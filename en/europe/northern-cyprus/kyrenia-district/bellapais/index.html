Introduction to Bellapais

Bellapais is a charming village located in the Kyrenia district of Northern Cyprus, in Europe. It is known for its rich cultural heritage, picturesque landscapes, and traditional architecture. The village is home to the famous Bellapais Abbey, which is considered one of the most beautiful Gothic buildings in the world.

The village also boasts several museums, art galleries, and historical landmarks that offer a sneak peek into the island’s fascinating past. Whether you’re a history buff, a nature lover, or an adventure seeker, Bellapais has something to offer to everyone. Read on to discover the must-visit places, best things to do, and local customs of Bellapais.

List of Places to Visit in Bellapais

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Bellapais Abbey: A stunning 13th-century Gothic abbey that offers a panoramic view of the island. It is considered one of the most important landmarks in Cyprus and is a must-visit attraction for architecture lovers.

  • Museum of Folk Art: This museum displays a vast collection of traditional Cypriot crafts and objects, such as pottery, embroidery, and weaving. It offers a glimpse into the island’s rich cultural heritage and traditions.

  • St. Hilarion Castle: A medieval castle that dates back to the 10th century. It offers a stunning view of the coastline and the surrounding mountains and is an ideal place for nature lovers and adventure seekers.

  • Icon Museum: This museum displays a vast collection of Byzantine and post-Byzantine icons and frescoes. It offers visitors a unique opportunity to explore the island’s religious and cultural heritage.

Natural Attractions of Bellapais: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Kyrenia Mountains: Bellapais is situated at the foothills of the Kyrenia Mountains, which offer a variety of hiking and biking trails. The mountains are also home to several traditional villages that offer a peek into the island’s rural life and traditions.

  • Bellapais Forest: This forest is located towards the east of the village and offers a serene and peaceful environment for nature lovers. The forest is home to several types of trees, such as pine, cypress, and eucalyptus.

  • Escape Beach: This beach is located at the foot of the Kyrenia Mountains and is considered one of the best beaches in Northern Cyprus. The crystal clear waters, golden sand, and stunning mountain backdrop make it an ideal place for swimming, sunbathing, and water sports.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Bellapais

Bellapais is a haven for nature lovers and adventure seekers, thanks to its numerous hiking and biking trails. Some of the popular routes include:

  • Bellapais Abbey Trail: This trail takes you through the charming village of Bellapais, offering a glimpse into its traditional architecture and cultural heritage.

  • Kyrenia Mountains Trail: This trail takes you through the lush green forests and traditional villages of the Kyrenia Mountains, offering stunning views of the coastline and the surrounding mountains.

  • Escape Beach Trail: This trail takes you along the coastline towards Escape Beach, offering panoramic views of the Mediterranean Sea and the Kyrenia Mountains.

Best Things to Do in Bellapais: Activities and Tours

  • Visit Bellapais Abbey: Explore the stunning Gothic architecture of the Bellapais Abbey and enjoy the panoramic view of the island.

  • Explore the Traditional Villages: Take a stroll through the traditional villages of the Kyrenia Mountains and explore their traditional architecture and rural life.

  • Enjoy Water Sports: Escape Beach offers a variety of water sports, such as jet skiing, parasailing, and windsurfing.

  • Take a Boat Tour: Take a boat tour around the coast of Northern Cyprus and explore its hidden coves, beaches, and rocky cliffs.

Food and Drink Scene of Bellapais

Must-Try Dishes

  • Kleftiko: A delicious lamb dish that is slow-cooked with herbs, garlic, and potatoes.

  • Meze: A selection of small dishes that include grilled meats, vegetables, dips, and cheeses.

  • Halloumi: A traditional Cypriot cheese that is commonly grilled or fried and served as a side dish.

Local Beverages

  • Zivania: A traditional Cypriot spirit that is distilled from grapes.

  • Kolokasi: A refreshing drink that is made from the root vegetable known as taro.

  • Cyprus Coffee: A strong, rich coffee that is served in small cups and is traditionally sweetened with sugar.

What to Buy in Bellapais: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Embroidery: Bellapais is known for its traditional embroidery, which is still made by local women.

  • Lace: Cyprus is also famous for its lace, which is made from cotton or linen and is intricately crafted by local artisans.

  • Wine and Spirits: Cyprus produces a variety of wines and spirits, such as Zivania and Commandaria, which make great souvenirs and gifts.

Local Culture and Customs of Bellapais

Cyprus has a rich and diverse cultural heritage that is reflected in its traditions, celebrations, and customs. Some of the important customs of Bellapais include:

  • Hospitality: Cypriots are known for their warm hospitality and generosity towards guests.

  • Religion: The majority of the population in Cyprus follows the Greek Orthodox Church, which plays an important role in the island’s cultural and social life.

  • Celebrations: Cyprus celebrates several festivals and holidays throughout the year, such as Easter, Christmas, and Independence Day.

Best Time to Visit Bellapais

The best time to visit Bellapais is between April and October when the weather is warm and sunny, and the island is lively with festivals and celebrations. The peak season is from June to August, so it’s advisable to book accommodations and flights in advance.

Do’s and Don’ts of Bellapais

Do:

  • Respect Local Customs: Always respect local customs and traditions, such as covering your head and shoulders while entering a church.

  • Take Your Shoes Off: Take off your shoes while entering a mosque or someone’s home.

  • Try Local Cuisine: Cyprus has a rich and diverse food scene, so be sure to try the local cuisine and beverages.

Don’t:

  • Disrespect Religion: Avoid making offensive comments about religion, as it is a sensitive topic in Cyprus.

  • Litter: Cyprus is known for its pristine beaches and natural landscapes, so avoid littering and use designated trash bins.

  • Overly Show Off: Avoid showing off your wealth or belongings, as it can be considered impolite.

Safety and Health

Safety in Bellapais

Bellapais is a safe and secure destination, with low crime rates and friendly locals. However, it’s always advisable to take basic precautions, such as:

  • Avoid Walking Alone at Night: Avoid walking alone in dark or isolated areas, especially at night.

  • Keep Your Belongings Safe: Keep your belongings safe and secure in your hotel room or guesthouse.

  • Follow Local Customs and Laws: Always follow local customs and laws, such as wearing helmets while riding a bike or a scooter.

Health in Bellapais

Cyprus has a good healthcare system, with several public and private hospitals and clinics. Visitors should ensure that they have adequate health insurance before traveling and carry a first aid kit for emergencies. It’s also advisable to drink bottled water and avoid tap water.

Getting to Bellapais: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Bellapais

  • Ercan International Airport: Located in Northern Cyprus, this is the nearest international airport to Bellapais.

  • Larnaca International Airport: Located in South Cyprus, this is the nearest international airport to Northern Cyprus.

Bus Stations Near Bellapais

  • Kyrenia Bus Station: Located in Kyrenia, this is the main bus station that connects Bellapais with other parts of the island.

  • Lefkosa Bus Station: Located in the capital city of Nicosia, this bus station also connects Bellapais with other parts of Northern Cyprus.

Other Options

  • Taxi: Taxis are widely available in Bellapais and are a convenient way to explore the village and its surroundings.

  • Car Rental: Car rental is also available in Bellapais and is a popular option for those who want to explore the island at their own pace.

How to Get Around in Bellapais

Bellapais is a small village that can be easily explored on foot. However, if you want to explore the surrounding areas, you can use local buses, taxis, or rent a car or a bike.

Where to Go Next from Bellapais: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Kyrenia: Located just a few kilometers from Bellapais, Kyrenia is a bustling city that offers a variety of tourist attractions, such as the Kyrenia Castle, the Venetian Harbour, and the Kyrenia Mountains.

  • Nicosia: The capital city of Cyprus, Nicosia, is located about an hour’s drive from Bellapais and offers a unique blend of ancient and modern architecture, such as the Selimiye Mosque and the Leventis Municipal Museum.

  • Famagusta: Located in the eastern part of the island, Famagusta is known for its beautiful beaches, such as Golden Beach, and its historical landmarks, such as the Othello Castle and the Lala Mustafa Pasha Mosque.
