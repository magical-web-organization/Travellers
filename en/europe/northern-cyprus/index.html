Introduction to Northern Cyprus

Northern Cyprus is a self-declared state recognized only by Turkey and is located on the northern coast of the island of Cyprus. The country is steeped in history and offers a mix of natural beauty, cultural attractions, and outdoor adventures. The local culture is heavily influenced by Turkish and Greek elements, making it a unique tourist destination.

List of Places to Visit in Northern Cyprus

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  1. St. Hilarion Castle: This medieval castle sits atop a mountain and offers breathtaking views of the surrounding area.
  2. Kyrenia Castle: Built in the 16th century by the Venetians, this castle is home to a museum and provides a glimpse into the island’s history.
  3. Bellapais Abbey: A well-preserved monastery that dates back to the early 13th century, featuring stunning architecture and a serene atmosphere.
  4. Ancient Salamis: An ancient city with ruins dating back to the 11th century BC, including a Roman amphitheater, gymnasium, and baths.

Natural Attractions of Northern Cyprus: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  1. Karpaz Peninsula: A pristine area with untouched beaches and wildlife. Visitors can hike, bike, or take a guided tour to explore the area.
  2. Kyrenia Mountains: Perfect for hiking and trekking, these mountains offer stunning views of the coast and beyond.
  3. Golden Beach: A serene beach known for its golden sand and clear waters, offering a peaceful retreat for visitors.
  4. Cape Andreas: The northernmost point of the island, Cape Andreas offers stunning views of the Mediterranean Sea.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Northern Cyprus

  1. Nightingale Trail: A 6km long trail that passes through the Kyrenia Mountains, offering breathtaking views and a glimpse into local wildlife and vegetation.
  2. Buffavento Castle Trail: A difficult but rewarding hike that leads to Buffavento Castle, the highest castle in the Kyrenia mountain range.
  3. Karpaz Peninsula Trail: A 30km trail that passes through the Karpaz Peninsula’s natural wonders, including untouched beaches and wildlife.

Best Things to Do in Northern Cyprus: Activities and Tours

  1. Scuba Diving: Northern Cyprus has several diving sites, including the Zenobia Wreck, the fourth-best wreck diving spot in the world.
  2. Catamaran Day Trip: Explore the coastline and enjoy the Mediterranean Sea on a catamaran day trip.
  3. Quad Bike Tours: Take a quad bike tour of Northern Cyprus’s scenic spots and enjoy a thrilling adventure.
  4. Visit Local Markets: Explore the local markets and buy traditional Cypriot products, including locally sourced fruits, vegetables, and handicrafts.

Food and Drink Scene of Northern Cyprus

Must-Try Dishes

  1. Meze: A selection of small dishes including dips, grilled meat, and salads.
  2. Kebabs: Delicious grilled meat skewers.
  3. Dolma: Stuffed vegetables usually stuffed with rice.
  4. Baklava: A sweet pastry filled with nuts and honey.

Local Beverages

  1. Turkish Coffee
  2. Raki: A traditional Turkish alcoholic drink
  3. Ayran: A yogurt-based drink

Where to Stay in Northern Cyprus: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  1. Salamis Bay Conti Resort Hotel & Casino: A luxury resort offering stunning sea views and excellent amenities.
  2. Buyuk Han Guesthouse: A historic guesthouse located in the heart of Nicosia’s Old City.
  3. Rose Gardens Holiday Village: Self-catering accommodation surrounded by gardens and offering an outdoor pool and restaurant.

What to Buy in Northern Cyprus: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  1. Lefkara Lace: Traditional Cypriot lace.
  2. Zivania: A local spirit made from grapes and infused with flavors.
  3. Turkish Delight: A sweet treat made from sugar and starch.

Local Culture and Customs of Northern Cyprus

Northern Cyprus has a unique blend of Turkish and Greek cultures, reflected in its historic architecture and cuisine. Visitors should respect the conservative Muslim culture and dress modestly, especially when visiting religious sites.

Best Time to Visit Northern Cyprus

The best time to visit Northern Cyprus is between April and June and September and November. During these months, the weather is mild, and the island is less crowded. Summer months can be hot and crowded, and winter months can be rainy.

Do’s and Don’ts of Northern Cyprus

Do:

  • Respect local customs and traditions
  • Carry cash, as not all businesses accept credit cards
  • Try local food and drink

Don’t:

  • Wear revealing clothing in public areas
  • Take photographs of military installations
  • Disrupt the peace, as public displays of anger and aggression are not tolerated.

Safety and Health

Safety in Northern Cyprus

Northern Cyprus is generally safe for tourists. However, visitors should take precautions, such as avoiding unlit areas at night and not leaving valuables unattended.

Health in Northern Cyprus

Visitors should take necessary precautions, such as using sunscreen, staying hydrated, and wearing mosquito repellent to avoid insect bites.
