A Comprehensive Guide to Nicosia (North)

Nicosia (North) is located in the Nicosia district (North) of Northern Cyprus, in Europe. While the city is divided between the Republic of Cyprus and the Turkish Republic of Northern Cyprus, the northern side of Nicosia is rich in history, culture, and natural beauty. In this travel guide, you’ll discover all the top things to do, places to visit, and food to try, along with cultural attractions and practical information.

List of Places to Visit in Nicosia (North)

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Nicosia (North) boasts a rich history dating back to the Bronze Age, and there’s no better place to explore the city’s past than its many museums and historical sites. Here are some of the top attractions:

  • Selimiye Mosque: formerly known as the Cathedral of Saint Sophia, this 12th-century landmark is a stunning example of Gothic architecture, with intricate stonework, stained glass windows, and beautiful marble columns.
  • Buyuk Han: once a caravanserai, or roadside inn, this 16th-century building is now a cultural center with shops, restaurants, and galleries showcasing local crafts and artwork.

Natural Attractions of Nicosia (North): Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Beyond the city’s rich history and culture, there are plenty of opportunities to experience Northern Cyprus’s scenic beauty. From hiking to biking, here are some top natural attractions in Nicosia (North):

  • Kyrenia Mountains: hiking and mountain biking trails are abundant, ranging from easy nature walks to challenging ascents into the slopes of the Kyrenia Range.
  • St. Hilarion Castle: nestled in the mountains, this stunning castle looks like something out of a fairytale, and visitors can explore its maze-like corridors and breathtaking views.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Nicosia (North)

Nicosia (North) is a paradise for outdoor enthusiasts, with beautiful trails and paths winding throughout the city and surrounding countryside. Here are some of the best hiking and biking routes:

  • Buffavento Castle Hike: a challenging 10 km trek from the village of Five Finger Mountains to the ancient Buffavento Castle, featuring stunning views and a chance to explore the castle ruins.
  • Nicosia-Lefkosa City Cycle Tour: a 12 km guided tour of the city’s most iconic landmarks, including the Selimiye Mosque, Buyuk Han, and the Great Inn.

Best Things to Do in Nicosia (North): Activities and Tours

From cultural tours to food and wine experiences, here are some of the best things to do in Nicosia (North):

  • Old City Walk: a guided tour of the city’s most historic sites, including the Selimiye Mosque, the Buyuk Han, and the Great Inn.
  • Traditional Cyprus Meze: a food and drink tour, featuring local specialties like halloumi cheese, souvlaki, and traditional meze platters.

Week-Long Itinerary for Nicosia (North)

If you’re planning a week-long trip to Nicosia (North), here’s a sample itinerary:

Day 1: Arrive and explore the Old City Day 2: Visit the Selimiye Mosque and the Buyuk Han Day 3: Take a hike in the Kyrenia Mountains Day 4: Tour the St. Hilarion Castle and the remains of the old city walls Day 5: Take a food and wine tour, sampling local specialties and visiting wineries Day 6: Explore the island’s northern coast, including the seaside town of Kyrenia Day 7: Depart from Nicosia (North)

Food and Drink Scene of Nicosia (North)

When it comes to food and drink in Nicosia (North), you’ll encounter a delicious mix of Mediterranean and Middle Eastern flavors, with plenty of fresh produce, grilled meats, and seafood on the menus. Here are some must-try dishes and beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Halloumi cheese: a local specialty made from sheep and goat milk, grilled and served as a side dish or appetizer.
  • Souvlaki: grilled meat skewers, typically served with pitta bread and yogurt sauce.
  • Kleftiko: slow-cooked lamb or goat, often served with potatoes and vegetables.

Local Beverages

  • Zivania: a potent, clear brandy made from grapes, typically served as a digestive.
  • Commandaria: a sweet dessert wine made from sun-dried grapes, prized for its complex flavors and aromas.

Where to Stay in Nicosia (North): Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Whether you’re looking for luxurious accommodations, budget-friendly hostels, or cozy guesthouses, Nicosia (North) has plenty of options to suit your needs and tastes. Here are some top picks:

  • The Arkin Colony Hotel: a luxurious property located in the heart of the Old City, featuring elegant rooms, a spa, and a rooftop bar with stunning views.
  • The Kemerli Konak Boutique Hotel: a charming boutique hotel in the historic Buyuk Han, featuring traditional architecture, colorful decor, and a tranquil courtyard.

What to Buy in Nicosia (North): Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

If you’re looking for a unique souvenir, Nicosia (North) is home to a range of artisanal crafts and local products. Here are some top picks:

  • Embroidery: handcrafted lace, tablecloths, and clothing items featuring intricate embroidery work.
  • Pottery: traditional, hand-painted pottery with a range of colors and designs.
  • Olive oil: a range of high-quality olive oils made from locally grown olives.

Local Culture and Customs of Nicosia (North)

Northern Cyprus has a unique culture and history, influenced by its location at the crossroads of Europe and the Middle East. Here are some key cultural customs to keep in mind:

  • Dress modestly: the island is predominantly Muslim, so it’s important to respect local customs by dressing modestly and covering up when visiting religious sites.
  • Be polite and respectful: politeness and respect are highly valued in Northern Cyprus, so be sure to say please and thank you, and avoid rude or confrontational behavior.

Best Time to Visit Nicosia (North)

The best time to visit Nicosia (North) is during the shoulder seasons of spring and fall, when the weather is mild and comfortable, and the crowds are smaller than in the height of summer.

Do’s and Don’ts of Nicosia (North)

Do:

  • Visit the Selimiye Mosque and the Buyuk Han.
  • Try local dishes like halloumi cheese and souvlaki.
  • Dress modestly when visiting religious sites.

Don’t:

  • Engage in political discussions or debates.
  • Sunbathe topless or nude.

Safety and Health

Safety in Nicosia (North)

Nicosia (North) is a safe city to visit, but it’s always important to take normal travel precautions, such as keeping your valuables secure and avoiding walking alone at night.

Health in Nicosia (North)

Northern Cyprus has a high standard of healthcare, but it’s always a good idea to have comprehensive travel insurance that provides coverage for medical emergencies and hospitalization.

Getting to Nicosia (North): Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Nicosia (North)

Ercan International Airport is the closest airport to Nicosia (North), located 20 km away. Flights to and from Istanbul, Ankara, and other major Turkish cities are available.

Bus Stations Near Nicosia (North)

There are several bus stations in Nicosia (North), including the Famagusta Gate Bus Station, which connects the city to other parts of Northern Cyprus.

How to Get Around in Nicosia (North)

The best way to get around Nicosia (North) is on foot, as many of the city’s top attractions are located within walking distance of one another. Taxis and car rental services are also available, but be prepared for heavy traffic during peak tourist season.

Where to Go Next from Nicosia (North): Nearby Destinations and Excursions

If you’re looking for more things to do in Northern Cyprus, there are plenty of other destinations and excursions to consider:

  • Kyrenia: a charming coastal town with a scenic harbor, historic castle, and beautiful beaches.
  • Famagusta: a historic town known for its ancient city walls, citadel, and impressive Gothic cathedral.

Conclusion

Nicosia (North) is a fascinating destination steeped in history, culture, and natural beauty. Whether you’re interested in exploring the city’s museums and historical landmarks, hiking its scenic mountains, or trying local foods and drinks, there’s something for everyone to enjoy in Nicosia (North).
