Introduction to Angolemi

Located within the Nicosia district of Northern Cyprus in Europe, Angolemi is a small yet charming town that offers visitors a unique blend of culture, history, and natural beauty. The town is known for its ancient ruins, historic buildings, and scenic landscapes, attracting travelers from all over the world. In this comprehensive travel guide, we will take you through some of the best things to do in Angolemi, including must-visit attractions, outdoor adventures, hiking/biking routes, local cuisine, where to stay, and much more.

List of Places to Visit in Angolemi

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Angolemi Castle: Also known as the St. Hilarion Castle, this ancient castle is considered one of the most beautiful and well-preserved castles in Cyprus. Built by the Byzantines in the 11th century, the castle provides some of the most breathtaking views of the town and its surroundings.

  • Beylerbeyi Palace: This historic palace was built in the 18th century and served as the residence of Ottoman governors. Today, it is a popular tourist attraction for those interested in Ottoman architecture and design.

  • Kyrenia Harbour: One of the most picturesque locations in Angolemi, Kyrenia Harbour is a must-visit spot for anyone interested in history and architecture. It is also home to a wide array of restaurants, bars, and cafes, making it the perfect place to spend an evening.

  • Bellapais Abbey: A well-preserved Gothic-style abbey located in the mountains overlooking the town, Bellapais Abbey is a true masterpiece of medieval architecture. Visitors can explore the abbey’s vaulted nave, chapter house, cloisters, and refectory while taking in the stunning views of the surrounding area.

Natural Attractions of Angolemi: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Korucam Forest: A dense forest located on the slopes of the Kyrenia mountains, Korucam’s varied flora and fauna make it one of the most popular hiking destinations in the area. The forest is also home to some of the most scenic picnic spots in Angolemi.

  • Alevkaya Forest: Another forested area near Angolemi, Alevkaya is home to an abundance of wildlife and some of the most stunning natural vistas in the region. Visitors can enjoy a variety of outdoor activities here, including hiking, camping, and birdwatching.

  • Buffavento Castle: A historic castle located on the Kyrenia mountain range, Buffavento Castle offers visitors some of the best panoramic views of Angolemi. Visitors can take a leisurely hike up the mountain to reach the castle, or enjoy a guided tour to learn about its history and architecture.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Angolemi

For those who love outdoor activities, Angolemi offers a plethora of hiking and biking routes that traverse the town’s historic streets, mountainous terrain, and natural landscapes.

  • Kyrenia Mountains: The hills and mountains around Angolemi offer some of the best hiking trails on the island, stretching from the foothills all the way up to the highest peaks. Some of the popular routes through the Kyrenia Mountains include The Five Finger Mountains, St. Hilarion Castle to Bellapais Abbey, and many more.

  • Coastal Path: Angolemi’s picturesque coastline offers a beautiful backdrop for a leisurely bike ride or hike. The 16 km walk from Korucam to Kormacit is one of the most popular routes for visitors to the town.

Best Things to Do in Angolemi: Activities and Tours

  • Explore Ancient Ruins: Cyprus is known for its rich history and culture, and Angolemi is no exception. Visitors can explore the ancient ruins of the Kyrenia mountains and the town itself, including the ruins of the Bellapais Abbey and St. Hilarion Castle.

  • Visit Museums: Angolemi is home to a number of museums, including the Kyrenia Castle Museum, the Icon Museum, and the Folk Art Museum, which offer visitors the opportunity to learn about the local culture and history.

  • Dine Out: Angolemi is known for its diverse mix of restaurants, offering a wide variety of cuisines to suit all palates. Visitors can choose from Mediterranean, Turkish, and Cypriot-style cuisine, among others.

  • Golfing: For those who enjoy golf, Angolemi has a number of golf courses that offer players some of the most scenic views on the island. Some of the most popular golf courses here include the Korineum Golf Course and the Cyprus Golf Club.

Food and Drink Scene of Angolemi

Must-Try Dishes

  • Meze: A well-known Turkish and Cypriot cuisine, Meze is a collection of small dishes that are served with drinks and are meant to be shared among patrons at the table.

  • Baklava: One of the most popular desserts in the region, Baklava is a sweet pastry made with layers of filo dough and filled with nuts and honey.

  • Kleftiko: A slow-cooked lamb dish, Kleftiko is a must-try for meat lovers. The lamb is cooked until it is tender and succulent, with the meat falling off the bone.

Local Beverages

  • Raki: A clear spirit made from distilled grapes, Raki is the national drink of Turkey and Cyprus. It is usually consumed with water and served with Meze.

  • Ayran: A refreshing and healthy yogurt drink popular in Turkey and Cyprus, Ayran is a perfect complement to spicy or heavy dishes.

Where to Stay in Angolemi: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

There are a range of accommodation options available to travelers in Angolemi, from high-end hotels to budget-friendly hostels and guesthouses.

  • Korineum Golf and Beach Resort: A luxury resort in the heart of the Kyrenia Mountains, Korineum offers visitors a wide range of amenities, including a golf course, spa, and restaurants.

  • Bellapais Monastry Village: A historical hotel located within the village of Bellapais, this hotel offers visitors a chance to stay in a beautiful monastery that dates back to the 12th century.

  • Kyrenia Palace Boutique Hotel: Located in the heart of Kyrenia, this boutique hotel offers guests cozy, stylish rooms with unbeatable views of the harbor.

What to Buy in Angolemi: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Visitors to Angolemi will find numerous local craft shops, boutiques, and markets offering a range of unique souvenirs and traditional products. Some of the best items to take home include:

  • Cypriot Laces and Embroideries: These intricate needlework pieces are a traditional handicraft in Cyprus and make a perfect gift or souvenir.

  • Olive Oil and Olives: Cyprus is famous for its olive groves, and visitors can buy locally produced olive oil and olives in many shops and stalls around Angolemi.

Local Culture and Customs of Angolemi

When visiting Angolemi, it is important to remember that the town is a conservative and religious area, and visitors should be respectful of local customs and traditions. Dress modestly when visiting religious sites and keep in mind that smoking is banned in public areas.

Best Time to Visit Angolemi

The best time to visit Angolemi is during the spring and fall seasons, when the weather is mild and pleasant. The summer months can be very hot, so visitors should be prepared for high temperatures and crowded beaches.

Do’s and Don’ts of Angolemi

Do:

  • Respect local customs and traditions
  • Dress appropriately when visiting religious sites
  • Try the local cuisine and drinks
  • Explore the town’s historic landmarks
  • Enjoy the outdoor activities and natural beauty

Don’t:

  • Smoke in public areas
  • Take photographs of locals without their permission
  • Wear revealing clothing in public areas
  • Speak in a loud or disrespectful manner

Safety and Health

Safety in Angolemi

Angolemi is generally a safe town for visitors, with low crime rates. However, visitors should still keep their valuables safe and exercise caution, especially at night.

Health in Angolemi

The healthcare system in Northern Cyprus is considered very good, and visitors can access medical help at any time at the local hospitals and clinics. Visitors are advised to bring their own prescription medications and seek medical advice before traveling to the country.

Getting to Angolemi: Transportation Options and Logistics

Angolemi is easily accessible by air, sea, and road.

Airports Near Angolemi

The nearest airport to Angolemi is the Ercan International Airport, which is about a 45-minute drive from the town.

Bus Stations Near Angolemi

The main bus station in Kyrenia is the Kyrenia Bus Terminal, which offers regular connections to Angolemi and other towns in the region.

Other Options

Visitors can also take taxis or hire a car to get around Angolemi and the surrounding region. Car rentals are readily available at the airport, as well as in the town itself.

How to Get Around in Angolemi

Angolemi is a small town, and it is easy to explore on foot. Visitors can also take taxis or hire a car to get around and explore the beautiful scenery at their own pace.

Where to Go Next from Angolemi: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Angolemi is a great base for exploring other parts of Northern Cyprus. Some nearby destinations and excursions include:

  • Nicosia: The capital city of Northern Cyprus is a lively and vibrant city with a rich history and culture.

  • Famagusta: A historic city with a stunning coastline and beautiful beaches, Famagusta is a must-visit destination for beach lovers.

  • Lefka: Located in the western part of Northern Cyprus, Lefka is a small town known for its traditional architecture and beautiful landscapes.

Conclusion

Angolemi is a small town that packs a big punch, offering visitors a unique blend of history, culture, and natural beauty. Whether you’re interested in exploring ancient ruins, hiking in the mountains, or enjoying local cuisine, Angolemi has something to offer everyone. With this comprehensive travel guide, you can plan an unforgettable trip to this picturesque town and its surrounding areas.
