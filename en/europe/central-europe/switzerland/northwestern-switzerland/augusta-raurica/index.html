Introduction to Augusta Raurica

Augusta Raurica is a historic site located in Northwestern Switzerland, near the city of Basel. It was a prosperous Roman colony and played an important role in the development of the Roman Empire. Today, Augusta Raurica is a popular tourist destination due to its well-preserved ruins and fascinating history.

List of Places to Visit in Augusta Raurica

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • The Augusta Raurica Museum: This museum outlines the history and importance of Augusta Raurica. It also has an extensive collection of exhibited artifacts found in the archaeological site.
  • The Roman Theatre: This theatre was used for various performances and was capable of accommodating over 8,000 spectators.
  • The Forum: This was the heart of the Roman town and was used as a public square. Today, it features remnants such as the basilica (administrative office) and the temples.

Natural Attractions of Augusta Raurica: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • The Garden of Four Seasons: The Garden of Four Seasons is an outdoor space that was used to grow exotic plants from different regions of the Roman Empire. Visitors can explore the reconstructed garden and learn about the different types of plants grown during Roman times.
  • The Roman Aqueduct: The aqueduct was used to transport water throughout the town of Augusta Raurica. Today, visitors can hike or bike along the historic structure and enjoy the scenic views.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Augusta Raurica

  • Augusta Raurica – Bruderholz Loop: An 8-mile hike or bike ride that takes you on a journey through stunning forest landscape and past mysterious ruins.
  • Rhine Route: A biking route that follows the Rhine river through Switzerland, Germany, and the Netherlands. The route passes through Augusta Raurica, taking riders past cultural sites and scenic landscapes.

Best Things to Do in Augusta Raurica: Activities and Tours

  • Guided tours: Get the most out of your visit by taking a guided tour. Professional guides will share their wealth of knowledge about the town and its history.
  • The Roman Festival: Every year, Augusta Raurica hosts a Roman Festival in which visitors can experience Roman culture, food, and even see gladiator fights.

Food and Drink Scene of Augusta Raurica

Must-Try Dishes

  • Rösti: A Swiss dish made with grated potatoes and often served with eggs and sausages.
  • Eierschwämmli: A local mushroom variety that is usually served with the traditional Swiss dish of Älplermagronen (macaroni with potatoes, cheese, and onions).

Local Beverages

  • Fendant wine: A white grape variety that is typically grown in Switzerland.
  • Birchermüesli: A Swiss breakfast dish made with oats, fruit, and yoghurt.

  • B&B Zimba: This charming guesthouse is located in Augst and provides comfortable rooms and a complimentary breakfast.

What to Buy in Augusta Raurica: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Swiss chocolate: Switzerland is famous for its chocolate, and Augusta Raurica offers a variety of local chocolate shops.
  • Handmade pottery: Local artisans create beautiful handmade pottery that can serve as a unique souvenir.

Local Culture and Customs of Augusta Raurica

In Switzerland, it is customary to greet others with a handshake and to use formal titles such as “Herr” or “Frau”. Smoking is prohibited in public places, and it’s customary to leave a tip of 10% in restaurants.

Best Time to Visit Augusta Raurica

The best time to visit Augusta Raurica is during the warmer months from May to September. During this time, visitors can enjoy the scenic outdoor activities, cultural events, and festivals. The temperature averages between 50-70 °F (10-20°C), making it comfortable for outdoor activities.

Do’s and Don’ts of Augusta Raurica

Do:

  • Respect the history and culture of the town and its people.
  • Take care of the environment, including not littering.

Don’t:

  • Smoke in public places where it’s not allowed.
  • Touch any of the historical artifacts or ruins.

Safety and Health

Safety in Augusta Raurica

Augusta Raurica is a safe destination. Tourists should take standard precautions while traveling, such as being mindful of pickpocketing in crowded areas.

Health in Augusta Raurica

Switzerland has an excellent healthcare system. Visitors from abroad are advised to take out travel insurance before they visit. Visitors should also bring any prescription medication they require.

Getting to Augusta Raurica: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Augusta Raurica

  • EuroAirport Basel-Mulhouse-Freiburg: The nearest airport to Augusta Raurica is EuroAirport, which is approximately 45 minutes away by car.

Bus Stations Near Augusta Raurica

  • Augst, Riburgstrasse (bus line 64): This bus stop is located a short walking distance from Augusta Raurica.

Other Options

  • Train: It is also easy to travel by train, with regular services from Basel SBB Station to Augst Station.

How to Get Around in Augusta Raurica

The best way to get around Augusta Raurica is by foot. The town is compact, and most attractions are located within walking distance. Visitors can also rent bicycles from local shops and explore the nearby scenic routes.

Where to Go Next from Augusta Raurica: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Basel: The third-largest city in Switzerland, Basel is located only 10 miles away from Augusta Raurica. It’s known for its impressive museums, galleries, and historic architecture.
  • Black Forest: The Black Forest is a mountainous forest region located across Germany, France, and Switzerland. It offers beautiful views, outdoor activities, and traditional cuisine.

Conclusion

Augusta Raurica is a must-visit destination for history buffs and outdoor enthusiasts. With well-preserved ruins, scenic routes, cultural events, and delicious local cuisine, it’s a place that offers something for everyone. Plan your trip now to explore this fascinating destination.
