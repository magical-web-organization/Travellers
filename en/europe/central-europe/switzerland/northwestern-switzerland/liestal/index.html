Introduction to Liestal

Liestal is a picturesque town located in Northwestern Switzerland, in the canton of Basel-Country. It is the capital of the canton and an ideal destination for travelers looking for a mix of culture, history, and outdoor activities. Liestal is situated in the Swiss Plateau and is surrounded by lush green hills, forests, and scenic valleys that offer breathtaking views.

This travel guide will provide you with all the information you need to make the most of your trip to Liestal. From top attractions to hiking/biking routes, food and drink, and where to stay, we have got you covered.

List of Places to Visit in Liestal

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  1. Liestal Castle - One of the most significant landmarks in Liestal, Liestal Castle, offers stunning views of the town and the surrounding countryside. Built-in the 13th century, the castle has a rich history and is now used as a museum.
  2. Museum.BL - Learn about the region’s history and culture in this museum. Exhibitions range from art and archaeology to natural history and ethnography.
  3. Alte Rheinbrücke - This historical bridge dates back to the 14th century and is now used as a pedestrian walkway, offering a view of the Rhine River.
  4. Martinskirche - This is a gothic church that boasts beautiful stained glass windows, and the best part is it’s free to enter.
  5. Schloss Ebenrain - located nearby in Sissach, this castle is a great place to explore the history and culture of Basel country.

Natural Attractions of Liestal: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  1. Chienberg - This hill offers hiking trails and magnificent views of the surrounding hills and forests.
  2. Eptingen - This small village with beautiful architecture is surrounded by green hills, and this is also a great starting point for various hiking and biking trails.
  3. Hinteri Egg - This hill offers panoramic views of Liestal, including the Liestal Castle and the town’s old town.
  4. Schartenfels - This hill’s summit offers a beautiful view of the town and the surrounding countryside and is an ideal place for picnicking.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Liestal

  1. Chienberg Trail - This trail offers stunning views of the surrounding hills and forests. It is suitable for both hiking and mountain biking.
  2. Liestal to Sissach Trail - This 11km path is ideal for cycling enthusiasts and offers a chance to explore the countryside.
  3. Hinteri Egg Trail - This trail is located near the Liestal Castle and offers a 2-hour hike through forests and hills.
  4. Zur Farnsburg Trail - This trail begins in the town of Liestal and ends at the Farnsburg Castle. It covers an area of about 12kms and takes about 4 hours to complete.

Best Things to Do in Liestal: Activities and Tours

  1. Take a guided tour of the Liestal Castle and learn about the canton’s rich history.
  2. Explore the town’s old town and its charming cafés, boutiques, and restaurants.
  3. Take a picnic to one of the hills and enjoy the stunning views.
  4. Attend the Basel-Landschaft fair held in August, an annual showcase of the region’s culture and traditions.
  5. Enjoy a relaxing spa day at the Panorama Resort & Spa, located just a few minutes from Liestal.

Week-Long Itinerary for Liestal

Day 1: Visit Liestal Castle and the Museum.BL. Day 2: Take a guided bike tour of the countryside, starting from Liestal to Farnsburg Castle. Day 3: Hike the Chienberg Trail, followed by a picnic at the summit. Day 4: Take a trip to Alte Rheinbrücke and Martinskirche. Day 5: Explore the town’s old town and enjoy some shopping and local culinary delights. Day 6: Visit the historical Schloss Ebenrain, followed by a relaxing spa day at Panorama Resort & Spa. Day 7: Take a guided tour of Hinteri Egg Trail.

Food and Drink Scene of Liestal

Must-Try Dishes

  1. Basler Leckerli - Traditional Basel biscuits made with hazelnuts and honey.
  2. Cordon Bleu - a popular dish made from sliced pork, ham, and cheese.
  3. Rösti - A Swiss potato dish that is a common breakfast item, made from grated potatoes and served with sautéed onions and other toppings like eggs, bacon, or ham.
  4. Basler Mehlsuppe - A hearty soup made from flour, onions, and butter and is served with small pieces of cheese and bread.

Local Beverages

  1. Röteli - A Swiss cherry liqueur that is perfect for a nightcap or as a dessert drink.
  2. Sissacher Flaschen - A locally-made white wine, locally produced and often served with meals.
  3. Feldschlösschen Beer - a popular Swiss beer brewed in nearby Rheinfelden, it is available in various varieties like hoppy, smooth, or fruity.

Where to Stay in Liestal: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  1. Engel Liestal - This hotel is in the heart of Liestal and offers traditional Swiss-style rooms.
  2. Hotel Bienenberg - Located south of Liestal in an idyllic setting surrounded by green forests, the hotel offers comfortable rooms and a natural setting perfect for relaxation.
  3. B&B Hersberg - This bed and breakfast is located in Hersberg and offers comfortable rooms with stunning views of the surrounding countryside.

What to Buy in Liestal: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  1. Swiss chocolate - Chocolaterie am Rathaus or Schiesser Confiserie, both located in the old town, offer some of the best Swiss chocolate.
  2. Local cheese - head to the market to find local cheese from farmers.
  3. Swiss Army Knife - Victorinox, the original Swiss Army Knife, is made in nearby Ibach and is a great gift to bring home.

Local Culture and Customs of Liestal

The people of Liestal are warm and friendly, and visitors are always welcomed with open arms. It is customary to say hello and smile when meeting strangers.

Best Time to Visit Liestal

The best time to visit Liestal is between May and September when the weather is pleasant and conducive to outdoor activities.

Do’s and Don’ts of Liestal

Do:

  1. Follow the designated hiking and biking routes
  2. carry cash, as many establishments don’t accept credit cards.
  3. Try the local cuisine and drinks

Don’t:

  1. Litter in public areas
  2. Take pictures of people without permission
  3. Expect shops or businesses to be open on Sundays.

Safety and Health

Safety in Liestal

Switzerland is a safe country, but visitors should still exercise caution. Keep an eye on your belongings while traveling, and avoid walking alone in isolated areas at night.

Health in Liestal

Switzerland has excellent health care facilities, but it is still advisable to get travel insurance before traveling. Tap water in Liestal is safe to drink, and you can also buy bottled water in grocery stores or supermarkets.

Getting to Liestal: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Liestal

The nearest airports are EuroAirport Basel-Mulhouse-Freiburg and Zurich Airport. Once you arrive at either of the airports, take a train to Liestal Station, which takes approximately 30 minutes.

Bus Stations Near Liestal

Liestal has several bus stations that link it to other towns and cities in Switzerland.

Other Options

You can reach Liestal by car, train, or bus. The Swiss Federal Railways provides reliable connections between Liestal and other Swiss cities like Basel and Zurich.

How to Get Around in Liestal

Liestal is a small town and most of its attractions are accessible on foot. Buses are available for those who prefer public transportation. Biking is also a popular way to explore the town and its surrounding countryside.

Where to Go Next from Liestal: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  1. Basel - the cultural capital of Switzerland, is just a short train ride away from Liestal.
  2. The Rheinfelden Thermal Baths - a relaxing spa located in nearby Rheinfelden.
  3. The Swiss Knife Valley - a museum dedicated to the production of Victorinox Swiss Army knives, located in nearby Brunnen.

Conclusion

Liestal is a hidden gem that boasts of a rich history, fascinating culture, and beautiful natural scenery. It’s the perfect destination for those seeking adventure in the outdoors while also wanting to immerse themselves in the town’s culture and local cuisine. With this comprehensive guide, you’re well equipped to plan an unforgettable trip to Liestal.
