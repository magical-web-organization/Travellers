A Complete Guide to Appenzell, Switzerland

Located in Eastern Switzerland, in the heart of the Alps, Appenzell is a charming town known for its traditional architecture, scenic landscapes, and outdoor activities. In this comprehensive guide, you will discover the best things to do, places to visit, and where to stay in Appenzell.

Introduction to Appenzell

Appenzell is a picturesque town in the rolling hills of Eastern Switzerland, known for its traditional architecture, cultural heritage, and outdoor activities. The town is divided into two parts: Appenzell Inner Rhodes, which is the historic old town, and Appenzell Outer Rhodes, which is the newer part of the town. Appenzell is easily accessible by public transportation, making it a popular destination for day trips from nearby cities such as Zurich, Lucerne, and St. Gallen.

List of Places to Visit in Appenzell

Whether you are interested in outdoor activities or cultural attractions, there is something for every traveler in Appenzell. Here are some of the must-visit places in Appenzell:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Museum Liner: This museum showcases the work of modern artists from the region as well as international artists.
  • Landesmuseum Appenzell: The museum houses exhibits on Appenzell’s history, traditions, and customs.
  • Appenzeller Volkskunde-Museum: This museum showcases the traditional lifestyles of the Appenzell region’s inhabitants.
  • Zeil: This historic street in Appenzell is lined with traditional houses and shops.

Natural Attractions of Appenzell: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Ebenalp: This mountain offers spectacular scenic views of the surrounding mountains.
  • Santis: Another towering peak in the region, Santis also offers panoramic views of the Alps. Hiking is a popular activity here, with many trails starting from the cable car station.
  • Hoher Kasten: This mountain offers views of the Rhine Valley and the neighboring Liechtenstein. The spectacular revolving restaurant at the top is a must-visit.
  • Seealpsee: This crystal-clear lake is surrounded by forests and scenic hiking trails, making it a popular location for outdoor activities.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Appenzell

Appenzell is a paradise for hikers and bikers, with over 500 kilometers of well-marked trails. Here are some of the best hiking and biking routes in Appenzell:

  • Wasserauen–Ebenalp: This is one of the most popular hikes in Appenzell, starting from Wasserauen and leading to the Ebenalp mountain. The route passes through stunning Alpine landscapes and is suitable for hikers of all levels.
  • Appenzell – Weissbad: This easy and scenic hike begins in Appenzell and passes through the village of Weissbad.
  • Mountain Biking at the Stoss Pass: This mountain bike route passes through forests, hills, and mountain meadows, making it a challenging yet scenic ride for experienced bikers.
  • Alpstein Traverse: This challenging hiking route covers more than 100 kilometers and traverses the entire Alpstein mountain range.

Best Things to Do in Appenzell: Activities and Tours

In addition to hiking and biking, Appenzell offers a range of outdoor and cultural activities. Here are some of the best things to do in Appenzell:

  • Cheese Making Tour: Take a tour of the Appenzeller cheese factory and learn about the traditional cheese-making process.
  • Appenzell Folklore Show: Watch a traditional Appenzell music and dance show performed by locals in traditional costumes.
  • Appenzeller Bahnen: Take a scenic train ride through the beautiful landscapes of Eastern Switzerland.
  • Swiss Tasting Tour: Join a tour of local producers and sample various Swiss specialties, such as chocolate, cheese, and wine.

Week-Long Itinerary for Appenzell

If you’re planning to spend a week in Appenzell, here is a suggested itinerary:

Day Activity
Day 1 Explore the historic Appenzell Old Town, including the Zeil and the Landesmuseum
Day 2 Hike to the Ebenalp mountain and see the famous Wildkirchli caves
Day 3 Take a cheese-making tour and visit the local producers. In the evening, watch the Appenzell folklore show
Day 4 Take a scenic train ride on the Appenzeller Bahnen and visit the village of Gais
Day 5 Visit the Staubern mountain for panoramic views
Day 6 Take a day trip to Zurich, Lucerne, or St. Gallen
Day 7 Enjoy a leisurely day at Seealpsee and go for a swim.

Food and Drink Scene of Appenzell

Appenzell is known for its cheese, beer, and spirits. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Appenzeller Cheese: This cheese is made using a secret recipe that has been passed down through generations.
  • Schnitz und Durscht: A hearty dish of breaded and fried veal served with potatoes and a garlic cream sauce.
  • Alplermagronen: A Swiss version of macaroni and cheese, featuring potatoes, bacon, and cream.

Local Beverages

  • Appenzeller Beer: This local beer is brewed using traditional methods and has a fruity, hoppy taste.
  • Appenzeller Most: This regional cider is made from apples grown in the region and has a tangy taste.
  • Appenzeller Alpenbitter: This herbal digestif is made from a secret recipe featuring over 40 different herbs.

Where to Stay in Appenzell: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Appenzell offers a range of accommodation options, from luxury hotels to budget-friendly hostels. Here are some recommendations:

  • Romantik Hotel Säntis: This historic hotel is housed in a 17th-century building and offers panoramic views of the mountains.
  • Gasthaus Krone: This cozy inn features traditional wooden interiors and is located in the heart of the old town.
  • Pension Alpenblick: This charming guesthouse offers stunning views of the Alps and is located within walking distance of the Ebenalp hiking trail.

What to Buy in Appenzell: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Appenzell offers a range of locally made products and souvenirs. Here are some suggestions:

  • Appenzell Cheese: Bring home a wheel of the famous Appenzeller cheese.
  • Appenzeller Alpenbitter: This herbal digestif is a popular souvenir among tourists.
  • Appenzeller Crafts: Browse the many shops in the Zeil to find traditional handicrafts such as wooden toys, leather goods, and textiles.

Local Culture and Customs of Appenzell

Appenzell is known for its unique cultural traditions and customs. Here are some interesting facts about Appenzell:

  • Costumes: Appenzell locals proudly wear traditional costumes, often featuring bright colors and intricate patterns.
  • Yodeling: The Appenzeller folk music includes yodeling, which is a distinctive and enchanting vocal style.
  • Landsgemeinde: This is a traditional form of direct democracy where the community gathers in a public square to vote on local issues.

Best Time to Visit Appenzell

Appenzell is beautiful year-round, but the best time to visit depends on your preference. Summer is the best time for outdoor activities such as hiking and biking, while autumn is perfect for admiring the fall foliage. Winter is ideal for skiing and snowboarding, while spring is when the wildflowers are in bloom.

Do’s and Don’ts of Appenzell

Here are some do’s and don’ts to keep in mind when visiting Appenzell:

Do:

  • Respect Local Customs: Observe local customs and traditions, such as removing your shoes when entering someone’s home.
  • Try the Local Food: Appenzell is famous for its cheese, beer, and spirits. Be sure to try them all!
  • Shop Locally: Support the local economy by buying locally made products and souvenirs.

Don’t:

  • Litter: Keep the environment clean by disposing of trash properly.
  • Be Loud: Appenzell is a peaceful town, so be mindful of your noise level and surroundings.
  • Ignore Safety Precautions: Follow safety guidelines, especially when participating in outdoor activities.

Safety and Health

Appenzell is a safe and healthy destination, with excellent healthcare facilities and low crime rates. However, it is always advisable to take precautions when traveling.

Safety in Appenzell

  • Weather: Check weather forecasts before embarking on outdoor activities.
  • Trails: Stick to marked trails and avoid steep or dangerous paths.
  • Wildlife: Observe wildlife from a safe distance and never approach or feed them.

Health in Appenzell

  • Insurance: Make sure you have adequate health insurance for your trip.
  • Vaccinations: Check if you need any vaccinations before traveling.
  • Altitude Sickness: If hiking at high altitudes, be aware of the symptoms of altitude sickness and take necessary precautions.

Getting to Appenzell: Transportation Options and Logistics

Appenzell is easily accessible by public transportation. Here are some options for getting to Appenzell:

Airports Near Appenzell

  • Zurich Airport: Located 80 kilometers from Appenzell.

Bus Stations Near Appenzell

  • Appenzell Bahnhof: This is the main bus stop in Appenzell.

Other Options

  • Train: The Appenzeller Bahnen is a scenic train that connects Appenzell to nearby towns and cities.
  • Car: Appenzell is well-connected by roads and highways.

How to Get Around Appenzell

Appenzell is a small town, and most attractions are within walking distance of each other. Additionally, the Appenzeller Bahnen train connects the town with nearby villages and towns. For more flexibility, renting a bike or car is a good option.

Where to Go Next from Appenzell: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Appenzell is located in Eastern Switzerland, making it an excellent base for exploring neighboring towns and cities. Here are some nearby destinations and excursions:

  • St. Gallen: This historic city is located just 20 kilometers from Appenzell and is known for its Baroque architecture and cultural attractions.
  • Zurich: Switzerland’s largest city is located 80 kilometers from Appenzell and offers a range of cultural, historical and outdoor experiences.
  • Lake Constance: This beautiful lake is located just a short drive from Appenzell, and is a popular destination for water sports and outdoor activities.

Conclusion

Appenzell is a charming town that offers something for every type of traveler. From hiking and biking to cultural and food experiences, Appenzell is a must-visit destination in Eastern Switzerland. Plan your itinerary and explore all that this picturesque town has to offer.
