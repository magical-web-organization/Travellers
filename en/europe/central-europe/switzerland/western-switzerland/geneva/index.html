Introduction to Geneva

Located in Western Switzerland and known to be the second most populous city in Switzerland, Geneva is a beautiful city that is rich in history, culture, and diversity. From the stunning architecture and breathtaking scenery to the delicious cuisine and vibrant nightlife, Geneva is a must-visit destination for any traveler. In this comprehensive travel guide, we’ll explore the best places to visit, the most exciting things to do, and where to stay and eat in Geneva.

Best Things to Do in Geneva: Activities and Tours

Geneva is a city of adventure and culture that offers countless activities and tours to fill your days. Here are some of the top things to do:

  • Visit the United Nations Office at Geneva and take a guided tour of the Palace of Nations.
  • Explore the Lake Geneva waterfront with a scenic boat tour
  • Take a free bike tour of the city with Geneve Roule.
  • Try some local Swiss chocolate with the Geneva Chocolate Factory Tour.
  • Experience the beautiful Alpine scenery with a tour of Mont Blanc and Chamonix.
  • Take a Segway tour around Geneva Old Town.
  • Visit the CERN particle physics laboratory and take the underground tour.

Week-Long Itinerary for Geneva

Geneva offers so much to see and do that it can be challenging to fit everything into a few days. Here’s a week-long itinerary that covers the best of Geneva:

Day 1: Geneva City Tour & Boat Cruise

  • Take a guided city tour of Geneva and explore some of the most famous landmarks, including the Reformation Wall, the Palace of Nations, and St. Pierre Cathedral.
  • End your day with a relaxing boat cruise on Lake Geneva and watch the sunset over the mountains.

Day 2: Museum Day

  • Visit the International Red Cross and Red Crescent Museum and learn about the humanitarian work of the organization.
  • Explore the Patek Philippe Museum and admire the impressive collection of timepieces.
  • End the day at The Museum of Modern and Contemporary Art and indulge in some contemporary art.

Day 3: Geneva Nature Day

  • Take the cable car to visit the Mont Saleve summit and take in the stunning panoramic view of the surrounding areas.
  • Hike through the Jura Mountains and take in the beautiful scenery before enjoying a picnic lunch in the park.
  • Stop by the Botanical Gardens and relax in their serene atmosphere before heading back to your hotel.

Day 4: Genevan Delights

  • Take a Geneva Chocolate Factory Tour and sample some of Switzerland’s famous sweets.
  • Go on a wine tasting tour in the Satigny region.
  • Visit Les Trois Verres for a mouth-watering dinner of traditional dishes.

Day 5: Culture Day

  • Start the day at the Geneva History Museum and take a trip through time and relive the city’s history.
  • Visit the St. Pierre Cathedral and climb the tower to enjoy the breathtaking view of the city.
  • Take a walk past the Art and History Museum or the Maison Tavel.
  • Finally, admire the contemporary installments at the Bains des Paquis community pool

Day 6: Geneva Curiosity Tours

  • Take the underground tour within the CERN particle physics laboratory and learn how accelerators work.
  • Visit the Palace of Nations and take a guided tour of the United Nations’ international offices.
  • End the day with a free bike tour of the city, provided by Geneve Roule.

Day 7: Geneva Relaxation-Day

  • Visit the Bain Bleu Hammam & Spa, the city’s most luxurious spa for some relaxation and rejuvenation.
  • Have a relaxing stroll along the lakefront, before having lunch in the Old Town. Head to the Parc des Bastions, take a whipped cream hot chocolate at La Clemence before strolling through the flea market.

Local Culture and Customs of Geneva

Geneva is known as the “Peace Capital of the World” and is home to the United Nations’ European Headquarters. It’s a city with rich history, culture, and diversity. The locals are generally polite, the language spoken is French (and English in tourist areas) and the currency is the Swiss Franc. Switzerland, in general, has the highest standard of living globally, and the Swiss are very proud of their local products, traditions, and heritage.

Best Time to Visit Geneva

Geneva is a year-round destination, but the best time to visit is from late spring to early fall (May to September) when the weather is mild, and outdoor activities and tours are available. Prices tend to increase during the summer due to increased tourism, but it’s also the busiest time for festivals and events. Winter (December to February) in Geneva can be quite cold, but the city is beautifully decorated for the holidays.

Do’s and Don’ts of Geneva

Do:

  • Greet people in French, either with “Bonjour” or “Bonsoir” (Good morning, Good evening) as it’s always courteous.
  • Look both ways before crossing streets, as drivers won’t stop unless you’re at a pedestrian crossing.
  • Be respectful of people’s privacy - On public transportation, don’t strike a conversation with locals unless it’s necessary.

Don’t:

  • Assume that Swiss German speakers will understand you speaking Swiss French, it’s better to stick to English or French.
  • Smoke in public areas, as it’s banned from most outdoor locations.
  • Show up late, the Swiss are known for their punctuality, so it’s always better to be on time.

Safety and Health

Safety in Geneva

Geneva is a safe city with straightforward laws; however, like any city, crime can occur. The city centre is well lit and safe for walking during the day and night, but it’s better to avoid deserted areas during late-night walks and generally keep a close watch on your belongings.

Health in Geneva

Switzerland has an excellent healthcare system, and Geneva is no exception. The city has several hospitals, private clinics, and doctors available for emergencies, as well as pharmacies that are well stocked with over the counter medications.

How to Get Around in Geneva

Geneva is a walkable city, and public transportation is easily accessible and efficient. Here are some of the best ways to get around:

  • Public transport - Geneva’s public transportation, known as the TPG, includes buses, trams, and boats. A free transportation card is given when you check-in to your hotel or hostel.
  • Taxis - A taxi ride around the city should not cost above CHF 20.00.
  • Bike rental – you can use Genève Roule or grab a bike from one of the many cycle rental points parked around the city.
