Introduction to Lausanne

Lausanne is a stunning city located in the French-speaking area of Switzerland, in the canton of Vaud, on the banks of Lake Geneva. Known for being the Olympic capital, Lausanne has plenty to offer to its visitors such as beautiful natural surroundings, a bustling cultural scene, and a rich history. Whether you want to explore museums and galleries, hike in the mountains, eat delicious Swiss cuisine or simply enjoy the lakeside views, Lausanne has something for everyone.

List of Places to Visit in Lausanne

Lausanne is packed with sights and experiences that can’t be missed. Here’s our list of must-see places in Lausanne.

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Lausanne boasts many museums, galleries, and historical sites. Here are some of the most notable cultural attractions:

  • Olympic Museum: The Olympic Museum is one of the most visited tourist destinations in Switzerland, showcasing the history of the Olympic games through interactive exhibits and displays.
  • Lausanne Cathedral: The Lausanne Cathedral is a stunning Gothic cathedral dating back to the 13th century. Visitors can enjoy sweeping views of the city from the top of the tower.
  • Hermitage Foundation Museum: Nestled in a beautiful park on the shore of Lake Geneva, the Hermitage Foundation Museum features a collection of impressionist and post-impressionist artwork.
  • Chateau de Chillon: The Chateau de Chillon is a well-preserved medieval castle located on a small island in Lake Geneva. It is the most visited historical monument in Switzerland.

Natural Attractions of Lausanne: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Lausanne also offers a variety of outdoor experiences, including hiking and biking trails, picturesque parks, and stunning viewpoints. Here are some of the top natural attractions in Lausanne:

  • Flon district: The Flon district is a trendy area of Lausanne that was once an industrial zone. It now features a pedestrian street lined with restaurants, bars, and shops.
  • Lavaux Vineyard Terraces: The Lavaux Vineyard Terraces are a UNESCO World Heritage Site that offer stunning views of the Alps and Lake Geneva. Visitors can explore the vineyards on foot or by bike.
  • Parc de Milan: Located on the shore of Lake Geneva, Parc de Milan is a popular spot for picnics, jogging, and enjoying the lake views.
  • Sauvabelin Forest: The Sauvabelin Forest is a beautiful nature reserve located on a hill above Lausanne. Visitors can enjoy hiking trails, a treetop adventure park, and a stunning view of the city.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Lausanne

Lausanne offers a variety of hiking and biking trails that allow visitors to explore the city and its surroundings. Here are some of the best routes:

  • The Great Escape Trail: This 17 km trail takes hikers from the city center of Lausanne to the vineyards of Lavaux, providing stunning views of Lake Geneva along the way.
  • The Lausanne Loop: This 31 km route takes cyclists on a tour of Lausanne and the surrounding area, passing by urban areas, rural landscapes, and vineyards.
  • Le Moléson Trail: This challenging 40 km route takes hikers through the foothills of the Alps, offering stunning views of the mountains from the summit of Le Moléson.

Best Things to Do in Lausanne: Activities and Tours

Lausanne has plenty of activities and tours to offer visitors, including:

  • Wine Tasting: Lausanne is located in the heart of the Lavaux wine region, where visitors can enjoy wine tasting tours and visit local vineyards.
  • Lake Geneva Cruise: A cruise on Lake Geneva is a must-do when visiting Lausanne, offering stunning views of the Swiss and French Alps and the lake’s crystal-clear waters.
  • Lausanne Art Tour: Lausanne has a rich cultural scene, and visitors can take a guided tour of the city’s art galleries and museums, including the Olympic Museum, The Fine Arts Museum, and the Museum of Contemporary Design and Applied Arts.

Week-Long Itinerary for Lausanne

Here’s a suggested itinerary for a week-long visit to Lausanne:

  • Day 1: Visit the Olympic Museum and explore the city center of Lausanne.
  • Day 2: Hike the Great Escape Trail and have a picnic in the vineyards of Lavaux.
  • Day 3: Take a day trip to the Chateau de Chillon and the Montreux Riviera.
  • Day 4: Rent a bike and tour the city, stopping by local shops and markets.
  • Day 5: Take a Lake Geneva cruise and visit the charming lakeside towns of Vevey and Montreux.
  • Day 6: Explore the Lausanne Cathedral and enjoy the stunning views of the city from the tower.
  • Day 7: Relax in the Sauvabelin Forest and enjoy the natural beauty of Lausanne before departing.

Food and Drink Scene of Lausanne

Lausanne boasts a rich culinary scene, with local restaurants serving traditional Swiss cuisine, as well as international dishes. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages to experience when visiting Lausanne.

Must-Try Dishes

  • Cheese Fondue: Cheese fondue is a quintessential Swiss dish made from melted cheese and served with bread. It’s a must-try when visiting Lausanne.
  • Raclette: Raclette is another Swiss cheese dish made by melting cheese and scraping it onto boiled potatoes, vegetables, and meats.
  • Chocolate: Switzerland is famous for its chocolate, and Lausanne is home to some of the best chocolatiers in the country.

Local Beverages

  • Chasselas: Chasselas is the most well-known wine produced in the Lavaux wine region around Lausanne.
  • Absinthe: Absinthe is a traditional Swiss liqueur produced with wormwood and other herbs.

Where to Stay in Lausanne: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Lausanne offers a variety of lodging options, ranging from luxury hotels to budget-friendly hostels. Here are some top picks:

  • Beau-Rivage Palace: This luxurious hotel overlooks Lake Geneva and offers stunning views and amenities, including a spa and gourmet restaurants.
  • Lausanne Youth Hostel: This budget-friendly hostel is located in the city center and offers comfortable rooms and a communal kitchen.
  • Hotel Carlton Lausanne: This charming hotel offers affordable accommodations in a prime location in Lausanne.

What to Buy in Lausanne: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

When it comes to souvenir shopping, Lausanne offers a variety of local products and handmade crafts that make for perfect mementos. Here are some top picks:

  • Lavaux Wine: Bring home a bottle of the region’s famous Chasselas wine to enjoy back home.
  • Swiss Chocolate: Stock up on some of the best chocolate in the world at local chocolatiers such as Blondel and Durig.
  • Wooden Crafts: Visit local markets or shops to find beautiful handmade wooden crafts such as cheese boards or wine openers.

Local Culture and Customs of Lausanne

Switzerland has a rich cultural heritage, and Lausanne offers a unique blend of traditional Swiss customs and modern influences. Here are some customs to keep in mind when visiting Lausanne:

  • Tipping: While not mandatory, tipping is appreciated in Switzerland. A tip of 10% is typical for good service.
  • Language: French is the official language of Lausanne, but most locals also speak English.
  • Timeliness: Swiss people are known for their punctuality and expect visitors to respect arranged meeting times.

Best Time to Visit Lausanne

The best time to visit Lausanne is during the summer months from June to August when the weather is warm and outdoor activities are in full swing. The fall is a great time to visit for those who enjoy hiking and wine tasting, as the vineyards around Lausanne come alive with vibrant autumn colors.

Do’s and Don’ts of Lausanne

Do:

  • Try the local cuisine: Swiss dishes like cheese fondue and raclette are must-tries when visiting Lausanne.
  • Take a Lake Geneva cruise: The boats offer stunning views of the city and surroundings.
  • Respect the Swiss culture: Swiss culture values punctuality and a calm demeanor.

Don’t:

  • Be late: Swiss people place a high value on punctuality and being on time is expected.
  • Neglect to greet people: It is considered rude to not greet someone with a hello or goodbye in Switzerland.
  • Ignore proper attire: Swiss people take dressing appropriately seriously, so it’s important to dress accordingly.

Safety and Health

Safety in Lausanne

Lausanne is considered a very safe city, with a low crime rate. However, travelers should still exercise caution, particularly in tourist areas and during late night outings.

Health in Lausanne

Switzerland has a high standard of healthcare, and Lausanne is home to several excellent hospitals and clinics. Travelers should ensure they have adequate health insurance coverage before their trip.

Getting to Lausanne: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Lausanne

The nearest airport to Lausanne is Geneva International Airport, which is located approximately 45 minutes away by car or one hour by train. Other nearby airports include Bern and Zurich, both of which are approximately two hours away by train.

Bus Stations Near Lausanne

Lausanne has a central bus station, which offers connections to other cities in Switzerland and Europe.

Other Options

Lausanne can be reached by train, which is a highly efficient and convenient means of transportation in Switzerland. The city also has an extensive public transportation network, including buses, trams, and metro lines.

How to Get Around in Lausanne

Lausanne has an efficient public transportation system, including buses, trams, and a metro line that connects the city center and suburbs. Visitors can also rent bikes to explore the city on their own.

Where to Go Next from Lausanne: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Lausanne is ideally located for day trips to nearby attractions. Here are some recommended excursions:

  • Montreux: Montreux is a charming lakeside town located just a short train ride or boat trip from Lausanne. Visitors can explore the town’s picturesque old town, boardwalk, and the legendary Chateau de Chillon.
  • Gruyères: Gruyères is a picturesque medieval town famous for its cheese-making and beautiful castle.
  • Geneva: Geneva, home of the United Nations and the International Red Cross, is an international hub of diplomacy, culture, and finance.

Conclusion

Lausanne is a stunning destination that offers a wide range of experiences, from cultural attractions to outdoor adventures and delicious cuisine. Whether you’re looking to relax by the lake or tackle a challenging hiking trail, Lausanne has something for everyone. Plan your perfect trip with our comprehensive guide.
