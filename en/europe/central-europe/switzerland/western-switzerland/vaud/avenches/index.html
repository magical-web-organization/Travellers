Introduction to Avenches

Avenches is a small town located in the Vaud region of Western Switzerland. Known for its rich cultural heritage and natural beauty, Avenches is a popular destination for travelers looking to explore Switzerland’s history and outdoor beauty. In this guide, we will take an in-depth look at Avenches, including where to go, what to do, and what to see.

List of Places to Visit in Avenches

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Avenches has a rich history, and there are several museums and historical sites that reflect this heritage.

The Roman Theatre is definitely worth a visit, as it is the biggest in Switzerland, dating back to the first century. The Roman Museum is also a must-see for history buffs, offering exhibitions showcasing the region’s Roman past.

The Castle of Avenches is one of the highlights of this town which was a medieval fortress that dates back to the 11th century. It is currently a private property but some parts like the great hall, the chapel, and the twin towers with a panoramic view to the Alps are open to visitors.

Natural Attractions of Avenches: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

For nature enthusiasts, Avenches offers plenty of outdoor activities and natural attractions. Lake Murten is a serene and picturesque lake that offers beautiful views and is ideal for swimming and water sports. The Aventicum Museum, located on a hilltop, offers panoramic views of the surrounding countryside and is a perfect spot for a picnic.

The Nouvelle Promenade is a walking and cycling trail that extends from Murten to Avenches. There is also the Broye river that flows past the town which makes it the ideal destination for outdoor activities. You can go kayaking, paddle boating or you can just go for a walk on its shorelines.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Avenches

The Canton of Vaud has one of the best-developed networks of paths and in Avenches the choice is infinite. You can start by hiking the Jura Crest Trail (157 kilometers) that starts from Zurich, goes through Avenches, and finishes in Geneva. There is also a wide range of well-groomed biking trails starting from Avenches linked to the cycling routes that go through the canton of Fribourg and Bern.

Best Things to Do in Avenches: Activities and Tours

In addition to museums and outdoor activities, Avenches offers plenty of fun and exciting things to do. One of the top activities is horseback riding on the surrounding hills. You can choose between supervised courses, trip supplies, and “voyageurs à cheval” tours.

Another attraction is the Avenches Horse Racing Track. The most anticipated event is the Prix du Comité de la Société Avenchoise de Courses (CSAC), which is the primary race held at the track.

There are also many organized tours of the Roman ruins, the town’s historic center, and its surroundings.

Week-Long Itinerary for Avenches

A week in Avenches will give you enough time to see all of the town’s highlights, including its historic sites and natural beauty. To help plan your week-long visit to Avenches, here is a suggested itinerary:

  • Day 1: Visit the Roman theatre, the Roman museum, and the castle of Avenches.
  • Day 2: Stroll along the Broye river and take a kayaking or paddle boating tour on the river.
  • Day 3: Spend the day exploring Lake Murten, including swimming, boating, and relaxing on the shore.
  • Day 4: Hike or bike the trails surrounding Avenches, including the Jura Crest or the Nouvelle Promenade.
  • Day 5: Visit the Avenches Horse Racing Track and attend a horse racing event.
  • Day 6: Take an organized tour of the town’s historic center and surroundings, including the Aventicum Museum.
  • Day 7: Enjoy the local cuisine and bring back some of the souvenirs home.

Food and Drink Scene of Avenches

Avenches also has some memorable food and drink options that you can enjoy during your stay.

Must-Try Dishes

Swiss cuisine is a unique blend of German, French, and Italian influences. Some traditional dishes of this region include:

  • Cholera Pie: A stuffed pastry made with potatoes, leeks, cheese, and bacon.
  • Raclette: Melted Swiss cheese served with potatoes, pickles, and onions.
  • Zurchergeschnetzeltes: Sliced veal in a cream sauce served with rosti, which are crispy fried potatoes.

Local Beverages

Avenches is located within the wine-growing region of Vaud, and the local wines are definitely worth a taste. There are several vineyards in the area, and many offer wine tastings. Local lagers and ales are also available for a change.

Where to Stay in Avenches: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Avenches has several options for those looking for affordable accommodation ranging from hotels, hostels, and guesthouses. In addition, there are also several bed and breakfasts available in the area which offer a more personal touch to your stay.

What to Buy in Avenches: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Local souvenirs to look out for include candles from Le Candescent, the Perrochet dolls from the local toy museum, or Taste Vaud specialties.

Local Culture and Customs of Avenches

Switzerland has a rich and diverse culture, and Avenches is no exception. Some of the customs and traditions unique to this region include:

  • Fasnacht: A carnival celebrated the week before Lent begins.
  • Alphorn: A traditional Swiss musical instrument that is often played in the area.
  • Cheese making: Swiss cheese is world-renowned, and the cheese-making process is unique to this country.

Best Time to Visit Avenches

The best time to visit Avenches is during the summer months, from June to August, when the weather is mild and pleasant. This is also peak tourist season, so be sure to book your accommodations in advance. Spring (April-May) and autumn (September-October) offer milder weather, fewer crowds, and cheaper accommodation rates. Winters (November - March) are cold, with plenty of snow.

Do’s and Don’ts of Avenches

Do:

  • Respect the local culture and customs.
  • Follow the rules at historical sites and museums.
  • Try the local cuisine and beverages.
  • Bring comfortable walking shoes for hiking and biking.

Don’t:

  • Litter anywhere. The Swiss are renowned for their cleanliness.
  • Take pictures of locals, especially children, without permission.
  • Try to haggle for prices, as this is not a common practice in Switzerland.
  • Damage the historical sites in any way.

Safety and Health

Safety in Avenches

Avenches is a safe place to visit, with low crime rates. However, you should still take basic precautions, such as locking your accommodations while you are out and keeping an eye on your belongings in public places.

Health in Avenches

Switzerland has excellent healthcare, and there are several hospitals and clinics in the area. They have both public and private hospitals, but they are expensive. Travelers should have comprehensive travel health insurance to cover emergency medical costs.

Getting to Avenches: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Avenches

The nearest airport is Zurich International Airport, which is about a two-hour drive away. Another nearby airport is the EuroAirport Basel-Mulhouse-Freiburg which is also accessible by public transport.

Bus Stations Near Avenches

Avenches has a central bus station in town which is surrounded by several bus stops.

Other Options

If you prefer to arrive by train or car, there are several scenic routes available that connect with other destinations in Switzerland and all major highways in Europe.

How to Get Around in Avenches

The best way to get around in Avenches is by foot, bike or public transportation like buses and trains. However, if you want to explore the surrounding countryside, you may prefer to rent a car or use a hired driver.

Where to Go Next from Avenches: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

There are several nearby destinations that you can visit from Avenches, including:

  • Murten: A charming medieval town located on the shores of Lake Murten
  • Berne: The Capital of Switzerland with its interesting old town and amazing views

Conclusion

Avenches is a beautiful and charming town in Western Switzerland, with a rich history and natural beauty. Whether you are looking for adventure, culture, or relaxation, Avenches has something for everyone. We hope this guide helps you plan your visit and enjoy all that Avenches has to offer.
