Introduction to Neuchatel

Neuchatel is a stunning city in the western region of Switzerland. It is known for its fascinating history, picturesque architecture, and rich culture. Surrounded by the Jura mountains on one side and Lake Neuchatel on the other, the city is blessed with natural beauty.

In this comprehensive guide, we will take you through the best places to visit, things to do, where to stay, and what to eat in Neuchatel. So, let’s start exploring!

List of Places to Visit in Neuchatel

Neuchatel is dotted with numerous natural and cultural attractions. Here are some of the must-visit places in the city:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  1. Neuchatel Castle: This stunning castle sits majestically on a hilltop overlooking the city and offers visitors an impressive view of the town. It houses several historical exhibits and paintings.

  2. Laténium: The Laténium is a must-visit for history buffs. It is a museum of archeology showcasing artifacts and history of the region ranging from the times of the Roman Empire up until the Middle Ages.

  3. Chateau de Vaumarcus: A stunning castle featuring beautiful architecture surrounded by a peaceful forest, the Chateau de Vaumarcus is a perfect place to spend an afternoon.

Natural Attractions of Neuchatel: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  1. Creux du Van: Creux du Van is a natural amphitheater carved out of rock by the river. The cliff walls rise up to 500 feet, making for a spectacular sight.

  2. Les Brenets: The Les Brenets waterfall is a beautiful and serene place where you can swim, paddle, hike, or just relax by the lakeside.

  3. Gorges de l’Areuse: The Gorges de l’Areuse are a series of stunning natural gorges that wind through the mountainsides, making for a relaxing and adventurous walk.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Neuchatel

Neuchatel is a hiker’s paradise. There are several different hiking and biking routes in the city.

  1. The Walenpfad: This hiking trail is one of the most scenic and popular hikes in the region. It takes you through the beautiful countryside of Neuchatel.

  2. The Jura Crest Trail: This trail runs along the ridgeline of the Jura mountains, providing stunning views of the surrounding area.

  3. The Emme Valley Trail: The Emme Valley Trail is a beautiful and relaxing hike that takes you through a lovely valley and passes several scenic villages.

Best Things to Do in Neuchatel: Activities and Tours

  1. Taste the Local Wine: Neuchatel is known for its vineyards. So why not take a tour of the vineyards or taste the local wine?

  2. Visit the Markets: Neuchatel has a few markets that are a must-see. Check out the market in Place des Halles and the farmers’ market on Place du Seyon.

  3. Take a Boat Tour: A boat tour on Lake Neuchatel is one of the best ways to discover the beauty of the region. Marvel at the stunning views of the lake and surrounding mountains.

Week-Long Itinerary for Neuchatel

Here is a suggested itinerary for a week-long trip to Neuchatel:

Day Activity
Day 1 Explore the historic Old Town of Neuchatel and visit the castle
Day 2 Hike the Jura Crest Trail and take in the stunning views
Day 3 Visit the Laténium museum and learn about the region’s history
Day 4 Taste the local wine on a vineyard tour
Day 5 Stroll through the Gorges de l’Areuse and take a boat tour on the lake
Day 6 Take a bike ride through the Emme Valley trail and visit the Chateau de Vaumarcus
Day 7 Relax at Les Brenets waterfall and visit the local markets

Food and Drink Scene of Neuchatel

Switzerland is known for its delicious and high-quality food and beverages, and Neuchatel is no exception. Here are some of the must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  1. Rosti: A classic Swiss dish made of grated potatoes and served as a side dish or main course.

  2. Cheese Fondue: A dish of melted cheese served in a communal pot heated with a candle. Perfect for a group meal.

  3. Chocolate: Switzerland is the home of chocolates, so it’s a must-try while you are in Neuchatel.

Local Beverages

  1. Neuchatel Wine: The region is known for its vineyards and produces several delicious wines like Pinot Noir, Chardonnay, and Sauvignon Blanc.

  2. Absinthe: While not officially a food, Absinthe was first drank in Neuchatel and is a must-try for anyone wanting to sample the local drinks.

Where to Stay in Neuchatel: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Neuchatel has many excellent accommodation options that cater to all budgets. Here are some recommendations for hotels, hostels, and guesthouses in the city:

  1. Beau-Rivage Hotel: Located on the shores of Lake Neuchatel, this is a luxurious hotel offering stunning views and first-class service.

  2. Hotel des Arts: A charming hotel located in the heart of the old town with comfortable rooms and friendly service.

  3. Youth Hostel: The Youth Hostel located in Neuchatel offers budget-friendly accommodations and is perfect for backpackers and solo travelers.

What to Buy in Neuchatel: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  1. Watches: Switzerland is famous for watches, and Neuchatel is home to several watchmaking companies, including Tag Heuer and Omega.

  2. Cheese: Switzerland is famous for its cheese, and Neuchatel produces several types of cheese.

  3. Beer: Switzerland may be known for its wine, but the region produces several excellent beers like la neuchateloise and la meule.

Local Culture and Customs of Neuchatel

The people of Neuchatel are known for their friendliness and hospitality. They are very proud of their history and culture and are glad to share it with guests. Some customs to keep in mind include:

  1. Proper Greetings: When greeting someone, it is customary to shake hands or kiss on the cheeks.

  2. Punctuality: The Swiss are punctual, and it is essential to be on time for appointments and meetings.

  3. Respectful Behavior: Swiss culture values respect and politeness, so it is important to be courteous and respectful to locals.

Best Time to Visit Neuchatel

The best time to visit Neuchatel is during the summer months of June to September. The weather is warm, and outdoor activities are in full swing. That being said, Neuchatel has something to offer all year round, so a visit during the winter months can be just as enjoyable.

Do’s and Don’ts of Neuchatel

Here are some do’s and don’ts to keep in mind while in Neuchatel:

Do:

  1. Try the local food, wine, and beer.

  2. Respect the local culture and customs.

  3. Take advantage of the outdoor activities like hiking and biking.

Don’t:

  1. Arrive late for meetings or appointments.

  2. Be loud or disrespectful to locals.

  3. Forget to pack appropriate clothing for the weather and activities.

Safety and Health

Safety in Neuchatel

Switzerland is a very safe country, and Neuchatel is no exception. Violent crime is rare, and visitors can feel safe walking around the city at any time of day or night. However, visitors should still take the usual precautions, such as not leaving valuables unattended.

Health in Neuchatel

Neuchatel has excellent healthcare facilities, and visitors can rest assured that they will receive high-quality medical treatment if needed. Visitors should ensure they have adequate health insurance before traveling.

Getting to Neuchatel: Transportation Options and Logistics

Neuchatel is easily accessible by public transportation and several different routes:

Airports Near Neuchatel

  1. Bern-Belp Airport: This is the closest airport to Neuchatel, located approximately 45 minutes away by car.

  2. Geneva International Airport: This airport is located approximately 90 minutes away by car.

Bus Stations Near Neuchatel

  1. Neuchatel Bus Station: Located in the city center, this bus station is served by several local and regional bus lines.

  2. FlixBus Station: FlixBus, a popular European bus company, operates several routes to and from Neuchatel.

Other Options

  1. Train: There are several excellent train connections to Neuchatel. The TGV runs direct to Zurich, Geneva, Paris, and other major cities.

  2. Car: Neuchatel is easily accessible by car, and several motorways surround the city.

How to Get Around in Neuchatel

Neuchatel is a small city, and getting around is relatively easy. Public transportation is excellent, with several bus and train lines serving the city. Visitors can also explore Neuchatel with electric bikes or scooters available for rent.

Where to Go Next from Neuchatel: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Neuchatel’s central location makes it the perfect base for exploring other nearby attractions:

  1. La Chaux-de-Fonds: A UNESCO World Heritage Site home to several incredible Art Nouveau buildings.

  2. Bern: The capital of Switzerland and a city boasting stunning architecture, green parks, and a rich cultural heritage.

Conclusion

Neuchatel is a city that has something to offer everyone. From natural wonders to cultural treasures, visitors will never tire of exploring this charming Swiss town. We hope this comprehensive guide has inspired you to explore Neuchatel and its surroundings. Safe travels!
