Introduction to Uster

Located in the Zurich region of Switzerland, Uster is a charming town that offers a perfect blend of natural beauty and rich history. Situated on the pristine shores of Lake Greifensee, Uster has a population of around 36,000, making it the third-largest town in the canton of Zurich. Throughout the town, visitors will find plenty of activities and attractions to explore, from hiking and biking routes to museums and historical sites.

List of Places to Visit in Uster

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Uster Castle: Considered the most important landmark in Uster, Uster Castle is a former residence of the governor of Zurich. This iconic building is now home to the town’s museum, displaying an impressive collection of paintings, sculptures, and historical artifacts.
  • Reformed Church of Uster: Built in 1898, the Reformed Church has a beautiful Gothic architecture that attracts visitors from around the world. The stunning interior features stained-glass windows and ornate decorations.
  • Kapelle St. Andreas: Located in the center of Uster, this charming chapel dates back to the 14th century. Visitors can admire the beautiful frescoes and sculptures inside the chapel while learning about its rich history.

Natural Attractions of Uster: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Lake Greifensee: Surrounded by beautiful greenery and nestled in the heart of Uster, Lake Greifensee is a picturesque spot to explore. Visitors can take a peaceful stroll along the lakeshore, go fishing or boating.
  • Usterberg: Get ready to climb the hills of Usterberg, where you will be rewarded with stunning views of the town and Lake Greifensee. On a clear day, you can even see as far as the majestic Swiss Alps.
  • Uster Wald: Located on the eastern side of the town, the Uster Wald is a beautiful wooded area that is ideal for hiking and mountain biking. Visitors can explore its many trails and enjoy the scenic views.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Uster

Uster is surrounded by beautiful natural scenery, making it a popular destination for hiking and biking enthusiasts. Some of the most popular routes include:

  • Zugerberg to Rigi Panorama Trail: This route is ideal for experienced hikers and offers stunning views of the Swiss Alps.
  • Uster to Ortstockhaus Hike: A relatively easy route taking you through beautiful meadows and along picturesque lakeshores.
  • Uster Panoramaweg: One of the most popular hiking routes in Uster, it offers scenic views of Lake Greifensee and the surrounding mountains.

Best Things to Do in Uster: Activities and Tours

  • Uster Festival: Held annually in August, this is the biggest event in Uster’s cultural calendar. It features live music, street performances, food, and wine stalls, and carnival rides.
  • Craft Beer Tour: Uster is home to several local breweries that produce some of the best craft beers in Switzerland. Take a tour, and you can taste some of the best beers made right in town.
  • Graffiti Tour: Uster has an impressive collection of street art and murals that reflect the town’s vibrant culture. Take a guided tour to learn about the creative artists and the inspiration behind their artwork.

Week-Long Itinerary for Uster

Day 1: Arrive in Uster and check into your hotel. Explore the town’s central district and visit the Uster Castle.

Day 2: Take a hike around Lake Greifensee and then visit the Reformed Church of Uster.

Day 3: Visit the Kapelle St. Andreas, the oldest church in Uster. Then take a stroll through the picturesque Usterberg.

Day 4: Rent a bike and explore the Usterberg and the surrounding countryside. In the evening, try some of the local beer at one of the town’s breweries.

Day 5: Visit the Uster Wald, where you can go hiking or mountain biking. Afterwards, relax on the shores of Lake Greifensee.

Day 6: Take a guided tour of the town’s street art and murals.

Day 7: Depart Uster to your next destination.

Food and Drink Scene of Uster

Must-Try Dishes

  • Züri-Geschnetzeltes: A traditional Swiss dish made of small pieces of veal cooked with cream and mushrooms.
  • Rosti: A Swiss potato pancake that is crisp on the outside and soft on the inside.
  • Birchermüesli: A delicious Swiss breakfast dish made of oats, yogurt, and fruit.

Local Beverages

  • Uster Bräu Beer: Brewed in town, Uster Bräu is a popular local craft beer that is must-try for beer enthusiasts.
  • Swiss Wine: Switzerland may not be known for its wine, but it produces some fantastic white wines that are often paired with local seafood dishes.

Where to Stay in Uster: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Uster has several accommodation options, including hotels, hostels, and guesthouses that cater to different budgets. Some of the most popular options include:

  • Hotel Illuster: This four-star hotel is located in the heart of Uster and offers modern amenities and a spa.
  • Youth Hostel: Situated on the shores of Lake Greifensee, this hostel is a budget-friendly option for backpackers.
  • B&B Brigitte: Located on the outskirts of Uster, this charming guesthouse is surrounded by greenery and is perfect for those who want a peaceful stay.

What to Buy in Uster: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Swiss Chocolate: No trip to Switzerland is complete without a box of delicious Swiss chocolate.
  • Swiss Army Knife: A useful tool and a must-have souvenir from any trip to Switzerland.
  • Swiss Cheese: Switzerland is famous for its cheese, and Uster is no different, with several local cheese makers producing some of the tastiest cheese in the country.

Local Culture and Customs of Uster

Uster’s culture is heavily influenced by its proximity to Zurich, but it also has a distinct identity of its own. The people of Uster are friendly and welcoming, and they are proud of their rich history and natural surroundings. Visitors should respect the local customs and traditions, such as being punctual, dressing appropriately, and being courteous to others.

Best Time to Visit Uster

The best time to visit Uster is during the summer months (June-September) when the weather is warm, and the days are long. Uster enjoys a mild climate throughout the year with occasional snowfall in winter.

Do’s and Don’ts of Uster

Do:

  • Take a bike ride around Lake Greifensee
  • Try the local beers and cheese
  • Respect local customs and traditions

Don’t:

  • Forget to dress appropriately when visiting religious sites
  • Litter in public areas
  • Make noise late at night

Safety and Health

Safety in Uster

Uster is a safe and friendly town, but visitors should exercise caution and take the usual safety precautions. Keep your valuables safe and secure, and avoid going to isolated areas alone at night.

Health in Uster

Uster has a modern health care system that provides excellent medical care to locals and tourists alike. Visitors should ensure that they have adequate travel insurance before arriving in town.

Getting to Uster: Transportation Options and Logistics

Uster is easily accessible by air, rail, bus or car. Some of the most popular ways to travel to Uster include:

Airports Near Uster

  • Zurich Airport: Located around 30 minutes from Uster, the Zurich airport is the closest international airport to the town.

Bus Stations Near Uster

  • Uster Station: Uster has a train station that connects the town to other major cities in Switzerland.
  • Bahnhofplatz: Bahnhofplatz is a central bus station in downtown Uster, serving several routes.

Other Options

  • Car Rental: Visitors can rent a car from the Zurich airport or the city center and drive to Uster.

How to Get Around in Uster

Uster is a relatively small town that is easily navigable on foot or by bike. The town’s bus and tram system is also an excellent way to travel around Uster and the surrounding areas.

Where to Go Next from Uster: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Uster is well located for exploring some of Switzerland’s other famous destinations, such as Zurich and Lucerne, which are both easily accessible by train. Other popular excursions include visiting nearby towns such as Rapperswil and St. Gallen.

Conclusion

Uster may not be a well-known tourist destination, but it has plenty to offer visitors who are looking for a more off-the-beaten-track experience. From its stunning natural scenery to its rich history and culture, there is something for everyone in Uster. Whether you’re a hiker, a culture enthusiast, or a foodie, Uster is a town that is sure to captivate you.
