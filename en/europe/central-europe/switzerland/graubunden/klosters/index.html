Food and Drink Scene of Klosters

From traditional Swiss dishes to international cuisine, Klosters has something to satisfy every appetite. Some must-try dishes include:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Rösti: This Swiss dish is made of shredded potatoes and is often served with cheese or meat.
  • Zürcher Geschnetzeltes: This dish consists of sliced veal in a creamy mushroom sauce.
  • Bündner Nusstorte: This traditional Swiss dessert is a sweet pastry filled with caramelized nuts.

Local Beverages

  • Fendant: This white wine is made from Swiss-grown Chasselas grapes.
  • Rivella: This Swiss soft drink is made from milk whey and is a popular non-alcoholic beverage.

Where to Stay in Klosters: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

From luxury resorts to budget-friendly accommodations, Klosters has a variety of options for every type of traveler. Here are some recommendations:

  • The Chedi Andermatt: This luxurious hotel offers stunning views of the surrounding mountains and features a world-class spa.
  • Hotel Steinbock: This charming hotel is located in the heart of Klosters and offers comfortable rooms at an affordable price.
  • Youth Hostel Klosters: This budget-friendly hostel is perfect for backpackers and offers a cozy and welcoming vibe.

What to Buy in Klosters: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

There are plenty of unique souvenirs and local products to purchase while in Klosters. Some of our favorites include:

  • Bündnerfleisch: This air-dried meat is a staple of Swiss cuisine and makes for a delicious snack.
  • Swiss chocolate: Switzerland is known for its chocolate, so be sure to pick up some local favorites like Lindt or Toblerone.
  • Traditional Swiss clothing: From leather jackets to woolen hats, traditional Swiss clothing can be found in many shops around Klosters.

Local Culture and Customs of Klosters

Switzerland has a unique culture with many customs and traditions. Here are a few things to keep in mind while in Klosters:

  • Language: German is the official language of Klosters, but English is widely spoken.
  • Tipping: Tipping is not expected at restaurants or cafes in Switzerland, as service charges are usually included in the bill.
  • Public transportation: It is considered common courtesy to give up your seat to elderly or disabled passengers on public transportation.

Best Time to Visit Klosters

Klosters is a year-round destination, but the best time to visit depends on your interests. Here’s a breakdown:

  • Winter: The winter months (December to March) are ideal for skiing and winter sports.
  • Spring: Spring (April to May) is a great time to visit for hiking and outdoor activities.
  • Summer: Summer (June to August) is perfect for water sports and scenic drives.
  • Fall: Fall (September to November) is a great time to visit for the changing colors of the leaves.

Do’s and Don’ts of Klosters

Here are some do’s and don’ts to keep in mind during your visit to Klosters:

Do:

  • Do bring appropriate outdoor gear: Klosters is known for its outdoor activities, so be sure to bring the proper gear for hiking, skiing, and other activities.
  • Do try local dishes: Swiss cuisine is delicious, so be sure to indulge in some local favorites.
  • Do be respectful of local customs: Switzerland has a unique culture with many customs and traditions, so be mindful and respectful during your visit.

Don’t:

  • Don’t forget your sunscreen: The Swiss Alps may be chilly, but the sun can be strong, especially at high altitudes.
  • Don’t skip out on public transportation: Switzerland has an efficient public transportation system, so don’t hesitate to use it to explore the surrounding area.
  • Don’t litter: Switzerland takes environmental conservation seriously, so be sure to dispose of your trash properly.

Safety and Health

Safety in Klosters

  • Klosters is generally a safe and crime-free town, but it’s still important to exercise common sense and take basic safety precautions like you would in any other location.

Health in Klosters

Getting to Klosters: Transportation Options and Logistics

Klosters is easy to reach by public transportation or by car. Here’s what you need to know:

Airports Near Klosters

  • Zurich Airport: Located approximately two and a half hours away by train or car.
  • Altenrhein Airport: Located approximately one and a half hours away by car.

Bus Stations Near Klosters

  • Klosters Platz Bus Station: Located in the heart of town, this bus station offers regional and long-distance connections.

Other Transportation Options

  • Train: Swiss rail is a great way to travel throughout the country and to Klosters.
  • Car: Driving in Switzerland is easy and convenient, with well-maintained roads and plenty of scenic routes to take.

How to Get Around in Klosters

Klosters is a small town, so it’s easy to get around on foot. Alternatively, there are buses and trains that can take you to nearby towns and attractions.
