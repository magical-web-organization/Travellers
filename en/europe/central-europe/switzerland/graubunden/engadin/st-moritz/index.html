Introduction to St. Moritz

St. Moritz is a beautiful and luxurious Alpine resort town in Switzerland’s Graubünden region. It’s known for its glamourous skiing, world-class restaurants, high-end shopping, and breathtaking natural beauty. St. Moritz attracts high-end tourists and celebrities from all over the world who come to ski, relax, and enjoy the town’s sophistication.

With a history dating back to 1864, St. Moritz is one of the world’s original winter destinations. The town is located 1,800 meters above sea level and overlooks a beautiful alpine lake, Lake St. Moritz. St. Moritz is a bustling town that’s fully prepared to welcome tourists with all there is to offer – from iconic skiing opportunities, exciting outdoor activities, excellent food, and wine options, to a fantastic nightlife scene.

To help you plan your trip to St. Moritz, we’ve put together this comprehensive travel guide.

List of Places to Visit in St. Moritz

Here’s a list of places to include in your itinerary.

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Engadiner Museum: This museum features exhibits on the history and culture of the Engadin region, including traditional costumes, furniture, and handicrafts.
  • St. Moritz Church: The town’s oldest building and one of its most significant landmarks, dating back to the 16th century.
  • Segantini Museum: Located near St. Moritz, this museum features works by Giovanni Segantini, one of Switzerland’s most celebrated painters.

Natural Attractions of St. Moritz: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Corviglia Skiing Resort: Where you’ll find some of the world’s best skiing, combined with stunning views of the surrounding landscapes.
  • Lake St. Moritz: A beautiful alpine lake surrounded by breathtaking scenery that can be enjoyed by walking along the panoramic trails that run along the edges of the lake or taking a guided boat trip for a unique perspective.
  • Glacier Express: One of the world’s most iconic railways, the Glacier Express runs right through St. Moritz offering scenic views through the Swiss Alps that will undoubtedly blow your mind.

Hiking/Biking Routes of St. Moritz

St. Moritz isn’t just a winter destination; it’s equally gorgeous in every season of the year. With over 48 hiking routes, including the famous Via Engiadina trail that stretches over 150 kilometers, the town offers plenty of opportunities for hiking, biking, and other outdoor activities. Here are some of the notable ones:

  • Diavolezza-Lagalb hike: A four-hour hike through the stunning glaciers of Bernina that ends at the iconic mountain peak of Diavolezza.
  • The Suvretta Loop Trail: A beautiful six-kilometer trail that goes around the Suvretta hill and gives you breathtaking vistas of the surrounding landscape.
  • Albula Pass, Julier Pass & Flüela Pass: A popular cycling trail that features three of Switzerland’s most popular passes.

Best Things to Do in St. Moritz: Activities and Tours

Apart from outdoor activities and sightseeing, there’s a wide variety of things to do in St. Moritz for every type of traveler. From luxury shopping boutiques, spa treatments to street food experiences, here are some things to add to your to-do list:

  • Skiing: St. Moritz has a reputation as one of the world’s best skiing destinations, and it lives up to expectations.
  • St. Moritz Gourmet Festival: A yearly festival celebrating the region’s gastronomy and culture.
  • St. Moritz Nightlife: With a vibrant nightlife scene, there are plenty of nightclubs, pubs, and bars around St. Moritz to keep the night going.

Food and Drink Scene of St. Moritz

Switzerland is known for having some of the world’s best food, drinks, and chocolates, and St. Moritz is no exception.

Must-Try Dishes

  • Bündnerfleisch: A salty meat dish made from beef from the Bündner region.
  • Pizokel: A Grisons specialty made from freshly grated potatoes, Alpine cheese, and Ham.
  • Nusstorte: A delicious Swiss almond cake baked in St. Moritz by high-end bakeries.

Local Beverages

  • Bombardino: A warm and creamy schnapps drink that’s perfect for cold winter nights.
  • Chasellas: An exclusive wine from the vineyards located in the hills to the north-east of St. Moritz.

Where to Stay in St. Moritz: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

St. Moritz has a broad range of accommodations that cater to every budget and preference. Here are some of our recommendations:

  • Kulm Hotel St. Moritz: The most luxurious hotel in town that’s been around since 1856.
  • Hauser Hotel St. Moritz: A family-run four-star hotel located in the heart of the town, which boasts excellent food and comfortable rooms.
  • Youth Hostel St. Moritz: A budget option that offers shared dorms and private rooms with impressive views of the mountains.

What to Buy in St. Moritz: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

St. Moritz has a host of authentic souvenirs that visitors can bring back home to commemorate their trip. Here are some things you should consider bringing back home:

  • Swiss Army Knives: The iconic Victorinox brand Swiss army knives make for great gifts.
  • Engadine Crystal: Handmade traditional crystal glasses and vases are stunning souvenirs to bring home.
  • Duty-Free Shopping: St. Moritz is near the Italian border, and therefore, Duty-free goods are prevalent at cheaper prices.

Local Culture and Customs of St. Moritz

Switzerland has a unique culture that reflects its geography, location, and history. Below are some customs and traditions specific to St. Moritz:

  • Winter Spengata: A traditional carnival celebrated every year with concerts, processions, and lots of food and drinks.
  • Languages: St. Moritz has three official languages: German, Romansh, and Italian, which reflect its cultural and historical diversity.
  • Tourist Tax: Be aware of the tourist tax, which is compulsory in St. Moritz and is charged per person per night.

Best Time to Visit St. Moritz

The best time to visit St. Moritz depends on what you want to do during your trip. For skiing enthusiasts, December to February is the ideal time as there’s plenty of snow. However, if hiking and other outdoor activities are your top priorities, May to October is a better time to visit as the weather is perfect for all kinds of outdoor activities.

Do’s and Don’ts of St. Moritz

Do:

  • Bring comfortable, warm clothing, and sturdy hiking boots.
  • Purchase the hiking and biking maps in case you get lost.
  • Respect the locals and their customs.

Don’t:

  • Don’t litter; it’s considered rude and disrespectful to the town’s environment.
  • Avoid imposing your beliefs without understanding the local customs.
  • Don’t use flash photography in museums.

Safety and Health

St. Moritz is a safe alpine town that’s popular among tourists, but it’s always essential to take the standard precautions. Here are some things to consider when it comes to local safety and health:

Safety in St. Moritz

  • Be aware of ice and slippery walkways.
  • Wear sunscreen, even on cloudy days.
  • Always carry your identification documents for any unexpected check-in.

Health in St. Moritz

  • Drink bottled water even though the water is usually safe to drink in Switzerland.
  • Use insect repellent and medication to prevent tick bites.
  • Seek medical assistance in case of an emergency.

Getting to St. Moritz: Transportation Options and Logistics

St. Moritz is well-connected to Switzerland’s national transport network, which makes it easily accessible by train, bus or private cars.

Airports Near St. Moritz

The closest airports to St. Moritz are Zurich, Geneva, and Milan. All of these airports have direct train or bus services to St. Moritz.

Bus Stations Near St. Moritz

St. Moritz bus station is located near the train station and is well-connected to other parts of Switzerland.

Other Options

St. Moritz is a lovely and walkable town, but if you’re not into walking, there are plenty of options for transportation, including buses, taxis and car rental services.

How to Get Around in St. Moritz

St. Moritz is a compact town that’s easy to navigate on foot. But, if you’re looking to explore the surrounding areas or move from one point to another faster, here are your options:

  • Ski Lifts: In winter, take the ski lifts for great views of the alpine landscape.
  • Bicycles: Use rental bicycles to explore the nearby areas and take in the scenery.
  • Taxi Services: Plenty of modern taxis provide services around the town

Where to Go Next from St. Moritz: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Switzerland is a small country with a myriad of other beautiful destinations that are easily accessible from St. Moritz. You can make a day trip or head there directly from St. Moritz. Here are a few options:

  • Zermatt: A gorgeous skiing resort that’s home to one of the world’s most famous peaks, the Matterhorn.
  • Arosa: Another popular Swiss skiing resort that offers breathtaking views.
  • Bernina Express: A scenic railway line that connects St. Moritz with Tirano, a beautiful town in Italy.

Conclusion

St. Moritz may be an expensive town, but it’s well worth exploring because of the unique outdoor activities, natural beauty, world-famous skiing, luxury shopping, and great food. We hope this comprehensive travel guide will help you plan your trip and make the most out of your visit to St. Moritz.
