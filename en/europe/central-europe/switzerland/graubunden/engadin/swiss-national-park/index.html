Introduction to Swiss National Park

Located in the Engadin valley of Graubünden, Switzerland, Swiss National Park is a protected area of natural beauty covering an area of 174 square kilometers. Established in 1914, it was the first national park in the Alps and the only national park in Switzerland. The park is home to a diverse range of flora and fauna including chamois, ibexes, and marmots, making it a popular destination for hikers and nature lovers.

List of Places to Visit in Swiss National Park

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • National Park Centre Zernez: This museum showcases the natural history of Swiss National Park. It contains interactive exhibits, a cinema, and a library.

Natural Attractions of Swiss National Park: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Piz Languard: The most famous peak in Swiss National Park, Piz Languard is a popular hiking and skiing destination. On top of this peak, hikers can enjoy a stunning view of the park and the surrounding mountains.
  • Lake Sils: The largest lake in Swiss National Park, Lake Sils is popular among hikers and campers who seek out the park’s picturesque landscapes.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Swiss National Park

Swiss National Park offers many hiking and biking trails that cover the park’s diverse terrain. Here are some of the most popular routes:

  • The En forest clearing trail: This 2.5 km hike passes through an old-growth forest and is great for viewing wildlife.
  • The Fuorcla Champatsch trail: This 7 km intermediate route provides a scenic view of the towering peaks of Swiss National Park.

Best Things to Do in Swiss National Park: Activities and Tours

  • Wildlife Spotting tours: Join a tour led by a local guide to see the park’s wildlife up close.
  • Guided Hikes: Hike with a guide who can share the park’s natural and cultural history.

Week-Long Itinerary for Swiss National Park

Day 1: Visit the National Park Centre in Zernez in the morning. Follow the En forest clearing trail in the afternoon.

Day 2: Hike to Piz Languard. Have lunch at the summit and enjoy the panoramic views. Descend to the valley and spend the night in the village of Sent.

Day 3: Spend the day hiking the Val Sinestra trail. This 10-km trail winds through a narrow valley surrounded by majestic peaks.

Day 4: Take a wildlife spotting tour. Have lunch in the park’s only restaurant located in Chamanna Cluozza, and spend the night in the hostel or campsite next to the restaurant.

Day 5: Take a guided hike to Venice pass in the morning. In the afternoon visit the Swiss National Park Museum in Scuol.

Day 6: Visit Lake Sils, have a picnic, and rent a boat to explore the lake.

Day 7: Spend the day biking or hiking around the park’s various trails.

Food and Drink Scene of Swiss National Park

Must-Try Dishes

  • Pizokel: A dish made from pasta, potatoes, and cheese.
  • Gerstensuppe: A barley soup with vegetables.
  • Capuns: A mixture of meat, vegetables and Spätzle (dumplings) rolled in chard leaves.

Local Beverages

  • Bündner Bergtee: A herbal tea made from flowers and herbs found in the mountains of Graubünden.
  • Bündner Wein: A red wine produced in the region of Graubünden.

Where to Stay in Swiss National Park: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Chamanna Cluozza: This mountain hut located in the heart of Swiss National Park is the only place to stay within the park boundaries.
  • Zernez Youth Hostel: This hostel is located in the town of Zernez, where the park’s main entrance is located.
  • Hotel Parc Naziunal Il Fuorn: Located in the nearby village of Zernez, this upscale hotel enjoys beautiful views of Swiss National Park.

What to Buy in Swiss National Park: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Swiss cheese: Bring back some of the region’s famous Alpine cheese.
  • Engadine nut cake: A sweet dessert made with hazelnuts and caramel.
  • Bündnerfleisch: A type of dried meat, similar to beef jerky.

Local Culture and Customs of Swiss National Park

The people who live in the area surrounding Swiss National Park speak Romansh, a language endemic to Graubünden, and which is recognized as one of the national languages of Switzerland. Engelbert Joos, a local expert guide, offers tours that provide an in-depth look at the cultural traditions and history of the area.

Best Time to Visit Swiss National Park

The best time to visit Swiss National Park is between May and October, when the weather is mild and the trails are open. July and August are the busiest months in the park, so be sure to book accommodations well in advance if you plan to visit during this time.

Do’s and Don’ts of Swiss National Park

Do:

  • Stick to the marked trails.
  • Respect the park’s wildlife and ecosystem.
  • Take sturdy shoes and plenty of water with you.

Don’t:

  • Stray off the marked trails: Hikers are discouraged from leaving the trails to prevent damage to the park’s fragile ecosystem.
  • Approach wildlife: The park’s animals are protected, and any interference with them is not allowed.

Safety and Health

Safety in Swiss National Park

Swiss National Park is a safe destination for visitors. However, hikers should take precautions to ensure their safety, including:

  • Checking the weather forecast before setting out.
  • Taking plenty of water and snacks.
  • Knowing your physical limitations and taking breaks as needed.

Health in Swiss National Park

Swiss National Park is home to a variety of allergenic plants and vegetation. Visitors who suffer from allergies or asthma should take necessary precautions before visiting the park, including bringing medication and avoiding contact with plants that trigger allergies.

Getting to Swiss National Park: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Swiss National Park

The closest airports to Swiss National Park are located in Zurich, Geneva, and Milan. From there, visitors can use bus, train, or car to reach the park’s entrance.

Bus Stations Near Swiss National Park

Swiss National Park is accessible by bus from Zernez, a small town located near the park’s main entrance. Bus routes also connect Zernez to surrounding towns and cities in Switzerland and Italy.

Other Options

Swiss National Park can also be accessed by car. Roads to the park’s entrance are well-maintained.

How to Get Around in Swiss National Park

The best way to get around Swiss National Park is on foot. The park’s many hiking trails offer an up-close view of the park’s natural beauty.

Where to Go Next from Swiss National Park: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • St. Moritz: A world-renowned destination for winter sports. It offers many outdoor activities such as paragliding or skiing almora.
  • Scuol: A picturesque village located near Swiss National Park. It’s known for its traditional Engadine architecture and its spa center.

Conclusion

Swiss National Park is a natural wonder that offers visitors a chance to explore and appreciate the beauty of the Swiss Alps. Hiking, biking, and guided tours are just some of the many activities available to visitors. In addition to its natural wonders, Swiss National Park also gives visitors a unique glimpse into the region’s local culture and traditions.
