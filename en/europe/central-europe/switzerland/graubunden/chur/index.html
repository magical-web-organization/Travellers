Introduction to Chur

Chur is the capital city of Graubünden, located in Central Europe, Switzerland. Known for its charming Old Town, stunning natural landscapes, and rich cultural heritage, Chur is a must-visit destination for travelers looking for an authentic Swiss experience. Whether you are interested in history, outdoor adventures, or simply enjoying the local cuisine and hospitality, Chur has something for everyone.

List of Places to Visit in Chur

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

For history buffs, Chur offers a range of culturally significant attractions, including:

  • Bündner Kunstmuseum Chur – Known as one of the best art museums in Switzerland, Bündner Kunstmuseum Chur features a range of artwork from artists hailing from the Graubünden region.
  • Rhaetian Museum – The Rhaetian Museum explores the history and culture of the Graubünden region from the Stone Age to modern times.
  • Bishop’s Palace and Cathedral – Built in the 7th century, the Bishop’s Palace and Cathedral in Chur is a stunning example of Romanesque architecture in the region.
  • Obere Gasse and Untere Gasse – Obere Gasse and Untere Gasse are two historic streets in Chur that offer a glimpse into the city’s medieval past.

Natural Attractions of Chur: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

If you’re looking for some of the most beautiful natural scenery in Switzerland, Chur has a lot to offer, such as:

  • Parc Ela – Parc Ela is one of the largest natural parks in Switzerland, and it offers endless opportunities for outdoor adventures and scenic hikes.
  • Rhine Gorge (Rheinschlucht) – The Rhine Gorge is one of the most popular natural attractions in Chur, with stunning views of the rugged mountains and crystal-clear waters.
  • Brambrüesch Mountain – Brambrüesch Mountain is known for its world-class ski resorts during the winter months, and hiking trails with incredible views during the summer months.
  • Lake Palpuogna – Lake Palpuogna is a peaceful alpine lake surrounded by stunning mountain views, perfect for a serene picnic with family and friends.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Chur

If you enjoy hiking or biking, then you’ll love exploring Chur’s diverse trails. Some of the best routes include:

  • Albula Pass – Albula Pass is a popular mountain pass with both hiking and cycling trails, offering breathtaking views of the surrounding mountain range.
  • Viamala Gorge – A stunning geological formation that offers hiking trails with spectacular views and photo opportunities.
  • Chur-Rheinfelden Route – A popular scenic cycling route that stretches from Chur to Rheinfelden, featuring beautiful views and unforgettable experiences.

Best Things to Do in Chur: Activities and Tours

Chur has a wealth of activities and tours to offer visitors, including:

  • Walking Tours – A walking tour of Chur is the perfect way to learn about the city’s history, art, and culture, and explore its hidden gems.
  • Wine Tasting – Switzerland is known for its wine, and Chur’s vineyards produce some of the best. Enjoy a wine tasting to experience the local flavors.
  • Skiing and Snowboarding – Chur has world-class ski resorts with excellent facilities and stunning views that are perfect for skiing and snowboarding enthusiasts.
  • Cosmic Swim – Unique to Chur, enjoy a swim in a natural outdoor pool where you can swim with the stars under the night sky.

Week-Long Itinerary for Chur

To make the most of your trip to Chur, consider the following itinerary:

  • Day 1: Explore the Old Town and visit the Chur Cathedral and Bishop’s Palace
  • Day 2: Take a walking or bike tour of the Rhine Gorge
  • Day 3: Hike or ski on Brambrüesch Mountain
  • Day 4: Visit the Bündner Kunstmuseum Chur and Rhaetian Museum
  • Day 5: Wine-tasting tour of the vineyards around Chur
  • Day 6: Relax at Lake Palpuogna or enjoy Cosmic Swim
  • Day 7: Explore Parc Ela

Food and Drink Scene of Chur

Switzerland is known for its cheese, chocolate, and wine, and Chur is no exception. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Capuns – Made with Swiss chard, flour, and bacon, Capuns is a traditional Graubünden recipe that offers a savory taste of the region.
  • Maluns – Maluns is a potato recipe that’s typically served with apple sauce or meats, the perfect comfort food after a long day of exploring.
  • Alpenbitter – Alpenbitter is a traditional Swiss herb liqueur that’s commonly enjoyed as an aperitif after dinner.

Local Beverages

  • Graubünden Wine – Graubünden produces some of the best wines in Switzerland, including Pinot Noir and Chardonnay.
  • Bündner Whisky – Considered one of the best whiskies in Switzerland, Bündner Whisky offers a unique taste of the region.
  • Swiss Hot Chocolate – There’s nothing quite like a cup of Swiss hot chocolate, it’s the perfect way to warm yourself up on a cold day.

Where to Stay in Chur: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Chur offers a range of accommodations to fit any budget, including luxury hotels, hostels, and guesthouses. Some of the best options include:

  • Romantik Hotel Stern – A luxurious five-star hotel located in the heart of Chur’s Old Town.
  • Ibis Chur – A budget-friendly hotel that offers clean, comfortable rooms in a convenient location.
  • Chur Backpackers Hostel – A cozy and welcoming hostel that’s ideal for backpackers looking for affordable accommodations.

What to Buy in Chur: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

If you’re looking for unique souvenirs and local products to bring back home, consider the following:

  • Swiss Cheese – Cheeses from Graubünden are some of the most delicious in Switzerland, a perfect gift for cheese lovers.
  • Swiss Chocolate – No trip to Switzerland is complete without trying Swiss chocolate, a perfect gift for chocolate lovers.
  • Graubünden Wine – Switzerland is known for its wines, and Graubünden produces some of the best, a perfect gift for wine lovers.

Local Culture and Customs of Chur

Chur’s rich cultural heritage is visible in its architecture, traditions, and celebrations. Some customs to keep in mind include:

  • Punctuality – Punctuality is valued in Swiss culture, so be sure to arrive on time for appointments and meetings.
  • Respect for Personal Space – Swiss culture values personal space and privacy, so be sure to maintain an appropriate distance from others.
  • Language – The official languages of Chur are German, Romansh, and Italian, so try to learn a few basic phrases in each language to show respect for the local culture.

Best Time to Visit Chur

The best time to visit Chur is between May and October when the weather is warm and sunny, and outdoor activities are in full swing. However, Chur has something to offer throughout the year with Christmas markets during the winter months, ski resorts during the snow season, and museums and indoor activities that can be enjoyed throughout the year.

Do’s and Don’ts of Chur

Do:

  • Try the Local Cuisine – Chur offers a range of local dishes and beverages that are unique to the region.
  • Explore the Old Town – Chur’s Old Town is a must-visit destination for any traveler looking to experience the city’s rich history and culture.
  • Take Advantage of Outdoor Activities – Chur offers a wealth of outdoor activities, including hiking, skiing, and cycling routes.

Don’t:

  • Forget to Respect Local Customs – Be respectful of local customs and traditions, including the importance of punctuality and personal space.
  • Overestimate Your Hiking/Cycling Abilities – Chur’s outdoor activities offer incredible scenery but require physical effort, so make sure you are in good shape and know your ability level.

Safety and Health

Safety in Chur

Chur is generally a safe destination for travelers, but it’s always important to be aware of your surroundings and take the necessary precautions, such as locking your doors and securing your valuables. If you are planning to hike or bike in Chur, be sure to check the weather conditions and stay on designated trails.

Health in Chur

Switzerland offers excellent medical facilities, but it’s always a good idea to have travel insurance to cover any unexpected medical expenses. Be sure to pack any necessary medication or prescriptions you need for your trip.

Getting to Chur: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Chur

The closest airports to Chur are Zurich Airport (ZHR) and Geneva International Airport (GVA), which offer frequent flights to destinations around the world.

Bus Stations Near Chur

The main bus station in Chur is located in the city center and offers connections to other destinations throughout Switzerland.

Other Options

Chur is connected to the Swiss railway network, which offers connections to nearby cities and destinations.

How to Get Around in Chur

Chur is a small city that can easily be explored on foot or by bike. If you need to travel further afield, the city offers excellent public transportation options, including buses and trains.

Where to Go Next from Chur: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Chur is conveniently located near some of the best destinations in Switzerland, such as:

  • St. Moritz – A luxury ski resort that’s widely considered one of the most glamorous destinations in Switzerland.
  • Davos – Known for its world-class ski resorts, Davos is an outdoor lover’s paradise.
  • Lake Como, Italy – Just a short drive from Chur over the Swiss-Italian border, Lake Como is a stunning destination with beautiful scenery and charming towns.

Conclusion

Chur offers a unique blend of history, culture, and natural beauty that makes it a must-visit destination for any traveler to Switzerland. Whether you’re looking for outdoor adventures, exploring the local cuisine, or soaking up the rich culture and history of the region, Chur has something to offer everyone.
