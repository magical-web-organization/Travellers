Introduction to Lenzerheide

Nestled in the heart of Graubünden in central Switzerland, Lenzerheide offers visitors a charming blend of adventure, culture, and beauty. With its breathtaking mountain landscapes, world-class ski slopes, and picturesque Alpine villages, Lenzerheide is a perfect destination for nature lovers, history enthusiasts, and adventure seekers.

This ultimate travel guide to Lenzerheide will help you plan an unforgettable trip to this Swiss paradise. From the best places to visit and things to do, to where to eat and stay, we’ve got you covered.

List of Places to Visit in Lenzerheide

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

If you’re a history buff or are simply interested in learning about the local culture, Lenzerheide has plenty to offer. Some of the must-visit museums and historical places in Lenzerheide are:

  1. Rothorn Railway: This 7 km railroad connects Lenzerheide to Tiefencastel and offers unparalleled scenic views. The railway has been in operation since 1908 and is a testament to Swiss engineering.

  2. Parpaner Rothorn Mountain: One of the highest peaks in the region, it offers breathtaking views of the Swiss Alps. Take the cable car to the top where you can also visit the observatory.

  3. Valbella: Lenzerheide’s charming village is a great place to explore. With its narrow streets, old houses, and traditional architecture, it’s a slice of traditional Swiss life.

  4. Belfort Castle: A well-preserved historical fortress that dates back to the 13th century. It’s a great place to explore and learn about Swiss military history.

Natural Attractions of Lenzerheide: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Lenzerheide is a paradise for outdoor enthusiasts. Whether you’re interested in hiking, biking, skiing, or just soaking in the stunning alpine scenery, Lenzerheide has something for everyone.

  1. Parpaner Rothorn Bike Trail: This 18 km trail takes you through some of the most breathtaking natural landscapes in the region.

  2. Lake Heidsee: A gorgeous turquoise lake surrounded by the mountains, it’s a great place for swimming, boating, or just relaxing.

  3. Lenzerhorn Mountain: One of the highest peaks in the region, Lenzerhorn offers panoramic views of the Graubünden region.

  4. Piz Scalottas: Lenzerheide’s highest peak, it’s a challenging hike that is worth it for the stunning views of the Swiss Alps.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Lenzerheide

Lenzerheide is a hiker’s and biker’s paradise with dozens of trails and routes to choose from. Here are some of the best:

  1. St. Cassian - Tgantieni - Canols: A beautiful hike that takes you through traditional Swiss villages and offers stunning views of the surrounding Alps.

  2. The Lenzerheide Panorama Trail: This 12 km trail gives you panoramic views of the valley as you hike through the Alp Fops alpine pasture and on to Heidbüel.

  3. The Scalottas bike trail: With a length of 12 km and an elevation of 1000-meters, this trail is not for beginners.

Best Things to Do in Lenzerheide: Activities and Tours

There’s never a dull moment in Lenzerheide. Here are some of the best activities and tours to add to your itinerary in Lenzerheide:

  1. Skiing and snowboarding: With over 225 km of slopes, Lenzerheide is a world-class ski resort.

  2. Guided hikes and tours: Join a guided tour to explore the beautiful Swiss landscapes.

  3. Paragliding: Get a bird’s eye view of Lenzerheide with a thrilling paragliding experience.

  4. Wellness and relaxation: Lenzerheide has plenty of spas and wellness centers where you can unwind and relax.

Week-Long Itinerary for Lenzerheide

To make the most of your trip to Lenzerheide, here’s a suggested week-long itinerary:

Day 1: Explore the picturesque village of Valbella and visit the Belfort Castle.

Day 2: Spend the day skiing or snowboarding on the slopes of Lenzerheide.

Day 3: Take a guided hike to the Parpaner Rothorn Mountain and enjoy the panoramic views.

Day 4: Relax and unwind at one of Lenzerheide’s spas or wellness centers.

Day 5: Explore the Lenzerheide Panorama Trail, taking in the beautiful landscapes.

Day 6: Go paragliding or take a scenic helicopter tour to see Lenzerheide from up above.

Day 7: Take a day trip to nearby destinations like Chur or St. Moritz.

Food and Drink Scene of Lenzerheide

Lenzerheide boasts a vibrant food and drink scene that blends traditional Swiss cuisine with modern gastronomic delights. Here are some of the must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  1. Rösti: A classic Swiss dish made with grated potatoes, butter, and salt.

  2. Capuns: A traditional Swiss dish made with chard, flour, and bacon.

  3. Fondue: Melted cheese with bread is a Swiss delicacy that you can not miss in Lenzerheide.

  4. Älplermagronen: A macaroni dish with potatoes, onions, cream, and cheese.

Local Beverages

  1. Swiss wine: Switzerland produces some excellent wines, and Lenzerheide has a great selection to try.

  2. Swiss beer: The region is home to several breweries and boasts a great selection of Swiss beers.

  3. Kirsch: This cherry brandy is a popular local spirit that packs a punch.

  1. Summer (June-August): The summer season is perfect for hiking, biking, swimming, and outdoor activities.

Do’s and Don’ts of Lenzerheide

Getting to Lenzerheide: Transportation Options and Logistics

Lenzerheide is easily accessible by several transportation options. Here are some of the key things to know:

Airports Near Lenzerheide

The closest airport to Lenzerheide is Zurich International Airport, which is approximately 135 km away.

Bus Stations Near Lenzerheide

The Chur bus station is the closest to Lenzerheide, and there are regular bus services that run between the two towns.

Other Options

If you’re driving, Lenzerheide is easily accessible via the A13 highway, which connects with other major Swiss cities.

How to Get Around in Lenzerheide
