Introduction to Saint-Maurice

Located in Valais, Switzerland, Saint-Maurice is a picturesque town nestled in the heart of the Rhone Valley. With a rich cultural history dating back to the Roman era, Saint-Maurice boasts a variety of museums, historical landmarks, and natural attractions. Surrounded by the Swiss Alps and close to Lake Geneva, the town is a popular destination for outdoor enthusiasts of all levels.

Whether you’re interested in exploring the local cuisine, hiking and biking routes, or simply soaking up the serene, Alpine atmosphere, Saint-Maurice has plenty to offer.

List of Places to Visit in Saint-Maurice

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Abbaye de Saint-Maurice

Founded in the 6th century, Abbaye de Saint-Maurice is one of the oldest monasteries in Europe. The abbey’s museum has a vast collection of artifacts that take visitors through the abbey’s history. As an attraction, it is not only a historic site but also a religious pilgrimage destination.

Fort de Cindey

Nestled in the mountains, Fort de Cindey was an integral part of Switzerland’s defense system during World War II. Today, visitors can explore the underground tunnels and barracks and learn about the fort’s history.

Château de Saint-Maurice

The 13th-century Château de Saint-Maurice is home to a comprehensive military museum. The exhibits showcase the history of Swiss military weaponry, armor, and badges through the centuries.

Natural Attractions of Saint-Maurice: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

La Dents-du-Midi

La Dents-du-Midi is a spectacular mountain range that towers over Saint-Maurice. Adventurers have the opportunity to hike up to the summit and enjoy stunning views of the surrounding area.

Lac de Tanay

The tranquil Lac de Tanay is located in the heart of the mountains and is surrounded by hiking trails. The clear waters of the lake reflect the beauty of the mountains, and visitors can rent rowboats or enjoy a picnic on the shore.

Gorges du Trient

The Gorges du Trient is a geological wonder where the Trient River has carved its way through the mountains over the ages. Visitors can explore the gorge on a hike past waterfalls and caves.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Saint-Maurice

Saint-Maurice is known for its scenic hiking and biking trails. Some of the must-try routes include:

  • The Alpage de Noveli hike, which offers stunning views of the Rhone Valley.
  • The Chemin des Bisses trail, which includes a 16km hiking path that follows irrigation canals through the mountains.
  • The Le Bouveret to St. Gingolph bike trail, which skirts Lake Geneva and offers views of both Switzerland and France.

Best Things to Do in Saint-Maurice: Activities and Tours

Saint-Maurice has plenty of activities and tours to enjoy, including:

  • A guided tour of the Abbaye de Saint-Maurice, which provides an in-depth look at the abbey’s history and cultural significance.
  • A ride on the historic Bex Villars-Bretaye railway line, which offers breathtaking views of the Alps.
  • A wine-tasting tour of the nearby vineyards, where visitors can sample local varieties.

Week-Long Itinerary for Saint-Maurice

Day 1-2: Explore the historical sites including Abbaye de Saint-Maurice, Fort de Cindey, and Château de Saint-Maurice Day 3-4: Hike the Alpage de Noveli, Chemin des Bisses, and Gorges du Trient. Day 5-6: Take the Bex Villars-Bretaye railway line, walk Lake Geneva, and enjoy some of the local wine. Day 7: Relax and soak up the tranquil surroundings of Saint-Maurice.

Food and Drink Scene of Saint-Maurice

Must-Try Dishes

The local cuisine of Saint-Maurice includes hearty mountain dishes like Fondue, Raclette, and Rosti. Visitors shouldn’t miss tasting the traditional Swiss chocolate and cheese.

Local Beverages

Valais is the largest wine-producing canton in Switzerland. Saint-Maurice offers wine-tasting tours, and local breweries also serve beer.

Where to Stay in Saint-Maurice: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

There are several accommodation options available in Saint-Maurice. Some of the best accommodations include Hostellerie Du Pas De L’Ours, Hotel Eden Palace Au Lac, and Hotel Carlton.

What to Buy in Saint-Maurice: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Visitors can bring home some of Saint-Maurice’s local wine, chocolate, traditional swiss meals, or precious St. Maurice earrings as souvenirs.

Local Culture and Customs of Saint-Maurice

Valais, in general, is considered to be one of the most conservative Swiss cantons with Roman Catholic domination. Visitors should dress modestly and respect religious customs like dressing up to religious sites and removing hats.

Best Time to Visit Saint-Maurice

The best time to visit Saint-Maurice is spring (March-May) and autumn (September-November). It is less crowded, and accommodations are cheaper.

Do’s and Don’ts of Saint-Maurice

Do:

  • Respect religious customs and dress appropriately when visiting religious sites.
  • Try traditional Swiss dishes and beverages.
  • Explore the scenic hiking and biking trails.

Don’t:

  • Disrupt the peaceful atmosphere or natural surroundings with loud noise or littering.
  • Wear inappropriate clothing that may offend locals or religious customs.

Safety and Health

Safety in Saint-Maurice

Saint-Maurice has a relatively low crime rate. It’s important to take precautions to prevent theft, such as keeping valuables in a locked room or safe while out.

Health in Saint-Maurice

Switzerland is known to have a high standard of healthcare. Saint-Maurice has several medical facilities, and visitors should be covered by comprehensive travel insurance to avoid any healthcare emergencies.

Getting to Saint-Maurice: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Saint-Maurice

Geneva International Airport is the closest airport to Saint-Maurice, located approximately 100km away. From the airport, visitors can take a train or rent a car to reach Saint-Maurice.

Bus Stations Near Saint-Maurice

There are several bus stations around Saint-Maurice that connect to the surrounding cities, making it convenient for travelers to get around.

Other Options

Travelers can arrive in Saint-Maurice via the Bex-Villars-Bretaye railway line. It offers a picturesque view of the region and makes for a memorable journey.

How to Get Around in Saint-Maurice

Saint-Maurice is a small town, and many of its attractions are within walking distance. Visitors can also rent a bike or take the local bus to get around.

Where to Go Next from Saint-Maurice: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Montreux: A beautiful lakeside resort town which hosts the famous Montreux Jazz Festival.

  • Chamonix: A picturesque town in France known for snow sports.

  • Zermatt: A popular ski resort town located in the heart of the Swiss Alps.

Conclusion

Saint-Maurice is a charming town with a rich cultural history and stunning natural beauty. Visitors can enjoy the local cuisine, explore the historical landmarks, and embark on outdoor adventures in the Swiss Alps. Whether you’re interested in hiking, biking, or simply enjoying the serene atmosphere, Saint-Maurice is a must-visit destination in Switzerland.
