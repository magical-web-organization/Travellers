Introduction to Zermatt

Zermatt is a picturesque mountain village in Valais, Switzerland, nestled at the foot of the iconic Matterhorn mountain. Known for its skiing and hiking opportunities, Zermatt attracts travelers year-round with its stunning scenery and warm hospitality. In this guide, we’ll share everything you need to know to plan your trip to Zermatt, from the best things to do and see to where to stay and eat.

List of Places to Visit in Zermatt

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Zermatt has a rich history and culture that can be explored through its museums and historical sites. Some must-visit attractions include:

  • Matterhorn Museum: Learn about the history and culture of Zermatt and the impressive feats of mountaineering that have taken place in the region.
  • St. Mauritius Church: Admire the beautiful frescoes and altarpieces in this historic church, which has been standing for over five centuries.
  • Zermatlantis: This museum houses a collection of precious objects and photographs that tell the story of Zermatt’s evolution from a remote mountain village to a world-famous resort town.

Natural Attractions of Zermatt: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Zermatt’s natural beauty is undeniable, and there are plenty of ways to explore it. From scenic train rides to thrilling outdoor adventures, here are some of the best natural attractions to visit:

  • Gornergrat: Take the cogwheel train to this summit for panoramic views of the Matterhorn, glacier fields, and surrounding peaks.
  • Matterhorn Glacier Paradise: This high-altitude vantage point boasts year-round snow and ice, as well as a suspended glacier palace and ice tunnel.
  • Schwarzsee: Take the cable car up to this alpine lake for breathtaking views of the Matterhorn and an easy lakeside stroll.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Zermatt

Zermatt is a popular destination for hiking and biking enthusiasts, with over 400 km of trails to explore. Whether you’re an experienced mountaineer or a novice hiker, there are plenty of routes for every skill level. Here are some of the best hiking and biking routes of Zermatt:

  • The Matterhorn Glacier Trail: This relatively easy hike takes you through stunning glacier landscapes in a valley surrounded by towering peaks.
  • The Five Lakes Walk: Discover five mountain lakes on this moderate hike, each with its unique turquoise hue.
  • The Zermatt Trail: This bike-friendly trail takes you through forests, pastures, and traditional Swiss villages, with stunning views of the Matterhorn along the way.

Best Things to Do in Zermatt: Activities and Tours

In addition to hiking and biking, there are numerous activities and tours to keep you busy in Zermatt. Here are some of the best things to do:

  • Skiing and snowboarding: Zermatt is one of the top ski destinations in Europe. With over 360 km of pistes and trails, there’s something for every level of skier.
  • Paragliding: Take to the skies and get a bird’s eye view of the Matterhorn and surrounding mountains.
  • Glacier Express: This scenic train ride takes you through the picturesque Swiss Alps, offering awe-inspiring views along the way.

Week-Long Itinerary for Zermatt

If you’re planning a week-long trip to Zermatt, here’s a suggested itinerary to help you make the most of your time:

  • Day 1-2: Hike the Matterhorn Glacier Trail and ride the Gornergrat cogwheel train.
  • Day 3-4: Take a ski or snowboarding lesson and explore the Zermatt Trail on bike.
  • Day 5-6: Take a day trip to nearby Lake Geneva or Lucerne and visit some of Switzerland’s historic sites and museums.
  • Day 7: Relax in Zermatt, take a stroll in the village, and enjoy a traditional Swiss meal.

Food and Drink Scene of Zermatt

Must-Try Dishes

Swiss cuisine is known for its hearty, comforting dishes, and Zermatt offers some of the best examples of this cuisine. Some must-try dishes include:

  • Cheese Fondue: Melted cheese served with bread and boiled potatoes, the ultimate Swiss comfort food.
  • Rösti: A fried potato dish that’s crispy on the outside and tender on the inside, often served with bacon or cheese.
  • Älplermagronen: Also known as Alpine macaroni, this pasta dish is made with potatoes, onions, cheese, and cream.

Local Beverages

Switzerland is also known for its wine and beer, with several local options to try in Zermatt:

  • Valais Wine: The Valais region is known for its delicious wines, made from the many grape varieties grown in the area.
  • Matterhorn Beer: This local brew is made with pure glacier water and natural ingredients, and is a great way to refresh after a day of hiking or skiing.

Where to Stay in Zermatt: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

There are plenty of accommodation options to choose from in Zermatt, whether you’re looking for luxury hotels or budget-friendly hostels. Here are some options to consider:

  • Riffelalp Resort: This five-star hotel offers breathtaking views of the Matterhorn and plenty of luxury amenities.
  • Youth Hostel Zermatt: This budget-friendly accommodation is perfect for backpackers, with shared and private rooms available.
  • Hotel Welschen: This family-run hotel offers cozy rooms and a convenient location within walking distance of the village center.

What to Buy in Zermatt: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Zermatt offers plenty of unique souvenirs and local products for you to bring home as a reminder of your trip. Some items to look out for include:

  • Swiss Chocolate: Switzerland is famous for its chocolate, and Zermatt offers plenty of opportunities to indulge in this sweet treat.
  • Matterhorn Whiskey: This local whiskey is distilled with glacier water and aged for four years, resulting in a smooth and complex flavor.
  • Zermatt Cowbells: These traditional cowbells are a reminder of the region’s agricultural heritage and make for a charming home decoration.

Local Culture and Customs of Zermatt

Switzerland is known for its strong traditions and customs, and Zermatt is no exception. Here are some things to keep in mind during your visit:

  • Respect Quiet Hours: In Switzerland, there are strict laws about noise disturbances during certain hours of the day. Be mindful of these and keep noise levels low during quiet hours.
  • Be Polite and Courteous: The Swiss value manners and courtesy, so be sure to greet people with a friendly “Bonjour” or “Grüezi” and say “Merci” or “Danke schön” when appropriate.
  • Tipping is Optional: While it’s common to tip in restaurants and cafes, it’s not expected. If you do choose to tip, rounding up to the nearest Franc is appreciated.

Best Time to Visit Zermatt

The best time to visit Zermatt depends on your interests and preferences. Here are some things to keep in mind when planning your trip:

  • Ski season: The ski season in Zermatt typically runs from November to April, with peak season being in December and January.
  • Hiking season: If you want to explore Zermatt’s hiking trails, the best time to visit is from June to September.
  • Low season: If you’re looking for a quieter time to visit, consider traveling in May or October when the crowds are smaller and accommodations are cheaper.

Do’s and Don’ts of Zermatt

Do:

  • Do enjoy the stunning mountain scenery and soak up the alpine atmosphere.
  • Do try traditional Swiss foods and beverages like cheese fondue, Rösti, and Valais wine.
  • Do take part in outdoor activities like hiking, skiing, and paragliding.

Don’t:

  • Don’t litter or leave trash behind on hiking trails.
  • Don’t take photos of locals without their consent.
  • Don’t compromise your safety by attempting to climb mountains without a guide or proper equipment.

Safety and Health

Safety in Zermatt

Zermatt is a safe destination with low crime rates. However, it’s important to keep safety in mind when engaging in outdoor activities like skiing and hiking. Here are some safety tips to keep in mind:

  • Stay on designated trails and slopes and adhere to posted safety signage.
  • Dress appropriately for the weather and wear proper safety gear like helmets and goggles when skiing or snowboarding.
  • If you’re planning a backcountry adventure, hire an experienced guide to ensure your safety.

Health in Zermatt

Switzerland has a high standard of health care, and there is a hospital in Zermatt in case of medical emergencies. However, it’s crucial to take preventative measures to stay healthy during your trip:

  • Stay hydrated and bring plenty of water on hikes and outdoor activities.
  • Wear sunscreen and protective clothing to avoid sunburn and heatstroke.
  • If you have any pre-existing medical conditions, take appropriate precautions and consult with your doctor before traveling.

Getting to Zermatt: Transportation Options and Logistics

Zermatt is a remote mountain village, but there are plenty of transportation options available to get you there.

Airports Near Zermatt

The closest airports to Zermatt are Geneva International Airport (GVA) and Zurich Airport (ZRH), both of which offer direct trains to Zermatt.

Bus Stations Near Zermatt

If you’re traveling by bus, there are regular services from nearby cities like Visp and Täsch.

Other Options

If you’re feeling adventurous, you can also hike or bike to Zermatt! However, keep in mind that these options require extensive planning and preparation.

How to Get Around in Zermatt

Zermatt is a pedestrian-only village, meaning that cars are not permitted within the village. Fortunately, there are plenty of other transportation options available:

  • Electric Taxis: Electric taxis are available to transport you and your luggage to your accommodation.
  • Electric Buses: Electric buses operate within the village limits and can take you to different parts of Zermatt.
  • Trains: Zermatt is well-connected to nearby towns and cities via train, making it easy to explore the region.

Where to Go Next from Zermatt: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

If you’re looking to continue your Swiss adventure, there are plenty of nearby destinations and excursions to consider. Here are some options:

  • Lake Geneva: Visit the stunning Lake Geneva region, known for its vineyards, watersports, and historic cities.
  • Interlaken: This picturesque Swiss town is a popular base for outdoor activities like hiking and paragliding.
  • Gornergrat: Extend your stay in Zermatt and take a trip on the Gornergrat cogwheel train to the top of the mountain for stunning views of the surrounding landscape.

Conclusion

Zermatt is a picture-perfect Swiss mountain village offering endless opportunities for outdoor adventures, cultural exploration, and relaxation. With this comprehensive guide, you’re ready to plan your next trip to Zermatt and discover all that this stunning destination has to offer. Bon voyage!
