Introduction to Riederalp-Bettmeralp

Nestled in the heart of Valais in Switzerland, Riederalp-Bettmeralp provides visitors with stunning panoramic views of the Swiss Alps, making it an ideal destination for nature fanatics.

This charming village is situated at an altitude of 2000 metres above sea level. Its car-free zones ensure a tranquil and peaceful environment for tourists. It is a great place to be with your family during the summer season, with a variety of outdoor activities like hiking, mountain biking, golfing and skiing during the winter season.

In this comprehensive guide to Riederalp-Bettmeralp, we will take you through the best places to visit, hiking routes, must-try dishes, where to stay, and transportation options.

List of Places to Visit in Riederalp-Bettmeralp

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • ProSuonen Irrigation Paths: Dating back to the 14th century, the irrigation channels in the Valais region are instrumental in farming. The irrigation paths around Riederalp-Bettmeralp are popular hiking destinations.

  • Villa Cassel: This villa is of historical importance and is well-preserved. The cultural history of the region is outlined through the building, paintings, and other artifacts.

  • Rope Park Bettmeralp: Rope Park Bettmeralp is a popular attraction located in the trees of Bettmeralp. It features several courses ranging in difficulty for adult and child visitors.

Natural Attractions of Riederalp-Bettmeralp: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Aletsch Glacier: At 23 km long, the Aletsch glacier is the longest glacier in the Alps; it is a UNESCO World Heritage site. Hiking around the glacier gives visitors a unique opportunity to connect with nature.

  • Bettmerhorn: From this mountain, one can see the Matterhorn, Mischabel and Fletschhorn peaks. It is a great spot for capturing those Instagram-worthy pictures.

  • Sphinx Observatory: The highest observatory in Europe, situated on the Jungfrau mountain top, and offers stunning views of the Swiss Alps. To get there, visitors can take a 30-minute train ride to Jungfraujoch.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Riederalp-Bettmeralp

  • Aletsch Panoramaweg: Known for its scenic view, it is one of the most popular hiking trails in Riederalp-Bettmeralp. The trail leading from Moosfluh to Bettmerhorn is graded easy to moderate and is suitable for walkers of all levels.

  • Bettmeralp to Riederalp: This trail takes a scenic route through the picturesque mountain environment of Riederalp-Bettmeralp and features stunning views of natural beauty.

  • Rhone trail: This biking trail course stretches from the Furka Pass to Lake Geneva, passing through Riederalp-Bettmeralp. It is suitable for bikers of all levels and goes through the Rhone valley and alpine passes.

Best Things to Do in Riederalp-Bettmeralp: Activities and Tours

  • Alpine golf course: Situated on a high plateau and nestled in the beautiful surroundings of the Swiss Alps, the Riederalp-Bettmeralp golf course offers golf enthusiasts a scenic setting in which to enjoy their favourite sport.

  • Skiing: Winter in Riederalp-Bettmeralp means skiing in the Aletsch Arena, which has 104 km of pistes. Skiing and snowboarding are the most popular activities, with courses suited for both beginners and experts.

  • Swiss Alps Experience: This unique experience is perfect for learning about the history and culture of Switzerland. Visitors can explore the traditional food and drink, folklore, music, and more.

Food and Drink Scene of Riederalp-Bettmeralp

Must-Try Dishes

  • Rosti: This traditional Swiss dish consists of grated potatoes, fried until crispy and golden-brown. It is best served with Swiss cheese and onion rings.

  • Raclette: Melting cheese in an electric grill, then scraping it onto a plate and served with boiled potatoes, gherkins, and pickled onions.

  • Bündnerfleisch: A dried meat originating from the Canton of Grisons. It is usually served with a side of Swiss cheese and bread.

Local Beverages

  • Glühwein: A must-try during winter in Riederalp-Bettmeralp. It is a hot, spiced wine typically served as a warming drink.

  • Swiss White Wine: Switzerland’s cool climate makes the perfect environment for growing white grapes. The wine is dry and aromatic, with unique flavours varying according to the region.

  • Swiss Hot Chocolate: An indulgent treat to try after a long day in the outdoors. Swiss chocolate is melting chocolate and is best served with cream.

Where to Stay in Riederalp-Bettmeralp: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Hotel Royal: This hotel offers breathtaking views of the Alps. The rooms are spacious and well-furnished, with a sauna and hot tub.

  • Bettmerhof: Located just a few minutes from the Bettmeralp cable car station, this hotel offers a rooftop terrace with a bar, sauna and steam bath, and a restaurant with regional and Swiss cuisine.

  • Riederfurka: A perfect place to stay for those who are looking for a peaceful and tranquil environment. It is an eco-friendly hotel that is situated in a magnificent alpine setting.

What to Buy in Riederalp-Bettmeralp: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Swiss Chocolate: Switzerland is known for its chocolate. Visitors can buy a variety of high-quality Swiss chocolate, perfect for sharing with loved ones back home.

  • Cheese: The Valais region of Switzerland has many unique cheeses. Visitors can get a variety of different kinds, including Walliser Raclette, Gomser Bergkäse, and Tomme Vaudoise.

  • Swiss Army Knife: One of Switzerland’s most famous inventions, it’s a perfect souvenir to take home.

Local Culture and Customs of Riederalp-Bettmeralp

Riederalp-Bettmeralp has a strong cultural heritage. Visitors can learn about local customs, such as the Alphorn, which is a musical instrument made of wood. The Swiss people have a tradition of yodelling, an artful vocal performance involving rapid changes between the falsetto and chest register.

Best Time to Visit Riederalp-Bettmeralp

The best time to visit Riederalp-Bettmeralp is between May to November for hiking and outdoor activities. Winter season between December to April is perfect for skiing lovers, as the area has more than 100km of pistes with different levels for beginners and specialists.

Safety and Health

Safety in Riederalp-Bettmeralp

Riederalp-Bettmeralp is a safe and peaceful village where crime is minimal. Still, visitors should be careful while going for hikes, especially in slippery areas or at high altitudes.

Health in Riederalp-Bettmeralp

Visitors are advised to avoid overexerting themselves in high altitudes, which may cause altitude sickness. The village also has a hospital and a pharmacy to cater to any emergency medical needs.

Getting to Riederalp-Bettmeralp: Transportation Options and Logistics

Riederalp-Bettmeralp is easily accessible through various modes of transport.

Airports Near Riederalp-Bettmeralp

Bern and Zurich airports have daily flights to different parts of Europe and the world. Both airports are connected through a train service convenient for visiting Riederalp-Bettmeralp.

Bus Stations Near Riederalp-Bettmeralp

Visitors can opt to take the bus, which runs directly to Riederalp-Bettmeralp from most of the nearby towns.

Other Options

  • Car: Visitors can rent a car from any of the near the airports and drive to Riederalp-Bettmeralp. Visitors should know that the village is car-free.

  • Train: Riederalp-Bettmeralp can be reached using the Swiss travel system. Visitors can take the train to Brig, followed by the cable car to Bettmeralp or Riederarp.

How to Get Around in Riederalp-Bettmeralp

As mentioned, the village is car-free. Visitors can walk around and explore the beauty of the village with cable cars, which are a convenient and efficient way of getting around.

Where to Go Next from Riederalp-Bettmeralp: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Zermatt: Home to the Matterhorn peak, which is one of Europe’s most famous landmarks.
  • Brig: This charming town has a crucial historical background and has many castles and museums.
  • Saas-Fee: Is an alpine village home to skiing, snowboarding, and is also known as the green pearl of the Swiss Alps.

Conclusion

Riederalp-Bettmeralp offers visitors a stunning Swiss alpine experience with a variety of activities. Whether exploring the historic villa Cassel or experiencing Swiss wine on the Alpine golf course, the village is a perfect destination for anyone looking to connect with nature, culture, and history.
