Introduction to Burgenland

Located in the eastern part of Austria, Burgenland is a beautiful region that shouldn’t be missed when visiting Central Europe. Known for its natural beauty, history, and culture, Burgenland offers a diverse range of activities and sights for all types of travelers.

List of Places to Visit in Burgenland

Whether you’re interested in history, nature, or culture, there are plenty of places to visit in Burgenland. Here are some of the must-see attractions:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Burg Forchtenstein: This castle dates back to the 14th century and is home to a museum showcasing the history of the region.
  • Schloss Esterházy: Located in the town of Eisenstadt, this palace served as the home of the Esterházy family, one of the most prominent noble families in Hungary and Austria.
  • Haydn-Haus: This museum in Eisenstadt is dedicated to the life and work of the famous composer, Joseph Haydn.
  • Fertőrákos Quarry: A UNESCO World Heritage site, this quarry was used for the construction of many buildings in Vienna and is now a museum.

Natural Attractions of Burgenland: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Neusiedler See: This large lake is perfect for swimming, windsurfing, and sailing. It’s also a great place to enjoy a picnic while taking in the picturesque views.
  • National Park Neusiedler See - Seewinkel: This protected area is a haven for birdwatchers and nature enthusiasts with its diverse flora and fauna.
  • Rosalia-Kogelberg Nature Reserve: With its hiking trails and stunning views over Burgenland, this nature reserve is a great place for outdoor activities.
  • Riegersburg Castle: This medieval fortress offers panoramic views of the surrounding countryside. It’s also home to a birds of prey exhibition and a maze for children.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Burgenland

Burgenland is an excellent destination for hiking and biking enthusiasts. Here are some of the best routes to explore:

  • Welterbesteig: This 240km-long trail offers breathtaking views of the Wachau Valley.
  • Leithagebirge Trail: This trail is ideal for a challenging hike or bike ride along the ridge of the Leitha Mountains.
  • Neusiedler See Radweg: This 124km-long cycle path takes you through charming towns and beautiful natural landscapes.

Best Things to Do in Burgenland: Activities and Tours

Beyond hiking and biking, there are plenty of other things to do in Burgenland, such as:

  • Wine Tasting Tours: Burgenland is famous for its wine, especially red wines like Zweigelt and Blaufränkisch. Take a tour of the vineyards and wineries for a taste of the local produce.
  • Horse Riding: Burgenland is home to several equestrian centers that offer horseback riding lessons and trail rides.
  • Spa and Wellness: Relax and rejuvenate in one of Burgenland’s many spas and wellness centers.
  • Castle Tours: Visit some of Burgenland’s most impressive castles and palaces on a guided tour.

Week-Long Itinerary for Burgenland

Here is a suggested itinerary for a week-long trip to Burgenland:

Day 1-2: Eisenstadt, including the Schloss Esterházy and Haydn-Haus museums.

Day 3: Visit Burg Forchtenstein and the Fertőrákos Quarry before heading to the town of Rust for the night.

Day 4: Explore the Neusiedler See and Seewinkel National Park.

Day 5: Cycle the Neusiedler See Radweg and sample some of the local wines in the towns along the way.

Day 6: Hike in the Rosalia-Kogelberg Nature Reserve before heading to Riegersburg Castle.

Day 7: Relax in one of Burgenland’s many spas and enjoy the tranquil countryside before departing.

Food and Drink Scene of Burgenland

No visit to Burgenland is complete without sampling the delicious local cuisine and wine. Here are some of the must-try dishes and beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Gulaschsuppe: A hearty beef stew served with bread or noodles.
  • Schnitzel: Thinly sliced veal or pork filet covered in breadcrumbs and fried.
  • Langos: A deep-fried dough topped with garlic, sour cream, and cheese.

Local Beverages

  • Blaufränkisch: A full-bodied red wine that Burgenland is famous for.
  • Zweigelt: Another popular red wine with a fruity flavor.
  • Uhudler: A unique wine made from red grape varieties that grew wild in the area.

Where to Stay in Burgenland: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Burgenland offers a range of accommodation options, including hotels, guesthouses, and hostels. Some of the best options include:

  • Hotel Burgenland: A modern hotel located in Eisenstadt, offering comfortable rooms and an on-site restaurant.
  • Gästehaus Steiner: A charming guesthouse in the heart of Rust, offering cozy rooms and a delicious breakfast.
  • Youth Hostel Rust: A budget-friendly option with shared and private rooms, located right by the Neusiedler See.

What to Buy in Burgenland: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

When it comes to souvenirs and local products, Burgenland has something for everyone. Some of the best things to buy include:

  • Wine: Take home a bottle or two of Blaufränkisch or Zweigelt to enjoy back home.
  • Paprika: This spicy seasoning is a staple in Hungarian and Austrian cuisine and can be found in most markets.
  • Lace: Burgenland is famous for its traditional handmade lace products, such as tablecloths and doilies.

Local Culture and Customs of Burgenland

Burgenland has a rich history and culture, with many unique customs and traditions. Here are a few things to keep in mind:

  • Burgenland has a strong Hungarian influence, with many locals speaking both Hungarian and German.
  • The region is also known for its folk music and dance, with several festivals throughout the year.
  • Wine is an essential part of Burgenland’s culture, with many vineyards and wineries open for tours and tastings.

Best Time to Visit Burgenland

The best time to visit Burgenland is between May and September when the weather is mild, and the countryside is in full bloom. The peak season is in July and August, so consider visiting in May or September to avoid the crowds.

Do’s and Don’ts of Burgenland

Do:

  • Try the local wines and cuisine.
  • Take a guided tour of the castles and historical sites.
  • Respect the local customs and traditions.

Don’t:

  • Be overly loud or boisterous in public places.
  • Go off-trail during hikes or bike rides.
  • Dress inappropriately when visiting religious sites.

Safety and Health

Safety in Burgenland

Burgenland is a safe destination for travelers, and violent crime against tourists is rare. However, be aware of pickpockets and petty theft in crowded tourist areas.

Health in Burgenland

No specific vaccinations are required to visit Burgenland, but it’s always a good idea to be up to date on routine vaccinations. Stay hydrated, use sunscreen, and be mindful of ticks when hiking or biking.

Getting to Burgenland: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Burgenland

Vienna International Airport is the closest major airport to Burgenland, located about 50km away.

Bus Stations Near Burgenland

There are several bus stations located throughout Burgenland, with connections to Vienna and other nearby cities.

Other Options

Renting a car is a great way to explore Burgenland’s countryside at your own pace.

How to Get Around in Burgenland

Burgenland is well-connected by public transportation, including buses and trains. Cycling is also a popular way to get around, with many bike rental companies available.

Where to Go Next from Burgenland: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Vienna: The Austrian capital is just a short train ride away and offers endless opportunities for sightseeing, shopping, and dining.
  • Budapest: The Hungarian capital is located just across the border and is easily reachable by train or car.
  • Bratislava: The Slovakian capital is also a short distance away and offers a mix of historical landmarks and modern attractions.

Conclusion

Burgenland is a hidden gem of Austria, with its natural beauty, unique culture, and impressive history. Whether you’re looking for an outdoor adventure or a cultural excursion, Burgenland has something for everyone. Plan your trip today and discover all that this charming region has to offer.
