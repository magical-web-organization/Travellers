Introduction to St. Johann im Pongau

Located in the heart of the Austrian Alps, St. Johann im Pongau is a picturesque town in the Salzburg state, surrounded by majestic mountains and stunning natural landscapes. This charming town is a popular destination for both winter and summer vacations, offering a range of outdoor activities, cultural attractions, and a delightful food and drink scene.

With this comprehensive travel guide, you can explore the best of St. Johann im Pongau, from the top places to visit, must-try dishes, and exciting activities to week-long itineraries and nearby destinations.

List of Places to Visit in St. Johann im Pongau

St. Johann im Pongau has plenty of attractions to keep you entertained throughout your trip, including culturally important museums and historical sites, as well as natural wonders and scenic outdoor adventures.

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

The town has a rich history and cultural heritage, as evident by the number of museums and historical places to visit:

  • Pongau Cathedral: This magnificent Baroque-style cathedral is the largest in the region and boasts exquisite architecture and artwork.
  • St. Johann Museum: Learn about the town’s history and culture through its collection of artifacts, photographs, and artworks.
  • Alte Burg (Old Castle): This medieval castle dates back to the 12th century and now houses the town’s administration offices.

Natural Attractions of St. Johann im Pongau: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Nature lovers will enjoy exploring the town’s breathtaking landscapes, hiking and biking trails, and skiing slopes:

  • Liechtensteinklamm: This spectacular gorge is the perfect spot for a refreshing walk or hike through its stunning rock formations and waterfalls.
  • Gernkogel Alpine Garden: A beautiful mountain garden filled with Alpine flowers, herbs, and rock formations.
  • Snow Space Salzburg: With over 120 km of ski slopes, this ski resort is perfect for winter sports enthusiasts and offers stunning views of the surrounding mountains.

Hiking/Biking Routes of St. Johann im Pongau

St. Johann im Pongau boasts a range of hiking and biking trails that cater to all levels of fitness and outdoor adventure enthusiasts. With more than 300 kilometres of groomed cycling routes and 300 km of hiking trails, there’s something for everyone.

Some of the top hiking and biking routes include:

  • Liechtensteinklamm Gorge Trail: This 4 km scenic trail takes you through the stunning gorge, offering picturesque views of waterfalls and rock formations.
  • Alpenblumenweg Trail: This 4 km hiking trail is perfect for nature lovers, and its marked path will lead you through a lush alpine meadow full of alpine flowers.
  • Tauernradweg: This long-distance bike trail follows the Salzach River and stretches over 270 km, through beautiful towns and picturesque scenery.

Best Things to Do in St. Johann im Pongau: Activities and Tours

Besides hiking and biking, there are many other activities and tours to enjoy in St. Johann im Pongau, ranging from adrenaline-pumping experiences to more relaxing and laid-back ones.

Some of the top things to do in St. Johann im Pongau include:

  • Zip Line: Get your adrenaline pumping with a zip line experience that takes you over the tree canopies and offers stunning views of the Austrian Alps.
  • Horseback Riding: Explore the town and its surroundings on horseback, experiencing the natural landscapes and beauty of the region.
  • Beer Tasting: Sample some of the region’s best beers and learn about the brewing process at the Salzburger Hofbräu brewery.

Week-Long Itinerary for St. Johann im Pongau

Here’s a sample week-long itinerary to help you make the most of your visit to St. Johann im Pongau:

  • Day 1: Visit Pongau Cathedral, St. Johann Museum, and go on the Liechtensteinklamm Gorge Trail.
  • Day 2: Explore the town on horseback, enjoy some beer tasting, and visit the Alte Burg (Old Castle).
  • Day 3-4: Go skiing or snowboarding at Snow Space Salzburg, and indulge in some après-ski activities at one of the mountain restaurants.
  • Day 5: Visit Gernkogel Alpine Garden and go on a hike in the nearby mountains.
  • Day 6: Take a day trip to the nearby cities of Salzburg or Zell am See.
  • Day 7: Rest and relax in one of the town’s spas, such as the Aqua Salza or Therme Amadé.

Food and Drink Scene of St. Johann im Pongau

St. Johann im Pongau has a rich culinary tradition, crafting some of the region’s most delicious dishes and beverages. From hearty meats to sweet treats, there’s something for everyone.

Must-Try Dishes

  • Wiener Schnitzel: A thin, breaded, and fried veal or pork dish served with a side salad, potatoes, or fries.
  • Kaiserschmarrn: A fluffy pancake sliced into pieces and served with a fruit compote or jam.
  • Salzburger Nockerl: A light and fluffy dessert soufflé made with eggs, sugar, and flour.

Local Beverages

  • Stiegl Beer: A local beer brewed in Salzburg, known for its refreshing taste and high quality.
  • Firn Schnapps: A traditional Schnapps made from the meltwater of alpine snow, with a unique and refreshing taste.

Where to Stay in St. Johann im Pongau: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

St. Johann im Pongau offers a range of accommodation options to suit all budgets and preferences, from cozy guesthouses to luxury hotels.

Some of the top places to stay in St. Johann im Pongau include:

  • Alpina Family, Spa & Sporthotel: A luxurious family-friendly hotel with a spa, pool, and fitness centre.
  • Hotel Alpendorf: A romantic hotel with a gorgeous mountain view, located at the foot of the ski slopes.
  • Pension Edelweiss: A homely and affordable guesthouse that offers a comfortable and cozy stay.

What to Buy in St. Johann im Pongau: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

St. Johann im Pongau has a range of unique souvenirs and local products to take home as a reminder of your trip:

  • Salzburg Mozartkugeln: A chocolate treat with a sweet marzipan center, known as the signature candy of Salzburg.
  • Dirndl Dress and Lederhosen: Traditional Austrian clothing made from high-quality materials and perfect as a gift or souvenir.
  • Handmade Candles: Made locally in the town, these candles come in unique designs and scents, such as cinnamon, lavender, and lemon.

Local Culture and Customs of St. Johann im Pongau

St. Johann im Pongau has a rich culture and customs that are worth experiencing and learning about during your visit.

Some of the unique traditions of St. Johann im Pongau include:

  • Krampus Run: A traditional parade that takes place every year, where people dress up as Krampus, the horned creature that accompanies Saint Nicholas, and carry torches through the town.
  • Christmas Markets: During the holiday season, the town transforms into a beautiful winter wonderland, with Christmas markets selling local handicrafts, sweet treats, and hot beverages.
  • Alpine Farming: The town has a strong farming culture, with many locals practicing traditional alpine farming methods, such as sheep herding and cheese making.

Best Time to Visit St. Johann im Pongau

St. Johann im Pongau is a year-round destination, with activities and attractions available in every season. The best time to visit, however, depends on your interests and preferences.

  • Winter: December to March is the best time to visit for winter sports, such as skiing and snowboarding.
  • Spring: April to June is great for hiking, biking, and exploring the town’s natural beauty.
  • Summer: July to August is peak tourist season in St. Johann im Pongau, with warm temperatures, outdoor concerts and festivals, and plenty of family-friendly activities.
  • Fall: September to November is perfect for experiencing the town’s autumn foliage and harvesting season.

Do’s and Don’ts of St. Johann im Pongau

To make the most of your trip to St. Johann im Pongau, keep these do’s and don’ts in mind:

Do:

  • Try local dishes and beverages, such as Wiener Schnitzel, Kaiserschmarrn, Stiegl Beer, and Firn Schnapps.
  • Explore the town’s natural beauty and landscapes, by going on hikes, bike rides, and horseback riding.
  • Respect local customs and traditions, especially during the Krampus Run and Christmas Markets.

Don’t:

  • Litter or leave trash behind in natural areas. Always take your trash with you.
  • Ignore safety warnings and guidelines while participating in outdoor activities.
  • Take photographs of people without their permission.

Safety and Health

St. Johann im Pongau is a safe and healthy place to visit, with quality medical facilities and emergency services available in case of an accident or injury.

Safety in St. Johann im Pongau

  • Always follow safety instructions and guidelines when participating in outdoor activities, especially skiing, hiking, and biking.
  • Be cautious while walking or driving on snow and ice, especially during the winter.

Health in St. Johann im Pongau

  • Be sure to have travel insurance before your trip to cover any medical emergencies.
  • Protect yourself from tick-borne diseases, such as Lyme disease, by wearing protective clothing and using insect repellents.

Getting to St. Johann im Pongau: Transportation Options and Logistics

St. Johann im Pongau is easily accessible by various transport options, including:

  • Airports Near St. Johann im Pongau: The nearest airports are Salzburg Airport, approximately 60 km away, and Munich International Airport, approximately 200 km away.
  • Train: The town is well-connected by train, with hourly connections to Salzburg and other major cities.
  • Bus: There are plenty of bus connections to St. Johann im Pongau, with daily services to and from Salzburg, Munich, and Vienna.

How to Get Around in St. Johann im Pongau

St. Johann im Pongau is a small town that can easily be explored on foot, or by bike or car. Public transportation is also available, with buses, taxis, and trains running regularly.

Where to Go Next from St. Johann im Pongau: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

St. Johann im Pongau is ideally located for making day trips to other nearby destinations in Austria and Germany, including:

  • Salzburg: The birthplace of Mozart and a UNESCO World Heritage site, Salzburg is an enchanting city full of history and culture.
  • Zell am See: A charming town nestled in the Austrian Alps, offering a range of outdoor activities and stunning scenery.
  • Hallstatt: A picturesque town located on the banks of a lake, known for its unique salt mines and beautiful architecture.
