Introduction to Hohe Tauern National Park

Nestled in Salzburg state in Central Europe, Hohe Tauern National Park is the largest protected natural area in all of Austria. It spans over 1,800 square kilometers and features snow-capped peaks, alpine valleys, glaciers, and pristine lakes.

The park is a haven for nature lovers seeking an escape from city life. The majestic landscape of Hohe Tauern National Park offers endless opportunities for outdoor activities, awe-inspiring sights, and cultural immersion.

List of Places to Visit in Hohe Tauern National Park

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

The rich history of Hohe Tauern National Park is reflected in the many museums and historical sites throughout the park. These include:

  • Hohe Tauern National Park Centre: Located in Matrei, this center offers exhibits, tours, and educational programs that focus on the park’s wildlife, geology, and history.
  • Schloss Bruck: This castle in Lienz is now a museum featuring an impressive collection of art and cultural artifacts.
  • Krimml Waterfalls: The highest waterfall in Austria, and the fifth highest waterfall in the world, is located in the park.
  • The Großglockner High Alpine Road: This scenic mountain road takes you up to amazing vistas of the surrounding mountains.
  • St. Johann in Tirol: This picturesque alpine village has a long history and is known for its scenic hiking paths, and beautiful architecture.

Natural Attractions of Hohe Tauern National Park: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Hohe Tauern National Park is an outdoor lover’s paradise and is home to countless outdoor adventures and scenic views. Here are some of the best places to visit:

  • Grossglockner Mountain: This is the highest mountain peak in Austria and offers breathtaking views of the park and surrounding areas.
  • Grossglockner Glacier: Covering an area of over 7km², this glacier is a must-visit destination for anyone traveling to the park.
  • Krimmler Wasserfalle: These series of spectacular waterfalls offer hiking trails and amazing vistas as they cascade down the mountain.
  • Zell am See: This mountain town is surrounded by beautiful scenery, with opportunities for skiing on the glacier, and hiking up to Schmittenhohe.
  • Hohe Tauern National Park: As Austria’s largest national park, Hohe Tauern is home to a myriad of different hiking trails, bike paths, and nature walks.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Hohe Tauern National Park

With 350 km of hiking trails and 80 km of bike paths, Hohe Tauern National Park offers endless opportunities for outdoor adventures. Here are some of our favorite hiking and biking routes in the park:

  • The Adlerweg: This eagle trail covers 413 kilometers and takes you through some of the best alpine scenery in Austria.
  • The St. James Way: This ancient pilgrimage route runs through the park, offering a unique perspective on the park’s history and culture.
  • The Tauern Cycle Path: This 270-kilometer trail takes cyclists through beautiful mountain scenery, along the foot of Hohe Tauern.
  • The Hohe Tauern National Park Trail: This 150-kilometer trail offers an unforgettably immersive journey through the heart of the park.

Best Things to Do in Hohe Tauern National Park: Activities and Tours

Whether you are an adrenaline junkie or just seeking a leisurely day, Hohe Tauern National Park is a great destination for all. Here are some of the best activities and tours to enjoy in the park:

  • Rafting on the Salzach River: Enjoy a thrilling adventure on the Salzach River with scenic views of Hohe Tauern National Park.
  • Skiing and Snowboarding: Hohe Tauern is the perfect destination for winter sports enthusiasts, with plenty of ski resorts and snow-capped mountains.
  • Grossglockner High Alpine Road: Hit the scenic road and take in the stunning views from high above the park.
  • Wildlife Watching: With over 10,000 different animal species, there are plenty of opportunities to watch wildlife such as chamois, ibex, and eagles.
  • Guided Tours: Reach out to the park’s rangers to arrange for guided tours, custom tailored to your interest and fitness level.

Week-Long Itinerary for Hohe Tauern National Park

Here’s a week-long itinerary to help you explore and enjoy everything Hohe Tauern National Park has to offer:

  • Day 1: Get an overview of Hohe Tauern National Park at the park center in Matrei.
  • Day 2-3: Explore the valley of Rauris and visit the nearby towns of Bruck an der Glocknerstraße and Saalfelden.
  • Day 4-5: Embark on a scenic drive along the Grossglockner High Alpine Road and explore the surrounding areas.
  • Day 6: Take a leisurely day of wildlife watching in the park.
  • Day 7: Embark on a guided hike to one of the park’s breathtaking glaciers.

Food and Drink Scene of Hohe Tauern National Park

No trip to Austria would be complete without indulging in the delicious local cuisine and beverages. Here are some of our favorite must-try dishes and local beverages to sample:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Wiener Schnitzel: A classic Austrian dish consisting of breaded and fried veal or pork cutlet.
  • Kaiserschmarrn: A sweet and fluffy pancake, traditionally served with stewed fruits.
  • Sacher Torte: A famous chocolate cake which was invented by Austrian Chef Franz Sacher.
  • Spaetzle: A soft and chewy egg noodle, often served with bacon and cheese.
  • Goulash: A hearty meat stew made with beef, onions, and paprika.

Local Beverages

  • Austrian Beer: Austrian lagers are known for their light, easy-drinking style.
  • Wines: Austria’s cool climate makes it an ideal location for growing grapes that produce delicious, crisp white wines.
  • Schnapps: A strong fruit-infused spirit, often served after dinner as a digestif.

Where to Stay in Hohe Tauern National Park: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Hohe Tauern National Park offers a wide array of accommodations to suit every budget and travel style. Here are some places to consider staying:

  • Alpine-style Luxury: For a indulgent stay, consider checking in at Tauern Spa Kaprun. This posh hotel features an on-site spa, stylish rooms, and fine dining.
  • Budget-friendly: If you’re traveling on a budget, consider staying at a hostel like the Hohe Tauern Youth Hostel. This basic, no-frills accommodation is clean, comfortable, and budget-friendly.
  • Charming Guesthouses: If you’re after a laidback and homey experience, check out the Gasthof Friedburg, located in the quaint town of Westendorf.

What to Buy in Hohe Tauern National Park: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Here are some souvenirs and local products that you can bring back for your loved ones or to remember your time in Hohe Tauern National Park:

  • Chocolate: The Sacher Torte is a famous chocolate cake from Austria, and a great souvenir to bring back in the form of chocolate!
  • Trachten clothing: The traditional attire of Austria is called Trachten, and it can make for a unique and interesting souvenir.
  • Local honey: Hohe Tauern National Park is home to many bees, and the honey they produce is tantalizingly rich and flavorful.

Local Culture and Customs of Hohe Tauern National Park

Austria has a rich cultural heritage, and Hohe Tauern National Park offers a chance to explore some of it. Here are some of the most important customs and traditions in the Hohe Tauern region:

  • Folk Music and Dancing: Traditional Austrian folk music and dancing can be experienced throughout the region.
  • Local Festivals: Many towns and villages throughout the area celebrate local festivals, especially at peak tourism season in the summer months.
  • Crafts and Workshops: The park offers a range of craft workshops and experiences, from cheese making to woodcarving.

Best Time to Visit Hohe Tauern National Park

The best time to visit Hohe Tauern National Park depends on what you want to do:

  • Summer: Mid-June to late September is the busiest time of the year as the weather is perfect for outdoor activities such as hiking, biking, and swimming. The days are longer and warmer, making it easier to enjoy the spectacular scenery under sunny skies.
  • Winter: Mid-December to March is the best time to visit if you’re interested in winter sports such as skiing and snowboarding. The park’s mountains are blanketed in snow, making it a picturesque destination for any winter enthusiast.

Do’s and Don’ts of Hohe Tauern National Park

Do:

  • Respect Nature: It’s important to remember that Hohe Tauern National Park is a protected park. It’s essential to follow the rules and regulations put in place to preserve the park’s natural beauty.
  • Carry a Map: The park can be vast, so it’s essential to carry a map or guidebook to help you navigate the area.
  • Carry Water and Snacks: Make sure to stay hydrated and energized during your adventures by carrying water and snacks with you.

Don’t:

  • Feed the Wildlife: It’s essential not to feed the wild animals as they can lose their natural instincts and become dependent on human beings.
  • Leave Trails: It’s vital that you take care not to leave trails as it can damage the park’s natural ecosystem and hinder the wildlife living in the area.
  • Leave Trash: Make sure to dispose of any trash properly, so you don’t leave a negative impact on the environment.

Safety and Health

Safety in Hohe Tauern National Park

Hohe Tauern National Park is a safe place to visit as long as you take care and stay vigilant. Here are some safety tips to keep in mind:

  • Be Aware of Changes in Weather: The weather in the area can change quickly, so it’s essential to be prepared for all types of weather, even in the summertime.
  • Watch for Wildlife: It’s important to watch out for wildlife when hiking or walking around the area. Give the animals plenty of space and don’t disturb them.
  • Inform Someone of Your Plans: Whenever you go hiking or exploring, it’s important to let someone know where you’ll be and when you’re expected to return.

Health in Hohe Tauern National Park

It’s important to take care of yourself to enjoy your stay in Hohe Tauern National Park, and there are some essential things to keep in mind:

  • Stay Hydrated: Make sure to drink plenty of water, especially during hot weather or when you’re undertaking rigorous activities.
  • Wear Sun Protection: The sun in Austria can be harsh, so make sure to bring sunscreen, hats, and other protective gear if you’ll be out in the sun.
  • Watch for Altitude Sickness: If you’re going to be hiking in the mountains, make sure to watch for altitude sickness and take breaks as needed.

Getting to Hohe Tauern National Park: Transportation Options and Logistics

There are many transportation options available to get to Hohe Tauern National Park, depending on your budget and preferences. Here are your options:

  • Airports Near Hohe Tauern National Park: The closest airports are Salzburg, Innsbruck, and Klagenfurt airports, all of which have good transport links to the area.
  • Bus Stations Near Hohe Tauern National Park: Buses run from most major towns and cities in Austria to the park.
  • Other Options: Renting a car is also a great option if you’re looking for more flexibility to explore the area.

How to Get Around in Hohe Tauern National Park

There are different ways to travel around Hohe Tauern National Park, depending on your preference, fitness, and budget:

  • Car: Renting a car is a great option for families or groups of people who want more flexibility in their travel plans, especially if they want to visit multiple locations in the park.
  • Bike: Biking is an affordable and healthy way to enjoy a scenic ride around the park.
  • Public Transport: Public buses can take you to most destinations in the park, but check bus schedules at your convenience.

Where to Go Next from Hohe Tauern National Park: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

There are many destinations and excursions within easy reach of Hohe Tauern National Park. Here are some ideas to consider for your next trip:

  • Salzburg: The city of Salzburg is famous for its music, culture, and architecture, and it’s less than a two hours’ drive from the park.
  • Innsbruck: Innsbruck is another nearby city, which offers beautiful alpine scenery, museums, and architecture.
  • Zell am See: This nearby town is another tourist spot, famous for its clear lake, perfect for swimming and water sports.

Conclusion

Hohe Tauern National Park offers a unique experience, combining nature, culture, and adventure all in one thrilling package. With endless opportunities for outdoor activities, beautiful scenery, and cultural immersion, it’s no wonder that Hohe Tauern is one of Austria’s top destinations. Make the most out of your trip with this comprehensive guide, and get started on planning your unforgettable Austrian adventure!
