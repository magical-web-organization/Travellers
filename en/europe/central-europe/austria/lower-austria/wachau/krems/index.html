Introduction to Krems

Krems is a historic town in Lower Austria that is situated in the heart of the Wachau Valley, a picturesque UNESCO World Heritage Site. Known for its vineyards, stunning architecture, and rich culture, Krems is a popular destination for travelers looking to experience the beauty of Austria. From museums and historical sites to scenic nature trails and outdoor adventures, Krems offers something for everyone.

List of Places to Visit in Krems

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Krems is home to a variety of museums and historic sites that will take visitors on a journey through the town’s past. Some of the top cultural attractions include:

  • Krems Caricature Museum: This museum displays the works of Austrian caricaturist Manfred Deix and other notable artists.
  • Kunsthalle Krems: A contemporary art museum that showcases works from local and international artists.
  • Steiner Tor: One of Krems’ most famous landmarks, this town gate dates back to the 15th century.
  • Gozzoburg Castle: Built in the 13th century, Gozzoburg Castle now houses the town’s museum.
  • Karikaturmuseum: This museum features caricatures and cartoons from all over the world.
  • Forum Frohner: A contemporary art museum that displays the works of Austrian artist Adolf Frohner.

Natural Attractions of Krems: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Krems is surrounded by breathtaking natural beauty and offers a variety of outdoor activities for visitors. Some of the top natural attractions include:

  • Wachau Valley: A UNESCO World Heritage Site, the Wachau Valley boasts stunning vineyards, historic towns, and scenic river views.
  • Donausteig: A 450-kilometer-long hiking trail that offers incredible views of the Danube River and the surrounding countryside.
  • Jauerling Mountain: This mountain is a popular spot for hiking and skiing in the winter months.
  • Dürnstein Castle Ruins: A historic castle that offers stunning views of the Danube River.
  • Felix Austria Viewing Platform: This observation deck provides visitors with a panoramic view of Krems and the Wachau Valley.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Krems

Krems offers a variety of hiking and biking trails that showcase the natural beauty of the town and surrounding areas. Some of the most popular routes include:

  • The Wachau World Heritage Trail: This 180-kilometer trail takes hikers through the heart of the Wachau Valley and offers stunning views of the Danube River.
  • The Krems-Dürnstein Cycle Route: This 22-kilometer route takes cyclists through the heart of the Wachau Valley and offers stunning views of vineyards, castles, and the Danube River.
  • The Krems-Hollenburg Cycle Route: This 11-kilometer route takes cyclists through the rural countryside and offers stunning views of the Danube River and surrounding vineyards.
  • The Danube Bike Trail: This 1,200-kilometer-long route spans from Germany to Hungary and offers a variety of scenic stops, including Krems and other charming towns in Lower Austria.

Best Things to Do in Krems: Activities and Tours

Krems offers a variety of activities and tours that showcase the town’s unique culture and rich history. Some of the top things to do in Krems include:

  • Wine Tasting and Vineyard Tours: Krems is known for its award-winning wines, and visitors can explore the town’s vineyards and sample the local wines on a guided tour.
  • Danube River Cruise: A scenic cruise down the Danube River is a great way to experience the town’s stunning scenery and historic landmarks.
  • Explore the Old Town: The historic center of Krems is filled with charming shops, restaurants, and cafes. Visitors can explore the town’s medieval architecture and learn about its rich history.
  • Gottweig Abbey: A stunning Baroque monastery that offers guided tours and beautiful views of the surrounding countryside.
  • Schloss Grafenegg: A historic castle that now serves as a music festival venue, offering a variety of concerts and performances throughout the year.

Week-Long Itinerary for Krems

For visitors looking to spend a week in Krems, the following itinerary provides a comprehensive guide to the top attractions and activities:

  • Day 1: Arrival and check-in at a hotel in Krems. Explore the town’s historic center and enjoy a traditional Austrian dinner.
  • Day 2: Take a tour of the Caricature Museum in the morning. In the afternoon, explore the stunning vineyards and sample the local wines at a vineyard tour.
  • Day 3: Take a scenic bike ride down the Danube Bike Trail and visit the historic Dürnstein Castle Ruins.
  • Day 4: Visit the Kunsthalle Krems and explore the contemporary art exhibits. For lunch, enjoy a traditional Austrian meal at a local restaurant.
  • Day 5: Take a guided tour of the Gozzoburg Castle and learn about the town’s rich history. In the afternoon, visit the Felix Austria Viewing Platform for breathtaking views of Krems and the surrounding countryside.
  • Day 6: Spend the day skiing or snowboarding on Jauerling Mountain or take a winter hike through the Wachau Valley.
  • Day 7: Departure from Krems.

Food and Drink Scene of Krems

Krems is known for its rich culinary culture and award-winning wines. Visitors can enjoy a variety of traditional Austrian dishes and sample the local wines at the town’s many restaurants and cafes. Some of the must-try dishes include:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Wiener Schnitzel: A traditional Austrian dish made with breaded and fried veal or pork.
  • Sachertorte: A famous Viennese chocolate cake.
  • Tafelspitz: Boiled beef served with horseradish and apple sauce.
  • Austrian Knödel: A type of dumpling made with bread or potatoes.
  • Kaiserschmarrn: A type of sweet pancake served with fruit compote.

Local Beverages

  • Wine: Krems is known for its award-winning white wines, including Grüner Veltliner, Riesling, and Gelber Muskateller.
  • Schnapps: A type of fruit brandy made with local fruits, including apricots and plums.
  • Bier: Austrian beer is known for its crisp, refreshing taste, and there are many local breweries in and around Krems.

What to Buy in Krems: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Local Culture and Customs of Krems

Austria is known for its rich cultural heritage, and Krems is no exception. Visitors should familiarize themselves with the following customs and traditions:

  • Greeting: Austrians place great importance on greeting others, and it is customary to say “Guten Tag” upon meeting someone.
  • Tipping: Tipping is customary in Austria, and it is common to leave 10-15% for good service.
  • Language: German is the official language, but many locals speak English as well.
  • Punctuality: Austrians value punctuality, and it is considered rude to be late for appointments.

Best Time to Visit Krems

The best time to visit Krems depends on what you hope to experience. For mild temperatures, fewer crowds, and lush green landscapes, visit during the spring (March to May) or fall (September to November). For warm weather and a variety of outdoor activities, visit during the summer (June to August). For winter sports and a festive atmosphere, visit during the winter (December to February).

Getting to Krems: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Krems

The nearest airport to Krems is Vienna International Airport, which is located approximately 70 kilometers from the town. From there, visitors can take a train or bus to Krems.

Bus Stations Near Krems

Krems has a bus station in the center of town, which is served by local and regional bus companies.

Other Options

Visitors can also reach Krems by train or car. The town is located near the A1 and S5 motorways, making it easily accessible from other parts of Austria and Europe.

How to Get Around in Krems

Krems is a small town that is easily navigable by foot or bike. Visitors can rent bikes or take a taxi if needed to travel longer distances.
