Wachau Travel Guide - Your Ultimate Companion to Wachau, Austria

Welcome to Wachau, Austria. A paradise for nature lovers, foodies, wine enthusiasts and history buffs. Located in Lower Austria, this UNESCO World Heritage Site offers breathtaking views of vineyards, orchards, castles, and monasteries.

In this comprehensive Wachau travel guide, we’ll give you all the information you need to enjoy a chock-full of adventure and make unforgettable memories in this beautiful region.

Introduction to Wachau

Wachau is a picturesque region that stretches along the Danube River, covering an area of 33 km in length. The region is home to small villages, each with its unique culture that is worth experiencing. The valley, with its stunning beauty, is peppered with green hills and vineyards, making it a perfect destination for wine enthusiasts.

Best Time to Visit Wachau

Wachau is a year-round destination, but the best time to visit Wachau is during the spring and autumn seasons. The weather is pleasant, and the crowd is less. The spring season in Wachau starts from March, and autumn lasts from September to November.
