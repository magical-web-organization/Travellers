Introduction to Spitz

Spitz is a picturesque town located in Wachau, Lower Austria. It is a popular destination for tourists due to its stunning location on the banks of the Danube River and its well-preserved historic buildings. The town has a population of around 1,500 and is known for its wine production and local cuisine.

List of Places to Visit in Spitz

When it comes to places to visit, Spitz has a lot to offer. Whether you are interested in history, culture, or outdoor adventures, there is something for everyone.

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Spitz has several museums and historical attractions that are worth a visit.

  • Spitz Castle: A well-preserved castle that dates back to the 13th century. It offers a stunning view of the town and the Danube River.
  • Wachaumuseum: A museum that showcases the cultural history of the Wachau region.
  • St. Mauritius Church: A beautiful church that dates back to the 15th century. It has an impressive Baroque altar and other significant artworks.

Natural Attractions of Spitz: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Spitz is also famous for its natural attractions that offer exciting outdoor adventures and scenic views.

  • Danube River: A must-visit attraction, the Danube River offers boat rides and cruises that offer stunning views of the surrounding landscapes.
  • Wachau Valley: A UNESCO World Heritage Site, the Wachau Valley offers stunning scenic views and a chance to explore the region’s vineyards and wineries.
  • Hinterhaus Castle Ruins: An excellent hiking destination that offers stunning views of the Danube River and the surrounding landscapes.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Spitz

Spitz has several hiking and biking routes that offer a chance to explore the stunning landscapes of the Wachau region.

  • Wachau World Heritage Trail: A 180 km long trail that offers a chance to explore the entire Wachau Valley.
  • Spitz Panoramic Trail: A 10 km long trail that offers a stunning view of the town and the Danube River.
  • Danube Bike Path: A 365 km long bike path that runs along the Danube River and offers stunning views of the surrounding landscapes.

Best Things to Do in Spitz: Activities and Tours

Spitz offers several exciting activities and tours that give visitors a chance to explore the town and the surrounding region’s attractions.

  • Wine Tasting Tours: Spitz is known for its wine production, and visitors can enjoy wine tasting tours of the local vineyards and wineries.
  • Boat Cruises: Boat cruises on the Danube River offer stunning views of the surrounding landscapes.
  • Cycling Tours: Cycling tours are an excellent way to explore the Wachau region and its stunning landscapes.

Week-Long Itinerary for Spitz

Day 1: Arrival in Spitz, check into accommodation, and explore the town’s historic buildings and attractions.

Day 2: Wine tasting tour to the local vineyards and wineries, followed by a boat cruise on the Danube River.

Day 3: Day trip to the nearby cities of Vienna and Salzburg.

Day 4: Hiking or cycling tour of the Wachau region, followed by a visit to the Hinterhaus Castle ruins.

Day 5: Visit the Wachaumuseum and the St. Mauritius Church.

Day 6: Free day to explore the town and its surroundings at leisure.

Day 7: Departure from Spitz.

Food and Drink Scene of Spitz

Spitz has a vibrant food and drink scene that offers visitors a chance to explore the local cuisine and beverages.

Must-Try Dishes

  • Schupfnudeln: A local dish made from potato noodles, served with sauerkraut and meat.
  • Wachauer Marille: A local apricot that is used in various dishes and desserts.
  • Kaiserschmarrn: A sweet dish made from shredded pancakes and served with fruit.

Local Beverages

  • Wachau Wine: The region is known for its excellent white wines, especially the Riesling and Grüner Veltliner.
  • Schnapps: The local schnapps is a popular drink that comes in various flavors, such as apricot, raspberry, and pear.

Where to Stay in Spitz: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Spitz has several accommodations that cater to all budgets and preferences.

  • Gasthaus “Zum Goldenen Löwen”: A historic guesthouse that offers comfortable rooms and excellent dining options.
  • Hotel “Zur Post”: A family-run hotel that offers spacious rooms and a scenic location on the banks of the Danube River.
  • Pension Mair: A budget-friendly guesthouse that offers clean and comfortable rooms.

What to Buy in Spitz: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Spitz has several local products and souvenirs that visitors can bring home as a reminder of their trip.

  • Wachau Wine: The local wines make an excellent souvenir to bring back home.
  • Apricot Products: The Wachauer Marille apricots are used in various products, such as jams, liquors, and chocolates.
  • Handmade Crafts: There are several shops in Spitz that sell handmade crafts, such as ceramics and woodcrafts.

Local Culture and Customs of Spitz

Spitz has a rich cultural heritage that is reflected in its historic buildings, local cuisine, and wine production.

Best Time to Visit Spitz

The best time to visit Spitz is from May to October, when the weather is pleasant, and there are several events and festivals that take place.

Do’s and Don’ts of Spitz

Do:

  • Try the local cuisine and wine.
  • Explore the historic buildings and attractions.
  • Take a boat cruise on the Danube River.

Don’t:

  • Disrespect local customs and traditions.
  • Litter or damage the natural landscapes.

Safety and Health

Safety in Spitz

Spitz is generally a safe town, but tourists should take standard safety precautions, such as keeping their valuables safe and being aware of their surroundings.

Health in Spitz

There are no particular health risks in Spitz, and tourists should take standard precautions, such as drinking bottled water and washing their hands regularly.

Getting to Spitz: Transportation Options and Logistics

Spitz is easily accessible by several modes of transportation.

Airports Near Spitz

The nearest airports to Spitz are Vienna International Airport, which is around 100 km away, and Linz Airport, which is around 90 km away.

Bus Stations Near Spitz

There are several bus stations that connect Spitz to nearby cities, such as Vienna and Linz.

Other Options

Visitors can also reach Spitz by train or car. There are several railway stations near Spitz, and the town is accessible by car via the A1 motorway.

How to Get Around in Spitz

Spitz is a small town, and visitors can easily explore it on foot or by bicycle. There are also several bus routes that connect the town to nearby cities and attractions.

Where to Go Next from Spitz: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Spitz is a great starting point for exploring the nearby cities and attractions.

  • Vienna: The capital city of Austria, Vienna, is around 80 km away and is an excellent destination for history, culture, and architecture.
  • Salzburg: The birthplace of Mozart, Salzburg is around 300 km away and offers stunning scenery, historic buildings, and cultural events.
  • Krems: A nearby town, Krems, is around 20 km away and offers several museums and historic attractions.

Conclusion

Spitz is a charming town located in the heart of the Wachau region and is an excellent destination for tourists interested in history, culture, outdoor adventures, and local cuisine. Whether you have a day or a week, Spitz has a lot to offer, and this comprehensive guide will help you plan your perfect trip.
