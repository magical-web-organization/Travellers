Introduction to Melk

Melk is a small town located in Wachau, in Lower Austria, in Central Europe, and is famous for its stunning baroque-style Melk Abbey, a UNESCO World Heritage Site. Melk is also known for its picturesque landscapes, charming town center, and rich cultural heritage. The town is situated on the banks of the Danube River, and visitors can enjoy various outdoor activities and scenic views.

In this travel guide, we will provide you with all the necessary information to plan your trip to Melk, including the top places to visit, hiking and biking routes, food and drink recommendations, local customs, transportation, and nearby destinations.

List of Places to Visit in Melk

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Melk Abbey: This stunning baroque-style Benedictine monastery, dating back to the 11th century, is one of the most visited attractions in Austria. The abbey features impressive architecture, a stunning library, and a museum with a collection of religious artworks.
  • Schallaburg Castle: This impressive castle is located just 20 minutes from Melk and features stunning architecture and a beautiful courtyard. The castle also features a museum of Austrian history, with interactive exhibits and a collection of historical artifacts.
  • The Wachau Valley: This beautiful region is known for its picturesque vineyards, charming towns, and stunning scenery. Visitors can explore the valley by boat, bike, or on foot, and enjoy the local wine and food.

Natural Attractions of Melk: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Danube River: The Danube River is the second-longest river in Europe and offers visitors stunning views of the Wachau Valley. Visitors can take a Danube River cruise or rent a bike and explore the region on their own.
  • Melk River Promenade: This beautiful promenade offers visitors stunning views of the Danube River and the Wachau Valley. Visitors can enjoy a leisurely walk or bike ride along the promenade, and stop at one of the many cafes or restaurants along the way.
  • Jauerling: This 1000-meter-high mountain offers visitors stunning views of the Danube River Valley and the surrounding forests and vineyards. Visitors can hike to the top of the mountain or take a cable car.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Melk

Melk is a great destination for outdoor enthusiasts, with plenty of hiking and biking trails to explore. Here are some of the top routes:

  • Wachau World Heritage Trail: This 180-kilometer trail takes visitors through the stunning Wachau Valley, passing by charming towns and historic landmarks.
  • Maria Taferl Trail: This 10-kilometer hiking trail takes visitors from Melk to Maria Taferl, a small town known for its stunning basilica.
  • Danube Cycle Path: This 1,300-kilometer trail takes visitors along the banks of the Danube River, passing through charming towns and beautiful landscapes.

Best Things to Do in Melk: Activities and Tours

  • Wine Tasting: The Wachau Valley is known for its delicious wines, particularly the Grüner Veltliner and Riesling varieties. Visitors can take a wine tour and sample some of the region’s best wines.
  • River Cruise: Visitors can take a scenic boat ride along the Danube River, enjoying stunning views of the Wachau Valley.
  • Melk Abbey Tour: Visitors can take a guided tour of the stunning Melk Abbey, learning about its history, architecture, and religious significance.

Week-Long Itinerary for Melk

Day 1: Explore Melk Abbey and the town center, sampling some of the local cuisine at one of the restaurants in the area. Day 2: Take a bike ride along the Danube River, exploring the charming towns of the Wachau Valley. Day 3: Visit Schallaburg Castle and the nearby town of Krems, known for its wine culture. Day 4: Take a hike to the top of Jauerling mountain, enjoying stunning views of the Danube River Valley. Day 5: Explore the picturesque village of Dürnstein, known for its castle ruins and charming streets. Day 6: Take a scenic boat ride along the Danube River, enjoying stunning views of the region’s vineyards and landscapes. Day 7: Visit the nearby city of Vienna, known for its stunning architecture and cultural heritage.

Food and Drink Scene of Melk

Must-Try Dishes

Melk is known for its delicious cuisine, featuring regional specialties and local ingredients. Some of the top dishes to try include:

  • Wachauer Marillenknödel: These delicious dumplings are made with apricots, a local specialty.
  • Rindfleisch: This traditional beef dish is slow-cooked and served with a variety of sides, including dumplings and sauerkraut.
  • Tafelspitz: This traditional Austrian dish features boiled beef served with various sides, including potatoes and creamed spinach.

Local Beverages

The Wachau Valley is known for its delicious wines, particularly the Grüner Veltliner and Riesling varieties. Visitors can sample some of the region’s best wines at local wineries and wine taverns.

Where to Stay in Melk: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Melk offers a variety of accommodation options, including hotels, hostels, and guesthouses. Here are some of the top options:

  • Hotel Restaurant zur Post: This charming hotel is located in the heart of Melk, just a few minutes’ walk from Melk Abbey.
  • Gasthof Prankl: This cozy guesthouse offers comfortable rooms and traditional Austrian cuisine.
  • Hostel Melk: This affordable hostel offers dorm-style and private rooms, as well as a communal kitchen and outdoor terrace.

What to Buy in Melk: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Visitors to Melk can bring home a variety of souvenirs and local products, including:

  • Apricot products: The Wachau Valley is known for its delicious apricots, and visitors can bring home apricot jams, liqueurs, and other products.
  • Wine: Visitors can bring home bottles of the region’s best wines as a souvenir.
  • Handcrafted goods: Visitors can purchase handcrafted goods like pottery, jewelry, and textiles at local shops and markets.

Local Culture and Customs of Melk

Melk is known for its rich cultural heritage, featuring traditional music, dance, and cuisine. Visitors should expect to encounter friendly locals, many of whom are proud of their cultural heritage.

Best Time to Visit Melk

The best time to visit Melk is during the summer months, from June to August, when the weather is warm and sunny. However, visitors can also enjoy the region’s stunning landscapes and cultural attractions during the spring and fall months.

Do’s and Don’ts of Melk

Do:

  • Respect local customs and traditions.
  • Try the local cuisine and wine.
  • Be friendly and courteous to locals.

Don’t:

  • Wear revealing clothing in religious sites.
  • Litter or disturb the natural environment.
  • Be disrespectful to locals or their traditions.

Safety and Health

Safety in Melk

Melk is a safe destination for travelers, with low crime rates and a friendly local population. However, visitors should take precautions to protect their belongings and stay aware of their surroundings.

Health in Melk

Melk has a good healthcare system, with hospitals and clinics available in the region. Visitors should ensure they have adequate travel insurance and any necessary vaccines before traveling.

Getting to Melk: Transportation Options and Logistics

Melk is located just a few hours from Vienna and offers easy access to other major European cities. Here are some of the top transportation options:

Airports Near Melk

  • Vienna International Airport: This is the closest major airport to Melk, located just over an hour away by car or train.

Bus Stations Near Melk

  • Melk Bus Station: This local bus station offers connections to nearby towns and cities.

Other Options

  • Train: The nearest train station is in Melk, with connections to Vienna and other major European cities.
  • Car: Visitors can rent a car and explore the region at their own pace.

How to Get Around in Melk

Melk is a small town that can easily be explored on foot. Visitors can rent bicycles to explore the surrounding region, or can take a bus or taxi to nearby towns and attractions.

Where to Go Next from Melk: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Vienna: Visitors can take a day trip to Vienna, the cultural capital of Austria, known for its stunning architecture, museums, and cultural attractions.
  • Krems: This charming town is located just 30 minutes from Melk and is known for its wine culture and historic landmarks.
  • Dürnstein: This picturesque village is just a short drive from Melk and features stunning castle ruins and charming streets.

Conclusion

Melk is a must-visit destination for travelers to Austria, offering stunning landscapes, rich cultural heritage, and delicious cuisine. We hope this travel guide has helped you plan your trip to Melk, and we wish you a safe and enjoyable journey!
