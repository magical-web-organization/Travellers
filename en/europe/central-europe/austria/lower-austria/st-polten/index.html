Introduction to St. Pölten

St. Pölten is a small city located in Lower Austria, about 60 kilometers west of Vienna. It is one of the youngest capital cities in Europe, having only become the capital of Lower Austria in 1986. Despite its small size, St. Pölten has a lot to offer visitors, from historical landmarks and museums to beautiful parks and outdoor activities.

List of Places to Visit in St. Pölten

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

St. Pölten Cathedral

St. Pölten Cathedral, also known as the New Cathedral or the Cathedral of St. Hippolytus, is a beautiful baroque church located in the center of the city. It was built in the 18th century and is famous for its stunning interior, including frescoes, paintings, and sculptures by renowned artists of the time.

Lower Austrian State Museum

The Lower Austrian State Museum is located in the former residence of the local governor. It features a collection of artifacts that showcase the region’s history, art, and culture. Visitors can also see temporary exhibitions and attend various events, including concerts, lectures, and workshops.

The Domplatz

The Domplatz is a historic area in St. Pölten that includes the Cathedral, governor’s residence, and other important buildings. It is the perfect place to take a peaceful stroll and admire the architecture and rich history of the city.

Natural Attractions of St. Pölten: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Viehofner Seen

The Viehofner Seen are a group of three small lakes located just outside of St. Pölten. They are popular for swimming, fishing, and boating, and are surrounded by beautiful forests and meadows.

Naturpark Buchberg

Naturpark Buchberg is a nature reserve located on a hill just outside of St. Pölten. It offers panoramic views of the city and surrounding areas, as well as hiking trails, picnic areas, and a visitor center.

Harzberg

Harzberg is another hill located near St. Pölten that offers stunning views of the city and surrounding countryside. Visitors can hike to the top of the hill, which has a small chapel and a popular restaurant.

Hiking/Biking Routes of St. Pölten

St. Pölten offers a variety of hiking and biking routes, from easy paths for beginners to challenging routes for experienced hikers. Some popular routes include:

  • Mariazell Pilgrimage Trail
  • Traisen Valley Cycle Path
  • Wachau World Heritage Trail

Best Things to Do in St. Pölten: Activities and Tours

City Tours

Explore the city with a guided city tour and learn about the history and culture of St. Pölten. Tours are available by foot, bike, Segway, and even horse-drawn carriage.

Wine Tasting

St. Pölten is located in the heart of Lower Austria’s wine region, and there are many vineyards and wineries where visitors can taste local wines and learn about the winemaking process.

Festivals and Events

St. Pölten hosts a variety of festivals and events throughout the year, including the St. Pölten Summer Festival, the Lower Austria State Fair, and the St. Pölten Christmas Market.

Week-Long Itinerary for St. Pölten

Day 1: Explore the city center and visit the St. Pölten Cathedral and Lower Austrian State Museum.

Day 2: Hike or bike through the beautiful Traisen Valley or visit the Naturpark Buchberg.

Day 3: Take a wine tour and taste local wines at one of the region’s many vineyards.

Day 4: Visit the Viehofner Seen and enjoy swimming, fishing, or boating.

Day 5: Attend a festival or event in St. Pölten.

Day 6: Visit the historic town of Melk and the famous Melk Abbey.

Day 7: Take a day trip to Vienna, located just an hour away by train.

Food and Drink Scene of St. Pölten

Must-Try Dishes

St. Pölten and Lower Austria are known for their hearty and traditional cuisine. Some must-try dishes include:

  • Wiener Schnitzel: a thin, breaded and fried veal cutlet
  • Tafelspitz: boiled beef served with root vegetables and horseradish
  • Kartoffelpuffer: potato pancakes served with applesauce or sour cream

Local Beverages

Lower Austria is famous for its wines, particularly Grüner Veltliner and Riesling. Beer is also popular, and St. Pölten has its own local brewery, Stiegl Brauwelt.

Where to Stay in St. Pölten: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

St. Pölten has a variety of accommodation options to suit any budget. Some top choices include:

  • Hotel Graf: a modern, centrally located hotel
  • Hotel Metropol: a stylish, eco-friendly hotel
  • JUFA Hotel St. Pölten: a family-friendly hotel with outdoor playground and activities

What to Buy in St. Pölten: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Some popular souvenirs and local products to bring home from St. Pölten include:

  • Wine and beer from the local vineyards and brewery
  • Handmade pottery and ceramics from the region’s artisans
  • Bienenwachs-Kerzen: handmade beeswax candles from a local shop

Local Culture and Customs of St. Pölten

St. Pölten is a small and traditional city, and visitors should be respectful of local customs and culture. It is common to greet strangers with a polite “Grüß Gott” (hello), and tipping is generally expected at restaurants and cafes.

Best Time to Visit St. Pölten

The best time to visit St. Pölten is during the summer months when the weather is warm and there are many outdoor events and activities. However, the city is also beautiful during the fall when the leaves change color and the vineyards are harvest.

Do’s and Don’ts of St. Pölten

Do:

  • Try traditional Austrian cuisine and local wines
  • Attend a festival or event to experience the local culture
  • Explore the beautiful outdoor areas and hiking trails

Don’t:

  • Forget to bring comfortable walking shoes for exploring the city and hiking trails
  • Smoke in public areas, as it is prohibited in many areas of the city
  • Be loud and disrespectful to locals or other visitors

Safety and Health

Safety in St. Pölten

St. Pölten is generally a safe city, but it is always important to take precautions while traveling, such as keeping your valuables secure and being aware of your surroundings.

Health in St. Pölten

There are no specific health concerns or vaccinations necessary for travel to St. Pölten, but it is always a good idea to have travel insurance and consult with a doctor before traveling to any foreign country.

Getting to St. Pölten: Transportation Options and Logistics

St. Pölten is located just an hour away from Vienna by train or car. There are also several bus lines that connect St. Pölten to neighboring cities and towns.

Airports Near St. Pölten

The nearest airport to St. Pölten is Vienna International Airport, located approximately 80 kilometers east of the city.

Bus Stations Near St. Pölten

The main bus station in St. Pölten is located just a short walk from the train station and city center.

Other Options

There are many car rental companies located near the St. Pölten train station for those who prefer to drive.

How to Get Around in St. Pölten

St. Pölten is a small city, and most of the main attractions are located within walking distance of the city center. There is also a regional bus system that connects to neighboring towns and villages.

Where to Go Next from St. Pölten: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

St. Pölten is located in the heart of Lower Austria, and there are many nearby destinations and excursions worth visiting, including:

  • Vienna: the historic capital city of Austria, just an hour away by train or car
  • Wachau Valley: a scenic wine-growing region located along the Danube River
  • Krems: a picturesque town located in the heart of the Wachau Valley

Conclusion

St. Pölten may be a small city, but it is rich in history, culture, and natural beauty. Whether you are looking to explore the city’s historic landmarks, sample local wines, or hike through the beautiful countryside, St. Pölten has something to offer every type of traveler.
