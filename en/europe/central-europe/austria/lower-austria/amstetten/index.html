Introduction to Amstetten

Located in Lower Austria in Central Europe, Amstetten is a charming town that is rich in history and natural beauty. Its proximity to Vienna, Salzburg, and Linz make it the perfect destination for a day trip or a longer stay. This comprehensive travel guide will provide you with all the information you need to plan your visit to Amstetten.

List of Places to Visit in Amstetten

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

If you are a history buff, Amstetten is the perfect place for you. The town has a rich cultural heritage and boasts several museums and historical places that are definitely worth visiting.

  • Schloss Ulmerfeld is a beautifully preserved Renaissance castle that was built in the 16th century. Today, it houses a museum that showcases the history of the castle and the surrounding area.

  • Amtshaus Amstetten is a historic building that used to serve as the town hall. Today, it is home to the museum of local history, which provides visitors with an insight into the history of the town.

  • Schloss Greifenberg is a former fortress that has been turned into a wonderful museum that features local history exhibits and artifacts.

Natural Attractions of Amstetten: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

If you are looking for some adventure, exploring Amstetten’s natural attractions is definitely a must-do. The town and its surrounding countryside offer numerous opportunities for outdoor activities.

  • Mostviertelbahn is a scenic railway that takes you through the beautiful landscape of Amstetten, passing through towns and villages along the way.

  • Erlaufschlucht is a breathtakingly beautiful gorge that offers stunning views of the surrounding forest and river valley. Visitors can hike or cycle through the area.

  • Wallfahrtskirche Sonntagberg is a stunning pilgrimage church that sits on top of a hill overlooking the town of Amstetten. The church is surrounded by beautiful hiking trails, making it the perfect spot for a leisurely walk.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Amstetten

For hiking enthusiasts, the area surrounding Amstetten offers several beautiful hiking trails that are definitely worth exploring.

  • Panoramaweg Amstetten is a scenic hiking trail that offers stunning views of the Alps and the surrounding countryside.

  • Kollmitzberg Pielachtal is a beautiful hiking trail that leads visitors through forests, meadows, and fields, offering breathtaking views of the landscape.

  • Mostviertler Hügelland Radrunde is a scenic cycling route that takes you through picturesque small towns and villages, offering an insight into the local way of life.

Best Things to Do in Amstetten: Activities and Tours

Apart from the cultural and natural attractions, Amstetten offers plenty of activities and tours that will make your visit even more memorable:

  • Amstetten Farmers’ Market is the perfect place to taste local delicacies and buy souvenirs.

  • Amstetten Walking Tour provides a fascinating insight into the town’s history and architecture and is a great way to get to know the town.

  • Kartonfabrik Steyrermühl is a cardboard factory that offers guided tours, providing visitors with an insight into the production process.

Week-Long Itinerary for Amstetten

If you are planning a longer stay in Amstetten, here is a sample week-long itinerary that will give you a taste of the many attractions the town has to offer:

Food and Drink Scene of Amstetten

No visit to Amstetten is complete without trying some of the local cuisine.

Must-Try Dishes

  • Goscherte Suppe is a traditional soup made from smoked pork and root vegetables, served with freshly baked bread.

  • Mostbauerntopfen is a local cheese made from cottage cheese and served with bread or crackers.

  • Pancakes with Plum Sauce is a local dessert made from fluffy pancakes and sweet plum sauce.

Local Beverages

  • Most is a traditional alcoholic beverage made from fermented apple juice.

  • Schnaps is a strong, clear brandy made from fruit.

  • Wein is a locally produced wine that pairs perfectly with Amstetten’s cuisine.

Where to Stay in Amstetten: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Amstetten has plenty of affordable accommodation options:

  • Hotel Exel is a great option for those who want to stay in the heart of the town.

  • Gasthaus-Pension zur Linde is a cozy guesthouse that offers warm hospitality and comfortable rooms.

  • Jugendgästehaus Amstetten is a hostel located in the outskirts of the town that offers budget accommodation for travelers.

What to Buy in Amstetten: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

There are plenty of souvenirs and local products you can bring home from Amstetten to remind you of your trip:

  • Mostbauerntopfen is a great souvenir for cheese lovers.

  • Local wines can be great gifts for friends and family back home.

  • Handmade crafts and jewelry can be found at local shops and markets.

Local Culture and Customs of Amstetten

Some cultural and customs practices that are unique to Amstetten and worth learning about:

  • Kirchtag is the town’s annual harvest festival that is held in September, featuring parades, live music, and traditional cuisine.

  • Traditional clothing is still worn on special occasions.

  • Proper etiquette is important when meeting new people. A firm handshake and maintaining eye contact are important gestures of respect.

Best Time to Visit Amstetten

The best time to visit Amstetten is between May and September. During this time, the weather is warm, and the town’s natural beauty is in full bloom.

Getting to Amstetten: Transportation Options and Logistics

Amstetten is easily accessible by various means of transportation.

Airports Near Amstetten

  • Vienna International Airport is the closest airport to Amstetten, located about 160 km away.

  • Linz Airport is another option, located about 50 km away.

Bus Stations Near Amstetten

  • Amstetten bus station is the main bus station in the town. There are regular bus connections to other towns and cities in Austria.

  • Linz bus station is another option, located about 45 km away.

Other Options

  • Train is a popular option for traveling within Austria. Amstetten has its own train station that connects the town to other major cities in the country.

  • Car rental is also a convenient option for those who prefer to explore the area at their own pace.

How to Get Around in Amstetten

Amstetten is a small town, and the best way to get around is on foot or by bike. It is also possible to take local buses or use a taxi service.

Where to Go Next from Amstetten: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

If you have more time to explore, there are several nearby destinations that are definitely worth a visit:

  • Linz is a vibrant city known for its museums, modern architecture, and cultural scene.

  • Vienna is a must-visit destination for those who want to explore Austria’s rich history and enjoy its music and art scene.

  • Graz is another beautiful city with a rich cultural heritage and a lively nightlife.

Conclusion

Amstetten is a charming town that offers visitors a unique blend of history, culture, and natural beauty. Whether you are interested in exploring its museums and historical places or taking part in outdoor activities, Amstetten has something to offer everyone.
