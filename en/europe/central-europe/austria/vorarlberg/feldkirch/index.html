If you’re looking for an off-the-beaten-path destination in Austria, look no further than Feldkirch. This picturesque town located in the Vorarlberg region of Western Austria is known for its cobbled streets, well-preserved medieval architecture, and stunning natural surroundings. In this comprehensive travel guide, we’ll take you through all the best places to visit, things to do, where to stay, and more in Feldkirch.

Introduction to Feldkirch

Feldkirch is a small town located in western Austria, near the borders of Germany, Liechtenstein, and Switzerland. With a population of just over 30,000, it’s a quiet and peaceful destination that’s perfect for a relaxing getaway. Despite its small size, there’s plenty to see and do in Feldkirch, from exploring its medieval old town to hiking in the surrounding mountains.

List of Places to Visit in Feldkirch

Here are some of the top places to visit in Feldkirch:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Schattenburg Castle: This 800-year-old castle is one of the most well-preserved medieval castles in Austria. It’s home to a museum that showcases the history of the castle and the surrounding region.
  • Marktgasse: This picturesque street in the heart of Feldkirch’s old town is lined with ornately decorated buildings dating back to the 15th and 16th centuries.
  • Cathedral St. Nikolaus: This beautiful Gothic cathedral dates back to the 15th century and features stunning stained glass windows and intricate carvings.
  • Palais Liechtenstein: Located just outside the old town, this baroque palace was built in the 17th century and is now used as a conference center and event space.

Natural Attractions of Feldkirch: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Muttersberg: This mountain peak overlooking Feldkirch is accessible by gondola and offers stunning views of the surrounding Alps.
  • Ill River: The Ill River flows through Feldkirch and is a popular spot for kayaking, rafting, and fishing.
  • Rosengarten: This botanical garden in the heart of the old town features over 500 varieties of roses and other flowers.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Feldkirch

Feldkirch is a paradise for outdoor enthusiasts, with miles of hiking and biking trails to explore. Some of the top routes include:

  • Three Castles Tour: This 9-kilometer hike takes you through the woods and past three medieval castles.
  • Montafon High Trail: This 120-kilometer hiking trail winds through the stunning Montafon Valley, offering breathtaking views of the surrounding mountains.
  • Via Claudia Augusta: This ancient Roman road runs through Feldkirch and is now a popular cycling route.

Best Things to Do in Feldkirch: Activities and Tours

  • Take a guided tour of Schattenburg Castle to learn about the history of the castle and the surrounding region.
  • Explore Feldkirch’s old town on foot, admiring the ornately decorated buildings and picturesque streets.
  • Go skiing or snowboarding on the nearby slopes during the winter months.
  • Visit one of the local breweries to taste some of the finest beer in Austria.

Week-Long Itinerary for Feldkirch

Here’s a sample itinerary for a week in Feldkirch:

  • Day 1: Explore the old town of Feldkirch and visit the Schattenburg Castle.
  • Day 2: Take a guided hike to Muttersberg for stunning views of the surrounding Alps.
  • Day 3: Go skiing or snowboarding on the nearby slopes.
  • Day 4: Take a day trip to nearby Liechtenstein to explore Vaduz and its famous castle.
  • Day 5: Go on a cycling tour of the Via Claudia Augusta.
  • Day 6: Visit one of the local breweries to taste some of the finest beer in Austria.
  • Day 7: Relax and enjoy a picnic in Rosengarten, Feldkirch’s botanical garden.

Food and Drink Scene of Feldkirch

Feldkirch is known for its hearty Austrian cuisine and locally produced beer. Here are some of the must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Käsespätzle: A traditional dish made with soft egg noodles and melted cheese.
  • Wiener Schnitzel: A thin, breaded and fried cutlet of veal, typically served with potatoes and cranberry sauce.
  • Germknödel: A delicious dessert made with yeast dough and filled with a sweet plum jam.

Local Beverages

  • Mohrenbrauerei: This local brewery has been producing beer in Feldkirch since 1834 and offers tours and tastings.
  • Schnapps: This fruit brandy is a popular digestif in Austria and comes in a variety of flavors, including pear, apple, and plum.

Where to Stay in Feldkirch: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Feldkirch offers a variety of accommodation options to suit every budget and preference. Here are some of the best places to stay in Feldkirch:

  • Hotel Weisses Kreuz: This historic hotel is located in the heart of Feldkirch’s old town and offers cozy and comfortable rooms.
  • Montfort das Hotel: This modern, 4-star hotel is located just outside the old town and offers stylish rooms and a spa.
  • YOHO International Youth Hostel: This budget-friendly hostel is located near the train station and offers affordable dorm rooms and private rooms.

What to Buy in Feldkirch: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Here are some of the best souvenirs and local products to bring home from Feldkirch:

  • Hand-carved wooden souvenirs: Feldkirch is known for its hand-carved wooden souvenirs, such as cuckoo clocks and figurines.
  • Mohrenkopf: These chocolate-covered marshmallows are a local specialty and make a great gift.
  • Local wine and schnapps: Vorarlberg is a wine-producing region, and its apple, pear, and plum schnapps are also popular.

Local Culture and Customs of Feldkirch

Feldkirch is a predominantly Catholic town, and many of its traditions and customs are related to Christianity. It’s also a region that’s proud of its traditional crafts, such as woodcarving and cheesemaking. The locals are friendly and welcoming to visitors, but it’s important to respect their culture and customs.

Best Time to Visit Feldkirch

The best time to visit Feldkirch depends on your interests and preferences. The summer months (June to August) offer warm weather and outdoor activities like hiking and cycling. The winter months (December to March) offer skiing and snowboarding on the nearby slopes. Spring and fall are both great times to visit for mild weather and fewer crowds.

Do’s and Don’ts of Feldkirch

Here are some do’s and don’ts to keep in mind when visiting Feldkirch:

Do:

  • Respect the local culture and customs.
  • Try the local cuisine and beer.
  • Explore the nearby mountains and natural surroundings.

Don’t:

  • Be loud or disruptive in public spaces.
  • Wear inappropriate clothing in churches or other religious sites.
  • Overstep boundaries when it comes to local customs and traditions.

Safety and Health

Feldkirch is a safe destination for travelers, with low levels of crime and a good healthcare system. However, it’s always important to take basic safety precautions like keeping an eye on your belongings and being aware of your surroundings. For health concerns, there are several medical clinics and hospitals in the area.

Getting to Feldkirch: Transportation Options and Logistics

Feldkirch is easily accessible by train, bus, or car. Here are some of the transportation options available:

Airports Near Feldkirch

  • Zurich International Airport (ZRH): This airport is located 117 kilometers from Feldkirch and is the closest major international airport.
  • Innsbruck Airport (INN): This airport is located 137 kilometers from Feldkirch and offers regional flights within Europe.

Bus Stations Near Feldkirch

  • Feldkirch Bus Station: Located in the town center, this bus station offers regional and international connections.
  • Zurich Bus Station: This major bus station in Zurich offers connections to Feldkirch and other destinations in Austria and Switzerland.

Other Options

  • Train: Feldkirch has a train station that’s connected to major cities like Zurich and Vienna.
  • Car: If you’re driving to Feldkirch, it’s easily accessible from nearby cities like Zurich, Innsbruck, and Munich.

How to Get Around in Feldkirch

Feldkirch is a small town that’s easily explored on foot. However, if you want to venture out into the surrounding mountains or nearby villages, it’s best to rent a car or take public transportation.

Where to Go Next from Feldkirch: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Here are some of the nearby destinations and excursions you can take from Feldkirch:

  • Liechtenstein: This tiny principality is located just a short drive from Feldkirch and is known for its stunning castle and picturesque towns.
  • Bregenz: This lakeside town is located just 30 minutes from Feldkirch and offers stunning views of Lake Constance.
  • Montafon Valley: This stunning valley is located just 45 minutes from Feldkirch and offers miles of hiking and skiing trails.

Conclusion

Feldkirch is a hidden gem in the heart of Austria, offering stunning natural surroundings, well-preserved medieval architecture, and delicious local cuisine. With this comprehensive travel guide, you’re ready to plan your trip to Feldkirch and make the most of your time in this beautiful town.
