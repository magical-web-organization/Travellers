Looking for a unique travel destination in Europe? Look no further than Vorarlberg, a stunning region in western Austria that is often overshadowed by nearby tourist hotspots like Vienna and Salzburg. But don’t be fooled by its lesser-known status - Vorarlberg is packed with natural beauty, cultural landmarks, and outdoor adventures that will leave you breathless. In this comprehensive travel guide, we’ll help you discover the best of Vorarlberg, from must-see attractions to hidden gems off the beaten path.

Introduction to Vorarlberg

Nestled between Switzerland, Liechtenstein, and Germany, Vorarlberg is a landlocked region with a rich history and diverse cultural heritage. Its idyllic Alpine landscapes, quaint villages, and modern cities make it an ideal destination for travelers seeking both relaxation and adventure. Vorarlberg boasts a unique blend of Austrian and Swiss influences, reflected in its art, architecture, and cuisine. Whether you’re a history buff, an outdoor enthusiast, or simply looking to unwind in a picturesque setting, Vorarlberg has something for everyone.

List of Places to Visit in Vorarlberg

When it comes to sightseeing in Vorarlberg, the options are endless. Here are a few must-see attractions to add to your itinerary:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • The Jewish Museum Hohenems - devoted to the history and culture of the Jewish community in Vorarlberg.
  • The Vorarlberg Museum in Bregenz - a must-visit for anyone interested in the region’s history and culture.
  • The Angelika Kauffmann Museum in Schwarzenberg - dedicated to the life and work of the famous Austrian artist.

Natural Attractions of Vorarlberg: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • The Bregenzerwald - a paradise for hikers, bikers, and skiers, with an extensive network of trails and breathtaking views.
  • The Rätikon Range - a popular destination for mountaineers and rock climbers, with towering peaks and challenging routes.
  • The Lake Constance region - the perfect spot for sailing, swimming, and exploring picturesque towns on the border of Austria, Germany, and Switzerland.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Vorarlberg

With miles of hiking and biking trails crisscrossing the region, Vorarlberg is a paradise for outdoor enthusiasts. Here are a few top routes to consider:

  • The Lechweg - a long-distance hiking trail that follows the course of the Lech River, with stunning views of the Alps and quaint villages along the way.
  • The Bregenzerwald Cheese Trail - a leisurely hike that takes you through the picturesque Bregenzerwald region, with plenty of opportunities to sample local cheese along the way.
  • The Lünersee - a scenic hiking route that takes you to the crystal-clear Lünersee lake, surrounded by majestic peaks and glaciers.

Best Things to Do in Vorarlberg: Activities and Tours

Looking for unique experiences in Vorarlberg? Here are a few activities and tours to consider:

  • Visit the KäseStrasse Bregenzerwald for a taste of the region’s famous cheeses.
  • Take a boat tour on the beautiful Lake Constance.
  • Explore the historic city of Feldkirch, with its well-preserved medieval architecture.
  • Take a cable car ride to the top of the Karren mountain for breathtaking views of the surrounding landscape.

Week-Long Itinerary for Vorarlberg

If you’re planning a longer stay in Vorarlberg, here’s a sample itinerary to help you make the most of your time:

Day 1-2: Explore the city of Bregenz and its surrounding areas, including the Vorarlberg Museum and the Bregenzerwald.

Day 3-4: Head to the Lechweg trail for a scenic hike and a taste of local cheese along the way.

Day 5-6: Journey to the Rätikon Range for some challenging mountaineering and rock climbing.

Day 7: End your trip with a relaxing boat tour on Lake Constance before departing from nearby Friedrichshafen.

Food and Drink Scene of Vorarlberg

No trip to Vorarlberg is complete without sampling its delicious cuisine and refreshing beverages. Here are a few must-try dishes and local drinks to add to your list:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Käsknöpfle - a hearty pasta dish made with local cheese and typically served with caramelized onions.
  • Riebel - a cornmeal-based dish typically served for breakfast or as a snack, often with apple sauce or jam.
  • Sura Kees - a unique type of local cheese that is sharp and tangy, often served with bread and a glass of wine.

Local Beverages

  • Schnapps - a strong, locally produced brandy.
  • Alpenbitter - a herbal liqueur that is typically consumed as an aperitif or digestif.
  • Riesling - a crisp, dry white wine that is grown in the region’s vineyards.

Where to Stay in Vorarlberg: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Vorarlberg has a variety of accommodations to suit every traveler’s budget and style. Here are a few top picks:

  • Arlberg Hospiz Hotel - a luxury hotel in the heart of the Arlberg ski region, with five-star amenities and breathtaking mountain views.
  • Gasthof Hirschen Schwarzenberg - a traditional guesthouse in the picturesque village of Schwarzenberg, with cozy rooms and a charming atmosphere.
  • Youth Hostel Feldkirch - an affordable and comfortable option for budget travelers, located in the city center of Feldkirch.

What to Buy in Vorarlberg: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Looking for the perfect souvenir to remember your trip to Vorarlberg? Here are a few local products to consider:

  • Hand-carved wooden crafts, such as figurines and kitchen utensils.
  • Locally produced cheese, wine, and schnapps.
  • Beautiful handwoven textiles, including traditional costumes, blankets, and tablecloths.

Local Culture and Customs of Vorarlberg

Vorarlberg has a rich cultural heritage that reflects its unique blend of Austrian and Swiss influences. Here are a few customs and traditions to keep in mind:

  • The Vorarlberg dialect is unique and can be difficult to understand for non-native speakers, but locals are generally happy to help visitors communicate.
  • Traditional costumes, including leather pants and embroidered blouses, are still worn for special occasions and festivals.
  • The region’s art and architecture reflect a mix of modern and traditional styles, with many buildings featuring innovative designs and environmentally friendly features.

Best Time to Visit Vorarlberg

The best time to visit Vorarlberg depends on your preferences and travel plans. For winter sports enthusiasts, the ski season runs from December to April, with plenty of opportunities for skiing, snowboarding, and other winter activities. Spring and summer are ideal for hiking, biking, and exploring the region’s natural beauty, while fall is a great time to sample local cuisine and attend harvest festivals.

Do’s and Don’ts of Vorarlberg

As with any travel destination, there are a few cultural norms and customs to keep in mind while visiting Vorarlberg:

Do:

  • Respect local customs and traditions, such as dress codes and language usage.
  • Try local cuisine and beverages, and ask locals for their recommendations and tips.
  • Make an effort to learn a few phrases in the local dialect, such as “Servus” for “hello” or “Tschüss” for “goodbye.”

Don’t:

  • Assume that everyone speaks English; while many locals do, it’s always appreciated when visitors make an effort to speak the local language.
  • Wear inappropriate clothing or behave impolitely in public spaces.
  • Overlook the quieter, less-touristy areas of Vorarlberg - some of the most rewarding experiences can be found off the beaten path.

Safety and Health

While Vorarlberg is generally a safe and healthy travel destination, it’s always important to take certain precautions when traveling:

Safety in Vorarlberg

  • Be aware of the terrain and weather conditions before embarking on outdoor activities, and always follow guidelines and safety rules.
  • Take care when driving on winding mountain roads, especially during winter months when conditions can be slippery.

Health in Vorarlberg

  • Pack appropriate clothing and gear for your planned activities, including sturdy hiking boots and warm layers for winter sports.
  • Consult with a healthcare provider before traveling to ensure you have any necessary vaccines or medications.

Getting to Vorarlberg: Transportation Options and Logistics

Getting to Vorarlberg is easy thanks to its convenient location and transportation options. Here are a few tips on getting there:

Airports Near Vorarlberg

  • Friedrichshafen Airport (FDH) - located across the border in Germany, and served by several major airlines.
  • Zurich Airport (ZRH) - located about two hours from Vorarlberg, and served by numerous airlines.

Bus Stations Near Vorarlberg

  • FlixBus - offers frequent and affordable bus service to Vorarlberg from major cities throughout Europe.

Other Options

  • Train - Vorarlberg is well-connected to the German and Swiss rail network, making it easy to travel to other destinations in Europe by train.
  • Car - renting a car is a popular option for exploring Vorarlberg’s scenic routes, but be aware of the challenges of mountain driving.

How to Get Around in Vorarlberg

Once you’ve arrived in Vorarlberg, there are several transportation options to help you navigate the region:

  • Public transportation - Vorarlberg has a reliable and efficient bus and train system that connects major towns and attractions.
  • Car rental - renting a car is a good option for exploring more remote areas of the region.
  • Bicycle rental - many towns and attractions in Vorarlberg offer bike rental services, making it easy to explore the region on two wheels.

Where to Go Next from Vorarlberg: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

If you’re looking to extend your trip beyond Vorarlberg, there are plenty of nearby destinations and excursions to consider:

  • The Swiss cities of St. Gallen and Zurich - both easily accessible from Vorarlberg by train or car.
  • The Bavarian Alps - located just across the border in Germany, with stunning mountain scenery and charming towns.
  • The Rhine Falls - one of Europe’s largest waterfalls, located just a short drive from Vorarlberg.

Conclusion

From natural beauty to cultural heritage, Vorarlberg has something for everyone. Whether you’re seeking adventure or relaxation, you’ll find plenty to enjoy in this hidden gem of western Austria. Take the time to explore the region’s stunning landscapes, quaint villages, and bustling cities, and discover what makes Vorarlberg such a unique and unforgettable travel destination.
