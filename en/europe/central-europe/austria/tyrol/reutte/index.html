Introduction to Reutte

Located in the heart of the Tyrol region, Reutte is a charming Austrian town that offers a perfect blend of natural beauty and cultural heritage. Nestled in a valley surrounded by snow-capped peaks, Reutte is a paradise for nature lovers and adventure seekers. With its pristine countryside, historical landmarks, and relaxed pace of life, Reutte is a perfect getaway for travelers looking for authentic Alpine experiences. In this comprehensive travel guide, we will introduce you to the best places to visit, outdoor adventures, accommodations, food and drink, and much more in Reutte.

List of Places to Visit in Reutte

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Reutte has a rich history that is reflected in its many museums and historical sites. Whether you want to learn about local traditions, explore historic castles or visit medieval churches, there is something for everyone.

  • Ehrenberg Castle: Located on a hilltop overlooking the town, Ehrenberg Castle is a significant historical monument of Reutte. Built in the Middle Ages, the castle is open to visitors, providing an insight into the life and times of Austrian nobility.
  • Museum im Grünen Haus: This small museum showcases the cultural heritage of Reutte and its surroundings through ancient artifacts, paintings, and photographs. The museum is housed in an old home with a sprawling garden that provides an excellent spot for picnicking.
  • St. Anna Chapel: This impressive chapel is a masterpiece of baroque architecture that dates back to the 17th century. Inside the chapel, you’ll find ornate decorations and religious artworks that depict the town’s religious history.

Natural Attractions of Reutte: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

While Reutte might be a small town, it offers endless opportunities for nature lovers and thrill-seekers. From lakes and rivers to mountains and forests, there’s no shortage of stunning scenery to explore.

  • Plansee: This idyllic alpine lake is famous for its crystal-clear waters and stunning mountain backdrop. It’s a popular spot for swimming, fishing, and kayaking.
  • Highline 179: This panoramic suspension bridge spans the valley between Reutte and Ehrenberg Castle. At a length of 406 meters, it’s the longest of its kind in the world and offers incredible views of the surrounding mountains.
  • Lechweg Trail: This long-distance hiking trail spans over 125 kilometers and winds its way through some of the region’s most scenic landscapes. The trail passes through Reutte and provides an excellent opportunity for a day hike.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Reutte

Reutte is the perfect destination for outdoor enthusiasts, with a variety of hiking and biking routes available for all levels of experience.

  • Burgenwelt Trail: This easy circular hike takes you around the castle of Ehrenberg and offers stunning views of the surrounding mountains.
  • Tannheimer Tal: This valley has over 300 kilometers of well-marked hiking trails, ranging from easy walks to more challenging multi-day treks.
  • Bike Trail Tirol: This 100-kilometer biking trail takes you through some of the most beautiful scenery in Tirol and is suitable for all levels of mountain bikers.

Best Things to Do in Reutte: Activities and Tours

If you’re looking for something unique and exciting to do in Reutte, there are plenty of fun activities and tours to choose from.

  • Hang Gliding: Soar through the skies and take in the spectacular views of the Alps with a hang gliding session.
  • Cheese-making: Learn about the traditional art of cheese-making at one of the local dairy farms and try your hand at crafting your own cheese.
  • Beer Tasting: Take a tour of the Reuttener Brauhaus, a local brewery with a long history of producing high-quality beer. You’ll have a chance to sample some of their brews and learn about the brewing process.

Week-Long Itinerary for Reutte

If you’re planning a week-long trip to Reutte, here’s an itinerary that will help you make the most of your time:

  • Day 1: Walk through the town center and visit the Museum im Grünen Haus.
  • Day 2: Visit Ehrenberg Castle followed by a hike through the Plansee Nature Reserve.
  • Day 3: Take a day trip to nearby Füssen, Germany, and visit the iconic Neuschwanstein Castle.
  • Day 4: Explore the Lechweg Trail and take in the stunning views of the alpine landscape.
  • Day 5: Visit the St. Anna Chapel and then take the Highline 179 suspension bridge for panoramic views of the region.
  • Day 6: Take the Burgenwelt Trail and visit some of the old castles around the town.
  • Day 7: Participate in a cheese-making workshop in a local dairy farm followed by a trip to Reuttener Brauhaus to learn about beer brewing.

Food and Drink Scene of Reutte

Reutte has a vibrant food and drink scene with plenty of local delicacies to try and enjoy.

Must-Try Dishes

  • Kaspressknödel: These traditional dumplings are made with cheese, onions, and flour and are a popular local snack.
  • Tiroler Gröstl: This hearty dish is made with potatoes, onions, and meat, and is a staple in Austrian cuisine.
  • Kaiserschmarrn: This sweet dish is essentially a shredded pancake, typically served with fresh berries and whipped cream.

Local Beverages

  • Schnaps: This potent fruit brandy is a popular drink in Austria and comes in a variety of flavors, including plum, grape, and apple.
  • Beer: Reutte is home to several breweries that produce excellent beer, including Reuttener Brauhaus, which has been brewing beer for over 400 years.

Where to Stay in Reutte: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Reutte has a variety of accommodation options, from luxury hotels to affordable hostels and guesthouses.

  • Hotel Maximilian: This four-star hotel offers upscale rooms with balconies that offer breathtaking views of the surrounding mountains and countryside.
  • Pension Leuprecht: This cozy guesthouse offers comfortable rooms with shared bathrooms and a hearty breakfast in a central location.
  • Youth Hostel Reutte: This affordable hostel offers clean and comfortable rooms and amenities like free Wi-Fi and a shared kitchen.

What to Buy in Reutte: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

If you’re looking for souvenirs and local products to bring home, Reutte has plenty to offer. Here are some ideas:

  • Hand-carved wooden figures: These intricate carvings are a testament to the region’s wood carving tradition.
  • Loden jackets: This traditional woolen jacket is a popular souvenir, and you’ll find plenty of stores selling them around the town.
  • Schnaps: Take home a bottle of locally produced schnaps as a memento of your trip to Reutte.

Local Culture and Customs of Reutte

The people of Reutte value their local traditions and customs deeply. Here are some things to keep in mind when visiting the town:

  • Dress modestly when visiting churches and religious sites.
  • Respect the locals’ privacy and avoid taking photographs without permission.
  • Tipping is not mandatory in Austria, but rounding up the bill is appreciated.

Best Time to Visit Reutte

The best time to visit Reutte is between May and October when the weather is mild and pleasant. The town is especially beautiful during the summer months, when the surrounding hills are green, and the wildflowers are in full bloom. However, if you’re looking for winter activities like skiing, December to March is the perfect time to visit.

Do’s and Don’ts of Reutte

Here are some things to keep in mind during your stay in Reutte:

Do:

  • Respect the local culture and customs.
  • Try the local delicacies and beverages.
  • Explore the natural beauty of the region through hiking or biking.

Don’t:

  • Public displays of affection are frowned upon in Austria.
  • Don’t litter or damage the natural environment.
  • Don’t smoke in public areas like restaurants and bars.

Safety and Health

Reutte is a safe and friendly town with a low crime rate. However, it’s always a good idea to take some precautions when travelling.

Safety in Reutte

  • Be aware of your surroundings and avoid areas that are poorly lit or deserted at night.
  • Keep your valuables like passports, cash, and electronics in a safe place.
  • Carry a map and avoid getting lost in remote areas.

Health in Reutte

Reutte has modern medical facilities and hospitals in case of emergencies. The town is relatively clean, and tap water is safe to drink.

Getting to Reutte: Transportation Options and Logistics

Reutte is well-connected to other parts of Austria and Europe, with several convenient transportation options.

Airports Near Reutte

The nearest airport is the Innsbruck Airport, which is approximately an hour-drive from Reutte. Other airport options include Munich Airport and Zurich Airport, which are both about a three-hour drive away.

Bus Stations Near Reutte

Reutte has a bus station in the town center that connects to other parts of Tyrol region and Austria. Buses run frequently, and schedules can be found online.

Other Options

  • Train: Reutte has a train station that connects to other major cities in Austria and Germany.
  • Car: If you’re driving, Reutte is easily accessible from the A12 motorway, which runs between Innsbruck and Munich.

How to Get Around in Reutte

Reutte is a compact town, and many of the attractions are within walking distance. Bikes are available for rent, and there is also a well-connected bus system that runs frequently and is an affordable option for those looking to explore the surrounding areas.

Where to Go Next from Reutte: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Reutte is an excellent base camp to explore other nearby destinations and excursions in Tyrol and beyond.

  • Visit the charming towns of Innsbruck and Salzburg.
  • Take a trip to the Zugspitze, the highest peak in Germany, which is only a short drive away.
  • Explore the nearby Tannheimer Tal valley, which has plenty of outdoor adventures to offer.

Conclusion

Reutte is a hidden gem in the heart of Austria, offering a unique blend of natural beauty, cultural heritage, and outdoor adventures. Whether you’re a nature lover, history buff, or adrenaline junkie, Reutte has something to offer for everyone. This comprehensive travel guide introduces you to the town’s best places to visit, outdoor adventures, accommodations, food and drink, and much more. Plan your next trip to Reutte and experience the best of Austrian in one compact town.
