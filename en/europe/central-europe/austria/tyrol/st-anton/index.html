Introduction to St. Anton

Located in Tyrol, Austria, St. Anton is a popular ski resort town famous for its perfect slopes, stunning mountain views, and exciting nightlife. But this charming town is more than just a winter destination. In the summer months, St. Anton transforms into a paradise for outdoor enthusiasts, with excellent hiking, mountain biking, and rock climbing options. The town also boasts rich cultural attractions, local culinary delights, and a warm and welcoming community, making it a must-visit for travelers of all types.

List of Places to Visit in St. Anton

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Museum St. Anton am Arlberg: This museum provides an insight into the region’s history and culture, with displays of traditional clothing, tools, and furniture dating back centuries.
  • Galzigbahn Museum: Learn about the history of skiing in St. Anton, from the first wooden skis to the latest ski equipment.
  • St. Anton Parish Church: A beautiful 17th-century church with stunning frescoes and paintings.

Natural Attractions of St. Anton: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Arlberg Pass: A stunning mountain pass with breathtaking vistas that connects St. Anton to the nearby towns.
  • Rendl Mountain: Take the gondola to the top of Rendl Mountain, where you can enjoy stunning views of the surrounding peaks and valleys.
  • Verwalltal Valley Nature Reserve: Home to endless hiking trails that take you through mountainous terrain, lush green forests, and clear alpine lakes.

Hiking/Biking Routes of St. Anton

  • Verwall Runde: A challenging but stunning hike that takes you through the Verwall Valley Nature Reserve, with stunning views of the Austrian Alps.
  • St. Anton Mountain Bike Trail: A challenging and technical ride with plenty of steep climbs and rocky descents.
  • Gampen Trail: A flowy and fun downhill trail with stunning panoramic views of the surrounding mountains.

Best Things to Do in St. Anton: Activities and Tours

  • Skiing and Snowboarding: In the winter, St. Anton is a paradise for skiers and snowboarders, with over 300 km of slopes and some of the best powder in the Alps.
  • Paragliding: Take to the skies and experience the stunning mountain views from a bird’s eye view.
  • Rock Climbing: St. Anton has plenty of challenging rock climbing routes, with options for all skill levels.

Food and Drink Scene of St. Anton

Must-Try Dishes

  • Kaiserschmarrn: Austria’s famous shredded pancake dessert, served with fruit compote and whipped cream.
  • Tiroler Gröstl: A traditional dish made with fried potatoes, onions, and bacon, served with a fried egg on top.
  • Schnitzel: A thin, breaded and fried cutlet of meat, traditionally pork, served with potatoes and lingonberry sauce.

Local Beverages

  • Glühwein: A warm, spiced mulled wine served during the winter months.
  • Almdudler: A refreshing, non-alcoholic herbal soda.
  • Schnapps: A strong, distilled fruit brandy that is a popular after-dinner drink.

Where to Stay in St. Anton: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Hotel Post: A luxurious hotel with an amazing spa, located in the heart of St. Anton.
  • Nesslerhof: A stylish and modern hotel with a great location near the ski slopes.
  • Haus Bergkristall: A cozy and affordable guesthouse in the heart of St. Anton.

What to Buy in St. Anton: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Traditional Austrian clothing, such as dirndls and lederhosen.
  • Local handmade gifts, such as wooden toys, ceramics, and textiles.
  • Locally produced schnapps and other spirits.

Local Culture and Customs of St. Anton

St. Anton has a rich and unique culture, with traditions that are still alive and celebrated today. Visitors should respect these customs and be aware of the following:

  • Dress appropriately when visiting churches and other religious sites.
  • Greetings are important, and it is customary to shake hands with everyone present when entering a room.
  • The traditional dress code for men is lederhosen and for women is dirndls.

Best Time to Visit St. Anton

The best time to visit St. Anton depends on your preferred activities. If you want to ski or snowboard, the winter months from December to April are the best time to visit. For hiking and mountain biking, the summer months from June to September are ideal.

Do’s and Don’ts of St. Anton

Do:

  • Respect local customs and traditions.
  • Dress appropriately for the weather and activities.
  • Try local cuisine and beverages.

Don’t:

  • Ski or snowboard off-piste without a guide or proper training.
  • Use drones in public spaces without permission.
  • Be loud or disrespectful in quiet areas, such as churches or residential neighborhoods.

Safety and Health

Safety in St. Anton

St. Anton is a relatively safe place to visit, but visitors should be aware of the following:

  • Always carry a map and a charged phone, and let someone know your travel plans.
  • Be aware of the risks of outdoor activities such as hiking and mountain biking.
  • Beware of pickpockets and theft in crowded areas.

Health in St. Anton

With its mountainous terrain and high altitude, St. Anton can present health challenges to some visitors. Visitors should be aware of the following:

  • Altitude sickness is a risk in St. Anton, especially for visitors coming from low-lying areas.
  • The weather can be unpredictable, so visitors should be prepared for sudden changes in temperature and weather conditions.
  • Tap water is safe to drink in St. Anton, but visitors with sensitive stomachs may prefer bottled water.

Getting to St. Anton: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near St. Anton

  • Innsbruck Airport: A 1-hour drive to St. Anton.
  • Friedrichshafen Airport: A 1.5-hour drive to St. Anton.
  • Zurich Airport: A 2.5-hour drive to St. Anton.

Bus Stations Near St. Anton

  • St. Anton am Arlberg Bus Station: Direct connections to Innsbruck, Munich, and other nearby towns.
  • Landeck Bus Station: Direct connections to major cities in Austria, Germany, and Italy.

Other Options

  • Train: St. Anton has a train station with connections to major cities in Austria and beyond.
  • Car: Visitors can rent a car and easily explore the region on their own.

How to Get Around in St. Anton

There are several ways to get around St. Anton:

  • Walking: The town is easily walkable and many attractions are within walking distance.
  • Public transportation: There are local buses that run throughout the town and surrounding areas.
  • Taxis: Taxi services are available in St. Anton.
  • Bike rental: Visitors can rent bikes from several rental shops in town.

Where to Go Next from St. Anton: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Innsbruck: Tyrol’s capital city is only a short drive or train ride from St. Anton and offers stunning architecture, cultural attractions, and amazing shopping.
  • Bregenzerwald: A picturesque alpine region in Vorarlberg that boasts traditional mountain villages, excellent hiking, and delicious local cuisine.
  • Lake Constance: A stunning lake that borders Germany, Austria, and Switzerland, Lake Constance is a great destination for swimming, boating, and exploring charming lake towns.

Conclusion

St. Anton is a destination that truly has it all, from stunning mountain views and exciting outdoor activities to charming cultural attractions and delicious local cuisine. Whether you’re visiting in the winter or the summer, there is always something to see and do in this charming Tyrolean town. With this comprehensive guide, you’ll be able to plan your trip and make the most of your time in St. Anton.
