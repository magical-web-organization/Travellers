Introduction to Hintertux

Nestled in the beautiful Tyrol region of Austria, Hintertux is a must-see destination for those who love the great outdoors. With a range of activities to enjoy, from skiing and snowboarding to hiking and biking, Hintertux caters to all ages and abilities. Here, we provide a comprehensive tour guide to help you make the most of your trip to this stunning Alpine location.

List of Places to Visit in Hintertux

While Hintertux may be a small town, there’s certainly no shortage of things to do and see. Here’s a list of some of the top attractions that you shouldn’t miss:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Tuxer Museum
  • Kirche Tux
  • Swarovski Crystal Worlds

Natural Attractions of Hintertux: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Hintertux Glacier
  • Tuxer Valley Nature Reserve
  • Zillertal Alps National Park

Hiking/Biking Routes of Hintertux

With its stunning Alpine scenery, Hintertux is the perfect place for hiking and biking enthusiasts. Here are some of the top trails to explore:

  • The Tuxerjochhütte Trail
  • The Hintertux Glacier Trail
  • The Tuxer Peaks Trail

Best Things to Do in Hintertux: Activities and Tours

  • Skiing and snowboarding on Hintertux Glacier
  • Sleigh rides
  • Paragliding and tandem flights
  • Mountain biking trails
  • Hiking and mountaineering
  • Zipline and rope courses

Week-Long Itinerary for Hintertux

If you’re planning to stay in Hintertux for a week, here’s a sample itinerary to help you make the most of your time:

Day 1 Arrival and check-in at your chosen accommodation
Day 2 Ski or snowboard on Hintertux Glacier
Day 3 Explore the Tuxer Valley Nature Reserve on foot or by bike
Day 4 Take a scenic train ride to Mayrhofen and walk around the town
Day 5 Visit the Swarovski Crystal Worlds and learn about the history of crystals in Tyrol
Day 6 Take a guided mountain hike
Day 7 Relax and enjoy the spa facilities at your accommodation

Food and Drink Scene of Hintertux

No trip to Hintertux would be complete without indulging in the local cuisine. Here’s an overview of some of the must-try dishes and beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Tiroler Gröstl: A hearty dish made with potatoes, beef, onions, and bacon
  • Kaiserschmarrn: A sweet dish made with shredded pancakes, raisins, and fruit compote
  • Tafelspitz: A boiled beef dish served with root vegetables and horseradish sauce

Local Beverages

  • Almdudler: A popular Austrian soft drink made with herbs and natural flavors
  • Schnapps: A strong, distilled spirit made from fruit, herbs, and spices

What to Buy in Hintertux: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Take a piece of Hintertux back with you by buying some of the local products and souvenirs:

  • Hand-carved wooden crafts
  • Tyrolean hats and woolen clothes
  • Local wines and schnapps

Local Culture and Customs of Hintertux

Hintertux, like much of Austria, has a rich and unique culture. Here are a few customs and traditions you might encounter during your visit:

  • Frühschoppen: An Austrian custom of drinking beer and eating food early in the day on weekends
  • Tyrolean hat dance: A traditional dance performed by men wearing Tyrolean hats and lederhosen
  • Dirndl and lederhosen: Traditional Austrian clothing worn on special occasions

Best Time to Visit Hintertux

The best time to visit Hintertux depends on your interests. For skiing and snowboarding, the winter months of December to April are ideal. For hiking and outdoor activities, the summer months from June to September are perfect.

Do’s and Don’ts of Hintertux

Here are a few do’s and don’ts to keep in mind while exploring Hintertux:

Do:

  • Take advantage of the outdoor activities
  • Try the local cuisine, including schnapps and Almdudler
  • Respect local customs and traditions

Don’t:

  • Forget to bring plenty of warm clothing if visiting in winter
  • Leave valuables unattended
  • Disturb wildlife in the nature reserves and national parks

Safety and Health

Your safety and health are paramount while on holiday in Hintertux.

Safety in Hintertux

  • Be aware of the altitude in the higher areas
  • Keep track of the weather forecast and adjust your plans accordingly
  • Follow all signage and advice around the national parks and mountain trails

Health in Hintertux

  • Drink plenty of water to stay hydrated at higher altitudes
  • Be aware of the signs of altitude sickness and take rest breaks when required
  • Take care when skiing and snowboarding to avoid injury

  • Cable cars and chairlifts
  • Taxis and car rentals
  • Buses and trains

Where to Go Next from Hintertux: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

If you’ve had your fill of Hintertux, here are some nearby destinations and excursions that are worth exploring:

  • Innsbruck, the capital city of the Tyrol province
  • Zell am Ziller, a pretty mountain village known for its traditional architecture
  • The Zillertal High Alpine Road, which boasts stunning views of the surrounding mountains

Conclusion

Whether you’re an adrenaline junkie or simply looking for a relaxing break in stunning surroundings, Hintertux has something for everyone. From hiking and biking to skiing and snowboarding, there are plenty of activities to keep you busy. Add to that the delicious local cuisine, unique customs and traditions, and welcoming locals, and you have the perfect destination for your next European adventure.
