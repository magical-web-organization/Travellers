Introduction to Maurach

Located in the stunning Achen Valley, in the heart of Tyrol, Austria, Maurach is a picturesque village that offers visitors a true taste of authentic Austrian mountain life. It is a popular destination for outdoor enthusiasts and adventure-seekers, with stunning natural landscapes and plenty of activities to keep you busy.

In this comprehensive travel guide, we’ll delve into all that Maurach has to offer, from the best places to visit to the must-try local dishes and insider tips. Whether you’re planning a week-long itinerary or just passing through, this guide has everything you need to make the most of your time in Maurach.

List of Places to Visit in Maurach

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Museum of Tyrolean Farmsteads: This fascinating open-air museum showcases traditional Tyrolean architecture and rural life from the 16th to the 19th centuries.

  • Schloss Tratzberg: This beautifully preserved Renaissance castle offers visitors a glimpse into the grandeur of Austria’s past with its well-preserved paintings, tapestries, and artifacts.

  • Johanneskapelle Maurach: This charming old chapel is a must-see for its beautiful frescoes and impressive architecture.

Natural Attractions of Maurach: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Lake Achen: The largest lake in Tyrol offers visitors stunning vistas and numerous water sports activities like swimming, boating, and fishing.

  • Zwölferkopf Mountain: This mountain offers breathtaking views of the surrounding landscapes and is a popular spot for hiking, climbing, and skiing.

  • Karwendel Nature Reserve: As one of Austria’s largest nature reserves, this area offers visitors a chance to see native animals and birds, as well as a wide range of plant species.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Maurach

  • Achen Promenade: This riverside trail is ideal for walkers, runners, and cyclists who want to enjoy the beautiful landscapes without too much effort.

  • Rofan Mountains: This range offers a range of hiking and biking trails, suitable for all levels of ability, from gentle walks to challenging climbs.

  • Eben Am Achensee to Pertisau: This route takes you through some of the most beautiful natural landscapes in the region and offers fantastic views of the surrounding mountains.

Best Things to Do in Maurach: Activities and Tours

  • Sailing and Boating: Lake Achen is the perfect spot for sailing and boating enthusiasts of all skill levels.

  • Skiing and Snowboarding: Maurach is home to several ski resorts, offering visitors a chance to enjoy some of the best winter sports activities in Austria.

  • Paragliding: For the more adventurous, Maurach offers a range of paragliding options, allowing you to take in the stunning views from above.

Food and Drink Scene of Maurach

Must-Try Dishes

  • Kaiserschmarrn: This traditional pancake dish is usually served with plum compote and is a must-try for anyone visiting Austria.

  • Tiroler Gröstl: This hearty dish consists of fried potatoes, bacon and beef, topped with a fried egg.

  • Sachertorte: This famous Viennese chocolate cake is a real treat and a must-taste for anyone with a sweet tooth.

Local Beverages

  • Zillertal beer: This refreshing local beer is a popular choice among visitors to the region.

  • Schnapps: Made from a range of fruits such as plums, pears, and apricots, schnapps is a traditional Austrian drink enjoyed by locals and visitors alike.

Where to Stay in Maurach: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Hotel Huberhof Maurach: This charming hotel offers guests comfortable rooms, panoramic views, and a range of spa facilities to help you relax after a busy day exploring.

  • Pension Steinbock: This family-run guesthouse offers visitors a home away from home, with cozy rooms and a welcoming atmosphere.

  • JUFA Hotel Achensee: This eco-friendly hotel offers stylish rooms with stunning views of the surrounding landscapes, as well as a range of activities for all ages.

What to Buy in Maurach: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Handicrafts: From wooden toys to traditional clothing, the markets of Maurach offer visitors a chance to take home a genuine piece of Tyrolean culture.

  • Local produce: Whether it’s cheese, schnapps, or honey, the local produce of the region is a great gift for any food lover.

Local Culture and Customs of Maurach

Austria has a rich cultural heritage, and visitors to Maurach will encounter a range of traditions and customs that date back centuries. Visitors are encouraged to respect local customs, dress modestly, and observe local etiquette.

Best Time to Visit Maurach

The best time to visit Maurach depends on what you want to do. Summer is the ideal time for hiking, boating, and outdoor activities, while winter offers world-class skiing and snowboarding opportunities.

Do’s and Don’ts of Maurach

Do:

  • Respect local customs and traditions

  • Dress modestly when visiting churches and other cultural sites

  • Try the local cuisine and take home some traditional souvenirs

Don’t:

  • Be loud or disruptive in public spaces

  • Ignore local etiquette and customs

  • Leave litter or damage the natural environment

Safety and Health

Safety in Maurach

Maurach is a safe destination for visitors, with low crime rates and a welcoming atmosphere. Visitors are reminded to take standard precautions when traveling and to be aware of mountain safety when hiking or skiing.

Health in Maurach

Austria has an excellent healthcare system, and visitors to Maurach should have no trouble finding medical care in case of an emergency. Visitors should make sure they have adequate health insurance when traveling and bring any necessary medication with them.

Getting to Maurach: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Maurach

The closest airports to Maurach are Innsbruck Airport (INN), which is around a 45-minute drive away, and Munich Airport (MUC), which is around a 2.5-hour drive away.

Bus Stations Near Maurach

The nearest bus station to Maurach is Jenbach station, which is around 10km away and can be reached by taxi or local bus.

Other Options

Visitors can also travel to Maurach by train, with Jenbach station serving as a hub for regional and national train connections. Rental cars and taxis are also available from the nearby town of Schwaz.

How to Get Around in Maurach

Maurach is a small village, and most attractions are within walking distance. Visitors can also hire bikes or e-bikes to explore the surrounding area or take a taxi to nearby towns and attractions.

Where to Go Next from Maurach: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Innsbruck: This bustling city is the capital of Tyrol and is home to a range of cultural attractions and historical sites.

  • Kitzbühel: This charming town is famous for its winter sports activities and offers visitors a range of picturesque walks and hikes during the summer months.

  • Salzburg: This beautiful city is a UNESCO World Heritage Site and is renowned for its stunning architecture, music scene, and rich cultural heritage.

Conclusion

Maurach is a beautiful destination that offers visitors a chance to experience the traditional culture and natural beauty of Austria. From stunning scenery and world-class outdoor activities to delicious local cuisine and historic attractions, Maurach truly has something for everyone. Whether you’re seeking adventure, relaxation, or just a taste of traditional Austria, Maurach is a destination you won’t want to miss.
