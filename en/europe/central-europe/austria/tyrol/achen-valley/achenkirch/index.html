Introduction to Achenkirch

Nestled in the heart of Tyrol, Achenkirch is a charming village surrounded by the stunning Alps and breathtaking natural scenery. Located in Achen Valley, Achenkirch is a popular destination for adventure seekers, nature lovers, and cultural enthusiasts alike. This travel guide will take you on a journey through the best places to visit, things to do, where to eat, and stay, to help you make the most out of your trip to Achenkirch.

List of Places to Visit in Achenkirch

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Achenseer Museumswelt: A museum complex comprised of four different museums that explore the culture, history, and traditions of the region.
  • Achenseebahn: A historic cogwheel train that takes you on a scenic ride through the Tyrolean countryside.
  • Augustinian Museum: A museum showcasing the history and art of the Augustinian Monastery.

Natural Attractions of Achenkirch: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Achensee: A crystal-clear lake surrounded by towering mountains that offer a range of activities, including swimming, fishing, boating, and hiking.
  • Rofan Mountains: A range of mountains that offer stunning panoramic views and hiking trails for all levels of experience.
  • Karwendel Mountains: Another range of mountains that offer breathtaking views and a variety of outdoor activities, from hiking to skiing.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Achenkirch

  • Rundweg Pertisau-Achenkirch: A scenic hiking trail that takes you from the village of Pertisau to Achenkirch while offering beautiful views of the Achensee and the mountains.
  • Tiefenbachklamm: A stunning gorge with hiking trails and waterfalls.
  • Bike Trail Achensee: A popular mountain bike trail that takes you on a scenic journey through the Tyrolean countryside.

Best Things to Do in Achenkirch: Activities and Tours

  • Water Sports: Take a dip in the crystal-clear waters of Achensee and indulge in various water sports such as Stand-up paddleboarding, kitesurfing, and windsurfing.
  • Skiing: Hit the slopes in winter and enjoy skiing, snowboarding, and other winter activities in the Karwendel Mountains.
  • Achensee Adventure Park: A fun-filled adventure park featuring a high ropes course and a giant swing that offers breathtaking views of the lake.

Week-Long Itinerary for Achenkirch

Day 1: Explore the Achenseer Museumswelt and the Augustinian Museum and learn about the history and culture of the region.

Day 2: Take a scenic ride on the Achenseebahn and enjoy the stunning views of the Tyrolean countryside.

Day 3-4: Hit the hiking trails of the Rofan Mountains and the Tiefenbachklamm and indulge in outdoor activities.

Day 5: Experience the scenic beauty of the Achensee on a boat ride and indulge in water sports.

Day 6: Hit the slopes in the Karwendel Mountains and indulge in winter activities.

Day 7: Visit the Achensee Adventure Park and have a fun-filled day with family and friends.

Food and Drink Scene of Achenkirch

Must-Try Dishes

  • Tiroler Gröstl: A delicious dish made of potatoes, beef, and onions.
  • Kaiserschmarrn: A sweet dish made of shredded pancakes, raisins, and almonds.

Local Beverages

  • Achensee Beer: A locally brewed beer made from natural ingredients and spring water from the Achensee.
  • Obstler: A potent fruit brandy made from local fruits such as apples, pears, and cherries.

Where to Stay in Achenkirch: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Pirchner Hof: A wellness hotel that offers a range of facilities such as a spa, sauna, and outdoor pool.
  • Gasthof St. Hubertus: A cozy guesthouse located in the heart of the village.
  • Jugendherberge Achenkirch: A budget-friendly hostel located in a historic building surrounded by the mountains.

What to Buy in Achenkirch: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Tyrolean Cheese: A variety of handcrafted cheese made in the region’s mountain dairies.
  • Handmade woolen products: A range of woolen products such as socks, sweaters, and hats made by local artisans.

Local Culture and Customs of Achenkirch

  • Music: Tyrolean folklore music is an important part of the local culture, and you can experience it at various traditional events and festivals.
  • Costume: The Tyrolean costume, with its lederhosen and dirndl dresses, is a distinctive feature of the region’s culture.

Best Time to Visit Achenkirch

The best times to visit Achenkirch are during the summer months (June-August) and winter (December-March) when the weather is ideal for outdoor activities and the region’s natural beauty is at its peak.

Do’s and Don’ts of Achenkirch

Do:

  • Explore the outdoor activities and scenic beauty of the region.
  • Try the local food and beverages.
  • Respect the local culture and customs.

Don’t:

  • Leave your trash in the mountains or near the lake.
  • Bring pets to the trails and hiking areas.

Safety and Health

Safety in Achenkirch

  • Always follow all safety instructions while indulging in outdoor activities.
  • Check the weather forecast before heading out for any outdoor activity.

Health in Achenkirch

  • The water in the Achensee is safe for drinking.
  • The area has excellent healthcare facilities and hospitals.

Getting to Achenkirch: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Achenkirch

  • Innsbruck Airport: 42 miles away

Bus Stations Near Achenkirch

  • Bus 4074 runs regularly between Jenbach and Achenkirch.

Other Options

  • Train: Take a train to Jenbach and then hop on a bus that runs regularly between Jenbach and Achenkirch.
  • Taxi: Taxis are available from Innsbruck Airport, and Jenbach train station.

How to Get Around in Achenkirch

  • Walking: The village of Achenkirch is small and can easily be explored on foot.
  • Biking: Rent a bike and explore the trails around the Achensee.
  • Car: Rent a car, and explore the surrounding regions at your own pace.

Where to Go Next from Achenkirch: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Salzburg: A charming city with a UNESCO-listed historic center and famous for its music and culture.
  • Zell am See: A picturesque mountain town located on the shores of Lake Zell and surrounded by beautiful mountains.

Conclusion

Achenkirch is a charming village with stunning natural beauty and a rich cultural heritage. Whether you are an adventure seeker or a cultural enthusiast, Achenkirch has something to offer for everyone. With this comprehensive travel guide, you can plan your next trip to Achenkirch and make the most out of your visit to this beautiful region.
