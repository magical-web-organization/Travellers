Introduction to Achen Valley

Perched high in the spectacular Tyrol region of Austria, Achen Valley is renowned for its breathtaking natural beauty, charming alpine villages, and rich cultural heritage. Whether you’re seeking outdoor adventures, want to soak up some history and culture, or simply want to enjoy some relaxation amid the stunning surroundings, Achen Valley is the perfect destination. In this comprehensive travel guide, we’ll explore the best places to visit, things to do, where to stay, and local food and culture in Achen Valley.

List of Places to Visit in Achen Valley

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Archäologisches Museum Inntal: This museum offers a fascinating glimpse into the history of the Tyrol region, from prehistoric times to the present day.
  • Kaiserhaus: Built in the 19th century, this grand palace was once the summer residence of Emperor Franz Joseph.
  • St. Georgenberg-Fiecht Abbey: Dating back to the 12th century, this stunning abbey boasts some of the finest architecture in the Tyrol region.

Natural Attractions of Achen Valley: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Achensee Lake: Surrounded by majestic mountains, Achensee is one of the most beautiful lakes in Austria and offers plenty of opportunities for water sports and outdoor activities.
  • Rofan Mountains: This rugged mountain range is perfect for hiking, mountain biking, and skiing.
  • Karwendel Mountains: With its stunning peaks and valleys, the Karwendel Mountains are a paradise for hikers and climbers.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Achen Valley

  • Rofan Circuit: This popular hiking trail offers stunning views of the Rofan Mountains and Achensee Lake.
  • Hinterautal Valley: This gentle, family-friendly hike is perfect for those looking to enjoy the natural beauty of Achen Valley without too much exertion.
  • MTB Karwendel Circuit: This challenging mountain biking route takes you through some of the most spectacular scenery in the region.

Best Things to Do in Achen Valley: Activities and Tours

  • Take a boat tour of Achensee Lake.
  • Visit the Rattenberg Castle.
  • Explore the Hundalm Ice Cave.

Food and Drink Scene of Achen Valley

Must-Try Dishes

  • Tiroler Gröstl: A hearty dish consisting of potatoes, onions, and bacon served with a fried egg on top.
  • Kaiserschmarrn: A sweet dish made with shredded pancakes, raisins, and plum compote.
  • Apfelstrudel: A classic Austrian dessert made with sweet pastry and filled with spiced apples.

Local Beverages

  • Schnapps: A potent liquor made with fruit or herbs - the perfect way to warm up on a cold winter night.
  • Almdudler: A refreshing herbal lemonade that’s popular throughout Austria.
  • Tyrolean Beer: Produced locally with natural ingredients, Tyrolean beer is a must-try for any beer lover.

Where to Stay in Achen Valley: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Hotel Achensee: Located right on the shores of Achensee Lake, this charming hotel boasts stunning views and top-notch amenities.
  • Gasthof St. Georg: This traditional guesthouse offers comfortable accommodation and delicious local cuisine.
  • Youth Hostel Jenbach: A budget-friendly option for backpackers and solo travelers.

What to Buy in Achen Valley: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Handmade ceramics and pottery from Rattenberg.
  • Local schnapps, beer, and wine.
  • Traditional Austrian clothing and accessories, like the dirndl or trachten jacket.

Local Culture and Customs of Achen Valley

Achen Valley has a rich cultural heritage, with traditions that are still celebrated today. Some of the most significant cultural events include:

  • The Tyrolean Farmers’ Market, held every other Saturday in Jenbach, where you can sample and buy local produce.
  • The Achensee Steam Cog Railway, a historic railway that still runs today and offers stunning scenic views.
  • The Tyrolean Schützenfest, a festival that celebrates the region’s shooting culture and marks the beginning of summer.

Best Time to Visit Achen Valley

The best time to visit Achen Valley depends on what you plan to do there. For outdoor activities like hiking and mountain biking, the summer months from June to September offer the best weather and conditions. For winter sports like skiing and snowboarding, the months from December to April are ideal. Spring and autumn are also great times to visit, with fewer crowds and mild temperatures.

Do’s and Don’ts of Achen Valley

Do:

  • Take your time to appreciate the stunning natural beauty that surrounds you.
  • Respect local customs and traditions.
  • Try the local food and drink.

Don’t:

  • Ignore safety signs when hiking or skiing.
  • Wear inappropriate clothing in churches or other religious sites.
  • Litter or damage the natural environment.

Safety and Health

Safety in Achen Valley

While Achen Valley is a relatively safe destination, it’s important to be aware of potential hazards when enjoying outdoor activities like hiking and skiing. Always follow posted safety signs, stay on marked trails, and bring appropriate gear and clothing.

Health in Achen Valley

There are no specific health concerns for visitors to Achen Valley. However, it’s always a good idea to have travel insurance and to consult with a doctor before traveling to ensure you are up-to-date with any necessary vaccinations.

Getting to Achen Valley: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Achen Valley

The nearest airports to Achen Valley are Innsbruck Airport (INN) and Salzburg Airport (SZG), both of which offer frequent flights to major European cities.

Bus Stations Near Achen Valley

The bus station in Jenbach is the main transport hub for the region and offers connections to a range of destinations throughout the Tyrol region and beyond.

Other Options

If you prefer to travel by train, the Achensee Steam Cog Railway is a scenic and historic option. If you’re driving, Achen Valley is easily accessible via the A12 motorway.

How to Get Around in Achen Valley

The best way to get around Achen Valley is by car or by bike. Many of the region’s best attractions and scenic routes are easily accessed by car, and bike rentals are available throughout the area.

Where to Go Next from Achen Valley: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Innsbruck: The capital of the Tyrol region is just a short drive from Achen Valley and offers plenty of cultural attractions and outdoor opportunities.
  • Zillertal Valley: This beautiful region to the east of Achen Valley is renowned for its stunning alpine scenery and outdoor adventures.
  • Salzburg: The birthplace of Mozart and one of Austria’s most beautiful cities, Salzburg is just a few hours’ drive from Achen Valley.

Conclusion

Achen Valley is a truly magical destination that combines stunning natural beauty with rich cultural heritage and plenty of opportunities for outdoor adventures. From hiking and mountain biking to exploring local museums and savoring delicious traditional cuisine, there’s something for everyone in Achen Valley. Whether you’re planning a short visit or a longer stay, we hope this comprehensive travel guide has helped you discover the best that Achen Valley has to offer.
