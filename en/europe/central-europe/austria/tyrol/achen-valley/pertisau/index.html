Introduction to Pertisau

Nestled in the Achen Valley, in the heart of the Tyrol region of Austria, Pertisau is a charming village that offers stunning natural scenery and a range of activities for visitors. The village is situated on the shores of the Achensee, the largest lake in the region, and is surrounded by towering mountains and forests.

List of Places to Visit in Pertisau

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Pertisau is rich in history and culture, with several museums and historical sites that are worth visiting, including:

  • Achenseer Museumswelt: This museum complex showcases the history, culture, and traditions of the Tyrol region and includes exhibits on agriculture, handicrafts, and local customs.

  • The Pertisau Parish Church: This beautiful church dates back to the 17th century and features stunning frescoes and artwork.

Natural Attractions of Pertisau: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Pertisau is surrounded by some of the most breathtaking natural scenery in Europe, offering endless opportunities for outdoor adventures and scenic views. Some of the must-visit spots include:

  • Karwendel Nature Park: This vast nature reserve is home to some of the most incredible mountain landscapes in the Tyrol region, with rugged peaks, deep valleys, and crystal-clear alpine lakes.

  • Rofan Mountain: This stunning peak offers incredible views of the surrounding mountains and valleys and can be accessed via a cable car from Pertisau.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Pertisau

Pertisau is surrounded by some of the most scenic hiking and biking trails in Austria, with varying degrees of difficulty and terrain. Some of the top routes include:

  • Lake Achensee Circular Trail: This paved trail circles the entire lake and is suited for an easy bike ride or leisurely walk, with beautiful views of the lake and surrounding mountains.

  • Pertisau to Achenkirch Hike: This popular hiking route takes visitors through lush forests, past crystal-clear streams and offers stunning views of the Rofan and Karwendel mountain ranges.

Best Things to Do in Pertisau: Activities and Tours

Pertisau offers visitors a wide range of activities to enjoy, from outdoor adventures to cultural experiences. Some of the best things to do in Pertisau include:

  • Boat Trips on Lake Achensee: Visitors can take a scenic boat ride on the lake, which offers incredible views of the surrounding mountains and forests.

  • Nordic Walking: Pertisau has a number of dedicated Nordic walking trails, which allow visitors to explore the village and its surroundings at their own pace.

Week-Long Itinerary for Pertisau

To make the most of your time in Pertisau, consider following this week-long itinerary:

Day 1: Explore the village, visit local museums and historical sites.

Day 2: Take a boat ride on Lake Achensee and hike to the summit of Rofan Mountain.

Day 3: Enjoy a leisurely day at the beach, swimming and sunbathing on the shores of Lake Achensee.

Day 4: Take a day trip to Innsbruck, the capital of the Tyrol region and a city rich in history and culture.

Day 5: Go on a guided mountain biking tour through the Karwendel Nature Park.

Day 6: Spend the day exploring the forests and alpine meadows around Pertisau on a scenic hiking trail.

Day 7: Relax and enjoy the local food and drink scene, sampling traditional Tyrolean dishes and local beverages.

Food and Drink Scene of Pertisau

Tyrolean cuisine is known for its hearty, flavorful dishes and excellent local produce. Some of the must-try dishes and drinks in Pertisau include:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Tiroler Gröstl: This hearty dish is made with fried potatoes, caramelized onions, and bacon, topped with a fried egg.

  • Kaiserschmarrn: This classic Austrian dessert is made with shredded pancakes, raisins, and apple sauce, and is perfect for sharing.

Local Beverages

  • Achensee Beer: This local beer is brewed using water from Lake Achensee and is known for its crisp, refreshing taste.

  • Schlehenfeuer: This fiery digestif is made from sloe berries and distilled vodka and is the perfect way to warm up after a day on the slopes.

Where to Stay in Pertisau: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Pertisau has a variety of accommodations to suit travelers of all budgets, from cozy guesthouses to luxurious hotels. Some of the top options include:

  • Travel Charme Hotel: This elegant hotel is located on the shores of Lake Achensee and offers stunning views of the surrounding mountains and forests.

  • Pension Alpengruß: This family-run guesthouse offers comfortable rooms and a warm, welcoming atmosphere.

What to Buy in Pertisau: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Pertisau is known for its excellent local produce, including cheese, honey, and sausages. Visitors can also find a variety of handmade crafts and souvenirs, including:

  • Hand-carved wooden figurines and toys

  • Local ceramics and pottery

Local Culture and Customs of Pertisau

Tyrolean culture is rich and diverse, with a focus on traditional music, dress, and customs. Some of the key cultural events and traditions in Pertisau include:

  • The Achensee-Pertisau Trumpet Festival: This annual festival celebrates the region’s rich brass band culture and features performances and parades by local bands.

Best Time to Visit Pertisau

The best time to visit Pertisau is in the summer months (June - August), when the weather is warm and sunny, and outdoor activities like hiking and biking are at their best. Winter sports enthusiasts will want to visit in the colder months (December - March), when the region is transformed into a winter wonderland and offers a range of activities like skiing, snowboarding, and ice skating.

Safety and Health

Safety in Pertisau

Pertisau is generally a safe and peaceful village, with a low crime rate. Visitors should still exercise caution and not leave valuables unattended.

Health in Pertisau

There are no specific health risks associated with traveling to Pertisau, but visitors should take standard travel precautions, including staying hydrated and avoiding excessive alcohol consumption.

Getting to Pertisau: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Pertisau

  • Innsbruck Airport: This is the closest airport to Pertisau, located approximately 40 kilometers away.

Bus Stations Near Pertisau

  • Pertisau Bus Station: This is the main bus station in the village, with connections to Innsbruck and other nearby towns and villages.

Other Options

Visitors can also rent a car or take a taxi from the airport or train station to Pertisau.

How to Get Around in Pertisau

Pertisau is a small village, and most sights and activities can be reached on foot or by bike. Visitors can also use the local bus service or rent a car to explore the surrounding areas.

Where to Go Next from Pertisau: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Visitors to Pertisau can also visit nearby towns and villages, including:

  • Innsbruck: The capital of the Tyrol region and a city rich in culture and history.

  • Zell am Ziller: A picturesque alpine village known for its winter sports and outdoor activities.

Conclusion

Pertisau is a hidden gem in the Tyrol region of Austria, offering visitors a range of outdoor activities, cultural experiences, and excellent local cuisine. With this comprehensive guide, you should have all the information you need to plan your trip and make the most of your time in Pertisau.
