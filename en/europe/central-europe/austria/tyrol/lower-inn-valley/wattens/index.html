Introduction to Wattens

Located in the Lower Inn Valley, in Tyrol, Austria, Wattens is a charming small town surrounded by beautiful mountains, crystal-clear lakes, and lush forests. The town is known for its rich cultural heritage, outdoor adventures, and world-renowned art installation - the Swarovski Crystal Worlds. With so many attractions, Wattens is a must-visit destination for travelers who crave a mix of nature, culture, and adventure.

List of Places to Visit in Wattens

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Swarovski Crystal Worlds: A unique art museum showcasing the magnificent works of crystal and art installations designed by world-famous artists. The museum is a wonderland of art, light, and sound, perfect for visitors of all ages.
  • Haus Steht Kopf: A quirky attraction where everything is upside down - the furniture, the walls, and even the car parked outside. This house offers an entertaining experience for the whole family.
  • St. Laurentius church: A stunning baroque-style church built-in 1442, housing impressive religious art and beautiful frescoes.

Natural Attractions of Wattens: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Wattens Trek: A 42 km hiking trail, surrounded by picturesque scenery, clean air, and vast meadows. This hike is perfect for nature lovers and families, as it is easily accessible and has a well-marked pathway.
  • Central Inn Valley Nature Park: A vast protected area with snow-capped peaks, alpine lakes, varied fauna, and flora, an ideal spot for wildlife lovers and nature enthusiasts.
  • Breitenbach waterfalls: A series of eight waterfalls descending over inclined rocks, offering a beautiful sight to behold. It is an excellent place for a picnic or photography.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Wattens

Wattens offers some of the best hiking and mountain biking trails in Austria. Whether you’re a beginner or an experienced trekker, Wattens has something for everyone. Some popular hikes and bike routes are:

  • The Zirbenweg Trail: A 7-kilometer-long hiking trail along the ridges of the Patscherkofel mountain, where you’ll see stunning views of the Inn Valley.
  • Karwendel Nature Park: A 727 km² nature park with excellent hiking and mountain biking trails, stunning valleys, and majestic peaks.
  • Inntal cycle path: A 250 km cycling route along the Inn River, passing through beautiful landscapes and traditional villages.

Best Things to Do in Wattens: Activities and Tours

  • Visit the Swarovski Crystal Worlds Museum: The main attraction in Wattens, Swarovski Crystal Worlds Museum is a world of words wonder, perfect for all ages and a must-visit experience while in Wattens.
  • Explore the Tyrolean Heritage Museum: A fascinating museum showcasing the history of the Tyrol region, showcasing folk art, costumes and sharing the stories of how the Tyrolean people lived in the past.
  • Try Local Wines: Tyrol is known for its wines, especially its white wines. Take a guided tour around the vineyards and sample some of the wines.
  • Learn a new craft at the Creative Craft Workshops: Try your hand at making traditional Tyrolean crafts and learn several new skills.

Week-Long Itinerary for Wattens

Day Itinerary
Day 1 Tour the Swarovski Crystal Worlds Museum, watch the light and sound show, and enjoy the outdoor park.
Day 2 Explore the Old Town, visit the St. Laurentius church and the Haus Steht Kopf.

Food and Drink Scene of Wattens

Must-Try Dishes

  • Tiroler Gröstl: A dish made with fried potatoes, bacon, onions, and sliced beef, typically served with a fried egg.
  • Kaspressknödel: Cheese dumpling made with bread and cheese; one of the popular dishes in Tyrol.
  • Germknödel: A dessert made from sweet dough with a filling of plum jam and topped with vanilla sauce and poppy seeds.

Local Beverages

  • Zirbenschnapps: A liqueur made from the cones of the dwarf pine tree, which is abundant in the Tyrol region.
  • Tyrolean White Wine: Tyrolean white wines have a unique taste thanks to the cool climate and the area’s distinct geological makeup. The wine is refreshing and crisp, a must-try for wine lovers.

Where to Stay in Wattens: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Hotel Goldener Adler: Located in the heart of Wattens, this hotel serves an array of international and Tyrolean cuisine and offers an indoor pool, sauna, and steam bathroom.
  • Haus Fabro: This guesthouse is located near the Swarovski Crystal Worlds Museum, and it offers comfortably furnished rooms at a budget-friendly price.
  • Hostel Nabla: A hostel located near the river Inn, offering comfortable dorms and private rooms, a restaurant serving excellent food, and a rooftop terrace with stunning views of the region.

What to Buy in Wattens: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Swarovski Crystal: You can purchase Swarovski Crystal products and souvenirs from the Swarovski Crystal Worlds Museum.
  • Zirbenschnapps: A unique souvenir that you can buy from any of the local stores in Wattens.
  • Tyrolean-style Clothing: You can purchase traditional Tyrolean clothing, hand-crafted souvenirs, and other local products from any of the local stores or markets.

Local Culture and Customs of Wattens

The Tyrolean people are known for their relaxed lifestyle and traditional values. Visitors are expected to respect their customs and culture during their stay. It is customary to greet people with a simple “Gruess Gott” (“Hello” in Tyrolean) while walking around town. It is also common to remove shoes before entering someone’s home. The Tyrolean people appreciate punctuality and expect visitors to arrive on time for appointments.

Best Time to Visit Wattens

The best time to visit Wattens is between May and September when the temperature is comfortable, and the days are long. Summer is also the peak tourist season, so be sure to book your accommodations in advance. It’s also best to avoid the winter season, as the area’s roads can be quite treacherous and prevent easy exploration of the area.

Do’s and Don’ts of Wattens

Do:

  • Wear comfortable shoes for exploring the town, and bring along a sweater or jacket for the cool evenings.
  • Respect the local customs and culture, and be polite to the locals.
  • Try the local dishes, white wines, and the unique Zirbenschnapps.

Don’t:

  • Don’t litter while exploring the natural attractions and hiking trails.
  • Don’t be late for appointments as the Tyrolean people appreciate punctuality.
  • Don’t be loud or boisterous in public places and respect the country’s rules and regulations.

Safety and Health

Safety in Wattens

Wattens is a safe town to visit, and there are no significant safety concerns. However, as a visitor, it’s advisable to safeguard your belongings, and be aware of your surroundings while exploring the area.

Health in Wattens

There is no major health concern while traveling in Wattens, but as with any trip, it is advisable to have adequate travel insurance coverage in case of any medical emergency.

Getting to Wattens: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Wattens

The nearest airport is Innsbruck Airport, which is around 20 km from Wattens town center. You can take a taxi, hire a rental car, or take the bus or train service to Wattens from the airport.

Bus Stations Near Wattens

Wattens is connected with several neighboring towns, and there is an excellent public bus service to/from the town center. Bus services are frequent, and they run throughout the day.

Other Options

You can also take the train from Innsbruck to Wattens. The rail connections in Austria are reliable, and the trains run regularly. Wattens is well connected with Salzburg and Vienna as well. Taxis and rental cars are also excellent alternatives for travelers who want to explore the region on their own.

How to Get Around in Wattens

Wattens is a small town, and you can easily explore it on foot or by cycle. Local buses are available for visitors who want to venture around the town’s outskirts or neighboring towns. Taxis and car rentals are available for travelers who want to explore the region on their own.

Where to Go Next from Wattens: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Innsbruck: The capital of Tyrol, Innsbruck is a popular tourist destination known for its medieval architecture, museums, and beautiful alpine scenery.
  • Hall in Tirol: A charming, small town in the Tyrolean region, famous for its historic architecture and museums.
  • Rattenberg: A lovely, quaint town known for its charming streets, glass crafts, and summer festivals.

Conclusion

Wattens is an excellent destination for travelers who want to experience the natural beauty of the Tyrolean region. With a plethora of outdoor activities, cultural attractions, and traditional cuisine, Wattens has something for all travelers. Don’t forget to add Wattens to your bucket list and experience Austria’s fascinating landscapes and traditions!
