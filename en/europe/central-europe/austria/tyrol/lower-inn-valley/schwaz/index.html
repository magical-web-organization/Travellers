Introduction to Schwaz

Nestled in the heart of Lower Inn Valley, in the picturesque region of Tyrol, lies the magical town of Schwaz. With its rich cultural heritage, stunning natural beauty, and delicious cuisine, Schwaz is a must-visit destination for all those seeking a truly authentic Austrian experience. Whether you’re a history buff, a foodie, an outdoors enthusiast or simply looking for a quiet retreat, Schwaz has something to offer everyone.

List of Places to Visit in Schwaz

Schwaz offers a plethora of cultural and natural attractions to its visitors. Here’s a list of must-visit places in Schwaz:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Schwaz Silver Mine: Take an underground tour of one of the world’s largest silver mines and learn about the town’s rich mining history.
  • Schwaz Parish Church: Admire the stunning Baroque architecture of this 15th-century church, which is one of the most iconic landmarks of Schwaz.
  • Schwaz Castle: Explore the ruins of this medieval castle built in the 13th century, learn about its past, and enjoy breathtaking views of the town.

Natural Attractions of Schwaz: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Kellerjoch: Go for a scenic hike on this beautiful mountain, which offers stunning views of the surrounding valleys and mountains.
  • Zillertal Alps: Explore the stunning beauty of one of the highest mountain ranges in the Eastern Alps through hiking and mountaineering.
  • Achensee Lake: Enjoy a variety of water sports, including swimming, sailing, and kayaking in the pristine waters of this beautiful lake.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Schwaz

Schwaz is a paradise for hikers and bikers, offering a wide range of trails with different difficulty levels. Some of the popular hiking and biking routes include:

  • Via Claudia Augusta: Follow in the footsteps of ancient Roman traders as you hike or bike along this famous route that connects Italy with Germany.
  • Inntal Cycle Path: Take a leisurely bike ride along the river Inn and enjoy stunning views of the surrounding landscape.
  • Hallerangerhaus Hut Trail: Hike through lush green forests and mountain meadows to reach the Hallerangerhaus hut, which offers breathtaking views of the Zillertal Alps.

Best Things to Do in Schwaz: Activities and Tours

In addition to hiking, biking, and exploring museums and historical places, there are many other fun things to do in Schwaz. Here are some of the top activities and tours:

  • Swarovski Crystal Worlds: Discover the fascinating world of crystal art and design at this iconic museum.
  • Tyrolean Cuisine Cooking Class: Learn the art of cooking traditional Tyrolean dishes at a local cooking class and savor the delicious local delicacies.
  • Paragliding: Experience the thrill of flying like a bird and enjoy stunning views of the Tyrolean Alps.

Week-Long Itinerary for Schwaz

Here’s a suggested itinerary for a week-long trip to Schwaz:

Day 1 Arrival in Schwaz, check-in at hotel, and self-guided walking tour of the town
Day 2 Visit Schwaz Silver Mine and explore the town’s historical places
Day 3 Hike or bike on the Kellerjoch mountain and enjoy stunning views of the surrounding region
Day 4 Take a day trip to Innsbruck and explore the city’s cultural, historical, and natural attractions
Day 5 Visit Swarovski Crystal Worlds and take a cooking class to learn traditional Tyrolean cuisine
Day 6 Visit the Hallerangerhaus hut and enjoy breathtaking views of the Zillertal Alps
Day 7 Relax and unwind, or take a paragliding tour for a bird’s eye view of the town and region

Food and Drink Scene of Schwaz

No trip to Schwaz is complete without sampling its delicious cuisine and local beverages. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Tiroler Gröstl: This traditional Tyrolean dish is a delicious blend of roasted potatoes, bacon, and onions, topped with a sunny-side-up egg.
  • Kaiserschmarrn: A fluffy, caramelized pancake served with fruit compote, Kaiserschmarrn is a popular dessert among locals and visitors alike.
  • Schnitzel: One of Austria’s most iconic dishes, schnitzel is a breaded and fried meat cutlet, usually made from veal or pork.

Local Beverages

  • Almdudler: This refreshing non-alcoholic drink is a blend of Alpine herbs and sparkling water and is the perfect drink to quench your thirst on a hot summer day.
  • Stiegl Beer: Produced and brewed in Salzburg, Stiegl beer is one of Austria’s most popular beers and is widely available in bars and restaurants throughout the country.
  • Jägermeister: Originally produced in Germany, Jägermeister is a popular herbal liqueur and is often drunk as a digestif after dinner.

Where to Stay in Schwaz: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Schwaz offers a wide range of accommodation options to suit all budgets and preferences. Some of the most popular options include:

  • Hotel Post Schwaz: This elegant four-star hotel is located in the heart of the town and offers spacious rooms, a spa, and a restaurant serving traditional Tyrolean cuisine.
  • Jugendherberge Schwaz: This budget-friendly hostel is just a short walk from the town center and offers dormitories and private rooms, as well as a communal kitchen and lounge.
  • Gasthof Einhorn Schaller: This cozy guesthouse is located in the picturesque village of Pill, just a short drive from Schwaz, and offers comfortable rooms and a restaurant serving delicious local cuisine.

What to Buy in Schwaz: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

There are many unique souvenirs and local products to take home from Schwaz, including:

  • Swarovski Crystal Art: Buy beautiful crystal pieces from one of the most iconic museums in the world.
  • Tyrolean Hats and Costumes: Tyrol is famous for its traditional hats and costumes, and you can find a wide variety of them in Schwaz.
  • Handmade Wooden Crafts: Purchase handmade wooden crafts, including picture frames, crosses, and other home decor items, which are widely available in local shops and markets.

Local Culture and Customs of Schwaz

Schwaz is deeply rooted in its Tyrolean heritage and has preserved many of its cultural customs and traditions. One of the most famous customs is the “Krampuslauf,” a traditional festival held in December, where people dress up as Krampus, a horned demon figure from Austrian folklore, and parade through the town. Other customs include traditional Tyrolean costumes, rustic music, and folk dancing.

Best Time to Visit Schwaz

The best time to visit Schwaz depends on your preferences and interests. Peak season, from June to September, offers warm and sunny weather, ideal for outdoor activities. However, this is also the busiest time of the year, and prices may be higher. Winter, from December to February, offers a magical experience, with snow-capped mountains, Christmas markets, and skiing opportunities. Spring and fall offer moderate temperatures, fewer crowds, and lower prices, perfect for exploring the town’s cultural and historical attractions.

Do’s and Don’ts of Schwaz

To make the most of your trip to Schwaz, here are some do’s and don’ts:

Do:

  • Dress warm and bring appropriate gear when visiting in winter.
  • Respect the local culture and customs.
  • Try local cuisine and beverages in local restaurants and cafes.

Don’t:

  • Ignore safety precautions, especially when engaging in outdoor activities.
  • Wear inappropriate clothing when visiting churches and other religious sites.
  • Rely solely on credit cards for transactions, as many smaller businesses prefer cash payments.

Safety and Health

Safety in Schwaz

Schwaz is generally a safe town for visitors. However, it’s always advisable to exercise caution and be aware of your surroundings, especially when out hiking or engaging in other outdoor activities. If you’re driving, be aware of the narrow, winding roads in the region, and keep an eye out for wildlife.

Health in Schwaz

Austria has an excellent healthcare system, and Schwaz is no exception. There are several hospitals, clinics, and pharmacies in the town, and most medical professionals speak English. It’s always advisable to have travel insurance in case of medical emergencies.

Getting to Schwaz: Transportation Options and Logistics

Schwaz is located approximately 30 km east of Innsbruck, the capital city of Tyrol. Here are some transportation options to get to Schwaz:

Airports Near Schwaz

  • Innsbruck Airport: The nearest international airport to Schwaz, located approximately 35 km to the west.
  • Munich Airport: Located approximately 120 km to the north, Munich Airport is one of the busiest airports in Europe.

Bus Stations Near Schwaz

  • Schwaz Autobus Terminal: Located in the town center, this bus terminal offers connections to various destinations within Tyrol, Salzburg, and Bavaria.
  • Innsbrucker Verkehrsbetriebe und Stubaitalbahn GmbH (IVB): This is the main public transport company in Innsbruck, offering connections to various destinations in the region.

Other Options

  • Train: There are several train stations in the region, with connections to various destinations in Austria and Germany.
  • Car: Schwaz is easily accessible by car, with good road connections to other towns and cities in the region.

How to Get Around in Schwaz

Schwaz is a small town, and most attractions are within walking distance. However, if you want to explore the surrounding areas, there are many public transport options available, including buses and trains. Taxis and car rentals are also readily available.

Where to Go Next from Schwaz: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Schwaz is an excellent base for exploring the surrounding regions. Some popular nearby destinations and excursions include:

  • Innsbruck: The capital city of Tyrol, Innsbruck, is just a short drive from Schwaz and offers a variety of cultural, historical, and natural attractions.
  • Zillertal Valley: Located just south of Schwaz, the Zillertal Valley is a paradise for hikers and skiers, offering stunning mountain scenery year-round.
  • Hall in Tirol: Located just a short drive from Schwaz, the charming town of Hall in Tirol is a cultural and historical treasure, with a well-preserved old town and many museums and galleries.

Conclusion

Schwaz is a beautiful, culturally rich town located in the heart of Tyrol, Austria. With its stunning natural beauty, delicious cuisine, and plethora of cultural and outdoor attractions, it’s no wonder that Schwaz has become a popular tourist destination. We hope that this comprehensive travel guide has given you all the information you need to plan your next trip to Schwaz, and we can’t wait for you to experience this magical town for yourself!
