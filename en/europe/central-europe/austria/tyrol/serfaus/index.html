Introduction to Serfaus

Serfaus is a charming village nestled in the heart of Tyrol in Austria. Surrounded by snow-capped mountains and picturesque landscapes, it is a popular holiday destination that offers a perfect blend of outdoor adventures and cultural experiences. With a plethora of activities to choose from, Serfaus can make for an unforgettable trip.

List of Places to Visit in Serfaus

If you are planning to visit Serfaus, then make sure you check out these top attractions:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Serfaus Museum: Located in the town center, the Serfaus Museum offers insights into the history, culture, and way of life of Serfaus. It features traditional handicrafts, regional costumes, and exhibits on the village’s development.
  • Cthe Via Claudia Augusta: This well-preserved Roman road that once connected Venice and Augsburg passes right through Serfaus. Take a walk along the historic route and discover ancient ruins and relics.
  • St. Georg’s Chapel: This stunning, 18th-century landmark is a beautiful example of the Tyrolean baroque style. Take a moment to appreciate its elaborate frescoes and ornate altar.

Natural Attractions of Serfaus: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Seresa and See Snow Lake: Serfaus boasts two beautiful mountain lakes, Seresa and See, which are often referred to as the “Pearls of Tyrol.” Surrounded by verdant landscapes and snow-capped peaks, these lakes offer a peaceful retreat.
  • Bikepark Serfaus-Fiss-Ladis: Ride the gondola up to the Bikepark for panoramic views of the valley and adrenaline-pumping runs. Try out the 7 trails, suitable for beginner to advanced riders and suitable for all ages.
  • Hiking Trails: There are a total of 200km of hiking trails throughout the Serfaus and Fiss-Ladis regions. Explore the wonders of nature, stunning Alpine views and tranquil mountain scenery. The trails are clearly marked and cater to all levels, ranging from easy strolls to advanced hikes.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Serfaus

There are plenty of hiking and mountain bike tours in and around Serfaus. Here are some of the best routes:

  • Serfaus Roundtrip: A 2-hour scenic hike suitable for beginners that passes through the picturesque village. You will enjoy spectacular views over the valley.
  • Schneidersjoch: This advanced hike takes you to the top of Schneidersjoch mountain. The tour takes around 7 hours and offers breathtaking views of the surrounding Alpine peaks and tranquil streams.
  • From Serfaus to Fiss: Rent a mountain bike and cycle from Serfaus to Fiss along the “Sun Route” for glorious Alpine scenery. The trail takes around 50 minutes and is an easy ride.

Best Things to Do in Serfaus: Activities and Tours

There is a wide range of activities to suit all ages, interests, and skill levels. Here are our recommendations:

  • Sledging: A thrilling ride down the mountain after dark, on the Fun-Park Serfaus-Fiss-Ladis track from KOMPERDELL or FROMMES Alm followed by dinner at a warm and cozy mountain hut.
  • Paragliding: Experience the sensation of flying over the mountains with a tandem paragliding flight. On your journey, take in the stunning views of fields, forests, and dramatic valley landscapes.
  • Serenade in the Tyrolean Evenings: Wear traditional Tyrolean clothing and enjoy music, folklore, and delicious regional delicacies in a cozy hut in the mountains under the stars.

Week-Long Itinerary for Serfaus

If you are visiting Serfaus for a week, here’s an itinerary to help you plan your days:

Day 1 Arrive in Serfaus, take a stroll through the village and experience the local delicacies.
Day 2 Go hiking: trying one of the easier routes such as the Serfaus Roundtrip or take a scenic bike ride.
Day 3 Visit the lush areas around the lake, go to Seresa and See Snow Lake and explore the region.
Day 4 Visit the local landmarks, Museums, and chapels.
Day 5 Take a day trip to the Kaunertal glacier and discover the beauty and splendor of the surrounding countryside.
Day 6 Go sledging after sunset on the Fun-Park Serfaus-Fiss Ladistrack from the KOMPERDELL or FROMMES Alm.
Day 7 Attend the Tyrolean Evenings for a true cultural experience, before departing Serfaus for home.

Food and Drink Scene of Serfaus

Serfaus is a paradise for food and drink lovers, with a range of delicious regional specialties. Here are some of the must-try dishes:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Tiroler Groestl: A hearty dish of potatoes, bacon and eggs fried together, with crispy onion rings.
  • Kaiserschmarrn: A sweet dessert pancakes dish cut up into little pieces served with applesauce and powdered sugar.
  • Schnitzel: A classic Austrian dish - breaded or battered and fried thin meat cutlets made of pork or veal.

Local Beverages

  • Almdudler: A popular Austrian soft drink made of herbs and lemonade.
  • Stiegl Beer: A famous Austrian beer brand that offers a variety of flavors.

Local Culture and Customs of Serfaus

Serfaus has a rich cultural heritage, with the local population proud of their traditional customs and festivals. One of the most celebrated festivals is called “Schnitzel dance” where while dancing, they serve and eat Schnitzel.

Best Time to Visit Serfaus

The best time to visit Serfaus is between June and September when the weather is mild, and temperatures range between 10°C and 25°C. If you can’t bear large crowds and enjoy skiing the snow the following season is perfect for you.

Safety and Health

Serfaus is a relatively safe destination and has adequate health care facilities to cater to the needs of visitors.

Safety in Serfaus

  • Always follow trails and instructions when hiking or biking.
  • Check weather reports before embarking on outdoor activities.
  • Be cautious around wildlife encounters and do not feed animals

Health in Serfaus

  • Observe standard health and hygiene practices, especially during covid-19.
  • Carry a travel health insurance policy.

Getting to Serfaus: Transportation Options and Logistics

Serfaus is easily accessible via different modes of transport. Here are your options:

Airports Near Serfaus

The nearest airports to Serfaus are:

  • Innsbruck Airport (85km)
  • Munich Airport (213km)

Bus Stations Near Serfaus

The closest bus stations to Serfaus are:

  • Landeck (30 km)
  • St. Anton (40km)

Other Options

Guests can drive from wherever they are to reach Serfaus, or they can take the train and then a bus from Landeck.

How to Get Around in Serfaus

Serfaus is accessible on foot, so walking is, by far, the best way to get around. Alternatively, any hotel that you book will give you a “Super card” that offers unlimited use of all the available Bus routes of Serfaus.
