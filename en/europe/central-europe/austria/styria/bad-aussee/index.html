Introduction to Bad Aussee

Nestled in the Styrian mountains of Austria, Bad Aussee is a small town that attracts visitors with its picturesque landscapes, rich history, and quaint local culture. A popular destination for outdoor enthusiasts, Bad Aussee offers an array of outdoor activities including hiking, biking, skiing, and swimming. Apart from outdoor adventures, Bad Aussee is also home to numerous museums, cultural attractions, and local markets that showcase the region’s unique customs and crafts.

List of Places to Visit in Bad Aussee

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Kammerhof Museum showcases the history and culture of the Ausseerland region with its collection of traditional costumes, artifacts, and handicrafts.
  • Gmunden Castle is a magnificent palace built in the 17th century that houses a museum dedicated to the history of the region.
  • Loser Panoramastraße is a scenic road that offers breathtaking views of the Loser mountain range.

Natural Attractions of Bad Aussee: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Altausseer See is a serene lake surrounded by towering peaks that offers swimming, fishing, and boating opportunities.
  • Grunberg Mountain is a must-visit spot for hiking lovers with its panoramic views, mountain huts, and alpine meadows.
  • Narzissenbad Spa is a natural hot spring spa known for its healing properties and therapeutic treatments.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Bad Aussee

Bad Aussee offers numerous hiking and biking routes that cater to all levels of fitness and experience. Some of the popular routes include:

  • Narzissentourweg is a 10 km hike that takes you through the lush meadows and fields of Narcissen Valley.
  • Altausseer See Rundwanderung is a 7.5 km circular trail around the serene Altausseer lake that takes you through dense forests and alpine meadows.
  • Panoramaweg is a scenic route that offers breathtaking views of the Dachstein glacier and the valley below.

Best Things to Do in Bad Aussee: Activities and Tours

  • Narzissenfest is a popular annual event that celebrates the blooming of the narcissus fields with live music, crafts, and local food.
  • Loser Bergbahnen is a cable car that takes you to the top of the Loser mountain range from where you can enjoy panoramic views and go skiing in winter.
  • Ausseerland Salzkammergut offers a guided tour of the salt mines of Hallstatt and Bad Ischl, showcasing the region’s rich mining history.

Food and Drink Scene of Bad Aussee

Must-Try Dishes

  • Steirerkas is a local cheese made from raw cow’s milk that is traditionally served with bread.
  • Reindling is a sweet bread made with raisins, cinnamon, and lemon zest that is served as a dessert or snack.
  • Schilcher is a rosé wine that is brewed in the nearby region of Weststeiermark and is the perfect accompaniment to the local cuisine.

Local Beverages

  • G’spritzter is a popular local drink made with white wine and sparkling water.
  • Birnenschnaps is a pear brandy that is distilled locally and has a distinct fruity flavor.
  • Honiglecken is a locally brewed beer that is made with honey and has a sweet taste.

Where to Stay in Bad Aussee: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Bad Aussee offers numerous accommodation options to suit all budgets and preferences. Some of the popular options include:

  • Hotel Erzherzog Johann is a charming hotel located in the town center that offers comfortable rooms and a restaurant serving local cuisine.
  • JUFA Hotel Bad Aussee is a family-friendly hotel located near the Grunberg Mountain that offers spacious rooms and a host of outdoor activities.
  • Pension Egger is a cozy guesthouse located in the scenic Altaussee that offers comfortable rooms and personalized service.

What to Buy in Bad Aussee: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Tracht is the traditional costume of the region that is made of high-quality cloth and is available in numerous local shops and markets.
  • Salzsteckerl is a popular souvenir made from local salt crystals that are shaped into various forms and sizes.
  • Holzschnitzereien is a unique local craft that involves carving wooden figures and objects, and is available in numerous local markets and shops.

Local Culture and Customs of Bad Aussee

Bad Aussee is known for its unique local culture and customs that are deeply rooted in the region’s history and natural surroundings. The locals celebrate numerous festivals throughout the year that showcase their music, crafts, and culinary skills. Dressing up in traditional Tracht is also a common custom on special occasions such as weddings and festivals. The region is also known for its unique dialect, which has its roots in the Bavarian language.

Best Time to Visit Bad Aussee

The best time to visit Bad Aussee is from late spring to early autumn when the weather is mild and the region is at its scenic best. The summer months of July and August are the peak tourist season and offer numerous festivals and activities, while the winter months offer excellent skiing and snowboarding opportunities.

Safety and Health

Safety in Bad Aussee

Bad Aussee is a safe destination for tourists, with a low crime rate and friendly locals. However, tourists are advised to take necessary precautions and keep their valuables safe while travelling. The region is also prone to natural calamities such as avalanches and landslides, so it is advisable to stay alert and heed any warnings or advisories issued by the local authorities.

Health in Bad Aussee

Health facilities in Bad Aussee are of high standards and readily available. However, tourists are advised to carry their medical insurance cards and any required medication while travelling. The region is also prone to extreme weather conditions, so it is advisable to dress appropriately and take necessary precautions while enjoying outdoor activities.

How to Get Around in Bad Aussee

Bad Aussee is a small town that can be explored on foot, with most tourist attractions located within walking distance. For longer distances, tourists can hire bicycles or take public transport options such as buses or taxis.

Where to Go Next from Bad Aussee: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Hallstatt is located 18 km away from Bad Aussee and is a UNESCO World Heritage Site known for its salt mines and picturesque landscapes.
  • Altaussee Salt Mine is located 3 km away from Bad Aussee and offers an insight into the region’s rich mining history and natural wonders.
  • Wolfgangsee is a scenic lake located 70 km away from Bad Aussee and offers swimming, boating, and hiking opportunities.

Conclusion

Bad Aussee is a hidden gem in the heart of Austria that offers an unforgettable experience for tourists seeking adventure, culture, and scenic beauty. With an array of outdoor activities, cultural attractions, and local markets, Bad Aussee has something for everyone. Visit this charming town to explore the rich history, unique culture, and breathtaking scenery of Austria’s Styrian region.
