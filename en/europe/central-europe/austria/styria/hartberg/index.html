Introduction to Hartberg

Located in the beautiful region of Styria, Austria, Hartberg is a charming city with a rich history and culture. It’s famed for its stunning architecture, beautiful countryside, and traditional local cuisine. In this comprehensive guide, we’ll take you through the best things to see and do in Hartberg, including hiking and biking routes, cultural attractions, local food and drink scene, accommodations, and much more.

List of Places to Visit in Hartberg

Here are the must-visit places in Hartberg:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Hartberg boasts a rich cultural heritage, and there is no better place to experience it than at its museums and historical sites. Some of the best ones include:

  • Hartberger Schlossberg: This fortification is located on a hill overlooking the city. It was built in the 13th century and was an important military stronghold.

  • Heimatmuseum Hartberg: This museum showcases the cultural history of Hartberg and its surrounding areas. It has an impressive collection of artifacts, including old documents, costumes, and household items.

  • Pfarrkirche zum hl. Georg: This beautiful church dates back to the 14th century and is a prime example of Gothic architecture.

Natural Attractions of Hartberg: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Hartberg is surrounded by breathtaking countryside, offering plenty of opportunities for outdoor adventures and scenic views. Some of the top natural attractions in Hartberg are:

  • Naturpark Pöllauertal: A natural park covering 6,300 hectares with a beautiful landscape of hills, meadows, and forests.

  • Der Ringkogel: This mountain offers fantastic hiking opportunities with breathtaking views of the region.

  • Stubenbergsee: A beautiful lake surrounded by pristine forest and hills. It’s an ideal spot for fishing, swimming, and hiking.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Hartberg

Hartberg offers plenty of hiking and biking routes. Here are the top ones:

  • Hartberger Schleifenweg: A 13-kilometer long trail that circles Hartberg. It takes you through the beautiful countryside surrounding the town, and you can enjoy stunning views of vineyards, orchards, and rolling hills.

  • Kornberg-Apfelstraße: A beautiful bike trail that takes you through fields of apple trees, picturesque villages, and historic castles.

Best Things to Do in Hartberg: Activities and Tours

Hartberg has so much to offer visitors, and it’s difficult to narrow it down to a few options. Here are some of the best things to do:

  • Explore the charming old town: stroll around the picturesque streets, admire the historic buildings, and take in the beauty of Hartberg.

  • Visit a traditional wine tavern: family-owned wine taverns offer the chance to taste some of the local wines and traditional Styrain cuisine.

  • Take a scenic drive: Hartberg’s picturesque countryside is best explored on a driving tour, where you can stop at scenic spots and take in the views.

  • Take a hot air balloon ride: Get a bird’s eye view of Hartberg’s countryside by taking a hot air balloon ride.

Week-Long Itinerary for Hartberg

If you’re planning a week-long trip to Hartberg, here’s an itinerary to help you make the most of your time:

  • Day 1: Explore the charming old town, visit Hartberger Schlossberg

  • Day 2: Hike or bike the Kornberg-Apfelstraße trail

  • Day 3: Visit Naturpark Pöllauertal, Stubenbergsee

  • Day 4: Take a scenic driving tour around the area

  • Day 5: Take a hot air balloon ride, visit Heimatmuseum Hartberg

  • Day 6: Taste some of the local wines and traditional Styrain cuisine at a wine tavern

  • Day 7: Relax and enjoy the beauty of Hartberg

Food and Drink Scene of Hartberg

No trip to Hartberg is complete without experiencing its local food and drink scene. Here are some of the must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Blutwurst: A local sausage made with pig’s blood, perfect for adventurous eaters.
  • Steirisches Backhendl: Breaded and fried chicken, a staple of traditional Styrian cuisine.
  • Kürbisgolatsche: A savory pastry made with pumpkin.

Local Beverages

  • Schilcher: A light and refreshing wine, perfect to accompany traditional Austrian cuisine.
  • Apfelsaft: Freshly pressed apple juice, a staple of the Austrian beverage scene.

Where to Stay in Hartberg: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Hartberg offers a range of accommodation options for all budgets. Some of the top-rated hotels include:

  • Hotel Restaurant Pusswald: A luxurious hotel with an on-site restaurant serving traditional Styrian cuisine.

  • Hotel Alter Gerichtshof: Located in the heart of the old town, this hotel offers stylish rooms in a historic building.

  • JUFA Hotel Hartberg: A family-friendly hotel with a range of amenities including a playground, sports facilities, and an on-site restaurant.

What to Buy in Hartberg: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Here are some of the best souvenirs and local products to bring home from Hartberg:

  • Pfefferbeißer: A local sausage that’s perfect for snacking.
  • Kürbiskernöl: A delicious pumpkin seed oil that’s a staple of Austrian cuisine.
  • Styrian wine: Austria is known for its great wine, and Styrian wine is among the best.

Local Culture and Customs of Hartberg

In Hartberg, people are friendly and welcoming. It’s common to greet people with a brief “Grüss Gott” (hello), and it’s polite to use “Sie” when addressing strangers or older people.

Best Time to Visit Hartberg

The best time to visit Hartberg is from May to September when the weather is mild, and the countryside is lush and green.

Do’s and Don’ts of Hartberg

Do:

  • Do visit the local wine taverns and try the local wines and traditional cuisine.

  • Do take a hot air balloon ride for a unique bird’s eye view of Hartberg’s countryside.

  • Do greet people with a brief “Grüss Gott”.

Don’t:

  • Don’t forget to tip 10% for excellent service at restaurants and bars.

  • Don’t litter in the beautiful countryside.

Safety and Health

Safety in Hartberg

Hartberg is a safe and friendly city, and visitors are unlikely to encounter crime or other safety issues. However, it’s always best to take precautions and be aware of your surroundings, especially at night.

Health in Hartberg

Austrian health care is known to be world-class. For minor ailments, you can visit a pharmacy. For emergencies and major medical issues, it’s best to contact the nearest hospital.

Getting to Hartberg: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Hartberg

The nearest airport to Hartberg is Graz Airport, which is located approximately 60km away. It’s the best option if you’re traveling from outside Europe.

Bus Stations Near Hartberg

The Hartberg bus station is located in the city center, and it services several regional and intercity bus lines.

Other Options

The best way to get around Hartberg is by car, as it allows you to explore the beautiful countryside surrounding the city.

How to Get Around in Hartberg

The best way to get around Hartberg is by bike or on foot, as most of the city’s attractions are located in the picturesque old town.

Where to Go Next from Hartberg: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Hartberg is located in the heart of Styria, and there are plenty of nearby destinations to explore. Some of the top ones include:

  • Graz: The capital of Styria is a beautiful city with stunning architecture and a vibrant cultural scene.
  • Bad Waltersdorf: Known for its thermal spas and wellness centers, this beautiful village is just a short drive from Hartberg.
  • Vienna: The stunning capital of Austria is just a few hours’ drive away.

Conclusion

Hartberg is a charming city with a rich cultural heritage and stunning countryside. Whether you’re a history buff, outdoor enthusiast, or foodie, there’s something for you in Hartberg. We hope this comprehensive guide has inspired you to visit and explore this beautiful part of Austria.
