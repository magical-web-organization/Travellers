Introduction to Styria

Styria is a picturesque region located in Austria’s southeastern part. This stunning destination boasts a charming mix of natural beauty, cultural heritage, and culinary delights. With its snow-capped mountains, rolling hills, and lush valleys, Styria offers endless outdoor adventures for nature enthusiasts. Additionally, the region’s rich history is celebrated through ancient castles, museums, and cultural festivals all year round. This comprehensive travel guide to Styria will help you plan the perfect trip by highlighting the best places to visit, things to do, and local experiences to enjoy.

List of Places to Visit in Styria

Whether you are a history buff, a nature lover, or a gourmet enthusiast, Styria has something for everyone. Here is a list of the top must-visit attractions and destinations:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Schloss Eggenberg Palace: This stunning palace dates back to the 17th century and is a UNESCO World Heritage Site. Its elaborate Baroque architecture, beautiful gardens, and extensive art collection make it a must-see destination.
  • Graz Old Town: This charming Medieval town is home to numerous historical landmarks and buildings. It boasts narrow cobblestone streets, ancient alleyways, and picturesque squares that transport visitors back in time.
  • Landeszeughaus: The Landeszeughaus is the world’s largest arsenal museum and holds over 32,000 pieces of weaponry dating back to the 16th century. This immersive museum brings the region’s military history to life.
  • Admont Abbey: This stunning monastery is a masterpiece of Baroque architecture and houses one of the world’s most exquisite library collections.

Natural Attractions of Styria: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Dachstein Glacier: The Dachstein Glacier is one of the most spectacular natural attractions in Styria. This stunning glacier can be reached via car or cable car, and offers breathtaking views of the surrounding mountains.
  • Schladming-Dachstein Region: The Schladming-Dachstein Region is a paradise for outdoor enthusiasts. It offers a variety of activities such as hiking, skiing, cycling, and mountain climbing.
  • Graz Woods: The Graz Woods offers a beautiful escape from the city’s hustle and bustle. This lush forest boasts numerous hiking trails, picnic areas, and scenic views.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Styria

One of the best ways to explore Styria’s natural beauty is by hiking or biking. The region offers a wide range of trails for all levels of experience. Some popular routes include:

  • Riesachsee Tour: This stunning hiking trail takes visitors through the Schladming-Dachstein Region and offers breathtaking views of the Riesachsee Lake and the surrounding valleys.
  • Enns Cycling Trail: The Enns Cycling Trail is a popular route for cyclists looking to explore the region’s picturesque landscape. This cycling track spans over 200km and runs along the Enns River.
  • Mur Cycle Path: The Mur Cycle Path is another scenic way to explore Styria. This cycling route follows the Mur River and takes visitors through charming towns, rolling hills, and lush countryside.

Best Things to Do in Styria: Activities and Tours

Styria offers a wide variety of activities and tours for travelers looking for an immersive and authentic experience. Here are some of the best things to do:

  • Wine Tasting: Styria is famous for its wine production, particularly the white wines. Visitors can enjoy a wine tasting tour that takes them through local vineyards and wine cellars.
  • Thermal Baths: Styria is dotted with thermal springs that have been used as natural spas for centuries. Visitors can relax and rejuvenate in one of the many thermal baths located throughout the region.
  • Culinary Tours: Styria boasts an exquisite culinary tradition, with specialties like pumpkin seed oil, Styrian apples, and traditional dishes like schnitzel and goulash. Visitors can enjoy a culinary tour that takes them to local markets, farms, and restaurants.

Week-Long Itinerary for Styria

Here is a sample itinerary for a week-long trip to Styria:

Day 1: Explore Graz Old Town and visit Schloss Eggenberg Palace. Day 2: Hike the Riesachsee Tour in the Schladming-Dachstein Region. Day 3: Go on a wine tasting tour in the Southern Styria Wine Road. Day 4: Enjoy a relaxing day at one of the many thermal baths in Styria. Day 5: Cycle the Enns Cycling Trail and explore the Enns River Valley. Day 6: Visit the Landeszeughaus and Admont Abbey. Day 7: Take a scenic drive through the Graz Woods and enjoy a country-style lunch in one of its many charming restaurants.

Food and Drink Scene of Styria

Styria is a food and wine lover’s paradise. With its rich culinary tradition and world-class wine production, visitors are in for a treat. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Styrian Pumpkin Seed Oil: This locally produced oil is a staple of Styrian cuisine and can be used in salads, soups, and sauces.
  • Styrian Apple Strudel: This delicious dessert is made with fresh, locally grown apples and served with whipped cream.
  • Schnitzel: This Austrian classic is a must-try dish when visiting Styria. It consists of a breaded and fried meat cutlet and is often served with potatoes or salad.

Local Beverages

  • Styrian White Wine: Styria is renowned for its white wine production, particularly the Sauvignon Blanc and Welschriesling varieties.
  • Styrian Beer: Styria also has a thriving beer scene, with a variety of local craft beer breweries like Gösser and Puntigamer.
  • Schnapps: This strong distilled spirit is a popular after-dinner drink in Styria and can be found in various flavors like apricot, pear, and cherry.

Where to Stay in Styria: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Styria offers a wide range of accommodation options for visitors of all budgets and preferences. Here are some recommendations:

  • Schloss Gabelhofen: This stunning castle hotel is located in the heart of Styria and offers luxurious rooms and suites, a gourmet restaurant, and breathtaking views of the surrounding landscape.
  • JUFA Graz: This modern hostel is located in the city of Graz and offers affordable and comfortable rooms, a bar, and communal areas for socializing.
  • Gasthof Steinerwirt: This charming guesthouse is located in the Southern Styria Wine Road and offers cozy rooms, a traditional restaurant, and a beautiful garden.

What to Buy in Styria: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Visitors to Styria can take home some unique and authentic souvenirs and local products. Here are some recommendations:

  • Styrian Pumpkin Seed Oil: This delicious and nutritious oil is a popular souvenir from Styria and can be found in various sizes and packaging.
  • Murau Beer: This local craft beer is brewed in the town of Murau and can be found in various flavors.
  • Local Handicrafts: Styria is home to many talented artisans who produce traditional handicrafts like pottery, woodcarvings, and textiles. Visitors can find these products at local markets and fairs.

Local Culture and Customs of Styria

Styria has a rich cultural heritage that is celebrated through various festivals, events, and traditions. Here are some things to keep in mind when visiting:

  • Greetings: Austrians are known for their politeness and formality. Visitors should greet people with a handshake and use formal titles like “Herr” (Mr.) and “Frau” (Mrs./Ms.) when addressing them.
  • Dress Code: Styria’s culture has a strong emphasis on dressing elegantly and appropriately for different occasions. Visitors should avoid wearing casual or revealing clothing in formal settings.
  • Tipping: Tipping is common in Styria’s restaurants and cafes, with a standard rate of 10-15% of the bill.

Best Time to Visit Styria

Styria’s climate varies throughout the year, with warm summers and cold winters. The best time to visit depends on personal preferences and interests. Here are some recommendations:

  • Spring (March-May): Spring is a great time to visit Styria for nature lovers, as the countryside is in full bloom.
  • Summer (June-August): Summer is ideal for outdoor activities like hiking, cycling, and swimming in the region’s lakes and rivers.
  • Winter (December-March): Winter is perfect for skiing and snowboarding, as Styria boasts several ski resorts.

Do’s and Don’ts of Styria

Here are some do’s and don’ts to keep in mind when visiting Styria:

Do:

  • Respect Local Customs: Styria’s culture has a strong emphasis on tradition and respect. Visitors should respect local customs and etiquette when in formal settings.
  • Explore the Countryside: Styria’s countryside is stunning and offers a wide range of outdoor activities and adventures.
  • Taste the Local Cuisine: Styria’s culinary traditions are unique and delicious. Visitors should take the opportunity to try local dishes and beverages.

Don’t:

  • Ignore Dress Code: Dressing appropriately for different occasions is an important aspect of Styrian culture and shows respect for formal settings.
  • Be Loud or Rude: Styrians are known for their politeness and formality. Visitors should avoid being loud or rude in public settings.
  • Forget to Tip: Tipping is common in Styria’s restaurants and cafes and shows appreciation for good service.

Safety and Health

Styria is a safe and healthy destination. However, travelers should take necessary precautions and be aware of safety and health concerns. Here are some things to keep in mind:

Safety in Styria

  • Crime: Styria has a low crime rate, and most crimes are petty theft or pickpocketing in tourist areas.
  • Natural Disasters: The region is prone to heavy snowfall and floods, particularly in winter and spring. Visitors should stay informed about weather conditions and follow local authorities’ instructions.

Health in Styria

  • Medical Care: Styria has a high standard of medical care and facilities.
  • Vaccinations: No special vaccinations are required to travel to Styria.

How to Get Around in Styria

Where to Go Next from Styria: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Styria’s central location makes it an ideal base for exploring neighboring destinations. Here are some recommendations:

  • Vienna: The Austrian capital is located just a few hours away from Styria and is a must-visit destination for culture, history, and cuisine.
  • Salzburg: This charming city is famous for its baroque architecture, music heritage, and stunning Alpine scenery.
  • Lake Balaton: Located in Hungary, Lake Balaton is Europe’s largest freshwater lake and offers a variety of outdoor activities and cultural attractions.

Conclusion

Styria is a charming and picturesque region that offers something for everyone, from beautiful natural landscapes to rich cultural heritage and exquisite cuisine.
