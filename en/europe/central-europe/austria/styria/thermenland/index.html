Introduction to Thermenland

Thermenland is a stunning region situated in Styria, Austria’s southeastern state. It is famous for its thermal spas, unique regional cuisine, and scenic landscapes that attract visitors from all around the world. Also known as the Styrian thermal region, Thermenland has a plethora of attractions that cater to all kinds of travelers, from adventure-seekers to history buffs to those looking for a relaxed getaway.

With so many options to choose from, it can be overwhelming to decide what to explore in Thermenland. This travel guide intends to help you plan your trip to Thermenland by highlighting the must-visit places, the best things to do, where to stay, and much more.

List of Places to Visit in Thermenland

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Schloss Kornberg: This iconic castle has stunning architecture and is situated on a hill that provides panoramic views of the Thermenland region.
  • Schloss Kapfenstein: This beautiful castle is located in the south-eastern Styrian hills and houses a museum and high-quality regional culinary offerings.
  • Schloss Tabor: This chateau is a mix of different architectural styles and features Baroque Salons, the Museum of the Castle and the Garden of Reflection.
  • Riegersburg Castle: A historic gem that dates back to the 11th century, Riegersburg Castle is an imposing hilltop fortress that offers breathtaking views and a glimpse into Austria’s rich cultural history.
  • Graz: The second-largest city in Austria, Graz is a UNESCO World Heritage Site known for its well-preserved historic architecture, cultural landmarks, and museums.

Natural Attractions of Thermenland: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • The Styrian Wine Roads: This network of roads meanders through the beautiful wineries and vineyards of the region; an ideal day trip for wine lovers or those who enjoy scenic drives.
  • Nature Park Pöllau Valley: This nature reserve covers a wide variety of fauna and flora. With a ‘hidden gem’ ambiance, visitors can also trek around up to 11 nature trails of varying degrees of difficulty.
  • Raabklamm Gorge: This beautiful natural gorge in Eastern Styria is a perfect spot for hiking, sightseeing, and outdoor adventure activities like rafting and canyoning.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Thermenland

Thermenland has a network of popular hiking and biking routes that offer spectacular views of the region’s beautiful landscapes. These paths include:

  • The Sulm Valley Trail: It is a 54 km long biking route that passes through vineyards, orchards, and lush forests in the Sulm Valley.
  • The Römerwein trail: A wine-hiking trail that explores the vineyards and winemakers of Southern Styria.
  • The Thermenland-Weinland Nature Trails: A network of hiking trails of varying difficulty levels, which include historical landmarks, vineyards, and scenic views.

Best Things to Do in Thermenland: Activities and Tours

  • Relax in a Thermal Spa: The region’s thermal spas are known for their soothing and healing properties; a great way to unwind and rejuvenate.
  • Join a Wine Tour: Take a tour of the region’s wineries and vineyards- delectable wines amid the scenic views is indeed something you won’t forget anytime soon.
  • Visit the Zotter Chocolate Factory: The Zotter Chocolate Factory is a popular attraction for chocolate lovers; visitors can see how chocolate is made from bean to bar, taste the products, and take some home as souvenirs!
  • Explore Traditional Villages: A visit to traditional Styrian villages like Bad Gleichenberg or Bad Radkersburg is a journey back in time, with historic architecture and local customs to explore.

Week-Long Itinerary for Thermenland

Below is a suggested itinerary for exploring the region in detail over a week -

  • Day 1: Arrive in Thermenland, take a day to unwind at your hotel or explore the surrounding area
  • Day 2-3: Explore the history and culture of the region by visiting some of the popular cultural attractions and landmarks.
  • Day 4: Go on a quaint village tour, sample the local cuisine, join a wine tour or indulge in a chocolate factory tour, and take a thermal bath.
  • Day 5-6: Go hiking or biking, visit Natural Attractions like the Raabklamm Gorge or Nature Park Pöllau Valley.
  • Day 7: Departure from Thermenland.

Food and Drink Scene of Thermenland

Must-Try Dishes

Thermenland’s cuisine is an amalgamation of Styrian and Hungarian influences. Some regional dishes are:

  • Pot roast of roast pork with crackling and sauerkraut called “Surbratl”
  • A sweet dessert called “Kaiserschmarrn” made of pancakes and fruits
  • A sour and spicy soup called “Pickle Soup”
  • A hearty lamb stew called “Erdäpfelgulasch”

Local Beverages

Styria is famous for its wine, pumpkin seed oil, and beer. Some of the most popular local beverages include:

  • Styrian white wines like Sauvignon Blanc and Morillon
  • Bushels of pumpkin seed oil
  • Hopsch - a local beer brewed by local breweries in the region

Where to Stay in Thermenland: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Thermenland has a range of accommodation options that cater to all budgets and requirements. Some good options include:

  • Schloss Kapfenstein
  • Das Sonnreich
  • Geniesserhotel Goger
  • Grand Hotel Loipersdorf

What to Buy in Thermenland: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Some local products to bring home include:

  • Pumpkin seed oil
  • Styrian wines
  • Zotter chocolates
  • Handicrafts from the traditional villages in the region

Local Culture and Customs of Thermenland

Styria has a rich history and culture, largely influenced by its neighboring countries. Customs like wine-making, spa culture, food traditions, and horse riding are integral to Thermenland’s heritage.

Best Time to Visit Thermenland

Thermenland is beautiful in all seasons, but the best time to visit is from late spring to early autumn. The mild temperatures allow for outdoor activities like hiking and swimming without it being too warm or cold.

Do’s and Don’ts of Thermenland

Do:

  • Respect local customs and traditions
  • Try the local cuisine and beverage offerings
  • Take a thermal bath, it’s a unique experience.

Don’t:

  • Be too loud or disruptive in public places
  • Litter or deface public property
  • Disrespect locals or their traditions.

Safety and Health

Safety in Thermenland

Thermenland is a safe region to visit, but travelers should always be cautious and beware of pickpockets.

Health in Thermenland

Thermenland is known for its thermal spas that are believed to have therapeutic effects on the body. The quality of water and spas are usually regulated, but check for that before using them to avoid any issues.

Getting to Thermenland: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Thermenland

Thermenland doesn’t have its airport. The closest airports to Thermenland are Graz Airport and Vienna Airport, both well-connected to major European destinations.

Bus Stations Near Thermenland

There are a few regional bus stations that connect to Thermenland, including Hartberg, Fürstenfeld and Bad Waltersdorf.

Other Options

It’s best to hire a car or take a taxi to explore the region thoroughly.

How to Get Around in Thermenland

Having a rental car is the best way to get around Thermenland; public transportation might be limited.

Where to Go Next from Thermenland: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Graz: The Riverside city of Graz is just one hour away from Thermenland by car.
  • Vienna: A visit to the Austrian capital Vienna is a must while in the region as it oozes history around its every corner.
  • Hungary: Bordering Styria, Hungary, and its cultural hubs like Budapest makes a great opportunity for an exciting day trip.

Conclusion

Thermenland, with its relaxing spas and natural beauty, is the perfect getaway for all kinds of travelers. From scenic drives to wine tours to cultural landmarks, there is something for everyone in this Austrian gem. Use our guide to plan your itinerary, book your stay, and explore Thermenland to its fullest!
