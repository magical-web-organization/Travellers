Introduction to Schladming

Schladming is a picturesque town located in the heart of the Styria region in Austria. Famous for its skiing and winter sports activities, Schladming is equally charming and exciting during the summer months when visitors can explore the beautiful countryside and enjoy a range of outdoor activities such as hiking, mountain biking, and white water rafting.

In this comprehensive tour guide, we will take you through all that Schladming has to offer so that you can plan your trip with ease.

List of Places to Visit in Schladming

Here is a list of the top attractions and places to visit in Schladming:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  1. Schladming Museum: The museum displays the cultural and historical heritage of the region with more than 6,000 exhibits.

  2. Stadtmuseum Schladming: Located in the town center, the museum showcases the development of Schladming through the ages.

  3. Castle Schladming: This building dates back to the 18th century and now serves as a cultural center

Natural Attractions of Schladming: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  1. Dachstein Glacier: The largest glacier in Austria offers breathtaking views of the Alps and snow activities like skiing and snowboarding.

  2. Riesachfälle: The Riesachfälle is the highest waterfall in Styria and offers a beautiful hiking trail and a view of green forests and a roaring waterfall

  3. Schladminger Tauern: The mountain range provides great hiking trails that lead to remarkable alpine lakes, mountain peaks, and waterfalls.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Schladming

Schladming is paradise for nature lovers who are into hiking and mountain biking.

  1. Planai - Hochwurzen mountain circuit: The most popular and well-organized hiking and biking trail network in the region.

  2. Rohrmoos-Untertal: It is a low mountain range that offers easy and moderate hiking trails with scenic views.

3.Talbachklamm Trail: A beautiful half-day walk from Schladming that will take you to an impressive gorge with 18 waterfalls.

Best Things to Do in Schladming: Activities and Tours

Schladming has a varied range of exciting activities to offer that will give you the perfect Austrian holiday experience.

  1. Schladming’s Sports World: One of the most famous skiing and winter sports resorts in Europe with 123 kilometers of ski slopes.

  2. Adventure Park Planai: With exciting obstacle courses and a mountain cart ride, the park is an excellent option for adventure lovers.

  3. White Water Rafting: The Enns River in Schladming provides an adrenaline-pumping experience, where visitors can experience white water rafting.

Food and Drink Scene of Schladming

Austrian cuisine is rich and diverse and so is the cuisine in Schladming. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  1. Wiener Schnitzel: A thin slice of buttered breaded veal cutlet

  2. Tafelspitz: A boiled beef dish served with traditionally made horseradish sauce

  3. Kaiserschmarrn: A sweet dish made from a shredded pancake topped with fruit and cream

Local Beverages

  1. Austrian beer: Austrian breweries make rich and flavorful beers that are of excellent quality

  2. Schnapps: A fruit brandy that has a pleasant taste and a strong aroma

Where to Stay in Schladming: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Schladming has plenty of accommodation types that cater to everyone’s needs and budget. It is recommended to book accommodations in advance, especially during the peak season.

  1. Budget: Mein Haus am Teich and Apartments Tritscher

  2. Mid-Range: Hotel Schladmingerhof and Hotel Mitterhofer

  3. Luxury: Hotel Pichlmayrgut and Hotel Schwaigerhof

What to Buy in Schladming: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Local Culture and Customs of Schladming

Austria is a country rich in traditions, and Schladming reflects that very well. Here are some customs that you may come across while visiting:

  1. Traditional costume: Many locals dress in traditional clothing, and you may see them while walking through the town center.

  2. Decoration of houses: During the Christmas season, houses and businesses are adorned with advent decorations.

Best Time to Visit Schladming

Schladming’s peak season is during the winter months, from December to February, when tourists flock to the Ski World Cup and other winter sports events. Visiting during the off-season, from May to September, will allow you to enjoy hiking, biking, and white water rafting without the crowds.

Do’s and Don’ts of Schladming

When visiting Schladming, it’s important to mind local customs and practices. Here are some do’s and don’ts:

Do:

  1. Greet locals in German: Austrian people appreciate it when visitors try to learn a bit of German.

  2. Bring cash: Many small businesses and restaurants do not accept cards.

Don’t:

  1. Be too loud: Austrians take noise pollution seriously and consider it a breach of etiquette.

  2. Forget to tip: Tipping is expected in Austria, especially in restaurants.

Safety and Health

Schladming is generally safe, but you should still take precautions to protect yourself and your belongings.

Safety in Schladming

  1. Be aware of your surrounding when participating in outdoor activities.

  2. Stay on designated trails to avoid getting lost.

Health in Schladming

  1. The tap water in Schladming is safe to drink.

  2. Be sure to wear sunscreen and insect repellent when spending time outdoors.

Getting to Schladming: Transportation Options and Logistics

Here are the different transportation options for reaching Schladming:

Airports Near Schladming

  1. Salzburg W. A. Mozart International Airport (75km away)

  2. Graz Airport (180km away)

Bus Stations Near Schladming

  1. Schladming bus station

  2. Central bus station in Salzburg

Other Options

  1. Train: Schladming is on the main railway line that runs from Salzburg to Graz.

  2. Car: Renting a car is a great way to explore the surrounding countryside at your leisure.

How to Get Around in Schladming

The most convenient and easy way to get around Schladming is by renting a car. However, there are other transportation options available like buses and taxis.
