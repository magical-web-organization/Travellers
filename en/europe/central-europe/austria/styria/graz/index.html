Introduction to Graz

Nestled in the heart of Styria, Austria, Graz is a charming city with a rich cultural heritage, stunning architecture, and outdoor adventures. This city is a UNESCO World Heritage Site and is renowned for its harmonious blend of Gothic, Renaissance, and Baroque architecture. Graz is also a cosmopolitan city, with a vibrant nightlife, diverse food scene, and welcoming locals.

In this travel guide, we’ll explore the top attractions of Graz, recommend the best places to stay and dine, and offer tips for getting the most out of your trip. Whether you’re a cultural enthusiast, an adventure seeker, or a foodie, Graz has something for everyone.

List of Places to Visit in Graz

Here are some of the must-visit destinations in Graz:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Graz has a rich history with cultural landmarks and museums that tell the story of the city. Here are the top cultural attractions in Graz:

  • Schlossberg Clock Tower: Also known as the Uhrturm, this tower overlooks the city and has been an iconic landmark of Graz for centuries.

  • Graz Old Town: The Old Town of Graz is a UNESCO World Heritage Site. It’s home to many Gothic, Renaissance, and Baroque architectural wonders, including the Graz Castle, the Landhaus, and the Glockenspiel.

  • Kunsthaus Graz: Kunsthaus Graz is a modern art museum located in the heart of Graz. The museum showcases contemporary art from Austria and around the world.

  • Styrian Armoury: The Landeszeughaus Graz is the world’s largest and oldest armory, with over 32,000 objects on display.

Natural Attractions of Graz: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Graz is surrounded by natural beauty and is known for its hiking and biking trails. Here are some of the outdoor adventures and scenic views worth exploring in Graz:

  • Murinsel: Murinsel is an artificial island in the middle of the Mur River. It’s a great place to relax and enjoy the views of the city.

  • Eggenberg Palace and Gardens: Eggenberg Palace is a Baroque palace located on the outskirts of Graz. The palace is surrounded by beautiful gardens, including an English Garden and a Japanese Garden.

  • Schlossberg Hill: Schlossberg Hill is a popular destination in Graz for hiking, biking, and enjoying scenic views of the city.

  • Graz City Park: Graz City Park is a beautiful public park located in the heart of the city. It’s a great place to relax and enjoy nature.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Graz

Graz and its surrounding areas have some of the best hiking and biking trails in Austria. Here are some popular routes:

  • Rundwanderweg Graz: This circular route around Graz offers stunning views of the city and countryside.

  • Mur Cycle Path: The Mur River provides a scenic backdrop for this 365-kilometer cycling trail from Salzburg to Bad Radkersburg.

  • Thalersee-Runde: This hiking trail is an excellent option for families with young children. It’s a circular trail around the Thalersee Lake, which has several playgrounds and picnic areas.

Best Things to Do in Graz: Activities and Tours

Graz has plenty of activities and tours to offer, from wine-tasting to sightseeing. Here are some of the best things to do in Graz:

  • Graz Tourist Tram: The Graz Tourist Tram is a unique way to explore the city’s top attractions.

  • Wine Tasting in South Styria: South Styria is known for its excellent wine. Take a tour of the vineyards and taste some of the area’s best wines.

  • Segway Tour of Graz: A Segway Tour of Graz is a fun and unique way to explore the city and its top attractions.

  • Go-Karting: If you’re looking for some adrenaline-pumping fun, head over to the Bad Waltersdorf Go-Karting Track.

Week-Long Itinerary for Graz

If you’re planning a week-long trip to Graz, here’s a suggested itinerary:

Day 1 Explore Graz Old Town, including the Graz Castle and the Landhaus

Day 6 Take a wine-tasting tour in South Styria

Food and Drink Scene of Graz

Graz is known for its diverse food scene, ranging from traditional Austrian dishes to modern, international cuisine. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Käferbohnensuppe: A traditional Styrian soup made with beans, pumpkin seed oil, and potatoes.

  • Steirisches Wurzelfleisch: A pork-based dish served with root vegetables and potatoes.

  • Schnitzel: A traditional Austrian dish made with breaded and fried veal.

  • Schilcher: A local rosé wine made from the Blauer Wildbacher grape.

Local Beverages

  • Stiegl Beer: The Stiegl Brewery in Salzburg produces one of Austria’s most popular beers.

  • Ottakringer Beer: Ottakringer is a Viennese brewery that produces a range of craft beers.

  • Graz Gin: Made with locally sourced botanicals, Graz Gin is a premium gin that’s gaining popularity among locals and visitors.

Where to Stay in Graz: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Graz has a range of accommodation options to suit every budget and preference. Here are some of the top places to stay in Graz:

  • Schlossberg Hotel: Located on the Schlossberg Hill, this hotel offers stunning views and luxurious amenities.

  • Hotel Wiesler: Hotel Wiesler is a stylish, boutique hotel located in the heart of Graz.

  • Jugendherberge Graz: The youth hostel in Graz is an affordable option for budget-conscious travelers.

  • Pension Alt-Strassgang: This guesthouse is located on the outskirts of Graz and offers a quaint, peaceful retreat.

What to Buy in Graz: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Graz has a range of local products and souvenirs worth bringing home. Here are some top picks:

  • Pumpkin Seed Oil: Pumpkin seed oil is a specialty of Styria and is used in a range of dishes. It’s a great souvenir to bring back home.

  • Handmade Soaps: Graz has several soap-making shops that sell handmade soaps made with natural ingredients.

  • Styrian Apple Schnapps: The Steiermark region is known for its high-quality apples, and the schnapps made from these apples is a popular local product.

Local Culture and Customs of Graz

Graz has a rich cultural heritage and a friendly, inclusive community. Here are some customs and cultural practices to keep in mind during your visit:

  • Dress Code: While Graz is a modern, cosmopolitan city, dressing smartly when visiting museums and cultural landmarks is a sign of respect.

  • Languages: The majority of people in Graz speak German, but most people also speak English.

  • Tipping: Tipping is common in Graz, and it’s customary to leave a 10% tip in restaurants and cafes.

Best Time to Visit Graz

Graz is a year-round destination, but the best time to visit depends on your preference and interests. Here’s an overview of the best times to visit Graz:

  • March to May: Spring is a great time to visit for the mild weather and blooming flowers.

  • June to August: Summer is a popular time to visit for the warm weather and outdoor activities.

  • September to November: Fall is a great time to visit for the changing leaves and harvest festivals.

  • December to February: Winter is a great time to visit for the Christmas markets and winter sports.

Do’s and Don’ts of Graz

Here are some do’s and don’ts to keep in mind during your stay in Graz:

Do:

  • Visit the top landmarks and museums in Graz.

  • Try traditional Styrian dishes and local wines and beers.

  • Take a hike or bike ride around Schlossberg Hill and the surrounding areas.

  • Treat locals with respect and be mindful of their customs and practices.

Don’t:

  • Skip the opportunity to try traditional Styrian cuisine.

  • Be disrespectful or dismissive of local customs and traditions.

  • Litter or damage the natural beauty of Graz.

  • Underestimate the importance of safety and security during your stay in Graz.

Safety and Health

Graz is a safe city, and the crime rate is low. However, like any other urban area, it’s important to take precautions to ensure your safety. Here are some tips to keep in mind:

Safety in Graz

  • Keep an eye on your belongings, particularly in crowded areas.

  • Avoid walking alone at night, especially in remote areas.

  • Always follow the rules of the road and use designated bike lanes and pedestrian crossings.

Health in Graz

  • Graz has an excellent healthcare system, and hospitals and clinics are readily available.

  • Tap water in Graz is safe to drink.

  • If you’re planning on hiking or participating in outdoor activities, wear appropriate clothing and bring water and snacks.

Getting to Graz: Transportation Options and Logistics

Graz is easily accessible by several modes of transportation. Here are some options:

Airports Near Graz

  • Graz Airport: Graz Airport is the city’s primary airport, and it’s located just 10 kilometers from the city center.

  • Vienna International Airport: Vienna International Airport is about 200 kilometers away from Graz and is the largest airport in Austria.

Bus Stations Near Graz

  • Graz Central Station: Graz Central Station is the primary train station in Graz.

  • Eurolines Bus Station: Eurolines Bus Station is located in the city center and offers regular bus services to major cities in Europe.

Other Options

  • Car Rental: Car rental agencies are available at Graz Airport and throughout the city.

  • Bicycle Rental: Bicycles can be rented at several locations in Graz, and the city has an extensive network of bike lanes.

How to Get Around in Graz

Graz is a compact city that’s easily explored on foot, bicycle or public transportation. Here are your options:

  • Walking: Graz is a pedestrian-friendly city, and many of the top attractions are within walking distance of each other.

  • Cycling: Graz has an extensive network of bicycle lanes, and renting a bike is an affordable and eco-friendly way to get around the city.

  • Public transportation: Graz has an efficient public transportation system that includes buses and trams.

Where to Go Next from Graz: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Graz is surrounded by natural beauty and charming towns and villages. Here are some nearby destinations worth visiting:

  • Schladming: Schladming is a popular ski resort town located about 100 kilometers from Graz.

  • Hallstatt: Hallstatt is a picturesque town located on the banks of Lake Hallstatt, about 200 kilometers from Graz.

  • Vienna: Vienna is Austria’s capital city and is located about 200 kilometers from Graz.

Conclusion

Graz is a charming city with a rich cultural heritage, stunning architecture, and outdoor adventures. This travel guide offers a comprehensive view of the best things to see and do in Graz, as well as tips for making the most of your trip. Whether you’re a cultural enthusiast, an adventure seeker, or a foodie, Graz has something for you. Start planning your trip today and experience the beauty of Styria first-hand.
