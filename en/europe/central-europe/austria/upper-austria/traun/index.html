Introduction to Traun

Located in Upper Austria, Traun is a beautiful town that offers visitors a perfect blend of history, nature, and modern vibes. With its stunning landscapes, cultural attractions, and recreational activities, Traun has something for everyone. This town is also an ideal base to explore the rich history and natural beauty of the Upper Austrian region. In this comprehensive travel guide, we will take you on a journey through the top attractions, activities, and must-visit places in Traun.

List of Places to Visit in Traun

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Traun is a town rich in history and culture. There are several museums and historical landmarks that offer a glimpse into the past of Traun and its people. One of the must-visit places in Traun is the Schloss Traun, which is a stunning 16th-century castle. This castle features a beautiful courtyard, a chapel, and a museum that showcases the history of Traun. Another popular attraction is the Stadtmuseum Traun, which showcases the history of the town and its people. The museum has several exhibits, including one on local folklore and another on the history of the town’s industries.

Natural Attractions of Traun: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Traun also offers several natural attractions that allow visitors to explore the stunning landscapes of the region. One of the most popular natural attractions is the Pleschinger See, a beautiful lake located on the outskirts of Traun. This lake is surrounded by lush greenery and is a great place to relax and unwind. Traun also has several parks and nature reserves, including the Traun Donaupark, which offers stunning views of the Traun River and the surrounding mountains. Another popular attraction is the Linzerberg, which is a mountain range that offers great hiking and biking trails.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Traun

Traun is a great destination for outdoor enthusiasts, and there are several hiking and biking routes available. The Traunfall is one of the most popular hiking routes in Traun, which offers stunning views of the Traun River and the surrounding mountains. The Traun-Ursprung hiking trail is another popular route. It leads through the beautiful Traun river valley and features several geological and botanical highlights. If you prefer biking, the Traun Ökopfad is a great option. It’s an easy and flat bike route that takes you through the beautiful Traun River Valley.

Best Things to Do in Traun: Activities and Tours

If you’re looking for an adventure, Traun offers several activities that you can enjoy during your visit. One of the most popular activities is paragliding. Take to the skies and enjoy stunning views of Traun and the surrounding mountains. If you want to explore Traun’s waterways, you can go kayaking or canoeing. Traun also has several golf courses and horseback riding facilities. Finally, Traun offers several tours that take you around the town’s attractions and offer insights into the town’s history and culture.

Week-Long Itinerary for Traun

If you’re planning to spend a week in Traun, here’s a sample itinerary:

  • Day 1: Visit Schloss Traun and the Stadtmuseum Traun.
  • Day 2: Explore the Traun Donaupark and take a tour of the town.
  • Day 3: Go paragliding or hiking on the Traunfall hiking trail.
  • Day 4: Relax at the Pleschinger See and enjoy the beautiful scenery.
  • Day 5: Take a bike tour of the Traun Ökopfad.
  • Day 6: Visit the Linzerberg and go on a horseback riding tour.
  • Day 7: Explore the town and sample the local cuisine.

Food and Drink Scene of Traun

Must-Try Dishes

Traun has a rich culinary tradition, and there are several local dishes that you must try during your visit. One of the most popular dishes is the ‘Guglhupf,’ which is a sweet cake that’s usually served for breakfast or dessert. Another local favorite is the ‘Linzer Torte,’ which is a type of pastry that’s filled with raspberry jam and covered with lattice pastry. Finally, the ‘Eierschwammerl’ (chanterelle mushrooms) is a popular ingredient in several dishes, including soups, stews, and risottos.

Local Beverages

Traun is in the heart of Austria’s wine region, and there are several local wineries that offer tastings and tours. Some of the best-known wines from the Upper Austrian region include Gruner Veltliner and Riesling. Traun also has several breweries that produce local beers, including the Traunseer Bier and the Rieder Bier.

Where to Stay in Traun: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Traun has several accommodations options that cater to different budgets and needs. If you’re looking for a luxury stay, the Stadthotel and the Hotel Almtalerhof are two great options. If you’re on a budget, you can stay at the Jugendgästehaus Linz or the Pension Bergheuriger. Traun also has several guesthouses and holiday apartments that offer a more cozy and homely feel.

What to Buy in Traun: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

One of the best ways to remember your visit to Traun is by buying souvenirs and local products. Some of the must-buy souvenirs include the handcrafted pottery made by local artisans, which you can find at the Traun Pottery Museum. You can also buy a bottle of local wine or beer as a gift or souvenir. Finally, you can buy traditional Austrian clothing, such as the Dirndl or Lederhosen, which are available in several shops in Traun.

Local Culture and Customs of Traun

Austria has a rich culture and history, and Traun is no exception. The locals are proud of their traditions and customs, and visitors are encouraged to immerse themselves in the local culture. One of the most important customs is the ‘Krampuslauf,’ a traditional parade that takes place during the Christmas season. Another important tradition is the ‘Ostermarkt,’ an Easter market that features local crafts and delicacies.

Best Time to Visit Traun

The best time to visit Traun is during the summer months, from June to August when the weather is warm and sunny, and the outdoor activities are in full swing. However, Traun has something to offer all year round. The winter months are especially beautiful, with the Christmas markets and winter sports activities.

Safety and Health in Traun

Safety in Traun

Traun is a safe town, and visitors don’t have to worry about their safety. However, it’s always advisable to take precautions, especially when engaging in outdoor activities. Always follow the safety guidelines and instructions when hiking, biking, or paragliding.

Health in Traun

Traun has several medical facilities that offer medical care services to both residents and visitors. The town also has several pharmacies that sell over-the-counter medicines.

Getting to Traun: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Traun

The nearest airport to Traun is the Linz International Airport, which is located 10 kilometers from Traun. From the airport, you can take a taxi or a bus to Traun.

Bus Stations Near Traun

Traun has several bus stations that connect the town to other cities and towns in the region. The most prominent bus stations are the Traun Bahnhof and the Traun Industriezeile.

Other Options

Traun is well connected to the rest of the Upper Austrian region, and you can also reach the town by train or car.

How to Get Around in Traun

Traun is a small town, and the best way to explore it is on foot. However, you can also use a taxi or a bike to move around the town. Traun also has a public transportation system that connects the town to other nearby cities and towns.

Where to Go Next from Traun: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

There are several nearby destinations and excursions that you can explore from Traun. One of the most popular destinations is the city of Linz, which is located just 11 kilometers from Traun. Linz is the third-largest city in Austria and offers several cultural attractions, including museums, galleries, and theaters. Another popular destination is the Hallstatt, a picturesque town located near the Hallstätter See.

Conclusion

Traun is a beautiful town that offers visitors a unique blend of history, nature, and modern vibes. With its stunning landscapes, cultural attractions, and recreational activities, Traun has something for everyone. Whether you’re an outdoor enthusiast or a culture lover, Traun is a destination that you must visit. Use this comprehensive travel guide to plan your trip to Traun and make the most of your visit to this beautiful Austrian town.
