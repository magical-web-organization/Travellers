Introduction to Upper Austria

Upper Austria, an administrative district in the northern part of Austria, is a must-visit destination for travelers seeking to explore culturally rich cities, scenic landscapes, and outdoor adventures. The region is home to some of the most visited places in Austria, including the beautiful city of Linz, Salzkammergut (a stunning lake district), and the Dachstein Mountains, with endless opportunities for outdoor activities like hiking, cycling, and skiing.

List of Places to Visit in Upper Austria

Upper Austria offers a perfect blend of man-made and natural wonders, ranging from elegant museums to verdant landscapes. Here are the top places to visit in Upper Austria.

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • The Ars Electronica Center: A museum of modern and contemporary art, science, and technology.
  • Linz Castle: A historical landmark that houses a museum showcasing the rich culture and history of Upper Austria.
  • Mauthausen Memorial: A former concentration camp, which now serves as a tribute to the victims of World War II.
  • Schloss Ort: A castle located on a beautiful peninsula in the Traunsee Lake.

Natural Attractions of Upper Austria: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Hallstatt: A charming and picturesque village in the Salzkammergut region which has been declared a UNESCO World Heritage site.
  • Dachstein Mountains: A natural wonder that offers breathtaking views and opportunities for hiking, snowboarding, and skiing.
  • Lakes District Salzkammergut: A stunning lake district that offers ample opportunities for water sports, sightseeing, and exploration.
  • Pyhrn-Priel: A stunning mountain region with scenic views, 220 km of hiking trails, and six lakes.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Upper Austria

Upper Austria is a paradise for hikers and bikers, offering a vast network of well-marked trails and routes. Here are some of the best routes to explore:

  • Salzkammergut Lake District Trail: A 350 km long trail along 30 lakes, leading through quaint villages and breathtaking views.
  • Enns Cycle Path: A popular route for cyclists that stretches from the Salzkammergut area, through the Ennstal Valley, and down to the river Danube.
  • Dachstein-Tauern Hiking Trail: A 120 km long hiking trail that navigates through the spectacular Dachstein Mountains.

Best Things to Do in Upper Austria: Activities and Tours

Apart from hiking and cycling, there are plenty of other activities and tours to enjoy in Upper Austria. From spa experiences to cultural city tours, here are the best things to do:

  • Explore the “Linz City of Media Art” by visiting the Ars Electronica Center, the historic castle, and the notable museums present in the city.
  • Enjoy the UNESCO World Heritage-listed Salzburg, known for its majestic castle, baroque architecture, and music festivals.
  • Go on a scenic boat tour on Lake Wolfgang and experience its stunning surroundings and mesmerizing beauty.

Week-Long Itinerary for Upper Austria

Here is a suggested itinerary for a week-long visit to Upper Austria:

  • Day 1: Arrive in Linz and explore the city’s museums, galleries, and parks.
  • Day 2: Take a day trip to the charming and picturesque village of Hallstatt.
  • Day 3: Visit the Salzburg town for its castles, museums, and music festivals.
  • Day 4: Experience the Dachstein Mountains by taking a cable car and enjoying the panoramic views.
  • Day 5: Take a scenic boat tour of Lake Wolfgang and explore the surrounding region.
  • Day 6: Visit the Mauthausen Memorial to pay tribute to the victims of World War II.
  • Day 7: Relax and unwind at one of the many spas in the region.

Food and Drink Scene of Upper Austria

Upper Austria offers a unique culinary experience where local food is served with a twist of international flavors. Here is a list of must-try dishes:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Linzer Torte: A traditional Austrian dessert made with a crust of ground nuts and a filling of raspberry jam.
  • Griesnockerlsuppe: A savory soup with semolina dumplings.
  • Most: A cider-like drink that is popular in Upper Austria.

Local Beverages

  • Grüner Veltliner: A dry white wine with a crisp, fruity aroma.
  • Steyrer Bier: A local beer brewed in Steyr.

Where to Stay in Upper Austria: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Upper Austria offers a variety of accommodation options, ranging from budget-friendly hostels to luxurious hotels. Here are some of the best options:

  • Park Inn by Radisson Linz: A centrally located hotel offering spectacular views of the city and comfortable amenities.
  • Salzkammergut Lodge: A cozy and welcoming hostel that is perfect for travelers on a budget.
  • Villa Langer: A charming bed and breakfast located in the serene countryside, perfect for a peaceful escape.

What to Buy in Upper Austria: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Upper Austria is known for its distinctive handicrafts, ranging from wooden toys to handmade glassware. Here are some of the best souvenirs to buy as a reminder of your visit:

  • Dirndl: A traditional Austrian dress that makes for an excellent keepsake or gift.
  • Linzer Torte: You can purchase authentic, freshly baked Linzer Torte at one of the bakeries in Upper Austria.
  • Handmade Glassware: The region is known for its handmade glassware, which makes for an attractive and unique souvenir.

Local Culture and Customs of Upper Austria

Upper Austria has a rich cultural heritage, which is apparent in the architecture, art, and cultural practices of its citizens. The region is known for its friendly and welcoming locals, who take great pride in their customs and traditions.

Best Time to Visit Upper Austria

The best time to visit Upper Austria is in the spring and summer months (April-September), when the weather is pleasant, and outdoor activities are available. However, the region is stunning year-round, with winter sports and Christmas markets attracting visitors during the winter months.

Do’s and Don’ts of Upper Austria

Do:

  • Respect the customs and traditions of the locals.
  • Take care of the environment and follow the “leave no trace” principle.
  • Dress appropriately when visiting churches and other religious sites.

Don’t:

  • Disrespect the local customs and traditions in any way.
  • Litter or damage the environment in any form.
  • Get involved in any illegal activities during your trip.

Safety and Health

Safety in Upper Austria

Upper Austria is a safe region, and the crime rate is low. However, visitors should take caution and stay alert, especially in crowded areas.

Health in Upper Austria

Upper Austria offers an excellent healthcare system, with well-equipped hospitals and clinics in all major cities and towns. Visitors should carry adequate travel insurance to cover any medical emergencies during their trip.

Getting to Upper Austria: Transportation Options and Logistics

Upper Austria is well-connected to major cities and towns in Austria and neighboring countries. Here are some of the transportation options available:

Airports Near Upper Austria

  • Blue Danube Airport Linz: Located just outside of Linz and serves as the main airport for Upper Austria.
  • Salzburg Airport: Located around an hour and a half away from Upper Austria and serves national and international flights.

Bus Stations Near Upper Austria

  • Central Bus Station Linz: Serves as the central hub for regional and national bus services in Upper Austria.
  • Wels central bus station: Located in the city of Wels and serves as a stop for regional and national bus services.

Other Options

  • Train: Rail connections are available between Upper Austria and other cities in Austria and Europe, with Linz serving as the main hub for railway transport.
  • Car: Visitors can rent a car to drive around Upper Austria and explore its scenic routes and landmarks.

How to Get Around in Upper Austria

Upper Austria offers excellent public transportation services, including buses, trams, and a comprehensive rail network. Bike rentals are also available in major cities, making it easy to explore the region on two wheels.

Where to Go Next from Upper Austria: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Upper Austria is just a short distance away from many other popular destinations, including:

  • Vienna: The capital city of Austria, famous for its art, music, and culture, is located around 1.5 hours from Upper Austria.
  • Salzburg: Another popular destination in Austria, known for its baroque architecture and rich culture.
  • Munich: Located just across the border in Germany, Munich is a vibrant city famous for its beer halls, museums, and art galleries.

Conclusion

Upper Austria is a culturally and naturally rich destination that offers something for every type of traveler. From museums to scenic landscapes and outdoor adventures, Upper Austria is a must-visit destination in Europe.
