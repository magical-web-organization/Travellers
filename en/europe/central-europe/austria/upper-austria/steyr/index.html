Introduction to Steyr

Located in the picturesque region of Upper Austria, Steyr is a charming town that has preserved its historical character while keeping up with modern times. Its cobblestone streets, romantic buildings, and stunning Alpine surroundings make it a perfect destination for travelers searching for an authentic Austrian experience.

With a population of around 40,000, Steyr has a small-town feel with plenty of cultural and natural attractions for visitors to enjoy. From exploring the town’s museums and historical landmarks to hiking in the nearby mountains, Steyr has something for everyone.

List of Places to Visit in Steyr

Whether you’re interested in history, culture, or outdoor adventures, there are many must-visit attractions in Steyr. Here are some of the top places to add to your itinerary:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Bummerlhaus: This 16th-century Renaissance building houses a museum dedicated to the local history and culture of Steyr. It also has a beautiful courtyard and a café.
  • Schloss Lamberg: Overlooking the town, this castle has a history dating back to the 13th century and is now home to the City Museum. Take a tour of the castle and enjoy the panoramic views from the tower.
  • Christkindl Pilgrimage Church: A baroque church located just outside of Steyr in the village of Christkindl. It’s known for the legendary Christkindl Market and is a must-visit during the Christmas season.

Natural Attractions of Steyr: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Tabor Mountain: The perfect place to hike with panoramic views of Steyr and the surrounding Alpine scenery. Take an easy walk or a more challenging hike to the top.
  • Schwarzindien Nature Reserve: A protected nature reserve situated along the Enns River, with plenty of biking and hiking trails, birdwatching, and fishing opportunities.
  • Steyrdurchbruch: A scenic gorge formed by the Steyr River. Take a walk along the river, and see the wild beauty of the area up close.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Steyr

Steyr is a paradise for outdoor enthusiasts, with plenty of hiking and biking trails to explore. The town is situated near the foothills of the Alps, and there are many trails that offer stunning views of the surrounding mountains. Here are some of the best hiking and biking routes:

  • Enns Cycle Path: A 260km-long (162 miles) bike path that follows the Enns River from its source to where it meets the Danube River. It’s a beautiful route that takes in plenty of Austrian culture and natural landscapes.
  • Along the Steyr River: Start from the city center and walk along the river to see the Steyr Gorge, pastures, and charming villages.
  • Kalkalpen National Park: Located south of Steyr, the park has over 200km-long (124 miles) hiking trails. It has diverse flora and fauna and is perfect for adventure seekers and nature lovers.

Best Things to Do in Steyr: Activities and Tours

Steyr offers plenty of activities and tours for travelers to experience the town’s history, culture, and natural beauty. Here are some of the best things to do in Steyr:

  • Steyr Tram Tour: Take a ride on a nostalgic tram and explore Steyr’s charming Old Town. The tour covers all the major attractions, including the castle and the Bummerlhaus.
  • Horse-Drawn Carriage Ride: Enjoy a romantic ride through the picturesque streets of Steyr in a horse-drawn carriage while taking in the stunning views of the Alps and town.
  • Beer Tour: Visit some of the town’s breweries and learn about the history of beer in Steyr while sampling some of the best local brews.

Food and Drink Scene of Steyr

Steyr’s food and drink scene is focused on local ingredients and traditional recipes. Here are some of the must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Goulash: A hearty stew made with beef or pork, onions, paprika, and spices.
  • Wiener Schnitzel: A breaded and fried meat cutlet, usually made with veal and served with potatoes and lingonberry sauce.
  • Strudel: A sweet or savory pastry made with thin layers of dough filled with fruit, nuts, or vegetables.

Local Beverages

  • Beer: Steyr is known for its excellent beer, with a long tradition of brewing. Try a Steyrer Gold, a local institution with a 400-year-old history.
  • Cider: A refreshing apple cider made with locally grown apples and sometimes mixed with other fruit juices like blackcurrant.

Where to Stay in Steyr: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Steyr has a range of accommodation options suitable for every budget and preference. Here are some of the best places to stay:

  • Hotel & Restaurant Minichmayr: A centrally located historic hotel with elegant rooms and an excellent on-site restaurant.
  • Mader Hostel: A budget-friendly option situated in a historical building in the heart of the Old Town.
  • Gasthof Bauer: A charming countryside guesthouse overlooking the Enns Valley, with comfortable rooms and cozy indoor and outdoor dining areas.

What to Buy in Steyr: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

When in Steyr, you should bring home some unique souvenirs to remember your trip. Here are some local products to look out for:

  • Eisstockschießen: A traditional Austrian sport similar to curling, where players slide ice stocks towards a wooden target. You can buy miniature ice stocks to take home as a souvenir.
  • Steyrer Gold: The town’s famous beer is available in bottles or cans to take home.
  • Woodwork: Steyr is known for its skilled woodworkers who make hand-carved souvenirs like bowls, boxes, and ornaments. You can find them in gift shops throughout town.

Local Culture and Customs of Steyr

Austria has a rich culture and history, and Steyr is no exception. Here are some of the local customs and traditions to keep in mind when visiting:

  • Greetings: When meeting someone for the first time, it’s polite to shake their hand. When saying goodbye, it’s customary to say “Auf Wiedersehen.”
  • Etiquette: Austrians value punctuality and keeping appointments. It’s also essential to dress appropriately for different occasions, such as visits to churches or formal events.
  • Tipping: It’s customary to round up the bill and leave a small tip of 5-10%.

Best Time to Visit Steyr

The best time to visit Steyr depends on your interests and preferences. The town has a temperate climate with mild summers and cold winters. Here are some things to consider when deciding on the time to visit:

  • Summer (June to August): The weather is pleasant, and there are many outdoor activities to enjoy, like hiking and biking. However, it’s peak tourist season, and prices are higher.
  • Fall (September to November): The foliage is beautiful, and there are many harvest festivals and events to attend. The weather can be unpredictable, and some tourist attractions may have limited hours.
  • Winter (December to February): The town becomes a winter wonderland with Christmas markets, ice skating, and skiing. It’s an excellent time to experience traditional Austrian cuisine and customs. The weather can be cold and snowy, and some attractions may be closed.
  • Spring (March to May): The flowers are blooming, and there are fewer tourists. The weather can be mild, but some outdoor activities may be limited.

Do’s and Don’ts of Steyr

To have a pleasant and respectful experience in Steyr, here are some do’s and don’ts to keep in mind:

Do:

  • Be respectful of cultural differences: Austrians appreciate when visitors respect their customs and traditions.
  • Pack appropriate clothing: Depending on the time of year you visit, it’s essential to bring the appropriate clothing for the weather and activities.
  • Try the local cuisine: Austria has a rich culinary tradition, and Steyr has plenty of unique dishes and drinks to try.

Don’t:

  • Be loud or disruptive: Austrians value peace and tranquility, and it’s essential to avoid making excessive noise or being disruptive in public places.
  • Ignore etiquette: It’s crucial to be respectful and courteous when interacting with locals or visiting tourist attractions.
  • Waste food: It’s considered impolite to waste food or leave a significant amount on your plate, so it’s essential to order only what you can eat.

Safety and Health

Steyr is a generally safe destination for travelers, with low crime rates and a well-functioning healthcare system. However, it’s essential to take some precautions and be aware of the following:

Safety in Steyr

  • Be aware of the weather: The Alpine region experiences rapidly changing weather conditions, so it’s crucial to be prepared and dress accordingly, especially while hiking or biking.
  • Watch your belongings: Although Steyr is a safe destination, it’s still important to keep your belongings safe, especially in crowded areas.

Health in Steyr

  • Stay hydrated: It’s essential to drink plenty of water, especially during hot summer months or while hiking.
  • Be aware of ticks: Steyr is in a region where ticks can carry diseases. It’s important to take the necessary precautions while exploring nature, such as wearing long sleeves and pants and using insect repellent.

Getting to Steyr: Transportation Options and Logistics

Steyr is easily accessible by various modes of transportation, making it a convenient destination to visit. Here are some of the options:

Airports Near Steyr

  • Linz Airport (LNZ): The closest airport, located around 20km (12 miles) from Steyr. It offers flights to several European cities, including Copenhagen, London, and Frankfurt.

Bus/Train Stations Near Steyr

  • Steyr Train Station: The train station is located in the heart of the town and offers connections to major Austrian cities like Vienna, Salzburg, and Linz.
  • Steyr Bus Station: The bus station is located next to the train station and offers local and regional connections to nearby towns and cities.

Other Options

  • Car Rental: Renting a car is a convenient way to explore Steyr and the surrounding areas, especially if you plan on doing outdoor activities or visiting nearby destinations.

How to Get Around Steyr

Steyr is a small town that you can easily explore on foot, but there are also other options for getting around. Here are some of the most common ones:

  • Public Transportation: Steyr has an efficient public transportation system, with buses and trams connecting different parts of the town.
  • Biking: Steyr has numerous bike rental shops and many bike trails to explore the town and surrounding areas.
  • Car Rental: Renting a car is a great idea if you plan on exploring the surrounding areas, especially the mountains and nearby national parks.

Where to Go Next from Steyr: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Steyr is situated in a prime location, making it easy to visit other nearby destinations and excursions within a day trip. Here are some of the best places to visit:

  • Linz: The capital city of Upper Austria, Linz is an excellent destination for art lovers and history buffs. It’s a vibrant city with a beautiful old town, galleries, and museums.
  • Salzburg: The city of Salzburg is a UNESCO World Heritage Site and well-known for its baroque architecture, music, and cultural history. It is home to several iconic landmarks like the Hohensalzburg Fortress.
  • Hallstatt: A beautiful small town situated on the shores of Lake Hallstatt and the Dachstein Mountains. Hallstatt is famous for its salt mines, stunning scenery, and rich history.

Conclusion

Steyr is a hidden gem in Upper Austria that offers a perfect balance of culture, history, and natural beauty. Whether you’re interested in hiking, biking, exploring museums, or just soaking up the local culture, Steyr has something for you. Plan your trip with this comprehensive guide, and experience the best of what this charming town has to offer.
