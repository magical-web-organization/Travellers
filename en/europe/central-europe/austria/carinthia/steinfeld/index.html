Introduction to Steinfeld

Nestled in the picturesque region of Carinthia, Austria, in Central Europe, Steinfeld is a charming little town that offers a perfect blend of history, culture, and nature. With its stunning Alpine backdrop, Steinfeld is a popular destination for hikers, bikers, and nature enthusiasts. The town boasts rich cultural heritage, exquisite cuisine, beautiful architecture, and friendly locals. Whether you’re visiting Steinfeld for a day or a week, there’s plenty to keep you occupied.

List of Places to Visit in Steinfeld

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Steinfeld Abbey: Founded in 1091, the Steinfeld Abbey is an architectural masterpiece, showcasing Baroque and Gothic elements. The abbey is well-known for its religious and educational significance.

  • Steinfeld Open Air Museum: Discover the history and culture of Steinfeld as you take a stroll through the open-air museum. The museum features historical buildings, exhibits, and demonstrations of traditional crafts.

  • St. John the Baptist Church: Built-in the 16th century, the St. John the Baptist Church is an impressive example of Gothic architecture. Its colorful frescoes and carved altars are some of the highlights of the church.

Natural Attractions of Steinfeld: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Lake Millstatt: Located just a few kilometers away from Steinfeld, Lake Millstatt is a stunning natural attraction that’s perfect for swimming, boating, and fishing.

  • Gurktal Alps: The Gurktal Alps form a beautiful backdrop to Steinfeld. The area is popular for hiking and mountain biking, and you can easily find trails that cater to different skill levels and preferences.

  • Heinrich Harrer Museum: A must-visit for mountaineering enthusiasts, the Heinrich Harrer Museum celebrates the life and achievements of the famous Austrian mountaineer. The museum features a collection of artifacts, photographs, and memorabilia related to Harrer’s expeditions.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Steinfeld

  • Gurktaler Alpenrunde: This 57.3 km route takes you along the picturesque Gurktaler Alps. The trail is suitable for experienced hikers and cyclists.

  • Millstätter Alpe: The Millstätter Alpe trail is a moderate 32 km route that offers stunning panoramic views of Lake Millstatt and the surrounding mountains.

  • Glocknerrunde: This challenging 165-km route takes you through the Hohe Tauern National Park, the largest nature reserve in the Alps. The trail offers breathtaking views of the Glockner group of mountains.

Best Things to Do in Steinfeld: Activities and Tours

  • Explore the town: Take a walk around Steinfeld’s old town and marvel at its beautiful architecture. Be sure to visit the Steinfeld Abbey and the St. John the Baptist Church along the way.

  • Go on a nature adventure: Hike or bike through the Gurktaler Alps or take a dip in the refreshing waters of Lake Millstatt.

  • Join a tour: Relax and let a local guide show you the best sights and hidden gems of Steinfeld.

Food and Drink Scene of Steinfeld

Must-Try Dishes

  • Kaiserschmarrn: This sweet dessert is a favorite among locals and is usually served with plum compote or berry sauce.

  • Golatschen: A pastry filled with sweetened cream cheese, poppy seeds, or fruit.

  • Krapfen: A sweet fried doughnut filled with jam or custard.

Local Beverages

  • Gurktaler Alpenkräuter: A herbal liqueur that’s made from a blend of 42 alpine herbs.

  • Weissburgunder: A white wine that’s produced in the region.

  • Gelber Muskateller: A fruity white wine that’s made from Muscat grapes.

Where to Stay in Steinfeld: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Hotel Steinfeld - A modern hotel that offers comfortable and spacious rooms. The hotel is located in the heart of Steinfeld and is close to all major attractions.

  • Gasthof Zum Goldenen Löwen - A traditional guesthouse that’s famous for its homemade dishes and cozy atmosphere.

  • Jugendherberge Steinfeld - A budget-friendly hostel that’s ideal for backpackers and solo travelers. The hostel offers dorms and private rooms and is located in a quiet neighborhood.

What to Buy in Steinfeld: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Carinthian Honey: Carinthia is known for its delicious honey, which is made from wildflowers that grow in the region.

  • Handmade Crafts: Shop for unique handmade crafts, such as pottery, glassware, and jewelry, at the Steinfeld Open Air Museum.

  • Gurktaler Alpenkräuter: Bring home a bottle of the famous Gurktaler Alpenkräuter, a herbal liqueur that’s made in the region.

Local Culture and Customs of Steinfeld

  • Music and Dance: Music and dance play an important role in Steinfeld’s culture. Be sure to attend a local festival or event to witness traditional dance performances and music.

  • Etiquette: When in Steinfeld, it’s important to greet people with a handshake and address them by their formal titles unless otherwise invited to use first names.

Best Time to Visit Steinfeld

The best time to visit Steinfeld is between May and September when the weather is mild and perfect for outdoor activities. The peak tourist season is in July and August, so expect crowds and higher prices during this time.

Do’s and Don’ts of Steinfeld

Do:

  • Respect the local culture and customs.

  • Take care of the natural environment and avoid littering.

  • Try the local cuisine and beverages.

Don’t:

  • Wear inappropriate clothing when visiting religious or cultural sites.

  • Disturb the natural wildlife when hiking or biking.

  • Ignore basic social etiquette.

Safety and Health

Safety in Steinfeld

Steinfeld is a safe destination, but it’s still important to take precautions like you would in any other foreign country.

  • Keep your belongings safe and be alert to pickpockets in crowded areas.

  • Check the weather forecast before heading out on outdoor activities.

  • Follow safety guidelines and instructions when exploring the Gurktaler Alps.

Health in Steinfeld

  • Bring necessary medications and prescription drugs with you.

  • Be mindful of food hygiene and drink only bottled water.

  • Bring sunscreen and protective gear if engaging in outdoor activities.

Getting to Steinfeld: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Steinfeld

The nearest airport to Steinfeld is the Klagenfurt Airport, which is approximately 38 km away. From the airport, you can take the train or bus to Steinfeld.

Bus Stations Near Steinfeld

The Steinfeld bus station is located in the center of town and is serviced by regional buses.

Other Options

You can also reach Steinfeld by train via the Spittal-Millstättersee station, which is about 15 km away from Steinfeld.

How to Get Around in Steinfeld

Steinfeld is a small town, and it’s easy to explore by foot or bike. Taxis are also available if you need them. If you plan on exploring the surrounding area, renting a car is recommended.

Where to Go Next from Steinfeld: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Klagenfurt: The capital of Carinthia, Klagenfurt, is a vibrant city that’s famous for its Renaissance architecture, parks, and cultural attractions.

  • Villach: Villach is a beautiful city that’s situated on the banks of the Drau River. It’s known for its festivals and events, outdoor activities, and historic landmarks.

  • Ljubljana: The Slovenian capital of Ljubljana is only a 2-hour drive from Steinfeld. The city is known for its charming old town, stunning bridges, and vibrant cultural scene.

Conclusion

Steinfeld is a beautiful and charming destination that offers a perfect blend of culture, nature, and adventure. From exploring the town’s rich history and culture to hiking the picturesque Gurktaler Alps, there’s something for everyone in Steinfeld. So pack your bags and start planning your trip to this hidden gem in Carinthia, Austria.
