Introduction to Spittal an der Drau

Nestled in the heart of Carinthia, Spittal an der Drau is a small town in Austria that boasts stunning natural scenery, a rich cultural history, and plenty of outdoor adventures for every type of traveler. Whether you prefer hiking, biking, skiing or simply admiring the beautiful landscapes, Spittal an der Drau is the perfect destination for a memorable and picturesque vacation in Central Europe.

List of Places to Visit in Spittal an der Drau

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Spittal an der Drau is home to several significant cultural sites. Visit the impressive Renaissance-era Schloss Porcia, a quirky castle filled with art, sculptures, and historical artifacts. Another must-visit location is the Roman Museum, which features exhibits on the rich ancient Roman history of the region. The Spittal Museum is also worth a visit for its impressive collection of medieval artifacts and exhibits on the history of the town.

Natural Attractions of Spittal an der Drau: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

The countryside surrounding Spittal an der Drau is known for its stunning alpine peaks, pristine lakes, and lush forests perfect for outdoor exploring. Take a hike up to the summit of Goldeck for panoramic views of the surrounding region or explore the Nockberge National Park with its enchanting landscapes and hidden valleys. In the winter, skiers can hit the slopes at the nearby skiing destination of Bad Kleinkirchheim.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Spittal an der Drau

The Spittal an der Drau region is home to some of the most scenic hiking and biking trails in Austria. For a scenic and moderately paced hike, we recommend the 12-kilometer route around Millstätter See, which offers breathtaking lake views and lush forest trails. For a more challenging experience, head up to the summit of the Goldeck mountain, which offers unparalleled summit views and beautiful alpine landscapes. Looking to hit the bike trails? The Drauradweg cycling path follows the course of the river Drau, offering a 366-kilometer flat and scenic route ideal for cyclists of all skill levels.

Best Things to Do in Spittal an der Drau: Activities and Tours

In Spittal an der Drau, there is never a shortage of fun activities and tours for every type of traveler. Take a guided rafting tour down the river Drau, explore the region’s rich culinary heritage with a food and wine tour, or visit one of the local farms for a taste of authentic Austrian rural life. For something a little more unique, try a tandem paragliding or hot air balloon experience, both offering unforgettable bird’s-eye views of the surrounding area.

Week-Long Itinerary for Spittal an der Drau

Here’s an ideal itinerary for a week-long trip to Spittal an der Drau:

  • Day 1: Arrival and check-in in Spittal an der Drau, transfer to the Schloss Porcia for a guided tour
  • Day 2: Hiking at the Goldeck mountain, followed by a traditional Austrian dinner at a local eatery
  • Day 3: A guided bike tour of the region on the Drauradweg, with stops at local wineries and farms
  • Day 4: Off-road Jeep tour of the nearby Alps, with stops at hidden waterfalls and mountain lakes
  • Day 5: A day trip to the historic city of Klagenfurt, with guided tours of the city’s museums, art galleries, and landmarks
  • Day 6: Skiing day in the nearby skiing destination of Bad Kleinkirchheim
  • Day 7: Departure from Spittal an der Drau

Food and Drink Scene of Spittal an der Drau

Must-Try Dishes

The Spittal an der Drau region is famous for its hearty and delicious cuisine, with a variety of dishes that reflect the area’s rich cultural history. Must-try dishes include Kaiserschmarren, a sweet and fluffy pancake topped with powdered sugar and served with fruit compote, as well as Kasnudel, a traditional pasta dish filled with cheese and served with butter sauce. Other local specialties include Carinthian-style sausages and freshly baked rye bread.

Local Beverages

The Carinthian region is also famous for its excellent wines, including a range of white wines made from local grapes. For beer lovers, the region boasts several local breweries offering a range of excellent brews, including the classic Goldfassl Pilsner and the Zwickl Kellerbier. Additionally, herbal schnapps and liquors made from local ingredients such as cranberries and juniper berries are also popular.

Where to Stay in Spittal an der Drau: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Spittal an der Drau offers a range of accommodation options to suit any budget. A few local favorites include Hotel Ertl, a modern and centrally located hotel and spa, Hotel Edlingerwirt, offering rustic charm and excellent dining, and Jugendhotel Egger, a clean and budget-friendly hostel with stunning mountain views.

What to Buy in Spittal an der Drau: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

The region around Spittal an der Drau is known for its high-quality handmade crafts and artisanal products. Some popular local products include handmade ceramics and pottery, hand-carved wooden home decorations, and traditional woven textiles. Additionally, the region is known for its excellent honey products, including honey-infused skincare products and delicious honey-based treats.

Local Culture and Customs of Spittal an der Drau

The people of Spittal an der Drau are known for their warm hospitality and strong sense of community. Local customs and traditions include hearty and delicious traditional cuisine, local festivals celebrating the harvest season, and handicraft markets featuring locally made goods. Visitors should also be aware of Austrian etiquette, which includes being punctual and polite, and removing shoes before entering some homes and public places.

Best Time to Visit Spittal an der Drau

The best time to visit Spittal an der Drau is between May and October, when the weather is mild and outdoor activities are plentiful. From skiing in the winter to hiking and biking in the summer, there is always plenty to do in this beautiful alpine region. Springtime brings blooming wildflowers, while autumn offers stunning fall foliage and the chance to sample local food at harvest festivals.

Do’s and Don’ts of Spittal an der Drau

Do:

  • Respect local customs and traditions
  • Try local foods and beverages
  • Be mindful of outdoor etiquette, such as taking your trash with you and staying on marked trails
  • Use “Sie” instead of “du” when speaking to older or more senior Austrian people

Don’t:

  • Eat or drink in churches or public transportation
  • Talk loudly or aggressively in public places
  • Wear shoes inside some homes or public places
  • Jaywalk or disobey traffic rules

Safety and Health

Safety in Spittal an der Drau

Spittal an der Drau is a very safe destination with low levels of violent crime. However, visitors should be aware of pickpocketing and other minor crimes in crowded tourist areas. Additionally, it is essential to be cautious when participating in outdoor activities such as hiking and skiing, ensuring that you have appropriate gear and do not take unnecessary risks.

Health in Spittal an der Drau

The water in Spittal an der Drau is safe to drink, and visitors should not need any vaccinations before traveling to the area. However, it is always advisable to have comprehensive travel insurance to cover medical emergencies and accidents.

Getting to Spittal an der Drau: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Spittal an der Drau

The closest airport to Spittal an der Drau is Klagenfurt Airport, located about 70 kilometers away. Visitors can also access the region via Salzburg Airport, located about 180 kilometers away, or Ljubljana Airport in Slovenia, located about 100 kilometers away.

Bus Stations Near Spittal an der Drau

Spittal an der Drau is well connected with frequent bus connections provided by Flixbus, ÖBB, and other coach operators. The town also has a local bus service, which provides connections to nearby towns and attractions.

Other Options

Visitors can also travel to Spittal an der Drau via train, with several routes connecting the town with major cities throughout Austria and Europe. Additionally, car rental is a popular option for visitors who want to explore the region at their own pace.

How to Get Around in Spittal an der Drau

Spittal an der Drau is a relatively small town that is easy to navigate on foot or by local bus. For visitors who want to explore the surrounding countryside and nearby attractions, renting a car is a good option. The area’s hiking and biking trails are also easily accessible via public transportation, with several buses and trains connecting the region’s major trailheads and starting points.

Where to Go Next from Spittal an der Drau: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Spittal an der Drau is located in the heart of Carinthia, making it an excellent base for exploring the surrounding area. Nearby destinations worth visiting include the historic city of Klagenfurt, the picturesque town of Villach, and the stunning lakeside resort of Hallstatt. Additionally, the nearby National Park Nockberge and the ski resort of Bad Kleinkirchheim offer excellent outdoor activities and adventures.

Conclusion

Spittal an der Drau is a hidden gem in the heart of Austria, offering stunning natural scenery, a rich cultural history, and plenty of outdoor adventures for every type of traveler. Whether you’re looking to hike, bike, ski, or simply relax and soak up the local culture, Spittal an der Drau is the perfect destination for a unique and memorable vacation in Central Europe.
