Introduction to Outer East

Outer East is a lively district in Vienna, Austria, filled with culturally important attractions, natural wonders, and a vibrant food and drink scene. Whether you’re a history buff, an outdoor enthusiast, or a foodie, there’s something for everyone in Outer East.

List of Places to Visit in Outer East

Here are some of the must-see attractions in Outer East:

Culturally Important Attractions

  • The Museum of Military History: This museum showcases the rich military history of Austria and its role in world events.
  • The Zentralfriedhof: A famous cemetery in Vienna, where many famous musicians and artists are buried, including Mozart and Beethoven.
  • Belvedere Palace: A beautiful Baroque palace and gardens that house an impressive collection of Austrian art.

Natural Attractions of Outer East

  • The Vienna Woods: A vast forest in the outskirts of Vienna, offering numerous hiking trails and scenic views.
  • The Danube River: Take a boat ride and enjoy the picturesque scenery or go for a swim in designated areas.
  • Donau-Auen National Park: A vast wetland bordering the Danube, with a diverse ecosystem and wildlife.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Outer East

Outer East offers several hiking and biking routes, catering to different levels of fitness and experience. Some of the popular ones include:

  • The Vienna Woods Trail: A 120 km long-distance trail through the Vienna Woods, with stunning views and picturesque villages.
  • The Danube Cycle Path: A 365 km long-distance cycling route along the Danube River, spanning Austria, Germany, and Hungary.
  • The Wiener Alpenbogen: A circular hiking trail around the Vienna Alps, with numerous peaks and valleys to explore.

Best Things to Do in Outer East: Activities and Tours

Here are some recommended activities and tours to experience the best of Outer East:

  • Vienna Food Tour: Sample delicious Austrian dishes and drinks, led by a knowledgeable local guide.
  • Viennese Wine Tour: Discover the wine culture of Austria, visiting vineyards and wine cellars in the Vienna Woods.
  • Urban Hike and City Tour: Explore the city on foot, with a mixture of urban and natural scenery.

Week-Long Itinerary for Outer East

Here’s a suggested itinerary for a week-long trip to Outer East:

Day Activity
1 Explore the Museum of Military History and nearby Belvedere Palace
2 Cycle along the Danube River to Donau-Auen National Park
3 Take a day trip to the picturesque town of Klosterneuburg
4 Hike to the peak of Kahlenberg, with stunning views of Vienna
5 Take a food tour of Outer East, sampling local delicacies and wine
6 Visit the Zentralfriedhof and the nearby thermal baths
7 Final day to relax and enjoy the city on your own.

Food and Drink Scene of Outer East

Outer East has a vibrant food and drink scene, with plenty of local specialties to try. Here are some must-try dishes:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Wiener Schnitzel: A classic Austrian dish, made with breaded veal or pork, served with potato salad or fries.
  • Tafelspitz: Boiled beef in broth, served with root vegetables.
  • Sachertorte: A rich chocolate cake, invented in Vienna.

Local Beverages

  • Wiener Gemischter Satz: A white wine blend, unique to Vienna, made from grapes grown in multiple vineyards.
  • Ottakringer: A locally brewed beer, available in various flavors.
  • Melange: A Viennese coffee drink, similar to a cappuccino but with more milk.

Where to Stay in Outer East: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Outer East has a variety of accommodation options, ranging from budget-friendly hostels to luxurious hotels. Here are some recommended options:

  • MEININGER Hotel Vienna Downtown Franz: A modern and affordable option in the heart of the city.
  • Best Western Plus Amedia Wien: A stylish and comfortable hotel, with easy access to public transportation.
  • Hotel Donaustadt Kagran: A cozy and traditional guesthouse, located near the Danube River.

What to Buy in Outer East: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

If you’re looking for souvenirs or local products to take home, here are some ideas:

  • Porcelain from Augarten: A famous porcelain manufacturer in Vienna, offering high-quality products with intricate designs.
  • Mozartkugeln: A famous Austrian chocolate wrapped in silver foil, with a marzipan and nougat filling.
  • Wachau Apricots: A type of apricot grown in the Wachau Valley, near Vienna, known for their sweetness and juicy flavor.

Local Culture and Customs of Outer East

Visitors to Outer East should be aware of local customs and traditions, such as:

  • Greeting people with a handshake, even in informal settings.
  • Refraining from smoking in public areas, as it is prohibited in most places.
  • Observing Sunday as a day of rest and avoiding loud activities, as many shops and restaurants are closed.

Best Time to Visit Outer East

The best time to visit Outer East is during the spring and fall, when the weather is mild and there are fewer crowds. However, the district is also worth exploring in the summer, when outdoor activities are at their prime, and the winter, when the city is magical with its Christmas markets.

Do’s and Don’ts of Outer East

Here are some do’s and don’ts to keep in mind when visiting Outer East:

Do:

  • Greet people with a handshake.
  • Observe local customs and traditions.
  • Try local food and drinks.

Don’t:

  • Smoke in public areas, as it is prohibited in most places.
  • Underdress for formal occasions, such as visiting museums or theaters.
  • Skip tipping in restaurants and cafes.

Safety and Health

Safety in Outer East

Outer East is a relatively safe district, but it’s always a good idea to take precautions, such as:

  • Avoiding poorly lit areas at night.
  • Watching out for pickpockets in crowded places.
  • Keeping an eye on your belongings when using public transportation.

Health in Outer East

Austria has a high standard of healthcare, with both public and private facilities available. It’s recommended to have travel insurance, and to bring any necessary medication with you.

Getting to Outer East: Transportation Options and Logistics

  • Vienna International Airport: Located approximately 20 km from Outer East, offering connections to various European and international destinations.

Bus Stations Near Outer East

  • Erdberg Bus Terminal: A major bus terminal in Vienna, located near the city center, offering connections to many Central and Eastern European destinations.

Other Options

  • Train: Vienna has a well-developed train system, offering connections to many European destinations.
  • Car: Driving to Vienna is possible, but be aware of different traffic laws and regulations.

How to Get Around in Outer East

Outer East is well-connected by public transportation, with several options available:

  • U-Bahn (subway): The U-Bahn network is extensive in Vienna, with several stations in Outer East.
  • Bus: Buses are a convenient way to explore Outer East, with several lines connecting different neighborhoods.
  • Tram: The tram network is also widespread in Vienna, offering scenic routes through different parts of the city.

Where to Go Next from Outer East: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

If you have time to explore further, here are some nearby destinations and excursions to consider:

  • Bratislava, Slovakia: Only an hour away from Vienna by train or boat, Bratislava is a picturesque city with a rich history and cultural heritage.
  • Burgenland: A wine-growing region south of Vienna, offering beautiful landscapes and quaint villages.
  • Graz: A charming city in southern Austria, known for its cultural and culinary scene.

Conclusion

Outer East is a district of Vienna, Austria, filled with fascinating history, beautiful nature, and delicious food. Whether you’re a first-time visitor or a local, there’s always something new to discover in this vibrant part of the city.
