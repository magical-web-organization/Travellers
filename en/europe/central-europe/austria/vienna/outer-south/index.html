Introduction to Outer South

Located in Vienna, Austria, Outer South is a peaceful and verdant district with a mix of cultural and natural attractions. Spread over an area of 49.02 km², Outer South is bordered by the Danube River to the east and the Vienna Woods to the west. Due to its suburban setting, this district is perfect for travelers who wish to relax and unwind while still being close enough to explore the city’s urban core.

List of Places to Visit in Outer South

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  1. Heeresgeschichtliches Museum - A museum that showcases the history of the Austrian army and its role in various wars and conflicts over the years.
  2. Schönbrunn Palace - A stunning imperial palace that was the summer residence of the Habsburg emperors. The palace is surrounded by a beautiful park and gardens.
  3. Imperial Furniture Collection - A museum that showcases the evolution of furniture design under the Habsburg monarchy. It features an impressive collection of furniture, interiors, and decorations.

Natural Attractions of Outer South: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  1. Lainzer Tiergarten - A massive park in the west of the district, famous for its wildlife such as wild boar, fallow deer, and mouflon. It offers several hiking and cycling routes and a stunning view of the city from the Hermesvilla observation platform.
  2. Riederberg Mountain - A small mountain with beautiful scenic views and several hiking trails. It also has a small chapel at the top with a statue of Saint Anne.
  3. Alte Donau - A former sidearm of the Danube River that is now a recreational lake with several swimming and watersport activities.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Outer South

  1. Vienna Woods hiking trail - A 120 km long hiking trail that leads through the picturesque Vienna Woods, starting at the Wienerwaldgürtel in the north and winding its way through the district to the southern Vienna city limits.
  2. Lainzer Tiergarten cycling route - A 10 km long cycling trail that offers stunning views of Vienna’s skyline and takes you through the heart of the Lainzer Tiergarten park.
  3. Bisamberg hiking trail - A 25 km long trail that leads through the Bisamberg Hills, offering stunning views of the Danube Valley.

Best Things to Do in Outer South: Activities and Tours

  1. Guided bike tour - Explore the district’s hidden gems on a guided bike tour and learn about its rich history and cultural attractions.
  2. Guided hiking tour - Discover the district’s natural beauty on a guided hiking tour that takes you through the lush green forests and scenic viewpoints.
  3. Wine tasting at Bisamberg Hill - Sample some of the district’s best wines at Bisamberg Hill, a popular wine-growing region.

Food and Drink Scene of Outer South

Must-Try Dishes

  1. Wiener Schnitzel - A classic Austrian dish made from breaded veal cutlets, served with potato salad or fries.
  2. Tafelspitz - A slow-cooked beef dish served with horseradish sauce, fried potatoes, and vegetables.
  3. Sachertorte - A famous chocolate cake with a layer of apricot jam, invented in Vienna.

Local Beverages

  1. Wiener Gemischter Satz - A type of wine that is only produced in the Vienna wine-growing region, made from a mix of white grape varieties.
  2. Sturm - A young wine that is still in the fermentation stage, popular during the autumn season.
  3. Vienna Lager - A local beer with a mild, malty flavor.

Where to Stay in Outer South: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  1. Austria Trend Parkhotel Schönbrunn Wien - A luxurious 4-star hotel located near Schönbrunn Palace.
  2. Hostel Hütteldorf - A budget-friendly hostel that offers private and shared rooms.
  3. Gasthof Gerhart - A charming guesthouse located in the village of Perchtoldsdorf, just a short distance from Vienna.

What to Buy in Outer South: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  1. Wiener Glas - Handcrafted glassware from Vienna that is known for its high quality and unique designs.
  2. Wiener Schmankerl - A variety of local delicacies, such as jams, honey, and mustards, that are made using traditional recipes.
  3. Austria Trend Shop - A souvenir shop that sells traditional Austrian souvenirs, such as Mozartkugeln chocolates and vintage postcards.

Local Culture and Customs of Outer South

Austrian culture is heavily influenced by its history and traditions, and Outer South is no exception. Visitors can experience the district’s culture through its museums, landmarks, and local events such as the Perchtoldsdorf Wine Festival and the Schönbrunn Palace Concerts. It is important to be respectful of local customs, such as greeting people with a handshake and maintaining eye contact during conversations.

Best Time to Visit Outer South

The best time to visit Outer South is during the summer months from June to August, when the weather is warm and sunny, and the parks and gardens are in full bloom. However, the district is also beautiful during the autumn season, when the leaves turn to vibrant shades of red and yellow.

Do’s and Don’ts of Outer South

Do:

  • Learn a few basic German phrases before your trip.
  • Respect local traditions and customs.
  • Try the local cuisine and wines.
  • Explore the outdoor attractions, such as the parks and hiking trails.

Don’t:

  • Wear inappropriate clothing in religious sites.
  • Litter or leave trash in public spaces.
  • Smoke in non-designated areas.
  • Be too loud or disruptive in public spaces.

Safety and Health

Safety in Outer South

Outer South is generally a safe district for tourists, with low levels of crime and few safety concerns. However, visitors should still be vigilant and take necessary precautions, such as keeping an eye on their belongings and avoiding poorly lit or deserted areas at night.

Health in Outer South

Austria has a high standard of healthcare, and there are several hospitals and clinics located in Vienna. Visitors are advised to have travel insurance and to bring any necessary medications or medical items with them.

Getting to Outer South: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Outer South

Vienna International Airport is approximately 20 km from Outer South and is the main international airport in Austria. Visitors can reach the district by taxi, shuttle, or public transportation.

Bus Stations Near Outer South

The Vienna International Bus Station is located in the city center and offers several regional and international bus connections. From there, visitors can reach Outer South by public transportation or taxi.

Other Options

Visitors can also reach Outer South by car or train. The district is well-connected to Vienna’s public transportation network, with several bus and train stations located throughout the district.

How to Get Around in Outer South

The best way to get around Outer South is by public transportation, with several bus and tram lines connecting the district to other parts of the city. The Vienna U-Bahn metro system also serves the district, with several stations located throughout the area.

Where to Go Next from Outer South: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  2. Vienna Woods - Discover the picturesque Vienna Woods, which offer several outdoor activities and scenic views.

Outer South is a beautiful and peaceful district that offers a mix of cultural and outdoor attractions.
