Are you planning a trip to Vienna? Don’t forget to add Neubau, one of the city’s prime districts, to your itinerary. Located in central Vienna, Neubau is among the city’s most charming and trendy neighborhoods. This vibrant district is packed with exciting things to do, places to see, foods to taste, and adventures to experience.

In this Neubau travel guide, we’ll delve into some of the most popular and exciting things visitors can expect to see and do in this trendy Austrian district.

Introduction to Neubau

Neubau is situated on the western side of Vienna’s Inner City, across from Mariahilf. It is accessible via Vienna’s public transport and is just a 20-minute walk from the heart of the city. The district is known for its trendy, European aesthetic and localized feel.

The district is home to various shops, restaurants, and bars, and boasts a great blend of both historical and modern architecture. The vast majority of this district’s materialistic and cultural gems were built between the mid-19th and early 20th century. In recent decades, the area has undergone an extensive transformation, which has greatly contributed to its current appearance.

List of Places to Visit in Neubau

Are you wondering what else Neubau has to offer? Here’s a list of must-visit attractions in the district, broken down into “Culturally important attractions” and “Natural attractions”:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Museum of Technology: This museum features exhibitions that explore the history of technology and science.
  • Leopold Museum: This museum offers various exhibitions of Austrian art from the 19th century onward, featuring artists such as Gustav Klimt, Egon Schiele, and Oskar Kokoschka.
  • Theater an der Wien: A beautiful and historic theatre that regularly hosts operas, musicals, and plays.

Natural Attractions of Neubau: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Stadtpark: Stadtpark is a beautiful park situated in the heart of Vienna, right by the natural water supply of the Wien river. It is popular for walks, picnics, live music and more.
  • Piaristenkeller: a restaurant and bar that features a cozy dining space with a brick-vaulted ceiling, and some of the best views you can enjoy whilst having a brew and a meal.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Neubau

While there are no mountains in Neubau, it still offers some fantastic hiking and biking opportunities.

  • Danube Island: This island is a popular hiking and biking destination and spans over 13 kilometers, complete with cycling paths, fishing platforms, swimmable holes and more. This recreational destination is perfect for those looking for a nature escape!

Best Things to Do in Neubau: Activities and Tours

Neubau is packed with activities and tours. Here are a few:

  • Walking Tour: Join a walking tour of Neubau to learn about the rich history of this fascinating district.
  • Culinary Tour: Join a food tour to sample some of the local cuisine in Neubau.
  • Bike Tour: If you’re looking for an active way to explore the district, join a bike tour of Neubau.

Week-Long Itinerary for Neubau

Here’s a proposed week-long itinerary for exploring and experiencing the best of Neubau:

Day 1: Stadtpark Day 2: Museum of Technology Day 3: Leopold Museum Day 4: Theater an der Wien Day 5: Piaristenkeller Day 6: Danube Island Day 7: Walking Tour of Neubau

Food and Drink Scene of Neubau

Neubau boasts a fantastic food and drink scene. Here are a few must-try dishes:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Schnitzel: This traditional Austrian dish is made with a thin slice of veal that is coated in breadcrumbs and fried until crispy.
  • Sachertorte: This decadent chocolate cake is a Viennese classic and is a must-try dessert while in Neubau.

Local Beverages

  • Beer: Sample some of the local beer in Neubau, including unique blends such as Ottakringer.
  • Wine: Austria is known for its excellent wine, so why not try a glass or bottle of local Austrian wine in Neubau?

Where to Stay in Neubau: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

There are plenty of accommodation options in Neubau. Here are a few of our top-rated options:

  • Motel One Wien-Westbahnhof: This modern hotel offers clean and comfortable rooms, a friendly staff, and a great location in the district.
  • Wombat’s City Hostel Vienna - The Naschmarkt: This hostel offers affordable accommodations with a unique and quirky atmosphere.
  • Hotel Kugel: A charming boutique hotel that features a beautiful courtyard and individually designed rooms.

What to Buy in Neubau: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Looking to bring some of Neubau’s local culture and products home with you? Here are a few great options:

  • Mozartkugeln: These are delicious chocolate confections named after the famous composer, Wolfgang Amadeus Mozart.
  • Dirndl or Lederhosen: These traditional Austrian clothes have become quite fashionable over the years, and are a great souvenir from Neubau.
  • Coffee Beans: Vienna has a unique coffee culture, so why not bring some of the local, robust coffee beans back home with you?

Local Culture and Customs of Neubau

When visiting Neubau, it’s important to respect the local culture and customs. Here are a few things to keep in mind:

  • Austrians typically have formal greetings even during casual conversations, so it’s best to start with a friendly “Good morning!” or “Good afternoon!” depending on the time of day.
  • Tipping between 5% to 10% is a standard practice in Austria.
  • Smoking indoors is not allowed in public areas, and smoking in Austria is only allowed in designated areas.

Best Time to Visit Neubau

The best time to visit Neubau is during the spring (March to May) or autumn (September to October). The temperatures are mild, the crowds are smaller than in peak summer, and the prices are lower than during the summer months.

Do’s and Don’ts of Neubau

Here are a few do’s and don’ts to keep in mind while visiting Neubau:

Do:

  • Do try some of the local delicacies. Austrian cuisine is famous for a reason, and Neubau offers some fantastic dining options.
  • Do bring comfortable shoes. Exploring Neubau on foot is the best way to experience its unique character, so make sure you have comfortable footwear to make the most of your visit.

Don’t:

  • Don’t forget cash. While many places in Neubau accept credit cards, there are still some that do not. Always have some cash with you just in case.
  • Don’t forget to respect the locals. This should be common sense, but just because Neubau is a tourist hub doesn’t mean locals like to be treated like tour attractions. Always be respectful and courteous to the local community.

Safety and Health

Neubau is generally a safe district for tourists. However, as with any travel destination, it’s best to exercise caution and take necessary safety precautionary measures. Here’s some additional information about safety and health in Neubau:

Safety in Neubau

  • Be wary of pickpockets and thieves, especially in crowded areas such as squares or a packed subway train.
  • Keep your passport and other important documents in a hotel safe, and only bring them with you when necessary.

Health in Neubau

  • Tap water is safe to drink in Austria, including in Neubau.
  • Austria has an extensive healthcare system, and visitors can access public or private medical care if necessary.

Getting to Neubau: Transportation Options and Logistics

Getting to Neubau is simple and straightforward. Vienna’s public transportation system is well-developed and covers the entire city. Here are a few ways to get to and around Neubau:

Airports Near Neubau

  • Vienna International Airport: This airport is situated just 17 kilometers from Neubau.

Bus Stations Near Neubau

  • Westbahnhof: This bus station is located in the heart of Neubau, making it a convenient transport option.

Other Options

  • Vienna U-Bahn: Vienna’s subway system is one of the city’s most efficient transportation options. Neubau is served by two major subway stations.

How to Get Around in Neubau

Getting around Neubau is remarkably easy thanks to the number of transportation options available. Here are a few of our recommended options:

  • Public Transit: Vienna’s public transport system (U-Bahn, tram, and bus) is a convenient and affordable way to get around Neubau.
  • Walking and Biking: Neubau is a great district to explore on foot or by bike.

Where to Go Next from Neubau: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

There’s no shortage of great tourist destinations in Austria. Here are a few nearby places to explore after visiting Neubau:

  • Inner city: Vienna’s historic city center is a must-see for any first-time visitor to the city.
  • Schönbrunn Palace: This imperial palace is just a few kilometers away from Neubau and offers stunning gardens and views.
  • Danube River: A great place for walks, river cruises, and adventures.

Conclusion

Neubau is an exciting and vibrant district in the heart of Vienna. Whether you’re looking for cultural attractions, natural wonders, or delicious local cuisine, Neubau offers something for everyone. Follow our guide and be sure to explore all the district has to offer.
