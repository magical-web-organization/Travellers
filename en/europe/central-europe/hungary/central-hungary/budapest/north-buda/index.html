Introduction to North Buda

North Buda is one of the most fascinating districts in Budapest, Hungary. It is located on the western bank of the Danube River and offers visitors a unique blend of urban and outdoor experiences. With its lush hills, amazing viewpoints, historical landmarks, and bustling food and drink scene, North Buda has something to offer for everyone.

List of Places to Visit in North Buda

Here is a list of the top attractions that you shouldn’t miss when exploring North Buda.

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • The historical castle district: Explore the cobblestone streets of the historic castle district where you will find ancient buildings, quaint shops, restaurants, and cafes, and panoramic views of the city.
  • Fisherman’s Bastion: A neo-Gothic terrace with panoramic views of the Danube River and the city of Budapest. It is one of the most iconic landmarks in the city.
  • Matthias Church: A neo-Gothic church that has been in place since the 14th century. It features stunning stained glass windows and a magnificent fresco.
  • Hospital in the Rock: WWII bomb shelter hospital preserved exactly as it was during the siege of Budapest.

Natural Attractions of North Buda: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Normafa viewpoint: Offers panoramic view of Budapest.
  • Elizabeth Lookout Tower: Offers stunning views of the city from the highest point in Budapest
  • Janos Hill and Chairlift: Popular hiking destination with a chairlift service up for those who prefer a less challenging option.
  • Margaret Island: A beautiful park in the middle of the Danube that offers an escape from the city.

Hiking/Biking Routes of North Buda

North Buda is also a great place for hikers and bikers. Here are some of the best routes to check out:

  • Janos Hill and Chairlift: Popular hiking destination with a chairlift service
  • Buda Hills: A network of trails ranging from easy walks to challenging hikes.
  • Danube Cycle Path: A popular biking route along the Danube that passes through North Buda.

Best Things to Do in North Buda: Activities and Tours

Here are some of the best activities and tours to experience in North Buda:

  • Segway Tours: A fun and convenient way to explore the city,
  • Thermal baths: There are several thermal baths in North Buda, including the Gellért, Rudas, and Széchenyi Baths.
  • Private walking tour of the Castle District or hiking tour in the Buda hills.

Week-Long Itinerary for North Buda

Day 1: Castle District and Fisherman’s Bastion Day 2: Matthias Church, Heroes’ Square and City Park Day 3: Danube Bend tour from Budapest Day 4: Margaret Island Day 5: Buda Hills Hike or Danube bike ride Day 6: Gellert Baths and surrounds Day 7: Pop into local cafes and gain insight into Budapest for a relaxed day.

Food and Drink Scene of North Buda

North Buda has a great food and drink scene. Here are some of the must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Goulash Soup: A Hungarian specialties, meat and vegetable soup served in a bread bowl.
  • Chimney Cake: A sweet pastry baked over an open flame.
  • Langos: Deep-fried dough topped with sour cream and grated cheese.

Local Beverages

  • Hungarian Wine: Hungary is famous for its world-class wines from the Eger, Tokaj, and Villány wine regions.
  • Palinka: Hungarian fruit brandy that packs a punch, often used as a digestive.
  • Dreher Beer: A local lager that is widely available.

Where to Stay in North Buda: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

North Buda offers a variety of accommodation options that suit all budgets:

  • Four Seasons Hotel Gresham Palace Budapest: One of the most luxurious hotels in the city, set in an Art Nouveau landmark.
  • Hilton Budapest: Located in the Castle District, with stunning views over the Danube, comfortable room and excellent facilities.
  • Lánchíd 19 Hotel: A stylish boutique hotel located in the Castle District.
  • Green Hotel Budapest: An eco-friendly budget accommodation option located in Újbuda.

What to Buy in North Buda: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

When visiting North Buda, you should consider buying some of these unique souvenirs:

  • Traditional Hungarian Embroidery: Handcrafted items will soon become your treasure
  • Hand-painted Zsolnay ceramic figures: Unique elegantly carved figurines that make fantastic decor.
  • Unicum: A Hungarian herbal liqueur.

Local Culture and Customs of North Buda

If you want to experience North Buda’s local culture and customs, here are some things to keep in mind:

  • Hungarian Culture: The country is known for its unique culture that is a combination of Eastern and Western traditions.
  • Tipping: Tipping between 10-15% is typically expected in restaurants and cafes.
  • Dress Code: Smart-casual attire is recommended for most dining and cultural events.

Best Time to Visit North Buda

The best time to visit North Buda is during the spring (April to May) or fall (September to October) when the weather is cool but pleasant, and the crowds are thinner than during the peak season.

Do’s and Don’ts of North Buda

Do:

  • Try local cuisine and Palinka
  • Spend time outdoors and enjoy the natural scenery.
  • Use public transportation to get around the city.

Don’t:

  • Forget to validate your ticket when using public transportation.
  • Smoke in non-designated areas.
  • Take pictures of religious services without permission.

Safety and Health

Safety in North Buda

North Buda is generally safe for tourists. However, like any other big city, tourists should be cautious and use common sense when traveling through any district in Budapest.

Health in North Buda

Hungary has a national health service that is available to everyone. Travel insurance is recommended to cover the cost of medical treatments and in case of any emergency.

Getting to North Buda: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near North Buda

Budapest Ferenc Liszt International Airport is the nearest airport to North Buda, 23 km (~14 mi) away from the district.

Bus Stations Near North Buda

The largest bus terminal in Budapest, Népliget Coach Station, offers international and national bus services. Other bus stations available include Batthiány Square and Árpád Bridge.

Other Options

Train, car, and boat are other transportation options available.

How to Get Around in North Buda

North Buda can be easily explored on foot but public transportation is also widely available and cost-effective. The most common forms of public transportation are buses, trams, and the metro.

Where to Go Next from North Buda: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

North Buda is just the tip of the iceberg when it comes to exploring Hungary. Here are some nearby destinations and excursions to explore:

  • Szentendre: A beautiful town known for its art galleries and museums.
  • Esztergom: A town known for its historic castle and cathedral.
  • Visegrád: A town known for its fortress and wonderful hiking trails.

Conclusion

North Buda is a beautiful district with so much to offer for both locals and tourists. Whether you’re looking to explore the history of Hungary, indulge in delicious food and wine, enjoy outdoor adventures, or experience the unique culture, North Buda is an unforgettable destination that should be high on your list.
