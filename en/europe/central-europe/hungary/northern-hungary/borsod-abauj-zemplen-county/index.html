Introduction to Borsod-Abaúj-Zemplén County

Borsod-Abaúj-Zemplén County is located in Northern Hungary, in Central Europe. This county is known for its spectacular natural beauty, rich cultural heritage, and an abundance of outdoor activities. From the stunning landscapes of Bükk National Park to the historic towns and cities, Borsod-Abaúj-Zemplén County offers something for everyone.

In this guide, we will explore the best places to visit, the top things to do, must-try foods, and local customs of this beautiful county. We will also give you essential information on how to get there, where to stay, and transportation options.

List of Places to Visit in Borsod-Abaúj-Zemplén County

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Borsod-Abaúj-Zemplén County’s rich cultural heritage is evident in its many museums and historical places. Here are some of the top attractions:

1. Castle of Diósgyőr

The Castle of Diósgyőr is one of the most historically significant landmarks in Miskolc. This castle dates back to the 12th century and has undergone many renovations over the years. Today, visitors can explore the castle’s medieval architecture and learn about the rich history of the area.

2. Szent Istvan Cave in Lillafured

Located in the picturesque town of Lillafured, the Szent Istvan Cave is one of the most popular tourist attractions in Borsod-Abaúj-Zemplén County. The cave features stunning rock formations and underground lakes that have been formed over millions of years.

3. Ottó Herman Museum

The Ottó Herman Museum in Miskolc is home to a vast collection of archeological artifacts, art pieces, and natural history exhibits. This museum is a must-visit for history buffs and culture lovers.

Natural Attractions of Borsod-Abaúj-Zemplén County: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Borsod-Abaúj-Zemplén County’s natural beauty is truly something to behold. From towering mountains to tranquil lakes, this region is home to some of the most stunning natural attractions in Hungary.

1. Bükk National Park

Bükk National Park is Hungary’s largest national park and covers an area of 197 square miles. This park is a popular destination for hikers and nature lovers and features breathtaking landscapes, deep valleys, and countless wildlife species.

2. Lake Hámori

Lake Hámori is a stunning alpine lake located in the heart of the Bükk Mountains. This tranquil lake offers visitors the opportunity to fish, swim, or canoe, while surrounded by natural beauty.

3. Lázbérc Reservoir

Lázbérc Reservoir is one of the largest artificial lakes in Hungary and is a popular spot for fishing and water sports.
