Encs is a small but charming town located in the Borsod-Abaúj-Zemplén County of Hungary. With its rich cultural and historical heritage, stunning natural beauty, and warm hospitality, it offers a perfect mix of adventure, relaxation, and immersion in the local culture. Whether you’re a history buff, nature-lover, or food enthusiast, Encs has something for everyone. In this comprehensive travel guide, we’ll explore the top things to do, see, and eat in Encs, and provide useful information to help you plan your trip.

Introduction to Encs

Encs is a picturesque town with a population of around 9,000 people. It is located in Northern Hungary, in the beautiful Zemplén Mountains, and is easily accessible by car or public transportation. Encs is famous for its rich history, stunning architecture, scenic natural beauty, and warm hospitality. The town is an excellent destination for travelers who want to experience the real Hungary, away from the hustle and bustle of the big cities.

List of Places to Visit in Encs

There are plenty of places to visit in Encs that reflect the town’s rich history and culture, as well as its natural beauty. Here are some of the top attractions:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • The Károlyi Castle: This iconic castle was built in the 18th century and is one of the most impressive landmarks of the town. Visitors can explore the castle and its beautiful gardens and learn about its fascinating history.
  • The Reformed Church: This beautiful church was built in the 14th century and is a true masterpiece of Gothic architecture. Visitors can admire its stunning interior and learn about its important role in the town’s history.
  • The Vernacular Architecture Museum: This unique museum offers a glimpse into the traditional way of life in the region. Visitors can see the authentic peasant houses, barns, and other structures, and learn about the customs and traditions of the locals.

Natural Attractions of Encs: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • The Zemplén Mountains: The Zemplén Mountains offer some of the most stunning landscapes in Hungary. Visitors can hike, bike, or horseback ride through the scenic trails and discover the hidden treasures of the region.
  • The Cserehát Hills: The Cserehát Hills are another natural wonder of Encs. They offer breathtaking views of the surrounding valleys and are an excellent destination for those who love to explore the great outdoors.
  • The Tokaj Wine Region: The Tokaj wine region is located just a short drive from Encs and is famous for its delicious wines and stunning vineyards. Visitors can take a tour of the wineries and taste the local wines, or explore the charming villages in the area.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Encs

There are plenty of hiking and biking routes in Encs that offer the perfect combination of adventure and relaxation. Here are some of the top routes:

  • The Zemplén Mountains Trail: This trail is suitable for hikers and bikers of all levels and offers stunning views of the surrounding mountains and valleys.
  • The Cserehát Hills Trail: This trail is ideal for those who love to explore the rural countryside and discover the hidden gems of the region.
  • The Tokaj Wine Region Trail: This trail is perfect for wine enthusiasts who want to combine their passion for wine with nature.

Best Things to Do in Encs: Activities and Tours

Encs offers plenty of activities and tours that cater to all interests and budgets. Here are some of the top things to do:

  • Take a Tour of the Károlyi Castle: Visitors can explore the stunning castle and its beautiful gardens and learn about its fascinating history.
  • Visit the Tokaj Wine Region: Visitors can take a tour of the wineries and taste the local wines, or explore the charming villages in the area.
  • Hike, Bike, or Horseback Ride in the Zemplén Mountains: Visitors can explore the scenic trails and discover the hidden treasures of the region.

Week-Long Itinerary for Encs

A week is enough time to explore the main attractions of Encs and its surrounding areas. Here’s a sample itinerary:

Day 1: Explore Encs – Károlyi Castle, Reformed Church, Vernacular Architecture Museum Day 2: Outdoor Adventure – Hike or Bike in the Zemplén Mountains Day 3: Food and Drinks – Visit the Tokaj Wine Region and taste the local wines Day 4: Relaxation – Thermal Baths in the area Day 5: Cultural Excursion – Visit nearby towns (Miskolc, Eger, Kosice in Slovakia …) Day 6: Rural Countryside – Hike or Bike in the Cserehát Hills Day 7: Departure

Food and Drink Scene of Encs

Hungarian cuisine is famous for its rich flavors and hearty dishes, and Encs is no exception. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Goulash: A traditional Hungarian dish made with beef, potatoes, paprika, and other spices.
  • Lángos: A popular street food made with fried dough, cheese, and sour cream.
  • Tokaji Aszú: A sweet wine made from grapes grown in the Tokaj wine region.

Local Beverages

  • Pálinka: A popular fruit brandy made from apricots, plums, or cherries.
  • Unicum: A herbal liqueur that is said to have medicinal properties.
  • Fröccs: A refreshing summer drink made from wine and soda water.

Where to Stay in Encs: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Encs offers plenty of accommodation options to suit all budgets and preferences. Here are some of the best places to stay:

  • Hotel Royal: A luxurious hotel located in the heart of the town, with stunning views of the Károlyi Castle.
  • Andante Pension: A cozy guesthouse located in a quiet residential area, with comfortable rooms and a friendly atmosphere.
  • Cserehát Vendégház: A budget-friendly guesthouse located in the Cserehát Hills, with stunning views of the surrounding landscape.

What to Buy in Encs: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Encs is famous for its traditional crafts, handmade products, and local delicacies. Here are some of the best souvenirs to bring home:

  • Pottery: Encs is famous for its traditional pottery, with intricate designs and beautiful colors.
  • Szalonna: A type of Hungarian bacon that is perfect for grilling or roasting.
  • Tokaji Wine: One of Hungary’s most famous wines, made from grapes grown in the Tokaj wine region.

Local Culture and Customs of Encs

Hungarian culture is deeply rooted in tradition and folklore, and Encs is no exception. Visitors can experience traditional Hungarian music, dance, and festivals, and learn about the customs and traditions of the locals.

Best Time to Visit Encs

The best time to visit Encs is during the summer months (June-August) when the weather is warm and sunny, and the outdoor activities and festivals are in full swing. However, the town is also beautiful in the spring (April-May) when the flowers are in bloom, and in the fall (September-October) when the foliage turns into stunning shades of red and gold.

Do’s and Don’ts of Encs

Do:

  • Greet people with a handshake or a kiss on both cheeks.
  • Try the local food and wine.
  • Explore the countryside and discover the hidden gems of the region.

Don’t:

  • Don’t insult the local customs or traditions.
  • Don’t start eating before everyone is seated at the table.
  • Don’t forget to tip in restaurants and cafes.

Safety and Health

Encs is a safe and friendly town, but like any other destination, visitors should take basic precautions to avoid any mishaps. Here are some tips:

Safety in Encs

  • Be aware of pickpockets in crowded areas
  • Don’t leave valuables in cars or unattended bags
  • Follow safety guidelines when hiking or biking in the mountains

Health in Encs

  • The tap water in Encs is safe to drink, but visitors can also buy bottled water if they prefer.
  • It’s a good idea to bring insect repellent in the summer to avoid mosquito bites.

Getting to Encs: Transportation Options and Logistics

Encs is easily accessible by car or public transportation.

Airports Near Encs

The nearest airports to Encs are:

  • Debrecen International Airport (DEB)
  • Budapest Ferenc Liszt International Airport (BUD)

Bus Stations Near Encs

There are several bus stations in the region that connect Encs with other cities and towns:

  • Encs Bus Station (local and regional buses)
  • Miskolc Bus Station (national and international buses)

Other Options

  • Train: The nearest railway station is in Miskolc, which is accessible by bus or taxi.
  • Car Rental: There are several car rental companies in the region that offer services to visitors.

How to Get Around in Encs

Encs is a small town that can be easily explored on foot or by bicycle. Visitors can also use local buses or taxis to get around the town and its surrounding areas.

Where to Go Next from Encs: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Encs is located in a region that offers plenty of exciting destinations and excursions. Here are some of the best places to visit:

  • Miskolc: A bustling city with plenty of cultural and historical attractions, as well as shopping and dining options.
  • Eger: A charming town famous for its castle, thermal baths, and wine region.
  • Kosice, Slovakia: A beautiful city with stunning architecture, museums, and a rich cultural heritage.

Conclusion

Encs offers a perfect mix of history, culture, nature, and warm hospitality. With its stunning landscapes, rich traditions, and delicious food and wine, it’s a destination that should be on every traveler’s bucket list. Whether you’re looking for adventure, relaxation, or cultural immersion, Encs has something for you. Plan your trip today and discover the treasures of this beautiful Hungarian town!
