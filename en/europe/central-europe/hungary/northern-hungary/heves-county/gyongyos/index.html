Introduction to Gyöngyös

Gyöngyös is a small city situated in Heves County, in Northern Hungary, in Central Europe. Home to over 30,000 residents, Gyöngyös is a popular tourist destination, thanks to its rich cultural history, stunning natural landscapes, and a plethora of outdoor activities for tourists to enjoy.

In this guide, you will discover everything you need to know about planning a memorable trip to Gyöngyös, including where to stay, what to see and do, the best food and drink options, local customs, safety, and transportation tips, among other things.

List of Places to Visit in Gyöngyös

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

If you are a history buff looking to explore Gyöngyös’s rich cultural heritage, there are numerous attractions that you can visit, such as:

  • Erdődy Castle: Located at the heart of Gyöngyös, Erdődy Castle is a majestic 18th-century castle that was the former residence of Hungarian aristocratic families. Visitors can take guided tours of the castle to learn about its history and admire its stunning baroque-style architecture.

  • Mátra Museum: The Mátra Museum is an excellent destination for those interested in learning about the history and culture of Gyöngyös and the surrounding region. The museum showcases local art, crafts, and artifacts, including traditional costumes, historical documents, and photographs.

  • St. Bartholomew Church: St. Bartholomew Church is a stunning Gothic-style church that dates back to the 14th century. The church features beautiful stained-glass windows, intricate carvings, and an impressive bell tower, making it a great place to visit for architecture enthusiasts.

Natural Attractions of Gyöngyös: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Gyöngyös is home to breathtaking natural landscapes, including hills, meadows, and forests, making it an excellent destination for outdoor enthusiasts. Some of the top natural attractions in Gyöngyös include:

  • Mátra Mountains: The Mátra Mountains are the highest range of the North Hungarian Mountains, known for their stunning natural beauty and diverse wildlife. Visitors can enjoy hiking, biking, and skiing in the mountains, as well as admiring the picturesque views of the surrounding landscape.

  • Sástó: Sástó is a picturesque lake located near Gyöngyös that offers plenty of opportunities for swimming, boating, and fishing. The lake is also surrounded by lush forests that are perfect for hiking and taking in the natural scenery.

  • Mátra Wine Region: The Mátra Wine Region is a popular destination for wine lovers, offering a variety of wine tasting tours and events. The region has a long history of winemaking, and visitors can sample local wines and learn about the traditional wine-making process.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Gyöngyös

One of the best ways to explore Gyöngyös and the surrounding region is by hiking or biking through the stunning natural landscapes. Some of the top hiking and biking routes in Gyöngyös include:

  • Mátra Mountains National Park: The Mátra Mountains National Park is home to a variety of hiking trails of various difficulties, ranging from easy family-friendly walks to challenging hikes. Visitors can enjoy stunning views of the surrounding landscape, as well as the chance to spot local flora and fauna.

  • Sástó-Kékestető Bike Trail: This 25 km bike trail starts at the picturesque Sástó Lake and leads up to the peak of Kékestető Mountain, offering cyclists stunning views of the surrounding area.

Best Things to Do in Gyöngyös: Activities and Tours

In addition to hiking, biking, and exploring the city’s cultural heritage, there are plenty of other fun things to see and do in Gyöngyös, including:

  • Spa and Wellness: Gyöngyös is home to several spas and wellness centers that offer a variety of treatments and relaxation experiences, perfect for those who want to unwind and rejuvenate.

  • Traditional Craft Workshops: Visitors can take part in traditional craft workshops to learn about local crafts, such as pottery, weaving, and wood carving, as well as creating their own unique souvenirs to take home.

  • Horse Riding: Gyöngyös is surrounded by scenic countryside, making it an excellent destination for horse riding and carriage tours. Visitors can explore the surrounding area on horseback, experiencing the Hungarian countryside at a leisurely pace.

Week-Long Itinerary for Gyöngyös

If you’re planning a week-long itinerary for Gyöngyös, here are some suggested activities for each day:

  • Day 1: Visit Erdődy Castle and take a walking tour of the city center.

  • Day 2: Hike in the Mátra Mountains National Park.

  • Day 3: Take a wine tour of the Mátra Wine Region.

  • Day 4: Relax and rejuvenate at one of the local spas.

  • Day 5: Take part in a traditional craft workshop.

  • Day 6: Explore the surrounding countryside on horseback or take a carriage tour.

  • Day 7: Visit the Mátra Museum and St. Bartholomew Church.

Food and Drink Scene of Gyöngyös

Hungarian cuisine is known for being hearty, flavorful, and rich, and Gyöngyös is no exception. Some of the must-try dishes in Gyöngyös include:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Goulash: Goulash is a traditional Hungarian soup made with beef, potatoes, carrots, and paprika.

  • Stuffed Peppers: Stuffed peppers are another Hungarian favorite, made with ground beef, rice, and tomatoes.

  • Langos: Langos is a popular Hungarian street food made with deep-fried dough, topped with sour cream and cheese.

Local Beverages

  • Palinka: Palinka is a traditional Hungarian fruit brandy, available in a variety of flavors.

  • Local Wines: The Mátra Wine Region is renowned for producing some excellent wines, including reds, whites, and rosés.

Where to Stay in Gyöngyös: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Whether you’re looking for budget-friendly accommodations or luxury options, Gyöngyös has plenty of accommodations to offer, including:

  • Öreg Miskolcz Hotel: A historic hotel located in the heart of Gyöngyös, Öreg Miskolcz Hotel offers comfortable rooms and suites, as well as a restaurant and bar.

  • Lux Panzió: A budget-friendly option, Lux Panzió is a cozy guesthouse that offers clean and comfortable rooms and free Wi-Fi.

  • **Wellness Hotel-M: A luxurious spa hotel located in the Mátra Mountains National Park, Wellness Hotel-M offers rooms and suites with stunning views, as well as a variety of spa and wellness treatments.

What to Buy in Gyöngyös: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Gyöngyös is known for its traditional crafts and unique local products, making it an excellent destination for shopping and souvenirs. Some of the top souvenirs and local products to bring home from Gyöngyös include:

  • Pottery: Gyöngyös is known for producing beautiful handmade pottery, available at local shops and markets.

  • Sausages: Hungarian sausages are famous for their flavor and quality, and Gyöngyös is no exception. Visitors can purchase sausages and other cured meats at local markets and specialty shops.

  • Wines and Palinka: The Mátra Wine Region produces some excellent wines and palinka, making them excellent souvenirs to bring back home.

Local Culture and Customs of Gyöngyös

Hungarian culture is rich in traditions and customs, and Gyöngyös is no exception. Some of the customs and traditions that visitors can experience in Gyöngyös include:

  • Folk Music and Dance: Hungarian folk music and dance are an essential part of the country’s cultural heritage, and visitors can experience them at local festivals and events.

  • Easter Celebrations: Easter is one of the most important holidays in Hungary, and Gyöngyös celebrates it with traditional food, music, and events, such as the “watering of the girls,” where men sprinkle women with water in exchange for painted eggs.

Best Time to Visit Gyöngyös

The best time to visit Gyöngyös is between May and September when the weather is warm and sunny, and the outdoor activities are in full swing. However, visitors can also enjoy the city’s beauty during the winter season, when the Mátra Mountains transform into a snowy wonderland, making it an excellent destination for winter sports enthusiasts.

Do’s and Don’ts of Gyöngyös

Do:

  • Learn a few basic Hungarian phrases, such as “hello” and “thank you.”

  • Respect local customs and traditions, such as removing your shoes when entering a home.

  • Taste the local food and drink, including palinka and traditional Hungarian dishes.

Don’t:

  • Wear revealing clothing when visiting religious sites or attending formal events.

  • Photograph people without their consent.

  • Drink tap water unless it has been boiled or treated.

Safety and Health

Safety in Gyöngyös

Gyöngyös is generally a safe city, but visitors should still take the necessary precautions to ensure their safety, such as:

  • Avoid walking alone at night, especially in quiet and poorly lit areas.

  • Keep an eye on your belongings, especially in crowded places and public transportation.

  • Always use licensed taxis or ride-sharing services.

Health in Gyöngyös

Hungary has a modern and efficient healthcare system, and visitors can access medical care at public hospitals or private clinics. Visitors are also recommended to have travel insurance to cover any medical emergencies that may arise during their trip.

Getting to Gyöngyös: Transportation Options and Logistics

Visitors can reach Gyöngyös by air, bus, or train.

Airports Near Gyöngyös

  • The nearest international airport is Budapest Ferenc Liszt International Airport, located about 100 km from Gyöngyös.

Bus Stations Near Gyöngyös

  • Gyöngyös Bus Station is located in the city center and serves both local and long-distance buses.

Other Options

  • Visitors can also reach Gyöngyös by train from Budapest, Eger, and other nearby cities.

How to Get Around in Gyöngyös

The best way to get around in Gyöngyös is by public transportation, including buses and taxis. Visitors can also rent bicycles or cars to explore the surrounding areas on their own.

Where to Go Next from Gyöngyös: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

For those who want to explore more of Hungary, there are several nearby destinations and excursions that visitors can go to, including:

  • Budapest: Hungary’s capital city is located just over an hour away from Gyöngyös by train or car.

  • Eger: A charming Baroque town located about 20 km from Gyöngyös, famous for its thermal baths, wine, and monuments.

  • Hollókő: A traditional Hungarian village located about an hour’s drive from Gyöngyös that has been designated as a UNESCO World Heritage site due to its well-preserved traditional architecture.

Conclusion

Gyöngyös is a captivating destination that combines cultural heritage, natural beauty, and outdoor adventures, making it an excellent destination for any traveler. With our comprehensive travel guide, you can plan your trip to Gyöngyös confidently, taking in all the top attractions, food, and drink options, and local customs while enjoying a memorable journey through Hungary.
