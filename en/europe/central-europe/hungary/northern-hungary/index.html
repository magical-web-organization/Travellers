Introduction to Northern Hungary

Northern Hungary, located in the north-eastern part of the country, is a region known for its rich history, breathtaking landscapes, and diverse cultural attractions. From traditional Hungarian villages and stunning natural landmarks to modern cultural centers and delicious local cuisine, there’s something for travelers of all interests in Northern Hungary.

List of Places to Visit in Northern Hungary

Here are some of the top places to visit in Northern Hungary:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Castle of Eger: This 16th-century castle is one of the most important historical buildings in Hungary and offers stunning views of the surrounding area.
  • Tokaj Wine Region: This UNESCO World Heritage site is known for producing some of the best wines in the world and is a must-visit for wine enthusiasts.
  • Miskolc Zoo: This popular zoo located in Miskolc offers visitors the chance to see a wide range of unique animals from Hungary and around the world.
  • Aggtelek National Park: This massive national park offers breathtaking views of the surrounding landscape and is home to a vast network of underground caves.

Natural Attractions of Northern Hungary: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Bükki National Park: This expansive park is home to vast forests, stunning rock formations, and a range of outdoor activities, including hiking, mountain biking, and rock climbing.
  • Lillafüred: This charming town is located in a picturesque valley surrounded by mountains and offers visitors the opportunity to explore beautiful gardens, a castle, and stunning waterfalls.
  • Szilvásvárad: This small town is known for its scenic beauty, including the Szalajka Valley, which features breathtaking waterfalls, deep gorges, and a range of hiking trails.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Northern Hungary

For outdoor enthusiasts looking to explore Northern Hungary on foot or bike, there are plenty of great hiking and biking routes to choose from. Some of the most popular include:

  • Bükk Mountains: With over 700 km of hiking trails and bike paths, the Bükk Mountains offer endless options for outdoor exploration.
  • Zemplén Mountains: This scenic mountain range offers a range of hiking trails, including the popular trail up to the summit of Szádvár Castle.
  • Tokaj Wine Region: For a unique way to explore the region’s stunning vineyards, consider biking along the Tokaj Wine Route, which offers miles of scenic views and plenty of opportunities to taste some of Hungary’s best wines.

Best Things to Do in Northern Hungary: Activities and Tours

Whether you’re interested in history, culture, or outdoor adventures, there’s no shortage of things to do in Northern Hungary. Here are some of the best must-do activities and tours:

  • Explore the region’s unique wine culture by taking a wine tour of the Tokaj Wine Region.
  • Visit the stunning medieval castle of Eger and soak up the history and culture of the region.
  • Take a dip in one of the region’s many thermal baths, such as the Egerszalók Thermal Bath or the Cave Bath in Miskolctapolca.
  • Embark on a hot air balloon ride for a bird’s-eye view of the region’s stunning landscapes.

Week-Long Itinerary for Northern Hungary

For those with a week to spend in Northern Hungary, consider the following itinerary:

  • Day 1: Arrive in Eger and explore the beautiful castle and surrounding area.
  • Day 2: Head to the Tokaj region and spend the day exploring wineries and vineyards.
  • Day 3: Visit the Aggtelek National Park and explore the stunning underground caves.
  • Day 4: Take a scenic bike ride through the Bükk Mountains.
  • Day 5: Relax and unwind in one of the region’s many thermal baths.
  • Day 6: Explore the stunning Lillafüred area, including the waterfalls and castle.
  • Day 7: Spend the day hiking in the beautiful Zemplén Mountains.

Food and Drink Scene of Northern Hungary

No trip to Northern Hungary is complete without indulging in the region’s delicious local cuisine and unique wines. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Goulash: A Hungarian classic, this hearty beef soup is a must-try while in Northern Hungary.
  • Sztrapacska: A delicious dumpling dish made with potatoes and served with bacon.
  • Chimney Cake: A sweet pastry made of dough wrapped around a spit and baked over an open flame.

Local Beverages

  • Tokaji Wine: This sweet dessert wine made from grapes grown in the Tokaj region is famous around the world for its unique flavor.
  • Unicum: A traditional Hungarian herbal liqueur with a distinctive taste.

Where to Stay in Northern Hungary: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

From luxury hotels to cozy B&Bs, there are plenty of accommodation options available in Northern Hungary. Here are some of the top choices:

  • Hotel Palazzo Wellness Villa (Eger): This luxurious hotel offers stunning views of the surrounding mountains and features a range of amenities, including an indoor pool and spa services.
  • Hotel Azur (Szeged): This modern hotel is located in the heart of Szeged and features spacious rooms and a range of convenient amenities.
  • Borbarátok Háza (Tokaj): This charming guesthouse is located in the heart of the Tokaj wine region and offers a range of comfortable rooms and suites.

What to Buy in Northern Hungary: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

From local wines to handmade crafts, there’s plenty to bring home as a souvenir of your trip to Northern Hungary. Here are some of the top items to look out for:

  • Tokaji Wine: Take home a bottle (or several) of this sweet wine from the famous Tokaj region.
  • Handmade Crafts: From traditional embroidered clothing to handmade pottery, there are plenty of unique crafts to bring home as a souvenir.
  • Paprika: A staple of Hungarian cuisine, this spicy pepper is a must-have for any foodie.

Local Culture and Customs of Northern Hungary

Hungarian culture is rich and diverse, with a blend of traditional and modern elements. In Northern Hungary, some of the most prominent cultural customs include:

  • Traditional Folk Music and Dance: Throughout the region, you’ll find a range of festivals and events celebrating traditional Hungarian music and dance.
  • Historical Traditions: There are many traditional festivals and events in Northern Hungary that are deeply rooted in the region’s history, such as the annual Castle Games in Eger.
  • Local Cuisine: Northern Hungary is known for its delicious local cuisine, which blends traditional Hungarian dishes with regional specialties.

Best Time to Visit Northern Hungary

The best time to visit Northern Hungary depends on your interests. If you’re interested in outdoor activities like hiking and biking, the spring, summer, and early fall are optimal times to visit. The winter months are ideal for those interested in snow sports, while the fall is a great time to visit for wine enthusiasts.

Do’s and Don’ts of Northern Hungary

Here are some important do’s and don’ts to keep in mind while visiting Northern Hungary:

Do:

  • Try the local wines and cuisine.
  • Dress appropriately when visiting religious sites.
  • Respect local customs and traditions.

Don’t:

  • Drink alcohol in public places.
  • Be disrespectful towards locals or their customs.
  • Unnecessarily disturb or harm the local wildlife or natural environment.

Safety and Health

While Northern Hungary is generally a safe place for travelers, it’s also important to take certain precautions and keep in mind some health considerations when visiting the region.

Safety in Northern Hungary

  • Be aware of petty theft and pickpocketing in crowded tourist areas.
  • Take care when hiking or exploring the natural areas, as there may be uneven terrain or hidden hazards.
  • Follow all safety guidelines and instructions when participating in outdoor activities or tours.

Health in Northern Hungary

  • Be sure to pack any necessary medications or allergy remedies with you.
  • Drink bottled water to avoid any potential health risks.
  • Wear sunscreen and protective clothing to avoid sunburn and heatstroke when spending time outdoors.

Getting to Northern Hungary: Transportation Options and Logistics

Northern Hungary is easily accessible by multiple modes of transportation, including:

Airports Near Northern Hungary

  • Debrecen International Airport: The closest airport to Northern Hungary, located approximately one hour from the city of Eger.

Bus Stations Near Northern Hungary

  • Budapest Bus Station: The largest bus station in the region, offering connections to a range of cities and towns in Northern Hungary.

Other Options

  • Train: There are multiple train stations throughout Northern Hungary, including in Eger and Miskolc.
  • Car: Renting a car is a great way to explore the region at your own pace.

How to Get Around in Northern Hungary

Public transportation in Northern Hungary can be limited, so renting a car or joining a guided tour may be your best options for getting around. Taxis and private transfers are also available for shorter journeys.

Where to Go Next from Northern Hungary: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

If you’re looking to explore more of Hungary after visiting Northern Hungary, there are plenty of nearby destinations worth checking out. Some top options include:

  • Budapest: Just a few hours’ drive from Northern Hungary, Budapest is a must-visit destination for anyone traveling to Hungary.
  • Lake Balaton: Hungary’s largest lake is located just a couple of hours from Northern Hungary and offers stunning vistas, beaches, and a range of water sports.
  • Slovak Paradise National Park: Just across the border from Northern Hungary, this stunning national park offers beautiful hikes and breathtaking views.

Conclusion

From exploring medieval castles and natural parks to indulging in delicious food and drink, Northern Hungary offers something for everyone. Plan your trip using this comprehensive travel guide and get ready to experience all that this beautiful and vibrant region has to offer.
