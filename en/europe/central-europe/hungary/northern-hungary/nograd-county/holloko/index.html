Introduction to Hollókő

Located in Nógrád County, in Northern Hungary, Hollókő is nothing short of a fairytale village. This settlement retains its rich cultural heritage through traditional architecture, folklore clothing, and centuries-old customs. The area came into cultural prominence when in 1987; it was declared a UNESCO World Heritage site and has since attracted travelers from all over the world to enjoy its picturesque beauty.

Surrounded by rugged, forested hills and the Bükk National Park, Hollókő offers an escape to the countryside’s tranquility. Here’s a comprehensive guide to help you explore the best of what Hollókő has to offer.

List of Places to Visit in Hollókő

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  1. Hollókő Castle: One of Hungary’s oldest castles, ruins of the Hollókő castle are the principal, historically significant attraction here. The castle’s oldest sections date back to the 13th century, with additions over the subsequent centuries. Visitors can climb up to its highest point, from where panoramic views lend a beautiful picture of the scenic surroundings.

  2. Hollókő Skansen: A ‘skansen’ is a museum that operates on the principle of giving visitors a taste of life from the past. The Hollókő Skansen was established in the early 1960s and offers a glimpse into how Hungarian villagers went about their daily life centuries ago. It features traditional houses, barns, and other buildings, each containing items used by the residents at that time. As you navigate through the various buildings, you learn about traditional crafts, dress-ware, cooking methods, and other traditional practices.

Natural Attractions of Hollókő: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  1. Hollókő Hills: A must-visit for hikers and nature lovers, the Hollókő hills provide a scenic backdrop to the village. The hilly terrain offers various routes for hiking and biking suitable for all levels of fitness. A hike at Hollókő offers a chance for visitors to explore the region’s unique fauna and flora that is rare to find elsewhere.

  2. Nógrád Geopark: The Nógrád Geopark is known for its geological riches - its unique geology dates back over four billion years. Visitors can take a tour of the park and learn more about its geological history while experiencing its natural wonders.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Hollókő

The hilly terrain of Hollókő offers several hiking and biking trails for all skill levels. The most popular routes include the ‘Hollókő-Aploy’ route, which is a moderate hike that takes visitors through the countryside and off-the-beaten-path areas.

The ‘Hollókő bike trail’ is also a hit with cycling enthusiasts. It’s around 36 km long and takes riders through the village and its rolling hills. The path offers a beautiful view of the village and its greenery.

Best Things to Do in Hollókő: Activities and Tours

  1. Folktour: This tour offers an insight into Hungarian village life and allows visitors to experience Hollókő’s unique culture. The tour takes you through the village’s significant cultural and historical sites, with short stops on each route.

  2. Horse riding experience: For visitors keen on exploring Hollókő’s countryside, horse riding is an excellent option. You get to experience the Hungarian way of life while riding through the hills and forests of Nógrád.

Week-Long Itinerary for Hollókő

Day 1-2: Explore the village and its historical sites, including the Hollókő Castle and Skansen.

Day 3: Hike on the ‘Hollókő-Apoly’ trail and catch a glimpse of the region’s unique flora and fauna.

Day 4: Visit Nógrád Geopark and learn about Hungary’s geological history.

Day 5-6: Try the Folktour and engage in traditional Hungarian life activities. Learn cooking techniques and other crafts.

Food and Drink Scene of Hollókő

Must-Try Dishes

  1. Fish soup: A must-try traditional Hungarian soup made with carp fish and paprika.

  2. Paprikás csirke: A chicken paprika stew on a bed of noodles, whose recipe has its roots in the rural areas of Hungary.

Local Beverages

  1. Tokaj wine: Known for its unique taste and aroma, Tokaj wine is produced in northeastern Hungary and is famous all over the world.

  2. Pálinka: A popular Hungarian fruit brandy made from different fruits like cherries, grapes, apricots, or plums.

Where to Stay in Hollókő: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Hollókő offers various accommodation options that cater to different budgets and preferences. Some popular options include:

  1. Castellum Hotel Hollókő: A cozy hotel located in the heart of the village.

  2. Öreg Holló Guesthouse: A beautiful guesthouse located near the castle and Skansen.

What to Buy in Hollókő: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  1. Lacework decorations and embroidered ornaments made in the village are popular among visitors. The intricate needlework is difficult to replicate, so they make for a unique souvenir.

  2. Palóc cheese: This cheese is made from sheep milk and has a distinct taste and texture. It is a perfect souvenir for cheese lovers.

Local Culture and Customs of Hollókő

The language, attire, artifacts, and customs of the locals of Hollókő are unique and provide insight into the Hungarian rural way of life. The village hosts several festivals throughout the year, such as the “Paloc Festival,” where visitors can experience the local culture and traditions.

Best Time to Visit Hollókő

The best time to visit Hollókő is during the spring and fall seasons when the temperatures are comfortable, and the village looks beautiful against the scenic backdrop of greenery. Summer months can be too hot for some visitors, and winter can be harsh and limit outdoor activities.

Do’s and Don’ts of Hollókő

Do:

  1. Engage in the traditional activities like folk dancing, cooking, and crafting.

  2. Respect the locals and their way of life.

Don’t:

  1. Leave the marked trails while exploring the hills.

  2. Litter the environment.

Safety and Health

Safety in Hollókő

Hollókő is a safe place, but visitors should take the usual safety measures and maintain vigilance in crowded places.

Health in Hollókő

The village has a small medical center that caters to minor ailments. Visitors with any preexisting health conditions should carry sufficient medication as there is no significant hospital in the vicinity.

Getting to Hollókő: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Hollókő

Budapest Ferenc Liszt International Airport is the closest airport, around 2 hours away, and is well-connected to major global cities.

Bus Stations Near Hollókő

The best option is to take a direct bus from Budapest to Hollókő.

Other Options

Driving to Hollókő is also possible, and visitors can rent cars from major rental agencies in Budapest.

How to Get Around in Hollókő

Walking is the best option to get around the village as it is small and easily accessible. Biking is also a great way to explore the village’s outskirts and hills.

Where to Go Next from Hollókő: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  1. Szécsény: A beautiful Baroque town known for its striking castle and historic churches.

  2. Cserhát Hills: A hiker’s paradise with dense forests and scenic trails.

Conclusion

Hollókő is one of Hungary’s hidden gems, with its picturesque beauty and rich cultural heritage. A visit to the village offers a glimpse into rural Hungarian life, with a unique opportunity to explore the region’s natural and historical wonders. Use this travel guide to make your trip to the charming village of Hollókő a memorable one.
