Introduction to Szombathely

Welcome to Szombathely, a hidden gem in Western Hungary that will surprise you with its rich history, cultural heritage, and natural beauty. Szombathely is the oldest city in Hungary, with a history dating back to the Roman Empire. As a result, the city is full of impressive archeological sites, museums, and historical landmarks that tell the story of its past.

But Szombathely is not just a city of the past. It is a modern and vibrant city that offers an array of activities, events, and experiences for every type of traveler. Whether you are a history buff, a nature lover, an adventure seeker, or a foodie, Szombathely has something to offer. In this travel guide, we will show you the best places to visit, things to do, and experiences to have in Szombathely. So, get ready to explore this charming city and discover its hidden treasures.

List of Places to Visit in Szombathely

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Savaria Museum: Located in the heart of Szombathely’s historic city center, Savaria Museum is a must-visit for anyone interested in the city’s past. The museum showcases the history of Szombathely from the Roman era to the present day, with exhibitions on archeology, art, and ethnography.

  • Roman Ruin Garden: This outdoor museum showcases the remains of the ancient Roman city of Savaria, which was the administrative center of the province of Pannonia. Visitors can see the ruins of public buildings, temples, bathhouses, and housing estates from the 1st to the 4th century AD.

  • Iseum Savariense: This museum houses the largest collection of Roman artifacts in Hungary, with over 60,000 artifacts from the ancient city of Savaria. Visitors can see mosaics, statues, inscriptions, and everyday objects from Roman times.

  • Bishop’s Palace and Cathedral: This complex of buildings includes the Bishop’s Palace, the Cathedral, and the Crypt. The Bishop’s Palace is a Baroque-style building that now houses a museum of religious art. The Cathedral is one of the oldest churches in Hungary, with a mix of Romanesque, Gothic, and Baroque architecture. The Crypt is the final resting place of bishops and other religious figures.

Natural Attractions of Szombathely: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Harmashatarhegy Nature Reserve: This nature reserve is located just a few kilometers from Szombathely and is perfect for a day hike. Visitors can see rare plants, animals, and birds in their natural habitats and enjoy stunning views of the surrounding landscape.

  • Lake Csónakázó: This man-made lake is a popular destination for swimming, fishing, and boating. Visitors can rent boats and kayaks and explore the lake’s picturesque surroundings.

  • Fő Street and Main Square: These two pedestrian zones are the heart of Szombathely’s historic city center and are the perfect place to take a stroll and admire the city’s architecture and atmosphere.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Szombathely

  • Greenbelt Hiking Trail: This hiking trail is a 20 km loop that starts and ends in Szombathely and takes visitors through the city’s outskirts, including forests, meadows, wetlands, and hills. Along the way, hikers can see historic churches, chapels, and farmhouses.

  • Burgundy Bike Trail: This 35 km bike trail takes visitors through the scenic countryside of Szombathely and its neighboring villages. Along the way, bikers can see vineyards, orchards, and farms, and taste local wine and food.

Best Things to Do in Szombathely: Activities and Tours

  • City Walking Tour: A walking tour of Szombathely is the best way to get to know the city’s history, culture, and hidden treasures. Visitors can choose from several guided tours, including ones that focus on Roman history, Baroque architecture, and Jewish heritage.

  • Wine Tasting and Vineyard Tour: Szombathely is located in a wine region, and visitors can taste local wines and learn about winemaking traditions on a vineyard tour. Popular wineries include Jandl Birtok and Varga Pince.

  • Horse Riding: Horse riding is a popular activity in Szombathely, with several stables offering guided tours of the nearby countryside.

Food and Drink Scene of Szombathely

Hungarian cuisine is famous for its hearty, flavorful dishes, and Szombathely is no exception. The city has a great food and drink scene, with many restaurants, cafes, and bars serving traditional and modern Hungarian cuisine and local wines.

Must-Try Dishes

  • Hortobágyi Palacsinta: Savory pancakes filled with minced meat, vegetables, and spices, served with sour cream.

  • Pörkölt: A stew made with beef or pork, onions, paprika, and other spices, served with dumplings or potatoes.

  • Lecso: A vegetable stew made with peppers, onions, tomatoes, and paprika, often served with sausages.

  • Toltott Kaposzta: Stuffed cabbage rolls filled with meat, rice, and vegetables, served with sour cream.

Local Beverages

  • Szombathelyi Kékfrankos: A local red wine made from the Kékfrankos grape variety, with fruity and spicy notes.

  • Chardonnay Szombathely: A white wine that is crisp, dry, and refreshing, with citrus and floral aromas.

  • Erős Pista: A hot pepper paste that is used as a condiment or a flavoring in many Hungarian dishes.

What to Buy in Szombathely: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Visitors to Szombathely can bring home many souvenirs and local products that represent the city’s culture and heritage. Some popular ones include:

  • Savaria Scarf: A traditional scarf made with wool and silk that features patterns inspired by Roman mosaics.

  • Local Wines: Szombathely is located in a wine region, and visitors can bring home bottles of local wines such as Kékfrankos and Chardonnay.

  • Pottery: Szombathely has a long history of pottery making, and visitors can buy handmade ceramics and pottery products at local stores.

Local Culture and Customs of Szombathely

Hungarian culture is rich and diverse, with many traditions and customs that are unique to the country. Visitors to Szombathely can experience some of these cultural traditions and learn more about the city’s heritage by:

  • Attending Festivals: Szombathely hosts several festivals throughout the year, including the Savaria Historical Carnival, the Szombathely Wine Festival, and the St. Martin’s Day Festival.

  • Visiting Churches and Religious Sites: Religion has played an important role in Hungarian history and culture, and visitors can see many beautiful churches and religious sites in Szombathely, such as the Bishop’s Palace and Cathedral and the Synagogue.

  • Trying Local Food and Drink: Hungarian cuisine is a reflection of the country’s history and culture, and visitors can taste local dishes and wines that represent Szombathely’s heritage.

Best Time to Visit Szombathely

The best time to visit Szombathely is from April to October, when the weather is warm and sunny, and most of the outdoor attractions and activities are open. The peak season is from July to August when many tourists visit Hungary, and prices may be higher.

Do’s and Don’ts of Szombathely

Do:

  • Respect Local Customs and Traditions: Hungarian culture is unique and different from what you may be used to, so make an effort to learn about local customs and traditions and show respect for them.

  • Try Local Food and Drink: Hungarian cuisine is delicious and diverse, so try as many local dishes and wines as you can during your stay.

  • Explore the Outskirts of Szombathely: While the city center has many attractions, the surrounding villages, forests, and vineyards offer a glimpse of rural Hungarian life and natural beauty.

Don’t:

  • Be Disrespectful to Locals: Hungarians are friendly and hospitable people, so show respect and courtesy to them, especially when visiting religious sites and heritage areas.

  • Expect Everyone to Speak English: While many Hungarians speak English, don’t expect everyone to do so. It’s always helpful to learn a few basic Hungarian phrases to communicate with locals.

  • Forget to Tip: Tipping is not mandatory in Hungary, but it is customary to leave a small tip in restaurants and cafes, especially if the service was good.

Safety and Health

Safety in Szombathely

Szombathely is a safe city to visit, with low crime rates and friendly locals. However, visitors should take the usual precautions when traveling, such as not leaving valuables unattended, keeping an eye on their belongings in crowded places, and staying in well-lit areas at night.

Health in Szombathely

Hungary has a good healthcare system, and visitors can use the public hospitals and clinics in case of emergencies. However, it’s always wise to have travel insurance that covers medical expenses and repatriation in case of illness or injury.

Getting to Szombathely: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Szombathely

The closest airport to Szombathely is Vienna International Airport, which is about a 2-hour drive away. Other airports that are within driving distance include Budapest Ferenc Liszt International Airport and Graz Airport in Austria.

Bus Stations Near Szombathely

Szombathely has a central bus station that connects it to other parts of Hungary and neighboring countries, such as Austria and Slovakia.

Other Options

Visitors can also reach Szombathely by train or car. The city has a train station that connects it to major cities in Hungary and neighboring countries.

How to Get Around in Szombathely

Szombathely is a compact city, and most of the attractions and activities are located within walking distance of each other. However, visitors can also use public transportation, taxis, or bikes to get around the city.

Public Transportation

Szombathely has a good public transportation system that includes buses and trams. Visitors can buy tickets from vending machines or newsstands, or use a travel card.

Taxis

Taxis are available in Szombathely, and visitors can find them at taxi stands or hail them on the street. It’s always wise to agree on a price before getting into the taxi.

Bikes

Bike rentals are available in Szombathely, and visitors can explore the city and its surroundings on two wheels. Some popular rental places include Ecobike and Bike Boutique.

Where to Go Next from Szombathely: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Szombathely is located in a region that is rich in history, culture, and natural beauty, and visitors can use it as a base to explore other nearby destinations and excursions. Some popular ones include:

  • Kőszeg: A charming town known for its Baroque architecture, castle ruins, and scenic countryside.

  • Sopron: A historic town that is home to medieval churches and buildings, and is located in a wine region.

  • Lake Balaton: The largest lake in Central Europe, known for swimming, boating, and watersports.

  • Vienna: The capital of Austria, located about a 2-hour drive from Szombathely, is known for its rich history, culture, and architecture.

Conclusion

Szombathely is a city that will surprise you with its history, culture, and natural beauty. Whether you are interested in Roman history, hiking, winemaking, or Hungarian cuisine, Szombathely has something to offer.
