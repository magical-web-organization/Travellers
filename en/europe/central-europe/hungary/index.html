A Comprehensive Guide to Traveling in Hungary

Hungary is a beautiful Central European country that offers unique experiences for travelers. Whether you’re interested in exploring the cultural and historical attractions or taking part in outdoor adventures, Hungary has something for everyone. In this guide, we’ve compiled a list of the best places to visit, things to do, and important cultural customs to keep in mind.

Introduction to Hungary

Hungary is a landlocked country in Central Europe, bordered by Austria, Slovakia, Ukraine, Romania, Serbia, Croatia, and Slovenia. It is known for its stunning architecture, thermal spas, and rich cultural history. The official language is Hungarian, and the currency is the Hungarian Forint (HUF). Budapest, the capital city of Hungary, has been called the “Paris of the East” and offers a dazzling blend of architectural styles and cultural attractions.

List of Places to Visit in Hungary

Hungary has a diverse range of attractions that cater to different interests. Here are the top places to visit in Hungary:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Buda Castle: A UNESCO World Heritage Site, Buda Castle is a must-visit for history buffs. The castle dates back to the 13th century and was the residence of Hungarian kings.
  • Hungarian National Museum: One of the oldest museums in Hungary, this museum showcases the history, art, and culture of Hungary.
  • Hungarian State Opera House: A beautiful neoclassical building that stages operas, ballets, and concerts throughout the year.
  • Széchenyi Thermal Baths: One of the largest thermal bath complexes in Europe, the Széchenyi Baths offer relaxation and rejuvenation in an opulent setting.

Natural Attractions of Hungary: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Lake Balaton: The largest lake in Central Europe, Lake Balaton is a popular spot for swimming, sailing, and fishing.
  • Danube Bend: A scenic region along the Danube River, with picturesque towns and volcanic hills.
  • Aggtelek National Park: A UNESCO World Heritage Site, this park is home to the Baradla Cave system, as well as rare plant and animal species.
  • Bukk National Park: A popular destination for hiking, Bukk National Park boasts stunning views and is home to rare wildlife.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Hungary

If you’re looking for outdoor adventure, there are several hiking and biking routes in Hungary that offer unbeatable scenery. Some popular trails include:

  • The Blue Trail: A 1128 km-long hiking trail that runs from the Austrian border to the Hungarian-Slovakian border.
  • The Balaton Bike Path: A 200 km-long bike path that winds along the shores of Lake Balaton.
  • Buda Hills: A popular spot for hiking and nature walks, Buda Hills offers spectacular views of Budapest.

Best Things to Do in Hungary: Activities and Tours

Hungary offers a range of activities and tours for travelers. Here are a few that are worth checking out:

  • Wine Tasting Tour: Hungary is known for its excellent wines; take a vineyard tour and sample some of the country’s best.
  • Thermal Spa Experience: Thermal spas are an integral part of Hungarian culture; try one of Budapest’s famous thermal baths for a truly unique experience.
  • Danube River Cruise: Experience the beauty of Budapest by taking a Danube River cruise.
  • Horseback Riding: Take a horseback riding tour through Hungary’s rolling hills and scenic countryside.

Week-Long Itinerary for Hungary

If you’re planning a week-long trip to Hungary, here’s a suggested itinerary:

Day Activity
1 Explore Budapest: visit Buda Castle, the Hungarian Parliament, and St. Stephen’s Basilica
2 Take a Danube River Cruise and enjoy the panoramic views of Budapest
3 Visit Lake Balaton and spend the day swimming and relaxing on the beach
4 Explore the natural wonders of Aggtelek National Park
5 Take a wine tasting tour in the Tokaj wine region
6 Relax at one of Budapest’s famous thermal baths
7 Visit the picturesque town of Szentendre and explore its museums and galleries

Food and Drink Scene of Hungary

Hungarian cuisine is hearty and flavorful, with influences from Turkish, Austrian, and Balkan cuisine. Here are a few must-try dishes:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Goulash: A stew made with beef, vegetables, and spices, and served with noodles.
  • Langos: A deep-fried dough that is topped with sour cream and grated cheese.
  • Chimney Cake: A sweet pastry that is rolled in cinnamon and sugar.
  • Fisherman’s Soup: A spicy fish soup made with river fish and paprika.

Local Beverages

  • Tokaji: A sweet dessert wine that is considered the “wine of kings.”
  • Pálinka: A traditional Hungarian brandy that is made from fruit.
  • Unicum: A herbal liqueur that is known for its medicinal properties.

Where to Stay in Hungary: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Hungary offers a range of accommodation options, from luxury hotels to budget-friendly hostels and guesthouses. Here are a few options:

Luxury Hotels

  • Four Seasons Hotel Gresham Palace: A luxurious five-star hotel located in the heart of Budapest.
  • Aria Hotel: A boutique hotel that offers a unique musical theme.

Budget-Friendly Options

  • Maverick City Lodge: A trendy and affordable hostel located in Budapest’s city center.
  • Palazzo Zichy: A stylish and affordable hotel located in Budapest’s historic Palace Quarter.

What to Buy in Hungary: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Hungary is known for its artisanal products and handicrafts. Here are a few souvenirs to consider:

  • Hungarian Folk Art: Handmade pottery, embroidery, and textiles.
  • Paprika: A popular spice that is used in Hungarian cuisine.
  • Rubik’s Cube: The Rubik’s Cube was invented by a Hungarian sculptor in the 1970s.

Local Culture and Customs of Hungary

Hungary has a rich cultural heritage that is reflected in its customs and traditions. Here are a few things to keep in mind:

  • Dress Code: Dress modestly when visiting churches or other religious sites.
  • Greetings: Hungarians shake hands when meeting new people.
  • Tipping: It is customary to tip in Hungary, with 10% being the standard.
  • Language: Hungarian is the official language of Hungary, and English is widely spoken in tourist areas.

Best Time to Visit Hungary

The best time to visit Hungary is during the spring (March to May) or fall (September to November). The weather is mild, and there are fewer crowds than in the summer months.

Do’s and Don’ts of Hungary

Do:

  • Try the local cuisine and wines.
  • Explore Hungary’s stunning architecture and cultural landmarks.
  • Visit the thermal baths for a relaxing soak.
  • Take a Danube River cruise for stunning views of Budapest.

Don’t:

  • Smoke in public places, as it is illegal in Hungary.
  • Discuss controversial topics like politics or religion with locals.
  • Speak loudly or draw attention to yourself in public places.

Safety and Health

Safety in Hungary

Hungary is a relatively safe country, with a low crime rate. However, travelers should still exercise caution and avoid unlit and isolated areas at night.

Health in Hungary

There are no specific health risks associated with Hungary. However, it is always wise to take basic precautions, such as drinking bottled water and wearing sunscreen.

Getting to Hungary: Transportation Options and Logistics

Hungary is easily accessible by air, train, or road. Here are a few options:

Airports Near Hungary

  • Budapest Ferenc Liszt International Airport: The largest airport in Hungary, with flights to and from major European cities.

Bus Stations Near Hungary

  • Budapest Népliget Bus Station: The largest international bus station in Budapest, with connections to several European cities.

Other Options

  • Train: Hungary has a well-developed train system, with connections to several European cities.
  • Car: If you’re driving to Hungary, be aware that the roads can be narrow and winding in rural areas.

How to Get Around in Hungary

Hungary has an efficient public transportation system that includes buses, trams, and the metro. Taxis are also readily available in major cities. If you’re planning to explore the countryside, renting a car is a good option.

Where to Go Next from Hungary: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Hungary is located in the heart of Europe and is easily accessible to several neighboring countries. Here are a few nearby destinations to consider:

  • Austria: Visit Vienna, Salzburg, or Innsbruck for more stunning architecture and cultural landmarks.
  • Croatia: Explore the stunning Adriatic coastline and visit towns like Dubrovnik and Split.
  • Czech Republic: Visit Prague, one of Europe’s most beautiful cities.

Conclusion

Hungary is a unique travel destination that offers a rich blend of cultural and natural attractions. Whether you’re interested in exploring Budapest’s stunning architecture, soaking in the thermal baths, or hiking through the countryside, Hungary has something for everyone. Use this comprehensive guide to plan your trip and make the most of your visit to this beautiful country.
