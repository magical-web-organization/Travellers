Introduction to Csongrád-Csanád County

Csongrád-Csanád County is located in Southern Great Plain, Hungary and is one of the largest counties of the country. The county is situated at the confluence of the Tisza and Maros rivers and is known for its stunning natural beauty, rich history, and cultural heritage. The county has a population of approximately 425,000 and is home to many charming towns and villages.

List of Places to Visit in Csongrád-Csanád County

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

If you are interested in history and culture, Csongrád-Csanád County has plenty of museums and historical places to explore. Here are some of the top picks:

  • Móra Ferenc Museum: This museum in Szeged is dedicated to local history, art and folklore. It has a wide range of permanent and temporary exhibitions, and a library with over half a million volumes.
  • Szeged Synagogue: This beautiful synagogue is one of the largest in Europe and houses a museum with a collection of Jewish art and artifacts.
  • Szeged National Theater: This art nouveau building hosts opera, ballet, and theater performances and is the cultural hub of the city.
  • Castle of Gyula: A stunning castle built in the 14th century, which now holds a museum showcasing the history of the castle and the town.

Natural Attractions of Csongrád-Csanád County: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

From hiking to biking, Csongrád-Csanád County has plenty of outdoor activities to thrill visitors. Here are some of the best natural attractions to explore:

  • Hortobágy National Park: This UNESCO World Heritage Site is Hungary’s largest protected area and is home to rare flora and fauna. Visitors can take a horseback ride through the park or join in a birdwatching tour.
  • Ópusztaszer National Heritage Park: A historical park featuring traditional Hungarian buildings, the park gives a immersive experience into Hungarian history and culture.
  • Szeged Zoo: Located on the outskirts of Szeged, this zoo houses over 500 animals and is a favourite among families.
  • Tisza River: Flowing through the county, the Tisza River offers numerous opportunities for boating and fishing.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Csongrád-Csanád County

Csongrád-Csanád County is a perfect destination for hikers and cyclists, with numerous designated routes to explore. Here are some of the top routes to consider:

  • Tisza River Greenway: This nearly 70 km-long bike path takes in scenic town of Szeged, forested areas and wildlife observation stations.
  • Bugac Puszta Trail: This trail in Kiskunság National Park is perfect for horse riders, cyclists and hikers, who can come across grazing cattle or local indigenous animals.
  • Nyírjes Nature Trail: This route showcases the beautiful countryside and lush green forests of Körös-Maros National Park.

Best Things to Do in Csongrád-Csanád County: Activities and Tours

If you’re looking for activities to do during your visit, Csongrád-Csanád County has plenty to offer. Here are some of the most popular options:

  • Thermal Baths: There are many thermal baths in the county with healing waters, such as Hagymatikum in Makó and Szeged Spa and Wellness Centre.
  • Wine Tasting: The county has a rich wine growing culture and its wines are known for their unique character. Take a tour the vineyards and sample the local wines in the towns of Szekszárd and Villány.
  • Hungarian Folk Dance: Take part in a Hungarian folk dance workshop and learn the basic steps from professional dancers.
  • Chocolate Museum: The Kakasbonbon Chocolate Museum in Szeged is a favourite among chocolate lovers, offering interactive experiences and hands-on workshops for kids and adults alike.

Food and Drink Scene of Csongrád-Csanád County

When it comes to dining, Csongrád-Csanád County has much to offer. From street food to fine dining, you’ll find it all here. Here are some of the must-try dishes of the county:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Fisherman’s Soup: A classic Hungarian spicy fish soup made with paprika, carp and catfish
  • Goulash Soup: A hearty, meaty soup made with potatoes, paprika, and onions
  • Szegedi Halászlé: Another fish soup variety, with a sour and spicy flavour
  • Székelykáposzta: A pork and sour cabbage dish seasoned with paprika and garlic
  • Chimney Cake: A sweet, spiralled bread dessert cooked over a charcoal fire, topped with sugar or cinnamon

Local Beverages

  • Tokaji Wine: Considered one of the finest dessert wines in the world, Tokaji wine is a must-try if you planning to visit Hungary.
  • Pálinka: Traditional Hungarian fruit brandy made from apricots, plums, or pears.
  • Beer: There is a large number of microbreweries found throughout the county, and many excellent beers on offer.

Where to Stay in Csongrád-Csanád County: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

From budget-friendly hostels to upscale hotels, Csongrád-Csanád County has plenty of accommodation options for travellers. Here are some of the best places to stay:

  • City Hotel Szeged: Located in the heart of Szeged, this hotel is a short walk from the city’s main attractions.
  • Hotel Makar Sports & Wellness: A luxury hotel in Szeged, perfect for relaxation and indulgence.
  • Liza Aqua & Conference Hotel: A family-friendly hotel in Mórahalom with a large spa water park.
  • Kiskajári Fogadó: For a more rural holiday experience, stay in this guesthouse located in Csongrád village.

What to Buy in Csongrád-Csanád County: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

There are many options for visitors looking to buy local products and souvenirs in Csongrád-Csanád County. Here are some of the best things to buy:

  • Handmade Pottery: The county has a rich tradition of pottery, and visitors can purchase handmade pieces from local artists.
  • Paprika: Hungary is famous for its spicy paprika, and you can find many varieties of it in the county.
  • Szegedi Szalámi: A popular Hungarian sausage made with pork and paprika, perfect for snacking or taking home as a gift.
  • Hungarian Folk Art: Csongrád-Csanád County is home to many talented folk artists, whose work can be found in local shops and galleries.

Local Culture and Customs of Csongrád-Csanád County

Hungary has a rich cultural heritage, and Csongrád-Csanád County is no exception. Here are some of the local customs and traditions to look out for:

  • Folk Dance: Hungarian folk dance is an important part of the country’s culture and is performed at festivals and events throughout the year.
  • Horse Shows: The county is known for its exceptional horsemen, and visitors can attend equestrian shows and competitions.
  • Paprika Gathering: In the autumn, there is an annual paprika gathering in Kalocsa, where visitors can learn how paprika is harvested and processed.
  • Embroidery: Traditional Hungarian embroidery can be found on clothing, tablecloths, and other items sold in local shops.

Best Time to Visit Csongrád-Csanád County

Csongrád-Csanád County can be visited all year round, but the best time to visit is between April to October when the weather is mild and festivals are held. The county has a warm and dry climate, with hot summers and cool winters.

Do’s and Don’ts of Csongrád-Csanád County

Do:

  • Try the local cuisine and beverages
  • Respect local customs and traditions
  • Learn some Hungarian phrases before visiting
  • Visit the thermal baths and enjoy the relaxing waters

Don’t:

  • Do not photograph people without permission
  • Do not overindulge in the local alcoholic beverages
  • Do not litter in nature areas or public spaces
  • Do not disregard traffic laws while driving

Safety and Health

Safety in Csongrád-Csanád County

Csongrád-Csanád County is relatively safe, and visitors should take standard safety precautions while travelling. Keep an eye on your belongings, especially in crowded areas and tourist attractions.

Health in Csongrád-Csanád County

There are several public and private hospitals and clinics in the county that provide a high standard of medical care. It is recommended that visitors obtain travel insurance before visiting.

Getting to Csongrád-Csanád County: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Csongrád-Csanád County

The nearest international airport to Csongrád-Csanád County is Budapest Ferenc Liszt International Airport, which is approximately 170 km away. There are also domestic airports in Debrecen and Szeged.

Bus Stations Near Csongrád-Csanád County

Csongrád-Csanád County is well connected to major cities and towns in Hungary by bus. The main bus stations are located in Szeged and Szentes.

Other Options

Visitors can also travel to the county by train or car. The M5 motorway runs through the county, connecting it to the rest of Hungary.

How to Get Around in Csongrád-Csanád County

Visitors can easily get around Csongrád-Csanád County by public transport or by renting a car. There are also private taxi services in the county.

Where to Go Next from Csongrád-Csanád County: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Csongrád-Csanád County is within easy reach of several other destinations in Hungary. Here are some nearby places to consider:

  • Budapest: The capital of Hungary, Budapest is just a few hours’ drive away, known for its stunning architecture, fantastic cuisine, and vibrant culture.
  • Debrecen: The second-largest city in Hungary, Debrecen is located east of Csongrád-Csanád County, and has many interesting museums and cultural attractions.
  • Pécs: The city of Pécs in the southwest of Hungary is known for its charming old town and beautiful architecture.

Conclusion

Csongrád-Csanád County is a beautiful region of Hungary, with much to offer visitors. From the rich cultural heritage to the stunning natural beauty, there is something for everyone here. So what are you waiting for? Pack your bags and discover this hidden gem of Hungary for yourself!
