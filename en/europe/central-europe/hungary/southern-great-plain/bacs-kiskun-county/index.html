Introduction to Bács-Kiskun County

Located in Southern Great Plain in Hungary, Bács-Kiskun County is a hidden gem waiting to be explored. The county boasts a unique mix of cultural and natural attractions that are sure to leave a lasting impression. From its diverse museums and historic sites to its breathtaking landscapes and charming towns, there is something for everyone in Bács-Kiskun County. Plan your trip carefully, and get ready to experience the best this stunning region has to offer.

List of Places to Visit in Bács-Kiskun County

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Kecskemét City Museum: This museum showcases the region’s rich history through a vast collection of artifacts, photographs, and documents, including a significant collection of folk art.

  • Kiskunság National Park Visitor Centre: This visitor centre provides a comprehensive overview of the national park, its flora and fauna, and the region’s cultural and historical heritage.

  • Cifra Palace: This beautiful Art Nouveau building in Kecskemét houses a stunning collection of folk art and embroidery, including the famous “szűr,” a traditional Hungarian cloak.

  • Lajosmizse Skanzen: This open-air museum aims to preserve and showcase traditional Hungarian village life and culture, with exhibits including farmhouses, barns, and crafts workshops.

Natural Attractions of Bács-Kiskun County: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Kiskunság National Park: Covering nearly 570 square kilometers, Kiskunság National Park encompasses an incredible range of habitats and landscapes, from wetlands to sand dunes, from forests to grasslands. It is home to a wide range of flora and fauna, including some rare and endangered species.

  • River Danube: The second-longest river in Europe, the Danube is a vital lifeline for many Central European countries, including Hungary. The river’s scenic beauty is best appreciated on a boat tour.

  • Hortobágy: One of Hungary’s most iconic landscapes, Hortobágy is a vast plain dotted with traditional shepherds’ huts, wild horses, and beautiful seasonal blooms. The park covers over 80,000 hectares of wetlands, meadows, and forests.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Bács-Kiskun County

For those who love the great outdoors, Bács-Kiskun County has plenty of amazing hiking and biking routes to explore. Here are a few to get you started:

  • Lake Velence Bike Route: A 43-km bike loop that takes you around Hungary’s third-largest lake, offering stunning views of the surrounding countryside.

  • Kecskemét City Hike: A gentle walk around the heart of Kecskemét, highlighting the city’s most iconic landmarks.

  • Bike Route along the Danube: A 90-km route that runs along the Danube, starting from Szekszárd and finishing in Mohács, passing through beautiful villages and scenic countryside.

Best Things to Do in Bács-Kiskun County: Activities and Tours

  • Wine Tasting in Szekszárd and Villány: Hungary is a country with a long tradition of wine-making, and Bács-Kiskun County boasts some of the best wine regions in the country. Take a wine tour to taste award-winning wines and learn about Hungary’s wine-making history.

  • Horse-riding in Lajosmizse: Lajosmizse is known for its famous horse shows, in which skilled horsemen demonstrate their expert equestrian skills, including the Hungarian version of the Puszta five! Afterward, visitors can enjoy a horseback ride through the surrounding countryside.

  • Hot Air Balloon Tour in Kecskemét: Take a hot air balloon to enjoy stunning views of the city and its surrounding regions. This is a must-do activity for those who love a bit of adventure, and also for those who want breathtaking views of Bács-Kiskun County.

Week-Long Itinerary for Bács-Kiskun County

For those who want to explore Bács-Kiskun County in-depth, here is a week-long itinerary to get you started:

Day 1 - Kecskemét

Visit Kecskemét’s famous Cifra Palace, the Kecskemét City Museum, and the Hungarian Museum of Naive Art. Spend the evening exploring the city’s nightlife and dining options.

Day 2 - Lake Velence

Spend the day cycling around Lake Velence, stopping off at charming villages along the way.

Day 3 - Szekszárd

Take a wine tour of Szekszárd’s famous vineyards and sample some award-winning Hungarian wines.

Day 4 - Lajosmizse

Experience traditional Hungarian life in the open-air museum, Lajosmizse Skanzen, and have a horse ride in the surrounding countryside.

Day 5 - Hortobágy

Spend a day exploring Hortobágy National Park, including a visit to the traditional shepherds’ huts called “csikós” and the famous Nine-holed Bridge.

Day 6 - Danube

Take a boat or kayak tour of the Danube to see some of Hungary’s stunning landscapes and floodplains.

Day 7 - Mohács

Visit Mohács, a town with a rich cultural and historical heritage, including the famous Busó Festivities.

Food and Drink Scene of Bács-Kiskun County

Must-Try Dishes

  • Goulash Soup: One of Hungary’s most popular dishes, goulash soup is a hearty stew that generally consists of meat, vegetables, and spices. It is a must-try dish for any visitor to Hungary.

  • Langos: A deep-fried flatbread topped with cheese, sour cream, and garlic, langos is a delicious snack that can be found almost everywhere in Hungary.

  • Fisherman’s Soup: A delicious bowl of hot soup made from various types of freshwater fish, Fisherman’s Soup, or “Halászlé,” is a must-try for seafood lovers.

Local Beverages

  • Palinka: Hungary’s national drink, Palinka is a fruit brandy that comes in various flavors, including plum, apricot, and pear.

  • Tokaji Wine: Known as the “king of wines and the wine of kings,” Tokaji is a sweet wine that is produced in the northeastern region of Hungary, and is world-famous for its distinctive flavor.

  • Four Points by Sheraton Kecskemét: A luxury hotel located in the heart of Kecskemét, Four Points offers comfortable and modern rooms, free Wi-Fi, and an indoor swimming pool.

  • Hotel Három Gúnár: Located in a quiet, picturesque village, Hotel Három Gúnár is an eco-friendly hotel that offers a unique and comfortable experience.

  • Kovács Vendégház (Guesthouse): Situated in the middle of a vineyard, the Kovács Vendégház offers stunning views of the surrounding region and comfortable accommodation at an affordable price.

What to Buy in Bács-Kiskun County: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Traditional Embroidery: Bács-Kiskun County is known for its beautiful traditional embroidery, which includes intricate patterns and designs that reflect Hungarian culture and heritage.

  • Paprika: Hungary is famous for its paprika, which is used in many traditional dishes. Be sure to pick up some high-quality paprika to take back home with you.

Local Culture and Customs of Bács-Kiskun County

Hungarian culture is a unique blend of influences from various European nations, including Turkey, Austria, and Russia. Visitors are encouraged to respect local customs, including wearing modest clothing when visiting churches and other religious sites.

Best Time to Visit Bács-Kiskun County

The best time to visit Bács-Kiskun County is during the spring and fall months (April-June and September-November), when the region experiences mild, pleasant temperatures and fewer crowds. Summer is a popular time for tourism, but also a complete contrast to peaceful winters filled with snow-covered landscapes and local festivals.

Do’s and Don’ts of Bács-Kiskun County

Do:

  • Try Local Food: Hungarian cuisine is world-famous for its delicious and hearty dishes. Be sure to try traditional Hungarian dishes like goulash soup, Fisherman’s Soup, and langos.

  • Visit the National Park: Kiskunság National Park is a must-visit destination for nature lovers, offering a range of habitats and landscapes to explore.

  • Respect Local Rules and Customs: Hungary has its unique culture and customs that should be respected and understood as an integral aspect of their history and heritage.

Don’t:

  • Underestimate the Weather: Hungary experiences extreme weather conditions, from heavy snow to extreme heat. Be sure to pack appropriate clothing for the season.

  • Be Disrespectful in Religious Sites: Hungary has some beautiful religious sites, including churches and monasteries. Always dress appropriately and behave respectfully.

  • Overdo the Paprika: Hungary is famous for its paprika, but it can be quite spicy for those not accustomed to it. Start off with a little, and then work your way up.

Safety and Health

Safety in Bács-Kiskun County

Overall, Bács-Kiskun County is a safe and welcoming destination for tourists. However, visitors should still exercise caution when traveling, particularly when using public transportation or visiting tourist sites. As with any travel, it is essential to keep your belongings secure and be cautious in unfamiliar surroundings.

Health in Bács-Kiskun County

Bács-Kiskun County is generally a healthy destination, and visitors are unlikely to encounter any major health risks. However, as with any travel, it is essential to follow good hygiene practices, including washing your hands regularly, drinking bottled water, and using sunscreen when needed.

Getting to Bács-Kiskun County: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Bács-Kiskun County

The nearest airport to Bács-Kiskun County is Budapest Ferenc Liszt International Airport. From there, you can take a bus or taxi to Kecskemét and other towns in the county.

Bus Stations Near Bács-Kiskun County

Buses are a convenient and affordable way to travel to Bács-Kiskun County from other parts of Hungary and neighboring countries. The county has several bus stations, including Kecskemét Bus Station and Szeged Bus Station.

Other Options

If you prefer to travel by train, the county has several railway stations, including Kecskemét Railway Station and Kiskunhalas Railway Station.

How to Get Around in Bács-Kiskun County

The easiest way to get around Bács-Kiskun County is by car or bicycle. Rental cars are available in most towns, and bike rentals can be found in some tourist areas. Bus and train services also connect the major towns in the county and are an affordable alternative.

Where to Go Next from Bács-Kiskun County: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Bács-Kiskun County is surrounded by other stunning destinations that are worth exploring:

  • Szentendre: a charming town on the Danube known for its artist colony and traditional Hungarian handicrafts.
  • Budapest: the capital city of Hungary, packed with museums, historic sites, and cultural landmarks.
  • Pécs: a city with a rich cultural and architectural heritage, featuring stunning buildings and a vibrant arts scene.
  • Szeged: a vibrant university town known for its beautiful architecture, lively festivals, and excellent dining options.

Conclusion

Bács-Kiskun County is a hidden gem in Hungary’s Southern Great Plain, offering a unique mix of cultural and natural attractions. With its rich history, breathtaking landscapes, delicious local food, and vibrant culture, there are plenty of reasons to visit this stunning region. Plan your trip carefully, and get ready to experience the best that Bács-Kiskun County has to offer.
