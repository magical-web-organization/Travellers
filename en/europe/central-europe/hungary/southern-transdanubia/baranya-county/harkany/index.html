Introduction to Harkány

Nestled in Baranya County, in Southern Transdanubia, Hungary, Harkány is a charming little town known for its thermal waters. The town’s history dates back to the Roman times when the hot springs were discovered as a cure for various ailments. Today, Harkány is a popular tourist destination, both for its natural beauty and cultural significance.

List of Places to Visit in Harkány

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Harkány Museum Explore the town’s history and cultural heritage through various exhibits, including artifacts from the Roman times and World War II.
  • Harkány Catholic Church Admire the intricate architecture and religious artwork of this beautiful, centuries-old church.
  • Pécs Cathedral Just a short drive from Harkány, Pécs Cathedral is a UNESCO World Heritage Site and one of the most impressive pieces of architecture in Hungary.

Natural Attractions of Harkány: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Harkány Thermal Spa Relax in the thermal waters and indulge in the spa treatments at this renowned attraction.
  • Harkány Arboretum Take a stroll through this tranquil garden, featuring various botanical specimens from around the world.
  • Zengővárkony Lookout Tower Climb up the tower and enjoy stunning views of Harkány and the surrounding countryside.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Harkány

  • Harkány-Siklós Cycling Route A 13 km scenic cycling trail connecting Harkány to the town of Siklós, passing by the beautiful vineyards and orchards along the way.
  • Zengővárkony-Liszkay Vineyard Hiking Trail A 4 km hiking trail starting from Zengővárkony and leading to the picturesque Liszkay Vineyard, where you can taste some of the best wines of the region.

Best Things to Do in Harkány: Activities and Tours

  • Horse-Drawn Carriage Tour Take a leisurely ride through the town and explore its highlights in style.
  • Wine Tasting Tour Visit some of the best wineries in the region, taste local wines and learn about the production process from the experts.
  • Guided Tour of Pécs Join a guided tour of Pécs, the charming nearby town, and explore its many landmarks, including the historic Mosque Church and the Zsolnay Porcelain Museum.

Week-Long Itinerary for Harkány

Day 1: Explore Harkány Thermal Spa and Harkány Museum in the morning. Have traditional Hungarian goulash for lunch, and then visit the Harkány Catholic Church.

Day 2: Take the cycling route to Siklós Castle in the morning, and have a picnic lunch at the castle. In the afternoon, visit the Siklós Castle Museum.

Day 3: Go on a horse-drawn carriage tour of Harkány, and enjoy a wine tasting tour of nearby wineries in the afternoon.

Day 4: Head to Pécs and explore its many attractions, including the Pécs Cathedral, Zsolnay Porcelain Museum, and the historic Mosque Church.

Day 5: Visit Zengővárkony, climb up the lookout tower, and take the hiking trail to Liszkay Vineyard for a wine tasting session.

Day 6: Relax and spend the day at the thermal spa, indulging in spa treatments and soaking in the natural thermal waters.

Day 7: Take a guided tour of the town’s historic centre, and then head to Pécs for a last-minute souvenir shopping session.

Food and Drink Scene of Harkány

Must-Try Dishes

  • Halászlé A spicy fish soup made with various freshwater fish, hot paprika, and vegetables. Served with bread or boiled potatoes.
  • Gulyásleves Hungarian goulash, a hearty soup made with beef, potatoes, and paprika. Served with bread or noodles.
  • Rántott Csirkemell Breaded and deep-fried chicken breast served with mashed potatoes and seasonal vegetables.

Local Beverages

  • Tokaji A sweet dessert wine made from the Furmint grape variety, famous across Hungary and the world.
  • Pálinka A traditional Hungarian fruit brandy, available in various fruit flavors such as apricot, plum, and cherry.

Where to Stay in Harkány: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Baranya Hotel A three-star hotel located just a minute’s walk from the Harkány Thermal Spa.
  • Thermal Hotel A four-star hotel that offers spacious, air-conditioned rooms and suites, an indoor and outdoor pool, and a spa and wellness center.
  • Arboretum Hotel Harkány A boutique-style hotel overlooking the Harkány Arboretum, offering comfortable rooms and a restaurant serving delicious local cuisine

What to Buy in Harkány: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Traditional Hungarian Embroidery Handmade tablecloths, runners, and napkins featuring traditional Hungarian embroidery in vibrant colors and unique patterns.
  • Paprika Hungarian paprika is a must-try ingredient in Hungarian cuisine, and you can find various types and qualities of paprika in different shops around Harkány.
  • Local Wines Hungary produces some of the best wines in the world, and you can buy the most delicious ones at local wineries and shops.

Local Culture and Customs of Harkány

Hungarian culture is deeply rooted in traditions and customs, many of which you can experience in Harkány. Hungarians are known for their love of food, wine, and music, and you can expect to see and hear these things everywhere you go in Harkány.

Best Time to Visit Harkány

The best time to visit Harkány is in the spring or autumn when the temperatures are mild, and the town is not too crowded with tourists. Summer is the peak tourist season, and the town can get quite busy and hot. Winter is also a good option, as the thermal spa is open year-round, and the town is peaceful and quiet, offering a unique experience.

Do’s and Don’ts of Harkány

Do:

  • Try the local cuisine and wines.
  • Take a dip in the thermal waters.
  • Visit nearby towns and attractions.

Don’t:

  • Forget to pack comfortable shoes for hiking and cycling.
  • Leave your valuables unattended in public places.
  • Be disrespectful of local customs and traditions.

Safety and Health

Safety in Harkány

Harkány is a safe town with low crime rates. However, it’s always a good idea to take precautions and be aware of your surroundings, especially when traveling alone at night.

Health in Harkány

Harkány is known for its thermal waters and spas, which are believed to have healing properties for various ailments, including arthritis and chronic pain. If you have any health conditions, it’s always best to consult your doctor before traveling and taking spa treatments.

Getting to Harkány: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Harkány

The nearest airports to Harkány are in Pécs-Pogány (20 km), Osijek (77 km), and Budapest (220 km). There are regular bus services from Pécs and Osijek to Harkány, and you can take a train or shuttle service from Budapest.

Bus Stations Near Harkány

There are regular bus services from Pécs, Osijek, and other nearby towns to Harkány. The Harkány Bus Station is located in the town center, just a few minutes’ walk from the thermal spa and other attractions.

Other Options

You can also hire a car or taxi to get to Harkány from nearby towns or cities. There are several car rental agencies and taxi companies in Pécs and other nearby towns, and you can book them online or via phone.

How to Get Around in Harkány

The best way to get around in Harkány is on foot or by bike. The town is small and compact, and you can easily explore its attractions and landmarks within a few hours. There are also several bike rental services in the town. If you want to explore nearby towns or attractions, you can take a bus or hire a car or taxi.

Where to Go Next from Harkány: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Pécs The charming nearby town of Pécs is full of historical landmarks, cultural attractions, and delicious cuisine.
  • Villány The famous wine region of Villány is just a short drive from Harkány and offers some of the best wine tasting experiences in Hungary.
  • Orfű A tranquil town located just outside Pécs, Orfű is known for its stunning scenery, lakes, and outdoor activities.

Conclusion

Harkány is a hidden gem in Hungary, offering a unique blend of natural and cultural attractions, delicious food and wine, and a relaxing spa experience. Whether you come for a day trip or a week-long vacation, Harkány is sure to surprise and delight you with its charm and beauty.
