Pécs Travel Guide - A Comprehensive Tour Guide

Pécs, the fifth-largest city in Hungary, is a fascinating blend of stunning scenery, vibrant culture, ancient history, and modern amenities, located in the southern part of the country. Pécs is known for its rich architectural heritage and university town attractions. Whether you are wandering through the town’s peaceful parks, admiring the historical landmarks, or enjoying the lively student life, Pécs will not disappoint you.

Introduction to Pécs

Pécs, a cultural melting pot and one of Europe’s hidden treasures, has been inhabited since ancient times by various civilizations, all of which left indelible traces including ancient tombs, palaces, and other historic sites. The city is located in Southern Transdanubia region and is one of the centers of the Baranya County.

The first settlement here was founded by the Celts on the foothills of Mecsek Mountain in the Iron Age. After being under Roman, Ottoman, and Habsburg conquerors, they also left their own marks on the city. The Roman city of Sopianae was built here in the 2nd century AD. After the Ottoman Empire, the city became the center of Baranya County, which is a culturally important region in Hungary.

Visitors to the city can experience a juxtaposition of modern and medieval architecture which makes the city an ideal destination for everyone interested in history, culture, and art.

List of Places to Visit in Pécs

While in Pécs, you will have plenty of places to visit. Here is a list of the top tourist destinations to include in your travel itinerary:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Pécs has a rich cultural heritage that dates back to ancient times. During your stay in the city, you should make sure to visit some of the culturally important attractions, including:

  • Pécs Cathedral (Szent Péter és Pál-bazilika): one of the most important and beautiful buildings in Hungary.
  • Early Christian Necropolis: a UNESCO World Heritage Site, contains impressive tombs and sarcophagi carved out between the 4th and the 5th-centuries.
  • Zsolnay Cultural Quarter: It is a vibrant hub of art, culture, and entertainment containing Zsolnay Ceramics Museum, Pécs Gallery, Csontváry Museum, and more.
  • Mosque of Pasha Qasim: A striking example of Ottoman architecture, constructed in the 16th-century, the mosque is a reminder of the Ottoman Empire’s rule in Hungary.
  • Cella Septichora: An underground burial chamber with well-preserved frescoes.

Natural Attractions of Pécs: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Pécs and its surrounding areas have natural beauty and a fantastic outdoor adventure scene. Discover some of the natural attractions of Pécs, including:

  • Mecsek Mountain: A popular destination for hiking, camping, and skiing, with many trails and mountainous terrain to explore.
  • Villany Wine Region: A beautiful countryside with lush vineyards, tasting splendid wines made from traditional grape varieties.
  • Zoo Pécs: The animal welfare center offers an excellent opportunity to get up close and personal with a diverse collection of wildlife species.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Pécs

Hiking and biking are popular activities in Pécs, and the Mecsek Mountains provide an excellent opportunity for those looking to explore on foot or by bike. Some of the popular hiking trails include:

  • Mecsek Trail: A trail spanning over 60 km across the Mecsek Mountains, through forests, and along streams.
  • Jakab Hill Trail: A moderate hike to the top of Jakab Hill, with lovely views of the city, the surrounding countryside, and the Mecsek hills.
  • Hosszúhetény-Harkány Cycle Trail: A cycle route through the picturesque vineyards and thermal springs of Harkány.

Best Things to Do in Pécs: Activities and Tours

Pécs offers a wide range of exciting activities and tours for visitors to experience. Here are some of the best things to do in Pécs:

  • Take a walking tour of Pécs’s historical and cultural highlights with a local guide.
  • Experience the city’s vibrant student culture at student rock concerts, street festivals, and sports events.
  • Visit the Csontváry Museum to see the most extensive collection of Hungarian art.
  • Relax in a thermal bath at one of the city’s historic Turkish bath houses.
  • Take an hour-long architectural sightseeing bus tour of Pécs with a guide in multiple languages.

Food and Drink Scene of Pécs

Hungarian cuisine is rich, varied, and delicious, with influences from Eastern Europe, Austria, and the Balkans. Pécs is no exception and has some of the best restaurants, cafes, and bars in Hungary.

Must-Try Dishes

  • Goulash (gulyás): A Hungarian National dish, consisting of a hearty beef soup with paprika and other spices.
  • Lángos: Fried bread dough, topped with garlic, sour cream, and grated cheese.
  • Chimney cake (kürtőskalács): A traditional cake from Transylvania, a sweet pastry served hot and sprinkled with sugar.
  • Töltött káposzta: Stuffed cabbage rolls, filled with rice, meat and vegetable fillings, and a sweet tomato sauce.
  • Somlói Galuska: A dessert made with three kinds of sponge cake, vanilla custard, whipped cream, and rum or chocolate sauce.

Local Beverages

  • Pálinka: A traditional fruit brandy, usually from apricot, plum, or apple, with a distinct and potent flavor.
  • Unicum: Hungary’s best-known herbal liqueur, made with a secret formula of more than 40 herbs, roots, and spices.
  • Egri Bikavér: A red blend wine from the Eger wine region, with a rich and spicy flavor.
  • Tokaji Wine: An excellent white wine for dessert, with a honey-like sweetness and a rich, fruity flavor.

Where to Stay in Pécs: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Pécs has many options for accommodation, from luxury hotels to budget-friendly hostels. Here are some of the best places to stay in Pécs:

  • Hotel Palatinus: A historic hotel located in downtown Pécs, featuring elegant décor and comfortable guest rooms.
  • Corso Hotel: A modern hotel located near Pécs city center, with spacious rooms and suites with city views.
  • Hotel Arkadia: A hotel located near the city center, with air-conditioned rooms and modern amenities ideal for families.
  • Centrum Panzió: A charming guesthouse with comfortable and simple rooms, located in a historic building.
  • Hostel Pecs: A budget-friendly hostel providing a warm and welcoming environment to guests, with dormitories and private rooms.

What to Buy in Pécs: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Pécs has many unique and authentic products that can serve as souvenirs or gifts for your friends and family back home. Here are some of the must-buy items:

  • Zsolnay Porcelain: The beautiful, hand-made ceramics have been produced in Pécs since the 19th century and make excellent gifts with unique and extraordinary designs.
  • Wine from Villány and Pécsely: Both the two of the most fantastic wine-producing regions are known for producing wine full of character and unique flavor.
  • Paprika: Pécs is known for producing some of the best Hungarian paprika, with rich and aromatic flavors that can add a distinctive touch to your cooking.
  • Hand-made souvenirs: Pécs has a diverse range of crafts, so look for hand-made jewelry, textile art, and artisanal pottery, which make for unique and memorable gifts to bring home.

Local Culture and Customs of Pécs

Hungary has its own unique cultural customs and traditions, and Pécs is not an exception. To experience the local culture, you should:

  • Attend a Hungarian folk dance performance. Pécs has several annual folk festivals, where visitors can watch professional dancers perform.
  • Learn how to cook traditional Hungarian dishes. For example, local cooking classes can give you the opportunity to make your own Hungarian dishes.
  • Visit the Pécs-Egyházmegyei Kincstár (Diocesan Treasury) to explore the city’s ecclesiastical heritage.

Best Time to Visit Pécs

Pécs is a great year-round destination, although the summer months are optimal for outdoor activities such as hiking and biking. The best time to visit to enjoy all the outdoor activities is between April to October, when the weather is warm and mild, and everything outdoors is in full bloom.

Do’s and Don’ts of Pécs

Do:

  • Be respectful towards local customs and traditions while exploring Pécs.
  • Dress comfortably and bring a good and comfortable pair of walking shoes to explore Pécs on foot.
  • Try the local cuisine, Hungarian paprika, and wines.
  • Ask for permission before taking photographs of people, especially in markets or crowded places.

Don’t:

  • Don’t smoke indoors, and be sure to check with your accommodation beforehand.
  • Don’t litter or be rude to local people and respect their privacy.
  • Don’t forget to tip your waiter or have a coffee in a quaint café or restaurant; it is customary in Hungary to leave a tip of up to 10%.

Safety and Health

Before traveling to Pécs, it’s essential to know some safety and health concerns.

Safety in Pécs

Pécs is a safe city, although visitors should always take care of their valuables and watch their surroundings, especially in crowded places, just as one should when traveling to any new destination.

Health in Pécs

Health services and medical care are available in Pécs. Visitors should get travel insurance, recommend over-the-counter medication, carry a first aid kit, and for those who need medical services or seek medical advice, visit local medical centers or hospitals.

Getting to Pécs: Transportation Options and Logistics

Here are the different transportation options available when getting to Pécs:

Airports Near Pécs

The nearest airport to Pécs is Pecs-Pogany Airport, only about 10 kilometers from the city center, although many visitors fly to Budapest, arriving at Ferenc Liszt International Airport, which is about 200 kilometers away and take a bus or train to Pécs.

Bus Stations Near Pécs

Pécs operates a regular bus service, where travelers can arrive in Pécs from cities across Hungary and all over Europe. The central bus station is a half-hour walk to the city center, but it is easy to find taxis and car rental services from the bus terminal.

Other Options

Tourists can also opt for a private car, with taxis and rental cars also available from the airport and the city center.

How to Get Around in Pécs

Would you like to walk, ride a bike or take the bus? Pécs is a great city to see on foot, but public transit and bike rental services are also available.

Public transportation options include buses. Services usually operate between 5:00 a.m. until 11:00 pm daily. Biking in Pécs is also a great way to explore the city; the Mecsek hills offer great protected cycling routes which offer scenic views of the surrounding countryside, and the city center has many bike rental companies to choose from.

Where to Go Next from Pécs: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

When you are finished exploring Pécs, there are plenty of exciting places to visit nearby:

  • The picturesque lake fishing town of Orfű.
  • Take a trip to the beautiful city of Szekszárd, which is known as the wine capital of Hungary.
  • The charming city of Kaposvár, with its quaint streets, alleys, and pretty landmarks, is less than an hour away from Pécs.
  • Take a trip to the elegant city of Szeged, located in southeast Hungary, known for its vibrant summer festivals and architecture.

Conclusion

Pécs is a town of incredible cultural, artistic, and historical richness. With its charm and various cultural offerings, Pécs is truly a jewel of Hungary, making it a must-see destination for all travelers interested in history, culture, and natural beauty. The city has plenty of tourist attractions, popular festivals, and great restaurants to make you feel welcome. A journey to Pécs, Hungary, might change your view of the world forever!
