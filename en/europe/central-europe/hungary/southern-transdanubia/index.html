Introduction to Southern Transdanubia

Southern Transdanubia is a region in Hungary, located in central Europe and bordered by Croatia and Serbia. This region boasts beautiful natural scenery, rich cultural history, and plenty of outdoor adventure activities that make it a must-visit destination for travelers.

In this comprehensive travel guide, we aim to provide you with a complete rundown of the top places to visit in Southern Transdanubia, including culturally significant attractions, natural wonders, hiking/biking routes, amazing things to do, week-long itinerary, where to eat and drink, where to stay, local culture and customs, getting around, and much more.

List of Places to Visit in Southern Transdanubia

From cultural highlights to natural wonders, Southern Transdanubia offers a variety of must-visit destinations for tourists.

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Southern Transdanubia is home to many historical and cultural landmarks that give travelers an extensive understanding of Hungary’s rich past and present. Here are some of the top attractions in the region:

  • Pécs Cathedral: This iconic structure is an architectural marvel built in the Gothic style. Built over several centuries, this cathedral is one of the most significant in all of Hungary.
  • Zsolnay Museum: Visitors can get a sneak peek into the creativity of Zsolnay ceramics at this unique museum, showcasing vibrant and innovative designs from this Hungarian brand.
  • Castle of Siklós: This monument from the 1200s is one of the best-preserved castles in Hungary. It offers a fascinating glimpse into the region’s past.
  • Medieval Synagogue of Sopron: A medieval synagogue constructed in the 1300s is still in remarkable condition.
  • Ópusztaszer National Heritage Park: A national museum and nature reserve dedicated to Hungary’s premodern history back to the time of Árpáds, the founder of Hungary.

Natural Attractions of Southern Transdanubia: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Southern Transdanubia’s natural beauty has inspired countless visitors to explore its lush forests, tranquil lakes, and breathtaking landscapes. Here are some of the top natural attractions of the region:

  • Lake Balaton: This large freshwater lake is one of the most popular spots in the region and offers picturesque views and plenty of water activities.
  • Mecsek Mountains: This mountain range is the region’s biggest draw for hikers and cyclists, with the highest peak elevation reaching 694 meters. It is also famous for its underground caves and wine-cellars.
  • Drava River: The Drava river is a popular spot for fishing, picnics, and general outdoor relaxation.
  • Gemenc Forest: This forest is the largest contiguous woodland area in Central Europe and is home to around 7,000 different species of animals and plants.
  • Pécs Arboretum: This collection of trees and plants features the best of Hungarian dendrology with 600 different tree, shrub, and herb species.

Hiking/Biking Routes in Southern Transdanubia

Southern Transdanubia boasts several routes for both hiking and biking enthusiasts. Taking on any of these routes is an excellent way to experience the best of the natural scenery on foot or on wheels. Here are some of the best hiking/biking routes:

  • Mecsek Trail: This trail covers much of the Mecsek mountain range and is ideal for both novice and experienced hikers.
  • Villány Wine Trail: This easy trail takes hikers through lovely vineyards on hillsides and charming villages, finishing with wine tasting.
  • Drava Bike Path: This 145-kilometer-long bike route runs along the Drava river from Barcs to Legrad in Croatia offers exceptional views of the river and picturesque towns.
  • Szekszárd-Bátaszék Bike Path: A 25-kilometer bike path that lays between Szekszárd and Bátaszék, offering great views of the Danube river, and perfect for beginners.
  • Transdanubian Hills: This trail offers excellent views of the valleys and hills that make up the landscape of southern Transdanubia. Trek goes through Pannonhalma (a UNESCO World Heritage Site) and the Ság hills.

Best Things to Do in Southern Transdanubia: Activities and Tours

Southern Transdanubia has an abundance of things to do, from exploring cultural sites and eco-tourism activities to wine-tasting and geocaching. Here are some of the top things to do:

  • Explore Pécs: Visit the city of Pécs, which is renowned for its rich history, beautiful streets, and bustling nightlife.
  • Villány Wine Region: Visit the wine-making region of Villány and taste different varieties of Hungarian wines in its cellars.
  • Szentendre: A picturesque village with streets full of artists and charming houses.
  • Pécs Mecsek Caving: Take on an expedition into the heart of the Mecsek Mountains and uncover the underground natural wonders and intricate cave systems.
  • Play an Escape Game: Visitors can take part in exciting escape games within the castle of Siklós.

Week-Long Itinerary for Southern Transdanubia

Suppose you’re planning a week-long adventure in Southern Transdanubia, taking in the top destinations in the region. In that case, we suggest this itinerary:

Day 1: Pécs

Explore Pécs with a visit to its historical Mosque Church and beauty-lined streets. Walk the Zsolnay tile-lined streets to the Zsolnay Museum or Szechenyi Square, which is the city’s center of social life.

Day 2: Szentendre

This day is primarily focused on a trip to Szentendre: A small village with an artist colony tucked away in central Transdanubia.

Day 3: Villány Wine Region

Villány is a unique wine region in Hungary, famous for its rich and dense red wines. The day is dedicated to wine-tasting and exploring the region’s wine cellars.

Day 4: Boat Ride in Drava River

Spend the day on the Drava River, one of the most picturesque rivers in Hungary. Among the highlights: spotting rare bird species and fishing.

Day 5: Mecsek Mountains

Explore the Mecsek Mountains with a trek in the refreshing nature, visit the medieval monastery of Pécsvárad and explore its underground caves and widen your lungs.

Day 6: Castle of Siklós

Take a guided tour of the medieval castle of the Siklós Castle while learning about its history and the fascinating while enjoying an escape game experience.

Day 7: Wrap up and Departure

Take the last day to explore and shop for fantastic souvenirs in different villages before departing from bustling Mediterranean-style Pécs’ Airport.

Food and Drink Scene of Southern Transdanubia

Hungarian cuisine is a wonderful blend of cultural influences that make it unique. Southern Transdanubia’s cuisine is particularly enjoyable, with classic Hungarian dishes washed down with famous local wines.

Must-Try Dishes

  • Gulyás: Hungarian goulash is a delicious meat stew prepared with a rich paprika sauce.
  • Halászlé: This hot and spicy fish soup is distinct to Hungarian cuisine. It originates from Southern Transdanubia, and usually, it is prepared with carp fish.
  • Lecsó: A traditional vegetarian Hungarian dish made with peppers, tomatoes, and onions.
  • Pörkölt: A beef stew with a rich, paprika-based sauce.
  • Lángos: A deep-fried pastry with toppings like garlic, sour cream, or cheese.

Local Beverages

Southern Transdanubia is a wine-making region, and the wines produced here are of excellent quality. The region’s signature varieties are the rose and red wines of Villány and the white wines of Szekszárd. Be sure to try:

  • Villány Red Wines: Full-bodied and rich wines with shades of blackcurrant and cherry flavors.
  • Szekszárd White Wines: Dry and fragrant wines with floral and fruity notes.

Where to Stay in Southern Transdanubia: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Southern Transdanubia has an extensive range of accommodation options for different budgets, ranging from luxury hotels to budget hostels to guesthouses and apartments. Here are some of the best options:

  • Boutique Hotel in Pécs: A gorgeous boutique with 17th-century architecture in the heart of Pécs
  • La Contessa Castle Hotel in Szilvásvarad: A modern luxury boutique hotel located in the charming region of Szilvásvarad.
  • Hotel Palatinus in Pécs: A world-renowned hotel with a brilliant location in the center of Pécs, dating back to 1915.
  • Guest House in Szekszard: Offers excellent views and accommodations for budget travelers who want more privacy.

What to Buy in Southern Transdanubia: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Southern Transdanubia’s markets have some of the most beautiful local products, including ceramics, wines, carved sculptures, and traditional clothes. Here are some of the best things to buy:

  • Zsolnay Porcelain: A beautiful Hungarian porcelain brand from the 1800s.
  • Villány and Szekszárd Wines: Excellent red and white wine varieties, distinctive to Southern Transdanubia.
  • Hungarian Embroidery: A lovely souvenir that showcases the region’s traditional craftsmanship.
  • Herend Porcelain: Herend is a world-renowned porcelain brand that produces elegant and extravagant items.

Local Culture and Customs of Southern Transdanubia

Southern Transdanubia has a rich cultural history inherited from the Ottoman and Roman Empires. Locals are proud of their heritage and their food and drinks, and winemaking traditions. The Hungarian language is distinct, with Cyrillic letters, and many locals preserve their ancient heritage.

Best Time to Visit Southern Transdanubia

The best time to visit Southern Transdanubia is during the summer months, from June to September, when the weather is pleasant and perfect for outdoor activities. Spring and autumn are also fantastic months to visit as the air is fresh, and the landscape turns beautiful shades of red and gold. Winter is cold in Southern Transdanubia, although the Mecsek Mountains provide a fantastic winter experience for skiing and snowboarding.

Do’s and Don’ts of Southern Transdanubia

Do:

  • Respect the culture and traditions.
  • Try the local wines and cuisine.
  • Explore the serene natural landscapes.
  • Take on an underground tour of the local caves.
  • Learn a few phrases in Hungarian.

Don’t:

  • Do not litter or damage the natural environment.
  • Do not drink and drive.
  • Don’t wear shoes inside temples and monasteries.
  • Don’t take photos without permission.

Safety and Health

Southern Transdanubia is generally a safe place to travel to, but travelers must still follow standard safety precautions as they would in any city. Mosquito problems can affect the area during the summer months, and tick-borne diseases are common in some areas. Make sure to use insect repellent and check your skin after outdoor activities.

Safety in Southern Transdanubia

  • Stay alert when traveling alone or at night.
  • Do not leave any valuable items within view in your car.
  • Beware of pickpockets and shoplifting.

Health in Southern Transdanubia

  • Drink bottled or sealed water.
  • Wear long sleeves and pants to prevent insect bites.
  • Treat and check for tick bites after exploring the natural spaces.
  • Vaccinations are not compulsory, but it is suggested for visitors to be updated on DPT, Hepatitis A, and tick-borne Encephalitis (TBE).

Getting to Southern Transdanubia: Transportation Options and Logistics

Southern Transdanubia is accessible by several transportation options, including air, road, and rail. Here are some of the best options to consider:

Airports Near Southern Transdanubia

  • Pécs-Pogány International Airport: The international airport is 10km south of Pécs, and it serves regional and international flights.
  • Budapest Ferenc Liszt International Airport: The main international airport serving Hungary, 200km northeast of Pécs.

Bus Stations Near Southern Transdanubia

Most cities and towns in Southern Transdanubia, served by public transport from Pécs.

  • Pécs Bus Station: A bus station located in the city center, with connections to other cities in Hungary and abroad.

Other Options

  • Train: Train services are available between Pecs and Budapest, with connections to international train services.
  • Car Rental: Car rentals are available at airports and train stations, making it easier for visitors to move around the region.
  • Taxi: Taxis are a more expensive option for transportation.

How to Get Around in Southern Transdanubia

One of the best ways to explore Southern Transdanubia is by renting a car, especially if you plan on taking on the scenic routes. But there are also other modes of public transport:

  • Bus: Local buses are available in most cities and towns, connecting visitors to suburban areas.
  • Bicycle: There are several areas and trails perfect for bicycling around the Mecsek Mountains, and bicycling is an excellent way to enjoy the regions’ scenic beauty.

Where to Go Next from Southern Transdanubia: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Here are some of the nearby cities and towns that you can visit after exploring Southern Transdanubia:

  • Budapest: The capital city of Hungary, known for its rich history, culture, and nightlife.
  • Vienna: The capital of Austria, just two hours away by car or train.
  • Zagreb: The capital of Croatia, situated 230 km from Pécs.
  • Belgrade: The capital of Serbia, located 200 km southeast of Pécs.

Conclusion

Southern Transdanubia offers an abundance of activities, sightseeing, natural wonders, and breathtaking landscapes that tourists will thoroughly enjoy. From exploring ancient fortresses like the Castle of Siklós to wandering through picturesque forests like the Mecsek Mountains, there is something for everyone to relish.
