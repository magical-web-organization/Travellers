The Ultimate Travel Guide to Szabolcs-Szatmár-Bereg County, Hungary

Looking for an off-the-beaten-path destination for your next vacation in Europe? Look no further than Szabolcs-Szatmár-Bereg County, located in the Northern Great Plain region of Hungary. This beautiful region is rich in natural beauty and cultural heritage, making it the perfect destination for travelers looking to escape the crowds and explore a lesser-explored corner of Hungary. With this ultimate travel guide to Szabolcs-Szatmár-Bereg County, you’ll be able to plan a memorable trip that covers everything from must-see attractions and outdoor adventures to local cuisine and unique souvenirs to bring back home.

Introduction to Szabolcs-Szatmár-Bereg County

Szabolcs-Szatmár-Bereg County is located in the northeastern part of Hungary, bordering Slovakia and Ukraine. The county’s largest city is Nyíregyháza, which is also the region’s cultural and economic hub. The county is known for its rural landscapes dotted with charming villages, rolling hills, and vast plains. The region has a rich history, and its architecture reflects the different periods of its past, making it an ideal destination for history lovers.

List of Places to Visit in Szabolcs-Szatmár-Bereg County

Whether you’re interested in exploring the county’s natural beauty or its cultural heritage, Szabolcs-Szatmár-Bereg County has something for everyone. Check out some of the must-visit places in the county:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Nyíregyháza Zoo: One of the most popular attractions in the county, the Nyíregyháza Zoo is home to over 500 species of animals from all over the world. The zoo is also a center for research and education, making it a great destination for families with kids.
  • Nyíregyháza Castle: A beautiful castle built in the 18th century, the Nyíregyháza Castle is home to the county’s museum, which houses a fascinating collection of artifacts from the region’s history, including antique furniture, paintings, and ceramics.
  • Tokaj Wine Region: A UNESCO World Heritage Site, the Tokaj Wine Region is known for its sweet white wines that have been produced here for over 1,000 years. The region is home to several vineyards and wineries that offer tours and tastings of this unique wine.

Natural Attractions of Szabolcs-Szatmár-Bereg County: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Hortobágy National Park: The largest protected area in Hungary, the Hortobágy National Park is known for its unique landscapes and biodiversity. Visitors can hike or bike along the park’s trails and observe the local flora and fauna, including rare bird species.
  • Tiszavirág Bridge: One of the most picturesque spots in the county, the Tiszavirág Bridge is a suspension bridge that spans the Tisza River. The bridge is especially stunning during sunrise and sunset, offering visitors amazing photo opportunities.
  • Lake Tisza: A large artificial lake created by damming the Tisza River, Lake Tisza offers visitors several options for water activities, including swimming, fishing, and boating. The lake is also surrounded by a beautiful natural reserve that is home to several species of birds.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Szabolcs-Szatmár-Bereg County

Szabolcs-Szatmár-Bereg County is full of scenic hiking and biking trails that allow visitors to explore its natural beauty up close. Here are some of the best routes to try out:

  • The Blue Trail: A long-distance hiking trail that passes through several counties in Hungary, including Szabolcs-Szatmár-Bereg. The trail offers hikers the chance to explore the county’s diverse landscapes, including hills, valleys, and forests.
  • Tokaj Wine Tour Bike Trail: A scenic bike trail that runs through the Tokaj Wine Region, the Tokaj Wine Tour Bike Trail offers visitors the chance to pedal through vineyards and scenic villages, while also stopping to taste the local wine at wineries along the way.
  • Hortobágy National Park Bike Trail: A circular bike trail that takes visitors through the heart of the Hortobágy National Park, offering breathtaking views of its unique landscapes and wildlife.

Best Things to Do in Szabolcs-Szatmár-Bereg County: Activities and Tours

Whether you’re in the mood for a relaxing day at the spa or an adrenaline-pumping adventure, Szabolcs-Szatmár-Bereg County has plenty of activities and tours to choose from. Here are some of the best:

  • Horseback Riding: The county is known for its horse breeding traditions, and visitors can experience this aspect of local culture by taking a horseback riding tour through the countryside.
  • Thermal Spas: The county is home to several thermal spas, including the Nyíregyháza Thermal Spa and the Zsóry Thermal Spa. These spas offer visitors the chance to relax in mineral-rich waters and enjoy a range of spa treatments.
  • Canoeing: The Tisza River and Lake Tisza offer excellent opportunities for canoeing, whether you’re looking for a leisurely paddle or a more challenging adventure.

Week-Long Itinerary for Szabolcs-Szatmár-Bereg County

Here’s a sample week-long itinerary for visitors to Szabolcs-Szatmár-Bereg County:

Day 1-2: Explore Nyíregyháza, including the Nyíregyháza Castle and Zoo, and discover the local food scene.

Day 3-4: Discover the natural beauty of the Hortobágy National Park, including hiking or biking along the park’s trails and observing the local wildlife.

Day 5-6: Visit the Tokaj Wine Region to learn about its wine-making traditions and sample some of its unique sweet wines.

Day 7: Spend a relaxing day at one of the county’s thermal spas before departing.

Food and Drink Scene of Szabolcs-Szatmár-Bereg County

Hungarian cuisine is known for its hearty meat dishes, flavorful soups, and sweet pastries, and Szabolcs-Szatmár-Bereg County is no exception. Here are some of the must-try dishes and local beverages to sample during your visit:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Goulash: A classic Hungarian beef soup traditionally made with paprika, onions, and potatoes.
  • Stuffed Cabbage: A dish made from cabbage leaves stuffed with ground pork, rice, and spices.
  • Lángos: A deep-fried dough topped with garlic, sour cream, and grated cheese.

Local Beverages

  • Tokaji Wine: A sweet white wine made from grapes grown in the Tokaj Wine Region.
  • Pálinka: A fruit brandy traditionally made from fruits like plums or apricots.
