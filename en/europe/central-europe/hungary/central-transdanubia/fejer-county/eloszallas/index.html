Introduction

Előszállás is a charming town located in Central Transdanubia, Hungary, offering a unique blend of historical landmarks, outdoor adventures, and local culture. If you are planning a trip to this part of the world, make sure to include Előszállás in your itinerary. In this travel guide, we will take a closer look at the top attractions, things to do, food, and more.

List of Places to Visit in Előszállás

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

To get a deeper understanding of the local culture and history, visit these culturally important attractions:

  • Előszállás Castle: This 13th-century castle is one of the most popular landmarks in the town. It offers a glimpse into the medieval history of the region.
  • Museum of Folk Art: The museum displays a wide collection of traditional Hungarian textiles, ceramics, woodcrafts, and metalworks. It’s an excellent place to learn about the local customs and traditions.
  • St. Mary’s Church: This beautiful Baroque-style church dates back to the 18th century and is a must-visit attraction in the town.

Natural Attractions of Előszállás: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Előszállás is known for its natural beauty and offers various outdoor adventures and scenic views for visitors, including:

  • Pannonhalma Hills: This nature reserve is just a short drive away from the town and offers several hiking trails for nature enthusiasts.
  • Dunakanyar: This is the Danube Bend where the river makes a sharp turn, creating some of the most breathtaking views in the region. It’s a popular spot for kayaking and rafting.
  • Ruzsa Island: A small island in the Danube River known for its sandy beaches and excellent swimming spots.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Előszállás

Előszállás and its surroundings offer various hiking and biking routes for travelers, including:

  • Előszállás – Kisbajom Trail: A moderate-level hiking trail that takes you through the picturesque countryside of the region. It’s a great way to experience the natural beauty of Hungary.
  • Csató Stream Trail: This mountain biking trail offers a challenging route for experienced bikers, with breathtaking views of the surrounding hills and valleys.

Best Things to Do in Előszállás: Activities and Tours

Apart from the attractions mentioned above, there are many other things to do in Előszállás, including:

  • Wine Tasting Tour: The region is known for its excellent wines, and many local wineries offer wine tasting tours for visitors.
  • Fishing Tour: The Danube River is home to many fish species, including catfish, bream, and pike. Join a fishing tour and try your luck at catching some fish.
  • Horseback Riding: Experience the beauty of the countryside while riding on horseback. Many local companies offer horseback riding tours.

Week-Long Itinerary for Előszállás

Here’s a suggested week-long itinerary for Előszállás:

Day 1: Visit Előszállás Castle and Museum of Folk Art, take a stroll in the town center, and dine at a local restaurant.

Day 2: Hike in the Pannonhalma Hills and enjoy the beautiful scenery.

Day 3: Visit St. Mary’s Church and take a guided tour of the town’s historical landmarks.

Day 4: Take a wine tasting tour in one of the local wineries and enjoy some local food.

Day 5: Go fishing in the Danube River and enjoy the tranquil surroundings.

Day 6: Horseback riding tour in the countryside.

Day 7: Relax on Ruzsa Island’s beaches and enjoy the beautiful views of the river.

Food and Drink Scene of Előszállás

Must-Try Dishes

Hungarian cuisine is known for its hearty, flavorful dishes. Here are some must-try dishes in Előszállás:

  • Goulash: This traditional Hungarian dish is a hearty, meaty stew with paprika and vegetables. It’s a staple dish in the region.
  • Stuffed Cabbage: Another traditional dish made with pork and rice, rolled in cabbage leaves and cooked in a flavorful tomato-based sauce.
  • Dobos Torte: A Hungarian dessert made with layers of sponge cake and chocolate buttercream, topped with caramel.

Local Beverages

When it comes to beverages, Hungary is known for its excellent wines and beers. Here are some local beverages to try:

  • Tokaji Wine: This sweet wine is known as the “king of wines” and is made from grapes grown in the Tokaj region of Hungary.
  • Unicum: This is a traditional Hungarian herbal liqueur made from a secret recipe of more than 40 herbs and spices.
  • Pálinka: A fruit brandy made from various fruits, including plum, apple, and pear.

Where to Stay in Előszállás: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Előszállás offers several options for accommodation, including:

  • Széchenyi Inn: This cozy inn is located in the town center and offers comfortable rooms and excellent food.
  • Kutya Guesthouse: This rustic guesthouse is located on the outskirts of the town, offering beautiful views of the countryside.
  • Hotel Csalogány: This hotel is located in the nearby town of Tatabánya and offers comfortable rooms and excellent amenities.

What to Buy in Előszállás: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

If you are looking for souvenirs or local products to bring home, here are some things to buy in Előszállás:

  • Handcrafted Woodwork: Browse the local markets and shops to find beautiful woodwork, including wooden bowls, plates, and toys.
  • Embroidery: Hungarian embroidery is famous for its intricate designs and vibrant colors. You can find beautiful embroidered textiles in the local markets.
  • Wine: Előszállás and the surrounding regions produce high-quality wines. Bring home a bottle or two to enjoy later.

Local Culture and Customs of Előszállás

Hungary has a rich cultural heritage, and Előszállás is no exception. Here are some local customs and traditions to keep in mind:

  • Greetings: When greeting someone, it’s customary to shake hands and then say, “Jó napot” (good day) or “Szia” (hello).
  • Dining Etiquette: Wait for the host to invite you to sit down and start eating. Do not begin eating before the host, and don’t leave any food on your plate.
  • Festivals and Celebrations: Hungary has many festivals and celebrations throughout the year, including the Busójárás carnival, the Szeged Open Air Festival, and the Rosalia Wine Festival.

Best Time to Visit Előszállás

The best time to visit Előszállás is during the spring (March to May) and fall (September to November) when the weather is mild, and the crowds are smaller. Summer (June to August) is also a good time to visit, but it can be crowded and hot.

Do’s and Don’ts of Előszállás

Do:

  • Learn a few Hungarian phrases: Hungarian is the official language, but English is widely spoken. It’s polite to learn a few basic phrases like “Köszönöm” (thank you) and “Bocsánat” (excuse me).
  • Try the local food and drinks: Hungarian cuisine is delicious, and there are many local dishes and beverages to try.
  • Respect the local culture and customs: Be mindful of the local culture and customs and respect them.

Don’t:

  • Be disrespectful: Avoid being disrespectful or rude to the locals.
  • Waste food: It’s considered impolite to leave food on your plate or waste food.
  • Expect everything to be the same as at home: Remember that you are visiting a foreign country, and things may be different from what you are used to.

Safety and Health

Safety in Előszállás

Előszállás is a safe town, but it’s always good to take some precautions, including:

  • Be aware of your surroundings: Keep an eye on your surroundings and avoid walking alone at night.
  • Protect your valuables: Keep your valuables like cash, passport, and electronics safe and secure.
  • Take care when driving: Hungary has different traffic rules from other countries, so be careful when driving.

Health in Előszállás

Hungary has a good healthcare system, and Előszállás has several medical facilities, including:

  • Előszállás Health Center
  • Tatabánya Hospital

It’s advisable to have travel insurance that covers medical emergencies and repatriation.

Getting to Előszállás: Transportation Options and Logistics

Előszállás is well-connected by various transportation options, including:

  • Airports Near Előszállás: Budapest Ferenc Liszt International Airport is the closest airport, located about 90 km away from the town.
  • Bus Stations Near Előszállás: Tatabánya has a central bus station, with connections to several major cities in the region.
  • Other Options: You can also reach Előszállás by train or car.

How to Get Around in Előszállás

Előszállás is a small town that can easily be explored on foot. Biking or renting a car is also an option.

Where to Go Next from Előszállás: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

If you have some extra time, here are some nearby destinations and excursions to consider:

  • Pannonhalma Abbey: This Benedictine monastery is a UNESCO World Heritage Site and is located just a short drive away from Előszállás.
  • Győr: This beautiful baroque-style town is located about 60 km away from Előszállás and offers many historical landmarks and attractions.
  • Lake Balaton: One of the most popular tourist destinations in Hungary, Lake Balaton offers many activities and attractions like boating, fishing, and hot springs.
