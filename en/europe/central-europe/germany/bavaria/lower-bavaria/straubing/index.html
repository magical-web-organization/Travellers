Introduction to Straubing

Located in Lower Bavaria, Straubing is a charming town situated in the heart of Germany. Founded by the Romans in 16 BC, Straubing boasts a rich history and culture, and its cobbled streets, baroque buildings, and medieval architecture make it a must-visit destination for history buffs and culture enthusiasts. Besides its historical significance, Straubing is also famous for its scenic beauty and outdoor adventures. From cycling the Danube bike path to hiking in the Bavarian Forest, Straubing is a paradise for nature lovers.

List of Places to Visit in Straubing

Straubing is a small town with a range of attractions to suit all tastes. Here are some of the must-visit destinations in Straubing:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  1. Gäubodenmuseum - The Gäubodenmuseum is one of the top-rated museums in Straubing. Located in the heart of the town, it is home to a range of exhibits showcasing the region’s cultural heritage, including artifacts from the prehistoric era to the Middle Ages.
  2. Stadtmuseum Straubing - Another top-rated museum in Straubing, the Stadtmuseum houses a variety of exhibits that provide visitors with insight into the town’s rich history and culture.
  3. Basilica of St. Jacob - This stunning basilica is one of the most important landmarks in Straubing. With its Gothic architecture and beautiful stained-glass windows, it is a must-visit destination for history and architecture enthusiasts.
  4. Roman Museum Straubing - As Straubing was founded by the Romans, it has a fascinating history that is showcased in the Roman Museum. Visitors can see a variety of artifacts from the Roman era, including pottery, jewelry, and tools.
  5. Theresientor - A medieval gatehouse, the Theresientor is a popular tourist spot that provides visitors with a glimpse into Straubing’s past.

Natural Attractions of Straubing: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  1. Danube Bike Path - Straubing is located along the Danube, which means tourists can enjoy cycling along the Danube bike path. This scenic route provides cyclists with breathtaking views of the Danube and is an excellent way to explore the town’s beauty.
  2. Bavarian Forest National Park - Located a short distance from Straubing, the Bavarian Forest National Park is a haven for outdoor enthusiasts. Visitors can enjoy hiking, biking, and picnicking on the park’s trails while experiencing the region’s natural beauty.
  3. Churpfalzpark Loifling - This beautiful park is an excellent destination for families and features idyllic gardens, playgrounds, and a range of thrilling rides.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Straubing

Straubing is a paradise for outdoor enthusiasts, with a variety of hiking and biking routes to explore. Here are some of our top picks:

  1. Straubing to Bogenberger Wald - A 16-kilometer hike, this route takes hikers through the stunning Bogenberger forest and provides stunning views of the region’s natural beauty.
  2. Laber Valley Cycle Route - With its breathtaking views of the Laber Valley, this 25-kilometer route is an excellent way to explore Straubing’s natural beauty.
  3. Gäubodenbike - This 50-kilometer bike path through the rolling hills of the Gäuboden region is perfect for advanced cyclists looking to challenge themselves.

Best Things to Do in Straubing: Activities and Tours

In addition to its natural beauty and cultural attractions, Straubing also offers a range of tours and activities:

  1. Belles and Whistles Historical Tour - This walking tour takes visitors through the heart of Straubing’s Old Town to explore the town’s rich history and culture.
  2. Brewery Tour - Beer enthusiasts will enjoy a tour of the local breweries.
  3. Wine Tasting Tour - Visitors can sample the region’s finest wines on this guided tour through the vineyards of Lower Bavaria.
  4. Day Trip to Regensburg - Located an hour’s drive from Straubing, Regensburg is a UNESCO world heritage site and a must-visit destination for culture lovers.

Week-Long Itinerary for Straubing

Day 1: Arrive in Straubing and explore the town’s Old Town district and the Gäubodenmuseum.

Day 2: Take a day trip to the quaint town of Regensburg, explore the town’s medieval architecture, and visit St. Peter’s Cathedral.

Day 3: Go hiking in the Bavarian Forest National Park and explore the unique flora and fauna of the region.

Day 4: Enjoy a day of cycling along the Danube bike path, taking in the stunning views of the river and surrounding hills.

Day 5: Take a wine tasting tour through the vineyards of Lower Bavaria and sample some of the region’s finest wines.

Day 6: Explore Straubing’s natural beauty on the Churpfalzpark Loifling and enjoy thrilling rides and idyllic gardens.

Day 7: Spend a day exploring the town’s cultural attractions, including the Stadtmuseum Straubing and Basilica of St. Jacob.

Food and Drink Scene of Straubing

The food and drink scene of Straubing is heavily influenced by traditional Bavarian cuisine. Here are some of the must-try dishes and local beverages in Straubing:

Must-Try Dishes

  1. Schweinshaxe - A traditional Bavarian dish, this roasted pork knuckle is crispy on the outside and tender on the inside.
  2. Brezen - Brezen, or pretzels, are a staple of Bavarian cuisine and are served with a variety of dishes.
  3. Leberknödelsuppe - A savory liver dumpling soup that is the perfect comfort food on a cold day.
  4. Obatzda - A soft and creamy cheese spread that pairs perfectly with Brezen.

Local Beverages

  1. Weißbier - A light and refreshing wheat beer that is a staple of Bavarian cuisine.
  2. Schnapps - A strong alcoholic beverage made from fruit that is traditionally served after dinner.
  3. Glühwein - A mulled wine that is perfect for warming up on a cold winter’s day.

Where to Stay in Straubing: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Straubing offers a range of accommodation options to suit all budgets. Here are some of our top picks:

  1. Gäubodenhof - A family-run guest house located in the heart of Straubing’s Old Town, Gäubodenhof offers comfortable, traditional accommodation at an affordable price.
  2. Hotel Theresientor - With its central location and modern design, Hotel Theresientor is the perfect choice for visitors looking for a more luxurious stay.
  3. Jugendherberge Straubing - This youth hostel is perfect for budget-conscious travelers and offers a range of room types, from shared dormitories to private rooms.

What to Buy in Straubing: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Straubing is known for its traditional handicrafts and local products. Here are some of the must-buy souvenirs and local products to bring home:

  1. Bavarian Glassware - Bavaria is known for its high-quality glassware, and visitors to Straubing can find a range of beautiful glasses and hand-blown glass ornaments.
  2. Lebkuchen - A traditional German gingerbread, Straubing’s Lebkuchen is a delicious treat that makes the perfect souvenir.
  3. Handicrafts - Visitors to Straubing can find a range of traditional handicrafts, including handmade pottery, wooden toys, and embroidered linens.

Local Culture and Customs of Straubing

Straubing has a rich cultural heritage, and visitors should respect local customs and traditions. Here are some of the local customs and traditions to be aware of:

  • Germans are punctual, so arrive on time for appointments and meetings.
  • Dress conservatively when visiting churches and other religious sites.
  • Tipping is common in restaurants and bars.

Best Time to Visit Straubing

The best time to visit Straubing is in the spring or autumn, when the weather is mild, and tourists can enjoy Straubing’s natural beauty and cultural attractions without the crowds.

Do’s and Don’ts of Straubing

Do:

  • Try the local cuisine.
  • Respect local customs and traditions.
  • Take care of the environment and adhere to responsible tourism practices.

Don’t:

  • Be late for appointments and meetings.
  • Wear revealing clothing when visiting religious sites.
  • Forget to tip in restaurants and bars.

Safety and Health

Safety in Straubing

Straubing is a safe destination for tourists. However, visitors should take the same precautions they would in any other city, such as being aware of their surroundings, keeping their valuables close, and not walking alone late at night.

Health in Straubing

The standard of healthcare in Straubing is high, and tourists can access medical treatment if needed. Visitors should ensure they have adequate travel insurance before arriving in Straubing.

Getting to Straubing: Transportation Options and Logistics

Straubing is well-connected to other parts of Germany and Europe and is easily accessible by a range of transportation options.

Airports Near Straubing

Munich Airport is the closest airport to Straubing and is located approximately 100 kilometers away. From Munich airport, visitors can take a train or hire a car to reach Straubing.

Bus Stations Near Straubing

Straubing has a central bus station that offers regular services to other destinations in Germany and Europe.

Other Options

Visitors can also reach Straubing by train or car. The nearest train station is Straubing Hauptbahnhof, located in the town center.

How to Get Around in Straubing

The best way to get around Straubing is on foot or by bicycle. The town is small and easy to navigate, and visitors can explore the town’s cobbled streets and historic architecture on foot. Bicycle rentals are also available throughout Straubing.

Where to Go Next from Straubing: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Straubing is located in the heart of Bavaria and is surrounded by other charming towns and cities. Here are some of the nearby destinations and excursions that visitors can take from Straubing:

  1. Regensburg - Just an hour’s drive from Straubing, the medieval town of Regensburg is a UNESCO world heritage site and a must-visit destination for culture lovers.
  2. Munich - The cosmopolitan city of Munich is located just a short distance from Straubing and is known for its beer gardens, cultural attractions, and bustling nightlife.
  3. Salzburg - Located just across the border in Austria, Salzburg is known for its stunning architecture, cultural heritage, and being the birthplace of Mozart.
  4. Passau - Located approximately 40 kilometers from Straubing, Passau is a charming town located at the confluence of three rivers: the Danube, Inn, and Ilz.
