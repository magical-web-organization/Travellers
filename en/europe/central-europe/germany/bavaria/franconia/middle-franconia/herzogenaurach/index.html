Introduction to Herzogenaurach

Located in the region of Franconia, in Bavaria, Germany, Herzogenaurach is a small but charming town full of history and natural beauty. Also known as “Herzo,” the town is home to several multinational corporations, including Adidas and Puma. Despite its industrial prominence, the town is surrounded by lush green forests and offers numerous outdoor activities such as hiking and biking.

List of Places to Visit in Herzogenaurach

Here are some of the top places to visit during your trip to Herzogenaurach:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Herzogenaurach Local History Museum: Located in the heart of the old town, the museum showcases the history and culture of Herzogenaurach.

  • St. Magdalena Church: This historic church dates back to the 12th century and features beautiful frescoes and Gothic architecture.

  • Adidas and Puma Headquarters: Be sure to visit the headquarters of the world-famous sports brands, Adidas and Puma, which were both founded in Herzogenaurach.

Natural Attractions of Herzogenaurach: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Hesselberg Mountain: This 689m-high mountain offers stunning views of Herzogenaurach and the surrounding areas. Visitors can hike or bike up the mountain and enjoy a picnic at the top.

  • Aurach River: Take a leisurely stroll or a peaceful bike ride along the Aurach River, which runs through the town and offers a picturesque view.

  • Nature Reserve Wiesengrund: This nature reserve is home to several rare flora and fauna species and is an excellent spot for bird watching and nature hikes.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Herzogenaurach

Herzogenaurach is an outdoor lover’s paradise, with plenty of hiking and biking trails to explore. Here are some of the best routes to take:

  • Hesselberg-Höhenweg Trail: This 18 km circular route takes you around Hesselberg mountain and offers stunning views of the surrounding landscape.

  • Aurach Valley Cycle Path: This easy 14 km route takes you along the Aurach River and through the scenic Franconian countryside.

  • Steinberg Circular Hike: This 7.5 km hike takes you through the beautiful forests and meadows surrounding Herzogenaurach and leads to the historical Steinberg Castle ruins.

Best Things to Do in Herzogenaurach: Activities and Tours

  • Herzogenaurach Brewery Tour: Take a tour of the historic Herzogenauracher Brauhaus brewery and sample some of their locally brewed beers.

  • Parachuting in Herzogenaurach: Experience a thrilling tandem skydiving jump over the beautiful Franconian countryside.

  • Herzogenaurach Segway Tour: Explore the town in a fun and unique way by taking a Segway tour.

Week-Long Itinerary for Herzogenaurach

Here is an itinerary you can follow to make the most of your week-long stay in Herzogenaurach:

  • Day 1: Explore the old town and visit St. Magdalena Church and the Herzogenaurach Local History Museum.
  • Day 2: Hike up Hesselberg Mountain and enjoy a picnic at the top.
  • Day 3: Take a bike ride along the Aurach River and through the Franconian countryside.
  • Day 4: Visit the Adidas and Puma headquarters and learn about the history of the two world-famous sports brands.
  • Day 5: Take a tour of the Herzogenauracher Brauhaus brewery and sample some of their locally brewed beers.
  • Day 6: Experience a tandem skydiving jump over the Franconian countryside.
  • Day 7: Relax and unwind at one of the town’s spas or take a leisurely walk through the nature reserve Wiesengrund.

Food and Drink Scene of Herzogenaurach

Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages to sample during your stay in Herzogenaurach:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Schäufele: This traditional Franconian dish features slow-roasted pork shoulder with crispy crackling and is often served with sauerkraut and dumplings.

  • Nürnberger Rostbratwürste: These small, grilled pork sausages are a Franconian specialty and are often served with sauerkraut and potatoes.

  • Krenfleisch: This hearty meat dish features boiled beef and vegetables served with horseradish sauce.

Local Beverages

  • Herzogenauracher Brauhaus beer: This locally brewed beer is made with high-quality hops and malt and has a distinct and refreshing taste.

  • Frankenwein: Franconian wine is known for its exceptional quality and flavor, and there are several local wineries that offer tastings and tours.

What to Buy in Herzogenaurach: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Local Culture and Customs of Herzogenaurach

Herzogenaurach is a friendly and welcoming town, and visitors are encouraged to respect local customs and traditions. Here are some tips to keep in mind:

  • Greet locals with a friendly “Grüß Gott” or “Guten Tag”.
  • Dress appropriately when visiting churches or other places of worship.
  • Be mindful of noise levels, particularly in residential areas.

Best Time to Visit Herzogenaurach

The best time to visit Herzogenaurach is during the spring and summer months (April-September) when temperatures are mild, and the weather is ideal for outdoor activities.

Do’s and Don’ts of Herzogenaurach

Do:

  • Take advantage of the town’s beautiful natural surroundings by hiking or biking through the scenic countryside.
  • Explore the town’s rich history and culture by visiting museums and other cultural attractions.
  • Sample the delicious local food and drink, including Franconian wine and beer.

Don’t:

  • Make excessive noise, particularly in residential areas.
  • Dress inappropriately when visiting places of worship.
  • Litter or damage the natural environment.

Safety and Health

Safety in Herzogenaurach

Herzogenaurach is a safe and welcoming town, but visitors should still take precautions to ensure their safety. Here are some tips:

  • Keep valuables out of sight and secure, particularly when in crowded areas.
  • Stay away from unlit or isolated areas at night.

Health in Herzogenaurach

Herzogenaurach has excellent healthcare facilities, including several modern hospitals and clinics. Visitors are encouraged to purchase travel insurance before their trip to ensure that they are covered in the event of a medical emergency.

Where to Go Next from Herzogenaurach: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Here are some nearby destinations and excursions to consider when planning your trip from Herzogenaurach:

  • Nuremberg: This historic city is located approximately 20 km from Herzogenaurach and offers a wealth of cultural and historical attractions.

  • Bamberg: This medieval city is located approximately 80 km from Herzogenaurach and is known for its historic architecture and world-renowned beer.

  • Franconian Switzerland: This stunning natural region is located approximately 50 km from Herzogenaurach and offers dramatic rock formations, deep valleys, and dense forests.

Conclusion

Herzogenaurach offers a unique blend of natural beauty, history, and modern industry that makes it an excellent destination for visitors of all ages and interests. Whether you’re interested in exploring the town’s rich culture, hiking in the surrounding countryside, or relaxing with a beer at a cozy local pub, Herzogenaurach is sure to provide an unforgettable experience.
