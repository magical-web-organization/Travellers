Introduction to Spessart

Spessart is a forested mountain range located in Lower Franconia, Franconia, in Bavaria, Germany. With an area of 2,440 square kilometers, this region is an ideal travel destination for nature lovers and adventure seekers. Known for its beautiful landscapes, charming small villages, and cultural heritage, Spessart offers a lot to see and do. Whether you enjoy hiking, biking, or simply exploring the local cuisine and culture, Spessart has something for you. In this comprehensive tour guide, we will explore the best places to visit, hiking and biking routes, accommodations, food and drink, and more.

List of Places to Visit in Spessart

Exploring Spessart will immerse you in natural scenery and culture. Check out below for some beautiful places to visit in Spessart.

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Mespelbrunn Castle: A beautiful water castle which appeared as a location in the film “The Spessart Inn.”
  • Aschaffenburg: A picturesque town with stunning gardens, museums, historical places, and a vibrant nightlife scene.
  • Würzburg: A charming Bavarian town, known for its baroque architecture, wine, and Unesco World Heritage Site, the Würzburg Residence.

Natural Attractions of Spessart: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Spessart Forest: In Spessart, you will find the largest contiguous forest in Germany, with varied landscapes stunning views, hiking, and bike trails.
  • Lohr a.M.: This is a town famous for its half-timbered houses and the beautiful River Main.
  • Mainleus: A paradise for cyclists, with various cycling routes, and one of the top destinations in Spessart for people who love nature.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Spessart

The landscapes of Spessart offer an incredible setting for hiking and biking activities. The Spessart Region has a vast network of hiking and biking paths with several options for every level of experience.

Some of the popular hiking trails are:

  • Five Valleys Trail: A 60 km long hiking trail that traverses through five valleys
  • Spessart Höhenweg: This trail offers ample views to admire, making the hike worth it.
  • Bad Orb Salt Trail: A famous hiking trail due to its salt works, and it is relatively easy.

Moreover, there are several mountain-bike trails here, including the “Spessart-Mountainbike-Runde” with a stone garden section, and the “Heigenbrücken” for bike lovers.

Best Things to Do in Spessart: Activities and Tours

  • Wine Tasting in Würzburg and Aschaffenburg: Wine lovers can enjoy wine tasting in Franconia’s vineyards and witness the art of winemaking in the local wineries.
  • Exploring the water Castle Mespelbrunn
  • River Cruises in the River Main: An opportunity to discover historic locations while sailing and taking in the fantastic views.
  • Climbing the Forest Adventure Park: Found in Schöllkrippen, with varied climbing routes with climbing the massive trees, flying foxes and more

Food and Drink Scene of Spessart

The food and drink scene in Spessart is a unique experience, making it a must-visit region for foodies. The local cuisine here reflects Franconian traditions in a unique way. You will notice that Franconia has several unique delicacies to offer.

Must-Try Dishes

  • Schäufele: Roast pork shoulder a Bavarian delicacy
  • Bratwurst: A Bavarian sausage made with local pork meat
  • Leberknödel: A rich, liver dumpling soup, and an age-old favorite of the Franconian cuisine.

Local Beverages

No trip to Spessart can be complete without a taste of the locally brewed beer. Apart from beer, there is the Franconian wine, which is unique in Germany. You can also find several local distilleries that produce them.

Where to Stay in Spessart: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Spessart has plenty of options to stay for tourists to stay. Some of the popular accommodations options in Spessart are:

  • Fair Hotel: Located in the Aschaffenburg district, which offers several room options that can suit every budget.
  • Landhotel Zum Hirschen: A family-friendly hotel located in the Graefenberg district, which offers an excellent hospitality rooming experience.
  • Burg Rothenfels: Located in Rothenfels, it is a castle-turned-hostel, which can be an excellent option for adventure-seekers.

What to Buy in Spessart: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Spessart has a wide range of local products that can take home as souvenirs that reflect the uniqueness of the region. Some of them are:

  • Franconian Wine
  • Handcrafted wooden items
  • Franconian Stone Beer Mugs
  • Local Distilled Beverages from Distilleries in and around the region.

Local Culture and Customs of Spessart

Spessart pays great attention to local customs and traditions. Franconian culture is one that is recognized worldwide as a great cultural experience, German-style. Enjoy their festivals, crafts, and culinary traditions. Moreover, the culture of Spessart will vary according to the geography.

Best Time to Visit Spessart

Spessart is open all year-round with remarkable activities and attractions available all year long. However, the best time to visit Spessart will depend on what you plan to do and see during your visit. Spring and Summer offer audiences opportunity to take an outdoor adventure and enjoy local specialties. Autumn on the other hand offers excellent mushrooming opportunities. Winter brings skiing and snowboarding activities in the region and the famous Franconian Christmas Markets.

Do’s and Don’ts of Spessart

Do:

  • Respect local customs and traditions.
  • Be polite and courteous.
  • Try local cuisine and beverages.
  • Take care of the natural environment

Don’t:

  • Don’t be loud and disrespectful.
  • Don’t litter and damage the natural environment.
  • Don’t disregard traffic laws and hiking safety guidelines.

Safety and Health

Safety in Spessart

Spessart is a safe region with a low crime rate. It is very safe to explore the area and enjoy the sights. However, like any other place, it is important to be vigilant of petty harms such as pickpockets and traffic.

Health in Spessart

The medical facilities across Spessart are excellent, with clinics and hospitals that you can access to in case of a medical emergency. Foreign visitors are required to have travel insurance covering healthcare before entering Germany.

Getting to Spessart: Transportation Options and Logistics

Spessart is situated in an easy location to get to, with different transportation options.

Airports Near Spessart

  • Frankfurt Airport: The fourth largest airport connecting to most parts of Europe and the world.
  • Nurnberg Albrecht Dürer Airport: Connecting to a few European and international cities.

Bus Stations Near Spessart

There are limited bus stations in the region, but the large ones include Frankfurt Bus Station and Aschaffenburg Bus Station.

Other Options

If you plan to tour Spessart, a rental car may be an excellent option to explore the region.

How to Get Around in Spessart

Transportation options for getting around within Spessart include trains, buses, taxis, and cycling. Most areas of Spessart are accessible from Aschaffenburg by public transport.

Where to Go Next from Spessart: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

There are many excursion sites near Spessart, including:

  • Frankfurt: A thriving economy, a city of finance, excellent museums, and plenty of activities.
  • Heidelberg: A city full of historical landmarks like The Heidelberg Castle and The Philosopher’s Path.
  • Rothenburg ob der Tauber: A beautiful medieval town with half-timbered houses and a thriving Christmas market in winter

Conclusion

Spessart is an outdoor enthusiast’s paradise with stunning natural attractions and rich cultural heritage. Whether you are keen on hiking or biking routes, enjoying local cuisine and beverages or exploring the various museums and historical places, Spessart has it all. Plan your next visit, and we are confident that you will fall in love with the region.
