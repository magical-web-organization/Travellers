Introduction to Mödlareuth

Nestled in the Upper Franconia region of Franconia in Bavaria, Germany, Mödlareuth is a quaint village with a rich history dating back to the Cold War era. The village sits right on the border between East and West Germany, with a wall separating the two sides, which has been preserved to this day as a symbol of Germany’s reunification. Mödlareuth offers an enriching cultural experience, with fascinating museums, historical monuments, natural attractions, hiking trails, and much more.

List of Places to Visit in Mödlareuth

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Mödlareuth Museum The Mödlareuth Museum highlights the history of the village and the division of Germany. Visitors can see the preserved wall that divided the village and explore various exhibitions, including photographs, videos, and archaeological discoveries from the Cold War era.

  • St. Katherine’s Church Built-in 1748, St. Katherine’s Church is one of the oldest buildings in Mödlareuth. The church’s beautiful baroque architecture and unique frescoes make it a must-visit destination for history and art enthusiasts.

Natural Attractions of Mödlareuth: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Landscape Garden This beautiful garden is a perfect spot for a relaxed stroll and a picnic. The garden features exotic plant species, a pond, and stunning views of the surrounding countryside.

  • Thuringian Slate Mountains The Thuringian Slate Mountains are a paradise for nature lovers looking for some adventure. The mountains offer several hiking trails, mountain biking routes, and rock climbing areas that offer breathtaking views of the surrounding nature.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Mödlareuth

  • The Wall Trail The Wall Trail is a scenic hiking trail that follows the original path of the wall that once separated the village. The trail is perfect for history enthusiasts, offering several historical sites and landmarks that offer insights into the Cold War era.

  • Mödlareuther Rundweg Mödlareuther Rundweg is a circular hiking trail that takes you through the beautiful countryside surrounding Mödlareuth. The trail offers stunning views of the Thuringian Slate Mountains, lush forests, and several historical sites.

Best Things to Do in Mödlareuth: Activities and Tours

  • The Cold War Tour The Cold War Tour is a guided tour that takes you through the heart of the village, exploring the history of the division of Germany. The tour includes visits to several historical sites, including the wall, the Mödlareuth Museum, and the St. Katherine’s Church.

  • The Bike Tour The Bike Tour is perfect for adventurous visitors looking to explore the beautiful countryside surrounding Mödlareuth. The tour takes you through several scenic routes, including the Thuringian Slate Mountains and the Landscape Garden.

Food and Drink Scene of Mödlareuth

Must-Try Dishes

  • Bratwurst A classic German dish loved by locals and visitors alike, the Mödlareuth bratwurst is a must-try dish. It is usually served with potato salad, sauerkraut, and mustard.

  • Schnitzel Schnitzel is a breaded cutlet served with fries or potato salad. It is a popular dish in Bavaria, and Mödlareuth has several restaurants that serve delicious schnitzel.

Local Beverages

  • Prickelnde Prickelnde is a locally brewed sparkling wine that is a perfect refreshment on a hot summer day.

  • Heller Bock Heller Bock is a strong, malt-forward beer that is popular in Bavaria and is perfect for those who love their beer.

Where to Stay in Mödlareuth: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Gasthof zum Taufstein This family-run guesthouse offers comfortable accommodations with a cozy atmosphere and an excellent traditional Bavarian restaurant.

  • Hotel Pension Munsch Located in the heart of the village, Hotel Pension Munsch offers spacious rooms and a restaurant serving regional cuisine.

What to Buy in Mödlareuth: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Thuringian Slate The Thuringian Slate Mountains are an abundant source of gorgeous natural slate, used worldwide for roofing and construction. You can buy souvenirs made of slate, such as coasters, key chains, and trivets.

Local Culture and Customs of Mödlareuth

Mödlareuth is deeply rooted in the tradition of Bavarian folk art and crafts. Visitors can buy traditional Bavarian clothing and visit local artisan stores for unique souvenirs.

Best Time to Visit Mödlareuth

The best time to visit Mödlareuth is from May to September, as the weather is pleasant and perfect for outdoor activities.

Getting to Mödlareuth: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Mödlareuth

The closest airport to Mödlareuth is Hof-Plauen Airport, which is about 50 km from the village. Alternatively, visitors can fly into the Nuremberg Airport which is about 150 km from Mödlareuth.

Bus Stations Near Mödlareuth

Regular bus services operate from nearby cities and towns of Bavaria to Mödlareuth.

Other Options

Visitors can also rent a car and drive to Mödlareuth from nearby cities or take a train to Hof and then take a bus to Mödlareuth.
