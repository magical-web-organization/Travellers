Introduction to Oberammergau

Nestled in the foothills of the Bavarian Alps, surrounded by lush forests and rolling green hills, Oberammergau is an idyllic destination for travelers seeking to experience Bavaria’s rich culture and natural beauty. The town’s picturesque chalets, colorful frescoes, and ornate wood carvings showcase its deep reverence for Bavarian traditions. And the town’s history is equally fascinating, with tales of plague and triumph woven into its tapestry.

List of Places to Visit in Oberammergau

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Oberammergau is a treasure trove of museums and historical places, taking visitors on a journey through the town’s past. Here are our top picks:

  • Passion Play Theatre: The town’s iconic theater brings to life the Passion Play, a theatrical tradition that dates back to the 17th century.

  • Pilatushaus Museum: This museum specializes in woodcarving, a quintessential Oberammergau tradition.

  • Wieskirche: A UNESCO World Heritage Site, this church is renowned for its stunning Rococo architecture and breathtaking beauty.

Natural Attractions of Oberammergau: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Oberammergau’s natural beauty is breathtaking, with a wealth of outdoor adventures and scenic views to explore. Here are our top picks:

  • Laber: Take a cable car up the mountain to enjoy spectacular views of the Alps.

  • Kofel: Stunning views await adventurous hikers who ascend this peak.

  • Ettal Abbey: This stunning abbey offers visitors a chance to engage in spiritual reflection while enjoying the surrounding nature.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Oberammergau

Oberammergau boasts a plethora of hiking and biking routes, catering to both novices and experienced outdoor enthusiasts. Here are our top recommendations:

  • Laber Circuit: This popular hiking route combines stunning views of the Alps with a refreshing alpine lake swim.

  • Kofel Peak Trail: An adventurous hike that rewards those who undertake it with stunning panoramic views of Oberammergau and the surrounding area.

  • The Flying Fox Path: This biking route takes you through the beautiful Ammergau Alps with the added thrill of a zip line.

Best Things to Do in Oberammergau: Activities and Tours

In addition to hiking, biking, and sightseeing, Oberammergau offers a range of activities and tours for visitors to enjoy. Here are our top picks:

  • Oberammergau Passion Play: This 6-hour theatrical performance is a must-see for anyone visiting Oberammergau.

  • Sightseeing Tours: Take a guided tour of the town’s historic landmarks, including St. Peter and Paul Church, or explore the surrounding countryside on a day trip to the Bavarian Alps.

  • Rafting and Canyoning: The Ammer River offers a chance to experience thrilling rapids and beautiful landscapes.

Week-Long Itinerary for Oberammergau

To make the most of your trip to Oberammergau, we recommend the following week-long itinerary:

  • Day 1-2: Settle in and explore the town’s historic landmarks.

  • Day 3: Take a day trip to the nearby Ettal Monastery and enjoy the beautiful surrounding countryside.

  • Day 4-5: Experience outdoor adventures, either biking or hiking through the scenic trails.

  • Day 6: Attend the Oberammergau Passion Play.

  • Day 7: Relax and enjoy your last day, perhaps by visiting a local restaurant and sipping a cold beer while enjoying the scenery.

Food and Drink Scene of Oberammergau

Oberammergau’s food and drink scene is a reflection of its rich Bavarian heritage. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Schweinshaxe: Hearty roasted pork knuckle served with mashed potatoes and sauerkraut.

  • Kaiserschmarrn: A delightful dessert made from chopped pancakes, raisins, and apples, topped with sugar and served with fruit compote.

  • Leberknödel Suppe: A flavorful soup featuring hearty liver dumplings.

Local Beverages

  • Bavarian Beer: Germany is known for its excellent beer, and Oberammergau is no exception. You can find a range of local brews available, including the famous Hofbräuhaus beer.

  • Schnapps and Liqueurs: Oberammergau has a long tradition of distilling schnapps and liqueurs made from local fruits such as cherries and plums.

Where to Stay in Oberammergau: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Oberammergau has a range of accommodations to fit any budget. Here are some of our top recommendations:

  • Hotel Alte Post: This hotel features Bavarian-style decor and stunning mountain views.

  • Gasthof Zur Rose: This charming guesthouse is the perfect place to experience the town’s traditional woodcarving culture.

  • Jugendherberge Oberammergau: This hostel offers affordable accommodations, including breakfast and dinner, and is an excellent choice for those looking to socialize and make new friends.

What to Buy in Oberammergau: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Oberammergau has a rich cultural and artistic heritage, and this is reflected in the beautiful souvenirs and local products available for sale. Here are some of our recommendations:

  • Woodcarvings: Oberammergau is known for its intricate and beautifully carved wooden statues, crosses, and handicrafts.

  • Lüftlmalerei: These colorful frescoes adorn many of the buildings in town and make for a unique and vibrant souvenir.

  • Local Spirits: Homemade schnapps and liqueurs make for a delicious and highly authentic souvenir from your trip to Oberammergau.

Local Culture and Customs of Oberammergau

Oberammergau is a place deeply rooted in Bavarian culture, and there are many customs and traditions that visitors should be aware of. Some of these include:

  • Dress Code: Traditional Bavarian attire, including dirndls and lederhosen, is often worn by locals, especially at special events like the Passion Play.

  • Greetings: Bavarians tend to be more formal in their greetings, with handshakes and nods being more common than hugs or kisses on the cheek.

Best Time to Visit Oberammergau

Oberammergau is beautiful year-round, but the best time to visit depends on your interests and preferences. Summer is ideal for outdoor activities, while winter offers the chance to enjoy snow sports and Christmas markets.

Do’s and Don’ts of Oberammergau

Do:

  • Respect Local Customs: Take time to learn about local customs and traditions.

  • Be Polite: Mit Bitte (please) and Danke (thank you) are common phrases in Bavaria and will be appreciated by locals.

Don’t:

  • Assume English is Spoken Everywhere: While English is often spoken fluently, not everyone will speak or understand it.

  • Disrespect Religious Sites: Churches and religious artifacts are an important part of life in Oberammergau, and visitors should be respectful of these places.

Safety and Health

Safety in Oberammergau

Oberammergau is a safe town, with low crime rates and a welcoming atmosphere. However, it’s always essential to take precautions when traveling to a new destination, such as staying aware of your surroundings and keeping an eye on your belongings.

Health in Oberammergau

Bavaria has excellent medical facilities, including hospitals and clinics. Visitors should ensure they have adequate travel insurance before arriving and consider taking any necessary medications and vaccines.

Getting to Oberammergau: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Oberammergau

  • Munich Airport: This airport is the closest major airport, located approximately 90km away from Oberammergau.

Bus Stations Near Oberammergau

  • Oberammergau Train Station: The town has a train station with connections to the wider Bavarian region.

Other Options

  • Rental Cars: Renting a car is an excellent option for travelers looking to explore the surrounding countryside.

How to Get Around in Oberammergau

The town is small and highly walkable, with bike rentals and taxis also available for visitors. Hiking trails connect the town to nearby sites of interest.

Where to Go Next from Oberammergau: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Oberammergau is ideally located for exploring the wider Bavarian region, with numerous destinations worth a visit. Here are some of our top recommendations:

  • Munich: Bavaria’s capital city is a must-see for anyone visiting the region, with stunning architecture, world-class museums, and excellent food and drink.

  • Neuschwanstein Castle: This iconic castle is a popular day trip from Oberammergau for its stunning architecture and beautiful scenery.

Conclusion

Oberammergau is a charming town with a deep cultural heritage, stunning natural beauty, and a warm and welcoming atmosphere. Whether you’re interested in adventure sports or cultural experiences, there’s something to suit everyone in this stunning destination.
