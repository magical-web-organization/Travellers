Introduction to Tegernsee

Tegernsee is a picturesque town located in Upper Bavaria, in the southern region of Germany. Situated in the heart of the Alps, the town boasts stunning mountain views and is home to Lake Tegernsee, one of the most beautiful lakes in the Bavarian region. Tegernsee has been a popular destination for travelers for centuries, with many coming to enjoy the natural beauty, rich history, and vibrant culture of the town.

List of Places to Visit in Tegernsee

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Tegernsee Abbey: Founded in the 8th century, the abbey is a stunning example of Baroque architecture and is home to a collection of art and historical artifacts.
  • Museum Tegernseer Tal: This museum showcases the history and culture of the Tegernsee region, including exhibits on traditional crafts, local customs, and folklore.
  • Olaf Gulbransson Museum: This museum is dedicated to the work of Norwegian artist Olaf Gulbransson, who lived in Tegernsee for many years and is famous for his satirical illustrations.

Natural Attractions of Tegernsee: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Lake Tegernsee: With crystal clear water and stunning mountain views, this lake is the perfect spot for swimming, boating, and relaxing.
  • Wallberg Mountain: Hiking to the top of this mountain rewards hikers with stunning views of the surrounding countryside.
  • Neureuth Mountain: This mountain offers challenging hiking trails and a breathtaking view of Lake Tegernsee from the summit.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Tegernsee

Tegernsee is a paradise for hikers and bikers, with a variety of routes to suit all skill levels. Some popular routes include:

  • Tegernsee-Runde: A 27-kilometer bike ride that circles the lake and takes in stunning views of the surrounding mountains.
  • Riederstein: A moderate hike that offers panoramic views of the lake and neighboring towns.
  • Roßsteinalm: A more challenging hike that takes hikers to the top of the Roßstein mountain and offers breathtaking views of the countryside.

Best Things to Do in Tegernsee: Activities and Tours

  • Beer Tasting Tour: Tegernsee is home to several breweries, and a beer tasting tour is a great way to discover the local brews.
  • Boat Tour of Lake Tegernsee: With stunning mountain views and crystal clear water, a boat tour of Lake Tegernsee is a must-do activity.
  • Paragliding: For the adventurous traveler, paragliding offers a unique perspective of the beautiful countryside.

Week-Long Itinerary for Tegernsee

Day 1: Explore the town center and visit the Tegernsee Abbey.

Day 2: Hike to the top of the Wallberg Mountain and take in the stunning views of the surrounding countryside.

Day 3: Take a boat tour of Lake Tegernsee and enjoy a beer tasting at one of the local breweries.

Day 4: Visit the Museum Tegernseer Tal and learn about the history and culture of the region.

Day 5: Hike to the top of the Neureuth Mountain and enjoy the breathtaking view of the lake from the summit.

Day 6: Take a paragliding lesson and experience the thrill of soaring over the mountains.

Day 7: Relax by the lake and take in the scenic views of the beautiful countryside.

Food and Drink Scene of Tegernsee

Must-Try Dishes

  • Weisswurst mit Brez’n: A traditional Bavarian dish of white sausage and pretzel.
  • Schweinshaxe: A hearty dish of roasted pork knuckle.
  • Apfelstrudel: A delicious apple pastry that is a staple of Bavarian cuisine.

Local Beverages

  • Tegernsee Bier: A local brew that is a popular favorite among residents and visitors alike.
  • Bavarian Schnapps: A distilled spirit made from a variety of fruits and herbs.
  • Glühwein: A hot, spiced wine that is popular during the holiday season.

Where to Stay in Tegernsee: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Hotel Bachmair Weissach: A luxurious hotel located on the shores of Lake Tegernsee.
  • Gasthof zur Post: A charming guesthouse in the center of town, offering comfortable rooms and a cozy atmosphere.
  • Hostel Tegernsee: A budget-friendly option that offers clean and comfortable accommodations for backpackers and budget travelers.

What to Buy in Tegernsee: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Bavarian Lederhosen: Traditional Bavarian leather pants that are a popular souvenir among visitors.
  • Beer Steins: Ceramic beer mugs that are a staple of Bavarian culture.
  • Traditional Bavarian Clothing: Dirndl dresses for women and lederhosen for men are traditional clothing items that make for great souvenirs.

Local Culture and Customs of Tegernsee

Tegernsee is a town steeped in tradition and Bavarian culture. Travelers should be sure to try the local food and drink, including the famous Tegernsee Bier. Visitors should also be respectful of local customs, including dress code and punctuality.

Best Time to Visit Tegernsee

The best time to visit Tegernsee is in the summer months, from June to September, when the weather is warm and pleasant. Winter sports enthusiasts can visit in the winter months for skiing and snowboarding in the surrounding mountains.

Do’s and Don’ts of Tegernsee

Do:

  • Try the local food and drink, including Tegernsee Bier and Bavarian cuisine.
  • Explore the beautiful natural scenery and outdoor activities, including hiking and swimming.
  • Be respectful of local customs and traditions.

Don’t:

  • Wear inappropriate clothing, such as shorts and flip-flops, when visiting cultural attractions.
  • Be late for appointments or meetings, as punctuality is highly valued in Bavarian culture.
  • Disrespect local customs, particularly when it comes to conservative dress and public behavior.

Safety and Health

Safety in Tegernsee

Tegernsee is a safe and welcoming town, and travelers should not encounter any major safety concerns. However, as with any destination, travelers should be aware of their surroundings and take normal precautions to ensure their safety.

Health in Tegernsee

Travelers to Tegernsee should ensure that they are up to date on routine vaccinations, including measles, mumps, and rubella, and should also consider getting vaccinated against hepatitis A, as food and water can be a potential source of infection.

Getting to Tegernsee: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Tegernsee

The closest airport to Tegernsee is Munich Airport, which is approximately 70 kilometers away. From Munich Airport, travelers can take a train or a rental car to Tegernsee.

Bus Stations Near Tegernsee

Buses run regularly from Munich to Tegernsee, with several departures throughout the day.

Other Options

Travelers can also drive to Tegernsee, with several major highways connecting the town to the rest of Germany and Europe.

How to Get Around in Tegernsee

Tegernsee is a small town that is easy to navigate on foot or by bike. Buses run regularly throughout the town and surrounding countryside, and rental cars are also available for travelers who prefer to drive.

Where to Go Next from Tegernsee: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Munich: Bavaria’s vibrant capital city is just a short drive or train ride away from Tegernsee.
  • Salzburg: The picturesque Austrian city of Salzburg is located just across the border from Germany and is a popular day trip destination.
  • Innsbruck: The charming Alpine town of Innsbruck is just a short drive from Tegernsee and offers stunning mountain views and cultural attractions.

Conclusion

Tegernsee is a hidden gem in the Bavarian Alps, offering beautiful scenery, rich history, and a vibrant cultural scene. With its stunning lake, challenging hikes, and delicious cuisine, Tegernsee is a must-visit destination for any traveler looking to experience the best of Bavarian culture and outdoor adventure.
