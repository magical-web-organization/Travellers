Nestled in the heart of Upper Bavaria, Spitzingsee is a hidden gem that offers a perfect escape from the hustle and bustle of city life. Introduction to Spitzingsee

Spitzingsee is a small village located in the Bavarian Alps, roughly 50 km south of Munich. The village sits at an elevation of 1084 m above sea level, and is home to the beautiful Spitzingsee lake. The village is a popular destination for winter sports, but also attracts visitors during the summer months for its scenic beauty and outdoor activities.

List of Places to Visit in Spitzingsee

There are plenty of attractions to see and things to do in Spitzingsee. Here’s a list of the top places to visit:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Markus Wasmeier Freilichtmuseum - an open-air museum showcasing Bavarian life and culture
  • Schliersee Historic Museum - a museum displaying the history of the Schliersee region

Natural Attractions of Spitzingsee: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Spitzingsee Lake - a beautiful crystal-clear lake, perfect for swimming, boating, and fishing
  • Taubenstein Mountain - offers breathtaking views of the surrounding landscape
  • Valepp Valley - a picturesque valley offering hiking and biking trails

Hiking/Biking Routes of Spitzingsee

Spitzingsee is home to several hiking and biking trails suitable for beginners and pros alike. Here are some of the best routes:

  • Taubenstein Circuit Trail - a challenging route with stunning views of the valley
  • Rotwand Trail - a moderate-level hike to the Rotwand peak
  • Spitzingsee Round Trail - an easy-level hike around the lake

Best Things to Do in Spitzingsee: Activities and Tours

Spitzingsee offers many activities and tours to keep you busy during your stay. Here are some of the best things to do:

  • Skiing and snowboarding - with over 20 km of pistes, Spitzingsee is a popular winter sports destination
  • Stand-up paddleboarding - explore the lake on a Stand-up Paddleboard (SUP)
  • Paragliding - enjoy the stunning views of the Alps from above

Food and Drink Scene of Spitzingsee

Bavarian cuisine is famous for its hearty dishes and local beers. Here are some of the must-try dishes and local beverages in Spitzingsee:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Schweinshaxe - a roasted pork knuckle served with sauerkraut and bread
  • Kaiserschmarrn - a sweet dish made of shredded pancakes
  • Obatzda - a cheese spread served with bread

Local Beverages

  • Weissbier - a light, wheat-based beer
  • Radler - a beer mixed with lemonade for a refreshing drink
  • Schnapps - a strong distilled fruit liquor

Local Culture and Customs of Spitzingsee

Bavarian culture is rich in customs and traditions, some of which can still be seen in Spitzingsee. Here are a few things to keep in mind:

  • Bavarians are proud of their culture and language, so try to learn a few basic phrases in German
  • Beer and wine are a big part of Bavarian culture, but excessive drinking is frowned upon
  • Sundays are considered a day of rest, so most shops and businesses are closed

Best Time to Visit Spitzingsee

The best time to visit Spitzingsee depends on what you’re looking for. Winter sports enthusiasts should visit between December and March, while those who want to enjoy the outdoors and hiking should come between May and October.
