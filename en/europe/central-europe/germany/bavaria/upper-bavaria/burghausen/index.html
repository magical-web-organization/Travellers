Introduction to Burghausen

Burghausen is a picturesque town situated in Upper Bavaria, in Germany, in Central Europe. Known for its stunning castle, town squares with baroque architecture, and cobblestone streets, Burghausen has been a popular travel destination for locals and tourists alike. It is known for its cultural importance, natural beauty, and rich history. This travel guide aims to provide you with all the necessary information to plan a memorable trip to Burghausen, including places to visit, outdoor activities, hiking/biking routes, food and drink scene, where to stay, and more.

List of Places to Visit in Burghausen

Burghausen has plenty of culturally important attractions, museums, historical places, natural attractions, outdoor adventures, and scenic views that are worth exploring. Here are some of the must-visit places in Burghausen:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Burghausen Castle: The main tourist attraction in Burghausen is the 1,000-year-old Burghausen Castle, which is Europe’s longest castle complex. Visitors can take a guided tour to learn about the castle’s history and architecture, or just wander through the castle’s many courtyards and terraces.

  • Stadtmuseum Burghausen: This museum offers a glimpse into Burghausen’s history and culture through its exhibits, including pottery and old textiles from the Middle Ages, paintings from the 16th to 19th centuries, and sculptures from the 20th century.

  • Kapuzinerkloster Burghausen: This cloister is an impressive architectural masterpiece with beautiful frescoes and a colorful history.

Natural Attractions of Burghausen: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Tittmoning Castle: Located only 30 minutes away from Burghausen, Tittmoning Castle is a well-preserved medieval castle that has been adapted to modern times for visitors to experience the feel of the Middle Ages.

  • Salzach River: Burghausen is located on the banks of the Salzach River, which is well-known for its picturesque views, and visitors can enjoy a leisurely walk or cycle along the river.

  • Rottal Valley Nature Park: This vast nature reserve, with its unspoiled landscapes, is the perfect place for hikers and nature lovers. You can explore the park on foot, bike or by horse-drawn carriage.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Burghausen

Burghausen offers some of the best hiking and biking routes in the region. Here are some of the popular trails to explore:

  • Burgenweg: Burgenweg is a well-marked hiking trail that takes visitors on a tour of several castles, including Burghausen Castle. The trail offers beautiful views of the surrounding countryside and is highly recommended for nature lovers and photographers.

  • Rottal Cycling Trail: This cycling trail runs for 256 km from Burghausen to Passau and offers beautiful views of the countryside, lakes, and small villages along the route.

Best Things to Do in Burghausen: Activities and Tours

Burghausen offers visitors a variety of activities and tours to choose from. Here are some of the best things to do in Burghausen:

  • Burghausen Castle Tour: Guided tours of Burghausen Castle are available, and visitors can learn about the castle’s history and architecture while enjoying the stunning views from the castle terraces.

  • Outdoor Activities: Visitors can indulge in a variety of outdoor activities such as cycling, hiking, and horseback riding in the beautiful Rottal Valley Nature Park.

  • Beer Tasting Tour: Beer is a big part of the Bavarian culture, and visitors can enjoy a tasting tour of local beers to get a taste of the region’s brewing history.

Week-Long Itinerary for Burghausen

If you have a week to spend in Burghausen, here is a suggested itinerary:

Food and Drink Scene of Burghausen

Burghausen’s food and drink scene offers visitors a taste of Bavarian cuisine and beer culture. Here are some of the must-try dishes and drinks:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Kaiserschmarrn: A traditional sweet dish made from shredded pancakes served with fruit compote.

  • Leberknödelsuppe: A soup made with liver dumplings and vegetables.

  • Schweinshaxe: Roasted pork knuckle served with sauerkraut and potato dumplings.

Local Beverages

  • Bavarian beer: Bavaria is known for its beer culture, and Burghausen has several breweries that visitors can tour and taste the local beer.

  • Apfelschorle: A refreshing drink made with apple juice and sparkling water, perfect for a hot summer day.

Where to Stay in Burghausen: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Burghausen offers a wide range of accommodation options for visitors, including hotels, hostels, and guesthouses. Here are some of the popular options:

  • Hotel Post: This four-star hotel is centrally located and offers comfortable rooms with modern amenities.

  • Stadthotel Schratt: This three-star hotel is located in the heart of the old town and offers rooms with traditional Bavarian decor and an on-site restaurant serving regional cuisine.

  • JUFA Hotel Burghausen: This hostel is located just a few minutes away from Burghausen Castle and offers affordable dormitory rooms and private rooms.

What to Buy in Burghausen: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Burghausen is known for its unique handicrafts, ceramics, and local food products. Here are some of the items you can buy as souvenirs or gifts:

  • Burghausen ceramics: Handmade ceramics with beautiful designs and patterns are popular souvenirs in Burghausen.

  • Bavarian chocolates: Bavarian chocolates are known for their quality and taste, and there are several chocolate shops in Burghausen where visitors can buy handmade chocolates.

Local Culture and Customs of Burghausen

Bavaria has a unique culture and customs, and Burghausen is no exception. Here are some of the local customs and traditions to be aware of:

  • Dirndl and Lederhosen: Traditional Bavarian dress is still worn on special occasions, such as festivals and weddings.

  • Oktoberfest: Bavaria’s most famous festival is Oktoberfest, which is celebrated in Munich but has been replicated in several other locations, including Burghausen.

Best Time to Visit Burghausen

The best time to visit Burghausen is during the summer months from June to August when the weather is warm and sunny. Visitors can enjoy a variety of outdoor activities and attend some of the local festivals.

Safety and Health

Burghausen is a safe and healthy travel destination. However, visitors should take the necessary precautions to ensure their safety and health.

Safety in Burghausen

  • Be aware of your surroundings and avoid carrying large amounts of cash and valuables.
  • Keep your passport and other important documents safe.
  • Follow traffic rules when driving or cycling.

Health in Burghausen

  • Visitors should have a basic first-aid kit with them at all times.
  • Stay hydrated and protect yourself from the sun when participating in outdoor activities.
  • Visitors should have travel insurance to cover any emergencies.

Getting to Burghausen: Transportation Options and Logistics

Burghausen is well-connected by public transport and is easily accessible from nearby airports and cities.

Airports Near Burghausen

  • Munich International Airport: The nearest international airport is Munich Airport, which is about 1.5 hours away from Burghausen by car or train.

  • Salzburg Airport: Salzburg Airport is about 45 minutes away from Burghausen by car or train.

Bus Stations Near Burghausen

  • Burghausen Bus Station: The main bus station in Burghausen is located near the old town and is well-connected to other cities in the region.

Other Options

  • Visitors can also travel to Burghausen by train to the nearest train station, Bahnhof Burghausen.

How to Get Around in Burghausen

Burghausen is a small town, and most of the attractions are within walking distance of each other. Visitors can also explore the town on a bike or take public transport, such as buses or taxis.

Where to Go Next from Burghausen: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Burghausen is located in a beautiful region of Bavaria, and visitors can explore several nearby destinations and excursions, including:

  • Munich: Munich is the capital of Bavaria and is about a 1.5-hour drive from Burghausen. Visitors can explore the city’s beer gardens, museums, parks, and historical sites.

  • Hallstatt: Hallstatt is a picturesque village in Austria known for its beautiful lakes and mountains. It is about a 2-hour drive from Burghausen.

Conclusion

Burghausen is a charming town with a rich history and culture. Whether you’re interested in exploring medieval castles, hiking through unspoiled landscapes, or indulging in Bavarian cuisine and beer culture, Burghausen has something for everyone.
