A Comprehensive Travel Guide to Berchtesgaden National Park

Berchtesgaden National Park is located in Upper Bavaria, in the state of Bavaria, in Germany. The park has stunning natural beauty and is home to a diverse range of flora and fauna, making it a popular destination for nature lovers. To help you plan your trip, here’s a comprehensive travel guide to Berchtesgaden National Park, including everything you need to know about places to stay, things to do, and much more.

Introduction to Berchtesgaden National Park

Berchtesgaden National Park is a protected area in the Bavarian Alps, close to the Austrian border. The park encompasses an area of 210 square kilometers and was established in 1978 to protect the unique flora and fauna of the region. It is also home to the Watzmann, the third-highest mountain in Germany.

List of Places to Visit in Berchtesgaden National Park

There are many spectacular places to visit in Berchtesgaden National Park. Here are some of the most popular:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • The Eagle’s Nest: This historical site was a gift from the Nazi party to Adolf Hitler and is situated at a height of 1,834 meters. Today, it is open to the public and offers stunning views of the surrounding mountains.
  • The Berchtesgaden Salt Mine: Visitors can explore this fascinating underground salt mine, which has been in operation for over 500 years. The tour includes a ride on a mining train and a boat ride on an underground lake.
  • Dokumentation Obersalzberg: This museum documents the history of the region during the Nazi era and is located in the former Nazi Guest House in Obersalzberg.

Natural Attractions of Berchtesgaden National Park: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Königssee: This picturesque lake is surrounded by mountains and is one of the most scenic spots in Berchtesgaden National Park.
  • Jennerbahn: This cable car takes visitors to the summit of Jenner Mountain, where they can enjoy panoramic views of the surrounding landscape.
  • Wimbachklamm: This striking gorge is located near Ramsau and is a popular hiking spot with waterfalls and stunning views.
  • The Berchtesgaden National Park Information Centre: Visitors can learn about the flora and fauna of the park and pick up maps and information about hiking trails.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Berchtesgaden National Park

Berchtesgaden National Park is home to many hiking and biking trails, ranging from easy walks to challenging mountain climbs. Here are some of the most popular:

  • The Almbachklamm Gorge: A beautiful hiking trail that takes visitors through a narrow gorge with waterfalls and stunning scenery.
  • The Jenner Summit: This hike takes visitors to the summit of Jenner Mountain, where they can enjoy panoramic views of the surrounding landscape.
  • The Kehlstein-Bahn Trail: This hiking trail takes visitors to the Eagle’s Nest and offers fantastic views of the surrounding mountains.

Best Things to Do in Berchtesgaden National Park: Activities and Tours

There are many activities and tours to enjoy in Berchtesgaden National Park. Here are some of the best:

  • Paragliding: Visitors can take a tandem paragliding flight and enjoy stunning views of the park from above.
  • Boat Tours: Visitors can take a boat tour of Königssee and enjoy the stunning views of the surrounding mountains.
  • Guided Hikes: There are many guided hikes available in the park, ranging from easy walks to challenging mountain climbs.

Week-Long Itinerary for Berchtesgaden National Park

For visitors who want to spend more time in the park, here’s a suggested week-long itinerary:

  • Day 1-2: Explore the town of Berchtesgaden and visit the local historical sites, such as the Eagle’s Nest and the Berchtesgaden Salt Mine.
  • Day 3-4: Take in the stunning views of Königssee and explore the hiking trails in the area.
  • Day 5-6: Explore the Wimbachklamm gorge and take a cable car ride to the summit of Jenner Mountain.
  • Day 7: Relax and enjoy the scenery before departing for your next destination.

Food and Drink Scene of Berchtesgaden National Park

Berchtesgaden National Park is home to many delicious local dishes and beverages. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Schweinshaxe: Roasted pork knuckle.
  • Kaiserschmarrn: Sweet shredded pancake.
  • Weisswurst: A traditional Bavarian sausage usually eaten for breakfast or brunch.

Local Beverages

  • Weißbier: Wheat beer.
  • Glühwein: Mulled wine.
  • Obstler: Fruit brandy.

Where to Stay in Berchtesgaden National Park: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

There are many accommodation options available in Berchtesgaden National Park, ranging from budget-friendly hostels to luxury hotels. Here are some top choices:

  • Kempinski Hotel Berchtesgaden: This luxurious hotel offers stunning views of the surrounding mountains and a wide range of amenities.
  • Berghotel Rehlegg: This hotel offers cozy rooms and fantastic views of the surrounding landscape.
  • Haus Bergfrieden: This family-oriented guesthouse offers comfortable rooms and easy access to local hiking trails.

What to Buy in Berchtesgaden National Park: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

There are many great souvenirs and local products available in Berchtesgaden National Park. Here are some top picks:

  • Local Cheese: Berchtesgaden is known for its delicious local cheese, which makes a great gift to bring home.
  • Traditional Clothing: Visitors can find traditional Bavarian clothing, such as lederhosen and dirndls, at local shops.
  • Hand-carved Wooden Figurines: This traditional craft is popular in the region, and visitors can find unique wooden figurines to bring home as souvenirs.

Local Culture and Customs of Berchtesgaden National Park

Berchtesgaden National Park is steeped in local culture and traditions. Some customs to keep in mind include:

  • Dressing Appropriately: Visitors should dress modestly when visiting churches, and swimming in natural bodies of water is generally not allowed.
  • Table Manners: When dining at local restaurants, it is common to share tables with strangers and to toast before drinking.
  • Unique Festivals: The region has many unique festivals throughout the year, including the Herbstfest and the Oktoberfest.

Best Time to Visit Berchtesgaden National Park

The best time to visit Berchtesgaden National Park is during the summer months, from June to September when the weather is mild, and the hiking trails are open. The fall months are also a beautiful time to visit, with colorful foliage and fewer crowds. Winter sports enthusiasts should visit from December to March.

Do’s and Don’ts of Berchtesgaden National Park

Here are some do’s and don’ts to keep in mind when visiting Berchtesgaden National Park:

Do:

  • Take your time to explore the hiking trails.
  • Respect the local customs and traditions.
  • Try the local food and beverages.

Don’t:

  • Litter in the park.
  • Ignore safety rules when hiking.
  • Dress inappropriately when visiting churches.

Safety and Health

Safety in Berchtesgaden National Park

To ensure your safety while visiting Berchtesgaden National Park, here are some important safety tips to keep in mind:

  • Follow the marked hiking trails and stay on the path.
  • Be aware of weather conditions and changes.
  • Respect any wildlife you encounter and keep a safe distance.

Health in Berchtesgaden National Park

There are no specific health risks associated with Berchtesgaden National Park, but visitors should take the following precautions:

  • Wear appropriate clothing and footwear when hiking.
  • Stay hydrated and carry water with you at all times.
  • Be aware of any allergies you may have when trying new foods.

Getting to Berchtesgaden National Park: Transportation Options and Logistics

Berchtesgaden National Park is easily accessible by car, bus or train. Here are some transportation options:

Airports Near Berchtesgaden National Park

The closest international airport is Munich Airport. From there, visitors can take a train to Berchtesgaden.

Bus Stations Near Berchtesgaden National Park

There are regular bus services to Berchtesgaden from Salzburg, Austria, and Munich, Germany.

Other Options

Visitors can also rent a car to drive to Berchtesgaden National Park, and there are shuttle services available from the airport to Berchtesgaden.

How to Get Around in Berchtesgaden National Park

The best way to get around in Berchtesgaden National Park is by car or public transportation. The local bus system is reliable and connects many of the popular attractions in the area.

Where to Go Next from Berchtesgaden National Park: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

There are many nearby destinations and excursions from Berchtesgaden National Park. Here are some top choices:

  • Salzburg, Austria: This beautiful city is just a short drive from Berchtesgaden and is home to many historical sites and cultural attractions.
  • Chiemsee Lake: This stunning lake is just a short drive from Berchtesgaden and is a popular destination for swimming and boating.
  • Munich, Germany: This vibrant city is just a few hours’ drive from Berchtesgaden and is home to many museums, art galleries, and cultural attractions.

Conclusion

Berchtesgaden National Park is a stunning destination for nature lovers and history buffs alike. With its beautiful scenery and rich cultural heritage, there are endless activities and attractions to enjoy in this unique destination.
