Introduction to Eichstätt

Located in Upper Bavaria, Germany, Eichstätt is a small but charming town known for its historical significance and natural beauty. With its medieval architecture, cobblestone streets, and vibrant cultural scene, Eichstätt is a must-visit destination in Germany. This guide will provide you with all the information you need to plan your trip to Eichstätt, from the best places to visit to the top activities and tours to do, and much more.

List of Places to Visit in Eichstätt

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Willibaldsburg Castle: This 14th-century castle now houses the Eichstätt Museum, showcasing the town’s history and culture, including archaeology, geology, and art collections.

  • Dom St. Peter: The town’s most famous landmark is this grand cathedral with a beautiful interior decorated with frescoes.

  • Altmühl Valley Nature Park: This park is situated in the heart of Eichstätt and is perfect for those who love hiking, biking, and exploring the great outdoors.

Natural Attractions of Eichstätt: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Jura-Adventure Trail: This trail is the perfect way to experience the natural beauty of Eichstätt and the surrounding areas.

  • The Eichstätt Fossil Museum: The museum features a variety of fossils from the region and is a great attraction for everyone, especially those who are interested in geology.

  • Ludwig-Maximilians-University Munich Botanic Garden in Eichstätt: This beautiful garden is the perfect place to spend a relaxing afternoon enjoying the scenery.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Eichstätt

Eichstätt is famous for its incredible hiking and biking trails that offer breathtaking views of the surrounding countryside. One of the top-rated routes is Jura-Adventure Trail, which is approximately 14 km long and starts at the Eichstätt railway station. The trail takes you through scenic views of the Franconian Jura, including steep cliffs and lush greenery, dotted with traditional Bavarian houses.

Best Things to Do in Eichstätt: Activities and Tours

  • Visit Dom St. Peter: The historic cathedral is a must-visit for any traveler looking to experience the traditional Bavarian culture.

  • Willibaldsburg Castle Museum: Visit the castle to learn about the town’s history and see incredible art and historic artifacts.

  • Explore the Jura-Adventure Trail on foot or by bike: The trail is considered the best way to experience the stunning natural beauty of Eichstätt and the surrounding areas.

Week-Long Itinerary for Eichstätt

Here is an approximate itinerary for a week-long stay in Eichstätt:

  • Day 1: Arrive in Eichstätt and explore the town.

  • Day 2: Visit the Altmühl Valley Nature Park and enjoy a scenic hike along the trail.

  • Day 3: Explore the Ludwig-Maximilians-University Munich Botanic Garden in Eichstätt.

  • Day 4: Check out the Eichstätt Fossil Museum and the stunning fossils from the region.

  • Day 5: Visit the Willibaldsburg Castle Museum and learn the history and culture of the town.

  • Day 6: Take a day trip to the nearby city of Nuremberg and explore its beautiful sights.

  • Day 7: Visit the beautiful Dom St. Peter cathedral and take in the stunning architecture and frescoes.

Food and Drink Scene of Eichstätt

Must-Try Dishes

  • Schweinshaxe: A Bavarian delicacy, this traditional dish features roasted pork knuckles served with sauerkraut and potato dumplings.

  • Weißwurst: This traditional Bavarian sausage is typically eaten for breakfast with sweet mustard, pretzels, and a cold beer.

  • Leberknödelsuppe: A hearty soup made with liver dumplings, vegetables, and broth that’s sure to warm you up on a chilly day.

Local Beverages

  • Bier: Bavarian beer is some of the best in the world, and Eichstätt has some great local breweries where you can sample a wide variety of delicious beers.

  • Wein: Bavaria might not be as famous for wine as other European regions, but Eichstätt has a growing wine scene, and you can sample some great local wines while you are here.

Where to Stay in Eichstätt: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Eichstätt is a small town but offers a great selection of accommodations for visitors. Here are some of the best options:

  • Hotel & Gasthof zur Brücke: A charming family-run hotel located in the heart of the town.

  • Hotel Adlerbräu: A historic hotel with a cozy atmosphere and excellent service.

  • Gasthof Krone: A traditional Bavarian-style guesthouse offering comfortable accommodations and great food.

What to Buy in Eichstätt: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Eichstätt Beer: Take home a six-pack of the town’s famous beer as a souvenir.

  • Fossils: The town’s famous fossil museum sells a variety of items related to fossils, including postcards, replicas, and souvenirs.

Local Culture and Customs of Eichstätt

  • Bavarians are incredibly proud of their traditions, and Eichstätt is no different. Visitors should be respectful of these traditions and try to learn about them during their stay.

Best Time to Visit Eichstätt

The best time to visit Eichstätt is in the summer months when the weather is pleasant. However, the town has its charm throughout the year, and visitors can enjoy the stunning scenery of the town whenever they visit.

Do’s and Don’ts of Eichstätt

Safety and Health

Safety in Eichstätt

Eichstätt is a safe destination for visitors. However, visitors should take precautions such as keeping an eye on their belongings and not wandering into unfamiliar areas alone.

Health in Eichstätt

There are many hospitals, clinics, and pharmacies in Eichstätt that can provide visitors with necessary medical assistance.

Getting to Eichstätt: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Eichstätt

The nearest airport to Eichstätt is Nuremberg Airport, which is approximately an hour away by car.

Bus Stations Near Eichstätt

Eichstätt has a bus station located in the town center that provides local and regional transport.

Other Options

Visitors can also travel to Eichstätt by train, and the town has a railway station located on the main railway line between Nuremberg and Munich.

How to Get Around in Eichstätt

Visitors can easily explore the town on foot or by bicycle. Buses and taxis are also available to get around the town and neighboring areas.
