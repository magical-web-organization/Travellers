Introduction to Freising

Freising is a charming town located in Upper Bavaria, in the heart of Bavaria, Germany. Known for its magnificent landscapes, historical architecture, and traditional Bavarian customs, Freising is a hidden gem waiting to be explored by travelers.

Visitors to Freising can enjoy a variety of experiences that range from hiking in the nearby hills to exploring museums, delicious Bavarian cuisine, and experiencing the local culture.

In this guide, we’ll cover the best things to do, see, and eat in Freising, as well as information about local culture and customs, transportation options, and nearby excursions.

List of Places to Visit in Freising

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Freisinger Dom (Freising Cathedral): This awe-inspiring structure dates back to the 12th century and boasts stunning baroque architecture. Visitors can take a guided tour and marvel at the cathedral’s impressive interiors.
  • Bishop’s Palace: Home to a museum showcasing local history, artwork and artifacts dating back as early as the Roman era.
  • Weihenstephan Abbey: Founded in 725 AD, Weihenstephan Abbey is home to a brewery, research center, and agriculture facility. Visitors can take tours and beer tasting as well.

Natural Attractions of Freising: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Isar River: A popular destination for fishing, swimming, canoeing, and kayaking.
  • Freisinger Hill: Offers a challenging hiking route to the highest point in Freising three viewpoints presenting a tranquillizing bird-view of the town.
  • Freisinger Moos: A natural reserve located just outside the town provides an opportunity to explore the wetlands and its vibrant biodiversity.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Freising

Freising offers a range of hiking and biking routes that allow visitors to take in the town’s beautiful natural scenery. Here are some of our top picks:

  • Freising - Moosburg Cycling Route: A popular 25 km route that offers scenic views as it passes through several smaller Bavarian towns and stunning landscapes.
  • Weihenstephaner Hill Hike: A two-hour trek to the highest point in Freising offering three viewpoints along the way
  • Isar River Hiking Trail: A route that runs from the mountains to the town, boasting stunning views of the river and its surroundings.

Best Things to Do in Freising: Activities and Tours

  • Beer Tasting and Brewery Tour at Weihenstephan Abbey: Learn about the history of beer production in Bavaria while enjoying crisp, refreshing beers in a picturesque rustic setting.
  • Traditional Bavarian Cooking class: Learn how to prepare Bavarian delicacies like Weiner schnitzel, apple strudel, and spätzle with the help of an experienced local chef.
  • Cultural Walking Tour: Explore the beautiful historical sites the town has to offer and learn about local history and customs.

Week-Long Itinerary for Freising

Day 1 Arrive in Freising and take a guided tour of Freisinger Dom Cathedral.
Day 2 Enjoy a traditional Bavarian Breakfast, followed by a cooking class.
Day 3 Explore Weihenstephan Abbey and take a guided tour of the brewery.
Day 4 Hike up to the highest point in Freising and enjoy the scenic views.
Day 5 Take a cultural walking tour of the town.
Day 6 Go for a swim in the Isar River and enjoy a picnic in the park.
Day 7 Take a day trip to Nuremberg and enjoy shopping and sightseeing.

Food and Drink Scene of Freising

Must-Try Dishes

  • Weisswurst: A traditional Bavarian breakfast dish made with white sausage, sweet mustard, and a soft pretzel.
  • Leberkäse: A hearty meatloaf made with veal and pork, typically served with a slice of bread.
  • Schweinshaxe: A roasted pork knuckle served with sauerkraut and potato dumplings.

Local Beverages

  • Beer: Bavaria is renowned for its beer production and Freising is home to several breweries, including Weihenstephan Abbey.
  • Schnapps: A distilled spirit made from fruit, herbs, or nuts that is popular in Bavaria.
  • Wine: While not as well-known as other wine-producing regions, Bavaria produces some delicious white wines.

Where to Stay in Freising: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Freising boasts a range of accommodation options, from high-end hotels to affordable hostels and guesthouses.

  • Hotel Bayerischer Hof: A luxury hotel located in the heart of Freising, offering spacious rooms and a famous restaurant
  • Corbin Feng Shui Business Hotel: A centrally located hotel that offers rooms with unique Feng Shui features.
  • HI Munich-Park Hostel: A budget-friendly option located just outside of town, with clean and comfortable rooms.

What to Buy in Freising: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Beer Stein: A traditional German beer mug made from ceramic or glass and adorned with intricate designs.
  • Traditional Bavarian Clothing: Lederhosen for men and Dirndl for women are traditional clothing items that can be found in many souvenir shops throughout town.
  • Bavarian Delicacies: Stock up on sweet treats like gingerbread cookies, Christmas stollen, and chocolate-covered marzipan.

Local Culture and Customs of Freising

  • Lederhosen and Dirndl: Traditional Bavarian clothing is still worn during festivals and celebrations. It’s not uncommon to see locals wearing lederhosen or dirndl on a daily basis as well.
  • Fasching: A carnival is an annual tradition in Freising, where locals dress up in costumes and parade through the town streets.
  • Beer Culture: Beer is a significant aspect of Bavarian culture. Many towns host annual beer festivals - most notably the world-famous Oktoberfest.

Best Time to Visit Freising

The Summer (June-August) is the most popular time to visit Freising, as the weather is warm, and many outdoor activities are available. However, the Spring (March - May) and Fall (September - November) have their charm. In Spring, the cherry blossoms and magnolias in the cathedral garden bloom at its best while autumn brings in a unique experience of wine harvest and colorful leaves.

Do’s and Don’ts of Freising

Do:

  • Learn some basic German phrases. Many locals might not speak English, and it’s always polite to attempt their language.
  • Respect the local culture and customs.
  • Be punctual and prompt.

Don’t:

  • Don’t litter in public places or parks.
  • Don’t be too loud in public spaces, particularly in residential zones at night.
  • Don’t jaywalk, always use the designated crossing paths.

Safety and Health

Safety in Freising

Freising is generally considered a safe town with a very low crime rate, but as with any destination, visitors should exercise caution and take sensible measures to keep themselves and their belongings safe.

Health in Freising

There are no significant health risks associated with visiting Freising. Drink plenty of water throughout the day, wear sunscreen, and use insect repellant when going outdoors.

Getting to Freising: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Freising

The Munich Airport is the nearest airport to Freising, located just 15 minutes away. Several airlines fly to and from Munich, making it convenient for international travelers.

Bus Stations Near Freising

There is a regular bus service available from Munich Centre to Freising that runs daily, taking roughly 40 minutes.

Other Options

Travelers may also consider renting a car or booking a private transfer service.

How to Get Around in Freising

Walking is the best way to explore the town’s many sights and experiences. Bikes can also be rented from several locations in town.

Where to Go Next from Freising: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Nearby destinations include:

  • Munich (25 km): Bavaria’s capital city offers an array of cultural and historical experiences.
  • Nuremberg (165km): Germany’s medieval town attracts tourists with its historic architecture and museums.
  • Regensburg (116 km): Known for its well-preserved medieval town center, Regensburg offers plenty of dining and historic architecture.

Conclusion

Freising is a beautiful and peaceful town, with a rich history and culture that will delight travelers of all ages. Take time to explore the town’s sights and sounds, and experience all the beauty Bavaria has to offer. With this comprehensive guide, we hope you’ll be well-equipped to plan your perfect trip to Freising.
