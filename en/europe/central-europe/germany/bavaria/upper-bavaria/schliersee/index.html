Introduction to Schliersee

Schliersee is a picturesque town located in Upper Bavaria, in the heart of Germany. This charming destination is surrounded by the stunning Bavarian Alps, providing visitors with breathtaking views and endless outdoor activities. Whether you are interested in hiking, skiing, or just relaxing, Schliersee has something for everyone.

Visitors to Schliersee will enjoy exploring the town’s rich history and culture, as well as indulging in delicious Bavarian cuisine and local beer. With so much to see and do, it’s no wonder that Schliersee is becoming an increasingly popular vacation spot.

List of Places to Visit in Schliersee

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • The Schliersee Museum showcases the history and culture of the town and surrounding area. Visitors can learn about the town’s origins, traditional crafts, and the local economy.
  • The St. Sixtus Church is a beautiful Baroque-style church that dates back to the 18th century. The church houses numerous works of art and is a must-see for history and architecture buffs.
  • The Markus Wasmeier Freilichtmuseum is an open-air museum that showcases traditional Bavarian life. Visitors can see how locals lived and worked in the past, and even sample traditional dishes.

Natural Attractions of Schliersee: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • The Schliersee lake is perfect for swimming, paddling, or taking a boat ride. The crystal clear water and stunning mountain backdrop make for an unforgettable experience.
  • The Wendelstein mountain provides visitors with amazing views of the surrounding area. Hiking to the top is a challenging but rewarding experience, and visitors can also take a cable car to the summit.
  • The Mangfalltal nature reserve is home to a wide variety of flora and fauna. Visitors can explore the reserve on foot, bike, or horseback and get up close and personal with nature.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Schliersee

  • The Schliersbergalm circular hike is a family-friendly route that offers stunning views of the surrounding mountains and the lake. The trail is approximately 7km long and takes around 2-3 hours to complete.
  • The Spitzingsee circuit is a challenging hike with plenty of elevation gain to tackle. The beautiful views along the way make the effort well worth it.
  • The Chiemsee-Alpenland mountain bike tour is a 42km circular route that begins in Schliersee and ends in Bayrischzell. The route takes visitors through stunning alpine scenery and is suitable for intermediate to advanced riders.

Best Things to Do in Schliersee: Activities and Tours

  • The Schliersee Sommer Festival is a 10-day event that takes place in August and features live music, food and drink vendors, and traditional Bavarian dancing. It’s a great way to experience local culture and immerse yourself in the festive atmosphere.
  • The Christkindlmarkt is a traditional German Christmas market that takes place in December. Visitors can shop for handcrafted gifts, enjoy festive treats, and soak up the holiday cheer.
  • The Schliersee Adventure Trail is a fun family activity that takes visitors through a series of challenges and obstacles. It’s a great way to get outside and enjoy the beautiful scenery while also getting some exercise.

Week-Long Itinerary for Schliersee

Food and Drink Scene of Schliersee

Must-Try Dishes

  • Schweinshaxe (roasted pork knuckle)
  • Obatzda (a spread made from camembert cheese and butter)
  • Brezen (pretzels)
  • Leberkäse (a type of meatloaf)
  • Kaiserschmarrn (a sweet dish made from shredded pancakes)

Local Beverages

  • Schlierseer beer is a must-try for beer enthusiasts. This local brew is made using traditional methods and has a distinct taste.
  • Obstler is a strong fruit brandy that is a popular digestif after a hearty meal.

Where to Stay in Schliersee: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Arabella Alpenhotel am Spitzingsee is a luxury hotel with stunning views of the nearby lake and mountains.
  • Gästehaus Hubertus is a cozy guesthouse located in the heart of town.
  • Jugendherberge Schliersee is a budget-friendly option for travelers looking for affordable accommodation.

What to Buy in Schliersee: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Local beer or schnapps
  • Handcrafted wooden items such as beer steins or figurines
  • Locally made honey and jams
  • Bavarian textiles such as dirndls and lederhosen

Local Culture and Customs of Schliersee

Schliersee and the surrounding Bavarian region are deeply rooted in tradition and customs. Visitors should be respectful of local culture and should consider attending some of the local festivals and events to get a taste of the local customs.

Best Time to Visit Schliersee

The best time to visit Schliersee is during the summer months (June-August) when temperatures are mild and the outdoor activities are in full swing. However, the town is also a popular winter destination for skiing and other winter sports.

Do’s and Don’ts of Schliersee

Do:

  • Do try the local beer and cuisine
  • Do attend one of the town’s festivals or events
  • Do explore the beautiful natural scenery

Don’t:

  • Don’t ignore local customs and traditions
  • Don’t be disrespectful of nature or wildlife
  • Don’t forget to pack appropriate clothing and gear for outdoor activities

Safety and Health

Safety in Schliersee

Schliersee is a safe destination for travelers, but visitors should exercise caution when participating in outdoor activities such as hiking or biking. It’s important to be aware of the weather conditions and to follow local rules and regulations.

Health in Schliersee

It’s important to stay hydrated and protect yourself from the sun when participating in outdoor activities in Schliersee. Visitors should also be aware of ticks, which can transmit diseases such as Lyme disease.

Getting to Schliersee: Transportation Options and Logistics

Schliersee is located approximately 60km south of Munich, making it easily accessible by car or public transportation.

Airports Near Schliersee

  • Munich International Airport is the closest major airport to Schliersee, located approximately 90km away.

Bus Stations Near Schliersee

  • The town has its own bus station, which connects to nearby destinations such as Munich and Salzburg.

Other Options

  • Visitors can also take the train to nearby cities such as Munich or Rosenheim and then transfer to a local bus or taxi to reach Schliersee.

How to Get Around in Schliersee

The town is small enough to explore on foot, but visitors may also choose to rent a bike or other form of transportation for longer excursions.

Where to Go Next from Schliersee: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Munich, the capital of Bavaria, is just a short train ride away and offers visitors plenty of attractions and activities.
  • Salzburg, Austria, is just a few hours’ drive from Schliersee and is a beautiful destination for a day trip.
  • Innsbruck, Austria, is just over an hour’s drive from Schliersee and is a popular destination for winter sports enthusiasts.

Conclusion

Schliersee is a beautiful destination that offers visitors a unique blend of history, culture, and natural beauty. Whether you are interested in outdoor activities, local cuisine, or cultural events, Schliersee has something to offer. Don’t miss the opportunity to explore this stunning region of Bavaria.
