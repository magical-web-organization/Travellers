Introduction to Dachau

Dachau is a small town in Upper Bavaria, Germany, and is most famous for being the location of the Dachau concentration camp during World War II. While this tragic history can be seen in the town’s museums and memorials, there is much more to Dachau than just its past. The town is full of cultural and natural attractions, making it a great getaway for those looking to explore the beautiful region of Bavaria.

List of Places to Visit in Dachau

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Dachau Concentration Camp Memorial Site: A sobering reminder of the atrocities committed during World War II, the memorial site includes a museum, exhibits, and tours of the former camp.
  • Schloss Dachau: This castle was the residence of the Bavarian kings and is now home to the Dachau Palace Museum.
  • Stadtmuseum Dachau: This museum showcases the history and culture of Dachau through exhibits on art, crafts, and daily life.

Natural Attractions of Dachau: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Amper River: Take a leisurely walk along the Amper River, which flows through the town and offers beautiful views of the Bavarian countryside.
  • Karlsfelder See: Located just outside of Dachau, this lake is great for swimming, picnicking, and hiking.
  • English Garden: Take a stroll through this serene garden, complete with winding paths, impressive sculptures, and a cozy beer garden.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Dachau

  • Dachauer Moos: This nature reserve offers several hiking and biking trails through marshes and meadows.
  • Dachauer Schlossberg: This trail takes you up a steep hill to the ruins of the Dachau Castle, offering impressive views of the surrounding area.
  • Amperauen: Follow the Amper River along this hiking trail, passing by fields and forests.

Best Things to Do in Dachau: Activities and Tours

  • Dachau Bike Tours: Explore the town and its surroundings on a guided bike tour, with options for different skill levels.
  • Dachauer Volksfest: This annual festival in August includes carnival rides, live music, and plenty of local food and beer.
  • Dachau Old Town: Stroll through the charming streets of Dachau’s historic district, lined with colorful buildings and quaint shops.

Food and Drink Scene of Dachau

Must-Try Dishes

  • Leberkäse: A Bavarian specialty, this meat dish is similar to bologna and is typically served with a side of potato salad.
  • Weisswurst: A traditional white sausage made with veal and pork, served with sweet mustard and a pretzel.
  • Schweinshaxe: A roasted pork knuckle, often served with sauerkraut and potatoes.

Local Beverages

  • Bavarian beer: The region is known for its beer, with local breweries offering a variety of styles and flavors.
  • Apfelschorle: A refreshing drink made with apple juice and sparkling water.
  • Schnaps: This fruit brandy is a popular after-dinner drink and can be found in many local bars and restaurants.

Where to Stay in Dachau: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Hotel Burgmeier: This family-run hotel is located in the heart of Dachau’s Old Town and offers comfortable rooms and a cozy atmosphere.
  • Bavaria Safari Hotel: This unique hotel is decorated in an African safari theme and offers spacious rooms and a hearty breakfast buffet.
  • Stadt Petershausen: Located just outside of Dachau, this charming guesthouse offers a peaceful countryside retreat with easy access to the town’s attractions.

What to Buy in Dachau: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Dachau Beer Stein: A traditional ceramic beer stein with the town’s crest on it.
  • Bavarian Trachten: Traditional clothing, including dirndls and lederhosen, can be found in many local shops.
  • Obatzda: A delicious spread made from soft cheese, butter, and spices, often served with bread or pretzels.

Local Culture and Customs of Dachau

  • Bavarian traditions: Dachau, like many other towns in the region, has a strong sense of Bavarian culture, with traditional clothing, food, and music being an important part of everyday life.
  • Oktoberfest: This world-famous beer festival takes place in Munich every year in September and October, and is a popular event among locals and visitors alike.
  • Language: While many locals in Dachau speak English, learning a few basic phrases in German can be helpful and appreciated.

Best Time to Visit Dachau

The best time to visit Dachau is during the summer months (June to August), when the weather is warm and sunny and many outdoor activities are available. However, this is also peak tourist season, so expect large crowds and higher prices. Spring and fall are good times to visit as well, with milder weather and fewer tourists. Winter can be cold and snowy, but also offers opportunities for winter sports and festive Christmas markets.

Do’s and Don’ts of Dachau

Do:

  • Respect local customs and traditions, especially when it comes to attire and behavior in public places.
  • Try the local food and drinks, especially the Bavarian beer!
  • Visit historical sites and learn about the town’s past.

Don’t:

  • Don’t litter or damage natural or cultural heritage sites.
  • Don’t be late! Punctuality is important in German culture.
  • Don’t be loud or disruptive in public places.

Safety and Health

Safety in Dachau

Dachau is generally a safe town, with low levels of crime. However, as with any destination, travelers should still take normal precautions such as being aware of their surroundings, not leaving valuables unattended, and avoiding poorly lit or unsafe areas at night.

Health in Dachau

Germany has a high standard of healthcare, with a variety of hospitals and clinics located throughout the country. Visitors should ensure they have adequate health insurance coverage and bring any necessary medications with them on their trip.

Getting to Dachau: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Dachau

The closest airport to Dachau is Munich Airport, which is approximately 30 kilometers away. From the airport, visitors can take a train or bus to Dachau, or rent a car.

Bus Stations Near Dachau

Dachau has a central bus station located in the town center, with connections to surrounding towns and cities.

Other Options

Visitors can also reach Dachau by train, with regular connections from Munich and other Bavarian towns and cities. Those traveling by car can take the A8 or A92 highways to reach Dachau.

How to Get Around in Dachau

Dachau is a relatively small town and can easily be explored on foot or by bike. Public transportation is also available, with a bus system that connects to surrounding towns and cities.

Where to Go Next from Dachau: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Munich: Germany’s third-largest city is just a short train ride away from Dachau, and offers a wealth of cultural attractions, including museums, theaters, and historic sites.
  • Neuschwanstein Castle: This fairytale castle is one of Germany’s most popular tourist attractions and is located just a few hours’ drive from Dachau.
  • Zugspitze: Germany’s highest mountain is located in the nearby Bavarian Alps and offers breathtaking views and outdoor recreation opportunities.

Conclusion

Dachau is a fascinating destination that offers a unique blend of culture, history, and natural beauty. Whether you’re interested in exploring the town’s tragic past or just want to take in the stunning Bavarian scenery, Dachau has something to offer every visitor.
