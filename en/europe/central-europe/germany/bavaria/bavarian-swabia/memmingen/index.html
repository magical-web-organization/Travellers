Memmingen is a charming town in Bavarian Swabia, in the heart of Bavaria, in Germany. Its historic town center, cultural landmarks, traditional food and drink scene, and easy access to the Bavarian Alps make Memmingen a popular destination among travelers. In this comprehensive tour guide, we will take you on a journey to discover the best places to visit, eat, stay and explore in Memmingen.

Introduction to Memmingen

Nestled between Munich and Stuttgart, Memmingen is a picturesque town that has managed to preserve its medieval heritage. The town dates back to pre-Roman times and was an important trading post in the Middle Ages. Today, its charming old town center is a blend of Gothic and Baroque architecture and is home to many must-see sights. Memmingen is also known for its cultural landmarks, such as museums and historical places, as well as its natural attractions, including outdoor adventures and scenic views.

List of Places to Visit in Memmingen

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • The Town Museum – This museum showcases the history of Memmingen from the Roman era to the present day. It’s a great place to learn about the town’s heritage and culture.
  • The Baroque Palace – This impressive palace was built in the 18th century and served as the residence of the Swabian counts.
  • The Stadtmauer – The city walls are a testament to Memmingen’s past and offer great views of the town.
  • The Fischerviertel – This historic quarter is known for its colorful fishermen houses and charming cobblestoned streets.
  • The Renaissance Town Hall – This beautiful building is one of the most iconic landmarks in Memmingen.

Natural Attractions of Memmingen: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • The Bavarian Alps – The mountains surrounding Memmingen offer a wide range of outdoor activities, from hiking and biking to skiing and snowboarding.
  • The Geltnach Gorge – This natural preserve is a must-visit destination for nature lovers. It features a stunning waterfall and scenic trails.
  • The Iller River – This river runs through the town and offers great opportunities for kayaking and sailing.
  • Schwäbische Bauernhofmuseum - This museum is an open-air exhibition that displays the traditional, rural lifestyle of Swabia. Visitors can explore the recreated farmhouses, gardens and livestock pens.
  • Ottobeuren Abbey - A historic Benedictine monastery located near Memmingen, famed for its Baroque-era architecture and a renowned choir.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Memmingen

Memmingen is nestled between rolling hills and the picturesque Lake Constance. The town is surrounded by beautiful landscapes and offers a wide range of hiking and biking trails for all skill levels. Some popular routes include:

  • The Allgäuer Panoramaweg – This scenic trail runs from Oberstaufen to Obergünzburg and offers stunning views of the Bavarian Alps.
  • The Illerradweg – This biking trail follows the path of the Iller River and offers great opportunities for sightseeing and exploring the local flora and fauna.
  • The Memminger Rundweg – This circular hiking trail starts in the town center and takes visitors through the surrounding countryside and forests.
  • The Westallgäuer Hügellandweg - A series of hiking and biking trails in the west Allgäu hills that take you through charming villages and bucolic landscapes.

Best Things to Do in Memmingen: Activities and Tours

  • Guided Historic Town Tour – A great way to learn about Memmingen’s rich history and culture is to take a guided tour through its historic center. It’s a perfect introduction to the town’s landmarks and attractions.
  • Castle Tours – There are several castles near Memmingen, including the imposing Neuschwanstein Castle. Visitors can take a guided tour and learn about the history of these magnificent structures.
  • Concerts and Festivals – Memmingen is known for its lively arts scene and hosts several festivals throughout the year, such as the Medieval Festival, the Christmas Market and the International Dixieland Festival.
  • Winter Sports – The surrounding mountains offer great opportunities for winter sports, such as skiing, snowboarding and ice-skating.

Food and Drink Scene of Memmingen

Memmingen is known for its traditional cuisine, which features hearty, meat-based dishes and sweet pastries. Some must-try dishes include:

Must-Try Dishes

  • The Allgäuer Kässpätzle – A hearty, cheese-filled pasta dish that is a specialty of the Allgäu region.
  • The Allgäuer Rindfleischroulade – Thinly sliced beef rolled and stuffed with onions, bacon, and pickles.
  • The Bier – Some of the local breweries are world-renowned. Try brewery tours and tastings at Zötler Brauerei or Farny Brauerei.
  • The Mostviertler Schnitz - Grilled pork belly marinated in vinegar, fennel seeds, garlic and bay leaves.

Local Beverages

  • The Weißbier – This is a traditional Bavarian beer, made with wheat and yeast, with a distinct clove and banana flavor.
  • The Schnapps – Locally distilled fruit brandies, often served as digestive after a heavy meal.

Where to Stay in Memmingen: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Memmingen has a great selection of hotels, hostels, and guesthouses to choose from. Here are some recommended options:

  • Hotel Weisses Ross – This charming hotel is located in the heart of town and offers traditional Bavarian hospitality in a historic setting.
  • Jugendherberge Memmingen – This budget-friendly hostel is perfect for travelers looking for clean and secure accommodations with a central location.
  • Die Post Hotel** - An upscale hotel with elegant décor, excellent restaurant and relaxing spa facilities.

What to Buy in Memmingen: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Allgäu Cheese – This regional cheese is a must-try and can be purchased at local markets.
  • Wooden Toys and Crafts – Hand-crafted wooden toys and household items are a staple of the region and make great souvenirs.
  • Local Wines and Beer – These make great gifts and souvenirs, and can be found at local shops and tasting rooms.

Local Culture and Customs of Memmingen

Memmingen is proud of its Swabian heritage and traditions. Respect for authority and punctuality are highly valued in the local culture. The town celebrates various traditional festivities such as the Kempter Fest or the Fischertag. It’s necessary to dress appropriately for cultural events and religious ceremonies.

Best Time to Visit Memmingen

The best time to visit Memmingen is during the summer months (June to August) when the weather is warm and sunny. The town is also popular during the ski season (December to February), when the nearby mountains offer great opportunities for winter sports.

Do’s and Don’ts of Memmingen

Do:

  • Respect local customs and traditions.
  • Dress appropriately for cultural events and religious ceremonies.
  • Try local cuisine, beer, and wines.
  • Explore the surrounding countryside and natural preserves.

Don’t:

  • Be late or show up uninvited to appointments or meetings.
  • Be loud or disruptive in public spaces.
  • Wear inappropriate clothing in religious sites.
  • Break any road rules or speed limits when driving.

Safety and Health

Safety in Memmingen

Memmingen is a very safe town with a low crime rate. However, visitors should still take the necessary precautions to protect their belongings and avoid petty theft.

Health in Memmingen

Memmingen has excellent medical facilities and services. Visitors should consider obtaining health insurance before travel and familiarize themselves with emergency numbers and medical centers.

Getting to Memmingen: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Memmingen

The nearest airport to Memmingen is Memmingen Airport, which offers connections to several German and European cities. Other nearby airports include Munich Airport and Stuttgart Airport.

Bus Stations Near Memmingen

Memmingen has a centrally located bus station with connections to several German and European cities. The town is also well-served by regional bus routes.

Train Stations Near Memmingen

Memmingen has a centrally located train station with connections to several German and European cities.

Other Options

Driving to Memmingen is very easy. The town is located close to the A7 and A96 highways, which offers easy access to other parts of Bavaria and Germany.

How to Get Around in Memmingen

Memmingen is a very walkable town, and the historic center can be easily explored on foot. Bikes are also a popular mode of transportation, and there are several bike rental shops in town. The town also has an efficient public transportation system, including buses and trains, to take you in and around the area.

Where to Go Next from Memmingen: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Lake Constance – This picturesque lake is a great place for hiking, biking, and water sports, and features several charming towns and historic sites.
  • Neuschwanstein Castle – This fairytale castle is one of the most popular destinations in Germany and is only a few hours’ drive from Memmingen.
  • Munich – The vibrant and historic capital of Bavaria, Munich is a must-visit destination for any traveler to the region, with its rich history, culture, and entertainment.
  • Stuttgart and the Swabian Alps – Stuttgart is the gateway to the Swabian Alps, a beautiful mountain range that offers great hiking and skiing. The area is also known for its picturesque towns and charming villages.

Conclusion

Memmingen is a charming and historic town in the heart of Bavaria that offers something for every traveler. With its rich history, cultural landmarks, natural attractions, and traditional cuisine, Memmingen is a must-visit destination. Whether you’re interested in outdoor adventures, cultural experiences, or just relaxing in a beautiful setting, Memmingen has something to offer. We hope this comprehensive guide has helped you plan your trip and discover all the best things to do and see in Memmingen.
