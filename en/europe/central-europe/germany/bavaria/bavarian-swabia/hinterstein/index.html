Hinterstein is a hidden gem located in Bavarian Swabia, one of the seven districts of the German state of Bavaria. The quaint village with a population of about 500 people is the epitome of old world charm, with lush green meadows, snow-capped mountains, and traditional Bavarian architecture.

Whether you’re a history buff, adventure seeker, or nature lover, Hinterstein has something in store for you. In this comprehensive travel guide, we’ve listed down all the essential information you need to plan your trip to Hinterstein. So, let’s get started!

List of Places to Visit in Hinterstein

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Hinterstein is a place of natural beauty and history that tourists can explore. Local museums are located in nearby towns, all of which provide a glimpse into Germany’s rich heritage. You can visit St. Nicholas’ Church, Hinterstein’s oldest existing building dating back to the 15th century, and witness its unique architecture.

Another fascinating attraction is the “Höllritzertal” natural heritage site that dates back to the 1700s, which consists of a rustic timber mill, sawmill, and oil mill that still works. The Haus des Gastes in Oberstdorf offers exhibits on the cultural heritage of Allgäu.

Natural Attractions of Hinterstein: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

A trip to Hinterstein is incomplete without exploring its natural beauty. With numerous hiking and biking trails weaving through the mountains, you can explore the region’s stunning vistas at your own pace. The Breitachklamm (Breitach Gorge), just a few kilometers away from Hinterstein, is a stunning gorge with walkways over the rushing waters. It makes for a beautiful and memorable hike.

For winter sport enthusiasts, Hinterstein Ski School offers skiing and snowboarding lessons to people of all ages and skill levels. From cross-country skiing to snowshoeing, there’s plenty to do in the winter wonderland of Hinterstein.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Hinterstein

Hiking and biking enthusiasts will have a lot to choose from in Hinterstein. The region’s vast alpine terrain offers numerous options for all skill levels. One of the most popular hikes is the Hirschsprung hike, which takes you through the Breitachklamm up to the Hirschsprung viewpoint. Another must-do is the Grünten hike, a 1738-meter high mountain that offers stunning views of both Hinterstein and the lush forests of the region.

Best Things to Do in Hinterstein: Activities and Tours

Apart from hiking and skiing, there are plenty of activities in Hinterstein for tourists. For instance, you can take a guided e-bike tour through the mountains or paraglide over the stunning vistas of Allgäu. The nearby Oberstdorf town offers plenty of activities, such as swimming, horseback riding or climbing, which can be easily accessible via a short bus ride.

Week-Long Itinerary for Hinterstein

If you’re planning to spend an entire week in Hinterstein, we’ve got you covered. Here’s an itinerary that covers all the must-visit attractions:

  • Day 1: Arrive in Hinterstein, settle down and explore the village at leisure.
  • Day 2-3: Explore the natural attractions of Hinterstein, including the Breitachklamm, Hirschsprung viewpoint and Grünten hike.
  • Day 4: Take a day trip to the historic city of Fussen, also known as “King’s town” because of its famous castles - Hohenschwangau Castle and Neuschwanstein Castle.
  • Day 5-6: Dig deeper into Hinterstein’s history by visiting St. Nicholas’ Church and the Höllritzertal natural heritage site.
  • Day 7: Bid adieu to Hinterstein and the Allgäu region.

Food and Drink Scene of Hinterstein

Must-Try Dishes

The Allgäu region boasts a hearty cuisine, with a range of dishes perfect for cold winter days and warm summer evenings. Some must-try dishes in Hinterstein include:

  • Kässpatzen: a form of pasta served with cheese and onions.
  • Weißwurst: traditional white sausage.
  • Rindfleisch: beef dishes such as sauerbraten or Tafelspitz, consisting of boiled beef and vegetables.
  • Kaiserschmarrn: a shredded pancake with raisins and almonds.

Local Beverages

Allgäu is famous for its beer culture. Some of the best brews in the town are: Ayinger, Ettaler Kreuzberg, and Hofmühl. You can also try regional wines like Müller-Thurgau or Riesling.

Where to Stay in Hinterstein: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Hinterstein offers a range of accommodation options for tourists, including hotels, hostels, and guesthouses. Some of the best places to stay include the Alpenhof Hotel, Gasthaus Zum Hirsch, and Berghof Engel. If you would like to stay in Oberstdorf, there are plenty of properties for every budget and taste.

What to Buy in Hinterstein: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Hinterstein has many little shops that offer traditional German fare, including Bavarian clothing items, porcelain, glassware, and ornaments. The famous regional alcoholic beverage is Allgäu-Bärwurz-Schnaps which is distilled from a type of wormwood.

Local Culture and Customs of Hinterstein

Hinterstein is well known for its traditional Bavarian attire and culture. The locals celebrate numerous harvest festivals, and the region’s rich culture and history are reflected in its architecture and handicrafts.

Best Time to Visit Hinterstein

Hinterstein can be visited any time of the year. The region’s mountainous terrain offers breathtaking views throughout the year, with optimal hiking and skiing conditions in the winter months.

Do’s and Don’ts of Hinterstein

Do:

  • Respect the region’s natural beauty and environment.
  • Dress appropriately when visiting religious sites.
  • Tip between 5% and 10% in restaurants.

Don’t:

  • Be loud and disrespectful towards the locals.
  • Litter in the region’s protected areas.
  • Ignore hiking and skiing safety rules.

Safety and Health

Safety in Hinterstein

Although Hinterstein is known for its tranquility, it’s essential to be vigilant and informed about emergency services. Be sure to carry a first aid kit with you while on outdoor adventures and always inform someone of your hiking plans.

Health in Hinterstein

The town has a medical center that tourists can visit for primary healthcare concerns, however, medications, sun protection, insect repellent, and heavy clothing such as warm jackets are essential to pack depending on the season.

Getting to Hinterstein: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Hinterstein

The closest airport to Hinterstein is the Memmingen Airport, which is about 75 kilometers away from the town. You can also fly into Munich Airport, which is about 210km from Hinterstein. From both airports, you can hire cars or take a bus to reach the destination.

Bus Stations Near Hinterstein

The nearest bus station to Hinterstein is in Sonthofen. From the bus station, head to the Hinterstein Tourist Information Center, which is within walking distance, to make your way to the town.

Other Options

If you’re traveling from a nearby city or town, you can also drive into Hinterstein or take a train to Sonthofen and then proceed to the town by bus or taxi.

How to Get Around in Hinterstein

Although the town is small, it’s ideal to rent a bike or a car to explore the area’s beauty comfortably. There are also public buses that take you from Hinterstein to other nearby areas.

Where to Go Next from Hinterstein: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

There are plenty of nearby destinations you can visit after your vacation in Hinterstein. Some of the popular ones include Schwangau, known for its beautiful castles, and Lindau, a cozy island town that houses the beautiful Lake Constance.

Conclusion

Hinterstein is a perfect getaway for anyone looking for a peaceful yet eventful vacation in Germany. With its natural beauty, historic landmarks, and outdoor adventure, the town caters for everyone. From indulging in Bavarian cuisine to hiking through mountainous terrains, Hinterstein is a destination you cannot miss.
