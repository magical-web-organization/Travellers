Introduction to Kempten

Kempten, located in Bavarian Swabia, is one of the oldest and most beautiful cities in Germany. It is also the largest city in the Allgäu region. Kempten boasts a rich historical and cultural heritage, beautiful natural landscapes, delicious food, and friendly people.

Visitors can enjoy exploring the city’s museums and historic sites, hiking in the surrounding mountains, and experiencing the local food and drink scene. Kempten is also well connected to other cities in Germany and makes for a great starting point for exploring the Allgäu region.

List of Places to Visit in Kempten

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

1. Archaeological Park Cambodunum

The Archaeological Park Cambodunum is a Roman site located on the outskirts of Kempten. Visitors can explore the preserved ruins of an ancient military camp, baths, and homes.

2. Allgäu-Museum

The Allgäu-Museum showcases the history and culture of the Allgäu region. Visitors can learn about traditional crafts, clothing, and music, as well as the city’s history as a Roman settlement.

3. St. Lorenz Basilica

The St. Lorenz Basilica is a beautiful church located in the heart of Kempten. It dates back to the 17th century and features stunning frescoes and an ornate interior.

Natural Attractions of Kempten: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

1. Grünten

Grünten is a mountain located near Kempten that is a popular spot for hiking and skiing. Visitors can take a cable car to the top for incredible views of the surrounding mountains and valleys.

2. Alpsee

The Alpsee is a beautiful lake surrounded by mountains. Visitors can swim, paddle boat, and enjoy the tranquil atmosphere.

3. Illerkanal

The Illerkanal is a canal that runs through Kempten. Visitors can enjoy a leisurely stroll along the banks and take in the city’s sights.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Kempten

1. Kempten Circular Trail

The Kempten Circular Trail is a 14 km hiking and biking trail that takes visitors through the city and its surrounding countryside. Along the way, visitors can see historic sites, natural landscapes, and beautiful views.

2. Wasserläufer Trail

The Wasserläufer Trail is a 6 km hiking and biking trail that takes visitors along the Iller River. The route features scenic views of the river and the surrounding forests.

Best Things to Do in Kempten: Activities and Tours

1. Kempten City Tour

The Kempten City Tour is a guided tour that takes visitors through the city’s historic sites and landmarks. The tour includes stops at the St. Lorenz Basilica, the Archaeological Park Cambodunum, and the Allgäu-Museum.

2. Brewery Tour

Kempten is home to several traditional breweries, and visitors can take guided tours to learn about the brewing process and sample local beers.

Food and Drink Scene of Kempten

Must-Try Dishes

1. Kässpatzen

Kässpatzen is a traditional Allgäu dish of soft egg noodles topped with melted cheese and fried onions.

2. Allgäuer Käse

Allgäuer Käse is a type of cheese made from cow’s milk that is native to the region. It is often served with bread and beer.

Local Beverages

1. Allgäuer Bier

Allgäuer Bier is a type of beer brewed in the Allgäu region. It is traditionally made with local ingredients and is known for its rich, malty flavor.

2. Obstler

Obstler is a type of fruit brandy that is popular in the Allgäu region. It is made from a mix of apples, pears, and plums and has a smooth, sweet flavor.

Where to Stay in Kempten: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

1. St. Raphael

St. Raphael is a centrally located hotel that offers comfortable rooms and a restaurant serving traditional Bavarian cuisine.

2. DJH Jugendherberge Kempten

The DJH Jugendherberge Kempten is a budget-friendly hostel located near the city’s train station. It offers a variety of dormitory-style rooms, as well as private rooms, and features a communal kitchen and lounge area.

What to Buy in Kempten: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

1. Allgäuer Trachten

Allgäuer Trachten are traditional clothing items worn in the Allgäu region. Visitors can purchase dirndls and lederhosen as souvenirs.

2. Allgäuer Käse

Allgäuer Käse is a popular souvenir among foodies. Visitors can purchase the cheese at local markets and specialty food shops.

Local Culture and Customs of Kempten

The people of Kempten are proud of their Allgäu heritage and are known for their hospitality and warmth. Traditional customs, such as wearing Allgäu Trachten and celebrating local festivals, are still observed in the city.

Best Time to Visit Kempten

Kempten can be visited year-round. Summers are warm and sunny, making it the best time for outdoor activities. Winters are cold and snowy, making it perfect for skiing and snowboarding.

Do’s and Don’ts of Kempten

Do:

  • Do try local dishes and drinks.
  • Do wear comfortable walking shoes to explore the city and its surroundings.
  • Do learn about the city’s rich history and culture.

Don’t:

  • Don’t litter or damage the natural landscapes.
  • Don’t speak too loudly in public or disturb other visitors.
  • Don’t ignore local customs and traditions.

Safety and Health

Safety in Kempten

Kempten is a safe city for visitors, with low crime rates. Visitors should still take precautions against pickpocketing and theft, particularly in crowded tourist areas.

Health in Kempten

Kempten has excellent healthcare facilities, with several hospitals and clinics located in the city. Visitors should have comprehensive travel insurance and take necessary health precautions, such as getting vaccinations if needed.

Getting to Kempten: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Kempten

The nearest airport to Kempten is Memmingen Airport, which is approximately 30 km away. Other nearby airports include Friedrichshafen Airport, Munich Airport, and Innsbruck Airport.

Bus Stations Near Kempten

The Kempten Bus Station is located near the city’s train station and offers connections to other cities in Germany and Europe.

Other Options

Visitors can also reach Kempten by train or car. The city is well connected to other cities in Germany, making it an accessible destination.

How to Get Around Kempten

Kempten is a small city that can easily be explored on foot or bike. Visitors can also use public transportation, such as buses and trains, to get around.

Where to Go Next from Kempten: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

1. Füssen

Füssen is a picturesque town located near Kempten. It is known for its beautiful Renaissance architecture and proximity to the famous Neuschwanstein Castle.

2. Oberstdorf

Oberstdorf is a popular ski resort town located in the Alps. It features high peaks, glaciers and traditional Bavarian buildings and restaurants.

Conclusion

Kempten is a beautiful and vibrant city that offers something for every traveler. Whether you are interested in history and culture, outdoor adventures, or food and drink, Kempten has it all. Plan your visit now to experience the best of Bavarian Swabia!
