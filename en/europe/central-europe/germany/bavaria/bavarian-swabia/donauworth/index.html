Introduction to Donauwörth

Donauwörth is a picturesque town located in Bavarian Swabia, in Bavaria, Germany. Situated where the Wörnitz and Danube rivers meet, it is a popular tourist destination thanks to its rich history and beautiful natural surroundings. Similar to many other German towns, Donauwörth was once a divided city with a bridge connecting two parts of the town. It is now reunited and boasts a charming old town that is filled with historical landmarks and stunning architecture.

List of Places to Visit in Donauwörth

With so many things to do and see, it’s hard to decide where to start in Donauwörth. Here are some of the must-visit attractions in the area.

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Donauwörth is a town steeped in history, dotted with museums and other cultural landmarks that offer a glimpse into the town’s past. The following are some of the best must-visit sites:

  1. Reichsstrasse and the Rieder Tower - Take a stroll down Reichsstrasse, the main street in Donauwörth, and admire its richly decorated buildings. You can also climb the Rieder Tower for stunning views of the town and the surrounding countryside.
  2. Käthe-Kruse-Puppen-Museum - This museum is dedicated to the history of Käthe Kruse dolls, which are famous handmade dolls that have become collectors’ items worldwide.
  3. Mangold’sche Haus - This beautiful house is an excellent example of Baroque architecture. The 17th-century building is now a museum about local history.
  4. Liebfrauenmünster - The gothic church with its beautiful stained glass windows and impressive altar is a must-see for anyone. Don’t forget to climb up to the tower for panoramic views of the town.
  5. Färberturm - Climb up to the top of this tower, originally a watchtower for guarding the city walls, to enjoy a spectacular view of the town.

Natural Attractions of Donauwörth: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

The town of Donauwörth offers much more than just architecture and history. With its natural beauty, there are plenty of outdoor activities to indulge in. Here are some of the top natural attractions:

  1. Danube River - Rent a canoe and paddle down the Danube River to experience the picturesque landscapes of the Swabian Alps. Alternatively, take a boat trip and admire the view from the water.
  2. Wörnitz Park - The park runs along the Wörnitz River, and it’s the perfect spot for a picnic, hiking, biking or enjoying a leisurely stroll.
  3. Harburg Castle - This perfectly preserved castle, built in the late medieval period, is located about an hour from Donauwörth. With its beautiful mountain views and forest surroundings, it is worth a visit.
  4. The Altmühltal Nature Park - This stunning natural park is ideal for hiking, cycling, and exploring the breathtaking scenery of the Bavarian countryside.
  5. Baggersee Donauwörth - This lake is located on the outskirts of Donauwörth. With its clear water and sandy beach, it’s the perfect spot for swimming on a hot summer’s day.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Donauwörth

Donauwörth boasts some of the most beautiful scenic routes in Bavaria. The town is well connected with a network of hiking and biking trails that offer a unique experience for nature lovers and adventurer seekers. Here are some of the best hiking and biking routes when visiting Donauwörth:

  1. The Rieskrater bicycle route - This 50 km long bike path leads through nature reserves, past ponds, and small towns with beautiful churches.
  2. Augsburg - Donauwörth - This long-distance bike tour passes through two impressive cities, three idyllic baroque towns, and a beautiful stretch of the Danube River.
  3. The Swabian Jura cycle path - This circular route leads through the foothills of Swabian Jura, offering stunning panoramic mountain views and charming small towns.
  4. Fünf-Flüsse-Radweg (Five Rivers Route) - This cycling route leads along the Altmühl-, the Naab-, the Vils-, the Pegnitz-, and the Schwarzach rivers.

Best Things to Do in Donauwörth: Activities and Tours

Apart from the cultural and natural attractions, there are plenty of fun activities and tours for visitors to Donauwörth. Here are some of the best:

  1. Abenteuer Dschungelland - This adventure park offers an exciting experience for families, with tree-top trails, playgrounds, bumper cars, and more.
  2. Donauwörth Guided Tour - Discover the town’s rich history and its famous landmarks by taking a guided tour of the old town.
  3. Shopping in Donauwörth- The town center is filled with a variety of shops selling everything from traditional handicrafts to modern souvenirs interested in tourists.
  4. Minigolf am Friedensplatz - This mini-golf course located in the town center is the perfect place for an afternoon of fun with the family.
  5. Golf club Donauwörth - Golf enthusiasts can hire clubs and take in a round of golf at this stunning course. Beware though, the course can be challenging!

Week-Long Itinerary for Donauwörth

A week-long itinerary gives visitors enough time to experience Donauwörth fully. Here is a week-long itinerary that covers the best sights and experiences in the beautiful town:

Day 1: Reichsstrasse and the Rieder Tower, followed by Käthe-Kruse-Puppen-Museum

Day 2: Donauwörth Guided Tour, followed by lunch in one of the town center cafes or restaurants.

Day 3: Wörnitz Park, followed by a picnic lunch and an afternoon enjoying the natural scenery.

Day 4: Harburg Castle - A day trip to this stunningly preserved castle to experience the history and picturesque mountain views.

Day 5: Altmühltal Nature Park - Hike or cycle through this stunning natural park.

Day 6: Baggersee Donauwörth - Spend a sunny day swimming and relaxing by the lake.

Day 7: Liebfrauenmünster, Färberturm - Walk around the old town, visit two of the town’s top attractions, and take in the beauty of Donauwörth.

Food and Drink Scene of Donauwörth

The food scene in Donauwörth is unique, with a wide variety of traditional local cuisines that are a must-try for any visitor. Here are some of the best:

Must-Try Dishes

  1. Schweinshaxe - A roasted pork knuckle is a Bavarian classic and is often served with sauerkraut and a crispy crust.
  2. Spätzle - A type of soft egg noodle that is served throughout Germany. It is most commonly served as a side dish with roasted meats.
  3. Schupfnudeln - The Swabian version of potato dumplings, these are delicious served with sauerkraut and roasted meat.
  4. Kaiserschmarrn - A sweet shredded pancake that is often served at breakfast or as a dessert.
  5. Pretzels - Soft, chewy Bavarian pretzels are a must-try snack when in Donauwörth.

Local Beverages

  1. Bavarian Beer - The town boasts several small breweries, and visitors can try some of the best Bavarian beer at local pubs.
  2. Schnapps - Try some of the local fruit brandies and schnapps, which are made from locally grown fruits and traditional methods.

Where to Stay in Donauwörth: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Donauwörth has a variety of accommodation options ranging from budget-friendly hostels to luxurious hotels. Here are some of the best:

  1. Parkhotel Donauwörth - This stunning hotel is located in a peaceful park and offers luxurious rooms with all the amenities you would expect.
  2. Hotel-Gasthof zur Promenade - A charming and affordable hotel offering comfortable rooms and excellent food.
  3. Hotel Zur Post - A budget-friendly option, located in the town center and offering cozy rooms at a good price.

What to Buy in Donauwörth: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Donauwörth has plenty of shops selling souvenirs and local products. Here are some of the must-buys:

  1. Käthe-Kruse dolls - Handmade dolls that are available for purchase in the Käthe-Kruse-Puppen-Museum.
  2. Beer Steins - Traditional, uniquely-shaped, Bavarian beer steins are the perfect souvenir or gift.
  3. Bavarian Pretzels - Pretzels make an excellent snack or souvenir, and they can be purchased from local bakeries.
  4. Wine - The region surrounding Donauwörth is known for producing outstanding white wines that make a perfect gift to take home.

Local Culture and Customs of Donauwörth

The town of Donauwörth is steeped in rich traditions, and it’s essential to respect the local customs and culture. Visitors should follow these general tips:

  1. Dress modestly when visiting churches and other religious landmarks.
  2. Try to speak a few words of German when communicating with the locals.
  3. Follow local customs and traditions during local festivities, such as the yearly Carnival festival.

Best Time to Visit Donauwörth

The best time to visit Donauwörth is from May to October when the weather is warm and sunny. The summer months, in particular, offer the best hiking and outdoor activities, although this is also the busiest time in the town.

Do’s and Don’ts of Donauwörth

Here are some general tips on what to do and what to avoid while visiting Donauwörth:

Do:

  1. Respect local customs and traditions.
  2. Take in the beautiful town views while walking along Reichsstrasse.
  3. Try the local cuisine and beer.
  4. Visit the top attractions, both indoors and outdoors.

Don’t:

  1. Don’t litter in the streets.
  2. Don’t wear inappropriate clothing when visiting religious landmarks.
  3. Don’t forget to tip wait staff at restaurants (usually around 10%).

Safety and Health

While Donauwörth is a safe town, visitors should always be cautious and aware of their surroundings, especially when visiting crowded tourist areas. Here are some basic safety and health tips:

Safety in Donauwörth

  1. Always keep valuables in a safe place, such as a hotel safe.
  2. Avoid walking alone at night.
  3. Always cross roads carefully and only at designated crossings.

Health in Donauwörth

  1. Protect yourself from the sun with a hat or sunscreen when out hiking or sightseeing.
  2. Drink bottled water instead of tap water.
  3. In case of an emergency, dial 112 on the phone and ask for assistance.

Getting to Donauwörth: Transportation Options and Logistics

There are several transportation options for visitors to reach Donauwörth, including:

Airports Near Donauwörth

The nearest airports are Munich International Airport and Stuttgart International Airport, each located approximately two hours from Donauwörth.

Bus Stations Near Donauwörth

Several bus services connect Donauwörth to other major cities in Germany, including Flixbus and Deutsche Bahn.

Other Options

Other options for getting to Donauwörth include train services from Munich and Stuttgart.

How to Get Around in Donauwörth

Walking is the best way to get around Donauwörth as the town is small and easily accessible. Visitors can also rent bicycles to explore the town and its surroundings.

Where to Go Next from Donauwörth: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Donauwörth is close to several other popular tourist destinations in Germany. Here are some nearby towns and cities that are worth visiting:

  1. Augsburg - A vibrant city located 40 minutes from Donauwörth, with plenty of history, culture, and shopping.
  2. Ulm - Ulm is an hour’s drive from Donauwörth and is famous for its enormous gothic church and half-timbered houses.
  3. Nuremberg - Nuremberg is about 1.5 hours from Donauwörth and is the largest city in Franconia. It’s famous for its castle, markets, and beer.

Conclusion

Donauwörth is a town of unique landmarks and breathtaking natural scenery. Travellers can enjoy a variety of activities in Donauwörth, from hiking and biking to exploring museums and historical sites. Along with its rich history and varied cuisine, Donauwörth is the perfect destination for tourists looking for a German adventure.
