Introduction to Neu-Ulm

Neu-Ulm is a charming town located in Bavarian Swabia, in the state of Bavaria, in Germany. It is a small town with a population of around 50,000 people, and it holds a lot of historical significance. The town is divided by the Danube River, with the Bavarian town on one side and the Swabian town on the other. Neu-Ulm is known for its beautiful landscapes, scenic views, and friendly locals. This guide is designed to help you plan your trip to Neu-Ulm with ease.

List of Places to Visit in Neu-Ulm

There are numerous places to visit in Neu-Ulm, ranging from museums and historical landmarks to outdoor adventures and scenic views. Here are some of the top attractions:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Kunsthaus Weishaupt: This art museum features contemporary art from artists all over the world.
  • Stadtmuseum Neu-Ulm: A museum that portrays the town’s history, with an impressive collection of antiques and artifacts.
  • Edwin-Scharff-Museum: A museum that showcases the works of the famous sculptor Edwin Scharff.
  • Ulm Minster: This church is located across the Danube River in Ulm, but it is easily accessible from Neu-Ulm. The church features Gothic architecture and is the tallest church in the world.

Natural Attractions of Neu-Ulm: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Glacis Park: This park is ideal for picnics and long walks with lots of green spaces and gardens.
  • Donaurieden Nature Reserve: This nature reserve is an outdoor enthusiast’s paradise, with countless hiking trails and bike routes.
  • Adenauerpark: Another great park in Neu-Ulm with a playground for kids and stunning flower gardens.
  • Danube River: Take a walk along the Danube River promenade and enjoy the scenic views of the river and its surroundings.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Neu-Ulm

Neu-Ulm has some of the best hiking and biking trails in Germany. Here are some popular ones to try if you’re up for some adventure:

  • Legoland Bike Tour: This route takes you through the beautiful countryside and ends at Legoland Germany.
  • Donauradweg: This bike route is perfect for those who want to explore the Danube River and its surroundings.
  • Iller-Radweg: A bike route that takes you along the Iller River, another river that runs through Neu-Ulm.
  • Hoher Kuhberg: This hiking trail is ideal for nature lovers who want to experience the fantastic panoramic views of Neu-Ulm.

Best Things to Do in Neu-Ulm: Activities and Tours

In addition to the above attractions, here are some other exciting things to do in Neu-Ulm:

  • Visit the Ulmer Münster (Ulm Minster): The largest church in the world that serves as the symbol of the city of Ulm and the region.
  • Experience the Danube: Take a riverboat tour on the Danube River and enjoy the beautiful scenery along the way.
  • Legoland Germany: An amusement park that features captivating rides, fun activities, and Lego creations.
  • Neu-Ulm Street Festival: This annual festival is held every summer and is the most anticipated event in the town. It features live music, food vendors, and much more.

Week-Long Itinerary for Neu-Ulm

Here’s a suggested itinerary for a week-long trip to Neu-Ulm:

Day 1: Arrive in town and take a stroll along the Danube River promenade and see the beautiful landscapes.

Day 2: Head to Ulm Minster to explore the church’s architecture and climb to the top for magnificent views of the town.

Day 3: Spend the day at Legoland Germany and enjoy various rides and activities.

Day 4: Visit Kunsthaus Weishaupt and explore contemporary art from artists worldwide.

Day 5: Go on a bike ride along the Danube River and appreciate the stunning views.

Day 6: Explore the Stadtmuseum Neu-Ulm and learn about the town’s rich history.

Day 7: Take a trip to Donaurieden Nature Reserve and hike the beautiful trails.

Food and Drink Scene of Neu-Ulm

When it comes to Neu-Ulm’s food and drink scene, there is something for everyone. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Spätzle: a local dish, similar to pasta, made from flour, eggs, and water, usually served with cheese, onions, or meat.
  • Käsflädle: a Swabian dish made from flour, eggs, milk, and cheese, cooked like a pancake.
  • Schwäbische Maultaschen: a Swabian dish similar to ravioli, but with a meat or vegetable stuffing.

Local Beverages

  • Beer: Neu-Ulm has a local brewery, Familienbrauerei Bauhöfer, that produces some of the region’s best beer.
  • Apfelschorle: a refreshing mix of apple juice and carbonated water.
  • Weißwein: white wine, which is grown in the region and is known for its crisp and refreshing taste.

Where to Stay in Neu-Ulm: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

There are many accommodation options in Neu-Ulm to suit different budgets and preferences. Here are some top-rated options:

  • Meinl Hotel & Restaurant: A luxurious hotel with an on-site restaurant and a beautiful garden terrace.
  • B&B Hotel Neu-Ulm: A budget-friendly option with comfortable rooms and a central location.
  • Hotel Garni Am Zehntstadl: A guesthouse that offers cozy rooms and is located in a quiet residential area.

What to Buy in Neu-Ulm: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

When shopping for souvenirs and local products in Neu-Ulm, keep an eye out for:

  • Handmade pottery finished with a Swabian design
  • Spätzle noodles, either ready-made or as a mix to prepare at home
  • Local wine and beer

Local Culture and Customs of Neu-Ulm

The locals of Neu-Ulm are friendly and welcoming to visitors. The town has a rich cultural heritage, and the locals take pride in preserving their traditions and customs. It is essential to respect their customs and behave appropriately.

Best Time to Visit Neu-Ulm

The best time to visit Neu-Ulm is between May and September when the weather is mild and pleasant for outdoor activities. The town hosts various festivals and events during this time, making it an excellent time to experience Neu-Ulm’s culture.

Do’s and Don’ts of Neu-Ulm

Do:

  • Do try local food and beverages, such as Spätzle and local beer.
  • Do take a boat ride on the Danube River.
  • Do explore the town’s history at Stadtmuseum Neu-Ulm and Kunsthaus Weishaupt.

Don’t:

  • Don’t litter or smoke in public places.
  • Don’t take pictures of people without their consent.
  • Don’t be loud or disrespectful, especially in public places.

Safety and Health

Safety in Neu-Ulm

Neu-Ulm is a safe town with low crime rates. However, it is essential to take standard safety precautions while traveling, such as locking your valuables in the hotel safe and being careful in crowded areas.

Health in Neu-Ulm

Germany has an excellent healthcare system, and Neu-Ulm has several hospitals and clinics with top-notch medical facilities. It is essential to have travel insurance before visiting Neu-Ulm.

Getting to Neu-Ulm: Transportation Options and Logistics

Neu-Ulm is accessible via various transportation options:

Airports Near Neu-Ulm

The closest airport to Neu-Ulm is Memmingen Airport, which is about 50 kilometers away. Other airports in the region include Stuttgart Airport and Munich Airport.

Bus Stations Near Neu-Ulm

There are several bus stations that connect Neu-Ulm to other destinations in Germany. The closest bus station is the Neu-Ulm Busbahnhof.

Other Options

Neu-Ulm is also accessible by train and car, with excellent connectivity to all major cities in Germany.

How to Get Around in Neu-Ulm

Neu-Ulm is a small town, so walking is the best way to explore the town and its surroundings. However, there are also buses and trains that connect different parts of the town.

Where to Go Next from Neu-Ulm: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Neu-Ulm is the perfect starting point to explore the Bavarian Swabia region and its surroundings. Some nearby destinations and excursions are:

  • Augsburg: a historic city known for its architecture, museums, and galleries.
  • Füssen: a charming town famous for its beautiful lakes and castles.
  • The Black Forest: a vast forested mountain range with hiking trails, ski resorts, and scenic views.

Conclusion

Neu-Ulm is a hidden gem in Germany, and it offers unique experiences that are not found elsewhere. From its historical landmarks, natural attractions, outdoor adventures, and excellent food and drinks, Neu-Ulm has something for everyone. This comprehensive guide is an excellent starting point for planning your trip to Neu-Ulm.
