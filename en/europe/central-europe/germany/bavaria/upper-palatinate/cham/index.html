Introduction to Cham

Located in Upper Palatinate, Bavaria, in the heart of central Europe, Cham is a charming medieval town that is rich in cultural heritage and natural beauty. Its streets are lined with traditional half-timbered houses, Gothic and baroque churches, and modern museums that showcase its fascinating history. Outdoors enthusiasts will find an abundance of hiking and biking trails, scenic river walks, and picturesque parks that offer breathtaking views of the surrounding countryside. For food lovers, Cham is a culinary paradise that’s known for its flavorful regional dishes and world-class beers. In this guide, we’ve compiled a comprehensive list of the best things to see and do in Cham, including cultural attractions, outdoor adventures, local cuisine, accommodation options, and neighboring destinations.

List of Places to Visit in Cham

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Cham Museum: This historical museum showcases the rich history of Cham and Bavaria. Visitors can explore the town’s past through a variety of exhibits, including ancient artifacts, traditional crafts, and modern multimedia displays.

  • Roding Castle: This atmospheric castle dates back to the 12th century and is one of the oldest buildings in Cham. It now houses a museum that displays medieval art, arms, and armor, as well as an impressive collection of antique furniture and tapestries.

  • The Church of St. Jakob: This stunning Gothic church is one of Cham’s most iconic landmarks. Visitors can admire its ornate facade, towering spires, and intricate frescoes, or attend a traditional Roman Catholic mass.

Natural Attractions of Cham: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • The Regen River: This scenic river flows through the heart of Cham and offers a peaceful retreat for nature lovers. Visitors can take a leisurely stroll along its banks, rent a kayak or canoe, or fish for trout and pike.

  • The Bavarian Forest: This beautiful forest covers over 200 square kilometers and is home to an abundance of wildlife, including wolves, lynx, and eagles. Visitors can hike along one of its many trails, go mountain biking, or enjoy a picnic in the shade of the trees.

  • The Upper Palatinate Lake District: This stunning region is dotted with picturesque lakes that offer stunning views of the surrounding mountains. Visitors can swim, fish, or simply relax on the shore.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Cham

Cham is a paradise for hikers and cyclists alike. There are many trails and routes that cater to all levels of fitness and experience. Some of the most popular include:

  • The Regental-Radweg: This 106-kilometer cycle path follows the Regen River through the towns and villages of the region. Along the way, visitors can admire the beautiful landscapes, historic buildings, and traditional crafts.

  • The Goldsteig Trail: This 660-kilometer trail is one of Bavaria’s most famous long-distance hiking routes. It connects several nature reserves and national parks and offers stunning views of the Alps and the Bohemian Forest.

  • The Baumwipfelpfad in St. Englmar: This unique trail is a treetop walk that takes visitors through the canopy of the trees. It offers a unique perspective on the forest and offers breathtaking views of the surrounding countryside.

Best Things to Do in Cham: Activities and Tours

  • The Cham Christmas Market: This festive market takes place every year during the last two weeks of November. Visitors can browse the stalls for traditional German holiday treats, gifts, and crafts while enjoying live music and other performances.

  • The Bavarian Beer and Food Tour: This guided tour takes visitors through the town’s local brewery, where they can taste some of the best beers in the region. Afterwards, they’ll be treated to a traditional Bavarian meal at a local restaurant.

  • The Nightwatchman Tour: This unique tour takes visitors on a journey through Cham’s past. The guide, dressed in historic costume, leads visitors through the darkened streets of the town, stopping at various locations to tell stories and legends from times gone by.

Week-Long Itinerary for Cham

Day 1: Visit the Cham Museum and the Church of St. Jakob, followed by dinner at a local Bavarian restaurant.

Day 2: Take a scenic hike along the Goldsteig Trail, followed by a cozy night in at a local guesthouse.

Day 3: Explore the Roding Castle and the surrounding parks and gardens, followed by a picnic lunch by the river.

Day 4: Visit the Baumwipfelpfad in St. Englmar, followed by a relaxing afternoon at the lakes in the Upper Palatinate Lake District.

Day 5: Take a cycling tour along the Regental-Radweg, stopping at local breweries and eateries along the way.

Day 6: Enjoy a visit to the Cham Christmas Market, followed by an evening of live music and entertainment at a local bar.

Day 7: Take a day trip to nearby Regensburg, which is known for its UNESCO-listed Old Town and historic landmarks.

Food and Drink Scene of Cham

Must-Try Dishes

  • Bratwurst: This delicious sausage is a staple of Bavarian cuisine and can be found in many local restaurants and markets.

  • Schweinshaxe: This hearty pork knuckle is popular with locals and visitors alike. It’s often served with sauerkraut and potato dumplings.

  • Kaiserschmarrn: This sweet dessert is made from fluffy pancakes, which are torn into pieces and served with fruit compote and whipped cream.

Local Beverages

  • Beer: Bavaria is famous for its world-class beers, and Cham is no exception. Visitors can sample a variety of local brews at the town’s many breweries and taverns.

  • Schnapps: Made from local fruits and herbs, Schnapps is a traditional Bavarian drink that can be enjoyed as an aperitif or digestif.

Where to Stay in Cham: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Cham offers a variety of accommodation options to suit all budgets and preferences. Some of the most popular include:

  • Hotel “Am Regenbogen”: This cozy hotel is located in the heart of Cham and offers comfortable rooms, a restaurant, and a bar.

  • Jugendherberge Cham: This modern youth hostel is located on the outskirts of town and offers affordable accommodation for backpackers and budget-conscious travelers.

  • Gasthaus Zur Linde: This charming guesthouse is located in the historic town center and offers spacious rooms, free Wi-Fi, and a traditional Bavarian restaurant.

What to Buy in Cham: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Beer Steins: These traditional ceramic mugs are a great souvenir for beer lovers.

  • Trachten Clothing: These traditional Bavarian garments are popular with locals and visitors alike. Visitors can find a variety of styles and designs in many local shops.

Local Culture and Customs of Cham

Cham is a proud and traditional town with a rich cultural heritage. Visitors should be respectful of local customs and traditions, including dress codes for churches and other religious sites.

Best Time to Visit Cham

The best time to visit Cham is during the summer months, from June to August. The weather is warm and sunny, and there are many outdoor activities and festivals to enjoy. The winter months, from December to February, are also a great time to visit Cham, especially for those who enjoy winter sports and the holiday festivities.

Do’s and Don’ts of Cham

Do:

  • Try the local cuisine and beverages, including Bavarian beer and traditional dishes like Schweinshaxe.

  • Visit the local museums and historical sites to learn more about Cham’s rich cultural heritage.

  • Respect local customs and traditions, including dress codes for churches and other religious sites.

Don’t:

  • Drink and drive. Bavaria has strict laws against drunk driving. Visitors should always use public transportation or arrange for a designated driver.

  • Touch or climb on historic buildings or monuments. These important cultural landmarks should be treated with respect and care.

  • Litter or damage natural areas or parks. Cham is known for its pristine and beautiful surroundings, and visitors should do their part to keep it that way.

Safety and Health

Safety in Cham

Cham is a relatively safe town, but visitors should take the usual precautions against petty crime, such as pickpocketing and theft. They should also be careful when hiking or biking in the area and always carry a map and plenty of water.

Health in Cham

Cham has several medical facilities and pharmacies that are open during regular business hours. Visitors should also make sure they have adequate travel insurance before arriving in Cham.

Getting to Cham: Transportation Options and Logistics

  • By Car: Cham is easily accessible by car from all major cities in Germany and many neighboring countries.

  • By Train: Cham has a train station that is served by several regional and national rail lines.

  • By Bus: Cham has a bus station that connects it to many nearby towns and cities.

Airports Near Cham

  • Munich Airport: Located about 150 kilometers south of Cham, Munich Airport offers flights to many international destinations.

  • Nuremberg Airport: Located about 110 kilometers north of Cham, Nuremberg Airport offers flights to many European destinations.

Bus Stations Near Cham

  • Regensburg Bus Station: Located about 65 kilometers west of Cham, Regensburg Bus Station offers connections to many nearby towns and cities.

  • Straubing Bus Station: Located about 40 kilometers south of Cham, Straubing Bus Station offers connections to many local and regional destinations.

Other Options

  • Cycling: Cham is easily accessible by bicycle from many nearby towns and cities.

How to Get Around in Cham

Cham is a relatively small and walkable town, but visitors can also take advantage of its many bicycle and bus routes.

Where to Go Next from Cham: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Regensburg: This beautiful town is known for its UNESCO-listed Old Town and historic landmarks, including Regensburg Cathedral and the Stone Bridge.

  • Straubing: This charming town is famous for its traditional Bavarian architecture, its many museums, and its local beer and food scene.

  • The Bavarian Forest National Park: This stunning national park covers over 200 square kilometers and offers a variety of hiking trails, scenic drives, and wildlife watching opportunities.

Conclusion

Cham is a town that truly offers something for everyone. Whether you’re a history buff, an outdoors enthusiast, a food lover, or all of the above, Cham has plenty of sights, experiences, and adventures to keep you entertained and inspired. We hope this guide has given you a taste of all that this beautiful town and its surrounding region has to offer.
