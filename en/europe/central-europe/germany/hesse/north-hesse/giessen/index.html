Introduction to Gießen

Located in North Hesse, Gießen is a picturesque city with a rich cultural heritage and beautiful natural landscapes. The city is known for its prestigious university, which draws in students from all over the world. Visitors to Gießen will find a charming mix of history, culture, and nature, making it a must-visit destination in Germany.

List of Places to Visit in Gießen

Gießen has a variety of attractions to offer visitors, including culturally important sites, beautiful natural landmarks, and exciting outdoor activities.

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Gießen has a long and fascinating history, and visitors can explore this heritage at the numerous museums and historical sites located throughout the city. Some of the must-visit cultural attractions in Gießen include:

  • Mathematikum: This museum is dedicated to all things mathematical. Visitors can participate in interactive exhibits and experiments, making it an excellent destination for families with children.

  • Liebig-Museum: This museum is dedicated to the life and work of Justus von Liebig, a famous German chemist who spent time teaching at the University of Gießen.

  • Stadttheater Gießen: Built in 1906, this theater is a beautiful example of neo-baroque architecture. Visitors can catch a variety of performances throughout the year, including opera, theater, ballet, and concerts.

Natural Attractions of Gießen: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

In addition to its rich history and culture, Gießen is also known for its beautiful natural landscapes. Visitors can explore the city’s natural beauty by visiting:

  • Botanischer Garten Gießen: This botanical garden is home to a variety of plants and flowers from around the world. The garden also features a greenhouse and a water garden.

  • Mathematikum Park: Located next to the Mathematikum, this park is a beautiful place to relax and take in the natural beauty of Gießen.

  • Schiffenberg: This hill overlooks the city of Gießen and offers a stunning panoramic view of the surrounding landscape. There are several hiking trails and picnic areas located on top of the hill.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Gießen

Gießen is also an excellent destination for outdoor enthusiasts. The city has numerous hiking and biking trails for visitors to explore, including:

  • Lahnwanderweg: This hiking trail follows the Lahn River and offers stunning views of the German countryside. The trail is approximately 290 kilometers long and is divided into 15 stages.

  • Lahntalradweg: This biking trail follows the Lahn River and offers breathtaking views of the German landscape. The trail is approximately 245 kilometers long and is divided into 14 stages.

Best Things to Do in Gießen: Activities and Tours

In addition to the natural and cultural attractions, visitors to Gießen can also enjoy a variety of activities and tours, including:

  • Walking tours: There are several walking tours available in Gießen that explore the city’s rich history and culture.

  • Boat tours: Visitors can explore the Lahn River by taking a boat tour of the area.

  • Wine tasting: The surrounding region is known for its excellent wines, and visitors can enjoy a wine tasting tour at one of the many local vineyards.

Week-Long Itinerary for Gießen

Food and Drink Scene of Gießen

Gießen has a rich culinary scene, with several local dishes and beverages worth trying.

Must-Try Dishes

  • Grüne Soße: Also known as green sauce, this dish is made with seven herbs and served with potatoes.

  • Handkäs with music: This is a local cheese served with raw onions and bread.

  • Sülze: This is a dish made from various meat parts, including tongue, heart, and ears.

Local Beverages

  • Apfelwein: This is a type of apple cider that is popular in the surrounding region.

  • Ebbelwoi: This is another type of apple cider, but it is a bit sweeter than Apfelwein.

What to Buy in Gießen: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Visitors to Gießen can find a variety of souvenirs and local products to bring home, including:

  • Local wines: The surrounding region is known for its excellent wines, and visitors can find a variety of local bottles to take home.

Local Culture and Customs of Gießen

Gießen has a unique culture and customs that visitors should be aware of. For example:

  • Punctuality: Germans are known for their punctuality, and visitors should make sure to be on time for appointments and meetings.

  • Respect for the Environment: Germans have a strong respect for the environment, and visitors should make sure to properly dispose of trash and conserve resources.

Best Time to Visit Gießen

The best time to visit Gießen is during the summer months, from May to September. During this time, the weather is mild, and there are many outdoor activities and festivals to enjoy.

Do’s and Don’ts of Gießen

Do:

  • Be polite: Germans place a high value on politeness, and visitors should make sure to be respectful and polite to locals.

  • Try the local food and drink: German cuisine is delicious, and visitors should make sure to try some of the local dishes and beverages.

Don’t:

  • Jaywalk: Germans take traffic laws seriously, and visitors should make sure to cross the street at designated crosswalks.

  • Be loud in public: Germans value peace and quiet in public spaces, and visitors should try to avoid being loud or disruptive.

Safety and Health

Safety in Gießen

Gießen is a safe city for visitors, but visitors should still take precautions to ensure their safety, such as:

  • Watch out for pickpockets: Visitors should be mindful of their belongings when in crowded areas.

  • Stick to well-lit areas at night: Visitors should avoid walking alone in dark or poorly lit areas.

Health in Gießen

Gießen has excellent healthcare facilities, and visitors should have no problem finding medical care if needed. However, visitors should still take precautions to avoid illness, such as:

  • Drink bottled water: Tap water in Germany is generally safe to drink, but visitors may prefer to stick to bottled water to avoid any potential issues.

  • Wash hands frequently: Visitors should make sure to wash their hands frequently to avoid spreading germs.

Getting to Gießen: Transportation Options and Logistics

Gießen is easily accessible by a variety of transportation options, including:

Airports Near Gießen

The closest airports to Gießen are Frankfurt Airport and Frankfurt-Hahn Airport. Both airports offer connections to major cities around the world.

Bus Stations Near Gießen

Bus travel is a convenient way to reach Gießen, with several bus stations located throughout the city. Some of the most popular stations include the Gießen Central Bus Station and the Gießen University Bus Station.

Other Options

Visitors can also reach Gießen by train or car, with several major highways and train stations located in the surrounding area.

How to Get Around in Gießen

Gießen is a compact city that is easily navigable on foot or by bike. Visitors can also use public transportation, such as buses or trains, to get around the city.

Where to Go Next from Gießen: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Visitors who have more time to explore the region can take day trips from Gießen to nearby destinations. Some popular options include:

  • Marburg: This picturesque town is located just a short distance from Gießen and is known for its beautiful architecture and historic university.

  • Frankfurt: One of Germany’s largest cities, Frankfurt is located less than an hour away from Gießen and offers a variety of cultural and historical attractions.

  • Heidelberg: This stunning university town is located approximately two hours away from Gießen and is known for its beautiful castle and charming old town.

Conclusion

Gießen is a charming city that offers a unique mix of history, culture, and nature. Visitors to Gießen can explore the city’s museums and historical sites, enjoy the natural beauty of the surrounding landscape, and indulge in delicious local cuisine. Whether you’re interested in hiking, biking, or simply relaxing in a beautiful setting, Gießen has something for everyone.
