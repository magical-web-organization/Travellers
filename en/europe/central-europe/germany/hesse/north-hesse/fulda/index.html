Introduction to Fulda

Fulda is a charming town located in North Hesse, Germany. It boasts a rich history and culture, stunning natural landscapes, and a vibrant food and drink scene. Fulda is often overlooked by travelers, but it is a hidden gem worth exploring.

In this comprehensive travel guide, we will take you through the top attractions, hiking and biking routes, must-try local dishes, best places to stay, and nearby excursions. You’ll also learn about the local customs and etiquette, safety and health tips, transportation options, and the best time to visit Fulda.

List of Places to Visit in Fulda

Fulda offers a plethora of attractions for travelers of all kinds. Here are some of the top places to visit in Fulda:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  1. Fulda Cathedral: This is the main attraction in Fulda and an excellent example of Baroque architecture. It was built in the 18th century and houses many artworks and treasures, including the tomb of St. Boniface, who was a Benedictine monk and missionary.

  2. Stadtschloss Fulda: This palace served as the residence of the Prince-Abbots of Fulda for over 250 years. It now houses the city museum, where you can learn about the history and culture of Fulda.

  3. Vonderau Museum: This museum showcases the history and art of the region, including the famous Fulda tapestries.

Natural Attractions of Fulda: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  1. Rhön Biosphere Reserve: This reserve is a paradise for nature lovers and outdoor enthusiasts. It boasts breathtaking landscapes, hiking trails, and wildlife.

  2. Wasserkuppe: This is the highest peak in the Rhön Mountains and a popular spot for hiking, paragliding, and skiing.

  3. Schlosstheater Fulda: This is a beautiful theatre located in the city centre. It hosts many cultural events, including plays, musicals, and concerts.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Fulda

Fulda is home to some of the most scenic hiking and biking routes in Germany. Here are some of the hidden gems that you must explore:

  1. Kassel-Fulda Cycle Path: This route takes you through the heart of Germany, along the Fulda River. You’ll enjoy breathtaking views of the countryside and charming villages.

  2. Rhön Rennsteig Trail: This trail is a must-do for hikers. It traverses the Rhön Mountains, offering stunning views of the surrounding landscape.

  3. Milseburg Panorama Trail: This trail takes you through the Milseburg region and offers some of the most beautiful views of the Rhön Mountains.

Best Things to Do in Fulda: Activities and Tours

Fulda offers numerous activities and tours for travelers. Here are some of the best things to do:

  1. Visit the Christmas Market: This is a must-do activity during the holiday season. The market is held in the city centre and is filled with twinkling lights, festive music, and delicious food and drinks.

  2. Take a Walking Tour: A walking tour of Fulda is an excellent way to learn about the city’s history and culture. You can discover the hidden gems, beautiful architecture, and charming streets.

  3. Fulda Brewery Tour: Germany is famous for its beer, and Fulda is no exception. Take a brewery tour and learn about the art of brewing beer while tasting some of the best beers in the region.

Food and Drink Scene of Fulda

Fulda offers delicious local cuisine that you must try during your visit. Here are some must-try dishes:

Must-Try Dishes

  1. Schlachteplatte: This is a traditional German dish that consists of smoked pork, sausages, and sauerkraut.

  2. Rolle mit Musik: This is another traditional German dish that’s a delicacy in Fulda. It’s made of beef or pork and is served with a tasty onion sauce.

  3. Fuldaer Plätzchen: This is a local dessert made of shortbread, buttercream, and apricot marmalade.

Local Beverages

  1. Fulda Beer: Fulda Brauhaus is the city’s oldest brewery and serves some of the best beers in the region.

  2. Apple Wine: This is a popular drink in the region and is made from locally grown apples.

Where to Stay in Fulda: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Fulda offers a variety of accommodation options that cater to all types of travelers. Here are some of the best places to stay in Fulda:

  1. Hotel Jägerklause: This is a charming hotel located in the city centre and is perfect for those who want to explore the city’s attractions.

  2. Maritim Hotel am Schlossgarten: This is a luxury hotel located near Stadtschloss Fulda and offers stunning views of the palace gardens.

  3. Sleep & Go Hotel: This is a budget-friendly hotel located near the train station and is perfect for backpackers and solo travelers.

What to Buy in Fulda: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Fulda is famous for its local products that make for excellent souvenirs. Here are some things that you should consider buying:

  1. Fuldaer Plätzchen: This local dessert makes for a perfect gift for your friends and family back home.

  2. Rhön Gin: This is a locally produced gin that’s made from regional herbs and spices.

  3. Handmade Crafts: Fulda has a thriving arts and crafts scene, where you can find unique handmade items such as pottery, textiles and woodwork.

Local Culture and Customs of Fulda

Fulda is a town with a rich history and culture. Here are some local customs and etiquette tips that you should be aware of:

  • Germans place a high value on punctuality, so make sure you’re on time for meetings and appointments.

  • When visiting someone’s home, always remove your shoes before entering.

  • It’s considered rude to interrupt someone while they’re speaking.

Best Time to Visit Fulda

The best time to visit Fulda is during the spring and summer months (April to September). The weather is mild, and the outdoor adventures are in full swing.

If you’re interested in experiencing Fulda’s winter wonderland, visit during the holiday season (December). The Christmas market is a must-see, and the town is decked out in twinkling lights and festive decorations.

Do’s and Don’ts of Fulda

Do:

  • Do try the local cuisine and beverages.

  • Do visit the Rhön Biosphere Reserve for outdoor adventures.

  • Do respect the local customs and etiquette.

Don’t:

  • Don’t be late for meetings and appointments.

  • Don’t forget to remove your shoes when entering someone’s home.

  • Don’t interrupt someone while they’re speaking.

Safety and Health

Safety in Fulda

Fulda is a safe town, and the crime rate is low. However, it’s always best to take some precautions, such as:

  • Keep your valuables, such as passport and money, in a safe place.

  • Avoid carrying a lot of cash with you.

  • Be aware of your surroundings and avoid dark, isolated areas at night.

Health in Fulda

Fulda has a good healthcare system, and you can find many pharmacies and hospitals in town. If you need medical assistance, you can dial 112 (emergency services) for help.

Getting to Fulda: Transportation Options and Logistics

Fulda is well-connected to other parts of Germany and Europe. Here are some transportation options:

Airports Near Fulda

  1. Frankfurt Airport: This is the closest international airport, and you can take a train or bus from the airport to Fulda.

  2. Kassel Airport: This is a regional airport located about 90 km from Fulda.

Bus Stations Near Fulda

  1. Fulda Bus Station: This is the main bus station in Fulda and connects the town to other cities and towns in the region.

  2. Frankfurt Central Bus Station: This is a major bus station in Frankfurt, and you can take a bus from here to Fulda.

Other Options

  • Train: Fulda has a train station that connects the town to other parts of Germany and Europe.

  • Car: You can also rent a car and drive to Fulda, which is a convenient option if you want to explore the surrounding region.

How to Get Around in Fulda

Fulda is a small town, and you can easily explore it on foot. However, if you want to visit the surrounding areas or do some outdoor adventures, you can rent a bike or a car. Local buses are also available for getting around town.

Where to Go Next from Fulda: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Fulda is a good base for exploring other parts of Germany and Europe. Here are some nearby destinations and excursions that you can consider:

  1. Frankfurt: This city is only an hour away by train and offers many cultural and historic attractions.

  2. Eisenach: This town is located in Thuringia and is famous for its medieval Wartburg Castle.

  3. Prague: If you’re looking for an international destination, consider a trip to Prague, which is only a four-hour drive from Fulda.

Conclusion

Fulda is a charming hidden gem in Germany, offering a perfect mix of history, culture, nature, and food and drink. We hope this comprehensive travel guide has helped you plan your trip to Fulda and discover the best things to see, do, eat, and where to stay. Safe travels!
