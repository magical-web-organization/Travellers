Introduction to Hanau

Located in Germany’s Central Europe, Hanau is a charming city brimming with historical significance, natural beauty, and cultural heritage. It is the birthplace of the famous brothers of Grimm fairy tale fame and has a rich history dating back to the 16th century. Hanau is an excellent destination for travelers looking for an off-beat, yet strikingly beautiful, German experience.

List of Places to Visit in Hanau

Here are some of the top attractions to visit in Hanau:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • The German Goldsmiths’ House Museum, showcasing the traditional goldsmithing techniques and the history of Hanau’s jewelry industry.
  • The Brüder Grimm Festival, an annual festival to celebrate the works and lives of the famous Grimm brothers.
  • The Hanau Art Museum, featuring a collection of German art from the 19th and 20th century.
  • Philippsruhe Castle, a palace dating back to the 18th century, now housing the Hanau Historical Museum.

Natural Attractions of Hanau: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Wilhelmsbad Park, a picturesque park with beautiful gardens, fountains, and a lake.
  • Kinzig River, offering picturesque views and opportunities for biking, hiking and boating activities.
  • Käthe-Kollwitz-Park, a beautiful park featuring a playground, a giant chessboard, and an open-air theater.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Hanau

Hanau offers plenty of opportunities for biking and hiking enthusiasts. Some of the best routes include:

  • Hessischer Radfernweg R3, a picturesque 360-mile bike trail passing through Hanau’s forests, meadows, and wine regions.
  • Main-Radweg, a popular route along the Main River, featuring scenic views and popular landmarks.
  • Grimmsteig, a hiking trail that leads to all the places related to the Grimm brothers in Hanau.

Best Things to Do in Hanau: Activities and Tours

  • Visit the tradition-steeped flea market at the Hanau Schlossplatz
  • Go on a culinary tour sampling Hanau’s traditional dishes
  • Take a guided tour of Hanau on a Segway, electric bike or rickshaw
  • Explore the Kinzig valley, either on foot, by bike or boat

Week-Long Itinerary for Hanau

Day 1: Visit the Goldsmiths’ House Museum, the Hanau Historical Museum, and Philippsruhe Castle.

Day 2: Explore the Wilhelmsbad Park, followed by a visit to Hanau’s Art Museum.

Day 3: Take a guided bike tour of Hanau’s surroundings, including the Main-Radweg and Kinzig River.

Day 4: Go on a culinary tour, tasting the traditional dishes of Hanau.

Day 5: Explore the Grimm brothers’ haunts, including the Grimm Fountain and the Grimm Monument.

Day 6: Take a Segway or electric bike tour of Hanau’s scenic routes, including the Käthe-Kollwitz-Park.

Day 7: Take a day trip to nearby attractions, such as Frankfurt, Würzburg or Heidelberg.

Food and Drink Scene of Hanau

Hanau’s food and drink scene is heavily influenced by traditional German cuisine, including local favorites such as:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Frankfurter Schnitzel, a breaded and fried pork dish
  • Grankuchen, a type of potato pancake
  • Bethmännchen, a traditional almond pastry

Local Beverages

  • Apfelwein, a type of hard cider
  • German beer, with a variety of local breweries and pubs

Where to Stay in Hanau: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Hanau offers a range of accommodation options to suit all budgets and preferences. Some of the best options include:

  • Best Western Premier Hotel, a four-star hotel located close to the city center.
  • Hotel zur Linde, a charming hotel located in the picturesque town of Steinheim.
  • Jugendherberge Hanau, a budget-friendly hostel located on the banks of the Kinzig river.

What to Buy in Hanau: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Hanau is known for its traditional German handicrafts and jewelry. Some of the best local products to buy include:

  • Gold and silverware, including traditional German mettwurst dishes and beer steins.
  • Glassware and ceramics, including hand-blown glass and traditional German ceramics.

Local Culture and Customs of Hanau

Hanau has a rich cultural heritage, with a number of traditional festivals and customs celebrated throughout the year. Some of the most popular events include:

  • The Brüder Grimm Festival, a week-long festival celebrating the works and lives of the Grimm brothers.
  • The Schlossplatz-Fest, a traditional German folk festival held in Hanau’s central square.
  • The annual Hanau Wine Festival, celebrating the region’s wine-producing heritage.

Best Time to Visit Hanau

The best time to visit Hanau is from May to September, when the weather is warm and sunny, and the city is buzzing with cultural events and festivals. The winter months are typically colder, with the possibility of snow, but offer the chance to experience the city’s charming Christmas markets.

Do’s and Don’ts of Hanau

Do:

  • Do visit the traditional flea market on Hanau’s Schlossplatz
  • Do try the traditional local dishes and beverages
  • Do take a guided tour of Hanau’s scenic routes

Don’t:

  • Don’t forget to bring comfortable walking shoes, as the city’s historical sites and museums are best explored on foot.
  • Don’t forget to bring cash, as some of the local markets and restaurants may not accept credit cards.
  • Don’t forget to pack for the weather as the German climate varies throughout the year.

Safety and Health

Safety in Hanau

Hanau is a safe city with low crime rates. However, travelers should still take the necessary precautions, such as keeping valuables safe and being aware of their surroundings.

Health in Hanau

Hanau has a good healthcare system. There are several hospitals and clinics throughout the city, and most healthcare services are covered by German national health insurance.

Getting to Hanau: Transportation Options and Logistics

Hanau is easily accessible by various transportation options, including:

Airports Near Hanau

  • Frankfurt Airport, the largest international airport in Germany, is located approximately 15 miles from Hanau.

Bus Stations Near Hanau

  • Hanau Bus Station, located in the city center, offers connections to various destinations in Germany.

Other Options

  • Hanau is easily accessible by train, with several direct connections to Frankfurt and other major cities in Germany.

How to Get Around in Hanau

Hanau is a compact city, and most of its attractions are within walking distance of each other. However, travelers can also take buses, trains, or taxis to explore the city.

Where to Go Next from Hanau: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Frankfurt, a bustling metropolis with world-class museums, shopping, and nightlife.
  • Würzburg, a beautiful town with a rich baroque history and a scenic wine region.
  • Heidelberg, a charming city with a picturesque castle, historical university, and a bustling old town.

Conclusion

Hanau is a hidden gem in the heart of Germany, offering travelers a unique blend of cultural heritage, natural beauty, and traditional cuisine. Whether you’re exploring the city’s historic sites and museums, hiking and biking in its scenic surroundings, or immersing yourself in its local festivals and customs, Hanau is sure to leave you with lasting memories.
