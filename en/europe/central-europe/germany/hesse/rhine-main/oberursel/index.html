Introduction to Oberursel

Located in the heart of Rhine-Main in the Hesse region of Germany, Oberursel offers a satisfying blend of history, culture, and nature. The town is situated in the idyllic Taunus mountain range, providing a diverse landscape that’s waiting to be explored. From picturesque hiking and biking routes to museums and historical attractions, Oberursel has something for everyone. Here is a comprehensive travel guide that will help you plan your next visit to this charming German town.

List of Places to Visit in Oberursel

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Oberursel has various museums and historical attractions that highlight its rich cultural heritage. The following list covers the most significant landmarks to witness:

  • St. Ursula Church: Located in the town center, this church was built in the 14th century and combines Gothic and Romanesque architectural styles.

  • Marktplatz: This is the central plaza of Oberursel, surrounded by historic buildings like the Rathaus (town hall). There’s also a weekly farmers market that takes place here every Saturday.

  • Hohemark Railway: A historic railway that runs from Oberursel to the plateau of the Taunus Mountains. You can take a ride on this scenic railway to experience the beautiful landscape of Taunus mountain range.

  • Taunus Wunderland: A family-friendly adventure park that has a range of thrilling rides, shows, and activities. The perfect spot for a fun day out with family and friends.

Natural Attractions of Oberursel: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

If you love the great outdoors, Oberursel has plenty to offer. There are various parks, hiking trails, and nature reserves in the area that offer stunning views and plenty of outdoor adventures. Here are some top picks:

  • Opel Zoo: A delightful zoo that houses over 1,500 animals from all around the world. Here, visitors can see exotic creatures such as pandas, tigers, elephants, and bears.

  • Altkönig Mountain: The highest peak in the Taunus range, offering breathtaking views of the surrounding landscape. A perfect spot for a scenic hike or picnic.

  • Hohemark: A popular hiking and biking route that runs across the Taunus Mountains, connecting several towns and villages in the area.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Oberursel

Oberursel offers various hiking and biking routes, catering to all skill levels. Here are some top picks, starting from easy to difficult:

  • Altkönig Hiking Trail: A leisurely 2.5-hour hike with breathtaking views of the Taunus Mountains.

  • Hohemark to Feldberg: A moderate 4-hour hike that takes you past some of the most scenic spots in the Taunus Mountains.

  • Taunus Enduro Trail: A challenging 40km biking trail that takes you through narrow forest paths and rocky terrain.

Best Things to Do in Oberursel: Activities and Tours

In addition to hiking and biking, there are various other activities and tours that visitors can enjoy in Oberursel. Here are some top picks:

  • Hot Air Balloon Ride: A unique way to experience the stunning views of Oberursel and the Taunus Mountains.

  • Wine Tasting Tour: Take a guided tour of the vineyards surrounding Oberursel and sample some of the region’s famous wines.

  • Oberursel Nightlife: The town has various pubs, bars, and clubs that offer live music and fun-filled evenings.

Week-Long Itinerary for Oberursel

Here is a sample itinerary that covers the best of Oberursel in a week:

Day 1: Visit the St. Ursula Church and Marktplatz and explore the town center.

Day 2: Take a ride on the Hohemark railway and visit the Opel Zoo.

Day 3: Hike the Altkönig trail and enjoy a picnic on the mountain top.

Day 4: Take a guided tour of the vineyards around Oberursel and indulge in a wine tasting session.

Day 5: Go on a hot air balloon ride and witness the stunning views of the town and the Taunus Mountains.

Day 6: Visit the Taunus Wunderland adventure park and enjoy the thrilling rides and activities.

Day 7: Relax and unwind at one of the town’s spa centers.

Food and Drink Scene of Oberursel

Oberursel has a rich culinary scene, with a range of local and international dishes to try. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Frankfurter Grüne Sauce: A signature dish in the region, made with seven types of herbs, boiled eggs, and potatoes.

  • Apfelwein: A local apple wine, served in traditional ceramic jugs called Bembel.

  • Frankfurter Würstchen: A type of sausage that originates from the Frankfurt region.

Local Beverages

  • Licher Pils: A local beer that’s mild and refreshing.

  • Kelterei Heil: A local cider made from freshly pressed apples.

Where to Stay in Oberursel: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Oberursel has various accommodation options to suit all budget ranges. Here are some top picks:

  • Hotel Central: A modern hotel in the town center, offering comfortable rooms and easy access to nearby attractions.

  • Altkönig Lodge: A cozy guesthouse that’s perfect for a romantic getaway, located in the heart of the Taunus Mountains.

  • Jugendherberge Oberursel: A budget-friendly hostel that’s ideal for backpackers and solo travelers.

What to Buy in Oberursel: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

There are various souvenirs and local products that visitors can take home as a memory of their trip to Oberursel. Here are some top picks:

  • Frankfurt Kitchen Towel: A traditional kitchen towel that’s unique to the Frankfurt region.

  • Bembel: A ceramic jug that’s used to serve Apfelwein.

  • Handmade Soap: These soaps are made using locally sourced ingredients and are available in various fragrances.

Local Culture and Customs of Oberursel

Oberursel is a town that’s proud of its cultural heritage and has various customs and traditions that have been passed down from generation to generation. Here are some key customs and traditions:

  • Fasching: A carnival celebration that takes place in February or March. The town center comes alive with music, dance, and colorful costumes.

  • Apfelwein Festival: A festival that celebrates the region’s famous apple wine.

  • Maypole: A tradition where a wooden pole is erected in the town center to celebrate the arrival of spring.

Best Time to Visit Oberursel

The best time to visit Oberursel is from May to September when the weather is pleasant, and the town is buzzing with outdoor events and activities. However, the town is also charming during the winter months, with Christmas markets and snow-covered landscapes making for a magical experience.

Do’s and Don’ts of Oberursel

Do:

  • Learn a few German phrases before your trip to make your interactions with locals smoother.

  • Respect the town’s customs and traditions.

  • Remember to tip your servers at restaurants.

Don’t:

  • Smoke in public areas as it’s considered rude.

  • Take photographs of people without their permission.

  • Litter or damage public property.

Safety and Health

Safety in Oberursel

Oberursel is a relatively safe town, with low crime rates. However, visitors are advised to take standard precautions like keeping their valuables safe and being aware of their surroundings at all times.

Health in Oberursel

Oberursel has well-equipped medical centers and hospitals that offer excellent medical care. Visitors are advised to carry travel insurance to cover any unforeseen medical emergencies.

Getting to Oberursel: Transportation Options and Logistics

Here are the various transportation options available to reach Oberursel:

Airports Near Oberursel

  • Frankfurt Airport: Located about 22 km away from Oberursel, it’s the closest international airport.

  • Hahn Airport: Located about 110 km away, but offers budget airlines and charter flights.

Bus Stations Near Oberursel

  • Oberursel Train Station: The town has a well-connected train and bus station that links it to other parts of Germany and Europe.

Other Options

  • Rental Cars: Visitors can rent cars from Frankfurt airport or local car rental companies.

  • Taxis: Taxis are readily available for hire at the airport and train station.

How to Get Around in Oberursel

Oberursel is a small town that’s easy to navigate on foot or bicycle. However, there are also buses and taxis available for visitors who prefer public transportation.

Where to Go Next from Oberursel: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Oberursel’s central location makes it an ideal base to explore other parts of the Rhine-Main region. Here are some nearby destinations and excursions to consider:

  • Frankfurt: A bustling metropolis that’s just a short train ride away from Oberursel.

  • Bad Homburg: A charming spa town that’s known for its luxurious spas and wellness centers.

  • Römerberg: A historic town that’s famous for its well-preserved medieval architecture.

Conclusion

Oberursel is a hidden gem in the Rhine-Main region, offering a blend of history, culture, and nature. From scenic hikes and bike routes to museums and historical landmarks, there’s something for everyone in this charming German town. With this comprehensive travel guide, you’ll have all the information you need to plan your next trip to Oberursel.
