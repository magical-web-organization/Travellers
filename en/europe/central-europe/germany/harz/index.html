Introduction to Harz

Harz is a breathtaking region located in Central Germany, famous for its unspoiled natural beauty, cultural treasures, and rich history. The region encompasses the Harz Mountains, which stretch for over 100 km through Lower Saxony, Saxony-Anhalt, and Thuringia. This mountain range has several peaks that reach more than 1,000 meters in height, making it an ideal destination for hikers, bikers, and nature lovers.

Harz is also well-known for its picturesque villages, historic towns, and cultural landmarks. It has numerous museums, galleries, and historic sites that offer a fascinating glimpse into the region’s past. Moreover, Harz has a lively food scene that showcases local cuisine and traditional dishes.

In this Harz travel guide, we’ll take you through the region’s must-see attractions, hiking and biking routes, cultural and natural landmarks, local customs, and logistics. Whether you’re looking for adventure, relaxation, or a mix of both, Harz has something for everyone.

List of Places to Visit in Harz

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums, Castles, and Historical Places

Harz region has a rich cultural heritage that is evident through its numerous museums, historic sites, and castles.

  • Harz National Park: This national park is spread over 247 km2 and is home to many rare and endangered species, including lynx, wildcats, and the black stork. It has many hiking trails, educational exhibits, and guided tours that showcase Harz’s unique ecosystem.
  • Wernigerode Castle: This castle is perched high atop a hill and offers breathtaking views of the region. It’s a prime example of Gothic architecture, and it houses a museum with a vast collection of historic artifacts.
  • Goslar Old Town: This medieval town is a UNESCO World Heritage Site and is home to several beautiful half-timbered houses, historic churches, and museums. It also has a rich mining history that is evident through its numerous mines, shafts, and industrial sites.
  • Quedlinburg: This town features over 1,000 timber-framed houses and narrow cobblestone streets that are lined with cafes, restaurants, and boutiques.
  • Brocken: This is one of the most famous peaks in Harz, offering a spectacular view of the region. It’s accessible by a scenic railway that runs through the mountain range.

Natural Attractions of Harz: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Harz’s natural beauty is unparalleled, and there are several outdoor activities that visitors can enjoy, including hiking, biking, skiing, and camping.

  • Rappbode Dam: This is the largest dam in Germany, and it offers a wide range of water sports, including boating, kayaking, and fishing. It also has a beautiful beach and scenic walking trails.
  • Bodetal: This valley is renowned for its stunning rock formations, deep gorges, and rich flora and fauna. It’s an ideal destination for hiking, mountain biking, and rock climbing.
  • Harz Narrow Gauge Railway: This scenic train ride offers breathtaking views of the region and runs through picturesque towns and villages.
  • Hexentanzplatz: This mountain plateau offers panoramic views of the region and has several hiking trails and an open-air theater that features folklore performances.
  • Baumwipfelpfad: This is a treetop walk that offers a unique perspective of Harz’s natural beauty. It spans over 1,000 meters and features several observation points and interactive exhibits.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Harz

Harz is a paradise for hikers and bikers, and there are several well-marked trails that visitors can explore. Here are a few of the most popular routes in the region:

  • Harzer Hexenstieg: This trail is 94 km long and runs through the Harz Mountains, offering breathtaking views of the valleys, peaks, and forests. It’s a challenging route that takes around six days to complete.
  • Harz Witches’ Trail: This trail is 135 km long and takes visitors through several cultural landmarks, including castles, museums, and historic villages. It’s an easy route that takes around ten days to complete.
  • Rennsteig Trail: This trail is 170 km long and takes visitors through Thuringia’s dense forests, offering spectacular views of the region. It’s a challenging route that takes around ten days to complete.
  • Harz Mountain Bike Trail: This trail is 98 km long and takes visitors through the Harz Mountains, offering a mix of challenging terrain and scenic views. It’s a popular route among mountain bikers, and it takes around three days to complete.

Best Things to Do in Harz: Activities and Tours

Harz offers a wide range of activities, tours, and experiences that visitors can enjoy. Here are a few of the most popular ones:

  • Skiing: Harz has several ski resorts, including Braunlage, Wurmberg, and Hohegeiß, that offer excellent skiing conditions during the winter months.
  • Rock Climbing: Bodetal valley is an excellent destination for rock climbing enthusiasts, offering a variety of climbing routes and impressive rock formations.
  • Harzquerbahn: This traditional steam train offers an exceptional experience through Harz’s picturesque landscape.
  • Gastronomy Tour: Harz is famous for its traditional cuisine, and visitors can take a gastronomy tour to discover local flavors and dishes.
  • Spa and Wellness: Harz has several wellness resorts that offer a range of treatments and therapies, including massages, saunas, and hot springs.

Week-Long Itinerary for Harz

Here’s a sample week-long itinerary for visitors who want to explore Harz’s best attractions:

Day 1: Explore the Harz National Park and take a guided tour to discover the region’s unique ecosystem.

Day 2: Visit the Wernigerode Castle and take a walk around the town’s picturesque streets.

Day 3: Hike through the Bodetal valley and enjoy its stunning rock formations and deep gorges.

Day 4: Take a ride on the Harz Narrow Gauge Railway and enjoy the scenic views of the mountains and valleys.

Day 5: Ski at one of Harz’s excellent ski resorts and enjoy the winter sports.

Day 6: Visit Goslar Old Town and discover its rich mining history.

Day 7: Take a gastronomy tour and taste Harz’s traditional cuisine and beverages.

Food and Drink Scene of Harz

Harz’s gastronomy is a unique blend of traditional cuisine and local ingredients. Here are a few must-try dishes and beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Harzer Cheese: This is a local cheese made from sour milk and tastes best when served with bread.
  • Kassler: This is a smoked pork chop that is seasoned with bay leaves, juniper berries, and garlic.
  • Rote Grütze: This is a popular dessert made from red berries, sugar, and starch.
  • Klöße: These are dumplings made from potatoes and flour, often served with meat dishes.

Local Beverages

  • Kalterer: This is a local wine made from the red grape variety Schiava Grossa.
  • Gose: This is a slightly sour beer that is brewed with coriander and salt.
  • Apfelwein: This is a local apple cider that is often served in small, stoneware jugs.

Where to Stay in Harz: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Harz offers a wide range of accommodation options that cater to different budgets and preferences. Here are a few of the best options:

  • Schlosshotel Blankenburg: This castle-turned-hotel offers a luxurious stay and is located amidst beautiful gardens and forests.
  • Hexenstieg Hostel: This hostel is located close to the Harzer Hexenstieg trail and offers affordable accommodation for hikers and bikers.
  • Landhaus Foresta: This guesthouse is located in a picturesque village and offers comfortable rooms and excellent food.

What to Buy in Harz: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Harz has several unique products that visitors can bring back home as souvenirs. Here are a few of the best options:

  • Hand-painted Easter Eggs: These are a local specialty, they are traditional painted by hand and are a lovely to bring back home as a souvenir.
  • Harzer Cheese: This is a local cheese made from sour milk and is well-packaged for travel.

Local Culture and Customs of Harz

Harz has a rich cultural heritage that is celebrated through its music, dance, art, and festivals. The region has several folk traditions, including the Walpurgis Night festival, which celebrates the arrival of spring.

The region is also famous for its mining heritage, which is evident through its many mines, shafts, and industrial sites. Visitors can learn about this history by exploring the region’s mining museums and historic sites.

Best Time to Visit Harz

Harz is a year-round tourist destination, but the best time to visit depends on your interests. The summer months offer excellent hiking and biking conditions, while the winter months offer skiing and snowboarding opportunities.

Moreover, Harz’s many festivals and cultural events make it an ideal destination throughout the year. The Walpurgis Night festival in April and the Christmas markets in December are particularly popular.

Do’s and Don’ts of Harz

Do:

  • Take time to explore Harz’s natural beauty and outdoor activities.
  • Try the local cuisine and beverages.
  • Respect the local customs and traditions.
  • Take a guided tour to learn about the region’s rich history.

Don’t:

  • Litter or damage the region’s natural habitat.
  • Disrespect the local culture or customs.
  • Ignore safety guidelines while hiking or biking.

Safety and Health

Safety in Harz

Harz is generally a safe region, but visitors are advised to take normal precautions to avoid theft and other petty crimes. It’s also essential to respect the region’s natural environment and follow all safety guidelines while hiking, biking, or enjoying other outdoor activities.

Health in Harz

Harz has excellent healthcare facilities, and visitors can avail of medical services in case of an emergency. It’s also essential to carry adequate travel insurance before visiting the region.

Getting to Harz: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Harz

The nearest airports to Harz are:

  • Leipzig/Halle Airport
  • Hanover Airport
  • Berlin Brandenburg Airport

Bus Stations Near Harz

Harz is well-connected by bus services, and there are several bus stations in the region, including:

  • Wernigerode Bus Station
  • Goslar Bus Station
  • Quedlinburg Bus Station

Other Options

Visitors can also reach Harz by train, car, or private transfer. Some of the popular routes include:

  • From Berlin to Harz: 2 h 30 min (by car)
  • From Hanover to Harz: 1 h 30 min (by car)
  • From Leipzig to Harz: 2 h (by train)

How to Get Around in Harz

Harz has an extensive public transport system that includes buses and trains. Visitors can also rent a car or hire a taxi to explore the region’s many attractions. Additionally, biking and hiking are excellent options for discovering the region’s natural beauty.

Where to Go Next from Harz: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Harz is surrounded by several exciting destinations that visitors can explore. Here are a few of the best options:

  • Berlin: This vibrant city is located approximately two hours away from Harz and is renowned for its art, culture, and nightlife.
  • Dresden: This city is home to several historic landmarks, including the impressive Dresden Zwinger Palace and the Frauenkirche church.
  • Magdeburg: This city features many attractions, including the Magdeburg Cathedral, the Green Citadel, and the Elbauenpark.

Conclusion

Harz is a hidden gem in Central Germany that offers a unique blend of natural beauty, cultural landmarks, and outdoor activities. This travel guide has showcased some of the region’s must-visit attractions, hiking and biking routes, food and drink scene, local customs, and logistics. Whether you’re planning a day-trip or a week-long adventure, Harz is an ideal destination for visitors who want to experience the best of Germany.
