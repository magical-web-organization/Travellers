Introduction to Stralsund

Stralsund is a beautiful and historic city located in the state of Mecklenburg-Western Pomerania, Germany. Founded in the 13th century, the city is known for its well-preserved architecture, historic sites, and cultural attractions. Stralsund is also situated in close proximity to the Baltic Sea, which provides visitors with opportunities for outdoor recreation. This comprehensive tour guide to Stralsund will provide you with all the necessary information to make the most of your visit.

List of Places to Visit in Stralsund

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

St. Mary’s Church

St. Mary’s Church is one of the most significant landmarks in Stralsund and is known for its impressive Gothic architecture. The church was built in the 14th century and is a UNESCO World Heritage Site.

German Oceanographic Museum

The German Oceanographic Museum is a must-visit attraction in Stralsund and is one of the largest marine biology museums in Europe. Visitors can explore a wide range of exhibits related to oceanography, marine ecosystems, and marine life.

Stralsund City Museum

The Stralsund City Museum is an excellent place to learn about the city’s history, culture, and traditions. The museum features several exhibits, including paintings, sculptures, and historical artifacts.

Natural Attractions of Stralsund: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Stralsund Beach

Located on the Baltic Sea, Stralsund beach is a popular destination for sunbathing, swimming, and water sports. The beach is also surrounded by picturesque landscapes and offers stunning views of the sea.

Knieper Wall

The Knieper Wall is a historic wall surrounding the old town of Stralsund. Visitors can take a leisurely walk along the wall, which offers breathtaking views of the harbor and the neighboring islands.

Rügen Island

Rügen Island is the largest island in Germany, situated just off the coast near Stralsund. The island is known for its natural beauty, white sandy beaches, and stunning landscapes.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Stralsund

Stralsund offers several hiking and biking routes that provide an opportunity to explore the city’s natural beauty and scenic landscapes. The most popular routes include the Altefähr - Stralsund cycle path, Rügen cycle path, and the Sundische Wiesen nature reserve trail.

Best Things to Do in Stralsund: Activities and Tours

Guided City Tour

Join a guided city tour to learn more about Stralsund’s history and culture. The tour covers the city’s main attractions and is an excellent way to explore the city in-depth.

Sailing Tour

Explore Stralsund and the neighboring islands on a sailing tour. Enjoy the sea breeze and stunning views of the coastline as you sail across the Baltic Sea.

Scuba Diving

Embark on an adventurous scuba diving trip to explore the reefs and marine life beneath the Baltic’s crystal-clear waters.

Week-Long Itinerary for Stralsund

Day 1: Explore the historic old town of Stralsund, visit St. Mary’s Church, and the German Oceanographic Museum.

Day 2: Take a guided city tour, visit the Stralsund City Museum, and enjoy some shopping in the city’s old town.

Day 3: Visit the Knieper Wall, take a stroll along the Sundische Wiesen nature reserve trail, and catch a sunset at Stralsund beach

Day 4: Visit the neighboring island of Rügen and explore the Jasmund National Park, located on the northern tip of the island.

Day 5: Embark on a sailing tour of Stralsund and its neighboring islands.

Day 6: Take a scuba diving trip to explore the marine life beneath the Baltic.

Day 7: Enjoy a day trip to the Hanseatic city of Greifswald, located just 30 minutes away from Stralsund.

Food and Drink Scene of Stralsund

Must-Try Dishes

Stralsund Pasty

Stralsund Pasty is a well-known local dish, consisting of a pastry filled with beef, onions, and bacon. It is a perfect snack for on-the-go.

Fischbrotchen

Fischbrotchen is a fresh fish sandwich made using locally sourced fish, bread, and vegetables. This local dish is a fresher and healthier option for those who like to eat light.

Local Beverages

Pilsner

Pilsner is a popular local beer with a crisp and refreshing flavor. It makes it perfect for winding down after a long and tiresome day.

Riesenbecher

A Riesenbecher (giant scoop) is an ice cream specialty, which is a must-try when in Stralsund. It is a healthier snack for those with a sweet tooth.

Where to Stay in Stralsund: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Hotel Scheelehof

Hotel Scheelehof is a luxurious hotel located in the heart of old town Stralsund. It offers spacious rooms, an on-site spa, and a restaurant.

Hostel Alter Fritz

Hostel Alter Fritz is a budget-friendly hostel located in the center of Stralsund. It offers comfortable rooms, a communal kitchen, and a game room.

Pension Altstadt Mönch

Pension Altstadt Mönch is a charming guesthouse situated in the heart of the old town. It offers cozy and comfortable rooms, free Wi-Fi, and a delicious breakfast.

What to Buy in Stralsund: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Amber

Amber is a fossilized tree resin that is known for its beauty and rarity. Stralsund is known for its amber, and visitors can buy amber jewelry, souvenirs, and ornaments from local shops.

Stralsunder Pilsner

Stralsunder Pilsner is a popular local beer that can be purchased from local liquor stores. It makes for the perfect souvenir for beer lovers.

Local Culture and Customs of Stralsund

Stralsund has a rich culture and traditions deeply ingrained in its historical roots, which are still celebrated today. The annual Störtebeker Festival, which celebrates the life of a legendary pirate, is an example of the city’s vibrant cultural scene that is worth experiencing when in the city.

Best Time to Visit Stralsund

The best time to visit Stralsund is during the summer months, from June to August, when the weather is warm and sunny. Visitors can make the most of outdoor activities and events during this period.

Do’s and Don’ts of Stralsund

Do:

  1. Dress for the weather
  2. Visit the German Oceanographic Museum
  3. Explore the city’s historic old town
  4. Sample the local cuisine

Don’t:

  1. Litter in public areas
  2. Wear shoes inside homes
  3. Be rude to locals
  4. Drink and drive

Safety and Health

Safety in Stralsund

Stralsund is a safe city to visit. Visitors are recommended to be cautious and vigilant of their belongings when in crowded areas.

Health in Stralsund

Stralsund has an efficient and well-equipped healthcare system. Visitors are advised to have valid health insurance to avail of medical assistance in case of emergencies.

Getting to Stralsund: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Stralsund

Rostock-Laage Airport is the nearest airport serving Stralsund, located approximately 83 kilometers away, and operates several daily domestic and international flights.

Bus Stations Near Stralsund

Stralsund Bus Station connects the city with several destinations across Germany and is located near the city’s railway station.

Other Options

Stralsund is well-connected with the rest of Germany via rail and road.

How to Get Around in Stralsund

Stralsund is a compact city that can be easily explored on foot. Bikes and public transport (trams and buses) are also readily available for those who need them.

Where to Go Next from Stralsund: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Hanseatic city of Greifswald

The Hanseatic city of Greifswald is located just 30 minutes away from Stralsund and is known for its historic buildings, museums, and beautiful parks.

Jasmund National Park

Jasmund National Park is situated on Rügen Island, known for its unique chalk cliffs and virgin beech forests that attract hikers and nature enthusiasts alike.

Conclusion

Stralsund is a vibrant and charming city that offers visitors a perfect blend of history, culture, and natural beauty. This comprehensive tour guide has provided all the necessary information to make the most of your visit to Stralsund, Germany. So, plan your trip now and immerse yourself in the city’s splendid beauty and unique culture.
