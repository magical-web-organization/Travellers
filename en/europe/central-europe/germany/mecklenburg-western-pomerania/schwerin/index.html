Introduction to Schwerin

Located in the Mecklenburg-Western Pomerania region of Germany, Schwerin is a charming city surrounded by beautiful lakes and forests. The city’s rich history and cultural significance make it a popular tourist destination for those interested in exploring the country’s fascinating past and present.

List of Places to Visit in Schwerin

Schwerin offers a host of exciting places to visit that cater to all kinds of interests. From culturally significant museums and historical sites to scenic natural attractions, there is something for everyone to enjoy.

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Schwerin is home to several museums and historical sites that offer a glimpse into the city’s rich cultural heritage. Some of these major tourist attractions include:

  • Schwerin Castle: One of the most visited tourist destinations in Schwerin, this remarkable castle sits on an island in the middle of the lake. It boasts an impressive collection of art, furniture, and armory, dating back to the 16th-century.

  • Mecklenburg State Theatre: Located in the heart of Schwerin, the Mecklenburg State Theatre is renowned for its stunning stage productions, featuring highly talented actors and performers.

  • Schwerin Cathedral: Built-in 1260, Schwerin Cathedral is one of the oldest buildings in the city. Its stunning Gothic architecture, including the ornate stained glass windows, is a must-see for any visitor to Schwerin.

Natural Attractions of Schwerin: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Schwerin also has a lot of natural attractions to offer to its visitors, from outdoor adventures to scenic views. Some of the top-rated natural sites to explore in Schwerin include:

  • Schwerin Lake: A picturesque lake that serves as the focal point of the city, this stunning body of water offers a peaceful respite from the hustle and bustle of the city life. You can take a boat tour around the lake and marvel at the beautiful architecture and scenery as you experience the tranquility of Schwerin.

  • Palace Garden: Adjacent to the Schwerin Castle, the Palace Garden is a beautiful park characterized by lush lawns, magnificent trees, and exotic flowers.

  • Zoo Schwerin: For those who are fond of animals, the Zoo Schwerin is the perfect tourist attraction with its various species of animals such as lions, elephants, and monkeys.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Schwerin

Schwerin is the perfect destination for outdoor enthusiasts, with a range of remarkable hiking and biking trails that wind their way through the city’s stunning landscape. Here are a few of the most popular ones:

  • The Schwerin Lakes Trail: A 72 km long cycling and hiking trail around the Schwerin lake that takes you through dense forests, charming villages, and captivating shorelines.

  • Bathing Beach Loop: A shorter but no less scenic trail that winds its way around the city’s bathing beach, offering spectacular views of the lake and coastline.

  • Schwerin Circular Trail: An easy to walk trail that offers an excellent introduction to the beauty of the city and takes you to significant cultural and natural points of interest.

Best Things to Do in Schwerin: Activities and Tours

Apart from hiking, biking, and sightseeing, Schwerin offers various activities and tours for visitors to enjoy. Here are a few of the most popular ones:

  • Canoeing on Schwerin Lake: Visitors can rent a canoe and paddle around the scenic Schwerin Lake, soaking up the serene surroundings while getting some exercise.

  • Schwerin Night Watchman Tour: A unique way of discovering the city’s history, the night watchman tour takes you on a walk around the city at night, with the guide dressed in full medieval costume and regaling you with tales of the city’s fascinating past.

  • The Schwerin Christmas Market: Held every year, the Schwerin Christmas market is a marvelous place to soak up the festive spirit and enjoy traditional German treats.

Week-Long Itinerary for Schwerin

For those looking to spend a week in Schwerin, here is an itinerary that covers some of the city’s top tourist attractions:

Day 1: Visit the Schwerin Castle and explore the art, architecture, and history of one of Germany’s most famous castles.

Day 2: Take a stroll through the Palace Garden, visit the Schwerin State Museum, and experience one of the stunning productions at the Mecklenburg State Theatre.

Day 3: Rent a bike and explore the scenic Schwerin Lakes Trail and discover the beauty of the city’s surrounding countryside.

Day 4: Visit the Zoo Schwerin, where you can see a range of exotic animals and learn about their habitats and lifestyles.

Day 5: Take a boat tour around Schwerin Lake, marveling at the stunning architecture and picturesque scenery surrounding it.

Day 6: Visit one of the city’s many charming cafes and indulge in some traditional German cuisine.

Day 7: Take a day trip to nearby cities like Rostock, Lubeck, or Hamburg, and explore the cultural and historical significance of these landmark locations.

Food and Drink Scene of Schwerin

Schwerin has a diverse and fascinating food and drink scene that boasts authentic German cuisine as well as international flavors.

Must-Try Dishes

  • Potthucke: A traditional local dish made of grated potatoes, butter, onion, and bacon, Potthucke is a savory and filling meal ideal for cold winter days.

  • Eisbein: A pork knuckle dish that is slow-roasted until tender and served with sauerkraut and potatoes.

  • Rote Grütze: A popular German dessert made of red fruit like cherries, raspberries, and strawberries, served with a sweet vanilla cream or ice cream.

Local Beverages

  • Beer: Germany is known for its excellent beer, and Schwerin is no exception. Traditional brews such as Mecklenburg Pils and Schweriner Pils are beloved by locals and visitors alike.

  • Mecklenburg-Schwerin Schnaps: A popular local liqueur that comes in many flavors like plum, cherry, and raspberry.

Where to Stay in Schwerin: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Schwerin has a wide range of accommodation options, from luxury hotels to affordable hostels and guesthouses. Here are a few that are highly recommended:

  • IntercityHotel Schwerin: A stylish hotel in a central location, boasting comfortable rooms, polite staff, and all the amenities you need for a comfortable stay.

  • Pension am Schloss: A cozy bed and breakfast near the Schwerin Castle that offers a delicious breakfast and panoramic views of the city.

  • Hostel Schwerin: A budget-friendly hostel situated in the heart of the city that provides a comfortable and friendly environment for visitors.

What to Buy in Schwerin: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

From local handicrafts, food, and drink to souvenirs that capture the essence of Schwerin culture, there are plenty of things to buy and bring home as a reminder of your trip. Here are a few top picks:

  • Mecklenburg-Schwerin Schnaps: The locally made liqueur is an excellent choice to bring home as a reminder of your visit to Schwerin.

  • Handmade Soaps: Schwerin is famous for its handmade soaps, made with all-natural ingredients and delightful scents that remind you of nature.

Local Culture and Customs of Schwerin

Schwerin is a city with a rich and fascinating culture, renowned for its art and architecture, music, food, and drink, and more. Visitors should be aware of some local customs and etiquette, such as:

  • Dress code: Germans tend to dress conservatively, so you should always dress modestly and avoid wearing revealing clothing or beachwear in public places.

  • Tipping: In most restaurants, service is included in the bill, so tipping is not mandatory. However, rounding up the bill or leaving some small change as a thank you is always appreciated.

Best Time to Visit Schwerin

Schwerin has a pleasant, mild to warm climate that is ideal for outdoor activities. The best time to visit would be between May and September when the weather is perfect for outdoor adventures, and the city comes alive with summer festivals and cultural events.

Do’s and Don’ts of Schwerin

Do:

  • Do try traditional German cuisine like Potthucke, Eisbein, and Rote Grütze.

  • Do take a bike tour around the Schwerin Lake.

Don’t:

  • Don’t litter in public places - Germans are generally very environmentally-conscious, and littering is considered impolite.

  • Don’t forget to always carry your ID as it is mandatory in Schwerin.

Safety and Health

Safety in Schwerin

Schwerin is generally safe and peaceful, with low crime rates. Visitors are advised to be vigilant and take standard safety precautions, such as keeping an eye on their valuables when visiting crowded places.

Health in Schwerin

Schwerin has excellent healthcare facilities, and visitors can access quality medical care if required. However, visitors are advised to purchase travel insurance before visiting the city, as medical care can be expensive for those without coverage.

Getting to Schwerin: Transportation Options and Logistics

Schwerin is well-connected to Germany and Europe’s major cities and offers a range of transportation options for visitors.

Airports Near Schwerin

The closest international airport to Schwerin is the Hamburg Airport, located approximately 125 km away from the city.

Bus Stations Near Schwerin

Schwerin has convenient bus connections with several German cities like Berlin, Hamburg, and Rostock.

Other Options

Trains are another popular transportation option, with the Schwerin Hauptbahnhof (main railway station) serving as the central hub for trains from all major cities.

How to Get Around in Schwerin

Schwerin is a walkable city, and many of its top tourist destinations are within walking distance. Visitors can also rent bikes, take public transport, or use taxis to explore the city’s attractions.

Where to Go Next from Schwerin: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Schwerin’s proximity to other major German cities like Hamburg and Berlin makes it an excellent base for exploring nearby destinations and taking exciting excursions. Here are a few options for those looking to explore the surroundings:

  • Lubeck: A charming town renowned for its medieval architecture, Lubeck is just a short drive from Schwerin and offers stunning viewpoints of the Baltic Sea.

  • Berlin: The vibrant German capital is only two hours away from Schwerin and offers a range of exciting attractions, including the Brandenburg Gate, the Berlin Wall, and the famed Berlin Museum Island.

  • Rostock: A beautiful port town located on Germany’s northern coast, Rostock is a great place to enjoy the stunning scenery and unique culture of the region.

Conclusion

Schwerin is a captivating city with a rich history, culture, and natural beauty that makes it an ideal destination for travelers from all over the world. With its range of activities, attractions, food, and drink options, Schwerin is a destination that offers something for everyone, making it a must-visit destination for any traveler to Germany.
