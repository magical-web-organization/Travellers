Introduction to Zinnowitz

Zinnowitz is a charming seaside resort town located in the northern part of the island of Usedom in Mecklenburg-Western Pomerania, Germany. It is a popular vacation destination for both locals and international tourists, known for its natural beauty, long sandy beaches, and family-friendly atmosphere.

With its mild climate, Zinnowitz is an ideal destination all year round, whether you’re looking for a relaxing summer holiday or a peaceful winter getaway. In this guide, we’ll take you through the top things to see and do in Zinnowitz, as well as the best places to eat, stay, and shop.

List of Places to Visit in Zinnowitz

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Zinnowitz has a rich history and culture that is still visible in its many museums and historical monuments. Some of the top cultural attractions in the town include:

  1. Blechbüchsenmuseum: This quirky museum is dedicated to tin cans, displaying over 16,000 different patterns dating from the 18th century to the present day.
  2. Haus des Gastes: A cultural center that hosts concerts, exhibitions, and other events throughout the year.
  3. Bäderarchitektur in Zinnowitz: Take a walking tour to admire the beautiful seaside architecture in the town, with grand villas and bathhouses from the late 19th and early 20th centuries.

Natural Attractions of Zinnowitz: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

For lovers of the great outdoors, Zinnowitz has a wealth of natural attractions to explore. From hiking and biking trails to wildlife watching and beach activities, there is plenty to keep you entertained.

  1. Zinnowitzer Strand: This 3.5 km long sandy beach is perfect for swimming, sunbathing, and water sports.
  2. Achterwasser: The Achterwasser lagoon is a great spot for sailing, windsurfing, and nature-spotting.
  3. Usedomer Naturpark: The island’s beautiful nature reserve offers guided walks, cycling tours, and birdwatching opportunities.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Zinnowitz

With its flat terrain and well-maintained paths, Zinnowitz is a paradise for cyclists and hikers. Some of the most popular routes in the area include:

  1. Usedomer Radweg: A 200 km long cycling trail that runs around the island, passing through Zinnowitz.
  2. Kaiserbäderweg: A 14 km long hiking trail that connects the three imperial spas of Ahlbeck, Heringsdorf, and Bansin.
  3. Hauptstraße: A short, scenic cycle route that takes you through the heart of Zinnowitz.

Best Things to Do in Zinnowitz: Activities and Tours

Beyond sightseeing, there are plenty of activities and tours in Zinnowitz to suit all tastes and interests, such as:

  1. Wellness at the spa: Relax and rejuvenate at one of the many spas in or near the town, offering a range of treatments and activities.
  2. Segway Tour: Explore the town on a Segway, with a guided tour taking you to some of the best sights and hidden corners.
  3. Watersports: Whether you’re into swimming, surfing, or fishing, the beaches and lagoons around Zinnowitz offer plenty of opportunities for water-based activities.

Week-Long Itinerary for Zinnowitz

For those planning a longer stay in Zinnowitz, here’s a sample itinerary to help you make the most of your time:

Food and Drink Scene of Zinnowitz

Must-Try Dishes

Zinnowitz and the surrounding area are known for their fresh seafood and regional specialties. Some must-try dishes include:

  1. Soleier: Pickled eggs served with onions and spices, often eaten as a snack or appetizer.
  2. Fischbrötchen: A fish sandwich made with local fish like herring or salmon, often served with pickles and onions.
  3. Kartoffelsuppe: A hearty potato soup that is a staple of traditional German cuisine.

Local Beverages

Zinnowitz is home to a number of local breweries and vineyards, including:

  1. Usedomer Brauhaus: A craft brewery that produces several different types of beer, including light and dark ales.
  2. Weingut Schloss Rattey: This vineyard grows both red and white grapes, producing high-quality wines that are sold locally and internationally.

Where to Stay in Zinnowitz: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Zinnowitz offers a variety of accommodation options to suit all budgets and preferences. Some of the best places to stay in the town include:

  1. Hotel Asgard: A luxury hotel with modern rooms and a spa, located just steps from the beach.
  2. Hotel Pommerscher Hof: A historic hotel housed in a restored villa, offering stylish rooms and a great location in the town center.
  3. Jugendherberge Zinnowitz: A youth hostel with cheap dorms and private rooms, perfect for budget travelers.

What to Buy in Zinnowitz: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Zinnowitz has a variety of shops and markets selling local products and souvenirs. Some popular items include:

  1. Amber jewelry: The island is famous for its amber, which is often made into jewelry and sold in local shops.
  2. Local beer and wine: Breweries and vineyards in the area produce high-quality drinks that make great gifts for friends and family back home.
  3. Handmade soaps and cosmetics: The town has several artisanal soap shops that use natural ingredients to create unique products.

Local Culture and Customs of Zinnowitz

Like many small towns in Germany, Zinnowitz has its own distinct culture and customs that visitors should keep in mind. Some local traditions include:

  1. Korso: A weekly parade that takes place every Thursday evening during the summer months, featuring colorful floats and live music.
  2. Usedomer Literaturtage: An annual literary festival that takes place in September, attracting writers and book-lovers from around the world.
  3. Räuchernacht: A festival held in late October that celebrates the local fishing culture, with live music, food stalls, and an evening fish-smoking ceremony.

Best Time to Visit Zinnowitz

Zinnowitz is a great destination all year round, but the best time to visit depends on your preferences and activities. The summer months (June to August) are the busiest, with warm weather and plenty of outdoor activities and festivals. Winter (December to February) can be cold but peaceful, with fewer crowds and opportunities for winter sports.

Do’s and Don’ts of Zinnowitz

Do:

  1. Respect the local culture and customs, and try to learn some basic German before your trip.
  2. Take advantage of the town’s natural beauty and outdoor activities, such as cycling, hiking, and water sports.
  3. Enjoy the local food and drink scene, including fresh seafood and regional wines and beers.

Don’t:

  1. Swim outside designated areas, as the waters can be dangerous due to strong currents.
  2. Leave valuables unattended on the beach or in public places.
  3. Drive under the influence of alcohol or drugs, as the police are strict on enforcement.

Safety and Health

Safety in Zinnowitz

Zinnowitz is a safe town with low crime rates, but visitors should still take basic precautions such as keeping their belongings secure and avoiding walking alone at night in quiet areas.

Health in Zinnowitz

Visitors to Zinnowitz should have up-to-date routine vaccinations, as well as Hepatitis A and B vaccines if they plan to engage in water activities. The town has several medical clinics and pharmacies in case of emergency.

Getting to Zinnowitz: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Zinnowitz

The nearest airport to Zinnowitz is Heringsdorf Airport, located about 30 km away. It offers direct flights to several cities in Germany and Europe.

Bus Stations Near Zinnowitz

Zinnowitz has a central bus station with connections to several cities in the region, including Heringsdorf, Wolgast, and Anklam. Buses are a convenient and affordable way to travel around the area.

Other Options

Visitors to Zinnowitz can also arrive by train, with regular connections from Berlin and other major German cities to the nearby town of Züssow. From there, it’s a short bus or taxi ride to Zinnowitz.

How to Get Around in Zinnowitz

Zinnowitz is a small town that can easily be explored on foot or by bicycle. There are also local buses and taxis available for longer or more distant trips.

Where to Go Next from Zinnowitz: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

From Zinnowitz, visitors can explore the rest of the beautiful island of Usedom, with its charming towns, beaches, and nature reserves. Some nearby destinations and excursions include:

  1. Ahlbeck: A picturesque town with beautiful villas and gardens, located on the Polish border.
  2. Peenemünde: A former rocket testing site that now features a museum and scientific exhibitions.
  3. Insel Usedom Tour: A boat tour that takes visitors on a scenic ride around the island, highlighting its natural beauty and cultural heritage.

Conclusion

Whether you’re looking for a relaxing seaside vacation or an active outdoor adventure, Zinnowitz has something to offer. With its scenic beaches, rich culture, and welcoming atmosphere, it is a destination you won’t soon forget.
