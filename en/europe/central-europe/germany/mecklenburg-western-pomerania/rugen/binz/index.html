Introduction to Binz

Located in Germany’s Mecklenburg-Western Pomerania region, on the island of Rügen, Binz is one of the country’s most charming seaside towns. Known for its sandy beaches, outdoor adventures, and rich history and culture, Binz attracts visitors from all over the world. Whether you’re looking to spend a relaxing vacation by the sea or embark on exciting excursions, Binz has something to offer everyone.

List of Places to Visit in Binz

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Prora Museum - Learn about the history of the massive Nazi-era resort complex in Prora and how it was ultimately transformed into a cultural destination.
  • Granitz Hunting Lodge - Visit this enchanting mansion that once hosted the Prussian royal family and enjoy stunning views of the surrounding countryside from its tower.
  • Ernst Moritz Arndt Museum - Explore the life and work of Ernst Moritz Arndt, a prominent German author and political figure.

Natural Attractions of Binz: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Binz Beach - Relax on one of the town’s most beautiful beaches, which features crystal-clear waters and soft, white sand.
  • Jasmund National Park - Hike through this stunning nature reserve, which is home to ancient beech forests, towering cliffs, and the iconic Königsstuhl chalk rock formation.
  • Schmachter Lake Park - Enjoy a peaceful stroll through this picturesque park, which is filled with lush greenery, tranquil waterways, and scenic views.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Binz

  • Rügen Coastal Path - Take on this legendary hiking trail, which runs along the entire perimeter of Rügen and offers breathtaking views of the Baltic Sea.
  • Rasender Roland Bicycle Trail - Rent a bike and cycle through this stunning nature reserve, which features winding trails, rolling hills, and incredible wildlife sightings.
  • Binz Forest Trail - Discover the lush forests and scenic vistas around Binz on this easy hiking route, which features well-maintained trails and plenty of wildlife viewing opportunities.

Best Things to Do in Binz: Activities and Tours

  • Sea Kayaking - Explore the picturesque coastline of Binz from a different angle, paddling on a sea kayak. Experience the stunning natural setting of Binz and have close encounters with sea life.
  • Segway Tours - Take a Segway tour of Binz and feel the wind as you roll down the seaside promenade, offering stunning views of the scenic landscape.
  • Horseback Riding - Saddle up and enjoy a scenic ride through the countryside, passing through beautiful forests and observing the local wildlife.

Week-Long Itinerary for Binz

Day Activity
Day 1 Explore the town of Binz, stroll along the beach, and visit the Binz Pier.
Day 2 Hike through Jasmund National Park and take in the breathtaking views of the chalk cliffs.
Day 3 Visit the Prora Museum and learn about the history of the massive complex, followed by a visit to Granitz Hunting Lodge.

Day 6 Relax at the Schmachter Lake Park and soak up the tranquil atmosphere.

Food and Drink Scene of Binz

Must-Try Dishes

  • Rügener Heringsfilet - This traditional dish consists of canned herring fillets in a savoury marinade, perfect as a snack or light dinner.
  • Zuckerkuchen - Similar to crumb cake, this sugary treat is a common dessert in the region, often served with warm black tea.
  • Rügener Inselkäse - Cheese lovers must try this delicious cheese variety, produced only on the island of Rügen.

Local Beverages

  • Rügener Bier - A popular local beer brand, brewed in the neighboring town of Bergen.
  • Rügenwalder Teespezialitäten - Warm up with a cup of locally produced tea, available in many different blends and flavours.

Where to Stay in Binz: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Binz offers a wide range of accommodation options for every budget and preference.

  • Grand Hotel Binz - A luxury hotel located right on the seafront, with elegant rooms and a world-class spa.
  • Hostel & Bistro Kastanie - A cozy hostel which offers private and dorm rooms at an affordable price, with a friendly and welcoming atmosphere.
  • Gasthaus Zur Linde - This traditional guesthouse features charming rooms and a popular restaurant serving up signature local dishes.

What to Buy in Binz: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Amber Jewellery - Binz is famous for its amber production, so make sure to pick up a piece of elegant jewellery made from this precious material.
  • Binz Beach Sand - Take home a bottle of the town’s famous white sand as a unique and memorable souvenir.
  • Rügen Honey - Sample the island’s natural, delicious honey, available at specialty shops around town.

Local Culture and Customs of Binz

Binz has a rich cultural heritage, with unique traditions and customs that are still alive to this day. The town is known for its vibrant music scene, as well as its rich artistic and literary history. Visitors can immerse themselves in the local culture by attending concerts, visiting art galleries, and participating in traditional festivals and celebrations.

Best Time to Visit Binz

The best time to visit Binz is during the summer months, from June to August, when the weather is warm and sunny, and the town is bustling with activity. However, the autumn months can also be a beautiful time to visit, with vibrant fall foliage and fewer crowds.

Do’s and Don’ts of Binz

Do:

  • Bring plenty of sunscreen and a hat to protect yourself from the sun.
  • Respect the local culture and customs, including dress codes and traditional etiquette.
  • Try the local cuisine and sample the regional specialties.

Don’t:

  • Leave trash on the beach or in other public areas.
  • Wear revealing clothing on the beach or in other public places.
  • Be loud and disruptive in residential areas, especially at night.

Safety and Health

Safety in Binz

Binz is a very safe town, with low levels of crime and a friendly, welcoming atmosphere. However, visitors should be aware of potential hazards in the natural environment, such as steep cliffs and rough seas along the coastline.

Health in Binz

Binz has modern medical facilities and a good standard of healthcare. It’s recommended to purchase travel insurance before visiting, in case of any unexpected medical emergencies.

Getting to Binz: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Binz

The nearest airport to Binz is Heringsdorf Airport, located around 2 hours away by car. Alternatively, visitors can also fly into Rostock-Laage Airport or Berlin-Tegel Airport, both of which are within a few hours’ drive of Binz.

Bus Stations Near Binz

Binz is well connected by bus, with regular services linking it to other towns and cities in the region. The nearest major bus station is in Bergen auf Rügen, around 13 km away.

Other Options

Visitors can also reach Binz via train, with frequent connections from Berlin, Hamburg, and other major cities in Germany. Driving is also an option, with the town located around 300 km from Berlin and 230 km from Hamburg.

How to Get Around in Binz

Binz is a very walkable town, with most major attractions and amenities located within a short distance of each other. Visitors can also rent bikes or take the local buses to explore the surrounding area.

Where to Go Next from Binz: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Sassnitz - Explore this charming harbour town, which features a picturesque marina, fascinating museums, and dramatic cliffside views.
  • Putbus - Check out this historic town, which was once the seat of Rügen’s noble family and is home to several impressive theatres and palaces.
  • Cape Arkona - Visit this stunning natural landmark and enjoy the panoramic views of the Baltic Sea from its towering cliffs.

Conclusion

Binz is truly a one-of-a-kind destination that offers a winning combination of natural beauty, cultural richness, and unique charm. Whether you’re looking for a relaxing seaside vacation or exciting outdoor adventures, Binz has it all. With this comprehensive travel guide, you’ll have all the information you need to plan the perfect trip and make unforgettable memories in this enchanting corner of Germany.
