Introduction to Sassnitz

Situated in Rügen, an island in Germany’s Mecklenburg-Western Pomerania region, Sassnitz is a beautiful coastal town known for its natural beauty, outdoor adventures, and cultural landmarks. With a population of around 9,000 people, Sassnitz has a small, charming atmosphere that draws in visitors looking for a peaceful getaway or an active adventure. In this comprehensive travel guide, we’ll delve into all of the must-visit places, things to do, and travel tips to ensure you have an unforgettable visit to Sassnitz.

List of Places to Visit in Sassnitz

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Begin your journey in Sassnitz with a visit to the town’s cultural landmarks. Sassnitz is home to several museums that highlight the town’s history and culture:

  • Sassnitz Maritime Museum: Get up close and personal with maritime history by exploring over 300 years of shipbuilding and sailing culture in Sassnitz.
  • Sassnitz Hunting and Fishing Museum: Discover Sassnitz’s history of hunting and fishing and learn about the different species of animals and fish that call the island of Rügen home.
  • Alte Mühle (Old Mill): Known as the “Gate to the Jasmund National Park”, Alte Mühle showcases the unique industrial heritage of Sassnitz and its surrounding area.

Natural Attractions of Sassnitz: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Sassnitz is famously known for its beautiful natural landscape. There are several natural attractions that visitors won’t want to miss:

  • Jasmund National Park: A UNESCO World Heritage Site, Jasmund National Park features stunning chalk cliffs that are unlike anything else in the world. Hike through the park’s forests and take in breathtaking views of the Baltic Sea.
  • Königsstuhl: Known as the “King’s Chair,” Königsstuhl is one of the most famous natural landmarks in Germany. Visitors can take in the stunning views of the cliff overlooking the Baltic Sea.
  • National Park Centre Königsstuhl: This visitor center explains the formation and history of the Jasmund National Park and provides an interactive educational exhibition.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Sassnitz

Sassnitz is an outdoor enthusiast’s paradise with its abundance of hiking and biking trails that showcase the town’s natural beauty. Here are a few of our recommendations:

  • Jasmund National Park:
    • Rügen Circular Trail: This trail runs along the coast and is 16 km long. It offers stunning views of the cliffs and Baltic Sea and takes around 4-5 hours to complete depending on the pace.
    • Herthasee Loop: This trail is 6 km long and is easy to hike with minimal difficulty. It passes by the stunning Herthasee Lake.
  • Sassnitz-Putbus Trail: This 40 km long route is a great option for experienced cyclists. It runs through beautiful landscapes and passes several historical landmarks.

Best Things to Do in Sassnitz: Activities and Tours

There’s plenty to see and do in Sassnitz, whether you prefer active outdoor pursuits or more laid-back cultural experiences. Here are some of our top picks for things to do:

  • Sunset Boat Tours: Experience a magical sunset over the Baltic Sea with a sunset boat tour from the Sassnitz harbor.
  • Sea Kayaking: Explore the beautiful coastline from a sea kayak and get up close and personal with the stunning natural scenery.
  • Fishing Charters: Go fishing with local experts and take in the beauty of the Baltic Sea.
  • Harbour Festival: Held annually in August, the Sassnitz harbor festival features a wide range of local traditions, food, and music.

Week-Long Itinerary for Sassnitz

Here’s a week-long itinerary to make the most of your visit to Sassnitz:

  • Day 1: Explore Jasmund National Park and take in the stunning landscape from the Königsstuhl cliff.
  • Day 2: Take a sailing trip around the Baltic Sea or explore the town’s maritime history at the Sassnitz Maritime Museum.
  • Day 3: Experience the local culture via the town’s museums such as the Hunting and Fishing Museum and the Old Mill.
  • Day 4: Take a bike tour along Sassnitz-Putbus Trail and see the stunning landscapes and historical landmarks.
  • Day 5: Try your hand at fishing and enjoy the beautiful shoreline of Sassnitz.
  • Day 6: Visit the little town of Putbus by bike, famous for its white city center.
  • Day 7: Enjoy the local food and drinks at the Sassnitz harbor festival.

Food and Drink Scene of Sassnitz

Sassnitz has a wonderful selection of traditional German, Polish and Scandinavian cuisines to choose from. Here are some of the must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Solyanka - a hearty soup with sausages or ham, one of the most traditional dishes in Eastern Europe.
  • Bierpfanne - pork, onions, bell peppers, and mushrooms tossed in beer gravy.
  • Fischbrötchen - a common German Fish sandwich with a wide variety of fish available.

Local Beverages

  • Rugener - a beer that is brewed locally on the island of Rügen.
  • Klabunt - a local soft drink available in flavors such as cherry and elderflower.

Where to Stay in Sassnitz: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

It’s important to pick the right accommodation for your Sassnitz stay. Here are some of our top picks:

  • Hotel Parkidyll Sassnitz: Located near the harbor, this cozy hotel features modern amenities and lovely views of the countryside.
  • Pension Villa Elisabeth: This beautiful guesthouse features garden views and free Wi-Fi, it is an ideal place for a quiet stay.
  • Hostel Seascape Sassnitz: This newly renovated hostel is right near the harbor area. It features modern design touches throughout.

What to Buy in Sassnitz: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

If you’re looking for souvenirs and local products to bring home, Sassnitz has several options. Here are some of our top picks:

  • Amber Jewellery: Rügen is famous for its amber, and visitors can find many stores selling beautiful amber jewellery.
  • Rugen-Doll: This handmade doll is designed after the traditional clothing worn in the region and makes for a perfect souvenir.
  • Binz-Brick: These bricks are a symbol of Rügen’s architectural history and make for a unique and interesting souvenir.

Local Culture and Customs of Sassnitz

The people of Sassnitz have a strong regional identity with a unique blend of German and Polish culture. Traditional festivals and cuisine play a huge role in the town’s identity and visitors can get a glimpse of daily life by visiting local establishments, tasting traditional dishes, and engaging with local people.

Best Time to Visit Sassnitz

Sassnitz is beautiful year-round but summer is the most popular time to visit the town. June through August sees warm temperatures, sunshine, and long days to enjoy outdoor adventures. Still, visitors can enjoy a variety of activities during the shoulder seasons of spring and fall and discover fewer tourist crowds.

Do’s and Don’ts of Sassnitz

Here are some do’s and don’ts to keep in mind while visiting Sassnitz:

Do:

  • Respect the local traditions and culture
  • Enjoy the natural beauty that Sassnitz has to offer
  • Try local food and drink

Don’t:

  • Litter in the natural parks and surroundings
  • Be intrusive while taking photographs

Safety and Health

Safety in Sassnitz

Sassnitz is a relatively safe town, but visitors should still keep a few things in mind:

  • Beware of strong waves or currents at the beach

Health in Sassnitz

Sassnitz has medical facilities that can provide you with access to basic medical care in case of an emergency. Emergency services can be contacted by dialing 112.

Getting to Sassnitz: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Sassnitz

The two closest airports for visitors flying in are:

  • Heringsdorf Airport (HDF) - located in Heringsdorf, 120 km from Sassnitz.
  • Rostock-Laage Airport (RLG) - located in Rostock, 175 km from Sassnitz.

Bus Stations Near Sassnitz

There are several bus lines that serve Sassnitz throughout the day. Visitors can travel to Sassnitz by bus and arrive at Sassnitz Bus Station.

Other Options

Visitors can also travel to Sassnitz by train, arriving at the train station in Mukran Port. Alternatively, driving to Sassnitz by car is also an option.

How to Get Around in Sassnitz

Several transport options are available to explore Sassnitz, including bike rentals, taxis, and buses. Walking is also an option for those who want to explore the town at a leisurely pace.

Where to Go Next from Sassnitz: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Sassnitz is located near several quaint towns and cities that are worth exploring. Here are a few of our top picks:

  • Stralsund: This beautifully preserved Hanseatic city offers medieval architecture and a vibrant nightlife. It’s only a 1-hour drive from Sassnitz.
  • Binz: A lovely coastal resort town that is known for its white beach and architecture. It is only a 30-minute drive from Sassnitz.
  • Putbus: This charming town is known for its stunning white city center, and arrival is just a 40-minutes by bike.

Conclusion

With its stunning natural beauty, rich cultural heritage, and wide range of activities and experiences, Sassnitz is a perfect destination for any traveler. Whether you’re looking to explore the town’s outdoor adventures, sample traditional cuisine, or take in the local culture, Sassnitz truly has something for everyone.
