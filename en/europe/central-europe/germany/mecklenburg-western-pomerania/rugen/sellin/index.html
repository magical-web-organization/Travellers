Introduction to Sellin

Nestled in the charming island of Rügen in Mecklenburg-Western Pomerania, Sellin is a picturesque resort town that has been attracting visitors for over a century. Known for its sandy beaches, crystal-clear waters, and iconic pier, Sellin is a popular destination for families and couples looking for a relaxing yet fun-filled holiday. From its cultural and natural attractions to its mouthwatering cuisines, Sellin has something to offer every type of traveler.

List of Places to Visit in Sellin

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • The Amber Museum: Learn about the history and cultural significance of amber, one of the most precious resources on the island of Rügen. The museum boasts an impressive collection of amber artifacts, including jewelry, sculptures, and inclusions.
  • Sellin pier: inaugurated in 1906, is one of the landmarks of the town. Take a stroll along the pier to catch panoramic ocean views and visit the pier’s historical museum
  • Granitz Hunting Lodge: Once served as a retreat for German nobles and is a stunning example of Neo-Gothic architecture. Take a guided tour of the lodge to learn about its history and significance.

Natural Attractions of Sellin: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Sellin Cliff: Take a scenic hike up to this beautiful cliff to enjoy panoramic views of the town and the nearby coastline. It’s an ideal spot for nature photography and birdwatching.
  • Sellin Beach: Spend a day relaxing on the town’s main beach, a wide expanse of white sand and crystal-clear water. It also offers plenty of water activities such as windsurfing and kayaking.
  • Naturerbe Zentrum Rügen: Considered one of the most exceptional natural experiences in Germany, the center offers the chance to explore the stunning UNESCO biosphere reserve woodland, experience the treetop walk, or even navigate through a giant maze.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Sellin

Sellin is a great location for bikers and hikers alike. Cyclists can explore the town’s surrounding area on the Baltic Coast cycle path, stopping at quaint villages and beautiful beaches en route. Hikers can opt for the ‘Selliner See Tour’ that takes them around lake Selliner or the guided ‘Rügen’s dream paths’ that allow hikers to enjoy the natural surroundings of the Sellin forest.

Best Things to Do in Sellin: Activities and Tours

  • Sea kayaking: Rent a kayak and paddle out into the Baltic Sea for a unique perspective of the town’s coastline.
  • Zipline adventure: Experience adrenaline-pumping adventure in the middle of a forest at Rügenpark.
  • Wiener Caféhaus Museum: for all coffee lovers, indulge in a delicious cake in a Viennese-style cafe at the Wiener Caféhaus museum.

Week-Long Itinerary for Sellin

Day 1-3: Relax on Sellin’s beaches, visit the pier and spend some time taking in the stunning views.

Day 4: Take a guided tour of Sellin’s historical landmarks, such as the Granitz Hunting Lodge and the Amber Museum.

Day 5: Explore the natural beauty of the area with a hike up the Sellin Cliff, a visit to Naturerbe Zentrum Rügen, or a bicycle ride along the coast.

Day 6: Try one of Sellin’s outdoor adventure activities like ziplining or sea kayaking.

Day 7: Shop for souvenirs and local products before departing home.

Food and Drink Scene of Sellin

Must-Try Dishes

  • Rügener Heringsfilet: A local specialty that features smoked herring fillets, often served with potatoes or on rye bread.
  • Rügener Inselbier: A refreshing local beer that pairs well with traditional German cuisine.
  • Selliner Räucherfisch: Enjoy fresh smoked fish from one of several smokehouses in the town.

Local Beverages

  • Selliner Hexenklöppel: A local specialty liquor that features a combination of fruit brandy and chocolate.
  • Rügener Inselbier: A refreshing local beer that pairs well with traditional German cuisine.

Where to Stay in Sellin: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Hotel Bernstein: A luxurious beachfront hotel that offers spectacular ocean views and first-class amenities.
  • SEETELHOTEL Villa Meeresgruss: A charming villa hotel located near the pier and beach.
  • Jugendherberge Sellin: A budget-friendly hostel in the heart of the town that’s perfect for backpackers and groups.

What to Buy in Sellin: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Amber jewelry: Buy unique and authentic amber jewelry from the Amber Museum or the town’s artisan shops.
  • Handcrafted goods: Browse handmade goods like pottery, textiles, and woodworking items made by local artisans.
  • Rügener Inselbier: Bring home a taste of the town with a selection of local beers and spirits.

Local Culture and Customs of Sellin

Sellin’s local culture is rich in tradition and history. Visitors are encouraged to respect local customs and observe local traditions. It is customary to greet locals with a friendly “Moin” in Sellin, meaning good morning or good day.

Best Time to Visit Sellin

The best time to visit Sellin is from May to September when the weather is warm and the town is in full swing with summer activities and events. However, visitors can still enjoy many outdoor activities even during the winter months, such as snowshoeing, cross-country skiing, and ice fishing.

Do’s and Don’ts of Sellin

Do:

  • Respect local customs and traditions.
  • Try the local cuisine and beverages.
  • Explore the natural beauty of the area.

Don’t:

  • Leave trash behind on beaches or hiking trails.
  • Ignore safety protocols for outdoor adventures.
  • Engage in sky-lantern releasing or any activities that harm the natural environment.

Safety and Health

Safety in Sellin

Sellin is a generally safe destination for visitors. However, like any tourist destination, visitors should be aware of pickpocketing and keep their valuables safe. Lock valuables in a hotel safe, and take extra precaution in crowded areas.

Health in Sellin

Visitors should take extra precaution for tick-borne diseases like Lyme disease by wearing insect repellent and dressing appropriately, particularly in wooded areas.

Getting to Sellin: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Sellin

Rostock-Laage Airport is the nearest airport to Sellin, located approximately 130 km away. Alternatively, visitors can fly into Berlin airport and catch a train to Sellin.

Bus Stations Near Sellin

Sellin has several local bus stations that connect the town to neighboring villages and cities.

Other Options

Visitors can reach Sellin by taking the train to the nearby towns of Bergen or Binz and then taking a bus or taxi.

How to Get Around in Sellin

Sellin is a small town that’s easily navigated on foot or by bike. Visitors can also take a local bus or taxi to explore the surrounding area, or rent a car for convenience.

Where to Go Next from Sellin: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Sassnitz: A quaint fishing village located just 30 km from Sellin, famous for its picturesque old town and stunning natural scenery.
  • Rügen National Park: One of the most significant national parks in Germany highlights rugged chalk cliffs, beautiful beaches, and rare underwater plant life.
  • Stralsund: A historic Baltic port city known for its architecture and historic sites like St. Mary’s Church and the Störtebeker Theater.

Conclusion

Sellin is a charming and diverse seaside resort town that’s both relaxing and fun-filled. From its historical landmarks to its natural beauty and unique cuisine, Sellin offers visitors a unique experience on the island of Rügen. Get ready for an unforgettable experience and start planning your trip to Sellin today.
