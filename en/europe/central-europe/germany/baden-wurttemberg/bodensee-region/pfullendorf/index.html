Introduction to Pfullendorf

Located in the Bodensee region of Germany, Pfullendorf is a hidden gem waiting to be explored. It is a small town with a population of about 13,000 people, offering a perfect escape from the hustle and bustle of the city. Pfullendorf is a town that has maintained its medieval charm, with cobblestone streets and ancient buildings. The town has both natural and man-made attractions, making it a popular destination for tourists.

List of Places to Visit in Pfullendorf

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

1. Heuneburg Museum

Heuneburg Museum is a must-visit destination for history enthusiasts. The museum showcases the history and culture of the Celts, who inhabited the area more than 2000 years ago. Visitors can see ancient artifacts and learn about the daily life of the Celts.

2. Schloss Pfullendorf

Schloss Pfullendorf, also known as Pfullendorf Castle, is a 13th-century castle that has been restored to its former glory. Visitors can take a guided tour to learn about the history of the castle and see the magnificent architecture.

3. St. Jakobus Church

St. Jakobus Church is a beautiful church that has been standing for over 700 years. Visitors can see the beautiful stained glass windows and the unique pipe organ.

Natural Attractions of Pfullendorf: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

1. Seepark Linzgau

Seepark Linzgau is a beautiful park that is perfect for a picnic or a leisurely stroll. The park has a lake where visitors can rent paddle boats or kayaks to explore the water.

2. Pfullendorf High Ropes Course

Located in the forest, Pfullendorf High Ropes Course is an adventure park with rope courses and zip lines. Visitors can enjoy an adrenaline-filled day in the forest.

3. Hohentwiel

Hohentwiel is a volcanic cone that is over 700 meters high. Visitors can take a hike to the top to see stunning views of the town and the Bodensee region.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Pfullendorf

Pfullendorf is surrounded by forests and nature reserves, making hiking and biking popular activities. Some of the best hiking and biking routes in Pfullendorf include:

  • Lake Constance Cycle Path
  • Donauberglandweg
  • Bodensee-Königssee Cycle Path

Best Things to Do in Pfullendorf: Activities and Tours

1. Take a Guided Tour of the Town

A guided tour of the town is a great way to learn about the history and culture of Pfullendorf. Guided tours are led by knowledgeable local guides who will take you through the town’s top attractions.

2. Attend the Pfullendorf Festival

The Pfullendorf Festival is an annual event that takes place in July. The festival is a celebration of the town’s history and culture, with food, music, and games for all ages.

3. Visit the Pfullendorfer Wochenmarkt

The Pfullendorfer Wochenmarkt is a weekly market that takes place on Fridays. Visitors can buy local produce, handmade crafts, and souvenirs.

Week-Long Itinerary for Pfullendorf

Day 1: Visit Heuneburg Museum and take a guided tour of the town. Day 2: Hike to Hohentwiel and enjoy scenic views. Day 3: Visit Seepark Linzgau and rent a paddle boat. Day 4: Take a guided tour of Schloss Pfullendorf and see the beautiful architecture. Day 5: Visit St. Jakobus Church and explore the cobblestone streets of the town. Day 6: Spend the day at Pfullendorf High Ropes Course. Day 7: Attend the Pfullendorf Festival.

Food and Drink Scene of Pfullendorf

Must-Try Dishes

1. Maultaschen

Maultaschen is a traditional German dish that is similar to ravioli. It is a dough filled with meat, spinach, and onions.

2. Spätzle

Spätzle is a German pasta that is served as a side dish or main dish. It is made with flour, eggs, and water and is served with meat or vegetables.

Local Beverages

Pfullendorf is known for its local beer, which is brewed using traditional methods. The beer is rich and flavorful and is a must-try for beer lovers.
