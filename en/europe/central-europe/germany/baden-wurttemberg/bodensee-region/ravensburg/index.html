Introduction to Ravensburg

Ravensburg is a city located in the Bodensee Region, in Baden-Württemberg, Germany. It’s known for its medieval architecture, picturesque alleys, and unique museums. The city is situated at the edge of the Alps and offers fantastic views of the surrounding landscape. Travelers interested in history, culture, and outdoor adventures are bound to find something to do in Ravensburg. In this comprehensive tour guide, we’ll explore the best places to visit, things to do, where to stay, and how to get around in Ravensburg.

List of Places to Visit in Ravensburg

Ravensburg is packed with attractions that cater to a variety of interests. In this section, we’ll explore the culturally important attractions and natural wonders of Ravensburg.

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Ravensburg has a rich history, and its museums and historical places offer a fascinating insight into the city’s past. Here are some must-visit cultural attractions:

1. Museum Humpis-Quartier

Housed in a 14th-century merchant’s house, this museum showcases the art, culture, and history of Ravensburg and the surrounding area. Visitors can see an extensive collection of paintings, sculptures, and artifacts from the Middle Ages to the modern era.

2. Ravensburger Spieleland

Ravensburger Spieleland is a fun-filled amusement park that is perfect for families with children. The park boasts rides, games, and activities based on the famous Ravensburger board games.

3. Mehlsack

Mehlsack, also known as the “Flour Sack,”is a prominent landmark in Ravensburg. It is a watchtower located in the city center and used to be a part of the city wall. From the top of the tower, visitors can enjoy panoramic views of the city and the Alps.

Natural Attractions of Ravensburg: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Besides its rich cultural heritage, Ravensburg has plenty of outdoor activities and breathtaking scenery to offer. Here are some outdoor attractions:

1. Lake Constance

Lake Constance is one of the largest lakes in Europe and is situated near Ravensburg. Visitors can take a relaxing boat ride, enjoy water sports, or simply soak up the stunning views of the lake and its surroundings.

2. Schussental Viaduct

The Schussental Viaduct is an impressive railway bridge, a great place to take in views of the city and the surrounding countryside. It’s also a popular spot for hiking and cycling.

3. Ravensburg Castle

Ravensburg Castle is located on a hill overlooking the city, and it offers visitors a stunning view of the city. The Castle dates back to the 12th century and is now a museum showcasing the city’s rich history.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Ravensburg

Ravensburg is a great place for hikers and mountain bikers, with plenty of beautiful routes that cater to different skill levels. Here are some of the best:

1. Ravensburger Strudelweg

The Ravensburger Strudelweg is a circular hiking trail that takes visitors through picturesque valleys and hills. The trail is about 8km long and offers fantastic views of the city and its surroundings.

2. Mountain Biking in Bodensee Region

The Bodensee Region is known for its mountain biking trails that take riders through its forests and hillsides. The Bodensee-Radweg trail is a particularly popular route that offers fantastic views of Lake Constance.

3. Salem Walk

The Salem Walk is a popular hiking trail that takes visitors from Ravensburg to the town of Salem. This trail is known for its historic landmarks, beautiful landscapes, and breathtaking views.

Best Things to Do in Ravensburg: Activities and Tours

Ravensburg offers a wide range of activities and tours. Here are some of the best things to do in Ravensburg:

1. Segway Tours

Segway tours are a great way to discover the city’s most popular attractions while enjoying a thrilling ride. It’s an exciting way to explore the city while getting the inside scoop on local history and culture.

2. Ravensburger Spieleland

Ravensburger Spieleland is a great place for families to have fun and bond over fun games and activities. The park has plenty of games and rides inspired by Ravensburger board games, as well as a water park and adventure park.

3. Walking Tours

Walking tours are an excellent way to discover the city’s hidden gems and attractions. Guided tours are available, or visitors can opt for a self-guided tour and explore the city at their own pace. Most tours focus on the city’s rich history and cultural heritage.

Week-Long Itinerary for Ravensburg

To get the most out of your visit to Ravensburg, you may want to consider this week-long itinerary:

Day 1: Arrive in Ravensburg, get settled in your hotel, and take a walking tour of the city center.

Day 2: Visit the Museum Humpis-Quartier and the Mehlsack Tower.

Day 3: Take a day trip to Lake Constance and enjoy a boat ride or water sports.

Day 4: Visit the Ravensburger Spieleland amusement park.

Day 5: Take a cycling tour or hike on the Ravensburger Strudelweg or Salem Walk.

Day 6: Explore Ravensburg Castle and enjoy scenic views of the city from the Castle.

Day 7: Enjoy a Segway tour or a self-guided walking tour of the city and discover its hidden gems.

Food and Drink Scene of Ravensburg

Foodies are in for a treat in Ravensburg –the city has plenty of delicious local dishes and beverages for travelers to discover.

Must-Try Dishes

1. Vespers

Vespers is a traditional snack that typically consists of bread, cheese, cold cuts, and sausage. It’s perfect for a quick bite on the go.

2. Käsespätzle

Käsespätzle is a local dish that consists of tender egg noodles and melted cheese. It’s a popular comfort food that’s perfect for a chilly evening.

3. Maultaschen

Maultaschen are a German version of the classic dumpling dish. They’re typically filled with minced meat, spinach, and breadcrumbs.

Local Beverages

1. Local Wines

Germany is known for its great wines, and the Bodensee region is no exception. Visitors can enjoy a wide range of local wines, including the famous white wines such as the Müller-Thurgau and the Silvaner.

2. Beer

German beer is known worldwide for its quality and flavor. Ravensburg has several local breweries that offer a variety of delicious beers from light to dark.

Where to Stay in Ravensburg: Hotels, Hostels and Guesthouses

Ravensburg has plenty of accommodation options, ranging from luxurious hotels to affordable guesthouses and hostels. Here are some of the best places to stay in Ravensburg:

1. Hotel Waldhorn

Hotel Waldhorn is a charming hotel located in the city center. It offers comfortable rooms, a restaurant, and an outdoor seating area.

2. Hotel Residenz

Hotel Residenz is a beautiful hotel that offers elegant rooms and suites. It has a restaurant, a bar, and a spa available for guests’ use.

3. Jugendherberge Ravensburg

For travelers on a budget, Jugendherberge Ravensburg offers affordable dormitory and private room options. It has a common area and a kitchen available for guests’ use.

What to Buy in Ravensburg: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Ravensburg has plenty of unique souvenirs and local products that visitors can take home as a memento of their trip. Here are some of the best:

1. Puzzles and Board Games

Ravensburger, the world-famous puzzle manufacturer, is headquartered in Ravensburg. Visitors can buy puzzles, games, and other entertainment products at its store in the city.

2. Handicrafts

Ravensburg has a vibrant arts and crafts scene, and visitors can purchase locally-made pottery, baskets, and textiles at various shops and markets throughout the city.

3. Wine and Beer

Visitors can take home locally produced wines and beers that are a perfect souvenir of their trip to Ravensburg. Most local wineries and breweries sell their products on-site.

Local Culture and Customs of Ravensburg

Ravensburg has a rich cultural heritage and customs that date back centuries. Visitors should familiarize themselves with the local traditions and etiquette to enhance their travel experience.

Best Time to Visit Ravensburg

The best time to visit Ravensburg is during the summer months (June to August) when the weather is sunny and warm, and there are plenty of outdoor activities available. The city is also busy during the Christmas markets (November to January) when the city comes alive with festive lights and events.

Do’s and Don’ts of Ravensburg

Do:

  • Try the local cuisine, including Vespers, Käsespätzle, and Maultaschen.
  • Take a walking tour of the city center to discover its hidden gems.
  • Visit the Museum Humpis-Quartier and Ravensburg Castle.
  • Take a cycling tour or hike on the Ravensburger Strudelweg and Salem Walk.
  • Attend seasonal events such as the Christmas markets.

Don’t:

  • Don’t forget to tip. It’s customary to tip around 10% of the bill in restaurants.
  • Don’t disrespect local customs or traditions.
  • Don’t litter or vandalize historical landmarks.
  • Don’t use your phone in public transportation.

Safety and Health

Safety in Ravensburg

Ravensburg is generally a safe destination for travelers. Visitors should take standard precautions such as safeguarding valuables, sticking to well-lit areas, and avoiding unlicensed taxis.

Health in Ravensburg

The city has several medical facilities, including hospitals and clinics. Visitors should have travel insurance that covers medical expenses.

Getting to Ravensburg: Transportation Options and Logistics

Ravensburg is located near the Austrian and Swiss borders, making it a convenient travel destination for visitors exploring central Europe. In this section, we’ll explore transportation options and logistics for getting to Ravensburg.

Airports Near Ravensburg

The closest international airports to Ravensburg are:

  • Friedrichshafen Airport (24 km)
  • Memmingen Airport (60 km)
  • Zurich Airport (98 km)

Bus Stations Near Ravensburg

Ravensburg Bus Station is located near the city center and offers connections to various cities in Germany and Europe.

Other Options

Visitors can also travel to Ravensburg by train. Ravensburg Station is located near the city center and offers connections to various German cities.

How to Get Around in Ravensburg

Ravensburg is a compact city that is easy to navigate on foot. The city also has an extensive public transportation network that includes buses and taxis. Visitors can purchase a Ravensburg Welcome Card that offers unlimited access to public transportation and discounts on popular attractions.

Where to Go Next from Ravensburg: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Ravensburg is the perfect base for exploring the region. Here are some nearby destinations and excursions to consider:

1. Friedrichshafen

Friedrichshafen is a picturesque town located on the shores of Lake Constance. It’s known for its beautiful coastline, historic landmarks, and aviation museums.

2. Meersburg

Meersburg is a charming town located on the shores of Lake Constance. It’s known for its medieval castle and the beautiful vineyards surrounding the town.

3. Allgau

Allgau is a region in the German Alps that is known for its stunning landscapes, beautiful castles, and outdoor activities. It’s a perfect day trip from Ravensburg.

Conclusion

Ravensburg is a city that offers something for every traveler – rich history, cultural heritage, outdoor adventures, and unique attractions. From its picturesque alleys to its scenic views, visitors are in for a treat. This comprehensive tour guide should help visitors explore the best places to visit, things to do, where to stay, and how to get around in Ravensburg and make their trip a memorable one.
