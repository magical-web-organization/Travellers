Introduction to Rottweil

Located in the Black Forest region of Germany, Rottweil is a serene town that has managed to preserve its medieval heritage while progressing into modernity. Rottweil is often overlooked by tourists in favor of more famous German cities like Berlin and Munich, but its unique blend of culture, history, and outdoor adventures make it a hidden gem.

This comprehensive guide will take you on a journey through Rottweil, from its culturally important attractions to its natural wonders. You’ll also learn about the best things to do in Rottweil, local food and drink, where to stay, local customs, the best time to visit, and safety tips. Let’s dive in and explore this charming town!

List of Places to Visit in Rottweil

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  1. Rottweil Town Museum: This museum is located in the old town hall and showcases the town’s rich history through exhibits that take you through the town’s earliest days up to present times.

  2. Fasnet Museum: This museum gives visitors a peek into Rottweil’s annual carnival called “Fasnet” and the history behind it.

  3. Altstadt: Rottweil’s old town is full of well-preserved medieval architecture and is perfect for a leisurely walk. Make sure to check out the half-timbered houses and cobblestone streets.

  4. Kapuziner Convent and Church: The Convent has been standing for over 400 years and today it houses a museum that showcases the history of the Capuchin order and its time in Rottweil. The adjacent St. Konrad church is known for its beautiful stained glass windows.

  5. Heilig-Kreuz-Munster: This Gothic-style church was built in the 13th century and is the largest church in the town. It has beautiful stained glass windows, a Baroque-style altar, and a crypt that holds the remains of the town’s patron saint.

Natural Attractions of Rottweil: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  1. Neckarburg-Ruine: This ruined castle offers some of the most picturesque views in Rottweil and is ideal for hiking enthusiasts. The hike to the castle can take up to 45 minutes but the stunning views of the Black Forest make it worthwhile.

  2. Baar Mountains: If you love hiking and outdoor activities, then a visit to the Baar Mountains is a must. This beautiful range has a diverse range of flora and fauna that makes for a perfect day trip.

  3. Rottweil Observatory: This observatory is located atop a hill and has breathtaking views of the surrounding landscape. Visitors can attend star-gazing events and astrophotography sessions.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Rottweil

  1. Rottweil to Neckarburg Ruin Hike: This 5-kilometer hike takes you through the scenic Neckar valley, and ends at the ruins of Neckarburg Castle.

  2. Schleifenbachklamm Hike: This trail is a bit challenging but the views make it worth it. The trail follows a picturesque stream that leads to a waterfall.

  3. Rottweil Bike Trail: This trail takes you on a leisurely bike ride through the picturesque town and its surroundings. It’s great for families and casual bikers.

Best Things to Do in Rottweil: Activities and Tours

  1. Fasnet Carnival: Rottweil’s annual carnival is a must-visit. The carnival involves a parade with traditional costumes, wooden masks, and live music, and is a great way to experience the local culture.

  2. Rottweil Tower Climb: The town has beautiful medieval towers that offer panoramic views of the surrounding landscape. Climb up the towers to get the best views of the red roofs of the town and the Black Forest.

Week-Long Itinerary for Rottweil

Day 1: Arrive in Rottweil, explore centrally located museums, Visit Heilig-Kreuz-Munster, Altstadt.

Day 2: Hike to Neckarburg-Ruine for panoramic views

Day 3: Take part in the Fasnet carnival and explore the local culture and traditions.

Day 4: Go on a bike ride through the town and its surroundings

Day 5: Hike Schleifenbachklamm and experience the stunning waterfall.

Day 6: Visit the Baar Mountains and soak in the natural beauty of the region.

Day 7: Visit the Rottweil observatory and attend a night-time star-gazing event.

Food and Drink Scene of Rottweil

Must-Try Dishes

  1. Nonnenfürzle: This specialty pastry is made of fried dough and traditionally served during carnival season.

  2. Rostbraten: A typical Swabian dish made of beef, served with red wine sauce and Spätzle noodles.

  3. Schupfnudel: These potato dumplings are a Swabian classic and are served as a side dish with meat or vegetables.

Local Beverages

  1. Baden wine: Rottweil is located in the Baden wine region and has a rich history of wine production. Make sure to try the local wine during your visit.

  2. Schnapps: The local schnapps is a must-try and is made of regional fruits like cherries and blackberries.

Where to Stay in Rottweil: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Budget Accommodation

  1. Pension Himmelreich: This budget-friendly guesthouse is located in the town center and offers comfortable rooms at an affordable rate.

  2. DJH Jugendherberge Rottweil: This hostel is located in a former nunnery and offers comfortable rooms at a reasonable price.

Mid-Range Accommodation

  1. Hotel Garni Maier: A well-equipped hotel in Rottweil offering cozy and spacious rooms with friendly and attentive service.

  2. Hotel Bären: This hotel is located in the heart of Rottweil and offers guests a comfortable room at a reasonable price.

High-End Accommodation

  1. Dormero Hotel Rottweil: A luxurious hotel located at the edge of town that offers guests modern rooms, a rooftop bar, and a spa.

  2. Hotel Adler: This hotel blends medieval charm with modern comfort and style, offering guests spacious rooms, fine dining, and wellness facilities.

What to Buy in Rottweil: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  1. Black Forest Ham: This smoked and cured ham is a locally produced delicacy that visitors can take home with them.

  2. Black Forest Cuckoo Clocks: Traditional cuckoo clocks are a staple of the Black Forest region and make for a great souvenir to bring back home.

Local Culture and Customs of Rottweil

  1. Fasnet Carnival: This annual event is one of the most important traditions in Rottweil. During the carnival, locals dress up in wooden masks and traditional costumes, and parade through the town streets.

  2. Swabian dialect: The majority of people in the Black Forest region speak Swabian dialect, which is very different from standard German. It’s always fun to try out some local phrases and see how much you can pick up.

Best Time to Visit Rottweil

The best time to visit Rottweil is between May and October when the weather is pleasant and perfect for outdoor activities. The carnival season in February is another great time to visit if you want to experience Swabian culture at its best.

Do’s and Don’ts of Rottweil

  • Try out the local food and drink.

  • Enjoy the outdoors and nature.

Don’t:

Safety and Health

Safety in Rottweil

Overall Rottweil is a safe place to visit with a low crime rate. However, it’s always important to be vigilant and take necessary safety precautions.

Health in Rottweil

Germany has excellent healthcare facilities, and Rottweil is no exception. In case of any emergency, dial 112, which is the emergency number in Germany.

Getting to Rottweil: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Rottweil

  1. Stuttgart Airport (STR): This airport is located about 100 km from Rottweil and serves several European and International destinations. From Stuttgart Airport, take the train to Rottweil.

  2. Zurich Airport (ZRH): This airport is located about 120 km from Rottweil and serves several European and International destinations. From Zurich Airport, take the train to Rottweil.

Bus Stations Near Rottweil

  1. Rottweil Busbahnhof: This is the central bus station in Rottweil and connects the town to several regional and national destinations.

Other Options

  1. Train: Rottweil is well-connected by trains, and there are frequent connections from major cities like Stuttgart and Munich.

How to Get Around in Rottweil

  1. Walking: Rottweil is a small town and many of its attractions are easily accessible on foot.

  2. Biking: Visitors can rent bikes and explore the town and its surroundings via bike trails.

  3. Public Transport: The town has a reliable bus network that connects the town with its surroundings.

Where to Go Next from Rottweil: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  1. Triberg: This charming town is located about 50 km from Rottweil and is known for its waterfalls and Black Forest souvenirs.

  2. Freiburg: This is a vibrant university town with a rich culture and history, and is located about 75 km from Rottweil.

  3. Stuttgart: This bustling city is located about 95 km from Rottweil and has plenty of attractions, including the Mercedes-Benz Museum and the Stuttgart Zoo.
