Kehl is a quaint town located in the scenic region of Black Forest, in Baden-Württemberg, Germany. This charming destination is known for being the gateway to the neighboring city of Strasbourg in France, making it a popular spot for tourists visiting both countries. Kehl is an ideal place to explore the beauty of the Black Forest, with its lush forests, rolling hills, and picturesque towns. This comprehensive guide will help you plan your trip to Kehl, and discover the best places to visit, things to do, where to stay, and more.

Introduction to Kehl

Kehl is a small town located on the Rhine River and is separated from Strasbourg by a bridge. The town has a rich history, dating back to Roman times, and has retained its old-world charm. Kehl is ideal for travelers looking for a relaxing vacation, outdoor activities, and cultural experiences. The town boasts several museums, a beautiful natural park, hiking and biking trails, and a vibrant food scene.

List of Places to Visit in Kehl

Here are some of the top destinations to visit in Kehl:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • St. Stephan Church: This beautiful church dates back to the 13th century and is known for its stunning stained-glass windows.

  • Municipal Museum of Kehl: Learn about the town’s history and culture through various exhibits, including art, artifacts, and exhibitions.

  • The Bridge Museum: This museum is located in the old toll station building and showcases the history of the bridge connecting Kehl and Strasbourg.

Natural Attractions of Kehl: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Taubergießen Nature Reserve: This impressive park is home to a diverse range of flora and fauna and offers excellent hiking, boating, and birdwatching opportunities.

  • Rhine Promenade: Enjoy a leisurely stroll along the Rhine River, taking in the picturesque views of the river and the surrounding town.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Kehl

Kehl is a paradise for outdoor enthusiasts with several hiking and biking trails to explore. The Taubergießen Park and Rhine Promenade are excellent places to start your adventure. The bike path along the Rhine River is a popular route for cyclists, offering stunning views of the Black Forest and the river.

Best Things to Do in Kehl: Activities and Tours

  • Wine Tasting: Kehl is famous for its wines, and visitors can experience wine tasting at several vineyards and wine cellars in the area.

  • Boat Tours: Explore the Rhine River aboard a boat tour, which offers a unique perspective of the region.

  • Segway Tours: Discover the town in a new way with a Segway tour, taking in the sights and sounds of the town in a fun and exciting way.

Week-Long Itinerary for Kehl

Day 1: Explore the town center and visit the Municipal Museum of Kehl

Day 2: Hike through the Taubergießen Nature Reserve

Day 3: Take a boat tour on the Rhine River

Day 4: Visit the Bridge Museum and St. Stephan Church

Day 5: Indulge in wine tasting at the local vineyards

Day 6: Cycle along the Rhine River

Day 7: Enjoy a Segway tour of the town

Food and Drink Scene of Kehl

Kehl is famous for its wines and local cuisine. Here are some of the must-try dishes and local beverages.

Must-Try Dishes

  • Black Forest Ham: This smoked ham is a local delicacy and is known for its rich flavor.

  • Flammkuchen: This specialty dish is a type of pizza, featuring a thin crust topped with crème fraiche, onions, and bacon.

Local Beverages

  • Baden Wine: Sample the local wines, which are known for their delicate flavors and aromas.

  • German Beer: Kehl has several local breweries, serving up a range of German beers.

Where to Stay in Kehl: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Kehl has several accommodation options for visitors, from boutique hotels to cozy guesthouses.

  • Hotel Belle Vue: This charming hotel overlooks the Rhine River and offers comfortable rooms and a relaxing atmosphere.

  • Hotel Salm: Situated in the heart of the town, this hotel features elegant rooms and a restaurant serving traditional German cuisine.

  • Hostel Kehl: This affordable hostel is ideal for budget-conscious travelers, offering private and shared rooms.

What to Buy in Kehl: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Black Forest Cherry Cake: This sweet dessert is a local specialty and makes a great souvenir to take back home.

  • Cuckoo Clocks: Kehl is known for its traditional cuckoo clocks, which make an excellent souvenir or gift.

Local Culture and Customs of Kehl

Kehl has a rich cultural heritage, with several festivals and traditions to look out for, including the Kehl street festival in July and the Harvest Festival in October. Visitors should also be aware of local customs and etiquettes, such as being punctual, avoiding loud conversations in public places, and greeting strangers with a polite “hello.”

Best Time to Visit Kehl

The best time to visit Kehl is in the spring and fall, when the weather is mild and the crowds are thin. Summer is the peak season, and the town can get quite crowded, although this is also when most of the festivals and events take place. Winter is a quieter time, but the town is still charming and offers plenty of activities and attractions for visitors.

Do’s and Don’ts of Kehl

Do:

  • Try the local wines and cuisine

  • Respect local customs and traditions

  • Explore the natural beauty of the Black Forest

Don’t:

  • Speak loudly in public places

  • Be late for appointments

  • Forget to greet people with a polite “hello”

Safety and Health

Kehl is a safe destination, but visitors should take the usual precautions and be aware of petty crime in tourist areas. The town has several medical facilities, and medical services are of high quality.

Safety in Kehl

  • Avoid carrying large amounts of cash when walking around tourist areas

  • Be aware of your surroundings

Health in Kehl

  • Tap water is safe to drink

  • Make sure you have all your necessary medications

Getting to Kehl: Transportation Options and Logistics

Kehl is easily accessible by several transportation options.

  • Airports Near Kehl: The nearest airport is Strasbourg airport, which is located just across the river in France. The town is also accessible by Frankfurt airport, which is a two-hour drive from Kehl.

  • Bus Stations Near Kehl: Kehl has several bus stations, offering connections to nearby towns and cities.

  • Other Options: Visitors can also reach Kehl by train or car.

How to Get Around in Kehl

Kehl is a small town and is best explored by foot or bicycle. Visitors can also use the local buses, which offer affordable transportation to nearby towns and attractions.

Where to Go Next from Kehl: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Strasbourg: This vibrant French city is just a bridge away from Kehl, offering excellent shopping, dining, and cultural experiences.

  • Black Forest: Explore the natural beauty of the Black Forest, which is home to several charming towns and hiking trails.

Conclusion

Kehl is a hidden gem in the heart of the Black Forest, offering visitors a chance to explore nature, culture, and history. With this comprehensive guide, visitors can plan their trip to Kehl and explore the town’s must-visit attractions, try the delicious local cuisine, and indulge in outdoor adventures. Kehl has something for everyone, making it an excellent destination for families, couples, and solo travelers.
