Introduction to Reutlingen

Located in the heart of Baden-Württemberg, Reutlingen is a charming town nestled in the Swabian Mountains. This southern German town is a mix of old-world charm and modernity that is perfectly integrated into its landscape. Reutlingen is a perfect bustling city that is perfect for those looking for outdoor adventures, cultural experiences, and relaxing getaways.

List of Places to Visit in Reutlingen

There are plenty of things to see and do in Reutlingen. This section highlights the top places to visit during your stay in the town.

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Reutlingen offers a glimpse into its past with the various historical sites and museums located throughout the town.

  • Reutlingen Castle: This imposing castle, also known as “Achalm Castle,” sits atop the picturesque Achalm Mountain. A climb to the top of this mountain offers breathtaking views of the entire town below.
  • Gedenkstätte Synagoge: This memorial site commemorates the destruction of the Jewish synagogue during the Night of Broken Glass in 1938.
  • Heimatmuseum: The Heimatmuseum offers visitors a chance to explore Reutlingen’s past. The museum showcases the town’s rich history, from the Middle Ages to the present day.
  • Marienkirche: This beautiful church was built in the 14th-15th centuries, and its intricate Gothic architecture will take your breath away.

Natural Attractions of Reutlingen: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Nature enthusiasts and adventure-seekers will love the outdoor activities Reutlingen has to offer. Here are some of the top natural attractions you should visit.

  • Echaz Valley: This beautiful valley runs through the center of Reutlingen and offers scenic views of the town and its surroundings. You can go for a scenic walk or a bike ride to explore this valley.
  • Grafenberg: This hill located on the outskirts of Reutlingen is a popular hiking spot, with beautiful panoramic views of the town.
  • Wolfsberg: This hill is situated in the north of Reutlingen, and it is a popular destination for biking enthusiasts.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Reutlingen

Reutlingen is a hiker’s and biker’s paradise. Here are some of the most popular hiking and biking routes you should explore during your visit.

  • Achalm trail: This trail takes you to the top of the Achalm Mountain, offering panoramic views of Reutlingen and its surrounding areas.
  • Panorama trail: This trail is an easy hike that takes you through the scenic Echaz Valley.
  • Schwäbische Alb: Explore the Swabian Alps on foot or bike through this popular trail.

Best Things to Do in Reutlingen: Activities and Tours

Besides hiking and biking, Reutlingen has plenty of other activities and tours to offer. Here are some of the best things to do during your stay:

  • Go shopping: Reutlingen has plenty of stores, including the famous “Hirschstraße” shopping area.
  • Visit the market: Head over to “Marktplatz” and experience the traditional German farmer’s market Thursdays and Saturdays.
  • Take a brewery tour: Experience one of Reutlingen’s traditional breweries and learn more about Germany’s beer culture.
  • Attend a festival: Summer is usually packed with various events and activities, such as the traditional “Stadtfest” and the “Reutlinger Kulturnacht.”

Week-Long Itinerary for Reutlingen

If you’re planning to spend a week in Reutlingen, this itinerary will help you plan for the best trip.

Day Activity
Day 1 Explore “Altstadt” (Old Town), and visit Marienkirche, Museum im Dorf, and Reutlingen Castle.
Day 2 Take a hike on Achalm trail and visit Gedenkstätte Synagoge in the evening.
Day 3 Take a bike ride on the Panorama trail and visit Heimatmuseum.
Day 4 Visit the market at “Marktplatz” in the morning, followed by a brewery tour in the afternoon.
Day 5 Visit Tubingen and explore the town’s traditional architecture and picturesque areas.
Day 6 Take a day trip to Stuttgart and explore the capital of Baden-Württemberg.
Day 7 Relax and enjoy the town’s beauty by exploring its parks and leisure areas like Echaz Valley or Grafenberg.

Food and Drink Scene of Reutlingen

Reutlingen’s food scene offers visitors a chance to taste traditional Swabian and German dishes. Here’s what you should try:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Maultaschen: A Swabian dish similar to dumplings, filled with minced meat and onions.
  • Schupfnudeln: Small, finger-shaped potato dumplings, often served with bacon and sauerkraut.
  • Spätzle: A type of egg noodle dish served with cheese and onions.

Local Beverages

  • Feuerwehrschluck: A herbal liqueur that translates to “Fire Brigade Gulp.”
  • Radler: A popular German drink made of beer and lemonade.

Where to Stay in Reutlingen: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Whether you prefer a luxurious hotel or a cozy guesthouse, Reutlingen has plenty of options to choose from.

  • Achalm Hotel: This beautiful hotel is located at the top of the Achalm mountain and offers stunning panoramic views of Reutlingen and its surroundings.
  • Hotel Fortuna: Located in the town’s center, Hotel Fortuna offers guests an opportunity to explore the town’s restaurants, museums, and historic attractions.
  • Gasthof Ochsen: This beautiful guesthouse combines traditional Swabian architecture with modern amenities.

What to Buy in Reutlingen: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Reutlingen offers plenty of local products and souvenirs to bring back home.

  • Achalm Mountain honey: Locally sourced honey from the Achalm mountain.
  • Reutlingen glass: Handmade glass products that are unique to the town.

Local Culture and Customs of Reutlingen

Reutlingen, like the rest of the Swabian region, is known for its unique language and customs. The town has a proud heritage of crafts, art, and architecture, which are on full display throughout the town.

Best Time to Visit Reutlingen

The best time to visit Reutlingen is during the summer months from June through August when the weather is warm and sunny. Autumn in September is also a great time to visit, with beautiful foliage, cooler temperatures, and fewer crowds.

Do’s and Don’ts of Reutlingen

Do:

  • Try the local Swabian cuisine and drink.
  • Explore the town’s many hiking and biking trails.
  • Attend a festival or event.

Don’t:

  • Expect everyone to speak English. Learning a few German words will go a long way.
  • Take pictures of people without their permission.
  • Litter.

Safety and Health

Safety in Reutlingen

Reutlingen is a relatively safe town, but it is essential to take normal precautions such as avoiding dark alleyways at night and keeping your valuables safe.

Health in Reutlingen

Reutlingen has plenty of hospitals and medical facilities to handle any medical emergency. For minor ailments and injuries, you can visit the local pharmacy.

Getting to Reutlingen: Transportation Options and Logistics

Reutlingen is located about 30 km from Stuttgart, making it easy to reach by car or public transportation.

Airports Near Reutlingen

  • Stuttgart Airport: The nearest airport to Reutlingen is Stuttgart, about 30 km away. From the airport, you can take a taxi, train, or bus to reach Reutlingen.

Bus Stations Near Reutlingen

  • Reutlingen Central Station: This is the town’s central bus station, connected to most major German cities.

Other Options

  • Car rental: Renting a car is another option, and it will give you the freedom to explore the surrounding areas on your own.

How to Get Around in Reutlingen

Reutlingen is a small town, and most of its attractions are within walking distance. For those who prefer to take public transportation, the town has an efficient network of buses.

Where to Go Next from Reutlingen: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Reutlingen is in a beautiful location, with many interesting destinations nearby that are worth visiting.

  • Tubingen: A picturesque town located about 20 km southwest of Reutlingen, with beautiful traditional architecture and a relaxed atmosphere.
  • Stuttgart: The capital of Baden-Württemberg, Stuttgart is about 30 km away from Reutlingen and is famous for its museums, shopping districts, and the Mercedes-Benz Museum.
  • Schwäbische Alb: This beautiful, mountainous region is located about 20 km east of Reutlingen and is perfect for hiking, skiing, and exploring traditional German villages.

Conclusion

Reutlingen is a hidden gem in Germany that has something to offer for everyone. Whether you’re interested in exploring its rich history, hiking in the beautiful Swabian Mountains, or just want to relax and enjoy the town’s beauty, Reutlingen is the perfect destination.
