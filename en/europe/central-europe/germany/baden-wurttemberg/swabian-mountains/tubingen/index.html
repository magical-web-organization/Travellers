Ultimate Tübingen Travel Guide: Explore the Best of Tübingen

Located in the heart of Swabian Mountains, Tübingen is a scenic university town known for its stunning half-timbered buildings, cobbled streets, and picturesque riverbanks. With a rich history dating back to the Middle Ages, Tübingen has plenty to offer to history buffs, nature lovers, and foodies alike. In this ultimate Tübingen travel guide, we will take you through the best places to visit, things to do, where to stay, and more.

Introduction to Tübingen

Tübingen is a charming university town located in the southwestern German state of Baden-Württemberg. The town is known for its picturesque location on the banks of the River Neckar and its well-preserved medieval half-timbered houses. Tübingen is also home to Germany’s oldest university, founded in 1477, making it a hub of academic and cultural life in the region.

List of Places to Visit in Tübingen

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Hohentübingen Castle: A beautiful castle founded in the 11th century overlooking the town with a great view of the Vosges Mountains.

  • Stadtmuseum: Learn about the town’s history, culture, and traditions at this local museum.

  • Hauff Museum: This natural history museum is dedicated to the works of the famous paleontologist and geologist Gustav Hauff.

Natural Attractions of Tübingen: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Bebenhausen Abbey: The stunning abbey was founded in the 12th century and surrounded by beautiful gardens.

  • Schönbuch Natural Park: This picturesque nature reserve offers woodland walks, hikes, bike rides, and stunning views of the surrounding countryside.

  • Ammer Valley trail: A beautiful trail that runs from Tübingen to the contrasting industrial city of Reutlingen.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Tübingen

  • The Swabian Mountain Trail: A route that traverses most of the Swabian Mountain range with stunning views along the way.

  • The Panorama Trail: This breathtaking hike offers unparalleled views of the town and surrounding countryside.

  • The Neckar Valley Cycle Path: A beautiful bike path that follows the River Neckar, passing through stunning scenery and charming towns.

Best Things to Do in Tübingen: Activities and Tours

  • Boat Tours: Take a relaxing boat ride down the River Neckar and see the sights from a different perspective.

  • Explore the Old Town: Explore the cobbled streets, half-timbered houses, and quaint cafes of the Old Town.

  • Beer and Wine Tours: Explore the local beer and wine scene with a guided tour of the town’s best breweries and vineyards.

Food and Drink Scene of Tübingen

Must-Try Dishes

  • Maultaschen: A local specialty, similar to a large ravioli, filled with meat, breadcrumbs, and herbs.

  • Spätzle: A soft egg noodle dish, often served with cheese or served as a side dish.

  • Kässpätzle: Similar to regular Spätzle, but with extra cheese on top.

Local Beverages

  • Beers: Tübingen has a small but thriving beer scene, with several local breweries producing unique and delicious beers.

  • Wine: The surrounding vineyards produce some of the best wines in all of Germany, with local favorites including Riesling and Pinot Noir.

Where to Stay in Tübingen: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Hotel Krone: This historic hotel is located in the heart of the Old Town and offers traditional rooms.

  • Hotel am Schloss: Located next to the Hohentübingen Castle, this hotel offers stunning views and modern amenities.

  • Youth Hostel Tübingen: This affordable hostel is located just a short walk from the Old Town and offers basic yet comfortable accommodations.

What to Buy in Tübingen: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Beer and Wine: Pick up a bottle of local beer or wine to bring home with you.

  • Handmade Crafts: The town is known for its traditional craft shops, selling items such as pottery and woodcarvings.

  • Schwäbisch Haller: This herb liquor is a local specialty and makes for a great souvenir.

Local Culture and Customs of Tübingen

The people of Tübingen are warm and welcoming, and often take pride in their Swabian heritage and traditions. Locals tend to speak German, but English is widely spoken, especially in tourist areas. Tipping is usually expected in restaurants and cafes.

Best Time to Visit Tübingen

The best time to visit Tübingen is from May to September, when the weather is warm and there are plenty of outdoor activities to enjoy. The town is especially beautiful during the fall, when the surrounding forests and vineyards change color.

Do’s and Don’ts of Tübingen

Do:

  • Try the local food and drinks

  • Explore the Old Town and Hohentübingen Castle

  • Take a bike ride or hike through the surrounding countryside

Don’t:

  • Forget to tip in restaurants and cafes

  • Be disrespectful of local customs and traditions

  • Litter or damage any of the town’s historic buildings

Safety and Health

Safety in Tübingen

Tübingen is generally a very safe town, with low levels of crime. However, travelers should be aware of their surroundings and take standard precautions while exploring the town at night.

Health in Tübingen

Visitors to Tübingen should have up-to-date routine vaccinations, including measles, mumps, rubella, diphtheria-tetanus-pertussis, varicella, and the annual flu shot. Travelers may also wish to speak with their healthcare provider regarding additional vaccinations.

Getting to Tübingen: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Tübingen

The nearest airport is Stuttgart Airport, located about 30 miles from Tübingen. From there, visitors can take a train or bus to Tübingen.

Bus Stations Near Tübingen

The town has a central bus station located in the Old Town, with regular connections to nearby towns and cities.

Other Options

Tübingen is easily accessible by train, with regular connections to Stuttgart and other major cities in the region.

How to Get Around in Tübingen

The town is easily navigable on foot, with most of the main attractions located within walking distance of each other. Bikes and e-bikes can be rented from several locations throughout the town, making it easy to explore the surrounding countryside.

Where to Go Next from Tübingen: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Stuttgart: The capital of Baden-Württemberg, known for its museums, parks, and impressive architecture.

  • Heidelberg: An idyllic university town known for its stunning Baroque architecture and romantic river views.

  • Schwarzwald: The Black Forest region offers stunning natural beauty and charming villages, known for their traditional crafts and cuisine.

Conclusion

Tübingen is a unique and vibrant town that has managed to preserve its rich history and culture while also embracing modernity. From exploring the picturesque Old Town to hiking in the surrounding countryside and sampling local delicacies, there’s something for everyone in Tübingen. With this ultimate Tübingen travel guide, we hope you’ll be well-equipped to plan an unforgettable trip to this charming town.
