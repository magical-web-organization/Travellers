Introduction to Solingen

Solingen is a charming city located in the heart of Bergisches Land, North Rhine-Westphalia, Germany. Known as the “City of Blades”, Solingen is famous for its production of high-quality steel and cutlery, which has been a major industry in the area for over 700 years. However, there is much more to Solingen than just knives and swords. With a rich cultural heritage, breathtaking natural landscapes, and a diverse range of attractions, Solingen is the perfect destination for travelers looking to explore and experience the best of what Germany has to offer.

List of Places to Visit in Solingen

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Solingen’s fascinating history and cultural heritage can be discovered in its many museums and historical sites. Some of the must-visit attractions include:

  • The Schloss Burg Castle: Located on top of a hill above the Wupper river, this impressive castle dates back to the 12th century and offers stunning views of the surrounding countryside. It is also the perfect starting point for hiking and cycling tours.
  • The Museum Plagiarius: Named after the infamous “Plagiarius” awards, which are given out each year to companies that produce counterfeit products, this unique museum showcases a collection of counterfeit items in various categories.
  • The Deutsches Klingenmuseum: Housed in a former cutlery factory, this museum is entirely dedicated to the art and history of blades and swords. With an impressive collection of over 15,000 pieces from all over the world, it is a must-visit for anyone interested in metallurgy and cutlery.

Natural Attractions of Solingen: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Solingen’s idyllic countryside offers plenty of opportunities for outdoor activities and scenic adventure. Some of the best natural attractions include:

  • The Bergischer Panorama-Radweg: This scenic cycling route runs for over 260 kilometers through the beautiful landscapes of the Bergisches Land region, passing through Solingen along the way.
  • The Wupper River: This picturesque river runs through the heart of Solingen and offers numerous opportunities for hiking, cycling, and water sports. The Wupperweg hiking trail is also an excellent way to explore the area’s stunning natural landscapes.
  • The Solingen Botanical Garden: Covering an area of over 14 hectares, this botanical garden is a paradise for nature lovers. With over 10,000 species of plants from around the world, it is the perfect place to relax and explore.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Solingen

Solingen is renowned for its extensive network of hiking and cycling trails, which traverse through its stunning countryside landscapes. Some of the best trails include:

  • The Bergisches Wanderland: With over 5,000 kilometers of hiking trails, this is one of the largest hiking regions in Germany. It offers a diverse range of hiking options for all levels, with stunning scenery along the way.
  • The Panorama Cycle Path: This 45-kilometer-long route takes riders through some of the most beautiful parts of the Bergisches Land region, including Solingen. It offers picturesque views of rivers, mountains, and forests.

Best Things to Do in Solingen: Activities and Tours

Solingen offers a wide range of activities and tours that cater to all kinds of travelers. Some of the best things to do include:

  • The Solingen Knife-Making Workshop: Visitors can join a knife-making workshop and learn how to create their own custom knife under the guidance of a professional blade-smith. It’s a unique souvenir to take home.
  • The Solingen Food Tour: This guided tour takes visitors on a culinary journey through Solingen’s best local restaurants and food stands, allowing them to taste some of the area’s most delicious and traditional dishes.
  • The Bergischer Panorama-Steig: This stunning hiking route takes visitors through the Bergisches Land region’s most beautiful landscapes, including Solingen, and offers breathtaking panoramic views of the surrounding countryside.

Week-Long Itinerary for Solingen

For those who wish to explore Solingen in-depth, here is a suggested itinerary for a week-long trip:

Day 1: Explore the historical city center and visit the Plagiarius Museum.

Day 2: Take a guided tour of the Deutsches Klingenmuseum and visit the Solingen Knife-Making Workshop.

Day 3: Hike the Wupperweg hiking trail and spend an afternoon at the Bergisch Botanical Garden.

Day 4: Visit Schloss Burg Castle and explore the nearby Bergischer Panorama-Steig trail.

Day 5: Take a bike ride along the Bergischer Panorama-Radweg trail and stop for lunch at one of the trail’s charming small towns.

Day 6: Take part in a guided Solingen Food Tour and explore the city’s culinary scene.

Day 7: Relax and enjoy a day trip to nearby cities and towns such as Düsseldorf, Cologne, or Wuppertal.

Food and Drink Scene of Solingen

Must-Try Dishes

Solingen’s food scene is a diverse mix of international cuisines and traditional local dishes. Some of the must-try dishes include:

  • Solinger Spießbraten: A unique dish made with marinated pork, bacon, onions, and mustard, grilled slowly on a spit.
  • Bergische Kaffeetafel: A traditional afternoon tea service found in the Bergisch Land region, which includes a variety of sweet and savory treats such as local cheeses, smoked salmon, and sweet pastries.
  • Reibekuchen: Fried potato pancakes served with apple sauce or sour cream, a popular German dish especially popular during the winter months.

Local Beverages

Solingen’s beverage scene is equally diverse, with a range of local beers and spirits on offer. Some of the most popular drinks include:

  • Korn: A traditional German spirit made from fermented and distilled rye, often enjoyed as a shot with a slice of lemon or lime.
  • Bergisch Land Beer: Locally brewed, this beer is renowned for its flavorful and refreshing taste.
  • Apfelschorle: A refreshing drink made from apple juice and carbonated water, which is a popular non-alcoholic drink in Germany.

Where to Stay in Solingen: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Solingen offers a variety of accommodation options for visitors, ranging from budget-friendly hostels and guesthouses to luxury hotels. Some of the best places to stay include:

  • Waldhotel Eskeshof: A charming four-star hotel located in the heart of the Bergisches Land region, offering luxurious rooms, spa facilities, and a golf course.
  • City Hotel Solingen: A modern hotel located in the heart of Solingen’s city center, with comfortable rooms and easy access to local attractions.
  • DJH Bergischer Hof: A budget-friendly hostel located just outside the city center, offering simple but comfortable rooms and a friendly atmosphere.

What to Buy in Solingen: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Solingen is famous for its high-quality cutlery and blades, making it the perfect destination for anyone looking to buy a unique and functional souvenir. Some of the best products to bring home include:

  • Solingen knives and scissors: Widely regarded as some of the best cutlery in the world, Solingen’s knives and scissors are available in a variety of styles and designs.
  • Solingen swords and sabers: For those with a passion for history and martial arts, Solingen’s swords and sabers are excellent souvenirs.
  • Bergisch Land artisanal products: The Bergisches Land region is home to a variety of artisanal products, including crafts, pottery, and textiles, which make perfect souvenirs to take home.

Local Culture and Customs of Solingen

Solingen has a rich cultural heritage, shaped by its long history and regional identity. Some of the unique cultural customs and traditions in Solingen include:

  • The Solingen Kirmes: This annual festival, held in late August, is a popular event that celebrates the region’s traditional customs and cultural heritage with a variety of food and drink stands, live music, and carnival attractions.
  • Bergisch Breakfast: A traditional breakfast service found in the Bergisches Land region, which includes local products such as cheeses, cold cuts, and sweet pastries.

Best Time to Visit Solingen

The best time to visit Solingen is during the summer months, between June and September, when the weather is warm and pleasant. However, Solingen is also an excellent destination for winter sports enthusiasts, with numerous opportunities for skiing and snowboarding in the nearby mountains.

Do’s and Don’ts of Solingen

Do:

  • Do try the local cuisine, including Solinger Spießbraten and Bergische Kaffeetafel.
  • Do visit the Deutsches Klingenmuseum and learn about the city’s long history of cutlery production.
  • Do explore Solingen’s scenic countryside on foot or by bike.

Don’t:

  • Don’t forget to try the local Korn, a traditional spirit made from rye.
  • Don’t be afraid to haggle at local street markets or bazaars.
  • Don’t forget to bring suitable outdoor clothing, as the weather in the Bergisches Land region can be unpredictable.

Safety and Health

Safety in Solingen

Solingen is a safe and welcoming city, and visitors are unlikely to encounter any major safety issues. However, travelers should be cautious of pickpockets in busy tourist areas and keep an eye on their belongings at all times.

Health in Solingen

Solingen has excellent healthcare facilities, and visitors can access medical treatment easily if needed. However, visitors should have comprehensive travel insurance that covers medical expenses and repatriation in case of an emergency.

Getting to Solingen: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Solingen

Cologne-Bonn Airport is the closest airport to Solingen, located approximately 50 kilometers away. Düsseldorf International Airport is also an excellent option, located 40 kilometers away.

Bus Stations Near Solingen

Solingen has excellent bus connections to neighboring cities and towns, including Düsseldorf and Cologne. Solingen Central Station is the main bus station in the city, offering connections to local and regional destinations.

Other Options

Solingen is also well connected by train and car, with easy access to major highways and rail lines. Visitors can take a direct train from Cologne or Düsseldorf to Solingen Central Station, or rent a car and drive to Solingen using the A46 motorway.

How to Get Around in Solingen

Solingen has a comprehensive public transportation system, including buses and trains, which are an affordable and convenient way to get around the city. Visitors can also rent bikes from local rental shops or explore the city on foot.

Where to Go Next from Solingen: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Solingen is located in the heart of the Bergisches Land region, offering numerous opportunities for excursions and day trips to nearby destinations such as:

  • Düsseldorf: One of Germany’s most vibrant cities, with a rich cultural heritage and world-class shopping and nightlife.
  • Cologne: Known for its stunning cathedral and vibrant arts scene, Cologne is a must-visit destination for anyone exploring the region.
  • Wuppertal: A charming city located just 20 kilometers away from Solingen, known for its unique suspension railway and rich cultural heritage.

Conclusion

Solingen is a captivating city located in the heart of Germany’s Bergisches Land region, offering visitors a unique blend of cultural heritage, stunning natural landscapes, and exciting activities. Whether you want to explore the city’s rich history, indulge in its culinary delights, or explore the beautiful countryside on foot or by bike, Solingen has something to offer everyone.
