Introduction to Mettmann

Located in the picturesque Bergisches Land region of Germany, Mettmann is a charming small town that sits on the edge of beautiful countryside. It’s an ideal destination for those looking for an escape from the hustle and bustle of city life and a chance to enjoy the natural beauty of Central Europe. The town is also home to a rich cultural heritage, making it a great place to explore the history and traditions of this part of Germany.

List of Places to Visit in Mettmann

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • The Neanderthal Museum: This world-renowned museum is dedicated to the study of Neanderthal man. Visitors can explore reconstructed habitats, artifacts, and interactive exhibits that bring to life the world of our ancient ancestors.

  • Museum Abteiberg: A modern art museum located in the nearby city of Mönchengladbach. The museum has an extensive collection of contemporary art from around the world.

Natural Attractions of Mettmann: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Neanderthal Valley: This beautiful valley is where the Neanderthal man was discovered in 1856. Visitors can hike through the stunning landscape of rolling hills, wooded areas, and picturesque streams.

  • Bergische Streifzüge: A network of hiking and biking trails that cover the picturesque countryside surrounding Mettmann. There are trails of various lengths and difficulties, so hikers and bikers of all levels can find something to suit them.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Mettmann

Mettmann is surrounded by stunning natural landscapes, and there’s no better way to explore them than on foot or on a bike. The Bergische Streifzüge is the perfect place to start, offering a network of trails that cover the surrounding hills and valleys.

Best Things to Do in Mettmann: Activities and Tours

  • Take a guided tour of the Neanderthal Museum to learn about the fascinating history of Neanderthal man.

  • Visit Düsseldorfer Straße, the town’s main shopping street, for a chance to explore local boutiques and shops.

Week-Long Itinerary for Mettmann

Day 1: Explore the Neanderthal Museum. Day 2: Hike or bike through the Bergische Streifzüge trails. Day 3: Visit the nearby Museum Abteiberg in Mönchengladbach. Day 4: Relax in the town’s beautiful parks and gardens. Day 5: Take a day trip to nearby Düsseldorf to explore its rich culture and heritage. Day 6: Enjoy outdoor activities, such as golf or horseback riding, in the surrounding countryside. Day 7: Explore the local food and drink scenes.

Food and Drink Scene of Mettmann

Must-Try Dishes

  • Mett: A traditional German dish made from minced raw pork and served on bread.

  • Sauerbraten: A hearty beef roast marinated in vinegar and served with potatoes or noodles.

Local Beverages

  • Altbier: A dark, malty beer that is a specialty of the nearby city of Düsseldorf.

  • Killepitsch: A herbal liqueur distilled from over 98 different herbs, roots, fruits, and spices.

Where to Stay in Mettmann: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

There are several great accommodation options in Mettmann, from budget hostels to luxury hotels. Some popular options include:

  • Hotel Alberga: This welcoming hotel is located in the heart of the town and offers comfortable rooms, free breakfast, and easy access to local attractions.

  • Hotel Gut Höhne: For those looking for a five-star experience, this beautiful hotel is set in lush gardens and offers luxurious rooms, spa services, and gourmet dining.

What to Buy in Mettmann: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Some great souvenirs to bring back from Mettmann include:

  • Local beer and wine.
  • Traditional German handicrafts, such as woodcarvings, pottery, and glassware.
  • Sweets and chocolates from local confectioners.

Local Culture and Customs of Mettmann

Germany has a rich cultural heritage, and Mettmann is no exception. The town is famous for its wine and beer, which are both deeply ingrained in German culture. Visitors should also be aware of the country’s strong tradition of punctuality and respect for personal space.

Best Time to Visit Mettmann

The best time to visit Mettmann is during the spring or fall, when the weather is mild and the crowds are thinner. Summer is also a popular time to visit, but it can be quite hot and crowded.

Do’s and Don’ts of Mettmann

Do:

  • Try the local food and drink, especially the beer and wine.
  • Explore the town’s beautiful parks and gardens.
  • Respect local customs and traditions.

Don’t:

  • Be overly loud or boisterous in public.
  • Ignore the town’s rich cultural heritage.

Safety and Health

Safety in Mettmann

Mettmann is generally a safe and peaceful town, but visitors should still take basic precautions against theft and other petty crimes. Be aware of your surroundings and avoid walking alone at night.

Health in Mettmann

Germany has excellent healthcare facilities, and visitors can generally expect high-quality medical care if needed. It’s always a good idea to have travel insurance in case of emergencies.

Getting to Mettmann: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Mettmann

The nearest international airports to Mettmann are Düsseldorf Airport (DUS) and Cologne Bonn Airport (CGN). Both airports are about 30 minutes away by car or public transportation.

Bus and Train Stations Near Mettmann

Mettmann is served by several bus lines and a train station, making it easy to get around the town and the surrounding area.

Other Options

For those driving to Mettmann, there are several major highways that run through the area, and there is ample parking in the town and surrounding countryside.

How to Get Around in Mettmann

The best way to get around Mettmann is by walking or biking. The town is small enough to explore on foot, and there are plenty of bike trails that cover the surrounding countryside.

Where to Go Next from Mettmann: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

There are several great destinations and excursions within easy reach of Mettmann, including:

  • Düsseldorf: A bustling city famous for its vibrant cultural scene and exciting nightlife.

  • Cologne: Home to the magnificent Cologne Cathedral and numerous museums and galleries.

Conclusion

Mettmann is a hidden gem, offering a charming mix of natural beauty, rich cultural heritage, and delicious food and drink. Whether you’re looking for outdoor adventures, cultural experiences, or just a chance to relax and unwind, this beautiful town is sure to delight.
