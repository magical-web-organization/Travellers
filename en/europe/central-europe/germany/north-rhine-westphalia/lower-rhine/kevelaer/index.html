Introduction to Kevelaer

Kevelaer is a charming town located in Lower Rhine, in North Rhine-Westphalia, Germany. Situated in the heart of Central Europe, this little town is known for its rich cultural and religious heritage, picturesque churches and chapels, and beautiful natural surroundings. Kevelaer attracts millions of visitors every year, who come here to explore its many attractions, take part in outdoor activities, and experience the local Bavarian culture.

In this comprehensive tour guide, we’ll take you on a journey to discover the best of Kevelaer. From the must-visit museums and historical sites to the best hiking and biking routes, food and drink scene, where to stay, and much more. We’ve got you covered for all your Kevelaer travel needs.

List of Places to Visit in Kevelaer

Kevelaer has a lot to offer, from historic landmarks to natural wonders, there is something for every traveler. Let’s take a look at the top places to visit in Kevelaer.

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Marienbasilika: This impressive Baroque church is one of the key highlights of Kevelaer. The church is intricately decorated with ornate murals, gold-leaf detailing, and features a large dome painted with biblical scenes.
  • Kerzenkapelle: Also known as the “Candle Chapel,” this small but charming chapel is famous for its twelve apostles and magnificent altar with life-size statues of Mary and Jesus.
  • Museum Kurhaus: This museum showcases the works of local artists and hosts temporary exhibitions throughout the year.
  • Niederrheinisches Museum: Located in the heart of the town, the museum displays over 2000 years of regional history, including archaeological artifacts and rare paintings.

Natural Attractions of Kevelaer: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Klingenheide Nature Reserve: Hike through the serene landscapes of the Klingenheide Nature Reserve, which is home to a diverse array of flora and fauna. Visitors can see the wild orchids and explore the marshes and lakes on the hiking trails.
  • Irrland: This family-friendly adventure park is located on the outskirts of Kevelaer and features an array of outdoor activities like ziplining, pedal boats, and extensive playgrounds for kids of all ages.
  • Marienweg: A pilgrimage walk that starts from the Marienbasilika and takes you through breathtaking landscapes, passing many chapels and stations of the cross.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Kevelaer

Kevelaer is a perfect destination for hiking and biking enthusiasts. Whether you’re a seasoned pro or a beginner exploring the town’s scenic routes, there’s an adventure for everyone. Here are some of the best hiking/biking routes to explore:

  • Wasserburgroute: This 45 km-long route passes through the beautiful countryside and the historic city of Kevelaer, passing water castles and scenic vistas.
  • Bokernroute: This 18 km-long route starts at Niederrheinisches Museum and takes you through forests and scenic farmlands.
  • Krippenwegrunde: This 14 km-long hiking route passes through the most important pilgrimage sites in Kevelaer. The picturesque scenery and rich history make this route a must for tourists.

Best Things to Do in Kevelaer: Activities and Tours

  • Take part in a guided tour of the town: Many local tour companies offer guided tours of the town, providing insights into the history, culture, and architecture of Kevelaer.
  • Visit Kevelaer’s Christmas Market: The Christmas market in Kevelaer is a must-visit for anyone traveling during the holiday season. Enjoy live music, traditional food, and crafts while admiring the dazzling lights.
  • Try the local cuisine: Kevelaer is famous for its hearty Bavarian cuisine. Visitors can taste local delicacies like Sauerbraten and Reibekuchen, or enjoy a traditional German beer at one of the many cozy pubs and restaurants.
  • Attend the annual Marienfest: Marienfest is a religious festival celebrated annually in Kevelaer in honor of the town’s patron saint, Our Lady of Kevelaer. The festival features parades, processions, and religious ceremonies, attracting visitors from all over the world.

Week-Long Itinerary for Kevelaer

Day 1: Visit the Marienbasilika and Kerzenkapelle, followed by a tour of the Niederrheinisches Museum.

Day 2: Explore the Klingenheide Nature Reserve and enjoy scenic hiking and biking trails.

Day 3: Take part in a guided tour of the historic city center and visit the Museum Kurhaus.

Day 4: Visit Irrland Adventure Park and enjoy outdoor activities, games, and rides.

Day 5: Walk the Marienweg pilgrimage route and take in the breathtaking views.

Day 6: Attend the annual Marienfest and take part in the religious procession and festivities.

Day 7: Relax and explore the local culture and cuisine at one of Kevelaer’s many pubs and restaurants.

Food and Drink Scene of Kevelaer

Must-Try Dishes

  • Sauerbraten: A classic Braised beef dish, Sauerbraten is one of the staples of Kevelaer’s cuisine.
  • Reibekuchen: These hearty potato pancakes are a popular snack in Kevelaer and are often served with apple sauce or sour cream.
  • Erbsensuppe: A traditional pea soup made with dried peas, potatoes, smoked sausages, and herbs.

Local Beverages

  • Kolsch: This light, crisp beer originated in Cologne and is a popular choice in Kevelaer.
  • Gluhwein: This mulled wine is a staple at Christmas markets, typically made with red wine and spices.

Where to Stay in Kevelaer: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Kevelaer has a range of accommodation options for all budgets. Here are some of our top picks:

  • Landhaus Spickermann: Located in the countryside, this charming guesthouse has rooms with garden views and offers a delicious breakfast buffet.
  • Hotel Klostergarten: This 3-star hotel offers comfortable accommodation, meeting facilities, and a restaurant with a terrace.
  • Hostel La Locanda: A budget-friendly option, Hostel La Locanda offers shared dorms and private rooms.

What to Buy in Kevelaer: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Kevelaer candles: These candles come in various sizes and colors, making for a perfect souvenir or gift.
  • Knecht Ruprecht: This nutty liqueur is a popular choice in Kevelaer and can be found in most liquor stores.
  • Traditional clothing: Shops in Kevelaer offer a range of traditional Bavarian clothing like lederhosen, dirndls, and hats.

Local Culture and Customs of Kevelaer

Kevelaer has a rich religious and cultural history, with Catholicism playing a prominent role in the town’s identity. The town is also known for its Bavarian customs, hearty cuisine, and warm hospitality.

Best Time to Visit Kevelaer

The best time to visit Kevelaer is during the summer months (June-August) when the weather is warm and sunny. Visitors can enjoy outdoor activities like hiking, biking, and swimming. The Christmas market in December is also a popular time to visit Kevelaer.

Do’s and Don’ts of Kevelaer

  • Be polite and courteous to locals
  • Enjoy the local cuisine and beer
  • Take part in the religious festivals and ceremonies

Don’t:

  • Be loud and disruptive in public places

Safety and Health

Safety in Kevelaer

Kevelaer is a safe town, but travelers should exercise the same level of caution as they would in any other destination. Keep an eye on your belongings, avoid walking alone at night, and follow basic safety precautions.

Health in Kevelaer

No specific health risks are associated with traveling to Kevelaer, and medical facilities in the town are adequate.

Kevelaer is a small town, and the best way to explore it is on foot or by bike. Taxis and buses are also available for longer distances.

Where to Go Next from Kevelaer: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Cologne: This vibrant city is only 1 hour away from Kevelaer and is famous for its Gothic cathedral and bustling shopping streets.
  • Dusseldorf: The “fashion city” of Germany is only 50 minutes away from Kevelaer and is famous for its creative art scene and nightlife.
  • Xanten Archaeological Park: Located just 30 minutes away from Kevelaer, this open-air museum showcases the remnants of a Roman settlement and offers various activities for all ages.
