Introduction to Neuss

Located in the picturesque Lower Rhine region of North Rhine-Westphalia, Neuss is a perfect blend of ancient and modern. The Roman empire once held the city’s grounds, and a lot of its historical landmarks represent the culture of those times. You can spend a relaxing day in the city, discovering its beautiful museums, monuments, gardens, and traditional cuisine. Neuss is a great spot to experience authentic German hospitality and countryside lifestyle, away from the tourist crowds.

List of Places to Visit in Neuss

Neuss has several attractions to keep you engaged for days. Here’s a list of must-visit places:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • The Clemens-Sels Museum: This museum houses art collections, cultural pieces, and local history displays. It is the perfect place for art lovers and history buffs.
  • The Obertor: This tower was once the main entrance to the city walls. Now, you can explore a museum, a commercial place, and local handicraft exhibitions inside the tower’s walls.
  • The Quirinus Münster: A Roman Catholic church that has been around since the 4th century, the Quirinus Münster is an architectural marvel worth witnessing.
  • The Museum Insel Hombroich: Spread over 62 hectares, this museum exhibits contemporary arts and sculptures amidst natural landscapes.

Natural Attractions of Neuss: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • The Rhine Promenade: This is the most peaceful and scenic spot in the city, perfect for a stroll along the Rhine’s banks. You can also take a relaxing bike or boat ride here.
  • The Uedesheimer Rheinbogen: It’s a long stretch along the Rhine river ideal for jogging and cycling while enjoying nature and picturesque views.
  • The Hammfeld: Featuring a lake, a park, and hiking trails, the Hammfeld is a perfect place to spend time with family or friends.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Neuss

Neuss has several hiking and cycling routes available for visitors to explore its natural beauty. The following are the most popular ones:

  • Neuss to Kaiserswerth: This route is around 17km long and features a beautiful natural scenery along the Rhine banks.
  • The “12-Seen-Route” in Meerbusch: The perfect place for avid cyclists, this route of around 45km takes you through several small lakes and parks.
  • The Erft Cycle Route: Ideal for families, this route is around 40km long and takes you through several small towns and natural reserves around Neuss.

Best Things to Do in Neuss: Activities and Tours

  • The Rhine Boat Tour: Take a boat ride, sip on delicious local beverages, and admire the stunning scenery of the Rhine river.
  • The Neuss Old Town Tour: Explore the city’s rich history by taking a guided tour of its medieval alleys, traditional pubs, and other historical landmarks.
  • The Alligator Park: A unique natural park housing several exotic reptiles, amphibians, and other animals from all around the world.

Week-Long Itinerary for Neuss

Day 1: Visit the museums and historical landmarks, like The Obertor and The Clemens-Sels Museum. Day 2: Explore the city’s natural beauty by visiting The Rhine Promenade and the Uedesheimer Rheinbogen. Day 3: Take a bike ride on the “12-Seen-Route” in Meerbusch. Day 4: Check out the famous Quirinus Münster and soak in the religious history. Day 5: Go shopping for local products and souvenirs in the city markets. Day 6: Take a relaxing boat ride along the Rhine river. Day 7: Relax and unwind at the Hammfeld while enjoying local food and beverages.

Food and Drink Scene of Neuss

Must-Try Dishes

  • Met Ochsreipe: It’s raw pork, typically served with onion, black pepper, and salt. It’s an absolute must-try if you’re fond of traditional German cuisine.
  • Rheinischer Sauerbraten: This dish is a marinated roast beef with sweet and sour sauce, served with red cabbage, potatoes, and apple sauce.
  • Himmel und Ääd: It’s a famous German dish that includes “heaven and earth” - potatoes and apples, mashed together.

Local Beverages

  • Kölsch: A light beer famous in Neuss and other parts of Western Germany.
  • Altbier: A dark beer unique to this region, brewed using traditional methods.
  • Rheinischer Riesling: A white wine with a fruity, aromatic taste, produced on the banks of the Rhine river.

Where to Stay in Neuss: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Hotels

  • Dorint Kongresshotel Düsseldorf Neuss: A five-star hotel ideal for travelers who want some luxury and comfort.
  • Holiday Inn Düsseldorf-Neuss: A popular mid-range hotel that is excellent for family vacations.
  • Commundo Tagungshotel Neuss: A budget-friendly hotel ideal for solo travelers and backpackers.

Hostels

  • Junges Hotel / Hostelling International: A great hostel ideal for backpackers and young travelers, offering free breakfast and a shared community space.
  • Stay in Style Hostel: A relatively new hostel in Neuss that offers a modern, comfortable atmosphere along with shared and private rooms.

Guesthouses

  • Haus am Rosengarten: A cozy and homely guesthouse that offers a pleasant stay in the city center.
  • Gästehaus Viktoria: A family-run guesthouse featuring charming rooms that provide a welcoming stay.

What to Buy in Neuss: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Clothing: Traditional German clothing, such as lederhosen, drindls, and German hats are readily available.
  • Souvenirs: Located in the Rhineland district, Neuss offers iconic souvenirs like authentic cuckoo clocks, beer steins, and glassware.
  • Local Products: If you’re fond of local food products, you might want to purchase Spekulatius (local biscuits), Nürnberger Lebkuchen (gingerbread made with almonds), and Riesling (local white wine).

Local Culture and Customs of Neuss

The people of Neuss are friendly and welcoming, and they take great pride in their heritage. If you’re visiting the town, it’s always good to respect their cultural customs. Also, communal or public nudity is strictly prohibited in the city area.

Best Time to Visit Neuss

The months from April to September are ideal for visiting Neuss. During these months, the weather is warm and pleasant, making it perfect for outdoor activities. This is also the time when most of the town’s festivals and events take place.

Do’s and Don’ts of Neuss

Do:

  • Always carry your passport or ID with you.
  • Explore the city by foot and bike to discover the traditional German way of life.
  • Try the local German cuisine and drinks.

Don’t:

  • Don’t indulge in public nudity.
  • Don’t litter and always keep the city clean.
  • Don’t be loud and always respect the local culture.

Safety and Health

Safety in Neuss

Neuss is a safe city to visit during the day and night time. However, don’t carry unnecessary valuables while traveling, and always keep your belongings safe. It’s also important to take care when walking or biking along the busy roads.

Health in Neuss

The health facilities in Neuss are top-notch and accessible. However, it’s always good to have travel insurance before visiting.

Getting to Neuss: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Neuss

  • Düsseldorf International Airport: This is the nearest airport, a 30-minute drive from the city center.

Bus Stations Near Neuss

  • Neuss Central Bus Station: This is the main bus station of the city, and it connects Neuss with several other cities and towns in the region.

Other Options

  • Train: Neuss is well connected via train, and it’s easy to reach the city from other major German cities.
  • Car: Neuss is well connected via highways, and it’s easy to reach the city from other places by car.

How to Get Around in Neuss

The best way to explore Neuss is on foot or on rented bicycles. The city has a well-developed public transportation network, including buses and trains, that connect all major tourist attractions.

Where to Go Next from Neuss: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

If you have more days in hand, here are the top places to visit near Neuss:

  • Düsseldorf: Located nearby, it’s a beautiful city famous for its art, local beer, fashion, and shopping spots.
  • Cologne: A beautiful city that has an awe-inspiring history and many iconic landmarks.
  • Maastricht: A stunning city located in Southeastern Netherlands that is famous for its art, fashion, and historic architecture.

Conclusion

Neuss is a charming and relaxing town that offers a perfect getaway from the hustle and bustle of city life. The city has a perfect blend of history, culture, and nature, making it an ideal spot for all types of travelers. The stunning natural beauty, authentic German cuisine, and friendly locals will surely win your heart. So start planning your trip to Neuss today!
