Introduction to Düsseldorf

Located in Lower Rhine, in North Rhine-Westphalia, Germany, Düsseldorf is a bustling, modern city with a rich cultural heritage. Known for its fashion, art scene, and world-class museums, Düsseldorf is also home to a variety of outdoor activities and stunning natural landscapes.

Whether you’re traveling for business or leisure, there’s something for everyone in Düsseldorf. In this comprehensive tour guide, we’ll cover the best places to visit, eat, and stay, as well as hiking and biking routes, local culture and customs, transportation options, and nearby excursions.

List of Places to Visit in Düsseldorf

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Düsseldorf boasts numerous museums and historical attractions, including:

  • Kunstpalast Museum
  • K20 and K21 Museum
  • Hetjens Museum
  • Stadtmuseum
  • Neanderthal Museum
  • Schloss Benrath

These museums and historical landmarks showcase the city’s art, history, and cultural heritage, and are a must-see for history and culture enthusiasts.

Natural Attractions of Düsseldorf: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Visitors to Düsseldorf can also enjoy a variety of outdoor activities and scenic landscapes, including:

  • Grafenberger Wald
  • Rheinpark
  • Unterbacher See
  • Kaiserswerth
  • Rheinuferpromenade

These outdoor attractions offer stunning views of nature and ample opportunities for hiking, biking, and picnicking.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Düsseldorf

Düsseldorf is a great destination for outdoor enthusiasts, with plenty of hiking and biking trails to explore. Some popular routes include:

  • Kaiserswerth to Neuss Trail
  • Rhine Cycle Path
  • Neanderlandsteig Trail
  • Nierstal Trail
  • Erkrath-Hochdahl Trail

Whether you’re a seasoned hiker or a casual cyclist, you’re sure to find a trail that suits your interests and skill level in Düsseldorf.

Best Things to Do in Düsseldorf: Activities and Tours

In addition to outdoor activities and cultural attractions, Düsseldorf offers a variety of activities and tours to keep visitors entertained. Some popular options include:

  • Rhine River Cruise
  • Tour of Altstadt (Old Town)
  • Hop-On Hop-Off Bus Tour
  • Düsseldorf Panoramic Sightseeing Tour
  • Walking Food Tour

These activities offer a fun, unique perspective on the city and are great for first-time visitors.

To make the most of your visit to Düsseldorf, we’ve put together a week-long itinerary that includes the city’s top attractions, activities, and excursions:

Food and Drink Scene of Düsseldorf

Must-Try Dishes

Düsseldorf is known for its hearty, flavorful cuisine, with traditional dishes like:

  • Rheinischer Sauerbraten
  • Himmel und Erde
  • Rievkooche
  • Flönz
  • Senfrostbraten

These dishes are often served with local beers and wines and are a must-try for foodies visiting Düsseldorf.

Local Beverages

Düsseldorf is also home to a variety of locally brewed beers, including traditional Altbier and Kölsch. Visitors can sample these beers at local breweries and beer halls throughout the city.

Where to Stay in Düsseldorf: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Visitors to Düsseldorf will find a variety of lodging options to suit any budget, including:

  • Steigenberger Parkhotel
  • Breidenbacher Hof
  • Hotel Nikko Düsseldorf
  • Backpackers Düsseldorf
  • A&O Düsseldorf Hauptbahnhof

These hotels, hostels, and guesthouses offer comfortable, convenient accommodations for visitors to Düsseldorf.

What to Buy: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Visitors to Düsseldorf can take home a variety of unique souvenirs and local products, including:

  • Killepitsch liqueur
  • Traditional Altbier glasses
  • Rhenish mustard
  • Scherenschnitte (traditional German paper art)
  • Local handicrafts and pottery

These souvenirs make great gifts for friends and family and are a way to bring a piece of Düsseldorf home with you.

Local Culture and Customs of Düsseldorf

Düsseldorf is a modern, cosmopolitan city with a rich cultural heritage. Visitors should be aware of local customs and traditions, including:

  • Tipping: It is customary to leave a tip of 10-15% at restaurants and cafes.
  • Dress Code: Dress is typically casual in Düsseldorf, though visitors may want to dress up for certain events or restaurants.
  • Punctuality: Germans value punctuality, so make sure to arrive on time for meetings and appointments.

Best Time to Visit Düsseldorf

The best time to visit Düsseldorf is from May to September when the weather is mild and sunny. Visitors can enjoy outdoor activities and events during this time, including beer festivals and music concerts.

Do’s and Don’ts of Düsseldorf

Do:

  • Try traditional Altbier.
  • Visit the city’s art museums and landmarks.
  • Go hiking or biking in Grafenberger Wald.
  • Explore Kaiserswerth and its castle.
  • Take a Rhine River Cruise.

Don’t:

  • Forget to tip at restaurants and cafes.
  • Be late for meetings or appointments.
  • Underestimate the city’s rich cultural heritage and contributions to the arts.

Safety and Health

Safety in Düsseldorf

Düsseldorf is generally a safe city for visitors, with low crime rates and a polite, friendly population. Visitors should still exercise basic safety precautions, such as not leaving valuables unattended and being aware of their surroundings at night.

Health in Düsseldorf

Visitors to Düsseldorf do not need any specific vaccinations. The city has excellent medical facilities, including hospitals and pharmacies, for any health needs or emergencies.

Getting to Düsseldorf: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Düsseldorf

The closest airport to Düsseldorf is Düsseldorf International Airport (DUS), which serves many major airlines and destinations. Other nearby airports include Cologne-Bonn Airport and Weeze-Niederrhein Airport.

Bus Stations Near Düsseldorf

Düsseldorf has several bus stations that serve regional and international destinations, including Düsseldorf Central Bus Station and Düsseldorf Airport Terminal Bus Station.

Other Options

Visitors can also reach Düsseldorf by train or car, with numerous train stations and highways connecting the city to nearby destinations.

How to Get Around in Düsseldorf

Düsseldorf has an extensive public transportation system, including buses, trams, and trains, that make it easy to get around the city. Visitors can also rent bikes or take taxis to explore the city at their own pace.

Where to Go Next from Düsseldorf: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Visitors to Düsseldorf can explore other nearby destinations and excursions, including:

  • Cologne
  • Bonn
  • Essen
  • Duisburg
  • Mönchengladbach

These cities offer their own unique attractions and culture, and are a great way to extend your trip and explore more of the region.

Conclusion

Düsseldorf is a vibrant, cosmopolitan city that offers something for everyone. Whether you’re interested in outdoor adventures, cultural attractions, or local cuisine, there’s plenty to see and do in Düsseldorf. Use this comprehensive tour guide to plan your trip and make the most of your visit to this beautiful city.
