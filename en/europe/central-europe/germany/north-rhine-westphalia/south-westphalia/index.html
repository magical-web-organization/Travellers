Introduction to South Westphalia

South Westphalia is a region located in the state of North Rhine-Westphalia, Germany, in Central Europe. It is a cultural and scenic area that attracts visitors throughout the year. South Westphalia boasts an assortment of cultural landmarks, scenic locations, and outdoor adventures for tourists.

List of Places to Visit in South Westphalia

Below is a list of top tourist spots in South Westphalia that you should include in your itinerary:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums, Castles, and Historical Places

  1. LWL Museum for Archeology
  2. Burg Altena Castle
  3. Hohenlimburg Castle
  4. Niederheide Open-Air Museum
  5. St. Mary’s Church in Soest
  6. The Westphalian Bell Museum

Natural Attractions of South Westphalia: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  1. Bigge Reservoir
  2. Kahler Asten Mountain
  3. Attahöhle Goblin Caves
  4. Arnsberg Forest and Wildlife Park
  5. Lahn-Dill Heights for Hiking, Biking and Winter Sports
  6. Balver Cave and Hiking Trail

Hiking/Biking Routes of South Westphalia

South Westphalia offers many paths for hiking and biking enthusiasts including the Sauerland High Height Trail, RuhrtalRadweg and the Lahn-Dill Heights Trail. The Lahn-Dill Heights Trail is famous for its extensive network of trails and passes through three different states of Germany.

Best Things to Do in South Westphalia: Activities and Tours

  1. Visit the famous archeological museum and Burg Altena Castle
  2. Take a hike in the scenic region of Rothaargebirge in Bigge Reservoir
  3. Explore the historic Hohenlimburg Castle and learn about the events that have shaped its history
  4. Go skiing or snowboarding in Winterberg, Sauerland
  5. Admire the unique natural landscape of Atta Cave

Week-Long Itinerary for South Westphalia

Day 1 - Visit LWL Museum for Archeology and explore the city of Soest Day 2 - Take a bike ride along the Ruhr River, visit bigge reservoir and relax at a nearby cafe Day 3 - Explore Hohenlimburg Castle and then travel to Arnsberg Forest and Wildlife Park Day 4 - Admire the beautiful Kahler Asten Mountain and take a ride on the panoramic cable car Day 5 - Visit the Niederheide Open-Air Museum and take a tour around the town of Balve Day 6 - Go shopping and bring home unique souvenirs and locally-made products Day 7 - End your trip in Buergerschaftshaus in Attendorn and enjoy the local food and drinks

Local Culture and Customs of South Westphalia

South Westphalia has a rich cultural heritage, and one of its most significant customs is ‘Schützenfest’. It is a traditional festival celebrated every year in this region featuring a parade, music, and dance.

Best Time to Visit South Westphalia

The best time to visit South Westphalia is from May to September when the weather is warm, but those who love winter sports should visit between December and March. For those who enjoy seasonal festivals, the best time would be in August during the annual Schützenfest, or November during the famous ‘Martinszug’ parade.

Do’s and Don’ts of South Westphalia

Do:

  1. Respect the local customs and traditions
  2. Try the traditional local delicacies
  3. Explore the scenic spots and natural attractions
  4. Bring cash to make purchases from local vendors

Don’t:

  1. Litter on the streets or natural parks
  2. Wear inappropriate clothing in holy sites or public places
  3. Take photographs without permission of the sites or individuals
  4. Be disrespectful of local customs and customs.
