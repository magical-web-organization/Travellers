Introduction to Meschede

Meschede is a picturesque town located in South Westphalia, North Rhine-Westphalia, Germany. It is situated in the heart of Europe, making it easily accessible to travelers from around the globe. Meschede boasts a plethora of cultural, natural, and outdoor attractions, making it an ideal destination for travelers who want to experience the best of Germany.

List of Places to Visit in Meschede

Meschede has a lot to offer travelers in terms of places to visit, including:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Kloster Grafschaft: A Benedictine monastery founded in the year 1072, this historical site is a must-visit destination for history buffs and art enthusiasts. The monastery houses a fascinating museum that showcases the rich cultural heritage of the region.
  • Schloss Laer: A beautiful baroque-style castle built in the 14th century, Schloss Laer is a testament to the architectural heritage of the region. Visitors can take a guided tour of the castle to learn more about its history and architecture.
  • Stadtmuseum Meschede: Located in the heart of the town, this museum showcases the history of Meschede and the surrounding area through an extensive collection of artifacts and exhibits.

Natural Attractions of Meschede: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Diemelsee: A large lake situated in the breathtakingly beautiful Sauerland region, the Diemelsee is a popular destination for outdoor activities such as hiking and fishing. Visitors can also enjoy a scenic boat ride on the lake.
  • Rothaarsteig: This famous hiking trail stretches over 150 kilometers through the beautiful landscape of North Rhine-Westphalia. It is a paradise for outdoor enthusiasts and nature lovers.
  • Hennetalsperre: A famous dam that is a popular destination for hiking and cycling. It is also an excellent location for bird watching and nature photography.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Meschede

Meschede has an extensive network of trails for hiking and biking. The top hiking and biking routes in Meschede are:

  • Rothaarsteig: As mentioned earlier, this famous trail is a must-visit destination for hiking enthusiasts.
  • Henne- und Ruhrquelle: This 8-kilometer hiking trail takes visitors to the source of the Henne and Ruhr rivers. It is an excellent option for beginners and families with children.
  • SauerlandRadring: This 84-kilometer bike trail takes visitors on a scenic tour of the Sauerland region. It is suitable for bikers of all skill levels.

Best Things to Do in Meschede: Activities and Tours

Meschede has plenty of activities and tours to keep visitors entertained, including:

  • Kayaking on the Ruhr river: Meschede offers visitors the opportunity to experience the thrill of kayaking down the Ruhr river.
  • Wine tasting at Weinkellerei Sauerland: Visitors can sample the local wine at this famous winery in Meschede.
  • Guided tour of Kloster Grafschaft: Visitors can take a guided tour of this ancient Benedictine monastery to learn more about its history and architecture.

Food and Drink Scene of Meschede

Meschede is famous for its local cuisine and wine. Some must-try dishes and beverages in Meschede are:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Pumpernickel: A type of German bread made from rye flour, pumpernickel is a staple food in Meschede.
  • Ruhrpottpfanne: A hearty dish made with potatoes, sausage, bacon, and onions, Ruhrpottpfanne is a must-try for foodies visiting Meschede.

Local Beverages

  • Sauerland wine: The Sauerland region is known for its excellent wine, and visitors can sample the best of it at Weinkellerei Sauerland.
  • Störtebeker beer: A famous German beer, Störtebeker is a popular choice among locals in Meschede.

Local Culture and Customs of Meschede

Meschede has a rich cultural and historical heritage. Visitors can experience the local culture and customs by:

  • Visiting local festivals: Meschede hosts several festivals throughout the year, including the Summer Festival, Christmas Market, and Oktoberfest.
  • Shopping at local markets: Visitors can shop for local handicrafts and souvenirs at the weekly market held in Meschede.

Best Time to Visit Meschede

The best time to visit Meschede is from May to September, when the weather is mild, and the outdoor attractions are open. Visitors can enjoy hiking, biking, and water activities in Meschede during the summer months.

Do’s and Don’ts of Meschede

Here are some do’s and don’ts for visitors to Meschede:

Do:

  • Tip your server: It is customary to leave a tip of 10% of the total bill at restaurants in Meschede.
  • Respect local customs and traditions: Visitors should respect local customs and traditions, including dress codes and social etiquette.

Don’t:

  • Drink in public: It is illegal to drink alcohol in public places in Meschede.
  • Smoke in public: Smoking is not allowed in public places in Meschede.

Where to Go Next from Meschede: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Meschede is located in the heart of the Sauerland region, making it an excellent base for visitors who want to explore the surrounding area. Some nearby destinations and excursions from Meschede are:

Conclusion

Meschede is a hidden gem in Germany, offering visitors a wealth of cultural, natural, and outdoor attractions. From the historical Kloster Grafschaft to the breathtakingly beautiful Sauerland region, Meschede has something to offer every type of traveler. With its safe and hospitable environment, Meschede is an excellent destination for travelers looking to experience the best of Germany.
