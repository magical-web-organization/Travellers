Introduction to Oberhausen

Located in the heart of the Ruhr area in North Rhine-Westphalia, Germany, Oberhausen is a bustling city with a rich history and exciting attractions. With a population of over 210,000, the city is a popular destination for tourists seeking a blend of culture, entertainment, and outdoor adventures. In this comprehensive tour guide, we explore everything you need to know for a successful visit to Oberhausen. From the top places to visit and things to do to the best places to stay and eat, we’ll cover it all.

List of Places to Visit in Oberhausen

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Oberhausen is home to several museums and historical sites that highlight the city’s diverse history and culture. Here are some of the must-visit spots:

  • Ludwiggalerie Schloss Oberhausen: Located in a historic castle, this museum showcases contemporary art collections, including paintings, sculptures, and photography.
  • Gasometer Oberhausen: This former gas storage tank has been transformed into a unique event space with interactive exhibitions, shows, and more.
  • Oberhausen Castle: This Renaissance-style castle dates back to the 16th century and houses the city’s municipal library.

Natural Attractions of Oberhausen: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Oberhausen offers plenty of outdoor activities for nature lovers. Check out the following spots for breathtaking views and outdoor adventures:

  • Kaisergarten Park: This 28-hectare park lies in the center of the city and is a scenic spot for a relaxing afternoon stroll or picnicking.
  • AQUApark Oberhausen: This water park offers a range of indoor and outdoor pools, slides, and other aquatic activities for families and groups.
  • Bottrop-Kirchhellen Mining Park: This industrial park once served as a coal mine but now offers visitors an opportunity to explore the site’s history and natural beauty.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Oberhausen

If you’re seeking an active adventure, Oberhausen has many hiking and biking trails to explore. Here are some of the best routes:

  • Emscher Park Route: This scenic biking route covers 153 kilometers, connecting cities in the Ruhr area and crossing Oberhausen’s unique landscapes and cultural landmarks.
  • Naturpark Hohe Mark Route: This 156-kilometer-long route passes through Oberhausen’s neighboring city of Bottrop and provides access to the mining and natural park.
  • Rhein-Herne Canal Route: This 230-kilometer-long path spans the entire Ruhr area, including the canal that flows through Oberhausen and highlights the area’s industrial development and history.

Best Things to Do in Oberhausen: Activities and Tours

Oberhausen offers a range of activities for visitors of all ages and interests. Here are some of the best things to do:

  • Visit SEA LIFE Oberhausen: Home to thousands of sea creatures, this indoor aquarium also includes interactive touch pools, feeding times, and shows.
  • Enjoy a Musical or Stage Show: Oberhausen’s Metronom Theater and Theater Oberhausen host a variety of musicals, plays, and other performances throughout the year.
  • Experience CentrO Shopping Mall: With over 250 stores, restaurants, and cafes, CentrO is considered one of Europe’s largest shopping centers and offers visitors a unique shopping experience.
  • Explore the Centro Promenade: This outdoor pedestrian mall features artists, performers, and other festivities, including a giant Christmas market from November to December.

Week-Long Itinerary for Oberhausen

To make the most of your visit to Oberhausen, here’s a week-long itinerary:

Day 1: Visit SEA LIFE Oberhausen, then explore the neighboring Kaisergarten Park.

Day 2: Take a bike tour along the Rhein-Herne Canal and stop by Gasometer Oberhausen.

Day 3: Discover the city’s art scene at Ludwiggalerie Schloss Oberhausen and grab lunch at a local restaurant.

Day 4: Hike the Naturpark Hohe Mark Route and explore the Bottrop-Kirchhellen Mining Park.

Day 5: Enjoy a musical or stage show at the Metronom Theater or Theater Oberhausen.

Day 6: Shop at CentrO Shopping Mall and enjoy the festive atmosphere of Centro Promenade.

Day 7: Visit Oberhausen Castle and relax at AQUApark Oberhausen.

Food and Drink Scene of Oberhausen

Oberhausen offers a range of dining options, from local specialties to international cuisine. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Reibekuchen: This traditional dish is a potato pancake typically served with apple sauce.
  • Schnitzel: A popular German dish made of breaded and fried meat, often accompanied by fries or potato salad.
  • Currywurst: A grilled or fried sausage typically served with curry sauce and fries.

Local Beverages

  • Alt Bier: This dark beer is brewed in the Düsseldorf area and is a popular local drink.
  • Korn: A type of schnapps made from grains and widely consumed in Germany.

Where to Stay in Oberhausen: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Oberhausen offers a range of accommodation options, from budget-friendly hostels to luxury hotels. Here are some recommended places to stay:

  • Hotel Residenz Oberhausen: This modern hotel is located in the city center and offers comfortable rooms and a host of amenities.
  • Turmhotel Oberhausen: This unique hotel is housed in a former gas storage tank and offers stunning views of the city.
  • Arthotel ANA Soul: This stylish hotel is located near the Hauptbahnhof train station and is ideal for business or leisure travelers.

What to Buy in Oberhausen: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

When it comes to souvenirs, Oberhausen offers plenty of choices. Here are some popular items to bring home:

  • Bottrop-Kirchhellen Mining Park Merchandise: The mining park offers various souvenirs that reflect the mining industry and its history, such as hats, shirts, and mining tools.
  • CentrO Shopping Mall Gifts: Many stores in the mall offer unique souvenirs, including clothes, jewelry, and handicrafts.
  • Oberhausen Beer: Several breweries in the area produce unique beer varieties that make great gifts for beer lovers.

Local Culture and Customs of Oberhausen

Germany has a rich cultural heritage, and Oberhausen is no exception. Here are some local customs and traditions to keep in mind:

  • Greeting: The most common greeting in Germany is “Guten Tag” or “Hello.”
  • Tipping: In Germany, tipping is common but not expected. However, rounding up to the next euro or leaving a 5-10% tip is considered polite.
  • Sunday Rest: In Germany, most stores and businesses are closed on Sundays, so plan accordingly.

Best Time to Visit Oberhausen

The best time to visit Oberhausen is from May to September when the weather is mild and ideal for outdoor activities. However, the city is worth visiting year-round, and each season offers its unique charm.

Do’s and Don’ts of Oberhausen

Do:

  • Explore: Oberhausen is a vibrant city with many hidden gems, so don’t be afraid to venture off the beaten path.
  • Learn Basic German: English is widely spoken in Oberhausen, but learning some German phrases can help you navigate the city and connect with locals.
  • Try Local Dishes: German cuisine is delicious and varied, so be sure to try some local specialties like schnitzel and currywurst.

Don’t:

  • J-Walk: In Germany, jaywalking is illegal and can result in a fine.
  • Be Loud or Disruptive: Germans value their quiet and private space, so avoid being loud or disruptive, especially at night.
  • Forget Your ID: In Germany, you typically need to carry an ID with you at all times, so make sure to bring your passport or another official document.

Safety and Health

Safety in Oberhausen

Oberhausen is a relatively safe city to visit, but as with any urban environment, visitors should exercise caution, especially when traveling alone or at night. Here are some safety tips:

  • **Stay in well-lit areas and avoid isolated streets and alleys.
  • **Be aware of your surroundings and avoid carrying valuables in public.
  • **Use official taxis or ride-sharing services when traveling at night.

Health in Oberhausen

Germany has an excellent healthcare system, and visitors can receive medical attention at local hospitals and clinics. It’s also recommended that visitors purchase travel insurance before their trip.

Getting to Oberhausen: Transportation Options and Logistics

Oberhausen is well-connected to both domestic and international destinations. Here are some transportation options:

  • Airports Near Oberhausen: The closest airports are Düsseldorf International Airport and Cologne Bonn Airport. Both offer a range of domestic and international flights.
  • Train Stations Near Oberhausen: Oberhausen Hauptbahnhof is the city’s main train station and offers frequent connections to major cities in Germany and Europe.
  • Other Options: Visitors can also access Oberhausen by bus or car.

How to Get Around in Oberhausen

Oberhausen has a reliable public transportation system that includes buses and trains. Visitors can purchase tickets at local ticket machines or online. Biking and walking are also excellent options for exploring the city.

Where to Go Next from Oberhausen: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Oberhausen is conveniently located near many other exciting German destinations. Here are some nearby cities and sites worth a visit:

  • Essen: Just 12 kilometers away from Oberhausen, this city is famous for its vibrant food and art scenes.
  • Düsseldorf: Known for its luxury shopping, historic landmarks, and lively nightlife, Düsseldorf is just 30 kilometers away.
  • Cologne: One of Germany’s oldest cities, Cologne is famous for its stunning cathedral and festive Christmas markets.

Conclusion

Oberhausen is a unique destination that offers visitors a blend of history, culture, and outdoor adventures. We hope this comprehensive tour guide has helped you plan your visit to the city and made your trip unforgettable.
