Introduction to Hamm

Hamm is a charming city suspended in time, located in the heart of the Ruhr district in North Rhine-Westphalia, Germany. The city boasts a rich history and culture, making it an ideal destination for history fans and tourists seeking an authentic German experience. As such, it’s an excellent place to visit for those looking for a tranquil and rewarding city break.

List of Places to Visit in Hamm

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

There are several top-tier cultural attractions to visit in Hamm, Germany.

Gustav-Lubcke-Museum

One of the most popular cultural attractions in Hamm, Gustav-Lubcke-Museum, has exhibited art and historical items from distinctive time periods since 1899. The museum comprises artistic collections from the Middle Ages to the present day, including breathtaking masterpieces from world-renowned artists.

Maximilianpark

Head to Maximilianpark and see the “Hamm Stammring,” also known as Europe’s most outstanding pipe organ. The park is a perfect place to spend your day walking and meeting Hamm’s inhabitants.

Natural Attractions of Hamm: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Hamm boasts a variety of stunning natural attractions, making it an ideal destination for nature lovers and outdoor enthusiasts.

Kurpark Hamm

For those looking for outdoor adventures, Kurpark Hamm is the perfect spot. This beautiful oasis features scenic walking and cycling paths, relaxing ponds, and a luscious lawn for picnicking and sunbathing.

Tierpark Hamm

The Tierpark Hamm is another natural attraction that’s worth exploring. Located in the wonderful Kurpark, this zoo is home to a variety of animal species, including tigers, leopards, and monkeys.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Hamm

Hamm is a city that is easily indulged on foot, bike, or car. The town is blessed with various cycling and hiking routes.

Lippe Route

Start your day in Hamm by jumping on the Lippe Route, which follows Germany’s longest river. Enjoy the breathtaking sceneries along with its riverside for a perfect outing for you and your loved ones.

Best Things to Do in Hamm: Activities and Tours

Hamm has plenty to offer, from cultural experiences to outdoor activities. Here are some of the city’s top things to do:

Experience Sudfeldmark Nature Reserve

Explore and experience nature at the Sudfeldmark Nature Reserve. It is nestled in the northeast of Hamm and houses a variety of flora, fauna, and creatures. Walkthrough the trails for an exciting day out.

Visiting the Wasserturm Hamm

A visit to the Wasserturm is a must-do activity for anyone planning to visit Hamm. The tower presents a beautiful view of the town and the surrounding nature.

Week-Long Itinerary for Hamm

Day 1:

Arrive in Hamm and check-in at the friendly Hotel Herzog Garni in the town center. Later, visit the Gustav-Lubcke-Museum. In the evening, enjoy dinner at the popular Gasthaus Schulte.

Day 2:

Take a bike tour of the historic buildings in the town center. After enjoying lunch at the Alter Bahnhof Hamm, visit the Maximilianpark with its scenic spots.

Day 3:

Take the Lippe Route north through beautiful countryside landscapes. See the Lippeauen nature reserve and a catamaran harbor.

Day 4:

Enjoy a day trip to the city of Dortmund, highlighting its beer culture and breweries. Take a stroll through the famous Reinoldikirche to learn about the city’s rich history.

Day 5:

Visit the Sudfeldmark nature reserve and witness the fresh, natural beauty located in Hamm’s northeast area.

Day 6:

For your final day in Hamm, visit the Wasserturm, a large water tower built in the late 19th century. Later in the night, have a savory meal at Restaurant Clemens.

Food and Drink Scene of Hamm

Must-Try Dishes

Hamm’s food scene primarily features traditional German cuisine. Here are some of the top dishes you should try:

Pork Knuckle

The pork knuckle is a staple dish in Germany, and you can find it in virtually every restaurant in Hamm. Enjoy a tender, juicy pork knuckle served with a hearty side of potatoes or sauerkraut.

Schwartenhals with Sauerkraut

Another dish worth trying is Schwartenhals, which is basically pork belly. This dish pairs well with sauerkraut, a traditional German side dish made from fermented cabbage.

Local Beverages

When it comes to beverages, beer tends to dominate the German drink culture. Hamm has a beautiful selection of local breweries that you should try during your stay.

Brauhaus 1516

It is excellent for beer aficionados and people seeking an authentic German beer experience

Where to Stay in Hamm: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Hotel

When visiting Hamm, Hotel Herzog Garni is an excellent choice. The hotel features luxury services, such as free Wi-Fi, complimentary breakfast, and a 24-hour front desk.

Guesthouse

Another option is the Gasthof Ostermann guesthouse. The place offers comfortable beds, excellent food, and scenic surroundings.

What to Buy in Hamm: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

When looking for artisanal gifts and souvenirs, Hamm’s food markets should be your destination.

Farmers Market

The farmers’ market is a perfect spot for procuring farm-fresh food items such as fruit, cheese, and wine. It is also an excellent destination for souvenir shopping as there is an assortment of local producers with handmade crafts and trinkets.

Local Culture and Customs of Hamm

Hamm has a rich culture and history, which is depicted in its architectural landmarks and traditional festivals.

Krabbenkirmes

One of the famous events in Hamm is the Krabbenkirmes festival. Dress up in your finest Lederhosen and dirndls and enjoy Oktoberfest-style festivities with rides, music, and beer.

Best Time to Visit Hamm

The best time to visit Hamm depends on the individual’s preferences. The festival season like Krabbenkirmes is usually from August to September, making it an ideal time to visit for those seeking an authentic German festival experience.

  • Be polite to the locals

  • Don’t miss local festivals and fairs

Where to Go Next from Hamm: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Cologne
