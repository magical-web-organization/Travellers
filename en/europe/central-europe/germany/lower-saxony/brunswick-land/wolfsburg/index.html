Introduction to Wolfsburg

With a population of around 120,000, Wolfsburg is a city located in the state of Lower Saxony in Germany, known mostly for being the headquarters of the Volkswagen Group. However, Wolfsburg also offers a range of fascinating attractions from cultural and historical points to natural and adventurous spots.

In this comprehensive guide, we will take a closer look at everything you need to know for a memorable trip to Wolfsburg.

List of Places to Visit in Wolfsburg

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Wolfsburg has a rich cultural heritage that is beautifully preserved in its museums and historical landmarks. Here are some must-visit spots for history buffs and culture enthusiasts:

Wolfsburg Castle

This grand castle is located in the heart of Wolfsburg and boasts some of the best architecture in Germany. Originally built in the 13th century, it was destroyed during World War II and later rebuilt to its former glory. Today it is a popular tourist attraction and a cultural and historical landmark of the city.

Autostadt

Autostadt or the Automobile City is a park dedicated to the Volkswagen group and celebrates the history of automobiles. With interactive exhibits, attractions, and a huge collection of classic cars, this theme park is a must-visit destination for everyone interested in cars.

The Phaeno Science Centre

The Phaeno Science Centre is a modern science museum that features interactive exhibits and hands-on experiences. With various sections dedicated to physics, chemistry, and biology, this is a fascinating place for science enthusiasts of all ages.

Natural Attractions of Wolfsburg: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Wolfsburg is famous for its natural beauty with various outdoor spots that offer scenic views and activities. Here are some must-visit natural attractions of Wolfsburg:

Allerpark

Allerpark is a beautiful park located in the heart of Wolfsburg. Covering an area of around 130 hectares, this park offers various outdoor activities such as cycling, running, and soccer. With a lake, playgrounds, and a beach, Allerpark is perfect for families and friends looking for outdoor fun.

Drömling Nature Reserve

The Drömling Nature Reserve is a haven for nature lovers with over 6,000 hectares of natural beauty. From marshes, forests, and meadows to biking and hiking trails, this nature reserve offers something for everyone.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Wolfsburg

Wolfsburg is a city that is perfectly suited to be explored by bike or on foot. Here are some popular hiking/biking routes of Wolfsburg:

Wolfsburg-Sandkämper Winkel-Wolfsburg

This biking route takes you through the beautiful countryside of Wolfsburg. Covering a distance of around 29 km, the route starts and ends in Wolfsburg and offers stunning views of the city and its surroundings.

Kreyenbrücke Nature Reserve

Kreyenbrücke Nature Reserve is a popular hiking destination that offers various trails that are perfectly suited for beginners and experts alike. With a beautiful lake, dense forests, and various wildlife, this reserve is a perfect escape from the hustle and bustle of the city.

Best Things to Do in Wolfsburg: Activities and Tours

Here are some other exciting activities and tours to explore in Wolfsburg:

Factory Tour at Volkswagen

For car enthusiasts, a factory tour at Volkswagen headquarters is an absolute must. Touring the production lines offers an incredible insight into the car-making process and the history of the brand.

Wolfsburg Water World

The Wolfsburg Water World is a fantastic place to spend a day with the family. With various water attractions, swimming pools, and play areas, it’s a great way to cool off and have some fun.

Week-Long Itinerary for Wolfsburg

If you have a week to spend in Wolfsburg, there are loads to explore. Here’s a sample itinerary of what you could do in a week in Wolfsburg:

Day Activity
1 Visit the Wolfsburg Castle and explore the city center
2 Take a bike tour through the countryside and visit the Drömling Nature Reserve
3 Visit the Autostadt and take a factory tour at Volkswagen
4 Visit the Phaeno Science Centre
5 Visit Allerpark and go cycling or have a picnic
6 Take a day trip to the nearby city of Braunschweig and explore its medieval center
7 Visit Kreyenbrucke Nature Reserve and go hiking

Food and Drink Scene of Wolfsburg

Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages to try in Wolfsburg:

Must-Try Dishes

Blankenauer Meatballs

Blankenauer meatballs are a local speciality made with meat, onions, mustard, and marjoram. Served with potato salad or mustard sauce, this dish is a real treat for meat lovers.

Braunkohl

Braunkohl is a traditional North German dish made of kale, bacon, and sausage. It is a hearty meal that is usually eaten in the wintertime and served with potatoes.

Local Beverages

Wolfsburger Hofbräu

Wolfsburger Hofbräu is a popular local beer brewed in Wolfsburg. This refreshing beer is available in various flavors and is a must-try for beer enthusiasts.

Gewürztraminer

Gewürztraminer is a popular white wine variety that is produced in the surrounding regions of Wolfsburg. With floral and fruity notes, this wine is a perfect accompaniment for local dishes.

Where to Stay in Wolfsburg: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Wolfsburg has a range of options for accommodation. Here are some suggestions for places to stay:

Marriott Hotel Wolfsburg

Marriott Hotel Wolfsburg is located in the city center and offers comfortable, modern rooms with excellent amenities like a swimming pool and gym.

Volkswagen Hostel

The Volkswagen Hostel is located inside the Autostadt and is perfect for visitors who want to be close to the theme park and stay in a unique setting.

What to Buy in Wolfsburg: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Here are some souvenirs and local products to bring home from Wolfsburg:

Volkswagen Merchandise

As the headquarters of Volkswagen, Wolfsburg is the perfect place to buy a Volkswagen-branded merchandise like t-shirts, hats, and keychains.

Blankenauer Meatballs

Blankenauer meatballs are a unique souvenir to bring home from Wolfsburg. You can buy them in any local supermarket and take them home to cook for your friends and family.

Local Culture and Customs of Wolfsburg

The people of Wolfsburg are friendly and welcoming to visitors. However, it’s important to keep in mind a few cultural customs:

  • Be punctual for appointments and events
  • Address people formally in business and government settings
  • Always greet people with a handshake

Best Time to Visit Wolfsburg

The best time to visit Wolfsburg is between May and September when the weather is mild and pleasant. During this time, the city comes alive with various outdoor activities and events.

Do’s and Don’ts of Wolfsburg

Do:

  • Try local dishes like Blankenauer Meatballs and Braunkohl
  • Visit museums like the Phaeno Science Centre or Autostadt
  • Rent a bike and explore the countryside of Wolfsburg

Don’t:

  • Be disrespectful to local customs and traditions
  • Litter or smoke in public places
  • Take photos with flash in museums and cultural exhibits

Safety and Health

Safety in Wolfsburg

Wolfsburg is a safe city with low crime rates. However, it’s important to be aware of pickpocketing in crowded areas and to stay cautious during the nighttime.

Health in Wolfsburg

Wolfsburg is a modern city with various healthcare facilities and hospitals in case of an emergency. It’s important to have travel insurance when visiting to cover any medical costs.

Getting to Wolfsburg: Transportation Options and Logistics

Wolfsburg is well-connected to major cities in Germany and Europe through several transportation options. Here are some suggested routes:

Airports Near Wolfsburg

  • Hannover Airport (Haj) – 70 km from Wolfsburg
  • Berlin Tegel Airport (TXL) – 160 km from Wolfsburg

Bus Stations Near Wolfsburg

  • Wolfsburg Central Bus Station
  • Braunschweig Central Bus Station

Other Options

  • Train: Wolfsburg is connected to major German cities like Berlin and Hannover by train.
  • Car: The city is easily accessible by car, with excellent road networks connecting it to major German cities.

How to Get Around in Wolfsburg

Wolfsburg is a bike-friendly city with various cycling trails and lanes throughout the city. Visitors can also use the public transport system, which includes buses and trams, or hire a car to explore the city at their own pace.

Where to Go Next from Wolfsburg: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Wolfsburg is the perfect starting point for exploring other destinations in the region. Here are some nearby destinations and excursions to consider:

  • Braunschweig – This medieval city is located just 20 km away from Wolfsburg and boasts beautiful architecture and historical landmarks.

  • Harz Mountains – An hour’s drive from Wolfsburg, the Harz mountains offer stunning views and excellent hiking opportunities.

  • Berlin – The German capital is located just two hours away from Wolfsburg and offers a fascinating mix of history, culture, and modernity.

Conclusion

Wolfsburg is a city that offers a range of exciting attractions from culture and history to nature and adventure. With various outdoor spots, hiking and biking trails, and fascinating museums, it’s the perfect destination for families, couples, and solo travelers. We hope this comprehensive guide has helped you plan your trip to Wolfsburg and made your visit a memorable one.
