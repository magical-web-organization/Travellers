Introduction to Goslar

Nestled in the southern part of Lower Saxony, Goslar is a charming town with a rich history that dates back to the 10th century. The town’s architecture features a mix of half-timbered houses, Gothic buildings, and modern structures, giving it a unique character. Goslar has been recognized as a UNESCO World Heritage Site thanks to its historical significance and cultural heritage.

In this in-depth guide to Goslar, we’ll explore the town’s top attractions, outdoor adventures, cultural and culinary experiences, and practical travel tips. Let’s begin!

List of Places to Visit in Goslar

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Goslar is home to a plethora of historical and cultural attractions that are worth exploring. Some of the must-visit museums and historical places include:

  • Goslar Imperial Palace: A great example of Romanesque architecture that dates back to the 11th century.

  • Rammelsberg Mines: Explore historical iron mines and learn about the history of mining in Goslar.

  • Kaiserpfalz: A former residence of the German emperor that now houses a museum dedicated to the history of the palace and the town.

  • Goslar Museum: A comprehensive museum showcasing the town’s history from the Middle Ages to the present.

Natural Attractions of Goslar: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Goslar is surrounded by nature, making it the perfect destination for outdoor activities. Some of the top natural attractions in the town include:

  • Harz National Park: Located just a few miles away from Goslar, this national park offers scenic hiking trails, waterfalls, and wildlife.

  • Mountain Bike Arena Harz: A mountain biking paradise with over 200 km of trails for bikers of all levels.

  • Steinberg Alm: A scenic mountain pasture perfect for hiking and enjoying the panoramic views of the Harz Mountains.

  • Huttaler Widerwaage: A scenic lake surrounded by walking trails and beautiful landscapes.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Goslar

Goslar has a lot to offer when it comes to hiking and biking. Some of the most popular routes include:

  • Liebesbankweg: A romantic hiking trail with an emphasis on love and romance. With over 20 stops along the way, visitors can enjoy the scenic views of the Harz Mountains.

  • Harz Mountains Hiking Trail: A long-distance trail that crosses the Harz Mountains and offers stunning views of the landscape.

  • Okertalsperre: A scenic cycling route that follows the beautiful lake and its surroundings.

Best Things to Do in Goslar: Activities and Tours

Whether you’re interested in cultural experiences or outdoor adventures, Goslar has something to offer to every traveler. Here are some of the best things to do in Goslar:

  • Take a guided tour of Goslar Imperial Palace to learn about the town’s history and architecture.

  • Visit the Rammelsberg Mines and discover the history of mining in Goslar.

  • Go hiking or biking in one of the many scenic routes around the town.

  • Explore the town’s streets and architecture by taking a self-guided walking tour.

Week-Long Itinerary for Goslar

If you’re planning to spend a week in Goslar, here’s a suggested itinerary that covers the top attractions and must-visit places in the town:

Day 1: Explore the town’s streets and architecture by taking a self-guided walking tour.

Day 2: Visit Goslar Imperial Palace and the Kaiserpfalz museum.

Day 3: Discover the history of mining in Goslar by going on a tour of Rammelsberg Mines.

Day 4: Head out to Harz National Park and spend the day hiking or cycling.

Day 5: Explore the town’s museums and cultural attractions, including Goslar Museum.

Day 6: Take a scenic drive through the Harz Mountains and visit the Steinberg Alm.

Day 7: Visit the Huttaler Widerwaage and spend the day relaxing by the lake.

Food and Drink Scene in Goslar

Goslar has a vibrant food and drink scene that showcases local flavors and ingredients. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Harzer Käse: A type of cheese made from low-fat milk. It has a distinctive odor and taste that’s beloved by locals.

  • Goslarer Pflastersteine: A delicious dessert made of chocolate and marzipan.

  • Wild Boar Sausages: A local specialty sausage made from wild boar meat.

Local Beverages

  • Goslarer Kaiserpfalz Beer: A traditional beer that’s brewed in Goslar and named after the Kaiserpfalz palace.

  • Harzer Kräuterlikör: A herbal liqueur made from local herbs and spices.

Where to Stay in Goslar: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Goslar offers a wide range of accommodation options for every budget. Some of the best hotels, hostels, and guesthouses in the town include:

  • Hotel Der Achtermann: A luxurious hotel located in the heart of Goslar.

  • Kutscherplatz Hotel Goslar: A budget-friendly hotel that’s great for travelers on a tight budget.

  • Haus Brigitte: A charming guesthouse with a rustic ambiance and cozy rooms.

What to Buy in Goslar: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Goslar is known for its local crafts, souvenirs, and products that make great gifts and mementos. Some of the best items to buy in Goslar include:

  • Local honey: Goslar is surrounded by scenic landscapes that are ideal for beekeeping, and local honey is a delicious souvenir to bring back home.

  • Wooden crafts: Goslar has a thriving craft scene, and wooden crafts like toys and figurines are popular products to buy in the town.

Local Culture and Customs of Goslar

Goslar has a rich cultural heritage that’s reflected in its architecture, traditions, and customs. The town has a unique character that’s shaped by its rich history and natural surroundings.

Best Time to Visit Goslar

The best time to visit Goslar is during the summer months (June-August) when the weather is temperate, and outdoor activities are in full swing. However, the town is beautiful throughout the year, and each season brings its own charm.

Do’s and Don’ts of Goslar

Do:

  • Try local specialties like Harzer Käse, Goslarer Pflastersteine, and Wild Boar sausages.

  • Take a self-guided walking tour of Goslar to get a feel of the town’s architecture and streets.

  • Go hiking or biking in one of the many scenic routes around Goslar.

Don’t:

  • Don’t litter or damage the environment while exploring the natural attractions in and around Goslar.

  • Don’t forget to carry warm clothes if you’re visiting during the winter months.

Safety and Health

Safety in Goslar

Goslar is a safe town with a low crime rate. However, travelers must remain vigilant and take necessary precautions like avoiding unfamiliar neighborhoods and keeping valuables safe.

Health in Goslar

Goslar has a well-established healthcare system, and visitors can easily find medical help in case of an emergency.

Getting to Goslar: Transportation Options and Logistics

Goslar is well-connected to major cities in Germany and Europe and can be easily accessed through various modes of transport. The closest airports to Goslar are:

Airports Near Goslar

  • Hannover Airport (85 km)
  • Berlin Tegel Airport (267 km)
  • Frankfurt Airport (323 km)
  • Hamburg Airport (235 km)

Bus Stations Near Goslar

  • Central Bus Station (ZOB) in Goslar

Other Options

Visitors can also reach Goslar by train or by car. The town is located on the B6 and B82 highways, making it easily accessible by road.

How to Get Around in Goslar

Goslar is a small town that can be easily explored on foot or by bicycle. Buses and taxis are also available for visitors who prefer public transport.

Where to Go Next from Goslar: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Goslar is surrounded by many exciting destinations worth exploring. Some popular nearby destinations and excursions from Goslar include:

  • Quedlinburg: A UNESCO World Heritage site known for its medieval architecture and Castle Hill.

  • Wernigerode: A charming town with timber-framed buildings and the famous Wernigerode Castle.

  • Bad Harzburg: A spa town located in the Harz Mountains with natural thermal springs.

Conclusion

Goslar is a hidden gem that offers a unique blend of history, culture, and natural beauty. With its charming streets, picturesque landscapes, and exciting activities, it’s the perfect destination for travelers looking for an off-the-beaten-path adventure.
