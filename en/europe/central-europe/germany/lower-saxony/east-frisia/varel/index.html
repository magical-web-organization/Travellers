Introduction to Varel

Varel is a town located in East Frisia, Lower Saxony, Germany. This charming town is situated in the heart of Central Europe and is a popular tourist destination for those looking to explore the beauty of the German countryside. Surrounded by idyllic landscapes, Varel offers a variety of cultural and natural attractions that will captivate any traveler’s heart.

List of Places to Visit in Varel

Here’s a list of the top places to visit in Varel:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Schlossmuseum
  • Windmill Museum
  • Bürgerhaus Museum
  • Vareler Heimatmuseum

Natural Attractions of Varel: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Hundsmühler Berg, a scenic view point
  • Jade Bay, a beautiful coastal area
  • Zeteler Marsh, an untouched natural reserve
  • Lake Wapelersiel, a peaceful retreat for water sports activities

Hiking/Biking Routes of Varel

Varel offers some of the best hiking and biking routes in Germany. From stunning coastlines to rugged terrain, there’s a trail for every level of adventurer. Here are some of our top picks:

  • The Jade Cycle Path: This 350 km long route leads along the Jade Bay coast and offers breathtaking views of the sea and the countryside.
  • UNESCO Geopark Tour: An educational geological trail that leads through the Zeteler Marsch natural reserve.
  • Around the Lake Wapelersiel: A flat and easy trail that offers beautiful views of the lake and is perfect for a relaxing family bike ride.

Best Things to Do in Varel: Activities and Tours

Here are some of the best things to do in Varel:

  • Take a sightseeing boat tour around the Jade Bay and explore the scenic coastline.
  • Rent a kayak and venture out on the crystal-clear waters of Lake Wapelersiel.
  • Visit the weekly farmer’s market and try some of the local and regional delicacies.
  • Go on a guided tour of the Schlossmuseum and learn about the rich history of the town.

Week-Long Itinerary for Varel

Here’s our recommended itinerary for a week in Varel:

Day Activities
Day 1 Arrive in Varel and explore the town center.
Day 2 Visit the Schlossmuseum and Windmill Museum.
Day 3 Hike or bike through the Zeteler Marsh.

Food and Drink Scene of Varel

Varel is known for its traditional German cuisine and fresh seafood. Here are some dishes and beverages that you must try:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Kartoffelpuffer: Fried potato pancakes served with apple sauce or sour cream.
  • Grünkohl: A traditional German recipe made with kale, bacon, and sausage.
  • Smoked fish platter: A popular and delicious seafood dish that includes smoked salmon, trout, and eel.

Local Beverages

  • Jever Pilsener: A crisp and refreshing beer that is brewed in the town of Jever, just a short drive from Varel.
  • Krabben Schnaps: A schnaps flavored with fresh shrimp.
  • Ostfriesentee: A black tea served with cream and Kluntje, a type of roasted sugar cube.

Where to Stay in Varel: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Varel offers a variety of accommodation options, including hotels, hostels, and guesthouses. Here are some of the top picks:

  • Hotel Friesische Wehde: A cozy and modern hotel located in the heart of Varel.
  • Yado Hotel Varel: A budget-friendly hotel that offers comfortable rooms and a central location.
  • Pension Ankommen: A charming guesthouse located in the countryside, away from the hustle and bustle of the town center.

What to Buy in Varel: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Here are some souvenirs and local products that you can bring back from Varel:

  • Jade jewelry: A popular and beautiful souvenir made from the green stones found in the region.
  • Local honey: A sweet and tasty souvenir that is often sold at the local farmer’s market.
  • Ceramic souvenirs: Varel is famous for its ceramic industry, and you can find a variety of handmade ceramic souvenirs in the town.

Local Culture and Customs of Varel

Varel has a rich and unique culture that is steeped in centuries-old traditions. Here are some customs and traditions that you may encounter during your stay:

  • Frisian languages: East Frisian is the main language spoken in Varel, and you may encounter some locals who speak Plattdeutsch, a low German dialect.
  • East Frisian tea culture: Drinking tea is a big part of the local customs, and you can try out the local tea ceremony at many cafes around town.
  • Carnival celebrations: Varel is famous for its Carnival celebrations, which take place every year between November and February.

Best Time to Visit Varel

The best time to visit Varel is during the summer months, between May and September, when the weather is warm and sunny. However, if you’re interested in experiencing the local Carnival celebrations, plan your trip between November and February.

Do’s and Don’ts of Varel

Here are some do’s and don’ts to keep in mind during your stay in Varel:

Do:

  • Try out the local tea culture and indulge in some traditional East Frisian tea.
  • Explore the town center and visit the many historical and cultural attractions.
  • Try out the local cuisine and seafood.

Don’t:

  • Don’t forget to bring a raincoat, as Varel is known for its unpredictable weather.
  • Don’t litter or leave trash behind, as the town takes pride in its clean and pristine natural environment.
  • Don’t forget to greet locals with a friendly “Moin” (hello) or “Tschüss” (goodbye).

Safety and Health

Varel is generally a safe and healthy town. However, like in any new destination, it is recommended to take some precautions.

Safety in Varel

  • Be aware of your surroundings and don’t carry large amounts of cash or valuables while exploring the town.
  • Take extra care while hiking or biking, especially in rugged and unfamiliar terrain.
  • Always follow the traffic rules while driving or renting a car.

Health in Varel

  • Make sure you have comprehensive travel insurance before embarking on your trip.
  • Drink plenty of water and stay hydrated, especially during the warmer months.
  • Consult with your doctor about any vaccinations or health precautions you may need before traveling to Germany.

Getting to Varel: Transportation Options and Logistics

Varel is easily accessible via various modes of transportation.

Airports Near Varel

The closest airport to Varel is Bremen Airport, located just over an hour’s drive away. Other international airports within a two-hour drive are Hamburg Airport and Hannover Airport.

Bus Stations Near Varel

Varel can be reached by bus from surrounding towns and neighboring cities. The bus station in Varel offers connections to other parts of Lower Saxony.

Other Options

If you’re looking to travel to Varel by train, the closest train station is in Oldenburg, which is approximately a 20-minute drive away from Varel.

How to Get Around in Varel

Varel is a small town that can easily be explored by foot or bike. Another option is to rent a car, which gives you the ability to explore the surrounding areas at your own pace. There are also public buses that offer transportation to nearby towns and cities.

Where to Go Next from Varel: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Varel is located in the heart of Lower Saxony and offers easy access to many interesting and beautiful destinations:

  • Oldenburg: A charming university town with a rich history and cultural heritage.
  • Bremerhaven: A bustling port city that offers a variety of museums and cultural events.
  • Wilhelmshaven: A coastal town that is famous for its maritime history and picturesque beaches.

Conclusion

Varel is a hidden gem in Germany that offers a unique blend of natural beauty, rich history, and cultural heritage. With so much to see and do, this charming town is a must-visit destination for anyone looking to explore the beauty of Lower Saxony.
