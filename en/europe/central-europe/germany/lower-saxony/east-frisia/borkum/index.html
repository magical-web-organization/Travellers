A Comprehensive Travel Guide to Borkum, Germany

Get to know Borkum, Germany – a beautiful island destination in Lower Saxony. This travel guide provides you with everything you need to know about Borkum, from places to visit to cultural attractions, hiking routes, food and drink scene, and how to get around.

Introduction to Borkum

Borkum is a small island situated in the East Frisian region of Lower Saxony, Germany. Known for its fresh sea breeze, long sandy beaches, and stunning natural landscapes, the island attracts tourists from all over the world. Borkum is also one of the seven inhabited East Frisian Islands, making it an excellent place to explore both German culture and nature.

List of Places to Visit in Borkum

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Borkum is rich in history and culture, and there are several museums and historical places that visitors can explore. Some of the most popular cultural attractions in Borkum include:

  • Borkum Lighthouse – The iconic lighthouse is one of the main landmarks of Borkum and offers an incredible view of the island and its surroundings.

  • Borkum Museum – The museum showcases the history of the island, its people, and their way of life.

  • Heimatmuseum Dykhus – This outdoor museum represents the traditional East Frisian architecture and showcases the rural way of life on the island.

Natural Attractions of Borkum: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Borkum is blessed with an array of natural attractions, making it a paradise for outdoor enthusiasts. Visitors can explore the stunning beaches, dunes, and wildlife that can be found on the island. Some of the most popular outdoor activities include:

  • Biking – Borkum is home to many bike routes that provide visitors with a chance to explore its surroundings. You can rent bikes and explore the beaches and forests of the island.

  • Hiking – Visitors can take a hike through the beautiful dune landscape of Borkum, which also provides a fantastic view of its flora and fauna.

  • Natural Thermal Baths – Take a dip in the warm seawater of Borkum, which is believed to have healing properties. The saltwater bath is perfect for relaxation and rejuvenation.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Borkum

Borkum is renowned for its picturesque hiking and biking routes that offer a breathtaking view of the natural landscape of the island. Here are some of the most popular routes to explore:

  • Duenenplantage – This route takes visitors through the beautiful dune landscape of the island, offering a chance to see the local flora and fauna.

  • Ostlandroute – The route is ideal for cyclists, taking them through the east side of Borkum, where they can enjoy the stunning views of the North Sea.

  • Westlandroute – The route takes visitors through the west side of the island, where they can take in the sights of the picturesque landscape and wildlife.

Best Things to Do in Borkum: Activities and Tours

There is no shortage of things to do and tours to take when on the island. Here are some of the popular activities and tours visitors can enjoy in Borkum:

  • Beach Hopping – Explore the different beaches that Borkum has to offer, such as Nordstrand, Sudstrand, and the nudist beach.

  • Wadden Sea Tour – Take a guided tour of the Wadden Sea, which is a UNESCO World Heritage site. Visitors can explore the tidal mudflats and see the diverse wildlife.

  • Borkum Adventure Golf – A fun activity for the family. Explore the island’s mini-golf course, which is a replica of the island with its landmarks.

Week-Long Itinerary for Borkum

To make the most of your trip to Borkum, here’s a week-long itinerary to follow:

Day 1 - Arrive in Borkum
Day 2 - Visit Borkum Lighthouse and Museum
Day 3 - Take a bike ride to the natural thermal baths
Day 4 - Explore the Wadden Sea and local wildlife
Day 5 - Take a walk in Duenenplantage and explore local flora and fauna
Day 6 - Enjoy a day at the beach
Day 7 - Shop for souvenirs and try local cuisine

Food and Drink Scene of Borkum

Must-Try Dishes

Borkum is home to several local dishes that visitors can try while on the island. Some of the traditional dishes include:

  • Borkumer Sausage – A unique pork sausage that is spiced with ginger and caraway.

  • Borkumer Muscheln – A dish of steamed mussels that has been cultivated in the waters around the island.

  • Borkumer Pastete – A type of cake that is traditionally eaten on the island.

Local Beverages

While in Borkum, visitors can try some of the local beverages, including:

  • BORKUM-BRAU Beer – A local beer brewed on the island using local ingredients.

  • Borkumer Klipper – A spiced rum which is a blend of different spices and flavors.

Where to Stay in Borkum: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Borkum offers visitors a variety of options for accommodation, from comfortable hotels to cozy guesthouses. Here are some of the top choices for visitors:

  • Hotel Strandvilla Janine – A boutique hotel located near the beach, offering stylish rooms and excellent service.

  • Upstalsboom Seehotel – A luxury hotel situated near the ‘Ocean Wave’ water park, offers spacious rooms and an extensive spa.

  • Sleep at Sail – A hostel offering comfortable bedding and a cozy atmosphere ideal for solo travelers and backpackers.

What to Buy in Borkum: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Visitors to Borkum can take home some souvenirs and local products as mementos of their trip. Some of the most popular souvenirs to buy in Borkum include:

  • Borkum Socks – A unique souvenir which has been knitted on the island for over a century.

  • Carved wooden turtles – A typical souvenir of the island which represents the diverse wildlife of the Wadden Sea.

  • Kwelderpop – Local popcorn flavoured with seaweed.

Local Culture and Customs of Borkum

Borkum has a rich traditional culture that visitors can experience when they visit. Visitors should be aware of the following customs:

  • Greeting – It is customary to greet people with ‘Moin!’ which is a local greeting meaning ‘Good Morning’.

  • Dress Code – There is no dress code in Borkum. Visitors should dress comfortably for outdoor adventures and comfortable attire for restaurants and nightlife.

Best Time to Visit Borkum

The best time to visit Borkum is during the summer months from June to August, when weather conditions are optimal for outdoor activities. The island is also busy during this period, and visitors are advised to book accommodation and tours in advance.

Do’s and Don’ts of Borkum

It is essential to be aware of the following Do’s and Don’ts when visiting Borkum:

Do:

  • Greet people with the traditional Borkum greeting ‘Moin.’

  • Follow the rules of the beaches and nudist areas.

  • Try the local cuisine and beverages.

Don’t:

  • Make loud noises that may disturb the peace and tranquility of the island.

  • Litter on the island.

  • Cycle or walk on the protected dunes and areas.

Safety and Health

Safety in Borkum

Borkum is a relatively safe place to visit, with low levels of crime. Visitors should be aware of the following safety tips:

  • Follow the instructions of local authorities in case of emergencies.

  • Take care when swimming or taking part in water activities due to strong currents.

Health in Borkum

Borkum’s healthcare system is of high quality, and there are several hospitals and medical centres on the island. Visitors should take the necessary precautions, such as using sun protection and insect repellent, to avoid sunburn and insect bites.

Getting to Borkum: Transportation Options and Logistics

Borkum is easily accessible from the mainland, and visitors can choose from various transportation options to reach the island.

Airports Near Borkum

  • Borkum Airport – located on the island, connecting the island to the nearest big city Emden occasionally through Düsseldorf.

Bus Stations Near Borkum

  • Borkum Bus Station – local TÖP service provider has stations on every key spot of the island at Borkum.

  • Ferry Service – the main transport between Borkum and Emden is via ferry service which has a regularity of several every day.

Other Options

Visitors can also access Borkum by car or train through Emden, where they can take a ferry to the island.

How to Get Around in Borkum

The best way to get around and explore Borkum is by walking or cycling. Visitors can rent bikes on the island for a day or week and explore its surroundings efficiently.

Where to Go Next from Borkum: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

While on the island, visitors can take day trips to nearby destinations such as Emden, Norderney Island, and Groningen, a major cultural city in the Netherlands.

Conclusion

Borkum is a must-visit destination in Lower Saxony, Germany, with stunning natural landscapes, a rich cultural history, and fantastic outdoor adventures. Whether you’re looking to relax or explore the island’s many attractions, this comprehensive travel guide will help you plan your visit and experience Borkum to the fullest.
