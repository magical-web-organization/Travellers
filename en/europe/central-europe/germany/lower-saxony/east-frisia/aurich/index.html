Introduction to Aurich

Nestled in the heart of East Frisia, in Lower Saxony, Germany, Aurich is a charming town full of cultural and natural treasures. With its beautiful parks and gardens, impressive museums and historical landmarks, delicious cuisine, and warm and hospitable locals, Aurich has something to offer to every traveler.

List of Places to Visit in Aurich

If you’re planning to visit Aurich soon, make sure to add these must-visit places to your itinerary:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • The State Museum in Aurich: This museum showcases the history, culture, and art of East Frisia and Lower Saxony, including paintings, sculptures, archeological finds, and rare books.

  • The Old Town Hall: Built in the 1500s, this half-timbered building is one of Aurich’s most famous landmarks and a great example of Lower Saxony’s traditional architecture.

  • The Ewiges Meer Nature Reserve: This vast wetland area is home to many rare plant and animal species and offers beautiful scenery and hiking trails.

Natural Attractions of Aurich: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • The Schlosspark: This beautiful park is located in the heart of Aurich and features gardens, ponds, and a castle, dating back to the 14th century.

  • The Kletterwald Forest Adventure Park: Located just outside Aurich, this outdoor park offers thrilling zip lines, treetop walks, and other fun activities for all ages.

  • The Wadden Sea: A UNESCO World Heritage Site, the Wadden Sea is a unique ecosystem that stretches along the coasts of Germany, Denmark, and the Netherlands.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Aurich

Exploring Aurich’s beautiful countryside on foot or by bike is a great way to experience the town’s natural beauty. Some popular routes are:

  • The East Frisian Way: A long-distance hiking trail that stretches across the region and passes through Aurich.

  • The Fehn Route: A cycling trail that takes you through East Frisia’s idyllic villages, canals, and windmills.

  • The North Sea Cycle Route: This international cycling trail runs along the Wadden Sea coast and offers stunning views of the sea and the surrounding islands.

Best Things to Do in Aurich: Activities and Tours

If you’re looking for fun and exciting things to do in Aurich, here are some great options:

  • Take a guided tour of the State Museum and learn more about East Frisian history and culture.

  • Visit the Ekel Castle and its beautiful gardens, and enjoy a picnic on the castle’s grounds.

  • Attend one of Aurich’s many festivals and events, such as the Aurich Christmas Market or the Summer Concerts in the Schlosspark.

Week-Long Itinerary for Aurich

If you’re planning to spend a week in Aurich, here’s a sample itinerary that covers some of the town’s must-see attractions:

Day Activities
Day 1 Explore the Old Town and its landmarks, such as the Old Town Hall, the St. Lambert’s Church, and the Marktplatz.
Day 2 Visit the State Museum and the nearby Botanical Garden, and enjoy a picnic lunch in the park.
Day 3 Take a day trip to the nearby city of Emden and explore its harbor, museums, and galleries.
Day 4 Discover the town’s natural beauty by hiking the East Frisian Way or cycling the Fehn Route.
Day 5 Visit the Ekel Castle and its gardens, and enjoy a guided tour of the castle interiors.
Day 6 Take a boat tour of the Wadden Sea and explore the nearby islands and sandbanks.
Day 7 Shop for souvenirs at the local markets and enjoy a traditional East Frisian meal at one of Aurich’s cozy restaurants.

Food and Drink Scene of Aurich

One of the best ways to experience Aurich’s local culture is to indulge in its culinary delights. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Labskaus: A hearty sailor’s dish made of mashed potatoes, beef, onions, and fried egg.

  • Bratwurst: A grilled sausage typically served with mustard and sauerkraut.

  • Pumpernickel: A traditional dark rye bread that is sweetened with molasses.

Local Beverages

  • Ostfriesentee: A strong black tea served with cream and rock sugar, and typically enjoyed with a small cookie or pastry.

  • Altbier: A dark beer brewed using traditional methods in Lower Saxony.

  • Apfelkorn: A sweet apple liqueur that is a popular after-dinner drink.

Where to Stay in Aurich: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Aurich offers many accommodation options for all budgets, from affordable hostels and guesthouses to luxurious hotels and apartments. Here are some popular choices:

  • Altstadt Hotel: Located in the heart of Aurich’s Old Town, this charming hotel offers comfortable rooms and a cozy restaurant.

  • Hotel am Schloss: Featuring modern rooms and a spa area, this hotel is located near the Schlosspark and the State Museum.

  • Jugendherberge Aurich: A budget-friendly hostel situated in a historic building and offering shared and private rooms.

What to Buy in Aurich: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

If you’re looking for unique gifts and souvenirs to bring back home, here are some suggestions:

  • East Frisian tea blends and tea accessories, such as tea cups and tea strainers.

  • Traditional East Frisian clothing, such as dirndl dresses and leather pants.

  • Arts and Crafts made by local artisans, such as wood carvings, pottery, and jewelry.

Local Culture and Customs of Aurich

Aurich has a rich and diverse culture, shaped by its history, geography, and traditions. Some local customs and beliefs to keep in mind while visiting include:

  • East Frisians are known for their hospitality and friendliness, so don’t hesitate to strike up a conversation with locals and ask for recommendations or advice.

  • Tea is a central part of East Frisian culture, and locals take great pride in their tea-making rituals and traditions. Try to experience a traditional tea ceremony if possible.

Best Time to Visit Aurich

Aurich is a year-round destination, but the best time to visit depends on your preferences and interests. The summer months (June-August) offer pleasant weather and a wide range of outdoor activities, while the winter months (December-February) are perfect for enjoying the town’s Christmas markets and cozy restaurants.

Do’s and Don’ts of Aurich

Here are some basic do’s and don’ts to keep in mind while visiting Aurich:

Do:

  • Respect local customs and traditions.

  • Be polite and friendly to locals.

  • Ask for permission before taking pictures of people or their belongings.

Don’t:

  • Litter or damage natural or historical landmarks.

  • Enter private property without permission.

  • Ignore posted signs or warnings.

Safety and Health

Aurich is generally a safe and healthy town to visit, but it’s always good to take necessary precautions and prepare accordingly.

Safety in Aurich

  • Keep your valuables and important documents in a secure place.

  • Watch for traffic, especially when cycling or walking on busy roads.

  • Follow safety guidelines when engaging in outdoor activities, such as hiking or boating.

Health in Aurich

  • Make sure to have adequate health insurance before traveling.

  • Bring necessary medication and first-aid supplies.

  • Follow basic hygiene and sanitation practices, especially during flu season.

Getting to Aurich: Transportation Options and Logistics

Aurich is easily accessible via various means of transportation, including:

Airports Near Aurich

  • Bremen Airport (BRE): located 125 km away

  • Hamburg Airport (HAM): located 200 km away

  • Hannover Airport (HAJ): located 180 km away

Bus Stations Near Aurich

  • Aurich Bus Terminal: located in the town center

  • Emden Bus Station: located 27 km away

  • Leer Bus Station: located 18 km away

Other Options

  • Train: The Aurich Train Station is located on the Bremen to Emden line.

  • Car: Aurich is easily accessible via the A31 autobahn, with several exits leading to the town.

How to Get Around in Aurich

Getting around Aurich is easy and convenient, with many options available:

  • Walking: The town center and most attractions are within walking distance.

  • Cycling: Aurich has a dense network of bike paths and is a great way to explore the town and the surrounding countryside.

  • Public transport: The town has a network of buses and taxis that operate regularly.

Where to Go Next from Aurich: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Aurich is conveniently located near many other exciting destinations and attractions, such as:

  • Emden: A charming port city with a rich maritime history and many cultural landmarks.

  • Leer: A picturesque town with a lovely riverfront, historic buildings, and quaint shops and cafes.

  • Greetsiel: A scenic fishing village known for its traditional architecture and beautiful harbor.

Conclusion

Whether you’re interested in history, nature, culture, or simply having a relaxing vacation, Aurich has something to offer to every traveler. With its rich heritage, vibrant local culture, and idyllic natural surroundings, Aurich is a hidden gem waiting to be discovered. Plan your trip today and experience the charm and beauty of East Frisia for yourself!
