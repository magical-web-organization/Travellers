Introduction to Hildesheim

Located in the Hanover Region of Lower Saxony, Hildesheim is a picturesque city rich in culture and history. A former bishopric, Hildesheim has a wealth of historical buildings, museums, and art treasures dating back to the Romanesque and Gothic periods. Its charming old town, lush green parks, and tranquil waterways make it an ideal destination for travelers seeking a peaceful getaway in a unique European setting. In this guide, we provide you with a comprehensive overview of the best things to see and do in Hildesheim, including hiking and biking routes, museums and historical places, natural attractions, outdoor adventures, and much more.

List of Places to Visit in Hildesheim

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • St. Michael’s Church: A UNESCO World Heritage site, this magnificent church is a masterpiece of Romanesque architecture.
  • St. Magdalena Chapel: A small church with beautifully preserved frescoes dating back to the Romanesque period.
  • Roemer-und-Pelizaeus-Museum: This museum showcases an impressive collection of artifacts from around the world, including ancient Egyptian and Peruvian treasures.
  • Knochenhauer Amtshaus: This half-timbered building houses the city’s museum and offers a glimpse into Hildesheim’s rich history.

Natural Attractions of Hildesheim: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Magdalena Garden: A beautifully landscaped park with sweeping views of the city and the surrounding countryside.
  • Burgberg: This hill offers stunning panoramic views of the countryside and is a popular spot for hiking and picnicking.
  • Hohnsensee: A popular lake with a wide range of water activities, including swimming, boating, and fishing.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Hildesheim

R1 Route

This 220 km long-distance cycling route connects Hildesheim with the city of Dortmund. Along the way, cyclists can enjoy beautiful landscapes, charming villages, and historical landmarks.

Harz Mountains

Located just a short drive from Hildesheim, the Harz Mountains offer a range of hiking and biking trails for all levels of outdoor enthusiasts. The trails wind through lush forests, alongside sparkling streams, and up to breathtaking vistas.

Best Things to Do in Hildesheim: Activities and Tours

City Walking Tour

Explore the charming old town with a walking tour led by an experienced guide who will introduce you to the city’s rich history and culture.

Cathedral Museum Tour

Discover the treasures of Hildesheim’s impressive Cathedral Museum, home to a vast collection of art and artifacts from the Romanesque and Gothic periods.

Week-Long Itinerary for Hildesheim

Day 1 Explore Hildesheim’s charming old town with a walking tour of its historic buildings, churches, and narrow streets.
Day 2 Visit St. Michael’s Church, one of Germany’s most important Romanesque buildings.
Day 3 Take a day trip to the Harz Mountains for hiking or biking.
Day 4 Visit Roemer-und-Pelizaeus-Museum for a glimpse of ancient Egyptian and Peruvian treasures.
Day 5 Explore the beautiful lakeside park, Hohnsensee, and enjoy a day of swimming, boating, and fishing.
Day 6 Take a guided tour of St. Magdalena Chapel, home to some of the most beautiful frescoes in Germany.
Day 7 Visit Knochenhauer Amtshaus, a half-timbered building now housing the city’s museum, for an insight into Hildesheim’s rich history.

Food and Drink Scene of Hildesheim

Must-Try Dishes

  • Bockwurst: A local specialty, a type of sausage served with mustard and a side of potato salad.
  • Kohl und Pinkel: A traditional German dish made with kale, sausage, and potatoes.
  • Stollen: A Christmas bread made with fruit and nuts, covered in powdered sugar.

Local Beverages

  • Haller Löwenbräu: A local beer brewed in the nearby town of Schwäbisch-Hall.
  • Apfelwein: A local cider made from fermented apples.

Where to Stay in Hildesheim: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Van der Valk Hotel Hildesheim: A modern hotel with comfortable rooms and a convenient location.
  • Novotel Hildesheim: A stylish hotel with a great location near the city center.
  • Ibb Hotel Hildesheim: A budget-friendly hotel with basic but comfortable rooms.

What to Buy in Hildesheim: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Hildesheim Rose: A traditional symbol of Hildesheim, this handmade rose is crafted from gilt and enamel and makes a great souvenir.
  • Haller Salzgebäck: A type of cracker made with salt from the nearby town of Schwäbisch-Hall.
  • Lebkuchen: A traditional German Christmas cookie made with gingerbread, icing, and nuts.

Local Culture and Customs of Hildesheim

  • Silent Night: Hildesheim is believed to be the birthplace of the famous Christmas carol “Silent Night,” which was composed by Franz Xaver Gruber in 1818.
  • Half-Timbered Houses: Many of Hildesheim’s buildings are half-timbered, a construction style dating back to the Middle Ages.

Best Time to Visit Hildesheim

The best time to visit Hildesheim is between May and September, when the temperatures are mild, and the city is alive with festivals and outdoor events.

Do’s and Don’ts of Hildesheim

Do:

  • Take a guided tour of the city’s historical buildings and landmarks.
  • Try local specialties like Bockwurst and Kohl und Pinkel.
  • Respect the city’s cultural heritage and traditions.

Don’t:

  • Disrespect the city’s historic buildings and landmarks.
  • Litter or behave in an unruly manner in public places.
  • Be loud or disruptive in residential areas.

Safety and Health

Safety in Hildesheim

Hildesheim is a safe city, but visitors should exercise the usual caution when walking around, especially at night, and keep an eye on their belongings in crowded places.

Health in Hildesheim

No specific vaccinations are required to visit Hildesheim, and the healthcare system is excellent. However, visitors should make sure they have adequate medical insurance coverage and consult a doctor before traveling if they have any underlying health conditions.

Getting to Hildesheim: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Hildesheim

The nearest airport to Hildesheim is Hanover Airport, which is about 45 minutes away by car or train.

Bus Stations Near Hildesheim

Hildesheim has several bus stations, including the Hildesheim Hauptbahnhof, which is the city’s main train station and bus terminal.

Other Options

Travelers can also reach Hildesheim by car, with several major highways leading to the city, or by train from major German cities.

How to Get Around in Hildesheim

Hildesheim is a compact city that is easy to explore on foot, although there are also public buses and trams available for those who prefer to use public transportation.

Where to Go Next from Hildesheim: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Hanover: The vibrant capital of Lower Saxony is just a short train ride away from Hildesheim.
  • Braunschweig: Explore the fascinating medieval city center of Braunschweig, just a half-hour away by train.
  • Harz Mountains: This stunning mountain range offers a range of outdoor activities, including hiking, biking, skiing, and more.

Conclusion

Hildesheim is a true hidden gem of Germany, with a wealth of cultural and historical attractions, as well as beautiful natural scenery. Whether you’re interested in exploring the city’s art and architecture, hiking in the Harz Mountains, or simply enjoying the local cuisine and culture, Hildesheim has something for everyone. We hope this guide has provided you with all the information you need to plan your perfect trip to this charming city.
