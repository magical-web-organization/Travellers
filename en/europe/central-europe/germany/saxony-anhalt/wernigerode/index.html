Introduction to Wernigerode

Nestled in the Harz Mountains in Saxony-Anhalt, Germany, Wernigerode is a picturesque town that attracts visitors with its historic charm and natural beauty. The town is known for its colourful half-timbered houses, cobbled streets, and medieval castle. Wernigerode is also an ideal base for exploring the Harz National Park, which offers ample opportunities for hiking, biking, and skiing.

In this comprehensive tour guide, we will explore the best things to do in Wernigerode, from historic museums to scenic outdoor adventures and local cuisine. Whether you’re planning a day trip or a week-long getaway, this guide will help you make the most out of your visit to Wernigerode.

List of Places to Visit in Wernigerode

Here’s a list of the top places to visit in Wernigerode:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

1. Wernigerode Castle

Wernigerode Castle is a must-see attraction that sits atop a hill overlooking the town. The castle’s origins date back to the 12th century, and it has since been expanded and renovated several times. Visitors can take a guided tour to learn about the castle’s history and enjoy the panoramic views of the town.

2. Museum of Aviation and Technology

The Museum of Aviation and Technology is located at the Wernigerode Airport and features a vast collection of aircraft, engines, and other aviation-related exhibits. The museum is open year-round and hosts several special events and educational programs.

3. Museum Schiefes Haus

Located in one of the town’s most crooked half-timbered houses, the Museum Schiefes Haus showcases the history of the town’s architecture and the evolution of Wernigerode’s urban landscape.

Natural Attractions of Wernigerode: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

1. Brocken

The Brocken is the highest peak in the Harz Mountains and offers panoramic views of the entire region. Visitors can hike to the summit or take the Brockenbahn, a historic steam train that runs daily.

2. Harz National Park

The Harz National Park covers over 2,000 square kilometers and is home to a diverse array of flora and fauna. The park offers a variety of hiking and biking trails, as well as cross-country skiing and snowshoeing in the winter.

3. Rappbode Dam

The Rappbode Dam is the tallest dam in Germany, standing at 106 meters tall. Visitors can take a guided tour to learn about the history of the dam and enjoy the scenic views of the surrounding area.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Wernigerode

Wernigerode and its surrounding areas offer numerous hiking and biking routes for both beginners and experienced adventurers. Here are some of the most popular routes:

  1. Harzer-Hexen-Stieg
  2. Brocken Loop Trail
  3. Wernigerode Castle to Schierke

Best Things to Do in Wernigerode: Activities and Tours

Wernigerode offers a range of activities and tours for visitors to enjoy. Here are some of the best things to do in Wernigerode:

1. Take a Steam Train Tour

The Harz Narrow-Gauge Railways offer several steam train tours that take passengers through the stunning Harz Mountains. The tours depart from Wernigerode and offer a unique way to explore the region.

2. Take a Segway Tour

Another unique way to explore Wernigerode is by taking a Segway tour. Visitors can rent a Segway and explore the town and its surroundings with a knowledgeable guide.

3. Attend a Traditional Festival

Wernigerode hosts several traditional festivals throughout the year, including the Harz Burgfest and the Christmas market. These festivals offer visitors a chance to experience the town’s rich culture and traditions.

Week-Long Itinerary for Wernigerode

If you’re planning a week-long trip to Wernigerode, here’s a suggested itinerary to help you make the most out of your visit:

Day 1: Explore the town of Wernigerode, visit the castle and Museum Schiefes Haus. Day 2: Take a steam train tour to the Brocken and hike to the summit. Day 3: Explore the Harz National Park and its hiking and biking trails. Day 4: Take a guided tour of the Museum of Aviation and Technology. Day 5: Visit the Rappbode Dam and take a guided tour. Day 6: Take a Segway tour of the town and its surroundings. Day 7: Attend a traditional festival or explore more of Wernigerode at your leisure.

Local Culture and Customs of Wernigerode

Wernigerode is known for its rich culture and traditions, including its distinctive half-timbered architecture, traditional festivals, and local cuisine. Visitors are encouraged to respect these customs and learn more about the town’s history and heritage.

Best Time to Visit Wernigerode

Wernigerode is best visited in the spring or fall when the weather is mild and the town is less crowded. However, the town offers a range of activities and events year-round, so visitors can plan their trip based on their interests and preferences.

Safety and Health

Safety in Wernigerode

Wernigerode is a safe town, but visitors should be cautious when hiking or exploring the Harz National Park. Visitors should also be aware of their surroundings when exploring the town’s historic landmarks and half-timbered houses.

Health in Wernigerode

Visitors to Wernigerode should ensure they have adequate health insurance coverage and bring any necessary medications with them. The town has several hospitals and medical facilities, so visitors can rest assured that they will be well taken care of in case of emergency.

Getting to Wernigerode: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Wernigerode

The closest airport to Wernigerode is Leipzig/Halle Airport, which is approximately 90 minutes away by car.

Bus Stations Near Wernigerode

Visitors can take a bus from nearby cities such as Goslar, Braunlage, or Quedlinburg to reach Wernigerode.

Other Options

Visitors can also travel to Wernigerode by train or car. The town is well connected to other major cities in Germany, making it easily accessible to visitors from all over the world.

How to Get Around in Wernigerode

Wernigerode is a walkable town, but visitors can also rent a bike or take the town’s public transportation system to explore further afield.
