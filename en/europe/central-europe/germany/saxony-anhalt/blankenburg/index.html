Introduction to Blankenburg

Blankenburg is a charming town located in the Harz Mountains of Saxony-Anhalt, Germany. It is known for its stunning natural landscapes, diverse architectural styles, and rich history. Blankenburg presents a perfect combination of scenic beauty and cultural stimulation to its visitors.

This comprehensive travel guide will help you explore and experience the best of Blankenburg.

List of Places to Visit in Blankenburg

There are many must-visit places in Blankenburg that offer something for every type of traveler. Here are some of our top recommendations:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Blankenburg Castle: Constructed in the 12th century, Blankenburg Castle is a magnificent example of medieval architecture. Once serving as a residence of the counts of Blankenburg, now it serves as a museum and cultural center.
  • Blankenburg Town Hall: The Town Hall is a beautiful building showcasing a blend of the Gothic and Renaissance styles.
  • Museum Schloss Blankenburg: Located inside the Blankenburg Castle, this museum showcases the history of the castle and its significance to the region.
  • Fachwerkmuseum Blankenburg: This museum is dedicated to half-timbered houses and their construction, and it provides insight into how this building style has changed over time.

Natural Attractions of Blankenburg: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Blankenburg Quarry: The Blankenburg Quarry is a picture-perfect spot for nature and photography enthusiasts, easily accessible via the Schlossgraben trail.
  • Blankenburg City Park: Enjoy the scenery at this park, which features gardens, streams, and fountains.
  • Blankenburger Teufelsmauer: This natural rock formation is a great spot for hiking and rock-climbing. The panoramic views from the top are simply breathtaking!
  • Rappbode Dam: Marvel at this large dam, which is an important source of hydroelectricity and a great location for water sports such as kayaking and rafting.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Blankenburg

Blankenburg provides a variety of hiking and biking trails for adventurers. Here are some of our top picks:

  • The Harz Witches’ Trail: A 9 km trail that leads to a rock formation known as the Brocken, which is the highest peak in the Harz Mountains.
  • The Teufelsmauerweg Trail: This trail meanders through the Teufelsmauer rock formation, providing stunning views of the surrounding countryside.
  • The Harzer Kloster-Wanderweg Trail: This trail takes you through the picturesque villages and historic monasteries of the Harz Mountains.

Best Things to Do in Blankenburg: Activities and Tours

There are plenty of activities to partake in Blankenburg, including:

  • Joining a guided tour of the Blankenburg Castle and its museum.
  • Visiting one of the many annual festivals Blankenburg has to offer.
  • Going on a scenic flight over Blankenburg to witness the beauty of this region from the sky.
  • Exploring the many beautiful gardens in Blankenburg.

Food and Drink Scene of Blankenburg

Try out some of the delicious local dishes and drinks during your stay in Blankenburg:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Harzer Käse: This cheese is a local specialty that is traditionally eaten sliced or grated on bread.
  • Sauerbraten: A hearty dish of marinated roast beef served with potato dumplings or spätzle.
  • Thüringer Rostbratwurst: A sausage renowned for its spicy and smoky flavor.

Local Beverages

  • Mead: An alcoholic drink made by fermenting honey and water. Mead is said to be one of the oldest alcoholic beverages ever produced.
  • Harzer Kräuterlikör: This herbal liqueur is made with locally sourced herbs and spices. It is best served chilled as an aperitif or digestif.
  • Harzer Hexentrunk: A traditional drink associated with witchcraft and the culture surrounding the Harz Mountains.

  • Harz Mountains’ honey and mead.
  • Local herbal liqueurs.

Local Culture and Customs of Blankenburg

Blankenburg is a melting pot of cultures and traditions. However, Germans, in general, have some cultural customs that are important to observe, such as:

  • Greeting people by shaking hands is a common practice.
  • Germans value punctuality, so showing up late is frowned upon.
  • Tipping is appreciated, but not compulsory.

Best Time to Visit Blankenburg

The best time to visit Blankenburg is during the summer months. The weather is warm, and the scenery is at its most beautiful. Spring and fall are also ideal times to visit, as the temperatures are mild and fewer crowds gather.

Do’s and Don’ts of Blankenburg

Do:

  • Do take a guided tour of Blankenburg Castle for a historical and cultural experience.
  • Do hike the Harz Witches‘ Trail for splendid natural views of the region.
  • Do take an early morning flight over Blankenburg to take on a bird’s eye view of the region.

Don’t:

  • Don’t litter in public spaces or parks in Blankenburg.
  • Don’t leave your accommodation without carrying some cash, as some places may not accept credit cards.
  • Don’t forget to follow cycling laws while mountain biking in Blankenburg.

Safety and Health

Safety in Blankenburg

Blankenburg is generally safe, and the crime rate is low. However, it’s advisable to be cautious with your belongings while in crowded places and to pay attention to your surroundings on late-night walks.

Health in Blankenburg

It’s always a good idea to take out travel insurance before embarking on any trip. Blankenburg has excellent medical facilities and pharmacies that provide quality services to visitors in need.

Getting to Blankenburg: Transportation Options and Logistics

Blankenburg is easily reachable from other cities in Germany and Europe.

Airports Near Blankenburg

  • Leipzig/Halle Airport: This is the closest airport to Blankenburg, approximately 95km away.
  • Hannover-Langenhagen: The second closest airport is approximately 130km away.

Bus Stations Near Blankenburg

  • Busstation Blankenburg: This is the primary bus station in Blankenburg.
  • Busstation Timmenrode: This bus station is located on the outskirts of the city.

Other Options

  • Train: The Blankenburg railways station is connected to many cities and towns in Germany and Europe.
  • Car Rental: Car rental services available in the area offer visitors increased mobility for their travels.

How to Get Around in Blankenburg

The town of Blankenburg is rather small and easily accessible on foot. However, there is a local bus network in place for longer journeys or visitors who would like to see more of the surrounding countryside.

Where to Go Next from Blankenburg: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Blankenburg is surrounded by many other tourist destinations that are worth exploring. Here are some ideas:

  • The Harz Mountains: The Harz Mountains offer beautiful landscapes, wooden bridges, and the famous snowy peaks of Brocken.
  • Quedlinburg: Quedlinburg is a medieval town, featuring cobbled streets, half-timbered houses, and a quaint castle.
  • Wernigerode: Wernigerode is an enchanting town featuring narrow streets, a castle, and quaint alleys.

Conclusion

Blankenburg is a picturesque town that offers visitors the best of Germany’s natural and architectural beauty.
