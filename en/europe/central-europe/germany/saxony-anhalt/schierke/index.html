Introduction to Schierke

Nestled in the Harz Mountains at the foot of the Brocken, Schierke is a small town in Saxony-Anhalt, Germany, surrounded by an enchanting natural landscape with rich culture and history. The town’s name stands for “shepherd’s cottage,” and its rich heritage and scenic charm make it a must-visit destination for tourists and adventurers alike.

List of Places to Visit in Schierke

Take a look at the top places to visit in Schierke:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Harz National Park: A stunning national park that boasts the region’s rich flora and fauna, with plenty of hiking trails and outdoor activities.
  • Brockenbahn: The historic railway that has been operating since 1899, it offers picturesque views of the mountains, forests, and valleys, while telling visitors of the railway’s history.
  • Schierke Train Station: A small and charming railway station that represents the traditions and memories of the town, worth stopping and exploring during your trip.
  • Church of St. Nicholas: The church is located in the center of Schierke and is the key monument of the town.

Natural Attractions of Schierke: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • The Brocken: The highest mountain peak in northern Germany, standing at 1,141 meters tall. It’s a popular place for hiking, skiing, snowboarding and other outdoor activities.
  • Wurmberg: Another notable mountain peak famous for its views, outdoor activities, and Wurmberg Cable Car, which offers visitors spectacular aerial views over the Harz mountains.
  • Okertalsperre: A beautiful freshwater reservoir surrounded by streams and mountains. The area is perfect for hiking or picnicking.
  • Rappbode Dam: A multi-purpose dam that provides energy, recreation, and water supply. Here, visitors can indulge in adventure activities such as rappelling, kayaking or zip lining.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Schierke

Schierke is a hiking and biking paradise, with stunning trails for almost every level. Check out the following recommendations:

  • The Brocken: One of the most popular routes in the Harz region, the hiking trail to the Brocken is perfect for nature lovers and adventure seekers. The trail stretches for around ten kilometers and is manageable for all levels.
  • The Selketalstieg: A long distance hiking trail stretching over 71 kilometers, taking hikers through some of the Harz region’s most breathtaking landscapes.
  • Panorama Trail: A 10.5-kilometer hiking trail around the narrow Selketal valley, offering stunning views of the river, mountains, and forests.

Best Things to Do in Schierke: Activities and Tours

Here are some recommended activities and tours in Schierke:

  • Winter sports: The town is a hub for winter sports lovers, with cross-country and downhill skiing, snowboarding, and snowshoeing available in abundance.
  • Hiking and Biking: Schierke has several hiking and mountain biking trails, catering to the likes of every level of adventure.
  • Exploring the Local Flora and Fauna: Harz National Park is home to a range of wildlife, including red deer, Eurasian lynx, and wildcats, and 4,000 plant species that visitors can explore during their visit.
  • Brockenbahn Railway Trip: Take a historic scenic railway trip to the highest mountain peak in the region.

Week-Long Itinerary for Schierke

Here’s a suggested itinerary to help you make the most of your week-long stay in Schierke:

Day 1: Explore the town of Schierke and take a train ride to Brocken. Day 2: Visit the Harz National Park for a day of wildlife exploring. Day 3: Take a trip to Wurmberg and experience their cable car Day 4: Visit Okertalsperre for a day of picnic and hiking Day 5: Bike the Selketalstieg trail and enjoy the village’s scenery. Day 6: Enjoy a day of winter sports, snowboarding or skiing Day 7: Take a trip to Rappbode Dam for adventure activities such as zip lining, kayaking or rappelling.

Food and Drink Scene of Schierke

Schierke is known for its delicious local cuisine, and here are some must-try dishes and drinks that you can plan to indulge in:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Harzer Cheese: This unique cheese is named after the surrounding mountains and has a distinct sour taste.
  • Bockwurst: A German sausage made from veal, beef, or pork, and is popular throughout the country.
  • Rouladen: A comfort meal made from thin beef slices, rolled up with filling, and cooked in a sauce.

Local Beverages

  • Beer: Germany is famous for its beer, and Schierke has no shortage of refreshing varieties.
  • Schnaps: A popular German clear spirit, distilled from fermented fruit such as pears, apples, and grapes.

Where to Stay in Schierke: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Whether you’re looking for luxury accommodation or budget-friendly places, here are some of the top accommodation options in Schierke:

  • Hotel Brockenstube: Located in the heart of Schierke, this 3-star hotel is a popular option with comfortable rooms and excellent service.
  • Hostel Harz: A budget-friendly option, this hostel offers clean and cozy rooms, perfect for solo travelers or large groups.
  • Pension Schierke: This guesthouse offers rooms and apartments for a more homely feel, with free WiFi and parking.

What to Buy in Schierke: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Schierke has a range of local products that would make great souvenirs to bring back home, including:

  • Harzer cheese
  • Local beer and schnapps.
  • Brocken herbal tea: Made from herbs foraged in the Brocken area.
  • Heart-Shaped craftwork: Local artisans create beautiful craftwork in the shape of wooden hearts from the Harz forest that make an excellent reminder of your visit to Schierke.

Local Culture and Customs of Schierke

Schierke is a vibrant town with lots of local customs and traditions, including:

  • The Brocken Fire Pile: A ritual that takes place every Walpurgis Night on April 30th, where townspeople and visitors gather to light a big fire and celebrate spring.
  • Advent Market: During the Christmas season, Schierke holds an Advent market, where visitors can explore various stalls selling craftwork, souvenirs, and local food.
  • Local Dance: Schierke is known for its traditional folk dancing, with several concerts and events throughout the year to showcase the tradition.

Best Time to Visit Schierke

The best time to visit Schierke is during the summer months, from June to August, when temperatures are mild, and outdoor activities are plentiful. The winter months from December to February draw in a lot of visitors for their winter sports.

Do’s and Don’ts of Schierke

Do:

  • Try the local cuisine and drink.
  • Take part in local festivals and events.
  • Hike the numerous trails the region offers.
  • Dress appropriately for the weather and be sure to pack your sunscreen in the summers.

Don’t:

  • Do not litter, or leave any damage to the natural landscapes.
  • Do not take any wildlife as souvenirs.
  • Do not forget to take note of any safety tips given by the hotel and local authorities.

Safety and Health

Safety in Schierke

Schierke is a safe town, but visitors should take the usual precautions when going on hikes or winter sports. Beware of slippery and rocky surfaces, and be mindful of any natural habitats and animals that could pose a risk.

Health in Schierke

Bring or purchase all necessary medication before arriving in Schierke to avoid encountering any inconvenience in the mountain area. It is advised to wear appropriate clothing and footwear even in the warmer summer months, as temperatures may drop substantially on the summits of the mountains.

Getting to Schierke: Transportation Options and Logistics

Here are some ways to get to Schierke:

Airports Near Schierke

The nearest airport to Schierke is Leipzig/Halle Airport, standing at around 150 km from the town center.

Bus Stations Near Schierke

Visitors may take the regular bus service operated by HarzBus from Goslar, Wernigerode, Braunlage, and Ilsenburg. The station in the heart of Schierke is however conveniently located close to the Brockenbahn station area.

Other Options

Visitors may opt for car rentals to get around Schierke and nearby attractions where public transportation may not be available.

How to Get Around in Schierke

Visitors can make use of numerous public transportation options such as train or bus services. Hiking and biking are also popular ways of exploring the region’s scenic beauty.

Where to Go Next from Schierke: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Schierke lies in proximity to some fantastic nearby destinations well worth a visit, including:

  • Wernigerode – A charming medieval town near Schierke, famous for its timber-framed houses, castle, and beautiful gardens.
  • Goslar – Another beautiful medieval town with UNESCO World Heritage sites and a rich history, only 40 minutes’ drive from Schierke.
  • Quedlinburg – A quaint town, also a World Heritage Site, known for its historic timber-framed homes and weekly markets.

Conclusion

Schierke is a beautiful mountain town that offers something for everyone. From outdoor adventures and scenic trails to rich cultural history and traditions, Schierke is a perfect destination for people of all ages and backgrounds. With this comprehensive guide, you can plan your trip to Schierke to ensure you cover all of your travel needs and desires.
