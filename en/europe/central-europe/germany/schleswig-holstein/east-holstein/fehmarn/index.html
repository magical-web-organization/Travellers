Introduction to Fehmarn

Fehmarn is a stunning island located in the Baltic Sea, just off the northern coast of Germany. This hidden gem is the perfect destination for travelers seeking a unique, cultural experience. With its rich history, natural landscapes, and exciting activities, Fehmarn offers something for everyone. This comprehensive guide will provide you with all the information you need to plan your next adventure to this charming island.

List of Places to Visit in Fehmarn

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Fehmarn has a rich cultural heritage that is reflected in the numerous museums and historical sites located throughout the island. Some of the must-visit cultural attractions in Fehmarn include:

  • Fehmarn Museum
  • St. Nicholas Church
  • Fehmarnsund Lighthouse
  • Fehmarn Castle

Natural Attractions of Fehmarn: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Fehmarn is renowned for its pristine nature and breathtaking views. Some of the must-visit natural attractions in Fehmarn include:

  • Ifjord Natural Reserve
  • Krummsteert Nature Reserve
  • Grüner Brink Cliffs
  • Sheeps’ Pasture Nature Reserve

Hiking/Biking Routes of Fehmarn

Fehmarn is a paradise for hikers and bikers, with its beautifully landscaped routes and scenic views. Some of the most popular hiking and biking routes in Fehmarn include:

  • Fehmarn Belt Path
  • Baltic Sea Cycle Route
  • Oldenburg Trail
  • Coastal Bridle Path

Best Things to Do in Fehmarn: Activities and Tours

Fehmarn has a wide range of activities and tours to offer. Some of the best things to do in Fehmarn include:

  • Windsurfing and kitesurfing
  • Sailing and boat trips
  • Fishing
  • Horseback riding
  • Golfing
  • Guided tours of the island

Week-Long Itinerary for Fehmarn

Day 1: Visit Fehmarn Museum and St. Nicholas Church. Enjoy a stroll through the charming Nordmarkt in the heart of Burg. Day 2: Take a boat trip around Fehmarn and explore its stunning coastline. Day 3: Hike or bike the Baltic Sea Cycle Route and enjoy the breathtaking views of the sea. Day 4: Visit Grüner Brink Cliffs and Sheeps’ Pasture Nature Reserve for a scenic nature experience. Day 5: Spend the afternoon windsurfing or kitesurfing in the warm Baltic waters. Day 6: Explore Fehmarn’s culinary scene and try some traditional dishes like smoked fish and potato salad. Day 7: Relax on Fehmarn’s white sandy beaches or take a day trip to nearby Lübeck.

Food and Drink Scene of Fehmarn

Must-Try Dishes

Fehmarn has a rich culinary scene that offers a range of delicious local dishes. Some of the must-try dishes in Fehmarn include:

  • Smoked fish
  • Potato salad
  • Fehmarn Knüppel (a type of bread roll)
  • Fischbrötchen (fish sandwich)

Local Beverages

Fehmarn’s local beverage scene is not to be missed. Some of the best local beverages in Fehmarn include:

  • Fehmarn beer
  • Nordischer Wein (a local wine)
  • Fehmarn Kräuter (a local spirit)

What to Buy in Fehmarn: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Fehmarn offers a range of unique souvenirs and local products for visitors to take home. Some of the best souvenirs and local products to buy in Fehmarn include:

Local Culture and Customs of Fehmarn

Fehmarn has a rich cultural heritage that is reflected in its customs and traditions. Visitors to Fehmarn should be aware of the following customs and traditions:

  • Respect for nature and the environment is highly valued on the island
  • Fehmarners are known for their hospitality and friendliness
  • It is considered impolite to interrupt someone who is speaking

Best Time to Visit Fehmarn

Fehmarn is a year-round destination, with each season offering its own unique charm. The best time to visit Fehmarn is during the summer months between June and August when the weather is warm and sunny.

Do’s and Don’ts of Fehmarn

Do:

  • Respect the island’s natural environment and wildlife
  • Try some of the local cuisine and beverage
  • Engage with the locals and their customs and traditions

Don’t:

  • Disrespect the island’s natural environment and wildlife
  • Be noisy or disruptive in public spaces
  • Ignore the customs and traditions of the locals

Safety and Health

Safety in Fehmarn

Fehmarn is a relatively safe destination, with low levels of crime. Visitors should, however, take the usual precautions to ensure their safety, such as not leaving their belongings unattended in public spaces.

Health in Fehmarn

Medical facilities on the island are limited, so visitors to Fehmarn should ensure they have comprehensive travel insurance that covers medical emergencies.

Getting to Fehmarn: Transportation Options and Logistics

Fehmarn is easily accessible from mainland Germany. Some of the best transportation options for getting to Fehmarn include:

  • Train: Take a train to Lübeck station and then take a bus or taxi to Fehmarn.
  • Bus: Flixbus offers regular bus services to Fehmarn from major cities in Germany.
  • Car: Visitors can drive to Fehmarn and take the ferry from Puttgarden to the island.

Airports Near Fehmarn

The closest airports to Fehmarn are:

  • Lübeck Airport (72 km away)
  • Hamburg Airport (145 km away)

Bus Stations Near Fehmarn

The closest bus stations to Fehmarn are:

  • Burg auf Fehmarn bus station
  • Puttgarden-Fährbahnhof bus station

Other Options

Visitors can also take the ferry to Fehmarn from Puttgarden.

How to Get Around in Fehmarn

The best way to get around Fehmarn is on foot, by bike, or by car. Visitors should beware that public transportation options on the island are limited, so it is recommended to rent a bike or car to fully explore the island.

Where to Go Next from Fehmarn: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Fehmarn is located in close proximity to several other stunning destinations in northern Germany. Some of the best nearby destinations and excursions from Fehmarn include:

  • Lübeck: A charming medieval city, located just a short drive from Fehmarn.
  • Kiel: A bustling port city, located just over an hour’s drive from Fehmarn.
  • Holstein Switzerland: A region of stunning natural beauty, located just over an hour’s drive from Fehmarn.

Conclusion

Fehmarn is a unique and charming destination that offers a rich cultural heritage, stunning natural landscapes, and exciting activities. With this comprehensive guide, visitors to Fehmarn can plan their perfect adventure to this hidden gem in the Baltic Sea.
