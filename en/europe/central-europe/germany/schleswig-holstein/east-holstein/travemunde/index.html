Introduction to Travemünde

Travemünde is a picturesque seaside town located in East Holstein, in Schleswig-Holstein, in Germany. It is known for its stunning beaches, historic landmarks, cultural attractions, and outdoor adventures. This charming town offers something for everyone, whether you’re a solo traveler, honeymooner, or family vacationer. With this guide, you’ll discover the best of Travemünde and learn everything you need to know for an unforgettable trip.

List of Places to Visit in Travemünde

Travemünde has a wealth of attractions to discover. You can explore museums and historical landmarks, enjoy scenic natural vistas, hike or bike through the town’s beautiful surroundings, or try out exciting activities and tours. Here is a list of must-visit spots in Travemünde:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Viermastbark Passat: This museum ship is located on the Trave river and offers a glimpse into the history of German shipping.
  • Lübeck-Travemünde Golf Club: Located in the heart of Travemünde, this golf course dates back to 1921, making it one of the oldest golf clubs in Germany.
  • Maritimes Denkmal Passat: This bronze statue is dedicated to the crews of the German sailing ships that were lost at sea.

Natural Attractions of Travemünde: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Travemünder Strand: This sandy beach is one of the most beautiful in Europe and is perfect for swimming, sunbathing, and water sports.
  • Brodtener Ufer: This scenic coastal forest is perfect for hiking or biking and offers incredible views of the Baltic Sea and the town.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Travemünde

Travemünde has several hiking and biking trails that allow visitors to explore the town’s natural beauty. Some of the best routes include:

  • Travemünde to Brodtener Steilufer: This 11-kilometer trail offers breathtaking views of the coast and is perfect for hikers and bikers of all experience levels.
  • Priwall circular hiking route: This 7.5-kilometer loop takes visitors through the charming town of Priwall and offers stunning views of the Trave estuary.

Best Things to Do in Travemünde: Activities and Tours

There are plenty of things to do and see in Travemünde. Here are some of the best activities and tours:

  • Sailing trips: Explore the Baltic Sea and the Trave river by joining a boat or sailing tour.
  • Water sports: Try out a range of water activities like windsurfing, kitesurfing, kayaking, and paddleboarding.
  • Historical walking tour: Discover the town’s rich history by joining a guided walking tour of the historic landmarks.

Food and Drink Scene of Travemünde

The food and drink scene of Travemünde is both diverse and delicious. Here are a few must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Marzipan: This famous Lübeck sweet is made of almond paste and is a favorite among locals and tourists alike.
  • Labskaus: A flavorful, hearty stew made of beef, potatoes, pickles, and fried eggs.
  • Herring: A variety of herring dishes are popular in Travemünde, including smoked herring and pickled herring.

Local Beverages

  • Lübzer Pils: A refreshing beer brewed in the nearby town of Lübz.
  • Rotspon: This red wine is made from grapes grown in France and matured in the nearby town of Hamburg.
  • Rum: Travemünde has a long history with rum, and several local bars and shops offer tastings and tours.

What to Buy in Travemünde: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Travemünde offers a range of unique souvenirs and local products that make perfect gifts for friends and family or a treat for yourself. Some of the best items to buy include:

Local Culture and Customs of Travemünde

Travemünde has a rich culture and history. The locals are friendly and welcoming and love to share their love for the town with visitors. Here are a few important customs to keep in mind:

  • Always greet people with a handshake and use formal titles.
  • Respect the town’s history and cultural landmarks.
  • Be mindful of noise levels and maintain a peaceful and respectful environment.

Best Time to Visit Travemünde

The best time to visit Travemünde is during the summer when the weather is warm and sunny, and the town is alive with activity. However, the town also has its charms during the offseason, such as in the winter season where visitors can still enjoy hot cuisine or simply a walk along the shore with the Baltic Sea’s chilly air in your face.

Getting to Travemünde: Transportation Options and Logistics

Travemünde is well-connected to major German cities and can be reached by several modes of transportation. Here are a few options to consider:

Airports Near Travemünde

  • Hamburg Airport: This international airport is located just over an hour’s drive from Travemünde and offers direct flights to several major European and international cities.

Bus Stations Near Travemünde

  • Travemünde Strandbahnhof: This bus station is located in the heart of town and offers connections to several nearby cities in Germany.

Other Options

  • Car rental: Visitors can rent a car and drive to Travemünde from nearby cities and towns.
  • Train: Travemünde has its own train station, which offers connections to nearby cities like Lübeck and Hamburg.

How to Get Around in Travemünde

Travemünde is a compact town and can easily be explored by foot, bike, or public transportation. Here are a few options to consider:

  • Walking: Many of the town’s attractions and landmarks are within walking distance of each other.
  • Biking: Travemünde has several bike rental stations and dedicated bike paths that make it easy to explore the town on two wheels.
  • Public transportation: The town has several bus and train lines that connect it to nearby cities and towns.

Where to Go Next from Travemünde: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Travemünde is a great starting point for exploring the surrounding region of Schleswig-Holstein. Here are a few nearby destinations and excursions to consider:

  • Lübeck: This cultural city is just a short train ride away from Travemünde and is known for its beautiful historic landmarks and bustling food scene.
  • Timmendorfer Strand: This nearby seaside town is perfect for a day trip and offers stunning beaches and picturesque coastal views.
  • Fehmarn Island: This scenic island is a popular destination for outdoor adventurers and beachgoers alike.
