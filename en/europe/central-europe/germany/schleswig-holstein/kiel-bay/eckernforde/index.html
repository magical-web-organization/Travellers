Eckernförde is a picturesque town situated in Kiel Bay, a stunning inlet in Schleswig-Holstein, Germany. With its charming harbor, lively promenade, and proximity to nature, Eckernförde has become a popular destination for vacationers seeking a relaxing and rejuvenating getaway. In this guide, we’ll take you through the best places to visit in Eckernförde, outdoor adventures, delicious cuisine, accommodations, and more, to help you plan the perfect trip.

Introduction to Eckernförde

Eckernförde has over 700 years of history and is dotted with historic architecture. With its beautiful Baltic Sea beaches, vibrant food scene, and fantastic arts and culture offerings, it’s no surprise that Eckernförde is a favourite of many. This lovely town offers a different experience than other holiday spots, as it’s off the usual tourist paths and offers visitors a closer view of German daily life.

List of Places to Visit in Eckernförde

Let’s start with an overview of the top must-see spots in Eckernförde:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Eckernförde is rooted in rich history and has several places to explore and understand the life of the town’s history. Have a look at the following cultural attractions:

  • The Fishing Museum showcases the life of fishermen and the history of angling in Eckernförde. Explore the various exhibits, including vessels, fishing gear, and a fascinating collection of photographs.
  • The Carlshöhe District is a fantastic tourist attraction worth visiting, with cobblestone streets and mysterious underground tunnels that date back to World War II.
  • The former Krenzienhaus now houses the Eckernförde town and regional museum. Guests can explore the city’s past and present, including seafaring life, and German customs.

Natural Attractions of Eckernförde: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Eckernförde has no shortage of natural attractions, from sandy beaches to breathtaking forests. Here are our must-visit natural attractions:

  • The Eckernförde Bay Nature Reserve houses one of the largest remaining salt marsh areas in Germany, making it a haven for wildlife and peaceful walks.
  • The Seawater Wave Pool in Eckernförde is a perfect spot to go on an underwater adventure and enjoy swimming, surfing, and sunbathing all in one place.
  • The fantastic Eichhörnchenwald Nature Reserve overflows with wildlife and plant life, including a wide variety of birds, deer, rabbits, and squirrels available for exploration and sight-seeing.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Eckernförde

Eckernförde’s incredible landscapes make outdoor adventures even more exciting, making it the best place to explore on foot or by bike. On both sides of Eckernförde Bay, there are plenty of scenic paths to uncover, such as:

  • The Ochsenweg trail is known for “oxen-way.” This long-distance path connects different parts of northern Schleswig-Holstein with 330 miles of some of the most stunning trails for biking and hiking.
  • The Hüttener Berge Nature Park has a vast range of hills, valleys, and lakes, while you also get to enjoy close contact to nature, animals and the farmland. It is a perfect place to escape from the bustle of the town and indulge in outdoor activities.

Best Things to Do in Eckernförde: Activities and Tours

Eckernförde is primarily known for its remarkable sightseeing spots teeming with natural elegance, but besides that, the town brims with activities and tours to catch those memorable moments, even on rainy days. Here are the best:

  • Eckernförde has plenty to offer on the water, from sailing to kayaking. A trip from Eckernförde Bay up until the north German landscape is a perfect way to take some photo snaps and make a memorable day.
  • Go on a shopping excursion to Eckernförde’s various retails shops and boutiques, selling exclusive handmade ceramic pieces, postcards, and souvenirs that will make your journey to the town worth remembering.

Food and Drink Scene of Eckernförde

Must-Try Dishes

Eckernförde is famous for its fresh seafood and traditional German dishes. When in Eckernförde, indulge yourself in these must-try dishes from great restaurants:

  • Räucherfisch (“smoked fish”) is a delicious speciality in Eckernförde, but you may also want to grab some smoked salmon, cod, or trout.
  • Labskaus is a traditional dish containing corned beef, beetroot, fried egg, and pickle, served with fried herring or canned corn beef.
  • Bratkartoffeln is another must-try dish. It’s a hearty, homey side dish consisting of pan-fried potatoes and onions, served alongside sausages or meatballs and gravy.

Local Beverages

Eckernförde has a renowned beverage scene that reflects the town’s rich history. Here are some of the local beverages to try out:

  • The Holsten brewery produces some of the best German beers, such as Pils, Special, and the traditional Amber ale. You can visit the brewery for a tour and tasting.
  • Wacken brewery is an uncommonly exceptional spot where you can have micro, home-brewed beers and have some great food from their kitchen.

Where to Stay in Eckernförde: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Trying to find a place to stay in Eckernförde can be stressful. However, the town has some brilliant guesthouses, hotels, and hostels that cater to both luxury and budget travellers. Here are our top recommendations:

  • Rento Apart Hotel Eckernförde is an excellent place to stay, with an optimal location, well-stocked kitchens, and luxury at a fair price.
  • Strandschlösschen is an option for travellers who appreciate grandeur, with an ideal waterfront location and comprehensive amenities.
  • Aalreuse Guesthouse is perfect for budget travellers looking for an affordable and cosy stay. The rooms are bright and clean, providing a refreshing experience.

What to Buy in Eckernförde: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

When travelling, taking souvenirs back home is essential. Eckernförde has several shops and markets that offer unique and handmade items that are worth buying. Here are some souvenirs to consider picking up:

  • Karl Walther Porcelain Manufacturing is a perfect spot to grab some ceramic souvenirs, including tableware, decorative objects, and gift ideas.
  • Eckernförde’s weekly market is where you can buy local delicacies such as smoked sausages, honey, and other edible things.
  • Finally, Eckernförde is a beach lover’s dream, so pick up some souvenirs from maritime shops selling seashells, seaglass, or beach-stones made into attractive necklaces and bracelets.

Local Culture and Customs of Eckernförde

Like everywhere else in Germany, Eckernförde has its own set of values, customs, and etiquette. Here are some of the things to keep in mind:

  • Greetings are essential and usually comprise a firm handshake, direct eye contact, and a “Guten Tag” or “Moin” as a greeting.
  • Dress well and appropriately for events or occasions, as Germans are usually conservative in their dressing style. Casual dressing is acceptable in regular situations, while formal wear is necessary for exceptional events.
  • Drinking culture is a significant part of Germany. However, drinking in public is prohibited in several places and attracts heavy fines. Beer and wine are often served in moderate amounts with meals, so don’t do excessive drinking on an empty stomach.

Best Time to Visit Eckernförde

Eckernförde is an all-year-round destination, but Summer (June-September) is the best time to visit. The weather is generally warm and pleasant, and you can enjoy the Baltic Sea beaches and outdoor activities. However, bear in mind that this is peak travel season, and prices and crowds may be higher. Winter is also a great time to visit for Winter sports and Christmas markets but with colder temps.

Do’s and Don’ts of Eckernförde

Here are some essential do’s and don’ts to keep in mind:

Do:

  • Treat people with respect, kindness, and good manners.
  • Carry your trash with you and dispose of them properly to help maintain the town’s cleanliness.
  • Always have cash on you; not every shop accepts credit cards.

Don’t:

  • Don’t smoke in public areas, including restaurants and bars.
  • Avoid being vocally critical of locals or the town’s history.
  • Don’t litter or leave your trash behind.

Safety and Health

Safety in Eckernförde

Eckernförde is a very safe town. However, visitors should still exercise caution and stay alert, especially when in crowded or touristy areas. Here are some helpful safety tips to stay safe:

  • Be aware of pickpockets, particularly in busy tourist areas, such as markets or festivals.
  • Stay within marked trails when hiking or biking outdoors, avoid crossing privately-owned lands or unmarked routes.
  • Avoid jogging or walking on secluded streets at night.

Health in Eckernförde

Germany has an excellent healthcare system, and Eckernförde has several excellent clinics and hospitals. However, visitors should ensure they have comprehensive travel insurance that covers medical treatment and repatriation if required.

Getting to Eckernförde: Transportation Options and Logistics

Eckernförde is easily accessible by various transportation. Here are the options:

Airports Near Eckernförde

Hamburg airport is the closest airport to Eckernförde, located approximately 83 miles to the south, and is serviced by Germany’s main airlines and international carriers. From there, visitors can take a train or rental car to Eckernförde, which would take around 60-90 minutes.

Bus Stations Near Eckernförde

Eckernförde has a comprehensive, affordable bus service that connects the town to other parts of the region. Visitors can take a bus from the Kiel or Neumünster bus station, which operate daily with frequent services.

Other Options

Travelling by train is arguably the best way to get to Eckernförde. The town has excellent connections to Denmark and central Germany via the Hamburg-Westerland train line, which also stops in several towns en route. From there, taxis or local buses are also available to connect to Eckernförde.

How to Get Around in Eckernförde

Eckernförde is a small town, and visitors can quickly get around on foot or bike, enjoying the scenery. Alternatively, there’s an extensive bus network, with different routes that provide convenient access to various parts of the town centre, including the waterfront and the marina.

Where to Go Next from Eckernförde: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Eckernförde is situated in an ideal location for exploring other parts of Schleswig-Holstein and even Denmark. Some of the best destinations include:

  • Kiel is a nearby bustling city with an exciting arts and culture scene, an excellent shopping experience, and an extensive harbour network making it the perfect place to explore maritime vessels. It is only a 20-minute drive from Eckernförde.
  • Legoland Denmark: If you’re traveling with children or fans of the iconic Danefan, a day trip to Legoland Billund directly over the border in Denmark is an incredibly entertaining diversion.

Conclusion

Eckernförde is a charming gem, packed with exciting possibilities and natural elegance. There’s something for everyone here and always worth a visit.
