Introduction to Amrum

Amrum is a small and beautiful island situated in the North Sea Coast of Germany. Located in the Schleswig-Holstein region, this Central European island is part of the North Frisian Islands. With a rich history, diverse culture, and a plethora of natural and man-made attractions, Amrum is the perfect getaway for travelers seeking a truly unforgettable experience.

This comprehensive tour guide will provide you with everything you need to know before embarking on your Amrum adventure.

Local Culture and Customs of Amrum

  • Respect for nature and the environment plays a significant role in the Amrumer culture.
  • The island has a rich tradition of preserving its history and heritage through cultural events such as the annual Island Hopping Festival.
  • The Frisian language is still spoken and taught in the local schools, making it an essential aspect of the local culture.

Best Time to Visit Amrum

The best time to visit Amrum is during the summer months (June to August) when the weather is warm and sunny. The island experiences a moderate maritime climate, making it an ideal destination all year round.

Do’s and Don’ts of Amrum

Do:

  • Respect nature and its surroundings.
  • Try the local cuisine, including fresh fish and seafood.
  • Take a guided tour to learn more about the island’s history and culture.

Don’t:

  • Litter or damage the environment.
  • Remove seashells or other natural artifacts from the beaches.
  • Wear shoes indoors, as this is considered disrespectful in local customs.

Safety and Health

Safety in Amrum

Amrum is a safe place to visit, with low crime rates. However, travelers should still exercise caution and be mindful of their surroundings, especially when walking alone or traveling at night.

Health in Amrum

Travelers to Amrum do not need any specific vaccinations. However, it is advisable to purchase travel insurance, including medical coverage.

Getting to Amrum: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Amrum

  • Hamburg Airport (HAM)
  • Bremen Airport (BRE)

Both airports are around three hours away by car or public transport.

Bus Stations Near Amrum

The nearest bus stations are in Niebüll and Dagebüll.

Other Options

Ferries from Nordstrand and Dagebüll connect to Wittdün.

How to Get Around in Amrum

Bikes are the primary mode of transportation on the island, with numerous rental options available. Alternatively, visitors can also opt for buses or taxis to get around the island.
