Introduction to Frankfurt an der Oder

Frankfurt an der Oder is a charming city in the state of Brandenburg, Germany, located in Central Europe. It is situated on the border with Poland, divided by the Oder River. It is a popular destination for tourists who want to experience German culture mixed with a touch of Polish flair. The city is famous for its impressive architecture, rich history, and picturesque landscapes.

In this comprehensive travel guide to Frankfurt an der Oder, we will give you detailed information and tips on the best places to visit, where to stay, what to eat, and how to get to this beautiful city.

List of Places to Visit in Frankfurt an der Oder

Frankfurt an der Oder has a lot of places to visit that will interest all types of travelers. Here is a list of the must-see attractions.

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Frankfurt (Oder) Town Hall: Built in 1253, the town hall is an excellent example of Gothic architecture. It is home to the Museum of Local History.
  • Kleist Museum: Dedicated to the life and works of German poet Heinrich von Kleist.
  • Marienkirche: A beautiful cathedral that dates back to the 14th century.
  • Eisenhüttenstadt Museum: A fascinating museum that showcases the transformation of a small village into a modern industrial city under communist rule.

Natural Attractions of Frankfurt an der Oder: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Oderbruch: A vast and beautiful marshland that is home to many species of birds and animals.
  • St. Marienkirche: The oldest church in Frankfurt an der Oder with a stunning interior.
  • Kliestpark: A beautiful park with lush greenery and a stunning fountain.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Frankfurt an der Oder

Frankfurt an der Oder has several hiking and biking routes that offer stunning views of the countryside. Here are some of the best routes to explore:

  • Oder-Neiße Cycle Path: A scenic route that runs along the Oder and Neiße rivers and passes through neighboring Poland.
  • Jägerstieg Trail: A long-distance hiking trail that covers approximately 25 kilometers through the countryside and forests.
  • Kleistpark Trail: A short trail that takes you through a lush park to the outskirts of the city.

Best Things to Do in Frankfurt and der Oder: Activities and Tours

Frankfurt an der Oder has a lot of activities and tours that will keep your vacation exciting and entertaining. Here are some of the best things to do:

  • Explore the city on foot: Frankfurt an der Oder is a walkable city, so exploring it by foot is the best way to discover its hidden gems.
  • Take a boat tour: A boat tour on the Oder is a great way to see the city from a unique perspective.
  • Visit a local market: The local market is the perfect place to find fresh produce, crafts, and souvenirs.
  • Attend a cultural event: Frankfurt an der Oder has a vibrant cultural scene, and it hosts many festivals and events throughout the year.

Week-Long Itinerary for Frankfurt an der Oder

If you’re planning on spending a week in Frankfurt an der Oder, here’s an itinerary that will allow you to see all the best attractions:

  • Day 1: Explore the town center and visit the town hall and the Museum of Local History.
  • Day 2: Take a boat tour on the Oder and visit Kleist Museum in the afternoon.
  • Day 3: Visit the cathedral of Marienkirche in the morning and explore Eisenhüttenstadt Museum in the afternoon.
  • Day 4: Take a biking or hiking trail in the morning and visit the local market in the afternoon.
  • Day 5: Visit the beautiful park, Kliestpark, and attend a cultural event in the evening.
  • Day 6: Explore the neighboring town of Słubice in Poland.
  • Day 7: Spend the last day exploring the city at your leisure and soaking in the atmosphere.

Food and Drink Scene of Frankfurt an der Oder

Frankfurt an der Oder has a fantastic food and drink scene that is sure to satisfy your cravings. Here are some of the must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Eintopf: A hearty vegetable stew that is perfect for cold weather.
  • Boiled beef: A traditional German dish served with root vegetables and potatoes.
  • Maultaschen: A delicious German dumpling stuffed with meat and vegetables.

Local Beverages

  • Frankfurt an der Oder beer: A locally brewed beer that is light and refreshing.
  • Słubice vodka: A smooth and potent vodka from the neighboring town of Słubice.

Local Culture and Customs of Frankfurt an der Oder

Frankfurt an der Oder has a rich culture and customs that visitors should be aware of. Here are some things to keep in mind:

  • Language: German is the official language, but many people also speak Polish.
  • Tipping: Tipping is not mandatory, but it is appreciated, especially at restaurants.
  • Etiquette: It is customary in Germany to greet people with a handshake, and it is considered rude to be late to an appointment.

Best Time to Visit Frankfurt an der Oder

The best time to visit Frankfurt an der Oder is during the summer months, from June to August, when the weather is warm and ideal for outdoor activities.

Getting to Frankfurt an der Oder: Transportation Options and Logistics

Frankfurt an der Oder is well-connected to other German cities and neighboring countries. Here are some ways to get to the city:

  • By Plane: The closest airport to Frankfurt an der Oder is Berlin Tegel Airport, which is 75 km away.
  • By Train: Frankfurt an der Oder has a train station that is well-connected to other German cities.
  • By Car: Frankfurt an der Oder is easily accessible by car via the A12 Autobahn.

Airports Near Frankfurt an der Oder
