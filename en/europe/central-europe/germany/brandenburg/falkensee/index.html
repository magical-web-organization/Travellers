Introduction to Falkensee

Located in the heart of Brandenburg, Falkensee is a charming city that offers a perfect blend of natural beauty, cultural heritage, and outdoor adventures. Situated in Central Europe, Falkensee is easily accessible from Berlin, making it an ideal destination for a day trip or a weekend getaway.

List of Places to Visit in Falkensee

Falkensee has a plethora of attractions to offer to its visitors. Whether you are interested in history, nature, or culture, this city has something for everyone. Here’s a list of places you shouldn’t miss visiting on your trip to Falkensee:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Falkensee Museum: This museum offers a glimpse into the history and culture of Falkensee. It showcases the city’s rich heritage through exhibits, artifacts, and interactive displays.
  • Evangelical Church of Falkensee: Built-in 1776, this church is an architectural marvel and an iconic symbol of Falkensee’s history.
  • Spandau Citadel: Located just a few kilometers away from Falkensee, the Spandau Citadel is a 16th-century fortress that has been converted into a museum showcasing the history of Spandau and Berlin.

Natural Attractions of Falkensee: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Falkenhagener See: This expansive lake is a popular spot for swimming, fishing, and boating. With its serene setting and picturesque views, Falkenhagener See is a must-visit for nature lovers.
  • Nauener Platte Nature Reserve: This protected area boasts a diverse range of flora and fauna and is home to many rare bird species. The reserve offers many scenic hiking trails that wind through dense forests and open fields.
  • Seegefelder Weg: This cycling route takes you through the scenic countryside of Falkensee and is an excellent way to explore the surrounding area.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Falkensee

Falkensee is a paradise for outdoor enthusiasts and offers many exciting hiking and biking routes. Here are a few trails that you should add to your itinerary:

  • Pinewood Trail: This 12-kilometer trail takes you through the pine forests of Falkensee and offers breathtaking views of the surrounding countryside.
  • Havel Cycle Route: This popular cycling trail runs along the River Havel and passes through many picturesque towns and villages, including Falkensee.
  • Döberitzer Heide Nature Reserve: This nature reserve offers many trails that wind through the forests and meadows of the former military training area.

Best Things to Do in Falkensee: Activities and Tours

Here are a few more exciting things to do in Falkensee:

  • Falkensee Farmer’s Market: Enjoy a taste of local culture at the weekly Falkensee Farmer’s Market, where you can sample fresh produce and local specialties.
  • Go on a guided tour of the city and discover the hidden gems that Falkensee has to offer.
  • Attend one of the many cultural events that take place in Falkensee throughout the year, including music festivals, arts-and-crafts fairs, and theater performances.

Food and Drink Scene of Falkensee

Falkensee may be a small town, but it has no shortage of delicious food and drink options. Here are some of Falkensee’s must-try dishes:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Currywurst: a popular German fast food dish consisting of a sausage seasoned with curry ketchup and served with French fries.
  • Eisbein: this traditional German dish is made from a pickled and boiled pork knuckle and served with potato dumplings and sauerkraut.
  • Kasseler: a smoked and cured pork chop that is typically served with mashed potatoes and sauerkraut.

Local Beverages

  • Berliner Weisse: a tart and refreshing beer that is often served with raspberry or woodruff syrup.
  • Apfelwein: also known as “apple wine,” this alcoholic beverage is made from fermented apples and has a crisp and tangy flavor.
  • Kräuterlikör: this herbal liqueur is a popular after-dinner drink in Germany and is said to aid digestion.

Where to Stay in Falkensee: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

There are plenty of accommodation options in Falkensee to suit every budget and taste. Here are a few top picks:

  • Landhotel Falkensee: This family-run hotel is located in the heart of Falkensee and offers comfortable rooms, a cozy restaurant, and a charming garden.

  • Pension Krause: This guesthouse boasts spacious rooms and a lush garden and is a popular choice among travelers who value tranquility and relaxation.

  • Ferienwohnung Falkensee: If you’re looking for a self-catering option, this apartment is an excellent choice. It features a fully equipped kitchen, free wifi, and a beautiful terrace.

What to Buy in Falkensee: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Falkensee may not be a shopping mecca, but it has plenty of unique souvenirs and local products that are worth bringing home. Here are a few ideas:

  • Hand-crafted pottery from the local artisanal shops
  • Locally produced honey and jams
  • Regional wines and spirits

Local Culture and Customs of Falkensee

Germans are known for being punctual and courteous, and Falkensee is no exception. Here are a few cultural customs to keep in mind:

  • Tipping is customary in restaurants. A tip of 10% is considered generous.
  • It’s considered impolite to address people by their first name unless they have given you permission to do so.
  • German society places great importance on punctuality, so be sure to arrive on time for appointments and meetings.

Best Time to Visit Falkensee

The best time to visit Falkensee is during the summer months, from June to August, when the weather is warm and sunny, and the outdoor activities are in full swing. However, keep in mind that this is also the peak tourist season, and accommodation and attractions may be crowded. If you prefer a quieter and more laid-back experience, you might consider visiting in the spring or fall when the crowds have thinned, and the weather is still pleasant.

Do’s and Don’ts of Falkensee

Here are a few do’s and don’ts to keep in mind when visiting Falkensee:

Do:

  • Explore the city’s cultural and natural attractions
  • Try the local cuisine and beverages
  • Respect the local customs and culture

Don’t:

  • Be loud and disruptive in public places
  • Litter or damage the natural beauty of the town
  • Wear revealing clothing in churches or other religious sites

Safety and Health

Falkensee is a safe destination for tourists, and the crime rate is low. However, it’s always a good idea to take standard safety precautions, such as no carrying valuables, staying alert in crowded areas, and locking your doors at night.

Safety in Falkensee

  • Use caution when crossing roads, as drivers may not always yield to pedestrians.
  • Keep an eye on your belongings in crowded areas, such as train stations and tourist attractions.
  • Avoid walking alone at night and stick to well-lit and populated areas.

Health in Falkensee

  • Ensure you have adequate medical insurance for your trip.
  • Check with your doctor before leaving for any necessary vaccines or medications.
  • Insect repellent is advisable, particularly if you plan to spend time outdoors in the summer months.

Getting to Falkensee: Transportation Options and Logistics

Falkensee is easily accessible by various modes of transportation:

Airports Near Falkensee

The nearest airports to Falkensee are the Berlin Tegel Airport and the Berlin Schönefeld Airport, both located within a 30-minute drive of the city.

Bus Stations Near Falkensee

Falkensee is well-connected by bus, with regular services running from Berlin and other nearby towns and cities.

Other Options

Falkensee is easily accessible by car, with several major highways running through the area. Additionally, the city boasts an extensive network of cycling trails, making it easy to explore the town and its surrounding natural beauty on two wheels.

How to Get Around in Falkensee

Falkensee is a small town, and much of it is easily walkable. However, if you need to travel further afield, there are several options available:

  • The local bus service connects Falkensee to surrounding towns and cities.
  • Taxis are readily available in the city center.
  • Renting a bike is an excellent option for exploring the town and its surrounding countryside.

Where to Go Next from Falkensee: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

If you’re looking to explore further afield, Falkensee is well-positioned to serve as a base for excursions to nearby destinations, including:

  • Berlin: Just a 25-minute train ride away, Germany’s capital offers a wealth of attractions, including iconic landmarks, world-class museums, and a vibrant nightlife scene.
  • Potsdam: This picturesque town, located just 20 kilometers from Falkensee, boasts a stunning collection of palaces, parks, and gardens, including the famous Sanssouci Palace.
  • Spreewald: This unique region is known for its network of small canals and waterways, making it a popular destination for boating and kayaking.

Conclusion

Falkensee is a hidden gem in Germany’s Brandenburg region, offering a unique blend of history, nature, and culture. Whether you’re interested in hiking, biking, or relaxing in the stunning countryside, Falkensee is sure to delight and inspire. Be sure to add it to your travel itinerary and experience the town’s charm and beauty for yourself!
