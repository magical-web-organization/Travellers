Introduction to Nassau

Nassau is a charming small town located in the beautiful North-East Rhineland-Palatinate region of Germany. The town, nestled in the hills of the Taunus Mountains, has a rich history and is known for its picturesque views, historical buildings, and outdoor activities. In this guide, we will explore the best things to see, do, eat, and stay in Nassau.

List of Places to Visit in Nassau

Nassau is home to a variety of attractions, from cultural landmarks to natural scenery. Here are some must-visit places to add to your itinerary:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Nassau Castle: A well-preserved castle that dates back to the 13th century.
  • Nassau Museum: Houses an extensive collection of local artifacts, including pottery, textiles, and artwork.
  • St. Martin’s Church: Built in the 12th century, the church is a fantastic example of Romanesque architecture.

Natural Attractions of Nassau: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Lahn River: A picturesque river that runs through Nassau and offers plenty of opportunities for kayaking, hiking, and cycling.
  • Lahneck Castle: A stunning vista, perched on a hilltop overlooking the Lahn River and the surrounding hills.
  • Taunus Mountains: Offering a variety of hiking trails, take a walk in the Taunus hills and enjoy the beautiful views over the region.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Nassau

Nassau and its surrounding areas are a hiker’s paradise. There are numerous hiking trails in the region, such as the Lahnwanderweg, which takes you through the lush landscape along the river. For more experienced hikers, there is the Taunusstrasse, which offers challenging climbs and rewarding views. Cycling enthusiasts can opt for the Lahntalradweg, a beautiful cycling path that follows the course of the river through the region.

Best Things to Do in Nassau: Activities and Tours

  • Take a boat tour down the Lahn River for a relaxing and scenic experience.
  • Visit the weekly farmer’s market to purchase local handmade crafts, food items, and other souvenirs.
  • Participate in an outdoor adventure, such as rock climbing, ziplining, or kayaking, with local tour companies.

Week-Long Itinerary for Nassau

Day 1: Visit Nassau Castle and the Nassau Museum.

Day 2: Hike the Taunusstrasse trail and enjoy the scenic views over the Taunus Mountains.

Day 3: Explore Lahneck Castle and hike the Lahneck trail.

Day 4: Go kayaking or canoeing on the Lahn River.

Day 5: Take a cycling tour of the Lahntalradweg.

Day 6: Visit St. Martin’s Church and explore the town’s Old Town district.

Day 7: Participate in outdoor activities such as rock climbing, zip-lining, and high ropes courses offered by local tour companies.

Food and Drink Scene of Nassau

Must-Try Dishes

  • Rheinische Rindsroulade: Beef is rolled with onions, bacon, and pickles, then cooked in a rich sauce made from red wine and beef broth.
  • Apfelwein: A popular local apple cider and a refreshing drink choice on a warm summer day.
  • Handkäse mit Musik: A local cheese dish made from sour milk and marinated with garlic and onions.

Local Beverages

  • Bitburger beer: Produced in nearby Bitburg, it is a popular pilsner beer that is widely available in Nassau.
  • Riesling wine: Grown in the surrounding vineyards, Riesling is a quintessential German wine and a must-try in Nassau.

Where to Stay in Nassau: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Hotels

  • Hotel Oranien: Located in the center of Nassau, this historic grand hotel offers comfortable rooms and excellent service.
  • Hotel Kopp: A cozy hotel that has been family-run for generations, offering a welcoming and comfortable atmosphere.

Bed and Breakfasts

  • Villa Alexander: A charming villa that offers antique furnishings within restored historic rooms and a beautiful garden.

Hostels

  • Burg Schaumburg Hostel: A youth hostel located in a castle, offering reasonable prices and a unique accommodation experience.

What to Buy in Nassau: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Handmade pottery and textiles from the Nassau Museum gift shop.
  • Locally produced honey, apple cider, and wine from the farmer’s market.
  • Handcrafted wooden toys, jewelry, and artworks from local artisans.

Local Culture and Customs of Nassau

  • The region is known for its traditional festivals, including the Nassau Castle Festival and the Wine Festival.
  • It is customary to greet people with a handshake.
  • Tipping in restaurants and cafes is typically 10% to 15% of the total bill.

Best Time to Visit Nassau

The best time to visit Nassau is between May and September when the weather is warm and the outdoor activities are in full swing. However, the beautiful fall foliage and the picturesque Christmas markets in winter are also worth visiting.

Do’s and Don’ts of Nassau

Do:

  • Do try the local wines and beers.
  • Do pack comfortable hiking shoes.
  • Do be respectful of customs and traditions.

Don’t:

  • Don’t expect to find many restaurants open on Sundays.
  • Don’t forget to bring appropriate clothing for the weather.

Safety and Health

Safety in Nassau

Nassau is a safe destination for tourists, but it is always wise to take basic safety precautions such as not leaving valuables in public view and keeping an eye on belongings.

Health in Nassau

The tap water is safe to drink, and there are several pharmacies in the town if needed.

Getting to Nassau: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Nassau

The nearest airport to Nassau is Frankfurt International Airport, located 60 miles away. from Frankfurt Airport, taxis or buses are readily available for transportation.

Bus Stations Near Nassau

Several buses connect Nassau to neighboring towns and cities, including Koblenz, Wiesbaden, and Frankfurt.

Other Options

Another popular option is to travel by train. Nassau is located along the scenic Iron-Rhine railway, which runs from Düsseldorf to Antwerp.

How to Get Around in Nassau

The best way to explore Nassau’s main attractions is on foot or by bike. However, if you plan to travel to nearby towns or hiking trails, public transportation and rental cars are also available.

Where to Go Next from Nassau: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Koblenz: A historic city located at the confluence of the Rhine and Moselle rivers, famous for its fortress and ancient Roman architecture.
  • Wiesbaden: A spa town known for its hot springs and grand old buildings, including a magnificent Russian Orthodox church.
  • Frankfurt: The financial capital of Germany, Frankfurt brims with cultural landmarks such as the famed cathedral, quality restaurants, pubs, and museums.

Conclusion

Nassau is a small charming destination located in North-East Rhineland-Palatinate, in Rhineland-Palatinate, in Germany. Whether you prefer hiking, indulging in local cuisine, or discovering cultural and historical landmarks, Nassau has something for all traveler types.
