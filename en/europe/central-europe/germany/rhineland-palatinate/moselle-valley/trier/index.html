Introduction to Trier

Trier, situated in the picturesque Moselle Valley of Rhineland-Palatinate, is one of the oldest cities in Germany. Founded by the Celts in the late 4th century BC, Trier enjoys a rich heritage that dates back over two millennia and is home to numerous UNESCO World Heritage Sites.

Often called the “Rome of the North,” Trier boasts stunning Roman ruins, medieval architecture, and a vibrant cultural scene. From exploring ancient relics to uncovering local customs, there is something for everyone in this breathtaking city.

The best time to visit Trier is from late May to September when the weather is pleasant, and the city is bustling with events and festivals.

Safety and Health

Safety in Trier

Trier is a safe city with low crime rates, but visitors should take normal precautions and keep an eye on their belongings.

Health in Trier

The standard of health care in Trier is high, and there are many hospitals and clinics in the city. Visitors from the EU and the UK are advised to bring their European Health Insurance Card (EHIC).
