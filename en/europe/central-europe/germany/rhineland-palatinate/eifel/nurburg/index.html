Introduction to Nürburg

Nürburg, located in the Eifel region of Rhineland-Palatinate, Germany, is a beautiful town surrounded by lush forests and stunning landscapes. The town is best known for the Nürburgring, a famous race track that has hosted some of the world’s biggest motor racing events. But Nürburg is much more than just a racing destination. The town offers plenty of natural beauty, cultural attractions, and outdoor adventures that make it a perfect holiday destination for everyone.

In this comprehensive travel guide, we’ll help you explore everything Nürburg has to offer.

List of Places to Visit in Nürburg

Nürburg has a lot to offer visitors, from fascinating museums to breathtaking natural attractions. Here are some of the top places to visit in Nürburg:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Nürburg Castle: This impressive castle dates back to the 12th century and has been restored to its former glory. Visitors can explore the castle grounds and enjoy stunning views of the surrounding landscapes.

  • Nürburgring Motorsport Museum: This museum is dedicated to the history of motorsports and showcases an extensive collection of racing cars, motorcycles, and other artifacts.

  • Ring Werk: This interactive museum allows visitors to experience the excitement of racing firsthand. Visitors can explore various exhibits, take a driving simulator tour, and even take a spin on the adjacent go-kart track.

Natural Attractions of Nürburg: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Teufelsloch: This natural phenomenon is an impressive cave system located in the hills near Nürburg. Visitors can explore the caves and enjoy the stunning views of the surrounding landscapes.

  • Hohe Acht: This is the highest peak in the Eifel region and offers breathtaking views of the surrounding countryside.

  • Nürburg-Maare-Trail: This scenic hiking trail takes visitors through some of the most beautiful parts of the Eifel region. The trail ends at the stunning volcanic crater lakes in the nearby town of Daun.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Nürburg

Nürburg is surrounded by lush forests and beautiful landscapes, making it the perfect destination for outdoor enthusiasts. Here are some of the best hiking and biking routes in and around Nürburg:

  • Nürburgring Panorama: This 25-kilometer long trail offers stunning views of the Nürburgring race track and the surrounding landscapes.

  • Eifelsteig Trail: This long-distance hiking trail runs for over 300 kilometers and passes through some of the most beautiful parts of the Eifel region.

  • Cycling Routes: Nürburg is located on the Moselle Cycle Path, which runs along the Moselle River from Metz, France, to the Rhine River at Koblenz, Germany. This is a perfect route for cyclists of all levels.

Best Things to Do in Nürburg: Activities and Tours

Nürburg offers plenty of exciting activities and tours for visitors. Here are some of the best things to do in Nürburg:

  • Tour the Nürburgring: Visitors can take a tour of the iconic race track and learn about its history, architecture, and various sections.

  • Drive a Racecar: Visitors can experience the thrill of driving a high-performance race car on the Nürburgring track itself.

  • Admire the Views: Visitors can take a relaxing hot air balloon ride and enjoy stunning views of the Nürburg region from above.

Week-Long Itinerary for Nürburg

Here’s a sample itinerary for a week-long visit to Nürburg:

Day 1 Arrive in Nürburg, check into your hotel, and explore the town
Day 2 Take a tour of the Nürburgring and visit the Nürburgring Motorsport Museum
Day 3 Hike the Nürburg-Maare-Trail and explore the crater lakes in Daun
Day 4 Visit Nürburg Castle and the Ring Werk museum
Day 5 Take a hot air balloon ride to admire the stunning views of the region
Day 6 Drive a racecar on the Nürburgring track
Day 7 Explore the Eifelsteig Trail and enjoy the natural beauty of the area

Food and Drink Scene of Nürburg

Nürburg has a vibrant food and drink scene, with plenty of local specialties to try. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Himmel und Ääd: This local dish is made with mashed potatoes, applesauce, and black pudding.

  • Pfälzer Saumagen: This traditional dish is made with pork, potatoes, and spices.

  • Sauerbraten: This popular German dish features roasted beef marinated in vinegar and spices.

Local Beverages

  • Bäcker-Bier: This local beer is brewed with traditional methods and features a unique flavor.

  • Nürburg Quelle: This natural mineral water is sourced from the Eifel region and is known for its purity and refreshing taste.

  • Riesling: The Moselle region where Nürburg is located is famous for its wines, especially its Rieslings.

Where to Stay in Nürburg: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Nürburg has plenty of accommodation options to suit all budgets. Here are some top choices:

  • Lindner Nürburgring Motorsport Hotel: This hotel is located right next to the Nürburgring and offers luxurious rooms with stunning views of the race track and the Eifel landscape.

  • Land-gut-Hotel zur Burg Nürburgring-Eifel: This cozy hotel is located in the heart of Nürburg and features comfortable rooms and a restaurant serving local specialties.

  • Downhill Hotel: This budget-friendly hotel is located near the Nürburgring and is a perfect choice for those looking for affordable accommodation in the area.

What to Buy in Nürburg: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Nürburg has plenty of souvenirs and local products to bring home. Here are some top choices:

  • Nürburgring Memorabilia: Take home a piece of the iconic race track with merchandise like t-shirts, hats, and posters.

  • Eifel Honey: This natural honey is sourced from the Eifel region and is known for its delicious taste and health benefits.

  • Moselle Wines: The Moselle region produces some of the best wines in Germany, and you can find a wide variety to bring home as gifts or souvenirs.

Local Culture and Customs of Nürburg

Nürburg is a small town with a rich cultural heritage and customs. Here are some things to keep in mind when visiting Nürburg:

  • Respect the Local Customs: Nürburg is a small town, and locals take pride in their traditions and customs. Be respectful when visiting churches, castles, and other cultural attractions.

  • Tipping: Tipping is generally expected in Nürburg, like in the rest of Germany. A good rule of thumb is to tip around 10% of the bill, but it’s always appreciated to keep the change of your payment.

Best Time to Visit Nürburg

The best time to visit Nürburg is from May to September when the weather is warm and sunny, and outdoor activities are in full swing. However, keep in mind that this is peak tourist season, so expect higher prices and crowds. For a more peaceful and budget-friendly trip, consider visiting during the shoulder season of April and October.

  • Do take advantage of the outdoor activities and scenic views.

  • Do explore the Nürburgring and its fascinating history.

Safety and Health

Nürburg is a safe destination, and visitors should be able to enjoy their trip without any safety concerns. Here are some safety and health tips to keep in mind:

Safety in Nürburg

  • Be careful when hiking or biking in the natural areas, and always follow the marked trails.

  • When driving on the Nürburgring, follow the speed limits and be aware of other drivers.

  • Leave your valuables and important documents in the hotel safe.

Health in Nürburg

  • Germany has a high standard of health care, and visitors can access excellent medical care in Nürburg if needed.

  • Be sure to bring any prescription medications with you and bring a copy of your prescription just in case.

  • Drink plenty of water, especially during the hot summer months.

Getting to Nürburg: Transportation Options and Logistics

Nürburg is located in the Eifel region of Rhineland-Palatinate, Germany, and is easily accessible by various transportation options.

Airports Near Nürburg

  • Frankfurt International Airport: This is the closest major airport to Nürburg, with direct flights from all major cities in Europe and the world.

  • Köln Bonn Airport: This airport is located about 130 km from Nürburg and offers domestic and international flights.

Bus Stations Near Nürburg

  • Nürburg Train Station: Although it’s a train station, there are also bus connecting lines that can take you around the city.

  • Adenau Train Station: This is just 8 km north of Nürburg and is a popular starting point for hiking trails.

Other Options

If you’re already in Germany, you can also reach Nürburg by train or by car. Both options provide a scenic journey through the beautiful Eifel landscape.

How to Get Around in Nürburg

Nürburg is a small town and can easily be explored on foot. However, if you want to explore the surrounding areas, renting a bike or a car is a good option.

Where to Go Next from Nürburg: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Nürburg is located in the heart of the Eifel region, making it a perfect starting point for exploring the area’s other attractions. Here are some nearby destinations and excursions worth considering:

  • Cochem: This beautiful town is located on the Moselle River and is famous for its castle and vineyards.

  • Ahr Valley: This scenic valley is known for its beautiful landscapes, hiking trails, and wineries.

  • Trier: Germany’s oldest city, Trier, is located just 120 km from Nürburg and offers ancient Roman ruins, churches, and museums to explore.

Conclusion

Nürburg is a beautiful town that offers visitors a chance to explore Germany’s natural beauty, rich cultural heritage, and exciting outdoor adventures. Whether you’re a motorsports fan or just looking for a relaxing vacation, Nürburg has something to offer everyone. With this comprehensive travel guide, you’re sure to have an unforgettable trip to Nürburg, Germany.
