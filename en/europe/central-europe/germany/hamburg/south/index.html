Introduction to South

Hamburg, one of the largest cities in Germany, is divided into seven districts - one of which is South. Located centrally in the city, South Hamburg, or simply South, is known for its perfect blend of nature and modernity, making it a must-visit destination in Hamburg.

List of Places to Visit in South

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Museum of Hamburg History - Explore the rich history, culture, and development of Hamburg from medieval times to the present day.
  • Miniature Wonderland - The largest model railway exhibition in the world with detailed miniatures of famous global landmarks.
  • Rickmer Rickmers - A historic ship turned museum showcasing the maritime history of Hamburg.

Natural Attractions of South: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Elbstrand Beach - A scenic beach offering panoramic views of the Elbe river and industrial harbors, perfect for a picnic or a walk.
  • Heide Park Resort - Amusement park with thrilling rides and rollercoasters with a variety of themes for all age groups.
  • Wittingen Nature Reserve - Enjoy a lake tour on a bike or foot, surrounded by diverse and breathtaking natural beauty.

Hiking/Biking Routes of South

  • Alster Trail - A 3-hour long trail along the Alster River with numerous picture-perfect spots along the way.
  • Elbe Cycle Path - A 1230 km trail extending from the Czech Republic to the north sea coast in Hamburg; with South Hamburg being one of the beautiful stops.

Best Things to Do in South: Activities and Tours

  • Harbor Boat Tour - Explore Hamburg’s historic seaport and its stunning architecture from a unique perspective on a tour.
  • Climbing Center - Climb to new heights at the climbing center with various routes and levels.
  • Discovering South by Foot - Go on a walking tour to explore the historical parts and natural beauty of South with an experienced guide.

Food and Drink Scene of South

Must-Try Dishes

  • Franzbrötchen - A cinnamon pastry.
  • Himmel un Ääd - Heaven and Earth, mashed potatoes, and apples served with fried black pudding and onions.
  • Birnen, Bohnen und Speck - Pears, beans, and bacon.

Local Beverages

  • Astra - A locally brewed beer with a unique taste.
  • Sekt - A type of sparkling wine, grown and produced in the region.

Where to Stay in South: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Motel One Hamburg Alster - A modern and budget-friendly hotel located near the Alster river.
  • The Madison Hotel Hamburg - A high-end hotel known for its spacious rooms and friendly staff.
  • Pyjama Park Hotel & Hostel - A unique, affordable, and stylish hostel experience with a cozy atmosphere.

What to Buy in South: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Fish Market - Visit the iconic fish market for handmade crafts, local goods, fish sandwiches, and souvenirs.
  • Stoffmarkt Holland - A Dutch fabric market, perfect for souvenirs for craft enthusiasts and sewers.
  • Viktualienmarkt - A farmer’s market with fresh local products, cheese, wine, and souvenirs.

Local Culture and Customs of South

  • Germans are punctual, and it’s best to arrive on time or even a few minutes early for appointments and meetings.
  • Germans are eco-conscious citizens, and it’s common to recycle and take eco-friendly measures whenever possible.
  • Germans typically adhere to their schedules and appreciate it when others do the same.

Best Time to Visit South

The best time to visit South Hamburg is from May to September when the weather is warm, and the outdoor activities are at their finest. Hamburg has a maritime climate, so it is generally mild all year round; however, tourists may experience rainy days.

Do’s and Don’ts of South

Do:

  • Learn a few German phrases before you come; basic greetings are always appreciated.
  • Drink plenty of tap water; it’s safe and free in Hamburg.
  • Respect the rules and cleanliness of public places.

Don’t:

  • Assume everyone speaks English; try to use simple German phrases or use a translation app to communicate.
  • Jaywalk; wait for the green light at the pedestrian crossings.
  • Smoke in public places, cafes, and bars.

Safety and Health

Safety in South

South Hamburg is a safe place to visit, with low crime rates. However, tourists should take the usual safety measures such as not leaving their valuables unattended and avoiding questionable neighborhoods at night.

Health in South

As a developed and modern city, Hamburg has excellent healthcare facilities. The European Health Insurance Card (EHIC) is accepted in all medical facilities; however, it’s always recommended to have travel insurance as well.

Getting to South: Transportation Options and Logistics

Hamburg has excellent public transportation options, including buses, trains, and trams. The Hamburg airport is well-connected to the central city, and taxis are also easily available.

Airports Near South

  • Hamburg Airport (HAM) - 8.3 km.

Bus Stations Near South

  • Hamburg ZOB - 14.2 km.
  • Hamburg Bus Terminal - 6.9 km.

Other Options

  • Train services from Hamburg Hauptbahnhof to other German and European cities are frequent and efficient.

How to Get Around in South

Public transportation, such as buses and metros, is an efficient and budget-friendly option for getting around South. Bicycles can also be rented for exploring the city on two wheels.

Where to Go Next from South: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Stade - A medieval town just outside of Hamburg, with stunning architecture and beautiful gardens.
  • Lübeck - A UNESCO World Heritage site with its rich history and mesmerizing architecture.
  • Lake Schwerin - A picturesque lake in Schwerin, with a mesmerizing palace and beautiful gardens.

Conclusion

South Hamburg is a unique and incredible destination for nature and urban lovers alike. With its picturesque surroundings, diverse local culture, and delicious food and drink, South Hamburg should be on every traveler’s bucket list.
