Introduction to Tempelhof and Neukölln

Tempelhof and Neukölln are two exciting districts of Berlin, of enormous historical and cultural importance, that many visitors overlook. Located in southern Berlin, the districts are thriving melting pots of new and old, where visitors can explore the former Tempelhof Airport, shop in futuristic fashion stores, or get lost in one of several beautiful parks.

List of Places to Visit in Tempelhof and Neukölln

Here is a list of must-visit attractions in Tempelhof and Neukölln:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Tempelhof Airport: A former airport that now houses events, public spaces, and sports facilities.

  • Days of Berlin: One Street - One Story: a guided walking tour that explains the culture, history, and architecture of the Karl-Marx-Strasse.

Natural Attractions of Tempelhof and Neukölln: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Körnerpark: A small park located in Neukölln that has an amphitheater, flower gardens, and a wading pool.

  • Hasenheide: A massive park located in the heart of Neukölln that attracts dogs and their owners, joggers, and sunbathers.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Tempelhof and Neukölln

Hiking and biking are great ways to explore what Tempelhof and Neukölln have to offer, and here are two recommended routes:

  1. Start at Hasenheide, then head towards the Tempelhofer Feld park. Continue biking until you reach the Landwehr Canal, a great spot for resting and watching the boats go by.

  2. Follow the Neukölln canal to the Funkhaus Nalepastrasse, and then proceed south to the Britzer Garten.

Best Things to Do in Tempelhof and Neukölln: Activities and Tours

From exploring the rich history of the area to experiencing street art and a vibrant food scene, there is much to do in Tempelhof and Neukölln. Some of the best things to do while you’re here include:

  • Street Art Walk Tour: A guided walking tour that takes you through the streets of Berlin to discover some of the world’s best street art.

  • Tempelhof City Farm: Experience farm-life in the heart of Berlin, where you’ll find dozens of animals and plants.

Week-Long Itinerary for Tempelhof and Neukölln

Here is a suggested itinerary if you have a week to explore Tempelhof and Neukölln:

Day 1: Visit the Tempelhof Airport.

Day 2: Go on a street art tour.

Day 3: Head over to the Körnerpark and enjoy the gardens and outdoor amphitheater.

Day 4: Rent a bike and take the recommended bike route through Tempelhof and Neukölln.

Day 5: Visit the Canal Park.

Day 6: Visit the Hasenheide park and take a picnic.

Day 7: Explore the Bars and restaurants in Neukölln

Food and Drink Scene of Tempelhof and Neukölln

Must-Try Dishes

  • Currywurst: A traditional German sausage topped with a tomato-based sauce and curry powder.

  • Schnitzel: A breaded meat cutlet, usually pork, which is then fried.

Local Beverages

  • Beer: German beer has a long tradition. Berliner Pilsner and Berliner Kindl are the most famous beers in Berlin and quite popular in Tempelhof and Neukölln.

  • Club Mate: a caffeinated drink with herbal flavors

Where to Stay in Tempelhof and Neukölln: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Visitors to Tempelhof and Neukölln can find a variety of affordable and comfortable lodging options, including hotels, hostels, and guesthouses.

Hotels

  • Hotel Prens Berlin: A budget-friendly hotel in Neukölln with comfortable rooms and good reviews.

  • Hotel Columbia Berlin: Just a three-minute walk from the Tempelhof station.

What to Buy in Tempelhof and Neukölln: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

There are many local products and souvenirs to bring back home from Tempelhof and Neukölln that you won’t find anywhere else:

  • Berlin honey: Berlin beekeepers produce not only regular honey but also linden honey.

  • Craft Beer: There are a few breweries in Neukölln that produce craft beer such as Heidenpeters, Berliner Berg, and Schoppe Bräu.

Local Culture and Customs of Tempelhof and Neukölln

The people of Berlin usually greet each other with a handshake or a hug. While walking on the streets, make sure don’t click your fingers or whistle on the public transport.

Best Time to Visit Tempelhof and Neukölln

The best time to come to Tempelhof and Neukölln is during the summer season, which runs from June through August. During this time, there are many outdoor activities, events, and festivals, making it a great time to experience the area’s rich culture and natural beauty.

Do’s and Don’ts of Tempelhof and Neukölln

Do:

  • Embrace the local street art culture.

  • Try a craft beer.

Don’t:

  • Speak loudly in parks or other public spaces.

  • Ignore traffic signals when crossing the street.

Safety and Health

Safety in Tempelhof and Neukölln

Like any other major city, there are certain safety precautions visitors should take while exploring Tempelhof and Neukölln. It is always wise to stay in well-lit areas or travel in groups when exploring at night.

Health in Tempelhof and Neukölln

In general, the tap water in Berlin is safe to drink. Visitors can also find plenty of pharmacies and medical facilities throughout Tempelhof and Neukölln in case of an emergency.

Getting to Tempelhof and Neukölln: Transportation Options and Logistics

Tempelhof and Neukölln are easily accessible by several means of transportation.

Airports Near Tempelhof and Neukölln

Berlin Schönefeld Airport and Berlin Tegel Airport are the nearest airports to Tempelhof and Neukölln. From there, visitors can take public transportation or taxis to reach their destination.

Bus Stations Near Tempelhof and Neukölln

ZOB is the central bus station in Berlin and has connections to major cities in Europe.

Other Options

Visitors to Berlin can also travel via train, with the Berlin Hauptbahnhof train station located just a few kilometers away.

How to Get Around in Tempelhof and Neukölln

Some of the best ways to explore Tempelhof and Neukölln include biking, walking, or taking public transportation. The city’s public transportation system is well-organized and affordable, with buses and trains running regularly throughout the day.

Where to Go Next from Tempelhof and Neukölln: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

With Berlin being a city rich in history and culture, there are many nearby destinations and excursions that visitors can take from Tempelhof and Neukölln. Some of the most popular destinations include:

  • Kreuzberg: One of the most colorful districts in Berlin, known for its lively street culture and diversity.

  • Potsdam: Located about 30 minutes away from Berlin, this city is home to several royal palaces and beautiful parks.

Conclusion

Whether you’re interested in history, art, or just looking for an unforgettable outdoor adventure, Tempelhof and Neukölln have something to offer every visitor. From lush parks to museums and historical landmarks, the districts are the perfect destination for travelers looking to experience the best of Berlin.
