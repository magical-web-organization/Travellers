Introduction to East

East Berlin serves as a historical site for Germany as it marks a generation of divided Berlin. This vibrant district is located in the eastern part of Berlin, Germany. East is well-known for its contrasting architecture and graffiti-covered walls that tell tales of yesterdays. This district is also known for its bustling nightlife, cultural scene, and culinary delights.

Discovering East will be a journey of nostalgia that will take you back in time. You will see the remnants of the past and how East Berlin has transformed into the vibrant district that it is today. Ready to explore? Here’s your guide to discovering East Berlin.

List of Places to Visit in East

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

East is a district steeped in history and culture. The following museums and landmarks are must-sees if you’re interested in learning more about the history of East Berlin.

  1. DDR Museum: A museum dedicated to showcasing everyday life in East Germany.

  2. Stasi Museum: A museum dedicated to the history of the Stasi, East Germany’s secret police.

  3. Berlin Wall Memorial: A memorial to the Berlin Wall that once divided East and West Germany.

  4. East Side Gallery: A section of the Berlin Wall that is covered in murals and street art.

  5. Alexanderplatz: A central square with historic landmarks like the Berlin TV Tower.

Natural Attractions of East: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

East is not only rich in history and culture, but also in natural beauty. Here are some natural attractions that you can explore in East Berlin:

  1. Treptower Park: A large public park with plenty of walking paths, gardens, and a riverfront view.

  2. Volkspark Friedrichshain: Berlin’s oldest public park, filled with gardens, waterfalls, and grassy picnic areas.

  3. Berlin Zoo: The oldest and most famous zoo in Germany, home to over 20,000 animals.

  4. Tierpark Berlin: One of the largest zoos in Europe with over 8,000 animals representing 800 species.

  5. Müggelsee: The largest lake in Berlin with beaches, boat rentals, and hiking trails.

Hiking/Biking Routes of East

If you love hiking or biking, then East is the perfect place for you. Here are some of the most popular routes to choose from:

  1. Berlin Wall Trail: A 160km trail that traces the route of the Berlin Wall.

  2. Wuhletal Trail: A 12km hike through the forest that follows a stream.

  3. Tempelhofer Feld: A former airport turned public park where you can bike or skate on the runway.

  4. Rummelsburger Bucht: A popular trail for hikers and bikers that follows the Spree River.

  5. Mauerradweg: Another trail that follows the route of the Berlin Wall, but this one is specifically for bikers.

Best Things to Do in East: Activities and Tours

East is a district full of adventures. Here are some of the best things to do:

  1. Street Art Tour: East is known for its street art. Join a street art tour to learn about the history of the area and see some of the best murals.

  2. Trabi Safari: Drive around East Berlin in a Trabant, a car that was used in East Germany.

  3. Walking Tour of East: Take a guided walking tour of East Berlin to learn more about its history and culture.

  4. Nightlife: East Berlin is known for its bustling nightlife. Check out some of the local bars and dance clubs.

  5. Try Escape Rooms: There are plenty of escape rooms in East Berlin to put your skills to the test.

Week-Long Itinerary for East

Here’s a sample itinerary for a week-long trip to East Berlin:

Day 1: Explore Alexanderplatz and the surrounding area.

Day 2: Take a street art tour and visit the East Side Gallery.

Day 3: Hike or bike the Berlin Wall Trail.

Day 4: Visit the DDR Museum and learn about everyday life in East Germany.

Day 5: Spend the day at Berlin Zoo.

Day 6: Take a walking tour of East Berlin.

Day 7: Relax at Treptower Park.

Food and Drink Scene of East

East Berlin’s food and drink scene is as vibrant as its culture. Here’s what you need to try:

Must-Try Dishes

  1. Currywurst: A popular German fast food dish made of sausage, ketchup, and curry powder.

  2. Döner kebab: A Turkish dish that consists of meat cooked on a spit, served in a sandwich.

  3. Boulette: A meatball dish that’s a Berlin specialty.

  4. Schnitzel: Breaded and fried meat that’s popular in Germany.

  5. Apfelstrudel: A traditional German dessert made of apples and pastry.

Local Beverages

  1. Berliner Weisse: A sour beer that’s a Berlin specialty.

  2. Club-Mate: A popular German soft drink that has a high caffeine content.

  3. Berliner Kindl: A lager beer that’s widely available in East Berlin.

  4. Berliner Luft: A mint-flavored schnapps that’s popular in Berlin.

  5. German wines: Germany is known for producing excellent white wines, so give them a try!

Where to Stay in East: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

There are plenty of accommodations to choose from in East Berlin. Here are some recommendations:

  1. Michelberger Hotel: Located in the heart of East Berlin. A boutique hotel with unique rooms and an on-site restaurant.

  2. PLUS Berlin: A modern hostel with private rooms and a swimming pool.

  3. Almodóvar Hotel Berlin: A sustainable hotel with a rooftop terrace that offers views of the city.

  4. EastSeven Berlin Hostel: A budget-friendly hostel with private rooms and a communal kitchen.

  5. nhow Berlin: A music-themed hotel that’s located near the river, with a rooftop bar and restaurant.

What to Buy in East: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Want to bring home some souvenirs from East Berlin? Here’s what to buy:

  1. Ampelmann products: A popular East German Ampelmann symbol found on traffic lights.

  2. Berlin Wall souvenirs: Look for sections of the wall that have turned into souvenirs.

  3. German beer steins: Pick up a traditional German stein as a souvenir.

  4. Local fashion: East Berlin has plenty of local fashion boutiques worth checking out.

  5. Botanical soap: Berlin has plenty of natural soap shops. Give it a try!

Local Culture and Customs of East

Here are some things to know before you go:

  • Dress code: Dress casually. East Berlin is a bohemian district, so it’s all about comfort and style.

  • Tipping: Tipping in restaurants and cafes is appreciated but not required. A good rule of thumb is to round up the bill to the nearest euro.

  • Greetings: Greet someone with a handshake and say “Guten Morgen” (good morning), “Guten Tag” (hello), or “Guten Abend” (good evening).

  • Language: German is the official language, but most people speak English.

Best Time to Visit East

The best time to visit East Berlin is during the summer months from June to August when the weather is warm and sunny. The temperatures during these months range from 20-25°C, making it perfect for outdoor activities. If you want to avoid the crowds, consider visiting during the shoulder seasons (April-May and September-October).

Do’s and Don’ts of East

Do:

  • Do be open to new experiences.

  • Do take advantage of the street food scene.

  • Do learn some basic phrases in German.

  • Do be respectful of the local culture.

  • Do take a walking tour of East Berlin.

Don’t:

  • Don’t assume that everyone speaks English.

  • Don’t forget to carry cash with you.

  • Don’t litter.

  • Don’t wear High Heels on cobbled stones.

  • Don’t forget to smile!

Safety and Health

Safety in East

East Berlin is generally a safe district with low crime rates. However, it’s always better to be cautious and aware of your surroundings, especially at night. Pickpocketing in tourist areas can also be a problem, so keep your valuables safe.

Health in East

Germany has a good healthcare system, and East Berlin is no exception. However, it’s always a good idea to have travel insurance in case of unexpected emergencies.

Getting to East: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near East

The closest airport to East Berlin is Berlin Schönefeld Airport (SXF). It’s about a 30-minute drive from East Berlin.

Bus Stations Near East

The main bus station for long-distance buses is the ZOB Berlin. From there, you can take a metro to East Berlin.

Other Options

The easiest way to get around in Berlin is by public transportation. The U-Bahn (underground) and S-Bahn (suburban train) are efficient options. Rental bikes are also available, or you can take a taxi or Uber.

How to Get Around in East

The best way to get around in East Berlin is by foot, bike, or public transportation. The U-Bahn and S-Bahn networks run throughout the district, and biking or walking is a great way to see the city.

Where to Go Next from East: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

East Berlin is perfectly located for exploring more of Germany and Europe. Here are some nearby destinations and excursions:

  • Potsdam: The capital city of the state of Brandenburg and home to many palaces and gardens.

  • Dresden: The capital city of the state of Saxony, known for its baroque architecture.

  • Prague: The capital city of the Czech Republic, with a rich history and beautiful architecture.

  • Leipzig: The largest city in the state of Saxony, known for its classical music scene.

  • Hamburg: The second-largest city in Germany and one of the country’s most important ports.

Conclusion

East Berlin is a district that’s rich in history, culture, and natural beauty. From museums and landmarks to outdoor adventures and delicious food, there’s something for everyone in East. Plan your trip using this comprehensive travel guide and get ready to explore a unique part of Germany!
