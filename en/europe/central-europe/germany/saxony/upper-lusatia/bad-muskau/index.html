Introduction to Bad Muskau

Bad Muskau, located in Upper Lusatia, is a small charming town in the Saxony region of Germany. The town lies on the banks of the Neisse River, which forms a natural border with Poland. Bad Muskau is a well-known spa town, popular for its stunning natural beauty, picturesque parklands, and historical monuments and buildings. The town is a perfect destination for nature lovers, history enthusiasts and cultural explorers.

Week-Long Itinerary for Bad Muskau

If you’re planning to spend a week in Bad Muskau, here’s a sample itinerary for you:

  • Day 1: Explore Muskau Park and take a boat tour on the Neisse River
  • Day 2: Visit the Wendish Museum and the Bad Muskau Castle
  • Day 3: Take a day trip to Görlitz (50km, 1 hr by car)
  • Day 4: Cycle on the Neisse Cycle Route and head to the Muskau Arch Bridge
  • Day 5: Visit the Rhododendron Park and go hiking in Muskau Forest
  • Day 6: Take a day trip to Cottbus (90km, 1 hr 20 min by car)
  • Day 7: Visit the German Watch and Clock Museum and enjoy some leisure time by the river.
