Introduction to Görlitz

Görlitz, located in Upper Lusatia, Saxony, Germany, is a hidden gem for travelers looking to venture off the beaten path. With a long history dating back to the 10th century, Görlitz is a picturesque town filled with Renaissance and Gothic architecture.

Known for being a popular filming location for movies such as “The Grand Budapest Hotel” and “Inglorious Basterds,” Görlitz charms visitors with its stunning landmarks, historical attractions, scenic views and vibrant outdoor activities. Travelers wishing to avoid the crowds and get off the beaten path should definitely consider Görlitz for their next European adventure.

List of Places to Visit in Görlitz

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Silesian Museum in Görlitz: A comprehensive museum dedicated to the rich history of Silesia and its people.
  • Görlitz City Museum: Exhibits the history of the town, its people, and the surrounding region of Upper Lusatia.
  • Zgorzelec Museum: Located in the neighboring Polish town, Zgorzelec Museum boasts an impressive collection of artifacts from ancient times to present day.
  • The Görlitz Synagogue: One of the few synagogues in Europe to survive the Holocaust, now serves as a memorial and cultural center.
  • The Görlitz Town Hall: Recognized for its stunning late Gothic architecture and one of the most iconic landmarks in the city.

Natural Attractions of Görlitz: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Berzdorfer See: A nearby lake that offers various water sports, such as swimming, windsurfing, and sailing, as well as hiking and cycling trails influenced by Europe’s Green Belt.
  • Schlesischer Gebirgsverein e.V. (SGV): This popular hiking club provides various tours throughout the year and a chance to discover Görlitz’s scenic countryside, such as the trails at Landskron Castle or the Three Regions Trail.
  • The Görlitz Park: A charming park in the city center, featuring beautiful flower beds and sculptures, is the perfect spot to relax and enjoy a picnic.
  • The Bismarck Tower: This tower stands about 29 meters high and offers the most impressive views of the city and surrounding regions. The tower base also has a restaurant for those who want a café break in Görlitz.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Görlitz

Görlitz has a vast network of hiking and cycling trails, all of which provide visitors an opportunity to explore the natural beauty of the place in more detail. Some of the best hiking trails include the Three Regions Trail, the Kunstlandschaft Loop, and the Kaiserkrone Trail.

Best Things to Do in Görlitz: Activities and Tours

  • The Görlitz University of Applied Sciences tour: Join the student-led tour of this university in Görlitz and find out why life here is the “best-kept secret in Germany.”
  • The Görlitz Night Watchman’s Tour: A fascinating tour that takes you back to medieval times and into the fascinating world of the town’s elaborate history.
  • The Görlitz Children’s Museum: This museum is tailored for children and offers various interactive exhibits, aimed at engaging young visitors.

Week-Long Itinerary for Görlitz

Day 1: Start your trip by exploring the beautiful landmarks, including the Görlitz Town Hall, the Görlitz Synagogue, and the Reichenbach Tower. Take in the views of the town from the Bismarck Tower in the evening.

Day 2: Take a guided tour of the Görlitz Night Watchman, venture into the narrow alleys and learn about Görlitz’s fascinating history and myths.

Day 3: Visit nearby attractions, such as Schöpstal bike and hiking trail and the culture-rich town of Bischofswerda in the Oberlausitz Nature Reserve.

Day 4: Visit the Silesian Museum and learn about the intriguing history, culture, and traditions of Silesia. Take a stroll around the museum’s tranquil courtyard gardens.

Day 5: Spend the day outdoors at Berzdorfer See. Swim, boat, or just relax at the Sandy Beach, or explore the extensive trails around the lake.

Day 6: Visit the pretty Polish town of Zgorzelec just across the Görlitz Bridge. Explore Zgorzelec Museum and discover pieces of history from antiquity down to modern-day Poland.

Day 7: Spend the last day wandering around the beautiful Görlitz Park, filled with flower beds, fountains, and tranquil ponds.

Food and Drink Scene of Görlitz

Must-Try Dishes

  • Krautwickler: Cabbage leaves stuffed with minced meat and rice, served with potatoes or bread.
  • Silesian duck: Sephardic cherry pulp with dumplings and red cabbage.
  • Silesian pike-perch: A delicious fish dish in a creamy sauce made from regional sour cream. Served with boiled potatoes and carrots.

Local Beverages

  • Wise Blood: A smoked beer offered by the local brewery Hufeisen Brewery
  • Überdrüssig: A traditional wheat ale
  • Blechbüchse Ac-Cider: A refreshing local cider made from locally-sourced apples.

Where to Stay in Görlitz: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Görlitz has a range of excellent accommodation options that cater to all types of travelers. The Grand City Hotel and the Hotel Schwibbogen are fantastic options for those in search of luxury, while the Gästehaus Lisakowski and the Hostel 100 Zimmer are perfect for budget travelers. The city also has a range of guesthouses that offer an authentic local experience, such as the Alte Apotheke and Pension Hartmann.

What to Buy in Görlitz: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Görlitz is home to local artisans creating unique handmade crafts, such as ceramics, candles, and textiles. Visitors can also find authentic local food and drink products, like whiskey, beer, and cheese. The city’s flagship product is the handcrafted “Reichenbach Feinbrand,” a locally produced grain liquor that is famous in the region.

Local Culture and Customs of Görlitz

Görlitz is known for its rich cultural heritage and traditions. One of the most prominent events is the Görlitz Schaffer Festival, which takes place every five years and is dedicated to the town’s famous wood carvers. Other annual events include the Görlitzer Tippelmarkt, Görlitz Old Town Festival and Jazz Festival Görlitz.

Best Time to Visit Görlitz

The best time to visit Görlitz is between April and September when the days are longer, and the weather is warm. July and August are peak tourist seasons, while shoulder seasons in April and September are perfect for avoiding extreme crowds.

Do’s and Don’ts of Görlitz

Do:

  • Take the Görlitz Night Watchman’s Tour for a glimpse into the town’s intriguing history.
  • Respect the local culture and customs, especially when entering historical sites and places of worship.
  • Try the famous “Reichenbach Feinbrand,” a speciality drink made exclusively in Görlitz.

Don’t:

  • Litter in public spaces or historical sites.
  • Ignore the local road signs or regulations when driving around.
  • Take photographs of other people without asking for permission.

Safety and Health

Safety in Görlitz

Görlitz is generally a safe place to visit, with a low crime rate. However, like any destination, visitors should take precautions, such as keeping valuables out of sight and avoid carrying large amounts of cash. Travelers to Görlitz should also be vigilant around public transport areas and busy tourist sites, like busy markets and festivals.

Health in Görlitz

Görlitz offers excellent health care facilities, including medical centers and hospitals. Visitors to the city should ensure they have adequate travel insurance that covers any medical costs. It is also advisable to carry any prescription medications required in the original packaging and carry a copy of the prescription to avoid any issues at customs.

Getting to Görlitz: Transportation Options and Logistics

Görlitz is a popular stop for those traveling around Central Europe. The town is well-served by road, rail, and air links.

Airports Near Görlitz

The closest airport to Görlitz is Dresden International Airport, which is only about 100 kilometers from the town center. The city also has international connections to airports in Berlin and Prague.

Bus Stations Near Görlitz

The regional bus station is located near the Görlitz train station, serving local towns and cities in the region. It is also possible to take a long-distance bus from Berlin or other large cities.

Other Options

Some travelers prefer to rent a car, which can be a good option, given the town’s location in the countryside.

How to Get Around in Görlitz

Görlitz city center is easy walking distance to walk on foot, but travelers can also use buses or bikes to explore the nearby town. The town is also easy to navigate by car, with plenty of public parking options available.

Where to Go Next from Görlitz: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Dresden is a must-visit destination for those who enjoy Baroque architecture, museums and charming cobbled streets.
  • Leipzig is also known for its historical landmarks, art, and lively nightlife.
  • The historic city of Krakow in Poland is within driving distance and offers a wealth of cultural attractions and a fascinating history.

Conclusion

Görlitz is a hidden gem of Central Europe that offers visitors a wealth of exciting history, culture, and natural surroundings.
