Introduction to Zittau

Located in Upper Lusatia in Saxony, Germany, Zittau is an ancient town known for its cultural heritage, outdoor adventures, and scenic views. It is situated at the foot of the 800m-high Lausche peak and shares borders with the Czech Republic and Poland. Zittau’s rich history and architecture date back to the Middle Ages and the Renaissance period, making it an excellent place for history buffs and architecture enthusiasts. Visitors can explore the town on foot or by bike, taking in the stunning sights and sounds of this picturesque place. In this comprehensive guide, we will explore the must-visit attractions, outdoor activities, food and drink scene, accommodation options, culture, do’s and don’ts, safety tips, transportation options, and nearby destinations.

List of Places to Visit in Zittau

There are many interesting places to visit in Zittau including:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  1. Zittau Church of the Holy Trinity – A Gothic style church completed in 1497 and still in use as a place of worship today.

  2. Zittau Town Hall – A beautiful Gothic style building that has been standing since 1845 and houses the town council and administrative offices.

  3. Zittau Castle – A medieval castle dating back to the 15th century that has been converted to a museum housing various exhibitions about regional architecture and art.

Natural Attractions of Zittau: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  1. Lausche Mountain – The highest peak in the Zittau Mountains located in the tri-border region of Poland, Germany, and the Czech Republic offering scenic views and popular hiking trails.

  2. Olbersdorfer See – A scenic lake perfect for swimming, hiking, and boating.

  3. Schrammsteine – A picturesque sandstone formation on the Czech border known for its outstanding natural beauty.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Zittau

Zittau offers many scenic hiking and biking trails for all levels of experience. Here are some of the best routes to explore the natural beauty of the region:

  1. Mandau Cycle Path – A beautiful 15km cycle path that takes you past some of the most picturesque spots in Zittau.

  2. Lausche – A challenging hike that takes you up the Lausche mountain and rewards you with panoramic views of the surrounding landscape.

  3. Hochwald Panorama Trail – A beautiful hiking trail that takes you from the Hochwald lookout tower to the Olbersdorfer See lake through dense forests and picturesque meadows.

Best Things to Do in Zittau: Activities and Tours

Zittau offers a wide range of activities and tours. Here are some of the best:

  1. Zittauer Gustav – A steam train that takes you through the beautiful Zittau Mountains.

  2. Zoo Zittau – A modern zoo with different types of animals, including giraffes, monkeys, lions, and many more.

  3. Theater Zittau – A town theater that hosts a variety of cultural events, such as opera, theater performances, and concerts.

Week-Long Itinerary for Zittau

Here’s an ideal itinerary to explore Zittau in a week:

Day Activities
Day 1 Explore the town center, including the Town Hall and Church of the Holy Trinity
Day 2 Hike up to Lausche mountain for breathtaking views
Day 3 Take a steam train ride on the Zittauer Gustav
Day 4 Enjoy a day of swimming and boating at Olbersdorfer See
Day 5 Go on a guided tour at Zittau Castle and regional museum
Day 6 Visit the nearby town of Görlitz and explore its historic buildings
Day 7 Relax and enjoy the local cuisine and drinks.

Food and Drink Scene of Zittau

Zittau is home to a diverse food and drink scene that is influenced by the surrounding regions. Some must-try dishes and local beverages include:

Must-Try Dishes

  1. Silesian dumplings – A traditional dish made with potato dough and stuffed with meat or vegetables.

  2. Lusatian fish dishes – Trout and carp dishes served with local herbs and spices.

  3. Saxon-style potato dishes – Different potato dishes such as potato salad, potato dumplings, and potato pancakes.

Local Beverages

  1. Saxon wine – The wine produced in Zittau and its neighboring regions is quite popular and delicious.

  2. Beer – Different types of beer such as Pilsner, Helles, and Schwarzbier produced locally.

Where to Stay in Zittau: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Zittau offers various accommodation options for every budget and preference:

  1. Hotel BB – Located near the city center, a 3-star hotel with cozy rooms, good amenities, and friendly service.

  2. Pension Johanna – A family-run guesthouse located near the Olbersdorfer See lake, offering comfortable rooms and hearty breakfast.

  3. Jugendherberge Zittau – A modern hostel with clean rooms, a good location, and many different activities provided for guests.

Local Culture and Customs of Zittau

Zittau is known for its rich cultural heritage and customs. The town celebrates various festivals throughout the year, such as the “Zittau Fastnacht” traditional carnival in February, and Christmas markets in December. Visitors are encouraged to respect local customs and traditions.

Best Time to Visit Zittau

The best time to visit Zittau is in the summer months between June and August when the weather is mild, and the landscapes are in full bloom. However, winter is also an excellent time to visit Zittau as it offers a unique experience with its traditional Christmas markets and winter sports activities.

Do’s and Don’ts of Zittau

Here are some of the things to do and not do in Zittau:

Do:

  1. Respect local customs and traditions.

  2. Try the local cuisine and drinks.

  3. Engage in outdoor activities like hiking, biking, and swimming.

Don’t:

  1. Litter or damage the natural surroundings.

  2. Disrespect the local culture or traditions.

  3. Visit or share pictures of war memorials in the nearby regions.

Safety and Health

Safety in Zittau

Zittau is a relatively safe town with low crime rates. However, visitors are advised to take standard safety precautions, such as keeping an eye on your belongings in crowded areas, locking your accommodation, and avoiding walking alone at night.

Health in Zittau

Zittau has good health facilities for both locals and visitors. In case of a medical emergency, call the emergency number 112.

Getting to Zittau: Transportation Options and Logistics

Zittau is well connected to various European cities via different modes of transport:

Airports Near Zittau

  1. Dresden Airport – The nearest airport to Zittau, located about 100km away.
  2. Wrocław Airport – A popular airport located in Poland and about 150km away from Zittau.

Bus Stations Near Zittau

  1. Zittau Bus Station – Located in the city center, offers regional and national bus services.

Other Options

  1. Train – The Zittau train station is well connected, with trains running to major cities such as Dresden, Prague, and Berlin.

  2. Car – Zittau has good road connections to major cities in Germany and neighboring countries.

How to Get Around in Zittau

Zittau is a relatively small town that is easy to navigate by foot, bike, or public transport. Visitors can rent bikes or take the local buses.

Where to Go Next from Zittau: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Zittau makes an excellent base for exploring the surrounding regions:

  1. Gorlitz – A stunning medieval town situated on the Polish border with beautiful architecture and a rich cultural heritage.
  2. Liberec – A Czech city known for its historic castles, glass art, and winter sports activities.

Conclusion

Zittau offers visitors a unique blend of cultural heritage, natural beauty, and outdoor adventures that make it an excellent destination for travelers of all ages and interests.
