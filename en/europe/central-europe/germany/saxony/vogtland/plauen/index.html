Plauen, a city nestled in the heart of Saxony’s Vogtland region, is a hidden gem waiting to be explored. With a rich history dating back to the Middle Ages, this small city boasts a wealth of cultural attractions, natural beauty, and outdoor activities to suit any interest. Introduction to Plauen

Plauen is a small city located in the western part of Saxony, Germany. Its history dates back to the Middle Ages, and it has long been an important center for trade and commerce in the region. Today, Plauen is known for its rich cultural heritage, stunning natural beauty, and warm hospitality. It is a popular destination for tourists interested in learning more about the history and culture of eastern Germany, as well as for outdoor enthusiasts seeking to explore the region’s many hiking and biking routes.

List of Places to Visit in Plauen

A visit to Plauen offers a wealth of things to see and do. Whether you’re interested in museums and historical sites, or prefer to immerse yourself in the natural beauty of the region, Plauen has something for everyone.

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Plauen is home to a number of museums and historical sites that offer visitors a glimpse into the city’s rich cultural heritage. Highlights include:

  • The Plauen lace museum, which offers a fascinating look at the city’s long history of lace-making.
  • The Vogtland Museum, which features exhibits on the region’s history and culture, including a collection of locally-made musical instruments.
  • The Alte Elsterbrücke, a historic bridge that spans the River Elster and offers stunning views of the city and surrounding countryside.

Natural Attractions of Plauen: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

If you’re a nature lover, Plauen has plenty to offer. From scenic hiking and biking routes to tranquil parks and gardens, visitors can enjoy a wide range of outdoor activities and experiences. Here are a few highlights:

  • The Vogtland Arena, a world-class ski jumping facility that hosts international competitions.
  • The Park der Generationen, a beautiful park with walking paths, gardens, and a small lake.
  • The Göltzschtalbrücke, the largest brick bridge in the world and a stunning feat of engineering.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Plauen

For outdoor enthusiasts, Plauen is a paradise of hiking and biking routes. There’s something for every level of experience, from gentle walks through nearby parks to challenging mountain hikes. Here are a few routes to consider:

  • The Vogtland Panorama Weg, a 225-kilometer hiking trail that winds through the hills and valleys of the region.
  • The Elstertal-Weg, a scenic hiking route that follows the River Elster and offers stunning views of the surrounding countryside.
  • The Plauener Seenland, a network of cycling and hiking routes that leads through forests, meadows, and along the shores of beautiful lakes.

Best Things to Do in Plauen: Activities and Tours

Plauen offers a wide range of activities and tours for visitors. From cultural experiences to outdoor adventures, you’ll find something to suit your interests. Here are a few highlights:

  • The Plauener Spitze Lace Festival, a celebration of the city’s rich lace-making heritage, held annually in September.
  • The Vogtlandbahn, a scenic train ride that takes visitors through the beautiful landscapes of the Vogtland region.
  • The Plauen City Tour, a guided walking tour that takes visitors through the city’s most important sights and landmarks.

Week-Long Itinerary for Plauen

For visitors looking to spend a week in Plauen, there’s plenty to see and do. Here’s a sample itinerary that takes in some of the city’s most important attractions:

Day 1: Visit the Vogtland Museum and explore the Historic Town Center.

Day 2: Hike the Vogtland Panorama Weg or cycle the Plauener Seenland routes.

Day 3: Visit the Plauen lace museum and take a guided walking tour of the Old City.

Day 4: Explore the Park der Generationen and enjoy a picnic lunch.

Day 5: Take a scenic train ride on the Vogtlandbahn and visit the Göltzschtalbrücke.

Day 6: Ski or snowboard at the Vogtland Arena or hike the Elstertal-Weg.

Day 7: Relax on your final day and enjoy the local food and drink scene before departing.

Food and Drink Scene of Plauen

Plauen boasts a wide variety of traditional dishes and locally-produced beverages that are sure to delight any visitor. Here are a few highlights:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Plauener Döner, a local twist on the classic kebab sandwich made with locally-sourced ingredients.
  • Vogtlandische Rindfleischpfanne, a hearty beef stew with potatoes, carrots, and other vegetables.
  • Stollen, a traditional German holiday fruit cake that is enjoyed year-round in Plauen.

Local Beverages

  • Vogtlandisches Bier, a locally-brewed beer that is a favorite among visitors and locals alike.
  • Vogtlandischer Wein, a local wine produced from grapes grown in the vineyards around Plauen.

What to Buy in Plauen: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Plauen is known for its lace-making, and visitors can purchase a wide variety of handmade lace products to bring home as souvenirs. Other local products worth checking out include locally-produced wines, beers, and spirits, as well as handmade arts and crafts.

Local Culture and Customs of Plauen

The people of Plauen are proud of their local heritage and culture, and visitors should respect local customs and traditions. Some things to keep in mind include being respectful of the local environment and avoiding loud or disruptive behavior in public places.

Best Time to Visit Plauen

The best time to visit Plauen is during the summer months, from June to August, when the weather is warm and sunny, and outdoor activities like hiking and cycling are at their best. However, visitors can also enjoy the city during the winter months, when snow sports and Christmas markets are in full swing.

Getting to Plauen: Transportation Options and Logistics

Plauen can be easily accessed by a variety of transportation options, including:

Airports Near Plauen

The nearest airport to Plauen is Leipzig/Halle airport, which is approximately a two-hour drive away. Other airports within reasonable distance include Dresden and Nuremberg.

Bus Stations Near Plauen

Plauen can be reached by bus from many nearby cities, including Dresden, Leipzig, and Munich.

Other Options

Visitors can also access Plauen by train, with regular services available from Leipzig, Dresden, and Munich.
