Introduction to Neunkirchen

Neunkirchen is a small town in Saarland, located in southwestern Germany. The town is known for its beautiful landscape, charming architecture, and rich history. Neunkirchen was founded in the 15th century and has since then served as an important industrial center. Visitors to Neunkirchen can enjoy exploring the town’s many historical sites, hiking trails, and local cuisine.

List of Places to Visit in Neunkirchen

Neunkirchen has several interesting places to visit. Here’s a list:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • The Saarland Mining Museum: The museum showcases the history and economic impact of mining in the region. Visitors can see various mining exhibits, machinery, and equipment used during mining operations.

  • The Stummplatz: Also known as the ‘Iron City’, this unique attraction is a historical industrial complex used for iron production. It has been turned into a museum, showcasing the history of the town’s steel industry.

  • The Parish Church of Neunkirchen: The church is of significant importance to the town’s religious and cultural history. It is a beautiful spot to admire the architecture and stained-glass windows.

Natural Attractions of Neunkirchen: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Hoxberg Mountain: The Hoxberg is the highest mountain peak in Neunkirchen. The hill offers a panoramic view of the town and its surroundings.

  • Neunkircher Zoo: Home to over 1,200 animals, the zoo allows visitors to take a stroll amidst the natural beauty of the area while they interact with the animals.

  • Knackeboul: A popular hiking trail that traverses through the town and offers stunning views of the landscape.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Neunkirchen

Neunkirchen has some fantastic hiking and biking routes that are easy to navigate. Here are a few popular ones:

  • Panoramaweg: This trail runs along the edge of the Hoxberg Mountain, offering scenic views of Neunkirchen and the surrounding countryside.

  • Saarland’s River Route: This route extends from Saarbrücken to Trier and is ideal for bikers and hikers looking to explore the region’s natural beauty and river views.

  • The Knackeboul Trail: This trail runs through the countryside, passing by historic sites, including old mills and factories.

Best Things to Do in Neunkirchen: Activities and Tours

In addition to hiking and exploring the town’s historical landmarks, there are other interesting activities to engage in while in Neunkirchen:

  • Attend a Music Concert: The town is famous for its open-air concerts held annually at the Stummplatz, which attracts many renowned artists.

  • Zip Lining at Westpfalz Park: Westpfalz Park offers unique outdoor experiences, including a 1300m zipline which offers a bird’s eye view of the area.

  • Swimming at Schaumberg: This swimming pool located in a prominent park offers a peaceful day of relaxation.

Week-Long Itinerary for Neunkirchen

Here’s a proposed itinerary for a week-long stay in Neunkirchen:

  • Day 1: Visit the local museum to learn about the town’s history and mining industry. Spend the afternoon hiking along the Panoramaweg trail.

  • Day 2: Take a guided tour of the Stummplatz and enjoy lunch at one of the local cafes. In the afternoon, visit the parish church of Neunkirchen and admire its architecture.

  • Day 3: Visit the Westpfalz Park for a day of adventure, including zip-lining and hiking.

  • Day 4: Take a walk through the countryside and explore the historic mills and factories.

  • Day 5: Spend the day at the Neunkircher Zoo, interacting with the animals and learning about the local fauna.

  • Day 6: Take a scenic bike ride through the Saarland’s River Route and enjoy its views.

  • Day 7: Visit the Hoxberg for a panoramic view of Neunkirchen and the surrounding area.

Food and Drink Scene of Neunkirchen

Must-Try Dishes

  • Maultaschen: A dish made from flour, eggs, and herbs filled with minced meat, spinach and served in broth or soup.

  • Sauerbraten: A traditional German dish made from marinated beef with vinegar, onions, and spices cooked slowly on low heat.

  • Schwenker: A popular dish consisting of marinated pork cooked extensively over an open flame.

Local Beverages

  • Beer: Germany is known for its beer, and the town has a generous range of beer varieties to choose from.

  • Wine: The town is part of the Mosel wine region which is globally famous for its white wines.

Where to Stay in Neunkirchen: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Neunkirchen offers a range of accommodation options for visitors to choose from. Here are a few recommendations:

  • Holiday Inn Express: A modern and comfortable hotel situated in the town center.

  • Hotel Am Markt: A hotel with a homely ambiance located near the Stumplatz.

  • Gastehaus Walleck: A budget-friendly guesthouse that offers clean and spacious rooms.

What to Buy in Neunkirchen: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Knifes and swords: Neunkirchen has a long history of knife-making, allowing tourists to purchase a souvenir of the town’s heritage.

  • Felt products: Locally made felt products, including hats, slippers, and bags, make unique souvenirs.

Local Culture and Customs of Neunkirchen

The region has a rich history and culture. Visitors should respect the local customs and observe the following:

  • Tipping: It is customary to leave a 10% tip at restaurants and cafes.

  • Greetings: It is polite to greet someone with a handshake.

Best Time to Visit Neunkirchen

The best time to visit Neunkirchen is during the summer months, between June and August. The weather is favorable for outdoor activities and exploring the natural scenery.

Do’s and Don’ts of Neunkirchen

Do:

  • Make sure to carry cash as many cafes, and small shops do not accept cards.

  • Learn a few basic phrases in German, including please and thank you to show respect.

Don’t:

  • Do not litter in the countryside and the national parks.

  • Do not drink alcohol in public places.

Safety and Health

Safety in Neunkirchen

Neunkirchen is a safe town to explore. Visitors should practice standard safety precautions, including being aware of their surroundings, especially during the late hours of the night.

Health in Neunkirchen

Germany has an excellent public health system, and tourists can confidently seek medical attention if needed. It is essential to carry relevant health insurance information when traveling to the country.

Getting to Neunkirchen: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Neunkirchen

The nearest international airport to Neunkirchen is Frankfurt Airport, which is approximately two hours away. Visitors can hire a taxi or take a train or bus from the airport to the town.

Bus Stations Near Neunkirchen

The town has a central bus station that operates regular bus services within town and to nearby cities and towns.

Other Options

Visitors can hire a car or take a train to travel to Neunkirchen.

How to Get Around in Neunkirchen

The town is small enough to explore on foot. Visitors can also rent a bike or take a bus to explore the area.

Where to Go Next from Neunkirchen: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Saarbrücken: Less than 20 km away from Neunkirchen, this is a vibrant city famous for its architectural beauty and culinary offerings.

  • Trier: Located approximately 80 km away from Neunkirchen, Trier is a historic city famous for its Roman buildings and sites.

Conclusion

Neunkirchen is a hidden gem in Southwestern Germany, offering visitors the opportunity to explore its rich culture, history, and leisure activities. From hiking to trying out the local cuisine, there is something for everyone to enjoy in this charming town. With this comprehensive guide, visitors can make the most of their trip to Neunkirchen.
