Introduction to Abov

Abov is a hidden gem on the eastern edge of Slovakia, nestled amidst the beautiful Beskids and Tatra Mountains. Here, visitors can enjoy a unique blend of rich cultural heritage, stunning natural scenery, and outdoor adventure opportunities. This comprehensive travel guide will take you through the top must-see attractions, activities, accommodations, food, and local culture in Abov.

List of Places to Visit in Abov

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Spišský hrad (Spiš Castle)

Undoubtedly one of the most dominant and picturesque castles in Slovakia, Spiš Castle is a UNESCO World Heritage site. Explore the fortress’s intricate medieval architecture, walk through its courtyards and dungeons, and enjoy picturesque views of the surrounding landscape.

Spišská kapitula (Spiš Chapter)

Located near Spiš Castle, Spiš Chapter is a quaint town with a rich ecclesiastic history. Here, visitors can explore its many churches and monasteries, including the impressive Spišská Kapitula Cathedral.

Medzev Salt Mine

A unique tourist attraction in Slovakia, the Medzev Salt Mine takes visitors deep beneath the earth’s surface to explore the ancient salt mines used by miners for centuries.

Natural Attractions of Abov: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Slovak Paradise National Park

Slovak Paradise National Park, located on the eastern edge of the country, is a breathtaking natural wonderland full of winding gorges, hiking trails, and waterfalls. The park is ideal for hiking, climbing, and cycling.

High Tatras

The Tatra Mountains, located in the heart of Europe, are a magnificent landscape of jagged peaks, green valleys, and crystal-clear lakes. Hiking, skiing, and outdoor adventure opportunities abound here.

Dobšinská Ice Cave

Dobšinská Ice Cave, one of the most spectacular natural wonders of Slovakia, is a cave system filled with impressive ice sculptures and winding tunnels.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Abov

Abov is a paradise for outdoor enthusiasts, and there are countless hiking and biking routes that allow visitors to explore the region’s stunning natural beauty. The most popular trails include:

  • The Tatranská Magistrála
  • Geravy - Betlanovce Hike
  • Litmanová - Horný Tisovník Hike

Best Things to Do in Abov: Activities and Tours

Skiing in the Tatras

The Tatras are Slovakia’s premier skiing destination, with dozens of ski resorts and an extensive network of trails for cross-country skiing too.

Gothic Route

Explore the Gothic jewels of Spiš! This route goes through the Spiš region and its surroundings and visits fascinating monuments of Gothic art, as well as natural wonders.

Hungarian Wine Tasting

Discover the world-renowned wines of nearby Tokaj and discover the rich flavors of Hungary’s wine tradition, which originated here.

Food and Drink Scene of Abov

Must-Try Dishes

Bryndzové halušky

The national dish of Slovakia, Bryndzové halušky, is a hearty bowl of potato dumplings covered in sheep cheese and bacon.

Kapustnica

Kapustnica is a traditional cabbage soup made with smoked pork, sausage, and lots of spices. It is commonly enjoyed during Christmas.

Local Beverages

Slivovica

Slivovica is a plum brandy that serves as a Slovakian rite of passage. It is commonly served as an aperitif before dinner or as a digestif after it.

Tokaj Wine

Tokaj wines are some of the most highly regarded in the world, and their distinct sweet taste is attributed to the “noble rot” that develops on the grapes as they hang on the vines.

What to Buy in Abov: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Slovakia is known for its traditional crafts, and Abov is a prime place to pick up some authentic souvenirs. Some of the popular items include:

  • Woodcarvings and wooden toys
  • Hand-woven fabrics and textiles
  • Pottery and ceramics

Local Culture and Customs of Abov

During your visit to Abov, it’s essential to respect the local culture and customs. Here are some things to keep in mind:

  • Be Quiet in the Cemeteries: In Slovakia, cemeteries are considered sacred places, and noises are frowned upon.
  • Be Punctual: Slovaks value punctuality and consider it a sign of respect.
  • Dress Appropriately: If you are visiting churches or other religious sites, it’s important to dress conservatively.

Best Time to Visit Abov

The best time to visit Abov is during the summer months of June to August, when the weather is warm, and the hiking trails and outdoor activities are at their best.

Do’s and Don’ts of Abov

Do:

  • Try the national dish, Bryndzové halušky.
  • Explore the incredible natural wonders of the Tatras National Park.
  • Be mindful of local customs and traditions.

Don’t:

  • Be loud or disrespectful in sacred places like cemeteries or churches.
  • Leave valuables unattended in crowded areas.

Safety and Health

Safety in Abov

Abov is generally a safe destination, with low levels of crime. Visitors should be cautious in crowded areas, however, and be wary of pickpockets.

Health in Abov

Slovakia’s healthcare system is highly rated, and visitors can obtain medical care at hospitals and clinics throughout the country. It’s always a good idea to purchase travel insurance before visiting to ensure you’re covered for any emergencies.

Getting to Abov: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Abov

The closest airport to Abov is Poprad–Tatry Airport, located approximately an hour’s drive away. Other nearby international airports include Krakow Airport in Poland (3 hours by car) and Vienna International Airport in Austria (4 hours by car).

Bus Stations Near Abov

Abov is well-connected by bus to other Slovakian cities and towns. The closest bus station is Spišská Nová Ves Bus Station, which is located at the heart of the town.

Other Options

Visitors can reach Abov by car, and the town is also well-connected to other Slovakian cities and towns by train. Additionally, there are numerous tour companies that offer guided tours of the region, including pick-up and drop-off in Abov.

How to Get Around in Abov

The best way to get around Abov is by car or bike, as public transport is limited. Taxis are available but can be expensive, particularly for longer journeys.

Where to Go Next from Abov: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

High Tatras

The High Tatras, Slovakia’s premier mountain range, is easily accessible by car or bus from Abov.

Košice

Located 90 minutes away by car, Košice is Slovakia’s second-largest city and home to a rich history full of museums, galleries, and impressive Gothic architecture.

Bardejov

A UNESCO World Heritage site, Bardejov is a beautifully-preserved medieval town located just over an hour’s drive away from Abov.

Conclusion

Abov is a hidden gem on the eastern edge of Slovakia, offering a unique blend of rich cultural heritage, breath-taking natural scenery, and outdoor adventure opportunities. Whether you’re looking to explore medieval towns and ancient castles, ski in the mountains, or taste some of Slovakia’s most delicious dishes, Abov is a destination that should not be missed.
