Introduction to Košice

Košice is a city located in Eastern Slovakia, in the region of Abov. It is the second-largest city of Slovakia, after Bratislava, and has a population of over 240,000 people. The city has a rich history and is known for its stunning architecture, vibrant culture, and beautiful natural surroundings.

List of Places to Visit in Košice

Here are some of the top places to visit when in Košice.

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Košice Old Town: Explore Košice’s historic center, which is the largest preserved town area in Slovakia. The Town Hall, St. Elisabeth’s Cathedral, and the Mikluš Prison are just some of the attractions you can see here.

  • East Slovak Museum: The museum has numerous permanent exhibitions on natural history, history, and ethnography and is a must-visit for history buffs.

  • Mikluš Prison: This Gothic-style prison is located in the heart of the Old Town and is a popular tourist attraction.

  • St. Elisabeth’s Cathedral: This 14th-century Gothic cathedral is a must-visit for its architecture and historic significance.

  • State Theatre Košice: Known for its stunning architecture, State Theatre Košice is one of the most important cultural centers in Slovakia.

Natural Attractions of Košice: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Zoo Košice: Located in the southeastern part of the city, Zoo Košice is a popular attraction for families. Home to over 100 species of animals, including wolves, bears, and tigers.

  • Košice Forest Park: Ideal for a day out with family and friends, Košice Forest Park is a paradise for nature lovers. Visitors can go hiking, biking, or take a stroll in the park’s peaceful surroundings.

  • Dobšinská Ice Cave: This UNESCO World Heritage Site is located less than an hour’s drive from Košice. The ice cave is open for tourists from May to September.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Košice

  • Route to Bukovec Dam: A popular hiking trail, this 14 km route is perfect for people who enjoy breathtaking views and exploring nature’s beauty.

  • Bike Trail Via Nicolaus: This 13 km trail is perfect for cycling enthusiasts and takes you through some of the city’s scenic spots.

  • Botanic Garden Trail: This is an easy trail that runs through the beautiful Botanic Garden in Košice. Visitors can learn about the various plant species found here.

Best Things to Do in Košice: Activities and Tours

  • City Tour: A guided walking tour of the city will give you an insight into Košice’s rich history and culture.

  • Craft Brewery Tour: Take a guided tour of one of Košice’s breweries and learn about the history of beer making in the region.

  • Adventure Tour: Go kayaking, bungee-jumping, or paragliding in the stunning natural surroundings of Košice.

Food and Drink Scene of Košice

Košice has a fantastic food and drink scene, especially when it comes to traditional Slovakian cuisine.

Must-Try Dishes

  • Bryndzové halušky: A popular dish made with potato dumplings and sheep cheese.

  • Kapustnica: A traditional Slovakian soup made with sauerkraut, potatoes, and meat.

  • Goulash: A meat stew served with bread or dumplings.

Local Beverages

  • Slovakian Beer: Slovakia has some excellent breweries, and beer lovers must try out some local brews when in Košice.

  • Kofola: A popular soda made in Slovakia and the Czech Republic.

Where to Stay in Košice: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Košice has a range of accommodation options that cater to all kinds of travelers. Here are some options:

  • Bristol Hotel: A luxurious hotel located in the heart of the city.

  • Pension Horse Inn: A traditional guesthouse located in the Old Town.

  • Hostel Šenk: An affordable hostel located in the city center.

What to Buy in Košice: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

If you’re looking for souvenirs to take home, here are some suggestions:

  • Painted Easter Eggs: These colorful eggs are hand-painted and make for a unique souvenir.

  • Ceramics: Slovakian ceramics are famous for their intricate designs and vibrant colors.

Local Culture and Customs of Košice

Slovakia has a rich culture, and Košice is no exception. Here’s what you need to know:

  • Clothing: Slovaks dress quite conservatively, and visitors are advised to dress modestly.

  • Language: Slovak is the official language, but many people speak English.

Best Time to Visit Košice

The best time to visit Košice is between May and September when the weather is pleasant, and outdoor activities are possible.

Do’s and Don’ts of Košice

Here are some Do’s and Don’ts to keep in mind when visiting Košice:

Do:

  • Respect Local Customs: Be respectful of local customs and traditions.

  • Explore the Outdoors: Košice has a lot of outdoor activities to offer, so make sure to get out and explore.

Don’t:

  • Be Disrespectful: Don’t be disrespectful towards locals or their customs.

  • Litter: Make sure to keep the streets clean and dispose of garbage responsibly.

Safety and Health

Košice is generally a safe city for tourists, but it’s always important to take precautions.

Safety in Košice

  • Petty Crime: Petty crime like pickpocketing can occur in crowded areas, so be vigilant.

Health in Košice

  • Vaccinations: Make sure to get vaccinated before traveling to Slovakia.

  • Medical Care: Medical care is available, but it’s always important to have travel insurance.

Getting to Košice: Transportation Options and Logistics

Košice is well connected to other parts of Slovakia as well as neighboring countries.

Airports Near Košice

  • Košice International Airport: Located about 6 miles from the city center, Košice International Airport is the major airport serving Košice.

Bus Stations Near Košice

  • Košice Bus Station: Located in the city center, the Košice Bus Station is the major bus terminal serving the city.

Other Options

  • Train: There are daily trains from other Slovakian cities to Košice.

  • Car rentals: Car rentals are available from the airport and other locations in the city.

How to Get Around in Košice

Public transportation in Košice is efficient and affordable, and visitors can use buses and trams to get around the city.

Where to Go Next from Košice: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Košice is located in an area of Slovakia that is rich in natural and cultural beauty. Here are some nearby destinations worth visiting:

  • Slovak Paradise National Park: Known for its stunning scenery and hiking trails, Slovak Paradise National Park is a must-visit.

  • Spiš Castle: This UNESCO World Heritage Site is located about an hour’s drive from Košice and is one of the largest castle complexes in the region.

Conclusion

Košice is a beautiful city that is well worth visiting. Whether you’re interested in history, culture, or outdoor activities, there’s something for everyone here. Use our travel guide to plan your perfect trip to Košice, and get ready for an unforgettable experience.
