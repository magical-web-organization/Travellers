Introduction to Bardejov

Nestled in the rolling hills of Eastern Slovakia, Bardejov is a hidden gem that offers travelers a glimpse into the past. The town’s well-preserved medieval architecture and quaint cobblestone streets are so remarkable that they have been awarded UNESCO World Heritage Site status.

The town is surrounded by stunning natural scenery, including the High Tatras mountain range, which is just a short drive away. Bardejov’s rich cultural heritage, combined with its abundance of outdoor activities, makes it a perfect destination for travelers who want to experience everything Slovakia has to offer.

List of Places to Visit in Bardejov

Whether you’re interested in history, culture, or outdoor activities, Bardejov has something to offer everyone. Here are some of the top places to visit in the town:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • St. Egidius Basilica: This Gothic church was built in the 15th century and is one of the town’s most important landmarks. Inside, you’ll find stunning frescoes and intricate woodcarvings.
  • Bardejov City Walls and Bastions: These well-preserved fortifications were built in the 14th century and offer a stunning glimpse into Bardejov’s medieval past.
  • Bardejov Town Hall: This Renaissance-style building once served as a meeting place for the town’s leaders. Today, it houses the town’s museum.
  • Slovak National Museum - Bardejov Castle: This former royal castle houses a variety of exhibitions on the town’s history and culture.

Natural Attractions of Bardejov: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Bardejov Spa: Located just outside the town, this thermal spa offers a range of wellness treatments and a stunning indoor/outdoor pool complex.
  • Kyselica Leisure Complex: This complex offers a range of activities, including hiking, biking, and horseback riding.
  • Lubovna Castle: This stunning castle, located about 30 minutes from Bardejov, offers magnificent views of the surrounding countryside.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Bardejov

Bardejov is surrounded by some of Slovakia’s most stunning natural scenery, making it a popular destination for outdoor enthusiasts. Here are some of the best hiking and biking routes in the area:

  • Bardejovske Kupalisko to Mihalov: This 12-kilometer hike offers stunning views of the surrounding hills and forests, as well as a visit to a quaint rural village along the way.
  • Rozhanovce to Bardejov: This 15-kilometer bike ride takes you through rolling farmland and past historic churches and synagogues.
  • Nizna Pisanova to Topla: This moderate hiking route takes you through pristine forests and along babbling streams, with stunning views of the surrounding mountains at every turn.

Best Things to Do in Bardejov: Activities and Tours

In addition to the town’s museums and natural attractions, there are plenty of activities and tours available to visitors. Here are some of the top things to do in Bardejov:

  • Wine Tasting: Slovakia is known for its excellent wines, and Bardejov is home to several vineyards that offer tastings and tours.
  • Folklore Evenings: Bardejov is home to several folklore ensembles that perform traditional Slovak music and dance.
  • Photography Tours: The town’s medieval architecture and stunning natural scenery make it a favorite destination for photographers, and several local tour guides offer photography tours.

Week-Long Itinerary for Bardejov

If you’re planning a week-long trip to Bardejov, here’s a suggested itinerary that covers all the top attractions:

Day 1: Explore the town’s historical and cultural attractions, including St. Egidius Basilica and the Bardejov City Walls and Bastions.

Day 2: Take a day trip to Lubovna Castle and enjoy the stunning views of the surrounding countryside.

Day 3: Spend the day at the Bardejov Spa, relaxing in the thermal pools and enjoying a range of wellness treatments.

Day 4: Go hiking or biking in the surrounding hills and forests, following one of the suggested routes listed above.

Day 5: Take a wine tour and enjoy some of Slovakia’s delicious local wines.

Day 6: Attend a folklore performance at one of Bardejov’s local ensembles.

Day 7: Take a photography tour of the town and its surrounding countryside, capturing the town’s medieval architecture and stunning natural scenery.

Food and Drink Scene of Bardejov

Slovakia is known for its hearty cuisine, and Bardejov is no exception. Here are some of the must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Bryndzove Halusky: This traditional Slovak dish consists of potato dumplings smothered in a rich sheep cheese sauce.
  • Cabbage Soup: This hearty soup is made with smoked sausage, potatoes, and sauerkraut.
  • Lokse: These flatbreads are often served with meat and gravy, and are a staple of Slovak cuisine.

Local Beverages

  • Slovakia is known for its excellent wines, with several vineyards located near Bardejov that offer tastings and tours.
  • Borovicka: This clear, juniper-flavored brandy is a popular spirit in Slovakia.
  • Tatratea: This herbal liqueur is made with black tea and a blend of herbs and spices, and is often served as an after-dinner drink.

Where to Stay in Bardejov: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Bardejov offers a range of accommodation options to suit every budget and style. Here are some of the top options:

  • Hotel Pod Hradom: This cozy hotel is located in the heart of Bardejov’s old town and offers stunning views of St. Egidius Basilica.
  • Penzion Le Monde: This charming guesthouse is located just outside the town and offers comfortable rooms and a beautiful garden.
  • Hostel Bardejov: This budget-friendly option offers dormitory-style rooms as well as private suites, and is located just a short walk from the town center.

What to Buy in Bardejov: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

If you’re looking to bring home a piece of Bardejov, here are some of the top souvenirs and local products to consider:

  • Handmade pottery: Bardejov is known for its traditional ceramic pottery, which is often adorned with intricate designs and patterns.
  • Cheese: Sheep cheese is a local specialty, and can be purchased at several specialty food stores in the town center.
  • Folk Crafts: Bardejov is home to several local artisans who produce beautiful traditional Slovak crafts such as embroidered linen and wooden toys.

Local Culture and Customs of Bardejov

Slovakia has a rich cultural heritage, and Bardejov is no exception. Visitors should be aware of the following customs and cultural practices:

  • Dress Code: Dress modestly when visiting churches and other religious sites.
  • Greetings: Slovakians typically greet each other with a handshake, and it is considered polite to say “dobry den” (good day) or “dobry vecer” (good evening).
  • Folklore: Traditional Slovak folklore is an important part of the town’s cultural heritage, and visitors should take the opportunity to attend a folklore performance during their stay.

Best Time to Visit Bardejov

Bardejov can be visited year-round, but the best time to visit is during the spring or summer months, when the weather is mild and there are plenty of outdoor activities to enjoy.

Do’s and Don’ts of Bardejov

Here are some tips to help you make the most of your stay in Bardejov:

Do:

  • Take the time to explore the town’s historical and cultural attractions, such as St. Egidius Basilica and Bardejov Castle.
  • Try some of the local cuisine and beverages, including bryndzove halusky and Slovak wines.
  • Enjoy the town’s beautiful natural surroundings by hiking or biking in the surrounding hills and forests.

Don’t:

  • Forget to dress modestly when visiting churches and other religious sites.
  • Neglect to take a photography tour - the town’s medieval architecture and natural scenery are perfect for capturing stunning photos.
  • Overlook the opportunity to attend a folklore performance - it’s a vital part of the town’s cultural heritage.

Safety and Health

Slovakia is a safe country, and Bardejov is no exception. Visitors should take the same precautions they would in any other European town. Here are some tips to help you stay safe and healthy:

Safety in Bardejov

  • Be aware of your surroundings and keep your belongings secure.
  • Avoid going out alone at night, particularly in less well-lit areas.
  • Use only licensed taxi services.

Health in Bardejov

  • Tap water is safe to drink in Bardejov.
  • Medical care is generally good, but it is recommended that visitors have comprehensive travel insurance.

Getting to Bardejov: Transportation Options and Logistics

Bardejov is located in Eastern Slovakia, and is easily accessible by several modes of transportation. Here are some of the options:

Airports Near Bardejov

  • Kosice International Airport: This is the closest airport to Bardejov and is located about 80 kilometers away.
  • Poprad-Tatry Airport: This airport is located about 100 kilometers from Bardejov and is a good option for travelers coming from Western Europe.

Bus Stations Near Bardejov

  • Bardejov bus station is located in the town center and offers regular services to major nearby towns and cities.
  • Kosice bus station offers national and international services, including connections to Vienna and Budapest.

Other Options

  • Train: The nearest train station is located in Kosice, which offers national and international connections.
  • Car: Bardejov is easily accessible by car, with good roads and highways connecting it to nearby towns and cities.

How to Get Around in Bardejov

Bardejov is a small town, and most attractions are within easy walking distance of the town center. However, there are several other transportation options available, including:

  • Taxis: Taxis in Bardejov are relatively inexpensive and can be hailed on the street or booked in advance.
  • Buses: The town has a comprehensive bus network that connects it with nearby towns and cities.
  • Bike Rental: Several bike rental companies operate in Bardejov, offering a great way to explore the town’s natural surroundings.

Where to Go Next from Bardejov: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

If you’re looking to explore more of Eastern Slovakia, there are several nearby destinations and excursions to consider. Here are some top options:

  • High Tatras: This stunning mountain range is just a short drive from Bardejov and offers some of the best hiking and skiing in Slovakia.
  • Levoca: This charming town is home to some of Slovakia’s most impressive Gothic architecture and is just an hour’s drive from Bardejov.
  • Spis Castle: This sprawling castle complex is located about 90 minutes from Bardejov and is one of the largest castle ruins in Central Europe.

Conclusion

Bardejov may not be as well-known as some of Slovakia’s larger cities, but it offers visitors a chance to experience the country’s rich cultural heritage and stunning natural surroundings in an intimate and charming setting. Whether you’re interested in history, culture, or outdoor adventure, Bardejov has something to offer everyone.
