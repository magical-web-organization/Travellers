Introduction to Levoča

Located in eastern Slovakia, in the beautiful region of Spiš, Levoča is a charming and historic town that will transport you back in time to the Renaissance era. With its well-preserved medieval architecture, thriving local culture, and stunning natural scenery, Levoča is a must-visit destination in Slovakia. This comprehensive travel guide provides you with all the information you need to plan your trip to Levoča.

List of Places to Visit in Levoča

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Levoča boasts a rich history and a plethora of culturally important attractions that are a must-visit for any traveler.

St. James’s Church

One of the most iconic landmarks in Levoča, St. James’s Church boasts the highest wooden altar in the world. Built in the 14th century, the church also features an impressive wall painting of the Dance Macabre.

Levoča City Walls

The city walls of Levoča offer a glimpse into the town’s medieval past. The walls were built in the 14th century and stretch for over 2 kilometers, with several gates and towers, including the breathtaking Gothic tower.

Spiš Museum

The Spiš Museum showcases the region’s rich cultural heritage, from its traditional crafts to its history of mining and metallurgy. The museum’s permanent exhibition also features a wide collection of Gothic art from the region.

Natural Attractions of Levoča: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Aside from its historical sites, Levoča is also home to a variety of natural attractions, making it the perfect destination for outdoor enthusiasts.

Slovenský Raj National Park

Located just a short drive from Levoča, Slovenský Raj National Park is a haven for hikers and nature lovers. The park features stunning waterfalls, gorges, and limestone rock formations that are unique to this part of Slovakia.

Prielom Hornádu

Prielom Hornádu is a breathtaking gorge located in the Slovak Paradise National Park. The gorge boasts crystal-clear turquoise waters, towering cliffs, and verdant forests, making it the perfect day trip destination from Levoča.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Levoča

With its stunning natural scenery and diverse terrain, Levoča offers a variety of hiking and biking routes for travelers of all levels.

The Gothic Route

The Gothic Route is a popular hiking trail that takes you through the heart of Levoča’s historic district, including its iconic city walls, St. James’s Church, and the Gothic tower.

The Bike Trail to Spiš Castle

If you’re a cycling enthusiast, then you’ll love the bike trail to Spiš Castle, one of the most iconic castles in Slovakia. The trail takes you through the majestic Spisska Kapitula, a historic settlement in the heart of Slovakia.

Best Things to Do in Levoča: Activities and Tours

Apart from sightseeing, Levoča offers plenty of activities for visitors of all ages.

Folklore Festival

One of the biggest events in Levoča’s cultural calendar, the Folklore Festival takes place every summer and features traditional music, dance, and crafts from Slovakia and beyond.

Wine Tasting Tour

Located in the heart of Slovakia’s wine region, Levoča is an ideal base for a wine tasting tour. You can explore the vineyards, sample award-winning wines, and indulge in delicious local cuisine.

Week-Long Itinerary for Levoča

Here’s a suggested itinerary for a week-long trip to Levoča:

Day 1 - Explore the Old Town of Levoča including St. James’s Church, the Gothic tower and the city walls, Spevakov Court, and Rákoczi House. Day 2 - Visit Spiš Castle and Spisska Kapitula. Day 3 - Take a day trip to Slovenský Raj National Park, go hiking along the Suchá Belá gorge. Day 4 - Enjoy a leisurely stroll through the historic town of Kežmarok and visit the Kežmarok Castle. Day 5 - Take a dip in one of Slovakia’s famous thermal spas in the nearby towns of Liptovsky Jan or Vrbov. Day 6 - Go on a wine tasting tour and visit one of Slovakia’s best wineries. Day 7 - Visit Prielom Hornádu in the Slovak Paradise National Park.

Food and Drink Scene of Levoča

Slovakia is famous for its hearty cuisine, and Levoča is no exception. Here are some of the must-try dishes and local beverages in Levoča.

Must-Try Dishes

Bryndzové Halušky

Bryndzové Halušky is Slovakia’s national dish. It consists of small potato dumplings cooked with a sheep’s milk cheese called bryndza and topped with bacon bits.

Pirohy

Pirohy is a traditional Slovak dish that resembles dumplings and is filled with either a sweet or savory filling.

Local Beverages

Tokaj Wine

Levoča is located in the Tokaj wine region, famous for producing some of Slovakia’s best wines. Tokaj wine is a sweet white wine, perfect for pairing with local cheeses and meats.

Tatratea

Tatratea is a traditional Slovak liqueur made from herbs and fruits. It comes in various flavors and is often served as a digestif after a hearty meal.

Where to Stay in Levoča: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Levoča offers a variety of accommodation options to suit every traveler’s budget and preferences.

Grand Hotel Levoča

The Grand Hotel Levoča is a luxurious hotel located in the heart of the town. It offers spacious rooms with beautiful views, an on-site restaurant serving traditional Slovak cuisine, and a spa and wellness center.

Penzión u Jakuba

Penzión u Jakuba is a charming guesthouse located in the historic district of Levoča. It offers comfortable rooms with traditional decor, a garden terrace, and an on-site restaurant serving local specialties.

What to Buy in Levoča: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Levoča is famous for its traditional crafts, including pottery, woodcarving, and lace-making. You can find a wide variety of souvenirs and local products in the town’s souvenir shops and markets.

Levoča Pottery

Levoča pottery is famous for its unique designs and intricate details. You can purchase a wide variety of pottery items, from plates and bowls to decorative figurines.

Wooden Souvenirs

Wooden souvenirs are another popular item to bring back from Levoča. You can find a wide variety of handmade wooden furniture, decor items, and figurines at local markets and souvenir shops.

Local Culture and Customs of Levoča

Slovakia is a country with a rich cultural heritage, and Levoča is no exception. The locals are friendly and welcoming, and they take pride in their history and traditions.

Best Time to Visit Levoča

The best time to visit Levoča is during the months of May to September, when the weather is mild and pleasant, and the town comes alive with various cultural events and festivals.

Do’s and Don’ts of Levoča

When visiting Levoča, here are some do’s and don’ts to keep in mind:

Do:

  • Try the local cuisine and beverages, including bryndzové halušky, Tokaj wine, and Tatratea.

  • Learn about the town’s rich history and culture by visiting its museums and historical sites.

  • Take part in cultural events and festivals to experience the local way of life.

Don’t:

  • Wear revealing clothing or act disrespectfully in religious sites such as St. James’s Church.

  • Take photographs of people without their consent.

  • Litter or damage the environment in any way.

Safety and Health

Safety in Levoča

Levoča is a safe destination with low crime rates. However, it’s always important to exercise caution, especially when traveling alone or at night.

Health in Levoča

Slovakia has a high standard of healthcare, with modern medical facilities and well-trained doctors. Make sure you have adequate health insurance before traveling to the country.

Getting to Levoča: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Levoča

The closest airports to Levoča are Košice International Airport and Krakow Airport. Both airports are well-serviced by international airlines, with direct flights to major European cities.

Bus Stations Near Levoča

Levoča is well-connected by bus to other towns and cities in the region. The town’s main bus station is located near the city center, with frequent buses to nearby destinations.

Other Options

You can also rent a car, hire a taxi, or take a train to Levoča. However, public transportation is reliable and affordable, making it the best option for most travelers.

How to Get Around in Levoča

Levoča is a small town, and most of the major attractions are located within walking distance of each other. However, if you want to explore the town’s surroundings, you can rent a bike or a car.

Where to Go Next from Levoča: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Levoča is located in the heart of the Spiš region, making it the perfect base for exploring other nearby destinations. Here are some ideas for day trips from Levoča:

High Tatras National Park

The High Tatras National Park is a popular destination for hiking, skiing, and outdoor activities. It’s located just a short drive from Levoča and offers breathtaking views of Slovakia’s tallest peaks.

Bardejov

Bardejov is a UNESCO World Heritage Site famous for its well-preserved medieval architecture and charming town square. It’s located in the nearby region of Šariš and is worth a visit for history buffs.

Conclusion

Levoča is a hidden gem in Slovakia that offers a perfect blend of historical sites, natural attractions, and cultural experiences. This comprehensive travel guide will help you plan a memorable trip to Levoča and its surrounding regions. Whether you’re a history buff, an outdoor enthusiast, or a foodie, Levoča has something for everyone.
