Introduction to Levice

Located in Western Slovakia, Levice is a small town in the Tekov region. This picturesque town is surrounded by beautiful landscapes, steeped in rich history, and home to an array of cultural attractions, making it the perfect destination for travelers who want to experience true Slovakian heritage.

In this comprehensive tour guide for Levice, we will explore the best places to visit, the most exciting activities and tours, and where to stay and eat. We’ll also delve into the local customs, health and safety tips, and how to get around. So, without further ado, let’s embark on this journey of the small but beautiful town of Levice.

List of Places to Visit in Levice

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Levice’s historical and cultural significance is evident as soon as you set foot in the town. There is an abundance of museums and historical places that offer an unparalleled experience to the visitors.

Castle Museum

A must-visit historical landmark in Levice is the Castle Museum. The castle is a significant cultural and historical symbol of the town and a masterpiece of Gothic architecture. Established in the 16th century, the castle was reconstructed in the Baroque and Renaissance styles. Visitors can explore the beautiful atrium, elegant courtyard, chapel, and stunning exhibitions that showcase the castle’s impressive history.

Municipal Museum

Another place to embrace the rich culture of Levice is the Municipal Museum. The three exhibition rooms of the museum chronicle the history of the town, including archeological finds from the surrounding area, ethnographic collections, and the art of the 20th century. The exhibitions are interactive and engaging, making it a perfect place to explore the town’s past.

St. Michael’s Church

Built-in the 15th century, St. Michael’s Church is a significant religious monument in Levice. The church is a fine example of Gothic architecture, possessing a beautiful interior space with colorful stained-glass windows and unique Baroque features.

Natural Attractions of Levice: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Levice’s natural beauty is another reason to visit the town. From the scenic views to outdoor adventures, there’s something for everyone to explore.

Hodonin Reservoir

The Hodonin Reservoir, located near Levice, is an excellent place to enjoy outdoor activities like swimming, fishing, and boating while enjoying the views of Levice’s spectacular landscapes. The reservoir also has a beach, many playgrounds, and cycling lanes.

Krupina Valley

Nature lovers will appreciate the Krupina Valley, which is a natural reserve encircling the beautiful Krupinica Creek. Over 65 hectares of lush greenery, streams, and picnic areas make it one of the top-rated spots for a day in nature.

Best Things to Do in Levice: Activities and Tours

Apart from the above landmarks and natural beauties, Levice is a destination that offers several activities and tours to suit your interests. Whether you’re looking for an adrenaline rush or peaceful sightseeing, Levice has it all.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Levice

Levice has several hiking and biking trails that weave through the beautiful surroundings of town. And the best part is, these routes present diverse challenges for hikers and bikers of all abilities. Some of the most popular routes include the Blue-White trail and the Podhorie trail.

Week-Long Itinerary for Levice

A perfect way to explore Levice’s hidden gems is by planning an itinerary for a week-long trip. From visiting historical places and natural reserves to exploring local culture and nightlife, Levice has got it all. The following is a sample week-long itinerary for Levice:

Day 1 Visit the Castle Museum and Castle Gardens
Day 2 Hike to Krupina or visit the Hodonin Reservoir
Day 3 Explore Levice’s local food and drink scene

Food and Drink Scene of Levice

Slovakia is known for its distinctive cuisine, and Levice is no exception. The town features several restaurants, pubs, and cafes that serve traditional Slovakian dishes and local specialties.

Must-Try Dishes

Some must-try dishes in Levice include:

  • Bryndzove Halusky: Potato dumplings with sheep cheese and bacon.
  • Kapustnica: Traditional Slovakian soup made with sauerkraut and smoked meat.
  • Klobasa: Slovakian grilled sausages.
  • Ciganska Pecienka: Fried pork liver with onion and paprika.
  • Zelnik: A layered pastry filled with cabbage and bacon.

Local Beverages

Slovakian beers have made their place in the world with their rich, crisp taste. You can enjoy local brews in Levice, which has a prominent local beer scene. Alongside beer, try the homemade brandies in local pubs, such as Borovicka and Slivovica.

Where to Stay in Levice: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Levice has several accommodations to suit different preferences and budgets.

If you’re looking for a luxurious stay, the Chateau Krakovany offers a comfortable place to stay and enjoy your trip. Other top-rated options include Retro Hotel Satov, Hotel Golden Eagle, and Hotel Lev.

Budget-conscious travelers can find more affordable hostels and guesthouses in the town center or near the Hodonin Reservoir.

What to Buy in Levice: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Levice’s traditional products make perfect souvenirs for visitors and a great gift from Slovakia. From woodcarvings to rock salt lamps, Levice has some unique items to buy. Some popular souvenirs include:

  • Hand-carved wooden products
  • Folk costumes and clothes
  • Pottery products
  • Slovakian wines
  • Roasted nuts

Local Culture and Customs of Levice

Understanding the local culture and customs can give you a unique insight into the town’s history and people. Slovakians are hospitable and respect their traditions. If you’re invited for a meal or a drink, it’s customary to bring a small gift for your host.

Best Time to Visit Levice

The best time to visit Levice is in spring and summer (April to August). With milder temperatures, visitors can enjoy outdoor activities and explore the natural beauty of the town comfortably.

Do’s and Don’ts of Levice

Do:

  • Respect the local customs and traditions.
  • Explore the local food and drink scene.
  • Take care of the natural reserves and carry your litter.
  • Learn some simple Slovakian phrases.

Don’t:

  • Ignore the safety instructions in outdoor activities.
  • Leave your valuable belongings unattended.
  • Drink tap water in the town.

Safety and Health

Safety in Levice

Levice is a safe town for tourists with a low crime rate. However, visitors should be careful and take necessary precautions, such as not carrying valuables on them and locking the hotel room doors.

Health in Levice

Visitors from the EU countries can access the free health care in Levice. Non-EU visitors may require travel insurance. Remember to take necessary shots before traveling to Slovakia.

Getting to Levice: Transportation Options and Logistics

Levice is located 145 kilometers from Slovakia’s capital city, Bratislava. Visitors can reach Levice via several means of transportation.

Airports Near Levice

The nearest international airports to Levice are:

  • Bratislava Airport (BTS) - located 152 kilometers from Levice.
  • Kosice International Airport (KSC) - situated 139 kilometers from Levice.

Bus Stations Near Levice

Levice has a central bus station connecting several Slovakian cities and towns regularly.

Other Options

Alternatively, visitors can also travel to Levice by train for a more scenic journey. The train route from Bratislava to Levice passes through the beautiful Slovakian countryside.

How to Get Around in Levice

Levice is a small town, and visitors can easily explore the town on foot or bike. However, those looking for more comfort can hire a taxi or use the local bus service that connects the town and nearby attractions.

Where to Go Next from Levice: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Levice’s central location makes it an ideal place to explore the nearby attractions and destinations. Some popular places to visit from Levice include:

  • Banská Štiavnica: A UNESCO World Heritage Site, around 75 kilometers from Levice.
  • Nitra: A town with many historical buildings, situated 40 kilometers from Levice.
  • Bojnice Castle: A fairy-tale castle, located 73 kilometers from Levice.

Conclusion

Levice has a lot to offer visitors and is an excellent destination for those seeking culture, history, and natural beauty. This comprehensive tour guide has presented the best places to visit, eat, and stay in Levice, along with safety and health tips and transportation options. Now that you know everything about Levice, pack your bags and get ready to explore this hidden gem of Slovakia.
