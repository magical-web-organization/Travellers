Introduction to Myjava

Myjava is a small town located in the Považie region of Western Slovakia, surrounded by the beautiful mountains of the White Carpathians. This picturesque town is known for its rich cultural heritage, historical sites, and outdoor adventures. It is one of the must-visit destinations for anyone traveling to Slovakia, with a perfect blend of natural beauty, cultural activities, and outdoor recreation.

List of Places to Visit in Myjava

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Myjava Museum Myjava Museum is a cultural institution that highlights the historical, social, economic, and cultural development of the Myjava region. The museum houses a collection of historical artifacts, paintings, and sculptures depicting the history and traditions of the region.

  • Pušovský Castle Pušovský Castle is a well-preserved castle located on a hilltop overlooking the town of Myjava. The castle was built in the 13th century and served as a strategic point for controlling trade routes across the region. Today, it serves as an ideal spot for visitors to explore the history and architecture of the region.

  • The Church of St. Helena The Church of St. Helena is a beautiful Roman Catholic church located in the heart of the town. The church’s architecture is a blend of Gothic and Baroque styles, making it a must-visit for art and architecture enthusiasts.

Natural Attractions of Myjava: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Javorníky Mountains The Javorníky Mountains are an ideal destination for outdoor enthusiasts, offering a range of outdoor activities such as hiking, cycling, and skiing. The mountains are dotted with picturesque villages, dense forests, and crystal-clear mountain streams, offering scenic views at every turn.

  • Vršatec Vršatec is a prominent sandstone mountain located in the Myjava region. The mountain is famous for its unique rock formations and is a popular destination for rock climbing enthusiasts.

  • Chleb Hill Chleb Hill is the highest peak in the White Carpathians, offering a breathtaking panoramic view of the region. The hill is accessible via a hiking trail, making it a popular destination for hikers and trekkers.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Myjava

Myjava region boasts an excellent network of well-maintained hiking and biking trails that wind through lush forests, picturesque villages, and scenic mountains. Some of the popular hiking/biking routes include:

  • Myjava Hills Trail The Myjava Hills Trail is a 40 km long circular trail that passes through the most beautiful parts of the region, including the Javorníky Mountains.

  • Along the Myjava River Trail This 20 km long trail follows the beautiful Myjava river and offers stunning views of the surrounding countryside.

Best Things to Do in Myjava: Activities and Tours

  • Wine Tasting Tour Wine tasting is a popular activity in the Myjava region, with a long tradition of winemaking. There are several wine cellars around Myjava that offer wine tasting tours, including Zoya cellar, Ján Hrádek cellar, and Ivan Hudec cellar.

  • Skiing The Myjava region is an ideal destination for skiing, with several ski resorts located in the nearby Javorníky Mountains, such as the Zahradki ski resort, Rohace ski resort, and Terchová ski resort.

Week-Long Itinerary for Myjava

Day 1: Visit the Myjava Museum and explore the town’s historical sites. Day 2: Hiking in the Javorníky Mountains. Day 3: Wine Tasting Tour. Day 4: Visit the Chleb Hill. Day 5: Skiing in the Javorníky Mountains. Day 6: Visit the Pušovský Castle. Day 7: Departure.

Food and Drink Scene of Myjava

Must-Try Dishes

  • Bryndzové halušky Bryndzové halušky is a traditional Slovak dish that consists of soft potato dumplings topped with sheep cheese and bacon.

  • Kapustnica Kapustnica is a hearty soup made with sauerkraut and various types of meat, such as ham, sausage, and bacon.

Local Beverages

  • Slovak Beer Slovakia has a rich beer culture, with several popular beer brands like Zlatý Bažant, Topvar, and Birell.

  • Wine Wine is an essential part of the Myjava region’s culture and heritage, with several wineries located around the town.

Where to Stay in Myjava: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

There are several accommodation options in Myjava, ranging from budget-friendly hostels to luxury hotels. Some popular options include:

  • Hotel Kriváň Hotel Kriváň is a luxurious hotel located in the center of Myjava, offering comfortable rooms and excellent amenities.

  • Penzion Beryl Penzion Beryl is a budget-friendly guesthouse located on the outskirts of the town, offering comfortable rooms and friendly service.

What to Buy in Myjava: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Hand-Painted Pottery Myjava is famous for its unique style of hand-painted pottery, with several stores around the town selling these beautiful and vibrant pieces.

  • Wine Wine is one of the most popular products to buy in Myjava. Visitors can purchase high-quality wines from the local wineries.

Local Culture and Customs of Myjava

Myjava is steeped in rich folklore and traditions, with several festivals and celebrations throughout the year, such as the Myjava Folk Festival and the Myjava Kollárov Festivities. The town is also famous for its traditional dances and music, with several folk groups and ensembles performing regularly.

Best Time to Visit Myjava

The best time to visit Myjava is during the spring and summer months, from March to September, when the weather is warm and pleasant. This is also the best time to enjoy outdoor activities such as hiking, cycling, and wine tasting.

Do’s and Don’ts of Myjava

Do:

  • Respect local culture and customs.
  • Dress appropriately while visiting religious sites.
  • Try the local cuisine and wine.

Don’t:

  • Be loud or disruptive in public places.
  • Smoke in non-designated areas.
  • Take photographs of people without their permission.

Safety and Health in Myjava

Safety in Myjava

Myjava is a safe and peaceful town to visit, with low crime rates. However, visitors should take necessary precautions, such as locking their valuable possessions.

Health in Myjava

The medical facilities in Myjava are well-equipped to handle emergencies. Visitors are advised to carry their medical insurance and necessary medication.

Getting to Myjava: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Myjava

The closest international airport to Myjava is the Bratislava Airport, located approximately 130 km away.

Bus Stations Near Myjava

Myjava has a small bus station located in the center of the town, offering regular local and regional services.

Other Options

Visitors can also reach Myjava by train, with several connecting stations across Slovakia.

How to Get Around in Myjava

Visitors can easily explore Myjava on foot, with most of the attractions located in the town center. Taxis and rental cars are also available for hire.

Where to Go Next from Myjava: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Trenčín The city of Trenčín is located approximately 35 km from Myjava and is famous for its well-preserved medieval castle and beautiful architecture.

  • Zázrivá Zázrivá is a picturesque village located approximately 25 km from Myjava and is famous for its traditional wooden houses and beautiful countryside.

Conclusion

Myjava’s natural beauty, rich cultural heritage, and outdoor adventures make it an ideal destination for anyone looking to explore Western Slovakia. From the wine tasting tours to the picturesque mountains, there is something for everyone in this stunning destination. Plan your visit today to experience the best of Myjava.
