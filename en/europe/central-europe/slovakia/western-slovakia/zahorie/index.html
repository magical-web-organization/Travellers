Introduction to Záhorie

Záhorie is a hidden gem located in Western Slovakia, bordering Austria. It is home to picturesque countryside, historic towns, beautiful castles, and a variety of outdoor activities. This region is relatively unknown and untouched by tourism, making it perfect for travellers who want to escape the crowds while still enjoying a unique European destination. In this travel guide, we’ll show you all the best places to visit, things to do, and where to eat and stay in Záhorie.

List of Places to Visit in Záhorie

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Devin Castle

Devin Castle is a historical monument that dates back to the 9th century. This castle played a significant role in the country’s history, and today it is a popular tourist attraction. The castle is located on a hill overlooking the Danube River, and visitors can enjoy scenic views of Austria and Slovakia while exploring the castle’s ruins. The castle is open to the public, and guided tours are available.

St. John the Baptist Church in Stupava

St. John the Baptist Church is an excellent example of baroque architecture in Záhorie. The church’s interior boasts ornate decorations, marble columns and a grand altar. Visitors can also climb atop the church’s tower for panoramic views of Stupava.

Museum in Skalica

The museum in Skalica is a must-visit place for history and art lovers. It holds a variety of exhibits ranging from traditional costumes to beautiful art collections. Visitors can learn about the history and culture of Záhorie and its people.

Natural Attractions of Záhorie: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Pajstun Castle

Pajstun Castle is situated on a hill overlooking the forest and town of Borinka. Visitors can hike to the castle through forest trails. The castle ruins offer magnificent views of the region and the perfect opportunity to explore the history of Záhorie.

Small Carpathians

The Small Carpathians mountain range features abundant hiking and cycling trails that lead visitors to scenic viewpoints, including two scenic hikes - Kamzík and Vtáčnik - that provide stunning vistas of the surrounding Slovakian landscape.

Záhorie Protected Landscape Area

The Záhorie Protected Landscape Area covers over 500km² of land that is home to various wildlife. Visitors can explore the area through scenic drives, biking, or hiking. The area is dotted with charming traditional villages, rural landscapes, and rich flora and fauna.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Záhorie

Záhorie offers several spectacular cycling and hiking routes that take travelers through scenic countryside and offer stunning views of the surrounding region.

Stupava-Závod-Seneská skala-Pajštún

The route is popular with hikers who enjoy scenic nature trails. Visitors can explore the ruins of Pajštún Castle and enjoy magnificent views from the top of the hill. The route is around 15 kilometers long, and it takes approximately 5 hours to complete on foot.

Small Carpathian Wine Route

Small Carpathian Wine route is an ideal way to explore the area’s vineyards and rural landscapes. This cycling route showcases the region’s rich wine culture and takes cyclists through picturesque villages and stunning countryside views.

Best Things to Do in Záhorie: Activities and Tours

Záhorie Wine Tasting

Záhorie is home to a considerable wine-producing industry, and travelers can join guided tours of vineyards and wineries in the area. Visitors can sample various wines and learn about the region’s wine history, production, and culture.

Thermal Baths

Záhorie boasts several thermal baths and spas, including the popular family-friendly Aquapark Senec and the luxurious Wellness Hotel Park Inn Palace in Bratislava. These facilities offer visitors the chance to unwind and rejuvenate amidst natural surroundings.

Week-Long Itinerary for Záhorie

Day 1: Explore Devin Castle and its surroundings Day 2: Visit the Museum in Skalica and the St. John’s Church in Stupava Day 3-4: Hike in the Small Carpathian Ranges Day 5: Visit Pajstun Castle and its surroundings Day 6: Relax at one of the thermal baths Day 7: Enjoy a wine tour along the Small Carpathian wine route

Food and Drink Scene of Záhorie

Must-Try Dishes

Halušky

Halušky is a traditional Slovakian dish that is a must-try for visitors to Záhorie. It is a type of potato dumplings smothered in sheep cheese and bacon.

Rascovy Gulaš

Rascovy Gulaš is a goulash made of venison that is slow-cooked in a tomato and paprika-based sauce.

Local Beverages

Záhorie Wines

Záhorie is a well-known wine region, and visitors can enjoy local wines on tours of vineyards and wineries in the Small Carpathians. Local wine varieties include Frankovka, Rizling, and Veltlínske Zelené.

Borovička

Borovička is a popular Slovakian spirit made from juniper berries that are widely used in cocktails and as a digestif after meals.

Where to Stay in Záhorie: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Záhorie offers a wide range of accommodation options, from luxury hotels to budget-friendly guesthouses and hostels. Visitors can stay at traditional wooden cottages or modern apartments, depending on their preferences and budget.

Luxury Hotels

Resort Senec

Resort Senec is a popular family-friendly hotel located near Aquapark Senec. The hotel offers an extensive range of activities, including swimming pools, saunas, and fitness centers, making it perfect for leisure travelers.

Hotel Park Inn Palace

Hotel Park Inn Palace is a luxurious hotel with lavish amenities. It overlooks the Danube River and features an indoor pool, spa, and fitness center. The hotel is known for its high standard of service and prime location in Bratislava.

Budget-Friendly Accommodation

Penzión Cosmopolitan II.

Penzión Cosmopolitan II is a budget-friendly guesthouse located in the charming town of Senec. The guesthouse offers comfortable accommodation and excellent services.

Hostel Folks

Hostel Folks is a popular hostel located in the heart of Bratislava. The hostel offers various dormitory rooms and private rooms at reasonable prices, making it ideal for budget-conscious travelers.

What to Buy in Záhorie: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Záhorie Wine

Visitors can bring home various local wines, including Rizling, Frankovka, and Veltlínske Zelené, all wines indigenous to the region.

Folk Crafts

Záhorie is known for its traditional wooden crafts, including carved figurines and kitchen decorations that make beautiful and authentic souvenirs.

Local Culture and Customs of Záhorie

Záhorie has a rich history and culture that is reflected in its traditional crafts, architecture, and cuisine. Visitors can experience local customs through popular events such as the Stupava Fair and traditional dances and festivals that take place throughout the year.

Best Time to Visit Záhorie

The best time to visit Záhorie is between April and October, when the weather is warm and sunny, and popular outdoor activities, such as hiking and cycling, are possible.

Do’s and Don’ts of Záhorie

Do:

  • Try traditional Slovakian dishes and wines.
  • Respect the local customs and traditions.
  • Be adventurous and explore the region’s outdoor wonders.

Don’t:

  • Make excessive noise and disturbance in residential areas and public places.
  • Litter or leave a mess in public places or natural areas such as parks and hiking trails.
  • Disrespect the local culture and customs.

Safety and Health

Safety in Záhorie

Záhorie is a safe region, but visitors should take usual precautions as in any new place. Look after your belongings, particularly in crowded spots and major touristic areas.

Health in Záhorie

Medical facilities in urban areas meet high standards and are well equipped. Visitors from European Union countries are entitled to have free health care under the reciprocal healthcare agreements. Travellers from non-European Union countries will need private travel insurance.

Getting to Záhorie: Transportation Options and Logistics

Záhorie has good transport links with various European destinations through air, rail and coach travel.

Airports Near Záhorie

The nearest airports are Bratislava Airport and Vienna International Airport, with both airports having multiple transfer options to the region.

Bus Stations Near Záhorie

Záhorie is accessible from Bratislava’s main bus station, Mlynské Nivy, which has direct connections to various destinations.

Other Options

Renting a car is an option for getting around the region and exploring it at your own pace, Additionally, cycling is a popular way to discover Záhorie’s natural beauty and attractions.

How to Get Around in Záhorie

Záhorie can be explored by car, train, bus or bike. Car rental is the most convenient way to explore the region.

Where to Go Next from Záhorie: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Bratislava

Bratislava is the closest major city to Záhorie. It is a charming destination known for its cultural events, castles, and historic landmarks.

Vienna

Vienna is a two and a half hour drive or train ride from Záhorie. It has plenty of attractions, including museums, art galleries and vibrant nightlife. Visitors can enjoy the Opera House or explore the castle and gardens of Schönbrunn Palace.

Conclusion

Záhorie is a hidden gem that offers visitors a unique experience of Slovakian heritage, culture and nature. From hiking to wine tasting, to exploring historic castles and scenic views, there is something for everyone. Book a trip and experience this beautiful and untouched region before the crowds discover it.
