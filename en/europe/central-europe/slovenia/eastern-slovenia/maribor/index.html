Introduction to Maribor

Located in Eastern Slovenia, Maribor is a charming city that features a mix of historical landmarks, natural wonders, and modern amenities. Known for its beautiful wine vineyards, technical universities and cultural events, it is a popular tourist destination that offers a plethora of activities for visitors to enjoy. Whether you’re a history buff or an outdoor enthusiast, you’ll find plenty to see and do in Maribor.

List of Places to Visit in Maribor

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Maribor Castle: A historical landmark that once served as a fortress, it now houses the Regional Museum of Maribor, where visitors can learn about the city’s rich history.
  • St. John the Baptist’s Cathedral: An impressive Gothic-style cathedral that dates back to the 13th century and features beautiful frescoes and stained glass windows.
  • Franciscan Church: A stunning Baroque-style church that features magnificent frescoes and a beautiful marble altar.
  • Maribor Synagogue: The oldest surviving synagogue in Slovenia and is now a cultural center, museum, and exhibition space.

Natural Attractions of Maribor: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Pohorje: A stunning natural park that spans over 1,600 hectares of forests, lakes, waterfalls, and hills. It offers a range of outdoor activities, from hiking and cycling to skiing and snowboarding.
  • Mariborsko Pohorje Ski Resort: An excellent ski resort located on Pohorje mountain that features over 41 kilometers of ski runs and 25 ski lifts.
  • Maribor City Park: A beautiful park located in the heart of the city that features landscaped gardens, walking paths, and a small lake.
  • Drava River: A picturesque river that flows through Maribor and offers opportunities for kayaking, fishing, and riverboat cruises.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Maribor

  • Maribor Pohorje UNESCO Tourist Route: A 13.5-kilometer hiking trail that takes visitors through the stunning forests and hills of Pohorje mountain and offers beautiful views of the surrounding countryside.
  • Drava Cycling Route: A 145-kilometer cycling route that follows the course of the Drava River from Austria to Croatia.
  • Maribor-Ljubljana Green Route: A popular cycling route that passes through some of Slovenia’s most scenic landscapes, including the Alpine foothills, the Sava River Valley, and the Karst region.

Best Things to Do in Maribor: Activities and Tours

  • Wine Tasting Tour: Take a tour of some of Maribor’s famous wine vineyards and experience the flavors of the region’s best wines.
  • City Sightseeing Tour: Learn about Maribor’s rich history and culture with a tour of the city’s key landmarks and attractions.
  • Pohorje Adventure Park: A high ropes course and adventure park located in the heart of Pohorje mountain that offers challenging obstacle courses, zip lines, and other adrenaline-pumping activities.
  • Wine Fountain: A unique wine tasting experience where visitors can sample various Slovenian wines from a wine fountain located in a public square.

Food and Drink Scene of Maribor

Must-Try Dishes

  • Gibanica: A traditional Slovenian layered pastry made with poppy seeds, walnuts, and honey.
  • Prekmurska gibanica: A traditional layered cake from the Prekmurje region, made with poppy seeds, walnuts, apples, and cottage cheese.
  • Idrijski žlikrofi: A type of pasta dumpling filled with potatoes and served with a variety of sauces.

Local Beverages

  • Slovenian Wine: Slovenia is known for producing high-quality wines, so don’t miss the opportunity to try some of the region’s famous wines such as the Styrian Golding from Maribor.
  • Beer: Slovenian beer is also worth a try, particularly Lasko, the country’s top-selling beer.

Where to Stay in Maribor: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Hotel Orel: A historic hotel located in the city center and is less than a five-minute walk from Maribor Castle and other key landmarks.
  • Hotel Piramida: A modern hotel located in the city center, near the main train and bus stations, which is a perfect base for exploring the city.
  • Gostilna Maribor Pohorje: A traditional guesthouse located on the edge of Pohorje mountain, offers stunning views of the region’s surrounding hills.

What to Buy in Maribor: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Honey: Slovenia is famous for its honey, particularly from the regions of Prekmurje and Pomurska.
  • Ceramics: Maribor is known for its beautiful ceramic pottery, particularly for its handcrafted pots and plates.
  • Wine: Don’t leave Maribor without a bottle of acclaimed Styrian Golding wine.

Local Culture and Customs of Maribor

  • Respect the tradition: Slovenians are extremely proud of their culture and traditions, so be respectful of them. Richly illustrated traditional Slovene attire, particularly in rural areas and among the older generation, continue to be worn.
  • Recycling: Maribor is committed to being environmentally friendly, and visitors are encouraged to recycle whenever possible.
  • Punctuality: Slovenes take punctuality seriously, and it’s considered rude to arrive late for appointments, so be on time.

Best Time to Visit Maribor

The best time to visit Maribor is during the summer months (June to August) when the weather is ideal for outdoor activities such as hiking, cycling, and swimming. The spring months (March to May) are also a great time to visit, with fewer crowds and mild temperatures. Winter sports enthusiasts should visit Maribor during the winter months (December to February) when the region’s ski resorts are open.

Do’s and Don’ts of Maribor

Do:

  • Respect local customs and traditions.
  • Carry cash as many restaurants and small shops may not accept credit cards.
  • Try sampling the local cuisine and wines and shop for local products such as honey, ceramics, and wine.
  • Take public transportation, such as the bus or a bike, to get around the city.
  • Take advantage of the city’s outdoor activities such as hiking and biking.

Don’t:

  • Don’t litter or ignore the city’s recycling program.
  • Avoid smoking indoors as it is prohibited in all indoor public spaces.
  • Don’t show up late to appointments, Slovenes are punctual people.
  • Don’t limit your exploration to the city center, explore the surrounding areas like the Pohorje Mountains.

Safety and Health

Safety in Maribor

Maribor is a safe city, with a low crime rate, however, visitors should still take common-sense precautions such as staying aware of their surroundings and not walking alone in unlit areas at night.

Health in Maribor

Medical services in Maribor are of good quality, but visitors are advised to purchase travel insurance before travelling for peace of mind. The city’s hospitals and pharmacies have well-trained English-speaking staff.

Getting to Maribor: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Maribor

The closest airport to Maribor is the Graz Airport in Austria, which is 45 kilometers away. Alternatively, you can fly into the Ljubljana Jože Pučnik Airport, which is 120 kilometers away from Maribor and offers more flights.

Bus Stations Near Maribor

Maribor has two bus stations, Maribor Main Bus station and Maribor City Bus station. The main bus station is located next to the train station and provides services to other Slovenian cities as well as some cities in Austria, Croatia, and Serbia. The city bus station, located in the city center, provides local and regional bus services.

Other Options

Maribor is well-connected by trains with many cities in Slovenia and abroad, buses, and taxis. The city center is small enough to be explored entirely on foot or by bike.

How to Get Around in Maribor

Public transportation in Maribor, like many other Slovenian cities, is efficient and reliable. Local buses, which run regularly in the city and surrounding areas, are the easiest way to get around. Taxis are also readily available.

Where to Go Next from Maribor: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Slovenia is a small country, and it’s easy to visit nearby destinations for day trips or overnight stays. From Maribor, you could visit the following destinations:

  • Ptuj: A medieval town located 24 kilometers southeast of Maribor, is known for its impressive castle and excellent thermal spas.
  • Lake Bled: A picturesque lake located in the Julian Alps and one of Slovenia’s top tourist attractions.
  • Ljubljana: The capital city of Slovenia, known for its charming cobblestone streets, historic architecture, and vibrant nightlife.

Conclusion

Maribor is a beautiful and exciting city that offers something for everyone. Whether you’re interested in exploring the city’s rich history, getting out into nature, or indulging in the local food and wine scene, Maribor is the perfect destination to explore. With this comprehensive travel guide, you have all the information you need to plan the perfect trip to Maribor, Slovenia.
