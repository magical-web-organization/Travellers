Introduction to Czechoslovakia

Czechoslovakia is a small landlocked country in Central Europe that was formed by the merger of Czech Republic and Slovakia in 1993. It is a popular destination among tourists for its rich culture, history, natural beauty, and year-round festivals. The country has a temperate climate, with warm summers and cold winters.

Czechoslovakia is a country with a plethora of attractions, beautiful landscapes, vibrant cities, and rich history. Here is a list of the most famous places to visit in Czechoslovakia:

Best Things to Do in Czechoslovakia: Activities and Tours

There is never a dull moment in Czechoslovakia, no matter what your interests are. Here are some of the best things to do in the country:

  • Attend a classical music concert at the National Theater or Municipal House
  • Take a walking tour of Prague, the capital city
  • Take a hot air balloon ride over Český Krumlov, a UNESCO World Heritage site
  • Go skiing in the Krkonoše or Jeseníky Mountains
  • Visit a vineyard in Moravia and taste some of the local wine
  • Attend a beer festival, such as Pilsner Fest or Czech Beer Festival
  • Take a boat tour of the Vltava River

Local Culture and Customs of Czechoslovakia

Czechoslovakia has a rich cultural heritage that can be experienced through art, music, and dance. The country is renowned for its classical music and has produced many composers and performers.

Best Time to Visit Czechoslovakia

The best time to visit Czechoslovakia is during the spring and fall, when the weather is mild, and there are fewer tourists. The summer months can be crowded, and the winters can be cold and snowy.

Do’s and Don’ts of Czechoslovakia

When visiting Czechoslovakia, it is important to be aware of the local customs and etiquette. Here are some tips:

Do:

  • Greet people with a handshake
  • Try the local cuisine and drinks
  • Dress appropriately when visiting religious sites
  • Tip 10-15% in restaurants

Don’t:

  • Smoke in public places
  • Take photos of people without permission
  • Make excessive noise in public areas
  • Interrupt people when they are speaking

Safety and Health

Czechoslovakia is a safe country, but like any other place, it is important to take precautions when traveling. Here are some tips:

Safety in Czechoslovakia

  • Keep your valuables in a safe place
  • Be aware of your surroundings
  • Use common sense when traveling alone at night

Health in Czechoslovakia

Czechoslovakia is a modern country with good medical facilities. It is advisable to have travel insurance when visiting, and to get vaccinated against hepatitis A and B.

How to Get Around in Czechoslovakia

Public transportation is the best way to get around Czechoslovakia. The country has a well-developed network of buses, trams, and trains. Taxis are also widely available.
