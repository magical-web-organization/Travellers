Introduction to Pomerania

Pomerania is a breathtakingly beautiful region located in Central Europe. With its stunning beaches, lush forests, historic sites, and vibrant culture, it has something for every type of traveler. This guide will take you on a virtual journey through the wonders of Pomerania, from must-see attractions to off-the-beaten-path gems.

Food and Drink Scene of Pomerania

Pomerania’s cuisine is a fusion of German, Slavic, and Scandinavian flavors. Here are some dishes and drinks you must try:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Bigos (Hunter’s Stew)
  • Pierogi (Polish Dumplings)
  • Kiszka (Blood Sausage)
  • Kartacze (Potato Dumplings)
  • Rosol (Chicken Soup)

Local Beverages

  • Polish Vodka
  • Kvass (Traditional Slavic Bread Drink)
  • Soplica (Polish Liqueur)
  • Piwo (Polish Beer)
  • Mead (Honey Wine)

Best Time to Visit Pomerania

The best time to visit Pomerania is from May to September. During these months, the weather is warm, and the outdoor activities and attractions are open.

Do’s and Don’ts of Pomerania

Do:

  • Try the local food and drink
  • Respect the culture and customs
  • Explore the natural beauty
  • Learn some basic Polish phrases
  • Try to learn about the history of the region

Don’t:

  • Smoke in public places
  • Be loud in public areas
  • Talk about politics
  • Express anti-Semitic or xenophobic views
  • Wear shoes in homes

Safety and Health

Safety in Pomerania

Pomerania is a safe travel destination. However, like any other place you travel to, be aware of your surroundings and avoid reputed unsafe areas, especially at night.

Health in Pomerania

Tap water in Pomerania is safe to drink. Visitors are required to have travel insurance while visiting Poland.
