Introduction to Dziwnów

Tucked away in the Zachodniopomorskie province of Poland, Dziwnów is a coastal town renowned for its natural beauty and serene atmosphere. Surrounded by the crystal-clear waters of the Baltic Sea and set amidst lush greenery, Dziwnów is a picture-perfect destination that is often overlooked by tourists. Despite this, the town has plenty of attractions and activities to offer, whether you’re seeking relaxation or adventure.

In this travel guide, we will take you through everything you need to know about Dziwnów, from the must-see sights and natural wonders to the local cuisine and cultural experiences. With our help, you’ll be able to experience the best of Dziwnów and discover why this hidden gem is a must-visit destination for your next trip to Poland.

List of Places to Visit in Dziwnów

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Dziwnów may be a peaceful coastal town, but it also boasts plenty of cultural and historical significance. These are some of the top museums and historical places to visit in Dziwnów:

  • Museum of Fishing and Marine Ecology: This is a fascinating museum that offers insight into the local fishing industry and marine ecology. Here, you can learn about the history of fishing in Dziwnów, examine fishing gear and boats from different time periods, and view specimens of local marine life.
  • Fortified Church of St. Mary’s: The church dates back to the 14th century and is one of the oldest and most fascinating buildings in Dziwnów. The exterior is a stunning example of Gothic architecture, and the interior houses several impressive works of art.
  • Pomeranian Dukes’ Castle in Darlowo: Though not technically in Dziwnów, this castle is worth checking out if you’re interested in history. The castle is located about 20 km from Dziwnów, in the town of Darłowo, and was built in the 14th century.

Natural Attractions of Dziwnów: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Dziwnów is a haven for nature lovers, with its idyllic natural surroundings offering plenty of opportunities for outdoor exploration and adventure. Here are some of the top natural attractions to explore in Dziwnów:

  • Dziwna River: The Dziwna River is a stunning waterway that flows through Dziwnów and into the Baltic Sea. Whether you’re interested in boating, fishing, or simply strolling the scenic riverside trail, this is a must-visit spot in Dziwnów.
  • Beaches: Dziwnów is known for its beautiful, sandy beaches. Some of the most popular beaches in Dziwnów include the West Beach, East Beach, and the “Wild Beach” (Plaża Dzikich).
  • Wrzosowska Bay: This is one of the most picturesque spots in Dziwnów, with clear, turquoise waters and a peaceful atmosphere. It’s a great place to swim, kayak, or simply relax and admire the view.
  • Mount Chełmska: This is a favourite spot for hiking and scenic views. Rising 86 metres above sea level, it offers panoramic views of Dziwnów and the surrounding area.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Dziwnów

Dziwnów is a great spot for hikers and cyclists, with plenty of scenic routes to explore. Some of the top routes to check out include:

  • The Dziwna River Trail: This is a 10-km riverside trail that runs from Dziwnów to the village of Łukęcin. It’s an easy route that offers stunning views of the river and the surrounding greenery.
  • Chełmska Mountain Trail: This is a more challenging route that takes you up the scenic Mount Chełmska. The trail is approximately 3 km in length and offers breathtaking views of Dziwnów and the Baltic Sea.

Best Things to Do in Dziwnów: Activities and Tours

Dziwnów has plenty of exciting activities and tours to offer visitors. Here are some of the best things to do in the town:

  • Boat Tours: One of the best ways to explore Dziwnów’s natural surroundings is by boat. There are several boat tour operators in the town, offering everything from guided fishing trips to sightseeing cruises along the Dziwna River.
  • Water Sports: Whether you’re an experienced water sports enthusiast or a beginner, there are plenty of opportunities for water-based fun in Dziwnów. You can try your hand at kayaking, windsurfing, jet-skiing, and much more.
  • Horse Riding: If you’re looking for a unique way to explore the town and its surroundings, consider taking a horseback riding tour. There are several horse riding operators in Dziwnów offering guided tours of the countryside and beaches.
  • Relaxing on the Beaches: Of course, one of the best things to do in Dziwnów is simply to relax on the town’s stunning beaches.

Week-Long Itinerary for Dziwnów

If you’re planning a week-long trip to Dziwnów, here’s a sample itinerary that will help you make the most of your visit:

  • Day 1: Arrive in Dziwnów and check into your hotel or guesthouse. Spend the afternoon exploring the town and getting your bearings.
  • Day 2: Take a boat tour along the Dziwna River in the morning, and then spend the afternoon relaxing on one of Dziwnów’s stunning beaches.
  • Day 3: Spend the morning exploring the town’s cultural attractions, including the Museum of Fishing and Marine Ecology and the Fortified Church of St. Mary’s. In the evening, head to a local bar or restaurant to sample some of the town’s delicious cuisine.
  • Day 4: Head out on a horseback riding tour of the town’s surrounding countryside, followed by a picnic lunch. Spend the rest of the day relaxing on the beach or exploring the town at your leisure.
  • Day 5: Take a day trip to the nearby town of Darłowo to visit the Pomeranian Dukes’ Castle and explore the town’s other cultural sights.
  • Day 6: Spend the morning hiking or cycling along one of Dziwnów’s scenic trails, and then spend the afternoon relaxing on the beach or exploring the town’s shopping and dining options.
  • Day 7: Depart from Dziwnów, feeling refreshed and rejuvenated after a week of exploring this beautiful coastal town.

Food and Drink Scene of Dziwnów

Must-Try Dishes

The local cuisine of Dziwnów is a blend of Polish and European influences, with an emphasis on fresh seafood and locally sourced ingredients. These are some of the top dishes to try in Dziwnów:

  • Fish Soup (Zupa rybna): This hearty soup is made with local fish and a blend of aromatic vegetables and herbs.
  • Grilled Fish (Ryba na grillu): Freshly caught fish is grilled to perfection and served with a variety of sides, including potatoes, salad, and bread.
  • Smoked Fish (Ryba wędzona): Smoked fish is a popular local delicacy in Dziwnów and can be enjoyed as a snack or served as a main course.
  • Pierogi: These traditional Polish dumplings can be found throughout the country, but in Dziwnów they are often served with a unique seafood twist.

Local Beverages

In Dziwnów, you’ll find a variety of local beverages to try, including beer, wine, and spirits. These are some of the most popular drinks in the town:

  • Polish Beer: Poland is known for its delicious beer, and you’ll find plenty of local and national brands to try in Dziwnów.
  • Vodka: Vodka is a popular drink in Poland, and you’ll find a variety of local and national brands in Dziwnów.
  • Wine: Though wine isn’t as popular as beer or vodka in Poland, there are still some delicious local varieties to try in Dziwnów.

Where to Stay in Dziwnów: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

There are plenty of accommodation options in Dziwnów, ranging from affordable hostels to luxurious hotels. These are some of the top options for your stay:

  • Sandy Beach Hotel: Located just a few steps from the beach and offering stunning sea views, Sandy Beach Hotel is a popular choice for visitors to Dziwnów.
  • Hotel&Spa Stary Dziwnów: This luxurious hotel and spa is located in the heart of Dziwnów and offers a range of amenities, including a pool, sauna, and massage services.
  • Pokoje Gościnne u Andrzeja i Barbary: This guesthouse offers comfortable and affordable accommodation, with a charming garden and close proximity to the town’s attractions.

What to Buy in Dziwnów: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

When in Dziwnów, there are several local products and souvenirs worth picking up as a reminder of your trip. These are some of the best things to buy in Dziwnów:

  • Amber Jewellery: Amber is a popular souvenir in Poland, and in Dziwnów you’ll find a variety of locally made amber jewellery.
  • Handmade Crafts: Many local craftsmen in Dziwnów create handmade items, such as pottery, woodwork, and textiles, that make unique and meaningful souvenirs.
  • Local Honey: The greenery surrounding Dziwnów makes it a perfect spot for beekeeping. You can find local jars of honey in many of the town’s artisan shops.

Local Culture and Customs of Dziwnów

Dziwnów is a welcoming and inclusive town, with a rich cultural heritage. Some of the local customs and traditions to be aware of include:

  • Religious Customs: Dziwnów and the surrounding region have a significant Catholic population, and religious holidays and customs are an important part of local life.
  • Fishing Culture: Fishing has long been a way of life in Dziwnów and the surrounding area, and the town maintains a deep respect for the sea and its bounty.
  • Local Music and Art: Dziwnów is home to several talented musicians and artists, whose works can be seen and heard at local events and festivals throughout the year.

Best Time to Visit Dziwnów

The best time to visit Dziwnów is during the summer months, from June to August, when the weather is warm and the town’s beaches and outdoor activities are in full swing. However, if you prefer a quieter atmosphere and don’t mind cooler temperatures, the spring and fall months can also be a great time to visit.

Safety and Health

Safety in Dziwnów

Dziwnów is generally a safe town for visitors, but like any destination, it’s important to take basic safety precautions. Always be aware of your surroundings and keep an eye on your belongings, especially in crowded areas or on the beach.

Health in Dziwnów

Poland has a high standard of healthcare, and there are several hospitals and clinics in the nearby town of Kamień Pomorski that offer medical assistance. If you require medical assistance while in Dziwnów, ask your accommodation provider or the local tourist office for recommendations.

Getting to Dziwnów: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Dziwnów

The nearest airport to Dziwnów is Szczecin-Goleniów Airport, located approximately 60 km away. Alternatively, you can also fly into the Berlin Tegel or Schönefeld airports, both of which are approximately 200 km away.

Bus Stations Near Dziwnów

There are several bus stations near Dziwnów, including those in Kamień Pomorski, Świnoujście, and Goleniów.

Other Options

If you’re travelling within Poland or nearby countries, you may also be able to reach Dziwnów by train. The nearest train station is in Kamień Pomorski, approximately 15 km away from the town.

How to Get Around in Dziwnów

Dziwnów is a small town that is easily navigable on foot or by bicycle. However, if you need to travel a bit farther afield, there are also taxis and local buses available. For longer journeys, you can rent a car or take a train or bus from one of the nearby towns.

Where to Go Next from Dziwnów: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Wolin Island: This beautiful island is located just a short drive from Dziwnów and features a national park full of hiking and biking trails, as well as several beaches.
