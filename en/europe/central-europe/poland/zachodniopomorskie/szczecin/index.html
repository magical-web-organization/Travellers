Introduction to Szczecin

Szczecin is a beautiful city situated in the northwest of Poland, near the German border. The historical city was shattered in World War II but has been impressively rebuilt since the 1950s. Szczecin has a wealthy history and was known as a significant Hanseatic city. Thanks to its central location in Europe, there are plenty of ways to get to Szczecin.

List of Places to Visit in Szczecin

Szczecin has plenty of attractions and things to do in the city. Here are some of the top places to visit in Szczecin:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

The National Museum in Szczecin

One of the most significant museums in Szczecin is the National Museum. This museum presents several exhibitions about history, archaeology, and contemporary art. The National Museum in Szczecin is located in the center of town and should be on anyone’s list who wants to immerse themselves in Szczecin.

The Pomeranian Dukes’ Castle

A once-residence of the dukes of Pomerania now has become a lovely museum of history and art. The Pomeranian Dukes’ Castle contains a vast collection of paintings, sculptures, ceramics, and handcrafts from Poland, Germany, and The Netherlands of the 18th and 19th centuries.

Natural Attractions of Szczecin: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Walking Tour of Szczecin

One of the best ways to get to know Szczecin is taking a walking tour. There are several tours offered by the Szczecin Tourist Board, but you can also go for a self-guided tour. Some highlights of a walking tour are the Waly Chrobrego promenade, the Old Town Hall, Cathedral Basilica of St. James the Apostle and more.

National Park Wolin

The National Park Wolin is located on the island of Wolin, which is famous for its white cliffs and the Baltic sea. There are different types of activities you can do in the park, such as hiking, bird watching, or horseback riding. The park is an excellent way to escape Szczecin’s more urban environment and enjoy nature.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Szczecin

Szczecin is a city with plenty of green space, making it an ideal place for a hiking or biking trip. Here are some of the biking and hiking routes in Szczecin:

The Oder River Trail

The Oder River Trail is an excellent 15-kilometer cycling route that follows the Oder River on both sides. The trail is scenic and includes the National Museum, Pomeranian Dukes’ Castle, and the Pomeranian Princes’ Park.

Szczecin to Swinoujscie

The bike route from Szczecin to Swinoujscie is about 70 kilometers long. The route passes through lovely towns and villages along the way. It’s a perfect day trip from the city.

Best Things to Do in Szczecin: Activities and Tours

Szczecin Aquapark

The Szczecin Aquapark is a great place to spend a hot summer day. This water park has different pools, slides, and a lazy river. Szczecin Aquapark is ideal for families with children of all ages.

Szczecin Segway Tour

One of the best ways to explore Szczecin is by a segway tour. The tour takes about two hours and covers places like the Waly Chrobrego Promenade, the Castle of the Pomeranian Dukes, and the Pomeranian Princes’ Park.

Food and Drink Scene of Szczecin

Must-Try Dishes

Stargardzka Pork Roulade (pieczony rolada ze schabu)

Stargardzka Pork Roulade is stuffed pork loin, usually with meat and herbs, served with potatoes and beetroot salad. It’s one of Szczecin’s most famous dishes.

Pyzy

Pyzy is a potato-based dish stuffed with meat, cheese, or fruits. It is usually served with fried onions and is a popular lunch item in Szczecin.

Local Beverages

Szczecin’s Beer

Szczecin has a long tradition of beer brewing. Today, there are a few local breweries to try, such as the “Browar Amber” in the city center.

Where to Stay in Szczecin: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Radisson Hotel Szczecin

Radisson Hotel Szczecin is a luxurious four-star hotel with comfortable rooms and a fitness center. The hotel is located in the city center, making it easy to explore Szczecin.

Hotel Novotel Szczecin Centrum

Hotel Novotel Szczecin Centrum is situated in the heart of the city and has excellent access to all of Szczecin’s touristic locations. The hotel has modern and spacious rooms, along with a fitness center and sauna.

What to Buy in Szczecin: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Amber Jewelry

Amber jewelry is a popular souvenir to buy in Szczecin. There are several amber shops in Szczecin, where you can find high-quality items at reasonable prices.

Local Culture and Customs of Szczecin

Polish culture is still dominant in Szczecin, but because of the proximity to Germany, there are a few customs that may seem odd to non-natives. It’s best to respect these customs when traveling to Szczecin.

Best Time to Visit Szczecin

The best time to visit Szczecin is during the summertime, from June to August. The weather is warm and comfortable, perfect for exploring the city and enjoying outdoor activities.

Do’s and Don’ts of Szczecin

Do:

  • Learn some basic phrases in Polish
  • Respect Polish customs and traditions
  • Eat Polish food

Don’t:

  • Speak German unless necessary
  • Be too loud in public areas
  • Bring up political discussions

Safety and Health

Safety in Szczecin

Szczecin is generally a safe city. However, travelers should take precautions against pickpocketing in tourist areas.

Health in Szczecin

Travelers who plan to spend a lot of time outdoors should take appropriate measures to protect themselves from tick-borne diseases, such as Lyme disease.

Getting to Szczecin: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Szczecin

The nearest airport to Szczecin is Szczecin–Goleniów Airport, located about 40 kilometers northeast of the city.

Bus Stations Near Szczecin

There are several main bus stations in Szczecin, including the Szczecin Bus Station and the Szczecin Port Bus Station. Both stations offer international connections to neighboring countries.

Other Options

Travelers who prefer to travel between cities by train can take advantage of PKP trains. There are also car rentals available in the city.

How to Get Around in Szczecin

Travelers who want to get around Szczecin can use public transport, such as bus, tram, or taxi. It’s also possible to rent a bike and explore the city’s bike routes at their own pace.

Where to Go Next from Szczecin: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Szczecin is close to many beautiful destinations; here are some of the top destinations near Szczecin:

Miedzyzdroje

Miedzyzdroje is a small town located on the Polish coast near the German border. One of its most famous sights is the pier, which is over 400 meters long.

Swinoujscie

Swinoujscie is a small coastal town located on the Polish-German border. The town is known for its beaches and the Gothic church of Christ the King.

Conclusion

Szczecin is a hidden gem in Poland, offering many historical and natural attractions, an exciting food scene, and cultural activities.
