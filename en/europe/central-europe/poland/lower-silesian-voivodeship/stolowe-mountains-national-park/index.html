Introduction to Stołowe Mountains National Park

Stołowe Mountains National Park is a hidden gem located in Lower Silesian Voivodeship, in Poland, in Central Europe, in Europe. The park covers an area of 63.39 sq km, and it’s known for its unique rock formations, scenic hiking trails, and abundant wildlife.

The park is home to the Stołowe Mountains, which are part of the Sudety Mountains. The highest peak in the park is Szczeliniec Wielki, which rises to an elevation of 919 meters. The park is a popular destination for outdoor enthusiasts, nature lovers, and families looking for a fun day out.

List of Places to Visit in Stołowe Mountains National Park

Stołowe Mountains National Park is full of interesting places to visit, from culturally important attractions to natural wonders and outdoor adventures.

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Bliżniaki rocks: The Bliżniaki rocks are a unique rock formation that looks like twin peaks. The rocks are popular among hikers and offer stunning panoramic views of the area.

  • Kłodzko Fortress: The Kłodzko Fortress is an imposing fortress that dates back to the 13th century. Visitors can take a guided tour of the fortress and learn about its history.

  • Wambierzyce: Wambierzyce is a historic town that is known for its beautiful Baroque architecture. Visitors can explore the town’s many churches and take a walk around the central square.

Natural Attractions of Stołowe Mountains National Park: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Szczeliniec Wielki: The Szczeliniec Wielki is the highest peak in the park and offers stunning panoramic views. Visitors can take a hiking trail to the summit and enjoy the view.

  • Babia Skała: The Babia Skała is a towering rock formation that looks like a giant mushroom. Visitors can take a hiking trail to the top of the rock and enjoy panoramic views of the area.

  • Kudowa Waterfall: The Kudowa Waterfall is a picturesque waterfall that is surrounded by lush forest. Visitors can take a hiking trail to the waterfall and enjoy the view.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Stołowe Mountains National Park

Stołowe Mountains National Park is a hiker’s paradise, with over 80 km of well-marked hiking trails. The parks’ trails lead visitors through stunning rock formations, past scenic viewpoints, and through lush forests.

Here are some of the most popular hiking and biking routes in Stołowe Mountains National Park:

  • Szczeliniec Trail: This trail leads visitors to the summit of Szczeliniec Wielki and offers stunning panoramic views of the area.

  • Błędne Skały Trail: This trail takes visitors through the stunning rock formations of Błędne Skały.

  • Babia Skała Trail: This trail leads visitors to the top of the Babia Skała and offers panoramic views of the area.

Food and Drink Scene of Stołowe Mountains National Park

Stołowe Mountains National Park is known for its delicious local cuisine, which is influenced by the region’s rich agricultural heritage. Visitors can enjoy a range of dishes that are made with locally sourced ingredients, including game meats, mushrooms, and fresh vegetables.

Must-Try Dishes

  • Kluski Śląskie: These are small, doughy dumplings that are made with potatoes and flour. They are a staple of the local cuisine and are often served with a meat dish.

  • Golonka: This is a traditional Polish dish that is made with pork shank. The dish is slow-cooked in a mixture of herbs and spices until it falls off the bone.

  • Karkówka: This is a popular Polish dish that is made with marinated pork loin. The dish is usually served with potatoes and vegetables.

Local Beverages

  • Piwo: Polish beer is world-famous for its rich flavor and high-quality ingredients. Visitors can sample a range of local beers at one of the park’s many pubs and bars.

  • Wodka: Poland is famous for its high-quality vodka, which is distilled from potatoes or rye. Visitors can try a range of local vodka brands at one of the park’s many bars and restaurants.
