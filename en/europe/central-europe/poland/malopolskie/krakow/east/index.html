Introduction to East

Kraków, Poland’s second-largest city, is divided into several districts, each with its unique charm. East, located in the Małopolskie region, is a perfect blend of history, art, and contemporary culture. East’s streets are lined with colorful buildings and adorned with intricate details. It is home to some of Kraków’s most renowned museums, historic sites, and quirky galleries. The district’s popularity among tourists can be attributed to its proximity to the city center and easy access to public transportation. Here’s what you need to know before visiting East, Kraków.

Local Culture and Customs of East

Kraków has a rich cultural heritage, and the people here take pride in their traditions. Here are some local customs and habits to keep in mind during your visit to East:

  • Respect religious traditions and customs
  • Don’t drink in public places
  • Dress appropriately while visiting churches and museums
  • Greet locals with a friendly “dzień dobry” (good morning)

Best Time to Visit East

The best time to visit East, Kraków, is between May and September when the weather is warm, and the days are longer. The peak season for tourism is during July and August, so plan your trip accordingly to avoid crowds.
