Introduction to Nowy Sącz

Nestled in Małopolskie, in central Europe, Nowy Sącz is a charming city in Poland that offers a blend of natural beauty, cultural and historical landmarks, and delightful cuisine. Despite being a lesser-known destination, it has a lot to offer visitors, from scenic hiking and biking routes to museums, galleries, and stunning castles.

List of Places to Visit in Nowy Sącz

Here is a list of the top places to visit in Nowy Sącz:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Nowy Sącz Castle
  • Regional Museum
  • Jewish History Museum
  • Cathedral Basilica of the Holy Spirit

Natural Attractions of Nowy Sącz: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Beskid Sądecki Mountains
  • Poprad Landscape Park
  • Czarny Potok Waterfall
  • Sądecki Ethnographic Park

Hiking/Biking Routes of Nowy Sącz

Nowy Sącz is a hiker’s and biker’s paradise, with trails that cater to different levels of difficulty and interests. Some popular routes include:

  • Beskid Sądecki Mountain Range Trail
  • Dunajec River Trail
  • Sądecki Ecomuseum Trail

Best Things to Do in Nowy Sącz: Activities and Tours

Apart from hiking and biking, there are plenty of other fun things to do in Nowy Sącz. Some great options include:

  • Take a guided walking tour of the city
  • Go on a river rafting adventure
  • Experience the local cuisine on a food tour
  • Visit a local vineyard

Week-Long Itinerary for Nowy Sącz

If you have a week to explore Nowy Sącz, here’s a suggested itinerary:

1 Explore Nowy Sącz Old Town and the Nowy Sącz Castle

Food and Drink Scene of Nowy Sącz

Must-Try Dishes

Nowy Sącz is known for its delicious cuisine, heavily influenced by its proximity to the nearby mountains. Here are some local specialties that you shouldn’t miss:

  • Kwaśnica: a sour soup made with sauerkraut, meat, and potatoes
  • Kotlet Schabowy: a Polish-style pork chop served with mashed potatoes and pickled cabbage
  • Pierogi: Polish dumplings filled with a variety of ingredients, from meat to cheese and fruit

Local Beverages

While in Nowy Sącz, don’t miss the chance to try some local beverages:

  • Sądecki regional beer
  • Homemade fruit brandies, known as nalewka
  • Wines produced in the vineyards surrounding Nowy Sącz

Local Culture and Customs of Nowy Sącz

Nowy Sącz has a rich cultural heritage, with strong influences from both Polish and Slovak traditions. Some customs and traditions to be aware of include:

  • Respect for religion, especially during church services
  • Traditional folk dances and music
  • Połomiański Carnival, a carnival celebrated in February

Best Time to Visit Nowy Sącz

The best time to visit Nowy Sącz is during the summer months, from June to August, when the weather is warm and perfect for outdoor activities. The fall season, from September to November, also offers crisp weather and beautiful foliage.

Do’s and Don’ts of Nowy Sącz

Do:

  • Respect local customs and traditions
  • Explore the natural beauty of the region
  • Try local cuisine and beverages

Don’t:

  • Show disrespect to religious sites or customs
  • Litter or damage natural areas
  • Make excessive noise late at night

Safety and Health

Safety in Nowy Sącz

Nowy Sącz is a relatively safe destination, with low levels of crime. However, it’s still important to remain aware of your surroundings and take precautions to keep your belongings safe.

Health in Nowy Sącz

Poland has a high standard of healthcare, and Nowy Sącz has several hospitals and medical facilities. Visitors should ensure they have adequate health insurance coverage before traveling.

Getting to Nowy Sącz: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Nowy Sącz

The closest airport to Nowy Sącz is Kraków John Paul II International Airport, which is approximately 100 km away. From there, you can take a bus or a train to Nowy Sącz.

Bus Stations Near Nowy Sącz

Nowy Sącz has its own bus station, which serves as a hub for regional and international travel. FlixBus and PolskiBus are just some of the companies that operate buses to Nowy Sącz.

Other Options

Train travel is also popular in Poland, and Nowy Sącz has its own train station that connects to other major cities in Poland and beyond.

How to Get Around in Nowy Sącz

Nowy Sącz is a relatively small city, and most destinations can be reached on foot. Taxis and buses are also available, with the latter being the most cost-effective way to travel around the city.

Where to Go Next from Nowy Sącz: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

If you’re ready for more adventures after exploring Nowy Sącz, here are some nearby destinations worth visiting:

  • Zakopane, Poland’s winter sports capital
  • Kraków, a city steeped in history and culture
  • Tatras Mountains, a hiker’s paradise
  • Wieliczka Salt Mine, a UNESCO World Heritage site

Conclusion

Nowy Sącz may be a lesser-known destination, but it has a lot to offer visitors - from stunning natural beauty to rich cultural heritage and exciting adventures. With this guide, you’re equipped to make the most of your trip and to discover all the best things to do in the city.
