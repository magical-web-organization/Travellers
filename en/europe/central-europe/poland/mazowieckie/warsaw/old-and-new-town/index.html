Introduction to Old and New Town

Old and New Town is a historical district located in Warsaw, Poland. It is divided into two parts separated by the Royal Route, which is a street linking St. Anne’s Church in the Old Town with Wilanów Palace in the southern part of the city. Old Town is a UNESCO World Heritage site and one of the most popular tourist attractions in Warsaw. The district is full of beautifully restored buildings, picturesque alleys, and colorful markets that attract visitors from all over the world.

In this comprehensive travel guide, we will explore the history, culture, and beauty of Old and New Town and give you tips on the best things to do, places to stay, and local cuisine to try in this stunning district.

Food and Drink Scene of Old and New Town

One of the best things about visiting Old and New Town is the opportunity to try traditional Polish cuisine. Here are some must-try dishes:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Pierogi (dumplings)
  • Barszcz (beetroot soup)
  • Bigos (hunter’s stew)
  • Kotlet Schabowy (breaded pork cutlet)
  • Makowiec (poppy seed cake)

Local Beverages

  • Żubrówka (vodka infused with bison grass)
  • Piwo (beer)
  • Kompot (fruit juice)

Local Culture and Customs of Old and New Town

Poland has a rich cultural heritage, and Old and New Town is no exception. Here are some interesting customs and traditions to note:

  • Polish hospitality is warm and welcoming.
  • Meals are often served family-style, with everyone sharing dishes.
  • Personal space is important, so it is advisable not to touch or hug strangers.

Best Time to Visit Old and New Town

The best time to visit Old and New Town is during the summer months (June to August) when the weather is warm and sunny. However, this is also peak tourist season, and prices may be higher than other times of the year. Spring (April to May) and fall (September to October) are also good times to visit, with generally mild weather and fewer crowds.

Safety and Health

Safety in Old and New Town

Old and New Town is a very safe area, but it’s always advisable to take precautions. Here are some safety tips:

  • Keep valuables out of sight
  • Be aware of pickpockets and scammers
  • Use licensed taxis or public transportation

Health in Old and New Town

Poland has a good healthcare system, and most pharmacies offer over-the-counter medication. Here are some useful tips:

  • Drink bottled water to avoid stomach problems
  • Wear sunscreen to protect against sunburn
  • Seek medical assistance if necessary
