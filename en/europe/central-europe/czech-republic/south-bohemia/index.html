Introduction to South Bohemia

South Bohemia is a beautiful region located in the southern part of the Czech Republic, in Central Europe. It is known for its stunning landscapes, historic towns, and castles. The region has a rich culture and is home to several UNESCO World Heritage Sites. It is also famous for its beer, which is said to be some of the best in the world. South Bohemia is a perfect destination for those looking to relax, explore, and experience the best of what Czech culture has to offer.

List of Places to Visit in South Bohemia

  • Cesky Krumlov: A UNESCO World Heritage Site and one of the most popular tourist destinations in the Czech Republic.
  • Ceske Budejovice: The capital city of South Bohemia, famous for its beer and historical town square.
  • Trebon: A picturesque town known for its beautiful Renaissance architecture and nearby nature reserve.
  • Hluboka Castle: A stunning chateau located in South Bohemia and a premier attraction for those exploring the region.
  • Tabor: A medieval town known for its well-preserved city walls and underground passages.

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Cesky Krumlov Castle: A must-visit attraction in South Bohemia, the castle offers visitors a glimpse into the country’s rich history.
  • Museum Fotoatelier Seidel: The museum showcases the photographic works of renowned photographer Joseph Seidel.
  • Egon Schiele Art Center: The museum is dedicated to the Austrian painter Egon Schiele and is located in Cesky Krumlov.
  • South Bohemian Museum: The museum showcases the region’s cultural heritage and history.

Natural Attractions of South Bohemia: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Sumava National Park: One of the largest national parks in the Czech Republic, Sumava is home to diverse wildlife and stunning natural scenery.
  • Krizik Fountain: A beautiful fountain located in Ceske Budejovice that offers stunning views of the surrounding area.
  • Lipno Lake: A popular spot for water sports and outdoor activities like cycling and hiking.

Hiking/Biking Routes of South Bohemia

  • Sumava Mountains: A stunning mountain range that offers several trails with varying levels of difficulty, perfect for hikers and bikers alike.
  • Cesky Krumlov to Rozmberk: A scenic route that runs through picturesque towns and lush forests.
  • Pisek to Orlik Castle: A bike route that runs along the Vltava river, with stunning views of the surrounding countryside.

Best Things to Do in South Bohemia: Activities and Tours

  • Beer Tasting Tour: A must-do in South Bohemia, visitors can experience the region’s famous beer culture and sample some of the area’s best brews.
  • River Rafting: For those seeking some adrenaline-pumping adventure, the Vltava river offers a great opportunity for river rafting.
  • Cesky Krumlov Walking Tour: A guided tour that takes visitors through the town’s well-preserved medieval streets, historic landmarks, and hidden corners.

Food and Drink Scene of South Bohemia

Must-Try Dishes

  • Roast pork with dumplings and sauerkraut
  • Traditional Czech goulash
  • Fried cheese
  • Potato soup
  • Trdelník (chimney cake dessert)

Local Beverages

  • Pilsner Urquell: One of the most famous beers in the world, this premium beer originated in the Czech Republic and is a must-try for any beer lover.
  • Budvar: Another famous beer brand that originated in the Czech Republic.
  • Becherovka: A herbal liquor that is a popular drink in the area.

Where to Stay in South Bohemia: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Bellevue Hotel: Located in Cesky Krumlov, this hotel features modern amenities and stunning views of the town and surrounding countryside.
  • Hostel Singer: A budget-friendly option that is centrally located in Cesky Krumlov.
  • Penzion Dvorce: Located in Tabor, this guesthouse features traditional Czech-style accommodation and home-cooked meals.

What to Buy in South Bohemia: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Bohemian glassware: South Bohemia is known for its intricately crafted glassware, which is perfect for souvenirs or gifts.
  • Puppets and marionettes: A popular souvenir in the region, these handmade puppets and marionettes make for unique keepsakes.
  • Hand-painted ceramics: The region is home to many skilled ceramic artists, and visitors can find beautifully painted ceramics in various shops and markets.

Local Culture and Customs of South Bohemia

The people of South Bohemia are known for their hospitality and warmth towards visitors. Czech is the official language of the region, and English is widely spoken in tourist areas. The area has a rich cultural heritage and is known for its music, art, and literature.

Best Time to Visit South Bohemia

The best time to visit South Bohemia is during the summer months from June to August when the weather is warm and pleasant, and outdoor activities are in full swing. Spring (April-May) and fall (September-October) are also good times to visit when the weather is mild, and you can avoid the crowds. Winter (December-February) is the least popular time to visit due to the harsh weather conditions.

Do’s and Don’ts of South Bohemia

Do:

  • Learn a few Czech phrases to help you communicate with the locals.
  • Be respectful of the local customs and culture.
  • Tip appropriately for good service.
  • Try the famous Czech beer and local cuisine.

Don’t:

  • Wear revealing clothing when visiting churches or religious sites.
  • Take pictures of locals without their permission.
  • Eat or drink in public transportation.

Safety and Health

Safety in South Bohemia

South Bohemia is a safe and peaceful region, with low crime rates, making it a great destination for solo travelers and families alike. As with any destination, it is always important to be aware of your surroundings and practice common sense safety precautions, especially when traveling at night or in remote areas.

Health in South Bohemia

The Czech Republic has a well-developed healthcare system with modern facilities and highly trained medical professionals. If you need any medical assistance during your visit, there are several hospitals and clinics in the region.

Getting to South Bohemia: Transportation Options and Logistics

  • Prague Vaclav Havel Airport: The nearest international airport located approximately 180 km from South Bohemia.

Bus Stations Near South Bohemia

  • Ceske Budejovice Bus Station: The main bus station in the region, with connections to major cities in the Czech Republic and Europe.

Other Options

  • Train: The region has several train stations connecting it to other major cities in the country.
  • Car rental: Renting a car is a great option for those looking to explore the region independently.

How to Get Around in South Bohemia

  • Public transportation: There are several bus and train routes connecting the major towns and cities in the region.
  • Bike rentals: Visitors can rent a bike and explore the region’s stunning countryside on your own.
  • Car rental: Renting a car is a great option for those looking to explore the region independently.

Where to Go Next from South Bohemia: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Prague: The capital city of the Czech Republic is located approximately 140 km north of South Bohemia and is easily accessible by train or bus.
  • Vienna: The Austrian capital, Vienna, is located approximately 250 km south of South Bohemia and is accessible by train or car.
  • Brno: The second-largest city in the Czech Republic, Brno, is located approximately 140 km east of South Bohemia and is easily accessible by train or bus.

Conclusion

South Bohemia is a unique and special destination that offers stunning landscapes, rich history, and a vibrant local culture. Whether you’re looking to relax, explore, or experience the best of Czech cuisine and beer culture, South Bohemia is the perfect destination for you.
