Introduction to Sumava

Located in South Bohemia, Czech Republic, Sumava is a stunning mountainous region in Central Europe that offers breathtaking natural views, rich cultural history, and diverse outdoor activities. Sumava is known for its protected forests, glacial lakes, and rivers, making it a popular destination for nature lovers, hikers, and bikers. The region is also known for its diverse wildlife, including brown bears, lynx, wolves, and chamois. This travel guide to Sumava provides a comprehensive overview of everything you need to know about this charming destination.

List of Places to Visit in Sumava

When exploring Sumava, make sure to visit these culturally important attractions and natural wonders:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Cesky Krumlov Castle: a UNESCO World Heritage site that features baroque-style architecture and beautiful gardens.
  • Klatovy catacombs: underground crypts with over 200 mummified bodies, dating back to the 17th century.
  • Prachatice city: a historic city with a picturesque town square and colorful Renaissance and Gothic architecture.

Natural Attractions of Sumava: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Lipno Dam: the largest reservoir in the Czech Republic that offers extensive water sports such as sailing, windsurfing, canoeing and rowing.
  • Boubin Primeval Forest: a protected area with an extensive network of hiking trails and amazing views.
  • Chalupska Slat: a vast fen that is home to rare and endangered plant species.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Sumava

Sumava offers a range of scenic hikes and bike routes, including:

  • Sumava National Park Trail: a 120-km trail that covers various terrain through the forest and across mountains.
  • Boubin Trail: a challenging 19-km trek that goes through the Boubin Forest.
  • Lipno Trail: a 40-km route that passes through the scenic Lipno area.

Best Things to Do in Sumava: Activities and Tours

In addition to hiking and biking, there are several other exciting activities and tours to enjoy in Sumava:

  • Whitewater rafting: navigate the rapids of the Vltava river with experienced guides.
  • Cycling tours: explore Sumava’s mountainous terrain on two wheels with a guided tour.
  • Canoeing and kayaking: discover Sumava’s waterways and stunning landscapes at your own pace.

Week-Long Itinerary for Sumava

Here is a suggested itinerary for a week-long trip to Sumava:

Day 1: Explore Cesky Krumlov Castle and take a leisurely stroll through the town.

Day 2: Take a guided biking tour through the nearby forests and mountains.

Day 3: Visit the Klatovy catacombs and spend the afternoon hiking through the Boubin Forest.

Day 4: Enjoy a day of whitewater rafting on the Vltava river.

Day 5: Relax by the Lipno reservoir and try your hand at watersports.

Day 6: Take a day trip to Prague, the Czech Republic’s capital city.

Day 7: Visit Prachatice and explore the town’s picturesque streets and colorful architecture.

Food and Drink Scene of Sumava

Sumava is known for its hearty and delicious cuisine, which features meat, potatoes, and rich sauces. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages when visiting the region:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Svickova na Smetane: beef sirloin in a creamy sauce served with dumplings.
  • Bramborak: potato pancakes made with grated potatoes, garlic, and onion.
  • Knedliky: soft dumplings made of flour, bread or potatoes that are usually served with gravy.

Local Beverages

  • Kofola: a popular soft drink that is similar to Cola, but with a unique blend of herbs.
  • Becherovka: a liquor made from a secret blend of herbs and spices that has been produced for over 200 years.

Where to Stay in Sumava: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Sumava offers a range of accommodation options to suit all budgets. Here are some of our top picks:

  • Hotel Ruze: a luxurious hotel located in Cesky Krumlov that features an on-site spa and wellness center.
  • U Slona Hostel: a budget-friendly hostel in Cesky Krumlov with cozy rooms and friendly staff.
  • Penzion Pohoda: a charming guesthouse in Prachatice with spacious rooms and a beautiful garden.

What to Buy in Sumava: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

When shopping in Sumava, pick up some of these local products and souvenirs:

  • Šumava cheese: a homemade cheese ideally accompanied with beer or wine.
  • Glassware: Zlatá Koruna and Klatovy have glass factories still operating.
  • Traditional crafts: Šumava is famous for woodworking, weaving, and pottery

Local Culture and Customs of Sumava

When visiting Sumava, it’s essential to respect the local culture and customs. Here are some things to keep in mind:

  • Czech is the official language of Sumava, but many people speak English.
  • Despite its rich history and culture, remember Sumava is still a rural area so don’t be surprised to see farmers and wildlife coexisting.

Best Time to Visit Sumava

The best time to visit Sumava is from June to September, which is the region’s summer months. During this time, you’ll enjoy warm temperatures, long sunny days, and a vibrant atmosphere. However, fall colors are excellent too, with picturesque landscapes, and cheaper accommodation. Winter is not recommended due to cold weather and snow can deteriorate some roads and trails.

Do’s and Don’ts of Sumava

Here are some do’s and don’ts to keep in mind when visiting Sumava:

Do:

  • Respect the environment and wildlife.
  • Try the local cuisine and beverages.

Don’t:

  • Litter or damage the natural habitats and protected forests.
  • Scare wildlife or touch anything inside catacombs or museums.

Safety and Health

Safety in Sumava

Sumava is generally a safe destination, but please keep these things in mind:

  • Stay on designated trails when hiking and biking.
  • Be aware of ticks in the forest.
  • Keep safe distances from the wildlife, especially bears and wolves.

Health in Sumava

No special vaccines or health precautions are necessary when traveling to Sumava. However, ticks and mosquitoes are present in the region, so it’s essential to protect against insect bites.

Getting to Sumava: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Sumava

The closest international airports to Sumava are:

  • Václav Havel Airport Prague
  • Linz, Blue Danube Airport

Bus Stations Near Sumava

There are several bus stations in the region, including:

  • Český Krumlov Bus Station
  • Prachatice Bus Station

Other Options

Driving to Sumava is an option, with many available car rentals at nearby airports. A small railway line services in the area, but it is limited.

How to Get Around in Sumava

The best way to get around Sumava is by car. However, several bike rental companies operate in the region, offering affordable bike rental options. Public transportation options, such as buses, are also available.

Where to Go Next from Sumava: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

There are many nearby destinations and excursions you can take from Sumava, including:

  • Prague: the capital city of Czech Republic, just a few hours’ drive away.
  • Vienna, Austria: a couple of hours’ drive and enjoy the beautiful Austrian capital.
  • Linz, Austria: a great walking city with high-quality museums and galleries.

Conclusion

Sumava is a beautiful region that offers a range of experiences for every kind of traveler. From hiking and biking to cultural attractions, delicious food, and stunning landscapes, Sumava is well worth a visit. With this comprehensive travel guide to Sumava in hand, you’re all set to enjoy an unforgettable trip to this charming part of Central Europe!
