Introduction to České Budějovice

Located in South Bohemia, in the Czech Republic, České Budějovice is a picturesque city with a rich history and a bustling cultural scene. Surrounded by rolling green hills, the city is known for its stunning architecture, charming streets, and friendly locals. Visitors can explore the many museums, historical sites, and natural attractions that make České Budějovice a top destination for travelers.

List of Places to Visit in České Budějovice

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Black Tower: Climb the 72-meter tall tower for amazing views of the city and its surroundings.
  • Budweiser Budvar Brewery: Learn how beer is made at one of the world’s most famous breweries.
  • Přemysl Otakar II Square: Admire the stunning architecture of the buildings surrounding the square and take a selfie with the famous Samson Fountain.
  • České Budějovice Museum: Discover the history of the city through exhibits featuring artifacts and works of art from the area.
  • St. Nicholas Cathedral: Marvel at the towering spires and ornate interior of this beautiful Baroque church.

Natural Attractions of České Budějovice: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Borovany Hill: Hike to the top of this hill for incredible panoramic views of the city and the surrounding countryside.
  • Pískovna Hlinsko: This beautiful lake is perfect for swimming, fishing, and picnicking on a sunny day.
  • Motel Na Prádle: Rent a bike or take a leisurely stroll through this scenic park and the nearby forests.

Hiking/Biking Routes of České Budějovice

  • Budějovická Plynová kolo: This bike path follows the Vltava River for over 20km and offers stunning scenic views.
  • Borovany Hill: Hike to the top of this hill for incredible panoramic views of the city and the surrounding countryside.
  • Pískovna Hlinsko: This beautiful lake is perfect for swimming, fishing, and picnicking on a sunny day.

Best Things to Do in České Budějovice: Activities and Tours

  • Budweiser Budvar Brewery Tour: Learn how beer is made and taste some of the finest brews around.
  • Boat Tour on the Vltava River: Take a relaxing tour down the river and admire the city from a unique perspective.
  • Black Tower Climb: Climb the 72-meter tall tower for amazing views of the city and its surroundings.
  • Horse-drawn Carriage Rides: Take a romantic ride through the city streets in an old-fashioned carriage.
  • Paddle-Boarding on Vltava: Take a paddle-board out on the river for an adventurous way to explore the city.

Week-Long Itinerary for České Budějovice

Day Activities
1 Explore the city center, visit Přemysl Otakar II Square, and take a brewery tour.
2 Visit the Black Tower, St. Nicholas Cathedral, and the České Budějovice Museum.
3 Go for a bike ride along the Budějovická Plynová kolo trail, have a picnic at Pískovna Hlinsko, and do paddle-boarding on Vltava.
4 Take a relaxing boat tour down the Vltava River, enjoy the rest of the day’s to-do at parks along the river.
5 Go for a horse-drawn carriage ride through the city streets of České Budějovice, visit the city’s unique shops and boutiques.
6 Rent a bike and explore the forests of Motel Na Prádle, have a swim and recharge yourself.
7 Visit the Borovany Hill, hike to the top of the hill, and enjoy stunning scenic views of the city and the surrounding countryside.

Food and Drink Scene of České Budějovice

Must-Try Dishes

  • Knedlo-vepřo-zelo: Traditional Czech dish of pork with dumplings and sauerkraut.
  • Smažený Sýr: Czech fried cheese, a popular snack food in the Czech Republic.
  • České Budějovice Roast Duck: One of the best-rated local dishes – slow-roasted duck with garlic, onions, and red wine.
  • Chlebicky: Small sandwiches with various toppings, a popular snack food in the Czech Republic.

Local Beverages

  • Budweiser Budvar Beer: Try one of the world’s most famous beers, created in České Budějovice.
  • Becherovka: A herbal liqueur made in Karlovy Vary, a popular after-dinner drink in the Czech Republic.

Where to Stay in České Budějovice: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Hotel Gomel: A modern and comfortable hotel located in the city center.
  • Pension U Tří Sedláků: A small and cozy guesthouse in a historic building.
  • Hostel Singer: A cheap and cheerful budget hostel in the heart of the city.

What to Buy in České Budějovice: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Budweiser Budvar Beer: Bring some of the famous beer back home with you.
  • Gingerbread: Handmade gingerbread cookies are a traditional Czech treat.
  • Glass products: České Budějovice is famous for its beautiful crystal glassware.

Local Culture and Customs of České Budějovice

  • Czechs are very proud of their country’s rich history and cultural heritage.
  • Tipping is common in restaurants and cafes in the Czech Republic, usually around 10% of the total bill.
  • Czechs are generally reserved and polite, but may appear unfriendly to visitors at first.

Best Time to Visit České Budějovice

The best time to visit České Budějovice is during the summer months from June to August when the weather is warm and the city is full of life.

Do’s and Don’ts of České Budějovice

Do:

  • Try a pint of Budweiser Budvar beer.
  • Explore the city center on foot or by bike.
  • Take a brewery tour.
  • Try some traditional Czech food.

Don’t:

  • Don’t forget to try the local gingerbread.
  • Don’t be afraid to ask locals for directions or recommendations.
  • Don’t be too loud or rowdy in public areas.

Safety and Health

Safety in České Budějovice

České Budějovice is a safe city, but like anywhere in the Czech Republic, visitors should be aware of pickpockets and keep an eye on their belongings.

Health in České Budějovice

There are no significant health risks when visiting České Budějovice. It is recommended to have travel health insurance and seek medical attention if needed.

Getting to České Budějovice: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near České Budějovice

  • Václav Havel Airport Prague: The largest airport in the Czech Republic, is 150km from České Budějovice.
  • Linz Airport: In Austria, is about 100km away from České Budějovice.

Bus Stations Near České Budějovice

  • České Budějovice Central Bus Station: A major bus hub in the city, with connections to other Czech destinations, as well as international destinations such as Austria, Germany, and Slovakia.

Other Options

  • Train Travel: There are regular trains from major cities such as Prague, Vienna, and Munich that connect to České Budějovice.

How to Get Around in České Budějovice

České Budějovice is a compact city and is easy to get around on foot. Alternatively, renting a bike is a great way to explore more of the city and its surroundings.

Where to Go Next from České Budějovice: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Český Krumlov: A beautiful medieval city with a stunning castle, located approx. 30km from České Budějovice.
  • Hluboka Castle: One of the most photographed castles in the Czech Republic, located in Hluboka nad Vltavou, approx. 10km from České Budějovice.

Conclusion

České Budějovice is a charming and vibrant city that has something to offer for every kind of traveler. From its rich historical sites to its stunning natural scenery and diverse cultural scene, České Budějovice is a must-visit destination for anyone planning a trip to the Czech Republic.
