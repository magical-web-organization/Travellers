Introduction to Třeboň

Třeboň is a picturesque town located in South Bohemia, Czech Republic. Known for its charming cobblestone streets, colorful buildings and historical landmarks, Třeboň is a popular tourist destination for those looking to experience Czech culture and the beauty of Central Europe.

The town’s history dates back to the 12th century, when it was established as a royal hunting preserve. Today, Třeboň is a UNESCO biosphere reserve and a mecca for outdoor adventurers due to its vast network of cycling and hiking trails, beautiful lakes and forests. In this comprehensive travel guide, we will highlight the best things to do in Třeboň, as well as where to stay, what to eat and how to get around.

List of Places to Visit in Třeboň

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Třeboň Castle: a stunning Renaissance-style castle built in the 16th century for the wealthy Rosenberg family. The castle includes a museum featuring original period furniture and artwork.
  • Schwarzenberg Tomb: a beautifully crafted tomb, also known as the Schwarzenberg crypt, located next to the Church of St. Gilles. The tomb houses the embalmed bodies of the Schwarzenberg family, a prominent aristocratic family in Central Europe.

Natural Attractions of Třeboň: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Lake Svět: the largest natural lake in the Czech Republic, and a UNESCO protected site. Visitors can rent boats or bikes to explore the area.
  • Třeboňsky Rosarium: a beautiful rose garden in Třeboň, home to over 2,000 varieties of roses.
  • Třeboň fish ponds: an extensive system of man-made fish ponds established in the 14th century. A unique and beautiful ecosystem.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Třeboň

  • Kratochvíle Nature Trail: a beautiful hiking trail through the Kratochvíle Forest, with information boards along the way explaining the unique flora and fauna of the area.
  • Třeboň-Lipno Cycle Route: a 70 km cycle route that passes through stunning forests, meadows and picturesque villages.

Best Things to Do in Třeboň: Activities and Tours

  • Třeboň Brewery Tour: a tour of the local brewery with the opportunity to try some of the best beer in the region.
  • Třeboň Spa: a spa that offers a range of treatments, including massages, facials and hydrotherapy.
  • Třeboň Castle Ghost Tour: a guided tour through the castle at night, with tales of the castle’s ghosts and paranormal activity.

Week-Long Itinerary for Třeboň

Day 1 Arrival and check-in at hotel, explore the old town, dine at a local restaurant
Day 2 Visit Třeboň Castle and Museum, followed by a tour of the Schwarzenberg Tomb
Day 3 Hiking along the Kratochvíle Nature Trail, followed by a visit to the Třeboňsky Rosarium
Day 4 Cycling along the Třeboň-Lipno Cycle Route
Day 5 Relax at the Třeboň Spa and participate in some of the offered treatments
Day 6 Visit the Třeboň Brewery and try their famous beer, followed by a trip to Lake Svět
Day 7 Departure day.

Food and Drink Scene of Třeboň

Must-Try Dishes

  • Carp: a traditional Czech dish often served fried, roasted or in a soup. The fish is caught fresh from the Třeboň fish ponds and is a must-try when visiting Třeboň.
  • Svíčková na smetaně: beef sirloin with a creamy vegetable sauce served with dumplings and cranberry jam.

Local Beverages

  • Třeboň beer: the town is home to several breweries, including the 500-year-old Regent brewery. Visitors can sample the beer at local pubs and restaurants.

Where to Stay in Třeboň: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Hotel Zlatá Hvězda: a historic hotel located in the center of Třeboň, offering comfortable rooms and on-site dining.
  • Penzion U Zámku: a popular guesthouse located in a historic building, just steps from Třeboň Castle.
  • Hostel Travellers: a budget-friendly hostel located in the old town, offering dormitory rooms and private rooms.

What to Buy in Třeboň: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Carp products: including smoked carp, carp pâté and carp sausages.
  • Local honey: made from bees that feed on the flowers around Třeboň.
  • Bohemia Crystal: high-quality handcrafted crystal that is a famous product of the region.

Local Culture and Customs of Třeboň

  • Czechs are known for their beer-drinking culture, and Třeboň is no exception. Visitors can enjoy a cold beer at local pubs or restaurants.
  • Czechs are also known for their love of nature and outdoor activities. Visitors to Třeboň can participate in hiking, cycling, fishing, and other outdoor adventures

Best Time to Visit Třeboň

The best time to visit Třeboň is during the summer months (June-August) when the weather is warm, and outdoor activities are at their peak. Fall is also a great time to visit, with fewer crowds and beautiful autumn foliage.

Do’s and Don’ts of Třeboň

Do:

  • Explore the town’s historic landmarks, including the Třeboň Castle and the Schwarzenberg Tomb.
  • Try the local beer and seafood; it’s outstanding!
  • Rent a bike or boat at Lake Svět and enjoy the stunning scenery.

Don’t:

  • Don’t be noisy or disrespectful towards locals or their customs.
  • Don’t litter anywhere, respect the nature and the local environment.
  • Don’t skip the Třeboň Spa, it’s worth checking out.

Safety and Health

Safety in Třeboň

Třeboň is generally safe, with a low crime rate. Visitors should take standard precautions, such as securing valuables when going out.

Health in Třeboň

Czech Republic offers high-quality medical care, and Třeboň is no exception. The main hospital in the area is the Nemocnice Jindřichův Hradec . Visitors should have travel insurance to cover any unexpected medical costs.

Getting to Třeboň: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Třeboň

The closest airports to Třeboň are Prague’s international airport and Vienna’s international airport. Prague airport is approximately 140 km from Třeboň, and Vienna Airport is approximately 200 km from Třeboň.

Bus Stations Near Třeboň

Třeboň has its own bus station, with connections to the major cities in the region, including České Budějovice and Prague.

Other Options

It’s also possible to rent a car from Prague and make the drive to Třeboň.

How to Get Around in Třeboň

Třeboň is a small town, and most of the main attractions and points of interest are within walking distance of each other. Visitors can also rent a bike to explore the surrounding area more thoroughly.

Where to Go Next from Třeboň: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • České Budějovice: the regional capital is just 24 km away from Třeboň and is home to one of the largest and most recognizable squares in Europe.
  • Krumlov Castle: a stunning castle complex located in the nearby town of Český Krumlov. The castle dates back to the 13th century and has been declared a UNESCO World Heritage site.

Conclusion

Třeboň is an excellent destination for those looking to experience the beauty of Central Europe, immerse themselves in Czech culture, and participate in outdoor adventures. With this comprehensive travel guide, you are now well-placed to enjoy all Třeboň has to offer. From feasting on traditional Czech food and beer, relaxing at a local spa, exploring historic landmarks, cycling and hiking in the beautiful forests and nature reserves, there is something for everyone to enjoy in Třeboň.
