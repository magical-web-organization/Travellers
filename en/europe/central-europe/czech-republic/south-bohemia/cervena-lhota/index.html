Introduction to Červená Lhota

Located in the picturesque region of South Bohemia in the Czech Republic, Červená Lhota is a charming little town that boasts a rich history and a stunning natural landscape. With its centuries-old castle, tranquil lake, and breathtaking countryside views, this town is a gem waiting to be discovered by travelers seeking an off-the-beaten-path destination.

Whether you’re a cultural enthusiast, a nature lover, or an adrenaline junkie, there’s something for everyone in Červená Lhota. In this comprehensive travel guide, we’ll take a closer look at the town’s top attractions, hiking/biking routes, best things to do, food and drink scene, local culture and customs, when to visit, and much more. Let’s dive in!

List of Places to Visit in Červená Lhota

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Červená Lhota Castle - Built in the 14th century, this impressive castle sits on an island surrounded by a man-made lake and is considered one of the most beautiful castles in the country. You can take a guided tour of the castle interior, including the chapel, great hall, and dungeons.

  • Church of St. Andrzej Bobola - A Baroque-style church built in the early 18th century, this religious landmark is famous for its beautiful frescoes and ornate altar.

  • Museum of Czech Puppets and Circus - This unique museum showcases the history and art of Czech puppets and circus performances, including a collection of puppets, costumes, and other artifacts.

Natural Attractions of Červená Lhota: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Červená Lhota Lake - A popular spot for fishing, boating, and picnicking, this tranquil lake offers stunning views of the castle and the surrounding countryside.

  • Blanice River Valley - This scenic valley boasts picturesque hills, forests, and rolling fields, and offers excellent opportunities for hiking, cycling, and horseback riding.

  • Devil’s Stones - These unique rock formations are located just a few kilometers from Červená Lhota and are believed to have mystical powers.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Červená Lhota

  • Blanice River Trail - This 45-kilometer trail runs along the Blanice River and offers stunning views of the surrounding countryside.

  • Červená Lhota Castle Trail - This 10-kilometer trail takes you through the picturesque countryside and leads to the iconic castle.

Best Things to Do in Červená Lhota: Activities and Tours

  • Boat Tour of Červená Lhota Lake - Discover the beauty of the castle and the lake on a boat tour with a local guide.

  • Horseback Riding - Explore the stunning countryside on horseback and enjoy the fresh air and stunning views.

  • Fishing - Anglers can try their luck in the lake or nearby rivers and streams.

Food and Drink Scene of Červená Lhota

Must-Try Dishes

  • Smažený sýr s hranolky - A Czech classic, deep-fried cheese served with French fries.

  • Knedlíky - Dumplings made from bread or potatoes and served with meat, gravy, and sauerkraut.

  • Český guláš - A hearty beef stew with paprika and other spices, served with bread or dumplings.

Local Beverages

  • Beer - Czech Republic is famous for its world-class beer, and Červená Lhota has plenty of options to offer.

  • Slivovice - A strong fruit brandy made from plums.

  • Herb Liquor - An aromatic liquor made from various herbs and spices. Becherovka is a popular brand.

Where to Stay in Červená Lhota: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Chateau Červená Lhota - This historic chateau offers luxurious rooms and apartments with stunning views of the castle and the lake.

  • Penzion - A small guesthouse offering comfortable rooms and local hospitality.

  • Kemp - A family-friendly camping site located near the lake, offering a range of facilities and activities.

What to Buy in Červená Lhota: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Puppets - Take home a unique Czech puppet as a souvenir or gift for loved ones.

  • Crystal Glassware - Czech Republic is famous for its high-quality glassware, and there are many local shops selling beautiful, hand-crafted glass products.

  • Local Honey - Made from wildflowers found in the surrounding countryside, this honey is a delicious and healthy treat.

Local Culture and Customs of Červená Lhota

  • Language - Czech is the official language, but English is widely spoken in tourist areas.

  • Currency - The official currency is the Czech Koruna (CZK).

  • Etiquette - It is polite to say hello and goodbye when entering and leaving a store or restaurant. Tipping 10% is customary in restaurants.

Best Time to Visit Červená Lhota

The best time to visit Červená Lhota is from May to October, when the weather is mild and the countryside is in full bloom. July and August are the busiest months, while spring and autumn offer a quieter experience.

Do’s and Don’ts of Červená Lhota

Do:

  • Explore the castle and the surrounding countryside.

  • Try the local food and beer.

  • Take a boat tour of the lake.

Don’t:

  • Swim in the lake, as it is not permitted.

  • Ignore local customs and manners.

  • Leave trash in public areas.

Safety and Health

Safety in Červená Lhota

Červená Lhota is a safe destination for travelers, with low crime rates and friendly locals. However, you should still take basic precautions such as keeping an eye on your belongings and avoiding poorly lit areas at night.

Health in Červená Lhota

There are no major health risks in Červená Lhota, but it is advisable to have travel insurance and any necessary vaccinations before traveling. In case of an emergency, dial 112 for assistance.

Getting to Červená Lhota: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Červená Lhota

The nearest international airport is Prague Vaclav Havel Airport, located about 150 kilometers north of Červená Lhota. From there, you can take a train or bus to the town.

Bus Stations Near Červená Lhota

There are several bus stations in nearby towns such as Pelhřimov, Jindřichův Hradec, or Tábor that offer regular services to Červená Lhota.

Other Options

If you’re coming from Prague, you can also rent a car and drive to Červená Lhota. The journey takes about 2.5 hours.

How to Get Around in Červená Lhota

The best way to get around Červená Lhota is by foot, as the town is small and easily walkable. You can also rent a bike or a boat to explore the countryside and the lake at your own pace.

Where to Go Next from Červená Lhota: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Tábor - A historic town famous for its Hussite history and beautiful medieval architecture.

  • České Budějovice - Home of the famous Budweiser beer, this lively town offers a vibrant nightlife scene and plenty of cultural attractions.

  • Český Krumlov - One of the most popular destinations in the Czech Republic, this UNESCO-protected town boasts a stunning castle, charming narrow streets, and a picturesque river.

Conclusion

Červená Lhota is a hidden gem waiting to be explored by travelers seeking an authentic, off-the-beaten-path destination. With its stunning castle, tranquil lake, and breathtaking countryside views, this town has something for everyone. From hiking and biking to exploring the local culture and cuisine, there’s no shortage of things to do and see in Červená Lhota. Plan your trip now and discover the beauty of this unique Czech town.
