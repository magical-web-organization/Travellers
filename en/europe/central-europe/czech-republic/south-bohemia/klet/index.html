Introduction to Kleť

Kleť is a small village nestled in the heart of South Bohemia, Czech Republic. It is famous for its ancient observatory atop the Kleť Mountain, which boasts magnificent views of the region and beyond. Whether you are a nature lover, history aficionado, or simply looking for a peaceful escape, Kleť has something for everyone. In this comprehensive travel guide, you will discover the best things to do, places to visit, food to try, and where to stay in Kleť, Czech Republic.

List of Places to Visit in Kleť

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Kleť Observatory: Built in 1957, the Kleť observatory is the oldest mountain-top observatory in Europe. Visitors can take a guided tour and marvel at the telescopes, scientific instruments, and exhibitions showcasing the stars, planets, and galaxies.
  • Zámek Orlík: A magnificent castle perched on a hill overlooking the Vltava River, Zámek Orlík is the summer residence of the Czech president. Visitors can tour the castle, stroll through the gardens, and admire the baroque architecture and antique furnishings.
  • Český Krumlov: A UNESCO World Heritage Site, Český Krumlov is a medieval town located just 30 minutes from Kleť. Visitors can wander the winding cobblestone streets, admire the Gothic and Renaissance buildings, and soak up the charming atmosphere.

Natural Attractions of Kleť: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Kleť Mountain: At 1,084 meters above sea level, Kleť Mountain is the highest peak in the Blanský les mountain range. Visitors can hike to the summit, take the cable car, or climb the north face for a challenge. The views from the top are breathtaking, with panoramas of the Bohemian Forest, Mramorník, and even the Alps on a clear day.
  • Blanský les Nature Reserve: Home to an array of native flora and fauna, the Blanský les Nature Reserve is a must-visit for nature lovers. Visitors can explore the network of hiking trails, spot birds of prey, and admire the stunning natural scenery.
  • Lipno Dam: One of the largest man-made lakes in Central Europe, Lipno Dam offers a wide range of water sports and outdoor activities. Visitors can go swimming, sailing, kayaking, windsurfing, or simply relax on the sandy beach.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Kleť

  • Kleť Mountain Trail: The Kleť Mountain Trail is a challenging hike that takes you up to the summit of Kleť Mountain. The trail is 10 km long and takes around 4 hours to complete. Along the way, you will pass through beech forests, rocky outcrops, and alpine meadows.
  • Blanský les Nature Trail: The Blanský les Nature Trail is an easy hike that takes you through the heart of the Blanský les Nature Reserve. The trail is 4 km long and takes around 1 hour to complete. Along the way, you will see rare plant species, butterflies, and lizards.
  • Vltava Cycle Route: The Vltava Cycle Route is a popular cycling trail that follows the meandering course of the Vltava River. The trail is 270 km long and takes around 5 days to complete. Along the way, you will see charming towns, beautiful castles, and stunning natural scenery.

Best Things to Do in Kleť: Activities and Tours

  • Observatory Tour: Take a guided tour of the Kleť observatory and learn about the stars, planets, and galaxies.
  • Horseback Riding: Explore the countryside on horseback with a guided tour of the Blanský les mountain range.
  • Wine Tasting: Sample local wines and learn about the wine-making traditions of South Bohemia with a wine tasting tour.
  • Skiing: Hit the slopes at the Lipno Ski Resort, which offers a range of ski runs for all skill levels.

Food and Drink Scene of Kleť

Must-Try Dishes

  • Knedlíky: A traditional Czech dish made with dumplings served with a variety of meats and sauces.
  • Vepřová pečeně: Roasted pork served with cabbage and dumplings, a staple of Czech cuisine.
  • Smažený sýr: Breaded and fried cheese served with fries and tartar sauce, a popular pub food.

Local Beverages

  • Pilsner Urquell: The most famous Czech beer, Pilsner Urquell is a light, refreshing lager.
  • Becherovka: A herbal liqueur made with a secret blend of herbs and spices, often served as a digestif.
  • Moravian wine: The South Bohemian region is known for its white wines, including Riesling, Sauvignon Blanc, and Chardonnay.

Where to Stay in Kleť: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Observatory Hotel: A comfortable hotel located at the base of Kleť Mountain, offering panoramic views of the surrounding countryside.
  • Penzion u Studánky: A charming guesthouse in the heart of Kleť village, offering cozy rooms and homely atmosphere.
  • Lipno Lake Resort: A sprawling resort complex located on the shores of Lipno Dam, offering a range of accommodation options and outdoor activities.

What to Buy in Kleť: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Bohemian Crystal: Known for its exquisite craftsmanship and beauty, Bohemian crystal is a popular souvenir among visitors to the region.
  • Traditional Handicrafts: South Bohemia is also famous for its traditional handicrafts, including pottery, woodcarving, and glassblowing.
  • Local Wines: Take home a bottle of Moravian wine, a unique and delicious souvenir of your trip to Kleť.

Local Culture and Customs of Kleť

  • Language: The official language of Kleť is Czech, although many locals also speak English and German.
  • Religion: The majority of the population in South Bohemia is Roman Catholic, with some Protestant and Jewish minorities.
  • Etiquette: When greeting someone, it is polite to shake hands and say “dobrý den.” Tipping is customary in restaurants and cafes, usually around 10% of the total bill.

Best Time to Visit Kleť

The best time to visit Kleť is during the summer months (June-August), when the weather is warm and sunny, and outdoor activities are in full swing. The autumn months (September-November) also offer stunning scenic views and pleasant weather. Winter (December-February) can be cold and snowy, but it is also a great time for skiing and winter sports.

Safety and Health

Safety in Kleť

Kleť is a safe destination for travelers, with low crime rates and friendly locals. However, visitors should take basic precautions to avoid theft and pickpocketing, especially in crowded tourist areas.

Health in Kleť

No specific vaccinations are required for travelers to Kleť, Czech Republic. However, it is always recommended to ensure that your routine vaccinations are up-to-date before traveling. Visitors should also take care to avoid insect bites, especially during the summer months, by wearing long sleeves and pants, using insect repellent, and avoiding outdoor activities during peak mosquito hours.

Getting to Kleť: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Kleť

The nearest airport to Kleť is Vaclav Havel Airport Prague (PRG), which is approximately 120 km away. From the airport, visitors can take a bus or a taxi to reach Kleť.

Bus Stations Near Kleť

Kleť is served by regular bus connections from Ceske Budejovice, the nearest major city. Buses depart hourly and take around 30 minutes to reach Kleť.

Other Options

Visitors can also reach Kleť by car, either by renting a car or hiring a taxi from Ceske Budejovice. The journey takes approximately 40 minutes.

How to Get Around in Kleť

The best way to get around Kleť is by car or by bike, especially if you plan to explore the surrounding countryside and natural reserves. Alternatively, visitors can also use the local bus service, which runs regularly throughout the day.

Where to Go Next from Kleť: Nearby Destinations and Excursions
