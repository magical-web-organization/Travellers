Slavonice is a hidden gem in South Bohemia, Czech Republic, featuring picturesque views, cultural landmarks, outdoor adventures, and delicious cuisine. This guide provides a comprehensive overview of what to expect in Slavonice, including top places to visit, where to stay, and local customs to keep in mind.

Introduction to Slavonice

Slavonice, often referred to as “the pearl of the Renaissance,” is a small town located in the Czech Republic’s South Bohemian region. The town’s history dates back to the 13th century when it rose to prominence as a major trading center. Today, Slavonice is renowned for its exceptional Renaissance architecture.

The town attracts tourists with its well-preserved Renaissance buildings, labyrinthine alleys, historical landmarks, and beautiful natural scenery. With a vast range of cultural and leisure activities, this destination is an ideal spot to unwind and enjoy a perfect vacation experience.

List of Places to Visit in Slavonice

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Slavonice Castle: This medieval castle was built in the 14th century. There are several exhibits inside, including paintings from the Renaissance.
  • Church of St. Wenceslas: This cathedral-style church is known for its Baroque architecture.
  • Mikulske Square: This is the main square in Slavonice and the heart of the old town. Visitors can explore different shops and cafes here, as well as see the well-preserved historical buildings.

Natural Attractions of Slavonice: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Slavonice-Petrovice National Nature Reserve: This is one of the most beautiful natural reserves in Europe, featuring abundant wildlife, majestic landscapes, and breathtaking lookout points.
  • Slavonice Cycling Trail: This 37km cycling trail takes visitors on a picturesque journey through the town’s surroundings, highlighting the local scenery.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Slavonice

  • Retz-Slavonice Trail: This 17 km trail leads visitors through diverse scenery, from rocky cliffs to lush forests and tranquil fields.
  • Krumlov to Trebon Cycle Route: This cycling route has well-marked paths, plenty of restaurants, and takes visitors on a scenic tour of medieval castles and Gothic churches.

Best Things to Do in Slavonice: Activities and Tours

  • Renaissance Tour: A guided tour of the historical sites and beautiful architecture in Slavonice.
  • Horseback Riding: Visitors can enjoy a guided horseback ride through the idyllic landscapes of Slavonice.

Week-Long Itinerary for Slavonice

For a week-long itinerary in Slavonice, visitors can follow this plan:

  • Day 1: Visit Slavonice Castle and Church of St. Wenceslas.
  • Day 2: Explore the natural beauty of Slavonice-Petrovice National Nature Reserve.
  • Day 3: Take a guided horseback ride through the countryside.
  • Day 4: Visit Mikulske Square and try out local cuisine at one of Slavonice’s great restaurants.
  • Day 5: Cycle the Krumlov to Trebon Cycle Route.
  • Day 6: Hike the Retz-Slavonice Trail.
  • Day 7: Explore the surrounding towns of Jindrichuv Hradec and Cesky Krumlov.

Food and Drink Scene of Slavonice

Must-Try Dishes

  • Svickova: This is a traditional Czech beef dish with creamy vegetable puree and bread dumplings.
  • Knedliky: Dumplings made with bread and served as a side with many Czech dishes.
  • Goulash: This hearty soup is prepared with beef, paprika, and vegetables.

Local Beverages

  • Slavonice Beer: This is a popular local brew that visitors can find in most bars and pubs in town.
  • Moravian wine: This type of wine is known for its rich texture and distinctive flavor.

Where to Stay in Slavonice: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Slavonice has a range of accommodation options for tourists of different preferences and budgets. Some of the most popular options include:

  • Renaissance Hotel: A luxurious hotel situated in the heart of Slavonice’s old town.
  • Penzion IVOS: A cozy guesthouse with comfortable rooms and impeccable service.
  • Hostel Slavonice: A budget-friendly option with shared rooms and basic amenities.

What to Buy in Slavonice: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Bangles: These intricately-designed bangles are a timeless souvenir from Slavonice.
  • Wheatgrass Products: Slavonice is known for its wheatgrass skincare and beauty products which are popular among tourists.
  • Handmade Gifts: There are many handmade wooden toys, candles, and pottery in Slavonice shops that make for unique and thoughtful souvenirs.

Local Culture and Customs of Slavonice

In Slavonice, it is customary to greet people with a handshake. Visitors should generally avoid talking loudly in public places, as Czech culture places a high value on politeness and consideration.

Best Time to Visit Slavonice

The best time to visit Slavonice is during the summer season, from May to September. During this time, visitors can enjoy outdoor activities and festivals, as well as the beautiful natural landscapes.

Do’s and Don’ts of Slavonice

Do:

  • Greet locals in a friendly manner.
  • Try out the local cuisine and drinks.
  • Show respect to cultural landmarks and historical sites.

Don’t:

  • Litter in public places.
  • Ignore local customs and traditions.
  • Behave in a disruptive or disrespectful manner.

Safety and Health

Safety in Slavonice

Slavonice is known for its low crime rates and a friendly, welcoming community. Visitors should be cautious when walking or driving, as the town has narrow roads and steep hills.

Health in Slavonice

The water is safe to drink in Slavonice. The town has a range of medical facilities, including a hospital, clinics, and pharmacies.

Getting to Slavonice: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Slavonice

  • Prague Airport
  • Vienna International Airport

Bus Stations Near Slavonice

  • Ceske Budejovice bus station
  • Jindrichuv Hradec bus station

Other Options

Visitors can also rent a car or take a taxi from Prague or Vienna.

How to Get Around in Slavonice

Slavonice is a small town that is best explored on foot. Biking is also a popular option for those who want to see the surroundings of the town.

Where to Go Next from Slavonice: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Cesky Krumlov: A stunning medieval town in the southern Bohemian region.
  • Jindrichuv Hradec: A city located close to the Vajgar Lake and known for its beautiful castle.
  • Telc: A small but charming town with a stunning historical square and castle.

Conclusion

Slavonice is a scenic, culturally-rich, and charming town that offers visitors a memorable vacation experience. With a wide range of activities, attractions, and hidden gems, this destination is worth exploring and discovering.
