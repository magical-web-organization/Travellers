Introduction to Nové Hrady

Nové Hrady is a picturesque town located in the South Bohemian Region of the Czech Republic in Central Europe. The town is famous for its historical sites, natural wonders, and outdoor adventures, making it an ideal destination for travelers from all over the world.

List of Places to Visit in Nové Hrady

Below is a list of attractions and things to do in Nové Hrady.

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Nové Hrady Château: Built in the 14th century, the château is one of the most important historical sites in the town. The château houses a museum that showcases the life and history of the town.
  • Sochy u kostela Sv. Jiljí: The Sochy u kostela Sv. Jiljí is a chapel located near the church of St. Giles. The chapel was built in the 18th century and is adorned with spectacular frescoes and sculptures.
  • Church of St. Giles: The church of St. Giles is a Gothic-style church that dates back to the 14th century. The church has been well-preserved over the years and is a must-visit for architecture enthusiasts.

Natural Attractions of Nové Hrady: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Kraví Hora: Kraví Hora is the highest point in the Nové Hrady area. Visitors can take a hike up to the top to enjoy panoramic views of the town and its surroundings.
  • Mlýnařská strouha: The Mlýnařská strouha is a canal that runs through Nové Hrady. Visitors can take a leisurely walk along the canal and enjoy the beautiful scenery.
  • Špičák: Špičák is a hill located close to Nové Hrady. Visitors can take a hike up to the top to enjoy scenic views of the town and the surrounding countryside.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Nové Hrady

Nové Hrady is a great destination for outdoor enthusiasts, with plenty of trails and routes for hiking and biking. Some of the popular routes include:

  • The Nové Hrady Trail: This 9 km long trail takes you through the town and its surroundings, including the Mlýnařská strouha canal.
  • The Kraví Hora Trail: This 10 km long trail takes you up to Kraví Hora, the highest point in Nové Hrady.
  • The Špičák Trail: This 8 km long trail takes you up to Špičák hill, where you can enjoy spectacular views of the town and its surroundings.

Best Things to Do in Nové Hrady: Activities and Tours

  • Take a guided tour of the Nové Hrady Château: A guided tour of the château is a great way to learn more about the history and culture of the town.
  • Enjoy a picnic at Kraví Hora: Pack a picnic and head up to Kraví Hora to enjoy a relaxing day in the great outdoors.
  • Visit the Nové Hrady Brewery: The Nové Hrady Brewery is one of the oldest breweries in the Czech Republic. Visitors can take a tour of the brewery and enjoy some of the local beers.

Week-Long Itinerary for Nové Hrady

Day 1: Visit the Nové Hrady Château and take a guided tour.

Day 2: Take a hike up to Kraví Hora and enjoy a picnic.

Day 3: Take a leisurely walk along the Mlýnařská strouha canal.

Day 4: Visit the Church of St. Giles.

Day 5: Enjoy a beer at the Nové Hrady Brewery.

Day 6: Take a bike ride on the Nové Hrady Trail.

Day 7: Visit the nearby town of České Budějovice.

Food and Drink Scene of Nové Hrady

Must-Try Dishes

  • Knedlíky: Knedlíky are Czech dumplings that are typically served with meat and gravy.
  • Guláš: Guláš is a traditional Czech dish made with beef, onions, and paprika.
  • Svíčková: Svíčková is a beef dish that is served with cream sauce, cranberries, and dumplings.

Local Beverages

  • Beer: The Nové Hrady Brewery produces a range of local beers that are a must-try for beer enthusiasts.
  • Slivovice: Slivovice is a strong plum brandy that is popular in the Czech Republic.

Where to Stay in Nové Hrady: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Nové Hrady has a range of accommodation options to suit all budgets.

  • Château Nové Hrady Hotel: The Château Nové Hrady Hotel is located within the Nové Hrady Château complex, making it an ideal location for visitors who want to explore the town’s history and culture.
  • Hotel Relax: Hotel Relax is a budget-friendly hotel located in the heart of Nové Hrady.
  • Pension U Pava: Pension U Pava is a guesthouse that offers comfortable accommodation at reasonable prices.

What to Buy in Nové Hrady: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Nové Hrady beer: The Nové Hrady Brewery produces a range of local beers that make great souvenirs.
  • Handmade wooden toys: Nové Hrady is known for its handmade wooden toys that are popular with children and collectors alike.
  • Local honey: Nové Hrady is surrounded by beautiful countryside, making it an ideal location for beekeeping. Local honey is a popular souvenir that visitors can bring home.

Local Culture and Customs of Nové Hrady

The people of Nové Hrady are proud of their history and culture. Visitors are encouraged to respect local customs and traditions, including dressing appropriately when visiting places of worship.

Best Time to Visit Nové Hrady

The best time to visit Nové Hrady is during the summer months (June-August) when the weather is warm and pleasant. However, the town is beautiful throughout the year and visitors can enjoy various seasonal activities and festivities.

Do’s and Don’ts of Nové Hrady

Do:

  • Respect local customs and traditions.
  • Dress appropriately when visiting places of worship.
  • Tip waiters and bartenders.

Don’t:

  • Don’t litter in public areas.
  • Don’t be rude to locals.
  • Don’t drink and drive.

Safety and Health

Safety in Nové Hrady

Nové Hrady is a safe town with low crime rates. However, visitors are advised to take basic precautions and keep their valuables safe.

Health in Nové Hrady

The medical facilities in Nové Hrady are adequate and visitors can expect to receive prompt and efficient treatment at the local hospital.

How to Get Around in Nové Hrady

Nové Hrady is a small town and is best explored on foot or by bike. Rental bikes are available for visitors who want to explore the town on two wheels.

Where to Go Next from Nové Hrady: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • České Budějovice: České Budějovice is a nearby city known for its historic buildings and beautiful town square.
  • Český Krumlov: Český Krumlov is a UNESCO-listed town famous for its well-preserved Renaissance-era architecture.

Conclusion

Nové Hrady is a beautiful town that offers visitors a range of cultural, historical, and natural attractions. Whether you’re an outdoor enthusiast, a history buff, or a foodie, there’s something for everyone in Nové Hrady. Plan your trip today and discover the hidden gems of this charming Czech town.
