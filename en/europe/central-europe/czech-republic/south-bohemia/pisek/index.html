Introduction to Písek

Nestled in South Bohemia, Písek is a picturesque town in the Czech Republic, situated on the banks of the Otava River. The town is famous for its impressive architecture, scenic landscapes, and rich cultural heritage that dates back to the 13th century.

In this comprehensive guide, we will take you through the best places to visit in Písek, including its natural and cultural attractions, hiking and biking routes, the best activities and tours, its food and drink scene, local customs, and traditions.

Food and Drink Scene of Písek

Must-Try Dishes

The traditional Czech cuisine in Písek includes goulash, roasted pork knuckle, beef steak, and schnitzel. Here are our top picks:

  • Svíčková - marinated beef in a cream sauce served with bread dumplings
  • Roast duck with dumplings and red cabbage
  • Smažený sýr - deep-fried cheese served with tartar sauce
  • Pilsner or Budvar beer
  • Becherovka - a traditional Czech herbal liquor

Local Beverages

Apart from the Czech beer and liquor, Písek is also known for its vineyards that produce high-quality wines such as:

  • Chardonnay
  • Riesling
  • Pinot Noir

Local Culture and Customs of Písek

The people of Písek are proud of their rich cultural heritage, which includes the traditional Czech customs. Respect, politeness, and punctuality are highly valued, and often, punctuality is considered a sign of respect.

Best Time to Visit Písek

The best time to visit Písek is from May to September. During this time, you’ll enjoy pleasant weather, indoor and outdoor activities, and lively festivals.

Do’s and Don’ts of Písek

Do:

  • Respect local customs and traditions
  • Try the traditional Czech cuisine and beverages
  • Be friendly and courteous
  • Carry small change

Don’t:

  • Be disrespectful or rude
  • Forget to tip in restaurants or cafes
  • Drink in public places
  • Photograph people without their permission

  • Keep your belongings secure

Getting to Písek: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Písek

The nearest airport to Písek is Vaclav Havel Airport Prague, which is approximately 126 km away. From the airport, you can take a bus or taxi to Písek.

Bus Stations Near Písek

The Písek bus station is located in the center of the town. Several buses run daily to and from nearby towns and cities.

Other Options

If you wish to take a train, Písek has a train station that is well-connected to nearby destinations. You can also rent a car and drive to Písek from Prague or nearby cities.

How to Get Around in Písek

The best way to get around Písek is on foot, as most of the popular attractions are located within walking distance of each other. However, you can also rent a bike or a taxi to explore the town and surrounding areas.
