Introduction to Ostrava

Ostrava is a hidden gem located in the northeastern corner of Czech Republic. This city has a rich industrial history, but it has recently evolved into a vibrant destination with a growing arts and culture scene. Visitors can explore historic landmarks, stunning natural attractions, incredible cuisine, and more. In this travel guide, we will explore the best things to do, see, eat, and experience in Ostrava, so you can make the most of your visit to this fascinating city.

List of Places to Visit in Ostrava

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Ostrava is a city with a rich history. Some of the must-visit museums and historical landmarks in the city include:

The Lower Area of ​​the Vítkovice Ironworks (Dolní oblast Vítkovic)

A UNESCO World Heritage Site, the Lower Area of the Vítkovice Ironworks is a unique industrial monument that tells the story of the city’s industrial past. Visitors can explore preserved factory buildings, blast furnaces, and mines.

The Mining Museum Landek Park

This museum takes visitors deep underground into a real coal mine. It’s a unique opportunity to learn about the mining history of the region.

Silesian Ostrava Castle

The Silesian Ostrava Castle is located in the heart of the city and offers a glimpse into the history of the region. Visitors can explore the castle and its grounds, which include a beautiful park.

The New City Hall Viewing Tower

The New City Hall Viewing Tower is the perfect spot to take in panoramic views of Ostrava and the surrounding areas. It’s a unique vantage point that offers breathtaking views.

Natural Attractions of Ostrava: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Ostrava is home to some stunning natural attractions, including:

The Beskydy Mountains

The Beskydy Mountains are a must-visit destination for outdoor enthusiasts. From hiking and biking to skiing and snowboarding, there are plenty of outdoor adventures to experience in this area.

The City’s Parks

Ostrava has a number of beautiful parks that are perfect for a leisurely stroll or a picnic. Some of the must-visit parks include the Komenského sady and the Černá Louka.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Ostrava

For visitors looking for outdoor adventure, there are plenty of hiking and biking routes in and around Ostrava. Some of the best routes include:

  • Hiking to the top of Mount Lysá hora for stunning views of the Beskydy Mountains.
  • Cycling along the Ostravice River for a scenic riverside ride.
  • Exploring the trails at the Poodří Protected Landscape Area.

Best Things to Do in Ostrava: Activities and Tours

Aside from the museums and natural attractions, there’s plenty to see and do in Ostrava. Some of the best activities and tours include:

  • Joining a guided tour of the city to learn about its history and culture.
  • Exploring the local street art scene.
  • Trying your hand at driving heavy machinery (such as bulldozers and excavators) at the Vítkovice Machinery and Sports Park.

Week-Long Itinerary for Ostrava

For visitors looking to spend a week in Ostrava, we recommend the following itinerary:

  • Day 1: Explore the Lower Area of the Vítkovice Ironworks and learn about the city’s industrial history.
  • Day 2: Take a guided tour of the city and explore some of its hidden gems, such as the street art scene.
  • Day 3: Discover the natural beauty of the Beskydy Mountains with a day hike.
  • Day 4: Learn about the mining history of the region with a visit to the Mining Museum Landek Park.
  • Day 5: Relax at one of Ostrava’s beautiful parks, such as the Komenského sady or the Černá Louka.
  • Day 6: Try your hand at driving heavy machinery at the Vítkovice Machinery and Sports Park.
  • Day 7: Take in panoramic views of the city from the New City Hall Viewing Tower and explore the Silesian Ostrava Castle.

Food and Drink Scene of Ostrava

Ostrava has a rich culinary scene that’s not to be missed. Some of the must-try dishes and local beverages include:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Ostravian dumplings: A traditional Czech dish made with potato dough and served with roasted meat or cabbage.
  • Smažený sýr: A deep-fried cheese dish served with fries.
  • Goulash: A hearty meat stew that’s perfect for colder weather.

Local Beverages

  • Ostravar: A local beer that’s brewed in Ostrava.
  • Kofola: A popular Czech soft drink that’s similar to Coca-Cola.

Where to Stay in Ostrava: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Ostrava has a wide range of accommodation options that cater to every budget. Some of the best hotels, hostels, and guesthouses in the city include:

  • Hotel Jan Maria: A luxurious hotel that’s located in the center of the city.
  • Hostel Ubytování u Arény: An affordable option that’s ideal for backpackers and budget-conscious travelers.
  • Guesthouse Ostrava: A cozy, family-run guesthouse located in the quiet neighborhood of Polanka nad Odrou.

What to Buy in Ostrava: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

When it comes to souvenirs and local products, Ostrava has plenty of options to choose from. Some of the best souvenirs include:

  • Local beer: Ostrava produces some of the best beer in Czech Republic.
  • Handmade crafts: The city is home to a number of talented artisans who create beautiful crafts, such as pottery and glassware.
  • Traditional clothing: Consider picking up a traditional Czech dress or shirt to bring a piece of Ostrava home with you.

Local Culture and Customs of Ostrava

Ostrava has a strong sense of community and an affinity for art, music, and culture. Visitors will find that locals are friendly and welcoming, eager to share their city with visitors.

Best Time to Visit Ostrava

The best time to visit Ostrava is during the summer months (June - August), when the weather is warm and sunny. However, visitors can also enjoy the city during the winter months (December - February), with plenty of opportunities for winter sports and festive events.

Do’s and Don’ts of Ostrava

Do:

  • Try the local beer.
  • Explore the city’s street art scene.
  • Learn about the history of the region with a visit to the Lower Area of the Vítkovice Ironworks.

Don’t:

  • Underestimate the weather. Be prepared for colder temperatures in the winter.
  • Overlook the natural beauty of the region. Make sure to explore the Beskydy Mountains and local parks.

Safety and Health

Safety in Ostrava

Ostrava is a safe city for tourists. Visitors should take standard precautions when traveling, such as staying aware of their surroundings and keeping an eye on their belongings.

Health in Ostrava

Like other parts of Czech Republic, Ostrava has a high-quality healthcare system. Visitors should make sure they have adequate health insurance coverage before traveling to the area.

Getting to Ostrava: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Ostrava

The closest airport to Ostrava is the Leoš Janáček Airport, which is located just outside the city.

Bus Stations Near Ostrava

Ostrava has a number of bus stations that provide easy access to other parts of the country and Europe.

Other Options

Visitors can also travel to Ostrava by train or car.

How to Get Around in Ostrava

Ostrava has an extensive public transportation system that includes buses, trams, and trolleybuses. Visitors can also rent bikes to explore the city.

Where to Go Next from Ostrava: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Ostrava is located in a beautiful region of Czech Republic, and there are plenty of other destinations to explore nearby. Some of the best include:

  • Prague: The capital of Czech Republic is just a few hours away by train or car.
  • Brno: This vibrant city is known for its stunning architecture and wine culture.
  • Olomouc: A charming city with a rich history and beautiful town square.

Conclusion

Ostrava is a hidden gem in Czech Republic that’s worth exploring. With a rich industrial history, stunning natural attractions, and a growing arts and culture scene, visitors will find plenty to keep them busy. Use this travel guide to plan your trip to Ostrava, and make the most of your visit to this fascinating city.
