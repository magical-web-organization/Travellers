Introduction to Prostějov

Prostějov is located in the Olomouc Region of the Czech Republic, in the north of Moravia. It is a city rich in culture, history, and art. Prostějov was founded in the 13th century and has since then become one of the Czech Republic’s most beautiful cities. The city is also known for its thriving textile and clothing industry. Besides, Prostějov has a lot to offer tourists, from its historic monuments and museums to its interesting hikes and outdoor adventures.

List of Places to Visit in Prostějov

Whether you are interested in cultural attractions, hiking/biking routes, scenic views, or outdoor adventures, Prostějov has something for everyone. Here are some top places to visit in the city:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

The Municipal Museum

Located in the former Jesuit College near the town square, the Municipal Museum is a museum that showcases the history of Prostějov and the surrounding area. The museum hosts a large collection of archaeological finds, as well as art and folk culture exhibitions.

Church of St. Nicholas

Built in the 18th century, the Church of St. Nicholas is one of the most important landmarks in the city. The church is famous for its impressive frescoes and Baroque architecture.

Jewish Cemetery

The Jewish Cemetery in Prostějov is one of the most beautiful in Europe and is a must-visit for those interested in Jewish history. The cemetery is home to a number of beautiful gravestones and tombs.

Natural Attractions of Prostějov: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

The Prostějov Reservoir

Ideal for a break from everyday stress, the Prostějov reservoir is a peaceful spot surrounded by beautiful wooded hills. The reservoir is a popular destination for swimming, fishing, and other water-related activities.

The Kostelec Nature Reserve

Located on the southeastern edge of the city, the Kostelec Nature Reserve is a protected area full of natural beauty. The reserve is home to a variety of rare plant species and is a popular destination for nature lovers.

The Havlíčkův Brook Valley

The Havlíčkův Brook Valley is a hidden gem of Prostějov and is a great destination for those looking to escape the city for the day. The area is home to several streams, waterfalls, and beautiful hiking trails that offer stunning scenery at every turn.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Prostějov

The surrounding countryside of Prostějov is abundant with hiking and cycling opportunities, with well-maintained trails and paths. Here are some of the best routes to trek or pedal in this picturesque town:

Local Culture and Customs of Prostějov

Prostějov is a city with a rich cultural history, and the locals are proud of their traditions and customs. Here are a few things to keep in mind:

  • The city has a strong textile and clothing industry, and the locals often dress in fashionable clothes.
  • The locals are friendly and hospitable to visitors.
  • The city is famous for its traditional Moravian embroidery.

Best Time to Visit Prostějov

The best time to visit Prostějov is during the summer months, from June to August, when the weather is warm and sunny. The city also hosts several music festivals and other outdoor events during the summer, making it a fun and vibrant place to visit.

Do’s and Don’ts of Prostějov

Do:

  • Respect the local customs and traditions.
  • Take a guided tour of the Municipal Museum to learn about the city’s rich history.
  • Try the local cuisine.

Don’t:

  • Forget to bring comfortable shoes, as the city has plenty of hiking and cycling opportunities.
  • Visit the Jewish Cemetery during Jewish holidays.

Safety and Health

Safety in Prostějov

Prostějov is a relatively safe city, with little to no violent crime. However, keep an eye out for pickpockets in crowded areas.

Health in Prostějov

Prostějov has several medical facilities that offer standard medical services. The local tap water is safe to drink.

Getting to Prostějov: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Prostějov

The nearest airport to Prostějov is Brno-Tuřany Airport, which is about 60 km away. Other airports nearby include Ostrava Leos Janacek Airport and Prague Václav Havel Airport.

Bus Stations Near Prostějov

Prostějov has a bus station that connects the city with other towns and cities in the region.

Other Options

Prostějov is also accessible by train, with regular connections to Brno and Olomouc.

How to Get Around in Prostějov

The best way to get around Prostějov is on foot or by bike. Taxis and buses are also available, but the city is small enough to explore on foot.
