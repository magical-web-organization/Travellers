Introduction to North Moravia and Silesia

North Moravia and Silesia is a region in Czech Republic that is rich in both culture and nature. Located in Central Europe, this region is known for its beautiful landscapes, towering mountains, and quaint historical towns. It is a popular destination for adventure seekers and history buffs alike, thanks to its wide range of activities and attractions.

The region comprises of two historical provinces- North Moravia and Silesia. North Moravia is famous for its Gothic architecture, wine cellars, and traditional folk music, while Silesia is known for its industrial heritage, wooden architecture, and vast countryside. Together, they make North Moravia and Silesia a dream destination for travelers who want to experience the best of Czech Republic.

List of Places to Visit in North Moravia and Silesia

If you’re planning a trip to this region, here are some must-visit places:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Olomouc: The capital of North Moravia, Olomouc is home to some of the most beautiful Baroque architecture in Europe. Visit the UNESCO-listed Holy Trinity Column, the Astronomical Clock, and the Archbishop’s Palace.
  • Ostrava: A city in Silesia that was once the hub of coal mining and metallurgy, Ostrava has transformed itself into a modern cultural center. Check out the industrial heritage sites, such as Dolni Vitkovice and Landek Park.
  • Opava: Another city in Silesia, Opava has a rich history that dates back to the Middle Ages. Visit the Opava Castle, the Silesian Museum, and the historic city center.

Natural Attractions of North Moravia and Silesia: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Beskydy Mountains: The Beskydy Mountains are located on the border of Czech Republic and Slovakia, and offer spectacular views of the surrounding countryside. Go hiking, skiing, or cycling, and enjoy the fresh mountain air.
  • Jeseniky Mountains: Home to the highest peak in Czech Republic, Praded, the Jeseniky Mountains have over 250km of hiking trails and countless natural wonders such as the Rejviz Nature Reserve and Bila Opava Waterfalls.
  • Moravian Karst: The Moravian Karst is a system of limestone caves and underground rivers that can be explored by boat or on foot. Visit the Macocha Abyss and the Punkva Caves for an unforgettable experience.

Hiking/Biking Routes of North Moravia and Silesia

North Moravia and Silesia is a paradise for hikers and bikers. With its vast countryside, towering peaks, and winding trails, this region offers endless opportunities for outdoor adventures. Here are some of the best hiking and biking routes:

  • Moravian-Silesian Beskydy: The Moravian-Silesian Beskydy is a hiking paradise with over 600km of trails that pass through forests, meadows, and mountaintops. The most popular trails are the Czech-Polish Friendship Trail, the Lysa Hora Trail, and the Vysoka Mountain Trail.
  • Jeseniky Mountains: The Jeseniky Mountains have over 250km of hiking trails that cater to all levels of hikers. The most popular routes include the Praded Trail, the Hugo Trail, and the Cervenohorske Sedlo Trail. For cycling enthusiasts, the Jeseniky Mountains offer the Trans Jeseniky Trail, a 350km route with breathtaking scenery.
  • Poodri: Poodri is a lowland area in North Moravia that is perfect for cycling. The Poodri Cycle Path is a 160km-long route that runs along the Odra River, passing through picturesque villages and historic sites.

Best Things to Do in North Moravia and Silesia: Activities and Tours

In addition to hiking, biking, and exploring the region’s natural wonders, North Moravia and Silesia has plenty of other activities and tours to offer. Here are a few:

  • Wine Tasting: North Moravia is famous for its wine cellars and vineyards. Take a wine tour and taste some of the best wines in Czech Republic.
  • Traditional Folk Music: Experience the vibrant folk music scene of North Moravia by attending a concert, festival, or workshop.
  • Cultural Festivals: North Moravia and Silesia host many cultural events throughout the year, such as the Olomouc Baroque Festival, the World Music Festival in Ostrava, and the Beskydy Fest.

Food and Drink Scene of North Moravia and Silesia

North Moravia and Silesia are known for their hearty cuisine and delicious beverages. Here are some must-try dishes and beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Svickova na smetane: A classic Czech dish made of beef stewed in cream sauce with dumplings and cranberry sauce.
  • Tatarak: A raw beef dish seasoned with onions, egg yolks, and spices, often served with bread and butter.
  • Hanacke kolache: Sweet pastries made with poppy seeds or plum jam, popular in North Moravia.

Local Beverages

  • Beer: Czech Republic is famous for its beer, and North Moravia and Silesia has many breweries that produce delicious brews such as Pilsner Urquell and Radegast.
  • Wine: North Moravia is a wine region that produces high-quality wines such as Rulandské šedé and Veltlínske zelené.
  • Slivovice: A strong fruit brandy made from plums, popular in North Moravia and Silesia.

Where to Stay in North Moravia and Silesia: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

North Moravia and Silesia have a wide range of accommodation options, from luxury hotels and resorts to budget-friendly hostels and guesthouses. Here are a few options:

  • Hotel Palac: A luxury hotel located in the heart of Olomouc, known for its elegant rooms and exceptional service.
  • Penzion St. Florian: A guesthouse in Opava with a cozy atmosphere and affordable prices.
  • Hostel Ostrava: A budget-friendly hostel in Ostrava that offers clean and comfortable dorm rooms and private rooms.

What to Buy in North Moravia and Silesia: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

North Moravia and Silesia have many souvenirs and local products that make great gifts for friends and family back home. Here are a few:

  • Glassware from Moser: Moser is a renowned glassware manufacturer based in Karlovy Vary, known for its beautiful design and high quality.
  • Leather goods from Silesia: Silesia is famous for its leather products such as boots, belts, and wallets, which are handmade by skilled artisans.
  • Krasohled: A traditional candle lantern made of colored glass, often used for decoration in North Moravia.

Local Culture and Customs of North Moravia and Silesia

North Moravia and Silesia have a rich cultural heritage that is deeply rooted in tradition. Here are a few customs and traditions that you might encounter:

  • Folk Music: North Moravia has a vibrant folk music scene, with many festivals and concerts that showcase traditional music from the region.
  • Woodcarving: Silesia is known for its wooden architecture and intricate woodcarvings, which are often used in churches and houses.
  • Christmas Markets: North Moravia and Silesia have some of the best Christmas markets in Czech Republic, where you can buy handmade gifts and decorations.

Best Time to Visit North Moravia and Silesia

The best time to visit North Moravia and Silesia is during the summer months (June-August), when the weather is warm and perfect for outdoor activities and sightseeing. The autumn months (September-November) are also a good time to visit, as the foliage in the mountains is particularly stunning. Winter (December-February) is a great time for winter sports, such as skiing and snowboarding. Spring (March-May) is a quieter time to visit, with fewer crowds and lower prices.

Safety and Health

Safety in North Moravia and Silesia

North Moravia and Silesia are generally safe regions to visit, but it’s always important to take precautions. Here are a few tips:

  • Keep an eye on your belongings in public areas
  • Be cautious when using ATM machines
  • Stay on marked trails when hiking

Health in North Moravia and Silesia

Czech Republic has a good healthcare system, and there are many hospitals and clinics throughout North Moravia and Silesia. Visitors should ensure that they have adequate health insurance before traveling.
