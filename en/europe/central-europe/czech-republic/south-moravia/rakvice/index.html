Introduction to Rakvice

Rakvice is a charming town located in the heart of South Moravia in Czech Republic, famous for its picturesque vineyards, stunning natural scenery, and cultural heritage. The town is surrounded by rolling hills, dominated by the towering White Carpathians, and is crisscrossed by many hiking and biking trails that offer breathtaking views of the surrounding countryside.

Despite being a relatively small town, Rakvice has plenty of attractions and activities on offer to keep visitors of all ages and interests entertained. Whether you are looking to explore the rich cultural heritage of the region, enjoy outdoor adventures, indulge in local cuisine and wine, or simply unwind in a serene setting, Rakvice has something for everyone. In this travel guide, we will take you on a comprehensive tour of Rakvice, highlighting all the must-visit places, best things to do, where to stay, and more.

List of Places to Visit in Rakvice

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Chateau Rakvice: Built in the 18th century, the chateau is a testament to the Baroque architecture of the region. Visitors can explore the grand interior, including a stunning chapel, the historic wine cellar and enjoy a guided tour.
  • Church of the Nativity of the Virgin Mary: Located in the town centre, the church is one of the oldest and most important religious buildings in Rakvice, dating back to the 16th century.
  • Museum of agriculture: This museum displays items from the history of agriculture near the Czech border. The exhibition is located in the local school of Rakvice.

Natural Attractions: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • White Carpathians: A range of mountains situated on the border between the Czech Republic and Slovakia. Visitors can enjoy scenic hikes through the lush forests and meadows, offering panoramic views of the area.
  • Mlýnský Pond: This small lake surrounded by forests is perfect for a quiet and peaceful retreat from the hustle and bustle of the town. It is also a good spot for fishing and bird-watching.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Rakvice

Rakvice has an extensive network of hiking and biking trails that wind through the beautiful landscape of South Moravia. Here are some popular routes that you can explore:

  • Rakvice-Náměšť na Hané: This 32.6 km long bike trail takes you through picturesque countryside and is perfect for nature lovers and cycling enthusiasts.
  • Buchlovice-Hodonin: This hiking trail allows visitors to explore the stunning architectural heritage of the region while taking in the scenic views of the hills and valleys.

Best Things to Do in Rakvice: Activities and Tours

  • Wine Tasting: South Moravia is famous for its wine production, and Rakvice is no exception. Visitors can enjoy tours of wine cellars and taste the famous local wines.
  • Cycling Tours: Rakvice and the surrounding villages provide many cycling routes for visitors to explore the splendid countryside.
  • Horse Riding: The area around Rakvice is a beautiful place to go horse-riding. Ride through the rolling hills and vineyards on horseback and take in the breathtaking views.

Week-Long Itinerary for Rakvice

Here is a sample itinerary for a weeklong visit to Rakvice:

Day 1: Arrive in Rakvice and explore the town centre. Visit the Church of the Nativity of the Virgin Mary.
Day 2: Take a hiking or cycling tour through the stunning White Carpathians.
Day 3: Explore the stunning Chateau Rakvice and its historic wine cellar. Enjoy wine-tasting at the local vineyards.
Day 4: Visit the Museum of Agriculture and learn about the history of farming in the region. 
Day 5: Go horseback riding and explore the picturesque countryside.
Day 6: Visit Mlýnský Pond, relax and enjoy the serene surroundings. 
Day 7: Depart from Rakvice.

Food and Drink Scene of Rakvice

Must-Try Dishes

  • Moravian smoked meat: A traditional dish of this region, made by smoking marinated beef or pork.
  • Vepřo-knedlo-zelo: A classic Czech dish made with pork roast, dumplings, and sauerkraut.
  • Koláč: A sweet pastry filled with fruit or jam, often served with coffee or tea.

Local Beverages

  • Wine: Rakvice wine is famous for its high quality and distinctive taste. Visitors can enjoy tours of local vineyards and taste the famous local wines.
  • Beer: Czechs are renowned for their great beer, and Rakvice is no exception. Be sure to try the local brews.

Where to Stay in Rakvice: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

There are several accommodation options in Rakvice to suit your needs and preferences:

  • Hotel Rakvice: This hotel is located in the town centre and offers clean and comfortable accommodation with modern amenities.
  • Penzion Rakvice: A guesthouse in the heart of the town, offering cozy and affordable rooms.
  • Chateau Buchlov: A historic chateau located a short drive from Rakvice. It offers luxurious accommodation in elegant surroundings.

What to Buy in Rakvice: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Rakvice Wine: The region is famous for its high-quality wines, and Rakvice has several vineyards that produce award-winning wines. Buy some bottles as souvenirs to take back home.
  • Pottery: The region is well-known for its pottery, and visitors can buy beautifully crafted ceramics and porcelain.
  • Honey: Take home jars of delicious honey from the local apiaries.

Local Culture and Customs of Rakvice

Rakvice has a rich cultural heritage, with several festivals and events throughout the year. Some of the most significant cultural events include:

  • Rakvice Wine Festival: Held in August every year, this festival celebrates the rich wine culture of the region with wine tasting, traditional music, and dance performances.
  • Christmas Markets: Held in December, the markets are a festive and magical experience, complete with handmade crafts, delicious local food, and holiday decorations.

Best Time to Visit Rakvice

The best time to visit Rakvice is during the summer months, from May to September, when the weather is warm and sunny, and the vineyards are lush and green. The wine festival in August is a particularly popular time, attracting visitors from all over the country.

Do’s and Don’ts of Rakvice

Do:

  • Respect local customs and traditions.
  • Try the local wine and cuisine.
  • Visit the historic chateau and vineyards.

Don’t:

  • Litter or damage the local environment.
  • Disturb the local flora and fauna.
  • Ignore safety precautions during outdoor activities.

Safety and Health

Safety in Rakvice

Rakvice is a safe and peaceful town, but visitors should always observe safety precautions, especially during outdoor activities like hiking and biking. It is essential to carry a map and sufficient supplies of water and food when embarking on hiking or cycling tours in the wilderness.

Health in Rakvice

Rakvice has a modern healthcare system, with several medical facilities and pharmacies available. Visitors should ensure that they have adequate travel insurance and necessary medication before embarking on the trip.

Getting to Rakvice: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Rakvice

  • Brno-Tuřany Airport: Located about 40 km away, this is the closest airport to Rakvice.
  • Vienna Airport: About 100 km from Rakvice, Vienna airport offers many international flights in Europe.

Bus Stations Near Rakvice

  • Rakvice: Daily buses from Brno, Lednice, and Breclav.

Other Options

  • Car or bike rental

How to Get Around in Rakvice

The best way to explore Rakvice is on foot or by bike. The town centre is easily navigable on foot, and the flat countryside is perfect for cycling tours.

Where to Go Next from Rakvice: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Brno: The second-largest city in the Czech Republic, famous for its stunning architecture, vibrant cultural scene, and delicious food.
  • Lednice-Valtice Cultural Landscape: A UNESCO World Heritage Site, located about 30 km away from Rakvice. Visitors can explore the stunning gardens, castles, and other architectural wonders.

Conclusion

Rakvice is a hidden gem in South Moravia, offering visitors a peaceful retreat from the busy cities, stunning nature, rich cultural heritage, and delicious local wines and cuisine. Whether you are a history buff, an outdoor enthusiast, or a wine lover, Rakvice has something to offer. Plan your trip today and explore the beauty of this charming town.
