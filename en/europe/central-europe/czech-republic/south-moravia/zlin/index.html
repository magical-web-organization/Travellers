Introduction to Zlín

Nestled in the heart of South Moravia, Zlín is a hidden gem of Central Europe. Known for its rich industrial history, lively cultural scene, and picturesque surroundings, Zlín has something for everyone.

Founded in the late 19th century as a textile manufacturing center, Zlín rose to prominence under the visionary leadership of Tomas Bata, who turned it into one of Europe’s most successful industrial and architectural centers. Today, Zlín is a vibrant city of more than 70,000 people, with a mix of old and new buildings, lush parks, and vibrant cultural scene.

List of Places to Visit in Zlín

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Zlín is a city proud of its cultural heritage. You will find plenty of museums and historic sites in the city. Some of the most important ones are:

  • Tomas Bata Memorial, which is dedicated to the founder of Zlín, and highlights his life, work, and vision for the city and its people.

  • Museum of Southeast Moravia, which showcases the history and culture of South Moravia, including its folk art, traditions, costumes, and handicrafts.

  • Zlín Chateau, which is a fine example of Baroque architecture, and houses a museum of regional history.

  • Zlín Film Festival, a renowned annual film festival that attract filmmakers, actors, and audiences from all over the world.

Natural Attractions of Zlín: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Zlín is surrounded by beautiful countryside. The Beskids Mountains, which are part of the Carpathian range, are ideal for hiking, biking, and skiing. Some of the popular destinations for nature lovers in and around Zlín include:

  • Lesna Chateau, a stunning palace that was once the home of the Bata family, and now serves as a cultural center and popular venue for concerts and exhibitions.

  • Zlín Zoo, home to a large variety of animals from around the world, including lions, tigers, and bears.

  • The Marianske Udoli Nature Reserve, which is a forested valley with a network of hiking trails and scenic viewpoints.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Zlín

Zlín is a great place to explore on foot or two wheels. With its ample green spaces and parks, Zlin is perfect for hiking and walking. There are several trails that you can take to explore the city and the surrounding countryside. Some of the most popular routes include:

  • The Zlín-Beskydy Cycle Route, which takes you from the city center to the Beskids Mountains, passing through beautiful countryside and quaint villages along the way.

  • The Zlín City Tour, which is a self-guided walking tour that covers the highlights of Zlín’s architecture, history, and culture.

Best Things to Do in Zlín: Activities and Tours

Zlín has plenty of activities to keep any traveler busy. Some of them include:

  • Take a walking tour of the city, led by experienced local guides.

  • Rent a bike and go on a cycling trip to the Beskids.

  • Attend a performance at the Zlín Congress Center, which hosts a variety of concerts, plays and other cultural events.

  • Have a picnic in one of Zlín’s many parks, or enjoy a coffee on one of its many terraces.

Week-Long Itinerary for Zlín

Day 1: Explore the city center, including the Tomáš Baťa Memorial, Zlín Chateau, and Republic Square.

Day 2: Visit the Museum of Southeast Moravia, and learn about the history and culture of the region. Later in the day, take a stroll in one of Zlín’s parks.

Day 3: Take the Zlín-Beskydy Cycle Route, and explore the Beskids Mountains.

Day 4: Visit Lesna Chateau, and enjoy a day trip to the nearby town of Kromeriz, which is famous for its UNESCO-listed Baroque gardens.

Day 5: Take a guided walking tour of the city, and explore its hidden nooks and crannies.

Day 6: Hike in the Marianske Udoli Nature Reserve, and take in the beautiful views of the surrounding countryside.

Day 7: Indulge in some of Zlín’s local cuisine, and shop for souvenirs at the local markets before departing.

## Food and Drink Scene of Zlín

Zlín is well known for its hearty cuisine, delicious beer, and sweet dessert wines. Be sure to try some of these local specialties:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Svickova, a traditional Czech dish of beef in cream sauce, served with dumplings and cranberry sauce.

  • Moravian sparrow, a roasted pork dish typically served with sauerkraut and bread dumplings.

  • Kyselica, a soup made of sauerkraut, meat, and potatoes.

  • Zlín rolls, a sweet pastry filled with jam and whipped cream.

Local Beverages

  • Zljn, a light, refreshing beer that is popular among locals and visitors alike.

  • Moravian wine, which is a sweet dessert wine made from local grapes such as Riesling or Gewurztraminer.

Where to Stay in Zlín: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Zlín has a variety of accommodation options to suit every budget and taste, from luxurious hotels to cozy guesthouses and hostels. Some of the most popular options include:

  • Hotel Baltaci Zlín, a 4-star hotel located in the city center.

  • Interhotel Moskva, which was built in the 1920s by Tomas Bata as a hotel for his guests.

  • Hostel Lípa, a budget-friendly option for backpackers and solo travelers.

  • Penzion Vévoda, which is a charming guesthouse located in a quiet residential neighborhood.

What to Buy in Zlín: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Zlín has a variety of shops and markets where you can find local handicrafts and souvenirs. Some of the most popular ones include:

  • Zlín Market, which is held every Saturday and is a great place to buy fresh produce, handicrafts, and souvenirs.

  • Gallery Sysel, which is a popular art gallery that showcases the work of local artists and craftsmen.

  • Bata Store Museum, which is dedicated to the history of the Bata shoemaking empire and sells vintage Bata shoes and accessories.

Local Culture and Customs of Zlín

Zlín is a city rich in culture and tradition. The local people are proud of their heritage, and you will find plenty of opportunities to experience it firsthand. Some of the most important cultural events and customs of Zlín include:

  • Zlín Film Festival, which is a renowned international film festival that takes place every September.

  • St. Nicholas Day, which is celebrated on December 6th and involves the arrival of St. Nicholas and his helpers, who give presents to children.

  • The Easter Market, which is held in the city center and offers a variety of handmade gifts and treats for the holiday.

Best Time to Visit Zlín

The best time to visit Zlín is between May and September, when the weather is warm and sunny, and there are plenty of outdoor activities to enjoy. The winters in Zlín are cold and snowy, but still offer opportunities for winter sports such as skiing and ice-skating.

Do’s and Don’ts of Zlín

Do:

  • Greet people with a friendly “dobrý den” (good day) or “ahoj” (hi).

  • Try some of the local cuisine and beer.

  • Respect the local customs and traditions.

Don’t:

  • Don’t litter or damage public property.

  • Don’t photograph people without their permission.

  • Don’t be loud or disruptive in public places.

Safety and Health

Safety in Zlín

Zlín is a safe city with low crime rates. However, as with any destination, travelers should take necessary precautions to ensure their safety, such as keeping valuables secure and being aware of their surroundings.

Health in Zlín

Zlín has a good healthcare system, with several hospitals and clinics in the area. Visitors should ensure they have adequate health insurance and bring any necessary prescriptions or medications with them.

Getting to Zlín: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Zlín

The nearest major airport to Zlín is Brno-Turany Airport, which is approximately 85 kilometers away. Other airports in the area include Ostrava Leos Janacek Airport (around 100 km away) and Vienna International Airport (around 200 km away).

Bus Stations Near Zlín

Zlín has several bus stations, including Zlín Bus Station and Zlín Main Bus Station, which offer connections to destinations all over the region.

Other Options

If you plan to arrive by car, Zlín is well connected to the Czech motorway and highway network. Train services are also available from major cities such as Prague, Brno, and Ostrava.

How to Get Around in Zlín

Zlín is a compact city that is easy to navigate on foot or by bike. Buses and trolleys are also available for travel around the city and to neighboring towns and villages.

Where to Go Next from Zlín: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Zlín is surrounded by beautiful countryside, charming small towns, and historic landmarks, making it an ideal base for day trips and excursions. Some of the popular places to visit near Zlín include:

  • Kromeriz, a picturesque town famous for its Baroque gardens and castle.

  • Vizovice, a town known for its distilleries and excellent plum brandy.

  • Buchlovice, a scenic town with a beautiful castle and historic gardens.

Conclusion

Zlín is a fascinating city with a rich heritage, beautiful surroundings, and plenty of activities for all ages and tastes. Whether you are interested in history, culture, nature, or simply looking for a relaxing vacation, Zlín has something for everyone.
