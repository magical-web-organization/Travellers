Introduction to South Moravia

Nestled in the southeast of the Czech Republic, South Moravia is a region known for its picturesque vineyards, vibrant culture, and beautiful architecture. The region is blessed with alluring landscapes that display a perfect mix of natural treasures complemented by well-preserved monuments, providing a sophisticated and serene place to explore. From delightful local cuisine to bustling markets, and from lakeside beaches to historic castles, South Moravia is an outstanding jewel that truly encapsulates what Central Europe has to offer.

List of Places to Visit in South Moravia

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

South Moravia is rife with historical and cultural landmarks with deep roots in the region’s past. It’s home to historical castles, chateaus, churches, and museums that offer a glimpse into the region’s rich history. Some of the must-visit cultural attractions include:

  • Lednice-Valtice Cultural Landscape: A UNESCO World Heritage Site that features splendid architecture, enchanting gardens, and a palace glistening with superb artwork.
  • Mikulov Castle: A Baroque-style castle that dates back to the 17th century, standing poised on a hill overlooking Mikulov town.
  • Museum of South Moravia: A fascinating museum that takes you on a journey through the region’s history, culture, and lifestyle.

Natural Attractions of South Moravia: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

South Moravia is a region that offers much more than just museums and historical landmarks. From picturesque landscapes to stunning lakes and parklands, South Moravia is a great spot for experiencing outdoor adventures. Some of the best natural attractions of South Moravia include:

  • Pavlov Hills Protected Landscape Area: A haven for nature lovers comprising of a hilly terrain with gorges, valleys, and meandering rivers.
  • Moravian Karst: A unique natural phenomenon with an extensive network of underground caves.
  • Nové Mlýny Reservoir: A popular destination for water sports such as windsurfing, swimming, and sailing.

Hiking/Biking Routes of South Moravia

South Moravia is a great place to explore on foot or on a bike, with a vast array of enigmatic hiking and biking routes. These routes offer scenic views, immersive nature experiences, and historical sites. Some of the most popular hiking and biking routes in South Moravia include:

  • Podyjí National Park hiking trail
  • Laa an der Thaya – Znojmo biking route
  • Buchlovice biking trail

Best Things to Do in South Moravia: Activities and Tours

South Moravia is filled with fun activities and tours that keep visitors engaged throughout their stay. Some of the best things to do in South Moravia include:

  • Wine tasting tours and visits to local vineyards.
  • Exploring the underground caves of the Moravian Karst.
  • Admiring the stunning gardens and architecture of Lednice-Valtice Cultural Landscape.

Week-Long Itinerary for South Moravia

To make the most of your time in South Moravia, it’s useful to have a week-long itinerary. Here is an ideal itinerary:

  • Day 1: Explore the historic city of Brno and visit the famous Brno Ossuary.
  • Day 2: Take a day trip to Lednice-Valtice Cultural Landscape and see the castle, palaces, and gardens.
  • Day 3: Discover the Moravian Karst caves and enjoy hiking through the trails of Podyjí National Park.
  • Day 4: Relax at the elegant spa town of Luhacovice.
  • Day 5: Explore the Baroque-style Mikulov Castle and the nearby wine cellars of Velke Pavlovice.
  • Day 6: Visit the enchanting chateaus of Slavkov and Buchlovice.
  • Day 7: Enjoy a peaceful day by the banks of Nové Mlýny Reservoir.

Food and Drink Scene of South Moravia

South Moravia is known for its rich and delicious cuisine, and traditional beverages that reflect the region’s cultural influences. Some of the must-try dishes and local beverages include:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Svickova: A classic Czech dish consisting of beef sirloin with cream sauce, dumplings, and cranberry sauce.
  • Palačinky: Similar to crepes, palačinky is a thin, round pastry filled with jam, sweets cream, or topped with fresh fruits.
  • Vepřo-knedlo-zelo: A hearty and filling dish comprising of pork, dumplings, and sauerkraut.

Local Beverages

  • Wine: South Moravia is known for its exceptional wine culture, producing a range of wines from white, to rose and red wines.
  • Pilsner Urquell beer: One of the most renowned Czech beer brands, originated in Pilsen (Plza) City.

Where to Stay in South Moravia: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

South Moravia has a range of accommodations from luxury hotels to affordable guesthouses. Some of the best options include:

  • Barceló Brno Palace: A stunning five-star hotel in the heart of Brno city.
  • Penzion U Mlyna: A charming family-owned guesthouse in Valtice village.
  • Hostel Mitte: A budget-friendly hostel located in the center of Brno.

What to Buy in South Moravia: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

South Moravia is home to many local products that make perfect souvenirs, including:

  • Wine: Take home a bottle (or more!) of the region’s signature wine.
  • Handmade ceramics: Buy ceramics, pottery, or porcelain handmade by local artisans.
  • Traditional folk costumes: You can find traditional Czech apparel and folk costumes in various shops in the region.

Local Culture and Customs of South Moravia

South Moravia has unique customs and traditions that have been passed down through generations. Some of these customs include:

  • The Masopust festival: A carnival-like event that takes place just before Lent.
  • The wine harvest festival: A celebration of the first grape harvest held annually in September.

Best Time to Visit South Moravia

The best time to visit South Moravia is during the summer months, from May to September, when the weather is warm and conducive for outdoor activities. The temperature can range from 20°C to 30°C, making it the perfect time to explore the region’s natural attractions, and enjoy activities like hiking, biking, and swimming.

Do’s and Don’ts of South Moravia

Do:

  • Try the local wine.
  • Respect the customs and traditions of the locals.
  • Learn a few Czech phrases to interact with the locals.

Don’t:

  • Drink and drive
  • Be disrespectful towards historical and cultural landmarks.
  • Leave without trying the local cuisine.

Safety and Health

Safety in South Moravia

South Moravia is a safe region with low levels of crime. However, it’s always advisable to take necessary precautions such as:

  • Avoiding walking alone at night.
  • Using secured transport systems.

Health in South Moravia

South Moravia has a well-functioning healthcare system with quality medical services, including emergency services that are available 24/7. It’s advisable to purchase medical insurance before traveling to South Moravia.

Getting to South Moravia: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near South Moravia

The closest airport to South Moravia is the Brno–Turany international airport (BRQ). However, Prague Airport (PRG) is the largest airport in the Czech Republic and offers more flight options and connectivity.

Bus Stations Near South Moravia

The main bus terminal for South Moravia is Brno central bus station, which offers both local and international bus connections.

Other Options

Alternatively, visitors can arrive in South Moravia by train, with regular train connections from Prague, Vienna, and Bratislava.

How to Get Around in South Moravia

Getting around in South Moravia is relatively easy and convenient. Visitors can use public transport such as buses and trams in Brno city, or rent a car to explore the region at their own pace.

Where to Go Next from South Moravia: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Given its prime location, South Moravia serves as a gateway to a host of other Central European destinations, including:

  • Vienna, Austria: A stunning city known for its Baroque architecture and diverse cultural heritage.
  • Budapest, Hungary: A city with a vibrant art scene, thermal baths, and breathtaking views.
  • Krakow, Poland: A city known for its medieval architecture and significant cultural history.

Conclusion

South Moravia is a region with unique charm and character that embodies everything that Central Europe represents. With a vast array of natural, cultural, and historical landmarks, visitors can expect an exciting and rewarding experience in this part of the Czech Republic. Whether you’re a wine enthusiast or an outdoor enthusiast, South Moravia offers a perfect balance of both worlds that will be sure to satisfy all interests.
