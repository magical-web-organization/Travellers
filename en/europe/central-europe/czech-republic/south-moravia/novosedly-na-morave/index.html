Introduction to Novosedly na Moravě

Novosedly na Moravě is a charming village located in the South Moravian region of the Czech Republic. It is situated about 53 km north of Brno and has a population of approximately 600. The village offers a unique blend of old-world charm, rich culture, and natural beauty, making it an ideal destination for travelers.

List of Places to Visit in Novosedly na Moravě

There are several interesting places to visit in Novosedly na Moravě, or nearby, that will appeal to all kinds of travelers.

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  1. Chapel of St. Anthony of Padua: A beautiful Baroque chapel located in the center of the village, built in the 18th century.
  2. Church of St. Martin: A medieval church dating back to the 15th century.
  3. Castle of Ždánice: A well-preserved Renaissance fortress located in the nearby village of Ždánice, about 7 km away.
  4. Pálava Landscape Protected Area: A UNESCO World Heritage site with numerous castles, ruins, and vineyards.

Natural Attractions of Novosedly na Moravě: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  1. Novosedlicky Rybnik: A picturesque pond that is perfect for swimming.
  2. Moravian Karst: A beautiful limestone formation with over 1100 caves and gorges, located about 35 km away from the village.
  3. Hikes around Pavlov Hills: A scenic hiking route that offers stunning views of the village’s picturesque surroundings.
  4. Protected Landscape Area Podyjí: A protected forested area on the border with Austria with lots of scenic trails to explore.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Novosedly na Moravě

Novosedly na Moravě is surrounded by beautiful natural areas and is an excellent destination for outdoor enthusiasts.

  1. Pálava Hills Hiking Trail: A 54 km hiking trail that leads through rugged limestone hills and picturesque vineyards.
  2. Pálava Wine Trail: A biking trail that takes you through some of the most scenic vineyards of the Pálava region.
  3. Moravian Karst Hiking Route: A 120 km route with scenic views of caves, hiking trails, and bike paths.
  4. Dolní Novoselsko Hiking Trail: A 7 km trail in the forested area with stunning views of Pálava Hills and Novosedly na Moravě.

Best Things to Do in Novosedly na Moravě: Activities and Tours

There is no shortage of exciting things to do in Novosedly na Moravě. Here are some of the best activities and tours:

  1. Wine Tasting Tour: Novosedly na Moravě has a rich history of winemaking, and there are many vineyards in the surrounding areas. Take a wine tour and sample the region’s amazing wines.
  2. Nature Walks: The picturesque countryside in and around Novosedly na Moravě demands exploration. Take a nature walk and see the wildlife up close.
  3. Boating on the Pond: Go boating on the beautiful Novosedlycky Rybnik, which is perfect for swimming and fishing.
  4. Horseback Riding: Take a horseback riding trail through the wooden paths in the village.

Food and Drink Scene of Novosedly na Moravě

If you’re a foodie, you’ll love the options available in Novosedly na Moravě!

Must-Try Dishes

  1. Svičková na smetaně (beef sirloin in cream sauce): a traditional Czech dish served with dumplings
  2. Chlebíčky (open-faced sandwiches): a local sandwich made with rye bread, pickled vegetables, and boiled eggs
  3. Bramboráky (potato pancakes): a crispy potato dish served with sour cream

Local Beverages

The region is famous for its wine, and you must try some of the local varieties. You can also try the locally-brewed beer or the famous Moravian plum brandy.

Local Culture and Customs of Novosedly na Moravě

Czech culture is distinct and has a rich history. Here are some of the customs you should be aware of:

  1. Greetings: It is customary to shake hands with acquaintances on arrival and departure.
  2. Dress code: Dress conservatively when visiting landmarks or religious sites.
  3. Etiquette: Customer service is generally speedy, don’t be discouraged if you feel ignored.

Best Time to Visit Novosedly na Moravě

The best time to visit Novosedly na Moravě is during the summer months (June to August) when the weather is warm. The vineyards are also in full bloom during this time, and it’s the perfect time to hike the Pálava hills or taste the local wine.

Do’s and Don’ts of Novosedly na Moravě

Here are some of the essential dos and don’ts you should be aware of when visiting Novosedly na Moravě:

Do:

  1. Try the Wine: Novosedly na Moravě is renowned for its vineyards that produce some of the best local wines ever made
  2. Use Public Transportation: The region is well-connected via public transportation services, and it is easy to get around
  3. Take a Nature Walk: Exploring the natural beauty of the countryside around the village is a must-do activity in the village

Don’t:

  2. Leave Without Trying the Local Cuisine: Czech food is delicious, so be sure to eat at local restaurants to taste the authentic flavors.
  3. Forget to Tip: It’s standard practice to tip service staff at restaurants and hotels.

Safety and Health

Novosedly na Moravě, like much of the Czech Republic, is a safe and healthy country to visit.

Safety in Novosedly na Moravě

  1. Watch out for Pickpockets: Take care of your belongings, especially in crowded areas.
  2. Follow Traffic Rules: Crossing roads outside of pedestrian crosswalks is illegal and dangerous.
  3. Ask the Locals: Don’t be afraid to reach out to locals for advice on areas to avoid and general safety tips

Health in Novosedly na Moravě

  1. Pack Medications: It’s wise to pack any medications you need before leaving home.
  2. Hydrate: The weather can be hot during the summer months, so it’s vital to stay hydrated.
  3. Visit a Doctor if unwell: In case of illness or injury, visit a doctor at the nearest medical facility.

Getting to Novosedly na Moravě: Transportation Options and Logistics

The village is well-connected via roads and public transportation, making it easy to travel within and to and from the village.

Airports Near Novosedly na Moravě

The nearest international airport to Novosedly na Moravě is Brno-Turany Airport.

Bus Stations Near Novosedly na Moravě

The village has a bus stop, where travelers can get buses from/to nearby cities such as Brno.

Other Options

There is a train station located about 6 km away at the town of Ždánice, where you can get a train to Prague or other parts of the Czech Republic.

How to Get Around in Novosedly na Moravě

Travelers can get around the village on foot, by bike or by using bus services.

Where to Go Next from Novosedly na Moravě: Nearby Destinations and Excursions
