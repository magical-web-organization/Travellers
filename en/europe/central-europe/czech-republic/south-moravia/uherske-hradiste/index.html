Introduction to Uherské Hradiště

Uherské Hradiště is a charming town nestled in the heart of South Moravia, Czech Republic. This hidden gem in Central Europe is known for its rich culture, picturesque architecture, and stunning natural beauty. Steeped in history, Uherské Hradiště is a perfect destination for travelers seeking an off-the-beaten-path experience. In this guide, we will delve deeper into what this town has to offer and how to make the most of your trip.

List of Places to Visit in Uherské Hradiště

Here are the must-visit places in Uherské Hradiště:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Open-Air Museum of Village Architecture – An impressive collection of over 50 traditional Moravian buildings, including a school, church, and pub. Visitors can take a guided tour and learn about the traditional lifestyle and culture of the region.
  • Museum of Moravian Slovakia – A comprehensive display of the town’s history, culture, and folklore. The museum boasts a diverse collection of artifacts, including traditional costumes, tools, and artwork.
  • Chateau Uherské Hradiště – A stunning Baroque palace that served as the residence of the local nobility. Visitors can take a guided tour of the palace, explore the original furnishings and learn about the daily life of the aristocracy.
  • Marian Column – A memorial column erected on the main square to commemorate the victims of the plague epidemic that devastated the town in the 18th century.
  • St. Lawrence Church – A magnificent Gothic church adorned with intricate sculptures and frescoes.

Natural Attractions of Uherské Hradiště: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Vizovice Hills – A beautiful mountain range located just outside the town. Visitors can enjoy hiking, mountain biking, or scenic drives on the winding roads. The hills offer breathtaking views of the surrounding countryside and a chance to explore the unspoiled wilderness.
  • Baťa Canal – A historic canal built in the 1930s to transport raw materials to the Baťa shoe factory. Visitors can take a leisurely boat tour and learn about the canal’s fascinating history. The canal is also a popular spot for fishing and birdwatching.
  • Uherské Hradiště Dam – A man-made lake created by a dam on the River Morava. Visitors can swim, sail, or sunbathe on the beach that surrounds the lake. The area also has several hiking trails and picnic spots.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Uherské Hradiště

Uherské Hradiště is a hiker’s paradise, with numerous trails that cater to all skill levels. Here are some of the top hiking/biking routes in the area:

  • Vizovice Hills Trail – A challenging route that traverses the scenic Vizovice Hills. The trail is 16 km long and features steep inclines and declines, making it suitable for experienced hikers and bikers.
  • Kroměříž-Holešov Trail – This 45 km long route begins in the historic town of Kroměříž and ends in Holešov. The trail winds through picturesque valleys and dense forests, passing by several historic landmarks and natural attractions.
  • Uherské Hradiště-Sv. Hostýn Trail – A moderately challenging 21 km long route that leads to the pilgrimage site of Sv. Hostýn. The trail rewards hikers with stunning views of the surrounding countryside and a chance to explore the local folklore and traditions.

Best Things to Do in Uherské Hradiště: Activities and Tours

Here are some fun and exciting activities to try in Uherské Hradiště:

  • Wine Tasting Tour – South Moravia is famous for its delicious wines, and Uherské Hradiště is no exception. Sign up for a wine tasting tour and sample some of the best local wines.
  • Historic Walking Tour – Take a guided walking tour of the town and learn about its rich history and culture. The tour includes visits to the main landmarks and attractions, as well as hidden gems and secret corners.
  • Traditional Crafts Workshop – Learn how to make traditional handicrafts, such as pottery, embroidery, and woodcarving. Workshops are conducted by local artisans and provide a unique insight into the local culture and traditions.

Week-Long Itinerary for Uherské Hradiště

Here is a suggested itinerary to help you plan your trip to Uherské Hradiště (feel free to modify it to suit your preferences):

Day 1: Arrival in Uherské Hradiště, check-in at your hotel, and explore the town center.

Day 2: Visit the Open-Air Museum of Village Architecture and take a walking tour of the historic center.

Day 3: Hike the Vizovice Hills Trail and enjoy lunch at a local restaurant.

Day 4: Take a boat tour of the Baťa Canal and visit the Museum of Moravian Slovakia.

Day 5: Spend the day at the Uherské Hradiště Dam and enjoy swimming, sunbathing, and hiking.

Day 6: Cycle the Kroměříž-Holešov Trail and explore the historic town of Kroměříž.

Day 7: Take a day trip to the nearby town of Zlín and visit the Tomas Baťa Museum.

Food and Drink Scene of Uherské Hradiště

The local cuisine of Uherské Hradiště is a delightful mix of traditional Czech and Moravian dishes. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Moravian sparrow – A hearty dish made with pork, onions, and caraway seeds, served with bread dumplings and sauerkraut.
  • Štramberské uši – A sweet pastry made with butter, eggs, and sugar, shaped like a pair of ears.
  • Lokše – A thin potato pancake, typically served with smoked meat, cheese, or vegetables.

Local Beverages

  • Moravian wines – Try the local red wines, such as Frankovka or Zweigeltrebe, which pair well with hearty meat dishes.
  • Slivovice – A potent plum brandy that is a popular after-dinner drink.

What to Buy in Uherské Hradiště: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Traditional handicrafts – Handmade pottery, embroidered textiles, and wooden toys make excellent gifts.
  • Trdelník – A sweet pastry similar to a chimney cake, coated with cinnamon, sugar, and nuts.

Local Culture and Customs of Uherské Hradiště

Uherské Hradiště has a rich cultural heritage, with a mix of Czech and Moravian traditions. Here are some local customs and traditions to keep in mind:

  • Folk Festivals – Uherské Hradiště hosts several folk festivals throughout the year, showcasing local music, dance, and folklore.
  • Easter Traditions – The locals decorate Easter eggs using a traditional wax batik technique, and the town holds an Easter egg market where you can buy these colorful eggs.
  • Wine Festivals – South Moravia is renowned for its wine, and Uherské Hradiště holds several wine festivals during the summer months.

Best Time to Visit Uherské Hradiště

The best time to visit Uherské Hradiště is during the summer months (June to August) when the weather is sunny and warm, and the outdoor attractions are in full swing. The town also hosts several festivals and cultural events during this time, providing a glimpse into the local traditions and customs. Spring (March to May) and autumn (September to November) are also excellent times to visit, with milder weather and fewer crowds.

Safety and Health

Uherské Hradiště is a safe town, with low crime rates and friendly locals. However, it is essential to take standard safety precautions, such as keeping your valuables secure and avoiding walking alone at night.

Uherské Hradiště has a good healthcare system, with several hospitals and clinics located in the town and surrounding areas. Visitors should have travel insurance that covers medical emergencies.

Getting to Uherské Hradiště: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Uherské Hradiště

The nearest airport to Uherské Hradiště is Brno-Turany Airport, which is located approximately 50 km away. Alternatively, visitors can fly into Vienna International Airport, which is about 150 km from Uherské Hradiště. From the airport, visitors can take a bus, train, or hire a car to reach the town.

Bus Stations Near Uherské Hradiště

Uherské Hradiště has a central bus station that is well-connected to several major cities in the region, such as Brno, Zlín, and Prague. Visitors can check the bus schedules and purchase tickets online or at the station.

Other Options

How to Get Around in Uherské Hradiště

Uherské Hradiště is a small town that can be easily explored on foot or by bike. Visitors can also use the local bus or taxi service to reach the outskirts of the town or nearby villages.
