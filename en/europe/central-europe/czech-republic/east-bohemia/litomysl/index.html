Introduction to Litomyšl

Litomyšl is a charming town located in East Bohemia, Czech Republic. This hidden gem of Central Europe is renowned for its beautiful castle and scenic old town, making it an ideal destination for history buffs and architecture enthusiasts. Whether you’re looking for a peaceful retreat or a thrilling outdoor adventure, Litomyšl has something for everyone.

List of Places to Visit in Litomyšl

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Litomyšl Castle: This UNESCO World Heritage Site is the town’s most iconic landmark, featuring magnificent Renaissance architecture and beautifully landscaped gardens. Take a guided tour of the castle’s interior to see its impressive collection of historic art and furnishings.

  • Bedřich Smetana Museum: Learn about the life and work of Czech composer Bedřich Smetana at this museum dedicated to his legacy. The museum contains a fascinating collection of personal mementos, musical instruments, and manuscripts.

  • Havlíček Gardens: Take a stroll through these picturesque gardens, located just outside the castle walls. The gardens feature a variety of exotic and native plants, as well as a tranquil pond and plenty of shaded paths.

Natural Attractions of Litomyšl: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Koruna Hill: Hike to the top of Koruna Hill for stunning panoramic views of the surrounding countryside. During the summer months, the hillside is covered in wildflowers, adding to the scenic beauty of the area.

  • Rybník Řeka: This peaceful pond is located just a short walk from the castle and is an ideal spot for fishing or picnicking. Rent a boat and explore the pond’s winding channels or simply sit back and enjoy the scenery.

  • Litomyšl Caves: Explore the underground wonders of Litomyšl, including intricate limestone formations and subterranean pools. Guided tours of the caves are available throughout the year.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Litomyšl

  • Litomyšl - Řetůvka - Litomyšl: This scenic hiking trail takes you through the rolling hills and lush forests surrounding Litomyšl. The trail is approximately 13 km in length and is suitable for all skill levels.

  • Cycling Trail No. 4033: This easy-to-follow cycling route winds its way through the picturesque countryside of East Bohemia, passing through charming villages and rural landscapes.

Best Things to Do in Litomyšl: Activities and Tours

  • Guided Castle Tour: Take a guided tour of Litomyšl Castle to learn about the town’s rich history and admire its exquisite architecture. Tours are available throughout the year.

  • Visit Smetana’s Birthplace: Head to the nearby village of Vysočina to see the birthplace of Bedřich Smetana, the famous Czech composer. The house has been converted into a museum dedicated to Smetana’s life and work.

  • Litomyšl Music Festival: Every year in July, Litomyšl hosts a music festival featuring orchestral, chamber, and vocal music performances. Visitors can enjoy classical music in the atmospheric setting of the castle courtyard.

Food and Drink Scene of Litomyšl

Must-Try Dishes

  • Chlebíčky: These open-faced sandwiches are a Czech specialty and are often served on special occasions.

  • Vepřo-knedlo-zelo: A classic Czech dish made with pork, dumplings, and sauerkraut.

  • Svíčková: Another classic Czech dish, this one made with beef sirloin in a creamy vegetable sauce.

Local Beverages

  • Pilsner Urquell: Litomyšl is located in the heart of Pilsner beer country, so be sure to try a pint of the local brew.

  • Becherovka: This herbal liqueur is a popular digestif in the Czech Republic and is made with a blend of herbs and spices.

Where to Stay in Litomyšl: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Hotel Aplaus: This modern hotel is located just a short walk from the castle and offers comfortable rooms and an on-site restaurant.

  • Penzion Atmosféra: This charming guesthouse is housed in a historic building and offers cozy rooms and a traditional Czech breakfast.

  • Hostel Žižka: This budget-friendly hostel is located in the heart of the old town and offers both private and dormitory-style rooms.

What to Buy in Litomyšl: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Herbal Teas: Litomyšl is known for its herbal teas, which are made from a variety of locally grown herbs and spices.

  • Czech Crystalware: The Czech Republic is famous for its crystalware, and Litomyšl is no exception. Shop for handcrafted glassware and crystal products at local shops and markets.

Local Culture and Customs of Litomyšl

The people of Litomyšl are proud of their cultural heritage and are welcoming to visitors from all over the world. Czech people are generally reserved and polite, and it’s important to remember to dress modestly when visiting churches or other religious sites.

Best Time to Visit Litomyšl

The best time to visit Litomyšl is during the summer months (June to August) when the weather is warm and sunny, and many outdoor events and festivals are held.

Do’s and Don’ts of Litomyšl

Do:

  • Try the local cuisine and beer
  • Respect the local culture and customs
  • Explore the scenic countryside

Don’t:

  • Wear revealing clothing when visiting religious sites
  • Drink and drive

Safety and Health

Safety in Litomyšl

Litomyšl is a safe town, and visitors are unlikely to encounter any serious safety issues. As with any tourist destination, it’s important to keep your valuables secure and to be aware of your surroundings when walking alone at night.

Health in Litomyšl

The healthcare system in the Czech Republic is modern and efficient, and visitors can access medical care at local hospitals and clinics if needed. It’s a good idea to bring a basic first aid kit with you when travelling, and to purchase travel insurance before your trip.

Getting to Litomyšl: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Litomyšl

The nearest international airport is Prague Václav Havel Airport, which is approximately 160 km from Litomyšl. From the airport, visitors can take a taxi or public transportation to Litomyšl.

Bus Stations Near Litomyšl

The main bus station in Litomyšl is located in the town center and serves a variety of destinations throughout the Czech Republic.

Other Options

Visitors can also reach Litomyšl by car or train. The nearest train station is located in nearby Česká Třebová, which is approximately 20 km from Litomyšl.

How to Get Around in Litomyšl

The old town of Litomyšl is easily walkable, and visitors can explore the town’s main attractions on foot. For those who would like to venture further afield, there are local taxi services and bike rentals available.

Where to Go Next from Litomyšl: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Český Krumlov: This fairy-tale town is located approximately 150 km from Litomyšl and is famous for its stunning castle and historic old town.

  • Kutná Hora: This UNESCO World Heritage Site is located approximately 200 km from Litomyšl and is renowned for its medieval architecture and fascinating silver mining history.

Conclusion

Litomyšl is a hidden gem of the Czech Republic, offering visitors a unique blend of history, culture, and natural beauty. Whether you’re looking to explore the town’s rich cultural heritage, indulge in the local cuisine, or simply relax and enjoy the scenic surroundings, Litomyšl is sure to delight and inspire you. Plan your trip today and discover the magic of this charming East Bohemian town.
