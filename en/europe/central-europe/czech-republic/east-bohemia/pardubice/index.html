Introduction to Pardubice

Pardubice is a charming city located in East Bohemia, Czech Republic, and is one of the oldest settlements in the region. It is known for its rich history, Gothic architecture, scenic landscapes, and exciting cultural events. Whether you’re a history buff, nature lover, or seeking a memorable adventure, Pardubice has something for everyone.

This comprehensive travel guide will take you through the best places to visit, things to do, and get valuable tips for your trip to Pardubice.

List of Places to Visit in Pardubice

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Pernstejnovy square

Pernstejnovy Square is the central square in Pardubice. Its architectural style is primarily Renaissance and Baroque, with the most prominent building being the Green Gate. Other significant sites include the Old City Hall, the Church of St. Bartholomew, and the Historical Museum of Eastern Bohemia, which displays artifacts from the region’s past.

Castle Pardubice

The Castle Pardubice is a striking baroque structure that used to be a renaissance castle. The interiors comprise of exhibitions of paintings and sculptures of Pardubice-born artists.

Church of St. John the Baptist

One of the oldest churches in Pardubice, Church of St. John the Baptist, is an impressive Gothic building that lies in the middle of a spacious baroque churchyard. The interiors showcase the art of medieval blacksmiths.

Natural Attractions of Pardubice: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Kunětická Hora Castle

Nestled on top of a hill, the Kunětická Hora Castle, an exemplar of medieval architecture, offers an enchanting panoramic view of the surrounding countryside. Visitors can also enjoy hiking and other outdoor activities in the neighboring forest.

Green Belt around Pardubice and Pardubice Lakes

Green Belt around Pardubice contains numerous hiking and biking trails which will take you to beautiful and tranquil places around the city. You can explore the picturesque northern banks of the Pardubice Lakes which are famous for their awe-inspiring sunsets and diverse bird species.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Pardubice

Pardubice is a great place for hikers and bikers. With picturesque views, lush green forests, and clean air, this city offers an abundance of trails for hiking and biking enthusiasts. The most popular trails include Stonožka Trail, Green Belt Trail, and the Leukosis Trail which are ideal for active tourists.

Best Things to Do in Pardubice: Activities and Tours

Visit Local Breweries

Pernštejn Brewery, Pivovar Pernštejn, and East Bohemian Brewery are some of the breweries that are famous for producing delicious beer in the Pardubice region. You can take a tour of the breweries where you can learn about the brewing process and sample some of the finest beers.

Horse Racing

Horse racing takes place at the racecourse in Pardubice every year since 1874. The most significant event in the racing calendar is The Great Pardubice Steeplechase, a daring event for horses and jockeys that take place every October.

Week-Long Itinerary for Pardubice

Day Activities
Day 1 Visit Pardubice Castle and the Green Gate, Pernstejnovy Square, and the Church of St. John the Baptist.
Day 2 Start your day with hiking on the Leukosis Trail, explore the Kunětická Hora Castle and end the day with dinner along with Pardubice Lakes.
Day 3 Experience the racing thrills first-hand by attending a horse race at the Pardubice racecourse.
Day 4 Learn about the production of Pardubice’s famous beer by visiting the Pernštejn Brewery, Pivovar Pernštejn, and East Bohemian Brewery
Day 5 Visit the Historical Museum of Eastern Bohemia that has an impressive collection of artifacts from the region’s past.
Day 6 Relax and explore the scenic beauty of the Green Belt around Pardubice.
Day 7 End your trip by visiting some of Pardubice’s souvenir shops, where you can find authentic local products to take home.

Food and Drink Scene of Pardubice

Must-Try Dishes

There are several traditional dishes in Pardubice that you must try while you’re here. Some of the popular ones include:

Pardubice gingerbread

A spiced, sweet, and delicately flavored gingerbread made from a blend of honey, flour, and ginger. You can taste this famous gingerbread and purchase these products from any of the local souvenir shops.

Buchteln

Buchteln is a sweet, yeast-based pastry filled with jam coated with butter and topped with powdered sugar. It’s a perfect dessert for your visit to Pardubice.

Local Beverages

The Pardubice region is known for producing some of the finest beers. Don’t forget to experience the region’s beer scene by sampling some of the best beers from local breweries such as Pivovar Pernštejn, East Bohemian Brewery, and Pernštejn Brewery.

Where to Stay in Pardubice: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

There are plenty of options for accommodations in Pardubice, ranging from budget hotels to luxury hotels. Some of the popular hotels include Hotel Euro, Hotel Atrium, and Hotel Zlatá Štika. For backpackers and budget-conscious travelers, you can find affordable options in hostels such as Hostel Špalíček Pardubice, Hostel Ex and Hostel Prima that offer comfortable rooms and amenities. Guesthouses like Penzion Zahrádka and Penzion Steak House offer a comfortable stay combined with mouth-watering local dishes.

What to Buy in Pardubice: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

When you’re visiting Pardubice, you can find authentic local products in souvenir shops. Traditional products include Pardubice gingerbread, beer mugs, pottery, and the Pardubice horseracing memorabilia.

Local Culture and Customs of Pardubice

The residents of Pardubice retain a close connection to the city’s history and culture and have a deep appreciation for nature. Visitors are encouraged to exhibit good manners and respect the city’s cultural heritage and traditions.

Best Time to Visit Pardubice

Pardubice is a year-round destination, but the ideal time to visit is in the spring and summer, when the weather is warm, and outdoor activities are in full swing. October is another popular time to visit, particularly for the Great Pardubice Steeplechase horse race.

Do’s and Don’ts of Pardubice

Do:

  • Dress appropriately when visiting churches and other religious sites.
  • Try the local beer.
  • Respect the local customs and traditions.

Don’t:

  • Don’t litter.
  • Don’t consume alcohol in public places.
  • Don’t ignore the city’s rich history and culture.

Safety and Health

Safety in Pardubice

Pardubice is generally a safe city, and violent crime is rare. However, it’s always wise to take usual precautions such as being aware of your surroundings and keeping your valuables secure.

Health in Pardubice

The health facilities in Pardubice are top-notch, and there are many hospitals and clinics located throughout the city. The tap water is safe for drinking, and there are no major health risks to be concerned about.

Getting to Pardubice: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Pardubice

The nearest airport to Pardubice is the Pardubice Airport, which has regular flights to several European destinations.

Bus Stations Near Pardubice

Pardubice bus station is located outside the city center and has many regional and international bus services.

Other Options

You can reach Pardubice via train. The train station is located in the city center and offers connections to other major cities in the Czech Republic and Europe.

How to Get Around in Pardubice

Public transportation in Pardubice is reliable and efficient, making it easy to get around. The city has an extensive network of buses and trams that connect most areas of the city. Taxis are also available at reasonable rates.

Where to Go Next from Pardubice: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Some nearby destinations and excursions from Pardubice include:

  • The city of Hradec Králové.

Conclusion

Pardubice is a city with an exciting history, natural beauty, and cultural events that attract visitors from all over the world.
