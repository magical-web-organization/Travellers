Introduction to Kutná Hora

Kutná Hora is a small town located in Central Bohemia, in Czech Republic, in Central Europe. It is a place with a rich history that is visible in its beautiful architecture, museums, and landmarks. The town is famous for its silver mines which served as the main source of wealth for the country in the past. Today, Kutná Hora is a UNESCO World Heritage Site and a popular tourist destination.

List of Places to Visit in Kutná Hora

If you are planning to visit Kutná Hora, here’s a list of must-see attractions that you shouldn’t miss:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Saint Barbara’s Church
  • Sedlec Ossuary
  • Italian Court
  • Church of the Assumption of Our Lady and Saint John the Baptist
  • Royal Mint

These landmarks of the past era are famous for their magnificent architecture, intricate designs, and historical significance. They offer a glimpse into the town’s past where you can learn about its culture, art, and architecture.

Natural Attractions of Kutná Hora: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Hradek Hill Park
  • Vysoka Park
  • Divci Kamen Lookout Point

For those who love nature and adventure, these places are some of the best attractions to visit in Kutná Hora. You can take a leisurely walk or hike to these parks and enjoy the spectacular views and fresh air that they offer.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Kutná Hora

Kutná Hora is surrounded by picturesque countryside and hiking/biking trails that are ideal for adventure enthusiasts. Some of the popular trails include:

  • Kutná Hora to Sedlec via Hřebeny
  • Kutná Hora to Žleby via Žižkův Hill
  • Kutná Hora to Čížová via Hůrka

These trails offer stunning views of the local flora and fauna and take you through the heart of the Czech countryside.

Best Things to Do in Kutná Hora: Activities and Tours

Apart from hiking and sightseeing, Kutná Hora offers many other activities such as:

  • Silver mines tours
  • Chocolate making workshops
  • Beer tastings tours
  • Segway tours

These activities are a great way to explore the town’s cultural heritage and have some fun too.

Food and Drink Scene of Kutná Hora

Kutná Hora offers a wide range of local delicacies and beverages that you must try during your visit:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Roast pork with dumplings and sauerkraut
  • Potato soup with forest mushrooms
  • Moravian sparrow

These dishes offer a taste of the local Czech cuisine that has remained unchanged for centuries.

Local Beverages

  • Czech beer
  • Slivovice (plum brandy)
  • Becherovka (herbal liqueur)

These are some of the best locally produced alcoholic beverages you can enjoy while in Kutná Hora.

What to Buy in Kutná Hora: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Kutná Hora offers a range of souvenirs and local products that make for great gifts and keepsakes for your loved ones. Some of the best things to buy in the town include:

These products are unique and add a touch of Czech culture to your home décor.

Local Culture and Customs of Kutná Hora

While visiting Kutná Hora, it is important to respect the local culture and customs. The Czechs value their traditions and etiquette. It is polite to use basic greetings like “Dobrý den” (Good day) and “Děkuji” (Thank you). The Czechs also have a rich food culture where meal times are often used to socialize with family and friends.

Best Time to Visit Kutná Hora

The best time to visit Kutná Hora is during the summer months (June to August) when the weather is warm and pleasant. However, the town is beautiful throughout the year, and winter months offer unique festivities and Christmas markets.

Do’s and Don’ts of Kutná Hora

Here are a few Do’s and Don’ts to keep in mind while visiting Kutná Hora:

Do:

  • Respect the local culture and customs
  • Visit the tourist attractions during the early hours to avoid the crowds
  • Try the local cuisine and drinks

Don’t:

  • Use loud voices or be disrespectful in public
  • Take pictures without permission
  • Litter or damage the town’s heritage sites

Safety and Health

It is safe to visit Kutná Hora, and the crime rate is low. However, it is always wise to stay vigilant and take precautions against pickpocketing and theft. In terms of health, the town has several clinics and medical service providers in case of emergencies.

How to Get Around in Kutná Hora

The best way to get around Kutná Hora is on foot since most of the town’s attractions are located close to each other. However, there are also local buses, taxis, and bike rentals available.

So, plan your visit to this UNESCO World Heritage site and take in all that Kutná Hora has to offer.
