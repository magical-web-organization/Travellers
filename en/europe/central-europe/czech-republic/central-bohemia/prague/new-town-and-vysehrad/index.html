Introduction to New Town and Vysehrad

Located in Prague, the capital city of Czech Republic, New Town and Vysehrad is a charming and historic district that offers a perfect blend of modernity and cultural heritage. New Town is a relatively newer area, built during the 14th century, while Vysehrad dates back to the 10th century and is home to some of the oldest and most architecturally significant buildings in Prague. Together, they offer a unique travel experience that combines history, culture, and modern amenities.

List of Places to Visit in New Town and Vysehrad

If you are planning to visit New Town and Vysehrad, there are several places that you must include in your itinerary. Here are some of our top picks:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Vysehrad Fort: Built in the 10th century, this ancient fort is now a popular tourist attraction that provides stunning views of the city.
  • Charles Square: One of the largest squares in the world, Charles Square features several important landmarks, including the New Town Hall and the Baroque-style St. Ignatius Church.
  • Havlicek Gardens: A beautiful park that features walking trails, fountains, and a beautiful orchard.
  • Church of St. Peter and St. Paul: An impressive neo-gothic church that boasts intricate stained glass windows and a striking interior.

Natural Attractions of New Town and Vysehrad: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Vysehrad Cemetery: Located on a hilltop, this historic cemetery is the final resting place of many famous Czech personalities, including Antonin Dvorak and Alfons Mucha.
  • Stromovka Park: This massive park is perfect for a leisurely stroll or a picnic, with stunning views of the Vltava River.
  • Vltava River Cruise: Take a cruise on the river to enjoy the scenic beauty of the city. Several companies offer cruises that include dinner and drinks.

Hiking/Biking Routes of New Town and Vysehrad

If you are an adventure lover, consider exploring the hiking and biking trails in New Town and Vysehrad. Here are some of our top picks:

  • Petrin Hill: A popular hiking spot that features panoramic views of the city from the top.
  • Biking through the Old Town: Explore the historic streets of Prague on a two-wheeler.
  • Hiking in Letna Park: This scenic park is perfect for a leisurely hike, with several trails to choose from.

Best Things to Do in New Town and Vysehrad: Activities and Tours

From sightseeing to adventure activities, there are several things to do in New Town and Vysehrad that cater to different interests. Some of our recommendations include:

  • Prague Castle Tour: Take a tour of the stunning Prague Castle, the largest castle complex in the world.
  • Beer Tasting Tour: Explore the local beer scene with a guided tasting tour that takes you to some of the best breweries in the city.
  • Hot Air Balloon Ride: For a truly unique experience, take a hot air balloon ride and enjoy stunning views of the city from above.

Week-Long Itinerary for New Town and Vysehrad

If you are planning to spend a week in New Town and Vysehrad, here is a sample itinerary that covers all the major attractions:

  • Day 1: Explore the Charles Square and St. Ignatius Church, followed by a visit to the Náprstek Museum of Asian, African, and American Cultures.
  • Day 2: Take a Prague Castle Tour, followed by a leisurely walk in the romantic gardens of Petrin Hill.
  • Day 3: Visit the Church of St. Peter and St. Paul in Vysehrad, followed by a walk through the Vysehrad Fortress.
  • Day 4: Go on a beer tasting tour of the city and experience the local beer culture.
  • Day 5: Take a hot air balloon ride and enjoy stunning views of the city.
  • Day 6: Explore the historic streets of the Old Town on a biking tour.
  • Day 7: Visit the Havlicek Gardens and the ancient Vysehrad Cemetery, before enjoying a cruise on the Vltava River.

Food and Drink Scene of New Town and Vysehrad

When it comes to food and drink, New Town and Vysehrad offer a diverse range of options that cater to different tastes and budgets. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Guláš: This hearty stew made with beef or pork is a local favorite.
  • Trdelník: A sweet treat made with dough, sugar, and cinnamon that is perfect for a quick snack.
  • Smažený sýr: A popular street food item, smažený sýr is a deep-fried cheese patty served with tartar sauce and fries.

Local Beverages

  • Pilsner Urquell: A classic Czech beer that is famous around the world for its distinct taste.
  • Becherovka: A herbal liquor that is a popular digestif after a meal.
  • Kofola: A carbonated soft drink that was developed as an alternative to Coca-Cola during the communist era.

Where to Stay in New Town and Vysehrad: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Hotel Kings Court: A luxury hotel located in the heart of New Town, with elegant rooms and a rooftop spa.
  • A&O Hostel Prague: A budget-friendly hostel that offers comfortable dormitory rooms and private rooms.
  • The Emblem Hotel: A boutique hotel located near the Charles Square that features stylish rooms and a rooftop terrace.

What to Buy in New Town and Vysehrad: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

If you want to bring home some authentic Czech souvenirs, here are some suggestions:

  • Bohemian Glassware: Fine glassware has been a Czech tradition for centuries, and you can find beautiful pieces to take home.
  • Marionettes: Prague is famous for its puppet theatre, and you can find lovely marionettes to add to your collection.
  • Garnet Jewelry: Bohemian garnets are renowned for their dark red color and are a popular souvenir item in Prague.

Local Culture and Customs of New Town and Vysehrad

Czechs are proud of their cultural heritage and value politeness and hospitality. It is customary to greet people with a handshake and maintain eye contact while speaking. When dining out, tipping 10% of the total bill is common practice, and it is polite to say “děkuji” (thank you) when receiving change.

Best Time to Visit New Town and Vysehrad

The best time to visit New Town and Vysehrad is during the spring and summer months (April to September), when the weather is pleasant and several outdoor activities are possible. The fall months (October to November) are also a good time to visit, as the scenery is beautiful with autumn foliage. The winter months (December to March) can be very cold, but the festive period of Christmas and New Year’s Eve is delightful.

Do’s and Don’ts of New Town and Vysehrad

When visiting New Town and Vysehrad, here are some do’s and don’ts to keep in mind:

Do:

  • Try the local cuisine: Czech cuisine is delicious and varied, and it is worth trying some of the local dishes during your visit.
  • Visit the historic sites: New Town and Vysehrad are full of interesting historical landmarks that are worth a visit.
  • Use public transportation: Prague has an excellent public transportation system that is efficient and affordable.

Don’t:

  • Drink tap water: It is recommended that you avoid drinking tap water in Prague, as it may be unsafe for consumption.
  • Be disrespectful in churches: When visiting churches, be respectful of the religious customs and avoid taking photographs where it is prohibited.
  • Strip naked in public: Public nudity is illegal in Prague, and offenders may face serious legal consequences.

Safety and Health

Safety in New Town and Vysehrad

Prague is a relatively safe city, but it is always important to a be vigilant and take basic precautions while traveling. Here are some tips to stay safe in New Town and Vysehrad:

  • Avoid isolated areas: Avoid walking alone in isolated areas, especially at night.
  • Watch your belongings: Keep your valuables such as cash, passport, and travel documents safe, and avoid carrying too much cash on you.
  • Use registered taxis: If you need to take a taxi, make sure it is a registered taxi with a yellow roof sign and meter.

Health in New Town and Vysehrad

Prague has a good healthcare system, and most major hospitals have English-speaking doctors. However, it is always advisable to have travel insurance when traveling to a foreign country. It is also recommended that you take necessary precautions to avoid common illnesses like influenza and gastric problems by maintaining proper hygiene, drinking bottled water, and avoiding street food.

Getting to New Town and Vysehrad: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near New Town and Vysehrad

The Vaclav Havel Airport is located 13 km from New Town and Vysehrad and is the main airport that serves Prague.

Bus Stations Near New Town and Vysehrad

Florenc Bus Station, which is the main bus station in Prague, is located 5 km from New Town and Vysehrad.

Other Options

Other transportation options include train, tram, and taxi. Prague has an efficient public transportation system that includes buses, trams, and metro. Taxis are available throughout the city, but it is important to ensure that the taxi is a registered one with a yellow roof sign and meter.

How to Get Around in New Town and Vysehrad

The best way to explore New Town and Vysehrad is on foot, as most of the attractions are within easy walking distance. If you need to travel further, there are several options available, including public transportation, taxis, and bike rentals.

Where to Go Next from New Town and Vysehrad: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Prague is centrally located, making it easy to explore nearby destinations and excursions. Here are some nearby destinations that you might want to explore:

  • Karlovy Vary: A historic spa town located about 120 km from Prague, famous for its thermal springs and stunning architecture.
  • Cesky Krumlov: A UNESCO World Heritage site that is located about 170 km from Prague, boasting a stunning castle and a picturesque old town.
  • Kutna Hora: A charming town located about 80 km from Prague, renowned for its Gothic churches and historic architecture.

Conclusion

New Town and Vysehrad are two of the must-visit districts in Prague, offering a unique blend of culture, history, and modernity. From exploring remarkable historic sites to enjoying nature at its best, New Town and Vysehrad offer a travel experience that caters to diverse interests. Be sure to follow our tips, and you are sure to enjoy an unforgettable trip to this charming district of Prague.
