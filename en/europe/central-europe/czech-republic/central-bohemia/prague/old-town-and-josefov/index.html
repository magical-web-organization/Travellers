Introduction to Old Town and Josefov

Old Town and Josefov are two of the most popular districts in Prague, the capital city of the Czech Republic. As the name suggests, Old Town is the oldest part of Prague, dating back to the 9th century, while Josefov, the Jewish quarter, has a rich history that reflects the country’s Jewish heritage. Today, both districts are popular tourist destinations, offering visitors a perfect blend of history, culture, and entertainment.

Local Culture and Customs of Old Town and Josefov

Here are some interesting customs and cultural traditions in Old Town and Josefov:

  • Czechs greet each other with a kiss on the cheek or a handshake.
  • Tipping is customary in restaurants, cafes, and bars.
  • In the Jewish quarter, it’s customary to remove your hat when visiting a synagogue or a Jewish cemetery.

Best Time to Visit Old Town and Josefov

The best time to visit Old Town and Josefov is during the shoulder seasons of spring (March to May) and fall (September to November). The weather is mild and less crowded than during the peak summer season.

Do’s and Don’ts of Old Town and Josefov

Do:

  • Try the Czech cuisine and beer.
  • Take a guided tour of the Jewish Museum.
  • Visit Prague Castle and St. Vitus Cathedral.
  • Explore Petrin Hill.

Don’t:

  • Take photos inside the synagogues or the Jewish Cemetery without permission.
  • Leave your valuables unattended in public places.
  • Overstay your visa.

Safety and Health

Safety in Old Town and Josefov

Old Town and Josefov are generally safe for tourists, but visitors should be vigilant about pickpockets and petty thefts, especially in crowded tourist areas.

Health in Old Town and Josefov

The tap water is safe to drink in Prague, and there are no major health hazards. Visitors should have adequate travel insurance and be aware of emergency medical facilities.
