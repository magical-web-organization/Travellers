Discovering the East Bank of Vltava: A Comprehensive Travel Guide

Are you planning a trip to the charming and historic city of Prague in Czech Republic? Then, make sure to include the east bank of Vltava in your itinerary. This part of Prague may not be as popular as the west bank, but it offers a lot to discover especially for travelers who love cultural attractions, scenic views, and outdoor adventures. In this comprehensive travel guide, we will take you on a virtual journey to the east bank of Vltava. You will learn about the must-visit places, best things to do, where to stay, what to eat, and more. Are you ready? Let’s go!

Introduction to East bank of Vltava

The east bank of Vltava is one of the two major parts of Prague divided by the Vltava River. It consists of several districts including Vyšehrad, Karlín, and Žižkov. The east bank is known for its rich cultural landmarks, lush parks, and stunning panoramic views of the city. Some of the iconic attractions in this part of Prague include the National Monument at Vítkov, the Church of Our Lady of the Snows, and the Vyšehrad Citadel.

List of Places to Visit in East bank of Vltava

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • National Monument at Vítkov: This impressive monument is dedicated to the memory of Czechoslovakian legionaries and Czech Army soldiers who fought for freedom and independence of Czech Republic. It is also home to the Military Museum and Memorial of National Heroes.
  • Church of Our Lady of the Snows: This beautiful Baroque-style church is one of the oldest religious sites in Prague. It is said to have been built in the 10th century by Prince Boleslaus II and was a center of missionary work for the Czech Republic.
  • Vyšehrad Citadel: Perched high on a hill overlooking the Vltava River, this historic fortress is a must-visit attraction in Prague. It was founded in the 10th century and was the seat of Czech princes and kings. Today, it is a popular venue for concerts, festivals, and other cultural events.

Natural Attractions of East bank of Vltava: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Rieger Gardens: This beautiful park is located in the Karlín district of Prague. It is a perfect place to unwind, relax and enjoy the green scenery. The park features well-manicured gardens, walking trails, and sports facilities.
  • Slavín: This hill is famous for its stunning panoramic views of Prague. It is a popular spot for tourists and locals alike to take photos and enjoy the scenic vistas. There is also a vineyard on the hillside where you can sample local wines.
  • Folimanka Park: Located in the Vinohrady district, this park is a great place for hiking, jogging, or just strolling. It is also home to a small lake where you can go fishing.

Hiking/Biking Routes of East bank of Vltava

  • The Vltava Riverside Walk: This scenic trail runs along the east bank of the Vltava River and offers breathtaking views of the city. It is a popular route for jogging, walking, or cycling.
  • Žižkov Hill Loop: This loop trail starts at the Žižkov TV Tower and follows a scenic route through the neighborhoods of Žižkov and Vinohrady. It is a challenging route that rewards you with stunning views of the city.

Best Things to Do in East bank of Vltava: Activities and Tours

  • Beer Tasting Tour: Prague is known for its delicious beers and the east bank has some of the best breweries. Take a tour of the local breweries and taste the different types of Czech beers.
  • Boat Cruise on the Vltava River: Explore the city from a different perspective by taking a boat cruise on the Vltava River. You will get to see the famous landmarks of Prague from the water.
  • Visiting Local Farmers’ Market: Experience the local culture by visiting a farmers’ market in Prague. Here you will find an array of fresh produce, handmade crafts, and souvenirs.

Food and Drink Scene of East bank of Vltava

Must-Try Dishes

  • Goulash: A traditional Czech dish made with beef, onions, and paprika, and usually served with bread or dumplings.
  • Svíčková: Another Czech classic made with sirloin beef in a cream sauce, served with dumplings and cranberry sauce.
  • Trdelník: A sweet pastry made from rolled dough, grilled over an open flame, and sprinkled with sugar and cinnamon.

Local Beverages

  • Pilsner Urquell: The most popular Czech beer, it has a light frothy consistency and a distinct flavor.
  • Becherovka: A Czech herbal liqueur made from a secret blend of herbs and spices, it is often served as a digestif.

Where to Stay in East bank of Vltava: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Vinohrady: This district has a variety of accommodation options ranging from hotels to apartments. It is a popular area for tourists because it is close to the city center.
  • Karlín: This district also has many accommodation options and is known for its trendy cafes and restaurants.

What to Buy in East bank of Vltava: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Bohemian Crystal: Prague is famous for its exquisite crystal products such as figurines, vases, and glasses.
  • Marionettes: These are wooden puppets that are traditional to Czech culture. They come in different sizes and designs.
  • Absinthe: A green alcoholic drink that is said to have originated in Czech Republic. It is made from wormwood and other herbs.

Local Culture and Customs of East bank of Vltava

  • Dress Code: Czechs generally dress conservatively. It is always best to dress modestly when visiting religious sites.
  • Tipping: Tipping is normal in Czech Republic. A 10% tip is standard in restaurants and cafes.
  • Public Transport: The public transport in Prague is excellent and inexpensive. Make sure to validate your ticket before boarding.

Best Time to Visit East bank of Vltava

The best time to visit the east bank of Vltava is during the shoulder seasons of spring and autumn. The weather is mild, and the crowds are smaller than during the summer months. However, if you want to experience the popular cultural events and festivals, then summer is the best time to go.

Do’s and Don’ts of East bank of Vltava

Do:

  • Respect the local culture and customs.
  • Explore the hidden gems of the city.
  • Take a boat cruise on the Vltava River.

Don’t:

  • Litter in public places.
  • Take photographs of locals without their permission.
  • Ignore the dress code when visiting religious sites.

Safety and Health

Safety in East bank of Vltava

Prague is a safe city, but like any other major city, you should take precautions to protect yourself and your belongings. Be aware of pickpockets, especially in crowded areas. Do not leave your valuables unattended in hotels or restaurants.

Health in East bank of Vltava

The standard of healthcare in Czech Republic is high. If you need emergency medical assistance, dial 112. Make sure to have adequate travel insurance before your trip.

Getting to East bank of Vltava: Transportation Options and Logistics

The easiest way to get to the east bank of Vltava is by flying into Prague’s international airport, Václav Havel Airport Prague. From there, you can take a taxi, or public transport to your accommodation. The public transport network in Prague is excellent and is the cheapest and most convenient way to get around the city.

Other Options

  • Train: Prague has several train stations where you can catch trains to other cities in Czech Republic and Europe.
  • Car Hire: If you want to explore the countryside and smaller towns around Prague, hiring a car is a good option.

How to Get Around in East bank of Vltava

The best way to get around the east bank of Vltava is by using the Prague public transport network. You can purchase tickets at metro stations, tram and bus stops, and newsagents. There are also taxi and bike rental services available.

Where to Go Next from East bank of Vltava: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Karlštejn Castle: A magnificent Gothic castle that dates back to the 14th century. It is located just outside of Prague and is a popular day trip destination.
  • Kutná Hora: A UNESCO World Heritage Site and a fascinating town located about 70 km east of Prague. It is known for its stunning architecture, the Sedlec Ossuary or the Bone Church, and the Saint Barbara’s Church.

Conclusion

The east bank of Vltava has much to offer both first-time and repeat visitors to Prague. From historical landmarks, lush parks, scenic views, to delicious food and drink, there is something for every type of traveler. We hope this comprehensive travel guide has inspired you to explore the east bank of Vltava on your next visit to Prague. Happy travels!
