Introduction to Prague-East District

Located in the Central Bohemia region of the Czech Republic, Prague-East District is a serene and peaceful destination that offers an ideal mix of natural and cultural attractions. The district is home to charming towns, picturesque landscapes, and a rich cultural heritage that draws tourists from all over the world. From castles to museums, hiking and biking routes, outdoor adventures, and local food scene, there’s something for everyone in Prague-East District.

List of Places to Visit in Prague-East District

There are numerous attractions in Prague-East District. Here are some of the must-visit destinations:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Kutná Hora: Also known as the “City of Silver,” Kutná Hora is a town that’s situated approximately 50 km east of Prague. The town is full of historical and cultural sites, including the St. Barbara’s Church, the Italian Court, and the Sedlec Ossuary, which is a chapel decorated with human bones.

  • Chateau Žleby: This 13th-century chateau boasts a mix of Gothic and Renaissance architecture, situated approximately 90 km east of Prague. The chateau features a collection of paintings, tapestries, furniture, and antique weapons.

  • Kolín: The town of Kolín is situated approximately 58 km east of Prague. The town has a long history, dating back to the 10th century when it served as an important trade center. The town’s main attraction is the Gothic St. Bartholomew Church.

Natural Attractions of Prague-East District: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Sázava River: The Sázava River is popular with kayakers and canoeists, offering a beautiful backdrop of lush forests and picturesque landscapes.

  • Bohemian-Moravian Highlands: Located in the northeastern part of Prague-East District, Bohemian-Moravian highlands feature rolling hills, valleys, and offers some of the most spectacular views in the region.

  • Kokorín Valley: The Kokorín Valley provides a perfect mix of stunning scenery and outdoor adventures like hiking and biking trails. The valley is home to Kokorín Castle, one of the oldest castles in the region.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Prague-East District

Prague-East District is a haven for hikers and bikers, with a wide range of trails that cater to all skill levels. Some of the best hiking/biking trails include:

  • Kokořínsko Natural Reserve: The reserve features several hiking trails that lead through forests, meadows, and hills, offering beautiful views of the countryside.

  • Sázava River Trail: This trail follows the course of the Sázava River, offering some of the most beautiful views in the region. The trail covers a distance of approximately 45 km and is suitable for both hiking and biking.

  • Bohemian-Moravian Highlands Trail: The trail covers a distance of approximately 200 km and leads through the Bohemian-Moravian highlands, offering stunning vistas of the hills, valleys, and forests.

Best Things to Do in Prague-East District: Activities and Tours

Prague-East District offers a wide range of activities and tours that cater to all interests and age groups. Some of the popular activities include:

  • Explore Local Culture: Visit local museums, art galleries, and cultural events to learn about the district’s rich cultural heritage.

  • Attend Beer Festivals: The Czech Republic is known for its beer, and Prague-East District hosts several beer festivals throughout the year, including the Kutná Hora Beer Festival and the Pivo na krásné Hoře.

  • Visit Local Food Markets: Prague-East District is known for its local food markets, where you can sample traditional Czech dishes, fruits, and vegetables.

Week-Long Itinerary for Prague-East District

Day 1: Visit the Sedlec Ossuary and the St. Barbara’s Church in Kutná Hora.

Day 2: Explore the Chateau Žleby and its surroundings.

Day 3: Hike the Kokorín Valley and visit the Kokorín Castle.

Day 4: Take a bike ride along the Sázava River.

Day 5: Visit the city of Kolín and its St. Bartholomew Church.

Day 6: Explore the Bohemian-Moravian Highlands.

Day 7: Attend a local beer festival or food market.

Food and Drink Scene of Prague-East District

The Prague-East District boasts a vibrant food and drink scene, with plenty of traditional Czech dishes and local flavors to try. Some of the must-try dishes and beverages in the region include:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Roast Pork: A traditional Czech dish, roast pork is typically served with sauerkraut and dumplings.

  • Svíčková: This dish features beef sirloin in a creamy vegetable sauce, served with potato dumplings.

  • Traditionální Rajská: This dish is made of breaded chicken or pork, served with a tomato sauce, rice, and a side of corn.

Local Beverages

  • Pilsner Urquell: One of the most popular beers in the Czech Republic, Pilsner Urquell is a light, crisp lager with a refreshing flavor.

  • Becherovka: A herbal liqueur that’s made from a secret recipe of herbs and spices, Becherovka has been produced in the Czech Republic since the early 19th century.

Where to Stay in Prague-East District: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Prague-East District offers several accommodation options that cater to all budgets and preferences. Some of the top-rated hotels, hostels, and guesthouses include:

  • Chateau Mcely: Situated within a 45-minute drive from Prague, this five-star chateau offers luxury accommodation and exceptional service.

  • Grand Hotel Praha: Located in the heart of Kutná Hora, this hotel boasts beautiful views of St. Barbara’s Church and has a traditional Czech restaurant.

  • Mocha Guesthouse: A cozy guesthouse situated in the charming town of Čelákovice, offering affordable accommodation and friendly service.

What to Buy in Prague-East District: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

If you’re looking for souvenirs and local products to bring home from Prague-East District, here are some items to consider:

  • Garnets: Bohemia is known for its garnet jewelry, and you can find some beautiful pieces in several shops in the region.

  • Czech Beer: The Czech Republic is famous for its beer, and you can buy several local varieties in the district.

Local Culture and Customs of Prague-East District

The Czech Republic has a rich cultural heritage, and you can experience it firsthand in Prague-East District. Here are some cultural customs to keep in mind:

  • Greetings: Czechs prefer a formal greeting when meeting strangers. A simple “Dobrý den” (hello) or “Ahoj” (hi) is appropriate for informal situations.

  • Tipping: Tipping is common in restaurants and cafes. A 10% tip is customary, and rounding up the bill is a common practice.

Best Time to Visit Prague-East District

The best time to visit Prague-East District is during spring (April and May) and autumn (September and October) when the weather is mild and the crowds are thinner. Summers can be busy, and winters can be harsh, so plan accordingly.

Do’s and Don’ts of Prague-East District

Do:

  • Explore the natural beauty: Prague-East District has plenty of beautiful parks, hiking/biking trails, and natural attractions that you shouldn’t miss.

  • Try the local cuisine: The district’s food scene is unique, with plenty of traditional Czech dishes to try.

Don’t:

  • Forget to brush up on your etiquette: Czechs are known for their politeness, so be sure to follow basic etiquette rules when interacting with locals.

  • Leave without visiting a local beer festival: The Czech Republic is famous for its beer, and there are several beer festivals in the region that you shouldn’t miss.

Safety and Health

Safety in Prague-East District

Prague-East District is generally a safe and peaceful destination, with low crime rates. However, it’s always advisable to take basic safety precautions like keeping an eye on your belongings, staying aware of your surroundings, and avoiding dimly-lit or deserted areas at night.

Health in Prague-East District

The medical facilities in Prague-East District are generally good. The main hospital in the district is the Hospital Kolín, which has emergency services available 24 hours a day.

Getting to Prague-East District: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Prague-East District

The main airport that serves Prague-East District is the Václav Havel Airport Prague, which is approximately 56 km from the district.

Bus Stations Near Prague-East District

The main bus station in the district is located in Kutná Hora and offers connections to several destinations within the country.

Other Options

Train and car rental services are also available in Prague, offering convenient options for travelers looking to explore the district on their own.

How to Get Around in Prague-East District

Public transport options in Prague-East District include buses and trains. Both options are reliable, and schedules are readily available. Taxis and car rental services are also available.

Where to Go Next from Prague-East District: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Prague: Just an hour away from Prague-East District, Prague is a must-visit destination in the Czech Republic. With its stunning architecture, rich history, and vibrant culture, the city offers a unique and unforgettable experience.

  • Karlovy Vary: Located approximately 113 km from Prague-East District, Karlovy Vary is a popular spa town known for its hot springs and spa resorts.
