Introduction to Nové Město na Moravě

Nové Město na Moravě is a charming town with a population of just over 10,000 people. Despite its small size, the town has a lot to offer for tourists. It is known for its Baroque architecture, charming streets, and beautiful landscapes.

Situated in the Highlands region of the Czech Republic, Nové Město na Moravě has a rich history, dating back to the 13th century. The town has been owned by various noble families throughout the centuries, which has left an indelible mark on the town’s cultural and architectural heritage.

List of Places to Visit in Nové Město na Moravě

When visiting Nové Město na Moravě, there is no shortage of places to visit and things to do. Whether you’re interested in culture, nature, or adventure, there’s something for everyone.

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

If you’re interested in history and culture, Nové Město na Moravě has plenty of attractions to offer. The town boasts several museums and historical sites worth visiting, including:

  • Štěpánka’s Trail Museum – a unique museum dedicated to the history of glass and crystal making in the Czech Republic, located in the historic setting of a former glass factory.
  • Vysocina Museum – a comprehensive museum showcasing the history and culture of the Highlands region, with exhibitions on folk traditions, geology, and natural history.
  • Fojtství and Castle Ruins – the remains of a former castle and a Renaissance-era administrative building, offering breathtaking views of the town and the surrounding countryside.
  • Church of St. Prokop – a beautiful Baroque church built in the 18th century, featuring stunning frescoes and richly decorated altars.

Natural Attractions of Nové Město na Moravě: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Nové Město na Moravě is surrounded by beautiful landscapes and natural attractions, making it an ideal destination for nature lovers and outdoor enthusiasts. Popular activities in the area include:

  • Hiking and Biking – Nové Město na Moravě is surrounded by lush forests and rolling hills, offering a wide range of hiking and biking trails for different skill levels. Some of the most popular routes include the Žďárské vrchy Mountains and the Škrdlovice Hills.
  • Skiing and Snowboarding – During the winter months, the nearby Ski Resort Nové Město na Moravě offers excellent skiing and snowboarding opportunities for all levels of experience.
  • Golfing – The town is home to a beautiful 18-hole golf course, offering great views of the surrounding countryside and challenging play.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Nové Město na Moravě

The Žďárské vrchy Mountains and the Škrdlovice Hills offer numerous hiking and biking trails for visitors to explore. Some of the most popular routes include:

  • The Žďárské vrchy Mountains – This scenic loop around the Žďárské vrchy Mountains is a great option for experienced hikers. The route features steep inclines and stunning views of the surrounding landscape.
  • The Škrdlovice Hills – This route takes visitors through the rolling hills of the Škrdlovice region, passing through small villages and picturesque countryside.

Best Things to Do in Nové Město na Moravě: Activities and Tours

Nové Město na Moravě offers a range of activities and tours to suit all interests. Some of the best things to do in the area include:

  • Šiklův mlýn Brewery Tour – This local brewery offers tours and tastings of their signature beers, which are brewed using traditional methods and local ingredients.
  • Harvest Festival – Held in late September, the annual Harvest Festival is a celebration of local traditions and culture, featuring food, music, and dancing.
  • Horseback Riding – Several local stables offer horseback riding tours of the surrounding countryside, providing a unique and unforgettable perspective on the area.

Week-Long Itinerary for Nové Město na Moravě

For visitors who want to make the most out of their time in Nové Město na Moravě, we recommend the following week-long itinerary:

Day 1 – Explore the historic town center, including the Fojtství and Castle Ruins, and the Church of St. Prokop. Day 2 – Visit the Štěpánka’s Trail Museum and the Vysocina Museum to learn more about the region’s history and culture. Day 3 – Take a hiking or biking tour of the Žďárské vrchy Mountains or the Škrdlovice Hills. Day 6 – Take a horseback riding tour of the area or go skiing/snowboarding at Ski Resort Nové Město na Moravě during the winter months. Day 7 – Relax and enjoy the local food and wine scene, including a brewery tour of the Šiklův mlýn brewery.

Food and Drink Scene of Nové Město na Moravě

Nové Město na Moravě boasts a vibrant food and drink scene, with plenty of local specialties and regional wines to try.

Must-Try Dishes

Some dishes to try when visiting Nové Město na Moravě include:

  • Štramberské Uši – A regional specialty consisting of deep-fried dough with poppy seed filling, served with powdered sugar and sour cream.
  • Moravian Sparrow – A hearty meat dish made from marinated pork, served with dumplings and sauerkraut.
  • Trdelník – A popular dessert made from rolled dough, cooked over an open flame, and coated in cinnamon and sugar.

Local Beverages

When it comes to drinks, Nové Město na Moravě is known for its beer and wine. The Šiklův mlýn brewery offers a range of locally brewed beers, while the region’s vineyards produce some of the finest white and rose wines in the country.

Local Culture and Customs of Nové Město na Moravě

Nové Město na Moravě has a rich cultural heritage, with a mix of Czech and Moravian traditions. The town is known for its lively festivals and celebrations, including the Harvest Festival, which takes place in late September.

When visiting Nové Město na Moravě, it’s important to be respectful of local customs and traditions, such as taking off your shoes when entering someone’s home and respecting the local dress code when visiting religious sites.

Best Time to Visit Nové Město na Moravě

The best time to visit Nové Město na Moravě depends on your interests and preferences. The summer months, from June to August, are the busiest and the warmest, with plenty of outdoor activities and festivals taking place. The winter months, from December to February, are ideal for winter sports enthusiasts, while the spring and fall months offer mild temperatures and fewer crowds.
