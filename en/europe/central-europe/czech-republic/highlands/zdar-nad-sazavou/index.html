Introduction to Žďár nad Sázavou

Located in the Highlands region of the Czech Republic, Žďár nad Sázavou is a picturesque town surrounded by rolling hills and lush forests. The town is named after the Žďár Monastery, which is a UNESCO World Heritage Site and one of the biggest tourist attractions in the area.

List of Places to Visit in Žďár nad Sázavou

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

1. Žďár Monastery

The Žďár Monastery, also known as the Pilgrimage Church of St. John of Nepomuk, is a must-visit attraction. The Baroque-style church was built in the 18th century and is a UNESCO World Heritage Site. Visitors can marvel at the exquisite frescoes, sculptures, and altars inside.

2. Museum of the Žďár Region

The Museum of the Žďár Region is a great place to learn about the local history and culture. The museum houses an impressive collection of artifacts, including archaeological finds and traditional costumes.

3. Nové Město na Moravě Chateau

The Nové Město na Moravě Chateau is a stunning Renaissance-style building that houses a gallery of contemporary art. Visitors can also take a tour of the chateau to learn about its history.

Natural Attractions of Žďár nad Sázavou: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

1. Žďárské vrchy

The Žďárské vrchy is a range of hills that offer stunning views of the surrounding countryside. Visitors can hike or bike through the hills and explore the beautiful forests and meadows.

2. Dlouhé Stráně Hydro Power Plant

The Dlouhé Stráně Hydro Power Plant is an impressive engineering feat. Visitors can take a tour of the facility and learn about how water is used to generate electricity.

3. Křižánky Lookout Tower

The Křižánky Lookout Tower offers panoramic views of the Žďárské vrchy and the Sázava River. Visitors can climb to the top of the tower for a bird’s-eye view of the surrounding countryside.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Žďár nad Sázavou

1. Žďár Monastery Trail

The Žďár Monastery Trail is a scenic hiking route that starts at the Žďár Monastery and winds through the Žďárské vrchy hills. The trail offers stunning views of the surrounding countryside and passes by several other popular attractions, including the Křižánky Lookout Tower and the Velké Dářko Reservoir.

2. Sázava River Bike Trail

The Sázava River Bike Trail is a popular cycling route that follows the Sázava River through the picturesque countryside. The trail passes by several charming villages and historic sites.

Best Things to Do in Žďár nad Sázavou: Activities and Tours

1. Žďár Monastery Tour

A tour of the Žďár Monastery is a fascinating way to learn about the history and architecture of this UNESCO World Heritage Site.

2. Sázava River Rafting

Sázava River Rafting is a thrilling adventure for those who like water sports. Visitors can rent rafts and paddle down the river, taking in the breathtaking scenery along the way.

Week-Long Itinerary for Žďár nad Sázavou

Day 1: Visit the Žďár Monastery and Museum of the Žďár Region. Day 2: Hike the Žďár Monastery Trail and visit the Křižánky Lookout Tower. Day 3: Take a bike tour of the Sázava River Bike Trail. Day 4: Explore the Nové Město na Moravě Chateau and take a tour of the contemporary art gallery. Day 5: Go rafting on the Sázava River. Day 6: Visit the Dlouhé Stráně Hydro Power Plant and learn about how water is used to generate electricity. Day 7: Relax and soak up the local culture in Žďár nad Sázavou.

Food and Drink Scene of Žďár nad Sázavou

Must-Try Dishes

1. Žďárské koleno

Žďárské koleno is a traditional dish made from pork knuckle that is slow-roasted until tender. It is typically served with sauerkraut and dumplings.

2. Perník

Perník is a gingerbread cake that is popular in the Czech Republic. It is typically flavored with cinnamon, cloves, and honey.

Local Beverages

1. Pivovar Vyčep

Pivovar Vyčep is a local brewery that produces a range of beers, including pale lagers and dark beers. Visitors can take a tour of the brewery and sample the various beers on offer.

2. Slivovice

Slivovice is a plum brandy that is popular in the Czech Republic. It is typically served as a digestif after a meal.
