Introduction to Pelhřimov

Located in the Highlands region of the Czech Republic, in central Europe, Pelhřimov is a charming town surrounded by picturesque rolling hills and valleys. The town is filled with historical and cultural sites, stunning natural scenery, and a vibrant local community. From outdoor adventures to cultural attractions, there is something for everyone in Pelhřimov.

List of Places to Visit in Pelhřimov

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Pelhřimov is a town steeped in history and culture, making it an excellent destination for history buffs and culture enthusiasts alike. The following museums and historical sites are must-visit places in Pelhřimov:

  • The Gothic Church of St. Bartholomew: Built in the 13th century, this stunning church is the oldest building in Pelhřimov and a prime example of Gothic architecture.
  • The Museum of Records and Curiosities: This quirky museum houses an extensive collection of bizarre and unique objects, such as two-headed animals and giant pumpkins, and has been listed in the Guinness World Records.
  • The Pelhřimov Town Hall Tower: Climb to the top of this 79-meter tower for stunning views of the town and surrounding countryside.
  • The Pelhřimov Castle: This 13th-century castle has been restored and turned into a regional museum with exhibits showcasing the history and culture of the region.

Natural Attractions of Pelhřimov: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

If you’re looking for outdoor adventure or simply want to admire the natural beauty of the surrounding area, Pelhřimov has much to offer. The following are some of the top natural attractions around Pelhřimov:

  • The Šikland Adventure Park: This park offers a range of outdoor activities, including zip-lining, rope climbing, and a 1200-meter tubing slide.
  • The Pelhřimov Forest Park: Covering an area of approximately 1700 hectares, the forest park is an ideal place for hiking and biking adventures.
  • The Křemešník Mountain: Located close to Pelhřimov, this mountain offers some truly stunning views of the region and is a popular spot for hiking and climbing.
  • The Pelhřimov Pond: This picturesque pond is an ideal spot for fishing, swimming, and relaxation.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Pelhřimov

The region around Pelhřimov offers some beautiful hiking and biking trails. The following are some of the top routes for those who want to explore the natural beauty of the area:

  • The Pelhřimov Forest Park Trail: This trail is approximately 25 km long and takes you through the beautiful forest park surrounding the town.
  • The Křemešník Mountain Trail: This trail is around 12 km long and is perfect for those who want to explore the stunning mountain landscape around Pelhřimov.
  • The Vysočina Bike Trail: This is an extensive network of bike trails that covers much of the Highlands region, including Pelhřimov.

Best Things to Do in Pelhřimov: Activities and Tours

Aside from natural attractions and cultural sites, Pelhřimov also offers a variety of activities and tours. The following are some of the best things to do in Pelhřimov:

  • The Pelhřimov Beer Tour: Explore the local beer scene and taste some of the region’s best brews on this guided tour.
  • The Pelhřimov Ghost Tour: Delve into the spooky history of Pelhřimov on this guided tour of the town’s haunted spots.
  • The Pelhřimov Farm Tour: Get a taste of rural life in Pelhřimov on this tour of local farms and their products.

Week-Long Itinerary for Pelhřimov

If you’re planning to spend a week in Pelhřimov, here’s a sample itinerary that covers the best the town has to offer:

  • Day 1: Explore the town’s historical and cultural landmarks, including the St. Bartholomew Church, the Museum of Records and Curiosities, and the Pelhřimov Town Hall Tower.
  • Day 2: Take a biking tour of the Pelhřimov Forest Park Trail.
  • Day 3: Visit the Pelhřimov Castle and take a guided tour of its exhibits.
  • Day 4: Take a day trip to the nearby Křemešník Mountain for a day of hiking and sightseeing.
  • Day 5: Relax at the Pelhřimov Pond or take a swim in its clear waters.
  • Day 6: Take a guided ghost tour of the town’s most haunted spots.
  • Day 7: Round off your week with a tour of local farms and breweries to sample food and drinks that embody the authentic Pelhřimov experience.

Food and Drink Scene of Pelhřimov

Must-Try Dishes

Pelhřimov boasts a rich culinary tradition, with several local dishes that are not to be missed:

  • Kulajda: A creamy soup made from potatoes, mushrooms, dill, and a hint of vinegar.
  • Lejoky: A sweet pastry filled with poppy seeds and raisins.
  • Frgál: A dense yeast cake topped with sweetened curd cheese.

Local Beverages

When in Pelhřimov, be sure to sample some of the local brews and spirits:

  • Pelhřimov Beer: Pelhřimov is home to several breweries that produce a range of beers, such as the Premium Pelhřimov, which is a lighter pilsner-style beer.
  • Slivovice: A strong, traditional plum brandy popular throughout the Czech Republic.

Where to Stay in Pelhřimov: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Pelhřimov caters to a range of budgets and tastes when it comes to lodging. The following are some of the best options for accommodations in Pelhřimov:

  • Štěkrov Castle: This historical castle has been converted into a hotel and offers stylish rooms with antique furnishings and beautiful views of the surrounding countryside.
  • Hotel U Zlatého Lva: This cozy hotel offers simple yet tastefully decorated rooms and an on-site restaurant serving local dishes.
  • Penzion Prislin: This affordable guesthouse offers comfortable, spacious rooms and is located in the heart of Pelhřimov.

What to Buy in Pelhřimov: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

If you’re looking to bring home a memento of your trip to Pelhřimov, consider the following local products:

  • Pelhřimov Beer: Pick up some locally brewed beer to share with friends and family back home.
  • Handmade Ceramics: Pelhřimov is known for its traditional ceramics, which make for unique and meaningful souvenirs.
  • Locally Produced Honey: Pelhřimov is home to several honey producers, so be sure to stop by a local shop and pick up some jars of delicious, pure honey.

Local Culture and Customs of Pelhřimov

Pelhřimov is a town with a strong regional identity and cultural traditions. The following are some of the customs and traditions that define Pelhřimov:

  • Easter Whip Tradition: During Easter, young men in the town head out to the streets, armed with pomlázka (braided whips), to gently “whip” the legs of young women in the hopes of bringing them health and vitality.
  • Pelhřimov Festival of Records and Curiosities: This annual festival celebrates the town’s quirky and bizarre side, with exhibits showcasing unusual and unique objects and performances from local musicians and artists.

Best Time to Visit Pelhřimov

The best time to visit Pelhřimov is in the spring or summer, from May through September. During this time, the weather is pleasant, and the town comes alive with cultural events and outdoor activities.

Do’s and Don’ts of Pelhřimov

Do:

  • Try the local food and drink.
  • Observe local customs and traditions.
  • Take advantage of the region’s natural beauty and outdoor activities.

Don’t:

  • Be disrespectful to local culture and customs.
  • Litter or damage the natural environment.
  • Fail to bring appropriate clothing and gear for outdoor adventures.

Safety and Health

Safety in Pelhřimov

Pelhřimov is a safe town, with very low rates of violent crime. However, as with any travel destination, it’s always best to be aware of your surroundings and take steps to keep yourself and your belongings safe.

Health in Pelhřimov

Pelhřimov has several healthcare facilities, including hospitals and clinics, where visitors can seek medical attention if needed. It’s always a good idea to have travel insurance and consult your doctor before traveling to ensure you’re up to date on any necessary vaccinations.

Getting to Pelhřimov: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Pelhřimov

The closest major airport to Pelhřimov is Vaclav Havel Airport Prague, located approximately 130 km northwest of Pelhřimov.

Bus Stations Near Pelhřimov

Pelhřimov is well-connected by bus, with several daily departures to Prague and other major cities in the region.

Other Options

The best way to get around Pelhřimov is by walking or biking. Taxis and car rentals are also available for those who prefer to travel by private vehicle.

How to Get Around in Pelhřimov

Pelhřimov is a small town that can easily be explored on foot or by bike. For those who prefer to travel by car, several car rental companies are available in town.

Where to Go Next from Pelhřimov: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Pelhřimov is an excellent base from which to explore the wider Highlands region. The following are some nearby destinations and excursions worth considering:

  • The Vysočina Region: This sprawling region is home to several charming towns and villages, stunning natural landscapes, and charming cultural attractions.
  • The UNESCO World Heritage Site of Telč: Located approximately 45 km from Pelhřimov, this well-preserved Renaissance town is a must-visit destination for history and architecture enthusiasts.
  • The Brno Villas: This collection of architecturally significant villas located in the nearby city of Brno is worth a visit for fans of modernist architecture and design.

Conclusion

Pelhřimov may be a small town, but it packs a punch when it comes to history, culture, outdoor adventure, and local charm. Whether you’re looking for quiet relaxation or an adrenaline-packed week of exploration, Pelhřimov has something to offer. With affordable lodgings, friendly locals, and a strong regional identity, Pelhřimov is a must-visit destination for anyone traveling to the Czech Republic.
