Highlands, Czech Republic: A Comprehensive Travel Guide

If you’re planning a trip to Central Europe, put Highlands, Czech Republic, on your list of must-visit destinations. With its rich cultural heritage, breathtaking natural beauty, and wide range of outdoor activities, the Highlands region has something for everyone.

In this comprehensive travel guide, we’ll give you all the information you need to plan your trip to Highlands, including places to visit, things to do, where to stay, and local culture and customs.

List of Places to Visit in Highlands

When it comes to places to visit in Highlands, there’s no shortage of options. Here are some of the must-visit attractions:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

The Highlands region is steeped in history and culture, and there are plenty of museums and historical places to explore. Here are some of the top attractions:

  • Vysoke Myto Museum
  • Litomysl Castle
  • Museum of Eastern Bohemia in Hradec Kralove
  • Church of Saint Mary Magdalene

Natural Attractions of Highlands: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

The Highlands region is also known for its natural beauty, which makes it a great spot for outdoor adventures and scenic views. Here are some of the top natural attractions:

  • Adrspach-Teplice Rocks
  • ZOO Dvur Kralove nad Labem
  • Krkonose National Park
  • Snezka Mountain

Hiking/Biking Routes of Highlands

If you’re looking to explore the natural beauty of Highlands on foot or bike, there are plenty of hiking and biking trails to choose from. Here are some of the best routes:

  • Mionsi Forest Trail
  • Krkonose Nature Trail
  • Elbe River Bikeway
  • Cesky Raj Nature Trail

Best Things to Do in Highlands: Activities and Tours

If you’re looking for fun things to do in Highlands, you won’t be disappointed. Here are some of the best activities and tours:

  • Brewery Tours in Policka
  • Horse-Drawn Carriage Tours in Litomysl
  • Bike Tours in Hradec Kralove
  • Balloon Flights in Krkonose

Week-Long Itinerary for Highlands

If you have a week to spare in Highlands, here’s an itinerary you might want to follow:

Day Activities
Day 1 Explore the historic town of Hradec Kralove and its museums
Day 2 Visit the Adrspach-Teplice Rocks and ZOO Dvur Kralove nad Labem
Day 3 Take a bike tour through the Krkonose Mountains
Day 4 Visit the Litomysl Castle and attend a classical concert
Day 5 Explore the Elbe River on a canoe or kayak
Day 6 Go hiking in the Cesky Raj Nature Reserve
Day 7 Visit the Policka Brewery and sample some local beers

Food and Drink Scene of Highlands

No travel guide is complete without a section on local cuisine, and Highlands is no exception. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Bramborak (potato pancake)
  • Smazeny syr (fried cheese)
  • Trdelnik (chimney cake)

Local Beverages

  • Pilsner Urquell beer
  • Becherovka (herbal liqueur)

Where to Stay in Highlands: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Whether you’re looking for luxury accommodations or budget-friendly options, Highlands has plenty of options. Here are some of the best hotels, hostels, and guesthouses in the region:

  • Hotel Cernigov in Hradec Kralove
  • Hotel Zlaty Lev in Litomysl
  • Hostel u Papouka in Adrspach
  • Penzion Barborka in Policka

What to Buy in Highlands: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

If you’re looking for souvenirs and local products to bring home from your trip to Highlands, here are some things to look out for:

  • Bohemian crystal glassware
  • Wooden toys and decorations
  • Garnets (semi-precious red stones)

Local Culture and Customs of Highlands

To truly immerse yourself in the local culture of Highlands, here are some customs and traditions you should be aware of:

  • Respect for elders is highly valued
  • Brno accent and dialect are prominent
  • Folk music and dance are part of everyday life

Best Time to Visit Highlands

The best time to visit Highlands is during the summer months, from June to August, when the weather is warm and sunny. However, if you’re looking to avoid crowds, you may want to consider visiting in the shoulder seasons of May and September.

Do’s and Don’ts of Highlands

To ensure a smooth and enjoyable trip to Highlands, here are some do’s and don’ts to keep in mind:

Do:

  • Respect local customs and traditions
  • Try local cuisine and beverages
  • Carry cash, as some places may not accept credit cards

Don’t:

  • Be loud and disruptive in public places
  • Touch historical artifacts in museums and historical places
  • Wear revealing or provocative clothing in religious places

Safety and Health

Highlands is a safe and relatively healthy destination for travelers. Here are some things to keep in mind:

Safety in Highlands

  • Petty crime, like pickpocketing and theft, can occur in popular tourist areas
  • Keep your belongings safe and secure at all times

Health in Highlands

  • Tap water is safe to drink in Highlands, but bottled water is readily available
  • Tick-borne diseases, like Lyme disease, can occur in wooded areas, so use insect repellent and check for ticks after outdoor activities

Getting to Highlands: Transportation Options and Logistics

Highlands is easily accessible by a variety of transportation options. Here are some things to consider when planning your trip:

Airports Near Highlands

  • Vaclav Havel Airport Prague (PRG) is the closest major airport to Highlands, about 130 km away

Bus Stations Near Highlands

  • Hradec Kralove Bus Station
  • Jicin Bus Station

Other Options

  • Car rentals are available at major airports and in larger cities
  • Train travel is available but may not be the most efficient option

How to Get Around in Highlands

Getting around Highlands is relatively easy, with plenty of transportation options to choose from. Here are some popular modes of transportation:

  • Buses and trams are available in larger cities
  • Taxis and car rentals are available throughout the region
  • Cycling is a popular mode of transportation for tourists

Where to Go Next from Highlands: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

If you have more time to explore Central Europe, here are some nearby destinations and excursions you might want to consider:

  • Prague, Czech Republic (137 km)
  • Berlin, Germany (370 km)
  • Vienna, Austria (310 km)
  • Krakow, Poland (350 km)

Conclusion

Highlands, Czech Republic, is a hidden gem in Central Europe, with something for every type of traveler. Whether you’re looking to explore the historic towns and museums, hike or bike through the mountains, or sample local cuisine and beverages, you won’t be disappointed. Use this travel guide to plan your trip, and we’re sure you’ll have an unforgettable experience in Highlands.
