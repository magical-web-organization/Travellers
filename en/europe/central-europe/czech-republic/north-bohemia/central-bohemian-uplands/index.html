Introduction to Central Bohemian Uplands

Central Bohemian Uplands is located in North Bohemia, in Czech Republic, in the heart of Europe. It is an idyllic destination for nature lovers, history enthusiasts, and adventure seekers alike. This region is renowned for its dramatic landscapes and picturesque villages.

The Central Bohemian Uplands offer a range of activities for visitors, including hiking, cycling, swimming, boating, fishing, rock climbing, and kayaking. The area is also known for its historical landmarks and cultural attractions such as museums and art galleries. It is a perfect destination for those looking for an authentic travel experience.

List of Places to Visit in Central Bohemian Uplands

Below are some of the must-see places in Central Bohemian Uplands:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Karlštejn Castle: A 14th-century Gothic castle in the picturesque village of Karlštejn is a popular tourist attraction. It was built by King Charles IV to store the crown jewels and other royal treasures.
  • Kutná Hora: A town designated as a UNESCO World Heritage Site. It is known for its well-preserved architecture from the Middle Ages and Gothic period, including St. Barbora’s Cathedral and the Sedlec Ossuary.
  • Průhonice Chateau and Park: The Chateau and Park of Průhonice is a UNESCO World Heritage Site, famous for its magnificent gardens and overwhelming beauty. The guided tour of the chateau gives visitors an insight into the lifestyle of the Czech aristocracy.

Natural Attractions of Central Bohemian Uplands: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Bohemian Paradise: A national park on the edge of the Giant Mountains, famous for sandstone formations, rock towns, and scenic walks.
  • Český Kras: A karst region with caves, gorges, and forests, including Konepruské caves that are open to visitors.
  • Vltava River: A scenic river running through the heart of Czech Republic that is perfect for boating, fishing, and swimming.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Central Bohemian Uplands

Central Bohemian Uplands boasts a network of well-marked trails and paths that offer visitors breathtaking views of the rolling hills, deep forests, and crystal-clear lakes. Some of the most popular hiking/biking routes include:

  • Bohemian Paradise Nature Reserve Trail: This 100 km path offers stunning views of the Trosky Castle, the Kozákov Mountain, and many other attractive sites.
  • The Elbe Trail: A 280 km trail that follows the course of the Elbe River as it winds through the Bohemian countryside and past Czech historical landmarks.
  • Cycling through the Central Bohemian Uplands: The region offers a cycling tourist route 190 km long, passing through five regions and providing a unique view of the Central Bohemian Uplands.

Best Things to Do in Central Bohemian Uplands: Activities and Tours

  • Hot Air Balloon Ride: A tour of Central Bohemian Uplands from a hot air balloon which offers a breathtaking view of the Castles, Traditional Czech countryside, and unique rock formations.
  • Czech beer brewing tour: A tour of one of the famous Czech beer brewing regions in Czech Republic with stops for tastings of different beers and to learn about brewing techniques.
  • Traditional Czech cuisine cooking tour: A tour of Bohemian Uplands, with a cooking class where visitors can learn how to prepare traditional Czech dishes such as roast pork, apple strudel, and dumplings.

Week-Long Itinerary for Central Bohemian Uplands

Day 1: Visit the historic and iconic Karlštejn Castle.

Day 2: Go for the Prague city tour and explore the popular historic landmarks of the city.

Day 3-4: Discover the picturesque Bohemian Paradise Nature Reserve.

Day 5: Explore the Kutná Hora, a town designated as a UNESCO World Heritage Site.

Day 6: Enroll in a hot air balloon ride tour and get a chance to see the beautiful Central Bohemian Uplands from above.

Day 7: Go for a traditional Czech cuisine cooking tour and enjoy the local food.

Food and Drink Scene of Central Bohemian Uplands

The cuisine of Central Bohemian Uplands consists of hearty, comforting dishes that are perfect for cooler weather. The region is also known for its beer brewing tradition. Below are some of the must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Svíčková: Marinated, sliced beef sirloin served with gravy, bread dumplings, and cranberry sauce.
  • Kolache: A traditional Czech sweet pastry made with yeast dough and filled with fruit, cheese, or poppy seed.
  • Trdelník: A sweet pastry that is rolled out and grilled over an open flame, then coated in cinnamon sugar.

Local Beverages

  • Pilsner Urquell: One of the most famous Czech beers, famous for its unique flavor.
  • Kofola: A popular Czech soft drink made from a mixture of herbs, medicinal plants, and fruit juices that is similar to cola.
  • Becherovka: A Czech herbal liquor, which can be enjoyed on its own or as a mixer in cocktails.

Where to Stay in Central Bohemian Uplands: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Central Bohemian Uplands offers many options for accommodation that cater to all budgets. Some of the best places to stay include:

  • Bohemian Paradise: A charming guesthouse situated in a beautiful, quiet location, not too far away from the Bohemian Paradise Nature Reserve.
  • Chateau Mcely: A luxurious hotel with a spa and wellness center located in an idyllic setting in the heart of Central Bohemian Uplands.
  • Pension Svět: A popular family-run guesthouse with a swimming pool situated in the picturesque village of Světlá nad Sázavou.

What to Buy in Central Bohemian Uplands: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Central Bohemian Uplands is famous for its handcrafted products, including glassware, crystal, porcelain, and jewelry made by local artists. Some of the best places to buy souvenirs include:

  • Glassworks Moser: A company that has been producing glassware for over 150 years.
  • Český Porcelán: A porcelain factory that offers beautiful, hand-painted porcelain products.
  • Artěl Design: A company that specializes in handmade, contemporary glassware that draws inspiration from traditional Czech designs.

Local Culture and Customs of Central Bohemian Uplands

Traditional Czech values and customs play a significant role in the everyday life and hospitality of the people of Central Bohemian Uplands. Greetings, thanking, and saying goodbye are essential social protocols, while tradition and religion have a profound influence on daily activities.

Best Time to Visit Central Bohemian Uplands

The best time to visit Central Bohemian Uplands is during the summer season from April to September and in the winter from November to February. The peak season is in July and August, but visitors can expect higher prices and larger crowds.

Do’s and Don’ts of Central Bohemian Uplands

Do:

  • Respect the local customs and traditions.
  • Try the traditional Czech cuisine.
  • Carry some cash as many places do not accept credit cards.

Don’t:

  • Smoke in public places.
  • Litter in public places.
  • Be disrespectful towards the elderly or locals.

Safety and Health

Safety in Central Bohemian Uplands

Central Bohemian Uplands is generally a safe destination for travelers. However, visitors should take necessary precautions to ensure their safety, such as keeping valuables in a safe place, being aware of their surroundings, and following traffic rules.

Health in Central Bohemian Uplands

There are no major health concerns in Central Bohemian Uplands, and the standard of medical care is high. Visitors should take necessary precautions, such as carrying a basic first aid kit, protecting themselves from the sun, and drinking bottled water.

Getting to Central Bohemian Uplands: Transportation Options and Logistics

Central Bohemian Uplands is well connected to major cities in the Czech Republic by a reliable transportation network. Below are some of the transportation options:

Airports Near Central Bohemian Uplands

  • Václav Havel Airport Prague: Located approximately 30 km from Prague, is the main international airport in the Czech Republic.

Bus Stations Near Central Bohemian Uplands

  • Prague Bus Station Florenc: The main bus station for international and regional services is located in Prague.

Other Options

  • Train: The train is an excellent option for navigating the Czech Republic, with several direct connections from Prague to Central Bohemian Uplands.
  • Car: Car rental is an option, but it can be expensive and challenging due to challenging driving conditions and limited parking.

How to Get Around in Central Bohemian Uplands

The best way to get around Central Bohemian Uplands is by car, especially if you plan on traveling to remote areas. Alternatively, visitors can use public transportation such as buses and trains to explore the area.

Where to Go Next from Central Bohemian Uplands: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

From Central Bohemian Uplands, visitors can travel to nearby destinations such as:

  • Prague: A world-renowned tourist destination known for its incredible architecture, museums, and art galleries.
  • Karlovy Vary: A picturesque spa city with famous hot springs, beautiful architecture, and a rich cultural heritage.
  • Český Krumlov: A medieval town designated as a UNESCO World Heritage Site, known for its picturesque castle, winding streets, and historic buildings.
