Introduction to Bohemian Paradise

Bohemian Paradise, or Český Ráj, is a hidden gem located in North Bohemia, Czech Republic. It’s a nature reserve full of hiking and biking trails, rock formations, and castles, making it an ideal destination for outdoor enthusiasts and history buffs alike. With its stunning scenery and cultural significance, Bohemian Paradise is one of the most visited destinations in the country.

List of Places to Visit in Bohemian Paradise

When in Bohemian Paradise, these are the must-visit places:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Hrubý Rohozec Castle: A beautiful Renaissance castle dating back to the 16th century. Explore the chambers and learn about the history of the region.

  • Trosky Castle: A symbol of Bohemian Paradise, Trosky Castle consists of two towers atop two volcanic formations. It offers amazing views of the surrounding area.

  • Drábské světničky: A former hermitage turned into a historical site. Explore the 14th-century chambers, marvel at the Gothic frescoes and learn about the history of the region.

Natural Attractions of Bohemian Paradise: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Prachov Rocks: This nature reserve is a popular climbing and hiking site with labyrinths of sandstone towers, canyons and rock formations.

  • Kozákov: The highest mountain in the region offers spectacular views of the countryside.

  • Bozkov Dolomite Caves: Go underground and explore stunning limestone formations, underground lakes and rivers.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Bohemian Paradise

Bohemian Paradise is an outdoor enthusiast’s paradise with numerous hiking trails, biking routes and cycle paths. Here are some of the best routes:

  • Hruboskalsko Nature Trail: A 6km loop trail that passes through beautiful rock formations and offers breathtaking views.

  • Jizera Cycle Route: A 164km cycling path that takes you through forests, meadows, and small villages along the Jizera River.

  • Trosky Trail: A 10km hike through forests and meadows, leading up to the iconic Trosky Castle.

Best Things to Do in Bohemian Paradise: Activities and Tours

There are many things to do while in Bohemian Paradise. Here are some of the best activities to try:

  • Rock climbing: With numerous rock formations, Bohemian Paradise is a popular spot for rock climbing.

  • Balloon rides: Take in the stunning scenery and amazing bird’s eye view of the region while floating in a hot air balloon.

  • Horse riding: Explore the forests and meadows on horseback.

Week-Long Itinerary for Bohemian Paradise

Here’s a sample itinerary for a week-long visit to Bohemian Paradise:

Day Activities
Day 1 Arrival, get settled in, and explore Turnov, the gateway to Bohemian Paradise
Day 2 Visit Hrubý Rohozec Castle, Hruboskalsko Nature Trail and Bozkov Dolomite Caves
Day 3 Hike the Trosky Trail and visit Trosky Castle
Day 4 Visit Jičín, the fairy tale town, and explore its many historical sites
Day 5 Bike the Jizera Cycle Route and visit the towns of Mala Skala and Besedice
Day 6 Take a hot air balloon ride and visit Kozákov
Day 7 Departure

Food and Drink Scene of Bohemian Paradise

Czech Republic is known for its delicious and hearty cuisine. Bohemian Paradise is no exception. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Svíčková: A hearty beef dish served with dumplings and cranberry sauce.

  • Knedlíky: Dumplings, a staple side dish in Czech cuisine, come in many varieties like potato, bread, and fruit.

  • Goulash: A meat stew flavored with paprika, served with bread.

Local Beverages

  • Pivo (beer): Czech Republic is known for its beer, and there are numerous local breweries in the region.

  • Becherovka: A herbal liquor made from a secret recipe of over 20 herbs and spices.

  • Slivovice: A strong fruit brandy made from plums.

Where to Stay in Bohemian Paradise: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

There are many lodging options available in Bohemian Paradise, ranging from high-end hotels to budget-friendly hostels. Here are some top options:

  • Hotel Skalní Mlýn: A four-star hotel located in Hrubá Skála, known for its stunning views and exceptional service.

  • Hotel Králíček: A family-friendly hotel located in Jičín, with comfortable rooms and a traditional Czech restaurant.

  • Hostel Estrella: A friendly hostel with comfortable beds located near the hiking trails and the town of Turnov.

What to Buy in Bohemian Paradise: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Bohemian Paradise offers many unique souvenirs and local products. Here are some top recommendations:

  • Czech crystal: Known for its exceptional quality, Czech crystal makes for an excellent souvenir.

  • Garnet jewelry: Bohemian Paradise is known for its gemstones, especially garnets.

  • Wooden toys: Hand-carved wooden toys are a popular souvenir for children.

Local Culture and Customs of Bohemian Paradise

Czech culture is filled with traditions and customs. Czechs are known for their hospitality and love for beer. Here are some customs to keep in mind:

  • Greetings: A handshake is the most common form of greeting in Czech Republic.

  • Drinking: When toasting, make sure to look each person in the eye and say ‘Na Zdraví!’ (cheers!).

  • Tipping: It’s customary to leave a 10% tip in restaurants and cafes.

Best Time to Visit Bohemian Paradise

The best time to visit Bohemian Paradise is from May to September when the weather is mild, and outdoor activities are in full swing. However, if you’re looking for a snow-covered wonderland, then visit in the winter months from December to February.

Do’s and Don’ts of Bohemian Paradise

Here are some do’s and don’ts to keep in mind when visiting Bohemian Paradise:

Do:

  • Respect nature and local wildlife.

  • Try the local cuisine and beverages.

  • Carry cash as some smaller establishments do not accept credit cards.

Don’t:

  • Litter or damage the natural sites.

  • Expect to find many English speakers in smaller towns and villages.

  • Wear shoes indoors, as it’s considered impolite.

Safety and Health

Safety in Bohemian Paradise

Bohemian Paradise is a safe destination for travelers. However, it’s always important to practice your basic safety rules like locking your doors and looking out for pickpockets.

Health in Bohemian Paradise

Czech Republic has a high standard of healthcare. In case of an emergency, dial 112 for assistance. Make sure you have proper travel insurance before embarking on your journey.

Getting to Bohemian Paradise: Transportation Options and Logistics

Here are the different transportation options available to you when getting to Bohemian Paradise:

Airports Near Bohemian Paradise

  • Václav Havel Airport Prague (PRG): This airport is the closest to Bohemian Paradise, located approximately 1.5 hours away by car.

  • Pardubice Airport (PED): Located approximately 1 hour away by car.

Bus Stations Near Bohemian Paradise

  • Turnov Bus Station: This is the main bus station and is well-connected to all major cities in Czech Republic.

  • Jičín Bus Station: Another major bus station in the region.

Other Options

  • Car rental: If you prefer to drive, car rental services are available at the airports and major cities.

  • Train: The nearest train station is in Turnov and is well-connected to all major cities in Czech Republic.

How to Get Around in Bohemian Paradise

The best way to get around Bohemian Paradise is by car. However, public transportation options like buses and trains are available. You can also rent bicycles and explore the region on two wheels.

Where to Go Next from Bohemian Paradise: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Here are some nearby destinations and excursions to explore:

  • Prague: The capital city of Czech Republic is only an hour and a half away by car or train.

  • Adršpach-Teplice Rocks: A stunning cluster of natural rock formations located 2 hours away by car.

  • Kutná Hora: A well-preserved medieval town known for its stunning architecture and UNESCO world heritage sites.

Conclusion

Bohemian Paradise is a nature-lover’s paradise with rich history and cultural significance. From rock formations and hiking trails to castles and museums, this hidden gem in Czech Republic has something for everyone. With this comprehensive tour guide, you’re all set to explore the best of Bohemian Paradise and make the most of your trip.
