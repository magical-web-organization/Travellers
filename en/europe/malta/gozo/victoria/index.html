Introduction to Victoria

Victoria, also known as Rabat, is the capital city of Gozo, Malta’s sister island. Located in the heart of Gozo, Victoria is a great place to explore if you want to experience the authentic Maltese lifestyle. The city is compact and easy to navigate, making it an ideal destination for a weekend getaway or a week-long vacation.

List of Places to Visit in Victoria

Victoria is a city rich in history, culture, and natural beauty. Here are some of the top attractions you must visit during your trip to Victoria:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Citadel: The Citadel is a fortified city that offers breathtaking views of Victoria and the surrounding countryside. The citadel has been used as a shelter during times of conflict and is now a UNESCO World Heritage Site.
  • Gozo Museum of Archaeology: The Gozo Museum of Archaeology showcases the rich cultural and historical heritage of Gozo, from the Neolithic period to the present day.
  • Cathedral of the Assumption: The Cathedral of the Assumption is an impressive Baroque building that dates back to the 17th century.

Natural Attractions of Victoria: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Victoria Gardens: The Victoria Gardens is a peaceful oasis in the heart of the city. The garden is home to a variety of plants and trees and is an ideal place to relax and enjoy nature.
  • Ta’ Cenc Cliffs: The Ta’ Cenc Cliffs offer panoramic views of the Mediterranean Sea and are a popular spot for hiking and sightseeing.
  • Xlendi Bay: Xlendi Bay is a picturesque inlet that is surrounded by towering cliffs. The bay is a popular destination for swimming, snorkeling, and diving.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Victoria

Victoria and the surrounding areas offer plenty of opportunities for outdoor activities like hiking and biking. Here are some of the best routes you can explore:

  • Victoria Ring Road: The Victoria Ring Road is a 7 km circular route that loops around the city and offers stunning views of the surrounding countryside.
  • Victoria to Xlendi Coastal Walk: This 3 km coastal walk takes you from Victoria to Xlendi Bay and offers breathtaking views of the Mediterranean Sea.
  • Victoria to Santa Lucija Circular Walk: This 10 km circular walk takes you through the countryside and offers a glimpse of the traditional Maltese lifestyle.

Best Things to Do in Victoria: Activities and Tours

Victoria offers plenty of activities and tours for all types of travelers. Here are some of the best things to do in the city:

  • Guided Tour of the Citadel: A guided tour of the Citadel is a must-do activity in Victoria. You’ll explore the historic fortifications, ramparts, and bastions of the city while learning about its fascinating history.
  • Wine Tasting Tour: Gozo is home to several reputable wineries, and a wine tasting tour is an excellent way to discover the island’s wine culture and heritage.
  • Kayaking Tour: Explore the coastline of Gozo on a kayaking tour. You’ll paddle through crystal-clear waters and discover hidden coves and sea caves.

Week-Long Itinerary for Victoria

If you’re planning to spend a week in Victoria, here’s a sample itinerary that will help you make the most of your trip:

  • Day 1-2: Explore the city of Victoria, visit the Citadel, and the Cathedral of the Assumption. Take a guided city tour to learn about the history and culture of the city.
  • Day 3: Visit the Gozo Museum of Archaeology and explore the traditional village of Gharb.
  • Day 4: Take a day trip to Comino Island and explore the Blue Lagoon.
  • Day 5: Explore the countryside of Gozo and hike along the Ta’ Cenc Cliffs.
  • Day 6: Go on a wine tasting tour and visit the local wineries.
  • Day 7: Relax on the beaches of Xlendi Bay and enjoy the Mediterranean Sea.

Food and Drink Scene of Victoria

Victoria is known for its delicious food and wine. Here are some of the must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Ftira: Ftira is a traditional Maltese bread that is soft and airy, similar to a focaccia.
  • Pastizzi: Pastizzi are crispy, flaky pastries that are stuffed with either cheese or peas.
  • Lampuki Pie: Lampuki pie is a savory pastry that is filled with fish, vegetables, and spices.

Local Beverages

  • Cisk Beer: Cisk beer is a traditional Maltese beer that is brewed using locally grown barley and hops.
  • Gozo Wine: Gozo is home to several wineries that produce world-class wines. Try the local red wine, Marsovin’s Gellewza or the white wine, Girgentina.

Where to Stay in Victoria: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Victoria offers a range of accommodation options for all types of travelers. Here are some of the best places to stay in the city:

Hotels

  • Hotel Ta’ Cenc: Hotel Ta’ Cenc is a luxury hotel located on the outskirts of Victoria. The hotel offers stunning views of the Mediterranean Sea and the surrounding countryside.
  • Grand Hotel Gozo: Grand Hotel Gozo is a mid-range hotel located in the heart of Victoria. The hotel offers comfortable rooms and excellent food.

Hostels

  • Hostel Malti: Hostel Malti is a charming hostel located in the heart of Victoria. The hostel offers dormitory-style accommodation and is ideal for budget travelers.
  • Hostel Xewkija: Hostel Xewkija is a budget-friendly hostel located in the village of Xewkija, just a few kilometers from Victoria.

Guesthouses

  • Cornucopia Hotel: Cornucopia Hotel is a family-run guesthouse located in the countryside outside Victoria. The guesthouse offers comfortable rooms and traditional Maltese food.
  • Quaint Boutique Hotel Xewkija: Quaint Boutique Hotel Xewkija is a charming guesthouse located in the village of Xewkija, just a few kilometers from Victoria.

What to Buy in Victoria: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

If you’re looking for souvenirs and local products to bring home, here are some of the best places to shop in Victoria:

  • The Old Prison: The Old Prison is now a center for artisanal crafts and is home to several shops selling local products like glassware and ceramics.
  • Gozo Glass: Gozo Glass is a glassblowing factory that produces beautifully crafted glassware, such as vases, bowls, and jewelry.
  • Victoria Market: Victoria Market is a lively market that sells fresh produce, souvenirs, and local products like honey and olive oil.

Local Culture and Customs of Victoria

Malta is a deeply religious country, and Victoria is no exception. The city is full of religious celebrations and festivals, which are an important part of the local culture. Here are some customs and traditions you should be aware of when visiting Victoria:

  • Dress Code: When visiting religious sites like churches and cathedrals, conservative clothing is required. This means covering your shoulders and knees.
  • Religious Festivals: Victoria is home to several religious festivals throughout the year, including the Feast of Santa Marija in August and the Good Friday processions.
  • Language: The official language of Malta is Maltese, although English is widely spoken and understood.

Best Time to Visit Victoria

The best time to visit Victoria is in the spring and autumn when temperatures are mild, and the crowds are smaller. During the summer months, the city can get crowded, and the temperatures can soar, making it uncomfortable to explore. Winter is also an excellent time to visit if you don’t mind the cooler temperatures.

Do’s and Don’ts of Victoria

When visiting Victoria, here are some do’s and don’ts to keep in mind:

Do:

  • Dress conservatively when visiting religious sites.
  • Respect the local culture and customs.
  • Try the local food and wine.

Don’t:

  • Sunbathe or swim in public places in conservative clothing.
  • Take pictures of people without their permission.
  • Litter or damage historical sites and cultural property.

Safety and Health

Victoria is a safe city, and violent crime is rare. However, you should take common-sense precautions like keeping your valuables secure, staying in well-lit areas at night, and avoiding unlicensed taxis. In terms of health, Malta has a good healthcare system, and hospitals in Victoria offer excellent care.

Getting to Victoria: Transportation Options and Logistics

Victoria is located on the island of Gozo and can be reached by ferry or helicopter. Here are some transportation options to consider:

Airports Near Victoria

  • Malta International Airport: Malta International Airport is located in Luqa, Malta, and is the primary airport serving the country. From the airport, you can take a taxi or a bus to the ferry terminal in Cirkewwa.

Bus Stations Near Victoria

  • Victoria Bus Station: Victoria Bus Station is located in the center of the city and is the main bus station on the island of Gozo. From here, you can take buses to other parts of the island, including the ferry terminal in Mgarr.

Other Options

  • Helicopter: If you’re looking for a more luxurious way to reach Victoria, you can take a helicopter from Malta International Airport to Gozo.

How to Get Around in Victoria

Victoria is a small city, and most of the attractions are within walking distance of each other. If you prefer, you can also rent a car or a scooter to explore the island. Buses are also available, and there are several taxi companies available in the city.

Where to Go Next from Victoria: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

If you’re looking for other places to visit in Malta, here are some destinations to consider:

  • Valletta: Valletta is the capital city of Malta and is a UNESCO World Heritage Site. The city is home to several historic sites, including St. John’s Co-Cathedral and the Grand Harbour.
  • Mdina: Mdina is a fortified city that dates back to the 8th century. The city is known for its stunning architecture and historic landmarks, including St. Paul’s Cathedral and the Mdina Gate.
  • Blue Grotto: The Blue Grotto is a natural wonder located on the southern coast of Malta. The grotto is a series of sea caves that are renowned for their stunning blue waters.

Conclusion

Victoria is an excellent destination for those looking to experience the authentic Maltese lifestyle. With its rich history, cultural heritage, and natural beauty, the city offers something for everyone. Whether you’re interested in exploring the historic Citadel, hiking along the Ta’ Cenc Cliffs, or savoring delicious Maltese cuisine, Victoria is sure to enchant you. So pack your bags, and start planning your trip now!
