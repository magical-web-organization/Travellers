Introduction to Comino

Comino is a small, picturesque island located in the northeast of Malta, in Europe. The island, which has an area of just 3.5 square kilometers, is a hidden gem and remains mostly untouched. With crystal-clear waters, hidden sandy beaches, rugged coastline, fascinating caves, and untouched nature, it’s a perfect destination for those seeking relaxation, adventure, or exploration.

Comino is a car-free island, meaning there is no transportation available. Therefore, the only way to move around is by foot, bicycle, or boat. The quiet and peaceful surroundings offer a refreshing break from the bustling cities.

In this complete guide to Comino, we will take you through the top sites to visit, the best hiking trails, food and drink culture, where to stay and more, so you can plan your trip to this stunning island with ease.

List of Places to Visit in Comino

Comino is a paradise for nature’s lovers and offers a variety of attractions to see.

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Visit the world-renowned Blue Lagoon, Crystal Lagoon and Santa Maria Tower in Comino’s southern part with its beautyful clear waters. The island itself is rural and uninhabited, but the tower symbolises cominos past as guard post.

Natural Attractions of Comino: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Comino is well known for its stunning natural beauty, with a vast range of outdoor adventures and scenic views to enjoy.

You’ll find impressively formed cliffs, crystal blue water and hidden coves while you hike or bike around Comino. The Santa Marija Bay area offers the best of Comino’s beaches with its soft sand and excellent snorkelling spots. The Blue Lagoon and Crystal Lagoon are also recommended for swimming, diving, and water sports.

Apart from beaches, there are a number of hidden spots on this tiny island waiting to be explored. Inside the caves found over the island, you’ll discover geological rock formations that reflect the island’s rich history. For bird enthusiasts, the island provides habitat for many rare species, particularly the comino shearwater.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Comino

Comino provides different types of trails that are sure to impress both beginner and advanced hikers. Hiking or biking gives you the opportunity to escape the crowds, enjoy the scenery, and explore the peacefulness of the island.

One of the highlights of Comino’s hiking trails is the “Comino Trail,” which takes approximately 2-3 hours to complete each lap. The easiest route is around the coast on the walking trails; this takes you around the island in 3 hours, depending on how many stops you take.

Best Things to Do in Comino: Activities and Tours

Comino offers many things to make your visit unforgettable. Here are some recommendations for unique experiences:

  • Boat Tour: A boat trip is a must as it provides breathtaking views of the island from different angles and gives the chance to visit Blue Lagoon, the Crystal Lagoon, St. Mary’s Tower and other spots. You can rent private boat tours or join a public tour.
  • Kayaking and SUPing: Stand up paddling and kayaking offer you the chance to connect with the environment and explore it up-close. Several shops provide rental services for equipment, or guided tours.
  • Scuba Diving and Snorkeling: Comino’s waters are home to a variety of fish and plant species, making it a popular destination for diving and snorkelling. Tours and equipment rentals are available.

Week-Long Itinerary for Comino

Here’s a sample itinerary for a week-long stay on the island:

  • Day 1: Arrive on Comino, take part in diving and snorkelling
  • Day 2: Go on a boat tour of the island
  • Day 3: Hike or bike around the island
  • Day 4: Kayak or SUP on the Blue Lagoon
  • Day 5: Take some downtime and relax by the beach
  • Day 6: Visit Santa Marija Bay and explore the caves
  • Day 7: Check-out and depart Comino

Customize this itinerary to fit your preferences, tour style, and travel duration.

Food and Drink Scene of Comino

Comino might not provide a broad selection of gastronomic options, though the local cuisine is worth tasting, and the flavors are unique.

Must-Try Dishes

There are a few local dishes visitors have to try when visiting Comino:

  • Stuffat Tal-Fenek: Rabbit stew is a traditional Maltese dish and highly recommended.
  • Qassatat: Small savoury pastries filled with cheese and sometimes also featuring spinach.
  • Pastizzi: A savoury pastry filled with either ricotta cheese or peas.

Local Beverages

Try out Comino’s locally brewed beer, Cisk, for beer enthusiasts. Alternatively, to raise your spirits, why not try Malta’s unique spirit, Kinnie, a refreshing citrus soft drink mixed with bitters.

Where to Stay in Comino: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

As Comino is uninhabited, there’s only one hotel, the Comino Hotel, which is just a stone’s throw from the Blue Lagoon. Another alternative is just to camp on the island.

If you are looking to stay on the mainland, nearby islands such as Malta and Gozo are perfect, and only a short ferry ride away.

What to Buy in Comino: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

The best souvenirs and local products to take home from Comino are handmade, local crafts, Maltese lace, sand from the Blue Lagoon and Gozo salt.

Local Culture and Customs of Comino

Comino is a small island with a friendly community. Most people on the island speak Maltese and/ or English. Observe local customs, dress respectfully when visiting sacred sites, and try to learn some basic Maltese phrases.

Best Time to Visit Comino

The best time to visit Comino is between May and September when temperatures range from 25°C to 30°C, offering the best weather for diving and water sports.

Do’s and Don’ts of Comino

Do:

  • Explore the Blue Lagoon and the Crystal Lagoon.
  • Respect the local culture and customs.
  • Take advantage of the island trail to bike or hike.

Don’t:

  • Litter: make sure to clean after yourself
  • Avoid bringing food or alcohol to the island.
  • Disrespect the natural environment.

Safety and Health

Apart from routine issues such as sunburn, Comino is generally known for being a safe travel destination.

Tourists visiting Comino are advised to bring mosquito repellents and consider check their skin for ticks.

Safety in Comino

Despite the generally good state of affairs regarding tourists’ safety, visitors to Comino are advised to take regular precautions such as controlling your expenditures and keeping an eye and control of your belongings.

Health in Comino

While there are no major health concerns travelling to Comino, it is wise to travel with sufficient insurance, maintain good hygiene, and stay conscious of COVID-19 guidelines.

Getting to Comino: Transportation Options and Logistics

There are several transportation options available to reach Comino.

Airports Near Comino

Malta International Airport is the closest airport to Comino located at a distance of 31km.

Bus Stations Near Comino

The closest bus station to Comino is located in Cirkewwa.

Other Options

The best transport alternative is by taking a ferry service that operates daily from Cirkewwa Harbour, Malta.

How to Get Around in Comino

The only way to get around Comino is by walking, biking, or boat. Choosing to walk around the island gives you the chance to explore different sites and escape the crowds. In case you’re not tiny enough to walk on water, boat tours and rental boats to the beaches are available.

Where to Go Next from Comino: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

With two other neighbouring islands, Malta and Gozo, your travel experience won’t be complete. Both islands have vast natural resources and incredible gastronomy, not to mention rich historical sites.

Conclusion

Comino offers a unique blend of adventure, serenity, and beauty found nowhere else. From the natural beauty to the distinct culture and cuisines, Comino is a true hidden gem and destination worth adding to your travel itinerary plans. Visit Comino and explore its hidden wonders and natural beauty.
