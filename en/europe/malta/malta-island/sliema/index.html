Ultimate Travel Guide for Sliema, Malta: A 2021 Tourist’s Handbook

Tucked away on the northeastern coast of Malta, Sliema is a gem of a town that attracts visitors from near and far. Renowned for its stunning coastlines, buzzing promenades, and vibrant culture, this coastal town promises a truly unique and unforgettable experience.

Whether you’re a first-time visitor or have been to Malta before, this comprehensive guide will give you everything you need to know about Sliema. From cultural landmarks to natural vistas, we’ve got you covered. So, let’s dive right in and explore the town’s fascinating history and amazing sights.

Introduction to Sliema

Sliema is a charming seaside town located on the northeastern coast of Malta, known for its beautiful sea views and unique culture. Its rich history is reflected in the peculiar architecture of the buildings and its ancient churches. With Malta’s capital city Valletta just across the bay, Sliema is a popular destination for those keen on exploring Malta’s history and culture.

Sliema has a Mediterranean climate with mild, wet winters and warm, sunny summers, making it a great destination all year round.

Sliema boasts cultural and natural landmarks that guarantee an unforgettable experience.

The town of Sliema is rich in history and culture, and there are plenty of museums and historical places that allow visitors to delve deep into Malta’s past.

Food and Drink Scene of Sliema

Maltese cuisine is synonymous with Mediterranean cuisine and has experienced several cultural influences over the centuries.

Must-Try Dishes

  1. Pastizzi: a puff-pastry-filled dough, typically either with ricotta or mushyd peas.
  2. Ftira: A sandwich made of Maltese bread with filling.
  3. Zalzett tal-Basal: A Maltese sausage flavored with onions.

Local Beverages

  1. Cisk Pale Ale Beer
  2. Kinnie, a distinctive bitter-sweet citrus-flavored soft drink

What to Buy in Sliema: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Sliema is famous for its handcrafted ceramics and glassware, making it the ideal place to purchase unique and locally made souvenirs.

Local Culture and Customs of Sliema

It is always good to learn about local cultures and customs when visiting a destination.

Maltese culture is heavily influenced by the Mediterranean, Arab, and Catholic religions and has a rich history and traditions.

Best Time to Visit Sliema

The best time to visit Malta is the summer months between June and August as the weather is sunny, and the temperatures are warm.

Do’s and Don’ts of Sliema

Do:

  1. Do dress appropriately when visiting religious sites or attending formal events.
  2. Do enjoy Maltese culture and traditions by participating in local events and festivals.
  3. Do explore Sliema’s natural beauty through countryside walks or outdoor activities.
  4. Do try the local cuisine.

Don’t:

  1. Don’t park illegally or park in unauthorized parking spaces.
  2. Don’t forget to tip in restaurants or cafes.
  3. Don’t be disrespectful when visiting religious sites or cultural events.

Safety and Health

Safety in Sliema

Sliema, like all Maltese towns, is a relatively safe place to visit. However, visitors should be aware of pickpocketing and car thefts.

Health in Sliema

Malta’s healthcare system is of high quality, and emergency care is widely available through public and private hospitals and clinics.

Getting to Sliema: Transportation Options and Logistics

There are several ways to get to Sliema, including by plane, bus, taxi and rental cars.

Airports Near Sliema

Malta International Airport is the main entry point into Malta and is located just outside of Valletta, only about 20 minutes from Sliema.

Bus Stations Near Sliema

The Valletta Bus Station serves Sliema and the rest of Malta, making it easy to get around the island.

Other Options

Car rental or taxi services are good options for exploring Malta at your convenience.

How to Get Around in Sliema

Sliema is a relatively small town, making it very easy to walk around or use public transportation to get around to that places of interests.

Where to Go Next from Sliema: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Conclusion

Sliema is a truly unique and special destination. Its picturesque coastlines, buzzing promenades, and vibrant culture make it a worthwhile destination, regardless of your interests. With this comprehensive guide , you’re now ready to embark on an unforgettable journey of a lifetime!
