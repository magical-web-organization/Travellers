Introduction to St. Julian’s

St. Julian’s is a well-known tourist destination located on the northeast coast of Malta Island. This picturesque town is famous for its vibrant nightlife, scenic views, and cultural attractions. St. Julian’s is an ideal place to visit for those who love to explore historical places, enjoy outdoor adventures, indulge in delicious food, and experience the local culture.

List of Places to Visit in St. Julian’s

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

If you’re interested in learning about St. Julian’s rich history and cultural heritage, there are many museums and historical places worth visiting. Here are some of the must-visit cultural attractions in St. Julian’s:

  • Spinola Palace: This 17th-century palace is a cultural icon and a symbol of Malta’s medieval past.
  • St. George’s Parish Church: This church is one of the most important religious sites in St. Julian’s, and it’s worth a visit to appreciate the beautiful architecture and historical significance.
  • Love Monument: A contemporary statue of two lovers embracing each other that is located next to the Spinola Bay Promenade.

Natural Attractions of St. Julian’s: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

If you’re a nature lover, St. Julian’s offers a variety of outdoor adventures and scenic views. Here are some of the must-visit natural attractions in St. Julian’s:

  • St. Julian’s Bay: This picturesque bay is the heart of St. Julian’s, offering a beautiful spot for a swim or a boat ride.
  • Balluta Bay: A natural rocky beach that offers excellent views of the Mediterranean Sea and is ideal for a romantic walk.
  • Portomaso Marina: A great destination to watch boats, shop, or dine.

Hiking/Biking Routes of St. Julian’s

St. Julian’s has numerous hiking and biking trails that offer a unique way to explore the town. Some of the best routes are:

  • Pembroke Park: A large urban park with extensive green areas and walking and biking paths.
  • Ta’Qali National Park: A popular destination among hikers that offers scenic views of Malta’s natural beauty and plenty of opportunities for taking photos.
  • Wied il-Ghasri: A scenic gorge that offers a challenging hiking trail and stunning views of the Mediterranean coastline.

Best Things to Do in St. Julian’s: Activities and Tours

St. Julian’s is not only about sightseeing but also about various activities and tours. Here are some of the best things to do in St. Julian’s:

  • Sailing and boat tours: There are several tour operators that offer boat tours around St. Julian’s Bay, offering a unique way to discover the town from the sea.
  • Water sports: St. Julian’s is a prime destination for water sports enthusiasts. From jet skiing to kayaking to paragliding, there are endless options for those who love adventure.
  • Nightlife: St. Julian’s has a reputation for its lively nightlife, with a wide range of bars, pubs, and nightclubs that cater to all kinds of tastes and preferences.

Week-Long Itinerary for St. Julian’s

If you’re planning to visit St. Julian’s for a week, here’s a sample itinerary to help you make the most out of your trip:

Day 1: Visit the Love Monument, Spinola Palace, and St. George’s Parish Church in the morning. Spend the afternoon in St. Julian’s Bay.

Day 2: Explore Balluta Bay in the morning, and head to Portomaso Marina for lunch. In the afternoon, visit the Malta National Aquarium.

Day 3: Take a hiking or biking tour at Pembroke Park in the morning. In the afternoon, visit the Ta’ Qali National Park.

Day 4: Join a boat tour to explore St. Julian’s Bay and Blue Lagoon in the morning. In the afternoon, experience the vibrant nightlife of St. Julian’s.

Day 5: Visit the War Museum in Valletta to learn about Malta’s history in the morning. In the afternoon, Shop in Sliema.

Day 6: Take a day trip to Gozo Island in the morning and explore Comino Island in the afternoon.

Day 7: Spend the last day relaxing at the beach or exploring the town’s scenic spots you don’t want to miss out on before heading home.

Food and Drink Scene of St. Julian’s

St. Julian’s has a vibrant and eclectic food scene that caters to all tastes and preferences. The town is known for its signature dishes that reflect the Maltese culture and heritage. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Ftira: a popular sandwich made of Maltese bread that’s filled with vegetables, olives, and various meats.
  • Stuffat Tal-Fenek: a traditional Maltese stew that’s prepared with rabbit meat.
  • Pastizzi: a savory pastry that’s filled with either cheese or mushy peas.

Local Beverages

  • Kinnie: a non-alcoholic carbonated drink made from bitter oranges.
  • Cisk Beer: a locally brewed beer that’s popular among the locals and tourists.
  • Maltese Wine: Malta has a long history of wine production, and you can find many local varieties in the town’s bars and restaurants.

Where to Stay in St. Julian’s: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

There are plenty of accommodation options in St. Julian’s suited for different budgets and preferences.

  • Hilton Malta: a luxury hotel that offers stunning views of the Mediterranean Sea and a host of amenities.
  • Cavalieri Art Hotel: an elegant boutique hotel that’s located close to St. Julian’s Bay and offers a panoramic view of Malta’s shoreline.
  • Hostels: some of the popular ones are Boho Hostel, St. Julian’s Bay Hotel, and Marco Polo Hostel.

What to Buy in St. Julian’s: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

St. Julian’s offers an extensive range of souvenirs and local products that make for excellent gifts for loved ones back home. Here are some of the top items to buy in St. Julian’s:

  • Maltese Lace: a traditional lace that is handcrafted by Maltese artisans and is used for decorating clothing and accessories.
  • Mdina Glass: this handmade glassware is a unique product of Malta and is appreciated for its intricate design and quality.
  • Filigree Jewelry: Malta is known for its stunning filigree jewelry that’s crafted from gold or silver wire twisted in fanciful patterns.

Local Culture and Customs of St. Julian’s

The Maltese people are proud of their unique cultural heritage, and their traditions and customs reflect their rich history. Here are some important ones to know:

  • Language: The official languages are Maltese and English, but most of the locals can speak Italian as well.
  • Religion: Roman Catholicism is the main religion on the island, and religious festivals are an essential part of the culture.
  • Dress Code: Malta is a conservative country, and it’s advisable to dress modestly, especially when visiting religious sites.

Best Time to Visit St. Julian’s

The best time to go to St. Julian’s is during the summer season, from June to September, when the weather is warm and pleasant, and there are loads of outdoor activities to enjoy. However, the town is an attractive destination all year round, with mild winters and plenty of indoor attractions to explore.

Do’s and Don’ts of St. Julian’s

Do:

  • Respect the local culture and traditions.
  • Carry your sunscreen and hat while visiting outdoor places.
  • Try the local cuisine and drinks.

Don’t:

  • Leave your valuables unattended.
  • Get into illegal activities such as drug trafficking.

Safety and Health

Safety in St. Julian’s

St. Julian’s is a relatively safe town, and the crime rate is low. However, it’s advisable to be mindful of your surroundings and keep your valuables safe.

Health in St. Julian’s

Malta has a well-developed healthcare system, and there are several private and public hospitals in St. Julian’s. It’s advisable to have health insurance that covers international visits, in case of any medical emergencies.

Getting to St. Julian’s: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near St. Julian’s

The Malta International Airport is the main gateway to St. Julian’s, and it’s located around 20 minutes drive from the town center.

Bus Stations Near St. Julian’s

St. Julian’s is well-connected by bus routes that operate around Malta Island, and there are several bus stops in the town.

Other Options

Taxis, rental cars, and private transfers are also popular ways to get around St. Julian’s and Malta Island.

How to Get Around in St. Julian’s

St. Julian’s is a small town that’s easy to navigate on foot or by bike. Public transportation, including buses and taxis, is also available.

Where to Go Next from St. Julian’s: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

There are several nearby destinations and excursion options that you can consider while in St. Julian’s. Some popular options are:

  • Valletta: Malta’s capital city that’s rich in history and culture.
  • Gozo Island: a peaceful and serene island that’s ideal for a day full of relaxation.
  • Blue Grotto: a sea cave that’s located on the southern coast of Malta and is famous for its stunning blue waters.

Conclusion

St. Julian’s is a mesmerizing town that offers a perfect blend of history, culture, adventure, and entertainment. With plenty of attractions and activities, a vibrant food scene, and excellent accommodation options, St. Julian’s is an ideal destination for solo travelers, couples, and families alike. Whether you’re looking for a serene vacation or a fun-filled adventure, St. Julian’s is sure to leave you with unforgettable memories.
