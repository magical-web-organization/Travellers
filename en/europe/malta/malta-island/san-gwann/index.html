Introduction to San Gwann

San Gwann is a small town located in the central region of Malta that boasts of a rich history, beautiful architecture, and diverse cultures. The town has become a popular travel destination for tourists who want to explore the authentic Maltese lifestyle, indulge in the delicious cuisine, and experience the Mediterranean climate. San Gwann has something for everyone: from historic sites, museums, and natural attractions, to activities, tours, and accommodations.

List of Places to Visit in San Gwann

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

San Gwann is home to numerous museums and historical sites that offer a glimpse into Maltese heritage and culture. The Heritage Trail is a must-visit attraction that takes visitors on a tour of the town’s historic landmarks, churches, and other notable sites. Other must-see cultural sites in San Gwann include the Church of Our Lady of Lourdes, Villa Francia, and the San Gwann Library.

Natural Attractions of San Gwann: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

San Gwann has a lot to offer outdoor enthusiasts, including scenic hiking trails, beautiful parks, and stunning beaches. The San Anton Gardens and the Ta’ Qali National Park are among the best places to relax and soak up the beautiful natural scenery. Adventurous travelers can also take part in water activities like scuba diving, kayaking, and paddleboarding at nearby beaches and bays.

Hiking/Biking Routes of San Gwann

San Gwann is ideal for those who want to explore beautiful hiking and cycling routes in the Mediterranean climate. The town is surrounded by several scenic routes like the Victoria Lines, Malta’s most extended line of fortification, and the Dingli Cliffs, offering stunning sea views. The countryside is full of winding roads with beautiful views of hills, vales and the sea.

Best Things to Do in San Gwann: Activities and Tours

Visitors to San Gwann can choose from a variety of activities and tours, depending on their preferences. For a unique experience, you can check out the city’s guided tours, where experienced guides walk you through the city’s most historic and culturally significant locations. Other popular activities include visiting the local wineries, taking boat tours to the nearby islands, horse-riding, or attending local festivals.

Week-Long Itinerary for San Gwann

Day 1: Visit the St. John’s Co-Cathedral in Valletta and then explore the old city.

Day 2: Take a hike on the Victoria Lines and enjoy the stunning sea views.

Day 3: Explore San Gwann’s museums followed by trying the local specialties, including pastizzi and rabbit stew.

Day 4: Take an early morning dip at Golden Bay Beach followed by some water activities like kayaking or snorkeling.

Day 5: Visit the nearby village of Mdina, a fortified city, and a former capital of Malta.

Day 6: Visit the Blue Grotto Cave and the Hagar Qim and Mnajdra Temples of Malta.

Day 7: Go on a walking tour of the San Anton Gardens and the Ta ‘ Qali National Park, followed by souvenirs shopping at one of the town’s street markets.

Food and Drink Scene of San Gwann

Must-Try Dishes

The Maltese cuisine is a blend of Mediterranean flavors with a unique twist that is sure to excite your taste buds. Some of the must-try traditional dishes in San Gwann include rabbit stew, pastizzi (maltese pastries), fried tal-majjal and lampuki pie (fish pie). Other popular Maltese foods are Gozo cheese, olives, ham, and honey.

Local Beverages

No visit to Malta would be complete without trying one of Malta’s local drinks, particularly Kinnie, a locally produced soft drink, or Cisk beer, one of the most popular beers in Malta. Wine lovers can sample Malta’s unique variety of grape varieties and its rich-colored wines.

Where to Stay in San Gwann: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

San Gwann has several lodging options to suit travelers of all budgets and preferences. Some of the popular options include the Valletta Boutique Apartments for those who want to stay in the heart of Malta, and the Palazzo Consiglia, a stylish boutique hotel located in the city’s old quarter. Other accommodations include Hostel Jones, a trendy hostel, and the Corinthia Palace Hotel and Spa, offering luxury and ultimate comfort.

What to Buy in San Gwann: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

San Gwann has a bustling street market that sells everything from fresh produce to souvenirs. Some of the most popular souvenirs to bring home from San Gwann include artisanal jewelry, pottery, and traditional Maltese lace. Local produce like dried tomatoes, olive oil and wine are also good options for a memorable gourmet experience in Malta.

Local Culture and Customs of San Gwann

San Gwann has a rich cultural heritage that can be seen in its architecture, food, music and religion. Most Maltese people are Catholic so it’s advised to dress modestly if entering churches. The Maltese people are also known for their friendly and hospitable nature, and it is always appreciated to greet the people you meet with a “hello” or “how are you?” The town has several festivals throughout the year, which showcase the best of Maltese culture.

Best Time to Visit San Gwann

The best time to visit San Gwann is during the months of April to June or September to November when the weather is mild and less crowded. The summer months between July and August are hot and crowded, and it can be overwhelming for visitors who prefer a peaceful and comfortable environment to explore.

Do’s and Don’ts of San Gwann

Do:

  • Dress modestly when visiting churches and religious sites.
  • Keep your surroundings clean and tidy.
  • Be polite and courteous at all times to locals and fellow travelers.
  • Learn some basic Maltese phrases using useful apps like Duolingo or Memrise.

Don’t:

  • Use drugs or engage in any illegal activities.
  • Litter the streets.
  • Photograph locals without their permission.
  • Insult or criticize the local culture.

Safety and Health

Safety in San Gwann

San Gwann is a safe town for tourists with minimal risks of violent crime. Visitors should be cautious of pickpocketing in crowded areas like street markets and bus rides. Likewise, it’s best to avoid any dealings with illegal vendors and scams.

Health in San Gwann

The town has several clinics and hospitals that offer medical care to tourists. However, it’s advisable to have travel or health insurance to ensure you’re covered for any emergencies. Visitors should stay hydrated and protect themselves from the harsh sun rays, especially during summer months.

Getting to San Gwann: Transportation Options and Logistics

San Gwann is accessible by several modes of transportation such as taxis, buses, and rental cars. The Malta International Airport is the closest airport, located around 8 km from the town center, followed by the Valetta bus terminal to San Gwann, which is a fifteen-minute ride-visit Malta public transport website for timetables.

Airports Near San Gwann

Malta International airport is the closest airport to San Gwann. From the airport, visitors can take a taxi, bus or car rental to reach the town center within 20-30 minutes.

Bus Stations Near San Gwann

The Valletta bus terminal is the closest bus station connecting to San Gwann with a frequency of every 15 minutes.

Other Options

Alternatively, renting a car is recommended as the island is rather compact and easy to navigate.

How to Get Around in San Gwann

Visitors can explore San Gwann by foot as it’s a small town. As an alternative, bicycle rentals are readily available all across the town. Taxis or renting a car is considered a convenient way of touring the town and the rest of the islands.

Where to Go Next from San Gwann: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

San Gwann serves as a gateway to several Maltese destinations, including the capital city, Valletta, and the nearby fishing village of St. Julians. Visitors can also take a short ferry ride to the neighboring island of Gozo, known for its excellent beaches and natural attractions. Additionally, travelers can experience the island’s nightlife at the lively Paceville entertainment district.

Conclusion

San Gwann is a hidden gem in Malta, offering a perfect blend of rich culture, history, natural beauty, and outdoor activities. Travelers who want to escape the crowd and relax can unwind on beautiful beaches or enjoy scenic routes on a hike or bicycle ride. For a memorable experience of the Mediterranean lifestyle, San Gwann offers endless possibilities.
