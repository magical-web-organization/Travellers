A Comprehensive Travel Guide to Cottonera, Malta

Located in the south-eastern part of Malta, Cottonera is a collection of three fortified cities - Vittoriosa, Cospicua, and Senglea - known for their rich history, culture, and architecture. Surrounded by the sea on three sides, Cottonera offers scenic views and outdoor activities along with plenty of cultural and historical attractions.

Introduction to Cottonera

Cottonera, also known as the Three Cities, was built by the Knights of St. John in the 16th century to defend the Grand Harbor from the Ottomans. Vittoriosa, the first city to be built, is home to many of Malta’s historical landmarks, palaces, and museums. Cospicua, the largest of the three cities, is famous for its dockyards and churches, while Senglea is known for its unique architecture and viewpoints.

List of Places to Visit in Cottonera

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • The Inquisitor’s Palace: A 16th-century palace that served as the headquarters for the Inquisition in Malta, now a museum showcasing Malta’s history and culture.
  • The Malta at War Museum: A museum located in Vittoriosa showcasing Malta’s role in World War II.
  • St. Lawrence Church: A baroque church located in Vittoriosa, built in the 17th century, known for its elaborate decorations and paintings.
  • The Maritime Museum: A museum located in Cospicua showcasing Malta’s naval history.
  • Senglea Basilica: A church located in Senglea, built in the 17th century, known for its beautiful frescoes and paintings.

Natural Attractions of Cottonera: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • The Cottonera Lines: A series of fortifications built by the Knights of St. John in the 17th century, offering panoramic views of the Grand Harbor and the surrounding areas.
  • Gardjola Gardens: Located in Senglea, offering breathtaking views of the Valletta skyline.
  • Senglea Point: A pier in Senglea offering stunning views of the surrounding area.
  • Fort St. Angelo: A fort located in Vittoriosa, originally built by the Arabs but later modified by the Knights of St. John, offering views of the Grand Harbor.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Cottonera

  • The Three Cities Walk: A walking tour of the three cities, taking you through narrow alleys, historic sites, and beautiful viewpoints.
  • Senglea to St. Peter’s Pool Walk: A stunning hike from Senglea to St. Peter’s Pool, a natural swimming pool located near Marsaxlokk with crystal-clear water and rocks for cliff-jumping.
  • The Vittoriosa Waterfront: A scenic walk along the Vittoriosa waterfront, offering stunning views of the harbor.

Best Things to Do in Cottonera: Activities and Tours

  • Harbor Cruise: Take a leisurely cruise around the Grand Harbor and see the historic fortifications and landmarks from the water.
  • Electric Car Tour: Take an electric car tour of Cottonera and explore the narrow streets and alleys of the three cities.
  • Cooking Class: Learn how to make traditional Maltese dishes in a cooking class led by a local chef.
  • Laser Tag: Enjoy a fun game of laser tag in an outdoor arena located in Cospicua.

Food and Drink Scene of Cottonera

Must-Try Dishes

  • Pastizzi: A savory pastry filled with either ricotta cheese or peas, a common snack found all over Malta.
  • Fenek: A traditional Maltese dish of rabbit stew, usually served with potatoes and vegetables.
  • Aljotta: A fish soup made with garlic and tomatoes, served with bread.

Local Beverages

  • Irxiexa: A locally-produced sweet liqueur made with cherries and honey.
  • Kinnie: A soft drink made from bitter oranges and spices, a popular local drink.

Where to Stay in Cottonera: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Casa del Cotoner: A 17th-century guesthouse located in Vittoriosa, offering traditional Maltese architecture and modern amenities.
  • Cavalieri Art Hotel: A luxury hotel located in St. Julian’s, offering stunning views of the Mediterranean and modern amenities.
  • Cugo Gran Macina: A converted 16th-century grain silo located in Senglea, offering luxurious rooms and stunning views.

What to Buy in Cottonera: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Filigree jewelry: Handmade jewelry made with delicate silver or gold wires, a traditional Maltese craft.
  • Lace products: Traditional Maltese lace made by local artisans, used for tablecloths, doilies, and clothing.
  • Gbejniet: A type of local cheese made from sheep’s milk, usually sold in packs or rounds.

Local Culture and Customs of Cottonera

Malta is a predominantly Catholic country, and many of the local customs and traditions revolve around the church. The people of Malta are friendly and welcoming, and visitors are expected to dress modestly when visiting churches or other places of worship.

Best Time to Visit Cottonera

The best time to visit Cottonera is from April to June or September to November when the weather is mild and the crowds are thinner. July and August are peak tourist months, and the crowds can be overwhelming, and the temperatures can be high.

Do’s and Don’ts of Cottonera

Do:

  • Dress modestly when visiting churches or other places of worship.
  • Try traditional Maltese dishes and drinks.
  • Take a harbor cruise and explore the Grand Harbor from the water.

Don’t:

  • Touch or climb on historic landmarks or fortifications.
  • Speak loudly or disturb the peace in residential areas.
  • Wear revealing clothing when visiting churches or other places of worship.

Safety and Health

Safety in Cottonera

Cottonera is a safe destination for travelers, but visitors are advised to take general safety precautions, such as keeping an eye on their belongings and avoiding poorly-lit areas at night.

Health in Cottonera

Visitors to Malta are advised to have up-to-date vaccinations for Hepatitis A and B. The local tap water is safe to drink, but visitors may prefer bottled water.

Getting to Cottonera: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Cottonera

Malta International Airport is located approximately 5 km from Cottonera.

Bus Stations Near Cottonera

The main bus station in Valletta offers connections to Cottonera and other destinations across Malta.

Other Options

Taxis and car rentals are readily available in Malta. Visitors can also take a ferry from Valletta to Cottonera.

How to Get Around in Cottonera

Many of the attractions in Cottonera are located within walking distance of each other. Visitors can also take a bus or taxi for longer distances or rent a bicycle for a fun and eco-friendly way to explore the area.

Where to Go Next from Cottonera: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Valletta: The capital city of Malta, known for its stunning architecture and historical landmarks.
  • Marsaxlokk: A small fishing village, famous for its colorful boats and seafood restaurants.
  • Mdina: A fortified medieval city located in the heart of Malta.

Conclusion

Cottonera is a gem of a destination, offering a perfect blend of history, culture, and nature. Visitors can explore the narrow alleys, historic landmarks, and scenic viewpoints before relaxing in a local restaurant or bar. With its location close to other destinations in Malta, Cottonera is the perfect base for exploring the rest of the island.
