Introduction to Malta Island

Malta Island is a small Mediterranean island nation located south of Sicily, Italy. It is known for its historic architecture, stunning coastline, and crystal-clear waters. The island is steeped in history and tradition, with a mix of cultures that reflects its position as a crossroads between Europe, Africa, and the Middle East. Malta Island is a year-round destination with mild winters and hot summers, making it an ideal location for visitors seeking to indulge in outdoor activities, archaeology, or just soaking up the sun.

Food and Drink Scene of Malta Island

Malta Island has a diverse food scene that draws influences from various cultural backgrounds. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages.

Must-Try Dishes

  • Pastizzi (flaky pastry filled with ricotta or mushy peas)
  • Hobż biż-żejt (Maltese bread with tomato paste, tuna, olives, and capers)
  • Ġbejniet (sheep’s milk cheese)
  • Fresh Seafood (salmon, swordfish, and squid)

Local Beverages

  • Kinnie (bitter orange flavored carbonated drink)
  • Cisk Beer (local lager)
  • Maltese Wine (various types of red, white, and rosé wines)

Best Time to Visit Malta Island

The best time to visit Malta Island is from April to June and September to November when the weather is mild, and the crowds are fewer. However, if you prefer a lively atmosphere, the summertime (July to August) is best, with several festivals, markets, and beach parties taking place.

Do’s and Don’ts of Malta Island

When visiting Malta Island, here are some do’s and don’ts to keep in mind:

Do:

  • Dress appropriately when visiting religious sites
  • Respect local traditions and customs
  • Take part in local festivities and events

Don’t:

  • Sunbathe or swim in public places

Safety and Health

Safety in Malta Island

Malta Island is considered a safe destination for tourists. However, visitors should take the same precautions they would in any other location to avoid theft, pickpocketing, or other crimes.

Health in Malta Island

Malta Island has a good healthcare system, with both public and private hospitals available. However, it’s recommended that visitors take out travel insurance before arriving in Malta Island.
