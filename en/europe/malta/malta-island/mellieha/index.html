Introduction to Mellieħa

Mellieħa is a small town located on the northernmost tip of Malta Island, a popular tourist destination in Europe. The town is known for its spectacular views of the Mediterranean Sea, unspoiled beaches, archaeological sites, and rich cultural heritage. Mellieħa is also a gateway to the most beautiful nature reserves in Malta, the Ghadira Nature Reserve and the Red Tower, both renowned for their scenic beauty.

List of Places to Visit in Mellieħa

Mellieħa offers an intriguing mix of cultural and natural attractions. Visitors can explore Museums and Historical places, experience the thrill of outdoor adventures or simply relax and enjoy the scenic views. Here are some recommended places to visit:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • The Mellieħa Parish Church- A Baroque-style church that is known for its exquisite architecture and interiors
  • The Our Lady of Victory Chapel - A small chapel that was built on the site of an old 15th-century chapel
  • The Mellieħa World War II Shelters - A refuge that was used by the Maltese people during World War II. It is now open to the public for tours.
  • The Red Tower - A watchtower built in 1649 that offers stunning panoramic views and historical significance.
  • The Mellieħa Air Raid Shelter and Disaster Relief Centre - An underground shelter that was used by the people during the second World War

Natural Attractions of Mellieħa: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Ghadira Nature Reserve - A nature reserve that is home to a wide range of flora and fauna. Visitors can enjoy bird-watching, cycling, hiking trails, and picnics.
  • Mellieħa Bay - The largest and most popular beach in Mellieħa. It is perfect for swimming, sunbathing, and water sports.
  • Anchor Bay - A small bay known for its crystal-clear waters, ideal for scuba diving, snorkeling and water activities.
  • Paradise Bay - A hidden gem that offers breathtaking views of the Mediterranean and is perfect for swimming and relaxation.
  • Selmun Palace - A former palace that has been converted into a hotel. It is a popular spot for hiking, picnics, and scenic walks.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Mellieħa

Mellieħa is a great place for outdoors enthusiasts, with numerous hiking and biking trails available. Some of the best trails include:

  • The Red Tower Trail - A 4-kilometer round trip trail that takes hikers to the Red Tower, offering stunning views of Mellieħa Bay and the surrounding countryside.
  • Mellieħa to Golden Bay Trail - A 4-kilometer trail starting at Mellieħa Bay and ending at Golden Bay, passing through scenic spots along the way.
  • Selmun Trail - A 10-kilometer trail that winds through the countryside and offers stunning views of the sea and the landscape.

Best Things to Do in Mellieħa: Activities and Tours

  • Mellieħa Beach Cleanup - Join the local community and help keep the beach clean.
  • Scenic Boat Ride - Explore the scenic coastline of Malta with a boat ride along the coast.
  • Horse Riding - Experience the beauty of Mellieħa on horseback.
  • Food Tour - Discover the local food scene and sample some of the traditional dishes.
  • Diving - Scuba dive or snorkel in the crystal-clear waters of Mellieħa.

Week-Long Itinerary for Mellieħa

Day 1: Visit the Mellieħa Parish Church and the Our Lady of Victory Chapel. In the afternoon, take a stroll through the town and explore the local markets.

Day 2: Head to Ghadira Nature Reserve and spend the day exploring the park.

Day 3: Relax at Mellieħa Bay, one of the most popular beaches in Malta.

Day 4: Take a boat ride along the scenic coastline of Malta.

Day 5: Explore the Red Tower and learn about its historical significance.

Day 6: Discover the local food scene and try some traditional dishes on a food tour.

Day 7: Take a horse ride in the countryside and enjoy the stunning views of Mellieħa.

Food and Drink Scene of Mellieħa

Must-Try Dishes

  • Pastizzi - Flaky pastry filled with ricotta or peas.
  • Stuffat tal-Fenek - A traditional Maltese stew made with rabbit, tomato, and garlic.
  • Qubbajt - A sweet nougat made with honey, almonds, and citrus zest.
  • Aljotta - A fish soup made with fresh fish, tomato, garlic, and cumin.

Local Beverages

  • Cisk Lager - A local beer that is easy to find and refreshing.
  • Kinnie - A refreshing citrus soda that is unique to Malta.
  • Gellewza - A local red wine made from the indigenous grape varietal.

Where to Stay in Mellieħa: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Mellieħa offers a wide range of accommodations to choose from, catering to a variety of tastes and budgets. Some of the most popular options include:

  • Solana Hotel & Spa - A luxurious 4-star hotel with rooftop pool and spa facilities.
  • Luna Holiday Complex - A budget-friendly apartment complex that offers comfortable accommodation.
  • The Ramla Bay Resort - A seaside resort that offers an all-inclusive package, perfect for families.

What to Buy in Mellieħa: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Handmade Maltese Lace - A traditional craft that has been passed down through generations.
  • Filigree Jewelry - Handcrafted jewelry made from tiny silver or gold threads that are twisted and interwoven to create delicate designs.
  • Maltese Honey - A sweet treat made from locally sourced honey.
  • Olive Oil - A fragrant and delicious local specialty.

Local Culture and Customs of Mellieħa

  • The Maltese are known for their hospitality and friendliness. It is common to greet people with a smile and a warm welcome.
  • The Maltese language is spoken alongside English.
  • Respect the local customs and traditions and cover up when visiting religious sites.

Best Time to Visit Mellieħa

The best time to visit Mellieħa is from April to June and September to November, when the crowds are fewer, and the weather is mild. This also makes it easier to find accommodation and avoid peak-season prices.

Do’s and Don’ts of Mellieħa

Do:

  • Dress appropriately when visiting religious sites.
  • Respect the natural environment and do not litter.
  • Learn a few words in Maltese, such as “bonġu” (good morning) and “merħba” (welcome).

Don’t:

  • Sunbathe topless or in the nude.
  • Use drugs, as it is illegal in Malta.
  • Be disrespectful to the local customs and traditions.

Safety and Health

Safety in Mellieħa

Mellieħa is a safe destination for tourists, with a low crime rate, cautious driving, and friendly locals. Visitors should still take precautions with their personal belongings and avoid leaving valuables in plain sight.

Health in Mellieħa

Visitors should have comprehensive travel insurance to cover any unforeseen medical expenses. It is also worth noting that tap water is safe to drink in Malta, and there are plenty of hospitals and clinics available in case of an emergency.

Getting to Mellieħa: Transportation Options and Logistics

Malta International Airport is the main airport that serves Mellieħa. From the airport, visitors can take a taxi or a bus to the town center. Buses are an affordable and convenient mode of transport in Malta, with frequent services connecting Mellieħa to other parts of the island.

Airports Near Mellieħa

  • Malta International Airport - The main airport that serves Mellieħa.

Bus Stations Near Mellieħa

  • Mellieħa Bus Terminus - The main bus station that serves Mellieħa.

Other Options

  • Car rental - Visitors can rent a car to explore the island at their own pace.

How to Get Around in Mellieħa

Mellieħa is a small town, and most of the attractions are within walking distance of each other. Visitors can also take buses or hire a taxi to explore other parts of the island.

Where to Go Next from Mellieħa: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • St. Julian’s - A vibrant seaside town known for its nightlife, casinos, and restaurants.
  • Valletta - The capital city of Malta, known for its stunning architecture and rich history.
  • Cirkewwa - A small port on the northern tip of the island, with regular ferries to the island of Gozo.

Conclusion

Mellieħa is a hidden gem of Malta that offers a perfect blend of cultural and natural wonders. With its scenic landscapes, rich history, and vibrant culture, Mellieħa is a must-visit destination for any traveler exploring Malta.
