Introduction to Southern Harbor of Malta

Southern Harbor of Malta is located on the southern coast of the beautiful Malta Island, in the Mediterranean Sea. The area is home to historic landmarks, natural attractions, and a thriving food and drink scene. It is the ideal destination for travelers seeking to explore Malta’s rich culture and stunning scenery.

List of Places to Visit in Southern Harbor of Malta

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

The Southern Harbor of Malta is filled with fascinating museums and historical sites, including:

  • The Malta Maritime Museum: showcasing Malta’s maritime history, with exhibits on boat building, fishing, and seafaring.
  • The Casa Rocca Piccola: a grand palace that has been home to the noble de Piro family for generations, with ornate decorations and beautiful gardens.
  • The Inquisitor’s Palace: an impressive Baroque-style building that has been the site of many historical events, including the interrogation of those accused of heresy.

Natural Attractions of Southern Harbor of Malta: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

The Southern Harbor of Malta is also a great destination for outdoor enthusiasts, with plenty of opportunities for hiking, biking, and scenic views:

  • The Three Cities: a group of fortified cities with stunning Baroque architecture, narrow streets, and beautiful views of the harbor.
  • Gardjola Gardens: lush gardens with stunning views of the harbor, located in the fortified town of Senglea.
  • The Ħal Saflieni Hypogeum: a unique underground prehistoric site, with impressive architecture and mesmerizing acoustics.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Southern Harbor of Malta

The Southern Harbor of Malta offers some of the best hiking/biking opportunities on the island. Some of the popular trails/routes are:

  • Senglea Point Battery Trail: a scenic trail that passes through old military fortifications, offering stunning harbor views.
  • The Grand Harbor Circuit: a 10 km circular route that starts and ends at the Vittoriosa waterfront, passing through all three cities of the Southern Harbor.
  • St. Peter’s Pool Trail: a popular hiking trail that runs from Marsaxlokk to St. Peter’s Pool, one of the most beautiful natural swimming pools in Malta.

Best Things to Do in Southern Harbor of Malta: Activities and Tours

The Southern Harbor of Malta is filled with exciting activities and tours for visitors of all ages, such as:

  • Boat tours: explore the picturesque harbors and bays of Malta from a different perspective, with plenty of swimming and snorkeling opportunities.
  • Segway tours: a fun and efficient way to explore the Southern Harbor of Malta, with knowledgeable guides to show visitors the best sights and attractions.
  • Wine tasting tours: experience the unique taste of Malta’s wine and learn about its history and production at one of the many wineries in the area.

Week-Long Itinerary for Southern Harbor of Malta

For travelers who want to make the most of their time in Southern Harbor of Malta, here is a sample week-long itinerary:

Day 1: Explore the Three Cities (Vittoriosa, Cospicua, and Senglea) and take a boat tour around the harbor. Day 2: Visit the Malta Maritime Museum and Inquisitor’s Palace. Day 3: Take a day trip to nearby Marsaxlokk and enjoy some seafood at the famous fish market. Day 4: Hike/bike the Grand Harbor Circuit and discover the magnificent architecture of the Southern Harbor. Day 5: Take a Segway tour around the historic parts of the Southern Harbor of Malta. Day 6: Relax and enjoy the beaches in the area, such as Pretty Bay, Marsascala Bay, and Birzebbuga Beach. Day 7: Take a wine tasting tour and indulge in Malta’s unique and flavorful wine culture.

Food and Drink Scene of Southern Harbor of Malta

Must-Try Dishes

The Southern Harbor of Malta is renowned for its seafood dishes, as well as its traditional Maltese cuisine. Some of the must-try dishes include:

  • Lampuki Pie: a traditional fish pie made with local lampuki fish, vegetables, and spices.
  • Rabbit Stew: a hearty and flavorful stew made with rabbit meat, vegetables, and red wine.
  • Pastizzi: a popular snack made with pastry filled with either ricotta cheese or peas, served hot and crispy.

Local Beverages

Malta has a long history of winemaking, with many vineyards producing top-quality wines. Visitors to the Southern Harbor of Malta should try some of the local beverages, such as:

  • Gellewza and Grenache: two of the most popular varieties of Maltese wines, with rich and fruity flavors.
  • Cisk Lager: a refreshing and easy-to-drink local beer, perfect for a hot summer day.

Where to Stay in Southern Harbor of Malta: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

There are plenty of accommodation options in the Southern Harbor of Malta, catering to all budget levels. Some of the popular options include:

  • Marina Hotel Corinthia Beach Resort: a luxurious beachfront hotel with stunning views of the Mediterranean Sea.
  • Ursulino Valletta: located in the heart of the historic city of Valletta, this boutique hotel is within walking distance of many top attractions.
  • The British Hotel: a charming hotel located in the scenic town of Valletta, offering comfortable rooms and friendly service.

What to Buy in Southern Harbor of Malta: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

The Southern Harbor of Malta offers plenty of unique and locally made souvenirs and products, such as:

  • Filigree jewelry: intricate and beautiful jewelry made from gold and silver wire, a traditional Maltese craft.
  • Maltese lace: delicate and beautiful lacework made from linen thread, another traditional Maltese craft.
  • Ġbejna: a type of cheese made from sheep’s or goat’s milk, a popular delicacy in Malta.

Local Culture and Customs of Southern Harbor of Malta

The Southern Harbor of Malta has a unique and vibrant culture, influenced by its Mediterranean location and historical heritage. Visitors should be respectful of local customs, such as:

  • Dress modestly when visiting churches and other religious sites.
  • Follow local customs and traditions, such as taking your shoes off before entering someone’s home.
  • Try to learn a few words of Maltese, the official language of the country, to show respect for the local culture.

Best Time to Visit Southern Harbor of Malta

The best time to visit the Southern Harbor of Malta is during the shoulder seasons of spring (April to June) and fall (September to November). The weather is mild, and the crowds are smaller, making it easier to explore the many attractions in the area.

Do’s and Don’ts of Southern Harbor of Malta

Do:

  • Respect local customs and traditions.
  • Enjoy the delicious and unique food and drink scene in the area.
  • Take a boat trip to explore the picturesque bays and harbors of the Southern Harbor of Malta.

Don’t:

  • Ignore local customs and traditions.
  • Swim in restricted areas, as they can be dangerous.
  • Litter or damage the environment in any way.

Safety and Health

Safety in Southern Harbor of Malta

The Southern Harbor of Malta is a safe destination, with low crime rates and friendly locals. However, visitors should be aware of their surroundings and take precautions, such as:

  • Keep an eye on your belongings while in crowded areas.
  • Be careful when swimming in the sea, as there may be strong currents or dangerous underwater structures.
  • Be cautious when walking or biking along narrow or busy roads.

Health in Southern Harbor of Malta

Malta has excellent healthcare facilities, but visitors should still take some precautions to stay healthy during their trip, such as:

  • Stay hydrated, especially during the hot summer months.
  • Use sunscreen to protect your skin from the strong Mediterranean sun.
  • Be careful when consuming local food and water, and make sure to wash your hands frequently to avoid getting sick.

Getting to Southern Harbor of Malta: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Southern Harbor of Malta

Malta International Airport is located just 8 km from the Southern Harbor of Malta, with frequent flights to and from major European cities.

Bus Stations Near Southern Harbor of Malta

The main bus station in the Southern Harbor of Malta is located in Vittoriosa, with regular services connecting the area with other parts of Malta.

Other Options

Visitors to the Southern Harbor of Malta can also hire a car or take a taxi to get around the area and explore the many attractions.

How to Get Around in Southern Harbor of Malta

The best way to get around the Southern Harbor of Malta is on foot or by bike, as many of the attractions and sights are within easy walking or cycling distance. Visitors can also take the local bus or hire a car/taxi to explore further afield.

Where to Go Next from Southern Harbor of Malta: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

The Southern Harbor of Malta is a great base for exploring other parts of Malta, such as:

  • The Blue Lagoon: a stunning natural swimming pool located on the nearby island of Comino, accessible by boat.
  • The Island of Gozo: a peaceful and picturesque island with beautiful scenery and historic sites, accessible by ferry from Cirkewwa.
  • Valletta: the capital city of Malta, with a rich history and many impressive landmarks, such as St. John’s Co-Cathedral and the Grand Master’s Palace.

Conclusion

The Southern Harbor of Malta is an enchanting destination with a rich cultural heritage and stunning natural beauty. Whether you’re interested in history, outdoor activities, food and drink or simply relaxing on the beach, you’ll find plenty to enjoy in this charming part of the Mediterranean.
