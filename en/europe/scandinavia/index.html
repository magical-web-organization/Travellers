Introduction to Scandinavia

Scandinavia is a beautiful and pristine region located in the northern part of Europe, consisting of Denmark, Finland, Iceland, Norway, and Sweden. The region is abundant with natural beauty ranging from fjords, mountains, and glaciers to vast lakelands and forests. Besides its natural beauty, Scandinavia is also known for its creative and modern design, innovative technology, and rich cultural heritage.

List of Places to Visit in Scandinavia

Whether you are looking for a relaxing vacation or an adventurous one, Scandinavia has something for everyone. From culturally significant attractions to scenic views and outdoor adventures, here are some must-visit places in Scandinavia:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • The Vasa Museum, Stockholm
  • The Royal Palace, Copenhagen
  • The Helsinki Cathedral, Helsinki
  • The Viking Ship Museum, Oslo
  • The National Museum of Iceland, Reykjavik

Natural Attractions of Scandinavia: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • The Geirangerfjord, Norway
  • The Northern Lights, Finland
  • The Blue Lagoon, Iceland
  • The Koli National Park, Finland
  • The Jotunheimen National Park, Norway

Hiking/Biking Routes of Scandinavia

For an active vacation, Scandinavia offers some of the best hiking and biking trails in Europe. Here are some popular routes:

  • The Fjällräven Classic, Sweden
  • The Rallarvegen Trail, Norway
  • The King’s Trail, Sweden
  • The Arctic Circle Trail, Greenland
  • The Laugavegur Trail, Iceland

Best Things to Do in Scandinavia: Activities and Tours

There are plenty of activities and tours to explore in Scandinavia, from whale watching to dog sledding. Here are some of our favorites:

  • Whale watching in Norway
  • Husky dog sledding in Finland
  • Ice-climbing in Iceland
  • Fjord kayaking in Norway
  • Midnight sun safari in Sweden

Week-Long Itinerary for Scandinavia

To make the most of your trip to Scandinavia, we have put together a week-long itinerary that includes some of the highlights of the region:

Day 1 - Copenhagen, Denmark: Tivoli Gardens, The Little Mermaid Statue, and Nyhavn Day 2 - Oslo, Norway: Viking Ship Museum, Fram Museum, and Akershus Fortress Day 3 - Stockholm, Sweden: Vasa Museum, Stockholm Palace, and Gamla Stan Day 4 - Helsinki, Finland: Suomenlinna Sea Fortress, Helsinki Cathedral, and Sibelius Monument Day 5 - Reykjavik, Iceland: The Golden Circle Tour - Gullfoss Waterfall, Geysir Geothermal Area, and Thingvellir National Park Day 6 - Tromso, Norway: Northern Lights Tour, dog sledding, and reindeer sledding Day 7 - Finish up with some shopping and dining in Copenhagen, or whichever city you started in.

Where to Stay in Scandinavia: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Scandinavia has a range of accommodation options to suit all budgets and preferences. Here are some options to consider:

  • Luxury hotels: The Thief, Oslo and The Grand Hotel, Stockholm
  • Mid-range hotels: The Radisson Blu, Copenhagen and The Scandic Hotel, Helsinki

Scandinavia offers some unique souvenirs and local products that are worth bringing back home. Here are some ideas:

  • Norwegian knitwear and mittens

Local Culture and Customs of Scandinavia

Scandinavia is known for its progressive attitudes, high standard of living, and strong social welfare programs. Being punctual, respectful, and reserved are important cultural values one should keep in mind while visiting Scandinavia.

Best Time to Visit Scandinavia

Scandinavia can be visited year-round, but the summer months, especially June to August, are ideal for pleasant weather and experiencing the midnight sun. Winter, from December to February, is another popular time to visit for the chance to see the Northern Lights.

Do’s and Don’ts of Scandinavia

Here are some common etiquette rules to follow when in Scandinavia:

Do:

  • Take off your shoes before entering someone’s home
  • Be punctual and respectful of people’s time
  • Be prepared for high prices and bring extra cash

Don’t:

  • Make small talk with strangers in public transport
  • Get drunk in public and disrupt the peace
  • Forget to tip at restaurants

Safety and Health

Scandinavia is a safe region to visit in terms of crime and public safety, but accidents can happen everywhere. It is also essential to have travel health insurance and prepare for the weather and climate.

Safety in Scandinavia

  • Follow local safety guidelines and respect nature
  • Keep valuables and travel documents secure
  • Take precautionary measures to avoid pickpocketing

Health in Scandinavia

  • Drink clean water and eat fresh food
  • Be prepared for drastic climate changes and weather conditions

Getting to Scandinavia: Transportation Options and Logistics

Scandinavia is well connected to the rest of Europe and can be reached by air, train, or ferry. Here are some options to consider:

Airports Near Scandinavia

  • Kastrup Airport, Copenhagen
  • Oslo Gardermoen Airport, Oslo
  • Arlanda Airport, Stockholm
  • Helsinki-Vantaa Airport, Helsinki
  • Keflavik International Airport, Reykjavik

Bus Stations Near Scandinavia

  • Nils Ericson Terminal, Gothenburg
  • Central Bus Station, Copenhagen
  • Oslo Bus Terminal, Oslo
  • Helsinki Bus Station, Helsinki
  • BSI Bus Terminal, Reykjavik

Other Options

  • Ferry: Ferries connect Denmark, Norway and Sweden, as well as some destinations in Finland
  • Train: Trains can be used between destinations within Scandinavian countries, except for Iceland, and are a great way to see the region

How to Get Around in Scandinavia

Scandinavia is easy to navigate, and most places can be reached by public transportation. Most cities have an efficient public transportation system, including buses, subways, and trams. Taxis and car rentals are also an option— just remember to drive on the right side of the road!

Where to Go Next from Scandinavia: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

If you have more time, consider venturing to some nearby destinations from Scandinavia:

  • Saint Petersburg, Russia
  • Tallinn, Estonia
  • Bergen, Norway
  • Lapland region, Finland
  • Faroe Islands

Conclusion

Scandinavia has something for everyone, from picturesque landscapes and outdoor adventures to modern design and rich cultural heritage. With this comprehensive tour guide, you are ready to explore the beauty and wonders of Scandinavia.
