Introduction to Kherson

Kherson is a beautiful city in southern Ukraine, located in Eastern Europe, and it’s the administrative center of the Kherson Oblast. It’s a strategic port city on the Black Sea, surrounded by natural beauty, with cultural and historical importance. Kherson is primarily known for its shipbuilding and agriculture industries, but it’s also an excellent destination for travelers looking for a unique cultural experience.

List of Places to Visit in Kherson

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Kherson Regional History Museum: This museum provides an excellent overview of the region’s history, where you can learn about the city’s founding and its cultural heritage.
  • Museum of Archeology and Local Lore: This museum showcases the archaeological treasures of Kherson, including ancient artifacts, ancient weapons, clothing, and ceramics.
  • Saint Catherine’s Cathedral: It is a beautiful and historically important cathedral, which is over 200 years old. It was constructed in the 18th century by Catherine the Great’s decree in honor of her visit to Kherson.
  • Fortress of Ochakov: This is an exceptional historic site located about 60 kilometers from Kherson. It was built in 1784 by the Russian Empire, and the fortress has seen many battles.

Natural Attractions of Kherson: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Askania-Nova Biosphere Reserve: This is a stunning natural reserve located 60 kilometers from Kherson. It is home to hundreds of wild animals and plant species from all over the world, including zebras, giraffes, bison, and more.
  • Dnieper River Delta National Park: It’s the largest delta in Europe, with breathtaking scenery, a variety of plant and animal life, and excellent options for birdwatchers.
  • National Park “Tavria”: It’s recognized as a Biosphere Reserve by UNESCO, where visitors can enjoy pristine natural habitats rich in flora and fauna.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Kherson

Kherson offers beautiful scenery and a relatively flat terrain, making it ideal for hikes and bike rides. Some fun and challenging routes include:

  • The Nature Trail of Oleshky Sands: is a 6 km trail known for its impressive sand dunes and unique flora.
  • The Dnieper River Trail: is a picturesque 15 km trail offering great views of the river delta in a tranquil countryside environment.

Best Things to Do in Kherson: Activities and Tours

  • Boat Tours of Dnieper River: It’s a wonderful way to explore the waterways and behold the natural beauty of Kherson.
  • Wine tasting tour: Experience a one-of-a-kind wine journey of the region’s best wines, including indigenous grape varieties.
  • Kherson skydiving club: An unforgettable and thrilling experience with spectacular views of the city.

Week-Long Itinerary for Kherson

Day 1: Arrive and explore the city center of Kherson, including Saint Catherine’s Cathedral.
Day 2: Visit Askania-Nova Biosphere Reserve.
Day 3: Dnieper River Delta National Park excursion.
Day 4: Wine Tasting Tour.
Day 5: Hiking/Biking the Oleshky Sands.
Day 6: Explore the Museums and Historical Sites.
Day 7: Departure to next destination.

Food and Drink Scene of Kherson

Must-Try Dishes

  • Borsch: A hearty vegetable soup made from beets, potatoes, and cabbage, seasoned with traditional Ukrainian spices.
  • Varenyky: Dumplings filled with potatoes, cheese, meat, or sour cherries, served with sour cream or butter on top.
  • Chicken Kiev: A classic dish consisting of chicken breast stuffed with garlic butter, breaded, and fried until golden brown.

Local Beverages

  • Kvass: A carbonated beverage made from fermented bread that comes in sweet or sour flavors.
  • Horilka: A Ukrainian distilled spirit made from rye, wheat, potatoes, or barley.

Where to Stay in Kherson: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Kherson caters to all kinds of travelers, offering accommodation options to suit every budget and preference. Some popular ones are:

  • Hotel “Korabel”: A modern hotel located in the heart of the city, with friendly staff, clean, and comfortable rooms.
  • City Club Hotel: A central and luxurious hotel with a beautiful spa and fitness center, plus excellent restaurants.
  • Hostel “Traveler”: A budget-friendly hostel located close to the city center, offering clean and comfortable rooms, helpful staff, and a shared kitchen.

What to Buy in Kherson: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Pottery crafts: Traditional and handmade ceramics, decorated with intricate designs and patterns, are famous in the region.
  • Embroidered textiles: This is a popular Ukrainian folk art, and you can find a plethora of items to choose from, including towels, tablecloths, and blouses.
  • Cheese and wine: Kherson produces some of Ukraine’s best wines and cheese.

Local Culture and Customs of Kherson

The people of Kherson are hospitable and friendly to visitors. As a sign of respect, it’s common to greet people with a handshake and say “hello” and “goodbye” (“Dobry den” and “Do pobachyennya”). It’s important to dress modestly, especially in religious sites, and remove your shoes before entering someone’s home.

Best Time to Visit Kherson

The best time to visit Kherson is from late spring to early autumn, from May to September. The weather is warm and pleasant during these months, with plenty of sunshine and fewer chances of rainfall.

Do’s and Don’ts of Kherson

Do:

  • Try the local food and drink.
  • Learn some basic phrases in Ukrainian.
  • Respect the local culture and customs.

Don’t:

  • Wear shoes inside someone’s home.
  • Speak loudly in public places.
  • Leave a bad impression with locals.

Safety and Health

Safety in Kherson

Kherson is generally a safe city for travelers. However, it’s advisable to avoid wandering alone in deserted areas at night, and keep an eye on your belongings in crowded places.

Health in Kherson

The healthcare system in Kherson is developing, but it’s still not as advanced as in Western Europe or North America. It’s recommended to have travel insurance before you visit, pack a first-aid kit, and drink only bottled water.

Getting to Kherson: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Kherson

  • Kherson International Airport is about 6.5 kilometers from Kherson’s city center. It serves domestic and international flights.

Bus Stations Near Kherson

  • Kherson Bus Station is the central station for intercity routes, with buses departing to/from cities in Ukraine, and neighboring countries.

Other Options

  • Train: The train station offers daily connections to Big Cities like Kyiv, Odesa, Mykolaiv, and Lviv, among others.
  • Car rental: several car rental companies offer cars available at the airport and city center to explore Kherson.

How to Get Around in Kherson

Kherson has an extensive and well-organized transport system. Public transport options include buses, trams, trolleybuses, and minivans. Taxis and bike rentals are also readily available for tourists.

Where to Go Next from Kherson: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Odesa: A beautiful port city located on the Black Sea, only 180 kilometers away from Kherson.
  • Mykolaiv: Another significant port city with a variety of museums and natural landmarks, situated only 95 kilometers away.
  • Zaporizhia: A scenic Ukrainian city rich in cultural and natural attractions, which is about 200 kilometers from Kherson.

Conclusion

Kherson is a beautiful and culturally rich city that offers an authentic Ukrainian experience, with plenty of natural and heritage sites to explore. Travelers can enjoy beautiful scenery, delicious food, and friendly locals in this charming city on the Black Sea.
