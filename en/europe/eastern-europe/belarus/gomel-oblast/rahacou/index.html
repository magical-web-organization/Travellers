Introduction to Rahačoŭ

Rahačoŭ is a small town with a population of less than 10,000 people, located in the eastern part of Belarus. It’s known for its stunning natural beauty and warm local hospitality. The town is surrounded by dense forests, rolling hills, and the Dnieper River. Unlike many other touristic destinations in Belarus, Rahačoŭ has managed to maintain its authenticity and traditional character, making it a unique destination for travelers who want to experience the real Belarus.

Food and Drink Scene of Rahačoŭ

No trip to Rahačoŭ is complete without trying out the local cuisine. Here are some dishes and local beverages you must try:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Draniki: a traditional Belarusian potato pancake, served with sour cream and dill.
  • Machanka: a hearty pork stew that’s full of flavor and served with bread.
  • Babka: a sweet yeast cake that’s perfect for dessert.

Local Beverages

  • Krambambula: a sweet alcoholic drink made with honey, spices, and vodka.
  • Kvass: a non-alcoholic drink made with fermented bread that’s perfect for hot summer days.
  • Sbiten: a sweet and spicy hot beverage made with honey, spices, and water, perfect for cold winter days.
