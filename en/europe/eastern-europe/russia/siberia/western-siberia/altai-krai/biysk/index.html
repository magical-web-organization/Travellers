Introduction to Biysk

Biysk is a beautiful city located in the Altai Krai region of Western Siberia, Russia. It is situated at the confluence of the Biya and Katun rivers and surrounded by the Altai Mountains, which makes it an ideal destination for outdoor enthusiasts. Biysk is also known for its rich history and culture, which can be seen through its many museums and historical landmarks.

List of Places to Visit in Biysk

Below is a list of the must-visit places in Biysk:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Biysk Historical Museum: Learn about the history of Biysk and its surrounding areas through a collection of exhibits that showcase the daily life of the locals from ancient times to the present day.

  • Museum of Local Lore: This museum has a vast collection of items that depict the culture and history of the Altai region, including artwork, traditional clothing, and archaeological finds.

  • The Church of the Holy Apostles Peter and Paul: A beautiful Russian Orthodox Church built in the 18th century, this church is a fine example of Baroque-style architecture and features colorful frescoes on its walls.

Natural Attractions of Biysk: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Katun Nature Reserve: This natural reserve is home to a diverse range of flora and fauna, including rare Siberian animals such as the snow leopard and the Altai argali. You can explore the reserve on a guided tour and witness the stunning landscapes of the Altai Mountains.

  • Lake Aya: Located just outside the city, Lake Aya is a popular destination for outdoor enthusiasts. Here, you can enjoy activities like swimming, fishing, and kayaking or simply soak in the serene beauty of the lake.

  • Mount Belukha: The highest mountain in the Altai Mountains, Mount Belukha is one of the most popular hiking destinations in Biysk. It offers breathtaking panoramic views of the surrounding valleys and mountains.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Biysk

Biysk is a paradise for hikers and bikers. Some of the best hiking and biking routes in Biysk are:

  • Chulyshman River Valley: This picturesque valley is located in the heart of the Altai Mountains and offers a range of paths for trekkers and bikers.

  • Kucherla Lake: This lake is located at an altitude of 1,568 meters and is surrounded by high mountains. It is a perfect spot for hikers and mountain climbers.

  • Rakhmanovskiye Waterfalls: This scenic waterfall is located on the river Rakhmanovka, surrounded by picturesque mountains, and is a great spot for a day hike.

Best Things to Do in Biysk: Activities and Tours

  • Altai Horseback Riding: Horseback riding is a popular activity in Biysk, and there are many opportunities for guided tours. Explore the Altai Mountains on horseback and take in the beautiful landscapes and fresh, crisp air.

  • Rafting on the Katun River: The Katun River is one of the best places to go rafting in Russia, with rapids ranging from Class II to Class IV. Guided tours are available for beginners and experts alike.

  • Altai Cuisine Tour: Discover the unique and delicious cuisine of Altai on a food tour. Try traditional dishes like koumiss (fermented mare’s milk) and buuzy (steamed dumplings filled with meat).

Food and Drink Scene of Biysk

When it comes to food, Biysk offers a range of options, from traditional Russian cuisine to international dishes. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Altai Dumplings: Filled with meat or vegetables, these dumplings are a staple of the Altai diet and are usually served boiled or fried.

  • Pelmeni: Similar to dumplings, pelmeni are filled with meat and boiled. They are usually served with sour cream or butter.

  • Shashlyk: A popular Russian dish similar to skewered meat, shashlyk is usually made from lamb or pork and served with onions and sour cream.

Local Beverages

  • Koumiss: A popular drink made from fermented mare’s milk, koumiss has a slightly sour taste and is said to have many health benefits.

  • Chaga Tea: Made from a type of mushroom found in the Altai region, chaga tea is a healthy and delicious beverage that’s high in antioxidants.

  • Baijiu: A popular Chinese liquor that’s made from fermented grains, baijiu is a strong drink with a unique flavor.

Where to Stay in Biysk: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

There are plenty of accommodation options in Biysk, ranging from budget-friendly hostels to luxury hotels. Here are some top picks:

  • Hotel Akvilon: Located in the city center, this 4-star hotel offers comfortable rooms and modern amenities, including a spa and wellness center.

  • Biysk Hostel: This budget-friendly hostel is located near the city center and offers dorm-style rooms.

  • Guesthouse Astana: This guesthouse is located in a quiet residential area and offers comfortable rooms in a peaceful setting.

What to Buy in Biysk: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Some popular souvenirs and local products to buy in Biysk include:

  • Altai Honey: Known for its high quality and unique taste, Altai honey is a must-try for anyone visiting the region.

  • Traditional Clothing: Local markets and shops offer a range of traditional clothing, including traditional dresses and hats.

  • Handicrafts and Artwork: Biysk is known for its handicrafts and artwork, including painted wooden spoons and handmade jewelry.

Local Culture and Customs of Biysk

The local culture of Biysk has been heavily influenced by the Altai people, who have lived in the region for thousands of years. The Altai people have a rich cultural heritage, and their traditions are celebrated throughout the region, including through music, dance, and traditional dress.

Best Time to Visit Biysk

The best time to visit Biysk is during the summer months (June-August), when the weather is warm and sunny. However, if you’re looking to go hiking or skiing, the winter months can also be a great time to visit.

Do’s and Don’ts of Biysk

Do:

  • Learn some basic Russian phrases before you go.

  • Try traditional Altai dishes and beverages.

  • Dress appropriately when visiting religious sites.

Don’t:

  • Take photographs of people without their permission.

  • Openly criticize the Russian government or religion.

  • Overstay your visa.

Safety and Health

Safety in Biysk

Biysk is generally a safe place to visit. However, as with any destination, it’s important to take basic safety precautions, such as avoiding walking alone at night and keeping an eye on your belongings in crowded areas.

Health in Biysk

The tap water in Biysk is generally safe to drink. However, it’s always a good idea to check with your accommodation provider to be sure. Medical facilities are available in the city if needed.

Getting to Biysk: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Biysk

The nearest airport to Biysk is the Barnaul Airport, located approximately 100 km away. Several airlines operate regular flights from Moscow to Barnaul.

Bus Stations Near Biysk

There are several bus stations in Biysk, including the Biysk Central Bus Station and the Biysk City Station.

Other Options

Train travel is another popular option for reaching Biysk, with daily trains departing from Moscow and other major cities in Russia.

How to Get Around in Biysk

Public transportation in Biysk includes buses and mini-buses known as marshrutkas. Taxis are also widely available.

Where to Go Next from Biysk: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

If you have time, here are some nearby destinations and excursions you can add to your itinerary:

  • Barnaul: The largest city in the Altai Krai region, Barnaul is known for its historic architecture and cultural landmarks.

  • Lake Teletskoye: Located in the Altai Mountains, Lake Teletskoye is considered one of the most beautiful lakes in Russia and is a popular destination for hiking and camping.

  • Gorno-Altaysk: The capital of the Altai Republic, Gorno-Altaysk offers a unique mix of Russian and Altai culture, with traditional music, dance, and handicrafts on display.

Conclusion

Biysk offers an array of natural and cultural attractions that appeal to a wide range of travelers. Whether you’re looking for outdoor adventures, historical landmarks, or unique cuisine, Biysk has something to offer. Plan your trip today and explore all that Biysk has to offer.
