Introduction to Kemerovo

Kemerovo is a city located in Western Siberia, Russia. It is the administrative center of Kemerovo Oblast and is situated on the banks of the Tom River. Kemerovo is known for its coal-mining industry and is also a hub for education, science, and culture. There is plenty to explore in Kemerovo, from museums and historical places to scenic natural attractions and outdoor excursions.

List of Places to Visit in Kemerovo

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

There are several museums and historical places to visit in Kemerovo, including:

  • The Kemerovo Regional Museum of Local Lore: This museum has an extensive collection of artifacts and exhibits, including natural history, ethnography, and archaeology.
  • The Kemerovo Regional Art Museum: This museum features works from local artists and hosts temporary exhibitions.
  • The Kemerovo Regional Museum of Fine Arts: This museum displays works of Russian art from the 18th-21st centuries.

Natural Attractions of Kemerovo: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Kemerovo is home to several natural attractions that offer scenic views and outdoor adventures. Some of them include:

  • The Shorsky National Park: This park features stunning landscapes and diverse flora and fauna and is home to the Shor people.
  • The Salair Ridge Mountains: These mountains offer incredible views of the landscape and some of the highest peak points in Western Siberia.
  • The Tom River: This river flows through Kemerovo and offers opportunities for water sports and other recreational activities.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Kemerovo

Kemerovo has many hiking and biking routes to explore. Some of the popular ones include:

  • The Salair Ridge Mountains: This range offers challenging hiking trails and incredible views of the surrounding landscape.
  • The Tom River: This river has a bike trail along its banks that offers scenic views of the city.

Best Things to Do in Kemerovo: Activities and Tours

Apart from museums and natural attractions, Kemerovo also offers a wide array of activities and tours. Some of the best things to do in Kemerovo include:

  • Skiing: Kemerovo has several ski resorts that offer skiing and other winter sports activities.
  • Visiting the Kuzbass State Technical University Planetarium
  • Exploring the Kemerovo Philharmonic Hall

Week-Long Itinerary for Kemerovo

If you’re planning to visit Kemerovo for a week, here’s an itinerary to help you make the most of your time:

  • Day 1: Visit the Kemerovo Regional Museum of Local Lore and explore the city center.
  • Day 2: Go skiing at the nearby ski resorts.
  • Day 3: Visit the Kemerovo Regional Art Museum and the Kemerovo Regional Museum of Fine Arts.
  • Day 4: Explore the Shorsky National Park.
  • Day 5: Go hiking or biking along Tom River.
  • Day 6: Visit the Kuzbass State Technical University Planetarium and the Kemerovo Philharmonic Hall.
  • Day 7: Visit the Salair Ridge Mountains and take in the scenic views.

Food and Drink Scene of Kemerovo

Must-Try Dishes

Kemerovo is known for its traditional Russian cuisine. Some of the must-try dishes include:

  • Pirozhki: These are small, savory pies filled with meat, fish, or vegetables.
  • Pelmeni: These are dumplings filled with meat or cottage cheese.
  • Solyanka: This is a hearty soup made with meat, vegetables, and sour cream.

Local Beverages

Kemerovo is known for its unique local beverages. Some of them include:

  • Kemerovo Birch Juice: This is a sweet drink made from the sap of birch trees, and it is believed to be beneficial for health.
  • Siberian Herbal Tea: This tea is made from herbs grown wild in Siberia and has a distinct flavor.

Where to Stay in Kemerovo: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Kemerovo has a range of accommodation options to suit all budgets. Some of the best places to stay in Kemerovo include:

  • Hotel Tom River: This hotel offers stunning views of the Tom River and is located near many of Kemerovo’s attractions.
  • Hostel Kedr: This hostel is centrally located and offers affordable accommodation.
  • Hotel Maxim: This hotel is located in the city center and has comfortable rooms and excellent amenities.

What to Buy in Kemerovo: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Kemerovo offers a range of unique souvenirs and local products to take home, including:

  • Kuzbass Coal: This is a unique souvenir that represents Kemerovo’s coal-mining industry.
  • Souvenirs made of Siberian copper: This is a popular local product, and you can find anything from jewelry to decorative items made of copper.

Local Culture and Customs of Kemerovo

Kemerovo has a rich culture and customs, and here are a few things you should know:

  • The Shor people are an ethnic minority group in Kemerovo, and they have a unique culture and traditions.
  • Russian is the official language in Kemerovo, but you’ll find many people speak in English as well.
  • Orthodox Christianity is the main religion in Kemerovo, and there are several Orthodox churches in the city.

Best Time to Visit Kemerovo

The best time to visit Kemerovo is from May to September when the weather is warm and pleasant.

Do’s and Don’ts of Kemerovo

Do:

  • Dress warmly if you’re visiting during the winter months as the temperature can get quite cold.
  • Try the local cuisine and beverages.
  • Explore the city’s rich history and culture.

Don’t:

  • Take photographs of government buildings or military personnel without permission.
  • Discuss political or religious views with strangers.

Safety and Health

Safety in Kemerovo

Kemerovo is generally a safe city, but you should take the usual precautions such as not leaving valuables unattended and being cautious when walking alone at night.

Health in Kemerovo

You should make sure to get all necessary vaccinations before traveling to Kemerovo. It is also advisable to carry a first aid kit and any necessary medication.

Getting to Kemerovo: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Kemerovo

Kemerovo International Airport is the main airport serving the city.

Bus Stations Near Kemerovo

Kemerovo Bus Station is the main bus station in the city, and it offers regular services to other cities in Kemerovo Oblast and nearby regions.

Other Options

You can also take a train to Kemerovo as the city has a well-connected railway network.

How to Get Around in Kemerovo

The best way to get around in Kemerovo is by public transportation. The city has a well-connected network of buses and trams that cover most of the city’s attractions.

Where to Go Next from Kemerovo: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

There are plenty of nearby destinations and excursions to explore after visiting Kemerovo, including:

  • Tomsk: This is a historic Siberian city known for its wooden architecture.
  • Novosibirsk: This is the largest city in Siberia and has several notable landmarks and attractions.

Conclusion

Kemerovo is a hidden gem in Western Siberia, with plenty of cultural and natural attractions to explore. Take the time to indulge in the local cuisine, immerse yourself in the city’s rich history and culture, and enjoy the beautiful natural scenery.
