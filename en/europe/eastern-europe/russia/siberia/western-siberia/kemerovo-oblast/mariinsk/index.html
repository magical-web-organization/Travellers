Mariinsk Travel Guide - Everything You Need to Know Before You Go

Mariinsk, located in the Kemerovo Oblast region of Western Siberia, is a small but charming town with a rich history and a stunning natural landscape. From its fascinating museums and cultural landmarks to its breathtaking outdoor adventures, Mariinsk offers something for everyone. In this guide, we’ll cover the best things to see and do in Mariinsk, where to stay, how to get there, and more, to help you plan an unforgettable trip.

List of Places to Visit in Mariinsk

Mariinsk is a small town but there are plenty of places to see and things to do. Here are some of the must-visit spots in Mariinsk:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Mariinsk Regional Museum of Local Lore: This museum contains exhibits highlighting the history and culture of Mariinsk and the surrounding region. You’ll find displays about the local flora and fauna, as well as information on the development of the town and the Siberian region.

  • Mariinskaya Yarmarka: This former fairground is now an important cultural and historical landmark in Mariinsk. You can see preserved buildings from the 19th century, such as the old railway station, the town hall and trading pavilions.

  • St. Nicholas Church: This lovely church is a favourite destination for all visitors. The church is painted in bright colors and boasts stunning architectural details both inside and out.

Natural Attractions of Mariinsk: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Luchinsky Waterfall: This stunning waterfall, located in the nearby Khakassia mountains, is a must-visit for those who enjoy trekking and hiking. The hike to the top of the waterfall offers stunning views of the surrounding mountains and waterfalls, so be sure to bring your camera!

  • Lake Teletskoye: This incredible lake is one of the biggest and most beautiful freshwater lakes in Russia. Located within the stunning Altai Mountains, the lake offers fantastic views and plenty of opportunities to enjoy water sports such as kayaking, swimming, and boating.

  • Mariinsk Forest Park: Also known as the “Lung of Mariinsk,” this park is a great place to escape the bustle of the city and immerse yourself in nature. The park features a variety of trails and paths for hiking and cycling, as well as picnic areas and campsites.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Mariinsk

Mariinsk and the surrounding region offer many opportunities for hiking and biking enthusiasts. Here are some of the best routes to explore:

  • Mariinsk Forest Reserve: This beautiful reserve is easily accessible from Mariinsk and offers plenty of trails for hiking and biking. You’ll see stunning views of the Siberian landscape, as well as many different types of flora and fauna.

  • Khakassia Mountains: The Khakassia Mountains, located just a short drive from Mariinsk, are a popular destination for hiking enthusiasts. You’ll find trails of varying difficulty levels, which offer stunning views of the surrounding mountains, valleys, and waterfalls.

Best Things to Do in Mariinsk: Activities and Tours

Whether you’re an adrenaline junkie or just looking for a leisurely way to explore this beautiful town, Mariinsk has plenty of activities and tours to offer. Below are some ideas:

  • Siberian Off-road Adventure: Explore the beautiful surrounding countryside in a 4x4 vehicle with a local guide. You’ll see stunning views and have the opportunity to photograph iconic landmarks and landscapes.

  • Horseback Riding: Take a scenic horseback ride through the stunning Siberian landscape. This is a great way to immerse yourself in nature while enjoying the company of friendly horses.

  • City Tour: Learn about the rich history and culture of Mariinsk on a guided city tour. You’ll visit important landmarks and monuments, and get insights into local culture and customs.

Week-Long Itinerary for Mariinsk

Here’s a sample itinerary for a week’s stay in Mariinsk:

  • Day 1: Explore Mariinsk’s museums and historical landmarks, such as the Mariinsk Regional Museum of Local Lore and Mariinskaya Yarmarka.
  • Day 2: Take a trip to the Luchinsky Waterfall and enjoy a hike up to the top.
  • Day 3: Spend a day exploring Mariinsk Forest Park and take a picnic lunch.
  • Day 4: Try a spot of horseback riding or take a guided city tour.
  • Day 5: Take a day trip to Lake Teletskoye and enjoy some watersports, such as kayaking or swimming.
  • Day 6: Take a trip to the Khakassia Mountains and enjoy a hike.
  • Day 7: Relax and enjoy some downtime in one of Mariinsk’s local cafes or restaurants.

Food and Drink Scene of Mariinsk

Mariinsk offers a range of traditional foods and local drinks, so be sure to sample some of the following when you visit:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Pelmeni: The traditional Russian dumplings filled with meat or potatoes are a must-try when in Mariinsk. They’re often served with sour cream or butter on top.

  • Borscht: This famous Russian soup is a staple in Mariinsk’s cuisine. It is a hearty soup made with beets, cabbage and beef, and often served with a slice of traditional Russian black bread.

  • Kasha: A traditional Russian cereal dish, typically made from buckwheat or oats and cooked in milk, it is served for breakfast or lunch.

Local Beverages

  • Kvass: A traditional Russian fermented drink made with bread, sugar, and yeast, it is served cold and is a favourite for those who enjoy a refreshing drink with a distinct flavour.

  • Sbiten: A traditional Russian hot drink made with honey, is a perfect drink to warm up in cold weather.

Where to Stay in Mariinsk: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Mariinsk offers many options for those looking for accommodation, you can find hotels, hostels, as well as cozy guesthouses where you can take your pick. Some of the best options are:

  • Mariinskaya Hotel: This hotel offers modern, stylish rooms with all the amenities you need for a comfortable stay. The location is also a great plus point, as it is located close to the city center and many of the town’s attractions.

  • Guest House Vysotka: This charming guesthouse offers cozy and comfortable rooms for an affordable price, while staying in the heart of the city.

What to Buy in Mariinsk: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

While in Mariinsk you can’t miss out on buying a matryoshka doll, a traditional Russian wooden doll. Other souvenir options are locally made chocolates and beautifully painted decorative wooden plates, you can buy these at stalls at the Mariinskaya Yarmarka.

Local Culture and Customs of Mariinsk

Russia and Siberia have a rich culture and history, including specific customs and traditions. Mariinsk has its unique customs and traditions that are worth learning about, such as their open basket hives for beekeeping, where the bees are kept in woven baskets that are placed around the area. Bear in mind that Russians appreciate personal space and often keep an arm’s length distance when interacting.

Best Time to Visit Mariinsk

Mariinsk can be visited throughout the year, but summer from May to September is the ideal time. During summers, temperatures are comfortable, and there are fewer crowds than in winter when temperatures plummet and snow covers the streets.

Do’s and Don’ts of Mariinsk

Do:

  • Carry your passport at all times as it’s a legal requirement in Russia.
  • Learn basic Russian phrases such as “hello”, “thank you”, and “goodbye”.
  • Dress appropriately for the weather and activity you are taking part in.
  • Be mindful of local customs and traditions.

Don’t:

  • Don’t take pictures inside churches or museums, as it may be forbidden.
  • Don’t address locals in an overly informal manner.
  • Don’t bring up political topics in discussions.

Safety and Health

Safety in Mariinsk

Mariinsk is considered a relatively safe place for tourists. The town has a low crime rate, but you should still take regular precautions, such as keeping your valuables close to you and being aware of your surroundings.

Health in Mariinsk

There is a hospital in Mariinsk, but for anything serious, you may need to travel to the nearest city, Kemerovo. It’s also a good idea to have travel insurance to cover any unexpected medical expenses.

Getting to Mariinsk: Transportation Options and Logistics

Mariinsk is easily accessible by a range of transportation options. Below are some ways to get there:

Airports Near Mariinsk

The nearest international airport to Mariinsk is Tolmachevo Airport in Novosibirsk. From Novosibirsk, there are regular trains and buses to Mariinsk.

Bus and Train Stations Near Mariinsk

Mariinsk has a centralised bus terminal and a railway station with regular trains running to Moscow and other parts of Siberia.

Other Options

You can also hire a private car or taxi to take you to Mariinsk from nearby cities, including Krasnoyarsk and Novosibirsk.

How to Get Around in Mariinsk

Mariinsk has a small town center, which you can explore on foot. Taxis are also readily available, and there is a local bus system that services the town and surrounding areas.

Where to Go Next from Mariinsk: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Mariinsk is surrounded by many nearby attractions that make for great day trips or longer excursions. Some of the best options include:

  • Novosibirsk: Just a few hours away, Novosibirsk is a bustling city with plenty of museums, galleries, and restaurants to explore.

  • Krasnoyarsk: This historic city sits along the Yenisey River and is considered the gateway to the stunning Stolby Nature Reserve.

  • Kemerovo: The city is only an hour away from Mariinsk, it’s known for its vibrant cultural scene and well-known museums.

Conclusion

Mariinsk may be small, but it is full of amazing activities, delicious food, and incredible natural scenery. With a rich cultural heritage, stunning outdoor adventures, and plenty of things to see and do, Mariinsk should be on every traveler’s list of must-visit destinations. Be sure to plan your trip using this guide, to make the most out of your time there.
