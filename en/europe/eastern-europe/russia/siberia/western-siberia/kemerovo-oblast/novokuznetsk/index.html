Introduction to Novokuznetsk

Novokuznetsk is a city located in Kemerovo Oblast of Western Siberia. It is situated at the foothills of the eastern Sayan Mountains. The city is an important center of industry and is one of the largest producers of coal in Russia. Novokuznetsk is a beautiful city with interesting natural and cultural attractions. In this article, we will guide you through the city and highlight the must-visit places and things to do.

List of Places to Visit in Novokuznetsk

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Novokuznetsk Museum of Local Lore

The Novokuznetsk Museum of Local Lore is a great place to learn about the history, culture, and lifestyle of the people of Novokuznetsk. The museum has more than 30,000 exhibits, including archeological finds, artworks, and ethnographic relics.

Kuznetsk Fortress

Kuznetsk Fortress is a historical monument located in the heart of Novokuznetsk. It was built in the 18th century and served as a defense against the nomads of the steppe. The fortress provides a glimpse into Russian military architecture and is a must-visit place for history buffs.

Cathedral of St. Innokenty

The Cathedral of St. Innokenty is a beautiful example of Russian Orthodox architecture. The cathedral was constructed in the early 20th century and is dedicated to the founder of the city, Saint Innokenty. Its colorful domes and ornate decorations make it a perfect place for photography.

Natural Attractions of Novokuznetsk: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Salair Ridge

Salair ridge is a mountain range located to the south of Novokuznetsk. The ridge is known for its stunning beauty and is a popular destination for hikers and mountain climbers. From the top of the mountains, one can enjoy panoramic views of the city and surrounding landscapes.

Nature Reserve “Yergaki”

Located in the Sayan Mountains, the Yergaki Nature Reserve is a protected natural area that offers a unique experience of nature and the outdoors. The reserve has a rich biodiversity with many shelters and species of wildlife. It is ideal for hiking, trekking, camping, and picnic activities.

Gornaya Shoriya Mountainous Land

The Gornaya Shoriya Mountainous Land is a scenic region located east of Novokuznetsk. The area is known for its pristine natural beauty and famous for its ski resorts. It is an ideal destination for skiing, snowboarding, and other winter sports.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Novokuznetsk

If you are an avid hiker or biker, here are some of the popular routes you can take in Novokuznetsk:

  • Salair Ridge route
  • “Velodrom” bike route
  • “Kolos” hiking route
  • “Vostok” hiking route

Best Things to Do in Novokuznetsk: Activities and Tours

Skiing and Snowboarding

If you are a fan of skiing and snowboarding, Novokuznetsk is the perfect destination for you. The city has several ski resorts, such as Gornaya Shoriya and Belmeken.

Visit an Ice Arena

Novokuznetsk’s ice arena is a great place to spend time with friends and family. Various events are held here, including hockey games and ice-skating competitions.

Attend Musical Concerts and Festivals

You can attend many musical concerts and festivals throughout the year in Novokuznetsk. The Novokuznetsk Philharmonic Orchestra holds regular concerts, while the international jazz festival “Kuzbass Jazz” is held annually in the city.

Week-Long Itinerary for Novokuznetsk

Here is an ideal week-long itinerary for exploring the city:

  • Day 1: Visit Novokuznetsk Museum of Local Lore, Cathedral of St. Innokenty and Kuznetsk Fortress.
  • Day 2: Visit the Salair Ridge and enjoy hiking/ biking.
  • Day 3: Go skiing at Gornaya Shoriya ski resort
  • Day 4: Explore the Yergaki Nature Reserve
  • Day 5: Attend a musical concert or festival
  • Day 6: Go shopping for souvenirs at the local markets
  • Day 7: Indulge in the local food and drink scene

Food and Drink Scene of Novokuznetsk

Must-Try Dishes

  • Shangi, a traditional Tatar dish, made from dough stuffed with meat and spices.
  • Borscht, a hearty soup made with beef, beets, and other vegetables, served with sour cream.
  • Pelmeni, Siberian dumplings filled with minced meat and served with a variety of sauces.

Local Beverages

  • Utyos, a fermented drink made from honey and water.
  • Sbiten, a hot drink made from water, honey, and spices.
  • Kvass, a popular drink made from fermented bread and sugar.

Local Culture and Customs of Novokuznetsk

The people of Novokuznetsk are known for their friendliness and hospitality. The city has a unique culture that is influenced by its history and geography. It is recommended that visitors learn the basics of the Russian language before visiting to help ease communication.

Best Time to Visit Novokuznetsk

The best time to visit Novokuznetsk is during the summer months when the weather is mild and perfect for outdoor activities. September and October are also good months for visiting as the fall colors are beautiful.

Do’s and Don’ts of Novokuznetsk

Do:

  • Try the local cuisine and drinks
  • Learn some basic Russian phrases to help with communication.
  • Respect local customs and traditions

Don’t:

  • Do not disrespect the country’s political or religious beliefs.
  • Do not litter or damage public property.
  • Do not attempt to photograph military installations or personnel without permission.

Safety and Health

Safety in Novokuznetsk

Novokuznetsk is generally a safe city, but visitors should still take precautions while traveling. Ensure to keep valuable items safely and avoid walking alone in secluded areas at night.

Health in Novokuznetsk

Visitors are not typically required to obtain vaccines or take specific health precautions when traveling to Novokuznetsk. However, it is always wise to check with your doctor before traveling.

Getting to Novokuznetsk: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Novokuznetsk

The closest airport to Novokuznetsk is the Spichenkovo airport, which receives regular domestic and international flights. It is about 40 kilometers from the city center.

Bus Stations Near Novokuznetsk

Novokuznetsk has modern bus stations that offer transportation to various other cities in the region. The main bus station is located in the city center.

Other Options

Train travel is also an excellent option for getting to Novokuznetsk. The railway station is near the city center and operates regular trains to various parts of Russia.

How to Get Around in Novokuznetsk

Novokuznetsk has several modes of transportation, such as buses, trams, and trolleybuses. Taxis are also available, and you can use smartphone apps to book them. It is essential to learn the basics of the Cyrillic alphabet to navigate the city efficiently.

Conclusion

Novokuznetsk is a beautiful city with unique natural and cultural attractions. It is an ideal destination for nature lovers, adventure seekers, history buffs, and food enthusiasts. We hope that this comprehensive guide has been helpful in planning your trip to Novokuznetsk.
