Introduction to Tashtagol

Located in Kemerovo Oblast, Western Siberia, Tashtagol is a small town located at the intersection of two rivers, Chulym and Tom. The town is surrounded by the beautiful Altai Mountains which makes it an ideal destination for anyone who loves nature and outdoor adventures. In this travel guide, we will highlight some of the hidden gems as well as must-see attractions in Tashtagol.

List of Places to Visit in Tashtagol

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

For those interested in learning about the rich culture and history of Siberia, Tashtagol has quite a few museums and historical places that offer a glimpse into the town’s past. The Kuzbass Museum of Local Lore is a great place to start. It is located in Kemerovo, a nearby city, and it has a vast collection of artifacts and exhibits that showcase the region’s natural history.

The Museum of Fine Arts is another must-visit for art lovers. It features both contemporary and classic art pieces from local artists.

Natural Attractions of Tashtagol: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

There is a lot to explore in Tashtagol’s nature. The town has several beautiful parks and reserves that offer exciting outdoor activities. One such park is the Taldinsky Reserve. It covers a vast area and is an ideal place to go hiking, biking, or camping.

Another beautiful natural attraction in Tashtagol is the Zavalishina Lake. It is a crystal-clear freshwater lake surrounded by mountains, wildflowers, and wildlife. It is a perfect place for swimming, kayaking, or just soaking in the stunning view.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Tashtagol

Tashtagol is an excellent place for hiking and biking enthusiasts. The town has a host of well-marked hiking and biking trails suitable for adventurers of all levels. Some of the popular hiking trails in the area include the Zmeinoe Lake Trail, the Ulagansky Circuit, and the Chulyshman Trail.

Best Things to Do in Tashtagol: Activities and Tours

Aside from hiking, there are other exciting things to do in Tashtagol. Here are some activities and tours that are worth checking out:

  • Visit the Dolina Krasoty, which is a popular ski resort in Tashtagol.

  • Take a boat ride along the Tom River.

  • Visit one of the local mines to learn about the town’s mining heritage.

  • Go fishing in one of the many nearby rivers.

Week-Long Itinerary for Tashtagol

If you are planning to spend a week in Tashtagol, here is a suggested itinerary:

Day 1-2: Explore the natural attractions in Tashtagol, including the Zavalishina Lake and the Taldinsky Reserve.

Day 3-4: Take a day trip to Kemerovo to visit museums, including the Museum of Fine Arts and the Kuzbass Museum of Local Lore.

Day 5: Enjoy skiing and snowboarding at the Dolina Krasoty ski resort.

Day 6-7: Go hiking along one of the popular trails and take in the scenic views.

Food and Drink Scene of Tashtagol

Must-Try Dishes

Tashtagol offers traditional Siberian cuisine made with locally sourced ingredients. Here are some dishes worth trying:

  • Pelmeni: Siberian dumplings stuffed with meat or cheese

  • Shashlik: Grilled meat skewers

  • Borscht: Beetroot soup served with sour cream

Local Beverages

Tashtagol has a thriving craft brewery scene. There are several microbreweries in the town that produce excellent craft beer using local ingredients.

Where to Stay in Tashtagol: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Tashtagol has several accommodation options, ranging from budget-friendly hostels to luxury five-star hotels. Here are some of the top options:

  • Hotel Altay: A luxury hotel with modern amenities and friendly staff.

  • Hostel TALDI: A budget-friendly hostel that offers dormitory-style accommodation.

  • Guest House Taiga: A cozy bed and breakfast located in a quiet area.

What to Buy in Tashtagol: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

If you are looking for souvenirs to bring back home, here are some of the local products that are unique to Tashtagol:

  • Kuzbass honey: Tashtagol is known for its locally produced delicious honey.

  • Souvenir dolls: Matryoshka dolls hand-painted by local artisans.

Local Culture and Customs of Tashtagol

The people of Tashtagol are friendly and hospitable. You will get an opportunity to learn about the local culture and customs by visiting cultural festivals, museums, and participating in cultural activities.

Best Time to Visit Tashtagol

The best time to visit Tashtagol is during the summer months, from June to August when the weather is warm and sunny. However, if you are into winter sports, consider visiting between December and February when there is plenty of snow for skiing and snowboarding.

Do’s and Don’ts of Tashtagol

Do:

  • Respect the local culture and customs.

  • Dress appropriately when visiting religious sites.

  • Tip your servers at restaurants and cafes.

Don’t:

  • Photograph people without their permission.

  • Litter in public areas.

  • Visit sensitive areas without a local guide.

Safety and Health

Safety in Tashtagol

Overall, Tashtagol is a safe destination. However, it is advisable to take precautionary measures such as avoiding traveling alone at night in remote areas.

Health in Tashtagol

Visitors should have travel insurance and be up to date on their routine vaccinations. Also, make sure to bring any necessary medication with you.

Getting to Tashtagol: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Tashtagol

The nearest airport to Tashtagol is the Kemerovo International Airport which is located about 3 hours drive away.

Bus Stations Near Tashtagol

Tashtagol has a bus station that connects to several nearby cities and towns.

Other Options

Tashtagol can also be accessed by train. The nearest train station is located in Kemerovo, which is approximately 230 km away.

How to Get Around in Tashtagol

The easiest way to get around Tashtagol is by car. Alternatively, you can rent a bicycle or join an organized tour group.

Where to Go Next from Tashtagol: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

If you’re thinking of exploring other destinations in Siberia, here are some nearby destinations worth considering:

  • Novokuznetsk: A nearby city that is popular for its cultural landmarks and outdoor activities.

  • Sheregesh: A beautiful ski resort located one hour away from Tashtagol.

  • Tomsk: A historic city known for its wooden architecture and cultural sites.

Conclusion

Tashtagol has a lot to offer visitors. With its scenic views, natural attractions, and cultural landmarks, it’s a destination that is worth adding to your bucket list.
