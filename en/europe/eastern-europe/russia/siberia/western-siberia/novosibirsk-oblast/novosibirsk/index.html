Introduction to Novosibirsk

Novosibirsk is the third-largest city in Russia, located in Western Siberia. Founded in 1893, it is the industrial and scientific center of Siberia, which has made it a sought-after destination for tourists and business travelers alike. It is a city that boasts of unique historical and cultural attractions, including museums, parks, and theaters. Being the gateway to Siberia, Novosibirsk serves as the perfect base for embarking on a trip through the vast region.

List of Places to Visit in Novosibirsk

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Novosibirsk State Art Museum

The Novosibirsk State Art Museum is one of the city’s most popular landmarks. It features an extensive collection of Russian and Western European art from the 16th to 21st centuries. Apart from art, the museum’s collection includes ceramics, glass, and precious metal items.

Novosibirsk State Museum of Local Lore

The museum showcases the history of the Siberian region with its exhibitions covering geology, flora and fauna, and ancient human settlements. The museum houses over 650,000 exhibits, including rare pieces like the Altai Princess.

Alexander Nevsky Cathedral

The Alexander Nevsky Cathedral is one of the largest Russian Orthodox churches in Siberia, located in the city center. It is known for its stunning architecture, adorned with golden domes and colorful frescoes.

Natural Attractions of Novosibirsk: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Novosibirsk Zoo

The Novosibirsk Zoo is located on the right bank of the Ob River and is one of Russia’s largest zoos. It houses over 11,000 animals from all over the world, including Amur tigers, snow leopards, and pandas.

Akademgorodok

Akademgorodok is a scientific research town located on the outskirts of Novosibirsk. Surrounded by the Siberian taiga forest, the town has become a popular destination for outdoor enthusiasts, offering activities like hiking, fishing, and skiing.

Novosibirsk Reservoir

The Novosibirsk Reservoir is a manmade lake located on the Ob River’s upstream. It is the city’s primary source of water and serves as a popular recreation spot for the locals. The reservoir offers a wide range of activities like boating, fishing, and swimming.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Novosibirsk

Kamenka River Hiking Trail

The Kamenka River Hiking Trail is a 20 km trail that follows the Kamenka River through the Siberian forest. The hike takes around four hours and offers a chance to see some of the region’s wildlife like brown bears, lynxes, and reindeer.

Toguchin Lake Bike Trail

The Toguchin Lake Bike Trail is a 50 km trail that starts at Kameshkovo village and ends at Toguchin Lake. It is a great way to explore the Siberian countryside and pass by local villages and historic landmarks like the Toguchin Church.

Best Things to Do in Novosibirsk: Activities, and Tours

Visit Academgorodok

Academgorodok is a scientific research town located on the outskirts of Novosibirsk, offering a chance to learn about the advancements in science and technology. Visitors can explore the town’s museums, labs, and research facilities.

Explore Novosibirsk City Centre

The city center is full of historical landmarks and museums showcasing the city’s cultural and historical heritage. From the Alexander Nevsky Cathedral to the Novosibirsk Opera and Ballet Theatre, there is something for everyone.

Food and Drink Scene of Novosibirsk

Must-Try Dishes

Pelmeni

Pelmeni is a traditional Siberian dish usually made of minced meat, onions, and spices wrapped in dough and boiled or fried.

Shangi

Shangi is a type of dumpling filled with various meats or vegetables, and you can often find it at street food stalls.

Local Beverages

Vodka

Russia is famous for its vodka, and Novosibirsk has no shortage of brands to sample. Be sure to try local brands like Sibiryachka or Syvoe Serebro.

Kvass

Kvass is a traditional fermented drink made of rye bread and has a sweet and sour taste.

Local Culture and Customs of Novosibirsk

Russian is the official language, and English is not widely spoken, so it’s helpful to learn some basic Russian phrases. Novosibirsk has a rich cultural heritage, and visitors should respect the local customs and traditions, especially in places of worship.

Best Time to Visit Novosibirsk

The best time to visit Novosibirsk is during the summer months of June to August when the weather is pleasant, and outdoor activities are in full swing.

Do’s and Don’ts of Novosibirsk

Do:

  • Dress appropriately when visiting religious sites
  • Exchange money at official currency exchange points
  • Learn some basic Russian phrases

Don’t:

  • Get into unlicensed taxis
  • Photograph military buildings
  • Drink tap water

Safety and Health

Safety in Novosibirsk

Novosibirsk is a relatively safe city, and visitors can move around without worrying too much about safety. However, visitors should remain vigilant in busy areas and avoid flashing valuables in public.

Health in Novosibirsk

Visitors should ensure that they have comprehensive medical insurance as medical treatment in Novosibirsk can be expensive. It is advisable to carry a first-aid kit and take precautions against insect bites, especially during the summer months.

Getting to Novosibirsk: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Novosibirsk

The city is served by the Novosibirsk Tolmachevo International Airport, which is the largest airport in Siberia. It is well-connected to several domestic and international destinations.

Bus Stations Near Novosibirsk

The city has several long-distance bus stations, including the Novosibirsk Bus Station and the Novosibirsk Central Bus Station. Buses run daily to various destinations in Russia and neighboring countries.

Other Options

Trains are an excellent option for those traveling within Russia, and Novosibirsk is served by the Novosibirsk-Glavny railway station, which connects to several major cities across the country.

How to Get Around in Novosibirsk

The city has an extensive public transportation system that includes trams, trolleybuses, and buses. Taxis are also readily available and are a convenient way to get around. Visitors can also rent bicycles to explore the city at their leisure.

Where to Go Next from Novosibirsk: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Tomsk

Tomsk is a historic city located about 4 hours away from Novosibirsk, known for its wooden architecture and vibrant student community.

Altai Mountains

The Altai Mountains are located to the south of Novosibirsk and offer some of the most spectacular natural scenery in Russia. Visitors can hike, camp, and explore the region’s rich cultural heritage.

Conclusion

Novosibirsk is a city that offers something for everyone, from its cultural landmarks to its natural beauty and outdoor adventures. With this comprehensive travel guide, visitors can plan the perfect trip to this fascinating destination.
