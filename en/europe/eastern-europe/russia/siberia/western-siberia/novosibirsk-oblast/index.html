Introduction to Novosibirsk Oblast

Novosibirsk Oblast is a region located in Western Siberia, Russia. Novosibirsk, its capital, is the third-largest city in Russia and home to many cultural attractions, museums, parks, and theaters. Novosibirsk Oblast is the perfect destination for travelers who enjoy the great outdoors, including hiking, biking, skiing, and fishing.

List of Places to Visit in Novosibirsk Oblast

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Novosibirsk State Museum of Local Lore
  • Novosibirsk State Art Museum
  • Alexander Nevsky Cathedral
  • Novosibirsk Opera and Ballet Theater
  • Novosibirsk Zoo

The Novosibirsk State Museum of Local Lore has a collection of natural and cultural artifacts from Siberia and the Far East, as well as interactive exhibits about the region’s geography, history, and culture. The Novosibirsk State Art Museum features an impressive collection of international and Russian art, including works by Kandinsky, Malevich, and Shishkin. The Alexander Nevsky Cathedral, completed in 1888, is a beautiful example of Russian Orthodox architecture. The Novosibirsk Opera and Ballet Theater is considered to be one of the best in Russia, with a repertoire of classical and contemporary performances. The Novosibirsk Zoo is one of the oldest zoos in Russia, with over 10,000 animals from 750 different species.

Natural Attractions of Novosibirsk Oblast: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Altai Mountains
  • Lake Teletskoye
  • Stolby Nature Reserve
  • Abalak Ski Resort

The Altai Mountains, located in the southern part of Novosibirsk Oblast, offer stunning views, crystal-clear rivers, and wildlife, making it a perfect destination for hiking, fishing, and horseback riding. Lake Teletskoye, also in the Altai Mountains, is the largest lake in the Altai region, and its clear waters are ideal for swimming, boating, and fishing. Stolby Nature Reserve, located 20 kilometers from Novosibirsk, is famous for its unique rock formations, which attract climbers and adventurers from all over the world. Abalak Ski Resort, located on the banks of the Ob River, offers skiing, snowboarding, and other winter activities in the heart of Siberia.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Novosibirsk Oblast

  • Altai Mountains: Belukha Mountain Route
  • Lake Teletskoye: Around the Lake Route
  • Stolby Nature Reserve: Stolby Route
  • Akademgorodok: Science and Education Route

The Belukha Mountain Route is one of the most challenging hiking trails in Novosibirsk Oblast, with steep hills, rugged terrain, and breathtaking views of the Altai Mountains. The Around the Lake Route is a moderately difficult hiking trail that loops around Lake Teletskoye, with stunning panoramic views and many picnic spots along the way. The Stolby Route is a popular hiking trail, famous for its towering rock formations and challenging ascent to the top of one of the Stolby pillars. The Science and Education Route is a cycling route that takes you through Akademgorodok, a science town known for its world-class research institutions and beautiful parks.

Best Things to Do in Novosibirsk Oblast: Activities and Tours

  • Novosibirsk City Tour
  • Siberian Music Hall of Fame
  • Skiing in the Altai Mountains
  • Russian Banya Experience
  • Visiting Local Markets and Restaurants

The Novosibirsk City Tour takes you through the city’s most famous landmarks, including Lenin Square, Krasny Prospekt, and the Ob River embankment. The Siberian Music Hall of Fame is a must-see for music lovers, with exhibitions and memorabilia dedicated to famous Siberian musicians. Skiing in the Altai Mountains is one of the best winter activities in Novosibirsk Oblast, with a variety of trails and slopes for every level of skier. The Russian Banya Experience is a traditional Russian sauna experience, including steam baths, cold plunges, and herbal tea. Visiting local markets and restaurants is an excellent way to sample traditional Siberian cuisine and buy local produce and souvenirs.

Week-Long Itinerary for Novosibirsk Oblast

Day 1: Novosibirsk City Tour: Lenin Square, Krasny Prospekt, and the Ob River Embankment. Day 2: Altai Mountains: Belukha Mountain Hike. Day 3: Lake Teletskoye: Around the Lake Hike. Day 4: Stolby Nature Reserve: Stolby Hike. Day 5: Russian Banya Experience. Day 6: Visiting Local Markets and Restaurants. Day 7: Siberian Music Hall of Fame and Departure.

Food and Drink Scene of Novosibirsk Oblast

Must-Try Dishes

  • Pelmeni
  • Shchi
  • Kasha
  • Blini

Pelmeni, small dumplings filled with meat or mushrooms, are a staple of Russian cuisine and can be found in many local restaurants and cafes. Shchi, a traditional Russian soup made from cabbage, potato, and meat, is perfect for cold Siberian winters. Kasha, a type of porridge made from boiled cereal grains and milk, is a healthy and nutritious breakfast or snack option. Blini, thin pancakes served with sweet or savory toppings, are a classic Russian dish that can be found in many cafes and restaurants.

Local Beverages

  • Samogon
  • Kvass
  • Chai

Samogon, also known as ‘Russian moonshine,’ is a strong alcoholic drink made from fermented grain or fruit. Kvass, a beverage made from rye bread and served cold, is a refreshing drink popular in Russia and other Eastern European countries. Chai, or Russian tea, is a traditional hot beverage served in a samovar with sugar, lemon, and a variety of herbs.

Where to Stay in Novosibirsk Oblast: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Novosibirsk Marriott Hotel
  • DoubleTree by Hilton
  • River Park Hotel
  • Hostel Kruiz

Novosibirsk Marriott Hotel and DoubleTree by Hilton are two luxury hotels located in the city center, with modern amenities, excellent service, and beautiful views of the city. River Park Hotel is a mid-range hotel located on the banks of the Ob River, with a spa, gym, and restaurant. Hostel Kruiz is a budget hostel located in the city center, with dormitory and private rooms available, free Wi-Fi, and a communal kitchen.

What to Buy in Novosibirsk Oblast: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Matryoshka Dolls
  • Birch Bark Items
  • Fur Hats and Coats
  • Siberian Pine Nuts

Matryoshka Dolls, a set of wooden nesting dolls, are a popular souvenir to bring home from Russia and can be found in many souvenir shops and markets. Birch bark items, including boxes, baskets, and jewelry, are a traditional Siberian handicraft and make a unique and eco-friendly gift. Fur hats and coats, made from high-quality fur, are a luxury product that can be found in many specialty stores. Siberian Pine Nuts, which are larger and tastier than regular pine nuts, are a healthy and tasty snack that can be found in local markets and shops.

Local Culture and Customs of Novosibirsk Oblast

The locals in Novosibirsk Oblast are known for their hospitality, warmth, and friendliness toward visitors. However, it is important to be aware of local customs and traditions, such as removing your shoes before entering a house and dressing modestly if visiting religious sites or areas.

Best Time to Visit Novosibirsk Oblast

The best time to visit Novosibirsk Oblast is during the summer months, from June to August, when the weather is warm and pleasant, and outdoor activities are available. However, winter in Novosibirsk Oblast is also magical, with plenty of opportunities to ski, sled, and enjoy the snow-covered landscapes.

Do’s and Don’ts of Novosibirsk Oblast

Do:

  • Learn a few basic Russian phrases.
  • Respect local customs and traditions.
  • Dress warmly and in layers during the cold winter months.

Don’t:

  • Drink tap water without boiling or filtering it.
  • Engage in loud or disrespectful behavior.
  • Trespass on private property.

Safety and Health

Safety in Novosibirsk Oblast

Novosibirsk Oblast is a safe destination for travelers. However, it is always important to be aware of your surroundings, keep your valuables close, and avoid wandering alone in isolated or unfamiliar areas, especially at night.

Health in Novosibirsk Oblast

Travelers should be up to date on routine vaccinations, including measles, mumps, and rubella (MMR) vaccine and the seasonal flu vaccine. It is also recommended to receive the Hepatitis A and B vaccines before traveling to Novosibirsk Oblast. It is important to consult with a professional healthcare provider before traveling to ensure proper preparation and medication.

Getting to Novosibirsk Oblast: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Novosibirsk Oblast

  • Tolmachevo Airport
  • Yemelyanovo International Airport
  • Barnaul Airport

Tolmachevo Airport is the main airport of Novosibirsk and is located 16 km from the city center. Yemelyanovo International Airport is located in Krasnoyarsk and is the second-largest airport in Siberia, approximately 4.5 hours from Novosibirsk by car or bus. Barnaul Airport is located in Barnaul, the capital of Altai Krai, approximately 5 hours from Novosibirsk by car or bus.

Bus Stations Near Novosibirsk Oblast

  • Novosibirsk Bus Station
  • Barnaul Bus Station

Novosibirsk Bus Station is located in the city center and is the main bus terminal for Novosibirsk Oblast. Barnaul Bus Station is located in Barnaul and is the main bus terminal for Altai Krai.

Other Options

  • Train: Novosibirsk is a major railway hub in Siberia, with connections to Moscow, St. Petersburg, and other major cities.
  • Car rental: Car rentals are available at Tolmachevo Airport and in the city center.
  • Taxi: Taxis are easily available in Novosibirsk, with metered and fixed pricing available.

How to Get Around in Novosibirsk Oblast

Public Transport

  • Bus
  • Trolleybus
  • Tram
  • Metro

Public transport is cheap and efficient in Novosibirsk, with buses, trolleybuses, and trams available in the city center. The Novosibirsk Metro is the third-largest metro system in Russia, with two lines and 13 stations. A single trip on public transport costs between 20 and 40 rubles (0.25 to 0.50 USD).

Taxi

  • Yandex.Taxi
  • Uber

Taxis are also easily available in Novosibirsk, with Yandex.Taxi and Uber being the popular options. A typical trip within the city center costs between 200 and 500 rubles (2.50 to 6 USD).

Where to Go Next from Novosibirsk Oblast: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Altai Republic
  • Tomsk
  • Krasnoyarsk
  • Lake Baikal

The Altai Republic is a neighboring region famous for its natural beauty and scenery, including the Altai Mountains, Katun River, and Teletskoye Lake. Tomsk, a university city located 4 hours from Novosibirsk, is known for its wooden architecture, vibrant cultural scene, and historic sites. Krasnoyarsk, located 12 hours from Novosibirsk by train, is a beautiful city situated on the Yenisei River, with many cultural, historical, and natural attractions. Lake Baikal, the deepest lake in the world, is located approximately 36 hours from Novosibirsk by train and is a top excursion for travelers interested in nature and outdoor activities.

Conclusion

Novosibirsk Oblast is a fascinating and diverse destination, with something to offer for every type of traveler. Whether you’re interested in cultural attractions, outdoor adventures, or simply immersing yourself in local customs and cuisine, Novosibirsk Oblast provides an unforgettable experience.
