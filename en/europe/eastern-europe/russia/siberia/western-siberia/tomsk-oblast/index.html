Introduction to Tomsk Oblast

Tomsk Oblast is a destination for travelers who appreciate natural beauty, outdoor adventures, and cultural experiences. Located in Western Siberia, in Russia, it is a region with a rich history, diverse landscapes, and a unique culture.

Tomsk, the oblast’s capital city, was established in 1604 and is home to numerous universities, museums, and historic sites. The region is known for its pristine forests, rivers, and lakes that offer ample opportunities for hiking, fishing, camping, and other outdoor activities.

Whether you come for the history, the culture, the nature, or all of the above, Tomsk Oblast will not disappoint. Keep reading to discover the best places to visit, things to do, and how to make the most of your trip to this fascinating destination.

List of Places to Visit in Tomsk Oblast

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Tomsk Oblast has a wealth of museums and historic sites that offer insights into the region’s unique culture and history. Here are some must-visit attractions:

  • Tomsk State University Museum of History and Culture: This museum is where you can learn about the history and culture of Siberia, including its indigenous people and Russian settlers. It has an extensive collection of artifacts, documents, and photographs that tell the story of the region.
  • Tomsk State Art Museum: This museum houses an impressive collection of art from the 18th century to the present day, with an emphasis on Siberian artists. Its collection includes paintings, sculptures, and decorative arts.
  • Tomsk Regional Museum of Local Lore: This museum showcases the flora and fauna, geology, and history of Tomsk Oblast. It includes exhibits on natural history and human history, with a particular focus on Siberian culture.

Natural Attractions of Tomsk Oblast: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Tomsk Oblast is blessed with natural beauty and a variety of landscapes, from dense forests to rolling hills, to vast steppes. Here are some of the best natural attractions to visit:

  • Stolby Nature Reserve: This is an excellent destination for hikers and climbers. It offers stunning views of the surrounding forests, hills, and rivers. The reserve has numerous trails of different lengths and difficulty levels, as well as rock formations that are popular for climbing.
  • Lake Chebakul: This lake is a popular destination for fishing, swimming, and camping. It is surrounded by pine forests and has plenty of campsites and cabins for visitors.
  • Putorana Plateau: This is a remote and pristine wilderness area in the northern part of Tomsk Oblast. It includes high plateaus, deep canyons, and numerous waterfalls. It is a UNESCO World Heritage Site and is a destination for adventurous travelers who seek solitude and pristine nature.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Tomsk Oblast

Tomsk Oblast has numerous trails and routes for hiking and biking enthusiasts. Here are some of the most scenic and popular trails:

  • Stolby Nature Reserve Trails: The reserve has several trails of different length and difficulty that offer stunning views of the surrounding forests and hills. Some of the most popular trails include the Blue Trail, the Green Trail, and the Yellow Trail.
  • Khibiny Mountains: These mountains offer challenging hikes and epic landscapes. The trails vary in difficulty and length, so there is something for hikers of all levels.
  • Tomsk River Greenway: This is a network of trails and paths that run along the Tom River. It offers scenic views of the river and the city and is a great choice for a leisurely bike ride or stroll.

Best Things to Do in Tomsk Oblast: Activities and Tours

Tomsk Oblast has many activities and tours that cater to a wide variety of interests and preferences. Here are some of the best things to do in the region:

  • Visit the Local Markets: Tomsk has several vibrant markets, including the Central Market and the Craftsmen’s Fair. They offer a variety of locally made products, including traditional crafts, food, and drink.
  • Take a River Cruise: Several companies offer scenic river cruises along the Tom River from Tomsk. They offer guided tours and stunning views of the city and the surrounding countryside.
  • Explore the Local Villages: There are several traditional Siberian villages in the region that offer insights into the local culture and way of life. Some of the most popular ones include Asino, Kozhevnikovo, and Kolpashevo.

Week-Long Itinerary for Tomsk Oblast

Here’s a suggested week-long itinerary for exploring the best of Tomsk Oblast:

  • Day 1-2: Explore Tomsk, including the museums and historic sites.
  • Day 3-4: Visit the Stolby Nature Reserve and hike the trails.
  • Day 5: Take a river cruise from Tomsk to explore the surroundings.
  • Day 6-7: Visit the Khibiny Mountains for hiking and outdoor adventures.

Food and Drink Scene of Tomsk Oblast

Tomsk Oblast is a destination for foodies who appreciate hearty, flavorful, and locally sourced cuisine. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Pelmeni: These are small dumplings filled with meat (or other stuffing) that are boiled and served with sour cream or butter.
  • Shangi: These are traditional Siberian pies filled with meat, potatoes, or cabbage.
  • Omul: This is a species of salmon that is caught in Lake Baikal and other Siberian lakes. It is smoked, grilled, or baked and served with bread and vegetables.

Local Beverages

  • Kvass: This is a refreshing non-alcoholic drink made from fermented bread that is popular in Russia and other Slavic countries.
  • Medovukha: This is a traditional Russian honey-based alcoholic beverage that is sweet and slightly carbonated. It is often served at festivals and celebrations.

Where to Stay in Tomsk Oblast: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Tomsk Oblast has a wide variety of accommodation options that cater to different budgets and preferences. Here are some of the best places to stay:

  • Tomsk City Centre: This is the most convenient location for exploring the city’s attractions and museums. There are several hotels and guesthouses in the area, ranging from budget-friendly to luxury options.
  • Stolby Nature Reserve: There are several cabins and campsites in the reserve that offer a unique and immersive nature experience.
  • Rural Guesthouses: There are several guesthouses in the surrounding rural areas that offer a glimpse into the local culture and way of life.

What to Buy in Tomsk Oblast: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Tomsk Oblast is known for its traditional crafts, including woodworking, pottery, and embroidery. Here are some souvenirs and local products to bring home:

  • Matryoshka Dolls: These are traditional Russian wooden dolls that are painted with colorful designs and stacked inside each other.
  • Siberian Cedar Products: Siberian cedar nuts and oil are known for their health benefits and are used in traditional medicine. Look for cedarwood handicrafts and souvenirs, including jewelry, boxes, and home decor.
  • Traditional Textiles: Look for traditional Siberian embroidered clothes and handicrafts, including shirts, dresses, shawls, and tablecloths.

Local Culture and Customs of Tomsk Oblast

Tomsk Oblast has a unique culture that is shaped by its history, geography, and climate. Here are some customs and traditions to be aware of:

  • Hospitality: Siberians are known for their warm hospitality and welcoming attitude towards guests. Expect to be offered food and drink and invited to share in local traditions and customs.
  • Russian Orthodox Christianity: The majority of the population in Tomsk Oblast is Russian Orthodox Christian, and the religion has a strong influence on local culture and traditions.
  • Siberian Indigenous Peoples: The region is home to several indigenous Siberian peoples, including the Khanty and the Mansi. Their customs and traditions have shaped the local culture and are celebrated in festivals and events.

Best Time to Visit Tomsk Oblast

The best time to visit Tomsk Oblast depends on your interests and preferences. Here’s a rundown of the seasons and what they offer:

  • Summer (June-August): This is the peak tourist season when the weather is warm and sunny. It’s the best time for outdoor activities and festivals.
  • Autumn (September-November): This is a great time for scenic drives and hikes when the fall foliage is at its peak.
  • Winter (December-February): This is the coldest and snowiest season, but it offers unique winter activities like skiing, ice fishing, and snowmobiling.
  • Spring (March-May): This is the season of renewal and rebirth when the days start getting longer and the flowers start blooming.

Do’s and Don’ts of Tomsk Oblast

Do:

  • Respect Local Customs: Be respectful of local customs and traditions, including dress codes and religious practices.
  • Try Local Food and Drink: Tomsk Oblast has a rich culinary tradition, so be sure to try the local dishes and beverages.
  • Pack for the Weather: Siberia’s weather can be unpredictable, so be prepared for different conditions.

Don’t:

  • Disrespect the Local Culture: Avoid making derogatory comments about local customs and traditions.
  • Photograph People Without Permission: Ask for permission before taking photos of local people.

Safety and Health

Safety in Tomsk Oblast

Tomsk Oblast is generally a safe destination for travelers, but it’s important to take usual precautions to ensure your safety. Here are some tips:

  • Be Aware of Your Surroundings: Be aware of your surroundings and avoid isolated areas at night.
  • Protect Your Valuables: Be cautious with your valuables and keep them out of sight in crowded areas.
  • Use Licensed Taxis and Transportation: Use only licensed taxis or transportation to ensure your safety.

Health in Tomsk Oblast

Travelers should be aware of the following health issues in Tomsk Oblast:

  • Vaccinations: Make sure your routine vaccinations are up to date. Hepatitis A and B, typhoid, and rabies vaccinations are also recommended.
  • Altitude Sickness: Some areas of Tomsk Oblast, including the Putorana Plateau, are at high altitude and require acclimatization.
  • Tick-borne Diseases: Lyme disease and tick-borne encephalitis are present in some areas of Tomsk Oblast. Take precautions against ticks when hiking or camping.

Getting to Tomsk Oblast: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Tomsk Oblast

  • Bogashevo Airport in Tomsk: This is the main airport serving Tomsk Oblast and has direct flights to several destinations in Russia.

Bus Stations Near Tomsk Oblast

  • Tomsk Bus Station: This is the main bus station in Tomsk, with regular services to nearby cities and towns.

Other Options

  • Trains: Tomsk is connected to the Trans-Siberian Railway, which runs from Moscow to Vladivostok.

Where to Go Next from Tomsk Oblast: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Here are some nearby destinations and excursions that you can consider:

  • Novosibirsk: This is the largest city in Siberia and is famous for its museums, theaters, and restaurants.
  • Lake Baikal: This is the deepest lake in the world and is a UNESCO World Heritage Site. It’s a popular destination for hiking and nature viewing.
  • Krasnoyarsk: This is a historic city on the Yenisei River with numerous museums, parks, and cultural events.
