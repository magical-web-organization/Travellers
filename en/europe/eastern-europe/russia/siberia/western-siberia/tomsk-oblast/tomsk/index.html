Introduction to Tomsk

Tomsk is a city located in Tomsk Oblast in Western Siberia, Russia, and is known for its blend of rich history and modern technology. Founded in 1604, Tomsk is one of the oldest Siberian cities and is home to several world-renowned universities. With a population of over 500,000 people, Tomsk attracts both business and leisure travelers from all over the world.

List of Places to Visit in Tomsk

When planning a trip to Tomsk, make sure to visit these must-see attractions:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Tomsk Regional Museum: This museum showcases the cultural and natural history of Tomsk and the surrounding regions.
  • The Tomsk House of Merchant Klychkov: Built in the early 19th century, this wooden mansion provides a glimpse into the lifestyle of wealthy merchants in imperial Russia.
  • Tomsk Art Gallery: This gallery houses over 13,000 works of art, including pieces from world-famous artists such as Ilya Repin and Ivan Shishkin.

Natural Attractions of Tomsk: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • The Tom River: Take a stroll along the banks of this beautiful river, which runs through the heart of Tomsk.
  • Tomskaya Pisanitsa: This archaeological site is the largest collection of petroglyphs in Siberia, dating back to the Neolithic period.
  • Stolby Nature Reserve: Located just outside of Tomsk, this nature reserve is known for its unique rock formations and offers great hiking trails.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Tomsk

Tomsk has several scenic routes for hiking and biking enthusiasts, including:

  • Red Route: This 12-kilometer route takes hikers through the Stolby Nature Reserve.
  • Green Route: This 15-kilometer route takes hikers and bikers through the forested areas surrounding Tomsk.
  • Blue Route: This 17-kilometer route takes hikers and bikers through the industrial parts of Tomsk and offers a unique view of the city.

Best Things to Do in Tomsk: Activities and Tours

Tomsk offers a range of activities and tours for travelers, including:

  • Visit the Local Markets: Tomsk has several local markets that offer a glimpse into the local way of life and are great places to discover local products.
  • Take a Walking Tour of the City: Join a local tour guide and explore the historic old town of Tomsk.
  • Visit the University Campus: Tomsk is home to several world-renowned universities, and visiting their campuses is a great way to immerse oneself in the local academic culture.

Week-Long Itinerary for Tomsk

Day 1: Explore the city on foot, visit the Tomsk Regional Museum, and attend a performance at the Tomsk Drama Theater.

Day 2: Take a hiking or biking tour of the city, and visit the Tom River.

Day 3: Visit Tomskaya Pisanitsa and Stolby Nature Reserve, and enjoy a picnic in the park.

Day 4: Explore the art scene in Tomsk, and visit the Tomsk Art Gallery.

Day 5: Take a tour of the local markets and try some traditional Siberian food.

Day 6: Visit the University campus and attend a lecture or seminar.

Day 7: Explore the surrounding areas of Tomsk, and take a day trip to Slavgorod, a nearby village known for its traditional handicrafts.

Food and Drink Scene of Tomsk

Tomsk offers a unique blend of Russian and Siberian cuisine:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Pelmeni: Siberian dumplings filled with minced meat or fish and served with sour cream.
  • Pirozhki: Deep-fried or baked buns filled with meat, vegetables, or sweet fillings.
  • Shashlik: Grilled chunks of meat or fish served with vegetables.

Local Beverages

  • Tarkhun: A non-alcoholic, carbonated drink flavored with tarragon.
  • Birch Sap: A refreshing, non-alcoholic drink made from the sap of birch trees.

Where to Stay in Tomsk: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Tomsk has several accommodation options for travelers:

  • Hotels: Hotel Tomsk, Crystal Palace Hotel, and Bon Apart Hotel are some of the top hotels in Tomsk.
  • Hostels: Siberian Hostel, Elephant Hostel, and Barnaul Hostel are affordable options for budget travelers.
  • Guesthouses: Guest House Prestige, Guest House Caramel, and Guest House Na Sadovoy offer comfortable and affordable accommodation with a personal touch.

What to Buy in Tomsk: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Tomsk is known for its handmade crafts and souvenirs:

  • Fur Products: Tomsk is known for its high-quality fur products, including hats, gloves, and coats.
  • Birchwood Products: Tomsk is surrounded by birch forests, and craftsmen use birchwood to make wooden spoons, bowls, and other decorative items.
  • Russian Nesting Dolls: These traditional Russian dolls are popular souvenirs and come in a range of sizes and styles.

Local Culture and Customs of Tomsk

Tomsk has a rich cultural heritage and traditions, including:

  • Music and Dance: Siberian folk music and dances are an integral part of the local culture, and visitors can attend traditional performances.
  • Handicrafts: Tomsk is known for its handmade crafts, such as embroidery, pottery, and wooden carvings.
  • Hospitality: Russians are known for their hospitality, and visitors to Tomsk can expect a warm welcome from locals.

Best Time to Visit Tomsk

The best time to visit Tomsk is from June to August, when the weather is mild and pleasant. However, if you want to experience winter in Siberia, visit from December to February, when the city is covered in snow and ice.

Do’s and Don’ts of Tomsk

Do:

  • Learn a few phrases in Russian to communicate with locals.
  • Dress warmly and in layers, especially in the winter.
  • Try traditional Siberian cuisine.

Don’t:

  • Go off the beaten path without a guide.
  • Drink tap water, which is not safe for consumption.
  • Visit without a visa, which is required for most international visitors.

Safety and Health

Safety in Tomsk

Tomsk is a safe city for travelers, but it’s always advisable to take basic safety precautions, such as keeping valuables safe and avoiding isolated areas at night.

Health in Tomsk

Tomsk has several medical facilities and hospitals, but it’s important to have travel insurance and carry any necessary medications, as healthcare in Russia can be expensive for foreigners.

Getting to Tomsk: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Tomsk

Tomsk has its own airport, which is located about 20 kilometers from the city center.

Bus Stations Near Tomsk

Tomsk has several bus stations, including Tomsk Bus Station, Tomsk Central Bus Station, and Tomsk Electromechanical Plant Bus Station.

Other Options

There are also train and taxi options for getting to Tomsk from other parts of Russia.

How to Get Around in Tomsk

Tomsk is a small city that is easy to navigate on foot, but there are also buses and trams for getting around. Taxis are also available, but make sure to negotiate the fare upfront.

Where to Go Next from Tomsk: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Tomsk is surrounded by natural beauty and historic sites, including:

  • Novosibirsk: The third-largest city in Russia, located about 4 hours from Tomsk by train.
  • Abakan: The capital of the Republic of Khakassia, known for its historic architecture and museums, located about 5 hours from Tomsk by train.
  • Altai Mountains: A UNESCO World Heritage Site, known for its stunning natural beauty, located about 6 hours from Tomsk by car.

Conclusion

Tomsk is a hidden gem in Western Siberia, offering a unique blend of history, culture, and natural beauty. Whether you visit for business or leisure, make sure to explore the local markets, try traditional Siberian cuisine, and immerse yourself in the local culture. Plan your trip to Tomsk today and experience the best of Siberia.
