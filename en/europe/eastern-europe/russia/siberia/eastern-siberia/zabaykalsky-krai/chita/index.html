Introduction to Chita

Located in Zabaykalsky Krai in Eastern Siberia, Chita is a unique and lesser-known destination for travelers seeking adventure and cultural experiences. The city is situated on the banks of the Chita River and boasts a rich history and diverse cultural scene. From museums and historical sites to stunning natural landscapes and outdoor activities, there is something for everyone in Chita.

Food and Drink Scene of Chita

Must-Try Dishes

  • Buryat Shorpa - a traditional soup made with beef, potatoes, carrots, onions, and spices
  • Pelmeni - Russian-style dumplings filled with meat and served with sour cream or butter
  • Pozy - similar to pelmeni but with a different shape and often containing additional vegetables or spices

Local Beverages

  • Chita Kvas - a traditional Uzbek soft drink made from fermented bread
  • Baikal Balsam - a herbal liqueur made with over 20 different herbs and spices

Local Culture and Customs of Chita

Chita is a melting pot of cultures, with a diverse population including Russians, Buryats, and other ethnic groups. Visitors should be respectful of local customs and traditions, especially when visiting religious sites such as Ivolginski Datsan. It is also important to note that smoking is prohibited in public places in Chita.

Best Time to Visit Chita

The best time to visit Chita is in the summer months from June to August when temperatures are warm and the weather is pleasant for outdoor activities. However, visitors should be prepared for occasional rain showers. Winter months can be quite cold and snowy, but offer unique winter sports and holiday celebrations.

Do’s and Don’ts of Chita

Do:

  • Try traditional Russian and Buryat dishes
  • Visit local museums and historical sites
  • Take advantage of outdoor activities and scenic views

Don’t:

  • Smoke in public places
  • Be disrespectful of local customs and traditions
  • Leave trash or litter in natural areas

Safety and Health

Safety in Chita

Chita is generally a safe city for travelers, but visitors should take usual safety precautions such as carrying a form of identification, avoiding wearing flashy jewelry or expensive items, and taking care when traveling alone at night. It is also important to be aware of the risks of wildlife encounters when exploring natural areas.

Health in Chita

Visitors to Chita should ensure they have adequate travel insurance and consider bringing a basic first aid kit. It is important to drink only bottled water and to be aware of the risks of altitude sickness when visiting higher elevations in Zabaykalsky Krai.

How to Get Around in Chita

Public transportation in Chita consists of buses and taxis, with regular service available throughout the city. It is also possible to rent a car or motorcycle for exploring the surrounding areas.
