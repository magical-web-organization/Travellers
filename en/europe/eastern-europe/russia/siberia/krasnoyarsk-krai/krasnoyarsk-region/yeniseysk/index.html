Introduction to Yeniseysk

Welcome to Yeniseysk, a hidden gem in the vast and beautiful Siberian region of Russia. Located in Krasnoyarsk Krai, Yeniseysk is a small town that packs a punch with its rich history, vibrant culture, and stunning natural beauty. Founded in 1619, Yeniseysk served as a strategic center for Russia’s fur trade, and its well-preserved architecture and museums reflect its heritage. Here’s a comprehensive guide to help you plan your trip to this unique destination:

List of Places to Visit in Yeniseysk

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Yeniseysk Museum of Local Lore: This museum houses over 50,000 exhibits that tell the story of Yeniseysk and its people, from its prehistoric era to contemporary times. The museum also offers guided tours that take you through its collection of rare artifacts.
  • Yeniseysk Historical and Architectural Museum-Reserve: This open-air museum is a collection of historical buildings and structures that reflect the town’s heritage. The complex includes a museum of wooden architecture, an old observatory, and several churches.
  • Ivan Susanin Museum: Located in the village of Berestye, just outside Yeniseysk, this museum is a tribute to the legendary hero, Ivan Susanin. Known for his bravery in leading the Polish troops astray during the invasion of Russia, Susanin is a national symbol of courage and sacrifice.

Natural Attractions of Yeniseysk: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Yenisey River: The Yenisey River, the fifth-longest river in the world, flows through Yeniseysk and provides a stunning backdrop for outdoor activities like fishing, boating, and camping.
  • Stolby Nature Reserve: Located just outside Krasnoyarsk, this nature reserve features unique rock formations that are popular with climbers and hikers. The reserve also has beautiful views of the Yenisey River and its surrounding forests.
  • Khakassia National Reserve: This reserve is located about 200 km from Yeniseysk and has a unique landscape of sand dunes, mountains, and lakes. It’s home to a diverse range of flora and fauna, including the endangered snow leopard.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Yeniseysk

  • Yeniseysk-Krasnoyarsk Hiking Trail: This 496 km trail starts in Yeniseysk and ends in Krasnoyarsk, passing through forests, mountains, and rivers along the way.
  • Yeniseysk-Baikal Biking Trail: This 1200 km trail takes you from Yeniseysk to Lake Baikal, the deepest freshwater lake in the world. The trail follows the Angara River and provides a unique opportunity to explore the rugged Siberian countryside.

Best Things to Do in Yeniseysk: Activities and Tours

  • Yeniseysk River Cruise: Take a leisurely cruise down the Yenisey River and enjoy the stunning scenery of the Siberian countryside.
  • Horseback Riding: Explore the nearby forests and steppe on horseback with a guided tour.
  • Ice Fishing: Head to the frozen Yenisey River during the winter months and try your hand at ice fishing.

Week-Long Itinerary for Yeniseysk

Day 1-2: Explore the town of Yeniseysk, visit the museums and historical sites. Day 3: Take a day trip to the Ivan Susanin Museum in Berestye. Day 4-5: Experience the natural beauty of the Stolby Nature Reserve. Day 6-7: Go on a Yenisey River cruise and enjoy outdoor activities like fishing or hiking.

Food and Drink Scene of Yeniseysk

Must-Try Dishes

  • Biryuza: This traditional Siberian soup is made with beef, potatoes, carrots, onion, and sour cream.
  • Pelmeni: These dumplings are filled with meat, mushrooms, or cheese and served with sour cream.
  • Oladushki: These pancakes are made from curdled milk and served with jam or honey.

Local Beverages

  • Vodka: A popular Russian liquor that’s a must-try while in the country.
  • Kvas: A sweet and fizzy fermented drink made from rye bread.

Where to Stay in Yeniseysk: Hotels, Hostels and Guesthouses

  • Hotel Yeniseyskaya: This hotel is located in the center of Yeniseysk and offers comfortable rooms and amenities.
  • Guest House Na Kolkhoznoi: This guesthouse is located on the outskirts of town and provides a quiet and peaceful accommodation option.

What to Buy in Yeniseysk: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Fur Products: Yeniseysk is known for its fur trade, so you can find a range of fur products, like hats, gloves, and coats.
  • Matryoshka Dolls: These traditional Russian dolls are a popular souvenir item.
  • Siberian Specialities: Local products like wild honey, smoked fish, and Ryazhenka, a fermented milk drink, make for unique and delicious souvenirs.

Local Culture and Customs of Yeniseysk

  • Religion: The majority of people in Yeniseysk are Russian Orthodox Christians.
  • Language: Russian is the official language of Yeniseysk.
  • Etiquette: When visiting someone’s home, it’s customary to bring a small gift and remove your shoes before entering the house.

Best Time to Visit Yeniseysk

The best time to visit Yeniseysk is during the summer months, from June to August. The temperatures are moderate, and outdoor activities, like hiking and river cruises, are in full swing.

Do’s and Don’ts of Yeniseysk

Do:

  • Respect local customs and traditions.
  • Take the time to learn some basic Russian phrases.
  • Bring comfortable shoes for outdoor activities.

Don’t:

  • Drink alcohol in public places.
  • Wear shoes inside someone’s home.
  • Be disrespectful to religious sites and practices.

Safety and Health

Safety in Yeniseysk

Yeniseysk is a relatively safe place to visit; however, be aware of petty crime, like pickpocketing, in crowded areas.

Health in Yeniseysk

Check with your doctor before traveling to Yeniseysk to make sure your vaccinations are up to date. The tap water is safe to drink, but bottled water is readily available.

Getting to Yeniseysk: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Yeniseysk

The closest international airport to Yeniseysk is Krasnoyarsk International Airport, which is about 280 km away.

Bus Stations Near Yeniseysk

Yeniseysk has a small bus station in the town center that serves regional destinations.

Other Options

If you’re feeling adventurous, you can take the Trans-Siberian Railway to Krasnoyarsk and then take a bus or taxi to Yeniseysk.

How to Get Around in Yeniseysk

Yeniseysk is a small town that can easily be explored on foot or by bike. Taxis are also available for those who prefer not to walk or bike.

Where to Go Next from Yeniseysk: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Krasnoyarsk: The capital city of Krasnoyarsk Krai, Krasnoyarsk is a bustling city that’s known for its museums, theaters, and nightlife.
  • Khakassia National Reserve: This reserve is rich in history and offers a unique landscape of sand dunes, mountains, and lakes.

Conclusion

Yeniseysk is a Siberian town that’s teeming with culture, natural beauty, and outdoor adventures. Its well-preserved architecture, museums, and historical landmarks showcase its rich history, while its scenic views of the Yenisey River and surrounding forests provide a peaceful escape from the hustle and bustle of city life. We hope this guide helps you plan an unforgettable trip to Yeniseysk.
