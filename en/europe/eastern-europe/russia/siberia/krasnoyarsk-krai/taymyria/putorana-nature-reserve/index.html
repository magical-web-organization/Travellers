Introduction to Putorana Nature Reserve

Putorana Nature Reserve is one of the largest reserves in Russia, located in the Krasnoyarsk Krai region of Siberia. It was declared a UNESCO World Heritage site in 2010 for its unique landscapes, rich biodiversity, and cultural significance. The reserve covers an area of over 1.8 million hectares and is home to stunning geological formations, ancient rock art, and various species of Arctic animals.

List of Places to Visit in Putorana Nature Reserve

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

If you are interested in the cultural history of the region, visit the open-air ethnographic complex in the village of Novy Port. This complex showcases the daily life of the indigenous Evenk people, highlighting their traditional fishing, hunting, and reindeer herding practices. The Putorana Plateau is also home to a large number of ancient rock art sites, including the famous Yalama petroglyphs. These engravings, dating back to the Bronze Age, depict animals, deities, and human activities.

Natural Attractions of Putorana Nature Reserve: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Putorana Nature Reserve boasts some of the most awe-inspiring natural scenery in Russia and offers a range of outdoor recreational activities. Travelers can explore the icy waters of the Putorana Plateau by taking boat or kayak tours through the deep canyons and waterfalls of the Kotui River. For a more adrenaline-inducing experience, visitors can go rafting or canoeing through the rapids. Hiking and biking enthusiasts can trek along the scenic trails, skirting around the icy cliffs and traversing the Arctic tundra. Wildlife watchers can observe the region’s unique fauna, including the Siberian musk deer and the elusive snow leopard.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Putorana Nature Reserve

There are several hiking and biking routes available in Putorana Nature Reserve, all with varying levels of difficulty. The most popular route is the “Golden Ring,” which is a 200-kilometer trail that encircles Lake Putorana. It offers hikers and bikers the opportunity to observe a host of natural wonders along the way, including waterfalls and unique rock formations. Another popular hike is to the Kamen Pillars, a series of striking rock formations that resemble towering pillars.

Best Things to Do in Putorana Nature Reserve: Activities and Tours

Besides hiking and biking, there are several other activities and tours available in Putorana Nature Reserve. Travelers can take a helicopter tour to witness the incredible aerial view of the reserve. They can also participate in horseback riding tours, fishing trips, and dog sledding excursions. A cultural immersion tour is also available, which allows visitors to learn about the culture and daily life of the local Evenk people.

Week-Long Itinerary for Putorana Nature Reserve

Day 1-2: Explore the Yalama petroglyphs and the open-air ethnographic complex in Novy Port.

Day 3-4: Take a boat or kayak tour through the Kotui River and explore its deep canyons and waterfalls.

Day 5-6: Go hiking or biking along the scenic trails of the Putorana Plateau and witness the Kamen Pillars.

Day 7: Take a helicopter tour to witness the breathtaking aerial view of the reserve before heading back home.

Food and Drink Scene of Putorana Nature Reserve

Must-Try Dishes

There are several traditional dishes that travelers must try while visiting Putorana Nature Reserve. The pirozhki is a pastry filled with meat, onion, and potatoes and is a favorite snack among locals. Smoked fish is another popular delicacy, often served with potatoes and sour cream. Another traditional dish is the pelmeni, a Russian dumpling filled with meat.

Local Beverages

The most popular local drink is kvass, a refreshing, non-alcoholic drink made from fermented bread that is commonly consumed during the summer months. For those who enjoy a stronger beverage, Russian vodka is also widely available in the area.

Where to Stay in Putorana Nature Reserve: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Accommodations within the reserve are limited and range from guesthouses to camping sites. The town of Norilsk, located 150 kilometers southeast of the reserve, offers more options, including hotels and hostels. In Norilsk, travelers can choose to stay in the Grand Hotel Polyarnaya Zvezda or the Tulip Inn Meridian Hotel. For those who prefer a homier atmosphere, several guesthouses and lodges are available, including the Putorana Guesthouse and the Kniazhe Ozero Lodge.

What to Buy in Putorana Nature Reserve: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Traditional handmade crafts and souvenirs are popular items to buy in Putorana Nature Reserve. Travelers can purchase items like handwoven baskets, traditional clothing, and hand-carved wooden objects as keepsakes. Another popular item is raw amber, which is commonly found in the area and is crafted into jewelry or sold as a souvenir.

Local Culture and Customs of Putorana Nature Reserve

The region is home to a diverse mix of indigenous peoples, including the Evenk, the Dolgan, and the Nganasan. Travelers visiting the Putorana Nature Reserve should be mindful of these cultures and respect their traditions and customs. The Evenk people practice shamanism and believe in the spiritual world; visitors should refrain from disrupting their customs or sacred sites.

Best Time to Visit Putorana Nature Reserve

The best time to visit Putorana Nature Reserve is during the summer months of June through August when the weather is relatively mild and the daylight lasts for nearly 24 hours. The winter months bring sub-zero temperatures, making outdoor activities more challenging.

Do’s and Don’ts of Putorana Nature Reserve

Do:

  • Respect the local culture and customs.
  • Bring warm clothing and gear suitable for the climate.
  • Use a certified tour guide for outdoor adventures.

Don’t:

  • Bring prohibited items like drugs or illegal hunting equipment.
  • Litter or disturb the delicate ecosystem of the region.
  • Travel without a proper permit or documentation.

Safety and Health

Safety in Putorana Nature Reserve

Travelers should take caution when exploring the rugged terrain of Putorana Nature Reserve. They should be aware of the weather conditions, quickly changing conditions, and the potential for avalanches during the winter months. Travelers should also be aware of wildlife in the area, including wolves, foxes, and bears.

Health in Putorana Nature Reserve

Due to the remote nature of Putorana Nature Reserve, visitors should ensure they carry all necessary medications, as the nearest medical facility is in Norilsk. Travelers should also take precautions against ticks by wearing protective clothing and using insect repellent. Drinking water should be boiled or bottled.

Getting to Putorana Nature Reserve: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Putorana Nature Reserve

The closest airport to Putorana Nature Reserve is the Norilsk Alykel Airport, which coincides with domestic arrivals. From there, travelers can take a car or bus to the reserve area.

Bus Stations Near Putorana Nature Reserve

The bus station is located in the town of Norilsk, which provides links to other cities and towns throughout the region.

Other Options

Travelers can also drive to Putorana Nature Reserve by following the federal road P-475, which leads to the town of Talnakh, offering a direct gateway to the reserve.

How to Get Around in Putorana Nature Reserve

The best way to explore Putorana Nature Reserve is by a guided tour, as the terrain can be challenging and requires a certain level of expertise. Tour guides offer excursions such as hiking, canoeing, and dog sledding trips, providing transportation options for getting around.
