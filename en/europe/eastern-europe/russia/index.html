Introduction to Russia

Russia is the world’s largest country, spanning over two continents: Europe and Asia. It is known for its fascinating history, rich culture, and incredible natural beauty. Russia has something for every type of traveler, whether you’re interested in exploring its stunning cities, discovering its cultural heritage, or having an outdoor adventure. In this comprehensive tour guide, we’ll cover everything you need to know to plan your perfect trip to Russia.

List of Places to Visit in Russia

Russia is home to some of the most iconic landmarks and incredible natural wonders. Here are some of the must-visit places in Russia:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • The State Hermitage Museum in St. Petersburg
  • The Kremlin and Red Square in Moscow
  • Catherine Palace and Park in Pushkin
  • The Peterhof Palace and Garden in Peterhof

Natural Attractions of Russia: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Baikal Lake in Siberia
  • Mount Elbrus in the Caucasus
  • Valley of Geysers in Kamchatka
  • The Golden Ring, a group of ancient towns northeast of Moscow

Hiking/Biking Routes of Russia

Russia is a country with vast landscapes and incredible natural beauty. Whether you enjoy hiking or prefer biking, Russia has something for everyone. Some of the best hiking/biking routes in Russia are:

  • The Trans-Siberian Railway from Moscow to Vladivostok
  • The Altai Mountains in Siberia
  • The Khibiny Mountains in the Arctic
  • The Kamchatka Peninsula

Best Things to Do in Russia: Activities and Tours

Russia is a country with something for every type of traveler. The following are some of the best things to do in Russia:

  • Watch a ballet at the Bolshoi Theatre
  • Visit the world-famous Hermitage Museum in St. Petersburg
  • Attend a football match at one of Russia’s many stadiums
  • Take a boat tour of the canals in St. Petersburg

Week-Long Itinerary for Russia

Here is a suggested itinerary for a week-long trip to Russia:

Day Activity
Day 1 Explore Moscow: Visit Red Square, Kremlin, and St. Basil’s Cathedral
Day 2 Discover the Treasures of the Hermitage Museum in St. Petersburg
Day 3 Travel to Pushkin and visit Catherine Palace and Park
Day 4 Take the high-speed train to Nizhny Novgorod and sightsee the Kremlin and the historic center
Day 5 Go on a nature adventure at Kamchatka Peninsula
Day 6 Enjoy the views of Taiga forest and Lake Baikal and explore Irkutsk
Day 7 Return to Moscow and visit the famous Kizhi Open-air Museum

Food and Drink Scene of Russia

Russian cuisine is hearty, flavorful, and comforting. Traditional Russian dishes often use simple ingredients like potatoes, cabbage, and meat. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages in Russia:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Borscht, a hearty soup made with beets, cabbage, and meat
  • Pirozhki, small, savory pies filled with meat, potatoes, or vegetables
  • Pelmeni, dumplings filled with meat
  • Olivier salad, a traditional Russian salad made with potatoes, carrots, pickles, and mayonnaise

Local Beverages

  • Russian Vodka
  • Kvass, a non-alcoholic drink made from fermented rye bread
  • Black tea with lemon and jam
  • Mors, a traditional berry drink

What to Buy in Russia: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Russia has a rich history and culture, and there are many locally made products that you can bring home as souvenirs. Some of the best things to buy in Russia include:

  • Matryoshka dolls
  • Amber jewelry
  • Russian shawls
  • Vodka

Local Culture and Customs of Russia

Russia has a unique culture that reflects its rich history and diversity. To have a better cultural experience, keep the following things in mind:

  • It is customary to take off shoes before entering someone’s home or a sacred place
  • Russians greet each other with a firm handshake
  • Do not talk loudly in public places
  • Dress modestly, particularly when visiting religious sites

Best Time to Visit Russia

The best time to visit Russia depends on the location and your preference for weather. Generally, the best time to visit Russia is in the summer months of June through August.

Do’s and Don’ts of Russia

The following are some general do’s and don’ts to keep in mind while visiting Russia:

Do:

  • Explore and appreciate the rich history and culture of Russia
  • Dress modestly
  • Learn a few Russian phrases
  • Try local food and drinks

Don’t:

  • Do not show disrespect to the Russian flag, anthem, or symbols
  • Do not talk about politics
  • Do not photograph sensitive buildings, such as military installations

Safety and Health

Although Russia is a relatively safe country, here are some things to keep in mind for your safety and health:

Safety in Russia

  • Be aware of pickpocketing and theft in crowded areas
  • Avoid sketchy neighborhoods and streets
  • Be cautious when taking taxis, use a trusted service like Uber

Health in Russia

  • To visit Russia, some vaccines are mandatory, such as Hepatitis A and B
  • Drink bottled water, avoid drinking tap water
  • Wear sunscreen to avoid sunburn
