A Travel Guide to Bakhchysarai, Crimea

Bakhchysarai is a small town situated in the Crimean peninsula of Southern Russia. It is a historically significant town with unique cultural and natural attractions that make it a must-visit destination in Crimea. This travel guide aims to provide you with all the information you need to have an enjoyable visit to Bakhchysarai.

Introduction to Bakhchysarai

Bakhchysarai was the capital of the Crimean Khanate, a Turkic state that existed from the mid-15th to the late 18th century. The town has retained its oriental atmosphere with its cobbled streets, ancient mosques, and mausoleums. It is now a UNESCO World Heritage Site. The town has a population of about 35,000 and is in an area of natural beauty, with mountains and forests surrounding the town. The best time to visit Bakhchysarai is from May to September, when the weather is mild and suitable for outdoor activities.

List of Places to Visit in Bakhchysarai

Bakhchysarai has a rich historical and cultural heritage, making it a fascinating destination for history buffs and culture lovers. The town boasts numerous museums and historical places, gardens, and natural landmarks. Here is a list of must-visit places in Bakhchysarai.

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Khan Palace - The palace was built in the 16th century and was the residence of the Crimean Khanate’s ruling family. The palace has a mix of oriental and European architecture, with beautiful gardens and fountains.
  • Chufut-Kale Cave City - A cave city that dates back to the 5th century, which was inhabited by Jews, Greeks, and Christians. The city includes houses, tombs, and a synagogue.
  • Uspensky Orthodox Monastery - The monastery was founded in the 8th century and is one of the oldest in Crimea. It has beautiful architecture and frescoes.
  • Historical and Cultural Reserve of Bakhchysarai - A large complex of historical and archaeological sites, including the Khan’s Palace, the Uspensky Monastery, and the Chufut-Kale Cave City.

Natural Attractions of Bakhchysarai: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Crimean Mountains - The town is surrounded by the Crimean Mountains, which offer excellent hiking, climbing, and mountain biking opportunities.
  • Grand Canyon of Crimea - A natural wonder that is worth a visit. It is a site of great scenic beauty where you can go hiking, rock climbing, and sightseeing.
  • Marble Caves - A wondrous cave system with beautiful colors and clear water streams.

Hiking/Biking Routes in Bakhchysarai

Bakhchysarai is well known for its beautiful hiking, biking and climbing routes through the Crimean Mountains, convenient for both beginners and experienced adventurers. Here are some of the most popular paths to try:

  • Chufut Yayla - A hike through hilly terrain with a breathtaking view of the city and its surroundings.
  • Yalta- Bakhchysarai bike route - A bike ride starting from Yalta, going to Bakhchysarai, and ending at Simferopol. The route is about 30 kilometers.
  • Crimean Peaks trail - A 120-kilometer trek from Bakhchysarai to the top of Tserkovka Peak by way of other peaks and summits.

Best Things to Do in Bakhchysarai: Activities and Tours

There are many enjoyable activities and tours available in Bakhchysarai:

  • Walking tour of Bakhchysarai - A great way to explore the town’s various sites and learn about its history and culture.
  • Tea tasting experience - Crimean tea is known for its high quality and delicious flavor. You can take a guided tea tour near Bakhchysarai to learn about tea production and taste different types of tea.
  • Cycling tour - Explore the town on a cycling tour by hiring a bike and exploring the beautiful surroundings.
  • Horse Riding - Go horse riding in the forested mountains or rural landscapes surrounding Bakhchysarai.

Food and Drink Scene in Bakhchysarai

Bakhchysarai has a rich culinary heritage with traditional Tartar and Crimean cuisine available. The Crimean Tatar restaurants are one of the best places to taste local cuisine, and here are some must-try dishes:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Pilaf - A rice-based dish with meat and vegetables flavored with spices and herbs served with salads or pickles
  • Cheburek - A fried pastry stuffed with minced meat, potatoes, and onions. It is a popular street snack in Bakhchysarai.
  • Samsa - A pastry filled with spiced meat and onions.

Local Beverages

  • Kvass - A non-alcoholic drink made from bread and flavored with fruits that come in different flavors
  • Crimean wine - Crimea is a wine-growing region producing some great wines.

Where to Stay in Bakhchysarai: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Bakhchysarai has a variety of places to stay for different budgets, including budget-friendly hostels, guesthouses, and luxury hotels. Here are some great options:

  • Ukraina Hotel - A 3-star hotel centrally located in Bakhchysarai. It has free Wi-Fi, air conditioning, and a restaurant.
  • Roshchinsky Krym Hotel - A comfortable hotel situated in a beautiful area with a swimming pool, restaurant, and free Wi-Fi.
  • Guest House MirBerg - A guesthouse with a great host and located near the town center.

What to Buy in Bakhchysarai: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Bakhchysarai has a variety of souvenirs and local products that make great gifts to bring home. Here are some ideas:

  • Zirka Kryma wine - Crimean wine is known for its great taste and makes a lovely gift.
  • Tatar handicrafts - Buy hand-made Tatar embroidery products, such as tablecloths, scarfs, or cushion covers from local markets or workshops.
  • Jewelry - The town has some lovely jewelry shops where you can find unique and affordable traditional pieces.

Local Culture and Customs of Bakhchysarai

Bakhchysarai has a unique cultural heritage, and there are some customs and traditions that travelers should be aware of:

  • Dress modestly when visiting mosques or other culturally significant sites.
  • Remove your shoes when entering mosques or religious places.
  • Respect the local cuisine and local Tatar traditions.

Best Time to Visit Bakhchysarai

May to September is the best time to visit Bakhchysarai, when the weather is mild and suitable for outdoor activities and sightseeing. The town can get crowded during this time of year, so plan accordingly.

Do’s and Don’ts of Bakhchysarai

Do:

  • Respect local customs and traditions.
  • Visit the Khan’s Palace and other historical and cultural monuments.
  • Try the local cuisine.

Don’t:

  • Wear revealing clothing when visiting cultural sites.
  • Take pictures of people without their permission.
  • Speak loudly in public areas.

Safety and Health

Safety in Bakhchysarai

Bakhchysarai is generally a safe town, and tourists have little to worry about. However, it’s best to be cautious and take general safety measures when traveling to any foreign destination.

Health in Bakhchysarai

The health care system in Ukraine is not of the highest standard, so travelers should take appropriate health and medical precautions when traveling to Bakhchysarai. It’s important to have travel insurance and to carry any necessary medication.

Getting to Bakhchysarai: Transportation Options and Logistics

Bakhchysarai is accessible by various transportation options. Here are some options to consider:

Airports Near Bakhchysarai

  • Simferopol International Airport is the nearest airport, located 60km away from Bakhchysarai.

Bus Stations Near Bakhchysarai

  • Simferopol Bus Station and Sevastopol Bus Station are the most significant bus stations nearby, where you can catch regional and intercity buses.

Other Options

  • Taxis are available but are relatively expensive.
  • Car rentals are available at Simferopol Airport and the city center.

How to Get Around in Bakhchysarai

The town is small enough to explore by foot, but you can also rent a bike or take a taxi or bus to get around.

Where to Go Next from Bakhchysarai: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Bakhchysarai is in the middle of a beautiful natural reserve, and there are many nearby destinations and excursions worth exploring, such as:

  • Yalta - A beautiful coastal city with old-world charm and scenic views.
  • Sevastopol - A historical port city with many attractions related to the Crimean War.
  • Sudak - A town known for its beautiful beaches and natural landscapes.

Conclusion

Bakhchysarai is an excellent destination for culture, history, and nature lovers. The town has a lot to offer, from its historic sites to its mountain scenery. Visitors will enjoy trying its local food, exploring its unique culture and relaxing in its beautiful surroundings. This comprehensive travel guide aims to help you plan your visit to Bakhchysarai and make the most of your time in this charming town.
