Introduction to Kerch

Kerch is a city in southern Russia and is the largest city in the Crimean Peninsula. It is a popular tourist destination for its rich history, beautiful natural attractions, and cultural significance.

Kerch has been inhabited since ancient times and has been an important center of trade and culture throughout history. It is a melting pot of cultures, where ancient Greek, Roman, Byzantine, and Ottoman influences can be seen.

In this guide, you will discover the best places to visit, stay, eat, and explore in Kerch. So get ready to immerse yourself in the rich cultural heritage and amazing natural wonders that Kerch has to offer.

List of Places to Visit in Kerch

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Kerch Historical and Cultural Museum

The Kerch Historical and Cultural Museum is an excellent place to start your cultural journey in Kerch. It features a collection of artifacts from ancient Greece, Rome, and the Golden Horde period. You will find an extensive collection of ancient Greek ceramics, Scythian gold, and ancient Egyptian mummies.

Adzhimushkay Quarry

During World War II, the Soviet army used Adzhimushkay Quarry as a secret underground factory to produce weapons. The location was chosen because it was difficult for German bombers to detect. Today, it is a museum where visitors can see the tunnels and learn about the bravery and heroism of Soviet soldiers during the war.

Dolphinarium Nemo

Dolphinarium Nemo is a fun place to visit for families. It is a marine life park that offers dolphin and sea lion shows, interactive programs, and educational exhibits about marine life.

Natural Attractions of Kerch: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

White Cliffs of Kara Dag

The White Cliffs of Kara Dag is a natural wonder of Kerch and is a must-visit for any nature lover. Located near the village of Kurortnoye, the cliffs are made of limestone and are white in color due to the high concentration of chalk. Visitors can hike along the trails for stunning views of the sea and the cliffs.

Mount Mithridates

Mount Mithridates is a 91-meter high mountain in the center of Kerch and is named after the ancient king of Pontus, Mithridates VI. It offers spectacular panoramic views of the city and the sea. There are several hiking trails that lead to the summit, and visitors can walk up or take the cable car to the top.

Kara-Tobe

Kara-Tobe is an ancient hilltop fortress that was built by the Byzantines in the 6th century. Today, it is a green park with walking paths and a playground for children. Visitors can climb to the top of the hill for a bird’s-eye view of the city and the sea.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Kerch

Dolphinarium Nemo to Mount Mithridates

This hike starts at Dolphinarium Nemo and follows the path through the city to Mount Mithridates. The trail is about 4 kilometers long and takes about 2 hours to complete. It offers stunning views of the city and the sea and is suitable for all skill levels.

Kara Dag Nature Reserve

Kara Dag Nature Reserve is a protected area in Kerch and is a great place for hiking and biking. There are several trails that range from easy to difficult, and visitors can explore the diverse flora and fauna of the region.

Best Things to Do in Kerch: Activities and Tours

Boat Tour of the Kerch Strait

A boat tour of the Kerch Strait is an excellent way to see the beautiful coastline of Kerch. Visitors can enjoy stunning views of the sea and the surrounding mountains while learning about the history and culture of the region.

Scuba Diving in the Black Sea

Scuba diving in the Black Sea is a unique experience that can’t be missed. The sea is home to a diverse marine life, including dolphins, seals, and sea turtles. There are several dive operators in Kerch that offer scuba diving tours for all skill levels.

Food and Drink Scene of Kerch

Must-Try Dishes

Borscht

Borscht is a traditional soup made with beets, cabbage, potatoes, and carrots. It is often served hot with a dollop of sour cream and a slice of rye bread.

Pelmeni

Pelmeni is a type of dumpling that is typically filled with meat and served with sour cream or butter.

Shashlik

Shashlik is a meat skewer that is grilled over an open flame. It is typically made with lamb or pork and is served with a side of vegetables and bread.

Local Beverages

Kvas

Kvas is a traditional fermented drink made from bread and water. It has a slightly sweet and tangy taste and is served cold.

Medovukha

Medovukha is a traditional Russian honey-based alcoholic drink. It is typically sweet and has a smooth, syrupy texture.

Where to Stay in Kerch: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Artek Hotel

Artek Hotel is a modern hotel located in the city center of Kerch. It offers comfortable rooms, a restaurant, and a spa.

Guest House Konsulat

Guest House Konsulat is a cozy guesthouse located in the heart of the old town of Kerch. It offers comfortable rooms with a traditional decor and a courtyard garden.

Hostel No. One

Hostel No. One is a budget-friendly hostel that offers dormitory-style rooms and private rooms. It is located near the city center and offers free Wi-Fi and a common kitchen.

What to Buy in Kerch: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Handmade Ceramics

Kerch is famous for its handmade ceramics that have been produced for centuries. Visitors can find beautiful plates, bowls, and vases that make excellent souvenirs or gifts.

Crimean Wine

Crimea is known for its excellent wine, and visitors can find a wide variety of local wines in Kerch. Some popular types include red wines made from the Saperavi grape and white wines made from the Rkatsiteli grape.

Local Culture and Customs of Kerch

Kerch is a multicultural city that has been influenced by many civilizations throughout history. Visitors should be respectful of the local customs and traditions and be open to experiencing different cultures.

Best Time to Visit Kerch

The best time to visit Kerch is during the summer months from June to September when the weather is warm and sunny. However, the city can be crowded during this time, so visitors should book accommodations in advance.

Do’s and Don’ts of Kerch

Do:

  • Dress appropriately when visiting religious sites or other cultural attractions.
  • Try the local cuisine and beverages.
  • Be respectful of the local culture and customs.

Don’t:

  • Photograph military installations or personnel.
  • Engage in political discussions or activities.
  • Disrespect religious or cultural symbols or customs.

Safety and Health

Safety in Kerch

Kerch is generally a safe city for visitors, but visitors should exercise caution when walking alone at night and avoid areas with low lighting.

Health in Kerch

Visitors should drink bottled water and avoid tap water. The health care system in Kerch is adequate, but visitors should have travel insurance that covers medical expenses.

Getting to Kerch: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Kerch

Simferopol International Airport is the closest airport to Kerch, and visitors can take a bus or taxi from the airport to the city.

Bus Stations Near Kerch

Kerch has a bus station that connects to many major cities in Crimea and Russia.

Other Options

Visitors can also travel to Kerch by train or ferry from other cities in Crimea or Russia.

How to Get Around in Kerch

Visitors can get around Kerch by using public transportation such as buses or taxis. Walking and biking are also popular options, especially for shorter distances.

Where to Go Next from Kerch: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Sevastopol

Sevastopol is a city in Crimea with a rich maritime history. It is home to several museums, historical attractions, and beaches.

Yalta

Yalta is a resort town on the Crimean Peninsula that is famous for its stunning views of the Black Sea and its historic palaces and gardens.

Conclusion

Kerch is a unique travel destination that offers a rich history, beautiful natural wonders, and a vibrant local culture. Whether you are a history buff, a nature lover, or a foodie, there is something for everyone in Kerch. Follow this comprehensive travel guide for an unforgettable trip to this amazing city.
