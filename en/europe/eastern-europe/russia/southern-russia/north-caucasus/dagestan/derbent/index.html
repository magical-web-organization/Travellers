Introduction to Derbent

Located in Dagestan, in North Caucasus, in Southern Russia, Derbent is a city steeped in history and culture. The city was founded in the 8th century and has seen many rulers over the centuries, including Arabs, Mongols, and Persians. Derbent is a UNESCO World Heritage site, known for its ancient fortresses, citadels, and mosques.

List of Places to Visit in Derbent

During your visit to Derbent, make sure to include these must-see attractions in your itinerary:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  1. Naryn-Kala Fortress - one of the oldest sites in Derbent, dating back to the 6th century
  2. The Citadel - an ancient fortress guarding the city’s southern entrance
  3. The Old City - an ancient city with ancient buildings and a labyrinth of narrow streets
  4. Juma Mosque - the oldest mosque in Russia, dating back to the 8th century
  5. Shakhristan Fortress - guarding the western outskirts of the city

Natural Attractions of Derbent: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  1. The Caspian Sea - enjoy a stroll along the shore or relax on one of the beaches
  2. Dagestan Mountains - take a hike or bike through the beautiful mountain ranges
  3. The Samur Forest - a dense forest with a river running through it, perfect for hiking and camping.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Derbent

Derbent has some of the most beautiful hiking and biking routes in Russia. Here are a few of our favorites:

  1. Kyrkhlyar - a hike to the ancient ruins of the Kyrkhlyar fortress
  2. Inzer-Khun - a scenic biking trail passing through the Derbent mountains
  3. Gunib Plateau - hike to one of the highest points in Dagestan

Best Things to Do in Derbent: Activities and Tours

  1. Derbent Walking Tour - explore the city’s ancient streets and learn about its rich history
  2. Russian Bathhouse Experience - unwind and relax in a traditional Russian bathhouse
  3. Dagestani Folklore Show - experience the traditional dance and music performances
  4. Derbent Food Tour - sample traditional Dagestani cuisine and learn about the local food culture

Week-Long Itinerary for Derbent

Day 1: Arrive in Derbent, explore the Old City and Juma Mosque Day 2: Visit Naryn-Kala Fortress and the Citadel Day 3: Hike to Kyrkhlyar fortress, enjoy a picnic in the mountains Day 4: Spend the day relaxing on the Caspian Sea beach Day 5: Explore the Gunib Plateau and take in the stunning views
Day 6: Enjoy a Russian bathhouse experience and try Dagestani food Day 7: Take a day trip to the nearby city of Makhachkala

Food and Drink Scene of Derbent

Dagestan is known for its rich cuisine, a blend of Russian, Caucasian, and Central Asian flavors. Here are some of the must-try dishes and drinks in Derbent:

Must-Try Dishes

  1. Khinkal - boiled dumplings filled with meat and served with a spicy sauce
  2. Shashlik - marinated meat skewers grilled over an open flame
  3. Piti - a hearty lamb soup with vegetables and spices
  4. Dagestani Kebab - seasoned minced meat grilled and served with fresh herbs

Local Beverages

  1. Chai - traditional Russian tea served with sugar and jam
  2. Kumis - a fermented drink made of mare’s milk
  3. Nardaran Sharbat - a sweet drink made of honey and spices

Local Culture and Customs of Derbent

Dagestan has a rich cultural heritage, and it’s important to respect local customs and traditions. Here are a few things to keep in mind during your visit:

  • Dress modestly when visiting mosques or other religious sites
  • Greet people with the traditional “salam aleikum”
  • Remove your shoes before entering a private home
  • Try to learn a few Russian or Dagestani phrases to communicate with locals

Best Time to Visit Derbent

The best time to visit Derbent is from May to September when the weather is mild, and outdoor activities are enjoyable. The peak tourist season is in July and August, so plan accordingly.

Do’s and Don’ts of Derbent

Do:

  • Try the local food and drinks
  • Learn about the local culture and customs
  • Respect religious sites and traditions
  • Ask permission before taking photos of people

Don’t:

  • Wear revealing clothing when visiting mosques or religious sites
  • Disrespect local customs or traditions
  • Take photos of people without their permission

Safety and Health

Safety in Derbent

Dagestan has a bit of a reputation for unrest, but Derbent is a safe city for travelers. Keep an eye on your belongings and avoid walking alone at night.

Health in Derbent

Make sure to drink bottled water and avoid street food to prevent stomach issues. Mosquitoes can be prevalent in the summer, so bring bug spray and take precautionary measures to avoid insect bites.

Getting to Derbent: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Derbent

The nearest airport to Derbent is Uytash Airport in Makhachkala, approximately 100 km away.

Bus Stations Near Derbent

The main bus station in Derbent is the Derbent Bus Station, which offers connections to nearby cities.

Other Options

A popular way to get to Derbent is by taking a train to nearby cities like Makhachkala or Baku, Azerbaijan, and then taking a bus or taxi to Derbent.

How to Get Around in Derbent

The best way to get around Derbent is by taxi or bus. Taxis are affordable and widely available. Buses connect the main tourist attractions in the city.

Where to Go Next from Derbent: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

If you have extra time, visit these nearby destinations and excursions:

  1. Makhachkala - the capital city of Dagestan, located approximately 130 km away
  2. Sulak Canyon - a stunning natural wonder with breathtaking views
  3. Quba, Azerbaijan - a quaint town known for its mountain scenery and traditional crafts

Conclusion

Derbent is a hidden gem in Southern Russia that offers a unique blend of history, culture, and natural beauty.
