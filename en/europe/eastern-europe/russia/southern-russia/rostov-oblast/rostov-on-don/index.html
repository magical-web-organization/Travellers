Introduction to Rostov-on-Don

Located in the southern part of Russia, Rostov-on-Don is a vibrant city that is known for its proud Russian history, rich cultural heritage, and scenic natural surroundings. The city is named after the Don River, which runs through it and is an important hub for transportation, industry, and trade. This bustling city has a lot to offer to travelers, including historic monuments, outdoor adventures, delicious cuisine, vibrant nightlife, and warm hospitality.

List of Places to Visit in Rostov-on-Don

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Rostov Regional Museum of Fine Arts: This museum is one of the oldest and most important cultural institutions in Rostov-on-Don. It features a vast collection of Russian and European art, including paintings, sculptures, and decorative arts from the 16th to the 20th century.

  • Rostov Regional Museum of Local Lore: If you want to learn more about the history and traditions of the people of Rostov-on-Don, this museum is a must-visit. It features exhibits on archaeology, ethnography, and history of the region.

  • Rostov State Museum of the History of Rostov-on-Don: This museum houses numerous artifacts and exhibits that showcase the rich history of the region, from the early times to the present.

  • Rostov State Musical Theater: This impressive theater is considered one of the best in Russia, with a diverse repertoire of performances that includes opera, ballet, drama, and musicals.

Natural Attractions of Rostov-on-Don: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Don River Embankment: A walk along the Don River Embankment offers beautiful views of the river and the city skyline. You can also find various restaurants, cafes, and entertainment venues along the way.

  • Central Park: This green space in the heart of the city offers a peaceful respite from the hustle and bustle of city life. The park features walking and biking trails, a pond, playgrounds, and picnic areas.

  • Botanical Garden of Rostov State University: This botanical garden is home to thousands of plant species, including rare and exotic plants from all over the world. It’s a great place to relax and enjoy some fresh air and nature.

  • Lake Manych-Gudilo: Located just outside the city, this beautiful lake is a popular destination for fishing, swimming, and boating.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Rostov-on-Don

Rostov-on-Don offers many great hiking and biking trails for outdoor enthusiasts. Some of the most popular routes include:

  • Don River Nature Trail: This route runs along the Don River and offers scenic views of the river and city. It’s a great spot for hiking, biking, and birdwatching.

  • Rostov Ring Road: This circular route around the city is perfect for a leisurely bike ride. It passes through residential areas, beautiful parks, and historic landmarks.

Best Things to Do in Rostov-on-Don: Activities and Tours

  • City Sightseeing Tour: Taking a guided tour of the city is a great way to see all the top attractions and landmarks in Rostov-on-Don. Many tours also include visits to nearby villages, natural landmarks, and historic sites.

  • Food and Wine Tour: Rostov-on-Don is known for its delicious cuisine and fine wine. Taking a food and wine tour is a great way to sample some of the best local dishes, drinks, and delicacies.

  • River Cruise: A scenic cruise down the Don River is a great way to enjoy the beautiful views of the city and the surrounding natural scenery.

  • Shopping: Rostov-on-Don has many shopping centers and markets where you can find unique souvenirs, local crafts, and fashion items.

Week-Long Itinerary for Rostov-on-Don

Day 1: Arrive in Rostov-on-Don and explore the city center. Day 2: Visit the Rostov Regional Museum of Fine Arts, Central Park, and the Don River Embankment. Day 3: Visit the Rostov Regional Museum of Local Lore, the Rostov State Musical Theater, and take a scenic river cruise. Day 4: Explore the Botanical Garden of Rostov State University and take a hiking or biking trail. Day 5: Visit the Rostov State Museum of the History of Rostov-on-Don, Lake Manych-Gudilo, and nearby villages. Day 6: Take a food and wine tour and go shopping for souvenirs and local products. Day 7: Relax and enjoy the city at your own pace before departing Rostov-on-Don.

Food and Drink Scene of Rostov-on-Don

Must-Try Dishes

  • Shashlik: A Russian version of barbecue, shashlik is a popular dish made with marinated and grilled meats, often served with vegetables and bread.

  • Borscht: A hearty soup made with beets, meat, and vegetables, borscht is a staple of Russian cuisine.

  • Pelmeni: Dumplings filled with meat, usually served with sour cream and herbs.

  • Syrniki: Sweet pancakes made with farmer’s cheese, served with jam or honey.

Local Beverages

  • Kvas: A fermented drink made from bread, sugar, and water. It’s a refreshing and non-alcoholic drink that’s popular in Russia.

  • Vodka: Russia is famous for its vodka, and Rostov-on-Don is no exception. Try some of the local vodka brands and flavors.

  • Wine: Rostov-on-Don is home to several vineyards and wineries, producing high-quality wines from local grape varieties.

Where to Stay in Rostov-on-Don: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Rostov-on-Don offers plenty of accommodation options for travelers of all budgets and preferences. Some popular choices include:

  • Azimut Hotel Rostov-on-Don: Located in the city center, this hotel offers comfortable rooms and suites, a fitness center, and a restaurant.

  • Guest House Pushkinskaya 10: This charming guesthouse is located in a historic building in the city center and offers cozy rooms, a courtyard, and a shared kitchen.

  • Olimp Hotel: This budget-friendly hotel is located near the airport and offers simple but clean rooms, a restaurant, and free parking.

What to Buy in Rostov-on-Don: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Don Cossack souvenirs: The Cossack heritage is an important part of Rostov-on-Don’s culture, and you can find many souvenirs and handicrafts made by local artisans, such as traditional costumes, knives, and decorative items.

  • Russian candies and chocolates: Russia is famous for its sweets and candies, and Rostov-on-Don has many shops and markets where you can find local varieties.

  • Local wine and vodka: If you enjoy alcoholic beverages, Rostov-on-Don is a great place to buy some local wine or vodka to take home.

Local Culture and Customs of Rostov-on-Don

Rostov-on-Don has a unique culture that is influenced by its history, geography, and people. The city is known for its hospitality, warm demeanor, and traditional values, such as respect for family and community. Russian is the official language, but many people also speak English and other languages. Dress codes are relaxed, but modest clothing is recommended when visiting churches and other religious sites.

Best Time to Visit Rostov-on-Don

The best time to visit Rostov-on-Don is from May to September, when the weather is warm and sunny, and many outdoor activities and events are happening. However, the city can be crowded with tourists during peak season, and prices may be higher than in other seasons. Some travelers prefer to visit Rostov-on-Don in the offseason, from October to April, when the weather is colder but the crowds are thinner, and prices are lower.

Do’s and Don’ts of Rostov-on-Don

Do:

  • Do try some of the local cuisine and drinks, including shashlik, borscht, kvas, and local wine and vodka.

  • Do visit the city’s museums and cultural landmarks to learn more about the history and traditions of Rostov-on-Don.

  • Do dress appropriately when visiting churches and other religious sites.

Don’t:

  • Don’t forget to bring comfortable shoes and clothing, especially if you plan to do some hiking or biking.

  • Don’t offend locals by showing disrespect for their culture, religion, or traditions.

  • Don’t leave your belongings unattended in public places, and be aware of pickpockets and scams.

Safety and Health

Safety in Rostov-on-Don

Rostov-on-Don is generally a safe city, but travelers should take basic precautions to avoid petty crime and scams. Avoid walking alone at night in dark or deserted areas, and keep your valuables in a safe place. If you need emergency assistance, dial 112.

Health in Rostov-on-Don

Rostov-on-Don has several medical facilities that offer high-quality care for both residents and tourists. Travelers should drink only bottled water, and use insect repellent to prevent mosquito bites, especially during the summer months.

Getting to Rostov-on-Don: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Rostov-on-Don

  • Rostov-on-Don Airport: Located about 8 miles from the city center, this airport serves several domestic and international airlines, including Aeroflot, S7 Airlines, and Turkish Airlines.

Bus Stations Near Rostov-on-Don

  • Rostov Bus Station: This is the main bus station in Rostov-on-Don, located in the city center. It serves many destinations within Russia and nearby countries.

Other Options

  • Train: Rostov-on-Don has several train stations that connect the city with other parts of Russia and neighboring countries.

  • Car Rental: Rental cars are available at the airport and in the city center, but be aware that driving in Rostov-on-Don can be challenging due to heavy traffic and narrow roads.

How to Get Around in Rostov-on-Don

Rostov-on-Don has a well-developed public transportation system that includes buses, trams, trolleybuses, and taxis. The city center is also easily walkable, especially for short distances. Biking is also a popular mode of transportation, and many public parks have dedicated bike lanes.

Where to Go Next from Rostov-on-Don: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Azov: Located about 20 miles from Rostov-on-Don, this historic town is known for its medieval fortress and beautiful churches.

  • Taganrog: This coastal town, located about 60 miles from Rostov-on-Don, is known for its scenic waterfront, historic architecture, and cultural landmarks.

  • Don River Delta National Park: This natural park, located about 50 miles from Rostov-on-Don, is a paradise for birdwatchers, with over 300 species of birds and diverse flora and fauna.

Conclusion

Rostov-on-Don is a fascinating destination that offers something for everyone, from history buffs and culture vultures to outdoor enthusiasts and foodies. Whether you’re looking to explore the city’s museums and landmarks, go on a scenic hike or bike ride, or simply relax and enjoy the local cuisine and hospitality, Rostov-on-Don is a great place to visit. By following our travel guide and tips, you can make the most of your trip and experience all the best that this beautiful city has to offer.
