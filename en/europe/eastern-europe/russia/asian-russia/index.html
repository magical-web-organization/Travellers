Introduction to Asian Russia

Located in the eastern part of Europe, Asian Russia is the largest part of Russia, covering one-third of the country’s total area. It is a vast and diverse region with a rich cultural heritage, stunning natural landscapes, and numerous historical landmarks. From the frozen tundra of Siberia to the sprawling metropolis of Moscow, Asian Russia offers something for every traveler.

Local Culture and Customs of Asian Russia

Russia has a unique culture that is based on a long and rich history. Visitors should be aware of the following customs:

  • It’s customary to remove your shoes when entering someone’s home.
  • Be sure to greet people with a firm handshake and make eye contact.
  • Tipping is expected in restaurants and cafes.
  • Dress modestly when visiting churches and religious sites.
  • Always carry your passport with you.

Best Time to Visit Asian Russia

The best time to visit Asian Russia is during the summer months from June to September, when the weather is mild and comfortable. However, if you’re looking to experience the winter wonderland of Siberia, then December to February is the best time to visit.

Do’s and Don’ts of Asian Russia

Here are some of the do’s and don’ts of Asian Russia:

Do:

  • Try to learn a few Russian phrases before your trip.
  • Be punctual when meeting with locals.
  • Dress appropriately for the weather.
  • Be respectful of the local culture and customs.
  • Carry a map with you.

Don’t:

  • Don’t drink tap water.
  • Don’t get into unlicensed taxis.
  • Don’t photograph military installations or government buildings.
  • Don’t speak negatively of the Russian government or leaders.
  • Don’t smoke in public places.

Safety and Health

When traveling to Asian Russia, it’s important to take the necessary precautions to ensure your safety and health.

Safety in Asian Russia

  • Be aware of your surroundings and stay alert in crowded areas.
  • Keep your valuables in a safe place, and don’t carry too much cash.
  • Don’t walk alone at night in unfamiliar areas.
  • Be cautious when using public transportation.

Health in Asian Russia

  • Make sure your routine vaccinations are up-to-date before traveling.
  • Pack mosquito repellent and anti-malaria medication if you plan to visit rural areas.
  • Drink only bottled or boiled water.
  • Wash your hands regularly.
  • Be aware of the risk of food poisoning.

How to Get Around in Asian Russia

When it comes to getting around in Asian Russia, here are your options:

  • Taxis are widely available in most cities.
  • Public transportation, including metro systems, buses, and trams, are affordable and efficient in major cities.
  • You can rent a car, but be aware that driving in Russia can be challenging.
  • Take a train, such as the Trans-Siberian Railway, to travel across long distances.
