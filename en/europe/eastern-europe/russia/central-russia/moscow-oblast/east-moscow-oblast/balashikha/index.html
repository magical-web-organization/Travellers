Introduction to Balashikha

Located in East Moscow Oblast, in Moscow Oblast, in Central Russia, Balashikha is a beautiful and often overlooked destination. The city may not be as famous or as popular as Moscow, but it has its own distinct charm and beauty.

Home to a number of historical and cultural attractions, outdoor adventures, and delicious food, Balashikha is ideal for travelers looking for a quiet and authentic Russian experience. In this travel guide, we will provide everything you need to know to plan your trip to Balashikha.

List of Places to Visit in Balashikha

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

For a taste of Balashikha’s cultural offerings, visit its museums and historical places. Here are some of the most popular:

Balashikha Museum of Local Lore

Explore the history of Balashikha and the surrounding area at the Balashikha Museum of Local Lore. The museum’s exhibits include archaeological finds, old photographs, and objects from the city’s past.

The Alexander Nevsky Cathedral

The Alexander Nevsky Cathedral is the largest Orthodox church in Balashikha and one of the city’s most important landmarks. The cathedral’s stunning architecture and interior design are sure to leave an impression on visitors.

Gorky Park

While not technically a museum or historical site, Gorky Park is still an important cultural icon in Balashikha. The park is a popular destination for locals and visitors alike, and it features a variety of attractions such as a Ferris wheel, a skating rink, and live performances.

Natural Attractions of Balashikha: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Balashikha is also home to a number of natural attractions, perfect for outdoor enthusiasts and nature lovers. Here are some of the top spots to check out:

Losiny Ostrov National Park

The Losiny Ostrov National Park is a nature reserve located just outside Balashikha. The park covers over 116 square kilometers of forests, wetlands, and meadows, making it a great place for hiking, bird watching, and wildlife viewing.

River Pekhorka

The River Pekhorka runs through Balashikha and offers scenic views of the city and surrounding area. Visitors can take a leisurely walk along its banks or rent a paddleboat and explore the river.

Balashikha Ice Palace

In the winter months, the Balashikha Ice Palace is a must-visit attraction. The ice palace offers skating, hockey, and other winter activities, and its beautiful ice sculptures are a sight to behold.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Balashikha

Balashikha is a great destination for hiking and biking enthusiasts. Here are some of the best routes to explore:

Balashikha Hiking Route

The Balashikha Hiking Route is a popular trail that offers a full-day hike through the surrounding forests and scenic views of the area’s natural beauty. The trail is well-maintained and easy to follow.

Balashikha Biking Route

The Balashikha Biking Route is a 15-kilometer loop through the city and surrounding countryside. The route features a mix of on- and off-road terrain and offers beautiful views of the city and surrounding countryside.

Best Things to Do in Balashikha: Activities and Tours

Visit a Traditional Russian Banya

A traditional Russian banya is a must-visit experience for anyone visiting Balashikha. A banya is a steam room similar to a sauna but with the addition of a small pool of cold water. Many banyas also offer massage services, making it the perfect way to relax after a day of exploring.

Take a Cooking Class

Russian cuisine is world-renowned, and many of the country’s most famous dishes originated in the Moscow region. Visitors to Balashikha can take a cooking class to learn how to prepare a traditional Russian meal.

Go Ice Skating or Cross-Country Skiing

In the winter months, ice skating and cross-country skiing are both popular activities in Balashikha. The city has a number of outdoor rinks and trails, making it easy for visitors to enjoy these winter sports.

Week-Long Itinerary for Balashikha

Day 1: Take a leisurely stroll through Gorky Park. Day 2: Visit the Balashikha Museum of Local Lore and learn about the city’s history. Day 3: Explore the Losiny Ostrov National Park and take a hike through its beautiful forests and wetlands. Day 4: Take a cooking class and learn how to prepare traditional Russian cuisine. Day 5: Visit the Alexander Nevsky Cathedral and take in its stunning architecture. Day 6: Spend a day at the Balashikha Ice Palace and enjoy skating and other winter activities. Day 7: Take a day trip to Moscow and explore the capital city.

Food and Drink Scene of Balashikha

Must-Try Dishes

When it comes to food, there are plenty of delicious options to try in Balashikha, including:

Borscht

Borscht is a traditional Russian soup made with beets, cabbage, and other vegetables. It is usually served hot and with a dollop of sour cream on top.

Pelmeni

Pelmeni is a type of Russian dumpling filled with minced meat, onions, and spices. They are typically served with sour cream or butter.

Blini

Blini are thin Russian pancakes served with various fillings such as caviar, smoked salmon, or sour cream.

Local Beverages

In addition to its delicious food scene, Balashikha also has a number of delightful local beverages. Here are some of the most popular:

Kvas

Kvas is a beverage made from fermented bread and is similar in taste to a very mild beer. It is often sweetened with sugar or honey.

Vodka

Vodka is, of course, a staple of Russian culture, and Balashikha has no shortage of places to try the iconic spirit. If you’re looking to sample some of Russia’s best vodkas, be sure to visit a local bar or restaurant during your visit.

Where to Stay in Balashikha: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Balashikha has a variety of accommodations to choose from to fit any traveler’s needs and budget. Here are some of the best options:

Hotel East Gate

The Hotel East Gate is located in the center of Balashikha and offers comfortable rooms with modern amenities. The hotel also features a restaurant and bar, making it a convenient option for visitors to the city.

Hostel Dolly

Hostel Dolly is a budget-friendly option for travelers looking for a comfortable place to stay. The hostel offers a variety of room types, including private rooms and dormitories, and features a shared kitchen and lounge area.

Guest House Vetryak

Guest House Vetryak is a great option for those looking for a more intimate and secluded accommodation. The guesthouse is located in a quiet neighborhood outside of the city center and features comfortable rooms surrounded by beautiful gardens.

What to Buy in Balashikha: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Matryoshka Dolls

Matryoshka dolls are a classic Russian souvenir, and Balashikha has no shortage of places to purchase them. The dolls are usually hand-painted and come in a variety of sizes and designs.

Kvas

Kvas is not only a popular local beverage, but it also makes for a unique and tasty souvenir. Many specialty shops in Balashikha sell bottled kvas that guests can bring back home with them.

Local Culture and Customs of Balashikha

Like many other small Russian cities, Balashikha has a rich and unique culture. Visitors to the city may enjoy the chance to learn more about local customs and traditions, such as:

The Importance of Family

Family is a core value in Russian culture and is taken very seriously. Visitors to Balashikha may notice the strong emphasis on family and community in the city’s social behavior.

Contemporary Art Scene

Balashikha is home to a thriving contemporary art scene, with many local artists showcasing their works in galleries and exhibitions throughout the city.

Best Time to Visit Balashikha

The best time to visit Balashikha is during the summer months, particularly between June and September. The weather is mild and pleasant during these months, and visitors can enjoy a variety of outdoor activities in the area’s natural attractions.

Do’s and Don’ts of Balashikha

Do:

  • Respect local customs and traditions
  • Dress appropriately for the weather and for visiting religious sites
  • Learn a few phrases in Russian to communicate with locals, or bring a phrasebook

Don’t:

  • Be late for meetings or appointments.
  • Wear shoes inside a Russian home.
  • Photograph anyone without their permission

Safety and Health

Safety in Balashikha

Balashikha is generally a safe place for visitors. However, as with any travel destination, visitors should take standard safety precautions such as keeping valuable items secure and avoiding walking alone at night.

Health in Balashikha

Visitors to Balashikha should be up to date on routine vaccinations, such as the measles vaccine. Tap water is generally safe to drink, but visitors may prefer to drink bottled water to avoid any possible health risks. Visitors should also bring any necessary medications they may need during their trip.

Getting to Balashikha: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Balashikha

Balashikha is located approximately 30 kilometers from Moscow’s Sheremetyevo International Airport and about 44 kilometers from Moscow’s Domodedovo International Airport. Visitors can reach the city from the airport via taxi or public transportation.

Bus Stations Near Balashikha

Balashikha has a variety of public transportation options, with a number of bus stations located throughout the city. Buses run regularly and are a convenient and affordable way to get around the city.

Other Options

Visitors to Balashikha may also choose to take a taxi or a private car service to get around the city. Uber and similar services are widely available in the area.

How to Get Around in Balashikha

Balashikha has a well-developed network of public transportation, including buses and trams. Taxis and car rental services are also available for visitors who prefer more private transportation.

Where to Go Next from Balashikha: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Moscow

For visitors looking to explore more of Russia’s capital city, Moscow is just a short train ride away from Balashikha. A visit to Moscow would not be complete without a trip to the Red Square, St. Basil’s Cathedral, and the famous Bolshoi Theatre.

Suzdal

Suzdal is a small, historic town located approximately 200 kilometers from Balashikha. The town is known for its stunning architecture and beautiful natural scenery, making it an ideal destination for a day trip or overnight stay.

Conclusion

Balashikha may not be as famous as its neighboring cities, but it has plenty of sights and experiences to offer visitors. From its cultural attractions to its natural beauty, hiking and biking routes, food scene, and traditional Russian banyas, Balashikha is a great destination for travelers seeking an authentic experience in Russia.
