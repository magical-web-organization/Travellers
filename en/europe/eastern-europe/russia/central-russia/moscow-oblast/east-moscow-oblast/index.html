Introduction to East Moscow Oblast

East Moscow Oblast is a beautiful region that lies in the heart of Russia, encompassing the eastern part of Moscow Oblast and stretching towards the city of Moscow. This region is known for its rich history, cultural attractions, and breathtaking natural landscapes.

In this comprehensive travel guide, we will take you on a journey to explore the top attractions, activities, hiking and biking routes, food and drink scene, accommodations, local culture, safety and health, and transportation options to East Moscow Oblast.

List of Places to Visit in East Moscow Oblast

Here’s a list of must-visit places in East Moscow Oblast:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Sergiev Posad: Known for its architectural beauty, Sergiev Posad is a must-visit attraction in East Moscow Oblast. It is home to the UNESCO World Heritage Site - the Trinity Lavra of St. Sergius.

  • Abramtsevo Estate Museum: It is a unique museum that features a collection of paintings, sculptures, and furniture from famous Russian artists like Ilya Repin and Vasily Polenov.

  • Kolomna Kremlin: This medieval fortress was built in the 16th century and is one of the oldest and most well-preserved structures in Russia.

Natural Attractions of East Moscow Oblast: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Zvenigorod: A small town located on the banks of the Moskva River offers breathtaking views of the countryside. This town is also known for its hiking and biking trails.

  • Ruza Reservoir: It is a popular destination for water sports enthusiasts, offering opportunities for swimming, fishing, and boating.

  • Meshchersky National Park: This park offers a perfect getaway from the bustling city life and has plenty of trails for hiking and skiing.

Hiking/Biking Routes of East Moscow Oblast

Here are some of the best hiking and biking routes that East Moscow Oblast has to offer:

  • Moscow Green Ring: A 170 km route that circles around the city of Moscow and offers a great biking trail for adventure enthusiasts.

  • Voronovo - Jungle Track: This 36 km trail is one of the most scenic routes in East Moscow Oblast that is perfect for mountain bikers.

  • Volen: A 70 km hiking trail that offers some of the best views of the Volen Nature Reserve.

Best Things to Do in East Moscow Oblast: Activities and Tours

  • Volen Nature Reserve: Explore the beautiful landscapes and wildlife of the Volen Nature Reserve.

  • Tsaritsyno Museum-Reserve: A beautiful palace complex with beautiful gardens and a lake.

  • Russian Banya Experience: Head to one of the many traditional Russian bathhouses in East Moscow Oblast to relax and unwind.

Food and Drink Scene of East Moscow Oblast

Must-Try Dishes

  • Pelmeni: Russian dumplings that are made with meat or vegetable filling.

  • Shashlik: A type of Russian kebab made with meat (usually pork), vegetables or seafood.

  • Blini: Russian pancakes made with buckwheat or wheat flour.

Local Beverages

  • Kvas: A traditional Russian beverage made from fermented bread that is slightly sweet and refreshing.

  • Vodka: Russia’s national drink, vodka is typically served as a shot or mixed with juice or soda.

  • Kvass: Another fermented drink made from rye bread or beetroot.

Where to Stay in East Moscow Oblast: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Here are some of the best accommodation options in East Moscow Oblast:

  • Hotel Vega Tver: A 4-star hotel located in Tver, offering luxurious rooms and amenities.

  • Hostel Leto: A budget-friendly hostel located in Sergiev Posad.

  • Guest House Park Hotel: A cozy guest house located in the heart of Zvenigorod.

What to Buy in East Moscow Oblast: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Matryoshka Dolls: These hand-painted wooden dolls are traditional Russian souvenirs.

  • Vodka: A bottle of Russian vodka is the perfect souvenir for anyone who loves to drink.

  • Oreshki: These walnut-shaped cookies are a popular Russian sweet treat.

Local Culture and Customs of East Moscow Oblast

East Moscow Oblast has a rich cultural heritage and a unique set of customs and traditions. The most popular cultural events are Maslenitsa, Easter, Victory Day, and the International Moscow Film Festival.

Best Time to Visit East Moscow Oblast

The best time to visit East Moscow Oblast is during the summer months (June to August) when the weather is warm and sunny, and the landscapes are green and vibrant.

  • Don’t litter the natural landscapes

  • Don’t disregard the local laws and regulations

Safety and Health

Safety in East Moscow Oblast

East Moscow Oblast is generally safe for travelers, with low crime rates. Visitors are advised to take the usual precautions they would in any other city or town, such as practicing vigilance while traveling on public transport.

Health in East Moscow Oblast

Visitors to East Moscow Oblast should make sure that they are up-to-date on their routine vaccinations and consult a healthcare provider about taking additional precautions against diseases such as tick-borne encephalitis.

Getting to East Moscow Oblast: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near East Moscow Oblast

The closest airports to East Moscow Oblast are Domodedovo International Airport and Sheremetyevo International Airport, both located in Moscow.

Bus Stations Near East Moscow Oblast

There are several bus stations in Moscow that provide connections to East Moscow Oblast, including the Moscow Bus Terminal and Schelkovsky Bus Terminal.

Other Options

The Moscow Metro is a great way to get around the city and connecting to East Moscow Oblast. Trains and buses also run between the towns and cities in the region.

How to Get Around in East Moscow Oblast

The best way to get around East Moscow Oblast is by car or taxi. However, the public transportation system - including buses, trains, and metro - is also available, although it may not be as extensive.

Where to Go Next from East Moscow Oblast: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Here are some of the best nearby destinations that you can travel to from East Moscow Oblast:

  • Moscow: The capital of Russia is a must-visit destination for any traveler to Russia.

  • Tver: Located to the north of East Moscow Oblast, Tver is a historic city known for its museums and ancient architecture.

  • Nizhny Novgorod: This city is one of Russia’s hidden gems, offering fantastic views, the Kremlin on the hill, and one of the biggest art festivals in Russia.

Conclusion

East Moscow Oblast is a hidden gem that offers a wealth of attractions and activities for travelers seeking to explore Russia’s history and nature. Plan your trip today and experience the unique culture and beauty of this stunning region.
