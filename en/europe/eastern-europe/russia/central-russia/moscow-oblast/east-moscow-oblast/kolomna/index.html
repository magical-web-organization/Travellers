The Ultimate Guide to Kolomna, Russia

Kolomna is a historic city located in East Moscow Oblast, in Moscow Oblast, in Central Russia, and is a popular destination for travelers looking to experience traditional Russian culture and architecture. The city boasts a rich history and is home to a variety of cultural attractions, scenic landscapes, and outdoor adventures. In this comprehensive travel guide, we’ll take you through everything you need to know to plan your visit to Kolomna, including must-see attractions, hiking/biking routes, local cuisine, and more.

List of Places to Visit in Kolomna

Visitors to Kolomna will have no shortage of places to see and things to do, with the city offering a wide variety of cultural and natural attractions.

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Kolomna is home to numerous historical sites, including the UNESCO-designated Kolomna Kremlin, a 16th-century fortress that was once used to protect the city from invaders. Other must-visit attractions include the Old Town area, the Museum of Russian Marshmallow, and the St. Nicholas Cathedral.

Natural Attractions of Kolomna: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Kolomna is surrounded by beautiful natural landscapes, making it a great destination for outdoor enthusiasts. There are numerous hiking and biking routes in the area, as well as scenic views of the Oka River. Additionally, the city is a popular destination for skiing and snowboarding during the winter months.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Kolomna

One of the best ways to experience the natural beauty of Kolomna is by exploring its many hiking and biking routes. The city boasts a variety of trails suited for all skill levels, including the Oka River Trail, the Kolomna-Lyubutskoe Forest Path, and the Moscow River Green Corridor.

Best Things to Do in Kolomna: Activities and Tours

In addition to hiking and biking, there are plenty of other activities and tours to enjoy in Kolomna. Visitors can explore the city on a traditional horse-drawn carriage ride, take a guided tour of the Kolomenskoye Estate, or visit the Kolomna Honey Market to sample local honey and other products.

Week-Long Itinerary for Kolomna

For those planning on spending a full week in Kolomna, we recommend the following itinerary:

Day 1: Explore the Kolomna Kremlin Day 2: Take a guided tour of the city’s historical sites and museums Day 3: Go hiking or biking on one of the local trails Day 4: Enjoy a traditional horse-drawn carriage ride through the city Day 5: Visit the Kolomenskoye Estate and Museum Day 6: Attend a local festival or event Day 7: Sample local cuisine and do some shopping for souvenirs

Food and Drink Scene of Kolomna

Kolomna is known for its traditional Russian cuisine, with many local restaurants and cafes serving up dishes like borscht, pelmeni, and shashlik. Additionally, the city is home to a variety of local beverages, including birch sap and medovukha, a traditional Russian honey wine.

Must-Try Dishes

Some of the must-try dishes in Kolomna include borscht, a traditional Russian soup made with beets and other vegetables, and pelmeni, a type of dumpling usually filled with minced meat and served with sour cream.

Local Beverages

Medovukha is a traditional Russian honey wine that is popular in Kolomna, as is birch sap, which is harvested from birch trees in the spring and has a refreshing, slightly sweet taste.

Where to Stay in Kolomna: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

There are plenty of options for accommodations in Kolomna, ranging from budget-friendly hostels to luxury hotels. Some of the best options include the Kolomna-Hotel, the Guest House U Vladi, and the Hotel Bella.

What to Buy in Kolomna: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Kolomna is known for its local products, including handmade crafts, traditional Russian sweets, and natural cosmetics. Visitors can find these items at the Kolomna Honey Market, the Kolomna Kremlin Gift Shop, or at one of the many local markets.

Local Culture and Customs of Kolomna

The people of Kolomna are proud of their city’s rich history and culture, and visitors are encouraged to learn about and respect local customs. Traditional Russian hospitality is also a hallmark of the city, with visitors often greeted with warm smiles and open hearts.

Best Time to Visit Kolomna

The best time to visit Kolomna is in the spring and early summer, when the weather is mild and the landscape is in full bloom. However, the city is also beautiful during the snowy winter months, when visitors can enjoy skiing and snowboarding in the surrounding mountains.

Do’s and Don’ts of Kolomna

Do:

  • Respect local customs and traditions
  • Try traditional Russian cuisine and beverages
  • Learn about the city’s history and culture

Don’t:

  • Disrespect local customs or traditions
  • Litter or damage local landmarks
  • Behave in a disruptive or disrespectful manner

Safety and Health

Safety in Kolomna

Kolomna is a safe city with low levels of crime. Visitors should still exercise normal precautions, such as being aware of their surroundings and avoiding traveling alone at night.

Health in Kolomna

There are no specific health concerns for travelers to Kolomna, but visitors should ensure that they have up-to-date travel insurance before arriving in the country.

Getting to Kolomna: Transportation Options and Logistics

Kolomna is easily accessible via several modes of transportation, including:

Airports Near Kolomna

  • Moscow Domodedovo Airport (DME) (60 km)
  • Moscow Sheremetyevo Airport (SVO) (100 km)
  • Moscow Vnukovo Airport (VKO) (75 km)

Bus Stations Near Kolomna

  • Kolomna Bus Station
  • Moscow Central Bus Terminal

Other Options

  • Train: Kolomna is well-connected to Moscow and other major cities via train.
  • Taxi: Taxis are readily available in the city and can be hired for short trips or longer excursions.

How to Get Around in Kolomna

The best way to get around in Kolomna is by foot or by bike. There are also local buses and taxis available for those who prefer not to walk or bike.

Where to Go Next from Kolomna: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

For those looking to explore more of Russia, nearby destinations like Moscow and Vladimir are easily accessible via train or bus. Alternatively, visitors can explore the countryside and book tours to visit nearby natural attractions like the Golden Ring.

Conclusion

Kolomna is a beautiful and historic city that offers a wide variety of cultural and outdoor attractions. From its rich history and architecture to its natural beauty and delicious cuisine, there’s something in Kolomna for everyone. Whether you’re a history buff, an outdoor enthusiast, or simply looking for a peaceful getaway, be sure to add Kolomna to your travel list.
