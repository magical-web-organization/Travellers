South Moscow Oblast, located in Central Russia, is a hidden gem that offers a diverse range of experiences for travelers. From cultural landmarks to outdoor adventures, there’s something for everyone in this region of Russia. In this guide, we’ll take a closer look at the top attractions and best things to do in South Moscow Oblast.

Introduction to South Moscow Oblast

South Moscow Oblast lies to the south of Moscow, the capital city of Russia. It is home to a mix of industrial and agricultural areas, as well as a thriving tourist industry. The region offers a rich cultural heritage, diverse natural landscapes, and a vibrant local food scene. Tourists can also enjoy a range of outdoor activities like hiking and cycling.

List of Places to Visit in South Moscow Oblast

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  1. Kolomna Kremlin: A medieval fortress first built in the 13th century, now housing a museum of local history.
  2. Vysotskoye Estate: A cultural and art center that contains a collection of works from various famous artists of the 20th century.
  3. Spaso-Staroobryadchesky Monastery: A 17th-century Russian Orthodox monastery with beautiful architecture and a stunning view of the Moscow River.

Natural Attractions of South Moscow Oblast: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  1. Lyudvigovsky Park: A park in Serpukhov that features a small lake and historic sites, including the ruins of an old castle.
  2. Oka River: A great spot for camping, fishing, and other outdoor activities, the Oka River has a beautiful landscape with green hills and meadows, making it a perfect spot for nature lovers.
  3. Lopasnya–Zachatievsky Park: A reserve in Balabanovo district that is home to a diverse range of wildlife and offers scenic views of the forest and meadows.

Hiking/Biking Routes of South Moscow Oblast

  1. Dedulya Mountain: A relatively easy hike up to the summit of the mountain, which offers breathtaking views of the surrounding landscape.
  2. Stolby Nature Reserve: A perfect spot for hiking and biking through its winding trails and diverse natural landscapes, including rocky formations and dense forests.
  3. Vorobyovy Gory: A popular recreational area with several hiking and biking trails suitable for beginners and advanced hikers alike.

Best Things to Do in South Moscow Oblast: Activities and Tours

  1. Boat tour of the Moscow River: Take a boat tour along the Moscow River to enjoy the breathtaking scenery, including some of the most iconic landmarks of the city.
  2. Russian Banya: The Russian Banya is a traditional steam bathhouse that offers an authentic Russian experience, which locals and tourists alike regularly visit.
  3. Horseback Riding Adventure: Go on a horseback ride through the countryside, a unique and beautiful way to experience the natural beauty of the region.

Week-Long Itinerary for South Moscow Oblast

Day 1: Kolomna Kremlin and Museum / Lyudvigovsky Park

Day 2: Vysotskoye Estate / Dedulya Mountain Hike

Day 3: Spaso-Staroobryadchesky Monastery / Oka River

Day 4: Stolby Nature Reserve / Russian Banya

Day 5: Lopasnya-Zachatievsky Park / Horseback Riding Adventure

Day 6: Vorobyovy Gory / Moscow River Boat Tour

Day 7: Relaxation Day

Food and Drink Scene of South Moscow Oblast

Must-Try Dishes

  1. Shashlik: grilled meat skewers served with various sides
  2. Pelmeni: dumplings filled with meat or vegetables, served with sour cream and butter
  3. Kvass: a popular traditional drink made from fermented bread, sweetened with honey or sugar

Local Beverages

  1. Vodka: a famous alcoholic beverage, often served with various dishes and snacks
  2. Birch juice: a refreshing and healthy non-alcoholic drink made from the sap of birch trees

Where to Stay in South Moscow Oblast: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  1. Hotel Kolomna: A three-star hotel located in the heart of Kolomna city, which offers comfortable rooms and excellent customer service.
  2. Friendly Hostel: Located in Moscow, the Friendly hostel offers a cozy and clean environment for budget travelers.
  3. Black&White Guest House: A countryside guesthouse located in Stupino, which offers excellent home-cooked meals and nature views.

What to Buy in South Moscow Oblast: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  1. Matryoshka Dolls: Beautiful hand-painted nesting dolls that come in a variety of sizes and designs, making them the perfect souvenir.
  2. Vologda Lace: Intricately woven lace made by Russian artisans that comes in various designs and styles, making them a beautiful and unique gift.

Local Culture and Customs of South Moscow Oblast

Russians value their traditions and customs highly, so it’s essential to show respect to their culture when you visit. Some of the cultural traditions in South Moscow Oblast include:

  • Tea is a popular beverage, which people drink typically more often than coffee.
  • Dress conservatively when visiting religious sites and remove your shoes before entering.
  • Russians have an excellent sense of hospitality and always offer food and drinks to guests.

Best Time to Visit South Moscow Oblast

The best time to visit South Moscow Oblast is from mid-May to September when the weather is mild and pleasant. July and August are the busiest and most expensive months. Late September and early October are also a beautiful time to visit due to the autumn colors.

Do’s and Don’ts of South Moscow Oblast

Do:

  • Learn some basic Russian phrases before your trip.
  • Try the local food and drinks.
  • Respect the local culture and traditions.

Don’t:

  • Take photographs of people without their permission.
  • Touch religious icons or decorations.
  • Discuss politics or religion with locals.

Safety and Health

Safety in South Moscow Oblast

South Moscow Oblast is generally a safe place to travel. However, you should take the usual precautions of avoiding dark and remote areas, being careful with your belongings, and not carrying large amounts of cash or valuables.

Health in South Moscow Oblast

It’s essential to ensure your routine vaccinations are up to date before traveling to South Moscow Oblast. The tap water in most hotels in urban areas is safe to drink, but it’s advisable to stick to bottled water if you’re unsure.

Getting to South Moscow Oblast: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near South Moscow Oblast

Domodedovo International Airport and Vnukovo International Airport both offer flights to destinations all around the world. Sheremetyevo International Airport is another major airport located outside the city but connected with express trains to reach the city center.

Bus Stations Near South Moscow Oblast

The Moscow city has several bus stations, including the Central Bus Station and the Moscow North Bus Station. From there, visitors can take a bus to different destinations within South Moscow Oblast.

Other Options

Visitors can also take a train or rent a car to reach South Moscow Oblast, depending on their preferences and budget.

How to Get Around in South Moscow Oblast

Public transport in Moscow and South Moscow Oblast includes metro lines, buses, and taxis. Visitors can also rent a car or bike from trusted providers.

Where to Go Next from South Moscow Oblast: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  1. Moscow: The capital of Russia presents its visitors with numerous attractions, like the Red Square and St. Basil’s Cathedral.
  2. Yasnaya Polyana: The former estate of the famous Russian writer Leo Tolstoy, now converted into a museum.
  3. Kaluga: A city located in the central region of Russia, which is also home to the Tsiolkovsky State Museum of the History of Cosmonautics.

Conclusion

South Moscow Oblast is an interesting destination, full of history, tradition, and natural beauty. Its rich heritage and beautiful sights provide an unforgettable experience for travelers of all ages. Whether you’re an active adventurer or a foodie, South Moscow Oblast has something to offer everyone. We hope this guide has convinced you to give a visit to Southern Moscow Oblast when next you plan a vacation in Russia.
