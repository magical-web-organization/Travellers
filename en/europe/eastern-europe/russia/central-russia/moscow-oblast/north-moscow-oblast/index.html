Welcome to North Moscow Oblast, the northern region of Moscow Oblast in Central Russia. This magnificent region boasts of rich cultural heritage and natural beauty, making it an ideal destination for travelers seeking adventure, tranquility, and an insight into Russia’s history and culture. In this guide, we’ll take you on a journey through North Moscow Oblast’s prominent attractions, places to visit, hiking and biking routes, food and drink scene, where to stay, how to get around, and nearby destinations.

Introduction to North Moscow Oblast

North Moscow Oblast is located in the Central Federal District of Russia, with Moscow its capital. The region is home to over 900,000 people and covers an area of 47,000 square kilometers. Tourists flock to the region to explore its distinct culture, scenic countryside, and grand architecture. The region is also accessible, with several transportation options available, making it easy to get around.

List of Places to Visit in North Moscow Oblast

North Moscow Oblast has a long list of iconic landmarks, cultural attractions, and natural wonders. Here are some of the top places that you should include on your itinerary:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • The Trinity Lavra of St. Sergius: This UNESCO World Heritage Site is a 14th-century Russian Orthodox Monastery and one of the most famous pilgrimage sites in Russia.
  • Abramtsevo Estate: This 19th-century estate is known for its significant contribution to the Russian Arts and Crafts movement.
  • Kuskovo Estate: This 18th-century estate was originally built as a summer country house for the Sheremetev family and is now a museum of Russian aristocracy lifestyle of that time.

Natural Attractions of North Moscow Oblast: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Elk Island National Park: The park is home to many species of animals, including elk, bison, moose, and deer. You can hike through the verdant woodlands, bike around the park’s lakes, or fish in its streams.
  • Zavidovo National Park: This scenic park is situated around Lake Zavidovo and forest-covered hills, a perfect spot for hiking and cycling.
  • Valdayskiye Uplands: This forest-covered region is known for its crystal-clear lakes, cultural landmarks, berry and mushroom picking.

Hiking/Biking Routes of North Moscow Oblast

North Moscow Oblast’s natural beauty and terrain make it an ideal place for hiking and biking. Here are some of the best routes that you can explore:

  • Sergiev Posad Loop: This 33-kilometer route takes you through forest, along riverside paths, and rural roads and passes three of the most significant religious sites in Russia.
  • Klin Loop: This 43-kilometer route starts from the Ivan Turgenev Museum - a writer’s country estate – through the countryside and forest and passes scenic villages such as Dubrovitsy and Firsanovka.
  • Dmitrov Loop: This 46-kilometer route starts from Dmitrov’s Kremlin and takes you through the city’s rustic villages and farmlands.

Best Things to Do in North Moscow Oblast: Activities and Tours

  • Enjoy a boat ride along the Moscow River and see the city’s stunning architectural landmarks from a different perspective.
  • Take a Master Class in Matryoshka making, an opportunity to learn about the history and techniques unique to this Russian folk art.
  • Wander around the markets, food halls, and shops in the capital, Moscow.

Food and Drink Scene of North Moscow Oblast

Russian cuisine is diverse and rich, and North Moscow Oblast is no exception. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Borscht: A rich soup made from beetroot, onion, potato, and meat.
  • Pelmeni: These traditional Russian dumplings are filled with minced meat, onion, salt, and pepper and served with butter, sour cream or vinegar.
  • Shashlik: A popular grilled meat skewer dish, which originated in the Caucasus and Central Asia.

Local Beverages

  • Vodka: Russia’s national drink and an integral part of its culture.
  • Kvass: A fermented beverage made from black or rye bread and brewed with yeast and sugar.
  • Sbiten: A traditional Russian mulled drink made with spices, honey, and water.

Where to Stay in North Moscow Oblast: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

North Moscow Oblast has several accommodation options that cater to a variety of preferences and budgets. Here are some recommendations:

  • Park Hotel Kokorino: This countryside hotel is in Solnechnogorsky District and surrounded by picturesque Russian nature.
  • Matreshka Hotel: This hotel is located in the heart of Sergiev Posad and surrounded by the city’s notable landmarks.
  • Hostel Eletskaya: A vibrant hostel in Moscow that offers comfortable rooms at very affordable prices.

What to Buy in North Moscow Oblast: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

If you’re looking for souvenirs to bring home, you can buy traditional Russian crafts, such as Matryoshka dolls, Khokhloma tableware, and Gzhel ceramics. Alternatively, you can purchase products made with local ingredients, such as honey, fish, berries, and mushrooms.

Local Culture and Customs of North Moscow Oblast

Russians have a rich and fascinating culture that’s worth exploring. As a visitor to North Moscow Oblast, you should be respectful of the local customs and traditions. Russians are generally warm and welcoming, but they can be sensitive when it comes to religion, politics, and sensitive issues.

Best Time to Visit North Moscow Oblast

The best time to visit North Moscow Oblast is during the summer months (June to August) when the weather is mild, and the countryside is teeming with lush greenery. However, the region has a charm to it throughout the year, hitting real beauty in autumn and winter seasons as well.

Do’s and Don’ts of North Moscow Oblast

Do:

  • Learn a few phrases in Russian before your trip.
  • Dress appropriately for the weather and terrain.
  • Try the local cuisine and drinks.

Don’t:

  • Take photographs in religious sites without getting permission.
  • Engage in political or sensitive discussions with locals.
  • Smoke in public places.

Safety and Health

Safety in North Moscow Oblast

North Moscow Oblast is generally a safe destination for visitors, but, like any foreign city, it’s best to exercise caution when traveling. Be aware of your surroundings, avoid solo travel at night, and keep your personal belongings safe.

Health in North Moscow Oblast

Russia has a fully-functional healthcare system, but it’s essential to have travel insurance and emergency funds, especially when traveling in remote areas. Don’t forget to pack essential over-the-counter medicine just in case they are required.
