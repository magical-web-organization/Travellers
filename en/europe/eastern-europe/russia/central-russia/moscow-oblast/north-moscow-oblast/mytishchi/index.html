Introduction to Mytishchi

Located in North Moscow Oblast, Mytishchi is a picturesque city known for its natural beauty, cultural landmarks, and vibrant food scene. Situated in Central Russia, the city features a myriad of outdoor adventures and scenic views. To help you plan your trip to Mytishchi, we’ve created this comprehensive travel guide. Whether you’re interested in exploring the city’s historical landmarks, hiking or biking in nature, or tasting local cuisine, we’ve got you covered!

List of Places to Visit in Mytishchi

To make the most out of your trip to Mytishchi, consider visiting the following top attractions:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Mytishchi Museum of Local Lore: This museum features exhibits on the history, culture, and folklore of Mytishchi and the surrounding region.
  • St. Nicholas Cathedral: This historic cathedral, built in the early 19th century, is one of the most iconic landmarks in Mytishchi.
  • Mytishchi Art Gallery: Art lovers will enjoy exploring the Mytishchi Art Gallery, which features a wide range of contemporary and traditional art in various mediums.

Natural Attractions of Mytishchi: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Arkhangelskoye Estate: This 18th-century estate, located just outside of Mytishchi, offers stunning views of the surrounding countryside and is home to beautifully landscaped gardens.
  • Losiny Ostrov National Park: Located just north of Mytishchi, this nature reserve offers over 10,000 hectares of pristine forest to explore.
  • Pirogovskoye Reservoir: This vast reservoir, located just outside of Mytishchi, is a popular spot for fishing and boating.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Mytishchi

For those looking to explore Mytishchi’s natural beauty, hiking and biking are great options. Here are some of the best routes to check out:

  • Mytishchi Forest Trail: This 6 km trail winds through the Mytishchi Forest and is suitable for both hiking and biking.
  • Losiny Ostrov Trail: This 20 km trail, located in the Losiny Ostrov National Park, offers hikers and bikers the chance to explore the park’s pristine forests and lakes.
  • Old Mytishchi Trail: This 8 km trail takes visitors on a journey through Mytishchi’s historic town center, with stops at key landmarks along the way.

Best Things to Do in Mytishchi: Activities and Tours

In addition to hiking and exploring the city’s cultural landmarks, Mytishchi also offers a variety of activities and tours. Here are some popular options:

  • Rachel’s Kitchen: This cooking school offers classes on traditional Russian cuisine, as well as other regional and international dishes.
  • Mytishchi Indoor Skydiving: Thrill-seekers can experience the feeling of skydiving without ever leaving the ground at this indoor skydiving facility.
  • Moscow Helicopter Tour: For a bird’s-eye view of Mytishchi and the surrounding region, consider booking a helicopter tour.

Food and Drink Scene of Mytishchi

Mytishchi offers a diverse and vibrant food scene, with a range of traditional and international options available. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Borshch: This traditional beet soup is a staple of Russian cuisine.
  • Pelmeni: A type of dumpling usually filled with meat and served with sour cream.
  • Pirozhki: Small baked or fried pies usually filled with meat, vegetables, or fruit.

Local Beverages

  • Kvass: A popular non-alcoholic drink made from fermented bread.
  • Vodka: No trip to Russia is complete without trying the country’s most famous beverage.

Where to Stay in Mytishchi: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Mytishchi offers a range of accommodations to suit all budgets. Here are some options:

  • Radisson Blu Hotel Moscow Sheremetyevo Airport: This upscale hotel is located near Sheremetyevo International Airport.
  • Hostel Okhotnik: This budget-friendly hostel offers dormitory-style accommodations in the heart of Mytishchi.
  • Guesthouse Stanislavsky: This small guesthouse features cozy and comfortable rooms with a friendly host.

What to Buy in Mytishchi: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

If you’re looking to bring a piece of Mytishchi home with you, consider purchasing some traditional Russian products or locally-made crafts. Some popular options include:

  • Matryoshka Dolls: These iconic nesting dolls are a classic souvenir from Russia.
  • Lacquer Boxes: These intricately-painted boxes are a unique and beautiful keepsake.
  • Birch Bark Crafts: Handmade from the bark of birch trees, these products come in various forms, including baskets, boxes, and decorative items.

Local Culture and Customs of Mytishchi

Like much of Russia, Mytishchi has a rich cultural history and unique customs. Here are some things to keep in mind during your visit:

  • Greetings: When meeting someone for the first time, it’s customary to shake hands and make eye contact.
  • Dress: Dressing conservatively is recommended when visiting religious or historical sites.
  • Gift-Giving: It’s customary to bring a small gift when visiting someone’s home in Russia.

Best Time to Visit Mytishchi

The best time to visit Mytishchi is during the summer months (May to September), when the weather is warm and outdoor activities are in full swing. However, visiting in the winter months can also be a unique and beautiful experience, complete with snowy landscapes and winter festivals.

Do’s and Don’ts of Mytishchi

Do:

  • Try traditional Russian cuisine.
  • Visit the city’s museums and cultural landmarks.
  • Dress appropriately when visiting religious or historical sites.
  • Learn some basic Russian phrases before your visit.

Don’t:

  • Overlook the city’s natural beauty and outdoor activities.
  • Assume that everyone speaks English – knowing some basic Russian can go a long way.
  • Wear revealing clothing in public places.

Safety and Health

Safety in Mytishchi

Mytishchi is generally a safe and peaceful place to visit. However, as with any city, it’s important to take basic safety precautions, such as keeping your valuables secure and being aware of your surroundings when out and about.

Health in Mytishchi

It’s recommended that visitors to Mytishchi have up-to-date vaccinations for routine diseases such as measles, mumps, and rubella. Visitors should also take precautions to protect themselves against tick-borne diseases when spending time in nature.

Getting to Mytishchi: Transportation Options and Logistics

Mytishchi is easily accessible from Moscow and the surrounding region. Here are some transportation options and logistics to keep in mind:

Airports Near Mytishchi

The closest airport to Mytishchi is Sheremetyevo International Airport, located approximately 20 km away.

Bus Stations Near Mytishchi

Mytishchi is well-connected to Moscow and the surrounding region by bus. Major bus stations in the area include Moscow’s Krasnogvardeyskaya Bus Station and the Mytishchi Bus Station.

Other Options

Mytishchi is also accessible by train from Moscow’s Belorussky Railway Station.

How to Get Around in Mytishchi

Mytishchi is a relatively small city and is easily walkable. However, for longer journeys or those looking to explore the natural surroundings, renting a bike or car may be a good option.

Where to Go Next from Mytishchi: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

If you have more time to explore the region, consider visiting some of these nearby destinations:

  • Moscow: The vibrant capital of Russia offers a wealth of cultural landmarks, delicious food, and exciting nightlight.
  • Suzdal: This picturesque town, located approximately 200 km east of Mytishchi, is known for its beautiful churches and historic architecture.
  • Sergiev Posad: This town, located approximately 70 km northeast of Mytishchi, is home to one of the most important monasteries in Russia.

Conclusion

Mytishchi offers a wealth of cultural attractions, natural beauty, and exciting activities for visitors. Whether you’re interested in exploring the city’s museums and historical landmarks, hiking and biking in nature, or trying the local cuisine, there’s something for everyone in Mytishchi. Plan your trip today and discover this hidden gem in Central Russia!
