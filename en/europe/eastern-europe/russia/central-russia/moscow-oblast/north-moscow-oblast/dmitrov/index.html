Introduction to Dmitrov

Dmitrov is a picturesque town located in North Moscow Oblast in Central Russia, surrounded by beautiful countryside and forests. This historical and cultural hub offers a perfect escape from the noise and haste of the big city, only a 65 km drive from Moscow.

Dmitrov’s rich history dates back to the 12th century when it was founded by Prince Dmitri Donskoy. Today, the town stands as a center of culture and tourism, with a variety of historical sites, museums, and nature reserves, making it a perfect destination for tourists who are interested in architecture, culture, outdoor activities, and history.

List of Places to Visit in Dmitrov

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Dmitrov Kremlin - This 14th-century fortress is the town’s centerpiece, and the most well-known attraction in Dmitrov, featuring towers and walls, and the Cathedral of the Assumption, which has been restored and is open to the public.

  • Museum of Dmitrov Local Lore - This museum includes exhibitions related to the town’s history and development, including some unique artifacts, such as antique weapons, a boat carved from a single piece of wood, and a large map of the town that dates back to the 17th century.

  • The Guslitsa Estate - This estate belonged to the famous Russian writer Ivan Turgenev’s mother, and now serves as a museum that features exhibitions devoted to Turgenev, paintings, sculptures, and some of the writer’s personal belongings.

Natural Attractions of Dmitrov: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Senezh Lake - The lake is located in the Senezh-Ozyorny nature reserve and is perfect for those who love to relax and enjoy nature. Visitors can swim, fish, or take a boat trip, and there are even some small beaches available for sunbathing.

  • Zapadny Les Nature Reserve - Located in the West of Dmitrov, this reserve is a perfect place to do some hiking, cycling, or cross-country skiing, depending on the season.

  • The Heritage Trail “Antiquities of Dmitrov” - This trail covers a distance of 10.5 km, and starts at the Kremlin. The trail passes through the town’s historical center and over some beautiful hilly terrain, including old churches, estates, and other historical buildings.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Dmitrov

Dmitrov has plenty of hiking and cycling trails to explore, with Zapadny Les Nature Reserve being the most popular destination for locals and tourists alike. This natural park has over 40 km of hiking trails, and offers perfect terrain for mountain bike enthusiasts.

Another popular route is the 26 km “Ryablino-Dmitrov” route, which takes visitors through the natural surroundings of Dmitrov to the village of Ryablino, and then back to Dmitrov, allowing visitors to experience the stunning natural views.

Best Things to Do in Dmitrov: Activities and Tours

A range of activities and tours are available to visitors in Dmitrov, including:

  • Horse-riding - Visitors can enjoy horse-riding in the countryside, taking in the sights and sounds of the natural surroundings.

  • River Rafting down Klyazma River - This adventure activity is a great way to experience the natural surroundings of Dmitrov, with the Rafting club “Dubrava” offering all the necessary equipment and experienced guides.

  • Skiing, Snowboarding, and Ice-Skating - During the winter months, there are opportunities to ski, snowboard, and ice-skate at the local skating rink or in Zapadny Les Nature Reserve.

Week-Long Itinerary for Dmitrov

Day 1: Visit the Kremlin and the Cathedral of the Assumption, explore the town center.

Day 2: Visit the Guslitsa Estate and Museum of Dmitrov Local Lore, take a dip in the Senezh Lake.

Day 3: Take a hiking/biking tour of Zapadny Les Nature Reserve.

Day 4: River rafting down Klyazma River.

Day 5: Horse-riding in the countryside or skiing, snowboarding or ice-skating.

Day 6: Take a walk along Heritage Trail “Antiquities of Dmitrov”.

Day 7: Rest day or visit any missed attractions.

Food and Drink Scene of Dmitrov

Must-Try Dishes

  • Borscht - A delicious beef and vegetable soup, with a slightly sweet and sour taste.

  • Pelmeni - Small, tasty meat-filled dumplings that are a popular dish in Russia.

  • Blini - Thin pancakes filled with savory or sweet toppings, such as caviar, ham, or berries.

Local Beverages

  • Mead - A sweet alcoholic beverage made from honey, often served at historic estates and gardens.

  • Samogon - A strong distilled beverage, usually made from potatoes.

Where to Stay in Dmitrov: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

There are several options for accommodation in Dmitrov, ranging from budget guesthouses and hostels to luxurious hotels:

  • Aqua-Hotel - This hotel is situated in the heart of the town and offers a spa, gym, and swimming pool.

  • Rancho San Lucas Guesthouse - This guesthouse has a beautiful garden and is located near the natural sites of Dmitrov.

  • Dmitrov Park Hotel - This hotel is located in a quiet area and offers a sauna, bar, and a restaurant.

What to Buy in Dmitrov: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Visitors to Dmitrov have plenty of options for souvenirs and local products, including:

  • Handmade Russian dolls - These colorful dolls feature traditional garb and are made by hand, often sold in the souvenir shops near the Kremlin.

  • Shawls and scarves - Made by local artisans, the shawls and scarves are warm and beautiful, perfect for a cool winter day.

  • Russian tea sets - Perfect for those who want to add some traditional Russian charm to their home, these tea sets are available in many of the town’s shops and markets.

Local Culture and Customs of Dmitrov

Dmitrov boasts a rich historical and cultural heritage, with a unique lifestyle and traditions, including:

  • Russian Orthodox Christianity - The town has a rich Orthodox Christian heritage that is present in the many churches and cathedrals in the area.

  • Folklore and Craftsmanship - Local artisans use traditional techniques and materials to create beautiful crafts, pottery, and embroidery.

Best Time to Visit Dmitrov

The best time to visit Dmitrov is from May to September when the weather is pleasant and warm, and nature is at its best.

Do’s and Don’ts of Dmitrov

Do:

  • Try the local food and drinks.

  • Respect local culture and customs.

  • Visit historical sites and parks.

Don’t:

  • Make negative comments about religion or politics.

  • Litter in the natural parks and reserves.

  • Be loud or disruptive near residential areas.

Safety and Health

Safety in Dmitrov

Dmitrov is generally a safe and peaceful town, but visitors should be aware of pickpocketing and other petty crimes in crowded markets and tourist areas. Taking basic safety precautions such as keeping valuables secure is recommended.

Health in Dmitrov

Tap water is safe to drink in Dmitrov, but be aware of street food vendors, and avoid consuming food that has not been properly cooked or prepared.

Getting to Dmitrov: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Dmitrov

The nearest airport to Dmitrov is Moscow Domodedovo Airport (DME), which is approximately a 75-minute drive away.

Bus Stations Near Dmitrov

The central bus station in Dmitrov has regular connections to Moscow, as well as to other regional destinations.

Other Options

Trains are also available from Moscow, with the journey taking approximately 90 minutes.

How to Get Around in Dmitrov

The best way to explore the town is on foot, but for those who prefer other modes of transportation, taxis and buses are also available.

Where to Go Next from Dmitrov: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Sergiev Posad - This historical town is located approximately 30 km away from Dmitrov and is famous for its magnificent Lavra Monastery.

  • Zelenograd - This technical town is located approximately 18 km away from Dmitrov, with many of its buildings featuring avant-garde architecture.

Conclusion

Dmitrov is a unique destination, offering a perfect balance between historical sites, museums, outdoor activities, and beautiful nature. With its peaceful surroundings, a wide range of attractions, and local cuisine, Dmitrov is a must-visit destination for anyone seeking a cultural and nature-packed getaway in Russia.
