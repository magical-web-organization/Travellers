Your Comprehensive Guide to Korolyov, Russia

Korolyov, an urban city located in the North Moscow Oblast of Russia, has a population of over 100,000 people. The city is known for its scientific and engineering achievements that have contributed to Russia’s aerospace industry. With its rich cultural history, stunning natural beauty, and array of outdoor activities, Korolyov is an ideal travel destination for adventurers and history buffs alike.

List of Places to Visit in Korolyov

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

One of the must-visit places in Korolyov is the Korolev Museum. Dedicated to the life and legacy of Sergei Korolev, the father of Russian astronautics, the museum features exhibits on Soviet space exploration, rocket engineering and design, and a replica of the Soyuz spacecraft.

Another must-visit attraction is the Russian Aviation Museum. With its large collection of aviation artifacts and planes, the museum showcases Russia’s aviation history and developments. Tourists can also visit the Yaroslavsky Railway Museum, which displays the history of the Russian railway system.

Natural Attractions of Korolyov: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Nature lovers could enjoy hiking at the Serdce Lesa forest park - a picturesque natural park situated on the bank of the Moscow River. The park has various walking trails, each with its scenarios. For those who want to enjoy scenic views of the city, the city offers a unique observatory tower, the Berkut Tower. The tower offers a bird’s eye view of the city and the surrounding landscape.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Korolyov

The city provides an array of biking and hiking trails. The Serdce Lesa forest park is an ideal location for hiking, while the nearby Petrovsko-Razumovskaya forest reserve offers a range of trails for both hiking and biking. The reserve is well known for its picturesque valleys, lakes, and forests.

Best Things to Do in Korolyov: Activities and Tours

If you’re a fan of extreme sports or want to challenge yourself, you can try bungee jumping at the A-club Adventure Park, which also offers paintball, zip-lining and other exciting outdoor activities. Adventurers can also engage in kayaking, canoeing, and fishing at the Chistyye Prudy recreation center which offers lake activities.

Food and Drink Scene of Korolyov

Must-Try Dishes

When in Korolyov, visitors should try traditional Russian dishes such as Borsch soup, Pelmeni dumplings, Blini pancakes, and Shashlik skewers. The city also boasts of an international cuisine variety, including Italian, Japanese, and Chinese.

Local Beverages

Visitors can try the famous Russian Kvass drink, made from fermented rye bread, and the Tarragon soda, a popular traditional drink with a unique taste.

Where to Stay in Korolyov: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Korolyov has a variety of accommodation options ranging from budget-friendly options to luxury five-star hotels, guesthouses, and hostels. Visitors can book their stay at the Hotel Soyuz, Hotel Olympiets and the hostels like Sputnik Hostel and Hostel Dom.

What to Buy in Korolyov: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Korolyov offers a variety of shopping opportunities, allowing visitors to bring back a piece of Korolyov with them. Among the souvenirs to get include the famous Russian Matryoshka dolls, Khokhloma handicrafts, and traditional Russian shawls.

Local Culture and Customs of Korolyov

The residents of Korolyov tend to be conservative, and visitors should dress modestly while visiting religious places and attending events. It is customary to remove shoes when entering a private residence and handshakes are a common form of greeting.

Best Time to Visit Korolyov

The best time to visit Korolyov is during the summer months between June and August. During this time, visitors can engage in outdoor activities and participate in cultural events.

Do’s and Don’ts of Korolyov

Do:

  • Learn a few Russian phrases to ease communication
  • Dress modestly when visiting religious sites
  • Taste the traditional Russian cuisine

Don’t:

  • Cross the street when the traffic light is red, wait for the green light
  • Take pictures of railway and military installations
  • Drink tap water without boiling or purifying

Safety and Health

Safety in Korolyov

Korolyov is generally a safe city, though it is always good to take precautionary measures such as avoiding dark alleys at night and keeping your valuables in a safe place.

Health in Korolyov

Visitors should drink bottled water, which is widely available in the city, to avoid water-borne diseases.

Getting to Korolyov: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Korolyov

The nearest airport to Korolyov is the Sheremetyevo International Airport, which is about 25 kilometers away. There are buses, taxis, and car rental services available at the airport.

Bus Stations Near Korolyov

Korolyov has a bus station located in the city center, providing local and intercity transportation to visitors.

Other Options

Visitors can also use the Moscow Metro and electric trains from Moscow.

How to Get Around in Korolyov

The city offers an efficient public transportation system, including buses, trams, and taxis. Visitors can also rent a car, bicycle or scooter from rental companies.

Where to Go Next from Korolyov: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Korolyov is in close proximity to the historic city of Sergiev Posad, which has several cultural landmarks and is home to the famous Trinity Lavra of St. Sergius Monastery - an iconic Russian Orthodox site. Visitors can also visit the ancient city of Zvenigorod, which boasts of rich history and well-preserved architecture.

Conclusion

With its great cultural history, beautiful natural scenery, and array of outdoor activities, Korolyov, Russia is an ideal travel destination for history buffs and adventurers. With this guide, visitors can plan their visit to Korolyov with ease, knowing the best place to visit, the most efficient means of getting around, and the best time to visit.
