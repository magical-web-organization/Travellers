Volokolamsk Travel Guide: A Complete Tour Guide to Volokolamsk, Russia

Located in the West Moscow Oblast in Central Russia, Volokolamsk is a charming town that attracts tourists from all over because of its rich cultural heritage, natural beauty, and outdoor activities. In this guide, we’ll take you on a tour of Volokolamsk, exploring its must-visit attractions, delicious cuisine, and unique local culture.

Introduction to Volokolamsk

Volokolamsk is a town in Russia that is known for its rich cultural heritage, ancient monasteries, and beautiful natural landscapes. This town has a lot to offer travelers who are interested in history, architecture, and outdoor activities.

List of Places to Visit in Volokolamsk

Here are some of the top places to visit in Volokolamsk:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Volokolamsk Kremlin: This is a monumental architectural ensemble situated in the center of the town. Built in the 14th century, the kremlin is one of the most prominent landmarks of Volokolamsk.
  • The Museum of Volokolamsk History and Art: This museum is an excellent place to learn about the history and culture of the town. It has a rich collection of exhibits showcasing archeological finds, traditional costumes, art, and other historical artifacts.
  • The Nativity of the Virgin Monastery: This is one of the most ancient monasteries in Russia, dating back to the 12th century. The monastery consists of beautiful churches, chapels, and other religious buildings, all of which are surrounded by a picturesque landscape.
  • The Resurrection Monastery: This monastery was founded in the 14th century and is famous for its magnificent frescoes from the 17th century.

Natural Attractions of Volokolamsk: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • The Volokolamsk Reservoir: This is a large artificial lake situated near the town, surrounded by beautiful forests and hills. It’s an excellent place to go fishing, camping, hiking, or simply relaxing in nature.
  • The Michurinskaya Grove: This is a preserved natural park situated near Volokolamsk. The park consists of a beautiful forest of pine, birch, and oak trees, and is home to various wildlife species.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Volokolamsk

Volokolamsk has many beautiful hiking and biking routes that offer stunning views of nature and the town. Here are some of the best routes to explore:

  • The Michurinskaya Grove route: This route starts from the town center and leads to the Michurinskaya Grove. It’s an easy trail suitable for beginners and offers beautiful views of the forest and the town.
  • The Resurrection Monastery route: This route starts from the Resurrection Monastery and leads to the Volokolamsk Kremlin. It’s a moderate trail suitable for intermediate hikers and offers stunning views of the monasteries and the town.
  • The Volokolamsk Reservoir route: This route starts from the Volokolamsk Reservoir and leads to the natural park near the town. It’s a challenging trail suitable for experienced hikers and offers breathtaking views of the lake and the surrounding mountains.

Best Things to Do In Volokolamsk: Activities and Tours

  • Take a walking tour of the ancient monasteries and churches of Volokolamsk and learn about the history and culture of the town.
  • Go fishing, camping, or boating on the Volokolamsk Reservoir, which is an excellent place for outdoor activities.
  • Visit the Museum of Volokolamsk History and Art, which has an impressive collection of exhibits showcasing the town’s history and culture.
  • Explore the Michurinskaya Grove, which is a beautiful natural park offering various hiking trails, picnic areas, and wildlife watching opportunities.

Week-Long Itinerary for Volokolamsk

Here’s a suggested week-long itinerary for your trip to Volokolamsk:

  • Day 1: Visit the Volokolamsk Kremlin and explore the town center.
  • Day 2: Visit the Nativity of the Virgin Monastery and the Resurrection Monastery.
  • Day 3: Take a tour of the Michurinskaya Grove and enjoy a picnic in the forest.
  • Day 4: Go fishing or camping on the Volokolamsk Reservoir.
  • Day 5: Visit the Museum of Volokolamsk History and Art and learn about the town’s history and culture.
  • Day 6: Explore the surrounding countryside and visit nearby villages.
  • Day 7: Enjoy a day of relaxation in nature or some last-minute shopping for souvenirs.

Food and Drink Scene of Volokolamsk

Russian cuisine is known for its hearty and delicious dishes, and Volokolamsk is no exception. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Borscht: A traditional Russian soup made with beets, cabbage, potatoes, and meat.
  • Pelmeni: Small and savory dumplings filled with minced meat, onions, and herbs.
  • Shashlik: Grilled meat skewers marinated in spices and served with vegetables.

Local Beverages

  • Kvass: A traditional fermented drink made from rye bread and flavored with herbs or fruit.
  • Medovukha: A sweet honey-based alcoholic drink that originated in medieval Russia.
  • Vodka: A popular distilled beverage made from grains or potatoes, which is enjoyed by Russians throughout the country.

  • Voznesensky Hotel: A centrally located hotel with comfortable rooms and excellent service.
  • Guest House Volokolamsk: A cozy guesthouse with affordable rates and friendly staff.
  • Hostel Nostalgia: A budget-friendly hostel offering dormitory-style accommodation and shared facilities.

What to Buy in Volokolamsk: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Russian dolls (Matryoshka): Handmade wooden dolls painted with traditional Russian designs.
  • Flaxseed oil: A traditional Russian product known for its health benefits and used in cooking.
  • Birch bark crafts: Handmade souvenirs and household items made from birch bark, such as baskets, boxes, and cups.

Local Culture and Customs of Volokolamsk

Volokolamsk has a rich cultural heritage, and it’s always good to be aware of local customs and traditions. Here are some things to keep in mind:

  • Religion: The majority of the population is Orthodox Christian, so it’s important to show respect for religious customs and traditions.
  • Dress code: Dress modestly when visiting religious sites, and avoid wearing revealing clothing or shorts.
  • Greetings: Russians generally greet each other with a handshake or a hug and expect a polite greeting when meeting someone new.

Best Time to Visit Volokolamsk

The best time to visit Volokolamsk is during the summer months (June-August) when the weather is warm and sunny, and outdoor activities are abundant. Winter in Volokolamsk can be harsh, with snow and freezing temperatures, so it’s best to avoid this period if you’re not used to the cold.

Do’s and Don’ts of Volokolamsk

Do:

  • Respect the local customs and traditions.
  • Learn some basic Russian phrases to communicate with locals.
  • Try the local cuisine and beverages.
  • Be aware of your surroundings and keep your valuables safe.

Don’t:

  • Be disrespectful to religion or local customs.
  • Take photographs of people without their permission.
  • Be too loud or disruptive in public places.
  • Wander off into the countryside without a guide or proper equipment.

Safety and Health

Volokolamsk is a safe town with low crime rates, but it’s always good to be aware of potential risks and take precautions. Here are some safety and health tips to keep in mind:

Safety in Volokolamsk

  • Avoid walking alone at night, especially in quiet and poorly lit areas.
  • Keep your valuables safe, and avoid wearing expensive jewelry or carrying large amounts of cash.
  • Be cautious when crossing busy roads or using public transportation.

Health in Volokolamsk

  • Be careful when drinking tap water, as it may not be safe for consumption. Stick to bottled water instead.
  • Bring insect repellent if you plan to spend time outdoors, especially during the summer months, when mosquitoes and ticks are prevalent.
  • Familiarize yourself with emergency contact numbers and the location of local hospitals and medical facilities.

Getting to Volokolamsk: Transportation Options and Logistics

Volokolamsk is located approximately 120 kilometers west of Moscow and is accessible via various modes of transportation. Here are some options to consider:

  • By Plane: The nearest airport to Volokolamsk is the Sheremetyevo International Airport in Moscow, which is approximately a 2-hour drive from the town.
  • By Bus: Regular bus services connect Volokolamsk to Moscow and other nearby cities.
  • By Car: Volokolamsk is easily accessible by car, with good roads linking the town to Moscow and other nearby cities.
  • By Train: The nearest train station to Volokolamsk is the Moscow Belorussky railway terminal, which is approximately a 1.5-hour drive from the town.

How to Get Around in Volokolamsk

Volokolamsk is a relatively small town, and most attractions are within walking distance of each other. However, if you prefer, you can also use local taxis or rental cars to get around town.

Where to Go Next from Volokolamsk: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Volokolamsk is located in a region rich in culture and history, and there are many nearby destinations and excursions you can explore. Here are some suggestions:

  • Moscow: The Russian capital is situated only a few hours away from Volokolamsk and is a must-visit destination for its beautiful architecture, art, and culture.
  • Tver: This historic town is located on the banks of the Volga River and is known for its stunning architecture and cultural landmarks.
  • Sergiev Posad: This town is known for its renowned Trinity Lavra of St. Sergius, a UNESCO World Heritage site and a major cultural landmark of Russia.

Conclusion

Volokolamsk is a hidden gem in Russia, offering visitors a perfect blend of history, nature, and culture. With its ancient monasteries, beautiful landscapes, and delectable cuisine, Volokolamsk is a must-visit destination for anyone interested in exploring the cultural heritage of Russia. Plan your trip to Volokolamsk today and experience the best of what Central Russia has to offer.
