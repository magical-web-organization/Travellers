Introduction to Krasnogorsk

Krasnogorsk is a city located in West Moscow Oblast, Central Russia. It is known for its beautiful natural landscapes and rich cultural heritage. The city boasts a range of museums and historical sites, outdoor adventures, scenic views, and exciting activities. Its well-developed infrastructure also makes it accessible to travelers.

If you are planning to visit Krasnogorsk, this comprehensive travel guide will help you experience the best of what the city has to offer.

List of Places to Visit in Krasnogorsk

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Museum of Military Medicine: This museum showcases the development of military medicine in Russia and its contribution to various wars and conflicts.
  • Krasnogorsk Local Lore Museum: Explore the history, culture and traditions of Krasnogorsk through different exhibitions and artifacts displayed at this museum.
  • Museum of the History of Krasnogorsk Cinema: This museum is dedicated to the history of Russian cinema and its contribution to world cinema.

Natural Attractions of Krasnogorsk: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Moscow Canal: Take a picturesque boat tour on the Moscow Canal and enjoy the beautiful views along the way.
  • Krasnogorsky Lesopark: This is a large forest park with a variety of recreational activities including hiking, biking, picnicking, and fishing.
  • Arkhangelskoye Estate: Explore the stunning gardens of the Arkhangelskoye Estate, a former aristocratic estate that is now open to the public.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Krasnogorsk

  • Krasnogorsk bike path: This bike path follows the Moskva river and runs parallel to the Moscow-Kiev highway. It offers scenic views of the river and the surrounding landscape.
  • Krylatskoye bike path: This bike path runs through the Krylatskoye district of Moscow and provides access to the Moscow River and the surrounding forested areas.

Best Things to Do in Krasnogorsk: Activities and Tours

  • The Pavel Tretyakov Gallery Tour: Take a guided tour of the Tretyakov Gallery in Moscow and explore one of Russia’s most famous art museums.
  • Russian banya experience: Take some time to relax and unwind at one of Krasnogorsk’s traditional Russian bathhouses.
  • Helicopter tour over Moscow: Get a bird’s-eye view of Moscow and its surrounding regions with this exciting and unforgettable helicopter tour.

Food and Drink Scene of Krasnogorsk

Must-Try Dishes

  • Blini: These thin pancakes are a popular Russian dish that can be served sweet or savory.
  • Pelmeni: These are Russian dumplings that are similar to Italian ravioli. They can be filled with beef, pork or even mushrooms.
  • Vodka: Russia is known for its vodka, and Krasnogorsk is no exception. Be sure to try a shot or two while you’re in town.

Local Beverages

  • Kvass: This is a traditional Russian drink made from fermented bread and served cold during the summer months.
  • Sbiten: This is a hot spiced tea that is popular during the winter months and is often served with honey or fruit.

Where to Stay in Krasnogorsk: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Silky Way Hotel: This hotel offers comfortable rooms, a gym, and a spa with a Russian bathhouse.
  • Apart Hotel NEP Dubki: This apart hotel offers spacious and fully equipped apartments for travelers looking for a more home-like experience.
  • Gostinitsa Port: This guesthouse is situated on the bank of the Moscow Canal and offers a tranquil and picturesque setting for travelers.

What to Buy in Krasnogorsk: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Matryoshka dolls: These are traditional Russian nesting dolls that come in a variety of sizes and designs.
  • Vodka: As mentioned earlier, vodka is a popular souvenir to bring home from Russia, and Krasnogorsk has several local distilleries that produce their own unique blends.
  • Russian lacquer boxes: These intricately painted boxes make for beautiful and unique souvenirs.

Local Culture and Customs of Krasnogorsk

Krasnogorsk has a rich cultural heritage, including traditional Russian architecture, cuisine, and art. Visitors are encouraged to respect local customs and engage with the community to get a better understanding of the local culture.

Best Time to Visit Krasnogorsk

The best time to visit Krasnogorsk is during the summer months of June to September when the weather is warm and dry. Winters can be extremely cold, with snow covering the city from December to March.

Do’s and Don’ts of Krasnogorsk

Do:

  • Respect local customs and traditions.
  • Learn some basic Russian phrases to communicate with locals.
  • Dress appropriately for the weather.

Don’t:

  • Drink in public places.
  • Smoke in non-designated areas.
  • Disrespect religious or cultural sites.

Safety and Health

Safety in Krasnogorsk

Krasnogorsk is a relatively safe city, but visitors should take precautions by not carrying too much cash, keeping valuables in a safe place and being aware of their surroundings, particularly in crowded areas.

Health in Krasnogorsk

Tap water is generally safe to drink in Krasnogorsk, but visitors are advised to stick to bottled water to avoid any health issues. Medical assistance is available at local hospitals and clinics.

Getting to Krasnogorsk: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Krasnogorsk

  • Sheremetyevo International Airport: This airport is located about 30 km from Krasnogorsk and is a major transport hub for Moscow.
  • Vnukovo International Airport: This airport is located about 42 km from Krasnogorsk and offers both domestic and international flights.

Bus Stations Near Krasnogorsk

  • Krasnogorsk bus station: This bus station is located in the city center and provides regular bus service to various destinations in and around Krasnogorsk.

Other Options

  • Taxi: Taxis are readily available in Krasnogorsk and can be hailed on the street or booked through a ride-hailing app.
  • Metro: Krasnogorsk has its own metro station, which provides access to central Moscow and other parts of the city.

How to Get Around in Krasnogorsk

Krasnogorsk has a well-developed public transportation system that includes buses and metro. Taxis and ride-hailing apps are also available for travelers who prefer private transportation.

Where to Go Next from Krasnogorsk: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Moscow: The capital city of Russia is an international cultural and political center with numerous museums, galleries, and other attractions.
  • Sergiyev Posad: This historic town is home to the Trinity Lavra of St. Sergius monastery, a UNESCO World Heritage Site.
  • Zvenigorod: This picturesque town is located about 40 km from Krasnogorsk and is known for its beautiful medieval architecture.

Conclusion

Krasnogorsk is a hidden gem in Central Russia with plenty to offer travelers looking for unique experiences. From rich cultural heritage and scenic landscapes to delicious local cuisine and exciting outdoor adventures, Krasnogorsk is a destination worth exploring. We hope this comprehensive travel guide has given you all the information you need to plan an unforgettable trip to Krasnogorsk.
