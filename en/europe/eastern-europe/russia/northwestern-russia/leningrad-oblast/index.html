Introduction to Leningrad Oblast

Leningrad Oblast is a region of northwestern Russia that borders Finland and the Gulf of Finland to the west, the Republic of Karelia to the north, and St. Petersburg to the east. The region is named after its capital city of Leningrad, now known as St. Petersburg. Leningrad Oblast is known for its diverse landscapes, ranging from dense forests and pristine lakes to sandy beaches and rocky coastlines. It is a popular destination for outdoor enthusiasts, history buffs, and culture lovers alike.

List of Places to Visit in Leningrad Oblast

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Hermitage–Kazan Exhibition Centre

Located in the small town of Kazan, this exhibition center displays temporary exhibits from the State Hermitage Museum in St. Petersburg. The exhibitions showcase the vast collection of art, artifacts, and historical objects from around the world.

Shlisselburg Fortress

Shlisselburg Fortress, also known as Oreshek Fortress, is a medieval fortress built on an island in the Neva River. The fortress played a strategic role in the defense of St. Petersburg and the surrounding area.

Peterhof Palace and Grand Cascade

Peterhof Palace, also known as the “Russian Versailles,” is a stunning palace complex built by Peter the Great in the early 18th century. The Grand Cascade is a magnificent fountain that features gold statues and waterworks.

Natural Attractions of Leningrad Oblast: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Lake Ladoga

Lake Ladoga is the largest freshwater lake in Europe and the second-largest lake in Russia. It is known for its crystal-clear waters, unspoiled nature, and secluded beaches.

Valaam Archipelago

Valaam Archipelago is a group of islands in Lake Ladoga that are home to the famous Valaam Monastery. The islands are also known for their scenic landscapes, hiking trails, and historic churches.

St. Petersburg Beaches

St. Petersburg is known for its sandy beaches, which are perfect for swimming, sunbathing, and water sports. The most popular beaches include the Gulf of Finland beaches, Krestovsky Island Beach, and Laskovy Beach.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Leningrad Oblast

Karelian Isthmus Hiking Trail

The Karelian Isthmus Hiking Trail is a 200 km long trail that stretches from St. Petersburg to the Finnish border. It takes hikers through the beautiful forests, lakes, and hills of the region.

Vyborg – Priozersk Cycling Route

This 100 km cycling route starts from the historic town of Vyborg and winds through the rolling hills and scenic countryside of Leningrad Oblast. The route ends in the town of Priozersk.

Best Things to Do in Leningrad Oblast: Activities and Tours

River Boat Cruise

A river boat cruise is a great way to explore the scenic waterways of Leningrad Oblast. Cruises typically offer stunning views of natural and historical landmarks.

Mariinsky Ballet Performance

The Mariinsky Ballet is one of the oldest and most renowned ballet companies in the world. Its performances in St. Petersburg are a must-see for fans of ballet and classical music.

Food and Drink Scene of Leningrad Oblast

Must-Try Dishes

Borscht

Borscht is a classic Russian soup made with beets, cabbage, and other vegetables. It is often served with sour cream and black bread.

Pirozhki

Pirozhki are small, savory pastries filled with meat, vegetables, cheese, or other ingredients. They are a popular snack in Russia.

Local Beverages

Kvass

Kvass is a traditional Russian drink made from fermented bread. It is usually sweetened with sugar and sometimes flavored with berries or herbs.

Vodka

Vodka is a Russian staple and is often served with food or as a standalone drink. It is typically served chilled and in small glasses.

Local Culture and Customs of Leningrad Oblast

Russians are known for their hospitality and friendliness towards visitors. When visiting Leningrad Oblast, it is important to respect the local customs and cultural practices.

Best Time to Visit Leningrad Oblast

The best time to visit Leningrad Oblast is during the summer months, from June to August, when the weather is warm and sunny. However, visitors can also enjoy the region’s winter activities from December to March, such as ice skating and cross-country skiing.

Do’s and Don’ts of Leningrad Oblast

Do:

  • Learn a few basic Russian phrases.
  • Respect local customs and traditions.
  • Dress appropriately for the weather and culture.

Don’t:

  • Take photographs of military or government buildings without permission.
  • Wear shoes indoors in someone’s home or a church.
  • Drink tap water.

Safety and Health

Safety in Leningrad Oblast

Leningrad Oblast is generally a safe destination for travelers, but visitors should still exercise caution and common sense, especially in crowded areas and at night.

Health in Leningrad Oblast

There are no major health risks in Leningrad Oblast, but travelers should still take precautions against common illnesses, such as the flu, by getting vaccinated and practicing good hygiene.

Getting to Leningrad Oblast: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Leningrad Oblast

The main airport serving Leningrad Oblast is Pulkovo Airport in St. Petersburg. There are also several smaller airports in the region, such as the Lappeenranta Airport in Finland.

Bus Stations Near Leningrad Oblast

There are several bus stations in Leningrad Oblast, including the St. Petersburg Bus Terminal and the Vsevolozhsk Bus Station.

Other Options

Other transportation options to Leningrad Oblast include trains and taxis.

How to Get Around in Leningrad Oblast

The best way to get around Leningrad Oblast is by car or public transportation. Local buses, trains, and subways are all reliable and affordable.
