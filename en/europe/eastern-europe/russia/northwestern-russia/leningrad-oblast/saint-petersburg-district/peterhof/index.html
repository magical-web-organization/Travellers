Introduction to Peterhof

Peterhof, also known as the “Russian Versailles,” is a town located in the St. Petersburg district of Leningrad Oblast in northwestern Russia. The town is best known for its magnificent palace complex, a UNESCO World Heritage site, which boasts some of the most impressive palace gardens and fountains in the world. In this comprehensive guide, we’ll take you through all the must-visit places in Peterhof, the best things to do, where to stay, and where to eat.

Best Time to Visit Peterhof

The best time to visit Peterhof is during the summer months of May to September, where you can enjoy the sunshine and outdoor activities. However, the gardens in the palace complex are also beautiful during the autumn foliage and winter snow.

Do’s and Don’ts of Peterhof

Do:

  • Dress appropriately when visiting religious sites.
  • Respect the local customs and traditions.
  • Carry cash, as credit cards are not widely accepted.

Don’t:

  • Take photographs inside churches and museums without permission.
  • Wear shoes inside religious sites.
  • Engage in public displays of affection.

Safety and Health

Safety in Peterhof

Peterhof is a relatively safe town. However, tourists should always be aware of pickpockets in busy areas, especially in St. Petersburg.

Health in Peterhof

Tourists should be up-to-date on routine vaccinations, and carry a first aid kit. If you need medical attention, the nearest hospital is in St. Petersburg.
