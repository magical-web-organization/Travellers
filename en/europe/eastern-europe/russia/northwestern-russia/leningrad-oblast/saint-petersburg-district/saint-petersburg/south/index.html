Introduction to South

Saint Petersburg is a popular destination for travelers seeking rich history, breathtaking architecture, and cultural landmarks. One of its most famous districts is South, known for its unique cultural heritage, beautiful scenery, and outdoor adventures. Whether you’re a nature lover, history buff, or simply seeking new experiences, South has something to offer everyone.

List of Places to Visit in South

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

State Hermitage Museum

The State Hermitage Museum is one of the world’s largest museums and home to over three million art and cultural artifacts. The museum boasts an impressive collection of art and antiquities from around the world. Visitors can marvel at works of art by artists such as Rembrandt, Leonardo da Vinci, and Michelangelo.

Catherine Palace and Park

Catherine Palace and Park is a beautiful palace that was once the summer residence of Russian emperors. The palace is known for its iconic golden-domed towers and stunning interiors. Visitors can take a tour of the palace and see the intricately decorated rooms and halls.

Peter and Paul Fortress

The Peter and Paul Fortress is one of the most famous landmarks in Saint Petersburg and a must-see attraction. The fortress was originally built by Peter the Great in 1703 and served as a prison for political opponents. Today, visitors can explore the fortress walls, admire the stunning cathedral, and learn about the history of the fortress.

Natural Attractions of South: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Strelka

Strelka is a beautiful peninsula that is located at the confluence of the Neva and Okhta rivers. Visitors can admire the stunning views of the Hermitage, the Winter Palace, and the Peter and Paul Fortress across the river. It is a perfect spot for a picnic or a stroll along the riverbank.

Yelagin Island

Yelagin Island is a tranquil oasis in the heart of Saint Petersburg, known for its beautiful park and scenic lake. Visitors can rent a bicycle or a boat and explore the island’s natural beauty. The island also hosts concerts, exhibitions, and other cultural events throughout the year.

Alexander Park

Alexander Park is a charming park that is famous for its picturesque scenery and beautiful views. Visitors can take a stroll through the park’s winding paths, admire the colorful flowers and fountains, and relax by the lakeside.

Hiking/Biking Routes of South

Hiking

The Vyborg Sky

The Vyborg Sky is a moderate hiking trail that leads you through the beautiful forests and hills of the Vyborg region. The trail takes you to the top of the highest peak, where you can enjoy panoramic views of the Gulf of Finland and the surrounding countryside.

The Karelian Isthmus

The Karelian Isthmus is a challenging hiking trail that takes you through the stunning landscapes of the Kurgalsky Nature Reserve. The trail offers breathtaking views of the lakes, forests, and mountains, and takes you to the highest point of the Karelian Isthmus.

Biking

The Bicycle Tour of St. Petersburg

The Bicycle Tour of St. Petersburg is a fun and exciting way to explore the city’s most famous landmarks on two wheels. The tour takes you to the Hermitage, the Peter and Paul Fortress, and other iconic sites, while a guide fills you in on the city’s rich history and culture.

The Leningrad Oblast Bike Trail

The Leningrad Oblast Bike Trail is a scenic and enjoyable trail that takes you through the stunning landscapes of Leningrad Oblast. The trail takes you through traditional villages, wooded hills, and picturesque countryside, offering beautiful views and an unforgettable biking experience.

Best Things to Do in South: Activities and Tours

Boat Tour of the Neva River

A boat tour of the Neva River is an excellent way to get a unique perspective on the city’s famous landmarks and beautiful architecture. You can enjoy a relaxing cruise along the river while taking in the stunning views of the Palace Square, the Hermitage, and the Peter and Paul Fortress.

Russian Bath Experience

A traditional Russian bath experience is a great way to relax and rejuvenate while soaking in the local culture. Visitors can indulge in a variety of treatments, including massage, aromatherapy, and sauna rituals, to leave feeling refreshed and energized.

Week-Long Itinerary for South

Day 1: Arrive in Saint Petersburg and explore the city center, including the Hermitage and Nevsky Prospekt.

Day 2: Visit Catherine Palace and Park and then take a walking tour of the South district.

Day 3: Take a boat tour of the Neva River and then explore the Peter and Paul Fortress.

Day 4: Rent a bicycle and discover the scenic beauty of Yelagin Island.

Day 5: Take a hiking tour to the Vyborg Sky and enjoy the stunning panoramic views.

Day 6: Relax and indulge with traditional Russian bath treatments.

Day 7: End your trip by visiting Strelka and exploring the picturesque peninsula at the confluence of the Neva and Okhta rivers.

Food and Drink Scene of South

Must-Try Dishes

Borscht

Borscht is a traditional Russian soup made with beets, potatoes, cabbage, carrots, and onions. It is served with a dollop of sour cream and fresh herbs. It is a hearty and delicious dish that is perfect for cold days.

Blini

Blini are thin Russian pancakes that can be filled with various savory or sweet fillings, such as smoked salmon, caviar, sour cream, or jam. They are a popular breakfast and snack food, and can be enjoyed with tea or coffee.

Local Beverages

Kvass

Kvass is a traditional Russian beverage made from fermented rye bread. It has a slightly sour taste and is often served with sugar and ice. It is a refreshing and low-alcohol alternative to beer.

Vodka

Vodka is a well-known Russian alcoholic beverage that is often consumed with hearty meals, such as meat and fish dishes. It is distilled from grains or potatoes, and has a clean and smooth taste.

Where to Stay in South: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Hotels

Angleterre Hotel

The Angleterre Hotel is a luxurious and elegant hotel that is located in the heart of the city, overlooking St. Isaac’s Cathedral. The rooms are spacious and well-appointed, and the hotel offers a variety of dining options and leisure facilities.

Four Seasons Hotel Lion Palace

The Four Seasons Hotel Lion Palace is a prestigious hotel that is housed in a restored 19th-century palace. The rooms are elegant and spacious, and the hotel offers a range of amenities, including a spa, fitness center, and fine dining restaurant.

Hostels

Soul Kitchen Hostel

Soul Kitchen Hostel is a cozy and friendly hostel that is located in the heart of the city. The rooms are clean and comfortable, and the staff is welcoming and helpful. The hostel offers a range of activities and events, such as cooking classes and movie nights.

Friends Hostel

Friends Hostel is a budget-friendly hostel that is located in a historic building. The rooms are basic but comfortable, and the hostel offers a shared kitchen and lounge area. The staff is friendly and helpful, and the hostel is a great option for solo travelers and budget-conscious visitors.

What to Buy in South: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Amber Jewelry

Amber is a semiprecious stone that is found in abundance in the Baltic region. It is often used to make beautiful jewelry, such as earrings, necklaces, and bracelets. Visitors can find a wide selection of amber jewelry in the local markets and shops.

Matryoshka Dolls

Matryoshka dolls, also known as Russian nesting dolls, are a popular souvenir and a symbol of Russian culture. They are made of wood and consist of a set of wooden dolls that fit inside each other.

Local Culture and Customs of South

Russian culture is rich and diverse, and visitors are encouraged to embrace the local customs and traditions. It is important to dress modestly when visiting religious sites and to remove your shoes before entering someone’s home. Russians are known for their love of hospitality and good food, so be prepared to eat and drink well during your visit.

Best Time to Visit South

The best time to visit South is during the summer months, from June to August, when the weather is warm and sunny. However, this is also the busiest time of the year, with large crowds and higher prices. Alternatively, visitors can consider visiting during the spring or fall months when the weather is mild and the crowds are thinner.

Do’s and Don’ts of South

Do:

  • Learn some Russian phrases before your visit.
  • Try the local cuisine and beverages.
  • Respect the local customs and traditions.
  • Visit the famous landmarks and cultural attractions.

Don’t:

  • Smoke in public areas.
  • Snap pictures in churches without permission.
  • Be loud and disrespectful in public places.

Safety and Health

Safety in South

Saint Petersburg is generally a safe city for visitors, but it is important to take precautions, such as avoiding dark or isolated areas at night, and keeping your valuables secure. Visitors should also take care when crossing the street, as traffic can be unpredictable.

Health in South

Visitors should be up-to-date on their routine vaccinations, such as measles, mumps, and rubella, and hepatitis A and B. It is also advisable to bring any necessary medications, such as insect repellent and sunscreen, and to take care when eating street food.

Getting to South: Transportation Options and Logistics

Visitors can arrive in Saint Petersburg via the Pulkovo International Airport, which is located about 15 km south of the city center. From there, visitors can take a taxi, bus, or metro to their destination in the South district. The city also has several bus and railway stations, which provide access to other cities in Russia and Europe.

Visitors can easily get around South on foot, as many of the attractions and landmarks are within walking distance of each other. Alternatively, visitors can take a taxi, bus, or metro to their destination.

Where to Go Next from South: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Vyborg

Vyborg is a historic town that is located about 120 km northwest of Saint Petersburg. The town is known for its beautiful castle, stunning landscapes, and rich cultural heritage.

Kronstadt

Kronstadt is a picturesque island that is located about 30 km west of Saint Petersburg. The island is known for its naval history and stunning scenery, and visitors can take a boat or ferry to explore the island’s unique attractions.

Conclusion

South is a vibrant and exciting district in Saint Petersburg, Russia, that offers visitors a unique blend of culture, history, nature, and adventure. Whether you’re a fan of museums, outdoor activities, or simply seeking a new experience, South has something for everyone. With its stunning architecture, scenic beauty, and warm hospitality, South is a destination that is sure to leave a lasting impression on any traveler.
