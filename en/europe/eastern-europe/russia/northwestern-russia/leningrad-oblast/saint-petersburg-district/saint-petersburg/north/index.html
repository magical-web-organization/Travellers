Introduction to North

North is a district in Saint Petersburg, Leningrad Oblast, Northwestern Russia. It is a relatively small area but houses several phenomenal attractions and natural beauty. A true embodiment of the Russian culture, North has everything from museums to historical places and natural attractions. In this comprehensive travel guide to North, Saint Petersburg, we will provide you with all the necessary information to plan a perfect trip.

List of Places to Visit in North

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • State Hermitage Museum: One of the most famous museums in the world, the State Hermitage Museum is an art and culture haven. It has three million pieces of art, including paintings, sculptures, and antiquities.
  • Peter and Paul Fortress: Built-in 1703, the Peter and Paul fortress is a cultural landmark that symbolizes the city. The fortress houses several museums and exhibitions related to the history of Saint Petersburg and Russia.
  • Catherine Palace: Once a residence of the Russian Emperors, Catherine Palace is an architectural masterpiece. The palace houses several rooms and exhibitions, including the famous Amber Room.

Natural Attractions of North: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Saint Petersburg’s Waterfront: One of the most beautiful attractions in Saint Petersburg, the city’s waterfront offers scenic views of the city’s canals and bridges.
  • Petrovskaya Aquatoria: Petrovskaya Aquatoria is a water park that offers several adventurous aquatic activities to tourists. It also has several indoor activities, including mini-golf, bowling, and a climbing wall.
  • Sestroretskiy Bor: Sestroretskiy Bor is a pine forest park and nature reserve in North. The park houses several walking and cycling trails, a beach, and a lake.

Hiking/Biking Routes of North

  • Primorsky Victory Park: This park offers a 10-kilometer cycling path that runs throughout the park, offering fantastic views of the Gulf of Finland.
  • Udelny Park: Udelny Park has several walking and jogging trails that run throughout the park. It is also one of the best places to explore the nature reserve of North.
  • Nevsky Forest Park: Covering over 200 hectares, Nevsky Forest Park offers excellent hiking trails and scenic views of its natural surroundings.

Best Things to Do in North: Activities and Tours

  • Boat Tours of the Canals of Saint Petersburg: Boat tours are an excellent way to explore the canals and rivers of Saint Petersburg. You can witness the beauty of the city from its waterways.
  • Russian Ballet in Mikhailovsky Theatre: The Mikhailovsky Theatre is a renowned opera and ballet venue in Saint Petersburg. It is an excellent place to experience cultural performances.
  • Banya Experience at North: The Banya experience is a traditional Russian sauna, known for its unique health benefits. Several places in North, including Sanduny Banya, offer this experience.

Week-Long Itinerary for North

Day 1: Visit the State Hermitage Museum and explore Nevsky Prospekt. Day 2: Visit Peterhof Palace and Gardens. Day 3: Visit Catherine Palace and Town of Pushkin. Day 4: Explore Petrovskaya Aquatoria and North’s beaches. Day 5: Take a boat tour of Saint Petersburg’s Canals. Day 6: Hike or cycle along Petrpavlovskiy Island and beach. Day 7: Explore Sestroretskiy Bor or enjoy a Banya experience.

Food and Drink Scene of North

Must-Try Dishes

  • Borscht: A traditional Russian beet soup, often served with sour cream.
  • Varenyky: A Ukrainian dumpling stuffed with potatoes, cheese, or meat.
  • Pelmeni: A type of dumpling, similar to Polish Pierogi, stuffed with meat and served with sour cream.

Local Beverages

  • Russian Vodka: A traditional alcoholic beverage, made from distilled grains or potatoes.
  • Kompot: A non-alcoholic beverage made from boiled fruits and containing an array of natural flavors.
  • Kvass: A fermented drink made from bread, water, sugar, and yeast.

Where to Stay in North: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Grand Hotel Europe: A luxury hotel located in the heart of Saint Petersburg.
  • Nevsky Grand Hotel: Centrally located near all the major attractions of the city.
  • Hostel Rusland: A budget-friendly hostel located in the central district of North.

What to Buy in North: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Matryoshka Dolls: Traditional wooden dolls that fit inside one another.
  • Baltic Amber Jewelry: Amber jewelry made from fossilized tree resin found in the Baltic Sea.
  • Russian Shawls and Scarves: Scarves and wraps made of traditional Russian fabric, often embellished with intricate embroidery.

Local Culture and Customs of North

Russia is a collectivist society, which means that people have strong ties to their family, community, and nation. It is customary to address people formally by their name and patronymic (derived from the father’s name). Russians also value punctuality and respect their history and culture.

Best Time to Visit North

The best time to visit North is during the summer months from June to August. The weather is pleasant, and several outdoor activities are open during this period. The winter months, from December to February, are also a good time to visit if you are interested in winter sports and activities.

Do’s and Don’ts of North

Do:

  • Learn a few Russian phrases.
  • Dress appropriately when visiting religious places and museums.
  • Respect the cultural traditions of the region.

Don’t:

  • Drink tap water.
  • Use currency exchanges at airports and train stations.
  • Take photographs where signs prohibit photography.

Safety and Health

Safety in North

It is generally safe to travel to North. However, you should remain vigilant, especially in crowded areas such as train stations and tourist spots. It is also essential to take care of your belongings and not leave them unattended in public places.

Health in North

The healthcare system in Russia is free but can be unreliable in some cases. It is recommended that tourists purchase comprehensive travel insurance that covers medical emergencies. The tap water is not safe to drink, so it is essential to drink bottled water.

Getting to North: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near North

  • Pulkovo International Airport: The primary airport serving Saint Petersburg.

Bus Stations Near North

  • Moskovskaya Bus Station: One of the largest bus stations in Saint Petersburg.

Other Options

  • Train: The railway station in North is well connected to all the major cities in Russia.

How to Get Around in North

The best way to get around North is by using the metro and bus system. The metro system is modern, efficient, and cost-effective. Taxis and car rentals are also available, but they can be quite expensive.

Where to Go Next from North: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Moscow: The capital city of Russia, Moscow is known for its stunning architecture, art, and rich history.
  • Karelia: A region known for its natural beauty, Karelia has several hiking trails, waterfalls, and other natural attractions.
  • Veliky Novgorod: A historic city located between Moscow and Saint Petersburg, Veliky Novgorod is known for its ancient architecture and rich history.

Conclusion

North, Saint Petersburg, is a unique blend of history, culture, and nature. It is a must-visit destination for anyone interested in exploring Russia’s rich history and culture. We hope this comprehensive travel guide will help you plan your trip to North and make the most of your travel experience.
