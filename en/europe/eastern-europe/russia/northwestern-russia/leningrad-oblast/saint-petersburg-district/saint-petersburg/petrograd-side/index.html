Introduction to Petrograd Side

Petrograd Side is a district of Saint Petersburg located on the left bank of the Neva River. It is one of the oldest parts of the city and is home to many historical and cultural landmarks. The district is packed with stunning architecture, beautiful parks, museums, and unique shops and restaurants.

List of Places to Visit in Petrograd Side

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Peter and Paul Fortress - This 18th-century fortress is one of the most famous landmarks in Saint Petersburg and was used as a political prison during the Tsarist era.
  • Kirov Museum - Originally built as a palace for Prince Menshikov, the Kirov Museum has been home to many different institutions. Today it is a branch of the State Hermitage Museum.
  • Museum of Political History - Shows the development of political life in Russia from the 18th century to the present day. Located in the former mansion of the industrialist M.K. Tenishev.
  • Artillery Museum - The largest artillery museum in the world with more than 850,000 exhibits in its collection.

Natural Attractions of Petrograd Side: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Petrogradskaya Storona Embankment - Take a leisurely walk along the beautiful embankment, while enjoying views of the Neva River.
  • Yelagin Island - A beautiful park located on an island in the Neva River that offers various outdoor activities throughout the year, including skiing in winter.
  • Krestovsky Island - Known as the “Island of the Fun,” this amusement park is a popular destination for both adults and children.
  • Botanical Garden of St. Petersburg University - This beautiful garden is home to an extensive collection of plants from all over the world, including tropical trees, flowers, and cacti.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Petrograd Side

  • Krasin Trail - A 7-kilometer trail that runs along the banks of the Neva River and offers great views of the city’s skyline.
  • Lakhta-Ozerskoye Trail - A 30-kilometer trail that takes you through the outskirts of the city and offers some beautiful countryside scenery.
  • Yelagin Island Trail - A 3-kilometer trail that takes you through the beautiful park of Yelagin Island and offers great views of the river.

Best Things to Do in Petrograd Side: Activities and Tours

  • Boat Tour - Take a boat tour along the Neva River to see many of the city’s landmarks from a different perspective.
  • Walking Tour - Explore the city’s history and architecture on a guided walking tour.
  • Ghost Tour - Learn about the ghosts and legends of Petrograd Side on a spooky evening walk.

Food and Drink Scene of Petrograd Side

Must-Try Dishes

  • Borsch - A soup made with beets, vegetables, and meat.
  • Pelmeni - Meat-filled dumplings that are boiled or fried.
  • Pirozhki - Small pastry pies filled with a variety of savoury or sweet fillings.

Local Beverages

  • Vodka - Russia’s national drink is best enjoyed with traditional Russian snacks.
  • Kvas - A sweet and sour drink made from fermented bread.

Where to Stay in Petrograd Side: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Petrogradsky District - Located near many of the city’s landmarks, this district has a variety of accommodation options, ranging from luxury hotels to budget hostels.

What to Buy in Petrograd Side: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Russian dolls - A traditional souvenir that comes in various sizes and designs.
  • Amber jewelry - Amber is a popular semi-precious stone found in the region.

Local Culture and Customs of Petrograd Side

  • Russians are known for their hospitable nature and visitors are often welcomed with open arms.
  • It is considered polite to bring a small gift when visiting someone’s home.

Best Time to Visit Petrograd Side

The best time to visit Petrograd Side is during the summer months (June-August) when the weather is mild and many outdoor activities are available. It is also the peak tourist season, so be prepared for larger crowds and higher prices.

Do’s and Don’ts of Petrograd Side

Do:

  • Visit the city’s landmarks and museums.
  • Explore the city’s parks and gardens.

Don’t:

  • Take photographs of military installations or personnel.
  • Drink alcohol in public places.

Safety and Health

Safety in Petrograd Side

Petrograd Side is generally a safe district, but as with any large city, visitors should exercise caution and be aware of their surroundings.

Health in Petrograd Side

Medical facilities in Russia are generally of a good standard, but healthcare can be expensive for visitors without travel insurance.

Getting to Petrograd Side: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Petrograd Side

  • Pulkovo Airport (LED) is the closest airport to Petrograd Side, located approximately 20km away.

Bus Stations Near Petrograd Side

  • The Volkhov Bus Station is located in the district of Petrograd Side and offers bus services to other cities and regions in Russia.

Other Options

  • Metro - The city’s metro system is efficient and convenient.
  • Taxi - Taxis are readily available but can be expensive.

How to Get Around in Petrograd Side

The best way to get around Petrograd Side is by public transportation. The city’s metro system is efficient, affordable, and easy to navigate. Visitors can also use buses or trams to explore the district.

Where to Go Next from Petrograd Side: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • The Hermitage Museum - One of the world’s largest and oldest museums, located in the historic centre of Saint Petersburg.
  • Tsarskoye Selo - A former imperial summer residence, located approximately 25km from Petrograd Side.

Conclusion

Petrograd Side is an incredible district of Saint Petersburg, with so much to offer for both tourists and locals alike. From its stunning architecture to its beautiful parks and gardens, there is no shortage of things to see and do. Plan your trip to Petrograd Side today and experience the magic of one of Russia’s most beautiful cities.
