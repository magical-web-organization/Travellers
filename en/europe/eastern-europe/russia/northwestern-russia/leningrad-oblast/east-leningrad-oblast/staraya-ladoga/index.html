Introduction to Staraya Ladoga

Located in East Leningrad Oblast, in the northwestern region of Russia, Staraya Ladoga is a charming town with a rich history and culture. Founded in the 8th century, it was once a bustling trading center and a capital of the Old Rus state. Today, it is a peaceful and picturesque destination that attracts visitors with its scenic views, ancient architecture, and outdoor activities.

List of Places to Visit in Staraya Ladoga

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • The Museum of Staraya Ladoga History and Culture: A fascinating collection of artifacts, documents, and photographs that tell the story of the town’s past and present.
  • The Fortress of Staraya Ladoga: A well-preserved medieval fortress that served as a defensive stronghold during numerous battles and wars throughout history.
  • The Church of St. George: Built in the 12th century, this beautiful white-stone church is a masterpiece of ancient Russian architecture.

Natural Attractions of Staraya Ladoga: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • The Okkervil River: A crystal-clear river that winds its way through the town’s countryside, offering excellent opportunities for fishing, kayaking, and canoeing.
  • The Ladoga Rocks: A unique geological formation that features surreal-looking rock pillars and caves, perfect for hiking and exploring.
  • The Pogost-Zagorskaya Chapel: A charming wooden chapel that sits atop a hill overlooking the town and provides a stunning panoramic view of the surrounding countryside.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Staraya Ladoga

  • The Okkervil River Trail: A scenic hiking and biking trail that follows the Okkervil River from Staraya Ladoga to the nearby town of Podberezye, passing through breathtaking countryside landscapes.
  • The Ladoga Rocks Trail: A challenging hiking trail that takes you through the Ladoga Rocks and offers stunning views of the rock formations and the surrounding valleys.

Best Things to Do in Staraya Ladoga: Activities and Tours

  • The Medieval Festival: Each year, in August, Staraya Ladoga hosts a medieval festival that brings the town’s past to life and offers visitors a unique chance to experience the culture and traditions of ancient Russia.
  • The Fishing Tour: A guided fishing tour that takes you to the best fishing spots on the Okkervil River, where you can catch various species of fish such as trout, grayling, and pike.
  • The Horseback Riding Tour: A tour that offers horseback riding lessons and scenic rides through the beautiful countryside of Staraya Ladoga.

Week-Long Itinerary for Staraya Ladoga

Day 1 - Arrival and Check-In Day 2 - Visit the Museum of Staraya Ladoga History and Culture and the Fortress of Staraya Ladoga. Day 3 - Explore the Ladoga Rocks and the Pogost-Zagorskaya Chapel. Day 4 - Hike the Okkervil River Trail and enjoy a picnic by the river. Day 5 - Take a fishing tour on the Okkervil River. Day 6 - Participate in the Medieval Festival or take a horseback riding tour. Day 7 - Departure.

Food and Drink Scene of Staraya Ladoga

Must-Try Dishes

  • Solyanka: A spicy and sour soup made with smoked meat, vegetables, and pickles.
  • Kulebyaka: A traditional Russian savory pastry filled with meat, fish, or mushrooms.
  • Blinis: Thin pancakes served with sour cream, butter, and jam.

Local Beverages

  • Kvass: A fermented drink made from bread, water, and sugar that has a slightly sour and sweet taste.
  • Medovukha: A mead-like beverage made from honey, water, and spices.

Where to Stay in Staraya Ladoga: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Guesthouse Petr I: A cozy guesthouse located in a historic building in the center of Staraya Ladoga.
  • Hotel Stary Dvor: A modern hotel with comfortable rooms, a sauna, and a restaurant.
  • Hostel Ladoga: An affordable hostel with clean and spacious dorms, a shared kitchen, and free Wi-Fi.

What to Buy in Staraya Ladoga: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Birch bark and wooden crafts: Handmade items, such as birch bark boxes, spoons, and figurines, and wooden toys and utensils.
  • Honey and jam: Delicious, locally produced honey and jams made from natural ingredients.
  • Souvenir badges and magnets: Cute and colorful badges and magnets with images of Staraya Ladoga’s landmarks and symbols.

Local Culture and Customs of Staraya Ladoga

Staraya Ladoga is a town with a unique cultural heritage, deeply rooted in the traditions of the Old Rus state. Visitors are expected to respect the local customs and observe the rules of etiquette. It is customary to remove your shoes before entering someone’s home, to greet people with a handshake, and to avoid discussing politics and religion in public places.

Best Time to Visit Staraya Ladoga

The best time to visit Staraya Ladoga is during the summer months, from June to September, when the weather is mild and pleasant, and the natural landscapes are at their most beautiful. The Medieval Festival takes place in August and is a must-see event for anyone interested in history and culture.

Do’s and Don’ts of Staraya Ladoga

Do:

  • Respect the local customs and traditions.
  • Dress appropriately for the weather and bring sunscreen and insect repellent.
  • Carry cash, as many smaller shops and restaurants do not accept credit cards.

Don’t:

  • Take photographs of people or places without their consent.
  • Smoke or drink alcohol in public places.
  • Litter or damage the natural and cultural landmarks of Staraya Ladoga.

Safety and Health

Safety in Staraya Ladoga

Staraya Ladoga is generally a safe town, with low levels of crime and violence. However, visitors should take basic safety precautions such as keeping their belongings close and avoiding walking alone at night.

Health in Staraya Ladoga

There are no major health risks in Staraya Ladoga. The town has a medical center and several pharmacies that provide basic healthcare services. Visitors should carry their own prescription medications and buy travel insurance before their trip.

Getting to Staraya Ladoga: Transportation Options and Logistics

  • By Air: The closest airport is Pulkovo International Airport in St. Petersburg, which is approximately 120 km away from Staraya Ladoga. From the airport, visitors can take a taxi or a bus to the town.
  • By Bus: Several bus companies operate regular routes to Staraya Ladoga from St. Petersburg and other nearby towns and cities.
  • By Car: Visitors can rent a car in St. Petersburg and drive to Staraya Ladoga, which takes about two hours.

How to Get Around in Staraya Ladoga

The town is small and easy to navigate on foot or by bicycle. Taxis are also available for hire, and there is a local bus service that connects the town’s center with the surrounding villages and neighborhoods.

Where to Go Next from Staraya Ladoga: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Lake Ladoga: The largest lake in Europe with beautiful islands, beaches and fishing spots.
  • Valaam Island: A scenic island in Lake Ladoga with an ancient monastery and a unique culture and traditions.
  • Novgorod: A historic city with impressive architecture, museums, and art galleries.

Conclusion

Staraya Ladoga is a hidden gem of northwestern Russia, a town that offers a perfect blend of natural beauty, cultural heritage, and outdoor activities. Whether you’re a history buff, an adventurer, or a foodie, there’s something for everyone in this charming destination. Plan your trip today and discover the wonders of Staraya Ladoga!
