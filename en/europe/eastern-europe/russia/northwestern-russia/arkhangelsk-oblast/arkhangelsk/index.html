Introduction to Arkhangelsk

Arkhangelsk is a stunning city located in Arkhangelsk Oblast, nestled in the northwest region of Russia. The city has a rich history and is a hub for culture, art, and adventure. The people of Arkhangelsk are warm and friendly, making the city a perfect destination for anyone looking to explore Russian culture beyond the popular cities.

In this travel guide, we will explore the best places to visit, the unique food and drink scene, where to stay, and the best activities to do in Arkhangelsk. We’ll also provide helpful information about transportation options, health and safety, and local customs to help you make the most out of your visit.

List of Places to Visit in Arkhangelsk

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

1. Solovetsky Monastery

The Solovetsky Monastery is a UNESCO World Heritage site located in the Solovetsky Islands of the White Sea in Arkhangelsk Oblast. It was founded in the 15th century and served as a famous spiritual center of the Russian Orthodox Church. The monastery is a testament to the history, culture, and religion of Arkhangelsk Oblast.

2. Museum of Fine Arts

The Museum of Fine Arts in Arkhangelsk displays a vast collection of Russian and European art from various art movements and periods. The building of the museum is an architectural masterpiece in itself, and it is a must-visit for all art enthusiasts.

3. Archangel Michael’s Church

The church is an important historical site built in the 17th century. It features stunning architecture with a beautiful exterior, and it is one of the most beautiful landmarks of the city.

Natural Attractions of Arkhangelsk: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

1. Northern Dvina River

The Northern Dvina River is a great spot to relax and enjoy the stunning views. You can enjoy a boat ride during the summer or watch the sunset over the riverbanks.

2. Island of Morzhovets

Morzhovets Island is a popular spot for nature lovers and hikers. The island features a beautiful lighthouse built-in 1916, stunning coastal views, and a great picnic area.

3. Kenozersky National Park

The Kenozersky National Park is one of the largest protected areas in Russia. It is home to numerous rare and endangered species of plants and animals. The park has a network of hiking trails that will take you through stunning landscapes and diverse ecosystems.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Arkhangelsk

1. Solovetsky Islands

The Solovetsky Islands offer some of the best hiking and biking opportunities in Arkhangelsk. You can explore the stunning landscapes, historic landmarks, and unique flora and fauna that can be found nowhere else in the world.

2. Pinega Nature Reserve

The Pinega Nature Reserve features miles of hiking and biking trails, which traverse the stunning forests, rivers, and wetlands. You can spot rare wildlife, bird species, and enjoy the breathtaking views of the rolling hills and valleys.

3. National Park “Russian North”

The National Park “Russian North” is a great spot for nature and outdoor enthusiasts. This park features many scenic hiking and biking trails and a range of activities, including horseback riding, fishing, and camping.

Best Things to Do in Arkhangelsk: Activities and Tours

1. Northern Lights

For a unique experience, visit Arkhangelsk from November to March to view the Northern Lights. The vibrant colors dance across the night skies, making the experience unforgettable.

2. Borvikha Ski Resort

Arkhangelsk’s winter season provides a great opportunity for skiing, and the Borvikha Ski Resort is the best winter sports destination in the city. The resort is located in the Pinega Nature Reserve, which makes for a stunning winter escape.

3. Shopping at the Arkhangelsk Market

The Arkhangelsk Market offers a range of local products and goods. You can shop for souvenirs, unique arts and crafts, traditional clothing, and local produce.

Week-Long Itinerary for Arkhangelsk

Day 1: Explore the city center and visit the Archangel Michael’s Church.

Day 2: Explore Solovetsky Islands and visit Solovetsky Monastery.

Day 3: Take a day trip to Kenozersky National Park for hiking and nature walks.

Day 4: Visit the Museum of Fine Arts in Arkhangelsk.

Day 5: Go skiing at the Borvikha Ski Resort.

Day 6: Enjoy the Northern Lights, one of the city’s main attractions.

Day 7: Go shopping at the Arkhangelsk Market, followed by a delicious meal in a local restaurant.

Food and Drink Scene of Arkhangelsk

Must-Try Dishes

1. Kulebyaka

Kulebyaka is a traditional Russian pie filled with fish, meat, or mushrooms. The dish is usually served during large celebrations and is considered one of the city’s signature dishes.

2. Okroshka

Okroshka is a cold soup made with boiled potatoes, cucumbers, eggs, and meat or fish. It’s perfect for the sweltering summer heat.

3. Russian Tea

Russian Tea is a popular drink in Arkhangelsk, made from a variety of herbs, berries, and flowers. It is usually served with honey and lemon for a sweet and tangy taste.

Local Beverages

1. Chaga Mushroom Tea

Chaga Mushroom Tea is a popular drink in Arkhangelsk, and it’s known for its numerous health benefits. The drink is made from the birch fungus, which grows on trees in the northern regions of Russia.

2. Kvass

Kvass is a fermented drink made from bread, sugar, and water. It’s a popular drink in Russia and is known for its refreshing and hydrating properties.

Where to Stay in Arkhangelsk: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Arkhangelsk Hotels

1. Hotel Argo

Hotel Argo is a modern, stylish hotel located in the city center. The hotel features comfortable rooms, a restaurant, and a bar.

2. Hotel Stolitsa Pomorja

Hotel Stolitsa Pomorja offers great views of the Northern Dvina River. The hotel is a comfortable and affordable option for visitors who want to be close to the city’s attractions.

Arkhangelsk Hostels

1. Hostel Troika

Hostel Troika is a budget-friendly option with bunk-style dorms and private rooms. The hostel features a shared kitchen, lounge, and Wi-Fi.

2. Hostel Seven Nights

Hostel Seven Nights is a modern and cozy hostel located in the city center. The hostel offers comfortable beds, a shared kitchen, and a lounge area.

Guesthouses

1. Sadko Guesthouse

Sadko Guesthouse is a traditional Russian-style guesthouse located in the city center. The guesthouse features comfortable rooms, a shared kitchen, and free Wi-Fi.

2. Dom 45 Guesthouse

Dom 45 Guesthouse is a modern and stylish guesthouse located in the city center. The guesthouse features comfortable rooms, a shared kitchen, and free Wi-Fi.

What to Buy in Arkhangelsk: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

1. Wooden souvenirs and crafts

Arkhangelsk is famous for its incredible wooden crafts, such as Matryoshka dolls, which make for great souvenirs to take home.

2. Pomor honey

Pomor honey is a popular product in Arkhangelsk, made from flowers in the region’s northern forests. It’s a great souvenir for those with a sweet tooth.

3. Pomor fir oil

Pomor fir oil is a traditional product in Arkhangelsk. It’s made from the resin of the Siberian fir tree and is used for a range of medicinal purposes.

Local Culture and Customs of Arkhangelsk

The culture of Arkhangelsk is heavily influenced by the region’s history, religion, and northern geography. The city has a rich musical tradition, and there is a love of dance and traditional folklore. Locals are proud of their cultural heritage, and visitors are encouraged to respect and appreciate their traditions.

Best Time to Visit Arkhangelsk

The best time to visit Arkhangelsk is during the summer months from June to August. These months offer the best weather conditions for outdoor activities and the longest days. The winter months from November to March are also beautiful, with the added bonus of the Northern Lights.

Do’s and Don’ts of Arkhangelsk

Do:

  • Respect the local customs and traditions.
  • Dress appropriately for the weather, especially during the winter months.
  • Try the local food and drink scene.
  • Learn some basic Russian phrases.
  • Take care while hiking and biking in natural areas.
  • Try to learn some history about the city before you visit.

Don’t:

  • Don’t underestimate the cold and harsh winter weather.
  • Don’t disrespect the local culture or traditions.
  • Don’t take photographs of people without their permission.
  • Don’t engage in political discussions.

Safety and Health

Safety in Arkhangelsk

Arkhangelsk is considered a safe destination for travelers. Visitors should still, however, remain aware of their surroundings and belongings, late at night or in secluded areas.

Health in Arkhangelsk

Visitors should ensure that their vaccinations are up to date. It is also advisable to bring some mosquito repellant as some areas are known for being mosquito-prone.

Getting to Arkhangelsk: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Arkhangelsk

Arkhangelsk’s Talagi airport is the closest airport to fly to. It receives regular flights from Moscow and St. Petersburg.

Bus Stations Near Arkhangelsk

The central bus station in Arkhangelsk is located near the train station. Buses run regularly from the city to other towns and cities in the region.

Other Options

Visitors can also reach Arkhangelsk by train or boat via St. Petersburg and Moscow.

How to Get Around in Arkhangelsk

The best way to get around Arkhangelsk is by public transportation. The city has a well-connected network of buses and trams, which are affordable and efficient. Taxis are also readily available.

Where to Go Next from Arkhangelsk: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Mezen

Mezen is a small town located near Arkhangelsk, and it’s a great spot for fishing and hunting.

Kargopol

Kargopol is a charming town known for its ancient wooden architecture and traditional Russian way of life.

Nenets District

The Nenets District is a unique and stunning region located in the far north. It’s home to the Nenets people, who have a rich culture and way of life.

Conclusion

Arkhangelsk is a hidden gem in the north of Russia and offers a unique and exciting experience for visitors. From the rich culture, natural beauty, and outdoor adventures to the food and drink scene, there is something for everyone. We hope this travel guide will help you make the most of your visit to Arkhangelsk and create unforgettable memories!
