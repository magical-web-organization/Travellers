Introduction to Severodvinsk

Severodvinsk is a small city located in the Arkhangelsk Oblast of Northwestern Russia. It is situated at the mouth of the Northern Dvina River, where it empties into the White Sea. This port city is known for its naval shipyards, where most of the Soviet Union’s submarines were built, along with its rich history and cultural heritage.

List of Places to Visit in Severodvinsk

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

If you are a history buff, Severodvinsk has a lot to offer. Visit the following places to get a deeper understanding of the city’s past:

  • Museum of Northern Shipbuilding - This museum showcases the history of shipbuilding in the region and has various exhibits on display. The museum is located near the city center and is easily accessible.

  • Museum of Local Lore - The Museum of Local Lore highlights the culture and traditions of Severodvinsk and the surrounding region. You can find various artifacts, archives, and exhibits here.

  • Monument to the Submariner - The monument is dedicated to the seamen who served in Soviet submarines from Severodvinsk. A visit here is a must to pay respects to the brave submariners.

Natural Attractions of Severodvinsk: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

If you are an outdoor enthusiasts or nature lover, Severodvinsk and its surrounding areas provides visitors a wide range of activities to be enjoyed. We recommend visiting the following places to experience some breathtaking views:

  • Pokrovskaya Hill - This spot provides a stunning panoramic view of the city. The hill gets its name from the nearby Church of the Protection of the Holy Mother of God and is a great spot for picnicking or taking a leisurely stroll.

  • Kola Bay - Situated northwest of the city, Kola Bay will leave visitors in awe with its picturesque views, clear waters, and beaches.

  • Belousov Island - Belousov Island sits in the White Sea and offers visitors the chance to relax on the beach, explore the diverse flora and fauna, and hike its nature trails.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Severodvinsk

If you enjoy hiking or biking, there are plenty of trails throughout the city. Here are some of the best:

  • Pokrovsky Forest Park - Pokrovsky Forest Park has numerous trails for hiking, biking, and cross-country skiing in the winter.

  • Kuznechikha and Korbolikha Trails - These trails offer hikers a chance to explore some stunning scenery and the chance to see some of the local wildlife.

  • Coastal Biking Trail - This trail is recommended for bikers who want to take in the coastal views while riding along the shores of the White Sea.

Best Things to Do in Severodvinsk: Activities and Tours

If you want to make the most of your time in Severodvinsk and experience some unique activities, we recommend the following:

  • Visit the Northern Maritime Museum - This museum features exhibits of all things maritime from the early 20th century to the present day.

  • Take a submarine tour - Visitors can take guided tours around the submarine docks and even get a chance to venture inside a submarine.

  • Experience the Icebreaker Lenin - Take a tour of one of the world’s most powerful icebreakers, the Lenin (which was decommissioned and converted into a museum)

Week-Long Itinerary for Severodvinsk

If you have a week to spare, here’s a suggested itinerary for exploring Severodvinsk:

Day 1: Arrive in Severodvinsk, explore the city center

Day 2: Visit the Museum of Northern Shipbuilding, the Museum of Local Lore, and the Monument to the Submariner

Day 3: Take a hike or bike tour on Pokrovsky Forest Park and Kuznechikha Trail

Day 4: Take a tour of the submarine docks and visit the Northern Maritime Museum

Day 5: Experience the Icebreaker Lenin

Day 6-7: Take a day trip to Belousov Island and Kola Bay

Food and Drink Scene of Severodvinsk

Must-Try Dishes

The cuisine of Severodvinsk is rich in seafood and other delicacies from the Northern Dvina River. Some must-try dishes include:

  • Arctic Char - This fish is famous throughout Russia, and you can enjoy it grilled, fried or even baked with some added herbs and spices.

  • Kulebyaka - A savory Russan pastry filled with all sorts of delicious ingredients such as salmon or finely chopped meat

Local Beverages

  • Kvass - This non-alcoholic fermented beverage is made from bread and is a popular traditional drink in Russia.

Where to Stay in Severodvinsk: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

For a more authentic experience, we recommend guests consider staying in a guesthouse or hostel to get an insight into the local culture. However, there are also many great hotels in Severodvinsk, such as:

  • Azimut Hotel - This hotel offers visitors comfortable rooms and excellent service for a reasonable price.

  • Pur-Navolok Hotel - This hotel offers a beautiful view of the city and provides excellent hospitality.

What to Buy in Severodvinsk: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Visitors can take home some unique souvenirs such as:

  • Matryoshka Dolls - This classic Russian souvenir is a must-have for tourists.

  • Northern Dvina River Caviar - Visitors who want to take back something more sophisticated can get their hands on Northern Dvina River’s caviar, one of Severodvinsk’s signature delicacies.

Local Culture and Customs of Severodvinsk

Russian culture is rich with traditions, music, dance, and lots of food, try to celebrate your stay in Severodvinsk to probe the city’s customs and traditions better.

Best Time to Visit Severodvinsk

The best time to visit Severodvinsk is from June to August when the daylight hours are long, and the weather is warm. Winter can be harsh with a considerable amount of snowfall, so visitors have to plan accordingly to make the most of their stay.

Do’s and Don’ts of Severodvinsk

Do:

  • Try to learn some basic Russian phrases before visiting Severodvinsk, as they are not fluent in English.

  • Respect the local traditions, customs and social norms.

Don’t:

  • Do not drink the tap water, only use bottled water.

Safety and Health

Safety in Severodvinsk

Severodvinsk is a relatively safe city but, visitors should keep their wallets safe while traveling, avoid petty theft or pickpocketing.

Health in Severodvinsk

Visitors should take care of their health while traveling. Visitors from different countries are recommended to get checked up before traveling.

Getting to Severodvinsk: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Severodvinsk

  • Talagi Airport - This airport is around 200 kilometers away from Severodvinsk, and there are regular flights from Moscow.

Bus Stations Near Severodvinsk

There are also some public transport options to travel to Severodvinsk, such as regular train services and buses that operate between Arkhangelsk and Severodvinsk.

Other Options

Visitors can also use ferry transportation for their trips.

How to Get Around in Severodvinsk

The best way to explore Severodvinsk is via car rental or hiring a taxi, although, the city also has public transport services that visitors can use for getting around Severodvinsk.

Where to Go Next from Severodvinsk: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

If you want to continue exploring after your visit in Severodvinsk, here are some nearby destinations and excursions worth visiting:

  • Arkhangelsk - the largest city in the Arkhangelsk region and is known for its historical landmarks, cultural heritage, and a variety of outdoor activities.

  • Solovetsky Islands - a group of islands situated in the White Sea is home to various historical landmarks such as the Solovetsky Monastery, a UNESCO World Heritage Site.

Conclusion

Severodvinsk is a unique city that offers visitors the chance to explore Russian history and culture, experience the city’s stunning natural beauty, and try some exquisite local cuisine. We hope our guide will help you in exploring all the things that Severodvinsk has to offer.
