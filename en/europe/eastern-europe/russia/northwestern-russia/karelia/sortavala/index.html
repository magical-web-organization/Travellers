Introduction to Sortavala

Sortavala is a bustling city in Karelia, situated in northwestern Russia, right near Lake Ladoga. The city boasts a rich history as it was founded in the early 17th century, and has been a subject of political and cultural shifts over the years. Today, Sortavala is a popular tourist destination, thanks to its museums, historical sites, natural attractions, hiking and biking routes, and other numerous activities to keep every visitor captivated.

List of Places to Visit in Sortavala

Sortavala has numerous attractions that cater to a wide range of interests. Here is a list of must-see destinations in Sortavala:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • The Sortavala Museum Reserve: This museum highlights the cultural and historical legacy of Karelia, and is a must-visit for anyone interested in regional history. It houses over 58,000 exhibits, including archaeological findings, folk costumes, and more.
  • The Museum of Porcelain: This museum features various porcelains made from the local factory and collections from different time periods from across the entire country.
  • The Sortavala Lutheran Church: This historic church dates back to 1684 and is one of the oldest wooden buildings in Karelia. It has a splendid interior and a high bell tower where one can catch spectacular views of the surrounding skyline.

Natural Attractions of Sortavala: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • The Ruskeala Mountain Park: This beautiful park features lake cruises, gemstone and marble workshops, and hiking trails that ensure visitors have unforgettable experiences.
  • The Valaam Archipelago: The archipelago is situated on the extensive area of Lake Ladoga and is known for its serene monasteries, untouched forests, and clear waters. Visitors can join a guided boat trip or walk around the islands to watch their historic structures.
  • Kivach Nature Reserve: This nature reserve is located around the Kivach waterfall, and it is home to over 400 plant species, 100 species of birds, and many mammals. It is perfect for nature lovers and photographers.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Sortavala

Sortavala offers numerous hiking and biking options that will satisfy adventurous spirits. Some popular trails include:

  • Kulta-Koti trail: This path is moderately challenging and has around 8km. It features stunning views of the Kulta-Koti ravine, which is a popular spot for swimming.
  • Kivach Trail: It’s a short distance trail of about 2km suitable for visitors who want to take it easy or visitors who are with children. The trail showcases wonderful views of the Kivach waterfall.
  • Valaam Island walking tour: This option is a walking tour around Valaam Island, which offers breathtaking views of the Valaam archipelago and its lush forests. It is ideal for experienced hikers or visitors who want to undertake a more challenging route.

Best Things to Do in Sortavala: Activities and Tours

Sortavala is packed with activities and tours that cater to various interests. Here is a list of some of the best things to do in Sortavala:

  • Try kayaking or canoeing on lake Ladoga.
  • Visit the local market area and sample some of the local delicacies and street food.
  • Go Fishing on Lake Ladoga which is the largest lake in the European continent.
  • Visit the local spa and enjoy a Finnish sauna.
  • Join a guided trip to the Marble Canyon.

Week-Long Itinerary for Sortavala

A week is a good amount of time to explore many of Sortavala’s great attractions, with some free time built-in to relax or explore. Here is a sample itinerary for a week in Sortavala:

Day 1: Arrival

Arrive at the airport, check-in at your preferred accommodation, and explore the city center.

Day 2: Museums and Churches

Visit the Porcelain Museum and the Sortavala Lutheran Church.

Day 3: Valaam Archipelago

Join a guided boat trip and explore the beautiful Valaam archipelago.

Day 4: Kivach Nature Reserve

Set out for a hiking trip at the Kivach Nature Reserve, and take in the breathtaking scenery and waterfalls.

Day 5: The Ruskeala Mountain Park

Visit the Ruskeala Mountain Park and explore the marble canyon and learn more about the local geology and history.

Day 6: Marina Strand

Relax and enjoy the sun at the Marina Strand on the banks of Lake Ladoga.

Day 7: Departure

Pack up and say goodbye to Sortavala as you head back to your next destination.

Food and Drink Scene of Sortavala

Sortavala’s food scene is best described as diverse, focusing on local cuisine. Here are some must-try dishes and beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Karelian pies: This is a popular snack that contains rice, potatoes, carrots, or turnips in rye flour pastry.

Local Beverages

  • Hapanolut: This is a traditional beer fermented using rye bread, smoked malt, and barley malt, which gives it a unique taste.

Where to Stay in Sortavala: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Sortavala has a wide range of accommodations to fit every budget. Here are some top-rated options:

  • Spa-hotel Valvilla. This hotel offers a swimming pool, fitness center, and Finnish sauna facilities.
  • Marinanranta Guesthouse. This guesthouse features an outdoor sauna, barbeque facilities, and bike rental.
  • Hostel Stariy Gorod. This hostel is within close proximity to many of the city’s attractions.

What to Buy in Sortavala: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Sortavala is known for its handcrafted ceramics, as well as Karelian handicrafts. Be sure to pick up the following souvenirs before you leave:

  • Handmade ceramics and plates
  • Birchwood utensils and baskets
  • Replicas of historical Karelian jewelry

Local Culture and Customs of Sortavala

Sortavala is a multicultural city, with most of its residents speaking a unique Karelian language, alongside Russian. The indigenous ethnic group of the Karelians in Sortavala are known for their singing, music, and their traditional costumes. Visiting during one of their annual festivals can provide an unforgettable cultural experience that is worth your time.

Best Time to Visit Sortavala

The best time to visit Sortavala is during summer when you can enjoy the city’s many outdoor activities and explore its natural wonders without feeling too cold.

Do’s and Don’ts of Sortavala

Do:

  • Dress in layers to keep warm and comfortable in different weather conditions.
  • Respect the local customs and traditions, especially when visiting sacred or religious places.
  • Carry cash as most local marketplaces prefer it over card payments.

Don’t:

  • Insult or ridicule local custom norms.
  • Take photographs without permission, especially in sensitive or sacred locations.

Safety and Health

Safety in Sortavala

Sortavala prides itself on being a safe tourist destination, with an excellent healthcare system for its residents. Visitors are advised to be careful when engaging in outdoor activities to avoid injuries or accidents.

Health in Sortavala

Visitors should be wary of tick-borne diseases and carry insect repellent at all times. Additionally, staying hydrated and seeking medical attention right away when feeling unwell is crucial.

Getting to Sortavala: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Sortavala

  • Lappeenranta Airport in Finland (129 km).
  • Joensuu Airport in Finland (190 km).
  • Saint Petersburg Pulkovo International Airport (286 km).

Bus Stations Near Sortavala

  • The main bus station is located in Sortavala city center and connects the city with many other cities in the country.

Other Options

Visitors can take the train directly to Sortavala from St. Petersburg Railway Station, or take a taxi or private tour to get to the city from the airport.

How to Get Around in Sortavala

Sortavala has an excellent transportation system, with regular buses, taxis, and hired shuttle services for visitors who want to explore the city.

Where to Go Next from Sortavala: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

There are many attractions to visit near Sortavala. Here are some top-rated destinations visitors can explore once they’re done exploring the city:

  • The cities of Joensuu and Lappeenranta in Finland.
  • The historic city of St. Petersburg, Russia.
  • The Petrozavodsk in the Karelia region.

Conclusion

Sortavala is an excellent destination for anyone who wants to explore Russia’s natural beauty, unique culture and history, and enjoy the local cuisine and hospitality. With many great attractions to explore and a friendly and safe environment, visitors who travel to Sortavala are guaranteed to have an unforgettable experience.
