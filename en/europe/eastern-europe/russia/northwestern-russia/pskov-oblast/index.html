Introduction to Pskov Oblast

Located in Northwestern Russia, Pskov Oblast is a region rich in history, culture, and natural beauty. Bordered by Estonia and Latvia, it’s a unique destination that offers a glimpse into Russia’s past and present.

The region is home to several ancient towns, medieval fortresses, and beautiful forests and lakes, making it a popular destination for those seeking adventure, history, and relaxation in one place.

List of Places to Visit in Pskov Oblast

Here’s a list of top attractions and places to visit in Pskov Oblast:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Pskov Kremlin and its Museums
  • Izborsk Fortress and Museum Reserve
  • Pechory Monastery
  • The Town of Velikie Luki
  • Porkhov Fortress

Natural Attractions of Pskov Oblast: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Lake Pskov
  • Gdov Canyon
  • Siva River Canyon
  • Velikaya River Delta

Best Things to Do in Pskov Oblast: Activities and Tours

  • Visit an authentic Russian banya (sauna)

  • Experience horseback riding through the forests

Week-Long Itinerary for Pskov Oblast

Here’s a suggested itinerary for your week-long trip to Pskov Oblast:

Day 1: Explore Pskov Kremlin and its Museums

Day 2: Take a trip to Izborsk Fortress and Museum Reserve and hike the Izborsk State Reserve Trail

Day 3: Visit Pechory Monastery and take a boat tour on Lake Pskov

Day 4: Biking tour to Lake Molebka

Day 5: Visit Velikiye Luki and its historical sites

Day 6: Relax at a local spa and enjoy a Russian banya experience

Day 7: Attend a traditional folk music concert

Food and Drink Scene of Pskov Oblast

Don’t miss these must-try dishes and local beverages of Pskov Oblast:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Pskov-style pirozhki (pastries with various fillings)
  • Kvas (a traditional Russian beverage made from fermented bread)
  • Fish soup with herbs and vegetables
  • Buckwheat pancakes with caviar

Local Beverages

  • Medovukha (a honey-based alcoholic beverage)
  • Birch or pine sap
  • Herbal teas and infusions

Local Culture and Customs of Pskov Oblast

The people of Pskov Oblast are proud of their rich tradition, which includes:

  • Festivals and celebrations of Russian Orthodox holidays
  • Folk music and dance performances
  • The Russian banya (sauna) culture

Best Time to Visit Pskov Oblast

The best time to visit Pskov Oblast is during the summer months, from June to August, when the weather is warm and sunny. Spring and autumn can also be pleasant, with fewer crowds and more affordable prices.

Do’s and Don’ts of Pskov Oblast

Here are some important things to keep in mind during your visit to Pskov Oblast:

Do:

  • Respect local customs and traditions
  • Dress appropriately when visiting religious sites
  • Sample local cuisine and beverages

Don’t:

  • Drink alcohol in public places
  • Litter or harm the natural environment
  • Photograph or film without permission

Safety and Health

Safety in Pskov Oblast

Pskov Oblast is generally a safe destination for travelers. However, be cautious when traveling alone or at night and keep an eye on your belongings.

Health in Pskov Oblast

The region is relatively safe in terms of health, but it’s always a good idea to take precautions, such as drinking bottled water and avoiding uncooked food.
