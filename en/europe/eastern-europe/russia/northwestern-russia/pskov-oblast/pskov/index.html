A Comprehensive Travel Guide to Pskov, Northwestern Russia

Located in Pskov Oblast in Northwestern Russia, Pskov is a hidden gem waiting to be discovered by travel enthusiasts. This historic city boasts beautiful scenery, rich culture, outdoor adventures, and a welcoming atmosphere. In this comprehensive travel guide, we’ll highlight the best things to do, see, eat and drink, and where to stay in Pskov. We’ll also provide useful information on transportation, safety, and local customs.

Introduction to Pskov

Pskov is a city with a rich history. Its ancient fortress walls have kept the city safe from invaders for centuries. Pskov is also known for its iconic onion-domed churches such as the Trinity Cathedral and the St. Nicolas Cathedral. The city is surrounded by beautiful nature, including Lake Peipus, Russia’s largest freshwater lake.

List of Places to Visit in Pskov

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Pskov offers a wealth of cultural and historical attractions, including museums, cathedrals, and fortresses. Some of the must-visit places are:

  • Pskov Kremlin: The fortress walls are the city’s most recognizable feature, and they date back to the 14th century.
  • Trinity Cathedral: an iconic 15th-century Russian Orthodox church with stunning frescoes and icons.
  • The Museum of Russian Icons: A museum dedicated to some of the oldest and most significant Russian Orthodox icons.
  • Museum of the Pskov-Pechersky Monastery: A museum highlighting the history of the Pskov-Pechersky Monastery, founded in the 15th century.

Natural Attractions of Pskov: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Pskov is surrounded by beautiful nature, making it the perfect destination for outdoor enthusiasts. Some of the must-visit natural attractions are:

  • Lake Peipus: The largest lake in Russia and the fifth-largest in Europe. It is home to a variety of fish species and is a popular spot for fishing and boating.
  • Pskov-Caves Nature Reserve: A protected nature reserve with a unique karst landscape. The reserve features caves, grottoes, and underground lakes.
  • Izborsk State Museum-Reserve: A preserved ancient fortress and village, surrounded by stunning nature.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Pskov

Pskov offers several scenic hiking and biking trails for nature enthusiasts. Some of the best routes are:

  • Pskov-Caves Nature Reserve Hiking Trail: A 3.7-km hiking trail that takes you through some of the reserve’s most scenic spots.
  • The Izborsk Fortress Route: A 13-km route that takes you through Izborsk’s scenic nature reserve.
  • Pskov-Veche Route: A 20-km biking route that takes you through Pskov’s scenic countryside and historical landmarks.

Best Things to Do in Pskov: Activities and Tours

There are many exciting things to do in Pskov, whether you’re a history buff, foodie, or adrenaline junkie. Some of the best activities and tours include:

  • Russian Banya Experience: A relaxing traditional Russian sauna experience paired with Russian herbal tea.
  • Pskov Brewery Tour: A tour of the Pivovarnya Na Pushkakh brewery, where you’ll learn about the brewing process and taste some of their best beers.
  • Pskov City Walking Tour: A guided tour through the city’s most iconic landmarks and historic sites.
  • ATV Tour: An adventurous ATV ride through Pskov’s scenic countryside.

Week-Long Itinerary for Pskov

A week in Pskov will give you ample time to explore the city’s most iconic landmarks and natural wonders. Here’s an example itinerary:

  • Day 1: Visit the Pskov Kremlin and the Trinity Cathedral.
  • Day 2: Go on a hiking or biking expedition through the Pskov-Caves Nature Reserve.
  • Day 3: Explore the Museum of Russian Icons and the Museum of the Pskov-Pechersky Monastery.
  • Day 4: Visit Lake Peipus and enjoy a day of fishing or boating.
  • Day 5: Take a city walking tour of Pskov.
  • Day 6: Visit the Izborsk Fortress, and explore the Izborsk State Museum-Reserve.
  • Day 7: Enjoy a relaxing day at a traditional Russian banya or go on an ATV tour of Pskov’s countryside.

Food and Drink Scene of Pskov

Pskov’s cuisine is influenced by its Eastern European and Russian roots. Some of the must-try dishes and local beverages are:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Kotlety pozharskie: a delicious dish made of minced meat and breaded.
  • Pskov-style pirogis: a local delicacy filled with a mix of meat, mushrooms, and potatoes.
  • Borscht: a classic Russian beetroot soup.

Local Beverages

  • Kvas: A traditional Russian drink made from fermented bread. It has a refreshing taste and a low alcohol content.
  • Kiselyov: A Russian fruit drink made from fermented berries.

Where to Stay in Pskov: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Pskov offers a variety of accommodations to fit any budget, including hotels, hostels, and guesthouses. Some of the best options are:

  • Hotel 903: A modern hotel located in the city center.
  • Old Estate Hotel & SPA: A luxurious hotel with a spa and an outdoor pool.
  • Hostel Okno: A cozy hostel in the city center with affordable rates.
  • Guesthouse Pogost: A charming guesthouse located near the Pskov-Pechersky Monastery.

What to Buy in Pskov: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Pskov is known for its local products, including handmade crafts, jewelry, and textiles. Some of the best souvenirs and local products to bring home are:

  • Pskov Honey: Made from the region’s unique wildflowers, this honey is delicious and healthy.
  • Kholuy Boxes: Hand-painted wooden boxes, popular with collectors worldwide.
  • Linen Textiles: Tablecloths, towels, and clothing made of quality linen, a staple of the region’s economy.

Local Culture and Customs of Pskov

Pskov’s culture is heavily influenced by its Eastern European and Russian roots, which manifest themselves in the city’s architecture, music, and food. Visitors are encouraged to respect local customs, traditions, and dress codes when visiting religious sites.

Best Time to Visit Pskov

The best time to visit Pskov is in late spring and early summer (May-June) or in the fall (September-early November). The weather is mild, and the scenery is beautiful during these seasons.

Do’s and Don’ts of Pskov

Do:

  • Dress in layers.
  • Respect local culture and customs.
  • Try the local food and beverages.
  • Carry small change when using public transportation.

Don’t:

  • Touch religious artifacts or icons.
  • Stand on the left side of the escalator.
  • Smoke in public places.
  • Over-tip waitstaff and service providers.

Safety and Health

Safety in Pskov

Pskov is a safe city for tourists. However, visitors should take precautions to avoid pickpockets and other petty crimes. It’s also advisable to avoid participating in political protests and demonstrations.

Health in Pskov

Tap water in Pskov is safe to drink, but it’s recommended to buy bottled water. Visitors should also beware of tick-borne diseases in the countryside during the summer months.

Getting to Pskov: Transportation Options and Logistics

Pskov is accessible by several means of transportation, including air, train, and bus.

Airports Near Pskov

The nearest airport to Pskov is Pskov Airport, located approximately 7 km east of the city center.

Bus Stations Near Pskov

Pskov Bus Station is the primary bus terminal in the city and is located near the train station.

Other Options

Pskov is also accessible by train from Moscow, St. Petersburg, and other major cities in Russia.

How to Get Around in Pskov

Pskov is a compact city that can be easily navigated on foot. Taxis and public transportation options, including buses and trolleybuses, are also available.

Where to Go Next from Pskov: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Pskov serves as an excellent base for exploring the surrounding regions, including several towns and cities rich in history and culture. Some of the best nearby destinations and excursions are:

  • Velikiye Luki: a charming town with a significant historical past.
  • St. Petersburg: a stunning city with world-famous landmarks, including the Hermitage Museum and the Peterhof Palace.
  • Novgorod: a peaceful town known for its centuries-old historic center and impressive river landscape.

Conclusion

Pskov is a fascinating city that offers much to see and do for travelers of all interests. From its rich history to its beautiful natural surroundings, Pskov is a hidden gem that is well worth a visit. So pack your bags and embark on an unforgettable journey to this historic and beautiful city!
