Nikel is a small town located in Murmansk Oblast, in Northwestern Russia. With its untouched natural landscapes, rich history, and unique culture, Nikel boasts many hidden gems waiting to be uncovered.

Introduction to Nikel

Nikel is situated in the heart of the Kola Peninsula on the banks of the Tuloma River. The town is surrounded by picturesque mountains and forests, providing a perfect backdrop for outdoor adventures. Nikel is home to several industrial enterprises, the most important being the nickel smelting factory, which is a major source of income for the town.

List of Places to Visit in Nikel

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Nikel is steeped in history and culture, with several museums and historical sites to explore. The Nikel Museum is a great place to start your journey, as it showcases the history and development of the town from its earliest times. The museum also has a section dedicated to the indigenous Sami people, highlighting their unique lifestyle and traditions.

The Pechenga Monastery is another popular tourist attraction, which dates back to the 16th century. The monastery houses a collection of ancient icons and religious artifacts and is regarded as an important historical and cultural landmark.

Natural Attractions of Nikel: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Nikel’s natural beauty is a must-see for all visitors. The town is surrounded by stunning mountain ranges and forests, providing perfect opportunities for hiking, cycling, and camping. The Khibiny Mountains are a popular destination for hiking enthusiasts, with several scenic trails that offer breathtaking views of the surrounding landscapes.

The nearby Kola Peninsula is home to several national parks, including the Pasvik Nature Reserve, which houses over 200 species of birds and animals. Another popular attraction is Lake Lovozero, which is surrounded by picturesque mountains and offers a perfect spot for fishing, boating, and relaxing amidst beautiful natural surroundings.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Nikel

For outdoor enthusiasts, Nikel is a heaven on earth! The town boasts several hiking and biking routes that take you through stunning landscapes and offer plenty of opportunities for adventure. Some popular trails include:

  • Khibiny Mountains Trail
  • Lake Lovozero Trail
  • Tuloma River Trail
  • Khibiny Plateau Trail

Best Things to Do in Nikel: Activities and Tours

Apart from natural and cultural attractions, Nikel also offers plenty of activities and tours for visitors. You can go on a guided tour of the nickel factory, which gives you a glimpse into the production and distribution of nickel in Russia. Another popular attraction is dogsledding, which is a traditional form of transportation in the Arctic region.

For those seeking an adrenaline rush, skiing and snowboarding are popular winter time activities. The nearby Khibiny Mountains offer several skiing resorts that cater to both beginners and advanced skiers.

Week-Long Itinerary for Nikel

  1. Day 1: Visit the Nikel Museum and learn about the town’s history and culture.
  2. Day 2: Take a guided tour of the nickel factory and learn about the production and distribution of nickel in Russia.
  3. Day 3: Hike the Khibiny Mountains Trail and enjoy the breath-taking views.
  4. Day 4: Indulge in dogsledding and enjoy the traditional form of transportation in the Arctic region.
  5. Day 5: Relax at Lake Lovozero and enjoy fishing, boating, and swimming.
  6. Day 6: Go skiing or snowboarding in the nearby Khibiny Mountains.
  7. Day 7: Take a day trip to the Pechenga Monastery and explore the ancient icons and religious artifacts.

Food and Drink Scene of Nikel

The local cuisine in Nikel is heavily influenced by Russian and Sami traditions. Don’t forget to try some of these local delights:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Kulebyaka (puff pastry filled with salmon or sturgeon)
  • Kvass (Russian beverage made from bread and yeast)
  • Sami bread (flatbread made from reindeer milk)

Local Beverages

  • Cranberry juice
  • Birch sap
  • Vodka

What to Buy in Nikel: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

If you’re looking to take a piece of Nikel back home with you, these are some of the local souvenirs and products to look out for:

  • Birch bark crafts
  • Wooden sculptures
  • Reindeer hides

Local Culture and Customs of Nikel

The Sami people are an important part of Nikel’s culture and customs. It is important to respect their culture and traditions while visiting the town. You can also witness some of their practices, such as reindeer herding and folk dancing.

Best Time to Visit Nikel

The best time to visit Nikel is from June to August, when the weather is mild and pleasant. During this time, you can also witness the phenomenon of the midnight sun, where the sun doesn’t set for several weeks. The winter months, from December to February, offer a chance to see the northern lights.

Do’s and Don’ts of Nikel

Do:

  • Respect the local culture and customs
  • Carry warm clothing, especially during winters
  • Try the local cuisine and drinks

Don’t:

  • Take photographs of the Sami people without permission
  • Litter in the natural surroundings
  • Ignore safety measures during outdoor activities

Safety and Health

Safety in Nikel

Nikel is a safe town, with a relatively low crime rate. However, it is important to take necessary precautions, such as keeping your valuables safe and avoiding secluded areas at night.

Health in Nikel

There are several medical facilities in Nikel, offering basic healthcare services. However, it is recommended to carry a basic medical kit and necessary medicines while visiting the town.

Getting to Nikel: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Nikel

The nearest airport to Nikel is the Murmansk International Airport, which is located around 200 km away. You can hire a taxi or take a bus to reach Nikel from the airport.

Bus Stations Near Nikel

There are several bus stations in Murmansk, from where you can take a bus to reach Nikel.

Other Options

You can also hire a car or take a train to reach Nikel. However, train services are limited, and it is recommended to check the schedule beforehand.

How to Get Around in Nikel

You can explore the town on foot, or hire a taxi to travel to different destinations within the town. It is important to keep warm clothing handy, especially during winters.

Where to Go Next from Nikel: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

The Kola Peninsula offers several nearby destinations and excursions that are worth visiting, such as:

  • The Arctic Ocean and its stunning landscapes
  • The Sami villages of Lovozero and Krasnoselskaya
  • The monument to the lost nuclear submarine, Kursk, in the city of Murmansk.

Conclusion

Nikel offers a unique blend of culture, history, outdoor adventures, and natural beauty. Whether you’re looking to explore the town’s historical sites or hike through its stunning landscapes, Nikel has something to offer for everyone. With its welcoming locals, rich culture, and unforgettable experiences, Nikel makes for a perfect travel destination.
