Your Complete Travel Guide to Umba, Russia

Are you looking for an off-the-beaten-path destination in North Western Russia? Look no further than Umba! This small town in the Murmansk Oblast region of Russia is not only rich in natural beauty, but also in cultural history. In this complete travel guide, we will explore the best places to visit, things to do, and cultural customs in Umba.

Introduction to Umba

Umba is a small town located in the Murmansk Oblast region of North Western Russia. It is situated on the shore of the Umba River and is surrounded by beautiful mountains, forests, and lakes. The town has a population of just over 5,000 people and is known for its natural beauty and outdoor activities.

List of Places to Visit in Umba

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Umba Museum: Learn about the history of Umba and the surrounding area at this local museum. The exhibits include artifacts from local indigenous tribes, as well as displays on the town’s history and culture.
  • The Church of the Assumption of the Blessed Virgin Mary: This 19th-century Russian Orthodox church is a must-visit for history and architecture buffs. The church’s beautiful domes and intricate interior decorations make it a stunning example of Russian Orthodox architecture.

Natural Attractions of Umba: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Umba River: Fishing enthusiasts will love the Umba River, which is known for its salmon fishing. The river also offers kayaking and rafting opportunities for those looking for a more adventurous experience.
  • Umba Waterfalls: These picturesque waterfalls are located near the town and are a great spot for hiking and photography.
  • Umba Mountains: The Umba Mountains offer beautiful views of the town and the surrounding area. Hiking trails range from easy to difficult, so there is something for hikers of all levels.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Umba

  • Kandalaksha Nature Reserve Hiking Trail: This trail, located near Umba, takes hikers through beautiful forests and offers stunning views of the surrounding mountains and lakes.
  • Umba River Bike Trail: This 40 km trail follows the Umba River through the stunning North Russian landscape. It is a great option for those who want to experience the beauty of Umba on two wheels.

Best Things to Do in Umba: Activities and Tours

  • Salmon Fishing: Umba is known for its world-class salmon fishing. Guided tours are available for those who want to try their hand at catching these impressive fish.
  • Guided Hiking Tours: Explore the Umba Mountains and surrounding area with a local guide who can show you the best spots for stunning views and natural beauty.

Week-Long Itinerary for Umba

Day 1: Arrive in Umba and check into your hotel. Day 2: Visit the Umba Museum and the Church of the Assumption of the Blessed Virgin Mary. Day 3: Go hiking in the Umba Mountains. Day 4: Take a guided tour of the Kandalaksha Nature Reserve. Day 5: Try your hand at salmon fishing on the Umba River. Day 6: Bike along the Umba River Bike Trail. Day 7: Relax and enjoy the natural beauty of Umba before departing the next day.

Food and Drink Scene of Umba

Must-Try Dishes

  • Rasstegai (Russian meat pies)
  • Salmon Soup
  • Pelmeni (Russian dumplings)

Local Beverages

  • Kvass (a low-alcohol, fermented drink made from bread)
  • Russian Vodka

Where to Stay in Umba: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Umba Lodge: This cozy guesthouse is located near the Umba River and offers stunning views of the surrounding mountains. The rooms are comfortable and the staff is friendly and helpful.
  • Umba Hotel: This centrally located hotel offers comfortable rooms and all the amenities you need for a relaxing stay in Umba.

What to Buy in Umba: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Salmon: Bring home some of Umba’s famous salmon as a delicious and unique souvenir.
  • Traditional crafts: Umba is known for its traditional crafts such as woven baskets and pottery.

Local Culture and Customs of Umba

Umba is a predominantly Russian town with a rich cultural history and customs. Visitors should be respectful of the local traditions and customs, such as dressing modestly when visiting churches and public places.

Best Time to Visit Umba

The best time to visit Umba is in the summer months of June to August, when the weather is mild and the natural beauty of the area is at its best. The winter months can be quite cold and snowy, but also offer unique winter sports opportunities.

Do’s and Don’ts of Umba

Do:

  • Take a guided hiking tour to fully appreciate the natural beauty of Umba.
  • Try the local cuisine, including the salmon.

Don’t:

  • Wear inappropriate clothing when visiting churches and public places.
  • Ignore the local customs and traditions of Umba.

Safety and Health

Safety in Umba

Umba is a safe place to visit, but visitors should be aware of the potential dangers of outdoor activities such as hiking, fishing, and kayaking. Visitors should also be aware of the cold winter weather and dress appropriately.

Health in Umba

Visitors should be up to date on their routine vaccinations before traveling to Umba. Water should be boiled or treated before drinking, and visitors should be aware of the potential for tick-borne illnesses when participating in outdoor activities.

Getting to Umba: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Umba

The nearest major airport to Umba is Murmansk Airport, which is located approximately 250 km from Umba.

Bus Stations Near Umba

Regular bus service is available from Murmansk to Umba, which takes approximately 5 hours.

Other Options

Private car rentals are also available for those who want to drive to Umba from nearby cities or towns.

How to Get Around in Umba

The town of Umba is small and easy to navigate on foot. For those who want to explore the surrounding area, private car rentals and guided tours are available.

Where to Go Next from Umba: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Murmansk: This nearby city is known for its beautiful northern lights displays.
  • Kandalaksha: This small town is located near the Kandalaksha Nature Reserve and offers beautiful natural scenery.

Conclusion

Umba is a hidden gem in Russia’s northwestern Murmansk Oblast region. Its natural beauty, cultural history, and outdoor activities make it a must-visit destination for any traveler looking for an off-the-beaten-path experience in Russia. With this comprehensive guide, you’ll be able to plan your perfect trip to Umba and experience all that this unique town has to offer.
