Introduction to Totma

Nestled in the heart of Vologda Oblast in Northwestern Russia, Totma is a charming town that’s often overlooked by tourists. This hidden gem offers a perfect mix of natural beauty, rich cultural heritage and warm hospitality that’s sure to make your trip unforgettable. Whether you’re a history buff, an outdoor enthusiast or you just want to escape the hustle and bustle of city life, Totma has plenty to offer. In this travel guide, we’ve put together a list of top attractions, things to do, where to stay, what to eat, and much more to help you plan your trip to Totma.

List of Places to Visit in Totma

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  1. St. Nicholas Church: Built in 1705, this stunning wooden church is one of the oldest landmarks in Totma. Adorned with intricate carvings and frescoes, it’s worth a visit just for its architectural beauty.
  2. Totma Museum of Local Lore: Discover the rich history and culture of Totma through its exhibits that showcase everything from ancient artifacts to traditional costumes.
  3. Ivanov-Bakharev House Museum: Step back in time and explore the life of a wealthy merchant family in the 19th century. The Ivanov-Bakharev House Museum offers a glimpse into the lifestyle and cultural practices of the town’s influential figures.

Natural Attractions of Totma: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  1. Vakhtovskoe Lake: Enjoy a peaceful getaway and soak up the stunning views of Vakhtovskoe Lake. Take a walk around the lake, or rent a boat for a more immersive experience.
  2. Totma Nature Park: Located on the outskirts of the town, Totma Nature Park is a paradise for nature lovers. Explore its rugged terrain and diverse wildlife through a variety of activities, such as hiking, biking, and camping.
  3. Chornaya River: One of the most idyllic spots in Totma, the Chornaya River offers a tranquil escape from the city. Go fishing, have a picnic, or simply relax by the riverside and watch the world go by.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Totma

  1. Totma-Verkhovazhye Hiking Trail: This 60 km trail takes you through some of the most breathtaking natural landscapes around Totma. Whether you want to challenge yourself or simply enjoy a leisurely hike, this trail has something for everyone.
  2. Cycling around Totma: Rent a bike and explore the town’s scenic routes at your own pace. Check out the Totma Nature Park or bike across the picturesque countryside to see the rural way of life.

Best Things to Do in Totma: Activities and Tours

  1. Totma Winter Fair: Experience the magic of winter in Totma by attending the annual winter fair. Enjoy local food, drinks, crafts, and entertainment while surrounded by enchanting winter scenery.
  2. Totma Green Tourism Festival: This festival showcases the best of Totma’s eco-tourism and sustainability efforts.
  3. Traditional Folk Music Concerts: Listen to the melodious tunes of traditional Russian folk music and enjoy a relaxed evening with the friendly locals.

Week-Long Itinerary for Totma

Day 1: Arrive in Totma, and check in to your preferred accommodation. Take a leisurely stroll along the town’s main square and explore the local shops and cafes.

Day 2: Visit St. Nicholas Church in the morning, followed by lunch at one of the many local eateries. In the afternoon, head to the Totma Museum of Local Lore for a deeper understanding of the town’s history.

Day 3: Rent a bike and explore Totma’s natural surroundings. Visit Vakhtovskoe Lake and have a picnic there, and go to Chornaya River for a short swim.

Day 4-5: Go to Totma Nature Park and spend a day or two enjoying the outdoor adventures. Camp or hike in the park and soak up nature’s pure beauty.

Day 6: Return to Totma for the Winter Fair and experience the local winter festivities. Have dinner at one of the food stalls and enjoy traditional music and dancing along the main square.

Day 7: Relax and unwind before departure. Visit the Ivanov-Bakharev House Museum for a glimpse of Totma’s traditional life before bidding farewell in the evening.

Food and Drink Scene of Totma

Must-Try Dishes

  1. Blini: A traditional Russian dish that’s similar to crepes - typically made with buckwheat flour and usually served with butter, sour cream or caviar.
  2. Shangi: A Totma specialty - a small meat-filled pie with a crispy crust.
  3. Kisel: A sweet jellied dessert made from fruit juice and starch, perfect for ending your meal with.

Local Beverages

  1. Kvass: A refreshing, slightly carbonated drink made from fermented bread - perfect for hot summer days.
  2. Medovukha: An alcoholic drink made from natural honey and spices - perfect for sipping on a cold winter evening.

Where to Stay in Totma: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses.

  1. Beryozka Guesthouse: Offering cozy rooms and excellent service, this guesthouse is perfect for those seeking a more homely experience.
  2. Totma Hotel: A three-star hotel located in the town center, offering comfortable rooms, an indoor pool, and on-site sauna.
  3. Hostel Kotelnaya: A budget-friendly hostel with clean rooms and a well-equipped kitchen facility.

What to Buy in Totma: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  1. Belomor Canal Souvenirs: A unique memorabilia of the Belomor canal project.
  2. Totma Birch Bark Crafts: Handmade gifts and souvenirs made by local artisans from birch bark.
  3. Totma Embroidered Towels: Colorful and intricate hand-embroidered towels with traditional Russian folk motifs.

Local Culture and Customs of Totma

Totma is a town with rich cultural traditions rooted in Orthodox Christianity and Russian folklore. It’s customary to take off your shoes when entering a home, and it’s important to dress conservatively if visiting religious sites. Local festivals and fairs are an important part of Totma culture and provide an opportunity to celebrate community spirit.

Best Time to Visit Totma

The best time to visit Totma is between May and September. The temperature is comfortable and perfect for outdoor activities, and there are plenty of cultural events and festivals held during these months.

Do’s and Don’ts of Totma

Do:

  1. Respect local customs and traditions while visiting religious sites and homes.
  2. Try the local cuisine and immerse yourself in the rich cultural heritage of Totma.
  3. Take guided tours or seek local advice before embarking on outdoor adventures.

Don’t:

  1. Don’t litter - Totma is a beautiful town, and we want to keep it that way.
  2. Don’t drink and drive - be aware of the consequences.
  3. Don’t be afraid to ask for help - the friendly locals are always happy to assist.

Safety and Health

Safety in Totma

Totma is generally safe, but it’s always advisable to be cautious while traveling. Be aware of your belongings in crowded areas, and keep your valuables locked up in your accommodation. Use common sense, and you’ll have a great time in Totma.

Health in Totma

Check with your doctor regarding vaccinations or any other preventive measures necessary before traveling to Russia. The local hospital in Totma provides basic healthcare services, but it’s always better to have comprehensive travel insurance to cover any unexpected health problems.

Getting to Totma: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Totma

The nearest airport to Totma is the Vologda Airport, which is approximately 80km away. From there, you can take a train or hire a taxi to reach Totma.

Bus Stations Near Totma

The Totma Bus Station offers daily connections to Vologda, Moscow, and other major cities in Russia.

Other Options

If you’re feeling adventurous, you can opt for the Trans-Siberian Railway from Moscow to Vologda, which is a great way to soak up the stunning Russian countryside while en route to Totma.

How to Get Around in Totma

Totma is a small, walkable town that you can easily explore on foot. However, if you want to venture beyond the town center, we recommend renting a car, bike, or hiring a taxi.

Where to Go Next from Totma: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  1. Kirillo-Belozersky Monastery: One of the most historic spiritual sites in Russia, the monastery boasts stunning architecture and deep cultural significance.
  2. Belozersk: A historic town located at the head of the White Sea-Baltic Canal, it offers an intriguing insight into a different side of Russian heritage.
  3. Vologda: A vibrant city famous for its architecture, culture, and friendly locals. With plenty of museums, theaters, and restaurants, it’s sure to provide a great Russian experience.

Conclusion

Totma is a charming town with a vibrant cultural heritage and natural beauty that’s waiting to be explored. From its pine forests, tranquil rivers to medieval churches, Totma offers a diverse range of attractions for every traveler. Our comprehensive travel guide has everything you need to know to plan your trip to Totma, to discover its hidden secrets, and to make the most of your stay in this fine Russian gem.
