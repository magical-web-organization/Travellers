Introduction to Perm

Nestled at the foothills of the Ural Mountains in Eastern Europe, Perm is a bustling city with an intriguing history. Founded in 1723, it has served as a cultural and industrial hub since the 19th century. Today, it offers visitors a mix of historical landmarks, modern amenities, and natural beauty. Whether you’re an adventurer, a history buff, or a foodie, Perm has something to offer you. In this comprehensive travel guide, we’ll explore the top attractions, best things to do, local culture, and more. Here’s everything you need to know to plan your dream trip to Perm!

List of Places to Visit in Perm

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Perm is a city steeped in history and culture, with plenty of museums and historical landmarks to explore. Here are some of the top ones to add to your itinerary:

  • Perm Museum of Contemporary Art: This modern museum showcases some of the finest examples of contemporary art in Russia, as well as international works.
  • Perm State Art Gallery: Housed in a neoclassical building, this gallery contains an impressive collection of Russian art from the 18th to 20th centuries.
  • Perm Opera and Ballet Theater: A must-visit for lovers of the performing arts, the theater is known for its striking architecture and world-class performances.
  • Perm-36: This former Soviet gulag has been preserved as a museum, offering visitors a sobering look at the country’s prison system during the Stalinist era.

Natural Attractions of Perm: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Perm is also home to some stunning natural attractions, perfect for outdoor enthusiasts. Here are some of the best places to explore:

  • Kungur Ice Cave: This natural wonder is located just outside the city and features stunning ice formations, underground rivers, and more.
  • Ural Mountains: Hikers and nature lovers will enjoy exploring the nearby Ural Mountains, a stunning range that stretches across Russia and Kazakhstan.
  • Gubakha Park of Culture and Leisure: This scenic park covers over 100 hectares and features a lake, hiking trails, and seasonal activities.
  • Perm City Beach: Yes, you read that right! Perm is home to a sandy beach, located along the banks of the Kama River.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Perm

For those looking to explore the great outdoors on foot or bike, Perm has plenty of options. Here are some of the best routes to take:

  • Perm to Kungur Trail: This 80 km trail takes hikers and bikers through forests, hills, and small villages, ultimately ending at the Kungur Ice Cave.
  • Mount Yaksha Hike: This challenging 10 km hike takes visitors up Mount Yaksha, offering stunning views of the Kungursky Nature Reserve.
  • Kama River Trail: This scenic trail follows the banks of the Kama River, offering hikers and bikers stunning views of city and countryside alike.

Best Things to Do in Perm: Activities and Tours

Beyond natural beauty and cultural landmarks, Perm also offers plenty of fun activities and unique tours. Here are some of the top ones to add to your itinerary:

  • Perm Food Tour: This culinary tour takes visitors to some of the best restaurants and cafes in the city, offering a taste of local cuisine and culture.
  • White Water Rafting: Brave the rapids of the Kama River on a thrilling white water rafting excursion.
  • Skydive Perm: For the adventurous, Skydive Perm offers a chance to jump out of a plane and take in panoramic views of the city and surrounding area.
  • Perm Bicycle Tour: With over 800 km of bike paths in the city, a guided bicycle tour is a great way to see the sights and stay active.

Week-Long Itinerary for Perm

To make the most of your time in Perm, consider following this week-long itinerary:

Day 1: Visit the Perm Museum of Contemporary Art and the Perm State Art Gallery.

Day 2: Explore the Kungur Ice Cave and spend the afternoon at the Gubakha Park of Culture and Leisure.

Day 3: Take a guided hike up Mount Yaksha, followed by dinner at one of the city’s top restaurants.

Day 4: Enjoy a white water rafting excursion on the Kama River, followed by an evening performance at the Perm Opera and Ballet Theater.

Day 5: Take a Perm Bicycle Tour, exploring the city’s top landmarks and parks.

Day 6: Visit Perm-36 and learn about the history of the Soviet prison system.

Day 7: Take a day trip to the nearby city of Yekaterinburg, a bustling metropolis with its own unique history and culture.

Food and Drink Scene of Perm

Must-Try Dishes

Perm is known for its hearty, comforting cuisine, which often features smoked meats, potatoes, and pickled vegetables. Some of the must-try dishes include:

  • Shangi: A traditional meat pie that’s perfect for a quick snack or light meal.
  • Kulebyaka: A savory pie filled with fish, meat, or mushrooms.
  • Sbiten: A popular hot beverage made with honey, spices, and herbs.

Local Beverages

In addition to Sbiten, Perm is also known for its craft beer scene. Some of the top breweries in the area include:

  • Bakunin Brewery: Located in the heart of Perm, this brewery offers a variety of flavorful beers and a lively atmosphere.
  • Pivashi Punks: A community-focused brewery that prides itself on using local ingredients and supporting sustainable practices.

Where to Stay in Perm: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Perm offers a range of accommodations to suit different budgets and preferences. Here are some top options:

  • Hotel Ural: A well-appointed hotel with modern amenities, located in the heart of the city.
  • Hostel Gallery: A more affordable option for budget travelers, located near the Perm Opera and Ballet Theater.
  • Hotel Expedition: A guesthouse-style hotel that’s perfect for those seeking a more authentic, local experience.

What to Buy in Perm: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

When it comes to unique souvenirs and local products, Perm has plenty to offer. Here are some top items to consider:

  • Birch Bark Crafts: Local artisans make a variety of items from birch bark, including baskets, bowls, and even jewelry.
  • Semibratovo Honey: This natural, unfiltered honey comes from a nearby village and is known for its unique taste.
  • Perm-36 Memorabilia: Show your support for this important museum by picking up a t-shirt, pin, or other souvenir.

Local Culture and Customs of Perm

As with any destination, it’s important to respect the local culture and customs when visiting Perm. Here are some tips to keep in mind:

  • Dress appropriately: While Perm is a modern city, it’s still important to dress conservatively when visiting religious sites or attending formal events.
  • Learn some basic Russian: While many locals in Perm speak English, it’s always appreciated when visitors make an effort to speak the local language.
  • Embrace the local way of life: Perm is a city with a rich history and culture, and it’s worth taking the time to learn about and appreciate it.

Best Time to Visit Perm

The best time to visit Perm depends on your priorities. For outdoor activities and natural beauty, visit during the summer months (June to August). For cultural events and festivals, consider a winter visit (December to February).

Do’s and Don’ts of Perm

Do:

  • Take the time to explore the city’s museums, cultural landmarks, and natural attractions.
  • Try the local cuisine and beverages, including craft beer and honey.
  • Respect the local customs and dress appropriately when visiting religious sites or attending formal events.

Don’t:

  • Expect to find a lively nightlife scene, as Perm is more known for its cultural and natural attractions.
  • Disrespect local customs or behave inappropriately in public.

Safety and Health

Safety in Perm

Like any city, there are some safety concerns to keep in mind when visiting Perm. Here are some tips:

  • Keep an eye on your belongings in crowded areas, as pickpocketing can be an issue.
  • Use caution when crossing the street, as traffic can be heavy and chaotic.
  • Be aware of your surroundings when out at night, and avoid walking alone in poorly-lit areas.

Health in Perm

It’s always important to take care of your health while traveling. Here are some health tips to keep in mind:

  • Drink bottled water or bring a filter to ensure you have access to safe drinking water.
  • Protect yourself from mosquito bites, especially during the summer months.
  • Consider bringing any necessary medications or prescriptions with you, as they may not be readily available in Perm.

Getting to Perm: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Perm

Perm International Airport (PEE) is located about 20 km outside the city and offers flights to several major cities in Russia and Europe.

Bus Stations Near Perm

Perm Central Bus Station is located in the city center and offers bus connections to several nearby cities and towns.

Other Options

Train travel is also a popular option for getting to and from Perm. The city’s main train station is located in the city center and offers frequent connections to major cities across Russia.

How to Get Around in Perm

Perm has a well-developed public transportation system that includes buses, trams, and trolleybuses. Tickets can be purchased on board or at kiosks throughout the city. Taxis and rental cars are also available for those who prefer a more individualized mode of transport.

Where to Go Next from Perm: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

If you’re looking to continue your travels beyond Perm, here are some nearby destinations to consider:

  • Yekaterinburg: A bustling city known for its rich history and modern architecture, Yekaterinburg is located about 300 km east of Perm.
  • Chusovaya River: This scenic river is perfect for rafting, kayaking, and other outdoor activities.
  • Nizhny Tagil: A historic industrial city that’s home to several important museums and landmarks.

Conclusion

Whether you’re interested in history, nature, culture, or cuisine, Perm offers something for every traveler. With this comprehensive travel guide, you’ll be able to plan the perfect trip to Russia’s Ural Mountains. Don’t forget to pack your sense of adventure and curiosity – Perm is waiting to be explored!
