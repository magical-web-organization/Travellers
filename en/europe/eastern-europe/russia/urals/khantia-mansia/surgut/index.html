Introduction to Surgut

Surgut is situated in the autonomous region of Khantia-Mansia, Russia. It is a significant industrial city best known for its oil and gas production. Despite its industrial nature, Surgut boasts of beautiful parks, museums, and historical centers, making it an exciting travel destination. In this comprehensive travel guide to Surgut, Russia, we will look at the must-visit places, biking/hiking routes, activities, local culture, food, safety tips, and much more.

List of Places to Visit in Surgut

Surgut has numerous culturally important attractions, natural attractions, and amazing outdoor activities that you can enjoy. Below is a list of the top attractions that you must visit in Surgut.

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Surgut Regional Museum of Local Lore

This museum offers an in-depth look at the history and culture of Surgut and its environs. You will learn about the flora and fauna of the region, the indigenous peoples’ way of life, and the early settlers.

Church of St. Sergius of Radonezh

This is one of Surgut’s oldest and most beautiful churches, situated in the city center. The church’s interiors are exquisitely designed with an iconostasis, frescoes, and murals that tell the history of the Russian Orthodox Church in Khantia-Mansia.

Maiden Moor Monument

This monument is dedicated to a legendary story of two lovers who drowned after escaping disapproving authorities. It is quite popular among tourists and locals who come here to take photos or watch the fountain displays.

Memorial Complex of Militiamen

The complex honors the memory of the region’s police officers who lost their lives in the line of duty. You will find sculptures and exhibits that explain the work of the police and their significance in the history of Surgut.

Natural Attractions of Surgut: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Gagarin Culture and Leisure Park

If you are a lover of nature, walks, and outdoor activities, Gagarin Park is an excellent choice. You can rent a bike, walk along the trail, or have a picnic in the park’s beautiful surroundings. There is also an amusement park, sports facilities, and a small zoo for children to enjoy.

Ob River Embankment

The Ob River embankment is a beautiful spot to watch the sunrise and sunset. The embankment boasts picturesque views of the river, the city, and the surrounding landscape, which is truly breathtaking.

Pine Grove Park

This park is situated just outside Surgut and offers a unique view of the taiga forests, hills, and rivers that surround the city. The park is perfect for hiking, fishing, and camping trips.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Surgut

Surgut offers numerous trails for hiking and biking, ranging from beginner to advanced. The most popular routes in Surgut include

Kedrovy Les

Kedrovy Les is a scenic hiking trail, perfect for nature lovers. The trail is well-marked and offers beautiful views of the stunning landscapes and taiga forest.

Ob Riverside Promenade

If you are looking for an easy bike ride with scenic views, the Ob River Promenade is ideal. The promenade stretches for several miles along the river, and you can rent bikes from various rental shops in the city center.

Best Things to Do in Surgut: Activities and Tours

Yermak Salty Lake

Located 20 miles from Surgut, Yermak Lake is the largest saltwater lake in the Urals. You can go swimming, fishing, or just spend time relaxing by the beach.

City Tour

A city tour thought the vibrant city center of Surgut, will give you the chance to see the unique architecture designs of various houses, apartments, and commercial buildings of the city. The city tour also covers historical and cultural destinations.

Week-Long Itinerary for Surgut

Day 1-2: Arrive, check-in and relax in the city center; explore Surgut Regional Museum of Local Lore.

Day 3-4: Go on a hiking or biking excursion at Kedrovy Les or the Ob River promenade; visit Pine Grove Park.

Day 5-6: Take a day trip to the Yermak Salty Lake, walk along the beach, have a picnic, and enjoy the sun.

Day 7: Shop for souvenirs, buy local crafts, and enjoy the local food scene at the Surgut Central Market (Tsentralny Rynok).

Food and Drink Scene of Surgut

Must-Try Dishes

Shangi

Shangi is a traditional Khanty food made of venison or elk and served with sour cream and potatoes. It is quite delicious.

Siberian Pelmeni

Pelmeni is a traditional Russian dish made by wrapping meat or fish in thin dough and boiled. They are best served with melted butter.

Local Beverages

Kvass

Kvass is a traditional Russian drink made from bread, honey, sugar, and water and is considered healthy. You will find it in many food shops and street vendors.

Vodka

If you love alcohol, then vodka is the drink that you can’t miss in Russia. It is the national drink, and you are sure to find it in many bars and restaurants in Surgut.

Hilton Garden Inn is a plush hotel that offers impeccable services combined with top-notch amenities. The hotel is close to the city center, and its rooms come with air conditioning, free Wi-Fi, and cable tv.

Park Inn by Radisson offers comfortable rooms, state-of-the-art facilities, and friendly staff. The hotel is in a prime location, close to the city center and major attractions.

What to Buy in Surgut: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Honey

Honey is a traditional food in Khantia-Mansia, and Surgut produces high-quality honey. Honey is available in many shops and street markets in Surgut, making it the perfect Souvenir to bring home.

Traditional Crafts

Surgut produces many handicrafts, such as wooden figures, baskets, and trinkets. You can buy these beautiful pieces at street markets or specialty shops in the city center.

Local Culture and Customs of Surgut

The Khanty and Mansi peoples form the Indigenous population of Surgut, and they have a rich history and culture. You will find them having their unique music, language, clothing, and lifestyles in Surgut.

Best Time to Visit Surgut

The ideal time to visit Surgut is during the summer months, between June and August, as the weather is relatively warm and most outdoor activities are available.

Do’s and Don’ts of Surgut

Do:

  • Dress warmly in winter, as it gets incredibly cold and dark in Surgut.

  • Try local delicacies such as shangi and Siberian Pelmeni

  • Respect the local cultures and customs of Surgut, especially during traditional festivals.

Don’t:

  • Drink excessively in public as it is illegal in Russia.

  • Wear clothing that is too revealing as it is considered impolite in Russian culture.

  • Take photographs at military or governmental installation without permission.

Safety and Health

Safety in Surgut

Surgut is a relatively safe city, but petty crimes such as pickpocketing or theft can occur. It is advisable to be careful in crowded areas or when walking alone at night.

Health in Surgut

Surgut’s health facilities are well-equipped with modern facilities, and you can access quality medical care in case of an emergency. Ensure you have adequate health insurance before traveling to Russia.

Getting to Surgut: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Surgut

Surgut International Airport is the nearest airport, located 7 miles southeast of the city center, with regular flights from Moscow, Saint Petersburg, and other major Russian cities.

Bus Stations Near Surgut

Surgut has several bus stations that offer daily routes to cities within the Urals and Moscow. You can book tickets online or visit the bus stations located near the airport or the city.

Other Options

You can also get to Surgut by train, private car, or taxi from other major Russian cities such as Moscow or Saint Petersburg.

How to Get Around in Surgut

You can use buses, taxis, or rental cars to move around Surgut, and all these services are readily available within the city at affordable rates.

Where to Go Next from Surgut: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Tobolsk

Tobolsk is a historic city located about 180 miles from Surgut and is one of the oldest Siberian cities. The city boasts unique architectural designs, museums, and cultural centers.

Khanty-Mansiysk

Khanty-Mansiysk is an ancient city located 217 miles away from Surgut, and it offers the best skiing destinations. The city’s architecture, natural scenery, and traditional cultures make it a perfect travel destination.

Conclusion

Surgut is an exceptional travel destination, with numerous attractions, outdoor activities, its enriched culture, and unique landscapes. It is the perfect place for those who want to explore the great outdoors, delve into local cultures or simply enjoy relaxing amidst stunning views. We hope this comprehensive travel guide has given you the information and inspiration you need to plan a memorable trip to Surgut, Russia.
