Introduction to Bolshekhekhtsirsky Nature Reserve

Located in Khabarovsk Krai of the Russian Far East, Bolshekhekhtsirsky Nature Reserve is a must-visit destination. It was established in 1934 and covers an area of 1,440 sq km. The reserve got its name from the Bolshe-Khekhtsir River, which runs through it. This stunning reserve is home to rare species of flora and fauna and offers breathtaking views of mountains, rivers, and waterfalls.

In this comprehensive guide, we will take you through the must-visit places, activities, hikes, biking routes, food and drink scenes, and much more in Bolshekhekhtsirsky Nature Reserve.

List of Places to Visit in Bolshekhekhtsirsky Nature Reserve

  • Khanka Lake - This lake is a popular spot for birdwatchers due to its diverse range of bird species. One can indulge in fishing or take a boat ride to explore the place.
  • Bolshe-Khekhtsirsky Waterfalls - These popular waterfalls are one of the must-visit spots in the reserve.
  • Khekhtsirsky Ridge - Hiking this ridge is a great way to experience the nature reserve and enjoy the stunning views of surrounding mountains and rivers.
  • Khanagai Natural Monument - This natural monument is a popular spot for tourists who want to explore the geological history of the area.

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Nanai Village Museum - This museum depicts the life and culture of the Nanai people who have been living in the reserve for generations.
  • Komsomolsk-on-Amur - This bustling town situated close to the reserve is renowned for its industrial and cultural heritage.

Natural Attractions of Bolshekhekhtsirsky Nature Reserve: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Hiking and Biking - The reserve offers a wide range of hiking and biking routes for enthusiasts, varying from moderate to difficult levels.
  • Rafting and Kayaking - Experience the beauty of Khekhtsirsky Ridge and Bolshe-Khekhtsirsky River by indulging in rafting or kayaking.
  • Camping - Camping is a great way to experience nature in its true form and enjoy the quiet and peaceful environment of the reserve.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Bolshekhekhtsirsky Nature Reserve

  • Tungus Trail - This trail is a three-night trek through the reserve and takes hikers through the beautiful forest, the Khekhtsirsky Ridge, and the waterfalls.
  • Khabarovsk to Khanka Lake Trail - This four-day trek takes travelers through the reserve, the Mukhen and Komsomolsky mountain ranges, and Khanka Lake.

Local Culture and Customs of Bolshekhekhtsirsky Nature Reserve

The locals, mostly the Nanai people, follow traditional customs and practices in their daily life. Visitors are requested to respect their culture and traditions.

Best Time to Visit Bolshekhekhtsirsky Nature Reserve

The best time to visit Bolshekhekhtsirsky Nature Reserve is between June to September when the temperature is pleasant and most of the hiking and biking routes are accessible.
