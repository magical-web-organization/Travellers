Introduction to Khabarovsk

Located in Khabarovsk Krai, in the Russian Far East, Khabarovsk is a city that carries a rich history and culture. The city also acts as a gateway to explore nearby nature reserves, such as the Sikhote-Alin Biosphere Reserve and Bikin National Park.

Khabarovsk benefits from a moderate climate. Warm summers and cool winters with occasional snow offer enjoyable seasonal activities all year round. The city is situated on the Amur River, and its European architecture and vibrant culture attract tourists from all over the world.

List of Places to Visit in Khabarovsk

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Khabarovsk Regional Museum

The Khabarovsk Regional Museum is dedicated to documenting the natural and cultural history of the region. It houses displays on local flora, fauna, and indigenous cultures, as well as exhibits on the local economy and the development of the city.

Khabarovsk Regional Museum of Art

Located in the heart of Khabarovsk, the Regional Museum of Art is one of the largest art museums in Russia. It houses a vast collection of Russian and European art, including paintings, sculptures, and decorative arts.

Amur Cliff

One of the main landmarks of Khabarovsk is the Amur Cliff. This scenic spot lies on the bank of the Amur River, offering breathtaking views of the city skyline and the river. Visitors can climb up the steps to the viewpoint on top of the cliff and enjoy the panorama.

Natural Attractions of Khabarovsk: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Sikhote-Alin Biosphere Reserve

The Sikhote-Alin Biosphere Reserve is one of the largest protected areas in Russia that extends over 400,000 hectares. Situated near Khabarovsk, the Reserve is home to the endangered Siberian tiger, Himalayan black bear, and Amur leopard. Visitors can take a guided tour of the reserve to witness the wildlife and nature in its glory.

Bikin National Park

Bikin National Park is a UNESCO World Heritage Site and is located about 300 kilometers from Khabarovsk. The park is known for its vast wilderness, spectacular mountain ranges, and crystal-clear lakes. Visitors can experience hiking and trekking through the park’s thick forests, catch a glimpse of the Amur tiger, or visit the many Buddhist temples dotting the park.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Khabarovsk

Amur River Promenade

The Amur River is the pride of Khabarovsk. The newly built promenade, stretching 14 kilometers along the river, is an excellent place for hiking and cycling. The path offers scenic views of the river, the hill, and the city, as well as several rest areas, cafes, and parks.

Lenin Stadium

Lenin Stadium is where you can go hiking, jogging, or biking in the heart of the city. The stadium was built in 1957 and has a capacity of 15,500. The running track that circles the field offers the best view of the stadium and the surrounding landscape.

Best Things to Do in Khabarovsk: Activities and Tours

City Tour

A city tour is the best way to experience all the hidden gems and attractions of Khabarovsk. Visitors can take a guided tour of the city by foot, bike, or boat and witness the rich history and culture of the city.

Amur River Cruise

A cruise on the Amur River provides an unparalleled view of the cityscape from the river. During the cruise, visitors will enjoy stunning views of the city’s skyline, historical landmarks, and the surrounding natural beauty.

Food and Drink Scene of Khabarovsk

Must-Try Dishes

Borsch

Borsch is a soup made with cabbage, beets, and potatoes and is very popular in Russia. The soup is served hot and is perfect for cold weather.

Blini

Blini is a traditional Russian pancake, served with sour cream, jam, or syrup.

Local Beverages

Kvass

Kvass is a brewed, non-alcoholic beverage made from rye bread. The drink has a slightly sour and sweet taste and is perfect for the summer season.

Vodka

Vodka is a popular alcoholic drink in Russia that is best served cold.

Where to Stay in Khabarovsk: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Parus Hotel

Parus Hotel is located in the center of Khabarovsk, and it offers comfortable rooms with a stunning view of the city. The hotel features a restaurant, sauna, and a fitness center.

Tourist Hotel Khabarovsk

Tourist Hotel Khabarovsk is situated near Lenin Stadium, and it offers cozy rooms with all the necessary amenities. The hotel features a restaurant, bar, and sauna.

What to Buy in Khabarovsk: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Matryoshka Dolls

Matryoshka dolls are handmade wooden dolls that are famous all over Russia. The dolls come in many shapes and sizes, and they make a perfect gift to bring back home.

Birch Bark Souvenirs

Birch bark souvenirs are a must-buy item when in Khabarovsk. These souvenirs are made from the bark of the birch tree and come in the form of baskets, boxes, and other decorative items.

Local Culture and Customs of Khabarovsk

Khabarovsk has a vibrant culture and friendly locals. It is customary to greet people with a handshake, and it is polite to remove your shoes when entering someone’s home. Russians are known for their hospitality, and they often invite guests to share a meal or drink.

Best Time to Visit Khabarovsk

The best time to visit Khabarovsk is from May to September when the weather is warm and comfortable. During these months, visitors can take advantage of outdoor activities and enjoy the offerings of the city.

Do’s and Don’ts of Khabarovsk

Do:

  • Try authentic Russian food and drinks
  • Take a guided tour of the city and its attractions
  • Observe local customs, such as greeting with handshakes and removing shoes

Don’t:

  • Engage in public displays of affection
  • Take photos of military installations or personnel
  • Ignore Russian language and customs

Safety and Health

Safety in Khabarovsk

Khabarovsk is a relatively safe city for tourists. However, visitors should remain vigilant and aware of their surroundings, especially in areas unfamiliar to them.

Health in Khabarovsk

There are no significant health concerns specific to Khabarovsk. However, visitors should be up to date with their routine vaccinations, such as measles and rubella.

Getting to Khabarovsk: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Khabarovsk

The Khabarovsk Novy International Airport is the primary airport in the city and connects Khabarovsk with various cities in Russia and Asia.

Bus Stations Near Khabarovsk

Khabarovsk has two bus stations, the Main Bus Station and the East Bus Station, which connect the city with various locations in Russia.

Other Options

Visitors can also opt for trains and flights from Moscow, Vladivostok, and other major cities in Russia.

How to Get Around in Khabarovsk

Khabarovsk has an extensive public transportation system, including buses, trams, and trolleybuses. Taxis are also readily available and are an affordable and convenient option for traveling around the city.

Where to Go Next from Khabarovsk: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Vladivostok

Situated about 680 kilometers from Khabarovsk, Vladivostok is a beautiful city located on the Primorsky Region’s coast. Visitors to Vladivostok can enjoy the city’s historic landmarks, such as the Vladivostok Fortress, and beaches, including the Golden Horn Bay.

Lake Bolon

Lake Bolon is situated near the Chinese border and is about 300 kilometers from Khabarovsk. The lake is known for its pristine waters and pine tree-lined shores. Visitors can take a relaxing walk along the lakeside, fish, or swim.

Conclusion

Khabarovsk is a hidden gem in Russia’s Far East that offers tourists a perfect blend of natural beauty, cultural heritage, and modern amenities. With its mild climate, beautiful landscapes, and welcoming locals, Khabarovsk is an ideal destination for travelers looking for an off-the-beaten-path experience.
