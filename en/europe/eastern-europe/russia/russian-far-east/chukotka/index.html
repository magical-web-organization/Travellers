Introduction to Chukotka

Tucked away in the far east of Russia, Chukotka is a hidden gem in Eastern Europe that is just waiting to be explored. The region, with a population of just 50,000, boasts breathtaking landscapes, unique cultural experiences, and fascinating history.

List of Places to Visit in Chukotka

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Chukotka is home to unique indigenous cultures and history, which are celebrated in the area’s museums and historical sites. Don’t miss:

  • Discover the local history at The Museum of Local Lore and Culture in Anadyr.
  • Visit Uelen - a village which is the world’s easternmost settlement of indigenous peoples.
  • Take a trip to the abandoned town of Egvekinot, home to the Soviet Union’s northernmost gulag.

Natural Attractions of Chukotka: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Chukotka is a paradise for nature lovers, boasting a vast and diverse natural landscape. Here are some must-visit destinations:

  • Explore the majestic landscapes of Wrangel Island - the world’s largest polar desert.
  • Embark on a whale watching tour and spot majestic gray whales.
  • Take a scenic flight over the region to marvel at its amazing beauty.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Chukotka

Chukotka is paradise for hiking and biking enthusiasts. There are several routes to consider, including:

  • The Anadyrsky Reserve, a protected territory with several hiking trails
  • The Beringia National Park, spanning over 200,000 hectares, offering a variety of hiking and biking trails
  • The Shmidt Peninsula, featuring rugged terrain and stunning coastal views.

Best Things to Do in Chukotka: Activities and Tours

Chukotka offers a wide range of activities and tours, designed to provide visitors with an immersive experience in the region’s culture and history. Here are some of the activities you can indulge in:

  • Experience traditional reindeer sled rides and witness Nenets culture and traditions.
  • Try your hand at fishing in one of Chukotka’s pristine rivers or lakes.
  • Join a dog sledding adventure and explore the beautiful wilderness of Chukotka’s vast open land.

Week-Long Itinerary for Chukotka

For a comprehensive Chukotka experience, plan a week-long itinerary:

Day Itinerary
1 Arrival to Anadyr, check into accommodation
2 Explore the local culture and history of Anadyr
3 Take a boat tour to Beringia national park
4 Hike the Shmidt Peninsula or visit Uelen village
5 Embark on a whale watching tour
6 Visit Wrangel Island
7 Depart Chukotka

Food and Drink Scene of Chukotka

Must-Try Dishes

Chukotka’s cuisine reflects the region’s remote and rugged terrain. Some dishes you must try include:

  • Stroganina - a dish consisting of thin slices of frozen fish or meat served with soy sauce and mustard.
  • Reindeer meat - cooked or smoked dishes made from this traditional Chukotka ingredient.
  • Khashyl - fermented mare’s milk that has a tangy, slightly sour taste.

Local Beverages

Chukotka’s cold climate is conducive to the brewing of strong and robust alcoholic drinks. Some of the local favorites include:

  • Chukotka gin - made from local berries and infused with juniper leaves.
  • Vodka - a staple and traditional drink, often flavored with various herbs and spices.
  • Chukotka’s craft beers - brewed and named after the different regions in the area.

Where to Stay in Chukotka: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Chukotka has several accommodation options ranging from budget-friendly to high-end luxury. Some of the most popular options include:

  • Chukotka Hotel, offering comfortable and affordable stays in the heart of Anadyr.
  • Beringian Heritage, located in the picturesque village of Lavrentiya and offering stunning sea views.
  • Northern Nights Hotel, located in the peaceful town of Pevek and offering luxury accommodation options.

What to Buy in Chukotka: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Some unique souvenirs and local products to take home from Chukotka include:

  • Reindeer skins and antlers, which are often made into decorative items and souvenirs.
  • Traditional crafted items made out of fur, bones and antlers
  • Traditional jewelry, made from walrus or whale products.

Local Culture and Customs of Chukotka

Chukotka’s indigenous people, the Chukchi and Yupik people, have a rich and unique culture that is worth experiencing. Visitors are encouraged to respect the local customs and traditions.

Best Time to Visit Chukotka

The best time to visit Chukotka is during the summer months, from June to September. During this time, the weather is milder, and the landscapes are in full bloom.

Do’s and Don’ts of Chukotka

Do:

  • Respect the local customs and traditions.
  • Take necessary precautions while embarking on outdoor adventures, as the terrain can be challenging.

Don’t:

  • Take any natural resources from the region, like animal pelts, without proper permission.
  • Disrespect the environment or the locals in any way.

Safety and Health

Safety in Chukotka

Chukotka is a safe region, but visitors must adhere to safety measures, especially when embarking on adventurous activities and tours.

Health in Chukotka

Visitors are advised to carry basic medications more uncommon ailments, due to the region’s remote location. Healthcare facilities are limited, and emergencies require medical evacuation from the region.

Getting to Chukotka: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Chukotka

  • Anadyr Airport - the main gateway to Chukotka, with regular flights from Moscow.
  • Lavrentiya Airport - offering limited flights to and from Anadyr.

Bus Stations Near Chukotka

  • There are no bus services in Chukotka

Other Options

  • Helicopter tours and private charters

How to Get Around in Chukotka

The primary mode of transportation in Chukotka is by air. Helicopter or private charter services are hired for transportation between different locations.

Where to Go Next from Chukotka: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Visit Kamchatka - another remote region in Russia that boasts stunning natural landscapes and wildlife, and adventure opportunities.
  • Visit neighboring country of Alaska via Bering Strait

Conclusion

Chukotka is a hidden gem in Eastern Europe that provides visitors with a unique and immersive experience. With its rich cultural heritage, stunning natural scenery, and exciting outdoor adventures, Chukotka is a destination worth adding to anyone’s travel itinerary.
