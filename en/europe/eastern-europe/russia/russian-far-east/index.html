A Comprehensive Guide to Russian Far East

Located in eastern Russia, the Russian Far East is a stunning, remote region of the country that is a must-visit for adventure seekers. From the rugged coastlines of the Sea of Okhotsk to the mountainous terrain of Kamchatka, there is no shortage of natural beauty in this part of the world. In this comprehensive guide, we’ll explore the top things to see and do in the Russian Far East, including where to stay, what to eat, and how to get around.

List of Places to Visit in Russian Far East

The Russian Far East is an expansive region that encompasses several provinces, including Kamchatka Krai, Primorsky Krai, and Khabarovsk Krai. Here are some of the top destinations to add to your itinerary:

  • Kamchatka Peninsula
  • Vladivostok
  • Sakhalin Island
  • Primorye National Park
  • Lake Baikal
  • Khabarovsk
  • Magadan
  • Anadyr

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

The Russian Far East has a rich cultural heritage that is showcased in its many museums and historical landmarks. Here are some must-visit attractions:

  • Arsenyev Regional Museum, Vladivostok
  • Khabarovsk Regional Museum
  • Magadan Regional Museum
  • Sakhalin Regional Museum
  • Museum of Russian-German Settlement History, Primorye Region
  • Peter and Paul Fortress, Vladivostok

Natural Attractions of Russian Far East: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

The Russian Far East is known for its pristine wilderness areas and breathtaking scenery. Outdoor enthusiasts will find plenty to do here, including:

  • Hiking in Kamchatka
  • Whale watching in the Sea of Okhotsk
  • Visiting the Valley of Geysers on the Kamchatka Peninsula
  • Exploring the Kuril Islands
  • Trekking in the Sikhote-Alin Mountains
  • Climbing the Avachinsky volcano

Hiking/Biking Routes of Russian Far East

There are numerous hiking and biking routes in the Russian Far East, catering to both experienced and novice adventurers. Here are some top picks:

  • Avacha Bay to the Pacific Ocean trail, Kamchatka Peninsula
  • Vityaz Bay to Kuthiny Baty trail, Sakhalin Island
  • Gornovka to Tyatya Volcano trail, Primorsky Krai
  • Obmanzhayushchiy Bay to Kholm to Cape Romanzof trail, Kamchatka Peninsula
  • Sikhote-Alin Nature Reserve trek, Primorsky Krai

Best Things to Do in Russian Far East: Activities and Tours

There are many things to do in the Russian Far East, from exploring its natural wonders to experiencing its vibrant culture. Here are some must-try activities and tours:

  • Visiting the Valley of Geysers
  • Kayaking in Kamchatka
  • Whale watching in the Sea of Okhotsk
  • Relaxing in the hot springs of Uzon Caldera
  • Exploring Magadan’s Gulag Museum
  • Taking the Trans-Siberian Railway to Vladivostok

Week-Long Itinerary for Russian Far East

Here’s an example itinerary for a week-long trip to the Russian Far East:

Day 1: Arrive in Vladivostok, Explore the city Day 2: Take a day trip to Russky Island, visit Voroshilovskaya Battery and Lighthouse Day 3: Fly to Petropavlovsk-Kamchatsky, visit Avacha Bay and the Pacific Ocean coast Day 4: Visit the Valley of Geysers, Uzon Caldera Day 5: Visit Milkovo village, explore the Bystrinsky Nature Park Day 6: Visit the volcanic plateau of plateau Plosky Tolbachik Day 7: Return to Vladivostok, visit Arsenyev Regional Museum

Must-Try Dishes:

  • Kamchatka crab
  • Red salmon caviar
  • Pickled fish
  • Smoked fish
  • Dried squid

The Russian Far East has a range of accommodation options to suit different budgets. Here are some top picks:

  • Hotel Gavan, Vladivostok
  • Parus Hostel, Kamchatka Peninsula
  • Sapporo Hotel, Khabarovsk
  • Sakhalin Sapporo Hotel, Sakhalin Island

What to Buy in Russian Far East: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Here are some souvenirs and local products to bring home from the Russian Far East:

  • Traditional handicrafts, such as nesting dolls and embroidered items
  • Seafood products, such as salmon caviar and smoked fish
  • Russian tea
  • Kamchatka honey
  • Local vodka and wine

Local Culture and Customs of Russian Far East

The Russian Far East has a unique culture that is influenced by its geography and history. Here are some things to keep in mind when visiting:

  • Locals are generally friendly and hospitable towards visitors.
  • It is respectful to remove your shoes when entering someone’s home.
  • Russian Orthodox Christianity is the predominant religion.

Best Time to Visit Russian Far East

The best time to visit the Russian Far East is during the summer months (June to August), when the weather is milder and more conducive to outdoor activities.

Do’s and Don’ts of Russian Far East

Do:

  • Dress in layers, as the weather can be unpredictable.
  • Try to learn a few basic phrases in Russian before your trip.
  • Respect local customs and traditions.

Don’t:

  • Overlook the importance of sunscreen, even on cloudy days.
  • Be surprised if locals stare at you – it’s not considered impolite.

Safety and Health

Safety in Russian Far East

The Russian Far East is generally safe for travelers. However, it’s always a good idea to take precautions against petty crime, such as keeping your valuables secure and being aware of your surroundings.

Health in Russian Far East

Visitors to the Russian Far East should ensure that their vaccinations are up to date and take precautions against tick-borne diseases, particularly in rural areas.

Getting to Russian Far East: Transportation Options and Logistics

There are several transportation options for getting to the Russian Far East, including:

  • Flights to Vladivostok International Airport or Petropavlovsk-Kamchatsky Airport
  • The Trans-Siberian Railway, which stops in several cities in the region
  • Ferries from Japan or South Korea to Vladivostok or Sakhalin Island

How to Get Around in Russian Far East

The most convenient way to get around the Russian Far East is by car, particularly if you plan to explore the region’s more remote areas. Public transportation options include buses, trains, and ferries.

Where to Go Next from Russian Far East: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Here are some nearby destinations and excursions to consider after visiting the Russian Far East:

  • Mongolia
  • China
  • South Korea
  • Japan

Conclusion

The Russian Far East is a stunning, remote part of the world with plenty to offer adventurous travelers. With this comprehensive guide, you’ll be equipped to explore this fascinating region to the fullest.
