Introduction to Birobidzhan

Birobidzhan is a city located in the Jewish Autonomous Oblast in the Russian Far East. It serves as the administrative center of the region and has a population of approximately 75,000 people. The city was established in 1931 as a Jewish settlement, and it remains to be a unique cultural center for Jews. Birobidzhan is an interesting destination with a rich history and culture, beautiful natural attractions, and a range of activities for tourists.

List of Places to Visit in Birobidzhan

Here are some must-visit places in Birobidzhan:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Jewish Cultural and Educational Center Museum - This museum showcases Jewish traditions and customs through art exhibitions, photographs, and educational programs.

  • History Museum of the Jewish Autonomous Oblast - This museum documents the history of the Jewish Autonomous Oblast and its founding.

  • Birobidzhan Synagogue - The synagogue is an important religious center for Jewish people in Birobidzhan.

  • Uglovaya Settlement - This settlement is a historical monument that displays the life of the first Jewish settlers who arrived in Birobidzhan in the 1920s.

Natural Attractions of Birobidzhan: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Kuldur Waterfall - The Kuldur waterfall is an impressive natural attraction located in Kuldur village near Birobidzhan. The waterfall cascades 30 meters and is surrounded by beautiful lush green forests.

  • Amur River - The Amur River flows through the city and offers a beautiful view of its banks. Take a stroll along the river, or enjoy a boat ride during your trip.

  • Khingansky Nature Reserve - The Khingansky Nature Reserve is situated in Birobidzhan and features impressive natural landscapes and wildlife. Visitors can explore the reserve by hiking or on an organized tour.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Birobidzhan

Birobidzhan offers numerous hiking and biking options for adventurers. Here are some popular routes to try:

  • Zmeinaya Pad’ Trail - This trail is a 6.5-kilometer route that leads to Zmeinaya Pad’, a rock formation with a panoramic view of the surrounding forest.

  • Khludnevskie Lakes Trail - This trail leads to Khludnevskie Lakes, a beautiful cluster of lakes in the Khingansky Nature Reserve. The route provides beautiful views of the surrounding forests and wildlife.

Best Things to Do in Birobidzhan: Activities and Tours

Regardless of whether you’re looking for adventure or relaxation, there is something for everyone in Birobidzhan. Here are some suggestions:

  • Take a cultural tour - Birobidzhan has a unique cultural history, and it is worth taking a cultural tour to learn about the Jewish traditions and the history of the Jewish Autonomous Oblast.

  • Skiing and Snowboarding at Tigrovaya Balka - Tigrovaya Balka is a ski resort located in Birobidzhan that offers skiing and snowboarding adventures for both beginners and experienced skiers.

Week-Long Itinerary for Birobidzhan

Here is an itinerary for a week in Birobidzhan:

Day 1 - Visit the Jewish Cultural and Educational Center Museum and the Birobidzhan Synagogue.

Day 2 - Hike the Zmeinaya Pad’ Trail and enjoy the panoramic view of surrounding forests.

Day 3 - Visit the History Museum of the Jewish Autonomous Oblast and take a cultural tour of the region.

Day 4 - Visit Kuldur Waterfall and relax amidst lush green forests.

Day 5 - Enjoy skiing and snowboarding at Tigrovaya Balka ski resort.

Day 6 - Visit Khingansky Nature Reserve and hike the Khludnevskie Lakes Trail.

Day 7 - Visit Uglovaya Settlement and experience the life of the first Jewish settlers who arrived in Birobidzhan.

Food and Drink Scene of Birobidzhan

When it comes to food, Birobidzhan offers a unique blend of Russian and Jewish cuisine. Here are some must-try dishes:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Borscht - This is a traditional Russian soup that is made with beetroot and meat.

  • Khachapuri - A traditional Jewish pastry filled with cheese and sometimes vegetables or meat.

  • Pelmeni - Pelmeni are a type of dumpling that is popular in Russia and often filled with meat.

Local Beverages

  • Kvass - Kvass is a traditional Russian fermented drink made from bread.

  • Tea - Tea culture is prevalent in Russia, and one can find numerous tea houses in Birobidzhan.
