Introduction to Susuman

Located in the eastern part of Russia, Susuman is a small town situated in the Magadan Oblast. Despite its remoteness, Susuman has a rich history and culture that attracts many visitors. From breathtaking natural landscapes to culturally significant attractions, Susuman has much to offer. In this travel guide, we have compiled a comprehensive list of top-rated places to visit, best things to do, where to stay, and more, to help make your visit to Susuman a memorable experience.

List of Places to Visit in Susuman

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Susuman Museum of Local Lore: Located in the heart of Susuman, this museum showcases the history and culture of the town, including exhibits on local flora and fauna, indigenous native tribes, and Soviet Gulag camps.

  • Monument to the Miners of Susuman: This monument is dedicated to the brave workers who labored in the gold mines of Susuman. It is located in the city center and is considered a significant landmark of the town.

  • Old Camp 17: This abandoned Soviet Gulag camp provides a sobering glimpse into the town’s history during the Soviet era. The camp is best experienced through a guided tour.

Natural Attractions of Susuman: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Verkhoyansk Mountain Range: The picturesque Verkhoyansk Mountains offer breathtaking panoramic views of Susuman and the surrounding landscape.

  • Kolyma River: A popular destination for fishing enthusiasts, the Kolyma River in Susuman is known for its crystal clear waters and a variety of fish species, including salmon.

  • Susumanie Natural Park: The park is located on the outskirts of the town and offers some of the most stunning views of the surrounding landscape. It features hiking trails, picnic areas, and several lookout points.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Susuman

  • Northern Kolyma: A scenic route that takes you through the northern section of the Kolyma River and offers breathtaking views of the surrounding wilderness.

  • Verkhoyansk Mountains: This route offers challenging terrain and spectacular views of the mountain range.

Best Things to Do in Susuman: Activities and Tours

  • Gold Panning: Visitors can try their hand at gold panning in a local stream. This activity is best experienced through a guided tour.

  • Tsar’s Gold Route: A guided tour that takes you through the historic Tsar’s Gold Route, where you can learn about the area’s rich mining history.

  • Dog Sledding: A popular winter activity, dog sledding takes visitors through the snowy wilderness, offering a magical experience.

Food and Drink Scenes of Susuman

Must-Try Dishes

  • Stroganina: A traditional Siberian dish made of frozen raw fish, typically served with salt, pepper, and onions.

  • Ukha: A traditional Russian fish soup made with salmon or other fish, potatoes, onions, and carrots.

  • Blini: Thin Russian pancakes typically served with caviar, sour cream, or jam.

Local Beverages

  • Sbiten: A hot, spicy drink made with honey, spices, and sometimes vodka.

  • Chifir: A tea-like drink made with jam or preserves.

  • Kvass: A slightly alcoholic drink made from fermented rye bread.

Where to Stay in Susuman: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Hotel Vega: A modern hotel with comfortable rooms and amenities, including a sauna and a restaurant specializing in local cuisine.

  • Hostel Belukha: A cozy hostel in the city center, offering private and shared rooms.

  • Guesthouse Kolyma: A family-run guesthouse located outside the city center, offering stunning views of the surrounding countryside.

What to Buy in Susuman: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Amber jewelry: Susuman is known for its high-quality amber, which is used to make jewelry and other decorative items.

  • Local handicrafts: Visitors can purchase traditional handicrafts made by indigenous tribes in the region, such as woven baskets and wooden carvings.

  • Local honey: Susuman is home to several honey farms, producing a variety of natural honey products.

Local Culture and Customs of Susuman

Susuman has a unique blend of Russian and indigenous cultures. Visitors should be respectful of local customs and traditions, including dressing appropriately in places of worship and adhering to local etiquettes when interacting with locals.

Best Time to Visit Susuman

The best time to visit Susuman is during the warmer months, between June and August, when the weather is milder and outdoor activities are more accessible.

Do’s and Don’ts of Susuman

Do:

  • Consider hiring a local guide for outdoor activities and tours.

  • Dress appropriately for the weather and outdoor activities.

  • Try local cuisine and cultural experiences.

Don’t:

  • Disrespect local customs and traditions.

  • Leave trash or litter in natural areas.

  • Venture beyond designated hiking or outdoor areas without proper equipment and preparations.

Safety and Health

Safety in Susuman

Susuman is considered relatively safe for tourists. However, visitors should exercise caution when hiking or participating in outdoor activities and take necessary precautions to prevent accidents.

Health in Susuman

Visitors should ensure that they have adequate travel insurance and receive the necessary vaccinations before traveling to Susuman. Medical facilities in Susuman are limited, and visitors should bring enough medicine if they have specific medical conditions.

Getting to Susuman: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Susuman

The nearest airport to Susuman is the Magadan Sokol Airport, located approximately 520 kilometers away. From the airport, visitors can take a bus or car rental to reach Susuman.

Bus Stations Near Susuman

Susuman has a central bus station that offers regular services to nearby towns and cities.

Other Options

Visitors can also reach Susuman by car rental or private taxi services.

How to Get Around in Susuman

The best way to get around Susuman is on foot or by car rental. Visitors can also take a taxi to reach nearby attractions or participate in guided tours.

Where to Go Next from Susuman: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Magadan: A nearby town that serves as the capital of the Magadan Oblast. Magadan offers several museums, cultural attractions, and outdoor activities.

  • Yakutsk: A city situated approximately 2000 kilometers away from Susuman, known for its unique culture, museums, and natural wonders, including the Lena Pillars.

Conclusion

Susuman is a unique travel destination that offers a blend of history, culture, and breathtaking natural landscapes. Visitors can enjoy a range of activities, including outdoor adventures, cultural experiences, and sampling local cuisine. With this comprehensive travel guide, visitors can make the most of their visit to Susuman and experience all that this remote town has to offer.
