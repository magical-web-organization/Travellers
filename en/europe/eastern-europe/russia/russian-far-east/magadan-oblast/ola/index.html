Introduction to Ola

Nestled among the dramatic landscapes of Magadan Oblast, Ola is a small town in Eastern Russia that packs a big punch when it comes to natural beauty, cultural attractions, and outdoor adventures. While it’s not a destination that’s on the radar of most tourists, those who venture to this remote corner of the country will be rewarded with a one-of-a-kind experience that’s hard to forget.

In this travel guide to Ola, we’ll show you what makes this town such a special destination. From the must-visit historical landmarks and museums to the picturesque hiking and biking trails, there’s something for everyone in Ola. Plus, we’ll also share tips on where to stay, what to eat and drink, and how to make the most of your time in this fascinating part of Russia.

List of Places to Visit in Ola

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Ola is a town with a rich cultural and historical heritage. Visitors interested in learning more about the area’s past and its people will find plenty of museums and historical sites to explore. Here are a few highlights:

  • Magadan Oblast Museum: This museum is dedicated to the history and culture of the Magadan region. Exhibits cover everything from the indigenous peoples who once called the area home to the Soviet labor camps that operated here in the 20th century.
  • Ola Local Lore Museum: This museum tells the story of Ola itself, with exhibits showcasing the traditions, customs, and daily life of the town’s residents.
  • Kolyma Highway: This road was built in the mid-20th century to connect the labor camps in the region, and it remains an important part of the area’s history. Visitors can drive or hike along sections of the highway to get a sense of its impact on the region.

Natural Attractions of Ola: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

While Ola’s history and culture are certainly fascinating, it’s the town’s natural beauty that will take your breath away. Whether you’re an avid hiker, nature lover, or just looking to escape the hustle and bustle of city life, there are plenty of outdoor adventures to be had in Ola. Here are a few things to add to your itinerary:

  • Mount Ola: This mountain peak offers stunning views of the surrounding landscape, making it a popular destination for hikers. There are several routes up the mountain, ranging from easy to challenging.
  • Serebryanka River: This pristine river is a great spot for fishing, kayaking, and hiking. It’s also home to a variety of wildlife, including bears, moose, and wolves.
  • Gulag Memorial: This memorial site is a somber reminder of the suffering that took place in the region during the Soviet era. The site includes a monument to the victims of the labor camps, as well as a small museum.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Ola

Ola is a paradise for outdoor enthusiasts, with dozens of hiking and biking trails that traverse the region’s stunning landscapes. Whether you’re a seasoned hiker or just starting out, there’s a trail in Ola that’s perfect for you. Here are a few favorites:

  • Ola to Omsukchan Trail: This 75-kilometer trail is one of the most popular in the region, thanks to its stunning views and challenging terrain. The trail takes hikers through remote forests and along the banks of the picturesque Ola River.
  • Mount Ola Trail: This trail takes hikers to the summit of Mount Ola, offering incredible panoramic views of the surrounding mountains and forests.
  • Serebryanka River Trail: This trail follows the banks of the Serebryanka River, offering hikers the chance to spot wildlife and enjoy the peace and quiet of the natural surroundings.

Best Things to Do in Ola: Activities and Tours

In addition to hiking and exploring the region’s natural beauty, there are plenty of activities and tours to enjoy in Ola. Here are a few options to consider:

  • Siberian Husky Tour: Take a tour through the snow-covered taiga on a sled pulled by Siberian huskies. This is a one-of-a-kind experience that’s not to be missed.
  • Fishing Tour: The rivers and lakes around Ola are home to a variety of fish species, including salmon and trout. Join a fishing tour to try your luck and enjoy the stunning natural scenery.
  • Ola Wildlife Observation Tour: Join a local guide for a tour of the region’s forests and fields, where you’ll have the chance to spot a variety of wildlife, including moose, bears, and wolves.

Week-Long Itinerary for Ola

If you’re planning a trip to Ola and looking for a week-long itinerary to help you make the most of your time, here’s a suggested schedule:

  • Day 1: Arrive in Ola and visit the Magadan Oblast Museum and Ola Local Lore Museum.
  • Day 2: Hike Mount Ola in the morning, then take a Siberian husky tour in the afternoon.
  • Day 3: Take a fishing tour on the Serebryanka River.
  • Day 4: Visit the Gulag Memorial in the morning, then enjoy a picnic along the banks of the Ola River.
  • Day 5: Take a wildlife observation tour in the morning, then hike the Ola to Omsukchan Trail in the afternoon.
  • Day 6: Explore the town of Ola, including its local markets and shops.
  • Day 7: Depart Ola.

Food and Drink Scene of Ola

One of the best ways to experience a new culture is through its food and drink. Here are a few must-try dishes and local beverages to sample in Ola:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Kholodets: This is a meat aspic dish made with gelatin, cooked meat, and spices. It’s typically served cold and is a popular dish throughout Russia.
  • Okroshka: This is a cold soup made with sour cream, kvass (a fermented drink), and a variety of vegetables, including cucumbers, radishes, and potatoes.
  • Stroganina: This dish is made with thin slices of frozen raw fish or meat, often served with salt and herbs.

Local Beverages

  • Kvass: Made from fermented rye bread, kvass is a popular beverage throughout Russia. It’s slightly sweet and sour, and is often served chilled.
  • Sbiten: This is a traditional hot drink made with honey, spices, and water. It’s a great way to warm up on a cold day.

Where to Stay in Ola: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

While Ola is a small town, there are several accommodation options to choose from. Here are a few recommendations:

  • Hotel Ola: This hotel offers comfortable rooms with modern amenities, including Wi-Fi and satellite TV. It’s located in the center of town, making it a convenient base for exploring the area.
  • Shikotan Guesthouse: This guesthouse offers cozy, traditional-style rooms and a restaurant serving local cuisine. It’s located in a secluded area outside of town, offering a peaceful retreat.
  • Ola Hostel: This hostel offers dormitory-style accommodation, perfect for budget travelers. It’s located on the outskirts of town, offering easy access to hiking and biking trails.

What to Buy in Ola: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

If you’re looking for souvenirs and local products to bring home from Ola, here are a few suggestions:

  • Matryoshka dolls: These nesting dolls are a classic souvenir from Russia. Look for ones that are hand-painted for an authentic souvenir.
  • Amber jewelry: Amber is a popular material for jewelry in Russia, and Ola is no exception. Look for locally-made pieces for a unique souvenir.
  • Fur hats and accessories: With the cold climate in Ola, fur hats and accessories are a practical and stylish souvenir. Look for ones made with local furs, such as fox or sable.

Local Culture and Customs of Ola

The people of Ola are proud of their cultural heritage and traditions. Here are a few things to keep in mind when visiting:

  • Respect local customs: Ola is a small town where people tend to be more conservative in their dress and behavior. Be respectful of local customs and avoid dressing inappropriately or behaving in ways that could be seen as offensive.
  • Learn a few Russian phrases: While many people in Ola speak English, it’s always appreciated when visitors make an effort to speak the local language. Learn a few basic phrases before your trip.
  • Observe religious traditions: Orthodox Christianity is the dominant religion in the region, and there are many churches and monasteries to visit. Be respectful of religious traditions and customs, such as removing your hat before entering a church.

Best Time to Visit Ola

The best time to visit Ola depends on what you’re looking for. The summer months (June-August) are the warmest and offer the best weather for outdoor activities like hiking and fishing. However, these months can also be the busiest and most expensive. The winter months (December-February) offer a unique opportunity to experience the region’s snowy landscapes and winter sports, such as dog sledding. However, the cold temperatures can be extreme, with temperatures dropping below -20°C (-4°F).

Do’s and Don’ts of Ola

Here are a few do’s and don’ts to keep in mind when visiting Ola:

Do:

  • Respect local customs and traditions.
  • Dress warmly and in layers, as the climate can be very cold.
  • Try the local cuisine and beverages.

Don’t:

  • Behave rudely or disrespectfully toward locals.
  • Venture off marked trails when hiking, as the area can be dangerous.
  • Travel alone in remote areas after dark.

Safety and Health

Safety in Ola

Overall, Ola is a safe destination for travelers. However, it’s important to take basic safety precautions, such as avoiding dark, isolated areas at night and keeping valuables out of sight. When hiking or engaging in outdoor activities, it’s important to stay on marked trails and carry a map and compass.

Health in Ola

Travelers to Ola should be up-to-date with routine vaccinations, such as measles, mumps, and rubella. If you plan to hike or spend time in remote areas, consider getting vaccinated against tick-borne diseases. To avoid food-borne illness, stick to cooked foods and avoid drinking tap water.

Getting to Ola: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Ola

The closest airport to Ola is the Sokol Airport in Magadan, which is approximately a 5-hour drive from the town. The airport offers flights to several destinations in Russia.

Bus Stations Near Ola

There are several bus stations in Magadan that offer service to Ola. The journey takes approximately 6 hours and offers stunning views of the surrounding countryside.

Other Options

While there are no trains that go directly to Ola, visitors can take a train to the nearby town of Susuman and then continue their journey by bus or car.

How to Get Around in Ola

The best way to get around in Ola is by car, as many of the hiking trails and natural attractions are located outside of town. However, visitors can also hire a local guide or join a tour to explore the area.

Where to Go Next from Ola: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

After exploring the natural beauty and cultural attractions of Ola, there are several nearby destinations and excursions to consider. Here are a few suggestions:

  • Magadan: The capital city of the Magadan Oblast offers a stark contrast to the small town of Ola. Visitors can explore the city’s museums and cultural attractions, as well as the lively local markets.
  • Susuman: This nearby town is known for its historic gold mines and the spectacular Kolyma Highway, which passes through the area.
  • Bukhta Ola: This picturesque bay is located just outside of Ola and offers a beautiful, secluded beach perfect for swimming and picnicking.

Conclusion

With its stunning natural beauty, rich history, and unique cultural identity, Ola is a destination that’s well worth exploring. From hiking and fishing to museum-hopping and Siberian husky tours, there’s no shortage of things to do and see in this fascinating part of Russia. So pack your bags, brush up on your Russian, and get ready for an adventure you won’t soon forget!
