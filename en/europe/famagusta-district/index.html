Introduction to Famagusta district

Located in the eastern part of Cyprus, Famagusta district offers a wide range of attractions for any type of traveler. Whether you are a history buff, nature lover, or adrenaline junkie, you will find something to do in this region. Famagusta district is known for its historical sites, stunning beaches, delicious food, and lively atmosphere. In this comprehensive guide, we’ll cover the best places to visit, things to do, where to eat and drink, where to stay, and much more.

List of Places to Visit in Famagusta district

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Salamis Ancient City: The ancient city of Salamis is a must-visit attraction for history lovers. You can explore the well-preserved ruins of this city, which was once an important harbor town dating back to 11th century BC.
  • St. Barnabas Monastery: The St. Barnabas Monastery is located in the village of Famagusta. This historical site is dedicated to the apostle Barnabas, who was born in Salamis. You can explore the museum and church on the site.
  • Othello’s Tower: The Othello’s Tower in Famagusta was built by the Venetians in the 14th century. It is believed that Shakespeare’s play “Othello” was inspired by this tower.

Natural Attractions of Famagusta district: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Cape Greco: Cape Greco is a national park located in the southeastern part of Cyprus. This area offers breathtaking views of the Mediterranean Sea and rugged cliffs. You can hike, bike, or take a boat tour to explore the natural beauty of this area.
  • Konnos Bay: Konnos Bay is a popular beach located near Cape Greco. This beach is known for its crystal clear waters and soft sand. It’s an ideal place for swimming, sunbathing, and snorkeling.
  • Ayia Napa Sculpture Park: This park is located in Ayia Napa and features a collection of contemporary sculptures by international artists. You can stroll through the park and admire the art while enjoying the beautiful scenery.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Famagusta district

  • Cape Greco Trail: This trail covers a distance of 8 kilometers and offers breathtaking coastal scenery. You can hike or bike along the trail while enjoying the fresh air and stunning views.
  • Stavrovouni Monastery Trail: This trail starts at the Stavrovouni monastery and covers a distance of 10 kilometers. You can hike through the rugged terrain while enjoying the panoramic views of the surrounding area.

Best Things to Do in Famagusta district: Activities and Tours

  • Water Sports: Famagusta district offers a wide range of water sports activities, including snorkeling, scuba diving, Jet Skiing, and parasailing. You can take guided tours to get the most out of your experience.
  • Food Tours: Famagusta district is known for its delicious cuisine, and there are many food tours available in this area. You can taste local dishes and learn about Cypriot culture at the same time.
  • Nightlife: Famagusta district is known for its lively nightlife, with numerous nightclubs and bars open until the early morning hours. You can dance the night away and enjoy the vibrant atmosphere of this region.

Food and Drink Scene of Famagusta district

Must-Try Dishes

  • Halloumi Cheese: Halloumi cheese is a Cypriot specialty and is a must-try in Famagusta district. This cheese is both soft and chewy and can be grilled or fried and served with bread or salad.
  • Souvlaki: Souvlaki is a popular grilled meat dish served with pita bread, tzatziki, and vegetables. You can find souvlaki stands throughout the district.
  • Meze: Meze is a traditional Cypriot meal that consists of many small dishes. You can taste a variety of local specialties in one sitting.

Local Beverages

  • Zivania: Zivania is a traditional Cypriot spirit made from grapes. It is a high-proof drink, and it is often served with meze.
  • Commandaria Wine: Commandaria wine is a sweet dessert wine that is made in Cyprus. It is a popular drink throughout the district.

Where to Stay in Famagusta district: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Grecian Park Hotel: This five-star hotel is located on the edge of Cape Greco and offers breathtaking views of the Mediterranean Sea. It features multiple swimming pools, a spa, and several restaurants.
  • Napa Suites: Napa Suites is a luxury adult-only hotel located in Ayia Napa. It features spacious suites, a private pool, and a restaurant.
  • The Sandcastles: The Sandcastles is a budget-friendly guesthouse located in Famagusta. It features clean rooms and a central location.

What to Buy in Famagusta district: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Lefkara Lace: Lefkara lace is a traditional handmade lace that is famous in Cyprus. It is a perfect souvenir to bring home.
  • Pottery: Pottery is also a popular souvenir in Famagusta district. You can find handcrafted pottery throughout the region.

Local Culture and Customs of Famagusta district

Famagusta district has a rich cultural heritage that is reflected in its traditions and customs. The people of Famagusta district are friendly and hospitable, and they love to share their culture with visitors. It is common to greet people with a handshake or a kiss on both cheeks. When entering a mosque, visitors are expected to remove their shoes and dress modestly. Tipping is appreciated, but not mandatory.

Best Time to Visit Famagusta district

The best time to visit Famagusta district is during the spring (March - May) or fall (September - November). The temperature during these months is mild, and the crowds are much smaller than in the summer months. The summer months (June - August) can be very crowded and hot.

Do’s and Don’ts of Famagusta district

Do:

  • Try the local cuisine.
  • Dress modestly when entering religious sites.
  • Be respectful of the local culture and customs.

Don’t:

  • Drink tap water.
  • Smoke in public areas.
  • Haggle in shops and markets.

Safety and Health

Safety in Famagusta district

Famagusta district is a safe place to visit, and crime rates are low. However, visitors should still be mindful of their surroundings and take precautions against pickpockets and theft. It is also important to be aware of the high temperatures during the summer months and to wear sunscreen and drink plenty of water.

Health in Famagusta district

The healthcare system in Famagusta district is of a good standard. Visitors should ensure they have comprehensive travel insurance to cover any medical expenses.

Getting to Famagusta district: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Famagusta district

The closest airport to Famagusta district is Larnaca International Airport, which is located approximately 40 minutes away. Paphos International Airport is also an option, but it is further away, approximately 2 hours by car.

Bus Stations Near Famagusta district

The main bus station in Famagusta district is located in the city of Famagusta. Buses run regularly from this station to other parts of the region.

Other Options

Taxis and rental cars are also available in Famagusta district. However, visitors should be aware that driving in Cyprus can be challenging, and the roads are often narrow and winding.

How to Get Around in Famagusta district

The best way to get around Famagusta district is by car or taxi. Rental cars are available in Famagusta, and taxis can be hailed on the street or booked in advance.

Where to Go Next from Famagusta district: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Larnaca: Larnaca is a city located south of Famagusta district and is known for its beautiful beachfront and picturesque marina.
  • Limassol: Limassol is a city located southwest of Famagusta district and is known for its historical sites and lively nightlife.
  • Nicosia: Nicosia is the capital city of Cyprus and is located northwest of Famagusta district. It is a city rich in history and culture and is home to several museums and galleries.

Conclusion

Famagusta district is a beautiful region of Cyprus that offers something for everyone. From its rich cultural heritage to its stunning natural scenery, you won’t be disappointed with a visit to this area. Plan your trip using this guide and get ready for an unforgettable adventure.
