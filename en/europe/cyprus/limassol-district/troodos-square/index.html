Introduction to Troodos Square

Located in the Limassol district in Cyprus, Troodos Square is a picturesque high-altitude location in the Troodos Mountains. As the highest point in Cyprus, it is a popular destination for tourists due to its scenic views, outdoor activities, and cultural attractions. With its rich history, diverse natural beauty, unique culture, and fantastic food scene, Troodos Square has something to offer every visitor. Whether you’re into hiking, biking, history, or cuisine, Troodos Square is a must-visit destination.

List of Places to Visit in Troodos Square

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Church of Panagia Podithou
  • Church of Archangel Michael
  • Troodos Geopark Visitor Centre
  • Troodos Folk Art Museum
  • The Tomb of Archbishop Makarios

Natural Attractions of Troodos Square: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Mount Olympus
  • Caledonia Waterfall
  • Troodos National Forest Park
  • Troodos Botanical Garden
  • Cedar Valley

Hiking/Biking Routes of Troodos Square

With its beautiful landscapes and terrain, Troodos Square is a paradise for hikers and bikers. There are numerous hiking and biking routes in the area, inclusive of the following:

  • Artemis Nature Trail
  • Atalante Trail
  • Kalidonia Trail
  • Persephone Trail
  • Psilo Dendro Trail
  • Troodos Square to Atalante Trail

Best Things to Do in Troodos Square: Activities and Tours

  • Take a cable car ride to the top of Mount Olympus
  • Visit mountain villages such as Platres and Pedoulas
  • Attend a traditional wine-tasting session
  • Explore the Troodos National Forest Park
  • Take ATV tours through the mountains
  • Watch out for the stars at Troodos Observatory

Week-Long Itinerary for Troodos Square

Here’s our recommended itinerary for a week-long trip to Troodos Square:

Day 1: Explore Troodos Square and take a cable car ride to Mount Olympus. Day 2: Visit the Troodos National Forest Park and go hiking in nature. Day 3: Visit Platres and Pedoulas, two of the most beautiful mountain villages. Day 4: Discover the cultural attractions of the Church of Archangel Michael, Troodos Geopark Visitor Centre, and Troodos Folk Art Museum. Day 5: Go on a wine-tasting session and visit a local winery. Day 6: Take an ATV tour through the mountains and end the day with stargazing at Troodos Observatory. Day 7: Relax and enjoy the local cuisine in Troodos Square.

Food and Drink Scene of Troodos Square

Must-Try Dishes

  • Souvla: delicious charcoal-grilled lamb or pork roasted on a spit.
  • Afelia: a pork dish with red wine sauce and coriander seeds.
  • Sheftalia: a savory sausage made with pork, onions, and parsley wrapped in caul fat.
  • Halloumi: a cheese made with goat and sheep milk, served grilled or fried.

Local Beverages

  • Zivania: a colorless, high-alcohol spirit made from grapes.
  • Commandaria: a Cypriot dessert wine made from two grape varieties.

Where to Stay in Troodos Square: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Forest Park Hotel: a luxurious hotel with an outdoor swimming pool, spa, and gym facilities.
  • New Helvetia Hotel: a cozy guesthouse with beautiful mountain views and a communal lounge area.
  • Rodon Hotel and Resort: an elegant hotel with stunning views of Mount Olympus and a good wine selection.
  • Avalon Traditional Village Houses: a quaint guesthouse with traditional-style lodgings and incredible views.

What to Buy in Troodos Square: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Handmade pottery from the local artisan village of Fasoula.
  • Bottles of locally-made wine or zivania.
  • Traditional Cypriot sweets like loukoumades, soaked in honey and cinnamon.

Local Culture and Customs of Troodos Square

Cyprus is a rich and diverse country, and Troodos Square is no exception. The island’s culture includes a mix of Greek and Turkish influences, and customs such as hospitality, food, and family values are deeply ingrained in the local culture.

Best Time to Visit Troodos Square

The best time to visit Troodos Square is between May and October when the weather is mild and sunny. The temperature in the mountains is typically lower than in other parts of Cyprus, making it the perfect escape from the summer heat.

Do’s and Don’ts of Troodos Square

Do:

  • Do explore the beautiful landscapes and go on a hike.
  • Do engage with the locals and try the traditional food and wine.
  • Do bring warm clothes as it gets colder at night.

Don’t:

  • Don’t litter or damage the natural environment.
  • Don’t disrespect religious sites and cultural institutions.
  • Don’t make loud noise in residential areas.

Safety and Health

Safety in Troodos Square

The crime rate in Cyprus is relatively low, and safety is not usually an issue in Troodos Square. Visitors are advised to take general safety precautions to keep themselves safe.

Health in Troodos Square

Healthcare in Cyprus is generally good, and visitors can expect to receive high-quality medical attention at reasonable prices. Visitors are advised to have comprehensive travel insurance and bring adequate medication as certain medicines may not be readily available.

The best way to get around Troodos Square is by car, taxi, or bus. Buses are relatively inexpensive, but car rentals give you the ability to explore the area at your own pace.

Where to Go Next from Troodos Square: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Limassol: visit the old town and the beach.
  • Nicosia: check out the architecture and museums.
  • Paphos: see the ancient ruins and the beautiful beaches.
