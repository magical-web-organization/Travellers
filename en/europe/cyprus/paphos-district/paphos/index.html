Introduction to Paphos

Paphos is a picturesque coastal town located in the southwestern part of Cyprus. This beautiful town boasts a rich history and culture, making it a popular tourist destination. From ancient ruins to stunning beaches, there’s something for everyone in Paphos. In this comprehensive travel guide, discover the best places to visit, food to try, activities to do, and more.

List of Places to Visit in Paphos

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Paphos is a hub of ancient history and culture. The town is dotted with numerous museums and historical places that showcase the region’s impressive heritage. Here are the must-visit cultural attractions in Paphos:

  • Paphos Archaeological Park: This vast park sprawl s over the ruins of the ancient city of Paphos and houses famous structures like the House of Dionysus and the House of Theseus.
  • Tombs of the Kings: A UNESCO World Heritage Site, the Tombs of the Kings comprise stunning underground tombs and chambers.
  • Paphos Byzantine Museum: Showcasing an extensive collection of Byzantine artifacts and religious objects, this museum features well-preserved mosaics from the 6th century.
  • Paphos Ethnographic Museum: This museum is housed in a 19th-century building and showcases traditional Cypriot clothing, furniture, pottery, and household items.

Natural Attractions of Paphos: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Paphos is famed for its natural beauty, with lush forests, golden beaches, and crystal-clear waters. Here are the top natural attractions in Paphos:

  • Aphrodite’s Rock: Also called Petra tou Romiou, this stunning natural rock formation is steeped in mythology; legend has it that the Greek goddess of love and beauty, Aphrodite, was born out of the sea foam here.
  • Coral Bay: This beautiful stretch of golden sand and crystal-clear waters is popular among locals and tourists alike.
  • Avakas Gorge: This natural wonder is perfect for adventurous travelers; explore the narrow canyon, gushing waterfalls & unique rock formations.
  • Akamas Peninsula: This protected national park is a biodiversity hotspot, with rare wildflowers, birds, and sea turtles. Take a boat tour to view the park’s stunning coastlines and bays.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Paphos

For those who love outdoor adventure and want to explore the natural beauty of Paphos on foot or by bike, there are plenty of trails and routes to explore. Here are some of the best hiking and biking routes in Paphos:

  • The Aphrodite Trail: This 7km hiking trail is one of the most popular and takes you through lush vegetation, historic sites and offers breathtaking sea views.
  • Baths of Aphrodite to Akamas: This 8km trail runs through Akamas national park and offers striking vistas of gorges and beaches as you trek through the rugged terrain..
  • Paphos Forest Nature Trail: This hiking trail takes you through pine forests, gurgling streams, and waterfalls.

Best Things to Do in Paphos: Activities and Tours

Paphos is a hub of fun and entertainment, with plenty of activities and tours for visitors to enjoy. Here are some of the best things to do in Paphos:

  • Water Sports: Enjoy jet-skiing, parasailing, or scuba diving at Paphos’s beaches and take in views of the stunning coastline.
  • Wine Tasting: Explore Paphos’s vineyards; the town is famous for its wine, and there are plenty of options for wine tasting and tours.
  • Boat Tours: From sunset cruises to fishing trips, take a boat tour and enjoy the stunning views of Paphos’s coastlines.
  • Golfing: Paphos has beautiful golf courses and is a popular destination for golf lovers.

Food and Drink Scene of Paphos

Paphos has a rich culinary heritage and is known for its delicious cuisine, be it traditional Cypriot fare or international cuisine. Here are some must-try dishes and beverages in Paphos:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Meze: A traditional Cypriot meal consisting of various small dishes, including dips, meats, and vegetables.
  • Halloumi: A cheese made from sheep and goat milk that’s a popular in Cypriot cuisine.
  • Stifado: A meat and onion stew that’s slow-cooked to perfection.

Local Beverages

  • Zivania: This traditional Cypriot spirit is made from grape or wine juice and flavored with anise. It can be enjoyed on its own or mixed into delicious cocktails.
  • Commandaria: This sweet dessert wine is unique to Cyprus and dates back to the ancient Greek times.

Where to Stay in Paphos: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

From luxury resorts to budget-friendly hostels, Paphos has plenty of options for accommodations. Here are some of the best places to stay in Paphos:

  • Almyra Hotel: A luxurious spa resort located on the seafront.
  • Akteon Holiday Village: A budget-friendly apartment complex located close to the beach.
  • Villa Argaka: A charming villa with a private pool and a stunning view of the sea.

What to Buy in Paphos: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Paphos has plenty of unique souvenirs and local products to bring home, from traditional textiles to delicious wine. Here are some must-buy souvenirs and local products:

  • Lace: Paphos is famed for its traditional lacework, which is mostly done by hand.
  • Pottery: Traditional Cypriot pottery is known for its intricate designs and striking colors.
  • Wine: The town is famous for its wine, and there are numerous wineries where you can sample local wines before bringing home a bottle or two.

Local Culture and Customs of Paphos

Paphos has a rich culture, with a mix of Greek and Cypriot traditions. Here are some local customs to be aware of when visiting Paphos:

  • Respect dress codes: When visiting churches or mosques, dress modestly and remove your shoes before entering.
  • Don’t touch religious artifacts: When visiting religious sites, be respectful and avoid touching religious artifacts or displays.
  • Practice basic etiquette: Always say ‘please’ and ‘thank you’ when interacting with locals.

Best Time to Visit Paphos

The best time to visit Paphos is in the shoulder seasons of spring (March to May) and autumn (September to November). The weather is pleasant and mild, and there are fewer crowds than in the peak summer season.

Do’s and Don’ts of Paphos

Do:

  • Try local cuisine and wine: Paphos is known for its delicious food and wine, so don’t miss out on trying them when you’re here.
  • Take a boat tour and explore Paphos’s coastline: Take a relaxing boat tour and enjoy the stunning views of the coastline.

Don’t:

  • Disrespect local customs and traditions: Be respectful of local customs and practices and follow etiquette when visiting religious sites or interacting with locals.
  • Litter: Keep Paphos clean and dispose of trash responsibly.

Safety and Health

Safety in Paphos

Paphos is a generally safe destination, with a low crime rate. However, tourists should exercise caution, especially in crowded areas, and keep their valuables safe.

Health in Paphos

Paphos has a good healthcare system, and visitors are advised to take out comprehensive travel insurance before visiting.

Getting to Paphos: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Paphos

Paphos International Airport is the main airport in the region and is located around 10km from the town center. Direct flights to Paphos are available from major European airports..

Bus Stations Near Paphos

Paphos Intercity Bus Station is located in the town center and has regular buses connecting the town to other parts of Cyprus.. Bus taxis are available from the station to reach various destinations.

Other Options

Taxis are widely available in Paphos and are a convenient option for transportation within the town.

How to Get Around in Paphos

Paphos is a relatively small town and can be easily explored on foot. However, taxis, buses, and rental cars are also available for those who prefer commuting.

Where to Go Next from Paphos: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Paphos is a perfect base for exploring other nearby destinations in Cyprus, Here are some must-visit nearby destinations:

  • Limassol: A bustling coastal town with stunning beaches and lively nightlife.
  • Nicosia: The capital city of Cyprus is a hub of culture, history, and art.
  • Troodos Mountains: This beautiful mountain range is famous for its hiking trails, skiing, and scenic views.

Conclusion

Paphos is a beautiful coastal town packed with rich history, culture, and natural beauty. With its ancient ruins, stunning beaches, and delicious cuisine, Paphos is a perfect destination for those wanting to experience the beauty of Cyprus.
