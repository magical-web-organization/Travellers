Avakas Gorge, located in the Paphos district of Cyprus, Europe, is a natural wonder that offers breathtaking vistas and outdoor adventures. This stunning gorge is a must-visit attraction for those looking to explore the beauty of Cyprus. In this guide, we will take you on a journey to discover the best things to do in Avakas Gorge, where to stay, what to eat, and much more.

Introduction to Avakas Gorge

Avakas Gorge is a natural limestone canyon that extends for approximately 3 kilometers in length. It is a protected area that is home to many species of flora and fauna such as the Cyprus oak, strawberry tree, and the Cyprus mouflon. The gorge is a popular hiking destination due to its stunning scenic views and rugged terrain.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Avakas Gorge

Avakas Gorge has numerous hiking and biking trails for visitors to explore, ranging from easy to challenging levels. The most popular hiking trail is the Avakas Gorge Trail which takes approximately 2-3 hours to walk. The terrain is rugged and rocky, so it is recommended that visitors wear appropriate footwear and bring enough water.

Food and Drink Scene of Avakas Gorge

Avakas Gorge has a unique and diverse food and drink scene that reflects the local culture and traditions. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Souvla (grilled meat on a skewer)
  • Meze (a small plate dish of various local foods)
  • Halloumi cheese

Local Beverages

  • Zivania (traditional grape-based alcohol)
  • Commandaria (sweet dessert wine)

Local Culture and Customs of Avakas Gorge

The local culture of Avakas Gorge is deeply rooted in the island’s history and traditions. Visitors should be respectful of the local customs and dress modestly when visiting religious sites.

Best Time to Visit Avakas Gorge

The best time to visit Avakas Gorge is during the spring and autumn months when the weather is mild, and the flora is in full bloom. The summer months can be hot and humid, whereas the winter months can be rainy, making it difficult to hike the trails.

Do’s and Don’ts of Avakas Gorge

Here are some important do’s and don’ts to keep in mind when visiting Avakas Gorge:

Do:

  • Bring plenty of water and wear appropriate footwear when hiking.
  • Respect the local culture and dress modestly when visiting religious sites.
  • Take your litter with you and keep the area clean and beautiful.

Don’t:

  • Feed or disturb the local wildlife.
  • Remove or damage any plants or rocks in the gorge.
  • Smoke in the natural areas.

Safety and Health

Safety in Avakas Gorge

Visitors should be cautious when hiking in the Avakas Gorge due to the rugged terrain. It is recommended that visitors wear appropriate footwear and bring enough water. Trekking poles or walking sticks can be useful for uneven terrain and rocky surfaces.

Health in Avakas Gorge

Visitors should have a first-aid kit with them on the hike, and also pack sunscreen and insect repellent along with their medicines.

Getting to Avakas Gorge: Transportation Options and Logistics

Avakas Gorge is located on the west coast of Cyprus, and it is accessible by car. There are several buses that run from Paphos city to the gorge, but the schedules are limited. The closest airports are Paphos International Airport (18 km) and Larnaca International Airport (139 km).

Airports Near Avakas Gorge

  • Paphos International Airport
  • Larnaca International Airport

Bus Stations Near Avakas Gorge

  • Paphos Bus Station

Other Options

Visitors can also hire a taxi or book a guided tour to Avakas Gorge.

How to Get Around in Avakas Gorge

The best way to get around Avakas Gorge is on foot. Visitors can also rent a bike or hire a horse to explore the gorge.
