Introduction to Paphos district

Located in the southwestern part of Cyprus, Paphos district is a popular tourist destination known for its rich history, beautiful beaches, stunning landscapes, and friendly locals. It encompasses the town of Paphos, which is the capital of the district and one of the most significant historical and cultural centers on the island. The district is famous for its UNESCO World Heritage Sites, including the Tombs of the Kings and the ancient city of Kourion. Whether you’re an avid history buff or a nature lover, there are plenty of things to see and do in Paphos district for a memorable vacation experience.

List of Places to Visit in Paphos district

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Paphos Archaeological Park

The Paphos Archaeological Park is located at the heart of the town and is home to some of the most significant ancient ruins on the island, including the famous House of Dionysus, an impressive Roman villa with stunning mosaics.

Tombs of the Kings

The Tombs of the Kings is an ancient necropolis situated near the Paphos harbour. It is a UNESCO World Heritage Site and features several underground tombs that are lavishly decorated with frescoes and carvings.

Kato Paphos Archaeological Site

The Kato Paphos Archaeological Site is located in the town of Paphos and is home to several historical buildings, including the House of Theseus, the Villa of Orpheus, and several ancient marketplaces.

Agios Neophytos Monastery

The Agios Neophytos Monastery is a beautiful monastic complex located near Tala Village. It was founded in the 12th century and is home to some stunning Byzantine frescoes.

Natural Attractions of Paphos district: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Akamas Peninsula

Akamas Peninsula is a stunning nature reserve with diverse wildlife, including rare bird species and sea turtles. The area is perfect for hiking, cycling, or exploring on horseback.

Petra Tou Romiou

Petra Tou Romiou is a popular landmark located on a rocky coast just outside of Paphos. According to legend, the Goddess Aphrodite emerged from the sea at this spot.

Avakas Gorge

Avakas Gorge is a natural wonder located on the Akamas Peninsula. It features breathtaking limestone formations, lush vegetation, and a river that runs through the gorge.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Paphos district

Akamas Peninsula Trails

The Akamas Peninsula has several hiking trails, including the Aphrodite Trail, the Adonis Trail, and the Smigies Trail. These trails vary in difficulty and offer stunning views of the sea and countryside.

Troodos Mountains

If you’re up for a challenge, the Troodos Mountains have several hiking and mountain biking trails. The trails range from easy to difficult and offer spectacular views.

Best Things to Do in Paphos district: Activities and Tours

Water Sports

Paphos district has some of the best beaches in Cyprus, and they offer plenty of activities to keep you entertained. You can try watersports such as jet skiing, parasailing, snorkeling, and much more.

Boat Tours

Boat tours are popular in Paphos district, and you can choose from several options, including sightseeing cruises, sunset cruises, and even fishing trips.

Food and Drink Scene of Paphos district

Must-Try Dishes

No trip to Paphos district would be complete without trying the traditional Cypriot cuisine. Some popular dishes include souvlaki, moussaka, grilled halloumi, and traditional pies.

Local Beverages

For drinks, try the local wines, which are fresh, smooth, and have a distinct fruity flavor. You can also try ‘zivania’ - a local brandy.

Where to Stay in Paphos district: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Whether you’re looking for luxury hotels, hostels, or guesthouses, Paphos district has a wide selection of accommodation options to choose from. Some popular options include:

  • Anemi Hotel & Suites
  • Azia Resort & Spa
  • Paphos Gardens Holiday Resort

What to Buy in Paphos district: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Some popular souvenirs to bring home from Paphos district include olive oil, traditional textiles, handmade pottery, and jewelry made of local materials such as silver.

Local Culture and Customs of Paphos district

The locals in Paphos district are friendly, hospitable, and take pride in their rich heritage. When visiting historical sites, it is important to dress modestly out of respect for the culture.

Best Time to Visit Paphos district

The best time to visit Paphos district is during the spring (March to May) or autumn (September to November) when the weather is mild, and the crowds are smaller.

Do’s and Don’ts of Paphos district

Do:

  • Dress modestly at historical sites
  • Try the local cuisine and wines
  • Respect the locals

Don’t:

  • Litter on the beaches or in the towns
  • Disrespect the local customs and traditions

Safety and Health

Safety in Paphos district

Paphos district is generally a safe place to visit, but tourist areas can be targeted by pickpockets and thieves, so it’s important to stay aware of your surroundings and keep your valuables secure.

Health in Paphos district

The healthcare system in Paphos district is good, and there are several public and private hospitals and clinics available.

Getting to Paphos district: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Paphos district

Paphos International Airport is the main airport serving the district, and it is located about 8km from the town of Paphos.

Bus Stations Near Paphos district

There are several bus stations in the district, including the Paphos Main Bus Station and the Karavella Bus Station, which provide routes to other parts of the island.

Other Options

You can also rent a car or take a taxi to explore the district at your own pace.

How to Get Around in Paphos district

The best way to explore Paphos district is by car, especially if you want to visit the outlying areas. There are also buses, taxis, and bicycles available for transportation.

Where to Go Next from Paphos district: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

If you want to explore more of Cyprus, there are several nearby destinations worth visiting, including:

  • Limassol: The second-largest city in Cyprus, known for its beaches, nightlife, and cultural heritage.
  • Nicosia: The capital city of Cyprus, known for its museums, galleries, and charming old town.
  • Larnaca: A coastal city known for its beaches, historic sites, and lively nightlife.

Conclusion

Paphos district is a beautiful and historic part of Cyprus, with something to suit every type of traveler. From exploring ancient ruins to hiking scenic trails and enjoying the stunning beaches, there is never a dull moment in Paphos district. With this comprehensive travel guide, you’re sure to have an unforgettable trip to Paphos district.
