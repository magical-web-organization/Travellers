Introduction to Akrotiri and Dhekelia

Akrotiri and Dhekelia is a British Overseas Territory on the island of Cyprus, located in the eastern Mediterranean. The area covers two military bases, which are used primarily by British armed forces. However, tourists are welcome to visit and explore the beautiful scenery and interesting history of this unique part of Cyprus.

List of Places to Visit in Akrotiri and Dhekelia

There are many exciting places to visit in Akrotiri and Dhekelia, from fascinating museums to breathtaking natural attractions. Here are some of the top sights to see:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Akrotiri Archaeological Site - This ancient Minoan settlement was buried in volcanic ash and preserved for centuries. Visitors can see well-preserved structures, frescoes, and artifacts from the Bronze Age.
  • Dhekelia Power Station - This decommissioned power plant was built in the 1950s and is now open for guided tours.
  • The Old City of Famagusta - This historic city was once a thriving center of commerce, and is now a fascinating look at ancient architecture and history.
  • Larnaka Salt Lake - A stunning natural wonder, this lake is home to an array of migratory birds as well as picturesque walking paths.

Natural Attractions of Akrotiri and Dhekelia: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Cape Greco - A popular destination for hikers, Cape Greco is a protected nature reserve that offers stunning views of the Mediterranean Sea, as well as a range of flora and fauna.
  • Ayia Napa Sea Caves - Located near the popular beach resort of Ayia Napa, these sea caves offer a unique geological experience, as well as snorkeling and diving opportunities.
  • Fig Tree Bay - One of the most popular beaches in Cyprus, Fig Tree Bay offers calm waters and soft golden sand.
  • Kourion Amphitheatre - This ancient Greek amphitheater is still used for events and performances today, and commands a stunning view of the surrounding landscape.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Akrotiri and Dhekelia

For those looking to explore on foot or by bicycle, there are numerous hiking and biking routes available in Akrotiri and Dhekelia. Here are some of the most popular:

  • Akrotiri Salt Lake Trail - This easy trail takes you through the scenic wetlands of Akrotiri Salt Lake, home to a variety of migratory birds.
  • Cape Greco Nature Trail - A moderate hike through the stunning Cape Greco nature reserve that offers amazing views of the Mediterranean Sea.
  • Dhekelia Garrison Loop - A flat and easy bike ride that takes you around the perimeter of the Dhekelia Garrison.

Best Things to Do in Akrotiri and Dhekelia: Activities and Tours

From water sports to cultural tours, there are plenty of activities and tours to keep you entertained in Akrotiri and Dhekelia.

  • Swim and sunbathe on one of the many beautiful beaches in the area.
  • Take a guided tour of the Akrotiri Archaeological Site to learn about its rich history.
  • Explore the area on horseback with one of the local riding tours.
  • Go scuba diving or snorkeling in the crystal-clear waters of the Mediterranean Sea.

Week-Long Itinerary for Akrotiri and Dhekelia

Here’s a recommended itinerary for those planning to spend a week in Akrotiri and Dhekelia:

  • Day 1: Explore the Akrotiri Salt Lake and visit the Akrotiri Archaeological Site.
  • Day 2: Visit the Old City of Famagusta and the Dhekelia Power Station.
  • Day 3: Spend the day at Cape Greco, hiking or biking the nature trail and enjoying the amazing views.
  • Day 4: Take a guided tour of the Larnaka Salt Lake and the surrounding area.
  • Day 5: Visit Fig Tree Bay and nearby Kourion Amphitheatre.
  • Day 6: Explore the Ayia Napa sea caves and enjoy water sports or relaxation at the beach.
  • Day 7: Take an organized day trip to the nearby Troodos Mountains to see a different side of Cyprus.

Food and Drink Scene of Akrotiri and Dhekelia

No trip to Akrotiri and Dhekelia is complete without sampling some of the delicious local cuisine and refreshing beverages.

Must-Try Dishes

  • Souvla - A popular dish made from slowly roasted pork or lamb on a spit.
  • Stifado - A hearty stew made with beef or rabbit, onions, and red wine.
  • Halloumi cheese - A local cheese that’s typically grilled or fried and used in a variety of dishes.

Local Beverages

  • Commandaria wine - A local sweet dessert wine that’s been produced in the region for over 2000 years.
  • Zivania - A potent local spirit made from grapes or figs.

Where to Stay in Akrotiri and Dhekelia: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

There are a variety of accommodations available in Akrotiri and Dhekelia to suit any budget, including hotels, hostels, and guesthouses. Some popular options include:

  • Palm Beach Hotel & Bungalows - A 4-star beachfront hotel offering stunning views and convenient access to local attractions.
  • Bella View Hotel - A luxurious hotel set in the hills of Kyrenia, offering a quieter, more secluded atmosphere.
  • Dhekelia Beach Hotel - A popular beachfront hotel offering a range of amenities and easy access to water sports and other activities.

What to Buy in Akrotiri and Dhekelia: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Visitors to Akrotiri and Dhekelia can find a variety of unique souvenirs and local products to bring home, including:

  • Halloumi cheese - A popular souvenir, many local shops will vacuum-seal cheese so it can be transported easily.
  • Traditional Cypriot pottery - Hand-crafted pottery items are a beautiful and practical souvenir.
  • Olive oil - Cyprus is known for its high-quality olive oil, which makes a great gift for foodies.

Local Culture and Customs of Akrotiri and Dhekelia

The culture of Akrotiri and Dhekelia is a unique blend of British and Cypriot influences. Visitors should be respectful of local customs and traditions, and dress modestly when visiting religious sites.

Best Time to Visit Akrotiri and Dhekelia

The best time to visit Akrotiri and Dhekelia is in the spring or fall, when temperatures are mild and comfortable. Summers can be hot and crowded, while winters can be chilly and rainy.

Do’s and Don’ts of Akrotiri and Dhekelia

Do:

  • Dress modestly when visiting religious sites.
  • Respect local customs and traditions.
  • Try the local cuisine and beverages.

Don’t:

  • Take photographs of military installations or personnel.
  • Leave valuables unattended, as petty crime can be an issue in tourist areas.
  • Expect everyone to speak English fluently - it’s always appreciated to learn a few basic phrases in the local language.

Safety and Health

Safety in Akrotiri and Dhekelia

Akrotiri and Dhekelia is a safe destination, but visitors should take precautions against petty crime, especially in busy tourist areas.

Health in Akrotiri and Dhekelia

Visitors should ensure that their routine vaccinations are up to date before traveling to Akrotiri and Dhekelia. The area has a high sun index, so it’s important to use sunscreen and stay hydrated.

Getting to Akrotiri and Dhekelia: Transportation Options and Logistics

There are several ways to get to Akrotiri and Dhekelia, depending on your location and preferences.

Airports Near Akrotiri and Dhekelia

  • Larnaca International Airport is the closest airport to Akrotiri and Dhekelia, located about 50 kilometers away.
  • Paphos International Airport is also located nearby, about 130 kilometers away.

Bus Stations Near Akrotiri and Dhekelia

  • The Larnaca Bus Station is the closest bus station to Akrotiri and Dhekelia, located about 5 kilometers away.

Other Options

  • Taxis are readily available in the area, and car rental is also a popular option for those looking to explore at their own pace.

How to Get Around in Akrotiri and Dhekelia

Renting a car is the easiest way to get around Akrotiri and Dhekelia, as there are few public transportation options available. Taxis and organized tours are also options for those who prefer not to drive.

Where to Go Next from Akrotiri and Dhekelia: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Cyprus is a beautiful island with many other destinations worth exploring. Some nearby destinations and excursions include:

  • Limassol - A bustling coastal city known for its nightlife and cultural events.
  • Nicosia - The capital city of Cyprus, which offers a mix of modern attractions and ancient history.
  • Paphos - A coastal city with a rich archaeological history, including several UNESCO World Heritage sites.

Conclusion

Akrotiri and Dhekelia is a fascinating and unique destination that offers a blend of British and Cypriot culture and history. Visitors can explore ancient ruins, stunning natural scenery, and sample delicious local cuisine and beverages. We hope this comprehensive guide helps you plan your next trip to Akrotiri and Dhekelia!
