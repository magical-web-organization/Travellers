Introduction to Kapparis

Located in the Famagusta district (South) of Cyprus, Kapparis is a small coastal town that offers visitors a blend of rich culture and natural beauty. The town is known for its serene atmosphere, white sandy beaches, crystal-clear waters, and mouth-watering cuisine. Whether you are looking for a peaceful holiday or an adventurous one, Kapparis has something to offer.

List of Places to Visit in Kapparis

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Kapparis is rich in history and culture, and visitors can experience it through the town’s museums and historical places. One must-visit attraction is the Byzantine Museum, which houses an impressive collection of Byzantine art and artifacts. Another attraction is the Church of Profitis Elias, a beautiful 19th-century church perched atop a hill, providing stunning views of the town and the sea.

Natural Attractions of Kapparis: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Kapparis is home to several natural attractions where visitors can enjoy a range of outdoor activities. Cape Greco, a protected nature park, is a must-visit for its breathtaking views of the sea and its diverse flora and fauna. Visitors can also enjoy water sports activities, such as jet skiing and paddleboarding, or take a relaxing boat trip to explore the town’s stunning coastline.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Kapparis

Kapparis offers a range of hiking and biking trails, providing visitors with an opportunity to explore the town’s natural beauty. The Kapparis Trail is a scenic trail that takes visitors through the town’s hills and offers panoramic views of the surrounding countryside. For more challenging trails, visitors can head to Cape Greco or visit the Troodos Mountains for a day trip.

Best Things to Do in Kapparis: Activities and Tours

Visitors to Kapparis can enjoy a range of activities and tours, including cultural tours, water sports, and food tours. A popular activity is visiting a local winery to sample the town’s delicious wines or going on a food tour to discover the town’s authentic cuisine. A trip to Kapparis is incomplete without trying fresh seafood, including octopus, calamari, and fish.

Week-Long Itinerary for Kapparis

For visitors planning a week-long itinerary for Kapparis, we recommend exploring the town’s natural and cultural attractions in the first two days. On the third and fourth days, visitors can take up hiking or biking trails or enjoy water sports activities. On the fifth and sixth days, visitors can take a cultural tour, food tour or visit a winery. On the last day, visitors can relax on the beach or enjoy a boat trip around the coast.

Food and Drink Scene of Kapparis

Must-Try Dishes

Kapparis is known for its delicious Mediterranean cuisine. The town’s signature dishes include fresh seafood, meze, and souvlaki. Visitors can enjoy a range of meze dishes, including grilled halloumi, hummus, and tzatziki. As for seafood, visitors must try the grilled octopus, calamari, and fresh fish.

Local Beverages

The town’s local drinks include Commandaria, a sweet wine made from sun-dried grapes, and Zivania, a traditional Cypriot drink made from distilled grapes. Visitors can also enjoy the town’s delicious coffee and lemonades made from fresh lemons.

Where to Stay in Kapparis: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Kapparis offers a range of accommodation options, including hotels, hostels, and guesthouses. Visitors looking for a luxurious stay can book a room at the Malama Beach Holiday Village or the Golden Coast Beach Hotel. For a more affordable stay, visitors can opt for the Sunwing Sandy Bay Beach Hotel or the Myro Androu Hotel Apartments.

What to Buy in Kapparis: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Visitors can buy several local products from Kapparis to take home as souvenirs. One can buy delicious honey, wine, and olive oil, all of which are produced locally. Visitors can also buy handmade pottery and jewelry or traditional lace pieces unique to Cyprus.

Local Culture and Customs of Kapparis

Cyprus has a unique culture that visitors can experience in Kapparis. Visitors must be respectful of the local customs, such as covering their head, arms, and legs when visiting religious places. Visitors can also participate in traditional events, such as the Kataklysmos festival, which celebrates the coming of summer.

Best Time to Visit Kapparis

The best time to visit Kapparis is between April and October when the weather is sunny and pleasant. Summers in Kapparis can get hot, making the beaches a perfect spot for relaxation and water activities.

Do’s and Don’ts of Kapparis

Do:

  • Do try the local cuisine, especially the fresh seafood and meze dishes.
  • Do take part in the town’s cultural events and traditions.
  • Do be respectful of the local customs and dress modestly while visiting religious sites.

Don’t:

  • Don’t litter on the beaches or in public areas.
  • Don’t take photographs of people without their permission.
  • Don’t be loud or disrespectful in public places.

Safety and Health

Safety in Kapparis

Kapparis is a safe town for tourists, with low crime rates. However, visitors must be cautious and keep their belongings safe, especially when visiting crowded areas and beaches. Visitors must also follow the safety instructions provided while taking part in any adventure or water sport activities.

Health in Kapparis

Kapparis has several medical facilities, including hospitals and clinics, that offer high-quality healthcare services. Visitors must carry necessary medication and follow the local COVID-19 health protocols.

Getting to Kapparis: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Kapparis

Larnaca International Airport is the nearest airport to Kapparis, situated only 68.5 km away. Visitors can take a taxi or a bus to reach Kapparis from the airport.

Bus Stations Near Kapparis

Kapparis is well-connected to nearby towns and cities through buses. Visitors can take a bus from Paralimni to reach Kapparis; the journey takes 15 minutes.

Other Options

Visitors can also rent a car or take a taxi to reach Kapparis from any of the nearby towns or airports.

How to Get Around in Kapparis

Kapparis is a small town that one can easily explore on foot. Visitors can also rent a bike or a car to visit nearby attractions. Buses also run between Kapparis and nearby towns and attractions.

Where to Go Next from Kapparis: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Visitors can explore nearby towns and cities, such as Larnaca, Limassol, and Paphos, to experience more of Cyprus’s culture and history. Visitors can also take day trips to the Troodos Mountains or the Akamas Peninsula to experience the natural beauty of Cyprus.

Conclusion

Kapparis offers visitors a unique experience, blending the town’s natural beauty, history, and culture. With this comprehensive travel guide, visitors can easily explore the town’s best attractions, food, accommodation, and transportation options. Plan your next trip to Kapparis and experience all that Cyprus has to offer.
