Introduction to Protaras

Located in the Famagusta district of Cyprus, Protaras is a picturesque resort town that is perfect for a summer getaway. With its crystal clear waters, sandy beaches, and rugged landscapes, Protaras is a breathtaking destination that has something for everyone. In this comprehensive tour guide, we explore the best places to visit, things to do, and where to stay in Protaras, as well as some local customs and safety information to help you plan your perfect vacation.

List of Places to Visit in Protaras

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

To discover the rich history of Protaras, visit some of the following museums and historical places:

  • Church of Profitis Ilias: This church was built in 1984 and is dedicated to Prophet Elijah. It is located on top of a hill and offers stunning views of the surrounding area.
  • Thalassa Municipal Museum: This museum is housed in a traditional Greek village house and showcases the history and culture of Protaras, with a special focus on fishing and marine life.
  • Agia Napa Monastery: Though technically not in Protaras, a visit to this 16th-century monastery is definitely worth the short journey. It features stunning Baroque architecture and beautiful frescoes.

Natural Attractions of Protaras: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

For those who love the outdoors, Protaras has no shortage of natural attractions and outdoor adventures:

  • Cape Greco National Park: Located just a short drive from Protaras, this park offers stunning views of the coastline, hiking trails, and sea caves.
  • Fig Tree Bay: This is one of the most beautiful beaches in Protaras, with its long stretch of fine white sand and crystal-clear water.
  • Konnos Bay: Another beautiful beach located just a few kilometers south of Protaras, Konnos Bay is a great spot for snorkeling and swimming.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Protaras

One of the best ways to discover the natural beauty of Protaras is by hiking or biking. Some popular routes include:

  • Cape Greco Trail: This trail winds through the Cape Greco National Park and offers stunning views of the coastline.
  • Protaras Coastal Promenade: This is a 9km trail that runs along the coast from Fig Tree Bay to Konnos Bay, offering breathtaking views of the Mediterranean Sea.

Best Things to Do in Protaras: Activities and Tours

To get the most out of your stay in Protaras, be sure to check out some of the following activities and tours:

  • Scuba Diving: With its crystal-clear waters, Protaras is a great spot for scuba diving, with a range of dive sites available.
  • Boat Tours: Explore the coastline of Protaras with a boat tour, which often includes stops at secluded beaches and sea caves.
  • Water Sports: There are plenty of water sports available in Protaras, including jet skiing, parasailing, and wakeboarding.

Week-Long Itinerary for Protaras

Here’s a suggested week-long itinerary for your stay in Protaras:

  • Day 1: Visit Fig Tree Bay and relax on the beautiful beach.
  • Day 2: Hike the Cape Greco Trail and explore the sea caves.
  • Day 3: Take a day trip to Agia Napa and visit the Monastery.
  • Day 4: Go scuba diving and discover the underwater world of Protaras.
  • Day 5: Take a boat tour along the coastline of Protaras.
  • Day 6: Visit the Thalassa Municipal Museum and learn about the history and culture of Protaras.
  • Day 7: Relax and enjoy the beautiful beaches of Protaras.

Food and Drink Scene of Protaras

Must-Try Dishes

To sample some of the local cuisine of Protaras, be sure to try some of these dishes:

  • Souvlaki: Grilled meat skewers served with pita bread, salad, and tzatziki.
  • Kleftiko: A traditional Cypriot dish made with slow-cooked lamb, potatoes, and vegetables.
  • Halloumi: A salty cheese made from goat and sheep milk. It’s commonly served grilled or fried.

Local Beverages

When it comes to drinks, Protaras offers a range of options:

  • Zivania: A traditional Cypriot spirit made from grape pomace, typically served as a digestive after a meal.
  • Keo Beer: A popular local beer that comes in both lager and ale varieties.
  • Commandaria: A sweet dessert wine that’s been produced in Cyprus for centuries.

Where to Stay in Protaras: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Protaras has no shortage of accommodation options to fit any budget:

  • Grecian Park Hotel: A 5-star hotel located on a hill with stunning views of the Mediterranean Sea.
  • Byzantio Hotel Apartments: A budget-friendly option featuring air-conditioned apartments with kitchenettes.
  • Sunrise Pearl Hotel & Spa: A luxury beachfront hotel with a spa and infinity pool.

What to Buy in Protaras: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Take home a piece of Protaras with some of these local products:

  • Lefkara Lace: This traditional Cypriot lace is handmade and makes for a beautiful and unique souvenir.
  • Loukoumia: Also known as “Turkish Delight,” this sweet treat comes in a variety of flavors and is a must-try.
  • Commandaria Wine: Take a bottle home to enjoy a taste of Cyprus long after your vacation has ended.

Local Culture and Customs of Protaras

To respect the local culture and customs of Protaras, keep these tips in mind:

  • Dress conservatively when visiting religious sites.
  • Remove your shoes before entering a Cypriot home.
  • Be mindful of noise levels, especially during the afternoon siesta.

Best Time to Visit Protaras

The best time to visit Protaras is in the summer months, from June through August, when the weather is warm and sunny. However, if you prefer cooler weather and fewer crowds, the spring and fall months can also be a great time to visit.

Do’s and Don’ts of Protaras

Do:

  • Respect the local culture and customs.
  • Visit the beautiful beaches and natural attractions.
  • Try the local cuisine.

Don’t:

  • Leave trash on the beaches or in natural areas.
  • Be disrespectful or loud in public spaces.
  • Wear revealing clothing when visiting religious sites.

Safety and Health

Safety in Protaras

Protaras is a safe destination for tourists, with low crime rates and friendly locals. However, as with any destination, it’s important to take basic precautions, such as locking your doors and windows at night and keeping valuables out of sight.

Health in Protaras

Protaras has a modern healthcare system, with several hospitals and clinics available in the region. It’s always a good idea to have travel insurance when visiting any foreign country to ensure that you’re covered in case of a medical emergency.

Getting to Protaras: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Protaras

The closest international airport to Protaras is Larnaca International Airport, which is located about 65 km away. There are frequent buses and taxis available to transfer you to Protaras from the airport.

Bus Stations Near Protaras

The Intercity Buses of Cyprus connect Protaras with major cities throughout the country, including Nicosia and Limassol. The bus station in Protaras is located on the main street, making it easy to access from anywhere in town.

Other Options

If you prefer to travel by sea, there are ferry services available that connect Cyprus with neighboring countries like Greece and Turkey.

How to Get Around in Protaras

The best way to get around in Protaras is by renting a car or scooter. Alternatively, there are also taxis and buses available, though buses can be infrequent and not always reliable.

Where to Go Next from Protaras: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

If you’re looking to explore more of Cyprus, here are some nearby destinations that are worth a visit:

  • Larnaca: A charming coastal city with beautiful beaches and a rich history, located just an hour’s drive from Protaras.
  • Limassol: The second largest city in Cyprus and a bustling cultural hub, located about 1.5 hours’ drive from Protaras.
  • Paphos: Located on the western side of Cyprus, Paphos is known for its archaeological sites and stunning coastline.

Conclusion

Protaras is a paradise for nature lovers, adventure seekers, and beach bums alike. With its beautiful beaches, rugged landscapes, and rich culture, it’s the perfect destination for a summer getaway. This tour guide will help you get the most out of your stay in Protaras, from exploring the natural attractions to discovering local culture and customs, ensuring that your vacation is unforgettable.
