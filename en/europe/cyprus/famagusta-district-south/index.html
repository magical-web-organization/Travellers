Introduction to Famagusta District (South)

Famagusta District (South) is a region located in the eastern part of the Republic of Cyprus. It is known for its stunning coastline, rich history, and diverse culture. Famagusta District (South) is divided into two parts, namely, the Northern Cyprus and the Republic of Cyprus. This travel guide will focus on the southern part of the district, which is governed by the Republic of Cyprus.

Week-Long Itinerary for Famagusta District (South)

Day 1: Visit St. Nicholas Cathedral, Othello Castle, and Famagusta Walled City.

Day 2: Explore the ancient ruins of Salamis Ancient City and relax at Palm Beach.

Day 3: Hike the Avakas Gorge Hiking Trail and explore Ayia Napa Sea Caves.

Day 4: Take a boat tour to Konnos Beach and Fig Tree Bay.

Day 5: Visit the Ayia Napa Monastery and experience the nightlife in Ayia Napa.

Day 6: Bike the Kantara to Kalavasos Bike Trail and visit the Cape Greco National Forest Park.

Day 7: Relax and enjoy the local cuisine and culture.

Local Culture and Customs of Famagusta District (South)

Famagusta District (South) has a rich and diverse culture that has been influenced by various civilizations throughout history. Some customs and traditions to be aware of include:

Best Time to Visit Famagusta District (South)

The best time to visit Famagusta District (South) is during the months of May to October when the weather is warm and sunny. July and August tend to be the busiest months, while June and September offer more moderate crowds and lower prices.
