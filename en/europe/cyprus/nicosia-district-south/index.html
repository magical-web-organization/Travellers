Introduction to Nicosia district (South)

Nicosia is the capital and largest city of Cyprus. The south part of Nicosia has a very rich history dating back to the Bronze Age. The present-day Nicosia district (South) offers an unparalleled experience with its narrow streets, medieval fortifications, ancient churches, and museums. There is a lot to explore for travellers who are passionate about history, culture, and nature.

List of Places to Visit in Nicosia district (South)

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

The south part of Nicosia has an abundance of museums and historical places that offer a glimpse into the rich history of the area. Some of the most popular museums and historical places are:

  • Cyprus Museum: This is the oldest and largest archaeological museum in Cyprus. It is home to a vast collection of antiquities and artefacts that showcase Cyprus’s history and culture.

  • Old City Walls: The Venetian walls surrounding the old city of Nicosia. These walls have survived countless attacks and battles throughout history and are a must-see for any visitor.

  • Büyük Han: This 16th-century inn is one of the most beautiful Ottoman buildings in Cyprus. It has been restored and converted into a cultural centre where traditional crafts and goods are showcased.

Natural Attractions of Nicosia district (South): Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

For nature lovers, the south part of Nicosia offers many outdoor adventures. These are some of the most popular natural attractions:

  • Troodos Mountains: These mountains are the highest mountain range in Cyprus, attracting hikers, mountain bikers, and nature enthusiasts.

  • Machairas Forest Station: This forest station is located at the heart of the Machairas Mountains. It offers hiking and cycling trails of various levels of difficulty.

  • Kionia Mountain: This mountain is a popular destination for hiking and photography. The views from the top are breathtaking.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Nicosia district (South)

The mountainous terrain of Nicosia district (South) offers numerous hiking and biking routes for visitors of all levels of experience. Here are some of the most popular routes:

  • Artemis Nature Trail: This trail offers magnificent views of Troodos Mountains and is ideal for families with children.

  • Madari Circular Walk: This walk takes you through the stunning Madari Range and offers breathtaking views of the area.

  • Tzielefos Nature Trail: This panoramic trail is a must-do if you are an experienced hiker. It offers a challenging hike with stunning views along the way.

Best Things to Do in Nicosia district (South): Activities and Tours

Nicosia district (South) offers plenty of activities and tours for visitors of all ages. Here are some of the most popular things to do:

  • Explore the Old City: The old city of Nicosia offers a glimpse into the rich history and culture of the area. Take a guided tour or explore on your own.

  • Visit the Cyprus Museum: This museum houses a vast collection of artefacts from the Bronze Age to the present day.

  • Join a Wine Tasting Tour: Cyprus produces some excellent wines. Join a wine tasting tour to discover the best of what the area has to offer.

Week-Long Itinerary for Nicosia district (South)

Here is a sample itinerary for a week-long trip to Nicosia district (South):

Day 1: Explore the old city of Nicosia, visit the Selimiye Mosque and walk along the Venetian walls.

Day 2: Take a guided tour of the Cyprus Museum and explore the Machairas Mountains.

Day 3: Discover the beautiful Kionia Mountain and enjoy a picnic near the river.

Day 4: Join a wine tasting tour and taste some of the best wines produced in Cyprus.

Day 5: Visit the Stavrovouni Monastery and hike to the top of Mount Olympus.

Day 6: Explore the charming village of Lefkara and shop for some local handicrafts.

Day 7: Relax at one of the many beaches along the southern coast of Cyprus.

Food and Drink Scene of Nicosia district (South)

Must-Try Dishes

Cyprus has a vibrant food culture with a mix of Greek and Middle Eastern influences. Here are some of the must-try dishes in Nicosia district (South):

  • Halloumi: This cheese is a staple of Cypriot cuisine. It is made from a mix of goat’s and sheep’s milk and is grilled or fried.

  • Souvlaki: This is a popular street food made with marinated meat, usually lamb, chicken, or pork, grilled over a charcoal fire.

  • Kleftiko: This is a traditional Cypriot dish made with slow-cooked lamb, potatoes, and vegetables.

Local Beverages

Cyprus produces some excellent wines. Here are some of the local beverages to try:

  • Commandaria: This is a sweet dessert wine and is considered the oldest wine in the world.

  • Zivania: This is a traditional Cypriot spirit made from distilling grape pomace.

  • Coffee: Cyprus has a vibrant coffee culture, and you can taste some traditional coffee varieties in Nicosia district (South).

Where to Stay in Nicosia district (South): Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Nicosia district (South) has a wide range of accommodation options, from backpacker hostels to luxury hotels. Here are some of the best places to stay:

  • The Classic Hotel: This boutique hotel is located in the heart of old Nicosia city and offers excellent views of the area.

  • Asty Hotel: This is a modern, comfortable hotel in the heart of Nicosia city, offering great amenities and services.

  • Agroktima Agios Georgios: This is a traditional guesthouse located in a beautiful rural setting and offers a tranquil getaway.

What to Buy in Nicosia district (South): Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Nicosia district (South) is famous for its traditional handicrafts and local products. Here are some of the top items to bring home:

  • Lefkara Lace: This traditional handicraft is made in the village of Lefkara and has been used for centuries to decorate clothes and household items.

  • Hand-Painted Ceramics: Cyprus has a rich tradition of ceramics, and you can find beautiful hand-painted ceramics in Nicosia district (South).

  • Olive Oil: Cyprus produces some of the best extra-virgin olive oil in the world. You can buy some of the high-quality olive oil to take home with you.

Local Culture and Customs of Nicosia district (South)

Cyprus has a rich cultural heritage, influenced by Greek, Turkish, and Middle Eastern cultures. Here are some of the local customs to keep in mind:

  • Dress code: Dress modestly when visiting places of worship or other public places.

  • Tipping: Tipping is not mandatory, but it’s customary to leave a small tip for good service.

  • Socializing: Cypriots are friendly and enjoy socializing. It’s common to greet people with a handshake and exchange pleasantries.

Best Time to Visit Nicosia district (South)

The best time to visit Nicosia district (South) is from March to May and from September to November. During these months, the temperature is mild, and there are fewer tourists.

Getting to Nicosia district (South): Transportation Options and Logistics

Travellers can reach Nicosia district (South) by air, sea, and land. Here are the transportation options to consider:
