Introduction to Nicosia

Nicosia, the capital of Cyprus, is a city with a rich cultural and historical heritage. It is divided into two parts: the north, which is the capital of the self-declared Turkish Republic of Northern Cyprus, and the south, which is the capital and largest city of the Republic of Cyprus. The city has many attractions, ranging from museums to outdoor adventures, and offers visitors an authentic taste of Cyprus.

List of Places to Visit in Nicosia

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Nicosia is home to a variety of museums, including the Cyprus Museum, the Byzantine Museum, and the Leventis Municipal Museum of Nicosia. The Cyprus Museum is the oldest and largest archaeological museum in Cyprus and displays artifacts from the Neolithic to the Byzantine periods. The Byzantine Museum has a collection of icons, frescoes, and other artifacts from the Byzantine period, while the Leventis Municipal Museum of Nicosia showcases the history of the city.

Nicosia is also known for its historic sites, including the ancient city walls, the Venetian walls and moat, the Selimiye Mosque, and the Omeriye Mosque. The ancient city walls date back to the 16th century and are some of the most well-preserved in the world. The Venetian walls and moat were built in the 16th century to protect the city from Ottoman invasion. The Selimiye Mosque, formerly known as the Cathedral of Saint Sophia, was built in the 13th century and is now a UNESCO World Heritage site. The Omeriye Mosque was built in the 17th century and is one of the most important Ottoman-era buildings in Cyprus.

Natural Attractions of Nicosia: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Nicosia is not just about museums and historic sites. The city has many natural attractions, such as the Troodos Mountains, the Machairas Forest, and the Pedieos River. The Troodos Mountains are located in the center of Cyprus and offer scenic views and hiking trails. The Machairas Forest is home to the Machairas Monastery, which dates back to the 12th century and is an important religious site in Cyprus. The Pedieos River is the longest river in Cyprus and offers opportunities for hiking and cycling.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Nicosia

Nicosia has many hiking and biking routes for visitors to explore. One of the most popular routes is the Pedieos Trail, which follows the Pedieos River and offers scenic views of the surrounding countryside. Another popular route is the Machairas Trail, which leads visitors through the Machairas Forest to the Machairas Monastery.

Best Things to Do in Nicosia: Activities and Tours

Nicosia has a variety of activities and tours for visitors, such as wine tasting tours, cooking classes, and day trips to nearby attractions. One popular activity is the Nicosia Food Tour, which takes visitors to local markets and restaurants to taste local Cypriot cuisine. Another popular activity is the Cyprus Wine Tour, which takes visitors to wineries in the Troodos Mountains to taste local wines.

Week-Long Itinerary for Nicosia

Day 1: Visit the Cyprus Museum and the Venetian walls and moat. Day 2: Explore the Selimiye Mosque and the Omeriye Mosque. Day 3: Hike the Pedieos Trail or the Machairas Trail. Day 4: Visit the Leventis Municipal Museum of Nicosia and the Ledra Street Crossing Point. Day 5: Take a day trip to nearby attractions, such as the Kyrenia Castle or the Aphrodite’s Rock. Day 6: Attend a cooking class and learn to make local Cypriot cuisine. Day 7: Relax and enjoy the local food and drink scene.

Food and Drink Scene of Nicosia

Must-Try Dishes

Nicosia is famous for its food scene, which is influenced by the island’s Greek, Turkish, and Middle Eastern cultures. Some must-try dishes include:

  • Souvlakia: Grilled meat skewers served with pitta bread and salad.
  • Moussaka: Layers of minced meat, eggplant, and potatoes, topped with bechamel sauce.
  • Kleftiko: Slow-cooked lamb with potatoes and vegetables.
  • Halloumi: Traditional Cypriot cheese, usually grilled or fried.

Local Beverages

Cyprus produces a variety of local beverages, such as:

  • Commandaria: A sweet dessert wine made from the Xynisteri and Mavro grape varieties.
  • Zivania: A clear, strong spirit made from grape pomace and flavored with aniseed.
  • Cypriot coffee: Similar to Greek or Turkish coffee, served with a small glass of water.

Where to Stay in Nicosia: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Nicosia has a variety of accommodation options for visitors, ranging from luxury hotels to budget hostels and guesthouses. Some popular options include the Hilton Cyprus, the Centrum Hotel, and the Asty Hotel.

What to Buy in Nicosia: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Nicosia is a great place to buy souvenirs and local products to bring back home. Some popular items include:

  • Lefkara lace: Handmade lacework originating from the village of Lefkara.
  • Handicrafts: Items made from locally sourced materials, such as ceramics, woodwork, and embroidery.
  • Halloumi: Traditional Cypriot cheese, usually vacuum-sealed for travel.

Local Culture and Customs of Nicosia

Nicosia has a rich cultural heritage, shaped by its Greek, Turkish, and Middle Eastern influences. Visitors should be respectful of local customs and traditions, such as dressing modestly when visiting religious sites and not walking on prayer rugs in mosques.

Best Time to Visit Nicosia

The best time to visit Nicosia is during the shoulder seasons of spring (March to May) and autumn (September to November), when the weather is mild and the crowds are smaller. The summer months (June to August) can be hot and crowded, while the winter months (December to February) can be rainy.

Do’s and Don’ts of Nicosia

Do:

  • Dress modestly when visiting religious sites.
  • Try the local cuisine and drinks.
  • Be respectful of local customs and traditions.

Don’t:

  • Photograph military installations or checkpoints.
  • Smoke in public places.
  • Be disrespectful to local customs and traditions.

Safety and Health

Safety in Nicosia

Nicosia is a generally safe city, but visitors should still take precautions to stay safe. Some tips include:

  • Avoid isolated areas, especially at night.
  • Be aware of pickpockets in crowded areas.
  • Follow local customs and traditions to avoid offending locals.

Health in Nicosia

Nicosia has good health facilities and medical care is generally accessible. Visitors should make sure they have adequate travel insurance before visiting and should take precautions to avoid heat stroke and dehydration during the hot summer months.

Getting to Nicosia: Transportation Options and Logistics

Nicosia is easily accessible by air and land. Some transportation options include:

Airports Near Nicosia

  • Larnaca International Airport: Located 50 km from Nicosia, provides international and domestic flights.
  • Paphos International Airport: Located 150 km from Nicosia, provides international and domestic flights.

Bus Stations Near Nicosia

  • Ledra Palace Border Crossing: Provides bus service to and from Northern Cyprus.
  • Nicosia Bus Terminal: Provides bus service to other cities in Cyprus, such as Larnaca and Limassol.

Other Options

  • Car rental: Available at both airports and in the city center.
  • Taxi: Available throughout the city center.

How to Get Around in Nicosia

Nicosia is a walkable city, but visitors can also use public transportation or rent a car to explore the surrounding areas. The city has a public bus system operated by OSEL, which provides service to most areas of the city.

Where to Go Next from Nicosia: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Nicosia is a great base for exploring the surrounding areas of Cyprus, such as:

  • Kyrenia: A seaside town in Northern Cyprus with a beautiful harbor and historic castle.
  • Limassol: A coastal city in southern Cyprus known for its beaches and nightlife.
  • Paphos: A historic city in southwestern Cyprus known for its archaeological sites and sandy beaches.

Conclusion

Nicosia is a fascinating city with a rich cultural and historical heritage. From its museums and historic sites to its natural attractions and local cuisine, there is something for everyone in Nicosia. With this comprehensive travel guide, visitors can explore all that the city has to offer and make the most of their trip to Cyprus.
