Introduction to Arouca

Nestled in Douro Litoral, in Northern Portugal, Arouca is a quaint town with picturesque landscapes and rich cultural heritage. It is known for its vast valleys, stunning mountains, and historical monuments that tell stories of the past. This guide is designed to help you discover the best of Arouca, from its cultural attractions to natural wonders and culinary delights.

List of Places to Visit in Arouca

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  1. Arouca Monastery: Arouca Monastery, also known as Monastery of St. Mary of Arouca, is a 10th-century primeval Benedictine abbey that boasts of a fascinating history and beautiful medieval architecture. The highlights of the monastery include the beautiful main altar, crypt, cloister, and chapter house.

  2. Mother Church of Arouca: Known as Igreja Matriz de Arouca, this church was built in the 16th century and is adorned with baroque and neoclassical elements. Its facade with decorated tiles is an attraction in itself but do not miss the main altar.

Natural Attractions of Arouca: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  1. Paiva Walkways: The Paiva Walkways is a magical wooden boardwalk that runs along the Paiva River, a stunning set of rock formations and natural waterfalls. The trail is 8km with several spots to relax and take in the beautiful views.

  2. Serra da Freita: The Serra da Freita mountain range is a natural wonder that offers an escape from the hustle and bustle of the city. From several vantage points atop the mountain, you can get a panoramic view of the valley below.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Arouca

  1. Freita Route: A moderate level route that starts in the charming village of Drave, the Freita route highlights picturesque natural landmarks such as waterfalls, lakes, and granite rocks.

  2. Arouca Geopark Route: This loop trail showcases the geology of Arouca Geopark. The trail is about 15km, going high up into the hills of Serra da Freita.

Best Things to Do in Arouca: Activities and Tours

  1. Visit Frecha da Mizarela Waterfall: A jaw-dropping waterfall that tumbles almost 75 meters. You can walk a path on the side of the cliff up to the top of the falls.

  2. Kayak the Paiva River: Take a kayak and paddle through the rapids and twists of the Paiva River.

Food and Drink Scene of Arouca

Must-Try Dishes

  1. Arouquesa Steak: Meat lovers should not miss the succulent meat from the Arouquesa cow, a native breed to the region.

  2. Veal Feijoada: Feijoada is a dish made out of beans and meat. It’s particularly delicious with veal and is a staple of the Arouquense cuisine.

Local Beverages

  1. Regional Wines: Douro Litoral region is known for its wines. One of the most famous is Vinho Verde, which is produced in the Vinho Verde Wine Region.

  2. Grilled Corn Juice (Caramelo): Arouca is known for its traditional sweet drink made from boiled corn. It is often served with sweet pastries or dessert.

Where to Stay in Arouca: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  1. Casa do Abel: A guesthouse located in the heart of Arouca, Casa do Abel is perfect for travelers looking for comfortable, affordable accommodation. The property includes cozy rooms and a beautiful garden.

  2. Quinta de Anterronde: A luxurious farmhouse with fantastic views of the countryside. It’s a perfect place for complete relaxation and enjoying the countryside.

What to Buy in Arouca: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  1. Honey: Arouca’s honey is one of the best in the world. It’s great to enjoy it on your own or to gift it to friends and family.

  2. Flavors of the Earth: This store showcases products made in Arouca, including wines, cheese, olive oil, and branded products like soaps and souvenirs.

Local Culture and Customs of Arouca

Arouca has a rich culture and customs that are an interesting blend of rural and urban life.

Festivals

  1. Festa das Papas de Sarrabulho: Arouca’s most famous festival where the locals prepare the famous Sarrabulho dish, a spicy pork stew eaten with flour.

  2. Feira das Colheitas: A traditional autumn market that celebrates the region’s harvest.

Best Time to Visit Arouca

The best time to visit Arouca is in the summer months from June to August when you can enjoy the warm weather, beautiful scenery, and lively festivals.

Do’s and Don’ts of Arouca

Do:

  1. Try the delicious local cuisine, particularly the Arouquesa steak and Sarrabulho dish.

  2. Respect local customs and dress modestly when visiting religious sites.

Don’t:

  1. Litter or leave trash behind when hiking or biking.

  2. Be loud or disturb the tranquility of the villages and natural parks.

Safety and Health

Safety in Arouca

Arouca is a safe destination, but visitors should take necessary precautions as with any other place in the world. Make sure to lock your doors and windows when leaving your accommodation, and do not leave valuables unattended in public places.

Health in Arouca

Visitors to Arouca should ensure their routine vaccines are up to date. The town has a local medical center, and pharmacies are easily accessible.

Getting to Arouca: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Arouca

  1. Porto Airport (OPO) - 56km/35 miles from Arouca

  2. Vigo Airport (VGO) - 151km/94 miles from Arouca

Bus Stations Near Arouca

  1. Arouca Bus Station - located in the town center, with buses to and from surrounding cities and towns.

  2. Porto Bus Station - Buses connect the city with Arouca.

Other Options

Renting a car is recommended for those who want to explore the region independently while trains connect the cities from Lisbon and Porto rail stations.

How to Get Around in Arouca

Exploring Arouca’s surroundings on foot, by bike or car are the best options. Several hiking and biking routes are available around Arouca for visitors to enjoy.

Where to Go Next from Arouca: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Porto

Porto, Portugal’s second-largest city and UNESCO World Heritage Site, is only an hour’s drive from Arouca. The city is renowned for its scenic riverfront, historic architecture, and vibrant nightlife.

Viseu

Viseu, located about an hour and a half drive east of Arouca, is a charming city that boasts of museums, art galleries, and beautiful parks, including the majestic Mata do Fontelo.

Conclusion

Arouca is a hidden gem in Northern Portugal, boasting stunning natural landscapes, captivating history, delicious cuisine, and welcoming locals. This comprehensive guide has something fresh for everyone and is sure to make your trip to Arouca memorable.
