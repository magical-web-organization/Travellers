Introduction to Amarante

Amarante is a charming little town located in the Northern region of Portugal, nestled in the Douro Litoral. The town is perched on the banks of the Tamega River and boasts beautiful countryside views with rolling hills and scenic valleys. Amarante is best known for its historic center with narrow cobbled streets and Renaissance and Baroque architecture, where several attractions can be found.

List of Places to Visit in Amarante

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Sao Goncalo Church: The highlight of Amarante is the Sao Goncalo Church, dedicated to the town’s patron saint, Saint Goncalo. The church features a famous Portuguese altar piece that depicts lemons, oranges, and limes, which is a rare sight.

  • Monastery of Saint Goncalo: Located on a hilltop near the Sao Goncalo Church, it offers panoramic views of the city. The monastery has a beautiful chapel with an impressive gilded altar and several paintings.

  • Municipal Museum Amadeo Souza-Cardoso: The museum houses a collection of works by the famous Portuguese artist Amadeu de Souza-Cardoso, along with exhibits showcasing contemporary art and traditional crafts.

  • Casa da Cerca: This is an art center and museum within a beautiful park that features contemporary art and exhibitions.

Natural Attractions of Amarante: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Fregim Dam: The dam is a popular spot for swimming and water sports, with stunning views of the surrounding hills.

  • Amarante Golf Course: The 18-hole course is set in the beautiful hills, and it offers an enjoyable experience for beginners and experienced golfers.

  • Serra do Marao: The mountain range boasts stunning scenery and a great network of hiking trails that take you through forests, valleys, and rivers.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Amarante

  • PR1 - Trilho dos Moinhos: This 9km circular route passes through stunning landscape, past traditional mills and the town’s historic center.

  • PR8 - Rota das Capelas: This is a 9km hike through beautiful countryside and takes you to the hilltop chapels with panoramic views of the town.

Best Things to Do in Amarante: Activities and Tours

  • Food and Wine Tours: Experience the town’s culinary delights by taking a guided tour and tasting the local cuisine.

  • River Cruise: Take a boat trip down the Tamega River, passing through the beautiful countryside and admiring the landscapes.

  • Horseback Riding: Enjoy horseback riding through the picturesque landscape that surrounds Amarante.

Week-Long Itinerary for Amarante

Day Activity
Day 1 Explore the town’s historic center and visit Sao Goncalo Church, Monastery of Saint Goncalo, and Municipal Museum Amadeo Souza-Cardoso.
Day 2 Take the PR1 hiking trail to Fregim Dam and enjoy water sports and swimming.
Day 3 Relax and enjoy the scenic views of Serra do Marao Mountain, including the hiking trails and local wildlife.
Day 4 Explore the town’s wine and food scene, including vineyards and wineries.

Day 6 Visit Casa da Cerca and continue exploring the town’s history and culture.

Food and Drink Scene of Amarante

Must-Try Dishes

  • Cozido: A local delicacy that consists of a soup bowl filled with meats, beans, and vegetables.

  • Rojoes: A stew-like dish made with marinated pork and potatoes.

  • Bola de Lamego: A bread filled with smoked ham and sausages.

  • Caldo Verde: A soup made with kale, potatoes, and chorizo.

Local Beverages

  • Vinho Verde: Light and crisp white wine that pairs well with seafood and fish dishes.

  • Port Wine: A fortified wine that is sweet and usually served with dessert.

  • Cerveja: Portuguese beer with various brands available in local restaurants and bars.

Where to Stay in Amarante: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Casa da Calcada Relais & Chateaux: This elegant 16th-century estate has a restaurant, indoor pool, and a terrace with beautiful views.

  • Monastery Hostel: This historic property was once a monastery and is now a hostel with dorms and private rooms.

  • Residencial - A Tasca: A guesthouse located in the heart of Amarante, featuring comfortable rooms with great views.

What to Buy in Amarante: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Local wines and cheeses: Visit the local wineries and cheese shops to try and purchase the flavors Amarante is known for.

  • Handmade crafts and pottery: Shop at Casa da Cerca for locally made crafts and pottery.

Local Culture and Customs of Amarante

Amarante has retained its traditional customs, including the annual Sao Goncalo Festival, held every year from June 7th to 10th. The festival features parades, processions, and musical performances honoring the town’s patron saint.

Best Time to Visit Amarante

The best time of the year to visit Amarante is during the spring and summer months, from March to September, when the warm weather and scenic countryside make for an enjoyable and pleasant stay. However, the winter months (December-February) offer a quieter season and more affordable accommodations.

Do’s and Don’ts of Amarante

Do:

  • Respect the local customs and traditions.

  • Try the local cuisine and wines.

  • Bring comfortable hiking shoes and clothing.

Don’t:

  • Litter or damage the natural surroundings.

  • Disrespect the town’s historic and cultural sites.

  • Wear revealing clothing while visiting religious sites.

Safety and Health

Safety in Amarante

Amarante is a safe place for tourists to travel, with low crime rates and helpful locals. However, take the usual precautions such as keeping valuables secure and being aware of your surroundings.

Health in Amarante

Visitors do not require any vaccines to visit Amarante, but it is always a good idea to stay up-to-date on routine vaccinations. If you need medical assistance while visiting Amarante, visit the local health center or hospital.

Getting to Amarante: Transportation Options and logistics

Airports Near Amarante

Amarante can be reached from Porto Airport, which is approximately 65 kilometers away. You can take a bus or rent a car to make your way to Amarante.

Bus Stations Near Amarante

Amarante has a bus station that connects to various nearby towns and cities. Regular services are available from the cities of Porto, Vila Real, Guimaraes and Lamego.

Other Options

You can rent a car to explore the region around Amarante. Local taxis can be found in the town center.

How to Get Around in Amarante

You can easily explore the historic center of Amarante on foot, but for longer distances, consider renting a car or taking a taxi. Buses run to the surrounding towns and cities.

Where to Go Next from Amarante: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Porto: Take a day trip to Porto and explore the city’s famous wine cellars and historic center.

  • Douro Valley: Consider visiting the Douro Valley, where you can enjoy wine tasting, scenic views, and river cruises.

  • Guimaraes: Known as the cradle of Portuguese nationality, Guimaraes offers historic sites and a vibrant culture worth visiting.

Conclusion

Amarante is a delightful town that offers a glimpse into Portugal’s rich history, culture, and natural surroundings. From its historic center to its scenic countryside, Amarante offers a range of activities and attractions suitable for all types of travelers. Enjoy the local cuisine and wines, explore the hiking trails, and take in the beautiful views all around.
