Introduction to Santa Maria da Feira

Santa Maria da Feira is a charming town in the Douro Litoral region of Northern Portugal. With a rich history that dates back to the Roman era, this town is known for its cultural attractions, outdoor adventures, and delicious gastronomy.

Whether you’re an art lover, history buff, foodie, or nature enthusiast, Santa Maria da Feira has plenty of activities and sights for everyone.

In this comprehensive travel guide, we’ll help you plan your trip to Santa Maria da Feira with information on places to visit, best things to do, where to stay, local culture, and more.

List of Places to Visit in Santa Maria da Feira

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Castelo de Santa Maria da Feira: This 11th-century castle is one of the best-preserved medieval castles in Portugal. Visitors can explore the castle walls, towers, moats, and even watch medieval reenactments.
  • Museu Convento dos Lóios: Housed in a former 16th-century convent, this museum offers a glimpse into the history and culture of Santa Maria da Feira. Highlights include religious art, pottery, and local handicrafts.
  • Castro de Romariz: This Iron Age settlement is located just outside of Santa Maria da Feira and offers visitors a chance to see the ruins of an ancient community that existed over 2,000 years ago.

Natural Attractions of Santa Maria da Feira: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Parque das Ribeiras de Rio Uíma: This park is perfect for nature lovers with its scenic hiking trails and lush riverbanks. Visitors can also enjoy kayaking and fishing in the Uíma River.
  • Serra da Freita: Located just outside of Santa Maria da Feira, Serra da Freita is a mountain chain that offers stunning views of the surrounding landscape. Popular activities here include hiking, mountain biking, and rock climbing.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Santa Maria da Feira

  • PR1 - Rota dos Moinhos: This 11-km hiking route takes visitors to several traditional watermills in the surrounding countryside. Along the way, hikers can enjoy scenic views of the river valley.
  • PR2 - Rota dos Vinhos Verdes: This 9-km hiking route takes visitors through the vineyards and wine cellars of Aveiro, where they can sample some of the region’s famous Vinho Verde wine.
  • Ecovia do Rio Uíma: This 4-km cycling route follows the Uíma River and offers a scenic ride through the surrounding countryside.

Best Things to Do in Santa Maria da Feira: Activities and Tours

  • Medieval Fair: Held every summer, the Medieval Fair is one of the town’s most popular events. Visitors can experience medieval life with reenactments, puppet shows, and market stalls selling traditional crafts and food.
  • Wine Tasting Tour: Santa Maria da Feira is located in the heart of Vinho Verde wine country, and visitors can take a guided tour of the local vineyards and cellars to learn about the production process and sample the region’s wines.
  • Horseback Riding: Visitors can explore the countryside around Santa Maria da Feira on horseback with guided tours that take them through scenic forests, rivers, and farmland.

Food and Drink Scene of Santa Maria da Feira

Must-Try Dishes

  • Arroz de Sarrabulho: A traditional meat dish made with rice, pig’s blood, and a variety of spices.
  • Francesinha: A sandwich filled with ham, sausage, steak, and covered in a layer of melted cheese and tomato sauce.
  • Leitão à Bairrada: Roasted suckling pig with crispy skin and tender meat.

Local Beverages

  • Vinho Verde: A light, crisp white wine that’s perfect for sipping on a hot day.
  • Licor Beirão: A popular digestive liqueur made from herbs and spices.

Where to Stay in Santa Maria da Feira: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Hotel dos Loios: This hotel is located in a former convent and offers guests a unique experience in the heart of Santa Maria da Feira.
  • Casa da Quinta da Gruta: This guesthouse is a great option for those who prefer a more secluded and peaceful location. Surrounded by lush gardens and countryside, it’s a great place to relax and unwind.
  • The Feira Hostel & Suites: This modern hostel is located in the heart of Santa Maria da Feira and offers guests affordable and comfortable accommodations.

What to Buy in Santa Maria da Feira: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Ceramics and Pottery: Santa Maria da Feira is known for its traditional ceramics and pottery, and visitors can find a variety of products at local markets and shops.
  • Renda de Bilros: This traditional lacework is made using a bobbin lace technique and can be found at local handicraft shops.
  • Vinho Verde: Take a bottle (or several) of the region’s famous Vinho Verde wine home with you, and savor the memories of your trip to Santa Maria da Feira.

Local Culture and Customs of Santa Maria da Feira

  • Festivals and Traditions: Santa Maria da Feira is known for its many festivals and traditions, including the Medieval Fair, the Carnival of Ovar, and the Feira do Livro (Book Fair).
  • Religion: The predominant religion in Santa Maria da Feira is Roman Catholicism, and visitors can see evidence of this in the many churches and chapels throughout the town.
  • Language: The official language of Portugal is Portuguese, with many locals in Santa Maria da Feira also speaking English and Spanish.

Best Time to Visit Santa Maria da Feira

The best time to visit Santa Maria da Feira is between May and October when the weather is warm, and the town is alive with festivals and events. However, if you’re looking for a quieter experience, visiting during the winter months can also be pleasant.

Do’s and Don’ts of Santa Maria da Feira

Do:

  • Try the local cuisine and wine
  • Attend one of the town’s many festivals
  • Respect local customs and traditions

Don’t:

  • Pick flowers or plants from parks or nature reserves
  • Smoke in public areas where it is prohibited
  • Leave trash behind in natural areas or on hiking trails

Safety and Health

Safety in Santa Maria da Feira

Santa Maria da Feira is a safe and welcoming town, with low crime rates and friendly locals. However, as with any place you visit, it’s important to take basic safety precautions like watching your belongings and being aware of your surroundings.

Health in Santa Maria da Feira

Portugal has a high-quality healthcare system, and visitors can access medical care at hospitals, clinics, and pharmacies throughout the country. It’s also recommended to have travel insurance that covers medical expenses in case of emergencies.

Getting to Santa Maria da Feira: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Santa Maria da Feira

The closest airport to Santa Maria da Feira is Porto Airport, which is located approximately 35 km away. From the airport, visitors can take a taxi, bus, or train to Santa Maria da Feira.

Bus Stations Near Santa Maria da Feira

Santa Maria da Feira has a centrally located bus station with connections to cities throughout Portugal. Visitors can also take a bus from nearby cities like Oporto or Lisbon.

Other Options

Visitors can also rent a car or take a taxi from nearby cities to Santa Maria da Feira.

How to Get Around in Santa Maria da Feira

Santa Maria da Feira is a small town, and most attractions and amenities are within walking distance. However, visitors can also use local buses or taxis to get around town.

Where to Go Next from Santa Maria da Feira: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Porto: Portugal’s second-largest city is located just 30 km from Santa Maria da Feira and is famous for its colorful architecture, port wine, and vibrant culture.
  • Aveiro: Known as the “Venice of Portugal,” Aveiro is a picturesque coastal city with colorful boats, canals, and delicious seafood.
  • Coimbra: A charming university town, Coimbra is famous for its historic university and stunning architecture.

Conclusion

Santa Maria da Feira is a hidden gem in Northern Portugal, offering visitors a unique blend of history, culture, and natural beauty. From hiking and biking to delicious food and wine, this town has something for everyone, and we hope this travel guide has helped you plan your perfect trip to Santa Maria da Feira.
