Introduction to Vila Nova de Foz Côa

Vila Nova de Foz Côa is a charming town in the Trás-os-Montes e Alto Douro region, located in Northern Portugal. Founded back in 1836, this picturesque town is filled with historic and cultural landmarks, stunning natural attractions, and delicious local cuisine.

List of Places to Visit in Vila Nova de Foz Côa

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

The Museu do Côa is the main cultural highlight of Vila Nova de Foz Côa, displaying prehistoric rock art that dates back over 20,000 years. Visitors can explore the exhibitions, learn about the history of the valley and see the rock art up close.

Another must-see is the Igreja Matriz de Vila Nova de Foz Côa, the town’s main church. Built in the 18th century, it features a beautiful baroque façade and a stunning interior filled with ornate decorations.

Natural Attractions of Vila Nova de Foz Côa: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

The Douro River is the backdrop of the scenic landscape of Vila Nova de Foz Côa. Take a boat tour along the river and explore the stunning scenery of cliffs, vineyards, hills and mountains.

The Vale do Côa Natural Park is a vast area of rugged terrain and spectacular scenery, offering a variety of outdoor activities such as hiking, cycling and horseback riding. It’s also home to an abundance of fauna and flora, including rare and endangered species.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Vila Nova de Foz Côa

The best way to explore the natural beauty of Vila Nova de Foz Côa is by hiking or cycling through its trails. The Rota do Côa is a popular hiking route that leads you across the valley, along with the river and nearby hills, covering around 20 kilometers.

Best Things to Do in Vila Nova de Foz Côa: Activities and Tours

For a unique experience, take a guided tour of the ancient rock art at Côa Valley, where you can witness the beauty of prehistoric human art that speaks volumes about the region’s rich cultural heritage. There are also many opportunities for bird-watching, canoeing, and fishing, and more adventurous activities such as rock-climbing and canyoning.

Week-Long Itinerary for Vila Nova de Foz Côa

Day 1: Arrive in Vila Nova de Foz Côa and visit Museu do Côa. Day 2: Take a boat tour on the Douro river in the morning, and hike the Rota do Côa in the afternoon. Day 3: Visit the Igreja Matriz de Vila Nova de Foz Côa in the morning, and have lunch at a local restaurant offering regional cuisine, followed by an afternoon picnic in the Vale do Côa National Park. Day 4: Take a guided tour of the ancient rock art at Côa Valley, with an optional visit to the nearby Quinta da Ervamoira for wine tasting. Day 5: Visit the beautifully preserved historic village of Mós and have lunch there followed by an afternoon trip to the almond blossom route. Day 6: Take a canyoning adventure in Penedono or visit the castle, and then enjoy a scenic drive to nearby Pinhão for vineyards and olive groves tours. Day 7: Visit the nearby town of Guarda for a day trip and shop for souvenirs.

Food and Drink Scene of Vila Nova de Foz Côa

Must-Try Dishes

The regional specialties of Vila Nova de Foz Côa include:

  • Cozido à portuguesa : a savory stew made with various meats and vegetables.
  • Arroz de Carqueja : a rice dish made with a wild herb found in the region, and often served with a variety of mushrooms.
  • Bola de Lamego : a bread filled with ham, sausage, and other local meats.
  • Bacalhau : salted cod prepared in various ways, a Portuguese classic.

Local Beverages

For wine lovers, Vila Nova de Foz Côa is a dream come true, offering a range of locally produced wines, including Port, Dao, and Douro wines. The region is famous for its fortified wines, with a selection of 40-year-old tawnies and vintage ports that are worth trying. Additionally, the town is known for its brandy and sweet liqueurs, such as Muscatel.

Where to Stay in Vila Nova de Foz Côa: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

There are several accommodation options in Vila Nova de Foz Côa, ranging from budget-friendly hostels up to luxurious hotels. For those seeking authentic experiences, Pousada Convento de Vila Nova de Foz Côa is a recommended spot to stay, as it is a historical building restored to serve as a hotel.

What to Buy in Vila Nova de Foz Côa: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

For a taste of the region’s cuisine, try picking up some olive oil from the valley, which is recognized for its high-quality production, almonds, jams made with regional products, and honey. Additionally, you can explore the handicrafts produced in the area, including pottery and hand-woven blankets.

Local Culture and Customs of Vila Nova de Foz Côa

Vila Nova de Foz Côa is a rural town, where locals are hospitable and welcoming. The region has a rich cultural heritage that is visible in its museums, festivals, and traditions.

Best Time to Visit Vila Nova de Foz Côa

The best time to visit Vila Nova de Foz Côa is between May and October, when the weather is mild and pleasant, and the outdoor activities are in full swing.

Do’s and Don’ts of Vila Nova de Foz Côa

Do:

  • Try the local cuisine: it’s both delicious and authentic.
  • Attend one of the town’s annual festivals, showcasing the best of its culture, traditions and historical heritage.
  • learn some Portuguese before arrival so that you can communicate better.

Don’t:

  • Be careless around the valley landscapes, stick to marked trails to avoid damaging prehistoric cultural sites and the ecosystem.
  • Leave valuables or your luggage unattended.

Safety and Health

Safety in Vila Nova de Foz Côa

Vila Nova de Foz Côa is a safe town, with very low crime rates. However, it’s always best to take precautions such as not leaving valuables in visible places, using a safe to store them, and following local guidelines.

Health in Vila Nova de Foz Côa

The healthcare system in Portugal is of high quality. There is a health center in the town, which offers primary healthcare services. Travelers are advised to have adequate travel insurance, including emergency medical expenses.

Getting to Vila Nova de Foz Côa: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Vila Nova de Foz Côa

The nearest international airport is Porto Airport, situated around 200 kilometers south of Vila Nova de Foz Côa. From there, you can hire a car or take a bus to reach the town.

Bus Stations Near Vila Nova de Foz Côa

The town has a bus station, serviced by regional buses that connect Vila Nova de Foz Côa to several neighboring towns and cities.

Other Options

Alternatively, you can hire a car and drive to the town, offering the flexibility to explore the surrounding areas.

How to Get Around in Vila Nova de Foz Côa

The best way to explore the town is on foot, as most of its attractions are conveniently located nearby. Additionally, hiring bikes is also a popular transportation option, allowing you to explore the scenic landscapes around the town.

Where to Go Next from Vila Nova de Foz Côa: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

There are several destinations in the nearby region worth exploring, including:

  • The city of Porto, known for its historical heritage, architecture, and Port wine.
  • Douro Valley, a renowned wine-producing region with picturesque landscapes.
  • The city of Braga, a historic city filled with religious landmarks.

Conclusion

Vila Nova de Foz Côa is a charming town with a rich history and culture that deserves a visit. With its stunning natural beauty, delicious cuisine, and outdoor activities plus the many cultural attractions, it’s no wonder why it is becoming a popular destination for travelers. Plan your trip now and experience the beauty of this hidden gem of Northern Portugal.
