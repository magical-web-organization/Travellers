Introduction to Guimarães

Located in Minho, in Northern Portugal, Guimarães is a charming city known for its rich cultural heritage and natural beauty. As one of the country’s oldest cities, Guimarães is recognised as the cradle of Portuguese culture and was named a UNESCO World Heritage site in 2001. Its mixture of Romanesque, Gothic, and Renaissance architecture makes it a perfect spot for history lovers. Besides, it boasts numerous delicious regional dishes, lively markets, and gorgeous landscapes to be explored. So, whether you are interested in culture, food, outdoor adventures, or simply want to relax amid breathtaking views, Guimarães is the perfect destination for you.

List of Places to Visit in Guimarães

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Guimarães is dotted with culturally important attractions, having a rich historical past. The famous monuments that attract tourists from far across include:

  • Guimarães Castle - Built in the tenth century, the castle is one of the most iconic landmarks of Guimarães and was home to the first Portuguese king.

  • Ducal Palace of Bragança - The palace is a significant symbol of the city’s past, with its Baroque architecture and impressive interiors.

  • Alberto Sampaio Museum - Among the must-visit museums in Guimarães, this unique place showcases the history and culture of the city and its surroundings.

  • São Miguel do Castelo Church - Another historical monument with a mix of Gothic and Romanesque architecture is São Miguel do Castelo, dating back to the 13th century.

Natural Attractions of Guimarães: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

If you’re an outdoors type, Guimarães does not disappoint. The city and its surroundings offer numerous hiking and biking trails that meander through the stunning hills and valleys of Minho. Besides, there are a vast number of natural sites to visit, including:

  • Penedo da Saudade Urban Park - Ideal for a relaxing afternoon stroll, this small park has a beautifully landscaped garden area, a playground, and a lake where you can enjoy a picnic.

  • Penha Mountain - A natural wonder that offers panoramic views of the region, Penha Mountain is a favourite spot for hikers and nature enthusiasts. The Penha Sanctuary is also nearby, a majestic religious complex with a baroque church, fountains, and gardens.

  • Tahiti Waterfall - An oasis of tranquillity, the Tahiti Waterfall is only a short distance from the city centre and is an excellent spot for swimming on hot summer days.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Guimarães

There are plenty of hiking and biking routes in and around Guimarães, catering to all levels of fitness and ability. Passionate travellers can’t go wrong with the following:

  • Circular Route of Penha - An 8km circular trek through the Penha Mountains, this route has stunning landscapes and natural view of the region while allowing you to explore historic landmarks as well.

  • Ecotrail of Guimarães - This 12km trail crosses through many of Guimarães’ parks and green spaces, from Penha Mountain to the valley of the Sol.

Best Things to Do in Guimarães: Activities and Tours

There are plenty of things to see and do in Guimarães, ranging from cultural experiences to outdoor adventures. Some of the top activities and tours include:

  • Explore the medieval historic centre - Wander through the Rua de Santa Maria and admire its charming shops, cafes, and bars. You can also visit Largo da Oliveira to see the statue of Dom Afonso Henriques.

  • Take a food tour - Guimarães boasts some of the most delicious regional dishes. From traditional codfish dishes such as Bacalhau à Brás to the hearty cozido, a stew of meat and vegetables, and more.

  • Visit the handicrafts fair - Guimarães is famous for its handicrafts, and visitors should take the opportunity to explore the local markets and shops.

Food and Drink Scene of Guimarães

Must-Try Dishes

Guimarães has a rich gastronomical culture, and its dishes are an essential part of its offer. Here are some of the must-try dishes:

  • Bacalhau à Brás - A staple in Portuguese cuisine, this dish consists of thinly sliced cod cooked with onions, garlic, and eggs.

  • Cozido - A hearty Portuguese caldero, Cozido is slow-cooked beef, pork, and chicken, along with beans, potatoes, carrots, and cabbage.

  • Bifanas - These Portuguese pork sandwiches are served in small cafes all around the city and are a perfect lunchtime snack.

Local Beverages

While in Guimarães, don’t forget to indulge in the local beverages. Below are some recommendations:

  • Vinho Verde - Vinho Verde is unique to Minho, and the region produces some of the finest Verde wines in the world from grapes with hints of green to them.

  • Alambique de Ouro - A traditional liquor made with the flavour of a range of herbs and spices, Alambique de Ouro is a must-try. It’s often served as an aperitif.

Where to Stay in Guimarães: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Guimarães offers plenty of accommodation options to suit every budget. Here are some recommendations:

  • Santa Luzia ArtHotel - A luxury hotel with an art gallery and views of the city and its surroundings.

  • Hostel Hybrid Guimarães - An excellent hostel for budget travellers, offering shared and private rooms with free Wi-Fi and breakfast included.

  • Casa Dos Pombais - A charming guesthouse that offers air-conditioned rooms, free Wi-Fi, and a delightful garden area.

What to Buy in Guimarães: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

When it comes to souvenir shopping, visitors to Guimarães are spoiled for choice. The best souvenirs and local products to bring home include:

  • Pottery - The region of Guimarães is famous for its pottery; you can buy handmade dishes, mugs, and other ceramics.

  • Embroidery and Lace - Take back home a piece of Portugal’s culture, bobbin lace and embroidery from Guimarães are considered some of the best in the country.

  • Regional Delicacies - Take home some preserved sardine pate, Guedeja (dry sausage) or dried codfish.

Local Culture and Customs of Guimarães

  • Guimarães locals are known for their friendliness and hospitality

  • Etiquette demands that visitors greet people, learn and use basic Portuguese phrases like “Bom Dia,” “Por Favor,” and “Obrigado”

Best Time to Visit Guimarães

The best time to visit Guimarães is from March to October when the weather is pleasant, and tourists can enjoy the city at leisure.

Safety and Health

Safety in Guimarães

Guimaraes is one of the safest places in Portugal and generally has a low crime rate. However, visitors should take normal precautions and stay vigilant in tourist areas.

Health in Guimarães

Guimaraes does not pose any significant health risks, but travellers should take general precautions issued by WHO.

Getting to Guimarães: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Guimarães

The closest airport to Guimaraes is Francisco Sá Carneiro Airport in Porto, about 47km away.

Bus Stations Near Guimarães

Guimarães has good domestic transport connections, with regular buses from Porto to Guimarães and other nearby cities.

Other Options

Travellers who prefer car rentals can find several options at the airport.

How to Get Around in Guimarães

The best option for getting around Guimarães is on foot. The historic centre is easily walkable, and many of the attractions are within walking distance of each other. Taxis and buses are also available for visitors to travel to areas outside the city centre.

Where to Go Next from Guimarães: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Nearby towns that are worth visiting from Guimarães include:

  • Porto: The second-largest city in Portugal also has a lot of history and culture.

  • Braga: A city of rich history, Braga has a world-class cathedral and hosts a national celebration – Semana Santa (Holy Week).

  • Douro Valley: Take a wine tour and discover the stunning Douro Valley and vineyards in the countryside.

Conclusion

Guimarães is a charming and historic city that offers endless possibilities for travellers. From its rich history and culture to its natural beauty, local cuisine, and lively markets, there is something for everyone. Besides, with its convenient location, visitors can explore other great Portuguese cities and areas nearby. Whatever your interests, you will be sure to find an unforgettable travel experience in Guimarães.
