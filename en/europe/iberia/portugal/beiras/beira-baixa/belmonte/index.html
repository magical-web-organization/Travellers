Belmonte is a charming town in Beira Baixa, located in the Beiras region of Portugal, on the Iberian Peninsula in Europe. This beautiful town boasts a rich and fascinating history, and it is a great destination for history buffs, culture lovers, and outdoor enthusiasts alike. With this comprehensive travel guide to Belmonte, you will discover the top things to see and do, where to stay, what to eat, and much more.

Introduction to Belmonte

Belmonte is a small and picturesque town that is rich in history and culture. The town is best known for its Jewish heritage, which dates back to the medieval period. The Jewish community in Belmonte managed to maintain its traditions and religious practices despite the Inquisition and the persecution that they faced for centuries. Today, Belmonte is the only town in Portugal where a Jewish community still exists, and visitors can learn more about this fascinating history by visiting the Jewish Museum and the Synagogue.

Apart from its Jewish heritage, Belmonte is also known for its rich architecture, delicious local cuisine, and stunning natural beauty. The town is surrounded by the Serra da Estrela Mountains, which offer breathtaking views and numerous outdoor activities. Whether you’re interested in history, culture, or adventure, there is plenty to see and do in Belmonte.

List of Places to Visit in Belmonte

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Jewish Museum
  • Synagogue
  • Belmonte Castle
  • Church of Santiago
  • Museum of Discovery

The Jewish Museum is one of the main attractions in Belmonte, as it offers a fascinating insight into the rich history of the Jewish community in the town. The museum exhibits various items and artifacts that showcase the daily life, customs, and traditions of the Jews in Belmonte. The Synagogue is also worth visiting, as it is the only synagogue in Portugal that is still active, and visitors can attend services there. Belmonte Castle is a medieval castle that has been well-preserved, and it offers spectacular views of the surrounding landscape. The Church of Santiago is another notable historical attraction, with beautiful Baroque features and important religious significance. The Museum of Discovery is a modern museum that celebrates Portuguese explorers and their achievements.

Natural Attractions of Belmonte: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Serra da Estrela Natural Park
  • Zêzere River
  • Belmonte Historic Village
  • Pessegueiro River Beach

Belmonte is surrounded by nature, making it an ideal destination for outdoor enthusiasts. The Serra da Estrela Mountains are a popular destination for hiking, skiing, and snowboarding in the winter. The Zêzere River is also a great place for water sports, such as canoeing, kayaking, and swimming. Belmonte Historic Village is a charming little village with narrow streets, traditional houses, and a rich history dating back to the Roman times. Pessegueiro River Beach is a stunning natural swimming pool in the Serra da Estrela Natural Park, surrounded by lush vegetation and crystal-clear waters.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Belmonte

Belmonte offers a variety of hiking and biking routes that are perfect for all skill levels. Some of the most popular routes include:

  • Rota dos Judeus (Jewish Route)
  • Rota dos Castelos (Castles Route)
  • Rota dos Viadutos e Túneis (Viaducts and Tunnels Route)
  • Serra da Estrela Hiking Trail

These routes offer stunning views of the natural beauty of Belmonte and its surroundings, as well as insights into the historical and cultural significance of the town.

Best Things to Do in Belmonte: Activities and Tours

  • Jewish Heritage Tour
  • Castle and Church Walking Tour
  • Serra da Estrela Jeep Tour
  • Local Food and Wine Tour

Belmonte offers a variety of guided tours that cater to different interests and preferences. The Jewish Heritage Tour takes visitors to the most important Jewish sites in Belmonte, while the Castle and Church Walking Tour offers a glimpse into the medieval history of the town. The Serra da Estrela Jeep Tour takes visitors to the heart of the mountain range, where they can enjoy stunning views and learn about the local flora and fauna. The Local Food and Wine Tour is perfect for foodies, as it showcases the best of Belmonte’s cuisine, accompanied by delicious local wines.

Week-Long Itinerary for Belmonte

Day 1: Arrive in Belmonte and check into your hotel. Take a leisurely walk around the town to get your bearings.

Day 2: Join a Jewish Heritage Tour to learn more about the town’s rich Jewish history, and visit the Jewish Museum and Synagogue.

Day 3: Take a Castle and Church Walking Tour to explore the medieval history of Belmonte, and visit Belmonte Castle and the Church of Santiago.

Day 4: Go on a Serra da Estrela Jeep Tour to discover the natural beauty of the mountain range and enjoy breathtaking views.

Day 5: Explore the Serra da Estrela Natural Park, and hike one of the many trails available.

Day 6: Join a Local Food and Wine Tour to sample Belmonte’s delicious cuisine and wines.

Day 7: Relax and enjoy the beauty of Belmonte at your leisure before departing.

Food and Drink Scene of Belmonte

Belmonte has a rich culinary tradition that is influenced by the Jewish and Portuguese cultures. Some of the must-try dishes of Belmonte include:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Maranhos: A traditional sausage made from bread, pork, and herbs, and cooked with cabbage.

  • Sopa da Pedra: A hearty soup made with beans, vegetables, and meat, and cooked with a hot stone to give it a unique flavor.

  • Migas: A dish made with breadcrumbs, olive oil, garlic, and pork, and served with fried eggs.

Local Beverages

  • Queijo da Serra: A local cheese made from sheep’s milk, with a strong and pungent flavor.

  • Beira Baixa Wine: A regional wine characterized by its ruby-red color and fruity flavor.

Where to Stay in Belmonte: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Belmonte offers a variety of accommodation options that cater to different budgets and preferences. Some of the most popular options include:

  • Pousada Convento de Belmonte: A luxurious hotel set in a 13th-century convent, with elegant rooms and stunning views.

  • Casa da Chandeirinha: A charming guesthouse with cozy rooms and a beautiful garden, located in the heart of Belmonte.

  • Hostel da Vila Belmonte: A budget-friendly hostel with comfortable dorms and private rooms, located next to the castle.

What to Buy in Belmonte: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Belmonte is known for its traditional handicrafts, such as:

  • Woven baskets
  • Embroidery work
  • Pottery
  • Wooden sculptures

Visitors can find these products in local shops and markets, as well as at the Jewish Museum and other historical attractions in the town.

Local Culture and Customs of Belmonte

Belmonte has a rich cultural heritage that is shaped by its Jewish and Portuguese influences. Visitors should respect local customs and traditions, especially when visiting religious sites. It is recommended to dress appropriately and behave respectfully at all times.

Best Time to Visit Belmonte

Belmonte can be visited all year round, but the best time to visit is from May to October when the weather is mild and sunny. The Serra da Estrela Mountains are a popular destination for winter sports enthusiasts, and the town is also beautiful during the winter months, especially during the Christmas season.

Do’s and Don’ts of Belmonte

Do:

  • Respect local customs and traditions.
  • Try the local cuisine and beverages.
  • Explore the stunning natural beauty of Belmonte.

Don’t:

  • Disrespect religious sites.
  • litter or damage the natural surroundings.
  • behave inappropriately in public places.

Safety and Health

Safety in Belmonte

Belmonte is a safe destination for visitors, but it is recommended to take the usual precautions to avoid petty crime, such as keeping valuables safe and avoiding dark streets at night.

Health in Belmonte

Belmonte does not require any specific vaccinations, and the standard of healthcare is generally good. Travellers are advised to take out comprehensive travel insurance before visiting.

Belmonte is a small town, and most attractions are within walking distance. Visitors can also rent a bike or take a taxi to explore the surrounding areas.

Where to Go Next from Belmonte: Nearby Destinations and Excursions
