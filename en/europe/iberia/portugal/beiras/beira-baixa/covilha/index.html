Introduction to Covilhã

Nestled in the Beira Baixa region of Beiras, Portugal, Covilhã is a charming town that has plenty to offer visitors. Situated at the foothills of the Serra da Estrela mountain range, Covilhã boasts a beautiful landscape, rich cultural heritage, and delicious food and drink scene. Whether you’re a history buff, an outdoor enthusiast, or a foodie, Covilhã has something to offer for everyone.

List of Places to Visit in Covilhã

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

1. Museu de Lanifícios

The Museu de Lanifícios, or the Wool Museum, is one of Covilhã’s most popular attractions. The museum is housed in a historic 18th-century wool factory and showcases the town’s rich textile heritage. Visitors can explore the museum’s collection of antique looms, machines, and textiles and learn about the town’s wool industry.

2. Torre do Relógio

Located in the heart of the town, Torre do Relógio is a clock tower that dates back to the 16th century. The tower is a great example of the town’s Gothic architecture and offers stunning views of the town and surrounding countryside.

3. Igreja de Santa Maria Maior

Igreja de Santa Maria Maior is an impressive church that combines Gothic and Baroque architecture. The church, which dates back to the 13th century, is known for its ornate altarpieces, intricate frescoes, and beautiful stained glass windows.

Natural Attractions of Covilhã: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

1. Serra da Estrela Natural Park

Serra da Estrela Natural Park is a rugged natural park that boasts a diverse range of landscapes, from snow-capped mountains to crystal-clear lakes. Visitors can go hiking, biking, or skiing in the park, depending on the season.

2. Vale Glaciar do Zêzere

Vale Glaciar do Zêzere is a glacier valley that is home to a stunning array of flora and fauna. Visitors can take a leisurely hike through the valley and admire the wildflowers, birds, and butterflies that call it home.

3. Penhas da Saúde

Penhas da Saúde is a mountain resort located in Serra da Estrela that is known for its breathtaking views and outdoor activities. Visitors can go skiing, snowboarding, or hiking in the resort and relax in one of the many cozy lodges and restaurants.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Covilhã

Covilhã is an ideal destination for hiking or biking enthusiasts. One of the most popular routes is the Rota das Aldeias Históricas, which takes visitors on a journey through the historic villages around Covilhã. Other popular trails include the Rota das Faias and the Rota do Chocalho.

Best Things to Do in Covilhã: Activities and Tours

1. Taste the Local Cuisine

Covilhã is known for its delectable cuisine, which includes a variety of meats, cheeses, and pastries. Visitors can take a food tour of the town and taste the local delicacies, such as Queijo da Serra and Castanhas.

2. Visit a Local Winery

The Beira Baixa region is known for its exceptional wine. Visitors can take a tour of one of the many wineries in Covilhã and sample some of the region’s best wines.

3. Attend a Local Festival

Covilhã hosts several festivals throughout the year, including the Wool Festival, which celebrates the town’s textile heritage, and the Cherry Festival, which celebrates the town’s cherry orchards.

Week-Long Itinerary for Covilhã

Day 1: Visit the Museu de Lanifícios and explore the historic wool factory.

Day 2: Take a hike through Vale Glaciar do Zêzere and admire the natural beauty of the glacier valley.

Day 3: Visit the Torre do Relógio and explore the town’s Gothic architecture.

Day 4: Go skiing or snowboarding in Penhas da Saúde and relax in one of the cozy lodges.

Day 5: Take a food tour of Covilhã and taste the local delicacies.

Day 6: Visit a local winery and sample some of the region’s best wines.

Day 7: Attend one of Covilhã’s festivals and experience the local culture.

Food and Drink Scene of Covilhã

Must-Try Dishes

1. Queijo da Serra

Queijo da Serra is a creamy cheese that is made from the milk of Serra da Estrela sheep. The cheese is typically served with bread and is a popular staple in Covilhã.

2. Castanhas

Castanhas, or chestnuts, are a beloved snack in Covilhã. Visitors can enjoy roasted chestnuts from street vendors or find them incorporated into various dishes, such as meat stews and pastries.

3. Bacalhau à lagareiro

Bacalhau à lagareiro is a delicious dish that combines salted cod with potatoes and garlic. The dish is typically baked in the oven and is a popular seafood dish in Covilhã.

Local Beverages

1. Vinho Verde

Vinho Verde is a refreshing white wine that is popular in the Beiras region. The wine has a slight sparkle to it, and pairs well with summer salads and seafood.

2. Ginja

Ginja is a cherry liqueur that is typical of the Beiras region. The liqueur is typically served in a shot glass and is enjoyed as an aperitif or digestif.

Where to Stay in Covilhã: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

1. Pousada da Serra da Estrela

The Pousada da Serra da Estrela is a luxurious hotel that is situated in the heart of Serra da Estrela Natural Park. The hotel boasts stunning views and a range of amenities, including an indoor pool and a spa.

2. Covilhã Boutique Hotel

The Covilhã Boutique Hotel is a stylish hotel that is located in the town center. The hotel features modern decor, comfortable rooms, and a rooftop terrace with views of the town.

3. Casas do Côro

Casas do Côro is a charming guesthouse that is situated in a historic village outside of Covilhã. The guesthouse features cozy rooms, a restaurant, and a pool.

What to Buy in Covilhã: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

1. Woven Textiles

Covilhã is known for its woven textiles, particularly blankets and carpets made from local wool. Visitors can purchase handmade textiles at local markets and shops throughout the town.

2. Cherry Liqueur

Visitors can bring home a bottle of Ginja, the local cherry liqueur, as a tasty souvenir from their trip to Covilhã.

Local Culture and Customs of Covilhã

Covilhã has a rich cultural heritage that is reflected in its architecture, cuisine, and festivals. Visitors should be respectful of local customs and traditions, such as dressing modestly when visiting churches and other religious sites.

Best Time to Visit Covilhã

The best time to visit Covilhã is during the spring and summer, when the weather is mild and the landscape is in full bloom. Visitors can also enjoy outdoor activities, such as hiking and biking, during this time of year.

Do’s and Don’ts of Covilhã

Do:

  • Do taste the local cuisine, which is known for its delicious meats, cheeses, and pastries.

  • Do visit the Museu de Lanifícios to learn about the town’s textile heritage.

  • Do explore the Serra da Estrela Natural Park and admire the stunning natural scenery.

Don’t:

  • Don’t forget to dress modestly when visiting churches and other religious sites.

  • Don’t disrespect local customs and traditions.

  • Don’t litter or damage the natural environment while enjoying outdoor activities.

Safety and Health

Safety in Covilhã

Covilhã is a relatively safe town, but visitors should still exercise common sense and take precautions to safeguard their belongings. Visitors should avoid walking alone late at night and be aware of their surroundings when in crowded areas.

Health in Covilhã

Visitors should make sure that they are up to date on their routine vaccinations and check with a healthcare provider regarding any additional vaccines they may need. Covilhã has several medical facilities and pharmacies for visitors who need medical attention.

Getting to Covilhã: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Covilhã

The closest airport to Covilhã is Francisco Sá Carneiro Airport in Porto, which is approximately 2.5 hours away by car. Visitors can also fly into Lisbon Portela Airport or Madrid-Barajas Airport and take a train or bus to Covilhã.

Bus Stations Near Covilhã

Covilhã has a bus station that is located in the town center. Visitors can take a bus to and from other cities in Portugal, such as Lisbon and Porto.

Other Options

Visitors can also take a train to Covilhã from Lisbon or Porto. The train station is located in the town center and is an easy walk from most hotels and guesthouses.

How to Get Around in Covilhã

Covilhã is a small town and is easy to explore on foot. Visitors can also take a taxi or rent a car to explore the surrounding areas.

Where to Go Next from Covilhã: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Visitors can take a day trip to nearby villages, such as Belmonte or Sortelha, to experience the local culture and historic architecture. Visitors can also explore other cities in Portugal, such as Lisbon or Porto, which are easily accessible by train or bus.

Conclusion

Covilhã is an authentic and charming destination that offers visitors a wide range of cultural, outdoor, and culinary experiences. With its rich history, natural beauty, and delicious cuisine, Covilhã is a must-visit destination for any traveler to Portugal.
