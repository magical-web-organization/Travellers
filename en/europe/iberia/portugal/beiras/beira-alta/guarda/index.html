Introduction to Guarda

Guarda is located in Beira Alta, Beiras, in Portugal and is the highest city in the country, with an altitude of 1056 meters above sea level. The city’s history is intertwined with the Celts and has since been ruled by the Visigoths and Moors. Guarda is known for its cultural heritage, natural beauty, and traditional cuisine.

List of Places to Visit in Guarda

There are several museums, historical places, natural attractions, and outdoor activities in Guarda.

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  1. The Cathedral of Guarda: A magnificent cathedral designed in the Gothic style, which perfectly represents the city’s rich cultural heritage.
  2. The Guarda City Museum: The museum displays the history of the city from the Pre-Roman era to the present day. It also exhibits the region’s handcrafts, music, and cuisine.
  3. The Museum of Jewish Presence: The museum exhibits the Jewish presence in the region and their contribution to the city’s development.

Natural Attractions of Guarda: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  1. Serra da Estrela Natural Park: Guarda is the gateway to the Serra da Estrela mountain range, the highest in continental Portugal. The park is home to several hiking trails, wildlife, and waterfalls.
  2. Guarda Castle: The castle is situated on a hill overlooking the city, offering a stunning view of Guarda and the surrounding mountains.
  3. Aldeias Históricas de Portugal: These are historic villages situated near Guarda. Belmonte, Sortelha and Vila Nova de Foz Côa are the most popular ones to visit.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Guarda

Guarda is a paradise for outdoor enthusiasts. There are several hiking and biking routes that one can take in and around the city. Here are some of the most popular ones:

  • The Rota dos Castelos: A 35 km route that connects seven castles in the region.
  • The Rota dos Incensos: A 12 km route that follows the old path of charcoal burners.
  • PR9 Guarda: An 11 km route that offers stunning views of Guarda and the surrounding mountains.

Best Things to Do in Guarda: Activities and Tours

  1. Join a guided tour of the city to learn about the local history and culture.
  2. Try traditional cuisine at Restaurante Dom Sancho.
  3. Visit the weekly market on Saturday morning in Largo da Sé in Guarda, where you’ll find fresh produce, art, and handicrafts.
  4. Attend a traditional festival to experience the local culture.

Week-Long Itinerary for Guarda

Day 1: Visit the Cathedral of Guarda and the Guarda City Museum. Day 2: Explore the Serra da Estrela Natural Park. Day 3: Take a guided tour of the city and visit the Jewish Museum. Day 4: Visit the Aldeias Históricas de Portugal. Day 5: Take a biking tour of the Rota dos Castelos. Day 6: Try traditional cuisine at Restaurante Dom Sancho. Day 7: Attend a local festival or event.

Food and Drink Scene of Guarda

Guarda’s cuisine is a reflection of its rich history and culture. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  1. Beira-style lamb chops
  2. Cozido à Portuguesa: a stew with roasted meats, vegetables and beans
  3. Queijo da Serra: a pungent, buttery cheese made from the milk of Serra da Estrela sheep

Local Beverages

  1. Aguardente: a type of brandy made in the Beiras region.
  2. Dão wine: a full-bodied, aromatic wine made in the nearby Dão region.
  3. Licor Beirão: a sweet, herb-infused liquor made in the Beiras region.

Where to Stay in Guarda: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  1. Hotel Vanguarda: A modern hotel located in the city centre.
  2. Solar De Alarcao: a traditional guesthouse in the Old Quarter of Guarda, offering an authentic experience.
  3. HI Guarda Youth Hostel: A budget-friendly option for travellers.

What to Buy in Guarda: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  1. Ceramics from MosaicOriente: A local craftsman who makes handmade ceramics using traditional techniques.
  2. Wool Products: The region is known for its high-quality wool, and therefore you can buy a range of warm clothing or blankets at the local shops.

Local Culture and Customs of Guarda

The people of Guarda have a deep connection with their traditions and culture, which is reflected in their music, festivals, and food. Visitors are encouraged to learn about and participate in these cultural events to get a better understanding of the region’s history and culture.

Best Time to Visit Guarda

The best time to visit Guarda is during the spring and summer months when the weather is warmer and there are several festivals taking place.

Do’s and Don’ts of Guarda

Do:

  1. Respect local customs and traditions.
  2. Try the traditional cuisine and beverages.
  3. Learn some basic Portuguese phrases.

Don’t:

  1. Disrespect the local culture.
  2. Leave trash in public places.
  3. Ignore safety protocols when hiking or engaging in outdoor activities.

Safety and Health

Safety in Guarda

Guarda is a generally safe city, but visitors should take basic safety precautions such as staying alert and aware of their surroundings.

Health in Guarda

Guarda has a well-developed healthcare system, but visitors are recommended to purchase travel insurance and bring any necessary medication with them.

Getting to Guarda: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Guarda

The nearest international airport is in Porto, approximately 180 km away.

Bus Stations Near Guarda

There are several bus routes connecting Guarda to other cities in Portugal.

Other Options

Visitors can also drive to Guarda or take the train from Porto or Lisbon.

How to Get Around in Guarda

The best way to get around Guarda is on foot. Taxis and buses are also available.

Where to Go Next from Guarda: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  1. Coimbra: A university town, with a rich history and culture.
  2. Aveiro: Known as the Venice of Portugal, Aviero is famous for its colorful boats and charming canals.
  3. Viseu: A charming, traditional town with excellent food and wine.

Conclusion

Guarda is a city with a rich culture and heritage, stunning natural beauty, and traditional cuisine. Whether you’re interested in outdoor adventures or cultural experiences, Guarda has something for everyone. Plan your trip well and prepare to be enchanted by the city and its people.
