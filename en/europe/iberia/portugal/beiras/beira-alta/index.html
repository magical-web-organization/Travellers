Introduction to Beira Alta

Beira Alta is a beautiful area nestled in the Beiras region of Portugal, known for its stunning landscapes and cultural attractions. From ancient castles and historic museums to natural wonders and outdoor adventures, Beira Alta is a perfect destination for anyone who wants to explore the beauty of Portugal. In this comprehensive tour guide, we will take you on a journey to discover the best places to visit, things to do, where to eat, stay and more in Beira Alta.

List of Places to Visit in Beira Alta

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Linhares Castle: The Linhares Castle is a majestic medieval fortress, located on a hilltop with stunning views of the surrounding countryside. The castle dates back to the 12th century and is a must-see attraction for any history lover.

  • Bussaco National Forest: A former royal hunting ground, Bussaco National Forest is a spectacular area with great historical and botanical importance. Here, you can explore the ancient convent of Santa Cruz - Bussaco Palace Hotel, and the unique Manueline-style Royal Chapel.

  • Viseu Cathedral: Viseu Cathedral is a stunning architectural masterpiece, built in the 16th century. The cathedral is also home to some of the most remarkable works of art in the region.

Natural Attractions of Beira Alta: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Serra da Estrela Natural Park: Serra da Estrela is a stunning mountain range and natural park, home to the highest peak in continental Portugal. Sporting some of the best views in the region and a paradise for outdoor sports such as hiking, skiing, and snowboarding.

  • Douro Valley: Known for its sprawling vineyards, Douro Valley is a beautiful region that produces some of the best wine in the world. With its breathtaking scenery and winding river, it is also a great place for a scenic drive or river cruise.

  • Mata do Fontelo: The Mata do Fontelo is a beautiful park that dates back to Roman times. The park is home to a wide range of flora and fauna, and it is a great place to relax and enjoy nature.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Beira Alta

Beira Alta is a perfect destination for outdoor enthusiasts, boasting of scenic hiking and biking trails.

  • Ecopista do Dao: An easy and flat 49-kilometer trail that takes you through lush green landscapes and calm river banks. With beautiful views from start to finish, Ecopista do Dao is a perfect trail to explore with no struggle.

  • PR5 Lapa dos Dinheiros Trail: This 12-kilometer trail is an excellent option for those who like to challenge themselves, with its steep slopes and rocky descents. It offers breathtaking views of the surrounding mountains, as well as rivers and several waterfalls along the way.

  • PR7 Linhares da Beira Circuit: This 15-kilometer trail takes you through beautiful forests and rural landscapes, passing by small creeks and rivers, and the historic town of Linhares da Beira.

Best Things to Do in Beira Alta: Activities and Tours

  • Wine Tours: Beira Alta is home to excellent wine regions where you can take a guided tour of vineyards and wineries.

  • Adventure Sports: From hiking, canoeing, and mountain biking to rock climbing and paragliding, Beira Alta offers a vast range of activities for adventure sports enthusiasts.

  • Hot Springs: Enjoy the therapeutic and rejuvenating properties of the region’s natural hot springs.

Week-Long Itinerary for Beira Alta

Day 1 - Arrive in Viseu and explore some of the city’s landmarks like the Viseu Cathedral and Grao Vasco Museum

Day 2 - Take a scenic drive along Douro Valley and visit a few vineyards and wineries for some wine tasting.

Day 3 - Spend a day at Bussaco National Forest, explore the Manueline-style Royal Chapel and Santa Cruz convent.

Day 4 - Visit some of the authentic villages of Beira Alta, like Linhares da Beira and Playa del Lameira.

Day 5 - Visit the monumental Neolithic stone circles - Cromeleque do Vale do Coa - in the Vale do Coa Archaeological Park.

Day 6 - Take a hike to the peak of Serra da Estrela and enjoy scenic views of the surrounding valleys and the highest peak in continental Portugal.

Day 7 - Relax in the natural hot springs of Beira Alta.

Food and Drink Scene of Beira Alta

Must-Try Dishes

  • Cozido à Portuguesa: a stew made with various meats and vegetables, such as potatoes, carrots, beans, and cabbage.

  • Cabrito Assado: Roasted young goat served with roasted potatoes and vegetables.

  • Chanfana: Roasted pork or goat that is marinated in garlic, red wine, and spices

Local Beverages

  • Vinho Verde: A crisp, refreshing white wine that pairs perfectly with local seafood.

  • Dao Wines: The unique soil conditions and climate of Beira Alta make it the perfect place for the production of excellent red wines.

  • Licor Beirão: A traditional, sweet, herb-flavored liqueur that is perfect for a post-dinner drink.

Where to Stay in Beira Alta: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

There is a vast range of accommodation options in Beira Alta, catering to every budget and preference. From luxury hotels to cozy guesthouses and budget-friendly hostels, the Beira Alta has accommodations that suit all tastes.

What to Buy in Beira Alta: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Portuguese ceramics
  • Traditional hand-painted tiles
  • Wines and Liqueurs
  • Traditional Embroidery
  • Local Cheeses (Brie de Meaux, Petit Fromage, and Cabra da Serra)

Local Culture and Customs of Beira Alta

Beira Alta has a rich tradition and cultural events that include festive holidays, music, and dance. Make sure to immerse yourself in the local culture by taking part in their festivals and experiencing their food and drinks.

Best Time to Visit Beira Alta

The best time to visit Beira Alta is between March and October when temperatures are moderate and less rainy. However, if you’re a winter activity lover, visiting during wintertime (November to February) is also ideal for activities of skiing and snowboarding at Serra da Estrela Natural Park.

Do’s and Don’ts of Beira Alta

Do:

  • Try the local food and drinks, and immerse yourself in the local culture.
  • Be respectful and polite to locals, and appreciate their customs and traditions.

Don’t:

  • Do not litter when you are enjoying Beira Alta’s nature.
  • Avoid engaging in conversations about politics or Religion without earning trust first.

Safety and Health

Safety in Beira Alta

Beira Alta is known for its relatively safe environment, but it is always advisable to take basic precautions while traveling through Portugal. Always be alert to your surroundings and avoid walking in deserted or poorly lit areas at night.

Health in Beira Alta

Beira Alta has a high standard of healthcare, with access to excellent medical facilities across the region. Visitors require European Health Insurance Card to get any medical treatment.

Getting to Beira Alta: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Beira Alta

  • Porto’s International Francisco Sá Carneiro Airport (OPO) - 133 km away

  • Lisbon’s Portela Airport (LIS) - 320 km away

Bus Stations Near Beira Alta

  • Terminal Rodoviário do Porto - 130 km away

Other Options

Driving or renting a car is a great way to explore the region at your own pace.

How to Get Around in Beira Alta

Public transport in Beira Alta includes buses and trains. However, renting a car is the perfect option to effectively explore its countryside and picturesque views.

Where to Go Next from Beira Alta: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Visit the Alentejo region, known for its wine, olive groves, and vast plains.

  • Discover the medieval city of Coimbra and its stunning palaces and universities.

  • Venture to nearby Aveiro, the “Venice of Portugal,” famous for its canals and colorful boats.

Conclusion

No matter how long or short your trip is, Beira Alta will offer you a perfect blend of history, culture, and nature. We hope this tour guide has helped you plan your visit and enjoy all the breathtaking beauty and cultural richness of the Portuguese Beira Alta region.
