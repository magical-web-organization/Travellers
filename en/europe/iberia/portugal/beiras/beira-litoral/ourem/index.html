Introduction to Ourém

Located in the Beira Litoral region of Portugal, the medieval town of Ourém is a charming destination steeped in history, culture, and natural beauty. Known for its iconic castle and stunning views of the surrounding countryside, Ourém offers a plethora of activities for visitors of all ages. From exploring its winding alleyways and admiring its ancient architecture to hiking in the nearby mountains, there’s something for everyone in this enchanting town.

List of Places to Visit in Ourém

Here’s a list of must-visit places during your stay in Ourém:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Ourém Castle: This iconic landmark is the highlight of Ourém, offering breathtaking views of the town and the surrounding landscape from its walls.
  • Casa-Museu de São Thiago: Located within the castle walls, this museum features an array of historic artifacts and exhibits that showcase the region’s rich cultural heritage.
  • Igreja de Nossa Senhora das Misericórdias: This 16th-century church boasts stunning mock-Baroque designs and is home to several beautiful paintings and sculptures.

Natural Attractions of Ourém: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Serra de Aire e Candeeiros Natural Park: Just a short drive from Ourém, this national park is a haven for outdoor enthusiasts, offering breathtaking hiking trails, fragrant olive groves, and stunning limestone caves.
  • Parque Linear de Ourém: A green oasis in the heart of the town, this park is perfect for a leisurely afternoon stroll or a picnic with family and friends.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Ourém

For those who love the great outdoors, Ourém has several hiking and biking trails to explore. Some notable trails include:

  • Caminho da Pia do Urso: This 8.5-kilometer trail starts in Alvados and takes hikers through lush forests, across rocky ledges, and past tiny villages.
  • Trilho da Via Romana: This trail follows the ancient Roman road from Ourém to Tomar, offering beautiful views of the surrounding landscape and historic landmarks.
  • BTT Trilho do Lagar da Lameira: This 19-kilometer mountain biking route begins in the village of Caxarias and takes riders through pristine forests, past waterfalls, and over steep hills.

Best Things to Do in Ourém: Activities and Tours

  • Wine Tasting: Take a tour of one of the many vineyards surrounding Ourém and taste some of the region’s delicious wines.
  • Hot Air Balloon Ride: Soar above the stunning countryside on a hot air balloon ride, which offers breathtaking panoramic views of the landscape.
  • Horseback Riding: Explore the stunning mountains and valleys of Ourém on horseback, with several stables and equestrian centers offering rides for all skill levels.

Food and Drink Scene of Ourém

Must-Try Dishes

  • Arroz de Pato (duck rice)
  • Caldeirada de Peixe (fish stew)
  • Leitão à Bairrada (suckling pig)

Local Beverages

  • Ginja de Ourém: a sweet cherry liqueur
  • Vinho Verde: a crisp, refreshing white wine

Where to Stay in Ourém: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

There are several accommodation options in Ourém, ranging from budget-friendly hostels to luxurious hotels and guesthouses. Some notable options include:

  • Hotel de Santa Isabel: Located just steps from Ourém Castle, this charming hotel offers comfortable rooms with spectacular views.
  • Casa Alta: This guesthouse is located in the heart of the town and features beautifully decorated rooms and a cozy ambiance.
  • Hostel 2300 Thomar: This budget-friendly hostel is perfect for travelers on a budget, offering simple yet comfortable accommodations.

What to Buy in Ourém: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Ginja de Ourém: A bottle of this sweet cherry liqueur makes a great souvenir or gift for friends and family.
  • Hand-painted Pottery: The region is well-known for its tradition of pottery-making, so be sure to pick up a unique piece of ceramics to take home.
  • Olive Oil: The region is also renowned for its high-quality olive oil, which makes a delicious gift for food-loving friends and family.

Local Culture and Customs of Ourém

The people of Ourém are warm and friendly, with a strong sense of community. Traditional customs and festivals play a significant role in the town’s culture, including the annual Feira de São Mateus, which takes place in August and features live music, dancing, and colorful parades. Respect for local cultural practices and traditions is encouraged.

Best Time to Visit Ourém

The best time to visit Ourém is during the spring and fall, when the weather is mild and comfortable for outdoor activities. Summers tend to be hot and crowded, while winters can be chilly and rainy. The weather is perfect for strolling around the town, visiting the landmarks, and going on outdoor adventures like hiking and biking.

Do’s and Don’ts of Ourém

Do:

  • Respect local customs and traditions
  • Explore the natural beauty of the region
  • Try local cuisine and wine

Don’t:

  • Disrespect cultural or religious practices
  • Leave trash behind in natural areas
  • Ignore safety precautions during outdoor activities

Safety and Health

Safety in Ourém

Ourém is generally a safe town for tourists. However, it’s important to exercise caution when walking alone at night or in secluded areas. Avoid carrying large amounts of cash or valuable items and be aware of pickpockets in crowded areas.

Health in Ourém

Medical facilities in Ourém are of a high standard, with several hospitals and clinics available. Medical insurance is recommended for visitors in case of emergencies.

Getting to Ourém: Transportation Options and Logistics

Here are some transportation options for getting to Ourém:

Airports Near Ourém

  • Lisbon Portela Airport: Located about 140 km south of Ourém, this is the nearest international airport.

Bus Stations Near Ourém

  • Ourém Bus Station: Located in the center of town, this bus station has regular connections to nearby cities.

Other Options

  • Car Rental: Renting a car is a convenient option for travelers who want to explore the region on their own.

How to Get Around in Ourém

Walking and biking are the best ways to get around in Ourém, as the town is small and easy to navigate. Taxis and buses are also available for those who prefer not to walk or bike.

Where to Go Next from Ourém: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Tomar: This historic city is just a short drive from Ourém and is home to the stunning Convento de Cristo, a UNESCO World Heritage Site.
  • Fátima: A pilgrimage site for Catholics around the world, Fátima is just a 20-minute drive from Ourém.
  • Leiria: This charming city is home to a medieval castle, stunning beaches, and delicious seafood restaurants.

Conclusion

Ourém is a charming destination that offers visitors a unique blend of history, culture, and natural beauty. With its historic landmarks, stunning landscapes, and friendly locals, it’s the perfect destination for a memorable holiday. This comprehensive travel guide will help you plan your trip and make the most of your stay in Ourém.
