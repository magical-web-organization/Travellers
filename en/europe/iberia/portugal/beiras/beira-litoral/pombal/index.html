Introduction to Pombal

Located in the beautiful region of Beira Litoral, in Beiras, Portugal, Pombal offers a perfect blend of natural beauty, rich culture, and history. The city is known for its outdoor activities, beautiful landscapes, and traditional cuisine. If you’re planning a trip to Portugal, Pombal should definitely be on your list.

List of Places to Visit in Pombal

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Pombal Castle: This 12th-century castle is a symbol of strength and resilience. It has been restored and converted into a museum, which provides an insight into the city’s history.
  • Municipal Museum: Housed in a former convent, the Municipal Museum showcases the local history and culture through artefacts, paintings, and sculptures.
  • Monastery of Santa Clara: The Monastery of Santa Clara is a must-visit attraction that dates back to the 17th century. It is known for its beautiful architecture and religious significance.

Natural Attractions of Pombal: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Serras de Aire e Candeeiros Natural Park: The park is perfect for nature lovers. It features beautiful views of limestone cliffs, caves, and waterfalls. You can go hiking, go bird watching or take a leisurely picnic.
  • Rio Arunca: Rio Arunca is a beautiful river that winds through Pombal, Batalha, and Soure. You can take a kayak or canoe and explore the stunning scenery or go fishing in the river.
  • Lagoa da Ervideira: This lagoon is known for its crystal clear waters, beautiful beach, and surrounding areas. You can swim, picnic, or enjoy the scenery while kayaking.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Pombal

  • Ecotrail dos Pardieiros: This trail is 12 km long and features stunning views of the landscape, including waterfalls and picturesque villages.
  • Rota da Madeira: The Rota da Madeira is a 22 km long trail that takes you through the beautiful Forest of Leiria. It is perfect for bird watching, nature lovers, and hiking enthusiasts.
  • Circular da Serra da Boa Viagem: This trail is 6 km long and provides beautiful views of the Atlantic Ocean. It is perfect for a leisurely hike or bike ride.

Best Things to Do in Pombal: Activities and Tours

  • Hot Air Balloon Ride: See Pombal from a unique perspective by taking a hot air balloon ride over the city.
  • Wine Tasting: Visit one of the region’s wineries and experience the traditional taste of Portuguese wines.
  • Surfing: Hit the waves for a day of surfing on the Atlantic coast within driving.

Food and Drink Scene of Pombal

Must-Try Dishes

  • Leitão Assado: This traditional dish is roasted suckling pig, which is a regional delicacy of Pombal.
  • Bacalhau: Bacalhau is dried and salted cod, which is a common ingredient in Portuguese cuisine and is served in many restaurants in Pombal.
  • Arroz de Marisco: This seafood rice dish is a must-try in Pombal and is available in many restaurants around the city.

Local Beverages

  • Vinho Verde: Vinho Verde is a refreshing, young wine, which is produced in the region. It is the perfect drink to accompany any meal and is available in most bars and restaurants in Pombal.
  • Ginja: This traditional Portuguese liquor is made from sour cherries and is a must-try for all visitors to Pombal.

Where to Stay in Pombal: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Hotel Pombalense: Conveniently located in the heart of the city, this hotel offers comfortable rooms and is perfect for visitors who want to stay close to all the main attractions.
  • Eco Hostel: This eco-friendly hostel is perfect for travelers who are looking for an affordable, sustainable, and cozy place to stay.
  • Casa do Outeiro: This charming guesthouse offers a beautiful view of the countryside and is the perfect place for those who want to experience the local life.

What to Buy in Pombal: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Ceramics: Pombal is known for its pottery and ceramics, which are handmade and hand-painted. You can buy a variety of items, including bowls, vases, and plates.
  • Liqueurs: Many local producers offer a range of different liqueurs, including ginja and honey liqueur, which make perfect gifts or souvenirs.
  • Traditional Handicrafts: You can buy traditional handicrafts, including embroidery, lace, and baskets, which are made by local artisans.

Local Culture and Customs of Pombal

  • Language: Portuguese is the official language of Pombal. However, many locals also speak English.
  • Religion: Portugal is a predominantly Catholic country, and Pombal is no exception. Expect to see several churches and religious monuments throughout the city.
  • Festivals: Pombal hosts several festivals throughout the year, including the Festival da Juventude, which celebrates youth culture, and the Feira De São Matias, a traditional fair.

Best Time to Visit Pombal

The best time to visit Pombal is between April and October when the weather is mild, and the days are longer.

Do’s and Don’ts of Pombal

Do:

  • Explore the Outdoors: Pombal is known for its natural beauty, so be sure to spend some time exploring the beautiful parks, forests, and beaches.
  • Try the Local Cuisine: Portuguese food is delicious, so be sure to try some of the traditional dishes, including leitão and arroz de marisco.
  • Respect the Customs and Traditions: Portugal is a traditional country, so be respectful of the local customs, religion, and culture.

Don’t:

  • Bargain Prices in Shops and Markets: Unlike some Southeast Asian countries, bargaining is not a part of Portuguese culture. So, don’t be a haggler when shopping in Pombal.
  • Be Rude to Locals: Portuguese people are friendly and welcoming, so be polite and respectful to the locals.

Safety and Health

Safety in Pombal

Pombal is a safe city for travelers. However, it’s always a good idea to take basic precautions like avoiding poorly lit areas and not leaving your valuables unattended.

Health in Pombal

Medical facilities in Pombal are excellent and easily accessible in case of an emergency. However, it’s always a good idea to carry travel insurance that covers medical expenses.

Getting to Pombal: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Pombal

  • Porto Airport: 1 h 30 min by car or 1 h 50 min by train.
  • Lisbon Airport: 2 h by car or 2 h 30 min by train.

Bus Stations Near Pombal

  • Rodoviária do Tejo: Located on Largo do Pelourinho, Pombal town center.
  • Expresso: Located near the train station on Rua António José da Silva.

Other Options

  • Train: Pombal is located on the main train line between Lisbon and Porto.
  • Car Rental: You can rent a car from either Porto or Lisbon and drive to Pombal.

How to Get Around in Pombal

The best way to get around Pombal is on foot, especially if you’re staying in the town center. However, car rentals and taxis are also available for those who prefer not to walk.

Where to Go Next from Pombal: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Coimbra: Coimbra is just a short drive or train ride away from Pombal. It’s a historic city known for its prestigious university and rich culture.
  • Batalha: Batalha is known for its stunning monastery, which is a UNESCO World Heritage site and is just a 20-minute drive from Pombal.
  • Figueira da Foz: This coastal town is known for its beautiful beach, which is perfect for swimming and sunbathing.

Conclusion

Pombal is a city that has something to offer for every type of traveler. Whether you’re interested in outdoor adventures, history, culture or food, Pombal is the perfect destination. Plan your trip today and experience the best that Portugal has to offer.
