Introduction to Ovar

Located in Beira Litoral, Beiras, Portugal, Ovar is a charming riverside town on the Atlantic Coast. It is a haven for outdoor enthusiasts, boasting scenic views, hiking and cycling trails, and water-based activities. The town is also steeped in history and culture, with several museums and monuments showcasing the rich heritage of the region. In this travel guide, we’ll explore the top attractions, food and drink scene, and cultural customs that make Ovar a must-visit destination in Portugal.

List of Places to Visit in Ovar

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Ovar has many historically significant sites that showcase Portugal’s past. Visitors can immerse themselves in the local culture by exploring the following:

  • Museu de Ovar - This museum is located in a 19th-century palace and houses an extensive collection of art and artifacts from the region’s history.
  • Igreja Paroquial de Ovar - This beautiful church features unique tile work and ornate carvings.
  • Antigo Aljube de Ovar - This former jail is now an exhibition space with displays about the local history of the town.
  • Casa-Museu Júlio Dinis - This charming house museum was the birthplace of renowned Portuguese writer Júlio Dinis.

Natural Attractions of Ovar: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Ovar is home to stunning natural beauty, from rolling hills to picturesque beaches. Here are some must-see outdoor attractions:

  • Ria de Aveiro - This scenic lagoon is perfect for bird-watching, kayaking, and fishing.
  • Praia da Cortegaça - This beautiful beach is popular among surfers and is known for its impressive sand dunes.
  • Serra da Freita - This mountain range offers spectacular views and is perfect for hiking and cycling.
  • Ecopista do Rio Caima - This hiking and biking trail follows the banks of the Caima River and is a great way to explore the surrounding countryside.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Ovar

Ovar offers many hiking and biking trails for outdoor enthusiasts. Some notable routes include:

  • PR5 (Vale de Cambra) - A scenic 16-kilometer hiking trail that winds through the beautiful countryside of Vale de Cambra, offering stunning vistas of the area.
  • PR2 (Ovar) - A 14-kilometer hiking trail that takes you through the forests and mountains of Ovar.
  • Rota da Linha do Vouga - This cycling route follows the Vouga river, passing through pretty villages and rural landscapes.

Best Things to Do in Ovar: Activities and Tours

Here are some of the best things to do in Ovar:

  • Take a guided tour of the Ria de Aveiro to see the beautiful lagoon and its wildlife up close.
  • Visit the weekly farmer’s market in the town square to sample local produce and handicrafts.
  • Attend a local festival, such as the Feira das Cebolas (Onion Fair).
  • Take a cooking class and learn how to make traditional Portuguese dishes, such as arroz de tamboril (monkfish rice).

Week-Long Itinerary for Ovar

Here’s a suggested itinerary for a week-long stay in Ovar:

Day Activities
1 Walk through the historic center of Ovar and visit the Museu de Ovar
2 Explore the natural beauty of Serra da Freita
3 Take a guided tour of the Ria de Aveiro
4 Sample the local food scene and visit the Casa-Museu Júlio Dinis
5 Relax on Praia da Cortegaça

Food and Drink Scene of Ovar

No trip to Ovar is complete without trying the delicious local cuisine. Here are some must-try dishes and beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Caldeirada de Enguias - A stew made with eels, potatoes, and onions.
  • Tripas à Murteira - A hearty dish made with tripe, white beans, and spices.
  • Pão de Ló - A soft, fluffy cake made with eggs and sugar.

Local Beverages

  • Vinho Verde - A refreshing, slightly effervescent white wine that pairs well with seafood dishes.
  • Licor de Aveiro - A sweet liqueur made with local herbs and spices.
  • Porto - A fortified wine that is typically served as an after-dinner drink.

Where to Stay in Ovar: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Ovar has plenty of accommodation options for all budgets and preferences, from luxury hotels to budget-friendly hostels. Here are some suggestions:

  • Hotel Meia Lua - This elegant hotel is located in the heart of Ovar and offers comfortable rooms and a restaurant serving traditional Portuguese cuisine.
  • Ovar Village Hostel - This cozy hostel is perfect for backpackers and budget travelers. It offers shared and private rooms and is located near the town center.
  • Casa da Ria - This charming guesthouse is located in a restored 19th-century house and offers spacious rooms and a lovely garden.

What to Buy in Ovar: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Here are some of the best souvenirs and local products to bring home from Ovar:

  • Azulejos - These beautiful hand-painted tiles are a popular souvenir from Portugal.
  • Bordado de Ovar - Traditional embroidery work that is unique to Ovar and makes for a stunning home decor item.
  • Doce de Ovos - A sweet pastry made with egg yolks and sugar that is a specialty of the region.

Local Culture and Customs of Ovar

Ovar has a rich cultural heritage that is reflected in its architecture, festivals, and food. Some important customs and traditions to keep in mind when visiting Ovar include:

  • Respecting religious sites and customs.
  • Dressing modestly when entering churches or other sacred spaces.
  • Participating in local festivals and events, such as the famed Carnaval de Ovar.

Best Time to Visit Ovar

The best time to visit Ovar is during the summer months, from June to September when the weather is warm and sunny. However, if you prefer cooler temperatures and less crowds, the spring and fall months are also good options.

Do’s and Don’ts of Ovar

Do:

  • Try the local cuisine and wine.
  • Respect the local culture and customs.
  • Take time to explore the natural beauty of the region.

Don’t:

  • Ignore the dress code when entering religious sites.
  • Leave valuables unattended on the beach or in public spaces.
  • Disrespect the local traditions and customs.

Safety and Health

Safety in Ovar

Ovar is a safe place for tourists, but it’s always wise to take precautions when traveling. Here are some safety tips to keep in mind:

  • Keep a close eye on your belongings in crowded areas.
  • Avoid walking alone at night in unfamiliar areas.
  • Be aware of your surroundings when using public transport.

Health in Ovar

Portugal has a good standard of healthcare, and Ovar has several medical facilities should you need care during your visit. Remember to pack any necessary medications and make sure you have adequate travel health insurance.

Getting to Ovar: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Ovar

The closest airport to Ovar is Francisco Sá Carneiro Airport in Porto, which is about a 45-minute drive away.

Bus Stations Near Ovar

Ovar has a bus station that offers connections to other cities in Portugal. There are also regular bus services to nearby towns and attractions.

Other Options

Taxis and car rentals are also available in Ovar for those who prefer private transportation.

How to Get Around in Ovar

Ovar is a compact town, and most of the attractions and restaurants are within walking distance of each other. However, if you want to explore the surrounding countryside, renting a car or bike is a good option.

Where to Go Next from Ovar: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Ovar is located in the heart of the Beiras region, making it a great base for exploring the surrounding areas. Some nearby destinations and excursions to consider include:

  • Aveiro - Known as the “Venice of Portugal,” this beautiful town is just a short drive from Ovar and offers picturesque canals and historic architecture.
  • Porto - Portugal’s second-largest city is less than an hour’s drive from Ovar and offers world-class museums, food, and wine.
  • Serra do Buçaco - This mountain range is home to the beautiful Buçaco Palace Hotel and Park, known for its stunning gardens and hiking trails.

Conclusion

Ovar is a charming town in the heart of the Beiras region, offering a perfect blend of natural beauty, history, culture, and delicious food and wine. With so many activities and attractions to choose from, there’s something for every type of traveler in Ovar.
