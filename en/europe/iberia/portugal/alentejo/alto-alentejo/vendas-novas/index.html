Introduction to Vendas Novas

Located in the heart of Alto Alentejo in Portugal, Vendas Novas is a charming, unspoiled town that boasts a unique blend of culture, history, and natural beauty. The town is conveniently located just 45 minutes away from both Lisbon and Évora, making it a popular stop for day trips or weekend getaways.

List of Places to Visit in Vendas Novas

Vendas Novas is packed with cultural and natural attractions, making it an excellent destination for those looking for an off-the-beaten-path experience. Here’s our list of must-see places in Vendas Novas:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Vendas Novas Palace: Built in the 17th century, this historic palace was once a royal summer residence. Today, it houses the town hall and is open to the public for guided tours.

  • Museum of Soy Sauce: This unique museum showcases the history of soy sauce production in Vendas Novas. Visitors can take part in guided tours and learn about the production process from start to finish.

  • Church of Nossa Senhora das Vitórias: This beautiful church features stunning baroque architecture and is home to a collection of religious art and artifacts.

Natural Attractions of Vendas Novas: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Monte das Flores: This nature reserve covers more than 6,000 hectares and offers a variety of hiking and biking trails, as well as breathtaking views of the surrounding countryside.

  • Praia Fluvial de Vale do Poço: Located on the banks of the Poço River, this popular spot offers a sandy beach and crystal-clear waters, ideal for swimming and sunbathing.

  • Serra D`Ossa: This mountain range boasts stunning landscapes, rugged terrain, and is a popular destination for avid hikers and outdoor enthusiasts.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Vendas Novas

  • Rota do Xerez: This 10-kilometer route takes hikers through the scenic countryside of Vendas Novas, passing by vineyards and olive groves.

  • Alto de São Francisco: This challenging 6-kilometer trek takes hikers to the top of the Serra D`Ossa mountain range, offering breathtaking panoramic views of the surrounding landscape.

Best Things to Do in Vendas Novas: Activities and Tours

  • Soy sauce factory tour: Take a guided tour of the local soy sauce factory, where you’ll learn about the production process and get to sample some of the finished product.

  • Wine tasting: Vendas Novas is home to a variety of excellent wineries, where visitors can take part in guided wine tastings and learn about the local grape varieties.

Week-Long Itinerary for Vendas Novas

Day 1: Arrive in Vendas Novas and explore the historic town center, including the Palace and the Church of Nossa Senhora das Vitórias.

Day 2-3: Take a guided tour of the local soy sauce factory and spend a day hiking in the Serra D`Ossa mountain range.

Day 4: Explore the stunning landscapes and hiking trails of Monte das Flores.

Day 5: Relax on the white sandy beaches of Praia Fluvial de Vale do Poço.

Day 6: Take a wine tasting tour of the local wineries and sample some of the excellent Alentejo wines.

Day 7: Depart from Vendas Novas.

Food and Drink Scene of Vendas Novas

Vendas Novas is known for its delicious local cuisine and excellent wines. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Cozido de Grão: A hearty stew made with chickpeas, beef, and other meats.

  • Bifanas: Thinly sliced pork sandwiches served on a soft roll with mustard and hot sauce.

  • Sopa de Cação: Also known as dogfish soup, this flavorful fish soup is a staple of the Alentejo region.

Local Beverages

  • Alentejo Wines: Vendas Novas is home to a variety of excellent wineries, producing some of the best wines in the Alentejo region.

  • Ginjinha: A sweet cherry liqueur made from locally grown cherries.

Where to Stay in Vendas Novas: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Pousada de Vendas Novas: A historic palace converted into a luxurious hotel, featuring a swimming pool, spa, and restaurant.

  • Casa do Sol: A cozy guesthouse located in the historic town center, offering comfortable rooms and a charming courtyard garden.

  • Hostel Vendas Novas: A budget-friendly hostel located just a few minutes’ walk from the town center, offering shared and private rooms.

What to Buy in Vendas Novas: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Ceramics: Vendas Novas is known for its unique handmade ceramics, featuring traditional Alentejo-inspired designs.

  • Soy Sauce: Take home some of the delicious local soy sauce, made from all-natural ingredients and free from additives.

Local Culture and Customs of Vendas Novas

The people of Vendas Novas are proud of their rich cultural heritage and tradition of hospitality. Visitors are always welcome and are encouraged to participate in local festivals and events.

Best Time to Visit Vendas Novas

The best time to visit Vendas Novas is between March and October when the weather is mild, and outdoor activities can be enjoyed without extreme heat or cold. Vendas Novas is a vibrant town year-round, but summer is the busiest season, with many festivals and events taking place.

Do’s and Don’ts of Vendas Novas

Do:

  • Learn some Portuguese phrases, as many locals do not speak English.

  • Respect local customs and traditions, such as dress codes when visiting religious sites.

  • Try the local food and wine, as it is a big part of the cultural experience in Vendas Novas.

Don’t:

  • Leave your valuables in plain sight, especially in busy tourist areas.

  • Wear revealing clothing in religious sites or in public spaces.

Safety and Health

Safety in Vendas Novas

Vendas Novas is a safe town, with a low crime rate. However, visitors should still exercise caution while travelling and keep an eye on their belongings.

Health in Vendas Novas

Vendas Novas has a modern hospital and several health clinics. Visitors should ensure they have proper health insurance and be aware of any necessary vaccinations before travelling to Portugal.

Getting to Vendas Novas: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Vendas Novas

The closest airport to Vendas Novas is in Lisbon, approximately 80 kilometers away. From the airport, visitors can take a bus or rent a car to reach Vendas Novas.

Bus Stations Near Vendas Novas

Vendas Novas has a central bus station, offering regular connections to nearby cities and towns in the Alentejo region.

Other Options

Driving to Vendas Novas is also an option, and visitors can rent a car at the airport in Lisbon.

How to Get Around in Vendas Novas

The town center of Vendas Novas is easily explored on foot, but for longer distances or trips outside of town, renting a car or taking a taxi is recommended.

Where to Go Next from Vendas Novas: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Évora: Located just 45 minutes away, Évora is a UNESCO World Heritage site known for its historic city walls, Roman ruins, and medieval architecture.

  • Lisbon: Portugal’s capital city is just over an hour’s drive from Vendas Novas, offering visitors world-class museums, historic landmarks, and fantastic dining and shopping options.

Conclusion

Vendas Novas is a hidden gem in the heart of Portugal’s Alentejo region, offering visitors a unique blend of culture, history, and natural beauty. From hiking and biking to exploring the region’s rich culinary traditions, Vendas Novas is a must-visit destination for those looking to experience the best of Portugal’s unspoiled countryside.
