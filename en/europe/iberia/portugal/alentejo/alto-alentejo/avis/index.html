Introduction to Avis

Located in the heart of Alentejo region in Portugal, Avis is a small town with a population of approximately 4,000 residents. Avis is known for its stunning natural beauty, rich history, and cultural significance. The town has a lot to offer to travelers who are looking for an authentic and unforgettable experience.

In this comprehensive travel guide, we’ll explore the top attractions, natural wonders, and cultural highlights of Avis. From hiking and biking to exploring historical sites, Avis has something to offer for every traveler.

List of Places to Visit in Avis

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Castle of Avis

The Castle of Avis is a fascinating historical site located on a hilltop. Visitors can get a glimpse of medieval life in the castle as well as learn about the town’s history through the exhibitions and displays.

Pillory of Avis

Pillory of Avis is a landmark historical place located in the center of the town. It was a place where punishments were carried out during the medieval period. Nowadays, it is a popular spot for tourists to take photographs.

Nossa Senhora da Consolação church

This church is an impressive and beautiful piece of art from the 16th century. Visitors can explore its ancient art and devote themselves to a peaceful meditation or prayer that will allow them to connect with the spiritual traditions of Portugal.

Natural Attractions of Avis: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Albufeira de Maranhão Reservoir

The Albufeira de Maranhão reservoir is a beautiful spot popular for fishing, swimming, and water sports such as kayaking and canoeing. The reservoir is surrounded by lush green forests, and the landscape is breathtaking.

Monte da Tojeirinha Windmill

Monte da Tojeirinha Windmill is an impressive creation, standing tall and proud in the lush countryside. Visitors can hike up to the windmill and admire the impressive view of the Alentejo landscape and the stunning sunset in the region.

Mata Nacional de Avis

Mata Nacional de Avis is a beautiful natural park, located just outside of the town. It is perfect for forest bathing and hiking while enjoying the beautiful landscape and scenery. The forest features a selection of stunning trails and paths that are suited for hikers and cyclists.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Avis

Avis has several hiking and biking trails that are perfect for nature enthusiasts. Here are some of the best trails to explore in Avis:

  1. Rota dos Moinhos (Windmill Route) - A scenic cycling route that is approximately 30km long, featuring scenic countryside, and stunning views of the windmills.

  2. PR1 Avis – Trail of the Churches – A 13km hiking route perfect for exploring the local culture and heritage.

  3. Rota do Carrascalinho – A beautiful 6km hiking and biking route that allows visitors to explore the stunning landscape and breathtaking views of the region.

Best Things to Do in Avis: Activities and Tours

Take a scenic drive

Avis is home to beautiful landscapes and scenic roads, which are perfect for a leisurely drive. Visitors can discover the hidden gems of the region while enjoying the stunning natural beauty of the Alentejo region.

Visit the weekly market

Visitors to Avis can explore the town’s weekly market, held every Saturday, where they can shop for handmade crafts, local products, and fresh produce that are unique to the town and the region.

Week-Long Itinerary for Avis

Day 1: Visit the Castle of Avis and the Pillory of Avis.

Day 2: Take a scenic drive on the roads around Avis and explore the countryside.

Day 3: Take a bicycle tour around Avis and explore the local countryside trails.

Day 4: Visit Albufeira de Maranhão Reservoir and have a wonderful day exploring the reservoir.

Day 5: Explore Mata Nacional de Avis and spend some time in nature.

Day 6: Visit the Monte da Tojeirinha Windmill and take in the beautiful sunset views.

Day 7: Visit Nossa Senhora da Consolação church and attend a local service to experience the spiritual traditions of Portugal.

Food and Drink Scene of Avis

Must-Try Dishes

Avis cuisine is known for its hearty and delicious dishes. Here are some of the best dishes to try in Avis:

Açorda de Bacalhau

Açorda de Bacalhau is a popular Portuguese dish made with bread and salted cod, garlic, olive oil, and poached eggs. It is a classic Portuguese dish that is flavorful and hearty.

Camezola

This creamy cheese, a combination of goat and sheep milk, is an Avis specialty. It’s often used in dishes such as omelets and salads, and it has a distinctive, creamy taste.

Local Beverages

Vinho Regional Alentejano

Vinho Regional Alentejano is a fine wine produced in the Alentejo region, famous for its full-bodied red wine. The region is known for its unique soil and warm climate that helps the grape varieties grow.

Ginjinha

Ginjinha is a Portuguese cherry liquor, made by mixing sour cherry liqueur with aguardente, sugar, and water. The drink is refreshing with a light and fruity flavor, making it perfect for sipping on a hot day.

Where to Stay in Avis: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Avis offers a variety of accommodations options, from luxury hotels to budget hotels and guesthouses. Here are a few recommendations:

Herdade da Calada

Herdade da Calada is a beautiful historic hotel that offers a luxury experience to guests. The hotel is located in a beautiful farm that dates back to the 15th century, which has been carefully restored to provide a comfortable and relaxing stay.

Casa da Moita Nova

Casa da Moita Nova is a charming guesthouse located in the countryside, offering an authentic country experience. Rooms are comfortable and beautifully decorated, and the guesthouse has a quiet and peaceful ambiance, perfect for relaxing.

What to Buy in Avis: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Camezola Cheese

Camezola cheese is a must-try when visiting Avis. It’s a unique combination of goat and sheep milk, and it’s a great souvenir to bring home.

Pottery

Avis is known for its traditional and stunning pottery, which has been made in the region for centuries. Visitors can find a range of styles and types of pottery to choose from that make great gifts and souvenirs.

Local Culture and Customs of Avis

The people of Avis are proud of their heritage and customs. Family and community values are significant in Avisian culture, and there is a close connection between people and the surrounding landscape.

Best Time to Visit Avis

The best time to visit Avis is in the spring or fall when the weather is mild and pleasant. Summer temperatures can get hot, making outdoor activities challenging. Winter is a less favorable option, as the weather can be rainy and cold.

Do’s and Don’ts of Avis

Do:

  • Respect the local culture and customs.
  • Leave no trash behind while hiking or exploring the outdoors.
  • Learn a few basic Portuguese phrases to show respect to locals.

Don’t:

  • Leave valuables unattended in public spaces.
  • Disrespect the natural environment by littering.
  • Dress too revealingly or inappropriately in public places.

Safety and Health

Safety in Avis

Avis is a safe town, and crime rates are low. However, visitors should still take precautions and lock their cars or bikes when left unattended and avoid carrying large amounts of cash.

Health in Avis

Avis has a few medical clinics and pharmacies, but visitors with specific medical needs should bring all necessary medications and supplies with them.

Getting to Avis: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Avis

Lisbon Portela Airport is the nearest international airport to Avis, and it is located approximately 1.5 hours away. Visitors can rent a car or take a taxi to reach Avis.

Bus Stations Near Avis

The nearest bus station to Avis is located in the nearby town of Ponte de Sor, approximately 15 minutes by car from Avis.

Other Options

Visitors can also reach Avis by train, and the nearest train station is located in the nearby town of Portalegre, approximately 30 minutes by car.

How to Get Around in Avis

The best way to explore Avis is by bike or on foot. Visitors can rent bicycles or walk around the town and take in the sights and sounds at a leisurely pace.

Where to Go Next from Avis: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Marvão

Marvão is a nearby town that is known for its beautiful castle and stunning views. Visitors can explore the narrow streets, taste the local cuisine, and enjoy the beautiful views of the town.

Serra de São Mamede Natural Park

Located just 45 minutes away from Avis, Serra de São Mamede Natural Park boasts stunning landscape, walking trails, and natural wonders. Visitors can explore the beautiful scenery and enjoy the stunning views of the region.

Conclusion

Avis is a hidden gem that offers visitors an authentic Portuguese experience filled with unique history, natural wonders, and delicious cuisine. With this comprehensive travel guide, visitors can explore the best of Avis and create an unforgettable travel experience.
