Introduction to Alto Alentejo

Located in the heart of Alentejo, Alto Alentejo is one of the most stunning and culturally rich regions in Portugal. The area is home to picturesque vineyards, beautiful olive groves, and unspoiled countryside, offering visitors a perfect escape from the hustle and bustle of city life.

Alto Alentejo features a rich blend of cultures, including Roman, Moorish, and Christian, which have left their mark on the region’s architecture, art, and customs. Whether you’re interested in exploring culture and history, hiking and biking, or enjoying an outdoor adventure, Alto Alentejo is sure to leave a lasting impression.

In this guide, we’ll introduce you to the best places to visit, hiking and biking routes, must-try food and drink, transportation options, and more.

List of Places to Visit in Alto Alentejo

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Castelo de Marvão

Castelo de Marvão is a stunning medieval castle located at the top of a hill in the town of Marvão. The castle dates back to the 13th century and offers visitors an incredible panoramic view of Alto Alentejo.

Museu da Tapeçaria de Portalegre Guy Fino

The Museu da Tapeçaria de Portalegre Guy Fino is an exquisite museum that showcases Portuguese tapestry art. The museum features works dating back to the 16th century, as well as pieces from contemporary artists.

Igreja de Santo Amaro

Built in the 16th century under the Renaissance style, Igreja de Santo Amaro is a beautiful church located in the town of Portalegre. The church features stunning works of art and architecture, making it a must-visit attraction in the region.

Natural Attractions of Alto Alentejo: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Parque Natural da Serra de São Mamede

Parque Natural da Serra de São Mamede is a stunning natural park that covers an area of 56,000 hectares. The park is home to a diverse range of flora and fauna, as well as scenic hiking trails and biking routes.

Crato Castle

Crato Castle is a picturesque castle located in the town of Crato. The castle dates back to the 13th century and is perched on a hill, offering visitors incredible views of the surrounding countryside.

Portas de Rodão Natural Monument

Portas de Rodão Natural Monument is a striking natural feature, consisting of towering cliffs and a winding river. The monument is ideal for hiking and exploring.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Alto Alentejo

Alto Alentejo is a paradise for outdoor enthusiasts, offering a range of challenging and scenic hiking and biking routes. Some of the top routes include:

  • GR48 - Rota das Vinhas e dos Vinhos
  • Rota dos Miradouros
  • PR2 - Rota do Marvão

Best Things to Do in Alto Alentejo: Activities and Tours

Alto Alentejo is home to a range of activities and tours, including:

  • Wine tasting at local vineyards
  • Horseback riding
  • Hot air balloon rides
  • Jeep tours of the countryside

Week-Long Itinerary for Alto Alentejo

If you’re planning a week-long trip to Alto Alentejo, consider this itinerary:

  • Day 1: Arrive in Portalegre and explore the town.
  • Day 2: Visit the Museu da Tapeçaria de Portalegre Guy Fino and hike the Rota do Marvão trail.
  • Day 3: Explore Marvão and visit the Castelo de Marvão.
  • Day 4: Visit the town of Crato and hike the Rota dos Miradouros trail.
  • Day 5: Take a hot air balloon ride over the Parque Natural da Serra de São Mamede.
  • Day 6: Go horseback riding in the countryside and visit a local vineyard for a wine tasting.
  • Day 7: Depart from Alto Alentejo.

Food and Drink Scene of Alto Alentejo

Must-Try Dishes

Alto Alentejo is famous for its hearty soups and stews, including:

  • Sopa de Peixe - a fish soup with vegetables and bread.
  • Migas - a dish made with bread, garlic, paprika, and olive oil.
  • Bacalhau à Lagareiro - cod roasted with garlic and olive oil.

Local Beverages

Alentejo wine is famous throughout Portugal, and Alto Alentejo is home to several vineyards and wineries. Be sure to try some of the local wines, including:

  • Vinho Verde
  • Vinho Tinto
  • Vinho Branco

Where to Stay in Alto Alentejo: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Alto Alentejo offers a range of accommodation options to suit all budgets. Some of the top hotels, hostels, and guesthouses include:

  • Pousada do Marvão
  • Torre de Palma Wine Hotel
  • Casa da Moira

What to Buy in Alto Alentejo: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Alto Alentejo is known for its handicrafts, including pottery, tapestry, and handwoven blankets. You’ll also find local cheeses, olive oils, and wines that make perfect souvenirs to bring home.

Local Culture and Customs of Alto Alentejo

Alto Alentejo is an area rich in culture and traditions, including:

  • Festa da Flor & Festa das Tochas Floridas - a flower festival that takes place in the town of Castelo de Vide.
  • Nossa Senhora da Penha - a religious festival that takes place in the town of Marvão.
  • Tapetes de Arraiolos - traditional handmade carpets.

Best Time to Visit Alto Alentejo

The best time to visit Alto Alentejo is in the spring (April-June) or fall (September-November) when the weather is mild and pleasant.

Do’s and Don’ts of Alto Alentejo

Do:

  • Explore the local towns and villages.
  • Try the local food and wine.
  • Hike or bike the stunning countryside.

Don’t:

  • Forget to pack sunscreen and a hat.
  • Leave trash in the natural park or countryside.
  • Disrespect local customs and traditions.

Safety and Health

Safety in Alto Alentejo

Alto Alentejo is a safe destination for travelers, but it’s always a good idea to take basic safety precautions, such as keeping valuables out of sight and using a safe at your hotel.

Health in Alto Alentejo

Alto Alentejo is generally a healthy destination, but travelers should be aware of ticks when hiking or biking in the countryside. It’s recommended to wear long pants and sleeves and use insect repellent.

Getting to Alto Alentejo: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Alto Alentejo

The closest airport to Alto Alentejo is Lisbon Portela Airport, which is approximately a 2-hour drive from the region.

Bus Stations Near Alto Alentejo

There are several daily bus routes that connect Alto Alentejo with Lisbon and other nearby cities.

Other Options

Renting a car is a great option for travelers who want to explore Alto Alentejo at their own pace.

How to Get Around in Alto Alentejo

Getting around Alto Alentejo is best done by car or bike. However, there are local buses that run between the towns and villages.

Where to Go Next from Alto Alentejo: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Nearby destinations and excursions from Alto Alentejo include:

  • Évora
  • Monsaraz
  • Serra da Estrela Natural Park

Conclusion

Alto Alentejo is a stunning region of Portugal with a rich cultural heritage and beautiful countryside. Whether you’re interested in exploring medieval castles, hiking and biking, or trying local food and wine, Alto Alentejo has something for everyone. Start planning your trip today and discover this hidden gem for yourself.
