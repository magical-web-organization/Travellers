Introduction to Setúbal

Setúbal is a charming coastal city located on the Setúbal Peninsula in Portugal’s Estremadura, a region famous for its wine and olive oil. This picturesque city is rich in culture and history and offers visitors the perfect mix of urban and natural attractions. Setúbal boasts stunning beaches, impressive architecture, and mouth-watering food and drink scene. Whether you’re looking for an adventure or a relaxing vacation, Setúbal has something for you.

List of Places to Visit in Setúbal

When traveling to Setúbal, make sure to visit these top attractions:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Setúbal Museum and Archaeological Center - This museum offers a glimpse into the region’s history, showcasing artifacts from prehistoric times to the present day.

  • Monastery of Jesus - A UNESCO World Heritage site, this stunning monastery features intricate Gothic architecture and beautiful gardens.

  • Castelo de São Filipe - This 16th-century fortress offers panoramic views of the city and the Sado River.

  • Casa da Cultura - A former palace-turned-cultural center showcasing art exhibitions and various cultural events.

Natural Attractions of Setúbal: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Arrábida Natural Park - A stunning nature reserve known for its scenic views, crystal clear waters, and superb hiking trails.

  • Serra da Arrábida - This rugged mountain range is a popular destination for hiking and rock climbing.

  • Parque Urbano de Albarquel - A charming urban park located along the Sado River, popular for its walking trails, picnic spots, and children’s playground.

  • Praia de Galapos - This secluded beach nestled between the cliffs boasts crystal clear waters and lush greenery, making it a perfect spot for sunbathing and swimming.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Setúbal

Setúbal is a paradise for outdoor enthusiasts. Whether hiking or biking, the city has something for every level of adventurer.

  • Arrábida Route - A challenging hiking route that takes you along the scenic coastline of the Arrábida Nature Reserve.

  • Rota das Praias Selvagens - A coastal hike that takes you through Setúbal’s wildest beaches

  • Trilho da Pedra da Anixa - A moderate difficulty hiking trail that offers breathtaking views of the region’s unique flora and fauna.

  • Rota Vicentina - This long-distance hiking trail starts in Setúbal and goes along the southwest coast of Portugal, offering breathtaking views and varying degrees of difficulty.

Best Things to Do in Setúbal: Activities and Tours

  • Boat Tour - Take a boat tour along the Sado River and marvel at the dolphins, or visit the Arrábida Nature Park and relax on one of the most beautiful beaches in Portugal, Praia do Creiro.

  • Wine Tasting Tour - Explore the vineyards and wineries of the Setúbal region and taste some of the best wines in Portugal, such as Muscatel and Moscatel de Setúbal.

  • Food Tour - Discover Setúbal’s gastronomy scene and taste some of the traditional dishes such as choco frito (fried cuttlefish), grilled sardines, and arroz de tamboril (monkfish rice).

  • Scuba Diving - Experience the underwater world of the Sado Estuary and admire the local fauna, such as octopuses, seahorses, and colorful fish.

Week-Long Itinerary for Setúbal

Day 1-2: Arrive in Setúbal and visit the Monastery of Jesus, Casa da Cultura and the Setúbal Archaeological Museum.

Day 3-4: Explore the Arrábida Natural Park, and relax on one of Setúbal’s stunning beaches, such as Praia de Galapos.

Day 5: Take a wine tasting tour, and taste some of Portugal’s best wines.

Day 6: Visit the Castelo de São Filipe and enjoy the panoramic views of Setúbal and the Sado River.

Day 7: Take a boat tour around the Sado Estuary and visit the local marine reserve, home to a resident dolphin family.

Food and Drink Scene of Setúbal

Two of Setúbal’s must-try dishes are:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Choco Frito - This traditional dish consists of fried cuttlefish served with white rice and beans.

  • Arroz de Tamboril - A savory rice dish made with monkfish, clams, and vegetables.

Local Beverages

  • Moscatel de Setúbal - A sweet fortified wine famous throughout Portugal, particularly popular in Setúbal.

  • Ginjinha - A cherry liqueur usually drunk as an aperitif or after-dinner digestif.

Where to Stay in Setúbal: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Setúbal has accommodation options for all budgets. Here are some places to consider:

  • Arrábida Resort & Golf Academy - A luxurious hotel with a beautiful golf course, spa, and wellness center.

  • Luna Esperança Centro Hotel - A cozy and affordable hotel located in the heart of Setúbal, close to the city’s major attractions.

  • Orange Terrace Hostel - A comfortable and stylish hostel located in the old town of Setúbal, ideal for budget-conscious travelers.

What to Buy in Setúbal: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Muscatel de Setúbal - Bring a bottle of Portugal’s famous wine home as a souvenir.

  • Olive Oil - Estremadura is famous for its high-quality olive oil.

  • Azulejos - The traditional blue-and-white tiles that adorn the walls of many buildings in Portugal and make for unique wall hangings or tableware.

Local Culture and Customs of Setúbal

Setúbal is a friendly and welcoming city, here are a few customs and traditions to keep in mind:

  • When dining, it is customary to have a soup course, followed by a main course, and then dessert.

  • Portuguese are known for being late so, if you have plans with someone, don’t be surprised if they arrive a bit late.

  • Portuguese people love to drink coffee, and it is normal to have a coffee after every meal.

Best Time to Visit Setúbal

The best time to visit Setúbal is between May and September. During these months, Setúbal boasts warm temperatures, perfect for outdoor activities and water sports.

Do’s and Don’ts of Setúbal

Do:

  • Try the local food and drink specialties of the Setúbal region.

  • Carry cash, as many shops and restaurants do not accept credit cards.

  • Learn some basic Portuguese phrases, such as ‘bom dia’ (good morning), ‘boa tarde’ (good afternoon) and ‘obrigado’ (thank you).

Don’t:

  • Don’t forget to tip in restaurants. A tip of 10% is customary in Setúbal.

  • Don’t disrespect the local culture or customs.

  • Don’t forget to carry sunscreen, sunglasses, and a hat, as the sun can be strong during the day.

Safety and Health

Setúbal is, for the most part, a safe destination for travelers. However, it is always a good idea to be aware of your surroundings and take basic safety precautions.

Safety in Setúbal

  • Keep money and valuables in a secure place and avoid carrying large amounts of cash when going out.

  • Be careful when walking alone at night, especially in less busy areas of the city.

  • Always be aware of pickpockets and street scams.

Health in Setúbal

  • Tap water in Setúbal is safe to drink, but It is advisable to drink bottled water.

  • Portugal has a public health system accessible to all travelers. Private medical facilities are also available.

Getting to Setúbal: Transportation Options and Logistics

Setúbal is easily accessible from nearby cities and airports.

  • Lisbon Airport is 30 minutes away from the city by car or taxi. Several car rental companies are available at the airport.

  • Setúbal has a bus station with several daily buses from Lisbon and other cities.

  • Trains depart hourly from Lisbon’s Oriente and Entrecampos stations and arrive at Setúbal’s train station in just over an hour.

Airports Near Setúbal

  • Lisbon Airport, 30 minutes away from Setúbal.

  • Faro Airport, 2.5 hours by car from Setúbal.

Bus Stations Near Setúbal

  • Setúbal Bus Station, located in the city center.

Other Options

  • Renting a car is a good option for exploring the surrounding countryside.

  • Taxis are easy to find and relatively inexpensive.

How to Get Around in Setúbal

Setúbal is a walkable city and to get around in the city; it is best to do it on foot.

  • Buses are also available for getting to further areas of the city.

  • Taxis are easy to find, with several stands throughout the city.

Where to Go Next from Setúbal: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Setúbal is located in the region of Estremadura, home to numerous attractions and scenic towns.

  • Lisbon - Portugal’s capital city, is just one hour away from Setúbal and is well worth a visit.

  • Palmela - A medieval town situated on a hilltop with its impressive castle.

  • Sintra - A UNESCO World Heritage Site famous for its stunning palaces and gardens.

  • Ericeira - A fishing village with charming streets, great beaches, and impressive surfing spots.

Conclusion

Setúbal is a picturesque destination with a rich history and culture, stunning natural scenery, and a delicious culinary scene. Whether you’re exploring the Arrábida Natural Park, taking a wine tasting tour, or relaxing on a beautiful beach, there is something for everyone in this charming Portuguese city. Plan your trip today and discover the beauty of Setúbal!
