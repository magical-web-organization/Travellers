Introduction to Seixal

Located in the Setúbal Peninsula, in Estremadura, in Tagus Valley, in Portugal, in Iberia, in Europe, Seixal is a quaint town with a rich history and culture. It is located on the southern bank of the Tagus River and offers scenic views of Lisbon.

Seixal is a popular destination among tourists, both due to its beautiful beaches and its proximity to Lisbon. Whether you’re interested in exploring the cultural heritage of Portugal, hiking through its beautiful countryside, or relaxing on the beach, Seixal has something for everyone. Here is a comprehensive guide to help you plan your visit to Seixal.

List of Places to Visit in Seixal

Here are the top places to visit in Seixal:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Seixal Museum - The Seixal Museum has one of the richest collections of contemporary art in Portugal. It is housed in a beautiful 15th-century building and features works by local and international artists.

  • Alambique de Ouro Museum - This museum is dedicated to the traditional production of “aguardente” (sugar cane liquor) and “pão de rala” (Portuguese almond sweets). It is a great way to learn about the cultural traditions of the area.

  • Castelo de Sesimbra - This castle was built in 1165 and played a significant role in the defence of the region. While the castle itself is in ruins, it is still a great place to visit for the beautiful views of the coast.

Natural Attractions of Seixal: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Sesimbra Beach - Also known as Praia de Califórnia, Sesimbra Beach is the most popular beach in Seixal. It is known for its clear water and golden sand and is great for swimming, sunbathing, and water sports.

  • Arrábida Natural Park - The Arrábida Natural Park is known for its beautiful beaches, breathtaking views of the coast, and rich marine life. It is great for hiking, birdwatching, and exploring the flora.

  • Figueirinha Beach - Figueirinha Beach is located just outside Seixal and has crystal clear water and white sand. It is ideal for swimming, sunbathing, and water sports.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Seixal

Seixal is known for its natural beauty, and there are many hiking and biking trails for travellers to explore. Some of the most popular trails include:

  • Rota Vicentina - This hiking trail covers over 400km of trails along the coast of the Alentejo and Algarve regions of Portugal.

  • Serra da Arrábida - The Serra da Arrábida is a mountain range located east of Setúbal. It is known for its beautiful views of the coast and is a great place for hiking and mountain biking.

Best Things to Do in Seixal: Activities and Tours

There are plenty of things to do in Seixal, including:

  • Boat Tours - Take a boat tour of the Tagus River and marvel at the beautiful views of Lisbon.

  • Wine Tasting Tours - Explore the many local wineries in the area and sample some of the best wine that Portugal has to offer.

  • Surfing - Seixal has some of the best surfing spots in Portugal, and there are plenty of surfing schools for beginners to learn the sport.

Week-Long Itinerary for Seixal

Here’s a sample itinerary for a week-long visit to Seixal:

Day 1: Explore the cultural heritage of Seixal by visiting the Seixal Museum, Alambique de Ouro Museum, and Castelo de Sesimbra.

Day 2: Take a boat tour of the Tagus River and explore the nearby coast.

Day 3: Visit Sesimbra Beach and enjoy a day of swimming, sunbathing, and water sports.

Day 4: Visit Figueirinha Beach and check out the local wineries.

Day 5: Take a hike through the Arrábida Natural Park and explore the beautiful flora and fauna.

Day 6: Go surfing in one of the many surf spots in Seixal.

Day 7: Visit Lisbon and explore the vibrant capital city of Portugal.

Food and Drink Scene of Seixal

Seixal has a rich culinary culture, and visitors can enjoy some of the local dishes and drinks. Here are some of the must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Bacalhau à Gomes de Sá - This is a Portuguese dish made with salted cod, onions, garlic, potatoes, and eggs.

  • Açorda de Marisco - This is a famous seafood dish made with bread, garlic, coriander, and shrimp.

  • Arroz de Tamboril - This is a rice dish made with monkfish, onions, tomatoes, and red pepper.

Local Beverages

  • Ginjinha - This is a cherry liqueur that is popular in Portugal.

  • Port Wine - This is a fortified wine that is made in the Douro Valley and is a must-try for wine lovers.

Where to Stay in Seixal: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

There are many great places to stay in Seixal, ranging from budget-friendly hostels to luxurious hotels. Here are some of the most popular options:

  • Hotel Quinta dos Moinhos de Sa - This is a charming hotel located in the Sesimbra countryside, featuring a beautiful garden and an outdoor pool.

  • Sesimbra Hotel and Spa - This is a luxurious hotel with beautiful views of the coast and a spa.

  • Sesimbra Hotel & Spa is a hotel located centrally in the town with access to the beach and local attractions.

What to Buy in Seixal: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

There are plenty of local products to bring back home as souvenirs from Seixal:

  • Cork products - Portugal is the largest producer of cork products in the world, making this a great souvenir to take back home.

  • Ceramic products - Portugal is also famous for its ceramics, with many beautiful and unique pieces available in Seixal.

  • Wine - Portugal is known for its excellent wine, and there are plenty of local wineries in the area.

Local Culture and Customs of Seixal

Portugal is a country with a rich culture and history, and Seixal is no exception. Some local customs you might encounter in Seixal include:

  • Greetings - Portuguese people are usually very warm and friendly, so expect to be greeted with a smile and a handshake.

  • Dress code - Dress is usually casual in Seixal, but it’s important to dress appropriately when visiting religious sites.

Best Time to Visit Seixal

The best time to visit Seixal is during the summer months of June to August, when the weather is warm and sunny. However, this is also the peak tourist season, so expect the region to be crowded.

Do’s and Don’ts of Seixal

Here are some Do’s and Don’ts to keep in mind when visiting Seixal:

Do:

  • Try the local food and drink.

  • Take a boat tour of the Tagus River.

  • Explore the local wineries.

Don’t:

  • Leave valuables unattended.

  • Ignore local customs and etiquette when visiting religious sites.

  • Litter or damage the environment.

Safety and Health

Safety in Seixal

Seixal is generally a safe place to visit, but it’s always important to take precautions to protect your belongings and stay safe. Some tips to keep in mind include:

  • Don’t leave valuables unattended.

  • Stay in well-lit areas at night.

  • Be aware of your surroundings.

Health in Seixal

Visitors to Seixal should carry travel insurance and ensure that they have all the necessary vaccinations before travelling. The Portuguese healthcare system is generally very good, but it’s always important to have insurance in case of emergency.

Getting to Seixal: Transportation Options and Logistics

Seixal is easily accessible by various modes of transport, including:

Airports Near Seixal

  • Lisbon Portela Airport is the closest airport to Seixal and is roughly 30 minutes away by car.

  • Faro Airport in the Algarve region is another option.

Bus Stations Near Seixal

  • Setúbal Bus Station is the closest bus station to Seixal and is about a 15-minute drive away.

Other Options

  • You can also take a train from Lisbon to Setúbal and then take a bus to Seixal.

How to Get Around in Seixal

There are several transportation options in Seixal, including:

  • Bus - There are several bus routes throughout Seixal, making it easy to get around.

  • Taxi - Taxis are readily available throughout Seixal, but prices can be steep.

Where to Go Next from Seixal: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

There are plenty of nearby destinations and excursions to consider after visiting Seixal, including:

  • Lisbon - Lisbon is the vibrant capital of Portugal and is known for its history, culture, and vibrant nightlife.

  • Sintra - Sintra is a beautiful town located just outside of Lisbon and is known for its fairy-tale castles and beautiful palaces.

Conclusion

Seixal offers visitors a unique opportunity to explore the rich culture, natural beauty, and outdoor adventure of Portugal. With its beautiful beaches, picturesque countryside, and charming hospitality, Seixal is a must-visit destination for anyone looking to explore Portugal.
