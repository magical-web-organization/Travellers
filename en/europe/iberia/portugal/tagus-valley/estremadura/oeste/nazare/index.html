Nazaré Travel Guide - Explore the Best of Nazaré

Located in Oeste, in Estremadura, in Tagus Valley, in Portugal, in Iberia, in Europe, Nazaré is a charming coastal town that attracts travelers from all over the world. With its picturesque beaches, rich history, and vibrant culture, Nazaré offers an unforgettable experience to its visitors. In this detailed guide, we’ll take you through all the must-see attractions, best things to do, where to stay, and more in Nazaré.

List of Places to Visit in Nazaré

Nazaré has something for everyone, from history buffs to adventure seekers. Here are the top places to visit in Nazaré:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Forte de São Miguel Arcanjo: A 16th-century fortress located on the beach with stunning views and history.
  • Nazaré Museum Dr. Joaquim Manso: An art and culture museum dedicated to the works of Dr. Joaquim Manso, a renowned writer and painter from Nazaré.
  • Igreja de Nossa Senhora da Nazaré: A beautiful church with an impressive interior, dedicated to the patron saint of Nazaré.
  • Praça Sousa Oliveira: A bustling square with bars, restaurants, and shops, perfect for a leisurely stroll.

Natural Attractions of Nazaré: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Praia do Norte: Famous for its giant waves, Praia do Norte is a must-visit spot for surfers and adrenaline junkies.
  • Miradouro do Suberco: A viewpoint that offers panoramic views of the ocean and Nazaré beach.
  • Sítio: A historic part of Nazaré with narrow streets, picturesque houses, and stunning views of the town and sea.
  • Parque Natural das Serras de Aire e Candeeiros: A national park with rugged limestone cliffs, underground caves, and hiking trails.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Nazaré

Nazaré is a great destination for hiking and biking enthusiasts. The town and its surroundings offer several trails through stunning natural landscapes. Some of the best hiking/biking routes in Nazaré are:

  • Nazaré to Sítio Circuit: A scenic 2-mile circuit that takes you from Nazaré beach to Sítio through narrow streets and stairways.
  • Fórnea Trail: A moderate 5-mile trail that takes you through the natural park of Serras de Aire e Candeeiros.
  • Rota dos Moinhos: A 7.5-mile trail that takes you through old windmills and rural landscapes.

Best Things to Do in Nazaré: Activities and Tours

Whether you’re seeking adventure or relaxation, Nazaré has something for everyone. Here are some of the best things to do in Nazaré that you shouldn’t miss:

  • Surfing lessons: Nazaré is known for its giant waves, and surfers from all over the world come here to surf.
  • Dolphin watching: Book a boat tour and witness the natural beauty of Nazaré’s coastline, including the chance to spot dolphins.
  • Traditional fishing experience: Take a guided tour with local fishermen and learn about traditional fishing techniques.
  • Wine tasting: Taste the best wines of Portugal on a wine-tasting tour in Nazaré.

Week-Long Itinerary for Nazaré

To make the most of your trip to Nazaré, here’s a one-week itinerary that covers all the essential attractions and experiences of the town.

Day 1: Arrival and relax at Praia do Norte. Day 2: Visit the Forte de São Miguel Arcanjo and Nazaré Museum Dr. Joaquim Manso followed by Praça Sousa Oliveira in the evening. Day 3: Hike the Nazaré to Sítio Circuit and explore the panoramic views of the town. Day 4: Take a surfing lesson and visit Praia do Norte again in the evening. Day 5: Visit Igreja de Nossa Senhora da Nazaré followed by Miradouro do Suberco for sunset views. Day 6: Take a boat tour to spot dolphins and enjoy local seafood by the beach. Day 7: End the trip with a wine-tasting tour in nearby wineries.

Food and Drink Scene of Nazaré

Nazaré is a paradise for food lovers, with its fresh seafood and traditional dishes. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages in Nazaré:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Caldeirada: A seafood stew featuring a variety of fish, potatoes, and vegetables in a tomato and white wine broth.
  • Arroz de Marisco: A savory seafood rice dish.
  • Bacalhau à Lagareiro: A roasted codfish dish typically served with potatoes, garlic, and olive oil.

Local Beverages

  • Ginjinha: A sweet cherry liqueur often served as an aperitif or digestive.
  • Vinho Verde: A crisp white wine, perfect to pair with seafood.

Where to Stay in Nazaré: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Nazaré has a wide range of accommodation options, from luxurious hotels to budget-friendly hostels. Here are some of the best options to consider:

  • Hotel Miramar Sul: A luxurious hotel with sea views, an outdoor pool, and a spa.
  • Pousada de Juventude de Nazaré: A budget-friendly hostel located in the town center.
  • Hotel Mar Bravo: A mid-range hotel with sea views and a sun terrace.

What to Buy in Nazaré: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

You’ll find plenty of souvenirs and local products to bring home from Nazaré. Some of the best things to buy are:

  • Nazaré Beachwear: Shop for beachwear and swimsuits in local stores near the beach.
  • Pottery: Find unique pottery and ceramics in local markets and shops.
  • Wine: Take home a bottle of the finest Vinho Verde from local wineries.

Local Culture and Customs of Nazaré

Nazaré is known for its rich cultural heritage and traditions, including:

  • Fishermen’s traditions: Fishing is an integral part of Nazaré’s culture, and you’ll still find fishermen in traditional dress, selling their catches in the town’s markets.
  • Folklore: Nazaré has a long history of folklore, and you can still see folkloric dancers and musicians performing at festivals and events.
  • Religious traditions: As a religiously diverse town, Nazaré celebrates several festivals throughout the year, such as the Feast of Our Lady of Nazaré in September.

Best Time to Visit Nazaré

The best time to visit Nazaré is during the summer months, from June to August. The weather is warm and sunny, and the town is bustling with tourists. However, the waves at this time of year can be calmer, so if you’re looking to surf, visit between October and March when the waves are at their biggest.

Do’s and Don’ts of Nazaré

Do:

  • Respect local traditions and customs, especially around religious events.
  • Try local seafood and wine.
  • Be aware of the dangers of surfing and swimming in the ocean.

Don’t:

  • Wear revealing clothing in religious areas.
  • Ignore the warnings of lifeguards when surfing or swimming in the ocean.
  • Litter on the beach or streets.

Safety and Health

Safety in Nazaré

Nazaré is considered a safe town for tourists, but it’s always important to take the necessary safety precautions, such as:

  • Avoid walking alone at night.
  • Keep an eye on your belongings in crowded areas.
  • Beware of the strong ocean currents and wear a lifejacket when swimming or surfing.

Health in Nazaré

Nazaré has several pharmacies and hospitals in case of a medical emergency. Additionally, tap water in Nazaré is safe to drink.

Getting to Nazaré: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Nazaré

The closest airport to Nazaré is Lisbon Airport, approximately 1 hour and 30 minutes away by car.

Bus Stations Near Nazaré

Nazaré has a central bus station with several daily buses to Lisbon, Porto, and other nearby cities.

Other Options

Taking a taxi or renting a car is also a viable option for getting around, especially if you want to explore nearby attractions.

How to Get Around in Nazaré

Walking or biking is the best way to get around Nazaré, especially for short distances. Taxis and buses are also readily available, and renting a car is a great option if you want to explore the surrounding areas.

Where to Go Next from Nazaré: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

There are plenty of nearby destinations and excursions worth visiting from Nazaré, including:

  • Coimbra: A historic city located approximately 2 hours away from Nazaré, known for its art, culture, and university.
  • Óbidos: A charming medieval town located approximately 45 minutes away from Nazaré, with stunning architecture and shops.
  • Fátima: A famous pilgrimage site located approximately 1 hour and 30 minutes away from Nazaré, famous for its shrine and religious events.

Conclusion

Nazaré is a hidden gem on the Portuguese coast, with its stunning natural landscapes, rich culture, and delicious seafood. Whether you’re looking to surf, explore history and culture, or simply relax on the beach, Nazaré has something for everyone. Follow this comprehensive guide to plan your trip to Nazaré and explore all that this charming town has to offer.
