Introduction to Belém

Belém is an enchanting neighborhood located in western Lisbon, in the Greater Lisbon region of Estremadura. It sits on the bank of the Tagus River, where ships set sail on their voyages of discovery in the Age of Exploration. Today, Belém is a bustling yet laid-back district that has become one of Lisbon’s top tourist destinations.

Belém is a perfect place to explore charming streets lined with trendy cafes, cozy restaurants, and craft shops. It is famous for its cultural attractions, natural wonders, and excellent food and drink scene. Whether you are interested in history, architecture, outdoor activities, or just relaxing by the riverside, Belém will not disappoint.

List of Places to Visit in Belém

Belém is home to some of the most iconic landmarks and attractions in Lisbon. Here are the top places to visit:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Jerónimos Monastery: A UNESCO World Heritage Site that dates back to the 16th century and showcases stunning Manueline architecture.
  • Belém Tower: Another World Heritage Site and one of the most recognizable symbols of Lisbon, built in the early 16th century.
  • Discoveries Monument: A monumental sculpture that honors the Portuguese explorers, located by the riverbank near Belém Tower.
  • National Coach Museum: A fascinating museum housed in the former imperial riding school, which features an extensive collection of royal coaches, carriages, and vehicles.
  • Berardo Museum of Modern and Contemporary Art: A world-class museum that houses a vast collection of contemporary art from around the world.

Natural Attractions of Belém: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Belém Palace Gardens: A vast, well-manicured park that boasts a variety of trees, plants, and fountains. It is a perfect spot for picnics, walks, or just enjoying the outdoors.
  • Ajuda Botanical Garden: A lush and shady garden that hosts a broad variety of plants and trees from all over the world. It is a peaceful retreat from the bustle of the city.
  • Tagus Riverfront: Enjoy a stroll along the scenic riverside promenade and take in the views of the water and the iconic monuments.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Belém

Belém offers excellent opportunities for hiking and biking. Here are some of the best routes:

  • Monsanto Forest Park: A vast green space with over 10,000 acres of hiking and biking trails that feature stunning views of the city and the river.
  • Lisbon Greenway: A scenic route that passes through forests, farms, and vineyards, connecting Lisbon and the historic town of Sintra.
  • Ecovia do Litoral: A coastal route that stretches over 200 km from Cascais to Santiago do Cacém, featuring breathtaking views of the Atlantic Ocean and the coastal cliffs.

Best Things to Do in Belém: Activities and Tours

Belém offers a wide range of activities and tours that cater to all interests. Here are some of the top choices:

  • Food and Wine Tasting Tour: Sample some of the best food and wine that Belém has to offer, from traditional Portuguese dishes to sophisticated cuisine.
  • Belém Sightseeing Tour: Take a guided tour of the neighborhood and discover its fascinating history, art, and architecture.
  • Kayaking or Paddleboarding on the Tagus River: Enjoy a unique perspective of Belém and Lisbon’s waterfront from a kayak or paddleboard.
  • Lisbon Bike Tour: Explore Lisbon and Belém on two wheels, covering famous landmarks, neighborhoods, and hidden gems.

Food and Drink Scene of Belém

Belém is a food paradise, offering a variety of traditional and modern dishes that cater to all tastes. Here are some of the top dishes and drinks to try:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Pastel de Belém (Belém Pastry): A traditional pastry made of egg custard, crispy pastry, and sprinkled with cinnamon and powdered sugar.
  • Bacalhau à Brás (Codfish and Eggs): A classic Portuguese dish made of flaked cod, fried potatoes, onions, eggs, and parsley.
  • Cozido à Portuguesa (Portuguese Stew): A hearty stew made of meats, sausages, vegetables, and beans.

Local Beverages

  • Vinho Verde: A young and fruity white wine that pairs well with seafood and salads.
  • Ginjinha: A cherry liqueur that is traditionally served in a small chocolate cup.
  • Coffee: Portugal is famous for its strong and flavorful coffee, which can be enjoyed in various ways, from an espresso shot to a creamy cappuccino.

Where to Stay in Belém: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Belém has plenty of accommodation options for travelers, ranging from affordable hostels to luxury hotels. Here are some of the top choices:

  • Palácio do Governador: A 5-star hotel located in a former palace, featuring elegant rooms, a spa, and a pool.
  • Jerónimos 8: A modern and stylish hotel located steps away from Jerónimos Monastery, offering comfortable rooms and a rooftop bar.
  • Lisbon Marriott Hotel: A family-friendly hotel with spacious rooms, an outdoor pool, and a restaurant.

What to Buy in Belém: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Belém offers a wide range of souvenirs and local products that make perfect gifts or mementos. Here are some of the top choices:

  • Hand-painted tiles: Lisbon is famous for its beautiful and colorful tiles, which can be found in many craft shops and street vendors.
  • Port wine: A sweet and fortified wine that is produced in the Douro Valley, northeast of Lisbon.
  • Handicrafts: From cork products to embroidered linen, there are many unique and beautiful handicrafts that can be found in Belém and Lisbon.

Local Culture and Customs of Belém

Belém, like the rest of Portugal, values family, friends, and social gatherings. Portuguese people are friendly and hospitable, and they appreciate good food, wine, and music. Here are some customs and traditions to keep in mind:

  • Respect the elders: Portuguese culture values respect for the elderly, so be polite and courteous to older people you encounter.
  • Dress appropriately: When visiting churches, monasteries, or museums, avoid wearing shorts, tank tops, or flip-flops.
  • Say “obrigado/a”: Portuguese people appreciate politeness, so always say “thank you” (obrigado for men, obrigada for women) when you receive something or someone helps you out.

Best Time to Visit Belém

The best time to visit Belém is from April to June or from September to October, when temperatures are mild, and the crowds are not overwhelming. Summer months (July and August) can be hot and crowded, while winter months (November to March) can be rainy and chilly. However, Lisbon and Belém are relatively mild year-round, so you can plan your trip according to your preferences and interests.

Do’s and Don’ts of Belém

Here are some things to do and not do when visiting Belém:

Do:

  • Try new foods and drinks: Belém offers a wide range of culinary delights that you won’t find back home.
  • Explore beyond the tourist hotspots: Belém has many hidden corners and alleyways that are worth exploring.
  • Take public transportation: Lisbon’s public transportation system is efficient and affordable, so take advantage of it.

Don’t:

  • Litter: Keep Belém and Lisbon clean by disposing of your trash properly.
  • Over-tip: It is customary to leave a tip of 5-10% in restaurants, but excessive tipping may be considered rude.
  • Forget your sunscreen: Lisbon and Belém can be hot and sunny, so protect your skin by wearing sunscreen.

Safety and Health

Belém is generally safe and secure, but like any big city, it’s important to take precautions. Here are some safety and health tips to keep in mind:

Safety in Belém

  • Be aware of your surroundings: Keep an eye on your belongings and avoid dark or deserted streets at night.
  • Watch out for pickpockets: Lisbon is known for petty thefts, so be mindful of your wallet, phone, and other valuables.
  • Follow traffic rules: Lisbon can be busy and chaotic, so always look both ways when crossing the street and use pedestrian crossings.

Health in Belém

  • Stay hydrated: Lisbon can be hot and dry, so drink plenty of fluids, especially water.
  • Protect yourself from the sun: Wear sunscreen, sunglasses, and a hat to avoid sunburns and heatstroke.
  • Seek medical help if needed: Lisbon has excellent medical facilities, but keep your health insurance card and emergency numbers handy just in case.

Getting to Belém: Transportation Options and Logistics

Belém is easily accessible by various modes of transportation. Here are some of the options:

Airports Near Belém

  • Lisbon Airport (LIS): Belém is only a short drive or taxi ride (20-30 minutes) from the airport, which is served by many international and domestic airlines.

Bus Stations Near Belém

  • Belém Station: Belém has a train station that connects to other parts of Lisbon and Portugal.

Other Options

  • Metro: The Lisbon Metro is a fast and efficient way to get around the city and includes a stop in Belém.
  • Tram: Lisbon’s famous Tram 15 passes through Belém and goes all the way to the city center.

How to Get Around in Belém

Belém is a walkable neighborhood, but there are also several transportation options available:

  • Tram 15: A classic yellow tram that connects Belém to the city center and popular landmarks.
  • Bus: Several bus lines pass through Belém, connecting to other neighborhoods and suburbs.
  • Tuk-Tuk: A fun and quirky way to see Belém and Lisbon’s sights, tuk-tuks can be found in many tourist areas.
  • Bike: Rent a bike and explore Belém and Lisbon on two wheels.

Where to Go Next from Belém: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Belém is an excellent starting point for further adventures in Portugal and beyond. Here are some nearby destinations to consider:

  • Sintra: A UNESCO World Heritage Site famous for its palaces, castles, and gardens.
  • Cascais: A picturesque seaside town known for its sandy beaches and gourmet restaurants.
  • Óbidos: A medieval walled town with charming narrow streets and a castle.
  • Madeira: A lush and verdant island in the Atlantic Ocean, famous for its beautiful landscapes and wines.

Conclusion

Belém is a charming and vibrant neighborhood of Lisbon that has something for everyone. From cultural attractions and natural wonders to delicious food and drink, Belém offers a unique and unforgettable experience to visitors. Whether you are interested in history, art, adventure, or relaxation, Belém is sure to captivate your heart and soul. Plan your trip to Belém today and see for yourself what this gem of Lisbon has to offer.
