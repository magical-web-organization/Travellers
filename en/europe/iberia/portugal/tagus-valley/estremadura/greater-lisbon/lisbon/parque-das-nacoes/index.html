Introduction to Parque das Nações

Parque das Nações, also known as the Park of Nations, is a modern district in Lisbon built for the 1998 World Exposition. It is located on the Tagus River and offers a range of attractions, from museums to outdoor activities, and an exquisite culinary scene. It is a great destination for families, couples, and solo travelers who want to experience the futuristic side of Lisbon.

List of Places to Visit in Parque das Nações

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Oceanário de Lisboa – the second-largest aquarium in Europe, home to over 8,000 marine creatures and several ecosystems.
  • Pavilhão do Conhecimento – an interactive science museum with over 250 exhibits related to technology, math, and physics.
  • Museu do Oriente – showcases the influence of Asian cultures in Portugal through its exhibitions.

Natural Attractions of Parque das Nações: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Jardins Garcia da Horta – a peaceful and shaded garden with manicured lawns, palm trees, and fountains.
  • Cabeço das Rolas – a scenic viewpoint located on a hill with panoramic views of the river and surroundings.
  • Tejo Estuary Nature Reserve – a protected area of 14,000 hectares at the confluence of the Tagus River and the Atlantic Ocean, home to diverse flora and fauna.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Parque das Nações

  • Parque Tejo Trail – a scenic 5 km trail that runs along the Tagus River, connecting Parque das Nações to the city center.
  • Parque das Nações Loop Trail – a 3 km loop trail that passes through gardens, viewing platforms, and the Tejo Estuary Nature Reserve.
  • Parque das Nações Bike Ride – a 12 km bike ride that follows the riverfront and connects to other bike-friendly routes in the city.

Best Things to Do in Parque das Nações: Activities and Tours

  • Kayak Tours – explore the Tagus River and its hidden coves by kayak with a local guide.
  • Vasco da Gama Tower Climb – climb over 140 meters to reach the observation deck of this iconic tower built for the World Exposition.
  • Go Karting – race with friends or family on an indoor track with electric go-karts.

Week-Long Itinerary for Parque das Nações

Food and Drink Scene of Parque das Nações

Must-Try Dishes

  • Bacalhau com natas – a creamy codfish dish often served with potatoes and onions.
  • Francesinha – a sandwich filled with cured ham, sausage, steak, and smothered in cheese and sauce.
  • Pastel de Nata – a traditional Portuguese custard tart with a crispy crust and creamy filling.

Local Beverages

  • Port Wine – a fortified wine from the Douro Valley, usually served as a digestif.
  • Ginjinha – a cherry liqueur often served in a chocolate cup.
  • Sagres Beer – a refreshing beer with a light and crisp taste.

Where to Stay in Parque das Nações: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Myriad by SANA Hotels – a luxurious 5-star hotel located near the riverfront with a panoramic pool and spa.
  • Olissippo Oriente Hotel – a modern and comfortable 4-star hotel with river views and a rooftop bar.
  • Lisbon Destination Hostel – a stylish and affordable hostel offering private and shared rooms with a trendy bar and restaurant.

What to Buy in Parque das Nações: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Azulejos Tiles – handcrafted tiles with traditional motifs and colors.
  • Cork Products – bags, wallets, and accessories made from eco-friendly cork.
  • Portuguese Preserves – flavorful jams, chutneys, and condiments made from local fruits and vegetables.

Local Culture and Customs of Parque das Nações

  • Siesta – it’s common for shops, restaurants, and museums to close for a few hours in the afternoon for a siesta.
  • Tipping – it’s customary to leave a 10% tip in restaurants and cafes.
  • Nightlife – the local bars and clubs stay open until the early hours of the morning.

Best Time to Visit Parque das Nações

The best time to visit Parque das Nações is during the spring or fall when the weather is mild, and there are fewer crowds. The summer months can be hot and crowded, and the winter can be rainy and chilly.

Do’s and Don’ts of Parque das Nações

Do:

  • Do try the local cuisine and beverages.
  • Do visit the museums and cultural attractions.
  • Do take a boat ride on the Tagus River.
  • Do try new outdoor activities and tours.

Don’t:

  • Don’t litter in the public areas.
  • Don’t leave your valuables unattended.
  • Don’t ignore the local customs and culture.

Safety and Health

Safety in Parque das Nações

Parque das Nações is a relatively safe neighborhood, but tourists are advised to be cautious of pickpockets in crowded areas and to avoid walking alone in deserted areas at night.

Health in Parque das Nações

There are no major health concerns in Parque das Nações, but it’s always a good idea to have travel insurance and to check with a doctor before traveling.

Getting to Parque das Nações: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Parque das Nações

  • Lisbon Portela Airport – located 4 km away from Parque das Nações, with daily domestic and international flights.

Bus Stations Near Parque das Nações

  • Gare do Oriente Bus Station – located within walking distance of many Parque das Nações attractions, with regional and national bus routes.

Other Options

  • Metro – the nearest metro station is Gare do Oriente, which connects to the city center and other neighborhoods.
  • Train – the Oriente train station offers regional and national routes.

How to Get Around in Parque das Nações

There are several options for getting around Parque das Nações, including walking, cycling, and using public transportation. Buses, metro, and taxis are readily available, and there are several bike rental companies in the area.

Where to Go Next from Parque das Nações: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Parque das Nações is well-connected to other neighborhoods and cities, making it a great starting point for day trips and excursions. Some nearby destinations worth visiting include:

  • Belém – a historic neighborhood known for its iconic monuments, such as the Jerónimos Monastery and the Belem Tower.
  • Sintra – a fairy tale town with palaces, castles, and scenic natural surroundings.
  • Cascais – a charming coastal town with beaches, restaurants, and a lively atmosphere.

Conclusion

Parque das Nações has something for every type of traveler, from cultural attractions to outdoor activities, and a top-notch food and beverage scene. Its modern and futuristic architecture, combined with the stunning views of the Tagus River, make it a must-visit destination in Lisbon. It’s an ideal destination for those who want to experience the contemporary side of Lisbon while still enjoying the local customs and traditions.
