Introduction to Cascais

Located in the Greater Lisbon region of Portugal, Cascais is a charming coastal town nestled between the Sintra Mountains and the Atlantic Ocean. This picturesque town is a popular tourist destination due to its stunning beaches, vibrant nightlife, and historical landmarks.

List of Places to Visit in Cascais

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  1. Museum of the Sea: This museum showcases the maritime history of Cascais and Portugal, featuring exhibits on fishing, navigation, and boatbuilding.
  2. Cascais Citadel: Built in the 17th century, this historic fortress was used to defend the town against pirates and foreign invaders. It now houses the Municipal Museum of Cascais and the Cascais Tourism Office.
  3. Casa das Histórias Paula Rego: This museum is dedicated to the works of the Portuguese artist, Paula Rego, and showcases a range of her paintings, drawings, and prints.

Natural Attractions of Cascais: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  1. Guincho Beach: This secluded beach is popular with surfers and windsurfers due to its strong waves and wind.
  2. Cabo da Roca: This westernmost point of mainland Europe offers breathtaking views of the Atlantic Ocean.
  3. Parque Natural de Sintra-Cascais: This nature reserve encompasses the Sintra Mountains and the coastline, with diverse flora and fauna and numerous walking trails.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Cascais

  1. Rota Vicentina: This hiking trail stretches over 400 km along Portugal’s southwestern coast, including a section that passes through Cascais.
  2. Serra de Sintra Trails: These walking trails offer stunning views of the Sintra Mountains and the coastline, with varying levels of difficulty.
  3. Cascais EcoTrail: This circular walking trail offers a scenic walk through the town and surrounding countryside.

Best Things to Do in Cascais: Activities and Tours

  1. Surfing Lessons: Take a surfing lesson on the waves of Guincho Beach with a professional instructor.
  2. Wine Tasting: Discover Portugal’s renowned wine culture with a wine tasting tour in one of Cascais’ many vineyards.
  3. Boat Tours: Explore the coastline and nearby caves with a scenic boat tour departing from Cascais Marina.

Week-Long Itinerary for Cascais

  • Day 1: Explore the historic district of Cascais, including the Citadel and the Municipal Museum.
  • Day 2: Visit nearby Sintra, with its iconic palaces and castles.
  • Day 3: Take a day trip to Lisbon, the capital of Portugal.
  • Day 4: Relax on the beaches of Cascais, including the popular Praia do Tamariz.
  • Day 5: Explore the fishing village of Cascais and enjoy fresh seafood.
  • Day 6: Hike the trails of the Serra de Sintra, with stunning views of the coastline.
  • Day 7: Take a boat tour along the coast, with stops at nearby beaches and caves.

Food and Drink Scene of Cascais

Must-Try Dishes

  1. Bacalhau à Brás: A traditional Portuguese dish made with salt cod, potatoes, onions and eggs.
  2. Sardinhas Assadas: Grilled sardines, a popular seafood dish in Portugal.
  3. Pasteis de Nata: A delicious Portuguese egg tart pastry, often served with coffee.

Local Beverages

  1. Vinho Verde: A refreshing, light wine with citrus and fruit notes, commonly grown in the north of Portugal.
  2. Ginjinha: A sweet cherry liqueur, commonly served in small chocolate cups.
  3. Bica: A short, strong espresso, a staple of Portuguese coffee culture.

Where to Stay in Cascais: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  1. Farol Hotel: A luxurious hotel located on the waterfront, with stunning views of the sea and marina.
  2. Cascais Miragem Health & Spa: A 5-star hotel with a spa, indoor and outdoor pools, and a rooftop terrace.
  3. Nice Way Cascais: A budget-friendly hostel located in the historic district, with a cozy atmosphere and comfortable dorm rooms.

What to Buy in Cascais: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  1. Portuguese Olive Oil: A high-quality olive oil, commonly produced in Portugal.
  2. Azulejos: Traditional Portuguese tiles, often used for decoration.
  3. Cork Products: Portugal is the largest producer of cork in the world, and creates a range of products, including bags, shoes, and jewelry.

Local Culture and Customs of Cascais

  • Portuguese is the official language of Portugal, and English is also widely spoken in tourist areas.
  • Tipping in restaurants is not mandatory but is usually appreciated for good service.
  • Dress codes are generally more relaxed in Cascais, with beachwear acceptable at the beach and more formal attire at upscale restaurants.

Best Time to Visit Cascais

The best time to visit Cascais is during the summer months of June to September when the weather is warm and sunny. However, the town can be crowded during this time, so consider visiting in the shoulder seasons of April to May or September to October for fewer crowds and comfortable temperatures.

Do’s and Don’ts of Cascais

Do:

  • Explore the historic town center and enjoy the local architecture.
  • Try the fresh seafood, a specialty of Cascais.
  • Take a day trip to nearby Sintra, a UNESCO World Heritage Site.

Don't:

  • Underestimate the power of the waves at Guincho Beach, always follow the safety rules and regulations.
  • Touch or climb on the cliffs to avoid accidents and damaging environment.
  • Drink tap water, bottled or purified water is always recommended.

Safety and Health

Safety in Cascais

Cascais is generally a safe town for tourists, with a low crime rate. However, it is always important to be aware of your surroundings and take precautions such as keeping valuables in a safe place and avoiding unlit or deserted areas at night.

Health in Cascais

Portugal has a good public healthcare system, and Cascais also has several private clinics. It is recommended that tourists have travel insurance that covers healthcare and medical expenses.

Getting to Cascais: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Cascais

The closest airport to Cascais is Lisbon Portela Airport (LIS), which is approximately 30 km away. From Lisbon Airport, visitors can take a taxi, the metro or a bus to Cascais.

Bus Stations Near Cascais

Cascais has a bus station located in the town center, where regular buses travel to and from Lisbon, Sintra, and nearby towns.

Other Options

Visitors can also hire a car or take a taxi to get to Cascais from Lisbon airport.

How to Get Around in Cascais

The best way to get around Cascais is on foot, as the town center is compact and easy to navigate. For longer distances, visitors can take the bus or a taxi.

Where to Go Next from Cascais: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  1. Lisbon: The vibrant capital of Portugal is only a short train ride away from Cascais and offers countless attractions, including the historic Alfama district and the UNESCO World Heritage Site of Belem Tower.
  2. Sintra: Located just 15 km from Cascais, Sintra is a fairy-tale town with a rich history and stunning architecture, including the iconic Pena Palace and the Moorish Castle.
  3. Cabo da Roca: This stunning cliff-top lookout is one of the most popular landmarks in Portugal, located just 18 km from Cascais.

Conclusion

Cascais is a charming coastal town with a rich history, stunning natural beauty, and a vibrant food and drink scene. Whether you’re looking for outdoor adventures or cultural landmarks, Cascais has something to offer for every type of traveler. With our comprehensive tour guide, you’ll be able to explore all that this beautiful town has to offer and create unforgettable memories of your trip to Portugal.
