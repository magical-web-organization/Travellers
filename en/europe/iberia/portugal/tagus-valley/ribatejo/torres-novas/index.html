Introduction to Torres Novas

Torres Novas is a charming town located in Ribatejo, within the Tagus Valley region of Portugal. It is known for its rich history, cultural attractions, and natural beauty. With its central location, Torres Novas makes an ideal base for exploring the nearby cities of Lisbon and Coimbra. This comprehensive travel guide will help you plan your trip to Torres Novas, including the top attractions, activities, and travel tips.

List of Places to Visit in Torres Novas

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Castelo de Torres Novas: The Castle of Torres Novas is an imposing fortress that dates back to the 12th century. It played an important role in defending the region during the Middle Ages. Visitors can explore the castle walls, towers, and battlements, as well as enjoy panoramic views over the town from the top.
  • Igreja de São Pedro: The Church of St. Peter is a beautiful 16th-century church that features a richly decorated interior with works by several renowned artists.
  • Museu Municipal Carlos Reis: This museum is dedicated to the life and work of Carlos Reis, one of Portugal’s most famous painters. It features a collection of his paintings, as well as other works by regional artists.
  • Aqueduto dos Pegões: The Pegões Aqueduct is a remarkable engineering feat that was built in the 16th century to supply water to the town. It stretches over 6 kilometers and is lined with arches and towers.

Natural Attractions of Torres Novas: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Parque Natural das Serras de Aire e Candeeiros: The Serras de Aire e Candeeiros Natural Park is a stunning area of limestone hills, deep valleys, and underground caves. It is an ideal place for hiking, biking, and rock climbing.
  • Jardim das Rosas: The Rose Garden is a peaceful oasis in the heart of the town. It features several walking paths, scenic views, and a wide variety of flowers and plants.
  • Rio Almonda: The Almonda River is a popular spot for fishing, kayaking, and swimming. It is surrounded by lush vegetation and is home to several species of birds and fish.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Torres Novas

Serras de Aire e Candeeiros Natural Park

  • PR1 - Pedra da Ferida Trail: This challenging 11-kilometer trail takes you through a rugged landscape of cliffs, valleys, and forests. It offers stunning views of the surrounding hills and is home to several species of wildlife.
  • PR2 - Vale do Tejo Trail: This scenic 10-kilometer trail follows the Almonda River and offers several lookout points with panoramic views. It passes through several quaint villages and is a great option for families.
  • PR3 - Nascentes do Almonda Trail: This easy 5-kilometer trail takes you to the source of the Almonda River and is surrounded by lush vegetation. It is a great option for birdwatching and picnicking.

Best Things to Do in Torres Novas: Activities and Tours

  • Guided Historic Tour: A guided tour of the town will take you through its charming streets, squares, and historic landmarks. You will learn about its rich history, architecture, and culture.
  • Segway Tour: A Segway tour is a fun and unique way to explore the town and its surroundings. You will glide through the streets and enjoy scenic views without getting tired.
  • Wine Tasting Tour: A wine tasting tour of the region will introduce you to its rich wine-making tradition. You will visit some of the best wineries in the area and sample some of their finest wines.

Food and Drink Scene of Torres Novas

Must-Try Dishes

  • Bacalhau à Lagareiro: This traditional dish consists of grilled codfish with garlic and olive oil. It is usually served with potatoes and roasted peppers.
  • Cozido: Cozido is a hearty stew made with different types of meat, vegetables, and beans. It is a popular dish during the winter months.
  • Sopa da Pedra: Stone Soup is a famous local dish made with bread, potatoes, beans, and meat. It is traditionally cooked in a clay pot with a hot stone.
  • Queijadas de Torres Novas: These small cheese cakes are a local specialty and are made with fresh cheese, sugar, and eggs.

Local Beverages

  • Portuguese Wine: Portugal is renowned for its wines, and Torres Novas is no exception. The region is home to several vineyards that produce both red and white wines.
  • Ginjinha: This sweet cherry liqueur is popular throughout Portugal and makes a great after-dinner drink.

Where to Stay in Torres Novas: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Hotel Torres Novas: This modern hotel is located in the town center and features comfortable rooms, a restaurant, and a bar.
  • Hostel Norte: This budget hostel is housed in a charming old building and offers comfortable dorm rooms and private rooms.
  • Casa do Adro Hotel: This rustic hotel is located near the castle and features charming rooms, a pool, and a garden.

What to Buy in Torres Novas: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Cheese: Torres Novas is known for its delicious cheeses, including the goat cheese and sheep’s milk cheese.
  • Clay Pots: The region is renowned for its traditional clay pots that are used for cooking and serving food.
  • Handicrafts: You can find several local handicrafts, such as embroidered linens, pottery, and woodcarvings.

Local Culture and Customs of Torres Novas

Torres Novas has a rich cultural heritage that is celebrated throughout the year with festivals, music, and traditional dances. The locals are friendly and welcoming, and visitors are encouraged to participate in the festivities.

Best Time to Visit Torres Novas

The best time to visit Torres Novas is during the spring and autumn months when the weather is mild and pleasant. The summer months can be hot and crowded, while the winter months can be rainy and cold.

Do’s and Don’ts of Torres Novas

Do:

  • Learn some Portuguese phrases before your trip.
  • Try traditional local food and drinks.
  • Respect local customs and traditions.

Don’t:

  • Be disrespectful to local people or their culture.
  • Leave trash in public areas.
  • Ignore safety warnings.

Safety and Health

Safety in Torres Novas

Torres Novas is a safe and welcoming town, and visitors are unlikely to encounter any problems. However, visitors should take normal safety precautions, such as protecting their personal belongings and avoiding isolated areas at night.

Health in Torres Novas

Medical facilities in Torres Novas are of a high standard, and there are several pharmacies located throughout the town. Visitors are advised to have comprehensive travel insurance that includes medical coverage.

Getting to Torres Novas: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Torres Novas

The nearest airport to Torres Novas is Lisbon Portela Airport, which is approximately 120 kilometers away. Visitors can take a taxi, bus, or train from the airport to Torres Novas.

Bus Stations Near Torres Novas

Torres Novas is served by several bus companies, including Rede Expressos and Rodoviária do Tejo. There are several daily buses from Lisbon, Coimbra, and other nearby towns.

Other Options

Visitors can also rent a car or take a taxi from Lisbon or other nearby towns to Torres Novas. The town is located near several major highways, making it easy to access by car.

How to Get Around in Torres Novas

The best way to get around Torres Novas is on foot, as most attractions are located within easy walking distance of each other. Visitors can also rent a bicycle or motorbike to explore the surrounding areas.

Where to Go Next from Torres Novas: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Tomar: Located just 25 kilometers away, Tomar is a historic town that is known for its castle, churches, and museums.
  • Fátima: Fátima is a famous pilgrimage site that attracts millions of visitors each year. It is located approximately 35 kilometers away from Torres Novas.
  • Coimbra: Coimbra is a lively university town that is known for its historic architecture, restaurants, and classic bars. It is located approximately 100 kilometers away from Torres Novas.

Conclusion

Torres Novas is a hidden gem in Portugal that is worth visiting for its historic landmarks, natural beauty, and friendly locals. This comprehensive travel guide will help you plan your trip and make the most of your stay in this charming town.
