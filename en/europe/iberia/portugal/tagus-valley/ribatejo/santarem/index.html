Santarém is a picturesque city in the Ribatejo region of Portugal, nestled in the Tagus Valley. Known for its cultural sites, natural beauty and delicious gastronomy, it is a must-visit destination for anyone traveling to Portugal. This comprehensive guide will help you plan your trip to Santarém, highlighting the top attractions, best places to stay, where to eat, and much more.

Introduction to Santarém

Santarém is a charming city located in central Portugal, with a rich history dating back to Roman times. It is a perfect destination for history enthusiasts, foodies and nature lovers alike. The city is renowned for its gothic and Renaissance architecture, which can be seen in its many churches and cultural landmarks. With many natural attractions such as rivers, valleys, and hills, the outdoors enthusiasts will find an array of exciting activities to enjoy.

List of Places to Visit in Santarém

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Casa-Museu Anselmo Braamcamp Freire is an 18th-century complex with a collection of paintings, furniture, and decorative arts. The museum also features an extensive library and a garden open to the public.
  • Convent of São Francisco dates back to the 13th-century and is one of the most important examples of Gothic architecture in Portugal. It now hosts a cultural center with an art gallery that you can visit.
  • Igreja de Santa Maria da Graça is a 13th-century church renowned for its beauty and artistry. It features the tomb of Pedro Alvares Cabral, the navigator who discovered Brazil in the 15th century.

Natural Attractions of Santarém: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Serra de Montejunto is a natural park that offers hiking trails, birdwatching sites, and spectacular views of the surrounding landscapes. It’s a perfect destination for nature lovers.
  • Portas do Sol is a scenic viewpoint located on the Tagus River with breathtaking vistas across the city and beyond.
  • Zé-Manel dos Ossos Cave is a mysterious underground cave in Casais Velhos, famous for its decorated walls and skeletons. A local guide will take you through the narrow tunnels and reveal the fascinating history behind this unique site.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Santarém

  • Rota das Lezírias is a 10km cycle route that takes you on a tour of the Ribatejo region, passing through vineyards, woods, and fields.
  • Caminho do Tejo is a walking trail alongside the Tagus river, running from Vila Franca de Xira to Santarém. This trail offers stunning views of the river and the city.
  • Trilho dos Castelejos is a 9km hiking trail that takes you through the hills and valleys of the Serra de Montejunto Natural Park and offers some of the most stunning vistas of the surrounding landscapes.

Best Things to Do in Santarém: Activities and Tours

  • Gastronomic tours to taste local delicacies such as the “gado estufado,” an iconic Ribatejan stew, and “pastel de feijão,” a sweet pastry made with beans.
  • Wine tours to visit the local vineyards and taste the famous wines of the region, such as Ribatejo and Tejo.
  • Hot-Air Balloon Rides to see the city from above and enjoy the stunning views of the surrounding landscapes.

Food and Drink Scene of Santarém

Must-Try Dishes

  • Gado Estufado is one of the most popular dishes in Ribatejo, made with beef, carrots, mushrooms and red wine, usually served with rice.
  • Sopa da Pedra is a traditional soup made with beans, meat, and vegetables. Legend has it that a beggar made this soup by asking for help from local residents, slowly adding ingredients until he had a full meal.
  • Farturas are a Portuguese variety of churros, usually coated in sugar and cinnamon.

Local Beverages

  • Vinho Regional Tejo is grown in the region of the same name and is famous for its fruity flavor and excellent quality.
  • Ginjinha is a liqueur made from sour cherries, popular throughout Portugal, with a distinctive tartness and sweetness.

Where to Stay in Santarém: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Santarem Hotel, located in the city center, offers comfortable rooms, an outdoor pool, and free Wi-Fi.
  • Hotel Umu, a charming boutique hotel with a rooftop terrace overlooking the city.
  • Pousada de Santarém Hotel, a 17th-century palace that has been converted into a luxury hotel with a pool and stunning views of the surrounding landscapes.

What to Buy in Santarém: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Handmade pottery from the local artisans, featuring traditional motifs and designs.
  • Ginja liqueur to take home as a gift for friends and family.
  • Casa do Campino scarves and handkerchiefs are a regional specialty made with traditional patterns and colors.

Local Culture and Customs of Santarém

Santarém is deeply rooted in Portuguese tradition, and there are several cultural customs to be aware of.

  • Religious Festivals: Santarém celebrates several religious festivals throughout the year, such as the Festa de Sao José, the Festa de N. Sra da Paz, and the Festa de Nossa Senhora da Oliveira.
  • Portuguese Hospitality: The people of Santarém are known for their generosity and hospitality, so it’s common for locals to invite guests over to their homes for homemade food and drink.

Best Time to Visit Santarém

The best time to visit Santarém is from April to June and from September to November when temperatures are mild, and there is little rainfall. The summer months can be quite hot and busy with tourists.

Do’s and Don’ts of Santarém

Do:

  • Drink wine: Wine is an significant part of Santarém’s culture and history. enjoying a local bottle at the dinner table or at a vineyard tour is a must-do in the area.
  • Try the local cuisine: Santarém boasts some of the best cuisine in Portugal. Gado Estufado and Sopa da Pedra are two local dishes that you must try while in the city.
  • Explore the outdoors: Santarém is surrounded by natural beauty, and there are plenty of hiking and cycling trails to explore.

Don’t:

  • Litter in the streets: Santarém is a beautiful city and has recently invested in keeping their streets clean and tidy. Make sure that you dispose of your trash properly in designated areas.
  • Wear revealing clothing in religious places: Santarém has many beautiful churches and cathedrals. When visiting them, make sure to be respectful and dress modestly.
  • Assume everyone speaks English: While many people in Santarém speak English, it is always polite to try to communicate in Portuguese as it shows a level of respect for the local culture.

Safety and Health

Safety in Santarém

Santarém is a safe city with a low crime rate, but it is always important to be aware of pickpockets in crowded areas. It’s also advised to avoid traveling alone at night in some areas of the city.

Health in Santarém

There are several hospitals and clinics in Santarém city for medical emergencies. The European Health Insurance Card (EHIC) is also valid in Portugal.

Getting to Santarém: Transportation Options and Logistics

Santarém can be easily reached by car, train, or bus from Lisbon or Porto. Traveling by car is the best option, as it allows for flexibility in exploring the surrounding areas.

Airports Near Santarém

  • Lisbon Airport is the closest international airport, located approximately 80 km south of Santarém.

Bus Stations Near Santarém

  • Santarém Bus Terminal is at the city center with daily services to Lisbon and other destinations in Portugal.

Other Options

  • Trains from Lisbon take about an hour and a half to reach Santarém, with frequent departures throughout the day.

How to Get Around in Santarém

Santarém is a relatively small city that can be easily explored by foot. Taxis and buses are also available for travel outside of the city center.

Where to Go Next from Santarém: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Tomar is a nearby city that has one of the most amazing and stunning monuments from the Portuguese renaissance, the Convent of Christ and was home to the where new conquistadors received their training.
  • Obidos is a charming medieval town with historic walls, a castle, and a maze of narrow, cobbled streets lined with white-washed houses.
  • Lisbon is the capital of Portugal and a must-visit destination, located just a short drive from Santarém.

Conclusion

Santarém is a true Portuguese gem and a must-visit destination for anyone traveling to Portugal. This comprehensive guide has highlighted the top attractions, best places to stay, where to eat, and much more to help you plan your visit to this beautiful city. Whether you’re exploring the historical landmarks, wandering through nature or indulging in the local cuisine, Santarém is sure to provide you with an unforgettable holiday.
