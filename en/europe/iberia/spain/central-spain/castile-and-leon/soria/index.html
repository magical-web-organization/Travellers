Introduction to Soria

Soria is a hidden gem of Central Spain that boasts a rich culture and history, stunning landscapes, intriguing architecture, and mouth-watering cuisine. This medieval city, founded in the 1st century BC, sits on a plateau overlooking the Duero valley and is surrounded by rugged hills and mountains. Soria is home to several historical landmarks such as ancient castles, impressive churches, and medieval fortresses. In this travel guide, you will find useful information about the best places to visit, activities to do, local food and drinks, accommodation options, transport, and safety tips for your trip.

Local Culture and Customs of Soria

Soria is a conservative and religious town where traditions and customs are highly respected. Local people tend to respect social hierarchy and show proper manners in public places. It’s also common to say “buenos días” (good morning), “buenas tardes” (good afternoon) or “buenas noches” (good evening) when meeting someone.

Best Time to Visit Soria

The best time to visit Soria is between May to July and September to November when the weather is mild, and tourist crowds are minimal. The summer months can be hot, while winter can be cold and rainy.

Do’s and Don’ts of Soria

Do:

  • Respect their culture and traditions
  • Dress appropriately while visiting religious sites
  • Try local cuisine and beverages

Don’t:

  • Ignore the Siesta timing, which is between 2:00 p.m. to 5:00 p.m.
  • Ask for Tapas in the wrong time of the day
  • Take photographs of strangers without permission

Safety and Health

Safety in Soria

Soria is a safe city for tourist attraction. However, like any other city, it’s essential to keep an eye on your belongings, avoid walking on secluded streets, and take phone numbers for emergency services.

Health in Soria

Soria city has adequate health care facilities, but medical treatment can be expensive. Bring proper travel medical insurance and keep the list of medicines with you.

How to Get Around in Soria

You can explore the town by walking or biking to the destination. Alternatively, you can rent a car, or take local buses.
