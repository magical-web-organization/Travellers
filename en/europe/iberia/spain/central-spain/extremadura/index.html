Introduction to Extremadura

Located in central Spain, Extremadura is a region known for its beautiful natural landscapes, rich history, and vibrant culture. With a history that dates back to ancient times, this region is home to a number of fascinating historical sites, including ancient Roman ruins, majestic castles, and stunning cathedrals.

Aside from its cultural attractions, Extremadura is also home to numerous natural wonders, including stunning mountains, deep valleys, and tranquil rivers. Whether you’re an outdoor enthusiast, a history buff, or simply looking to soak up some local culture, this region has something to offer every visitor.

List of Places to Visit in Extremadura

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Mérida - The ancient Roman city of Mérida is a UNESCO World Heritage Site and boasts a number of impressive Roman ruins, including a well-preserved amphitheater and a stunning aqueduct.
  • Cáceres - Dubbed the “Monumental City”, Cáceres is a beautiful medieval city with a well-preserved old town that dates back to the 14th century.
  • Trujillo - This small town is home to a number of impressive castles, palaces, and monasteries, and is also famous for being the birthplace of Francisco Pizarro, the Spanish conquistador who conquered Peru.
  • Plasencia - This charming town has a well-preserved old town that boasts a number of impressive monuments, including a stunning cathedral and several medieval churches.

Natural Attractions of Extremadura: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Monfragüe National Park - This stunning park is famous for its dense forests, deep gorges, and spectacular rock formations, as well as its diverse wildlife, including eagles, vultures, and numerous other bird species.
  • Jerte Valley - This picturesque valley is known for its stunning natural beauty and is particularly famous for its cherry trees, which bloom in the spring and provide an incredibly beautiful sight.
  • Sierra de Gata - This mountain range is known for its rugged terrain, deep valleys, and stunning waterfalls, and is a popular destination for hikers and nature lovers.
  • Las Hurdes - This hilly region is home to numerous small villages, each with its own unique charm and character, as well as stunning natural landscapes, including deep ravines, lush forests, and crystal-clear streams.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Extremadura

  • Camino Real de Guadalupe - This historic trail starts in the medieval town of Cáceres and takes hikers and bikers through stunning scenery, including forests, valleys, and mountain peaks, before ending in the town of Guadalupe.
  • Via de la Plata - This popular hiking and biking route follows the path of an ancient Roman road and takes visitors through stunning landscapes, including rolling hills, rural villages, and historic towns.
  • TransAndalus - This challenging mountain biking route takes riders through some of the most beautiful and remote areas of Andalusia, including the stunning Sierra de Gata mountain range.

Best Things to Do in Extremadura: Activities and Tours

  • Hot air balloon ride - Take in the stunning natural beauty of Extremadura from above with a hot air balloon ride.
  • Go birdwatching - With its diverse range of habitats and landscapes, Extremadura is a birdwatcher’s paradise and home to over 400 bird species, including rare and endangered species like the Spanish imperial eagle and the Eurasian black vulture.
  • Explore the medieval towns - Extremadura is home to numerous well-preserved medieval towns, each with its own unique charm and character, including Cáceres, Plasencia, and Trujillo.
  • Take a wine tour - Discover the rich wine culture of Extremadura, which is home to numerous wineries and vineyards that produce high-quality wines, including the Montánchez region, which is famous for its traditional grape harvest festival.

Week-Long Itinerary for Extremadura

Day 1: Arrive in Mérida and explore the ancient Roman ruins and historic city center Day 2: Visit the Monfragüe National Park and take a hike or birdwatching tour Day 3: Explore the medieval town of Cáceres, including its ancient city walls, cathedrals, and palaces Day 4: Visit the Jerte Valley during cherry blossom season and explore the picturesque village of Valdastillas Day 5: Take a guided tour of the historic town of Trujillo, including its impressive castle and palaces Day 6: Discover the stunning Sierra de Gata mountain range and take a hike or mountain bike ride along the TransAndalus route Day 7: Visit a local winery or vineyard and sample some of the region’s finest wines.

Food and Drink Scene of Extremadura

Must-Try Dishes

  • Iberian ham - Made from acorn-fed black pigs, this cured ham is a true delicacy in Extremadura.
  • Migas - This traditional dish is made from fried bread crumbs, garlic, and paprika, and is often served with chorizo and fried eggs.
  • Gazpacho - This cold soup is made from tomatoes, peppers, garlic, and olive oil, and is a refreshing dish during the hot summers of Extremadura.
  • Criadillas de Tierra - These earthworms are fried and served with olive oil, garlic, and parsley, and are a popular dish in some parts of Extremadura.

Local Beverages

  • Cerveza de Trigo - This wheat beer is brewed in a traditional style and is a popular local favorite.
  • Vino Tinto - Extremadura is home to numerous vineyards that produce high-quality red wines, including the Ribera del Guadiana region.
  • Pitarra - This local cider is made from fermented apple juice and is a popular beverage in rural areas of Extremadura.

Where to Stay in Extremadura: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Parador de Cáceres - Located in the heart of Cáceres’ historic old town, this stunning hotel is housed in a beautifully restored 14th-century palace.
  • Hostal Real - Located in the picturesque town of Trujillo, this charming guesthouse is housed in a traditional stone building and offers stunning views of the town’s historic center.
  • Hospes Palacio de Arenales & Spa - This luxurious hotel is located just outside of Cáceres and boasts stunning views of the surrounding countryside as well as a spa and outdoor pool.

What to Buy in Extremadura: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Iberian ham - Take home a taste of the region with some high-quality Iberian ham.
  • Ceramics - Extremadura is home to numerous artisans that produce beautiful hand-painted ceramics and pottery.
  • Paprika - The town of La Vera is famous for its high-quality paprika, which is used in many local dishes and adds a delicious smoky flavor.

Local Culture and Customs of Extremadura

Extremadura is known for its rich cultural and artistic heritage, with numerous festivals, events, and traditions that are celebrated throughout the year. Some of the most popular cultural events include the annual grape harvest festival, which takes place in the Montánchez region, and the Festival Internacional de Teatro Clásico de Mérida, which showcases some of the best classical theater performances in Spain.

Best Time to Visit Extremadura

The best time to visit Extremadura is during the spring and fall months, when the temperatures are mild and the landscapes are particularly beautiful. The summer months can be hot and dry, while the winter months can be chilly and rainy.

Do’s and Don’ts of Extremadura

Do:

  • Try the local cuisine and wines
  • Explore the region’s natural and cultural attractions
  • Attend some of the local festivals and events

Don’t:

  • Be disrespectful towards local customs and traditions
  • Litter or damage natural landscapes
  • Ignore safety precautions when hiking or biking

Safety and Health

Safety in Extremadura

Extremadura is generally a safe region, with low crime rates and friendly locals. However, visitors should be aware of pickpocketing and other minor crimes in crowded areas and tourist hotspots.

Health in Extremadura

Extremadura has a well-developed healthcare system, with numerous hospitals and medical facilities throughout the region. However, visitors should be aware of the potential health risks associated with hiking and other outdoor activities, including sunburn and dehydration.

Getting to Extremadura: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Extremadura

The two closest airports to Extremadura are Adolfo Suárez Madrid-Barajas Airport and Seville Airport, both of which offer regular flights from major European and international destinations.

Bus Stations Near Extremadura

Extremeña de Autocares is the main bus company in Extremadura and operates regular services to and from major cities throughout Spain.

Other Options

Driving to Extremadura is also an option, with numerous highways and motorways that connect the region to other parts of Spain and Europe.

How to Get Around in Extremadura

The best way to get around Extremadura is by car, as this allows visitors to explore the region’s natural and cultural attractions at their own pace. However, public transportation is also available, with regular bus and train services connecting the major cities and towns throughout the region.

Where to Go Next from Extremadura: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Seville - Just a few hours’ drive from Extremadura, Seville is a beautiful city known for its stunning architecture, rich history, and vibrant culture.
  • Lisbon - The Portuguese capital is just a few hours’ drive from Extremadura and boasts a stunning mix of ancient and modern architecture, world-class museums, and fantastic restaurants.
  • Granada - Located in the heart of Andalusia, Granada is home to some of Spain’s most famous historical sites, including the stunning Alhambra palace and fortress.

Conclusion

Whether you’re a history buff, an outdoor enthusiast, or simply looking to soak up some local culture, Extremadura has something to offer every visitor. With its stunning natural landscapes, rich history, and vibrant culture, this region is truly one of the hidden gems of central Spain. So why not plan your next vacation to Extremadura and discover all that this fascinating region has to offer!
