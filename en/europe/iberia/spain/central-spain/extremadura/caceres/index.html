Introduction to Caceres

Caceres is a fascinating and historic city located in Extremadura, in Central Spain. Its well-preserved medieval architecture and UNESCO World Heritage status make it a popular destination for travelers from all over the world. Caceres boasts an excellent food scene, stunning natural landscapes, and a rich cultural heritage, making it an ideal destination for those looking for an authentic and immersive Spanish experience.

List of Places to Visit in Caceres

Here are some of the top attractions and places to visit in Caceres:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Plaza Mayor: The heart of Caceres’ Old Town, Plaza Mayor is surrounded by stunning architecture and is the perfect place to sit and watch the world go by.
  • Old Town: The historic center of Caceres is one of the most well-preserved medieval towns in Europe and is a UNESCO World Heritage Site.
  • Casa de los Golfines de Abajo: This grand 16th-century palace houses the Caceres Museum, which features exhibits on the city’s history, art, and culture.
  • Palacio de los Becerra: A beautiful Renaissance palace that now houses the Provincial Museum of Caceres, which showcases art and artifacts from Extremadura’s history.
  • San Francisco Javier Church: This ornate 18th-century church houses an impressive collection of baroque art and architecture.

Natural Attractions of Caceres: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Parque del Principe: A beautiful and peaceful park in the heart of Caceres, perfect for a relaxing afternoon stroll.
  • Los Barruecos Natural Monument: This beautiful nature reserve, located just outside Caceres, is known for its stunning rock formations and crystal-clear swimming holes.
  • Sierra de Gata: This picturesque mountain range is perfect for hiking, with trails offering sweeping mountain views and the chance to spot local wildlife.
  • Monfrague National Park: A bird-watcher’s paradise, Monfrague National Park is home to a variety of endangered species and breathtaking natural landscapes.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Caceres

Caceres is an ideal destination for hiking and biking enthusiasts, with its stunning natural landscapes and well-marked trails. Here are some of the best routes to explore:

  • Ruta de Carlos V: This 6.5 km trail takes you through the heart of the Old Town, past some of Caceres’ most historic and beautiful landmarks.
  • Via Verde de la Plata: A 50 km long walking and cycling route that passes through the beautiful landscapes of Extremadura.
  • Hike to El Chorro de San Juan: This stunning waterfall is a favorite spot for locals and visitors alike, with trails passing through lush forests and picturesque valleys.

Best Things to Do in Caceres: Activities and Tours

Caceres offers a wealth of experiences, perfect for those looking for an authentic and immersive Spanish adventure. Here are some of the best things to do in Caceres:

  • Wine Tasting: Extremadura is known for its excellent wines, and there are several vineyards and bodegas in the area that offer tours and tastings.
  • Tapas Tour: Caceres’ food scene is legendary, and a tapas tour is the perfect way to sample some of the city’s culinary delights.
  • Hot Air Balloon Ride: Take in the stunning landscapes of Extremadura from above, with a breathtaking hot air balloon ride.
  • Flamenco Show: No trip to Spain is complete without experiencing the passion and energy of a flamenco show.

Week-Long Itinerary for Caceres

Here’s a suggested itinerary for a week-long visit to Caceres:

  • Day 1: Arrival and explore the Old Town
  • Day 2: Visit the San Francisco Javier Church and the Provincial Museum of Caceres
  • Day 3: Take a day trip to Los Barruecos Natural Monument
  • Day 4: Hike or bike the Via Verde de la Plata
  • Day 5: Explore the food scene with a tapas tour
  • Day 6: Visit Monfrague National Park and go bird watching
  • Day 7: Departure

Food and Drink Scene of Caceres

Caceres’ food scene is a highlight of any trip to the region. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Iberian Pork: The Iberian pigs of Extremadura produce some of the world’s best pork, and it is a staple of the local cuisine.
  • Migas: A traditional Spanish dish made from breadcrumbs, garlic, and pork fat, often served with bacon and fried eggs.
  • Torta del Casar: A soft and creamy cheese made from sheep’s milk and served with crusty bread.

Local Beverages

  • Extremadura Wine: This region produces excellent red and white wines, with Ribera del Guadiana being the most well-known.
  • Cerveza Extremeña: A local beer made with 100% natural ingredients and no additives.

Where to Stay in Caceres: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Caceres has a wide range of accommodations to choose from, including hotels, hostels, and guesthouses.

  • Parador de Caceres: This stunning historic hotel is located in the heart of the Old Town.
  • NH Collection Caceres Palacio de Oquendo: A modern and stylish hotel located in a renovated 16th-century palace.
  • Hotel Don Carlos Caceres: A boutique hotel located near the city center, with stylish rooms and a rooftop terrace.

What to Buy in Caceres: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Caceres offers a wide range of souvenirs and local products, perfect for bringing a piece of the region home with you.

  • Iberian Ham: This world-famous cured ham is a must-buy for any foodie.
  • Ceramics: Caceres is known for its high-quality pottery, with many local artisans creating beautiful and unique pieces.
  • Dulces de Caceres: These traditional sweets and pastries, made with local ingredients like almonds and honey, make excellent gifts.

Local Culture and Customs of Caceres

Caceres has a rich cultural heritage, with many unique traditions and customs.

  • Festivals and Celebrations: Caceres hosts numerous festivals and celebrations throughout the year, including the Carnival of Caceres, the Womad Festival, and the Medieval Market.
  • Siesta: Like many Spanish cities, Caceres observes a mid-day siesta, during which many shops and businesses close.
  • Friendliness: The people of Caceres are known for their hospitality and friendliness, and visitors can expect a warm welcome wherever they go.

Best Time to Visit Caceres

The best time to visit Caceres is during the spring and fall, when the weather is mild and pleasant. Summers can be hot and crowded, while winters can be cold and rainy.

Safety and Health

Caceres is generally a safe and healthy destination, with low levels of crime and a high standard of healthcare. However, it’s always important to take precautions to ensure a safe and enjoyable trip.

Safety in Caceres

  • Be aware of your surroundings and keep an eye on your belongings in crowded areas.
  • Use licensed taxis or ride-sharing services to avoid scams or dangerous drivers.
  • Don’t leave valuables in your car or unattended in public spaces.

Health in Caceres

  • Ensure that all of your vaccinations are up to date before traveling.
  • Pack any necessary medications and bring a copy of your prescriptions or a doctor’s note.
  • Drink bottled water and avoid tap water to prevent stomach upset.

Getting to Caceres: Transportation Options and Logistics

Caceres is easily accessible by several forms of transportation.

Airports Near Caceres

The nearest airport to Caceres is Madrid-Barajas Airport, which is located approximately 300 km away. From there, visitors can take a train or bus to Caceres.

Bus Stations Near Caceres

Caceres has a modern bus station with connections to many Spanish cities, including Madrid, Seville, and Lisbon.

Other Options

It’s also possible to rent a car and drive to Caceres. However, it’s worth noting that driving in the city center can be challenging due to narrow streets and limited parking.

How to Get Around in Caceres

Caceres’ compact size and pedestrian-friendly streets make it easy to explore on foot. However, there are also several public transportation options available, including buses and taxis.

Where to Go Next from Caceres: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Caceres is surrounded by many other fascinating destinations and excursions.

  • Trujillo: This beautiful historic town is only a short drive from Caceres and is known for its stunning architecture and rich history.
  • Guadalupe: A small town located in the Sierra de Gudalupe, known for its stunning monastery and breathtaking natural landscapes.
  • Merida: This ancient Roman city is located approximately 80 km from Caceres and is home to some of the best-preserved Roman ruins in Spain.

Conclusion

Caceres offers an authentic and immersive Spanish experience, with its stunning medieval architecture, rich cultural heritage, and excellent food scene. Whether you’re hiking in the mountains, exploring the Old Town, or relaxing in a local bar with a drink, Caceres has something for everyone.
