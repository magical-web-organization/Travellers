Introduction to Northern Suburbs

Madrid’s Northern Suburbs offer a perfect escape from the fast-paced city lifestyle. They are the ideal place to relax, rejuvenate and experience life in a unique way. Located in the Community of Madrid, Central Spain, and home to Spain’s cultural and natural treasures, Northern Suburbs offer breathtaking views, pristine landscapes, and historic architecture.

Whether you’re a culture vulture, adventurer, or a foodie, the Northern Suburbs of Madrid have something in store for everyone. Explore the best things to do in Northern Suburbs, get to know the local culture, and experience the beauty of this Spanish destination.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Northern Suburbs

Some of the fantastic hiking and biking routes worth exploring in Northern Suburbs include:

  • The Cuenca Alta del Río Manzanares Natural Park
  • Monte del Pardo
  • Cercedilla and Cotos
  • The foothills of the Sierra de Guadarrama

Best Things to Do in Northern Suburbs: Activities and Tours

  • Take a Hot Air Balloon ride over the breathtaking Guadarrama Mountains.
  • Explore the medieval buildings and ancient Roman walls of Alcala de Henares.
  • Visit the Casa de Campo, the largest public park in Madrid.
  • Discover the peaceful Valle de los Caidos, a historical Catholic complex built to honor the victims of the Spanish Civil War.

Week-Long Itinerary for Northern Suburbs

Day Activity
Day 1 Visit the El Prado Museum and explore the Old Madrid district at night.
Day 2 Stroll around Parque Nacional de la Sierra de Guadarrama and marvel at the stunning mountainous landscape.
Day 3 Take a biking tour of Monte del Pardo.
Day 4 Spend a day with the kids at the Warner Bros theme park.
Day 5 Visit the Royal Palace of Madrid and take a walking tour of the Madrid Rio.
Day 6 Explore the medieval town of Alcalá de Henares.
Day 7 Go for a hot air balloon ride over Guadarrama Mountains and finish the day with a traditional Spanish Flamenco show.

Food and Drink Scene of Northern Suburbs

Must-Try Dishes

  • Cocido Madrileño, a combination of meats, vegetables, and chickpeas
  • Patatas bravas, a traditional potato dish, served with spicy tomato sauce
  • Huevos rotos, a breakfast dish with fried eggs and broken potatoes

Local Beverages

  • Tinto de verano, a refreshing wine beverage, perfect for the Spanish heat
  • Horchata, a sweet, chilled almond drink popular in the summer months

Local Culture and Customs of Northern Suburbs

Madrid’s culture can be appreciated through frequent events like its festivals, popular bylanes, good food, and a cohesive community. Visitors can join one of the numerous walking tours in the area or explore on their own to get a glimpse of its local cultural roots.

Best Time to Visit Northern Suburbs

The best time to visit the Northern Suburbs of Madrid is from April to June when the weather is mild, and all the tourist attractions are open without being crowded.

Do’s and Don’ts of Northern Suburbs

Do:

  • Treat the locals with respect.
  • Dress modestly and appropriately while visiting religious or cultural sites.
  • Always make reservations in advance.

Don’t:

  • Jaywalk—use the pedestrian crossings.
  • Talk loudly on the streets, especially in the late evening and early morning.
  • Forget to carry a refillable bottle of water.

Safety and Health

Safety in Northern Suburbs

The Northern Suburbs are relatively safe. The emergency number in Spain is 112, which can be used for both medical and non-medical emergencies.

Health in Northern Suburbs

Health services are of high quality in Northern Suburbs. Several hospitals and clinics are available for travelers.
