Introduction to Aranjuez

Located in the Community of Madrid, Aranjuez is a charming town with a rich history and culture. Home to the Royal Palace of Aranjuez, the town is known for its beautiful gardens, historic landmarks, and delicious food. Whether you’re looking for a peaceful getaway or an exciting adventure, Aranjuez has something for everyone.

List of Places to Visit in Aranjuez

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Royal Palace of Aranjuez: Built in the 16th century, the Royal Palace of Aranjuez is a beautiful example of Spanish architecture. Visitors can explore the majestic halls, lavish rooms, and stunning gardens.
  • Museum of Faluas Reales: A museum dedicated to the carriages used by Spanish royalty, the Museum of Faluas Reales has a collection of over 20 carriages, including the carriage used by King Carlos III during his coronation.
  • Jardin del Parterre: Located next to the Royal Palace, the Jardin del Parterre is a stunning garden that dates back to the 16th century. Visitors can admire the beautiful fountains, sculptures, and flowers.

Natural Attractions of Aranjuez: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Jardin de la Isla: A beautiful garden located on an island in the Tagus River, the Jardin de la Isla offers breathtaking views of the river and the surrounding landscape. Visitors can explore the garden’s many paths, fountains, and ponds.
  • Tagus River: The Tagus River runs through Aranjuez, offering opportunities for swimming, kayaking, and fishing.
  • Bicycle Trail: Aranjuez has a dedicated bicycle trails that offers visitors the chance to explore the town and its surroundings at their own pace.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Aranjuez

For avid hikers and bikers, Aranjuez offers several scenic routes. The most popular ones are:

  • Real Cortijo Golf Course: A scenic path that offers stunning views of the Sierra de Guadarrama Mountains and the surrounding countryside.
  • Dehesa de Montesinos: A route that takes you through the beautiful Dehesa de Montesinos, a natural park home to many different species of birds.
  • El Regajal-Mar de Ontígola: A route that takes you through El Regajal-Mar de Ontígola, a protected wetland area that’s home to many rare bird species.

Best Things to Do in Aranjuez: Activities and Tours

Whether you’re looking for adventure or relaxation, Aranjuez has something for everyone. Here are some of the best things to do:

  • Visit the Royal Palace: No trip to Aranjuez is complete without a visit to the Royal Palace of Aranjuez. Take a guided tour to learn about the history and culture of the Spanish royals.
  • Explore the Gardens: Aranjuez is known for its beautiful gardens, so make sure to explore as many as you can. Some of the best ones are Jardin del Principe, Jardin del Parterre, and Jardin de la Isla.
  • Take a Bike Tour: Rent a bike and explore Aranjuez on two wheels. You can join a guided tour or explore on your own.
  • Go Hiking: Aranjuez has several scenic hiking routes, with beautiful views of the surrounding countryside.
  • Indulge in Local Cuisine: Aranjuez is known for its delicious cuisine, so make sure to try some of the town’s specialties, such as strawberries, asparagus, and trout.

Week-Long Itinerary for Aranjuez

If you’re planning a week-long trip to Aranjuez, here’s a sample itinerary:

  • Day 1: Arrive in Aranjuez and explore the town center.
  • Day 2: Visit the Royal Palace and explore the nearby gardens.
  • Day 3: Take a bike tour of the town and the surrounding countryside.
  • Day 4: Hike one of the scenic hiking routes.
  • Day 5: Visit the Museum of Faluas Reales.
  • Day 6: Take a day trip to nearby Toledo.
  • Day 7: Relax and soak up the local culture before departing.

Food and Drink Scene of Aranjuez

Must-Try Dishes

  • Esparragos de Aranjuez: Asparagus is a specialty of Aranjuez, and it’s often served with hollandaise sauce.
  • Fresas de Aranjuez: Another specialty of Aranjuez, strawberries are often served with whipped cream or in a strawberry tart.
  • Trucha: Trout is also popular in Aranjuez, and it’s often prepared with garlic and lemon.

Local Beverages

  • Anis del Mono: A sweet liquor made with anise, Anis del Mono is a popular after-dinner drink in Aranjuez.
  • Manzanilla: A dry sherry that pairs well with tapas, manzanilla is a popular drink in Spain.

Where to Stay in Aranjuez: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

There are many options for accommodations in Aranjuez, ranging from budget-friendly hostels to luxurious hotels. Some of the best options include:

  • Parador de Aranjuez: Located in the Royal Palace, the Parador de Aranjuez is a luxurious hotel with stunning views of the Tagus River.
  • NH Collection Palacio de Aranjuez: A four-star hotel located in the heart of Aranjuez, the NH Collection Palacio de Aranjuez offers comfortable rooms and modern amenities.
  • El Cocherón 1919: A boutique hotel with six rooms, El Cocherón 1919 offers a cozy and intimate atmosphere.

What to Buy in Aranjuez: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

When shopping in Aranjuez, look for:

  • Ceramics: Aranjuez is famous for its beautiful ceramic plates, bowls, and vases.
  • Strawberries: You can take home some of Aranjuez’s famous strawberries, which are often sold in jam or candy form.
  • Asparagus: Fresh asparagus is another local specialty that you can bring back home.

Local Culture and Customs of Aranjuez

The people of Aranjuez are known for their friendly and welcoming nature. The town’s culture is heavily influenced by the Spanish royals, and many of the town’s traditions and customs revolve around its rich history.

Best Time to Visit Aranjuez

The best time to visit Aranjuez is during the spring (April to June) and fall (September to November) when the temperatures are mild and the gardens are in full bloom. Summer can be very hot and crowded, while winter can be cold and rainy.

Do’s and Don’ts of Aranjuez

Do:

  • Do try the local cuisine, especially the asparagus and strawberries.
  • Do explore the town’s many gardens and parks.
  • Do visit the Royal Palace and Museum of Faluas Reales.
  • Do respect the local customs and traditions.

Don’t:

  • Don’t litter or damage the town’s natural areas.
  • Don’t bring pets into the gardens.
  • Don’t take photos of the inside of the Royal Palace.

Safety and Health

Safety in Aranjuez

Aranjuez is a safe town, but visitors should still take the usual precautions, such as keeping an eye on their belongings and avoiding unlit areas at night.

Health in Aranjuez

Visitors should take care to drink bottled water and avoid tap water, which may not be safe to drink.

Getting to Aranjuez: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Aranjuez

The closest airport to Aranjuez is Madrid Airport, which is about 40 km away. From the airport, visitors can take a taxi or rental car to Aranjuez, or take public transportation.

Bus Stations Near Aranjuez

Aranjuez has a central bus station, with regular bus service to many nearby destinations. Visitors can also take buses from Madrid to Aranjuez.

Other Options

Visitors can also take a train from Madrid to Aranjuez. The train station is located in the town center, making it easy to explore the town on foot.

How to Get Around in Aranjuez

Aranjuez is a small town, and it’s easy to get around on foot or by bike. Visitors can also rent a car or take a taxi for longer journeys.

Where to Go Next from Aranjuez: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Aranjuez is located in the heart of Spain, making it a great base for exploring other nearby destinations. Some of the best places to visit include:

  • Toledo: A historic city with stunning architecture and cultural landmarks.
  • Chinchon: A charming town famous for its wine and beautiful plaza mayor.
  • Segovia: A UNESCO World Heritage Site with a stunning castle and aqueduct.

Conclusion

Aranjuez is a beautiful town that offers visitors a glimpse into Spain’s rich history and culture. With its stunning gardens, historic landmarks, delicious food, and nearby attractions, Aranjuez is the perfect destination for anyone looking for an authentic Spanish experience.
