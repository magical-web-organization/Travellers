Introduction to Benalmádena

Located in the Costa del Sol region of Andalusia, Spain, Benalmádena is a charming town surrounded by the Mediterranean Sea and the mountains. The town offers something for everyone, from its rich cultural heritage to its natural attractions, outdoor activities, and stunning views. Whether you’re a history buff, a nature lover, or an adventure seeker, you’ll find plenty to do in Benalmádena. This guide lists the top places to visit, best things to do, and where to stay and eat in Benalmádena.

List of Places to Visit in Benalmádena

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

1. Colomares Castle

This unique castle was built by Dr. Esteban Martín y Martín, a doctor who was passionate about Christopher Columbus and his voyages. The castle is a tribute to the discovery of America and incorporates Byzantine, Gothic, and Moorish architectural styles. The castle houses a small museum with exhibits about Columbus and his voyages.

2. Stupa de la Iluminación

The Stupa de la Iluminación is the largest stupa (a Buddhist monument) in Europe. This stunning monument was built using traditional techniques and is a symbol of peace, harmony, and enlightenment. Visitors can take guided tours of the stupa and attend meditation sessions.

3. Benalmádena Pueblo

Benalmádena Pueblo is the historic center of the town, and it retains its Andalusian charm with narrow streets, white houses, and picturesque squares. The pueblo is home to several historical landmarks, including the Church of Santo Domingo and the Casa de la Cultura.

Natural Attractions of Benalmádena: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

1. Benalmádena Cable Car

The Benalmádena Cable Car takes visitors on a scenic ride to the top of Mount Calamorro, which offers panoramic views of the coast and the surrounding mountains. At the top, visitors can enjoy a nature reserve, falconry displays, and a restaurant.

2. Puerto Marina

Puerto Marina is a beautiful marina with a wide range of restaurants, cafes, and shops. Visitors can take boat trips, go fishing, or just enjoy the beautiful sea views. The marina is also home to the Sea Life aquarium, which showcases the marine life of the Mediterranean.

3. Tivoli World

Tivoli World is a fun-filled theme park with rides, shows, and attractions for all ages. Visitors can also enjoy the stunning gardens and beautiful views of the coast.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Benalmádena

Benalmádena has several hiking and biking trails that offer stunning views of the coast and the mountains. The most popular routes include the Sierra de Mijas Natural Park, the Arroyo de la Miel, and the Camino de Colmenar. These routes offer varying levels of difficulty, so visitors can choose a trail that suits their fitness level and preferences.

Best Things to Do in Benalmádena: Activities and Tours

1. Dolphin-Watching Tours

Benalmádena is home to a large population of dolphins, and visitors can take boat tours to see these amazing creatures up close. These tours also offer stunning views of the coast and the mountains.

2. Water Sports

Benalmádena offers a wide range of water sports, including jet skiing, paragliding, scuba diving, and snorkeling. These activities allow visitors to explore the beautiful underwater world of the Mediterranean and enjoy the thrill of the water sports.

3. Flamenco Shows

Flamenco is an important part of Andalusian culture, and visitors can attend flamenco shows in Benalmádena. These shows offer an insight into the flamenco traditions and are an excellent way to experience the local culture.

Week-Long Itinerary for Benalmádena

Day 1: Visit Colomares Castle and the Stupa de la Iluminación

Day 2: Explore the historic center of Benalmádena Pueblo

Day 3: Take the Benalmádena Cable Car to Mount Calamorro

Day 4: Visit Puerto Marina and the Sea Life aquarium

Day 5: Spend the day at Tivoli World theme park

Day 6: Hike or bike in the Sierra de Mijas Natural Park

Day 7: Take a dolphin-watching tour or enjoy water sports

Food and Drink Scene of Benalmádena

Must-Try Dishes

Some of the must-try dishes in Benalmádena include:

  • Paella: A traditional Spanish dish made with rice, saffron, and a variety of seafood or meat.

  • Gazpacho: A cold soup made with tomatoes, cucumber, peppers, and garlic.

  • Pescaíto frito: Fried fish served with a variety of dipping sauces.

  • Tortilla española: A Spanish omelette made with potatoes and onions.

Local Beverages

Benalmádena is known for its local wines, including the sweet Malaga wine and the dry white wine. Visitors can also try the local beer, Cerveza Victoria, which is brewed in Malaga.

Local Culture and Customs of Benalmádena

Benalmádena is a friendly and welcoming town, and visitors are expected to respect the local culture and customs. Dress modestly when visiting religious sites and be respectful of the local traditions and customs.

Best Time to Visit Benalmádena

The best time to visit Benalmádena is during the spring and autumn seasons when the weather is mild, and the crowds are smaller. Summer is the peak tourist season, and the temperatures can soar, which makes it quite crowded, but it is an excellent time to enjoy the beaches.

Don’t:

  • Ignore the local customs and traditions

Safety and Health

Safety in Benalmádena

Benalmádena is a safe town, but visitors should always take basic precautions, such as keeping an eye on their belongings and staying in well-lit areas at night.

Health in Benalmádena

Visitors should take basic precautions to avoid getting sick, such as drinking bottled water and avoiding street food. They should also bring any necessary medications and have travel insurance that covers medical emergencies.

Getting to Benalmádena: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Benalmádena

The nearest airport to Benalmádena is Malaga Airport, which is about 15 km away. The airport is well-connected to major European cities and offers several transportation options, including taxis, buses, and trains.

Bus Stations Near Benalmádena

Benalmádena has a bus station that offers connections to other towns and cities in Costa del Sol and Andalusia.

Other Options

Visitors can also reach Benalmádena by train, car, or ferry.

How to Get Around in Benalmádena

Benalmádena has a good public transportation system, including buses and trains. Taxis and rental cars are also available for visitors who prefer more flexibility in their transportation.

Where to Go Next from Benalmádena: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Some of the nearby destinations and excursions from Benalmádena include:

Conclusion

Benalmádena is a beautiful town with a rich cultural heritage, natural attractions, and outdoor activities.
