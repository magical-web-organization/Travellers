Introduction to Mijas

Located in the heart of Costa del Sol, in Andalusia, Spain, Mijas is a timeless gem that truly captures the essence of Andalusian culture. This charming town boasts a unique blend of Moorish, Roman and Spanish influences, coupled with its breathtaking natural scenery, vibrant food scene, and a rich history worth exploring.

List of Places to Visit in Mijas

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Mijas has a fair share of museums and historical places that are not only culturally rich but also visually stunning. Some significant culturally important landmarks include:

  • Mijas Watchtower
  • The Iglesia de la Inmaculada Concepción (Church of the Immaculate Conception)
  • Mijas Museum

Natural Attractions of Mijas: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

For nature enthusiasts, Mijas offers a myriad of opportunities to immerse oneself in the natural beauty and serenity of the town. Some natural attractions to check out are:

  • The Sierra de Mijas Mountain Range
  • La Cala de Mijas Beach
  • The Mijas Aquatic Park

Hiking/Biking Routes of Mijas

Mijas presents a plethora of leisurely activities for the physically active, which include hiking, biking, and walking. Some of the popular routes are:

  • Pueblo Mijas to Mijas Costa
  • The Mijas - Benalmádena Trail
  • The Sierra de Mijas Mountain Range Trek

Best Things to Do in Mijas: Activities and Tours

To make the most of your time in Mijas, here are some fantastic experiences to add to your itinerary.

  • Donkey Taxi Tour
  • A visit to the Bullring of Mijas
  • Mijas Wine Tour

Food and Drink Scene of Mijas

Must-Try Dishes

Mijas’ thriving food scene includes traditional Andalusian food with a rustic and flavorful twist. Some must-try dishes include:

  • Gazpacho
  • Serrano ham
  • Freshly caught fried squid

Local Beverages

Mijas has a variety of drinks that boost the flavor of traditional Andalusian cuisine. You can try the local sherry, Alhambra beer, and Malaga wine

Where to Stay in Mijas: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Mijas offers a host of choices for accommodation, ranging from budget-friendly hostels to luxury hotels. Some of the best places to stay in Mijas are:

  • Eurostars Mijas Golf & Spa
  • La Cala Resort
  • TRH Mijas

What to Buy in Mijas: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Mijas has a host of traditional and cultural souvenirs, including:

  • Pottery and ceramics
  • The Solaz de Andalucia Cosmetics
  • Myrrah Mijas Soap

Local Culture and Customs of Mijas

Mijas has a very rich and unique culture that visitors should learn by heart to fully immerse themselves in the town’s ambiance. Locals cherish family values and are proud of their pueblos blancos (white villages). Also, they have a love for wines and gastronomy, uphold religious and cultural traditions and dress vibrantly on special occasions.

Best Time to Visit Mijas

The best time to visit Mijas is from April to October. This period offers the best weather conditions and allows visitors to explore Mijas’ many outdoor treasures without restrictions.

Do’s and Don’ts of Mijas

Do:

  • Respect local customs and traditions
  • Be mindful of those around you and refrain from littering
  • Try as much local food and drink as you can

Don’t:

  • Don’t leave the table before asking for your bill
  • Don’t speak loudly in public spaces
  • Don’t ignore dress codes in religious spaces

Safety and Health

Safety in Mijas

Mijas is generally safe for travelers, but it is recommended to keep your valuables safe and secure, especially in crowded places like markets and bus stations.

Health in Mijas

Standard healthcare services are available in Mijas, with both public and private hospitals located in the town. Visitors with medical conditions are advised to travel with a health insurance policy that covers international medical care.

Getting to Mijas: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Mijas

The nearest airports to Mijas are the Malaga Airport (AGP) and the Gibraltar International Airport (GIB).

Bus Stations Near Mijas

The town of Mijas is directly accessible via the Fuengirola bus station.

Other Options

The town of Mijas also has two train stations; one located in Mijas Pueblo and another in La Cala de Mijas.

How to Get Around in Mijas

Mijas is a small town, and visitors can comfortably navigate the area on foot or use public transportation services like buses, taxis or trains. Alternatively, visitors can opt to rent a car, bike or scooter for a more flexible mode of transport.

Where to Go Next from Mijas: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Conclusion

Mijas in Andalusia offers visitors a vibrant cultural, gastronomical, natural, and historical experience that will leave them with unforgettable memories. With careful planning and research, Mijas has everything one needs for a most rewarding experience.
