Introduction to Alhama de Granada

Located in the province of Granada, Andalusia, Southern Spain, Alhama de Granada is a picturesque town known for its breathtaking natural landscapes, deep history, and cultural gems. With its name meaning “hot springs” in Arabic, Alhama is home to natural springs and hot springs that attract visitors year-round. The town’s stunning architecture, ancient fortresses, and Arab baths all speak to its rich cultural heritage. Whether you’re traveling solo, with friends or family, Alhama provides something for everyone with its tranquil landscapes, outdoor activities, and delicious cuisine.

List of Places to Visit in Alhama de Granada

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Arab Baths: Dating back to the 12th century, the Arab Baths are a must-visit attraction in Alhama. Used for healing and purification rituals, these baths offer a unique glimpse into the town’s past.
  • Church of the Incarnation: Known for its Mudejar-Moorish architecture, the Church of the Incarnation is an elegant and unique building worth visiting.
  • Los Tajos: The limestone gorge that surrounds Alhama is an incredible natural wonder, and Los Tajos offers a bird’s eye view of the stunning terrain.
  • Plaza de la Constitución: Alhama’s bustling town square is home to a fantastic foodie market and the iconic town hall building.
  • Alhama Castle: Dating back to the 8th century, Alhama Castle is a historical monument that has stood the test of time.

Natural Attractions of Alhama de Granada: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • The Rio Alhama: The river that runs through the center of Alhama provides ample opportunities for kayaking, swimming, and hiking along its banks.
  • Sierra de Tejeda Natural Park: The park, covering over 40,000 hectares of olive groves, cork trees, and more, offers stunning views of the surrounding landscapes.
  • Hot Springs: Relax and rejuvenate in Alhama’s hot springs, which are said to be therapeutic and healing.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Alhama de Granada

  • Ruta del Vino: This wine route takes you past Alhama de Granada’s famed vineyards with views of the Sierra Nevada mountains along the way.
  • Ruta de las Alpujarras: This route spans over 40 kilometers and takes you through charming, historical villages surrounded by stunning landscapes.

Best Things to Do in Alhama de Granada: Activities and Tours

  • Hot Air Balloon Rides: Take a hot air balloon tour of Alhama and admire the town’s stunning views from the sky.
  • Wine Tasting: Sample the delicious wines from Alhama’s vineyards on a wine tour.
  • Horse Riding: Explore Alhama on a horseback riding tour and soak in the beauty of the town’s natural landscapes.

Food and Drink Scene of Alhama de Granada

Must-Try Dishes

  • Tapas: Spain’s famous small plates are the perfect way to try a variety of different dishes, including jamón ibérico, tortilla española, and patatas bravas.
  • Gazpacho: This refreshing traditional cold soup is made of tomatoes, peppers, cucumbers, and garlic.
  • Churros con Chocolate: A beloved dessert in Spain, churros are long, thin donuts that pair perfectly with rich, creamy chocolate sauce.

Local Beverages

  • Wine: Alhama’s vineyards produce an array of delicious local wines, including the Tempranillo and the Garnacha.
  • Cerveza: Spain’s beer scene is thriving, and Alhama has some great options to try, including Alhama’s local lager, Alhambra.

Where to Stay in Alhama de Granada: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Hotel Balneario de Alhama: This charming historical hotel is located in the heart of Alhama and offers incredible views of the town and the surrounding terrain.
  • Hotel La Seguiriya: This boutique hotel offers stunning rooms and an outdoor pool with views of Sierra Nevada.
  • Casa Rural Torre Hueca: This cozy guesthouse is tucked away in Alhama’s charming side-streets and provides a serene and peaceful retreat.

What to Buy in Alhama de Granada: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Olive Oil: Spain is known for its high-quality olive oil, and Alhama is no exception.
  • Handmade Ceramics: Located close to Granada, pottery is a significant traditional craft in Alhama and is an excellent souvenir to take home.
  • Local Wines: Alhama’s wines are produced using locally grown grapes and offer unique flavor notes; these are perfect souvenirs to bring home.

Local Culture and Customs of Alhama de Granada

Alhama de Granada is steeped in history and tradition, and these are reflected in the town’s customs and cultural practices. Inhabitants tend to have a laid-back and relaxed lifestyle, taking time to enjoy good food, wine, and company. The Flamenco dance style is popular in this region and showcases the local pride in its heritage. Spaniards often have a long midday break, where they enjoy a siesta and take a rest.

Best Time to Visit Alhama de Granada

The best time to visit Alhama de Granada is from mid-March to early June and September to the end of October when the weather is pleasant and mild. During this period, visitors can also take part in the various festivals that take place in the town. Alhama de Granada’s main festival is “The Day of the Tourist,” which pays homage to the town’s historical significance and vibrant culture.

Do’s and Don’ts of Alhama de Granada

Do:

  • Try the local wine and cuisine
  • Take a walk around the Rio Alhama
  • Take time to relax and enjoy the town
  • Visit the Alhama de Granada Castle

  • Don’t litter in the beautiful natural surroundings
  • Don’t underestimate the heat during summer months
  • Don’t skip out on the Arab Baths

Safety and Health

Safety in Alhama de Granada

Alhama de Granada is generally a safe town to visit. However, visitors should take care of their belongings and keep an eye on their surroundings, particularly in crowded areas.

Health in Alhama de Granada

Spain has excellent health care facilities in the event of an emergency. Visitors should ensure that they have adequate insurance coverage before they go, as medical care can be expensive.

Getting to Alhama de Granada: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Alhama de Granada

The closest airport to Alhama de Granada is Granada Airport (GRX), which is approximately 45 kilometers away. Malaga Airport (AGP) is the nearest international airport and is around 100 kilometers from Alhama.

Bus Stations Near Alhama de Granada

The closest bus station to Alhama de Granada is located in Granada. Visitors can take a direct bus from Granada city center to Alhama de Granada, which takes around 1 hour and 30 minutes.

Other Options

Driving is also an option, and if on a road trip visitors can enjoy the stunning views along the way. Taxis and car-sharing services are also available, and visitors can take advantage of these options for comfortable and convenient travel.

How to Get Around in Alhama de Granada

The best way to discover Alhama de Granada is on foot - visitors can take their time and explore every nook and corner at their leisure. Visitors who prefer not to walk can rent a bike or take a taxi to explore the town or surrounding areas.

Where to Go Next from Alhama de Granada: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

If you enjoy Alhama’s peaceful ambiance and historical significance, you might also consider planning day trips to nearby destinations, including:

  • Granada: Explore the famous Alhambra with its stunning architecture and gardens.
  • Nerja: Visit the stunning beaches while exploring the town’s Moorish and Christian history.
  • Seville: Known as the cultural hub of flamenco and tapas, Seville also boasts many historical landmarks.

Conclusion

Alhama de Granada is a hidden gem in Southern Spain that combines natural beauty, history, and culture. From Alhama’s unique Arab Baths to outdoor adventures and delicious cuisine, the town offers visitors a wide range of experiences. With this comprehensive guide, visitors can explore one of Spain’s most charming destinations and create unforgettable memories.
