Introduction to Calvià

Calvià is a town located in the Serra de Tramuntana mountain range on the island of Mallorca, which is part of the Balearic Islands in Spain. The town is surrounded by stunning mountains and beautiful beaches, making it a popular destination for both adventure-seekers and those looking for a relaxing vacation. Calvià is a culturally rich town that has preserved its traditional atmosphere, while also offering modern amenities to cater to visitors.

List of Places to Visit in Calvià

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Bellver Castle: Built in the 14th century, this Gothic-style castle is located in Palma and is one of the few round castles in Europe.
  • Museu de Sa Jugueta Antiga: A museum in Santa Ponça that exhibits antique toys from the 19th and 20th centuries.
  • Museu Municipal de Calvià: This museum displays the history and culture of the town through various exhibits, including a collection of pottery and tools.
  • Església de Sant Joan Baptista: A beautiful 17th-century church located in the heart of Calvià.

Natural Attractions of Calvià: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Parc Natural de la Serra de Tramuntana: A natural park that stretches along the northern coast of Mallorca, offering breathtaking views of the mountains and sea. Visitors can go hiking, biking, or horseback riding.
  • Playa de Santa Ponsa: A beautiful sandy beach located in the town of Santa Ponsa, perfect for swimming and sunbathing.
  • The Calvià Mountains: These mountains offer a range of hiking trails and breathtaking scenic views of the surrounding landscape.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Calvià

  • Puig de Galatzó: A challenging hiking trail that leads to the top of Galatzó Mountain, offering stunning panoramic views of the area.
  • Camí de Sa Volta des General: A hiking and biking trail that spans 6 km and offers views of the island’s landscape, including mountains, valleys, and forests.
  • Serra de Na Burguesa: A mountain range that offers numerous hiking and mountain biking routes, with breathtaking views of the sea and surrounding islands.

Best Things to Do in Calvià: Activities and Tours

  • Scuba Diving: The crystal-clear waters of Mallorca are perfect for scuba diving. Visitors can explore underwater caves and discover the island’s diverse marine life.
  • Wine Tasting: Mallorca is known for its vineyards and wineries, and visitors can take a wine tour to taste some of the best wines the island has to offer.
  • Hot Air Balloon Rides: For a unique experience, visitors can take a hot air balloon ride and get stunning aerial views of the island.

Week-Long Itinerary for Calvià

Day Activities
1 Explore Calvià town and visit the historic sites such as the Església de Sant Joan Baptista and Museu Municipal de Calvià.
2 Take a hiking or biking tour in the Serra de Tramuntana mountain range.
3 Visit Parc Natural de la Serra de Tramuntana and enjoy outdoor activities such as horseback riding or camping.
4 Relax on the beach at Playa de Santa Ponsa and take a dip in the crystal-clear waters.
5 Take a wine tour and explore the vineyards and wineries of Mallorca.
6 Go scuba diving and explore the underwater caves and marine life of the island.
7 End the trip with a hot air balloon ride and get stunning aerial views of the island.

Food and Drink Scene of Calvià

Must-Try Dishes

  • Tumbet: A traditional Mallorcan dish made with layers of potatoes, eggplant, red peppers, and tomato sauce.
  • Paella: A Spanish rice dish made with saffron, seafood, and various meats.
  • Sobrasada: A cured sausage made from minced pork, paprika, and other spices.

Local Beverages

  • Hierbas: A herbal liqueur made with local herbs and spices.
  • Pomada: A refreshing drink made with gin and lemonade.

Where to Stay in Calvià: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Castell Son Claret: A luxurious hotel located on the outskirts of Calvià town, featuring a spa and outdoor pool.
  • Hotel Son Caliu Spa Oasis: A modern hotel located in Santa Ponsa, featuring a spa and various outdoor activities.
  • Can Boqueta: A quaint guesthouse located in the heart of Calvià town, offering a traditional Mallorcan experience.

What to Buy in Calvià: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Local handicrafts: Mallorca is known for its pottery, woodworking, and woven baskets, which make unique souvenirs to bring back home.
  • Wine: The island is famous for its wines, particularly the red variety. Visitors can bring home a bottle or two to enjoy at home.

Local Culture and Customs of Calvià

Mallorca has a rich cultural heritage that has been influenced by various civilizations throughout history. The local people are proud of their heritage and often celebrate it through festivals and traditional events. Visitors should respect the local customs and traditions, dress appropriately, and be mindful of noise levels in residential areas.

Best Time to Visit Calvià

The best time to visit Calvià is from April to October when the weather is warm and sunny. The peak tourist season is from June to August, so visitors should book their accommodations in advance during this time.

Do’s and Don’ts of Calvià

Do:

  • Respect the local culture and customs.
  • Try the local cuisine and wine.
  • Explore the natural beauty of the island.

Don’t:

  • Wear inappropriate clothing in public areas, such as beaches or churches.
  • Be loud and disruptive in residential areas.
  • Disrespect the local people or their traditions.

Safety and Health

Safety in Calvià

Calvià is generally a safe town for travelers, but visitors should still take precautions to protect themselves and their belongings. Avoid leaving valuables out in the open and be aware of pickpockets in crowded areas.

Health in Calvià

Mallorca has a good healthcare system, but visitors should still have travel insurance that covers medical emergencies. It’s also advisable to bring any necessary medications and consult with a doctor before traveling.

Getting to Calvià: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Calvià

  • Palma de Mallorca Airport: This is the main airport of Mallorca and is located approximately 30 minutes from Calvià town.

Bus Stations Near Calvià

  • Estació Intermodal de Palma: This is the main bus station in Palma, where visitors can take a bus to Calvià town.

Other Options

  • Car Rental: Visitors can rent a car at the airport or in Palma and drive to Calvià.
  • Taxi: Taxis are available at the airport and in Palma, but can be expensive.

How to Get Around in Calvià

Visitors can get around Calvià by bus, taxi, or rental car. The town is also small enough to explore by foot or bicycle.

Where to Go Next from Calvià: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Palma de Mallorca: The capital city of Mallorca, featuring a stunning cathedral and numerous cultural attractions.
  • Sóller: A charming town located on the northwest coast of Mallorca, famous for its citrus groves and historic tramway.

Conclusion

Calvià is a beautiful town located in the scenic Serra de Tramuntana mountain range of the Balearic Islands. The town offers a range of cultural, natural, and outdoor attractions, making it an ideal destination for travelers looking for a relaxing vacation or an adventurous excursion. By following the tips and advice in this guide, visitors can enjoy a safe and enjoyable visit to this beautiful town.
