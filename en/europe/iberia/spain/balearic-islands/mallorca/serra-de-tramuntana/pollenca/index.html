Introduction to Pollença

Located in the picturesque Serra de Tramuntana mountain range on the island of Mallorca in Spain’s Balearic Islands, Pollença is a charming town known for its rich history, natural beauty, and vibrant culture. From exploring ancient ruins to hiking through scenic landscapes to indulging in delicious local cuisine, there is no shortage of things to see and do in Pollença.

List of Places to Visit in Pollença

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Museu de Pollença: Housed in a beautiful 17th-century convent, this museum features artefacts and exhibits that depict the town’s history and culture.
  • El Calvari: A stunning 365-step staircase that leads up to a small chapel and offers spectacular views of the town and surrounding countryside.
  • Roman Bridge: A well-preserved Roman bridge built in the 1st century.

Natural Attractions of Pollença: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Puig de Maria: A hilltop monastery and popular hiking destination with sweeping views of the town and mountains.
  • Cala Sant Vicenç: A stunning beach surrounded by rocky cliffs and crystal-clear water, perfect for swimming and snorkelling.
  • Formentor Peninsula: A rugged and wild region with stunning viewpoints, hiking opportunities, and idyllic beaches.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Pollença

  • GR-221: A long-distance trail that runs through Serra de Tramuntana and passes through beautiful mountain villages, wild landscapes, and ancient ruins.
  • Talaia d’Alcudia: A moderate hike that offers stunning views of the bay of Pollença and surrounding mountains.
  • Lluc Monastery: A challenging yet rewarding hike that takes you through a dense forest and up to a stunningly beautiful monastery.

Best Things to Do in Pollença: Activities and Tours

  • Bike Tour: Explore the town and surrounding countryside on two wheels with a guided bike tour.
  • Kayaking Trip: Paddle through the crystal-clear waters of Cala Sant Vicenç on a guided kayaking trip.
  • Wine Tasting Tour: Sample delicious local wines and learn about the region’s wine-making history on a guided wine tasting tour.

Week-Long Itinerary for Pollença

Day Activity
Day 1 Explore the town and visit the Museu de Pollença and El Calvari
Day 2 Hike to the top of Puig de Maria monastery
Day 3 Visit the Roman Bridge and spend the afternoon at Cala Sant Vicenç
Day 4 Explore the Formentor Peninsula and its beaches
Day 5 Take a guided bike tour of the town and surrounding countryside
Day 6 Sample local wines on a guided wine tasting tour
Day 7 Relax on the beach or hike one of the many trails in Serra de Tramuntana

Food and Drink Scene of Pollença

Must-Try Dishes

  • Tumbet: A vegetarian dish made with potatoes, peppers, and aubergine.
  • Lechona: Slow-roasted suckling pig marinated with a blend of spices and herbs.
  • Sobrassada: A soft cured sausage made with pork, paprika, and other spices.

Local Beverages

  • Hierbas: A local herbal liqueur made with a blend of aromatic herbs and spices.
  • Mallorcan Wine: Pollença is situated in one of Spain’s most renowned wine regions, and there are many vineyards and wineries in the area worth visiting.

Where to Stay in Pollença: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Hotel Cala Sant Vicenç: A luxurious hotel located just a short walk from the stunning Cala Sant Vicenç.
  • Pollentia Hotel: A charming hotel with a central location in Pollença’s old town.
  • Hostal Paris: A budget-friendly guesthouse with comfortable accommodations and friendly staff.

What to Buy in Pollença: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Handicrafts: Pollença is known for its traditional handicrafts, including ceramics, textiles, and basketry.
  • Olive Oil: Mallorca produces some of Spain’s best olive oils, and you can find a variety of local brands in Pollença’s shops and markets.
  • Hierbas: A bottle of the local herb liqueur makes a great souvenir and an even better gift.

Local Culture and Customs of Pollença

Pollença is a town steeped in history and tradition, and it is important to respect the local culture and customs. Visitors can expect to find a relaxed and friendly atmosphere, but should be mindful of local traditions and etiquette.

Best Time to Visit Pollença

The best time to visit Pollença is from April to June and from September to October, when the weather is mild and the crowds are smaller. July and August can be very busy and hot, but also offer the most vibrant and lively atmosphere.

Do’s and Don’ts of Pollença

Do:

  • Do respect the local culture and customs, particularly when visiting churches or religious sites.
  • Do greet people with a friendly “hola” or “bon dia” (good day).
  • Do take advantage of the many outdoor activities and adventures that Pollença offers, from hiking to beaches to watersports.

Don’t:

  • Don’t neglect to dress appropriately when visiting churches or other religious sites.
  • Don’t be overly loud or boisterous in public places.
  • Don’t expect everyone to speak English; it’s always polite to learn a few basic Spanish phrases before visiting Pollença.

Safety and Health

Safety in Pollença

Pollença is a safe destination for travellers, with a low crime rate and friendly locals. Visitors should always be aware of their surroundings, however, particularly in crowded areas or at night.

Health in Pollença

The Spanish healthcare system is generally of a high standard, and there are several medical clinics and hospitals in and around Pollença. Visitors should make sure they have adequate health insurance before travelling and should bring any necessary medications with them.

Getting to Pollença: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Pollença

  • Palma de Mallorca Airport: Located approximately 60 km from Pollença, this airport is served by a number of international and domestic airlines.

Bus Stations Near Pollença

  • Pollença Bus Station: Located in the centre of town, this bus station provides regular services to other destinations in Mallorca.

Other Options

  • Car Rental: Visitors can rent a car at the airport or in Pollença to explore the island at their own pace.

How to Get Around in Pollença

Pollença is a small town that is easily navigable by foot. Visitors who wish to explore the surrounding countryside can rent a bike, car, or moped, or take a guided tour.

Where to Go Next from Pollença: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Alcudia: A charming medieval town located just a few kilometres from Pollença.
  • Palma de Mallorca: The capital city of Mallorca, known for its stunning Gothic cathedral, vibrant nightlife, and excellent shopping.
  • Serra de Tramuntana: This mountain range is a natural wonderland of rugged peaks, verdant forests, and sparkling waterfalls.

Conclusion

Pollença is an enchanting destination that offers a rich blend of culture, history, and natural beauty. Whether you are interested in hiking, beachcombing, or exploring local cuisine and culture, Pollença has something for everyone. Start planning your trip today and discover the many wonders of this charming Spanish town.
