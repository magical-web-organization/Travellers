Introduction to Llevant de Mallorca

Llevant de Mallorca is a stunning region located on the eastern side of the island of Mallorca, part of the Balearic Islands in Spain. It is known for its picturesque landscapes, pristine beaches, and rich cultural heritage. With its small charming towns, historic landmarks, and outdoor activities, Llevant de Mallorca draws visitors from across the world.

In this comprehensive travel guide, we’ll explore the top places to visit in Llevant de Mallorca, the best activities to do, the food and drink scene, local customs and traditions, and much more.

List of Places to Visit in Llevant de Mallorca

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Llevant de Mallorca has a rich cultural heritage, and you can explore it by visiting museums, historic sites, and landmarks. Here are the top attractions in this category:

  • The Castle of Capdepera: Perched on a hilltop overlooking the town, this is an impressive fortified castle dating back to the 14th century. It’s a perfect place to soak up some history and take in the stunning views.

  • Talayotic Settlement of Ses Païsses: This is an ancient settlement of the Talayotic culture, which flourished in the Balearic Islands from 2,000 BC to 123 BC. The site is well-preserved and fascinating to explore.

  • Museum of Art and History of Capdepera: This museum is located inside the castle of Capdepera and showcases historical artifacts, art pieces, and information about the town’s history.

Natural Attractions of Llevant de Mallorca: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Llevant de Mallorca is a paradise for nature lovers and outdoor enthusiasts. Here are some of the top natural attractions:

  • Cala Ratjada Beach: Located near the town of Cala Ratjada, this is an idyllic white-sand beach with crystal-clear water, perfect for swimming, sunbathing, or simply relaxing.

  • Parc Natural de Llevant: This nature reserve is an unspoiled wilderness of hilly terrain, cliffs, and Mediterranean vegetation. It’s a great place for hiking, birdwatching, or simply enjoying the stunning views.

  • Caves of Artà: Located near the town of Capdepera, this is a natural cave system with spectacular stalactites and stalagmites formations, said to be over a million years old.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Llevant de Mallorca

Llevant de Mallorca is an excellent destination for hiking and biking enthusiasts. Here are some of the best trails:

  • Ruta Artà-Lluc: This is a challenging hike that takes you along the coastline and up to the highest summit in the area, Puig de Randa. The views from the top are worth the effort.

  • Ruta de las Ermitas: This is a gentle hike that takes you through some of the most picturesque towns and landmarks in the area, including the Talayotic settlements and the Chapel of Santuario de Cura.

Best Things to Do in Llevant de Mallorca: Activities and Tours

Here are some of the top activities and tours you can do in Llevant de Mallorca:

  • Wine Tasting Tour: The region is famous for its wines, and you can explore the local vineyards and wineries with a guided tour. You can sample some of the finest wines and learn about the production process.

  • Snorkeling and Diving: With its crystal-clear water and diverse marine life, Llevant de Mallorca is an excellent destination for snorkeling and diving. You can explore the underwater world with a guided tour.

  • Boat Tours: You can explore the coastline and nearby islands with a guided boat tour, which usually includes stops at beautiful beaches and coves.

Week-Long Itinerary for Llevant de Mallorca

Here is a suggested week-long itinerary for Llevant de Mallorca:

  • Day 1: Explore the historic Castle of Capdepera and Museum of Art and History of Capdepera.

  • Day 2: Visit the Talayotic Settlement of Ses Païsses and hike around Parc Natural de Llevant.

  • Day 3: Take a tour of local vineyards and wineries and sample some of the finest wines in the region.

  • Day 4: Explore the stunning Caves of Artà and enjoy some beach time at Cala Ratjada.

  • Day 5: Do a snorkeling or diving trip and explore the rich marine life of the region.

  • Day 6: Hike the Ruta Artà-Lluc and enjoy the stunning views from the summit.

  • Day 7: Take a boat tour of the coastline, explore some hidden beaches and enjoy the Mediterranean scenery.

Food and Drink Scene of Llevant de Mallorca

Llevant de Mallorca is a gastronomic paradise, known for its fresh seafood, traditional dishes, and local wines. Here are some of the must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Sopes Mallorquines: A hearty stew with vegetables, meat, and bread, traditionally cooked over an open fire.

  • Sobrasada: A cured sausage made from ground pork, paprika, and other spices. It’s usually served with bread and cheese.

  • Frito Mallorquin: A stir-fry of pork meat and vegetables, seasoned with garlic, paprika, and other local spices.

Local Beverages

  • Hierbas: A traditional liqueur made from herbs, usually served as an after-dinner drink.

  • Mallorcan Wine: The region is known for its wines, including the famous Binissalem and Pla i Llevant varieties.

Where to Stay in Llevant de Mallorca: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Llevant de Mallorca offers a range of accommodation options to suit different budgets and tastes. Here are some of the top choices:

  • Hotel Casa Toni: This charming guesthouse is located in the heart of Capdepera, with comfortable rooms and a beautiful garden terrace.

  • Hotel Cases de Son Barbassa: This luxurious hotel is nestled in the picturesque hills of Llevant de Mallorca, with stunning views and a range of amenities.

What to Buy in Llevant de Mallorca: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Llevant de Mallorca offers a range of unique souvenirs and local products to bring back home, including:

  • Pearls: The region is famous for its high-quality pearls, which make unique and elegant gifts.

  • Handmade Crafts: You can find a range of traditional crafts, including pottery, textiles, and woodwork, made by local artisans.

Local Culture and Customs of Llevant de Mallorca

Llevant de Mallorca has a rich cultural heritage, with a strong influence from the Talayotic and Moorish cultures. Some of the local customs and traditions include:

  • Festivals: The region hosts many traditional festivals throughout the year, including the Sant Antoni festival in January and the Fira de la Flor Mallorca in May.

  • Music and Dance: You can experience the local music and dance traditions at festivals and cultural events.

Best Time to Visit Llevant de Mallorca

The best time to visit Llevant de Mallorca is between May and October, when the weather is warm and sunny and there are many outdoor activities and festivals to enjoy.

Safety and Health

Safety in Llevant de Mallorca

Llevant de Mallorca is a safe destination for tourists, with low crime rates and a friendly local population.

Health in Llevant de Mallorca

There are no major health risks or concerns in Llevant de Mallorca, but it’s always a good idea to have comprehensive travel insurance and take basic precautions.

Getting to Llevant de Mallorca: Transportation Options and Logistics

Llevant de Mallorca is easily accessible by different modes of transportation. Here are some options:

  • Airports Near Llevant de Mallorca: The nearest airport is Palma de Mallorca Airport, which is about an hour’s drive from the region.

  • Bus Stations Near Llevant de Mallorca: You can take the bus from Palma to the towns in Llevant de Mallorca, with frequent services throughout the day.

  • Other Options: You can also rent a car, take a taxi, or use a private transfer service to reach Llevant de Mallorca from other parts of the island or from the mainland.

How to Get Around in Llevant de Mallorca

The best way to get around Llevant de Mallorca is by car or bike, as public transportation options are limited. You can also join guided tours or hikes to explore the region’s attractions.

Where to Go Next from Llevant de Mallorca: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

If you’re looking to explore nearby destinations from Llevant de Mallorca, here are some suggestions:

  • Cala Millor: Located just 20 minutes’ drive from Llevant de Mallorca, this is a popular resort town with a long sandy beach and lively nightlife.

  • Palma de Mallorca: The capital city of the island is located about an hour’s drive from Llevant de Mallorca, with stunning architecture, museums, and cultural attractions.

  • Menorca: You can take a ferry from nearby towns to the neighboring island of Menorca, known for its pristine beaches and unspoiled natural beauty.

Conclusion

Llevant de Mallorca is a hidden gem of Mallorca, offering stunning natural beauty, rich cultural heritage, and a range of activities and attractions for visitors to enjoy. With this comprehensive travel guide, you can make the most of your visit and experience the best of this beautiful region.
