Introduction to Alcudia

Alcudia is a picturesque town located in the north of Mallorca, the largest of the Balearic Islands in Spain. It is a popular tourist destination known for its unspoiled beaches, charming Old Town, and agreeable Mediterranean climate.

Alcudia has a rich history that dates back to the 13th century when it was founded by the Moors. Since then, it has seen numerous invasions, attacks, and battles and has a mix of architectural styles that tell the town’s fascinating past.

List of Places to Visit in Alcudia

There are several places to visit in Alcudia, ranging from culturally important attractions to outdoor adventure sites. Here are a few of the must-visit destinations:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • The Roman ruins of Pollentia: the remnants of a Roman settlement that highlight the historical importance of Alcudia
  • Museo de Sa Bassa Blanca: a contemporary art museum set in a former wine estate
  • The walls of Alcudia: the medieval walls that encircle the city
  • Church of Saint Jaume: a beautiful example of Gothic architecture that dates back to the 13th century
  • The Old Town of Alcudia: a maze of charming streets and squares surrounded by ancient walls

Natural Attractions of Alcudia: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • S’Albufera Natural Park: a breathtaking natural park that stretches through wetlands, dunes, forests, and lagoons
  • Formentor Cape: a scenic drive with breathtaking views of the Mediterranean Sea, where you’ll find secluded beaches and coves
  • Playa de Alcudia: a long, sandy beach that stretches for miles and a great place to sunbathe and relax
  • Paradise Gin & Tonic Boat: a family-friendly, fun-filled boat ride with opportunities to swim, snorkel, and enjoy drinks onboard
  • Kayaking Adventure: rent a kayak and explore the waters of the Bay of Pollensa, discover hidden beaches, and have a unique experience exploring the coastline.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Alcudia

Alcudia has many picturesque hiking and biking routes. La Victoria Monastery is an ancient Gothic construction where you can enjoy a good view of Alcudia Bay. The path to it passes through olive groves, and the shade of the trees provides respite from the heat during summer.

There is a biking route that goes from Alcudia to Puerto Pollensa through an impressive road that goes along the sea coast and takes you to one of the most remarkable viewpoints in Mallorca. The ride takes approximately sixty minutes.

Best Things to Do in Alcudia: Activities and Tours

There are many activities and tours available to enjoy in Alcudia, including:

  • Boat Tours: enjoy a day at sea with scenic boat tours available throughout the year
  • Stand-Up Paddleboarding: hire a paddleboard and paddle up and down the coast
  • Horse-Riding: enjoy a day out horse-riding through the scenic rural landscape
  • Hot Air Balloon Ride: enjoy the views of the island from high up in the sky
  • Albufera Natural Park Guided Tour: discover the stunning scenery and wildlife on offer in this beautiful natural park.

Week-Long Itinerary for Alcudia

For those with a week to spare, here is an itinerary to maximise your sightseeing and relaxation:

Day One: Explore the Old Town of Alcudia, Museum Sa Bassa Blanca, and Sa Portassa tower.

Day Two: Take a boat trip to Pollensa Bay The trip includes swimming in the crystal-clear waters of Cap Formentor.

Day Three: Hike Victoria’s mountain and end the day relaxing in one of the secluded bays of Vall d’en Marc/ Cala Castell.

Day Four: Visit the natural park of S’Albufera in the morning. In the afternoon, take a bike tour of the area around the park.

Day Five: Visit the nearby towns of Pollensa and Caves of Campanet with an organized tour (optional).

Day Six: Explore the coast and secluded bays of the Formentor Peninsula by car or excursion.

Day Seven: Spend the last day leisurely, sunbathing on the Alcudia beach and soaking up the last rays of the Mediterranean sun.

Food and Drink Scene of Alcudia

As a consequence of its historical past and geographic location, Alcudia’s cuisine is a delicious mix of Moorish, Mediterranean, and Spanish flavors. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages to sample during your stay:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Arros Brut: a traditional rice dish with meat, vegetables, and saffron
  • Sopas Mallorquines: a hearty and nourishing soup made with vegetables and bread
  • Coca de Alcudia: a sweet pastry filled with almonds, cottage cheese, and pumpkin crema

Local Beverages

  • Hierbas: a traditional liqueur made from herbs, often enjoyed as a digestive after meals
  • Wine: Mallorca produces several excellent wines, including the popular red Binissalem

Where to Stay in Alcudia: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Alcudia offers a variety of accommodation options to suit all budgets, including luxury hotels, cozy guesthouses, and affordable hostels. These are some of the best picks:

  • For luxury travelers: Hotel Santo Domingo, Hotel Can Mostatxins, Alcudia Petit Hotel
  • For budget travelers: Alcudia Garden Aparthotel, Apartamentos Carlos V, Club Mac

What to Buy in Alcudia: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

There are plenty of souvenirs and local products to take home from Alcudia. You can typically find them in the gift shops in the old town or in the local markets. Here are some unique and authentic items:

  • Pearls: Mallorca produces several types of pearls, and you can find them at the local jewelry shops
  • Pottery: Mallorcan pottery is famous for its unique designs and colors
  • Olives and Olive Oil: Mallorca is home to several olive groves, and you can purchase olive oil and olives harvested from the region.

Local Culture and Customs of Alcudia

The people of Alcudia are friendly and welcoming, and Spanish is the main language spoken. Visitors should respect the local customs and be aware of the dress codes when visiting the religious and cultural sites.

Best Time to Visit Alcudia

The best time to visit Alcudia is during the shoulder seasons of late spring (May-June) or early autumn (September-October). The weather is still warm, but the crowds are smaller. July and August are typically the hottest months, and tourist numbers are at their peak.

Do’s and Don’ts of Alcudia

Do:

  • Do try the local cuisine: Alcudia’s cuisine is a unique blend of Spanish and Moorish flavors it’s worth trying
  • Do make time to explore the Old Town.: The charming streets and squares in the Old Town are gorgeous and full of texture.
  • Do bring appropriate clothing: Be respectful and aware of your surroundings; cover-up when visiting cultural and religious sites.

Don’t:

  • Don’t be excessively loud or disruptive: Be respectful of the locals and the surrounding environment
  • Don’t drink tap water: tap water is not recommended for drinking purposes; bottled water is widely available.
  • Don’t litter: Respect the nature around you, and don’t leave any trash behind.

Safety and Health

Safety in Alcudia

Alcudia is a safe place to visit, and crimes against tourists are rare. However, it is essential to take the necessary precautions like keeping valuables in a safe place and not leaving them unattended in public areas. It is also essential to be careful on the roads, which can be winding.

Health in Alcudia

For visitors from countries outside of the European Union, vaccinations might be necessary. Check with your doctor to see what is required. Bring sunscreen and avoid direct sunlight at peak hours, as the sun can be intense.

Getting to Alcudia: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Alcudia

Palma de Mallorca Airport is the closest airport to Alcudia, located approximately 60 km from the town. From the airport, you can hire a car or take a taxi to Alcudia. There are also bus services available at the airport.

Bus Stations Near Alcudia

The Alcudia Bus Station is located close to the center of Alcudia town and is well-connected to the rest of Mallorca. There are several bus services available that connect to nearby towns and cities, including Palma.

Other Options

Hire cars are a widespread means of getting around the island of Mallorca. Some visitors prefer getting around on a bike, which is an excellent way to take in the scenic views around Alcudia town.

How to Get Around in Alcudia

There are several means of getting around in Alcudia, including bus services and car hire. Visitors can also explore the town on foot or rent a bicycle/motorbike for a more authentic and flexible experience.

Where to Go Next from Alcudia: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

There are several nearby destinations that visitors can explore from Alcudia, including:

  • Palma de Mallorca: the capital of Mallorca and home to several notable sights, including the Almudaina Palace, Bellver Castle and numerous art museums
  • The Caves of Drach: a popular tourist spot with cave tours, underground lakes, and concerts
  • The Port of Pollenca: a small fishing village with a beach, a scenic promenade, and numerous cafes and restaurants.

Conclusion

Alcudia is a stunning destination with something to offer every visitor. From historical and cultural sites to scenic natural parks, this town is full of unique experiences and unforgettable moments. Plan your trip to Alcudia with this comprehensive guide to ensure that you get the most out of your stay.
