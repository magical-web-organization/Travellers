If you’re looking for a hidden gem in Spain, Barbastro is the place to be. Located in the province of Huesca in Aragon, Barbastro is nestled in between the Pyrenees Mountains and the Ebro River Valley. With a population of just over 16,000 and a rich history dating back to the Roman era, Barbastro is a great destination for those looking to experience authentic Spanish culture. In this guide, we’ll help you plan your trip to Barbastro by outlining the best places to visit, things to do, and eat.

Introduction to Barbastro

Barbastro is a small but vibrant city that offers a mix of historic charm and modern amenities. Visitors will find plenty to explore in the city’s well-preserved old town, which is home to many of its top attractions. From Roman ruins to Gothic cathedrals, there’s no shortage of cultural landmarks to discover in this town.

List of Places to Visit in Barbastro

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Barbastro is home to a variety of museums and historical landmarks that offer a glimpse into the region’s rich cultural heritage. One must-visit museum is the Diocesan Museum of Barbastro-Monzón, which houses an impressive collection of religious art and artifacts from the Romanesque and Gothic eras. Other notable attractions include the Barbastro Cathedral, a beautiful Gothic-style church, and the Palacio Episcopal (Episcopal Palace), which dates back to the 16th century and features stunning Renaissance architecture.

Natural Attractions of Barbastro: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

For outdoor enthusiasts, Barbastro offers plenty of opportunities to explore the region’s natural beauty. The Sierra y Cañones de Guara Natural Park, located just a short drive away, is a must-visit destination for outdoor adventures. This park features stunning canyons, cliffs, and landscapes that offer breathtaking views and outdoor activities like hiking, climbing, and canyoning. The nearby Ebro River Valley, on the other hand, is the perfect place to go kayaking, fishing, or birdwatching.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Barbastro

Barbastro is also an ideal location for hiking and biking enthusiasts. The Natural Park of Sierra y Cañones de Guara offers many trails with varying degrees of difficulty and distances, making it accessible for hikers and bikers of all skill levels. One popular trail is the Barranco de la Pillera, a 9.5 km trek that offers stunning views of the park’s canyons and cliffs.

Best Things to Do in Barbastro: Activities and Tours

Barbastro offers plenty of activities and tours to keep visitors entertained. From wine-tasting tours and culinary experiences to guided city walks and adventure tours, there’s something for everyone. One unique activity to try is the “Vineyards from Above” tour, which offers a hot air balloon ride over the Somontano wine region and a visit to a local winery. Another popular option is the “Taste of Barbastro” culinary tour, which offers a chance to taste some of the region’s best food and wine.

Week-Long Itinerary for Barbastro

For those planning a week-long trip to Barbastro, here’s a suggested itinerary:

Day 1: Explore the historic old town of Barbastro and visit the Barbastro Cathedral and Diocesan Museum.

Day 2: Take a trip to the Sierra y Cañones de Guara Natural Park and spend the day hiking or biking.

Day 3: Visit the nearby town of Alquezar and explore its well-preserved medieval streets and castle.

Day 4: Take a wine-tasting tour of the Somontano wine region and visit a local winery.

Day 5: Enjoy a day of kayaking or fishing on the Ebro River Valley.

Day 6: Take a hot air balloon ride over the Somontano wine region and enjoy a visit to a local winery.

Day 7: Visit the Palacio Episcopal and the Santuario de la Misericordia (Sanctuary of Mercy), and enjoy some of the best food and wine in the region.

Food and Drink Scene of Barbastro

Barbastro is also well-known for its delicious cuisine and wine scene. Some of the must-try local dishes include Migas (a traditional bread dish), Longaniza (a sausage made with pork and paprika), and Ternasco (lamb roasted in the oven). When it comes to local beverages, the Somontano wine region is a must-visit destination. This region is known for producing high-quality wines, such as the Garnacha and Tempranillo, and offers many chances to try the wine at local wineries.

Where to Stay in Barbastro: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Barbastro has plenty of options when it comes to accommodation. From cozy guesthouses to luxurious hotels, there’s something for every budget and preference. One popular option is the Hotel Ciudad de Barbastro, which offers comfortable, modern rooms and a convenient location in the heart of the city. Another great option is the Casa Clavería, a charming guesthouse with traditional Spanish décor and cozy rooms.

What to Buy in Barbastro: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

If you’re looking to bring home some unique local products from Barbastro, here are some ideas:

  • Wine from the Somontano wine region
  • Local cheeses and cured meats
  • Handmade ceramic dishes and pottery
  • Olive oil and vinegar from local producers

Local Culture and Customs of Barbastro

Barbastro has a rich and vibrant culture that’s rooted in its history and traditions. One example is the annual Fiesta del Vino (Wine Festival), which takes place in late August and celebrates the region’s wine culture with parades, wine tastings, and live music. Another tradition is the Holy Week celebrations, which feature processions, concerts, and traditional Spanish food.

Best Time to Visit Barbastro

The best time to visit Barbastro is during the spring or fall months, when the weather is mild and the crowds are smaller. However, if you’re interested in attending one of the town’s festivals or events, it’s best to check the dates beforehand as they can vary from year to year.

Do’s and Don’ts of Barbastro

Here are some do’s and don’ts to keep in mind during your visit to Barbastro:

Do:

  • Try the local cuisine and wine
  • Explore the historic old town and its landmarks
  • Attend one of the town’s festivals or events
  • Pack light clothing for warm weather

Don’t:

  • Forget to respect local customs and traditions
  • Wear revealing clothing when visiting churches or religious sites
  • Leave your valuables unattended

Safety and Health

Safety in Barbastro

Barbastro is generally a safe destination for tourists and has a low crime rate. However, visitors should still take standard precautionary measures, such as not leaving valuables unattended and staying aware of their surroundings.

Health in Barbastro

Barbastro has a modern healthcare system and visitors can expect to receive quality medical care if needed. However, it’s important to have travel insurance that covers medical emergencies.

Getting to Barbastro: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Barbastro

The closest airport to Barbastro is the Zaragoza Airport (ZAZ), which is approximately 144 km away.

Bus Stations Near Barbastro

Barbastro has a central bus station that connects to many of the nearby towns and cities.

Other Options

Another option is to rent a car and drive to Barbastro. This can be a great way to explore the surrounding areas and to visit the nearby Natural Park of Sierra y Cañones de Guara.

How to Get Around in Barbastro

Barbastro is a small town that’s easy to navigate on foot or by bike. However, if you’re looking to explore the surrounding areas, renting a car or taking a bus are good options.

Where to Go Next from Barbastro: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

If you’re looking to continue your adventure in Spain after leaving Barbastro, here are some nearby destinations to consider:

  • Ainsa
  • Ribagorza
  • Huesca

Conclusion

Barbastro is a hidden gem that offers a mix of history, culture, outdoor adventures, and delicious cuisine. With its convenient location, friendly locals, and rich culture, it’s a destination that’s sure to leave a lasting impression on any visitor. Whether you’re interested in hiking, wine-tasting, or exploring historic landmarks, Barbastro has something for everyone. We hope this guide helps you plan your trip to this charming Spanish town!
