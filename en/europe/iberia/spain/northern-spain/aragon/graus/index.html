A Comprehensive Travel Guide to Graus, Spain

Nestled in the northern part of Spain’s Aragon province, Graus is a charming town that holds much historical and cultural significance in the region. With beautiful natural landscapes, historic architecture, and a rich food and drink scene, Graus has much to offer its visitors. In this comprehensive travel guide, we will take a deeper look into the top places to visit, things to do, where to stay, and more in Graus, Spain.

List of Places to Visit in Graus

Here are some of the top places to visit during a trip to Graus:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Museo de Historia: This museum provides a detailed look into the history of Graus and the surrounding areas. With exhibits showcasing the town’s Roman and medieval past, it’s a must-visit for history enthusiasts.
  • La Plaza Mayor: A picturesque town square with stunning medieval architecture that has been preserved over the centuries.
  • La Casa de la Cultura: A beautiful, historic building that serves as a cultural center, hosting various exhibitions, performances, and events throughout the year.

Natural Attractions of Graus: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Sierra y Cañones de Guara Natural Park: With stunning canyons, cliffs, and rock formations, this natural park provides incredible opportunities for outdoor adventures like hiking, rock climbing, and canyoning.
  • Pico Turbón: A stunning mountain peak with breathtaking views from the summit. Take a hike to the top for an unforgettable experience.
  • Embalse de Barasona: A beautiful lake surrounded by rolling hills, ideal for a relaxing day of swimming, fishing, or simply enjoying the beauty of the Spanish countryside.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Graus

The beautiful mountain ranges and natural parks around Graus provide countless opportunities for outdoor enthusiasts to explore. Some popular hiking and biking routes to explore include:

  • GR 1: A long-distance hiking trail that passes through Graus and provides a glimpse of the stunning Pyrenees mountain range.
  • Senda de Camille: A challenging hiking route that ventures deep into the canyons of Guara Natural Park.
  • BTT Zona Zero Graus: This extensive network of mountain bike trails around Graus is suited for all skill levels, from beginners to experienced riders.

Best Things to Do in Graus: Activities and Tours

Besides hiking and biking, there are plenty of other activities to do in Graus, such as:

  • Wine Tour and Tasting: Taste the unique local wines and learn about the wine-making process on a guided tour of the local wineries.
  • Hot Air Balloon Ride: Take in the stunning Spanish countryside from above on a relaxing hot air balloon ride.
  • Cultural Walking Tour: Discover the fascinating history and architecture of Graus on a guided walking tour of the town.

Week-Long Itinerary for Graus

Here is a sample itinerary for a week-long visit to Graus:

Day Activity
1 Arrival in Graus, evening stroll around La Plaza Mayor
2 Visit to Museo de Historia and Casa de la Cultura, cultural walking tour
3 Hike to Pico Turbón, evening wine tasting tour

7 Hot air balloon ride, departure from Graus

Food and Drink Scene of Graus

A visit to Graus isn’t complete without indulging in the local cuisine and drinks.

Must-Try Dishes

  • Chiretas: A traditional Aragonese dish made with lamb intestine stuffed with rice, onions, and spices.
  • Trinxat: A hearty potato and cabbage dish that’s common in the mountainous regions of Spain.
  • Ternasco de Aragón: Slow-roasted lamb served with potatoes, a true testament to the region’s culinary heritage.

Local Beverages

  • Somontano Wines: Made from local grapes, Somontano wines are known for their unique and complex flavors.
  • Cerveza Rondadora: A local beer produced in Graus that is known for its refreshing taste and crisp finish.

Where to Stay in Graus: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Graus has a range of different accommodation options to suit any traveler’s needs. Here are some recommendations:

  • Hotel Palacio del Obispo: Located in a historic palace in the heart of Graus, this hotel offers elegance and luxury for travelers seeking a more upscale experience.
  • Hostal Casa Barranco: A charming guesthouse with comfortable rooms and stunning mountain views.
  • Camping Las Clotas Graus: A family-friendly option for travelers who prefer a more rustic experience, with campsites and bungalows surrounded by nature.

What to Buy in Graus: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Saffron: Graus is known for producing high-quality saffron, perfect for adding a burst of flavor to your cooking at home.
  • Honey: The surrounding areas around Graus are home to an abundance of unique wildflowers, making it the perfect place for producing delicious honey.
  • Pantorrillas: These delicious, crisp wafers are a popular snack in the Aragon region and make a great souvenir to take home with you.

Local Culture and Customs of Graus

Graus has a rich and diverse cultural heritage, with various traditional customs and events held throughout the year. One of the most significant is the Compromiso de Caspe, a medieval ceremony that commemorates the election of a new king of Aragon.

Best Time to Visit Graus

The best time to visit Graus is during the summer months when the weather is warm and pleasant, making it the perfect time to take advantage of the many outdoor activities that the town has to offer. Spring and fall are also great seasons to visit when the weather is mild and the landscapes are painted in vibrant colors.

Do’s and Don’ts of Graus

Do:

  • Try the local cuisine and wines
  • Respect the local customs and traditions
  • Take advantage of the many outdoor activities

Don’t:

  • Go off-trail in natural parks without a guide
  • Disrespect historic landmarks or architecture

Safety and Health

Safety in Graus

Graus is a relatively safe town, with low crime rates and friendly locals. However, visitors should take the same precautions that they would in any other unfamiliar place, such as being aware of their surroundings and keeping valuables safe.

Health in Graus

Graus has several medical clinics and pharmacies for minor health concerns. In case of an emergency, dial 112 for immediate assistance.

Getting to Graus: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Graus

The nearest airport to Graus is Barcelona-El Prat Airport, which is about 200km away.

Bus Stations Near Graus

Alsa Bus offers daily bus services to Graus from Barcelona and Zaragoza.

Other Options

Renting a car is a great option for travelers who prefer to have more freedom and flexibility during their trip.

How to Get Around in Graus

Graus is a relatively small town that can easily be explored on foot. For travelers who prefer to travel further afield, renting a car or joining a guided tour is a good option.

Where to Go Next from Graus: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Benasque: Located in the heart of the Pyrenees, this charming town is known for its stunning natural landscapes and world-class skiing.
  • Barbastro: A beautiful town that is known for producing some of Spain’s finest wines.
  • Huesca: A bustling city with numerous historic attractions and an exciting cultural scene.

Conclusion

Graus, Spain, is a hidden gem that is steeped in history, culture, and natural beauty. Whether you’re looking for outdoor adventures, a taste of local cuisine, or an immersive cultural experience, Graus is the perfect destination for a memorable vacation. With this comprehensive travel guide, you’re ready to start planning your trip to this charming town!
