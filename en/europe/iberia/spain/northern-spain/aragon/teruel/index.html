Teruel is a beautiful city in the Teruel Province of Aragon, Northern Spain. The city boasts a rich cultural heritage, stunning art and architecture, beautiful natural landscape, vibrant food and drink scene, and a relaxed, welcoming atmosphere that beckons visitors with open arms. This comprehensive travel guide will provide you with everything you need to know to explore Teruel to the fullest.

Introduction to Teruel

Teruel is a medieval town that was once a prosperous city under the Moors. Over the years, it has witnessed several battles and invasions, which led to the construction of several fortified walls, towers, and castles that can still be seen today. Teruel is known for its Mudéjar architecture, a style characterized by the combination of Islamic and Christian elements, which is a fusion unique to the Iberian Peninsula. Teruel has also been declared a UNESCO World Heritage Site due to the numerous examples of Mudéjar art found throughout the city.

List of Places to Visit in Teruel

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Teruel Cathedral: The cathedral is a significant example of Mudéjar architecture, and it includes a tower that was once part of a mosque. One of the cathedral’s most notable features is its wooden ceiling, which is one of the oldest examples of Mudéjar art in Spain.

  • Tower of El Salvador: The tower is another example of Mudéjar architecture that is unique to Teruel. The tower consists of nine sections, each built in a different style, and it is one of the most iconic landmarks of the city.

  • The Mausoleum of the Lovers of Teruel: The mausoleum is dedicated to two lovers whose story has become a symbol of love and devotion in Spain. The mausoleum is located in the church of San Pedro and is a popular tourist attraction.

  • Museo Provincial de Teruel: The museum displays a collection of religious art, Roman and Iberian artifacts, and art pieces from local sculptors and painters.

Natural Attractions of Teruel: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Fuente del Berro: This natural spring is one of the most beautiful and refreshing places in Teruel. Locals come here to relax, swim, and picnic amongst the beautiful lush greenery.

  • Valle del Jiloca: This valley is a hidden gem in Teruel, offering stunning views and hiking trails. It’s a great place to escape the city and connect with nature.

  • Pico de los Obispos: This peak offers the most exceptional views of Teruel, and it’s worth the hike to the top for the stunning panoramic views.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Teruel

Teruel is a great place for hiking and cycling enthusiasts. The city is surrounded by mountains and valleys, offering some of the most breathtaking views in Spain. Here are some of the best hiking and biking routes in Teruel:

  • El Barranco del Río Blanco: This hiking trail is perfect for beginners, offering an easy route with beautiful views of the surrounding countryside.

  • Pine Forest de Valdelinares: This biking trail is one of the most challenging in Teruel, offering steep ascents and fun descents. It’s a great route for experienced cyclists looking for a challenge.

  • Montes Universales: This hiking trail is excellent for those looking for a more challenging hike. It’s a long and arduous trek, but the views at the end make it all worth it.

Best Things to Do in Teruel: Activities and Tours

Apart from hiking, Teruel offers a variety of activities and tours that cater to all interests. Here are some of the must-do activities and tours in Teruel:

  • Medieval Tours: Teruel offers guided tours of its medieval walls, towers, and castles, allowing visitors to explore the city’s rich history and architecture.

  • Hot Air Balloon Rides: Explore the beauty of Teruel from a different perspective. Hot air balloon rides offer breathtaking views of the city and surrounding countryside.

  • Local Food and Wine Tours: Teruel is known for its delicious gastronomy, and a food and wine tour is a great way to experience the city’s culinary delights.

Week-Long Itinerary for Teruel

Here is a week-long itinerary we recommend for a visit to Teruel:

Day Activity
Day 1 Explore the city center and visit Teruel Cathedral, the Tower of El Salvador, and the Mausoleum of the Lovers.
Day 2 Take a hot air balloon ride over the city in the morning, followed by a visit to the Museo Provincial de Teruel in the afternoon.
Day 3 Hike the beautiful Pico de los Obispos and enjoy the stunning panoramic views of the city.
Day 4 Go on a local food and wine tour in Daroca, a nearby town that is known for its culinary delights.
Day 5 Take a day trip to Albarracín, a beautiful medieval town that is a UNESCO World Heritage Site.
Day 6 Visit Cella, a town known for its Mudéjar architecture, and explore its beautiful churches and buildings.
Day 7 Spend the day relaxing at Fuente del Berro and enjoy the beautiful natural landscape.

Food and Drink Scene of Teruel

Teruel offers some of the best food and drink in Spain, known for its hearty dishes and unique ingredients. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Jamón: Teruel is known for its world-class cured ham, Jamón de Teruel, which is a gourmet delicacy.

  • Migas: A traditional Spanish dish made with bread crumbs, garlic, bacon, and chorizo.

  • Gazpacho: A refreshing cold soup made with tomatoes, peppers, and garlic.

Local Beverages

  • Wine: Teruel is famous for its quality wine, with several wineries offering wine tasting tours and activities.

  • Cerveza: Teruel also has several craft breweries that offer unique and delicious beer.

Where to Stay in Teruel: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Teruel offers several options for accommodation, ranging from luxury hotels to budget-friendly hostels and guesthouses. Here are some of the best places to stay in Teruel:

  • Parador de Teruel: A luxury hotel located in a historic building that offers spacious rooms and stunning views.

  • Hostal el Cartero: A budget-friendly guesthouse located in the city center that offers comfortable rooms and excellent service.

  • Hotel SPA Ciudad de Teruel: A modern hotel with a spa that offers a relaxing stay in Teruel.

What to Buy in Teruel: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Teruel offers several unique souvenirs and local products that visitors can bring home. Here are some of the best products to buy in Teruel:

  • Jamón de Teruel: A gourmet delicacy that makes the perfect gift for foodies.

  • Mudéjar Pottery: Exquisite pieces of pottery made using traditional Mudéjar techniques.

  • Handmade Lace: Beautiful handmade lace pieces that are unique to Teruel.

Local Culture and Customs of Teruel

Teruel has a rich and vibrant culture that is deeply rooted in its history and traditions. Some customs and traditions that visitors should be aware of include:

  • Semana Santa: The Holy Week celebrations in Teruel include processions and reenactments that are a beautiful expression of religious devotion.

  • Festivals: Teruel hosts several festivals throughout the year, including the Festival of La Vaquilla, a popular event that has been celebrated for over 700 years.

Best Time to Visit Teruel

The best time to visit Teruel is during the spring and fall when the weather is mild, and the crowds are thin. Summer can be hot and crowded, while winter can be cold and snowy.

Do’s and Don’ts of Teruel

Do:

  • Respect local customs and traditions.

  • Try the local cuisine and wines.

  • Explore the city’s medieval architecture and art.

Don’t:

  • Snap pictures of religious processions without asking for permission.

  • Disrespect local culture and traditions.

  • Leave your litter behind in the natural areas.

Safety and Health

Safety in Teruel

Teruel is a safe city to visit, but visitors should take normal precautions such as keeping an eye on their belongings while in public places.

Health in Teruel

Teruel has an excellent healthcare system, and visitors from EU countries can receive emergency care under the European Health Insurance Card. Visitors from outside the EU should ensure they have adequate travel insurance before visiting.

Getting to Teruel: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Teruel

The nearest airport to Teruel is Zaragoza Airport, which is approximately 130 kilometers away. From Zaragoza, visitors can take a bus or train to Teruel.

Bus Stations Near Teruel

Teruel has a bus station that offers connections to several Spanish cities, including Madrid and Valencia.

Other Options

Visitors can also reach Teruel by car or train, with regular connections from Madrid and Valencia.

How to Get Around in Teruel

The best way to get around in Teruel is on foot, as most of the city’s attractions are located in the city center and are easily accessible by walking. Visitors can also hire a bike or take public transport in the city center.

Where to Go Next from Teruel: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Teruel is located in Aragon, a region that offers several destinations for visitors to explore. Here are some nearby destinations and excursions that are worth a visit:

  • Albarracín: A beautiful medieval town that is a UNESCO World Heritage Site.

  • Daroca: A nearby town known for its gastronomy and ancient walls.

  • Gúdar-Javalambre: A mountain range that offers several hiking and outdoor activities.

Conclusion

Teruel is a beautiful city that offers a unique blend of history, culture, and natural beauty. From its stunning architectural landmarks to its outdoor adventures and delicious cuisine, Teruel has something to offer for everyone. Plan your visit to Teruel today and experience the magic of this extraordinary city.
