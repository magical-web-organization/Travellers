Introduction to Haro

Haro is a picturesque town in the La Rioja region of Northern Spain, known for its rich history, stunning landscapes, and world-class wine. The town is located in the heart of the Rioja wine region, which is renowned for its red wine production. Haro is an ideal destination for wine lovers, history buffs, and outdoor enthusiasts. This guide covers everything you need to know to plan a visit to Haro.

List of Places to Visit in Haro

  1. Bodegas López de Heredia Viña Tondonia: A historic winery dating back to 1877, this renowned bodega produces some of the finest Rioja wines. Visitors can take a guided tour of the winery and taste the wines.

  2. Basilica de Nuestra Senora de la Vega: This beautiful basilica is the most important religious building in Haro. It is located on a hill overlooking the town and offers stunning panoramic views.

  3. Museo del Torreón: This museum is housed in a medieval tower and showcases the history and culture of Haro and its surroundings.

  4. Plaza de la Paz: This charming square in the heart of Haro is lined with cafes and restaurants and is the perfect place to relax and soak up the atmosphere.

  5. Parque de la Grajera: This beautiful park is located just outside Haro and offers a range of outdoor activities, including hiking, cycling, and picnicking.

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  1. Museo del Torreón: This museum is housed in a historic tower and showcases the history and culture of Haro and its surroundings. It is a must-visit attraction for history buffs.

  2. Bodegas López de Heredia Viña Tondonia: This winery, founded in 1877, is one of the oldest in the region and offers guided tours to visitors. It is an important cultural landmark in Haro.

  3. Basilica de Nuestra Senora de la Vega: This historic basilica is the most important religious building in Haro and offers visitors a glimpse into the town’s rich history.

  4. Palacio de Bendaña: This historic palace is located in the heart of Haro and is a fine example of the town’s architecture and cultural heritage.

Natural Attractions of Haro: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  1. Parque de la Grajera: This beautiful park is perfect for outdoor enthusiasts and offers a range of activities, including hiking, cycling, and picnicking.

  2. Mirador de Toloño: This scenic viewpoint offers stunning panoramic views of the surrounding landscape and is a great spot for photography.

  3. Rioja Alavesa Wine Route: This stunning route takes visitors through the vineyards and wineries of the Rioja wine region and is a must-do for wine lovers and nature enthusiasts.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Haro

  1. Parque de la Grajera: This beautiful park offers a range of hiking and biking trails suitable for all levels of ability.

  2. Rioja Alavesa Wine Route: This scenic route is perfect for cycling and takes visitors through some of the region’s most beautiful landscapes.

Best Things to Do in Haro: Activities and Tours

  1. Wine Tasting Tours: Haro is famous for its red wines, and there are many wineries in the area that offer guided tours and tastings. Some of the most popular are Bodegas Lopez de Heredia, Muga Winery, and Bodegas Bilbainas.

  2. Hot Air Balloon Rides: For those looking for a unique experience, a hot air balloon ride over the Rioja region is a must-do. It offers breathtaking views of the vineyards and surrounding landscape.

  3. Hiking and Biking: Haro and its surroundings offer beautiful hiking and biking routes suitable for all levels of ability. The Parque de la Grajera and Rioja Alavesa Wine Route are popular among visitors.

Food and Drink Scene of Haro

Haro is a foodie’s paradise, and the town is famous for its local cuisine and wine. Some must-try dishes and drinks include:

Must-Try Dishes

  1. Patatas a la Riojana: A hearty potato stew made with chorizo, garlic, and paprika.
  2. Cordero a la Riojana: A flavorful lamb stew made with red wine and local herbs.
  3. Piquillo peppers: Sweet roasted red peppers stuffed with goat cheese or other fillings.

Local Beverages

  1. Rioja Wine: Haro is located in the heart of the Rioja wine region, and there are many wineries in the area that offer tastings and tours. Some of the most popular wines include Tempranillo, Garnacha, and Graciano.
  2. Pacharán: A local liqueur made with sloe berries and aniseed.
  3. Txakoli: A sparkling white wine from the Basque Country that is popular in Haro and the surrounding region.

Where to Stay in Haro: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  1. Hotel los Agustinos: This historic hotel is located in a former convent and offers stylish rooms and a central location.

  2. Hostal Haro: A budget-friendly option, this cozy hostel is located in the heart of Haro and offers comfortable rooms and a friendly atmosphere.

  3. Casa rural Barranco de la Salud: This charming guesthouse is located just outside Haro and offers beautiful views of the surrounding countryside.

What to Buy in Haro: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  1. Rioja wine: A bottle of Rioja wine is a must-buy souvenir from Haro. Wine tastings and tours are available at many wineries in the area.

  2. Piquillo peppers: These sweet roasted red peppers stuffed with goat cheese are a local specialty and make a great gift or souvenir.

Local Culture and Customs of Haro

Haro has a rich cultural heritage, and its inhabitants are proud of their traditions. Some customs and traditions to look out for include:

  1. Batalla del Vino (Wine Fight): A festival held every year in June, the Battle of Wine is a unique event where participants throw wine at each other.

  2. Fiesta de San Juan: This festival is held on June 24th and celebrates the summer solstice with bonfires, music, and dancing.

Best Time to Visit Haro

The best time to visit Haro is from May to October when the weather is warm and the vineyards are at their most beautiful. The peak tourist season is from June to September, so it’s best to book accommodation in advance if you plan to visit during this time.

Do’s and Don’ts of Haro

Do:

  1. Try the local cuisine and wine
  2. Take a hot air balloon ride over the vineyards
  3. Visit a winery and learn about the wine-making process

Don’t:

  1. Drink and drive
  2. Leave valuables unattended
  3. Wear inappropriate clothing when visiting religious sites

Safety and Health

Safety in Haro

Haro is a safe town, but it’s always best to take precautions when traveling. Keep an eye on your belongings and avoid walking alone at night in secluded areas.

Health in Haro

Spain has a public health system that provides free or low-cost medical care to EU citizens. Travelers from other countries should have travel insurance that covers medical expenses.

Getting to Haro: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Haro

The nearest airports to Haro are:

  • Bilbao Airport (116km)
  • Vitoria Airport (57km)
  • Logrono Airport (40km)

Bus Stations Near Haro

  • Haro Bus Station
  • Logroño Bus Station

Other Options

  • Train: The nearest train station is in Miranda de Ebro, approximately 32km from Haro.
  • Car Rental: Rental cars are available at all major airports and train stations.

How to Get Around Haro

Haro is a small town, and most attractions are within walking distance. Taxis and buses are also available for those who prefer not to walk.

Where to Go Next from Haro: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  1. Logroño: The capital of La Rioja, Logroño is famous for its historic center, wine bars, and tapas.
  2. Vitoria-Gasteiz: The capital of the Basque Country, Vitoria-Gasteiz is a beautiful city with stunning parks, historic architecture, and great cuisine.
  3. Bilbao: Home to the famous Guggenheim Museum, Bilbao is a vibrant city with a rich cultural heritage and plenty of things to see and do.

Conclusion

Haro is a charming town with a rich history, stunning landscapes, and great wine and cuisine. It is an ideal destination for wine lovers, history buffs, and outdoor enthusiasts. This guide covers everything you need to know to plan a memorable trip to Haro.
