Introduction to Ezcaray

Ezcaray is a charming town nestled in the heart of the picturesque region of La Rioja in Northern Spain. The town is renowned for its rich cultural heritage, natural beauty, and exquisite culinary traditions. Ezcaray has something to offer everyone, from history buffs to outdoor enthusiasts to foodies.

List of Places to Visit in Ezcaray

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Museum of Traditional La Rioja Culture

The Museum of Traditional La Rioja Culture is a must-visit spot for history buffs. The museum offers a fascinating insight into the daily life and traditions of the region’s people. The exhibits feature everything from traditional costumes to farming tools to elaborate handicrafts.

Parish Church of Santa María la Mayor

The Parish Church of Santa María la Mayor is an impressive example of Gothic architecture. The church features a beautiful altarpiece made by the renowned artist Damián Forment.

Natural Attractions of Ezcaray: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Sierra de la Demanda Natural Park

The Sierra de la Demanda Natural Park is a paradise for outdoor enthusiasts. The park offers countless hiking and biking trails, as well as breathtaking vistas of the surrounding mountains. Visitors can also enjoy water sports, fishing, and bird-watching.

Valdezcaray Ski Resort

During the winter months, Valdezcaray Ski Resort is a must-visit spot for skiers and snowboarders. The resort features over 22 kilometers of slopes and 21 ski lifts.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Ezcaray

Ezcaray is an ideal destination for those looking for hiking or cycling adventures. From the stunning peaks of the Sierra de la Demanda to the rolling hills of the Ebro Valley, the region offers a wide range of trails for outdoor enthusiasts. Some of the most notable trails include the Camino de Santiago, the GR-93, and the PR-86.

Best Things to Do in Ezcaray: Activities and Tours

La Rioja Wine Tour

La Rioja is renowned for its wine culture, and no trip to Ezcaray is complete without a wine tour. Visitors can indulge in some of the region’s most celebrated wines and learn about the history and culture behind them.

Guided Tour of Ezcaray

A guided tour of Ezcaray is an excellent way to explore the region’s rich cultural heritage. A knowledgeable guide can take visitors to some of the town’s most significant landmarks, including the Parish Church of Santa María la Mayor and the Museum of Traditional La Rioja Culture.

Week-Long Itinerary for Ezcaray

Day 1: Arrival and Acclimatization Day 2: Explore the town of Ezcaray and the Parish Church of Santa María la Mayor. Day 3: Hike or Bike in the Sierra de la Demanda Natural Park. Day 4: Take a La Rioja wine tour and visit some of the region’s vineyards. Day 5: Visit Valdezcaray Ski Resort for some skiing or snowboarding. Day 6: Enjoy a day of relaxation at one of the town’s spas or thermal baths. Day 7: Departure.

Food and Drink Scene of Ezcaray

Must-Try Dishes

Ezcaray is renowned for its exquisite culinary traditions. Some of the most popular dishes include:

  • Riojan-style potato stew
  • Grilled chorizo
  • Sopa de ajo (garlic soup)
  • Roast lamb

Local Beverages

No trip to Ezcaray is complete without sampling some of the region’s celebrated wines. Some of the most notable varieties include Crianza, Reserva, and Gran Reserva.

Where to Stay in Ezcaray: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Hotel Palacio Azcárate

The Hotel Palacio Azcárate is an elegant and historic hotel located in the heart of Ezcaray. The hotel features luxurious guest rooms and top-notch amenities, including a restaurant, bar, and spa.

Hostal Casa Masip

The Hostal Casa Masip is a charming and cozy guesthouse located in the historic center of Ezcaray. The guesthouse is renowned for its warm hospitality and excellent cuisine.

What to Buy in Ezcaray: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Visitors to Ezcaray should be sure to pick up some of the region’s celebrated local products. These include handcrafted items such as ceramics, jewelry, and textiles. Wine and culinary products such as extra virgin olive oil, cheese, and sausages are also popular souvenirs.

Local Culture and Customs of Ezcaray

Ezcaray is known for its vibrant and friendly community. Locals take great pride in their town’s cultural heritage, and many festivals and celebrations are held throughout the year. Visitors should be sure to respect local customs and traditions.

Best Time to Visit Ezcaray

The best time to visit Ezcaray is during the spring or fall when the weather is mild, and crowds are less significant. The summer months of July and August can be hot and crowded, while the winter months can be cold and snowy.

Do’s and Don’ts of Ezcaray

Do:

  • Sample local wines and cuisine.
  • Take a guided tour of the historic landmarks.
  • Explore the natural beauty of the Sierra de la Demanda.

Don’t:

  • Ignore local customs and traditions.
  • Leave trash or litter behind in natural areas.
  • Overlook smaller nearby towns and villages.

Safety and Health

Safety in Ezcaray

Ezcaray is a relatively safe destination, with a low crime rate. Visitors should take standard precautions by avoiding unlit areas at night and keeping valuables secure.

Health in Ezcaray

Visitors should ensure they have adequate health insurance coverage before traveling to Ezcaray. The town has a medical center and a pharmacy in case of emergency.

Getting to Ezcaray: Transportation Options and Logistics

Ezcaray is easily accessible by car or public transportation. The closest airports are Bilbao Airport and Pamplona Airport. Visitors can also take a train or bus to nearby Haro or Logrono and then take a taxi to Ezcaray.

Airports Near Ezcaray

  • Bilbao Airport (115km)
  • Pamplona Airport (95km)

Bus Stations Near Ezcaray

  • Haro Bus Station
  • Logrono Bus Station

Other Options

  • Taxi and car rental services are available.

How to Get Around in Ezcaray

Ezcaray is a small town, and most sights are within easy walking distance of each other. Visitors can also rent a bicycle or take a taxi to explore the surrounding area.

Where to Go Next from Ezcaray: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Haro
  • Logrono
  • San Millán de la Cogolla
  • Santo Domingo de la Calzada

Conclusion

Ezcaray is a hidden gem in Northern Spain, with a rich cultural heritage and a stunning natural beauty. With something to offer everyone, from history buffs to foodies to outdoor enthusiasts, Ezcaray is a must-visit destination for anyone looking to experience the best of Spanish culture and hospitality.
