Introduction to Balmaseda

Balmaseda is a small town located in Basque Country, in Northern Spain. The town lies on the banks of the Kadagua river, surrounded by lush green hills. Balmaseda is known for its rich culture, beautiful sceneries, and delicious food scene. It is a perfect destination for nature enthusiasts, history buffs, and foodies. The town has a warm and welcoming atmosphere that will make you feel right at home.

List of Places to Visit in Balmaseda

When in Balmaseda, there are plenty of places to see and experience. Below is a list of some of the top attractions to visit:

Culturally Important Attractions

  • Museo de Balmaseda: This museum is dedicated to the history of the town. It houses a collection of artifacts and exhibits that tell the story of Balmaseda from the prehistoric times to the present day.
  • San Juan Bautista Church: This is the largest church in Balmaseda. The church is known for its impressive Gothic architecture and beautiful stained glass windows.
  • Puente Viejo: This is the oldest bridge in Balmaseda. The bridge dates back to the 14th century and is a great spot for taking pictures.

Natural Attractions

  • Monte Kolitza: This is one of the highest peaks in Basque Country. The mountain offers breathtaking views of the surrounding landscape and is a great spot for hiking and picnicking.
  • Parque de los hermanos Larrinaga: This is a beautiful park located in the heart of Balmaseda. The park has a lake, a playground, and plenty of benches where you can relax and enjoy the scenery.
  • El Corralón: This is a natural swimming pool located on the outskirts of town. The pool is fed by a freshwater spring and is a great spot for cooling off on a hot day.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Balmaseda

Balmaseda is a paradise for hikers and bikers. There are several trails in and around Balmaseda that offer stunning views of the scenery. Some of the most popular trails include:

  • Monte Kolitza trail: This is a strenuous hike that takes you to the summit of Monte Kolitza. The trail is about 5 km long and offers panoramic views of the surrounding mountains.
  • Ruta de los Dólmenes: This is a circular trail that takes you to some of the prehistoric dolmens in the area. The trail is about 12 km long and is suitable for hikers of all levels.
  • Vía Verde del Vasco-Navarro: This is a paved bike trail that follows the route of an old railway track. The trail is about 37 km long and offers beautiful views of the surrounding countryside.

Best Things to Do in Balmaseda: Activities and Tours

Aside from hiking and biking, there are plenty of fun activities and tours to do in Balmaseda. Some of the best things to do include:

  • Kayaking on the Kadagua river: This is a fun way to explore the scenic Kadagua river. You can rent a kayak from one of the local providers and paddle downstream while enjoying the beautiful views.
  • Wine Tasting: Balmaseda is known for its excellent wine. There are several wineries in the area that offer wine tastings and tours.
  • Hot Air Balloon rides: For a unique experience, take a hot air balloon ride over Balmaseda. The views from above are truly breathtaking.

Food and Drink Scene of Balmaseda

Balmaseda has a rich food scene that is deeply rooted in local tradition. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Bacalao al Pil Pil: This is a traditional Basque dish made with cod, garlic, olive oil, and chili peppers.
  • Txakoli: This is a light, sparkling wine made in the Basque Country. It pairs well with seafood and pintxos.
  • Lechazo Asado: A roasted lamb dish from Castilla y León.

Local Beverages

  • Cerveza La Salve: This is a local craft beer made in Balmaseda. It is known for its distinctive flavor and refreshing taste.
  • Sidra: This is a cider drink that is popular in Asturias and the Basque Country. It is made from fermented apple juice and has a slightly tart taste.

Where to Stay in Balmaseda

Balmaseda has plenty of accommodation options to choose from. Whether you’re looking for a hotel, hostel, or guesthouse, you’ll find something to suit your needs. Some of the top options include:

  • Posada Casa de la Merced: This is a charming guesthouse located in the heart of Balmaseda. It is housed in a renovated 17th-century building and offers comfortable rooms and excellent service.
  • Hotel Convento San Roque: This is a luxury hotel located on a hill overlooking Balmaseda. The hotel has a spa, a restaurant, and offers stunning views of the surrounding countryside.
  • Albergue de Balmaseda: This is a hostel located in the center of town. It offers budget-friendly accommodation and is a great option for backpackers and solo travelers.

What to Buy in Balmaseda

If you’re looking for souvenirs and local products to bring home, here are some ideas:

  • Txakoli wine: This is a local wine that makes a great gift for wine lovers.
  • Espeletako biperra: This is a spicy chili powder from the town of Espelette, in the French Basque Country.
  • Lodosa piquillo peppers: These are sweet red peppers from the village of Lodosa, in Navarre. They are often used in local dishes.

Local Culture and Customs of Balmaseda

Balmaseda has a rich culture that is deeply rooted in Basque tradition. The town hosts several festivals throughout the year, including:

  • Fiestas de San Severino: This is a festival that takes place in January. It is a celebration of the town’s patron saint, and features parades, music, and fireworks.
  • Fiestas de San Roque: This is a festival that takes place in August. It is a celebration of the town’s protector, and features bull runs, concerts, and traditional dances.
  • Carnival: This is a festival that takes place in February or March. It is a time of feasting and celebration before the start of Lent.

Best Time to Visit Balmaseda

The best time to visit Balmaseda is between May and September. During this time, the weather is warm and sunny, and there are plenty of outdoor activities to enjoy. The town can get crowded in August during the San Roque festival, so plan accordingly.

Do’s and Don’ts of Balmaseda

Do:

  • Respect the local customs and traditions: Balmaseda has a strong Basque identity, so it’s important to be respectful of the local culture.
  • Try the local food and drink: Balmaseda has a rich culinary tradition that is worth exploring.
  • Explore the outdoors: The surrounding countryside is beautiful and offers plenty of opportunities for hiking and biking.

Don’t:

  • Wear inappropriate clothing in religious sites: When visiting churches or other religious sites, dress modestly.
  • Leave trash on the trails: Help keep Balmaseda clean by carrying your trash out with you.
  • Be loud and disruptive at night: Balmaseda is a small town, and noise carries easily. Be considerate of your neighbors by keeping noise levels down at night.

Safety and Health

Safety in Balmaseda

Balmaseda is a safe town, with low crime rates. However, it’s always a good idea to take basic precautions, like avoiding unlit areas at night and keeping your valuables out of sight. In case of an emergency, dial 112 for assistance.

Health in Balmaseda

Balmaseda has several healthcare centers and pharmacies, so you should have no trouble finding medical assistance if you need it. Tap water is safe to drink in Balmaseda.

Getting to Balmaseda: Transportation Options and Logistics

Balmaseda is easily accessible by plane, train, or car.

Airports Near Balmaseda

The closest airport to Balmaseda is Bilbao Airport, which is about 35 km away. From the airport, you can take a taxi or bus to Balmaseda.

Bus Stations Near Balmaseda

Balmaseda has a bus station that serves several local and regional routes. The bus station is centrally located, making it easy to get around town.

Other Options

If you’re driving to Balmaseda, the town is easily accessible from the A-8 motorway. There is also a train station in Balmaseda that offers connections to Bilbao and other nearby towns.

How to Get Around in Balmaseda

Balmaseda is a small town that is easily explored on foot. However, if you want to explore the surrounding countryside, you may want to rent a bike or car. There are several rental shops in town that offer bikes and cars for rent.

Where to Go Next from Balmaseda: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

If you have more time to explore the area, here are some nearby destinations and excursions to consider:

  • Bilbao: This vibrant city is the largest in Basque Country. It is known for its modern architecture, world-class museums, and excellent food scene.
  • San Sebastián: This picturesque coastal town is known for its beautiful beaches, historic buildings, and Michelin-starred restaurants.
  • Rioja wine region: This world-famous wine region is just a short drive from Balmaseda. It offers stunning landscapes and some of the best wines in the world.

Conclusion

Balmaseda is a hidden gem in Basque Country that deserves to be on every traveler’s radar. Whether you’re interested in history, nature, or food, you’ll find something to love in this charming town. We hope this comprehensive tour guide has inspired you to explore Balmaseda and all it has to offer.
