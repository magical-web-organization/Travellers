Introduction to Mundaka

Nestled in the heart of Basque Country in Northern Spain, Mundaka is a picturesque town known for its beautiful coastline, rich cultural heritage, and world-class surfing. This charming town, with its stunning natural scenery and friendly locals, has something to offer every traveler.

List of Places to Visit in Mundaka

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Mundaka boasts a rich cultural heritage, and there are several museums and historical places to explore in the town. The following are some of the must-visit attractions for culture enthusiasts:

  • The Basque Museum - This museum showcases the history and culture of Basque Country. It has a vast collection of traditional objects, including clothing, household items, and tools.

  • The Lighthouse of Mundaka - This lighthouse offers breathtaking views of Mundaka’s coastline and the Bay of Biscay. It is still in operation and is a great spot for photography.

  • The Church of Santa Maria - Built in the 16th century, this church is a beautiful example of Gothic architecture. It houses several religious artifacts and offers a glimpse into the town’s religious history.

Natural Attractions of Mundaka: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Mundaka’s natural beauty is one of its biggest attractions. From surfing to hiking, there are plenty of outdoor activities to enjoy in this quaint town. Some of the must-visit natural attractions are:

  • Mundaka Beach - This beautiful beach is famous for its world-class surfing waves. It is a popular spot for both beginners and experienced surfers.

  • Monte Ogono - This mountain offers panoramic views of Mundaka and the surrounding area. There are several hiking trails that lead to the top of the mountain.

  • Urdaibai Bird Center - This center is a must-visit for bird watchers. It houses an extensive collection of birds, including migratory birds that pass through the area.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Mundaka

Mundaka has several hiking and biking routes that offer stunning views of the town’s natural beauty. Some of the popular routes include:

  • Monte Ogono Trail - This trail is a moderate 3-hour hike that leads to the top of Monte Ogono. It offers breathtaking views of Mundaka and the surrounding area.

  • Santimamiñe Caves and Olatzagutia Dolmen Trail - This trail is a 10-kilometer hike that leads past several archaeological sites, including the Santimamiñe Caves and Olatzagutia Dolmen.

  • The Basque Coast Geopark - This geopark has several hiking and biking routes that offer stunning views of the Basque coast.

Best Things to Do in Mundaka: Activities and Tours

Mundaka is a town that offers something for everyone, regardless of interests. From surfing to cultural activities, there are plenty of things to do in Mundaka. Some of the most popular things to do are:

  • Surfing - Mundaka is known for having some of the best surfing waves in the world. Surf lessons and equipment rentals are available in the town.

  • Explore Basque Culture - Basque Country has a unique culture, and there are several ways to explore it in Mundaka. Join a guided tour of the town or take a cooking class to learn about Basque cuisine.

  • Wine Tasting - Basque Country is known for its excellent wine, and there are several wineries in the area that offer tasting tours.

Week-Long Itinerary for Mundaka

For travelers who want to explore Mundaka and the surrounding area in-depth, a week-long itinerary is recommended. Here is a sample itinerary:

2 Surf lesson at Mundaka Beach and wine tasting tour at local wineries

Food and Drink Scene of Mundaka

Mundaka has a vibrant food and drink scene, with several excellent restaurants and cafes to choose from. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Marmitako - A Basque dish made with tuna, potatoes, onions, and red peppers.

  • Pintxos - A Basque variation of tapas, with a variety of small dishes served on a slice of bread.

  • Cod a la Vizcaína - A traditional Basque dish made with cod, tomatoes, onions, and red peppers.

Local Beverages

  • Txakoli - A slightly sparkling white wine made in Basque Country.

  • Basque Cider - A dry, tart cider made from Basque apples.

  • Patxaran - A sweet liqueur made from sloe berries.

Where to Stay in Mundaka: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Mundaka has several accommodation options available, including hotels, hostels, and guesthouses. Here are a few recommended options:

  • Hotel El Puerto - This hotel has a prime location overlooking the Mundaka estuary. It offers comfortable rooms and stunning views.

  • Casa Rural Itxaspe - This guesthouse is located in the countryside outside of Mundaka. It offers peace and tranquility and is a great option for nature lovers.

  • Batzarre Hostel - This hostel is located in the heart of Mundaka’s town center. It offers budget-friendly accommodation and a communal kitchen for guests.

What to Buy in Mundaka: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Mundaka is known for its unique souvenirs and local products. Here are a few things to consider bringing home:

  • Basque Berets - These traditional wool hats are a quintessential part of Basque culture.

  • Txakoli Wine - This sparkling white wine is unique to Basque Country and makes for an excellent gift.

  • Basque Cheese - Basque Country is known for its delicious cheese, including Idiazabal, a smoked sheep’s cheese.

Local Culture and Customs of Mundaka

Mundaka has a rich cultural heritage and a unique way of life. Learning about local customs and traditions can enhance your travel experience. Here are a few customs to keep in mind:

  • Basque Language - Basque is the official language of Basque Country, and many locals speak it fluently. However, most people also speak Spanish, so communication shouldn’t be an issue.

  • Pintxos Culture - Pintxos are an important part of Basque cuisine and culture. It’s common to go on a “pintxos crawl,” moving from bar to bar and trying different dishes.

  • Religious Festivals - Mundaka, like many towns in Spain, celebrates several religious festivals throughout the year. If your visit coincides with a festival, be prepared for crowds and lots of celebrations.

Best Time to Visit Mundaka

The best time to visit Mundaka depends on your interests. For surfers, the best time is from October to April when the waves are at their best. For nature lovers, the spring and fall are ideal, with mild temperatures and fewer crowds. Keep in mind that summers can be hot, crowded, and expensive.

Do’s and Don’ts of Mundaka

Do:

  • Try surfing, even if you’ve never done it before.

  • Explore Basque culture and cuisine.

  • Respect local customs and traditions.

Don’t:

  • Ignore local customs and traditions.

  • Leave valuables unattended on the beach.

  • Be overly loud or rowdy in public spaces.

Safety and Health

Mundaka is a safe town, but it’s always wise to take precautions and be aware of your surroundings. Here are a few safety and health tips to keep in mind:

Safety in Mundaka

  • Keep an eye on your valuables when in public places.

  • Use caution when surfing, and always follow safety guidelines recommended by instructors.

  • Follow local traffic laws when driving or biking around the town.

Health in Mundaka

  • The tap water in Mundaka is safe to drink.

  • Wear sunscreen and a hat when spending time outdoors to protect yourself from the sun.

  • If you require medical attention, there are several medical centers and hospitals in the area.

Getting to Mundaka: Transportation Options and Logistics

Mundaka is relatively easy to get to, with several transportation options available. Here are a few tips to make your journey to Mundaka smoother:

Airports Near Mundaka

  • Bilbao Airport is the closest airport to Mundaka, located about 35 kilometers away.

  • Biarritz Airport in France is another option, located about 90 kilometers away.

Bus Stations Near Mundaka

  • Autobuses La Union is the company that operates buses in the area. The bus from Bilbao to Mundaka takes about an hour.

Other Options

  • Renting a car is a good option if you plan to explore the surrounding areas. There are several car rental agencies in Bilbao and Biarritz airports.

  • Trains are not available in Mundaka, but there are several train stations nearby in Bilbao and San Sebastian.

How to Get Around in Mundaka

Mundaka is a small town, and it’s easy to explore on foot. However, if you want to explore the surrounding areas, renting a car or bike is recommended. Taxis are also available in the town center.

Where to Go Next from Mundaka: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Mundaka is a great base for exploring Basque Country and the surrounding areas. Here are a few nearby destinations that are worth a visit:

  • San Juan de Gaztelugatxe - This stunning monastery is perched on a rock in the sea and is accessible via a winding footbridge.

  • Bermeo - This charming fishing village is located just a few kilometers from Mundaka. It’s home to several museums and historical sites, including the Ercilla Tower.

  • Biarritz - This French town is a popular surfing destination and has several excellent beaches and restaurants.

Conclusion

Mundaka is a must-visit destination for travelers looking for a unique and authentic experience in Northern Spain. This coastal town has something to offer for every interest, from surfing to cultural exploration. Use this comprehensive guide to plan your visit to Mundaka and make the most of your time in Basque Country.
