Introduction to Eastern Gran Canaria

Eastern Gran Canaria is a place of stunning beauty, with its volcanic landscapes, charming coastal villages, and pristine beaches. This part of Gran Canaria, situated in the Canary Islands archipelago, boasts a unique mix of cultural and natural attractions that attract tourists from far and wide.

From water sports to hiking, sightseeing, and sunbathing, Eastern Gran Canaria caters to all different tastes and preferences. In this travel guide, we have compiled a list of must-see places, activities, and historical landmarks to help you plan your perfect vacation in Eastern Gran Canaria.

List of Places to Visit in Eastern Gran Canaria

Eastern Gran Canaria is full of places to visit, from cultural landmarks to natural treasures. Here’s a list of some of the best:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  1. Cueva Pintada Museum and Archaeological Site: This museum is a great starting point for exploring Eastern Gran Canaria’s cultural heritage, with countless exhibitions that provide insight into the pre-Columbian Canarian culture.

  2. San Juan Church: Built in the 17th century, San Juan Church is a fine example of Canarian Baroque architecture that boasts intricate details and stunning interior decorations.

Natural Attractions of Eastern Gran Canaria: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  1. Roque Nublo: One of Gran Canaria’s most iconic natural landmarks, Roque Nublo rises 80 meters from the sea level, offering stunning views of the surrounding valley and the Atlantic Ocean.

  2. Caldera de los Marteles: With its volcanic landscape, this caldera offers an opportunity for a unique hiking experience with panoramic views of the island.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Eastern Gran Canaria

Eastern Gran Canaria is a hub of outdoor activities, with plenty of hiking and biking trails for all levels of experience. Here are some options for hiking and biking enthusiasts:

  1. Barranco de la Mina: This is an easy hiking trail suitable for families with children that runs parallel to a mountain stream and passes through natural rock formations.

  2. Fataga to San Bartolome: This moderately difficult hike starts and ends in picturesque villages and passes through the stunning Barranco Guariche gorges.

Best Things to Do in Eastern Gran Canaria: Activities and Tours

Whether you’re seeking adventure or relaxation, Eastern Gran Canaria has plenty to offer. Here are some of the best things to do in this area:

  1. Water Sports: With its pristine beaches and clear waters, Eastern Gran Canaria is a haven for water sports enthusiasts. You can try your hand at windsurfing, kitesurfing, paddle boarding, and scuba diving.

  2. Camel Safari: Feel like Lawrence of Arabia and explore the stunning landscape of Eastern Gran Canaria on a camel-back ride.

Week-Long Itinerary for Eastern Gran Canaria

If you are planning a week-long trip to Eastern Gran Canaria, here’s how you can make the most of your vacation:

Food and Drink Scene of Eastern Gran Canaria

No vacation is complete without a taste of the local cuisine. Here are some must-try dishes and beverages of Eastern Gran Canaria:

Must-Try Dishes

  1. Papas Arrugadas: A local dish consisting of small boiled potatoes served with a spicy salsa called mojo.

  2. Sancocho Canario: A hearty stew made with salted fish, sweet potatoes, and root vegetables.

Local Beverages

  1. Ron Miel: A local specialty is a honey rum that you can sample in local bars and restaurants.

  2. Vino de Gran Canaria: The wine in Gran Canaria is made from Malvasia grapes with a dry finish and is worth sampling.

Where to Stay in Eastern Gran Canaria: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Eastern Gran Canaria has plenty of accommodation options tailored to suit different budgets and tastes. You can find luxurious resorts, budget-friendly hostels, guesthouses, and self-catering apartments. Some popular spots to stay include:

  1. Playa del Ingles: One of the most popular tourist hotspots in Eastern Gran Canaria, with plenty of accommodation options ranging from budget-friendly hostels to luxury resorts.

  2. Melenara: Ideal for families, Melenara offers a range of self-catering apartments and guesthouses that offer stunning sea views.

What to Buy in Eastern Gran Canaria: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

If you’re looking for souvenirs or gifts to bring back, here are some of the local products to consider:

  1. Aloe Vera Products: Gran Canaria is known for its Aloe Vera products, and you can find an array of creams, lotions, and gels to buy.

  2. Wine: Gran Canaria’s wine region in the north of the island produces some excellent Malvasia wine.

Local Culture and Customs of Eastern Gran Canaria

Eastern Gran Canaria celebrates its cultural heritage with countless fiestas and festivals throughout the year. The locals are warm and welcoming, and visitors are encouraged to respect their customs and traditions, such as attending religious parades and local festivals.

Best Time to Visit Eastern Gran Canaria

The high season for tourism in Eastern Gran Canaria is between December and February, when the weather is pleasant and mild, and the island is quiet. However, the shoulder season between March and May is also a good time to visit, with fewer crowds and warm temperatures, particularly for hiking and beach activities.

Do’s and Don’ts of Eastern Gran Canaria

Do:

  1. Respect the local culture and customs.
  2. Visit local markets and festivals.

Don’t:

  1. Climb a monument or a local resident’s wall.
  2. Go topless at beaches other than designated spots.

Safety and Health

Safety in Eastern Gran Canaria

Eastern Gran Canaria is generally a safe place to visit, with low crime rates. However, tourists should be aware of pickpocketing and take standard precautionary measures as in any other place where there are a lot of visitors.

Health in Eastern Gran Canaria

The healthcare system in Gran Canaria is excellent. However, tourists are advised to have good travel insurance and to take necessary precautions to avoid dehydration during the hot summers.

Getting to Eastern Gran Canaria: Transportation Options and Logistics

Eastern Gran Canaria is well connected via air, road, and sea transportation.

Airports Near Eastern Gran Canaria

The island has one major airport, Gran Canaria International Airport, which is served by several major airlines flying domestically, internationally, and to mainland Spain.

Bus Stations Near Eastern Gran Canaria

Global is a major bus company that connects Eastern Gran Canaria with other parts of the island as well as with other Canarian islands.

Other Options

Another way to reach Eastern Gran Canaria is by ferry from other Canary Islands or from mainland Spain.

How to Get Around in Eastern Gran Canaria

The best way to explore Eastern Gran Canaria is by renting a car, but there are also bus and taxi services available for tourists.

Where to Go Next from Eastern Gran Canaria: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

If you have more time to explore, consider visiting nearby destinations:

  1. Tejeda: This mountain village offers stunning views of the island and is ideal for nature lovers.

  2. Las Palmas de Gran Canaria: The island’s capital is a vibrant city with beautiful beaches and rich cultural heritage.

Conclusion

Eastern Gran Canaria is a unique and beautiful place to visit that caters to all kinds of travelers.
