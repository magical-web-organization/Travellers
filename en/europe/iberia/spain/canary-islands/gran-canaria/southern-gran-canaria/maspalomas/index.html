Introduction to Maspalomas

Maspalomas is a popular coastal town located in the southern part of Gran Canaria, one of Spain’s Canary Islands. With its pristine beach, towering sand dunes, gorgeous natural landscapes, and year-round sunny weather, it’s no surprise that Maspalomas is a favorite holiday destination for tourists worldwide.

This vibrant town also boasts an excellent food scene, adventure sports, and cultural landmarks showcasing the rich history and diversity of the Canarian people. Whether you are traveling solo, with a partner, or with your family, Maspalomas offers something for everyone. In this guide, we will help you discover the best attractions, activities, tips, and tricks for your trip to Maspalomas.

List of Places to Visit in Maspalomas

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Museo Castillo de la Fortaleza

Museo Castillo de la Fortaleza is a historical museum located in the heart of Maspalomas’ old town. The museum showcases the Canarian history and traditions, including an exhibit of the early human inhabitants of the archipelago and the islands’ pre-Hispanic era.

The museum’s main attraction is the Castillo de la Fortaleza, a 16th-century fortification that played a critical role in defending Maspalomas from pirate invasions. The fort’s imposing walls and watchtowers offer an excellent view of the town and the surrounding ocean.

Templo Ecuménico de San Salvador

The Templo Ecuménico de San Salvador is a modernist church designed by Catalan artist and architect, Esteban Prieto Vicioso, in the 1960s. The church’s unique architecture combines elements of both Christian and non-Christian religions, making it a unique landmark in Maspalomas.

Natural Attractions of Maspalomas: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Maspalomas Dunes Natural Reserve

The Maspalomas Dunes Natural Reserve covers an area of around 400 hectares and consists of a series of golden sand dunes that stretch out to the sea. The reserve offers endless opportunities for outdoor adventures, including hiking, biking, and camel riding.

The breathtaking landscape of the dunes makes it a popular spot for photographers and nature enthusiasts. The reserve is also home to a vibrant ecosystem of flora and fauna, where you can spot a wide range of birds, lizards, and other wildlife.

Playa de Maspalomas

Playa de Maspalomas is the main beach in Maspalomas, characterized by its warm turquoise waters and golden sand. The beach covers over 2.5 kilometers of coastline, providing plenty of space to relax and soak up the sun.

Located near the sand dunes, Playa de Maspalomas is also a popular spot for water sports such as windsurfing, paddleboarding, and kiteboarding. The beach has plenty of amenities, including showers, changing rooms, and sunbeds.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Maspalomas

Maspalomas offers an excellent network of hiking and biking trails that covers a wide range of landscapes, from the sand dunes to the mountain peaks. Here are some of the best routes to explore:

Maspalomas Lighthouse Trail

The Maspalomas Lighthouse Trail is a scenic coastal route that offers spectacular views of the ocean and the surrounding cliffs. The route begins at the Faro de Maspalomas and runs along a well-maintained path to the Playa de San Agustin.

Roque Nublo Trek

Roque Nublo Trek is a challenging hike that takes you through the heart of Gran Canaria’s mountain range. The trek begins at the town of Tejeda and takes around six hours to complete. The route offers breathtaking views of the island’s volcanic landscape, including the iconic Roque Nublo rock formation.

Best Things to Do in Maspalomas: Activities and Tours

Water Sports

Maspalomas offers a wide range of water sports activities, including windsurfing, paddleboarding, and kiteboarding. The warm waters of the ocean and the steady winds make it an ideal destination for water sports enthusiasts.

Dolphin Watching Tour

One of the most popular tours in Maspalomas is the dolphin watching tour. The tour takes you on a boat ride along the coast, where you can spot different dolphin species in their natural habitat.

Nightlife

If you’re looking for a lively night out, Maspalomas has plenty of bars and clubs to choose from. The Yumbo Centre is the go-to destination for a fun night out, with its wide variety of bars, restaurants, and nightclubs.

Week-Long Itinerary for Maspalomas

If you’re planning a week-long trip to Maspalomas, here’s a suggested itinerary:

Day 1: Explore the Maspalomas Dunes Reserve and relax at Playa de Maspalomas.

Day 2: Take a hiking or biking tour in the mountain range.

Day 3: Visit the Museo Castillo de la Fortaleza and explore Maspalomas’ old town.

Day 4: Enjoy a day trip to nearby towns like Puerto de Mogán or Puerto Rico.

Day 5: Go on a dolphin watching tour.

Day 6: Spend the day at Aqualand Maspalomas, a water park with rides and slides for all ages.

Day 7: Take a day trip to Las Palmas, the capital city of Gran Canaria.

Food and Drink Scene of Maspalomas

Must-Try Dishes

Papas Arrugadas

Papas arrugadas is a Canarian specialty made by boiling small potatoes in salted water until they become wrinkled. The potatoes are typically served with mojo sauce, made with garlic, red chili pepper, and olive oil.

Gofio

Gofio is a Canarian flour made from toasted grains, usually wheat or maize. It’s a staple ingredient in many Canarian dishes, such as gofio escaldado, a popular fish stew.

Local Beverages

Ron Miel

Ron Miel is a honey rum made in the Canary Islands, with a sweet and smooth taste. It’s a popular after-dinner drink in Maspalomas and throughout the Canary Islands.

Where to Stay in Maspalomas: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Maspalomas offers plenty of accommodation options, ranging from luxury hotels to budget-friendly hostels. Here are some of the best options:

Hotel Faro

Hotel Faro is a luxurious 5-star hotel located near the lighthouse, offering panoramic views of the ocean. The hotel features spacious rooms, an outdoor pool, a spa, and a fitness center.

Villa Adler Gay Men Only

Villa Adler is a gay men-only hotel located in the heart of the Yumbo Centre, offering comfortable rooms and a friendly atmosphere.

What to Buy in Maspalomas: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Maspalomas is known for its local crafts and specialties. Here are some of the best souvenirs to bring home:

Canarian Wine

Canarian wine is famous for its unique flavor, with an excellent selection of red, white, and rose wines. A bottle of Canarian wine makes for a perfect souvenir for wine connoisseurs.

Aloe Vera Products

Aloe vera is a prevalent plant in Maspalomas, with numerous health benefits. You can find plenty of aloe vera products, including creams, lotions, and other skincare items.

Local Culture and Customs of Maspalomas

Canarian culture is rich and diverse, with a mix of Spanish, African, and Latin American influences. The Canarian people are known for their hospitality and friendliness, with a relaxed and easy-going attitude.

Best Time to Visit Maspalomas

Maspalomas enjoys a pleasant climate all year round, with average temperatures ranging from 20°C to 25°C. The best time to visit Maspalomas is between October and May when the weather is mild and pleasant.

Do’s and Don’ts of Maspalomas

Do:

  • Respect the local culture and customs.
  • Drink plenty of water and stay hydrated.
  • Wear sunscreen and protective clothing.

Don’t:

  • Leave valuables unattended on the beach.
  • Park in restricted areas.
  • Take souvenirs from the beach or the natural reserve.

Safety and Health

Safety in Maspalomas

Maspalomas is generally a safe destination for travelers, with low crime rates. However, it’s essential to take precautions, such as avoiding walking alone at night and keeping an eye on your belongings.

Health in Maspalomas

Maspalomas has excellent medical facilities, including public and private hospitals, clinics, and pharmacies. It’s essential to have comprehensive travel insurance that covers medical emergencies.

Getting to Maspalomas: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Maspalomas

The nearest airport to Maspalomas is Gran Canaria Airport, located around 20 kilometers from the town center. The airport has regular flights to and from major cities in Europe and other destinations globally.

Bus Stations Near Maspalomas

Maspalomas has a well-connected bus station that provides services to different destinations on the island. The buses are reliable, cost-effective, and convenient.

Other Options

Another popular way to get to Maspalomas is by taxi or car rental. Taxis are widely available, and car rentals are available at the airport and in the town center.

How to Get Around in Maspalomas

There are several options for getting around Maspalomas, including:

Walking

Maspalomas is a pedestrian-friendly town, with plenty of well-maintained walking paths and promenades.

Biking

Biking is an excellent way to explore Maspalomas, with several bike rental shops available.

Public Transportation

Maspalomas has a well-connected network of buses, making it easy to get around the town and the surrounding areas.

Where to Go Next from Maspalomas: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Las Palmas

Las Palmas is the capital city of Gran Canaria, located around 45 minutes from Maspalomas. The city offers a mix of history, culture, and modern amenities, with stunning beaches, historic landmarks, and vibrant shopping and dining scenes.

Puerto de Mogán

Puerto de Mogán is a charming fishing village located around 15 kilometers from Maspalomas. The town boasts colorful buildings, a picturesque marina, and cozy restaurants and cafes, offering a perfect getaway for travelers seeking a break from the hustle and bustle of town.

Conclusion

Maspalomas offers a perfect blend of natural beauty, adventure, culture, and relaxation, making it a memorable holiday destination. From the stunning sand dunes and golden beaches to the charming old town and vibrant Yumbo Centre, Maspalomas has something to suit every traveler’s taste and budget. We hope this guide will help you plan and enjoy an unforgettable trip to Maspalomas.
