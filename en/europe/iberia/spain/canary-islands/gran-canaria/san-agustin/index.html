San Agustín is a charming city in the south of Gran Canaria, one of Spain’s Canary Islands. Known for its stunning beaches, lush greenery, and vibrant culture, San Agustín is a must-visit destination for every traveler. Our comprehensive travel guide offers a glimpse into the city’s hidden gems, food and drink scene, natural attractions, and much more. Here is everything you need to know to plan your perfect trip to San Agustín.

Introduction to San Agustín

San Agustín is a lively and vibrant seaside resort area located on the south coast of Gran Canaria. This gem of the Canary Islands is perfect for sun-seekers, outdoor adventurers, and history buffs alike. Known for its warm weather, crystal-clear waters, and an eclectic mix of cultural attractions, San Agustín is the ideal destination for travelers looking to unwind and immerse themselves in a new culture.

List of Places to Visit in San Agustín

No trip to San Agustín is complete without exploring the city’s many attractions. Here is a list of the must-visit places in San Agustín:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Iglesia de San Agustín: This historic, 18th-century church is a must-visit attraction in San Agustín. Known for its impressive architecture and religious significance, the Iglesia de San Agustín is a cultural landmark in the city and is worth a visit.

  • Maspalomas Dunes Conservation Area: A unique and scenic natural reserve located just outside San Agustín, the Maspalomas Dunes are a must-see for anyone interested in outdoor adventure and nature. Visitors can take a stroll along the sandy pathways, go biking, or even rent a dune buggy or camel to explore the area.

Natural Attractions of San Agustín: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Pico de las Nieves: This mountain range is known for its breathtaking views and outdoor adventures. Hiking and mountain biking trails are available for visitors, and the area is perfect for adventurous travelers looking to get off the beaten path.

  • Playa de San Agustín: One of the best beaches in San Agustín, the Playa de San Agustín features crystal-clear waters and soft, white sands. Visitors can relax on the beach, play beach volleyball, or go snorkeling to discover the colorful marine life.

Hiking/Biking Routes of San Agustín

San Agustín is surrounded by lush greenery and scenic trails that offer stunning views of the surrounding landscape. Here are some of the best hiking and biking routes in San Agustín:

  • Maspalomas Beach Walk: A scenic walkway that starts at the Maspalomas Lighthouse and runs along the coast, offering beautiful views of the Atlantic ocean.

  • San Agustín Coastal Way: A walking trail that runs along the coast, offering stunning views of the ocean and the surrounding landscape.

Best Things to Do in San Agustín: Activities and Tours

San Agustín offers an array of exciting activities and tours to visitors. Here are some of the must-do things in San Agustín:

  • Hop-On Hop-Off Bus Tours: A great way to explore the city’s many attractions, the Hop-On Hop-Off bus tour takes visitors to some of San Agustín’s most popular tourist attractions.

  • Boat Tours: Visitors can take a boat tour of the Atlantic Ocean to explore the surrounding islands and discover the stunning wonders of the ocean.

Food and Drink Scene of San Agustín

San Agustín is a food lover’s paradise, with some of the best local dishes and international cuisines. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Puchero Canario: A hearty stew made with local herbs and spices, potatoes, carrots, meat, and garbanzo beans.

  • Papas Arrugadas: A typical Canarian dish made with small, boiled potatoes and served with a spicy and garlicy sauce.

Local Beverages

  • Ron Miel: A sweet honey rum that is perfect for sipping on a sunny afternoon.

  • Gran Canaria Wine: Produced on the island, these aromatic, bold wines are perfect for pairing with local cuisine.

Where to Stay in San Agustín: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

San Agustín offers a range of accommodation options for visitors, from luxurious resorts to budget-friendly hostels and guesthouses. Here are some of the best options:

  • Hotel Nayra: A luxurious, high-end hotel located in the heart of San Agustín. With stylish rooms and an array of amenities, Hotel Nayra is perfect for travelers looking to indulge.

  • Bungalows Adonis: A budget-friendly option for travelers who want to stay near the beach. Bungalows Adonis offers a range of accommodations to suit everyone’s needs.

What to Buy in San Agustín: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

San Agustín is a shopper’s paradise, with local markets and stores offering a range of unique souvenirs and local products. Here are some of the best things to buy in San Agustín:

  • Aloe Vera Products: The island is known for its aloe vera farms, and visitors can buy a range of aloe vera products like creams, lotions, gels, and soaps.

  • Local Handicrafts: Visitors can purchase locally made handicrafts like embroidery, pottery and other unique gifts.

Local Culture and Customs of San Agustín

San Agustín is a lively city that celebrates the arts, music, and culture. Visitors can take part in an array of local festivals and events throughout the year, like the Carnaval of Gran Canaria, which is a must-see for anyone visiting the island.

Best Time to Visit San Agustín

San Agustín is a year-round destination, but the best time to visit the city is during the spring and summer months when the weather is warm and pleasant. June to August is the busiest time of year when the city is bustling with activity.

Do’s and Don’ts of San Agustín

Here are some do’s and don’ts of San Agustín to keep in mind while visiting the city:

Do:

  • Try the local cuisine and wines.

  • Wear comfortable shoes for hiking and walking.

Don’t:

  • Swim outside designated areas.

  • Litter on the beach or in public areas.

Safety and Health

San Agustín is a safe and healthy destination for visitors. Here are some safety and health tips to keep in mind while visiting the city:

Safety in San Agustín

  • Be aware of pickpockets in crowded areas.

  • Avoid walking alone at night in secluded areas.

Health in San Agustín

  • Drink plenty of water to avoid dehydration.

  • Wear sunscreen and a hat to avoid sunburns.

San Agustín is a small city, and visitors can easily explore the area by foot or bike. Taxis and buses are also available for visitors who want to venture further afield.

Where to Go Next from San Agustín: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

San Agustín is an ideal base for exploring the rest of Gran Canaria. Here are some nearby destinations that are worth a visit:

  • Las Palmas: The capital of Gran Canaria is a vibrant city with an array of cultural attractions and entertainment.

  • Puerto Rico: A popular tourist destination known for its beautiful beaches and natural beauty.

Conclusion

San Agustín is a beautiful city that offers something for every traveler.
