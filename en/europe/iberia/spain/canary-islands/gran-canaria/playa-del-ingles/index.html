Introduction to Playa del Inglés

Playa del Inglés is a popular beach resort town located on the southern coast of Gran Canaria in the Canary Islands, Spain. The town is a well-known travel destination and is one of the largest resort towns in Europe. It offers something for everyone, from beautiful beaches and stunning natural attractions to hiking and biking trails, vibrant nightlife, and delicious local dishes.

List of Places to Visit in Playa del Inglés

Here are some of the top-rated places to visit in Playa del Inglés:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Casa-Museo Tomás Morales: This museum is dedicated to Tomás Morales, a significant modernist poet of the Canary Islands. Visitors can take a tour of the museum and see memorabilia from Morales’ life.
  • Cenobio de Valerón: This pre-Hispanic granary has been restored and offers insight into the lives of the island’s early inhabitants.
  • Maspalomas Lighthouse: The lighthouse was built in 1890 and is a popular tourist spot for its spectacular views of the ocean.

Natural Attractions of Playa del Inglés: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Maspalomas Sand Dunes: These stunning sand dunes are a protected area and are located next to the beach in Maspalomas. Visitors can take a camel ride or hike through the dunes.
  • Pico de las Nieves: This peak offers some of the most spectacular views of the island, and visitors can go hiking or mountain biking on the nearby trails.
  • Roque Nublo Natural Monument: A volcanic monolith that is one of the most iconic landmarks on the island. Visitors can hike to the top for panoramic views of the surrounding area.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Playa del Inglés

Hiking and biking enthusiasts will have plenty to enjoy in Playa del Inglés. Here are some of the best trails and routes:

  • Barranco de Guayadeque: This hike takes visitors through the picturesque Guayadeque Valley, where they can see caves, ancient cave homes, and beautiful natural landscapes.
  • Cumbre Trail: This off-road biking trail offers some of the best mountain biking experiences on the island.
  • The Transgrancanaria: An annual trail running competition that covers over 120km of the island’s most challenging terrain.

Best Things to Do in Playa del Inglés: Activities and Tours

There’s no shortage of fun activities and tours in Playa del Inglés. Here are some of the best things to do:

  • Water Sports: Visitors can rent paddleboards, jet skis, kayaks, and more for some fun out on the water.
  • Nightlife: The town is famous for its vibrant nightlife, with countless bars and nightclubs offering everything from live music to karaoke.
  • Boat Tours: Visitors can take a boat tour around the island to see some of its most spectacular coastal landscapes, such as the towering cliffs of Los Gigantes.

Week-Long Itinerary for Playa del Inglés

Food and Drink Scene of Playa del Inglés

Playa del Inglés has a vibrant food and drink scene, with plenty of delicious local dishes to try. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Papas Arrugadas: A local specialty of wrinkled potatoes boiled in sea salt and typically served with a spicy mojo sauce.
  • Gofio: A type of flour made from roasted grains that is used in many local dishes, such as gofio escaldado or gofio amasado.
  • Ropa Vieja: This is a delicious stew made from chickpeas, vegetables, and meat.

Local Beverages

  • Ron Miel: A popular honey rum that is perfect for sipping on a balmy evening.
  • Canarian Wine: The island is known for producing excellent wine, with the Malvasía grape being a particular favorite.
  • Barraquito: A layered coffee drink that includes milk, espresso, condensed milk, cinnamon, and lemon peel.

Where to Stay in Playa del Inglés: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Playa del Inglés offers a variety of accommodation options, ranging from budget-friendly hostels to luxurious hotels. Here are some of the best places to stay:

  • Barceló Margaritas: This hotel is located in the heart of Playa del Inglés and offers a range of amenities, including several swimming pools and an on-site spa.
  • Bungalows Doñana: This charming bungalow complex is surrounded by lush gardens and is a great choice for families looking for a relaxing getaway.
  • Bohemia Suites & Spa: This chic and modern hotel is located near the Maspalomas sand dunes and offers stunning views of the surrounding landscape.

What to Buy in Playa del Inglés: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Visitors to Playa del Inglés will find a variety of souvenirs and local products to bring home. Here are some of the best things to buy:

  • Canarian Mojo Sauce: This spicy sauce is a staple of Canarian cuisine and is perfect for dipping potatoes or adding to stews.
  • Aloe Vera Products: The island is home to many aloe vera farms, and visitors can buy a range of products made from the plant, such as creams, lotions, and gels.
  • Pottery: The town has a strong pottery tradition, and visitors can buy handcrafted ceramics and other pottery items as souvenirs.

Local Culture and Customs of Playa del Inglés

The people of Playa del Inglés are known for their laid-back and friendly attitudes. The town has a large population of immigrants from various parts of Europe, creating a diverse and cosmopolitan atmosphere. The locals are proud of their heritage and are happy to share their customs and traditions with visitors.

Best Time to Visit Playa del Inglés

The best time to visit Playa del Inglés is during the spring and fall seasons, when the weather is mild, and crowds are thinner. However, the town is a year-round destination, and visitors can enjoy beach weather and outdoor activities throughout the year.

Do’s and Don’ts of Playa del Inglés

Do:

  • Respect the locals and their culture.
  • Take care of the environment and keep the town clean.
  • Try the local cuisine and beverages.

Don’t:

  • Assume that everyone speaks English.
  • Leave valuables unattended.
  • Wear inappropriate clothing in public places.

Safety and Health

Safety in Playa del Inglés

Playa del Inglés is a relatively safe town, but visitors should take basic precautions, such as locking their valuables and being aware of their surroundings. Visitors should also follow water safety guidelines and be aware of any signage or warnings at the beach.

Health in Playa del Inglés

The town has excellent healthcare facilities, including private clinics and hospitals with English-speaking staff. Visitors should take travel insurance and bring any required medications with them.

Getting to Playa del Inglés: Transportation Options and Logistics

Visitors to Playa del Inglés can arrive via air, land, or sea. Here are some transportation options:

  • Air: The Gran Canaria airport is located in Las Palmas, and visitors can take a taxi, bus, or shuttle to Playa del Inglés.
  • Bus: The island’s public transport system is reliable and affordable, with buses running regularly between Playa del Inglés and other towns on the island.
  • Car: Visitors can rent a car or hire a taxi to get around the island and explore its many attractions.

How to Get Around in Playa del Inglés

The town is relatively small and easy to navigate on foot, but visitors can also rent bicycles or take a taxi to get around. Public buses run regularly to nearby towns and attractions.

Where to Go Next from Playa del Inglés: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Playa del Inglés is an excellent base for exploring the surrounding area. Here are some nearby destinations and excursions to consider:

  • Las Palmas: The island’s capital city is just a short bus ride away and offers historical attractions, museums, and great shopping and dining experiences.
  • Puerto Rico: A popular beach resort town located on the southern coast of Gran Canaria. Visitors can enjoy activities like water sports, swimming, and sunbathing.
  • Tejeda: A charming mountain village with a rich cultural heritage and stunning natural landscapes.

Conclusion

Playa del Inglés has a lot to offer visitors, from its beautiful beaches and natural attractions to its vibrant nightlife and delicious cuisine. Whether you’re looking for adventure or relaxation, this town in Gran Canaria is a must-visit destination.
