Introduction to Las Palmas

Dubbed as the capital city of Gran Canaria, Las Palmas is a popular destination for holidaymakers from around the world. The city is home to one of the largest urban beaches in Europe, and the climate is mild throughout the year with sunny days that make it a perfect destination for vacation year-round. In this comprehensive guide, we will offer information on the must-visit places in Las Palmas, the best outdoor and natural attractions, the top food and drink scene, an itinerary for travelers, and everything else you need to know about Las Palmas.

List of Places to Visit in Las Palmas

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Casa de Colón: Explore the history of Columbus’ voyages to the Americas and the influence of the Canary Islands in the new world.
  • Museum of the Canaries: Discover the pre-hispanic culture of the Canary Islands through the pottery, art, and jewellery exhibitions.
  • Cathedral of Santa Ana: The major church in Las Palmas, the Santa Ana Cathedral is a neoclassical style building that boasts of three naves and 17 chapels.

Natural Attractions of Las Palmas: Outdoor Adventure and Scenic Views

  • La Playa de las Canteras: The urban beach is a perfect spot for swimmers and sun-seekers and has a coral reef that extends parallel to the shore, making it an excellent site for snorkelling lovers.
  • Parque Natural de Doramas: A nature reserve in Grand Canary, it offers hiking trails, picnic sites and several endemic plants and birds.
  • Roque Nublo: A breathtaking rocky peak that offers stunning views of Gran Canaria.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Las Palmas

  • Caldera de Bandama: A scenic viewpoint and hiking attraction, the Bandama volcano is a perfect destination for nature lovers and hikers.
  • Barranco de Guayadeque: A popular hiking destination and the perfect opportunity to explore caves, the Barranco de Guayadeque, is a stunning ravine with well-marked trails for the adventurous and fit.

Best Things to Do in Las Palmas: Activities and Tours

  • Santa Catalina Park: A green oasis in the heart of the city, it serves as a central hub for a wide array of activities for children and adults, including fairs, concerts, and a children’s playground.
  • Triana Street: Lined with boutiques, restaurants, and cafes, it offers a perfect spot for shopping or taking a relaxing stroll.
  • Banana Boat Rides: Popular among the younger crowd, the banana boat rides offer a fun and thrilling experience.

Week-Long Itinerary for Las Palmas

Day Activity
Day 1 Explore the historic district of Vegueta and the prominent Cathedral de Santa Ana
Day 2 Go on a day trip to Roque Nublo, one of the most scenic locations on the island
Day 3 Visit the Museum of Canaries and Las Canteras Beach for an afternoon of sunbathing
Day 4 Head to the Parque Natural de Doramas and get lost in the beauty of nature
Day 5 Take a guided tour of the Bandama Volcano with a stopover for a tasty lunch at the nearby winery
Day 6 Visit the Casa de Colón museum and discover the history of Christopher Columbus
Day 7 Explore the local culture and cuisine at the Triana Street for shopping and memorable dining experiences

Food and Drink Scene of Las Palmas

Must-Try Dishes

  • Papas arrugadas y mojo: Salted and wrinkled potatoes served with mojos (sauce) made from garlic, oil, and spices.
  • Sancocho Canario: A flavourful stew made of salted fish, sweet potato, potatoes, and other spices.
  • Gofio: A staple ingredient in Canary cuisine ground roasted grain flour.

Local Beverages

  • Ron Miel Canario: The Canarian honey rum is a sweet and smooth drink made from honey and rum.
  • Tuno Indio: A fruit liquor made from cactus fruits, usually served over ice.

Where to Stay in Las Palmas: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Santa Catalina Hotel: A luxurious 5-star hotel with a spa, pool, and fitness center located near the heart of Las Palmas.
  • The House of Blues Hostel: A budget-friendly hostel located a short walk from Las Canteras beach, featuring funky designs and youthful ambiance.
  • La Casa de Vegueta: A guesthouse with a scenic courtyard located in the historic district, offering guests a chance to stay closer to landmarks.

What to Buy in Las Palmas: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Mojo Sauces: With garlic and chili flavors, the mojo sauces of Las Palmas are a great dip or salad dressing.
  • Handmade Pottery: Created by Canarian potters, this handmade pottery finds inspiration in the prehispanic style of the island.
  • Ron Miel: The honey rum of Las Palmas with a unique flavor.

Local Culture and Customs of Las Palmas

Canarian culture is a blend between Spanish, African, and Latin American cultures. The locals are called Canarios, and they are known for their friendly nature and hospitality. The Canary Islands have their distinctive dialect, and Las Palmas primarily speaks Canario but is also comfortable in Spanish.

Best Time to Visit Las Palmas

Las Palmas’ weather is warm and sunny year-round, with mild temperatures making it a pleasant destination to visit throughout the year. The summer season from June through August is the peak season, and the winter months from December through February is the low season. The shoulder seasons from April to May and September to October offer the best deals on travel and accommodation while enjoying the cooler temperature than the peak months.

Do’s and Don’ts of Las Palmas

Do:

  • Enjoy the Canarian cuisine in the local eateries
  • Visit the natural parks and go for hikes to explore the island
  • Try to speak a few phrases in Canario to make a great impression on the locals.

Don’t:

  • Do not wear swimwear or shorts in public areas other than the beach.
  • Do not break the local traffic rules, as they are strictly implemented
  • Do not attempt to climb the historic buildings or statues even for a photograph.

Safety and Health

Safety in Las Palmas

Las Palmas is considered a safe destination for travelers. However, tourists are advised to remain cautious and aware of pickpocketing incidents in crowded areas. Always make sure to keep your belongings safe while traveling, especially in public areas such as buses and markets.

Health in Las Palmas

The healthcare system of Las Palmas is efficient, and several public facilities offer medical services round the clock. Visitors are advised to purchase medical insurance before traveling and be aware of the sunstroke, as the sun can be very strong in summer.

Getting to Las Palmas: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Las Palmas

  • Gran Canaria International Airport: Located about 18 km from Las Palmas and is the main airport serving the Canaries.

Bus Stations Near Las Palmas

  • Parque San Telmo: The main bus station in Las Palmas that serves as the central hub for Transportes Interurbanos Gran Canaria, the public intercity bus system.

Other Options

Travelers can also opt for ferry services to Las Palmas from other neighboring islands of the Canaries. Renting a car can be an excellent option for those looking to explore the island at their own pace.

How to Get Around in Las Palmas

Las Palmas has a comprehensive local bus service system and a modern metro system for getting around the city. Taxis are also readily available throughout the city and are a reliable way to get around. For those looking for a more adventurous and eco-friendly vacation, renting a bike is an excellent option.

Where to Go Next from Las Palmas: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Maspalomas: A stunning beach resort located on the southern tip of the island, offering beautiful beaches and stunning dunes.
  • Tejeda: A charming mountain village known for its stunning views of Roque Bentayga and Roque Nublo.
  • Fuerteventura: A neighboring island of the Canaries with stunning sun-bleached beaches and sand dunes.

Conclusion

With its scenic beauty, fascinating culture, and exceptional cuisine, Las Palmas is an excellent destination for a once-in-a-lifetime vacation. Whether you’re looking for adventure or relaxation or want a mix of both, Las Palmas has everything you need. With this comprehensive guide, you have all the information needed to plan an exciting and memorable trip to Las Palmas that will forever remain etched in your memory.
