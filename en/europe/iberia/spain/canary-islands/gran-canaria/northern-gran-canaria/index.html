Introduction to Northern Gran Canaria

Northern Gran Canaria is a beautiful region located on the island of Gran Canaria, part of the Canary Islands in Spain. This stunning region has it all, from modern tourist resorts to traditional villages, from spectacular beaches to rugged mountain ranges. Northern Gran Canaria is a must-visit destination for travelers who want to explore the best of Spanish culture and nature.

In this comprehensive tour guide, we will take you through the top attractions, must-visit places, hiking/biking routes, food and drink scene, accommodation options, local culture, transportation, safety, and health of Northern Gran Canaria.

List of Places to Visit in Northern Gran Canaria

Here are some top places to visit in Northern Gran Canaria:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Cueva Pintada Museum and Archaeological Site: Explore the history and culture of Gran Canaria at this museum that houses ancient artifacts and features a reconstructed pre-Hispanic village.
  • Gáldar Old Town: Stroll through the cobblestone streets of this charming town and visit the famous church of Santiago de los Caballeros and Casa-Museo Antonio Padrón.
  • Church of Santa Maria de Guía: Admire the exquisite architecture and the ornate façade of this magnificent church, which houses a valuable collection of religious art.

Natural Attractions of Northern Gran Canaria: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Roque Nublo: Hike to this iconic natural landmark, a tall rock formation that offers stunning panoramic views of the island.
  • Los Tilos de Moya: Take a leisurely stroll through this beautiful laurel forest and enjoy the fresh air, cool shade, and peaceful ambiance.
  • Agaete Valley: Relax in this picturesque valley with its towering palm trees, charming white houses, and a lovely harbor with seafood restaurants.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Northern Gran Canaria

Northern Gran Canaria offers a plethora of hiking and biking trails, ranging from easy walks for beginners to challenging treks for experienced hikers. Here are some popular routes:

  • Barranco de los Cernícalos: This trail winds through a stunning ravine, with waterfalls, natural pools, and lush vegetation. It is a moderate hike that takes about three hours.
  • Pico de las Nieves: This is the highest peak of Gran Canaria, which offers breathtaking views of the island. The trail is challenging and takes about six hours.
  • Playa de las Canteras to El Confital: This is a scenic coastal trail that connects two beautiful beaches and offers stunning views of the sea and the cliffs. It is an easy walk that takes about one hour.

Best Things to Do in Northern Gran Canaria: Activities and Tours

Here are some top things to do in Northern Gran Canaria:

  • Surfing and Windsurfing: Experience the thrill of riding the waves at Playa de las Canteras, one of the best surf spots in Europe.
  • Day Trip to Tenerife: Take a ferry or a plane to the neighboring island of Tenerife to explore its stunning natural parks, charming towns, and the famous Mount Teide.
  • Food and Culture Tour: Join a guided tour that takes you to the best local restaurants, where you can savor the traditional Canarian cuisine and learn about the culture and history of the island.

Week-Long Itinerary for Northern Gran Canaria

Here is a comprehensive week-long itinerary for Northern Gran Canaria:

Day Activities
1 Explore Las Palmas de Gran Canaria, the capital city. Visit the historical district of Vegueta, the beaches of Las Canteras and Alcaravaneras, and the modern shopping centers.
2 Hike to Roque Nublo and enjoy the panoramic views. Have lunch in Tejeda, a charming village known for its almond sweets and artisanal pottery.
3 Visit the charming towns of Gáldar and Guía, and learn about the local history and culture. Have dinner at a seafood restaurant in Agaete and watch the sunset over the sea.
4 Relax in the natural pools of Los Charcones in El Risco and have a picnic lunch. Visit the Cueva Pintada Museum and Archaeological Site in Gáldar in the afternoon.
5 Take a coastal walk from Playa del Inglés to Maspalomas and admire the dunes and the lighthouse. Have lunch in Meloneras, a luxurious resort area with stylish restaurants and shops.
6 Rent a car and drive to the north coast, where you can enjoy the green landscapes of Firgas and Teror. Have lunch in a bar in Valleseco and taste the traditional “papas arrugadas” with mojo sauce.
7 Take a day trip to Tenerife and climb Mount Teide, the highest peak in Spain. Have lunch in the charming town of La Orotava and visit the Botanical Garden in Puerto de la Cruz.

Food and Drink Scene of Northern Gran Canaria

Northern Gran Canaria has a rich culinary heritage, influenced by its Spanish, African, and Latin American roots. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Papas arrugadas: Small, wrinkled potatoes boiled in saltwater and served with mojo sauce (a spicy blend of garlic, peppers, and cumin).
  • Gofio: Roasted flour made from maize or wheat, used to make bread, cakes, and porridge. It is a staple food of the Canarian cuisine.
  • Conejo en salmorejo: Rabbit meat marinated in a sauce of vinegar, garlic, and spices. It is a traditional dish of the region.

Local Beverages

  • Ron miel: Honey rum, a sweet and smooth liquor made from local honey and aged rum.
  • Cerveza Tropical: A refreshing beer with a fruity flavor, brewed in Las Palmas de Gran Canaria.
  • Café cortado: Espresso coffee with a drop of milk, often served as a mid-morning or mid-afternoon break.

Where to Stay in Northern Gran Canaria: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Northern Gran Canaria offers a wide range of accommodation options, from budget hostels to luxury resorts. Here are some top choices:

  • Santa Catalina: A grand hotel located in the heart of Las Palmas de Gran Canaria, with elegant rooms, a spa, and a rooftop pool.
  • La Cueva de Doña Juana: A cave house turned guesthouse, located in Artenara, with rustic charm and stunning views.
  • Hotel Reina Isabel: A beachfront hotel with a modern design and a rooftop terrace, located in Las Palmas de Gran Canaria.

What to Buy in Northern Gran Canaria: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Northern Gran Canaria is a great place to buy souvenirs and local products, such as:

  • Handmade pottery: From plates to jugs, bowls to sculptures, the Canarian pottery is colorful and unique.
  • Spices and condiments: Buy some mojo sauce or pimentón (smoked paprika) to bring home the flavors of the island.
  • Aloe vera products: Gran Canaria is known for its aloe vera plants, which are used to make skin care and beauty products.

Local Culture and Customs of Northern Gran Canaria

The culture of Northern Gran Canaria is deeply rooted in the island’s history and geography. The Canarians are proud of their traditions, but also open to new influences and ideas. Here are some interesting facts:

  • Fiestas: The Canarians like to celebrate their heritage with colorful and lively festivals, such as Carnival, the Fiesta de la Rama, and the Romería.
  • Language: The official language is Spanish, but many people also speak English, German, and French.
  • Island lifestyle: The Canarians enjoy a relaxed and laid-back lifestyle, with plenty of outdoor activities, siestas, and social gatherings.

Best Time to Visit Northern Gran Canaria

The weather in Northern Gran Canaria is pleasant all year round, with mild temperatures and little rainfall. The best time to visit depends on your preferences:

  • Peak season: From June to September, the island is crowded with tourists and the prices are high. It is the best time for beach lovers and water sports enthusiasts.
  • Off-season: From October to May, the island is quieter and the prices are lower. It is a good time for hikers, culture enthusiasts, and foodies.

Do’s and Don’ts of Northern Gran Canaria

Here are some do’s and don’ts to keep in mind while visiting Northern Gran Canaria:

Do:

  • Do respect the local customs and traditions: When visiting villages and churches, dress modestly and behave respectfully.
  • Do try the local cuisine and beverages: Gran Canaria has a rich gastronomy, so don’t miss the opportunity to taste the local dishes and drinks.
  • Do use sunscreen: The sun is strong in Gran Canaria, so protect your skin with high SPF sunscreen.

Don’t:

  • Don’t leave valuables in your car: Car theft is common in the island, especially in tourist areas.
  • Don’t litter: Keep the island clean and dispose of your trash properly.
  • Don’t miss the opportunity to learn about the local culture: The Canarians are proud of their traditions and history, so take the time to explore the museums, festivals, and cultural sites.

Safety and Health

Northern Gran Canaria is a safe and healthy destination, with modern facilities and qualified medical staff. However, travelers should take some precautions:

Safety in Northern Gran Canaria

  • Be careful while driving: The roads in Northern Gran Canaria can be narrow, winding, and steep. Drive cautiously, especially at night.
  • Watch out for pickpockets: Tourist areas and beaches can be crowded with pickpockets and scam artists. Keep your valuables safe and avoid carrying large sums of cash.

Health in Northern Gran Canaria

  • Drink bottled water: The tap water in Gran Canaria is safe to drink, but it may have a different taste than what you are used to. To avoid stomach problems, drink bottled water.
  • Apply insect repellent: In some areas, especially near the mountains and forests, mosquitoes and other insects can be a nuisance. Apply insect repellent to avoid bites.

Getting to Northern Gran Canaria: Transportation Options and Logistics

Northern Gran Canaria is easily accessible from the Canary Islands and the Spanish mainland. Here are some transportation options:

Airports Near Northern Gran Canaria

  • Gran Canaria International Airport: Located in the south of the island, about 25 km from Las Palmas de Gran Canaria.

Bus Stations Near Northern Gran Canaria

  • Las Palmas de Gran Canaria Bus Station: Located in the central area of the city, it offers connections to all parts of the island.

Other Options

  • Ferry: There are regular ferry services from Tenerife, Fuerteventura, and Lanzarote to the ports of Las Palmas de Gran Canaria and Agaete.
  • Car rental: Renting a car is a popular option for exploring Northern Gran Canaria at your own pace. There are several car rental agencies at the airport and in the cities.

How to Get Around in Northern Gran Canaria

Getting around in Northern Gran Canaria is easy and convenient, thanks to the modern transportation network and the compact size of the island. Here are some options:

  • Bus: The bus network of Gran Canaria is efficient and reliable, with frequent services to all parts of the island.
  • Taxi: Taxis are widely available in the cities and tourist areas, but they are more expensive than the bus.
  • Car rental: Renting a car is the best option for exploring the rural areas and the remote beaches of Northern Gran Canaria.

Where to Go Next from Northern Gran Canaria: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Northern Gran Canaria is a great starting point for exploring the other Canary Islands and the Spanish mainland. Here are some nearby destinations and excursions:

  • Tenerife: Take a ferry or a plane to the neighboring island of Tenerife, where you can climb Mount Teide, visit the picturesque towns of La Orotava and Garachico, and swim with dolphins in the sea.
  • Spain: Take a flight to Madrid, Barcelona, Seville, or Valencia, and discover the rich history, art, and culture of mainland Spain.
  • Morocco: Take a ferry from Agaete or Las Palmas de Gran Canaria to the Moroccan port of Tanger, and explore the exotic markets, mosques, and palaces of this North African country.

Conclusion

Northern Gran Canaria is a fascinating destination for travelers who want to experience the best of Spanish culture and nature. With its stunning landscapes, historic sites, delicious food, and warm hospitality, Northern Gran Canaria is a must-visit destination that will leave you with lasting memories.
