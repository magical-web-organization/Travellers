Introduction to Corralejo

Corralejo is a beautiful coastal town with a vibrant atmosphere in Fuerteventura, Canary Islands. It has become a popular tourist destination because of its stunning beaches, exciting water sports, and outdoor adventures. Whether you want to relax on the beach or explore the local culture, Corralejo has everything you need for a memorable vacation. Here’s our comprehensive tour guide to help you plan your trip to Corralejo.

List of Places to Visit in Corralejo

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Tostón Lighthouse: This iconic lighthouse, built-in 1897, is one of the top attractions in Corralejo. It offers stunning views of the ocean and the surrounding landscapes.

  • Centro de Arte Canario-Museo Antonio Padrón: This museum showcases the work of Antonio Padron, a famous Canarian artist. You’ll see a collection of his paintings, sculptures, and other works of art.

  • Iglesia de Nuestra Señora del Carmen: This is a beautiful church with an impressive interior and exterior design. It’s a must-visit attraction for those interested in religious and cultural history.

Natural Attractions of Corralejo: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Isla de Lobos: Take a boat trip from Corralejo to Isla de Lobos and enjoy the stunning scenery on the way. You’ll see crystal clear waters, seabirds, and dolphins playing in the ocean.

  • Dunas de Corralejo Natural Park: This sand dune park is a unique natural wonder and a protected area. Here, you can go hiking, biking, or explore the sandy beaches and lagoons.

  • Calderon Hondo Volcano: For a challenging hike, head to Calderon Hondo, a dormant volcano. It takes about an hour to walk to the top, but the views are breathtaking.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Corralejo

Here are some popular walking and biking routes you can explore in Corralejo:

  • Route 1: This route takes you from Corralejo to El Cotillo, a small fishing village on the west coast of Fuerteventura. It’s a 7.5-mile (12-kilometer) walk or bike ride, and you’ll see stunning coastlines and landscapes.

  • Route 2: This route takes you through the Dunas de Corralejo Natural Park, a protected area with stunning sand dunes, beaches, and lagoons.

  • Route 3: This route takes you to the top of Calderon Hondo Volcano. It’s a challenging hike, but the views from the top are spectacular.

Best Things to Do in Corralejo: Activities and Tours

  • Water Sports: Corralejo and the surrounding areas are popular for water sports, including surfing, windsurfing, kiteboarding, and paddleboarding. You can rent equipment or take lessons from local instructors.

  • Boat Trips: Take a boat trip to explore the stunning coastline, including Isla de Lobos, Los Lobos, or Lanzarote.

  • Scuba Diving and Snorkeling: Discover the underwater world of Corralejo with scuba diving or snorkeling tours. You’ll see various marine life, including colorful fish, turtles, and stingrays.

Week-Long Itinerary for Corralejo

Here’s a suggested week-long itinerary to help you plan your trip to Corralejo:

  • Day 1: Arrive in Corralejo and explore the local area.

  • Day 2: Take a boat trip to Isla de Lobos and enjoy the stunning scenery.

  • Day 3: Visit Dunas de Corralejo Natural Park and go hiking or biking.

  • Day 4: Discover the local culture and visit the Centro de Arte Canario-Museo Antonio Padrón and Iglesia de Nuestra Señora del Carmen.

  • Day 5: Relax on the beach or take part in some water sports.

  • Day 6: Take a scuba diving or snorkeling tour and discover the underwater world.

  • Day 7: Take a day trip to Lanzarote or explore local markets and souvenir shops.

Food and Drink Scene of Corralejo

Must-Try Dishes

  • Papas arrugadas: These are small, boiled potatoes with a salty crust. They are typically served with a spicy sauce called Mojo picón.

  • Gofio: This dish is made from roasted corn or wheat and is a staple of the Canarian diet. It can be eaten in a variety of ways, including as a soup, pudding, or porridge.

  • Queso Majorero: This is a type of cheese made from goat’s milk and is one of the most popular cheeses in the Canary Islands.

Local Beverages

  • Ron miel: This is a honey rum and is a popular drink in the Canary Islands. It’s similar to a liqueur and is typically consumed as a digestif.

  • Malvasia wine: This is a sweet white wine that’s made from grapes grown in the Canary Islands. It’s perfect as an aperitif or with dessert.

  • Tropical beer: This is a locally brewed beer in the Canary Islands. It’s a refreshing drink to enjoy on a hot day.

Where to Stay in Corralejo: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Gran Hotel Bahía Real: This five-star hotel offers beautiful accommodations with stunning views of the ocean. It’s located close to the beach and has a spa, pool, and restaurant.

  • Oasis Duna Aparthotel: This aparthotel offers comfortable accommodations with a pool, restaurant, and bar. It’s located close to the beach and is perfect for families.

  • One Hostel & Suites: This hostel offers affordable accommodations with a communal kitchen and lounge area. It’s centrally located and is ideal for budget travelers.

What to Buy in Corralejo: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Aloe Vera Products: Aloe vera is grown in the Canary Islands and is used to produce a range of skin care products, including lotions, creams, and gels.

  • Handmade Pottery: Ceramics and pottery are a significant part of Canarian culture, and there are many shops selling handmade items.

  • Mojo sauce: Mojo sauce is a spicy sauce that’s commonly served with papas arrugadas. You can buy it in jars to take home with you.

Local Culture and Customs of Corralejo

The Canarian culture is unique and has a rich history. The locals are friendly and welcoming and take pride in their heritage. Here are a few cultural aspects to keep in mind while visiting Corralejo:

  • The official language is Spanish, but many locals speak English.

  • The locals love to eat and enjoy long meals with friends and family.

  • Sundays are typically reserved for spending time with family and attending church.

Best Time to Visit Corralejo

The best time to visit Corralejo is from April to October when the weather is warm and sunny. During the winter months, the weather can be mild but can also be quite windy. The summer months can be busy, so plan ahead and book your accommodations early.

Do’s and Don’ts of Corralejo

Do:

  • Try the local food and drink.

  • Be respectful of the local culture and traditions.

  • Learn a few basic Spanish phrases.

Don’t:

  • Leave your valuables unattended on the beach.

  • Wear inappropriate clothing in religious places.

  • Break any environmental or cultural regulations.

Safety and Health

Safety in Corralejo

Corralejo is a safe destination for tourists. However, it’s always recommended to take necessary safety precautions and avoid isolated areas at night. Make sure you keep your belongings safe and secure.

Health in Corralejo

The medical facilities in Corralejo are good, and many hospitals and clinics have English-speaking staff. If you require medication, make sure to bring a sufficient supply with you. The tap water is safe to drink, but bottled water is readily available.

Getting to Corralejo: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Corralejo

The nearest airport to Corralejo is Fuerteventura Airport, which is around 30 minutes away by car. There are direct flights to Fuerteventura from many European cities.

Bus Stations Near Corralejo

Corralejo has a bus station that connects it to other parts of Fuerteventura and the Canary Islands. There are also daily buses to and from the airport.

Other Options

Taxis and private transfers are also available from the airport to Corralejo, and car rentals are available for those who prefer to drive themselves.

How to Get Around in Corralejo

Corralejo is a small town, and most attractions are within walking distance. However, if you prefer, the local bus service is reliable and affordable. Taxis are also available.

Where to Go Next from Corralejo: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • El Cotillo: This small fishing village is only a short drive from Corralejo and has beautiful beaches and stunning coastlines.

  • Puerto del Rosario: This is the capital of Fuerteventura and has a vibrant atmosphere with a mix of modern and traditional architecture.

  • Lanzarote: Take a day trip to Lanzarote and explore Timanfaya National Park, home to the famous Fire Mountains.

Conclusion

Corralejo is a beautiful destination that offers stunning beaches, exciting outdoor adventures, and a vibrant culture. Whether you’re looking to relax or explore, Corralejo has something for everyone. Use this comprehensive tour guide to plan your trip to Corralejo and get the most out of your vacation.
