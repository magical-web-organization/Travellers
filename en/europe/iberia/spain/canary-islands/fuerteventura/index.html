Introduction to Fuerteventura

Fuerteventura is one of the Canary Islands, located off the coast of Africa but officially part of Spain. It is known for its sunny climate, pristine beaches, and volcanic landscapes. The island is relatively unspoiled, which makes it the perfect destination for those looking to get away from the crowds and relax in a quiet and peaceful environment.

The island has plenty to offer visitors, from cultural attractions and outdoor adventures to vibrant nightlife. With this comprehensive guide, you will discover everything you need to know to plan a memorable trip to Fuerteventura.

List of Places to Visit in Fuerteventura

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • La Casa Museo Unamuno: This museum is located in Puerto del Rosario and was the former home of Miguel de Unamuno, a famous Spanish philosopher and writer. The museum showcases some of his personal belongings and original manuscripts.

  • Iglesia de Nuestra Señora de la Candelaria: This church is one of the oldest buildings in Fuerteventura and has a rich history dating back to the 17th century. It is located in La Oliva and features ornate decorations and stunning stained glass windows.

Natural Attractions of Fuerteventura: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Corralejo Natural Park: This park is located in the north of the island and is home to stunning white sand dunes and crystal clear waters. It is the perfect place for a relaxing day at the beach or a fun-filled day of watersports.

  • Montana de Tindaya: This mountain is considered sacred by the indigenous tribes of Fuerteventura and offers breathtaking views of the island from the top. It is a popular hiking spot among locals and visitors alike.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Fuerteventura

  • El Cardon Route: This 10-kilometer hike is considered one of the best in Fuerteventura. It takes you through the heart of the island’s volcanic landscapes, including craters, lava fields, and extinct volcanoes.

  • Lajares to Majanicho Bike Route: This 25-kilometer bike route takes you along the coast of Fuerteventura, passing by some of the island’s most beautiful beaches and offering stunning views of the Atlantic Ocean.

Best Things to Do in Fuerteventura: Activities and Tours

  • Surfing: Fuerteventura is considered one of the best surfing spots in Europe, with consistent waves and warm waters. There are plenty of surf schools and rental shops around the island for beginners and experienced surfers alike.

  • Boat Tours: There are many boat tours available that take you around the island’s stunning coastline, offering breathtaking views of the ocean and the island’s natural beauty. Many of these tours also include snorkeling and swimming stops.

Week-Long Itinerary for Fuerteventura

If you are planning a week-long trip to Fuerteventura, here is a suggested itinerary:

  • Day 1: Explore Puerto del Rosario and visit La Casa Museo Unamuno.
  • Day 2: Spend the day at Corralejo Natural Park.
  • Day 3: Go hiking on the El Cardon Route.
  • Day 4: Visit the Iglesia de Nuestra Señora de la Candelaria and explore La Oliva.
  • Day 5: Take a boat tour around the island.
  • Day 6: Go surfing or take a bike tour.
  • Day 7: Relax on the beach and soak up the sun.

Food and Drink Scene of Fuerteventura

Must-Try Dishes

  • Papas Arrugadas: This traditional Canarian dish consists of potatoes boiled in salt water and served with “mojo,” a sauce made with garlic, oil, vinegar, and spices.

  • Gofio: This is a type of flour made from toasted grains, typically wheat or corn. It is used in many traditional Canarian dishes and can be served sweet or savory.

Local Beverages

  • Ron Miel: This is a honey-flavored rum that is very popular in the Canary Islands.

  • Malvasia: This is a sweet white wine made from grapes grown on the island.

Where to Stay in Fuerteventura: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

There are plenty of accommodations options in Fuerteventura to suit every budget. Some recommended options include:

  • Gran Hotel Atlantis Bahia Real GL: This luxury hotel is located in Corralejo and offers stunning views of the Atlantic Ocean.
  • Casa Santa Maria: This traditional guesthouse is located in La Oliva and offers cozy and comfortable accommodations.
  • Rocky Hostel: This hostel is located in the heart of Puerto del Rosario and offers affordable accommodations for budget travelers.

What to Buy in Fuerteventura: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Cheese: Fuerteventura is known for its delicious goat cheese, which is made using traditional methods and is widely available in many shops around the island.

  • Aloe Vera Products: Aloe Vera is grown extensively in the Canary Islands, and there are many shops that sell a range of skincare products made from this plant.

Local Culture and Customs of Fuerteventura

The local culture in Fuerteventura is heavily influenced by its history and geography. The island has a rich tradition of pottery and handicrafts, as well as traditional music and dance. It is also known for its vibrant Carnival celebrations, which take place in various towns and cities around the island.

Best Time to Visit Fuerteventura

Fuerteventura enjoys warm and sunny weather all year round, making it a popular destination for tourists from all over the world. However, the best time to visit is during the spring and autumn months when the weather is milder and there are fewer crowds.

Do’s and Don’ts of Fuerteventura

Do:

  • Do try the local cuisine and beverages.
  • Do respect the island’s natural beauty and avoid littering.
  • Do wear sunscreen and protective clothing, as the sun can be very strong.

Don’t:

  • Don’t leave your valuables unattended on the beach or in public places.
  • Don’t be disrespectful towards the local culture and customs.
  • Don’t touch or disturb the wildlife, especially the endangered species found on the island.

Safety and Health

Safety in Fuerteventura

Fuerteventura is a safe destination for tourists, with relatively low crime rates. However, visitors should take precautions to ensure their safety, such as keeping an eye on their belongings and avoiding walking alone at night in unfamiliar areas.

Health in Fuerteventura

Visitors to Fuerteventura do not require any specific vaccinations or health precautions. However, it is recommended to drink bottled water and take necessary precautions to prevent sunburn and dehydration.

Getting to Fuerteventura: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Fuerteventura

The island has a major airport, Fuerteventura Airport, which is located in El Matorral and serves many international and domestic flights.

Bus Stations Near Fuerteventura

The main bus station is located in Puerto del Rosario and offers regular services to all major towns and cities on the island.

Other Options

There are also ferry services available, which connect the island with other nearby destinations such as Lanzarote and Gran Canaria.

How to Get Around in Fuerteventura

The best way to get around the island is by car or bike, as public transportation is limited. Rental cars and bikes are widely available in all major towns and airports.

Where to Go Next from Fuerteventura: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

There are many nearby destinations and excursions that visitors to Fuerteventura can enjoy, including:

  • Lanzarote: This nearby island is home to stunning volcanic landscapes and beaches, as well as many cultural attractions and outdoor adventures.

  • Betancuria: This historic town is located in the center of Fuerteventura and is known for its Moorish and Spanish architecture.

Conclusion

Fuerteventura offers visitors a unique and unforgettable experience, with its stunning natural beauty, rich cultural heritage, and warm and welcoming locals. With this comprehensive guide, you are ready to plan the perfect trip to Fuerteventura and make memories that will last a lifetime.
