A Comprehensive Travel Guide to La Gomera

La Gomera is one of the smallest of the Canary Islands, yet it is a real hidden jewel that offers an authentic experience. It has something to offer every traveler - breathtaking scenery, stunning beaches, unique cuisine, and a rich cultural history. In this comprehensive guide, you will discover the best things to see, do, and experience in La Gomera.

Introduction to La Gomera

Located in the Canary Islands, La Gomera is a small island known for its stunning scenery and unique culture. The island’s landscape is characterized by deep valleys, rolling hills, and ancient forests, which are often shrouded in mist.

But La Gomera is more than just a beautiful island. Its history is rich and unique, marked by battles, piracy, and the conquest of former inhabitants, the Guanches. The island has also played a prominent role in the history of the Americas, as it was an important stop on the trade route between Europe and the New World.

List of Places to Visit in La Gomera

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

If you’re interested in history and culture, La Gomera won’t disappoint. Here are some must-visit cultural attractions.

  • La Gomera Archaeological Museum: Located in the capital city of San Sebastian, this museum is home to an impressive collection of Guanche artifacts.
  • Torre del Conde: This 15th-century tower is one of the most important historical sites in La Gomera. It is named after the governor who fended off the pirate attacks in the 15th century.
  • Church of La Asunción: This charming 15th-century church is a testament to the island’s religious heritage.

Natural Attractions of La Gomera: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

La Gomera is home to some of the most stunning natural attractions in the Canary Islands.

  • Garajonay National Park: This ancient forest is a UNESCO world heritage site and home to 400-year-old trees, many unique to La Gomera.
  • Valle Gran Rey: This beautiful valley is famous for its stunning beaches, clear waters, and dramatic cliffs.
  • Los Órganos: This natural monument is a series of basalt rock formations that resemble organ pipes. The views are spectacular, especially at sunset.

Hiking/Biking Routes of La Gomera

La Gomera is a hiker’s paradise, and there are trails that cater to all levels of experience.

  • Barranco de Vallehermoso: This hiking trail offers beautiful views of the valley and is perfect for experienced hikers.
  • Mirador de Abrante to Playa de Santiago: This trail is perfect for nature lovers and offers stunning views of the ocean.

Best Things to Do in La Gomera: Activities and Tours

Whether you’re traveling alone, with friends or family, La Gomera has something for everyone.

  • Whale watching: La Gomera is an important stopover for many species of whales, and there are several companies that offer whale-watching tours.
  • Scuba diving: The clear waters around La Gomera are a scuba diver’s dream.
  • Beach-hopping: La Gomera has some amazing beaches that cater to all types of travelers.

Week-Long Itinerary for La Gomera

For those who have more time to explore the island, here’s a week-long itinerary.

Day Activity
1 Explore San Sebastian and visit the Torre del Conde
2 Hike in the Garajonay National Park
3 Visit the charming village of Vallehermoso
4 Relax on one of the beaches in Valle Gran Rey
5 Go scuba diving and explore the underwater world
6 Take a day trip to neighbouring island of Tenerife
7 Visit the natural monument of Los Órganos and watch sunset

Food and Drink Scene of La Gomera

No visit to La Gomera would be complete without trying some of the local cuisine and beverages.

Must-Try Dishes

  • Almogrote: This is a delicious spread made from cheese, garlic, and other spices.
  • Gofio: This is a traditional Canarian dish made from ground toasted corn.
  • Papas arrugadas: These are small potatoes boiled in saltwater and served with a spicy sauce.

Local Beverages

  • Ron miel: This sweet honey rum is a favorite among locals.
  • Vino de La Gomera: Made from the unique grape varieties grown on the island, this wine is delicious and often overlooked by visitors.

Where to Stay in La Gomera: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

La Gomera offers plenty of accommodation options for all types of travelers.

  • Parador de La Gomera: This historic hotel is located in a restored 15th-century castle and offers stunning views of the ocean.
  • Jardin Tecina: This 4-star resort is located near Playa de Santiago and offers an outdoor pool and spa.
  • Casa Rural La Punta: This charming guest house is located in Vallehermoso and offers comfortable rooms and a beautiful courtyard.

What to Buy in La Gomera: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Crafts and ceramics: The island is home to many talented artists and craftsmen, and their creations are available in many shops around the island.
  • Gofio: This traditional Canarian dish is also available in souvenir tins that make great gifts.
  • Local wines and cheese: Wine and cheese made from unique local grape varieties and traditional recipes make great souvenirs.

Local Culture and Customs of La Gomera

The people of La Gomera have a rich cultural heritage, and their customs have been preserved over the centuries.

  • Whistling language: The people of La Gomera have a unique whistling language that was used to communicate across mountain valleys.
  • Festivals and fiestas: The island is home to many vibrant festivals throughout the year, celebrating everything from local saints to the harvest season.

Best Time to Visit La Gomera

The best time to visit La Gomera is in the spring and autumn when the weather is mild, and the crowds are smaller.

Do’s and Don’ts of La Gomera

Do:

  • Be respectful of local customs and traditions.
  • Try the local cuisine and beverage offerings.
  • Take advantage of the hiking trails and outdoor activities.

Don’t:

  • Leave litter or damage the natural environment.
  • Be noisy or disruptive in public places.
  • Ignore local advice regarding safety and health.

Safety and Health

Safety in La Gomera

La Gomera is generally a safe place to visit, but it’s always best to take basic precautions to avoid any risks. Always stay aware of your surroundings and keep an eye on your belongings. In case of emergencies, dial 112.

Health in La Gomera

It’s always wise to take the usual precautions when it comes to your health. Make sure you have adequate travel insurance and consult with your doctor before traveling to La Gomera. The island has a hospital in San Sebastian, and there are several medical centers around the island to cater to tourists.

Getting to La Gomera: Transportation Options and Logistics

Getting to La Gomera is easy, and there are several transportation options to choose from.

Airports Near La Gomera

La Gomera Airport is the only airport on the island and is served by Binter Canarias.

Bus Stations Near La Gomera

There are several bus stations around the island, and they offer regular services to major towns and attractions.

Other Options

Ferries are another popular option for those wanting to travel to or from La Gomera. There are regular ferries that connect the island with other Canary Islands and the mainland.

How to Get Around in La Gomera

Getting around La Gomera is easy, and there are several transportation options available.

  • Buses: La Gomera has a good public transportation system, and buses are the most popular mode of transport.
  • Taxis: Taxis are readily available, and fares are reasonable.
  • Car rental: Car rental is available on the island.

Where to Go Next from La Gomera: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

La Gomera is a great place to use as a base to explore other nearby destinations.

  • Tenerife: This neighboring island offers plenty of activities and attractions, including the famous Mount Teide and Loro Parque.
  • La Palma: This island is home to the famous La Caldera de Taburiente National Park.
  • Gran Canaria: This island offers a unique blend of stunning beaches and dramatic mountain landscapes.

Conclusion

La Gomera is a hidden jewel in the Canary Islands, offering a unique blend of natural beauty, cultural heritage, and outdoor adventures. The island’s rich history, unique cuisine, and friendly locals make it a must-visit destination for anyone traveling to the Canary Islands.
