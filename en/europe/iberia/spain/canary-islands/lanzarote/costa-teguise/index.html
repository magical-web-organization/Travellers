Introduction to Costa Teguise

Costa Teguise is a coastal town that sits on the east coast of Lanzarote, one of the Canary Islands. With its stunning beaches, laidback vibes, and year-round warm weather, Costa Teguise is a popular destination for tourists seeking sun, sea, and sand. But the town isn’t just for beach lovers; it is also teeming with cultural and natural attractions and activities that cater to all types of travelers.

List of Places to Visit in Costa Teguise

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Casa-Museo César Manrique: This is the former home of César Manrique, a well-known artist and architect who played a significant role in shaping the island’s aesthetic and cultural identity. Visitors can walk through the house and admire Manrique’s work and personal objects.
  • Pueblo Marinero: This is a charming complex of white buildings that house art galleries, bars, and shops. The buildings are designed to resemble a traditional Spanish fishing village, and the area hosts cultural events and festivals throughout the year.
  • Castillo de San José: This 18th-century fortress now serves as an art museum that showcases contemporary works from local and international artists.

Natural Attractions of Costa Teguise: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Lagomar Museum: This former home of actor Omar Sharif is carved out of a volcanic rock formation and is now a museum, showcasing a range of film memorabilia and artwork.
  • Jardín de Cactus: This garden is home to over 7,000 species of cacti from all over the world. Visitors can explore the garden’s winding paths and take in views of the surrounding landscapes.
  • Rancho Texas Lanzarote Park: This Western-themed park features animal shows, a water park, and rides.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Costa Teguise

  • La Cueva de los Verdes: This is a popular spot for hiking that takes visitors through a volcanic cave system and provides a glimpse into the island’s geological history.
  • Monte Corona: This is a popular spot for biking that takes riders through lava fields and provides stunning views of the coast.

Best Things to Do in Costa Teguise: Activities and Tours

  • Water Sports: Costa Teguise’s beaches offer a range of water sports, among which include windsurfing, scuba diving, and snorkeling.
  • Sunset Cruise: Visitors can take a sunset cruise along the coast and witness the island’s stunning colors as the sun goes down.
  • Wine Tour: The island is known for its wine production, and visitors can take a tour of the vineyards and sample the local wines.

Food and Drink Scene of Costa Teguise

Must-Try Dishes

  • Papas Arrugadas: This is a local dish of salt-baked potatoes that is often served with mojo, a spicy sauce made of garlic, olive oil, and other ingredients.
  • Sancocho: This is a traditional fish stew that is made with salted fish, sweet potatoes, and onions.
  • Gofio: This is a roasted cornflour that is often used as a flour substitute in bread, desserts, and sauces.

Local Beverages

  • Lanzarote Wine: This wine is produced on the island and enjoys a unique flavor due to the island’s volcanic soil and microclimate.
  • Café Barraquito: This is a local coffee that is often served with a dash of liquor and sweetened condensed milk.

Where to Stay in Costa Teguise: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Barceló Teguise Beach: This adults-only hotel offers a range of amenities, such as a pool, a spa, and a gym, and is located just a short walk from the beach.
  • Apartamentos Oceano: These apartments are located near the town center and offer a range of amenities, such as a pool, laundry services, and Wi-Fi.
  • Boutique Hotel Palacio Ico: This hotel is housed in a historic 18th-century palace and features luxurious rooms, a pool, and a restaurant.

What to Buy in Costa Teguise: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Aloe Vera Products: The island is known for its aloe vera production, and visitors can buy a range of skincare and beauty products made of the plant.
  • Local Artwork: Visitors can buy local artwork from galleries and shops in the town center.
  • Lanzarote Wine: Visitors can bring back bottles of the island’s unique wine as a souvenir.

Local Culture and Customs of Costa Teguise

The island’s culture is heavily influenced by its Spanish and African heritage, and its people are known for their warmth and hospitality. Visitors should take note of local customs, such as dressing modestly when visiting churches and other religious sites.

Best Time to Visit Costa Teguise

The island enjoys a warm climate all year round, but the best time to visit is from May to September, when temperatures are at their highest and the weather is generally dry and sunny.

Do’s and Don’ts of Costa Teguise

Do:

  • Dress appropriately for religious sites and events
  • Try the local cuisine and drinks
  • Engage with the locals and learn about their culture

Don’t:

  • Leave valuables unattended on the beach
  • Be disrespectful to the locals and their customs
  • Stray off designated paths and hiking trails

Safety and Health

Safety in Costa Teguise

The town is generally safe, but visitors should take the usual precautions, such as locking their valuables in a safe and being aware of their surroundings.

Health in Costa Teguise

Visitors should ensure they have adequate travel insurance and take precautions against sunstroke and dehydration due to the island’s warm temperatures.

Getting to Costa Teguise: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Costa Teguise

The island’s main airport is Lanzarote Airport, which is located about 15 kilometers from Costa Teguise.

Bus Stations Near Costa Teguise

The town’s bus station is located in the town center and offers services to other parts of the island.

Other Options

Visitors can also hire cars or take taxis to get to and from the town.

How to Get Around in Costa Teguise

The town is relatively small and can be easily explored on foot. Visitors can also rent bikes or cars if they want to explore the surrounding areas.

Where to Go Next from Costa Teguise: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Timanfaya National Park: This park is located about 30 minutes from Costa Teguise and is home to a range of volcanic landscapes and geological formations.
  • La Graciosa: This island is located off the coast of Lanzarote and is only accessible by ferry. It is known for its rugged landscapes and beautiful beaches.
  • Teguise: This is the island’s former capital and is home to a range of historic buildings, traditional markets, and cultural events.

Conclusion

Costa Teguise offers visitors a range of attractions and experiences, from stunning beaches and natural landscapes to cultural landmarks and unique cuisine. With its warm weather and laidback vibes, the town is the perfect destination for travelers seeking a relaxing and enjoyable vacation.
