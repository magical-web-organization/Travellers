Arrecife Travel Guide: Discover the Best of Lanzarote

Welcome to Arrecife, the capital city of Lanzarote in the Canary Islands! This vibrant city is a perfect blend of natural beauty and urban charm. With its stunning beaches, beautiful architecture, and rich cultural heritage, Arrecife is a must-visit destination for anyone planning a trip to the Canary Islands. In this guide, we’ll take you on a journey through the city, exploring all the best things to see and do in Arrecife. Let’s get started!

List of Places to Visit in Arrecife

Arrecife offers a wide range of attractions for visitors to enjoy. Here are some of the must-see places that you can’t miss:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Castillo de San Jose: This ancient castle-turned-museum features works of art from world-famous artists like Cesar Manrique, as well as an impressive collection of modern art exhibitions.
  • Casa de los Arroyo: Explore the heritage of Lanzarote with a visit to Casa de los Arroyo, a 17th-century house with a beautiful courtyard and displays that unravel the history of Lanzarote.
  • San Gines Church: One of the oldest churches in Lanzarote, this historic church features an exquisite interior and architectural design that dates back to the 17th century.

Natural Attractions of Arrecife: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Charco de San Gines: This beautiful lagoon surrounded by fishing boats will enchant its visitors with the mesmerizing emerald-green waters.
  • Playa del Reducto: This stunning beach is located in the centre of the city, boasting white sand and turquoise waters perfect for swimming, sunbathing, and enjoying the beautiful views across the bay. It also offers plenty of restaurants and cafes.
  • Parque Islas Canarias: An urban park where you can relax and unwind amidst the greenery and beautiful landscapes. A perfect place for picnic lovers with fascinating views.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Arrecife

Arrecife is a great destination for outdoor enthusiasts who enjoy biking and hiking. There are many routes that you can take for biking and hiking that will take you through beautiful landscapes, rocky cliffs and beaches. One of the most popular routes is the Timanfaya National Park which offers a range of trails that cater to all levels of skill and fitness.

Best Things to Do in Arrecife: Activities and Tours

From exploring the city’s culture and history to enjoying outdoor activities, Arrecife has something for everyone. Here are some of the best things to do in Arrecife:

  • Aqua Park Costa Teguise: A fantastic water park which offers a range of attractions and rides that cater to all ages. Perfect for a family day out.
  • Rancho Texas: A dream park for animal lovers, with attractions like the pool and shows featuring exotic animals, a cowboy ranch and more.
  • El Mercado: This vibrant market is perfect for foodies, featuring a range of fresh produce that you can taste, as well as local crafts and souvenirs to buy

Week-Long Itinerary for Arrecife

Here’s a suggested week-long itinerary that will help you to get the most out of your trip to Arrecife:

Day Activity
Day 1 Explore the history of the city with a visit to Castillo de San Jose and Casa de los Arroyo
Day 2 Enjoy the sun, sand, and sea at Playa del Reducto
Day 3 Take a tour of the beautiful Timanfaya National Park
Day 4 Discover the wildlife at Rancho Texas
Day 5 Visit San Gines Church and explore the beauty of Charco de San Gines
Day 6 Go hiking or biking and experience the beautiful landscapes of the city
Day 7 Relax and soak up the sun at Parque Islas Canarias

Food and Drink Scene of Arrecife

Arrecife also has a fantastic food and drink scene, with some of the freshest seafood in the Canary Islands, as well as wine and local beers. Here are some of the must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Papas Arrugadas: A traditional dish made with small potatoes boiled in saltwater and served with “mojo” sauce. A must-try for any foodie visiting the Canary Islands.
  • Conejo en Salmorejo: A delicious rabbit dish served in a sauce made with wine, garlic, and other herbs, creating a flavor explosion in your mouth.
  • Gofio: A traditional Canarian dish made with roasted corn or wheat flour. It can be mixed with milk, honey, or cheese.

Local Beverages

  • Lanzarote Wine: Lanzarote’s wine scene is growing rapidly, with some unique volcanic wines made from grapes grown in volcanic soil.
  • Cerveza Nao: The locally brewed beer is a full-bodied ale that goes down smoothly and is perfect for enjoying on a hot day.

Where to Stay in Arrecife: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Whether you’re looking for a luxurious hotel or budget-friendly accommodation, Arrecife has plenty of options that will suit your needs. Here are some of the best places to stay in Arrecife:

  • Barcelo Lanzarote: A four-star hotel located close to Playa del Reducto, offering comfortable rooms and a range of amenities.
  • Boutique La Isla y el Mar: This four-star hotel features an outdoor swimming pool, spacious rooms, and is located just a short distance from the city’s top attractions.
  • Hostal San Gines: A budget-friendly option that offers clean, comfortable rooms at a reasonable price.

What to Buy in Arrecife: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Arrecife is known for its handmade crafts, jewelry, and local delicacies, making it the perfect place to pick up unique souvenirs and gifts. Here are some of the best things to buy in Arrecife:

  • Semi-precious gemstones: Lanzarote is known for its unique semi-precious mineral stones, such as olivine, crystalline quartz, and obsidian.
  • Pottery: The city has a great tradition of pottery, and there are many stores and markets selling unique handmade pottery items that you can add to your collection.
  • Mojo sauce: A popular sauce used in Canarian dishes that can be bought in many stores across the city. It is a perfect gift for those who love to cook.

Local Culture and Customs of Arrecife

Arrecife is known for its rich cultural heritage and traditions, as well as its friendly and welcoming locals. Here are some customs that visitors should keep in mind when visiting Arrecife:

  • Siesta: Afternoon siestas are a common practice in Spain, so visitors should respect the local customs and take it easy during these hours.
  • Tipping: Tipping is not mandatory in Arrecife, but it is appreciated if you receive good service or meal.
  • Language: Spanish is the official language, but English and German are widely spoken as well.

Best Time to Visit Arrecife

The best time to visit Arrecife is between October and May. During this time, the weather is pleasant, and the city is less crowded with tourists. The peak tourist season is between June and September, when the weather is hotter, and prices are higher.

Do’s and Don’ts of Arrecife

Here are some do’s and don’ts to keep in mind while visiting Arrecife:

Do:

  • Try the local cuisine: Arrecife is known for its unique and delicious cuisine.
  • Explore the natural attractions: The city has some of the most beautiful natural attractions in the Canary Islands.
  • Respect the local customs: Be mindful of the local culture and customs, including taking afternoon siestas.

Don’t:

  • Litter: Keep the city clean and dispose of your waste properly.
  • Ignore the natural rules: The natural areas in Arrecife are important, so visitors should follow the rules to keep them preserved.
  • Overdress: The climate in Arrecife is warm, so visitors should avoid overdressing.

Safety and Health

Arrecife is a very safe city, with little to no reported crime incidents. However, visitors should take standard precautions such as avoiding dark, secluded areas at night and not flashing valuables. As for health, tap water is generally safe to drink, and there are medical facilities available throughout the city.

Getting to Arrecife: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Arrecife

The nearest airport is Lanzarote Airport, also known as Arrecife Airport. It is about five kilometres away from the city centre and is well connected to destinations across Europe and the Canary Islands.

Bus Stations Near Arrecife

There are several buses that connect Arrecife to other destinations on the island. The central bus station, Intercambiador de Arrecife, is located in the city centre, with frequent services.

Other Options

Visitors can also take a taxi, car rentals or private transfers from the airport directly to the city centre.

How to Get Around in Arrecife

Arrecife is a small and walkable city with plenty to see and do. However, visitors can also use public transportation, taxis, or car rentals to get around the city if need be.

Where to Go Next from Arrecife: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Arrecife is located in the heart of the island, making it a great base from which to explore other destinations on the island. Listed below are the best nearby destinations that you should consider visiting:

  • Teguise: A charming town located just 17 km from Arrecife, known for its historic center, arts and crafts market, and beautiful architecture.
  • Costa Teguise: A relaxing destination, home to some of the best beaches on the island, perfect for surfing and swimming.
  • La Graciosa: Take a day trip to this nearby island and explore its pristine beaches and beautiful landscapes.

Conclusion

Arrecife is a fascinating and vibrant city that offers so much to see and do. With its rich cultural heritage, natural attractions, and thriving food and drink scene, it’s no wonder why Arrecife is a must-visit destination for anyone traveling to the Canary Islands. We hope this guide has provided you with plenty of inspiration for your trip to Arrecife and happy travels!
