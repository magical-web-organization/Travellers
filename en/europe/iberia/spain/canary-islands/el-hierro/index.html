Introduction to El Hierro

El Hierro is one of the least visited and most remote islands in the Canary Islands. Located in the westernmost part of the archipelago, El Hierro is known for its peaceful atmosphere, unspoiled landscapes, and unique topography. With a population of only 11,000 inhabitants, El Hierro boasts a relaxed pace of life that makes it an ideal retreat for travelers looking to escape the hustle and bustle of big cities.

List of Places to Visit in El Hierro

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Cueva de Los Letreros

Cueva de Los Letreros is an archaeological site that features an extensive collection of prehistoric rock art. The paintings date back to between 1000 and 5000 BC and were probably created as a form of communication between the native Guanches.

Casa de Los Balcones

Casa de Los Balcones is a beautiful house built in a typical Canary Islands style in the village of La Frontera. The house is home to a museum that exhibits traditional handicrafts, including wood carvings, pottery, and embroidery.

Natural Attractions of El Hierro: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Roque de Los Muchachos

Roque de Los Muchachos is the highest point on the island, offering breathtaking views of the surrounding landscape. The mountain is also home to one of the world’s most important astronomical observatories, making it a prime spot for stargazing.

Los Verodes Natural Pools

Los Verodes Natural Pools is a series of natural rock pools along El Hierro’s northern coast. The pools are fed by the Atlantic Ocean and are a perfect spot for swimming and snorkeling.

Hiking/Biking Routes of El Hierro

El Hierro boasts plenty of hiking and biking routes that take you through some of the island’s most beautiful and unspoiled landscapes. Some of the best routes include:

  • El Golfo Valley
  • Los Roques de Salmor Natural Monument
  • The 12-kilometer trail from Sabinosa to El Mocanal

Best Things to Do in El Hierro: Activities and Tours

Snorkeling and Diving

El Hierro’s crystal-clear waters make it an ideal spot for snorkeling and diving. One of the most popular dive sites is La Restinga Marine Reserve, known for its underwater volcanic rock formations and abundant marine life.

Whale Watching

El Hierro and the surrounding waters are home to a variety of whale and dolphin species. Visitors can take a boat tour to see these majestic creatures up close in their natural habitat.

Week-Long Itinerary for El Hierro

Day 1: Discover Valverde and Isora

Start your trip with a visit to the capital, Valverde. Explore its colonial architecture and visit Casa de Las Quinteras, a museum dedicated to local crafts. Later, head to the charming village of Isora to see some of the island’s most beautiful landscapes.

Day 2: Hiking in the Golfo Valley

Spend the day hiking in the stunning Golfo Valley. See the famous Garoé Tree, the ancient Canaries believed the sacred tree, and enjoy the scenic views of the valley.

Day 3: Los Verodes Natural Pools and Snorkeling

Spend the morning relaxing in the cool waters of Los Verodes Natural Pools. In the afternoon, head to La Restinga Marine Reserve for some snorkeling.

Day 4: Visiting Cueva de Los Letreros and Mirador de Jinama

Explore the Cueva de Los Letreros and see some of the island’s most well-preserved rock paintings. Later, head to Mirador de Jinama for some stunning views of the coastline.

Day 5: Whale Watching

Take a boat tour to see some of the whales and dolphins that call El Hierro home.

Day 6: Visiting Roque de Los Muchachos Observatory

Spend the day exploring the Roque de Los Muchachos Observatory and learning about the stars and planets.

Day 7: Departure

Say goodbye to El Hierro and head back home.

Food and Drink Scene of El Hierro

Must-Try Dishes

  • Parro Soup: A traditional Canary Islands soup made with fish and gofio.
  • Pella de Gofio: A sweet pastry made with corn flour, honey, almonds, and raisins.
  • Mojo Verde: A green sauce made with garlic, peppers, and herbs.

Local Beverages

  • Ron Alzira: A local rum made in El Hierro.
  • Gofio Milkshake: A milkshake made with gofio, honey, and banana.

Where to Stay in El Hierro: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Parador de El Hierro: A luxury hotel with stunning sea views.
  • Casas Rurales Herreñas: Rustic houses in charming villages across the island.
  • Apartamentos Boomerang I: A budget-friendly apartment complex near the beach.

What to Buy in El Hierro: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Handicrafts: Pick up some traditional handicrafts, including pottery, embroidery, and wood carvings.
  • Canarian Cheese: Try some of the island’s most delicious cheeses, including Queso Blanco and Quesadilla.

Local Culture and Customs of El Hierro

El Hierro has a rich cultural heritage, with much of its history reflected in its handicrafts and food. The island is known for its laid-back lifestyle and its respect for tradition and nature.

Best Time to Visit El Hierro

The best time to visit El Hierro is between September and November when the temperatures are mild, and the island is less crowded. Visitors should be aware that the island can be subject to strong winds and occasional storms, so it’s always best to check weather reports before planning a trip.

Do’s and Don’ts of El Hierro

Do:

  • Respect the culture and customs of the locals.
  • Try the island’s delicious cuisine.
  • Explore the island’s stunning landscapes.

Don’t:

  • Leave trash on the beaches or in the countryside.
  • Take pictures of locals without asking for permission first.
  • Approach marine life when snorkeling or diving.

Safety and Health

Safety in El Hierro

El Hierro is a safe destination for visitors. Visitors should be aware of the strong currents that can occur on the island’s beaches and heed any warning signs posted.

Health in El Hierro

There are no specific health risks associated with traveling to El Hierro. Visitors should take normal precautions and be prepared for sun exposure.

Getting to El Hierro: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near El Hierro

The island has a small airport, El Hierro Airport (VDE), located 8 km from the island’s capital, Valverde. The airport serves flights to and from Tenerife and Gran Canaria.

Bus Stations Near El Hierro

There are several bus routes that operate across the island, connecting the major towns and attractions.

Other Options

Many visitors choose to travel to El Hierro by ferry. There are regular ferry services to and from Tenerife and La Palma.

How to Get Around in El Hierro

The best way to explore El Hierro is by renting a car. There are several car rental agencies on the island, and the roads are well-maintained and easy to navigate.

Where to Go Next from El Hierro: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

El Hierro is an ideal destination for travelers looking to explore the beauty of the Canary Islands. Visitors can continue their journey to nearby islands, such as La Palma and La Gomera.

Conclusion

El Hierro is a hidden gem in the Canary Islands, boasting some of Europe’s most spectacular landscapes and cultural riches. With its laid-back atmosphere, charming villages, and stunning natural attractions, El Hierro is the perfect place for a peaceful and rejuvenating getaway.
