Introduction to Puerto de la Cruz

Located in Tenerife, one of the seven Canary Islands of Spain, Puerto de la Cruz is a charming coastal town with a rich history and stunning natural beauty. The town features a mix of colonial architecture and modern amenities, and offers a range of activities for visitors of all ages and interests. In this travel guide, we’ll explore the best of Puerto de la Cruz, including its must-see sights, delicious cuisine, and cultural traditions.

List of Places to Visit in Puerto de la Cruz

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Casa de los Balcones: This 17th-century mansion is famous for its beautiful wooden balconies and intricate carvings. Today, it serves as a museum showcasing traditional Canarian crafts and culture.
  • Puerto de la Cruz Archeological Museum: This museum features exhibits highlighting the indigenous Guanche culture of the Canary Islands, as well as artifacts from the Spanish colonial period.

Natural Attractions of Puerto de la Cruz: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Loro Parque: This wildlife park is home to over 4,000 animals, including the largest collection of parrots in the world, as well as dolphins, whales, and sea lions.
  • Playa del Socorro: This wide, sandy beach is popular with surfers and bodyboarders due to its consistent waves and strong currents.
  • Lake Martianez: This man-made lagoon complex, designed by Canarian artist Cesar Manrique, is a popular spot for swimming and sunbathing.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Puerto de la Cruz

  • Anaga Mountains: This lush and scenic mountain range features several hiking trails that offer stunning views of the surrounding countryside.
  • La Orotava Valley: This picturesque valley is home to several small villages, and boasts hiking and biking trails that wind through lush greenery and offer panoramic views.

Best Things to Do in Puerto de la Cruz: Activities and Tours

  • Whale and Dolphin Watching: Take a boat tour to spot these magnificent sea creatures in their natural habitat.
  • Sunset Cruise: Enjoy a romantic sunset cruise along the coast of Puerto de la Cruz.
  • Botanical Garden: Visit the town’s lush botanical garden which features a wide variety of exotic flora and fauna.

Food and Drink Scene of Puerto de la Cruz

Must-Try Dishes

  • Papas Arrugadas: A local Canarian dish of small, wrinkled potatoes served with a spicy sauce.
  • Mojo Verde y Rojo: Traditional Canarian sauces made from a blend of garlic, olive oil, and hot peppers which is usually served alongside grilled seafood or meat.
  • Bienmesabe: A dessert made from almonds, honey, sugar, and lemon zest topped with a caramel sauce.

Local Beverages

  • Banana Liqueur: Made from the islands’ famous Canarian bananas, it is often served as a digestif.
  • Honey Rum: Canarian rum infused with local honey, it is a sweet and strong after-dinner drink.

Where to Stay in Puerto de la Cruz: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Hotel Botanico & The Oriental Spa Garden: A luxurious 5-star hotel featuring lush gardens, multiple swimming pools, and a world-class spa.
  • Hotel Marquesa: A charming 4-star hotel housed in a 16th-century building, it offers excellent service and a central location.
  • Puerto Palace: A 4-star hotel with a large outdoor pool, a range of dining options, and spacious rooms.

What to Buy in Puerto de la Cruz: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Local Wine: Tenerife is known for its wine production, particular those made from the malvasia grape.
  • Canarian Pottery: Beautifully crafted pottery is often sold at local markets throughout the town.
  • Mojo Sauces: Bring home a bottle of the famous Canarian Mojo Verde or Mojo Rojo sauces to spice up your meals at home.

Local Culture and Customs of Puerto de la Cruz

Puerto de la Cruz exhibits a mix of Spanish and Canarian culture, with a passion for music and dance. The Carnaval de Puerto de la Cruz is an annual festival with a history stretching back over 100 years that celebrates music, dance and magnificent costumes. Spanish is the official language, though English is widely spoken. Friendliness and hospitality are highly valued traits.

Best Time to Visit Puerto de la Cruz

The best time to visit Puerto de la Cruz is between November and April when the weather is pleasantly warm but not too hot. July and August are the busiest months and can be uncomfortably hot, so it is best to avoid these months if you’re not a fan of crowds.

Do’s and Don’ts of Puerto de la Cruz

Do:

  • Do try the local dishes and drinks, including Mojo sauces and Canarian wine.
  • Do bring comfortable shoes for hiking and biking tours.
  • Do try to visit during the Carnaval de Puerto de la Cruz festival.

Don’t:

  • Don’t be disrespectful or dismissive of the unique Canarian culture and traditions.
  • Don’t leave your valuables unattended while at the beach or other popular tourist locations.
  • Don’t litter any of the natural attractions or public areas.

Safety and Health

Safety in Puerto de la Cruz

Puerto de la Cruz is considered a safe town for tourists, with low levels of crime and a visible police presence in popular areas.

Health in Puerto de la Cruz

The Spanish healthcare system is recognized as one of the best in the world, and there are several health centers and hospitals in Puerto de la Cruz.

Getting to Puerto de la Cruz: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Puerto de la Cruz

  • Tenerife South Airport (TFS): Located about 60 km from Puerto de la Cruz, this airport is the most common airport for visitors to fly into.
  • Tenerife North Airport (TFN): Located about 20 km from Puerto de la Cruz, this airport offers flights to other destinations in the Canary Islands and the Spanish mainland.

Bus Stations Near Puerto de la Cruz

  • Estación de Guaguas de Puerto de la Cruz: Located in the town center, this bus station offers regular services to other towns and cities on the island.

Other Options

  • Taxi: Taxis are readily available throughout the town and offer a convenient way to get around.
  • Car Rental: Renting a car is recommended for those who wish to explore the island on their own.

How to Get Around in Puerto de la Cruz

Puerto de la Cruz is a small town and easily walkable. Taxis and buses are also available for those who prefer not to walk.

Where to Go Next from Puerto de la Cruz: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Santa Cruz de Tenerife: The vibrant capital city of Tenerife is only about 30 km away from Puerto de la Cruz, and features museums, shopping, and cultural attractions.
  • La Orotava: This historic town is only a short drive away from Puerto de la Cruz, and offers scenic views, beautiful churches, and charming shops.

Conclusion

Puerto de la Cruz is truly a gem of Tenerife, with its mix of natural beauty and cultural richness. From its beautiful beaches and natural wonders to its exceptional cuisine and robust nightlife, it offers something for everyone. With this comprehensive travel guide in hand, you’re sure to have an unforgettable Puerto de la Cruz experience!
