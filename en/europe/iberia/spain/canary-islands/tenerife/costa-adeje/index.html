Introduction to Costa Adeje

Located on the southern coast of Tenerife, Costa Adeje is a stunning destination that boasts plenty of sunshine, beautiful beaches, and a vibrant nightlife. With a mix of Spanish and Canarian culture, Costa Adeje has become a popular destination for tourists from all over the world. This guide aims to provide you with everything you need to know about Costa Adeje, including places to visit, activities to do, and where to stay.

List of Places to Visit in Costa Adeje

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Museo de Arte Contemporaneo: This museum showcases contemporary artwork by local artists.
  • Museo de Historia de Adeje: Discover the history of Adeje in this museum, which features exhibits on the Guanches, the original inhabitants of Tenerife.
  • Castillo de San Miguel: This medieval castle was built in the 18th century and is now used for traditional Canarian folklore shows.

Natural Attractions of Costa Adeje: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Teide National Park: Explore the impressive Mount Teide, the highest peak in Spain.
  • Siam Park: This Thai-themed waterpark is perfect for families, featuring breathtaking slides and waves.
  • La Caleta: This beach town offers calm waters and an escape from the hustle and bustle of the city.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Costa Adeje

  • Masca Valley: This three-hour hike showcases beautiful scenery, including Tenerife’s impressive cliffs.
  • Mount Teide: You can hike the peak of Mount Teide, either with a guide or on your own.
  • Barranco del Infierno: This trail leads to a stunning waterfall, but be warned it can be steep and rocky in parts.

Best Things to Do in Costa Adeje: Activities and Tours

  • Whale Watching: Take a boat tour to spot whales and dolphins in their natural habitat.
  • Parasailing: Soar high above the sea and take in panoramic views of the island.
  • Kayaking: Explore the coastline and secluded coves by renting a kayak.

Food and Drink Scene of Costa Adeje

Must-Try Dishes

  • Papas Arrugadas: A must-try traditional dish of boiled Canarian potatoes served with mojo sauce.
  • Gofio: A flour made from roasted maize or wheat, used in many Canarian dishes.
  • Canarian Mojo: A salsa-style sauce served with many dishes, including grilled meats and fish.

Local Beverages

  • Ron Miel: A popular Canarian rum infused with honey.
  • Vino del Pais: Local wine made from grapes grown in the volcanic soil of Tenerife.
  • Barraquito: A unique coffee drink made with condensed milk, cinnamon, lemon, and Licor 43.

Where to Stay in Costa Adeje: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • GF Gran Costa Adeje 5* Luxury Hotel: A luxurious hotel located near the beach, featuring a spa and multiple restaurants.
  • Flipper’s Family Hotel 4*: A family-friendly hotel with a kids’ club and swimming pool.
  • Sunset Bay Club By Diamond Resorts 3*: A budget-friendly option with apartment-style rooms and a pool.

What to Buy in Costa Adeje: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Canarian Mojo: Take home some Canarian mojo sauce to recreate your favorite dishes.
  • Aloe Vera Products: Aloe is grown extensively on the Canary Islands and is a popular ingredient in beauty products.
  • Wine and Honey: Take home locally-made wine and honey to enjoy at home.

Local Culture and Customs of Costa Adeje

Canarian culture is a mix of Spanish, African and Latin-American influences. The island has a rich history and is home to many indigenous Guanches, who left behind rock carvings and mummies. The traditional clothing worn on the island includes the “parranda,” a type of dress made from wool, and the “maguilla,” a traditional piece of clothing worn by shepherds.

Best Time to Visit Costa Adeje

The best time to visit Costa Adeje is between April and November when the weather is warm and dry. The peak summer months of July and August can be particularly crowded, so consider visiting in the shoulder seasons of May-June or September-October.

Do’s and Don’ts of Costa Adeje

Do:

  • Try the local food: Canarian cuisine is delicious and unique, so make sure to try some of the local dishes.
  • Visit Mount Teide: Tenerife’s highest peak offers amazing views of the island.
  • Take a boat tour: A whale watching or dolphins sighting tour is a must-do activity in Costa Adeje.

Don’t:

  • Leave your valuables unattended at the beach: Petty theft can occur, so make sure to keep an eye on your things.
  • Sunbathe topless: Although accepted on certain beaches, it is not common on the island and may offend some locals.

Safety and Health

Safety in Costa Adeje

Costa Adeje is a safe destination, but as with any tourist destination, petty crime can occur, so ensure you keep an eye on your valuables. In case of an emergency, dial 112 for assistance.

Health in Costa Adeje

No specific health concerns are associated with Costa Adeje, but it is always wise to stay hydrated, wear sunscreen, and take precautions against mosquito bites. Pharmacies can be found easily on the island, and medical attention is available should you need it.

Getting to Costa Adeje: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Costa Adeje

  • Tenerife South Airport (TFS): This is the main airport in Tenerife, located about 20 minutes from Costa Adeje.

Bus Stations Near Costa Adeje

  • Costa Adeje Bus Station: This is the main bus station in the town center.

Other Options

  • Taxis: Taxis are readily available and can be hailed at many locations.

How to Get Around in Costa Adeje

One of the best ways to get around Costa Adeje is by renting a car, but there are also buses and taxis available. Taxis are inexpensive and can be easily hailed on the street. Buses connect Costa Adeje with other parts of the island, including Santa Cruz and Los Cristianos, but they can be slow and crowded.

Where to Go Next from Costa Adeje: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Los Cristianos: This town offers a bustling beach scene, as well as plenty of restaurants and shops. It is also the departure point for ferries to other Canary Islands.
  • Santa Cruz: Tenerife’s capital is home to many museums and historic landmarks, as well as some excellent shopping opportunities.
  • Masca: A charming town located in the mountains, known for its traditional architecture and stunning views.

Conclusion

Costa Adeje is an ideal destination for those looking for a mix of outdoor adventure, beautiful beaches, and Canarian culture. With plenty of activities to do, places to visit, and delicious food to eat, a trip to Costa Adeje is one you won’t forget.
