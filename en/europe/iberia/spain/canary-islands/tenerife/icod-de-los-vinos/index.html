Introduction to Icod de los Vinos

Located on the northwestern coast of Tenerife, Icod de los Vinos is a charming town famous for its world-renowned Drago Tree, colonial architecture, and rich cultural heritage. The name “Icod” comes from the Guanche language, which means “water,” referring to the extensive network of springs, streams, and waterfalls in and around the town. The climate here is mild and pleasant throughout the year, with temperatures ranging between 16°C to 25°C. The town is surrounded by lush green forests, mountains, and stunning coastline, making it an ideal destination for hiking, nature exploration, and beach holidays.

List of Places to Visit in Icod de los Vinos

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Casa de los Caceres

Casa de los Caceres is a 17th-century mansion that has now been converted into a museum that showcases traditional Canarian architecture, interior design, and furniture. The museum preserves the legacy of Argote de Molina, the famous chronicler of Tenerife, who lived in this house and wrote about the history, culture, and people of the island.

Iglesia de San Marcos

Iglesia de San Marcos is the main church of Icod de los Vinos, built in the 16th century in the Baroque style. The facade is adorned with intricate carvings and statues, and the interior is richly decorated with frescoes, altarpieces, and religious artifacts. The church has a prominent role in the local festivals and is a symbol of the town’s religious identity.

Natural Attractions of Icod de los Vinos: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Parque del Drago

Parque del Drago is a botanical garden in the heart of Icod de los Vinos that houses the famous Drago Tree, a natural monument that is considered one of the oldest living trees in the world. The park has a collection of over 70 species of endemic and exotic plants, a lake, a waterfall, and a museum dedicated to the Drago Tree’s history and significance. The park is a popular spot for picnics, walks, and relaxing in the shade of the giant tree.

Cueva del Viento

Cueva del Viento is a volcanic underground complex that comprises a network of tunnels, galleries, and lava streams that were formed during the eruptions of Pico Viejo, one of the active volcanoes on Tenerife. The cave is a natural wonder that showcases the geological history of the island and offers guided tours that explain its features and processes.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Icod de los Vinos

Chinyero Volcano

Chinyero Volcano is a dormant volcano that last erupted in 1909 and is now a popular hiking trail that offers stunning views of the surrounding landscape, including a panoramic view of the Atlantic Ocean and the neighboring islands. The trail is relatively easy and takes around 3 hours to complete, making it accessible to all levels of hikers.

Monte del Agua

Monte del Agua is a natural park that covers an area of 160 hectares and is home to a rich variety of flora and fauna, including endangered species such as the Gran Canaria blue chaffinch and the Trocaz pigeon. The park has several hiking trails that lead to waterfalls, springs, forests, and viewpoints. There are also facilities for picnics, camping, and bird watching.

Best Things to Do in Icod de los Vinos: Activities and Tours

Wine Tasting and Vineyard Tours

Icod de los Vinos is known for its wine production, and there are several vineyards and bodegas that offer wine tastings and tours. The most famous wine variety is the malvasia, a sweet dessert wine that pairs well with local cheese and sweets.

Whale and Dolphin Watching

The waters around Tenerife are home to a diverse population of whales and dolphins that can be seen in their natural habitat through organized boat tours that depart from the nearby harbors. Complying with responsible tourism guidelines and minimizing noise and disturbance of the animals are essential on such tours.

Food and Drink Scene of Icod de los Vinos

Must-Try Dishes

Papas arrugadas

Papas arrugadas are small boiled potatoes that are typically served with mojo sauce, a pungent and spicy sauce made with garlic, oil, and vinegar. The locals often accompany this dish with a glass of local wine, preferably from the malvasia grape variety.

Conejo en salmorejo

Conejo en salmorejo is a traditional rabbit dish that is slow-cooked in a savory sauce made with garlic, herbs, and paprika. The dish is usually served with potatoes and red wine.

Local Beverages

Wine

Icod de los Vinos is famous for its wine production, with malvasia grape variety being the top choice for local production. There are several bodegas and vineyards in and around Icod de los Vinos that offer wine tastings and tours.

Barraquito

Barraquito is a typical Canarian coffee that is made with condensed milk, liquor, cinnamon, and lemon peel. It is usually served in a small glass with a layer of foam on top.

Where to Stay in Icod de los Vinos: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

There are several options for accommodation in Icod de los Vinos that cater to different budgets and preferences. Few of them are of note:

Hotel Emblemático San Marcos

The Hotel Emblemático San Marcos is a boutique hotel that is housed in a restored 18th-century mansion. The hotel has 6 rooms and suites that feature antique furniture, wooden floors, and modern amenities such as air conditioning and free Wi-Fi.

Casa el Morro

Casa el Morro is a rustic-style guesthouse that overlooks the coast of Icod de los Vinos and offers traditional Canarian hospitality. The guesthouse has 6 rooms that are decorated in a simple and functional style and have private balconies with sea views.

What to Buy in Icod de los Vinos: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Pottery

Icod de los Vinos has a long tradition of artisanal pottery-making, with several workshops and studios that produce functional and decorative ceramics. The designs are inspired by local flora, fauna, and landscapes and make for excellent souvenirs.

Aloe Vera Products

Tenerife is famous for its Aloe Vera plantations, which produce high-quality cosmetics and skin care products. These products are made from natural ingredients and are ideal for sensitive skin.

Local Culture and Customs of Icod de los Vinos

The locals of Icod de los Vinos are proud of their cultural heritage and celebrate it through various festivals and events. The most famous festival is the San Antonio Abad Festival, which takes place in January and involves the blessing of the animals, dancing, singing, and street parades. The town also has a strong musical tradition and boasts several choirs, bands, and orchestras.

Best Time to Visit Icod de los Vinos

The best time to visit Icod de los Vinos is between April and June and September to November when the weather is mild, and the crowds are thinner. July and August can get hot and crowded, while the winter months can be chilly and rainy.

Safety and Health

Safety in Icod de los Vinos

Icod de los Vinos is a safe and peaceful town, but visitors should take usual safety measures like usual. It is best to avoid dark, deserted areas at night and to keep an eye on your belongings in busy tourist areas.

Health in Icod de los Vinos

There are several medical facilities and pharmacies in Icod de los Vinos that provide quality medical care. EU citizens and nationals of countries with a bilateral health agreement with Spain are entitled to free or low-cost healthcare under the Spanish National Health Service.

Getting to Icod de los Vinos: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Icod de los Vinos

The closest airport to Icod de los Vinos is Tenerife Norte Airport, also known as Los Rodeos Airport, which is about 50 km away and is well-connected to the town via bus and taxi.

Bus Stations Near Icod de los Vinos

Icod de los Vinos has a bus station that is located in the town center and offers regular services to the nearby towns and cities.

Other Options

For those driving down from within Tenerife, the town is accessible via the TF-5 motorway that connects all the major towns and cities on the island.

How to Get Around in Icod de los Vinos

The town of Icod de los Vinos is walkable, and most of the attractions are within walking distance from each other. For those traveling long distances or carrying heavy luggage, there are buses and taxis available for hire.

Where to Go Next from Icod de los Vinos: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Garachico

Garachico is a historic town that was destroyed by a volcanic eruption in the 18th century and has been beautifully restored. The town has several monuments, churches, and houses of architectural interest, as well as a natural swimming pool and a small harbor.

Buenavista del Norte

Buenavista del Norte is a charming town that is known for its traditional architecture, lush gardens, and stunning coastline. The town has a few landmarks such as the church of Nuestra Señora de los Remedios and the historic mansion of Casa de la Real Aduana.

Conclusion

Icod de los Vinos is a beautiful town that is full of cultural and natural wonders and offers a lot of opportunities to explore and enjoy. From hiking to wine tasting and from museums to beaches, there is something for everyone in Icod de los Vinos. The town’s genuine hospitality, rich history, and natural beauty make it an ideal destination for a memorable holiday in Tenerife.
