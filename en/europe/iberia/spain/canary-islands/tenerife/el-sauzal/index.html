Introduction to El Sauzal

Nestled in the north of Tenerife, El Sauzal is a picturesque town that boasts both natural beauty and cultural richness. With a history that spans back to the pre-Spanish era, El Sauzal is home to a variety of attractions that will satisfy any traveler.

List of Places to Visit in El Sauzal

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Casa del Vino - This museum showcases the history of wine production in Tenerife, with exhibits ranging from traditional wine-making techniques to modern production methods. Don’t forget to sample some of the local wines as well!
  • Iglesia de San Pedro Apóstol - This impressive church dates back to the 16th century and is filled with beautiful artwork and frescoes.
  • Museo de la Historia y Antropología de Tenerife (MHA) - Located in the former town hall building, MHA offers a glimpse into the history and culture of the Canary Islands.

Natural Attractions of El Sauzal: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Parque San Francisco - This lush, green park is a great spot for a picnic or a relaxing walk. The park is also home to a small lake and a playground for children.
  • Costa de Acentejo - A scenic coastal area that offers amazing views of the ocean and the surrounding cliffs. Hiking trails also lead to secluded beaches and coves.
  • Los Lavaderos Nature Park - This beautiful park features a series of man-made waterfalls and pools, which were once used for laundry. Today, they offer visitors a refreshing swimming experience.

Hiking/Biking Routes of El Sauzal

  • La Toscas-Punta Hidalgo Trail - This coastal trail offers stunning views of the ocean and the surrounding scenery. The path is relatively easy, making it a great option for families.
  • El Sauzal-La Orotava Trail - This hike leads through lush forests and offers breathtaking views of the Teide volcano. The route is more challenging than other trails in the area but is well worth the effort.

Best Things to Do in El Sauzal: Activities and Tours

  • Wine Tasting Tour - With its fertile soil and favorable climate, Tenerife is famous for its wine production. Take a tour of the local vineyards and try some of the best wines the island has to offer.
  • Scuba Diving Tour - Explore the underwater world off the coast of El Sauzal with a scuba diving tour. The clear waters are home to a variety of marine life, including colorful fish, octopuses, and sea turtles.
  • Segway Tour - See the sights of El Sauzal on a Segway tour. These fun and easy-to-use vehicles allow you to cover more ground than walking while still enjoying the sights and sounds of the town.

Week-Long Itinerary for El Sauzal

Food and Drink Scene of El Sauzal

Must-Try Dishes

  • Papas Arrugadas con Mojo - A traditional Canary Islands dish made with small, salted potatoes and a spicy sauce called mojo.
  • Churros con Chocolate - Fried dough pastries served with hot chocolate sauce for dipping.
  • Carajillo - A popular local drink made with espresso and brandy.

Local Beverages

  • Wine - Tenerife is known for its wine production, with many vineyards located in and around El Sauzal. Make sure to try the local varieties, such as Listán Negro or Negramoll.
  • Cerveza Dorada - A locally produced beer that is refreshing and perfect for hot days.

Where to Stay in El Sauzal: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Hotel Rural Victoria - A charming hotel set in a restored 16th-century mansion, offering stunning views of the ocean and a peaceful, relaxing atmosphere.
  • Hotel Rural Casa Amarilla - This bed and breakfast is set amidst lush gardens and features traditional Canary Islands architecture.
  • Casa Rural Las Rosas - A peaceful guesthouse surrounded by beautiful countryside, offering comfortable rooms and a delicious breakfast.

What to Buy in El Sauzal: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Wine - Take home a bottle of the delicious local wine.
  • Canary Islands Cheese - The cheese of the Canary Islands is famous for its unique flavor, due to the local goats’ milk.
  • Pottery - Handmade pottery is a popular local craft, with many shops selling a variety of colorful and intricate designs.

Local Culture and Customs of El Sauzal

El Sauzal, like much of the Canary Islands, has a rich culture and history that is influenced by its proximity to Africa and Europe. The locals are friendly and welcoming, and visitors are often struck by the warm, relaxed atmosphere of the town. Some local customs include:

  • Fiestas - The town celebrates a variety of fiestas throughout the year, including the Fiesta de San Pedro, which takes place in late June.
  • Food and Wine - Food and wine play an important role in local culture, and many festivals and events are centered around these themes.
  • Religious Observances - Religion remains an important part of life in El Sauzal, with many locals attending church and participating in religious observances.

Best Time to Visit El Sauzal

The best time to visit El Sauzal is during the spring or fall when temperatures are mild and the crowds are thinner. Summers can be hot and crowded, while winters can be rainy and chilly.

Do’s and Don’ts of El Sauzal

Do:

  • Try the local food and wine - El Sauzal is known for its delicious cuisine, so make sure to indulge your taste buds.
  • Respect the locals - The people of El Sauzal are warm and friendly, so be polite and friendly in response.
  • Follow local customs - Whether attending a fiesta or religious observance, be respectful of local customs and traditions.

Don’t:

  • Disrespect the environment - El Sauzal is a beautiful natural setting, so make sure to dispose of your waste properly and respect the local flora and fauna.
  • Be too noisy - El Sauzal is a peaceful town, so keep noise levels down during the nighttime and respect your neighbors.
  • Overdress - El Sauzal is a laid-back town, so leave your fancy clothes at home and dress for comfort.

Safety and Health

Safety in El Sauzal

El Sauzal is a safe and secure town, but visitors should always be aware of their surroundings, especially at night. As in any area with a lot of tourists, pickpocketing can be a problem. Always keep your valuables in a secure place and never leave them unattended.

Health in El Sauzal

El Sauzal has excellent medical facilities, with a variety of private clinics and hospitals available. Visitors are advised to have travel insurance in case of unforeseen medical expenses.

Getting to El Sauzal: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near El Sauzal

The closest airport to El Sauzal is Tenerife North Airport (TFN), which is located 16 kilometers away. Another option is Tenerife South Airport (TFS), located about 80 kilometers away.

Bus Stations Near El Sauzal

El Sauzal is served by several bus lines, with connections to other towns on the island, and the main bus station is located in the nearby town of La Laguna.

Other Options

For those who prefer private transportation, there are several car rental agencies available at the airport.

How to Get Around in El Sauzal

El Sauzal is a compact town, and most attractions are within walking distance. Taxis and buses are available for those who prefer not to walk.

Where to Go Next from El Sauzal: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • La Laguna - A UNESCO World Heritage site, La Laguna is a beautiful colonial town filled with stunning architecture and rich history.
  • Santa Cruz de Tenerife - The capital of Tenerife is a vibrant city that boasts a lively cultural scene and a beautiful waterfront.
  • Teide National Park - Home to the highest peak in Spain, this national park is a must-visit destination for nature lovers.

Conclusion

El Sauzal is a hidden gem in the north of Tenerife, offering visitors a mix of cultural richness and natural beauty. Whether you’re interested in hiking, trying local wine, or exploring historical landmarks, El Sauzal has something for everyone. Plan your visit today and discover all that this charming town has to offer.
