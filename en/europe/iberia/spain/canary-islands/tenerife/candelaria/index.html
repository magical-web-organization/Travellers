A Comprehensive Travel Guide to Candelaria

Are you looking for a place in Spain that has both cultural and natural attractions? Look no further than Candelaria in Tenerife, Canary Islands. This charming town has a rich history and beautiful landscapes that will surely captivate you. In this travel guide, we will cover everything you need to know about visiting Candelaria, including places to visit, things to do, where to stay, what to buy, local customs, safety and health, transportation and nearby destinations.

Introduction to Candelaria

Candelaria is a picturesque town located in the southeastern part of Tenerife, the largest among the Canary Islands. It is known as the spiritual capital of the Canaries due to the presence of the famous Basilica of Our Lady of Candelaria, the patron saint of the islands. Aside from its religious significance, Candelaria is also a favorite among tourists for its scenic views, beautiful beaches, and natural attractions.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Candelaria

Candelaria has several hiking and biking routes that are perfect for outdoor enthusiasts.

  • Camino Real de Fasnia (3.5 km)
  • El Contador – La Crucita (8 km)
  • Sendero de El Rincón (10 km)
  • Playa de la Viuda – Tejina de Guía (11 km)
  • Ruta Verde del Sur (23 km)

Best Things to Do in Candelaria: Activities and Tours

  • Visit the Basilica of Our Lady of Candelaria
  • Explore the Ethnographic Museum of the Pyramids of Guimar

  • Go hiking or biking on the various trails available

  • Taste local cuisine at the Mercado del Agricultor

Local Culture and Customs of Candelaria

Candelaria is a town deeply connected to its religious roots. As such, visitors are expected to dress modestly when visiting religious sites. The town is also known for its traditional crafts, such as ceramics and woven baskets, which make great souvenirs.

Best Time to Visit Candelaria

The best time to visit Candelaria is during the summer months of June to September when the weather is warm and sunny. However, during peak season, the town can get crowded, so visitors who prefer a more laid-back experience can opt to visit during the shoulder season in spring or fall.

Do’s and Don’ts of Candelaria

Do:

  • Dress appropriately when visiting religious sites
  • Try the local cuisine and drink
  • Take a hike on the numerous trails available
  • Respect local customs and traditions

Don’t:

  • Take photos in places where photography is prohibited
  • Litter or leave your trash in public spaces
  • Behave disrespectfully and/or inconsiderately towards locals

Safety and Health

Safety in Candelaria

Candelaria is a safe town with low crime rates. Visitors should still be wary of pickpockets and take necessary precautions.

Health in Candelaria

Visitors should bring basic medication for common ailments such as headaches, stomachaches and colds and ensure that they have valid health insurance when travelling.

Getting to Candelaria: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Candelaria

The nearest airport to Candelaria is Tenerife South Airport, located approximately 40 km away from the town. Tenerife North Airport is a little further away at about 70 km.

Bus Stations Near Candelaria

Candelaria bus station is the main hub for buses that travel to and from other parts of the island, including Santa Cruz.

Other Options

Another option for getting around Candelaria is to rent a car from the airport or local car rental agencies.

How to Get Around in Candelaria

Candelaria is a small town that can easily be explored on foot. There are also plenty of taxis, buses, and rental cars available for those who prefer to travel around the town in comfort.

Where to Go Next from Candelaria: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Santa Cruz de Tenerife
  • Teide National Park
  • Puerto de la Cruz
  • La Laguna

Conclusion

Candelaria is a town that has something for everyone. From its cultural and historical sites to its natural attractions, there is no shortage of things to do and see. The town’s local cuisine, crafts and tranquil way of life make it not only a great place to visit but an ideal getaway from the hustle and bustle of daily life. So come and explore this hidden gem in Tenerife!
