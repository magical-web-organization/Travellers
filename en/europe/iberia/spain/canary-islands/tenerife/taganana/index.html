Introduction to Taganana

Taganana is a small village located in the north-eastern part of Tenerife, in the Canary Islands in Spain. Surrounded by the Anaga Mountains, this picturesque village offers visitors a unique blend of natural beauty, rich history, and culture. With its cobbled streets, whitewashed houses, and charming local atmosphere, Taganana is an ideal destination for travelers seeking an authentic and off-the-beaten-track experience in Tenerife.

List of Places to Visit in Taganana

There are plenty of places to explore in Taganana, from museums and cultural landmarks to outdoor attractions and scenic views. Here are some of the must-visit destinations in Taganana:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Iglesia de Nuestra Señora de las Nieves: This historic church is one of the most iconic landmarks in Taganana. Built in the 16th century, the church features an impressive Baroque-style façade and a beautiful collection of religious artwork.

  • Casa de los Mesa: A fine example of traditional Canarian architecture, Casa de los Mesa is a 17th-century mansion that has been converted into a museum. Visitors can explore the mansion’s well-preserved interiors, including antique furniture, ceramic tiles, and paintings.

  • Mirador de Taganana: Located at the top of a hill overlooking the village, this viewpoint offers breathtaking panoramic views of the coastline and the Anaga Mountains. It’s a perfect spot to watch the sunset.

Natural Attractions of Taganana: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Playa de Almáciga: Nestled among the cliffs and accessible via a winding road, this secluded beach is an ideal spot for a refreshing swim or sunbathing. The beach offers an ideal surf spot.

  • Parque Rural de Anaga: A protected natural area located in the Anaga Mountains, this lush forest is home to a diverse range of flora and fauna. There are several hiking trails here, including the famous “Mercedes” path.

  • Roque de las Bodegas: This impressive rock formation on the coast is the best natural landmark of Taganana. Visitors can explore nearby natural pools and watch seabirds.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Taganana

For adventure seekers, Taganana offers an incredible network of hiking trails and biking routes, with stunning views of the mountains and the ocean. The most popular hiking routes in Taganana are:

  • PR-TF 10: This well-signposted path takes trekkers from El Batán to Taborno, offering excellent views of the coastline along the way. The trail culminates in the Roque de los Dos Hermanos, a gorgeous natural landmark.

  • Sendero de los Sentidos: A beautiful nature trail that meanders from Afur to Taganana, passing through the Parque Rural de Anaga and offering breathtaking views of the mountains and the sea.

  • El Bailadero: A challenging hiking route that takes trekkers from Taganana to Roque Negro. This trail passes through stunning landscapes and offers some of the most magnificent views of the Anaga Mountains.

Best Things to Do in Taganana: Activities and Tours

There are plenty of activities and tours to enjoy during your visit to Taganana, including:

  • Surf Lessons: Taganana is known for its waves and is a popular surf spot. Take private or group surfing lessons with qualified instructors.

  • Boat Tours: Enjoy a leisurely boat tour along the stunning Anaga coastline, explore hidden coves, and watch for dolphins and other marine life.

  • Caving Tours: Discover the underground caves and explore the world of stalactites and stalagmites.

  • Traditional Cooking Classes: Get a taste of local cuisine, learn how to cook traditional Canarian dishes, such as Papas arrugadas or Grilled sardines, using locally sourced ingredients.

Week-Long Itinerary for Taganana

If you’re planning a week-long itinerary in Taganana, here’s what you can do:

  • Day 1: Arrive in Taganana and explore the village’s charming streets and traditional architecture. Visit the Casa de los Mesa Museum and the Iglesia de Nuestra Señora de las Nieves.

  • Day 2: Take a leisurely stroll along Playa de Almáciga and enjoy a swim in the refreshing ocean water. Hike at the nearby Anaga Rural Park and enjoy a picnic.

  • Day 3: Take a boat tour along the Anaga coastline and explore hidden coves and marine life. Visit Roque de las Bodegas natural landmark.

  • Day 4: Take a caving tour and explore the underground caves. Take a break in the afternoon and enjoy a cooking class and wine tasting.

  • Day 5: Hire a bike and explore the Taganana and El Batán area. From Afur, take the Sendero de los Sentidos, and see the beautiful vistas.

  • Day 6: Take a surfing lesson or go for some solo surfing and relax on the beach afterwards.

  • Day 7: Shop for local souvenirs and handicrafts. Visit nearby town of San Cristobal de La Laguna for sightseeing and last-minute shopping.

Food and Drink Scene of Taganana

Taganana offers a unique culinary experience, with many traditional dishes and local ingredients that reflect its rich cultural heritage. Here are some must-try dishes and drinks of Taganana:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Papas arrugadas: Small boiled potatoes served with canary island mojo sauces.

  • Grilled sardines: one of the freshest and tastiest seafood dishes.

  • Rancho canario: a hearty meat-based stew made with garbanzo beans and other vegetables.

Local Beverages

  • Wines: Tenerife is known for its wine production, and the local wine is a must-try. The volcanic soil and unique weather create wine with a distinct taste.

  • Ron miel: This sweet liqueur, made from honey and rum, is a unique local specialty.

Where to Stay in Taganana: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Taganana offers a range of accommodation options, from camping sites to guesthouses and small hotels. While the quality of the accommodation varies, most of them feature stunning views of the ocean and the mountains. Here are some recommendations:

  • Apartamentos el Chorro: A cozy apartment with excellent views of the coastline and the mountains.

  • La Laguna Gran Hotel: Beautiful colonial and contemporary architecture hotel in San Cristóbal de La Laguna, within 30 minutes’ drive from Taganana.

-Camping Albergue Montes de Anaga: As the name suggests, Montes de Anaga campsite is located in the heart of the Anaga Rural Park. A perfect base for nature and adventure lovers who want to explore the beautiful landscapes of the park.

What to Buy in Taganana: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

There are plenty of souvenirs and local products to bring home from Taganana, including:

  • Canary Island Embroidery: A speciality of the Island, hand-embroidered tablecloths, napkins and other linen items are beautiful and stylish souvenirs.

  • Canary Island ceramics: traditional pottery like plates and bowls are beautifully vibrant colored, hand-painted with delicate patterns and symbols.

  • Canary Island Honey and Honey Products: high-quality honey is a popular product in Tenerife, and of course, it’s used to make Ron miel too.

Local Culture and Customs of Taganana

Taganana has a rich and fascinating history and unique cultural traditions that are celebrated annually. One of the most important festivals is:

  • Fiestas de Nuestra Señora de las Nieves: A multi-day celebration that takes place in August and honors the patron saint of Taganana. The festivities include parades, music, dancing, and traditional food.

Best Time to Visit Taganana

Taganana is an all-year-round destination, with pleasant weather throughout the year. The best time to visit this small village is from March to May and from September to November when the weather is mild and the crowds are less dense. It’s perfect for hiking and outdoor activities.

Do’s and Don’ts of Taganana

Here are some helpful tips on what to do and what not to do when visiting Taganana:

Do:

  • Do respect the local traditions and customs of Tagananaans.

  • Do try the local cuisine and drinks.

  • Do wear comfortable and sturdy shoes when hiking.

Don’t:

  • Don’t litter and dispose of trash properly.

  • Don’t take souvenirs from natural environments.

  • Don’t underestimate the strength of the ocean and the mountains.

Safety and Health

Taganana is generally a safe village, but visitors should take precautionary measures when hiking and swimming in designated areas. Here are some health and safety tips for travelers:

Safety in Taganana

  • Always hike with a companion and never go alone.

  • Follow the trail markers and stay on marked paths while hiking.

  • Pay attention to weather forecasts and avoid hiking during adverse weather conditions.

  • Do not swim in dangerous currents, and always follow lifeguard advice.

Health in Taganana

  • Always wear sunscreen and a hat when outdoors.

  • Stay hydrated during hikes and outdoor activities.

  • Bring an insect repellent to protect against mosquito bites.

  • Bring any necessary medication with you and seek medical assistance if needed.

Getting to Taganana: Transportation Options and Logistics

Taganana is accessible by public transport, car, or taxi. Here are some transportation options for getting to Taganana:

Airports Near Taganana

  • Tenerife North Airport is the closest airport, 20 kilometers away.
  • Tenerife South Airport is 80 kilometers away.

Bus Stations Near Taganana

  • From Tenerife North Airport, take the bus that goes to Santa Cruz de Tenerife (Line 102) and from there take bus 947 to the town of Taganana.

Car rental

  • Car rental is available at both airports, giving the maximum flexibility to explore the island.

How to Get Around in Taganana

The best way to get around Taganana is by foot or bike. The village is relatively small, and most attractions are within easy walking distance. Alternatively, you can hire a taxi or car for a more comfortable way to explore the surrounding areas.

Where to Go Next from Taganana: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

If you have more time, there are plenty of nearby destinations and excursions worth exploring, including:

  • San Cristobal de La Laguna: a world heritage UNESCO city, beautiful mix of colonial and modern architecture.
  • Garachico: a tiny seaside town with black sand and unique volcanic rock formations.
  • Teide National Park: a UNESCO site, this park never ceases to amaze visitors with its impressive landscape and volcanic history.

Conclusion

Taganana is an off-the-beaten-track destination in Tenerife, perfect for travelers seeking a peaceful and authentic experience. With its rich history, beautiful landscapes, and unique culinary scene, Taganana offers something for everyone. By following this ultimate travel guide, visitors can make the most of their visit to Taganana and explore the best attractions this charming village has to offer.
