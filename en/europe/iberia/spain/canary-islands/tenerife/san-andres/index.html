Introduction to San Andrés

San Andrés is a hidden gem nestled in the northern coast of Tenerife, Canary Islands. It is a place of natural and cultural beauty that attracts visitors looking for an off-the-beaten-path getaway. Its secluded sands, stunning mountain views, historic buildings, and friendly locals never disappoint. This comprehensive travel guide will help you make the most out of your visit to San Andrés.

List of Places to Visit in San Andrés

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • (1) Iglesia de San Andrés Apostol: This 16th-century church is the centerpiece of the town, known for its unique architecture and stunning altarpiece.
  • (2) Casa El Capitán: This historic house-turned-museum showcases the traditional Canarian architecture, furniture, and everyday life of the past.
  • (3) Real Santuario del Cristo de La Laguna: This Catholic sanctuary houses the revered statue of Christ, believed to be miraculous. It is an essential pilgrimage site for devotees and religious tourists.

Natural Attractions of San Andrés: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • (1) Playa de las Teresitas: This crescent-shaped, golden sand beach, is a must-visit when in San Andrés, offering crystal-clear water, gentle waves, excellent restaurants, and a relaxed atmosphere.
  • (2) Anaga Natural Park: A hiker’s paradise, this stunning biosphere reserve, boasts dramatic mountainous terrain, lush laurel forests, and endemic flora and fauna. Trails range from easy to challenging, with breathtaking views at every turn.
  • (3) Mirador de San Andrés: This viewpoint, situated at the town’s outskirts, provides a stunning panoramic view of San Andrés, its coastline, and the Atlantic Ocean.

Hiking/Biking Routes of San Andrés

Feel the thrill of nature by hiking and biking in San Andrés, with various scenic routes that offer surreal experiences.

  • (1) Roque de Taborno: A moderately difficult hike that will take you 10 km inland, where the impressive rock formation awaits.
  • (2) El Pijaral: A family-friendly hike, where you can find the remains of a pre-Hispanic village and luscious greenery after 4 km.
  • (3) Teno Alto: A tough hike for experienced hikers that pay off with stunning views over the mountains and the coastline after 11 km.

Best Things to Do in San Andrés: Activities and Tours

  • (1) Surf at Playa de las Teresitas: Take notes from the local surfers and enjoy the waves of this stunning beach.
  • (2) Try your hand at Kayaking: Discover the coast of San Andrés in a kayak rental, and observe the marine life at its best.
  • (3) Guided Tour to Roque de Taborno: Get to know the history of the village, the flora, and the fauna with a guided tour to Roque de Taborno.

Week-Long Itinerary for San Andrés

Day 1: Arrival to San Andrés, settle down, and enjoy the evening on the beach.

Day 2: Visit the Iglesia de San Andrés Apostol and Casa El Capitán. Have lunch at a local restaurant and enjoy the town’s atmosphere.

Day 3: Spend the day at Anaga Natural Park hiking in one of the trails. End the day with dinner at a restaurant near the park.

Day 4: Take a kayaking tour around the coast of San Andrés, and then relax on the beach.

Day 5: Head over to Laguna and visit the Real Santuario del Cristo de La Laguna, have lunch in the area, and explore the city’s charming streets.

Day 6: Go on a guided tour to Roque de Taborno and learn about its history and nature.

Day 7: Last day in San Andrés, relax on the beach and say goodbye to the beautiful town.

Food and Drink Scene of San Andrés

Must-Try Dishes

  • (1) Papas arrugadas: These wrinkly, salted potatoes, served with red and green mojo sauce, are a Canarian staple.
  • (2) Ropa Vieja: this hearty beef stew with slightly spicy tomato sauce and garbanzo beans is a favorite for the chilly evenings.
  • (3) Churros and Chocolate: A sweet and satisfying tradition for a late-night snack.

Local Beverages

  • (1) Tenerife Wine: The volcanic soil and the oceanic climate make Tenerife a unique place for vineyards, producing exquisite wines to taste.
  • (2) Ron Miel: A sweet, thick liquor made from rum and honey, perfect to warm for those chilly nights.

Where to Stay in San Andrés: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • (1) Hotel Playa Canaria: A beachfront hotel with an outdoor pool, daily breakfast, and friendly staff.
  • (2) Las Casas del Chorro: These beautifully restored, traditional houses offer visitors an authentic experience of Canarian architecture and lifestyle.
  • (3) Casa Rural Tio Felix: A charming, rustic guesthouse nestled in the Anaga Mountains, offering stunning views, a garden, and traditional Canarian breakfast.

What to Buy in San Andrés: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • (1) Traditional pottery: Famed for its unique style, traditional Canarian pottery is a great souvenir to bring back home.
  • (2) Local cheese: from hard to soft, made from goat or sheep milk, the Canarian cheese never disappoints.
  • (3) Tenerife Wine: bring back home a bottle of the unique wines that Tenerife offers for the wine connoisseur.

Local Culture and Customs of San Andrés

San Andrés has a relaxed, welcoming atmosphere, and the locals are friendly and always willing to help. The Canarian tradition of fiestas joyfully alive in San Andrés, where festivals are celebrated year-round, with music, dancing, and a convivial ambiance. Festivities in honor of Saint Andrew, the town’s patron saint, are celebrated in late November, with the highlight being a colorful procession and fireworks display.

Best Time to Visit San Andrés

San Andrés is a year-round destination, with the pleasant, mild climate making it perfect for visitation in any season. Summer, with its steady temperatures of around 25°C, is the best time to visit for beach lovers. Winter, from December to February, is the low season, with fewer crowds and cheaper accommodation prices.

Do’s and Don’ts of San Andrés

Do:

  • Try the local food and drinks.
  • Respect the natural heritage.
  • Enjoy the festivals.

Don’t:

  • Leave trash or litter behind.
  • Disrespect the cultural heritage.
  • Forget your sunscreen.

Safety and Health

Safety in San Andrés

San Andrés is a safe destination, with a low crime rate. Petty theft, like pickpocketing and purse snatching, is the most common crime. Visitors should exercise normal safety precautions, such as not leaving valuables unattended and being aware of your surroundings, especially in crowded areas.

Health in San Andrés

There are no major health concerns in San Andrés, but visitors should be mindful of the potential risks when exploring the Anaga Mountains and the coast. Wear appropriate footwear, bring sunscreen, and carry enough water when hiking. Water-related activities have no particular risks other than the advice of following safety instructions.

Getting to San Andrés: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near San Andrés

The closest airport to San Andrés is Tenerife North Airport (TFN), located about 12 km away, with flights from several European and domestic destinations. Another option is Tenerife South Airport (TFS), which is about 50 km away and has more extensive flight options.

Bus Stations Near San Andrés

Buses are the most convenient and budget-friendly way to get around Tenerife, with several lines connecting San Andrés with other towns and cities. The nearest bus station is in the town of Santa Cruz, which is easily accessible from the beach and offers frequent departures.

Other Options

For those who prefer private transportation, car rental is another option. Several rental companies operate in both airports and other selected locations on the island.

How to Get Around in San Andrés

Walking is the best way to get around San Andrés town, which is compact and easy to navigate. For those who want to explore nearby attractions or the Anaga Mountains, car rental or public transportation is recommended.

Where to Go Next from San Andrés: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • (1) Santa Cruz de Tenerife: Explore the vibrant capital of Tenerife, with its colonial architectures, museums, and modern shopping centers.
  • (2) La Laguna: A UNESCO World Heritage Site, this charming town boasts well-preserved colonial architecture, pristine streets, and lively cafes.
  • (3) Taganana: A nature reserve like no other, Taganana is one of the few unspoiled natural beauties on the island. With its rugged coastline and breathtaking cliffs, this remote destination offers secluded beaches and the authentic island spirit.

Conclusion

San Andrés is a destination that offers an all-around experience, with a combination of natural beauty, cultural richness, and welcoming atmosphere. Whether you are looking to relax on the beach, hike the mountains, or explore the local customs, San Andrés has something to offer. By following this comprehensive travel guide, you will make the most out of your visit and take back unforgettable memories of Tenerife, Canary Islands.
