Introduction to Aviles

Aviles is a charming town located in Asturias, a region famous for its green landscapes and ancient traditions. This city is known for its cultural and historical heritage, as well as its breathtaking natural beauty. Aviles offers visitors a unique opportunity to explore ancient cobbled streets, taste local dishes, and learn about the traditional culture of Asturias.

List of Places to Visit in Aviles

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Calle Galiana and Plaza de España: These two beautiful landmarks take you back in time through the medieval history of Aviles.
  • Plaza del Mercado: This square is a center for local culture and traditions. There are usually street performances and art exhibitions that showcase the local scene.
  • Museo de la Ciudad de Avilés: The City Museum of Aviles has a great collection of art and artifacts that make up its historical cultural heritage.
  • Palacio de Valdecarzana: This magnificent palace-palace is an impressive example of medieval architecture.

Natural Attractions of Aviles: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • El Parque de Ferrera: This large park boasts of playgrounds, tennis courts, and large grassy areas perfect for a picnic.
  • Playa del Salinas: This is the perfect beach to relax and escape the hustle and bustle of city life.
  • Mirador de la Ría: The viewpoint on the river walkway overlooking the estuary of Aviles, with the industrial heritage city.
  • La Ruta de Las Riberas del Aviles: The hiking trail in the park, following the river course in the city.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Aviles

  • Ruta de las Brañas de la Güera: This is a trekking route that takes starts from Peñafrancia and goes through the Villaviciosa Valley, fantastic for the views of surrounding landscapes.
  • Ruta del Agua: A biking and hiking trail that makes an interesting Azud de Trasona viewing point.
  • Ruta de los Castros: This trail offers a glimpse into the early life of the Asturians during the Bronze Age.

Best Things to Do in Aviles: Activities and Tours

  • Walking Tour of Old Town: Join a walking tour of the Old Town and experience the magical ambiance of cobbled streets, historic buildings & landmarks.
  • Asturian Cider tasting: Asturias is famous for its cider, so a tasting tour is the best to have the full experience.
  • Kayaking on Ria de Aviles: This kayak experience offers a beautiful scenic route through the estuary while observing the city from a unique perspective.
  • Land Rover 4x4 adventure: A tour in this all-terrain vehicle through the rural area surrounding Aviles, in which you will visit incredible locations hard to reach.

Week-Long Itinerary for Aviles

Food and Drink Scene of Aviles

Must-Try Dishes

  • Fabada Asturiana: This hearty dish made with white beans, chorizo, and pork has become a signature dish of Asturias.
  • Seafood dishes: As Aviles is a port town, it boasts of fresh seafood from the sea, which makes it an essential to try in the local cuisine.
  • Potaje de berzas y fabes: A soup dish made with beans and cabbage that is a must-try in the winter season.

Local Beverages

  • Sidra Asturiana: Cider is the traditional drink of Asturias, so don’t miss a chance to try local cider straight from the barrel.
  • Asturian wines: Although Asturias is not to be known for its wines, it has some excellent varieties that are worth a try.

Where to Stay in Aviles: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Hotel NH Collection Palacio de Aviles: This hotel is located in a beautifully restored palace in the heart of the Old Town.
  • Hotel el Horreo de Avilés: This hotel is perfect for those looking for a budget-friendly option. It’s located close to the cathedral and market square.
  • Hotel 40 Nudos: This charming guesthouse is located in the historic center of Aviles and is one of the best choices for a more comfortable stay.

What to Buy in Aviles: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Cider: Cider is available in various sizes, and you can take a bottle or two home as a souvenir.
  • Local crafts: Various artisanal products such as pottery, textiles, and wooden products make great souvenirs as they are handmade locally.
  • Cheeses and honey: The region has traditional cheese with varying flavors, and honey has strong character from the local flowers making it unique to this region.

Local Culture and Customs of Aviles

Asturians are passionate about their cultural traditions, which have been passed down from generation to generation. The most famous festivals in Aviles are “Fiesta de San Agustin” and “Fiestas de la Ascensión.” Both festivals are celebrated in August and are a lively affair with music, dancing, and plenty of local food and drink.

Best Time to Visit Aviles

The best time to visit Aviles is during spring and summer when the weather is mild, and outdoor activities are in full swing. The season is ideal for hiking, kayaking, and beach trips.

Do’s and Don’ts of Aviles

Do:

  • Try local dishes and drinks, as they form an essential part of the culture and tradition of Asturias.
  • Learn some basic Spanish before your trip as most locals do not speak English.
  • Follow the cultural customs and practices of the city.

Don’t:

  • Don’t make loud noises and be disruptive in public spaces.
  • Don’t underestimate the time needed to explore the city.

Safety and Health

Safety in Aviles

Aviles is a relatively safe city with low crime rates. However, it is always advisable to be cautious and take the necessary precautions to safeguard your belongings.

Health in Aviles

Asturias has excellent health facilities with good quality medical care. As with any travel, make sure you are adequately insured to cover any emergency medical expenses.

Getting to Aviles: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Aviles

  • Asturias Airport: The nearest airport is Asturias Airport, situated 12km from Aviles.

Bus Stations Near Aviles

  • Aviles bus station: The main bus station is in the city center and offers connections to major cities in Spain.

Other Options

  • Train: RENFE operates trains to Aviles daily from major cities in Spain.
  • Car Rental: A rental car offers the best option for exploring the countryside and surrounding areas.

How to Get Around in Aviles

The city has an integrated public transportation system comprising buses, trains and taxis. Alternatively, hire bicycles are available for rent at several locations in the city.

Where to Go Next from Aviles: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Gijón: A short train ride from Aviles takes you to the coastal city of Gijón, famous for its beautiful beaches and cultural attractions.
  • Oviedo: Asturias’ capital, is a must-see city, full of beautiful architecture and cultural attractions.
  • Picos de Europa National Park: One of the most stunning natural parks in Spain, located two hours from Aviles, is a must-visit for any nature lover.

Conclusion

Aviles is a unique destination in Spain, offering a blend of cultural and natural attractions. Whether it’s for a weekend trip or more extended vacation, there is plenty to see and do. Use this comprehensive tour guide to Aviles when planning your next trip to explore this historic town.
