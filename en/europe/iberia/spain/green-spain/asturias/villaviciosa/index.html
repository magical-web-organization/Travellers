Introduction to Villaviciosa

Located in the green region of Asturias in Spain, Villaviciosa is a charming and historic town known for its beautiful beaches, rich culture, and delicious food. With its medieval architecture and stunning natural landscapes, Villaviciosa is the perfect destination for travelers looking for an authentic Spanish experience.

List of Places to Visit in Villaviciosa

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • The Palace of Valdes Bazan
  • The Church of Santa Maria de la Oliva
  • The Casa de los Hevia
  • The Archaeological Museum of Asturias
  • The Museum of Apple Cider

Natural Attractions of Villaviciosa: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Rodiles Beach
  • The Sierra del Sueve Mountains
  • El Puntal Beach
  • The Ria de Villaviciosa Nature Reserve
  • The Camino de Santiago

Hiking/Biking Routes of Villaviciosa

  • The Ruta de las Peregrinaciones
  • The Ruta de los Molinos
  • The Ruta de la Madera
  • The Ruta de la Sidra
  • The Ruta de los Merenderos

Best Things to Do in Villaviciosa: Activities and Tours

  • Visit the local cider houses (sidrerias) to taste the famous Asturian cider.
  • Take a boat tour of the Ria de Villaviciosa and explore its stunning natural beauty.
  • Learn about the local history and culture by visiting the museums and historic sites.
  • Go surfing at Rodiles Beach, one of the best surf spots in Spain.
  • Participate in the local festivals, such as the Fiesta de la Sidra in September.

Week-Long Itinerary for Villaviciosa

Day 1: Explore the town center and visit the Palace of Valdes Bazan and the Church of Santa Maria de la Oliva.

Day 2: Take a tour of the Museum of Apple Cider, followed by a visit to a local sidreria for a taste of Asturian cider.

Day 3: Hike the stunning Sierra del Sueve Mountains and enjoy the breathtaking views.

Day 4: Visit the Ria de Villaviciosa Nature Reserve and take a boat tour to explore its natural beauty.

Day 5: Relax on the beautiful beaches of El Puntal and Rodiles.

Day 6: Take a day trip to the nearby city of Oviedo and explore its rich history and culture.

Day 7: Participate in the local festivals and cultural events.

Food and Drink Scene of Villaviciosa

Must-Try Dishes

  • Fabada Asturiana (stew made with white beans and pork)
  • Cachopo (breaded and stuffed beef or pork)
  • Pixin a la Sidra (monkfish stewed in cider)
  • Merluza a la Sidra (hake cooked in cider)
  • Arroz con Leche (rice pudding)

Local Beverages

  • Sidra (Asturian cider)
  • Orujo (liquor made from skins and seeds of grapes)
  • Vino Asturiano (Asturian wine)
  • Cafe con Leche (coffee with milk)
  • Licor de Hierbas (herbal liqueur)

Local Culture and Customs of Villaviciosa

Asturians are known for their rich cultural heritage, including their traditional music, dance, and festivals. In Villaviciosa, the Festival de la Sidra in September is a popular event that celebrates the local cider culture. Visitors are encouraged to participate in these events and learn more about the local culture and customs.

Best Time to Visit Villaviciosa

The best time to visit Villaviciosa is from May to September, when the weather is warm and sunny. This is also the time when the local festivals and cultural events take place.

Do’s and Don’ts of Villaviciosa

Do:

  • Try the local cider (sidra) and participate in the cider culture.
  • Respect the local customs and traditions.
  • Learn some basic Spanish phrases before you go.
  • Explore the stunning natural landscapes of Villaviciosa.

Don’t:

  • Dress too casually when visiting churches or other religious sites.
  • Be disrespectful to the local culture or traditions.
  • Expect everyone to speak English.

Safety and Health

Safety in Villaviciosa

Villaviciosa is a safe and welcoming town, but visitors should take normal safety precautions to avoid petty crime, such as pickpocketing and bag snatching.

Health in Villaviciosa

The healthcare system in Spain is excellent, and visitors can access medical services at local clinics or hospitals if needed.

Getting to Villaviciosa: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Villaviciosa

The nearest international airport to Villaviciosa is Asturias Airport (OVD), which is located about 40 km away. Travelers can take a bus or taxi from the airport to Villaviciosa.

Bus Stations Near Villaviciosa

Villaviciosa has a bus station that connects it to other towns and cities in Asturias.

Other Options

Travelers can also rent a car or hire a private driver to get to Villaviciosa from other parts of Spain.

How to Get Around in Villaviciosa

The best way to get around Villaviciosa is on foot or by bike. Taxis and local buses are also available for travelers who need them.

Where to Go Next from Villaviciosa: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Oviedo: The capital city of Asturias is a must-see for its rich history and culture.
  • Gijon: A scenic coastal town with beautiful beaches and excellent seafood.
  • The Picos de Europa National Park: A stunning national park with breathtaking mountain landscapes.

Conclusion

Villaviciosa is a true gem of Asturias, offering a unique blend of rich history, stunning natural landscapes, and delicious food and drink. By using this comprehensive travel guide, visitors can plan the perfect trip to Villaviciosa and create unforgettable memories of their time in this beautiful Spanish town.
