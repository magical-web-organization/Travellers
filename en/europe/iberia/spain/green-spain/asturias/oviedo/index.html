Introduction to Oviedo

Nestled in the heart of Green Spain, Oviedo is the capital city of the Asturias region in northern Spain. The city boasts an intriguing mixture of Romanesque and Gothic architecture, beautiful natural surroundings, and a lively culture. Visitors to Oviedo are greeted with a medley of cultural, natural, and gastronomic attractions spread across narrow winding streets. Here is our comprehensive travel guide to Oviedo.

List of Places to Visit in Oviedo

Whether seeking cultural and historical landmarks or breathtaking scenery, Oviedo has got it all. Here’s a list of must-visit places when in Oviedo:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Oviedo has a rich cultural heritage that extends back to the Middle Ages. There are numerous historical landmarks in Oviedo that are worth visiting, including:

  • The Holy Chamber of Oviedo Cathedral
  • The Archaeological Museum of Asturias
  • The Museum of Fine Arts of Asturias
  • University of Oviedo Historic Building

Natural Attractions of Oviedo: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Oviedo is surrounded by lush green mountains, and it offers numerous opportunities for outdoor activities such as hiking, biking, and mountaineering. Some of the natural wonders you can experience include:

  • Naranco Hill
  • Los Lagos de Covadonga
  • Monte Naranco
  • Senda del Oso

Hiking/Biking Routes of Oviedo

Oviedo offers an excellent network of hiking and cycling trails throughout the Asturian countryside. These include routes like the Camino de Santiago, the Senda del Oso, and the Anillo Ciclista. The most popular routes start from Oviedo and lead to stunning natural landmarks and historical sites that are well worth a visit.

Best Things to Do in Oviedo: Activities and Tours

Oviedo has a lot to offer visitors of all ages and preferences. From historical and cultural sites to thrilling outdoor adventures, Oviedo has got it all. Some of the best things to do in Oviedo include:

  • Visit the landmarks of Oviedo’s Old Town.
  • Take a hike to Naranco Hill, which offers stunning views of the city.
  • Explore the Museum of Fine Arts of Asturias.
  • Take a guided tour of the Oviedo Cathedral and its Holy Chamber.

Week-Long Itinerary for Oviedo

To get the most out of Oviedo, we recommend spending at least a week here. Here’s an itinerary that covers the most popular sights and activities:

Day Itinerary
Day 1 Explore Oviedo’s historic Old Town and visit the Oviedo Cathedral.
Day 2 Hike or bike to Naranco Hill, which affords panoramic views of the city and the surrounding countryside.
Day 3 Visit some of the museums, such as the Archaeological Museum and the Museum of Fine Arts.
Day 4 Take a day trip to Covadonga and visit its beautiful lakes.
Day 5 Experience the Senda del Oso, a cycling route through stunning natural landscapes.
Day 6 Discover some of Oviedo’s gastronomic diversity, such as its local specialties.
Day 7 Take it easy and enjoy some shopping and leisure time in Oviedo’s plazas and streets.

Food and Drink Scene of Oviedo

Oviedo boasts a rich gastronomic offering that is a hallmark of the region’s identity. Whether you’re looking for hearty traditional dishes or gourmet cuisine, Oviedo has something for everyone.

Must-Try Dishes

  • Fabada Asturiana (a rich bean stew with meat and chorizo)
  • Cachopo (a meat-based dish consisting of two large veal fillets surrounding ham and cheese)
  • Cabrales cheese (a blue cheese made in Asturias)

Local Beverages

  • Apple cider (Sidra)
  • Vermouth (Vermut)

Where to Stay in Oviedo: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Oviedo offers a wide range of accommodation options to fit every budget and preference, from luxurious hotels to budget-friendly hostels. Some of the top places to stay in Oviedo include:

  • NH Oviedo Principado
  • Hotel Campoamor
  • Room Mate Marcos

What to Buy in Oviedo: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Oviedo is known for its unique cultural and traditional products. Some of the most popular souvenirs include:

  • Asturian cider
  • Capes and scarves made of wool
  • Local ceramics and pottery

Local Culture and Customs of Oviedo

Asturias is a region with a proud culture and well-defined identity. Asturian customs are still alive in Oviedo, and visitors can experience them through traditional festivities, dances, and music. If you’re lucky enough to visit during the festivities of El Asturcón, you’ll have the opportunity to see one of the region’s most remarkable cultural traditions, horse racing along the sand of the shores of San Juan de la Arena.

Best Time to Visit Oviedo

The best time to visit Oviedo is in spring and autumn, as the weather is mild, and the city is less crowded. However, the summer months are still popular for tourists, boasting warm temperatures and an exciting cultural calendar.

Do’s and Don’ts of Oviedo

Do:

  • Greet the locals with a smile and a greeting.
  • Taste the regional food and drink specialties.
  • Participate in the local festivals and traditions.

Don’t:

  • Trespass on private property.
  • Litter or leave trash behind.
  • Ignore safety signs and warnings.

Safety and Health

Safety in Oviedo

Oviedo is a safe city, but visitors should still be cautious and take the necessary precautions to avoid any mishaps. Follow basic safety tips and be aware of your surroundings at all times.

Health in Oviedo

Oviedo has excellent healthcare facilities, with a high standard of medical care. For visitors, it is advisable to ensure that personal health insurance covers emergencies abroad.

Getting to Oviedo: Transportation Options and Logistics

Oviedo enjoys excellent transport links to the rest of the region and beyond. Here are the most common transportation options:

Airports Near Oviedo

  • Asturias Airport

Bus Stations Near Oviedo

  • Oviedo Bus Station
  • Gijón Bus Station

Other Options

  • Train: Oviedo is connected to all major cities in Spain by train.
  • Car hire: There are several car hire companies in Oviedo.

How to Get Around in Oviedo

Oviedo is a relatively small city that is easy to explore on foot. However, for those wishing to travel farther afield, there are numerous options, including buses, taxis, and rental cars.

Where to Go Next from Oviedo: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Oviedo is a gateway to many other nearby destinations, including:

  • Gijón, a lively city with a vibrant cultural scene
  • Picos de Europa National Park, a stunningly beautiful mountain range
  • Avilés, a city with an impressive medieval heritage

Conclusion

Oviedo is a must-visit destination for anyone traveling in northern Spain. Its rich cultural heritage, beautiful natural surroundings, and delicious cuisine make it a truly unique and unforgettable experience. Whether visiting for a week or a weekend, there’s something for everyone in Oviedo. Be sure to take advantage of our comprehensive guide to plan your trip and make the most of your stay in this remarkable city.
