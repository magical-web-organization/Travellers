Introduction to Asturias

Asturias is a charming region located in Green Spain. Known for its stunning landscapes, rich history, and vibrant culture, Asturias attracts visitors from around the world.

One of the most remarkable features of Asturias is its diverse geography. From snow-capped mountains to sandy beaches, the region offers a wide range of outdoor activities and scenic views. The local cuisine is also a highlight, with hearty dishes and unique beverages that will tantalize your taste buds.

Whether you’re interested in exploring cultural landmarks, embarking on outdoor adventures, or simply relaxing in a picturesque setting, Asturias has something for everyone.

List of Places to Visit in Asturias

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Archaeological Museum of Asturias: Learn about the region’s ancient history and archeological discoveries, including the Celts and the Roman Empire.
  • Santa Maria del Naranco: This UNESCO World Heritage site is a stunning example of pre-Romanesque architecture.
  • Oviedo Cathedral: Completed in the 16th century, this cathedral is considered one of the most significant religious buildings in Spain.

Natural Attractions of Asturias: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Picos de Europa National Park: Home to towering peaks, verdant valleys, and diverse wildlife, this park is a must-visit for outdoor enthusiasts.
  • Senda del Oso: This trail winds through beautiful forests and valleys, and is an ideal setting for hiking and biking.
  • Cudillero: This charming fishing village is known for its colorful houses and stunning coastal views.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Asturias

  • Ruta del Cares: This challenging hike stretches 12km along a beautiful gorge and is one of the most popular hiking routes in the region.
  • Senda del Oso: This trail offers an easy path through breathtaking scenery, and is perfect for biking as well.
  • Covadonga Lakes: A picturesque hike, this trail takes you through dense forests and stunning mountain scenery.

Best Things to Do in Asturias: Activities and Tours

  • Cider Tasting Tour: Try the local drink, Asturian cider, and learn about the traditional cider-making process.
  • Surfing Lessons: The beaches of Asturias offer excellent surfing opportunities, and lessons are available for all skill levels.
  • Gijon Aquarium: This aquarium is home to a wide variety of marine life, including sharks, sea turtles, and penguins.

Week-Long Itinerary for Asturias

Day 1: Oviedo Cathedral, Archaeological Museum of Asturias

Day 2: Santa Maria del Naranco, Senda del Oso

Day 3: Picos de Europa National Park

Day 4: Cudillero, Surfing Lessons

Day 5: Covadonga Lakes, Gijon Aquarium

Day 6: Ruta del Cares, Cider Tasting Tour

Day 7: Relaxation, Beaches of Asturias

Food and Drink Scene of Asturias

Must-Try Dishes

  • Fabada Asturiana: A hearty stew made with beans, chorizo, and pork, this dish is a regional specialty.
  • Cachopo: A delicious dish consisting of two large veal fillets stuffed with ham and cheese and fried until crispy.
  • Pastel de Cabracho: A savory cake made with rockfish, tomato sauce, and breadcrumbs.

Local Beverages

  • Sidra: Asturian cider is a refreshing beverage made from locally grown apples.
  • Rioja Wine: The region is known for its excellent wines, particularly reds made with the Tempranillo grape.

Where to Stay in Asturias: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • NH Collection Palacio de Aviles: Located in the stunning coastal town of Aviles, this hotel offers luxurious accommodations and excellent service.
  • La Casona de Amandi: This charming guesthouse is located in the heart of the Asturian countryside, and features rustic decor and homemade breakfasts.
  • Youth Hostel Gijon: Perfect for budget travelers, this hostel is located in the center of Gijon and offers comfortable accommodations and communal spaces.

What to Buy in Asturias: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Asturian cheese: Local cheese is made from cow, sheep, or goat milk and is a must-try for cheese lovers.
  • Salmón ahumado: This smoked salmon is a delicacy in Asturias and can be found in local markets and food shops.
  • Pottery: Handmade pottery is a popular souvenir, with many local artisans selling their wares in markets throughout the region.

Local Culture and Customs of Asturias

  • Festivals: Asturias is known for its vibrant festivals, including the International Bagpipe Festival and the Festival of Ciders.
  • Etiquette: locals are friendly and welcoming, and it is customary to greet people with a smile and a handshake.

Best Time to Visit Asturias

The best time to visit Asturias is between May and October when the weather is mild and sunny. However, if you’re interested in skiing or other winter activities, December through February can also be a great time to visit.

Do’s and Don’ts of Asturias

Do:

  • Try the local cuisine and beverages, including Asturian cider, Fabada Asturiana, and cachopo.
  • Visit cultural landmarks and natural attractions to fully experience the beauty and history of the region.
  • Respect local customs and etiquette, and greet people warmly.

Don’t:

  • Expect to find many people who speak English, so brush up on your Spanish before you go.
  • Be disrespectful or dismissive of local customs or traditions.

Safety and Health

Safety in Asturias

Asturias is generally a safe region for travelers. However, it is important to take basic precautions such as keeping valuables secure and being aware of your surroundings in crowded areas.

Health in Asturias

Spain has a modern healthcare system, and hospitals and medical facilities are available throughout the region. Make sure you have travel insurance that covers medical emergencies.

Getting to Asturias: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Asturias

  • Asturias Airport: Located in Castrillón, the Asturias Airport has connections to major cities in Spain and Europe.

Bus Stations Near Asturias

  • Oviedo Bus Station: Located in the center of Oviedo, this station offers connections to cities throughout the region.

Other Options

  • Train: Spain has an extensive rail network, with connections to Asturias from major cities throughout the country.
  • Car: Renting a car is a great option for exploring the region on your own.

How to Get Around in Asturias

Public transportation is available throughout the region, with buses and trains connecting major cities and towns. However, if you’re interested in exploring the countryside, renting a car or bike is recommended.

Where to Go Next from Asturias: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • San Sebastian: This charming coastal city is known for its stunning architecture, top-notch cuisine, and beautiful beaches.
  • Bilbao: Home to the Guggenheim Museum and a thriving art scene, Bilbao is a must-visit for art enthusiasts.
  • Santiago de Compostela: This historic city is the end point of the Camino de Santiago, a popular hiking route.

Conclusion

Asturias is a unique and beautiful region that offers a wide range of cultural and natural attractions. Whether you’re interested in exploring historical landmarks, embarking on outdoor adventures, or simply relaxing in a stunning setting, Asturias has something for everyone. Use our comprehensive travel guide to plan your visit and discover the best that Asturias has to offer.
