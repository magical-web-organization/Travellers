Luanco is a hidden gem on the Bay of Biscay, located in the Asturias region of northern Spain. With its picturesque streets, traditional fishing port, and pristine beaches, Luanco has something for everyone. In this comprehensive travel guide, we will explore the best places to visit, outdoor adventures, hiking routes, food scene, and more in Luanco.

Introduction to Luanco

Luanco is a small fishing town that boasts a rich history and culture. It is famous for its traditional architecture, narrow streets, and the beautiful beach of Playa de Luanco. The town is also home to several cultural attractions, such as museums and historical sites, that offer a glimpse into the local history and heritage.

List of Places to Visit in Luanco

Luanco has a lot to offer to its visitors. Here are some of the must-visit places in the town:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Museo Marítimo de Asturias: This maritime museum showcases the history and culture of Asturias’ fishing industry and seafaring traditions.
  • Villa Romana de Veranes: These roman ruins can be seen in the town San Cucao de Llanera, the ruins were founded in the 17th century.
  • Plaza del Ayuntamiento: This town square is the heart of Luanco. It is lined with traditional Asturian houses, cafes, and shops.

Natural Attractions of Luanco: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Playa de Luanco: This beautiful beach is one of the top attractions of Luanco, with crystal-clear water and golden sand that stretch for over 1 km.
  • Cabo Peñas: This scenic spot offers stunning views of the Bay of Biscay and the Asturias coastline. It is a popular spot for hiking and birdwatching.
  • Ruta de los Miradores: This hiking trail traverses through the hills of Luanco, offering breathtaking views of the town and the sea.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Luanco

Luanco is surrounded by hills and mountains, making it a prime location for hiking and biking enthusiasts. Here are some of the best hiking and biking routes in and around Luanco:

  • Ruta de los Miradores: This 5 km hiking trail starts from the Plaza del Ayuntamiento and offers stunning views of the town and the sea.
  • Ruta de las Calas: This 10 km hiking trail starts from the Playa de Luanco and takes you along the beautiful coastline of Asturias.
  • Ruta de la Peña Furada: This 8 km hiking trail offers impressive views of the Asturian landscape and the sea.

Best Things to Do in Luanco: Activities and Tours

Here are some of the best things to do in Luanco:

  • Take a stroll through the old town and admire the traditional Asturian architecture and narrow streets.
  • Go surfing or paddleboarding in the Bay of Biscay.
  • Take a boat tour and explore the Asturias coastline from the sea.

Week-Long Itinerary for Luanco

Here is a suggested week-long itinerary for Luanco:

Day 1: Arrival in Luanco and exploring the old town. Day 2: Hiking the Ruta de los Miradores and visiting the Museo Marítimo de Asturias. Day 3: Beach day at Playa de Luanco and paddleboarding or windsurfing. Day 4: Hiking the Ruta de las Calas and visiting the Villa Romana de Veranes. Day 5: Cabo Peñas hike and exploring the nearby town of Avilés. Day 6: Boat tour of the Asturias coastline and seafood dinner at one of the local restaurants. Day 7: Departure from Luanco.

Food and Drink Scene of Luanco

Luanco is known for its delicious seafood and traditional Asturian cuisine. Here are some of the must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Fabada Asturiana: This bean stew is one of the most famous dishes of Asturias.
  • Chorizo a la Sidra: This chorizo is cooked in cider and is a popular local dish.
  • Sopa de Pescado: This traditional fish soup is a must-try for seafood lovers.

Local Beverages

  • Sidra: This traditional Asturian cider is famous for its unique taste and production process.
  • Cerveza Rubia Asturiana: This local beer is brewed in Asturias and is a popular drink among locals.

Where to Stay in Luanco: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Luanco offers a variety of accommodation options for visitors. Here are some of the best places to stay in Luanco:

  • Hotel Rural Playa de Aguilar: This charming rural hotel is located near the Playa de Luanco and offers stunning views of the sea.
  • Hotel Rural Los Galpones: This rustic hotel is located on the foothills of the mountains and offers a peaceful stay.
  • Apartamentos Mirapeñas: These modern apartments are located in the heart of Luanco and offer a comfortable stay for visitors.

What to Buy in Luanco: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Luanco is famous for its traditional Asturian crafts and delicacies, making it an ideal place to buy souvenirs and local products. Here are some of the best things to buy in Luanco:

  • Handcrafted pottery
  • Traditional Asturian cheese
  • Sidra

Local Culture and Customs of Luanco

Luanco has a rich cultural heritage that is reflected in its local customs and traditions. The locals are proud of their Asturian identity and are known for their warm hospitality and love for their traditions.

Best Time to Visit Luanco

The best time to visit Luanco is between May and September, when the weather is warm and sunny. However, the town is worth visiting year-round, thanks to its beautiful scenery and unique cultural offerings.

Do’s and Don’ts of Luanco

Do:

  • Try the local cuisine and traditional Asturian dishes.
  • Take a boat tour and explore the Asturias coastline.
  • Wander through the old town and admire the traditional Asturian architecture.

Don’t:

  • Overpack, as Luanco is a small town with limited luggage storage options.
  • Ignore the local customs and traditions.
  • Miss the opportunity to try the famous Asturian cider.

Safety and Health

Safety in Luanco

Luanco is a safe town for visitors. However, it is always advisable to take basic safety precautions like not leaving your belongings unattended and staying in well-lit areas at night.

Health in Luanco

Luanco has excellent healthcare facilities that cater to the needs of visitors. Visitors should ensure they carry any medication they may need and have adequate insurance coverage.

Getting to Luanco: Transportation Options and Logistics

Luanco is well-connected to other parts of Asturias and Spain. Here are some of the transportation options to reach Luanco:

Airports Near Luanco

  • Asturias Airport is the closest airport to Luanco.

Bus Stations Near Luanco

  • The main bus station in Luanco is at the Plaza del Ayuntamiento.

Other Options

  • Visitors can also reach Luanco by train or car.

How to Get Around in Luanco

Luanco is a small town that can be easily explored on foot. However, visitors who wish to explore the surrounding areas can rent a car or bicycle from one of the local rental companies.

Where to Go Next from Luanco: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Luanco is located in the Asturias region, making it an ideal base from which to explore other parts of Asturias and northern Spain. Here are some of the nearby destinations and excursions:

  • Oviedo: The capital of the Asturias region, Oviedo is famous for its medieval architecture and rich cultural heritage.
  • Avilés: This charming coastal town is known for its beautiful beaches and traditional Asturian architecture.
  • Picos de Europa National Park: This national park is home to some of the most stunning scenery and hiking trails in Spain.

Conclusion

Whether you’re looking for a laid-back beach vacation, outdoor adventure, or cultural experience, Luanco has it all. With its beautiful beaches, traditional architecture, and warm hospitality, Luanco is a must-visit destination in Spain.
