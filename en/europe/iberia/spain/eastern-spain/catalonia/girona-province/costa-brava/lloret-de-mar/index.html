Introduction to Lloret de Mar

Located in the Costa Brava region of Spain’s eastern Catalonia, Lloret de Mar is a popular beach destination that attracts visitors with its stunning coastal landscapes, vibrant nightlife, and cultural attractions. Known for its picturesque coves, turquoise waters, and lush greenery, Lloret de Mar is an ideal spot for sunbathing, swimming, and outdoor adventures. With a rich history that dates back to Roman times, Lloret de Mar is also home to numerous historical landmarks, museums, and art galleries.

In this comprehensive travel guide, we’ll take you through the best things to do and see in Lloret de Mar, including cultural attractions, natural wonders, outdoor adventures, and culinary experiences. Whether you’re looking for a family-friendly beach vacation or an adventurous getaway with friends, Lloret de Mar has something for everyone. So let’s start exploring!

List of Places to Visit in Lloret de Mar

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Museum of the Sea – housed in a 19th-century Modernist-style building, this museum offers an extensive collection of maritime artifacts, including ship models, navigation tools, and fishing equipment.

  • Sant Joan’s Castle – a medieval fortress that dates back to the 11th century, this castle offers stunning panoramic views of Lloret de Mar and the surrounding coastline.

  • Parish Church of Sant Roma – a beautiful Gothic-style church that was built in the 16th century and features a stunning altarpiece and stained-glass windows.

  • Can Comadran Museum – this museum is housed in a restored 18th-century farmhouse and features exhibits that explore the rural life of Lloret de Mar during the 19th and 20th centuries.

Natural Attractions of Lloret de Mar: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Santa Clotilde Gardens – a beautiful botanical garden that is perched on a hilltop and features lush greenery, winding paths, and stunning sea views.

  • Fenals Beach – a picturesque bay that is known for its clear waters, soft sand, and tranquil atmosphere.

  • Sa Caleta Cove – a hidden gem that is a favorite spot among locals, this rocky cove offers clear waters that are perfect for snorkeling and diving.

  • Montserrat Park – a beautiful natural park that covers over 200 acres and offers hiking trails, picnic areas, and stunning views of the Mediterranean coast.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Lloret de Mar

Lloret de Mar is a great destination for outdoor enthusiasts who enjoy hiking and biking. The following are some of the best trails in and around Lloret de Mar.

  • Camino de Ronda – a beautiful coastal path that follows the shoreline and offers stunning sea views.

  • Les Fonts de Sant Pere – a scenic hiking trail that leads hikers through lush forests, stunning waterfalls, and picturesque streams.

  • La Creu de Sant Joan – a popular hiking trail that leads hikers to the top of Sant Joan’s Castle, offering breathtaking panoramic views of Lloret de Mar and the surrounding coastline.

Best Things to Do in Lloret de Mar: Activities and Tours

  • Water Sports – Lloret de Mar is known for its excellent water sports opportunities, including kayaking, snorkeling, diving, and paddleboarding.

  • Boat Tours – take a boat tour and explore the stunning coastline of Lloret de Mar from a different perspective.

  • Nightlife – Lloret de Mar is known for its lively nightlife scene, with numerous bars, clubs, and pubs offering a variety of entertainment options.

  • Day Trips – take a day trip to nearby attractions such as the city of Girona, the Salvador Dali Museum in Figueres, or the medieval village of Tossa de Mar.

Week-Long Itinerary for Lloret de Mar

Day 1: Explore the town center and visit the Museum of the Sea.

Day 2: Spend a day at Fenals Beach and Santa Clotilde Gardens.

Day 3: Embark on the Camino de Ronda coastal hike.

Day 4: Take a boat tour and explore the stunning coastline of Lloret de Mar.

Day 5: Visit Sant Joan’s Castle and the Parish Church of Sant Roma.

Day 6: Enjoy water sports activities such as paddleboarding, snorkeling, and diving.

Day 7: Take a day trip to the medieval village of Tossa de Mar or the city of Girona.

Food and Drink Scene of Lloret de Mar

Must-Try Dishes

  • Paella – a classic Spanish rice dish that is made with seafood or meat, saffron, and vegetables.

  • Fideua – a local dish that is similar to paella but made with noodles instead of rice and typically served with squid or shellfish.

  • Esqueixada – a typical Catalan dish that consists of salt cod, tomatoes, onions, and olives.

  • Crema Catalana – a delicious dessert that is made with cream, sugar, and a crunchy caramelized sugar top.

Local Beverages

  • Sangria – a refreshing and fruity cocktail that is made with red wine, chopped fruit, brandy, and sugar.

  • Cava – a Spanish sparkling wine that is similar to champagne.

  • Vermouth – an aromatic fortified wine that is typically served with soda water and a slice of orange.

Where to Stay in Lloret de Mar: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Lloret de Mar offers a variety of accommodation options, including budget-friendly hostels, cozy guesthouses, and luxury beachfront hotels. Some of our top picks include:

  • Hotel Delamar Adults Only – a chic and modern adults-only hotel that is located just steps away from the beach.

  • Hostel Sun and Moon – a budget-friendly hostel that offers clean and comfortable accommodation in the heart of Lloret de Mar.

  • Hotel Rosamar Garden Resort – a family-friendly hotel that features an on-site water park, multiple swimming pools, and spacious rooms.

What to Buy in Lloret de Mar: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Catalan Ceramics – Lloret de Mar is known for its beautiful ceramic art, which comes in a range of styles and designs.

  • Handmade Fans – a typical Spanish souvenir that is both practical and decorative.

  • Local Wines and Vermouths – bring home a bottle of Catalan cava or vermouth as a delicious and authentic souvenir.

Local Culture and Customs of Lloret de Mar

Lloret de Mar is a welcoming and friendly destination, and visitors are encouraged to respect the local customs and traditions. Here are some important customs to keep in mind:

  • Tipping is not expected in restaurants, but it is common to round up the bill or leave a small amount of change.

  • It is considered polite to greet people in Spanish or Catalan, even if you don’t speak the language fluently.

  • Lloret de Mar is a Catholic town, so visitors should be mindful of local religious customs and traditions.

Best Time to Visit Lloret de Mar

The best time to visit Lloret de Mar is from May to October when the weather is warm and sunny, and the beaches are at their best. July and August are the peak tourist seasons, so expect higher prices and more crowded beaches during these months.

Do’s and Don’ts of Lloret de Mar

Do:

  • Enjoy the coastal scenery and outdoor activities.

  • Sample the local cuisine and drinks.

  • Learn some key phrases in Spanish or Catalan.

Don’t:

  • Leave your valuables unattended on the beach.

  • Disrespect local customs and traditions.

  • Drink excessively and engage in rowdy behavior.

Safety and Health

Safety in Lloret de Mar

Overall, Lloret de Mar is a safe destination for visitors. However, visitors should take common precautions to avoid theft and pickpocketing, especially in crowded areas and on public transportation.

Health in Lloret de Mar

Visitors to Lloret de Mar should have up-to-date vaccinations and be aware of potential health risks such as sunburn, heatstroke, and food poisoning. It’s recommended to have travel insurance that covers medical emergencies.

Getting to Lloret de Mar: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Lloret de Mar

The nearest airports to Lloret de Mar are Girona Airport and Barcelona–El Prat Airport. Both airports offer frequent flights to destinations across Europe and beyond.

Bus Stations Near Lloret de Mar

Lloret de Mar is served by a network of regional and local buses that connect the town with nearby destinations. The main bus station is located in the town center.

Other Options

Alternatively, visitors can rent a car and explore the region at their own pace. Lloret de Mar is well-connected to major roads and highways.

How to Get Around in Lloret de Mar

Lloret de Mar is a compact town that is easily navigable on foot. However, visitors can also take advantage of the local bus service or rent a bike to explore the surrounding countryside.

Where to Go Next from Lloret de Mar: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Lloret de Mar is located within easy reach of many other popular destinations in Catalonia, including:

  • Girona – a charming medieval city that is known for its rich history, stunning architecture, and gastronomic scene.

  • Tossa de Mar – a picturesque coastal town that is famous for its stunning beaches, charming old town, and medieval castle.

  • Barcelona – Spain’s vibrant and cosmopolitan capital city that offers a wealth of cultural attractions, including Gaudi’s iconic architecture, world-class museums, and delicious food.

Conclusion

Whether you’re looking for a relaxing beach vacation, an active outdoor adventure, or a cultural and historical experience, Lloret de Mar has something for everyone. This comprehensive guide has provided you with all the information you need to plan an unforgettable trip to this stunning destination in the heart of the Costa Brava. So, pack your bags, book your ticket, and get ready to experience the best of Lloret de Mar!
