Introduction to Pals

Pals is a charming town located in Costa Brava, an idyllic coastal region in Eastern Spain. This medieval town boasts stunning architecture, scenic beaches, and breathtaking landscapes. If you’re looking for the perfect mix of history and outdoor adventure, Pals is the ideal getaway destination. In this comprehensive tour guide, we’ll help you plan your trip to Pals, with information about the must-see attractions, where to stay, what to eat, and how to get around.

List of Places to Visit in Pals

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Torre de les Hores: This 15th-century tower was once used to communicate with other towers along the Mediterranean coast. Today, it houses a museum dedicated to the tower’s history and the medieval town of Pals.
  • Plaça Major: Pals’ main square, surrounded by beautiful historic buildings, including the Romanesque Church of Sant Pere.
  • Gothic Quarter: Wander through the winding streets of the Gothic Quarter and discover the town’s rich history, including the remnants of the old city walls.
  • Museu de la Pesca: Learn about the region’s fishing traditions at this fascinating museum, which includes exhibits on fishing techniques, boats, and tools.

Natural Attractions of Pals: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Playa de Pals: This stunning beach stretches for over three kilometers and is famous for its crystal-clear waters and golden sand. It’s the perfect place to relax, swim, or try out some water sports.
  • Ses Negres: A rocky cove with clear blue waters, perfect for snorkeling and exploring the local marine life.
  • Montgrí Natural Park: This protected area offers some of the best hiking and biking routes in the region, with stunning views of the coast and the Montgrí Massif.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Pals

  • Camí de Ronda: This coastal trail follows the rugged cliffs of the Costa Brava, with breathtaking views of the Mediterranean Sea.
  • GR-92: This long-distance trail runs along the entire Mediterranean coast of Spain, passing through Pals and offering incredible views of the coastline.

Best Things to Do in Pals: Activities and Tours

  • Scuba diving: Explore the underwater world of the Costa Brava with a scuba diving excursion.
  • Hot air balloon ride: See Pals and the surrounding areas from a unique perspective with a hot air balloon ride.
  • Wine tasting: Sample some of the region’s best wines at one of the local wineries.

Food and Drink Scene of Pals

Must-Try Dishes

  • Suquet de peix: A traditional Catalan fish stew, made with fresh seafood and potatoes.
  • Fideuà: Similar to paella, fideuà is a dish made with short noodles instead of rice and plenty of shellfish and squid.
  • Crema Catalana: A creamy dessert made with burnt sugar, eggs, and milk, similar to crème brûlée.

Local Beverages

  • Cava: Catalonia’s version of sparkling wine, made from the local grape varieties.
  • Ratafía: A sweet liqueur made with herbs, spices, and nuts, often served as a digestif.

Where to Stay in Pals: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Hotel Mas Salvi: A luxury hotel set in a restored 17th-century farmhouse, surrounded by lush gardens and stunning countryside views.
  • Hostal Sa Rascassa: A stylish, beachfront hostel with stunning sea views and direct access to the beach.
  • Can Barrull: This family-run guesthouse is located in the heart of the Gothic Quarter, with charming rooms and a rooftop terrace with panoramic views of Pals.

What to Buy in Pals: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Olive oil: Spain is famous for its high-quality olive oil, and Pals has several local producers.
  • Ceramics: Pals is home to several pottery studios, so you can take home some unique handmade ceramics.
  • Wine: Don’t forget to bring home a bottle or two of the region’s excellent local wines.

Local Culture and Customs of Pals

The local culture of Pals is deeply rooted in its medieval past, and the town celebrates its history with numerous festivals and traditions throughout the year. One of the most famous is the Fira Medieval de Pals, which takes over the town every summer with medieval markets, shows, and performances.

Best Time to Visit Pals

The best time to visit Pals is between May and September, when the weather is warmest and the beaches are at their best. However, keep in mind that this is also the busiest season, so be prepared for crowds and higher prices.

Do’s and Don’ts of Pals

Do:

  • Wear sunscreen and a hat during the summer months, as the sun can be intense.
  • Try the local cuisine and wine.
  • Explore the town on foot or by bike.

Don’t:

  • Forget to make reservations in advance, especially during peak season.
  • Leave valuables unattended on the beach or in your car.
  • Engage in any disrespectful behavior towards local customs or traditions.

Safety and Health

Safety in Pals

Pals is generally a safe destination, but be aware of pickpocketing in crowded areas such as the beach and the town center.

Health in Pals

There are no major health risks in Pals, but it’s always a good idea to have travel insurance and to pack any necessary medications.

Getting to Pals: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Pals

  • Girona-Costa Brava Airport: Located 50 kilometers from Pals, Girona airport is the closest airport to Pals, with numerous flights to European destinations.

Bus Stations Near Pals

  • Palafrugell bus station: Located 10 kilometers from Pals, this bus station has connections to major cities in the region.

Other Options

  • Car rental: Renting a car is a great option if you want to explore the surrounding areas at your own pace.

How to Get Around in Pals

The best way to get around Pals is on foot or by bike, as the town is small and easy to navigate. There are also local buses and taxis available.

Where to Go Next from Pals: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Cadaqués: This picturesque coastal town is a favorite among artists and offers stunning views of the Mediterranean Sea.
  • Figueres: The birthplace of artist Salvador Dalí, Figueres is home to the Dalí Theatre-Museum and numerous other art galleries and museums.
  • Girona: This historic city is home to a well-preserved old town, famous for its cathedral and Jewish Quarter.

Conclusion

Pals is a hidden gem that offers something for everyone, from its rich history and culture to its stunning natural landscapes and outdoor adventures. This comprehensive tour guide has covered everything you need to know to plan your trip to Pals, including the best things to do, where to stay, and how to get around. So pack your bags and get ready to explore this charming medieval town on the Costa Brava!
