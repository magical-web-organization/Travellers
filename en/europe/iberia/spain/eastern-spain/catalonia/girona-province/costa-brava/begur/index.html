Introduction to Begur

Begur is a quaint town in the Costa Brava region of Girona, Catalonia in Spain. This town is nestled between lush green forests and pristine beaches, making it a popular destination among tourists. Begur is known for its old alleys, seafront cafes, 17th-century houses, and beautiful beaches that offer a plethora of water sports and activities.

In this comprehensive travel guide, we will explore everything you need to know about Begur, including must-visit places, hiking and biking routes, activities and tours, local cuisine, where to stay, and how to get around.

List of Places to Visit in Begur

Begur has a rich history and unique culture that reflects through its architecture, museums, and other tourist attractions. Here are some of the top places you must visit when in Begur:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Castell de Begur

Castell de Begur is an ancient castle that dates back to the 17th century. This castle offers a panoramic view of the city and the coastline, making it an excellent spot for photography.

Museu de la Mediterrània

Museu de la Mediterrània is a museum that showcases the rich history and culture of the Mediterranean region. This museum has a vast collection of artifacts dating back to the Roman era.

Cap de Begur

Cap de Begur is a headland overlooking the Mediterranean sea. It is one of the most picturesque locations in Begur, offering stunning views of the surrounding landscape.

Natural Attractions of Begur: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Sa Riera Beach

Sa Riera Beach is a stunning sandy beach that offers crystal clear water and a wide range of water activities like scuba diving, kayaking, and paddleboarding.

Aiguablava Beach

Aiguablava Beach is another beautiful beach with crystal clear water, golden sand, and surrounded by lush green forests. It is a perfect spot for swimming, sunbathing, and picnicking.

Camí de Ronda

Camí de Ronda is a hiking and biking trail that skirts the coastline of Begur. This trail offers stunning views of the Mediterranean sea and the surrounding mountains and forests.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Begur

Begur has many hiking and biking trails, which offer an opportunity to explore the natural beauty of the region. Here are some of the top hiking and biking routes of Begur:

Camí de Ronda

Camí de Ronda is a 20 km hiking and biking trail, which covers the coastline around Begur. This trail offers stunning views of the Mediterranean sea and the surrounding mountains and forests.

Begur to Sa Tuna

This route starts from the town of Begur and leads to the quaint fishing village of Sa Tuna. This route offers beautiful views of the sea and the surrounding countryside.

Begur to Pals

This route leads to the town of Pals, passing through lush green forests and fields. The route is perfect for cycling and offers stunning views of the mountains and the surrounding countryside.

Best Things to Do in Begur: Activities and Tours

Besides hiking and biking, Begur has many other activities and tours that are worth exploring. Here are some of the best things to do in Begur:

Scuba diving

Begur offers excellent scuba diving opportunities, with crystal clear waters and abundant marine life.

Kayaking and Paddleboarding

Begur has many beautiful beaches and bays, making it an ideal spot for kayaking and paddleboarding.

Wine Tasting

Begur has many wineries that offer wine tastings, allowing visitors to sample some of the best wines in the region.

Food and Drink Scene of Begur

Begur has a diverse food and drink scene, with many local delicacies that are worth exploring. Here are some of the must-try dishes and beverages of Begur:

Must-Try Dishes

Arros Caldos

Arros Caldos is a traditional rice dish made with a variety of local seafood, including prawns and mussels. It is a hearty and delicious dish that is popular in Begur.

Paella

Paella is another traditional rice dish that is popular in Begur. It is made with rice, saffron, chicken, and seafood, including prawns and mussels.

Crema Catalana

Crema Catalana is a dessert made with custard, caramelized sugar, and cinnamon. It is a must-try dessert in Begur.

Local Beverages

Cava

Cava is a local sparkling wine that is made in Catalonia. It is a popular drink in Begur, and many wineries offer tastings of this bubbly wine.

Vermut

Vermut is a local vermouth that is made with white or red wine and infused with botanicals and spices. It is a popular drink that is often served before meals in Begur.

Where to Stay in Begur: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Begur has many options for accommodation, including hotels, hostels, and guesthouses. Here are some of the top places to stay in Begur:

Hotel Aiguaclara

Hotel Aiguaclara is a boutique hotel located in the heart of Begur. It is a beautifully restored 19th-century mansion that offers luxurious rooms and suites.

La Indiana de Begur

La Indiana de Begur is a charming guesthouse located in the center of Begur. It offers comfortable rooms and a cozy atmosphere, with a shared terrace and garden.

Sa Barraca

Sa Barraca is a hostel located on Sa Riera beach. It offers dormitory-style accommodation and is an excellent option for budget travelers.

What to Buy in Begur: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Begur has many souvenirs and local products that are worth bringing home. Here are some of the top things to buy in Begur:

Ceramic Products

Begur has a long tradition of pottery, and there are many ceramic shops that offer beautiful handmade products, including plates, bowls, and figurines.

Local Wine

Begur has many wineries that produce excellent wines, including Cava and Vermut. These local wines are perfect to bring home as a souvenir or gift.

Local Culture and Customs of Begur

Begur has a unique culture and customs that are worth exploring. Here are some of the local customs and practices to keep in mind:

Siesta

Many shops and businesses close during the afternoon for siesta, a traditional nap time.

Festivals

Begur hosts many festivals throughout the year, including the Fira d’Indians, which celebrates the town’s history and heritage.

Best Time to Visit Begur

The best time to visit Begur is from May to September when the weather is warm and sunny and there are many outdoor activities and events.

Do’s and Don’ts of Begur

Here are some do’s and don’ts to keep in mind when visiting Begur:

Do:

  • Respect the local customs and culture.
  • Try the local cuisine and wines.
  • Take part in the local festivals and events.

Don’t:

  • Leave trash on the beach or hiking trails.
  • Be loud and disruptive in public places.
  • Wear revealing clothing in religious places or historical monuments.

Safety and Health

Safety in Begur

Begur is a safe town, and the crime rate is low. However, travelers should still take normal precautions, like not leaving valuables unattended and being aware of their surroundings.

Health in Begur

Begur has many medical facilities, including hospitals, clinics, and pharmacies. Travelers should have travel insurance and seek medical help if needed.

Getting to Begur: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Begur

The closest airport to Begur is Girona-Costa Brava Airport, which is about 60 km away. Barcelona-El Prat Airport is also an option, as it is about 135 km away.

Bus Stations Near Begur

Estació d’Autobusos de Palafrugell is the closest bus station to Begur, located about 5 km away. From here, travelers can take a taxi or local bus to Begur.

Other Options

Many travelers choose to rent a car or hire a private transfer to get to Begur. This option gives them more flexibility and allows them to explore the surrounding areas.

How to Get Around in Begur

Begur is a small town, and many of the attractions and sights are within walking distance. However, travelers can also use local buses or taxis to get around town or rent a bike to explore the surrounding countryside.

Where to Go Next from Begur: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Here are some nearby destinations and excursions that travelers can explore after visiting Begur:

Girona

Girona is a historic city located about 50 km north of Begur. It has a beautiful old town, a gothic cathedral, and many museums and galleries.

Barcelona

Barcelona is a cosmopolitan city, located about 135 km south of Begur. It is known for its stunning architecture, world-class museums and galleries, and vibrant nightlife.

Cadaqués

Cadaqués is a charming fishing village, located about 35 km north of Begur. It is known for its narrow streets, white houses, and beautiful seafront promenade.

Conclusion

Begur is a small town with a rich history, culture, and natural beauty.
