Introduction to L’Estartit

L’Estartit is a charming town located on the Costa Brava in the province of Girona, Catalonia, in eastern Spain. It is situated at the base of the Montgrí Massif mountain range, offering visitors stunning views of both the sea and the mountains.

The town is known for its beautiful beaches and crystal clear waters, making it a popular destination among tourists. However, it also has a rich cultural heritage, with several museums and historical sites that are definitely worth exploring.

This comprehensive tour guide for L’Estartit will provide you with all the information you need to plan your trip, including a list of places to visit, things to do, where to stay, local culture and customs, and more.

List of Places to Visit in L’Estartit

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  1. Museu de la Mediterrània: A museum that showcases the cultural and natural heritage of the Mediterranean region, with exhibits on art, history, and archaeology.

  2. Castell de Montgrí: A medieval castle that was built in the 13th century and offers stunning panoramic views of the surrounding area.

  3. Illa Pedrosa: A small island located in the middle of the Medes Islands archipelago, where you can explore a 19th-century lighthouse and enjoy the beautiful scenery.

  4. Santa Catalina Watchtower: A watchtower dating back to the 16th century that was built to protect the town from pirate attacks.

Natural Attractions of L’Estartit: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  1. Medes Islands: A group of islands that are a protected marine park and offer fantastic opportunities for diving and snorkeling.

  2. Montgrí Massif Natural Park: A natural park that covers an area of over 8,000 hectares and features stunning landscapes, hiking trails, and wildlife.

  3. Beaches: L’Estartit has several beautiful beaches, including Playa de L’Estartit, Playa de la Gola, and Playa de la Pletera.

Hiking/Biking Routes of L’Estartit

Several hiking and biking trails are available in L’Estartit and its surrounding areas. Some popular routes include:

  1. Montgrí Massif Trail: A 20 km trail that takes you through the Montgrí Massif Natural Park and offers stunning views of the surrounding area.

  2. Cami de Ronda: A trail that follows the coastline of the Costa Brava and takes you through several picturesque villages and beaches.

  3. The Greenway: A 40 km biking/walking trail that runs along an old railway track and connects the towns of Girona and Sant Feliu de Guíxols.

Best Things to Do in L’Estartit: Activities and Tours

  1. Scuba Diving: L’Estartit is known for its fantastic scuba diving opportunities, with several diving centers offering guided dives and courses.

  2. Kayaking: You can rent a kayak and explore the beautiful coastline and hidden coves of L’Estartit.

  3. Boat Tours: Take a boat tour around the Medes Islands archipelago and enjoy the stunning views, wildlife, and crystal clear waters.

  4. Golf: You can play golf at the Empordanet Golf Club, which is located just a few kilometers away from L’Estartit.

Food and Drink Scene of L’Estartit

Must-Try Dishes

  1. Suquet de Peix: A traditional fish stew that usually includes monkfish, potatoes, onions, and garlic.

  2. Arròs a la cassola: A rice dish that is cooked with a variety of meats (usually chicken, pork, and sausage), vegetables, and spices.

  3. Espencat: A vegetable dish that usually consists of roasted red pepper, eggplant, and onion.

Local Beverages

  1. Cava: A sparkling wine that is produced in Catalonia and is similar to champagne.

  2. Vermut: Vermouth is a fortified wine that is often served as an aperitif.

Where to Stay in L’Estartit: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

L’Estartit has a range of accommodation options, from budget-friendly hostels to luxurious hotels and guesthouses. Some of the top-rated options include:

  1. Costa Brava Hotel: A 3-star hotel located just a few meters away from the beach.

  2. Hotel Bell Aire: A 2-star hotel located in the historic town center of L’Estartit.

  3. Hostal Santa Clara: A budget-friendly hostel that offers comfortable rooms and a convenient location.

What to Buy in L’Estartit: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  1. Pottery: L’Estartit is known for its beautiful pottery, which is usually handcrafted and decorated with intricate designs.

  2. Olive Oil: Catalonia is known for its high-quality olive oil, and you can find several local producers in L’Estartit.

Local Culture and Customs of L’Estartit

L’Estartit has a rich cultural heritage, and you can experience it by participating in one of the many local festivals and celebrations, including:

  1. Festa Major: A festival that takes place in August and includes parades, concerts, and fireworks.

  2. Carnaval: A carnival celebration that takes place in February, with costumes, music, and dancing.

Best Time to Visit L’Estartit

The best time to visit L’Estartit is during the summer months (June to September), when the weather is warm and sunny, and the beaches are perfect for swimming and sunbathing.

Safety and Health

Safety in L’Estartit

L’Estartit is a safe destination for travelers, with a low crime rate. However, it’s always a good idea to take the usual precautions and keep an eye on your belongings in crowded areas.

Health in L’Estartit

Spain has a high standard of healthcare, with many hospitals and clinics located throughout the country. Travelers with chronic medical conditions should bring all necessary medications with them and ensure they have adequate travel insurance coverage.

Getting to L’Estartit: Transportation Options and Logistics

  2. Barcelona-El Prat Airport: Located 150 km away from L’Estartit, with connections to several international destinations.

How to Get Around in L’Estartit

L’Estartit is a small town and can easily be explored on foot. However, there are also several bus and taxi services available for those who prefer not to walk.
