Introduction to Tossa de Mar

Tossa de Mar is a small town located on the Costa Brava in the Girona province of Catalonia, Spain. It is known for its beautiful beaches, natural landscapes, and ancient history. The town is divided into two parts – the historic walled town on a hill and the modern part, which is situated by the seafront. Tossa de Mar is becoming more and more popular with tourists who are looking for a quieter and more authentic Spanish experience. In this comprehensive travel guide to Tossa de Mar, we will introduce you to the town’s attractions, activities, accommodations, and travel tips.

List of Places to Visit in Tossa de Mar

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Tossa de Mar has a rich cultural heritage that dates back to the ancient times. Exploring its rich history can teach you a lot about the region’s evolution. Here are some of the most important historical and cultural places to visit in Tossa de Mar:

  • Vila Vella or the Old Town is the most remarkable part of Tossa de Mar. It is where the town’s main historical sites are located. The medieval walls that encircle the town are a must-see attraction. You can climb the winding streets paved with cobblestones that will take you to the top of the hill, where the magnificent castle stands.
  • Tossa de Mar Castle was built in the 12th century and still stands in tatters. The harsh sea winds and time have taken their toll on the castle, but visitors can still admire stunning views of the town and the coastline from the ruined walls.
  • Municipal Museum is where you can get up close and personal with local history. You will find traditional fishing gear, pottery, and many other curiosities of Tossa de Mar. The museum is open every day during the summer and is worth visiting if you want a glimpse into the town’s past.

Natural Attractions of Tossa de Mar: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Tossa de Mar is blessed with natural beauty that can take your breath away. The town is surrounded by pine forests, coves, and cliffs. The beaches and waters along Tossa de Mar are perfect for snorkeling, diving, and swimming. Here are some of the best natural attractions of Tossa de Mar:

  • Sant Joan Castle is perched on a hill overlooking the town and has some of the best scenic views of the Mediterranean Sea. The castle is a 45-minute walk from Tossa de Mar and is a great hike to take if you like photography or just want to enjoy the breathtaking views.
  • Sa Riera Beach is a relatively small beach, but it’s one of the prettiest in Tossa de Mar. This sandy beach is located in a bay and is surrounded by steep cliffs, making it a great place for swimming and snorkeling.
  • Cala Bona is a small cove with crystal clear waters. It’s a peaceful spot where you can relax and enjoy a swim or a picnic.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Tossa de Mar

Tossa de Mar is an ideal destination for exploring the outdoors and experiencing the region’s natural beauty. There are numerous hiking and biking trails that will take you through the hills, forests, and beaches around the town. Here are some of our favorites:

  • GR 92 Trail is a long-distance hiking trail that covers the entire coastline of Catalonia. The section that runs from Tossa de Mar to Sant Feliu de Guíxols is one of the most beautiful along Costa Brava. The trail offers spectacular views of the Mediterranean Sea.
  • Camí de Ronda or the Round Path is a hiking trail that hugs the coastline of Costa Brava. The section of the trail leading from Tossa de Mar to Lloret de Mar is quite challenging, but it offers some of the most magnificent views of the sea and surrounding landscapes.
  • Carrilet Greenway is a bike path that is perfect for cycling enthusiasts and families with young children. The trail takes you through the woods, farms, and vineyards around Tossa de Mar, providing a peaceful and scenic ride.

Best Things to Do in Tossa de Mar: Activities and Tours

Besides hiking and biking, there are many other exciting activities and tours to enjoy in Tossa de Mar:

  • Boat Tours provide a great opportunity to get up close and personal with the town’s beautiful beaches, cliffs, and coves. You can book a tour from the harbor in Tossa de Mar for a half-day or full-day excursion.
  • Snorkeling is a popular activity in Tossa de Mar. The waters are crystal clear and teeming with marine life, making it an ideal spot for exploration.
  • Nightlife in Tossa de Mar is lively but not as raucous as that in some other Spanish towns. You will find plenty of bars, lounges, and clubs where you can enjoy a glass of sangria or dance to some Spanish tunes.

Food and Drink Scene of Tossa de Mar

Tossa de Mar’s cuisine is a blend of traditional Catalan and Spanish dishes influenced by the town’s coastal location. Here are some of the must-try dishes and beverages of Tossa de Mar:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Suquet de Peix is a fish stew made with various types of fish, tomatoes, onions, and garlic. It is a staple of Catalan cuisine and is a must-try dish in Tossa de Mar.
  • Pa amb Tomàquet is a simple yet delicious dish that consists of bread, tomato, and olive oil. It is a popular Catalan breakfast or snack.
  • Coca de Tossa is a sweet pastry that originated in Tossa de Mar. It is made with flour, sugar, olive oil, and anise.

Local Beverages

  • Sangria is a typical Spanish drink made with red wine, chopped fruit, and some added spirits, and it’s perfect for a hot summer day.
  • Vermut is a wine-based drink flavored with botanicals, herbs, and spices. It’s a popular aperitif in Catalonia and is often served with soda or tonic water.

Where to Stay in Tossa de Mar: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Tossa de Mar offers a wide range of accommodation options for all budgets. Here are some of our recommendations:

  • Hotel Diana is located in the center of Tossa de Mar, just minutes from the beach and the historic town. It is a modern and clean hotel with excellent views of the sea.
  • Hostel Tossa de Mar is a budget-friendly hostel in the old town of Tossa de Mar. It offers room options for individuals, couples, and families.
  • Villa Hispania is a bed and breakfast in Tossa de Mar’s modern part. It provides a peaceful and tranquil retreat from the town’s busier areas.

What to Buy in Tossa de Mar: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Here are some of the best souvenirs and local products to buy in Tossa de Mar:

  • Coca de Tossa - as mentioned earlier, this is a sweet pastry unique to Tossa de Mar.
  • Anchovy fillets - Tossa de Mar is known for its anchovies, which are served in many local dishes.
  • Wine - the region around Tossa de Mar produces excellent wines, so you might want to take a bottle home as a souvenir.

Local Culture and Customs of Tossa de Mar

Tossa de Mar is a town steeped in ancient history and culture. The locals are friendly and welcoming, and they take pride in their traditions. Here are some of the customs and traditions you might come across:

  • Catalan language - Catalan is the official language of Catalonia, and you might hear it spoken more than Spanish in Tossa de Mar.
  • Festivals - Tossa de Mar has several festivals throughout the year, including La Joia, which is celebrated in September, and the International Fireworks Festival in August.
  • Siesta - like many other Spanish towns, Tossa de Mar observes the afternoon siesta. So, shops and businesses close from around 2 pm to 4 pm.

Best Time to Visit Tossa de Mar

The best time to visit Tossa de Mar is from June to September when the weather is warm and sunny, and the beaches are at their best. However, if you prefer fewer crowds, you could also visit Tossa de Mar in the off-season.

Do’s and Don’ts of Tossa de Mar

Here some do’s and don’ts to keep in mind when visiting Tossa de Mar:

Do:

  • Try the traditional dishes and enjoy the local cuisine.
  • Learn a few words and phrases in Catalan, the local language.
  • Respect the town’s history and cultural heritage.

Don’t:

  • Sunbathe naked on the beaches, it is not allowed in Tossa de Mar.
  • Leave the litter behind when visiting natural attractions.
  • Wear beachwear outside of the beach areas.

Safety and Health

Safety in Tossa de Mar

Tossa de Mar is a safe town for tourists, but like any other tourist destination, petty crimes like pickpocketing are not uncommon. Here are some safety tips to keep in mind:

  • Keep your valuables safe and secure and don’t carry too much cash on you.
  • Be aware of your surroundings when going out at night.
  • Take precautions when engaging in outdoor activities like hiking and swimming.

Health in Tossa de Mar

Tossa de Mar has several medical facilities that can cater to most health issues. There are several pharmacies in the town. If you need medical attention, you can visit:

  • CAP Tossa is a medical center located in the modern part of Tossa de Mar.
  • Hospital Comarcal de la Selva is the nearest hospital to Tossa de Mar, located in the nearby town of Santa Cristina d’Aro.

Getting to Tossa de Mar: Transportation Options and Logistics

Tossa de Mar is accessible by air, bus, and car. Here are some transportation options and logistics:

Airports Near Tossa de Mar

  • Girona-Costa Brava Airport is the nearest airport to Tossa de Mar, located 25 km away. It is the most popular choice for tourists, and there are regular direct flights to several European destinations.
  • Barcelona-El Prat is another airport that is a bit further from Tossa de Mar, with a distance of 85 km. There are several international flights that operate from here.

Bus Stations Near Tossa de Mar

  • Tossa de Mar Bus Station is located on the outskirts of the town, and it offers several daily connections to Barcelona, Girona, and other towns on the Costa Brava.

Other Options

  • Train - there is no train station in Tossa de Mar, but you can take a train to either Blanes or Girona and then take a bus or taxi to Tossa de Mar.
  • Car rental - several car rental companies operate in the region, and they offer competitive rates.

How to Get Around in Tossa de Mar

The best way to explore Tossa de Mar is on foot, especially in the old town. However, you may need transport to visit some of the beaches and other attractions around the town. Here are some of the options:

  • Bicycle Rental - this is a great way to get around Tossa de Mar and explore the surrounding countryside.
  • Car Hire - several car rental companies operate in Tossa de Mar. You can rent a car and explore the region at your own pace.
  • Taxi - taxis are available in Tossa de Mar, but they can be expensive.

Where to Go Next from Tossa de Mar: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

There are several other towns and attractions around Tossa de Mar that are worth visiting. Here are some suggestions:

  • Lloret de Mar is a town located about 15 km south of Tossa de Mar. It is known for its vibrant nightlife and beautiful beaches.
  • Barcelona - the bustling city of Barcelona is just 80 km from Tossa de Mar. Barcelona is home to many world-famous landmarks, including the Sagrada Familia and Park Güell.
  • Figueres - the town of Figueres is located 70 km to the north of Tossa de Mar. It is the birthplace of Salvador Dali and is home to the Dali Museum.

Conclusion

Tossa de Mar is a hidden gem in Catalonia that offers an authentic Spanish experience. Whether you’re interested in history, beaches, hiking, food, or wine, Tossa de Mar has something to offer. With this comprehensive travel guide, we hope you’ve got all the information you need to plan your trip and enjoy the charms of Tossa de Mar.
