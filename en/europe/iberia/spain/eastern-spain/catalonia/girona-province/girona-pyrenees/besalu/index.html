Introduction to Besalú

Besalú is a picturesque medieval village nestled in the heart of the Girona Pyrenees, in Catalonia, Spain. Known for its rich cultural heritage, Besalú boasts stunning Romanesque architecture, winding cobblestone streets, and a charming small-town vibe. It’s the perfect destination for history buffs, nature enthusiasts, foodies, and anyone looking to escape the hustle and bustle of modern life.

List of Places to Visit in Besalú

No visit to Besalú would be complete without exploring its enchanting sights and landmarks. Here are some of the must-visit places in Besalú.

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  1. Besalú Castle: A stunning 10th-century castle perched atop a hill overlooking the village. Offering breathtaking views of the surrounding countryside, Besalú Castle is a must-visit for history buffs.

  2. Sant Pere Church: A beautiful Romanesque church located in the heart of Besalú. Admire its intricate stonework and impressive bell tower.

  3. Jewish Quarter: Wander through the charming streets of Besalú’s Jewish Quarter, which dates back to the 12th century. See the remains of the Mikveh (Jewish bathhouse), the Synagogue, and the Jewish Quarter Interpretation Center.

Natural Attractions of Besalú: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  1. Plaça Llibertat: A bustling square in the heart of the village, surrounded by cafes and restaurants. A great place to relax and people-watch.

  2. Fluvià River: Take a stroll along the banks of the Fluvià River, which runs through Besalú. Enjoy the peaceful sound of the river and take in the stunning scenery.

  3. Pyrenees Mountains: Besalú is located at the foot of the Pyrenees Mountains, offering plenty of opportunities for outdoor adventurers. Go hiking, mountain biking, or fishing in this breathtaking natural wonder.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Besalú

Besalú offers plenty of opportunities for outdoor enthusiasts to hike and bike in the surrounding countryside. Some of the best routes include the Camí de Ronda de Besalú, which offers stunning views of the Pyrenees Mountains, and the Greenway Carrilet Route, a popular cycling route that spans 135 km through the Girona Pyrenees.

Best Things to Do in Besalú: Activities and Tours

There’s no shortage of fun things to do in Besalú. Here are some of our top recommendations:

  1. Hot Air Balloon Ride: See Besalú from a whole new perspective with a hot air balloon ride over the village and surrounding countryside.

  2. Cooking Class: Learn to cook traditional Catalan cuisine with a local chef. Sample delicious local wines and cheeses as you prepare your own paella and other dishes.

  3. Guided Walking Tour: Take a guided walking tour of the village to learn about its rich history and architecture. See the main sights and landmarks, and get insider tips on the best places to eat and shop.

Week-Long Itinerary for Besalú

Planning a week-long trip to Besalú? Here’s a sample itinerary to help you make the most of your time:

  • Day 1: Arrive in Besalú and explore the town on foot. Visit the Jewish Quarter and Sant Pere Church.
  • Day 2: Take a hot air balloon ride in the morning and go hiking in the Pyrenees in the afternoon.
  • Day 3: Visit the Besalú Castle and enjoy lunch in one of the village’s charming cafes.
  • Day 4: Take a guided walking tour of Besalú. Learn about the village’s history and architecture, and sample some local delicacies.
  • Day 5: Do a cooking class and learn how to make traditional Catalan cuisine.
  • Day 6: Visit the Fluvià River and go fishing or relax by the river.
  • Day 7: Explore nearby villages and towns, such as Olot and Figueres. See the Salvador Dali Museum in Figueres.

Food and Drink Scene of Besalú

Besalú is known for its delicious food and wine, influenced by Catalan and Spanish cuisine. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  1. Pa amb tomàquet: A traditional Catalan-style bread rubbed with garlic and ripe tomatoes, drizzled with olive oil and sprinkled with salt.

  2. Escudella i carn d’olla: A hearty stew made with vegetables, meats, and herbs.

  3. Canelons: Baked pasta filled with meat and topped with béchamel sauce.

Local Beverages

  1. Cava: A sparkling wine made in Catalonia, similar to champagne.

  2. Vermut: A popular aperitif made with white wine, spirits, herbs, and spices.

  3. Garnatxa: A sweet wine made from Grenache grapes, grown in the vineyards around Besalú.

Where to Stay in Besalú: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Besalú offers a range of accommodation options, from budget-friendly hostels to luxurious hotels and guesthouses. Here is a list of some top-rated options:

  1. Els Jardins de la Martana: A beautiful boutique hotel located in the heart of Besalú. Enjoy the stunning views from the rooftop terrace.

  2. Pensió Restaurant L’Estada: A cozy guesthouse with an on-site restaurant serving delicious Catalan cuisine.

  3. Can Fruitós: A charming bed and breakfast set in a restored medieval building in the old town.

What to Buy in Besalú: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Besalú is known for its handcrafted products and local delicacies. Here are some of the best souvenirs to bring home:

  1. Espadrilles: Traditional woven shoes made by local artisans.

  2. Local Cheese: Try the delicious Garrotxa cheese, which is made from goat’s milk in the surrounding countryside.

  3. Local Honey: Sample the sweet and fragrant honey produced by local beekeepers.

Local Culture and Customs of Besalú

Besalú is known for its rich cultural heritage, including its Jewish and Romanesque history. The locals are proud of their traditions and appreciate visitors who respect their customs.

Best Time to Visit Besalú

The best time to visit Besalú is during the spring and autumn months, when the weather is mild and the crowds are smaller. July and August can be crowded and hot, but the village is still charming and worth a visit.

Do’s and Don’ts of Besalú

Here are some dos and don’ts to keep in mind during your visit to Besalú:

Do:

  • Respect local customs and traditions.
  • Visit local businesses and support the local economy.
  • Take in the stunning views of the Pyrenees Mountains.
  • Try the local cuisine and wines.

Don’t:

  • Wander off-trail and disturb the local flora and fauna.
  • Leave trash or litter in public areas.
  • Disrespect the local culture or customs.

Safety and Health

Safety in Besalú

Besalú is a safe and welcoming village, with a low crime rate. However, visitors should still take precautions to protect themselves and their belongings. Be aware of pickpockets in crowded areas, and lock up valuables in your accommodation.

Health in Besalú

Besalú has modern healthcare facilities, but visitors should still have adequate travel insurance in case of emergency. It’s also important to stay hydrated and protect yourself from the sun during hot weather.

Getting to Besalú: Transportation Options and Logistics

Besalú is easily accessible by several modes of transport, including:

  • By Air: The nearest airport is Barcelona-El Prat Airport, which is approximately 120 km away.
  • By Train: The nearest train station is in Girona, which is approximately 25 km away from Besalú.
  • By Bus: There are several bus companies that operate daily routes to and from Besalú.

How to Get Around in Besalú

Besalú is a small village that can easily be explored on foot. However, taxis and rental cars are also available for those who prefer to travel by car.

Where to Go Next from Besalú: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Besalú is located in a region of Spain that offers plenty of nearby destinations and excursions. Some top recommendations include:

  • Olot: A charming town at the foot of the Pyrenees Mountains, known for its volcanic landscapes and beautiful parks.
  • Figueres: The birthplace of Salvador Dali, Figueres is home to the Dali Museum and other art galleries.
  • Costa Brava: The stunning Mediterranean coastline is just a short drive away from Besalú, offering beautiful beaches, coves, and pristine waters.

Conclusion

Besalú is a charming and historic village that offers visitors a glimpse into Catalan culture and heritage. With its stunning architecture, natural beauty, and delicious cuisine, Besalú is the perfect destination for travelers looking for a more authentic and off-the-beaten-path experience in Spain.
