Introduction to Llivia

Nestled in the Girona Pyrenees, Llivia is a charming town with a rich cultural and historical heritage. This small town, located in Eastern Spain, is a unique destination as it is situated entirely inside France. Llivia is a perfect combination of natural beauty, history, and culture, making it an ideal spot for travelers seeking a peaceful getaway that is rich in adventure.

In this guide, we will give you a rundown of the best places to visit, things to do, and local delicacies to try while in Llivia.

List of Places to Visit in Llivia

Llivia is a small town, but it boasts numerous places of historical, cultural, and natural significance. Here is a list of places worth visiting:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Llivia Pharmacy Museum: This historic pharmacy houses a vast collection of antique jars, vials, and other medical tools that date back to the Renaissance era.
  • Church of Mare de Deu dels Angels: With its impressive Romanesque architecture, decorative altarpiece, and remarkable stained-glass windows, this church is a must-visit spot for history enthusiasts.
  • Tower of Bernat de So: Built-in the thirteenth century, this tower served as a defense fortification against pirates and other invasions.

Natural Attractions of Llivia: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • El Castell de Llivia: This castle, situated on top of a hill, offers breathtaking panoramic views of the town and its surroundings.
  • Hiking in the Pyrenees: Llivia is surrounded by stunning natural landscapes of the Pyrenees, making it an ideal place for outdoor enthusiasts who enjoy hiking and trekking.
  • Estany de la Perxa: This beautiful lake, located just outside Llivia, is a perfect retreat to enjoy the peaceful and stunning natural beauty of the region.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Llivia

Llivia is an excellent base camp for hikers and bikers looking to explore the Pyrenees. Here are a few popular routes worth checking out:

  • Puig Pedrós: This summit stands at an elevation of 2901 meters, and it is one of the highest peaks in the Eastern Pyrenees.
  • Tristaina Lakes: This popular and photogenic route takes visitors through three beautiful mountain lakes.
  • The Cerdanya Cycle Tour: This cycling route, spanning over 30 kilometers, takes visitors through picturesque landscapes, charming villages, and historic towns.

Best Things to Do in Llivia: Activities and Tours

  • Hot air balloon ride: Enjoy a spectacular aerial view of the Pyrenees and Llivia with a hot air balloon ride.
  • Horseback riding: Take a horseback ride through mountain trails and lush forests and experience the beauty of Pyrenean nature from a different perspective.
  • Cultural Walking Tour: Take a guided walking tour of Llivia’s historic city center and learn about the town’s fascinating history and traditions.

Food and Drink Scene of Llivia

Must-Try Dishes

  • Trinxat: This hearty dish made from mashed potatoes and cabbage is a staple in Llivia’s cuisine.
  • Escudella i Carn d’Olla: A hearty stew made from beef, pork, and vegetables.
  • Cargols a la Llauna: Llivia’s take on snails, cooked in a garlicky tomato sauce.

Local Beverages

  • Cava del Penedés: A dry sparkling wine that is perfect for celebrations or a romantic dinner.
  • Cerveza Bealó: A local craft beer brewery that offers a wide range of artisanal beers.
  • Vermut del Priorat: A sweet and aromatic vermouth that is enjoyed as an aperitif or before dinner drink.

Where to Stay in Llivia: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Hotel Esquirol: This cozy hotel features rustic-style rooms, a spa, and a restaurant serving local and international cuisine.
  • Apartaments Sol i Muntanya: This family-run apartment complex offers airy and comfortable rooms with stunning balcony views of the Pyrenees.
  • Hostal l’Escon: This charming, value-for-money hostel offers a friendly and welcoming atmosphere and is located in the heart of Llivia.

What to Buy in Llivia: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Trinxat Mixture: Take home a package of Trinxat mixture and cook this authentic Llivia dish when you get back home.
  • Bealó beer: Grab a bottle of local craft beer from the Bealó brewery to enjoy back home.
  • Caganer Figurines: It may sound strange, but these Catalan figurines are a popular souvenir in Llivia and are often included in nativity scenes.

Local Culture and Customs of Llivia

Llivia’s cultural traditions are deeply rooted in the Catalan culture. Festivals, such as the Festa Major, celebrate the town’s heritage with music, dancing, and local cuisine. Additionally, the cultural events that take place in the town, such as classical music concerts in the church and theater performances, give visitors an opportunity to immerse themselves in Llivia’s unique cultural scene.

Best Time to Visit Llivia

The best time to visit Llivia is during the summer months, from June to September, when the weather is mild, and there are plenty of outdoor activities to enjoy. However, visitors can also enjoy winter sports and activities, such as skiing and snowshoeing, during the winter months from December to March.

Do’s and Don’ts of Llivia

Do:

  • Do try the local cuisine, especially the Trinxat, Escudella i Carn d’Olla, and Cargols a la Llauna.
  • Do pack comfortable hiking shoes, sunscreen, and mosquito repellent if you plan to explore the outdoors.
  • Do visit the Llivia Pharmacy Museum and learn about the antique medical tools housed there.

Don’t:

  • Don’t forget to bring your passport, as Llivia is situated entirely within France.
  • Don’t litter and respect the natural beauty of the Pyrenees.
  • Don’t miss out on the local cultural events and festivities.

Safety and Health

Safety in Llivia

Llivia is a safe and welcoming town, but visitors should be careful when hiking and participating in outdoor activities. Visitors should also be mindful of the weather conditions and plan their activities accordingly. In case of emergency, dial 112, the European emergency services number.

Health in Llivia

Llivia has a local medical center and pharmacy that can assist with minor health issues. Visitors with pre-existing conditions should ensure that they have enough medication for their trip and bring appropriate medical documents.

Getting to Llivia: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Llivia

  • Girona-Costa Brava Airport: Located 127 kilometers from Llivia, Girona-Costa Brava Airport offers flights to and from numerous European cities.
  • Barcelona-El Prat Airport: Located 168 kilometers from Llivia, Barcelona-El Prat is the second-largest airport in Spain and has direct flights to numerous international destinations.

Bus Stations Near Llivia

  • Llivia Bus Station: This local bus station connects Llivia to the surrounding areas and other cities in Spain, such as Barcelona.

Other Options

  • Train: The closest train station to Llivia is in Puigcerdà, which is around 8 kilometers away.
  • Car Rental: Renting a car is an excellent option for visitors who would like to explore the surrounding areas at their leisure.

How to Get Around in Llivia

The town is small and easy to navigate on foot, but visitors can also rent bicycles or cars to explore the surrounding area.

Where to Go Next from Llivia: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Puigcerdà: A charming mountain town located just a few kilometers from Llivia.
  • Font-Romeu: A popular ski resort with stunning mountain landscape and river valleys.
  • Nuria Valley: A scenic mountain valley located in the Pyrenees Mountains and accessible via a train journey from nearby Ribes de Freser.
