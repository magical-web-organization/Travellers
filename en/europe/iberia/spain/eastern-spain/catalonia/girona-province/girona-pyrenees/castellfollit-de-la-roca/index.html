Introduction to Castellfollit de la Roca

Located in the Girona Pyrenees, Castellfollit de la Roca is a picturesque village that sits atop a basalt cliff created by volcanic activity millions of years ago. The village is a unique sight that guarantees to impress any traveler. Castellfollit de la Roca is situated in the province of Girona, in Catalonia, Eastern Spain. The village offers numerous outdoor activities, cultural attractions, and a laid-back vibe that makes it a perfect stop for any type of traveler.

List of Places to Visit in Castellfollit de la Roca

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Visitors to Castellfollit de la Roca can enjoy its rich history and culture through its museums and historical attractions:

  1. The Ethnographic Museum is an excellent museum to learn about the customs and traditions of the village and the surrounding area.

  2. La Mare de Deu dels Desemparats Church is a stunning church located in the center of Castellfollit de la Roca. The church is the perfect place to witness the village’s religious heritage and admire its unique architecture.

  3. Puente de Besalu is a bridge that spans the Fluvia River. The bridge is an incredible piece of medieval architecture and used to be an important gateway to the village.

Natural Attractions of Castellfollit de la Roca: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Apart from its cultural attractions, Castellfollit de la Roca offers a variety of options for adventure enthusiasts and nature lovers:

  1. The Garrotxa Volcanic Zone Natural Park is a must-visit for visitors to Castellfollit de la Roca. The park offers a unique landscape of extinct volcanoes and lava flows. The park is ideal for hiking, mountain biking, and horseback riding.

  2. The Fluvià River is the perfect spot for visitors to enjoy a peaceful kayak or canoe trip. The river flows through the breathtaking landscape of the Garrotxa Natural Park.

  3. The Santa Margarida Crater is an extinct volcano located in the Garrotxa Volcanic Zone Natural Park. At its base, visitors can find a stunning medieval chapel surrounded by lush vegetation.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Castellfollit de la Roca

Castellfollit de la Roca and its surrounding area are ideal for hiking and biking enthusiasts. Some of the best routes are:

  1. The Via Verda is an old railway track that’s been transformed into a bike track. The track extends from Olot to Sant Feliu de Guixols, offering breathtaking views of the landscape.

  2. The Ruta de les Fonts is a hiking route that takes visitors through the Garrotxa Volcanic Zone Natural Park, allowing them to observe some of the park’s most breathtaking waterfalls.

  3. Camino de Santiago is a pilgrimage route that passes through Castellfollit de la Roca. Visitors can choose to hike some or all of the route, depending on their preference.

Best Things to Do in Castellfollit de la Roca: Activities and Tours

Besides hiking, biking, and kayaking, Castellfollit de la Roca and its surrounding area offer numerous other activities, including:

  1. Hot air balloon rides offer a unique perspective of the Garrotxa Volcanic Zone Natural Park.

  2. Horseback riding is a great way to explore the countryside around Castellfollit de la Roca.

  3. Guided tours offer the chance to learn more about the village’s history through its landmarks and cultural attractions.

Food and Drink Scene of Castellfollit de la Roca

Castellfollit de la Roca is a great destination for food lovers. Visitors can enjoy local Catalan cuisine and try some of the region’s famous dishes, including:

Must-Try Dishes

  1. Pa amb tomàquet is a classic Catalan dish of bread rubbed with tomato, olive oil, and garlic.

  2. Escalivada is a roasted vegetable dish consisting of eggplant, peppers, and onions.

  3. Calcots are a type of scallion that are grilled and served with romesco sauce.

Local Beverages

  1. Cava is the famous sparkling wine of Catalonia.

  2. Vermut is a popular aperitif served throughout the region.

  3. Gin Tonic is a favorite cocktail among locals, with many variations.

What to Buy in Castellfollit de la Roca: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Local Culture and Customs of Castellfollit de la Roca

Castellfollit de la Roca is influenced by the Catalan culture and tradition. Visitors can witness local customs through its festivals and events. Some of the popular events include:

  1. Fira de l’Avet de Castellfollit is a Christmas fair that takes place in December, where visitors can experience Catalan Christmas traditions.

  2. The Festa Major de Sant Roc is a popular summer festival with live music, parades, and traditional Catalan dances.

Getting to Castellfollit de la Roca: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Castellfollit de la Roca

The nearest airports to Castellfollit de la Roca are Barcelona-El Prat Airport (BNC) and Girona-Costa Brava Airport (GRO). From the airports, visitors can take a shuttle or taxi to the village.

Bus Stations Near Castellfollit de la Roca

The nearest bus station to Castellfollit de la Roca is in the nearby village of Olot. From Olot, visitors can take a local bus or taxi to Castellfollit de la Roca.

Other Options

Visitors can also reach Castellfollit de la Roca by hiring a car or taking a train to the nearby city of Girona and then taking a bus or taxi to the village.

How to Get Around in Castellfollit de la Roca

Castellfollit de la Roca is a small village that can easily be explored on foot. Hiking, biking, and renting a car are other popular ways to get around in the surrounding area.
