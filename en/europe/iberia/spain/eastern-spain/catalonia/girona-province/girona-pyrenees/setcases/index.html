Setcases, located in the heart of the Girona Pyrenees, is a picturesque town in Catalonia, Eastern Spain. A hidden gem amongst Spain’s popular tourist destinations, Setcases offers travelers a unique experience filled with heritage, natural beauty, and adventure. In this comprehensive guide, we explore all the things you need to know about Setcases, including the best places to visit, hike and bike routes, food and drink scene, where to stay, and more.

Introduction to Setcases

Setcases is a small village located in Catalonia’s Girona province, steeped in rich history. The town’s name derives from the Latin word secta, meaning a group of followers who live in the mountains. Setcases is situated in the valley of the River Camprodon, surrounded by lofty mountains that rise to more than 2500 meters in height.

List of Places to Visit in Setcases

When it comes to tourism, Setcases is a hidden gem waiting to be discovered. From culturally significant landmarks to natural wonders, Setcases has something to offer everyone.

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Setcases boasts a wealth of historical and cultural attractions that offer a glimpse into the town’s rich history. Some of the must-visit landmarks include:

  • Parish Church of St. Marti: A beautiful 17th-century church located in the heart of Setcases, known for its stunning baroque architecture.

  • Ethnographic Museum of Valleys: A museum dedicated to showcasing the region’s customs and traditions and is committed to preserving the cultural heritage for generations.

  • Farga Palau: Setcases has a rich history of iron mining and metallurgy, and Farga Palau is a museum that portrays this history through various exhibits, tools, and machines used in the mining process.

  • The Clock Tower: An iconic landmark in Setcases that stands tall at the town’s center, a popular spot to click photographs while soaking in the town’s beauty.

Natural Attractions of Setcases: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

The natural beauty of Setcases is breathtaking, with meandering rivers, verdant valleys, snow-capped mountains, and immense forests making it an ideal destination for outdoor enthusiasts. Some of the town’s top natural attractions include:

  • Ter and Freser Rivers: Two of the most well-known rivers in the region are the Ter and the Freser rivers, where tourists can indulge in trout fishing, kayaking, and rafting.

  • Mount Colomers: A summertime favorite, Mount Colomers has excellent hiking trails with different difficulty levels. It is also popular among bird watchers, who can spot a diverse range of bird species.

  • Els Anglesos Forest: Hiking or biking enthusiasts can venture into this beautiful forest, encompassing 8 kilometers of varied terrain, and at the top, they are greeted with a panoramic view of the town.

  • Orientation Table of Comanegra: Nature enthusiasts can head to this orientation table in the municipality of Camprodon. From here, you can breathe in the beauty of the Pyrenean peaks, like the Taga or the Puigmal.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Setcases

Setcases is an excellent destination for those who love hiking and cycling through breathtaking landscapes. With options for every type of adventurer, here are a few hiking and biking routes that you must try:

  • The Setcases-Coma Oriola trek: A hike spanning over 8 kilometers with stunning mountain views.

  • Ruta del Ferro i Carbó: A hiking and biking adventure that explores the coal and iron mines of Setcases and Campredó.

  • The Rocs de Tretze adventure route: A biking path of 25 kilometers long, it is perfect for advanced riders, providing excellent views of the valleys around the town.

Best Things to Do in Setcases: Activities and Tours

Setcases has several activities to make your stay worth remembering. Here are a few must-try experiences while you’re in Setcases:

  • Whitewater Rafting: Indulge in some adrenaline-pumping adventure with this activity amidst the rushing rapids of the Ter and Freser rivers.

  • Horse Riding: Explore the countryside from a unique perspective on horseback.

  • ATV tours: Take in the beautiful landscapes of the region by booking an ATV tour.

Week-Long Itinerary for Setcases

Setcases can be explored in a short weekend trip or a week-long adventure, offering you a glimpse into the town’s mesmerizing beauty and culture. Here’s a sample itinerary for a week-long trip:

Day 2 - Explore the town’s landmarks and museums

Day 6 - Relax and indulge in the scenic beauty of the town

Day 7 - Departure from Setcases

Food and drink Scene of Setcases

Setcases offers an array of cuisine options that showcase the town’s heritage and culinary style. Below are some must-try local dishes and beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Trinxat: A typical dish from Setcases made from potatoes, cabbage, and bacon, often served with sausage or blood sausage.

  • Escudella I Carn D’olla: A thick soup made from vegetables, potatoes, and meat, a staple dish enjoyed by locals during the cold winter months.

  • Coch: A pastry similar to chicken pie, made with veal or pork instead of chicken, wrapped in puff pastry and baked.

Local Beverages

  • Ratafia: A traditional liqueur made by Catalan families at home. It is a sweet and herbal drink known for its digestive properties.

  • Cava: Catalonia’s signature Champagne-like sparkling wine, perfect for celebrations and toasting.

Where to Stay in Setcases: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Setcases offers several accommodation options for different budgets and preferences, ranging from hotels, B&Bs, hostels, and guesthouses. Some options that come highly recommended:

  • Hotel L’Escaleta: A four-star hotel with a rustic touch that offers visitors a comfortable and cozy stay.

  • Pensión les Roques: One of the more affordable options in Setcases, Pensión les Roques offers visitors comfortable rooms and friendly service.

What to Buy in Setcases: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

When visiting a new place, it’s always nice to bring back a souvenir of your trip. Here are some unique and authentic souvenirs and local products to consider buying in Setcases:

  • Sausages: Setcases offers a wide range of sausages cured meats, such as pork and beef sausages, ham, and bacon, perfect for foodies.

  • Jewels and Crafts: Local craftsmen create intricate pieces and jewelry, and art that is a source of pride for the town.

Local Culture and Customs of Setcases

Setcases has a rich cultural heritage with a unique blend of Catalan, French, and Spanish influences. The town’s customs are very much tied to the people’s mountain way-of-life. Take the time to learn about the town’s traditions and mingle with locals during the town’s festivals like the Festa Major, the Sant Antoni Festa, and the Feria del Caracol.

Best Time to Visit Setcases

The best time to visit Setcases is from March to October, when the weather is pleasant and the town’s beauty is at its peak. The vibrant autumn foliage, the craggy mountain peaks in winter, and spring’s wildflowers make the town look surreal.

Do’s and Don’ts of Setcases

Do:

  • Respect the town’s culture and customs.

  • Try the local cuisine and beverages.

  • Engage in outdoor activities safely.

Don’t:

  • Wander off the marked trails while hiking in the mountains.

  • Litter or harm the natural environment.

  • Be noisy or disturb the town’s silent sanctuary.

Safety and Health

Safety in Setcases

Setcases is generally a safe place, but visitors must take necessary safety measures while indulging in outdoor adventures, like hiking and biking routes. Always let someone know where you are going and when you are supposed to return.

Health in Setcases

Spain has high-quality healthcare institutions with well-equipped facilities and skilled medical professionals. The nearest hospital is located in Camprodon, just 5.3 km away from Setcases.

Getting to Setcases: Transportation Options and Logistics

Setcases is easily accessible through several modes of transportation.

Airports Near Setcases

The nearest international airport to Setcases is the Barcelona–El Prat Airport, which is approximately 140 kilometers away. The airport has several domestic and international flights coming in and out regularly.

Bus Stations Near Setcases

Setcases has a bus stop called “Plaça Montsete”, but the bus frequencies are limited, and the schedules can change depending on the season.

Other Options

Driving is an excellent option to reach Setcases, and the journey itself is stunning and scenic. You can rent a car from Barcelona or Girona airports.

How to Get Around in Setcases

While visiting Setcases, walking could be a great option to get around the town. When you want to venture out further, renting a bike or car is the perfect way to reach outdoor adventure spots.

Where to Go Next from Setcases: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Setcases is in close proximity to many popular destinations in Spain. Some of the recommended destinations for excursions include:

  • Camprodon – A small town that boasts charming architecture and a beautiful natural setting.

  • Vall de Nuria – A quiet valley in the Pyrenees, offering pristine mountain landscapes.

  • Besalú – A medieval village located in the provinces of Girona, known for its ancient architecture.

Conclusion

Setcases is a town that offers visitors an escape from the hustle and bustle of the city. With its breathtaking landscapes, rich cultural heritage, and endless opportunities for adventure, it’s no surprise that Setcases is becoming an increasingly popular destination in Catalonia.
