Introduction to Girona (province)

Nestled in the northeastern region of Spain, Girona (Province) is a charming and picturesque destination that boasts of a rich history, vibrant culture, and natural beauty. Whether you’re an adventure seeker, a history buff, or a food enthusiast, Girona (Province) has something to offer for everyone.

List of Places to Visit in Girona (province)

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Girona (Province) is steeped in history, with its wealth of museums and historical landmarks just waiting to be explored. The following are some of the top cultural attractions:

  • Girona Cathedral
  • Sant Pere de Rodes Monastery
  • Museu d’Art de Girona
  • Jewish Quarter
  • Castle of Púbol

Natural Attractions of Girona (province): Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Girona (Province) is also a playground for outdoor enthusiasts, with its stunning coastline, rugged mountains, and sprawling national parks. Here are some of the top natural attractions:

  • Cap de Creus Natural Park
  • Pyrenees Mountains
  • Costa Brava Beaches
  • Garrotxa Volcanic Natural Park
  • Les Gavarres Natural Park

Hiking/Biking Routes of Girona (province)

Girona (Province) is a paradise for hikers and bikers, with its diverse landscapes and well-maintained trails. Whether you prefer a leisurely stroll or a challenging trek, here are some of the best hiking and biking routes:

  • Via Verda del Carrilet
  • Montseny Natural Park
  • Camino de Ronda
  • GR-11
  • Ruta del Ferro

Best Things to Do in Girona (province): Activities and Tours

Apart from its cultural and natural attractions, Girona (Province) also has a variety of activities and tours to offer. From wine-tasting to hot-air balloon rides, here are some of the best things to do:

Girona (Province) has a rich and diverse culture that is influenced by its Catalan roots. Here are some of the local customs and traditions that you should know:

Best Time to Visit Girona (province)

The best time to visit Girona (Province) is during spring (March-May) or fall (September-November), when the weather is mild and pleasant, and the crowds are fewer.

Safety and Health

Safety in Girona (province)

Girona (Province) is a relatively safe destination, but tourists should take standard precautions like being vigilant and staying aware of their surroundings. The Emergency number in Spain is 112.

Health in Girona (province)

There are no specific health risks associated with Girona (Province), but it is advisable to have travel insurance and take basic precautions like carrying sunscreen and insect repellent.

Getting to Girona (province): Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Girona (province)

The Girona-Costa Brava Airport is the primary airport that serves the province and is located about 12 km from Girona City Centre.

Bus Stations Near Girona (province)

Girona Bus Station is located about 20 minutes from the city centre and has several daily connections to regional and national destinations.

Other Options

The High-Speed Train (AVE) connects Girona with Barcelona and other major cities in Spain.

How to Get Around in Girona (province)

The best way to get around Girona (Province) is by car or public transport, with several bus companies offering regular services to the main destinations.
