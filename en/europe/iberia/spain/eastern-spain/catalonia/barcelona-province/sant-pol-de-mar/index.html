Introduction to Sant Pol de Mar

Nestled in the heart of Catalonia, Sant Pol de Mar is a charming seaside town that boasts a rich history and an authentic Catalan flavor. This small, picturesque town offers a peaceful getaway from the hustle and bustle of big cities, where visitors can experience the beauty of the Mediterranean with its crystal-clear waters and pristine beaches.

Unlike other tourist-heavy towns along the Costa Brava, Sant Pol de Mar remains an undiscovered gem, boasting an authentic Spanish experience with its laid-back atmosphere and friendly locals.

List of Places to Visit in Sant Pol de Mar

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Església de Sant Jaume: This 11th-century church is one of the town’s most important landmarks, boasting stunning Gothic architecture and intricate details that offer a glimpse into Catalonia’s rich history.
  • Museu Rull: Focusing on contemporary art, the Museu Rull is housed in a 19th-century building and showcases the works of local artists.
  • Castell de Sant Pol de Mar: Sitting atop a hill overlooking the town, the Castell de Sant Pol de Mar offers impressive panoramic views of the surrounding landscape.

Natural Attractions of Sant Pol de Mar: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Platja de Sant Pol: This 1-kilometer-long sandy beach is one of the town’s main attractions. Its tranquil waters and pristine sands make it the perfect spot for swimmers and sun-seekers.
  • Montnegre-Corredor Natural Park: This stunning park offers a myriad of hiking and biking trails, taking visitors through some of the region’s most breathtaking landscapes.
  • Mirador del Portixol: This scenic viewing point offers spectacular panoramic views of the Mediterranean Sea against the backdrop of the rugged coastline.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Sant Pol de Mar

  • Ruta de les Fonts: This popular hiking trail is a great introduction to the region’s natural beauty. Covering over 10 kilometers, the path takes you to a number of scenic water sources throughout the Montnegre-Corredor Natural Park.
  • Ruta de Sant Jaume: This trail takes you through the beautiful countryside from Sant Pol de Mar to Calella and offers breathtaking views of the coastline along the way.

Best Things to Do in Sant Pol de Mar: Activities and Tours

  • Kayak excursions: Taking a kayak excursion from Sant Pol de Mar allows visitors to explore the breathtaking coastline up close and personal. With a variety of trips available, ranging from beginner to advanced, there’s an option for everyone.
  • Food and wine tours: A food and wine tour is the perfect way to experience the region’s rich culinary traditions. Learn about Catalonia’s famous cuisine while indulging in local delicacies and sipping on some of the best wines of the region.
  • Beach hopping: With so many pristine beaches in the area, a beach hopping tour is a great way to explore some of the best swimming spots around.

Food and Drink Scene of Sant Pol de Mar

Must-Try Dishes

  • Paella: This traditional Spanish dish is a must-try in Catalonia. Made with saffron-infused rice, vegetables, and a variety of seafood, it’s a flavorful favorite among locals and visitors alike.
  • Calçots: These unique and delicious grilled onions are a regional specialty often served with romesco sauce.
  • Crema Catalana: This creamy dessert is a Catalan version of Crème Brûlée, flavored with cinnamon and lemon zest and topped with burnt sugar for the perfect crunch.

Local Beverages

  • Cava: Catalonia is famous for its sparkling wine, Cava, made from grapes grown in the region.
  • Vermut: A popular aperitif in Spain, Vermut is a fortified wine infused with various herbs and spices, often served with soda and an olive.

Where to Stay in Sant Pol de Mar: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Hotel Gran Sol: A luxury hotel overlooking the Mediterranean Sea, the Hotel Gran Sol offers stylish and upscale accommodations with a relaxing atmosphere.
  • Mas de Torrent Hotel & Spa: This stunning property is set in a restored 18th-century Catalan farmhouse and features a spa and outdoor pool.
  • Hostal Vimar: A cozy and affordable option, Hostal Vimar offers comfortable rooms in a convenient location close to the town center.

What to Buy in Sant Pol de Mar: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Local wine: Take a taste of Catalonia home with you by purchasing a bottle of local wine.
  • Handmade ceramics: Sant Pol de Mar is known for its beautiful handmade ceramics which feature intricate designs and vivid colors.
  • Local honey: Produced from the honeybees that inhabit the region, local honey makes a sweet and natural souvenir.

Local Culture and Customs of Sant Pol de Mar

The people of Sant Pol de Mar are proud of their Catalan heritage and celebrate it with local festivals and traditions throughout the year. Typical events include annual festivals such as the Festa Major in August, where locals come together to enjoy traditional music, fireworks, food, and drink.

Best Time to Visit Sant Pol de Mar

The best time to visit Sant Pol de Mar is from May to October, when the weather is warm and sunny, making it perfect for outdoor activities and enjoying the beach. The peak season is in July and August, when crowds can be larger, and reservations at hotels and restaurants are essential.

Do’s and Don’ts of Sant Pol de Mar

Do:

  • Respect the local culture and customs.
  • Try the local cuisine and wine.
  • Learn some basic Spanish or Catalan phrases to communicate with the locals.

Don’t:

  • Wear offensive clothing when visiting religious sites.
  • Drink excessively and cause a disturbance.

Safety and Health

Safety in Sant Pol de Mar

Sant Pol de Mar is a safe destination with a low crime rate, but travelers should always be aware of their surroundings and take normal precautions, especially at night.

Health in Sant Pol de Mar

The town has several medical centers and pharmacies, and emergency services are available if needed. Travelers should ensure they have comprehensive travel insurance that covers medical emergencies.

Getting to Sant Pol de Mar: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Sant Pol de Mar

The closest airport to Sant Pol de Mar is Barcelona-El Prat Airport, located approximately 55 kilometers south of the town. Girona-Costa Brava Airport is another option, located 40 kilometers north of Sant Pol de Mar.

Bus Stations Near Sant Pol de Mar

There is a bus stop in the town center with connections to other nearby towns and coastal cities.

Other Options

Renting a car is a great option to explore the region independently, and there are several car rental agencies both at the airports and in the city.

How to Get Around in Sant Pol de Mar

Walking or biking is the best way to get around Sant Pol de Mar, as many of the town’s attractions are within easy walking distance. Taxis are also available and can be hailed on the street.

Where to Go Next from Sant Pol de Mar: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Barcelona: The vibrant capital of Catalonia is just a short drive or train ride away and offers endless opportunities for sightseeing, dining, and shopping.
  • Girona: This picturesque medieval city features a beautiful historic center, museums, and stunning architecture.
  • Costa Brava: Scenic coastal towns dot the coast of the Costa Brava, offering swimming, sunbathing, and seafood.

Conclusion

Sant Pol de Mar is a hidden gem on the eastern coast of Spain, offering travelers a chance to relax and enjoy the beauty of the Mediterranean in an authentic Catalan setting. With its rich history, stunning natural landscapes, and delicious culinary traditions, Sant Pol de Mar is a must-see destination for those looking to experience the best of Catalonia.
