Introduction to Malgrat de Mar

Malgrat de Mar is a charming coastal town located in the province of Barcelona, in the Catalonia region, Eastern Spain. With its warm climate and breathtaking beaches, Malgrat de Mar is the perfect destination for those seeking a relaxing and unforgettable vacation.

List of Places to Visit in Malgrat de Mar

There are many attractions to discover in Malgrat de Mar, ranging from culturally significant museums to beautiful natural landscapes. Here is a list of the top places to visit in Malgrat de Mar:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Illa de l’Aire: Located in the heart of Malgrat de Mar, Illa de l’Aire is a small island that has been declared a Site of National Interest. Visitors can explore the ruins of the old monastery that once inhabited the island.
  • Torre del Castell: This historical tower dates back to the 16th century and is considered one of the most important landmarks in Malgrat de Mar. Visitors can climb to the top of the tower for panoramic views of the town and the sea.
  • Parc Francesc Macià: This park is home to a beautiful palace that has been converted into a museum dedicated to the town’s history and culture. Visitors can explore the exhibitions and learn about the town’s past.

Natural Attractions of Malgrat de Mar: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Malgrat Beach: One of the most popular attractions in Malgrat de Mar is its stunning beach. With crystal-clear waters and golden sand, this beach is the perfect place to relax and soak up the sun.
  • Sant Nicolau Hill: This hill offers some of the most beautiful views in Malgrat de Mar. Visitors can hike to the top of the hill to see panoramic views of the entire town and the surrounding landscapes.
  • Malgrat de Mar Botanical Garden: This garden offers a peaceful oasis to escape the hustle and bustle of the town. Visitors can stroll through the gardens and admire the various plants and flowers.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Malgrat de Mar

Malgrat de Mar is surrounded by beautiful natural landscapes that offer endless opportunities for outdoor adventures. Here are some of the best hiking and biking routes in Malgrat de Mar:

  • Riera de la Tordera Natural Path (Cycling): This cycling route connects Malgrat de Mar with nearby towns and follows the Tordera River. Along the way, visitors can see beautiful landscapes, historic architecture, and local wildlife.
  • Sant Nicolau Hill Circuit (Hiking): This hiking route takes visitors to the top of Sant Nicolau Hill and offers breathtaking panoramic views of the town and the sea.

Best Things to Do in Malgrat de Mar: Activities and Tours

There are many exciting activities and tours to explore in Malgrat de Mar. Here are some popular options:

  • Water Sports: Visitors can enjoy water sports like kayaking, paddleboarding, and parasailing on Malgrat Beach.
  • Boat Tours: There are several boat tours offered in Malgrat de Mar, including sunset tours and fishing excursions, that provide a unique perspective of the town and its surroundings.
  • Segway Tours: Explore Malgrat de Mar effortlessly and leisurely with a Segway tour.

Week-Long Itinerary for Malgrat de Mar

For visitors seeking a week-long itinerary in Malgrat de Mar, here is a suggested plan:

Day 1: Explore the town’s historical monuments and landmarks, like Torre del Castell and Illa de l’Aire.

Day 2: Relax on Malgrat Beach and enjoy water sports activities like kayaking or paddleboarding.

Day 3: Take a day trip to the nearby town of Blanes and visit the famous botanical gardens.

Day 4: Hike to the top of Sant Nicolau Hill and enjoy the panoramic views of the town and the sea.

Day 5: Take a boat tour and explore the town’s coastline from a unique perspective.

Day 6: Visit the Parc Francesc Macià and learn about the town’s history and culture.

Day 7: Relax and unwind with a spa day at one of Malgrat de Mar’s luxury resorts.

Food and Drink Scene of Malgrat de Mar

The food and drink scene in Malgrat de Mar is influenced by the Mediterranean cuisine of the region. Visitors can try a variety of delicious dishes and local beverages during their stay. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Paella: This classic Spanish dish is a must-try when visiting Malgrat de Mar. Made with rice, vegetables, and seafood, visitors can find this dish at many local restaurants.
  • Fideuà: Similar to paella, this dish is made with noodles instead of rice and can be found at many local seafood restaurants.
  • Escudella i carn d’olla: This hearty stew is made with meat, vegetables, and beans and is often served during the winter months.

Local Beverages

  • Cava: This sparkling wine is made in the Catalonia region and is the perfect drink to enjoy during a sunset dinner.
  • Sangria: This refreshing wine-based drink is popular throughout Spain and can be found at many local bars and restaurants.
  • Horchata: A traditional Spanish drink made from tiger nuts and is the perfect refreshing drink to enjoy on a hot summer day.

Where to Stay in Malgrat de Mar: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

There are many different types of accommodations in Malgrat de Mar to suit any traveler’s needs and budget. Here are some of the town’s top hotels, hostels, and guesthouses:

  • Aqua Hotel Silhouette & Spa: This luxury hotel offers stunning views of the sea and is the perfect place to relax and unwind.
  • Camping Resort Els Pins: For visitors seeking a camping experience, this resort offers a range of accommodations, from tent pitches to bungalows.
  • Hostal Bonavista: This quaint guesthouse offers comfortable rooms and a delicious breakfast each morning.

What to Buy in Malgrat de Mar: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

For visitors looking to bring back a piece of Malgrat de Mar, there are several unique souvenirs and local products to choose from. Here are some must-buy items:

  • Sea Salt: Malgrat de Mar is known for its high-quality sea salt, harvested from the Mediterranean Sea.
  • Handmade Pottery: Visitors can find handmade pottery and ceramics at several local shops and markets.
  • Local Wine: Visitors can find locally produced wines in nearby vineyards and wineries.

Local Culture and Customs of Malgrat de Mar

As a coastal town heavily influenced by Mediterranean culture, Malgrat de Mar has a unique and laid-back lifestyle. Some of the town’s customs and traditions include:

  • Siesta: During the afternoon hours, many local shops and businesses close to allow for a mid-day nap or rest.
  • Festivals: Malgrat de Mar holds several festivals throughout the year, including the Festival of Sant Joan, featuring fireworks, bonfires, and traditional dances.
  • Cuisine: As mentioned earlier, the town’s food and drink scene is heavily influenced by the Mediterranean culture, utilizing fresh, local ingredients.

Best Time to Visit Malgrat de Mar

The best time to visit Malgrat de Mar is during the months of May to September when the weather is warm and sunny, and the beaches are at their best. However, it is important to note that this is also the town’s high season, so it is advisable to book accommodations and activities in advance.

Do’s and Don’ts of Malgrat de Mar

Where to Go Next from Malgrat de Mar: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

There are several nearby destinations and excursions available from Malgrat de Mar, including:
