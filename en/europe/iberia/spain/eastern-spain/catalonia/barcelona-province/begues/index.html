A Complete Travel Guide to Begues, Spain

Located in the eastern region of Spain, Begues is a charming town situated in the province of Barcelona, in the Catalonia region. Encompassed by the beautiful Montserrat Mountains, Begues is a peaceful retreat to escape the bustling city life. The town boasts of a rich cultural heritage and spectacular natural landscapes, making it an ideal destination for travelers seeking adventure and tranquility.

List of Places to Visit in Begues

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Begues is home to several historical landmarks and museums that offer insight into the town’s fascinating past.

  • Santa Eulalia Church: Built in the Gothic style in the early 17th century, the church of Santa Eulalia is an architectural marvel and a popular tourist destination.

  • Museu de Begues: Located in the old town hall building, the museum showcases the history of Begues and its surrounding regions through various exhibits and artifacts.

  • Castellciurò: A historical castle situated on a hilltop, which offers stunning views of the surrounding landscapes.

Natural Attractions of Begues: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Begues is surrounded by natural beauty and offers ample opportunities for outdoor activities and scenic views.

  • Montserrat Mountains: A short drive away from Begues, the Montserrat Mountains offer breathtaking views, hiking, and rock climbing opportunities.

  • Parc del Garraf: Located in the Garraf Massif, the park offers hiking and cycling trails, stunning viewpoints, and a rich variety of fauna and flora.

  • Can Grau Valley: A beautiful valley within walking distance from the town, Can Grau Valley offers peaceful walking trails and spectacular views.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Begues

Begues is a haven for outdoor enthusiasts, with numerous hiking and biking trails to explore. Here are some of the popular routes:

  • Route of the Castles: A 12km route that starts at the Begues castle and passes through a few other castles in the region.

  • Garraf Natural Park Route: A difficult 9km route that offers stunning views of the Mediterranean sea, beautiful coastlines, and pine trees.

  • Begues-Potries: A 7km cycling route that passes through breathtaking landscapes and ends at the Potries Romanesque bridge.

Best Things to Do in Begues: Activities and Tours

  • Wine Tasting: Explore the region’s wineries and try out the popular Cava wine.

  • Hot Air Balloon Ride: Marvel at the stunning landscape of Begues while floating in a hot air balloon.

  • Horse Riding: Enjoy a peaceful ride on horseback through the beautiful countryside.

Week-Long Itinerary for Begues

Day 1 - Explore the old town of Begues and visit Santa Eulalia Church and Museu de Begues.

Day 2 - Hike or bike through the Parc del Garraf.

Day 3 - Take a day trip to the Montserrat Mountains.

Day 4 - Enjoy a wine tasting tour.

Day 5 - Take a hot air balloon ride and enjoy panoramic views of the region.

Day 6 - Go horse riding through the countryside.

Day 7 - Relax in the Can Grau Valley and take in the beautiful scenery.

Food and Drink Scene of Begues

Begues has a diverse gastronomic scene that offers excellent cuisine inspired by the surrounding regions. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Calçots: A local delicacy made from tender green onions that are grilled and served with a romesco sauce.

  • Fideuà: A seafood dish similar to paella but made with thin, noodle-like pasta.

  • Esqueixada: A refreshing salad made with salted cod, tomatoes, onions, and olives.

Local Beverages

  • Cava: A type of sparkling wine produced in the Catalonia region, similar to Champagne.

  • Vermouth: A fortified wine that is often enjoyed as an aperitif.

  • Ratafia: A herbal liqueur made from green walnuts, cinnamon, and other spices, often served as a digestif.

Where to Stay in Begues: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

There are several accommodation options in Begues to cater to all budgets and styles:

  • Mas de Baix: A charming country house that offers comfortable and spacious rooms surrounded by nature.

  • Hotel El Castell: A historic castle turned into a hotel that offers panoramic views of the surrounding landscape.

  • Casa Rural Can Feliu: A rustic guesthouse that offers peaceful surroundings and home-cooked meals.

What to Buy in Begues: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Ceramics: Begues is known for its high-quality ceramic products, including plates, bowls, and vases.

  • Extra Virgin Olive Oil: The olive oil produced in Begues is considered one of the best in Catalonia.

  • Local Wine: Bring home a bottle of Cava or other local wine to enjoy memories of your trip.

Local Culture and Customs of Begues

Begues celebrates several festivals throughout the year, including the Festa Major in August, which features concerts, dances, traditional processions, and fireworks.

Best Time to Visit Begues

The best time to visit Begues is during the spring and summer months, from April to September, when the weather is mild and perfect for outdoor activities.

Do’s and Don’ts of Begues

Do:

  • Try the local wine and cuisine.

  • Respect the local customs and traditions.

  • Explore the natural attractions and outdoor activities.

Don’t:

  • Ignore the dress code when visiting religious and historical sites.

  • Disrespect the local culture and traditions.

  • Litter in public spaces.

Safety and Health

Safety in Begues

Begues is a safe town, but like any other tourist destination, visitors should take necessary precautions, such as keeping an eye on their belongings and avoiding empty or poorly lit streets at night.

Health in Begues

Spain has a public healthcare system that provides emergency treatment to all tourists; however, it is advisable to get comprehensive travel insurance that covers all your medical expenses.

Getting to Begues: Transportation Options and Logistics

Begues is easily accessible from Barcelona and other nearby towns. Here are some transportation options:

Airports Near Begues

  • Barcelona-El Prat Airport: The nearest major airport, located 20km away from Begues, offers several international and domestic flights.

Bus Stations Near Begues

  • Barcelona Bus Station: Provides easy access to Begues on public buses.

Other Options

  • Car Rental: Rent a car from Barcelona, and enjoy a scenic drive to Begues.

How to Get Around in Begues

The best way to explore Begues is on foot or a bike. The town is small and easy to navigate, and biking is a popular way to explore the countryside.

Where to Go Next from Begues: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Sitges: A coastal town known for its beautiful beaches, historic landmarks, and vibrant nightlife.

  • Barcelona: Spain’s second-largest city, known for its stunning architecture, world-class museums, and great food scene.

Conclusion

Begues is a hidden gem of Spain that offers a perfect blend of natural beauty, rich culture, and adventure. Whether you prefer hiking through the mountains, wine tasting, or exploring historical landmarks, Begues has something for everyone. Follow this guide and plan your itinerary to Begues for an unforgettable Spanish adventure.
