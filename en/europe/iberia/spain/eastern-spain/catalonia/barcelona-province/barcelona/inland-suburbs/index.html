A Comprehensive Guide to Inland Suburbs in Barcelona

If you’re looking for a getaway from the hustle and bustle of Barcelona city, Inland Suburbs is the perfect destination for you. Located in Catalonia, eastern Spain, this picturesque region offers promising natural wonders, rich cultural attractions, and incredible hikes and biking routes.

In this guide, we’ll dive deep into Inland Suburbs’ top places to visit, things to do, where to stay, and more.

List of Places to Visit in Inland Suburbs

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  1. Montserrat Monastery - a stunning Benedictine abbey built in the Montserrat mountain range. The monastery is known for its breathtaking views and the Black Madonna, the patron saint of Catalonia.
  2. Museo de Montserrat - located in the same complex of the Montserrat Monastery, this museum features artwork from highly recognized artists like Picasso and Dalí.
  3. El Papiol Historical Complex - learn about the history of El Papiol with a visit to its castle, palace, and church.

Natural Attractions of Inland Suburbs: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  1. Collserola Natural Park - this sprawling park offers stunning views of the city of Barcelona and lush greenery with numerous hiking trails.
  2. Can Massana Natural Park - known for its perfect landscapes and tranquility, this park features several trails for hiking and mountain biking while admiring wild animals, including the Catalonian goat and Wild boars.
  3. Llobregat Delta Natural Park - a paradise for bird watchers, this beautiful delta region attracts numerous migratory birds every year.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Inland Suburbs

  1. Sant Bartomeu de la Quadra - an easy and scenic hiking trail located just outside the Barcelona city, with the beautiful views of Collserola Natural Park and the castle of Torre Baró.
  2. Ruta dels Tres Monts - a challenging 20-kilometer hike covering Montserrat, Montgrós, and Sant Llorenç del Munt mountains.
  3. Vallvidrera Superior Park - La Rierada Trail - a moderate 5.5-kilometer hike packed with stunning city views and truly enchanting natural landscapes.

Best Things to Do in Inland Suburbs: Activities and Tours

  1. Hot-air Balloon Flight - experience the thrill of floating in a hot-air balloon with jaw-dropping views of Inland Suburbs.
  2. Wine tours in Alella - known for producing top-class wine, Alella offers several high-quality wineries like Alta Alella, which offers stunning views of the Mediterranean.
  3. Paragliding in Montserrat - enjoy the thrill of flying high above the mountain range like never before with a professional guide.

Week-Long Itinerary for Inland Suburbs

Food and Drink Scene of Inland Suburbs

Must-Try Dishes

  1. Calçots - a traditional Catalan dish resembling long green onions with rich and hearty flavor.
  2. Pa amb Tomàquet - a Catalan tomato bread served as a side dish with most meals.
  3. Crema Catalana - an authentic Catalan dessert made with sugar, egg yolk, and lemon zest, similar to Crème Brûlée.

Local Beverages

  1. Cava - a Spanish sparkling wine that delivers excellent balance and refreshing taste.
  2. Wine from Alella - this wine region produces high-quality wine from locally grown grapes.

Where to Stay in Inland Suburbs: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  1. H2 Rubí - a budget-friendly hotel in Rubí with exceptional amenities and service.
  2. Hostal Sant Vicenç - a comfortable and efficient guesthouse in alella offering excellent breakfast.
  3. Gran Hotel La Florida - a luxurious hotel located in the Collserola hills with magnificent views of the city of Barcelona.

What to Buy in Inland Suburbs: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  1. Ceramics from El Papiol - hand made pottery with an enchanting design from the small town of El Papiol.
  2. Montserrat Black Madonna Replica - a religious souvenir featuring the patron saint of Catalonia.
  3. Wine from Alella - bring home a bottle of the authentic and high-quality wine produced in the Inland Suburbs region.

Local Culture and Customs of Inland Suburbs

Inland Suburbs is a region with a rich cultural heritage, mainly influenced by Catalonian traditions and beliefs. One of the notable customs of the region is the traditional Catalan dance, the “Sardana”. Additionally, the region is famous for producing high-quality wine and olive oil that blends into Catalan cuisine.

Best Time to Visit Inland Suburbs

The best time to visit Inland Suburbs is in the spring to early summer from March to June and in the fall from September to November. It is recommended to avoid visiting the region during peak summer when it can get incredibly hot.

Do’s and Don’ts of Inland Suburbs

Do:

  1. Respect the local culture and customs.
  2. Learn a little bit of Catalan and Castilian.
  3. Explore the outdoors and participate in nature activities.

Don’t:

  1. Raise your voice or act aggressively.
  2. Wear unflattering clothing or shorts when visiting the Montserrat Monastery.
  3. Leave your trash on hiking trails.

Safety and Health

Safety in Inland Suburbs

Inland Suburbs is generally safe to travel to, but it is advisable to beware of pickpocketing in touristy areas and use common sense when traveling at night.

Health in Inland Suburbs

The standard of healthcare in Inland Suburbs is excellent, but it is advisable to take out comprehensive travel insurance, including medical evacuation cover.

Getting to Inland Suburbs: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Inland Suburbs

The main airport servicing Inland Suburbs is Barcelona-El Prat Airport (BCN), located approximately 20 kilometers from the city. From the airport, you can catch a taxi, direct bus or train to your destination.

Bus Stations Near Inland Suburbs

There are numerous bus stations and stops in Inland Suburbs, with regular services to nearby destinations and some parts of Spain.

Other Options

You can also get to Inland Suburbs by car. There are a variety of rental options available in the Barcelona city.

How to Get Around in Inland Suburbs

The best way to explore Inland Suburbs is by car, but you can also explore easily by bike or hiking.

Where to Go Next from Inland Suburbs: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  1. Barcelona - the vibrant capital of Catalonia with beautiful architecture, beaches, and rich cultural heritage.
  2. Costa Brava - known for its stunning beaches and crystal clear waters, this Spanish coastline runs along the north-eastern part of Catalonia.
  3. Tarragona - located on the Mediterranean coast, this historical city is known for its ancient ruins and the Roman amphitheater.

Conclusion

Inland Suburbs is a destination worth exploring for adventure seekers, nature enthusiasts, and those interested in Catalan culture and history. From stunning natural wonders to rich cultural attractions, it offers something for everyone. Plan your visit, and you’ll be sure to leave with unforgettable memories.
