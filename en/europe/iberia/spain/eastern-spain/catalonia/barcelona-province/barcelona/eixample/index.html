Introduction to Eixample

Eixample is a district of Barcelona, Catalonia, known for its elegant architecture, excellent restaurants and cafes, museums, galleries, and nightlife. List of Places to Visit in Eixample

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Sagrada Familia - A masterpiece of Antoni Gaudi, this basilica is a must-visit attraction in Eixample. The intricate details of the facade and the stained glass windows will take your breath away.
  • Casa Batllo - Another architectural gem of Gaudi, this building is famous for its animal-inspired facade and swirling balconies.
  • La Pedrera/Casa Mila - This building stands out for its wavy chimney stacks and twisted iron balconies and rooftop terrace.
  • Palau de la Musica Catalana - Designed by the architect Lluis Domenech i Montaner, this music palace is renowned for its ornate facade and spectacular interiors.

Food and Drink Scene of Eixample

Must-Try Dishes

  • Esqueixada: A traditional Catalan salad made with salt cod, tomato, onion, peppers, and olives.
  • Pa amb tomaquet: A toasted bread rubbed with ripe tomato, garlic, olive oil, and sea salt.

Local Beverages

  • Cava: A sparkling white wine produced in Catalonia and is similar to Champagne.
  • Vermut: A red vermouth served on the rocks and is a popular apertif in Spain.

Eixample is generally a safe neighborhood, but it is best to stay alert and keep an eye on your belongings in crowded areas. Avoid walking alone at night, and take taxis or public transportation instead.

Getting to Eixample: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Eixample

The nearest airport to Eixample is Barcelona-El Prat Airport, which is about 15 km from the district. The airport is accessible by public transport, including buses, trains, and taxis.

Bus Stations Near Eixample

The major bus station in Barcelona is Estacio del Nord, located about 2.5 km from Eixample.

Other Options

The district is accessible by train, metro, and taxis from other parts of Barcelona and the surrounding areas.

How to Get Around in Eixample

The best way to get around Eixample is by walking or cycling. Public transport, including the metro and bus, is also an affordable and convenient option. Taxis are readily available but are more expensive compared to public transportation.
