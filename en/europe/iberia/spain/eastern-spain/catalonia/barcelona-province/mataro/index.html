Introduction to Mataró

Mataró is a city located on the Mediterranean coast of Catalonia, in the province of Barcelona, Spain. Known for its rich cultural heritage, natural beauty, and stunning architecture, Mataró attracts visitors from all over the world. This comprehensive travel guide will provide you with all the information you need to plan the perfect trip to Mataró, Spain.

List of Places to Visit in Mataró

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  1. Museu Arxiu de Santa Maria: This museum and archive focus on the history and cultural heritage of Mataró. It houses a collection of documents, photographs, and artefacts dating back to the Roman occupation of the area.

  2. Casa Coll i Regàs: This modernist-style building was designed by famous architect, Josep Puig i Cadafalch, and is considered to be one of the most iconic buildings in Mataró.

  3. Iglesia de Santa Maria: This Gothic-style church dates back to the 16th century and is known for its ornate interior and stunning stained glass windows.

Natural Attractions of Mataró: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  1. Parc de la Serralada Litoral: This natural park is a stunning place to hike and explore on foot. It offers breathtaking views of the Mediterranean Sea and the surrounding landscape.

  2. Beaches of Mataró: Mataró is blessed with a wide range of beaches, including the popular Playa de Poniente and Playa de Sant Simó. These beaches are perfect for sunbathing, water sports, and swimming.

  3. Montseny Natural Park: This protected area, located just outside of Mataró, is a beautiful place to hike and enjoy the natural beauty of Catalonia. It’s home to a variety of flora and fauna and is a popular spot for birdwatching.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Mataró

  1. Ruta del río Mogent: This hiking trail follows the path of the Mogent river and is a great way to explore the natural beauty of Mataró. It’s a moderate-level hike, and it takes about 3 hours to complete.

  2. Ruta de les Fonts: This hiking trail takes you through some of the most beautiful parts of Montseny Natural Park. It’s a challenging hike but offers breathtaking views of the surrounding landscape.

  3. Ruta de les ermites: This hiking trail takes you through several chapels and hermitages that dot the landscape around Mataró. It’s an excellent way to explore the rich cultural heritage of the area.

Best Things to Do in Mataró: Activities and Tours

  1. Visit the local markets: Mataró is known for its bustling street markets, where you can buy everything from fresh produce to handmade crafts.

  2. Take a city tour: A guided city tour is a great way to learn about the history and culture of Mataró.

  3. Explore the local cuisine: Mataró is known for its delicious seafood dishes, and there are plenty of restaurants where you can sample the local cuisine.

Food and Drink Scene of Mataró

Must-Try Dishes

  1. Fideuà: A dish similar to paella but made with noodles instead of rice.

  2. Escudella i carn d’olla: A hearty stew made with meat and vegetables.

  3. Suquet de peix: A fish stew made with potatoes and other vegetables.

Local Beverages

  1. Cava: A sparkling wine that is especially popular in Catalonia.

  2. Vermut: A fortified wine that is usually served as an aperitif.

  3. Ratafia: A local liqueur made with herbs, nuts, and spices.

Where to Stay in Mataró: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  1. Hotel Atenea Port: A luxury hotel located near the beach.

  2. Hostal Romaní: A budget-friendly option located in the heart of the city.

  3. Casa Bougainvillea Mataró: A charming guesthouse with a beautiful garden.

What to Buy in Mataró: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  1. Handmade crafts: Mataró is known for its handcrafted ceramics, textiles, and jewellery.

  2. Local wine and liqueurs: Cava and Ratafia are popular local drinks that make great gifts.

  3. Sweets: Turrón and neules are traditional Catalan sweets that are popular in Mataró.

Local Culture and Customs of Mataró

Mataró has a rich cultural heritage, which is reflected in its architecture, traditions, and local cuisine. The people of Mataró are proud of their Catalan identity, and you can see this in their language, music, and dance.

Best Time to Visit Mataró

The best time to visit Mataró is during the summer months, between June and August, when the weather is warm and sunny. However, if you prefer cooler weather, you can visit Mataró in the spring or autumn, when temperatures are mild.

Do’s and Don’ts of Mataró

Do:

  1. Learn a few Catalan phrases to show respect for the local language and culture.

  2. Try the local cuisine and drinks.

  3. Respect the local customs and traditions.

Don’t:

  1. Assume that everyone speaks Spanish.

  2. Be loud or disruptive in public places.

  3. Wear revealing clothing in conservative areas.

Safety and Health

Safety in Mataró

Mataró is a safe city for tourists, with a low crime rate. However, it’s always a good idea to take basic precautions, such as keeping your valuables safe and being aware of your surroundings.

Health in Mataró

Mataró has a range of healthcare facilities, including hospitals and clinics. If you require medical attention during your stay, you can visit one of these facilities.

Getting to Mataró: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Mataró

The nearest airport to Mataró is Barcelona-El Prat Airport, which is approximately 40 minutes away by car.

Bus Stations Near Mataró

Mataró has its own bus station, which offers connections to other cities and towns in the region.

Other Options

Alternatively, you can take a train from Barcelona, which takes approximately 30 minutes.

How to Get Around in Mataró

Mataró is a relatively compact city, and most of the major attractions can be reached on foot. Alternatively, you can take a bus or taxi to get around.

Where to Go Next from Mataró: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  1. Barcelona: This vibrant city is just a short train ride away from Mataró and is known for its stunning architecture, cultural attractions, and lively nightlife.

  2. Girona: This historic city is home to a beautifully preserved medieval quarter and is located just over an hour away from Mataró by train.

  3. Costa Brava: This stunning coastal region is known for its dramatic cliffs, hidden coves, and crystal-clear waters. It’s just a short drive from Mataró and is an excellent place to explore on a day trip.

Conclusion

Mataró is a beautiful city with a rich cultural heritage, natural beauty, and stunning architecture. Whether you’re interested in history, culture, adventure, or just relaxing on the beach, Mataró has something to offer everyone. With this comprehensive travel guide, you have all the information you need to plan the perfect trip to Mataró, Spain.
