A Comprehensive Guide to Calella, Spain

Located in Catalonia, Calella is a picturesque seaside town that delights visitors with its beautiful sandy beaches, historic landmarks, and delicious local cuisine. Whether you’re looking for a quiet getaway or an action-packed vacation, Calella offers something for everyone. In this comprehensive guide, we’ll share everything you need to know to plan your memorable trip to Calella.

Introduction to Calella

Calella is a charming coastal town nestled between the Mediterranean Sea and the stunning Montnegre-Corredor Natural Park. The town’s history dates back over 2,000 years, making it one of the oldest settlements on the Costa del Maresme. Calella offers visitors a unique mix of cultural heritage and modern amenities, with a range of accommodation options, restaurants, shopping areas, and entertainment venues.

List of Places to Visit in Calella

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Museu-Arxiu Municipal: This museum and archive is a fascinating place to explore the area’s history and culture. The museum houses a large collection of artifacts from Calella’s past, including prehistoric remains, Romanesque art, and local ceramics.

  • Església de Santa Maria i Sant Nicolau: This imposing Gothic church is the town’s most recognizable landmark. Inside, visitors can admire the intricate carvings and stained glass windows while learning about the church’s history.

  • Torre del Veguer: This fortified tower dates back to the 14th century and was originally used as a watchtower to protect against pirates. Today, it’s a popular spot for taking in panoramic views of the town and surrounding coastline.

Natural Attractions of Calella: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Parc Dalmau: Located in the heart of town, this park offers a peaceful respite from the bustling streets. Visitors can relax on the grass, take a stroll through the gardens, or even play a game of petanque.

  • Playa de Calella: This beautiful, sandy beach is the perfect place to spend a day soaking up the sun and listening to the waves. With crystal clear waters and a wide stretch of shoreline, it’s no wonder this beach is one of Calella’s most popular attractions.

  • Montnegre-Corredor Natural Park: For visitors who love the great outdoors, this nearby natural park is a must-visit. There are numerous hiking and biking trails winding through the forests and hills, offering breathtaking views of the Mediterranean Sea.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Calella

  • Camí de Ronda: This coastal trail stretches for over 20 miles and offers panoramic views of the sea and surrounding landscape. It’s an easy trail suitable for all skill levels, and there are numerous spots to stop and take a dip in the sea.

  • El Camino de Santiago: This historic trail passes through Calella and offers visitors the chance to walk the same path as Christian pilgrims have for centuries. It’s a challenging trek, but the stunning views and cultural significance make it well worth the effort.

Best Things to Do in Calella: Activities and Tours

  • Watersports: With the clear waters of the Mediterranean Sea, there’s no shortage of opportunities for watersports in Calella. Visitors can try their hand at kite surfing, kayaking, or stand-up paddleboarding, among other activities.

  • Segway Tour: For a unique way to explore the town, we recommend taking a Segway tour. You’ll zoom through the streets and alleys, seeing the sights while having a blast.

  • Gastronomy Tour: No trip to Calella is complete without tasting the local cuisine. A gastronomy tour will take you to the best restaurants in town, allowing you to sample traditional dishes like fideuà (a seafood paella made with noodles).

Food and Drink Scene of Calella

Must-Try Dishes

  • Fideuà: A local take on paella made with noodles instead of rice, this savory seafood dish is a must-try.

  • Calçots: These grilled onions are a seasonal delicacy typically enjoyed between January and April in Catalonia.

  • Crema Catalana: A delicious dessert made with caramelized sugar, egg yolks, and cream, this Catalan classic is the perfect sweet treat to cap off a meal.

Local Beverages

  • Cava: Catalonia’s answer to champagne, this sparkling wine is made using the same traditional methods and has a distinct regional flavor.

  • Vermut: A popular aperitif in Spain, vermut is a sweet, herb-infused wine that’s typically served over ice with a slice of orange.

  • Café amb llet: A classic Catalan breakfast drink made with espresso and steamed milk.

Where to Stay in Calella: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Calella offers a wide range of accommodation options to fit every budget and travel style. Some of our top recommendations include:

  • Hotel Volga: This modern, beachfront hotel offers stunning views of the sea and easy access to all of Calella’s top attractions.

  • Hostal Univers: This cozy guesthouse is located in the heart of town and offers comfortable rooms at an affordable price.

  • Sant Jordi Boutique Hotel: For those looking for a little luxury, this elegant hotel offers an incredible experience, with chic interiors, top-notch amenities, and impeccable service.

What to Buy in Calella: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Calella is known for its ceramics, artisanal products, and regional food items. Some great souvenirs and gifts to bring home include:

  • Ceramics: From brightly colored plates to hand-painted tiles, Calella has a rich tradition of ceramic craftsmanship that’s worth exploring.

  • Catalan Wine: Pick up a bottle of local cava or wine to take home and enjoy.

  • Olive Oil: Catalonia is famous for its high-quality olive oil, so don’t miss the opportunity to sample and purchase some during your visit.

Local Culture and Customs of Calella

Calella has a rich cultural heritage that’s reflected in its architecture, cuisine, and festivals. Visitors should be prepared to encounter the following customs:

  • Els Castells: A human tower-building competition that takes place during festivals and celebrations. It’s a thrilling sight to see!

  • Barcelona Football Club: Catalonia’s favorite soccer team, FC Barcelona, is followed with religious fervor by the locals.

  • Language: While Spanish is the official language of Spain, Catalan is also widely spoken in Catalonia, including in Calella.

Best Time to Visit Calella

The best time to visit Calella is during the summer months when the weather is hot and sunny, and the town is abuzz with activity. However, for those looking to avoid the crowds, spring and fall can also be good times to visit when the weather is milder.

Do’s and Don’ts of Calella

Do:

  • Try the local cuisine and sample the regional wines and beverages.

  • Take advantage of the town’s wealth of outdoor activities, including hiking, biking, and watersports.

  • Explore the town’s rich history and cultural heritage by visiting its museums, churches, and landmarks.

Don’t:

  • Forget to pack sunscreen and hats, especially if you’re visiting during the summer months.

  • Forget to bring cash, as many small businesses in town only accept cash payments.

  • Leave without trying the local cava, a sparkling wine that’s unique to the region.

Safety and Health

Safety in Calella

Calella is generally a safe town for visitors. However, it’s always a good idea to take basic precautions like keeping an eye on your belongings and not venturing out alone at night.

Health in Calella

Calella has several pharmacies and medical facilities should you need any medical attention. Visitors should also be aware of high temperatures and stay hydrated during the summer months.

Getting to Calella: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Calella

The closest airport to Calella is Barcelona-El Prat, located approximately 45 kilometers south of town. From there, visitors can take a train or bus directly to Calella.

Bus Stations Near Calella

Calella has a bus station located in the town center, offering connections to nearby towns and cities within Catalonia.

Other Options

Visitors can also reach Calella by train from Barcelona or Girona, as the town is located on the popular R1 line.

How to Get Around in Calella

Calella is a small town that’s easily navigable on foot or by bicycle. It’s also well-connected by public transportation, with buses and trains offering easy access to nearby towns and cities.

Where to Go Next from Calella: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Calella is an excellent base for exploring the surrounding Costa del Maresme region. Some nearby destinations and excursions worth considering include:

  • Barcelona: Just an hour’s drive or train ride from Calella, Barcelona is a world-class city that offers endless opportunities for sightseeing, shopping, and dining.

  • Montserrat: This breathtaking monastery located in the Montserrat mountain range is one of the most iconic landmarks in Catalonia. It’s a popular day trip from Barcelona or Calella.

  • Tossa de Mar: This charming coastal town is known for its winding streets and stunning beaches. It’s just a short drive or bus ride from Calella.

Conclusion

Calella is a hidden gem on the Costa del Maresme, offering visitors the chance to explore the rich history, culture, and natural beauty of Catalonia. From the stunning beaches and outdoor adventures to the delicious cuisine and vibrant festivals, Calella has everything you need for an unforgettable vacation.
