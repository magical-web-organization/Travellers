If you’re planning a trip to Eastern Spain, don’t miss out on Alcossebre. This small town, located in the Castellón province of Valencian Community, has become a popular destination among travelers for its breathtaking beaches, historical landmarks, and vibrant local culture. In this travel guide, we’ll explore everything you need to know to plan your trip to Alcossebre, including the best places to visit, food and drink scene, cultural attractions, and travel logistics.

Introduction to Alcossebre

Alcossebre is a picturesque town on the coast of the Mediterranean Sea, surrounded by stunning mountain ranges. This small town has a rich history that dates back to prehistoric times. It was once a Roman colony, and you can still see the remains of ancient buildings throughout town, such as the old watchtower in Cap i Corp.

Alcossebre is a place where beachgoers, history buffs, and nature lovers unite. With its golden sands, crystal-clear waters, and breathtaking views, the town’s beaches are some of the most beautiful in Spain. Plus, with more than 20 hiking and cycling trails running through the mountains, you can explore the natural beauty of this coastal paradise at your own leisure.

List of Places to Visit in Alcossebre

When it comes to sightseeing, Alcossebre has something for everyone. Here’s a list of some of the most popular places to visit in town:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • The Chapel of Santa Lucia: This 18th-century chapel is located in Cap i Corp and boasts stunning views of Alcossebre and the sea.
  • The Museum of Nature: This museum showcases the flora and fauna of the local area, with displays of fossils, insects, and different species of plants.
  • Torre de la Corda: This watchtower was once part of the town’s defense system, and visitors can climb up to the top for panoramic views of the region.
  • Ermita de San Benet: This chapel dates back to the 16th century and houses a beautiful sculpture of the Virgin Mary.

Natural Attractions of Alcossebre: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Playa del Cargador: This beach is one of the most popular in Alcossebre, with its calm waters and fine, golden sand.
  • Serra d’Irta Natural Park: This nature reserve is home to over 600 different species of plants and animals, including wild boars, eagles, and wildcats.
  • Sierra de Irta mountains: With over 20 hiking and cycling trails crisscrossing the park, this is the perfect place to explore the natural beauty of the area.
  • Cala del Moro: This secluded cove boasts crystal-clear waters that are perfect for swimming and snorkeling.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Alcossebre

Alcossebre is an excellent destination for hikers and cyclists, with a wide range of trails that offer stunning views of the Mediterranean Sea and the surrounding mountains. Some of the most popular routes include:

  • Senda del Peñón: This trail offers panoramic views of the coast, with stops at the Torre del Mollet and the Chapel of Santa Lucia.
  • Montemar Natural Park: This park has multiple trails suitable for hikers of all levels, with views of the Mediterranean, Martina’s rock, and the Sierra de Irta mountains.
  • La Talaia: This trail is a must-visit for experienced hikers. It leads to the tallest peak in the area, offering unobstructed views of the region.

Best Things to Do in Alcossebre: Activities and Tours

There is no shortage of things to do in Alcossebre. Here are some of our top picks:

  • Water sports: The clear waters of the Mediterranean make Alcossebre an excellent destination for water sports enthusiasts. Scuba diving, windsurfing, sailing, and kayaking are all popular activities.
  • Horseback riding: For a unique way to explore the countryside, take a horseback riding tour.
  • Boat tours: Catch a glimpse of the Mediterranean Sea’s beauty on a boat tour that stops in secluded coves and offers stunning coastal views.

Food and Drink Scene of Alcossebre

In Alcossebre, you’ll find a fusion of traditional Spanish cuisine and local dishes influenced by the town’s proximity to the sea. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Paella: Originating from the Valencia region, this classic Spanish dish is a must-try when visiting Alcossebre.
  • Calamari: This seafood dish is a local favorite, served fried or grilled.
  • Arroz a banda: This rice dish is cooked with fish stock and served with fresh seafood.

Local Beverages

  • Vino de Bodegas: This locally-produced wine is a must-try for wine lovers, made from grapes grown in the surrounding mountains.
  • Agua de Valencia: This classic cocktail is a mix of cava, orange juice, vodka, and gin.

Where to Stay in Alcossebre: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

There is a wide variety of accommodation options in Alcossebre, catering to a range of budgets. Some of the most popular options include:

  • Hotel Alcossebre: This four-star hotel boasts an outdoor pool and stunning sea views.
  • Apartamentos Carlos V: These apartments offer spacious, modern accommodation with a kitchen and living area.
  • Casa Azahar: This cozy guesthouse is a great option for budget travelers, located just a short walk from the beach.

What to Buy in Alcossebre: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

When in Alcossebre, you should consider buying souvenirs and local products to bring back home. Here are some ideas:

  • Wine produced in Bodegas
  • A hand-woven rug or tapestry made in Valencian Community
  • A traditional Castellón ceramic stove made in Onda

Local Culture and Customs of Alcossebre

Alcossebre has a rich cultural heritage that is still celebrated to this day. On October 7th, the Feast of Our Lady of Alcossebre is celebrated with colorful parades, street performances, and fireworks displays. The locals are proud of their traditions and will be happy to share them with visitors.

Best Time to Visit Alcossebre

The best time to visit Alcossebre is from June to September, when the weather is warm and the beaches are at their best. However, if you’re looking for a quieter, more relaxed experience, consider visiting in the spring or fall when the crowds have thinned out.

Do’s and Don’ts of Alcossebre

When visiting Alcossebre, here are some things to keep in mind:

Do:

  • Respect the local traditions and customs.
  • Wear sun protection - the sun can be very strong on the beaches.
  • Try the local cuisine - there are plenty of delicious dishes to try in Alcossebre.

Don’t:

  • Leave your belongings unattended on the beach.
  • Ignore local signs and warnings on the hiking trails.
  • Use loud music or speak loudly on the beach, especially at night.

Safety and Health

Safety in Alcossebre

Alcossebre is a safe destination for travelers, but you should take some precautions to avoid any accidents or mishaps.

  • Keep an eye on your belongings when visiting crowded areas like the beach.
  • Observe local signs on hiking trails and avoid going off-trail.
  • Only swim in designated areas of the beach and always stay within your ability level when engaging in water sports.

Health in Alcossebre

There are no major health risks associated with visiting Alcossebre, but travelers should take note of the following:

  • Stay hydrated and avoid direct sun exposure during peak hours.
  • Be aware that some hiking trails might be challenging - take breaks when needed.
  • Check with your doctor or local travel clinic about any necessary vaccinations before your trip.

Getting to Alcossebre: Transportation Options and Logistics

Alcossebre is easily accessible by car or public transportation. Here are some options for getting there:

Airports Near Alcossebre

The nearest airports to Alcossebre are:

  • Valencia Airport: located 130 km northwest of Alcossebre.
  • Castellón Airport: located approximately 25 km from Alcossebre.

Bus Stations Near Alcossebre

The nearest bus stations to Alcossebre are:

  • Vinaròs Bus Station: located approximately 10 km from Alcossebre.
  • Castellón Bus Station: located approximately 43 km from Alcossebre.

Other Options

Taxis and rental cars are also available for transportation to and from Alcossebre.

How to Get Around in Alcossebre

Alcossebre is a small town that can easily be explored on foot. However, if you plan on doing any hiking, cycling, or exploring the surrounding areas, renting a car is recommended.

Where to Go Next from Alcossebre: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Alcossebre is located in a beautiful part of Spain, with many other destinations nearby that are worth visiting. Some of the most popular options include:

  • Valencia: This vibrant city is located just a two-hour drive from Alcossebre and is known for its fantastic food, art, and culture.
  • Peñíscola: This beautiful coastal town is known for its picturesque old town, castle, and stunning beaches.

Conclusion

Alcossebre is a stunning destination that has something to offer travelers of all interests. Whether you’re looking for adventure, relaxation, or cultural experiences, you’ll find it all here. Follow this guide to plan your trip and create unforgettable memories in Eastern Spain.
