Introduction to Segorbe

Located in the province of Castellón in the Valencian Community of Eastern Spain, Segorbe is a charming town nestled in the foothills of the Sierra Calderona mountains. With a rich cultural heritage, natural beauty, and outdoor adventures, Segorbe is an ideal destination for travelers looking to experience the authentic side of Spain.

List of Places to Visit in Segorbe

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums, and Historical Places

  • Cathedral-Basilica of Segorbe: The focal point of the town’s historic district, this Gothic-style cathedral dates back to the 14th century. Be sure to check out its stunning nave, sacristy, and cloister.
  • Acueducto de Segorbe: A symbol of Segorbe’s Moorish past, the aqueduct dates back to the 12th century and is the town’s most significant architectural landmark.
  • Torre del Botxí: Located in the center of Segorbe, the Torre del Botxí is a well-preserved medieval tower that offers impressive views of the surrounding countryside.

Natural Attractions of Segorbe: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Sierra Calderona Natural Park: A short drive from Segorbe, this natural park offers hiking, mountain biking, and panoramic views of the Mediterranean Sea.
  • Alto Palancia Greenway: This converted railway line spans 40 kilometers between Segorbe and Barracas, and offers a leisurely route for cycling or hiking enthusiasts.
  • Fuente de los Baños: A natural hot spring located on the outskirts of Segorbe, offering visitors a relaxing and rejuvenating experience.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Segorbe

Segorbe and its surroundings are a nature lover’s paradise, offering numerous hiking and biking trails that take you through canyons, valleys, and picturesque towns. Some of the best trails include:

  • PR-CV 414: This hiking trail takes you through the Sierra Calderona Natural Park and offers stunning views of the mountain range.
  • Via Augusta Trail: A historical route that extends from Cadiz to Barcelona, the Segorbe stretch of the trail takes you through beautiful orchards and olive groves.
  • Vía Verde de Ojos Negros: A 160-kilometer bike path that runs from Teruel to Sagunto, this trail passes through picturesque villages and is flanked by stunning scenery.

Best Things to Do in Segorbe: Activities and Tours

  • Guided Tour of Segorbe: Discover the town’s rich history and culture on a guided tour, which can be arranged at the tourist office.
  • Paella Cooking Class: Learn the art of making the quintessential Spanish dish - paella, from a local chef.
  • Wine Tasting Tour: Visit local vineyards and bodegas to learn about the winemaking process and sample some of the best wines of the region.

Week-Long Itinerary for Segorbe

Day 1: Arrival in Segorbe, Explore the historic old town, and visit the Cathedral-Basilica.

Day 2: Hike in the Sierra Calderona Natural Park and visit the Fuente de los Baños hot spring.

Day 3: Cycle along the Alto Palancia Greenway and visit the charming village of Navajas.

Day 4: Take a day trip to Valencia, the third-largest city in Spain.

Day 5: Discover the stunning Albufera Natural Park, where the famous paella originated.

Day 6: Taste the local cuisine and wines by participating in a food and wine tour.

Day 7: Bid farewell to Segorbe and visit the neighboring towns of Sagunto and Castellón.

Food and Drink Scene of Segorbe

Must-Try Dishes

  • Paella: A traditional rice dish that originated in Valencia but is popular throughout Spain, try this dish with the local rabbit and snails.
  • Gazpacho: A refreshing cold soup made with tomatoes, cucumbers, peppers, and garlic that is perfect for hot summer days.
  • Fartons: Sweet, pastry-like breadsticks that are an excellent accompaniment to horchata, the local non-alcoholic beverage.

Local Beverages

  • Horchata: A refreshing, sweet, and milky drink made from tiger-nut that is popular in Valencia and the surrounding regions.
  • Wine: The region around Segorbe has an excellent tradition of winemaking, with local varieties such as Bobal, and Moscatel.

Where to Stay in Segorbe: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Hotel Spa Martín el Humano: A luxurious spa hotel with a central location and great amenities.
  • La Casa del Mercat: A cozy guesthouse in the heart of the old town with traditional decor and modern amenities.
  • Cueva Santa Hotel: Located in a natural cave in the Sierra Calderona mountains, this hotel offers a unique and memorable stay experience.

What to Buy in Segorbe: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Ceramics: The region around Segorbe has a rich tradition of pottery, where artisans make unique and colorful pieces.
  • Olive Oil: The nearby Millenary Olive Tree Grove in Segorbe produces some of the best olive oil in Spain.
  • Honey: The local honey is famous for its quality and is often used in traditional dishes and desserts.

Local Culture and Customs of Segorbe

Segorbe has a rich cultural heritage with Moorish, Roman, and Christian influences that are evident in its art, architecture, and traditions. The town celebrates several festivals throughout the year, with the most famous being the “Entrada de Toros y Caballos” held in September.

Best Time to Visit Segorbe

The best time to visit Segorbe is in spring or fall, when the weather is mild, and the countryside is green and vibrant. During the summer months of July and August, the town can be crowded, and temperatures can be scorching.

Do’s and Don’ts of Segorbe

Do:

  • Respect local customs and traditions.
  • Try the local cuisine and wines.
  • Take care of the natural environment when hiking or biking.

Don’t:

  • Pack valuable or precious items when traveling.
  • Ignore safety precautions when hiking or biking.
  • Litter or disturb the natural environment.

Safety and Health

Safety in Segorbe

Segorbe is a relatively safe town, with low crime rates, but it is always advisable to take the usual precautions when traveling.

Health in Segorbe

In case of a severe medical emergency, call 112 or visit the nearest hospital or medical center.

Getting to Segorbe: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Segorbe

The closest airports to Segorbe are Valencia Airport, which is 60 kilometers away, and Castellón-Costa Azahar Airport, which is around 40 kilometers away.

Bus Stations Near Segorbe

Segorbe has a bus station that connects it to other towns in the region, including Valencia and Castellón.

Other Options

The most convenient way to explore Segorbe and its surroundings is by renting a car or hiring a private car service.

How to Get Around in Segorbe

Segorbe is a small town, and its historic center is best explored on foot. The surrounding countryside and attractions can be reached by car or bike.

Where to Go Next from Segorbe: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Valencia: A vibrant and historic city known for its impressive architecture, cultural attractions, and delicious gastronomy.
  • Sagunto: A historical town with a rich cultural heritage, including a Roman theater and a medieval castle.
  • Peniscola: A picturesque coastal town famous for its sandy beaches, historic castle, and narrow streets lined with whitewashed houses.

Conclusion

Segorbe offers travelers an authentic and unforgettable experience in Eastern Spain, with its rich history, natural beauty, and outdoor adventures. From hiking and biking trails to tastings of local wines and cuisine, Segorbe is a hidden gem waiting to be discovered.
