If you’re planning a trip to Eastern Spain, you won’t want to miss out on Morella. This charming town, located in the province of Castellón in the Valencian Community, offers visitors an unforgettable experience blending history, nature, local cuisine, and adventure. In this comprehensive travel guide to Morella, we’ll share with you everything you need to know to make the most of your trip.

Introduction to Morella

Morella is located on a high hill, which makes it both a historical fortress town and lookout, as well as providing it with stunning panoramic views of the surrounding countryside. It’s located in the Bajo Maestrazgo comarca, bordering Aragon and Catalonia, which offers an interesting mix of cultures and landscapes.

List of Places to Visit in Morella

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Morella has a rich cultural heritage, and there are many museums and historical places to explore. For example, here are some recommended attractions:

  • Morella Castle: An ancient fortification that has stood since the 13th century, Morella Castle is an important symbol of the town’s history and is open for public visits.
  • Archpriestal Church of Santa Maria: A Gothic church on the main square of Morella, this church is over 700 years old and known for its beautiful stained glass and religious art.
  • San Francisco Convent: This convent was founded in 1423, and today, the building houses the Municipal Museum.
  • Casa de la Villa: A historic municipal building that showcases the architecture of the town, this building is now home to the Tourism Office.

Natural Attractions of Morella: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Morella offers visitors a chance to explore some of Spain’s most stunning natural scenery, including:

  • Morella Walls and Gates: Walking around the walled town of Morella is one of the most popular activities. The gates are original from medieval times, and the walls provide lovely views over the town and the surrounding hills.
  • Ports de Morella National Park: The park is a perfect blend of breathtaking scenery of mountains, wildflower-covered meadows, and lush forests. Hiking trails and picnic areas are available to visitors.
  • Embalse de la Roca: Ideal for nature lovers, this reservoir is surrounded by mountains and offers numerous scenic viewpoints and picnic areas.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Morella

One of the best ways to explore Morella and its natural surroundings is to go hiking or cycling. There are several routes that cater to different skill levels:

  • Camino del Cid: A long-distance hiking route that connects Burgos with Valencia and passes through Morella.
  • GR-7: This long-distance trail passes through Castellón’s countryside, eventually reaching Andalusia’s Alpujarra beholding splendid scenery.
  • Greenway of Val de Zafan: A cycling way along the old Val de Zafan railway line and connecting Tortosa and Alcañiz.

Best Things to Do in Morella: Activities and Tours

Aside from history, nature, and scenic hiking routes, there are plenty of activities and tours for visitors to enjoy during their stay in Morella. Here are some recommended things to do:

  • Wine Tasting Tour: Morella is located on the outskirts of the Terres dels Ports wine region, so a trip to a local winery or vineyard is a must.
  • Adventure Sports: The Ports de Morella National Park offers many opportunities for outdoor adventure activities, such as rock climbing, canyoning, and paragliding.
  • Cultural Events: Morella has several cultural festivals throughout the year, including the Medieval Fair in April and the International Music Festival in August.

Week-Long Itinerary for Morella

Visitors looking to spend a week in Morella should plan a trip that includes a variety of experiences and activities. Here’s a suggested itinerary for a 7-day stay:

  • Day 1: Explore Morella Castle and the Archpriestal Church of Santa Maria.
  • Day 2: Visit the San Francisco Convent and Municipal Museum.
  • Day 3: Hike in the Ports de Morella National Park and have a picnic at Embalse de la Roca.
  • Day 4: Go on a wine tasting tour in the Terres dels Ports wine region.
  • Day 5: Experience adventure sports in the Ports de Morella National Park.
  • Day 6: Join a cultural event such as the Medieval Fair or International Music Festival.
  • Day 7: Relax and explore the town at your leisure, including trying local food and purchasing souvenirs.

Food and Drink Scene of Morella

When it comes to cuisine, Morella has plenty to offer. From traditional foods to modern dishes and desserts, visitors will enjoy exploring the local food and drink scene.

Must-Try Dishes

Some typical dishes to try in Morella include:

  • Olleta: A hearty stew made with potatoes, beans, sausage, and pork.
  • Arròs d’arròs: A rice dish made with rabbit, snails, and vegetables.
  • Coca de recapte: A flatbread with roasted peppers, onions, and other vegetables.

Local Beverages

Morella is part of the Terres dels Ports wine region, so visitors can try a variety of local wines. The most famous wines of the region are the red wines of Venta d’Aubert, Mas de la Palaciosa, and La Fou. Visitors looking for something non-alcoholic can try a regional specialty of orxata, a sweet and refreshing drink prepared from the root of tiger nut.

Where to Stay in Morella: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Morella offers a variety of accommodations in various price ranges. Here are some options to consider:

  • Palau dels Osset o de la Diputació: This is the most upscale and comfortable of the options and located in a historic palace
  • Hotel Cardenal Ram: This is a cozy budget option in a central location.
  • Casa Rural Ah!Mas de Xetà: This is a popular guesthouse surrounded by nature.

What to Buy in Morella: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Visitors looking to bring home a souvenir can consider buying the following local products:

  • Local Wines: As part of the Terres dels Ports wine region, visitors can buy a bottle of local wine or a wine tour voucher.
  • Olive Oil: The local olives and olive oils are always a good choice. You can try some in the local stores, or even better, visit an olive grove.
  • Sweet pastries: Specialties like pastissets (small pastries made with sweet potato or courgette filling) and mantecadas (pastries made with lard and aniseed).
  • Local Crafts: One can browse the shops and peek in the potteries and weaver’s shops of artisans reviving medieval crafting techniques.

Local Culture and Customs of Morella

The Valencian Community is known for its rich cultural heritage, and Morella is no exception. Some cultural aspects of Morella include:

  • Dances of the Entrance Closures: Every year on August 15th, the residents of Morella participate in traditional dances that celebrate the closing of the town gates.
  • Moors and Christians festival: A commemoration of the 13th-century Battle of Morella, Moors and Christians festival is a lively celebration of the town’s historical importance.
  • Febre: A local custom, where each family from Morella introduces their new family members to other residents of the town.

Best Time to Visit Morella

The best time to visit Morella is from spring to autumn (April to October) when the weather is pleasant, and there are many outdoor festivals and activities. August is the peak season, especially during the International Music Festival.

Do’s and Don’ts of Morella

Do:

  • Wear comfortable walking shoes and dress in layers, as Morella is a hilltop town with variable weather throughout the day.
  • Respect the local customs and heritage of Morella.
  • Try the local cuisine and wine.

Don’t:

  • Litter or damage any of the historical sites or natural areas.
  • Make noise during the night. Morella is a quiet town, and the locals enjoy their rest.
  • Disrespect any religious or cultural celebrations.

Safety and Health

Safety in Morella

Overall, Morella is a safe town, but travelers should be aware of pickpockets and practice common sense safety. Visitors are advised to lock their valuables, use the hotel safe deposit box, and keep an eye on their personal belongings while in crowded areas or public transportation.

Health in Morella

Visitors should be aware of the potential risk of sunburn and dehydration during the summer months, especially those visitors from colder regions. And if anyone experiences health issues, there is a Health Center located at Carretera de la Portellada, 166, Morella, Castellón.

Getting to Morella: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Morella

The closest airport to Morella is Castellón-Costa Azahar Airport (CDT), which handles flights to and from different destinations in Europe. One can take a train or taxi to arrive in Morella.

Bus Stations Near Morella

There is a bus station in Morella that connects the town with the surrounding cities.

Other Options

Visitors can rent a car or hire a taxi service to get to Morella. It’s also possible to take a train from Valencia or Barcelona to Vinaros and then continue by bus to Morella.

How to Get Around in Morella

Most visitors prefer to explore the town on foot, as it’s relatively small and easy to navigate. For those traveling with kids or experiencing health issues, there are taxis available for hire in the town.

Where to Go Next from Morella: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Visitors can take a day trip from Morella to nearby towns and destinations, such as:

  • Peñíscola: A seaside fortress town of Catalonia boasting beautiful beaches and charming houses.
  • Valdelinares: A skiing resort located near Morella, and one can enjoy snow sports in the winter.
  • Ports de Tortosa-Beseit Natural Park: A natural park in the neighboring province of Tarragona.

Conclusion

Morella has everything that a passionate traveler could want, be it historic forts, natural beauty, delicious food and wine, and plenty of outdoor activities. As a town with cultural heritage and customs, Morella offers visitors a unique and unforgettable experience that visitors should not miss. No matter what your interests are, this town will give you enough reasons to come back again.
