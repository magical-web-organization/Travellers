A Comprehensive Guide to Villarreal: Discovering the Best of Eastern Spain

Welcome to Villarreal! This charming town in Eastern Spain is an underrated gem waiting to be explored. With its rich culture, natural beauty, and delicious food scene, there’s something for everyone here. In this comprehensive guide, we’ll take you through the best places to visit, things to do, where to stay, and more. Let’s get started!

List of Places to Visit in Villarreal

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Villarreal has a rich history and culture that’s reflected in its museums and historical landmarks. Here are some must-visit attractions:

  • La Vila Museum: This museum is housed in a restored 16th-century building and showcases the history, culture, and traditions of Villarreal.
  • Santuario de la Virgen de Gracia: This iconic church is a popular pilgrimage site and boasts beautiful Gothic and Baroque architecture.
  • Iglesia de San Pascual Bailón: Another stunning church to visit in Villarreal, with a striking bell tower, ornate altar, and beautiful paintings.
  • El Pósito: This historic building dates back to the 18th century and served as a grain storehouse. Now a cultural center, it hosts exhibitions and events.

Natural Attractions of Villarreal: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

If you’re a nature lover, Villarreal won’t disappoint. Here are some of the top natural attractions to explore:

  • Paraje Natural Municipal del Termet: This natural park is located just outside Villarreal and offers scenic walks and picnic areas.
  • El Camino del Agua: This picturesque route follows the path of an ancient aqueduct and offers stunning views of the countryside.
  • Real de la Plana: This scenic viewpoint offers panoramic views of Villarreal and the surrounding area.
  • Senda de los Íberos: This hiking trail takes you through the Sierra de Espadán mountains and offers breathtaking views and interesting historical sites.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Villarreal

Villarreal and its surrounding areas offer some fantastic opportunities for hiking and biking. Here are some top routes to try:

  • Via Verde Ojos Negros: This 65 km route takes you through beautiful countryside and former mining towns, and is ideal for both hiking and biking.
  • Ruta de los Molinos: This 12 km route is a great option for those looking for an easy hike or bike ride. It takes you through picturesque countryside and past old windmills.
  • Sierra de Espadán Natural Park: This park offers a range of hiking trails, from easy walks to more challenging routes. Don’t miss the stunning views from the summit of Montí.

Best Things to Do in Villarreal: Activities and Tours

There’s no shortage of things to do in Villarreal. Here are some top activities and tours to consider:

  • Wine Tasting Tour: Visit local vineyards and sample their delicious wines.
  • Cooking Class: Learn how to make authentic Spanish dishes and enjoy the fruits of your labor.
  • Flamenco Performance: Experience the passion and artistry of flamenco music and dance.
  • Bike Tour: Explore Villarreal and its surroundings on two wheels.

Week-Long Itinerary for Villarreal

If you’re planning to spend a week in Villarreal, here’s a suggested itinerary:

  • Day 1: Visit La Vila Museum and Santuario de la Virgen de Gracia
  • Day 2: Explore the Sierra de Espadán Natural Park
  • Day 3: Take a wine tasting tour
  • Day 4: Visit El Pósito and enjoy a cooking class
  • Day 5: Get active with a bike tour or hiking excursion
  • Day 6: Visit the Iglesia de San Pascual Bailón and take a flamenco performance
  • Day 7: Relax and soak up the atmosphere of Villarreal’s charming town center.

Food and Drink Scene of Villarreal

No trip to Villarreal is complete without sampling some of the delicious local cuisine. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Paella: This iconic Spanish dish is a must-try in Villarreal. Look out for local variations featuring fresh seafood and vegetables.
  • Arroz a banda: Another rice-based dish, this one is cooked in fish stock and served with a side of garlic and parsley sauce.
  • Fideuà: Similar to paella, but with noodles instead of rice.
  • Olla de la Plana: A hearty stew made with pork, chickpeas, and seasonal vegetables.

Local Beverages

  • Agua de Valencia: A refreshing cocktail made with orange juice, cava, vodka, and gin.
  • Clara: A beer mixed with lemonade.
  • Mistela: A sweet liqueur made from muscatel grapes.

Where to Stay in Villarreal: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Villarreal offers a range of accommodation options to suit all budgets and preferences. Here are some of the best:

  • Hotel Jendri: This stylish hotel is located in the heart of Villarreal and offers comfortable rooms and a rooftop terrace.
  • Hotel Villareal Palace: This elegant hotel is housed in a historic building and features a spa, pool, and restaurant.
  • Hostal Restaurante La Masía: A budget-friendly option located just outside Villarreal, offering simple rooms and traditional Spanish cuisine.

What to Buy in Villarreal: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

If you’re looking for unique souvenirs and gifts to bring home from Villarreal, here are some ideas:

  • Ceramics: Villarreal is known for its traditional ceramics, featuring colorful and intricate designs.
  • Wine: Stock up on the delicious local wines to enjoy when you’re back home.
  • Olive Oil: Spanish olive oil is some of the best in the world, and Villarreal has many local producers.
  • Handmade Soap: Made with natural ingredients and featuring local scents like orange blossom and lavender.

Local Culture and Customs of Villarreal

Villarreal has a rich cultural heritage that’s celebrated throughout the year with festivals, parades, and religious celebrations. The locals are friendly and welcoming, and visitors are encouraged to participate in the town’s traditions. Don’t miss the “Moros y Cristianos” festival, which takes place in August and features colorful parades and fireworks.

Best Time to Visit Villarreal

The best time to visit Villarreal is between March and October when the weather is warm and sunny. However, if you’re interested in participating in the town’s cultural celebrations, plan your visit around one of the festivals.

Do’s and Don’ts of Villarreal

When visiting Villarreal, it’s important to be respectful of the local culture and customs. Here are some do’s and don’ts to keep in mind:

Do:

  • Try to learn some Spanish phrases and words before you arrive.
  • Be polite and friendly to the locals.
  • Follow the local etiquette in restaurants and other public places.

Don’t:

  • Dress inappropriately when visiting churches or other religious sites.
  • Make loud and excessive noise after midnight.
  • Take photos of people without their permission.

Safety and Health

Villarreal is a safe place to visit, but as with any destination, it’s important to take the usual precautions. Here are some tips:

Safety in Villarreal

  • Be mindful of pickpockets in crowded areas.
  • Be aware of your surroundings when walking alone at night.
  • Use licensed taxi services.

Health in Villarreal

  • Check that your vaccinations are up to date before arriving.
  • Use sunscreen and stay hydrated when spending time outdoors.
  • In case of a medical emergency, call 112.

Getting to Villarreal: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Villarreal

The nearest airport to Villarreal is Valencia Airport, which is approximately a 50-minute drive away. You can also fly into Barcelona Airport, which is about a 2.5-hour drive away.

Bus Stations Near Villarreal

The main bus station in Villarreal is located on Calle del Llocnou. You can take buses to and from nearby cities and towns, as well as longer-distance buses to other parts of Spain.

Other Options

If you’re driving, Villarreal is easily accessible from the A-7 and AP-7 highways. You can also take the train to Castellón or Vila-real stations and then take a bus or taxi to Villarreal.

How to Get Around in Villarreal

Villarreal is a small town that’s easy to explore on foot. However, if you need to travel further or have mobility issues, there are taxis and buses available.

Where to Go Next from Villarreal: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

If you’re looking to explore more of Eastern Spain, here are some nearby destinations and excursions to consider:

  • Valencia: This vibrant city is home to stunning architecture, museums, and a buzzing cafe culture.
  • Peniscola: This historic town boasts a stunning castle and beautiful beaches.
  • Sierra de Espadán: This beautiful mountain range offers more opportunities for hiking and exploring.
  • Alcossebre: A nearby beach town with beautiful coves and crystal-clear waters.

Conclusion

Villarreal may be an underrated gem, but we think it’s one of the most charming and beautiful towns in Eastern Spain. Between its rich culture, natural beauty, delicious food, and friendly locals, what’s not to love? We hope you’ve enjoyed our comprehensive guide and that it inspires you to plan your next trip to Villarreal soon.
