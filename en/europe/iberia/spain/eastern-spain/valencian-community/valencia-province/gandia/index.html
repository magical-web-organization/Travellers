Introduction to Gandía

Nestled in the Valencia province in the Valencian Community, Gandía is a fascinating destination for travelers seeking a blend of culture, outdoor activities, and relaxation. Located in Eastern Spain, this coastal town is rich in history and natural beauty, offering visitors plenty of things to see and do.

List of Places to Visit in Gandía

Gandía offers a wide range of tourist attractions that cater to all types of visitors. Whether you are interested in history, culture, or nature, there is something for everyone.

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Gandía Palace: This impressive palace dates back to the 14th century and served as the residence of the Borgia family. It now serves as a museum, showcasing the town’s historical and cultural heritage.

  • Church of Santa Maria: Located in the heart of the old town, this church is a beautiful example of Gothic architecture and is worth visiting for its intricate ornamentation.

  • Museu Faller: If you’re visiting during the festival season, this museum is a must-visit. Exhibiting the history and craftsmanship of Fallas, a traditional celebration with papier-mâché sculptures and fireworks.

Natural Attractions of Gandía: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Gandía Beach: This blue flag beach is the main attraction of the town and for a good reason. With its golden sands, crystal-clear water, and various activities available, it is the perfect place to relax and unwind.

  • Montdúver Natural Park: A short drive from the town center, this park offers an abundance of hiking and biking trails that crisscross through Mediterranean woodlands.

  • Alqueria del Duc: A centuries-old country house and winery. Here you can wander its gardens and see the bodega, learn about the wine-making process, and sample some of the local wines.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Gandía

Gandía offers both seasoned hikers and biking enthusiasts a wide variety of routes to choose from, each showcasing the town’s natural beauty. The Montdúver Natural Park is a popular spot, providing numerous trails of varying difficulties that lead to some breathtaking views. The trail to the summit of the Montdúver peak is a challenging but rewarding hike, offering incredible views of the coastline and natural park.

Best Things to Do in Gandía: Activities and Tours

In addition to hiking and biking, Gandía offers a wide variety of activities and tours, including:

  • Water Sports: In addition to swimming, visitors can partake in watersports like kayaking, windsurfing, and paddleboarding.

  • Boat Tours: Explore the fascinating coastline of Gandía on a local boat tour, witnessing beautiful cliff formations, hidden coves, and caves.

  • Horse-riding: Scenic horse-riding tours are ideal for families and nature lovers. They offer a unique perspective of the natural surroundings while undertaking a comfortable horse ride.

Week-Long Itinerary for Gandía

Day 1: Explore Gandía’s historic attractions, including the Gandía Palace and Church of Santa Maria.

Day 2: Enjoy a lazy day on Gandía Beach, taking a dip in the Mediterranean.

Day 3: Experience the Montdúver Natural Park, either by hiking or biking.

Day 4: Take a cultural tour of the neighboring towns of Denía and Jávea.

Day 5: Enjoy a boat ride to explore the coastline and its unique features.

Day 6: Enjoy a wine-tasting experience at the Alqueria del Duc.

Day 7: Relax and enjoy your last day on Gandía Beach, soaking up the sun with a good book.

Food and Drink Scene of Gandía

Gandía is known for its mouth-watering cuisine, which combines the best of Mediterranean flavors and ingredients.

Must-Try Dishes

  • Arros Amb Fessols i Naps: Rice with beans and turnips

  • Suquet de Peix: Fish and shellfish stew

  • Clòtxina: A type of mussel served in various styles

Local Beverages

  • Horchata: A refreshing and creamy drink made from almonds and sugar with Valencian origin.

  • Agua de Valencia: A local cocktail made of orange juice, sparkling wine, gin, and vodka.

Where to Stay in Gandía: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Gandía features many hotels and guesthouses for all budgets, ranging from the luxury Hotel RH Bayren offering a stunning sea view, to the budget-friendly Hotel Borgia. For backpackers and budget travelers, Gandía has a variety of hostels to choose from offering all the necessary amenities.

What to Buy in Gandía: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Fallas Souvenirs: From small to large, little keepsakes, hanging ornaments, and pottery displaying the Fallas.

  • Local Wines: Gandía is home to several wineries, such as Alqueria del Duc, where visitors can taste and purchase local wine.

Local Culture and Customs of Gandía

The locals of Gandía are friendly and welcoming to visitors. As is Spanish custom, it is standard for people to dress smart-casually while walking around the old town, taking a stroll on the promenade, or enjoying a coffee. Tipping isn’t common in Spain, but if you feel particularly enamored of the service, tipping about 5-10% is a suitable gesture of appreciation.

Best Time to Visit Gandía

Gandía enjoys warm weather year-round, making it an ideal destination no matter the season. Summer months are particularly busy, but the weather and activities are well worth the crowds. Spring and autumn are excellent seasons to visit for mild weather and less bustling beaches.

Do’s and Don’ts of Gandía

Do:

  • Respect local customs, like siesta in the afternoon.

  • Try the local cuisine, including fresh seafood and traditional dishes.

Don’t:

  • Be too loud or disruptive, especially in residential areas.

  • Leave your belongings unattended on the beach or promenade.

Safety and Health

Safety in Gandía

Gandía is generally safe, but like any popular tourist destination, it’s essential to keep your belongings safe. Watch your wallet or purse in crowded areas, and avoid walking alone at nighttime.

Health in Gandía

Spain has an excellent public healthcare system, and tourists may access public hospitals and clinics. However, ensure you have comprehensive travel insurance before visiting.

Getting to Gandía: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Gandía

The closest international airport to Gandía is Valencia Airport, approximately a 50-minute drive away.

Bus Stations Near Gandía

The regional bus system provides an affordable and convenient way to travel within the province. Gandía’s bus station is located on Avinguda de la Pau.

Other Options

Car rental is effortless in Spain for those wanting to explore more of the region. Taxis and private transfers are also readily available.

How to Get Around in Gandía

Getting around Gandía is easy by foot, bike, or car. There are bike rentals available. Locals use cars, taxis and buses for moving around. Walking is a popular way to get around the old town area.

Where to Go Next from Gandía: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Valencia: A bustling, cosmopolitan city situated an hour’s drive away, offering visitors plenty to see and do.

  • Benidorm: A popular resort town, offering sandy beaches and nightlife, which is around an hour’s drive.

  • Denia: A charming fishing town located on a hilltop that also offers beautiful views of the coast and surrounding mountains.

Conclusion

Gandía is indeed a destination that has a little something for everyone. Whether you are interested in culture, history, nature, food, or just want to relax on a beautiful beach, Gandía will not disappoint. By following this guide, you will be sure to have an enjoyable and memorable trip.
