Introduction to Valencia (province)

Valencia province is located on the eastern coast of Spain, in the Valencian Community. It is a land of contrasts, where modern architecture is juxtaposed with ancient history, and natural landscapes merge with lively towns. This region boasts some of Spain’s most stunning beaches, traditional dishes like paella, and historic cities like Valencia. Whether you’re interested in exploring the arts, architecture, scenic beauty, or outdoor adventures, Valencia is sure to impress.

List of Places to Visit in Valencia (province)

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Valencia is rich in history and culture, with many museums and historical sites to visit. Some of the highlights include:

  • (1) City of Arts and Sciences: a complex of futuristic buildings dedicated to the arts and sciences, where you can explore the Prince Felipe Science Museum, the Oceanographic, and other attractions.
  • (2) Valencia Cathedral: located in the heart of Valencia old town, this cathedral dates back to the 13th century and has many interesting features, including the Holy Chalice, which is believed to be the cup used by Jesus Christ at the Last Supper.
  • (3) Lonja de la Seda: a UNESCO World Heritage Site that was once the silk exchange market in Valencia. This Gothic-style building is a must-visit for anyone interested in Valencia’s rich history.
  • (4) Central Market: one of the largest markets in Europe is located in Valencia’s old town. It is the perfect place to experience the city’s food scene and buy some souvenirs.

Natural Attractions of Valencia (province): Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Besides the historic attractions, Valencia province is full of natural beauty, ranging from stunning beaches to rolling hills. Some of the outdoor activities and natural attractions that you should not miss include:

  • (1) Costa Blanca Beaches: these beautiful beaches are among the most popular attractions in Valencia province, with crystal-clear waters, pristine sands, and countless water sports.
  • (2) Albufera Natural Park: located south of Valencia, this natural reserve covers 21,000 hectares and is a paradise for birdwatchers, hikers, and cyclists.
  • (3) Serra Calderona Natural Park: situated in the north of Valencia province, this park offers scenic hiking trails, waterfalls, and breathtaking views over the Mediterranean sea.
  • (4) Turia River Gardens: a unique park situated in the former Turia riverbed, now diverted around Valencia, the gardens cover around 110 hectares and are a popular destination for walkers, joggers, and cyclists.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Valencia (province)

There are several hiking and biking trails throughout Valencia province, offering beautiful natural scenery and stunning views. Some of the best routes include:

  • (1) Costa Blanca Coastal Walk: this popular route starts in Denia and follows the coastline southward, passing through several towns and offering spectacular views of the Mediterranean sea.
  • (2) Sierra de Espadan Natural Park: situated in the north of Valencia province, this park offers several hiking trails through scenic woodlands and mountains.
  • (3) La Albufera Bike Trail: this easy ride takes you through the Albufera Nature Reserve, showcasing the natural beauty of the area.
  • (4) Montgo Natural Park: located near Denia, this park offers hiking trails to the summit of Montgo Mountain, where you can get stunning views of the Mediterranean sea and surrounding countryside.

Best Things to Do in Valencia (province): Activities and Tours

Valencia province is full of interesting and exciting activities and tours. Here are some of the best:

  • (1) Taste the Local Cuisine: one of the most famous dishes in Valencia is paella, a tasty rice and seafood dish that originated in the region. You can sample this and other traditional dishes on a Food Tour of Valencia’s Old Town.
  • (2) Explore the City of Arts and Sciences: take a guided tour of this futuristic complex or explore the attractions on your own.
  • (3) Valencia Bike Tour: discover the city by bike, cycling through the old town, Turia River Gardens, and the Arts and Sciences Complex.
  • (4) Valencia Oceanographic: this aquarium showcases a wide variety of marine life from the Mediterranean and other oceans. It is a great day out for all the family.

Week-Long Itinerary for Valencia (province)

Valencia province offers plenty of options to create a week-long itinerary suited to suit your interests. Here is a suggested itinerary:

Day 1: Arrive in Valencia and explore the City of Arts and Sciences. Day 2: Visit Valencia old town and Valencia Cathedral. In the evening, experience Valencia’s nightlife. Day 3: Rent a car and drive south to the Albufera Nature Reserve, then continue to Gandia, a lively coastal town with stunning beaches. Day 4: Drive north to Montanejos, a picturesque town nestled in mountains and forests. Enjoy a day of hiking or soak in the hot springs. Day 5: Drive to Denia and explore the historic castle and town center. Take in the views from Montgo Natural Park. Day 6: Relax on the beaches of Costa Blanca or take a water sport activity. Day 7: Visit Xativa castle or Villena’s old town before heading back to Valencia.

Food and Drink Scene of Valencia (province)

Valencia is renowned for its local cuisine, mouthwatering paella, and fresh seafood. There are also local wines and beers to try, too. Here are some of the must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • (1) Paella: the dish originated in Valencia and is made with saffron-infused rice and a variety of meats and seafood.
  • (2) Arroz a banda: similar to paella, it is made with seafood and served with a side of alioli sauce.
  • (3) Horchata: a refreshing drink made from tiger nuts.
  • (4) Fartons: a traditional pastry that is often dipped into horchata.

Local Beverages

  • (1) Agua de Valencia: a tasty cocktail made from cava, orange juice, vodka, and gin.
  • (2) Cerveza Turia: a popular beer brewed in Valencia, it is a refreshing choice alongside many seafood dishes.
  • (3) Bodegas Enguera: located in the inland region of Valencia, this winery produces award-winning organic wines.

Where to Stay in Valencia (province): Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Valencia province has plenty of accommodations that cater to all budgets. Here are some of the best options:

  • (1) Hospes Palau de la Mar: Located in the heart of Valencia, this luxurious hotel boasts a spa, outdoor pool, and elegant rooms.
  • (2) La Mozaira: located in the Albufera Nature Reserve, this charming guesthouse is surrounded by orange groves and rice fields.
  • (3) The River Hostel: This hostel is right in the Turia River Gardens and is a unique hostel experience due to its location.
  • (4) Camping Los Pinos: Located in Denia, this beautiful campsite offers a range of accommodation choices, along with facilities for campers.

What to Buy in Valencia (province): Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Valencia province has a wide variety of traditional handicrafts and local products, including:

  • (1) Ceramics: Manises is famous for its ceramic art, ranging from tiles to dishes and sculptures.
  • (2) Leather Goods: The small town of Ontinyent is known for high-quality leather goods.
  • (3) Agua de Valencia: A bottle of this local cocktail mix should go down well with friends when you back home.
  • (4) Food Souvenirs: Cured ham, olive oil, and cheese are a few of the food souvenirs that Valencia is famous for producing and worth taking home with you.

Local Culture and Customs of Valencia (province)

Valencians are proud of their tradition and culture, with many festivals, fairs, and local customs observed throughout the year. Here are some of the significant events to watch out for:

  • (1) Las Fallas: celebrated in Valencia in March, it’s an annual festival honoring St. Joseph where huge papier-mache constructions are built and then set alight on the final night.
  • (2) La Tomatina: held annually in Buñol, near Valencia, where tons of tomatoes are thrown around in an organized food fight.
  • (3) Bullfighting: prevalent in Valencia, it’s a highly controversial sport. The bullfight at Valencia’s Plaza de Toros every July is the most significant bullfighting event in the region.

Best Time to Visit Valencia (province)

The best time to visit Valencia province is during the shoulder seasons of spring (March to May) and fall (September to November). The climate is pleasant, and there are fewer crowds, which means you can enjoy the attractions and outdoor activities more comfortably. The summer months (June-August) bring warm temperatures and an influx of beach-goers, while winter can be rainy and chilly.

Do’s and Don’ts of Valencia (province)

Do:

  • (1) Try Paella: you cannot leave Valencia without trying a plate of paella.
  • (2) Respect the Local Customs: if you find yourself invited to a local event, such as a festival, take part, and respect the cultural traditions.
  • (3) Learn Some Basic Spanish: some locals might not speak English, so learning some basic Spanish phrases will help you communicate better.
  • (4) Use Public Transportation: the city has an excellent public transportation system, including bus and subway lines.

Don’t:

  • (1) Wear Beach Attire in the City: Revealing clothing is frowned upon, especially in the city center.
  • (2) Get Drunk in Public: Drinking in public is legal, but getting loud and disorderly is not tolerated.
  • (3) Forget to Tip: Tipping is not mandatory in Valencia, but it is customary to leave some small change at restaurants, cafes, and bars.
  • (4) Leave Your Valuables Unattended: As with any international destination, leaving your valuables unattended can invite theft.

Safety and Health

Safety in Valencia (province)

Valencia is generally safe but, as with any urban area, you should be aware of pickpockets and other petty crimes, particularly in public transportation and other crowded areas. It’s also worth noting that beach areas can become busier and more welcoming to pickpockets during summer months. Take precautions such as keeping your valuables close to you and being aware of your surroundings.

Health in Valencia (province)

No vaccinations are required to enter Valencia, and the area is not prone to disease outbreaks. Nevertheless, tourists should drink bottled water and observe good hygiene practices to avoid food- and water-borne illnesses.

Getting to Valencia (province): Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Valencia (province)

Valencia airport is the primary airport in the region, served by many airlines with flights from major European cities. You can reach the city center by train, bus, or taxi from the airport.

Bus and Train Stations Near Valencia (province)

Valencia has a modern train station that is served by high-speed trains from Madrid and Barcelona. The city also has several bus stations, with domestic and international routes connecting Valencia with other cities and destinations.

Driving and Car Hire in Valencia (province)

Driving in Valencia province is relatively easy, with good roads connecting major cities and attractions. Car hire is available at Valencia airport and the city center. However, parking can be limited in cities like Valencia and beaches during summer months.

How to Get Around in Valencia (province)

Valencia province offers plenty of options for getting around, including public transportation, cycling, and rental cars. Valencia city boasts an excellent public transportation system, with buses and metro connecting all corners of the city.

Where to Go Next from Valencia (province): Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Nearby destinations and excursions from Valencia province are plentiful, including:

  • (1) Barcelona: a city rich in architecture, art & culture, and famous for its amazing food culture.
  • (2) Madrid: a bustling metropolis of culture, museums, amazing restaurants, and nightlife.
  • (3) Murcia: this ancient city is famous for its old town, where you can explore its many ornate churches and museums.
  • (4) Ibiza: a world-renowned party island famous for its electronic dance music, pristine beaches, and vibrant nightlife.

Conclusion

Valencia province is a stunning destination, blending history, culture, and natural beauty. With endless attractions and activities, it is the perfect location for an unforgettable trip. Make sure you taste the delicious local cuisine, see the impressive City of Arts and Sciences, and explore the stunning beaches and natural parks - and don’t forget to leave with a leather bag or ceramic tile souvenir for your memories.
