Introduction to Carrícola

Carrícola is a beautiful municipality located in the north of Valencia, Spain. It is a small, charming village nestled in the foothills of the Serra Mariola Natural Park. Carrícola offers visitors the opportunity to immerse themselves in Spanish countryside and culture, experience various outdoor activities and enjoy delicious local cuisine. Carrícola is a hidden gem off the beaten track, perfect for those looking to escape from the crowds and to discover a more authentic Spain.

List of Places to Visit in Carrícola

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Carrícola Castle: A must-visit for history lovers. Carrícola Castle was built in the 13th century and offers stunning views of the surrounding mountains and town.
  • Church of Our Lady’s Assumption: A beautiful Baroque-style church that dates back to the 17th century. The church features some impressive artwork, including a stunning altarpiece.
  • Archaeological Museum: This museum displays many ancient artifacts, including pottery, weapons, and everyday objects used by the Iberian tribes that inhabited the area in antiquity.

Natural Attractions of Carrícola: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Carrícola is the perfect destination for outdoor enthusiasts seeking adventure and breathtaking views. Here are some natural attractions and outdoor activities to enjoy in Carrícola:

  • Serra Mariola Natural Park: A beautiful natural park that features unique flora and fauna species and offers stunning vistas for hikers and bikers. It is a perfect place to relax and breathe in nature’s fresh air.
  • Font de Carrícola natural spring: This natural spring is located on the outskirts of the village, and its crystal-clear water attracts tourists and locals alike. It is a must-visit place to experience the Spanish countryside.
  • River Serpis: This river is ideal for river trekking, fishing and kayaking for all ages and skill levels.

Hiking/Biking Routes in Carrícola

Carrícola offers a wide range of hiking and biking routes for visitors to explore. Whether you prefer a leisurely stroll or more challenging routes, Carrícola’s surroundings have something to offer. There are several low or high altitude hikes to discover in Serra Mariola, where you can explore the area’s unique charms such as the keyhole route of the Mariola mountain range or the Ventisqueros route through the snowy heights of the Mariola mountain range.

Best Things to Do in Carrícola: Activities and Tours

There are plenty of things to do in Carrícola. Here are a few suggestions:

  • Join a hiking and biking tour: Carrícola offers guided tours for hikers and bikers who wish to discover the town’s surroundings.
  • Visit local wineries: The town has a long-lasting tradition of wine production. Tourists can visit and taste local wines at various bodegas located in the area.
  • Horseback riding tours: Carrícola’s natural surroundings make it an ideal place to go horseback riding. Join a guided horseback riding tour, and enjoy the spectacular views of the natural park.
  • Enjoy traditional farming activities: Carrícola’s surroundings are still largely rural. You can try some agricultural activities such as grape harvesting, olive oil production or cheese making.

Week-Long Itinerary for Carrícola

Day 1: Arrive in Carrícola and explore the town.

Day 2: Visit Carrícola Castle and the Archaeological Museum.

Day 3-4: Spend the day hiking or biking in Serra Mariola Natural Park.

Day 5: Enjoy horseback riding or farming activities.

Day 6: Visit some local wineries and enjoy a wine tasting.

Day 7: Relax by the Font de Carrícola natural spring or swim in the River Serpis.

Food and Drink Scene of Carrícola

Carrícola is famous for its local cuisine, which features fresh, locally-sourced ingredients. Some dishes to try include:

Must-Try Dishes:

  • Arroz al Horno: A traditional savory rice dish made with vegetables, meat, and chickpeas.
  • Arròs amb fessols i naps: A typical dish made of rice with beans, turnips, and different kinds of meat.
  • Pericana: A spicy dish made with roasted and pickled vegetables served with toasted bread.

Local Beverages:

  • Agua de Valencia: A refreshing cocktail made with sparkling wine, orange juice, vodka, and gin.
  • Mistela: A sweet wine made by blending grape juice and alcohol.
  • Horchata: A refreshing, dairy-free drink made with tiger nuts that is popular in the region.

Where to Stay in Carrícola: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Carrícola offers several options for visitors looking for a place to stay. Here are some suggestions:

  • Casa Rural Masía de Carrícola: This guest house has a swimming pool, a garden, and free parking. It is located within easy reach of the local attractions.
  • Casa Rural Balcón del Marques: This guest house is located in the heart of Carrícola, and it features a bar and restaurant serving delicious traditional Spanish cuisine.
  • Rural/Traditional Stay: Visitors can also choose to stay in one of the traditional rural farmhouses located in the town’s outskirts.

What to Buy in Carrícola: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Carrícola is known for its crafts and souvenirs. Some products to consider buying include:

  • Local honey: Carrícola is home to several beekeepers who produce high-quality honey that makes a great souvenir.
  • Ceramics: There are several ceramic workshops in the area producing vases, toothbrush holders, and other household items.
  • Wine and olive oil: You can find some local wineries and olive oil co-operatives that offer excellent quality products.

Local Culture and Customs of Carrícola

Carrícola has a rich cultural heritage and customs. While visiting the town, visitors are advised to respect the local traditions:

  • Siesta: After lunchtime, it is a common practice for locals to take a nap or relax, at least until early evening.
  • Festivals: The town celebrates a range of festivals and events throughout the year, including Fira de Sant Miquel in September and Moros y Cristianos festival in August.
  • Religious customs: The church plays an important role in the community and Catholic traditions are well preserved.

Best Time to Visit Carrícola

The best time to visit Carrícola is from late spring to early autumn (April to October). During this time, the weather is mild and perfect for outdoor activities. The town’s festivals and cultural events are also held during this time, providing visitors with a chance to experience more of the local culture.

Do’s and Don’ts of Carrícola

Do:

  • Respect the local traditions and customs.
  • Try local cuisine and products.
  • Enjoy the natural surroundings and landscapes.

Don’t:

  • Make too much noise after midnight.
  • Leave your trash in public areas.
  • Wear revealing clothing when entering religious buildings.

Safety and Health

Safety in Carrícola

Carrícola is considered a safe town with low crime rates. Visitors are advised to take standard precautions such as being aware of their surroundings and not leaving valuables in plain sight.

Health in Carrícola

Carrícola has several perfectly equipped health centers located closest in neighboring towns such as Bocairent and Ontinyent.

Getting to Carrícola: Transportation Options and Logistics

Carrícola is located in the north of the Valencian province in eastern Spain. Here are some transportation options:

Airports Near Carrícola

The nearest airport is the Valencia Airport, which is about 80 km south of Carrícola. Alicante Airport is also a good option and is approximately 90 km southeast of the village.

Bus Stations Near Carrícola

The nearest bus stations are Bocairent and Ontinyent.

Other Options

Another option is to rent a car and drive to Carrícola.

How to Get Around in Carrícola

The town is small enough that visitors can easily explore it on foot. Additionally, renting a bike is a good way to explore the picturesque surroundings of Carrícola.

Where to Go Next from Carrícola: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Carrícola is a great base for exploring eastern Spain’s other attractions. Here are some nearby destinations worth a visit:

  • Valencia: Visit the city for its modern architecture and delicious dining options.
  • Xàtiva: Surrounded by hills and mountains, this town is home to many historical sites, including a castle.
  • Gandia: A coastal town with beautiful beaches, Gothic architecture, and lively nightlife.

Conclusion

Carrícola is an excellent destination for visitors looking to discover the secrets of Spain. It offers a wide range of attractions, natural surroundings, and activities, delicious locally sourced cuisine and the hospitality of a small, but lively town. It is undoubtedly one of the lesser-known tourist destinations in Spain, but a place worth exploring for those seeking an authentic experience.
