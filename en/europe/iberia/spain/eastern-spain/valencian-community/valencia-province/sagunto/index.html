Introduction to Sagunto

Located in Valencia Province, in the Valencian Community of Eastern Spain, Sagunto is a beautiful town with a rich history dating back to the Roman times. The town is situated just 25 km north of the city of Valencia and offers visitors a glimpse into its ancient past through its well-preserved ruins, museums, and historic landmarks. With its alluring mix of cultural attractions, natural wonders, outdoor adventures, and delicious local cuisine, Sagunto is a destination that should not be missed on any trip to Spain.

List of Places to Visit in Sagunto

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  1. Sagunto Castle
  2. Roman Theater
  3. Jewish Quarter
  4. Sagunto History Museum
  5. Santa Maria Church

Sagunto is home to several culturally important attractions, such as the Sagunto Castle, which dates back to the 10th century and provides a breathtaking view of the town. The town’s Roman Theater, built in the 1st century, is one of the most well-preserved ancient theaters in Spain and is a must-see for history enthusiasts. The Jewish Quarter, consisting of cobblestone streets and whitewashed buildings, offers a glimpse into the town’s Jewish heritage.

Natural Attractions of Sagunto: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  1. Sierra Calderona Natural Park
  2. Sagunto Beach
  3. Algar River
  4. Garbí Mountain

Nature lovers will not want to miss the Sierra Calderona Natural Park, which is located just a short drive from Sagunto and offers stunning views of the surrounding valley. Sagunto Beach, located in the town of Canet d’en Berenguer, is a tranquil place to relax and enjoy the Mediterranean Sea. Visitors can also hike or bike along the Algar River and Garbí Mountain for an adventurous outdoor experience.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Sagunto

  1. PR-CV 37 Trail - This trail is a 6 km path that leads to the Roman Theater and offers scenic views of the surrounding countryside.
  2. Sierra Calderona Natural Park - Boasting over 17 hiking trails of varying difficulty levels, Sierra Calderona Natural Park provides several opportunities for visitors to hike and explore the park.
  3. La Sierra de la Calderona - This challenging trail is over 33 km long and passes through Garbí Mountain and several other natural areas.

Best Things to Do in Sagunto: Activities and Tours

  1. Segway Tour - Explore the town on a Segway and visit its most iconic landmarks with this popular tour option.
  2. Wine Tasting - Visit local wineries for a taste of the region’s famous wines.
  3. Kayaking - Paddle along the Algar River and discover the surrounding countryside in a unique way.

Week-Long Itinerary for Sagunto

Food and Drink Scene of Sagunto

Must-Try Dishes

  1. Paella - A classic Spanish dish, Sagunto’s paella is made with fresh seafood and delicious spices.
  2. Fideuà - A seafood-based dish, this is similar to paella but instead of rice, it’s prepared with short noodles or vermicelli.
  3. Arros Abranquetat - A rice-based dish that combines meat, vegetables and is slow-cooked to perfection.

Local Beverages

  1. Agua de Valencia - This refreshing cocktail made with cava, orange juice, vodka, and gin is a popular local drink in Sagunto.
  2. Navello Liquor - This traditional liquor is made from local fruits and herbs and is a must-try for anyone visiting Sagunto.

Where to Stay in Sagunto: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  1. Hotel Els Arenals - Located near the beach and offering scenic views, this hotel is a popular choice for visitors.
  2. Casa Rural La Belluga - A traditional Spanish guesthouse with modern luxuries and a tranquil atmosphere.
  3. Hostal Teide - A budget-friendly option with clean rooms and a friendly staff.

What to Buy in Sagunto: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  1. Wine - With several local wineries, wine is a popular souvenir for visitors to bring home.
  2. Ceramics - Handcrafted ceramic pots and plates are a popular souvenir in Sagunto.
  3. Artisan Jam and Honey - Locally produced small-batch jams and honey are a must-have for foodies.

Local Culture and Customs of Sagunto

Sagunto’s history is a blend of Roman, Jewish, and Moorish culture. The town is known for its colorful fiestas, such as La Mare de Deu de la Sang and the Festes del Roser, which celebrate the town’s religious and cultural heritage. The people of Sagunto are friendly and welcoming, and visitors should be respectful of the town’s culture and customs.

Best Time to Visit Sagunto

The best time to visit Sagunto is from May to June when the temperatures are mild and the town isn’t as crowded. September and October are also great months to visit, as they offer cooler temperatures while still maintaining pleasant weather conditions.

Do’s and Don’ts of Sagunto

Do:

  1. Respect the local culture and customs.
  2. Try the local cuisine and wines.
  3. Wear comfortable shoes and clothing to explore the town’s historical landmarks and natural attractions.

Don’t:

  1. Litter or damage any historical landmarks or natural sites.
  2. Ignore the local dress code at cultural and religious sites.
  3. Disrespect the local culture or customs in any way.

Safety and Health

Safety in Sagunto

Sagunto is a safe town, but visitors should be aware of pickpockets and petty crime in tourist areas. It’s always advisable to keep a close eye on your belongings and avoid carrying large sums of money or valuables.

Health in Sagunto

Spain has a well-developed healthcare system, and Sagunto boasts several medical centers and pharmacies. Visitors should have comprehensive travel insurance before arriving and be aware of any pre-existing conditions that may require specific medications.

Getting to Sagunto: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Sagunto

The Valencia Airport is the closest airport to Sagunto, just a 25 km drive from the town center. The Alicante Airport is also another option, about a 130 km drive from the town.

Bus Stations Near Sagunto

The Sagunto Bus Station offers connections to nearby towns and Valencia City. The Estación del Norte in Valencia is the main bus station and offers connections to other cities throughout Spain.

Other Options

The Sagunto Train Station provides connections to other cities in Spain, and visitors can also opt for a rental car or taxi to get around town.

How to Get Around in Sagunto

The best way to explore Sagunto is on foot, as the town’s main attractions are within walking distance of each other. Visitors can also opt for a rental bike or car to explore the surrounding countryside.

Where to Go Next from Sagunto: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  1. Valencia City - This bustling, vibrant city, located just 25 km south of Sagunto, offers visitors a glimpse into modern Spanish culture and history.
  2. Castellón de la Plana - Located about 60 km north of Sagunto, Castellón de la Plana is known for its beautiful beaches and tranquil atmosphere.
  3. Peñíscola - About 100 km south of Sagunto, Peñíscola is a charming coastal town famous for its impressive castle and beautiful beaches.

Conclusion

Sagunto offers visitors a fascinating history, beautiful natural surroundings, delicious food and wine, and warm hospitality. With its rich cultural heritage and tranquil atmosphere, Sagunto is a destination that should not be missed while exploring Spain’s Valencia Province.
