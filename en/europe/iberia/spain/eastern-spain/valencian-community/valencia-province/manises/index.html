Introduction to Manises

Located in the eastern part of Spain, Manises is well-known for its traditional ceramics and pottery. This Valencian town is just six kilometers away from the city of Valencia and boasts a rich history and culture that will leave any traveler in awe. In this comprehensive travel guide, we’ll explore everything that Manises has to offer, from its fascinating museums and historical places to its stunning natural scenery and outdoor activities. We’ll also cover the best places to stay, what to buy, and the dos and don’ts of this charming town.

List of Places to Visit in Manises

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Museu de Ceràmica de Manises: Learn about the town’s famous ceramics industry and see exquisite pottery pieces from different periods.
  • Iglesia de San Juan Bautista: A beautiful 18th-century church with stunning baroque architecture and beautiful interior decoration.
  • Castillo de Manises: A medieval fortress with a rich history offering great views of the surrounding landscape.
  • La Alquería del Moro: A beautiful historic building from the 12th century, restored with traditional techniques and worth a visit.

Natural Attractions of Manises: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Turia Natural Park: Perfect for hiking, cycling, or picnics, this park offers beautiful scenery and stunning views of the Turia River.
  • Coves de l’Horta: A network of underground caves with stalactites and stalagmites, perfect for outdoor enthusiasts and cave explorers.
  • El Cerro de la Carraixeta: A rocky hill offering great views of the city of Valencia and the surrounding areas.
  • El Parque de la Pinada: A lovely park with walking paths and playgrounds for children. It’s great for enjoying nature and a perfect spot for a picnic.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Manises

For outdoor enthusiasts, Manises offers some of the best hiking and biking routes in the region. Some popular routes include:

  • Turia Gardens: A peaceful and scenic route along the Turia River, ideal for walks, runs, or cycling.
  • Barranco del Carraixet: A 10-mile trail passing through beautiful landscapes, including forests, hills, and steep cliffs.
  • Barranco de la Murtera: A scenic hiking trail leading to a natural pool, perfect for a refreshing swim on a hot day.

Best Things to Do in Manises: Activities and Tours

Apart from the beautiful museums, historic places, and scenic views, Manises offers plenty of activities and tours that will make your visit unforgettable. Take a look at some of the top things to do:

  • Ceramics Workshop Tour: Discover the town’s centuries-old ceramics tradition by taking a guided workshop tour of a local pottery.
  • Hot Air Balloon Ride: Take a breathtaking balloon ride with stunning views of Manises and the Valencia Corridor.
  • Tapas Food Tour: Enjoy the town’s diverse, flavorful cuisine by taking a guided tour of local tapas bars, trying traditional dishes, and sampling delicious local wines.
  • Bicycle Tour: Explore the town’s beautiful parks, gardens, and quiet streets with a fun and informative bicycle tour of Manises.

Week-Long Itinerary for Manises

With so much to see and do, here’s an itinerary for a weeklong stay in Manises:

Day Activities
Day 1 Explore the town center, visit Iglesia de San Juan Bautista and Plaza del Ayuntamiento
Day 2 Take the ceramics workshop tour, visit Museu de Ceràmica de Manises, and have lunch at a local restaurant.
Day 3 Hike or bike through Turia Natural Park and enjoy a picnic lunch.
Day 4 Take a hot air balloon ride over Manises.
Day 5 Visit Castillo de Manises and La Alquería del Moro.
Day 6 Take a tapas food tour to sample the best local cuisine.
Day 7 Rent a bicycle and explore the town’s surrounding areas at your own pace.

Food and Drink Scene of Manises

Must-Try Dishes

Manises has a diverse cuisine that combines Valencian, Mediterranean, and Moorish influences. Here are some must-try dishes:

  • Paella Valenciana: A delicious rice dish with chicken, rabbit, and fresh vegetables.
  • Fideuá: A seafood dish made with short noodles instead of rice.
  • All i oli: A garlic and olive oil sauce served with potatoes.
  • Arròs amb fessols i naps: Rice with beans, turnips, and meat.
  • Cremaet: A traditional coffee liqueur served with burnt rum and lemon peel.

Local Beverages

In addition to the delicious cuisine, Manises is also known for its refreshing beverages, including:

  • Horchata: A sweet, cool, and milky drink made from ground tiger nuts.
  • Agua de Valencia: A boozy cocktail made with cava, vodka, gin, and orange juice.
  • Beer: Try the local beers, such as La Socarrada, made from rosemary and honey.

Where to Stay in Manises: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Manises offers a range of accommodations to suit all budgets, including hotels, hostels, and guesthouses. Some popular options include:

  • Xon’s Valencia: A modern, comfortable, and centrally located hotel with great amenities.
  • Ibis Budget Valencia Aeropuerto: An affordable option with clean, simple rooms and free Wi-Fi.
  • Hostal Mas: A charming, comfortable, and family-run guesthouse, offering personalized service and great breakfast.

What to Buy in Manises: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Make sure you bring home a unique souvenir or gift from Manises. Here are some suggestions:

  • Ceramics: Manises is famous for its artisanal ceramics, such as pottery, vases, plates, and tiles.
  • Handwoven baskets: Made by local artisans, these baskets come in all shapes and sizes and great for carrying groceries or storing items.
  • Wine: Valencia is home to some fantastic wineries, and Manises offers many wine shops where you can find delicious local wines.
  • Turron: A sweet treat made with almonds and honey, typical of the Valencia region.
  • Handcrafted Figurines: The region is also known for its handmade ceramic figurines, perfect for collectors or as ornaments.

Local Culture and Customs of Manises

When visiting Manises, make sure you respect local customs and culture. Here are some things to keep in mind:

  • Valencian Language: While many locals in Manises speak Spanish, the primary language is Valencian. Learn a few phrases to better communicate.
  • Ceramics production: Ceramics production is a crucial part of Manises’ history and culture, so make sure you visit Museu de Ceràmica de Manises and take the ceramics workshop tour.
  • Glass blowing: Glass blowing also forms part of Manises’ heritage, and you can see demonstrations of glass blowing at the Museu del Vidre.
  • Eating times: Lunchtime in Manises is usually around 2 pm, and dinner is around 9 pm.

Best Time to Visit Manises

The best time to visit Manises is between June and August, during the summer months when the weather is warm, and outdoor activities are in full swing. The town also celebrates various festivals during this period, such as the Feast of San Juan Bautista and the Feria de Agosto.

Do’s and Don’ts of Manises

Do:

  • Try the ceramics workshop tour: Get a chance to learn the art of pottery from a local artisan and create your designs.
  • Visit Turia Natural Park: Don’t miss the chance to bike and hike through the lush greenery and along the Turia river.
  • Taste the horchata: This traditional drink is popular in Manises and tastes refreshing on a hot day.

Don’t:

  • Overpay for souvenirs: Do your research and compare prices before making a purchase.
  • Ignore local customs: Respect the local customs and try to learn a few phrases of the local language, Valencian.
  • Speak too loud in public: Manises is a small, peaceful town, so try to keep the noise level low, especially in restaurants and cafes.

Safety and Health

Safety in Manises

Manises is a safe town, but travelers should still take precautions like they would in any other city, such as:

  • Avoid carrying large sums of money
  • Keep your valuables secure.
  • Be aware of pickpockets.
  • Be cautious when traveling alone at night.

Health in Manises

Manises has several well-equipped hospitals and medical centers providing excellent healthcare services. However, it’s always advisable to have travel insurance to be on the safe side.

Getting to Manises: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Manises

The Valencia Airport is just a ten-minute drive from Manises. From the airport, you can take a taxi, hire a car, or use public transportation, such as the metro, train, or bus.

Bus Stations Near Manises

Manises has a well-connected bus station offering transportation to other cities and towns in the Valencian region.

Other Options

If you prefer to travel by train, Valencia Joaquín Sorolla Station connects to various cities across Spain, including Madrid, Barcelona, and Almeria.

How to Get Around in Manises

The town center of Manises is small and easily walkable, but you can rent a bicycle or take public transportation to explore the surrounding areas.

Where to Go Next from Manises: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

If you have time, here are some excellent nearby destinations you can visit:

  • Valencia: Just six kilometers away from Manises, Valencia has world-renowned art museums, stunning architecture, and a beautiful beach.
  • Albufera Natural Park: A beautiful, untouched natural park located just outside Valencia, offering boat rides along the lagoon.
  • Sagunto: A beautiful, ancient town with a rich history featuring impressive Roman ruins, a castle, and an old Jewish Quarter.

Conclusion

Manises is a small town with a rich history and culture and offers plenty of opportunities to explore its stunning museums and natural surroundings, enjoy local cuisine, and relax in comfortable accommodations. We hope this travel guide has helped you plan your trip to Manises and make the most of your time in this beautiful town.
