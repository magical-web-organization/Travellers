Introduction to Xàtiva

Xàtiva, a charming town in the Valencia province of Spain, is one of the hidden gems of the Valencian Community. Located in eastern Spain, this historic town is filled with stunning architecture, museums, historical sites, and natural attractions. Xàtiva is a perfect location for a peaceful vacation, and it is less crowded than the adjacent cities of Valencia or Alicante.

List of Places to Visit in Xàtiva

If you’re planning to visit Xàtiva, you’ll be happy to know that there are plenty of attractions to explore. Here is a list of places to visit:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Xàtiva is steeped in history and culture, and there are several museums and historical sites in the town that honor its rich past. Some of the culturally important attractions are:

  • Castle of Xàtiva: The most significant cultural icon of Xàtiva, the castle is located on the top of the hill overlooking the town. Visitors can admire the breathtaking views of the entire region from this vantage point.
  • La Seu Collegiate: This is a beautiful church in the old town of Xàtiva and is worth a visit for its stunning Gothic architecture.
  • Museum of Fine Arts: This museum has an excellent collection of 18th and 19th-century paintings.
  • Palacio de la Vila: Located in the center of Xàtiva, this palace boasts Gothic and Renaissance architecture and is home to the town hall.

Natural Attractions of Xàtiva: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Located in a valley surrounded by mountains and hills, Xàtiva also offers some breathtaking natural wonders. Some of the natural attractions are:

  • Cova Negra: A fascinating set of caves located just a few kilometers away from Xàtiva. The caves have prehistoric wall paintings that are thousands of years old.
  • Llacuna Lake: A beautiful lake located near Xàtiva, where visitors can take a relaxing stroll and admire the stunning views.
  • Font Roja Natural Park: A perfect place for nature lovers, Font Roja Natural Park is home to several hiking and biking trails and boasts some beautiful flora and fauna.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Xàtiva

Xàtiva offers several hiking and biking routes that allow visitors to explore the natural beauty of the region. Some popular trails include:

  • PR-CV 390: A 7-kilometer hiking route that starts from the center of Xàtiva and takes visitors to the Sant Feliu de Terrassa hermitage.
  • PR-CV 389: A hilly 12-kilometer trail that takes visitors from Xàtiva to the Simat de la Valldigna Monastery.
  • PR-CV 23: A 13-kilometer trail that takes visitors from Bufali to Xàtiva through beautiful countryside views.

Best Things to Do in Xàtiva: Activities and Tours

Xàtiva offers lots of activities and tours that visitors can indulge in during their stay. Some of the best things to do in Xàtiva are:

  • Explore the Castle of Xàtiva: Visitors can explore the castle and admire the stunning views of the region.
  • Attend a Festival: Xàtiva is known for its colorful festivals, especially the Fira d’Agost, which celebrates the town’s patron saint.
  • Visit the Museums: Visitors can explore the town’s museums and learn more about the region’s history and culture.
  • Attend a Concert or Theater Performance: Xàtiva is home to several cultural centers that host concerts, plays, and other events throughout the year.

Food and Drink Scene of Xàtiva

Xàtiva’s food and drink scene is worth exploring, with a range of local dishes and beverages to try.

Must-Try Dishes

Some of the must-try dishes in Xàtiva include:

  • Arros al Forn: This is a delicious rice dish cooked in the oven with pork ribs and morcilla sausage.
  • Espencat: A cold vegetable dish made with roasted peppers and eggplants.
  • Fartons: A long and sweet pastry famous in the region.

Local Beverages

Xàtiva is famous for its local wine and beer. Some of the popular ones include:

  • Mistela: Sweet wine made by mixing the must of red or white grapes with alcohol.
  • Tinto de Verano: A refreshing summer drink made by mixing red wine and soda.
  • Almogàver: A local beer brewed in Xàtiva.

Where to Stay in Xàtiva: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Xàtiva offers a range of accommodations to choose from, catering to different budgets and preferences. Some of the best options include:

  • Hotel Casa Aldomar: A four-star hotel located in the town’s center, with comfortable rooms and excellent service.
  • La Maga Rooms: A boutique hotel located in a historic building, with stylish and cozy rooms.
  • Albergue de Xàtiva: A budget-friendly hostel located in the old town of Xàtiva, offering clean and comfortable dorms and private rooms.

What to Buy in Xàtiva: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Xàtiva offers several souvenirs and local products that are unique to the region and make for great gifts. Some of the things to buy in Xàtiva are:

  • Handcrafted pottery: Xàtiva is known for its handcrafted pottery and ceramics, which make for great souvenirs or gifts.
  • Local wines and spirits: Xàtiva is famous for its wines and spirits, which are unique to the region and make for great gifts.
  • Sweets and pastries: Xàtiva offers a range of sweet treats like fartons and handmade chocolates that make for great souvenirs or gifts.

Local Culture and Customs of Xàtiva

Like other Spanish towns, Xàtiva has its own unique culture and customs that visitors can learn about during their stay. Some notable customs include:

  • Festivals: Xàtiva has several colorful festivals throughout the year, honoring saints, local traditions, and cultural events.
  • Gastronomy: Xàtiva is known for its delicious local cuisine and the use of seasonal and fresh ingredients in all their dishes.
  • Bullfighting: Although it is a controversial issue, bullfighting has been an important part of Spanish culture for centuries, and there are several bullfighting events held in Xàtiva every year.

Best Time to Visit Xàtiva

The best time to visit Xàtiva is from March to June or from September to November, when the weather is pleasant, and the crowds are thinner.

Do’s and Don’ts of Xàtiva

Here are some do’s and don’ts to keep in mind while visiting Xàtiva:

Do:

  • Respect the locals: Spanish people are known for their hospitality, so respect the locals’ rules and customs. Greet them politely and try to learn a few common Spanish phrases.
  • Try the local cuisine: Xàtiva is known for its delicious food, so don’t hesitate to try some of the local dishes and beverages.
  • Visit the festivals: Xàtiva has several colorful festivals throughout the year, so try to attend one of them to experience the local culture and traditions.

Don’t:

  • Break the law: Spain has strict laws about drinking and driving, littering, and other offenses. Make sure you understand the rules and regulations before visiting the town.
  • Disrespect the culture: Spanish people are proud of their culture and heritage, so avoid any behavior or actions that may be disrespectful to their customs and traditions.

Safety and Health

Xàtiva is a safe town to visit, with low crime rates, and visitors can feel safe while exploring the region. However, tourists should take common precautions to protect themselves from petty crimes like pickpocketing and theft.

Safety in Xàtiva

  • Carry minimal valuables: Don’t carry more than what you need. Leave your passport and other important documents in a safe place at the hotel.
  • Keep an eye on your belongings: Be aware of your surroundings and keep an eye on your belongings, especially in crowded areas or while using public transport.
  • Avoid dark alleys or streets: Avoid unlit areas or dark streets at night, and stay in well-lit areas.

Health in Xàtiva

Spain has a good health care system, and Xàtiva has several hospitals and clinics that provide quality medical care to visitors in case of any emergency.

Getting to Xàtiva: Transportation Options and Logistics

Xàtiva is well-connected with the rest of Spain and can be reached via various modes of transportation.

Airports Near Xàtiva

The nearest airports to Xàtiva are Valencia Airport and Alicante Airport, which are both about an hour’s drive from Xàtiva.

Bus Stations Near Xàtiva

Xàtiva has a well-connected bus station that has regular bus services to other towns and cities in the region.

Other Options

Visitors can also reach Xàtiva by train, car, or taxi, depending on their budget and preferences.

How to Get Around in Xàtiva

Xàtiva is a small town that can easily be explored on foot. However, visitors can also rent a bike or a car to explore the region’s countryside and nearby attractions.

Where to Go Next from Xàtiva: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Xàtiva is a perfect location to explore the Valencian Community and the neighboring towns. Some of the nearby destinations and excursions are:

  • Valencia: Located just an hour’s drive from Xàtiva, Valencia is a beautiful city known for its stunning architecture, museums, and beaches.
  • Alicante: Another coastal city, Alicante is located about an hour’s drive south of Xàtiva and is known for its vibrant nightlife and scenic beauty.
  • Gandía: A beautiful coastal town located about 20 kilometers from Xàtiva, Gandía is a perfect location for a day trip or a weekend gateway.

Conclusion

Xàtiva is a beautiful town in the Valencia province of Spain, offering visitors a chance to experience its rich culture, history, and natural beauty. Whether you’re interested in exploring the town’s museums and historical sites or hiking and biking its trails, Xàtiva has something for everyone. With a range of accommodations, delicious local cuisine, and nearby attractions, Xàtiva is a perfect location for a peaceful and relaxing vacation in Spain.
