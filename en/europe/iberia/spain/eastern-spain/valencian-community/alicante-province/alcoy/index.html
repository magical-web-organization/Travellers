Alcoy is a historic city located in the Alicante province of Valencian Community, in Eastern Spain. It is known for its rich cultural heritage, impressive architecture, natural beauty, and outdoor adventures. In this comprehensive tour guide, we will explore the best places to visit, things to do, where to stay, and local culture and customs in Alcoy.

Introduction to Alcoy

Founded in pre-Roman times, Alcoy is a city with a rich history and heritage. The city has a blend of Gothic, Baroque, and Modernist architecture that provides an interesting visual landscape. Alcoy is also known for its Fiestas de Moros y Cristianos, a colorful festival that takes place in April. It is a cultural and historical festival that celebrates the Christian reconquest of Spain.

List of Places to Visit in Alcoy

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums, and Historical Places

There are several museums and historical places to explore in Alcoy. Some of the must-visit cultural attractions in Alcoy include:

  • Museu Arqueologic Camil Visedo Moltó: This museum displays archeological finds from the Paleolithic era to the Spanish Civil War.

  • Gruta de la Lloba: This is a unique sight that is a naturally formed underground cave with stalactites and stalagmites formation.

  • Plaza de España: This is the main square in Alcoy, where you can see the town hall and Casa de la Vila.

  • Barranc de Malafi: This is a unique museum that showcases the traditional trades in Alcoy.

Natural Attractions of Alcoy: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Alcoy is surrounded by natural beauty and has plenty of outdoor activities to offer. Some of the must-visit natural attractions in Alcoy include:

  • Parc Natural de la Font Roja: This is a natural park that has several hiking and biking trails with gorgeous views of the city.

  • Pantá de Beniarrés: This is a reservoir that offers plenty of opportunities for water sports like kayaking, fishing and windsurfing

  • Montcabrer: This is the highest peak in the Alicante province and offers fantastic views of the region.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Alcoy

Alcoy has plenty of hiking and biking trails that offer an opportunity to explore the natural beauty of the region up close. Here are some of the best hiking and biking routes in Alcoy:

  • La Sarga-Gorgo de la Escalera: This route has a distance of 14 km and offers spectacular views of the valley as well as the Gorgo de la Escalera waterfall.

  • Font Roja Natural Park: This park has an extensive network of hiking trails that range from easy to difficult. The routes offer fantastic views of the flora and fauna of the park.

Best Things to Do in Alcoy: Activities and Tours

Alcoy offers a range of activities and tours to explore and enjoy the city’s natural beauty and rich cultural heritage. Some of the best things to do in Alcoy include:

  • Visit the Fire Museum: This museum showcases the rich history of Alcoy’s firefighting brigade and houses period uniforms, equipment, and photographs.

  • Participate in the Fiestas de Moros y Cristianos: This festival is the most significant cultural event in the city and runs in the second week of April. The festival features colorful parades, music, dancing, and food.

  • Take a guided hike or biking tour: Alcoy has many expert guides who can take you on tours that offer an opportunity to explore the natural and cultural aspects of the region.

Week-Long Itinerary for Alcoy

If you are planning a week-long vacation in Alcoy, here is a suggested itinerary that covers the best attractions and activities in the city:

Day 1: Arrive in Alcoy and explore the historical center, with a visit to the town hall and Santa Maria Church.

Day 2: Visit the Fire Museum, the Casa de la Vila, and the Archaeological Museum.

Day 3: Explore the Parc Natural de la Font Roja and take a guided hike or biking tour.

Day 4: Visit the Pantá de Beniarrés and try water sports like kayaking and windsurfing.

Day 5: Visit Gorgo de la Escalera waterfall and take the La Sarga-Gorgo de la Escalera hiking route.

Day 6: Visit the Montacabrer mountain and enjoy the fantastic views of the region.

Day 7: Participate in the Fiestas de Moros y Cristianos festival.

Food and Drink Scene of Alcoy

Alcoy is known for its rich culinary heritage, and there are several local dishes and beverages that visitors must try. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages in Alcoy:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Arros amb fesols i naps: This is a traditional dish in Alcoy that is a hearty stew with beans, turnips, and rice, flavored with garlic, saffron, and fresh thyme.

  • Desfeta: This is a dish that has origins in the Moorish cuisine. It is a slow-cooked dish made with chicken or pork that is seasoned with a range of spices and cooked with almonds and raisins.

  • Pastissets de Boniato: These are sweet pastries made with sweet potatoes and filled with almond paste.

Local Beverages

  • Mistela: This is a sweet wine made with Moscatel grapes and flavored with aniseed. It is an aperitif to accompany local pastries.

  • Herbero: This is a herbal liqueur made with local herbs like fennel, thyme, rosemary, and mint.

Where to Stay in Alcoy: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Alcoy offers a range of accommodation options, from budget-friendly hostels to luxurious hotels. Here are some recommendations:

  • Hotel Reconquista: This is a 19th-century hotel that offers comfortable accommodations in a prime location in the historical center of Alcoy.

  • Hostal Savoy: This is a budget-friendly hostel that offers excellent accommodation options for backpackers and budget travelers.

  • Hotel Spa El Collao: This is a luxurious hotel that offers comfortable accommodations and a range of spa services.

What to Buy in Alcoy: Souvenirs, and Local Products to Bring Home

Alcoy is known for its local products like ceramics, chocolates, and pastries. Here are some recommendations for souvenirs and local products to bring home:

  • Ceramic Products: Alcoy is known for its ceramics, and you can find a range of handcrafted ceramic products that make for beautiful souvenirs.

  • Chocolates: Alcoy is known for its chocolates, and you can find chocolates in a range of flavors and textures.

Local Culture and Customs of Alcoy

Alcoy has a rich cultural heritage and is known for its Fiestas de Moros y Cristianos festival. Visitors should respect local customs, traditions, and dress codes when participating in the festival. It is also customary to greet people with a handshake and to use titles when addressing people, like Don or Dona.

Best Time to Visit Alcoy

The best time to visit Alcoy is in April when the Fiestas de Moros y Cristianos festival takes place. The weather is also pleasant in spring, and the natural beauty of the region is at its best.

Do’s and Don’ts of Alcoy

Do:

  • Respect local customs and traditions.

  • Be respectful when participating in the Fiestas de Moros y Cristianos festival.

  • Try the local cuisine and wines.

Don’t:

  • Wear revealing clothing when participating in the Fiestas de Moros y Cristianos festival.

  • Be noisy and disruptive during the festival or in public places.

  • Litter in public places.

Safety and Health

Safety in Alcoy

Alcoy is a safe city, but visitors should be cautious of pickpocketing in crowded tourist areas. Visitors should also be careful when indulging in water sports and hiking/biking in natural parks.

Health in Alcoy

Alcoy has a range of medical facilities and hospitals. Visitors should have travel insurance to cover any medical emergencies.

Getting to Alcoy: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Alcoy

The nearest airports to Alcoy are Valencia Airport (VLC) and Alicante Airport (ALC). Both airports offer shuttle bus services and car rentals to reach Alcoy, which is 90 km from Alicante and 110 km from Valencia.

Bus Stations Near Alcoy

Alcoy has a modern bus station that is well-connected to other parts of Spain. There are regular services from Valencia, Alicante, and Madrid.

Other Options

Visitors can also hire a car to explore Alcoy and the surrounding regions at their leisure.

How to Get Around in Alcoy

Alcoy is a compact city, and most tourist attractions are within walking distance. Visitors can also use public transportation like buses and taxis to explore the city and surrounding areas.

Where to Go Next from Alcoy: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Alcoy is well-connected to other parts of Spain, and there are several nearby destinations and excursions that visitors can explore. Some of the recommended places to visit near Alcoy include:

  • Guadalest: A picturesque village with Medieval architecture, located 45 km from Alcoy.

  • Alicante: A vibrant city with excellent beaches, located 90 km from Alcoy.

  • Valencia: A historic city with a range of cultural attractions, located 110 km from Alcoy.

Conclusion

With its rich cultural heritage, natural beauty, and outdoor adventures, Alcoy is a must-visit destination in Spain. From the Fiestas de Moros y Cristianos festival to the hiking/biking routes in the natural parks, Alcoy offers a range of activities and experiences to suit all travelers.
