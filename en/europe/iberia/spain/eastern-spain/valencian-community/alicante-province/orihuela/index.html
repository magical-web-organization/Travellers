Introduction to Orihuela

Located in the province of Alicante, in the Valencian Community of Eastern Spain, Orihuela is a town steeped in history and culture. With a rich heritage dating back to the Roman era, Orihuela boasts several museums and historical sites of interest to tourists.

Apart from its cultural significance, Orihuela boasts spectacular natural scenery and outdoor activities. The town’s strategic location in the heart of Vega Baja’s agricultural region also adds to its charm, with food and drink establishments offering local delicacies.

List of Places to Visit in Orihuela

If you’re planning a trip to Orihuela, here are the top places to visit:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Orihuela Cathedral

Considered the most impressive religious monument in Orihuela, the cathedral was built between the 14th and 16th centuries and boasts a mix of Gothic, Renaissance, and Baroque styles. Visitors can take a guided tour to explore the building’s history and architectural design.

Museo de la Muralla

Located in the basement of Casa del Paso, the museum features an extensive collection of archaeological finds from the prehistoric era to the modern age. The exhibits include artifacts from the Iberian, Roman, and Arab cultures.

Palacio Episcopal

An impressive neoclassical palace built in the early 18th century, the Palacio Episcopal is home to the Diocese Museum. Among the exhibits is a collection of religious paintings, sculptures, and artifacts from the Renaissance and Baroque periods.

Natural Attractions of Orihuela: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Sierra de Orihuela Natural Park

For nature enthusiasts, the Sierra de Orihuela Natural Park offers stunning hiking trails, mountain biking routes, and rock climbing opportunities. The wildlife in the park includes eagles, vultures, and wild boars, among others.

La Pedrera Reservoir

Located in the heart of Vega Baja, the La Pedrera Reservoir offers scenic views and a tranquil environment for picnics, boating, and other water activities. Bird watching is also popular here, with species such as flamingos, herons, and storks.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Orihuela

For those who want to immerse themselves in the natural beauty of Orihuela, here are some recommended hiking and biking routes:

Hiking Routes

PR-CV 201 Hoya de los Patos

This hiking route is 13.6-km long and allows visitors to explore the cliffs and ravines of the Sierra de Orihuela Natural Park. The trail also leads to the Fontcalent water mill, an ancient structure dating back to the 17th century.

PR-CV 238 Ruta del Azud

A shorter but equally scenic hike, the PR-CV 238 Ruta del Azud follows the course of the Segura River, passing through orchards and fields. Along the way, visitors can also admire the bridge of La Rambla, an impressive feat of engineering built in the 18th century.

Biking Routes

Vía Verde del Chicharra

This 22-km long bike trail follows the course of an abandoned railway line and offers breathtaking views of the Vega Baja countryside. The trail is suitable for all levels of experience, with several rest areas and picnic spots along the way.

La Ruta del Vino

For wine lovers, La Ruta del Vino is a bike trail that passes through several vineyards and wineries, offering wine-tasting opportunities and scenic views of the region. The trail also includes stops at historical sites such as the Castle of Guardamar.

Best Things to Do in Orihuela: Activities and Tours

Apart from the natural beauty and cultural significance of Orihuela, the town offers several activities and tours to entertain tourists. Here are some popular options:

Orihuela Walking Tour

A guided walking tour of Orihuela’s historic center offers a glimpse into the town’s rich past and architectural heritage, with stops at the cathedral, city hall, and bishop’s palace.

Segura River Boat Tour

Taking a boat tour along the Segura River allows visitors to admire the natural scenery of Vega Baja and appreciate the engineering marvels of ancient bridges and aqueducts.

Food and Drink Tasting Tour

A food and drink tasting tour of Orihuela takes visitors on a culinary journey, offering samples of local delicacies such as cocas (flatbreads), ensalada murciana (Murcian-style salad), and horchata (a refreshing drink made from tiger nuts).

Week-Long Itinerary for Orihuela

If you’re planning to spend a week in Orihuela, here’s a recommended itinerary:

Day 1: Guided walking tour of Orihuela’s historic center, including the cathedral and bishop’s palace.

Day 2: Hiking or biking in the Sierra de Orihuela Natural Park, followed by a picnic lunch at the La Pedrera Reservoir.

Day 3: Wine-tasting tour of the local vineyards and wineries.

Day 4: Boat tour along the Segura River, stopping at the ancient bridges and aqueducts.

Day 5: Visit to the Museo de la Muralla and Palacio Episcopal.

Day 6: Food and drink tasting tour, sampling local delicacies and drinks.

Day 7: Relaxation day, exploring the local markets and shops, or taking a cooking class to learn how to make cocas.

Food and Drink Scene of Orihuela

Orihuela’s food scene is influenced by the surrounding Vega Baja agricultural region, with an emphasis on fresh, seasonal produce. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

Cocas

A flatbread made with traditional ingredients such as flour, oil, and salt, cocas come in various flavors, with toppings such as olives, tomatoes, and tuna.

Caldero

A rice dish made with fish and seafood, caldero is a specialty of the Murcia region, but also popular in Orihuela.

Arroces

As a rice-growing region, Orihuela is famous for its arroces, or rice dishes, which come in various flavors, such as chicken, rabbit, or vegetables.

Local Beverages

Horchata

A refreshing drink made from tiger nuts, horchata is especially popular in summer, and can be found in many bars and cafes in Orihuela.

Wines

Several vineyards and wineries in the region produce high-quality wines, such as Monastrell and Tempranillo.

Where to Stay in Orihuela: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Orihuela offers several accommodation options for tourists, ranging from budget-friendly hostels to luxury hotels. Here are some recommended options:

Hostels

Albergue Triana

Located in the historic center of Orihuela, Albergue Triana offers affordable private and shared rooms, with a shared kitchen and lounge.

Hostal El Palmeral

A family-run hostel situated near the La Pedrera Reservoir, Hostal El Palmeral offers comfortable rooms and an on-site restaurant serving local cuisine.

Hotels

Hotel Palacio de Tudemir

Housed in a 19th-century palace, Hotel Palacio de Tudemir offers stylish rooms and suites, a spa, and a rooftop terrace with panoramic views of Orihuela.

Hotel Villa de Biar

Located in the nearby town of Biar, Hotel Villa de Biar is set in a restored 18th-century building and offers comfortable rooms, a restaurant, and a garden.

Guesthouses

Casa Rural Lo Soto

Nestled in the countryside, Casa Rural Lo Soto offers spacious rooms and apartments with rustic decor, a swimming pool, and a garden.

Casa Rural La Troná

Located in the heart of Orihuela’s historic center, Casa Rural La Troná is a charming guesthouse with a traditional Andalusian-style patio, comfortable rooms, and a terrace.

What to Buy in Orihuela: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

If you want to take home a piece of Orihuela, here are some local products to look out for:

Pottery

Orihuela is famous for its pottery and ceramic crafts, with several artisan shops and workshops in the historic center.

Olive Oil

The surrounding Vega Baja region produces high-quality olive oil, which can be found in local markets and shops.

Leather Goods

Spain is famous for its leather goods, and Orihuela is no exception, with several shops selling high-quality belts, bags, and shoes.

Local Culture and Customs of Orihuela

Orihuela is a town with a strong sense of identity, shaped by its history, traditions, and geographical location. Here are some customs and cultural events to look out for:

Holy Week

One of the biggest events in Orihuela’s religious calendar, Holy Week (Semana Santa) is celebrated with processions, music, and traditional costumes.

Moors and Christians Festivals

A celebration of the town’s history, the Moors and Christians festivals are a colorful display of parades, fireworks, and mock battles.

Lunchtime Siesta

As in the rest of Spain, the tradition of taking a siesta (a midday nap) is still prevalent in Orihuela, with many shops and eateries closing for a few hours in the early afternoon.

Best Time to Visit Orihuela

The best time to visit Orihuela depends on your preferences and interests. The town enjoys a Mediterranean climate, with mild winters and hot summers. Here are some things to consider:

Spring (March to May)

Spring is a popular time to visit Orihuela, with mild temperatures and colorful blossoms. The town is also lively during Holy Week and its festivities.

Summer (June to August)

Summer is the peak tourist season in Orihuela, with long sunny days, bustling beaches, and outdoor events. However, temperatures can be high, and crowds can be overwhelming.

Autumn (September to November)

Autumn is a good time to visit for those who prefer cooler temperatures and want to avoid the crowds. The town is also less busy during this season, allowing for a more relaxed experience.

Winter (December to February)

Winter is the low season in Orihuela, but the town still offers plenty of cultural activities and outdoor adventures, such as hiking and biking. Temperatures can be chilly, so warm clothing is recommended.

Do’s and Don’ts of Orihuela

To make the most of your visit to Orihuela, here are some do’s and don’ts:

Do:

  • Learn some Spanish phrases or bring a translation app to communicate with the locals.

  • Respect the cultural traditions and customs of the town, such as the lunchtime siesta.

  • Indulge in the local cuisine and beverages, such as cocas and horchata.

Don’t:

  • Disrespect the religious monuments and traditions of Orihuela.

  • Ignore the siesta and expect shops and eateries to be open all day.

  • Leave trash or litter in the natural parks or reservoirs.

Safety and Health

Orihuela is a safe and healthy town, with no major health risks or safety concerns. However, tourists are advised to take some precautions:

Safety in Orihuela

  • Keep your valuables and documents in a safe place, and be aware of pickpockets in crowded areas.

  • Respect the rules and regulations of the natural parks and reserves, and avoid risky behaviors such as unguided rock climbing or hiking alone.

Health in Orihuela

  • Spain has a universal healthcare system, but foreign visitors may need travel insurance to cover medical expenses.

  • Tap water in Orihuela is safe to drink, but bottled water is also widely available.

Getting to Orihuela: Transportation Options and Logistics

Orihuela enjoys good transportation links, and visitors can arrive by plane, train, or bus. Here are some options to consider:

Airports Near Orihuela

  • Alicante Airport: This is the closest airport to Orihuela, located about 50 km away. Several airlines offer regular flights to and from both domestic and international destinations.

  • Murcia-San Javier Airport: Located about 60 km from Orihuela, this airport offers flights to several European destinations.

Bus Stations Near Orihuela

  • Orihuela Bus Station: This is the main bus station in town, with several daily connections to nearby towns and cities.

  • Alicante Bus Station: Located about 45 km from Orihuela, the Alicante bus station offers connections to many national and international destinations.

Other Options

  • Train: Orihuela has a train station with daily connections to Alicante and other major cities in Spain.

  • Car: Orihuela is easily accessible by road, with several major highways and toll roads connecting it to other parts of the country.

How to Get Around in Orihuela

Orihuela is a relatively small town, and most of its attractions are within walking distance. However, visitors can also use public transportation, taxis, or rental cars to get around:

Public Transportation

Orihuela has a public bus system that connects the town with nearby villages and cities. The buses are affordable and reliable, with several stops throughout the town.

Taxis

Taxis are readily available in Orihuela, and fares are reasonable. Visitors can usually hail a taxi on the street or use ride-hailing apps such as Uber or Cabify.

Rental Cars

Several rental car companies operate in Orihuela, offering a range of vehicles at competitive prices. Driving in Orihuela is relatively easy, and roads are well-maintained.

Where to Go Next from Orihuela: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Orihuela is situated in a scenic region of Spain, with several nearby destinations and excursions to discover. Here are some options:

Torrevieja

A coastal town about 25 km from Orihuela, Torrevieja is famous for its salt flats, beaches, and seafood restaurants.

Elche

A historic city about 30 km from Orihuela, Elche is known for its palm groves, which are a UNESCO World Heritage site. The city also has several museums and cultural attractions.

Murcia

A vibrant university town about 60 km from Orihuela, Murcia offers a mix of historical monuments and modern amenities, including a lively nightlife and shopping scene.

Conclusion

Orihuela is a town that has something for everyone, from nature enthusiasts to history buffs, foodies to culture aficionados. With its picturesque scenery, welcoming locals, and rich heritage, Orihuela offers an authentic Spanish experience that is sure to leave a lasting impression on visitors.
