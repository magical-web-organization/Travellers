Introduction to Tabarca

Located in the Valencian Community of Eastern Spain, Tabarca is a small, picturesque island known for its rich cultural heritage, natural beauty, and scenic vistas. Situated off the coast of Alicante, which is also the name of the province, this island has a fascinating history that dates back to the 18th century when it was founded by Genoese sailors. Today, Tabarca is a popular destination for tourists who flock to its quaint streets, historic buildings, charming cafes, and stunning beaches.

List of Places to Visit in Tabarca

There are many must-see attractions in Tabarca, including cultural landmarks and natural wonders. Here are a few of our top picks:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Torre de San Jose: A 16th-century fortress-turned-museum that showcases the island’s history and cultural heritage.
  • Iglesia de San Pedro y San Pablo: A beautiful 18th-century church with an impressive baroque facade and intricate interior decorations.
  • Casa del Gobernador: A well-preserved 18th-century mansion that once served as the residence of the island’s governor.

Natural Attractions of Tabarca: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Playa de La Nau: A stunning beach that offers crystal clear waters, soft sand, and breathtaking views.
  • Parque Natural de las Salinas: A nature reserve that spans over 2,500 hectares and is home to several rare bird species.
  • Mirador de Tabarca: A popular viewpoint that offers panoramic views of the island and the surrounding sea.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Tabarca

Tabarca offers several scenic trails that are perfect for hiking or biking. Here are a few of our favorites:

  • Camino de San Juan: A 3-kilometer uphill trail that leads to the island’s highest point and offers stunning views at the top.
  • Camino de la Farola: A 5-kilometer trail that takes you on a scenic tour of the island’s lighthouse, cliffs, and rocky coves.
  • Camino de la Cantera: A 3-kilometer trail that passes by the island’s old quarry and offers views of its rugged coastline.

Best Things to Do in Tabarca: Activities and Tours

Whether you’re looking for adventure or relaxation, Tabarca offers plenty of fun activities and tours that cater to different interests. Here are a few ideas:

  • Snorkeling and diving: The island’s clear waters are perfect for exploring its vibrant marine life.
  • Boat tours: Take a boat tour around the island to discover its natural beauty and learn about its history.
  • Sunbathing: Soak up the sun at Tabarca’s beautiful beaches, which offer both quiet coves and bustling spots.

Week-Long Itinerary for Tabarca

Day 1: Arrival and exploration of the island’s historic landmarks and cultural attractions.

Day 2: Outdoor adventure activities such as hiking, biking, or snorkeling.

Day 3: Relaxation and sunbathing at the island’s beaches.

Day 4: Explore the island’s local cuisine and food scene.

Day 5: Boat tour and island hopping to nearby destinations.

Day 6: More outdoor activities and natural attractions.

Day 7: Departure day.

Food and Drink Scene of Tabarca

No trip to Tabarca is complete without indulging in its delicious cuisine and local fare. Here are a few dishes and beverages that you must try:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Calderos de Tabarca: A traditional rice dish made with fish caught off the island’s coast.
  • Guiso de calamares: A hearty squid stew flavored with chorizo, tomato, and red peppers.
  • Paella: A classic Spanish rice dish made with a variety of seafood and vegetables.

Local Beverages

  • Turrones de Tabarca: A sweet almond nougat that’s a local specialty.
  • Vino de la Marina: A regional wine made with grapes grown near the coast.
  • Agua de Valencia: A refreshing cocktail made with orange juice, cava, gin, and vodka.

Where to Stay in Tabarca: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Tabarca offers a range of accommodation options that cater to different budgets and preferences. Here are a few recommendations:

  • Hotel La Trancada: A charming hotel with a beautiful garden and ocean views.
  • Hostal Masin: A cozy hostel located in the heart of the island’s historic district.
  • Apartamentos Tabarca: Self-catering apartments that offer modern amenities and sea views.

What to Buy in Tabarca: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

If you’re looking for the perfect souvenir or gift to bring back from Tabarca, consider these local products:

  • Almond nougat: A traditional sweet that’s made with locally grown almonds.
  • Handcrafted ceramics: The island is known for its beautiful pottery made with traditional techniques.
  • Local wine: Wine made with grapes grown near the coast is a great way to taste the flavors of the region.

Local Culture and Customs of Tabarca

Tabarca has a rich cultural heritage that’s reflected in its architecture, cuisine, and traditions. Visitors are encouraged to respect the island’s customs and way of life, which include:

  • Traditional dress: Locals often wear their traditional costume during festivals and celebrations.
  • Religious festivals: The island celebrates several religious holidays throughout the year, including the feast of Saint Peter and Saint Paul in June.
  • Local language: Valencia, the local dialect, is widely spoken on the island.

Best Time to Visit Tabarca

The best time to visit Tabarca is during the summer months from May to September when the weather is warm and sunny, and the sea is calm. However, keep in mind that this is also the peak tourist season, so the island can be crowded and prices may be higher. If you prefer a quieter atmosphere and cheaper rates, consider visiting during the off-season months of October to April.

Do’s and Don’ts of Tabarca

Do:

  • Respect the local customs and traditions.
  • Try the local cuisine and specialties.
  • Explore the island’s natural beauty and outdoor attractions.

Don’t:

  • Litter or damage the island’s fragile ecosystems.
  • Interrupt or disturb local wildlife.

Safety and Health

Safety in Tabarca

Tabarca is generally a safe destination for tourists as long as you use common sense and take normal precautions. Be aware of your surroundings, particularly in crowded areas, and avoid carrying large amounts of cash or valuables with you.

Health in Tabarca

Travelers to Tabarca should ensure that they are up-to-date on routine vaccinations and take precautions to prevent insect bites, particularly if hiking or exploring the island’s natural areas.

Getting to Tabarca: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Tabarca

The nearest airport to Tabarca is Alicante–Elche Airport, which offers direct flights to several major European cities.

Bus Stations Near Tabarca

From Alicante or Santa Pola, visitors can take a bus to Santa Pola’s port, where they can take a ferry to Tabarca.

Other Options

Another option to travel to Tabarca is via private boat or yacht.

How to Get Around in Tabarca

Tabarca is a small island, so walking or biking are the most popular ways to get around. Alternatively, visitors can rent a scooter or take a taxi.

Where to Go Next from Tabarca: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

If you’ve already explored Tabarca, consider these nearby destinations and excursions:

  • Alicante: a bustling city with a rich history, beautiful beaches, and lively nightlife.
  • Santa Pola: a charming fishing town with a vibrant street market and lovely beaches.
  • Elche: a UNESCO World Heritage site known for its palm groves and impressive medieval architecture.

Conclusion

Whether you’re interested in history, culture, nature, or gastronomy, Tabarca offers something for everyone. With this comprehensive guide, you’ll be able to plan the perfect trip to this charming island and create unforgettable memories that you’ll cherish for years to come.
