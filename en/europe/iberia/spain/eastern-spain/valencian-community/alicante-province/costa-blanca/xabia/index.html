Introduction to Xàbia

Located in the province of Alicante, Valencian Community in Eastern Spain, Xàbia (also known as Jávea) is a coastal town that has a lot to offer visitors. With its pristine beaches, rich history, and stunning natural beauty, it is a must-visit destination for those looking for a unique travel experience.

List of Places to Visit in Xàbia

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  1. Soler Blasco Archaeological and Ethnographic Museum: This museum showcases the rich history and traditions of the region, with exhibits of ancient artifacts and everyday objects.

  2. The Church of San Bartolomé: This beautiful church, built in the 14th century, is a must-see for those interested in architectural history. It features stunning Gothic and Baroque styles and has a tower that provides breathtaking views of the town.

  3. The Old Town: The charming, historic Old Town is a maze of narrow streets and picturesque squares. It houses traditional houses, shops, and restaurants.

Natural Attractions of Xàbia: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  1. Montgó Natural Park: Towering over Xàbia, Montgó Natural Park offers excellent hiking and biking trails. The park is home to rare flora and fauna, and it provides a stunning view of the surrounding landscape.

  2. Granadella Beach: A picturesque cove with crystal clear waters, Granadella Beach is a popular spot for swimming, snorkeling, and sunbathing. A rocky cliff provides a stunning backdrop for this beach.

  3. La Plana Beach: This long stretch of beach is perfect for a day-long excursion. Famous for its crystal clear waters and its fine sand, La Plana is a haven for beachgoers.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Xàbia

  1. Montgó Natural Park: Monte Montgó is the top attraction in Xàbia offering visitors with a number of hiking trails. The treks are available in different lengths, scenic viewpoints and for people of all ages. The path is dense and easy-to-trek which takes about 4-5 hours for an experienced hiker.

  2. Via Verde: A favorite option for biking enthusiasts, Via Verde is a former railway track converted into a biking route. This route leads bikers from Oliva to Xàbia passing across the natural park of the river Girona.

Best Things to Do in Xàbia: Activities and Tours

  1. Kayaking: Take a kayak tour of the stunning coastal caves, secluded beaches, and cliffs of Xàbia guided by professionals in the region.

  2. Scuba Diving: Xàbia is a paradise for scuba diving lovers. With its crystal clear waters, divers can expect to see octopus, barracudas, and a variety of fish. There are also lots of diving schools near the town.

Week-Long Itinerary for Xàbia

Day 1 Arrive in Xàbia - visit the historic Old Town
Day 2 Rent a boat and visit the secluded beaches of Granadella and Portitxol
Day 3 Visit the Soler Blasco Archaeological and Ethnographic Museum - followed by an evening spent in the town’s tapas bars
Day 4 Hike the Montgó Natural Park
Day 5 Visit the local farmer’s market - followed by a traditional lunch of paella
Day 6 Scuba dive in the clear waters of Xàbia
Day 7 Explore the local vineyards and wineries surrounding the town - sampling the local wine

Food and Drink Scene of Xàbia

Must-Try Dishes

  1. Paella: This famous Spanish rice dish originated in Valencia and is a must-try in Xàbia, especially in the Old Town.

  2. Arroz a banda: A coastal specialty, this dish is made with monkfish, squid, and shrimp and is stewed slowly in fish broth and rice.

Local Beverages

  1. Horchata: A drink made from ground almonds or tiger nuts. It is both refreshing and healthy.

  2. Mistela: A sweet dessert wine that is perfect for after dinner.

Where to Stay in Xàbia: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  1. Hotel El Rodat: This 5-star hotel offers luxury accommodations and is located close to the beach.

  2. Parador de Jávea: A historic hotel located in the heart of the town.

  3. Hostal Buena Gente: This cozy guesthouse provides affordable accommodations in the Old Town.

What to Buy in Xàbia: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  1. Ceramics: The town is famous for its ceramics and pottery. The Plaza de la Constitución has several artisan shops selling handmade items.

  2. Wine: Take home a bottle of the local Mistela or Moscatel wine.

Local Culture and Customs of Xàbia

Xàbia is a town with strong ties to its history and culture. Its annual celebration in honor of San Juan, the patron saint of the town is a must-attend event, which takes place every June. Traditional music, firecrackers, and paella are just a few of the highlights of the event.

Best Time to Visit Xàbia

The best time to visit Xàbia is in the summer months, from June to September. The weather is beautiful, the beaches are lively, and there are lots of festivals and events happening in the town.

Do’s and Don’ts of Xàbia

Do:

  1. Wear sunscreen and a hat when visiting the beach.

  2. Respect local customs and traditions.

Don’t:

  1. Litter on the beach.

  2. Ignore the local dress codes.

Safety and Health

Safety in Xàbia

Xàbia is a safe town for visitors. However, it is always best to keep an eye on your belongings and take normal precautions when exploring the town.

Health in Xàbia

Spain has a public healthcare system which is free for EU citizens. All other visitors will need private health insurance.

Getting to Xàbia: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Xàbia

The nearest airport to Xàbia is Alicante Airport (ALC), which is approximately 100km away. There is a regular bus service from the airport to the town center.

Bus Stations Near Xàbia

The town has a modern bus station located approximately 1 km from the town center. There are regular bus services connecting Xàbia to other major Spanish cities such as Valencia and Barcelona.

Other Options

The town is also accessible by car, with free street parking available throughout the town centre and by the beach.

How to Get Around in Xàbia

The best way to get around Xàbia is on foot or by bike. The town is relatively small, so it’s easy to explore on foot. Renting a bike is a great option to explore the surrounding areas and the nearby natural park of Montgó.

Where to Go Next from Xàbia: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  1. Denia: A charming port town located to the north of Xàbia. This town offers great shopping, beaches, and a bustling marina.

  2. Valencia: The third-largest city in Spain, located approximately 110km north of Xàbia. Valencia boasts remarkable architecture, history, culture and is home to the futuristic City of Arts and Sciences.

Conclusion

Xàbia is a charming coastal town that offers visitors a unique travel experience. With its rich history, culture, stunning natural beauty, and array of things to do and see, this town is a must-see destination in Eastern Spain. From kayaking to Scuba diving, from local cuisine to pottery, Xàbia is an unforgettable destination perfect for a week-long trip, or a day-trip from nearby cities.
