Introduction to Altea

Nestled in Costa Blanca, in the province of Alicante, lies the picturesque town of Altea. Known for its charming old town, white-washed houses, blue-domed church, and stunning views of the Mediterranean, Altea is a hidden gem waiting to be explored.

List of Places to Visit in Altea

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Museo Parroquial de la Iglesia de Nuestra Señora del Consuelo: Explore the history of Altea and its patron saint at this museum located inside the church of Nuestra Señora del Consuelo.
  • Capilla de la Ocre: This 18th-century chapel is a testament to Altea’s rich architectural and religious history. Admire its Baroque-style façade and ornate interior.
  • Torre de la Galera: Once a defensive tower built in the 16th century to protect Altea from pirate attacks, today, it houses art exhibitions and cultural events.

Natural Attractions of Altea: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Mirador Cronistas de España: Take in stunning views of the Mediterranean and the surrounding mountains from this scenic viewpoint.
  • Platja de la Roda: Known for its calm waters and sandy beaches, Platja de la Roda is a popular spot for swimming and sunbathing.
  • Sierra de Bernia: This mountain range offers a range of hiking trails with breathtaking views of the coastline.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Altea

  • Sendero de la Sierra de Bernia: This 10-kilometer hiking trail leads adventurers through the stunning Sierra de Bernia mountain range.
  • Carrer de la Verge del Consol: Take a leisurely bike ride through Altea’s old town and admire its beautiful architecture and quaint charm.
  • Sierra Helada National Park: Another great spot for hiking and biking, the Sierra Helada National Park offers visitors the chance to explore a diverse range of flora and fauna.

Best Things to Do in Altea: Activities and Tours

  • Water Sports: Enjoy a range of water sports like kayaking, paddleboarding, and windsurfing at one of Altea’s many beaches.
  • Wine Tasting: Visit a local winery and sample some of the region’s best wines.
  • Hot Air Balloon Ride: Take a magical hot air balloon ride and enjoy stunning views of Altea from above.

Food and Drink Scene of Altea

Must-Try Dishes

  • Paella: A classic Spanish dish made with rice, saffron, and a variety of seafood or meat.
  • Arroz a banda: A flavorful dish of rice cooked in fish broth and served with garlic and aioli.
  • Tostada con tomate: A typical breakfast dish of toasted bread with tomato and olive oil.

Local Beverages

  • Mistela: A sweet liquor made from grape must and aged for several years.
  • Moscatell: A sweet dessert wine made from Muscat grapes.

Where to Stay in Altea: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Hotel Boutique La Serena: A luxury hotel with stunning sea views and modern amenities.
  • Hostal Fornet Altea: A charming guesthouse located in the heart of the old town.
  • Hostal El Trovador: A budget-friendly option that offers comfortable rooms and a central location.

What to Buy in Altea: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Ceramics: Altea is known for its traditional pottery, including colorful plates, vases, and tiles.
  • Handmade Soap: Local artisans craft handmade soaps using natural ingredients like olive oil and lavender.
  • Wine: Bring home a bottle of the region’s best wines as a delicious souvenir of your trip.

Local Culture and Customs of Altea

Altea is a charming and traditional town that values its heritage and customs. Visitors to Altea should respect its history and traditions, including the siesta, which is typically observed from 2 pm to 5 pm when many shops and businesses close.

Best Time to Visit Altea

The best time to visit Altea is during the spring (March to May) or fall (September to November) when temperatures are mild, and crowds are smaller. However, visitors can enjoy the beaches and water sports during the summer months (June to August) when temperatures are hotter but can be crowded.

Do’s and Don’ts of Altea

Do:

  • Respect local customs and traditions.
  • Wear comfortable shoes for walking on cobbled streets.
  • Try the local cuisine and wines.

Don’t:

  • Be loud or disrespectful in public places.
  • Leave valuables unattended on the beach or in public areas.
  • Ignore red flags on beaches, which indicate dangerous water conditions.

Safety and Health

Safety in Altea

Altea is a safe town, but visitors should take precautions to avoid theft or pickpocketing in crowded areas.

Health in Altea

Altea has an excellent healthcare system, with modern hospitals and clinics that cater to both locals and visitors.

Getting to Altea: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Altea

  • Alicante-Elche Airport (ALC) is the closest international airport, located about 70 kilometers from Altea.

Bus Stations Near Altea

  • Alsa operates bus services to Altea from nearby cities like Valencia and Alicante.

Other Options

  • Visitors can also reach Altea by train, with services operated by Renfe from nearby cities.

How to Get Around in Altea

The best way to explore Altea is by foot, as the town’s winding streets and alleys are ideal for a leisurely stroll. Alternatively, visitors can rent a bike or scooter to explore nearby areas.

Where to Go Next from Altea: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Valencia: Spain’s third-largest city is only a few hours’ drive from Altea and is known for its stunning architecture, vibrant nightlife, and delicious local cuisine.
  • Calpe: This fishing village on the Costa Blanca is a short drive from Altea and is home to stunning natural beauty, including the iconic Penyal d’Ifac rock formation.
  • Guadalest: This charming hilltop village is a short drive from Altea and is known for its stunning views, historic castle, and traditional craft shops.

Conclusion

Whether you’re looking to soak up some sun, explore the natural beauty of Costa Blanca, or immerse yourself in Altea’s rich cultural heritage and traditions, this charming town has something for everyone. With our comprehensive travel guide, you have everything you need to plan the ultimate trip to Altea, Spain.
