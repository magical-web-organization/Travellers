Introduction to Calpe

Calpe, located in the province of Alicante in Eastern Spain, is a picturesque coastal town that attracts both locals and tourists alike. Known for its stunning beaches, towering rock formations, and historic landmarks, Calpe is a must-visit destination for those seeking sun, sand, and sea.

List of Places to Visit in Calpe

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • The Calpe Museum: This museum houses an impressive collection of artifacts that detail the town’s rich history, including its origins as a fishing village.
  • The Olta Tower: This 16th-century watchtower played a crucial role in protecting the town from invading pirates and is a must-visit for history buffs.
  • La Fossa Beach Promenade: Take a stroll along this beautiful promenade lined with shops, cafes, and restaurants, and take in the stunning views of the Mediterranean Sea.

Natural Attractions of Calpe: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Ifach Rock: This towering rock formation is the most iconic natural landmark of Calpe. Visitors can hike to the top for breathtaking views of the coastline and the town below.
  • Arenal-Bol Beach: This beautiful beach is perfect for sunbathing, swimming, and water sports, and offers stunning views of the Ifach Rock.
  • Salinas de Calpe: These salt flats are home to a variety of bird species and offer a unique opportunity to observe wildlife in their natural habitat.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Calpe

  • Ifach Rock Hike: This challenging hike to the top of Ifach Rock is a must-do for those seeking adventure and stunning views.
  • Sierra de Oltá Trail: This trail offers stunning views of Calpe and the surrounding area and is perfect for hiking and mountain biking enthusiasts.

Best Things to Do in Calpe: Activities and Tours

  • Water Sports: Calpe offers a range of water sports such as jet skiing, paddleboarding, and kayaking for visitors seeking adventure.
  • Wine Tasting: Take a guided tour of the Bodegas Enrique Mendoza vineyard and enjoy some of the best wines of the region.
  • Visit the Fish Market: The Calpe fish market is a must-visit for seafood lovers. Visitors can watch the fishermen bring in their catch of the day and purchase fresh seafood to enjoy at home.

Food and Drink Scene of Calpe

Must-Try Dishes

  • Paella: No trip to Spain is complete without trying this iconic dish, and Calpe serves some of the best paellas in the region.
  • Arroz al Horno: This delicious baked rice dish is a perfect comfort food on a chilly evening and is a local favorite.
  • Fideuá: This dish, similar to paella but made with noodles instead of rice, is a must-try for those seeking a unique culinary experience.

Local Beverages

  • Horchata: This popular regional drink is made from tiger nuts and is a refreshing alternative to coffee or sodas.
  • Wines: The Alicante region is known for its excellent wines, and Calpe offers an opportunity to sample some of the best ones.

Where to Stay in Calpe: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Hotel AR Diamante Beach: This luxurious hotel is located just a short walk from Arenal-Bol Beach and offers stunning sea views and excellent amenities.
  • Hostal La Paloma: This charming guesthouse in the heart of the old town is perfect for budget-conscious travelers and offers a warm and welcoming atmosphere.
  • Gran Hotel Sol y Mar: This elegant hotel offers stunning sea views from its rooftop pool and is perfect for those seeking a luxurious beachfront stay.

What to Buy in Calpe: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Handmade Ceramics: Calpe is known for its beautiful handmade ceramics, including plates, mugs, and vases.
  • Olive Oil: The region produces some of the best olive oils in Spain, and you can purchase high-quality bottles to bring back home.
  • Leather Products: The region is known for its excellent leather products, including shoes, belts, and bags.

Local Culture and Customs of Calpe

Calpe’s cultural heritage is tied to its maritime roots and its history as a fishing village. Visitors can observe traditional fishing techniques and explore important cultural landmarks such as the old town and the Calpe Museum.

Best Time to Visit Calpe

The best time to visit Calpe is between May and October when the weather is warm and sunny, and the beaches are in full swing. However, to avoid the crowds, visit in the shoulder months of April, May, September, and October.

Do’s and Don’ts of Calpe

Do:

  • Visit the local markets to get a taste of the town’s fresh produce and seafood.
  • Try the local wines and food specialties like the traditional paella and horchata.
  • Respect the locals and their customs.

Don’t:

  • Leave your valuables unattended on the beach or in public areas.
  • Take photographs of people without their permission.
  • Be disrespectful toward the local culture and customs.

Safety and Health

Safety in Calpe

Overall, Calpe is a safe destination for travelers. However, as with any tourist destination, visitors should take care of their belongings and avoid walking alone at night.

Health in Calpe

Visitors should be aware of the risks associated with the strong Spanish sun and ensure they stay appropriately hydrated and wear sunscreen. Visitors should also be vigilant against mosquitoes and ticks during the summer months.

Getting to Calpe: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Calpe

The nearest airport is Alicante Airport, which is served by many airlines all year round.

Bus Stations Near Calpe

Calpe has its own bus station, which is connected to many other cities in Spain.

Other Options

Visitors can also reach Calpe by train or car.

How to Get Around in Calpe

Calpe is a small town and can easily be explored on foot or by bicycle. For those seeking a more leisurely way to see the town, there are regular tourist train services that run during the summer months.

Where to Go Next from Calpe: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Alicante: A vibrant coastal city with rich cultural heritage, beautiful beaches and excellent shopping and dining options.
  • Benidorm: A bustling resort town with stunning beaches, colorful nightlife, and numerous water sports activities.

Conclusion

Calpe is an excellent destination for visitors seeking sun, sand, and sea, and a rich cultural heritage. With its stunning beaches, outdoor adventures, and authentic local experiences, it is a must-visit destination for those looking to experience the best of Eastern Spain.
