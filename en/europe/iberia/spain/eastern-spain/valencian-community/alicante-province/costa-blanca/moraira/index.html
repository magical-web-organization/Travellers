Moraira is a charming coastal town located in Costa Blanca, Alicante, in the Valencian Community of Eastern Spain. This small paradise offers visitors a quiet and peaceful sanctuary with stunning views of the Mediterranean Sea, pristine beaches, charming historical sites, and a flourishing food scene. Whether you’re looking for a relaxing holiday or an exciting adventure, Moraira has something to offer. In this comprehensive travel guide, we will explore the best places to visit, things to do, and much more.

Introduction to Moraira

Moraira sets itself apart from other busy Spanish resorts, with its beautiful architecture, friendly locals, and relaxed atmosphere. The town is situated between the two major cities of Alicante and Valencia, within easy reach by car or public transportation. Moraira is blessed with ample sunshine throughout the year, which makes it an appealing destination for those seeking a beach holiday.

List of Places to Visit in Moraira

Moraira is a small town with plenty of historical sites, natural beauty, and scenic spots. Here are some must-visit places:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • The Castle of Moraira: A fortress built-in 1742 to defend the coast from pirate attacks that now serves as a cultural center.

  • The Parish Church of Our Lady of the Forsaken: A beautiful church located in the heart of Moraira, dating back to the 18th century.

  • Teulada Christ of the Good Death Church: A church that preserves the essence of the town’s religious tradition, dating back to the 17th century.

Natural Attractions of Moraira: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • The Moraira Beach: The town has beautiful beaches and coves, such as Playa del Portet, L’Andragó, and Les Platgetes, which offer clean waters and ample space to relax or engage in water sports like paddleboarding, kayaking, and snorkeling.

  • Cap D’or Watchtower: A watchtower built-in 1553 and located on a cliff, offering breathtaking views of the sea, mountains, and the town itself.

  • The Moraira Castle: A beautiful fortification built-in 1742, which now also serves as a cultural center.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Moraira

The town has several hiking and cycling routes for those who enjoy outdoor activities, including:

  • The Montgo Nature Park: A nature reserve between the towns of Denia and Jávea, featuring scenic trails, incredible mountain views, and interesting wildlife.

  • The Greenway Path: A disused railway line converted into a hiking route, making for a pleasant 6.5km walk while taking in the stunning views.

  • Cala Moraig to Granadella Beach Hike: This beautiful coastal route stretches for seven kilometers and connects the towns of Moraira and Jávea. You’ll get to explore secluded bays, stunning nature parks, and experience the best views of the famous coastline.

Best Things to Do in Moraira: Activities and Tours

Moraira provides a range of activities and tours for visitors to enjoy, such as:

  • Water Sports Activities: From sailing to paddleboarding, Moraira offers plenty of water sports for all age groups.

  • Wine Tours: Explore the town’s wine culture by visiting local wineries and vineyards.

  • Sailing and Boat Tours: Take in the stunning Mediterranean coastline aboard a luxurious catamaran or traditional boat.

Week-Long Itinerary for Moraira

Here is a daily breakdown of a weeklong itinerary when visiting Moraira:

Day 1: Arrival and Explore Moraira’s Historic Center

Check into your hotel and take a stroll through the old town, visit the Church of Our Lady of the Forsaken, and view the historic fortifications of the castle.

Day 2: Day Trip to Calpe

Drive or take a boat trip to Calpe and witness the incredible Calcó rock formation and enjoy local cuisine.

Day 3: Beach Day and Diving

Spend the day on one of the beautiful beaches of Moraira and dive into the Mediterranean’s crystal-clear waters and try Scuba Diving.

Day 4: Montgo Nature Park Hike

Embark on a day-long hike along the Montgo Nature Park and take in breathtaking views of the Mediterranean Sea.

Day 5: Javea Day Trip and Watersports

Take a day trip to Javea, enjoy the water sports and various activities on Arenal Beach, and savor the local delicacies.

Day 6: Wine Tasting Tour

Explore Moraira’s local wine culture with a wine tour of the town’s top wineries and vineyards.

Day 7: Sailing and Boat Tour

Take a boat tour and explore the Mediterranean coastline, observe the sea life, and experience adventure sports.

Food and Drink Scene of Moraira

The food scene in Moraira is exciting and diverse. Here are some local dishes and drinks to try out:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Paella: A Spanish rice dish with seafood or meat, cooked in a large shallow pan with broth.

  • Arroz a banda: Rice cooked in a fish stock made up of cuttlefish, crabs, and other seafood.

  • Gambas al Ajillo: Stir-fried shrimp with garlic.

Local Beverages

  • Horchata de chufa: A popular drink made of tiger nuts, sugar, and water.

  • Fino Sherry: A dry, light sherry wine made of Palomino grapes.

Local Culture and Customs of Moraira

Respectful behavior in Moraira is highly appreciated. It’s essential to adhere to the following etiquette:

  • Dress modestly for entering religious sites.

  • Respect local customs and traditions.

  • Do not over-indulge in alcohol.

Best Time to Visit Moraira

The best time to visit Moraira is from May to October, when temperatures are at their warmest, and you can enjoy outdoor activities without worrying about rain.

Do’s and Don’ts of Moraira

When visiting Moraira, keep these Do’s and Don’ts in mind:

Do:

  • Learn a little bit of Spanish.

  • Experience the town’s amazing food scene.

  • Visit the historical sites, museums, and churches.

Don’t:

  • Don’t be too loud in public places.

  • Avoid taking pictures of locals without permission.

  • Avoid drinking water from public fountains.

Safety and Health

Moraira is generally very safe to visit. However, visitors should take precautions against pickpockets in crowded areas.

Safety in Moraira

  • Keep an eye on your belongings.

  • Avoid carrying important documents or large sums of money around.

Health in Moraira

  • Make sure to drink bottled water.

  • Always use sunscreen when going out in the sun.

Getting to Moraira: Transportation Options and Logistics

Moraira is easily accessible by public transportation or car from major cities in Spain.

Airports Near Moraira

  • Alicante Airport (ALC): Approximately 79 km away.

  • Valencia Airport (VLC): Approximately 120 km away.

Bus Stations Near Moraira

  • Teulada Bus Station- 4km away from Moraira

  • Jávea Bus Station-10km from Moraira.

Other Options

  • Car Rental: Renting a car is an option for those who prefer to be independent and explore the area at their own pace.

How to Get Around in Moraira

Moraira is a small town, and you can easily get around on foot. Alternatively, renting a bike or a scooter is a fantastic way to explore the area.

Where to Go Next from Moraira: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Moraira is conveniently located to enable day excursions to the neighboring towns of Javea, Calpe, and Denia, to name a few.

Conclusion

Moraira is a charming town that offers a unique blend of natural beauty, historical sites, and a rich food culture. If you’re looking for a relaxing or adventurous holiday in the Mediterranean, Moraira is a destination worth exploring. This comprehensive guide provides a starting point to plan your Moraira trip and discover all that this charming coastal town has to offer.
