Alsace-Lorraine may be a small region in Europe, but it can offer a lot to curious travelers. Located in the northeastern part of France, between the Vosges Mountains and the Rhine River, this region boasts a unique blend of French and German cultures, stunning landscapes, sublime food and drinks, and historical treasures. To help you plan your trip and make the most out of it, here’s a comprehensive tour guide for Alsace-Lorraine.

Introduction to Alsace-Lorraine

Alsace-Lorraine is a region with a complicated history. It has been the subject of territorial disputes between France and Germany for centuries, resulting in a mix of architectural styles, cultural traditions, and languages. Today, the region is officially part of France. However, locals speak a distinct Alsatian dialect, and German influences are still present in the cuisine, art, and daily life.

Alsace-Lorraine is relatively easy to explore as it covers only around 14,500 square kilometers. You can either base yourself in Strasbourg, the capital city of the region and a UNESCO World Heritage Site, or in smaller towns such as Colmar, Mulhouse, or Metz.

List of Places to Visit in Alsace-Lorraine

Here are some of the top places to see and experience in Alsace-Lorraine:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Strasbourg Cathedral: This Gothic masterpiece has been the tallest building in the world for over two centuries. Its pink sandstone façade, intricate sculptures, and stained glass windows are awe-inspiring.
  • Haut-Koenigsbourg Castle: This medieval fortress offers panoramic views of the Alsace plain and the Black Forest. It was extensively restored in the early 20th century and now houses a museum showcasing the history of the castle and the region.
  • Musée Unterlinden: Located in Colmar, this museum features a vast collection of religious art, including the famous Isenheim Altarpiece.
  • Strasbourg’s Petite France: This charming neighborhood with half-timbered houses, canals, and flower-decked balconies is the historic heart of Strasbourg. It’s perfect for leisurely strolls, sipping coffee, and people-watching.

Natural Attractions of Alsace-Lorraine: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Vosges Mountains: This mountain range offers hiking and skiing opportunities, picturesque villages, and stunning viewpoints.
  • Alsace Wine Route: This 170-kilometer road takes you through the region’s vineyards, wine cellars, and quaint towns. You can taste some of the world-famous Alsatian wines such as Riesling, Gewürztraminer, and Pinot Gris.
  • Lorraine Regional Nature Park: This protected area covers over 200,000 hectares of forests, grasslands, and wetlands. It’s home to rare animal and plant species, scenic lakes, and historic sites such as the Abbey of Orval.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Alsace-Lorraine

Alsace-Lorraine is a paradise for outdoor enthusiasts. Here are some of the best trails and bike routes:

  • Grand Ballon: This 1,424-meter peak is the highest in the Vosges Mountains. You can hike or bike to the summit for breathtaking vistas.
  • La Bruche Canal Bike Trail: This 31-kilometer route takes you along the peaceful Bruche Canal, passing through forests, fields, and charming villages.
  • Plan d’Eau du Stock Lake: This artificial lake near Sarrebourg is an excellent spot for fishing, boating, and cycling. You can rent bicycles and explore the surrounding countryside.

Best Things to Do in Alsace-Lorraine: Activities and Tours

Besides hiking and biking, there are plenty of other exciting things to do in Alsace-Lorraine. Here are some ideas:

  • Take a boat ride on the Ill River in Strasbourg and learn about the city’s history and architecture.
  • Visit the local Christmas markets in December and taste some gingerbread, mulled wine, and handmade crafts.
  • Explore the traditional pottery workshops in Betschdorf and Soufflenheim. You can observe the artisans at work and buy some unique souvenirs.
  • Join a cooking class and learn how to make Alsatian specialties such as choucroute, tarte flambée, and baeckeoffe.

Week-Long Itinerary for Alsace-Lorraine

To make the most of your trip, here’s a suggested week-long itinerary for Alsace-Lorraine:

  • Day 1-2: Strasbourg and surroundings – visit the Cathedral, the Petite France, the European Parliament, and take a boat ride on the Ill River.
  • Day 3-4: Colmar and the Wine Route – explore the Unterlinden Museum, the Bartholdi Museum, and taste some Alsatian wines.
  • Day 5-6: Vosges Mountains and Haut-Koenigsbourg Castle – hike to the Grand Ballon, visit the castle, and experience the local folklore.
  • Day 7: Metz and Lorraine – discover the Saint-Etienne Cathedral, the Pompidou-Metz Museum, and the city’s Art Nouveau heritage.

Food and Drink Scene of Alsace-Lorraine

Alsatian cuisine is hearty, flavorful, and influenced by both French and German traditions. Here are some must-try dishes and beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Choucroute: This dish consists of fermented cabbage, sausages, meat, and potatoes. It’s a staple of Alsatian cuisine and perfect for chilly days.
  • Flammekueche: Also known as tarte flambée, this thin pizza-like dish is topped with crème fraiche, onions, and bacon.
  • Baeckeoffe: This casserole dish contains layers of marinated meats, potatoes, and vegetables, slow-cooked in white wine.
  • Kugelhopf: This sweet yeast cake with raisins and almonds is a popular breakfast treat in Alsace.

Local Beverages

  • Riesling: This dry white wine made from the Riesling grape is the most famous Alsatian wine. It’s crisp, acidic, and goes well with fish and seafood.
  • Gewürztraminer: This aromatic white wine has a fruity and spicy flavor and pairs well with Asian or Middle Eastern dishes.
  • Bière de Mars: This seasonal beer is brewed in March and has a fruity and herbal taste.

Where to Stay in Alsace-Lorraine: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Alsace-Lorraine has a broad range of accommodations, from luxury hotels to budget-friendly hostels. Here are some suggestions:

  • Hotel Hannong, Strasbourg: This elegant hotel is located in the city center and offers spacious rooms, a sauna, and a rooftop terrace.
  • Hostel Meisenthal, Meisenthal: This cozy hostel is set in a former glass factory and features dorms or private rooms, a communal kitchen, and a sauna.
  • Les Loges du Ried, Colmar: This guesthouse is housed in a restored farmhouse and has charming rooms, a garden, and a pool.

What to Buy in Alsace-Lorraine: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

When in Alsace-Lorraine, you can find many unique products and souvenirs to take back home, such as:

  • Alsatian wine: Choose from a variety of white, red, or rosé wines produced in the region.
  • Quetsch plum brandy: This eau-de-vie distilled from local plums has a fruity and velvety flavor.
  • Alsatian pottery: Visit the workshops in Betschdorf or Soufflenheim and buy some traditional pottery such as pitchers, vases, or plates.
  • Christmas ornaments: Alsace is known for its beautiful Christmas markets, where you can buy handmade ornaments such as glass baubles, wooden figurines, or candles.

Local Culture and Customs of Alsace-Lorraine

Alsace-Lorraine has a rich cultural heritage, reflected in its architecture, art, and traditions. Here are some customs to keep in mind:

  • Greetings: In small towns and villages, it’s customary to say “Guten Tag” (good day) or “Bonjour” (hello) to strangers.
  • Language: Although French is the official language, Alsatian dialect is widely spoken, and some locals may prefer it over French or English.
  • Cuisine: Alsatians take their food seriously and often gather around the table for long meals with family and friends. Don’t be shy to ask for second helpings or try new dishes.
  • Festivals: Alsace-Lorraine has many festivals throughout the year, such as the wine festivals, the Christmas markets, or the carnival of Mulhouse.

Best Time to Visit Alsace-Lorraine

Alsace-Lorraine is a year-round destination, but the best time to visit depends on your preferences. If you enjoy outdoor activities, spring and summer are ideal for hiking, cycling, and wine tasting. For ski lovers, winter is the peak season for hitting the slopes. The autumn is a popular time for wine cellar tours and harvest celebrations.

Do’s and Don’ts of Alsace-Lorraine

Here are some do’s and don’ts to keep in mind when traveling to Alsace-Lorraine:

Do:

  • Try the local cuisine and wines.
  • Learn some basic French or German phrases.
  • Explore the small villages and towns for a glimpse of authentic Alsatian life.
  • Be respectful of the local customs and traditions.

Don’t:

  • Be loud or disruptive in public places.
  • Leave without trying some traditional pastries or desserts.
  • Wear inappropriate clothing (such as revealing outfits in churches or historic sites).
  • Ignore the traffic rules or parking restrictions.

Safety and Health

Alsace-Lorraine is a safe region to visit, with a low crime rate and good healthcare facilities. However, it’s always advisable to take some precautions:

Safety in Alsace-Lorraine

  • Keep your valuables in a secure place and avoid showing off expensive items.
  • Be cautious when using public transport, especially at night.
  • Follow the safety rules when participating in outdoor activities such as hiking or skiing.
  • Keep emergency numbers and addresses handy.

Health in Alsace-Lorraine

  • Bring any necessary medication with you and keep them in their original packaging.
  • Drink bottled or boiled water to avoid stomach issues.
  • Wear sunscreen and hats to avoid sunburns or heatstroke.
  • Look out for ticks when hiking or camping, as they can transmit Lyme disease.

Getting to Alsace-Lorraine: Transportation Options and Logistics

Alsace-Lorraine is well-connected to other parts of Europe, and getting there is easy.

Airports Near Alsace-Lorraine

  • Strasbourg International Airport: This airport has direct flights to several European destinations such as London, Berlin, and Amsterdam.
  • EuroAirport Basel-Mulhouse-Freiburg: This airport is located in France but also serves parts of Switzerland and Germany. It has many connections with major European cities such as Paris, Madrid, and Rome.

Bus Stations Near Alsace-Lorraine

  • Flixbus and Eurolines operate regular bus services to Strasbourg and other cities in Alsace-Lorraine from major European cities such as Frankfurt, Munich, and Paris.
  • Local bus companies such as CTS (Compagnie des Transports Strasbourgeois) connect Strasbourg to surrounding towns and villages.

Other Options

  • Train: The region is well-connected by train, with major stations in Strasbourg, Colmar, and Metz. You can take high-speed trains from Paris or Munich to Strasbourg in less than two hours.
  • Car: Alsace-Lorraine is easily accessible by car, and driving around the region is a great way to explore the countryside. You can rent a car at the airports or train stations.

How to Get Around in Alsace-Lorraine

Alsace-Lorraine has a reliable and affordable transport system, including:

  • Trains: The TER (Transport Express Régional) provides regular train services between major cities and towns in the region.
  • Buses: Local bus companies such as CTS offer bus services for urban transportation, while numerous private companies offer intercity buses.
  • Trams: Strasbourg has an extensive tram network that covers the whole city and its surroundings.
  • Bikes: You can rent bicycles in Strasbourg and other towns and explore the region on two wheels.

Where to Go Next from Alsace-Lorraine: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Alsace-Lorraine is located in a strategic location between France, Germany, and Switzerland. Here are some nearby destinations you can explore:

  • Black Forest (Germany): This vast forested area is only a short drive from the Vosges Mountains and offers stunning scenery, hiking trails, and quaint towns such as Freiburg and Baden-Baden.
  • Basel (Switzerland): This cosmopolitan city on the banks of the Rhine River is renowned for its museums, art galleries, and architecture. You can also explore the nearby Swiss Alps and the picturesque towns of Lucerne or Interlaken.
  • Luxembourg City (Luxembourg): This small but charming capital city is a blend of French and German influences, with its medieval fortifications and elegant boulevards. You can also visit the scenic villages of the Ardennes region or the wine country of Moselle.

Conclusion

Alsace-Lorraine is a unique and fascinating region that offers something for everyone. Whether you’re interested in history, culture, nature, food, or adventure, you’ll find plenty of things to see and do here. Use this comprehensive tour guide to plan your trip, and make memories that will last a lifetime. Bon voyage!
