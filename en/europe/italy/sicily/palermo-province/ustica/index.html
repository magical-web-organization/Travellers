Introduction to Ustica

Ustica is a small, crescent-shaped island located off the northwestern coast of Sicily, Italy. With a population of just over 1,200 inhabitants, it’s the perfect destination for travelers seeking a quiet, peaceful escape from the hustle and bustle of big cities. Its pristine turquoise waters, glistening sunsets, and unspoiled coastline make it a paradise for beach lovers. It’s also home to some of the best diving and snorkeling spots in Sicily, with a diverse range of marine life and underwater caves waiting to be explored.

List of Places to Visit in Ustica

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Ustica Civic Museum - Located in the heart of the island’s historic center, this museum offers a glimpse into Ustica’s rich cultural history, including its Phoenician and Roman origins.

  • Tower of Santa Maria - One of Ustica’s iconic landmarks, this 16th-century watchtower provides panoramic views of the island’s coastline and is a popular spot for sunset-watching.

  • Catacombs of St. Bartolomeo - Discovered in the early 19th century, these catacombs showcase Ustica’s early Christian history, with a series of underground tombs and religious artwork.

Natural Attractions of Ustica: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Grotta Azzurra - This magical sea cave glows blue with the reflection of the sun’s rays and is best experienced by boat tour or snorkeling.

  • Punta Spalmatore - A remote, unspoiled beach located on the island’s western coast, offering a peaceful escape for sunbathing and swimming.

  • Monte Guardia dei Turchi - A challenging hiking trail that leads to stunning 360-degree panoramic views of the island and surrounding sea.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Ustica

Ustica offers a range of hiking and biking trails that allow visitors to explore the island’s natural beauty and spectacular views. Some of the most popular routes include the Monte Guardia dei Turchi trail, the Punta Cavazzi trail, and the Grotta del Bue Marino trail.

Best Things to Do in Ustica: Activities and Tours

  • Diving and snorkeling - With some of the clearest waters in the Mediterranean, Ustica is a paradise for underwater enthusiasts, with a diverse range of marine life and underwater caves to explore.

  • Boat tours - Discover the island’s hidden coves and sea caves, including the majestic Grotta Azzurra.

  • Wine tasting - The island has a small but growing wine industry, with several wineries offering tastings and tours.

Food and Drink Scene of Ustica

Must-Try Dishes

  • Pasta con le sarde - A traditional Sicilian pasta dish made with sardines, fennel, raisins, and pine nuts.

  • Caponata - A savory eggplant stew made with tomatoes, onions, capers, and olives.

  • Cassata siciliana - A colorful, sweet cake made with sponge cake, ricotta cheese, candied fruits, and marzipan.

Local Beverages

  • Malvasia delle Lipari - A sweet dessert wine made from grapes grown on the nearby Aeolian Islands.

  • Arrancione - A refreshing, citrusy liqueur made from the island’s oranges.

  • Granita di limone - A slushy, lemon-flavored dessert often enjoyed on hot summer days.

Where to Stay in Ustica: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Ustica offers a variety of accommodations, from budget-friendly hostels to luxury hotels. Some of the most popular options include:

  • Hotel Clelia - A charming, family-run hotel located in the heart of Ustica town.

  • Hotel Ustica Punta Spalmatore - A modern, beachfront hotel with breathtaking views of the sea.

  • Hostel Ustica - A budget-friendly option for backpackers and solo travelers, located just a short walk from the town center.

What to Buy in Ustica: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Ceramics and pottery - Ustica has a long tradition of pottery-making, with several local artisans producing unique and beautiful pieces.

  • Malvasia wine - A sweet dessert wine made from grapes grown on the nearby Aeolian Islands.

  • Local honey and olive oil - Made with the island’s own flora and fauna, these products make for delicious souvenirs.

Local Culture and Customs of Ustica

Ustica is a proud and close-knit community, with a strong tradition of fishing and seafaring. Visitors should respect the island’s customs and traditions, including its religious festivals and cultural celebrations. The island is also known for its unique dialect, which is a mix of Sicilian and Arabic.

Best Time to Visit Ustica

The best time to visit Ustica is between May and October, when the weather is warm and sunny, and the sea is calm. July and August can be particularly busy, as this is peak tourist season, so travelers looking for a more peaceful experience may want to consider visiting in May, June, or September.

Do’s and Don’ts of Ustica

Do:

  • Respect the island’s customs and traditions.

  • Try the local cuisine and wine.

  • Take care of the environment, particularly when diving or snorkeling.

Don’t:

  • Leave trash on the beach or in the water.

  • Ignore safety rules when diving or snorkeling.

  • Disturb the island’s marine life.

Safety and Health

Safety in Ustica

Ustica is generally a safe and peaceful destination, but visitors should take precautions to ensure a safe trip, including:

  • Avoiding isolated or unfamiliar areas at night.

  • Keeping valuable possessions out of sight.

  • Being cautious when swimming or engaging in water activities.

Health in Ustica

There are no major health risks in Ustica, but visitors should be aware that medical facilities on the island are limited. Travelers with pre-existing health conditions should consult with a doctor before visiting, and those taking prescription medication should bring enough supplies for the duration of their trip.

Getting to Ustica: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Ustica

The closest airports to Ustica are:

  • Falcone Borsellino Airport - Located in Palermo, Sicily, approximately 80 km from Ustica. From the airport, travelers can take a taxi or shuttle to the nearby port of Palermo, and then take a ferry or hydrofoil to Ustica.

  • Vincenzo Florio Airport - Located in Trapani, Sicily, approximately 125 km from Ustica. From the airport, travelers can take a taxi or shuttle to the nearby port of Trapani, and then take a ferry or hydrofoil to Ustica.

Bus Stations Near Ustica

There are several bus stations near Ustica, including:

  • Palermo Bus Station - Located in the center of Palermo, Sicily, approximately 80 km from Ustica. From the bus station, travelers can take a taxi or shuttle to the nearby port of Palermo, and then take a ferry or hydrofoil to Ustica.

  • Trapani Bus Station - Located in the center of Trapani, Sicily, approximately 125 km from Ustica. From the bus station, travelers can take a taxi or shuttle to the nearby port of Trapani, and then take a ferry or hydrofoil to Ustica.

Other Options

Travelers can also reach Ustica via ferry or hydrofoil from the nearby ports of Palermo and Trapani.

How to Get Around in Ustica

Ustica is a small island that can easily be explored on foot or by bike. Travelers can also rent a scooter or car to explore the island at their leisure.

Where to Go Next from Ustica: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Palermo - Explore the capital city of Sicily, with its stunning architecture, vibrant street markets, and rich cultural history.

  • Aeolian Islands - Take a ferry or hydrofoil to the nearby Aeolian Islands, a chain of volcanic islands known for their rugged beauty and picturesque villages.

  • Cefalù - Visit this charming coastal town, known for its long, sandy beaches, medieval cathedral, and picturesque historic center.

Conclusion

Ustica may be a small island, but it’s packed with history, culture, and natural beauty. Whether you’re looking for a relaxing beach vacation, a challenging hiking adventure, or a thrilling diving excursion, Ustica has something to offer. With this comprehensive guide, you’ll be able to plan the perfect trip to this hidden gem of Sicily.
