Introduction to Enna (Province)

Enna is a charming town situated in the heart of Sicily and is the capital of the province. The region is known for its idyllic locations, rich history, extraordinary cuisine, and outdoor activities. It’s perfect for travellers looking for an authentic Italian holiday experience.

This comprehensive guide to Enna (province) will help you plan an unforgettable trip and make the most of your time in this beautiful part of Italy.

List of Places to Visit in Enna (Province)

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Enna (province) is steeped in culture and history. The region is home to countless museums and historical places for visitors to explore. Here are some of the must-visit locations:

Castello di Lombardia

Castello di Lombardia is one of the most iconic landmarks of Enna. The castle dates back to the 11th century, and it’s perched on top of a hill, providing stunning views over the city. The castle has played a vital role in Enna’s history, and today it houses an archaeological and contemporary art museum.

Villa Romana del Casale

The Villa Romana del Casale, situated in Piazza Armerina, is a UNESCO World Heritage site. The villa features some of the best-preserved Roman mosaics in the world, and it’s a must-visit destination for those interested in ancient history.

Museo Archeologico di Aidone

The Museum of Aidone is an archaeological museum featuring a collection of ancient artefacts from Enna and the surrounding areas. The museum exhibits a fine collection of Roman artefacts, as well as a collection of finds from ancient Greek cultures.

Natural Attractions of Enna (Province): Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Enna (province) is home to some of the most breathtaking natural landscapes in Sicily. From mountains to lakes, the region offers visitors a plethora of outdoor exploration opportunities. Here are some of the must-visit destinations:

Calascibetta

Calascibetta, an ancient town situated on a hilltop, boasts picturesque views of the valleys and surrounding mountains. The village is popular among hikers and walkers who can explore the surrounding hills and mountains, including Mount Etna.

Monte Altesina

Monte Altesina, the highest peak in Enna (province), is a popular spot for hikers who want to take in the stunning views of the valley below. At the summit, you will find a small chapel, dedicated to the Madonna, offering breathtaking panoramic views of the region.

Pergusa Lake

Pergusa lake is a popular destination for bird watchers and those who want to enjoy a relaxing walk in nature. Here you will see numerous species of birds and other wildlife. You can also relax by the lake’s shore and enjoy a picnic or take a boat ride around the lake.

Hiking/Biking Routes in Enna (Province)

Enna (province) offers a vast network of hiking and biking routes for nature lovers. From stunning mountain passes to scenic coastal roads, the region has something for everyone. Here are some of the best hiking and biking routes to explore in Enna (province):

Sentiero Italia

Sentiero Italia is the national hiking trail of Italy, and it passes through Enna (province). The trail spans over 4000 km and offers a thrilling adventure to all hikers who take it. The most popular section of the trail is from Caltanissetta to Enna, offering spectacular views of the Madonie Mountains.

Etna Adventure

Etna Adventure offers visitors an opportunity to experience hiking and biking tours on Mount Etna. You can choose from various routes that offer breathtaking panoramic views of the region. The tours range from easy to challenging and cater to both experienced and inexperienced hikers.

Best Things to Do in Enna (Province): Activities and Tours

Here are some of the best things to do in Enna (province) during your visit:

Taste the Local Cuisine

Enna (province) is renowned for its authentic Sicilian cuisine. Local specialities include pasta dishes like Pasta alla Norma, which features tomatoes, basil, ricotta cheese and eggplant, and Pesto alla trapanese. To whet your appetite, you can visit the food markets in towns like Piazza Armerina and Leonforte, where you can sample the local produce.

Drive the SS117

The SS117 is one of the most scenic routes in Sicily, and it passes through Enna (province). The road is known for its winding roads and spectacular views of the mountains and valleys below. Along the way, there are numerous stops where you can admire the scenery or explore small villages.

Week-Long Itinerary for Enna (Province)

Here’s a week-long itinerary to make the most of your time in Enna (province):

Day 1: Enna

Explore the town of Enna, starting from Castello di Lombardia, before heading to the Duomo. From there, you can make your way to the best viewpoints overlooking the region.

Day 2: Piazza Armerina & Agrigento

Drive to Piazza Armerina, where you can visit the Villa Romana del Casale and its stunning mosaics. From there, head to Agrigento to explore the Valley of the Temples.

Day 3: Caltanissetta

Explore the historic town of Caltanissetta, known for its beautiful landscapes and medieval town centre. You can also visit one of the numerous wine cellars in the area.

Day 4: Mount Etna

Embark on a breathtaking adventure to Mount Etna, the highest active volcano in Europe. You can book a tour that takes you through the lava fields and rugged landscapes.

Day 5: Pergusa Lake

Take a leisurely stroll around Pergusa lake and enjoy a picnic and boat ride around the lake.

Day 6: Leonforte

Visit the mountain town of Leonforte, famous for its homemade cheeses and quaint town centre.

Day 7: Calascibetta

Hike to the beautiful town of Calascibetta, perched on a hilltop with beautiful views over the valley. Along the way, you will encounter scenic pathways and historic landmarks.

Food and Drink Scene of Enna (Province)

Enna (province) offers some of the best Sicilian cuisine. Here are some of the must-try dishes:

Must-Try Dishes

Pasta alla Norma

Pasta alla Norma is a famous Sicilian pasta dish made using fried eggplant, ricotta salata, tomatoes, and basil. It’s a must-try dish in Enna (province).

Cannoli

Cannoli, a famous Sicilian dessert, is made by filling a crispy pastry shell with ricotta cream, mascarpone cheese, and candied fruit.

Local Beverages

Nero d’Avola Wine

Nero d’Avola is a full-bodied and dark red wine that’s famous in Sicily. You can taste this wine in any of the numerous wine cellars in the province.

Limoncello

Limoncello is a famous Sicilian liqueur made from lemon zest, sugar, and alcohol. It’s a refreshing drink to enjoy after a meal.

Getting to Enna (Province): Transportation Options and Logistics

Enna (province) is well-connected to other destinations in Sicily, making it easily accessible. Here are some transportation options and logistics to consider:

Airports Near Enna (Province)

The nearest airport to Enna (province) is the Catania Fontanarossa Airport, which is located approximately 100km from Enna. From the airport, you can take a train or bus to get to Enna.

Bus Stations Near Enna (Province)

Buses are another popular way to get to Enna (province). The town has several bus stations, including the Enna bus station, which serves several routes from other destinations in Sicily.

Other Options

You can also take a train from other major cities like Palermo, Catania, and Messina to arrive in Enna.

How to Get Around in Enna (Province)

The best way to get around Enna (province) is by car. You can rent a car from the airport or any major city in Sicily. Alternatively, you can take a taxi or public transportation to get to major attractions.

Where to Go Next from Enna (Province): Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Enna (province) is a great base for exploring other destinations in Sicily. Here are some of the nearby destinations and excursions you can take:

Catania

Catania, located 90km from Enna, is a beautiful city that’s known for its architecture, food, and culture. You can explore the city on foot and visit attractions like Mount Etna, Piazza del Duomo, and the Via Etnea shopping district.

Palermo

Palermo, located 125km from Enna, is the capital of Sicily. The city is known for its history, museums, and food. It’s worth exploring some of the city’s landmarks like the Palermo Cathedral, the Palermo Botanical Gardens, and the Palatine Chapel.
