Selinunte is a charming town located in the Trapani province of Sicily, Italy. It’s a hidden gem in Europe where you can explore a mix of Greek and Roman architecture while taking in breathtaking views of the Mediterranean Sea. In this comprehensive travel guide of Selinunte, we’ll take a look at its attractions, outdoor activities, cultural and local experiences, local food, and where to stay and buy souvenirs.

Introduction to Selinunte

Selinunte derives from the ancient Greek word “Selinon” meaning wild celery. It’s a small archaeological site believed to have been founded in 628 BC by Greek colonists from Megara Hyblaea.

The town was abandoned in the 4th century AD, following attacks from Carthage forces. This provided a chance for the site to be preserved, and in 1986, it was declared a UNESCO world heritage site.

Today, the ruins are recognized as one of the most important archaeological sites in the Mediterranean. Visitors come to Selinunte to witness the impressive temple layout, ancient city walls, and stunning sea views from the Acropolis.

Week-Long Itinerary for Selinunte

Day 1-2: Visit the archaeological park, the Sanctuary of Demeter and Persephone, and explore the town’s historical monuments.

Day 3-4: Hike or bike through the Riserva Naturale Orientata Foce Del Fiume Belice e Dune Limitrofe and beach time at Marinella di Selinunte Beach.

Day 5-6: Learn more about local food by taking a cooking class and then head for a day trip to Mozia and Marsala for the wine tasting experience.

Day 7: Do some souvenir shopping in town, visit the Cave di Cusa or spend a lazy day on the beach.

Food and Drink Scene of Selinunte

Must-Try Dishes

  • panelle (chickpea fritters)
  • arancine: (stuffed rice balls)
  • caponata (a fragrant and sweet-and-sour eggplant stew)
  • pasta con le sarde (pasta with sardine sauce)

Local Beverages

  • wines: Cantine Grotte, Ceuso and Donna Fugata are some of the best wine producers in the area, and you can take a tour to learn more about wine production.
  • liquors: Amaro, a herbal liquor, is a local specialty.

Local Culture and Customs of Selinunte

The Selinunte community is a warm and hospitable one, where family, food, and religion are at the heart of daily life. The town’s residents take pride in their history and traditions, and visitors are always welcome to learn more about them.

Best Time to Visit Selinunte

The best time to visit Selinunte is between May and September when the weather is warm and sunny, and the sea is inviting. The town can become crowded during peak season, so plan accordingly.

Do’s and Don’ts of Selinunte

Do:

  • Be respectful of the town’s historical monuments and archaeological site.
  • Try the local food and wines.
  • Learn some Italian phrases to communicate with locals.

Don’t:

  • Ignore the dress code in churches or religious buildings.
  • Litter on the beaches or in public spaces.
  • Be too loud or disruptive during siesta hours (1-4 pm).

Safety and Health

Safety in Selinunte

Selinunte is a safe place to visit, but visitors should take the same precautions as in any other unknown place. Use common sense, and keep an eye on your belongings.

Health in Selinunte

The town has a medical center where you can receive emergency treatment, but it’s best to bring any necessary medications from home.
