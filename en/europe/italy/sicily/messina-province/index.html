Introduction to Messina (province)

Messina (Province) is situated in the northeastern part of Sicily, which is an island in the southern part of Italy. The region offers a mix of natural beauty, rich history, and unique culture, which are sure to make for a memorable travel experience. The region is bordered by the Ionian Sea to the east and the Tyrrhenian Sea to the north.

The region’s capital city, also called Messina, is known for its port and is the gateway to the Italian mainland. The city was almost entirely destroyed during World War II, but it has since been rebuilt and is home to some of the most significant cultural and historical sites in the region.

List of Places to Visit in Messina (province)

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Messina Cathedral

The Messina Cathedral is a stunning church built in the 12th century and located in the heart of the city. The cathedral features a mix of different architectural styles and is home to a collection of artwork from various periods.

Regional Museum of Messina

The Regional Museum of Messina is a must-visit for history buffs and art enthusiasts. The museum’s collection showcases the region’s cultural heritage with ancient artifacts, sculptures, ceramics, and much more.

Aragonese Castle

The Aragonese Castle is an impressive fortification that dates back to the 16th century. The castle is set atop a hill overlooking the Strait of Messina and offers breathtaking views of the sea and the surrounding hills.

Natural Attractions of Messina (province): Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Monti Peloritani

Monti Peloritani is a mountain range that runs along the northeastern coast of Sicily, offering some of the most beautiful hiking routes in Messina (Province). The range boasts stunning views of the countryside, the sea, and the nearby Aeolian Islands.

Gole dell’Alcantara

Gole dell’Alcantara is a spectacular natural attraction that must not be missed. The attraction features a rugged canyon carved out by the Alcantara River over thousands of years of erosion. Visitors can walk along the riverbed or take a boat tour to see the best views of the Gole.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Messina (province)

Messina (Province) boasts some of the best hiking and biking routes in Italy, with breathtaking natural scenery and challenging trails for outdoor enthusiasts.

Nebrodi National Park

Nebrodi National Park is the largest protected area in Sicily, covering over 86,000 hectares. The park offers a network of trails for hiking, biking, and horseback riding, and visitors can take in stunning views of the Sicilian countryside and the Tyrrhenian Sea.

Best Things to Do in Messina (province): Activities and Tours

Boat Tour of the Aeolian Islands

A boat tour of the Aeolian Islands is a must-do when visiting Messina (Province). The islands are a UNESCO World Heritage Site and boast some of the most stunning natural beauty in the world. A boat tour will provide visitors with the chance to see the islands up close and take in the stunning views of the sea and the islands.

Wine Tasting in the Etna Area

Messina (Province) is home to some of the best vineyards in Italy, and a wine tasting in the Etna area will give visitors a chance to sample some of the finest wines in the world. The region produces a range of wines, from red to white and rosé, and visitors can learn about the winemaking process while enjoying the stunning views of the countryside.

Week-Long Itinerary for Messina (province)

Day 1: Arrive in Messina and explore the city’s historical and cultural sites. Day 2: Take a boat tour of the Aeolian Islands. Day 3: Visit the Gole dell’Alcantara and hike the surrounding area. Day 4: Explore the Nebrodi National Park and hike some of the park’s trails. Day 5: Take a wine tasting tour in the Etna area. Day 6: Relax and unwind at one of the region’s stunning beaches. Day 7: Depart Messina.

Food and Drink Scene of Messina (province)

Must-Try Dishes

Pasta alla Norma

This dish is a classic from Messina and features eggplant, tomato sauce, and a sprinkling of grated cheese.

Arancini

Arancini is a Sicilian rice ball filled with meat or cheese, coated in breadcrumbs and fried until golden brown.

Local Beverages

Limoncello

Made from local lemons, this sweet liqueur is a perfect after-dinner drink.

Nero d’Avola

This bold red wine is a Sicilian classic, known for its rich flavor and deep, dark color.

Where to Stay in Messina (province): Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Messina (Province) offers a variety of accommodations to suit every budget and travel style. The options range from luxury hotels and beach resorts to budget-friendly hostels, guesthouses, and vacation rentals.

What to Buy in Messina (province): Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Ceramics and Pottery

Messina (Province) is home to some of Italy’s best pottery and ceramics. Visitors can find everything from intricate tiles and ornate plates to hand-crafted vases and bowls.

Local Honey and Olive Oil

The region is known for its high-quality honey and olive oil, both of which are excellent souvenirs to bring home.

Local Culture and Customs of Messina (province)

Messina (Province) is steeped in tradition, history, and culture. The region has a unique dialect, which differs from standard Italian, and its cuisine, arts, and crafts are distinctly Sicilian.

Best Time to Visit Messina (province)

The best time to visit Messina (Province) is in the spring (April to June) or fall (September to November), when the weather is mild and the crowds are smaller. The summer months (July to August) can be very hot and crowded, and many locals take their holidays during this time.

Do’s and Don’ts of Messina (province)

Do:

  • Try the local cuisine
  • Learn some basic Italian
  • Respect the local culture and customs

Don’t:

  • Overestimate your Italian language skills
  • Dress immodestly in religious sites
  • Ignore local driving laws

Safety and Health

Safety in Messina (province)

Messina (Province) is a relatively safe place to travel and has low crime rates. However, visitors should still be aware of pickpocketing in crowded areas and take the necessary precautions to keep their belongings safe.

Health in Messina (province)

Messina (Province) has several hospitals and medical clinics that offer medical care to visitors and locals. Visitors should ensure they have comprehensive travel insurance before traveling to Italy.

Getting to Messina (province): Transportation Options and Logistics

Messina (Province) can be reached by plane, train, bus, or car, making it easily accessible to visitors from all over the world.

Airports Near Messina (province)

The closest airport to Messina (Province) is Catania-Fontanarossa Airport, which is approximately a two-hour drive from the city center.

Bus Stations Near Messina (province)

Messina (Province) is well connected by bus to other parts of Sicily and mainland Italy. The main bus station in Messina is the Stazione Centrale, which is located in the city center.

Other Options

Visitors can also reach Messina (Province) by ferry from the Italian mainland or nearby Aeolian Islands.

How to Get Around in Messina (province)

Getting around Messina (Province) can be done by car, bus, train, or boat, depending on the destination. Visitors should be aware that parking in the city center can be difficult and that some of the roads in the countryside can be narrow and winding.

Where to Go Next from Messina (province): Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Taormina

Taormina is a picturesque town located on the east coast of Sicily, just a short drive from Messina. The town is known for its stunning views of the sea and the nearby Mount Etna, as well as its ancient Greek theater and beautiful beaches.

Cefalù

Cefalù is a charming coastal town located on the northern coast of Sicily, approximately a three-hour drive from Messina. The town is known for its beautiful beaches, quaint streets, and historic landmarks, such as the Cefalù Cathedral.

Conclusion

Messina (Province) is a must-visit destination for anyone interested in history, culture, and natural beauty. The region offers a unique mix of all three and is easily accessible from other parts of Italy and Sicily. From hiking and biking to wine tasting and beach hopping, there’s something for everyone to enjoy in Messina (Province).
