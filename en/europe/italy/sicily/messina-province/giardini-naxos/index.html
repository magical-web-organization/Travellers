Introduction to Giardini Naxos

Located in the province of Messina, in Sicily, Italy, Giardini Naxos is a picturesque town overlooking the Ionian Sea. This beautiful destination boasts a rich history, breathtaking views and vibrant culture that make it one of the most popular tourist spots in the region of Sicily.

It is believed that Giardini Naxos was the very first Greek colony on the island and this town is perfect for beach lovers, food enthusiasts and history buffs alike.

In this article, we’ve put together a comprehensive guide to Giardini Naxos that will tell you all you need to know about this stunning town.

List of Places to Visit in Giardini Naxos

Here are some of the top places to visit in Giardini Naxos:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  1. The Archeological Site of Naxos: This site is believed to have been the first Greek colony on the island of Sicily, dating back to the 8th century BC. The site, which contains ancient ruins and artifacts, is an important historical landmark that gives insight into the ancient history of the island.

  2. Giardini Naxos Beach: This stunning beach is a must-visit for anyone traveling to Giardini Naxos. The crystal-clear waters and pristine sands are perfect for sunbathing and swimming while enjoying the beautiful surroundings.

  3. Duomo of Taormina: This cathedral is located in the nearby town of Taormina, but it is certainly worth a visit for those who are interested in religious history and architecture. The cathedral, which was built in the 13th century, boasts an impressive facade and beautiful interior.

Natural Attractions of Giardini Naxos: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  1. Mount Etna: If you’re in Giardini Naxos, you can’t miss the opportunity to visit Mount Etna. This active volcano is one of the main attractions in Sicily. You can take a guided tour of the volcano or hike up to the summit for a breathtaking view of the island.

  2. Alcantara Gorges: Located in the nearby town of Taormina, these gorges offer a unique hiking experience in the midst of stunning natural surroundings. Visitors can enjoy the beautiful landscape and crystal-clear waters of the river that runs through the gorges.

  3. Isola Bella: This small island located off the coast of Taormina is known for its stunning natural beauty and crystal-clear waters. Visitors can enjoy swimming and sunbathing at the beach, or take a boat tour of the surrounding area.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Giardini Naxos

If you enjoy hiking or biking, there are several routes in and around Giardini Naxos that you can explore. Here are some of the best:

  1. Pizzo di Corvo: This mountain is located just outside Giardini Naxos and offers stunning views of the surrounding landscape. You can hike to the summit for a breathtaking view of the town and the sea.

  2. Monte Venere: This mountain is located in the nearby town of Letojanni. The hiking route offers beautiful views of the sea and the surrounding landscape.

  3. Monte Tauro: This mountain is located in the nearby town of Taormina. The hiking route takes visitors through the beautiful natural surroundings of the town and offers breathtaking views of the sea.

Best Things to Do in Giardini Naxos: Activities and Tours

There are plenty of activities and tours to take part in while in Giardini Naxos. Here are some of the best:

  1. Wine Tasting Tour: Sicily is known for its delicious wine, and there are several wineries in and around Giardini Naxos that offer wine tasting tours.

  2. Cooking Class: Learn how to cook authentic Sicilian cuisine from a local chef.

  3. Scuba Diving: There are several scuba diving centers in the area that offer diving courses and tours of the stunning underwater landscape.

Week-Long Itinerary for Giardini Naxos

If you’re planning to spend a week in Giardini Naxos, here’s a sample itinerary:

Day 1: Explore the town and visit the Archeological Site of Naxos.

Day 2: Visit Mount Etna and take a guided tour of the volcano.

Day 3: Take a boat tour of Isola Bella and explore the nearby town of Taormina.

Day 4: Take a cooking class and learn how to cook authentic Sicilian cuisine.

Day 5: Explore the Alcantara Gorges and go for a hike.

Day 6: Take a wine tasting tour and visit some of the local wineries.

Day 7: Relax on Giardini Naxos Beach and enjoy the beautiful surroundings.

Food and Drink Scene of Giardini Naxos

Sicilian food is world-renowned for its delicious flavors and unique ingredients. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages in Giardini Naxos:

Must-Try Dishes

  1. Arancini: These are deep-fried balls of rice that are typically filled with meat, cheese, or vegetables.

  2. Caponata: This dish is made with eggplant, celery, and tomato, and is often served as an appetizer.

  3. Pasta alla Norma: This pasta dish is made with eggplant and a tomato sauce and is a staple of Sicilian cuisine.

Local Beverages

  1. Limoncello: This lemon liqueur is a specialty in Sicily and is often served as an after-dinner drink.

  2. Marsala: This fortified wine is produced in the nearby town of Marsala and is a must-try for anyone visiting the region.

Local Culture and Customs of Giardini Naxos

Sicilian culture is a unique blend of Greek, Arabic, and Italian influences. The people are known for their warm hospitality and love of good food and wine. When visiting Giardini Naxos, it’s important to respect the local customs, such as dressing modestly when visiting religious sites and asking permission before taking photos of people.

Best Time to Visit Giardini Naxos

The best time to visit Giardini Naxos is during the summer months, from June to September, when the weather is warm and sunny. However, this is also the busiest time of year, so it’s important to book your accommodation and tours in advance.
