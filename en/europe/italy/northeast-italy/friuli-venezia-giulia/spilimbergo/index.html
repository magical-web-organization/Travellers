Introduction to Spilimbergo

Nestled in Friuli-Venezia Giulia, Spilimbergo is a charming town in Northeast Italy. With a history that dates back to the Roman times, the town boasts of a rich heritage, natural beauty, and cultural attractions that will leave you enthralled. Whether you’re on a solo trip, a family vacation, or a romantic getaway, Spilimbergo has something for everyone.

List of Places to Visit in Spilimbergo

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Palazzo Dipinto: The 16th-century palace is a prime example of Venetian architecture and houses the Museo del Territorio, showcasing the town’s agricultural and craft traditions.
  • Duomo di Spilimbergo: The cathedral is an architectural gem, with elements from Gothic, Renaissance, and Baroque styles.
  • Casa della Contadinanza: This 16th-century building once housed the farmers’ guild and now functions as a museum, showcasing the town’s agricultural history.
  • Castello di Spilimbergo: The medieval castle, dating back to the 13th century, is now a cultural center and often hosts exhibitions and events.

Natural Attractions of Spilimbergo: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Piancavallo: a stunning mountain resort, with hiking and skiing trails, and breathtaking views of the Friulian Dolomites.
  • Riserva Naturale Regionale dei Laghi di Doberdò e Pietrarossa: This safeguarded area comprises two beautiful lakes, surrounded by lush greenery and home to a variety of flora and fauna.
  • Parco di San Floriano: A lovely park located on a hill offering panoramic views of Spilimbergo and the surrounding countryside.
  • Forra del Rio Maggiore: A picturesque ravine that offers an exciting trekking experience.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Spilimbergo

  • Monte San Simeone: A challenging trail that rewards hikers with stunning views of the Friulian Dolomites.
  • Ciclovia Alpe Adria: A cycling route that runs from Austria to Italy, passing through charming towns, gorgeous countryside, and historic landmarks.
  • Sentiero della Luce: A moderate-level trail that winds up through the foothills of the Friulian Alps, offering spectacular views and historical monuments.

Best Things to Do in Spilimbergo: Activities and Tours

  • Palio di San Rocco: A traditional festival held in August, featuring a thrilling horse race and colourful parades.
  • Wine Tasting in the Colli Orientali del Friuli: Explore the vineyards and taste some of the region’s famous wines.
  • Cooking Classes: Learn to cook some of the local dishes with a professional chef.
  • Guided Tours of the Town: Explore Spilimbergo’s historic landmarks and hidden corners with a knowledgeable guide.

Food and Drink Scene of Spilimbergo

Must-Try Dishes

  • Gubana: A sweet bread filled with nuts, raisins, and pine nuts, often enjoyed with a glass of sweet wine.
  • Frico: A crispy cheese dish, usually made with Montasio cheese, potatoes, and onions.
  • Jota: A hearty soup made with beans, sauerkraut, and potatoes.
  • Prosciutto di San Daniele: A delicious dry-cured ham, produced locally and renowned the world over.

Local Beverages

  • Friulano: A dry, white wine made from the Friulano grape, typical of the region.
  • Picolit: A sweet dessert wine made from Picolit grapes, produced in small quantities, and known for its complex flavors.
  • Grappa: An alcoholic drink made from grape skins and stems, with a strong taste and high alcohol content.
  • Aperol Spritz: A refreshing cocktail made from Aperol, prosecco, and soda water, popular in the Veneto region.

Where to Stay in Spilimbergo: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Antico Borgo La Torre: A rustic-style guesthouse, located on a hill overlooking the town center, with a charming courtyard and a relaxed atmosphere.
  • Albergo Roma: A simple hotel, offering affordable rooms and a convenient location.
  • B&B La Tavernetta: A cozy bed and breakfast, located in the heart of the town, with clean rooms and a friendly host.

What to Buy in Spilimbergo: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Handmade Lace: Spilimbergo is famous for its lace-making tradition, and you can buy authentic handmade lace products, such as tablecloths, doilies, and curtains.
  • Gubana: Bring home a sweet loaf of Gubana, as a delicious reminder of your trip.
  • Prosciutto di San Daniele: A tasty souvenir that will make a great addition to your cheese board.
  • Montasio Cheese: A soft and creamy cheese, perfect for pasta dishes and grilled sandwiches.

Local Culture and Customs of Spilimbergo

Spilimbergo is a town proud of its traditions, and visitors should respect them. The locals are friendly and welcoming, and it is customary to greet people with a handshake, and exchange pleasantries. Dress modestly when visiting religious places and remove your shoes before entering. Do not litter or smoke in public areas, as this is considered disrespectful.

Best Time to Visit Spilimbergo

The best time to visit Spilimbergo is from April to October, when the weather is mild and pleasant, and outdoor activities are in full swing. Summers can be hot, and the town can get crowded, so plan accordingly.

Safety and Health

Safety in Spilimbergo

Spilimbergo is a safe town, with low crime rates. However, visitors are advised to take standard safety precautions, such as keeping valuables safe and secure, especially in crowded areas. Watch out for pickpockets in tourist spots, and do not leave your belongings unattended.

Health in Spilimbergo

There are no major health risks in Spilimbergo, and medical facilities are readily available. However, visitors are advised to take standard precautions, such as wearing sunscreen and insect repellent, especially when outdoors. Always carry medication for any pre-existing conditions you may have.

Getting to Spilimbergo: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Spilimbergo

The nearest airport to Spilimbergo is Trieste - Friuli Venezia Giulia (TRS), located approximately 40 km away. Other airports in the region include Venice Marco Polo Airport and Venice Treviso Airport.

Bus Stations Near Spilimbergo

Spilimbergo is well-connected by bus, with frequent services from nearby towns and cities. The most convenient bus station is the Pordenone Central Bus Station, located approximately 17 km away.

Other Options

There are regular train services to Pordenone Railway Station, which is well-connected to Spilimbergo. Taxis and car rentals are also available.

How to Get Around in Spilimbergo

Spilimbergo is a small town, and the best way to explore it is on foot. Bikes and e-bikes are available for rent and are a great way to explore the surrounding countryside. There are also local buses that connect the town to nearby areas.

Where to Go Next from Spilimbergo: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Udine: A charming city with historic buildings, squares, and museums, located approximately 35 km from Spilimbergo.
  • Venezia: The floating city of Venice needs no introduction, and is a must-visit when in the region, located approximately 110 km from Spilimbergo.
  • Grado: A delightful seaside town, known for its beautiful beaches, thermal baths, and ancient ruins, located approximately 50 km from Spilimbergo.
  • Trieste: A cosmopolitan city with a rich cultural heritage, located approximately 80 km from Spilimbergo.

Conclusion

Spilimbergo is a beautiful town, offering visitors a unique blend of history, art, nature, and cuisine. From museums to mountains, there’s something for everyone. We hope our comprehensive travel guide has inspired you to plan your trip and experience all that Spilimbergo has to offer.
