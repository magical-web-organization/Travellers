Introduction to Brixen

Situated in the heart of Trentino-Alto Adige, Brixen is a charming town that offers an authentic Italian experience. Surrounded by the Dolomite mountains with a rich history and culture, Brixen is a paradise for those looking to explore natural beauty and cultural attractions.

Brixen has something for everyone - visitors can hike in its scenic mountains, learn about its interesting history, indulge in local delicacies, and much more. With its quaint streets, majestic cathedrals, and stunning views, Brixen is a must-visit destination in Northeast Italy.

List of Places to Visit in Brixen

Whether you are a history buff, a culture enthusiast or an outdoor adventure lover, Brixen has a variety of attractions to suit all tastes. Here is a list of some of the top places to visit in Brixen.

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Brixen has a fascinating history that can be explored through its museums and historical sites. These include:

  • Brixen Cathedral: A beautiful and historic cathedral, Brixen Cathedral, or the Cathedral of Saint Maria, is known for its Gothic and Baroque architecture.
  • Hofburg Brixen: Once used as a fortress, the Hofburg Brixen is a beautiful building that now houses the district court. Visitors can explore the castle’s history and its various rooms, including the dungeon, the chapel and the great hall.
  • Pharmacy Museum: This unique museum showcases the history of medicine and pharmacy through the centuries, showcasing old medical equipment, herbal remedies, and antique pharmacy artifacts.

Natural Attractions of Brixen: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Brixen is nestled in the midst of the Dolomite Mountains, making it an ideal location for outdoor adventures. Here are some of the natural attractions that are a must-visit:

  • Plose Mountain: With numerous hiking and biking trails, Plose Mountain offers stunning panoramic views of the Dolomite mountains. Take a gondola up to the top of the mountain for an unforgettable experience.
  • Neustift Monastery: Surrounded by lush forests and rolling hills, Neustift Monastery is a picturesque setting and offers visitors the chance to enjoy nature at its best. Visitors can enjoy a peaceful walk through the expansive gardens and enjoy a tranquil oasis.
  • St. Hippolyt Church: Located in the nearby village of Lazfons, St. Hippolyt Church is a hidden gem with stunning views of the Brixen Valley. The church is at the end of a picturesque hike, which makes it a wonderful stop off for those looking for a bit of adventure while taking in the views.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Brixen

Brixen is an ideal destination for hiking and biking enthusiasts. There are many trails that offer scenic views of the town and the surrounding mountains. Here are some of the best hiking/biking routes to explore:

  • Plose Circuit Hike: This popular hiking trail takes you on a circuit around the Plose Mountain and offers breathtaking views of the Dolomite Mountains. The 9 km trail is a medium difficulty hike, and it’s recommended to start early in the morning to avoid the crowd.
  • Brixen Plose Bike Trail: This 12.7 km mountain bike trail takes you through the Gilfenklamm Gorge and the rolling hills of the Brixen Valley.
  • Villanderer Alm High Trail: Set at an altitude of 2,200 meters, this popular hiking trail is one of the most stunning hikes in Brixen and offers spectacular views of the Eastern Dolomites. The 13 km trail is a high difficulty hike and suitable only for experienced hikers.

Best Things to Do in Brixen: Activities and Tours

There are plenty of activities and tours in Brixen that cater to all interests and ages. Here are some of the best things to do in Brixen:

  • Brewery Tour: Take a tour of the Forst Brewery in Algund to learn about the brewing process and sample some of the delicious lagers that are brewed here.
  • Wine Tasting: Trentino-Alto Adige is known for its excellent wines, and Brixen is no exception. Take a wine tasting tour around the Brixen vineyards to sample some unique and delicious wines.
  • Skiing: During the winter months, Brixen transforms into a skiing paradise. With its excellent ski slopes and gondolas, there is plenty of skiing opportunities for all ages and abilities.

Food and Drink Scene of Brixen

When it comes to food and drink, Brixen does not disappoint. From local delicacies to excellent wines, visitors are sure to indulge in some delicious treats. Here are some of the must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Spatzle: This is a traditional South Tyrolean pasta dish made with flour, egg, and salt. Typically served with local cheese and topped with fried onions, it’s a must-try.
  • Tirtlen: A South Tyrolean pastry made with flour, butter, and eggs, and filled with spinach and cheese stuffing.
  • Dumplings: A popular dish in the region, Dumplings are made with bread rolls, milk, flour, and eggs. They are usually served as a side dish or with soup.

Local Beverages

  • Lagrein: One of the more popular red wines from the region, this full-bodied wine is known for its deep color and spicy notes.
  • Gewurztraminer: A refreshing and aromatic white wine, Gewurztraminer pairs perfectly with South Tyrolean cuisine.
  • Vernatsch: A light red wine known for its fruity notes and slightly sweet taste.

Where to Stay in Brixen: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Brixen has a range of accommodation options to cater to all travel styles and budgets. From luxurious hotels to budget-friendly hostels, visitors can find the perfect place to stay. Here are some top options:

  • Goldener Adler Hotel: A historic hotel located in the heart of Brixen’s old town, Goldener Adler is perfect for visitors looking for a luxurious and comfortable stay.
  • Youth Hostel Brixen: Ideal for budget travelers, the Youth Hostel Brixen is located just outside of the town centre and offers clean and comfortable rooms.
  • Gasthof Sonne: A family-run guesthouse located in the idyllic village of St. Andrä, Gasthof Sonne offers picturesque views of the surrounding mountains and a friendly atmosphere.

What to Buy in Brixen: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

When it comes to souvenirs, Brixen offers a range of unique and interesting gifts to bring back home. Here are some of the top souvenirs and local products to buy:

  • Handmade wooden objects: Brixen is known for its woodwork, and local craftsmen produce a range of handmade wooden objects, including Christmas ornaments, toys, and furniture.
  • Local food products: Bring back a taste of Brixen by purchasing local cheese, wine or cured meats to enjoy at home.
  • Traditional South Tyrolean clothing: Visitors can purchase traditional clothing items such as the Lederhosen for men or the Dirndl for women.

Local Culture and Customs of Brixen

Brixen has a unique culture and customs, shaped by its rich history and location. Here are some of the local customs and traditions to keep in mind:

  • Respect for nature: Brixen is surrounded by mountains and vast natural landscapes, and locals have a deep respect for nature. Visitors are expected to follow Leave No Trace principles and leave nature as they found it.
  • Languages spoken: Brixen is located in the predominantly German-speaking province of Trentino-Alto Adige, and most locals speak German. However, Italian is also an official language, and some locals may speak it.
  • Tipping: In Brixen, it is customary to tip around 10% for excellent service in restaurants and cafes.

Best Time to Visit Brixen

Brixen is a year-round destination, and the best time to visit depends on the traveler’s interests and preferences. For outdoor enthusiasts and skiers, the winter season (December to March) is perfect for skiing and other winter activities. Those looking to explore Brixen’s natural attractions should visit during the summer months (June to September) when the weather is warmer.

Do’s and Don’ts of Brixen

Do:

  • Try local cuisine and delicacies
  • Respect nature and leave no trace
  • Visit the historic attractions and museums
  • Explore the surrounding mountains and valleys
  • Learn some basic German phrases

Don’t:

  • Touch the historic artefacts in museums and churches
  • Wear hiking boots or ski gear inside restaurants and cafes
  • Litter or harm wildlife or plants

Safety and Health

Safety in Brixen

Brixen is a safe destination for visitors. However, as with any destination, it’s important to take common-sense precautions such as avoiding isolated areas at night and keeping your valuables safe.

Health in Brixen

Brixen has a high standard of medical care, and there are several medical facilities in the town, including a hospital and several health clinics. Visitors are encouraged to have adequate travel insurance that covers emergency medical care.

Getting to Brixen: Transportation Options and Logistics

Located in the heart of Trentino-Alto Adige, Brixen is easily accessible by different means of transportation.

Airports Near Brixen

The closest airports to Brixen are:

  • Innsbruck Airport (83km)
  • Verona Airport (171 km)
  • Milan Airport (298 km)

Bus Stations Near Brixen

Brixen is connected to several major cities by bus services. The main bus station is located in the heart of the city, and visitors can easily travel around the region using public transport.

Other Options

Travelers can also make use of the convenient train connections to get to Brixen. The main train station is located in the city center, and visitors can take trains to other major cities in Italy and Austria.

How to Get Around in Brixen

Brixen is a relatively small town, and most of the attractions are located within walking distance of each other. Alternatively, visitors can rent a bike or a car to explore the surrounding region.

Where to Go Next from Brixen: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Brixen is an excellent base for exploring other nearby destinations and excursions, including:

  • Bolzano: Located just half an hour from Brixen, Bolzano is a picturesque and historic town with a rich culture and history.
  • Lake Garda: Known for its scenic beauty and picturesque towns, Lake Garda is located just 2 hours from Brixen and is a popular destination for day trips.
  • Dolomites: The Dolomite Mountains are a UNESCO World Heritage Site and are located just a short drive from Brixen, making it the perfect destination for hiking adventures.

Conclusion

Brixen is a hidden gem that offers a perfect mix of natural beauty, cultural attractions, and outdoor adventures. Visitors can explore the best of Brixen by hiking in the mountains, indulging in local cuisine, visiting historic attractions, and more. With its welcoming locals, stunning views, and traditional charm, Brixen is a must-visit destination in Northeast Italy.
