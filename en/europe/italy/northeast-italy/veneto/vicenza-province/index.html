Introduction to Vicenza (province)

The Vicenza province is located in the Veneto region of Northeast Italy. It is a part of the UNESCO World Heritage Site and is known for its beautiful architecture, rich cultural heritage, and natural beauty. The province has a diverse landscape ranging from hills to lakes and is home to some of Italy’s most exciting destinations. In this travel guide, we will explore the best things to do and see in the Vicenza province, including its must-visit attractions, hiking trails, food, and culture.

List of Places to Visit in Vicenza (province)

Whether you are a history buff, adventure enthusiast, or food lover, the Vicenza province has something for everyone. Here is a list of the top places to visit in the province.

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  1. Vicenza’s Palladio’s Basilica - A masterpiece of Renaissance architecture designed by Andrea Palladio
  2. Villa Rotonda - A UNESCO World Heritage Site, known for its iconic architecture
  3. Teatro Olimpico - one of the oldest theaters in the world and a prime example of Renaissance architecture
  4. The Cathedral of Vicenza - A beautiful church dedicated to the Assumption of Mary

Natural Attractions of Vicenza (province): Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  1. Lake Fimon - A beautiful lake located in the Venetian hills, perfect for hiking and picnicking
  2. Mount Pasubio - A scenic peak perfect for hiking and trekking
  3. Monte Grappa - A beautiful mountain range ideal for hiking and paragliding
  4. Colli Berici - a beautiful hill range located in the south of the province with breathtaking views of Vicenza

Hiking/Biking Routes of Vicenza (province)

If you love hiking or biking, then the Vicenza province has some of the most spectacular trails in Italy. Here are some of our top picks.

  1. The Strada degli Eroi - a historic hike located on the Monte Grappa with stunning views of the Venetian plain and the Dolomites
  2. The Colli Berici bike trail - A 32 km trail offering magnificent views of the Venetian hills
  3. The Lungo Brenta bike path - A 115km trail starting from Bassano del Grappa and ending at the Venetian lagoon
  4. The Monte Summano Hike - a serene hike that takes you through forests and medieval villages

Best Things to Do in Vicenza (province): Activities and Tours

  1. Take a Wine and Food Tour - Italy is known for its cuisine, and Vicenza province is no exception. Indulge in local delicacies and wines with a food and wine tour
  2. Explore the UNESCO World Heritage Sites - from Palladio’s Basilica to the Villa Rotonda, the province is home to some of Italy’s most iconic sites.
  3. Visit the Asiago plateau - Known for its cheese, the Asiago plateau is a must-visit for foodies.
  4. Take a tour of the vineyards and wineries and try some of the local wines

Week-Long Itinerary for Vicenza (province)

To make the most of your visit to the Vicenza province, we’ve put together a week-long itinerary.

Day 1 - Explore the historic city of Vicenza, including the Palladio Basilica and the Teatro Olimpico

Day 2 and 3 - Take a food and wine tour. Visit vineyards and wineries and indulge in local delicacies

Day 4 - Take a hike on the Monte Grappa or Mount Pasubio

Day 5 - Visit the UNESCO World Heritage site - The Villa Rotonda

Day 6 - Visit Lake Fimon and relax by the lake

Day 7 - Take a bike or hike on the Colli Berici trail

Food and Drink Scene of Vicenza (province)

Italian food is known worldwide for its taste and preparation. The same exceptional quality applies to the food of the Vicenza province. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages.

Must-Try Dishes

  1. Bigoli pasta - This pasta is a specialty of the Vicenza region. It’s made by hand and pairs perfectly with ragu sauce.
  2. Sarde in Saor - A delicious Venetian dish consisting of fried sardines in a sweet and sour sauce.
  3. Asiago Cheese - A popular cheese in Italy made from cow’s milk. It may be enjoyed plain or grated over pasta dishes

Local Beverages

  1. Prosecco - A sparkling wine that is a must-try on your visit to the province
  2. Grappa - Italy’s most potent liquor and favorite of locals
  3. Spritz - A refreshing cocktail made with Prosecco, Aperol or Campari, and soda.

Where to Stay in Vicenza (province): Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

The Vicenza province offers a variety of accommodation options, ranging from hotels to guesthouses and hostels. Here are some of our top picks.

  1. Hotel Villa Michelangelo - a 5-star hotel with a view of the castle and the beautiful Veneto hills
  2. Villa da Schio - a 17th-century villa that has been converted into a luxury guesthouse.
  3. Ostello Olimpico - a hostel in the heart of Vicenza, perfect for budget travelers

What to Buy in Vicenza (province): Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

The Vicenza province is known for its local products, including wine, cheese, and artisanal items. Here are some souvenirs to buy during your visit.

  1. Vicenza’s jewelry - the city is known for its traditional and modern gold and silver jewelry
  2. Grappa - it makes a perfect gift for friends and family
  3. Asiago cheese - take home some locally made Asiago cheese, an excellent reminder of your visit to the province

Local Culture and Customs of Vicenza (province)

The Vicenza province is rich in cultural experiences. Here are some customs and traditions to keep in mind during your visit.

  1. Dress appropriately while visiting churches and other religious sites.
  2. Italians take their food and wine seriously, so avoid asking for customizations on meals
  3. Respect the cultural heritage and architecture of the region

Best Time to Visit Vicenza (province)

The best time to visit the Vicenza province is from May to September when the weather is mild, and outdoor activities are available.

Do’s and Don’ts of Vicenza (province)

Do:

  1. Try local cuisine and beverages
  2. Explore the city and countryside on a bike or foot
  3. Respect the local customs and traditions

Don’t:

  1. Dress inappropriately
  2. Argue with the locals or complain about their customs
  3. Be loud and disrespectful

Safety and Health

Safety in Vicenza (province)

The Vicenza province is a safe destination for travelers. However, it is always advisable to be cautious in crowded areas and busy streets.

Health in Vicenza (province)

The Vicenza province has excellent healthcare services available. It is always advisable to carry your health insurance card while traveling.

Getting to Vicenza (province): Transportation Options and Logistics

The Vicenza province is well connected to the rest of Italy by several modes of transportation.

Airports Near Vicenza (province)

The closest airport to Vicenza province is the Venice Marco Polo Airport

Bus Stations Near Vicenza (province)

The Vicenza train station is a major hub for bus transportation.

Other Options

The Vicenza province is well connected by road and rail.

How to Get Around in Vicenza (province)

The most cost-effective and authentic way to explore the Vicenza province is by bike or foot. Taxis, rental cars, and buses are also available.

Where to Go Next from Vicenza (province): Nearby Destinations and Excursions

If you have more days to spend in the region, here are some nearby destinations that are worth a visit.

  1. Venice - A city of canals, Venice is a must-visit destination when in Italy.
  2. Verona - A city famous for its association with Romeo and Juliet
  3. Padua - A city famous for its medieval university and cultural attractions

Conclusion

Vicenza province offers a perfect blend of history, culture, natural beauty, and culinary delights. A visit to this Italian province is a must if you’re searching for a unique Italian travel experience. We hope this comprehensive guide helps you plan your next trip to Vicenza province.
