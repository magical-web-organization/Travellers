Introduction to Burano

Burano is a small island located in the Venetian Lagoon, just 7 km from Venice. Known for its colorful houses, intricate lacework, and stunning scenery, Burano is a popular destination for travelers seeking a picturesque and culturally rich experience.

The island has a population of about 3,000 people, and its economy is mainly centered on tourism and fishing. Burano is made up of four small islands separated by narrow canals, connected by picturesque bridges, and surrounded by calm waters, making it an ideal place for exploring on foot or by boat.

In this guide, we will take you on a tour of Burano, from its must-visit tourist attractions to its local customs and cuisine, to help you make the most out of your visit to this beautiful island.

List of Places to Visit in Burano

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Museo Del Merletto

The Museo Del Merletto (Lace Museum) is dedicated to the art of Burano lace making. This museum showcases a vast collection of antique lace items, from traditional wedding veils to intricate table linen, some of which date back to the 18th century.

Chiesa di San Martino

The Chiesa di San Martino is a stunning Gothic-style church, known for its impressive bell tower. The church houses beautiful frescoes and houses renowned paintings by Giuseppe Angeli.

Casa dei Vescovi

Casa dei Vescovi is a 16th-century mansion that was formerly the home of the bishops of Torcello. It now serves as a museum, showcasing a collection of antique furniture, artwork, and tapestries.

Natural Attractions of Burano: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Burano Lagoon

The Burano Lagoon is an ideal location for a relaxing boat ride while taking in the stunning panoramic views of the island. Tourists can book a private boat tour or join a group tour.

Oratorio di Santa Barbara

Oratorio di Santa Barbara is a small church located in Burano that houses a painting by Tintoretto and affords scenic views of the lagoon and the surrounding area.

Parco di Burano

The Parco di Burano is a large park, perfect for families, where visitors can enjoy exploring walking paths, beautiful flower gardens and playgrounds.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Burano

Burano is a small island that can be explored by foot or bike. Visitors can enjoy leisurely walks and stunning scenery while exploring old streets or the scenic Burano lagoon. Bike rentals can be obtained on the island, and there are several recommended biking routes within the surrounding region.

Best Things to Do in Burano: Activities and Tours

Burano Lace Making Workshop

For those interested in traditional Italian handicrafts, Burano offers the perfect chance to experience the intricate art of lace making. Visitors can take part in a lace-making workshop and learn the techniques from the masters themselves.

Photograph the Colorful Houses of Burano

The colorful houses of Burano offer a lovely scene to photograph. Visitors can explore the streets of Burano and take numerous pictures of these beautiful houses.

Burano Food Tour

A food tour is the right way to explore Burano’s food scene. Visitors can indulge in the tastiest and unique dishes of this island.

Food and Drink Scene of Burano

Must-Try Dishes

Buzara

Buzara is a popular seafood dish made with local shellfish, garlic, and white wine. It’s served in most of Burano’s restaurants and is a must-try for seafood lovers.

Risi e Bisi

Risi e Bisi is a delicious Venetian dish made of rice and locally grown peas cooked with onion and bacon.

Local Beverages

Spritz

Spritz is a popular aperitif drink made from white wine, a bitter liqueur called Aperol, and soda water. The drink is often served with a slice of orange.

Prosecco

Prosecco is a quality sparkling wine produced in Veneto’s wine region, including Burano.

Where to Stay in Burano: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

There are several excellent accommodation options on the island. Visitors can choose from a range of hotels, hostels, and guesthouses offering comfortable and affordable stay options.

Best Hotels in Burano

Casa Burano

Casa Burano is an intimate and luxurious guesthouse in the heart of Burano. It offers lovely accommodations, friendly service, and delicious breakfast to its guests.

Venissa Wine Resort

Venissa Wine Resort is a highly recommended resort that offers excellent accommodations, fine dining, and exclusive wine tasting experiences. A lovely place to stay with its own vineyard and a serene atmosphere.

What to Buy in Burano: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Locally Produced Lace

Burano is famous for its world-renowned lace. Visitors can find many lace boutiques and shops on the island selling the intricate handmade fabrics.

Traditional Ceramics

Traditional ceramics are another popular souvenir you will find in Burano. Visitors can buy handcrafted ceramics made in the traditional Venetian style.

Local Culture and Customs of Burano

The island’s residents are primarily fishermen and lace makers who have preserved their traditions for generations. The locals are known for their friendly behavior and hospitality, making visitors feel welcome.

In Burano, there are many cultural festivals and events hosted throughout the year, like the Festa di San Martino or St Martin’s Day in November, and Regata delle Befane or The Befana Regatta in January.

Best Time to Visit Burano

The best time to visit Burano is during the spring or summer. The months of March through May and September through November offer the most pleasant weather, with fewer crowds. The summer months can be quite hot and crowded on the island.

Do’s

  • Rent a bike to explore the island.
  • Take pictures of the colorful homes.

Do:nt’s

  • Don’t swim in the canals as it’s illegal and can attract hefty fines.
  • Don’t litter the island, maintain cleanliness.

Safety and Health

Safety in Burano

Burano is a safe destination with little in the way of violent crime. The island is relatively safe, but it is best to avoid venturing onto the quieter lanes alone at night.

It’s best to keep an eye on your personal belongings, particularly in crowded areas like markets and while on public transport.

Health in Burano

Burano has excellent medical facilities, so visitors need not worry about their health while on the island.

Getting to Burano: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Burano

The closest airport to Burano is Marco Polo International Airport (VCE) in Venice. From there, visitors can take a taxi, bus, or boat service to reach Burano island.

Bus Stations Near Burano

The main bus terminal for Burano is located near the city center of Venice. From here, visitors can take a boat service to Burano island.

Other Options

Visitors can book a water taxi or private boat to reach Burano island straight from the airport or Venice city.

How to Get Around in Burano

The island is small enough to explore on foot, but visitors can also rent bikes to explore Burano. You can also take a boat tour of the lagoon.

Where to Go Next from Burano: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Venice

The city of Venice is just a short boat ride from Burano and is well worth a visit. Packed with culture, art, and history, Venice is a must-see for visitors exploring the Venetian lagoon.

Murano

Murano, a small island known for its glassblowing techniques is another popular destination located near Burano that visitors can explore. Visitors can take a 15-minute boat ride from Burano Island to reach Murano.

Conclusion

Burano is undoubtedly one of the most colorful islands in Italy, offering something unique for visitors of all ages. By following our comprehensive guide, you can create an itinerary that will allow you to make the most out of your visit to this hidden gem of Venice. From exploring brightly painted streets to indulging in local cuisine, Burano offers a memorable and culturally rich experience for travelers.
