Introduction to Belluno

Belluno is a charming city located in the province of Belluno, in the Veneto region of Northeast Italy. It is situated at the foothills of the Italian Dolomites, a UNESCO World Heritage site, and is known for its stunning natural beauty, rich history, and vibrant culture. If you are planning to visit Belluno, this comprehensive travel guide will help you make the most out of your trip.

List of Places to Visit in Belluno

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Cathedral of Belluno: The Cathedral of Belluno is a Roman Catholic cathedral located in the city center. Its impressive bell tower, which was built in the 15th century, dominates the city skyline. The cathedral houses several beautiful paintings and frescoes, including the famous Triptych of San Liberale.
  • Palazzo dei Rettori: Palazzo dei Rettori is a historic palace located in the center of Belluno. It was built in the 15th century and served as the residence of the Venetian governors. Today, it houses the Civic Museum, which showcases the history and culture of Belluno.

Natural Attractions of Belluno: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Dolomites: The Dolomites are a range of stunning mountain peaks that offer breathtaking views and outdoor activities such as hiking, skiing, and climbing. Some of the popular peaks in the area include Mount Pelmo, Mount Civetta, and Mount Marmolada.
  • Parco Nazionale delle Dolomiti Bellunesi: Parco Nazionale delle Dolomiti Bellunesi is a national park located in Belluno. It is one of the largest parks in Italy, home to a diverse range of flora and fauna, and offers several hiking and nature trails.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Belluno

  • Rifugio Pranolz: Rifugio Pranolz is a mountain hut located at an altitude of 1,393 meters in the heart of the Dolomites. It serves as a starting point for several trails that offer stunning views of the surrounding mountains.
  • Belluno Bike Path: The Belluno Bike Path is a 33-kilometer cycling route that winds through the Prosecco hills, the Dolomites, and the historic city center of Belluno.

Best Things to Do in Belluno: Activities and Tours

  • Wine Tasting: The Prosecco hills, situated near Belluno, are known for their excellent wine. Take a wine-tasting tour and discover the rich flavors of the region’s sparkling white wines.
  • Venetian Villas: Take a day trip to the nearby Venetian Villas, which are a group of 18th-century country estates that showcase the Venetian aristocratic lifestyle.

Week-Long Itinerary for Belluno

Food and Drink Scene of Belluno

Must-Try Dishes

  • Bigoli: Bigoli is a traditional pasta dish made with a flour and water dough, similar to spaghetti but thicker. It is usually served with a meat-based sauce or cacio e pepe.
  • Tiramisu: Tiramisu is a classic Italian dessert made of coffee-soaked ladyfinger biscuits, layered with a creamy mascarpone cheese filling.

Local Beverages

  • Prosecco: Prosecco is a sparkling white wine produced in the Prosecco hills near Belluno.
  • Grappa: Grappa is a grape-based alcoholic beverage that is popular in Italy. It is usually served as an after-dinner drink.

Where to Stay in Belluno: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Albergo Cappello e Cadore: Albergo Cappello e Cadore is a historic hotel located in the heart of the city center. It offers comfortable rooms and a restaurant that serves traditional Italian cuisine.
  • Casa Alpina Sant’Apollonia: Casa Alpina Sant’Apollonia is a cozy mountain lodge located near the Dolomites. It offers comfortable rooms, a restaurant, and a bar.

What to Buy in Belluno: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Prosecco: Bring home a bottle of the region’s famous sparking white wine.
  • Belluno Cheese: Belluno is known for its delicious cheese, particularly the Asiago and Morlacco.

Local Culture and Customs of Belluno

  • Family Values: Family values play an essential role in Belluno’s culture, and families often gather for meals and celebrations.
  • Religious Festivals: Belluno celebrates several religious festivals throughout the year, including the Feast of Saints, which commemorates the city’s patron saints.

Best Time to Visit Belluno

The best time to visit Belluno is between April and October when the weather is pleasant, and outdoor activities are available.

Do’s and Don’ts of Belluno

Do:

  • Respect local customs and traditions.
  • Explore the stunning landscape and enjoy outdoor activities.

Don’t:

  • Wear inappropriate clothing in religious sites.
  • Litter in public areas.

Safety and Health

Safety in Belluno

Belluno is a relatively safe city. However, as with any travel destination, it is always advisable to take precautions, such as keeping your valuables in a secure place and being aware of your surroundings.

Health in Belluno

Belluno has several hospitals and medical facilities. EU citizens can get medical care with their European Health Insurance Card (EHIC).

Getting to Belluno: Transportation Options and Logistics

  • Airports Near Belluno: The nearest airports to Belluno are Venice Marco Polo Airport and Treviso Airport.
  • Bus Stations Near Belluno: Belluno is well-connected to other Italian cities through regional bus services.
  • Other Options: Trains and rental cars are also available to reach Belluno.

How to Get Around in Belluno

The city center of Belluno is small enough to explore on foot. However, to reach nearby attractions and the Dolomites, cars or guided tours are recommended.

Where to Go Next from Belluno: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Venice: Venice, one of the most romantic cities in Italy, is located just a few hours away from Belluno and is easily accessible by train or car.
  • Cortina d’Ampezzo: Cortina d’Ampezzo is a popular ski resort town located in the Italian Dolomites.

Conclusion

Belluno is a beautiful city that offers visitors a mix of stunning natural landscapes, rich culture, and delicious food and wine. This comprehensive travel guide will help you plan your trip to Belluno and make the most out of your visit.
