Introduction to Feltre

Located in the province of Belluno in the Veneto region of Northeast Italy, Feltre is a charming town that offers a perfect blend of history, culture, and natural beauty to its visitors. Historically significant landmarks, outdoor adventure opportunities, and a lively food scene make Feltre a must-visit destination in Italy.

List of Places to Visit in Feltre

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Feltre is home to many historic sites that offer a glimpse into its rich cultural heritage. Some must-visit attractions include:

  • Palazzo della Ragione: a 13th-century building that served as a courthouse and now houses a museum dedicated to the history of Feltre.
  • Cathedral of St. Peter the Apostle: a beautiful 12th-century cathedral with stunning architecture and artwork.
  • Castello di Alboino: a fortress that dates back to the Middle Ages and offers spectacular views of the town and surrounding mountains.
  • Museo Civico: a museum that showcases the history and culture of Feltre through art, artifacts, and interactive exhibits.

Natural Attractions of Feltre: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Feltre is blessed with natural beauty and offers many outdoor adventure opportunities to its visitors. The breathtaking views of the mountains and valleys make Feltre a perfect place for hiking, biking, and other outdoor activities. Some of the best outdoor attractions include:

  • Dolomites: Located just a short drive away, the Dolomites offer fantastic trails for hiking, skiing, and snowboarding.
  • Monte Avena: A popular spot for paragliding, Monte Avena also offers stunning views of the surrounding mountains.
  • Parco Nazionale Dolomiti Bellunesi: A large national park located near Feltre that offers many hiking trails, waterfalls, and wildlife sightings.
  • Colle del Nevegal: A scenic spot near Feltre that offers skiing, hiking, biking, and other outdoor activities.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Feltre

Feltre offers many hiking and biking routes that vary in difficulty and distance. Some of the most popular trails include:

  • Feltre-Pedavena Bike Path: A 20-kilometer path that connects Feltre and Pedavena, with beautiful views of the mountains and rivers.
  • Feltre Ring: A 35-kilometer route that passes through many historic sites and scenic spots in and around Feltre.
  • Monte Grappa Circuit: A challenging 70-kilometer circuit that offers spectacular views of the Dolomites and the surrounding valleys.

Best Things to Do in Feltre: Activities and Tours

Apart from the above-mentioned attractions and activities, Feltre offers many other things to do and see. Some of the best activities and tours include:

  • Wine Tasting Tour: The Veneto region is known for its wine, and Feltre offers many tours that take visitors to the best wineries in the area.
  • Cooking Classes: Learn to cook authentic Italian dishes with fresh, locally sourced ingredients in Feltre.
  • Festivals and Events: Feltre hosts many festivals and events throughout the year, such as the Palio di Feltre, a medieval festival that takes place in September.

Week-Long Itinerary for Feltre

For a week-long visit to Feltre, here is a suggested itinerary:

  • Day 1: Visit historic sites in Feltre, such as Palazzo della Ragione and the Cathedral of St. Peter the Apostle.
  • Day 2: Take a hiking or biking trip along the Feltre-Pedavena Bike Path.
  • Day 3: Explore the Dolomites, either by hiking or skiing/snowboarding.
  • Day 4: Go paragliding at Monte Avena or explore the Colle del Nevegal.
  • Day 5: Visit the Castello di Alboino and the Museo Civico.
  • Day 6: Take a wine-tasting tour or attend a cooking class.
  • Day 7: Attend a festival or event in Feltre before departing.

Food and Drink Scene of Feltre

Must-Try Dishes

Feltre offers many delicious dishes that are unique to the area. Some must-try dishes include:

  • Maccheroni alla Chitarra: A handmade pasta dish that is usually served with a tomato-based sauce and spicy sausage.
  • Sopressa Veneta: A dry-cured pork sausage that is distinctive to the Veneto region.
  • Asiago Cheese: A semi-hard cheese made from cow’s milk that is commonly used in many traditional Italian dishes.

Local Beverages

Apart from wine, Feltre also offers many other local beverages that are worth trying. Some of the best local drinks include:

  • Grappa: A strong alcoholic drink made from pomace, which is the leftover residue from winemaking.
  • Spritz: A refreshing aperitif drink made with Aperol, Prosecco, and soda water.
  • Bellini: A classic cocktail made with peach puree and Prosecco.

Where to Stay in Feltre: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Feltre offers many accommodation options that cater to every budget and preference. Some of the best places to stay in Feltre include:

  • Villa San Liberale: A cozy B&B that offers beautiful views of the mountains and valleys.
  • Hotel Doriguzzi: A charming hotel located in the center of town that offers comfortable rooms and a restaurant.
  • Ostello San Giuseppe: A budget-friendly hostel that is ideal for backpackers and budget travelers.

What to Buy in Feltre: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Some of the best souvenirs and local products to buy in Feltre include:

  • Asiago Cheese: Freshly made Asiago cheese can be bought from local markets and shops.
  • Handmade Crafts: Feltre is known for its ceramic, glass, and wood crafts that make for unique souvenirs.
  • Wine and Grappa: The Veneto region is known for its wine and Grappa, which can be bought from local wineries and shops.

Local Culture and Customs of Feltre

Feltre has a unique culture that is influenced by its long history and stunning natural surroundings. The people of Feltre are warm and welcoming to visitors and take pride in their local traditions and customs. Some of the notable customs of Feltre include:

  • Palio di Feltre: A historic festival that takes place in September, where different neighborhoods of Feltre compete in a series of games and competitions.
  • Carnaval di Feltre: A colorful and exuberant carnival that takes place in February.

Best Time to Visit Feltre

The best time to visit Feltre is during the summer months (June to September) when the weather is warm and sunny, making it ideal for outdoor activities and sightseeing. However, Feltre is a year-round destination, and visitors can enjoy the snow-covered mountains in the winter and the colorful fall foliage in autumn.

Do’s and Don’ts of Feltre

Do:

  • Respect local traditions and customs.
  • Be friendly and polite to locals.
  • Try the local cuisine and beverages.
  • Learn some basic Italian phrases.

Don’t:

  • Wear revealing clothes in public places.
  • Be disrespectful towards historic sites and monuments.
  • Be loud and rowdy in public places.

Safety and Health

Safety in Feltre

Feltre is a safe town, and visitors are unlikely to face any safety concerns. However, it’s always advisable to take basic precautions while traveling, such as being aware of your surroundings and not carrying too much cash or valuables.

Health in Feltre

Feltre has excellent healthcare facilities, including hospitals and clinics that provide high-quality medical care to its residents and visitors. However, it’s always advisable to have travel insurance before traveling to Italy.

Getting to Feltre: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Feltre

The closest airport to Feltre is Venice Marco Polo Airport, which is about 120 km away. Other nearby airports include Treviso Airport and Verona Airport.

Bus Stations Near Feltre

Feltre has a central bus station from where visitors can take buses to other destinations in the region.

Other Options

Visitors can also reach Feltre by train from Venice, Milan, or Verona.

How to Get Around in Feltre

Feltre is a small town that can easily be explored on foot. Visitors can also rent a bike or take local buses or taxis to explore the town and surrounding areas.

Where to Go Next from Feltre: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Feltre is located in a stunning region that offers many nearby destinations and excursions for visitors. Some of the best places to visit include:

  • Venice: Located about 100 km away, Venice is a beautiful city that is famous for its canals, bridges, and stunning architecture.
  • Verona: A historic city located about 150 km away from Feltre, Verona is renowned for its Roman ruins, stunning gardens, and beautiful architecture.
  • Cortina d’Ampezzo: A ski resort town located about 40 km away from Feltre, Cortina d’Ampezzo is a popular destination for winter sports enthusiasts.

Conclusion

Feltre is a stunning destination that offers visitors a perfect blend of history, culture, and natural beauty. With its historic landmarks, outdoor adventure opportunities, and distinctive food scene, Feltre is a must-visit destination for anyone traveling to Italy.
