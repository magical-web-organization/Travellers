Your Ultimate Travel Guide to Verona, Italy

Nestled in the Northeast region of Italy, Verona is a beautiful and historic city known for its connections to Shakespeare’s Romeo and Juliet and its incredible food and wine scene. With a mix of ancient architecture, stunning views of the Alps, and endless opportunities for adventure, Verona is a must-visit destination for any traveler.

Introduction to Verona

Verona is a city in Veneto, Italy, located in the northern region of the country. As a UNESCO World Heritage site, Verona is known for its Roman ruins, ancient architecture, and cultural importance. The city offers a mix of urban excitement and natural beauty that makes it a popular destination for all types of travelers.

List of Places to Visit in Verona

When in Verona, there are numerous places you should visit to experience the city’s beauty and charm.

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Verona is rich in history and culture, and there are several museums and historical sites you should visit to gain a deeper understanding of both.

  • Arena di Verona: Built in the 1st century AD, this amphitheater is one of the most well-preserved Roman arenas in existence and still hosts events today.
  • Juliet’s House: A beloved attraction in Verona, Juliet’s House is said to have belonged to the Capulet family and is where Shakespeare’s Romeo and Juliet was set.
  • Castelvecchio Museum: A medieval castle turned museum that houses art and archaeological finds from the city’s history.
  • Basilica di San Zeno: A stunning Romanesque church that dates back to the 10th century.

Natural Attractions of Verona: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

While Verona is known for its ancient architecture, it is also home to natural beauty and opportunities for outdoor adventures.

  • Giardino Giusti: A gorgeous 16th-century garden with Venetian-inspired landscaping and stunning views of the city and the surrounding hills.
  • Lake Garda: Located just outside Verona, Lake Garda is the largest lake in Italy and offers stunning scenery and opportunities for outdoor activities like fishing and windsurfing.
  • Adige River: Flowing through the city, the Adige River offers opportunities for river cruises and kayaking.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Verona

For the more adventurous traveler, Verona offers several hiking and biking trails with incredible views of the surrounding hills and countryside.

  • Torricelle: A scenic route that takes you through the city’s vineyards, farms, and olive groves.
  • Lessinia: A mountain range just north of Verona with hiking routes that offer incredible views of the surrounding countryside.
  • Ciclopista del Sole: A 260 km bike trail that spans from Verona to Bolzano and takes you through some of Italy’s most beautiful landscapes.

Best Things to Do in Verona: Activities and Tours

There are countless things to do in Verona, so we’ve compiled a list of the best activities and tours to try during your visit.

  • Wine Tasting Tour: Verona is known for its excellent wine, and a tour of the surrounding vineyards and wineries offers a great opportunity to try some of the local varieties.
  • Cooking Classes: Take a cooking class to try your hand at the region’s delicious cuisine and bring back some new recipes to try at home.
  • Walking Tour: A walking tour of the city is a great way to explore the sights and learn about Verona’s rich history from a local guide.

Week-Long Itinerary for Verona

If you have a week to spend in Verona, here’s an itinerary that will take you to some of the city’s highlights and beyond.

Food and Drink Scene of Verona

Verona is a food lover’s paradise, offering a mix of hearty pasta dishes, fresh seafood, and, of course, wine.

Must-Try Dishes

  • Risotto alla Veronese: A rich and creamy risotto dish made with bone marrow and grated cheese.
  • Bigoli con l’anatra: A hearty pasta dish made with duck ragù and a thick spaghetti-like noodle.
  • Bollito Misto: A classic Veronese dish featuring a mix of boiled meats and vegetables.

Local Beverages

  • Valpolicella: A popular red wine produced in the surrounding hills and vineyards near Verona.
  • Amarone della Valpolicella: A rich red wine made from dried grapes and known for its complex, full-bodied flavor.
  • Spritz: A refreshing cocktail made with Aperol, Prosecco, and soda water.

Where to Stay in Verona: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

From budget-friendly hostels to luxury hotels, Verona offers a range of accommodations to suit all types of travelers.

  • Hotel Accademia: A luxury hotel located in the heart of Verona’s historic center.
  • B&B Cittadella: A cozy bed and breakfast located in a quiet area within the city walls.
  • Ostello Verona: A budget-friendly option with shared dormitory-style rooms located just outside the city center.
  • Hotel San Pietro: A family-friendly option with a pool located just a short distance from the center of Verona.

What to Buy in Verona: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Verona is known for its artisanal products and traditional crafts, with several shops selling handmade souvenirs and local products that make great gifts.

  • Ceramics and Pottery: Visit a local pottery shop to find handmade ceramics and pottery inspired by the region’s history and traditions.
  • Grappa: A traditional Italian alcoholic beverage made from pomace, the leftover skins, seeds and stems of grapes that were used to make wine.
  • Olive Oil: Verona’s surrounding farms are known for their high-quality olive oil, so make sure to bring some home as a souvenir.

Local Culture and Customs of Verona

Verona is a proud and hospitable city with a rich cultural heritage. As a tourist, it’s important to be respectful of the city’s traditions and customs, such as:

  • Dress Appropriately: Verona is a conservative city, so it’s important to dress modestly when visiting churches and other religious sites.
  • Greet Locals: When entering a shop or restaurant, it’s customary to greet the owner or staff with a friendly “Buongiorno” or “Buonasera.”
  • Be Polite: Italians value politeness, so make sure to say “Grazie” or “Prego” when appropriate and avoid being too loud or boisterous in public places.

Best Time to Visit Verona

Verona is a year-round destination, but the best time to visit depends on your preferences and what you want to experience in the city. The summer months are the busiest and most crowded, but also offer the warmest weather and a lively atmosphere. Spring and fall offer milder temperatures and fewer crowds, making it a great time to visit if you prefer a more relaxed pace. The winter months can be cold and rainy, but also offer a chance to experience the city’s cozy atmosphere and enjoy some of its festive traditions, like Christmas markets.

Do’s and Don’ts of Verona

When visiting Verona, there are a few important things to keep in mind to ensure a successful and enjoyable trip.

Do:

  • Do try the local wine and food.
  • Do respect the local culture and customs.
  • Do dress appropriately when visiting religious sites.
  • Do be polite and friendly to locals.

Don’t:

  • Don’t litter or leave trash behind.
  • Don’t be too loud or disruptive in public places.
  • Don’t forget to carry cash, especially in smaller shops and restaurants.
  • Don’t plan to visit too many attractions in one day - take your time and enjoy the city at a relaxed pace.

Safety and Health

Verona is generally a safe city, but it’s important to take the necessary precautions while traveling.

Safety in Verona

  • Keep your belongings close and be aware of pickpockets especially in crowded areas.
  • Avoid walking alone at night and stay in well-lit areas.
  • Be cautious when crossing the street as traffic can be busy in the city center.
  • Follow all health and safety guidelines, especially during the COVID-19 pandemic.

Health in Verona

  • Make sure to have adequate travel insurance.
  • Stay hydrated and carry a refillable water bottle.
  • Be wary of sunburn during the summer months.
  • Verify if a doctor’s visit or medical treatment is covered by your insurance.

Getting to Verona: Transportation Options and Logistics

Verona is well connected to the rest of Italy and beyond, with several transportation options available for travelers.

Airports Near Verona

  • Verona Villafranca Airport: The city’s main airport, located just 10 km from the city center.

Bus Stations Near Verona

  • Verona Porta Nuova Station: A major railway and bus station located in the city center with connections to other parts of Italy and Europe.

Other Options

  • Train: Verona Porta Nuova Station is also a major railway station that offers connections to other parts of Italy and Europe.
  • Car: Verona is easily accessible by car and there are several car rental companies available at the airport.

How to Get Around in Verona

Verona is a pedestrian-friendly city with most sights within walking distance of each other. If you prefer to use public transportation, there are several buses that run throughout the city, and taxis are also widely available. For a more unique experience, you can also rent a bike or a Vespa to explore the city.

Where to Go Next from Verona: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Verona is surrounded by several picturesque towns and cities that make great day trips from the city.

  • Mantua: A beautiful Renaissance city located just 45 minutes from Verona.
  • Valpolicella Wine Region: A nearby wine region with beautiful vineyards and wineries to visit.
  • Lake Garda: A stunning lake just outside Verona with several towns and attractions worth visiting.

Conclusion

Verona is a city that offers something for everyone. With its rich history, stunning architecture, beautiful natural scenery, and delicious food and wine, Verona is a destination that should be on every traveler’s bucket list. By following this comprehensive travel guide, you’re sure to have an unforgettable experience in this beautiful Italian city.
