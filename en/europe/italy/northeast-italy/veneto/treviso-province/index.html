Introduction to Treviso (province)

Located in the Veneto region of Northeast Italy, Treviso province is a charming destination that offers a mix of natural beauty, rich history, and a unique cultural heritage. Bordered by the Dolomite Mountains to the north, the province boasts a diverse landscape that ranges from vineyards and rolling hills to rivers and pristine lakes.

The province’s picturesque towns and cities are characterized by their traditional architecture, ancient churches, and historical landmarks, which provide a glimpse into the region’s past. Treviso province is also known for its vibrant local cuisine, which includes regional specialties such as seafood, truffles, and Prosecco wine.

Whether you’re interested in outdoor adventures, cultural attractions, or mouth-watering gastronomy, Treviso province has something for everyone. In this travel guide, we’ll explore the best places to visit, things to do, and experiences to have in the area. Let’s get started.

List of Places to Visit in Treviso (province)

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Treviso province has a rich cultural heritage that is reflected in its numerous museums, art galleries, and historical landmarks. Whether you’re fascinated by ancient architecture or modern art, there is something for every taste. Here are some of the top cultural attractions in Treviso province:

  • Museo Civico di Castelfranco Veneto: This museum is home to a collection of 16th-century Venetian paintings, as well as works by local artists.

  • Museo Civico di Treviso: Housed in a former monastery, this museum features a range of exhibits on local history, archeology, and art.

  • Cattedrale di Santa Maria Assunta: This impressive 12th-century cathedral is a prime example of Romanesque architecture and features stunning frescoes by Tomaso da Modena.

  • Villa Barbaro: This magnificent 16th-century villa in Maser was designed by Andrea Palladio and features frescoes by Veronese.

Natural Attractions of Treviso (province): Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Treviso province is blessed with a stunning natural landscape that offers plenty of opportunities for outdoor adventures and scenic views. From hiking and biking to swimming and sailing, there is no shortage of activities to enjoy. Here are some of the top natural attractions in Treviso province:

  • Lago di Revine: This pristine lake is a popular spot for swimming, fishing, and sailing.

  • Parco Naturale del Fiume Sile: This riverside park offers plenty of opportunities for hiking, cycling, and birdwatching.

  • Monte Grappa: This mountain in the Dolomites offers some of the best views in the province and is a popular destination for hiking, paragliding, and skiing.

  • Prosecco Vineyards: The hills around Valdobbiadene are known for their vineyards, which produce the world-famous Prosecco wine. A wine-tasting tour is a must-do activity for any wine lover.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Treviso (province)

Treviso province is a paradise for hikers and bikers, with a network of trails that crisscross the region’s beautiful landscape. Some of the top hiking and biking routes include:

  • Ciclovia dell’Acqua: This cycling route runs along the Sile River and offers plenty of picturesque views.

  • Sentiero delle Cime: This hiking trail in the Dolomites is a challenging but rewarding trek that leads to some of the best views in the province.

  • Anello del Prosecco: This circular route takes you through the heart of the Prosecco wine country, passing by hillside vineyards and quaint villages.

Best Things to Do in Treviso (province): Activities and Tours

In addition to hiking and biking, there are plenty of other activities and tours to enjoy in Treviso province. Here are some of the top things to do:

  • Take a walking tour of Treviso: This charming city is home to many ancient churches, beautiful squares, and traditional markets.

  • Go on a wine-tasting tour: The Prosecco wine country is a must-visit destination for wine lovers. Numerous vineyards offer tours and tastings.

  • Attend a local festival: Treviso province hosts many local festivals throughout the year, ranging from traditional fairs to cultural events to food and wine festivals.

Week-Long Itinerary for Treviso (province)

To make the most of your time in Treviso province, it’s a good idea to plan your itinerary in advance. Here’s a sample one-week itinerary to get you started:

Day 1: Explore the center of Treviso and visit the city’s historical landmarks, including the cathedral and the Palazzo dei Trecento.

Day 2: Take a wine-tasting tour of the Prosecco vineyards around Valdobbiadene and visit the charming villages of Follina and Cison di Valmarino.

Day 3: Take a day trip to Bassano del Grappa and visit the famous Grappa museum. Stop by Villa Barbaro in Maser on the way back.

Day 4: Hike the Anello del Prosecco trail and enjoy the views of the vineyards and countryside.

Day 5: Visit the medieval town of Castelfranco Veneto and the nearby Villa Emo in Fanzolo.

Day 6: Explore the natural beauty of Monte Grappa and take a paragliding or biking excursion.

Day 7: Attend a local festival or market in one of the province’s charming towns, such as Asolo or Conegliano.

Food and Drink Scene of Treviso (province)

Treviso province is renowned for its delicious traditional cuisine, which features fresh seafood, truffles, and the world-famous Prosecco wine. Here are some of the top dishes and drinks to try:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Baccalà alla vicentina: a traditional Venetian dish made with salted cod and served with polenta.

  • Pasta e fagioli: a hearty bean soup made with pasta and vegetables.

  • Tiramisù: a creamy dessert made with ladyfingers, espresso, and mascarpone cheese.

Local Beverages

  • Prosecco: Treviso province is home to some of the world’s best Prosecco wines, which are characterized by their light, crisp flavor.

  • Grappa: This traditional Italian spirit is made from leftover grape skins and seeds and is commonly served as a digestif.

  • Spritz: A popular aperitif in the Veneto region, made with Prosecco, Aperol, and soda water.

Where to Stay in Treviso (province): Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Treviso province offers a wide range of accommodation options to suit all budgets and preferences. From elegant hotels to cozy guesthouses, there’s something for every type of traveler. Here are some of the top places to stay:

  • Hotel Villa Soligo: This elegant hotel is located in the heart of the Prosecco wine country and features a swimming pool and spa.

  • Agriturismo Althea: This charming farmhouse offers cozy rooms and apartments surrounded by vineyards and hills.

  • Hotel Canova: This centrally located hotel in Treviso offers comfortable rooms and a delicious breakfast.

What to Buy in Treviso (province): Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Treviso province is a great place to shop for local products and souvenirs. From handmade crafts to regional delicacies, there’s something for everyone. Here are some of the top things to buy:

  • Prosecco wine: Bring home a bottle or two of the world-famous Prosecco wine.

  • Handcrafted lace: Burano lace is a traditional craft that originated in the nearby town of Burano.

  • Truffles: Treviso province is known for its truffle production, and you can find both black and white truffles in local markets.

Local Culture and Customs of Treviso (province)

Treviso province has a unique cultural heritage that is reflected in its traditions, cuisine, and architecture. Here are some aspects of the local culture and customs that you might encounter:

  • Venetian dialect: The Venetian dialect is still spoken in many parts of Treviso province, and you might hear locals using it in everyday conversation.

  • Traditional cuisine: Treviso province is known for its delicious seafood, truffles, and Prosecco wine, which are a staple of the local cuisine.

  • Ancient churches: The province is home to many ancient churches, each with its own unique architecture and history.

Best Time to Visit Treviso (province)

The best time to visit Treviso province depends on your preferences and interests. If you’re interested in outdoor activities and scenic views, the best time to visit is from late spring to early autumn (May to September). If you’re interested in cultural events and festivals, plan your visit around one of the many local celebrations held throughout the year.

Do’s and Don’ts of Treviso (province)

To make the most of your trip to Treviso province, here are some do’s and don’ts to keep in mind:

Do:

  • Try the local cuisine and wine: Treviso province is known for its delicious traditional dishes and the world-famous Prosecco wine.

  • Visit the historic landmarks: The province’s ancient churches and landmarks are a testament to its rich history and cultural heritage.

  • Learn a few phrases in local dialect: Locals will appreciate your efforts to speak their language and may be more willing to interact with you.

Don’t:

  • Wear revealing clothing to religious sites: If you plan to visit one of the province’s churches, be sure to dress conservatively.

  • Drink and drive: Italy has strict laws against driving under the influence of alcohol, so it’s best to avoid drinking and driving altogether.

  • Rely solely on public transportation: While public transportation is available, renting a car is the best way to explore the province’s many hidden gems.

Safety and Health

Safety in Treviso (province)

Treviso province is generally a safe destination for travelers, with a low crime rate and friendly locals. However, it’s always a good idea to take basic safety precautions, such as:

  • Avoid carrying large amounts of cash or valuables in crowded areas.

  • Be aware of your surroundings, especially in busy tourist areas.

  • Keep your belongings close at hand and be mindful of pickpockets.

Health in Treviso (province)

Treviso province has a good healthcare system, with many hospitals and clinics located throughout the region. If you need medical attention, you can go to any hospital emergency department or call an ambulance by dialling 118.

Getting to Treviso (province): Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Treviso (province)

The nearest airport to Treviso province is Treviso Airport (TSF), which is located around 3 km from the city center. Alternatively, you can fly into Venice Marco Polo Airport (VCE), which is around 30 km from Treviso province.

Bus Stations Near Treviso (province)

Treviso has a central bus station located near the train station, which serves many destinations throughout the region. There are also several private bus companies that offer routes to nearby towns and cities.

Other Options

If you prefer to drive, Treviso province is easily accessible by car and has an extensive network of highways and scenic roads.

How to Get Around in Treviso (province)

The best way to get around Treviso province is by car or bike. While public transportation is available, it can be unreliable and infrequent in some areas. To explore the province’s many hidden gems, it’s best to rent a car or bike.

Where to Go Next from Treviso (province): Nearby Destinations and Excursions

If you’re looking for other nearby destinations to visit after Treviso province, here are some options:

  • Venice: This world-famous city is just a short drive or train ride away from Treviso province and is known for its canals, cultural landmarks, and rich history.

  • Padua: This charming city is home to many ancient churches, art galleries, and a lively food scene.

  • Verona: This romantic city is known for its Roman amphitheater, Shakespearean associations, and charming old town.

Conclusion

Treviso province is a charming destination that offers a mix of natural beauty, rich history, and a unique cultural heritage. From its ancient churches and historical landmarks to its stunning vineyards and scenic trails, there is no shortage of things to see and do in this picturesque corner of Italy. Whether you’re looking for outdoor adventures, cultural experiences, or mouth-watering gastronomy, Treviso province is a must-visit destination for any traveler.
