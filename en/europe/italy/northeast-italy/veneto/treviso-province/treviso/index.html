Introduction to Treviso

Located in the heart of Veneto region, Treviso is a small city with a rich history and charming atmosphere. Often overshadowed by its more famous neighbours Venice and Verona, Treviso is an excellent destination for those seeking off-the-beaten-track experiences. With its picturesque canals, medieval architecture, and vibrant food scene, Treviso offers something for everyone.

List of Places to Visit in Treviso

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Museo Civico Bailo: This museum houses an impressive collection of medieval and Renaissance art and artifacts from Treviso and its surrounding areas.
  • Cathedral of San Pietro: Built in the 15th century, this impressive cathedral boasts exceptional art and architecture, including an impressive fresco cycle.
  • Palazzo dei Trecento: One of the most iconic landmarks in Treviso, this imposing palace served as the seat of power of the city’s governing council.

Natural Attractions of Treviso: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Parco Naturale del Fiume Sile: This nature park is a paradise for nature lovers, offering scenic trails for hiking, cycling, and bird-watching, as well as peaceful boat rides on the river.
  • Montello: This beautiful hill offers breathtaking views of the surrounding countryside, as well as excellent opportunities for hiking, mountain biking, and rock climbing.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Treviso

  • Trails of Montello: This area offers numerous trails winding through the hills and valleys surrounding Treviso. Ideal for hiking, cycling, and running enthusiasts, the Montello trails offer breathtaking views of the landscape and wildlife.

Best Things to Do in Treviso: Activities and Tours

  • Wine Tasting Tour: Veneto is known for its excellent wines, and Treviso is no exception. A guided wine tour, starting from Treviso, is an ideal way to explore the vineyards, taste some of the region’s best wines, and learn about the wine-making process.
  • Boat Tour of the Canals: Get a new perspective on Treviso by taking a guided boat tour of its canals. Along the way, you’ll pass by some of the city’s most beautiful bridges, monuments, and buildings.

Food and Drink Scene of Treviso

Must-Try Dishes

  • Risotto ai Radicchio: This creamy risotto made with Treviso’s signature radicchio is a must-try for those who love Italian cuisine.
  • Baccalà alla Vicentina: This delicious fish dish is a local specialty, flavoured with garlic, olive oil, parsley, and anchovies.

Local Beverages

  • Prosecco: The Prosecco region is located just a few kilometers from Treviso, and the city is an excellent starting point for a wine-tasting tour of the area.
  • Spritz: A popular aperitif in Veneto, Spritz is made by combining prosecco, Aperol (or Campari), and soda water.

Where to Stay in Treviso: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Hotel Continental: Located in the heart of Treviso’s historic center, this elegant hotel is a great choice for those seeking comfort and style.
  • Albergo Il Focolare: This cozy guesthouse offers comfortable rooms and a homely atmosphere at affordable rates.
  • Ostello San Biagio: Ideal for budget travellers, this youth hostel offers basic amenities and a friendly vibe.

What to Buy in Treviso: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Radicchio di Treviso: Treviso’s signature radicchio is a healthy and delicious vegetable, which can be found in various forms: fresh, pickled, and dried.
  • Prosecco: Bring back a bottle of the famous bubbly to share with friends and family.

Local Culture and Customs of Treviso

  • Language: Italian is the official language, but most people in Treviso speak Venetian, a dialect of the Italian language.
  • Carnival: Like other Italian cities, Treviso celebrates Carnival in February, a festival of joy and fun, marked by colorful parades, parties, and masquerades.

Best Time to Visit Treviso

Spring (April to May) and Autumn (September to November) are the best times to visit Treviso. The weather is mild, crowds are thinner, and prices are lower compared to the peak summer season.

Do’s and Don’ts of Treviso

Do:

  • Try the local food and wine
  • Take a boat tour of the canals
  • Learn a few words of Italian

Don’t:

  • Be disrespectful of local customs and traditions
  • Take photographs where signage forbids
  • Expect to hail taxis on the street easily

Safety and Health

Safety in Treviso

Treviso is a safe city, with low rates of violent crime and theft. However, as in any tourist destination, visitors should be cautious with pickpockets in crowded areas.

Health in Treviso

No vaccinations are required to visit Treviso, and the city’s water is safe to drink. Health care is of excellent quality, with many hospitals and clinics offering English-speaking staff.

Getting to Treviso: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Treviso

The nearest airport to Treviso is Venice Marco Polo Airport, which is served by many national and international airlines.

Bus Stations Near Treviso

Treviso has a bus station located close to the train station. You can take a bus to almost every town in Veneto, as well as many other Italian cities.

Other Options

  • Taxi: Taxis can be found at the airport and train station or can be booked through your hotel or online.
  • Rental Car: With excellent road access to many famous Italian cities and towns, hiring a car is a great option for exploring the region at your own pace.

How to Get Around in Treviso

Treviso is a compact city, and most of the attractions are within walking distance. However, to reach the surrounding areas, it’s best to rent a car or take public transport.

Where to Go Next from Treviso: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Venice

Just 30 minutes away by train, Venice is one of the most unique and beautiful cities in the world, famous for its canals, bridges, and stunning architecture.

Verona

Just an hour’s drive from Treviso, Verona is a charming city that’s famous for being the setting of Shakespeare’s Romeo and Juliet.

Conclusion

Whether you’re a history buff, outdoors enthusiast, or food lover, Treviso has something for everyone. So pack your bags and get ready for an Italian adventure that you’ll never forget.
