    Introduction to Ravenna (Province)

    Ravenna (Province) is a region of Italy known for its wealth of historical monuments and iconic mosaics from the Byzantine era. Many monuments have been classified as UNESCO World Heritage Sites, making Ravenna (Province) one of the most culturally significant areas in Italy. The beaches along the Adriatic coast and the Po Delta Natural Park add a beautiful touch to the region.

    Food and Drink Scene of Ravenna (Province)

    Ravenna (Province) has a rich culinary heritage, with plenty of delicious local dishes and refreshing beverages to try. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

    Must-Try Dishes

    1. Cappelletti in brodo, a hearty meat-filled pasta dish served in a flavorful broth.
    2. Piadina, a traditional flatbread served with cheese, prosciutto, or vegetables.
    3. Passatelli, a type of pasta made with breadcrumbs, eggs, parmesan cheese, and nutmeg.

    Local Beverages

    1. Sangiovese di Romagna: A popular Italian red wine that pairs well with local cuisine.
    2. Lambrusco di Romagna: A lightly sparkling red or rose wine that’s perfect for summer.
    3. Nocino: A liqueur made from green walnuts that’s perfect for after-dinner sipping.
