Introduction to Forlì

Forlì is a picturesque city located in the province of Forlì-Cesena, in the Emilia-Romagna region of Northeast Italy. The city boasts a rich heritage, influenced by centuries of Roman, Byzantine, and Renaissance cultures.

Home to a revered university, world-renowned museums, and beautiful natural parks, Forlì is a must-visit destination for anyone planning a trip to Italy. With its vibrant streets, quaint shops, and exquisite dining options, Forlì is a perfect blend of history, culture, and modernity.

List of Places to Visit in Forlì

Forlì is a city with an abundance of things to see and do. Here is a list of some of the must-visit attractions in Forlì.

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Museums

  • Musei San Domenico - This museum complex located in the heart of the city center offers an impressive collection of artworks, sculptures, and artifacts from the Emilia-Romagna region.
  • Museo Archeologico - Explore the region’s rich archaeological heritage, including the Etruscan and Roman ruins.
  • Museo del Risorgimento - This museum is devoted to Italy’s unification and the revolutionary changes that occurred in the 19th century.

Historical Places

  • Piazza Aurelio Saffi - This charming square in the city center is surrounded by medieval towers and has a vibrant nightlife.
  • Palazzo Hercolani - This magnificent palace has beautiful Renaissance architecture and houses the Biblioteca Malatestiana, one of the oldest public libraries in Italy.

Natural Attractions of Forlì: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Montefiore Conca - Visit this small hilltop village and enjoy stunning views of the surrounding hills and valleys.
  • Parco della Resistenza - This natural park located in the city center has beautiful gardens and a popular jogging trail with scenic views.
  • Parco delle Foreste Casentinesi - This national park located outside the city offers hiking trails, camping facilities, and wildlife sightings.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Forlì

Forlì offers several hiking and biking routes that cater to both beginners and experienced outdoor enthusiasts. These routes offer stunning views of the surrounding hills, valleys, and landscapes. Here are some of the popular routes:

  • The Montefiore Loop - This 12 km route covers the scenic hills and valleys around Montefiore Conca.
  • The Foreste Casentinesi Route - This 25 km route covers the hilly terrain of the Foreste Casentinesi National Park.
  • The Santerno Valley Trail - This 20 km route runs along the picturesque Santerno River and is ideal for cycling and running.

Best Things to Do in Forlì: Activities and Tours

Forlì offers plenty of activities and tours that cater to all types of visitors. Here are some of the best things to do in Forlì:

  • Visit the Musei San Domenico for an art and culture experience.
  • Take a stroll in the historic center and explore the exquisite architecture.
  • Take a bike tour around the city and the surrounding hills.
  • Visit Montefiore Conca, a beautiful hilltop village with stunning views and historical attractions.
  • Explore Parco delle Foreste Casentinesi, a national park with beautiful hiking trails and wildlife.

Week-Long Itinerary for Forlì

To make the most of your visit to Forlì, here is our suggested week-long itinerary:

Day 1: Arrive in Forlì and take a walk around the historic center.

Day 2: Explore the Musei San Domenico and the Palazzo Hercolani.

Day 3: Take a bike tour around the city and the surrounding hills.

Day 4: Visit Montefiore Conca and enjoy stunning views of the surrounding hills and valleys.

Day 5: Explore the Parco della Resistenza and its beautiful gardens.

Day 6: Visit the Museo Archeologico and the Museo del Risorgimento.

Day 7: Take a day trip to the nearby towns of Cesenatico and Rimini.

Food and Drink Scene of Forlì

Forlì’s food scene is rich in flavor, tradition, and variety. Here are some of the must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Piadina Romagnola - This traditional flatbread stuffed with cheese, cold cuts, or vegetables is a staple of the region.
  • Cappelletti - These small, stuffed pasta bites are a regional delicacy.

Local Beverages

  • Sangiovese - This red wine is produced in the surrounding hills and is a popular choice for locals and visitors alike.
  • Nocino - This sweet liquor made from green walnuts is a popular digestif.

Where to Stay in Forlì: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Forlì offers a wide range of accommodation options, from budget-friendly hostels to luxurious hotels. Here are some of the best places to stay in Forlì:

  • Grand Hotel Forlì - This five-star hotel boasts a spa center, a gourmet restaurant, and luxurious accommodations.
  • Hotel Michelangelo - This three-star hotel is centrally located and provides excellent value for money.
  • B&B La Tremarella - This cozy guesthouse offers a homely atmosphere and is ideal for budget travelers.

What to Buy in Forlì: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Forlì is famous for its local products and traditional crafts. Here are some of the best gifts and souvenirs to bring home:

  • Piadina-making kit - This kit includes all the ingredients to make traditional piadinas at home.
  • Local ceramics - Forlì has a rich tradition of ceramics and the city offers many artisan shops and markets where you can find beautiful, hand-made pottery.

Local Culture and Customs of Forlì

Forlì has a long and proud cultural heritage, rich in traditions and customs. Here are some of the highlights:

  • The city celebrates the feast of Saint Rocco every August, with processions, concerts, and fireworks.
  • Cycling is a popular sport in Forlì and the city regularly hosts national and international bike races.
  • The city has a vibrant nightlife, with several bars, nightclubs, and live music venues.

Best Time to Visit Forlì

Forlì can be visited year-round, but the best time to visit is from May to October when the weather is pleasant and the city is alive with festivities.

Do’s and Don’ts of Forlì

Do:

  • Learn a few Italian phrases to better communicate with locals.
  • Always carry a copy of your passport and other important documents.

Don’t:

  • Don’t buy fake designer goods from street vendors as they are illegal.
  • Don’t forget to tip at restaurants and cafes.

Safety and Health

Safety in Forlì

Forlì is a safe city, but visitors should take the usual precautions and be aware of pickpockets in crowded places.

Health in Forlì

Forlì has a good healthcare system, with many hospitals and clinics. Visitors should take out travel insurance before traveling to Forlì.

Getting to Forlì: Transportation Options and Logistics

Forlì is easily accessible by air, train, or car. Here are some of the transportation options:

Airports Near Forlì

  • Bologna Guglielmo Marconi Airport (BLQ) - This is the nearest major airport, located 70 km west of Forlì.

Bus Stations Near Forlì

  • Forlì bus station - Serviced by many intercity bus operators, including Flixbus and Eurolines.

Other Options

  • Train - The city is well-connected to the Italian rail network, with high-speed trains stopping at the Forlì train station.

How to Get Around in Forlì

The best way to get around Forlì is by bus, bike or car. The city has an extensive public transportation system that connects the various neighborhoods and attractions.

Where to Go Next from Forlì: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Forlì is located in a region rich in history and culture, and several nearby destinations and excursions are worth exploring. Here are some of the best:

  • Ravenna - A city known for its Byzantine mosaics and UNESCO World Heritage sites.
  • Bologna - A city famous for its architecture, cuisine, and vibrant culture.
  • Rimini - A seaside town with beautiful beaches and a rich cultural heritage.

Conclusion

Forlì is an exciting city with something for everyone. Whether you’re interested in history, art, culture, outdoor adventure, or cuisine, Forlì has it all. Plan your visit today and experience the beauty and charm of this amazing city.
