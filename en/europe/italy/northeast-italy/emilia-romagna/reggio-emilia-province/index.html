Introduction to Reggio Emilia (Province)

Reggio Emilia is a province located in Emilia-Romagna, in Northeast Italy. The province is known for its charming small towns, and a rich cultural heritage that dates back to the Roman Empire. The region is home to remarkable museums, architectural wonders, and natural wonders.

In this comprehensive travel guide of Reggio Emilia (Province), we will introduce you to the must-visit attractions, delicious cuisines, and hidden gems of the region. Read on to find out more!

List of Places to Visit in Reggio Emilia (Province)

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Reggio Emilia (Province) boasts a rich cultural heritage. Here are the must-visit cultural attractions that offer a glimpse into the history and artistry of the region.

  • The Municipal Museum of Reggio Emilia
  • The Archaeological Museum
  • The Loris Malaguzzi International Centre
  • The Basilica della Ghiara
  • The Ducal Palace of Sassuolo
  • The Castle of Scipione

Natural Attractions of Reggio Emilia (Province): Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Reggio Emilia is synonymous with the beauty of the Apennine Mountains and the lush plains of the Po River Valley. Here are the must-visit natural attractions in Reggio Emilia (Province):

  • The Emilia-Romagna Apennines National Park
  • The Regional Park of the Stirone River
  • The Parco dei Sassi di Roccamalatina
  • The Pietra di Bismantova
  • The Cerreto Laghi Ski Resort
  • The Corno alle Scale Regional Park

Best Time to Visit Reggio Emilia (Province)

The best time to visit Reggio Emilia (Province) is between May and June or September and October. With mild temperatures ranging between 50-70°F, you can comfortably explore the region, participate in outdoor adventures, and avoid the crowds of tourists.

Do’s and Don’ts of Reggio Emilia (Province)

Do:

  • Always carry some cash because cash is still the modus operandi for many small businesses, local markets, and restaurants.
  • When in a restaurant, it is customary to allow the host to order for the whole table to share.
  • Learn some basic Italian phrases

Don’t:

  • Avoid going to touristy restaurants. Mostly they aren’t authentic with food quality and prices inflated for visitors.
  • Don’t miss drinking the famous Emilia-Romagna wines.

Safety and Health

Safety in Reggio Emilia (Province)

Reggio Emilia is a safe destination. Serious crimes are rare, and small petty incidents occur mostly in crowded tourist areas. Always take appropriate safety measures.

Health in Reggio Emilia (Province)

Reggio Emilia offers high-quality health care services. If you’re an EU citizen, you can get free medical assistance, also make sure to have comprehensive travel medical insurance to be safe.

Getting to Reggio Emilia (Province): Transportation Options and Logistics

  • Milan Linate Airport: 1-hour 30-minute drive to Reggio Emilia.
  • Florence Peretola Airport: 1-hour 30-minute drive to Reggio Emilia.

Bus Stations Near Reggio Emilia (Province)

Reggio Emilia Bus Terminal links Reggio Emilia to other Italian cities, including Bologna, Modena, Parma, and Milan.

Other Options

Reggio Emilia is well-connected by rail. Travelers can take a train from Bologna and Parma, connecting the region to other cities in Italy.

How to Get Around in Reggio Emilia (Province)

The public transport system of Reggio Emilia is quite efficient, with buses serving the surrounding towns and central cities. You can also hire private taxis, bicycles or use car renting services to explore the region.
