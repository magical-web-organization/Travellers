Introduction to Craco

Perched on a hilltop in the Basilicata region of Southern Italy, Craco is a village known for its picturesque views and historical significance. This ancient hamlet is a quintessential Italian town filled with narrow streets, charming piazzas, and stunning views of the countryside. Craco is a fascinating place to visit, steeped in culture and surrounded by natural beauty. There is much to explore in this beautiful town, from the ancient ruins to the modern-day attractions.

List of Places to Visit in Craco

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Craco is a town steeped in history and rich cultural heritage, and there are several places worth visiting. One of the most prominent is the ancient Craco Castle, which was built in the 11th century and has a stunning panoramic view of the entire valley. The castle is now a museum, and visitors can discover the fascinating history of the town through its art and artifacts.

Another must-visit is the Church of San Nicola, dedicated to the patron saint of the town, which has been beautifully restored. The church is a stunning example of Baroque architecture, and its interior is adorned with frescoes and paintings.

Natural Attractions of Craco: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Craco is surrounded by natural beauty, with rolling hills, lush green countryside and scenic views. It is the perfect destination for those who love the great outdoors and hiking. The Pollino National Park is one such attraction that provides ample opportunity for hiking and exploring the natural beauty of the area. The park has a rich variety of flora and fauna and features beautiful waterfalls, caves, and rivers.

For adventure lovers, there is the opportunity to take a guided tour and go caving in the numerous underground caves of the area. The area is also popular for cycling tours and mountain biking.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Craco

Hiking and biking enthusiasts can explore the stunning beauty of Craco on the many nearby trails, bike paths, and hiking routes. The most popular hiking routes include the Monticchio Lakes, the Massiccio del Sirino trails, and the Capreria ravine.

If cycling is your thing, then you can take a guided tour that will take you through the countryside. Cycling tours are a great way to explore the area’s hidden gems while also getting a workout.

Best Things to Do in Craco: Activities and Tours

Besides exploring the beautiful attractions of the area and enjoying the countryside, there are many fun things to do in Craco. There are guided tours of the area that offer visitors the chance to learn about the history and culture of the area. Another popular activity is olive oil tasting - a great way to experience the local cuisine and learn about the importance of olive oil in Italian culture.

If you’re looking for something more adventurous, then rock climbing is an activity that may interest you. There are several climbing areas around Craco, from beginner to advanced levels.

Week-Long Itinerary for Craco

Craco offers visitors an authentic Italian experience with its rich cultural heritage, stunning natural beauty, and quaint town center. Here is a suggested itinerary for spending a week in the area:

  • Day 1: Arrive in Craco and explore the town center
  • Day 2: Visit the Castle and Museum
  • Day 3: Hike or bike the Monticchio Lakes
  • Day 4: Take a guided tour of the countryside
  • Day 5: Explore the Natural Science Museum
  • Day 6: Visit the Church of San Nicola
  • Day 7: Explore the Pollino National Park

Food and Drink Scene of Craco

Craco has a wide range of restaurants and bars where visitors can enjoy the local cuisine and drink.

Must-Try Dishes

One of the most famous regional dishes in the area is the lucanica, a type of sausage made with pork. Another popular dish is roast lamb, which is marinated with herbs and roasted in a wood-fired oven. The area is also known for its delicious pasta dishes.

Local Beverages

The region is well-known for its impressive wines, such as Aglianico del Vulture and Moscato di Trani. Visitors can also enjoy locally-produced liquor such as Amaro Lucano, a herbal Italian liquor.

Where to Stay in Craco: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Craco has a range of accommodation options, including hotels, hostels, and guesthouses. The hotels in the area offer modern amenities and facilities, while the guesthouses and hostels offer a more authentic Italian experience. Some popular accommodation options include:

  • Masseria La Chiusa
  • Hotel Giubileo
  • La Corte dei Principi

What to Buy in Craco: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Craco offers many local products that make excellent gifts for friends and family back home. Visitors can buy olive oil, wine, and liquor made in the region. The area is also famous for its pottery, including the traditional plates and cups made from local clay.

Local Culture and Customs of Craco

Italian culture is deeply ingrained in the people of Craco. Visitors can experience it first-hand through the area’s music and dance, cuisine, festivals and fairs.

Best Time to Visit Craco

The best time to visit Craco is from May to September when the weather is suitable for outdoor activities and excursions.

Do’s and Don’ts of Craco

Do:

  • Respect the local culture and customs
  • Carry cash as some shops and restaurants may not accept cards
  • Dress appropriately when visiting churches and museums

Don’t:

  • Leave the designated hiking trails
  • Take pictures of people without asking their permission
  • Drink tap water

Safety and Health

Safety in Craco

Craco is a relatively safe town with low crime rates. Visitors should be vigilant, avoid carrying large amounts of cash and keep valuables in a safe place.

Health in Craco

Craco has a modern-day healthcare system. Visitors can visit one of the many pharmacies in the area and seek medical assistance if required.

Getting to Craco: Transportation Options and Logistics

The easiest way to get to Craco is by flying into one of the nearby airports such as Bari or Naples. from Naples, visitors can take a train to the Salerno station and then catch a bus to Craco. Visitors can also reach Craco by car or bus from Rome or Naples.

Airports Near Craco

  • Bari Karol Wojtyła Airport
  • Naples International Airport

Bus Stations Near Craco

  • Craco PZ Bus Station

Other Options

Visitors can take a train from Rome to Salerno and then a bus to Craco.

How to Get Around in Craco

Walking is the most popular way to get around in Craco as it is a small town. However, visitors can also rent a car or take a guided tour to explore the area.

Where to Go Next from Craco: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Craco is ideally situated to explore some of Italy’s most beautiful regions. Visitors can take a day trip to the nearby towns of Matera, known for its ancient cave dwellings, or Maratea, famous for its stunning beaches. Other nearby destinations include the towns of Castelmezzano, Potenza, and Avigliano.

Conclusion

Craco is a stunning destination in Southern Italy that offers visitors a unique blend of history, culture, and natural beauty. From exploring the ancient castle to hiking the scenic countryside, there are many things to do and see in Craco. Sample the local cuisine, soak up the culture, and experience the beauty of this charming town on your next trip to Italy.
