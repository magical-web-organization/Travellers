Introduction to Barletta

Located in the Apulia region of southern Italy, Barletta is a picturesque coastal town that boasts a rich history, stunning natural scenery, and delicious cuisine. The city holds an important place in Italian history and has a unique culture that is both diverse and deeply rooted in tradition.

List of Places to Visit in Barletta

There are numerous places to explore in Barletta, ranging from historical landmarks to natural wonders. Here are some of the must-visit spots:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Castello Svevo di Barletta: Built in the 13th century, this impressive fortress is now a museum that offers a glimpse into Barletta’s rich history.
  • Pinacoteca De Nittis: This museum houses a collection of Impressionist art and features the works of local artist, Francesco Paolo De Nittis.
  • Andria Cathedral: A stunning example of Romanesque architecture, this cathedral is one of the most important religious sites in the region.

Natural Attractions of Barletta: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Pulo di Altamura: This natural limestone sinkhole is a popular spot for hiking and exploring unique geological formations.
  • Saline della Margherita: This coastal nature reserve is home to a variety of bird species and offers breathtaking views of the sea.
  • Ponte di San Cataldo: This historic bridge offers stunning views of the river and surrounding countryside.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Barletta

Barletta offers a variety of hiking and biking routes that allow visitors to explore the region’s natural beauty and stunning landscapes. The Alta Murgia National Park is a popular destination for outdoor enthusiasts and offers a range of hiking and biking trails.

Best Things to Do in Barletta: Activities and Tours

There are many exciting activities and tours available in Barletta that allow visitors to fully experience the rich cultural and historical heritage of the region. Some of the best things to do in Barletta include:

  • Join a walking tour of the city’s historic center
  • Take a boat tour of the coastline
  • Visit a local winery and sample the region’s famous wines

Week-Long Itinerary for Barletta

If you’re planning a week-long trip to Barletta, here’s a suggested itinerary:

Day Activity
1 Explore the historic center of the city and visit the Castello Svevo di Barletta
2 Take a walking tour of the city’s medieval churches and cathedrals
3 Visit the nearby nature reserve and take a boat tour of the coastline
4 Take a day trip to the nearby town of Trani
5 Join a wine-tasting tour and explore the region’s vineyards
6 Visit the Alta Murgia National Park and explore the hiking trails
7 Spend the day relaxing on the beach and enjoying the local cuisine

Food and Drink Scene of Barletta

Barletta is known for its delicious cuisine, which is heavily influenced by the region’s rich culinary heritage. From fresh seafood to local wines, there’s plenty to enjoy in Barletta.

Must-Try Dishes

  • Stracciatella: A creamy cheese that is a popular ingredient in many traditional dishes
  • Orecchiette con Cime di Rapa: A classic dish made with pasta, broccoli rabe, and garlic
  • Focaccia Barese: A delicious flatbread that is often topped with tomatoes, olives, and herbs

Local Beverages

  • Primitivo di Manduria: A full-bodied red wine that is produced in the nearby province of Taranto
  • Limoncello: A traditional Italian liqueur that is made from lemons and sugar

Where to Stay in Barletta: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

There are plenty of accommodation options throughout Barletta, ranging from budget-friendly hostels to luxurious hotels. Some of the best options include:

  • Best Western Hotel Dei Cavalieri: A modern hotel located in the heart of the city
  • La Disfida di Barletta: A charming guesthouse with a historic feel
  • Youthhostel Barletta: A budget-friendly hostel that offers comfortable accommodations

What to Buy in Barletta: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Barletta is known for its unique traditional crafts, including handmade lace and pottery. Visitors can also bring home local olive oil and wine, as well as other locally-made food products.

Local Culture and Customs of Barletta

Barletta has a unique culture that is both diverse and deeply rooted in tradition. The city is known for its vibrant festivals, including the famous Challenge of Barletta, which celebrates a historic battle that took place in the city in the 16th century.

Best Time to Visit Barletta

The best time to visit Barletta is during the spring or fall, when temperatures are mild and the crowds are smaller. The summer months can be hot and crowded, while the winter months can be damp and chilly.

Do’s and Don’ts of Barletta

Do:

  • Try the local cuisine and wine
  • Explore the city’s historic center and landmarks
  • Attend local festivals and events

Don’t:

  • Ignore local customs and traditions
  • Take photographs of people without their permission
  • Leave valuables unattended

Safety and Health

Safety in Barletta

Barletta is a relatively safe city, but visitors should still take precautions to avoid petty crime and theft. It’s also important to be aware of the risks associated with COVID-19 and to follow local guidelines and restrictions.

Health in Barletta

Barletta has a good healthcare system, but visitors should still take steps to stay healthy while traveling. This includes getting up-to-date on vaccinations and taking precautionary measures to avoid food and waterborne illnesses.

Getting to Barletta: Transportation Options and Logistics

Barletta is easily accessible by car, train, or bus. The closest airport is the Bari-Palese International Airport, which is located approximately 45 minutes away by car.

Airports Near Barletta

  • Bari-Palese International Airport
  • Brindisi-Salento Airport

Bus Stations Near Barletta

  • Barletta Bus Terminal

Other Options

  • Car rental services are available throughout Barletta
  • Taxis and private transfer services are also available

How to Get Around in Barletta

Barletta can easily be explored on foot, as the historic center is relatively compact. However, visitors can also use public transportation or rent a bike to explore the surrounding region.

Where to Go Next from Barletta: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Barletta is located in a beautiful region of southern Italy and there are numerous destinations and excursions available for visitors. Some of the best options include:

  • Trani: A nearby coastal town famous for its beautiful cathedral and seaside promenade
  • Matera: A historic city located just over an hour’s drive from Barletta, famed for its ancient cave dwellings and stunning architecture.
  • Castel del Monte: A UNESCO World Heritage Site located about an hour’s drive from Barletta, which is famous for its stunning 13th-century castle.

Conclusion

Barletta is truly a hidden gem in southern Italy, offering visitors a unique combination of history, natural beauty, delicious cuisine, and a vibrant local culture. Whether you’re looking for relaxation or adventure, Barletta has something for everyone.
