Bisceglie, located in the Barletta-Andria-Trani province in Apulia, Southern Italy, is a charming coastal town with an abundance of natural beauty, rich history, and vibrant culture. This travel guide will take you through an in-depth tour of Bisceglie and its surroundings, featuring must-see attractions, hiking/biking routes, food and drink scene, souvenirs, local culture and customs, safety and logistics, and much more.

Introduction to Bisceglie

Named after Bishop Elio, Bisceglie was once a wealthy port town that grew prosperous during the Roman Empire. Through the ages, it became a crucial bastion against raiders, sustaining it’s economy. Bisceglie has maintained its medieval charm thanks to its fortress walls, ancient castle ruins, and old town alleys.

List of Places to Visit in Bisceglie

Bisceglie has plenty of attractions that offer something for everyone. Here are some of the top places to visit:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Pinacoteca di Bisceglie: This museum is famous for its engravings, paintings, and other art pieces from the 16th to 20th centuries.
  • Chiesa di San Giovanni Battista: Completed in the 13th century, the church is home to numerous unique sculptures, and it serves as a beautiful example of medieval art.
  • Torre Normanna: This 12th-century tower, near the harbor, is believed to have played a significant defense role against pirates’ invasions. It has a peaceful view of the town/sunset.

Natural Attractions of Bisceglie: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Porto Mercantile: A beautiful waterfront with colorful boats and plenty of restaurants and bards serving fresh seafood dishes.
  • Spiaggia del Porto: A lovely beach with serene waters fit for sunbathing and swimming.
  • Parco Garibaldi: The largest park in Bisceglie offers plenty of green space to enjoy the outdoors and with benches and tress provide shade for you to enjoy.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Bisceglie

Suppose you prefer a more active experience in nature. In that case, Bisceglie’s hills and forests offer a range of hiking and biking trails, from gentle strolls through the olive groves to steep climbs up rocky cliffs. Some must-try routes include:

  • Sentiero Matteotti: A walking trail that spans gradually through olive and orange groves, eventually leading to a picturesque lookout point over the town.
  • Monte Cagnano: A mountain biking trail that takes you through the surrounding hills, and ancient villages like “Trani”, providing breathtaking landscapes.

Best Things to Do in Bisceglie: Activities and Tours

Enjoy Bisceglie through its bright culture and participate in these enjoyable activities and try these tours:

  • Alberobello: A UNESCO site of the trulli traditional houses that make Apulia internationally famous. It is a staple attraction surrounded by many locally sourced art stalls.
  • Argdine: Trip around the nearby islands, stopping to swim and dive on the crystal clear water surrounding them.

Week-Long Itinerary for Bisceglie

Below is a week-long itinerary of things to do in Bisceglie:

  • Day 1: Visit the medieval town center filled with old-world charm.
  • Day 2: Head to Parco Garibaldi to enjoy a more rural landscape and eat some sandwiches.
  • Day 3: Visit the local museum to witness Bisceglie’s culture and tradition.
  • Day 4: Head to the “Porto Mercantile” to witness natural marine art and indulge in seafood that is fresh off the boat.
  • Day 5: Experience the vibes of the neighboring town Trani, it’s ancient narrow streets, and internationally trendy bars/restaurants.
  • Day 6: Take the Monte Cagnano trail, it’s a famous hiking trail through the cumulus clouds and olive groves.
  • Day 7: Explore the nearby islands’ beauty, surrounded by crystal-clear water.

Food and Drink Scene of Bisceglie

Apulia is famous for its fresh produce and ingredients, especially seafood dishes. In Bisceglie, the food and drink scene reflect a rich flavorful combination of southern Italian cuisine.

Must-Try Dishes

  • Orecchiette: A type of pasta, typically served with turnip greens and sometimes with meat.
  • Burrata: A fresh cheese, made from mozzarella and cream, rich and flavorful mouth melting ‘soft-centered’ cheese.
  • Taralli: Small, circular, crunchy biscuits that are a popular snack in the area.

Local Beverages

  • Primitivo di Manduria: Red wine that’s made with locally grown grapes and has a rich, full-bodied flavor.
  • Limoncello: A lemon liqueur that is typically served cold and is an excellent after-dinner beverage.

Where to Stay in Bisceglie: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Bisceglie has plenty of accommodations available, whether you’re traveling on a budget or looking for a more luxurious stay. Some recommended places to stay include:

  • Hotel Salsello: Next to the beach, an excellent option for an elegant and relaxing stay.
  • B&B Ciasca Porta Piccola: A charming guesthouse in the heart of the old town for a cozy and authentic experience.
  • B&B Palazzo Ducale: Feel like royalty in the historical palace, renovated to serve as a hotel with excellent service.

What to Buy in Bisceglie: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Bisceglie has a unique range of locally produced goods and souvenirs available, including:

  • Ceramics: Famous locally crafted ceramics beautifully reflecting area landscapes.
  • Olive oil: Often called “liquid gold,” the Apulian olive oil is a unique flavor and reasonably priced. Be sure to taste and check out local stores like “Lorenzo Nostra Terra”.
  • Taralli: As mentioned earlier, a small crunchy snack that is perfect for souvenirs and personal consumption.

Local Culture and Customs of Bisceglie

As Bisceglie has a rich heritage, with its unique customs and traditions stemming largely from the area’s cultural richness, it gives the town a vibrant social atmosphere.

Best Time to Visit Bisceglie

The best time to visit Bisceglie is during spring or autumn, from March to May or September to October, as temperatures are pleasant, and the summer crowds have not arrived. Winters are mild and humid, but you’ll experience fewer crowds but bring in layers as cold snaps can occur.

Do’s and Don’ts of Bisceglie

Below are some essential do’s and don’ts to keep in mind when visiting Bisceglie:

Do:

  • Respect the local customs and traditions.
  • Try the local cuisine, explore on foot, and take in the town’s beautiful ambiance.

Don’t:

  • Litter on the streets or the historic monuments.
  • Wear beachwear at religious sites/historic places.

Safety and Health

Safety in Bisceglie

Bisceglie is a relatively safe town, but travelers should be aware of pickpockets on busy streets and practice caution in isolated areas. Standard precautions like keeping valuable possessions close are always useful.

Health in Bisceglie

Suppose you require medical help, Bisceglie has excellent facilities with well-trained medical professionals but keep in mind most spoke are local dialects. For extreme emergencies, find the medical center nearest you on Via Osanna Sarego 142.

Getting to Bisceglie: Transportation Options and Logistics

Transportation is available to travel to Bisceglie by car, train/bus and boat.

Airports Near Bisceglie

Bisceglie has two international airports within a reasonable distance, open year-round, with flights available throughout Europe.

  • Bari International Airport (53km)
  • Brindisi International Airport (126km)

Bus Stations Near Bisceglie

Travelers who prefer bus transportation can find regular lines via “Finnibus” services from the Bari central station to Bisceglie that takes approximately an hour to travel to the town.

Other Options

For travelers who prefer a ferry, the Port of Bisceglie is busy year-round, the nearby ports of call are Brindisi and Bari with regular daily connections.

How to Get Around in Bisceglie

Exploring Bisceglie both on foot and by rented bicycle is the best way to experience the town and surrounding areas. There are car rental services also available, but be aware of the limited parking space and medieval town center prefering pedestrians only.

Where to Go Next from Bisceglie: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Here are some nearby destinations that you may consider continuing your trip following Bisceglie:

  • Bari: The capital of the region, known for its architecture and notable Romanic churches.
  • Castel del Monte: A 13th-century, UNESCO World Heritage Site castle in Andria
  • Trani: A beautiful seaside town near Bisceglie, known for its stunning cathedral and charming old town.

Conclusion

Bisceglie is a spectacular town with something for everyone and a fantastic retreat from modern life. Its spectacular food, countless beaches, hiking trails, and history make Bisceglie an unforgettable stay. So, whether you’re looking for natural beauty, cultural richness, or a relaxing beachside vacation, Bisceglie is the place to be.
