Introduction to Canosa di Puglia

Nestled in the heart of Apulia, Canosa di Puglia is a hidden gem that is often overlooked by tourists. However, for those willing to explore, this charming town offers a wealth of cultural and natural attractions that are sure to leave you mesmerized.

Founded by the Greeks around the 5th century BC, Canosa di Puglia boasts a rich and diverse cultural heritage that is evident in its art, architecture, and cuisine. From ancient ruins and impressive cathedrals to scenic countryside and stunning national parks, Canosa di Puglia has it all.

In this comprehensive travel guide, we will take a closer look at the top things to see and do in Canosa di Puglia, along with useful tips and recommendations to make the most of your trip.

List of Places to Visit in Canosa di Puglia

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Archaeological Museum – Housed in a beautiful 16th-century palace, the Archaeological Museum is home to a vast collection of artifacts from the prehistoric, Greek, Roman, and Byzantine periods.
  • Cathedral of San Sabino – Built in the 12th century, the Cathedral of San Sabino is the largest and most impressive church in Canosa di Puglia, with a stunning Gothic façade and impressive frescoes.
  • Hypogeum of Torre di Satriano – Dating back to the 4th century BC, this complex of underground tombs and tunnels is a fascinating insight into the burial practices of the ancient Greeks.

Natural Attractions of Canosa di Puglia: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Gargano National Park – This sprawling national park is home to some of the most stunning natural landscapes in Italy, with rugged coastlines, dense forests, and crystal-clear lakes.
  • Gravina di Puglia – This deep ravine winds its way through the heart of Canosa di Puglia, offering incredible views of the surrounding landscape.
  • Alta Murgia National Park – This vast wilderness area is a haven for hikers and outdoor enthusiasts, with countless trails and scenic vistas to explore.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Canosa di Puglia

  • Canosa di Puglia to Gravina di Puglia – This scenic hike takes you along the winding banks of the Gravina di Puglia, with stunning views of the surrounding countryside.
  • Monte Vulture Loop – This challenging bike ride takes you through some of the most rugged and scenic parts of Canosa di Puglia, with breathtaking views of the Monte Vulture volcano.

Best Things to Do in Canosa di Puglia: Activities and Tours

  • Wine Tasting Tour – Canosa di Puglia is known for its excellent wines, and there are plenty of vineyards and wineries in the area to explore.
  • Cooking Class – Learn the secrets of traditional Apulian cuisine with a hands-on cooking class led by a local chef.
  • Cycling Tour – Explore the stunning countryside of Canosa di Puglia on a guided cycling tour.

Food and Drink Scene of Canosa di Puglia

Must-Try Dishes

  • Orecchiette alle cime di rapa – This classic dish features ear-shaped pasta served with broccoli rabe and garlic.
  • Burrata – This creamy cheese is a specialty of Apulia, and is typically served with fresh tomatoes and herbs.
  • Ricotta forte – This strong, savory cheese is made from sheep’s milk and is often served with crusty bread.

Local Beverages

  • Negramaro – This full-bodied red wine is a local favorite and pairs well with meat dishes.
  • Primitivo – This robust, fruity wine is perfect with pasta and spicy dishes.
  • Espresso – No trip to Italy is complete without a strong espresso, and Canosa di Puglia has some of the best.

Where to Stay in Canosa di Puglia: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Palazzo San Francesco – This elegant hotel is housed in a beautifully restored 16th-century building and offers stunning views of the surrounding countryside.
  • B&B La Cattedrale d’Oro – Located in the heart of Canosa di Puglia, this charming guesthouse is just steps away from the Cathedral of San Sabino.
  • Hostel Dalianova – This affordable hostel offers clean and comfortable accommodations in the heart of Canosa di Puglia.

What to Buy in Canosa di Puglia: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Olive oil – Apulia is known for its high-quality olive oil, and Canosa di Puglia has plenty of artisanal producers to choose from.
  • Ceramics – The region is famed for its intricate, hand-painted ceramics, which make great souvenirs.
  • Burrata Cheese – Take home a taste of Apulia with a package of fresh burrata cheese.

Local Culture and Customs of Canosa di Puglia

Canosa di Puglia is proud of its rich cultural heritage, and visitors can expect to see traditional festivals, music, and dance throughout the year.

Best Time to Visit Canosa di Puglia

The best time to visit Canosa di Puglia is from May to September, when the weather is warm and sunny. However, this is also peak tourist season, so be prepared for crowds and higher prices.

Do’s and Don’ts of Canosa di Puglia

Do:

  • Try the local cuisine – Apulia is known for its delicious food, so be sure to sample some of the local specialties.
  • Explore the countryside – Canosa di Puglia is surrounded by gorgeous landscapes, so take some time to explore the area on foot or by bike.
  • Learn about the local history – The town is steeped in history, so take some time to visit the museums and landmarks to learn more.

Don’t:

  • Forget to pack sunscreen – The sun can be strong in southern Italy, so be sure to protect your skin.
  • Skip the olive oil tasting – Olive oil is a local specialty, so be sure to take some time to taste the different varieties.
  • Miss out on the outdoor adventures – Canosa di Puglia is a great place for hiking and biking, so be sure to take advantage of the outdoor activities on offer.

Safety and Health

Safety in Canosa di Puglia

Canosa di Puglia is a safe town, but visitors should be aware of pickpocketing and other petty crimes in crowded areas. Be sure to keep your valuables safe and be aware of your surroundings.

Health in Canosa di Puglia

Italy has a modern and efficient healthcare system, and visitors can expect high-quality medical care in the event of an emergency. However, visitors should take care to drink plenty of water and use sunscreen to avoid dehydration and sunburn.

Getting to Canosa di Puglia: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Canosa di Puglia

The nearest airport to Canosa di Puglia is Bari International Airport, which is located about 60 km away. From the airport, visitors can take a train or a bus to reach Canosa di Puglia.

Bus Stations Near Canosa di Puglia

Canosa di Puglia has a central bus station, with regular services to nearby towns and cities.

Other Options

Visitors can also reach Canosa di Puglia by train or car, with easy access to the town from the nearby A14 highway.

How to Get Around in Canosa di Puglia

The best way to explore Canosa di Puglia and the surrounding countryside is by car or bike. Visitors can also take local buses or hire a taxi for shorter trips.

Where to Go Next from Canosa di Puglia: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Trani – This charming coastal town is just a short drive from Canosa di Puglia and offers a beautiful harbor, stunning cathedral, and great seafood restaurants.
  • Bari – The capital of Apulia is just an hour’s drive from Canosa di Puglia and offers a wealth of cultural attractions.
  • Matera – This UNESCO World Heritage site is known for its ancient cave dwellings and stunning architecture, and is just a short drive from Canosa di Puglia.

Conclusion
