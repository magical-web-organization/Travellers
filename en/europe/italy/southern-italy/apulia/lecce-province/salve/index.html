Introduction to Salve

Located in the province of Lecce, in Apulia, Southern Italy, Salve is a hidden gem waiting to be explored. This charming town is situated on the Salento Peninsula of Italy, near the Ionian Sea. Salve is a quiet Italian village where you can relax and enjoy the scenic beauty of the countryside while immersing yourself in the rich history and culture of Southern Italy.

List of Places to Visit in Salve

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Museo Civico di Maglie: A museum dedicated to the art and history of Salve and the wider Salento area, featuring artifacts and works from the ancient Greeks and Romans to the present day.
  • Chiesa di San Nicola di Myra: A historic church with stunning Baroque architecture, which houses impressive art pieces and frescoes.
  • Palazzo Saraceno: A magnificent building which has been restored to its former glory, and now houses exhibitions and events.

Natural Attractions of Salve: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Spiaggia Marina di Pescoluse: One of the most beautiful beaches in Salve, this sandy beach stretches for several kilometers and is famous for its crystal clear water.
  • Castello di Castiglione: A castle on a hilltop with a beautiful view of the Salento countryside, perfect for capturing Instagram-worthy photos.
  • Leuca Piccola: A natural pool located near the coastline of Salve, known for its inviting turquoise water and scenic views.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Salve

  • The Serra di Fiume Tricase Hiking Trail: A beautiful hike that covers 9 km in total and offers panoramic views across the southern region of Italy and the Ionian Sea.
  • The Coastal Route: Stretching for nearly 45 km, this bike route is the perfect way to discover the beauty of the Salento Peninsula, with stunning sea views and picturesque towns along the way.

Best Things to Do in Salve: Activities and Tours

  • Olive Oil Tasting: Discover the art of making some of the most exquisite olive oils in Italy by visiting some of the local farms in Salve.
  • Wine Tasting: Salve is known for producing some exquisite wines. Tour local vineyards and taste wines made from native grape varieties.
  • Cooking Classes: Learn how to make traditional Puglian dishes using fresh ingredients sourced from Salve’s farms.

Week-Long Itinerary for Salve

Day 1: Arrival in Salve and visit to historic landmarks including Chiesa di San Nicola di Myra and Palazzo Saraceno.

Day 2: Hiking the Serra di Fiume Tricase Hiking Trail.

Day 3: Relax on the Spiaggia Marina di Pescoluse beach and discover the nearby Leuca Piccola natural pool.

Day 4: Take a scenic bike ride along the coastal route to discover neighboring towns.

Day 5: Visit the ancient Cave of Zinzulusa and the Castello di Castro.

Day 6: Olive oil tasting at local farms and a cooking class to learn traditional Puglian dishes.

Day 7: Departure from Salve.

Food and Drink Scene of Salve

Must-Try Dishes

  • Orecchiette con le cime di rapa: A pasta dish made with broccoli and salted anchovies, a traditional dish in the Salento region.
  • Braciole al sugo: Thin slices of beef rolled with garlic, parsley, grated cheese, and breadcrumbs, served with tomato sauce.
  • Pasticciotto: A pastry filled with custard cream, typical of the pasticcerie of Salento.

Local Beverages

  • Primitivo di Manduria: A grape variety that is native to Apulia, the red wine is very popular in Salve.
  • Negroamaro: Another grape variety that is typical of Salento. It produces a dry red wine.

Where to Stay in Salve: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Masseria Amastuola: A luxury agriturismo that specializes in wine production, offering a unique experience to guests.
  • Agriturismo Masseria Cerase: Another agriturismo that offers guests a unique experience with its farm-to-table food and beautiful views of the countryside.
  • Le Antiche Mura: A charming guesthouse near the city center of Salve, where you can experience the local culture and hospitality of Southern Italy.

What to Buy in Salve: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Ceramics: Lecce and the greater Salento region are famous for their beautiful ceramics. You will find many shops in Salve selling traditional pottery.
  • Olive oil: Puglia is Italy’s largest producer of olive oil, and Salve produces some of the finest quality olive oil.
  • Wine: The wines from the Salento Peninsula, including Salve, are among Italy’s best. Wine lovers should visit the local vineyards and purchase some bottles as souvenirs.

Local Culture and Customs of Salve

Salve has a rich history and culture that is closely tied to the traditions of Southern Italy. The locals are friendly people who take pride in their customs, and they are always happy to share them with visitors. Some of the traditional customs in Salve include:

  • Celebrating religious festivals, such as La Festa in Piazza and Sant’Oronzo.
  • Traditional dances, like the Tarantella, which is a popular folk dance performed during weddings and other celebrations.
  • Enjoying local cuisine and wines with family and friends.

Best Time to Visit Salve

The best time to visit Salve is between April and June or September and November. These months offer the perfect climate to enjoy outdoor activities, food, and wine while avoiding the heat and crowds of peak tourist season.

Do’s and Don’ts of Salve

Do:

  • Try the local cuisine and wines.
  • Respect local customs and traditions.
  • Explore the beautiful countryside and take part in outdoor activities.

Don’t:

  • Expect to find a bustling city with a hectic nightlife.
  • Disrespect local customs and traditions.
  • Litter in public places.

Safety and Health

Safety in Salve

Salve is a safe place for both locals and tourists. However, it is always important to take basic safety precautions, like keeping your valuables out of sight, not walking alone at night, and being aware of your surroundings.

Health in Salve

The water quality in Salve is safe for drinking, and hospitals are available in nearby towns.

Getting to Salve: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Salve

  • Brindisi Airport (BDS): The closest airport to Salve, located about 95 km away.
  • Bari Airport (BRI): Located further north, but still accessible via a bus or rental car.

Bus Stations Near Salve

  • Lecce Bus Station: Buses from Salve to Lecce run frequently, and Lecce is a well-connected town with links to many other Italian cities.

Other Options

  • Rental Car: Renting a car is a great way to explore the Salento Peninsula and Salve’s surrounding area.
  • Taxi or Private Transfer: Taxis or private transfers are available from nearby airports or train stations.

How to Get Around in Salve

Getting around in Salve is relatively easy, as the town is small enough to explore on foot. However, to discover some of the nearby attractions, renting a bike or car is recommended.

Where to Go Next from Salve: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Gallipoli: Located only 35 km from Salve, this seaside town is known for its beautiful beaches, historic architecture, and vibrant nightlife.
  • Lecce: This beautiful Baroque city is located about 50 km away from Salve, perfect for a day trip to discover the vibrant history and cultural richness of Salento.
  • Ostuni: Also known as the “white city”, Ostuni is a stunning city famous for its white-washed houses and picturesque streets.

Conclusion

Salve is a quiet and charming Italian village with a rich history, beautiful countryside scenery, and vibrant local culture and customs. Whether you are a fan of the outdoors, food and wine, or history and architecture, Salve has something to offer every type of traveler. With this comprehensive tour guide, you will have all the information you need to plan your visit and immerse yourself in the beauty and wonder of this hidden gem in Southern Italy.
