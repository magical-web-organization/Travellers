Introduction to Specchia

Located in the beautiful province of Lecce in Apulia, Specchia is a charming town that embodies the essence of Southern Italy. With a rich history dating back to the 11th century, this quaint destination boasts plenty of cultural attractions, natural wonders, and outdoor adventures that cater to all kinds of travelers.

Whether you’re interested in exploring museums and historical places, hiking scenic routes, or indulging in delicious local cuisine, there’s no shortage of things to see and do in Specchia. In this comprehensive guide, we’ll take you through all the must-visit attractions and hidden gems of this incredible destination.

List of Places to Visit in Specchia

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • The Castle of Specchia: Originally built in the 11th century and restored in the 16th century by Prince Antonio Capece, this impressive castle features a stunning tower and a well-preserved ancient chapel. The castle houses the Museum of Rural Life, which showcases the traditional lifestyle of the local community.

  • Church of St. Euphemia: Dating back to the 15th century, this Baroque-style church is home to some of the finest artworks and sculptures in Southern Italy. Highlights include the stunning frescoes by Francesco Netti and the beautiful wooden altarpiece.

  • Palazzo Risolo: This magnificent palace dates back to the 18th century and features a stunning facade with intricate details and elegant columns. The palace now houses the Municipal Museum of Arts and Popular Traditions, which exhibits traditional crafts and artifacts from the region.

Natural Attractions of Specchia: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • The Specchia Pine Forest: Explore the beautiful pine forest that surrounds the town and enjoy a peaceful walk or a bike ride. The forest is home to a stunning variety of flora and fauna, and it offers some of the best views of the town and the surrounding countryside.

  • The Lama di Tricase Natural Park: This natural park is the perfect spot for nature lovers and outdoor enthusiasts. From hiking scenic trails to spotting unique wildlife, you’ll find plenty to do in this beautiful park.

  • The Coastal Area: Take a stroll along the picturesque coastline of Specchia and admire the crystal-clear waters and rocky cliffs. The beaches here are some of the best in Italy, and they offer plenty of opportunities for swimming, sunbathing, and water sports.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Specchia

  • The Specchia Circular Route: This circular hiking route takes you through the beautiful countryside surrounding the town. Along the way, you’ll pass through olive groves, vineyards, and charming villages, all while enjoying stunning views of the landscape.

  • The Coastal Hiking Trail: This scenic trail follows the coastline and takes you through some of the most beautiful spots in the area. From rugged cliffs to hidden coves, this trail offers plenty of photo opportunities and breathtaking views.

  • The Pine Forest Bike Trail: Perfect for cycling enthusiasts, this trail takes you through the beautiful pine forest that surrounds the town. The trail offers some stunning views of the forest and the surrounding countryside and is suitable for riders of all levels.

Best Things to Do in Specchia: Activities and Tours

  • Explore the Castle of Specchia and the Municipal Museum of Arts and Popular Traditions.

  • Visit the Church of St. Euphemia and admire the stunning artwork and architecture.

  • Hike or bike through the beautiful natural parks and coastal areas.

  • Relax on the pristine beaches and swim in the crystal-clear water.

  • Indulge in the delicious local cuisine and sample traditional dishes.

Week-Long Itinerary for Specchia

Food and Drink Scene of Specchia

Must-Try Dishes

  • Orecchiette alla Cima di Rapa: Small ear-shaped pasta with turnip greens and breadcrumbs.

  • Frisella: A traditional dish made with hard, twice-baked bread soaked in water and topped with veggies and olive oil.

  • Rustico Leccese: A savory pastry filled with tomato, mozzarella, and bechamel sauce.

Local Beverages

  • Primitivo di Manduria: A full-bodied, fruity red wine that pairs perfectly with local dishes.

  • Salice Salentino: Another popular red wine from the region that features notes of cherry and plum.

  • Negroamaro: A dry and tannic red wine made from a local grape variety.

Where to Stay in Specchia: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Palazzo Muro Leccese: A beautiful boutique hotel set in a restored 18th-century palace.

  • Masseria Borgo Mortella: A charming country hotel featuring beautiful grounds and a communal pool.

  • La Torre: A cozy guest house set in a historic building in the heart of the town.

What to Buy in Specchia: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Olive oil: The region is famous for its excellent olive oil, which you can find in many shops and markets.

  • Ceramics: From colorful plates to unique vases, the local ceramic shops offer a range of beautiful pottery.

  • Lacework: The town is known for its exquisite lacework, and you can find many lace shops selling unique and handmade pieces.

Local Culture and Customs of Specchia

The town of Specchia is known for its rich cultural heritage and traditional way of life. The locals are warm and welcoming, and they take pride in preserving their customs and traditions.

One of the most famous traditions is the “Pizzica” dance, which takes place during the summer months and is a celebration of music, dance, and local folklore. Another important custom is the food and wine culture, with the town being home to many excellent restaurants and wineries.

Best Time to Visit Specchia

The best time to visit Specchia is in the spring or autumn months, when the weather is mild and pleasant, and the crowds are fewer. The summer months can be hot and crowded, but they also offer plenty of opportunities for outdoor activities and beach days.

Do’s and Don’ts of Specchia

Do:

  • Try the local cuisine and sample traditional dishes.
  • Respect the local customs and traditions.
  • Explore the beautiful natural parks and coastal areas.

Don’t:

  • Overlook the hidden gems and off-the-beaten-path attractions.
  • Disrespect the locals or their way of life.
  • Leave without buying some local souvenirs to bring back home.

Safety and Health

Safety in Specchia

Specchia is a safe destination with low crime rates. However, it’s always wise to take precautions and keep your belongings safe, especially in crowded places.

Health in Specchia

The town has excellent medical facilities, and there are many doctors and pharmacies in the area. It’s always wise to have travel insurance that covers medical emergencies.

Getting to Specchia: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Specchia

The nearest airport is Brindisi, which is about 90 km away from the town. You can also fly to the Bari or Lamezia Terme airports, which are a bit further but offer more flight options.

Bus Stations Near Specchia

The town has a small bus station, and there are frequent buses that connect Specchia to the nearby cities and towns.

Other Options

You can also reach the town by train, as there are many trains that connect Lecce to other major Italian cities. If you’re driving, the town is easily accessible from the main highways.

How to Get Around in Specchia

The town is small and easy to navigate on foot. If you want to explore the surrounding areas, you can rent a bike or a car, or take a guided tour.

Where to Go Next from Specchia: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Lecce: Known as the “Florence of the South,” Lecce is a beautiful city famous for its baroque architecture and lively cultural scene.

  • Gallipoli: A charming seaside town with stunning beaches and a historic old town.

  • Santa Maria di Leuca: A picturesque seaside resort town located at the southernmost tip of the Salento peninsula.

Conclusion

Specchia is a hidden gem that offers plenty of things to see and do for all kinds of travelers. This comprehensive guide has provided you with all the essential information to plan your trip to this beautiful southern town and explore its cultural and natural wonders.
