Introduction to Otranto

Nestled in the southernmost tip of Italy’s ‘heel,’ Otranto is a beguiling coastal town which combines rich cultural heritage with mesmerizing natural beauty.

With its sparkling clear waters, fine sandy beaches, and Mediterranean climate, Otranto is the perfect destination to visit any time of the year. But that’s not all. The town boasts a diverse range of culturally enriched attractions, such as museums, stunning cathedrals, and a charming old town that has been designated a UNESCO cultural heritage site. We invite you to discover Otranto’s hidden gems and soul-stirring sights with our comprehensive travel guide.

List of Places to Visit in Otranto

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  1. Otranto Cathedral One of Otranto’s most striking landmarks, the cathedral is home to an impressive collection of 12th and 13th century frescoes. The cathedral also has a crypt that houses the remains of the town’s martyrs who refused to convert to Islam during the Turkish invasion of 1480.

  2. Byzantine Church of San Pietro This beautiful 11th-century church boasts striking Byzantine architecture and is adorned with stunning mosaics that date back to the 12th century.

  3. Castello Aragonese Built by the Spanish in the 15th century to protect the town from Turkish invasions, this stunning castle is perched on a rocky outcrop and offers panoramic views of the town and the sea.

Natural Attractions of Otranto: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  1. Marinas and Beaches Otranto is blessed with a stunning coastline that spans over 20km and is dotted with small coves, hidden bays, and pristine beaches. For a more authentic experience, head to the beaches of Alimini or Baia dei Turchi, both stunning natural oases where you can relax and soak up the sun.

  2. Coastal Hiking and Biking Routes Otranto’s natural beauty is ideal for those who love outdoor adventures. There are numerous hiking and biking trails that offer stunning sea and mountain views, and wind through ancient olive groves and Mediterranean scrublands.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Otranto

  1. Salentine Way This 230km long hiking trail vaults across Salento, from the Adriatic to the Ionian coast. Along the way, the trek takes in stunning coastal views and historical towns.

  2. Sentiero del Cipolliane For a shorter hiking route, try the Sentiero del Cipolliane. This 6km trail provides an excellent opportunity to explore the beautiful countryside surrounding Otranto.

Best Things to Do in Otranto: Activities and Tours

  1. Boat Tour of the Coastline One of the best ways to soak up Otranto’s natural beauty is by taking a boat tour along its breathtaking coastline. Along the way, you will have the chance to see the famous “Poesia Cave,” where the sea caves form lines of poetry on the walls.

  2. Cultural Tour of the Old Town Otranto’s historic old town is steeped in history, and exploring it on a cultural tour is a must-do. You can visit charming squares, ancient churches, and stunning palaces, each with their unique story to tell.

Food and Drink Scene of Otranto

Must-Try Dishes

  1. Friselle A traditional dish of Otranto, Friselle is a type of flatbread that is soaked in water and then topped with tomatoes, oregano and olives. Perfect for those hot summer days!

  2. Orecchiette alle cime di rapa This dish is made of handmade pasta shaped like little ears (orecchiette), served with a sauce made of olive oil, garlic and turnip. It’s one of the most popular dishes in the region.

Local Beverages

  1. Negramaro This indigenous red wine has an intense and rich flavor, with a hint of sour cherries and spices. Negramaro is the ideal wine to pair with Otranto’s hearty, spicy dishes.

  2. Espresso Coffee Otranto is famous for its strong, rich espresso coffee, which is prepared and served with great attention to detail.

Where to Stay in Otranto: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  1. Masseria Gattamora This charming guesthouse is housed in a renovated 18th-century farmhouse and is surrounded by idyllic gardens and olive groves. It’s the perfect place to experience the authentic atmosphere of Otranto.

  2. Relais Valle dell’Idro Nestled amid the verdant hills surrounding the town, this stylish boutique hotel is an excellent base for exploring Otranto and the surrounding countryside.

What to Buy in Otranto: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  1. Hand-crafted lace Otranto is known for its intricate and delicate lacework, which has been a local craft since the 17th century. The town has various workshops where you can purchase these works of art to keep as souvenirs or gifts.

  2. Olive Oil Olive oil is one of Salento’s flagship products. It is known for its rich flavor, aroma, and health benefits. You can buy locally produced olive oils in markets and grocery stores throughout the town.

Local Culture and Customs of Otranto

Otranto is a friendly, laid-back town, where traditional values and customs have been preserved for generations. The people are known for their warm hospitality and welcoming nature, and visitors are always made to feel at home.

Best Time to Visit Otranto

The best time to visit Otranto is from May to October, when the days are long, the skies are clear, and the weather is warm but not overwhelmingly hot. July and August can be very busy with tourists, but the town remains charming nonetheless.

Do’s and Don’ts of Otranto

Do:

  • Respect the local customs and traditions
  • Try the local cuisine and wine
  • Support local businesses
  • Visit the famous sites and attractions of the town

Don’t:

  • Litter the town or the beaches
  • Be loud in residential areas
  • Disrespect the religious symbols or architecture

Safety and Health

Safety in Otranto

Otranto is generally safe, with a low crime rate. Visitors should always be aware of their surroundings and take precautions against pickpocketing, especially in crowded areas.

Health in Otranto

The health facilities in Otranto are of a high standard, and there are several private clinics and hospitals available. European travelers should bring their European Health Insurance Card to receive free or reduced medical care in Italy.

Getting to Otranto: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Otranto

Brindisi airport is the nearest international airport to Otranto, located approximately 75 km away. The airport is serviced by major airlines like Ryanair, EasyJet, and Alitalia.

Bus Stations Near Otranto

There are several bus services connecting Otranto to other towns in Salento and the wider region of Puglia.

Other Options

For a unique and authentic experience, why not arrive in Otranto by ferry or private yacht? The town has a small, charming marina that can accommodate boats of all sizes.

How to Get Around in Otranto

The best way to explore Otranto is by foot, since most of its charms lie in its charming pedestrian-friendly old town and nearby natural beauty. However, if you want to venture further afield, renting a car or a bicycle is a good option.

Where to Go Next from Otranto: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Lecce

This magnificent Baroque city is located only 50km away from Otranto and is a must-see attraction for lovers of art and history.

Alberobello

Located in the Itria Valley, Alberobello is a captivating town known for its charming trulli, traditional cone-shaped houses made entirely out of limestone.

Conclusion

Otranto is a town that truly has it all- stunning beaches, beguiling natural beauty, and a rich cultural heritage that spans centuries. Whether you’re looking to explore ancient sites, enjoy outdoor adventures, or simply indulge in delicious food and wine, Otranto is the ideal destination for making memories that will last a lifetime. Book your trip today, and let Otranto take your breath away!
