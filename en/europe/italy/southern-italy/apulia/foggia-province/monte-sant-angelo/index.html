Introduction to Monte Sant’Angelo

Monte Sant’Angelo is a small town located in the province of Foggia, in the southern region of Apulia, Italy. The town is situated on a rocky hilltop overlooking the Gulf of Manfredonia. It is famous for the Sanctuary of Monte Sant’Angelo, a UNESCO World Heritage site, and is considered to be one of the most important pilgrimage destinations in Europe. The town also boasts stunning natural beauty and an intriguing history.

List of Places to Visit in Monte Sant’Angelo

Monte Sant’Angelo offers a variety of attractions ranging from culturally significant museums to breathtaking natural scenery.

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Sanctuary of Monte Sant’Angelo: The Sanctuary of Monte Sant’Angelo is a UNESCO World Heritage site that dates back to the 5th century. It is one of the most important pilgrimage destinations in Europe and attracts thousands of visitors every year. It is famous for its extraordinary bronze doors, mosaic floors, and a marble statue of Saint Michael the Archangel.

  • Castle of Monte Sant’Angelo: The Castle of Monte Sant’Angelo was built in the 10th century and is an important example of military architecture. The castle offers stunning views over the surrounding landscape, and it houses a museum that displays a collection of artifacts from the medieval period.

  • Museum of Popular Culture: The Museum of Popular Culture is dedicated to preserving the history and traditions of the region of Gargano. The museum features exhibits on folk culture, traditional handicrafts, and local customs.

Natural Attractions of Monte Sant’Angelo: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Foresta Umbra: Foresta Umbra is a beautiful national park that covers an area of 11,000 hectares. It is home to a diverse range of flora and fauna, including deer, wild boar, and wolves. The park offers a variety of hiking trails, picnic areas, and wildlife spotting opportunities.

  • Baia delle Zagare: Baia delle Zagare is a stunning beach located near Monte Sant’Angelo. It is famous for its crystal-clear waters and white sandy beaches. The beach is perfect for swimming, sunbathing, and relaxing.

  • Monte Saraceno: Monte Saraceno is a mountain located near Monte Sant’Angelo. It offers breathtaking views of the surrounding landscape and is popular among hikers and mountain climbers.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Monte Sant’Angelo

Monte Sant’Angelo is an ideal destination for hiking and biking enthusiasts. It is surrounded by beautiful mountains, forests, and beaches. Here are some of the most popular hiking and biking routes in the area.

  • Gargano trekking trails: Gargano is home to a variety of trekking trails that offer stunning views and challenging terrain. Some of the most popular trails include the Vieste-Diomede trail, the San Giovanni Rotondo-Corna Malata trail, and the Monte Pulsano trail.

  • Foresta Umbra: Foresta Umbra offers a variety of hiking trails that range from easy to difficult. The trails wind through beautiful forests and offer opportunities to spot wildlife and enjoy scenic views.

  • Monte Saraceno: Monte Saraceno offers a challenging hiking route that rewards hikers with stunning views of the surrounding landscape.

Best Things to Do in Monte Sant’Angelo: Activities and Tours

Monte Sant’Angelo offers a variety of activities and tours that cater to different interests and preferences. Here are some of the best things to do while in Monte Sant’Angelo.

  • Visit the Sanctuary of Monte Sant’Angelo: The Sanctuary of Monte Sant’Angelo is a must-visit attraction in Monte Sant’Angelo. It is considered to be one of the most important pilgrimage destinations in Europe, and it boasts beautiful architecture and artwork.

  • Explore the Castle of Monte Sant’Angelo: The Castle of Monte Sant’Angelo offers stunning views of the surrounding countryside, and it houses a museum that displays artifacts from the medieval period.

  • Go on a nature tour: Monte Sant’Angelo and its surrounding areas offer breathtaking natural beauty. Visitors can go on nature tours that explore the forests, mountains, and beaches of the area.

  • Taste the local cuisine: Monte Sant’Angelo is known for its delicious cuisine that features fresh seafood, local vegetables, and homemade pasta. Visitors can taste the local specialties at restaurants and street vendors throughout the town.

Food and Drink Scene of Monte Sant’Angelo

Monte Sant’Angelo is known for its delicious cuisine that features fresh seafood, local vegetables, and homemade pasta. Here are some must-try dishes and beverages in the area.

Must-Try Dishes

  • Orecchiette alle cime di rapa: Orecchiette alle cime di rapa is a local pasta dish with broccoli rabe and garlic. It is a must-try dish in Monte Sant’Angelo.

  • Pesce alla griglia: Fresh seafood is a staple in Monte Sant’Angelo. Visitors can taste delicious grilled fish at local restaurants and street vendors throughout the town.

  • Burrata: Burrata is a creamy cheese that is typical of the Apulia region. It can be tasted in salads, sandwiches, or on its own with fresh bread.

Local Beverages

  • Nero di Troia: Nero di Troia is a local red wine that is known for its rich flavor and aroma. It is produced in the surrounding countryside and can be tasted in local wine shops and restaurants.

  • Limoncello: Limoncello is a lemon liqueur that is popular throughout Italy. It can be tasted in local bars and restaurants in Monte Sant’Angelo.
