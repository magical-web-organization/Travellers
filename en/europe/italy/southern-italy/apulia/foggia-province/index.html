Introduction to Foggia (Province)

Foggia is a province in the Apulia region of Southern Italy. It is known for its rich history and culture, stunning natural scenery, and delicious food and drink. From the beauty of the Gargano National Park to the ancient Roman ruins of Lucera, Foggia has something to offer everyone.

  • Burrata Cheese
  • Risotto alla marinara (Seafood Risotto)

  • Limoncello (Lemon Liqueur)

Local Culture and Customs of Foggia (Province)

  • It is customary to greet people with a handshake and a kiss on each cheek
  • Tipping is not expected in restaurants, but rounding up the bill is appreciated
  • Dress modestly when visiting churches and other religious sites

Best Time to Visit Foggia (Province)

The best time to visit Foggia is during the summer months of June to August, when the weather is warm and sunny. However, spring and early autumn can also be pleasant times to visit, with fewer crowds and milder temperatures.

Do’s and Don’ts of Foggia (Province)

Do:

  • Try the local cuisine and wine
  • Respect local customs and traditions
  • Explore the stunning natural scenery

Don’t:

  • Wear revealing clothing when visiting churches and other religious sites
  • Expect to find many English speakers, especially in smaller towns
  • Leave your belongings unattended in public places

Safety and Health

Safety in Foggia (Province)

Foggia is a relatively safe area, but visitors should still take basic precautions, such as keeping valuables secure and being aware of their surroundings at all times.

Health in Foggia (Province)

Tap water is generally safe to drink in Foggia, but visitors may wish to stick to bottled water to avoid any stomach upsets. It is also advisable to take out comprehensive travel insurance before visiting.

Getting to Foggia (Province): Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Foggia (Province)

  • Bari Karol Wojtyła Airport (BRI)
  • Naples International Airport (NAP)

Bus Stations Near Foggia (Province)

  • Foggia Bus Terminal
  • Vieste Bus Terminal

Other Options

  • Hire a car and explore the area at your own pace

How to Get Around in Foggia (Province)

The best way to get around Foggia is by car, as public transportation options are limited, especially in rural areas.

Where to Go Next from Foggia (Province): Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Alberobello: a beautiful small town famous for its Trulli houses
  • Matera: a stunning UNESCO-listed city known for its cave dwellings
  • Salento: a beautiful coastal area with stunning beaches and historic towns

Conclusion

Foggia is a hidden gem in Southern Italy, with a rich history and culture, beautiful natural scenery, and delicious food and drink. With so much to see and do, it is the perfect destination for anyone looking to explore a lesser-known part of Italy.
