Introduction to Taranto

Located in the Southern region of Apulia in Italy, Taranto is a beautiful coastal city that offers a unique blend of culture, history, and natural beauty. Known as the city of two seas, Taranto is situated on a peninsula that divides the Ionian and Mar Piccolo seas. The city is a popular travel destination for those seeking to experience the rich history and culture of ancient Italy while soaking up the sun on its mesmerizing beaches.

List of Places to Visit in Taranto

If you’re planning a trip to Taranto, be sure to visit these must-see attractions:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Museo Nazionale Archeologico di Taranto: Visit this museum to explore the vast collection of Greek pottery, Roman sculptures, and artifacts from the Bronze Age and beyond. You can also admire the magnificent Temple of Poseidon, an ancient Greek temple located on the premises of the museum.
  • Castello Aragonese: This 15th-century castle is a famous tourist attraction in Taranto. The castle offers incredible views of the sea and houses a museum that showcases the city’s history and art.
  • Cattedrale di San Cataldo: This magnificent cathedral was built in the 11th century in the Romanesque architectural style. It’s located in the historic center of the city and houses some beautiful frescoes.

Natural Attractions of Taranto: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Parco Archeologico di Saturo: It’s a protected area and features a sandy beach, a forest of Aleppo pine trees, and a picturesque coastal landscape.
  • Riserva Naturale delle Saline di Taranto: Discover a unique ecosystem of saltwater ponds and marshland in this nature reserve. You can spot a range of bird species, including pink flamingos, herons, and egrets.
  • Grotte di Castellana: These caves are an incredible natural attraction located just a short drive from Taranto. The caves feature complex rock formations, underground lakes, and beautiful caverns.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Taranto

Taranto offers several scenic hiking and biking trails that allow you to explore the beautiful natural surroundings.

  • Via Appia Antica: This ancient road offers a unique opportunity to bike on a well-preserved Roman road while enjoying the sea breeze and picturesque views of the coastline.
  • Parco Terra delle Gravine: Enjoy a hike through this park and explore the breathtaking landscape that includes ravines, limestone formations, and oak forests.
  • Parco Cava D’Ispica: This regional park offers several hiking trails with varying difficulty levels. You can admire the scenic views of the canyon, the rocky outcrops, and the beautiful flora and fauna.

Best Things to Do in Taranto: Activities and Tours

There are plenty of exciting activities and tours to do and explore in Taranto. Here are a few must-try experiences:

  • Boat Tour of the Two Seas: Explore the beauty of Taranto’s two seas with a boat tour that takes you through the historic city center, under the old bridges, and along the breathtaking coastline.
  • Cooking Class: Learn how to prepare traditional Apulian cuisine with a cooking class organized by local chefs. You’ll get to taste the authentic flavors of Italy while learning new culinary skills.
  • Wine Tasting Tour in Manduria: This famous wine region is a short drive from Taranto and offers excellent opportunities to sample some fantastic local wine.

Food and Drink Scene of Taranto

Food and drink are an essential part of the Taranto culture. Here are a few must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Orecchiette con le cime di rape: It’s a classic Apulian pasta dish made with ear-shaped pasta and broccoli rabe.
  • Focaccia Barese: This traditional flatbread is made with tomato sauce, olives, and a variety of other toppings and is often eaten as a snack or for breakfast.
  • Crudo di Mare: This raw fish dish typically includes shrimp, squid, and other seafood, marinated with olive oil, lemon juice, and parsley.

Local Beverages

  • Negroamaro: This is a popular red wine grape variety, mainly grown in southern Italy, including the Taranto region.
  • Primitivo: This red wine grape is grown throughout the Apulia region and produces full-bodied, fruity wines.
  • Espresso: No trip to Italy is complete without trying a traditional espresso. A single shot of espresso is served in a small cup and is perfect for a quick pick-me-up throughout the day.

Where to Stay in Taranto: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

There is no shortage of accommodation options in Taranto, catering to all budgets and preferences. Here are a few highly recommended hotels, hostels, and guesthouses:

  • Hotel Mercure Delfino Taranto: Located on the seafront, this modern hotel offers beautiful sea views, a rooftop bar, and an on-site restaurant.
  • B&B San Domenico: This charming guesthouse is situated in the historic center of Taranto and offers comfortable, budget-friendly rooms.
  • Hotel Akropolis: This four-star hotel offers spacious rooms, elegant decor, and a restaurant serving delicious local cuisine.

What to Buy in Taranto: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Taranto has a rich local culture and produces several unique products that make great souvenirs. Here are some local products to bring home:

  • Ceramic Artwork: Taranto is famous for its exquisite ceramic artwork, including plates, bowls, vases, and other pottery pieces.
  • Olive Oil: The Apulia region produces some of the best olive oils in Italy, and you can find several local producers selling their premium-quality olive oil in Taranto.
  • Hand-Crafted Jewelry: Taranto is known for its beautiful handcrafted jewelry made with precious metals and semi-precious stones.

Local Culture and Customs of Taranto

Taranto has a rich and unique culture, influenced by its ancient Greek and Roman heritage. Here are a few local customs to keep in mind while visiting:

  • Siesta: Many shops and businesses in Taranto close for a few hours in the afternoon for siesta, so plan your activities accordingly.
  • Respectful Clothing: When visiting religious attractions, make sure to dress modestly and cover your shoulders and knees.
  • Greeting Customs: Italians typically greet each other with a handshake or a hug, depending on their relationship.

Best Time to Visit Taranto

The best time to visit Taranto is during the spring or fall months, when the temperatures are mild and the crowds are relatively small. The summers can be hot and crowded, and many locals go on holiday during this time, making it difficult to navigate the city.

Do’s and Don’ts of Taranto

Here are a few simple do’s and don’ts to keep in mind while visiting Taranto:

Do:

  • Learn Some Italian: Learning a few basic Italian phrases can go a long way in building friendly relationships with locals.
  • Try Local Cuisine: Taranto has a rich local food culture, and trying different dishes is the best way to experience the city’s unique flavors.
  • Be Respectful: Italians place a high value on respect and polite behavior, so make sure to always be courteous and polite.

Don’t:

  • Forget Cash: Many smaller shops and restaurants in Taranto don’t accept credit cards, so always carry some cash with you.
  • Be Loud: Italians often speak more softly than Americans, so avoid speaking too loudly in public places.
  • Take Photos Without Asking: When taking pictures of locals or their businesses, always ask for permission first.

Safety and Health

Taranto is a relatively safe city, with low crime rates. Here are a few safety and health tips to keep in mind:

Safety in Taranto

  • Avoid Flashy Jewelry: Wearing expensive jewelry can attract unwanted attention, so leave your valuable items at home.
  • Be Vigilant in Crowded Areas: Pickpockets are known to target crowded spaces, so always be aware of your surroundings.
  • Stay in Well-Lit Areas at Night: Avoid walking in dark or isolated areas at night and stick to well-lit streets and areas.

Health in Taranto

  • Stay Hydrated: Taranto can get very hot during the summer months, so make sure to drink plenty of water throughout the day.
  • Use Sunscreen: Protect your skin from the harmful rays of the sun and use a high SPF sunscreen when spending time outdoors.
  • Pack Essential Medications: If you have any medical conditions, be sure to pack your essential medications and carry them with you at all times.

Getting to Taranto: Transportation Options and Logistics

Taranto is easily accessible by several modes of transport. Here are a few convenient transportation options to consider:

Airports Near Taranto

  • Brindisi Airport: Located approximately 80 km from Taranto, Brindisi airport is the closest airport to Taranto, offering several domestic and international connections.

Bus Stations Near Taranto

  • Taranto Bus Station: The bus station is located in the heart of the city and offers connections to several destinations throughout the region.

Other Options

  • Train: Taranto has a well-developed railway network, offering several connections to major cities throughout Italy.
  • Car: If you prefer to drive, Taranto is easily accessible via the A14 highway, which connects major cities in the Apulia region.

How to Get Around in Taranto

Taranto is a relatively small city, and most attractions are within walking distance of each other. However, if you prefer to use public transport, there are several options, including buses and taxis.

Where to Go Next from Taranto: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

If you have more time to explore the Apulia region, here are a few nearby destinations that you can visit as a day trip from Taranto:

  • Lecce: This charming Baroque city is located approximately 100 km from Taranto and is known for its beautiful architecture and art.
  • Matera: Located in the neighboring Basilicata region, Matera is a historic city famous for its ancient cave dwellings and rock-cut architecture.
  • Alberobello: This charming village is famous for its trulli houses, unique cone-shaped homes built entirely of stone.

Conclusion

Taranto is a beautiful coastal city that offers a unique blend of history, culture, and natural beauty. Whether you’re exploring the city’s fascinating museums and historical sites, hiking through its scenic landscapes, or sampling the delicious local cuisine, Taranto has something for everyone. With this comprehensive guide, you’re all set to plan an unforgettable trip to this beautiful Italian city.
