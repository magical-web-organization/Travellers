Introduction to Ostuni

Ostuni, a small town located in the Brindisi Province of Apulia, Southern Italy, is popularly known as the “White City” due to its whitewashed architecture. The sun-kissed beaches, charming countryside, and the rich cultural heritage make it the perfect tourist destination for those seeking an authentic Italian experience. In this comprehensive guide, we’ll be exploring the many attractions and experiences that Ostuni has in store for you.

List of Places to Visit in Ostuni

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Ostuni Cathedral, also known as the Cathedral of Santa Maria Assunta, is a 15th-century Gothic cathedral located at the highest point of the town.
  • Museo Diocesano is a museum located at the right-hand side of the Cathedral that contains a collection of precious religious art.
  • Arco Scoppa is a beautiful archway that shelters a steep stone staircase that leads to Corso Vittorio Emanuele II.
  • Palazzo Vescovile is a palace designed by Giuseppe Zimbalo in the 18th century that houses the chamber of commerce.

Natural Attractions of Ostuni: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Parco Dune Costiere, the nature reserve park, stretches along the Adriatic coastline in the Brindisi province. It’s an ideal place for birdwatching, hiking, and cycling.
  • Torre Guaceto is a crystal blue-white sandy beach located close to the nature reserve park. It’s perfect for swimming, boating, and sunbathing.
  • The Apulian aqueduct is an engineering marvel as it transports water to Ostuni from the Cavanata springs, 30 kilometers away.
  • Thanks to its location on a hill, Ostuni boasts stunning panoramic views of the countryside, olive groves, and vineyards surrounding the town.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Ostuni

If you’re a hiking or cycling enthusiast, Ostuni offers several well-marked trails that are perfect for outdoor adventurers. The trails are suitable for all skill levels and offer a great opportunity to explore the beautiful countryside, forests, and coastline of Ostuni. Here are some of the popular routes:

  • The Sentiero del Vento trail is a 10-kilometer trail that starts at Piazzetta Sant’Oronzo in the historic town center and winds through olive groves, vineyards, and medieval watchtowers before reaching the coastline of the Adriatic Sea.
  • The Acquedotto Pugliese trail stretches for over 50 kilometers and follows the route taken by the aqueduct that supplies water to Ostuni from the Cavanata springs.
  • The Sentiero Adriatico is a coastal trail that runs along the beaches and cliffs of the Adriatic Sea, offering breathtaking views of the sea and the surrounding landscape.

Best Things to Do in Ostuni: Activities and Tours

There are plenty of things to do in Ostuni, making it an excellent holiday destination for families, couples, and solo travelers alike. Here are some of the top activities and tours that you can enjoy in Ostuni:

  • Visit the olive groves and wine cellars of Ostuni’s countryside, where you can taste the region’s famous olive oil and wine.
  • Take a guided walking tour through the historic town center, exploring its narrow lanes, piazzas, and local restaurants.
  • Attend the annual festivals, such as the White Night Festival in August or the Saint Oronzo Festival in September, which celebrates the town’s patron saint.
  • Take part in cooking classes, where you can learn to cook traditional Apulian dishes like homemade orecchiette pasta and local specialties such as seafood and meat dishes.
  • Explore the surrounding towns and villages, such as Alberobello, Lecce, and Locorotondo, which are known for their unique architecture, crafts, and traditions.

Week-Long Itinerary for Ostuni

If you’re planning a week-long trip to Ostuni, here is a sample itinerary that covers all the main attractions and activities:

Day Activity
Day 1 Relax on the beach at Torre Guaceto and explore the nature reserve.
Day 2 Visit Ostuni’s historic town center, including the Cathedral, Museo Diocesano, and Palazzo Vescovile.
Day 3 Take a cycling tour of the countryside, visiting local olive groves and wineries.
Day 4 Explore the neighboring towns of Cisternino and Martina Franca, which are known for their beautiful architecture and local cuisine.
Day 5 Attend the Saint Oronzo Festival and enjoy the street food and festivities.
Day 6 Take a cooking class and learn to prepare traditional Apulian dishes.
Day 7 Visit the surrounding beaches and the picturesque town of Polignano a Mare.

Food and Drink Scene of Ostuni

Must-Try Dishes

Ostuni is renowned for its delicious, traditional cuisine, which is based on fresh ingredients and simple recipes. Some must-try dishes include:

  • Orecchiette con le Cime di Rapa: Homemade pasta served with cime di rapa, a bitter green vegetable, and a sprinkling of grated Pecorino cheese.
  • Focaccia Barese: A soft bread topped with tomatoes, olives, onions, and olive oil, baked in a wood-fired oven.
  • Polpette di Melanzane: Eggplant fritters that are crispy on the outside and soft on the inside.
  • Burrata e Pachino: Fresh creamy cheese served with cherry tomatoes and basil.

Local Beverages

In Ostuni, you’ll find a wide variety of local beverages, including:

  • Primitivo: A deep red wine that is produced in the region and is famous for its full-bodied flavor.
  • Negroamaro: Another red wine that is also produced in the region and is known for its intense flavor and aroma.
  • Pugliese beer: Craft beer made from locally grown barley and hops.
  • Limoncello: A sweet lemon liqueur that is typically served as a digestif.

Where to Stay in Ostuni: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Ostuni has a wide range of accommodation options to suit all budgets and preferences, from luxury hotels to budget-friendly hostels and guesthouses. Some of the best places to stay in Ostuni include:

  • Hotel Monte Sarago: A luxurious hotel located in the historic town center, offering panoramic views of the sea and countryside.
  • Palazzo Mastronardi: A beautifully restored palazzo that has been converted into a boutique hotel in the heart of the town.
  • Ostuni Palace Hotel: A modern hotel with a rooftop pool and an excellent restaurant.
  • La Sommita Relais: A luxury hotel located in an 18th-century palace that features a stunning roof garden and spa.

What to Buy in Ostuni: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

When it comes to shopping in Ostuni, there are plenty of options for souvenirs and local products. Make sure to pick up some of these items:

  • Ceramics: Ostuni is famous for its intricate and colorful ceramic pottery, including plates, bowls, and mugs, among other things.
  • Olive oil: The region produces some of the best olive oil in Italy, so make sure to pick up a bottle or two to take home.
  • Handmade textiles: You’ll find a wide range of handmade textiles, including tablecloths, linens, and clothing, made from local fabrics and materials.
  • Taralli: A delicious and traditional snack, similar to a pretzel-like breadstick, that is perfect for sharing with friends and family.

Local Culture and Customs of Ostuni

The locals in Ostuni are warm and welcoming, and their customs and traditions are deeply rooted in the town’s history and culture. One notable custom that visitors should be aware of is the “white night” festival that takes place in August, where the entire town center is decorated with white lights, and there are street performances and live music all night long. It’s a celebration that shouldn’t be missed.

Best Time to Visit Ostuni

The best time to visit Ostuni is between April and June, or September and October, when the weather is mild, and the crowds are smaller. July and August can be hot and crowded, and many of the locals head to the coast, making it quieter in the town.

Do’s and Don’ts of Ostuni

Do:

  • Respect the local culture and customs by dressing modestly when visiting churches or religious sites.
  • Try the local cuisine, especially the fresh seafood and homemade pasta dishes.
  • Take part in local festivals and celebrations when possible.

Don’t:

  • Litter in public spaces or on the beaches.
  • Wear revealing clothing or swimwear outside of the beach or pool areas.
  • Expect late night parties and nightlife in Ostuni’s town center.

Safety and Health

Safety in Ostuni

Ostuni is a safe town, and violent crime is rare. However, visitors should take the same precautions they would take in any other tourist destination, such as being aware of their surroundings, watching their belongings, and avoiding unlit or isolated areas at night.

Health in Ostuni

The water in Ostuni is safe to drink, and medical facilities are available in the town center in case of emergency. It’s always a good idea to have travel insurance that covers the cost of medical treatment and to bring any necessary medications.

Getting to Ostuni: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Ostuni

The closest airport to Ostuni is Brindisi Airport, which is approximately 30 kilometers away. Bari Airport is also an option, but it’s about 100 kilometers away.

Bus Stations Near Ostuni

The bus station in Ostuni is located in Piazza della Libertà in the historic town center and offers connections to nearby towns and cities, such as Lecce, Martina Franca, and Alberobello.

Other Options

Aside from the bus, you can also take a train from Ostuni’s railway station, which provides links to other cities in Apulia and other parts of Italy. Taxis and car rental services are also available.

How to Get Around in Ostuni

Exploring Ostuni on foot is the best way to experience its charming streets and lanes, but for those who prefer to be more mobile, there is a local bus service that connects the town center to nearby beaches such as Villanova. Car and bike rentals are also available in the town center, providing easy access to the surrounding countryside.

Where to Go Next from Ostuni: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

If you have some extra time on your hands and want to explore the surrounding area, there are several nearby destinations that are worth visiting, including:

  • Alberobello: A UNESCO World Heritage Site known for its iconic trulli houses.
  • Lecce: Known as the “Florence of the South” for its stunning Baroque architecture.
  • Polignano a Mare: A charming seaside town known for its picturesque streets that tumble down to the sea.
  • Brindisi: The nearest major city, known for its seafront promenade, beautiful architecture, and ancient Roman ruins.

Conclusion

Ostuni offers a unique blend of history, culture, and natural beauty that makes it an ideal holiday destination for travelers looking to experience authentic Italy. From its white-washed historic town center to its stunning beaches and countryside, there’s something for everyone in Ostuni. We hope this comprehensive guide has helped you plan your trip and given you ideas for what to see and do while you’re there.
