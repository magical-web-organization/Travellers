Brindisi (province) is a hidden gem in Southern Italy that offers travelers spectacular natural scenery, rich cultural sites, and an array of outdoor activities. In this comprehensive travel guide, we’ll take you on a journey through Brindisi (province), sharing all the information you need to prepare for and plan your adventure.

Introduction to Brindisi (province)

Brindisi (province) is located in the Apulia region in Southern Italy. Home to over 400,000 people, Brindisi (province) is known for its picturesque beaches, historic architecture, and traditional charms. The climate in Brindisi (province) is typically Mediterranean, with hot summers and mild winters. It is easy to get around the region, with public transportation options like buses and trains.

List of Places to Visit in Brindisi (province)

Brindisi (province) is home to numerous cultural and natural attractions that are worth visiting. Here are a few highlights:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Roman Column of Brindisi: A historic site located in the heart of Brindisi (province) that dates back to the Second Century AD.
  • Museum of the Sea: A maritime museum located in Brindisi city that displays the history of the region’s maritime industry.
  • Oria Castle: A medieval castle dating back to the eleventh and twelfth centuries located in the town of Oria.

Natural Attractions of Brindisi (province): Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Otranto: A seaside town known for its stunning coastal views, charming historic center, and beautiful beaches.
  • Lecce: A city known for its Baroque architecture, Lecce is often called “The Florence of the South.”
  • Salento: A region located in the southernmost part of Italy filled with beautiful beaches, crystal-clear waters, and tranquil countryside.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Brindisi (province)

There are many hiking and biking trails throughout Brindisi (province). Some of the most notable routes include:

  • Acquedotto Pugliese: A 35 km bike route that follows the historic Acquedotto Pugliese, a water supply system for the region.
  • Salento Way: A popular hiking route that runs along the coastline of the Salento region.

Best Things to Do in Brindisi (province): Activities and Tours

There are many exciting activities and tours to experience in Brindisi (province). Here are a few recommended options:

  • Boat tour in Taranto: Take a boat tour along the coast of Taranto, enjoying stunning views and the peaceful atmosphere of the sea.
  • Cycling tour: Explore the beautiful countryside and charming towns of Brindisi (province) on a cycling tour.

Week-Long Itinerary for Brindisi (province)

To make the most of your visit to Brindisi (province), here is a suggested itinerary:

Day 1: Explore the historic center of Brindisi city and visit the Roman Column. Day 2: Take a boat tour of Taranto and visit the Museum of the Sea. Day 3: Go on a hiking tour of the Salento Way and experience the natural beauty of the region. Day 4: Visit the town of Oria and explore the Oria Castle. Day 5: Discover the charming town of Lecce and see its impressive Baroque architecture. Day 6: Relax on the beaches of Otranto and take in the stunning coastal views. Day 7: Visit a local winery and enjoy a wine tasting experience.

Food and Drink Scene of Brindisi (province)

The food and drink scene in Brindisi (province) is a vibrant mix of traditional local cuisine and modern, innovative flavors.

Must-Try Dishes

  • Orecchiette alle cime di rapa: A pasta dish that is a staple of the Apulian cuisine made with orecchiette (small ear-shaped pasta) and cime di rapa (a bitter green).
  • Tiella di riso, patate e cozze: A traditional casserole dish made with rice, potatoes, and mussels.

Local Beverages

  • Negroamaro: A red wine that is produced in the region and is known for its rich, earthy flavor.
  • Primitivo: A red wine that has a deep, full-bodied flavor.

Where to Stay in Brindisi (province): Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

There are many options for accommodations in Brindisi (province), ranging from budget-friendly hostels to luxury resorts. Some of the most popular options include:

  • Hotel Executive Inn: A four-star hotel located in Brindisi city with comfortable rooms and an on-site restaurant.
  • B&B Palazzo Corcioli: A charming bed and breakfast located in Oria with beautiful views of the castle.

What to Buy in Brindisi (province): Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Ceramics: Pottery is an ancient craft in Brindisi (province) and you will find many local pottery shops selling beautiful handmade ceramics.
  • Olive Oil: Olive oil is a staple in the cuisine of Brindisi (province) and is often sold in local shops and markets.

Local Culture and Customs of Brindisi (province)

Brindisi (province) has a rich cultural heritage, with several traditional customs still practiced today. Some customs and traditions include:

  • Festa dei Morti: A religious festival held in November which honors the dead.
  • Taranta Dance: A traditional dance native to Salento, which symbolizes the fight against tarantula bites.

Best Time to Visit Brindisi (province)

The best time to visit Brindisi (province) is during the summer months from June to August when the weather is warm and sunny. However, if you prefer to avoid crowds, you may want to visit during the shoulder seasons of spring or autumn.

  • Dress respectfully when visiting historical sites and churches.

Don’t:

  • Don’t wear revealing clothing in public places.

Safety and Health

Safety in Brindisi (province)

Brindisi (province) is generally a safe place to visit. However, it’s always wise to be aware of your surroundings and take precautions, especially when visiting crowded tourist areas.

Health in Brindisi (province)

The health care system in Brindisi (province) is of a high standard, and there are many hospitals and health clinics throughout the region.

Getting to Brindisi (province): Transportation Options and Logistics

Brindisi (province) is well connected to other parts of Italy and Europe, with several transportation options available.

Airports Near Brindisi (province)

  • Brindisi Salento Airport: Located just outside the city of Brindisi, this airport is serviced by several airlines, including Ryanair and Alitalia.

Bus Stations Near Brindisi (province)

  • Brindisi Bus Station: The main bus station in Brindisi city, with services to other parts of the region.

Other Options

  • Train: Brindisi (province) is serviced by the national rail network, with connections to other parts of Italy.
  • Car rental: Car rental options are available in Brindisi and are a good option for exploring the region at your own pace.

How to Get Around in Brindisi (province)

Public transportation options including buses and trains are widely available in Brindisi (province). Taxis and car rental services are also available if you prefer to have more flexibility.
