Introduction to Molfetta

Molfetta is a charming coastal town located in the Bari province of Apulia in Southern Italy. With its beautiful beaches lapped by the crystal clear waters of the Adriatic Sea, Molfetta is a popular summer destination for both domestic and international tourists. The town is dotted with historic churches, narrow lanes, and ancient ruins that tell the story of its long history.

List of Places to Visit in Molfetta

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Molfetta Cathedral - is an impressive structure, situated in the heart of the old town. This 12th-century Romanesque cathedral offers breathtaking views and intricate medieval architecture that will leave you awestruck.
  • Molfetta Museum - houses an impressive collection of archaeological artifacts and art that narrate the town’s rich history and cultural heritage.
  • Piazza Municipio and Piazza Roma - these two main squares are the liveliest parts of the town, featuring local shops, restaurants, and cafes.

Natural Attractions of Molfetta: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Molfetta Beaches - the beaches of Molfetta, such as Cala Corvino and Piazza San Domenico, offer stunning views of the Adriatic Sea and the perfect spot to sunbathe or swim.
  • Monte di Mola - located in the eastern part of Molfetta, is a beautiful and scenic area that is great for hiking and exploring.
  • Coastal Trail - a long and uninterrupted stretch of coastline that runs from Giovinazzo to Bisceglie is perfect for biking or walking.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Molfetta

  • Monte di Mola Trail - a popular trail that takes you past beautiful pine forests and ancient ruins.
  • Via Traiana - an ancient Roman road that connects Benevento and Brindisi that is great for hiking or biking.

Best Things to Do in Molfetta: Activities and Tours

  • Boat Tours - explore Molfetta’s surrounding islands by taking a boat tour.
  • Wine Tours - visit the nearby vineyards of Apulia and taste some of Italy’s finest wines.
  • Cooking Classes - learn the traditional ways of preparing local Italian cuisine with a cooking class.

Week-Long Itinerary for Molfetta

Here is a week-long itinerary to help plan your trip to Molfetta:

Day Activity
1 Explore the historic center of town and visit Molfetta Cathedral and Piazza Municipio
2 Spend the day at the beach, Cala Corvino or Piazza San Domenico are excellent options.
3 Enjoy outdoor activities, take the Monte di Mola trail and end the day with sunset views.
4 Take a boat tour and explore the nearby Tremiti Islands.
5 Head to the vineyards of Apulia for a wine tour.
6 Take a cooking class and learn to make traditional Italian dishes.
7 Enjoy a relaxing day soaking up the atmosphere of Piazza Roma, with local cafes, shops and markets.

Food and Drink Scene of Molfetta

Must-Try Dishes

  • Orecchiette con le Cime di Rapa - a traditional dish of homemade pasta with turnip greens, garlic, and anchovies.
  • Polpo alla Luciana - a classic octopus stew made with garlic, onions, tomatoes, and red pepper flakes.
  • Pane e Pomodoro - bread and tomato salad, a simple but delicious summer dish.

Local Beverages

  • Negroamaro - a popular red wine with notes of chocolate and spices from the Apulia region.
  • Puglia Vodka - a local alcoholic drink made with wheat, water, and honey.

Where to Stay in Molfetta: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Hotel Molo 44 - a luxurious hotel that offers stunning view of the Adriatic Sea.
  • B&B Terrazze Sul Mare - a charming B&B that offers scenic views of the sea and easy access to local beaches.
  • La Finestra Sul Porto - a cozy guesthouse located in the heart of the old town.

What to Buy in Molfetta: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Handmade Ceramics - Molfetta is renowned for its traditional pottery. You can buy beautiful handmade ceramic plates, cups, and bowls from local artisans.
  • Olive Oil - Nearby Bari is famous for its extra-virgin olive oil, which is considered the best in Italy.
  • Wine - The vineyards of Apulia produce some of Italy’s best wines, which are perfect gifts to bring home.

Local Culture and Customs of Molfetta

The people of Molfetta are friendly and welcoming. Observing local customs and etiquette can help make your visit more enjoyable. Here are some tips:

  • Dress respectfully, especially when visiting churches or religious sites.
  • Learn some basic Italian phrases, like “Ciao” (hello) and “Grazie” (thank you).
  • Tipping is not mandatory but is appreciated for exceptional service.

Best Time to Visit Molfetta

Molfetta is a year-round destination, but the best time to visit is during the summer months, from June to August, where the weather is warm and the sea is perfect for swimming.

Do’s and Don’ts of Molfetta

Do:

  • Do taste traditional Italian cuisine and have a gelato.
  • Buy locally made souvenirs and wine to take home with you.
  • Do respect the culture and customs of the locals.

Don’t:

  • Don’t litter or damage any of the ancient ruins or historical sites.
  • Don’t take pictures of people without their express permission.
  • Don’t talk too loudly in public places, especially at night.

Safety and Health

Safety in Molfetta

Molfetta is a relatively safe town with a low crime rate. However, petty theft like pickpocketing can occur in crowded areas. It is recommended to keep your belongings safely and avoid carrying too much cash.

Health in Molfetta

Molfetta has excellent medical facilities and hospitals that offer high-quality healthcare services. The tap water is safe to drink, and there are no major health concerns for tourists.

Getting to Molfetta: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Molfetta

The nearest international airport to Molfetta is Bari-Karol Wojtyła Airport. From there, you can hire a taxi or a private car to reach Molfetta, which is approximately 30-minutes by car.

Bus Stations Near Molfetta

Molfetta has bus stations with connections to major cities in Italy, including Bari, Brindisi, and Lecce.

Other Options

Renting a car is a popular and convenient way to reach Molfetta and explore the surrounding area at your own pace.

How to Get Around in Molfetta

Molfetta is a small town, and most of the attractions and sites can be easily explored on foot. You can also rent a bike to explore the area or take a local bus to reach farther distances.

Where to Go Next from Molfetta: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Bari - a lively coastal city that is famous for its historic center, authentic local food, and beautiful beaches.
  • Polignano a Mare - a stunning seaside town, nestled on a rocky cliff that offers natural beauty and cultural heritage.
  • Alberobello - a UNESCO World Heritage site known for its traditional Italian trulli houses.

Conclusion

Molfetta is a charming and beautiful town with a rich cultural heritage, stunning beaches, and a warm and friendly atmosphere. This itinerary and guide will help you make the most of your visit and discover the hidden gems of southern Italy. Enjoy your travels!
