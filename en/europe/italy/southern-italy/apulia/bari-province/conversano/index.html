Introduction to Conversano

Conversano is a historic town located in the province of Bari, in the Apulia region of Southern Italy. It is famous for its medieval castle, stunning architecture, and beautiful countryside. Whether you are a history lover, a foodie, or an outdoor adventurer, Conversano has something for everyone. In this ultimate travel guide, we will explore the best places to visit, food to try, and where to stay in Conversano, along with other useful information to help you plan your trip.

List of Places to Visit in Conversano

Conversano is known for its rich cultural heritage, stunning architecture, and natural beauty. Here are some of the best places to visit in Conversano:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Castle of Conversano

The Castle of Conversano is the most famous landmark of the city and one of the best-preserved medieval castles in Italy. The castle was built in the 11th century and has been owned by several nobles over the centuries. Today, it is a popular tourist attraction and hosts various cultural events throughout the year.

Monastery of San Benedetto

The Monastery of San Benedetto is a 12th-century Benedictine monastery located in the heart of Conversano. The monastery is known for its stunning architecture, beautiful frescoes, and ancient manuscripts. Visitors can take guided tours of the monastery and learn more about its rich history and cultural significance.

Cathedral of Conversano

The Cathedral of Conversano is a beautiful 11th-century cathedral located in the town center. The cathedral is known for its stunning architecture, beautiful frescoes, and ancient relics. Visitors can take guided tours of the cathedral and attend daily masses.

Natural Attractions of Conversano: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Cala Casamatta

Cala Casamatta is a beautiful beach located just outside of Conversano. The beach is known for its crystal-clear waters, soft sandy beach, and stunning cliffs. Visitors can enjoy swimming, sunbathing, and picnicking at this beautiful natural attraction.

Gravina di Conversano

Gravina di Conversano is a beautiful natural gorge located just outside of the town. The gorge is known for its stunning views, ancient rock formations, and unique flora and fauna. Visitors can take guided tours of the gorge and explore its natural beauty.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Conversano

Conversano is surrounded by beautiful countryside, making it the perfect destination for hiking and biking enthusiasts. Here are some of the best hiking and biking routes in Conversano:

Hiking Routes

Conversano to Polignano a Mare

This scenic hike takes visitors on a beautiful journey through the countryside, passing through olive groves, vineyards, and ancient ruins. The hike culminates at the beautiful seaside town of Polignano a Mare, where visitors can enjoy swimming, sunbathing, and dining at local seafood restaurants.

Guided Hiking Tours

Several guided hiking tours are available in Conversano, taking visitors on beautiful journeys through the country, discovering the unique flora and fauna of the region.

Biking Routes

Conversano to Castellana Grotte

This beautiful bike route takes visitors through the lush countryside, passing through olive groves, vineyards, and ancient ruins. The route culminates at the beautiful town of Castellana Grotte, where visitors can explore its underground caves and enjoy local cuisine.

Guided Biking Tours

Several guided bike tours are available in Conversano, taking visitors on beautiful journeys through the countryside, discovering the unique flora and fauna of the region.

Best Things to Do in Conversano: Activities and Tours

There are plenty of fun and interesting things to do in Conversano. Here are some of the best activities and tours to try:

Cooking Classes

Conversano is famous for its rich culinary heritage, and visitors can experience this first-hand by taking a cooking class. Learn how to make local dishes like orecchiette pasta, focaccia, and ricotta cheese.

Wine Tasting Tours

Apulia is famous for its delicious wines, and visitors can take wine tours to discover the region’s wineries and taste the best wines in the area.

Guided Walking Tours

Discover the rich cultural heritage and stunning architecture of Conversano by taking a guided walking tour. Visitors can explore the town’s medieval streets, historic buildings, and beautiful churches.

Week-Long Itinerary for Conversano

Conversano is a small town, but there are plenty of things to see and do in the surrounding area. Here is a suggested week-long itinerary for visitors to fully explore the region:

Day 1: Explore Conversano

Start your trip by exploring the town of Conversano. Visit the castle, monastery, and cathedral and take a guided walking tour of the town’s historic streets and beautiful architecture.

Day 2: Visit Polignano a Mare

Take a scenic hike from Conversano to Polignano a Mare and enjoy a day at the beach, swimming, sunbathing, and dining at local seafood restaurants.

Day 3: Explore Alberobello

Take a day trip to the beautiful town of Alberobello and explore its unique trulli houses, rich history, and local cuisine.

Day 4: Visit Bari

Visit the nearby city of Bari and explore its historic Old Town, stunning architecture, and delicious street food.

Day 5: Discover Castellana Grotte

Visit the underground caves of Castellana Grotte and learn about their unique geological formations and history.

Day 6: Experience Local Culture

Take a cooking class, wine tour, or guided hiking or biking tour to fully experience the cultural highlights of the region.

Day 7: Relax and Reflect

Take a day to relax and reflect on your trip, soaking in the stunning natural beauty and rich culture of Conversano and the surrounding area.

Food and Drink Scene of Conversano

Conversano is famous for its rich culinary heritage, delicious wines, and refreshing local beverages. Here are some of the must-try dishes and local beverages to try during your stay:

Must-Try Dishes

Orecchiette Pasta

Orecchiette pasta is a local specialty in Conversano. This unique pasta is shaped like little ears and is traditionally served with a tomato-based sauce and local cheeses.

Burrata Cheese

Burrata cheese is a soft, creamy cheese made from cow or buffalo milk. It is a local specialty in Apulia and is commonly served as an appetizer or dessert.

Focaccia

Focaccia is a type of bread that is famous in Apulia. It is soft, light and fluffy with a crispy exterior, and is typically flavored with olive oil, rosemary, or other local herbs.

Local Beverages

Primitivo Wine

Primitivo is a red wine grape that is native to the region of Puglia. It produces a fruity, full-bodied wine that pairs well with local dishes like orecchiette pasta and burrata cheese.

Negramaro Wine

Negramaro is another red wine grape that is native to the region of Puglia. It produces a dark, rich wine with notes of blackberries, cherries, and spice.

Limoncello

Limoncello is a refreshing local liqueur made from lemons, sugar, and alcohol. It is commonly served as a digestif after meals.

What to Buy in Conversano: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Conversano is famous for its local products and handmade souvenirs. Here are some of the best souvenirs and local products to bring home:

Handmade Ceramics

Conversano has a rich tradition of handmade ceramics. Visitors can find beautiful plates, bowls, and other decorative items made by local artisans.

Olive Oil

Apulia is known for its high-quality olive oil, and visitors can purchase bottles of this delicious oil to take home.

Taralli

Taralli is a popular snack in Conversano and the surrounding region. These small, crunchy biscuits are perfect for snacking on and make a great souvenir to bring home.

Local Culture and Customs of Conversano

Conversano has a rich cultural heritage, and visitors can experience this firsthand by attending local festivals and events. The most famous festivals in Conversano include the Carnival, held in February, and the Santa Maria della Vittoria, held in August.

Best Time to Visit Conversano

The best time to visit Conversano is during the spring and fall months, from March to May and from September to November. During these periods, the weather is mild, and the crowds are smaller, making it easier to explore the town’s historic streets and natural attractions.

Do’s and Don’ts of Conversano

Do:

  • Respect local traditions and customs.
  • Dress modestly when visiting religious sites.
  • Try the local cuisine and wines.

Don’t:

  • Hike or bike in the countryside alone.
  • Visit religious sites during mass.
  • Leave valuables unattended the streets.

Safety and Health

Safety in Conversano

Conversano is a safe town with a low crime rate. However, visitors should exercise caution when hiking or biking in the countryside and avoid walking alone at night.

Health in Conversano

Conversano has good healthcare facilities, and visitors can access medical care at local clinics and hospitals if needed.

Getting to Conversano: Transportation Options and Logistics

Conversano is easily accessible by train, bus, or car. Here are some transportation options to consider:

Airports Near Conversano

Bari Palese Airport is the nearest airport to Conversano, and it is approximately 45 kilometers away.

Bus Stations Near Conversano

Conversano Bus Station

Conversano Bus Station is located in the town center and offers bus services to nearby towns and cities.

Other Options

Visitors can also reach Conversano by car or taxi.

How to Get Around in Conversano

Conversano is a small town, and visitors can easily explore its historic streets and natural attractions on foot. However, visitors who wish to venture out to the surrounding countryside can rent a car or bike, or take guided tours.

Where to Go Next from Conversano: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Conversano is conveniently located near several other beautiful towns and cities in Apulia. Here are some nearby destinations and excursions to consider:

Alberobello

Alberobello is a beautiful town located approximately 25 kilometers from Conversano, famous for its unique trulli houses and local cuisine.

Bari

Bari is the capital city of the Apulia region and is located approximately 30 kilometers from Conversano. It is known for its stunning architecture, historic Old Town, and delicious street food.

Lecce

Lecce is a beautiful city located approximately 120 kilometers from Conversano. It is known for its stunning Baroque architecture, lively nightlife, and delicious local cuisine.

From its rich cultural heritage to its stunning natural beauty, this small town has something for everyone.
