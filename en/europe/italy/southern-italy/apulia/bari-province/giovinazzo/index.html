Introduction to Giovinazzo

Located in the Bari province of Apulia, in Southern Italy, Giovinazzo is a charming town that boasts a rich cultural and historical heritage. The town is perched atop a rocky promontory that juts out into the Adriatic Sea, and it offers breathtaking views of the sparkling waters and the surrounding countryside. With its labyrinthine streets, striking architecture, and vibrant local culture, Giovinazzo is a must-visit destination for anyone who loves exploring off-the-beaten-path destinations in Italy.

List of Places to Visit in Giovinazzo

When it comes to things to see and do in Giovinazzo, there’s no shortage of options. Here’s a list of the top attractions and experiences in Giovinazzo:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  1. Basilica Cattedrale Maria Santissima Assunta: This stunning cathedral was built in the Romanesque style and boasts an impressive bell tower. Inside, visitors can admire the intricate frescoes that adorn the walls and ceilings.
  2. Museum of Giovinazzo: This small but fascinating museum houses a collection of archaeological finds and artwork from the region, including a series of Roman mosaics that depict scenes from everyday life.
  3. Porta Vecchia: This arched gateway dates back to the 16th century and is one of the few remaining examples of Giovinazzo’s original city walls. Visitors can walk through the gate and explore the historic old town beyond.

Natural Attractions of Giovinazzo: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  1. Giovinazzo Beach: With its crystal-clear waters and stunning beachfront, Giovinazzo Beach is the perfect spot for a day of sun, swimming, and relaxation.
  2. Grotta San Michele: This natural cave is located just outside Giovinazzo and can be reached via a scenic walking trail. Inside, visitors can admire the stalactites and stalagmites that have formed over millions of years.
  3. Monte San Michele: This hilltop offers spectacular views of the town, the coastline, and the surrounding countryside. Visitors can hike to the top or take a scenic drive up the winding road.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Giovinazzo

Giovinazzo and the surrounding region offer numerous hiking and biking trails that allow visitors to explore the scenic countryside. Here are a few of the best routes:

  1. Coastal Trail: This trail follows the coastline from Giovinazzo to the neighboring town of Molfetta and offers stunning views of the sea along the way.
  2. Sentiero Naturale di Monte San Michele: This hiking trail leads visitors up to the top of Monte San Michele and offers breathtaking views of the town and the surrounding countryside.
  3. Ciclovia dell’Acquedotto: This bike path follows the route of an ancient aqueduct and winds its way through olive groves, vineyards, and charming rural villages.

Best Things to Do in Giovinazzo: Activities and Tours

Giovinazzo offers a range of activities and tours that cater to every type of traveler. Here are some of the best things to do in Giovinazzo:

  1. Cooking Classes: Learn how to make traditional Apulian dishes like orecchiette pasta, focaccia bread, and fresh seafood dishes.
  2. Wine Tasting: Sample the region’s famous wines, including Primitivo, Negroamaro, and Salice Salento, at local wineries.
  3. Boat Tours: Take a leisurely boat tour of the coastline and discover hidden coves and secluded beaches that are only accessible by water.

Food and Drink Scene of Giovinazzo

No trip to Giovinazzo would be complete without sampling the local cuisine and wine. Here are a few must-try dishes and beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  1. Orecchiette alle cime di rapa: This pasta dish is made with orecchiette (ear-shaped pasta) and topped with a sauce made from broccoli rabe, garlic, chili flakes, and olive oil.
  2. Tiella di patate, riso, e cozze: This hearty dish is made with potatoes, rice, and mussels and is baked in the oven until crispy and golden.
  3. Burrata: This creamy cheese is made from mozzarella and cream and is a delicious accompaniment to fresh bread and tomatoes.

Local Beverages

  1. Primitivo: This full-bodied red wine is made from the Primitivo grape and is known for its bold flavors and spicy notes.
  2. Fiano: This crisp white wine is made from the Fiano grape and pairs well with seafood dishes.
  3. Limoncello: This sweet lemon liqueur is the perfect after-dinner drink and is traditionally served cold.

Where to Stay in Giovinazzo: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Giovinazzo offers a range of accommodations to suit every budget and travel style. Here are a few options:

  1. Relais del Senatore: This elegant hotel is located in the heart of the old town and features stylish rooms and a well-equipped spa.
  2. B&B La Finestra sul Castello: This charming guesthouse is located near the castle and offers cozy, comfortable rooms and a homey atmosphere.
  3. Villa Serra: This budget-friendly hotel is located outside the town center and features a large swimming pool, a restaurant, and spacious rooms.

What to Buy in Giovinazzo: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

When it comes to souvenirs and local products, Giovinazzo has plenty to offer. Here are a few ideas:

  1. Olive Oil: Apulia is famous for its high-quality olive oil, and you can find a range of varieties for sale in Giovinazzo.
  2. Ceramics: The region is also known for its artisanal ceramic products, including colorful plates, bowls, and vases, which make great souvenirs or gifts.
  3. Jewelry: Giovinazzo is home to several local jewelers who create unique pieces using traditional techniques and materials.

Local Culture and Customs of Giovinazzo

Visitors to Giovinazzo should be aware of a few local customs and etiquette considerations. Here are a few things to keep in mind:

  1. Dress modestly when visiting churches and other religious sites.
  2. Italians often greet each other with a kiss on the cheeks, so don’t be alarmed if someone greets you this way.
  3. Tipping is not expected in Italy, but it is appreciated if you receive exceptional service.

Best Time to Visit Giovinazzo

The best time to visit Giovinazzo is during the spring (March to May) or fall (September to November), when the weather is mild and the crowds are smaller. During the summer months (June to August), the town can get crowded, and temperatures can soar.

Do’s and Don’ts of Giovinazzo

Here are a few do’s and don’ts to keep in mind during your visit to Giovinazzo:

Do:

  1. Learn a few key Italian phrases before your trip.
  2. Try the local cuisine and wine.
  3. Take a leisurely stroll along the waterfront.

Don’t:

  1. Drink the tap water - opt for bottled water instead.
  2. Wear revealing clothing when visiting religious sites.
  3. Forget to validate your train ticket before boarding.

Safety and Health

Giovinazzo is a safe place to visit, with a low crime rate and excellent health care facilities. Here are a few things to keep in mind when it comes to safety and health:

Safety in Giovinazzo

  1. Be aware of pickpockets in crowded areas.
  2. Use caution when crossing the streets, as traffic can be heavy.
  3. Beware of jellyfish in the water during the summer months.

Health in Giovinazzo

  1. Italy has a national health care system that provides affordable care to all residents and visitors.
  2. Be sure to bring any necessary prescription medications with you.
  3. Drink plenty of water and use sun protection when spending time outdoors.

How to Get Around in Giovinazzo

Giovinazzo is a small town that is easy to navigate on foot. However, if you want to explore the surrounding countryside or nearby towns, renting a bike or car is a good option.

Where to Go Next from Giovinazzo: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Giovinazzo is located in close proximity to a range of other charming Italian towns and destinations, making it the perfect base for day trips and excursions. Here are a few nearby places to visit:

Conclusion

Giovinazzo is a hidden gem in Southern Italy that offers visitors a rich cultural and historical experience.
