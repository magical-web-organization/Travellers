Introduction to Alberobello

If you’re looking for a unique travel experience in Italy, Alberobello is a destination you won’t want to miss. This small town in the Apulia region of Southern Italy is famous for its trulli, traditional dry stone huts with conical roofs that the area is known for.

Alberobello’s trulli have earned it a spot on the UNESCO World Heritage list, and visitors from around the world come to see them. But there’s more to this town than just its iconic architecture. From hiking to authentic Italian cuisine, there are plenty of reasons to visit Alberobello.

List of Places to Visit in Alberobello

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Trullo Sovrano: The largest and tallest trullo in Alberobello, this museum houses exhibits on the history and culture of the town and the trulli.
  • Museum of Rural Life: Learn about the traditional way of life in Apulia with displays of farm tools, furniture, and clothing from the past.
  • Basilica Santuario dei Santi Medici: A beautiful church with impressive frescoes and religious artifacts.

Natural Attractions of Alberobello: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Parco dei Megaliti: This park has prehistoric megaliths, created by the Peucetian people around 3000 BC.
  • San Michele in Monte: A hike up to the highest point in Alberobello rewards you with panoramic views of the town and surrounding region.
  • Grotte di Castellana: Visit these stunning limestone caves, with guided tours available.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Alberobello

Alberobello and the surrounding area have many beautiful hiking and biking trails with stunning views of the trulli and countryside. Here are a few options:

  • Trulli Trail: A moderate hike of around 8 kilometers, starting in Alberobello and passing through the scenic countryside.
  • Bike & Boat Tour: Combine biking and boating on this guided tour, which takes you through the countryside before ending with a boat ride.
  • Orecchie di Elefante Trail: This challenging hike is a 15-kilometer loop through the countryside with panoramic views.

Best Things to Do in Alberobello: Activities and Tours

  • Cooking Class: Learn how to make traditional Apulian dishes with a cooking class led by a local chef.
  • Wine Tasting Tour: Visit local wineries to sample some of the finest wines and olive oils in the region.
  • Folklore Show: Watch a traditional music and dance performance with local musicians and dancers in traditional dress.

Week-Long Itinerary for Alberobello

Day 1: Arrival and exploration of Alberobello, and a visit to Trullo Sovrano.

Day 2: Visit the Museum of Rural Life and hike the Trulli Trail.

Day 3: Take a bike and boat tour through the countryside.

Day 4: Visit the San Michele in Monte and explore the old town.

Day 5: Take a day trip to nearby Ostuni, known as the “White City” for its whitewashed buildings.

Day 6: Visit the Grotte di Castellana in the morning and attend a folklore show in the evening.

Day 7: Take a wine tasting tour and cooking class, ending your week with a taste of the local cuisine.

Food and Drink Scene of Alberobello

Must-Try Dishes

  • Orecchiette: A traditional pasta dish with a distinctive ear shape, often served with a tomato and meat sauce.
  • Pasticciotto: A sweet pastry filled with custard, typical of the Salento region of Apulia.
  • Burrata: A creamy cheese similar to mozzarella but with a softer texture and richer flavor.

Local Beverages

  • Negroamaro Red Wine: A bold and full-bodied red wine, often made with local grapes.
  • Primitivo Red Wine: A fruity and aromatic red wine, also made with local grapes.
  • Locally Brewed Craft Beer: A growing trend in Apulia with microbreweries popping up all over the region.

Where to Stay in Alberobello: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Trulli Holiday: Stay in a traditional trullo, with options for larger family groups or a romantic retreat for two.
  • Grand Hotel La Chiusa di Chietri: A luxurious resort with a pool and spa, set in beautiful gardens.
  • Mare Verde Bed & Breakfast: A cozy and affordable option, with friendly hosts and a central location.

What to Buy in Alberobello: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Ceramics: Local craftsmen create beautiful hand-painted ceramics, often with intricate designs and vibrant colors.
  • Olive Oil: Apulia is famous for its high-quality olive oil, often sold in decorative bottles as gifts.
  • Liquors: Try the locally made limoncello or other fruit-based liquors, often sold in small bottles as souvenirs.

Local Culture and Customs of Alberobello

  • Respect for the Trulli: Many of the trulli in Alberobello are still lived in, so it’s important to be respectful of the locals and their homes.
  • Siesta Time: Like much of Italy, Alberobello has a siesta tradition where many businesses close in the early afternoon for a few hours.
  • Catholic Influence: The region has a strong Catholic heritage, so be respectful of religious customs and traditions.

Best Time to Visit Alberobello

The best time to visit Alberobello is in the shoulder seasons of April to June and September to October. The weather is pleasant and there are fewer crowds. July and August can be hot and crowded, but offer many festivals and events.

Do’s and Don’ts of Alberobello

Do:

  • Try the local cuisine, especially the traditional pastas and cheeses.
  • Explore the countryside and enjoy the natural beauty of the region.
  • Learn about the history and culture of the trulli and the people of Apulia.

Don’t:

  • Be disrespectful of the locals and their trulli.
  • Visit during peak tourist season if crowds and hot weather are not your thing.
  • Forget to pack sunscreen and a hat for the hot summer weather.

Safety and Health

Safety in Alberobello

Alberobello is generally a safe place to visit, but it’s always good to take precautions like not leaving valuables unattended and being aware of your surroundings.

Health in Alberobello

Italian healthcare is generally of a high standard, but it’s important to have travel insurance in case of medical emergencies.

Getting to Alberobello: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Alberobello

The closest airports to Alberobello are Bari Karol Wojtyla (BRI) and Brindisi Casale (BDS), both around an hour’s drive away.

Bus Stations Near Alberobello

The town has a bus station with connections to surrounding towns and cities.

Other Options

The train station in Martina Franca, a nearby town, has connections to Bari and the rest of Italy.

How to Get Around in Alberobello

The town is small and walkable, but there are also bike rentals and local buses available.

Where to Go Next from Alberobello: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Locorotondo: A charming nearby town known for its white buildings and winding streets.
  • Matera: A fascinating city known for its cave dwellings, just over an hour’s drive away.
  • Polignano a Mare: A beautiful coastal town with stunning views and scenic beaches, around an hour away by car.

Conclusion

From its iconic trulli to its beautiful countryside and authentic Italian culture, Alberobello has something for everyone. Whether you’re looking for outdoor adventures, locally sourced cuisine, or a glimpse into traditional Italian life, Alberobello is a destination that won’t disappoint.
