Introduction to Benevento

Benevento is a beautiful city located in the Campania region of Southern Italy. The city has a rich history that dates back to the Romans, and it is a popular tourist destination for those who want to experience Italy’s ancient culture and natural beauty. Benevento is a great city for travelers looking for a quiet and authentic Italian experience.

List of Places to Visit in Benevento

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • The Arch of Trajan: Built in 114 AD, the Arch of Trajan is one of the most famous landmarks in Benevento. It is a historical monument meant to commemorate Emperor Trajan’s victory over the Dacians.
  • The Santa Sofia Church: This was a Lombard church built in the 8th century with many magnificent frescoes. It was later converted into a Benedictine monastery.
  • The Museo del Sannio: This museum holds historical artifacts and documents from ancient times, dating back to the Roman era, representing Benevento’s cultural heritage.

Natural Attractions of Benevento: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Mount Taburno: This is a well-known mountain located in Benevento. Visitors can spend an entire day hiking or biking up the mountain to enjoy the breathtaking views of the surrounding countryside.
  • The Telese Thermal Baths: The Telese Thermal Baths, which date back to Roman times, is known for its mineral-rich hot springs that are believed to have amazing healing properties.
  • The Matese Regional Park: The Matese Regional Park is a natural park that offers adventurous activities, including hiking, biking, and swimming in the crystal-clear lakes.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Benevento

Benevento offers several hiking and biking routes that are both challenging and breathtaking. Mount Taburno, the Apennine Range, and Matese Regional Park are some of the best places to hike and explore.

Best Things to Do in Benevento: Activities and Tours

  • Wine Tasting: Benevento is famous for its high-quality wines. Visitors can take a tour of the vineyards and enjoy some of the best wines Italy has to offer.
  • Cooking Classes: Visitors can learn how to prepare traditional Italian dishes by taking cooking classes with local chefs.
  • Guided Tours: Guided tours are available for those who want to learn more about the city’s history and culture.

Food and Drink Scene of Benevento

Must-Try Dishes

  • Sannio Soup: a hearty soup with meat and vegetables famous in the Sannio Region
  • Paccheri alla Beneventana: This is a signature dish in Benevento, made of homemade pasta, meat ragu, and Pecorino cheese.
  • Sfogliatelle: A baked pastry famous in Italy and a perfect breakfast choice.

Local Beverages

  • Local wines: Benevento is known for its quality wines, including Aglianico and Falanghina.
  • Limoncello: A refreshing, traditional Italian lemon liquor.

Where to Stay in Benevento: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Benevento has a variety of accommodations, including hotels, hostels, and guesthouses of different price ranges. For luxury stays, the Hotel Villa Traiano is an excellent option, while the La Piazzetta B&B is a comfortable accommodation for budget travelers.

What to Buy in Benevento: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Visitors can find many souvenirs and local products in Benevento, including handmade ceramics, wine, and traditional pasta.

Local Culture and Customs of Benevento

Benevento has preserved many of its ancient customs and traditions, such as the Feast of St. Bartholomew, which takes place every year in August, during which people enjoy music, food, and drink.

Best Time to Visit Benevento

The best time to visit Benevento is in the spring and fall. The temperature is mild, and there are fewer crowds. Also, some of the festivals, such as the Feast of St. Bartholomew, occur during this period.

Do’s and Don’ts of Benevento

Do:

  • Show respect toward locals at all times.
  • Dress modestly and appropriately, especially when visiting religious sites.
  • Enjoy Benevento’s cultural heritage and its delicious cuisine.

Don’t:

  • Litter or disrespect the city’s heritage sites.
  • Be disruptive or rowdy, especially in public areas.
  • Speak loudly when in religious sites.

Safety and Health

Safety in Benevento

Benevento is a relatively safe city, with a low crime rate, and travelers can feel safe while visiting here. As with any destination, it’s always wise to be aware of your surroundings, and take precautions like avoiding unlit areas when it’s dark outside.

Health in Benevento

Benevento has good medical facilities, and visitors who require medical attention can quickly get medical care. It’s advisable to have travel health insurance when visiting Benevento.

Getting to Benevento: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Benevento

The closest airport to Benevento is the Naples International Airport, which is approximately an hour’s drive away. Other nearby airports include Rome and Bari airports, both being around three hours from Benevento.

Bus Stations Near Benevento

The city is serviced by several buses, with a bus station in the city center, which connects Benevento to neighboring cities.

Other Options

Travelers may also choose to come by car. From Naples, the city is approximately an hour’s drive toward the east.

How to Get Around in Benevento

Benevento is a small city, and travelers can comfortably explore it on foot. Taxis and buses are other options available for getting around the city. Additionally, visitors can rent a car or bike to explore the surrounding countryside.

Where to Go Next from Benevento: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Benevento is situated in a great location for visiting other nearby cities, including Naples, Amalfi, Pompeii, and even further north to Rome and Florence.

Conclusion

Benevento is an excellent destination for travelers looking for a quiet and authentic Italian experience. The city boasts a rich cultural and historical heritage, with beautiful natural scenery that makes it an ideal getaway destination. From ancient monuments to modern-day wine tastings, Benevento has everything one could want in an Italian vacation.
