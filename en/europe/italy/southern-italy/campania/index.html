Introduction to Campania

Located in Southern Italy, Campania is a region that attracts travelers from all over the world. From the stunning Amalfi Coast to historical Naples, Campania offers a unique blend of history, natural beauty, and culinary delights.

The region is also home to three UNESCO World Heritage Sites: the archaeological ruins of Pompeii, Herculaneum, and Torre Annunziata; the Amalfi Coast; and the Royal Palace of Caserta.

List of Places to Visit in Campania

Campania has many popular tourist destinations that should not be missed when visiting the region. Here are some of the places you might want to include in your itinerary:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Museo Archeologico Nazionale di Napoli (National Archaeological Museum of Naples)
  • Castel dell’Ovo (Egg Castle)
  • Castel Nuovo (New Castle)
  • Capodimonte Museum
  • Paestum Archaeological Site

Natural Attractions of Campania: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Amalfi Coast
  • Sorrento Peninsula
  • Mount Vesuvius
  • The Phlegraean Fields
  • Cilento and Vallo di Diano National Park

Hiking/Biking Routes of Campania

Campania offers some incredible hiking and biking trails for adventure seekers. Here are a few:

  • Path of the Gods (Sentiero degli Dei)
  • Coastal Path (Sentiero Costiero)
  • The Path of the Forts (Sentiero dei Fortini)
  • Mount Vesuvius Trail (Sentiero del Vesuvio)
  • Valle delle Ferriere

Best Things to Do in Campania: Activities and Tours

If hiking and biking are not your thing, there are many other activities you can enjoy in Campania. Here are some popular tours and activities that you might find interesting:

  • Cooking classes
  • Wine tasting tours
  • Boat tours along the Amalfi Coast
  • Visiting lemon groves and Limoncello factories
  • Visiting the thermal baths

Week-Long Itinerary for Campania

If you’re planning to spend a week in Campania, here’s a sample itinerary to consider:

Day Activity
1 Explore Naples, visit the National Archaeological Museum
2 Visit Pompeii and Herculaneum from Naples
3 Take a day trip to Capri from Sorrento
4 Hike the Path of the Gods from Positano
5 Visit the Amalfi Coast towns of Amalfi and Ravello
6 Explore the Cilento National Park and Paestum Archaeological Site
7 Relax on the beaches of the Sorrento Peninsula

Food and Drink Scene of Campania

Italian cuisine is famous all over the world, and Campania is no exception. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Pizza (from Naples)
  • Spaghetti alle vongole (Spaghetti with clams)
  • Melanzane alla Parmigiana (Eggplant Parmigiana)
  • Mozzarella di Bufala (Buffalo Mozzarella)
  • Ragu alla napoletana (Neapolitan Ragù)

Local Beverages

  • Limoncello
  • Aglianico del Taburno
  • Fiano di Avellino
  • Taurasi
  • Greco di Tufo

Where to Stay in Campania: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

In Campania, you can choose from a wide range of accommodations, ranging from luxury hotels to budget-friendly hostels. Here are some of the best places to stay:

  • Grand Hotel Excelsior Vittoria (Sorrento)
  • Villa Franca (Positano)

What to Buy in Campania: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

If you’re interested in bringing back souvenirs or local products from your trip, here are a few things you might consider:

  • Ceramics and pottery from Vietri sul Mare
  • Limoncello and other liqueurs
  • Leather products from Naples
  • Traditional Neapolitan nativity scenes
  • Anchovies from Cetara

Local Culture and Customs of Campania

Campania has a rich cultural heritage that is celebrated throughout the year. Some important festivals to note include:

  • The Neapolitan Nativity scene (Il presepe napoletano)
  • Carnival of Venice (Carnevale di Venezia)
  • The Feast of San Gennaro (La festa di San Gennaro)
  • The Festival of Santa Lucia (Santa Lucia)

Best Time to Visit Campania

The best time to visit Campania is from April to June or September to October when the temperatures are comfortable, and the crowds are thinner than in peak season.

Do’s and Don’ts of Campania

Do:

  • Learn some basic Italian phrases
  • Try the local cuisine
  • Follow dress codes when visiting religious sites
  • Respect the local culture and customs
  • Use public transportation

Don’t:

  • Drink tap water
  • Ignore traffic laws
  • Touch historical monuments
  • Wear revealing clothes when visiting religious sites
  • Leave your belongings unattended

Safety and Health

Safety in Campania

Campania, like any other region in Italy, is generally safe for tourists. However, like in any other tourist destination, it’s always better to be cautious and aware of your surroundings.

Health in Campania

Before traveling to Campania, make sure to consult your doctor to get the necessary vaccinations and carry enough medication if you have any medical conditions. Also, make sure you have adequate health insurance that covers medical emergencies.

Getting to Campania: Transportation Options and Logistics

Campania is easily accessible by air, train, car, and ferry.

Airports Near Campania

  • Naples International Airport (NAP)
  • Salerno Costa d’Amalfi Airport (QSR)

Bus Stations Near Campania

  • Naples bus station
  • Salerno bus station

Other Options

  • Trains (Trenitalia)
  • Ferries (Alilauro, Caremar)

How to Get Around in Campania

In Campania, you can get around by using public transportation, renting a car, or taking taxis.

Public Transportation

Public transportation is an affordable way to get around Campania. The regional bus and train systems are well-connected and run frequently.

Renting a Car

Renting a car gives you the flexibility to explore Campania at your own pace. However, it can be challenging to navigate the narrow and winding roads in the region, especially in peak season.

Taxis

Taxis are a convenient option for shorter journeys, but they can be expensive. Make sure to negotiate the fare before getting into the taxi.

Where to Go Next from Campania: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Campania is an excellent base for exploring other parts of Italy. Here are some nearby destinations you might consider:

  • The Amalfi Coast
  • Naples
  • The island of Capri
  • Procida
  • Ischia

Conclusion

Campania is a diverse travel destination that offers something for everyone. Whether you’re interested in art and history, natural beauty, culinary delights, or outdoor adventures, Campania is sure to satisfy your travel cravings. Plan your itinerary wisely, and make the most of your time in this enchanting corner of Italy.
