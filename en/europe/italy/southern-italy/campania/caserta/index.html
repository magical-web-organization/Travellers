Introduction to Caserta

Located in the Campania region of southern Italy, the city of Caserta is home to some of Italy’s most intricate and beautiful architecture. Its most famous attraction is the Royal Palace of Caserta, a UNESCO World Heritage site that boasts 1,200 rooms spread out over five floors. Beyond the palace, Caserta has much to offer visitors, from its abundance of museums and historical sites to its stunning natural scenery.

Local Culture and Customs of Caserta

Like much of the south of Italy, the people of Caserta are passionate about food, family, and religious traditions. Visitors will often find themselves welcomed into family kitchens for home-cooked meals, and should take care to dress modestly when visiting churches.

Best Time to Visit Caserta

The best time to visit Caserta is during the spring or fall, when the weather is mild and the crowds are thinner. The summer months can get quite hot and crowded, and many businesses close for the month of August.

Do’s and Don’ts of Caserta

Do:

  • Dress modestly when visiting churches.
  • Tip baristas and waiters for good service.
  • Order the house wine with your meal.

Don’t:

  • Expect to find many English speakers.
  • Expect lunch to be served before 1 pm.
  • Order a cappuccino after noon.

Safety and Health

Safety in Caserta

Like much of Italy, Caserta is a safe city to visit. Visitors should take reasonable precautions against pickpocketing in crowded areas, but violent crime is rare.

Health in Caserta

Visitors should have up-to-date vaccinations and be prepared for the heat and sun exposure during the summer months. The tap water is safe to drink, but some visitors may experience mild digestive issues due to differences in bacteria and mineral content.

Getting to Caserta: Transportation Options and Logistics

Bus Stations Near Caserta

  • Caserta Bus Station
  • Naples Bus Station

Other Options

  • Train: Caserta has a train station with regular service from Rome and Naples.
  • Car: Caserta is easily accessible from the A1 motorway, which runs from Milan to Naples.

How to Get Around in Caserta

Caserta is a walkable city, and visitors can easily explore the city center on foot. Buses and taxis are available for travel further afield, and bicycles can be rented for exploring the surrounding countryside.

Where to Go Next from Caserta: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Naples: visit the birthplace of pizza and explore the archaeological sites of Pompeii and Herculaneum.
  • Sorrento: see the stunning views of the Amalfi Coast and enjoy the local lemons and limoncello.
  • Rome: visit the Colosseum, the Vatican, and the countless other sights of the eternal city.

Conclusion

From the Royal Palace of Caserta to the stunning natural beauty of the surrounding countryside, this city has much to offer visitors. Whether you’re looking to explore history, soak up culture, or just relax and enjoy the local food and wine, Caserta won’t disappoint.
