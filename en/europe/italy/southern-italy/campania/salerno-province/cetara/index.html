Are you looking for a hidden gem in Southern Italy to explore? Look no further than Cetara, located in the Salerno province of Campania. This small fishing village is nestled on the Amalfi Coast and boasts natural beauty, a rich culture, and incredible cuisine. In this ultimate travel guide to Cetara, we’ll showcase the best things to do, where to stay and eat, and provide a week-long itinerary to help you plan the perfect trip.

Introduction to Cetara

Cetara is a secret paradise in Italy that’s not overrun by tourists. There’s a population of only 2,000 residents, yet the town has all the charm of a big Italian city. The town’s historic center is compact and easy to explore on foot. Its narrow streets are lined with colorful buildings, gelato shops, and trattorias. Cetara has preserved its ancient fishing traditions and is considered one of the last fishing villages left on the Amalfi coast. Expect to see fishermen repairing their nets and tending to their boats, while their wives are busy making anchovy sauce, the town’s specialty.

What to Buy in Cetara: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

If you’re looking to bring home a taste of Cetara, make sure to grab some anchovy sauce (colatura di alici). There are several shops in town that sell this local specialty.

Local Culture and Customs of Cetara

When in Cetara, it’s important to respect the local culture and customs. The town is known for its fishing traditions, so it’s best to be mindful of that when interacting with locals. It’s also worth noting that the town is heavily influenced by the Catholic Church, so dress modestly when visiting religious sites.

Best Time to Visit Cetara

The best time to visit Cetara is during the shoulder season from April to June, or September to November. The weather is milder during these months, and there are fewer crowds.

Do’s and Don’ts of Cetara

Do:

  • Respect the local culture and customs.
  • Try the anchovy sauce, it’s the town’s specialty.
  • Take a fishing trip to get a real taste of the town’s fishing heritage.

Don’t:

  • Wear revealing clothing when visiting religious sites.
  • Be loud and disruptive in public places.
  • Leave trash on the beach or in public areas.

Safety and Health

Safety in Cetara

Cetara is generally a safe town, but it’s always a good idea to take general safety precautions. Keep an eye on your valuables and avoid walking alone at night.

Health in Cetara

The healthcare system in Italy is considered one of the best in the world. Visitors can seek medical attention at local clinics and hospitals, but it’s important to ensure that you have travel health insurance.

Getting to Cetara: Transportation Options and Logistics

Cetara is a small town, and getting there can be a bit challenging, but it’s worth the effort. Here are the best ways to get there:

Airports Near Cetara

The nearest airports to Cetara are Naples International Airport (NAP) or Rome Fiumicino Airport (FCO). From there, you will need to travel by train to Salerno, and then take a bus or taxi to Cetara.

Bus Stations Near Cetara

The local bus station in Cetara is located near Via Roma.

Other Options

You can also rent a car or hire a driver to take you to Cetara.

How to Get Around in Cetara

Cetara is a small town, and most places are easily accessible on foot. It’s also possible to rent a car or scooter to explore the surrounding areas.

Where to Go Next from Cetara: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Cetara is located on the Amalfi Coast, offering plenty of opportunities for nearby destinations and excursions. Some options include:

  • Amalfi
  • Ravello
  • Positano

Conclusion

Cetara is a small town with a big heart that’s sure to win you over. With its rich culture, incredible food, and natural beauty, it’s definitely worth adding to your Italian itinerary. Whether you’re interested in outdoor adventures or simply soaking up the laid-back Italian lifestyle, Cetara has something for everyone.
