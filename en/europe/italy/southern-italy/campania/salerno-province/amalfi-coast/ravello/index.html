Introduction to Ravello

Nestled in the hills overlooking the Amalfi Coast, Ravello is a charming destination that offers a respite from the tourist crowds that flock to the beaches and towns below. With stunning views, rich history, and a thriving arts scene, there’s plenty to see and do in this hidden gem of Italy.

List of Places to Visit in Ravello

Whether you’re interested in history, nature, or outdoor adventures, Ravello has something for everyone. Here are some of the top places to visit in Ravello.

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Villa Rufolo: This medieval villa boasts a stunning garden and unparalleled views of the coast. It was originally built for the wealthy Rufolo family and later purchased by composer Richard Wagner, who found inspiration for his works here.
  • Cathedral of Ravello: This 11th-century cathedral features beautiful mosaic designs and is a must-visit for history buffs.
  • Paper Mill Museum: Learn more about Ravello’s paper-making history at this fascinating museum housed in an old paper mill.

Natural Attractions of Ravello: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Valle delle Ferriere: This nature reserve is a great spot for hiking and offers breathtaking views of waterfalls and lush greenery.
  • Emerald Grotto: Take a boat tour or kayak through this stunning sea cave and see the natural rock formations and turquoise waters.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Ravello

Ravello is a great destination for outdoor enthusiasts, with plenty of hiking and biking routes to explore. Here are some of the top options:

  • Sentiero degli Dei: This popular hiking trail, known as the “Path of the Gods,” offers breathtaking views of the coast and takes you through charming villages along the way.
  • Monte Cerreto: This mountain is a popular spot for hiking and biking, and offers stunning views of the coast and surrounding countryside.

Best Things to Do in Ravello: Activities and Tours

From cooking classes to art exhibits, there’s plenty to do in Ravello. Here are some of the best activities and tours to check out:

  • Cooking classes: Learn the secrets of Italian cuisine and make your own pasta and pizza with a local chef.
  • Art exhibits: The town boasts a number of galleries and museums showcasing the work of local artists, including Villa Cimbrone and Villa Rufolo.
  • Wine tours: Visit local vineyards and learn about the winemaking process while enjoying some of the region’s best wines.

Week-Long Itinerary for Ravello

To make the most of your time in Ravello, we suggest the following week-long itinerary:

Day Activity
1 Explore the town center, including the Cathedral and Villa Rufolo
2 Take a hike on Monte Cerreto or Sentiero degli Dei
3 Visit the Paper Mill Museum and take a cooking class
4 Take a boat tour of the Emerald Grotto and enjoy a seafood lunch
5 Visit local vineyards for a wine tasting
6 Spend the day at the beach in nearby Maiori
7 Enjoy a day of relaxation and spa treatments at a local wellness center

Food and Drink Scene of Ravello

Italy’s culinary capital, Ravello boasts a vibrant dining scene that showcases the region’s fresh seafood and locally sourced ingredients. Here are some must-try dishes and beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Spaghetti alle Vongole: This classic pasta dish features clams cooked in white wine and garlic.
  • Bruschetta: Toasted bread topped with fresh tomatoes, basil, and olive oil.
  • Pizza Margherita: A classic Neapolitan pizza made with fresh tomato sauce, mozzarella cheese, and basil.

Local Beverages

  • Limoncello: A sweet and tangy lemon liqueur that is a specialty of the Amalfi Coast.
  • Red wine: The region is known for its bold and fruity red wines, including Aglianico and Taurasi.
  • Espresso: The Italian coffee culture is legendary, and Ravello is no exception.

Where to Stay in Ravello: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Ravello offers a variety of accommodation options to suit any budget and preference, from luxury hotels to cozy bed and breakfasts. Here are some top options to consider:

  • Belmond Hotel Caruso: This luxurious 11th-century palace offers stunning sea views and elegant accommodations.
  • Hotel Villa Fraulo: Housed in a renovated 13th-century building, this hotel features a rooftop terrace with panoramic vistas.
  • Villa Piedimonte: This boutique hotel offers cozy rooms and a lush garden with a pool for a relaxed stay.

What to Buy in Ravello: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Ravello is known for its paper-making history, and visitors can find a variety of paper products and stationary to take home as a souvenir. Other popular items include limoncello, handmade ceramics, and local olive oil.

Local Culture and Customs of Ravello

As with much of Italy, family and community play a central role in Ravello’s culture. Visitors should be aware of the siesta hours, during which many shops and restaurants close in the afternoon, and dress modestly when visiting religious sites.

Best Time to Visit Ravello

The best time to visit Ravello is in the shoulder seasons of spring and fall, when temperatures are mild and crowds are thinner. Summer can be hot and crowded, while winter brings cooler temperatures and rain.

Do’s and Don’ts of Ravello

Do:

  • Respect religious sites and traditions.
  • Try local cuisine and explore the region’s wine culture.
  • Take time to relax and enjoy the slower pace of life.

Don’t:

  • Wear revealing clothing when visiting churches or other religious sites.
  • Expect late-night party scenes – Ravello is a quieter destination.

Safety and Health

Safety in Ravello

Ravello is a safe destination for travelers, but visitors should be aware of pickpockets in crowded areas and take standard precautions when exploring the town and surrounding areas.

Health in Ravello

Italy has a high standard of healthcare, and visitors can expect quality medical care in case of emergency. It’s always recommended to have travel insurance before traveling.

Getting to Ravello: Transportation Options and Logistics

Ravello is easily accessible by several modes of transportation, making it an ideal destination for a day trip or a longer stay.

Airports Near Ravello

The closest airport to Ravello is Naples International Airport, which is about an hour’s drive away. From there, visitors can take a taxi, shuttle, or rental car to Ravello.

Bus Stations Near Ravello

Ravello is connected by bus to other destinations along the Amalfi Coast, including Amalfi, Positano, and Salerno.

Other Options

Visitors can also take a train or ferry to Salerno and then transfer to a bus or taxi to reach Ravello.

How to Get Around in Ravello

Ravello is a small town that can easily be explored on foot, though visitors should be prepared for some hills and steps. Taxis are also available for those who prefer to travel by car.

Where to Go Next from Ravello: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Ravello’s location on the Amalfi Coast makes it an ideal base for exploring other destinations in the region. Some nearby places to consider:

  • Amalfi: This charming coastal town is just a short bus ride away and offers stunning beaches and historic sites.
  • Capri: Take a boat tour to this idyllic island known for its dramatic coastlines, upscale shopping, and celebrity sightings.
  • Naples: Italy’s third-largest city is just an hour away by car or train and boasts some of the country’s best museums, pizzerias, and historic sites.

Conclusion

With its breathtaking views, rich history, and vibrant culture, Ravello is an ideal destination for anyone looking to explore the best of the Amalfi Coast and beyond. Whether you’re interested in outdoor adventures, art and culture, or simply relaxing and taking in the scenery, there’s something for everyone in this hidden gem of Southern Italy.
