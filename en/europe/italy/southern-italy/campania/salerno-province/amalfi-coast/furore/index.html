Your Ultimate Travel Guide to Furore, Italy

Nestled in the Amalfi Coast of southern Italy, Furore is a hidden gem waiting to be explored. Its stunning beauty, rich cultural heritage, and vibrant local scene make it an ideal holiday destination. Whether you’re looking for an outdoor adventure, a cultural immersion, or simply a relaxing escape, Furore has something for everyone. Use this ultimate travel guide to plan your next trip and discover the best things to do, see, and eat in Furore.

Introduction to Furore

Furore is a town in the Salerno province of Campania, Italy. It’s located in the heart of the Amalfi Coast, a UNESCO World Heritage Site renowned for its breathtaking beauty and historic charm. Furore is a small and charming town, with a population of just over 800 people. Its narrow streets and colorful houses are perched on the cliffs overlooking the sea, making for some spectacular views.

Furore is a place where time seems to stand still. The town has managed to preserve its authentic character, despite the influx of tourists in recent years. It’s a perfect destination for nature lovers, history buffs, and foodies alike.

List of Places to Visit in Furore

Furore has plenty of things to see and do, from culturally-important attractions to natural wonders and scenic routes. Here’s a list of must-visit places in Furore:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  1. The Church of Sant’Elia: This charming small church dates back to the 12th century and boasts stunning mosaics and frescoes.

  2. The Emerald Grotto: A natural wonder located in a small bay near Furore, the Emerald Grotto is a cave filled with crystal-clear, emerald-green waters. It’s believed to have been used as a place of worship by ancient Romans.

  3. The Amalfi Cathedral: Located in the nearby town of Amalfi, this magnificent cathedral dates back to the 13th century and boasts a stunning blend of Romanesque and Byzantine architecture.

Natural Attractions of Furore: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  1. The Path of the Gods: A scenic hiking trail that offers stunning views of the Amalfi Coast, the Path of the Gods is a must-visit for outdoor enthusiasts. The trail runs from Bomerano to Nocelle, passing through scenic valleys and rocky hillsides along the way.

  2. The Fiordo di Furore: A hidden gem on the Amalfi Coast, the Fiordo di Furore is a natural fjord that’s over 30 meters deep. It’s surrounded by cliffs and offers some of the most breathtaking views in the area.

  3. The Monti Lattari Regional Park: A nature reserve that covers over 9000 hectares of land, the Monti Lattari Regional Park is a paradise for hikers and nature lovers. It offers stunning views of the Amalfi Coast and is home to a wide range of flora and fauna.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Furore

Furore is a great destination for hiking and biking enthusiasts. The town is surrounded by scenic hills and valleys, offering a variety of trails to choose from. Here are some of the best routes to explore:

  1. The Path of the Gods: A scenic hiking trail that offers stunning views of the Amalfi Coast, the Path of the Gods is a must-visit for outdoor enthusiasts. The trail runs from Bomerano to Nocelle, passing through scenic valleys and rocky hillsides along the way.

  2. The Valley of the Mills Trail: A historic trail that passes through the ruins of ancient flour mills, the Valley of the Mills Trail is an easy and scenic hike that offers a glimpse into the history of the area.

  3. The Monti Lattari Regional Park: A nature reserve that covers over 9000 hectares of land, the Monti Lattari Regional Park is a paradise for hikers and nature lovers. It offers stunning views of the Amalfi Coast and is home to a wide range of flora and fauna.

Best Things to Do in Furore: Activities and Tours

Furore offers a wide range of activities and tours to choose from. Here are some of the best things to do in Furore:

  1. Amalfi Boat Tours: Take a boat tour of the Amalfi coast and explore the hidden beaches and coves that can only be accessed by sea.

  2. Cooking Classes: Learn how to cook traditional Italian dishes in a cooking class led by local chefs.

  3. Wine Tasting: Explore the wine country around Furore and sample delicious local wines.

Food and Drink Scene of Furore

Furore has a rich culinary tradition, with plenty of local specialties to try. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  1. Insalata di Polpo: A refreshing and light salad made with octopus, celery, olives, and lemon juice.

  2. Linguine ai Frutti di Mare: A seafood pasta dish that’s simple yet incredibly flavorful.

  3. Sfogliatella: A delicious pastry filled with sweetened ricotta cheese and candied fruit.

Local Beverages

  1. Limoncello: An iconic Italian liqueur made from lemon zest, alcohol, sugar, and water. It’s a refreshing drink that’s perfect for hot summer days.

  2. Aglianico: A full-bodied red wine with a rich flavor and a hint of spice. It’s one of the most famous wines of southern Italy.

Where to Stay in Furore: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Furore offers a range of accommodation options, from luxurious hotels to budget-friendly hostels. Here are some of the best places to stay in Furore:

  1. Hotel Bacco: A luxurious hotel with stunning views of the sea and the Amalfi Coast.

  2. La Rosa dei Venti: A charming guesthouse with a beautiful garden and a peaceful atmosphere.

  3. Domus Aquae: A budget-friendly hostel with a central location and friendly staff.

What to Buy in Furore: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Furore is known for its beautiful ceramics. You can find a variety of handcrafted pottery in the local shops, from decorative plates to vases and figurines. Another popular souvenir is limoncello, which makes for a great gift or a reminder of your trip to Furore.

Local Culture and Customs of Furore

The people of Furore are proud of their local traditions and like to celebrate them through festivals and events. One of the most famous events in the area is the Festa della Madonna Bianca, held in August. During this festival, a procession takes place, and a large white statue of the Virgin Mary is carried through the town.

Best Time to Visit Furore

The best time to visit Furore is during the shoulder seasons, from April to early June and from September to November. During these months, the weather is mild, and the crowds are fewer. If you visit during the summer months, be prepared for hotter temperatures and larger crowds.

Do’s and Don’ts of Furore

Do:

  1. Respect the local traditions and customs.

  2. Try the local cuisine and wine.

  3. Take care of the natural environment while hiking or biking.

Don’t:

  1. Wear revealing clothing in religious sites.

  2. Be loud or disruptive in public places.

  3. Leave trash behind when hiking or picnicking.

Safety and Health

Safety in Furore

Furore is a safe destination, but like any other place, travelers should take common-sense precautions such as keeping an eye on their belongings and avoiding walking alone at night.

Health in Furore

Furore has excellent medical facilities, and travelers should have no problem finding quality medical assistance if needed. It’s always a good idea to have travel insurance that covers medical expenses.

Getting to Furore: Transportation Options and Logistics

Furore is located on the Amalfi Coast, which is well connected to the rest of Italy by road and sea. Here are some transportation options to consider:

Airports Near Furore

The nearest airports to Furore are Naples International Airport and Salerno Costa d’Amalfi Airport. From there, you can reach Furore by car or bus.

Bus Stations Near Furore

Furore is well connected to nearby towns and cities by bus. The SITA bus company operates a regular bus service on the Amalfi Coast.

Other Options

You can also reach Furore by car or taxi. There are plenty of car rental companies available at the nearby airports.

How to Get Around in Furore

The best way to get around Furore is on foot or by car. The town is small and easy to explore on foot, and renting a car is a great way to explore the surrounding area and nearby towns.

Where to Go Next from Furore: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Furore is a great base for exploring the Amalfi Coast and the surrounding area. Here are some nearby destinations to consider:

  1. Positano: A picturesque town with colorful houses and a charming beach.

  2. Naples: One of the most vibrant and lively cities in Italy, Naples is famous for its historic sites, museums, and delicious food.

  3. Pompeii: A Roman city that was destroyed by the eruption of Mount Vesuvius in AD 79, Pompeii is a UNESCO World Heritage Site and a fascinating archaeological site to explore.
