Introduction to Ascea

Ascea is a charming town located in the province of Salerno in Campania, Southern Italy. This historic town boasts a rich cultural heritage, breathtaking natural scenery, and a vibrant food scene that will captivate any traveler. With its stunning beaches, ancient ruins, and winding hiking trails, Ascea is quickly gaining popularity as a must-visit destination for tourists.

List of Places to Visit in Ascea

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Ascea is steeped in history, with centuries-old ruins and structures dotting the town. Some of the must-visit culturally important attractions in Ascea include:

  • Velia Archaeological Site: This ancient Greek settlement features stunning ruins, including temples, amphitheaters, and tombs.

  • Palazzo dei Principi: A magnificent palace built during the 18th century that belonged to the Sanseverino family.

  • Tower of Velia: A towering monument that dates back to the 12th century, with breathtaking views of the surrounding landscape.

  • Battistero Paleocristiano di San Giovanni: This ancient baptistery dates back to the 5th century and is one of the oldest examples of Christian architecture in Southern Italy.

Natural Attractions of Ascea: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Ascea is a nature lover’s paradise, with towering mountains, magnificent beaches, and lush parks. Some of the most popular natural attractions in Ascea are:

  • Ascea Marina Beach: One of the most beautiful beaches in Italy, with crystal-clear waters and powdery sands.

  • Monte Stella: A towering mountain that offers a challenging climb but rewards hikers with stunning views of the Tyrrhenian Sea.

  • Monte Gelbison: Located in the Cilento National Park, this mountain is home to rare animal species and boasts a rich biodiversity.

  • Grotte Marine di Capo Palinuro: A magical sea cave that can only be reached by boat, with stunning rock formations and crystal-clear waters.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Ascea

Ascea is a hiker’s and biker’s paradise, with dozens of winding trails that take you through rugged mountain terrain, lush forests, and stunning seaside vistas. Some of the most popular hiking and biking routes in Ascea are:

  • Sentiero degli Dei: This breathtaking trail spans over 7 kilometers and offers stunning views of the Amalfi Coast, Capri, and the Bay of Naples.

  • Monte Stella to Punta degli Infreschi: This challenging trail takes you through the Cilento National Park and rewards hikers with stunning views of the coastline.

  • La Chiusa di Fontanelle: This winding trail takes you through the lush hills of Ascea and offers breathtaking views of the Tyrrhenian Sea.

Best Things to Do in Ascea: Activities and Tours

Ascea offers a wealth of activities and tours that cater to every traveler’s interests. Some of the best things to do in Ascea are:

  • Go on a Sea Kayak Tour: Explore the stunning coastline of Ascea on a sea kayak tour, and discover secluded coves and hidden sea caves.

  • Visit an Olive Oil Farm: Sample some of the best olive oil in Italy and learn about the olive oil making process on a tour of one of the local farms.

  • Take a Cooking Class: Learn how to cook traditional Italian dishes using fresh, local ingredients on a cooking class.

  • Attend a Wine Tasting: Sample some of the best wines that Campania has to offer on a wine tasting tour in Ascea.

Week-Long Itinerary for Ascea

Here’s a sample week-long itinerary for Ascea:

Day 1: Visit the Velia Archaeological Site and explore the town center. Day 2: Climb Monte Stella and admire the scenic views. Day 3: Spend a day at Ascea Marina Beach, lounging in the sun and swimming in the crystal-clear waters. Day 4: Take a sea kayak tour and explore the stunning coastline of Ascea. Day 5: Visit an olive oil farm and learn about the olive oil making process. Day 6: Spruce up your cooking skills on a cooking class and enjoy the fruits of your labor. Day 7: Take a wine tasting tour and discover the flavors of Campania.

Food and Drink Scene of Ascea

Ascea has a rich food and drink scene that combines traditional Italian flavors with fresh, local ingredients. Some of the must-try dishes and local beverages in Ascea are:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Frittata di Fiori: A traditional Italian omelet stuffed with fresh flowers.
  • Mozzarella di Bufala: Made from the milk of water buffalos that graze in the nearby fields, this cheese is a must-try for cheese lovers.
  • Pizza di Scarola: A savory pie made from escarole, olives, capers, and anchovies, topped with grated parmesan cheese.

Local Beverages

  • Falerno del Massico: A full-bodied red wine made from Aglianico grapes that are grown on the slopes of the Massico mountain range.
  • Limoncello: A sweet-tart lemon liqueur that is the perfect after-dinner digestif.
  • Spritz: A refreshing cocktail made from Aperol, soda water, and prosecco, originating from the Veneto region but popular all over Italy.

Where to Stay in Ascea: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Ascea offers a variety of accommodation options that cater to every traveler’s budget and preferences. Some of the best places to stay in Ascea are:

  • Hotel Venere: A stunning hotel that offers comfortable rooms, a rooftop terrace, and proximity to Ascea Marina Beach.

  • Agave Residence: An apartment-style accommodation that offers comfortable lodgings that are perfect for families and groups.

  • La Chiusa delle More: A charming bed and breakfast that offers cozy rooms, stunning views, and proximity to the town center.

What to Buy in Ascea: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Some of the best souvenirs and local products to bring home from Ascea are:

  • Olive Oil: Ascea is renowned for its high-quality olive oil, made from olives that are grown in fields that surround the town.

  • Ceramics: The nearby town of Vietri Sul Mare is famous for its beautiful ceramics, that are sold in shops all around Ascea.

  • Limoncello: This sweet-tart lemon liqueur is a popular souvenir among tourists.

Local Culture and Customs of Ascea

Ascea is a town that values traditions and heritage, and it’s home to many festivals and events that are an integral part of the local culture. Some of the most prominent customs and traditions in Ascea are:

  • The Feast of St. Giovanni: A religious festival that is held in late June and celebrates the town’s patron saint.

  • The Sagra della Triglia: A fish festival that is held in August and features live music, dancing, and fish dishes that are prepared using local ingredients.

  • The Sagra dell’Olio: An olive oil festival that is held in September and celebrates the town’s main agricultural product.

Best Time to Visit Ascea

The best time to visit Ascea is in the spring (April-June) and fall (September-November) when the weather is mild, and the crowds are thinner. In summer (July-August), the town can get crowded and hot, with temperatures that can reach up to 35 °C.

Do’s and Don’ts of Ascea

Do:

  • Try the local cuisine and wines.
  • Respect the local customs and traditions.
  • Explore the natural beauty of the town and the surrounding areas.

Don’t:

  • Leave trash or litter in public areas.
  • Wear revealing clothing to cultural or religious sites.
  • Disrespect the locals or their traditions.

Safety and Health

Safety in Ascea

Ascea is a safe town, with low crime rates and a friendly local population. However, tourists should exercise caution when walking alone at night and avoid leaving valuables unattended in public areas.

Health in Ascea

Ascea has adequate medical facilities, including a hospital and several clinics. Tourists should have comprehensive medical insurance that covers emergency care, as treatment can be expensive in Italy.

Getting to Ascea: Transportation Options and Logistics

Ascea is well-connected to the rest of the country, with several transportation options at your disposal. Here are some of the ways you can get to Ascea:

Airports Near Ascea

  • Naples International Airport.
  • Salerno Costa d’Amalfi Airport.

Bus Stations Near Ascea

  • Salerno Bus Station.
  • Napoli Centrale Bus Station.

Other Options

  • Train: There are regular train services that connect Ascea to major cities in Italy, including Naples, Rome, and Florence.

  • Car: Driving to Ascea is also an option, and the town can be reached by the A3 highway that connects Salerno to Reggio Calabria. However, be aware that Italian roads can be narrow and winding.

How to Get Around in Ascea

Ascea is a small town that is best explored on foot or by bike. Most of the attractions are located within walking distance of each other, and the town offers several bike rental services that are affordable and convenient.

Where to Go Next from Ascea: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Ascea is located in a region that offers a wealth of nearby destinations and excursions, some of which include:

  • Amalfi Coast: A breathtaking coastal area that is famous for its picturesque towns and stunning sea views.

  • Naples: A bustling city that is home to some of Italy’s most famous landmarks and attractions, including Pompeii and Mount Vesuvius.

  • Capri: A stunning island that can be reached by boat from Naples or Salerno, with crystal-clear waters and charming towns.

Conclusion

Ascea is a charming historic town that offers a wealth of attractions, activities, and experiences for every traveler. With its scenic vistas, natural wonders, and vibrant food scene, Ascea is a must-visit destination for anyone who loves exploring new places and experiencing diverse cultures.
