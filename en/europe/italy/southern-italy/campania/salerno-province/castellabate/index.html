Introduction to Castellabate

Located in the southernmost region of Italy’s Campania, Castellabate is a charming medieval village with narrow streets and stunning architecture. The town has managed to retain its traditional Italian character, and visitors are welcomed by a rustic charm that is often missing from more commercialized tourist destinations. Castellabate is known for its pristine beaches, crystal clear waters, historic architecture, and delicious food. This guide will take you on a journey through the lovely town of Castellabate and explore everything it has to offer.

List of Places to Visit in Castellabate

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

One of the most significant historical landmarks in Castellabate is the Castello dell’Abate, a castle built by the Abbot Costabile Gentilcore in 1123. The castle is host to a museum containing artifacts from the time of the Roman Empire through to the early 20th century. Other historical places to visit include:

  • The Church of Santa Maria a Mare, built in the 12th century.
  • The Church of San Costabile, built during the Norma period in the 10th century.
  • The Palazzo Belmonte, a castle built in the 17th century in a baroque style.

Natural Attractions of Castellabate: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

The stunning coastline of Castellabate is the main attraction of the town. Visitors can relax on nearby beaches, such as the Lido del Villaggio or enjoy a host of aquatic activities, such as swimming, snorkeling, and scuba diving. There are numerous lookout points and hills to explore, such as:

  • The Punta Licosa mountain, offering stunning views of the coast and is located close to the Lago di Castellabate.
  • Monte Stella, which towers over the town and provides an excellent view for those willing to make the climb.
  • The Bay of Trentova, a beautiful crescent-shaped coastline with clear blue waters and a powdery sandy beach.
  • The Natural Reserve of Punta Tresino, full of trails perfect for hiking, biking, and walking with a gorgeous landscape of beaches and hills.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Castellabate

One of the most popular things to do in Castellabate is hiking and biking. There are many trails around the town that offer beautiful views and varied terrain. Here are some of the most famous hiking trails in Castellabate:

  • The Sentiero degli Dei, or the Path of the Gods. This stunning route takes you through a scenic route to Positano with breathtaking views of the Mediterranean.
  • The Trekking Route of the Cilento Coast, a 20km trail that takes visitors through the most beautiful and unspoiled parts of the Cilento Natural Park.
  • The Grotta della Cala, a famous cave located on a cliff that requires a short trek through the forest to reach it.
  • Punta Licosa Trekking, taking you to one of the most beautiful locations in the entire region. A hike of about 3km to Punta Licosa follows a steep uphill path, but the breathtaking panorama is entirely worth it.

Best Things to Do in Castellabate: Activities and Tours

Castellabate has a host of activities designed to entertain visitors of all ages. Here are some of the top things to do in Castellabate:

  • Boat tours along the coast are an excellent way to explore the beauty of Castellabate from a different perspective. Visitors can choose from trips along the coast or a trip to nearby Poisitano.
  • Wine tasting tours to local wineries and cellars. Vineyards in the area are known for their distinct flavors and quality wine.
  • Cooking classes offer visitors an opportunity to learn to cook authentic Italian dishes from expert chefs.
  • Scuba diving excursions are an ideal way to explore the underwater world surrounding Castellabate.
  • Visit local markets to purchase handmade leather goods, olive oil, and other souvenirs.

Week-Long Itinerary for Castellabate

A week in Castellabate is enough to explore its natural and cultural offerings. Here is our suggested itinerary for seven days in Castellabate:

Day 1: Visit La Baia della Trentova and Punta Tresino Natural Reserve for a day filled with hiking and swimming.

Day 2: A day to discover the cultural treasures of Castellabate, including the Castello dell’Abate, the Church of San Costabile, and the Palazzo Belmonte.

Day 3: Rent a boat or join a boat excursion to explore the coves and hidden beaches of Castellabate’s coastline.

Day 4: Trek the Sentiero degli Dei path to Positano, arguably the most famous hike in the region.

Day 5: Explore the beautiful town of Agropoli, located nearby, for its picturesque harbor, ancient castle, and fascinating museums.

Day 6: Visit the vineyards of Cilento National Park, where visitors can enjoy wine-tasting and learn about traditional winemaking practices.

Day 7: Explore the Grotta della Cala and Monte Stella, to marvel at stunning views and discover natural hidden gems hidden in the hills of Castellabate.

Food and Drink Scene of Castellabate

Must-Try Dishes

Castellabate’s traditional cuisine is influenced by the sea and the surrounding countryside’s rich agricultural landscape. Here are some popular dishes:

  • Spaghetti alle vongole is a classic pasta dish made with fresh tomatoes, garlic, clams, and parsley.
  • Fritto di mare combines calamari, shrimp, and fish and is fried to create a crispy, delicious seafood platter.
  • Aglianico wine, produced in the nearby vineyards and known for its deep ruby color and rich tannins, is perfect to pair with local cuisine.

Local Beverages

Aside from Aglianico wine, Castellabate is also known for its local beer, with a brewery running in the village of San Marco di Castellabate.

What to Buy in Castellabate: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Here are some items to consider purchasing before you leave Castellabate:

  • Handmade leather goods, such as wallets, purses, and belts, from local artisans.
  • Olive oil, made from olives grown in the surrounding hills, is famous for its delicate taste and golden color.
  • Limoncello, a liqueur made from the locally harvested lemons.

Local Culture and Customs of Castellabate

The locals of Castellabate are known for their hospitality and laid-back approach to life. It is customary to greet others warmly when passing on the street, and it is often appreciated if tourists attempt to speak a few simple phrases in Italian when ordering food or asking for directions. Smoking is common in Italy, so be prepared to encounter smokers in public places.

Best Time to Visit Castellabate

The best time to visit Castellabate is in late spring or early summer, particularly from May to June, when the weather is mild and sunny. The temperatures can be hot during mid-summer, so visitors preferring the beach should attempt to arrive within the early months. September to November also offers warm and sunny weather perfect for hiking, trekking, and sightseeing.

Do’s and Don’ts of Castellabate

Do:

  • Speak Italian phrases when ordering food or asking for directions.
  • Dress modestly when visiting churches and other religious institutions.
  • Try the local cuisine and beverages.

Don’t:

  • Smoke in non-smoking areas.
  • Touch artifacts or exhibits in museums and historical landmarks.
  • Be rude or dismissive towards locals or other visitors.

Safety and Health

Safety in Castellabate

Castellabate is a safe town with the low crime rate, with little to no risk of tourists encountering any dangers. Police officers are highly visible and present across the town.

Health in Castellabate

Visitors are advised to keep hydrated, particularly when hiking or exploring the town on hot days. Although medical facilities are available in Castellabate, visitors from outside the EU will be required to pay the full price for any medical services.

Getting to Castellabate: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Castellabate

The closest airport to Castellabate is Napoli-Capodichino International Airport, which is about 100km away.

Bus Stations Near Castellabate

There are buses that go directly from the station to Castellabate throughout the day.

  • The bus schedule is accessible from the Salerno railway station and on the Busitalia Campania website.

Other Options

Visitors can choose to rent a car or take a taxi from Salerno or Naples to Castellabate at a fixed rate.

How to Get Around in Castellabate

One of the best ways to explore Castellabate is on foot, as the town is compact, and sightseeing is best suited for walking. There are limited taxis and buses, but they are rarely used and would require a lengthy wait in between.

Where to Go Next from Castellabate: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

There are other destinations nearby that visitors can explore:

  • The UNESCO World Heritage site of Paestum is just a 10-minute drive from Castellabate, boasting some of the best-preserved Greek temples of all time.
  • The nearby town of Agropoli, which has a lovely harbor, historic downtown center, and a fascinating museum dedicated to the region’s history.

Conclusion

Castellabate is one of the hidden gems of Italy, an ideal destination for travelers seeking an adventure and escape from the hustle and bustle of touristy places. The town offers a perfect blend of nature, culture, and cuisine that is unrivaled. This comprehensive travel guide contains all the essential information needed to plan a trip to Castellabate and experience the beauty of this charming medieval village.
