Introduction to Naples

Naples, the third-largest city in Italy, is a vibrant and bustling metropolis, known for its rich history, stunning artworks, and breathtaking views of the Gulf of Naples. Situated in the Campania region of Southern Italy, Naples is a popular travel destination for those looking to explore the rustic charm of Italy and indulge in some authentic Italian food.

List of Places to Visit in Naples

There is no shortage of attractions in Naples. The following are some of the must-visit places during your stay in Naples:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Naples is home to some of the world’s most impressive museums and historical landmarks. The following are some of the most popular ones:

  • National Archaeological Museum: This museum houses a vast collection of ancient Roman artifacts, including mosaics, sculptures, and frescoes.
  • Pompeii Archaeological Site: A must-visit site for history lovers. This ancient Roman city was buried in volcanic ash in AD 79 and excavated in the 18th century. Now, it’s one of the best-preserved ancient cities in the world.
  • Royal Palace of Naples: This 17th-century palace was once home to the Bourbon Kings of Naples and is home to stunning artwork, furniture, and decor.

Natural Attractions of Naples: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Naples is an ideal destination for nature lovers, as it offers plenty of outdoor activities and scenic views. The following are some of the top natural attractions in Naples:

  • Mount Vesuvius: A dormant volcano that is responsible for the destruction of Pompeii. Visitors can hike to the top of the volcano for a stunning view of the surrounding countryside.
  • Amalfi Coast: A stunning coastline that stretches for 50 kilometers from Sorrento to Salerno, offering picturesque fishing villages, pebble beaches, and crystal-clear waters.
  • Gulf of Naples: A scenic bay that offers breathtaking views of the city’s skyline, Mount Vesuvius, and the islands of Capri and Ischia.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Naples

If you’re looking for an active way to explore Naples, there are plenty of hiking and biking routes throughout the city. Here are some of the popular routes:

  • Posillipo Hill: A scenic hillside that overlooks the Gulf of Naples. You can hike or bike to the top and enjoy panoramic views of the city and its surroundings.
  • Capodimonte Park: A 130-hectare park with several walking trails and biking paths. This park is home to several historical landmarks, including the Royal Palace of Capodimonte.
  • Catacombs of San Gennaro: A historical site where you can explore underground tombs and tunnels. Guided tours are available for those who want to learn more about the history behind this unique location.

Best Things to Do in Naples: Activities and Tours

In addition to the above-listed attractions, Naples offers plenty of activities and tours for tourists. Here are some popular ones:

  • Pizza-Making Class: Naples is the birthplace of pizza, and taking a pizza-making class is a great way to learn how to make authentic Italian pizza.
  • Wine Tasting Tour: Italy is famous for its wine, and Naples has several wine tasting tours that allow you to sample some of the finest Italian wines.
  • Boat Tour of Capri: Naples is the gateway to the stunning island of Capri, and a boat tour is a great way to explore the island’s stunning coastline and crystal-clear waters.

Week-Long Itinerary for Naples

If you’re planning to spend a week in Naples, here’s a suggested itinerary to help you plan your trip:

Day 1: Explore the historical landmarks of Naples, including the Royal Palace of Naples and Castel dell’Ovo.

Day 2: Visit Mount Vesuvius, Pompeii, and Herculaneum to explore the ancient ruins of this once-thriving Roman city.

Day 3: Take a day trip to the beautiful island of Capri, known for its stunning coastline, crystal-clear waters, and picturesque villages.

Day 4: Explore the vibrant Spaccanapoli neighborhood, known for its narrow alleys, colorful buildings, and lively street markets.

Day 5: Take a hiking or biking trip to the Posillipo Hill and Capodimonte Park to enjoy the scenic views of Naples and its surroundings.

Day 6: Visit the National Archaeological Museum to explore some of the ancient Roman artifacts and related exhibits.

Day 7: End your trip by enjoying the panoramic views of Naples from the Castel Sant’Elmo, an iconic castle located on a hilltop in Naples.

Food and Drink Scene of Naples

No visit to Naples is complete without indulging in some authentic Italian food and wine. Here are some popular dishes and drinks to try while in Naples:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Pizza Margherita: The quintessential Neapolitan pizza, made with fresh tomatoes, mozzarella cheese, and basil.
  • Spaghetti alle Vongole: A classic Neapolitan pasta dish made with fresh clams, garlic, and olive oil.
  • Baba: A sweet pastry soaked in rum and filled with whipped cream.

Local Beverages

  • Limoncello: A lemon-flavored liqueur that’s traditional to the Amalfi Coast region.
  • Taurasi: An Italian wine that’s produced in the Campania region and known for its full-bodied flavor.

Where to Stay in Naples: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Naples offers a variety of accommodation options to suit every budget and preference. Here are some popular options:

  • Grand Hotel Vesuvio: A luxurious 5-star hotel located in the heart of Naples, offering stunning views of the Gulf of Naples.
  • Hostel of the Sun: A popular hostel that offers budget-friendly accommodation and is located in the historic center of Naples.
  • B&B La Concordia: A cozy guesthouse located just a stone’s throw away from the Naples city center, offering comfortable rooms and friendly service.

What to Buy in Naples: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Naples is known for its ceramics, carved stone figures, and leather goods. Here are some popular souvenirs and local products to bring home:

  • Ceramic Trinkets: Hand-painted ceramics are traditional to the Naples region, and you’ll find plenty of shops selling unique and colorful trinkets.
  • Coral Jewelry: Coral is traditional to the Naples region and can be found in the form of jewelry and other accessories.
  • Limoncello: Limoncello liqueur is a popular product of the Amalfi Coast region and makes for an excellent souvenir.

Local Culture and Customs of Naples

Naples has a rich cultural heritage and customs that visitors should be aware of. Here are a few:

  • Religious Celebrations: Naples is famously religious and has several festivals celebrating different saints. These celebrations are colorful and lively and are an excellent opportunity to experience the local culture.
  • Gestures: Neapolitans are known for their use of gestures to convey messages. Visitors should be aware that some of these gestures may have different meanings in different contexts.
  • Etiquette: Dressing casually is appropriate in most situations in Naples, but visitors should dress modestly when entering churches or other religious buildings.

Best Time to Visit Naples

The best time to visit Naples is from April to June or September to November. During these months, the weather is pleasant, and there are fewer crowds than in the peak summer months.

Do’s and Don’ts of Naples

To ensure a pleasant trip to Naples, here are some do’s and don’ts to keep in mind:

Do:

  • Do Explore: Naples is a beautiful city, full of history and culture. Take the time to explore and experience all the wonders it has to offer.
  • Do Learn Some Italian: While many Neapolitans speak English, learning some Italian can help you better interact with locals and appreciate the language and culture.

Don’t:

  • Don’t Leave Your Valuables Unattended: As with any city, visitors should be mindful of their belongings and avoid leaving them unattended.
  • Don’t Drink Tap Water: The tap water in Naples is not safe for drinking, so visitors should stick to bottled water.

Safety and Health

As with any travel destination, visitors to Naples should be aware of safety and health concerns. Here are some tips:

Safety in Naples

  • Be Aware Of Pickpockets: As with any tourist destination, visitors to Naples should be mindful of their belongings, especially in crowded areas or on public transportation.
  • Be Careful When Driving: Naples has a high incidence of traffic accidents, so visitors should exercise caution when driving or crossing the streets.

Health in Naples

  • Drink Bottled Water: As mentioned earlier, visitors should avoid drinking tap water and stick to bottled water.
  • Take Appropriate Precautions during COVID-19: Visitors should follow all local guidelines and recommendations regarding COVID-19 prevention.

Getting to Naples: Transportation Options and Logistics

Naples is well connected, and visitors can easily reach the city through several different transportation options. Here are some popular choices:

  • Air: Naples International Airport (NAP) is the primary airport serving Naples, and several airlines operate regular flights to and from the city.
  • Train: Naples is linked to many Italian cities by an extensive rail network, making train travel a convenient and affordable option.
  • Bus: Several bus companies operate regular services to and from Naples, connecting the city to several regions in Italy.

Airports Near Naples

  • Naples International Airport: As mentioned earlier, NAP is the primary airport serving Naples and is located about 6 kilometers from the city center.

Bus Stations Near Naples

  • Naples Capodichino Bus Station: Located next to the NAP airport, this bus station connects Naples to several other Italian cities.
  • Naples Garibaldi Bus Station: The primary bus station serving Naples, this station connects the city to several regions across Italy.

Other Options

  • Ferry: Naples is a popular departure point for several ferries that connect to other Italian cities and the islands of Capri, Ischia, and Procida.
  • Car: Visitors can also rent a car to explore Naples and its surroundings.

How to Get Around in Naples

Naples offers several transportation options for getting around the city. Here are some popular ones:

  • Metro: Naples has a metro system that connects the city center to several neighborhoods and suburbs.
  • Buses: Naples has an extensive bus network that covers the entire city and surrounding areas.
  • Taxi: Taxis are readily available in Naples, and fares are generally reasonable.

Where to Go Next from Naples: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Naples is an excellent base for exploring the surrounding regions and nearby cities. Here are some popular nearby destinations and excursions:

  • The Amalfi Coast: A scenic coast that stretches from Sorrento to Salerno, offering stunning views, picturesque villages, and crystal-clear waters.
  • Pompeii: As mentioned earlier, Pompeii is an excellent historical site to explore near Naples.
  • Capri: A beautiful island located just off the coast of Naples, known for its stunning coastline, crystal-clear waters, and picturesque villages.

Conclusion

Naples is a city that offers something for everyone. Whether you’re interested in history, culture, or just indulging in some authentic Italian food, Naples is a destination that won’t disappoint. Use this comprehensive travel guide to plan your trip and make the most of your time in Naples. Buon Viaggio!
