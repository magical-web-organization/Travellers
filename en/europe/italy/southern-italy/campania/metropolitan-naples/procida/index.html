Introduction to Procida

Procida is a small island located in the Gulf of Naples in southern Italy. It is part of the Flegrean Islands, which also include Ischia and Nisida. While not as popular as Ischia or Capri, this charming fishing village has its own unique charm and beauty that is worth exploring. Procida is known for its colorful buildings, narrow streets, and stunning views of the sea.

List of Places to Visit in Procida

When visiting Procida, there are a few places you simply cannot miss. Here are the must-visit places in Procida:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Casa di Terra Murata

Located at the highest point of Procida, Casa di Terra Murata is an important historical place that dates back to the 15th century. For centuries it served as a fortress and a defense against invading forces. Today, it has been turned into a museum and is open to visitors who can learn about Procida’s history and culture.

Chiesa di Santa Maria delle Grazie

This church located in the Marina di Corricella is a beautiful example of 16th-century Italian architecture, complete with stunning frescoes and a stunning bell-tower. It is also home to a famous bronze statue of Saint Michael the Archangel, commissioned by King Ferdinand I of the Two Sicilies.

Natural Attractions of Procida: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

La Spiaggia di Pozzo Vecchio

This stunning beach is located on the west coast of Procida and is arguably the island’s most beautiful. Its turquoise waters make it a popular spot for swimming and snorkeling, while the beautiful cliff faces provide a picture-perfect backdrop.

Sentiero dei Fortini

This coastal path provides walkers with stunning views of the island’s coastline. The trail takes you past six old watchtowers that were once used to spot and ward off pirates.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Procida

Procida’s small size makes it ideal for exploring on foot or by bike. There are several routes you can take to explore the natural beauty of the island, including the Sentiero dei Fortini coastal path and the steep path leading up to Terra Murata.

Best Things to Do in Procida: Activities and Tours

While Procida is mostly a place to relax, there are plenty of activities to keep visitors busy.

Boat Tours

Take a boat tour around the island to explore Procida’s stunning coastline and views of colorful houses.

Cooking Classes

Explore the local cuisine by taking part in a cooking class. Learn how to make traditional dishes, including “Linguine alla Procida” and the famous “Procida lemon cake.”

Week-Long Itinerary for Procida

Here’s a sample week-long itinerary for those looking to explore Procida’s culture, scenery, and unique charm.

Day Activity
1 Explore Terra Murata and Casa di Terra Murata
2 Visit the Chiesa di Santa Maria delle Grazie and go for a swim at the Spiaggia di Pozzo Vecchio
3 Take a boat tour around the island
4 Attend a cooking class and learn how to make traditional dishes
5 Explore the Sentiero dei Fortini coastal path
6 Visit the Museo Archeologico dei Campi Flegrei
7 Relax and enjoy the beach at Lingua or Marina di Chiaiolella

Food and Drink Scene of Procida

Procida is known for its fresh seafood and local cuisine. Here’s what you should try:

Must-Try Dishes

Linguine alla Procida

Pasta with tomatoes, garlic, and clams is one of the most famous dishes of the island.

Procida Lemon Cake

A delicious cake made with the island’s famous lemons.

Local Beverages

Limoncello

A sweet liquer made with the island’s famous lemons.

Ischitella

A local wine made from Fiano grapes that you can find at many of the island’s restaurants.

Where to Stay in Procida: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Procida offers a variety of accommodation options. Here are a few recommendations:

La Tonnara

Located on the west coast of the island, La Tonnara is a stunning hotel with an outdoor pool and a private beach.

Hotel Celeste

This charming hotel in Marina Grande offers stunning views of the coast and is just minutes away from the beach.

B&B Terra Murata

This charming bed and breakfast offers guests stunning views, delicious breakfasts, and a peaceful atmosphere.

What to Buy in Procida: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Procida is the perfect place to purchase souvenirs, including local products such as lemons, olive oil, and wine. Other popular souvenirs include handmade ceramics and linens made from Le Costantine fabrics.

Local Culture and Customs of Procida

Procida is known for its strong fishing culture, and many of the island’s residents are still involved in the industry. Additionally, visitors should respect local customs, including dressing appropriately when visiting religious sites and adhering to local customs when it comes to mealtimes.

Best Time to Visit Procida

The best time to visit Procida is between May and September when the weather is warm and sunny. Springtime is also a great time to visit, when the flowers and trees are in bloom.

Do’s and Don’ts of Procida

Do:

  • Respect the local culture and customs
  • Enjoy the local cuisine
  • Explore the island’s natural beauty

Don’t:

  • Wear inappropriate clothing when visiting religious sites
  • Litter on the island’s beaches or streets

Safety and Health

Safety in Procida

Procida is generally a safe place to visit. However, as with any location, visitors should be aware of their surroundings and take appropriate precautions to keep themselves and their belongings safe.

Health in Procida

Procida offers basic medical facilities, but visitors with pre-existing medical conditions should make sure to travel with appropriate medications and documentation.

Getting to Procida: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Procida

The closest airport to Procida is in Naples. From there, visitors can take a ferry to the island.

Bus Stations Near Procida

There are several bus stations in Naples that offer regular service to Procida.

Other Options

Visitors can also travel to Procida by car and take the ferry from nearby Pozzuoli or Naples.

How to Get Around in Procida

Procida is a small island that can be easily explored on foot or by bike. There are also regular bus routes that can take you around the island.

Where to Go Next from Procida: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Procida is close to several other beautiful destinations, including the islands of Ischia and Capri. Visitors can also explore the nearby city of Naples and the historical sites of Pompeii and Herculaneum.

Conclusion

Procida is a beautiful and charming destination that offers stunning natural beauty and a rich cultural heritage. Whether you want to relax on the beach, explore the island’s history, or indulge in local cuisine, Procida has something to offer everyone. With this comprehensive guide, you’re sure to have a memorable trip to this hidden gem in the Mediterranean.
