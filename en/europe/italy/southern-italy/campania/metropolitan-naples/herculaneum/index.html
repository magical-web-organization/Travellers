Introduction to Herculaneum

Herculaneum is a small city located in the shadow of Mount Vesuvius, in Campania, Southern Italy. It is famous for its well-preserved ancient ruins and beautiful scenery. The city was destroyed by the eruption of Mount Vesuvius in 79 AD and was buried for centuries under layers of ash and lava until its excavation in 1738. Today, it is a popular tourist attraction, offering visitors a glimpse into the past and the opportunity to explore ancient Roman life.

List of Places to Visit in Herculaneum

There are several places to visit and things to do in Herculaneum. We have categorized them into culturally important attractions and natural attractions.

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  1. Archaeological Site of Herculaneum Located in the heart of the city, the archaeological site is the main attraction in Herculaneum. It is home to ancient Roman ruins, including villas, public baths, and houses.
  2. Villa of the Papyri The Villa of the Papyri is an ancient Roman villa, famous for its library of papyrus scrolls. It is located on the outskirts of the city and is one of the most important archaeological discoveries of the 18th century.
  3. Museo Archeologico Virtuale The virtual archaeological museum is dedicated to the history and culture of Herculaneum. It offers visitors an interactive experience, allowing them to explore the city’s ruins virtually.

Natural Attractions of Herculaneum: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  1. Monte Vesuvio Mount Vesuvius is an active volcano and is the main attraction near Herculaneum. It is a popular destination for hiking and offers amazing views of the surrounding landscapes.
  2. Marina di San Giorgio Marina di San Giorgio is a small beach located along the Gulf of Naples. It is a popular spot for swimming, sunbathing, and relaxation.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Herculaneum

There are several hiking and biking routes in and around Herculaneum. Here are some of the best routes to explore:

  1. Villa of the Papyri to Monte Vesuvio This route takes you from the Villa of the Papyri to the peak of Mount Vesuvius, offering spectacular views of the city and surrounding landscapes.
  2. Herculaneum to Pompeii This route takes you along the ancient Roman road that connects Herculaneum and Pompeii. It offers a glimpse into the past and takes you through beautiful countryside.

Food and Drink Scene of Herculaneum

Herculaneum is well-known for its cuisine. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  1. Spaghetti con le Vongole Spaghetti with clams is a local favorite. It is a simple yet flavorful dish that is often served with a glass of white wine.
  2. Pizza Margherita Pizza Margherita is a classic Italian dish and is a must-try when in Herculaneum. It is a simple yet delicious dish made with fresh tomatoes, mozzarella, and basil.

Local Beverages

Local Culture and Customs of Herculaneum

Herculaneum is a small town and has a friendly and welcoming culture. The locals are proud of their heritage and are happy to share their knowledge and traditions with visitors. It is customary to say “ciao” when greeting locals and to show respect for historical and cultural monuments.

Best Time to Visit Herculaneum

The best time to visit Herculaneum is during the spring and fall seasons, when the weather is mild and crowds are fewer. The summer months can be hot and humid, and the winter months can be rainy and cold.
