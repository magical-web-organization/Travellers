Introduction to Pompeii

Pompeii is a city in southern Italy that dates back to the 6th century BC when it was founded by the Osci people. The city is situated at the foot of Mount Vesuvius and is best known for its ruinous condition after an eruption in 79 AD. Today, Pompeii receives millions of visitors every year who come to see the ancient city’s fantastic ruins, museums, and breathtaking views of the region.

List of Places to Visit in Pompeii

Pompeii is a vast archaeological site that contains several culturally important attractions and natural wonders. Here are some of the best places to visit in Pompeii:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Pompeii Archaeological Site: This ancient city is the main draw for visitors to Pompeii. It features extensive ruins of residential houses, public buildings, squares, and temples, besides a vast range of sculptures and frescoes.
  • Villa of the Mysteries: A famous villa of Pompeii adorned with some of the best-preserved frescoes from ancient Rome.
  • Pompeii Amphitheater: Built-in 70 BC, the amphitheater is still considered one of the most magnificent entertainment venues of all time.

Natural Attractions of Pompeii: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Mount Vesuvius: A stratovolcano in the Gulf of Naples, Mount Vesuvius is best known for its destructive 79 AD eruption, burying Pompeii under a layer of ash. Today, visitors can hike to its summit for panoramic views of the surrounding region.
  • Bay of Naples: A beautiful bay with azure water, it is an excellent spot for boating, swimming, and soaking in the sun.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Pompeii

Pompeii offers some fantastic hiking and biking routes that offer breathtaking views of the surrounding hills and coastline. Here are some of the top options:

  • The Path of the Gods: A challenging hike that takes visitors along the Amalfi coast for over 4 hours, this is one of the most scenic hiking routes in the region.
  • Mount Vesuvius Hike: A moderate, 5-hour trek that takes visitors up to the summit of the volcano, offering awe-inspiring views of the Bay of Naples and the countryside.
  • Pompeii to Sorrento Cycle route: A flat, 25 km route that follows the coastline to the beautiful town of Sorrento, offering fantastic views of the gulf, mountains, and coastline.

Best Things to Do in Pompeii: Activities and Tours

There are many exciting activities and tours to enjoy in Pompeii, ensuring visitors can make the most of their time in this ancient city. Here are the top things to do:

  • Guided tours: Taking part in a guided tour is a great way to explore Pompeii and learn about the city’s history and culture from experts.
  • Cooking classes: Visitors can take a cooking class in Pompeii to learn how to make traditional Italian dishes.
  • Wine tasting: There are many vineyards and wineries in the surrounding hills of Pompeii that produce fantastic wines, and tastings are always available.
  • Pompeii by Night: A unique experience to explore the ruins of Pompeii under a starry sky.

Week-Long Itinerary for Pompeii

Day 1: Explore Pompeii Archaeological Site, including Villa of the Mysteries and Pompeii Amphitheater.

Day 2: Hike Mount Vesuvius, visit the crater and take a picnic to enjoy panoramic views.

Day 3: Take a guided tour of Pompeii ruins and visit the Pompeii Museum.

Day 4: Enjoy a leisurely day on the Bay of Naples, including swimming, boating or sunbathing.

Day 5: Take a cooking class to learn about traditional Italian dishes.

Day 6: Visit the vineyards and wineries surrounding Pompeii, taste some local wines while enjoying the beautiful scenery.

Day 7: Take a trip to the nearby town of Sorrento to enjoy the picturesque coastal town before heading back home.

Food and Drink Scene of Pompeii

Pompeii offers a range of delicious local dishes, wines, and beverages for visitors to try.

Must-Try Dishes

  • Pizza: Naples is the birthplace of pizza, and Pompeii is sure to serve some of the most authentic and tasty variations of this local favorite.
  • Spaghetti alla carbonara: A traditional Italian pasta dish with eggs, cheese, and pork, this hearty meal is perfect after a day of exploring Pompeii.
  • Fish: This coastal region is known for its delicious seafood dishes; some of the must-tries include fried calamari and grilled octopus.

Local Beverages

  • Limoncello: A popular Italian lemon liqueur made from the lemons grown along the Amalfi Coast.
  • Prosecco: A sparkling wine from the Veneto region of Italy, frequently served as an aperitif.

Where to Stay in Pompeii: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Pompeii offers accommodation options to fit any budget, whether it’s luxury hotels or affordable guesthouses. Here are some of the top options:

  • Villa Franca: A luxury hotel located next to the ruins of Pompeii and offering amazing views of Mount Vesuvius.
  • Hotel Maiuri: A comfortable hotel located at the edge of Pompeii’s archaeological site and close to the city center.
  • Albergo Pompeii Valley: An affordable guesthouse located in the countryside near Pompeii, offering a tranquil environment and stunning views of Mount Vesuvius.

What to Buy in Pompeii: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Visitors to Pompeii can find numerous souvenirs and products to remind them of their trip. Here are some of the best options:

  • Cameos: Handcrafted miniature sculptures made of shell or coral that originated in Naples. They make for a unique and beautiful souvenir from Pompeii.
  • Limoncello: A souvenir from Pompeii that you can enjoy long after your trip. It is a perfect gift for friends and family back home.
  • Leather goods: Naples is known for high-quality leather products, including bags, shoes, and belts.

Local Culture and Customs of Pompeii

Pompeii is steeped in history, with much to learn about the original inhabitants of the city. Visitors are sure to come across customs and traditions, including the ritual way of life and an appreciation of the arts and architecture.

Best Time to Visit Pompeii

The best time to visit Pompeii is from March to October when the weather is dry and sunny, and the temperature is moderate. The peak season is July and August, and it can get crowded. Travel during the shoulder season is a good alternative as it’s less crowded, but still warm enough to enjoy the outdoors.

Do’s and Don’ts of Pompeii

Do:

  • Purchase Pompeii’s entry tickets in advance to avoid the long queues.
  • Wear comfortable shoes for exploring the vast archaeological site.
  • Take a water bottle, hat, sunscreen, and snacks as there aren’t many cafes and restaurants within the archaeological area.

Don’t:

  • Touch or move any of the ruins to avoid damaging them.
  • Take any food or drink into the archaeological site.
  • Wear inappropriate clothing, including short shorts, miniskirts, and tank tops, which are not permitted.

Safety and Health

Safety in Pompeii

Pompeii is a safe destination for tourists, but visitors should take the standard precautions:

  • Avoid carrying large amounts of cash and valuables.
  • Keep an eye out for pickpockets and scammers in crowded areas.
  • Follow the instructions of guides and authorities during hiking, biking, and adventure expeditions.

Health in Pompeii

Pompeii is a safe destination for health, however:

  • Visitors should come prepared with the necessary medications that they may need during their travels.
  • Make sure to stay hydrated, wear sunscreen, and take frequent breaks while exploring Pompeii to avoid dehydration and sunstroke.

Getting to Pompeii: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Pompeii

The closest airport to Pompeii is Naples International Airport, located roughly 30 minutes away by car. Additionally, visitors can fly to Rome or Florence and take an intercity train or bus to Pompeii.

Bus Stations Near Pompeii

Pompeii has excellent bus connections to several cities and towns nearby. Visitors can catch a bus to Naples, Sorrento, Capri, Amalfi, and Positano, among other destinations.

Other Options

Alternatively, visitors can also take an intercity train to Pompeii, which runs on the Naples – Sorrento railway line.

How to Get Around in Pompeii

The best way to explore Pompeii is on foot as it is a small and compact city. Visitors can also rent bicycles or scooters to explore the nearby hills and countryside.

Where to Go Next from Pompeii: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Visitors to Pompeii can travel to nearby destinations such as Sorrento, Capri, Amalfi, and Naples. Each of these is perfect for a day trip or an extended stay, offering rich cultural heritage, beautiful natural landscapes, and mouth-watering cuisine.

Conclusion

Pompeii is a fascinating ancient city that deserves a place on every travel wish list. With a rich history and culture, natural beauty, and fantastic food and drink, Pompeii offers something for every type of traveler. We hope this comprehensive travel guide will help you plan your own unforgettable trip to Pompeii.
