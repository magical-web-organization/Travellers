Afragola, located in Metropolitan Naples, Campania, is a hidden gem in Southern Italy. The city is rich in history and natural beauty, making it an ideal destination for travelers who want to experience the authentic Italian lifestyle. This comprehensive travel guide will give you an overview of the best places to visit, activities to do, food to eat and more in Afragola.

Introduction to Afragola

Afragola is a small city with a population of around 63,000 people. The city has a rich history that dates back to the Roman Empire. The name Afragola comes from the Latin word “Afra,” which means “African land.” It was named so because the Romans used the soil from Afragola to grow crops imported from Africa. Afragola is a unique blend of old and new, with modern cafes and boutiques alongside historic buildings and narrow winding streets.

List of Places to Visit in Afragola

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Palazzo Barlotti

Palazzo Barlotti is a beautiful historic building that dates back to the 17th century. The palace now serves as the headquarters of the Luigi Vanvitelli Foundation. Palazzo Barlotti houses an art museum containing several valuable works that belonged to the Vanvitelli family. Visitors can witness some of the elegant rooms preserved intact with fine frescoes and tapestries.

Castle of Afragola

The Castle of Afragola, also known as Castello Orsini di Afragola or Castello Ducale, is a medieval castle that dates back to the 14th century. The castle is open to the public and offers guided tours where visitors can get an insight into the rich history of the place.

Church of Santa Maria della Pace

The Church of Santa Maria della Pace is an ancient religious site that dates back to the 16th century. The church is home to several notable artworks and sculptures, including the crucifixion painting by Giuseppe Castellano.

Natural Attractions of Afragola: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Monte Somma

Monte Somma is a popular hiking destination for nature enthusiasts in Afragola. The mountain boasts a captivating view of the city, where visitors can have a bird’s-eye view of the surrounding landscape. The top of the mountain has several hiking routes that lead to the crater. To reach the trails’ starting points, go to the town of Somma Vesuviana and follow the signs for the Monte Somma car park.

Piscina Mirabilis

Piscina Mirabilis, located in the Bacoli district, is an ancient Roman cistern that dates back to the 1st century BC. The cistern was built to store freshwater for the Roman Navy. Visitors can witness the vast underground storage of water constructed using the Roman arch and vault technique.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Afragola

Afragola is well-known for its fantastic network of hiking and biking trails that provide visitors with an estimated 300km network of trails around Mount Vesuvius. The trails vary in difficulty and length, with some leading to the top of the volcano. The Monte Somma trail offers a fantastic scenic view of the Afragola city and the Gulf of Naples.

Best Things to Do in Afragola: Activities and Tours

Agriturismo La Morella

Agriturismo La Morella is an ideal location for those looking for a more relaxed experience in Afragola. The farm provides guests with an authentic Italian farming experience, where visitors get to enjoy horse-riding, vegetable picking, nature walks and wine tasting.

Amalfi Coast Tour

A thrilling experience to embark on is the Amalfi Coast tour that provides visitors with an opportunity to hop aboard a boat and discover beautiful Amalfi Coast’s hidden gems. The views are astounding, and the experience is incomparable.

Where to Stay in Afragola: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Hotel Palazzo Caracciolo

A beautiful historic building converted into a luxury hotel providing visitors a taste of modern amenities with a heritage vibe. Rooms are spacious, comfortable and provide a serene atmosphere.

B&B La Casetta di Annie

A more affordable accommodation option is the B&B La Casetta di Annie, offering home-like comforts. It is located in the heart of Afragola, providing easy access to some popular tourist destinations.

What to Buy in Afragola: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Lego Nativity Characters

Lego nativity scenes are a prevalent Christmas tradition in Italy. Several toy shops in Afragola offer unique lego nativity characters that make the perfect souvenir.

Limoncello

Limoncello, a beverage traditionally made from lemons, is a common souvenir in Afragola. Several small markets sell bottled limoncello, making the perfect gift for family or friends back home.

Local Culture and Customs of Afragola

The warm hospitality and pride in local traditions and customs permeate the city’s culture. Residents are passionate about their food, art and history, and have passed traditions down to generations.

Best Time to Visit Afragola

The best time to visit Afragola is in the spring (March - June) or autumn (September - November) when the weather is mild, and tourists crowds are fewer. During these months, the city is not too crowded, and visitors can enjoy attractions without the hassle of crowds.

Safety and Health

Safety in Afragola

Like any other city in Italy, Afragola is considered a safe destination for visitors. However, be aware of pickpockets in crowded tourist areas.

Health in Afragola

Italy’s healthcare system is comprehensive and accessible to all visitors. European entrants are eligible for free medical treatment, while non-European Union visitors must present proof of health insurance for medical emergencies.

Getting to Afragola: Transportation Options and Logistics

Afragola’s location in southern Italy makes it easily accessible from other nearby cities. Here are transportation options and logistics:

  • Naples International Airport (NAP) is the nearest airport to Afragola, 10 km east of the city.

Bus Stations Near Afragola

  • Afragola Rail Station, located on the high-speed railway network, provides visitors with easy access around Italy.

Other Options

How to Get Around in Afragola

Afragola is a small city that can easily be explored on foot or bike. Several bus routes also provide easy transportation within the city as well as to surrounding areas.

Where to Go Next from Afragola: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Naples, a city renowned for its history, food and museums is only 20 km from Afragola.
  • Pompeii, located only 26 km from Afragola, is another ancient Roman city that offers a chance to witness the preserved past.
  • Mount Vesuvius, standing tall at 1,281 meters above sea level, provides visitors with a unique opportunity to witness from afar the majestic volcano that consumed Pompeii.
