Introduction to Calitri

Calitri is a charming little hilltop town located in the Avellino province of the Campania region of Southern Italy. This picturesque town sits atop a hill 800 meters above sea level and is surrounded by beautiful landscapes of olive groves and rolling hills. Calitri has a rich history dating back to the Roman times, evident in the numerous landmarks found throughout the town. This town is a hidden gem, perfect for those looking for an authentic Italian experience off the beaten path.

List of Places to Visit in Calitri

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Calitri is a town steeped in history and culture. There are several important historical attractions that visitors must visit when in Calitri.

1. Castello dei Capitani - A medieval castle built in the 12th century. Today, it features an archaeological museum showcasing the history and culture of Calitri.

2. Museo del Costume e della Tradizione Contadina - A museum dedicated to the local costumes and peasant culture of Calitri. It showcases the rich and diverse heritage of Calitri.

3. Chiesa Madre - The main church of Calitri, built in the 18th century. The architecture and interior design are breathtaking.

Natural Attractions of Calitri: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Calitri is also renowned for its natural beauty. Visitors can enjoy hiking through the lush hillsides, cycling by the Alento river, or simply enjoying the stunning views of the landscape around Calitri.

1. Chiusano di San Domenico - A village nestled in the hills, this location affords a breathtaking view of the surrounding countryside.

2. Monte Fraiteve - The highest peak in Calitri at 1075 meters above sea level, Monte Fraiteve offers panoramic views of the surrounding countryside.

3. Parco Naturale di Monticchio - This nature reserve is the perfect location for nature lovers. Visitors can enjoy walking and hiking through the lush forests and scenic landscapes.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Calitri

Calitri is a paradise for hikers and bikers. Here are some of the popular routes for those who wish to discover the natural beauty of Calitri:

1. Monte Fraiteve Trek - A challenging trek to the highest peak of Calitri. It offers breath-taking views of the surrounding hills and beyond.

2. Alento Cycling Route - A cycling route that follows the Alento river, passing through scenic villages and hillsides.

3. The Natural Trail - A trail that combines the best of Calitri’s natural beauty. It passes through Monte Fraiteve, Parco Naturale di Monticchio and ends at the Alento River.

Best Things to Do in Calitri: Activities and Tours

Calitri offers a range of activities and tours that will interest both nature lovers and culture seekers.

1. Wine Tasting Tour - Visit the local vineyards and taste some of the best wine produced in Calitri.

2. Panoramic Helicopter Tour - Discover the beauty of Calitri from up above with a helicopter tour that showcases the scenic views and landmarks.

3. Guided Tour of Castello dei Capitani and Museo del Costume - Join a guided tour to learn more about the history and culture of Calitri.

Food and Drink Scene of Calitri

Calitri is renowned for its food and drink. Visitors should not miss these culinary jewels:

Must-Try Dishes

1. Strascinati - A type of homemade pasta, usually served with meat, tomato sauce, and Pecorino cheese.

2. Aglianico del Vulture - A full-bodied red wine made from the Aglianico grape that is grown in the Vulture area near Calitri.

3. Salsiccia di Calitri - A type of spicy sausage made from pork, flavored with red pepper flakes.

Local Beverages

1. Limoncello - A sweet lemon-flavored liqueur made from locally grown lemons.

2. Amaro Lucano - A herbal liqueur that has been made in Calitri since 1894.

Where to Stay in Calitri: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Calitri offers a range of accommodation options to suit different preferences and budgets. Here are some recommendations:

Villa Ninfa

A luxurious villa located in the heart of Calitri. Amenities include a swimming pool, a garden, and a restaurant serving local cuisine.

Casa del Capitano

A family-run guesthouse located in the Castello dei Capitani. It features comfortable rooms and an in-house restaurant that serves local delicacies.

La Casa del Nonno

A charming bed and breakfast located in the historic center of Calitri. It offers comfortable rooms and an in-house restaurant that serves typical Italian dishes.

What to Buy in Calitri: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Visitors to Calitri can bring home souvenirs and local products that are unique to the region. Some popular items include:

1. Local wines - Varieties include Fiano di Avellino DOCG and Aglianico del Vulture DOC.

2. Local cheese - Typical varieties include Pecorino and Caciocavallo Podolico.

3. Ceramics from Ceramiche d’Arte - This local shop sells handmade ceramics that are unique to the region.

Local Culture and Customs of Calitri

Calitri is renowned for its cultural heritage and traditions. Visitors should be aware of these customs and respect local culture. Here are some things to keep in mind:

1. Dress Modestly - When visiting churches and religious sites, dress modestly and cover your shoulders and knees.

2. Respect the Siesta - Calitri observes the traditional Italian siesta where shops and businesses close during the afternoon.

3. Be Courteous - Italians value courteous behaviour, so be polite when interacting with locals.

Best Time to Visit Calitri

The best time to visit Calitri is between April and October when the weather is mild and pleasant. However, visitors should be aware that July and August can be quite hot, so plan accordingly.

Do’s and Don’ts of Calitri

Here are some do’s and don’ts to keep in mind during your visit to Calitri:

Do:

  • Try the local cuisine and wine
  • Respect local culture and customs
  • Explore the natural beauty of Calitri

Don’t:

  • Litter or damage historical attractions
  • Touch or disturb the local wildlife
  • Ignore the siesta custom

Safety and Health

Safety in Calitri

Calitri is generally a safe place for visitors. However, it is advisable to take common-sense precautions like not leaving valuables unattended, especially in crowded areas. Visitors should be aware of pickpockets and should not leave their bags unattended.

Health in Calitri

Calitri has a good healthcare system with modern facilities and well-trained medical staff. Visitors should ensure they have adequate health insurance before travelling. Visitors should also ensure they have received all necessary vaccinations before travelling.

Getting to Calitri: Transportation Options and Logistics

Visitors can get to Calitri via several modes of transportation.

Airports Near Calitri

The nearest airports to Calitri are:

  • Naples International Airport
  • Bari Karol Wojtyła Airport

Bus Stations Near Calitri

Several bus companies operate regular schedules to Calitri from major cities such as Naples, Bari, and Rome.

Other Options

Visitors can also drive to Calitri, which offers an opportunity to explore the surrounding areas at their own pace.

How to Get Around in Calitri

Calitri is a small town, and visitors can easily explore the town on foot. However, visitors can also use local taxis or rent bicycles to explore the scenic countryside.

Where to Go Next from Calitri: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Calitri is a perfect base for exploring the beautiful surrounding region of Campania. Some popular destinations include:

1. Matera - A UNESCO World Heritage site famous for its ancient cave dwellings.

2. Pompeii - A historical city famous for its preserved ruins that date back to the Roman empire.

3. Amalfi Coast - A stunning coastline known for its beautiful beaches, cliffside villages and seafood cuisine.

Conclusion

Calitri is a hidden gem located in the heart of Southern Italy. This picturesque town with its rich history, local culture, and stunning landscapes makes it a perfect destination for those seeking an authentic Italian experience. A must-visit for any traveller to Italy.
