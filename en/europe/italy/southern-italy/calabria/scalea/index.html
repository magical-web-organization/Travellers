Introduction to Scalea

Nestled in the heart of Calabria, Scalea is a hidden gem of Southern Italy. Known for its rugged coastline, winding roads, and ancient history, this charming town offers a unique Italian experience that few visitors get to explore.

With a history dating back to the 9th century BC, Scalea offers a blend of ancient architecture, cultural traditions, and stunning natural wonders. Whether you’re an adrenaline junkie looking to explore the great outdoors, a history buff interested in exploring ancient ruins, or simply want to relax on the beach, Scalea has it all.

Here’s a comprehensive guide to exploring the best of Scalea:

List of Places to Visit in Scalea

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Palazzo dei Principi - This magnificent palace is one of Scalea’s most iconic attractions. Built in the 1800s by the noble De Sangro family, the palace features a unique combination of Baroque and Neo-Gothic architecture. Today, it houses the Scalea Museum, where you can explore the town’s rich history and culture.
  • Torre Talao - This medieval watchtower is a reminder of Scalea’s ancient past. Built in the 16th century, it served as a lookout point against pirate attacks. Today, visitors can climb to the top for panoramic views of the town and coastline.
  • Santa Maria del Carmine - This stunning church is a testament to Scalea’s religious heritage. Built in the 16th century, it features intricate frescoes and a beautifully preserved interior.

Natural Attractions of Scalea: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Palazzo storico di Cetraro - One of Scalea’s walking paths leads to this historical palazzo. This site is known for its spectaular sunset view and is among the best attractions in Scalea
  • Isole Eolie - This group of volcanic islands off the coast of Sicily is one of Southern Italy’s most stunning natural wonders. You can take a day trip to the islands from Scalea and explore the stunning volcanic landscapes.
  • Pollino National Park - Located just a short drive from Scalea, this national park boasts some of Italy’s most dramatic scenery. Hike through the rugged mountains, explore the ancient forests, and spot rare wildlife.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Scalea

For adventure seekers, Scalea offers a variety of hiking and biking routes that take you through some of Calabria’s most scenic landscapes. Some of the best routes include:

  • La Masseria - This hiking trail takes you through ancient olive groves, sprawling vineyards, and stunning coastal scenery. It’s an easy, family-friendly hike that’s perfect for all skill levels.
  • Riserva Naturale di Capo Rizzuto - Located just south of Scalea, this nature reserve boasts some of Calabria’s most dramatic scenery. The trail takes you through rocky cliffs, hidden beaches, and rugged terrain.
  • Stretti di Giaredo - This mountain biking trail takes you through some of Scalea’s most stunning scenery. The trail is challenging and is best suited for experienced bikers.

Best Things to Do in Scalea: Activities and Tours

Whether you’re traveling alone, with your family, or with a group of friends, Scalea offers a variety of activities and tours to suit every taste. Some of the best things to do in Scalea include:

  • Kayaking and snorkeling at Arcomagno - Explore the stunning turquoise waters and sea caves of the Arcomagno marine reserve on a kayak or snorkeling tour.
  • Cooking classes - Learn how to cook traditional Calabrian dishes like pasta with sardines, lamb roast, and baked eggplant at one of Scalea’s cooking classes.
  • Wine tasting - Take a tour of one of Calabria’s many vineyards and taste some of Italy’s best wines.

Food and Drink Scene of Scalea

No visit to Scalea is complete without indulging in Italy’s world-famous cuisine. Here are some of the must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Pasta with sardines - One of Calabria’s most popular pasta dishes features fresh sardines, toasted breadcrumbs, and raisins.
  • Lamb roast - This hearty and flavorful dish is a staple of Calabrian cuisine. It’s slow-cooked with herbs and spices until it’s tender and juicy.
  • Baked eggplant - This vegetarian dish features eggplant stuffed with breadcrumbs, parmesan cheese, and tomato sauce.

Local Beverages

  • Limoncello - This sweet and tangy liqueur is made from the freshest lemons grown in Calabria.
  • Ciro Rosso - This deep, full-bodied wine is made from the Gaglioppo grape, which is grown exclusively in Calabria.
  • Nduja - This spicy Calabrian salami is made from pork, pork fat, and Calabrian chili peppers.

Where to Stay in Scalea: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Scalea offers a variety of accommodations to suit every budget and taste. Some of the best options include:

  • La Bruca Resort - This luxury resort offers stunning views of the coast, a swimming pool, and a spa.
  • Bed & Breakfast La Mimosa - This cozy guesthouse is located in the historic center of Scalea and offers comfortable rooms and a warm, welcoming atmosphere.
  • Hostel Scalea - This budget-friendly hostel is perfect for travelers on a tight budget. It features clean, basic rooms, free Wi-Fi, and a communal kitchen.

What to Buy in Scalea: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Bring a piece of Calabria home with you by picking up some of these souvenirs and local products:

  • Ceramic pottery - Calabria is known for its stunning ceramic pottery, which is handmade by local artisans.
  • ‘Nduja - Take home a taste of Calabria with some of the spicy, flavorful ‘nduja salami.
  • Olive oil - Calabria produces some of Italy’s best olive oils, made from the freshest, most flavorful olives.

Local Culture and Customs of Scalea

Like most of Italy, Scalea has a rich cultural heritage that’s reflected in its art, music, and cuisine. Family and tradition are important values in Calabrian life, and you’ll find that many of the town’s customs revolve around these themes.

One of the most popular traditions in Scalea is the Feast of San Rocco, which takes place every August. This religious festival features live music, processions, and feasting.

Best Time to Visit Scalea

The best time to visit Scalea is during the summer months, from June to September. The weather is warm and sunny, and the sea is perfect for swimming and water sports. However, be prepared for crowds and higher prices during this time.

If you’re looking for a quieter, more peaceful vacation, visit in the fall or winter. The off-season is still a great time to explore the town’s ancient ruins, hike through the national parks, and enjoy the local culture and cuisine.

Do’s and Don’ts of Scalea

Do:

  • Do explore the town’s museums and monuments to learn about its rich history and culture.
  • Do try the local Calabrian cuisine, including pasta with sardines and lamb roast.
  • Do take a trip to the Aeolian Islands to experience some of Italy’s most stunning natural beauty.

Don’t:

  • Don’t visit in the summer months if you’re looking for a quieter, more peaceful vacation.
  • Don’t forget to bring comfortable hiking shoes and sunscreen if you plan on exploring the national parks.
  • Don’t hesitate to ask locals for recommendations on the best things to see and do in Scalea.

Safety and Health

Safety in Scalea

Scalea is a safe town for visitors, with a low crime rate and a friendly, welcoming atmosphere. However, be aware of pickpockets in crowded areas and always keep an eye on your belongings.

Health in Scalea

Calabria has a well-developed healthcare system, with modern hospitals and medical facilities. Be sure to take out comprehensive travel insurance before your trip to ensure that you’re covered in case of an emergency.

Getting to Scalea: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Scalea

The nearest airport to Scalea is Lamezia Terme International Airport, located about 120 kilometers north of the town. From the airport, you can take a bus or taxi to Scalea.

Bus Stations Near Scalea

Scalea has a bus station that connects to nearby towns and cities, including Naples and Rome.

Other Options

You can also rent a car and drive to Scalea, which allows you to explore the surrounding area at your own pace.

How to Get Around in Scalea

The best way to explore Scalea is on foot, as the town’s historic center is pedestrian-friendly. However, if you need to travel farther, you can take a bus or taxi.

Where to Go Next from Scalea: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

If you have more time to spend in Calabria, here are some nearby destinations and excursions to consider:

  • Tropea - This picturesque town is known for its stunning beaches, ancient ruins, and charming streets.
  • Reggio Calabria - Explore the town’s museums and monuments, including the world-famous Riace Bronzes.
  • Maratea - This seaside town offers stunning coastal scenery, beautiful beaches, and medieval architecture.

Conclusion

Whether you’re a history buff, an adventure seeker, or simply looking to relax on the beach, Scalea offers something for everyone. With its rich cultural heritage, stunning natural beauty, and warm, welcoming atmosphere, it’s no wonder that this hidden gem of Southern Italy is becoming an increasingly popular destination for travelers from around the world.
