Introduction to Cosenza

Located in the region of Calabria, in southern Italy, Cosenza is a charming city surrounded by hills and mountains that offer breathtaking views. Although it is not as popular as other Italian destinations, Cosenza is a hidden gem that deserves to be on everyone’s itinerary. The city boasts a rich cultural heritage, natural beauty, delicious local cuisine, and welcoming people. This comprehensive guide will help you plan your trip to Cosenza, covering everything from top tourist attractions to safety and health information.

List of Places to Visit in Cosenza

Here are some of the must-see attractions in Cosenza:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • The National Gallery of Cosenza: Located in the historic center of the city, the museum houses an impressive collection of artwork, including paintings, sculptures, and ceramics, from the 13th to the 19th century.

  • The Castle of Cosenza: A medieval fortress that overlooks the city and offers stunning views of the surrounding mountains. The castle also hosts several cultural events throughout the year.

  • The Cathedral of Cosenza: Built in the 11th century, this cathedral features a beautiful baroque facade and houses several impressive artworks, including a painting by the famous Italian artist Caravaggio.

  • The Archeological Museum: A great place to learn about the ancient history of the Calabria region, this museum showcases artifacts from the prehistoric, Greek, and Roman periods.

Natural Attractions of Cosenza: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • The Sila National Park: A vast natural reserve that covers an area of over 700 square miles and is home to several endangered species of animals and plants. Visitors can enjoy hiking, biking, skiing, and other outdoor activities in this beautiful park.

  • The Pollino National Park: Located on the border between Calabria and Basilicata, this park is the largest in Italy and offers stunning views of the mountains and the Mediterranean Sea. Visitors can go hiking, kayaking, and rock climbing in this beautiful natural setting.

  • The Crati River: The longest river in Calabria, the Crati is a great place to go for a scenic walk or picnic. Visitors can also go fishing and kayaking in the river.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Cosenza

Cosenza is a paradise for hikers and bikers, with several trails that offer stunning views of the mountains and the city. Here are a few popular routes:

  • The Sentiero dei Briganti: A challenging hiking trail that takes you through the mountains around Cosenza and offers breathtaking views of the landscape.

  • The Serre Trail: A scenic bike trail that takes you through the Serre mountain range and offers amazing views of the Mediterranean Sea.

  • The Corvo Trail: A popular hiking trail that takes you through dense forests and offers stunning views of the Crati River.

Best Things to Do in Cosenza: Activities and Tours

Apart from hiking and biking, there are several other things to do in Cosenza. Here are a few ideas:

  • Take a cooking class: Learn how to make traditional Calabrian dishes and sample some of the delicious local cuisine.

  • Visit a winery: Calabria is known for its delicious wines, and there are several wineries in the region that offer tours and tastings.

  • Go on a city tour: Explore the historic center of Cosenza and learn about the city’s rich cultural heritage.

  • Attend a festival: Cosenza hosts several festivals throughout the year, including the Jazz Festival, the International Short Film Festival, and the Medieval Festival.

Here is a sample itinerary for a week-long trip to Cosenza:

Food and Drink Scene of Cosenza

Calabria is known for its delicious cuisine, which is based on fresh ingredients and traditional recipes. Here are some of the must-try dishes and local beverages in Cosenza:

Must-Try Dishes

  • ‘Nduja: A spicy spreadable sausage that is a staple of Calabrian cuisine.

  • Caciocavallo cheese: A hard cheese that is often used in local dishes and pairs well with red wine.

  • Calabrian-style pizza: A delicious pizza that is topped with spicy nduja, onions, and cheese.

Local Beverages

  • Cirò wine: A famous red wine that is made from the Gaglioppo grape and pairs well with meat dishes.

  • Liquore alla liquirizia: A licorice-based liqueur that is often enjoyed as a digestif.

  • Amaro del Capo: A bitter herbal liqueur that is often served as an aperitif.

What to Buy in Cosenza: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Here are some of the best souvenirs and local products to buy in Cosenza:

  • Caciocavallo cheese: A delicious local cheese that makes a great gift.

  • Handmade ceramics: Cosenza is known for its beautiful hand-painted ceramics, which make for a unique and colorful souvenir.

  • Calabrian chili flakes: Spicy and flavorful, these chili flakes are a staple of Calabrian cuisine and make a great addition to any dish.

Local Culture and Customs of Cosenza

Cosenza is a friendly and welcoming city, and visitors are encouraged to embrace the local culture and customs. Here are a few tips:

  • Respect the dress code: When visiting churches and other religious sites, be sure to dress modestly and cover your shoulders and knees.

  • Learn some Italian: Although many locals speak English, learning some basic Italian phrases will help you communicate better and show respect for the local culture.

  • Tipping: Tipping is not expected in Italy, but rounding up the bill is a common practice.

Best Time to Visit Cosenza

The best time to visit Cosenza is during the spring and fall, when the weather is pleasant and there are fewer crowds. The summer months can be hot and crowded, while the winter months can be cold and rainy.

Do’s and Don’ts of Cosenza

Here are some do’s and don’ts to keep in mind when visiting Cosenza:

Do:

  • Try the local cuisine: Calabrian food is delicious and worth trying.

  • Respect the local customs: Italians take pride in their culture and customs, so be sure to show respect.

  • Take the train: The train is a convenient and affordable way to travel around Italy.

Don’t:

  • Be loud and disruptive: Italians value peace and quiet, so avoid being loud and disruptive in public places.

  • Forget to validate your train ticket: Train tickets must be validated before boarding, or you risk a fine.

  • Expect everything to be open on Sundays: Many shops and restaurants are closed on Sundays, so plan accordingly.

Safety and Health

Cosenza is a relatively safe city, but it is always important to take precautions when traveling. Here is some safety and health information to keep in mind:

Safety in Cosenza

  • Watch out for pickpockets: As in any other city, pickpocketing can be a problem in Cosenza, especially in tourist areas.

  • Stay in well-lit areas: When walking at night, stick to well-lit areas and avoid quiet and secluded places.

  • Be careful when driving: Italian drivers can be aggressive and unpredictable, so be sure to drive cautiously.

Health in Cosenza

  • Stay hydrated: The summer months can be hot and humid, so be sure to drink plenty of water.

  • Watch out for mosquitoes: Mosquitoes can be a problem in some parts of the city, especially in the summer months. Be sure to use repellant and wear long sleeves and pants.

  • Bring necessary medications: If you have any medical conditions, be sure to bring enough medication for the duration of your trip.

Getting to Cosenza: Transportation Options and Logistics

Cosenza is easy to get to, with several transportation options available.

Airports Near Cosenza

The closest airport to Cosenza is Lamezia Terme International Airport, which is located about 60 miles away. From the airport, you can take a train or a bus to Cosenza.

Bus Stations Near Cosenza

Cosenza has a central bus station, which offers connections to several destinations in Italy and Europe.

Other Options

If you are traveling by car, Cosenza can be reached via the A3 motorway, which connects it to Naples and other cities in southern Italy.

How to Get Around in Cosenza

Cosenza is a relatively small city, and most attractions are within walking distance of each other. However, if you prefer to use public transportation, the city has a reliable bus system that connects all parts of the city.

Where to Go Next from Cosenza: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Cosenza is a great base for exploring the beautiful Calabria region. Here are a few nearby destinations and excursions:

  • Tropea: A picturesque town located on the coast of the Tyrrhenian Sea, famous for its beautiful beaches and historic landmarks.

  • Reggio Calabria: A vibrant city located on the toe of the Italian peninsula, which is home to several museums, beaches, and cultural attractions.

  • Sila National Park: A natural reserve located in the mountains near Cosenza, which offers several hiking and biking trails.

Conclusion

Cosenza is a beautiful and underrated destination in Italy, offering a rich cultural heritage, natural beauty, delicious cuisine, and friendly people. We hope this comprehensive guide has helped you plan your trip to Cosenza and make the most of your time in this charming city.
