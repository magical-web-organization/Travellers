Isca Marina is a small town located on the Ionian Coast of Calabria, in Southern Italy. It is nestled between the mountains and the sea, offering visitors a unique and picturesque landscape. The town is known for its beautiful beaches, traditional cuisine, and friendly locals. Whether you are looking for an outdoor adventure or a relaxing beach holiday, Isca Marina has something to offer.

Introduction to Isca Marina

Isca Marina is a perfect destination for a summer holiday. It is characterized by its crystal-clear waters, turquoise bays, and white sandy beaches. The town is also a hub for cultural and natural landmarks, which makes it an ideal location for those who want to explore the local culture. It is a hidden gem of Calabria, not overrun with tourists, and allows visitors to experience the authentic Italian lifestyle.

List of Places to Visit in Isca Marina

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Isca Marina is a small town but rich with history. The town and the surrounding areas have been inhabited since ancient times due to the fertile land and strategic position. A few places to visit are:

  • The Castle of Isca: A medieval castle that offers a panoramic view of the town. It has now been converted into a museum, showcasing the town’s history.

  • The Church of Santa Maria degli Angeli: A church that dates back to the 16th century, has frescoes, decorations, and statues from the Baroque period. The church also has a bell tower that offers a stunning view of the coastline.

  • Archaeological Park of Scolacium: A Roman settlement located around 30-45 minutes from Isca Marina. The park houses well-preserved ruins of temples, baptismal fonts, and other structures.

Natural Attractions of Isca Marina: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Nature lovers will find plenty to explore in Isca Marina. From lush forests to crystal-clear waters, the town offers a range of outdoor activities, such as:

  • Grotta del Romito: A prehistoric cave with cave paintings, which dates back to about 20,000 years ago. The cave offers a glimpse into the life and art of early humans.

  • La Sila National Park: A beautiful mountainous national park located about 1.5 hours from Isca Marina. The park features forests, lakes, and wildlife, and is a perfect place for hiking, cycling, or picnicking.

  • Piedigrotta Church: A church located on a rock by the sea, named after the Piedigrotta Festival held annually in Naples. The intricate rock carvings and stunning sea views make it a must-visit location.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Isca Marina

Isca Marina and its surrounding areas are a paradise for hikers and bikers. Here are some of the best trails to explore:

  • Sentiero Italia: A trail that extends to over 7,000 km, runs through Isca Marina, offering some paths for those who enjoy long hikes.

  • Sila Mountains: The mountains boast of hiking trails of every difficulty level and are perfect for both beginners and experienced hikers.

  • Cycling Route of Serre: A breathtaking cycling trail that takes you through the Serre Mountains and the Ionian coast.

Best Things to Do in Isca Marina: Activities and Tours

Isca Marina has numerous activities for visitors to experience like:

  • Wine tasting: Calabria is famous for its wines, and there are numerous vineyards located around Isca Marina. Visitors can enjoy wine tasting and learn about the region’s wine-making history.

  • Cooking Classes: Visitors can learn to make local delicacies like pasta or desserts from the expert chefs in Isca Marina.

  • Snorkeling and Scuba Diving: The town offers visitors the chance to explore the rich marine life and crystal-clear waters through scuba diving or snorkeling.

Week-Long Itinerary for Isca Marina

Day 1: Arrival and Check-in Day 2: Explore Isca Marina, visit Santa Maria degli Angeli church and Su Copertino beach. Day 3: Discover the archaeological park of scolacium, grab a bite at “Casetta del Parco”. Day 4: Hike or bike in Sila and have lunch at one of the local trattorie, then visit the village of Camigliatello Silano. Day 5: Visit the Castle of Isca and Beach Hop to the beaches of Mastro and Capogrande. Day 6: Take a trip to the Piedigrotta church and the stunning Tonnara di Palmi beach for sunbathing. Day 7: Shop for souvenirs at the Mercato di Pizzo Calabro!

Food and Drink Scene of Isca Marina

Must-Try Dishes

Isca Marina has a rich culinary culture with dishes that are unique to the region. Here are some must-try dishes:

  • Nduja: A spicy spreadable salami with a unique taste and texture that is typically served on bread.

  • Fileja: A local handmade pasta that is served with various sauces. It is usually made with flour and water, and some variations incorporate hot water or red wine.

  • Melanzane a funghetto: A traditional dish of sautéed eggplant with garlic and fresh herbs.

Local Beverages

Calabria is known for its wines, particularly red wine. The most famous wines in the region are:

  • Cirò: A red wine produced in the town of Cirò in Calabria, with a long history dating back to the ancient Greeks.

  • Greco di Bianco: A white wine produced in the town of Bianco in Calabria, made from the Greco Bianco grape variety.

  • Liquore Strega: A herbal liqueur produced in the town of Benevento.

Where to Stay in Isca Marina: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Isca Marina’s accommodations offer guests a quintessential Italian experience, ranging from cozy bed and breakfasts to luxurious hotels. Here are some excellent options:

  • Marechiaro Hotel: This hotel is located right on the sea and is known for its excellent customer service and delicious food.

  • La Felce Imperial Hotel: This hotel is located in the heart of the Sila mountains and offers beautiful views and a spa facility.

  • B&B A Casa di Ida: A cozy bed and breakfast located in the heart of Isca Marina, known for its great service and cleanliness.

What to Buy in Isca Marina: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Visitors to Isca Marina can bring back some delightful souvenirs and local products, such as:

  • Handmade Ceramics: Calabria is well-known for its ceramics and pottery, with many shops selling handmade pieces.

  • Spices and Herbs: Calabria is known for its blend of herbs and spices, so look for shops selling local blends.

  • Nduja: While you can buy nduja in many stores in Italy, the nduja produced in Calabria is considered the best and is a popular souvenir.

Local Culture and Customs of Isca Marina

The people of Calabria and Isca Marina are known for their hospitality and friendliness. Visitors to Isca Marina should be respectful of the locals’ way of life, dress appropriately while visiting churches and historical sites, and also try to learn a few Italian words if they’re not already familiar with the language.

Best Time to Visit Isca Marina

The best time to visit Isca Marina is during the summer months, from June to September. During this period, the weather is warm, and there are numerous outdoor recreational activities and events.

Do’s and Don’ts of Isca Marina

Do:

  • Respect the local culture and customs.
  • Dress appropriately while visiting churches and historical sites.
  • Try the local cuisine and wines.

Don’t:

  • Litter in public areas or on the beaches.
  • Be disrespectful to the locals or other tourists.
  • Leave your valuables unattended on the beach.

Safety and Health

Safety in Isca Marina

Isca Marina is a safe place to visit. Visitors should take the usual precautions, such as keeping an eye on their belongings and not going to remote areas at night.

Health in Isca Marina

There are no major health risks in Isca Marina. Visitors should pack sunscreen, insect repellent, and any necessary medications.

Getting to Isca Marina: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Isca Marina

The nearest airports to Isca Marina are Lamezia Terme International Airport (SUF), located approximately 70 km away, and Catania-Fontanarossa Airport (CTA), located approximately 190 km away.

Bus Stations Near Isca Marina

There is a bus station in Isca Marina that connects the town to other nearby areas and towns in Calabria.

Other Options

Visitors can also travel to Isca Marina by train or renting a car.

How to Get Around in Isca Marina

The best way to get around in Isca Marina is by car, as there is a limited bus service in the town. Visitors can also rent a scooter or bicycle.

Where to Go Next from Isca Marina: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Isca Marina is located in the region of Calabria, which is rich in history, culture, and natural beauty. Some nearby destinations to visit include:

  • Tropea: A popular tourist destination, located about an hour’s drive from Isca Marina, famous for its beautiful beaches and historic landmarks.

  • Reggio Calabria: A buzzing coastal city located around 110 km from Isca Marina and is famous for the Riace Bronzes and its waterfront promenade.

  • Scilla: A seaside town located around 30 km from Isca Marina, known for its stunning beaches, quaint fishing villages, and historic castle.

Conclusion

Isca Marina is a fantastic destination for those wanting to explore Southern Italy. Visitors can soak up the sun on beautiful beaches or take an adventure through the natural wonders of the town. Isca Marina is a small town that packs a punch, with all that it offers, making it a perfect place to take your family or friends to explore the less popular part of Italy.
