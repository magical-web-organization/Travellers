Catanzaro is a charming city located in the Calabria region of Southern Italy. If you’re searching for a destination that offers a perfect blend of history, culture, and scenic beauty, Catanzaro is the place to be. With its picturesque landscapes, rich cultural heritage, and mouthwatering cuisine, you’re sure to have an unforgettable experience in this hidden gem of Italy. In this guide, we’ll delve into Catanzaro’s best places to visit, things to do, and local culture and customs, as well as providing practical information on transportation, accommodation, safety, and health.

Introduction to Catanzaro

Catanzaro is the capital city of the Calabria region and is perched on a hilltop overlooking the Gulf of Squillace. The city has a history dating back to the 7th century BC and is dotted with ancient monuments and historical landmarks that bear witness to its rich cultural heritage. The city is known for its stunning views, particularly from the hilltop district of Catanzaro Lido.

List of Places to Visit in Catanzaro

Catanzaro offers a wide variety of places to visit, from museums and historical sites to natural landscapes and outdoor adventures.

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  1. Catanzaro Cathedral: The stunning cathedral located in the center of Catanzaro’s old town is a must-visit destination. With its beautiful Baroque architecture, ancient artwork, and a breathtaking view over the city, the cathedral will leave you in awe.

  2. Villa Trieste: This historic villa is located in the heart of Catanzaro and is a beautiful example of Italian architecture dating back to the early 20th century. Now serving as a museum, Villa Trieste is home to a range of ancient artifacts, including Roman sculptures and ceramics.

  3. Museo delle Arti di Catanzaro: For art enthusiasts, this museum is a must-see. The museum displays a collection of contemporary art from artists from Calabria and around the world.

Natural Attractions of Catanzaro: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  1. Sila National Park: This park is a natural oasis in the heart of Calabria that offers a unique combination of natural beauty and outdoor adventure. This National Park is surrounded by stunning mountains and lakes, making it an ideal destination for hiking and trekking.

  2. Catanzaro Lido Beach: If you’re looking for some R&R, check out the Catanzaro Lido Beach. This beautiful, tranquil beach is located in the middle of a natural reserve, bringing together the best of both worlds - the sea and breathtaking scenery.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Catanzaro

  1. Sila National Park - Monte Botte Donato: This hiking route is the ideal blend of stunning views and challenging terrain. The route takes you to the top of Monte Botte Donato, rising to over 1900 meters, accompanied by scenic beauty and spectacular views.

  2. Catanzaro Trails: The Catanzaro trail is a network of mountain biking trails that traverse the hills and mountains surrounding the city. The trail offers stunning views of the landscape, as well as the opportunity to experience firsthand the unique flora and fauna of the region.

Best Things to Do in Catanzaro: Activities and Tours

  1. Food tour: What better way to experience the local culture than through its food? A food tour of Catanzaro can immerse you in cooking techniques, tastes, and flavors specific to Southern Italian cuisine.

  2. Center of Photography: This cultural center hosts rotating exhibitions, installations, and workshops, highlighting photography and film, with a focus on works that delve into social, environmental, and political themes.

Week-Long Itinerary for Catanzaro

If you’re planning to visit Catanzaro for a week, here’s an itinerary that will make the most of your time:

Day 1: Visit Catanzaro’s old town area, focusing on its rich cultural history Day 2: Excursion to Sila National Park and its neighboring lakes for hiking and trekking Day 3: Explore the charming seaside town of Catanzaro Lido and spend time at the beach. Day 4: Visit the Museo delle Arti di Catanzaro Day 5: Visit the Villa Trieste Day 6: Center of Photography, Catanzaro Cathedral, and shopping Day 7: Food tour of the local cuisine and cooking classes

Food and Drink Scene of Catanzaro

Catanzaro cuisine is reflective of Southern Italian culinary culture, with simple flavorful dishes, locally grown produce, and seafood being the highlights. Here are some traditional dishes and beverages you must try when in Catanzaro.

Must-Try Dishes

  1. Calabrian Spicy Sausage: This sausage is made with a mixture of beef and pork, garlic, Calabrian chilies, and other local spices, giving it a unique flavor.

  2. Nduja: Another Calabrian specialty, nduja is a type of spicy cured meat spread made from pork and chili peppers.

Local Beverages

  1. Greco di Bianco: This white wine is produced in the region of Bianco and is a perfect accompaniment to seafood dishes.

  2. Amaro del Capo: This is a classic Calabrian bitter liqueur made up of a blend of more than 29 herbs, roots, and citrus fruits, giving it a unique, distinct flavor.

Where to Stay in Catanzaro: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Catanzaro has a range of accommodation options, spanning from luxury hotels to guesthouses and B&Bs, providing something for every budget. Places to stay include:

  1. Historic Hotel Le Sirene: This hotel is located in the heart of Catanzaro, with views over the Gulf of Squillace, offering comfortable rooms and excellent views.

  2. B&B “Le Casette” di Loretta: This guesthouse is located near the old town area of Catanzaro and offers beautiful rooms immersed in history.

What to Buy in Catanzaro: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

If you’re looking for souvenirs to bring back home, Catanzaro has a range of options, including:

  1. Bagnara Calabra’s coral jewelry: The Calabrian coral jewelry is a classic souvenir from Catanzaro, with a range of unique, handmade jewelry to browse.

  2. Scilla-based lighthouse-shaped lamps: These lamps are a unique, practical souvenir, symbolizing the red lighthouse in the Scilla Village.

Local Culture and Customs of Catanzaro

The Calabrian culture is known for its warm hospitality and sense of community. Some of its finest traditions include the local dialect, known as Calabrese, which has its roots in the ancient Greek language and its unique cuisine, centered around local ingredients and flavors.

Best Time to Visit Catanzaro

The best time to visit Catanzaro is in the summer, between June and September when the weather is warm and the skies are clear. This is also the peak tourist season when the city is bustling with activity and there are various outdoor events and festivals to enjoy.

Do’s and Don’ts of Catanzaro

Do:

  1. Respect local customs and traditions and dress modestly when visiting churches and other holy places.

  2. Try the local cuisine and the traditional dishes of Catanzaro.

Don’t:

  1. Drink tap water, which is not considered safe.

  2. Disrespect the environment by littering or engaging in other destructive activities.

Safety and Health

Safety in Catanzaro

Catanzaro is generally a safe city and is safe to walk and explore, but travelers should still take standard safety precautions, such as keeping their possessions secure and avoiding unlit streets at night.

Health in Catanzaro

It is advisable to have travel insurance that includes medical coverage before traveling to Catanzaro. You can also consult a medical practitioner before traveling to seek recommendations and advice.

Getting to Catanzaro: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Catanzaro

The nearest airport to Catanzaro is the Lamezia Terme International Airport, which is approximately 35 miles away from the city. From the airport, you can take a train or a taxi to reach Catanzaro.

Bus Stations Near Catanzaro

Catanzaro has a central bus service for travelers that connect the city with the neighboring towns and cities.

Other Options

Traveling by car or renting a car within the Catanzaro region can give visitors more flexibility and the chance to explore more remote parts of the region.

How to Get Around in Catanzaro

The best way to get around Catanzaro is by foot or by using the city’s public transportation, including buses and trains. Taxis are also available for hire.

Where to Go Next from Catanzaro: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Many nearby destinations are worth a visit while exploring Catanzaro, including the charming coastal town of Tropea, the seaside resort of Soverato, and the historic town of Reggio Calabria.

Conclusion

With its stunning landscapes, rich heritage, and mouthwatering cuisine, Catanzaro offers visitors a truly unforgettable experience. Whether you’re a history buff or an outdoor enthusiast, it has something for everyone. Use this comprehensive guide to plan your trip and explore the best of Southern Italy in Catanzaro. So, pack your bags, get ready to explore, and make memories that will last a lifetime.
