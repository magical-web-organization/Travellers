Perugia is a picturesque city located in the heart of Italy’s Umbria region. This charming city is known for its rich history, stunning natural scenery, and mouthwatering cuisine. Whether you’re a history buff, nature lover, or food enthusiast, visiting Perugia is a must. In this comprehensive guide, we’ll cover all the necessary information, including must-visit places, local culture, food and drink scene, transportation options, and more. Let’s get started!

Introduction to Perugia

Perugia is a city in the Umbria region of central Italy. It is the capital of the province of Perugia and home to several universities, making it a bustling, lively city with much to see and do. Perugia is situated on a hill, offering sweeping views of the surrounding countryside.

The city’s history dates back to the Etruscan civilization and has since been shaped by various rulers, including the Romans, Papal States, and the Kingdom of Italy. Perugia has a wealth of historic monuments and architecture that reflects its rich past.

List of Places to Visit in Perugia

Perugia is home to a wide range of cultural and natural attractions, which make it a perfect destination for travelers.

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Perugia has plenty of museums and historical places that reflect the city’s long history and rich cultural heritage. Here are some must-visit places:

  • Rocca Paolina: A fortress built in the 16th century by Pope Paul III. It was destroyed by the citizens of Perugia following a revolt against papal rule. Today, you can explore the subterranean tunnels that were once used to move troops and goods under the Rocca.

  • Perugina Chocolate Factory: Learn about the history of chocolate in Perugia and discover how Perugina chocolates are made. Of course, there’s also a chance to taste some of the delicious products on offer.

  • Piazza IV Novembre: The heart of Perugia, the main square and is surrounded by the city’s most important buildings, including the Fontana Maggiore (the city’s main fountain), Palazzo dei Priori, and the Cathedral of San Lorenzo.

Natural Attractions of Perugia: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Perugia is also an excellent destination for nature lovers. The city is set amidst a stunning landscape that offers many opportunities for hiking, walking, and cycling.

  • Monte Subasio: This mountain is the highest peak in the region and has a variety of trails suitable for hikers and mountain bikers. On a clear day, you can see as far as Assisi and the Apennine Mountains in the distance.

  • Lake Trasimeno: One of the largest lakes in Italy, Lake Trasimeno is a popular destination for water sports activities like swimming, fishing, and canoeing.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Perugia

Perugia is a perfect destination for those who love outdoor activities like hiking and cycling. Here are some of the best routes you can take:

  • Eremo delle Carceri: A 7km hike through the Monte Subasio Natural Park, which takes you to the Hermitage of the Prisons. Along the way, you’ll be treated to stunning views of the mountains and valleys.

  • Assisi to Spello: A 22km cycling trip that takes you through ancient villages, vineyards, and olive groves.

Best Things to Do in Perugia: Activities and Tours

Perugia has plenty of activities and tours to offer visitors, and here are some of the best:

  • Learn to cook traditional Umbrian dishes: Cooking classes are an excellent way to learn about the local cuisine and culture.

  • Guided tours of the city: A walking tour of Perugia’s historical centre is an excellent way to learn about the city’s history and culture.

Week-Long Itinerary for Perugia

If you’re planning a longer stay in Perugia, here’s a week-long itinerary to help you make the most of your time:

  • Day 1: Explore the city’s historical centre, including Piazza IV Novembre, the Fontana Maggiore, and Perugina Chocolate Factory.

  • Day 2: Take a guided tour of the city’s museums and historical places, including Rocca Paolina.

  • Day 3: Explore the natural attractions of Perugia, such as Lake Trasimeno.

  • Day 4: Take a cooking class and learn about traditional Umbrian cuisine.

  • Day 5: Take a day trip to Assisi and explore the town’s religious and historical sites.

  • Day 6: Enjoy a day of hiking or cycling on Monte Subasio.

  • Day 7: Relax and enjoy the local culture, food, and drink scene.

Food and Drink Scene of Perugia

Perugia is famous for its fantastic food and drink scene. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages to taste:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Torta al Testo: A flatbread usually filled with cheese, vegetables, or meat, and is a popular street food in Perugia.

  • Porchetta: Roasted pork stuffed with herbs and spices, a typical Umbrian dish.

Local Beverages

  • Sagrantino di Montefalco: A full-bodied red wine produced in the Montefalco area of Umbria.

  • Perugina Chocolate: A local specialty, you can try different flavors at the Perugina Chocolate Factory.

Where to Stay in Perugia: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Perugia has plenty of accommodation options suitable for all budgets and travel styles. Here are some top choices:

  • The Nun Assisi Relais & Spa Museum: A 5-star hotel housed in a 1700s convent in the heart of Assisi.

  • Hotel La Rosetta: Located in the heart of Perugia’s city center.

  • Ostello di Perugia Centro: A budget-friendly hostel in the historical center of Perugia.

What to Buy in Perugia: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Perugia has many unique souvenirs and local products that make perfect gifts or keepsakes. Here are some top choices:

  • Ceramics: The Umbrian region is famous for its colorful ceramic plates and bowls.

  • Umbrian Wines: Look for wines from the Montefalco and Torgiano areas.

Local Culture and Customs of Perugia

Perugia has its unique culture and customs shaped by its long and rich history. Some of the customs and traditions to look out for include the Perugia Jazz Festival, which takes place every July.

Best Time to Visit Perugia

The best time to visit Perugia is between April and June or September and October. The weather is mild, and there are fewer crowds during these months.

Do’s and Don’ts of Perugia

Do:

  • Try the local cuisine and drinks, including Umbrian wines, Porchetta, and Sagrantino di Montefalco.

  • Explore the city’s historical sites and museums.

Don’t:

  • Avoid traveling during the peak season (July and August) if possible, as the city can get crowded.

  • Don’t forget to carry sunscreen and a hat during the summer months.

Safety and Health

Safety in Perugia

Perugia is a relatively safe city for travelers. However, it’s always a good idea to be aware of your surroundings and take necessary precautions to avoid thefts or pickpocketing.

Health in Perugia

There are several hospitals and health clinics in Perugia. Travelers are advised to buy travel insurance to cover any medical expenses.

Getting to Perugia: Transportation Options and Logistics

Perugia is easily accessible via several transportation options, including:

Airports Near Perugia

  • Perugia International Airport (PEG)

  • Umbria International Airport (RNO)

Bus Stations Near Perugia

  • Perugia Bus Terminal

Other Options

  • Renting a car is a good option for exploring the surrounding countryside.

How to Get Around in Perugia

Perugia is a relatively small city and is easily explored on foot. However, you can also use public transport, including buses and taxis.

Where to Go Next from Perugia: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Perugia is centrally located, making it an excellent starting point for exploring the surrounding region. Some nearby destinations and excursions to consider include Assisi, Montefalco, and Spello.

Conclusion

Perugia is a charming and picturesque city with much to offer travelers. From its rich history and cultural heritage to its stunning natural scenery and food and drink scene, Perugia is an excellent destination for all kinds of travelers. Plan your itinerary with our comprehensive guide and get ready to experience the magic of Perugia.
