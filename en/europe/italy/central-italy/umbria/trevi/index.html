Are you dreaming of a countryside escape with scenic views, a rich history, and a vibrant cultural scene? Then look no further than Trevi – a charming town tucked away in the heart of Umbria, Italy. With captivating landscapes, stunning historic buildings, and a buzzing local food scene, Trevi is a perfect destination for any traveler looking to immerse themselves in the authentic Italian lifestyle.

In this comprehensive Trevi travel guide, we’ll take you through all the key places to visit, the best things to do, and all the practical information you need to create your itinerary, including transportation, accommodation, food and drinks, and local customs. Let’s dive in!

Introduction to Trevi

Nestled on a hilltop overlooking the Umbrian Valley, Trevi is a historic town in Italy’s central region that offers both a peaceful getaway and an immersive cultural experience. It boasts a rich history that dates back to the Roman era, with several churches, palaces, and historic buildings that are a testament to its past. Today, the town is renowned for its unspoiled natural scenery, artisanal food and wines, and warm hospitality, which make it a perfect base for exploring Umbria and beyond.

List of Places to Visit in Trevi

Trevi offers a rich variety of places to visit and attractions to explore, ranging from stunning natural landscapes to captivating historic landmarks and museums. Here’s our pick of must-visit places to add to your itinerary:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Trevi Cathedral: Dedicated to the town’s patron Saint Emilianus, the cathedral is a beautiful example of Romanesque architecture and houses several precious artworks and artifacts.
  • Palazzo Lucarini Contemporary: One of the most interesting cultural attractions in Trevi, this museum features contemporary art exhibitions and installations by emerging and established artists from Italy and beyond.
  • Palazzo Valenti Gonzaga: This magnificent palace is an excellent example of Baroque architecture and hosts a permanent collection of artworks and furnishings from the 17th and 18th centuries.

Natural Attractions of Trevi: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Monte Serano: A majestic peak that offers stunning views of the Umbrian Valley and the surrounding hills, Monte Serano is a popular hiking and climbing destination for nature lovers.
  • Fonti del Clitunno: Known as the Umbrian River of Poetry, this natural spring is a charming spot that inspired poets and artists throughout the centuries.
  • Nature Reserve of Colfiorito: A protected area of outstanding beauty, the Nature Reserve of Colfiorito is a must-visit destination for birdwatchers, hikers, and nature enthusiasts.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Trevi

Trevi is a paradise for outdoor enthusiasts who enjoy hiking, walking or cycling while admiring the beautiful scenery of the Umbrian Valley. Here are some of the top routes to explore:

  • The Trevi - Spoleto route: A picturesque trail that connects the two towns over 34 kilometers of hills, bridges, and ancient ruins.
  • The Monte Bove south route: A challenging yet rewarding hike that takes you to the top of Monte Bove, the highest peak in the area, and offers breathtaking panoramic views.
  • The Chiascio Valley cycling route: A leisurely ride along the Chiascio River, crossing charming villages and rolling hills in a relaxed pace.

Best Things to Do in Trevi: Activities and Tours

Whether you’re a history buff, a foodie, or a thrill-seeker, Trevi has plenty of activities and tours to offer. Here are some of the best ways to explore the town:

  • Food and Wine Tour: Join a guided tour to discover the local culinary scene, taste the region’s delicacies, and learn about the history and traditions of Umbrian cuisine.
  • Wine tasting in the Sagrantino region: One of the most famous wine regions in Italy, the Sagrantino area offers wine tastings and vineyard tours that will delight any wine lover.
  • Explore the town on a Segway: A fun and innovative way to explore Trevi, join a Segway tour that takes you through the town’s historical center and scenic countryside.

Week-Long Itinerary for Trevi

Trevi is a perfect destination for a week-long break, allowing you to explore the best of Umbria and beyond. Here’s a suggested itinerary to make the most of your trip:

Day 1: Arrive in Trevi, visit the Cathedral and historical center, and have dinner at a local restaurant.

Day 2: Explore the Nature Reserve of Colfiorito and have a picnic lunch with local delicacies.

Day 3: Take a guided tour of the Palazzo Valenti Gonzaga and then enjoy a wine tasting session in the Sagrantino region.

Day 4: Travel to Assisi, the birthplace of St Francis, and explore its historical landmarks, including the Basilica of San Francesco.

Day 5: Visit the Perugina Chocolate Factory and taste the famous Baci chocolate. Spend the afternoon shops in Perugia.

Day 6: Hike the Monte Serano trail and watch the sunset over the Umbrian Valley.

Day 7: Depart from Trevi or stay an extra day to explore the beautiful town of Spoleto.

Food and Drink Scene of Trevi

Umbria is renowned for its artisanal food and wine scene, and Trevi is no exception. Here are some of the must-try local dishes and beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Strangozzi alla Spoletina: A classic Umbrian pasta dish made with handmade long pasta, a savory sauce of tomatoes, anchovies, garlic, and chili peppers, and sprinkled with grated Pecorino cheese.
  • Cinghiale in umido: A slow-cooked stew made with tender boar meat, herbs, red wine, and chestnuts, served with polenta or rustic bread.
  • Torta al testo: A flatbread made with flour, roasted pork fat, and cooked in a traditional cast iron pan, often served with cured meats and cheeses.

Local Beverages

  • Sagrantino di Montefalco: A robust and full-bodied red wine made with Sagrantino grapes, one of the rarest and most ancient varieties in Italy.
  • Grechetto di Umbria: A crisp and acidic white wine made from the Grechetto grape, traditionally paired with fish and seafood dishes.
  • Limoncello: A sweet and tangy liqueur made from locally grown lemons, perfect for sipping after dinner.

Where to Stay in Trevi: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Trevi offers a wide range of accommodation options, from luxury hotels to guesthouses and charming bed and breakfasts. Here are some of the top-rated ones:

Luxury Hotels

  • Hotel Le Mura: A beautifully restored 18th-century palace turned into a hotel, offering elegant rooms, a bar, and a spa.
  • Palazzo Seneca: A family-owned historic property featuring a fine-dining restaurant, a wellness center, and luxurious suites decorated in a classic Italian style.

Mid-Range Hotels

  • Il Castello di Rosceto: A charming stone castle surrounded by a green park, offering comfortable rooms and outdoor pool.
  • Agriturismo Flavia Constans: A rustic farmhouse tucked away in the countryside offering cozy apartments and a pool, plus free bike rentals for guests.

Budget Options

  • Casa Giulia: A cozy and affordable guesthouse located in the heart of the historical center offering comfortable rooms and free Wi-Fi.
  • Il Prato: A budget-friendly B&B featuring a garden terrace, rustic rooms, and a shared kitchen.

What to Buy in Trevi: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

If you’re looking for a unique souvenir or gift to remember your trip to Trevi, here are some of the best local products to bring home:

  • Olive oil: Buy a bottle of extra virgin olive oil, made from the region’s iconic Umbrian olive groves, known for their fruity and aromatic flavors.
  • Ceramics: Trevi is famous for its pottery and rocchetti, the spindle used for spinning wool. You can find beautiful ceramics and pottery in the local shops and markets.
  • Truffle products: Umbria is the land of truffles, and you’ll find plenty of places to buy truffle oil, truffle paste, or fresh truffles in Trevi’s markets.

Local Culture and Customs of Trevi

Trevi boasts a strong sense of community and a rich cultural heritage that reflects in its local festivals, events, and traditions. Here are some things to know before you go:

  • Dress code: In Italy, it’s customary to dress modestly and conservatively when visiting churches and other religious places. Wearing shorts, sleeveless shirts, or revealing clothes may not be allowed.
  • Tipping: Tipping in Italy is not mandatory, but a service charge may be included in your bill. If you receive excellent service, you can leave a small tip, rounding up the total amount.
  • Respect local customs: Italians value family, friendship, and hospitality, and it’s important to show respect for their culture and traditions. Learning a few words of Italian and greeting locals with a smile can go a long way in making your stay more enjoyable.

Best Time to Visit Trevi

The best time to visit Trevi is in the spring or the fall when the weather is mild, and the crowds are fewer. During these seasons, you can enjoy the town’s outdoor activities, such as hiking, sightseeing, and wine tasting, without the hassle of long lines or peak-season prices. If you’re visiting in the summer, be prepared for hot temperatures and a lively cultural scene, as many local festivals and events take place during this period.

Do’s and Don’ts of Trevi

To make your trip to Trevi more enjoyable, here are some do’s and don’ts to keep in mind:

Do:

  • Try the local cuisine and wines, especially Sagrantino and Grechetto.
  • Visit the local markets to buy fresh produce, souvenirs, and artisanal products.
  • Learn a few words of Italian and interact with locals.

Don’t:

  • Leave your valuables unattended on the beach or in crowded places.
  • Forget to dress modestly when visiting churches or other religious places.
  • Smoke in public parks or other non-smoking areas.

Safety and Health

Trevi is considered a safe destination, but as with any place, it’s important to take some precautions to ensure your safety and health during your trip. Here are some tips:

Safety in Trevi

  • Be aware of pickpockets in busy areas and avoid carrying large sums of cash.
  • Use only authorized taxis or transportation services.
  • Don’t leave your belongings unattended in public places.

Health in Trevi

  • Pack insect repellent, especially if you plan to hike in the countryside.
  • Stay hydrated during hot weather and use sunscreen to avoid sunburns.
  • Make sure you have travel insurance that covers medical emergencies and accidents.

Getting to Trevi: Transportation Options and Logistics

Trevi is located in the heart of Umbria, just a few hours away from major cities like Rome, Florence, and Perugia. Here’s how you can get to Trevi:

Airports Near Trevi

  • Perugia San Francesco d’Assisi Airport (PEG): Located 40 kilometers south of Trevi, it’s served by several airlines, including Ryanair and Wizz Air.
  • Rome Fiumicino Airport (FCO): The closest international airport to Trevi, it’s located about 160 kilometers south-west of the town, and offers direct connections to major cities in Europe and the world.

Bus Stations Near Trevi

  • Bus Italia: Provides frequent connections to and from Trevi to nearby towns and cities, including Perugia, Assisi, and Spoleto.
  • Umbria Mobilità: Offers bus connections to and from Trevi to major tourist destinations in Umbria, Tuscany, and Marche regions.

Other Options

  • Train: The closest train station to Trevi is Foligno, which is located about 12 kilometers away from the town. From there, you can take a bus or a taxi to reach Trevi.
  • Car: If you’re planning a road trip in Italy or renting a car, Trevi is easily reachable by car from the major highways A1 and E45, and offers plenty of parking options in the town center.

How to Get Around in Trevi

The best way to get around Trevi is on foot, as the town’s historical center is small and easily accessible on foot. However, if you need to go to other parts of the town, or if you’d like to explore the surrounding countryside, you can use the following transportation options:

  • Bus: Trevi is well-connected to nearby towns and villages through a network of local and regional buses.
  • Taxi: Taxis are available in the town center, and can also be booked in advance through local taxi companies.
  • Car rental: If you prefer to explore the area at your own pace, you can rent a car in Trevi or in nearby towns and cities.

Where to Go Next from Trevi: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Trevi is a perfect base for exploring Umbria and beyond, offering easy access to some of the most beautiful destinations in Italy. Here are some places you can visit from Trevi:

  • Assisi: A beautiful hilltop town famous for its rich spiritual heritage, charming streets, and religious landmarks, including the Basilica of San Francesco.
  • Spello: A picturesque medieval hill town known for its beautiful flower-filled streets, ancient Roman ruins, and scenic countryside views.
  • Montefalco: One of the most picturesque towns in Italy, Montefalco is famous for its Sagrantino wine, charming streets, and stunning views of the Umbrian Valley.

Conclusion

Trevi is a hidden gem in Umbria, a charming town with a rich history, delightful cuisine, and breathtaking natural landscapes. Whether you’re a seasoned traveler or a first-time visitor, Trevi has something for everyone, from cultural landmarks and outdoor activities to romantic getaways and family-friendly holidays. We hope our Trevi travel guide will help you plan a memorable trip to this beautiful corner of Italy, full of hidden surprises and unforgettable experiences. Buon viaggio!
