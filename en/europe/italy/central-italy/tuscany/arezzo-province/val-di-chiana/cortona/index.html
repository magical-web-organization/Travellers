Introduction to Cortona

Cortona, situated in Val di Chiana in Arezzo province of Tuscany, is a beautiful and charming hilltop town known for its rich history, stunning scenery, and vibrant culture. This ancient Etruscan city has managed to preserve its medieval atmosphere, charming streets, and beautiful architecture. Famous for its picturesque landscape, vineyards, and olive groves, Cortona is an ideal place to experience Tuscany’s rural charm.

Here are some must-visit places in Cortona:

Hiking/Biking Routes of Cortona

Exploring Cortona on foot or bike is the best way to explore its beautiful countryside, vineyards, and olive groves. Here are some popular hiking and biking routes in Cortona:

  • The Etruscan Trail: 9 km hike around Cortona that explores the city’s ancient Etruscan heritage.
  • The Trail of Franciscan Saints: 22 km trail that takes you to a series of chapels and holy sites associated with St. Francis of Assisi.
  • Cortona Ring: 57 km biking trail that takes you through the hills of Cortona, passing through enchanting medieval villages and hamlets.

Best Things to Do in Cortona: Activities and Tours

Cortona offers a wide range of activities and tours for its visitors. Here are some of the best things to do in Cortona:

  • Wine Tours and Tasting: Explore the picturesque vineyards and olive groves and taste local Tuscan wines and olive oils.
  • Cooking Classes: Take a cooking class to learn the art of Tuscan cooking, using fresh and seasonal local ingredients.
  • Renaissance Painting Classes: Learn painting techniques from professional artists in a class that focuses on the Renaissance period.
  • Hot Air Balloon Rides: Get an aerial view of Cortona’s beautiful landscape on a hot air balloon ride.
  • Shopping: Shop for souvenirs, pottery, and handicrafts at the local markets and boutiques.

Week-Long Itinerary for Cortona

Day 1: Explore Cortona’s Historical Center and Visit the City’s Museums.

Day 2: Take a Wine Tour and Olive Oil Tasting in Cortona’s Vineyards.

Day 3: Visit Lake Trasimeno and its Scenic Surroundings.

Day 4: Take a Cooking Class to Learn the Art of Tuscan Cooking.

Day 5: Visit Some of the Nearby Hill Towns such as Montepulciano, Pienza, or Siena.

Day 6: Go on a Hot Air Balloon Ride or Hike one of Cortona’s Scenic Trails.

Day 7: Relax and Enjoy Cortona’s Local Food and Drink Scene.

Food and Drink Scene of Cortona

Cortona’s food scene is known for its rustic and authentic Tuscan cuisine, using only fresh and seasonal ingredients. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages in Cortona:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Bistecca alla Fiorentina: Grilled Florence-style steak served with rosemary, olive oil, and lemon.
  • Pappardelle with Wild Boar Ragù: Broad, flat pasta served with a rich and hearty tomato-based sauce of wild boar meat.
  • Fettunta: Grilled or toasted bread rubbed with garlic and drizzled with extra virgin olive oil.

Local Beverages

  • Chianti: The famous Tuscan red wine.
  • Vin Santo: A sweet dessert wine made from dried grapes.
  • Grappa: A strong and aromatized liquor made from distilling grape pomace.

What to Buy in Cortona: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Cortona is famous for its local handicrafts and artisanal products. Here are some popular souvenirs and local products to bring home:

  • Pottery and Ceramic: Cortona is famous for its handmade pottery and ceramic that you can find in local boutiques and markets.
  • Leather Products: Tuscany is known for its exquisite leather products, from bags to belts and shoes.
  • Olive Oil: Tuscan olive oil is considered among the best in the world, so don’t forget to stock up on some to bring back home.
  • Wine: Cortona is a famous wine region, so try some local wine, such as Chianti or Vino Nobile di Montepulciano.

Local Culture and Customs of Cortona

Cortona’s local culture is a beautiful mix of ancient Etruscan traditions and modern Italian culture. Here are some cultural customs to keep in mind while in Cortona:

  • Italians are passionate about their food, so take your time to relish every meal and taste that comes your way.
  • Public displays of affection are common in Italy, so don’t be surprised to see couples kissing or holding hands in public.
  • Italians usually have a big lunch and a light dinner, so plan your meals accordingly.

Best Time to Visit Cortona

The best time to visit Cortona is from April to June or September to November when the weather is mild, and the tourist crowds are low. July and August are the hottest and most crowded months, while the winter months can be quite chilly and rainy.

Do’s and Don’ts of Cortona

Do:

  • Respect the local culture and customs.
  • Learn some basic Italian phrases.
  • Try the local cuisine and wines.
  • Take your time to explore the city and its surroundings.

Don’t:

  • Wear revealing clothing when visiting churches or historical sites.
  • Hesitate to order Tuscany’s most famous dish, the Florentine steak.
  • Forget to validate your train ticket when travelling by train.
  • Litter in public places.

Safety and Health

Safety in Cortona

Cortona is a safe and friendly place, but it’s always good to keep some safety tips in mind:

  • Be cautious of pickpockets when in crowded areas.
  • Don’t leave valuables in your car.
  • Be careful while driving on the narrow and winding roads of Tuscany.

Health in Cortona

Cortona has good medical facilities and pharmacies. It is always advisable to carry travel health insurance when travelling overseas.

Getting to Cortona: Transportation Options and Logistics

Cortona is well-connected by road and rail to other cities in Italy.

Airports Near Cortona

The nearest international airports to Cortona are Florence (FLR), Pisa (PSA), and Rome (FCO).

Bus Stations Near Cortona

Corticella is the main bus station in Cortona. It is well-connected to other major cities in Tuscany.

Other Options

You can also reach Cortona by train. The nearest train stations are in Camucia and Terontola.

How to Get Around in Cortona

Cortona’s historical center is best explored on foot, as cars are not allowed in the narrow and winding streets. Biking is also a popular mode of transport in and around Cortona.

Where to Go Next from Cortona: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Here are some nearby destinations and excursions that you can explore from Cortona:

  • Siena: A medieval city famous for its Gothic architecture and rich history.
  • Montepulciano: A picturesque hilltop town known for its wine and panoramic views.
  • Florence: The cradle of the Renaissance and one of the most beautiful cities in the world.
  • Rome: The Eternal City and the capital of Italy.

Conclusion

Cortona is an enchanting and charming hilltop town that holds a significant place in Italian history and culture. With its beautiful architecture, stunning landscapes, and delicious cuisine, Cortona offers something for everyone. Whether you want to explore its fascinating history, indulge in its local food and wine, or simply relax and enjoy its natural beauty, Cortona is sure to leave you enchanted and wanting more.
