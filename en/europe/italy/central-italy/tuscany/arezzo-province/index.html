Introduction to Arezzo (Province)

Arezzo (Province) is a picturesque region located in Tuscany, Central Italy. It is a land of rolling hills, vineyards, olive groves, and extensive woodlands. Arezzo province is known for its beautiful landscapes, rich cultural heritage, and authentic culinary traditions.

The region is home to several charming medieval towns and villages, offering visitors a chance to explore local history and culture. Arezzo (Province) is also famous for its churches, museums, and art galleries, showcasing the works of notable Italian artists and architects. The province has several hiking and biking trails, providing visitors with ample opportunities to explore the stunning natural beauty of the region.

In this comprehensive tour guide, we will explore the top places to visit, things to do, and local food and culture of Arezzo (Province), Tuscany. We will also cover transportation options, safety and health, and nearby excursions to help you plan your trip better.

List of Places to Visit in Arezzo (Province)

Here is a list of must-visit places in Arezzo (Province):

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Basilica of San Francesco: This historic church is a tribute to the life of Saint Francis of Assisi. The church is known for its stunning frescoes, including the famous Legend of the True Cross by Piero della Francesca.
  • Museum of Contemporary Art of the 21st Century: This modern museum showcases contemporary Italian art and design. It has a vast collection of artworks by Italian artists and hosts several temporary exhibitions throughout the year.
  • Badia delle Sante Flora e Lucilla: Located in the ancient town of Arezzo, this abbey is an example of Romanesque architecture. It boasts an impressive collection of frescoes dating back to the 13th and 14th centuries.
  • Palazzo del Capitano del Popolo: This 13th-century palace was the residence of the Governor of Arezzo during the Middle Ages. It is renowned for its stunning architecture and is now home to the Museum of Medieval and Modern Art.

Natural Attractions of Arezzo (Province): Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Valdarno: This area located between Arezzo and Florence is abundant in natural beauty and offers hikers and cyclists numerous trails to explore. The valley is famous for its wineries, and visitors can enjoy wine tasting tours.
  • La Verna: This massive mountain ridge is located in the Casentino area of Arezzo. It is a paradise for hikers longing for panoramic views of the Tuscan hills, valleys, and Apennines, along with exploring the hermitage of St. Francis.
  • Lago di Montedoglio: Located near Sansepolcro, this man-made lake offers scenic views and serves as a popular destination for water sports like fishing, swimming, and boating.
  • Pieve a Socana: This small village near Arezzo is renowned for its ancient Romanesque church of S.S. Ippolito and Cassiano and fantastic hiking and cycling routes.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Arezzo (Province)

Arezzo (Province) offers many trails and routes for hiking and biking, including the following:

  • Monte Cetona Trek: The trek takes you through forests and over hills, and you can spot beautiful landscapes while making your way to Monte Cetona in the southern part of the province.
  • Lago delle Trote Trail: Located near the town of Chiusi della Verna, this trail makes for a beautiful hike along the lake, surrounding hills, and alpine views.
  • Pratomagno: This scenic mountain located in Casentino has several hiking and biking routes, and it’s a favorite spot for wildlife enthusiasts.

Best Things to Do in Arezzo (Province): Activities and Tours

Arezzo (Province) offers several activities and tours, including:

  • Wine Tour in Valdarno: Visitors can explore the beautiful countryside of Valdarno and learn about its renowned vineyards, wine production, and taste the best Italian wines.
  • Food Tour in Arezzo: Visitors can take a food tour to sample the delicious Tuscan cuisine, including cheese, salami, bread, olive oil, and wine, among others.
  • Hot Air Balloon Ride: Taking a hot air balloon ride to appreciate the beauty of the hills, valleys, and vineyards of Tuscany is an unforgettable experience.
  • Visit to the Antique Market: Arezzo hosts one of the largest antique markets in Italy, held on the first Sunday of every month. It’s the perfect opportunity to explore the region’s history and collect authentic souvenirs.

Week-Long Itinerary for Arezzo (Province)

Here’s a suggested week-long itinerary for exploring Arezzo (Province):

Day 1: Arrival

Arrive in Arezzo and spend the day exploring the town’s historic landmarks and museums.

Day 2: The Natural Wonders of Arezzo (Province)

Explore Valdarno to visit vineyards and enjoy wine tasting in the region.

Day 3: Mountain Trekking

Take a guided trek to Monte Cetona in southern Arezzo (Province) and witness the natural beauty of the Apennines.

Day 4: Art and Architecture

Explore the Basilica of San Francesco and admire the works of Piero della Francesca, followed by wandering through the Renaissance art museums in Arezzo.

Day 5: Outdoor Adventure

Take a hike through the beautiful woodlands of Valdarno, followed by an authentic Tuscan picnic.

Day 6: Historical Exploration

Visit Pieve a Socana and explore the ancient Romanesque church of S.S. Ippolito and Cassiano.

Day 7: Departure

Visit the antique market of Arezzo before departure.

Food and Drink Scene of Arezzo (Province)

Arezzo (Province) is famous for its delicious cuisine, featuring authentic Tuscan flavors. Here’s a list of must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Bistecca alla Fiorentina: A thick and juicy steak cooked over an open flame with Tuscan herbs and spices.
  • Zuppa Toscana: A hearty soup with beans, vegetables, and spicy sausage.
  • Pappa al Pomodoro: A traditional Tuscan soup made with tomato, bread, and olive oil.

Local Beverages

  • Chianti Wine: Made from Sangiovese grapes, this wine is famous in all of Tuscany.
  • Vin Santo: A sweet dessert wine accompanied by biscotti.
  • Amari: A unique Tuscan after-dinner digestive liquor that is popular among locals.

Where to Stay in Arezzo (Province): Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Arezzo (Province) has several accommodation options, catering to varying budgets. Some of the top choices include:

  • Villa La Palagina: A luxurious hotel located in Figline Valdarno, surrounded by olive groves, vineyards, and woods.
  • Ostello del Chianti: A budget-friendly hostel located in Gaiole in Chianti, offering comfortable accommodation and breathtaking panoramic views.
  • Il Castellare: Located in the heart of Cortona, this guesthouse has traditional Tuscan style rooms and a beautiful garden.

What to Buy in Arezzo (Province): Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Some of the best souvenirs and local products to bring home from Arezzo (Province) include:

  • Wine: Valdarno produces some of the best wines in Tuscany, a perfect souvenir to bring home.
  • Olive Oil: The region’s olive groves produce some of the best olive oils in Italy, making it the perfect culinary souvenir.
  • Pottery: Arezzo is known for its unique ceramic production and you can take home some exquisite pieces.

Local Culture and Customs of Arezzo (Province)

Arezzo (Province) has a rich cultural heritage, and locals take great pride in sharing their customs and traditions with visitors. Here is an overview of some of their customs:

  • Mealtimes: Italians are strict when it comes to mealtimes, and lunch usually lasts from 1 pm to 3 pm, followed by dinner at 8 pm or later.
  • Greeting: Italians greet each other with a cheek kiss, even if they have just met.
  • Al Fresco Dining: Dining outside is a favorite pastime in Arezzo (Province), and the streets are lined with several small trattorias.

Best Time to Visit Arezzo (Province)

The best time to visit Arezzo (Province) is from April to June and September to November. The weather is pleasant, and fewer tourists mean you can enjoy the region’s attractions and activities without the crowds.

Do’s and Don’ts of Arezzo (Province)

Do:

  • Dress appropriately when visiting churches and other religious institutions.
  • Try the local wines and cuisine.
  • Learn some basic Italian phrases to interact with locals.

Don’t:

  • Don’t talk loudly in public.
  • Don’t skip the siesta; it’s an essential part of local life.
  • Don’t leave your belongings unattended in public places.

Safety and Health

Here are some tips to ensure your safety and health in Arezzo (Province):

Safety in Arezzo (Province)

  • Stay aware of pickpockets in busy tourist areas.
  • Don’t leave valuables in rental vehicles.
  • Be cautious when driving; Italian drivers are notorious for their aggressive driving.

Health in Arezzo (Province)

  • Ensure you have travel insurance into Italy.
  • Check if you need to vaccinate for Italian diseases.
  • Always carry necessary medications and learn about local emergency numbers.

Getting to Arezzo (Province): Transportation Options and Logistics

Arezzo (Province) is well connected to other Italian regions via several transportation options:

Airports Near Arezzo (Province)

  • Pisa Airport

Bus Stations Near Arezzo (Province)

Other Options

  • Car Rental: Renting a car can be an easy way to explore the region, but the Italian roads are narrow and take getting used to.

How to Get Around in Arezzo (Province)

Getting around Arezzo (Province) is best done by renting a car or taking local buses. Visitors can also enjoy leisurely walks to explore old towns and villages. Many tourist towns also have bicycle rentals, and there are plenty of hiking trails crisscrossing through the natural landscapes.

Where to Go Next from Arezzo (Province): Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Arezzo (Province) is an excellent hub for exploring several nearby towns and cities. Some of the best destinations to explore nearby include:

  • Siena: A stunning medieval city located in the heart of Tuscany, renowned for its Palio festival and architecture.
  • Florence: The art capital of Italy, boasting several museums, art galleries, and architectural marvels.
  • Cortona: An ancient medieval town located on a hilltop and popular for its beautiful piazzas and narrow streets.

Conclusion

Arezzo (Province) is a beautiful region offering visitors a fantastic blend of natural beauty, history, culture, and authentic Tuscan cuisine. Plan your trip well, use this tour guide, and enjoy exploring the region to the fullest.
