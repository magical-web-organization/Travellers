Introduction to Arezzo

Arezzo is a charming, historic town located in the heart of Tuscany, central Italy. Known for its stunning medieval architecture, cobbled streets, and picturesque countryside, it is a gem for tourists who love history, art, and natural beauty. Arezzo has been inhabited since Etruscan times, and it boasts a rich cultural heritage, from its Roman Amphitheater to the Basilica of San Francesco and Piazza Grande.

List of Places to Visit in Arezzo

Arezzo boasts a wide variety of tourist attractions, from ancient ruins to modern museums, beautiful parks, and scenic vistas. Here are some must-see places to add to your itinerary:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Arezzo is home to a wealth of cultural landmarks, from ancient ruins and Renaissance art to modern museums and galleries.

Piazza Grande

Piazza Grande is the heart of the city and the site of many important cultural events and festivals throughout the year. Surrounded by historic buildings and stunning architecture, it is a must-see destination for anyone visiting Arezzo.

Roman Amphitheater

Located in the city center, the Roman Amphitheater dates back to the 1st century and is one of the largest and most well-preserved amphitheaters in Italy. Visitors can take a tour of the site and learn about the history of the gladiators who fought here.

Basilica di San Francesco

The Basilica of San Francesco is a beautiful medieval church located in the heart of Arezzo. It is known for its beautiful frescoes by Piero della Francesca, which depict scenes from the life of Saint Francis.

Museo Archeologico Nazionale

The Museo Archeologico Nazionale is a fascinating museum that showcases the history of Arezzo and the surrounding region from prehistoric times to the present day. It features an impressive collection of artifacts, including Etruscan pottery, Roman coins, and medieval art.

Natural Attractions of Arezzo: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Arezzo is known for its stunning natural beauty, from rolling hills and lush forests to crystal-clear lakes and rivers. Here are some of the best outdoor destinations to explore:

Parco delle Foreste Casentinesi

Parco delle Foreste Casentinesi is a vast national park that covers over 36,000 hectares of forest, mountains, and rivers. It is home to a wide variety of wildlife, including deer, boars, and wolves, and it offers visitors a chance to explore the natural beauty of Tuscany.

Val d’Arno

Val d’Arno is a scenic valley that stretches from Arezzo to Florence, encompassing beautiful countryside, picturesque villages, and rolling hills. Visitors can take a scenic drive or hike through the area, admiring the stunning views along the way.

Lake Trasimeno

Lake Trasimeno is a picturesque lake located just outside of Arezzo. Visitors can enjoy swimming, fishing, or boating on the lake, or simply relax on its scenic beaches and soak up the sun.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Arezzo

Arezzo is a paradise for hikers and bikers, with a variety of trails and routes to explore.

La Verna Sanctuary Trail

The La Verna Sanctuary Trail is a scenic hiking trail that covers over 100 kilometers of forest, mountains, and valleys. It features stunning views, historic churches, and beautiful landscapes, and it is a great way to explore the natural beauty of Tuscany.

Valdichiana Cycling Route

The Valdichiana Cycling Route is a popular bike trail that circles the beautiful Valdichiana valley. It features beautiful countryside, picturesque villages, and stunning views, and it is suitable for cyclists of all skill levels.

Best Things to Do in Arezzo: Activities and Tours

Arezzo offers a wide variety of activities and tours, from wine tasting and cooking classes to horseback riding and hot air balloon rides. Here are some of the best things to do in Arezzo:

Wine Tasting

Tuscany is known for its world-renowned wines, including Chianti and Brunello di Montalcino. Visitors to Arezzo can take a wine tasting tour of the surrounding vineyards, learning about the history and production of these famous wines.

Hot Air Balloon Rides

For a truly unique experience, visitors can take a hot air balloon ride over the beautiful Tuscan countryside. It is a great way to admire the stunning views from above and experience the magic of Tuscany.

Week-Long Itinerary for Arezzo

Arezzo is a small town, but there is plenty to see and do here. Here is a suggested week-long itinerary for visitors:

Day 1: Explore the historic center of Arezzo, including Piazza Grande, the Roman Amphitheater, and the Basilica di San Francesco.

Day 2: Visit the Museo Archeologico Nazionale and the Casa Museo Ivan Bruschi, which showcases the history and culture of Arezzo.

Day 3: Take a day trip to the beautiful Val d’Arno, where you can go hiking, sightseeing, and wine tasting.

Day 4: Explore the Parco delle Foreste Casentinesi, where you can hike, bike, and explore the stunning natural landscapes of Tuscany.

Day 5: Take a cooking class or a wine tasting tour, where you can learn about the local cuisine and wines of Tuscany.

Day 6: Go on a hot air balloon ride over the beautiful Tuscan countryside.

Day 7: Relax and enjoy the local culture, food, and wine in Arezzo, before departing for your next destination.

Food and Drink Scene of Arezzo

Arezzo is known for its delicious cuisine, from hearty meat dishes to fresh pasta and seafood. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

Bistecca alla Fiorentina

Bistecca alla Fiorentina is a famous Tuscan steak dish made from the Chianina breed of beef. It is grilled over a wood fire and seasoned with salt, pepper, and olive oil, and it is best enjoyed with a glass of red wine.

Pici all’Aglione

Pici all’Aglione is a traditional Tuscan pasta dish made with thick, hand-rolled noodles, and a flavorful tomato-garlic sauce. It is a hearty and delicious vegetarian dish that is popular in Arezzo.

Local Beverages

Chianti Classico

Chianti Classico is a famous red wine produced in the Tuscan region, known for its fruity flavor and full-bodied taste. It is a great wine to pair with hearty meat dishes and aged cheeses.

Vin Santo

Vin Santo is a sweet dessert wine made from dried grapes and aged in small oak barrels for several years. It is a popular after-dinner drink in Tuscany and is often paired with cantucci, a type of almond biscuit.

Where to Stay in Arezzo: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Arezzo offers a variety of accommodation options, from luxury hotels to budget-friendly guesthouses and hostels. Here are some great places to stay:

Luxury Hotels

Hotel Continentale

Hotel Continentale is a luxurious five-star hotel located in the heart of Arezzo, featuring elegant rooms, a rooftop terrace, and a spa.

Hotel Le Capanne

Hotel Le Capanne is a luxurious villa hotel set in beautiful Tuscan countryside, featuring stunning views, a swimming pool, and an on-site restaurant.

Mid-Range Hotels

Hotel L’Aretino

Hotel L’Aretino is a mid-range hotel located in the historic center of Arezzo, featuring spacious rooms, a restaurant, and a bar.

Hotel Minerva

Hotel Minerva is a comfortable and affordable option located just outside of the city center of Arezzo. It features modern rooms, a swimming pool, and an on-site restaurant.

What to Buy in Arezzo: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Arezzo is known for its beautiful handmade crafts, from ceramics and jewelry to leather goods and textiles. Here are some great souvenirs and local products to bring home:

Arezzo Ceramics

Arezzo has a long tradition of producing beautiful hand-painted ceramics, including plates, bowls, and vases. Visitors can shop for these handcrafted ceramics at local markets and shops.

Arezzo Gold Jewelry

Arezzo is also known for its beautiful gold jewelry, crafted by skilled artisans in the town. Visitors can shop for unique and beautiful pieces at local jewelry shops and artisan markets.

Local Culture and Customs of Arezzo

Arezzo is a small town with a rich cultural heritage, known for its medieval architecture, famous festivals, and delicious cuisine. Here are some local customs and traditions to keep in mind when visiting:

The Giostra del Saracino

The Giostra del Saracino is a medieval jousting tournament that takes place every year in Arezzo. It is a famous and popular event that attracts visitors from around the world.

Tipica Fiera di San Luca

The Tipica Fiera di San Luca is a famous autumn festival that celebrates the harvest season with food, music, and local crafts. Visitors can sample traditional Tuscan dishes and enjoy live music and entertainment.

Best Time to Visit Arezzo

The best time to visit Arezzo is during the spring and fall, when the weather is mild and pleasant and the crowds are smaller. The summer months can be hot and crowded, while the winter months are cold and often rainy.

Do’s and Don’ts of Arezzo

When visiting Arezzo, it is important to keep some key do’s and don’ts in mind:

Do:

  • Try the local cuisine and wine
  • Explore the historic center of Arezzo
  • Visit the museums and cultural landmarks
  • Take a hot air balloon ride over the scenic countryside
  • Attend the Giostra del Saracino or the Tipica Fiera di San Luca

Don’t:

  • Leave valuables unattended or in plain sight
  • Dress immodestly when visiting religious sites
  • Take pictures of people without their permission
  • Be loud or disrespectful in public spaces
  • Ignore local customs and traditions

Safety and Health

Arezzo is a safe and healthy destination for travelers, but it is always important to take precautions to stay safe and healthy.

Safety in Arezzo

Arezzo is generally a safe place for travelers, but it is important to take precautions against pickpocketing and theft. Be sure to keep your valuables secure, especially in crowded areas.

Health in Arezzo

Arezzo is a healthy destination, but it is important to take precautions against sunburn and dehydration, especially during the summer months. Be sure to drink plenty of water, wear sunscreen, and take breaks from the sun as needed.

Getting to Arezzo: Transportation Options and Logistics

Arezzo is easily accessible by car, train, or bus, and there are several airports located within driving distance of the city.

Airports Near Arezzo

The nearest airports to Arezzo are Florence Airport and Pisa Airport, both of which offer regular flights to major European cities.

Bus Stations Near Arezzo

Arezzo is well-connected by bus to other cities and towns in Tuscany, including Florence, Siena, and Pisa.

Other Options

Visitors can also travel to Arezzo by train, which is a convenient and affordable option. The city is located on the main Florence-Rome train line, making it easy to reach from either city.

How to Get Around in Arezzo

Arezzo is a small town that is easy to navigate by foot or bike. Visitors can also take a bus or taxi to reach destinations outside of the city center.

Where to Go Next from Arezzo: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Arezzo is located in the heart of Tuscany, making it a great base for exploring other nearby destinations and excursions. Here are some great options:

Florence

Florence is one of the most famous cities in Italy, known for its Renaissance art and architecture, beautiful museums, and delicious cuisine. It is located just 75 kilometers from Arezzo and is easily accessible by train or bus.

Siena

Siena is a beautiful medieval town located just 50 kilometers from Arezzo. It is known for its stunning architecture, historic landmarks, and famous Palio horse race.

Conclusion

Arezzo is a beautiful and charming town that offers visitors a blend of history, culture, and natural beauty. Whether you are interested in exploring the local museums, hiking in the beautiful national parks, or simply soaking up the local culture and cuisine, there is something for everyone in Arezzo. With this comprehensive travel guide, you will be able to plan a fantastic trip and make the most of your time in this Tuscany gem.
