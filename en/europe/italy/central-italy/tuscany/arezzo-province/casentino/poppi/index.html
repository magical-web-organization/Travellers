The Ultimate Travel Guide to Poppi, Tuscany

Welcome to Poppi, a charming Tuscan village nestled in the heart of Casentino within the province of Arezzo. This picturesque town boasts a rich history, stunning natural landscapes, and a welcoming community. Whether you’re a history enthusiast, outdoor adventurer, or simply seeking a tranquil getaway, Poppi has something for everyone. In this comprehensive travel guide, we’ll take you through everything you need to know about this hidden gem in Tuscany.

Local Culture and Customs of Poppi

Poppi is a small, tight-knit community that values family and tradition. Be sure to greet locals with a friendly “buongiorno” and “grazie” when ordering or purchasing something. Dress modestly and appropriately when visiting churches or places of worship.

Best Time to Visit Poppi

The best time to visit Poppi is during the spring (April-June) or fall (September-November) when the weather is mild, and there are fewer crowds. The summer months can be crowded and hot, while winter is chilly and snow may affect travel.

Safety and Health

Safety in Poppi

Poppi is a relatively safe destination for travelers. However, exercise caution when traveling at night or in isolated areas. Pickpocketing and theft can occur in crowded tourist areas, so remain vigilant with your personal belongings.

Health in Poppi

Tap water is safe to drink in Poppi and throughout Tuscany. Be sure to bring any necessary prescription medications with you, as not all pharmacies carry American brands.

How to Get Around in Poppi

The best way to explore Poppi is by car, but the town is also walkable. Bicycles and scooters are also available for rent.
