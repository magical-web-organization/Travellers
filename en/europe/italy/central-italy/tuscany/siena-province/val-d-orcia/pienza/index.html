Introduction to Pienza

Set in the beautiful Val d’Orcia, in the heart of Tuscany, Pienza is a picturesque town that radiates history, culture, and tradition. Known as the “Ideal City,” Pienza was designed by Pope Pius II in the 15th century and reflects his vision of a utopian city that blended the perfect balance of religion, politics, and aesthetics.

Today, Pienza is a UNESCO World Heritage Site and attracts visitors from all over the world who come to admire its stunning architecture, breathtaking landscapes, and mouth-watering cuisine.

List of Places to Visit in Pienza

If you are planning a trip to Pienza, then you won’t be disappointed with the many places to visit and things to do. Here is a list of some of the must-see attractions.

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Palazzo Piccolomini: built by Pope Pius II, this beautiful palace boasts stunning Renaissance architecture and is home to a museum that showcases the history and culture of Pienza.
  • Cathedral of Santa Maria Assunta: boasts gorgeous frescoes, sculptures, and paintings from the early Renaissance era. Don’t miss the panoramic views of the Val d’Orcia from the cathedral’s bell tower.
  • Pieve di Corsignano: a beautiful Romanesque church that dates back to the 8th century, located just outside Pienza. It’s believed to be the birthplace of Pope Pius II.

Natural Attractions of Pienza: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Pienza is surrounded by breathtakingly beautiful countryside that is perfect for hiking, biking, and leisurely strolls in the Tuscan sun.
  • The Bagno Vignoni hot springs: just a short drive away from Pienza are these natural thermal springs that are fed by the River Orcia. They are said to have therapeutic properties and are a delightful way to relax after a long day of sightseeing and exploring.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Pienza

One of the best ways to explore Pienza and the surrounding countryside is by hiking or biking. Here are some of the best routes to try:

  • The Via Francigena: this famous pilgrim trail passes through Pienza on its way to Rome. The route is approximately 73 miles long and offers stunning views of the rolling Tuscan countryside.
  • The Orcia Valley Trail: this circular trail passes through some of the most beautiful towns and villages in the Val d’Orcia, including Pienza, and offers stunning panoramic views of the surrounding hills.

Best Things to Do in Pienza: Activities and Tours

There are plenty of activities and tours to choose from when visiting Pienza. Here are some suggestions:

  • Wine Tasting Tours: The Val d’Orcia is known for producing some of the finest wines in Italy, and there are plenty of vineyards and wineries to explore. Take a guided tour and sample some of the region’s delicious wines.
  • Cooking Classes: Learn how to make some of Tuscany’s most famous dishes, such as pasta and tiramisu. Many local restaurants and chefs offer hands-on cooking classes.

Week-Long Itinerary for Pienza

If you’re planning on spending a week in Pienza, here’s a suggested itinerary:

  • Day 1: Arrive in Pienza and explore the town’s stunning architecture, including the Palazzo Piccolomini and the Cathedral of Santa Maria Assunta.
  • Day 2: Take a guided wine tasting tour of the Val d’Orcia’s vineyards and wineries.
  • Day 3: Visit the nearby town of Montepulciano, famous for its delicious wine and staggering views of the Tuscan hills.
  • Day 4: Go on a scenic hike or bike ride in the hills surrounding Pienza.
  • Day 5: Take a day trip to Siena, and explore the historic city’s monuments, churches, and museums.
  • Day 6: Indulge in a cooking class and learn how to make homemade pasta and other delicious Tuscan dishes.
  • Day 7: Relax and unwind in the Bagno Vignoni hot springs, located just a short drive away from Pienza.

Food and Drink Scene of Pienza

Tuscany is known for its delicious cuisine, and Pienza is no exception. Here are some dishes and beverages you must try:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Pici all’Aglione: a delicious local pasta dish made with tomatoes, garlic, and chili pepper.
  • Cacio e Pepe: a classic Roman pasta dish that originated in Pienza. It’s made with pecorino cheese and black pepper and is rich and flavorful.
  • Bistecca alla Fiorentina: Tuscany is famous for its steaks, and Pienza is no exception. The Bistecca alla fiorentina is a mouth-watering dish made with a thick cut of beef steak.

Local Beverages

  • Vino Nobile di Montepulciano: a red wine that is famous in Tuscany and the result of exceptional production mastery.
  • Brunello di Montalcino: This wine is the most valuable and appreciated wine of the Montalcino area and is made from Sangiovese grapes grown around the town.
  • Chianti: this red wine is always a popular choice and is a staple of Italian cuisine.

Where to Stay in Pienza: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Pienza has plenty of accommodation options, ranging from budget-friendly hostels to luxury hotels. Some of the best places to stay include:

  • La Bandita Townhouse: A chic and stylish hotel located in the center of Pienza.
  • Il Chiostro di Pienza: This historic hotel is located in a former monastery and boasts a beautiful cloister courtyard.
  • Agriturismo Podere Lamberto: This cozy guesthouse is located in a restored farmhouse and offers stunning views of the Val d’Orcia.

What to Buy in Pienza: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

If you’re looking for some unique souvenirs or gifts to take home, then Pienza won’t disappoint. Here are some of the best things to buy:

  • Pecorino cheese: Pienza is famous for its delicious sheep’s milk cheese, which comes in various flavors, including truffle, black pepper, and red pepper.
  • Porcelain and ceramics:Mamerlad is a local shop that sells beautiful ceramic items, including plates, bowls, and vases, all handmade.
  • Honey: Pienza’s hills are home to a variety of different flowers that produce the sweetest and most unique honey you can find anywhere in Italy.

Local Culture and Customs of Pienza

Pienza is proud of its rich history and culture. When visiting, it’s essential to follow some of the local customs, such as:

  • Dressing for the Occasion: While clothing is generally relaxed, it’s worth dressing up in the evenings according to the place you’re going to visit.
  • Sunday is Sacred: Pienza is a Catholic town, and many stores and restaurants are closed on Sundays.

Best Time to Visit Pienza

Pienza is beautiful at any time of year, but the best time to visit is between May and October. During this time, the weather is warm and sunny, and the countryside is blooming with colorful wildflowers.

Do’s and Don’ts of Pienza

Do:

  • Respect the local culture of Italy, which includes dressing appropriately for public areas.
  • Try as much local food and wine as possible.

Don’t:

  • Don’t queue jump or cut lines as locals will react adversely
  • Don’t forget to carry cash, as many small bars and restaurants may not accept card payments

Safety and Health

Safety in Pienza

Pienza is a relatively safe town. However, it’s always worth keeping your valuables and money safe. Pickpocketing can become a problem in packed tourist areas.

Health in Pienza

It is essential to carry necessities such as sunscreen, hats, and sufficient amounts of water to drink when travelling outside.

Getting to Pienza: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Pienza

Bus Stations Near Pienza

  • Siena Bus Station

Other Options

Travelling by train is one of the most picturesque and popular ways to reach Pienza. From Florence or Siena, hop a train for an easy ride through stunning Tuscan countryside.

How to Get Around in Pienza

Pienza’s old town is easily approachable by walking, but if you would like to explore the countryside or visit other towns and villages in the area, renting a car or hiring a bike is a great option.

Where to Go Next from Pienza: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Some nearby destinations and attractions are:

  • Montalcino
  • Montepulciano
  • Siena

Conclusion

Pienza is a beautiful town that radiates history, culture, and tradition. With delicious food, excellent wine, and breathtaking landscapes, it’s a must-visit destination in Tuscany, and an excellent starting point for visiting more of the stunning countryside in the area. We hope this guide serves as a useful tool to plan your trip and helps you make the most of your time in Pienza.
