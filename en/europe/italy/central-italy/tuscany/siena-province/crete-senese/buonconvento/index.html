Buonconvento is a charming town nestled in Crete Senese, Siena (province) of Tuscany, Italy. This medieval hilltop town boasts of breathtaking landscapes, historic architecture, and a laid-back vibe that attracts tourists from all over the world.

In this comprehensive travel guide, we will take you through the top attractions, outdoor adventures, local food, accommodations, travel logistics, and much more. So let’s start exploring this treasure trove of Tuscan countryside -

Introduction to Buonconvento

Buonconvento is a small town with a population of around 4000 people. The town dates back to the 12th century and has several well-preserved architectural marvels that reflect the rich cultural heritage of Tuscany.

Buonconvento is a perfect getaway for those who want to experience the authentic Tuscan lifestyle, away from the hustle-bustle of the city. This town is known for its art, food, wine, and above all, its natural beauty.

List of Places to Visit in Buonconvento

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Museo d’Arte Sacra della Val d’Arbia

This museum is a must-visit attraction for art lovers and history enthusiasts. It has an impressive collection of Tuscan art from the 13th to the 19th century, including works by the famous Sienese painters like Duccio di Buoninsegna and Simone Martini.

Porta Senese

Porta Senese is a well-preserved medieval gate that dates back to the 14th century. It is one of the four gateways of the town and served as the entrance for the Siena-Buonconvento road.

Podestà Palace

This palace is a grand building that dominates the town’s main square. It was built in the 14th century as the residence of the Podestà- the medieval governor of the town.

Natural Attractions of Buonconvento: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Montalcino

Montalcino is a beautiful hilltop town located just a short drive away from Buonconvento. This town is famous for its Brunello di Montalcino wine and has several vineyards that offer wine-tasting sessions.

Val d’Orcia Natural Park

Val d’Orcia Natural Park offers breathtaking views of the rolling hills, vineyards, cypress trees, and fields of wildflowers. This park is a great place to go hiking, cycling, or horseback riding.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Buonconvento

La Via Francigena

La Via Francigena is an ancient pilgrimage route that connects Canterbury in England to Rome in Italy. The section of this route that passes through Buonconvento offers stunning views of the Tuscan countryside.

Monte Amiata

Monte Amiata is a mountain located near Buonconvento that offers several hiking trails. The summit of Monte Amiata offers panoramic views of the surrounding valleys and hills.

Best Things to Do in Buonconvento: Activities and Tours

Wine-tasting

Buonconvento is located in the heart of Tuscany’s wine country and has several vineyards that offer wine-tasting sessions. Take a guided tour of the vineyards and enjoy the local wine and food.

Hot springs

Buonconvento has several hot spring spas that offer relaxation and rejuvenation. Soak in the warm pools and enjoy the scenic views of the Tuscan countryside.

Week-Long Itinerary for Buonconvento

Day 1- Explore the town of Buonconvento, visit Porta Senese and Podestà Palace.

Day 2- Take a day trip to Montalcino and enjoy wine-tasting sessions.

Day 3- Visit Museo d’Arte Sacra della Val d’Arbia and enjoy a guided tour of the town.

Day 4- Go hiking in Val d’Orcia Natural Park.

Day 5- Enjoy the hot springs spa and relax.

Day 6- Go cycling on La Via Francigena.

Day 7- Enjoy the local food and culture of Buonconvento.

Food and Drink Scene of Buonconvento

Must-Try Dishes

Tuscan cuisine is simple, hearty and tasty. Some of the must-try dishes in Buonconvento include Pappardelle al Ragù di Cinghiale (pasta with wild boar sauce), Ribollita (vegetable soup), and Panzanella (bread salad).

Local Beverages

Buonconvento is famous for its wine, especially Brunello di Montalcino, which is a robust and full-bodied red wine. Try some of the local wines at the vineyards or visit the town’s wine bars.

Where to Stay in Buonconvento: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Buonconvento has several accommodations ranging from high-end hotels to budget-friendly hostels and guesthouses. Some of the popular options include Hotel Ghibellino, Podere Salicotto, and Podere Santa Pia.

What to Buy in Buonconvento: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Some of the popular souvenirs and local products to bring home from Buonconvento include hand-painted tiles and pottery, local wines, and olive oils.

Local Culture and Customs of Buonconvento

Tuscany is known for its rich cultural heritage, and Buonconvento is no exception. The locals are warm and hospitable, and the town has several festivals and events that celebrate the local culture and traditions.

Best Time to Visit Buonconvento

The best time to visit Buonconvento is during the spring (March to May) and the fall (September to November) when the weather is pleasant and mild, and the crowds are fewer.

Do’s and Don’ts of Buonconvento

Do:

  • Respect the local culture and customs.
  • Try the local cuisine and wines.
  • Visit the vineyards and olive groves.
  • Enjoy the scenic views of the Tuscan countryside.
  • Learn some basic Italian phrases.

Don’t:

  • Wear revealing clothing in religious sites.
  • Be too loud or disrespectful in public places.
  • Leave your valuables unattended.

Safety and Health

Safety in Buonconvento

Buonconvento is a safe town with a low crime rate. However, it’s always advisable to take general safety precautions.

Health in Buonconvento

Medical facilities in Buonconvento are good, and there are several pharmacies. It’s always advisable to carry your travel medical insurance and prescriptions.

Getting to Buonconvento: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Buonconvento

The nearest airports to Buonconvento are Florence Airport (FLR) and Pisa International Airport (PSA), both around 1.5 to 2 hours away.

Bus Stations Near Buonconvento

Buonconvento has a bus station that connects to nearby towns like Siena, Montalcino, and Florence.

Other Options

Tuscany is best explored by car, and there are several car rental options available at the airports and major towns.

How to Get Around in Buonconvento

Buonconvento is a small town, and it’s easy to explore on foot. However, if you want to explore the countryside, it’s better to rent a car or take a guided tour.

Where to Go Next from Buonconvento: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Some of the popular nearby destinations from Buonconvento include Siena, Montalcino, Pienza, and Montepulciano.

Conclusion

Buonconvento is one of the hidden gems of Tuscany. From its historic architecture, natural beauty to its delicious cuisine and wines, this town has everything to offer. Follow our travel guide to experience the best of Buonconvento, and make memories that will last a lifetime.
