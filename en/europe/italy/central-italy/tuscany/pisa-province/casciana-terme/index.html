Introduction to Casciana Terme

Nestled on the slopes of the Tuscan hills, Casciana Terme is a charming town with a rich cultural heritage, natural beauty, and excellent food and wine. Famous for its healing thermal springs, Casciana Terme has been a sought-after destination for spa-goers for centuries. In this guide, we’ll take you on an unforgettable journey through Casciana Terme’s must-visit attractions, outdoor activities, food and wine scene, and local culture and customs.

List of Places to Visit in Casciana Terme

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Terme di Casciana

The Terme di Casciana is the main thermal complex in the town and offers a range of spa and wellness services. The thermal water here is known for its healing properties, and visitors can indulge in therapies that range from mud and beauty treatments to massages and thermal pools.

Palazzo del Municipio

The Palazzo del Municipio is a beautiful example of Renaissance architecture and houses the town hall and the Municipal Art Gallery. Inside, visitors can admire paintings, sculptures, and other works of art from the 15th to 20th centuries.

Chiesa di San Giovanni Evangelista

The Chiesa di San Giovanni Evangelista is a stunning baroque-style church located in the center of Casciana Terme. Built in the 17th century, it features beautiful frescoes, sculptures, and a magnificent altar.

Natural Attractions of Casciana Terme: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Riserva Naturale Lago di Santa Luce

The Riserva Naturale Lago di Santa Luce is a haven for nature lovers and bird-watchers. The reserve is home to a large variety of plant and animal species, including rare birds such as the whiskered tern, the Mediterranean Gull, and the Kentish Plover.

Parco della Fonte

Located near the Terme di Casciana, the Parco della Fonte is a picturesque park with beautiful gardens and fountains. Visitors can take a leisurely stroll through the park’s numerous trails and enjoy the scenic views of the Tuscan hills.

Rocca di Pietracassia

Rocca di Pietracassia is an ancient fortress located on a hilltop near the town of Lari. The castle dates back to the 10th century and offers breathtaking views of the surrounding countryside. Visitors can explore the castle’s impressive architecture and learn about its fascinating history.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Casciana Terme

Casciana Terme is a paradise for outdoor enthusiasts and offers a wide range of hiking and biking trails that wind through the Tuscan hills. Some of the most popular routes include the trail to the Rocca di Pietracassia, the Monti Pisani trail, and the trail to the medieval village of Lari.

Best Things to Do in Casciana Terme: Activities and Tours

Wine Tasting Tours

Casciana Terme is located in one of the best wine-producing regions of Tuscany, and visitors can enjoy exclusive wine-tasting experiences at local vineyards. Discover the secrets of Tuscan winemaking and indulge in some of the world’s finest vintages.

Cooking Classes

Learn to cook like a local and discover the secrets of Tuscan cuisine with cooking classes led by professional chefs. Visitors can learn how to prepare traditional dishes such as fresh pasta, bruschetta, and tiramisu.

Hot Air Balloon Rides

Experience the breathtaking beauty of Tuscany from a unique perspective with hot air balloon rides. See the rolling hills, olive groves, vineyards, and medieval towns from high above, and enjoy a champagne breakfast upon landing.

Week-Long Itinerary for Casciana Terme

Day 1: Arrive in Casciana Terme and explore the town’s historic landmarks, such as the Terme di Casciana and the Chiesa di San Giovanni Evangelista.

Day 2: Take a day trip to the ancient city of Volterra, famous for its well-preserved medieval architecture and Etruscan heritage.

Day 3: Enjoy a leisurely morning at the Parco della Fonte, followed by an afternoon bike ride through the Tuscan countryside.

Day 4: Discover the beautiful wine country of Tuscany with a wine tasting tour at a local vineyard.

Day 5: Take a hot air balloon ride and see the stunning landscape of Tuscany from above.

Day 6: Spend the day at the Riserva Naturale Lago di Santa Luce, where you can hike, bird-watch, and admire the beautiful scenery.

Day 7: Depart Casciana Terme and head to your next destination in Tuscany.

Food and Drink Scene of Casciana Terme

Must-Try Dishes

Cacciucco

Cacciucco is a traditional Tuscan fish stew that includes a variety of seafood such as lobster, squid, shrimp, and mussels. The dish is typically served with toasted bread and garlic.

Bistecca alla fiorentina

Bistecca alla fiorentina is a mouthwatering grilled steak made with high-quality Chianina beef.

Ribollita

Ribollita is a hearty Tuscan soup made with bread, cannellini beans, and vegetables such as kale, chard, and carrots.

Local Beverages

Chianti

Chianti is one of the most famous wines in the world and is produced in the Chianti region of Tuscany. It’s a full-bodied red wine that pairs well with meats and cheese.

Vernaccia di San Gimignano

Vernaccia di San Gimignano is a crisp, dry white wine that’s produced in the town of San Gimignano. It’s perfect for pairing with seafood and light pasta dishes.

Where to Stay in Casciana Terme: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Casciana Terme offers a wide range of accommodations to suit all budgets and preferences. Some of the best options include:

Hotel Villa Margherita

Located in the historic center of Casciana Terme, Hotel Villa Margherita offers elegant rooms with stunning views of the surrounding hills. The hotel also features a spa and wellness center, a swimming pool, and a restaurant.

Podere la Chiusella

Podere la Chiusella is a beautiful, rustic farmhouse that’s been converted into a lovely guesthouse. The property has a large garden, a swimming pool, and a restaurant that serves delicious traditional Tuscan dishes.

Il Noce Guesthouse

Il Noce Guesthouse is a charming bed and breakfast located in the heart of Casciana Terme. The property features beautiful garden views, comfortable rooms, and a hearty breakfast served every morning.

What to Buy in Casciana Terme: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Visitors to Casciana Terme can take home a range of local products and souvenirs, including:

Terracotta pottery

Tuscany is famous for its terracotta pottery, and visitors to Casciana Terme can buy beautiful handcrafted items such as vases, candleholders, and olive oil jars.

Olive oil

Tuscan olive oil is some of the best in the world, and visitors to Casciana Terme can buy high-quality, extra-virgin olive oil produced by local farmers.

Local Culture and Customs of Casciana Terme

One of the best ways to experience the local culture of Casciana Terme is to attend a traditional festa, or festival. The town hosts several events throughout the year, including:

Festa di San Giovanni

The Festa di San Giovanni is celebrated on June 24th and includes a large procession through the town, live music, and fireworks.

Festa della Vendemmia

The Festa della Vendemmia is a wine festival that’s held in September and features wine tastings, grape stomping contests, and live music.

Best Time to Visit Casciana Terme

The best time to visit Casciana Terme is between April and October. During these months, the weather is warm and sunny, and there are plenty of outdoor activities to enjoy. The town can get crowded during peak season, so consider visiting in the shoulder seasons of April-May and September-October for smaller crowds and cheaper accommodations.

Do’s and Don’ts of Casciana Terme

Do:

  • Try the local cuisine and wine.
  • Visit the Terme di Casciana for a relaxing spa experience.
  • Explore the town’s historic landmarks and cultural attractions.
  • Attend a local festival or event.

Don’t:

  • Forget to bring comfortable walking shoes, as the town has many hills and cobblestone streets.
  • Overpack, as there are plenty of shops and markets where you can buy everything you need.
  • Drink and drive, as the winding roads in the Tuscan hills can be treacherous.

Safety and Health

Safety in Casciana Terme

Casciana Terme is a safe town, and travelers should not encounter any issues while visiting. However, it’s always a good idea to exercise caution and be aware of your surroundings.

Health in Casciana Terme

Casciana Terme is known for its healing thermal waters, and visitors can enjoy a range of spa and wellness services that promote relaxation and well-being. However, it’s always recommended to drink bottled water and take any necessary precautions when traveling.

Getting to Casciana Terme: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Casciana Terme

The nearest airports to Casciana Terme are:

  • Pisa Airport (PSA), located about 30 km away
  • Florence Airport (FLR), located about 70 km away

Bus Stations Near Casciana Terme

The closest bus station to Casciana Terme is in the nearby town of Pontedera, about 13 km away. From there, visitors can take a local bus or taxi to Casciana Terme.

Other Options

  • Train: The nearest train station to Casciana Terme is in Pontedera, where visitors can take a local bus or taxi to Casciana Terme.
  • Car rental: Visitors can rent a car at Pisa or Florence airport and drive to Casciana Terme, which is about an hour’s drive from either airport.

How to Get Around in Casciana Terme

The best way to get around Casciana Terme is by foot or bike, as the town is small and easy to navigate. However, for those who prefer not to walk, there are bus and taxi services available.

Where to Go Next from Casciana Terme: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Casciana Terme is ideally located in the heart of Tuscany, and visitors can easily explore the surrounding countryside and nearby towns. Some of the best destinations include:

Florence

Florence is the capital city of Tuscany and is renowned for its art, architecture, and cultural heritage. Visitors can explore world-famous landmarks such as the Duomo, the Uffizi Gallery, and the Ponte Vecchio.

Pisa

Pisa is known for its iconic leaning tower and charming medieval old town. Visitors can explore historic landmarks such as the Baptistery of San Giovanni and the Piazza dei Miracoli.
