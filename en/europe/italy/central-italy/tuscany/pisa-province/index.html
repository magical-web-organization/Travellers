Introduction to Pisa (Province)

Pisa (province) is located in the Tuscany region of Central Italy. It is well-known for the world-famous Leaning Tower of Pisa, but there is much more to discover in this charming small town and its surrounding areas. Pisa has a rich history, stunning natural landscapes, and a thriving food and wine culture that make it an attractive destination for travelers.

Whether you are planning a day trip or a week-long holiday, Pisa has something for everyone. In this comprehensive tour guide, we will cover the best places to visit, things to do, local culture and customs, safety and health, transportation options, and nearby destinations in Pisa (province).

List of Places to Visit in Pisa (Province)

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Pisa is home to many museums and historical landmarks that offer a glimpse into its rich cultural heritage. Here are some of the most popular ones:

  • Leaning Tower of Pisa: This iconic tower is one of the most recognizable landmarks in the world. Its unique lean has made it a popular tourist spot for several centuries. It is part of the Piazza dei Miracoli, a UNESCO World Heritage site that includes other stunning architectural gems. Visitors can climb up the tower for a spectacular view of the surroundings.
  • Cathedral of Santa Maria Assunta: The Pisa Cathedral is a masterpiece of Romanesque art and architecture. It was built in the 11th century and has since undergone several renovations and restorations. The cathedral houses many impressive artworks, including frescoes, sculptures, and paintings.
  • National Museum of San Matteo: This museum is dedicated to medieval and Renaissance art and artifacts. It is housed in a former Benedictine convent and features works by famous artists such as Bonaventura Berlinghieri and Andrea Pisano.

Natural Attractions of Pisa (Province): Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Pisa and its surrounding areas have many natural wonders that are worth exploring. Here are some of the most popular ones:

  • Migliarino San Rossore Massaciuccoli Natural Park: This park is a paradise for nature lovers. It covers over 23,000 hectares of forests, beaches, and wetlands. Visitors can hike, bike, or ride horseback through the park and observe the local flora and fauna.
  • Versilia Coast: The Versilia Coast is a stretch of beautiful beaches and rugged cliffs along the Tyrrhenian Sea. It is a popular spot for swimming, sunbathing, and water sports such as surfing and boating. It also has many seaside towns and fishing villages that offer a glimpse into local life.
  • Monti Pisani: The Monti Pisani is a mountain range that spans across the province. It is a popular spot for hiking and trekking, offering stunning views of the surrounding landscapes. There are many trails that cater to different fitness levels and interests.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Pisa (Province)

Pisa has many trails and routes that offer a perfect outdoor adventure. Here are some of the most popular ones:

  • Via Francigena: This is an ancient pilgrimage route that goes from Canterbury in England to Rome, passing through Pisa. It is a long-distance trail that covers over 1000 kilometers in Italy alone. Visitors can hike or bike short sections of the trail and appreciate its history and natural beauty.
  • San Rossore Estate Trail: This trail goes through the San Rossore Estate, a large park that used to be a hunting reserve. It covers about 10 kilometers and passes through rugged terrain, offering beautiful views of the surrounding landscapes.
  • Monti Pisani Trail: This trail goes through the Monti Pisani mountain range and covers about 20 kilometers. It is a challenging hike that rewards visitors with breathtaking views of the surrounding valleys and hills.

Best Things to Do in Pisa (Province): Activities and Tours

Apart from the popular attractions and outdoor adventures, there are many other things to see and do in Pisa. Here are some of the most popular ones:

  • Wine Tasting: Tuscany is famous for its wine, and Pisa is no exception. Visitors can take a wine tasting tour of the local vineyards and sample some of the best wines in the region.
  • Cooking Classes: Italian cuisine is world-renowned, and Pisa has many cooking schools that offer classes to visitors. Visitors can learn to prepare traditional dishes such as pasta, pizza, and seafood.
  • Boat Tours: Pisa is situated on the Arno River, and there are many boat tours that offer a unique perspective on the city and its surroundings. Visitors can enjoy a leisurely ride along the river and appreciate the beauty of the local landscapes.

Week-Long Itinerary for Pisa (Province)

Here is a week-long itinerary for Pisa (province):

  • Day 1: Visit the Piazza dei Miracoli and the Leaning Tower of Pisa. Explore the cathedral and the baptistery.
  • Day 2: Visit the National Museum of San Matteo and the Pisa Charterhouse.
  • Day 3: Hike the Monti Pisani trail and enjoy the panoramic views of the surrounding landscapes.
  • Day 4: Visit the city of Lucca, known for its medieval walls, museums, and churches.
  • Day 5: Take a wine tasting tour of the local vineyards and visit the town of Montecarlo.
  • Day 6: Explore the Migliarino San Rossore Massaciuccoli Natural Park and take a boat tour along the Arno River.
  • Day 7: Take a cooking class and learn to prepare traditional Italian dishes.

Food and Drink Scene of Pisa (Province)

Must-Try Dishes

Pisa has a rich culinary tradition that combines local ingredients and traditional Italian recipes. Here are some of the most popular dishes:

  • Pappardelle al Cinghiale: This is a pasta dish made with wide noodles and wild boar meat sauce. It is a hearty and flavorful dish that reflects the local hunting tradition.
  • Cecina: This is a savory pancake made with chickpea flour, olive oil, and salt. It is a popular street food in Pisa and its surrounding areas.
  • Baccalà alla Livornese: This is a salt cod dish cooked with tomatoes, onions, and olives. It is a regional specialty of Livorno, a town in the Pisa province.

Local Beverages

  • Chianti: This is a red wine made from Sangiovese grapes grown in the Chianti region. It is a full-bodied wine with notes of cherry, plum, and spices.
  • Vin Santo: This is a dessert wine made from Trebbiano and Malvasia grapes. It is a sweet wine with notes of honey, caramel, and dried fruits.
  • Limoncello: This is a lemon liqueur made from lemon zest, alcohol, and sugar. It is a popular digestive after meals.

Where to Stay in Pisa (Province): Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Pisa and its surrounding areas have many accommodation options that cater to different budgets and preferences. Here are some of the most popular ones:

  • Grand Hotel Bonanno: This is a four-star hotel located near the Leaning Tower of Pisa. It has comfortable rooms with modern amenities, a swimming pool, and a restaurant.
  • Hostel Pisa: This is a budget-friendly hostel located in the city center. It has dormitory rooms and private rooms, shared bathrooms, and a kitchen.
  • Podere la Fraschetta: This is a bed and breakfast located in the countryside near Lucca. It has cozy rooms with antique furniture, a swimming pool, and a garden.

What to Buy in Pisa (Province): Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Pisa has many local products and souvenirs that make for great gifts and mementos. Here are some of the most popular ones:

  • Lucca Olive Oil: This is a high-quality olive oil made from local olives. It is cold-pressed and has a rich and fruity flavor.
  • Ceramics: Pisa and its surrounding areas are known for their beautiful ceramics. Visitors can find handcrafted plates, bowls, and vases in many local shops.
  • Leather Products: Tuscany is famous for its leather goods, and Pisa is no exception. Visitors can find leather bags, shoes, and wallets in many local stores.

Local Culture and Customs of Pisa (Province)

Pisa has a rich cultural heritage that is reflected in its traditions and customs. Here are some aspects of the local culture to keep in mind:

  • Family Values: Family is an essential aspect of local life, and Italians place a high value on their relationships with their loved ones.
  • Religious Observances: Italy is a predominantly Catholic country, and Pisa observes many religious holidays throughout the year.
  • Food and Wine Culture: Pisa has a long and proud tradition of food and wine culture. Italians take their food seriously and view it as an essential part of their daily lives.

Best Time to Visit Pisa (Province)

The best time to visit Pisa is from March to May and September to November. The weather is mild, and the crowds are thinner than in the peak summer months. June to August can be hot and humid, with heavy tourist traffic. December to February can be cold and rainy, but it is a good time to enjoy the festive holiday atmosphere.

Do’s and Don’ts of Pisa (Province)

Do:

  • Dress modestly when visiting religious sites.
  • Tip for good service in restaurants and cafes.
  • Learn some basic Italian phrases, such as “Ciao” (hello) and “Grazie” (thank you).

Don’t:

  • Touch or climb on the Leaning Tower of Pisa. It is a UNESCO World Heritage site and needs to be preserved.
  • Wear revealing clothing in public places. Italians view modesty as a sign of respect.
  • Drink and drive. The legal blood alcohol level in Italy is low, and roadside checks are common.

Safety and Health

Safety in Pisa (Province)

Pisa is generally a safe place to visit, but visitors should still take common-sense precautions. Here are some tips:

  • Keep your valuables safe and secure.
  • Avoid walking alone at night in unlit or deserted areas.
  • Watch out for pickpockets in crowded tourist areas.

Health in Pisa (Province)

Pisa has a good healthcare system, and visitors can access medical care if needed. Visitors should also take precautions to avoid getting sick. Here are some tips:

  • Drink bottled water to avoid stomach problems.
  • Use sunscreen and wear a hat to protect against the sun.
  • Check if you need any recommended vaccinations before traveling to Italy.

Getting to Pisa (Province): Transportation Options and Logistics

Pisa is well-connected to other parts of Italy and Europe, making it easy to get there. Here are some transportation options:

  • Plane: The Galileo Galilei Airport is the main airport serving Pisa and has connections to many European cities.
  • Train: Pisa has a central train station that connects to other major cities in Italy, such as Rome, Florence, and Venice.
  • Bus: There are many bus companies that serve Pisa and its surrounding areas, offering a budget-friendly option for travelers.

Airports Near Pisa (Province)

  • Galileo Galilei Airport: This airport is located about 2 kilometers from Pisa city center and offers flights to many European cities.

Bus Stations Near Pisa (Province)

  • Pisa Central Bus Station: This station connects Pisa to other cities in Tuscany and Italy.

Other Options

  • Car Rental: Visitors can rent a car to explore Pisa and its surroundings at their own pace.

How to Get Around in Pisa (Province)

Pisa has a reliable public transportation system that includes buses and trains. Taxis are also available, but they can be expensive. Visitors who want to explore the surrounding areas may consider renting a car.

Where to Go Next from Pisa (Province): Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Pisa is a good starting point for exploring other parts of Tuscany and Italy. Here are some nearby destinations and excursions:

  • Florence: This city is the birthplace of the Renaissance and has many museums, galleries, and landmarks to explore. It is located about 80 kilometers from Pisa.
  • Siena: This medieval city is famous for its historic center, including the Piazza del Campo and the Gothic Cathedral. It is located about 160 kilometers from Pisa.
  • Cinque Terre: This coastal region comprises five small towns with colorful houses and stunning natural landscapes. It is located about 100 kilometers from Pisa.

Conclusion

Pisa (province) is a charming destination that offers something for everyone. From stunning architecture and historical landmarks to natural wonders and outdoor adventures, it has much to offer visitors. Start planning your trip to Pisa (province) today and discover all the beauty and richness of this wonderful place!
