Introduction to Mugello

Nestled in the heart of Tuscany, Mugello is a stunning valley surrounded by rolling hills, picturesque towns, and endless natural beauty. The region is home to many culturally and historically significant sites, as well as an abundance of outdoor adventure and activities.

List of Places to Visit in Mugello

Mugello is dotted with charming small towns, each with its distinct culture, traditions, and landmarks. Here are some of the must-visit places in the region:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Museo di Arte Sacra: This museum is housed in the charming town of San Piero a Sieve and houses a precious collection of religious art and artifacts.
  • Medici Villas: The Medici family, one of the most influential Italian dynasties, had several villas in Mugello. The Villa Demidoff in Pratolino, Villa di Cafaggiolo, and Villa di Trebbio are all stunning examples of Renaissance architecture and design.
  • Castello di Scarperia: Located in the town of Scarperia, this castle dates from the 14th century. It features a medieval-style tower, a museum of cutting utensils, and a beautiful garden.

Natural Attractions of Mugello: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Lake Bilancino: A man-made lake situated near Barberino di Mugello, it offers several water sports and recreational activities, including canoeing, kayaking, fishing, and more.
  • Monte Giovi: This mountain peak is a popular destination for hikers, bikers, and nature lovers. It offers stunning panoramic views of the valley and a challenging hiking trail.
  • Parco del WWF: This wildlife reserve encompasses a vast area of protected forest and wetlands. Visitors can enjoy guided hikes, bird watching, and nature photography.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Mugello

Mugello is a paradise for outdoor enthusiasts, with many well-marked hiking and biking trails with varying levels of difficulty. Some popular routes are:

  • Giro del Mugello: A 70 km bike route that circles the entire valley, offering stunning views of the countryside and passing through several charming towns.
  • Sentiero del CAI n.22: A challenging hiking trail that leads to the summit of Monte Giovi, one of the highest peaks in Mugello.
  • Giogo-Campanaio Trail: A moderate hiking trail that leads to the beautiful Giogo Pass and offers panoramic views of the Tuscan countryside.

Best Things to Do in Mugello: Activities and Tours

There is no shortage of things to do in Mugello, whether you’re interested in history, culture, gastronomy, or outdoor activities. Some of the best things to do in the region include:

  • Wine Tours: Mugello is home to several wineries, where visitors can taste and learn about the local wine varieties, such as Chianti Rufina.
  • Hot Air Balloon Rides: For a unique and memorable experience, take a hot air balloon ride over the valley and enjoy breathtaking views of the landscape.
  • Cooking Classes: Learn how to prepare traditional Tuscan dishes, such as ribollita, pappa al pomodoro, and tagliatelle al ragù, with a local chef.

Week-Long Itinerary for Mugello

If you’re planning to spend a week in Mugello, here’s a sample itinerary that covers all the must-see places and activities:

Day 1 Arrival and check-in at a hotel in Florence or Mugello
Day 2 Visit the Medici Villas and have lunch in Scarperia
Day 3 Hike the Monte Giovi trail and have dinner in San Piero a Sieve
Day 4 Take a wine tour in Chianti Rufina and have lunch in Pontassieve
Day 5 Spend the day at Lake Bilancino, enjoy water sports and have lunch in Barberino di Mugello
Day 6 Visit the Museo di Arte Sacra and have dinner in Borgo San Lorenzo
Day 7 Take a hot air balloon ride over the valley and have a farewell dinner in Florence

Food and Drink Scene of Mugello

Mugello boasts a rich culinary tradition, with many dishes featuring fresh local ingredients such as wild game, chestnuts, and mushrooms. Here are some of the must-try dishes and beverages in the region:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Ribollita: A vegetable soup made with bread, beans, and seasonal vegetables.
  • Tortelli Mugellani: A stuffed pasta dish filled with ricotta cheese and spinach, served with a tomato sauce.
  • Cinghiale al sugo: Slow-cooked wild boar stew.

Local Beverages

  • Chianti Rufina: A fruity red wine made from Sangiovese grapes, grown in the Rufina area.
  • Birra Mugello: A locally-brewed beer made with pure spring water and natural ingredients.

Where to Stay in Mugello: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Mugello offers a range of accommodation options to suit all budgets and preferences, from luxury hotels to charming guesthouses. Some recommended places to stay are:

  • Villa Campestri Olive Oil Resort: A beautiful 16th-century villa nestled in the countryside, offering spacious rooms, a swimming pool, and a wellness center.
  • Hotel dei Vicari: A modern hotel located in the historic center of Scarperia, offering comfortable rooms, a restaurant, and a bar.
  • Affittacamere Sotto i Cieli di Mugello: A cozy guesthouse in Borgo San Lorenzo, offering comfortable rooms and a lovely garden.

What to Buy in Mugello: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Mugello is known for its artisan products, including handmade ceramics, leather goods, and wood carvings. Some recommended souvenirs to bring home are:

  • Scarperia Knives: These handcrafted knives are made in the town of Scarperia using traditional methods and premium materials.
  • Marrons Glaces: These candied chestnuts are a local delicacy, made with the finest quality ingredients.
  • Ceramiche D’Arte: These colorful, handmade ceramics are a signature product of the region.

Local Culture and Customs of Mugello

Mugello is a region with a rich cultural heritage and many celebrations and festivals throughout the year. For example, the Mugello International Music Festival in the summer and the Scarperia Historical Parade in September.

Best Time to Visit Mugello

Mugello is a year-round destination, but the best time to visit is from April to June or September to November when the weather is mild, and crowds are fewer.

Do’s and Don’ts of Mugello

Do:

  • Respect local customs and traditions.
  • Try the local food and wine.
  • Explore the countryside on foot or by bike.

Don’t:

  • Wear revealing clothing in churches or other religious sites.
  • Order cappuccino with a meal or after dinner.
  • Park in restricted areas or without paying.

Safety and Health

Safety in Mugello

Mugello is a safe destination, but travelers should take usual precautions, such as avoiding isolated areas at night and keeping valuables in a safe place.

Health in Mugello

Mugello has excellent medical facilities and services. However, travelers should have health insurance that covers emergency medical treatment.

Getting to Mugello: Transportation Options and Logistics

Mugello is easily accessible by car, train, or bus.

Airports Near Mugello

Florence Airport (FLR) is the closest airport to Mugello, located 25 km away.

Bus Stations Near Mugello

Several bus companies connect Mugello to Florence, Prato, and other nearby towns.

Other Options

Renting a car is an excellent option for those who want to explore the region independently.

How to Get Around in Mugello

The best way to get around Mugello is by car, bike, or on foot. Public transportation options are limited, and taxis can be pricey.

Where to Go Next from Mugello: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Mugello is conveniently located between Florence, Bologna, and the northern regions of Tuscany and Emilia-Romagna. Some great nearby destinations to visit are:

  • Florence: Explore the city’s rich art and culture, visit its famous landmarks, and enjoy its excellent food and wine scene.
  • Bologna: Experience the city’s vibrant atmosphere, taste its famous cuisine, and visit its beautiful historic center.
  • Siena: Discover this charming medieval city, famous for its unique Palio horse race.

Conclusion

Mugello is a hidden gem in Tuscany, offering a perfect mix of history, culture, nature, and adventure. With this comprehensive tour guide, you have all the information needed to plan an exciting and unforgettable trip to the region.
