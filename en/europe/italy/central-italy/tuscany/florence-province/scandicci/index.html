Introduction to Scandicci

Welcome to Scandicci, an enchanting town surrounded by stunning hills and olive groves, nestled in the heart of Tuscany, Italy. Scandicci is located just a few kilometers away from Florence, and it’s a perfect base for those who want to explore the beauty of Tuscany without the hustle and bustle of the city.

List of Places to Visit in Scandicci

There are plenty of things to see and do in Scandicci, whether you are a culture enthusiast, an outdoor adventurer, or a foodie. Here’s a list of must-visit attractions to add to your itinerary:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Villa i Collazzi: A stunning 15th-century villa located on the outskirts of Scandicci, surrounded by lush gardens and fountains. The villa houses an impressive collection of Renaissance art and furnishings.
  • Museo Di Scandicci: A small yet charming museum that displays the history and culture of Scandicci, with a particular focus on the local terracotta production and medieval history.
  • Castello dell’Acciaiolo: A medieval castle located in the suburb of Scandicci, dating back to the 14th century. It is now a cultural center that hosts art exhibitions, music concerts, and theater performances.

Natural Attractions of Scandicci: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Parco della Villetta: A beautiful park located in the heart of Scandicci, featuring landscaped gardens, a small lake, and a playground for children.
  • Monte Morello: A mountain range located just a few kilometers away from Scandicci, offering breathtaking views of Tuscany. There are several hiking and biking trails that you can explore.
  • Tenuta di Artimino: A picturesque estate that offers vineyard tours, wine tasting, and a scenic view of the countryside.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Scandicci

Scandicci offers plenty of opportunities for outdoor enthusiasts to explore the stunning Tuscan hills. Here are some of the best hiking and biking routes:

  • Monte Morello Trail: A challenging hiking trail that takes you up to the summit of Monte Morello, offering magnificent views of Tuscany.
  • The Ring of Scandicci Trail: A moderate hiking trail that takes you through the beautiful countryside, passing by historic villas and churches.
  • La Bella Scandicci Bike Route: A scenic bike route that takes you through the hills surrounding Scandicci, passing by vineyards, olive groves, and picturesque villages.

Best Things to Do in Scandicci: Activities and Tours

Apart from the cultural attractions and outdoor adventures, Scandicci also offers several unique activities and tours that you can enjoy during your visit:

  • Cooking classes: Discover the secrets of Tuscan cuisine by taking a cooking class with a local chef, learning how to make classic dishes like pasta and pizza.
  • Wine-tasting tours: Explore the rich wine culture of Tuscany by taking a wine-tasting tour of the vineyards and wineries surrounding Scandicci.
  • Hot-air balloon rides: Experience the stunning beauty of Tuscany from a different perspective by taking a hot-air balloon ride over the hills of Scandicci.

Week-Long Itinerary for Scandicci

Here’s a suggested itinerary for a week-long stay in Scandicci:

Food and Drink Scene of Scandicci

Tuscany is known worldwide for its delicious cuisine and wine culture, and Scandicci is no exception. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Maccheroni al Forno: a classic Tuscan pasta dish made with macaroni, tomato sauce, bechamel, and Parmigiano Reggiano.
  • Bistecca alla Fiorentina: A grilled T-bone steak served rare, seasoned with salt and pepper, and drizzled with olive oil.
  • Cantucci: A type of almond biscotti that is typically served with Vin Santo, a sweet dessert wine.

Local Beverages

  • Chianti: A famous red wine from the Chianti region, known for its fruity flavor and robust body.
  • Vinsanto: A sweet dessert wine made from dried grapes, often served with cantucci.
  • Limoncello: A lemon liqueur that is popular in the region and is often served as an after-dinner digestif.

Where to Stay in Scandicci: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Scandicci offers plenty of accommodation options, catering to a wide range of budgets and preferences. Here are some of the best hotels, hostels, and guesthouses:

  • Hotel Marzia: A four-star hotel located in the center of Scandicci, offering modern rooms and excellent service.
  • Camping Internazionale Firenze: A budget-friendly camping site located just a few kilometers away from Scandicci, offering tent and mobile home rentals.
  • Il Castello delle Serre: A luxurious guesthouse located in the nearby village of Serre di Rapolano, offering beautiful rooms and a stunning view of the countryside.

What to Buy in Scandicci: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Scandicci is known for its traditional terracotta production, which dates back to the medieval times. Here are some of the best souvenirs and local products to bring home:

  • Terracotta products: You can find beautiful terracotta vases, plates, and tiles in various shops and markets around Scandicci.
  • Extra-virgin olive oil: Tuscany is famous for its high-quality olive oil, and Scandicci is no exception. You can buy it directly from the local producers.
  • Leather goods: Florence is known for its leather production, and Scandicci is just a stone’s throw away. You can find beautiful leather bags, belts, and wallets in various shops and markets.

Local Culture and Customs of Scandicci

Scandicci is a charming town that cherishes its traditions and customs. Here are some cultural insights that can help you blend in with the locals:

  • Dress appropriately: Italians are known for their sense of style, and Scandicci is no exception. Dress smartly, especially when visiting churches or formal events.
  • Try speaking Italian: Italians are proud of their language, and they appreciate when visitors make an effort to speak it. Learn a few basic phrases before you arrive.
  • Respect religious traditions: Tuscany is deeply rooted in Catholicism, and Scandicci is no exception. Visit churches and other religious sites with respect, and dress appropriately.

Best Time to Visit Scandicci

The best time to visit Scandicci is during the spring and autumn months, when the weather is mild and pleasant, and the crowds are fewer. The summer months can be hot and crowded, while the winter months can be cold and rainy.

Do’s and Don’ts of Scandicci

Here are some do’s and don’ts to keep in mind while visiting Scandicci:

Do:

  • Do dress smartly, especially when visiting churches or formal events.
  • Do try speaking Italian, even if it’s just a few basic phrases.
  • Do respect local customs and traditions.

Don’t:

  • Don’t litter or behave disrespectfully in public areas.
  • Don’t visit religious sites in inappropriate clothing.
  • Don’t make unnecessary noise or disturbance during the night.

Safety and Health

Safety in Scandicci

Scandicci is a safe town, but like any other tourist destination, there are some safety tips to keep in mind:

  • Keep your belongings secure and don’t leave them unattended in public areas.
  • Be aware of pickpockets, especially in crowded places like markets and public transportation.
  • Be cautious when driving on narrow and winding roads, especially in the hills.

Health in Scandicci

Scandicci is part of a developed country with a high standard of healthcare. However, it’s always a good idea to take some basic precautions:

  • Drink bottled or filtered water to avoid stomach issues.
  • Wear sunscreen and protective clothing to avoid sunburn.
  • Get vaccinated against common diseases like hepatitis A and B before your trip.

Getting to Scandicci: Transportation Options and Logistics

Scandicci is located just a few kilometers away from Florence, which makes it easily accessible by various means of transportation. Here are some options to consider:

Airports Near Scandicci

The closest airport to Scandicci is Florence Airport (FLR), which is located just 15 kilometers away. Other nearby airports include Pisa International Airport (PSA) and Bologna Guglielmo Marconi Airport (BLQ).

Bus Stations Near Scandicci

There are several bus stations that connect Scandicci with various destinations in Tuscany and beyond. The main bus station is located in the center of Scandicci, and it serves several bus companies.

Other Options

You can also reach Scandicci by train, taxi, or car. The main train station is located in Florence, and it’s well-connected with other cities in Italy and beyond. Taxis and car rentals are available at the airport or in Florence.

How to Get Around in Scandicci

Scandicci is a small town, and most of the attractions are within walking distance from each other. However, if you want to explore the nearby areas, here are some options:

  • Buses: Scandicci has a well-connected bus network that connects it with other towns and cities in Tuscany.
  • Taxis: Taxis are available in Scandicci, but they can be expensive. It’s best to negotiate the fare before you start your journey.
  • Car rental: You can rent a car from various rental companies in Florence or at the airport.

Where to Go Next from Scandicci: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Scandicci is a perfect base for exploring the beauty of Tuscany and beyond. Here are some nearby destinations and excursions to consider:

  • Florence: The cradle of Renaissance art and architecture, located just a few kilometers away from Scandicci.
  • Siena: A beautiful medieval city known for its Piazza del Campo and stunning cathedral, located about 70 kilometers away from Scandicci.
  • San Gimignano: The “city of towers”, famous for its ancient towers and its white wine, located about 50 kilometers away from Scandicci.

Conclusion

Scandicci is a charming town that offers an authentic Tuscan experience, with its unique blend of cultural heritage, natural beauty, and delicious cuisine. We hope this travel guide has provided you with all the essential information to plan your perfect trip to Scandicci. Buon viaggio!
