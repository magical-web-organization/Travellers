If you’re planning a trip to Tuscany, Italy, Pistoia Province is the perfect destination for those looking for a peaceful and serene escape. From stunning natural landscapes to culturally significant attractions and a thriving food scene, Pistoia has something for everyone. Explore this idyllic province with our comprehensive travel guide to Pistoia.

Introduction to Pistoia (Province)

Pistoia is located in the heart of Tuscany and is known for its charming medieval architecture, beautiful landscapes, and vibrant culture. The province is less touristy than other popular towns in Tuscany such as Florence and Siena, making it an ideal spot for travelers looking for an authentic Italian experience.

Reaching Pistoia is easy, as it is well-connected by public transport. You can reach the province by bus, train or car. The nearest airports are Florence and Pisa, which are both less than an hour away.

List of Places to Visit in Pistoia (Province)

There are many things to do and see in Pistoia. Here are some of the top ones:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places:

  • Cathedral of San Zeno: This is a beautiful Romanesque church located in the heart of Pistoia. It is a must-visit attraction for history and architecture buffs.

  • Palazzo dei Vescovi: Constructed in the 14th-century, Palazzo dei Vescovi is a magnificent building with intricate architecture. It currently houses various exhibitions and is a great attraction for art lovers.

Natural Attractions of Pistoia (Province): Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views:

  • Abetone: Abetone is a popular ski resort located in the province of Pistoia. It is an ideal destination for winter sports lovers.

  • Val di Luce: This is a scenic area located in the mountains with a variety of hiking trails that offer breathtaking views.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Pistoia (Province)

Pistoia is home to some of the most beautiful hiking and biking trails in Tuscany. Below are some of the top routes:

  • Svizzera Pesciatina: This is a beautiful trail that connects different medieval villages in the province. It is known for its scenic beauty and provides a great way to explore the region.

  • Pistoia to San Marcello: This is a beautiful bike route that connects the city of Pistoia to San Marcello. It offers scenic views of the mountains and lush green forests.

Best Things to Do in Pistoia (Province): Activities and Tours

From food tours to walking tours of the city, Pistoia offers something for everyone. Here are some of the best activities and tours to do in the province:

  • Food Tours of Pistoia: Pistoia is known for its delicious cuisine, and a food tour is a great way to taste the local delicacies and explore the culinary culture of the region.

  • Walking Tours of Pistoia: Walking tours of Pistoia are a great way to explore the medieval architecture of the city and learn about its rich history.

Week-Long Itinerary for Pistoia (Province)

Here is a great 7-day itinerary for Pistoia:

  • Day 1: Explore the city of Pistoia, visit the cathedral, and sample local delicacies.

  • Day 2: Take a biking tour of the surrounding countryside.

  • Day 3: Enjoy a day of skiing at Abetone.

  • Day 4: Explore the area around Val di Luce and take a scenic hike.

  • Day 5: Visit the Pescia paper mills, which are known for their handmade paper.

  • Day 6: Take a wine tour of the region and sample some of the local wines.

  • Day 7: Spend the day at the spa at Montecatini, which is known for its therapeutic waters and treatments.

Food and Drink Scene of Pistoia (Province)

Pistoia is known for its delicious cuisine, which is heavily influenced by the ingredients and flavors of Tuscany. Below are some of the best food and drink options to try in the province:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Bistecca alla fiorentina: This is a classic Tuscan dish and is a must-try when in Pistoia.

  • Arancini di Riso: This is a traditional dish made of rice, tomato sauce, and meat, and is a great option for lunch or a snack.

Local Beverages

  • Chianti: This is a popular wine from the Tuscany region and pairs well with the local cuisine.

  • Amaro del Cardinale: This is a popular after-dinner drink made from a blend of herbs and spices.

Where to Stay in Pistoia (Province): Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Pistoia offers a variety of accommodation options for travelers, from budget-friendly hostels to luxurious hotels. Here are some of the top options:

  • Hotel Piccola Firenze: This hotel is located in the heart of Pistoia and offers spacious rooms and great amenities.

  • Hostel Pistoia: This budget-friendly hostel is a great option for backpackers and solo travelers.

What to Buy in Pistoia (Province): Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Pistoia is known for its handmade paper and locally produced food products. These make for great souvenirs and gifts to take back home.

Local Culture and Customs of Pistoia (Province)

Pistoia has a rich cultural heritage, and locals are proud of their traditions. Visitors should be respectful of local customs, such as dress codes for visiting religious sites.

Best Time to Visit Pistoia (Province)

The best time to visit Pistoia is during the summer months when the weather is pleasant and outdoor activities are in full swing.

Do’s and Don’ts of Pistoia (Province)

Do:

  • Respect local customs and traditions.

  • Try the local cuisine and wines.

Don’t:

  • Wear revealing clothing when visiting religious sites.

  • Cause a disturbance in public places.

Safety and Health

Safety in Pistoia (Province)

Pistoia is a safe destination for travelers, but it is always important to be aware of your surroundings and take precautions to stay safe.

Health in Pistoia (Province)

Pistoia has a good healthcare system, with a number of hospitals and clinics located in the region. It is recommended that travelers have travel insurance in case of emergency medical treatment.

How to Get Around in Pistoia (Province)

Public transport is the best way to get around Pistoia and the surrounding area. Buses and trains are frequent, and taxis are readily available.
