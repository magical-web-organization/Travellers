Introduction to Forte Dei Marmi

Forte Dei Marmi is a charming town located on the Tuscan coast of Italy in the province of Lucca. It’s a popular summer destination for locals and tourists alike, known for its beautiful beaches and exclusive resorts. Despite its small size, Forte Dei Marmi is rich in history, culture, and natural beauty, offering visitors a wide variety of activities and sights to explore.

List of Places to Visit in Forte Dei Marmi

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Forte Dei Marmi has several museums and historical places that highlight the town’s rich heritage. Some of the must-visit attractions include:

  • Fortino Lorenese: A historic fort located at the heart of the town. The fort was built in the 18th century as part of the coastal defense system against pirates and foreign attacks. Today, it houses a museum that displays the history of Forte Dei Marmi and hosts several cultural events throughout the year.
  • Ugo Guidi Museum: This art museum is dedicated to the work of Ugo Guidi, a famous Italian artist who lived and worked in Forte Dei Marmi for several years. The museum features a permanent collection of Guidi’s paintings, sculptures, and drawings, as well as temporary exhibitions of contemporary art from around the world.
  • La Capannina: An iconic nightclub located on the beach promenade of Forte Dei Marmi. La Capannina has been attracting celebrities and VIPs since its opening in 1929, and it’s considered one of the symbols of Italian nightlife. The club has a vintage atmosphere and hosts live music and DJ sets every night during the summer season.

Natural Attractions of Forte Dei Marmi: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

In addition to its cultural attractions, Forte Dei Marmi also boasts stunning natural landscapes and outdoor activities. Here are some of the best things to do:

  • Beaches: Forte Dei Marmi is famous for its beautiful beaches, which are considered some of the best in Italy. The town has several private and public beaches, equipped with all the necessary facilities for a comfortable day in the sun. Some of the most popular beaches include Bagno Piero, Twiga Beach Club, and La Capannina.
  • Apuane Alps: The Apuane Alps are a mountain range located near Forte Dei Marmi, offering breathtaking views of the surrounding landscape. The area is perfect for hiking, mountain biking, and rock climbing, and there are several trails and routes of varying difficulty levels.
  • Versilia Golf Club: This 18-hole golf course is situated in the heart of Forte Dei Marmi, surrounded by lush vegetation and stunning views of the mountains and the sea. The club also has a driving range, a putting green, and a clubhouse with a restaurant and a bar.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Forte Dei Marmi

Forte Dei Marmi is a great destination for outdoor enthusiasts, with several hiking and biking routes to explore. Here are some of the most popular options:

  • Monte Forato: This hike is a moderately challenging trail that takes around 5-6 hours to complete. The highlight of the route is a natural archway known as the “eye of the needle,” located at the summit of the mountain. The trail offers stunning views of the Apuane Alps and the surrounding valleys.
  • Bike ride along the coast: Forte Dei Marmi has a beautiful bike path that runs along the coastline, connecting the town with neighboring seaside villages. The trail is flat and easy, suitable for all levels of fitness, and offers picturesque views of the sea and the pine forests.
  • Pietrasanta to Sant’Anna di Stazzema: This hiking trail is a challenging trek that takes around 8 hours to complete. The route starts in the artistic town of Pietrasanta and leads to the village of Sant’Anna di Stazzema, passing through chestnut forests, streams, and ancient ruins. The trail offers stunning views of the mountains and the coast.

Best Things to Do in Forte Dei Marmi: Activities and Tours

Forte Dei Marmi offers a wide range of activities and tours that cater to different interests and preferences. Here are some of the best things to do in the town:

  • Boat tour of the coast: Take a boat tour along the coast of Forte Dei Marmi and admire the stunning views of the sea and the mountains from a different perspective. Some tours also include stops at secluded coves and beaches for a swim or relaxation.
  • Cooking class: Learn how to cook traditional Tuscan dishes with a local chef and explore the culinary traditions of the region. Classes are held in small groups and usually include a visit to a local market to buy fresh ingredients.
  • Wine tasting: Tuscany is known for its high-quality wines, and Forte Dei Marmi is no exception. Take a wine tasting tour and sample some of the best local wines, accompanied by cheeses and cured meats.

Week-Long Itinerary for Forte Dei Marmi

To fully experience all that Forte Dei Marmi has to offer, here’s a sample week-long itinerary:

  • Day 1: Explore the town center and visit Fortino Lorenese and Ugo Guidi Museum.
  • Day 2: Relax on the beach and try some water sports activities.
  • Day 3: Take a bike ride along the coast and stop at some of the picturesque villages.
  • Day 4: Hike to Monte Forato and enjoy the stunning views of the Alps.
  • Day 5: Take a boat tour of the coast and swim in the crystal-clear waters.
  • Day 6: Learn how to cook traditional Tuscan dishes in a cooking class.
  • Day 7: Visit Pietrasanta and nearby villages and shop for local products.

Food and Drink Scene of Forte Dei Marmi

Tuscany is known for its rich gastronomy, and Forte Dei Marmi is no exception. The town has several restaurants, trattorias, and bars that serve delicious local dishes and drinks. Here are some of the must-try options:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Pappa al pomodoro: A traditional Tuscan soup made with stale bread, tomatoes, olive oil, and basil.
  • Bistecca alla Fiorentina: A grilled beef steak seasoned with rosemary, salt, and pepper. It’s usually served rare or medium-rare and accompanied by grilled vegetables.
  • Truffle pasta: A pasta dish made with fresh truffles, garlic, olive oil, and parmesan cheese. Truffles are a local specialty in Tuscany and add a unique flavor to any dish.

Local Beverages

  • Chianti wine: Chianti is one of the most famous wine regions in Tuscany, known for its full-bodied red wines made from the Sangiovese grape.
  • Limoncello: This lemon liqueur is a popular digestif in Italy and is made from lemon zest, alcohol, water, and sugar.
  • Negroni: A classic Italian cocktail made with gin, vermouth, and Campari. It’s typically served before dinner as an apéritif.

Where to Stay in Forte Dei Marmi: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Forte Dei Marmi has plenty of accommodation options to suit different budgets and preferences. Some of the best hotels, hostels, and guesthouses include:

  • Grand Hotel Imperiale: A luxury hotel located in the heart of Forte Dei Marmi, just a few steps away from the beach. The hotel has a spa, a swimming pool, and a restaurant that serves local and international cuisine.
  • B&B La Casa nel Bosco: A charming bed and breakfast located in a quiet residential area of Forte Dei Marmi. The B&B has a garden and a terrace and serves a continental breakfast every morning.
  • Ostello Palazzo Nizza: A budget-friendly hostel located in an elegant building in the town center. The hostel has shared and private rooms, a communal kitchen, and a lounge area.

What to Buy in Forte Dei Marmi: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Forte Dei Marmi is known for its high-end fashion boutiques, artisanal shops, and local markets. Some of the best souvenirs and products to bring home include:

  • Marble sculptures: Forte Dei Marmi is famous for its marble quarries, and many local artisans create beautiful sculptures and objects using the local stone. Look for small souvenirs or larger pieces to decorate your home.
  • Olive oil: Tuscany is known for its high-quality olive oil, and Forte Dei Marmi has several producers that sell the local product. Look for bottles labeled “olio extra vergine di oliva” and ask the producer for his or her recommendations.
  • Linen clothing: Linen is a popular fabric in Tuscany, thanks to its lightness and breathability. Look for linen clothing items such as shirts, dresses, and skirts, and choose from a variety of colors and patterns.

Local Culture and Customs of Forte Dei Marmi

Forte Dei Marmi is a small town that prides itself on its traditions and local identity. Some of the customs and cultural events to look out for include:

  • La Fiera di Sant’Ermete: This annual festival takes place in Forte Dei Marmi in late August and is dedicated to the town’s patron saint, Sant’Ermete. The festival features parades, live music, street food, and fireworks.
  • Baccalà alla Forte Dei Marmi: This is a local specialty dish that consists of dried and salted codfish served with potatoes, onions, parsley, and olive oil. It’s a must-try for seafood lovers.
  • Beach clubs: Forte Dei Marmi is famous for its exclusive beach clubs, where visitors can rent sun loungers, umbrellas, and cabins for a day or a season. Some clubs also offer entertainment and events during the summer months.

Best Time to Visit Forte Dei Marmi

The best time to visit Forte Dei Marmi is during the summer months, from June to August, when the weather is warm and sunny, and the beaches are at their best. However, this is also the busiest and most crowded season, so be prepared for higher prices and more tourists. For a quieter and more affordable experience, consider visiting in the shoulder seasons, from May to June or September to October.

Do’s and Don’ts of Forte Dei Marmi

Do:

  • Do try the local dishes and drinks, and explore the culinary traditions of Tuscany.
  • Do visit the local markets and shops and look for unique souvenirs and products.
  • Do respect the local customs and traditions, and learn some basic Italian phrases.

  • Don’t forget to pack sunscreen and a hat, as the sun can be strong during the summer months.

Safety and Health

Safety in Forte Dei Marmi

Forte Dei Marmi is a safe town with low crime rates. However, visitors should take basic precautions such as keeping an eye on their belongings and not leaving valuables unattended on the beach. It’s also important to follow the safety rules and regulations of the beach clubs, especially regarding swimming and water sports.

Health in Forte Dei Marmi

Forte Dei Marmi has several pharmacies and medical centers that offer basic health care services. Visitors should bring their prescription medicines and travel insurance, as well as take precautions against the sun and the heat.

  • Florence Airport: This airport is located approximately 108 km (67 miles) away from Forte Dei Marmi and offers flights to major Italian and European destinations.

Bus Stations Near Forte Dei Marmi

  • Forte Dei Marmi Bus Station: This is the main bus station of Forte Dei Marmi, located in the town center. The station offers regional and national connections to other cities in Tuscany and Italy.
  • Viareggio Bus Station: This bus station is located approximately 15 km (9 miles) away from Forte Dei Marmi and offers connections to other destinations along the coast.

Other Options

  • Car rental: Visitors can rent a car from either the Pisa International Airport or the Florence Airport and drive to Forte Dei Marmi. The town has several parking areas, but be aware that parking can be difficult during the summer months.
  • Train: The closest train station to Forte Dei Marmi is the Viareggio Train Station, located approximately 15 km (9 miles) away. The station offers connections to other cities in Tuscany and Italy, and there are frequent buses that run between the station and Forte Dei Marmi.

How to Get Around in Forte Dei Marmi

Forte Dei Marmi is a small town that can be easily explored on foot or by bike. There are also several taxis and buses that offer transportation services within the town and to other nearby destinations. Visitors can also rent a car or a scooter to explore the surrounding areas.
