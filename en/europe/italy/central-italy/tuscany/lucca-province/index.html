Introduction to Lucca (province)

Located in the picturesque region of Tuscany, Lucca (province) is a hidden gem that offers visitors an authentic Italian experience. The province’s capital, Lucca, is one of the most charming cities in Tuscany, with its ancient walls, cobblestone streets, and remarkable Renaissance-era architecture. However, Lucca’s appeal is not limited to just its capital city - this region is packed with historic landmarks, stunning landscapes, and diverse cultural experiences. Whether you plan to explore museums, embark on hiking or biking trails, or simply relax and savor local cuisine, Lucca has it all!

List of Places to Visit in Lucca (province)

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  1. Lucca Cathedral - a stunning Romanesque cathedral with intricate sculptures and frescoes.
  2. Palazzo Pfanner - a Baroque-style villa that houses impressive art collections.
  3. Museum of Villa Guinigi - an art museum inside a 14th-century palace, filled with ancient sculptures, ceramics and artworks.
  4. Puccini Museum - a tribute to the famous Italian opera composer, Giacomo Puccini, who was born in Lucca.
  5. San Giovanni church - A gothic styled church that was built in the 13th century.

Natural Attractions of Lucca (province): Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  1. Versilia - A coastal destination that offers sun, sand, and sea.
  2. Garfagnana - A mountainous area located to the north of Lucca. The region for trekking and outdoor activities.
  3. Apuan Alps - The mountain range with the highest peak of Pania della Croce. Offers multiple hiking trails and scenic spots.
  4. Isola Santa - A small island located in the Serchio River, with beautiful views of the surrounding greenery.
  5. Botanical Garden - An incredible collection of plants from Tuscany and beyond surrounded by the bastions of the city walls.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Lucca (province)

  1. The Lucca Wall - This magnificent wall is about 4 kilometers long. It is a great spot for walking and cycling, providing stunning views of the city.
  2. Montecarlo (in Lucca province) Wine Path - a 22-kilometer trail that winds through the charming Tuscan countryside and passes by several vineyards.
  3. The Orecchiella Natural Reserve - Offers some of the most beautiful hikes in the area and an opportunity to see some of the rarest animals in northern Tuscany.

Best Things to Do in Lucca (province): Activities and Tours

  1. Take a cooking class to learn about Italian cuisine.
  2. Visit some of the wineries in the province to experience wine tasting.
  3. Go truffle hunting in the nearby hills.
  4. Attend the Puccini Opera Festival in July and August.
  5. Explore the province’s beautiful landscapes on a guided tour.

Week-Long Itinerary for Lucca (province)

Day 1: Explore the charming city of Lucca, including the Lucca Cathedral, San Giovanni church and Puccini Museum. Day 2: Hike or cycle the Lucca Wall and enjoy the stunning views of the surrounding countryside. Day 3: Visit the Museum of Villa Guinigi and discover the province’s art scene. Day 4: Drive to the Garfagnana region and trek to the Orecchiella Natural Reserve. Day 5: Take a wine tour in the Montecarlo Wine Path and learn about Tuscany’s vineyards. Day 6: Discover Lucca’s local cuisine with a cooking class, followed by a truffle hunt. Day 7: Wrap up your trip by exploring the Botanical Garden and enjoying some leisure time in Versilia’s beaches.

Food and Drink Scene of Lucca (province)

Must-Try Dishes

  1. Bistecca alla Fiorentina - A grilled T-bone steak.
  2. Farro soup - A healthy and hearty soup made with spelt and vegetables.
  3. Pasta dishes - The province’s pasta dishes, such as tagliatelle al ragù and pesto pasta, are simply delicious.
  4. Cacciucco - a seafood stew of the nearby coastal regions.
  5. Zuccotto - a traditional dessert made with cake and chocolate.

Local Beverages

  1. Chianti - The most famous wine of Tuscany.
  2. Vin Santo - A dessert wine that pairs perfectly with local dishes.
  3. Limoncello - A refreshing lemon liqueur.

Where to Stay in Lucca (province): Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  1. Palazzo Alexander - A beautiful 14th-century palace turned hotel located in the heart of Lucca.
  2. Borgo La Sorbina - A luxurious resort surrounded by olive groves that offers an authentic Tuscan experience.
  3. Bed and Breakfast Villa Sunrise - Located in a quiet hillside of Lucca, the hotel offers great views and cozy accommodations.
  4. Il Casale di Nanni - Located near Montecarlo, this family-owned guesthouse offers an intimate and comfortable stay.
  5. Park Hotel California - Located in Versilia, the hotel is the perfect spot for those who want to relax and enjoy the nearby beaches.

What to Buy in Lucca (province): Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  1. Olive Oil - Tuscany is well-known for its delicious olive oil.
  2. Handmade Ceramics - The province has a long tradition of pottery-making.
  3. Local Wines and Liqueurs - Bring home a bottle or two of Tuscany’s most famous beverages.
  4. Leather products - Lucca is famous for its leather goods, including bags, shoes, and jackets.
  5. Truffles - Take home a taste of the Lucca countryside by bringing home some locally grown truffles.

Local Culture and Customs of Lucca (province)

Lucca’s culture is heavily influenced by its rich history and diverse geography. The locals are proud of their heritage and are happy to share it with visitors. In terms of customs, the province values family and good food. Lucca’s cuisine is known for its simple, yet flavorful dishes, and sharing meals with loved ones is a common custom. It is also customary to dress well when going out in public.

Best Time to Visit Lucca (province)

The best time to visit Lucca (province) is between April and June or September and October. During these months, the weather is mild, and the crowds are thinner. The summer months can be hot and crowded, while the winter months can be chilly and damp. Planning to visit during the Puccini Opera Festival season can also be a great idea.

Do’s and Don’ts of Lucca (province)

Do:

  1. Do try to speak Italian, even if it’s just a few simple words.
  2. Do respect local customs and dress appropriately when visiting churches or historic landmarks.
  3. Do try the local cuisine, including olive oil, cheeses, and wines.

Don’t:

  1. Don’t litter or leave your trash behind.
  2. Don’t expect everyone to speak English.
  3. Don’t touch or remove any artifacts in museums or historic landmarks.

Safety and Health

Safety in Lucca (province)

Lucca is generally a safe place to visit. However, it is recommended to be mindful of pickpockets in busy tourist areas. It is also advisable to travel with a partner or in a group when exploring the province’s natural areas.

Health in Lucca (province)

There are no specific health risks in Lucca (province). However, it is always best to have a basic travel insurance plan, as accidents can happen unexpectedly. If you need medical attention, the province has numerous hospitals and pharmacies to provide assistance.

Getting to Lucca (province): Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Lucca (province)

The closest airport to Lucca is the Pisa International Airport. From there, you can take a train or a bus to Lucca.

Bus Stations Near Lucca (province)

The city of Lucca has a central bus station, with regular connections to other cities and nearby towns.

Other Options

If you are traveling from Florence, you can take a train to Lucca. The journey takes approximately 1.5 hours.

How to Get Around in Lucca (province)

The best way to explore Lucca (province) is by road, so it’s best to rent a car. Alternatively, trains and buses are also available, which can take you to the major towns and cities within the province.

Where to Go Next from Lucca (province): Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  1. Florence - A city famous for its art, architecture, and history, located just 70 kilometers from Lucca.
  2. San Gimignano - A stunning medieval town located in the Tuscan countryside, only 100 kilometers from Lucca.
  3. Genoa - A picturesque port city located on the Ligurian coast, just 115 kilometers from Lucca.
  4. Cinque Terre - A group of charming coastal towns on the Italian Riviera, just 80 kilometers from Lucca.
  5. Siena - A picturesque town full of historic landmarks, including the stunning Siena Cathedral and the Piazza del Campo.

Conclusion

A trip to Lucca (province) is a marvelous way to experience Tuscany’s charm and natural beauty. From the province’s stunning landscapes to its rich culture and cuisine, Lucca boasts a wealth of experiences. Whether you plan to hike the hills, discover hidden gems in museums, or just relax and enjoy local wines, Lucca has something for everyone. Discover Lucca, and discover the beauty of authentic Italy.
