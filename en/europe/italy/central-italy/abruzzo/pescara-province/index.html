Your Ultimate Travel Guide to Pescara (Province), Italy

Welcome to Pescara (Province), a breathtaking destination in the heart of Central Italy’s Abruzzo region. Largely unspoiled by tourism, this hidden gem offers a perfect mix of tranquil countryside and stunning coastline. From hiking in the rugged mountains of the Gran Sasso National Park to exploring the beaches on the Adriatic coast, Pescara (Province) has something for everyone.

Introduction to Pescara (Province)

Pescara (Province) is a province in the Abruzzo region of Central Italy, which comprises four major zones - Aventino, Pescara, Vestina, and Valle Peligna. It is bordered by Teramo to the north, L’Aquila to the west, and the Adriatic Sea to the east. You’ll find a rich cultural heritage, gorgeous countryside with diverse flora and fauna, unspoiled beaches, and some of the best seafood in Italy.

List of Places to Visit in Pescara (Province)

Here are some must-visit attractions in Pescara (Province):

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Pescara Cathedral: This stunning 1930s cathedral is a must-visit site for its beautiful architecture and impressive decor.
  • Museo delle Genti d’Abruzzo: Located in Pescara, this museum features exhibits that showcase the region’s culture, art, and tradition.
  • Museo Civico di Penne: This museum in Penne houses artifacts and artwork from the historic town and surrounding areas.

Natural Attractions of Pescara (Province): Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Gran Sasso National Park: This national park boasts of lush green forests, picturesque towns, and wildflowers. It is an ideal destination for hiking, mountaineering, and skiing.
  • Corno Grande: The highest peak in the Apennine Mountains, Corno Grande offers spectacular views of the surrounding valleys, meadows, and lakes.
  • Riserva Naturale di Punta Aderci: This stretch of the Adriatic coast boasts of lovely beaches, crystal-clear waters, and stunning cliffs.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Pescara (Province)

Pescara (Province) offers a plethora of hiking/biking trails that cater to different levels of experience and fitness levels. Here are a few:

  • The Path of Abruzzo’s Great Falls: This epic trail covers picturesque landscapes, ancient villages, and stunning waterfalls.
  • Abruzzo National Park: The longest trekking routes go through the outstanding Gran Sasso National Park. With paths that link a range of mountains, there is a trail for everyone’s ability level.
  • The High Path of the Majella: This trail takes you through magnificent mountains, lush green valleys, and charming towns.

Best Things to Do in Pescara (Province): Activities and Tours

  1. Local Wine Tasting: Abruzzo is home to some of Italy’s most celebrated winemakers.
  2. Go Truffle Hunting: Experience the thrill of searching for black and white gold within Italy’s lush forests.
  3. Sea Kayaking: Paddle along the coastline of Pescara (Province) for a peaceful and scenic view of the area.

Food and Drink Scene of Pescara (Province)

Pescara (Province) is a gastronomic paradise with a food-loving culture that celebrates the local produce. Here are some must-try dishes and drinks:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Arrosticini: These tender pieces of skewered lamb are an Abruzzo specialty.
  • Scrippelle: These thin, rolled pancakes are served in a light broth and topped with cheese and herbs.
  • Pizzelle: These crisp, waffle-like cookies flavored with anise are popular in Pescara and the surrounding areas.

Local Beverages

  • Montepulciano Wine: A full-bodied red wine that pairs well with Abruzzo’s hearty cuisine.
  • Limoncello: This sweet and tangy liqueur is made from the region’s lemons.

Where to Stay in Pescara (Province): Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

From luxurious five-star hotels to intimate guesthouses, Pescara (Province) offers accommodation to suit all preferences and budgets. Here are some of the best places to stay:

  • Hotel Carlton: This stylish hotel in Pescara offers stunning views of the Adriatic Sea and is within easy reach of the city’s main attractions.
  • La Plaza residence: A delightful hotel, it is situated in the heart of Atri’s historical center.
  • Agriturismo Il Portone di Basso: This farmhouse-turned-guesthouse, located amid rolling hills, offers visitors a rustic Roman experience with modern amenities.

What to Buy in Pescara (Province): Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Make your trip to Pescara (Province) unforgettable with these local souvenirs:

  • Truffle oil: Bring home the taste of Abruzzo by picking up a bottle of truffle oil.
  • Regional cheeses and sausages: The rich and diverse flavors of the region’s cheeses and sausages make them an excellent souvenir.
  • Liquorice sweets: These locally made sweets are a popular dessert item in the region.

Local Culture and Customs of Pescara (Province)

The people in Pescara (Province) are warm, welcoming, and love to share their culture with visitors. To appreciate the local culture and customs, here are some things to note:

  • Respect the Dress Code: Many public buildings impose a dress code, so try to appropriately dressed in long pants or dresses and avoid shorts and tank tops.
  • Respect Quiet Hours: The towns of Pescara (Province) have an afternoon siesta, with many shops and restaurants being closed from around 2 pm to 5 pm.

Best Time to Visit Pescara (Province)

The ideal time to visit Pescara (Province) is from May to June or from September to October, when the weather is comfortable, and the crowds are few and far between.

Safety and Health

Pescara (Province) is known for being one of the safer provinces in Italy, but visitors should still take precautions to ensure their safety and health.

Safety in Pescara (Province)

  • Stay alert and aware of your surroundings when in crowded places.
  • Make sure you keep your valuables safe and secured.

Health in Pescara (Province)

  • Don’t forget to pack sunscreen as the summer months can get hot.
  • Be sure to stay hydrated and wash your hands regularly.
