Introduction to Pescara

Located in Abruzzo, Central Italy, Pescara is a lively coastal town that beautifully blends history and modernity. The town is known for its sandy beaches, vibrant nightlife, and delicious cuisine. With a rich history dating back to the Roman era, Pescara is a town that has something to offer for everyone.

List of Places to Visit in Pescara

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Museo delle Genti d’Abruzzo: This museum showcases the history and traditions of Abruzzo. It has a large collection of traditional costumes, tools, and handicrafts.

  • Museo Casa Natale di Gabriele D’Annunzio: This museum is dedicated to the famous Italian poet, Gabriele D’Annunzio, who was born in Pescara. It houses many of his personal belongings and memorabilia.

  • Le Sorgenti: This is an ancient Roman aqueduct that dates back to the 1st century AD. It is an impressive piece of engineering and a must-visit for history buffs.

Natural Attractions of Pescara: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Pinewood Nature Reserve: This is a beautiful nature reserve with hiking and biking trails, picnic areas, and a lake. It is a great place to spend a day with family and friends.

  • Pescara Beaches: Pescara is known for its long sandy beaches that are perfect for sunbathing and swimming. There are several public and private beaches to choose from.

  • Majella National Park: This is a large park with stunning mountain views, hiking trails, and abundant wildlife. It is a great place for a day trip or weekend getaway.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Pescara

  • Corno Grande: This is the highest peak in the Apennine Mountains, and it offers stunning views of the surrounding valleys.

  • Gran Sasso: This is a popular destination for hikers and bikers. It has several well-marked trails that lead to scenic viewpoints and waterfalls.

  • Route of the Trabocchi: This is a scenic biking route along the Adriatic coast that passes by several traditional fishing huts called trabocchi.

Best Things to Do in Pescara: Activities and Tours

  • Wine Tasting: Abruzzo is known for its excellent wines, and there are several wineries in and around Pescara that offer tastings and tours.

  • Cooking Classes: Learn how to make traditional Abruzzese dishes like arrosticini and chitarra pasta from local chefs.

  • Water Sports: Pescara’s beaches are perfect for water sports like surfing, kiteboarding, and paddleboarding. There are several schools and rental shops in the area.

Week-Long Itinerary for Pescara

Day 1: Visit the Museo delle Genti d’Abruzzo, followed by a relaxing afternoon at the beach.

Day 2: Take a hiking or biking trip to Corno Grande, followed by a wine tasting in the evening.

Day 3: Visit the Museo Casa Natale di Gabriele D’Annunzio, followed by a scenic drive to Gran Sasso.

Day 4: Spend a day at Majella National Park, hiking or biking in the morning and cooking classes in the afternoon.

Day 5: Relax at Pinewood Nature Reserve, followed by a seafood dinner at one of the trabocchi.

Day 6: Take a day trip to nearby towns like Loreto Aprutino or Chieti, known for their olive oil and wine respectively.

Day 7: Spend the last day shopping for souvenirs and enjoying the beach before heading home.

Food and Drink Scene of Pescara

Must-Try Dishes

  • Arrosticini: These are skewered lamb chops cooked over a wood fire. They are a staple of Abruzzese cuisine.

  • Chitarra alla Teramana: This is a type of spaghetti made with a special pasta cutter called a chitarra. It is typically served with a tomato-based sauce.

  • Scrippelle ‘Mbusse: These are crepes made with chicken broth and grated pecorino cheese. They are usually served as a starter.

Local Beverages

  • Montepulciano d’Abruzzo: This is the most famous wine of Abruzzo, known for its robust flavor and deep red color.

  • Cerasuolo d’Abruzzo: This is a rosé wine made with the same grape as Montepulciano. It is slightly lighter and fruitier.

  • Pecorino: This is a white wine made from the Pecorino grape. It is crisp and refreshing, with notes of citrus and minerality.

Where to Stay in Pescara: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Hotel Victoria: This is a centrally located hotel with comfortable rooms and friendly staff. It is within walking distance of the beach and many of the town’s attractions.

  • B&B Le Pietre Ricce: This is a charming bed and breakfast in the countryside just outside Pescara. It has a large garden and a swimming pool.

  • Hotel Carlton: This is a modern hotel near the train station, with spacious rooms and a rooftop bar.

What to Buy in Pescara: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Olive oil: Abruzzo is known for its high-quality olive oil, made from the local varieties of olives grown in the region.

  • Wines: Montepulciano and other Abruzzese wines are a great souvenir to bring home.

  • Artisanal crafts: Abruzzo is famous for its handmade ceramics, leather goods, and lace.

Local Culture and Customs of Pescara

Pescara is a friendly and welcoming town, with a strong sense of community. Respect for tradition and family values is important to the local people, and visitors should be aware of local attitudes towards dress and behavior.

Best Time to Visit Pescara

The best time to visit Pescara is from May to September, when the weather is warm and sunny, and the town is buzzing with activity. July and August are the busiest months, with many festivals and events taking place.

Do’s and Don’ts of Pescara

Do:

  • Dress modestly when visiting churches or other religious sites.
  • Try the local cuisine and wines.
  • Be respectful of local customs and traditions.

Don’t:

  • Smoke in public places.
  • Sunbathe topless on public beaches.
  • Be loud or disruptive in public places.

Safety and Health

Safety in Pescara

Pescara is a relatively safe town, but visitors should still take precautions to avoid theft and pickpocketing in crowded areas.

Health in Pescara

Medical facilities in Pescara are good, but visitors should have travel insurance and take precautions against sunstroke and insect bites during the summer months.

Getting to Pescara: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Pescara

The nearest airports to Pescara are Abruzzo Airport (PSR) and Rome Leonardo da Vinci Airport (FCO), both of which have good connections to major European cities.

Bus Stations Near Pescara

Pescara has a bus station located near the train station, with connections to other towns and cities in Abruzzo.

Other Options

Pescara is easily accessible by train, with connections to Rome and other major Italian cities. There are also several car rental companies located in the town.

How to Get Around in Pescara

Pescara is a relatively small town that can easily be explored on foot or by bike. There are also several bus routes that connect different parts of the town and the surrounding area.

Where to Go Next from Pescara: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Chieti: This historic town is known for its beautiful old town and excellent wine.

  • Sulmona: This is a picturesque town famous for its production of confetti (sugar-coated almonds).

  • Ortona: This is a coastal town with a beautiful old town and several beaches.

Conclusion

Pescara is a hidden gem that offers the perfect mix of history, nature, and culture. Its beautiful beaches, delicious cuisine, and friendly people make it a must-visit destination for anyone traveling to Italy.
