Introduction to Santo Stefano di Sessanio

Santo Stefano di Sessanio is a small medieval village, with a population of around 120 people. It is located in the province of L’Aquila, in the Abruzzo region of Italy. The town is famous for its perfectly preserved medieval architecture, which is a perfect example of the style of the earthquake-prone area of Abruzzo.

List of Places to Visit in Santo Stefano di Sessanio

Santo Stefano di Sessanio is home to many attractions and places to visit. Here are some of the must-see destinations that you should add to your itinerary:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • La Rocca: This well-preserved fortress is located at the top of the village and offers stunning views of the surrounding landscape.
  • Chiesa di San Pietro: This church was built in the 12th century and represents the Apulian Romanesque style. It hosts a beautiful fresco of Pietro da Morrone, later known as Pope Celestine V.
  • Museo dell’Arte Tessile: The Museum of Textile Art is a fascinating display of traditional Abruzzo weaving techniques and features machines, fabrics, and ancient textiles.

Natural Attractions of Santo Stefano di Sessanio: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Gran Sasso e Monti della Laga National Park: This stunning national park is located in the heart of Abruzzo and is home to a diverse range of flora and fauna species. Visitors can enjoy hiking, skiing, and wildlife viewing.
  • Campo Imperatore: This is the largest plain in the Apennines and is renowned for its unique flora and fauna, as well as its breathtaking views of the Gran Sasso Mountains.
  • Calascio Fortress: This stunning fortress offers views of some of the most beautiful natural landscapes of Santo Stefano di Sessanio, including the Apennines and the Adriatic Sea

Hiking/Biking Routes of Santo Stefano di Sessanio

For those looking to explore the natural beauty of Santo Stefano di Sessanio, hiking and biking routes are a must-try activity. Here are some of the best hiking and biking routes in Santo Stefano di Sessanio:

  • The Campo Imperatore circuit hike: This hike covers around 7km and takes hikers through a range of ecosystems, including the high-altitude plains of the Campo Imperatore.
  • The Stiffe Caves hike: The Stiffe Caves are a network of underground caves that offers a unique hiking experience through ancient rock formations.
  • The Grotta del Cavallone hike: The Grotta del Cavallone is a stunning cave that offers breathtaking views of the Gran Sasso Mountains and nearby valleys.

Food and Drink Scene of Santo Stefano di Sessanio

Santo Stefano di Sessanio is home to many traditional Abruzzo dishes and local beverages. Here are some of the must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Arrosticini: This is a traditional Abruzzo dish made from lamb skewers that are cooked over open coals.
  • Spaghetti alla Chitarra: A traditional Abruzzo pasta dish made from spaghetti-like noodles called chitarra. Often served with a tomato-based sauce.
  • Polenta e Salsiccia: Polenta served with grilled Abruzzo sausages.

Local Beverages

  • Montepulciano d’Abruzzo: A red wine that is one of the most famous wines in the region.
  • Pecorino: A white wine made from the Pecorino grape that is native to the Abruzzo region.
  • Genziana: A bitter liqueur that is made from the roots of the Gentian plant.
