Introduction to L’Aquila

Located in L’Aquila (province), in Abruzzo, in Central Italy, L’Aquila is a charming city with rich cultural heritage, beautiful natural attractions, and an abundance of outdoor activities.

List of Places to Visit in L’Aquila

If you’re planning a trip to L’Aquila, here are some of the top places that you shouldn’t miss:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Palazzo dell’Emiciclo is a beautiful 16th-century palace that is home to the Provincial Archaeological Museum of Abruzzo.
  • Basilica di San Bernardino is a magnificent church with beautiful frescoes and Renaissance artworks.
  • Museo Nazionale d’Abruzzo is a comprehensive museum that showcases the history, art, and culture of Abruzzo region.

Natural Attractions of L’Aquila: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Campo Felice is a popular ski resort that offers excellent skiing and snowboarding opportunities during the winter months.
  • Gran Sasso e Monti della Laga National Park is a beautiful nature reserve that boasts stunning landscapes, wildlife, and hiking trails.
  • Lago di Campotosto is a stunning lake that is perfect for swimming, fishing, and kayaking.

Hiking/Biking Routes of L’Aquila

L’Aquila is home to many beautiful hiking and biking trails that offer breathtaking views of the surrounding landscapes. Some of the best routes to explore include:

  • Gran Sasso Massif Trail, a challenging hike that takes you through the Gran Sasso e Monti della Laga National Park.
  • Campo Felice to Rocca di Cambio Trail, a scenic bike ride that takes you through the beautiful mountains and forests.
  • Grotta di Stiffe Trail, a moderate hike that takes you to the beautiful Stiffe Caves.

Best Things to Do in L’Aquila: Activities and Tours

Here are some of the best things to do in L’Aquila:

  • Take a guided tour of the city to explore its rich cultural heritage and history.
  • Join a cooking class to learn how to make traditional Abruzzese dishes.
  • Take a day trip to the stunning hilltop towns of Santo Stefano di Sessanio and Navelli.

Week-Long Itinerary for L’Aquila

If you have a week to explore L’Aquila, here’s a suggested itinerary that you can follow:

Day 4 Take a cooking class

Food and Drink Scene of L’Aquila

L’Aquila is famous for its delicious food and drink scene, which includes:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Arrosticini, grilled lamb skewers served with roasted vegetables.
  • Spaghetti alla chitarra, a traditional pasta dish made with eggs and flour.
  • Scrippelle, a soup made with pasta, chicken broth, and eggs.

Local Beverages

  • Montepulciano d’Abruzzo, a red wine with a rich, fruity flavor.
  • Pecorino, a white wine with floral and citrus notes.
  • Caffè d’orzo, a barley coffee that is popular in the region.

Where to Stay in L’Aquila: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

L’Aquila offers plenty of accommodation options to suit all budgets and preferences, including:

  • Hotel Federico II, a four-star hotel in the heart of L’Aquila.
  • Ostello di L’Aquila, a budget-friendly hostel that offers comfortable and clean accommodation.
  • B&B Sotto l’Arco, a charming guesthouse in the historic center of L’Aquila.

What to Buy in L’Aquila: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

If you’re looking for souvenirs and local products to bring home, here are some suggestions:

  • Porcelain figurines made by local artisans.
  • Tasty regional specialties, such as sausages, cheese, and sweets.
  • Handcrafted silver jewelry and accessories.

Local Culture and Customs of L’Aquila

L’Aquila has a rich cultural heritage that is reflected in its architecture, traditions, and festivals. Some of the customs and traditions that you might encounter during your visit include:

  • The “scoppio del carro,” a religious festival that takes place on Easter Sunday.
  • The “Sepoltella,” a traditional dance that is performed during weddings and other festive occasions.
  • The “aquilano” dialect, which is a unique and colorful language that is spoken in the region.

Best Time to Visit L’Aquila

The best time to visit L’Aquila is during the spring and fall months when the weather is mild, and the crowds are thinner. Winter is ideal for skiing activities, while summer is perfect for outdoor activities and enjoying the lake.

Do’s and Don’ts of L’Aquila

If you’re visiting L’Aquila, here are some things to keep in mind:

Do:

  • Respect the local customs and traditions.
  • Try the traditional food and drink specialties.
  • Learn some basic Italian phrases.

Don’t:

  • Don’t litter or damage the natural environment.
  • Don’t disrespect the religious customs and traditions.
  • Don’t forget to carry cash for small purchases and tips.

Safety and Health

L’Aquila is a safe city with low crime rates. However, it’s always essential to take necessary precautions to ensure your safety and health:

Safety in L’Aquila

  • Stay alert and aware of your surroundings, especially in crowded areas.
  • Keep your valuables secure and avoid carrying large sums of cash.
  • Read up on local scams and avoid falling prey to them.

Health in L’Aquila

  • Carry a first-aid kit and any necessary medication with you.
  • Drink bottled water and avoid tap water.
  • Apply sunscreen and wear appropriate clothing to protect yourself from the sun.

Getting to L’Aquila: Transportation Options and Logistics

L’Aquila is well-connected to other parts of Italy, making it easy to access by train, bus, and car.

Airports Near L’Aquila

The nearest airports to L’Aquila are Rome-Fiumicino and Rome-Ciampino.

Bus Stations Near L’Aquila

The main bus station in L’Aquila is the Terminal Bus di Piazza Maria Immacolata.

Other Options

You can also reach L’Aquila by train from Rome Termini or Pescara.

How to Get Around in L’Aquila

The best way to get around in L’Aquila is on foot or by bike. The city is quite compact, making it easy and enjoyable to explore on foot. Alternatively, you can rent a bike from one of the many rental services in the city.

Where to Go Next from L’Aquila: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

L’Aquila serves as an excellent base for exploring other parts of Abruzzo and central Italy. Here are some nearby destinations that are worth a visit:

  • Sulmona, a beautiful medieval town famous for its sugared almonds.
  • Rocca Calascio, a majestic fortress that offers stunning views of the surrounding mountains.
  • Teramo, a picturesque town that is home to numerous archaeological sites and museums.

Conclusion

L’Aquila is a captivating destination that offers a perfect blend of natural beauty, cultural heritage, and outdoor activities. Whether you’re a history buff, a nature lover, or simply looking for a relaxing getaway, L’Aquila has something for everyone. Plan your trip today and discover the best that this charming city has to offer.
