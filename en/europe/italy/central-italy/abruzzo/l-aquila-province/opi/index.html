Introduction to Opi

Nestled in the L’Aquila province of Abruzzo, Italy lies the tranquil village of Opi. Rich in history and surrounded by breathtaking scenery, Opi makes for the perfect getaway destination for travelers who wish to immerse themselves in Italy’s beauty and they will never forget. The village boasts of ancient traditions, baroque architecture, and lush forests. In this travel guide, we’ll take a closer look at the best things to do in Opi, where to stay, what to eat, and much more.

List of Places to Visit in Opi

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

For history buffs and art enthusiasts, Opi has a lot to offer. The village’s rich cultural heritage is evident in its museums and historical sites, including the following:

  • Opi Castle: A majestic castle that dates back to the medieval era, located in the heart of the village.
  • Church of Santissima Annunziata: A beautiful baroque church situated on Piazza Garibaldi that dates back to the 18th century.
  • Museum of Sacred Art: A museum that showcases the town’s religious art and artifacts dating back to the 17th century.

Natural Attractions of Opi: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Opi is surrounded by natural beauty - lush forests, towering mountains, and rolling hills. Here are some of the must-visit natural attractions:

  • Parco Nazionale d’Abruzzo: This national park is located near Opi and is home to a variety of wildlife species, including bears, wolves, and eagles. It offers excellent opportunities for hiking and wildlife watching.
  • The Scanno Lake: Situated 18 kilometers from Opi, this lake is one of the most beautiful natural spots in Italy and attracts many tourists for its photogenic view. Here, you can enjoy fishing, hiking, and photography.
  • Monte Meta: This towering mountain stands at 2242m tall and is great for hiking and trekking enthusiasts because it offers breathtaking panoramic views of the surrounding landscape.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Opi

Opi is an ideal destination for outdoor enthusiasts, with its surrounding lush forests, mountains, and hills. Here are some hiking and biking routes to take while in Opi:

  • Madonna di Pietracupa: This trail takes you into the heart of Abruzzo’s natural beauty. It’s an easy walk, with spectacular views of wildflowers, beech forest where you can spot the gorges.
  • Monte Marsicano: This peak can be reached after about an hour of climbing on a relatively steep path, but it’s worth it once you hit the top. The views are awe-inspiring and one can see a beautiful view of the surrounding valleys.

Best Things to Do in Opi: Activities and Tours

Apart from hiking and biking, there are plenty of other things to do in Opi, including the following:

  • Visit the farmers’ market to sample fresh local produce.
  • Try your hand at cheese-making in the local dairy farms.
  • Participate in the traditional festa and food festivals taking place throughout the year.
  • Join a guided tour of the village to learn more about its history and culture.

Week-Long Itinerary for Opi

Here’s a suggested week-long itinerary to experience the best of Opi:

Day 1: Arrival and check-in at your hotel, take a leisurely walk around the village. Day 2: Hike the Madonna di Pietracupa trail, then head to the town square for lunch. Day 3: Visit the Opi Castle, and the Church of Santissima Annunziata, then head to the Museum of Sacred Art. Day 4: Take a day trip to Monte Meta for a scenic view of the valley, picnic by the mountain, and return to your hotel in the evening. Day 5: Join a cheese-making tour in the local dairy farms and try your hand at making delicious cheese. Day 6: Visit Lago di Scanno, spend the day relaxing and taking photographs in this beautiful natural spot. Day 7: Departure day - check-out of your hotel and head to your next destination.

Food and Drink Scene of Opi

Italian cuisine is renowned all over the world, and Opi is no exception. Here are some of the must-try dishes and drinks while in Opi:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Arrosticini: Succulent, tender lamb skewers grilled over vine shoots; a quintessential Abruzzese dish.
  • Porchetta: Slow-roasted pork, seasoned with garlic, rosemary, and other aromatic herbs.
  • Pecorino cheese: A sharp, tangy, and salty cheese made from sheep’s milk and served with pasta or alongside bread as an appetizer.

Local Beverages

  • Montepulciano d’Abruzzo: This is a red wine made from Montepulciano d’Abruzzo grapes. It is rich, fruity with a full-bodied and high acidity.
  • Genziana: An alcoholic drink flavored with roots of the yellow gentian plant.

Where to Stay in Opi: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Opi offers plenty of accommodation options to suit all budgets. Here are some of our top picks:

  • Hotel La Réserve: This luxurious hotel is located in a quiet area in the center of the village, and it offers scenic views of the surrounding area.
  • B&B Il Nido dell’Aquila: This charming bed and breakfast is located in an old building that has been carefully restored, with beautifully decorated rooms and an inviting garden.
  • Agriturismo La Fonte: This farm stay provides guests with a unique experience, with comfortable rooms and the opportunity to take part in traditional farming activities.

What to Buy in Opi: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

When in Opi, here are some souvenirs and local products that you shouldn’t miss:

  • Local honey and jam made from fruit harvested in the region.
  • Homemade cheese and cured meats made in the local dairy farms
  • Local crafts like pottery, woolen sweaters, and lace.

Local Culture and Customs of Opi

Opi is a small village with a rich cultural heritage. Some customs and traditions that you might come across during your visit include:

  • Traditional festivals: Opi hosts several traditional festivals throughout the year, including the Festa di Sant’Antonio in January and the Sagra della Pecora in August.
  • Religious events: Expect to see religious processions through the village’s streets during events such as Holy Week and Corpus Domini.
  • Food and Drink: Opi’s culinary traditions are deeply ingrained in the community’s culture, and locals take pride in their traditional recipes and ingredients.

Best Time to Visit Opi

The best time to visit Opi is during the summer months of June to September when the weather is warm and sunny, and the village comes alive with festivals and events. You can enjoy hiking, biking and experience the area’s natural beauty with longer daylight hours.

Do’s and Don’ts of Opi

Do:

  • Respect the locals and their culture.
  • Try local food and house wine and savour the flavours of Abruzzese cuisine.
  • Admire the natural beauty, take photographs and leave nothing but memories.

Don’t:

  • Don’t litter, always dispose of any waste properly.
  • Don’t ignore the silence rules and signs in sensitive natural zones.
  • Don’t forget to dress appropriately for religious sites.

Safety and Health

Safety in Opi

Opi is a small village and generally safe for travelers. However, as with any destination, visitors should take basic precautions such as locking their hotel rooms and being aware of their surroundings.

### Health in Opi

The village has a medical center, and pharmacies are also available in the area. It’s always a good idea to check your health insurance coverage before traveling, and to carry with you essential medicine with a prescription.

Getting to Opi: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Opi

The closest airport to Opi is the Abruzzo Airport, located in Pescara, about 105 kilometers from the village. It is recommended to rent a car and drive through the scenic countryside.

Bus Stations Near Opi

Buses connect Opi to L’Aquila province’s major towns such as Sulmona or Castel di Sangro.

Other Options

The village of Opi is perfect for anyone who wants to explore on foot. It is easy to walk around the village and its surrounding hills. It is also possible to rent bicycles if you want to do a bit of cycling in the beautiful outdoors.

How to Get Around in Opi

The village is small, and it is easy to walk around. Visitors who want to explore the area further can rent a car or bike.

Where to Go Next from Opi: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

While in Opi, there are many nearby destinations worth visiting, including:

  • Roccaraso: A ski resort about 30 minutes from Opi.
  • Castel del Monte: A charming medieval town located about 40 kilometers from Opi.
  • Campo Imperatore: A plateau in the Gran Sasso e Monti della Laga National Park, about 1.5 hours from Opi.

Conclusion

From scenic hikes and bike rides to indulging in traditional Abruzzese cuisine, Opi has something to offer all types of travelers. With this comprehensive travel guide, you are now well equipped to plan your trip to this charming Italian village. So pack your bags, and get ready to explore the breathtaking beauty and experience the unique culture and history of Opi.
