Introduction to L’Aquila (province)

L’Aquila is a province in the Abruzzo region of Central Italy known for its scenic beauty, rich history, and culture. From breathtaking mountain ranges to charming medieval towns, L’Aquila is a hidden gem waiting to be explored. Whether you love outdoor activities or cultural experiences, there is something for everyone in this historic province.

List of Places to Visit in L’Aquila (province)

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Castello Piccolomini: Located in the town of Celano, Castello Piccolomini is a stunning medieval castle offering breathtaking views of the Apennine Mountains.

  • The Amphitheater and the Sanctuary of Ercole Curino: Located in the town of Avezzano, the Amphitheater and the Sanctuary of Ercole Curino are ancient Roman ruins that you will not want to miss.

  • Santo Spirito Church: Located in the heart of L’Aquila, this stunning church boasts an impressive Baroque façade that will leave you in awe.

Natural Attractions of L’Aquila (province): Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Gran Sasso National Park: Spanning over 1,300 square kilometers, Gran Sasso National Park is home to numerous hiking trails ranging from easy to challenging, as well as stunning landscapes and wildlife.

  • Campo Imperatore: Known as the “Little Tibet of Italy,” Campo Imperatore is a vast plateau located in the Gran Sasso mountain range that offers a unique and unforgettable experience.

  • Rocca Calascio: Perched high above the town of Calascio, this ancient fortress is a must-visit for history and nature enthusiasts alike. With its breathtaking views and unique architecture, it’s no wonder why it’s a popular filming location.

Hiking/Biking Routes of L’Aquila (province)

  • The Campo Imperatore Circuit: This challenging hike takes you through some of the most stunning landscapes and scenery in the area, including spectacular views of the Gran Sasso mountain range.

  • The Sirente-Velino Regional Park: With over 2,000 meters of hiking trails, this regional park is a paradise for hikers and bikers alike.

  • The Mount Magnola Trail: This trail takes you through some of the most beautiful forests and mountain landscapes in the area, and is a popular route for experienced hikers.

Best Things to Do in L’Aquila (province): Activities and Tours

  • Wine Tasting in The Vineyards: Take a tour of some of the region’s most popular vineyards and enjoy tasting the local wine.

  • Skiing in The Mountains: L’Aquila boasts some of the best skiing slopes in Italy. Whether you are a beginner or an expert, there are slopes for everyone.

  • The L’Aquila Walking Tour: Take a guided walking tour of the city and discover the rich history and culture that this beautiful town has to offer.

Food and Drink Scene of L’Aquila (province)

Must-Try Dishes

  • Arrosticini: A traditional skewered lamb dish from the region, cooked over an open grill with love and care.

  • Spaghetti Alla Chitarra: A classic dish from Abruzzo, Spaghetti Alla Chitarra is a delightfully simple yet delicious pasta dish that is a must-try.

  • Polenta Abruzzese: A rustic dish made with cornmeal, cheese, and sometimes spinach and sausages. It’s a warming and comforting dish that’s perfect for cold evenings.

Local Beverages

  • Montepulciano d’Abruzzo: This red wine is the hallmark of Abruzzo’s wine industry and is renowned for its rich, full-bodied flavor.

  • Centerbe: A strong herbal liqueur that is perfect for sipping while sitting in front of the fire on a chilly evening.

Where to Stay in L’Aquila (province): Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Locanda del Vecchio Borgo: Located in the heart of L’Aquila, this charming guesthouse offers cozy rooms and authentic Italian hospitality.

  • Eden Hotel: This hotel offers stunning mountain and valley views, as well as luxurious amenities, such as indoor pools and spas.

  • Agriturismo La Conca: Stay at this rustic farmhouse and experience life in the beautiful Abruzzo countryside. Guests can enjoy traditional Italian dishes in the on-site restaurant.

What to Buy in L’Aquila (province): Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Lacework and Embroidery: Abruzzo is famous for its intricate and beautiful lacework and embroidery, which makes a perfect souvenir to take home.

  • Jewelry: The region is known for its handmade jewelry, which is typically crafted using precious gems and metals.

Local Culture and Customs of L’Aquila (province)

  • Festivals: Abruzzo is famous for the many festivals held throughout the year, celebrating everything from Medieval pageantry to traditional food and wine.

  • Local Dances: Abruzzo has its fair share of traditional dances, many of which date back centuries to when the region was ruled by the Kingdom of the Two Sicilies.

Best Time to Visit L’Aquila (province)

The best time to visit L’Aquila is during the spring and summer months, when the weather is pleasant and the landscapes are lush and green. The autumn is also a good time to visit, with the added bonus of the beautiful fall foliage.

Do’s and Don’ts of L’Aquila (province)

Do:

  • Try the Local Cuisine Abruzzo is famous for its delicious food, so make sure to try as many local dishes as possible.

  • Respect The Culture Abruzzo is a region rich in culture and history, and it’s important to respect the customs and traditions of the locals.

Don’t:

  • Be Disrespectful As with any destination, it’s important to be respectful of the local people and their culture.

  • Litter Littering can have a negative impact on the environment and wildlife, so make sure to dispose of your waste properly.

Safety and Health

Safety in L’Aquila (province)

Overall, L’Aquila is a safe destination for travelers. However, it’s always important to take precautions, such as keeping your valuables safe and being aware of your surroundings.

Health in L’Aquila (province)

There are no specific health concerns in L’Aquila, but it’s always a good idea to have travel insurance and make sure you have any necessary vaccinations before traveling.

Getting to L’Aquila (province): Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near L’Aquila (province)

  • Rome Ciampino Airport (CIA): This is the closest airport to L’Aquila, and it’s approximately 100 km away.

  • Leonardo da Vinci-Fiumicino Airport (FCO): This airport is about 150 km away from L’Aquila.

Bus Stations Near L’Aquila (province)

  • L’Aquila Bus Station Located in the center of the city, this bus station has connections to major cities in Italy and beyond.

Other Options

  • Rent a Car Renting a car is a great option for those who wish to explore the province at their own pace.

  • Take a Train Trains are a quick and easy way to travel around Italy, and there are numerous options for getting to L’Aquila.

How to Get Around in L’Aquila (province)

The best way to get around L’Aquila is by car, as public transportation can be sparse, especially in the smaller towns and villages.

Where to Go Next from L’Aquila (province): Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Teramo: This beautiful town is known for its historic architecture and stunning landscapes.

  • Pescara: A charming coastal town that is perfect for beach lovers.

  • Rome: Italy’s capital city is just a few hours away from L’Aquila and is a must-visit for any trip to Italy.

Conclusion

L’Aquila is a hidden gem in Central Italy that offers something for everyone – stunning natural beauty, rich history and culture, and delicious food. Whether you’re an outdoor enthusiast, a history buff, or a foodie, L’Aquila has something to offer. So pack your bags and get ready for an unforgettable trip to this beautiful province!
