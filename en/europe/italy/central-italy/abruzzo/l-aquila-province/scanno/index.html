Introduction to Scanno

Located in the mountainous region of Abruzzo, Scanno is a charming town famous for its picturesque old town and traditional houses. The town is situated in the L’Aquila province, and it is surrounded by the enchanting Sagittario Gorges and lush green hills. Scanno is a popular vacation spot for Italians and tourists alike, offering breathtaking views, outdoor activities, and a glimpse into traditional Italian lifestyle.

In this comprehensive travel guide, we’ll explore everything you need to know about visiting Scanno, from the best places to visit and things to do, to food and drink recommendations.

List of Places to Visit in Scanno

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Scanno offers several historical and cultural attractions, showcasing the traditional way of life in the region.

Museo Civico “Palazzo Veneziano”

The Museo Civico “Palazzo Veneziano” is a museum situated in a picturesque 16th-century palazzo in the heart of Scanno’s old town. The museum exhibits sculptures, paintings, and local artifacts, including costumes from the region’s traditional festivals. Admission to the museum is €4.

San Francesco Church

The San Francesco Church is a 14th-century church situated in the town center. The baroque-style church boasts a beautiful interior with intricate frescoes and religious artifacts.

Chiesa di Maria Santissima della Valle

The Chiesa di Maria Santissima della Valle is a gorgeous church located just outside Scanno’s center. It’s a picturesque location with stunning views of the surrounding hills. The church is an architectural masterpiece, with a beautiful bell tower and an imposing structure.

Natural Attractions of Scanno: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Scanno is a beautiful region in the heart of Italy, surrounded by stunning natural landscapes. The town offers plenty of opportunities to immerse yourself in nature and enjoy outdoor activities.

Lake Scanno

Lake Scanno is a sparkling lake located just outside the town of Scanno. The lake is surrounded by high mountains, creating a beautiful backdrop for a picnic or a walk around the lake. You can also rent a boat and explore the lake’s calm waters.

Sagittario Gorges

The Sagittario Gorges is a nature reserve located about eight kilometers from Scanno. The reserve is home to several hiking trails where you can enjoy scenic views of the river and waterfalls.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Scanno

Scanno is a paradise for hikers and bikers, offering trails for all fitness levels and preferences. Here are some of the most popular routes to try:

Valle del Sagittario Trail

The Valle del Sagittario Trail is a popular hiking route, following the Sagittario River through breathtaking views of the surrounding mountains. The trail takes about three hours to complete.

Monte Greco Trail

If you’re up for a challenge, the Monte Greco Trail is a demanding climb that rewards you with stunning views of the region. The trail is about 12 kilometers and takes about six hours to complete.

Best Things to Do in Scanno: Activities and Tours

In addition to hiking and exploring Scanno’s natural beauty, there are several other things to do in town.

Take a Cooking Class

If you’re a foodie, take a cooking class in Scanno and learn how to make traditional Italian dishes like pasta and risotto.

Wine Tasting

Abruzzo is known for its excellent wines, and Scanno offers several opportunities for wine tasting. Visit the local wineries to taste the region’s unique and delicious wine varieties.

Week-Long Itinerary for Scanno

Day 1: Explore Scanno’s Old Town and visit the Museo Civico “Palazzo Veneziano.” Day 2-3: Hike the Valle del Sagittario Trail and explore the Sagittario Gorges. Day 4: Visit the Chiesa di Maria Santissima della Valle and San Francesco Church. Day 5: Take a cooking class and learn how to make traditional Italian dishes. Day 6: Rent a boat and explore Lake Scanno. Day 7: Wine tasting at the local wineries.

Food and Drink Scene of Scanno

Abruzzo’s cuisine is known for its emphasis on fresh, locally sourced ingredients and simple preparation methods. Here are some of the must-try dishes and local beverages in Scanno:

Must-Try Dishes

Arrosticini

Arrosticini is a popular dish in Abruzzo, consisting of lamb skewers grilled over an open flame. The dish is usually served with bread and a sprinkling of salt and lemon.

Spaghetti alla Chitarra

Spaghetti alla Chitarra is a local specialty in Abruzzo, made with egg-based pasta and served with a rich meat sauce.

Local Beverages

Montepulciano d’Abruzzo

Montepulciano d’Abruzzo is a red wine variety that’s famous in the region. It’s a robust, full-bodied wine with a smooth finish and pairs well with pasta and red meat.

Limoncello

Limoncello is a traditional lemon-flavored liqueur made in Sorrento, Italy. It’s a refreshing after-dinner drink and pairs well with dessert.

Where to Stay in Scanno: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

There are several accommodation options in Scanno, ranging from budget-friendly hostels to luxurious hotels.

Albergo Il Poggio

Albergo Il Poggio is a 3-star hotel located in the town center. The hotel offers cozy and comfortable rooms with stunning views of the surrounding hills.

Bed & Breakfast La Tana

Bed & Breakfast La Tana is a charming guesthouse situated in Scanno’s old town. The guesthouse offers cozy rooms and a friendly atmosphere.

What to Buy in Scanno: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Scanno is known for its high-quality handmade products and souvenirs. Here are some of the most popular things to buy:

Lace Embroidery

Scanno is famous for its intricate lace embroidery, made by local artisans using traditional methods.

Olive Oil

Abruzzo is known for its excellent olive oil, made from locally sourced olives. It’s a perfect souvenir to bring back home.

Local Culture and Customs of Scanno

Scanno is a small town with traditional customs and a strong sense of community. It’s not uncommon to see locals in traditional dress, and the town hosts several festivals throughout the year.

Best Time to Visit Scanno

The best time to visit Scanno is from April to June or from September to November, when the weather is mild, and the crowds are thinner. However, if you’re looking for winter sports, December to February is the best time to visit.

Do’s and Don’ts of Scanno

Do:

  • Respect the locals and their traditions.
  • Try the local cuisine and wine.
  • Explore the natural beauty of the town.

Don’t:

  • Wear revealing clothing in religious places.
  • Be loud and disruptive late at night.
  • Leave trash behind in the natural areas.

Safety and Health

Safety in Scanno

Scanno is a safe town with low crime rates. However, it’s always advisable to take standard safety precautions, like locking your room and being aware of your surroundings.

Health in Scanno

Scanno has excellent healthcare facilities, including a hospital and several clinics. However, it’s always advisable to have travel insurance when traveling abroad.

Getting to Scanno: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Scanno

The closest airport to Scanno is Rome Fiumicino International Airport, which is about two hours and thirty minutes away by car. Other nearby airports include Naples International Airport and Pescara International Airport.

Bus Stations Near Scanno

Scanno has a bus station connecting it to other towns and cities in the region. Buses run regularly to Rome and other major Italian cities.

Other Options

If you’re driving, Scanno is easily accessible via the A25 Highway from Rome. It’s about a two-hour drive. However, be aware that parking in Scanno’s narrow streets can be challenging.

How to Get Around in Scanno

Scanno is a small town, and most of the attractions are within walking distance. However, if you want to explore the surrounding areas, it’s advisable to rent a car or bike.

Where to Go Next from Scanno: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Scanno is located in Abruzzo, a region known for its beautiful national parks, picturesque towns, and historical landmarks. Here are some nearby destinations you might want to explore:

Sulmona

Sulmona is a charming medieval town located about 40 minutes from Scanno. The town is famous for its colorful buildings, beautiful palaces, and delicious confectionery.

Roccaraso

Roccaraso is a popular ski resort located about an hour and a half from Scanno. The town offers winter sports, such as skiing and snowboarding, and a vibrant nightlife.

Conclusion

Scanno offers a unique glimpse into Italian culture and lifestyle, amidst stunning natural landscapes and traditional architecture. Whether you’re looking for outdoor adventures, delicious food, or a relaxing vacation, Scanno has something to offer. This comprehensive travel guide will help you make the most of your visit, from the best places to visit, things to do, and where to stay and eat.
