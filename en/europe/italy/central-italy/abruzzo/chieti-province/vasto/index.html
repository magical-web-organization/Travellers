Introduction to Vasto

Nestled in the Chieti province in Abruzzo, Central Italy, Vasto is a picturesque town that offers a unique blend of history, culture, and natural beauty. The town is known for its well-preserved architecture, medieval alleys, and panoramic views of the Adriatic Sea. Whether you’re interested in exploring cultural attractions, hiking or biking trails, or simply relaxing on the beach, Vasto has something to offer for everyone.

List of Places to Visit in Vasto

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Vasto has an abundance of cultural attractions that showcase the town’s rich history and heritage. Some of the must-visit attractions include:

  • Palazzo d’Avalos: This historic palace was once home to the powerful Avalos family and now houses a museum that showcases the town’s history and art collection.

  • Castello Caldoresco: This impressive castle was built in the 15th century and features a mix of Gothic and Renaissance architecture.

  • Chiesa di Santa Maria Maggiore: This stunning Baroque church is known for its intricate frescoes, stuccos, and paintings.

Natural Attractions of Vasto: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Vasto boasts of some of the most scenic natural beauty in Italy. From sandy beaches to lush green forests, here are some of the top natural attractions that you shouldn’t miss:

  • Riserva Naturale Regionale di Punta Aderci: If you’re a nature lover, head to this protected area that boasts of crystal-clear water, a long sandy beach, and Mediterranean vegetation.

  • Vasto Marina: Vasto’s longest beach is ideal for long walks during sunset.

  • Parco Regionale del Monte Maiella: This national park offers a great opportunity to hike, bike, or trek through the gorgeous scenery of the Apennine Mountains.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Vasto

Vasto is perfect for those who are looking for an active adventure. Here are some of the best hiking and biking trails in the area:

  • Sentiero Naturale: This coastal walkway offers spectacular panoramic views of the Adriatic sea, and it is easy to navigate, making it perfect for a family day out.

  • Lungomare Cristoforo Colombo: This coastal road runs along the city’s beaches, making it an excellent biking route.

Best Things to Do in Vasto: Activities and Tours

Apart from its natural beauty, Vasto has an impressive array of activities and tours that will make your stay unforgettable:

  • Sunset boat tour: A sunset boat tour of the Adriatic Sea is the perfect way to enjoy scenic views of the coastline.

  • Wine Tasting: Abruzzo is known for its exceptional wines, so be sure to book a wine tasting experience from one of the local vineyards.

Week-Long Itinerary for Vasto

Here’s a comprehensive 7-day itinerary to help you make the most of your stay in Vasto:

Day 1: Explore Vasto’s historic center and visit Palazzo d’Avalos. Day 2: Hike along the Sentiero Naturale coastal walkway. Day 3: Go on a boat tour and watch the sunset over the Adriatic Sea. Day 4: Visit the charming town of Lanciano, just a short drive from Vasto. Day 5: Spend the day relaxing on the beach at Vasto Marina. Day 6: Go wine tasting in Abruzzo’s wine country. Day 7: Visit the Parco Regionale del Monte Maiella national park and enjoy a hike.

Food and Drink Scene of Vasto

Must-Try Dishes

Abruzzo’s cuisine is known for its simple and genuine flavors, and you’ll find some of Italy’s best food in Vasto. Here are some of the must-try dishes:

  • Sagne e fagioli: A hearty soup made with pasta, beans, and pancetta.
  • Arrosticini: Delicious skewers of grilled lamb or mutton.
  • Polenta e salsicce: A traditional dish made with polenta and spicy sausage.

Local Beverages

In Vasto, you’ll have a chance to try many refreshing local beverages, such as:

  • Montepulciano d’Abruzzo: A robust red wine made from the Montepulciano grape.
  • Pecorino d’Abruzzo: A white wine made from the Pecorino grape.
  • Limoncello: A delicious lemon liqueur that is the perfect after-dinner treat.

Where to Stay in Vasto: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Whether you’re looking for luxurious accommodation or budget-friendly options, Vasto has plenty to offer for all budgets:

  • Hotel Excelsior: This luxurious hotel is located right in the center of Vasto, with fantastic views of the Adriatic Sea.

  • B&B Palazzo D’Avalos: This lovely guesthouse is located inside a historic building and offers stylish and modern rooms.

What to Buy in Vasto: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

If you want to bring home a piece of Vasto, here are some of the best souvenirs and local products to look out for:

  • Olio Extravergine di Oliva (Extra Virgin Olive Oil): Abruzzo produces some of Italy’s finest olive oil, so look out for bottles authentic to the region.

  • Ceramics: Abruzzo is known for its traditional and visually captivating ceramics, perfect for decorating your home.

Local Culture and Customs of Vasto

Vasto is steeped in history and tradition, and the town’s locals are incredibly proud of their heritage. Be sure to respect their customs and follow some essentials like saying “buongiorno” to locals when you see them, be mindful of dress codes near holy places, and follow food and drink etiquettes.

Best Time to Visit Vasto

The best time to visit Vasto is during the Spring (May-June) or Autumn (September-October) seasons. During these times, the weather is pleasant, and the crowds aren’t as overwhelming as in the summer months.

Do’s and Don’ts of Vasto

Do:

  • Do sample the local wines and cuisines.
  • Do take a boat tour of the Adriatic Sea.
  • Do explore Vasto’s historic town center.

Don’t:

  • Don’t forget to dress appropriately when visiting holy places.
  • Don’t litter the beaches or natural areas when hiking.
  • Don’t be too loud or disruptive in public places.

Safety and Health

Safety in Vasto

Vasto is considered to be a safe town, but like any destination, it’s always wise to take a few precautions. Be mindful of your surroundings, avoid leaving personal items unattended in public, and if you have a medical emergency, dial 118 for assistance.

Health in Vasto

Vasto has several medical facilities to cater to your health needs. For minor illnesses, you’ll find many pharmacies in the town center that sells medicine or basic supplies.

Getting to Vasto: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Vasto

The closest airport is Abruzzo International Airport (PSR), located approximately 70km from Vasto. From there, you can take a local bus or taxi to the town center.

Bus Stations Near Vasto

Vasto is well-connected by bus to cities like Rome, Pescara, and Naples. The town’s central bus station is just a short walk from the historic center.

Other Options

Another popular way to get to Vasto is by car. From Rome, follow the A24 to Chieti, then take the SS647 to Vasto.

How to Get Around in Vasto

The best way to explore Vasto is on foot, as the town’s historic center is compact and walkable. However, if you’re looking to travel further afoot, a car or bicycle is an excellent option to access the best hiking trails.

Where to Go Next from Vasto: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Vasto is an ideal base for exploring other destinations in Abruzzo. Some excellent options include:

  • Chieti: This charming town is known for its Roman ruins and impressive medieval cathedral.

  • Scanno: A picturesque mountain town known for its traditional houses and beautiful lake.

Conclusion

Vasto is a hidden gem in Italy, perfect for those who want to experience Italian culture and natural beauty outside of the usual touristy spots. From the town’s ancient history to the stunning beaches and natural parks, Vasto ensures you a trip of a lifetime.
