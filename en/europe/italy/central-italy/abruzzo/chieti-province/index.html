Introduction to Chieti (province)

Chieti (province) is located in the Abruzzo region of Italy and it is well-known for its rich history, beautiful beaches, mountains, and picturesque villages. The province occupies an area of 2,391 square kilometers and has a population of around 400,000 people. Its capital city is Chieti, which is an ancient city with a rich history and culture that dates back to the 9th century BC. The province is mostly mountainous and hilly, with a beautiful coastline and sandy beaches that stretch for miles along the Adriatic Sea.

List of Places to Visit in Chieti (province)

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Chieti (province) boasts several museums and historical sites that showcase the rich history of the area. Here are some of the must-see cultural attractions:

  • Chieti National Archaeological Museum: This museum houses one of the most extensive collections of ancient artifacts in the country, with exhibits dating back to the 6th century BC.

  • Torre del Moro: The tower is one of the most iconic landmarks in Chieti and it has a long and fascinating history that dates back to the 10th century.

  • Cathedral of San Giustino: The cathedral is one of the most beautiful examples of Baroque architecture in Italy, and it houses a collection of art and religious artifacts.

Natural Attractions of Chieti (province): Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Chieti (province) is a paradise for nature lovers, offering a wide range of outdoor activities such as hiking, biking, and water sports. Here are some of the most picturesque natural attractions to discover:

  • Maiella National Park: The Maiella National Park is one of the most beautiful and breathtaking natural areas in Italy, with a diverse landscape that includes forests, meadows, mountains, and rivers. It is also home to a wide array of flora and fauna.

  • Costa dei Trabocchi: The Costa dei Trabocchi is a picturesque coastline that runs from Ortona to San Salvo, and it is famous for its traditional fishing machines known as trabocchi.

  • Punta Aderci Reserve: This nature reserve is situated along the Trabocchi Coast and it offers a spectacular view of the sea and the surrounding mountains. It is an ideal spot for hiking, cycling, and nature walks.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Chieti (province)

Chieti (province) is home to some of the most scenic hiking and biking routes in Italy. Here are some of the most popular trails to explore:

  • Majella Massif: The Majella Massif is a popular hiking destination, with a wide range of well-marked trails that offer stunning views of the mountains and surrounding valleys.

  • Gran Sasso National Park: The park is located in the heart of the Apennine Mountains and it offers a diverse landscape that includes mountains, forests, rivers, and lakes. There are several hiking and biking trails to explore, and it is an ideal destination for nature lovers.

  • Capestrano-San Valentino in Abruzzo Citeriore Trail: This 32-mile trail offers breathtaking views of the surrounding mountains and valleys. It passes through several picturesque towns and villages along the way, including Capestrano, San Giovanni in Venere, and San Valentino in Abruzzo Citeriore.

Best Things to Do in Chieti (province): Activities and Tours

Chieti (province) offers a wealth of activities and tours for visitors to explore. Here are some of the best things to do in Chieti (province):

Food and Drink Scene of Chieti (province)

No trip to Chieti (province) would be complete without trying some of its delicious local dishes and drinks. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Arrosticini: A traditional dish of Abruzzo, consisting of skewers of lamb or mutton that are grilled over an open flame and seasoned with salt and pepper.

  • Rigatoni alla Chitarra: A pasta made with semolina flour and egg and served with a tomato sauce and pecorino cheese.

  • Porchetta: Roasted pork that is marinated in a mixture of herbs and spices.

Local Beverages

  • Montepulciano d’Abruzzo: A red wine that is made from the Montepulciano grape. It is one of the most popular wines in Italy.

  • Pecorino: A white wine that is made from the Pecorino grape. It is a refreshing and crisp wine that pairs well with seafood dishes.
