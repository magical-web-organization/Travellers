Introduction to Valle San Giovanni

Located in the Teramo province of Abruzzo, Valle San Giovanni is a picturesque village that is perfect for those seeking an authentic Italian experience. This charming destination is recognized for its rich cultural heritage, stunning natural landscapes, and traditional local cuisine. As a small and quiet town, Valle San Giovanni offers a perfect place to relax, unwind and connect with nature. Here’s everything you need to know about this hidden gem in Abruzzo.

List of Places to Visit in Valle San Giovanni

Valle San Giovanni is a cultural and outdoor enthusiast’s paradise. From historical museums and landmarks to scenic countryside views, this quaint village has something for everyone. Here are some of the top places to visit in Valle San Giovanni.

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Museo della Civiltà Contadina: This museum showcases the typical daily life of an Abruzzo farm family in the late 19th and early 20th centuries. The exhibits include tools, furnishings, and artifacts that represent the rural life of Valle San Giovanni’s past.
  • Church of S. Gervasio and Protasio: Built in the 13th century, this church is a must-visit for those who admire Romanesque art and architecture. It houses frescoes that depict the life of St. John the Baptist and a beautiful canopy that encloses the wooden Crucifix.
  • Castle of Valle San Giovanni: The castle is a historical landmark and has been perfectly preserved since the 14th century. Its rich history includes being a hiding place for enemies in the Middle Ages and an ancient fortress.

Natural Attractions of Valle San Giovanni: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Monte della Laga National Park: This park covers an area of over 50,000 hectares, and it’s a haven for nature lovers. It’s popular for hiking, trekking, and exploring wildlife in the area. The park has several trails that pass through breathtaking mountain ranges, ranging from easy to challenging.
  • Valle San Giovanni’s Vineyards: Take a scenic stroll to the hills and vineyards of Valle San Giovanni where you can choose to do a wine tasting and learn about local wine production.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Valle San Giovanni

Valle San Giovanni offers fantastic scenery for outdoor adventure lovers who enjoy exploring on foot. The village boasts of breathtaking mountain views, rolling hills, and beautiful countryside. Several hiking and biking trails are available around Valle San Giovanni for visitors to explore. Here are some routes you may want to try:

  • “Monte di Indaco”: A moderately difficult hike with panoramic views of the entire region from the mountain ridge line.
  • “Monte San Giovanni - Monte Saltarello”: A challenging trek up the ridge-line of the two mountains with breathtaking views of the Apennines.

Best Things to Do in Valle San Giovanni: Activities and Tours

From exploring nature, the local cuisine, and historical landmarks, there’s sure to be something for everyone in Valle San Giovanni. Below are some of the best things to do in this beautiful village.

  • Take a guided tour of the village and visit the various historical landmarks.
  • Participate in a wine tasting tour at one of the local wineries or vineyards and experience the flavors of the region.
  • Enjoy the local cuisine, such as the famous Abruzzese pasta, which is made with a distinct garden herb.

Food and Drink Scene of Valle San Giovanni

Valle San Giovanni has a rich culinary tradition that is influenced by the simplicity of traditional Abruzzese cuisine. The food is prepared with fresh and locally sourced ingredients, giving it a unique and exceptional flavor. Here are some must-try dishes:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Maccheroni alla chitarra: A staple dish in Abruzzese cuisine, maccheroni alla chitarra is made of pasta noodles that are cut using a special tool, which gives it a unique texture and taste.
  • Arrosticini: These tiny skewers of lamb are a local specialty and are found in almost every restaurant in the village.
  • La Pecora incatenata: A cheese made from sheep’s milk that is aged in metal chains

Local Beverages

  • Montepulciano d’Abruzzo: A rich and strong red wine that is well-known outside of Italy.
  • Cerasuolo d’Abruzzo: A rose wine made in Abruzzo with Montepulciano d’Abruzzo grapes.

Where to Stay in Valle San Giovanni: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Valle San Giovanni offers a range of accommodation options that are suitable for short and extended stays. From hostels to guesthouses and hotels, visitors can find the perfect place to stay. Some of our recommendations include:

  • B&b La Peyora: A cozy guesthouse in the heart of the village that offers comfortable rooms and a friendly atmosphere.
  • B&B Palazzo Baldini: A beautifully restored and elegant palazzo that offers a unique and stylish experience.
  • Camping Crocerone: A family-friendly campsite that is perfect for those who love camping and outdoor adventure activities.

What to Buy in Valle San Giovanni: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Visitors to Valle San Giovanni have access to a variety of locally made products that make great souvenirs. Some popular items include:

  • Handcrafted ceramics and pottery: This traditional art form is an important part of Abruzzese culture and history and can be found in shops across the village.
  • Local olive oil and honey: These products are produced locally and are renowned for their quality and taste.
  • Woven items such as carpets and tablecloths: A craft that has been around for centuries and has been passed down by generations of women in Valle San Giovanni.

Local Culture and Customs of Valle San Giovanni

Valle San Giovanni has a rich cultural heritage that is reflected in its art, food, and celebrations. The village celebrates several festivals throughout the year, which include:

  • Sagra degli arrosticini: A festival celebrating the local specialty of lamb skewers.
  • Festa della Madonna del Carmine: A religious festival honoring the patron saint of the village.

Best Time to Visit Valle San Giovanni

The best time to visit Valle San Giovanni is in the spring (April to June) and autumn (September to November). The weather is mild, and the scenery is vibrant during these seasons. If you want to experience the festivals, it is recommended to visit during the summer months (July and August).

Do’s and Don’ts of Valle San Giovanni

Do:

  • Respect the local culture and customs.
  • Try the local cuisine and wine.
  • Take a guided tour to learn about the history and culture of the village.

Don’t:

  • Litter or damage the natural sites.
  • Smoke in non-designated areas in the park or other public places.
  • Wear inappropriate clothing or behave inappropriately in religious sites.

Safety and Health

Safety in Valle San Giovanni

Valle San Giovanni is a safe village, and crime rates are relatively low. However, visitors should always take necessary precautions to avoid theft and be aware of their surroundings, especially in busy tourist areas.

Health in Valle San Giovanni

Valle San Giovanni has a medical center, and healthcare is accessible to both residents and visitors.

Getting to Valle San Giovanni: Transportation Options and Logistics

Visitors to Valle San Giovanni can opt to travel by plane, bus or car.

Airports Near Valle San Giovanni

The closest airports to Valle San Giovanni are:

  • Pescara Airport: 70 km away
  • Rome Ciampino Airport: 180 km away

Bus Stations Near Valle San Giovanni

Those traveling by bus can use the following bus stations to get to Valle San Giovanni:

  • Teramo bus station: serves buses from Rome, Pescara, and other major Italian cities.
  • Valle San Giovanni Bus Station: offers local connections to other nearby towns.

Other Options

Visitors to Valle San Giovanni can also rent a car and travel by private car hire to the village.

How to Get Around in Valle San Giovanni

Valle San Giovanni is a small village and can be explored on foot or by bike. There are also car rental options in the village that provide a great way to explore the surrounding areas.

Where to Go Next from Valle San Giovanni: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Valle San Giovanni is relatively close to other major attractions in Abruzzo. Visitors who are looking to explore further can take a day trip to explore some of the following nearby destinations:

  • Teramo: A city located 17 km from Valle San Giovanni, known for its rich history and architecture.
  • Ascoli Piceno: A fascinating city located about 60 km from Valle San Giovanni, known for its Roman heritage and beautiful piazzas.

Conclusion

Valle San Giovanni is a hidden gem in Abruzzo that is worth visiting. With its breathtaking views, cultural heritage, and authentic local cuisine, visitors can expect to have a truly memorable experience. From enjoying nature hikes and bike rides to learning about the village’s rich history and culture, Valle San Giovanni offers an experience that travelers will never forget.
