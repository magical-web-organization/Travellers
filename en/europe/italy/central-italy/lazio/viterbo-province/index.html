Introduction to Viterbo (province)

Viterbo is a province in Lazio, Central Italy that covers an area of 3,615 square kilometers. It is known for its picturesque landscape, medieval towns, and charming villages that offer a glimpse into Italy’s rich history. The province has a population of around 315,000, with the city of Viterbo serving as its capital. Viterbo is a fascinating destination for travelers who are looking for an authentic Italian experience.

List of Places to Visit in Viterbo (province)

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Palazzo dei Papi is a fortress-like palace that served as home to several popes in the 13th century. It is a must-visit destination for history lovers.
  • Civita di Bagnoregio is a hilltop village that is accessible only by foot. It is known as the “dying city” due to its slow yet steady erosion, surely it is a fascinating destination for travelers.
  • Chiesa di San Francesco is a stunning gothic church in Viterbo that dates back to the 13th century. It has an impressive bell tower and a beautiful interior decorated with frescoes.
  • Viterbo’s Medieval Quarter is a preserved medieval town that has a maze of narrow streets, arches, and staircases.

Natural Attractions of Viterbo (province): Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Lake Bolsena is a volcanic lake that is ideal for swimming, fishing, and boating.
  • Terme dei Papi is a mineral-rich hot spring spa that has been in use since ancient Roman times. It is considered one of Italy’s best thermal spas.
  • Monti Cimini is a mountain range that is great for hiking and offers stunning views of the countryside.
  • Valle dei Calanchi is a scenic valley that has a unique landscape characterized by erosions and clay formations.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Viterbo (province)

Hiking Routes

  • Via Francigena is a popular pilgrimage route that runs through Italy, and Viterbo is one of its stops. It is a well-marked trail that passes through stunning landscapes.
  • Cimini Mountains Park has several hiking trails that range in difficulty, providing a chance to explore the mountain range’s unique flora and fauna.

Biking Routes

  • Monti Cimini Circuit is a 30-kilometer route that offers a challenging yet rewarding biking experience.
  • Bolsena Circuit is a 40-kilometer route that goes around the Bolsena Lake, offering scenic views along the way.

Best Things to Do in Viterbo (province): Activities and Tours

  • Cooking Classes are a great way to experience the local cuisine and culture. Viterbo has several cooking schools that offer classes on traditional Italian dishes.
  • Wine Tasting Tours are a chance to try some of the best wines from the region, like Est! Est!! Est!!!, and learn about wine production.
  • Horseback Riding is a way to explore the countryside while enjoying the fresh air and stunning views.
  • Boat Tours provide a unique perspective of Lake Bolsena and the surrounding areas.

Food and Drink Scene of Viterbo (province)

Must-Try Dishes

  • Acquacotta is a traditional soup that is made with vegetables, stale bread, and poached eggs.
  • Porchetta is a slow-roasted, savory pork dish that is popular in Viterbo.
  • Fettuccine alla papalina is a pasta dish with a creamy sauce that includes pancetta, parmesan, and eggs.
  • Ciaffagnone is a dessert made with flour, eggs, and honey that is shaped like a pancake.

Local Beverages

  • Est! Est!! Est!!! is a white wine that is made in the province and is known for its light and refreshing taste.
  • Torre Alfina is a red wine that is produced in the province and is considered to be one of the best in Italy.
  • Ponce alla romana is a warm drink made by mixing red wine with cinnamon, sugar, and lemon zest.

Where to Stay in Viterbo (province): Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Benessere in Valle is a spa hotel that offers comfortable rooms and a mineral-rich thermal pool.
  • La Loggia del Capitano is a charming guesthouse in the medieval quarter of Viterbo, featuring elegant rooms and great views.
  • Agriturismo La Corte di Vetralla is an agritourism property that provides rustic yet cozy rooms, featuring breakfast made from local products.

What to Buy in Viterbo (province): Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Ceramics are a popular souvenir that is made in several of Viterbo’s towns.
  • Extra-virgin olive oil is produced in the province and is considered to be of exceptional quality.
  • Chestnuts are a specialty of the area and are used in several traditional dishes.

Local Culture and Customs of Viterbo (province)

  • Italians are known for their hospitality, and locals in Viterbo are no exception. Tourists can expect a warm and friendly welcome.
  • The province has several festivals throughout the year that are a great way to experience the local culture. Some popular ones include the Festa di Santa Rosa, the Caffeina Festival, and the Chestnut Festival.
  • In Viterbo, it is customary to greet people with a smile and a friendly “buongiorno” or “buonasera.”

Best Time to Visit Viterbo (province)

The best time to visit Viterbo is from April to November when the weather is mild and ideal for outdoor activities. However, the province is a year-round destination, and tourists can enjoy its attractions throughout the year.

Do’s and Don’ts of Viterbo (province)

Do:

  • Dress appropriately when visiting religious sites.
  • Respect local customs and traditions.
  • Try the local cuisine and wines.
  • Bring comfortable shoes for hiking or exploring the medieval towns.

Don’t:

  • Wear revealing clothes when visiting religious sites.
  • Be loud or disrespectful in public.
  • Forget to carry cash, as some places might not accept credit or debit cards.
  • Leave without trying the local specialty, porchetta.

Safety and Health

Safety in Viterbo (province)

Viterbo is a safe destination for tourists. However, like any other place, visitors should be cautious of pickpockets and petty thefts in crowded areas. It is advisable to keep valuables in a safe place, and never leave belongings unattended in public areas.

Health in Viterbo (province)

Viterbo has several medical facilities, including hospitals and pharmacies. Visitors should have travel insurance to cover any medical emergencies that may arise during their stay.

Getting to Viterbo (province): Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Viterbo (province)

  • Fiumicino Airport, Rome (186 kilometers from Viterbo).
  • Ciampino Airport, Rome (163 kilometers from Viterbo).

Bus Stations Near Viterbo (province)

  • Viterbo Bus Station.
  • Orte Bus Station.

Other Options

  • Trains run from Rome to Viterbo, taking around 90 minutes.
  • Taxis and rental cars are also available from Rome.

How to Get Around in Viterbo (province)

Visitors can use buses or trains to get around the province. However, renting a car is the most convenient option for exploring the countryside.

Where to Go Next from Viterbo (province): Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Orvieto is a charming medieval town that is known for its stunning cathedral and underground tunnels.
  • Tarquinia is a coastal town that is famous for its Etruscan ruins and beautiful beaches.
  • Rome is just a few hours drive from Viterbo and offers an endless array of tourist attractions.

Conclusion

Viterbo is a hidden gem in Central Italy that has something to offer for every traveler. From stunning natural landscapes to rich cultural sites, visitors can experience the province’s unique history and traditions. With this comprehensive travel guide, tourists can plan their Viterbo itinerary, explore its attractions, and immerse themselves in the local culture.
