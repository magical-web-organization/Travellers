Introduction to Viterbo

Nestled in the province of Lazio, in the heart of Italy, Viterbo is a hidden gem awaiting discovery. This charming town embodies everything you could want from a quintessential Italian holiday, from its rich and varied history to the magnificent natural landscapes, and the delectable local food and wine.

List of Places to Visit in Viterbo

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Viterbo is a city steeped in history, and you will find plenty of places to explore and learn more about its past.

  • The Papal Palace - This beautiful palace, also known as the Palace of the Popes, is an excellent example of Gothic architecture and was the residence of the Pope when he ran away from Rome during the 13th century.
  • The Cathedral of San Lorenzo - A magnificent Romanesque-Gothic cathedral with a beautiful 13th-century rose window.
  • The Etruscan Museum - A must-visit museum to discover Viterbo’s ancient Etruscan civilization’s history.
  • The Church of Santa Maria Nuova - A beautiful 13th-century church with stunning frescoes and stained-glass windows.

Natural Attractions of Viterbo: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Aside from its rich history, Viterbo also boasts spectacular natural attractions, including:

  • The Nature Reserve of Lake Vico - A beautiful lake surrounded by lush green forests, which offer plenty of opportunities for outdoor adventures such as hiking, biking and horse riding.
  • The Terme dei Papi Spa - A luxurious spa resort that offers various treatments within ancient Roman baths, surrounded by beautiful gardens and natural hot springs.
  • The Cimini Mountains - A beautiful mountain range with scenic trails and sites ideal for hiking, biking, and bird watching.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Viterbo

Viterbo’s stunning natural landscape, including pristine lakes, rolling hills, and lush forests, is perfect for hiking and cycling. Below are some of the best routes to take:

  • The Via Francigena - This is a beautiful route starting from Acquapendente and leading all the way to Rome, passing through Viterbo. The route is marked, and you can follow the yellow-and-blue signs.
  • The Lake Vico Nature Reserve Trail - This is a beautiful hiking and biking trail around the spectacular Lake Vico.
  • The Cimini Mountains Trail - A challenging but rewarding hiking trail leading up to the top of the Cimini Mountains, offering stunning panoramic views of the surrounding landscapes.

Best Things to Do in Viterbo: Activities and Tours

Aside from exploring the town’s rich history and stunning natural landscapes, Viterbo has plenty of activities and tours that will keep you entertained:

  • The Viterbo Food Tour - This tour will take you on a culinary journey, showcasing some of the town’s most delicious and authentic dishes.
  • The Papal Palace Tour - This guided tour will take you through the fascinating history of the Palazzo dei Papi di Viterbo, immortalized in Dante’s Inferno.
  • The Villa Lante Tour - A magnificent Renaissance garden, designed by Cardinal Gambara in the 16th century, which features elaborate fountains, flower beds, and stunning vistas.

Week-Long Itinerary for Viterbo

Looking for a week-long itinerary to explore Viterbo and its surroundings? Here is a suggestion:

Day 1: Arrive in Viterbo and check into your hotel. Take a stroll around the town and visit some of the historical sites, such as The Papal Palace and The Cathedral of San Lorenzo.

Day 2: Take a guided tour of the Palazzo Dei Papi and learn more about the rich history of the town.

Day 3: Explore the beautiful countryside around Viterbo. Rent a bike and cycle around the Terme dei Papi Spa, and indulge in a spa treatment afterward.

Day 4: Visit the Lake Vico Nature Reserve, take a boat ride around the lake, and hike around its scenic trails.

Day 5: Take a day trip to Civita di Bagnoregio, a beautiful medieval town perched on a hilltop, and soak up its stunning views.

Day 6: Visit the Villa Lante and explore its magnificent Renaissance garden.

Day 7: Learn how to prepare an authentic Italian dish with a cooking class.

Food and Drink Scene of Viterbo

Viterbo’s food and drink scene is as rich and varied as the town itself. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Acquacotta - A hearty soup made with fish, vegetables, and bread. A traditional peasant dish.
  • Gnocchi alla Sorrentina - Traditionally made with potato, this dish is served with a rich tomato sauce, basil, and mozzarella.
  • Baccalà alla Viterbese - A delicious fried cod dish.

Local Beverages

  • Est! Est!! Est!!! di Montefiascone - This white wine is dry and delicate.
  • Vino Nobile di Montepulciano - A red wine that is full-bodied and fruity.
  • Amari - A bitter liqueur made from herbs, used as a digestif.

Where to Stay in Viterbo: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Viterbo offers a range of accommodation options to suit any budget and preference:

  • Palazzo Catalani - This historical 4-star hotel is located in the heart of Viterbo’s medieval town center.
  • B&B La Terrazza Medievale - This charming bed and breakfast offers beautiful views of the town, with easy access to numerous tourist attractions.
  • Hostel del Popolo - This affordable hostel offers dorms and private rooms, making it ideal for solo travelers and backpackers.

What to Buy in Viterbo: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Viterbo is famous for its various handicrafts and food products. When visiting, consider buying:

  • Pottery - Viterbo is known for its beautiful handmade ceramics, including vases, plates, and decorative pieces.
  • Truffles - The forests surrounding Viterbo are home to some of the most exquisite truffles, making them the ideal souvenir to bring home.
  • Extra-Virgin Olive Oil - Viterbo is known for producing high-quality olive oil, which makes a great gift for foodie friends and family.

Local Culture and Customs of Viterbo

Like the rest of Italy, Viterbo has a rich cultural heritage that is deeply rooted in its ancient past and Catholic traditions. Some Italian customs to keep in mind while staying here include:

  • Dressing modestly - When visiting churches or other religious sites, ensure that your attire is modest and respectful.
  • Siesta time - From 1 to 4 p.m., most shops and attractions are closed for a siesta.
  • Hand gestures and posture - Watching your posture and hand gestures can assist in keeping communication culturally aware.

Best Time to Visit Viterbo

The best months to visit Viterbo are from May to September, when the weather is pleasant, and there are plenty of outdoor activities available. The winter months, from December to February, can be cold and damp, so it is advised to layer appropriately when visiting.

Do’s and Don’ts of Viterbo

Do:

  • Tip - As a common gratitude, tip after a satisfactory local meal or excellent service.
  • Say hello - When entering a restaurant, family-owned shop, or locals, saying ciao or buongiorno is a common courtesy
  • Learn some Italian phrases - It is wise to have a little knowledge of the local language. Basic phrases will help you navigate around the town better.

Don’t:

  • Eat on the go - Eating on the go or drinking on the streets is not an Italian custom and should be avoided.
  • Forget to validate your train ticket - Train fare evasion is taken very seriously in Italy.
  • Touch sculptures - Many sculptures and art pieces have been around many years to date; It’s vital to avoid touching the art or setting cameras near them, as these actions have the probability to cause damage.

Safety and Health

Safety in Viterbo

Viterbo is an overall safe destination for anyone traveling alone or with family and friends. While pickpocketing is common in the more touristy areas, it should not be a cause for concern. As with any other Italian city, always be aware of your surroundings, and keep your belongings safe by taking the necessary precautions.

Health in Viterbo

Viterbo has excellent healthcare facilities, including hospitals and clinics that operate on both a public and private basis. Travelers should have travel insurance to cover any medical expenses and emergency medical care needs that may arise during their stay.

Getting to Viterbo: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Viterbo

The best option for air travel to Viterbo is the Rome Fiumicino Airport, which is located approximately 80 miles southeast of Viterbo. From there, travelers can take a train or bus to Viterbo.

Bus Stations Near Viterbo

The primary bus station in Viterbo is the Viterbo Porta Romana Bus station, which has regular services to many cities in the surrounding area.

Other Options

If arriving by car, Viterbo is located close to the A1 motorway, which links the town to Florence and Rome.

How to Get Around in Viterbo

Viterbo’s medieval town center is small enough to navigate on foot. However, the best way to explore the surrounding areas is by bike or car, and car hire is easily accessible from a rental agency in town.

Where to Go Next from Viterbo: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

If you’re looking to explore more of Italy after your visit to Viterbo, there are plenty of destinations nearby worth exploring:

  • Rome - The Eternal City is a must-visit destination for anyone interested in history and culture and is located only an hour’s drive from Viterbo.
  • Civita di Bagnoregio - A beautiful medieval town perched on a hilltop, only a 40-minute drive from Viterbo.
  • Siena - Rich in history, Siena is a charming town located 2 hours south of Viterbo.

Conclusion

Viterbo is a hidden gem in the heart of Italy, offering a perfect context for a getaway. It has magnificent natural landscapes and awe-inspiring history that can only be discovered by personally visiting. By following this comprehensive guide, tourists can discover the town’s magnificent culture, rich history, and memorable stay.
