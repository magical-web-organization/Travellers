Introduction to Calcata

Located in the province of Viterbo, in Lazio, Central Italy, Calcata is a small medieval village that has become popular amongst tourists for its picturesque beauty and authentic Italian charm. This car-free village, perched on a hilltop, boasts stunning views of the surrounding countryside and boasts a plethora of historical and cultural sites, making it a must-visit destination for history buffs and nature enthusiasts alike.

List of Places to Visit in Calcata

Here are some of the must-visit places in Calcata:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Church of San Michele Arcangelo: Built in the 12th century, this Romanesque-style church features impressive frescoes and stunning views of the valley below.
  • Museum of the Sacred Arts: Housed in the Church of Santa Maria, this museum displays a collection of religious objects dating back to the 15th century.
  • The Necropolis of the Via Amerina: This ancient burial site features Etruscan tombs that date back to the 7th-6th centuries BC.

Natural Attractions of Calcata: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • National Park of the Monti Cimini: Located just a few kilometers outside of Calcata, this stunning natural park offers breathtaking views of the surrounding hills and mountains, as well as opportunities for hiking, wildlife spotting, and picnicking.
  • The Gorges of Farfa: Only a short drive away from Calcata, these gorges offer spectacular views of the river, dramatic cliffsides, and plenty of hiking trails for adventurous visitors.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Calcata

Due to its proximity to the National Park of the Monti Cimini, Calcata is a perfect destination for hiking and biking enthusiasts. Some popular trails include:

  • The San Francesco Trail: This 5km hike takes you through the shadowy woods of the Monti Cimini and leads you to the picturesque town of Caprarola.
  • The Pilgrim Route: This scenic 7km trail begins in Calcata and takes you through the hills and countryside of Lazio and ends at the ancient town of Civita di Bagnoregio.

Best Things to Do in Calcata: Activities and Tours

Here are some of the best activities and tours to take in Calcata:

  • Visit the Calcata Market: Held on Saturdays, this market offers visitors the opportunity to sample local produce, crafts, and delicacies.
  • Explore the Village at Night: Calcata’s narrow cobbled lanes and stunning views are even more impressive at night when the village is illuminated.
  • Take a Cooking Class: Learn how to cook traditional Italian dishes from local chefs and enjoy a delicious meal made from fresh and locally sourced ingredients.

Week-Long Itinerary for Calcata

If you’re planning a week-long trip to Calcata, here’s an itinerary that covers all the must-see sights and activities:

Food and Drink Scene of Calcata

Calcata is known for its delicious, locally sourced cuisine and wine. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Fettuccine ai Funghi Porcini: A traditional pasta dish made with fresh porcini mushrooms and served with a creamy sauce.
  • Saltimbocca alla Romana: A Roman classic dish that consists of thin slices of veal cooked in a buttery sage sauce.
  • Coda alla Vaccinara: A hearty and flavorful oxtail stew that has been a staple of Roman cuisine for centuries.

Local Beverages

  • Est! Est!! Est!!! di Montefiascone: A crisp and refreshing white wine that is a perfect accompaniment to a meal.
  • Caffè al Ginseng: A delicious coffee drink that features a hint of ginseng and is perfect for boosting energy levels during a busy day of sightseeing.
  • Aperol Spritz: The classic Italian cocktail made with Aperol, Prosecco, and soda water, perfect for aperitivo hour.

Where to Stay in Calcata: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

While Calcata is a small village, there are several accommodation options available for visitors. Here are some top-rated hotels, hostels, and guesthouses:

  • La Torraccia: A charming bed and breakfast that is located in a historic building and features stunning views of the valley below.
  • Il B&B di Giulia e Gena: A cozy and warm guesthouse that is perfect for solo travelers and couples who are looking for a quiet and peaceful retreat.
  • Antica Locanda della Via Francigena: A beautiful hotel that is housed in a restored 13th-century building and features elegantly decorated rooms and a delicious breakfast spread.

What to Buy in Calcata: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

If you’re looking for unique and authentic gifts to bring back home, here are some local products and souvenirs to consider:

  • Biscotti all’Anice: These traditional Italian cookies are made with anise seeds and are a perfect accompaniment to espresso or tea.
  • Handcrafted Ceramics: Calcata is famous for its ceramic workshops that produce beautiful and unique pottery pieces.
  • Artisanal Jewelry: Many local artisans create stunning and original jewelry pieces using gold, silver, and precious stones.

Local Culture and Customs of Calcata

When visiting Calcata, it’s essential to respect the local customs and traditions. Here are some cultural insights to keep in mind:

  • Dress Conservatively: As a small village with many religious and cultural sites, it’s important to dress modestly and avoid revealing clothing.
  • Be Respectful to Locals: Calcata has a very tight-knit community, and it’s important to be respectful of the locals and their way of life.
  • Be Mindful of Noise Levels: Calcata is a quiet and peaceful village, and visitors should make an effort to keep noise levels down, especially during the evening.

Best Time to Visit Calcata

The best time to visit Calcata is during the spring and the fall when the weather is mild and perfect for outdoor activities. However, the village is a beautiful destination year-round, and each season has its own unique charm.

Do’s and Don’ts of Calcata

Here are some do’s and don’ts to keep in mind when visiting Calcata:

Do:

  • Do take the time to explore Calcata’s backstreets and hidden corners.
  • Do try local delicacies and sample the local wine.
  • Do respect the local customs and traditions.

Don’t:

  • Don’t litter or damage any of the historic buildings or cultural sites.
  • Don’t ignore the village’s strict car-free policy.
  • Don’t take photos of locals without their consent.

Safety and Health

Calcata is a safe and peaceful village, but it’s always important to take basic safety precautions and stay aware of your surroundings.

Safety in Calcata

  • Safe walking paths and trails
  • Police station available 24/7
  • No major safety concerns for travelers

Health in Calcata

  • No major health concerns for travelers
  • Calcata is a small village with limited medical facilities, so it’s recommended to bring any necessary medication and travel insurance.

Getting to Calcata: Transportation Options and Logistics

Calcata is a small village that can only be reached by foot as the village does not allow cars within the village limits. Here are some transportation options to get to Calcata:

Airports Near Calcata

  • Leonardo da Vinci International Airport in Rome (Fiumicino Airport) is the closest major airport to Calcata.
  • Ciampino G. B. Pastine International Airport in Rome (Ciampino Airport) is another airport option.

Bus Stations Near Calcata

  • The nearest bus stations are in Viterbo and Ronciglione.

Other Options

  • Taxi or private car hire.
  • Rent a bike or scooter.

How to Get Around in Calcata

Getting around Calcata is easy and convenient, as the village is small and pedestrian-friendly. Visitors can explore the village on foot, but it’s also possible to rent a bike or scooter and explore the surrounding countryside.

Where to Go Next from Calcata: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

If you want to explore more of Lazio after visiting Calcata, here are some nearby destinations and excursions to consider:

  • Viterbo: This charming medieval town is only 30 minutes away from Calcata and boasts stunning historical sites, delicious cuisine, and natural hot springs.
  • Civita di Bagnoregio: This picturesque hilltop town is only a short drive away from Calcata and is known for its stunning architecture and panoramic views of the valley.
  • Sutri: This small town is known for its historic Roman amphitheater and stunning Etruscan ruins.

Conclusion

Calcata is a hidden gem in the heart of Italy that offers visitors a chance to experience the authentic beauty and charm of Italian life. With its stunning natural vistas, rich cultural history, delicious cuisine, and unique local customs, Calcata is the perfect destination for travelers seeking an authentic Italian experience. Start planning your trip to Calcata today and discover the magic of one of Italy’s most enchanting villages!
