Introduction to Tarquinia

Tarquinia is a charming town located in Lazio, Central Italy. With its beautiful beaches, unique historical landmarks, and vibrant culture, it’s no wonder why Tarquinia is becoming a popular destination for travelers.

List of Places to Visit in Tarquinia

If you are planning a trip to Tarquinia, add these must-see places to your itinerary.

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  1. The National Museum of Tarquinia - Displays a rich collection of Etruscan artifacts, including pottery, sarcophagi, and other relics dating back to the 8th century BC.
  2. Necropolis of Monterozzi - A UNESCO World Heritage Site that is considered one of the most important Etruscan burial sites in Italy, known for its painted tombs.
  3. The Palazzo Vitelleschi - This 15th-century palace is an impressive example of Renaissance architecture. It houses a beautiful museum that exhibits various artworks, including paintings, frescoes, sculptures, and tapestries.

Natural Attractions of Tarquinia: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  1. The Tarquinia Beach - The long and unspoiled beach stretches for about 7 km, with clear waters and soft sand. A perfect spot for swimming or sunbathing, surrounded by picturesque views of cliffs and mountains.
  2. The Parco Naturale della Maremma - Located on the southern side of Tarquinia, this natural park offers various outdoor activities, including hiking, horseback riding, bird watching, and cycling. A stunning place to connect with nature.
  3. The Marta River - A beautiful river that flows into the Tyrrhenian Sea, near Tarquinia. It’s a scenic spot to enjoy a relaxing boat trip or fishing.

Hiking/Biking Routes in Tarquinia

If you are an outdoor enthusiast, try these hiking and biking routes that showcase Tarquinia’s natural beauty.

  1. The Etruscan Graves Park - A beautiful park that stretches for about 12 km, featuring dozens of Etruscan tombs, some of which are painted. It’s a challenging hiking track, but with amazing views of the surrounding countryside.
  2. The Tarquinia-Orbetello Bike Path - A 60 km-long cycling path that links Tarquinia to Orbetello, passing through charming medieval villages, pine forests, and breathtaking sea views.

Best Things to Do in Tarquinia: Activities and Tours

Experience Tarquinia’s culture and heritage through these activities and tours.

  1. Etruscan Painting Workshop - Learn the art of Etruscan painting and create your masterpiece with a local artist. An excellent way to dive into Tarquinia’s ancient art.
  2. Tarquinia Food Tasting Tour - A gastronomic journey across Tarquinia’s local cuisine, tasting delicacies such as handmade pasta, olive oil, pecorino cheese, and other regional specialties.
  3. Boat Trip to Isola di Marta - This little island is a hidden gem near Tarquinia, accessible by boat. It’s a perfect spot for swimming, snorkeling, and sunbathing.

Here’s a suggested week-long itinerary for a perfect trip to Tarquinia:

Food and Drink Scene of Tarquinia

Tarquinia boasts a rich culinary tradition that has been influenced by its history and location. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages.

Must-Try Dishes

  1. Tagliatelle alla Tarquiniese - Homemade pasta with a rich meat sauce, typical of Tarquinia’s cuisine.
  2. Pecorino Romano Cheese - A hard cheese made from sheep’s milk, often used in traditional recipes. Tarquinia’s pecorino is particularly delicious.
  3. Cacio e Pepe - A classic Roman dish consisting of pasta with cheese and pepper. It’s a simple yet delicious comfort food.

Local Beverages

  1. Est! Est!! Est!!! di Montefiascone - A famous local wine, made from Trebbiano grapes. It has a light and fruity taste, perfect to accompany Tarquinia’s cuisine.
  2. Birra Tarquinese - A local beer made in Tarquinia’s brewery, with quality ingredients and a unique flavor. A great choice for beer lovers.

Tarquinia offers different types of accommodation that cater to all traveler’s needs and budgets. Here are some recommendations.

  2. Agriturismo Podere Giulio - A charming farmhouse located in a beautiful countryside setting, only 5 km from Tarquinia. It offers cozy rooms, a swimming pool, and an organic restaurant.
  3. B&B Al Numero Civico - A budget-friendly option located in the heart of Tarquinia, with comfortable and clean rooms and a friendly staff.

What to Buy in Tarquinia: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Bring a piece of Tarquinia to your home with these local products and souvenirs.

  1. Etruscan Pottery - Beautifully crafted ceramics inspired by the Etruscan style of Tarquinia’s history.
  2. Olive Oil - Tarquinia’s olive groves produce high-quality extra virgin olive oil, perfect for cooking or as a gift.
  3. Artisanal Jams - Made with fresh fruits and carefully crafted by local producers, the artisanal jams of Tarquinia are a delicious souvenir.

Local Culture and Customs of Tarquinia

Tarquinia has a rich cultural heritage that dates back to the Etruscan era, and locals are proud of their history and traditions. Here are some things to keep in mind when visiting Tarquinia.

  1. Dress Respectfully - When visiting religious places or attending events, it’s essential to dress modestly, covering your shoulders and knees.
  2. Respect the Silence - As Tarquinia has many historical landmarks and museums, it’s important to keep quiet and respect the surroundings.
  3. Learn Some Italian - Knowing some basic Italian phrases can help you communicate with locals and show your appreciation for their language.

Best Time to Visit Tarquinia

The best time to visit Tarquinia is during the spring and fall, when the weather is moderate, and the crowds are fewer. The summer months can be hot and crowded, but the beaches are at their best.

Do’s and Don’ts of Tarquinia

Here are some tips on what to do and what not to do in Tarquinia.

Do:

  1. Try the Local Food - Tarquinia’s cuisine is delicious, and you should not miss the opportunity to savor its regional specialties.
  2. Enjoy the Beaches - Tarquinia’s long coastline offers beautiful beaches that are perfect for sunbathing and swimming.
  3. Appreciate the History - Tarquinia has a rich history dating back to the Etruscan era, and it’s worth exploring its museums and landmarks.

Don’t:

  1. Touch the Artifacts - In museums and historical sites, it’s not allowed to touch or interact with the artifacts, as they are fragile and need proper care.
  2. Leave Your Trash - Tarquinia is a beautiful town that deserves to be kept clean and tidy. So, do not litter in public places.
  3. Ignore the Dress Code - In some religious places, proper dress is required, so be respectful and dress accordingly.

Safety and Health

Tarquinia is a safe destination for travelers, with low crime rates and excellent healthcare services. However, it’s always good to keep some safety and health tips in mind.

Safety in Tarquinia

  1. Avoid Unlit or Deserted Areas - At night, it’s better to stick to well-lit areas and avoid deserted streets or alleys.
  2. Watch out for Pickpockets - As in any crowded place, it’s important to keep an eye on your belongings, especially in touristy areas.
  3. Follow Traffic Rules - When renting a car or driving, make sure you follow the traffic rules and signs, as they can differ from your home country.

Health in Tarquinia

  1. Stay Hydrated - The summer months can be hot, so it’s essential to drink plenty of water to avoid dehydration.
  2. Protect Your Skin - With Tarquinia’s long beaches and outdoor activities, it’s important to protect your skin from the sun with sunscreen and hats.
  3. Have Travel Insurance - It’s always good to have travel insurance that covers medical emergencies or unexpected events.

Getting to Tarquinia: Transportation Options and Logistics

Tarquinia is located about 90 km north of Rome, and it’s easily accessible by various means of transportation.

Airports Near Tarquinia

  1. Fiumicino Airport in Rome, located 100 km south of Tarquinia.
  2. Ciampino Airport in Rome, located 120 km southeast of Tarquinia.

Bus Stations Near Tarquinia

  1. The Cotral bus company operates various bus routes from Rome and other neighboring towns.
  2. The Italo bus company offers comfortable and direct connections from various Italian cities, including Rome.

Other Options

  1. Rent a Car - Tarquinia is easily reachable by car, and a rental car allows you to visit the nearby areas and enjoy the beautiful countryside.
  2. Take a Train - The nearest train station is in Civitavecchia, about 20 km southwest of Tarquinia, which connects to Rome and other Italian cities.

How to Get Around in Tarquinia

Tarquinia is a walkable town, and most of the attractions are located within the historic center. However, if you want to explore the nearby areas, here are some options:

  1. Bicycle - Renting a bike is a great way to explore Tarquinia’s beautiful countryside and the nearby bike paths.
  2. Bus - The local bus company offers connections to nearby towns and beaches.
  3. Car - Renting a car allows you to explore the surrounding areas with more freedom, but parking in the historic center can be challenging.

Where to Go Next from Tarquinia: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

If you have extra time, here are some nearby destinations and excursions that you can visit from Tarquinia.

  1. Civitavecchia - A charming seaside town with a beautiful harbor, located 20 km southwest of Tarquinia.
  2. Bolsena - A picturesque town situated on the shores of Lake Bolsena, known for its castle and beautiful views, located 40 km northeast of Tarquinia.
  3. Rome - The capital city of Italy, filled with historical landmarks and sites, located 90 km south of Tarquinia.

Conclusion

Tarquinia is a hidden gem in Italy, offering a perfect combination of culture, nature, and gastronomy. With its unique historical landmarks, beautiful beaches, and friendly locals, Tarquinia is a must-visit destination that should not be missed.
