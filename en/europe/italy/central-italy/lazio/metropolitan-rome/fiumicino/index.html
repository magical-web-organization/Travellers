Introduction to Fiumicino

Fiumicino is a picturesque town located in Metropolitan Rome, in the Lazio region of central Italy. This charming town is set on the shores of the Tyrrhenian Sea, just 35 km west of Rome. Known for its stunning natural beauty, rich history and culture, Fiumicino is fast becoming a popular destination for tourists from all over the world.

Best Things to Do in Fiumicino: Activities and Tours

There are many fun and exciting things to do in Fiumicino, such as:

  • Visit the Ostia Antica Archaeological site.
  • Take a guided biking tour of the Natural Reserve of Macchiagrande.
  • Visit the Museum of Ancient Navigation Instruments.
  • Experience stunning birdwatching in the WWF Oasis of Macchiagrande.
  • Explore the Cerveteri Necropolis.

Week-Long Itinerary for Fiumicino

Here is a suggested itinerary for a week in Fiumicino:

Day 1: Visit the Archaeological Site of Ostia Antica and the Necropolis of Porto.

Day 2: Take a guided biking tour of the Natural Reserve of Macchiagrande.

Day 3: Explore the Lido di Ostia Beach and spend some time relaxing on the beach.

Day 4: Visit the Museum of Ancient Navigation Instruments and experience the stunning birdwatching in the WWF Oasis of Macchiagrande.

Day 5: Explore the Natural Reserve of Macchiagrande hiking trail.

Day 6: Visit the Cerveteri Necropolis.

Day 7: Enjoy a day trip to the beautiful Santa Marinella Beach and relax by the sea.

Local Culture and Customs of Fiumicino

Fiumicino has a rich and interesting local culture. Here are some customs and traditions to keep in mind:

  • Dress Code: When visiting historical places and churches, dress modestly.
  • Tipping: Tipping is not expected, but it is polite to round off the bill.
  • Greetings: Italians greet each other with a hug or a kiss on both cheeks.
  • Siesta: Many shops and businesses close during the afternoon for a siesta.

Best Time to Visit Fiumicino

The best time to visit Fiumicino is during the shoulder months of April to June and September to November when the weather is mild, and the crowds are thinner. The peak season is during July and August, and this is also the busiest and hottest time to visit.

Do’s and Don’ts of Fiumicino

Do:

  • Explore the stunning natural beauty of Fiumicino.
  • Indulge in delicious Italian cuisine and local drinks.
  • Take a walking tour of Fiumicino’s historical places and museums.

Don’t:

  • Ignore dress codes when visiting churches or historical places.
  • Leave without trying the local Frascati wine.
  • Forget to bring sunscreen and insect repellent.

Safety and Health

Safety in Fiumicino

Fiumicino is generally a safe place for tourists. However, you should take necessary precautions while travelling.

Health in Fiumicino

There are several hospitals and clinics in Fiumicino. The emergency contact number in Italy is 112. It is always recommended to have travel insurance before visiting Fiumicino.
