Introduction to Civitavecchia

Civitavecchia, a town in the Metropolitan City of Rome, Lazio, Italy, is a popular port city for cruise ships sailing to Rome. The town has a rich history and a beautiful coast, making it a popular destination for tourists. Civitavecchia has a relaxed and serene atmosphere, perfect for those seeking a vacation that combines culture and leisure. In this travel guide, we will explore the top attractions, activities, and local customs of Civitavecchia, ensuring you have a memorable vacation.

List of Places to Visit in Civitavecchia

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Forte Michelangelo: A medieval fortification located in the city center, offering panoramic views of the sea. Inside is the Maritime Museum, featuring exhibits on the history of navigation.
  • National Archaeological Museum of Civitavecchia: A museum dedicated to the Etruscan civilization, showcasing artifacts and excavations from the area.
  • Cathedral of San Francesco d’Assisi: A 16th century cathedral that features beautiful Baroque architecture and houses the remains of Saint John of Prussia.

Natural Attractions of Civitavecchia: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Terme Taurine: Ancient thermal baths located on a hill overlooking the sea. The hot springs are known for their therapeutic properties.
  • Santa Marinella: A charming coastal town just outside of Civitavecchia, known for its beautiful beaches and clear waters.
  • Castello Odescalchi: A castle built in the 15th century overlooking the sea. It offers stunning views of the town and the harbor.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Civitavecchia

  • Monte di Civitavecchia: A small mountain located near the town that offers several hiking and biking routes. The mountain features stunning scenic views of the town and the sea.

Best Things to Do in Civitavecchia: Activities and Tours

  • Wine Tours: Civitavecchia is home to several vineyards that offer tours and wine tastings.
  • Boat Tours: Take a boat tour of the coastal town and explore the stunning Mediterranean waters.
  • Day Trips: Visit nearby destinations like Rome and Tuscany with guided tours.

Food and Drink Scene of Civitavecchia

Must-Try Dishes

  • Bucatini all’Amatriciana: A traditional pasta dish made with tomato sauce, pecorino cheese, and guanciale (Italian cured meat).
  • Fritto Misto: A classic Italian dish consisting of deep-fried seafood, including shrimp, squid, and octopus.
  • Porchetta: A slow-roasted pork dish flavored with garlic, rosemary, and fennel.

Local Beverages

  • Frascati wine: A light white wine produced in the Lazio region.
  • Nemi strawberry liquor: A sweet liquor made from Nemi strawberries, found only in the town of Nemi in Lazio.
  • Espresso: A strong and delicious coffee made in the traditional Italian style

Where to Stay in Civitavecchia: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • San Giorgio: This hotel is located just steps from the harbor and offers a rooftop terrace with sea views.
  • Hotel de La Ville: A historic hotel with stylish rooms and an outdoor pool located near the city center.
  • La Locanda del Castello: An elegant guesthouse located within the Castello Odescalchi castle.

What to Buy in Civitavecchia: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Ceramic artworks: Civitavecchia is known for its beautiful ceramics and pottery.
  • Olives and Olive oil: The region produces some of the best olive oil in Italy.
  • Limoncello: A sweet lemon-flavored liquor made in the region.

Local Culture and Customs of Civitavecchia

Civitavecchia is a friendly town where travelers can experience the local Italian culture. Visitors should be prepared for a slower pace of life and to take time to appreciate the scenery. The locals are welcoming and always happy to share their knowledge of the town.

Best Time to Visit Civitavecchia

The best time to visit Civitavecchia is from April to June and September to November, the weather is mild, and the crowds are smaller. Summer is the peak season, and the town can get crowded, making it challenging to enjoy the attractions fully.

Do’s and Don’ts of Civitavecchia

Do:

  • Try the local cuisine.
  • Explore the town’s history and culture.
  • Visit the thermal baths.

Don’t:

  • Trespass on private property.
  • Be disrespectful to the locals and their customs.
  • Ignore the safety warnings.

Safety and Health

Safety in Civitavecchia

Civitavecchia is generally a safe town, with a low crime rate. Visitors should be cautious while carrying their valuables to avoid pickpocketing.

Health in Civitavecchia

Visitors should have a valid medical insurance policy before traveling to Civitavecchia. Tap water is safe to drink, but there are no fluoridations.

Getting to Civitavecchia: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Civitavecchia

Civitavecchia is about 50 km from the Fiumicino–Leonardo da Vinci International Airport in Rome, Italy.

Bus Stations Near Civitavecchia

Buses are available from Rome to Civitavecchia. Eurolines buses usually stop at the port harbor.

Other Options

The train station is located in the center of the town, and trains connect Civitavecchia with Rome and other destinations in Italy.

How to Get Around in Civitavecchia

Walking is the best way to get around the town’s historic and pedestrian center. Taxis are readily available and serve the town and its surrounding areas. Local buses are available, but the schedules are limited.

Where to Go Next from Civitavecchia: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Rome: Take a day trip to Rome and explore its iconic landmarks.
  • Tuscany: Visit the vineyards of Tuscany and taste some of the world-famous wines.
  • Sardinia: Take a ferry from Civitavecchia harbor to Sardinia and explore the stunning island.

Conclusion

Civitavecchia is a picturesque Italian town that offers visitors a unique combination of history, relaxation, and stunning scenery. With our comprehensive guide, you will be able to plan your perfect vacation and experience the best the town has to offer, from the local cuisine to the stunning natural attractions.
