Introduction to Subiaco

Located in Metropolitan Rome, in Lazio, Central Italy, Subiaco is a tranquil small town in Italy that has a rich culture and history, with stunning architecture dating back to the Middle Ages. Even though it’s a relatively small town, it has a lot to offer, including gorgeous scenic views, outdoor adventures, and a vibrant food and drink scene. Subiaco is also home to several museums and historical sites, so there’s something to suit everyone’s tastes.

Subiaco is a small town, but it has a lot to offer to its visitors. From historical sites and museums to natural attractions, here’s a list of places that you must visit in Subiaco:

  1. Subiaco Monastery

  2. The Museum of the Order of St. Benedict

  3. Casa Museo di San Benedetto

  4. Cave of St. Benedict

Local Culture and Customs of Subiaco

Subiaco has a rich history and unique traditions that are still celebrated today. Some of the customs include:

  1. Festa di San Benedetto – a summer festival in honor of Saint Benedict

  2. Festa dell’Uva – a fall festival celebrating the grape harvest

  3. Processione del Venerdi Santo – a Good Friday procession

Best Time to Visit Subiaco

The best time to visit Subiaco is between April and October. During this time, the weather is ideal, and many events and festivals take place.

Safety and Health

Safety in Subiaco

Subiaco is a safe town, but as with any destination, visitors should take necessary safety precautions, such as being aware of their surroundings and keeping their valuables safe.

Health in Subiaco

Subiaco has a well-functioning healthcare system with many pharmacies and medical facilities available. Visitors should ensure that they have adequate medical travel insurance and keep a list of emergency numbers handy.

Getting to Subiaco: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Subiaco

The nearest airports to Subiaco are Rome Ciampino Airport and Rome Fiumicino Airport.

Bus Stations Near Subiaco

Subiaco has a bus station, which is connected to Rome’s public transportation network.

Other Options

Visitors can also take a train from Rome to the nearby train station, which is a short bus ride away from Subiaco.

How to Get Around in Subiaco

To get around Subiaco, visitors can use public transportation or hire a car. Taxis and bike rental services are also available in the town center.
