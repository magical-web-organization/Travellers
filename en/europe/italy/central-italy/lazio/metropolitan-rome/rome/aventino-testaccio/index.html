Aventino-Testaccio Travel Guide: Discover Rome’s Hidden Gem

Located in the heart of Rome, Aventino-Testaccio is a charming neighborhood that offers a perfect blend of rich history, unparalleled views, and mouthwatering cuisine. Whether you’re a history buff, an adventure seeker, or a foodie, this hidden gem has something in store for you.

In this ultimate travel guide, you’ll discover all the best things to see, do, and eat in Aventino-Testaccio.

List of Places to Visit in Aventino-Testaccio

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • The Pyramid of Cestius: One of the few remaining ancient Egyptian-style pyramids in Europe. It was built as a tomb for Gaius Cestius Epulo, who lived during the reign of Emperor Augustus.

  • The Protestant Cemetery: A serene and peaceful resting place for many famous figures such as Percy Bysshe Shelley, John Keats, and Antonio Gramsci.

  • The Museo della Via Ostiense: A fascinating museum dedicated to the history of Rome’s most ancient road, the Via Ostiense.

Natural Attractions of Aventino-Testaccio: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Giardino degli Aranci (Orange Garden): This scenic park is one of the most picturesque spots in Rome. Here, you can enjoy panoramic views of the city and enjoy a picnic among the orange trees.

  • Parco Savello: A beautiful park with a stunning view of the Tiber River and the Eternal City.

  • Monte Testaccio: A unique hill made entirely of ancient Roman amphorae. It’s a great spot for hiking and offers spectacular views of the city.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Aventino-Testaccio

  • Appia Antica (Appian Way): This ancient Roman road is one of the most famous hiking and biking routes in the world. It offers breathtaking views of the countryside and ancient ruins.

  • Monte Mario: The highest hill in Rome, Monte Mario, offers stunning vistas of the city and the surrounding countryside.

Best Things to Do in Aventino-Testaccio: Activities and Tours

  • Food Tours: Aventino-Testaccio is home to some of the best food in Rome. Take a tour and indulge in authentic dishes like carbonara, cacio e pepe, and supplì.

  • Rome Street Art Tour: Discover the vibrant street art scene of the neighborhood and learn about the artists who have left their mark in the area.

Week-Long Itinerary for Aventino-Testaccio

Day 1: Arrive and check-in at your accommodation. Visit the Pyramid of Cestius and have dinner in one of the local restaurants.

Day 2: Take a food tour and discover the local cuisine. In the evening, take a stroll in the Giardino degli Aranci.

Day 3: Visit the Appia Antica and cycle through the ancient ruins. In the evening, enjoy an aperitif at one of the local bars.

Day 4: Take a walking tour of the underground tunnels of the Catacombs of San Callisto and San Sebastiano.

Day 5: Visit Parco Savello and enjoy the scenic view of the Rome. In the evening, have dinner at a local restaurant.

Day 6: Take a Rome Street Art Tour and discover the vibrant street art scene in the neighborhood.

Day 7: Visit the Monte Testaccio and hike to the top. In the evening, enjoy a night out at one of the local bars.

Food and Drink Scene of Aventino-Testaccio

Must-Try Dishes

  • Carbonara: A pasta dish made with pecorino romano, guanciale (cured pork jowl), eggs and black pepper.

  • Cacio e Pepe: A simple pasta dish with pecorino cheese and black pepper.

  • Supplì: Fried rice balls filled with tomato sauce, mozzarella cheese, and ground meat.

Local Beverages

  • Frascati: A light and refreshing white wine from the nearby town of Frascati.

  • Peroni: A popular Italian lager.

Where to Stay in Aventino-Testaccio: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Hotel Aventino: A charming hotel located in a historic building near the Pyramid of Cestius.

  • Ostello Tramonti: A budget-friendly hostel located near the Appia Antica.

  • Casa Testaccio: A guesthouse located in the heart of Testaccio, close to the neighborhood’s many bars and restaurants.

What to Buy in Aventino-Testaccio: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Olive oil: Italy is known for its high-quality olive oil, and Aventino-Testaccio has some of the best.

  • Leather goods: Rome is home to some of the finest leather products in the world, and many local artisans sell their wares in Aventino-Testaccio.

Local Culture and Customs of Aventino-Testaccio

The neighborhood has a rich history and a strong sense of community. Many locals still live in the same apartment buildings that their families have lived in for generations.

Best Time to Visit Aventino-Testaccio

The best time to visit Aventino-Testaccio is in the spring (April to June) or the fall (September to November) when the weather is mild, and the crowds are smaller.

Do’s and Don’ts of Aventino-Testaccio

Do:

  • Try the local cuisine.

  • Take a walking tour of the neighborhood.

  • Visit the local markets.

Don’t:

  • Forget to try the local wines.

  • Get too loud at night, the neighborhood is largely residential.

Safety and Health

Safety in Aventino-Testaccio

The neighborhood is generally safe, but tourists should still take the usual precautions such as not carrying too much cash and avoiding deserted areas at night.

Health in Aventino-Testaccio

Italy has a high standard of healthcare, and there are many hospitals and clinics in Rome, including several in the Aventino-Testaccio area.

Getting to Aventino-Testaccio: Transportation Options and Logistics

  • Airports Near Aventino-Testaccio: The closest airport is Rome Fiumicino, which is about 30 km from the neighborhood.

  • Bus Stations Near Aventino-Testaccio: The closest bus stations are Termini and Ostiense.

  • Other Options: The neighborhood is also accessible by metro (Line B, Piramide station) and by taxi.

How to Get Around in Aventino-Testaccio

Most of the neighborhood can be explored on foot, and there are also many public transport options such as buses and the metro.

Where to Go Next from Aventino-Testaccio: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Trastevere: A trendy neighborhood with a lively nightlife scene.

  • Vatican City: Home to St. Peter’s Basilica and the Sistine Chapel.

  • The Colosseum: One of the most famous landmarks in Rome.

Conclusion

Aventino-Testaccio may not be as well-known as some of Rome’s other neighborhoods, but it’s a hidden gem that’s waiting to be discovered. With its rich history, stunning views, and mouthwatering cuisine, this neighborhood is a must-visit for any traveler to Rome.
