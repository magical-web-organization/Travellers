Introduction to Sassari (province)

Sassari (province) is a picturesque destination in Sardinia, Italy, with a rich history and stunning natural landscapes. The province is located in the northwest part of the island, and its capital city, also named Sassari, is the second-largest city in Sardinia. Sassari (province) has a breathtaking coastline, beautiful beaches, and a rugged hinterland that is ideal for outdoor adventurers. The local cuisine is also exceptional, and the region boasts a range of fascinating cultural and historical landmarks. In this comprehensive guide, we’ll take you on a journey to Sassari (province) and explore all the top attractions, activities, and logistics in the area.

List of Places to Visit in Sassari (province)

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • The National Archaeological Museum of Sassari is one of the oldest and most significant archaeology museums in Sardinia, exhibiting a vast collection of antiquities from the island’s prehistory to Roman times.

  • The Cathedral of San Nicola is a beautiful palatial church in the heart of Sassari, built in the Baroque style, and adorned with magnificent frescoes and sculptures.

  • The Binache Nuraghe is an ancient megalithic structure from the Bronze Age, situated near the town of Ittiri. This site is an important cultural landmark and offers visitors a glimpse into the island’s rich archaeological heritage.

Natural Attractions of Sassari (province): Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • The Asinara National Park is an idyllic marine reserve on the island of Asinara, just off the coast of Sassari. The park is home to a range of unique flora and fauna, including sea turtles, dolphins, and rare bird species. Visitors can go on boat tours, snorkeling, and scuba diving excursions to explore the park’s underwater marvels.

  • The Capo Caccia promontory is a stunning limestone cliff formation near the town of Alghero, overlooking the crystal-clear waters of the Mediterranean Sea. This natural attraction has hiking trails that offer breathtaking views of the coastline, perfect for adventurers seeking an outdoor adventure.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Sassari (province)

  • The Gennargentu National Park is the largest national park in Sardinia and is a hiker’s paradise, with a network of trails that lead through some of the island’s most beautiful landscapes. A popular route is the Selvaggio Blu, a challenging six-day trek that takes hikers through rugged terrain, beautiful beaches, and lush forests.

  • The Montiferru Regional Park is another hiking destination in Sassari, with trails that lead through oak woods, chestnut groves, and beautiful waterfalls.

Best Things to Do in Sassari (province): Activities and Tours

  • The Alghero Old Town Tour takes visitors on a journey through the streets of Alghero, exploring the town’s history, culture, and architecture. Visitors can also join culinary tours of the old town, sampling some of the best local cheeses, wines, and seafood.

  • The Sunset Boat Tour of the Gulf of Asinara is another popular activity, offering visitors stunning views of the coast as the sun goes down. Visitors can enjoy an aperitif on board the boat while taking in the breathtaking scenery.

Week-Long Itinerary for Sassari (province)

Whether you have just a few days or a whole week to explore Sassari (province), you’ll find plenty of things to do and see. Here’s a sample itinerary to help you plan your trip:

  • Day 1: Arrival in Sassari, check into accommodation, visit the National Archaeological Museum of Sassari

  • Day 2: Head to Alghero and explore the town, join a guided walking tour of the old town, try local cuisine

  • Day 3: Take a boat trip to the Asinara National Park, snorkel or scuba dive in the clear waters, enjoy lunch on board

  • Day 4: Visit the Binache Nuraghe and explore the town of Ittiri

  • Day 5: Hike the trails of the Montiferru Regional Park and enjoy a picnic lunch in the forest

  • Day 6: Relax on the beach, go swimming, try water sports

  • Day 7: Departure from Sassari, leave for the next destination

Food and Drink Scene of Sassari (province)

Must-Try Dishes

  • The famous dish “Culurgiones” is a typical Sardinian pasta filled with potatoes and mint, served with a tomato-based sauce, usually found in the Montiferru area.

  • The Seada is a delightful dessert, consisting of a pastry sheet, filled with fresh pecorino cheese or ricotta cream, fried, and drizzled with honey.

Local Beverages

  • Cannonau is a type of red wine produced in the province of Sassari, with a strong and full-bodied flavor that pairs well with local cuisine.

  • Mirto is a traditional liqueur made from berries that grow wild in the region, with a sweet and aromatic taste.

Where to Stay in Sassari (province): Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Hotel Calabona is a luxury hotel situated in Alghero, just minutes away from the city center and the beaches.

  • Hostel Alguer is an affordable and comfortable hostel located in the heart of Alghero, perfect for backpackers and solo travelers.

  • The B&B Logu d’Arbu is a charming bed and breakfast located near the Montiferru Regional Park, offering guests a peaceful and relaxing stay.

What to Buy in Sassari (province): Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Filigrana di Sardegna is a typical Sassari specialty, a type of jewelry made from gold or silver filigree wire.

  • The local pecorino cheese is another product that visitors can bring back home, known for its strong flavor and distinctive taste.

Local Culture and Customs of Sassari (province)

Sardinian people are extremely hospitable and welcoming, and visitors are encouraged to respect local customs and traditions. It is common for locals to speak the local dialect (Sardo) alongside Italian, and visitors can learn basic phrases to communicate with locals. There are also many traditional festivals and celebrations throughout the year, showcasing the best of Sassari culture, where visitors can experience authentically local customs.

Best Time to Visit Sassari (province)

The best time to visit Sassari (province) is during the summer months, from May to September, when the weather is warm and pleasant, and there are frequent events and festivals. Visitors can take advantage of the beautiful beaches, explore the hiking trails, and enjoy the outdoor and water sport activities fully. However, those looking for a quieter experience can visit in the shoulder season (April and October), when the crowds are thinner, but the weather is still pleasant.

Do’s and Don’ts of Sassari (province)

Do:

  • Try the local cuisine: Sassari (province) boasts some of the best traditional dishes and local specialties, and visitors should make it a priority to sample as much of the local food as possible.

  • Respect local customs: Visitors should respect local traditions, such as dress codes in religious sites and festivals, and be mindful of local beliefs and practices.

Don’t:

  • Get too sunburned: Visitors should be mindful of the strong sun in Sassari (province) and apply sunscreen regularly.

  • Overindulge in alcohol: Although Sardinian wine and liquor are delicious, visitors should drink in moderation and avoid excess.

Safety and Health

Safety in Sassari (province)

Sassari (province) is generally a safe destination for visitors, with low levels of crime and violence. However, visitors should take precautions to protect their valuables, such as keeping wallets and smartphones secure when in crowded areas.

Health in Sassari (province)

Sassari (province) has a high-quality healthcare system, with hospitals and medical centers situated throughout the region. Visitors should ensure they have adequate travel insurance and bring any necessary medications with them, as some prescription drugs may not be readily available.

Getting to Sassari (province): Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Sassari (province)

The nearest airport to Sassari (province) is Alghero-Fertilia Airport, which is served by several international airlines, including Ryanair, EasyJet, and Alitalia.

Bus Stations Near Sassari (province)

Sassari (province) has a network of local buses that connect the major towns and villages. Visitors can also take long-distance buses to other parts of the island from Sassari or Alghero.

Other Options

There are ferry services running from the mainland of Italy to Sardinia, which offers visitors another option, especially if travelers have cars.

How to Get Around in Sassari (province)

Visitors can rent cars, vespa scooters, or bicycles to explore Sassari (province) independently. Alternatively, visitors can choose to use the public transportation network or join guided tours, which offer a more structured way to see the sights.

Where to Go Next from Sassari (province): Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • The Emerald Coast is a stunning stretch of coastline in northeast Sardinia, famous for its exclusive resorts, deluxe villas, and crystal-clear waters. Visitors can take a day trip from Sassari (province) to explore the iconic Porto Cervo or Porto Rotondo.

  • The island of Corsica is just a short ferry ride away from Sassari (province), and visitors can explore its beautiful beaches, charming towns, and mountainous terrain.

Conclusion

Sassari (province) in Sardinia, Italy, is an ideal destination for travelers looking for a mix of culture, adventure, and relaxation. Explore magnificent historical landmarks, hike beautiful trails, enjoy delicious local cuisine, and soak up the stunning natural landscapes. We hope that this comprehensive guide has given you all the information you need to plan your trip to Sassari (province).
