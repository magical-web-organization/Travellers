If you’re looking for a charming, historic place to explore in Italy, look no further than Sassari. Located in the province of Sassari in Sardinia, this city offers visitors a mix of rich cultural attractions, natural beauty, and culinary delights. In this comprehensive travel guide, we’ll cover everything you need to know to plan an unforgettable trip to Sassari.

Introduction to Sassari

Sassari is the second-largest city in Sardinia and offers a charming and distinctive character that blends the old with new. The city is home to several noteworthy historical landmarks and a thriving food scene with many culinary specialties.

List of Places to Visit in Sassari

Visitors to Sassari can enjoy a mix of cultural landmarks, natural attractions, and great outdoor activities. Here are some of the top places to visit:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  1. St. Nicholas Cathedral: One of the most iconic landmarks in Sassari, this cathedral is a blend of Baroque and Gothic architecture.

  2. Palazzo Ducale: This former royal palace is now home to a museum with exhibits on Sassari’s history and culture.

  3. Museo Etnografico: This museum documents the daily life of Sardinia’s past inhabitants, showcasing traditional clothing, tools, and crafts.

Natural Attractions of Sassari: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  1. Mount Accoddi: A must-visit attraction for nature lovers, this mountain features a 4000-year-old megalithic altar at the summit.

  2. Asinara National Park: Located on the captivating Asinara Island, this national park features stunning beaches, crystal-clear waters, and unique flora and fauna.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Sassari

  1. Monte Ortobene: A hike to the top of this mountain offers breathtaking views of the city and the surrounding countryside.

  2. Nuraghe Santu Antine: A popular cycling route that takes you through pastoral landscapes to the Nuraghe Santu Antine, one of the largest nuraghe complexes in Sardinia.

Best Things to Do in Sassari: Activities and Tours

  1. Food and Wine Tours: Sassari is renowned for its high-quality wines and culinary traditions. Enjoy guided tours through local vineyards and wineries and try the local delicacies.

  2. Walking Tours: Discover the city’s rich heritage and explore its winding streets, alleys, and piazzas on foot.

Week-Long Itinerary for Sassari

Day 1: Explore the city center of Sassari, visiting the St. Nicholas Cathedral and the Palazzo Ducale.

Day 2: Take a hike up Mount Orbene to experience the stunning views of the city and surrounding countryside.

Day 3: Visit the Asinara National Park and enjoy its pristine beaches and crystal-clear waters.

Day 4: Take a food and wine tour of wineries and vineyards in the surrounding countryside.

Day 5: Discover everything there is to know about Sassari’s past at the Museo Etnografico.

Day 6: Take a cycling tour to the Nuraghe Santu Antine, one of the largest and most well-preserved nuraghe structures in Sardinia.

Day 7: Unwind with some shopping in Sassari’s historic markets, picking up some souvenirs and local products.

Food and Drink Scene of Sassari

No trip to Sassari is complete without tasting the local cuisine. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  1. Fregula: A type of pasta made from semolina flour and served with clams and tomatoes or other seafood.

  2. Porceddu arrosto: A roasted suckling pig served with myrtle branches.

Local Beverages

  1. Cannonau: A full-bodied red wine indigenous to Sardinia, made using the Grenache grape.

  2. Mirto: An herbal liqueur made with myrtle berries and leaves, found only in Sardinia.

Where to Stay in Sassari: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Sassari offers a variety of accommodation options for every budget. Here are some of the best places to stay:

  1. Hotel Leonardo Da Vinci: Located in the heart of the city, this hotel offers elegance and comfort.

  2. Kaliè Rooms Hotel: This modern hotel is situated in the historic center of Sassari, providing travelers with an authentic experience.

What to Buy in Sassari: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  1. Ceramics: Local ceramics are renowned for their intricate designs and vibrant colors, making them great gifts for loved ones.

  2. Jewelry: Sardinian jewelry is highly prized for its originality and unique designs.

Local Culture and Customs of Sassari

Sardinia is a region with a rich culture and history. It’s important to be mindful of local customs and traditions when visiting Sassari.

Best Time to Visit Sassari

Sassari can be visited year-round, but the best time to go is during the spring and autumn months. This season offers mild temperatures and fewer crowds.

Do’s and Don’ts of Sassari

Do:

  1. Learn a few Italian words and phrases; the locals will appreciate it.

  2. Try local food and drinks.

Don’t:

  1. Don’t leave garbage around, especially in nature reserves.

  2. Don’t forget to dress appropriately when visiting religious sites.

Safety and Health

Safety in Sassari

Sassari is a safe city to visit, but visitors should still take basic safety precautions such as keeping an eye on their belongings and staying aware of their surroundings.

Health in Sassari

Medical facilities in Sassari are excellent, and there are pharmacies located throughout the city.

Getting to Sassari: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Sassari

Alghero-Fertilia airport is just 20 miles from the city center and offers international and domestic flights.

Bus Stations Near Sassari

Sassari has a central bus station with regular services connecting to other parts of Sardinia.

How to Get Around in Sassari

The best way to explore Sassari is on foot, but there is also a reliable public transportation system. Taxis are another option for visitors who prefer not to walk or use public transit.

Where to Go Next from Sassari: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  1. Alghero: Located just 20 miles west of Sassari, this charming town offers cobbled streets, ancient architecture, and stunning beaches.

  2. Castelsardo: This town on the northern coast of Sardinia boasts a medieval castle overlooking the sea.

Conclusion

Sassari is a destination that offers something for every type of traveler, from the breathtaking landscapes and stunning views, to the fascinating cultural landmarks, exciting dining scene, and more. Follow this guide to make the most of your trip and create unforgettable memories in Sassari.
