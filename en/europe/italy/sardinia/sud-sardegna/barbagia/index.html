Introduction to Barbagia

Located in the heart of Sardinia, Barbagia is a rugged and picturesque area that boasts stunning landscapes, prehistoric ruins, and a rich cultural heritage. The region is characterized by its high mountains, deep valleys, and ancient forests, which provide a perfect setting for outdoor activities such as hiking, biking, and climbing. Barbagia is also famous for its traditional festivals, art, and handicrafts, which reflect the deep link between its people and land. In this guide, we’ll help you discover the best things to do, places to visit, and local cuisine to taste in Barbagia.

List of Places to Visit in Barbagia

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Visiting museums and historical places in Barbagia is a great way to learn about the unique history and traditions of this region. Some of the most popular cultural attractions in Barbagia include:

  • Museum of Mediterranean Masks in Mamoiada, which showcases over 200 traditional Sardinian masks used in local festivals such as Carnival.
  • S’Iscravamentu Museum in Sadali, which displays artifacts of ancient civilizations like the Nuragic, the Punic, and the Roman.
  • Ethnographic Museum Sanna in Sassari, which exhibits collections of rural crafts and objects that were used in daily life and local traditions.

Natural Attractions of Barbagia: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Barbagia is a great destination for nature lovers and adventure seekers. The region offers a wide range of outdoor activities and scenic views, such as:

  • Gennargentu National Park, which covers an area of over 730 sq km and includes the highest mountain in Sardinia, Punta La Marmora (1834m).
  • Tiscali, a natural amphitheater located in the Supramonte mountains, where you can see ruins from the Nuragic period and breathtaking views of the valley.
  • Cala Gonone, a picturesque coastal town known for its beaches, cliffs, and sea caves that can be explored by boat or kayak.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Barbagia

Barbagia is a paradise for hikers and bikers, with an abundance of trails that traverse the mountains, forests, and countryside. Some of the best hiking and biking routes in the region include:

  • Selvaggio Blu, a challenging hiking trail that takes you through the wildest and most scenic areas of Supramonte.
  • Goloritzè, a short but steep hike that leads to one of the most iconic beaches in Sardinia.
  • Barbagia Trekking, a 7-day excursion that allows you to explore the hidden gems of the region, from the Cedrino river to the mountains of Orgosolo.

Best Things to Do in Barbagia: Activities and Tours

If you’re looking for something more than hiking and biking, Barbagia offers a variety of activities and tours to choose from, such as:

  • Wine Tasting Tour, a guided tour of the local wine cellars and vineyards, where you can taste some of the best wines of Sardinia (e.g., Cannonau, Vermentino, and Nuragus).
  • Cheese Making Workshop, a hands-on experience that teaches you how to make traditional Sardinian cheese like Pecorino and Casu Marzu.
  • Festival Tour, a cultural journey that takes you to the most important festivals of Barbagia, such as the Mamuthones & Issohadores in Mamoiada and the Cavalcata Sarda in Sassari.

Food and Drink Scene of Barbagia

Sardinian cuisine is renowned for its simplicity, quality, and strong flavors. Barbagia, in particular, is famous for its cheese, meat, pasta, and dessert. Here are the must-try dishes and local beverages of Barbagia:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Culurgiones: a kind of handmade ravioli stuffed with potatoes and cheese, served with a tomato sauce and grated Pecorino cheese.
  • Porceddu: a suckling pig roasted in a wood oven or on a spit, often accompanied by potatoes and vegetables.
  • Seadas: a dessert made of fried dough filled with fresh sheep cheese and drizzled with honey or sugar.

Local Beverages

  • Cannonau Wine: a red wine with a high alcohol content and a strong taste, produced in the hills of central Sardinia.
  • Filu ‘e Ferru: a local brandy made from distilled grapes, often consumed as a digestive after meals.
  • Mirto: a liqueur made from the berries of the myrtle plant, often served as an aperitif or digestif.

Where to Stay in Barbagia: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Barbagia offers a variety of accommodations that suit different tastes and budgets. Here are some of the best options in the region:

  • Hotel Sant’Efisio, a 4-star hotel with panoramic views of the Gulf of Cagliari, situated in Pula.
  • Bed and Breakfast Dommu Asara, a cozy and eco-friendly inn located in the heart of Barbagia, in the village of Desulo.
  • Agriturismo Su Tiresi, a farmstay that produces its wine and olive oil, located in the countryside near Oliena.

What to Buy in Barbagia: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Barbagia is famous for its handicrafts and traditional products, which are unique to the region. Here’s a list of the best souvenirs and local products to bring home:

  • Ceramics: Sardinian ceramics are known for their bold and colorful designs. Look for handmade plates, bowls, and vases in the village of San Gavino Monreale or Oliena.
  • Rug: Take home a handmade Sardinian rug, made of wool or cotton. Find them in the looms of Samugheo and Nule.
  • Pain Nuraghe Candela: This is a typical Barbagia bread made of durum wheat, wood-baked, and with a characteristic hole in the center. Buy it in bakeries throughout the territory.

Local Culture and Customs of Barbagia

Barbagia has a vibrant and unique local culture that is deeply rooted in the traditions of its people. Some of the most distinctive customs include:

  • Carnival of Mamoiada: a pagan festival celebrated in February that involves the wearing of ancient masks and the offering of food and wine to the “Issohadores” (people wearing the masks).
  • Sheep Shearing Festival in Orgosolo: a spring festival that showcases the traditional shearing of sheep and the production of woolen items and cheese.
  • Cavalcata Sarda in Sassari: a colorful parade of horses and traditional costumes that takes place on the second-last Sunday of May.

Best Time to Visit Barbagia

Barbagia can be visited all year round, but the best time to enjoy its outdoor activities, festivals, and countryside is from April to October. The weather is mild and sunny, and the landscape is green and colorful. Keep in mind that August is the busiest month in Sardinia, and accommodation and transportation should be booked in advance.

Do’s and Don’ts of Barbagia

Do:

  • Respect the local customs and traditions of the region.
  • Try the local cuisine and wine.
  • Learn some basic Italian expressions and greetings.
  • Bring suitable outdoor clothing and equipment for hiking and biking activities.
  • Leave no trace and keep the environment clean.

Don’t:

  • Wear revealing or provocative clothing in public places.
  • Touch the masks or objects exhibited in the museums without permission.
  • Speak loudly or behave disrespectfully in religious or cultural events.
  • Violate the traffic rules and speed limits while driving.

Safety and Health

Safety in Barbagia

Barbagia is a safe destination for tourists, but like any other place, it’s important to take some precautions to avoid risks. Here are some tips:

  • Do not ignore the signs and warnings when hiking or climbing in the mountains.
  • Do not leave your valuables in the car, especially at night.
  • Do not swim in the sea when the weather condition is poor.

Health in Barbagia

Sardinia has a good healthcare system that provides public and private health services. Barbagia has several medical centers and hospitals that offer emergency and non-emergency treatments. It’s advisable to have travel insurance that covers medical expenses and repatriation.

Getting to Barbagia: Transportation Options and Logistics

Barbagia is not well connected with public transportation, and the best way to explore the region is by car or motorbike. Here are the options:

Airports Near Barbagia

The closest airports to Barbagia are:

  • Cagliari Elmas Airport, about 110 km from Barbagia.
  • Alghero-Fertilia Airport, about 150 km from Barbagia.
  • Olbia Costa Smeralda Airport, about 160 km from Barbagia.

Bus Stations Near Barbagia

The regional company ARST provides bus services that connect the major cities of Sardinia, such as Cagliari, Sassari, Nuoro, and Oristano. Some rural areas of Barbagia may not be covered by public transportation, so it’s advisable to check the schedules and route in advance.

Other Options

Taxis are available in the cities and towns of Barbagia, but they can be expensive, especially for long distances. Hitchhiking is not recommended because of the lack of public transportation and the safety risks.

How to Get Around in Barbagia

Driving is the most convenient way to get around in Barbagia, as it allows you to explore the region at your own pace and enjoy the scenery. However, roads can be narrow, winding, and steep, and caution is needed while driving. If you prefer not to drive, you can rent a bike or take a guided tour.

Where to Go Next from Barbagia: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Barbagia is an excellent base to explore the nearby destinations and excursions. Here are some suggestions:

  • Nuraghe Losa near Abbasanta: a Nuragic site that dates from the 14th century BC and consists of a central tower and a defensive wall.
  • Monte Arci near Oristano: a volcanic mountain that is famous for its obsidian outcrops and archeological sites.
  • Oasi di Capo Ferrato near Muravera: a protected marine area that offers white sand beaches, crystal-clear waters, and a great spot for snorkeling.

Conclusion

Barbagia is a unique and charming region that offers a perfect blend of nature, culture, and traditions. With our comprehensive travel guide, you can plan your trip to Barbagia with confidence and discover the best things to see, do, and taste in this authentic part of Sardinia.
