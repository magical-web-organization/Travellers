Introduction to Nuoro (Province)

Nuoro (province) is one of the four provinces in Sardinia, Italy. It covers a land area of 3,934 square kilometers and has a population of around 160,000 people. Nuoro is known for its rugged mountains, vast plateaus, deep gorges, and breathtaking coastal views. The capital of the province is the city of Nuoro, which is home to many museums, galleries, and cultural institutions. The other major towns in the province include Oliena, Orgosolo, and Dorgali.

List of Places to Visit in Nuoro (Province)

Nuoro (province) is full of incredible attractions, both natural and cultural. Here are some of the must-visit places in the region:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Museo Deleddiano: This museum is dedicated to Grazia Deledda, the first Italian woman to receive the Nobel Prize in literature. The museum showcases her works and personal belongings, as well as exhibitions on local culture and history.
  • Museo Etnografico Sardo: This museum is dedicated to the culture, traditions, and lifestyles of the Sardinian people. It features exhibits on Sardinian costumes, crafts, and music.
  • Su Nuraxi di Barumini: This is a UNESCO World Heritage Site and one of the most famous Nuraghe buildings in Sardinia. It’s a prehistoric fortress dating back to the Bronze Age, and it’s considered one of the most important archaeological sites on the island.

Natural Attractions of Nuoro (Province): Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Golfo di Orosei: This is a stunning stretch of coastline in eastern Sardinia, featuring crystal-clear waters and dramatic cliffs. Visitors can take boat tours to explore the hidden coves and beaches, such as Cala Goloritzè and Cala Luna.
  • Gennargentu National Park: This is the largest national park in Sardinia, and it’s home to many rare species of flora and fauna. Visitors can hike through the beautiful forests, canyons, and mountains, and spot wild boars, eagles, and mouflons.
  • Monte Ortobene: This is the highest mountain in the province, standing at 955 meters above sea level. From the top, visitors can enjoy a panoramic view of the surrounding landscape and the town of Nuoro below.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Nuoro (Province)

Nuoro (province) is a paradise for outdoor enthusiasts. Here are some of the best hiking and biking routes to explore:

  • Tiscali: This is a stunning natural amphitheater located in the heart of the Supramonte mountains. Visitors can hike through the rocky terrain to reach the amphitheater, which was once a prehistoric village.
  • Selvaggio Blu: This is a challenging and adventurous hiking trail that stretches for 7 days and 6 nights along the eastern coast of Sardinia. The trail covers 45 kilometers and includes steep climbs, deep gorges, and breathtaking views.
  • Pedra Longa: This is a prominent limestone rock formation located on the coast near Baunei. Visitors can hike to the top of the cliff for a panoramic view of the coast and the sea.

Best Things to Do in Nuoro (Province): Activities and Tours

Aside from hiking and biking, there are many other great things to do in Nuoro (province). Here are some of the top activities and tours:

  • Wine tasting tour: Sardinia is known for its excellent wines, and Nuoro is no exception. Visitors can take a wine tasting tour to sample some of the best local wines, such as Cannonau and Vermentino.
  • Olive oil tasting tour: Sardinia is also known for its high-quality olive oil. Visitors can take an olive oil tasting tour to learn about the production process and sample different varieties.
  • Caving tour: With its rugged mountains and deep gorges, Nuoro (province) is a great destination for caving. Visitors can take a guided tour to explore the hidden caves and underground lakes.

Food and Drink Scene of Nuoro (Province)

Sardinian cuisine is known for its simplicity and use of fresh, local ingredients. In Nuoro (province), you’ll find a variety of delicious dishes and drinks. Here are some must-try items:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Culurgiones: These are Sardinian ravioli stuffed with potato and mint, and served with tomato sauce and pecorino cheese.
  • Porceddu: This is roasted suckling pig, often served with potatoes and vegetables.
  • Sebadas: These are a type of dessert made with cheese-filled pastry, fried and sweetened with honey.

Local Beverages

  • Cannonau red wine: This is a robust red wine produced in the hills of the province, known for its high alcohol content.
  • Mirto: This is a traditional Sardinian liqueur made from myrtle berries.
  • Fil’e ferru: This is a strong, homemade liquor made from the fermentation of grape must and sugar.

What to Buy in Nuoro (Province): Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Baskets: Sardinian baskets are made with local reeds and come in various sizes and shapes.
  • Cheese: Sardinia is known for its delicious pecorino cheese, made with sheep’s milk. You can find various types of pecorino cheese in the province.

Local Culture and Customs of Nuoro (Province)

Nuoro (province) has a rich cultural heritage, rooted in its long history and unique location. Some of the local customs and traditions include:

  • Costumes: Sardinia is known for its colorful and intricate traditional costumes, which vary from region to region. The costumes include hand-woven fabrics, lace, and jewelry.
  • Shepherding: Many people in Nuoro still rely on sheepherding as a way of life. You can see shepherds tending their flocks in the countryside.
  • Barbagia Carnival: This is a traditional carnival celebrated in the villages of central Sardinia. The carnival features masked parades, dancing, and folklore performances.

Best Time to Visit Nuoro (Province)

The best time to visit Nuoro (province) is from April to September, when the weather is warm and dry. July and August are the busiest months, and many locals take their summer vacations during this time. If you want to avoid the crowds, it’s best to visit in April, May, or September.

Do’s and Don’ts of Nuoro (Province)

Here are some important do’s and don’ts to keep in mind when visiting Nuoro (province):

Do:

  • Respect the environment: Nuoro (province) is home to many rare and endangered species of flora and fauna. When hiking or exploring the outdoors, make sure to follow the Leave No Trace principles and be respectful of nature.
  • Try the local cuisine: Sardinian cuisine is a big part of the local culture. Make sure to try some of the traditional dishes and beverages, and support local businesses.
  • Learn some Italian: Although many people in Nuoro (province) speak English, it’s always helpful to know some basic Italian phrases.

Don’t:

  • Rush: Nuoro (province) is a place to slow down and enjoy the natural beauty and local culture. Don’t rush through your itinerary and take time to appreciate the surroundings.
  • Leave waste: Always take your trash with you and dispose of it properly. Littering is not only harmful to the environment but also disrespectful to the local people.
  • Ignore local customs: Sardinian culture is unique and has its traditions and customs. Be respectful of these customs, and ask for permission if you want to take photographs of people or their belongings.

Nuoro (province) is a safe destination for tourists, but it’s always important to take standard precautions to avoid any risks. Here are some safety and health tips:
