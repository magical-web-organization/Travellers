Introduction to Cala Gonone

Cala Gonone is a picturesque seaside town on the east coast of Sardinia. It is nestled at the foot of a mountain range and is surrounded by crystal-clear waters, stunning beaches, and towering cliffs. Cala Gonone is a popular destination for tourists seeking sun, sand, and sea.

The town is known for its excellent seafood, traditional architecture, and beautiful hiking trails. From natural wonders like Grotta del Bue Marino to cultural attractions like the Serra Orrios Nuraghe, there is something here for everyone.

List of Places to Visit in Cala Gonone

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Serra Orrios Nuraghe: One of the most impressive and best-preserved Nuraghe sites in Sardinia, the Serra Orrios Nuraghe dates back to the Bronze Age and is a must-visit.
  • Museum of Mediterranean Masks: This museum houses an impressive collection of masks from all over the Mediterranean, including Sardinia. It offers a great insight into the cultural traditions of the region.

Natural Attractions of Cala Gonone: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Grotta del Bue Marino: This stunning sea cave is accessible only by boat. It is home to a unique ecosystem of marine life and is a popular spot for scuba diving.
  • Cala Luna: This picturesque beach is famous for its clear waters and golden sand. Visitors can take a short boat ride to reach the beach or hike through the scenic wilderness trail.
  • Cala Fuili: A secluded beach nestled at the foot of the limestone cliffs, the Cala Fuili offers visitors plenty of opportunities for rock climbing, swimming, and snorkelling.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Cala Gonone

  • Sentiero Selvaggio Blu: The Selvaggio Blu is a challenging hiking trail that runs along the eastern coast of Sardinia. It is a multi-day trek that can take anywhere from five to eight days to complete.
  • Gola di Gorropu: This hike takes visitors through a rugged canyon with towering cliffs, crystal-clear streams, and stunning rock formations.

Best Things to Do in Cala Gonone: Activities and Tours

  • Boat Tours: There are various boat tours available in Cala Gonone which will take you to the nearby beaches and sea caves that are only accessible by boat.
  • Kayaking: Visitors can rent kayaks and explore the coastline on their own, stopping at various beaches, coves, and sea caves along the way.
  • Scuba Diving: The clear waters around the Cala Gonone offer some of the best diving spots in Sardinia. Visitors can explore underwater caves, swim with dolphins, and spot rare marine life.

Food and Drink Scene of Cala Gonone

Must-Try Dishes

  • Fregola con le arselle: A traditional Sardinian dish made with small pasta pearls and clams in a delicious broth.
  • Culurgiones: Stuffed pasta similar to ravioli, typically filled with potato and cheese or meat and served with a tomato sauce.
  • Porceddu: A roast piglet that is cooked slowly over an open fire for several hours.

Local Beverages

  • Cannonau: A robust red wine unique to Sardinia that pairs perfectly with meat dishes.
  • Filu e ferru: A strong, clear spirit made from locally grown grains.

Where to Stay in Cala Gonone: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Hotel Brancamaria: This four-star hotel is located in the heart of Cala Gonone and features an outdoor pool, wellness centre, and restaurant serving traditional Sardinian dishes.
  • Hotel Villa Gustui Maris: A boutique hotel with beautiful sea views, a private beach, and a restaurant serving fresh seafood.
  • B&B Dommu Agostina: A charming bed and breakfast located in the town center, it offers guests clean, comfortable rooms and a warm welcome from the owners.

What to Buy in Cala Gonone: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Coral Jewelry: Cala Gonone is famous for its coral jewelry, which makes for a unique and beautiful souvenir.
  • Sardinian Honey: The island is known for its honey, which comes in a range of flavors including chestnut, lavender, and myrtle.
  • Bottarga: A local delicacy made from fish roe, bottarga is often grated over pasta dishes or served on its own with fresh bread.

Local Culture and Customs of Cala Gonone

The people of Cala Gonone are proud of their Sardinian heritage and traditions, and visitors will often hear traditional music being played in the streets or see locals wearing traditional dress during festivals.

It is also worth noting that Sunday is a holy day in Sardinia, and many shops and businesses will be closed. Visitors should plan accordingly to avoid any inconvenience.

Best Time to Visit Cala Gonone

The best time to visit Cala Gonone is from May to October, when the weather is warm and sunny. July and August are the busiest months, so visitors should book accommodations and activities in advance.

Do’s and Don’ts of Cala Gonone

Do:

  • Respect the local culture and customs.
  • Try the local cuisine and beverages.
  • Take care of the environment and respect the natural surroundings.

Don’t:

  • Wear revealing clothing in public places.
  • Be loud or disruptive in residential areas.
  • Take souvenirs from archaeological sites or protected natural areas.

Safety and Health

Safety in Cala Gonone

Cala Gonone is generally a very safe destination for visitors. However, as with any tourist destination, visitors should be aware of pickpocketing and petty theft.

Health in Cala Gonone

The local water supply is generally safe to drink, but visitors should be cautious when eating raw or undercooked seafood. Visitors should also be aware of the risks of heat exhaustion and sunstroke during the hot summer months.

Getting to Cala Gonone: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Cala Gonone

The nearest airports to Cala Gonone are Olbia Costa Smeralda Airport (~100km) and Cagliari Elmas Airport (~210km). From the airport visitors can rent a car, take a taxi or take public transportation to reach Cala Gonone.

Bus Stations Near Cala Gonone

The main bus station in Cala Gonone is located in the town center and offers connections to nearby towns and cities, including Olbia and Cagliari.

Other Options

Visitors can also reach Cala Gonone by ferry from the mainland port of Civitavecchia to Olbia or Cagliari.

How to Get Around in Cala Gonone

Cala Gonone is a small town that can easily be explored on foot. Visitors can also rent a bicycle or scooter to explore the surrounding areas or take the local bus for a more comfortable mode of transportation.

Where to Go Next from Cala Gonone: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Golfo di Orosei: A stunning area of Sardinia’s east coast famous for its rugged cliffs, hidden coves, and beautiful beaches.
  • Oliena: An ancient town located in the mountains of central Sardinia, famous for its wine and traditional crafts.
  • Nuoro: A bustling city located in the heart of Sardinia, known for its vibrant arts scene and historical sites.

Conclusion

Cala Gonone is a hidden gem on the east coast of Sardinia, offering visitors a unique blend of natural beauty, cultural heritage, and outdoor adventure. With its stunning beaches, rugged hiking trails, and delicious cuisine, this is a destination that should not be missed.
