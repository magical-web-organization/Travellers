Introduction to Ogliastra

Located in the heart of Sardinia, the province of Ogliastra stands out for its unspoiled natural beauty, rugged coastline, and preserved traditions. This off-the-beaten-track destination is a paradise for adventure seekers, culture enthusiasts, and foodies alike. Visitors to Ogliastra can explore charming towns, hike in the mountains, and relax on some of the most beautiful beaches in Italy.

This comprehensive Ogliastra travel guide covers everything you need to know to plan your trip to this off-the-beaten-path destination in Sardinia.

List of Places to Visit in Ogliastra

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Museum of Ogliastra: Located in the town of Lanusei, this museum displays artefacts related to the history and culture of the province.
  • Ethnographic Museum of Jerzu: This museum showcases the traditional crafts, clothing, and agricultural tools of the local population.
  • Nuraghe Serbissi: One of the best examples of prehistoric architecture in Ogliastra, the Nuraghe Serbissi is a megalithic structure that dates back to the Bronze Age.
  • Ulassai Art Museum: Founded by the renowned painter Maria Lai, this museum exhibits contemporary art and traditional crafts by local artists.

Natural Attractions of Ogliastra: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Cala Luna: A stunning beach accessible only by boat or foot, Cala Luna is surrounded by towering cliffs and crystal-clear waters.
  • Gorroppu Canyon: A popular destination for hiking and rock climbing, this canyon features breathtaking views of the surrounding mountains and valleys.
  • Supramonte: Ogliastra’s mountain range offers a variety of outdoor activities, including trekking, caving, and canyoning.
  • Santa Maria Navarrese: A picturesque fishing village where you can rent a boat and explore the coastline, take a walk on the scenic promenade, or enjoy local seafood dishes.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Ogliastra

Selvaggio Blu

Considered one of the most challenging hiking routes in Europe, this 6-day trek takes visitors along the wild cliffs of the Ogliastra coast. The hike starts in Santa Maria Navarrese and ends in Cala Gonone and offers breathtaking views of the Mediterranean Sea, hidden coves, and rock formations.

Mountains of Ogliastra

Ogliastra’s mountains are ideal for hiking and mountain biking. Some of the most popular routes include:

  • Monte Oro: This mountain peak (1834 mt) offers panoramic views of the Gulf of Orosei and the Supramonte massif.
  • Perda Liana: Located near the town of Ulassai, this majestic rock formation offers stunning views of the surrounding countryside.
  • Taccu di Osoru: A challenging hike that leads to the highest peak in Ogliastra (1163 mt), offering 360° views of the mountain range.

Best Things to Do in Ogliastra: Activities and Tours

Kayaking Tour

Explore Ogliastra’s secluded coves, hidden beaches, and rock formations on a kayaking tour. Suitable for both beginners and experienced paddlers, this tour allows visitors to discover the coastline at their own pace.

Wine Tasting Tour

Visit some of Ogliastra’s wineries and taste the local Cannonau red wine. A knowledgeable guide will introduce you to the history of viticulture in the region, the grape varieties used, and the production methods.

Week-Long Itinerary for Ogliastra

  • Day 1-2: Explore the coast and the mountain range of Ogliastra, hiking or biking.
  • Day 3: Visit the Ethnographic Museum of Jerzu and the Nuraghe Serbissi.
  • Day 4: Take a boat tour from Santa Maria Navarrese to Cala Luna and enjoy the crystal-clear waters of the Mediterranean
  • Day 5: Explore the town of Lanusei, visit the Museum of Ogliastra and the Ulassai Art Museum.
  • Day 6: Discover the Gorroppu Canyon on a guided trekking tour.
  • Day 7: Take a wine Tasting tour.

Food and Drink Scene of Ogliastra

Must-Try Dishes

  • Culurgiones: Traditional Sardinian ravioli stuffed with potatoes, fresh cheese, and mint, served with tomato sauce.
  • Porceddu: Roasted suckling pig, a staple of Sardinian cuisine and one of the most iconic dishes in Ogliastra.
  • Sebadas: Fried cheese-filled pastries drizzled with honey, a sweet and savory delicacy.

Local Beverages

  • Cannonau: A robust red wine made from the Cannonau grape, which grows exceptionally well in Ogliastra’s rocky soil.
  • Filu e Ferru: A local grappa made from the distillation of grape skins and stems, infused with myrtle leaves and other herbs.

Where to Stay in Ogliastra: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

There are several accommodation options in Ogliastra, ranging from budget-friendly hostels to luxury hotels. Some of the best places to stay include the following:

  • Hotel La Torre: A seafront hotel located in Arbatax, with a swimming pool, private beach, and excellent restaurant.
  • Hotel La Bitta: A 4-star hotel in Santa Maria Navarrese, with a panoramic terrace, an outdoor pool, and a spa.
  • Ecoparco Neule: A camping site near Ulassai, ideal for nature lovers and outdoor enthusiasts.

What to Buy in Ogliastra: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Some of the best souvenirs to bring back home from Ogliastra include:

  • Filigree Jewelry: Ogliastra is known for its intricate filigree jewelry, made of gold and silver.
  • Ceramics: The town of Tortolì is famous for its ceramics, which depict Sardinian folk motifs and designs.
  • Bottarga: A local delicacy made of cured fish roe, often served grated on pasta or salads.

Local Culture and Customs of Ogliastra

Ogliastra has a rich cultural heritage and deeply-rooted traditions. Some of the most notable customs include the following:

  • Carnival of Oristano: One of Sardinia’s most colorful and lively carnival celebrations, which features elaborate costumes, parades, and traditional dances.
  • Santu Antine Festivity: A religious festival held in the village of Foni, in honor of Saint Constantine, featuring goat races and a traditional feast.
  • Cortes Apertas: A traditional festival held in many Ogliastra’s towns, where visitors can sample local foods, wines, and crafts.

Best Time to Visit Ogliastra

The best time to visit Ogliastra is between May and September when the weather is warm and pleasant. At this time of year, visitors can enjoy the beaches, hike in the mountains, and participate in outdoor activities.

Do’s and Don’ts of Ogliastra

Do:

  • Respect local customs and traditions.
  • Try the local cuisine and wines.
  • Explore the natural beauty of Ogliastra.

Don’t:

  • Leave trash in nature.
  • Enter private properties without permission.
  • Take pictures of people without asking for their consent.

Safety and Health

Safety in Ogliastra

Ogliastra is a safe destination, but visitors should take common-sense precautions to protect themselves and their belongings. These include the following:

  • Keep valuables out of plain sight
  • Lock doors and windows when leaving accommodations
  • Avoid walking alone at night in deserted areas.

Health in Ogliastra

There are no significant health risks associated with traveling to Ogliastra. However, it is advisable to carry a basic first aid kit and stay hydrated during outdoor activities.

Getting to Ogliastra: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Ogliastra

The nearest airports to Ogliastra are Olbia Airport and Cagliari-Elmas Airport. Both airports are serviced by major airlines such as Alitalia, Ryanair, and EasyJet.

Bus Stations Near Ogliastra

The main bus station in Ogliastra is in the town of Tortoli, which connects the province with major cities in Sardinia.

Other Options

Renting a car is another option for traveling to Ogliastra and allows visitors to explore the area at their own pace.

How to Get Around in Ogliastra

Public transport options in Ogliastra are limited, and car rental is the most convenient way to get around. Taxis are available, but they can be expensive for long distances.

Where to Go Next from Ogliastra: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Cagliari

The capital of Sardinia is located about two hours from Ogliastra and features several cultural attractions, museums, and a vibrant nightlife.

Alghero

A charming town on the northwest coast of Sardinia, Alghero is renowned for its medieval architecture, picturesque harbor, and delicious seafood.

Conclusion

Ogliastra is a unique destination in Sardinia, offering visitors a chance to discover unspoiled nature, ancient traditions, and delicious cuisine. Whether you’re an adventure seeker, culture enthusiast, or simply looking for a relaxing getaway, there’s something for everyone in Ogliastra. Use this comprehensive guide to plan your next trip to this enchanting part of Italy.
