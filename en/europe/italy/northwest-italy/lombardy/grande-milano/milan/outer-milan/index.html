Introduction to Outer Milan

Outer Milan is a picturesque region situated in the Grande Milano area of Lombardy, northwest Italy, known for its natural beauty, outdoor adventures, rich culture, and historical significance. It is a must-visit destination for travelers who wish to explore the hidden gems and local customs of Milan beyond the urban buzz of the city center. This tourist guide provides you with essential information on what to see, do, eat, buy, and avoid in Outer Milan.

List of Places to Visit in Outer Milan

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Outer Milan is home to many historically significant museums and monuments that reflect the region’s rich cultural heritage. Some of its most popular attractions include:

  1. Villa Arconati: This stunning 17th-century villa is an architectural masterpiece and features beautiful Italian-style gardens, a maze, and a lake. It has served as a cultural center for centuries and hosts various music and art events.
  2. Certosa di Garegnano: A former Carthusian monastery, this historic monument dates back to the 14th century and houses many valuable artworks and religious relics. The chapel is a must-see for its beautiful frescoes and intricate carved decoration.
  3. Castello Sforzesco: This magnificent medieval castle, built for the Sforza dynasty in the 15th century, is now a museum that showcases various collections of art and historical artifacts, including works by Michelangelo and Leonardo da Vinci.
  4. Museo del Novecento: A contemporary art museum that houses an extensive collection of works from the 20th century by Italian and international artists.
  5. Boscoincittà: A unique eco-museum that offers educational and cultural activities related to biodiversity, ecology, and sustainability.

Natural Attractions of Outer Milan: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Outer Milan boasts breathtaking natural landscapes and a wide array of outdoor adventures for hikers, campers, and nature enthusiasts. Some of the top natural attractions include:

  1. Parco delle Groane: Spread over 3,400 hectares, this park is a nature reserve and a top destination for cycling, hiking, and picnicking. It is home to a variety of flora and fauna, including red kites, owls, and foxes.
  2. Montevecchia Regional Park: Nestled in the Lombard Prealps, this natural reserve offers spectacular views of the Brianza valley and a chance to discover traditional farmhouses and rural life.
  3. Brianza Lakes: The Lakes of Brianza are a string of small lakes dotted among the green hills of Lombardy. They offer plenty of water sports and outdoor activities, such as swimming, kayaking, and sailboarding.
  4. Val Curone: This valley, located in the south of Lombardy, is a paradise for adventure seekers, with plenty of hiking and rock climbing opportunities.
  5. Monte San Giorgio: This mountain is a UNESCO World Heritage site for its geological importance and fossils.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Outer Milan

For outdoor enthusiasts, Outer Milan has a plethora of hiking and biking routes that offer a chance to explore the region’s natural beauty and rich cultural heritage. Some of the top hiking and biking routes include:

  1. The Via dei Monti Lariani: This hiking trail takes you through the beautiful scenery of Lake Como and the surrounding mountains. It offers stunning views of the lake and many historic sites along the way.
  2. Brianza Cycle Route: A 50 km bike route that takes you through the scenic Brianza hills and picturesque villages.
  3. Parco Nord: This park, located on the outskirts of the city, has many trails for hiking and biking, including a nature trail that takes you through oak woodlands and meadows.

Best Things to Do in Outer Milan: Activities and Tours

Outer Milan offers a wide range of activities and tours for travelers to experience the local culture, food, and nature. Some of the best things to do include:

  1. Food tours: Sample some of the best Italian cuisine, such as risotto alla milanese, polenta, and panettone.
  2. Wine tours: Visit the region’s vineyards and taste its world-famous wines, such as Chianti and Barolo.
  3. Fashion tours: Discover the history and culture of Italy’s fashion industry by visiting its famous fashion districts and boutiques.
  4. Photography tours: Capture the breathtaking scenery and cultural landmarks of Outer Milan through the lens.

Week-Long Itinerary for Outer Milan

Here’s a suggested week-long itinerary for Outer Milan:

Day 1: Visit the Museo del Novecento and explore the historic district of Brera. Day 2: Take a hiking/biking tour of Parco delle Groane and have a picnic. Day 3: Visit the Castello Sforzesco, and then explore the nearby Navigli district. Day 4: Take a food tour of Milan and visit some of its most famous restaurants. Day 5: Visit the Certosa di Garegnano, and then take a tour of Villa Arconati. Day 6: Take a wine tour of the Franciacorta wine region and taste some of its local wines. Day 7: Day trip to Lake Como and explore its beautiful scenery.

Food and Drink Scene of Outer Milan

Outer Milan offers a rich culinary tradition that reflects the region’s agricultural legacy and cultural diversity. Here are some of the must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  1. Risotto alla milanese: a creamy risotto dish made with saffron and Parmesan cheese.
  2. Osso buco: a traditional Lombardian dish made with veal shanks, vegetables, and white wine.
  3. Cassoeula: a hearty stew made with pork, cabbage, and beans.
  4. Negroni Sbagliato: a variation of the classic Negroni cocktail, made with sparkling white wine instead of gin.
  5. Panettone: a sweet bread loaf with raisins and candied fruits, served during the holiday season.

Local Beverages

  1. Campari: a popular Italian aperitif, made with herbs, spices, and fruit.
  2. Franciacorta wine: a sparkling wine made in the Franciacorta region, known for its dry and complex flavors.
  3. Grappa: a traditional Italian brandy made from grape pomace.

Where to Stay in Outer Milan: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Outer Milan has plenty of accommodations for travelers of all budgets and preferences. Here are some of the best options:

  1. NH Milano Fiera: A modern hotel located near the Milan Convention Center, with comfortable rooms and excellent amenities.
  2. Ostello Bello Grande: A popular hostel located in the historic Brera district, known for its stylish decor and friendly atmosphere.
  3. San Giorgio: A charming guesthouse located in the beautiful town of Bellagio, overlooking Lake Como.

What to Buy in Outer Milan: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Outer Milan offers many unique souvenirs and local products that make great gifts and keepsakes. Some of the popular items include:

  1. Fashion items: Milan is known for its fashion industry and many boutiques and outlets offer high-end clothing and accessories.
  2. Arts and crafts: Many local artisans create handmade goods, such as ceramics, glassware, and leather goods.
  3. Food and wine: Local markets offer a variety of fresh produce, meats, cheeses, and wines, that make great gifts.
  4. Olive oil: Lombardy is known for its high-quality olive oil, which is available at many food markets and stores.

Local Culture and Customs of Outer Milan

The people of Outer Milan are known for their hospitality, rich cultural heritage, and strong community spirit. Some of the local customs and traditions include:

  1. Art and fashion: Milan is a hub of contemporary art and fashion, and locals take great pride in their thriving creative industries.
  2. Religious celebrations: The people of Lombardy are devout Catholics, and religious celebrations are an important part of the region’s culture and traditions.
  3. Food and wine: Lombardy is known for its cuisine and wine-making, and locals take great pride in their delicious and high-quality products.

Best Time to Visit Outer Milan

The best time to visit Outer Milan is between April and October when the weather is mild and pleasant for outdoor activities and sightseeing. The months of May to June and September to October are ideal for hiking, while July and August are perfect for swimming and water sports.

Do’s and Don’ts of Outer Milan

Do:

  1. Respect local customs and traditions.
  2. Learn some basic Italian phrases.
  3. Dress appropriately for the weather and cultural sites.
  4. Try local cuisine and wine.

Don’t:

  1. Drink in public places.
  2. Touch historic artefacts or landmarks.
  3. Be too loud or disruptive in public places.
  4. Wear revealing or provocative clothing in churches or religious sites.

Safety and Health

Safety in Outer Milan

Outer Milan is a relatively safe region, but it’s always best to take precautions when traveling. Here are some safety tips to keep in mind:

  1. Avoid carrying large amounts of cash or expensive items.
  2. Be aware of pickpockets in crowded areas.
  3. Stick to well-lit and populated areas at night.
  4. Use licensed taxi services or public transportation.

Health in Outer Milan

Italy has a high standard of healthcare, and there are many hospitals and clinics in Outer Milan that offer medical services. It’s advisable to have travel insurance that covers medical emergencies and accidents.
