Introduction to North

North is a charming neighborhood in Grande Milano, located in the Lombardy region of Northwest Italy. This vibrant area has something for everyone, from world-class museums and historical landmarks to breathtaking natural scenery and exciting outdoor activities. Whether you are a history buff, an art lover, a foodie, or an outdoor enthusiast, North is sure to leave you with unforgettable memories.

In this guide, we will take a closer look at the top attractions, tours, accommodations, and dining options in North, as well as practical tips for getting there and exploring the area.

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

North is a treasure trove of cultural attractions, with many important historical landmarks and museums.

At the top of the list is the Castello Sforzesco, a majestic fortress built in the 15th century that now houses several museums and art collections. Inside, visitors can admire works by Italian masters such as Michelangelo and Leonardo da Vinci, as well as antique furniture, armor, and musical instruments.

Another must-see attraction is the Pinacoteca di Brera, a world-renowned art gallery showcasing masterpieces by Italian and European artists from the 13th to the 20th centuries. Visitors can admire works by Raphael, Caravaggio, Rembrandt, and other celebrated painters in the gallery’s stunning halls.

No visit to North would be complete without a stop at the Leonardo da Vinci Museum of Science and Technology, which boasts an impressive collection of scientific instruments, machines, and prototypes designed by the famous Italian Renaissance polymath.

Natural Attractions of North: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

North also offers plenty of opportunities for outdoor enthusiasts to explore the natural beauty of the area.

One of the most popular destinations is Parco Sempione, a vast green oasis in the heart of the city that offers scenic walking paths, bike trails, and lakeside picnic areas. Visitors can also admire the impressive Arco della Pace, a triumphal arch that dates back to the 19th century and offers stunning views of the park and the city.

For a different kind of adventure, head to the Navigli District, where you can take a romantic boat ride down the historic canals and admire the colorful houses, shops, and cafes along the waterfront.

Hiking/Biking Routes of North

If you want to explore North on two wheels or two feet, there are plenty of scenic routes to choose from.

One of the most popular bike trails is the Navigli Park Bikeway, which winds its way through the charming Navigli District and along the historic canals.

For walkers and hikers, the Sentiero Passeggiando con Gusto is an ideal route that takes you through the lush greenery of Parco Sempione and past some of North’s most scenic landmarks and monuments.

Best Things to Do in North: Activities and Tours

North offers a wide range of activities and tours to suit all tastes and interests.

For a unique and unforgettable experience, take a hot air balloon ride over the city and admire spectacular views of Milan’s skyline and countryside.

Art lovers should not miss the opportunity to take a private guided tour of the Pinacoteca di Brera or Teatro alla Scala, two of the city’s most iconic cultural institutions.

Finally, foodies can satisfy their cravings by taking a guided tasting tour of North’s best street food vendors, traditional trattorias, and wine bars.

Week-Long Itinerary for North

If you’re planning a trip to North and want to make the most of your time, here’s a suggested itinerary:

  • Day 1: Visit Castello Sforzesco and the museums inside, including the Pinacoteca di Brera and the Museum of Ancient Art.
  • Day 2: Explore the Navigli District and take a boat tour of the canals. Stop for lunch at one of the many popular restaurants and cafes along the waterfront.
  • Day 3: Enjoy a leisurely day at Parco Sempione. Rent bikes and explore the park’s scenic trails and lakeside areas.
  • Day 4: Take a private guided tour of the Teatro alla Scala or Leonardo da Vinci Museum of Science and Technology.
  • Day 5: Spend the day shopping and browsing at Galleria Vittorio Emanuele II, one of the world’s oldest and most beautiful shopping arcades.
  • Day 6: Take an early morning hot air balloon ride over the city and enjoy breathtaking panoramic views of Milan from above.
  • Day 7: Visit Milan Cathedral and check out the city’s vibrant street art scene and local markets.

Food and Drink Scene of North

North is an excellent destination for foodies. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages to sample during your stay:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Risotto alla Milanese: a creamy rice dish made with saffron and Parmesan cheese.
  • Cotoletta alla Milanese: breaded and fried veal cutlets, typically served with a side of potatoes or salad.
  • Panettone: a sweet Italian bread loaf traditionally eaten during the Christmas holidays.

Local Beverages

  • Negroni Sbagliato: a popular cocktail made from Campari, gin, and sparkling wine, served over ice.
  • Lombardia wines: the Lombardy region is known for its high-quality wines, including reds such as Franciacorta and whites such as Pinot Bianco.

Where to Stay in North: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

North offers a wide range of accommodations to suit all budgets and preferences.

For a luxurious stay, consider booking a room at the Hotel Principe di Savoia, a five-star hotel that exudes old-world elegance and charm. Alternatively, the Milan Marriott Hotel offers chic and modern accommodations in the heart of the city.

Budget travelers can opt for a comfortable hostel or guesthouse, such as the popular Ostello Bello or the charming B&B Brera.

What to Buy in North: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

If you’re looking to bring home some unique souvenirs or local products from your stay in North, here are some popular options:

  • Design items and furniture by famous Italian designers
  • Local food products such as olive oil, cheese, and truffles
  • Handcrafted leather goods and accessories by local artisans

Local Culture and Customs of North

The people of North are proud of their city and region, and they are generally friendly and welcoming to visitors. Italian is the official language, though many locals also speak English.

It’s important to dress modestly when visiting religious landmarks, such as Milan Cathedral, and to be respectful of local customs and traditions.

Best Time to Visit North

The best time to visit North is during the spring or fall, when the weather is mild and pleasant and the crowds are smaller than in the summer. However, if you’re planning to attend a specific event or festival, be sure to check the dates and plan accordingly.

Do’s and Don’ts of North

Do:

  • Tip waitstaff at restaurants (usually around 10%)
  • Greet people with a friendly “buongiorno” or “buonasera”
  • Try to speak a bit of Italian, even if it’s just a few words

Don’t:

  • Wear revealing clothing in religious landmarks
  • Expect to find many stores and restaurants open on Sundays
  • Drink cappuccino after midday (it’s considered a breakfast drink in Italy)

Safety and Health

Safety in North

North is a relatively safe neighborhood, though it’s always important to be aware of your surroundings and take common-sense precautions such as keeping your valuables close by and not walking alone at night.

Health in North

Italy has a high standard of healthcare, and visitors can find excellent medical care at private clinics and hospitals throughout North and Milan. However, it’s always a good idea to have comprehensive travel insurance in case of illness or injury.

Getting to North: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near North

The closest airport to North is Milan Linate Airport, which is about 6 kilometers southeast of the city center. Other nearby airports include Milan Malpensa Airport and Milan Bergamo Airport, both of which offer international and domestic flights.

Bus Stations Near North

The main bus station in Milan is the Lampugnano Bus Station, which is located about 7 kilometers west of North. The station offers connections to other Italian and European cities.

Other Options

Another popular way to get around Milan and North is by using the city’s efficient and extensive public transportation system, which includes buses, trams, and the underground metro.

How to Get Around in North

Walking and cycling are popular options for getting around North and exploring the city’s many attractions and landmarks. Taxis and public transportation are also widely available.

Where to Go Next from North: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

North is located in a strategic location that makes it a convenient base for exploring other destinations in Italy and Europe. Some popular nearby destinations include:

  • Lake Como: a picturesque lake north of Milan that offers scenic hiking trails, charming towns, and beautiful villas.
  • Genoa: a historic port city on the Italian Riviera known for its stunning architecture, seafood restaurants, and museums.
  • Turin: a lively city in the Piedmont region known for its chocolate, wine, and winter sports.

Conclusion

North, Milan is a charming and culturally rich neighborhood that offers something for everyone. Whether you’re interested in history, art, food, or outdoor adventures, North is sure to leave you with a lasting impression.
