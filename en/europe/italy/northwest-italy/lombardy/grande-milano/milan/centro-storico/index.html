Introduction to Centro Storico

Centro Storico, located in the heart of Milan, is a charming historic district that is home to many cultural and artistic attractions, as well as high-end shopping and fine dining. With its narrow cobblestone streets, elegant palaces, and ancient ruins, Centro Storico is an ideal destination for history buffs and art lovers. Here, you will find some of Milan’s most recognizable landmarks, such as the Milan Cathedral, and also some hidden gems, such as the unique antique shops.

List of Places to Visit in Centro Storico

Below is a list of must-see places in Centro Storico divided into culturally important attractions and natural attractions. From museums and historical places to outdoor adventures and scenic views, this list has something to offer everyone.

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • The Milan Cathedral: This Gothic-style cathedral is the most iconic landmark of the city. Visitors can climb its rooftop terraces to enjoy a panoramic view of Milan.
  • The Royal Palace of Milan: This palace has been home to several rulers of Milan throughout history. It now houses a museum and hosts temporary exhibitions.
  • Sforza Castle: A fortress that served as the residence of Milan’s rulers from the 14th to the 16th century. It now houses several museums, including one dedicated to Michelangelo’s unfinished sculpture, the Pieta Rondanini.
  • The Pinacoteca di Brera: A museum that showcases Milanese and Italian artwork, including those of some of the most famous Italian painters, from the Gothic era to modern times.
  • The Leonardo da Vinci Museum of Science and Technology: This museum is located within a former monastery complex and focuses on the work of Leonardo da Vinci and other Renaissance-era scientists.
  • The Galleria Vittorio Emanuele II: This shopping arcade is one of the oldest in the world. It features luxury boutiques, historic cafes, and a glass dome.

Natural Attractions of Centro Storico: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • The Sempione Park: This park covers an area of over 38 hectares and is home to several historic buildings, including the Arch of Peace, and the Arena Civica. It is a perfect place for a walk or a picnic.
  • Piazza dei Mercanti: A beautiful medieval square in the heart of Milan, surrounded by historic buildings, including the Palace of the Podestà and the Loggia degli Osii.
  • Via Dante: A pedestrian street surrounded by shops, cafes, and restaurants that leads from Piazza del Duomo to the Sforza Castle. It is perfect for a leisurely stroll.
  • The Naviglio Grande: A canal built during the Renaissance-era that served as the main artery for goods and people traveling between Milan and the countryside. Today, it is a popular destination for boat tours, cycling, or strolling along its banks.
  • The Columns of San Lorenzo: A well-preserved Roman ruin in the heart of Milan that dates back to the 2nd century AD. It is a perfect spot for a photo opportunity.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Centro Storico

Centro Storico is not known for its hiking or biking trails, as it is primarily an urban district. However, there are several parks and open spaces to explore, such as the Sempione Park, where visitors can walk or bike.

Best Things to Do in Centro Storico: Activities and Tours

Centro Storico offers a wide range of activities and tours for visitors of all ages and interests. Some of the best things to do in Centro Storico include:

  • Food tours: A great way to discover the local food scene of Milan. You can explore traditional markets, historic cafes, and taste some of the finest local dishes.
  • Art tours: Milan is known for its rich cultural heritage, and a guided art tour can take you to some of the most iconic sights of Centro Storico, such as the Milan Cathedral, the Royal Palace of Milan, or the Pinacoteca di Brera.
  • Fashion tours: Milan is also well-known as one of the top fashion capitals of the world. A guided fashion tour can take you to some of the top boutiques and designer studios in Centro Storico.
  • Day trips: Centro Storico is located in the heart of Milan, and offer easy access to several nearby attractions. You can visit Lake Como, Bergamo, or the Ligurian Coast in a day trip from Milan.

Food and Drink Scene of Centro Storico

Milan is famous for its culinary tradition, which combines Northern Italian and Milanese flavors. Centro Storico is no exception, and visitors can explore some of the top restaurants, cafes, and bars in the city.

Must-Try Dishes

  • Risotto alla Milanese: A traditional Milanese dish made with saffron, butter, Parmigiano-Reggiano cheese, and Arborio rice.
  • Cotoletta alla Milanese: A breaded and fried veal cutlet that is a Milanese specialty.
  • Panettone: A sweet bread loaf that is a traditional Milanese dessert, usually eaten around Christmas time.

Local Beverages

  • Negroni Sbagliato: A traditional Milanese cocktail made with Campari, sweet vermouth, and Prosecco.
  • Chinato: A wine-based aperitif flavored with cloves, bark, and other aromatic herbs.

Where to Stay in Centro Storico: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Centro Storico has a great selection of hotels, hostels, and guesthouses that cater to all budgets and preferences. Some of the top choices include:

  • The Four Seasons Hotel Milano: A luxury hotel that offers spacious rooms and suites, a rooftop terrace, and an on-site restaurant.
  • Ostello Bello: A trendy hostel located in the heart of the city, offering shared and private rooms, a rooftop terrace, and a lively bar.
  • B&B Milano Old Town: A cozy guesthouse that offers charming rooms with a vintage-style decor, a communal kitchen and lounge, and a terrace.

What to Buy in Centro Storico: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Centro Storico is an excellent place to shop for local souvenirs and products. Visitors can explore some of the traditional markets, antique shops, and designer boutiques in the area.

Local Culture and Customs of Centro Storico

Milanese culture is deeply rooted in the city’s long history, its diverse population, and its artistic heritage. Visitors may notice that Milanese people tend to be more reserved than their Southern Italian counterparts, but they are friendly and welcoming nonetheless. It is customary to greet someone with a handshake or a kiss on the cheek, depending on your relationship.

Best Time to Visit Centro Storico

The best time to visit Centro Storico is from April to June or September to November, when the weather is mild and pleasant, and the crowds are smaller. July and August can be very hot and humid, and many locals go on holiday during this time, leading to fewer open businesses and attractions.

Do’s and Don’ts of Centro Storico

Here are some tips to help you make the most of your Centro Storico experience:

Do:

  • Try the local food: Milan is famous for its cuisine, and Centro Storico is an excellent place to discover traditional Milanese dishes.
  • Visit the museums and art galleries: Centro Storico is rich in art and history, and visitors should explore some of the top cultural institutions.
  • Be respectful: Milanese people are friendly and welcoming, but it is essential to respect their customs and culture.
  • Use public transportation: Centro Storico is mostly a pedestrian-friendly area, but visitors can use public transportation to get around the city quickly.

Don’t:

  • Forget to validate your public transportation ticket: Failure to validate your ticket can result in a fine from the inspectors who regularly patrol the buses and trams.
  • Leave valuables unattended: Centro Storico is a safe district, but it is always advisable to keep a close eye on your belongings.
  • Ignore the dress code in sacred places: When visiting churches, cathedrals, or other religious buildings, it is essential to dress respectfully and avoid wearing shorts, tank tops, or revealing clothing.

Safety and Health

Safety in Centro Storico

Centro Storico is a safe district, and visitors should not encounter any particular safety concerns. However, it is always advisable to keep an eye on your belongings and be aware of your surroundings, especially in crowded areas.

Health in Centro Storico

Centro Storico is a modern, developed district that offers high-quality healthcare services. Visitors should not have any trouble finding a pharmacy or a medical clinic in case of emergency.

Getting to Centro Storico: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Centro Storico

Milan has two airports, Milan Malpensa Airport (MXP) and Milan Linate (LIN). Both airports are served by several airlines and offer connections to many domestic and international destinations. From either airport, visitors can take a taxi, shuttle bus, or public transportation (bus or train) to reach Centro Storico.

Bus Stations Near Centro Storico

Milan’s most significant bus station is the Autostazione Lampugnano, located in the northwest part of the city. From here, visitors can take buses to many domestic and international destinations.

Other Options

Milan is well-connected by train to many Italian and European cities. The city’s main train station, Milano Centrale, is in the northeastern part of the city and offers high-speed services to many destinations, including Rome, Florence, and Venice.

How to Get Around in Centro Storico

Centro Storico is a primarily pedestrian-friendly area, and many of its attractions are within walking distance. Visitors can also use Milan’s public transportation system, which includes metro, buses, and trams.

Where to Go Next from Centro Storico: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Centro Storico is located in the heart of Milan, and visitors have easy access to many nearby destinations and excursions. Some of the top choices include:

  • Lake Como: A picturesque lake located about an hour away from Milan by train. Bellagio, one of the most charming towns on the lake, is a popular destination for day trips.
  • Bergamo: A medieval town located about an hour away from Milan by train. Its Upper Town, Citta Alta, is a UNESCO World Heritage site that offers breathtaking views of the surrounding mountains.
  • Ligurian Coast: A stunning stretch of coastline located about 2 hours away from Milan by train. Portofino, a posh seaside resort, and Cinque Terre, a collection of five picturesque fishing villages, are the most popular destinations on the coast.

Conclusion

Centro Storico is a historic district that offers visitors a glimpse into the rich culture and heritage of Milan. With its countless museums, art galleries, historic landmarks, and excellent cuisine, Centro Storico is an ideal destination for travelers who want to explore the heart of Milan and enjoy its unique charm. Plan your next trip to Italy’s fashion capital, and don’t forget to visit Centro Storico.
