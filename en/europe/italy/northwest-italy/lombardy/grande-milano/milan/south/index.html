Introduction to South

South is a charming district located in Grande Milano, Lombardy in Northwest Italy. It is a perfect destination to explore the cultural heritage and natural beauty of the region. The district is known for its lively street markets, exquisite architecture, and tranquil parks.

This travel guide will take you through the must-visit places in South, things to do, food scene, where to stay, safety tips and more. So, pack your bags and get ready to experience an unparalleled voyage.

List of Places to Visit in South

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Milan Cathedral

The Milan Cathedral, also known as the Duomo, is a striking piece of Gothic architecture. Standing tall with its exquisite details and intricate designs, it is considered an icon of Milan. Take a guided tour or climb the steps to the top of the cathedral to enjoy picturesque views of the city.

Museo del Novecento

Museo del Novecento is a must-visit for art lovers. It houses a collection of contemporary Italian art from the 20th century. The museum is situated in the Palazzo dell’Arengario, adjacent to the Piazza del Duomo.

Sforza Castle

Sforza Castle, a 15th-century fortress, is located in the heart of Milan. It houses several art collections, including Michelangelo’s last work, the Rondanini Pietà, and the famous Sala delle Asse, a room decorated with Leonardo da Vinci’s mural paintings.

Natural Attractions of South: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Parco Sempione

Parco Sempione is situated behind the Sforza Castle and is considered a green oasis in the heart of Milan city. Take a leisurely walk through the park to admire the beautiful sculptures, fountains, and gardens.

Porta Ticinese

Porta Ticinese is one of the ancient city gates which opens to the charming Navigli district. Walk along the Naviglio Grande canal, and enjoy the picturesque views of the waterways and antique bridges.

Naviglio Pavese

Naviglio Pavese is another canal that is a must-visit for its beautiful views and vibrant atmosphere. It is home to several bars and restaurants where you can enjoy delicious food and drinks while admiring the scenic views.

Hiking/Biking Routes of South

South Milan has several hiking and biking trails that take you through beautiful landscapes and historic sites. Here are some of the best trails to explore:

  • Ticino Park
  • Parco Regionale della Valle del Lambro
  • Sasso di Guidino Natural Reserve

Best Things to Do in South: Activities and Tours

Milan Fashion Tour

Milan is known as the fashion capital of the world, and a Milan fashion tour is the perfect way to explore the city’s fashion industry. The tour takes you through the luxury fashion district where you can shop for some of the world’s most exclusive designer labels.

Cooking Classes

Milan is also famous for its cuisine, and taking a cooking class is a great way to learn more about the local food culture. Several cooking schools and restaurants offer classes where you can learn to cook traditional Italian dishes.

Wine Tasting Tour

Explore the Lombardy region’s vineyards with a wine-tasting tour. This tour takes you through the scenic hills of Lombardy, where you can sample some of the area’s best wines.

Week-Long Itinerary for South

Here is a week-long itinerary for exploring South in Milan:

  • Day 1: Start your trip with a visit to the Milan Cathedral and Museo del Novecento
  • Day 2: Take a leisurely stroll through Parco Sempione and visit Sforza Castle
  • Day 3: Explore the Navigli district, view the Porta Ticinese, and walk along the Naviglio Grande canal
  • Day 4: Take a bike tour of the city or discover the scenic views of the Ticino Park
  • Day 5: Enjoy a Milan Fashion Tour or a cooking class
  • Day 6: Visit the Sasso di Guidino Natural Reserve and enjoy trekking or hiking
  • Day 7: Take a wine-tasting tour and explore the Lombardy region’s vineyards.

Food and Drink Scene of South

South Milan has an exceptional food and drink scene. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

Risotto alla Milanese

Risotto alla Milanese is a traditional Italian dish based on saffron and chicken or beef broth. It is one of the signature dishes of Milan and is a must-try when visiting South.

Cotoletta alla Milanese

Cotoletta alla Milanese is a breaded and fried veal cutlet that is unique to Milan. This dish is perfect for meat lovers, and it is usually served with a side of risotto or boiled potatoes.

Local Beverages

Negroni Sbagliato

Negroni Sbagliato is a popular cocktail in Milan. It is a twist on the classic Negroni cocktail but is made with sparkling wine instead of gin.

Campari

Campari, a bitter Italian aperitif, is a staple drink in Milan. It is commonly served as an aperitif or mixed with soda water.

Where to Stay in South: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

South Milan has a wide range of accommodation options to choose from. Here are some of the best places to stay:

Hotel Milano Scala

Hotel Milano Scala is a luxurious hotel situated in the heart of Milan. It offers a range of services and amenities, including a fitness center, spa, and restaurant.

Ostello Bello

Ostello Bello is a budget-friendly hostel that offers private and shared accommodation options. It provides a communal kitchen, free breakfast, and a rooftop terrace.

B&B Porta Venezia

B&B Porta Venezia is a charming guesthouse located near the Milan Central Station. It offers a warm and friendly atmosphere, with comfortable rooms and a delicious breakfast.

What to Buy in South: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Milan is known for its fashion industry, and you can find several high-end designer shops. Apart from that, some souvenirs worth bringing back home are:

  • Italian Leathergoods
  • Handmade Italian ceramics
  • Wine and cheese

Local Culture and Customs of South

South Milan has a rich cultural heritage with unique customs and traditions. Some of the cultural practices include:

  • Dressing up elegantly while visiting churches or other religious sites
  • Saying “ciao” or “buongiorno” when entering or leaving a store or shop
  • Taking a break in the afternoons as most shops and business close for a few hours.

Best Time to Visit South

The best time to visit South Milan is during the spring (April to May) or the fall (September to October). The weather is mild during these months, and there are fewer tourists around compared to the summer months.

Do’s and Don’ts of South

Do:

  • Dress up elegantly while visiting churches or other religious sites
  • Say “ciao” or “buongiorno” when entering or leaving a store or shop
  • Take a break in the afternoons as most shops and business close for a few hours.

Don’t:

  • Wear revealing or inappropriate clothing while visiting churches or other religious sites
  • Smoke in public areas, including restaurants and bars
  • Touch or climb on historic monuments or sculptures.

Safety and Health

Safety in South

South Milan is a safe district for tourists, but like any other city, it is best to take precautions like not carrying too much cash or valuables, staying aware of your surroundings, and avoiding poorly lit or deserted areas at night.

Health in South

The health system in Milan is excellent, but it is always best to have travel insurance with good medical coverage. It is also essential to carry any necessary medications and consult your doctor before traveling.

Getting to South: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near South

Milan has two airports - Milan Malpensa Airport and Milan Linate Airport. Both airports have direct connections to South Milan through public transportation like buses or taxis.

Bus Stations Near South

South Milan is well connected through the public transportation system. The nearest bus station is the Milano Nord Cadorna Station.

Other Options

The city’s metro system is extensive and offers easy and affordable transportation. Milan also has a widespread bike-sharing system, which could be an interesting option for exploring the city.

How to Get Around in South

Public transportation options like metros, buses, and trams are available in South Milan. There are also taxis available for hire, but they can be expensive.

Where to Go Next from South: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

South Milan is centrally located, which makes it easy to explore other nearby destinations. Some of these destinations include:

  • Lake Como
  • Bergamo
  • Pavia
  • Cremona

Conclusion

South Milan is an excellent destination for those who want to explore the beauty of Milan. From art and architecture to nature and adventure, this district has something for everyone. Use this comprehensive guide to plan your trip and get ready for an unforgettable experience.
