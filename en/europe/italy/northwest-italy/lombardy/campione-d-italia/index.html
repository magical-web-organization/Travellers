Introduction to Campione d’Italia

Campione d’Italia is a small Italian enclave that sits on the eastern shore of Lake Lugano, completely surrounded by Switzerland. The town has a unique identity as it is technically a part of Italy, but Swiss francs are used as currency and Italian is not the official language. Despite its small size, Campione d’Italia offers visitors a range of outdoor adventures, cultural attractions, and entertainment options.

List of Places to Visit in Campione d’Italia

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Campione d’Italia Casino

The Casino of Campione d’Italia is one of the main attractions of the small town. It is the oldest casino in Europe and has been open since 1917. The casino featured in several movies and is equipped with bars, restaurants, and entertainment events.

San Zenone

Situated on the eastern hill of Campione d’Italia, the church of San Zenone has a great location with views of Lake Lugano. Visitors to the church can admire easily visible frescoes of the 17th century attributed to Giovanni Battista Pozzi and Giuseppe Rusnati.

Canonica di San Lorenzo

Built in the 14th century, this former monastery now houses a small museum of sacred art with sculptures and paintings of historical significance.

Natural Attractions of Campione d’Italia: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Monte Sighignola

The summit of Monte Sighignola offers visitors beautiful views of Lake Lugano and Campione d’Italia. The mountaintop is accessible by an excellent road that offers views along the way.

Lake Lugano

With crystal clear waters, Lake Lugano offers visitors a chance to swim, boat, and fish which is a fun activity for families and anglers alike.

Fossil Museum in Meride

Situated in the small village of Meride which is a 15-minute drive from Campione d’Italia, the fossil museum makes for an interesting visit. The museum has a vast collection of preserved fossils and is dedicated to the history of geology.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Campione d’Italia

Cap Parco Monte Sighignola

The Cap Parco Monte Sighignola is perfect for hiking enthusiasts, with numerous trails covering over ten kilometers. The park offers breathtaking views of Lake Lugano from the mountain summit and has trails with different levels of difficulty.

The Green Way del Lago

For cycling enthusiasts, the “Green Way del Lago” is a bike trail with a total length of around 10 kilometers. The trail is relatively flat and encompasses beautiful views of the lake.

Best Things to Do in Campione d’Italia: Activities and Tours

Lake Lugano Boat Tours

Boat tours offer an excellent way to explore the lake, soak in the scenery of the surrounding mountains. Visitors can take different boat tours to explore many facets of Lake Lugano. The environment-friendly boats around the lake are run on electricity, hence reducing emissions and noise level.

Trekking and Nordic Walking

Trekking and Nordic walking tours are offered at Monte Sighignola, which hosts various walking trails varying in difficulty. The park authorities of Cap Parco Monte Sighignola organize guided walks.

Week-Long Itinerary for Campione d’Italia

Day 1: Arrive and Check-in

Spend the day exploring the quaint town Center, casino, and museums

Day 2: Hiking to Monte Sighignola

Begin the day early, and go for an hour-long hike to the summit of Monte Sighignola. Spend the rest of the day relaxing, taking pictures of the breathtaking views, and savoring the food and drinks in the surrounding area.

Day 3: The Fossil Museum

Visit the Fossil Museum in nearby Meride followed by a visit to the nearby town of Mendrisio.

Day 4: Green Way del Lago Route

Cycling enthusiast can explore the Green Way del Lago Route. This bike trail offers picturesque views of Lake Lugano.

Day 5: The Brunate Lighthouse

Drive to the nearby town of Brunate, which is a mountain village and features the historic Brunate Lighthouse. Visitors can go up to the top of the lighthouse and enjoy panoramic views of Lake Como. Take in a lovely meal while resting at a picturesque location.

Day 6: San Zenone and the casino

Visit the historical church of San Zenone located on the eastern hill of Campione d’Italia, which has a superb view of Lake Lugano. Also, take some time to explore the famous casino.

Day 7: Relaxing day

Spend a relaxed day at one of the nearby beaches or take a leisurely stroll through the town.

Food and Drink Scene of Campione d’Italia

Must-Try Dishes

Polenta

Polenta is a rich, hearty dish made of maize flour and is typically served with seasonings or stews.

Risotto

Another local favorite dish served hot or cold is risotto. Visitors can try a mix of seafood or parmesan cheese with risotto.

Local Beverages

Brescia Spritz

Brescia Spritz is an alcoholic beverage made of wine and Aperol, an orange-colored liqueur; it’s perfect to sip on along with a meal.

Prosecco

Prosecco is a locally produced Italian sparkling wine that pairs perfectly with a hearty Italian meal.

Where to Stay in Campione d’Italia: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Hotel Campione

Hotel Campione is a contemporary, four-star hotel with spacious rooms and beautiful views of Lake Lugano.

Helianthus

Helianthus is a stunning guesthouse overlooking a beautiful garden with panoramic views of Lake Lugano.

What to Buy in Campione d’Italia: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Take back Italian perfumes and cosmetics, local wines, olive oils, and local cheese to enjoy the unique Campione d’Italia taste at home.

Local Culture and Customs of Campione d’Italia

Campione d’Italia is unusual as it is an Italian town but with a unique identity with Swiss currency and a Swiss influence. The town is discussed as a great example of coexistence between two countries.

Best Time to Visit Campione d’Italia

The best time to travel to Campione d’Italia is during the spring (April-May) and autumn (September-November) seasons when the weather is pleasant and mild. The town is quieter during off-seasons, making it easier to explore and enjoy.

Do’s and Don’ts of Campione d’Italia

Do:

  • Dress appropriately when at cultural attractions or religious sites.
  • Carry Swiss francs in the town, as it’s the primary currency.
  • Try local specialties: polenta, risotto, and savor the local cuisine.

Don’t:

  • Do not feed the local wildlife or litter in environmentally sensitive areas.
  • Avoid loud music or talking loudly inside restaurants and churches.
  • Do not click pictures of the local townspeople without their permission.

Safety and Health

Safety in Campione d’Italia

Campione d’Italia is a relatively safe town. Exercise caution and respect when exploring the area and being aware of your surroundings. Be sure to take adequate measures to protect belongings, especially when on the beach.

Health in Campione d’Italia

Campione d’Italia has state-of-the-art medical infrastructure and qualified medical professionals. The local hospital in nearby Switzerland is a few minutes away.

Getting to Campione d’Italia: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Campione d’Italia

The nearest airport to Campione d’Italia is Milan Malpensa Airport, which is located around an hour and a half away by car.

Train Stations Near Campione d’Italia

Como is the nearest town that offers rail connectivity to Campione d’Italia.

Other Options

Campione d’Italia is accessible by bus from the nearby town of Lugano. Taxis and rental cars are also readily available for exploring the surrounding area.

How to Get Around in Campione d’Italia

Visitors can make use of the local buses to travel around town, or rent a car or bike. Rental cars and bikes are readily available in town and can be an excellent way to explore the area.

Where to Go Next from Campione d’Italia: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Lake Como

Lake Como is an iconic Italian location made famous by celebrities from around the world. Visit the historical architecture, appreciate the scenic beauty, and head out on a boat tour by cruising the lake.

Swiss Towns

Campione d’Italia is surrounded by Swiss towns that are worth visiting. The nearest town is Lugano, which is just a stone’s throw away from Campione d’Italia, and it offers excellent chocolate and beautiful views of the alps.

Conclusion

Campione d’Italia is a small but unique town that offers visitors a range of cultural attractions, outdoor activities, and opportunities to relax. Whether it is hiking or exploring the historical architecture, there is always something to keep visitors engaged. The town’s unique identity means that it brings together Italian and Swiss influences, making it an excellent place for people to learn more about the area’s history and cultures. The town’s small size means that visitors can easily explore the main highlights in just a few days or relax for extended periods - the best way to experience this small town.
