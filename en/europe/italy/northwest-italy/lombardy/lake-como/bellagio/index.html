Introduction to Bellagio

Bellagio, known as the ‘pearl of Lake Como’, is a picturesque town located on the tip of the inverted Y-shape lake, surrounded by the majestic Alps. This charming town has been a famous vacation spot since Roman times, attracting artists and writers who were fascinated by its stunning views and renewing atmosphere, making it the perfect destination for those seeking a break from the bustling city life.

Apart from scenic beauty, Bellagio offers a variety of cultural attractions, including museums, churches, villas, and outdoor activities such as hiking and biking trails, water sports, and golf courses.

List of Places to Visit in Bellagio

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Villa Melzi d’Eril: A beautiful complex consisting of a botanical garden with exotic plants and flowers, a villa, and a neo-classical chapel, located on the shores of the lake, dating back to the early 19th century.

  • Villa Carlotta: A luxurious villa that houses an art museum, botanical garden, and a park, located in the neighboring town of Tremezzina.

  • Church of San Giacomo: An impressive building with a tall bell tower, located in the heart of Bellagio’s historic center.

Natural Attractions of Bellagio: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • La Punta Spartivento: A panoramic viewpoint located on the tip of the promontory, offering stunning views of the lake and surrounding mountains.

  • Sentiero del Viandante: A 45 km hiking route from Abbadia Lariana to Colico with breathtaking views of the lake, ancient roman ruins, and small villages.

  • Sailing and water sports: Visitors can enjoy the clear waters of Lake Como by renting a boat, paddleboard, or kayak.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Bellagio

Bellagio is popular among hikers and bikers because of its diverse terrain and scenic views. Some of the recommended routes are:

  • Greenway del Lago di Como: A 10 km trail that passes through small villages, historical buildings, and ancient Roman roads.

  • Monte San Primo: A 15 km hike that takes you through the dense forest of the Val Sanagra Nature Reserve, leading to the summit of Monte San Primo.

  • Ghisallo Pass: A challenging 42 km route that takes you through the mountains, passing by the famous Madonna Del Ghisallo chapel.

Best Things to Do in Bellagio: Activities and Tours

  • Boat tour of Lake Como: A scenic boat tour of the lake, offering views of the stunning villas and villages surrounding the lake.

  • Cooking class: Learn to cook authentic Lombardian dishes, such as risotto and panettone, from local chefs.

  • Wine tasting: Taste locally-produced wines from the region, including the famous Nebbiolo, Sangiovese, and Barbera.

Week-Long Itinerary for Bellagio

Day 1: Explore the historic center of Bellagio and visit the Church of San Giacomo.

Day 2: Hike the Greenway del Lago di Como trail, starting from Colonno and ending in Cadenabbia.

Day 3: Visit the Villas of Balbianello and Carlotta in Tremezzina.

Day 4: Take a boat tour around Lake Como, stopping at the famous villas, such as Villa del Balbianello and Villa Carlotta.

Day 5: Take a cooking class in Bellagio and learn to cook authentic Lombardian dishes.

Day 6: Explore the small towns and villages surrounding the lake, such as Varenna and Menaggio.

Day 7: Relax by the lake and enjoy the stunning views of the Alps.

Food and Drink Scene of Bellagio

Must-Try Dishes

  • Risotto alla Milanese: A typical Lombardian dish made with rice, saffron, butter, and parmesan cheese.

  • Cotoletta alla Milanese: Breaded and fried veal steak, served with potatoes or salad.

  • Panna cotta: A dessert made with cream, sugar, and gelatin, served with fruit or caramel sauce.

Local Beverages

  • Nebbiolo: A full-bodied red wine, made from Nebbiolo grapes, typical of the Piedmont region.

  • Sangiovese: A medium-bodied red wine, made from Sangiovese grapes, typical of the Tuscan region.

  • Amaro: A bitter herbal liqueur, served as a digestif after dinner.

Where to Stay in Bellagio: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Grand Hotel Villa Serbelloni: A luxurious hotel with beautiful views of the lake, located in the heart of Bellagio.

  • Hotel Bellagio: A mid-range hotel located near the lake and close to the historic center.

  • Casa la Rosa: A guesthouse with comfortable rooms and a garden, located in a quiet residential area.

What to Buy in Bellagio: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Silk scarfs and ties: Bellagio is famous for its silk products, including scarfs and ties, which make great souvenirs to bring back home.

  • Cheese: Lombardy is famous for its cheese, including Gorgonzola, Taleggio, and Parmesan, which can be bought at local markets.

  • Olive oil: Visitors can find excellent olive oil, produced locally in the region, at local markets.

Local Culture and Customs of Bellagio

  • Dress Code: Visitors are expected to dress modestly when visiting churches and other religious sites.

  • Tipping: Tipping is not customary in Italy, but rounding up the fare in restaurants or taxis is appreciated.

  • Language: Italian is the official language, but most people speak English in tourist areas.

Best Time to Visit Bellagio

The best time to visit Bellagio is from April to October, when the temperature is mild, and the weather is sunny, making it suitable for outdoor activities. Peak season is from June to August, when the crowds are the largest and prices are the highest.

Do’s and Don’ts of Bellagio

Do:

  • Greet people with a friendly “Buongiorno” (Good morning) or “Buonasera” (Good evening).

  • Try to speak Italian, even if it’s just basic phrases like “Grazie” (Thank you) or “Per favore” (Please).

  • Explore the small towns and villages around Lake Como, such as Varenna and Menaggio.

Don’t:

  • Don’t expect to shop on Sundays, as most shops and markets are closed.

  • Don’t be too loud or boisterous in public places, as it is considered impolite behavior.

  • Don’t forget to validate your train or bus tickets before boarding, as evasion is punishable by a fine.

Safety and Health

Safety in Bellagio

Bellagio is considered safe for travelers, but visitors should take precautions to avoid petty theft. It is recommended to keep valuable items in a safe or lock them in a luggage locker.

Health in Bellagio

Medical services are available at local hospitals and clinics. Visitors should have travel insurance in case of medical emergencies. It is also important to take precautions to avoid tick bites, which can cause Lyme disease.

Getting to Bellagio: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Bellagio

The closest international airport to Bellagio is Milan Malpensa Airport (MXP), located about one hour and a half away by car. Alternatively, visitors can also fly into Linate Airport (LIN) or Bergamo Orio al Serio Airport (BGY).

Bus Stations Near Bellagio

The closest bus stations to Bellagio are Como and Lecco, with regular services running between the two towns.

Other Options

Visitors can also rent a car to travel around the area or take a taxi.

How to Get Around in Bellagio

The best way to get around Bellagio is on foot, as the town center is pedestrian-friendly, and most of the attractions are within walking distance. Visitors can also take a local bus or a tourist train to explore the surrounding areas.

Where to Go Next from Bellagio: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Varenna

A charming village located on the eastern shore of Lake Como, known for its colorful houses, scenic promenade, and ancient castle.

Menaggio

A picturesque town located on the western shore of Lake Como, offering stunning views of the lake and surrounding mountains, as well as a variety of water sports and hiking trails.

Milan

The fashion capital of Italy, located about an hour and a half away by car, offers visitors a chance to explore the iconic landmarks, museums, and shopping districts.

Conclusion

Bellagio is a gem in the northern Italian region, with its stunning natural beauty, rich cultural history, and vibrant food scene. Whether visitors are attracted to outdoor adventures, cultural activities, or just seeking a break from the bustling city life, Bellagio offers something for everyone. With this comprehensive guide, visitors can plan their trip to Bellagio and explore all that this charming town and the surrounding area has to offer.
