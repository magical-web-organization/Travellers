Introduction to Lecco

Lecco is a stunning province situated in the region of Lombardy, northern Italy that offers visitors a plethora of activities. The province is known for its breathtaking natural landscapes, picturesque towns, and outdoor recreational options.

This guide will help you plan your trip to Lecco by outlining various things to do, interesting places to see, food and drink options, where to stay, and much more.

List of Places to Visit in Lecco

Followings are the top places to visit in Lecco:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  1. Palace of Fears: Once a prison until 1996, Palace of Fears now displays art, history, and culture of Lecco.

  2. Museum of Natural Sciences: This museum is a must-visit for science lovers. It features collections of flora, fauna fossils, and geology.

  3. Villa Manzoni: This 18th-century villa houses antique furnishings, paintings, drawings, and manuscripts.

Natural Attractions of Lecco: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  1. Lake Como: The spectacular Lake Como surrounds Lecco and provides visitors with scenic views, boat rides, and water sport activities.

  2. Monte Resegone: Lecco’s highest mountain that provides breathtaking panoramic views of the surrounding area.

  3. Adda River: The Adda River is about 313 km long and flows into Lake Como, where one can enjoy scenic boat rides as it flows through the province.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Lecco

Lecco is an ideal destination for outdoor enthusiasts, and there are several hiking and biking trails to explore. Here are some of the best hiking/biking routes:

  1. Sentiero del Viandante: This route runs along Lake Como and presents picturesque views of the lake, hills, and towns.

  2. The Wayfarer’s Trail: The trail is perfect for rock climbers as it involves climbing up a mountain and offers stunning views of Lake Como and its surrounding areas.

  3. Monte Barro Regional Park: This park offers several hiking trails with varying degrees of difficulty and spectacular views of Lecco.

Best Things to Do in Lecco: Activities and Tours

  1. Boating and Fishing: Visitors can rent boats and go fishing on Lake Como.

  2. Paragliding: Paragliding enthusiasts can soar through the skies overlooking the lake.

  3. Wine Tasting: Wine tastings are available in various vineyards and wineries throughout the region.

Week-Long Itinerary for Lecco

Here’s a week-long itinerary to help plan your trip to Lecco:

Food and Drink Scene of Lecco

Must-Try Dishes

  1. Casoncelli: a local pasta dish stuffed with cheese, breadcrumbs, and meat.

  2. Polenta and Osei: A traditional dish consisting of roasted bird over polenta.

  3. Pizzoccheri della Valchiavenna: A popular dish made of buckwheat pasta, cheese, and potatoes.

Local Beverages

  1. Valtellina Rosso: This red wine is produced in the Valtellina valley.

  2. Grappa: Grappa is an alcoholic beverage made from grape pomace.

  3. Caffè lecchese: A strong coffee served in a little cup with a piece of chocolate.

Where to Stay in Lecco: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Lecco has a wide range of accommodation options catering to every budget. Here are some of the hotels, hostels, and guesthouses worth considering:

  1. NH Lecco Pontevecchio: A four-star hotel in the heart of Lecco with rooms overlooking the river.

  2. Il Giardino di Lory: A cozy guest house with a beautiful garden and outdoor terrace.

  3. Lake Como Hostel: A budget-friendly hostel located in a quiet area, offering stunning views of Lake Como.

What to Buy in Lecco: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  1. Silk Scarves: Lecco is known for its silk production, and silk scarves can be found in many shops around town.

  2. Handmade Leather Bags: Lecco is well-known for its leather craftsmanship, and handcrafted leather bags are a great souvenir.

  3. Local Cheese: Visitors can find local cheese, such as Taleggio cheese and Bitto cheese, in several markets throughout the province.

Local Culture and Customs of Lecco

Lecco is a welcoming province that values and promotes the preservation of its culture and traditions. Locals are passionate about food, art, and music. Visitors should respect the local culture and engage with it as much as possible.

Best Time to Visit Lecco

The best time to visit Lecco is during the spring and summer months, between April and September, when the temperatures are warmer and nature is at its best.

Do’s and Don’ts of Lecco

Do:

  1. Learn a few Italian phrases to communicate with locals more effectively.

  2. Respect local customs and traditions.

  3. Explore the outdoor recreational options available.

Don’t:

  1. Wear revealing clothing in religious sites.

  2. Be excessively loud in public areas.

  3. Litter, and always dispose of your garbage in designated areas.

Safety and Health

Safety in Lecco

Lecco is a safe province to visit, but visitors should take standard safety precautions such as locking valuables and being careful in crowded public areas at all times.

Health in Lecco

Visitors should make sure they have proper medical insurance before their trip and take standard precautions with regards to food and drink hygiene to avoid any health issues.

Getting to Lecco: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Lecco

The closest airports to Lecco are Milan Linate Airport (LIN), Milan Malpensa Airport (MXP), and Orio al Serio International Airport (BGY).

Bus Stations Near Lecco

Lecco is accessible by bus from various places in Lombardy. The main bus station is located near the train station in the city center.

Other Options

Visitors can also reach Lecco by train or car from Milan or other parts of Italy.

How to Get Around in Lecco

Lecco is a small town, and visitors can explore it on foot. However, visitors can hire cars, bikes, or scooters to explore the province further.

Where to Go Next from Lecco: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  1. Bergamo: A beautiful town with picturesque architecture and cobbled streets.

  2. Como: A charming town located at the southern end of Lake Como, known for its high-end fashion, designer shops, and stunning scenery.

  3. Milan: The cultural hub of the region and the fashion capital of Italy, Milan is a must-visit destination.

Conclusion

Lecco is a perfect destination for those looking to explore natural landscapes, enjoy outdoor recreational activities, and immerse themselves in the local culture. This guide provides visitors with everything they need to know to plan their perfect trip to Lecco.
