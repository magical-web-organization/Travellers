Varenna Travel Guide - A Complete Tour Guide to Varenna, Italy

Introduction to Varenna

Nestled on the eastern shore of Lake Como, Varenna is a picturesque town in the Lombardy region of Italy. It boasts small alleys, charming pastel-colored houses, and beautiful waterfront promenades that overlook the crystal clear waters of Lake Como. Varenna is a perfect destination for romantics, foodies, and history lovers.

List of Places to Visit in Varenna

Whether you’re interested in exploring the town’s history, indulging in its food scene, or taking in the natural beauty that surrounds it, Varenna has something for everyone. Here are some of the most popular places to visit in Varenna:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Villa Monastero: A 13th-century villa with beautifully manicured gardens and a museum showcasing artifacts from the villa’s heyday.
  • Castello di Vezio: An 11th-century castle perched on a hilltop overlooking Varenna.

Natural Attractions of Varenna: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Lake Como: Known for its crystal clear waters and breathtaking views, Lake Como is the perfect place to relax on the beach, go fishing, or take a scenic boat ride.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Varenna

  • Sentiero del Viandante: A scenic trail that runs along the eastern bank of Lake Como.
  • Menaggio Greenway: A beautiful trail that winds through the hillside, offering stunning views of Lake Como and surrounding mountains.

Best Things to Do in Varenna: Activities and Tours

  • Cooking Classes: Learn to make traditional Italian dishes with a local chef.
  • Wine Tours: Visit local wineries and vineyards, and sample some of the region’s best wines.

Week-Long Itinerary for Varenna

Day 1: Arrival in Varenna; explore the town’s historical sites. Day 2: Rent a boat and spend the day on Lake Como. Day 3: Take a hiking tour of the nearby hills and mountains. Day 4: Visit a local winery and indulge in wine tasting. Day 5: Take a cooking class and learn to make traditional Italian dishes. Day 6: Relax on the beach, go swimming or fishing. Day 7: Departure from Varenna.

Food and Drink Scene of Varenna

Must-Try Dishes

  • Risotto al Pesce Persico: A creamy rice dish made with local fish.
  • Polenta: A staple of Northern Italian cuisine, polenta is often served with rich meat ragù.

Local Beverages

  • Grappa: A popular Italian liquor made from distilled grapes.
  • Franciacorta: A sparkling wine from the nearby Franciacorta region.

Where to Stay in Varenna: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Hotel Royal Victoria: A stylish hotel with views of Lake Como.
  • Villa Torretta: A cozy guesthouse located in the heart of Varenna.

What to Buy in Varenna: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Silk Scarves: Varenna is known for its silk production, and you’ll find a wide variety of beautiful scarves in local shops.
  • Olive Oil: The region’s olive groves produce some of the best olive oil in Italy.

Local Culture and Customs of Varenna

Varenna is a small and friendly town where people treasure their traditions and customs. Local festivals, including the Fireworks festival, are important events in the town’s cultural calendar. It’s important to dress modestly when visiting churches, and to respect local customs.

Best Time to Visit Varenna

The best time to visit Varenna is during the spring and autumn months when the weather is mild. July and August can be hot and crowded, while the winter months can be chilly and damp.

Do’s and Don’ts of Varenna

Do:

  • Sample the local cuisine and wine.
  • Take a scenic boat ride on Lake Como.
  • Hike the surrounding hills and mountains.

Don’t:

  • Dress immodestly when visiting churches.
  • Ignore local customs and traditions.
  • Leave your valuables unattended.

Safety and Health

Safety in Varenna

Varenna is a very safe town. However, it’s important to take usual precautions against pickpockets and keep an eye on your belongings.

Health in Varenna

Italy has a good standard of healthcare, and Varenna is no exception. However, it’s always advisable to have travel insurance in case of any medical emergencies.

Getting to Varenna: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Varenna

The nearest airports to Varenna are Milan Malpensa Airport and Bergamo Orio al Serio Airport.

Bus Stations Near Varenna

Varenna-Esino railway station is served by the Lecco-Sondrio-Tirano railway, which connects with Milan’s Stazione Centrale.

Other Options

Varenna is a small town, so it’s easy to get around on foot. However, there are also local buses and taxis available for transport.

How to Get Around in Varenna

Varenna is a small town that can easily be explored on foot. The town’s waterfront promenades are ideal for a leisurely stroll, while the surrounding hills and mountains can be accessed via hiking trails or a bike rental service.

Where to Go Next from Varenna: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Bellagio: A beautiful town on the western shore of Lake Como.
  • Milan: The fashion capital of Italy and a hub of culture and history.

Conclusion

Varenna is a charming town on the shores of Lake Como, offering a perfect blend of natural beauty, cultural heritage, and culinary delights. With its picturesque alleys, beautiful scenery, and range of activities, it’s an ideal destination for travelers looking for an authentic Italian experience. So, if you’re planning a trip to Italy, make sure you put Varenna on your itinerary!
