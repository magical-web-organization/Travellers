Introduction to Como

Located in Lombardy, Northwest Italy, Como is a breathtakingly beautiful city on the shores of Lake Como. It’s a popular tourist destination for its gorgeous landscapes, rich history, and vibrant culture. In this guide, you’ll discover the top attractions, outdoor activities, delicious food and drink, where to stay, and how to get around.

List of Places to Visit in Como

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

The city of Como is rich in history and culture, and there are plenty of landmarks to explore. One of the most famous ones is the Como Cathedral, built in the 14th century, which boasts a stunning Gothic-Renaissance architecture. Another must-see is the Tempio Voltiano, a museum dedicated to the Italian scientist Alessandro Volta, inventor of the electric battery.

For art lovers, the Pinacoteca Civica, a 17th-century palace showcasing Italian art from the Middle Ages to the 20th century, is a must-visit. You can also visit the Broletto, an ancient palace that now houses the city’s administrative offices and judiciary rooms.

Natural Attractions of Como: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Lake Como itself is arguably the most stunning attraction in the area, where visitors can enjoy boat trips on the lake and picturesque hikes along the surrounding mountains. Beyond that, the stunning Villa Carlotta is a must-visit, located in Tremezzina. Here you can take a stroll through the Italian gardens and admire the stunning villa and its paintings.

The funicular to Brunate, a village located on a hill overlooking the city of Como, offers scenic views of the lake and the city itself. There is also an abundance of bike and hiking routes in the area, with trails ranging in difficulty.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Como

Given its breathtaking landscapes and hilly surroundings, hiking and biking in and around Como are very popular. Some of the best-known routes include the Sentiero del Viandante, which takes hikers along the eastern shore of the lake, and the Greenway del Lago di Como which passes through ancient settlements, villas, and gardens.

There are also bike and e-bike rentals available in Como to help you explore the area further.

Best Things to Do in Como: Activities and Tours

In addition to exploring the city’s historical landmarks and natural wonders, there are plenty of activities and tours to choose from. For instance, you can try your hand at water sports such as kayaking and windsurfing or opt for a guided boat tour of the lake.

For thrill-seekers, bungee jumping is available in nearby Val di Mello, or you can try paragliding or take a tandem skydive.

Week-Long Itinerary for Como

Here’s a week-long itinerary to help you get the most out of your visit to Como:

Day 1: Explore the city of Como, including the main square, the cathedral, Broletto, and Tempio Voltiano.

Day 2: Take a boat tour of the lake and visit the fishing village of Bellagio.

Day 3: Go hiking along one of the trails surrounding the lake, such as Sentiero del Viandante, and visit the Villa Carlotta.

Day 4: Spend a day in Milan, located about an hour away from Como. Visit the famous Milan Cathedral, the Sforza Castle, and indulge in some shopping.

Day 5: Visit the ancient Romanesque church of San Fedele and enjoy some shopping and dining on the beautiful streets of Como.

Day 6: Take a cable car to the Brunate hill village and enjoy the scenic views. Consider activities such as paragliding, zip-lining, or a guided bike tour.

Day 7: Spend the day relaxing on the beach at Lido di Cadenabbia or head to Val di Mello for some thrill-seeking bungee jumping.

Food and Drink Scene of Como

No trip to Como would be complete without experiencing its gastronomic scene. The city is known for its delicious seafood dishes, including traditional risotto and pickled sardines.

Must-Try Dishes

Other popular dishes include the famous “cassoeula,” a pork rib and sausage stew, “minestrone,” a vegetable soup made with seasonal produce, and “polenta,” a starchy maize dish that can be served with various toppings.

Local Beverages

Some of the iconic beverages include “grappa,” a typical Italian brandy made from grape pomace, “Barolo,” a red wine produced in the Piedmont region, and “Campari,” a vibrant red bitter aperitif.

Where to Stay in Como: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Como offers a wide range of accommodation options, from luxurious villas to budget-friendly hostels. For a luxurious experience, the Grand Hotel Tremezzo, located in Tremezzina, is a popular choice, with its stunning views and extensive amenities. Other options include the Hotel Villa Flori, located right by the lake, and the Palace Hotel, a classic and elegant hotel located in the city center.

There are also plenty of budget-friendly options, such as the Hostel Como and the B&B Hotel Como.

What to Buy in Como: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Como is known for its high-quality silk production, so be sure to pick up some silk scarves or ties as souvenirs. You can also purchase local cheeses, chocolates, and wines.

Local Culture and Customs of Como

The city of Como is steeped in history and culture, and residents take great pride in their local traditions. Visitors should note that it is customary to greet locals with a simple “buongiorno” and say “ciao” when leaving, and it is considered polite to eat everything on your plate and use bread to soak up the sauce.

Best Time to Visit Como

The best time to visit Como is in the summer months, when the weather is warm and sunny. However, this is also the busiest time of year, so visitors should expect larger crowds and higher prices. Spring and early fall can be good alternatives, with lower prices and fewer tourists.

Do’s and Don’ts of Como

Do:

  • Dress modestly, particularly when visiting churches or other religious sites.
  • Be considerate of locals, especially when dining in authentic Italian restaurants.
  • Try the local cuisine and beverages.
  • Explore the city on foot, taking in the stunning architecture and surroundings.

Don’t:

  • Leave your belongings unattended.
  • Ignore traffic signals when crossing the street.
  • Be loud or disrespectful in public areas, particularly at night.
  • Tip in restaurants, as it’s already included in the bill.

Safety and Health

Safety in Como

Como’s crime rate is relatively low, but visitors should still take precautions. Avoid unlit areas at night, and be aware of potential pickpockets in crowded areas. If you rent a car, ensure that it is parked in a secure area and that valuables are not left inside.

Health in Como

Como is generally a safe and healthy destination. However, visitors should take precautions against mosquito-borne illnesses in the summer months by wearing insect repellent and covering up exposed skin.

Getting to Como: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Como

The nearest major airport to Como is Milan Malpensa Airport, about 40 km away. There is also a smaller airport, the Lugano airport, located in Switzerland, about 40 minutes from Como.

Bus Stations Near Como

Como is well-connected by bus, with two main bus stations, Piazza Matteotti and Piazzale Lario.

Other Options

Como can also be reached by train from Milan, with multiple train stations throughout the city.

How to Get Around in Como

The best way to explore Como is on foot, as the city is relatively small and walkable. However, public transportation is available, including buses, taxis, and ferries. There are also bike and scooter rentals available.

Where to Go Next from Como: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

There are plenty of destinations and excursions to consider after a visit to Como. Milano, located an hour away by train, offers visitors more historical landmarks, world-renowned fashion, and delicious Italian cuisine. The Swiss cities of Lugano and Locarno are also a good option for a day trip, located only a few kilometers from the Italian-Swiss border.

Conclusion

Visiting Como is a once-in-a-lifetime experience that allows travelers to dive deep into Italy’s rich history, culture, and natural landscapes. Whether you’re exploring the city’s historical landmarks, hiking along the scenic trails surrounding the lake, indulging in delicious Italian cuisine, Como offers endless opportunities to create unforgettable memories.
