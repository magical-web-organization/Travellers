Introduction to Nesso

Nestled on the eastern shore of Lake Como, in the Lombardy region of Northwest Italy, Nesso is a quaint village with stunning natural scenery, rich history, and charming local culture. Its picturesque location, surrounded by mountains and forests, attracts many visitors who come to enjoy outdoor activities, explore the historic landmarks and museums, and taste the local cuisine.

List of Places to Visit in Nesso

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Nesso has several landmarks and sites of cultural significance to explore, including:

  • The Nesso Bridge: dating back to the Roman period, this ancient stone bridge is one of the most iconic symbols of Nesso. It spans the gorge that divides the village in two, offering spectacular views of the Nesso waterfall and Lake Como.
  • The Church of Santi Pietro e Paolo: this 11th-century church features beautiful frescoes and artworks by local artists.
  • The Olive Press Museum: located in an old olive mill, this museum exhibits the traditional process of making olive oil and the history of olive cultivation in the region.
  • Nesso Art Gallery: showcasing contemporary local art, from paintings to sculptures, the gallery offers a glimpse into the vibrant art scene of Nesso.

Natural Attractions of Nesso: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Nesso is surrounded by stunning natural landscapes that provide opportunities for hiking, biking, and water sports, including:

  • The Nesso Waterfall: cascading from a height of 100 meters, this majestic waterfall is one of the most photographed spots in Nesso and a popular picnic and swimming area in the summer.
  • Mount San Primo: offering breathtaking views of the lake and the Alps, this mountain is a challenging hiking destination for experienced trekkers.
  • Lake Como: with its crystal-clear waters and picturesque shores, Lake Como is a paradise for water sports enthusiasts. Visitors can rent boats, kayaks, or stand-up paddleboards to explore the lake or swim in its refreshing waters.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Nesso

Nesso has several hiking and biking trails that offer stunning views of the lake and the surrounding mountains, including:

  • The Strada Regia: a historic trail that connects Nesso to the neighboring villages of Como and Bellagio, passing through scenic forests, hills, and ancient settlements.
  • The Sentiero del Viandante: following the eastern shore of the lake, this 45 km trail offers breathtaking views of the lake and mountains, passing through quaint villages, olive groves, and vineyards.

Best Things to Do in Nesso: Activities and Tours

In addition to hiking and biking, Nesso offers a variety of activities and tours that cater to different interests, including:

  • Cooking classes: learn how to make traditional Italian dishes, such as pasta, risotto, and tiramisu, with local chefs.
  • Wine tours: explore the vineyards and wineries of the nearby regions of Valtellina and Franciacorta, taste the local wine and learn about the winemaking process.
  • Water sports: rent a boat, kayak or paddleboard, and explore the lake on your own or with a guide.
  • Hot air balloon rides: experience the picturesque landscapes of Nesso and Lake Como from a bird’s eye view.

Week-Long Itinerary for Nesso

Day 1: Visit the Nesso Bridge and Waterfall, and take a boat tour of the lake. Day 2: Explore the Church of Santi Pietro e Paolo and the Olive Press Museum, and take a cooking class. Day 3: Hike or bike the Strada Regia trail to Como or Bellagio. Day 4: Visit the Nesso Art Gallery and take a wine tour in Valtellina. Day 5: Swim and sunbathe at the Lido di Bellagio, or take a hot air balloon ride. Day 6: Visit the nearby towns of Torno and Moltrasio, or take a day trip to Milan. Day 7: Relax at a local spa, or enjoy a sunset cruise on the lake.

Food and Drink Scene of Nesso

Must-Try Dishes

Nesso has a rich culinary tradition, with many local dishes that feature fresh seafood, seasonal vegetables, and homemade pasta. Some of the must-try dishes in Nesso include:

  • Risotto al pesce persico: a delicious risotto with perch, a freshwater fish that is abundant in Lake Como.
  • Pizzoccheri: a hearty dish made with buckwheat pasta, potatoes, and cheese, typical of the Valtellina region.
  • Fiorentina steak: a thick-cut T-bone steak, grilled to perfection, a classic dish of Tuscan cuisine.

Local Beverages

Nesso is also famous for its local wine and spirits, including:

  • Prosecco: a sparkling wine that is popular all over Italy and the world.
  • Grappa: a strong liquor made from grape pomace, a byproduct of winemaking.
  • Amaro: a bitter liqueur made with herbs and spices, usually served as a digestif.

Where to Stay in Nesso: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Nesso has several accommodation options, ranging from small B&Bs and guesthouses to luxurious hotels and villas. Some of the best places to stay in Nesso include:

  • Il Nido: a beautiful guesthouse with stunning views of the lake and the mountains, located in a tranquil spot near the Nesso waterfall.
  • Agriturismo La Over: a charming farmhouse that offers comfortable rooms and apartments, surrounded by olive groves and orchards.
  • Villa Castiglioni: a luxurious villa with a private pool and garden, perfect for a romantic getaway or a family vacation.

What to Buy in Nesso: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Nesso is famous for its handmade crafts and artisanal products, including:

  • Silk scarves and ties: Como is known for its silk production, and many shops in Nesso sell beautiful silk scarves and ties, printed with intricate designs.
  • Olive oil: Nesso is surrounded by olive groves that produce high-quality olive oil, which makes a perfect souvenir or gift.
  • Pottery: many local artists and craftsmen create beautiful ceramic objects, such as plates, bowls, and vases, inspired by the natural landscapes and traditions of Nesso.

Local Culture and Customs of Nesso

Nesso has preserved its local culture and traditions, which date back to Roman times and the Middle Ages. Some of the most important customs in Nesso include:

  • Festa del Torchio: a festival that celebrates the olive harvest, usually held in November, with food, music, and dances.
  • Palio del Baradello: a historical reenactment of a medieval joust, held in Como every September.
  • Patron saint festivals: each village in the region has its patron saint, and their feast day is celebrated with processions, parades, and fireworks.

Best Time to Visit Nesso

The best time to visit Nesso is during the spring and autumn, when the weather is mild, and the crowds are smaller. However, the peak season is from June to August when the temperatures are higher, and many festivals and events take place. Keep in mind that Nesso is a popular tourist destination, so it can get crowded, especially on weekends and holidays.

Do’s and Don’ts of Nesso

Do:

  • Respect local customs and traditions.
  • Dress appropriately when visiting religious sites.
  • Try the local dishes and drinks.
  • Explore the nature and outdoor activities.
  • Be polite and friendly to the locals.

Don’t:

  • Litter or damage the natural environment.
  • Drink and drive.
  • Hike or bike off the designated trails.
  • Swim in unsupervised areas.
  • Disturb the wildlife.

Safety and Health

Safety in Nesso

Nesso is a safe destination for travelers, with low crime rates and efficient emergency services. However, visitors should be aware of some safety measures, such as:

  • Follow the standard precautions for outdoor activities, such as hiking, biking, and water sports.
  • Keep your belongings secure and under supervision.
  • Avoid areas that seem unsafe or poorly lit.

Health in Nesso

Nesso has a good healthcare system, with several public and private hospitals and clinics, but visitors should take some precautions to stay healthy, such as:

  • Drink plenty of water, especially in hot weather.
  • Apply sunscreen and wear protective clothing when outdoors.
  • Follow the standard guidelines for food and water safety.
  • Bring your own medication and health insurance.

Getting to Nesso: Transportation Options and Logistics

Nesso is located about 50 km from Milan, and getting there is relatively easy, thanks to the efficient transportation system in the region.

Airports Near Nesso

The closest airports to Nesso are:

  • Milan Linate Airport: about 60 km from Nesso, well connected by public transportation and car rental services.
  • Milan Malpensa Airport: about 70 km from Nesso, with direct bus and train connections to Como and Milan.

Bus Stations Near Nesso

There are several bus stations near Nesso, including:

  • Como Nord Lago: the main bus station in Como, with direct connections to Nesso and other nearby villages.
  • Bellagio: a picturesque town on the other side of the lake, with frequent bus and ferry connections to Nesso.

Other Options

Visitors can also reach Nesso by car, train, or taxi, depending on their preferences and budget.

How to Get Around in Nesso

Getting around in Nesso is relatively easy, as the village is small and walkable. Visitors can also use public transportation, such as buses and ferries, to reach nearby destinations or explore the lake. Taxis and car rental services are also available for those who prefer more flexibility and convenience.

Where to Go Next from Nesso: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Nesso is a perfect base for exploring the Lake Como region and its many attractions, including:

  • Como: a charming town on the southwestern shore of the lake, famous for its historic center, elegant architecture, and shopping.
  • Bellagio: known as the “Pearl of Lake Como,” Bellagio is a picturesque village with colorful houses, narrow streets, and stunning views of the lake.
  • Villa del Balbianello: a beautiful villa on a promontory overlooking the lake, with lush gardens and a stunning panoramic terrace.

Conclusion

Nesso is a hidden gem in the Lake Como region, offering a perfect blend of natural beauty, history, and culture. From hiking and biking to tasting local dishes and wines, Nesso has something for everyone. Plan your trip carefully, follow the local customs and practices, and enjoy the unforgettable experience of visiting this charming village near Lake Como.
