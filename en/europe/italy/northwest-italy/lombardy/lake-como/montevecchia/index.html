Montevecchia, located in the beautiful province of Lecco in Lake Como, Lombardy, Italy, is a hidden gem that brims with natural beauty, historical landmarks, and breathtaking scenic views. The medieval village, nestled amidst scenic hills, offers outdoor enthusiasts an ideal destination for hiking, biking, and trekking, while history buffs can explore the numerous museums and historical landmarks scattered throughout the town. The vibrant food & drink scene is not to be missed either; enjoy locally produced wines and tantalizing dishes prepared with the freshest ingredients. Here is everything you need to know about planning your trip to Montevecchia, Italy.

Introduction to Montevecchia

Montevecchia, which translates to “old mountain,” is a traditional village that dates back to the Middle Ages. It is an ideal destination for those looking to experience the tranquility of an authentic Italian village with picturesque landscapes. Montevecchia is a popular area for wine production, with vineyards established on the surrounding hillsides.

List of Places to Visit in Montevecchia

Montevecchia has several places of interest that ensure tourists stay entertained:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Church of San Alessandro: A beautiful church located in the center of Montevecchia featuring an 18th-century Baroque altar and trompe l’oeil frescoes.
  • Casa del Fascio: A historical villa from the Fascist era, now used as a cultural center, exhibiting artwork and hosting cultural events.
  • Museum of Labor and Rural Life: An informative museum preserving the agricultural and rural life of the Lecco region.
  • Palazzo Arese Borromeo: A breathtaking 17th-century villa surrounded by a luscious park and displaying an impressive collection of artwork.

Natural Attractions of Montevecchia: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Montevecchia Regional Park: A beautiful park with miles of hiking trails and biking paths ideal for outdoor enthusiasts. It offers mesmerizing views of the surrounding countryside and is home to many species of wildflowers and wildlife.
  • Monte Tronco: A scenic viewpoint located next to the park, offering panoramic views of the surrounding areas.
  • Green Way: A biking route that runs for 10 km and passes through the lush greenery of Montevecchia, connecting it with other parts of Lombardy.
  • Wildlife Oasis: A nature reserve that boasts several hiking trails and is a habitat for many species of animals, including foxes, hedgehogs, and deer.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Montevecchia

Montevecchia is one of the favorite destinations of outdoor enthusiasts, with several hiking/biking routes. Some of the best ones are:

  • The Montevecchia Loop: It’s a loop trail that runs about 8.4 km and takes about 2 hours to complete. It offers mesmerizing views of the Lecco region and is suitable for all skill levels.
  • Colle di Brianza Loop: A popular 10.3 km loop trail that takes about 3 hours to complete and features a lake.
  • Monte Barro Trail: A challenging trail that runs for about 18.2 km and takes around 6 hours to complete. It passes through a nature reserve and is ideal for experienced hikers.

Best Things to Do in Montevecchia: Activities and Tours

With several cultural landmarks to visit and outdoor adventures awaiting, Montevecchia has something for every kind of traveler. Here are some of the must-do activities and tours in Montevecchia:

  • Wine tour: Embark on a tour of the region’s vineyards and indulge in some wine-tasting sessions.
  • Cultural tour: Take a guided tour of the numerous historical landmarks such as Palazzo Arese Borromeo and Church of San Alessandro.
  • Hiking trail: Hike through Montevecchia’s many scenic hiking trails to get spectacular views of the region’s natural beauty.
  • Bike tour: Rent a bike and explore the region’s scenic bike trails, which provide you with an up-close experience of Montevecchia’s natural beauty.

Food and Drink Scene of Montevecchia

Montevecchia’s food and wine scene is one of the town’s most significant attractions. It’s famous for its locally produced wine and a range of traditional dishes prepared with fresh and locally sourced ingredients.

Must-Try Dishes

  • Risotto alla Parmigiana: A creamy and rich rice dish with a hint of flavors from saffron and parmesan.
  • Pizzoccheri della Valtellina: A hearty pasta dish with buckwheat noodles, cabbage, garlic, and sage.
  • Ossobuco: A flavorful veal shank dish cooked with vegetables and broth.

Local Beverages

  • Merlino: A liqueur made with the essence of cherries, herbs, and water.
  • Grappa: A clear alcohol made from the pomace of grapes.
  • Franciacorta: A sparkling wine made from grapes grown in the Franciacorta region and bottled using the traditional champenoise method.

Where to Stay in Montevecchia: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Montevecchia offers a range of accommodation options that cater to all kinds of travelers. Here are some of the best places to stay:

  • Hotel De Charme L’Antico: A charming hotel in the heart of Montevecchia, offering stunning views of the region and comfortable rooms.
  • B&B Il Torchio: A cozy bed & breakfast with beautiful gardens and terraces offering panoramic views of the surrounding mountains.
  • Casa Amati Country House: A family-friendly guesthouse with spacious rooms and a refreshing outdoor pool.

What to Buy in Montevecchia: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Montevecchia has several charming stores and shops that sell locally produced products perfect for souvenir shopping. Some of the must-buy items include:

  • Montevecchia wine: The region’s vineyards produce some of the best wine, which are available for purchase locally.
  • Handmade chocolates: Montevecchia has several distinctive chocolate shops selling handmade chocolates with unique flavors.
  • Local cheeses: The region is well-known for producing locally made cheese, which is delicious and aromatic.

Local Culture and Customs of Montevecchia

Montevecchia’s local culture and customs are a reflection of Italy’s rich heritage, tradition, and history. The people of Montevecchia are known for their warm and welcoming nature, and they take pride in their local customs and traditions.

Best Time to Visit Montevecchia

The best time to visit Montevecchia is during the spring or summer months when the weather is pleasant, and the region’s natural beauty is in full bloom. The town hosts many festivals and events during this time, providing visitors with a glimpse into the region’s rich cultural heritage.

Do’s and Don’ts of Montevecchia

Do:

  • Respect the local customs and traditions.
  • Enjoy the outdoors responsibly.
  • Try the local cuisine and wine.
  • Carry sunscreen and water while exploring the natural attractions.

Don’t:

  • Pick flowers or plants while exploring the region.
  • Litter or damage the natural beauty of the region.
  • Wear inappropriate clothing when visiting churches and other historical landmarks.

Safety and Health

Safety in Montevecchia

Montevecchia is a safe destination for tourists. However, it’s always advisable to take the necessary precautions while exploring the outdoors, such as hiking and biking trails.

Health in Montevecchia

Montevecchia has several pharmacies and medical facilities in case of emergencies. It’s also recommended to carry a medical kit while exploring the natural attractions of the region.

Getting to Montevecchia: Transportation Options and Logistics

Montevecchia is well-connected to other parts of Italy and Europe. Here are some transportation options available:

Airports Near Montevecchia

  • Milan Linate Airport (LIN): The airport is the closest airport to Montevecchia and is about an hour’s drive away.
  • Milan Malpensa Airport (MXP): The airport is about an hour and a half drive from Montevecchia.

Bus Stations Near Montevecchia

  • Merate Bus Station: The station is about 20 minutes’ drive from Montevecchia and has regular bus connections to other parts of Italy.

Other Options

  • Train: The nearest train station is located in the town of Calco, a 15-minute drive from Montevecchia.
  • Car: Montevecchia is accessible by car, and several rental car agencies operate from Milan Linate Airport.

How to Get Around in Montevecchia

Montevecchia is a small town, and it’s best explored on foot. Bikes are also available for rent, providing visitors with an eco-friendly way to explore the region’s natural beauty.

Where to Go Next from Montevecchia: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Conclusion

Montevecchia is a beautiful destination that offers a wide array of natural attractions, historical landmarks, delicious cuisine, and wine. It’s the perfect getaway for those looking to escape the bustling city and immerse themselves in the tranquility of an authentic Italian village. With this comprehensive travel guide, you’re well-equipped to plan your trip to Montevecchia, Italy, and experience all the town has to offer.
