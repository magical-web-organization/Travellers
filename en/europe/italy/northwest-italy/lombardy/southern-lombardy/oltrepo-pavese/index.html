Introduction to Oltrepò Pavese

Located in Southern Lombardy, Oltrepò Pavese is a hidden gem in Italy. This stunning region boasts rolling hills, picturesque vineyards, and rich history. Oltrepò Pavese translates to “beyond the Po River” and is known for its connection to wine production, as well as its scenic views of the Lombardy countryside. Whether you’re a wine connoisseur or just looking for a peaceful escape from city life, Oltrepò Pavese has something for everyone.

List of Places to Visit in Oltrepò Pavese

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Oltrepò Pavese is a hub for history and culture. There are several museums and historical sites that give you a peek into the region’s rich heritage. Here are some notable attractions to add to your itinerary:

  • Casteggio Archaeological Museum: This museum houses stunning ancient artifacts and remains of a Roman settlement.
  • Rocca d’Olgisio: A medieval castle that towers over the lush countryside and offers breathtaking panoramic views.
  • The Abbey of Santa Maria di Rivalta: A Romanesque abbey with stunning frescoes and elaborate religious artwork.

Natural Attractions of Oltrepò Pavese: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Aside from its cultural offerings, Oltrepò Pavese is also known for its natural beauty and outdoor recreation. Whether you prefer hiking, biking, or merely soaking in the stunning views of the evergreen hills, there’s no shortage of options. Some must-visit outdoor attractions include:

  • Monte Penice: This is the highest peak in the region and offers an impressive vista of Lombardy and Apennine Mountains.
  • The Borbera Valley: This is a stunning natural reserve with flowing rivers, enchanting waterfalls, and lush greenery.
  • Casa degli Alberi Fossili: A prime location for fossil hunting, this hill features remarkable trees made of rock that are over 200 million years old.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Oltrepò Pavese

For hikers and bikers alike, Oltrepò Pavese offers several exciting routes that provide an exhilarating experience:

  • Alta Via dei Monti Liguri: This route offers relatively the best views of the region, and you get to see hilltops, valleys, and meadows, with the Ligurian Alps in the background.
  • The Via del Sale: This trail is ideal for bikers, and it follows a path that was frequently used to transport salt to the coast.
  • Via Francigena: This trail takes you on a pilgrimage going back to ages. Strarting from Canterbury, England, the trail leads up to Rome and along the way the trail passes Oltrepò Pavese, with fascinating religious and historic sites to explore.

Best Things to Do in Oltrepò Pavese: Activities and Tours

Some activities and tours shouldn’t be missed at Oltrepò Pavese:

  • Winery tours: Oltrepò Pavese is known for its wine production, with over 60 wineries in the area. Tour the vineyards, taste the local wine varieties, and learn about the winemaking process.
  • Cooking classes: Here, cooking enthusiasts can up their game by taking traditional Italian cooking lessons.
  • Horse riding: Enjoy the magnificent scenery on horseback. Riding tours are available for all levels of experience and ability.

Week-Long Itinerary for Oltrepò Pavese

Five days are a perfect amount of time to get a full taste of what Oltrepò Pavese has to offer. Here’s a sample itinerary for a week-long trip:

  • Day 1: Explore Casteggio Archaeological Museum and the nearby vineyards and sample the area’s local wine.
  • Day 2: Take a hike, bike or ride on Montespinato trail and to Rocca d’Olgisio for stunning panoramic views, and stop for lunch at one of the region’s charming quaint towns.
  • Day 3: Venture to Ciavaglia Landscapes’s park for trekking, swimming, and a picnic lunch.
  • Day 4: Guided day tour to the Santa Maria di Rivalta Abbey and head to the village of Varzi, for more wine tasting.
  • Day 5: Visit Volpedo, the home of the legendary painter, Pellizza Da Volpedo then head to Pieve Dell’Isola for a more relaxed activity like fishing or wine tasting.

Food and Drink Scene of Oltrepò Pavese

As mentioned earlier, Oltrepò Pavese is widely known for its wine, but the culinary landscape is equally impressive. With delicious, locally-sourced ingredients and a plethora of regional specialties, your taste buds are in for a treat.

Must-Try Dishes

Whether you’re looking for a filling meal or a quick snack, Oltrepò Pavese has plenty of dishes to tickle your fancy. Here are some must-try dishes to add to your foodie checklist:

  • Ravioli di Zucca: These are pumpkin-filled ravioli and one of the region’s most popular dishes.
  • Bresaola: A cured beef specialty that is thinly sliced and served with grated Parmesan cheese and rocket salad.
  • Salami & Mortadella: This region is also known for its impasto salami and pork-based mortadella.

Local Beverages

When it comes to beverages, there’s no question what Oltrepò Pavese is known for. Here are some local drink choices worth a sip:

  • Bonarda: One of the region’s most popular red wines. It’s deliciously light-bodied and packed with fruity notes.
  • Barbera: A rich, full-bodied red wine that pairs perfectly with a variety of Italian dishes.
  • Moscato: A sweet white wine with rich floral notes, perfect for sipping with dessert.

Where to Stay in Oltrepò Pavese: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

When it comes to accommodations, Oltrepò Pavese doesn’t disappoint. From luxurious hotels to charming guesthouses, you’re sure to find something that suits your style and budget.

Some popular choices include:

  • Agriturismo La Costa: This charming farmhouse offers an authentic Italian experience with stunning views of the hillside.
  • Hotel Il Castello: This is a historic hotel housed in a medieval castle. It’s located in the heart of the region, making it a convenient base for exploring Oltrepò Pavese.
  • OltrePo Pavese Hostel: This hostel provides comfortable, affordable accommodation in the region’s charming towns

What to Buy in Oltrepò Pavese: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Oltrepò Pavese’s local food and beverage scene are not to be missed. Here are some must-buy local products to bring back home:

  • Wine bottles: You can’t leave without a bottle of Bonarda, Barbera, or Moscato.
  • Cheese: Oltrepò Pavese is known for its artisanal cheese. So, be sure to grab a sampling of a few different varieties.
  • Honey, Jams, and Preserves: Small-batch jars of sweet treats make excellent gifts to bring back home.

Local Culture and Customs of Oltrepò Pavese

The people of Oltrepò Pavese are friendly and welcoming, and they hold their regional history and culture in high regard. The locals prize their traditional way of life and actively seek to preserve the region’s heritage.

Best Time to Visit Oltrepò Pavese

The weather in Oltrepò Pavese is mild, making it accessible during any season. However, the best time to visit is from late spring to early fall. The weather is comfortable during these months, and the countryside is at its most scenic.

Do’s and Don’ts of Oltrepò Pavese

Do:

  • Try at least three wine varietals in the area
  • Take a guided tour to learn about the famous historic sites and join some of the wonderful outdoor explorations.
  • Shop for souvenir treats, including wine, cheese, and olive oil in markets and local shops.

Don’t:

  • Overindulge in the local wines.
  • Dress too casually when visiting religious sites or participating in a significant cultural event.
  • Forget to greet locals with a friendly “Buongiorno!” whenever possible.

Safety and Health

Safety in Oltrepò Pavese

Oltrepò Pavese is a safe area with helpful residents. However, some things to note for your safety:

  • Beware of snakes or ticks when hiking in long grass.
  • Always store your belongings securely and be careful of petty theft in other crowded tourist locations.

Health in Oltrepò Pavese

With Italy’s universal healthcare system, medical care is easily accessible. If you need medical attention, head to the nearest hospital or medical center where the care is high and reputable.

Getting to Oltrepò Pavese: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Oltrepò Pavese

Milan’s two international airports - Malpensa and Linate – are the closest to Oltrepò Pavese. From there, you can take public transportation or rent a car to get to the region.

Bus Stations Near Oltrepò Pavese

There are frequent bus services operated by the state-owned transport company that goes to Milan, Pavia and along the maim highways. These buses are affordable and easy to use.

Other Options

If you’re coming from other parts of Italy, renting a car is the easiest and most convenient way to reach Oltrepò Pavese.

How to Get Around in Oltrepò Pavese

While there is public transportation available in Oltrepò Pavese, the most convenient way to get around is by car. Many scenic routes are inaccessible by bus, and having a car will allow you more freedom to explore the area.

Where to Go Next from Oltrepò Pavese: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Oltrepò Pavese has unique and picturesque sequences that will make you fall in love with the region. However, if you’re looking to explore further, consider visiting:

  • Liguria: A seaside region known for its colorful buildings, rich culture and extensive natural beauty.
  • Turin: Known for its museums, architecture and sporting scene, Turin is an exciting destination for travelers.

Conclusion

Whether you’re seeking adventure, relaxation, culture or stunning landscapes, Oltrepò Pavese is the perfect destination. Plan your trip and discover this hidden gem of Italy.
