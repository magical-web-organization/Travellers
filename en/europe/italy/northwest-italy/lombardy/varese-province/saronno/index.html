Saronno, located in the Varese province of Lombardy in Northwest Italy, is a charming destination that offers a mix of culture, history, and nature. From museums and historical landmarks to scenic trails and outdoor activities, Saronno has something for everyone. In this comprehensive travel guide, we’ll cover everything you need to know to plan the perfect trip to Saronno.

Introduction to Saronno

Saronno is a picturesque city of around 40,000 people, situated just 20 kilometers northwest of Milan. It’s known for its rich history and cultural landmarks, as well as for its delicious almond-flavored liqueurs, including the famous Amaretto di Saronno. The city celebrates its heritage by hosting a variety of festivals and events throughout the year, making it an exciting place to visit at any time.

List of Places to Visit in Saronno

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

One of the top cultural attractions in Saronno is the Chiesa di Santa Maria degli Angeli, a beautiful church that dates back to the Renaissance era. The church has impressive frescoes and artwork, and visitors can also see the tombs of important historical figures, including the renowned composer Antonio Vivaldi.

For art enthusiasts, a visit to the Pinacoteca di Saronno is a must. This museum houses an impressive collection of artwork from the 15th century to contemporary pieces. Among the notable artists showcased are Giovanni Bellini, Donato Bramante, and Giampietrino.

Another iconic landmark is the Villa Gianetti, which was built in the 18th century by Luigi Gianetti. This palace has been restored and is now home to a variety of cultural institutions and events, including art exhibitions, concerts, and conferences.

Natural Attractions of Saronno: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

For those seeking outdoor adventures and scenic views, Saronno has plenty to offer. One popular attraction is the Parco delle Groane, a regional park that covers over 11,000 hectares of stunning nature. Visitors can hike, bike, or simply relax and enjoy the beauty of the park, which is home to a variety of wildlife.

Another popular outdoor destination is the Lago di Como, one of the most beautiful lakes in Italy. Located just a short drive from Saronno, the lake has many picturesque towns and villages on its shores, including Bellagio, Menaggio, and Varenna. Visitors can take a boat tour, swim, or enjoy a picnic with a view.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Saronno

Saronno has several scenic routes for hiking and biking, which are perfect for anyone who wants to explore the countryside and take in the fresh air. One popular route is the Strada dei Cappuccini, which is a scenic path that leads through the groane forest to the charming sanctuary of the Cappuccini.

Another great option is the Pista Ciclabile Valmorea, a bike path that runs along the river that separates Italy from Switzerland. The path is relatively flat, making it an easy ride, and the scenery is breathtaking.

Best Things to Do in Saronno: Activities and Tours

In addition to the attractions listed above, Saronno has several activities and tours that visitors can take to make the most of their trip. One of the most popular is the Amaretto di Saronno factory tour, where visitors can learn about the history and production of this famous liqueur and even taste some samples.

Another recommended activity is a cooking class, where visitors can learn to prepare traditional Italian dishes and take home new cooking skills. Classes can be found at various locations in Saronno and are a fun way to experience the local culture.

Week-Long Itinerary for Saronno

If you’re planning to visit Saronno for a week, here’s a suggested itinerary that will help you make the most of your time:

Day 1: Visit the Chiesa di Santa Maria degli Angeli and explore the city center. Day 2: Take a bike ride on the Pista Ciclabile Valmorea. Day 3: Visit the Pinacoteca di Saronno and Villa Gianetti. Day 4: Go on a tour of the Amaretto di Saronno factory. Day 5: Hike the Strada dei Cappuccini and have a picnic. Day 6: Take a day trip to the Lago di Como. Day 7: Take a cooking class or explore the Parco delle Groane.

Food and Drink Scene of Saronno

When it comes to food and drink, Saronno has plenty to offer. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages to taste during your visit.

Must-Try Dishes

One of the most iconic dishes in Saronno is risotto con lo Zafferano, which is a creamy risotto made with saffron. Another popular dish is ossobuco, which is a slow-cooked veal shank served with saffron risotto or polenta.

For dessert, try Panettone, a sweet bread made with dried fruit and nuts. Panettone is a traditional Christmas treat in Italy but is available year-round in Saronno.

Local Beverages

The most recognizable local beverage of Saronno is, of course, the Amaretto di Saronno, a sweet almond-flavored liqueur that has been enjoyed for centuries. Other local wines to try include Nebbiolo and Bonarda.

Where to Stay in Saronno: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Saronno has a variety of accommodation options, from luxury hotels to budget-friendly guesthouses. Some top-rated hotels include the Hotel Cyrano, the Starhotels Grand Milan, and the Albergo Dei Laghi.

For those on a budget, the Bed and Breakfast Sogni d’Oro and the La Bottega del Falegname hostel are great options.

What to Buy in Saronno: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

If you’re looking for souvenirs or gifts to bring home, Saronno has plenty of local products to choose from. The most obvious choice is Amaretto di Saronno, which can be found in most stores throughout the city.

Another popular option is handmade pottery from the local artisans, which can be found at the weekly market on Wednesdays.

Local Culture and Customs of Saronno

Saronno is a welcoming city with a rich cultural heritage. Some customs to be aware of include greeting people with a handshake or a kiss on the cheek, respecting religious sites and traditions, and dressing modestly when visiting churches.

Best Time to Visit Saronno

The best time to visit Saronno is from April to June and September to November. During these months, the weather is mild, and the crowds are smaller. July and August can be hot and crowded, while December to March is the low season due to the cold weather.

Do’s and Don’ts of Saronno

Do:

  • Try the Amaretto di Saronno
  • Respect the local customs and traditions
  • Dress appropriately when visiting churches
  • Take a bike tour of the city

Don’t:

  • Wear revealing clothing when visiting churches or religious sites
  • Be loud or disrespectful in public places
  • Overpack, as many hotels have limited storage space

Safety and Health

Safety in Saronno

Saronno is a safe city, but visitors should still take precautions to protect themselves and their possessions. Be aware of pickpockets in crowded areas, and avoid leaving valuables unattended.

Health in Saronno

Saronno has good medical facilities and services, and visitors should have no trouble finding medical assistance if needed. Make sure to bring any necessary medication with you, and have a copy of your prescription and medical information on hand.

Getting to Saronno: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Saronno

The closest airport to Saronno is the Milan Malpensa airport, which is approximately 25 kilometers away. Other nearby airports include the Milan Linate airport and the Bergamo Orio al Serio airport.

Bus Stations Near Saronno

Saronno has a bus station located in the city center, which provides connections to various destinations throughout Italy.

Other Options

Train travel is a convenient way to reach Saronno, as it is located on the Milan-Como connection and has regular train schedules from Milan’s main station, Milano Centrale.

How to Get Around in Saronno

Saronno is a relatively small city and can easily be explored on foot or by bike. Taxis are also available for those who prefer not to walk or bike, and there is a local bus service, which is a great option for those who want to explore the surrounding areas.

Where to Go Next from Saronno: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Saronno is conveniently located near several other popular destinations, making it a great base for further exploration. Some nearby cities to visit include:

  • Milan
  • Como
  • Varese
  • Bergamo

Conclusion

Saronno is a hidden gem that offers something for everyone. With a rich cultural heritage, stunning natural scenery, and delicious food and drink, it’s a must-visit destination in Northern Italy. Use this guide to plan your trip, and discover everything this charming city has to offer.
