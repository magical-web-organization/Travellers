Introduction to Varese

Located in Lombardy, Northwestern Italy, Varese is a charming city that is perfect for a quiet getaway. Nestled between the Italian and Swiss Alps, Varese boasts stunning natural scenery, rich culture, and history. It is also known for its beautiful villas, gardens, and parks, making it an ideal destination for nature lovers. This guide will help you plan the ultimate trip to Varese, with tips on the best things to do, see, eat, and where to stay.

List of Places to Visit in Varese

Varese is a city with plenty of attractions and things to do. Here are some top places you shouldn’t miss:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Villa Ponti

Built-in the mid-19th century, Villa Ponti is one of the most beautiful villas in Varese. The villa houses the Civic Art Museum, with a collection of paintings, sculptures, and decorative arts.

Castello di Masnago

Built-in the 13th century, Castello di Masnago has been transformed into a museum of modern and contemporary art. It also boasts a beautiful park and gardens.

Sacro Monte di Varese

A UNESCO World Heritage Site, Sacro Monte di Varese is a pilgrimage site with fourteen chapels depicting the life of Christ. The chapels are surrounded by beautiful landscape and offer stunning views of the Alps.

Natural Attractions of Varese: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Campo dei Fiori Regional Park

Located on the slopes of Campo dei Fiori mountain, this beautiful park offers stunning views of the surrounding mountains and valleys. It is perfect for hiking and trekking.

Lake Maggiore

Located just a few kilometers from Varese, Lake Maggiore is one of the largest lakes in Italy. It offers stunning views, and you can take a boat tour to the Borromean Islands.

Varese Lake

Varese lake is a small glacial lake located at the foothills of Campo dei Fiori. The lake offers plenty of outdoor activities, like hiking, fishing, and swimming.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Varese

Varese is a paradise for hikers and bikers, with numerous trails and routes that cater to everyone from beginners to experts. Here are some of the best:

  • Sentiero 1: The trail circles Lake Varese, offering beautiful views of the lake and the surrounding hills.
  • Monte Martica: The mountain offers a challenging climb for experienced hikers and bikers. However, the stunning views from the top of the mountain are worth the effort.
  • Parco Regionale Campo dei Fiori: This park is a haven for hikers, with numerous trails that offer stunning views of the surrounding mountains.

Best Things to Do in Varese: Activities and Tours

Varese offers plenty of activities and tours for visitors. Here are some of the best:

  • Art and Culture Tour: This tour takes you through some of the most important cultural landmarks in the city.
  • Food Tour: Sample some of Varese’s local cuisine and delicacies on this walking tour.
  • Bike Tour: Explore the beautiful countryside around Varese on a guided bike tour.

Week-Long Itinerary for Varese

Here is a sample itinerary for spending a week in Varese:

  • Day 1: Visit Villa Ponti and the Civic Art Museum.
  • Day 2: Take a hike in Parco Regionale Campo dei Fiori.
  • Day 3: Visit Castello di Masnago and enjoy the modern and contemporary art on display.
  • Day 4: Take a trip to Lake Maggiore and the Borromean Islands.
  • Day 5: Explore the beautiful Sacro Monte di Varese.
  • Day 6: Take a food and wine tour around Varese.
  • Day 7: Biking and exploring the countryside around Varese.

Food and Drink Scene of Varese

Varese has a rich culinary heritage, and you’ll find plenty of delicious food to try while you’re in the city.

Must-Try Dishes

  • Risotto alla Milanese: A traditional rice dish flavored with saffron.
  • Polenta: A cornmeal dish that is a staple of Northern Italian cuisine.
  • Cotoletta alla Milanese: Breaded and fried veal cutlet, served with roasted potatoes.

Local Beverages

  • Varesino: A sparkling wine made from the Moscato grape.
  • Grappa: A strong, aromatic brandy made from grape pomace.

Where to Stay in Varese: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Varese has plenty of accommodation options to cater to all budgets and preferences. Here are some of the best:

  • Villa Porro Pirelli: A luxury hotel set in a beautiful villa, surrounded by lush gardens.
  • Hotel Di Varese: A budget-friendly hotel located in the heart of Varese.
  • Ostello Casa Rossa: A cozy hostel with a relaxed atmosphere.

What to Buy in Varese: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Porcelain: Varese is known for its exquisite porcelain, which you can find in boutiques and specialty shops in the city.
  • Textiles: Varese is also famous for its high-quality textiles and silk products, which you can purchase in local markets and shops.
  • Honey: Varese is home to numerous beekeepers who produce some of the best honey in Italy.

Local Culture and Customs of Varese

Varesian culture is rich and diverse, with influences from both Italy and Switzerland. The locals are proud of their heritage, and you’ll find plenty of festivals and celebrations throughout the year.

Best Time to Visit Varese

The best time to visit Varese is during the summer months, from June to September, when the weather is warm and sunny. However, the city is beautiful in all seasons, so you can plan your visit according to your preferences.

Do’s and Don’ts of Varese

Do:

  • Learn some basic Italian phrases before you go.
  • Respect the local customs and etiquette.
  • Try the local cuisine and delicacies.

Don’t:

  • Wear revealing clothing in religious sites.
  • Be loud and disruptive in public places.
  • Leave valuables unattended.

Safety and Health

Safety in Varese

Varese is a safe city with low crime rates. However, you should always take basic precautions, such as keeping an eye on your belongings and avoiding unlit and isolated areas at night.

Health in Varese

Italy has a well-developed healthcare system, and Varese has good medical facilities. However, it is recommended that you have travel health insurance before you go.

Getting to Varese: Transportation Options and Logistics

Varese is easily accessible by both air and land.

Airports Near Varese

  • Milan Malpensa Airport: Located about 30 kilometers from Varese.
  • Milan Linate Airport: Located about 50 kilometers from Varese.

Bus Stations Near Varese

  • Varese Bus Station: Located in the city center, with connections to several cities in Italy and Switzerland.

Other Options

  • Train: Varese has a train station with connections to several cities in Italy and Switzerland.

How to Get Around in Varese

Varese is a small city that is easy to navigate on foot or by bike. However, if you need to travel further afield, there are several buses and taxis available.

Where to Go Next from Varese: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Varese has plenty of nearby attractions that are worth visiting, including:

  • Milan: Known as Italy’s fashion and design capital, Milan is located less than an hour away from Varese.
  • Lake Como: One of the most beautiful lakes in Italy, Lake Como is located about an hour away from Varese.
  • Lugano, Switzerland: Located about an hour away from Varese, Lugano is a beautiful Swiss city that is perfect for a day trip.

Conclusion

Varese is a hidden gem in Northern Italy, with plenty of attractions and activities for visitors. From hiking and biking to exploring the city’s rich cultural heritage, there’s something for everyone in Varese. With this comprehensive guide, you’re all set to plan an unforgettable trip to this beautiful city in Lombardy, Italy.
