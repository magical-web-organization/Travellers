Introduction to Varese (Province)

Located in the Lombardy region of Northwest Italy, Varese (Province) is a beautiful area that boasts breathtaking natural landscapes, rich history and culture, and delicious food. The province is home to numerous stunning lakes, mountains, and forests, making it a popular destination for outdoor enthusiasts. Varese (Province) also features many museums, historical sites, and churches, making it a great spot for history buffs. In this comprehensive tour guide, we’ll explore all that Varese (Province) has to offer, giving you everything you need to plan a memorable trip.

List of Places to Visit in Varese (Province)

With so much to see and do in Varese (Province), it can be hard to know where to start. Here are some of the top places to visit when exploring the province:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Sacromonte di Varese: An important pilgrimage site, this impressive complex features over 1,000 chapels and grottoes built into the cliffside. Visitors can explore the winding pathways to marvel at the intricate and colorful depictions of religious scenes.
  • Villa Panza: Located in the beautiful town of Varese, this neoclassical villa is now a museum that features contemporary art. The villa houses the Panza Collection, one of the foremost collections of American Minimalist art in Europe.
  • Castello Visconteo: Situated in the heart of the town of Locarno, this castle dates back to the 13th century and is one of the most impressive castles in the region. Visitors can explore the castle and its beautiful gardens.

Natural Attractions of Varese (Province): Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Lake Maggiore: This stunning lake straddles the Swiss-Italian border and offers scenic views, boat trips, water sports, and charming lakefront towns to explore.
  • Monte Rosa: This majestic mountain range is one of the highest peaks in the Alps, and is a popular spot for skiing, snowboarding, and hiking.
  • Parco Regionale Campo dei Fiori: Perfect for nature lovers, this regional park boasts beautiful forests, wildlife, and numerous hiking trails.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Varese (Province)

For the outdoor enthusiasts, Varese (Province) offers plenty of scenic hiking and biking routes. Some of the most popular options include:

  • Sentiero del Viandante: This footpath follows the eastern shore of Lake Como and offers stunning views of the lake and the surrounding mountains.
  • Monte Campo dei Fiori: This mountain features several hiking trails that offer breathtaking views of the province and the Swiss Alps.
  • Ciclovia del Lago di Varese: This bike path circles around Lake Varese and encompasses scenic towns, cultural sites, and nature reserves.

Best Things to Do in Varese (Province): Activities and Tours

  • Wine Tasting Tour: Take a tour of the area’s vineyards with local guides and learn about the wine-making process while sampling some of the region’s best wines.
  • Boat Tour of Lake Como: Explore the stunning Lake Como by boat with an expert local guide who can provide insight into the area’s history and culture.
  • Hot Air Balloon Ride: Take in breathtaking views of the region from high above with a hot air balloon ride.

Week-Long Itinerary for Varese (Province)

Day 1: Explore the town of Varese, including Villa Panza and the Basilica of San Vittore

Day 2: Hike Monte Campo dei Fiori and enjoy the views

Day 3: Visit the Castello Visconteo and the town of Locarno, Switzerland

Day 4: Take a bike ride along Ciclovia del Lago di Varese and visit the museum of Villa Menafoglio Litta Panza

Day 5: Take a day trip to Lake Como and enjoy a boat tour and scenic lunch on the lake

Day 6: Relax and enjoy the natural beauty of Lake Lugano

Day 7: Explore the Sacromonte di Varese and enjoy a wine tasting tour

Food and Drink Scene of Varese (Province)

Must-Try Dishes

  • Risotto alla Milanese: A classic Italian dish made with rich, saffron-infused risotto and parmesan cheese.
  • Cotoletta alla Milanese: A delicious breaded and fried veal chop that is a specialty of Milan and the Lombardy region.
  • Panettone: A traditional sweet bread loaf that is typically eaten during the Christmas season.

Local Beverages

  • Franciacorta: A sparkling wine that is produced in the Lombardy region and is comparable in taste and quality to Champagne.
  • Grappa: A strong, clear brandy made from distilled grape skins and other leftovers from the wine-making process.
  • Campari: A popular Italian aperitif that is typically served before meals and is known for its bright red color and unique bitter flavor.

Where to Stay in Varese (Province): Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Albergo Del Sole: A charming hotel located in the town of Varese that features a gourmet restaurant and beautiful, tranquil gardens.
  • Hotel Berna: A modern hotel located near the train station in Varese, this hotel offers comfortable rooms and a complimentary breakfast.
  • Le Pinete: A cozy and intimate guesthouse located in the hills of Sacro Monte di Varese, offering stunning views of the valley below.

What to Buy in Varese (Province): Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Peperoni di Carmagnola: These small red peppers are a popular specialty of the region and are often used in cooking.
  • Silk Products: Varese (Province) is known for its silk production, and visitors can find a variety of beautiful scarves, ties, and other silk products.
  • Handmade Jewelry: Many local artisans create unique and beautiful jewelry pieces using traditional techniques and locally sourced materials.

Local Culture and Customs of Varese (Province)

  • Respect for religious sites: many of the sites in Varese (Province) are religious and visitors should dress modestly and act respectfully.
  • The concept of aperitivo: aperitivo is a pre-dinner drink and snack that is enjoyed in the early evening as a way to socialize with friends and family.

Best Time to Visit Varese (Province)

The best time to visit Varese (Province) is during the spring and fall months, when the weather is mild and the crowds are thinner. July and August can be very hot, and many locals leave for vacation during this time, which may limit the availability of certain activities and attractions.

Do’s and Don’ts of Varese (Province)

Do:

  • Take advantage of the ample hiking and biking opportunities in the area.
  • Sample the local wines and cuisine.
  • Respect the cultural and religious sites in the region.

Don’t:

  • Wear revealing clothing in religious sites.
  • Expect everything to be open and available during the summer months.

Safety and Health

Safety in Varese (Province)

Overall, Varese (Province) is a safe place to visit. However, visitors should take standard precautions against pickpocketing and other petty crimes in crowded areas.

Health in Varese (Province)

Visitors should ensure that they are up to date on all routine vaccinations before visiting Varese (Province). Healthcare facilities are generally good, but visitors should be aware that medical treatment can be expensive.

Getting to Varese (Province): Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Varese (Province)

  • Milan Malpensa Airport: Located approximately 19 miles from Varese, this is the nearest major airport, offering international and domestic flights.
  • Lugano Airport: Located in Switzerland, about 25 miles from Varese, Lugano Airport offers domestic and international flights.

Bus Stations Near Varese (Province)

  • Varese Bus Station: Located in the town center, the bus station offers connections to many other towns in the region.
  • Malpensa Shuttle: This shuttle service offers regular connections between Milan Malpensa Airport and Varese.

Other Options

  • Train: Varese is connected to Milan and other major cities via train.
  • Car: Visitors can also rent a car to explore the area independently.

How to Get Around in Varese (Province)

Varese (Province) is well-connected by public transport, with regular bus and train services. Visitors can also rent a car to explore the region independently. Bikes are also a great way to get around, especially for exploring the countryside and smaller towns.

Where to Go Next from Varese (Province): Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Milan: One of Italy’s most vibrant cities, Milan is renowned for its fashion, art, and architecture.
  • Lake Maggiore: This stunning lake straddles the Swiss-Italian border and offers more natural beauty and charming towns to explore.
  • Verona: Located a few hours’ drive from Varese (Province), Verona is home to many stunning cultural and historical sites, including the famous arena, a Roman amphitheater that still hosts concerts and operas today.

Conclusion

From amazing natural beauty to rich culture and history, Varese (Province) has so much to offer visitors. With this comprehensive tour guide in hand, you are sure to make the most out of your trip to this beautiful part of Italy.
