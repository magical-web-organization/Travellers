Introduction to Monte Isola

Monte Isola is a charming island located in the middle of Lake Iseo, in the Lombardian Alps and Prealps of Lombardy, Northwest Italy. The island is known for its picturesque villages, ancient traditions, high-quality fish dishes, and beautiful natural surroundings. Monte Isola is also famous for the Christo’s Floating Piers installation, which attracted thousands of visitors from around the world in 2016.

List of Places to Visit in Monte Isola

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Museo della Comunità di Peschiera Maraglio: A museum dedicated to preserving the cultural heritage of Peschiera Maraglio, one of the oldest fishing villages on Monte Isola.
  • Chiesa di San Michele: A beautiful church dating back to the 15th century, located in the heart of the village of Carzano. The church houses some impressive works of art, including wooden statues and frescoes.
  • Santuario della Madonna della Ceriola: A sanctuary perched on top of a hill overlooking Monte Isola and Lake Iseo. The sanctuary is accessible by foot or by cable car and offers spectacular panoramic views.

Natural Attractions of Monte Isola: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Passeggiata della Madonna: A scenic path that runs along the eastern coast of Monte Isola, offering panoramic views of the lake and the surrounding mountains.
  • Monte Isola Trekking Trail: A 9 km hiking trail that circumnavigates the island, passing through several villages and scenic spots.
  • Sasso Remenno: A natural rock formation featuring huge boulders, which is great for climbing and hiking. The site also offers beautiful views of the lake.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Monte Isola

Monte Isola is a paradise for hikers and bikers, offering several trails of various difficulties. Some of the most popular routes include:

  • Sentiero del Priore: A 6 km path that links the villages of Siviano and Cure, passing through ancient vineyards, olive groves, and historic buildings.
  • Strada Panoramica: A 10 km scenic road that runs along the western coast of Monte Isola, offering breathtaking views of the lake and the surrounding mountains.
  • Monte Isola Trekking Trail: A 9 km hiking trail that circumnavigates the island, passing through several villages and scenic spots.

Best Things to Do in Monte Isola: Activities and Tours

  • Go fishing: Monte Isola is known for its high-quality fish dishes, and visitors can experience the local fishing culture by joining a guided fishing tour.
  • Take a boat tour: Explore the lake and its surroundings by taking a boat tour, which offers fantastic views of the island and its picturesque villages.
  • Visit the Floating Piers: Don’t miss the opportunity to visit the site of Christo’s Floating Piers installation, which attracted thousands of visitors from around the world in 2016.

Week-Long Itinerary for Monte Isola

Day 1: Arrive in Monte Isola, and explore the village of Peschiera Maraglio and the Museo della Comunità. Day 2: Hike along the Sentiero del Priore and enjoy a picnic lunch overlooking the lake. Day 3: Take a boat tour around the island and visit some of the other villages, such as Carzano and Siviano. Day 4: Visit the Santuario della Madonna della Ceriola, and take the cable car to the top of the hill for stunning panoramic views. Day 5: Rent a bike and explore the Strada Panoramica, stopping at scenic spots along the way. Day 6: Go fishing with a local guide and enjoy a traditional fish lunch. Day 7: Visit the Floating Piers installation and say goodbye to Monte Isola.

Food and Drink Scene of Monte Isola

Must-Try Dishes

Monte Isola is famous for its freshwater fish dishes, especially sardines, trout, and pike. Some of the must-try dishes include:

  • Risotto di Tinca: A creamy risotto made with local tench fish, onions, and white wine.
  • Missoltini: Salted and dried sardines, which are a local specialty.
  • Brasaola: A cured beef meat, thinly sliced and usually served as antipasti.

Local Beverages

  • Franciacorta wine: A high-quality sparkling wine produced in the area surrounding Lake Iseo.
  • Grappa: A strong spirit made from distilled grape skins and stems.
  • Caffè corretto: An espresso shot served with a small glass of grappa.

Where to Stay in Monte Isola: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Monte Isola has several accommodation options, ranging from hotels to hostels to guesthouses. Some of the best options include:

  • Hotel Ristorante Vittoria: A charming hotel located in the heart of Peschiera Maraglio, offering comfortable rooms and a restaurant serving local dishes.
  • Hostel Monte Isola: A budget-friendly option located in the village of Menzino, offering dormitory rooms and private rooms.
  • B&B La Ceriola: A guesthouse located near the Santuario della Madonna della Ceriola, offering comfortable rooms and a garden with lake views.

What to Buy in Monte Isola: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Olive oil: Monte Isola is famous for its high-quality olive oil, which can be bought in local shops and markets.
  • Handmade pottery: The village of Carzano is known for its traditional ceramics, which are made and painted by hand.
  • Lavender products: The island has several lavender farms, and visitors can buy handmade soaps, lotions, and essential oils made with local lavender.

Local Culture and Customs of Monte Isola

Monte Isola has a rich cultural heritage, which is deeply rooted in the local fishing traditions. Some of the customs and festivals worth mentioning include:

  • Festa di San Michele: A festival held in honor of the patron saint of Monte Isola, featuring a procession of boats and a fireworks display.
  • Presepe di Botticino: A nativity scene made entirely of Botticino marble, which is located in the Santuario della Madonna della Ceriola and can be visited during the Christmas season.
  • Pesca di Valle: A traditional fishing technique used in the past, which involves creating a V-shaped trap with nets to catch fish.

Best Time to Visit Monte Isola

The best time to visit Monte Isola is from mid-April to mid-October, when the weather is mild and sunny. The island can get quite busy during the summer months, especially in August, when many Italians go on holiday.

Do’s and Don’ts of Monte Isola

Do:

  • Explore the island on foot or by bike to fully appreciate its natural beauty and charm.
  • Try the local fish dishes, especially sardines, trout, and pike.
  • Visit the Santuario della Madonna della Ceriola for panoramic views of the lake and the surrounding mountains.

Don’t:

  • Swim in the lake outside of designated areas, as the waters can be dangerous due to strong currents and cold temperatures.
  • Feed the wildlife, as it can be harmful to their health and the ecosystem.
  • Enter private property without permission from the owners.

Safety and Health

Safety in Monte Isola

Monte Isola is a relatively safe destination, with low crime rates and traffic. However, visitors should take the usual precautions to protect themselves and their belongings, such as keeping valuables out of sight and locking doors and windows.

Health in Monte Isola

Monte Isola has a few medical facilities, including a pharmacy and a small clinic. For emergencies, visitors can go to the hospital in nearby Brescia, which is about an hour’s drive from the island.

Getting to Monte Isola: Transportation Options and Logistics

Monte Isola is accessible by ferry from several towns around Lake Iseo, including Iseo, Sale Marasino, and Sulzano. There are also scheduled boat tours from other towns such as Sarnico. Visitors can park their cars in the designated parking area in Sulzano or Iseo, and take the ferry to the island.

Airports Near Monte Isola

The nearest airports to Monte Isola are Orio al Serio International Airport (BGY) and Milan Linate Airport (LIN), both of which are about an hour and a half’s drive from the island.

Bus Stations Near Monte Isola

The nearest bus stations to Monte Isola are in Iseo and Sulzano, which are both connected to major cities in the region.

Other Options

Visitors can also rent a car or a bike, or hire a taxi or a private transfer to reach Monte Isola.

How to Get Around in Monte Isola

The best way to get around Monte Isola is by foot or bike, as the island is car-free except for service vehicles. Visitors can also take the local bus, which runs along the eastern coast of the island and connects the villages of Peschiera Maraglio and Siviano.

Where to Go Next from Monte Isola: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Lake Garda: A stunning lake located about an hour and a half’s drive from Monte Isola, offering picturesque villages, beautiful landscapes, and plenty of outdoor activities.
  • Brescia: A historic city located about 40 minutes’ drive from Monte Isola, famous for its museums, galleries, and Roman ruins.
  • Franciacorta: A region located south of Lake Iseo, known for its high-quality wines and vineyards.

Conclusion

Monte Isola is a charming and picturesque destination in Italy, offering a perfect blend of natural beauty, cultural heritage, and gastronomic traditions. Whether you’re a hiker, a foodie, or a history buff, Monte Isola has something to offer for everyone, and it’s definitely worth a visit.
