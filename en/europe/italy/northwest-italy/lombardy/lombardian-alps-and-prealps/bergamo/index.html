Introduction to Bergamo

Located at the foothills of the Alps, in the Lombardy region of Italy, Bergamo is a historic city with plenty of culture, food, and outdoor adventures. This charming city is divided into two distinct areas- the old city (Citta Alta) and the modern city (Citta Bassa). The old city is perched atop a hill, and is surrounded by ancient walls that date back to the 16th century. It is home to some of the most iconic and culturally significant landmarks in Italy, such as the Palazzo della Ragione, the Basilica of Santa Maria Maggiore, and the Rocca di Bergamo. In contrast, the modern city of Citta Bassa is bustling with shopping streets, restaurants, bars and a lively atmosphere.

List of Places to Visit in Bergamo

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Accademia Carrara – One of the major art museums in Italy, featuring an extensive collection of paintings and sculptures by masters such as Botticelli, Canaletto, Raphael and many more.
  • Cappella Colleoni – An ornate, Renaissance mausoleum, which is located just outside the entrance to the Basilica di Santa Maria Maggiore. It is known for its exquisite decorations and vibrant colors.
  • Gamabate Art Museum – A modern museum that showcases contemporary art and design pieces from around the world.
  • Piazza Vecchia – Located in the heart of the old city, this beautiful square is surrounded by historical buildings such as the Palazzo della Ragione and the Torre dei Caduti. It is the perfect place for a relaxing stroll and to admire the remarkable architecture of the old town.

Natural Attractions of Bergamo: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Parco dei Colli di Bergamo – A natural park covering over 4000 hectares of hills and countryside, providing breathtaking views and hiking and nature trails.
  • Adda River – The perfect location for kayaking and rafting trips, this river stretches across Bergamo and the surrounding region.
  • San Vigilio Castle – Located on a hill that offers unparalleled views of the surrounding landscape, this castle is perfect for a scenic hike or a peaceful picnic.
  • Bergamo Alps – These beautiful alpine mountains offer opportunities for hiking, rock climbing, skiing, and breathtaking scenery.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Bergamo

  • Sentiero del Castagneto – A 14-km trail that starts from the Valbrembo village and features a variety of landscapes, including vineyards, woods, and historic sites.
  • The Orobie Trails – A network of various trails and routes that criss-cross the Orobie Mountains, offering stunning scenery and challenging hikes.
  • Bike Paths – Bergamo offers 120 km of bike-friendly paths that connect different neighborhoods and attractions, making it easy to explore the city on two wheels.

Best Things to Do in Bergamo: Activities and Tours

  • Food and Wine Tasting Tours – The Lombardy region is renowned for its cuisine, and Bergamo is no exception. A food or wine tasting tour is a great way to explore the culinary delights of the region, and visit wineries and farms.
  • Segway Tours – A fun and unique way to explore the city is by Segway, which provides a faster and easier way to cover the city’s attractions while enjoying the fresh air.
  • Art and Cultural Tours – Guided tours offer a comprehensive experience of the city’s rich cultural heritage, providing insights into the fascinating history and places of significance, that shaped this beautiful city.

Food and Drink Scene of Bergamo

Must-Try Dishes

  • Casoncelli – A type of pasta dumpling ,stuffed with meat and vegetables, in a butter and sage sauce.
  • Polenta – A staple of the Lombardy region, Polenta is made from cornmeal and often served with cheese or stews.

Local Beverages

  • Moscato di Scanzo – A rich dessert wine, made from the Moscato grape variety that is grown in the Scanzo Valley.
  • Birra Artigianale Bergamo – Bergamo has a thriving craft-beer scene, and it is the perfect beverage to accompany the city’s delicious cuisine.

Where to Stay in Bergamo: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Bergamo has a wide range of accommodations to choose from. From luxury hotels to budget-friendly hostels and home stays, there is something for everyone. Some popular options are:

  • Relais San Lorenzo – A high-end boutique hotel that provides superb service and luxury accommodations.
  • B&B Santa Lucia – Nestled in the old city, this charming guesthouse is housed in a historic building and is perfect for a romantic getaway.
  • Bergamo Hostel – Located in the heart of the modern city, this hostel offers budget-friendly accommodations and attracts people of all ages and backgrounds.

What to Buy in Bergamo: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Strachitunt – A soft, handmade cheese made from local cattle milk, it is a must-buy for cheese lovers.
  • Bergamo Candles – Made from natural beeswax, these candles are available in various sizes and shapes and are often decorated with lace or flowers.
  • Scarpette di Santa Lucia - Adorable ceramic baby shoes that make excellent gifts for friends and family members.

Local Culture and Customs of Bergamo

Bergamo is a relatively conservative city in terms of dress and behavior. It is customary to dress modestly while visiting churches or other places of worship. Smoking is prohibited in most public places, and littering is also heavily penalized. Engaging in conversations about local culture, food, and history with locals is highly recommended and appreciated.

Best Time to Visit Bergamo

The best time to visit Bergamo is between April and June or September to November when the weather is pleasant, and there are relatively less tourists. The summer months of July and August can be hot and crowded due to the peak tourist season.

Do’s and Don’ts of Bergamo

Do:

  • Carry a light jacket or sweater as the hilltops can be chilly, even in summer.
  • Purchase tickets to attractions in advance to avoid long queues.
  • Respect the city’s cultural and historical heritage by not littering or misusing public spaces.

Don’t:

  • Wear shorts or sleeveless tops while visiting churches or other places of worship.
  • Speak loudly or make unnecessary noises in public places.
  • Attempt to climb or damage ancient walls or monuments.

Safety and Health

Safety in Bergamo

Bergamo is a relatively safe city, but it is always advised to take standard precautions such as keeping your belongings close to you, and being aware of your surroundings, especially at night.

Health in Bergamo

The healthcare system in Italy is world-class, and Bergamo has multiple health facilities, including private clinics and hospitals. In case of any medical emergency, dial the European emergency number “112” for immediate assistance.

Getting to Bergamo: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Bergamo

Orio al Serio International Airport (BGY) is located just 5 km from the city center and is well-connected to major cities across Europe.

Bus Stations Near Bergamo

Bergamo Bus Station is a major transportation center, with buses connecting Bergamo to cities across Italy and Europe.

Other Options

Trains are an excellent option for traveling to Bergamo from other parts of Italy. The city is well-connected via railway lines to major Italian cities such as Milan, Verona, and Venice.

How to Get Around in Bergamo

Bergamo has an excellent public transportation system, including buses, trams, and trains. Purchase a Bergamo Card for discounted transportations and museum entry fees.

Where to Go Next from Bergamo: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Milan: Just an hour away by train, this fashion capital of Italy is a must-visit destination for art lovers and fashion enthusiasts.
  • Lake Como: Located an hour away, this scenic lake is widely recognized for its beauty and tranquility. Take a boat tour of the lake to get the full experience.
  • Brescia: Only an hour’s drive away, this historic city is famous for its Roman ruins and cobbled streets.

Conclusion

Bergamo is a must-visit destination in Italy, rich in culture, history, and extraordinary cuisine. With its charming narrow streets and iconic architecture, Bergamo offers a fantastic blend of old-world charm and modernity. Explore the city’s historic sites, museums, and monuments, savoring the local food and wine, and immersing yourself in the city’s rich cultural heritage. From hiking and biking to shopping and nightlife, Bergamo has something for everyone and will leave you with priceless memories.
