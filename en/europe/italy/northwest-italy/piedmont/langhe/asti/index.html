Asti, located in Langhe, Piedmont, is a picturesque town famous for its wine and stunning countryside scenery. If you are planning a trip to Italy and looking for a unique travel experience, Asti is a must-visit destination. This comprehensive guide will help you discover the best things to do and places to visit in Asti, Piedmont.

Introduction to Asti

Asti is a charming town located in the Piedmont region of Italy. It is known for its rich history, beautiful architecture, and, most notably, its wine. Asti is also home to many cultural attractions, including museums, historical buildings, and even a medieval tower. The town is surrounded by rolling hills and lush vineyards, making it a perfect destination for nature lovers.

List of Places to Visit in Asti

Asti offers a variety of places to visit, including culturally significant attractions, natural wonders, and hiking and biking routes. Here is a list of some of the must-visit places in Asti:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Asti Cathedral: This stunning church in the heart of Asti is a prime example of Gothic architecture and is known for its stunning paintings and sculptures.
  • Museo Civico: This museum is dedicated to the history and culture of Asti and includes exhibits on the town’s role in the Middle Ages and Renaissance.
  • Torre Troyana: Also known as the Tower of Troy, this medieval tower offers stunning views of Asti and serves as a symbol of the town’s rich history.
  • Palazzo Mazzetti: This museum showcases art and artifacts from the region’s history and features a beautiful Italian garden.

Natural Attractions of Asti: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Langhe landscape: The rolling hills and vineyards of Langhe, surrounding Asti, are breathtakingly beautiful. This is one of the most popular natural attractions in the area, and visitors can enjoy hiking, biking, and wine tours.
  • Po River: The Po River is the longest river in Italy, and it runs through Asti. Visitors can go kayaking or hire a boat to enjoy a relaxing day on the water.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Asti

Asti and its surrounding hills and vineyards offer a range of hiking and biking trails. Here are some of the best routes for outdoor enthusiasts:

  • Asti Surrounding Hills Bike Route: This is a 50 km circular route that begins and ends in Asti.
  • Barbera Wine Vineyards Hiking Trail: This 6.5 km hike offers stunning views of the vineyards and Asti’s hills.
  • Monferrato Hills Hiking Trail: This 7 km hike offers a challenging climb to a hilltop village and stunning views of the surrounding countryside.

Best Things to Do in Asti: Activities and Tours

Asti offers a range of activities and tours to suit every taste. Here are some of the best things to do in Asti:

  • Wine tours: Asti is famous for its wine, and visitors can enjoy wine tours of the region’s vineyards.
  • Cooking classes: Visitors can learn to cook traditional Piedmontese dishes using locally sourced ingredients.
  • Hot Air Balloon Rides: Experience the beauty of the Langhe hills and vineyards from a different perspective.

Week-Long Itinerary for Asti

If you plan to spend a week in Asti, you can follow this itinerary to make the most of your time:

Day Activity
Day 1 Visit the historic center of Asti, including the Cathedral and Palazzo Mazzetti
Day 2 Take a wine tour of the nearby vineyards
Day 3 Hike the Barbera Wine Vineyards Hiking Trail
Day 4 Take a cooking class and learn to cook traditional Piedmontese dishes
Day 5 Visit the museums of Asti, including Museo Civico and Torre Troyana
Day 6 Go kayaking on the Po River
Day 7 Enjoy a hot air balloon ride over the Langhe hills and vineyards

Food and Drink Scene of Asti

Asti’s food and drink scene is as rich as its history and culture. Visitors can enjoy traditional Piedmontese dishes made with locally sourced ingredients and paired with some of the region’s best wines.

Must-Try Dishes

  • Aglio e Olio: A simple but delicious pasta dish made with garlic, olive oil, and red pepper flakes.
  • Tajarin: Thin and delicate egg noodles served with a meat ragu.
  • Bollito Misto: A hearty stew made with beef, chicken, and vegetables.

Local Beverages

  • Asti Spumante: A sweet sparkling wine made from Moscato grapes.
  • Barbera: A red wine made from Barbera grapes, typically paired with meat dishes.

Where to Stay in Asti: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Asti offers a range of accommodation options to suit every budget. Here are some of the best places to stay in Asti:

  • Hotel Ristorante Reale: A luxury hotel in the heart of Asti, offering elegant rooms and a Michelin-starred restaurant.
  • Cascina Bella Vista: A cozy guesthouse surrounded by vineyards, offering stunning views of the surrounding hills.
  • Ostello di Asti: A budget-friendly hostel located in the historic center of Asti.

What to Buy in Asti: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Visitors to Asti can bring home a range of souvenirs and local products, including:

  • Wine: Asti is famous for its wine, and visitors can bring home bottles of Asti Spumante or Barbera.
  • Truffles: Piedmont is known for its truffles, and visitors can purchase fresh truffles or truffle-based products.
  • Chocolate: Asti is home to a variety of artisanal chocolate shops, offering delicious treats to bring home.

Local Culture and Customs of Asti

Asti is a town rich in history and culture, and visitors should respect local customs and traditions. Here are some things to keep in mind:

  • Dress Code: When visiting churches or other religious sites, visitors should dress modestly.
  • Respectful Behavior: Visitors should respect local customs and cultural practices.
  • Language: While English is spoken in many places, visitors should make an attempt to speak Italian.

Best Time to Visit Asti

The best time to visit Asti is during the spring and fall months when the weather is mild, and the crowds are smaller. However, Asti is a year-round destination, and visitors can enjoy different activities and events throughout the year.

Do’s and Don’ts of Asti

Here are some do’s and don’ts for visitors to Asti:

Do:

  • Try the wine: Asti is known for its wine, and visitors should take advantage of the opportunity to taste some of the region’s best.
  • Explore the countryside: The rolling hills and vineyards of Langhe are a must-visit for nature lovers.
  • Respect local customs and traditions: Visitors should be aware of local customs and make an effort to respect them.

Don’t:

  • Expect to find fast food: Asti is known for its traditional cuisine, and visitors should be prepared to try local dishes.
  • Be too loud: Italian culture values tranquillity, and visitors should try to be mindful of their noise levels.

Safety and Health

Safety in Asti

Asti is a safe city, and visitors should not encounter any major safety concerns. However, visitors should take normal precautions to ensure their safety, such as being aware of their surroundings and not leaving valuables unattended.

Health in Asti

The healthcare system in Italy is generally excellent, and visitors should have no problems accessing medical care if needed. Visitors should ensure they have comprehensive travel insurance before arriving in Asti.

Getting to Asti: Transportation Options and Logistics

Asti is located in the Piedmont region of Italy, approximately 73 km southeast of Turin. Visitors can arrive in Asti by air, train, or car.

Airports Near Asti

  • Turin Airport: The closest airport to Asti, located approximately 70 km away.
  • Milan Malpensa Airport: Located approximately 163 km away.

Bus Stations Near Asti

  • Asti Bus Station: Located in the heart of Asti, offering connections to nearby towns and cities.

Other Options

Visitors can also arrive in Asti by car or train. The town is located on the main railway line between Turin and Alessandria.

How to Get Around in Asti

Asti is a compact town, and visitors can easily explore it on foot. However, visitors can also use public transport, including buses and taxis, to explore the surrounding areas.

Where to Go Next from Asti: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Asti is located in the Piedmont region of Italy and offers visitors a range of nearby destinations and excursions, including:

  • Turin: The capital of the Piedmont region, home to stunning architecture and vibrant culture.
  • Alba: A picturesque town known for its truffles and wine.
  • Cuneo: A town located in the heart of the Italian Alps, offering stunning scenery and outdoor adventures.

Conclusion

Asti is a town rich in history and culture, with stunning architecture, delicious cuisine, and stunning natural landscapes. Visitors to Asti can enjoy a range of activities, from hiking and biking to wine tours and hot air balloon rides. Whether you’re a foodie, a culture lover, or a nature enthusiast, Asti has something to offer everyone.
