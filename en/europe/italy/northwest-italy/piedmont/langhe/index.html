Introduction to Langhe

Langhe, located in Piedmont in Northwest Italy, is known for its stunning views of rolling hills, vineyards, and charming hilltop towns. The region has been designated as a UNESCO World Heritage Site for its rich cultural and historical significance, as well as its beautiful natural landscapes. Its unique blend of art, history, and gastronomy make it an ideal destination for travelers seeking a taste of Italian culture.

List of Places to Visit in Langhe

Langhe has no shortage of stunning attractions, both natural and man-made, for visitors to explore. Here are some must-see places to add to your travel itinerary:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Museum of the History of Barolo: Discover the history of the famous Barolo wine variety.
  • Castello di Grinzane Cavour: A historical castle that served as the home of Camillo Benso, Count of Cavour.
  • Palazzo Barolo: Former residence of the Marquis of Barolo, now home to the Regional Enoteca of Piedmont.
  • Cathedral of Alba: A beautiful cathedral dedicated to Saint Lawrence.

Natural Attractions of Langhe: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Rocche dei Sette Fratelli: A hiking trail that takes you through the rocky cliffs overlooking the Tanaro River.
  • Monte Viso: The highest peak in the Ligurian Alps, visible from Langhe.
  • Grotte di Bossea: Natural caves filled with stalactites and stalagmites.
  • Langhe Hills Vineyards: Explore the vineyards and wineries of Langhe and taste some of the finest wines in the world.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Langhe

Langhe boasts of many amazing trails for hiking and biking enthusiasts. Some of the popular trails are:

  • The Vineyard Trail: This trail wanders through vineyards, giving you a chance to see several wineries.
  • The Matese Trail: This trail allows you to discover the charming villages of the region.
  • The Alta Via dei Monti Liguri Trail: This challenging trail takes you through several mountain passes.

Best Things to Do in Langhe: Activities and Tours

Aside from hiking and biking, Langhe offers many other activities for travelers to enjoy. Here are some of the best things you can do in Langhe:

  • Wine tasting at local vineyards, such as the Cantina del Glicine or the Azienda Agricola Gian Piero Marrone.
  • Truffle hunting with a local guide to discover some of the world’s most expensive and prized fungi.
  • Hot air ballooning to appreciate the idyllic views of Langhe from above.
  • A cooking class to learn how to make delicious dishes with local ingredients such as the risotto al Barolo or the traditional bagna càuda.

Week-Long Itinerary for Langhe

If you’re planning a week-long trip, we recommend the following itinerary for the perfect Langhe experience:

Day Activity

Food and Drink Scene of Langhe

The northern Italian region of Piedmont is renowned for its culinary delights, and Langhe, in particular its region, is home to numerous world-class restaurants. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Agnolotti del Plin: A local filled pasta made with meat or vegetables.
  • Bagna Cauda: A warm dip made from garlic, anchovies and olive oil.
  • Carne Cruda Battuta al Coltello: Raw meat chopped with a knife and seasoning.

Local Beverages

  • Barolo: This robust red wine is the most famous wine of the region.
  • Dolcetto di Dogliani: A fragrant red wine with a moderate alcohol content.
  • Asti Spumante: A sparkling wine made in the Asti region.

Where to Stay in Langhe: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Langhe offers a wide range of accommodation options, depending on your budget and preferences. Here are some recommendations:

  • La Corte di Langa: A boutique hotel located in the heart of Langhe with magnificent views.
  • Palas Cerequio: A five-star hotel that offers guests an experience in the heart of the Barolo vineyards.

What to Buy in Langhe: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Langhe is rich in shops selling local handicrafts, wine accessories, and food products. Here are some unique souvenirs worth taking home:

  • Hazelnut products, such as jellies, biscuits, and chocolates.
  • Ceramics and pottery made by local artisans.
  • Wine accessories such as corkscrews and decanters.

Local Culture and Customs of Langhe

Langhe has a rich cultural heritage, including its distinct dialect, architecture, and art. Religious and agricultural festivals are also prominent in the region, with each town having its unique celebrations.

Best Time to Visit Langhe

Langhe can be visited all year round, but the best time to explore its stunning landscapes and vineyards is between May and September. The grape harvest, for the production of Barolo and other prestigious wines, typically takes place in late September and early October, making these months a great time to visit.

Do’s and Don’ts of Langhe

Do:

  • Respect the local customs and traditions.
  • Learn a few words and phrases in the local dialect.
  • Tip restaurant staff between 5-10% for excellent service.

Don’t:

  • Don’t litter or damage any of the local buildings or monuments.
  • Don’t wear beachwear in public areas.

Safety and Health

Langhe is a relatively safe area to visit. However, it’s important to be aware of the following:

Safety in Langhe

  • Valuable items should be kept in a hotel safe.
  • Be mindful of pickpockets in tourist locations and crowded markets.

Health in Langhe

  • Visitors are recommended to have comprehensive travel health insurance.
  • It’s important to be cautious with unpasteurized milk and cheese products to avoid foodborne illness.

Getting to Langhe: Transportation Options and Logistics

Langhe is accessible via several modes of transportation.

Airports Near Langhe

  • Turin Airport (TRN): 1 hour and 20-minute drive from Langhe.
  • Genoa Airport (GOA): 2-hour drive from Langhe.
  • Milan Airport (LIN): 2-hour 30-minute drive from Langhe.

Bus Stations Near Langhe

The following bus companies offer services to Langhe:

  • SAVDA Bus Company
  • GTT Bus Company

Other Options

  • You can also travel to Langhe by Train. From Turin, take the train to Alba train station, then hop on a bus to Langhe.

How to Get Around in Langhe

The most convenient way of getting around when in the Langhe region is by car. Renting a car in Langhe can be easily arranged with most car rental companies. Some hotels, such as La Corte di Langa, provide shuttle services to nearby towns and places of interest.

Where to Go Next from Langhe: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

For those looking to extend their travels, there are several nearby destinations worth exploring.

  • Turin: The former seat of Italy’s royal family, and now a bustling cultural center offers a unique mix of art, history, and cuisine.
  • Genoa: A stunning port city known for its winding narrow streets, ancient churches, and delicious seafood.
  • Milan: The fashion and commerce capital of Italy with its own fair share of art, history, and culture.

Conclusion

Langhe, Italy is a unique travel experience, and this comprehensive travel guide is your key to ensuring you make the most of your trip. Plan your itinerary well in advance, research your accommodations, and pack a lot of comfortable shoes. With breathtaking landscapes, delectable cuisine, and rich cultural heritage, Langhe is a destination that should not be missed.
