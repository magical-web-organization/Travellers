Introduction to Biella

Nestled in the heart of Piedmont, Biella is a charming town with a rich history and natural beauty. With its stunning Alpine views and tranquil atmosphere, Biella is an ideal spot for nature lovers, foodies, culture vultures, and adventure seekers alike. In this guide, we’ll give you a detailed overview of what Biella has to offer, from its top attractions and natural wonders to its local cuisine and customs. Whether you’re planning a short visit or a longer stay, our guide has everything you need to know to make the most of your time in Biella.

List of Places to Visit in Biella

If you’re wondering what to see during your stay in Biella, here’s a list of the town’s top attractions.

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Biella boasts a rich history and vibrant culture that’s reflected in its museums and historical places. Here are some of the most culturally significant sites to visit:

  • Museo del Territorio Biellese (Biellese Territory Museum): Located in the town center, this museum showcases the history of Biella and the surrounding area, from its prehistoric roots to its modern industrial age.
  • Cathedral of Biella: This imposing cathedral is the town’s main religious institution, and it’s renowned for its impressive Baroque architecture.
  • Palazzo La Marmora: Built in the 18th century, this grand palazzo is a fine example of Baroque architecture and now houses the Province’s offices and the Civic Museum of Biella.
  • Palazzo Guidobono: This 17th-century palace is home to the Quintino Sella Museum of Agricultural Technology, which showcases traditional farming equipment and techniques.
  • Villa Piazzo: This elegant 18th-century villa was once the residence of the Sella family, who were instrumental in establishing the Biellese wool industry.

Natural Attractions of Biella: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Biella’s stunning natural landscapes and outdoor activities make it a popular destination among nature lovers. Here are some of the top natural attractions and activities to try during your stay:

  • Parco della Burcina (Burcina Park): Located just outside Biella, this beautiful park offers stunning views of the Alps and is home to numerous plant species, including rhododendrons, azaleas, and magnolias.
  • Oropa Sanctuary: A magnificent Baroque complex dedicated to the Virgin Mary, this sanctuary is located high in the Alps and offers breathtaking views of the natural surroundings.
  • Ricetto di Candelo: This 13th-century fortified village has been preserved as a cultural heritage site, and it’s a popular destination for hikers and history buffs alike.
  • Lago di Viverone (Lake Viverone): This picturesque lake is a popular spot for water sports, including swimming, boating, and windsurfing.
  • Monte Mucrone: At an altitude of 1,850 meters, this mountain offers stunning views of the Alps, and it’s a popular destination for hiking and skiing.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Biella

Whether you’re a seasoned hiker or a casual cyclist, Biella offers an abundance of trails and paths to explore. Here are some of the best hiking and biking routes:

  • Alta Via del Biellese: A 5-day trek that takes you through the scenic peaks and valleys of Biella and surrounding areas.
  • The Sanctuary Trail: A 1-day hike that takes you through the woods and villages surrounding Oropa Sanctuary.
  • My Biella Bike Days: A series of cycling events that take place from March to June, with different routes and distances for every skill level.
  • Via Francigena: This ancient pilgrimage route runs through Biella and is popular among hikers and cyclists.

Best Things to Do in Biella: Activities and Tours

If you’re looking for something to do beyond the top attractions, there are still plenty of activities and tours to consider. Here are some of our top picks:

  • Food tours: Biella is home to many delicious local specialties, such as Biellese macaroons, polenta concia (a hearty dish with cheese and butter), and Gorgonzola cheese. Take a food tour to taste some of the town’s best flavors and learn about the local produce and techniques.
  • Skiing: Biella is known for its excellent ski resorts, including Oropa, Bielmonte, and Zegna. Enjoy the fresh Alpine air and stunning views as you glide down the slopes.
  • Wine tours: Piedmont is known for its excellent wines, including Barolo, Barbera, and Asti Spumante, among many others. Take a wine tour to sample some of the region’s best wines and learn about the winemaking process.
  • Shopping: Biella is home to many artisanal shops and boutiques, selling everything from high-quality wool fabrics to local ceramics and jewelry. Take a stroll through the town center and discover some unique souvenirs and gifts.

Food and Drink Scene of Biella

No trip to Biella would be complete without sampling some of the town’s delicious local cuisine and drinks. Here are some of the must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Biellese macaroons: These bite-sized treats are made with almond flour, sugar, and egg white, and they’re a town specialty.
  • Polenta concia: A traditional dish made with polenta, local cheese, and butter.
  • Baraggia rice: A unique variety of rice grown exclusively in the Biella area, it’s typically used to make risotto.
  • Gorgonzola cheese: A rich and flavorful cheese that’s produced in the nearby town of Gorgonzola.
  • Agnolotti di Biella: These small pockets of fresh pasta are stuffed with meat and herbs and served with a butter and sage sauce.

Local Beverages

  • Biellese wines: Piedmont is known for its excellent wine scene, and Biella is no exception. Try some of the region’s best reds, whites, and sparkling wines.
  • Barley coffee: A caffeine-free alternative to traditional coffee, this roasted barley beverage is a local specialty.
  • Grappa di Nebbiolo: A strong, aromatic spirit made from the skins, seeds, and stems left over from winemaking.

Where to Stay in Biella: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Biella offers a variety of accommodations to suit every budget and preference. Here are some of the options:

  • Ostello di Biella (Biella Hostel): A budget-friendly option located in the town center, offering shared and private rooms.
  • Hotel Agata: A mid-range hotel located near the town center, offering comfortable rooms and a restaurant.
  • Agora Palace Hotel: A luxury hotel located on the outskirts of the town, offering spacious rooms, a wellness center, and a Michelin-starred restaurant.
  • B&B Il Gomitolo: A charming guesthouse located in the suburbs, offering cozy rooms and a garden.

What to Buy in Biella: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

If you’re looking for a unique souvenir or gift from Biella, here are some of the best local products to consider:

  • Wool products: Biella is known for its high-quality wool fabrics, and you’ll find a variety of woolen coats, scarves, and blankets in the local shops.
  • Ceramics: The nearby town of Chiavazza is known for its colorful, handmade ceramics, which make great gifts and keepsakes.
  • Jewelry: Biella has a thriving jewelry scene, with many artisanal shops selling unique, handcrafted pieces.
  • Baraggia rice: Bring some of the region’s unique rice variety home to cook in your own kitchen.
  • A bottle of local wine or grappa: Take a piece of Piedmont’s wine culture home with you.

Local Culture and Customs of Biella

Like many Italian towns, Biella has its own unique customs and traditions. Here are some of the things you might encounter during your stay:

  • The art of wool: Biella has a long history of wool production, and you’ll see many shops and workshops dedicated to this craft. Don’t be surprised if you see locals wearing woolen clothes all year round.
  • Religious festivals: Biella is a predominantly Catholic town, and many of its festivals and celebrations have religious origins.
  • Afternoon siesta: Like many small towns in Italy, Biella can be quiet in the afternoons as locals take a break from the heat. Some shops and attractions may be closed during this time.
  • Italian hospitality: The locals in Biella are known for their warm and hospitable nature, so don’t hesitate to strike up a conversation or ask for recommendations during your stay.

Best Time to Visit Biella

Biella is beautiful year-round, but the best time to visit depends on your preferences. If you’re a fan of winter sports, then the ski season (December to March) is the ideal time to visit. For milder temperatures, you might consider visiting in the spring (April to June) or the fall (September to November). If you prefer warmer weather, then the summer (July to August) is the best time to visit, though it can get quite hot during this time.

Do’s and Don’ts of Biella

Here are some basic guidelines to follow during your stay in Biella:

Do:

  • Learn some Italian: While many locals speak English, some knowledge of Italian will go a long way in making connections with the people and culture.
  • Respect religious traditions: Biella is a predominantly Catholic town, so be respectful of religious celebrations and customs.
  • Try the local cuisine: Biella has a rich culinary culture, and sampling the local dishes is a must.
  • Take your time: Biella is a laid-back town, so don’t rush through your itinerary. Take time to enjoy the local attractions and scenery.

Don’t:

  • Assume everyone speaks English: While English is widely spoken, not everyone will be fluent. Be patient and respectful if you encounter language barriers.
  • Be too flashy with your valuables: Biella is a safe town, but it’s always best to exercise caution and avoid attracting unwanted attention to your belongings.
  • Wear inappropriate clothing: When visiting religious sites or museums, be sure to dress modestly and appropriately.

Safety and Health

Biella is generally a safe and healthy town to visit, but it’s always best to take basic precautions and follow good health practices during your stay.

Safety in Biella

  • Avoid walking alone at night: While Biella is a safe town, it’s always best to exercise caution and avoid walking alone in unlit areas after dark.
  • Watch out for pickpockets: Like any tourist destination, Biella has its share of pickpockets and petty crime. Keep an eye on your belongings and be alert in crowded areas.

Health in Biella

  • Stay hydrated: Biella can get quite hot in the summer months, so be sure to drink plenty of water and stay cool during the day.
  • Protect yourself from the sun: When spending time outdoors, wear sunscreen and a hat to protect yourself from the sun’s harmful rays.
  • Watch out for ticks: If you’re hiking or spending time in wooded areas, be sure to check yourself for ticks, which can carry Lyme disease.

Getting to Biella: Transportation Options and Logistics

Biella is located in Northwest Italy, and it’s easily accessible by a range of transportation options.

Airports Near Biella

  • Turin Airport: Located about 70 km southwest of Biella, Turin Airport is the closest international airport to the town.
  • Milan Malpensa Airport: Located about 140 km southeast of Biella, Milan Malpensa is another major airport in the area.

Bus Stations Near Biella

  • Biella Bus Terminal: Located in the town center, this bus station offers connections to many other towns in the area.

Other Options

  • Trains: The nearest train station to Biella is Santhià, located about 20 km away, with connections to Turin and Milan.
  • Car rental: If you prefer to drive, you can rent a car at one of the airports and drive to Biella.

How to Get Around in Biella

Biella is a small town, so walking or biking is the best way to get around. However, if you need transportation, here are some options:

  • Buses: Biella has a local bus service that covers the town and surrounding areas.
  • Taxis: Taxis are available in Biella, with stands located in the town center.
  • Car rental: If you prefer to rent a car, there are several options located in the town center.

Where to Go Next from Biella: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

If you’re planning to extend your trip beyond Biella, here are some nearby destinations and excursions to consider:

  • Turin: Located about 80 km southwest of Biella, Turin is a historic city with a rich cultural scene and excellent food culture.
  • Aosta Valley: Located about 100 km northwest of Biella, the Aosta Valley is known for its stunning Alpine landscapes and ski resorts.
  • Genoa: Located about 140 km southeast of Biella, Genoa is a vibrant port city with a rich history and excellent seafood cuisine.

Conclusion

With its rich history, stunning natural beauty, delicious local cuisine, and warm hospitality, Biella is a town that has something for everyone. Whether you’re looking to explore the top attractions, hit the hiking trails, or simply soak up the local culture, Biella is a destination that’s well worth a visit. We hope this guide has provided you with all the information you need to plan your perfect trip to Biella.
