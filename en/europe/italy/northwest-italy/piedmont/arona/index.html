Introduction to Arona

Arona is a town located in the province of Novara, in the Piedmont region of Northwest Italy. It is situated on the western shore of Lake Maggiore and boasts a rich blend of culture, history, and nature, making it an ideal holiday destination. The town is easily accessible by car or public transport from Milan and major Italian cities, making it a popular weekend getaway spot.

List of Places to Visit in Arona

Are you looking to explore Arona’s rich cultural heritage, breathtaking natural beauty, and exciting outdoor activities? Here are some of the top places to visit in Arona:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • San Carlone Statue: A towering statue of Saint Charles Borromeo, built in the 17th century, is a must-visit when in Arona. Climbing to the top of the statue offers stunning views of Lake Maggiore and the surrounding landscape.

  • Rocca Borromeo: This fortress and castle built in the 14th century is situated on a hill overlooking the town. It houses the Celestial Academy Museum, which showcases art and cultural exhibits from the region.

  • Palazzo di Giustizia: A historic courthouse designed by Giuseppe Zanoia, the palace is an architectural gem worth exploring.

  • Santa Marta Church: The church dates back to the 13th century and houses a painting of the Last Supper by Gaudenzio Ferrari. It also features a beautiful facade and a bell tower that can be climbed for panoramic views of the town and lake.

Natural Attractions of Arona: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Lake Maggiore: A beautiful lake in the region that offers a range of water activities, including swimming, boating, and fishing. Visitors can take a leisurely stroll along the lakefront promenade and enjoy picturesque views of the lake and the Alps.

  • Parco della Rocca Borromea: A park surrounding the Rocca Borromea, filled with rare plants and trees. It features winding paths, caves and cascades, and scenic views from the belvedere.

  • Lagoni di Mercurago Nature Reserve: Home to several walking trails leading to archaeological sites, lush forests, and observation points with striking views of the surrounding areas.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Arona

Arona is a great place for hiking and biking enthusiasts. There are many trails, from easy to challenging, that offer panoramic views of the lake and stunning scenery. Some of the popular routes include:

  • Sentiero della Pace (Path of Peace): a 35 km trail that runs from the Swiss border to the Piedmont region, it offers breathtaking views of the Alps and the lake.

  • Monte San Carlo Trail: This trail takes you through chestnut forests and offers panoramic views of the town and the lake.

  • Greenway del Lago Maggiore: The trail runs from Arona to Stresa, along the lake’s shores. It is a flat and easy trail suitable for all ages and levels of fitness.

Best Things to Do in Arona: Activities and Tours

There is plenty to do and see in Arona for visitors of all ages and interests. Here are some of the best things to do in Arona:

  • Take a boat tour around Lake Maggiore and visit the Borromean Islands, famous for their historic villas and gardens.

  • Spend the day at the beach and enjoy swimming, sunbathing, and water sports activities.

  • Explore the medieval streets of Arona on foot or by bike and discover the town’s rich history and cultural heritage.

  • Take a guided tour of Arona’s major landmarks, such as the San Carlone Statue and the Rocca Borromeo.

  • Enjoy the local food and wine scene by trying out some of the delicious dishes and wines that are unique to the Piedmont region.

Food and Drink Scene of Arona

The Piedmont region is known for its rich food and wine scene, and Arona is no exception. Here are some of the must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Risotto alla Milanese: A delicious dish made with saffron, cheese, and butter.

  • Tagliatelle al Ragù: A pasta dish made with meat sauce and Parmesan cheese.

  • Focaccia: A local bread, flavored with olive oil and rosemary.

  • Gorgonzola: A type of blue cheese, famous in the region.

Local Beverages

  • Nebbiolo Wine: A red wine made from the Nebbiolo grape, famous in Piedmont.

  • Barolo Wine: A full-bodied red wine made from Nebbiolo grapes, known for its complexity and aging potential.

  • Vermouth: A fortified wine, flavored with herbs and spices, and used in cocktails like the Negroni and Americano.

Where to Stay in Arona: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Arona has a range of accommodation options, suitable for all budgets and preferences. Here are some of the best places to stay in Arona:

  • Hotel Spagna: A budget-friendly hotel located in the town center, offering comfortable rooms and a great breakfast buffet.

  • Hotel Atlantic: A boutique hotel situated on the lakefront, offering stunning views of the lake and the Alps.

  • Hotel Michelangelo: A luxury hotel, featuring elegant rooms, a spa, and an outdoor pool.

What to Buy in Arona: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Looking for souvenirs and local products to bring home? Here are some of the most popular items to buy in Arona:

  • Cheese: The region is famous for its cheeses like Gorgonzola, Parmesan, and Taleggio.

  • Croccantini: A local biscuit made with almonds, sugar, and honey.

  • Wine: The Piedmont region is known for its amazing red wines like Barolo and Barbaresco.

  • Chocolate: The region is also known for its delicious chocolates, including handcrafted bonbons and truffles.

Local Culture and Customs of Arona

Arona has a rich cultural heritage that has been shaped by its geographical location, historical events, and local traditions. Locals are known for their warm hospitality and love for food and wine.

Best Time to Visit Arona

The best time to visit Arona is between May and October when the weather is warm and sunny. The town has a packed events calendar during the summer months, including music festivals, food fairs, and cultural events.

Do’s and Don’ts of Arona

Here are some of the do’s and don’ts of Arona:

Do:

  • Try the local cuisine and wine.

  • Explore the town on foot or by bike.

  • Participate in local cultural events and festivals.

Don’t:

  • Speak loudly or make noise in public places.

  • Wear inappropriate clothing when visiting churches or religious sites.

  • Be disrespectful to locals or their customs.

Safety and Health

Safety in Arona

Arona is a safe town for visitors, with low crime rates. However, like in any other tourist destination, travelers should be aware of pickpocketing and theft in crowded areas.

Health in Arona

Arona has a high-quality healthcare system, and visitors can access medical care from public and private hospitals and clinics. It is advisable to have travel insurance that covers medical emergencies.

Getting to Arona: Transportation Options and Logistics

Arona is easily accessible by car or public transport from major Italian cities like Milan and Turin.

Airports Near Arona

Milan Malpensa Airport is the nearest airport to Arona, about a 30-minutes drive away. Other airports in the region include Milan Linate Airport and Turin Airport.

Bus Stations Near Arona

Arona has a bus station that connects to the major towns and cities in Piedmont and other regions of Italy.

Other Options

Visitors can also reach Arona by train, with railway connections from Milan and Turin. Alternatively, driving to Arona is a great option for those who prefer to explore the region at their own pace.

How to Get Around in Arona

Arona is a small town and can easily be explored on foot or by bike. There are also public transport options like buses and taxis available.

Where to Go Next from Arona: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

After exploring Arona, visitors can go on a day trip to nearby destinations like the charming town of Stresa and the stunning Borromean Islands. Other exciting excursions include visiting the Ossola Valley and Valsesia, known for their gorgeous landscapes and skiing opportunities.

Conclusion

Arona is a beautiful town that offers a rich blend of culture, history, and natural beauty. With its picturesque lake, historic landmarks, delicious cuisine, and outdoor activities, Arona is an ideal holiday destination for travelers looking for an authentic Italian experience.
