Introduction to Imperia

Located on the Ligurian coast, Imperia is a beautiful port city that boasts an intriguing mix of historical wonders, natural sights, and culinary delights. The city is famous for its stunning beaches, scenic promenades, and picturesque harbor, making it a popular destination among tourists from all over the globe.

List of Places to Visit in Imperia

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Museo dell’Olivo: This museum displays the history and culture of olive oil production in the region and exhibits a vast collection of olive oil presses and machinery.

  • Museo Navale del Ponente Ligure: Located in the historic seafront district of Oneglia, this naval museum showcases the maritime history of Liguria through an extensive collection of models, ships, and artifacts.

  • Cattedrale di San Maurizio: A magnificent religious edifice that features colorful stained-glass windows, ornate frescoes, and intricate artworks.

Natural Attractions of Imperia: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Parco Urbano Santa Barbara: A vast urban park with panoramic views of the city’s coastline and hills that is perfect for hiking, biking, and picnicking.

  • Porto Maurizio Beach: A sandy beach with crystal clear waters and a lively promenade that offers a plethora of water sports activities and seaside relaxation.

  • Villa Grock: A stunning villa built in the early 20th century by the famous Swiss clown Grock, offering an array of landscaped gardens and a large swimming pool.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Imperia

  • The Alta Via dei Monti Liguri: A long-distance trail that spans across the entirety of the Ligurian Mountains, passing through vast forests and breathtaking vistas.

  • Aregai to Cipressa: This is a relatively easy route that takes you through the charming villages of Aregai and Cipressa and offers stunning panoramic views of the gulf of Imperia.

Best Things to Do in Imperia: Activities and Tours

  • Take a boat tour: Sail along the city’s scenic coast and witness the impressive panoramic views.

  • Visit the local markets: Explore Piazza dei Fiori in Oneglia and grab some fresh, locally sourced produce and handmade crafts.

  • Tour of the olive groves: Get a glimpse of the local olive oil production process and sample some of the world-renowned local olive oil.

Week-Long Itinerary for Imperia

Day Plan
Day 1 Visit the Cattedrale di San Maurizio and explore the historic Oneglia district.
Day 2 Spend the day lounging on Porto Maurizio Beach and strolling along the Promenade.
Day 3 Cycle the easy route from Aregai to Cipressa and take in the stunning views.
Day 4 Visit the Museo dell’Olivo and take a guided tour of the olive groves.
Day 5 Hike the Alta Via dei Monti Liguri or visit Parco Urbano Santa Barbara.
Day 6 Take a boat tour around the coast of the city.
Day 7 Shop at the local markets and tour Villa Grock before leaving Imperia.

Food and Drink Scene of Imperia

Must-Try Dishes

  • Pesto Alla Genovese: A Ligurian sauce made of fresh basil, garlic, pine nuts, Parmesan cheese, and olive oil, typically served over pasta.

  • Focaccia: A famous Italian flatbread topped with olive oil, fresh herbs and sometimes even onions or tomatoes.

  • Seafood: Imperia being a coastal city, you’ll find a variety of seafood dishes that include sea bass, sardines, baccalà, and anchovies.

Local Beverages

  • Vermentino: A zesty, aromatic white wine that is typical of the Ligurian region.

  • Chinotto: A refreshing, slightly bitter citrus-flavored soda drink made from the fruit of the Chinotto tree.

Where to Stay in Imperia: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Hotel Miramare: A charming, seafront hotel close to the historic Oneglia district with beautiful views of the harbor.

  • La Casa di Jo: A cozy apartment in the heart of Imperia, with easy access to all the local attractions.

  • Villa Graziosa: A charming rustic villa nestled in the hills outside of Imperia, offering a peaceful and scenic retreat.

What to Buy in Imperia: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Olive Oil: The local olive oil is famous for its quality, so make sure to grab a bottle or two to take back home.

  • Handmade crafts: Visit the local markets to find unique handmade crafts and souvenirs to take as mementos of your trip.

Local Culture and Customs of Imperia

  • Religion: Imperia is a predominantly Catholic city, so you’ll find many religious shrines and monuments throughout the city.

  • Dress code: Dress appropriately when visiting religious sites or more formal restaurants; otherwise, dress casually and comfortably.

Best Time to Visit Imperia

The best time to visit Imperia is between May and September, when the weather is sunny and warm, and the city is at its liveliest.

Do’s and Don’ts of Imperia

Do:

  • Try the local cuisine and delicacies.

  • Learn some basic Italian phrases.

  • Explore the natural sights and walking/hiking trails.

Don’t:

  • Wear bikinis to town centers, stores, and restaurants.

  • Ignore any local customs and traditions.

Safety and Health

Safety in Imperia

Imperia is a safe city, but, as with all tourist hotspots, it’s always important to keep an eye on your belongings and be aware of your surroundings.

Health in Imperia

Liguria has a high-quality healthcare system that provides quality medical treatment to tourists, so there’s nothing to worry about in terms of health issues.

Getting to Imperia: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Imperia

  • Nice Côte d’Azur Airport: located 95 km from Imperia.

  • Genoa Cristoforo Colombo Airport: located 132 km from Imperia.

Bus Stations Near Imperia

Imperia is well connected with other cities in Italy through Interregional and Intraregional buses such as Flixbus, ATC, and B-Pod.

Other Options

You can also get to Imperia by train, taxi or rental car from major cities in Italy such as Genoa, Milan, and Rome.

How to Get Around in Imperia

The best way to get around Imperia is by foot, bike, or car. The city has a well-developed public transportation system, including buses and trains, that connect the major attractions.

Where to Go Next from Imperia: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Sanremo: A beautiful coastal town with a famous casino and stunning botanical gardens, located just 28 km from Imperia.

  • Alassio: A picturesque medieval town with beautiful beaches and local shops selling handmade pottery and ceramics, located 40 km from Imperia.

Conclusion

Imperia is a city that has something for everyone, whether you’re a nature lover, a foodie, or a history buff. The stunning views, the delicious cuisine, the intriguing culture, and the welcoming locals have made Imperia a must-visit destination in Italy.
