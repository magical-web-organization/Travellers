Introduction to Savona

Located in Liguria, Northwest Italy, Savona is a stunning coastal city steeped in history and culture. The city boasts a rich maritime heritage, beautiful beaches, natural wonders, delicious cuisine, and a relaxed Italian lifestyle that will have you falling in love with it.

List of Places to Visit in Savona

If you are planning a trip to Savona, here are some top attractions worth visiting:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Priamar Fortress: This historic fortress has a fascinating history that dates back to the 16th century. Today, it hosts art exhibitions, concerts, and events.

  • Savona Cathedral: Dating back to the 16th century, the Savona Cathedral is a stunning example of classic Romanesque architecture.

  • Museo del Tesoro della Cattedrale di Savona: The Cathedral Museum is home to a diverse collection of religious art, artefacts, and furnishings.

Natural Attractions of Savona: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Bergeggi Island: Take a boat ride to the tiny island of Bergeggi for a day of snorkelling and swimming in pristine crystal-clear waters.

  • Monte Beigua Regional Nature Park: Surrounded by lush forests, the Monte Beigua park is a great place for hiking and enjoying stunning views of the coast.

  • Grotte di Toirano: These stunning caves are said to date back millions of years and contain some of the most extensive cave paintings in Italy.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Savona

Hiking and biking in Savona are some of the best ways to explore the city’s natural beauty. Here are some popular routes:

  • Scola Tower Path: This trail takes you to the top of Scola Tower, where you can enjoy spectacular views of Savona’s coastline.

  • Beigua Park Bike Trail: This exhilarating 35 km stretch of off-road trails and country roads winds through valleys, forests and mountains in the Beigua Park.

Best Things to Do in Savona: Activities and Tours

  • Beaches: Spend some quality time at the beautiful beaches of Savona, like the stunning Baia dei Saraceni or the family-friendly Bagni Ligure beach.

  • Wine Tours: Take a wine tour of nearby vineyards, like the famed Albenga wine region.

  • Food Tours: Indulge in mouth-watering Ligurian cuisine with a food tour of Savona.

Week-Long Itinerary for Savona

Here is a sample week-long itinerary for your Savona vacation:

Day 1: Explore the city’s historical landmarks, including the Priamar Fortress, Savona Cathedral, and Museo del Tesoro della Cattedrale di Savona.

Day 2: Rent a bike and explore the Beigua park trail, which will take you through lush forests and scenic mountains.

Day 3: Visit Bergeggi Island and spend the day swimming and sunbathing in pristine waters.

Day 4: Take a wine tour of the Albenga wine region and indulge in some of the best Italian wines.

Day 5: Boasting some of the most beautiful beaches in Italy, you can’t leave Savona without spending a few days lounging on the beach or taking part in some water activities.

Day 6: Take a food tour of Savona and indulge in some delicious Ligurian cuisine, such as the local pesto or focaccia.

Day 7: Wrap up your trip by spending a relaxing day in nature at Grotte di Toirano.

Food and Drink Scene of Savona

Savona’s cuisine is heavily influenced by its coastal location and the use of local ingredients. Here are some of the must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Pesto: Savona is known for its delicious pesto, made with local basil, garlic, olive oil, and pine nuts.

  • Focaccia: This savoury bread, typically served drizzled with olive oil and rock salt, is a Savona classic.

  • Farinata: Made with chickpea flour, this dish is a staple in Ligurian cuisine, and often served as a snack or side dish.

Local Beverages

  • Rossese di Dolceacqua: This light-bodied red wine made from the Rossese grape variety is a Savona classic.

  • Vermentino: This dry white wine is famous for its floral and fruity aromas and is perfect for pairing with seafood dishes.

  • Lemoncello: Made with fresh lemon peels, sugar, and alcohol, this classic Italian digestif is a must-have in Savona.

Where to Stay in Savona: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Savona offers a range of accommodation options for every budget, from luxury hotels to budget-friendly hostels and guesthouses. Some popular options include:

  • NH Savona Darsena: A stylish hotel located in the heart of Savona’s old town, just a few steps from the seafront.

  • City Hotel: This modern hotel offers comfortable, spacious rooms and easy access to Savona’s top attractions.

  • Hostel Florida: Located just a short walk from the city centre, this budget-friendly hostel offers dorms and private rooms.

What to Buy in Savona: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

When in Savona, consider buying some of the local products and souvenirs to take back home. Here are some ideas:

  • Olive Oil: Liguria is known for its high-quality olive oil, and you can find some of the best in Savona.

  • Pesto: Bring back a jar of Savona’s delicious local pesto to enjoy with your pasta dishes.

  • Handicrafts: Savona has a thriving craftsmen culture, and you’ll find plenty of shops selling beautifully handcrafted ceramics, jewellery, and textiles.

Local Culture and Customs of Savona

Savona is a relaxed coastal town with a rich maritime history. The locals take pride in their city’s cuisine, traditions, and culture. When in Savona, make sure to respect the local customs and traditions, dress appropriately, and always ask before taking pictures of people or religious sites.

Best Time to Visit Savona

The best time to visit Savona is from April to June or September to November. During these months, the weather is mild, the crowds are thinner, and the prices are lower.

Do’s and Don’ts of Savona

Do:

  • Try the local cuisine, especially the traditional pesto, focaccia, and farinata.

  • Explore the city’s rich history and culture, including the museums, landmarks, and local handicrafts.

  • Take part in outdoor activities like hiking, biking, swimming, and snorkelling.

Don’t:

  • Assume that every Italian speaks English.

  • Leave valuables unattended on the beach, and always keep an eye on your belongings while out and about.

  • Ignore basic Italian etiquette, like covering your shoulders and knees while visiting religious sites.

Safety and Health

Safety in Savona

Savona is generally a safe city, but like anywhere else, it is always a good idea to be cautious and aware of your surroundings. Keep an eye on your belongings and avoid walking alone in poorly lit areas at night.

Health in Savona

If you need any medical assistance while in Savona, there are several hospitals and medical centres in the city. Be sure to have comprehensive travel insurance that covers medical expenses and repatriation.

Getting to Savona: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Savona

The closest airports to Savona are Genoa Cristoforo Colombo Airport, which is 45 km away, and Nice Côte d’Azur Airport, which is 150 km away.

Bus Stations Near Savona

Savona has good bus connections to other cities in Liguria and neighbouring regions. You can check the local bus schedule online or at the bus station.

Other Options

The train is also an excellent option for getting to Savona from other Italian cities like Milan and Genoa. Savona has a train station located in the city centre, with several trains arriving and departing daily.

How to Get Around in Savona

The best way to get around Savona is on foot, especially if you are staying in the city centre or old town. For longer distances, you can use public transportation like buses or taxis. Alternatively, you can rent a bike or car to explore the city and surrounding areas independently.

Where to Go Next from Savona: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Savona is in an excellent location, with several stunning cities and attractions nearby. Here are a few places to consider:

  • Genoa: This iconic Italian city is just an hour away by train and is home to some of the country’s best cuisine, art, and architecture.

  • Sanremo: Located on the beautiful Riviera di Ponente, Sanremo is famous for its beautiful gardens, beaches, and luxury boutiques.

  • Cinque Terre: This beautiful coastal region of five colourful villages is just an hour’s train ride away and is perfect for a day trip or a weekend getaway.

Conclusion

Savona is a charming coastal town that is sure to capture your heart. It boasts a rich history, stunning natural beauty, a relaxed lifestyle, delicious cuisine, and warm hospitality that will have you wanting to return again and again. Use this comprehensive travel guide to plan your trip to Savona, and I am sure the city will steal a piece of your heart too!
