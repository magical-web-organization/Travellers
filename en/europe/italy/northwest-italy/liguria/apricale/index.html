Introduction to Apricale

Apricale, a small village in the Liguria region of Italy, is often referred to as one of the most magical and enchanting villages in Italy. The village is surrounded by rugged mountains and verdant valleys, and its narrow cobbled streets and rustic stone houses have remained untouched for centuries. It is a perfect destination for travelers interested in history, culture, nature, and outdoor adventures.

List of Places to Visit in Apricale

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • The Castle of Apricale: This medieval castle is one of the most popular attractions in the village. Located at the highest point of Apricale, the castle offers stunning views of the surrounding area. It was built in the 10th century and has seen many changes throughout history.

  • The Church of Saint Anthony the Abbot: This beautiful 15th-century church is located in the heart of the village and is definitely worth a visit. The church has a mix of Gothic and Baroque architecture, and the frescoes on its walls and ceiling are simply stunning.

  • The Museum of Ethnography and History: This museum is located in the old town hall of Apricale, and it displays an impressive collection of ancient artifacts and historical documents. Visitors can learn about the local history, customs, and traditions.

Natural Attractions of Apricale: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • The Apricale-Olivetta Scenic Drive: This scenic drive that connects the village of Apricale to Olivetta in France is considered one of the most beautiful drives in Europe. The road winds through some of the most breathtaking scenery in the region, passing by olive groves, vineyards, and historic villages.

  • The Caves of Toirano: These prehistoric caves are located about an hour and a half from Apricale and are definitely worth the trip. Visitors can explore the caves and see spectacular stalactites and stalagmites formations.

  • The Maritime Alps National Park: This national park encompasses some of the most stunning natural scenery in Italy. Visitors can take guided hikes, bike rides, and boat cruises through the park and see its rugged mountains, verdant valleys, and crystal-clear lakes.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Apricale

Apricale is surrounded by mountains and hills, making it an ideal destination for hiking and biking enthusiasts. There are plenty of well-marked trails and bike routes in the vicinity of the village, all of which offer stunning views of the surrounding countryside. Some of the most popular routes include the

  • Apricale - Perinaldo - Dolce Aqua and
  • Apricale - Pigna - Castelvittorio

trails and the bike route from Apricale to Sanremo.

Best Things to Do in Apricale: Activities and Tours

  • Cooking Classes: Apricale is known for its delicious cuisine, and visitors can take cooking classes to learn how to prepare traditional Ligurian dishes using local ingredients.

  • Wine Tasting: Visitors can go on wine tours of the nearby wineries, where they can sample some of the region’s best wines.

  • Guided Tours: Guided walking tours of Apricale are available, during which visitors can learn about the village’s history, culture, and architecture.

Food and Drink Scene of Apricale

Must-Try Dishes

  • Trenette al Pesto: A dish of fresh egg pasta served with pesto made from locally grown basil, garlic, and olive oil.

  • Focaccia di Recco: A Ligurian take on classic focaccia, which is a flatbread topped with melted cheese.

  • Pansotti di Salsa: A dish of stuffed pasta served with a rich walnut sauce.

Local Beverages

  • Vermentino di Liguria: A refreshing white wine made from grapes grown in the Liguria region.

  • Limoncello di Ventimiglia: A lemon-flavored liqueur made from lemons grown in the nearby town of Ventimiglia.

Where to Stay in Apricale: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

There are several options for accommodation in Apricale, ranging from high-end hotels to budget hostels. Some of the best options include:

  • Relais del Maro: A charming 4-star hotel located in the heart of the village, complete with a restaurant and bar.

  • La Favorita: A budget-friendly guesthouse with comfortable rooms and a great location.

  • Il Nido delle Rondini: A cozy bed and breakfast with beautiful views of the surrounding mountains.

What to Buy in Apricale: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Olive Oil: Liguria is known for its high-quality olive oil, and visitors can buy bottles of locally produced oil as souvenirs.

  • Pesto: Genovese pesto is one of the most popular condiments in the world, and visitors can buy jars of authentic Ligurian pesto to take home.

  • Ceramics: The region is known for its beautiful handcrafted ceramics.

Local Culture and Customs of Apricale

The people of Apricale are known for their warm hospitality and love for food and wine. The village also has a rich tradition of music and art, and visitors can often find local musicians and artists performing in the streets and cafes.

Best Time to Visit Apricale

The best time to visit Apricale is between April and October when the weather is warm and dry. The summer months can be crowded, so visitors who prefer a quieter experience should consider visiting in the spring or fall.

Do’s and Don’ts of Apricale

Do:

  • Respect the local culture and customs.

  • Try the local food and wine.

  • Take guided tours to learn more about the village’s history and culture.

Don’t:

  • Litter or damage any of the historical sites.

  • Wear revealing clothing in public places.

Safety and Health

Safety in Apricale

Apricale is a very safe village, and crime is rare. However, visitors should always keep their valuables in a safe place and be aware of their surroundings, especially at night.

Health in Apricale

The healthcare system in Italy is of a high standard, and there are several hospitals and clinics in the region. Visitors should ensure that they have adequate travel insurance before arriving in Apricale.

Getting to Apricale: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Apricale

The closest airport to Apricale is Nice Cote d’Azur Airport in France, which is about an hour’s drive away. Alternatively, visitors can fly into Genoa Cristoforo Colombo Airport in Genoa, which is about two hours away by car.

Bus Stations Near Apricale

There are several bus stations near Apricale, including in the nearby towns of Ventimiglia and Sanremo. Visitors can take buses from these stations to reach Apricale.

Other Options

Renting a car is also a great option for visitors who want to explore the surrounding areas. There are several rental car companies in the region.

How to Get Around in Apricale

The best way to explore Apricale is on foot, as the village is small and its winding streets are not suitable for cars. Visitors can also rent bikes to explore the surrounding countryside.

Where to Go Next from Apricale: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • The French Riviera: The glamorous French Riviera is just a short drive away from Apricale.

  • The Cinque Terre: The stunning coastal villages of the Cinque Terre are a 2-hour drive from Apricale.

Conclusion

Apricale is a hidden gem in the Liguria region of Italy, and it has something to offer every type of traveler. Whether you’re interested in history, culture, nature, or outdoor adventure, Apricale is a must-visit destination.
