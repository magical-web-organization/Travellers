Introduction to East Coast and Midlands

Located in the heart of Ireland, East Coast and Midlands offer a perfect blend of history, culture, and outdoor activities. This region is home to some of the country’s most iconic landmarks, picturesque landscapes, and vibrant cities. The area includes counties such as Dublin, Kildare, Meath, Laois, and Offaly, each with its own unique charm.

Whether you are a history buff, an adventure seeker, a foodie, or simply looking for a relaxing getaway, East Coast and Midlands have something for everyone. This comprehensive tour guide will help you plan your trip to this incredible region.

List of Places to Visit in East Coast and Midlands

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Dublin Castle: This magnificent castle is an excellent example of Medieval and Georgian architecture. It served as a fortress, palace, and prison during its 800-year history.
  • Kilmainham Gaol: This former prison is now a museum and one of the most significant historical landmarks in Ireland. It played a crucial role in the fight for Ireland’s independence.
  • Newgrange: This prehistoric monument is one of the most significant archaeological sites in Ireland. It dates back to 3200 BC and is older than the Pyramids of Giza.

Natural Attractions of East Coast and Midlands: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Wicklow Mountains National Park: This park is a hiker’s paradise with several trails ranging from easy to challenging. It offers breathtaking views of mountains, lakes, and waterfalls.
  • Cliffs of Moher: These stunning cliffs rise 700 feet above the Atlantic Ocean and provide spectacular views of the sea, countryside, and villages.
  • Phoenix Park: This urban park in Dublin is one of the largest enclosed parks in Europe and home to the Dublin Zoo and the official residence of the President of Ireland.

Hiking/Biking Routes of East Coast and Midlands

  • Royal Canal Way: This 146-kilometer trail follows the route of the Royal Canal and passes through several picturesque towns and villages.
  • The Grand Canal Way: This 131-kilometer trail follows the route of the Grand Canal and passes through Dublin, Kildare, and Offaly.
  • The Slieve Bloom Way: This trail in the Slieve Bloom Mountains is a 73-kilometer loop that offers stunning views of the countryside and ancient ruins.

Best Things to Do in East Coast and Midlands: Activities and Tours

  • Guinness Storehouse Tour: This tour takes you through the history of Guinness and the brewing process. You can also enjoy a complimentary pint of Guinness at the rooftop bar with panoramic views of Dublin city.
  • Whiskey Distillery Tour: Ireland is famous for its whiskey, and some of the best whiskey distilleries are located in East Coast and Midlands. You can learn about the distilling process and sample some of the best whiskeys on a tour.
  • Medieval Banquet Experience: You can step back in time and enjoy a medieval feast in a castle or manor house. You will be entertained by musicians, dancers, and jesters while dining on roasted meats, vegetables, and ale.

Week-Long Itinerary for East Coast and Midlands

  • Day 1: Start your trip in Dublin and explore its rich history and culture. Visit Dublin Castle, Trinity College, and the National Museum of Ireland. Stroll through the vibrant streets of Temple Bar, known for its pubs, restaurants, and live music.

  • Day 2: Take a day trip to Newgrange and the Boyne Valley. Visit the prehistoric monument of Newgrange and learn about its fascinating history. Explore the 12th-century Trim Castle, one of the largest Norman castles in Ireland.

  • Day 3: Head to Wicklow Mountains National Park and enjoy a scenic hike through the mountains. Visit the Powerscourt Estate, a stunning 18th-century manor house surrounded by lush gardens and waterfalls.

  • Day 4: Visit Kilkenny, a medieval city famous for its castle, cathedrals, and historic buildings. Explore the Kilkenny Castle and the St. Canice’s Cathedral.

  • Day 5: Take a day trip to the Cliffs of Moher and the Burren. Admire the stunning views of the Atlantic Ocean and the Irish countryside. Explore the unique limestone landscape of the Burren.

  • Day 6: Visit Galway, a charming seaside city known for its art, culture, and traditional music. Explore the historic Latin Quarter, the Galway City Museum, and the Spanish Arch.

  • Day 7: End your trip in Dublin and spend the day shopping or relaxing in one of the city’s parks.

Food and Drink Scene of East Coast and Midlands

Must-Try Dishes

  • Irish Stew: A traditional meat and vegetable stew that is a staple of Irish cuisine.
  • Bangers and Mash: A popular dish that consists of sausages and mashed potatoes.
  • Fish and Chips: A classic British and Irish dish that consists of battered white fish and French fries.

Local Beverages

  • Guinness: A dark stout beer that is brewed in Dublin.
  • Whiskey: Ireland is known for its whiskey, which is made from malted barley and distilled three times.
  • Cider: An alcoholic beverage made from fermented apples that is popular in Ireland.

Where to Stay in East Coast and Midlands: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

There are several accommodation options in East Coast and Midlands, ranging from budget-friendly hostels to luxurious hotels and guesthouses. Some of the popular options include:

  • The Shelbourne Hotel in Dublin: A luxurious five-star hotel located in the heart of Dublin.
  • The K Club in Kildare: A five-star hotel set in a historic 18th-century mansion surrounded by 550-acre estate.
  • The Ardmore Guesthouse in Waterford: A cozy guesthouse located in the seaside town of Ardmore, known for its sandy beaches and coastal walks.

What to Buy in East Coast and Midlands: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Irish Whiskey: Ireland is famous for its whiskey, and you can find some of the best whiskeys in East Coast and Midlands.
  • Claddagh Ring: A traditional Irish ring that symbolizes love, loyalty, and friendship.
  • Aran Sweater: A traditional Irish sweater made from wool and known for its intricate patterns and warmth.

Local Culture and Customs of East Coast and Midlands

In East Coast and Midlands, the locals are friendly, hospitable, and welcoming to tourists. Irish culture is known for its love of music, dance, and storytelling. The traditional Irish language is still spoken in some parts of the region, and you may hear it in some of the rural areas.

Best Time to Visit East Coast and Midlands

The best time to visit East Coast and Midlands is from May to September when the weather is mild and pleasant. The peak tourist season is during the summer months, so expect higher prices and larger crowds.

Do’s and Don’ts of East Coast and Midlands

Do:

  • Try Irish whiskey, Guinness, and other local beverages.
  • Explore the region’s natural beauty by hiking or cycling.
  • Learn about Irish history and culture by visiting museums and historical landmarks.

Don’t:

  • Underestimate the weather - it can be unpredictable, so be prepared for rain and wind.
  • Disrespect local customs and traditions.
  • Forget to tip at restaurants and bars - tipping is customary in Ireland.

Safety and Health

Safety in East Coast and Midlands

East Coast and Midlands are generally safe for tourists. However, you should take normal precautions and keep an eye on your belongings in crowded areas.

Health in East Coast and Midlands

There are no specific health risks in East Coast and Midlands, and the tap water is safe to drink. However, it’s always a good idea to bring any necessary medication and travel insurance.

Getting to East Coast and Midlands: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near East Coast and Midlands

  • Dublin Airport: The largest airport in Ireland, with direct flights to major cities in Europe and North America.

Bus Stations Near East Coast and Midlands

  • Busaras Dublin: The central bus station in Dublin that provides services to other parts of Ireland.

Other Options

  • Car Rental: Renting a car is a popular option for exploring East Coast and Midlands, as it allows you to go off the beaten path and visit more remote areas.

How to Get Around in East Coast and Midlands

The best way to get around East Coast and Midlands is by car or public transportation. There are several train and bus services that connect major towns and cities in the region. Taxis and private tours are also available.

Where to Go Next from East Coast and Midlands: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Ring of Kerry: This scenic drive takes you through the rugged coastline and traditional villages of western Ireland.
  • Giant’s Causeway: A natural wonder and UNESCO World Heritage Site located in Northern Ireland.
  • Cork: A vibrant city in southern Ireland known for its food, music, and traditional Irish culture.

Conclusion

East Coast and Midlands offer a perfect blend of history, culture, and outdoor activities. From exploring the vibrant streets of Dublin to hiking the scenic mountains of Wicklow, this region has something for everyone. We hope this comprehensive tour guide helps you plan your trip to East Coast and Midlands and makes your visit unforgettable.
