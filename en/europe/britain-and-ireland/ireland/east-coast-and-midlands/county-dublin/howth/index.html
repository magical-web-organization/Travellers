Introduction to Howth

Howth is a picturesque coastal town in County Dublin, located on Ireland’s east coast and part of the greater Dublin area. With its stunning panoramic views, rugged hills, and quaint harbor, Howth is a must-visit destination for travelers exploring Ireland. The town has a rich history dating back to the early Viking settlements and served as an important outpost during the Anglo-Norman invasion. Today, Howth is known for its excellent seafood, outdoor adventures, and stunning natural beauty.

List of Places to Visit in Howth

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Howth Castle: A historic castle dating back to the 13th century that has been home to the St Lawrence family for over 800 years. The castle and gardens are open for tours during the summer months.

  • Howth Martello Tower: One of several Martello towers located along the Irish coast built during the Napoleonic Wars. Visitors can climb to the top of the tower for panoramic views.

  • National Transport Museum: A museum dedicated to Ireland’s transportation history, featuring vintage cars, bicycles, planes, and other vehicles.

Natural Attractions of Howth: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Howth Cliff Walk: A scenic walking trail that follows the cliffs around Howth Head, offering panoramic views of the town, bay, and the Dublin Mountains in the distance.

  • Balscadden Bay: A secluded beach located on the north side of Howth Head, offering stunning views and an opportunity for a refreshing swim.

  • Ireland’s Eye: A small uninhabited island located just off the coast of Howth, home to a variety of seabirds and accessible via ferry.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Howth

  • The Bog of Frogs: A challenging hiking trail that takes you to the summit of Howth Hill, offering stunning views of Dublin and the Irish Sea.

  • The Rhododendron Walk: A gentle walking trail that takes you through Howth Castle’s gardens, featuring an extensive collection of rhododendron plants.

Best Things to Do in Howth: Activities and Tours

  • Sea Kayaking: Enjoy a guided sea kayaking tour around Howth Head, exploring sea caves, rock pools, and marine wildlife.

  • Whale Watching: Take a boat tour from Howth harbor to spot dolphins, porpoises, and even Minke whales.

  • Fishing: Howth is renowned for its excellent seafood, and visitors can try their hand at fishing by booking a fishing trip with a local guide.

Food and Drink Scene of Howth

Must-Try Dishes

  • Dublin Bay Prawns: These delicious shellfish are a regional specialty and served in many of Howth’s seafood restaurants.

  • Irish Stew: A hearty lamb and potato stew, perfect for warming up on a chilly day.

  • Fresh Seafood: Howth’s location on the Irish coast means there is no shortage of fresh seafood, including cod, haddock, mackerel, and crab.

Local Beverages

  • Guinness: Ireland’s most iconic beer, and a staple in nearly every pub in Howth.

  • Poitin: A traditional Irish distilled spirit made from potatoes or barley.

  • Irish Whiskey: A smooth and flavorful whiskey that is popular in Ireland and around the world.

Where to Stay in Howth: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • The King Sitric: A luxurious boutique hotel located in the heart of Howth village, overlooking the pier and harbor.

  • The Howth YHA Hostel: A comfortable and affordable option for budget-minded travelers, located just a short walk from Howth village.

  • The Moorings: A charming guesthouse located on Howth harbor, offering panoramic views of the bay and a cozy atmosphere.

What to Buy in Howth: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Irish wool clothing: From Aran sweaters to woolen caps and gloves, there are plenty of high-quality woolen products available in Howth.

  • Irish pottery: Handmade pottery is a popular souvenir option, with many local artists offering unique and beautiful designs.

  • Irish whiskey: Bring home a bottle of Irish whiskey as a souvenir or gift for a whiskey-loving friend.

Local Culture and Customs of Howth

  • Irish culture is heavily influenced by Catholicism and respects religious traditions such as Sunday mass and abstaining from meat on Fridays during Lent.

  • Irish people are known for their warm hospitality and friendly nature, so visitors should feel free to strike up conversations with the locals.

Best Time to Visit Howth

The best time to visit Howth is during the summer months (June-August) when the weather is mild and sunny. However, the town’s stunning natural beauty can be enjoyed year-round, with each season offering its own unique charm.

Do’s and Don’ts of Howth

Do:

  • Do try the seafood! Howth is known for its excellent seafood offerings, so be sure to indulge in some traditional Irish dishes.

  • Do explore the outdoors! Howth has plenty of hiking trails and outdoor activities to offer, so don’t miss out on the scenic views and fresh air.

Don’t:

  • Don’t forget to pack rain gear. Ireland’s climate can be unpredictable, so be sure to prepare for some rainy days.

  • Don’t be disrespectful to local customs or traditions. Visitors should show respect for Ireland’s religion and traditions, even if they do not share the same beliefs.

Safety and Health

Safety in Howth

Howth is generally a safe place to visit, but visitors should take common-sense precautions such as keeping an eye on their belongings and avoiding walking alone at night.

Health in Howth

Ireland’s national health system provides excellent healthcare services, and visitors can find medical care at local clinics and hospitals in case of emergencies.

Getting to Howth: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Howth

  • Dublin Airport: Located just 12 miles from Howth, Dublin Airport is the most convenient option for visitors arriving by air.

Bus Stations Near Howth

  • Howth Dart Station: Located in the center of Howth village, Dart Station offers train service to Dublin and other nearby destinations.

Other Options

  • Taxis: Taxis are readily available in Howth, offering convenient transportation around the town and to nearby destinations.

  • Car Rentals: Visitors can rent cars at Dublin Airport or in Dublin City center and drive to Howth.

How to Get Around in Howth

Howth is a small town and easily walkable, with many attractions located within walking distance of Howth village. Visitors can also take buses, taxis, or rent bicycles for transportation.

Where to Go Next from Howth: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Dublin: The bustling capital of Ireland is just a short train ride away from Howth, offering plenty of opportunities to explore Irish culture and history.

  • The Cliffs of Moher: These stunning sea cliffs are a popular destination for day trips from Dublin, located on the west coast of Ireland.

  • Glendalough: This historic valley in County Wicklow is known for its stunning scenery and ancient ruins.

Conclusion

With its scenic views, outdoor adventures, and rich history, Howth is a must-visit destination for travelers exploring Ireland. Use this comprehensive guide to plan your Howth itinerary and make the most of your visit to this charming coastal town.
