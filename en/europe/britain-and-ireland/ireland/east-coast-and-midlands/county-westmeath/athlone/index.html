A Comprehensive Travel Guide to Athlone, Ireland

Athlone is a charming town located in County Westmeath, in the East Coast and Midlands region of Ireland, in Britain and Ireland, in Europe. Known for its natural beauty, rich history, and vibrant culture, Athlone is a hidden gem that should be on every traveler’s list.

Introduction to Athlone

Situated on the River Shannon, Athlone is a historic town that dates back to the 12th century. It has undergone significant changes throughout its history and has developed into a popular tourist destination. Athlone is now a hub of culture, art, and entertainment, with plenty of unique attractions and activities to offer visitors.

List of Places to Visit in Athlone

Athlone has something for everyone, whether you’re a culture buff, an adventure seeker, or a foodie. Here are some of the top places to visit and attractions you shouldn’t miss:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Athlone Castle - a 12th-century castle that tells the story of Athlone’s rich history and culture
  • Luan Gallery - a contemporary art gallery that showcases the best of Irish art and culture
  • Saint Peter & Paul’s Church - an impressive 19th-century cathedral that stands tall in the town center
  • Clonmacnoise - a significant monastic site and graveyard that holds centuries of Irish Christian heritage and treasures

Natural Attractions of Athlone: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Lough Ree - a vast lake ideal for fishing, boating, and water sports
  • Kilbeggan Racecourse - a stunning horse racing venue that offers visitors a glimpse of Ireland’s racing tradition
  • Mount Temple Golf and Country Club - scenic golf course set on the rolling hills of County Westmeath.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Athlone

Athlone offers some of the best hiking and biking routes in the country. Whether you’re a seasoned hiker or a beginner, there’s a route for every skill level. Here are some of the best:

  • The Old Rail Trail - a 40km route that takes you through picturesque landscapes and scenic towns
  • The Lough Ree Sanctuary Trail - a 9km trail that circles Lough Ree, offering beautiful views of the lake and its surroundings
  • The Royal Canal Greenway - a 144 km bike and walk trail that winds through County Westmeath, offering stunning views of the countryside and culminating in a lively town atmosphere.

Best Things to Do in Athlone: Activities and Tours

  • Take a cruise down the River Shannon - one of the best ways to explore the town’s waterways
  • Visit Athlone Castle - a must-see attraction that showcases the area’s rich history and the emblematic 12th-century structure
  • Take a cooking class and learn how to prepare classic Irish dishes
  • Visit the Irish Whiskey Museum and sample some of the finest whiskeys in the country
  • Try your hand at fishing in Lough Ree for a chance to catch some of the lake’s famous pike.

Week-Long Itinerary for Athlone

Day 1: Start your week-long itinerary with a visit to Athlone Castle, one of the most iconic landmarks in the town.

Day 2: Take a cruise down the River Shannon and explore the town’s waterways.

Day 3: Drive to Clonmacnoise and take a guided tour of the historic monastic site and graveyard.

Day 4: Spend the day playing golf at Mount Temple Golf and Country Club or take a horseback riding tour of the countryside.

Day 5: Explore the town’s art and culture scene by visiting Luan Gallery and Saint Peter & Paul’s Church.

Day 6: Take the Lough Ree Sanctuary Trail and enjoy the scenic views of the lake and the surrounding countryside.

Day 7: Visit the Irish Whiskey Museum and learn about the history of whiskey-making in Ireland.

Food and Drink Scene of Athlone

Athlone’s food and drink scene is an eclectic mix of traditional and contemporary cuisine. Here are some of the must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Irish Stew - a hearty stew made with lamb, potatoes, and carrots
  • Boxty - a classic Irish pancake made from grated potatoes, flour, and buttermilk
  • Seafood Chowder - a creamy and flavorful soup made with fresh seafood ingredients

Local Beverages

  • Guinness - Ireland’s famous stout that’s a must-try for any beer lover
  • Jameson Whiskey - a smooth and mellow Irish whiskey that’s perfect for sipping
  • Irish Coffee - a hot coffee drink made with Irish whiskey, sugar, and whipped cream.

Where to Stay in Athlone: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Athlone offers a range of accommodation options to suit every budget and preference. From luxurious hotels to cozy bed and breakfasts, here are some of the best:

  • Sheraton Athlone Hotel - A luxurious hotel located in the heart of town
  • Arch House B&B - A comfortable and homely bed and breakfast with excellent reviews
  • The Prince of Wales - A budget-friendly hostel with a prime location in the town center.

What to Buy in Athlone: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Athlone has plenty to offer for souvenir hunters and shopaholics. Here are some of the best local products to bring home:

  • Irish woolen sweaters and scarves
  • Celtic jewelry and Claddagh rings
  • Crystal and glassware from the local glass factory
  • Local handmade pottery and ceramics.

Local Culture and Customs of Athlone

Athlone’s culture is heavily influenced by Irish traditions and customs. The locals take pride in their heritage and embrace their history and culture. Visitors should expect to encounter traditional music, storytelling, and dancing during their stay in Athlone.

Best Time to Visit Athlone

The best time to visit Athlone is during the summer months, between May and August. The weather is mild and pleasant, with plenty of sunlight and clear skies that showcases the town’s natural beauty to its fullest.

Do’s and Don’ts of Athlone

Do:

  • Try the local cuisine and traditional Irish drinks
  • Learn about the town’s rich history and culture
  • Respect the locals and their customs
  • Take a River Shannon cruise for scenic views
  • Wear comfortable shoes and clothing for hiking and exploring the countryside.

Don’t:

  • Be disrespectful or rude to the locals
  • Litter or damage the natural environment
  • Underestimate the importance of Athlone Castle
  • Underdress for the weather, as it can be unpredictable.

Safety and Health in Athlone

Athlone is a safe and welcoming town, and visitors should not feel threatened or unsafe. However, it’s important to take the necessary precautions, such as staying aware of your surroundings and not carrying too much cash or valuables.

In terms of health, visitors should ensure they have travel insurance before embarking on their trip. Medical facilities are available in the town, but visitors should take precautions, such as drinking bottled water and practicing good hygiene.

Getting to Athlone: Transportation Options and Logistics

Athlone is easily accessible by various modes of transport, making it a convenient destination to explore. Here are some options for getting to Athlone:

Airports Near Athlone

  • Ireland West Airport Knock (86 km)
  • Dublin Airport (128 km)

Bus Stations Near Athlone

  • Bus Eireann
  • Citylink
  • GoBus

Other Options

  • Renting a car
  • Taking a train from Dublin to Athlone.

How to Get Around Athlone

Athlone is a small town, and most attractions are within walking distance. However, visitors who wish to explore further can take the bus or taxi, or rent a bike or car.

Where to Go Next from Athlone: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Athlone is a great base for exploring the surrounding area. Here are some nearby destinations and excursions to consider:

  • Cliffs of Moher - a breathtaking natural wonder located in County Clare
  • Galway City - a vibrant and lively city with a rich cultural scene
  • Dublin - Ireland’s capital city, known for its unique architecture, history, and pubs
  • The Burren - a unique landscape dotted with karst limestone hills and valleys.

Conclusion

Athlone is a must-visit destination in Ireland, offering visitors a unique mix of history, culture, and natural beauty. The town’s rich history, delicious cuisine, and stunning landscapes make it a destination that won’t disappoint.
