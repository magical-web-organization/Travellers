Introduction to County Westmeath

County Westmeath is a picturesque county located in the East Coast and Midlands region of Ireland. This county is known for its beautiful countryside, rich history, and vibrant culture. Visitors to County Westmeath can expect to find a warm welcome, stunning landscapes, and a range of outdoor activities.

List of Places to Visit in County Westmeath

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Belvedere House and Gardens

    Belvedere House and Gardens is a restored Georgian estate that overlooks Lough Ennell. This beautifully preserved house dates back to the 18th century and provides visitors with a glimpse into Ireland’s past. Visitors to Belvedere House and Gardens can explore the house and gardens and enjoy spectacular views of the lake.

  • Fore Abbey

    Fore Abbey is an ancient monastic site that dates back to the 7th century. This well-preserved site features medieval ruins, an historic church, and a round tower. Visitors to Fore Abbey can experience the peaceful and serene atmosphere of this historic site and learn about the history of the monks who lived here.

  • Kilbeggan Distillery

    Kilbeggan Distillery is one of Ireland’s oldest whisky distilleries. Visitors to this distillery can take a tour and learn about the whiskey-making process, see the old distillery equipment, and taste some of the award-winning tipples.

Natural Attractions of County Westmeath: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Lough Derravaragh

    Lough Derravaragh is a stunning lake that is located in the heart of County Westmeath. The lake is surrounded by rolling hills and lush countryside, making it the perfect spot for a picnic or a scenic walk.

  • Mullaghmeen Forest

    Mullaghmeen Forest is a 1,000-acre forest that is home to a range of wildlife and plant species. Visitors to the forest can explore the many trails, take in the stunning views of the countryside, and maybe even spot some red squirrels.

  • Hill of Uisneach

    The Hill of Uisneach is an ancient site that is said to be the heart of Ireland. This historic site was once the site of ancient rituals and ceremonies and is now a popular spot for visitors to take in the views of the surrounding countryside.

Hiking/Biking Routes of County Westmeath

  • Old Rail Trail

    The Old Rail Trail is a scenic route that runs from Mullingar to Athlone. This 40-kilometer trail is perfect for walkers, cyclists, and horse riders, and takes in some of the county’s most beautiful countryside.

  • Lough Ennell Walk

    The Lough Ennell Walk is a 13km looped walk that takes in the stunning scenery around Lough Ennell. Along the way, you’ll pass through woodlands and meadows, and enjoy incredible views of the lake.

Best Things to Do in County Westmeath: Activities and Tours

  • Lakeland Cruise

    A lakeland cruise is a great way to explore Lough Ennell and its surroundings. During the cruise, visitors can take in the stunning scenery, learn about the history of the lake, and maybe even spot some local wildlife.

  • Westmeath Way Cycle Route

    The Westmeath Way Cycle Route is a 45km route that takes in some of the county’s most picturesque countryside. Along the way, you’ll pass through quaint villages and historic sites, and enjoy stunning views of the countryside.

Week-Long Itinerary for County Westmeath

Food and Drink Scene of County Westmeath

Must-Try Dishes

  • Boxty

    Boxty is a traditional Irish potato pancake that is popular in County Westmeath. It is often served with bacon and eggs for breakfast, or with stew or corned beef for lunch or dinner.

  • Barm Brack

    Barm Brack is a fruit cake that is typically served around Halloween. It is made with dried fruit, spices, and sometimes even whiskey, and is a delicious treat for anyone with a sweet tooth.

Local Beverages

  • Whiskey

    County Westmeath is home to several whiskey distilleries, including Kilbeggan Distillery. These distilleries offer visitors the chance to taste some of the best whiskies Ireland has to offer.

  • Cider

    Cider is also a popular beverage in County Westmeath, with several local producers creating delicious ciders using locally-grown apples.

Where to Stay in County Westmeath: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Annebrook House Hotel

    Annebrook House Hotel is a luxurious hotel located in Mullingar. This hotel features beautifully decorated rooms, an award-winning restaurant, and an on-site bar.

  • Bloomfield House Hotel

    Bloomfield House Hotel is a 4-star hotel located on the shores of Lough Ennell. This hotel features spacious rooms, a spa, and an on-site restaurant.

  • Lough Bishop House

    Lough Bishop House is a charming bed and breakfast located in the heart of County Westmeath. This bed and breakfast features beautifully decorated rooms, a large garden, and a delicious breakfast menu.

What to Buy in County Westmeath: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Westmeath Blankets

    Westmeath blankets are a popular souvenir to bring back from your trip to County Westmeath. These woolen blankets are made using traditional weaving techniques and come in a range of colors and patterns.

  • Handmade Soap

    County Westmeath is home to several handmade soap producers. These producers create beautiful soaps using natural ingredients such as lavender and honey. They are a perfect gift for anyone who loves luxury bath products.

Local Culture and Customs of County Westmeath

County Westmeath has a rich cultural heritage, and visitors can expect to experience traditional music, dance, and storytelling during their visit. The Irish language, or Gaelic, is also still spoken in some parts of the county.

Best Time to Visit County Westmeath

The best time to visit County Westmeath is from May to September, when the weather is warm and dry, and the countryside is in full bloom.

Do’s and Don’ts of County Westmeath

Do:

  • Do be respectful of local customs and traditions.

  • Do try the local food and drink.

  • Do explore the beautiful countryside.

Don’t:

  • Don’t litter or damage the local environment.

  • Don’t be disrespectful to local people or their customs.

  • Don’t drive under the influence of alcohol or drugs.

Safety and Health

Safety in County Westmeath

County Westmeath is a safe place to visit, with relatively low crime rates. Visitors should still take basic precautions and be aware of their surroundings, particularly when walking or cycling in rural areas.

Health in County Westmeath

Ireland has a high standard of healthcare, and visitors to County Westmeath can expect to receive excellent medical care if needed. Visitors should ensure they have adequate travel insurance before traveling to Ireland.

Getting to County Westmeath: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near County Westmeath

  • Dublin Airport

    Dublin Airport is located approximately 80km from County Westmeath. From here, visitors can take a bus or train to Mullingar or Athlone.

Bus Stations Near County Westmeath

  • Mullingar Bus Station

    Mullingar Bus Station is located in the heart of Mullingar town and is served by several bus companies, including Bus Eireann and Irish Citylink.

Other Options

  • Train

    Iarnrod Eireann operates train services from Dublin to Mullingar and Athlone.

How to Get Around in County Westmeath

The best way to get around County Westmeath is by car, bike, or on foot. There are several car rental companies located in the county, and there are also several bike rental companies that offer bike hire on a daily or weekly basis.

Where to Go Next from County Westmeath: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Dublin

    Dublin is Ireland’s capital city and is located approximately 80km from County Westmeath. Visitors to Dublin can explore the city’s rich history, visit the many museums and galleries, and enjoy the vibrant nightlife.

  • Clonmacnoise

    Clonmacnoise is an ancient monastic site located on the banks of the River Shannon. Visitors to Clonmacnoise can explore the ruins of the old churches, see the high crosses, and learn about the history of this important site.

Conclusion

County Westmeath is a beautiful county with a rich cultural heritage, stunning landscapes, and a warm and friendly welcome. Whether you’re interested in history, outdoor activities, or just want to relax and enjoy the countryside, County Westmeath is the perfect destination for your next vacation.
