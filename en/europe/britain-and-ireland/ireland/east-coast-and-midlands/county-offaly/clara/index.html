Introduction to Clara

Located in County Offaly, in the East Coast and Midlands region of Ireland, Clara is a charming town that offers tourists a unique blend of cultural and natural attractions. Known for its historic architecture, scenic views, and warm locals, Clara is a great destination for anyone looking to explore Ireland’s rich heritage.

List of Places to Visit in Clara

Visitors to Clara will be spoilt for choice when it comes to must-visit attractions. Our list below highlights some of the top places to add to your itinerary.

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Clara Bog Visitor Centre: This museum is dedicated to showcasing the biodiversity of the Clara Bog Nature Reserve and its role in history. A visit here is a must for anyone interested in science and nature.
  • Esker Monastery: A well-preserved monastery dating back to the 6th century, Esker is a unique attraction that offers insight into Ireland’s religious history.
  • Charleville Castle: One of the most visited historical sites in Clara, this stunning castle is a beautiful example of Neo-Gothic architecture.

Natural Attractions of Clara: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Slieve Bloom Mountains: The mountain range offers some of the most scenic views in Clara and welcomes hikers of all levels. Suitable hiking trails, bike trails, and pony trekking routes are mapped out in the Slieve Bloom Mountains.
  • Glendeer Pet Farm: Ideal for families traveling with children, this farm offers the chance to see a variety of animals, including deer, llamas, and wallabies. Here, visitors can also enjoy an outdoor playground and indoor soft play area.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Clara

Clara is a great destination for outdoor enthusiasts, and visitors can explore some of the best hiking and biking routes in Ireland. The Slieve Bloom Mountains offer amazing landscapes and several trails of varying difficulty. The Clara to Kilbeggan Greenway which runs 4.5km and it is a delightful route for walkers and cyclists.

Best Things to Do in Clara: Activities and Tours

  • Clara Bog Walk: If you’re interested in flora and fauna, then the Clara Bog Walk is a must-visit attraction. You’ll be guided through one of Ireland’s best-preserved raised bogs, with the opportunity to spot rare species of animals along the way.
  • Kilbeggan Distillery Experience: Treat yourself to a guided tour of the Kilbeggan Distillery to learn about Irish whiskey-making. Bonus: there’s a free tasting session at the end of the tour!
  • Lough Boora Discovery Park: Known for its unique landscape, the Lough Boora Discovery Park is a great place to escape and unwind. You can opt to take a self-guided tour or hire a bike and explore the park at your own pace.

Food and Drink Scene of Clara

Must-Try Dishes

When it comes to food in Clara, visitors can expect hearty and comforting meals that are typical of Irish cuisine. Some of the must-try dishes include:

  • Irish stew
  • Boxty (potato pancakes)
  • Dublin Coddle (sausage and bacon stew)
  • Colcannon (potatoes and cabbage dish)
  • Shepherd’s pie

Local Beverages

Ireland is famous for its beers and whiskeys, and you can expect to find both in plentiful supply in Clara. Whether you’re going for a pint of Guinness or sampling a Jameson whiskey, there’s no shortage of great drinks to try.

Where to Stay in Clara: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

There are a variety of accommodation options available in Clara, from hotels to hostels and guesthouses. Here are some options to consider:

  • Clara House (hotel)
  • Hillside Bed and Breakfast (guesthouse)
  • Conroy’s Old Bar (quirky hostel)

What to Buy in Clara: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Visitors who enjoy shopping will appreciate the local products sold in Clara. Some of the must-buy items include traditional Irish tweed clothing, artisan cheese, and locally brewed beers and whiskeys.

Local Culture and Customs of Clara

Ireland has a rich cultural heritage, and you can expect to experience the warm hospitality and friendliness of the locals in Clara. It’s worth noting that etiquette in Ireland involves shaking hands when you meet someone new, and addressing individuals with titles such as “Sir” or “Madam” until given permission to address them by their first names.

Best Time to Visit Clara

The best time to visit Clara is during the summer months, as the weather is milder and there are more outdoor activities available. However, if you don’t mind the colder temperatures, then winter is an ideal time to enjoy winter sports and the festive season.

Do’s and Don’ts of Clara

Do:

  • Do try traditional Irish cuisine
  • Do learn a few words of Irish Gaelic
  • Do tip 10-15% in restaurants

Don’t:

  • Don’t assume that everyone in Ireland drinks Guinness
  • Don’t forget to look left when crossing the street (traffic is on the left side of the road)
  • Don’t’ be alarmed if you’re offered a “cuppa tae” – it’s just a cup of tea!

Safety and Health

Safety in Clara

Clara is a generally safe town, and visitors need not worry too much about their safety. The usual precautions of not leaving luggage or valuables unattended and being aware of your surroundings apply.

Health in Clara

Visitors should be aware of potential tick bites when hiking in the outdoors, and should also be up-to-date on their routine vaccinations. It’s always a good idea to bring any necessary prescription medications with you.

Getting to Clara: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Clara

  • Dublin Airport (70 km)
  • Shannon International Airport (102 km)

Bus Stations Near Clara

  • Bus Eireann (3 km)
  • Clara Train station (1.6 km)

Other Options

Visitors who prefer to drive can rent a car at either airport or transport stations.

How to Get Around in Clara

Clara is a relatively small town and can be easily explored on foot. Visitors can also hire a bike to explore the surrounding countryside or rent a car to venture further afield.

Where to Go Next from Clara: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Tullamore (12 km)

With its pretty canals, Tullamore is an ideal destination for a day trip. Highlights include the Tullamore D.E.W. Visitor Centre, the Tullamore Court Hotel, and Lloyd Town Park.

Shannonbridge (26 km)

This historic town is home to the Clonmacnoise, an ancient monastery that dates back to the 6th century. Visitors can also enjoy the warm local hospitality and artisan shops.

Conclusion

Clara may be small in size, but it offers visitors a wealth of attractions and activities that will appeal to all types of travellers. With its charming towns, historic landmarks, and stunning natural landscapes, Clara is a must-visit destination in Ireland.
