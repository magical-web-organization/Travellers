Introduction to Ashbourne

Ashbourne is a quaint town in County Meath, situated in the East Coast and Midlands of Ireland, and is a popular destination for tourists looking to escape the hustle and bustle of big cities. The town is just a 30-minute drive away from Dublin, the capital city of Ireland, making it easily accessible for travelers.

Ashbourne is a great place to visit for its rich cultural heritage, scenic natural landscapes, outdoor activities, and mouthwatering cuisine. Whether you are a history buff, an adventure seeker, or a food lover, Ashbourne has something to offer for everyone.

List of Places to Visit in Ashbourne

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Newgrange: This UNESCO World Heritage Site is a prehistoric monument built around 3200 BCE. It is one of the most important archaeological sites in Ireland and offers a fascinating glimpse into Ireland’s ancient past.
  • Tara Hill: An important site in Irish mythology and history, this hilltop site offers stunning panoramic views of the surrounding countryside and a chance to explore archaeological sites dating back to the Bronze Age.
  • Killegar Chess Set Museum: This unique museum houses the world’s largest collection of historic chess sets spanning over 1000 years.

Natural Attractions of Ashbourne: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Fairyhouse Race Course: Known for its picturesque setting, this racecourse hosts several popular horse racing events throughout the year.
  • Tayto Park: A family-friendly theme park with thrilling rides, animal attractions, and a treetop walkway.
  • Ashbourne Golf Club: This 18-hole course is known for its lush green landscapes and challenging holes.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Ashbourne

  • Boyne Valley Way: This 112-kilometer trail follows the River Boyne from Drogheda to Navan, passing through Ashbourne along the way. It offers breathtaking views of the valley and a chance to explore numerous historic sites.
  • The Royal Canal Greenway: This 144-kilometer trail follows the path of the Royal Canal, passing through picturesque towns and villages, and offering plenty of opportunities for biking and hiking.
  • The Dublin Mountains Way: This trail takes you through the stunning Dublin Mountains, offering panoramic views of Dublin Bay. It is a challenging hike, with steep climbs and descents, but well worth the effort for the breathtaking scenery.

Best Things to Do in Ashbourne: Activities and Tours

  • Horse Riding: Ashbourne is home to several equestrian centers, offering a chance to ride horses through the beautiful countryside.
  • Whiskey Tasting: The Slane Distillery, located just a short drive from Ashbourne, offers guided tours and tastings of their world-renowned whiskey.
  • Irish Dancing: The Ashbourne Céilí Club provides visitors with a chance to learn traditional Irish dances and participate in a lively community of dancers.

Food and Drink Scene of Ashbourne

Must-Try Dishes

  • Stew: A hearty and comforting dish made with tender beef, potatoes, and vegetables, perfect for cool days.
  • Boxty: A potato pancake that is a staple of Irish cuisine, typically filled with savory meats or vegetables.
  • Barmbrack: A traditional fruit bread that is served toasted and slathered with butter.

Local Beverages

  • Guinness: A dark, rich, and creamy stout that is a favorite beer of Ireland.
  • Irish Coffee: A warming and indulgent coffee, made with Irish whiskey, brown sugar, and whipped cream.

Where to Stay in Ashbourne: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Pillo Hotel Ashbourne: A four-star hotel with elegant rooms, an indoor swimming pool, and a top-notch restaurant.
  • Ashbourne Court Hotel: A comfortable hotel with spacious rooms, a lively bar, and live music in the evenings.
  • Aisling Guest House: A charming and cozy guesthouse with comfortable rooms, a delicious breakfast, and a friendly staff.

What to Buy in Ashbourne: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Handmade Pottery: Browse a collection of unique and beautiful pottery made by local artisans.
  • Fine Wines: Ashbourne is home to several wineries, and visitors can sample and purchase bottles of the finest wines.
  • Woollen Sweaters: Purchase a warm and cozy Aran sweater, a classic garment of Irish heritage.

Local Culture and Customs of Ashbourne

Ashbourne is located in County Meath, which is known for its deeply rooted cultural heritage and traditions. Visitors can immerse themselves in this culture by attending local festivals, music sessions, and ceilidh dances.

Best Time to Visit Ashbourne

The best time to visit Ashbourne is between May and September, when the weather is mild and pleasant, and the natural landscapes are in full bloom. The summer months also offer plenty of outdoor activities and festivals.

Do’s and Don’ts of Ashbourne

Do:

  • Do take part in a traditional Irish music session.
  • Do sample the local cuisine, including traditional dishes like stew and boxty.
  • Do try a pint of Guinness, one of Ireland’s most famous and beloved beers.

Don’t:

  • Don’t litter or damage the natural landscapes.
  • Don’t forget to tip in restaurants and bars.
  • Don’t forget to immerse yourself in the local culture and traditions.

Safety and Health

Safety in Ashbourne

Ashbourne is generally a safe town, but visitors should take common sense precautions like keeping valuables secured and being aware of their surroundings.

Health in Ashbourne

There are no known health risks in Ashbourne. Visitors should be vigilant and practice good hygiene practices to prevent the spread of common illnesses.

Getting to Ashbourne: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Ashbourne

  • Dublin Airport: Located just 20 miles away from Ashbourne, Dublin Airport is the nearest airport to the town.

Bus Stations Near Ashbourne

  • Ashbourne Bus Station: Located in the town center, the bus station offers regular services to and from Dublin and other surrounding towns.

Other Options

  • Car Rental: Visitors can rent a car from Dublin Airport or in Ashbourne town center.
  • Taxi Services: Taxis are readily available in Ashbourne and can be booked in advance or hailed on the street.

How to Get Around in Ashbourne

Ashbourne is a small town that is easy to navigate on foot or by bike. Visitors can also make use of local taxi services or rent a car for longer journeys.

Where to Go Next from Ashbourne: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Dublin: Ireland’s capital city is just a 30-minute drive away from Ashbourne, and is a must-visit destination for its rich history, vibrant culture, and exciting nightlife.
  • Malahide: A beautiful seaside town with picturesque beaches, scenic parks, and historic castles.
  • Kells: A charming market town with historic sites, including monastic ruins and a high cross.

Conclusion

Ashbourne is a hidden gem in County Meath, offering a delightful escape from the hustle and bustle of big cities. Whether you are interested in history, outdoor adventures, or simply relaxing in a cozy pub, Ashbourne has something to offer for everyone. Plan your trip today and discover all the beauty and charm that this quaint Irish town has to offer.
