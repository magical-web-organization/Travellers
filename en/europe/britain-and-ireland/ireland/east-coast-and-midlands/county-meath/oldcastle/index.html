Oldcastle Travel Guide: Best Places to Visit, Things to Do, and More

If you’re looking to explore the heart of Ireland, then Oldcastle in County Meath should be on your list! With its natural beauty, historical landmarks, and vibrant local culture, Oldcastle has something for everyone. In this comprehensive travel guide, we’ll take a closer look at the top attractions, hiking routes, food, accommodations, local culture and customs, and transport options in Oldcastle. Plan your trip now!

Introduction to Oldcastle

Oldcastle is a picturesque town in County Meath, located in the East and Midlands region of Ireland. The town dates back to the 17th century and is known for its stunning natural landscapes, historic landmarks, and friendly locals. Whether you’re interested in soaking up the local culture, exploring the great outdoors, or simply relaxing, Oldcastle has something for everyone.

List of Places to Visit in Oldcastle

Oldcastle has many attractions to offer, from museums and historical places to scenic views and outdoor adventures. Check out some of the top places to visit below.

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Oldcastle has a rich history and culture, which is reflected in its many museums and historical landmarks. Here are some places to add to your itinerary:

  • Loughcrew Cairns
  • Oldcastle Courthouse
  • St Brigid’s Church
  • Museum of Country Life

Natural Attractions of Oldcastle: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Oldcastle and its surrounding areas are well known for their natural beauty. Here are some of the must-see places:

  • Loughcrew Megalithic Centre and Adventure Park
  • Loughcrew Gardens
  • Mullaghmeen Forest
  • Fore Abbey

Hiking/Biking Routes of Oldcastle

Oldcastle is an excellent destination for hiking and biking enthusiasts, with several well-marked trails. Here are some of the most popular hiking and biking routes in and around Oldcastle:

  • Loughcrew Loop Walk
  • Mullaghmeen Forest
  • Fore Abbey Loop Walk
  • Boyne Greenway

Best Things to Do in Oldcastle: Activities and Tours

There’s plenty to do in Oldcastle, regardless of your interests. Here are some of the best things to do while you’re in the town:

  • Visit Loughcrew Adventure Centre for zip-lining and archery.
  • Take a boat tour of Loughcrew Lake.
  • Go fishing at the River Inny
  • Go horse-riding at Ross Castle Equestrian Centre

Week-Long Itinerary for Oldcastle

If you’re planning on spending a week in Oldcastle, here’s a suggested itinerary to make the most of your time:

Day Activities
Day 1 Visit Loughcrew Cairns and Adventure Park, then have a picnic at Loughcrew Gardens.
Day 2 Go hiking at Mullaghmeen Forest, then have dinner at a local restaurant in Oldcastle.
Day 3 Explore Oldcastle Courthouse and St Brigid’s Church, followed by a walk around Fore Abbey and Loughcrew Lake.
Day 4 Join a boat tour of Loughcrew Lake, then head to the Museum of Country Life.
Day 5 Go fishing at River Inny, then explore nearby villages such as Castlepollard and Mountnugent.
Day 6 Spend the day at Ross Castle Equestrian Centre and try horse-riding.
Day 7 End your trip with a relaxing spa day at nearby Knightsbrook Hotel and Spa.

Food and Drink Scene of Oldcastle

Ireland is famous for its delicious cuisine, and Oldcastle is no exception. Here are some of the must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Irish stew
  • Bacon and cabbage
  • Boxty (Irish potato pancake)
  • Seafood chowder

Local Beverages

  • Guinness
  • Whiskey
  • Blackcurrant cordial

Where to Stay in Oldcastle: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Whether you’re looking for luxury accommodations or budget-friendly options, Oldcastle has plenty of choices. Here are some of the best places to stay in the town:

  • The Headfort Arms Hotel
  • Ross Castle Equestrian Centre
  • Oldcastle House Hotel
  • Hazel Grove Bed and Breakfast

What to Buy in Oldcastle: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Oldcastle and its surrounding areas are known for their handmade crafts and local products. Here are some of the best things to buy as souvenirs:

  • Pottery from Loughcrew Megalithic Centre
  • Hand-woven blankets from Foxford Woolen Mills
  • Handmade jewelry from local artisans
  • Irish whiskey

Local Culture and Customs of Oldcastle

Oldcastle has a strong sense of community, and its people are known for their friendliness and hospitality. The town celebrates several festivals throughout the year, including St. Patrick’s Day and the annual Loughcrew autumn equinox event. Visitors are encouraged to join in the festivities and mingle with the locals.

Best Time to Visit Oldcastle

The best time to visit Oldcastle is during the summer months from June to August when the weather is warm and sunny. However, if you’re looking to avoid the crowds, the spring and fall months are also good choices.

Do’s and Don’ts of Oldcastle

Do:

  • Try the local cuisine
  • Attend a traditional Irish music session
  • Go hiking or biking in the surrounding areas

Don’t:

  • Ignore local customs and traditions
  • Litter or damage historical landmarks
  • Be disrespectful to the locals

Safety and Health

Safety in Oldcastle

Oldcastle is a relatively safe town, with low crime rates. Visitors are advised to exercise normal precautions, such as keeping an eye on their belongings in high-traffic areas.

Health in Oldcastle

Oldcastle has several medical facilities, including a local clinic and a hospital in nearby Navan. Visitors are advised to have travel insurance and to take any necessary vaccinations before traveling.

Getting to Oldcastle: Transportation Options and Logistics

Oldcastle is well-connected to other parts of Ireland, making it an easy destination to reach. Here are some transport options to consider:

Airports Near Oldcastle

  • Dublin Airport (77 kilometers)
  • Belfast City Airport (156 kilometers)
  • Knock Airport (129 kilometers)

Bus Stations Near Oldcastle

  • Bus Eireann
  • Dublin Coach
  • John McGinley Coaches

Other Options

Visitors can also rent a car or hire a taxi to get to Oldcastle.

How to Get Around in Oldcastle

Oldcastle is a small town and is easy to explore on foot. However, if you want to venture outside of the town, renting a car or a bike is recommended.

Where to Go Next from Oldcastle: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Oldcastle is located in the heart of Ireland, making it an excellent base to explore other parts of the country. Here are some nearby destinations worth visiting:

  • Dublin (95 kilometers)
  • Navan (23 kilometers)
  • Trim (32 kilometers)
  • Loughcrew Cairns (11 kilometers)

Conclusion

Oldcastle is a hidden gem, with its stunning landscapes, historic landmarks, and welcoming locals. Whether you’re looking for outdoor adventures, local culture, or delicious cuisine, Oldcastle has it all. Plan your trip today and explore this beautiful Irish town!
