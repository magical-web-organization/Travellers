Introduction to Navan

Located in the heart of County Meath, Navan is a vibrant and historic town with a population of approximately 30,000 people. It is situated in the East Coast and Midlands region of Ireland and is an excellent base for exploring the surrounding areas. Navan is steeped in history and has a rich cultural heritage, which is reflected in its many museums and historical sites. There are also plenty of outdoor activities, beautiful hiking and biking routes, and excellent dining options to keep visitors entertained.

List of Places to Visit in Navan

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Hill of Tara

Located just outside Navan, the Hill of Tara is an ancient neolithic site that was once the seat of the High Kings of Ireland. It is an incredibly powerful and atmospheric place, with stunning views of the surrounding countryside. Visitors can explore the ancient stone monuments and the impressive burial mounds and learn about the rich history and mythology of the site.

Trim Castle

Trim Castle is the largest Norman castle in Ireland and is located just a short drive from Navan. It has been used as a location in several films, including Braveheart, and is an impressive sight to behold. Visitors can take a guided tour of the castle and learn about its history and significance.

Solstice Arts Centre

The Solstice Arts Centre is a vibrant cultural hub in Navan that hosts a wide range of events and exhibitions throughout the year. It is home to a theatre, art gallery, and workshop spaces, and there is always something exciting going on.

Natural Attractions of Navan: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Boyne Valley

The Boyne Valley is a stunningly beautiful area of natural beauty that spans across County Meath and County Louth. It is home to some of Ireland’s most iconic landmarks, including the neolithic passage tombs of Newgrange, Knowth, and Dowth. Visitors can explore the rolling hills and peaceful valleys, take a boat trip along the River Boyne, or go hiking or biking through the beautiful countryside.

Navan Racecourse

Navan Racecourse is a popular spot for horse racing enthusiasts and those looking for a fun day out. It hosts several races throughout the year, with a great atmosphere and plenty of opportunities to place a bet.

Loughcrew Cairns

Loughcrew Cairns is a neolithic site located in County Meath, just a short drive from Navan. It consists of a complex of passage tombs and is believed to be over 5,000 years old. Visitors can explore the ancient monuments, take guided tours, or enjoy the stunning views of the surrounding countryside.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Navan

The Royal Canal Way

The Royal Canal Way is a popular hiking and biking route that runs through Navan and spans a total of 144km from Dublin to Longford. It follows the course of the Royal Canal and offers stunning views of the surrounding countryside, as well as a chance to explore some of the charming towns and villages along the way.

The Boyne Ramparts Walk

The Boyne Ramparts Walk is a beautiful 5.5km trail that follows the course of the River Boyne. It offers stunning views of the surrounding landscape, as well as a chance to explore some of the historic landmarks and monuments in the area.

Best Things to Do in Navan: Activities and Tours

Balrath Woods Walk

The Balrath Woods Walk is a beautiful 4km trail that winds through a stunning forested area just outside Navan. It is a peaceful and tranquil spot, with plenty of opportunities for birdwatching and wildlife spotting.

Navan Adventure Sports

Navan Adventure Sports is a popular spot for outdoor activities, including archery, clay pigeon shooting, and zip lining. It is suitable for all ages and abilities and offers a fun and exciting day out for the whole family.

Week-Long Itinerary for Navan

Day 1: Explore the Hill of Tara and the ancient sites of the Boyne Valley Day 2: Take a guided tour of Trim Castle and visit the Solstice Arts Centre Day 3: Go hiking or biking along the Royal Canal Way Day 4: Visit the Loughcrew Cairns and explore the surrounding countryside Day 5: Spend the day at the Navan Racecourse Day 6: Go on the Balrath Woods Walk and visit Navan Adventure Sports Day 7: Take a day trip to Dublin or one of the nearby coastal towns

Food and Drink Scene of Navan

Must-Try Dishes

Navan is renowned for its excellent cuisine, with plenty of traditional Irish dishes on offer, as well as more modern and international options. Some of the must-try dishes include:

  • Irish stew
  • Bacon and cabbage
  • Black pudding or white pudding
  • Seafood chowder
  • Boxty

Local Beverages

Ireland is famous for its beer and whiskey, and Navan is no exception. Some of the most popular local beverages include:

  • Guinness
  • Smithwick’s Ale
  • Jameson whiskey
  • Tullamore Dew whiskey
  • Irish coffee

Where to Stay in Navan: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Ardboyne Hotel

The Ardboyne Hotel is a popular choice for visitors to Navan, thanks to its central location and comfortable rooms. It is close to all the major attractions in the area and has excellent dining options.

The Yellow House B&B

The Yellow House B&B is a charming and welcoming guesthouse that offers a warm and friendly atmosphere. It is located just outside Navan and is the perfect spot for those looking for a peaceful and relaxing stay.

What to Buy in Navan: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Handmade Crafts

Navan is home to many skilled craftsmen and women, producing handmade pottery, knitted goods, and woven textiles. These make for excellent souvenirs and gifts, and can be found in the local markets and craft shops.

Whiskey

Ireland is famous for its whiskey, and Navan is home to several distilleries and breweries that produce some of the finest examples. A bottle of Jameson or Tullamore Dew whiskey makes for an excellent souvenir or gift for whiskey lovers.

Local Culture and Customs of Navan

The people of Navan are known for their friendly and welcoming nature, and visitors should expect to receive a warm greeting wherever they go. The traditional Irish culture is still very much alive in Navan, with music, dancing, and storytelling playing an important role in the community.

Best Time to Visit Navan

The best time to visit Navan is during the summer months, from June to September. The weather is mild and sunny, and there are plenty of outdoor activities and festivals to enjoy. However, Navan is a year-round destination, and there is something to see and do in every season.

Do’s and Don’ts of Navan

Do:

  • Try the local cuisine
  • Take part in traditional Irish music and dancing
  • Explore the surrounding countryside

Don’t:

  • Assume that everyone in Ireland drinks Guinness
  • Fail to dress appropriately for the weather
  • Underestimate the power of the local whiskey!

Safety and Health

Safety in Navan

Navan is a safe and welcoming town, with a low crime rate and friendly locals. Visitors should be aware of their surroundings and take standard safety precautions, but there are no major safety concerns to worry about.

Health in Navan

Ireland has a high standard of healthcare, and Navan is no exception. There are several hospitals and medical centers in the town, and visitors should ensure they have adequate travel insurance before they arrive.

Getting to Navan: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Navan

The nearest airport to Navan is Dublin Airport, which is approximately 45 minutes away by car or bus. There are also several other regional airports in Ireland that may be more convenient depending on your location.

Bus Stations Near Navan

Navan Bus Station is located in the town center and offers regular services to Dublin and other major towns and cities in Ireland. There are also several private bus companies that operate in the area.

Other Options

There are several car rental companies in Navan, making it easy to explore the surrounding areas at your own pace. Taxis and private hire cars are also readily available.

How to Get Around in Navan

Navan is a small town, and most of the major attractions are within walking distance of each other. There are also several public transport options, including buses and taxis, that are easy to use and affordable.

Where to Go Next from Navan: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Dublin

Dublin is just a short drive or bus ride away from Navan and is one of the most popular tourist destinations in Ireland. It is home to world-famous landmarks such as Trinity College and the Guinness Storehouse, as well as excellent dining, shopping, and nightlife options.

Slane

Slane is a charming town located just a short drive from Navan and is home to the iconic Slane Castle. Visitors can take a guided tour of the castle, explore the surrounding countryside, or attend one of the many cultural events and festivals that take place throughout the year.

Conclusion

Navan is an excellent destination for those looking for a mix of history, culture, and outdoor adventure. With its stunning natural landscapes, rich cultural heritage, and excellent dining and accommodation options, Navan has something to offer every type of traveler.
