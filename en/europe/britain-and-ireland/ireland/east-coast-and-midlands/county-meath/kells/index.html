Introduction to Kells

Kells, a small town in County Meath, is located in the East Coast and Midlands region of Ireland. Renowned for its rich history and stunning landscapes, Kells is a popular tourist destination that attracts thousands of visitors from around the world. From top-rated museums and historical sites to scenic views and outdoor adventures, Kells has a variety of attractions to offer.

List of Places to Visit in Kells

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Kells has preserved its cultural and historical heritage through various museums and historical sites worth exploring. Here are a few must-visit attractions:

  • The Kells Heritage Centre
  • St. Colmcille’s House
  • St. Columba’s Church
  • The High Crosses and Round Tower
  • Headfort House and Gardens

Natural Attractions of Kells: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Kells is home to a variety of natural attractions that offer breathtaking views and outdoor adventures. Here are some of the best natural attractions to visit in Kells:

  • Girley Bog Eco Walk
  • Mullaghmeen Forest
  • The Boyne River and Valley
  • Loughcrew Cairns
  • The Hill of Tara

Hiking/Biking Routes of Kells

Kells offers an array of hiking and biking trails that provide a fun and adventurous way to explore the town’s scenic landscapes. Here are some popular trails:

  • The Kells Trail
  • Navan to Kells Greenway
  • Mullaghmeen Forest Trail
  • The Boyne Way Trail

Best Things to Do in Kells: Activities and Tours

Kells has a plethora of activities and tours for visitors to enjoy. From historical walking tours to outdoor adventures, here are some of the best things to do in Kells:

  • Take a tour of Kells Round Tower
  • Explore St. Colmcille’s House
  • Attend the Kells Hay Festival
  • Go fishing on the Boyne River
  • Play golf at Headfort Golf Course

Week-Long Itinerary for Kells

Day 1: Visit the Kells Heritage Centre and explore St. Colmcille’s House and Church.

Day 2: Discover the historic round tower and high crosses of Kells, and take a relaxing walk in Girley Bog Eco Walk.

Day 3: Visit the Loughcrew Cairns, one of Ireland’s best-kept secrets.

Day 4: Hike the stunning Mullaghmeen Forest Trail, or spend a day at Headfort Golf Course.

Day 5: Visit the Hill of Tara, an ancient Irish site that offers stunning views of Meath and beyond.

Day 6: Go on a historical walking tour of Kells, and explore the town’s rich heritage.

Day 7: Take a day trip to the nearby Boyne Valley, where you can visit ancient sites like Newgrange and Knowth.

Local Culture and Customs of Kells

The people of Kells are proud of their Irish heritage and culture, which is reflected in their festivals and events throughout the year. The town celebrates St. Colmcille’s Day in June and the Kells Hay Festival in August, which is the town’s biggest event of the year.

Best Time to Visit Kells

The best time to visit Kells is during the summer months (June to August), when the weather is mild and the town has a lively atmosphere.

Do’s and Don’ts of Kells

Do:

  • Respect the local culture and customs.
  • Take part in the town’s festivals and celebrate with the locals.
  • Try the traditional Irish cuisine and local beverages.
  • Enjoy the scenic landscapes and natural attractions.

Don’t:

  • Disturb the wildlife and ecosystem.
