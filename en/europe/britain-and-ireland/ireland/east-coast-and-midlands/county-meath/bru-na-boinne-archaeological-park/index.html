Introduction to Brú Na Bóinne Archaeological Park

Brú Na Bóinne Archaeological Park is a UNESCO World Heritage site located in County Meath, Ireland. The park encompasses three large passage tombs, Knowth, Newgrange, and Dowth, which date back to the Neolithic period. These structures are over 5,000 years old, making them older than Stonehenge and the Pyramids of Giza.

In addition to the passage tombs, Brú Na Bóinne Archaeological Park has many other historical and cultural attractions. Visitors to the park can explore museums and historical sites, hike or bike along scenic routes, and enjoy a variety of outdoor activities. The park also has a thriving food and drink scene, and visitors can purchase unique souvenirs and local products to bring home with them.

List of Places to Visit in Brú Na Bóinne Archaeological Park

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Visitor Centre: The Visitor Centre at Brú Na Bóinne Archaeological Park provides information on the park’s history and the local area. Visitors can view exhibits and watch a video about the passage tombs.

  • Newgrange Monument: Of the three passage tombs located within the park, Newgrange is considered the most impressive. The monument is aligned with the winter solstice, and during this time the sun illuminates the passage and chamber of the tomb.

  • Knowth Monument: Knowth is the largest passage tomb in the park, and it has two passages that align with the equinox. Visitors can explore the many decorated stones inside the tomb.

  • Dowth Monument: Dowth is the least-visited passage tomb in the park, but it is still worth a visit. The tomb has a cruciform chamber, and there are many decorated stones on display.

  • Hill of Tara: The Hill of Tara is a historical site located a short distance from Brú Na Bóinne Archaeological Park. It was the traditional seat of the High Kings of Ireland and has many important historical monuments.

Natural Attractions of Brú Na Bóinne Archaeological Park: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • River Boyne: The River Boyne runs through the park and offers many opportunities for outdoor activities such as fishing and kayaking.

  • Scenic Walks: The park has many scenic walks that take visitors through beautiful landscape and past historical monuments.

  • Wildlife: The park is home to a variety of wildlife, including otters, foxes, and badgers.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Brú Na Bóinne Archaeological Park

  • Newgrange Farm Walk: This 6km looped walk takes visitors through the historic Newgrange Farm and along the River Boyne.

  • River Boyne Walk: The River Boyne Walk stretches for over 16km from Trim to Drogheda, passing through the heart of the park.

  • Boyne Valley Way: The Boyne Valley Way is a long-distance walking trail that passes through many important historical sites in the area.

Best Things to Do in Brú Na Bóinne Archaeological Park: Activities and Tours

  • Newgrange Farm: This working farm offers tours and activities for visitors, including sheepdog demonstrations and hay rides.

  • Heritage Island Tour: The Heritage Island Tour is a guided tour that takes visitors to the park’s most important historical sites.

  • Night Tour: The park offers a special Night Tour during the summer months that allows visitors to experience the passage tombs illuminated by the setting sun.

Day 7: Depart Brú Na Bóinne Archaeological Park.
