Longford (town) is a picturesque haven that lies in the heart of Ireland’s East Coast and Midlands region. This hidden gem of County Longford is blessed with natural scenic views, rich culture, historic landmarks, and a vibrant food scene. In this comprehensive travel guide, you’ll discover everything you need to know about Longford (town), from places to visit, outdoor adventures, food scene, travel, to accommodations, and local customs.

Introduction to Longford (town)

Longford is a charming town that boasts of being the main trading center in County Longford. The town is located 16 miles from the bustling city of Athlone and only 79 miles from Dublin. Longford town combines a unique blend of Irish historic landmarks and natural beauty. It has a population of 10,000 people, and the main language spoken is English.

List of Places to Visit in Longford (town)

Longford has a lot of places to visit that cater to a wide variety of interests, including history, nature, and culture.

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

● St. Mel’s Cathedral: A historic landmark cathedral that was destroyed by fire in 2009 but rebuilt between 2009 and 2014. The current cathedral houses valuable artworks and gift items presented by its well-wishers.

● Longford County Museum: This museum houses a vast collection of artifacts and archives that take visitors on a journey through Longford’s history and the Irish War of Independence.

● The Old Gaol: A former prison that was in use from 1796 to 1923 and was converted into an interesting museum that tells the grim tales of Irish culture and history.

Natural Attractions of Longford (town): Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

● Corlea Trackway Visitor Center: An interesting museum that tells the story of the world’s largest prehistoric wooden roadway, which was discovered in 1984, and its history from 148 BC!

● Lough Ree: The second-largest lake on the River Shannon boasts of excellent boating options, fishing opportunities, and breathtaking views.

● Ardagh Heritage Village: A captivating village that promises visitors a serene and relaxing atmosphere. The village features the St. Patrick’s Churches and a scenic view of the town.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Longford (town)

The Kilmore GAA and Gawn Forest area offer hiking trails and designated cycling routes that take you through the beautiful landscape of Longford.

Best Things to Do in Longford (town): Activities and Tours

● River Cruises: Boats offer delightful river cruises on the Shannon river, giving visitors a stunning view of the meandering watercourse, while enjoying a quiet and delightful time.

● Zipit Forest Adventures: An adrenaline-hiking outdoor adventure that offers visitors the possibility to climb, swing, balance, leape and zip through the tree-tops.

● Sliabh Bawn Windfarm: This wind farm produces energy from 20 turbines located in the Sliabh Bawn Mountains. Visitors can take a tour guided by local experts to understand the workings of the wind farm and discover the benefits of renewable energy sources.

Week-Long Itinerary for Longford (town)

Day 1: Visit St. Mel’s Cathedral and Longford County Museum. Day 2: Take a trip to Corlea Trackway Visitor Center and the beautiful Lough Ree Lake Day 3: Explore Ardagh Heritage Village. Day 4: Hike along the Kilmore GAA designated trails. Day 5: Visit The Old Gaol Museum and learn about the Irish War of Independence. Day 6: Get your adrenaline pumping at the Zipit Forest Adventures outdoor adventure park. Day 7: Take a guided tour of Sliabh Bawn Windfarm and learn about renewable energy sources.

Food and Drink Scene of Longford (town)

Longford is a town with a thriving food and drink scene, offering visitors a taste of the local Irish delicacies.

Must-Try Dishes

● Longford-style Shepards Pie: A traditional Irish dish that consists of lamb, mashed potatoes, mixed veg, and gravy.

● Longford-made Crubeens: This dish has deep Irish roots, and it involves pig’s feet that are boiled, pressed, and fried, then served with a dip of some sort.

Local Beverages

Longford has an active social scene with many pubs and clubs to suit different moods and tastes. Con’s Drapery pub in Kenagh provides an atmospheric environment to enjoy traditional music and a pint of beer.

Where to Stay in Longford (town): Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Longford town has many accommodations that match different budgets and preferences. Visitors can choose from luxurious hotels such as the Longford Arms Hotel, or more budget-friendly guesthouses like St. Albans.

What to Buy in Longford (town): Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Longford is renowned for its traditional hand-woven tweed products such as caps, jackets and bags, which are made in the region. Visitors can also purchase local artisan craftwork, jewelry, and homemade jams to bring home.

Local Culture and Customs of Longford (town)

Longford is a friendly town, with a rich culture that respects traditional Irish customs.

Best Time to Visit Longford (town)

Summer, between May and September, is the best time to visit Longford, as the weather is warm and pleasant.

Do’s and Don’ts of Longford (town)

Do:

● Greet the locals in a friendly and polite manner. ● Respect Irish culture and customs. ● Wear comfortable shoes and clothing, especially if you plan to hike or bike.

Don’t:

● Litter or disregard the environment. ● Drink and drive or engage in anti-social behavior. ● Engage in dangerous outdoor activities without a guide.

Safety and Health

Safety in Longford (town)

Longford is a relatively safe town. However, visitors are advised to take the necessary precautions when walking alone at night, especially in remote areas.

Health in Longford (town)

Longford boasts a well-equipped hospital and medical center that offers visitors high-quality emergency service 24/7. Visitors are advised to carry their travel insurance to ensure they access medical assistance should the need arise.

Getting to Longford (town): Transportation Options and Logistics

Visitors can access Longford via different means of transport, including air, bus, or train.

Airports Near Longford (town)

The nearest airports to Longford are Dublin Airport and Ireland West Airport Knock, which are 75 miles and 50 miles, respectively. Visitors can hire taxis or use public transport to get to Longford from the airport.

Bus Stations Near Longford (town)

Longford bus station is centrally located near the town center, and visitors can access different bus services that connect to other destinations in Ireland.

Other Options

Visitors can hire a car or use the train to get to Longford.

How to Get Around in Longford (town)

Walking and cycling are ideal ways to explore Longford. Visitors can also hire a car, take a taxi, or use local buses to travel around.

Where to Go Next from Longford (town): Nearby Destinations and Excursions

After exploring Longford town, visitors can visit nearby destinations such as Athlone, Mullingar, or explore the vast Lough Derg lake, which lies nearby.

Conclusion

Longford town offers visitors an unspoiled slice of traditional Irish culture, dramatic scenic views, and a relaxed atmosphere. Visitors can learn about Irish history and culture, take part in outdoor activities and sample the local cuisine.
