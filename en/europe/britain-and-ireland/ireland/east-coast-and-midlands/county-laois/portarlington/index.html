Introduction to Portarlington

Portarlington is a charming town located in County Laois, in East Coast and Midlands, in Ireland. Its proximity to Dublin, the capital city of Ireland, and other major cities such as Cork and Limerick, makes it an ideal destination for tourists who want to experience the beauty and culture of Ireland. Portarlington has a rich history, breathtaking natural scenery, and a bustling food and drink scene.

List of Places to Visit in Portarlington

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Portarlington has a rich cultural heritage, and there are many museums and historical sites for visitors to explore. The Emo Court and Gardens, located just a few miles from Portarlington, is a stunning Georgian mansion set in an 18th-century landscape. It offers visitors the opportunity to walk through the gardens and admire the architecture and design elements of the mansion.

The Rock of Dunamaise, a fortified hill with a commanding view of the surrounding countryside, is another popular cultural site to visit. The ruins of the castle and surrounding walls are believed to date back to the 9th century, and visitors can explore its rich history and take in the stunning views.

Natural Attractions of Portarlington: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Portarlington is surrounded by beautiful natural scenery, from towering mountains to rolling green hills and picturesque valleys. For hikers and nature lovers, the Slieve Bloom Mountains provide stunning views, with hiking trails ranging from easy strolls to challenging climbs.

The River Barrow, which flows through Portarlington, is a popular spot for fishing, kayaking, and boating. Visitors can also take a leisurely stroll along the riverbank and take in the beautiful scenery.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Portarlington

Portarlington is a great destination for hikers and bikers, with a variety of trails and paths to explore. The Slieve Bloom Way, a 72km hiking trail that winds through the Slieve Bloom Mountains, is a popular choice for experienced hikers.

For a more leisurely option, visitors could bike along the scenic Grand Canal, which runs through Portarlington and provides stunning views of the surrounding countryside.

Best Things to Do in Portarlington: Activities and Tours

In addition to outdoor activities, Portarlington offers many other exciting things to do for visitors. The Irish National Stud, located just a short drive from Portarlington, is a must-see attraction for horse lovers. Visitors can explore the stud farm, watch horse training sessions, and take a guided tour of the grounds and gardens.

The Portarlington Golf Club is a popular destination for golfers, with a well-maintained course and stunning views of the surrounding countryside.

For those looking for a more relaxed activity, the Jam Music and Art School offers visitors the opportunity to take music lessons, art classes and join jam sessions with local musicians.

Week-Long Itinerary for Portarlington

With so much to see and do in Portarlington and the surrounding area, a week-long itinerary gives visitors ample time to explore the town and its many attractions.

Day 1: Explore the Emo Court and Gardens Day 2: Visit the Rock of Dunamaise Day 3: Hike in the Slieve Bloom Mountains Day 4: Bike along the Grand Canal Day 5: Visit the Irish National Stud Day 6: Play golf at the Portarlington Golf Club Day 7: Relax and enjoy local music and art classes at the Jam Music and Art School.

Food and Drink Scene of Portarlington

Must-Try Dishes

Portarlington is known for its delicious food, with many local restaurants offering a range of traditional Irish dishes and international cuisine. Visitors should try the renowned Irish stew, a hearty dish made with lamb, vegetables, and potatoes.

Another popular dish is the fish and chips, a classic Irish dish that is usually eaten at the seaside. For a unique experience, visitors can try the local speciality, tripe and drisheen, a dish made with cow’s stomach and blood sausage.

Local Beverages

Ireland is famous for its beer, and Portarlington is no exception. Visitors should try a pint of Guinness, a traditional Irish beer that is famous worldwide. For those who prefer whiskey, Jameson Distillery is just a short drive from Portarlington and offers guided tours of the distillery and a chance to taste their famous whiskey.

Where to Stay in Portarlington: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

There are many accommodation options for visitors to Portarlington, ranging from budget hostels to luxury hotels. The Midlands Park Hotel is a popular choice, with comfortable rooms, excellent service and a range of amenities. The Portlaoise Heritage Hotel is another option, situated just a 15-minute drive from Portarlington, it offers a charming, historic ambiance.

For travelers on a budget, there are many guesthouses and hostels to choose from, such as the Ballyfin Demesne Hostel and the The Killeshin Hotel.

What to Buy in Portarlington: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Portarlington has many shops and boutiques where visitors can buy unique souvenirs and local products. The Portarlington Friday Market is held every Friday, and visitors can find many locally made crafts, jewelry, and food items.

Visitors should also check out the local pottery shop, Kiltully Pottery, which sells handmade pottery items made using traditional techniques.

Local Culture and Customs of Portarlington

Ireland has a rich cultural heritage, and Portarlington is no exception. Visitors should take the time to experience local customs and events, such as a traditional Irish music session at The Thatch Bar. Visitors should also partake in St. Patrick’s Day celebrations, listen to Irish language recordings and visit the local cinema-The Ridge Ace.

Best Time to Visit Portarlington

The best time to visit Portarlington is during the summer months, from June to August when the weather is mild and sunny. The town also hosts many festivals and events during the summer months, such as the Emo Court Concerts and the Waterways Ireland TriAthlone.

Do’s and Don’ts of Portarlington

Do:

  • Do try local delicacies such as Irish stew and tripe and drisheen.
  • Do try the local beer and whiskey.
  • Do explore local cultural sites, such as the Rock of Dunamaise and the Emo Court.
  • Do visit the local pottery shops, such as Kiltully Pottery.

Don’t:

  • Don’t forget to bring warm clothing during winter months, as the weather can be quite cool.
  • Don’t ignore local customs and traditions, such as Irish music sessions and St. Patrick’s Day celebrations.
  • Don’t leave without taking a scenic hike in the Slieve Bloom Mountains.

Safety and Health

Safety in Portarlington

Portarlington is a safe town with low levels of crime. Visitors should take the usual precautions when traveling in any unfamiliar area, such as avoiding flashing cash or valuables, staying in well-lit areas and following the local authorities’ Covid-19 guidelines.

Health in Portarlington

Ireland has a public health system available to all, and Portarlington has a hospital and medical center to attend to any health concerns. Visitors should take care to pack any necessary medications and travel insurance. Visitors may consult with the local medical center or hospital to be up to date with the country’s specific health regulations in regards to Covid-19.

Getting to Portarlington: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Portarlington

Dublin airport is the closest major airport to Portarlington, located about an hour’s drive away. Other airports that offer flights to Dublin Airport include Cork, Shannon, and Knock Airport. There are shuttle services and a bus service to Portarlington.

Bus Stations Near Portarlington

Portarlington has a bus stop that provides services locally. Also, there is a regular bus service from Dublin to Portarlington and from other major cities. Buses Eireann and Dublin Coach both offer routes to Portarlington.

Other Options

Visitors can take a train service from Dublin, Limerick or Cork to Portarlington station. Visitors can rent cars and hire taxi services and drive around the town at their own convenience.

How to Get Around in Portarlington

There are many ways to get around Portarlington, including walking, cycling, or driving. Visitors can also explore the surrounding area by hiring a car or using the Public Transport service – buses and trains also available.

Where to Go Next from Portarlington: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Some popular destinations near Portarlington include the historic town of Athlone, the beautiful countryside of County Wicklow, and the city of Cork. Visitors can take a road trip to these cities or the countryside for a day trip or weekend visit.

Conclusion

Portarlington is a beautiful town with a rich cultural heritage, stunning natural scenery, and exciting food and drink scene. With many festivals, events, and activities happening throughout the year, there is never a dull moment for visitors. Whether you’re a nature lover, a history buff, or just want to relax and enjoy the local cuisine and music, Portarlington has something for everyone.
