County Laois Travel Guide: Discover the Heart of Ireland

Nestled in the heart of Ireland’s East Coast and Midlands, County Laois boasts a unique blend of natural beauty, rich heritage, and friendly hospitality. Whether you’re seeking scenic vistas, outdoor adventures, or a traditional Irish experience, you’ll find it all in this charming region. Plan your trip to County Laois with this comprehensive travel guide, featuring must-see attractions, best things to do, local culture, and practical travel tips.

List of Places to Visit in County Laois

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Rock of Dunamase: A stunning hilltop fortress with panoramic views of the surrounding countryside. The ruins of this ancient castle date back to the 9th century and offer a glimpse into Ireland’s turbulent past.
  • Emo Court House & Gardens: A magnificent neoclassical mansion surrounded by picturesque gardens. This 18th-century masterpiece is a popular spot for weddings, walks, and picnics.
  • Donaghmore Famine Workhouse Museum: A haunting reminder of Ireland’s tragic history, the Donaghmore Famine Workhouse tells the story of the Great Famine and its impact on the local community.

Natural Attractions of County Laois: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Slieve Bloom Mountains: A hiker’s paradise, the Slieve Bloom Mountains offer breathtaking views and challenging trails. Explore the lush forests, hidden waterfalls, and ancient ruins that dot the landscape.
  • The Barrow Way: A scenic walking and cycling route that follows the River Barrow through the heart of County Laois. Experience the tranquil beauty of the countryside and discover charming towns and villages along the way.
  • The Durrow Scarecrow Festival: A quirky annual event that celebrates the artistry and creativity of local residents. Join the festivities and marvel at the colorful and whimsical scarecrows that line the streets of Durrow.

Hiking/Biking Routes of County Laois

County Laois offers a variety of hiking and biking routes that cater to all skill levels and preferences. Some of the popular trails include:

  • The Slieve Bloom Way: A 43km trail that traverses the Slieve Bloom Mountains, offering stunning views and varied terrain.
  • The Barrow Way: A 114km route that runs parallel to the River Barrow, passing through beautiful countryside and historic towns and villages.
  • The Grand Canal Way: A 117km trail that follows the route of the Grand Canal, offering scenic views of the countryside and the locks, bridges, and aqueducts that make up this impressive engineering feat.

Best Things to Do in County Laois: Activities and Tours

  • Ireland’s Ancient East Tour: Take a guided tour of Ireland’s Ancient East and explore the rich heritage of County Laois, including the Rock of Dunamase, the Slieve Bloom Mountains, and the ancient monastic site of Clonenagh.
  • Golfing: County Laois boasts several top-quality golf courses, including the renowned Portarlington Golf Club and The Heritage Golf Club.
  • Irish Whiskey Tasting: Discover the rich flavors and traditions of Irish whiskey with a guided tasting tour at the Kilbeggan Distillery, one of the oldest licensed distilleries in the world.

Week-Long Itinerary for County Laois

Day 1: Arrive in County Laois and check into your accommodation. Explore the local town or village and soak up the warm hospitality.

Day 2: Visit the historic Rock of Dunamase, followed by a hike in the beautiful Slieve Bloom Mountains.

Day 3: Take a guided tour of the ancient monastic site of Clonenagh, followed by a leisurely stroll in the Emo Court House & Gardens.

Day 4: Explore the charming towns and villages along the Barrow Way, including Portarlington, Monasterevin, and Athy.

Day 5: Play a round of golf at one of County Laois’ top courses, followed by a whiskey tasting tour at the Kilbeggan Distillery.

Day 6: Visit the Donaghmore Famine Workhouse Museum and learn about Ireland’s tragic past, followed by a relaxing picnic in one of County Laois’ beautiful parks or gardens.

Day 7: Depart County Laois, feeling enriched and rejuvenated by the natural beauty, rich heritage, and friendly people of this charming region.

Food and Drink Scene of County Laois

Must-Try Dishes

  • Black Pudding: A traditional Irish blood sausage, made with pork, barley, and spices.
  • Colcannon: A hearty dish made with mashed potatoes, cabbage or kale, and butter.
  • Irish Stew: A nourishing stew made with lamb or beef, potatoes, carrots, and onions.

Local Beverages

  • Irish Whiskey: A renowned Irish spirit, made with barley, water, and yeast, and matured in oak casks.
  • Guinness: Ireland’s most famous beer, a dark and creamy stout with a rich roasted flavor.

Where to Stay in County Laois: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

County Laois offers a range of accommodation options to suit all tastes and budgets. Some of the popular choices include:

  • The Heritage Killenard: A luxurious 5-star hotel set amid lush gardens and golf courses, offering world-class amenities and stunning views.
  • Ballyfin Demesne: A grand 19th-century mansion converted into a boutique hotel, with elegant rooms and a stunning lake view.
  • The Killeshin Hotel: A contemporary 4-star hotel in the heart of Portlaoise, with spacious rooms, a fitness center, and a stylish restaurant.

What to Buy in County Laois: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Laois Lace: A delicate and intricate type of lace, originating in County Laois in the 19th century.
  • Knitwear: County Laois is renowned for its high-quality knitwear, made from locally sourced wool and crafted by skilled artisans.
  • Pottery: Discover unique and charming handmade pottery at local studios and shops, including Castle Arch Pottery and Dunamaise Ceramics.

Local Culture and Customs of County Laois

County Laois’ rich heritage and strong sense of community are reflected in its vibrant local culture and customs. Some of the notable traditions include:

  • Music and Dance: Irish music and dance are integral parts of the local culture, with traditional sessions and performances taking place in pubs and community centers.
  • Festivals: County Laois hosts a range of festivals throughout the year, celebrating everything from music and arts to food and heritage.
  • Gaelic Games: Gaelic football and hurling are popular sports in County Laois, with passionate local teams and supporters.

Best Time to Visit County Laois

County Laois is a year-round destination, with each season offering its unique charm and atmosphere. The summer months (June-August) are the busiest time, with longer days, warmer temperatures, and plenty of outdoor events and activities. The autumn season (September-November) features stunning foliage and a quieter pace, while the winter months (December-February) are ideal for cozy activities like fireside chats, whiskey tasting, and Christmas markets. Spring (March-May) is a lovely time to visit, with milder weather, blooming flowers, and exciting festivals and events.

Do’s and Don’ts of County Laois

Do:

  • Do explore the beautiful countryside and natural attractions of County Laois, including the Slieve Bloom Mountains, the Barrow Way, and the stunning gardens and parks.
  • Do interact with the friendly locals, who are always happy to offer tips and insights on the best things to see and do in the region.
  • Do indulge in the delicious local food and drink scene, including traditional dishes like colcannon, Irish stew, and black pudding, as well as Irish whiskey and Guinness.

Don’t:

  • Don’t underestimate the weather, which can be unpredictable and changeable, particularly in the mountains and rural areas. Be sure to pack appropriate gear for the season, including rain jackets, warm layers, and sturdy shoes.
  • Don’t forget to respect the locals, their customs, and their natural surroundings. Be mindful of noise, litter, and other disturbances that can disrupt the peaceful and tranquil environment of County Laois.

Safety and Health

Safety in County Laois

County Laois is a safe and friendly region, with low crime rates and a welcoming community. However, visitors should still exercise common sense and take necessary precautions, particularly when hiking or engaging in outdoor activities. Be aware of your surroundings, carry a mobile phone and map, and inform someone of your itinerary before embarking on any adventure.

Health in County Laois

County Laois has a well-developed healthcare system, with several hospitals, clinics, and pharmacies available throughout the region. Visitors should ensure that they have comprehensive travel insurance and carry any necessary medication or prescriptions with them.

Getting to County Laois: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near County Laois

The closest airports to County Laois are Dublin Airport (80km) and Shannon Airport (120km). From there, visitors can rent a car, book a shuttle, or take public transport to County Laois.

Bus Stations Near County Laois

The main bus stations serving County Laois are located in Portlaoise and Roscrea, with frequent connections to Dublin, Cork, Limerick, and other major cities and towns.

Other Options

Visitors can also travel to County Laois by train, with stations in Portlaoise, Portarlington, and Ballybrophy connecting to major cities and towns across Ireland.

How to Get Around in County Laois

The best way to explore County Laois is by car, with several car rental companies available at airports and major cities. Alternatively, visitors can take public transport, including buses and trains, or book a taxi or shuttle service. Cycling is also a popular option, particularly along the Barrow Way and other scenic routes.

Where to Go Next from County Laois: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

County Laois is ideally located for exploring the wider East Coast and Midlands region of Ireland, including:

  • Kilkenny: A charming medieval city with a rich heritage and vibrant cultural scene, including the Kilkenny Castle, the Medieval Mile, and the lively pubs and restaurants.
  • Dublin: Ireland’s capital city, boasting a wealth of history, culture, and entertainment, including the Guinness Storehouse, the Trinity College, and the buzzing Temple Bar area.
  • Glendalough: A stunning monastic site and natural park located in the Wicklow Mountains, featuring ancient ruins, tranquil lakes, and scenic hiking trails.

Conclusion

County Laois is a hidden gem in the heart of Ireland, offering a perfect blend of natural beauty, rich heritage, and friendly hospitality. Plan your trip to County Laois with this comprehensive travel guide, and discover the best things to see, do, eat, and explore in this charming region. Whether you’re seeking adventure, relaxation, or cultural immersion, County Laois has something for everyone.
