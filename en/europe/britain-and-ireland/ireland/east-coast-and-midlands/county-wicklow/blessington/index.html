Introduction to Blessington

Located in County Wicklow, in East Coast and Midlands, in Ireland, in Britain and Ireland, in Europe, Blessington is a charming town that is known for its picturesque landscapes and historical landmarks. Visitors can enjoy a range of activities, including hiking, biking, fishing, and exploring local museums and attractions. Here is a comprehensive guide to planning your trip to Blessington.

List of Places to Visit in Blessington

Blessington offers a range of attractions for visitors to explore. Here are some of the must-visit places in Blessington.

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Russborough House: This 18th-century mansion features fine art and furniture, as well as beautifully landscaped gardens.
  • Blessington Martello Tower: Built in the early 19th century, this tower offers spectacular views of the surrounding countryside.
  • Tulfarris House and Golf Resort: This former 18th-century mansion is now a luxury hotel and golf course, with stunning views of the Blessington Lakes.

Natural Attractions of Blessington: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Blessington Lakes: These stunning lakes are a popular spot for fishing, boating, and hiking.
  • Wicklow Mountains National Park: This picturesque park offers a range of hiking and biking trails, and is home to waterfalls, lakes, and stunning views.
  • Blessington Greenway: This 6 km trail is perfect for cycling or walking and offers stunning views of the surrounding landscape.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Blessington

Blessington is surrounded by gorgeous countryside and offers a range of hiking and biking routes for all levels.

  • Wicklow Way: This 127 km trail is one of the most popular and scenic routes in Ireland, passing through the Wicklow Mountains National Park.
  • Blessington Greenway: This 6 km trail is perfect for a leisurely bike ride or walk and offers stunning views of the surrounding landscape.

Best Things to Do in Blessington: Activities and Tours

Visitors to Blessington can enjoy a range of activities and tours. Here are some of the best things to do in Blessington.

  • Boat tours of the Blessington Lakes: Visitors can take a boat tour and enjoy the stunning views of the lakes and surrounding landscape.
  • Fishing: The Blessington Lakes are a popular spot for fishing, with a range of fish to catch including pike, trout and perch.
  • Outdoor Adventures: Blessington is a great spot for hiking, biking, and other outdoor activities like zip-lining and archery.

Food and Drink Scene of Blessington

Blessington has a vibrant food and drink scene, with a range of delicious local dishes and beverages.

Must-Try Dishes

  • Irish Stew: A hearty dish made with lamb, potatoes, and vegetables.
  • Boxty: A traditional potato pancake served with a range of toppings.
  • Seafood chowder: A delicious soup made with local seafood.

Local Beverages

  • Guinness: This iconic Irish beer can be found in most local pubs.
  • Irish whiskey: Ireland is famous for its whiskey, and visitors can enjoy tastings and tours at local distilleries.

Where to Stay in Blessington: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Blessington has a range of accommodation options to suit all budgets. Here are some of the best places to stay in Blessington:

  • Tulfarris Hotel and Golf Resort: This luxurious hotel is set in stunning grounds and offers a range of amenities including a spa, swimming pool, and top-class restaurant.
  • The Avon: This stylish hotel located on the shores of the Blessington Lakes offers guests beautiful views, on-site dining, and a range of outdoor activities.
  • Ballyknocken House: This guesthouse offers guests a cozy and homely stay, with comfortable rooms and access to a hot tub.

What to Buy in Blessington: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Visitors to Blessington can bring home some unique and authentic souvenirs, including:

  • Handmade ceramics from Russborough House gift shop.
  • Traditional Irish tweed jackets and caps from local stores.
  • Local whiskey and craft beer from nearby distilleries and breweries.

Local Culture and Customs of Blessington

The people of Blessington are known for their warm hospitality, and visitors are sure to receive a warm welcome. Visitors should be aware of local customs, including:

  • Tipping: It is customary to tip between 10-15% in restaurants.
  • Respect for religion and traditions: Blessington is a predominantly Catholic town, and visitors should respect local customs and traditions.

Best Time to Visit Blessington

The best time to visit Blessington is during the summer months between June-August when the weather is warm and dry, and outdoor activities can be enjoyed comfortably.

Safety and Health

Safety in Blessington

Blessington is a safe and welcoming town, but visitors should take the necessary precautions to keep themselves and their belongings safe.

  • Avoid leaving valuables in the car.
  • Take care when hiking and/or cycling.

Health in Blessington

Visitors to Blessington do not require any specific vaccinations or health precautions, and the town has easy access to medical services.

Getting to Blessington: Transportation Options and Logistics

Visitors can get to Blessington via a range of transportation options, including:

Airports Near Blessington

The nearest airport to Blessington is Dublin Airport, located approximately 50 km away.

Bus Stations Near Blessington

There are several bus services offering links to Blessington from Dublin and nearby towns.

Other Options

Visitors can also drive to Blessington, with easy access from the N81 and M9 motorways.

How to Get Around in Blessington

The best way to get around Blessington is by car or bike. Visitors can also use local taxis to get around.

Where to Go Next from Blessington: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Blessington is ideally located for exploring nearby attractions and destinations. Here are some recommended excursions:

  • Dublin: Ireland’s capital city is only a short drive away, and visitors can explore its rich heritage and vibrant nightlife.
  • Glendalough: This beautiful valley located in the Wicklow Mountains National Park is known for its medieval monastic site and stunning scenery.
  • Kilkenny: This vibrant medieval town offers visitors a range of historic attractions, unique shops, and lively restaurants and pubs.

Conclusion

Blessington is a charming town with a rich history, stunning landscapes, and a friendly local culture. Visitors can enjoy a range of activities and attractions, as well as delicious local food and beverages. Plan your trip to Blessington today and discover all that this beautiful town has to offer.
