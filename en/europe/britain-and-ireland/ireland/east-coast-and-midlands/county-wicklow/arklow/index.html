Are you looking to explore the beauty of Ireland beyond the bustling cities? Look no further than Arklow! This charming town, located in County Wicklow on the East Coast and Midlands of Ireland, has everything from stunning natural landscapes to delicious cuisine and rich history. In this comprehensive travel guide, we’ll show you the best places to visit, things to do, where to stay, and travel tips to make your visit to Arklow unforgettable.

Introduction to Arklow

Arklow is a vibrant town located in County Wicklow, Ireland. It is known for its picturesque location on the mouth of the River Avoca and its rich history dating back to the Bronze Age. Today, Arklow is a thriving tourist destination, attracting visitors from all over the world with its stunning natural landscapes, scenic biking and hiking trails, and rich cultural heritage.

List of Places to Visit in Arklow

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

There are several museums and historical places in Arklow that boast the town’s rich cultural heritage. The Arklow Maritime Museum is a must-visit for maritime enthusiasts. It houses a collection of models, artifacts, and displays that showcase Arklow’s seafaring history. Another must-visit attraction is the 18th-century Avoca Handweavers. Here, visitors can witness the traditional weaving process and shop for beautiful handcrafted textiles.

Natural Attractions of Arklow: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

One of the main attractions of Arklow is its stunning natural beauty. The town is surrounded by breathtaking landscapes, including the Wicklow Mountains and the Irish Sea. The Tomnafinnoge Woods is a fantastic location for hiking and trekking. It is home to a diverse range of flora and fauna, including red deer and wild boars. Visitors can also enjoy water sports such as boating, fishing, and kayaking in the River Avoca.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Arklow

Arklow has a wide range of scenic hiking and biking routes, attracting adventure enthusiasts from all over the world. The Wicklow Way is one of the most famous and popular routes, starting in nearby Dublin and ending in County Wicklow. Another must-visit route is the Tomnafinnoge Woods Trail, which is perfect for nature lovers. The trail offers stunning views of the Avoca Valley, and the woodlands are home to many different species of birds and wildlife.

Best Things to Do in Arklow: Activities and Tours

For those looking for a bit of adventure, Arklow has plenty of activities and tours to offer. The town boasts a range of outdoor activities such as horseback riding, fishing, and kayaking. Visitors can also take part in guided tours of the town, exploring its rich history and cultural heritage.

Week-Long Itinerary for Arklow

For a week-long trip to Arklow, visitors can start by exploring the town’s cultural attractions such as the Arklow Maritime Museum and the Avoca Handweavers. The rest of the week can be spent hiking or biking through the beautiful Wicklow Mountains and enjoying water sports in the River Avoca. Visitors can also take part in guided tours of the town and nearby areas to learn about its rich history and cultural heritage.

Food and Drink Scene of Arklow

Must-Try Dishes

While in Arklow, visitors must not miss out on trying the delicious seafood that the town is famous for. Freshly caught salmon, lobster, crab, and mussels are the highlights of the local cuisine. Local specialties such as the Wicklow lamb roast and beef stew with turnips and carrots should also be on the list of things to try.

Local Beverages

Arklow has an excellent selection of local beverages, including its own local beer brand. Visitors can also enjoy traditional Irish whiskey at local pubs and live music venues.

Where to Stay in Arklow: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Arklow has a wide range of options for accommodation, ranging from luxurious hotels to budget-friendly guesthouses. Some popular hotels in Arklow include The Lighthouse, and The Arklow Bay Hotel.

What to Buy in Arklow: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Visitors can take home a piece of Arklow by shopping for unique souvenirs such as locally made textiles from the Avoca Handweavers. Handcrafted jewelry, pottery, and woodwork are also popular items to take home from Arklow.

Local Culture and Customs of Arklow

The people of Arklow are known for their friendliness and hospitality. Visitors should be sure to respect local customs and traditions, such as the Irish tradition of pub culture and the importance of family ties.

Best Time to Visit Arklow

The best time to visit Arklow is from May to September when the weather is mild and conducive to outdoor activities. However, visitors can enjoy the town’s natural beauty and cultural heritage throughout the year.

Do’s and Don’ts of Arklow

Do:

  • Respect local customs and traditions
  • Explore the stunning natural landscapes and outdoor activities
  • Try the local cuisine and beverages

Don’t:

  • Disrespect local customs and traditions
  • Litter in the natural areas
  • Overdrink and create a disturbance in local pubs and live music venues

Safety and Health

Safety in Arklow

Arklow is a safe town for visitors. However, visitors should be cautious when participating in outdoor activities and follow local safety guidelines and regulations.

Health in Arklow

The health infrastructure in Arklow is well-developed, and visitors should have no concerns about access to medical facilities. However, visitors should ensure that they have travel insurance to cover any medical emergencies.

Getting to Arklow: Transportation Options and Logistics

Visitors can easily access Arklow by air, rail, or road. The nearest airport is Dublin, and there are various bus and train options available from Dublin to Arklow.

Airports Near Arklow

The nearest airport to Arklow is Dublin Airport, which is approximately a one-hour drive from the town.

Bus Stations Near Arklow

Bus Eireann operates regular bus services from Dublin City Centre to Arklow.

Other Options

Visitors can also access Arklow via rail services that operate from Dublin to Arklow and the nearby Wicklow Mountains.

How to Get Around in Arklow

Visitors can rent bicycles or cars to explore the town and its surrounding areas. The town is also accessible on foot, and there is a local bus service available for visitors who want to explore further afield.

Where to Go Next from Arklow: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Visitors who want to explore nearby destinations can take a day trip to the nearby Wicklow Mountains, Glendalough, or Dublin City Centre.

Conclusion

Arklow is a stunning town in Ireland that boasts natural beauty, rich history, delicious cuisine, and charming culture. We hope this guide has shown you the best places to visit, things to do, and travel tips for your visit to Arklow, making your trip unforgettable.
