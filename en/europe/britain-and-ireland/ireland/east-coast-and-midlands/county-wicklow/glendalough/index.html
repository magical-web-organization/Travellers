Introduction to Glendalough

Glendalough, which means ‘the valley of the two lakes,’ is a breathtaking natural site located in County Wicklow, in East Coast and Midlands, in Ireland. The region boasts unparalleled beauty, rich history and culture, and a range of outdoor adventure activities, making it an excellent choice for travelers.

List of Places to Visit in Glendalough

When it comes to tourism, there are two types of travelers- those fascinated by culture, and those drawn to nature. Glendalough offers something for both with its culturually important attractions as well as nature’s beautiful outdoors.

Culturally Important Attractions

Museums and Historical Places

One of the most renowned attractions in Glendalough is the ancient monastic city of St. Kevin. The monastery is the finest example of early Christian monastic settlements in Europe and was founded during the 6th century. Visitors can take a guided tour to learn about the rich history of the monastery and explore the ruins of the Round Tower, St. Kevin’s Church, and St. Mary’s Church.

Natural Attractions of Glendalough

Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

The beauty of Glendalough lies in its natural and scenic beauty which can be seen through many adventure activites such as:

  • The Glendalough Valley hike, which takes travelers through some of the most stunning parts of the area.
  • Cycle Routes, enjoy seeing the area via bicycle.
  • Rock Climbing is also available if one desires something more adventurous.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Glendalough

There are plenty of opportunities for hiking, exploring and taking a scenic cycle into the heart of Glendalough. Wicklow Way is a popular hiking trail that cuts across the mountains and offers scenic views of the two lakes and the surrounding countryside. The nearby Djouce Mountain offers a range of challenging routes for experienced hikers. Moreover, visitors can explore the region on two wheels by taking one of the many cycling routes.

Best Things to Do in Glendalough: Activities and Tours

In addition to hiking, biking and rock climbing, visitors to Glendalough can join a guided tour to experience the sights and sounds of the area. Some popular tours include ring day tours, which take travelers to the best spots in and around Glendalough. Adventures like zip-lining across the valleys of Glendalough are also available for the more adventurous traveler.

Week-Long Itinerary for Glendalough

To explore the best of Glendalough travel plan for a week. Spend some time hiking, cycling, and exploring the ancient monastic city before heading out for a day’s driving trip around the other nearby attractions.

Food and Drink Scene of Glendalough

Glendalough and surrounding areas have a rich culinary tradition, influenced by the neighboring countries. With an emphasis on fresh, local produce there is an array of mouth-watering dishes and drinks waiting to be savored.

Must-Try Dishes

Some of the popular dishes include:

  • Irish stew.
  • Battered fish with a side of chips or fries.
  • Beef and Guinness Pie.
  • Boxty (traditional Irish potato pancakes)

Local Beverages

Some of the local beverages include

  • Pints of Guinness
  • ales and lagers from local breweries.
  • Traditional Irish Whiskey is an absolute must for anyone visiting Glendalough.

Where to Stay in Glendalough

Visitors to Glendalough can find a wide range of accommodation options, including hotels, hostels, holiday homes, and guesthouses. Some popular options include:

  • The Glendalough Hotel: an affordable option that offers comfortable rooms and modern amenities.
  • The Glendalough Youth Hostel: ideal for backpackers and budget travelers.
  • The Wicklow Heather Country House: a charming guesthouse that offers beautiful views of the nearby countryside.

What to Buy in Glendalough: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Glendalough has plenty to offer shoppers who are looking for authentic Irish souvenirs and local products. Some popular items include:

  • Aran sweaters.
  • Handcrafted jewelry and pottery.
  • Traditional Irish music instruments such as a tin whistle or Irish harp.
  • Delicious local food and drink including chocolates, teas, and whiskies.

Local Culture and Customs of Glendalough

The people of Glendalough are known for their warm hospitality and friendly nature. As a visitor to the area, it is important to respect local customs and traditions, which include:

  • Dressing appropriately when visiting religious sites.
  • Greeting people with a friendly ‘hello’ or ‘good morning’.
  • Always asking permission before taking photographs of people or buildings.

Best Time to Visit Glendalough

The best time to visit Glendalough is from April to June or from September to November when the weather is mild, and crowds are fewer and allow you to take in the area’s beauty and adventures.

Do’s and Don’ts of Glendalough

Do:

  • Dress for the weather - ensure that you pack some waterproof clothing for those rainy days.
  • Respect the local customs and traditions - it’s important to dress appropriately when visiting religious sites.
  • Try the local dishes and drinks - the food and drink scene in Glendalough is incredible, so be sure to sample some of the delicious local fare.

Don’t:

  • Littering - always dispose of your rubbish in the marked areas; the local environment and its wildlife are important to preserve.
  • Disturbing the wildlife - the county and its wildlife are vital, important to observe and respect them.
  • Not respecting the cultural sites - always respect its historical and spiritual importance, dress appropriately, and keep the grounds clean and pristine.

Safety and Health

Visitors to Glendalough should be aware of the following safety and health-related information:

Safety in Glendalough

  • The weather can be unpredictable in Glendalough, so be sure to pack appropriately and stay informed of any weather warnings in place.
  • Be mindful of your personal belongings when out and about.
  • Always respect local wildlife as Glendalough is a protected natural area.

Health in Glendalough

  • Visitors should ensure they have valid travel insurance before traveling to the area.
  • The region has quality medical facilities if medical attention is required.

Getting to Glendalough: Transportation Options and Logistics

Visitors can get to Glendalough via numerous transportation options, including:

Airports Near Glendalough

The nearest International airport is Dublin airport which is about 30 miles away from Glendalough and is connected to all major airports in the world. Visitors can take a bus or taxi towards Glendalough once at the Dublin airport.

Bus Stations Near Glendalough

Bus service is an excellent public transportation option for travelers. There are direct bus services available from Dublin, Wexford, and other major towns and cities from around Ireland.

Other Options

For those driving, the N11 motorway connects Dublin with Glendalough. Visitors can indulge in this car journey and also explore the other attractions that East and North Ireland has to offer.

How to Get Around in Glendalough

The best way to explore Glendalough and its surrounding areas is on foot or via bicycle. Public transportation options such as buses and taxis are available.

Where to Go Next from Glendalough: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

For travelers who are looking to extend their stay in Ireland after visiting Glendalough, there are a variety of nearby destinations and popular excursions including the Wicklow Mountains Range, Bray, and the capital, Dublin.

Conclusion

Glendalough’s unique blend of natural beauty, rich history, and culture, and a range of adventure activities make it an excellent choice for travelers. This comprehensive guide provides everything you need to plan your next trip to Glendalough and explore this stunning region’s beauty and enjoy the natural and historical experiences.
