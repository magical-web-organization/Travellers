Introduction to Skellig Michael

Skellig Michael is an isolated island located off the coast of County Kerry, in Southwest Ireland. This stunning island has a rich history and breathtaking natural beauty. It was once home to a monastic settlement, and today, it’s a popular tourist destination for those who want to explore Ireland’s outdoors and learn about its history.

List of Places to Visit in Skellig Michael

Skellig Michael has plenty of attractions to offer its visitors. There’s something for everyone, from culturally significant museums and historical places to exhilarating outdoor adventures and scenic views. Check out our list of must-see places on Skellig Michael:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Skellig Michael Visitor Center: The visitor center set on Valentia Island, not on Skellig Michael itself. At the center, you can learn about the island’s history, culture, and environment before heading out on your adventure to Skellig Michael.
  • Skellig Michael Monastic Settlement: The main attraction and reason to visit Skellig Michael. The settlement was founded in the 6th century and consists of a collection of beehive huts, oratories, and a church. You can take a guided tour of the settlement and learn about the early Christian monks who lived there.

Natural Attractions of Skellig Michael: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Skellig Michael Cliffs: The cliffs offer breathtaking panoramic views of the Atlantic Ocean and surrounding landscape. It provides an undeniably delightful vista with extremes of color, light, and texture from rough to smooth.
  • Skellig Michael Landing Tour: Visitors to Skellig Michael must land on the island via a tour boat. This tour will bring you safely to the island while enjoying the scenic views of the cliffs and sea. You’ll be able to take in the beauty of the rock formations and the surrounding nature during this journey.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Skellig Michael

Skellig Michael is an outdoors wonderland, and there are plenty of options available to hikers and bikers. Skellig Michael is a UNESCO World Heritage site, so the experience cannot be matched by any other place. Below are some of the best routes for those who want to explore the island on foot:

  • Skellig Michael Trail: It’s a strenuous and challenging route but rewards with breathtaking views. You should be careful when going downhill as the trail can be rocky, steep, and slippery.
  • Skellig Michael Loop Walk: This easy walk provides scenic views of the coastline and its fauna. It provides a refreshing and rejuvenating experience for visitors who seek a less challenging route.

Best Things to Do in Skellig Michael: Activities and Tours

Skellig Michael offers plenty of adventure-packed activities that cater to visitors of different age groups.

  • Boat tours: You will be taken around the island for a 2-hour-plus trip, allowing you to spot birds, seals, and dolphins.
  • Snorkeling and Diving: For people who want a close-up of the wildlife below the sea and explore vibrant coral reefs, Skellig Michael won’t disappoint.
  • Kayaking: Paddle among birds’ sanctuary to get close to the famous Skellig Michael Columbretes and the impressive South cliffs.
  • Scenic Flight: If you want a unique view of Skellig Michael, take to the skies and enjoy the beautiful scenery from a bird’s eye perspective.

Food and Drink Scene of Skellig Michael

Skellig Michael has amazing food options for visitors to try, and local cuisine is an experience everyone must have while visiting Skellig Michael.

Must-Try Dishes

  • Irish Breakfast: Start your day with a plate of eggs, bacon, sausage, black pudding, toast, and beans - A hearty breakfast that will give you the perfect energy for a day-long adventure.
  • Irish Stew: This dish is a traditional Irish stew made with beef, lamb or mutton, potatoes, carrots, and onions, cooked to perfection.
  • Scones: Enjoy freshly baked scones with butter, jam, and whipped cream for a midday snack - a perfect end to a great day.

Local Beverages

  • Guinness: Siit in one of the island’s historic pubs and order a pint of Guinness, which is famous all over the world.
  • Irish whiskey: If you prefer whiskey, try a glass of Irish whiskey, with its distinct malty sweetness and smoky aromas.

Where to Stay in Skellig Michael: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Skellig Michael has plenty of options for accommodation, ranging from luxury hotels to budget-friendly hostels. Below are some options:

  • Skellig Michael Hotel: The island’s most preeminent and beautiful hotel.
  • Skellig Michael Hostel: A reasonable option for travelers who are looking for budget-friendly accommodation.
  • Skellig Michael Guesthouse: A mix of both hotel and hostel, this guesthouse is perfect for families.

What to Buy in Skellig Michael: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Skellig Michael offers a variety of souvenirs and products that you can take home to remind you of your trip to this wonderful island.

  • Skellig Michael-themed souvenirs such as magnets, keychains, and postcards.
  • Local handicrafts such as hand-knitted scarves, woolen jumpers, and woolen hats.
  • Skellig Michael chocolate bars, made using local Irish ingredients, are a perfect gift for your loved ones.

Local Culture and Customs of Skellig Michael

The island holds a deep-rooted traditional culture, and the locals are always happy to share their heritage with visitors. Locals typically follow Celtic traditions, which are evident in their festivals and celebrations, such as St. Patrick’s Day.

Best Time to Visit Skellig Michael

The best time to visit Skellig Michael is during the summer months, between June and September. The weather is mild, and the island is vibrant with colorful flowers. Although the summer season attracts more tourists, it’s worth visiting during this time for better visibility.

Do’s and Don’ts of Skellig Michael

It’s essential to be respectful and considerate of the island’s fragile ecosystem and cultural heritage. Here are some do’s and don’ts that visitors should keep in mind:

Do:

  • Dress in layers and bring waterproof gear on your Skellig Michael adventure.
  • Carry any waste with you and help keep the island clean.
  • Follow the directions of your tour guide and attend any safety briefings.

Don’t:

  • Do not go outside of the designated area or disturb the wildlife on the island.
  • Do not litter or throw garbage in the sea or around the island.
  • Do not climb on the historical structures or take away any artifacts or souvenirs from the island.

Safety and Health

When traveling to Skellig Michael, it’s important to prioritize your safety and health for a delightful experience.

Safety in Skellig Michael

  • Follow the instructions given by your tour guide and stay on the designated path to ensure your safety.
  • Carry a first-aid kit and take necessary precautions while doing adventure activities.
  • Stay indoors or wear protective gear like sunblock, sunglasses, and hats to avoid sunburn.

Health in Skellig Michael

  • Make sure you are up-to-date on all your vaccinations before traveling to Skellig Michael.
  • Prepare for changes in altitude and weather to prevent altitude sickness.

Getting to Skellig Michael: Transportation Options and Logistics

Skellig Michael is accessible via the Skellig Michael Landing Tour, which is available from May to October. Here are some transportations options you can choose from while traveling to Skellig Michael:

Airports Near Skellig Michael

  • Kerry Airport: The closest airport to Skellig Michael is Kerry Airport, which is approximately an hour and a half away.

Bus Stations Near Skellig Michael

  • Paddywagon Tours are a good option for travelers who are looking for an organized tour that includes transportation to and from Skellig Michael.

Other Options

  • Private taxis and cars are available from nearby towns.

How to Get Around in Skellig Michael

While Skellig Michael is small, it’s advisable to hire a guide or take a tour for a safe and informed journey.
