Introduction to Kenmare

Nestled on the scenic Ring of Kerry, Kenmare is a charming town that offers a unique blend of natural beauty, culture, and modern amenities. With its idyllic location between the Kenmare Bay and the Caha Mountains, visitors to Kenmare can expect breathtaking views of the ocean, rolling hills, and lush forests. This town is ideal for a relaxing vacation, with an array of activities spanning from hiking and cycling to sightseeing and shopping.

List of Places to Visit in Kenmare

There are plenty of attractions worth visiting in and around Kenmare. Here are some of the top picks for tourists:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Kenmare Heritage Centre: This museum provides an in-depth look into the rich history of the area, from the Bronze Age to present day.
  • Kenmare Stone Circle: This well-preserved circle of standing stones dates back to the Bronze Age and is a must-visit for history buffs.
  • Molly Gallivan’s Cottage & Traditional Farm: Learn about the daily lives of the Irish people from the 1800s, including the art of butter making.

Natural Attractions of Kenmare: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Beara Peninsula: A scenic drive including views of the Atlantic Ocean, Kenmare Bay, and mountains. There are also plenty of hiking and cycling trails available.
  • Gleninchaquin Park: This park offers breathtaking scenery and numerous trails to explore.
  • Kenmare Bay: A perfect spot for watersports, fishing, or simply enjoying a day by the water.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Kenmare

Kenmare offers some of the best hiking and biking trails in Ireland. From challenging mountain treks to leisurely strolls along the coastline, there’s something for everyone. Some popular routes include:

  • Kerry Way: A 200-kilometer long trail that passes through Kenmare and offers stunning views of the surrounding countryside.
  • Old Kenmare Road: A scenic route that takes you through peaceful woodlands and leads to panoramic views over the sea and mountains.
  • Beara Way: A challenging but rewarding trail that covers 196 kilometers of the Beara Peninsula.

Best Things to Do in Kenmare: Activities and Tours

  • Ring of Kerry Tour: This stunning drive showcases some of the most picturesque landscapes in Ireland, including Killarney National Park, Torc Waterfall, and Muckross House.
  • Kenmare Bay Boat Trip: Take a boat tour and admire Kenmare Bay’s wildlife and unique scenery from a different perspective.
  • Kayaking: Discover the stunning coastline of Kenmare Bay with a guided kayak trip.

Week-Long Itinerary for Kenmare

For a week-long itinerary, visitors should consider:

Day Activity
1 Explore the town of Kenmare and learn about its history and culture
2 Hike one of the scenic routes like the Kerry Way or the Beara Way
3 Take a scenic drive on the Ring of Kerry, stopping at picturesque towns and landmarks
4 Enjoy a day by the sea, at Kenmare Bay or Derrynane Beach
5 Visit the Killarney National Park, home to Muckross House and Gardens and Torc Waterfall
6 Take a boat tour of Kenmare Bay, enjoy watersports, or go fishing
7 Spend your last day shopping for souvenirs and tasting the local cuisine.

Food and Drink Scene of Kenmare

Kenmare has a vibrant food and drink scene, known for its fresh, local ingredients, and traditional Irish dishes. Some must-try dishes include:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Irish Stew: A slow-cooked lamb or beef stew that’s hearty and satisfying.
  • Seafood Chowder: A thick and creamy soup made with a variety of fresh seafood, potatoes, and vegetables.
  • Black Pudding: A type of blood sausage made from pork, oatmeal, and spices.

Local Beverages

  • Guinness: Ireland’s most famous beer, brewed to perfection since 1759.
  • Irish Whiskey: A smooth and rich whiskey that’s distilled and aged in Ireland.
  • Irish Coffee: A delicious blend of coffee, Irish whiskey, and brown sugar, topped with whipped cream.

Where to Stay in Kenmare: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Visitors to Kenmare have a range of options for accommodation. Here are some recommendations:

  • Sheen Falls Lodge: A luxurious five-star hotel that offers stunning views of the Kenmare Bay and Sheen Waterfalls.
  • The Kenmare Bay Hotel & Resort: A family-friendly hotel with easy access to town, as well as leisure and spa facilities.
  • River Meadows: A charming bed-and-breakfast that’s perfect for couples or solo travelers.

What to Buy in Kenmare: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Don’t leave Kenmare without picking up some unique souvenirs. Some popular purchases include:

  • Local crafts: From pottery to jewelry, there’s an abundance of artisan crafts available in Kenmare.
  • Traditional Irish clothing: Cozy woolen sweaters, hats, and blankets are perfect for chilly Irish evenings.
  • Whiskey or beer: Bring a bottle of Irish whiskey or a pack of Irish beer to remember your trip.

Local Culture and Customs of Kenmare

The people of Kenmare are friendly and welcoming, with a strong sense of community. Visitors should take note of a few customs and practices:

  • The Irish are known for their love of conversation, so don’t be surprised if you strike up a conversation with a friendly local.
  • Tipping is customary in restaurants and bars, so it’s recommended to tip at least 10-15%.
  • The Irish are known for their love of music, so make sure to check out some traditional music sessions in local pubs during your stay.

Best Time to Visit Kenmare

The best time to visit Kenmare is in the summer months, from June to August, when the weather is mild, and the town is buzzing with activity. However, visitors should keep in mind that summer is also peak tourist season, so prices may be higher, and there may be more crowds. Spring and autumn months can be a quieter and more reasonably priced time to visit.

Do’s and Don’ts of Kenmare

Do:

  • Do try local cuisine like traditional Irish stews, seafood chowder, and black pudding.
  • Do explore the town’s museums, historic sites, and natural wonders.
  • Do bring appropriate clothing for changing weather conditions.

Don’t:

  • Don’t underestimate the weather, always be prepared with proper clothing.
  • Don’t forget to book accommodation in advance, especially during peak tourist seasons.
  • Don’t be afraid to ask friendly locals for recommendations and directions.

Safety and Health

Safety in Kenmare

Kenmare is a relatively safe town, with low crime rates. However, visitors should still take necessary precautions to ensure their safety, such as keeping valuables hidden and avoiding walking alone late at night.

Health in Kenmare

Visitors should ensure they have adequate international insurance coverage before arriving in Kenmare. Medical facilities in the area are generally of a high standard, but access may be limited in rural areas.

Getting to Kenmare: Transportation Options and Logistics

Visitors to Kenmare can arrive by air, rail, or car.

Airports Near Kenmare

The nearest international airports are Cork Airport and Shannon Airport. From there, visitors can take a bus or rent a car to reach Kenmare.

Bus Stations Near Kenmare

Bus Eireann provides regular bus services to Kerry and many other towns and cities throughout Ireland.

Other Options

Visitors can also travel to Kenmare by train or car. The nearest railway stations are in Killarney and Tralee. Visitors arriving by car can take the scenic routes, including the Ring of Kerry and the Wild Atlantic Way.

How to Get Around in Kenmare

One of the best ways to get around Kenmare is by foot, as the town is small and easily walkable. Taxis are also available for longer trips or late-night rides. Visitors can also rent bikes or cars to explore the surrounding area.

Where to Go Next from Kenmare: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

There are plenty of nearby destinations to explore from Kenmare, including:

  • Dingle Peninsula: A stunning coastal area that’s perfect for hiking, surfing, and exploring charming towns.
  • The Skellig Islands: A UNESCO World Heritage site that’s home to ancient monastic ruins and a diverse wildlife population.
  • Killarney: A beautiful town that’s home to historic landmarks like Muckross House and Gardens and St. Mary’s Cathedral.

Conclusion

Kenmare is a picturesque town that’s perfect for a relaxing vacation. With its stunning natural beauty, rich cultural heritage, and friendly locals, there’s something for everyone to enjoy. Whether you’re seeking outdoor adventures or just looking to explore a charming Irish town, Kenmare is an excellent choice.
