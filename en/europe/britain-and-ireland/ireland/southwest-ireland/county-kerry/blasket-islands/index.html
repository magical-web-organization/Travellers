Introduction to Blasket Islands

Blasket Islands, off the coast of County Kerry in Southwest Ireland, is a group of six islands with stunning natural beauty and a rich cultural history. Blasket Islands was inhabited until 1953, and is now an unoccupied nature reserve, giving visitors a glimpse of what life was like in this remote and picturesque part of the world.

List of Places to Visit in Blasket Islands

There are several must-see places to visit in Blasket Islands for both nature enthusiasts and history buffs.

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

A visit to the Blasket Island Centre is essential to learn about the history, traditions, and language of the island. The centre displays artefacts, manuscripts, photographs, and audio recordings that depict the daily life of those who lived on the island. Additionally, the ruins of the homes in the island village offer a glimpse into the past.

Natural Attractions of Blasket Islands: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

The Great Blasket Island is home to a wide variety of wildlife, including seals, dolphins, whales, and seals. It is also home to several bird species, including puffins, fulmar, and petrel. Visitors can take a boat ride to see them up close. Moreover, there are several scenic areas, including Dunmore Head, the highest point on the island, and the beautiful beach of Trá Bán.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Blasket Islands

Blasket Islands offers several options for both hiking and biking. For walkers, Dunquin to the Great Blasket Island offers incredible views of the coastline and the Blasket Sound. The island is also home to several designated bicycle trails, including the Dunquin Loop, which is a circular route around the Great Blasket Island.

Best Things to Do in Blasket Islands: Activities and Tours

Water-based activities are plentiful in Blasket Islands. Take a boat tour or go fishing for some of the freshest catch of the day. Terrifying cliff diving is also offered as an extreme adventure sport. The island also has kayaking, snorkelling, and swimming opportunities.

Week-Long Itinerary for Blasket Islands

Day 1: Visit the Blasket Island Centre and take a boat tour to the Great Blasket Island. Day 2: Take a cycling tour around the Great Blasket Island. Day 3: Spend the day on the Trá Bán beach. Day 4: Take a walking tour of Dunquin. Day 5: Go for birdwatching and seal spotting. Day 6: Enjoy cliff diving. Day 7: Indulge in local cuisine before departing.

Food and Drink Scene of Blasket Islands

A variety of local dishes on the Great Blasket Island is a must-try which ‘An Portán’ is famous for. It serves up delicious seafood dishes such as oysters and lobster, along with traditional Irish stew, and home-made bread. There is also a range of local beers and whiskeys.

Must-Try Dishes

  • Oysters
  • Lobster
  • Traditional Irish stew
  • Freshly baked bread

Local Beverages

  • Local beers
  • Whiskey

Where to Stay in Blasket Islands: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Accommodations in Blasket Islands are limited as it is a nature reserve with no permanent residents. The only hotel on the island is the Great Blasket Island Eco-Hotel, offering eco-friendly accommodations with basic facilities. Moreover, a camping experience is offered by the island centre.

What to Buy in Blasket Islands: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

The Blasket Island Centre gift shop offers a variety of gifts including handmade jewellery, pottery, art, textiles, and literature. However, local handicrafts and souvenirs are equally loved. In addition, a variety of local products, including seaweed, jams, jellies, and relishes may also be bought from the gift shops.

Local Culture and Customs of Blasket Islands

Traditions and customs on Blasket Islands are very much ingrained in Irish culture. The locals love to dance, sing, and play traditional Irish musical instruments like the fiddle and tin whistle.

Best Time to Visit Blasket Islands

The best time to visit Blasket Islands is during the summer months of June to August when the weather is at its best. Moreover, the migratory birds can be spotted during the months of May, June, and July.

Do’s and Don’ts of Blasket Islands

Do:

  • Respect the local customs and culture.
  • Follow safe hiking and biking practices.
  • Dispose of waste appropriately.

Don’t:

  • Remove anything from the nature reserve.
  • Cause damage to the environment.
  • Litter within the area.

Safety and Health

Safety in Blasket Islands

The terrain can be unpredictable, so be prepared for changes in weather, and make sure you have the appropriate gear. Also, remain hydrated and avoid drinking tap water.

Health in Blasket Islands

There are no medical facilities on the islands, therefore be cautious and bring your own supply of medication.

Getting to Blasket Islands: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Blasket Islands

Kerry and Cork airports are the closest to Blasket Islands and are well connected to different international destinations with daily flights.

Bus Stations Near Blasket Islands

Bus services operate from nearby towns to Dingle port, from where a ferry is to be boarded to reach Blasket Islands.

Other Options

Hire a car to reach the Dingle port, or a taxi service can also be availed.

How to Get Around in Blasket Islands

Blasket islands are best enjoyed by foot or bicycle, given the rough terrain and unexplored beauty.

Where to Go Next from Blasket Islands: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

After exploring the mystifying Blasket Islands, you can opt to discover its nearby destinations, like Dingle, a beautiful port town just 12 km away. The port town offers an insight into rich Irish heritage and also provides various cultural experiences.

Conclusion

Blasket Islands is home to an untamed terrain with a rich cultural history and endless opportunities for adventure. The destination offers an unforgettably unique experience for any traveller. With stunning landscapes, local traditions, and delectable cuisine, Blasket Island is undoubtedly one of the most beautiful places to visit in Ireland.
