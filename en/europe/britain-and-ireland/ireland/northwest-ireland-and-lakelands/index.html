Introduction to Northwest Ireland and Lakelands

Northwest Ireland and Lakelands is a picturesque region in Ireland that is relatively untouched by tourism. The region is a magical, off-the-beaten-path destination that offers a unique glimpse into Ireland’s rich cultural heritage and breathtaking natural beauty. The area is home to charming Irish towns, stunning lakes, rugged coastlines, and ancient heritage sites. In this article, we will explore the top attractions to visit, the best hiking and biking routes, must-try local dishes and beverages, accommodations, and tips for a safe and enjoyable visit to Northwest Ireland and Lakelands.

List of Places to Visit in Northwest Ireland and Lakelands

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Northwest Ireland and Lakelands is home to several culturally important attractions, including heritage sites, charming towns, and museums. Here are the top cultural attractions to visit in the region:

  • Sligo Abbey: A 13th-century monastery and burial site of Irish Kings
  • Galway City: A colorful and vibrant city that boasts traditional Irish music and culture
  • Westport: A charming town with stunning Georgian architecture and scenic views
  • Kylemore Abbey: An iconic Irish castle-turned-abbey with breathtaking views of the Connemara Mountains
  • The Museum of Country Life: A museum in County Mayo that showcases the traditional way of life in rural Ireland

Natural Attractions of Northwest Ireland and Lakelands: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Northwest Ireland and Lakelands is a nature lover’s paradise. From rugged coastlines to tranquil lakes, the region offers a variety of outdoor activities for adventure seekers and nature enthusiasts. Here are the top natural attractions to visit in the region:

  • Connemara National Park: A stunning national park with vast open landscapes, mountains, and lakes
  • Benbulbin: A striking flat-topped rock formation located in County Sligo
  • Croagh Patrick: A sacred mountain in County Mayo that offers stunning views of the surrounding landscape
  • The Cliffs of Moher: One of the most popular attractions in Ireland, these majestic cliffs offer breathtaking views of the Atlantic Ocean
  • Lough Key Forest and Adventure Park: An enchanting forest park that offers various outdoor activities such as zip-lining, boating, and hiking

Hiking/Biking Routes of Northwest Ireland and Lakelands

Northwest Ireland and Lakelands is a hiker’s paradise, with its stunning landscapes and natural beauty. Here are the top hiking and biking routes in the region:

  • The Western Way: A magnificent hiking trail that winds its way through the stunning Connemara National Park.
  • Croagh Patrick: A challenging hiking trail that leads to the summit of the sacred mountain in County Mayo.
  • Great Western Greenway: A beautiful cycling trail that follows the old Westport to Achill Island railway track and offers stunning views of the surrounding landscape.
  • The Sligo Way: A stunning hiking trail that takes you through the mountains, lakes, and valleys of County Sligo.

Best Things to Do in Northwest Ireland and Lakelands: Activities and Tours

Aside from hiking and exploring the natural landscapes, there are plenty of other things to do in the region. Here are the top activities and tours to try in Northwest Ireland and Lakelands:

  • Explore the charming towns of Galway and Westport: These two towns are full of traditional Irish charm and offer plenty of opportunities to explore local shops, cafes, and pubs.
  • Take a boat tour of Lough Corrib: This beautiful lake is full of history and mystery and the perfect place for a relaxing boat ride.
  • Visit the Arigna Mining Experience: Explore the fascinating history of coal mining in Ireland in this unique attraction located in County Roscommon.
  • Go fishing in the River Moy: The River Moy is one of Ireland’s top salmon fishing destinations, and there are plenty of local guides who can take you on a fishing trip.

Week-Long Itinerary for Northwest Ireland and Lakelands

If you’re planning to spend a week in Northwest Ireland and Lakelands, here’s a sample itinerary that you can use as a guide:

  • Day 1: Arrive in Galway City, explore the charming town center and enjoy a pint of Guinness in a local pub.
  • Day 2: Head to Connemara National Park for a scenic hike, and stop by Kylemore Abbey for a glimpse of breathtaking views of the Connemara Mountains and Lakes.
  • Day 3: Visit the enchanting village of Cong and explore the Ashford Castle grounds, and take a boat tour of Lough Corrib.
  • Day 4: Visit the Arigna Mining Experience and explore the scenic route to Sligo.
  • Day 5: Explore the stunning coastline of County Sligo, and take a surf lesson or relax on one of the many beaches in the area.
  • Day 6: Hike to the top of Croagh Patrick and take in the stunning views of the surrounding landscape.
  • Day 7: Visit the Cliffs of Moher and head back to Galway City for some last-minute souvenir shopping.

Food and Drink Scene of Northwest Ireland and Lakelands

Must-Try Dishes

Irish cuisine is renowned for its comforting and hearty meals. Here are the top dishes to try in Northwest Ireland and Lakelands:

  • Irish stew: A traditional Irish dish made with lamb or beef, potatoes, carrots, and onions.
  • Boxty: An Irish potato cake made with grated potato, flour, and milk.
  • Seafood chowder: A creamy soup made with fresh seafood caught off the coast of Ireland.
  • Scallops: Fresh scallops are a delicacy in Northwest Ireland and Lakelands and can be found in many local restaurants.

Local Beverages

Ireland is home to some of the world’s best beers and whiskeys. Here are the top beverages to try in Northwest Ireland and Lakelands:

  • Guinness: A dark Irish dry stout that is famous worldwide.
  • Smithwick’s: An Irish red ale that originated in Kilkenny.
  • Irish whiskey: Some of the best Irish whiskeys include Jameson, Bushmills, and Tullamore Dew.

Where to Stay in Northwest Ireland and Lakelands: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Northwest Ireland and Lakelands has a variety of accommodation options to suit every budget and preference. Here are the top accommodation recommendations in the region:

  • Ashford Castle: A luxurious castle hotel located near the village of Cong.
  • Ballynahinch Castle: Another castle hotel with stunning views of Connemara National Park
  • The Quay House: A charming guesthouse located in the quaint village of Clifden.
  • Hostel Connemara: A budget hostel located in the heart of Connemara.

What to Buy in Northwest Ireland and Lakelands: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

If you want to take a piece of Northwest Ireland and Lakelands back home, here are the top items to consider:

  • Aran sweater: A classic Irish sweater made from wool and traditionally worn by Irish fishermen.
  • Irish whiskey: Pick up a bottle of your favorite Irish whiskey to savor at home.
  • Handmade pottery and crafts: Many local shops in the region stock handmade pottery and crafts made by local artisans.

Local Culture and Customs of Northwest Ireland and Lakelands

Ireland is known for its warm and welcoming culture. Here are some of the local customs and traditions to be aware of when visiting Northwest Ireland and Lakelands:

  • The Irish language: Although English is the primary language, Irish is still spoken in some areas of Ireland, including Northwest Ireland and Lakelands.
  • Traditional Irish music and dance: Be sure to seek out a local pub where music and dancing occur, and join the fun.
  • Gaelic football: This traditional sport is incredibly popular in Ireland, so if you get the opportunity to see a game, take it.

Best Time to Visit Northwest Ireland and Lakelands

The best time to visit Northwest Ireland and Lakelands is during the summer months (June, July, and August), when the weather is mild, and the days are long. Fall (September, October) can also be a beautiful time to visit, with the changing of the leaves and fewer crowds. However, be prepared for frequent rain showers, no matter when you visit.

Do’s and Don’ts of Northwest Ireland and Lakelands

Do:

  • Do greet people: Irish people are friendly and welcoming, so be sure to say hello and engage in small talk.
  • Do try the local cuisine: Irish cuisine is hearty and comforting, and there’s something for everyone to try.
  • Do respect the natural landscape: The landscape in Northwest Ireland and Lakelands is breathtaking, so be sure to pick up after yourself and leave no trace.

Don’t:

  • Don’t be offended by teasing: Irish people have a teasing sense of humor, so don’t take things too seriously.
  • Don’t rush: Ireland is known for its relaxed and laid-back attitude, so take your time and enjoy the journey.
  • Don’t be afraid to ask for help: Irish people are always happy to help, so if you need directions or recommendations, ask a local.

Safety and Health

Safety in Northwest Ireland and Lakelands

Northwest Ireland and Lakelands is a relatively safe region to visit. However, it’s always essential to take precautions while traveling, such as locking valuables away and being aware of your surroundings. Be careful when hiking in remote areas and make sure to bring a map and compass.

Health in Northwest Ireland and Lakelands

In terms of health, Ireland has a state-funded healthcare system that provides medical care to all residents and visitors. It’s essential to have comprehensive travel insurance that covers medical expenses and emergency repatriation in case of a severe accident or illness.

Getting to Northwest Ireland and Lakelands: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Northwest Ireland and Lakelands

  • Ireland West Airport Knock: The nearest airport, located in County Mayo, approximately 40 minutes drive from Westport.
  • Dublin Airport: Located approximately 3.5 hours drive from Northwest Ireland and Lakelands.

Bus Stations Near Northwest Ireland and Lakelands

  • Bus Eireann: The national bus company that serves Ireland has several bus routes that connect to Northwest Ireland and Lakelands.

Other Options

Car rental is a popular option for visitors looking to explore the region. It’s essential to be cautious when driving in Ireland, as the roads can be narrow and winding.

How to Get Around in Northwest Ireland and Lakelands

The best way to get around Northwest Ireland and Lakelands is by car or bike. Public transportation options are limited in the region, and many of the top attractions are spread out over a large area.

Where to Go Next from Northwest Ireland and Lakelands: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

If you have time for a side trip, here are some nearby destinations to consider:

  • Dublin: The capital of Ireland is a vibrant and modern city that’s full of history and charm, located approximately 3.5 hours drive from Northwest Ireland and Lakelands.
  • The Wild Atlantic Way: This scenic drive takes you along the west coast of Ireland and offers breathtaking views of the Atlantic Ocean.

Conclusion

Northwest Ireland and Lakelands is truly a hidden gem in Ireland that’s just waiting to be explored. The region’s stunning natural landscapes, charming towns, and rich cultural heritage make it the perfect destination for adventure seekers, nature enthusiasts, and anyone looking for a unique Irish experience.
