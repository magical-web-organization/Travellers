Introduction to Clones

Located in County Monaghan, in the northwest of Ireland and Lakelands in Europe, Clones is a beautiful town that is known for its rich history, scenic landscape, and vibrant culture. With a population of approximately 1,600, it is a small but bustling town that is a popular destination for tourists looking for a taste of life in rural Ireland. Whether you are interested in exploring the town’s culture, history, or natural beauty, you will find plenty of things to see and do in Clones.

There are plenty of activities and tours to choose from in Clones, such as:

Week-Long Itinerary for Clones

Day 1: Explore the Heritage Trail and the Round Tower

Day 2: Visit St. Tiernach’s Park and the GAA Museum, and enjoy an afternoon of walking or biking along the Ulster Canal Greenway.

Day 3: Head to Cassidy’s Slieve Beagh Hotel and Trail Centre for an outdoor adventure, such as hillwalking, horse riding, or clay pigeon shooting.

Day 4: Discover the local cuisine with a food and drink tour, including a visit to Clones Distillery and tasting local cheese at Killyliss Country House.

Day 5: Spend the day fishing at Lough Fea or golfing at Clones Golf Club.

Day 6: Explore the history of the area with a visit to the Maudabawn Cultural Centre and the Clones Motte and Bailey.

Day 7: Enjoy a relaxing day exploring the beautiful Drumlin Trails and taking in the scenic views.

Local Culture and Customs of Clones

Clones is rich in culture and history, with a strong tradition of music, dance, and sport. When visiting Clones, be sure to see a traditional Irish dance performance and attend a local GAA match. It is also important to respect local customs, such as speaking softly and refraining from public displays of affection.

Best Time to Visit Clones

The best time to visit Clones is during the summer months, from June to August, when the weather is mild and the days are long. However, visitors can also enjoy the town’s charms during the winter months, when it is less crowded, and the scenery is beautiful.
