Introduction to County Monaghan

County Monaghan, also known as the “Drumlin County,” is located in the Northwest Ireland and Lakelands region. It shares borders with Northern Ireland to the north, and counties Armagh, Louth, Meath, and Cavan to the east, south, and west. The county is known for its rolling hills, small lakes, and charming towns.

List of Places to Visit in County Monaghan

  • Monaghan Town: The county town is a lovely destination with many historical and cultural landmarks, including the Monaghan County Museum, the Market House, and the St. Macartan’s Cathedral.
  • Castle Leslie: This stunning Victorian castle set amid 1,000 acres of majestic woodlands, gardens, and lakes is a must-visit attraction in County Monaghan.
  • Patrick Kavanagh Centre: Dedicated to the famous Irish poet Patrick Kavanagh, the center exhibits his literary works, personal collection, and memorabilia in an interactive and informative way.
  • Carrickmacross Lace Gallery: This small museum dedicated to lace-making showcases a beautiful collection of handmade lace garments, accessories, and artifacts made in the region.
  • Lough Muckno: A picturesque lake surrounded by woodlands, hills, and gardens perfect for swimming, fishing, boating, hiking, and picnicking.
  • Rossmore Forest Park: A beautiful park with lovely walking trails, cycling routes, picnic spots, and a walled garden with a lake.
  • Patrick Pearse Museum: Located in St. Enda’s Park, Dublin, this museum is dedicated to Patrick Pearse, one of the leaders of the 1916 Easter Rising.

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

County Monaghan has a rich history and culture that can be experienced by visiting several museums and historical places. Here are some of the must-visit cultural attractions:

  • Monaghan County Museum: Located in the center of Monaghan town, this museum showcases the rich cultural heritage of County Monaghan through artifacts, documents, and exhibitions.
  • Patrick Kavanagh Centre: As mentioned earlier, this center celebrates the life and legacy of the famous Irish poet Patrick Kavanagh.
  • Inniskeen Graveyard: The final resting place of Patrick Kavanagh located in the village of Inniskeen.
  • St. Macartan’s Cathedral: A stunning cathedral located in Monaghan town with beautiful stained-glass windows and intricate architecture.
  • Clones Round Tower: A well-preserved round tower built in the 12th century, located in Clones town, that offers stunning views of the surrounding countryside.
  • The Market House: A historical landmark in Monaghan town, the Market House was built in the early 19th century and now houses the Tourist Information Centre.

Natural Attractions of County Monaghan: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

County Monaghan is known for its natural beauty, and there are several outdoor adventures and scenic views that visitors can explore. Here are some of the must-visit natural attractions:

  • Lough Muckno: As mentioned earlier, Lough Muckno is a picturesque lake surrounded by hills and woodlands. Visitors can go swimming, fishing, boating, or just enjoy a leisurely walk.
  • Monaghan Way: A scenic hiking trail that passes through the county’s stunning countryside, from the village of Clontibret to the town of Monaghan.
  • Rossmore Forest Park: A beautiful park with several walking trails, cycling routes, and a walled garden with a lake.
  • Dún na Rí Forest Park: A lovely forest park with a scenic walking trail and picnic areas.
  • Drumlin Trail: A cycling route that takes visitors through the rolling hills and charming villages of County Monaghan.
  • Sliabh Beagh Way: A long-distance hiking trail that passes through the scenic Sliabh Beagh Mountains, with stunning views of the surrounding countryside.

Hiking/Biking Routes of County Monaghan

County Monaghan has several hiking and biking routes for outdoor enthusiasts to explore. Here are some of the must-visit routes:

  • Monaghan Way: As mentioned earlier, the Monaghan Way is a scenic hiking trail that passes through the county’s stunning countryside.
  • Drumlin Trail: This cycling route takes visitors through the rolling hills and charming villages of County Monaghan.
  • Sliabh Beagh Way: A long-distance hiking trail that passes through the scenic Sliabh Beagh Mountains, with stunning views of the surrounding countryside.
  • Ulster Canal Greenway: A scenic greenway along the old Ulster Canal towpath, suitable for both hiking and cycling.

Best Things to Do in County Monaghan: Activities and Tours

County Monaghan offers several exciting activities and tours for visitors to enjoy. Here are some of the must-do things in the county:

  • Kayaking on Lough Muckno: Explore the picturesque Lough Muckno on a kayak or a paddleboard.
  • Golfing at Castle Leslie: Play a round of golf at the stunning Castle Leslie estate.
  • Horseback riding at Castle Leslie: Experience a horseback ride through the beautiful woodlands and gardens of Castle Leslie.
  • Fishing: County Monaghan is known for its excellent fishing opportunities, particularly for pike and brown trout.
  • Food tours: Join a food tour of the county’s local produce and dishes, including the famous Monaghan Black Bacon.
  • Heritage tours: Learn about the county’s rich history and culture on a guided heritage tour.

Week-Long Itinerary for County Monaghan

Here is a sample week-long itinerary for visitors to experience the best of County Monaghan:

  • Days 1-3: Explore Monaghan town and its cultural attractions, including the Monaghan County Museum, St. Macartan’s Cathedral, and the Market House.
  • Days 4-5: Visit the natural attractions of the county, including Lough Muckno, Rossmore Forest Park, and Dún na Rí Forest Park.
  • Day 6: Enjoy an outdoor adventure, such as kayaking on Lough Muckno or hiking the Monaghan Way.
  • Day 7: Experience the food and drink scene of County Monaghan through a food tour or a visit to a local brewery or distillery.

Food and Drink Scene of County Monaghan

County Monaghan has a unique food and drink scene that visitors should not miss. Here are some of the must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Monaghan Black Bacon: A traditional Irish breakfast dish made from cured pork belly with a distinct smoky flavor.
  • Boxty: A potato pancake that is a favorite in the county, filled with various meats and vegetables.
  • Coddle: An Irish stew made with bacon, sausage, and potatoes.
  • Beef and Guinness Pie: A hearty dish made of slow-cooked beef and Guinness beer, topped with mashed potatoes.

Local Beverages

  • Monaghan Beer: Brewed by the Brehon Brewhouse, this craft beer has won several awards for its unique taste and quality.
  • Monaghan Whiskey: Produced by the MiN Di stillhouse, this Irish whiskey is made from locally grown barley and aged in oak casks.
  • Poitín: A traditional Irish distilled beverage made from potatoes or grains, this drink is considered as the “moonshine” of Ireland.

  • Hillgrove Hotel and Spa: A modern four-star hotel with excellent facilities, including a spa, gym, and swimming pool.

What to Buy in County Monaghan: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

County Monaghan offers several authentic and high-quality souvenirs and local products that visitors can bring back home. Here are some of the top choices:

  • Monaghan Black Bacon: This famous cured pork belly is available in local markets and shops throughout the county.
  • Handmade Lace Products: Carrickmacross Lace Gallery sells beautiful handmade lace garments, accessories, and artifacts made in the region.
  • Potato Vodka: Produced by the MiN Di stillhouse, this vodka is made from locally grown potatoes and is available in local shops and distilleries.
  • Craft Beer and Whiskey: Brehon Brewhouse and MiN Di stillhouse both produce high-quality craft beer and whiskey that are a must-try for visitors.

Local Culture and Customs of County Monaghan

County Monaghan has a rich culture and heritage that is worth exploring. Some of the interesting customs and traditions of the county include:

  • Irish Language: Irish Gaelic is still spoken in some areas of the county, and visitors can learn some basic phrases to interact with locals.
  • GAA Sports: The county has a strong affiliation with the Gaelic Athletic Association (GAA) and sports such as Gaelic football and hurling are widely played and followed.
  • Traditional Music: County Monaghan has a thriving traditional music scene, and visitors can experience live performances in local pubs and festivals.
  • Festivals and Events: The county hosts several festivals and events throughout the year, including the Monaghan County Fleadh, the Patrick Kavanagh Weekend, and the Harvest Time Blues Festival.

Best Time to Visit County Monaghan

County Monaghan can be visited throughout the year, but the best time to go is between May and September when the weather is pleasant and most attractions are open. The weather can be unpredictable, and visitors should bring appropriate clothing for rain and wind.

Do’s and Don’ts of County Monaghan

Visitors to County Monaghan should keep the following do’s and don’ts in mind:

Do:

  • Respect Locals: Irish people are friendly and welcoming, but visitors should always respect their customs and traditions.
  • Take Part in Festivals and Events: The county hosts several festivals and events throughout the year, which are a great way to experience local culture and traditions.
  • Drive Safely: Irish roads can be narrow and winding, and visitors should take extra caution while driving.
  • Take Part in Outdoor Adventures: County Monaghan offers several outdoor adventures, including hiking, biking, fishing, and kayaking, which are a great way to experience the county’s natural beauty.

Don’t:

  • Get Drunk and Disorderly: Irish people like to drink, but visitors should drink responsibly and avoid being drunk and disorderly.
  • Trespass Private Property: Visitors should not trespass private property or damage public property.
  • Littering: Visitors should dispose of their waste properly and not litter public areas.
  • Underestimate Weather: Irish weather can be unpredictable, and visitors should always be prepared for sudden changes in temperature and rainfall.

Safety and Health

Safety in County Monaghan

County Monaghan is generally a safe destination for visitors, but tourists should take basic safety precautions to ensure a safe and enjoyable stay. Visitors should avoid walking alone in isolated areas during late hours and stay alert when in crowded places. Visitors should also watch out for road safety, especially when driving on narrow country roads.

Health in County Monaghan

Most visitors to County Monaghan would not face any serious health risks. However, visitors should be cautious while consuming food and avoid eating undercooked or raw meat and fish. Visitors should also carry their required medications and travel insurance.

Getting to County Monaghan: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near County Monaghan

The nearest international airport to County Monaghan is Dublin Airport, which is about 120 kilometers away. However, there are no direct public transportation options to Monaghan from Dublin Airport.

Bus Stations Near County Monaghan

Bus Éireann operates frequent bus services to Monaghan from Dublin, Belfast, Derry, and other major towns and cities in Ireland.

Other Options

Visitors can also hire a car or take a taxi from the nearby airports and bus stations to reach County Monaghan.

How to Get Around in County Monaghan

The best way to get around County Monaghan is by car. Visitors can hire a car from several car rental companies located in Monaghan town. Visitors, who prefer public transportation, can use Bus Éireann services to get to different parts of the county.

Where to Go Next from County Monaghan: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

County Monaghan is well-connected to several neighboring counties and destinations. Here are some of the popular nearby destinations:

  • Lough Erne: A stunning lake in neighboring Fermanagh county, known for its water sports, fishing, and scenic views.
  • Armagh: The city of Armagh in Northern Ireland is known for its rich history, culture, and religious sites such as the Armagh Observatory and St. Patrick’s Cathedral.
  • Dublin: The capital city of Ireland is about a two-hour drive from County Monaghan and offers several attractions, including the Guinness Storehouse, Trinity College, and St. Stephen’s Green.
  • Belfast: The capital city of Northern Ireland is about a two-hour drive from County Monaghan and offers several attractions such as the Titanic Museum and St. George’s Market.

Conclusion

County Monaghan is a beautiful and charming county in the Northwest Ireland and Lakelands region, known for its natural beauty, culture, and history. Visitors can explore its outdoor adventures, scenic views, cultural attractions, and food and drink scene. With this comprehensive travel guide, visitors can make the most of their stay in County Monaghan.
