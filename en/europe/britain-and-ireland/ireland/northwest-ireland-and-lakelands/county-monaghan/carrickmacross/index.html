Introduction to Carrickmacross

Carrickmacross is a charming town located in County Monaghan, in the Northwest of Ireland. Known for its rich heritage and a vibrant community, the town attracts visitors from worldwide every year. With an array of tourist attractions, from historic landmarks to scenic outdoor adventures, there’s no shortage of things to do in Carrickmacross. In this travel guide, we’ll take you through all the best places to visit and activities to indulge in, for an unforgettable trip to the town.

List of Places to Visit in Carrickmacross

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Carrickmacross Lace Gallery

One of the oldest traditions of Carrickmacross is Lace-making, and the Carrickmacross Lace Gallery is the ideal place to discover and learn about lace-making. Visitors can witness the intricate details of the lace, see the stunning collection, and even enroll in lace-making courses.

St. Joseph’s Church

St. Joseph’s Church, built in 1866, is one of the most striking landmarks of Carrickmacross, with stunning stained glass windows and magnificent architecture.

Dun Na Rí Forest Park

A 565-acre forest park, Dun Na Rí offers scenic walking trails, along with fishing, flora, and fauna spotting opportunities. The park also has significant historical importance, with a preserved Ring Fort, where visitors can uncover the town’s history.

Natural Attractions of Carrickmacross: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Lough Muckno

Lough Muckno is an excellent location for water activities such as swimming, fishing, and kayaking. It has beautiful views and a picnic area ideal for families.

Sliabh Beagh

The mountain of Sliabh Beagh offers an invigorating hiking trail with picturesque views of the landscape of the region.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Carrickmacross

The Famine Trail

The Famine Trail is a 9km walk, which takes visitors through the history of Carrickmacross’ Great Famine period, passing through North Louth and South Armagh. En route, hikers can explore the local historical insights and witness the spectacular scenery.

The Monaghan Way

The Monaghan Way is an 80km trail, taking you through the stunning landscapes of North Monaghan, incorporating the magnificent beauty of the Monaghan mountains and forests.

Best Things to Do in Carrickmacross: Activities and Tours

Carrickmacross Golf Club

Golf lovers will find a remarkable 18-hole golf course by the scenic hills of Co. Monaghan.

Carrick Indoor Shooting Grounds

The Carrick Indoor Shooting range provides an indoor shooting experience like no other in Ireland, unlocking an exciting activity for couples, friends and family.

Food and Drink Scene of Carrickmacross

Must-Try Dishes

Carrickmacross is known for its delicious local cuisine with its emphasis on seasonal and fresh produce. Some must-try signature dishes are Carrickmacross trout, Lace pancake, and the famous Carrickmacross Shepherds Pie.

Local Beverages

The town boasts a splendid pub scene with an array of local and international drinks. Try the local beer- Killybawn Craft Beer, which is brewed in the town.

Where to Stay in Carrickmacross: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Carrickmacross Hotels

Shirley Arms Hotel

A four Star hotel with superb brasserie style restaurants, offering guests the perfect retreat in the heart of Carrickmacross.

Nuremore Hotel

If you’re searching for a luxurious stay, the Nuremore Hotel is your go-to destination. Relax in the elegant rooms or the heart of the hotel’s 72 acres of lush greens in the surrounding countryside.

Carrickmacross Guesthouses

Killyliss Lodge

Killyliss Lodge is a charming guesthouse with a warm welcome and a peaceful stay amid the idyllic countryside.

Keef Halla Country House

The Keef Halla Country House is located at the edge of Carrickmacross, providing breathtaking views of the town’s landscape.

What to Buy in Carrickmacross: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Carrickmacross has adorable local shops with a wide variety of handmade gifts. Some popular souvenirs to take back with you are lace-making products, Killybawn craft beer, handmade pottery, and jewelry.

Local Culture and Customs of Carrickmacross

Carrickmacross is a lively and hospitable town, and the people are cheerful and welcoming to tourists. The town celebrates its heritage through the annual Lace Festival, St. Patrick’s Day parade, and other cultural events throughout the year.

Best Time to Visit Carrickmacross

The best time to visit Carrickmacross is during the autumn and spring months when visitors can enjoy the scenic views of monochromatic seasons, with fewer crowds, and mellow temperatures.

Safety and Health

Safety in Carrickmacross

Carrickmacross is a safe destination, and travelers can wander around town with ease. Still, it is advisable to remain vigilant in crowded places and avoid traveling alone at night.

Health in Carrickmacross

There are no significant health concerns in Carrickmacross; however, water safety is essential when engaging in water activities.

Getting to Carrickmacross: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Carrickmacross

The nearest airport to Carrickmacross is Dublin International Airport, located approximately 75km from the town.

Bus Stations Near Carrickmacross

Bus Eireann, the national bus service, operates daily services from Dublin and Belfast to Carrickmacross.

Other Options

Taxis and rental cars are also available from major cities to Carrickmacross.

How to Get Around in Carrickmacross

Carrickmacross is a small town, and visitors can easily navigate their way around town on foot, but taxis and bicycles are also available for hire.

Where to Go Next from Carrickmacross: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Dublin

Dublin, the capital of Ireland, is a must-visit destination, home to a wealth of museums, historical landmarks, and culture.

Belfast

Belfast is another nearby destination that visitors can reach by bus or car, where you can explore the striking city, immerse yourself in Irish culture, and indulge in traditional cuisine.

Conclusion

Carrickmacross has everything a tourist could desire, rich culture, scrumptious cuisine, breathtaking scenery, and history- all rolled into one small town. Plan your trip to Carrickmacross and enjoy the beauty Ireland has to offer.
