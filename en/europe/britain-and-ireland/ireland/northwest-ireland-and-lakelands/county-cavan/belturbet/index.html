Introduction to Belturbet

Belturbet, a charming town in County Cavan, is a hidden gem in Northwest Ireland and Lakelands. Known for its natural beauty, cultural attractions, and outdoor adventures, it is a perfect destination for travelers seeking an authentic Irish experience. The town is steeped in history, and its stunning landscape makes it an ideal place to explore.

List of Places to Visit in Belturbet

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Belturbet has several culturally important attractions that are worth visiting. One of the most notable is the Belturbet Railway Station, which dates back to 1885. A unique aspect of this station is that it has preserved its original booking hall and platforms. Another must-visit attraction is the Belturbet Heritage Trail, which takes visitors on a walk through the town’s history. While on the trail, you will learn about the town’s industrial heritage, the influence of religion, and the role of the railway.

Natural Attractions of Belturbet: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Belturbet’s natural beauty is breathtaking, and there are several outdoor adventures and scenic views not to be missed. For example, the River Erne flows through the town, offering visitors an opportunity to enjoy water activities like canoeing and fishing. Also, the scenic Belturbet Greenway, is a 6km stretch from the town to the historic Crom Estate. This beautiful route offers stunning views of the Irish countryside.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Belturbet

Belturbet has several hiking and biking routes suited for all levels of experience. One of the most popular trails is the Ulster Canal Greenway, which crosses through three counties – Cavan, Monaghan and Armagh, and links the River Shannon to Upper Lough Erne. It is an incredible opportunity to enjoy the scenic Irish countryside while you exercise.

Best Things to Do in Belturbet: Activities and Tours

Belturbet has a variety of activities and tours for visitors of all ages. One of the most popular is the Belturbet Walking Tour, which takes visitors on a guided walk through the town’s most prominent sites, giving insights into its history and local life. Adventure-seekers can also explore the town’s waterways, with boating or fishing trips down the River Erne. If you want to explore beyond Belturbet, there are several tour operators that offer excursions to nearby attractions such as Marble Arch Caves, Ireland’s UNESCO Global Geopark.

Week-Long Itinerary for Belturbet

With so much to see and do, a week in Belturbet is never enough. Our suggested itinerary includes excursions to local attractions, walking tours, and hikes/biking routes. Day 1 - Start the day with a visit to the Belturbet Railway Station before taking a walking tour of the town’s most notable sites. Day 2 - Explore the River Erne with a boating trip, followed by a relaxing picnic on one of its banks. Day 3 - Take a day trip to Marble Arch Caves or the Enniskillen Castle Museum and explore the region’s rich history. Day 4 - Rent a bike and follow the Ulster Canal Greenway to enjoy the beautiful nature around – don’t miss quaint village of Clones. Day 5 - Head to Belturbet Golf Club for a round of golf with your friends or family during the day, and head back for a relaxing evening in the local pubs. Day 6 - Learn more about Belturbet’s heritage by visiting the Old Railway Line Walk and Belturbet Heritage Trail. Day 7 - Explore Belturbet’s many shopping streets and markets before ending the day with a relaxing sunset session at Lough Oughter Castle.

Food and Drink Scene of Belturbet

Belturbet’s food and drink scene is ideal for those who enjoy local, authentic cuisine. The town is home to several excellent restaurants and cafes, but it’s local dishes and beverages that make Belturbet a must-visit destination.

Must-Try Dishes

Some of Belturbet’s must-try dishes include Cavan bacon and pudding, the Irish fry (a full breakfast of eggs, sausages, and black pudding), and Shepherds Pie. Vegetarians will surely fall in love with vegetable medleys or the delicious Irish Stew.

Local Beverages

Additionally, a visit to Belturbet means experiencing its local beverages, and the town does not disappoint. Drumanilra Farm Kitchen is an excellent choice for those looking for organic, locally sourced coffee and tea. The local pubs near the river are great choices for sampling the region’s famous beers and other alcoholic beverages.

Where to Stay in Belturbet: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

While Belturbet is small, it has several accommodation options, ranging from hotels to hostels and guesthouses. One of the most popular hotels is the Slieve Russell Hotel, which offers a range of services, including a spa, golf course, and swimming pool. Other great accommodation options include the Railway Lodge and the Rivers Edge Guesthouse, both of which offer clean and comfortable rooms at affordable prices.

What to Buy in Belturbet: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

When in Belturbet, don’t forget to take home a piece of its culture and local products. You can always pick up a small souvenir from the town’s shops, but the better choice is to get familiar with locally made produce, such as Cavan Creamy Brie Cheese, Ballyconnell Black Pudding or Cavan Crafts.

Local Culture and Customs of Belturbet

Belturbet’s culture is typical of North West Ireland and Lakelands, but its small size offers an intimate experience. A few customs and practices that can help you blend into the local culture include being polite, respecting local customs, learning to speak Irish Gaelic and learning what to order in a pub. Fun fact: Belturbet is celebrating its heritage through Cavan County Museum’s permanent exhibition, focusing on rural life in the town from the early 1900s.

Best Time to Visit Belturbet

The best time to visit Belturbet is between late spring and early autumn, when the region’s mild weather invites visitors to enjoy its many outdoor adventures. Summer days are longer, and the streets and markets are buzzing with activity. If you prefer to enjoy a quieter town, then visiting during less peak seasons, like winter, may be a good choice.

Do’s and Don’ts of Belturbet

Do:

  • Respect local customs and traditions, which vary across towns and villages in Ireland.
  • Speak with locals and ask about their culture and customs for richer travel experience.
  • Visit the local markets to experience the community’s unique products.
  • Bring comfortable shoes for hiking and exploring local landmarks.

Don’t:

  • Over tip unless there is excellent service.
  • Make jokes about the political past of Northern Ireland.
  • Be too rowdy when visiting a local pub.

Safety and Health

Safety in Belturbet

Belturbet is generally a safe, low-crime area, but like any other tourist destination, it’s essential to use common sense and caution. To stay safe, it’s best to lock your doors and windows, not to walk alone at night and avoid flashing expensive electronic devices.

Health in Belturbet

Belturbet has several hospitals and clinics for visitors if needed. If you are travelling from outside of Ireland, be sure to have health insurance that covers travel-related issues. As with any location, it’s always a good idea to get any necessary vaccinations before travelling.

Getting to Belturbet: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Belturbet

There are a few airports near Belturbet, including Dublin Airport, which is about 2-3 hours away; Belfast International Airport, which is about 2 hours away; and Knock International Airport, which is approximately 3 hours away.

Bus Stations Near Belturbet

Bus Services are available from Cavan Town buses, which stop at Belturbet. You can also reach Belturbet by bus from Belfast or Dublin cities.

Other Options

Car rental services are also available at airports and the towns around Belturbet to allow you better leverage exploring this scenic region. If you prefer to avoid self-driving, book a group or private tour.

How to Get Around in Belturbet

Belturbet is a small town, which makes it easy to get around by foot or bike. Additionally, several companies offer car rental services which are perfect for those looking to explore the larger region. Furthermore, there is public transport services by bus.

Where to Go Next from Belturbet: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Belturbet is a great starting point for exploring the region’s other notable destinations. Some of the best nearby towns and cities to visit include Enniskillen, Ballybay, Ballyconnell, and Cavan town.

Conclusion

Belturbet is a hidden gem in Northwest Ireland and Lakelands, offering visitors incredible outdoor adventures, rich history, and delicious local cuisine. It’s the perfect winter getaway or summer vacation destination for those looking to experience Ireland authentically. Whether you’re interested in cultural attractions, natural wonders, delicious food, or simply exploring the town’s hidden gems, Belturbet has something to offer everyone.
