Introduction to Virginia

Located in County Cavan, in Northwest Ireland and Lakelands, Virginia is a charming town known for its beautiful landscapes, rich history, and friendly locals. Nestled amidst rolling hills and surrounded by lakes and rivers, Virginia is a nature lover’s paradise. Whether you’re interested in exploring cultural attractions, embarking on outdoor adventures, or simply relaxing in a quaint Irish village, Virginia has something to offer for everyone.

List of Places to Visit in Virginia

If you’re planning a trip to Virginia, here are some of the top attractions and landmarks to add to your itinerary:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Lough Ramor: This stunning lake is located just a few kilometers from Virginia and offers breathtaking views of the surrounding hills and countryside. Take a relaxing walk along the shore or rent a boat to explore the lake’s many islands and coves.

  • St. Kilian’s Heritage Center: Learn about the rich history and culture of Virginia and County Cavan at this fascinating museum. Exhibits cover everything from traditional crafts and music to the area’s political and social history.

  • Dun A Rí Forest Park: Take a hike through this beautiful forest to discover ancient ruins, stunning vistas, and abundant wildlife. With trails suitable for all ages and skill levels, Dun A Rí is a great place to spend a day in nature.

Natural Attractions of Virginia: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Lough Sheelin: This picturesque lake is known for its excellent trout and salmon fishing, as well as its stunning views. Rent a boat or fish from the shore to experience its tranquil beauty for yourself.

  • Cavan Burren Park: This unique park features ancient megalithic tombs, rock art, and other prehistoric structures. Walk the interpretive trail to learn about the area’s fascinating geology and history.

  • Virginia Golf Club: If golfing is your thing, then Virginia Golf Club is a must-visit destination. Boasting an 18-hole course that was designed by the legendary Eddie Hackett, this scenic course offers a challenge for golfers of all levels.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Virginia

For those looking to explore Virginia’s stunning countryside on foot or bike, here are some of the top routes to consider:

  • The Virginia Way: This 7.4 km trail takes you on a scenic loop through the nearby countryside, passing by historic sites and offering stunning views of Lough Ramor.

  • Cavan Way: This 26 km trail takes you through the heart of the beautiful Cavan countryside, passing through forests, across streams, and over rolling hills.

  • Virginia-Ballyjamesduff Loop: This 42 km bike trail takes you through some of the most picturesque landscapes in the area, passing by historic sites and offering stunning views of the surrounding countryside.

Best Things to Do in Virginia: Activities and Tours

If you’re looking for fun and unique experiences in Virginia, here are some of the best things to do:

  • Traditional Music Sessions: Virginia is known for its lively traditional music scene, and there are several pubs and restaurants in town that offer live music on a regular basis. Check out The Storehouse or The Deck for some excellent tunes and an authentic Irish pub experience.

  • Virginia Festival of the Lakes: Held every summer, this fun and family-friendly festival celebrates Virginia’s unique lake culture with events such as a boat parade, fishing competitions, and live music.

  • Horse Riding: Discover the beauty of the Irish countryside on horseback with a guided ride through the hills and forests near Virginia. Several local stables offer horseback riding tours for all experience levels.

Week-Long Itinerary for Virginia

If you’re planning a week-long trip to Virginia, here is a suggested itinerary:

  • Day 1: Visit St. Kilian’s Heritage Center and explore the town of Virginia.

  • Day 2: Take a hike through Dun A Rí Forest Park and enjoy a picnic lunch by Lough Ramor.

  • Day 3: Visit Cavan Burren Park and learn about the unique geological and historical features of the area.

  • Day 4: Take a bike ride along the Virginia-Ballyjamesduff Loop and enjoy the stunning scenery of the surrounding countryside.

  • Day 5: Spend the day fishing on Lough Sheelin and enjoy a traditional Irish dinner at one of the local pubs.

  • Day 6: Play a round of golf at Virginia Golf Club and enjoy a relaxing afternoon at a nearby spa.

  • Day 7: Take a guided horseback tour through the hills and forests near Virginia and enjoy a farewell dinner at one of the town’s excellent restaurants.

Food and Drink Scene of Virginia

No trip to Virginia would be complete without trying some of the delicious local cuisine and beverages. Here are some must-try dishes and drinks:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Cavan Bacon and Cabbage: This classic Irish dish features bacon and cabbage boiled together until tender and served with potatoes and a creamy sauce.

  • Boxty: A traditional potato pancake that can be served with a variety of toppings, from smoked salmon to melted cheese and bacon.

  • Seafood Chowder: A hearty and delicious soup made with fresh seafood, vegetables, and cream.

Local Beverages

  • Smithwicks Red Ale: This popular Irish beer has a smooth, malty flavor with a hint of hops.

  • Baileys Irish Cream: A sweet and creamy liqueur made with Irish whiskey, cream, and other flavorful ingredients.

  • Irish Coffee: A classic drink made with fresh-brewed coffee, Irish whiskey, and whipped cream.

Where to Stay in Virginia: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Whether you’re looking for a luxurious hotel or a cozy guesthouse, Virginia has plenty of accommodation options to choose from. Here are some top picks:

  • The Virginia Park Lodge: This elegant Georgian mansion offers luxurious accommodations, fine dining, and stunning views of Lough Ramor.

  • Cavan Crystal Hotel: Located just a short drive from Virginia, this modern hotel features spacious rooms, an excellent restaurant, and a relaxing spa.

  • The Lakeside Manor Hotel: Set on the shores of Lough Ramor, this charming hotel offers cozy rooms, a traditional Irish pub, and stunning views of the surrounding countryside.

What to Buy in Virginia: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

If you’re looking for unique souvenirs and local products to bring home from Virginia, here are some top picks:

  • Irish Whiskey: Take home a bottle of Ireland’s most famous drink from Virginia’s excellent liquor stores.

  • Handcrafted Jewelry: Browse the shops in town to find beautiful and unique pieces of handmade jewelry.

  • Irish Wool Sweaters: Stay warm and stylish with a cozy wool sweater, handcrafted by local artisans.

Local Culture and Customs of Virginia

If you want to make the most of your trip to Virginia, here are some important cultural customs and traditions to keep in mind:

  • The Irish Language: Although most people in Virginia speak English, Irish (Gaeilge) is still spoken by some locals and is recognized as an official language of Ireland.

  • Pubs: The Irish pub is an important part of the country’s culture, and Virginia has several excellent pubs and restaurants where you can enjoy traditional music, food, and drink.

  • GAA: The Gaelic Athletic Association oversees a variety of traditional Irish sports and games, including football, hurling, and camogie. If you’re interested in sports, try to catch a local game while you’re in town.

Best Time to Visit Virginia

The best time to visit Virginia is during the summer months of June, July, and August, when the weather is warm and sunny. However, if you’re looking for a quieter and more affordable trip, consider visiting in the shoulder seasons of April-May or September-October.

Do’s and Don’ts of Virginia

To make the most of your trip to Virginia, here are some important do’s and don’ts to keep in mind:

Do:

  • Do learn a few words of Irish: Even if you’re not fluent, attempting to speak the local language can go a long way in demonstrating your respect for Irish culture.

  • Do try the local cuisine: Irish food is delicious and often features fresh, locally-sourced ingredients.

  • Do take in a traditional music session: Virginia has a lively traditional music scene, and attending a local session is a great way to experience the town’s unique culture.

Don’t:

  • Don’t be afraid to ask for directions: Locals in Virginia are friendly and helpful, and are usually happy to give visitors directions or offer suggestions.

  • Don’t forget to bring warm clothing: Even in the summer, the weather in Ireland can be chilly and rainy, so be sure to bring layers and a waterproof jacket.

  • Don’t be too loud or rowdy in pubs: While Irish pubs are known for their lively atmosphere, it’s important to remember to be respectful of other patrons and not to overdo it when it comes to alcohol consumption.

Safety and Health

Virginia is generally a safe and welcoming destination, but it’s always important to take basic safety precautions when traveling. Here are some tips to keep in mind:

Safety in Virginia

  • Be aware of your surroundings: Keep an eye on your surroundings and be cautious when walking alone at night or in unfamiliar areas.

  • Take care when driving: Irish roads can be narrow with sharp turns, so be sure to take it slow and drive cautiously.

  • Follow basic safety protocols for outdoor activities: Whether you’re hiking, biking, or fishing, be sure to wear appropriate footwear and clothing, bring plenty of water and snacks, and inform someone of your plans and expected return time.

Health in Virginia

  • Drink clean water: While tap water in Ireland is generally safe to drink, it’s always a good idea to bring a reusable water bottle and fill up with bottled water when possible.

  • Use sunscreen: Irish summers can be surprisingly sunny, so be sure to wear sunscreen and protect your skin from harmful UV rays.

  • Pack any necessary medications: If you have any essential medications or medical equipment, be sure to pack them in your carry-on luggage and bring enough for the duration of your trip.

Getting to Virginia: Transportation Options and Logistics

Here are some transportation options to consider when traveling to Virginia:

Airports Near Virginia

The closest airport to Virginia is Dublin Airport, which is approximately 90 km to the southeast. From there, you can either rent a car or take a bus to Virginia.

Bus Stations Near Virginia

Bus Eireann operates regular buses to Virginia from Dublin and other major Irish cities. Check their website for schedules and fares.

Other Options

If you prefer to drive, Virginia is located just off the N3 motorway, which connects Dublin to Cavan. Alternatively, you can take a train from Dublin to Cavan and then take a short taxi ride to Virginia.

How to Get Around in Virginia

While it’s possible to explore much of Virginia on foot or by bike, having a car will give you the most flexibility and independence. Rental cars are available at Dublin Airport and other major Irish airports.

Where to Go Next from Virginia: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

If you’re looking to explore more of the beautiful Irish countryside, here are some nearby destinations to consider:

  • Belfast: The vibrant city of Belfast is located just a few hours’ drive from Virginia and offers a lively music scene, excellent restaurants, and fascinating cultural attractions.

  • Cliff of Moher: One of Ireland’s most iconic natural landmarks, the Cliff of Moher is located on the west coast and offers stunning views of the Atlantic Ocean.

  • Galway: Known for its colorful buildings, lively pubs, and charming pedestrian streets, Galway is a must-visit destination for anyone interested in Irish culture and history.

Conclusion

Whether you’re interested in exploring the rich history and culture of Virginia or simply relaxing in a beautiful Irish village, this charming town has something to offer for everyone. With its stunning natural landscapes, delicious cuisine, and friendly locals, Virginia is an unforgettable destination for travelers. We hope this guide has helped you plan your trip and discover the best that Virginia has to offer.
