Introduction to County Cavan

Located in Northwest Ireland and Lakelands, County Cavan is a hidden gem known for its picturesque landscapes, rich cultural heritage, and outdoor adventures. The county boasts of magnificent lakes, lush forests, rolling hills, and charming towns with friendly locals. County Cavan is an ideal destination for history buffs, nature lovers, adventure seekers, and culture enthusiasts.

List of Places to Visit in County Cavan

Here are some of the top places to visit in County Cavan:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Cavan County Museum: A state-of-the-art museum that explores the history and heritage of the county, from ancient times to the present day.
  • Kilmore Cathedral: A stunning Gothic-style cathedral built in the 19th century, featuring intricate stained glass windows and beautiful mosaics.
  • Cavan Burren Park: An archaeological park that showcases a prehistoric landscape with megalithic tombs, stone circles, and standing stones.

Natural Attractions of County Cavan: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Marble Arch Caves: A magnificent underground cave system with a subterranean river and impressive limestone formations.
  • Cuilcagh Boardwalk Trail: A scenic hiking trail that takes visitors through the spectacular Cuilcagh Mountain Park and offers breathtaking views of the surrounding countryside.
  • Lough Oughter Castle: A stunning 12th-century castle perched on a small island in Lough Oughter, accessible by boat.

Hiking/Biking Routes of County Cavan

Cavan is a paradise for outdoor enthusiasts, and there are plenty of hiking and biking trails to discover in the county. Some popular routes include:

  • The Cavan Way: A 26-mile hiking trail that takes visitors through scenic landscapes, forest trails, and rolling hills.
  • Killykeen Forest Park: A spacious forest park with numerous trails for hiking, cycling, and horseback riding.
  • Breffni Loop: A 25-mile scenic cycling route around the breathtaking Lough Oughter, taking you through charming towns, peaceful countryside, and rolling hills.

Best Things to Do in County Cavan: Activities and Tours

  • Cruising on the Shannon-Erne Waterway: A peaceful and scenic way to explore the county’s waterways, passing through numerous locks, canals, and lakes.
  • Golfing: Cavan has several superb golf courses, including the Cavan Golf Club, Farnham Estate Golf Club, and County Cavan Golf Club.
  • Fishing: Cavan is a popular destination for anglers, owing to its numerous lakes and rivers brimming with fish such as trout, salmon, and pike.

Week-Long Itinerary for County Cavan

If you’re planning a week-long trip to County Cavan, here’s a suggested itinerary:

Day Activity
Day 1 Visit Cavan County Museum, followed by a stroll in the historic town of Cavan
Day 2 Explore the Cavan Burren Park and take a guided tour of the Marble Arch Caves
Day 3 Embark on a hike on the Cuilcagh Boardwalk Trail, followed by a relaxing boat ride to Lough Oughter Castle
Day 4 Indulge in adventure sports such as kayaking, canoeing, or rock climbing at the Adventure Centre
Day 5 Spend a day playing golf at one of the county’s best golf courses, followed by a visit to Kilmore Cathedral
Day 6 Go on a fishing trip in one of the county’s lakes or rivers
Day 7 Take a relaxing cruise on the Shannon-Erne Waterway

Food and Drink Scene of County Cavan

Must-Try Dishes

County Cavan has a rich culinary heritage, and some of the must-try dishes include:

  • Cavan Bacon and Cabbage: A classic Irish dish made with locally sourced bacon and paired with boiled cabbage and potatoes.
  • Boxty: A type of potato pancake that is popular in the north of Ireland, often served with bacon, eggs, and black pudding.
  • Irish Stew: A hearty stew made with lamb, root vegetables, and herbs, that is perfect for cold days.

Local Beverages

Cavan is famous for its traditional pubs, and the county has a great selection of local drinks to try, including:

  • Smithwick’s Irish Ale: A smooth, malty beer that is brewed locally in Kilkenny and pairs well with Irish stews and hearty pub fare.
  • Irish whiskey: Cavan has several distilleries that produce excellent Irish whiskey, including Kilbeggan and Tullamore Dew.
  • Local Craft Beers: Cavan has many local brewers producing an array of delicious craft beers that are perfect for quenching your thirst on hot summer days.

Where to Stay in County Cavan: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

County Cavan has several accommodation options for all budgets, ranging from luxury hotels to cozy guesthouses and budget-friendly hostels. Some of the best places to stay in the county include:

  • Farnham Estate Spa and Golf Resort: A luxurious resort that boasts of a world-class spa, golf course, and wellness center.
  • The Cavan Crystal Hotel: A modern hotel that offers spacious rooms, excellent dining options, and a warm Irish welcome.
  • The Old Schoolhouse: A charming guesthouse that is perfect for budget travelers, offering comfortable rooms, free Wi-Fi, and a delicious breakfast.

What to Buy in County Cavan: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

When in County Cavan, you must shop for some unique and locally made souvenirs. Some of the best things to buy include:

  • Handcrafted Knitwear: County Cavan is famous for its high-quality and handcrafted knitwear made from wool or tweed.
  • Traditional Irish Musical Instruments: County Cavan is home to several traditional Irish music makers who create beautiful stringed instruments such as harps, fiddles, and bodhráns.
  • Locally Produced Pottery and Ceramics: Handmade pottery and ceramics are popular in County Cavan, and you can find unique and beautiful pieces to add to your collection.

Local Culture and Customs of County Cavan

Cavan locals are known for their welcoming and friendly nature towards visitors. The county has a rich cultural heritage, and some of the customs and traditions that you might encounter include:

  • Traditional Irish Music and Dancing: County Cavan has a strong tradition of Irish music and dance, and you can often find live performances in pubs and clubs throughout the county.
  • Gaelic Sports: Gaelic football and hurling are popular in the county, and you can catch a game during your visit.
  • Blessing and Celebrating Catholic traditions continue to be prevalent inland County Cavan. Sometimes tourists find themselves invited to a religious celebration during their stay.

Best Time to Visit County Cavan

County Cavan has a mild and rainy climate, making it a year-round destination. However, the best time to visit is from May to September when the weather is warm and pleasant, and the scenery is at its best. The shoulder seasons of March-April and October-November can also be charming with fewer visitors and lower prices.

Do’s and Don’ts of County Cavan

Do:

  • Enjoy the Scenery: Take your time to explore the beautiful countryside and breathtaking views of the county. Make the most of the fresh air and the great outdoors.
  • Try the Traditional Dishes: Don’t leave County Cavan without tasting some of the local dishes like bacon and cabbage or boxty.
  • Be Friendly: The locals are known for being friendly and welcoming, so make sure to say hello and engage in conversations with them.

Don’t:

  • Trespass: Always respect the private property of the locals and make sure to ask permission before entering.
  • Litter: Keep the county clean and tidy. Make sure to dispose of your rubbish in the appropriate bins.
  • Drink and Drive: Don’t drink and drive. Always have a designated driver or use public transportation.

Safety and Health

Safety in County Cavan

County Cavan is safe to visit, and the crime rate is relatively low. However, like any other destination, it is important to take the usual precautions and keep an eye on your belongings, especially in touristy areas.

Health in County Cavan

County Cavan has excellent healthcare facilities, including several hospitals, clinics, and pharmacies. However, it is always recommended to have travel insurance before your visit.

Getting to County Cavan: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near County Cavan

The nearest airports to County Cavan are the Dublin and Belfast airports, both located about two hours away by public transportation or private car.

Bus Stations Near County Cavan

There are several bus stations located in the county that provide local and intercity transportation, including Bus Éireann, Ulsterbus, and Local Link Cavan Monaghan.

Other Options

You can also get to County Cavan by train, with the nearest station located in nearby towns such as Longford or Enniskillen.

How to Get Around in County Cavan

The best way to get around County Cavan is by car, as it gives you the flexibility to explore the county at your own pace. However, there are also several options for public transportation, including buses, trains, and taxis.

Where to Go Next from County Cavan: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

If you have extra time, you can explore nearby destinations such as:

  • Belfast: A vibrant and culturally rich city with plenty of attractions, museums, and excellent dining options.
  • Dublin: Ireland’s capital city, with a rich history, stunning architecture, and lively nightlife.
  • Giant’s Causeway: A natural wonder and UNESCO World Heritage site located on the North Coast of Northern Ireland.

Conclusion

County Cavan is a magical destination that offers visitors a unique and authentic Irish experience. With its stunning scenery, rich culture, and friendly locals, there’s something for everyone in this hidden gem of Northwest Ireland and Lakelands. Follow this comprehensive guide to make the most of your trip to County Cavan!
