Introduction to County Clare

Located in the Shannon Region of Ireland’s west coast, County Clare is a must-visit destination for any traveler exploring the country. The rugged natural beauty and charming towns and villages make it a perfect place to discover unique Irish culture and enjoy outdoor adventures.

List of Places to Visit in County Clare

There’s no shortage of things to do and see in County Clare. Here are some of the top attractions:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • The Burren Center: Offers information and exhibits about the unique landscape of the Burren.
  • Cliffs of Moher Visitor Experience: A must-see attraction with breathtaking views of the Atlantic coastline.
  • Bunratty Castle and Folk Park: A 15th-century castle that offers visitors a glimpse into Irish life in the 19th century.

Natural Attractions of County Clare: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • The Burren National Park: A beautiful protected landscape with unique flora and fauna.
  • Loop Head Peninsula: A scenic drive with stunning views of Ireland’s west coast.
  • Lahinch Beach: A popular spot for surfing and other water sports.

Hiking/Biking Routes of County Clare

County Clare offers visitors plenty of opportunities for hiking and biking.

  • The Burren: Has many hiking trails, including the Burren Way.
  • West Clare Railway Greenway: A scenic biking route along the old railway line.

Best Things to Do in County Clare: Activities and Tours

  • Take a boat tour to visit the Aran Islands.
  • Go surfing at Lahinch Beach.
  • Visit a local pub for traditional Irish music.

Week-Long Itinerary for County Clare

Day 1: Arrive in Ennis and explore the town.

Day 2: Visit the Cliffs of Moher and explore the Burren.

Day 3: Explore the picturesque village of Doolin and take a boat tour to the Aran Islands.

Day 4: Visit Bunratty Castle and Folk Park and enjoy a medieval banquet dinner.

Day 5: Hike the Burren Way or take a guided tour of the Burren.

Day 6: Drive the scenic Loop Head Peninsula and visit the lighthouse.

Day 7: Relax and enjoy the beach in Lahinch or take a surfing lesson.

Food and Drink Scene of County Clare

Must-Try Dishes

  • Irish stew: A hearty lamb and vegetable stew.
  • Irish smoked salmon: A local specialty that’s often served with brown bread and cream cheese.
  • Soda bread: A heavy, rustic bread that’s perfect with butter.

Local Beverages

  • Guinness: A classic Irish stout.
  • Irish whiskey: A smooth and flavorful spirit.
  • Murphy’s Irish Red: A refreshing and malty red ale.

Where to Stay in County Clare: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Old Ground Hotel: A luxurious hotel in the heart of Ennis.
  • Lahinch Coast Hotel: A stunning hotel overlooking the Atlantic Ocean.
  • Doolin Hostel: A budget-friendly option in the charming village of Doolin.

What to Buy in County Clare: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Aran sweaters: Hand-knitted wool sweaters that are warm and cozy.
  • Irish pottery: Beautifully crafted pottery that makes for a unique souvenir.
  • Irish cheese and smoked salmon: Delicious local products that make for excellent gifts.

Local Culture and Customs of County Clare

  • The Irish are known for their warm and friendly hospitality.
  • Traditional music can be heard in many pubs and is an important part of Irish culture.
  • Tipping is generally expected in restaurants and bars.

Best Time to Visit County Clare

The best time to visit County Clare is during the summer months when the weather is warm and the days are long. However, the off-season can be a great time to visit for those looking to avoid the crowds and save some money.

Do’s and Don’ts of County Clare

Do:

  • Try the local cuisine.
  • Attend a traditional Irish music session in a pub.
  • Be respectful of the local customs and traditions.

Don’t:

  • Drive on the wrong side of the road.
  • Forget to bring rain gear, as the weather can be unpredictable.
  • Touch or climb on the Cliffs of Moher, as it can be dangerous.

Safety and Health

Safety in County Clare

Ireland is generally a safe place to travel, but visitors should always take precautions like they would in any other destination. Crime rates are low, and the locals are friendly and welcoming.

Health in County Clare

Visitors do not require any special vaccinations to travel to Ireland. However, it’s always a good idea to have travel insurance in case of unforeseen medical emergencies.

Getting to County Clare: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near County Clare

Shannon Airport is the closest airport to County Clare, and it’s only a short drive away. It’s also well-connected to other major cities in Europe and North America.

Bus Stations Near County Clare

Bus Eireann operates buses that connect County Clare to other destinations in Ireland, including Dublin, Galway, and Limerick.

Other Options

Visitors can also rent a car to explore County Clare at their own pace, or take a train to Ennis and explore the county by bus or taxi.

How to Get Around in County Clare

Public transportation is available in County Clare, but visitors may find it more convenient to rent a car. Biking and hiking are also great options for getting around and exploring the natural beauty of the area.

Where to Go Next from County Clare: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Galway: A vibrant city with great shopping and dining options.
  • Killarney National Park: A stunning park with hiking trails and beautiful scenery.
  • Dublin: The capital of Ireland, with historic landmarks and a thriving cultural scene.

Conclusion

County Clare is an incredible destination that offers visitors a unique blend of natural beauty, rich cultural heritage, and outdoor adventure. With so much to do and see, it’s definitely worth a visit!
