Introduction to Shannon

Located in County Clare, in the Shannon region of Ireland, Shannon is a charming town that boasts a rich history and stunning natural beauty. Founded in the 1960s as a planned town, Shannon’s strategic location near the Shannon Airport has made it a popular destination for tourists visiting Ireland.

Filled with lively pubs, traditional music sessions, and unique cultural attractions, Shannon is a great place to start your Irish adventure. This comprehensive travel guide will help you discover the best places to visit, activities to try, accommodations to stay in, and more in Shannon.

List of Places to Visit in Shannon

There’s no shortage of things to see and do in Shannon. From culturally important attractions to stunning natural wonders, here are the must-visit places in Shannon.

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Bunratty Castle and Folk Park: Explore this 15th-century castle and step back in time in the recreated 19th-century village.
  • Craggaunowen: Immerse yourself in early Irish history at this open-air museum that showcases Bronze and Iron Age structures and artifacts.
  • Hunt Museum: Marvel at the impressive collection of art and artifacts from around the world at this museum, housed in a 18th-century former customs house.

Natural Attractions of Shannon: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Cliffs of Moher: Take in the awe-inspiring coastal views from these towering cliffs that rise over 700 feet above the Atlantic Ocean.
  • Burren National Park: Hike through this otherworldly landscape of limestone rock formations, rare wildflowers, and underground caves.
  • Aillwee Cave: Journey into the depths of this vast underground cave system to discover stunning rock formations, waterfalls, and underground streams.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Shannon

Whether you prefer walking, hiking, or biking, Shannon has plenty of trails to suit all skill levels. Here are some of the best routes to explore.

  • The Burren Way: This 114-km trail takes you through the ancient limestone landscape of the Burren National Park.
  • Michael Cusack Way: This 50-km trail starts in the town of Doolin and takes you through stunning coastal scenery and countryside.
  • East Clare Way: This 172-km trail takes you through the scenic countryside and rural villages of County Clare.

Best Things to Do in Shannon: Activities and Tours

Shannon offers a wide range of activities and tours to keep you entertained during your stay. Here are some of the best things to do in Shannon.

  • Take a traditional Irish cooking class and learn how to make famous Irish dishes like Irish stew and soda bread.
  • Explore the Shannon Estuary on a kayaking tour and catch a glimpse of the resident bottlenose dolphins.
  • Join a guided walking tour of the charming town of Ennis and learn about its rich history and culture.

Week-Long Itinerary for Shannon

If you’re planning a week-long trip to Shannon, here’s a suggested itinerary to make the most of your time.

Day 1: Explore Shannon town and visit the Bunratty Castle and Folk Park. Day 2: Visit the Cliffs of Moher and the picturesque village of Doolin. Day 3: Take a trip to the Burren National Park and the Aillwee Cave. Day 4: Join a cycling tour around the countryside and visit the charming villages of County Clare. Day 5: Discover the rich history and culture of Ennis on a walking tour. Day 6: Explore the Shannon Estuary on a kayaking or boat tour. Day 7: Spend a relaxing day in Shannon town, trying traditional Irish food at local restaurants and pubs.

Food and Drink Scene of Shannon

Shannon offers a great selection of traditional Irish cuisine and international fare. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages.

Must-Try Dishes

  • Irish stew: This hearty meat and vegetable stew is a staple of Irish cuisine.
  • Boxty: This traditional Irish potato pancake is filled with savory ingredients like bacon, cheese, and mushrooms.
  • Seafood chowder: Made with locally caught seafood, this creamy soup is a delicious taste of the sea.

Local Beverages

  • Guinness: No visit to Ireland would be complete without trying the famous Irish stout.
  • Irish whiskey: Sip on a fine Irish whiskey, like Jameson or Tullamore Dew.
  • Poitín: This traditional Irish spirit is made from potatoes or barley and packs a punch.

Where to Stay in Shannon: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Shannon boasts a range of accommodations to suit all budgets and preferences, from cozy guesthouses to luxurious hotels.

  • The Oak Wood Arms Hotel: This family-owned hotel offers comfortable rooms, a restaurant, and a stylish bar.
  • The Bunratty Castle Hotel: This elegant hotel is located near the Bunratty Castle and offers lavish rooms, fine dining, and a fitness center.
  • The Rowan Tree Hostel: This popular hostel offers dorm-style accommodations and private rooms, as well as a cozy common area and a communal kitchen.

What to Buy in Shannon: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

For a unique souvenir from Shannon, consider these local products and gifts.

  • Aran sweaters: These traditional woolen sweaters are made from locally sourced wool and feature intricate patterns and designs.
  • Burren smoked salmon: This delicious smoked salmon is sourced from the nearby Burren region.
  • Irish pottery: Pick up a beautiful locally made piece of Irish pottery, like a ceramic vase or bowl.

Local Culture and Customs of Shannon

Ireland is known for its friendly and welcoming locals, and Shannon is no exception. Here are some unique customs and cultural practices to keep in mind during your stay.

  • Irish people love to chat, so don’t be surprised if strangers strike up a conversation with you in pubs or on the street.
  • It’s customary to buy a round of drinks for your friends or group in the pub.
  • Irish pub sessions can go late into the night, so be prepared for a late night out if you’re heading to the pub.

Best Time to Visit Shannon

The best time to visit Shannon is during the summer months, from June to August, when the weather is mild and sunny. However, this is also peak tourist season, so be prepared for crowds and higher prices.

For a quieter and more affordable experience, consider visiting in the shoulder season from April to May or September to October.

Do’s and Don’ts of Shannon

Here are some important Do’s and Don’ts to keep in mind when visiting Shannon.

Do:

  • Do try traditional Irish cuisine, like Irish stew and seafood chowder.
  • Do visit a local pub and enjoy a pint of Guinness or Irish whiskey.
  • Do take a day trip to the Cliffs of Moher and the picturesque village of Doolin.

Don’t:

  • Don’t forget to tip your server in restaurants or bartenders in pubs.
  • Don’t rush through the scenic countryside of County Clare, take your time to soak in the stunning views.
  • Don’t forget to pack warm and waterproof clothing, as the weather can be unpredictable.

Safety and Health

Shannon is a safe place to visit, but it’s always important to take the necessary precautions to ensure your safety and health.

Safety in Shannon

  • Be mindful of traffic when crossing the street, especially in busy areas.
  • Keep an eye on your valuables and be aware of pickpockets in crowded areas.
  • Check the weather forecast and plan your outdoor activities accordingly.

Health in Shannon

  • Bring any necessary prescription medications with you, and make sure you have travel insurance in case of emergencies.
  • Drink bottled water to avoid getting sick from the local tap water.
  • If you have any allergies or dietary restrictions, make sure to inform your server when dining out.

Getting to Shannon: Transportation Options and Logistics

Shannon is easily accessible by air, road, and rail. Here are some transportation options to consider when planning your trip to Shannon.

Airports Near Shannon

  • Shannon Airport: This airport offers daily flights to major cities in Europe and the United States.

Bus Stations Near Shannon

  • Shannon Town Centre: Regular bus services connect Shannon to nearby towns and attractions.

Other Options

  • Car rental: Renting a car is a great way to explore Shannon and the surrounding countryside at your own pace.
  • Rail: The nearest train station is in Ennis, a 20-minute drive from Shannon.

How to Get Around in Shannon

Shannon is a compact town that you can easily explore on foot or by bike. If you’re planning to visit nearby attractions and towns, renting a car is recommended. Public transportation options are also available and include bus and train services.

Where to Go Next from Shannon: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

If you’re looking to explore more of Ireland, there are plenty of nearby destinations and excursions to consider.

  • Galway city: Explore the charming streets of this vibrant city and immerse yourself in traditional Irish culture and music.
  • Killarney National Park: Hike through this stunning national park and experience the beauty of the Irish countryside.
  • Dingle Peninsula: Discover the rugged coastal scenery, charming villages, and Gaelic language on this spectacular peninsula.

Conclusion

Shannon is a hidden gem of Ireland that offers a unique blend of history, culture, and natural beauty. From exploring ancient castles to sampling traditional Irish cuisine, there’s something for everyone in Shannon. Use this comprehensive travel guide to plan your perfect Irish adventure in Shannon.
