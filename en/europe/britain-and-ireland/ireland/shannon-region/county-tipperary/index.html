Introduction to County Tipperary

County Tipperary is located in the Shannon Region in Ireland and is home to a variety of cultural and natural attractions that make it a must-visit destination. The county is steeped in history, with many castles and ancient ruins, and is dotted with charming towns and villages. Additionally, visitors can explore natural wonders such as the majestic Glen of Aherlow and the stunning Rock of Cashel. Whether you’re interested in history, nature, or outdoor adventures, County Tipperary has something for everyone.

List of Places to Visit in County Tipperary

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Rock of Cashel

One of the most iconic landmarks in Ireland, the Rock of Cashel is an impressive cluster of medieval buildings situated on a rocky outcrop. This ancient fortress was once the seat of the Kings of Munster and is steeped in history dating back to the 12th century. The complex includes a round tower, a Gothic cathedral, and a 15th-century castle.

Cahir Castle

Built in the 12th century, Cahir Castle is one of the largest and best-preserved castles in Ireland. Visitors can take a guided tour of the castle and explore its various defensive features, such as the drawbridge, towers, and gun ports.

Clonmel County Museum

Located in the town of Clonmel, the Clonmel County Museum provides insight into the history and heritage of County Tipperary. The museum houses a collection of artifacts related to local history, including a display on the Great Famine and the War of Independence.

Natural Attractions of County Tipperary: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

The Glen of Aherlow

Nestled between the Galtee Mountains and the Slievenamuck Ridge, the Glen of Aherlow is one of the most scenic areas in Ireland. Visitors can explore walking trails or go horseback riding and admire the breathtaking views of rolling hills, lush greenery, and cascading waterfalls.

Lough Derg

Lough Derg is the third-largest lake in Ireland and is famous for its fishing and sailing opportunities. Visitors can take a boat trip on the lake, go kayaking or canoeing, and enjoy the stunning scenery of the surrounding countryside.

Devil’s Bit Mountain

Located in the north of County Tipperary, Devil’s Bit Mountain is a popular hiking spot thanks to its breathtaking views and well-marked trails. Visitors can embark on hikes of varying difficulties, from easy walks to challenging treks that take you up to the summit.

Hiking/Biking Routes of County Tipperary

County Tipperary is a popular destination for hikers and cyclists alike, with many beautiful trails that offer stunning views. Some of the most popular routes include:

  • East Munster Way: a long-distance walking trail that covers 70 miles and takes in the scenic countryside and charming towns of County Tipperary.
  • Ormond Way: a 50-mile walking and cycling trail that runs through picturesque villages and rolling hills.
  • Slieve Felim Way: a 26-mile trail that takes you through the Slieve Felim Mountains and offers breathtaking views of the surrounding countryside.

Best Things to Do in County Tipperary: Activities and Tours

Discover the history of the county on a walking tour

Take a guided walking tour of iconic sites, such as Cahir Castle, and get up close and personal with the history of County Tipperary.

Enjoy a traditional music performance in a local pub

Take in the lively atmosphere of a local pub while experiencing traditional Irish music played live.

Discover Irish whiskey making with a distillery tour

Take a fascinating tour of a distillery and learn about the history and production of Ireland’s famous whiskey.

Week-Long Itinerary for County Tipperary

Day 1: Explore the town of Cashel and visit the famous Rock of Cashel. Day 2: Visit the Cahir Castle and take a boat trip on the River Suir. Day 3: Visit the Clonmel County Museum and go for a hike in the Comeragh Mountains. Day 4: Take a scenic drive through the Glen of Aherlow and visit the upper lake of Lough Derg. Day 5: Visit the Swiss Cottage in Cahir and take a guided tour of the Kilcash Castle ruins. Day 6: Take a distillery tour at the Jameson Experience in Midleton. Day 7: Go hiking on the Devil’s Bit Mountain and enjoy breathtaking views of County Tipperary.

Food and Drink Scene of County Tipperary

Must-Try Dishes

  • Irish Stew: A classic dish made with lamb, root vegetables, and hearty broth.
  • Black Pudding: A sausage made from pork and blood that’s commonly served for breakfast in Ireland.
  • Boxty: A traditional Irish potato pancake served with a variety of toppings like bacon and eggs.

Local Beverages

  • Guinness: A traditional Irish stout beer with a unique taste and smooth texture.
  • Irish Whiskey: A famous whiskey produced in Ireland with a distinct taste and aroma.

Local Culture and Customs of County Tipperary

The local culture in County Tipperary is deeply rooted in its rich history and traditions. Irish music and dance are an essential part of everyday life, and visitors can experience it firsthand at local festivals and events. The Irish also take pride in their hospitality and warmth towards visitors.

Best Time to Visit County Tipperary

The best time to visit County Tipperary is between May and September when the weather is warm and pleasant. Additionally, many festivals and events take place during this time, making it a lively and exciting time to visit.

Do’s and Don’ts of County Tipperary

Do:

  • Do Try the Traditional Irish Cuisine: Irish food is delicious, and sampling some of the local delicacies is an essential experience.
  • Do Learn a Bit of Irish: It’s a good idea to learn some basic phrases in Irish to communicate with the locals.
  • Do Respect the Local Culture and Customs: The Irish take pride in their heritage, and it’s important to show respect for their traditions and customs.

Don’t:

  • Don’t Drink and Drive: It’s illegal and dangerous to drink and drive in County Tipperary.
  • Don’t Forget to Tip: Tipping is customary in Ireland, and it’s a good idea to tip between 10% to 15% in restaurants and cafes.
  • Don’t Leave Without Seeing the Scenic Views: County Tipperary is home to some of the most beautiful scenery in Ireland, and it’s worth taking the time to see it.

Safety and Health

Safety in County Tipperary

County Tipperary is generally a safe place to visit, although visitors should take common sense precautions such as avoiding isolated areas at night and keeping a watchful eye on valuables in crowded areas.

Health in County Tipperary

There are no specific health concerns in County Tipperary, and visitors should ensure they have adequate health insurance before traveling to Ireland. It’s also a good idea to pack sunscreen and insect repellent, especially during the summer months.

Getting to County Tipperary: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near County Tipperary

The nearest major airport to County Tipperary is Shannon Airport, which has regular flights from the UK and Europe.

Bus Stations Near County Tipperary

Bus Eireann operates regular bus services throughout County Tipperary and connects it with other major towns and cities in Ireland.

Other Options

Visitors can also get to County Tipperary by train, with frequent services from Dublin and other major Irish cities.

How to Get Around in County Tipperary

The best way to explore County Tipperary is by car as it offers maximum flexibility and allows you to visit remote areas. Alternatively, you can use taxis, buses, and trains to get around.

Where to Go Next from County Tipperary: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Limerick

The city of Limerick is just a short distance from County Tipperary and is known for its historic buildings, museums, and galleries.

Waterford

The city of Waterford is one of Ireland’s oldest cities, dating back to Viking times. Visitors can explore the town’s rich history and take in the stunning views of the River Suir.

Conclusion

County Tipperary is a stunning destination that offers a unique blend of history, culture, and natural beauty. Visitors can explore ancient ruins, take scenic hikes, enjoy traditional Irish music and dance, and indulge in delicious local cuisine. With so much to see and do, it’s no wonder County Tipperary is one of Ireland’s most popular destinations.
