Introduction to Clonmel

Located in Shannon Region of Ireland, Clonmel is County Tipperary’s largest town, situated along the River Suir. With a rich history dating back to the 13th century, Clonmel is a charming town that offers plenty of sights and activities for visitors. This comprehensive travel guide will help you explore the best places to visit, things to do, where to stay, and helpful tips for your trip to Clonmel.

List of Places to Visit in Clonmel

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Clonmel County Museum - Explore the town’s history from the medieval period to the present-day.
  • Comeragh Mountains - This range of mountains is a designated park with beautiful views and nature trails.
  • St. Mary’s Church of Ireland - This beautiful 13th-century church is still in use today and holds regular services.
  • Ormonde Castle - The castle was built in the 16th century and it’s now a popular venue for weddings and events.
  • Old St. Mary’s Church and Graveyard - A beautiful cemetery with plenty of old gravestones, it is also a popular picnic spot.

Natural Attractions of Clonmel: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Glenary Forest Walk - A beautiful walk through a forest, with stunning views of the area.
  • Mount Mellary Abbey - A peaceful abbey to visit for a spiritual retreat.
  • Suicin Bridge - A beautiful walkway across the River Suir that gives beautiful views of the surroundings.
  • Clonmel Park - A large public park with plenty of lawns and a children’s playground
  • The Greenway - A cycling and walking route from Dungarvan to Clonea, passing through Clonmel.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Clonmel

  • Comeragh Mountains - A great place to explore for hikers, with plenty of well-marked routes.
  • Knockmealdown Mountains - A mountain range that offers some of the best trails in Ireland.
  • Suicin Bridge and River Walk - A moderate-level walk that gives stunning views of the River Suir and Clonmel town.

Best Things to Do in Clonmel: Activities and Tours

  • Visit the Main Street - A vibrant street full of shops, restaurants, and pubs.
  • Go Horseback Riding - There are several options for horseback riding in the area.
  • Visit Tipperary Excel - This cultural center combines a cinema, theater, art gallery, and heritage center.
  • Take a Boat Tour on the River Suir - A fun way to explore the countryside and the town.

Week-Long Itinerary for Clonmel

Day Activities
1 Visit Clonmel Country Museum and spend some time exploring the town.
2 Visit Old St. Mary’s Church and Graveyard. In the afternoon take a walk in Suicin Bridge.
3 Head to the Comeragh Mountains and go hiking.
4 Take a boat tour on the River Suir and enjoy the beautiful scenery.
5 Visit the Knocmealdown Mountains and go hiking.
6 Take a horseback riding session in the morning and go to Tipperary Excel for the afternoon.
7 Relax in Clonmel Park and then enjoy the nightlife of the town.

Food and Drink Scene of Clonmel

Must-Try Dishes

  • Black pudding - a type of sausage made from pork blood and oatmeal, often served as an appetizer or side dish.
  • Colcannon - a mixture of mashed potatoes, cabbage or kale, and butter, often served with meat dishes.
  • Irish stew - a hearty meat and vegetable stew cooked slowly in a pot with water or broth, served with soda bread.
  • Coddle - a traditional stew made with sausages, bacon, potatoes, and onions, typically served with brown bread.

Local Beverages

  • Whiskey - Irish whiskey is famous throughout the world and there are several distilleries that offer tours in the area.
  • Murphy’s Irish Stout - A popular stout beer you should try when visiting the town.
  • Irish Coffee - coffee blended with Irish whiskey, sugar, and cream, perfect for cold evenings.

Where to Stay in Clonmel: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Raheen House Hotel - A charming hotel located in an 18th-century Georgian house.
  • The Park Hotel - An elegant hotel located in a semi-rural location, near to the Suir River.
  • Clonmel Park Hotel - Ideal for family vacations in a location that’s within downtown Clonmel.

What to Buy in Clonmel: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Waterford Crystal - Handcrafted crystal ware produced in nearby Waterford is famous worldwide.
  • Barron’s Bakery and Coffee House - Delicious local baked goodies and coffee.
  • Tipperary Crystal - High-quality crystal items produced in Tipperary are a great souvenir to take home.

Local Culture and Customs of Clonmel

Gaelic is still widely spoken by locals and traditional music and dance are an integral part of the culture. The town has a rich history dating back to medieval times, and this legacy has been preserved and highlighted through Clonmel’s museums, churches, castles, and fortifications.

Best Time to Visit Clonmel

The best time to visit Clonmel is in the summer months, from June through September when the weather is warm, and there is plenty of outdoor activities to do. Winter can be quite cold and rainy, but the festive season is a great time to visit and experience the Christmas markets and carol services.

Do’s and Don’ts of Clonmel

Do:

  • Do try some of the famous local culinary specialties such as Irish stew and black pudding.
  • Do take a leisurely stroll along the River Suir or in the Comeragh Mountains.
  • Do visit one of Clonmel’s historic churches and learn more about the town’s history.

Don’t:

  • Don’t forget to respect the locals and their customs.
  • Don’t litter or damage the natural environment.
  • Don’t forget to bring an umbrella or raincoat, as it can rain anytime in Clonmel.

Safety and Health

Safety in Clonmel

Clonmel is generally a safe town to visit, and the crime rate is low. However, visitors need to be careful when visiting the city at night in the less busy streets or alleys.

Health in Clonmel

There are several clinics and hospitals in Clonmel that offer medical services to visitors. Visitors should observe personal hygiene and avoid consuming tap water.

Getting to Clonmel: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Clonmel

The nearest international airport to Clonmel is Shannon Airport, which is approximately 104 km away. Other airports are Dublin Airport, Cork Airport, and Waterford Airport.

Bus Stations Near Clonmel

A number of bus companies serve Clonmel, including JJ Kavanagh and Sons, Bus Éireann, and many more.

Other Options

Driving is by far the best way to get around Tipperary, as it will give you the flexibility and freedom to explore the area on your own terms.

How to Get Around Clonmel

Walking is the best way to see Clonmel, especially as many of the town’s historic landmarks are located within walking distance of each other. Taxis and buses are alternative options for getting around the town, and bicycle rentals are available.

Where to Go Next from Clonmel: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Cahir Castle - A stunning medieval castle located approximately 17 km northwest of Clonmel.
  • Rock of Cashel - An ancient site located approximately 24 km north of Clonmel.
  • Waterford - Visit the famous Waterford Crystal Center, and explore Ireland’s oldest city located approximately 70 km southeast of Clonmel.

Conclusion

Clonmel is a vibrant town that offers something for every visitor. Take a walk along the River Suir or explore the Comeragh Mountains, visit the town’s castles and museums, and taste the local delicacies while enjoying the Irish hospitality. With this comprehensive guide, you can plan your trip and make the most of your visit to Clonmel.
