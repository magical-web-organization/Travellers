Introduction to Kilkenny

Kilkenny is a beautiful medieval city that is often referred to as the ‘Marble City’ because of its abundance of limestone buildings. It is situated in County Kilkenny in Southeast Ireland and is known for its rich history, stunning architecture, lively cultural scene, and delicious food and drink offerings. Kilkenny is a popular destination for tourists from all over the world because of its charm and beauty.

List of Places to Visit in Kilkenny

Kilkenny has much to offer visitors, with a mix of both cultural and natural attractions. Here are some of the must-see places in Kilkenny:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  1. Kilkenny Castle - This iconic castle was built in the 12th century and is one of the most visited attractions in Kilkenny. It’s a great place to learn about the history of the city.

  2. St. Canice’s Cathedral - This is the second-longest cathedral in Ireland and is a significant symbol of Kilkenny,

  3. Rothe House - Rothe House is a museum and garden that showcases the lifestyle of wealthy 17th-century merchants.

Natural Attractions of Kilkenny: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  1. River Nore - Kilkenny is home to the River Nore, which is a beautiful spot for a stroll, picnic, or fishing.

  2. Jenkinstown Forest Park - Jenkinstown Forest Park is home to some of the most breathtaking forest trails in the city.

  3. Dunmore Cave - This cave is located outside of Kilkenny and is a unique natural attraction.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Kilkenny

Kilkenny has some fantastic hiking and biking trails, including the Barrow River Trail and the Nore Valley Walk. These trails offer stunning views of the city and provide an excellent way to explore the natural beauty of Kilkenny.

Best Things to Do in Kilkenny: Activities and Tours

  1. Medieval Mile Museum - This museum is dedicated to the history of Kilkenny and offers visitors an interactive and engaging experience.

  2. Kilkenny Way - This company offers visitors an opportunity to experience hurling, Ireland’s national sport, and learn about the sport’s history.

  3. Kilkenny Ghost Tour - This nighttime walking tour is a fun and spooky way to get to know Kilkenny’s history.

Week-Long Itinerary for Kilkenny

If you’re planning a week-long trip to Kilkenny, here’s an itinerary to help you make the most of your time:

Day Activity
Day 1 Visit Kilkenny Castle, Smithwick’s Brewery Tour, and walk the Medieval Mile
Day 2 Take the Nore Valley Walk, visit the Black Abbey and St. John’s Priory
Day 3 Explore Dunmore Cave, visit Rothe House, and take the Kilkenny City Ghost Tour
Day 4 Take a day trip to Jerpoint Abbey, Hore Abbey, and the Rock of Cashel
Day 5 Explore the Jenkinstown Woods, visit St. Canice’s Cathedral and Round Tower
Day 6 Go cycling along the Barrow River Trail and have a picnic
Day 7 Spend a day discovering the arts and crafts scene in Kilkenny, including pottery and glassblowing tours

Food and Drink Scene of Kilkenny

Kilkenny offers visitors a delicious mix of traditional Irish food, along with innovative and modern cuisine options.

Must-Try Dishes

  1. Kilkenny ‘Black’ Ale - A unique brew made exclusively in the city.

  2. Black Pudding - A savory sausage made from pig’s blood.

  3. Irish Stew - A hearty and warming lamb stew that is perfect for colder evenings.

Local Beverages

  1. Smithwick’s Ale - Ireland’s oldest ale brand, brewed in Kilkenny.

  2. Paddy Whiskey - A smooth and easy-to-drink Irish whiskey produced in nearby County Cork.

  3. Kilkenny Red Ale - An amber-colored ale with a sweet and malty flavor profile that pairs perfectly with hearty Irish meals.

Where to Stay in Kilkenny: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Kilkenny offers a range of accommodation options that cater to a variety of tastes and budgets. Here are some of the top-rated places to stay:

  1. Kilkenny River Court Hotel - A luxury hotel with stunning views of the River Nore.

  2. Kilkenny Hostel - An affordable yet comfortable hostel.

  3. Butler House - A stylish guesthouse located in a historic 18th-century building.

What to Buy in Kilkenny: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Kilkenny is known for its artisanal crafts and local products. Here are some items you may want to consider bringing home as souvenirs:

  1. Pottery - Kilkenny has a thriving pottery scene, with many local artists creating unique and beautiful pieces.

  2. Glassware - The city is home to several glass-making factories, and visitors can watch artisans at work and purchase their creations.

  3. Wool Products - Ireland is famous for its wool products, and Kilkenny is no exception. Visitors can find a range of handmade woolen clothing and accessories.

Local Culture and Customs of Kilkenny

Kilkenny has a rich culture and history that is still very much alive today. One of the most significant cultural events in the city is the Kilkenny Arts Festival, which takes place in August and is a must-see for culture lovers.

The city is also known for its traditional music scene, with many pubs hosting live music sessions throughout the week. Visitors should be mindful of local customs, such as tipping waitstaff in restaurants, and being respectful when visiting historic sites and religious buildings.

Best Time to Visit Kilkenny

The best time to visit Kilkenny is during the summer months, from May to September, when the weather is warm and dry, and the landscape is vibrant and green. However, the city is lovely all year round, thanks to its festive holiday vibes during December and its colorful autumn foliage scenes.

Do’s and Don’ts of Kilkenny

Do:

  1. Explore the city on foot - Kilkenny is an excellent city for walking, with many pedestrian-only streets and hidden alleyways to discover.

  2. Visit the Kilkenny Design Centre - This shop/gallery located in a medieval castle is the ideal place to purchase unique homeware accessories created by local artisans.

  3. Take a brewery tour - Smithwick’s Brewery offers informative tours, and you can sample the renowned ale while there.

Don’t:

  1. Litter - Kilkenny is a beautiful city, and it’s essential to keep it that way by disposing of your waste responsibly.

  2. Wear inappropriate clothing when visiting historical and religious sites - Visitors should dress modestly and respectfully when visiting sites like St. Canice’s Cathedral.

  3. Overlook Kilkenny’s surrounding areas. - Kilkenny is located in the heart of Ireland’s Ancient East, which offers many fascinating historical sites and natural attractions to explore beyond Kilkenny city limits.

Safety and Health

Safety in Kilkenny

Kilkenny is a safe city. However, visitors should always take the necessary precautions when traveling, such as being aware of their surroundings and keeping an eye on their belongings. Walk down lit streets at night, use well-lit popular public transportation, and always have a phone or map to help you get back to your lodging.

Health in Kilkenny

Ireland has a high standard of medical care, and Kilkenny is home to several hospitals and clinics. Visitors should ensure they have comprehensive health insurance coverage before traveling.

Getting to Kilkenny: Transportation Options and Logistics

Kilkenny is well connected to neighboring towns and cities, including Dublin, Cork, and Limerick. Here are some of the best ways to travel to Kilkenny:

Airports Near Kilkenny

The closest international airport to Kilkenny is Dublin Airport, which is about two hours away by car. Cork Airport and Shannon Airport are other nearby options.

Bus Stations Near Kilkenny

Kilkenny is well-served by several bus lines, with many operators offering direct services from Dublin, Cork, and other major cities.

Other Options

Trains run frequently between Dublin and Kilkenny and are a comfortable and affordable way to travel.

How to Get Around in Kilkenny

Kilkenny is best explored on foot or by bike. Still, visitors can also take buses or taxis to travel to and from the city center. Many of the city’s top attractions are located in close proximity to each other, making it easy to get around without having to rely on public transportation.

Where to Go Next from Kilkenny: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Kilkenny is located in the heart of Ireland’s Ancient East, and there are plenty of fascinating historical sites and natural attractions to explore beyond the city limits. Some nearby destinations include:

  1. Waterford - A historic city famous for its crystal-making.

  2. Carrick-on-Suir - A town situated on the banks of the River Suir, known for its colorful buildings and lively market.

  3. Glendalough - A stunning glacial valley in Wicklow that is home to ancient ruins and outdoor activities.

Conclusion

Kilkenny is a charming and vibrant city that offers visitors a taste of Irish culture, history, and natural beauty. With plenty of attractions, activities, and accommodation options to choose from, you’re sure to have a memorable visit to this incredible city. Whether you’re a history buff, nature lover, or foodie, there’s something for everyone in Kilkenny.
