Introduction to Carlow

Carlow, located in Southeast Ireland, is the county town of County Carlow and a hidden gem that not many know about. The town boasts a rich cultural history, friendly locals, and plenty of outdoor activities for visitors to enjoy. Whether it’s exploring its historical landmarks or enjoying the beautiful natural scenery, Carlow has something to offer everyone.

List of Places to Visit in Carlow

When it comes to places to visit in Carlow, the town offers a variety of culturally important attractions to explore as well as natural attractions for outdoor enthusiasts.

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Carlow is rich in history, and visitors can indulge in past times by visiting its numerous museums and historical places. If you’re looking to explore the historical significance of the town, these are a few must-visit places:

  • Carlow Castle - A magnificent castle built in the 13th century by William Marshal, featuring a scenic view of the River Barrow
  • Visual Centre for Contemporary Art & The George Bernard Shaw Theatre - A stunning art gallery featuring contemporary art exhibitions, together with a modern theatre that hosts plays, musicals, and concerts
  • Saint Canice’s Cathedral and Round Tower - An impressive cathedral that dates back to the 13th century, including a Round Tower with panoramic views of the town
  • Brownshill Dolmen - A megalithic tomb believed to be more than 4000 years old, located just outside Carlow town
  • Carlow County Museum - An excellent museum showcasing the history of Carlow and its surrounding regions

Natural Attractions of Carlow: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

If you’re an outdoor enthusiast, Carlow has plenty to offer in terms of natural attractions and outdoor activities. A few options include:

  • Mount Leinster - A mountain located on the border between Carlow and Wexford, offering a picturesque view of the surrounding countryside
  • The Blackstairs Mountains - A range of mountains located to the east of Carlow, known for their natural beauty and abundance of walking trails
  • The River Barrow - A popular destination for anglers and kayakers, featuring a scenic river valley and canal towpath
  • Altamont Gardens - A stunning garden featuring an abundance of plant and wildlife, perfect for a peaceful walk or picnic
  • The Wicklow Way - A hiking trail that stretches over 131 km, connecting the two towns of Carlow and Dublin

Hiking/Biking Routes of Carlow

Carlow offers some of the best hiking and biking trails in Ireland, with endless possibilities for enthusiasts. Some popular trails include:

  • The Barrow Way - A trail that runs alongside the River Barrow, featuring beautiful scenery, historical landmarks, and plenty of options to stop and enjoy the countryside.
  • The Slí na Sláinte Trails - A series of looped walking and jogging routes around Carlow town and its surrounding areas
  • The South Leinster Way - A 103 km trail that starts in Kildavin and runs through the Blackstairs Mountains, ending in Carrick-on-Suir in County Tipperary

Best Things to Do in Carlow: Activities and Tours

Carlow has plenty of activities and tours to offer visitors, catering to all interests. Here are a few must-try experiences:

  • The Carlow Brewing Company Tour - A tour through a local brewery that produces the O’Hara’s range of craft beers, including tastings of their products
  • Carlow Farmers Market - A weekly market held on Saturdays, where visitors can buy fresh local produce from farmers and artisan food producers
  • Carlow’s Escape Rooms - A thrilling activity requiring teamwork to solve puzzles and escape from a themed room
  • Carlow Golf Club - An 18-hole championship golf course that offers incredible views of the surrounding countryside
  • Quad Trekking in the Wexford Countryside - An off-road adventure through the countryside, riding on quad bikes.

Food and Drink Scene of Carlow

The food and drink scene of Carlow is a perfect reflection of its local cuisine and produce, with a few must-try dishes and local beverages.

Must-Try Dishes

  • Irish Stew - A traditional lamb stew made with root vegetables, potatoes, herbs, and spices
  • Boxty - A potato pancake made with grated potatoes, flour, and buttermilk, served with a variety of fillings
  • Coddle - A hotpot made with sausages, bacon, potatoes, carrots, and onions, traditionally eaten in Dublin and its surrounding regions

Local Beverages

  • O’Hara’s Beers - A range of craft beers brewed by the Carlow Brewing Company, which includes stouts, ales, and lagers
  • Irish Whiskey - A distilled spirit made from various grains and barley, aged for a minimum of three years to produce a strong and flavorful drink
  • Irish Coffee - A hot beverage made with coffee, whiskey, and cream, topped off with grated chocolate

Where to Stay in Carlow: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Carlow has a vast array of accommodation options available that cater to all budgets and preferences. Here are some options to consider:

  • Talbot Hotel Carlow - A comfortable and luxury hotel located in the heart of Carlow town, offering a fitness suite, a sauna, and swimming pool.
  • Seven Oaks Hotel Carlow - A family-owned hotel offering luxurious accommodation, award-winning dining services, and live music on weekends.
  • Kilgraney House - A bed and breakfast-style guesthouse located in a 19th-century manor house, featuring a small organic farm and gardens.
  • Borris House B&B - A traditional Irish B&B that offers comfortable accommodation in a historical home, surrounded by beautiful gardens.

Local Culture and Customs of Carlow

The people of Carlow are known for their friendly, welcoming nature, and visitors can embrace local customs by:

  • Saying Hello - It’s customary to say hello to people you come across, either in town or the countryside.
  • Politeness - Irish people place a high value on politeness and manners, so remember always to say please and thank you.
  • Sense of humor - Irish people often use humor as a way of breaking the ice, so don’t be afraid to joke around with locals.

Best Time to Visit Carlow

The best time to visit Carlow is from May to September when the weather is mild, and the town hosts various festivals and events. However, be prepared for occasional rain, as it can rain anytime in Ireland.

Do’s and Don’ts of Carlow

There are a few things to keep in mind when visiting Carlow:

Do:

  • Do embrace Irish culture and customs by saying hello to locals and being polite.
  • Do try local cuisine and beverages.
  • Do embrace the great outdoors and hiking trails that Carlow has to offer.

Don’t:

  • Don’t plan too many activities in one day; Carlow is a town to enjoy at a comfortable pace.
  • Don’t be inconsiderate of other visitors or locals; remember that you are sharing the town with others.

Safety and Health

Carlow is a safe and welcoming town to visitors, but like any other location, it’s essential to take precautions to ensure your safety and health.

Safety in Carlow

  • Be cautious while walking or hiking on rough terrain.
  • Don’t leave valuable items unattended in public areas.
  • Always wear a helmet when riding a bike, and follow safety guidelines for quad biking.

Health in Carlow

  • Visitors from countries outside of the European Union may need certain vaccinations before entering Ireland, so check with your healthcare provider in advance.
  • Always carry necessary medication with you and ensure you have access to emergency medical care if needed.

Getting to Carlow: Transportation Options and Logistics

Traveling to Carlow is easy, with various transportation options available.

Airports Near Carlow

The nearest airports to Carlow are Dublin Airport and Waterford Regional Airport. Both airports are served by major international airlines and offer domestic flights.

Bus Stations Near Carlow

The two main bus stations near Carlow are McSweeney’s Bus Service which operates a twice-daily bus service from Dublin to Carlow or Bus Eireann which operates frequent services throughout the county.

Other Options

  • Taxis - Taxis are available in Carlow and can be hailed on the street or booked in advance.
  • Car Rental - Car rental companies are available in the town, and visitors can rent a car to explore the surrounding areas.

How to Get Around in Carlow

Carlow Cabs offer an affordable and reliable taxi service in the town, and visitors can easily walk around the town center, but renting a car would give more flexibility in exploring the outskirts of the town and the surrounding areas.

Where to Go Next from Carlow: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

There are plenty of places to visit near Carlow, and a few recommendations include:

  • Kilkenny - A historic city known for its medieval buildings, narrow streets, and castle.
  • Dublin - A vibrant and bustling city with something to offer everyone.
  • Wexford - A popular coastal town known for its beaches and historical landmarks.

Conclusion

Carlow, in Southeast Ireland, is a town worth visiting for its rich cultural heritage, breathtaking natural scenery, and friendly locals. With a vast array of historical landmarks, outdoor activities, and must-try experiences, it’s not surprising why Carlow has become a popular destination for visitors to Ireland.
