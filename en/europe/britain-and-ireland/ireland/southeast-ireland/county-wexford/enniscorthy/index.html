Enniscorthy Travel Guide: Things to Do, Places to See, and Where to Stay

Enniscorthy is a lively town in County Wexford, Southeast Ireland, steeped in history and natural beauty. With a charming old-world feel and plenty of cultural landmarks, including museums and historical sites to explore, Enniscorthy is the ideal place for a relaxing holiday or outdoor adventure. This travel guide will help you plan your trip and make the most of your time in Enniscorthy.

Introduction to Enniscorthy

Located in Southeast Ireland, Enniscorthy boasts a rich history and culture. The town is situated on the River Slaney and is surrounded by rolling hills and green meadows. Its Old Town is particularly picturesque with its narrow streets, colorful buildings, and historic architecture. Enniscorthy is famous for hosting the annual Strawberry Fair, which showcases the region’s fresh produce and local crafts.

List of Places to Visit in Enniscorthy

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Enniscorthy Castle: This Norman castle-turned-museum houses a collection of artifacts, including historical weapons and costumes, and offers tours of the castle and its grounds.
  • National 1798 Rebellion Centre: This museum, located in the historic Vinegar Hill area, tells the story of the 1798 Rebellion and how it impacted Enniscorthy and Ireland.
  • St Aidan’s Cathedral: This beautiful Gothic cathedral is a must-see for architecture lovers and history buffs.

Natural Attractions of Enniscorthy: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Vinegar Hill: This hill overlooks Enniscorthy and is the site of a famous battle during the 1798 Rebellion. Visitors can climb the hill and take in the panoramic views of the town and countryside.
  • Curracloe Beach: This stunning beach, located just a short drive from Enniscorthy, is famous for its soft sand dunes and clear waters.
  • Irish National Heritage Park: This outdoor museum is set in a 35-acre park and showcases the country’s ancient history, including its Neolithic period and Viking invaders.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Enniscorthy

  • Kiltealy Loop: This 10km trail passes through the scenic countryside around Enniscorthy and takes in views of the River Slaney and the Blackstairs Mountains.
  • Vinegar Hill Trail: This 7km trail takes hikers up Vinegar Hill and around the Battle of Vinegar Hill battlefield.
  • Enniscorthy to Wexford: This 18km trail follows the River Slaney from Enniscorthy to the coastal town of Wexford and is suitable for walkers and cyclists.

Best Things to Do in Enniscorthy: Activities and Tours

  • Kayaking on the River Slaney: Book a guided kayak tour for a unique way to experience the River Slaney and its stunning surroundings.
  • Strawberry Fair: Held in June each year, this festival celebrates the town’s history and culture and features live music, food, and crafts.
  • Enniscorthy Drama Festival: This festival, held in March, showcases the best of Irish amateur drama performances.

Week-Long Itinerary for Enniscorthy

Day 1 Arrive in Enniscorthy, check into hotel, take a stroll around the Old Town, and visit Enniscorthy Castle
Day 2 Hike the Kiltealy Loop in the morning, enjoy lunch in the town, and explore the National 1798 Rebellion Centre in the afternoon
Day 3 Take a day trip to Curracloe Beach for sun, sand, and sea
Day 4 Rent a bike and cycle the Enniscorthy to Wexford trail
Day 5 Visit the Irish National Heritage Park in the morning, and go kayaking on the River Slaney in the afternoon
Day 6 Shop for local crafts and souvenirs in the town’s markets, and attend an Enniscorthy Drama Festival performance in the evening
Day 7 Depart Enniscorthy

Food and Drink Scene of Enniscorthy

Must-Try Dishes

  • Wexford Cheddar: This local cheese is made using milk from cows in County Wexford and is known for its flavor and texture.
  • Seafood Chowder: Being a fishing town, Enniscorthy has some of the freshest seafood chowders in Ireland.
  • Strawberry Treats: The town’s Strawberry Fair celebrates its locally grown strawberries, so be sure to try some while in town.

Local Beverages

  • Irish Whiskey: Ireland is known for its whiskey, and Enniscorthy has several options to sample.
  • Craft Beer: Wexford is home to several microbreweries, and visitors can enjoy a pint at one of the town’s many pubs.

Where to Stay in Enniscorthy: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Riverside Park Hotel: This four-star hotel overlooks the River Slaney and offers comfortable rooms, spa services, and an on-site restaurant.
  • Anam Cara Manor: This elegant bed and breakfast, located in a restored manor house, offers cozy rooms and a picturesque garden.
  • Blackwater Lodge: This family-run guesthouse offers comfortable rooms and is located within walking distance of the town’s attractions.

What to Buy in Enniscorthy: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Handmade Crafts: The town’s markets and shops offer a variety of handmade crafts, such as pottery, knitting, and jewelry.
  • Wexford Strawberries: Bring home some of the town and region’s fresh, sweet strawberries to enjoy after your trip.
  • Irish Whiskey and Craft Beer: Bring home a bottle or two of Irish whiskey or craft beer to savor and remember your trip.

Local Culture and Customs of Enniscorthy

Enniscorthy has a strong sense of community and tradition. Visitors should take care to be respectful of the town and its customs. Be sure to greet locals with a friendly “hello” or “how’s it going?” and be prepared for occasional country accents and slang.

Best Time to Visit Enniscorthy

The best time to visit Enniscorthy is in the summer when the weather is warm and sunny, and the town is bustling with festivals and events. June is an especially good time to visit when the Strawberry Fair takes place.

Do’s and Don’ts of Enniscorthy

Do:

  • Be respectful of the town and its traditions.
  • Try local foods and beverages.
  • Take the time to explore the charming Old Town.

Don’t:

  • Be overly aggressive or confrontational.
  • Ignore local customs and habits.
  • Litter or damage historical sites.

Safety and Health

Safety in Enniscorthy

Enniscorthy is a safe town, but visitors should take standard safety precautions, such as avoiding dark or isolated areas at night and keeping valuables secure at all times.

Health in Enniscorthy

Irish tap water is safe to drink, and visitors should have no issues with food or water. Travelers should bring any necessary medications and ensure they have travel insurance.

Getting to Enniscorthy: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Enniscorthy

The nearest airport to Enniscorthy is Dublin Airport, which is about a 2-hour drive away. Visitors can also fly into Cork Airport, which is about a 2.5-hour drive away.

Bus Stations Near Enniscorthy

Enniscorthy is well-connected by bus, with several daily services from Dublin and other cities in Ireland. The town’s bus station is located centrally, making it easy to access from anywhere in town.

Other Options

Visitors can also rent a car and drive to Enniscorthy from Dublin or Cork. This is a convenient option for those who want to explore more of Ireland during their trip.

How to Get Around in Enniscorthy

Enniscorthy is a walkable town, and most attractions are located within a short distance from each other. Visitors can also rent bicycles or take a taxi to get around.

Where to Go Next from Enniscorthy: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Wexford: This coastal town, located just a short distance from Enniscorthy, has a vibrant arts scene and plenty of restaurants, pubs, and museums to explore.
  • Hook Head Lighthouse: This historic lighthouse, located on the Hook Peninsula, is the oldest lighthouse in Ireland and offers stunning views of the coastline.
  • Waterford: This historic city is known for its crystal glassware and medieval architecture and is just a short drive from Enniscorthy.

Conclusion

Enniscorthy is a charming and welcoming town in County Wexford that offers plenty of attractions, natural beauty, and cultural landmarks. A trip to Enniscorthy is perfect for those looking to experience Ireland’s history, traditions, and stunning scenery. By following this guide, visitors can make the most of their time in Enniscorthy and take home unforgettable memories.
