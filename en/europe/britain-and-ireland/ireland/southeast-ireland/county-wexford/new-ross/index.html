Introduction to New Ross

Nestled in the beautiful County Wexford, New Ross is an Irish town that is often overlooked by tourists, as it is not on the usual Irish tourist trail. However, this underrated town is a real gem and offers a mix of historical, cultural, and natural attractions, as well as activities that will appeal to all types of travelers.

This comprehensive travel guide aims to help you explore the town of New Ross, we will provide the best tour guide information to help you plan your trip properly. Let’s dive in!

List of Places to Visit in New Ross

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Dunbrody Famine Ship

The Dunbrody Famine Ship is a replica of the original ship that sailed from Ireland to North America during the Great Famine. It is one of the most popular tourist attractions in the town, where you can learn about the history of the famine and delve into the experience of Irish emigrants who braved the Atlantic.

Ros Tapestry

Ros Tapestry is a collection of 15 unique embroidered tapestries made by locals of New Ross over a 12-year period. The tapestries showcase historical events that took place in Ros in a beautiful manner with a modern feel, and provide a fascinating insight into the history of the town.

The Kennedy Homestead

The Kennedy family is one of the most famous families in American politics, and the Kennedy Homestead is where JFK’s great-grandfather was born in 1820 before immigrating to America. This museum provides an opportunity to delve into the family history and to see displays and memorabilia from the Kennedy family.

Natural Attractions of New Ross: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Hook Lighthouse

Hook Lighthouse is the oldest operational lighthouse in the world and one of the main attractions in the town. The views from the top are truly breathtaking, and visitors can also learn about the history of the lighthouse and maritime safety.

John F. Kennedy Arboretum

The John F. Kennedy Arboretum is a 500-acre park with more than 5,000 types of trees and shrubs from around the world, making it a great place to experience the beauty of nature and the great outdoors. The park also offers hiking trails, picnic facilities and a winding drive to offer views of the stunning landscape.

Tintern Abbey

Located just a short drive from New Ross is the Tintern Abbey, which was founded in 1203 by William Marshall, Earl of Pembroke. The ruins of the abbey are set in beautiful woods, where you can enjoy a peaceful stroll and take in scenic views.

Hiking/Biking Routes of New Ross

The South Wexford Coastal 160km Route

The South Wexford Coastal 160km route is a challenging and scenic bike route, which takes you along the coast of Wexford, passing through several picturesque fishing villages rooted in ancient times.

Barrow Way

If you want a more leisurely route, Barrow Way is the perfect place to explore for a walking or biking trail. The 114 km route takes you along the Barrow River and the Grand Canal, through beautiful countryside, past charming towns, and past stunning views.

Best Things to Do in New Ross: Activities and Tours

River Cruises

New Ross is located on the River Barrow and the River Nore, and a perfect way to see the beautiful scenery is by taking a river cruise. The tour options available include sightseeing, dinner, and evening cruises.

Golfing

Golf enthusiasts can enjoy several courses near New Ross, such as St. Helen’s Bay and the New Ross Golf Club. Both courses offer a challenging game and beautiful views.

Week-Long Itinerary for New Ross

Here’s a sample itinerary for a week-long trip to New Ross:

  • Day 1: Explore New Ross and visit Dunbrody Famine Ship
  • Day 2: Visit Ros Tapestry and the Kennedy Homestead
  • Day 3: Visit Hook Lighthouse and hike the nearby cliffs
  • Day 4: Take a bike ride along the Barrow Way
  • Day 5: Visit Tintern Abbey and go on a river cruise
  • Day 6: Golfing at St. Helen’s Bay and the New Ross Golf Club
  • Day 7: Enjoy a relaxing day at John F. Kennedy Arboretum

Food and Drink Scene of New Ross

Must-Try Dishes

New Ross is a great place to discover traditional Irish cuisine, especially thanks to its pristine coastline and fertile farmland. Some must-try dishes are:

  • Fish and chips
  • Irish stew
  • Coddle
  • Potato gratin
  • Seafood chowder

Local Beverages

Ireland is known for its pub culture, and New Ross is no exception. Visitors can enjoy great local drinks like ales, Guinness, Irish whiskey and cider. For whiskey enthusiasts, make sure you check out the local pubs to taste some of the rarest and best whiskeys around.

Where to Stay in New Ross: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Whether you’re looking for a luxury stay or a budget-friendly option, New Ross has it all. For great views and exceptional service, you can stay at the Brandon House Hotel & Spa. For those looking to save and still experience excellent accommodation, try Griffin’s Garden Centre & Guesthouse or the Kennedy Boutique Hotel.

What to Buy in New Ross: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

New Ross is known for quality food and drinks that you can take back home. Try stopping at a local distillery or one of the town shops to purchase handmade craft pieces, souvenirs, or locally grown produce such as apples and berries.

Local Culture and Customs of New Ross

The locals of Ross are known for their welcoming and friendly nature, making it one of the most hospitable places to visit in Ireland. It’s also a place to experience traditional Irish culture and customs through their food, music, folklore, and dance.

Best Time to Visit New Ross

The best time to visit New Ross is in summer, from June to September. However, do not rule out the idea of visiting New Ross during springtime which has amazing blooming flowers, fall is equally delightful with warm colors and great weather.

Do’s and Don’ts of New Ross

Do:

  • Respect the locals and their customs
  • Be Polite, friendly, and approachable.
  • Enjoy the natural beauty of the town and its surroundings.
  • Participate in traditional Irish music, dance, and sports.

Don’t:

  • Leave trash behind.
  • Overdrink in public places.
  • Be disrespectful or insult locals or their religion.
  • Wander the countryside without informing anyone on where you are headed.

Safety and Health

Safety in New Ross

New Ross is a safe place to visit to the standard that follows most Irish towns. Be vigilant when wandering alone at night and avoid small alleys at most hours.

Health in New Ross

Private medical care is available across New Ross. Nevertheless, tourists are advised to have medical insurance before embarking on trips as accidents and emergencies can happen anytime, anywhere.

Getting to New Ross: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near New Ross

The nearest airports to New Ross are Waterford Airport, which is 40 km away, and Dublin airport, which is 123 km away. Both airports have regular flights operated by several major airlines.

Bus Stations Near New Ross

Bus Eireann provides regular services connecting New Ross with other parts of Ireland. The bus stations are located in the town center, with coaches traveling several times a day.

Other Options

You can also rent a car from the airports or towns nearby, giving you the liberty to explore at your own pace.

How to Get Around in New Ross

New Ross is a small town that can easily be explored on foot. Additionally, taxis are available if you wish to visit the surrounding areas, alternatively, renting a bike is a good idea to delve into the countryside.

Where to Go Next from New Ross: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Kilkenny

Kilkenny is about a 40-minute drive from New Ross, and is renowned for its medieval architecture, charming cobbled streets, and rich heritage.

Wexford Town

Wexford is 22 km away from New Ross and offers brilliant sea views, surfing opportunities, beach activities, great food, and friendly locals making it one of the pleasant small towns to visit in Ireland.

Conclusion

New Ross is a hidden gem that is an ideal destination for those who want to explore history, culture, and nature in the same place. Whether you are interested in outdoor activities or soaking up the local culture and traditions, New Ross has something to offer. So, pack your bags and include New Ross on your next trip to Ireland, always ready with welcoming arms to travelers.
