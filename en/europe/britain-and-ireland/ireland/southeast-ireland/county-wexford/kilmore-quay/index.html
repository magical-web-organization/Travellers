Introduction to Kilmore Quay

Kilmore Quay is a scenic fishing village and popular tourist destination located in County Wexford, on the southeast coast of Ireland. The town is surrounded by beautiful beaches, rugged cliffs, and stunning offshore islands. Visitors can explore the town’s rich history, enjoy outdoor adventures, sample delicious seafood, and experience the relaxed local lifestyle.

List of Places to Visit in Kilmore Quay

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Ballyteigue Castle - A 16th-century tower house that was once the residence of the Carew family. Visitors can explore the ruins and learn about the area’s history.
  • Kilmore Quay Maritime and Heritage Museum - This museum highlights the maritime history and traditions of the town, with exhibits on fishing, boat-building, and local life.
  • Saltee Islands - These two uninhabited islands just off the coast of Kilmore Quay are popular with birdwatchers and nature lovers. The islands are home to a variety of seabirds, including puffins, guillemots, and gannets.

Natural Attractions of Kilmore Quay: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Ballyteigue Bay - A large, sheltered bay that is perfect for swimming, kayaking, and other water sports. The bay is also home to a variety of wildlife, including seals and otters.
  • Hook Head Lighthouse - Located on the tip of the Hook peninsula, this historic lighthouse offers stunning views of the coastline and surrounding area.
  • Kilmore Quay Beach - A beautiful sandy beach that is popular with families and swimmers.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Kilmore Quay

  • The South Wexford Coastal Path - A scenic walking and cycling trail that runs for 35km along the coast, from the village of Rosslare to the Hook Lighthouse. The path passes through several towns and villages, and offers stunning views of the coastline.
  • The Saltee Islands - Visitors can take a boat tour to the islands and explore the rugged terrain and abundant wildlife.

Best Things to Do in Kilmore Quay: Activities and Tours

  • Angling - Kilmore Quay is known for its excellent fishing opportunities, with several charter boats available for hire.
  • Sailing - Visitors can book sailing lessons or take a leisurely sail around the bay.
  • Food tours - Guided tours of the local seafood restaurants and breweries are available, with plenty of opportunities to sample the local cuisine.

Food and Drink Scene of Kilmore Quay

Must-Try Dishes

  • Seafood chowder - A creamy soup filled with fresh fish, shrimp, and vegetables.
  • Fish and chips - A classic Irish dish made with crispy battered fish and thick-cut chips.
  • Irish stout - A rich, dark beer that is a local specialty.

Local Beverages

  • Whiskey - Irish whiskey is famous worldwide for its smooth, complex flavor.
  • Cider - Ireland is known for its refreshing apple cider, which is available in several flavors.
  • Craft beer - Kilmore Quay is home to several microbreweries, offering unique and flavorful beers.

Where to Stay in Kilmore Quay: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Quay House B&B - A charming guesthouse located in the heart of the village.
  • Stella Maris - A traditional Irish hotel with comfortable rooms and excellent dining.
  • Kilmore Quay Holiday Cottages - Self-catering cottages with full kitchens and stunning views of the bay.

What to Buy in Kilmore Quay: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Handmade pottery - Local artisans create beautiful, unique pieces using traditional techniques.
  • Knitwear - Cozy wool sweaters, hats, and scarves are popular souvenirs.
  • Local food products - Fresh seafood, artisanal cheeses, and homemade jams and chutneys are available at local shops and markets.

Local Culture and Customs of Kilmore Quay

Kilmore Quay is a friendly, welcoming village where visitors can experience the relaxed local lifestyle. The town is proud of its maritime traditions, and many residents still make their living from the sea. Visitors should respect the local customs and traditions, and avoid loud or disruptive behavior.

Best Time to Visit Kilmore Quay

The best time to visit Kilmore Quay is during the summer months, from June to August. The weather is warm and sunny, and there are plenty of outdoor activities and events. However, the village can be crowded during this time, so visitors who prefer a quieter experience may want to visit in the spring or fall.

Do’s and Don’ts of Kilmore Quay

Do:

  • Respect the local culture and customs.
  • Sample the local seafood and beer.
  • Visit the Saltee Islands for a unique wildlife experience.

Don’t:

  • Disturb the local wildlife or natural habitats.
  • Leave litter or waste on the beaches or trails.
  • Engage in disruptive behavior in the town or surrounding areas.

Safety and Health

Safety in Kilmore Quay

Kilmore Quay is a safe and friendly town, with low rates of crime and violence. Visitors should take normal precautions to protect their personal belongings, such as keeping valuables in a secure location and not leaving items unattended.

Health in Kilmore Quay

Medical services are available in nearby villages and towns, and there is a hospital in Wexford City, about 20km away. Visitors should take normal precautions to protect their health, such as drinking bottled water and wearing sunscreen.

Getting to Kilmore Quay: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Kilmore Quay

The closest airport to Kilmore Quay is Waterford Airport, located about 45km away. The larger Dublin Airport is about 180km away.

Bus Stations Near Kilmore Quay

Several bus companies operate routes to and from Kilmore Quay, with connections to other cities and towns in Ireland.

Other Options

Visitors can also travel by car or bike, or take a taxi or private shuttle service.

How to Get Around in Kilmore Quay

Most attractions in Kilmore Quay are within walking distance of the town center. Visitors can also rent bikes or take a taxi or shuttle service.

Where to Go Next from Kilmore Quay: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Wexford City - A historic town with a beautiful waterfront and plenty of cultural attractions.
  • Waterford City - Home of the famous Waterford Crystal factory and the Viking Triangle historic district.
  • Rosslare Harbour - A popular coastal resort town with beautiful beaches and excellent seafood.

Conclusion

Kilmore Quay is a beautiful, peaceful village with plenty to offer visitors. Whether you’re interested in outdoor activities, cultural attractions, or simply relaxing by the sea, you’re sure to find something to enjoy in this charming Irish town. From fresh seafood to stunning natural scenery, Kilmore Quay is a must-visit destination for anyone traveling to Ireland.
