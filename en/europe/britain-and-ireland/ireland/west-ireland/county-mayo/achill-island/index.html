Introduction to Achill Island

Achill Island is the largest island off the coast of Ireland, located in County Mayo on the west coast of the country. This beautiful and rugged island is renowned for its stunning landscapes, beautiful beaches, and friendly locals. Whether you are looking to relax, explore nature, or indulge in adventure sports, Achill Island has something for everyone. In this comprehensive travel guide, we will explore the must-visit places on Achill Island and provide practical tips on how to make the most of your trip.

List of Places to Visit in Achill Island

Achill Island boasts a wide range of attractions that cater to diverse interests. Here is a list of the must-visit places on Achill Island:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Achill Island Heritage Centre
  • Grace O’Malley Castle
  • Kildamhnait Castle
  • Deserted Village at Slievemore

Natural Attractions of Achill Island: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • The Silver Strand

  • The Great Western Loop

Best Things to Do in Achill Island: Activities and Tours

From surfing to horse-riding, there are endless activities to enjoy on Achill Island. Here are some of the must-do activities:

  • Surfing at Keel Beach
  • Horse-riding at the Achill Riding Centre
  • Sea kayaking with Achill Kayak Tours
  • Stand-up paddleboarding at Keem Bay
  • Fishing with Achill Anglers

Week-Long Itinerary for Achill Island

A week on Achill Island will allow you to fully immerse yourself in the island’s rich culture, stunning landscapes, and outdoor activities. Here is a suggested itinerary:

Day 1: Arrive on Achill Island, explore the Achill Island Heritage Centre, and visit Grace O’Malley’s Castle. Day 2: Hike up Croaghaun Mountain and enjoy the stunning views of the Atlantic Ocean. Day 3: Relax on Keem Bay Beach and try your hand at stand-up paddleboarding. Day 4: Take the Great Western Greenway cycling path and explore the surrounding countryside. Day 5: Go horse-riding at the Achill Riding Centre and explore the Deserted Village at Slievemore. Day 6: Go sea kayaking with Achill Kayak Tours and spend the afternoon fishing with Achill Anglers. Day 7: Take a scenic drive around Achill Island and stop at Kildamhnait Castle and the Silver Strand.

Local Culture and Customs of Achill Island

Achill Island has a rich culture and customs that are strongly rooted in its history and landscapes. Some of the unique customs include:

  • Annual Maybank holiday
  • Traditional Irish music sessions

Best Time to Visit Achill Island

The best time to visit Achill Island is from May to September when the weather is warm and dry, and the island is bustling with tourists. However, if you are looking for a quieter experience, visiting during the off-season will offer more solitude and a chance to experience the authentic island life.

Do:

  • Take time to explore the island’s natural beauty
  • Embrace the local food and drink scene

Don’t:

Safety and Health

Safety in Achill Island

Achill Island is a relatively safe destination with low rates of crime and theft. However, it’s always best to exercise common sense and take basic safety precautions such as locking your car and securing your valuables.

Health in Achill Island

Achill Island has a good healthcare system with local hospitals and clinics available for emergencies. If you are travelling from outside the EU, it’s essential to have travel insurance that covers medical expenses.
