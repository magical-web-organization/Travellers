Introduction to Knock

Knock is a small town located in County Mayo, in the west of Ireland. It is famous for its religious significance, as it is the site of an apparition of the Virgin Mary in 1879. Since then, Knock has become an important pilgrimage site and a popular destination for tourists.

But Knock is more than just a pilgrimage site. It has a rich history, stunning natural landscapes, and plenty of activities for visitors to enjoy.

In this comprehensive guide, we’ll show you the best places to visit, the top things to do, where to stay, and how to get there. Whether you’re a pilgrim or a tourist, there’s something for everyone in Knock.

List of Places to Visit in Knock

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Knock Shrine: The main attraction in Knock, the Shrine attracts over a million visitors annually. It features the apparition chapel, basilica, and various statues and artworks depicting the Virgin Mary.
  • Knock Museum: Learn about the history of the apparition and its impact on Knock and Ireland.
  • Knock Folk Museum: Exhibits on rural life and tradition in Ireland.

Natural Attractions of Knock: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Croagh Patrick: A mountain located 10 km from Knock, and a popular spot for hiking and pilgrimage.
  • Wild Atlantic Way: A scenic coastal drive that passes through Knock and County Mayo.
  • Ceide Fields: An archaeological site that features an extensive stone-age field system.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Knock

  • Croagh Patrick Hike: Take on the famous pilgrimage hike up Croagh Patrick.
  • Western Way: A long-distance trail that passes through Knock and County Mayo.
  • Great Western Greenway: A bike trail that stretches from Westport to Achill, with stunning views of the Mayo countryside.

Best Things to Do in Knock: Activities and Tours

  • Knock Heritage Trail: Follow the trail to learn about the history and culture of Knock.
  • Ireland West Tours: Guided tours covering the highlights of Knock and County Mayo.
  • Horse Riding: Take in the countryside on horseback with Slieve Aughty Riding Centre.

Food and Drink Scene of Knock

Must-Try Dishes

  • Irish stew: A traditional dish made with lamb, potatoes, and vegetables.
  • Boxty: A pancake made with grated potatoes, flour, and buttermilk.
  • Seafood chowder: A creamy soup made with fresh seafood.

Local Beverages

  • Guinness: A rich and creamy Irish stout.
  • Irish whiskey: A popular spirit, with famous brands like Jameson and Bushmills.
  • Poitín: A traditional Irish moonshine made from potatoes or grain.

Where to Stay in Knock: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Knock House Hotel: A comfortable hotel located across from the Shrine.
  • Knockranny House Hotel: A luxurious hotel with a spa, located in Westport.
  • The Western Greenway Hostel: A budget-friendly option for backpackers.

What to Buy in Knock: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Religious items: Rosaries, prayer cards, and other religious souvenirs are popular.
  • Aran sweaters: A traditional Irish sweater made from wool.
  • Handmade crafts: Knick-knacks and crafts made by local artisans.

Local Culture and Customs of Knock

Knock is a predominantly Catholic town, and visitors should respect the religious significance of the Shrine and the apparition. The locals are friendly and welcoming, and it’s common to strike up conversations and make new friends.

Best Time to Visit Knock

The best time to visit Knock is during the summer months, from June to August, when the weather is warm and there are plenty of outdoor activities to enjoy. The Shrine also hosts several events and festivals during the summer.

Do’s and Don’ts of Knock

Do:

  • Be respectful of the religious significance of the Shrine and the apparition.
  • Try the local food and drink, including the seafood and stouts.
  • Take in the stunning natural landscapes and go for a hike or bike ride.

Don’t:

  • Disrupt Mass or other religious services at the Shrine.
  • Litter or damage the natural landscapes.
  • Be disrespectful to the locals or their customs.

Safety and Health

Safety in Knock

Knock is a safe town, and crime is generally rare. Visitors should take normal precautions, like keeping an eye on their belongings and avoiding dark or isolated areas at night.

Health in Knock

Visitors should have up-to-date vaccinations and medical insurance when visiting Ireland. Knock does not have a dedicated medical facility, but there are hospitals and clinics in nearby towns and cities.

Getting to Knock: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Knock

  • Ireland West Airport Knock: The closest airport to Knock, located just 20 minutes away.

Bus Stations Near Knock

  • Bus Éireann: The national bus service that connects Knock to other cities and towns in Ireland.
  • CityLink: A private bus service that also provides transportation to and from Knock.

Other Options

  • Car rental: A popular option for visitors who want to explore the countryside on their own.
  • Taxi: Taxis are available, but can be expensive for long distances.

How to Get Around in Knock

The town of Knock is small enough to explore on foot or by bike. Taxis and public transportation are also available, but can be limited for longer distances.

Where to Go Next from Knock: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Westport: A charming town located 30 minutes from Knock, known for its history and outdoor activities.
  • Galway: A vibrant city located 90 minutes from Knock, with a rich cultural scene and nightlife.
  • Cliffs of Moher: A stunning natural attraction located 2 hours from Knock, featuring dramatic cliffs and breathtaking views.

Conclusion

Knock is a hidden gem in County Mayo, with a rich history, stunning natural landscapes, and a friendly and welcoming community. Whether you’re a pilgrim or a tourist, there’s something for everyone in Knock. We hope this comprehensive guide helps you plan your visit and make the most out of your time in Knock.
