Introduction to Lough Key

Located in County Roscommon, West Ireland, Lough Key is a natural wonder that has captivated tourists for centuries. The lake is surrounded by lush greenery, ancient woodlands, and scenic walking trails. Visitors can also discover the region’s rich cultural heritage while exploring museums, castles, and ancient ruins. The natural beauty and historical significance of Lough Key make it a must-visit destination for travelers seeking adventure, tranquility, and a deep connection with Irish culture.

List of Places to Visit in Lough Key

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Lough Key Forest Park – A vast park with a range of attractions, including the restored Moylurg Tower, a bog garden, a playground, and a cafe.
  • King House – A historical mansion where visitors can learn about the region’s rich cultural heritage and the role of Anglo-Irish families in history.
  • Boyle Abbey – A well-preserved Cistercian monastery that dates back to the 12th century.
  • Arigna Mining Experience – An interactive museum that showcases the history and technology of coal mining in Ireland.

Natural Attractions of Lough Key: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Lough Key Boats and Bikes – A rental service that offers paddle boats, kayaks, bicycles, and electric bikes for exploring the lake and the surrounding area.
  • Zipit Forest Adventures – An outdoor adventure park that features zip lines, rope bridges, and treetop obstacles courses.
  • The Woodland Segway Experience – A guided tour of the forest park on self-balancing electric vehicles.
  • Lough Key Forest and Leisure Park Tree Canopy Walkway – A 300-meter-long walkway that offers stunning views of the surrounding countryside.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Lough Key

  • The Lough Key Loop Walk – A 9-kilometer trail that covers the forested areas of Lough Key, including the Castle Island and the Bog Garden.
  • The Miner’s Way and Historical Trail – A 118-kilometer trail that follows the route taken by miners to reach the Arigna mines.

Best Things to Do in Lough Key: Activities and Tours

  • Lough Key Experience – A multimedia show that tells the story of the lake’s rich history and cultural heritage.
  • Fishing in Lough Key – A popular pastime for tourists and locals alike, with pike, bream, and trout being the most common fish that can be caught here.
  • Caravan Holiday Camping in Lough Key – A unique way to experience the region’s lush countryside and natural beauty, with a range of campsites and caravans available for rent.
  • Horse Riding Lough Key – A guided tour on horseback that covers scenic trails and ancient ruins.

Food and Drink Scene of Lough Key

Must-Try Dishes

  • Irish Stew – A traditional meat stew made with lamb, potatoes, and carrots.
  • Boxty – A local specialty that is a type of potato pancake.
  • Barmbrack – A sweet bread that is traditionally eaten on Halloween.

Local Beverages

  • Guinness – A dark beer that is synonymous with Ireland.
  • Irish Whiskey – A whiskey made from malted barley, corn, and other grains, aged in wooden barrels.

Where to Stay in Lough Key: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Lough Key Forest Park – A range of rental properties, including lodges, camping facilities, and caravan sites.
  • Rockingham House – A historical mansion that has been converted into a luxurious guesthouse.

What to Buy in Lough Key: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Irish Sweaters – A traditional woolen sweater that is warm and comfortable.
  • Irish Pottery – A range of ceramics that are unique to Ireland and make great souvenirs.
  • Irish Whiskey – A bottle of Irish whiskey makes an excellent souvenir or gift.

Local Culture and Customs of Lough Key

  • Greeting People – Always greet people with a polite “hello” or “good morning”.
  • Tipping – It is customary to tip waitstaff and taxi drivers.
  • Etiquette – Always be respectful of local customs and traditions, such as covering your head and shoulders when visiting a place of worship.

Best Time to Visit Lough Key

The best time to visit Lough Key is during the summer months of June, July, and August. The weather is pleasant, and the region is alive with festivals and outdoor events.

Do’s and Don’ts of Lough Key

Do:

  • Respect the Environment – Leave the natural areas you visit as you found them.
  • Try the Local Cuisine – Sample the traditional dishes and drinks unique to the region.
  • Learn About the Culture – Educate yourself about the history and culture of the region to appreciate it fully.

Don’t:

  • Litter – Dispose of your waste properly and keep the environment clean.
  • Disrespect the Local Culture – Always be respectful of local customs, traditions, and beliefs.

Safety and Health

Safety in Lough Key

  • Swimming – Do not venture too far off into the lake as strong currents can be dangerous.
  • Hiking and Biking – Follow the marked trails and roads to avoid getting lost in the woods.
  • Weather – Check the weather forecast before heading out, as weather conditions can change rapidly.

Health in Lough Key

  • Ticks – Wear long pants tucked into your socks and use a tick repellent as ticks are common in wooded areas.
  • Sun Protection – Use sunblock, a hat, and sunglasses to protect yourself from the sun while outdoors.

Getting to Lough Key: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Lough Key

  • Ireland West Airport Knock – The closest airport to Lough Key, which is about 42 kilometers away.
  • Dublin Airport – Dublin airport is about 150 kilometers away but has more international connections.

Bus Stations Near Lough Key

  • Boyle 5 Day Markets Bus Stop, Boyle – Regular bus services are available to Boyle from Dublin, Galway, and other major cities in Ireland.

Other Options

  • Car Rental – Renting a car is the most convenient way to travel to Lough Key.
  • Private Transfer – A private taxi cab service can be arranged from the airport to Lough Key.

How to Get Around in Lough Key

The best way to get around in Lough Key is by car, bike, or on foot. Renting a bike is a popular option as it allows visitors to explore the region’s winding paths and scenic trails. Driving a car also gives visitors the flexibility to explore the area at their own pace. Walking is ideal for those who seek a leisurely stroll through the park or the historical sites.

Where to Go Next from Lough Key: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Sligo – A coastal town that is known for its scenic beauty and literary history, located about 55 kilometers from Lough Key.
  • Galway – A vibrant city that is rich in history and tradition, located about 120 kilometers from Lough Key.
  • Dublin – The capital of Ireland, known for its friendly people, cultural attractions, and bustling nightlife, located about 140 kilometers from Lough Key.

Conclusion

Lough Key offers an unforgettable travel experience that blends natural beauty, rich cultural heritage, and outdoor adventures. From hiking and biking to exploring ancient ruins and enjoying local cuisine, there is something for everyone in this idyllic part of West Ireland. So, come and discover Lough Key, and create memories that will last a lifetime.
