Introduction to Roscommon

Located in the West of Ireland, Roscommon is a picturesque county with a rich history, stunning natural landscapes, and a warm, welcoming atmosphere. With a number of quaint towns and villages dotted throughout the countryside, visitors can explore ancient castles, museums, and monuments, sample delicious local cuisine, and experience the vibrant local culture.

List of Places to Visit in Roscommon

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Roscommon is steeped in history, and there are a number of culturally important attractions that visitors can explore. The Roscommon Castle, located in the heart of the town, is a well-preserved 13th-century Norman castle that tells the story of the town’s ancient past. The nearby Boyle Abbey is a beautifully preserved Cistercian monastery that was founded in the 12th century.

The County Roscommon Heritage and Genealogy Centre is an excellent resource for those looking to trace their Irish ancestry. Visitors can also explore the Roscommon County Museum, which features exhibits that cover the natural history, culture, and traditions of the county.

Natural Attractions of Roscommon: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Roscommon boasts some of the most breathtaking natural landscapes in Ireland. Lough Key Forest Park is an award-winning park with over 350 hectares of woodland, lakes, and gardens. Visitors can enjoy nature trails, boat tours, and a treetop canopy walkway.

The Suck Valley Way is a 105 km long-distance hiking trail that runs through the countryside and along the majestic River Suck. The Lough Glynn Walk is a 9-km trail that offers stunning views of the tranquil Lough Glynn lake.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Roscommon

Roscommon offers some of the best hiking and biking routes in Ireland. The Lough Key Forest Park has over 8 km of cycle tracks, while the Suck Valley Way offers 105 km of hiking trails. Other popular trails include the Arigna Miner’s Way and St. Kevin’s Way.

Best Things to Do in Roscommon: Activities and Tours

Aside from hiking and cycling, there are plenty of other things to do in Roscommon. Visitors can take a leisurely boat trip along the Shannon River, go fishing in one of the county’s many lakes and rivers, or try their hand at horse riding at the local equestrian centers. Cultural tours and cooking classes are also available for those interested in experiencing the local culture.

Week-Long Itinerary for Roscommon

Day 1: Arrival and check-in at a local hotel. Explore Roscommon castle and nearby attractions.

Day 2: Take a boat trip along the Shannon River and visit the Boyle Abbey.

Day 3: Hike or bike along the Suck Valley Way.

Day 4: Visit Lough Key Forest Park and enjoy the treetop canopy walkway.

Day 5: Go horse riding in one of the local equestrian centers.

Day 6: Explore the local culture with a guided tour and cooking class.

Day 7: Departure from Roscommon.

Food and Drink Scene of Roscommon

Must-Try Dishes

Roscommon is known for its hearty traditional cuisine. Visitors should try some of the more popular dishes, including coddle, bacon and cabbage, and colcannon. Local seafood, such as salmon and trout, are also highly recommended.

Local Beverages

Roscommon is also home to a thriving craft beer scene, with a number of local breweries producing delicious, refreshing beers. Visitors can also sample local spirits, such as whiskey and gin, at the nearby distilleries.

Where to Stay in Roscommon: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Roscommon offers a range of accommodation options to suit all tastes and budgets. Visitors can choose from quaint bed and breakfasts, comfortable guesthouses, or luxury hotels.

What to Buy in Roscommon: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Visitors can find a wide variety of locally produced goods in Roscommon. Crafts, such as pottery and ceramics, are popular souvenirs, while the local traditional foods such as bacon and cheeses make for delicious gifts.

Local Culture and Customs of Roscommon

The locals in Roscommon are warm and friendly, and visitors should take the time to chat and engage with them. The county is known for its traditional music and dance, and visitors should try to catch a live performance during their stay.

Best Time to Visit Roscommon

The best time to visit Roscommon is during the summer months, when the weather is warm and sunny, and visitors can take advantage of the many outdoor activities the county has to offer.

Do’s and Don’ts of Roscommon

Do:

  • Take the time to explore the local culture and traditions.
  • Try the local cuisine and drinks.
  • Engage with the locals and make new friends.

Don’t:

  • Ignore the natural beauty of the county; take the time to appreciate the landscape.
  • Be impolite to the locals.

Safety and Health

Safety in Roscommon

Roscommon is a safe and welcoming county, with little crime or violence reported. Visitors should still be vigilant, however, and take the usual precautions, such as not leaving valuables on display.

Health in Roscommon

Visitors to Roscommon should ensure that they have adequate health insurance that covers them for any medical treatment they may need during their stay.

Getting to Roscommon: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Roscommon

The nearest airport to Roscommon is Ireland West Airport Knock, which is located approximately 45-minutes’ drive from the county.

Bus Stations Near Roscommon

Buses are available from Dublin Airport direct to Roscommon. The main bus station in Roscommon is located in the town center, and there are regular bus services to and from all major towns and cities in Ireland.

Other Options

Visitors can also rent a car, take a taxi, or opt for a private transfer to and from Roscommon.

How to Get Around in Roscommon

Visitors can opt to rent a car or take public transportation, such as buses and taxis.

Where to Go Next from Roscommon: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Roscommon is located in the heart of Ireland, making it the perfect base for exploring the country. Visitors can take a day trip to the nearby city of Galway, or travel to Dublin to explore the capital city.

Conclusion

Roscommon is a charming county with a lot to offer visitors. From stunning natural landscapes to rich cultural heritage, visitors will have plenty to see and do during their stay. With a warm and welcoming atmosphere, visitors are sure to leave with fond memories of their time in Roscommon.
