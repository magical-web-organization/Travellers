Introduction to Inis Mór

Inis Mór is the largest of the three Aran Islands located off the west coast of Ireland. It is a popular destination for tourists looking for a secluded getaway with breathtaking scenery, rich history and culture, and outdoor activities. Inis Mór is steeped in Irish folklore, with its prehistoric and early Christian ruins providing insight into the island’s past.

Do’s and Don’ts of Inis Mór

When traveling to Inis Mór, it’s important to observe proper etiquette and practices.

Do:

  • Respect local customs and traditions
  • Pack appropriate clothing and gear for the possible weather conditions
  • Bring cash, as there are limited ATMs

Don’t:

  • Litter in public spaces
  • Damage the natural or cultural attractions
  • Ignore safety rules when engaging in outdoor activities

Safety and Health

Inis Mór is generally safe for tourists, but it’s essential to observe basic safety measures and health precautions.

Safety in Inis Mór

  • Always follow safety rules for outdoor activities and water sports
  • Keep your valuables safe and avoid night-time walking on desolate roads
  • Check the weather forecast before setting out for your activities

Health in Inis Mór

  • Bring necessary prescription medicines
  • Apply sunblock, as the sun can be harsh during summer in Inis Mór
  • Drink bottled water only
