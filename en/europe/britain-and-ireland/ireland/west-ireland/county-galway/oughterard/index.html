Introduction to Oughterard

Located in County Galway, in the western part of Ireland, Oughterard is a charming village that offers visitors a mix of natural beauty, culture, and history. Nestled on the shore of Lough Corrib, the village is surrounded by rolling hills, verdant forests, and a stunning landscape. Whether you’re an adventure seeker, history buff, or just someone looking to unwind, Oughterard has something for everyone.

List of Places to Visit in Oughterard

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Aughnanure Castle: a restored 16th-century castle, showcasing Ireland’s medieval history.
  • Glengowla Mines: visitors can experience the life of a miner and learn about the region’s mining history.
  • Quiet Man Museum: dedicated to the classic movie starring John Wayne and Maureen O’Hara, who filmed the movie in the village.

Natural Attractions of Oughterard: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Lough Corrib: Explore the lake by boat and take in the stunning views of the surrounding countryside.
  • Connemara National Park: A vast park with beautiful hiking trails and the breathtaking beauty of the Twelve Bens mountain range.
  • The Western Way: A hiking trail that takes you through some of the most stunning landscapes in western Ireland.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Oughterard

  • The Western Way: A hiking trail that starts in Oughterard and takes you through the scenic Connemara region.
  • Greenway Trail: A bike trail that runs along the old railway line connecting Galway city to Clifden.

Best Things to Do in Oughterard: Activities and Tours

  • Fishing: Lough Corrib is known for its impressive brown trout population and other species of fish, making it an excellent spot for anglers of all levels.
  • Kayaking: Rent a kayak and explore Lough Corrib, taking in the stunning views of the surrounding landscape from the water.
  • Horseback Riding: Go horse riding and explore Oughterard and its stunning surroundings on horseback.

Week-Long Itinerary for Oughterard

Food and Drink Scene of Oughterard

Must-Try Dishes

  • Seafood Chowder: Made with fresh seafood, this dish is a must-try when in Oughterard.
  • Irish Stew: A delicious meat and vegetable stew that is hearty and filling.
  • Boxty: A traditional Irish potato pancake.

Local Beverages

  • Guinness: A beloved Irish beer, Guinness is a must-try when in Oughterard.
  • Whiskey: Irish whiskey is renowned worldwide, and Oughterard has its own whiskey distillery, Micil Distillery.

Where to Stay in Oughterard: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Corrib Wave House: A beautiful guest house that overlooks Lough Corrib.
  • Ross Lake House Hotel: A charming hotel surrounded by beautiful gardens and parkland.
  • The Boat Inn: A cozy and welcoming inn with a lively atmosphere.

What to Buy in Oughterard: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Aran Sweaters: A traditional Irish wool sweater, perfect for keeping you cozy and warm.
  • Handmade Crafts: There are many handmade crafts available in the village, including pottery, glass, and textiles.
  • Irish Whiskey: Take home a bottle of Micil Distillery’s own whiskey, made in Oughterard.

Local Culture and Customs of Oughterard

The community is strongly connected with traditional Irish culture, and you might find yourself at a local pub enjoying a traditional Irish music session.

Best Time to Visit Oughterard

The best time to visit Oughterard is from April to September when the weather is mild, and the days are longer. The tourist season is at its peak during these months, so make sure to book your accommodations in advance.

Safety and Health

Safety in Oughterard

Oughterard is a relatively safe place to visit, with low crime rates. However, visitors should be cautious when exploring remote areas, and always follow safety guidelines.

Health in Oughterard

Ireland has a public healthcare system, and medical facilities are readily available around the country. Visitors from other European Union countries are eligible for free or reduced-cost medical treatment, while others should have travel insurance that covers medical expenses.

Getting to Oughterard: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Oughterard

  • Ireland West Airport Knock (NOC): 79 km.
  • Galway Airport: 27 km.

Bus Stations Near Oughterard

  • Galway, Eyre Square: A bus service is available from here to Oughterard.

Other Options

How to Get Around in Oughterard

Oughterard is a small village, and the best way to explore it is on foot or by bike. Taxis are also available for those who require transportation to areas outside the village.

Where to Go Next from Oughterard: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Galway City: The lively city of Galway is located just 26 km away from Oughterard and offers visitors a wealth of attractions and activities.
  • Clifden: Explore the charming town of Clifden, located just 38 km away from Oughterard, known for its stunning surroundings and picturesque streets.
