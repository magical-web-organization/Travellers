Introduction to Belfast

Belfast, the capital city of Northern Ireland, is an enthralling destination that boasts of a rich cultural history and diverse attractions. It is a city that is constantly evolving and transforming itself, yet remains rooted in its past. From museums and historical sites, to outdoor activities and scenic views, Belfast has something to offer for every type of traveler. This guide will provide a comprehensive overview of everything you need to know to plan your next trip to this fascinating city.

List of Places to Visit in Belfast

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Belfast is a city with a complicated and often contentious history. There are several museums and historical sites that offer visitors a glimpse into the city’s past and present. Here are some of the most significant ones:

  • Titanic Belfast Museum: This museum is dedicated to the city’s most famous ship, the RMS Titanic, and its ill-fated maiden voyage. The museum is located at the site where the original ship was built, and features interactive exhibits that bring the story of the Titanic to life.

  • Crumlin Road Gaol: This prison operated for over 150 years, and was known for its harsh conditions and infamous inmates. Today, visitors can take a guided tour of the facility, including the cells, execution chamber, and underground tunnel that once connected the prison to the courthouse.

  • Ulster Museum: The Ulster Museum showcases the history, culture, and natural environment of Northern Ireland. Visitors can explore exhibits on everything from ancient Irish artifacts to modern art.

Natural Attractions of Belfast: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Belfast is also home to stunning natural landscapes and outdoor activities that are perfect for adventure seekers. Here are some of the top natural attractions in the area:

  • Cave Hill Country Park: This park is located just north of the city center, and offers excellent hiking trails and stunning views of Belfast Lough and the surrounding countryside. At the summit of Cave Hill, visitors will find the famous Napoleon’s Nose, a rock formation that is said to resemble the profile of the French emperor.

  • Stormont Estate: This sprawling parkland is located in the eastern suburbs of Belfast, and is home to the impressive Stormont Castle, which houses the Northern Ireland Assembly. The estate offers numerous walking and cycling trails, as well as a collection of sculptures and monuments.

  • Belfast Botanic Gardens: These beautiful gardens, located in the Queen’s Quarter of the city, offer a peaceful respite from the hustle and bustle of urban life. Visitors can wander through the greenhouses and gardens, and admire the exotic plant and flower species from around the world.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Belfast

Belfast provides plenty of opportunities for active travelers, whether they prefer hiking or biking. Here are some of the top routes for both:

Hiking Routes

  • Divis Ridge Trail: This trail is located in the Divis and Black Mountain area just outside Belfast, and offers stunning views of the city and beyond. The trail is well-marked and relatively easy, making it a great option for all levels of hikers.

  • Lagan Towpath: This picturesque trail follows the River Lagan through the heart of Belfast. The route is mostly flat and paved, making it a great option for walkers, runners, and cyclists.

Biking Routes

  • Comber Greenway: This 7-mile route connects Belfast to the town of Comber, passing through beautiful countryside and past historic sites.

  • Belfast City Bike Tour: This guided bike tour takes visitors on a scenic loop through the heart of the city, visiting several top sights along the way.

Best Things to Do in Belfast: Activities and Tours

In addition to the attractions and outdoor activities listed above, there are plenty of other things to see and do in Belfast. Here are some of the top activities and tours:

  • Black Cab Tours: These private tours are led by local drivers, who take visitors to see the most significant sites in Belfast’s history, including the murals and peace walls that still divide the city.

  • St. George’s Market: This indoor market takes place on Fridays, Saturdays, and Sundays, and offers a lively atmosphere and plenty of local crafts, food, and drink.

  • Crown Liquor Saloon: This famous pub, located downtown, is a must-visit for anyone interested in the city’s cultural and social history. The ornately decorated interior, complete with snugs (private booths), dates back to the Victorian era.

Week-Long Itinerary for Belfast

To help visitors make the most of their time in Belfast, here is a suggested week-long itinerary:

Day Activity
Day 1 Explore Belfast City Center: Visit City Hall, St Anne’s Cathedral, and Victoria Square shopping center
Day 2 Titanic Belfast and SS Nomadic: Learn about the story of the Titanic and its passengers, and board the SS Nomadic, the only surviving White Star Line vessel
Day 3 Black Cab Tour of Belfast Murals: Spend the day learning about the complicated history of Northern Ireland and its ongoing peace process
Day 4 Giant’s Causeway and Antrim Coast Tour: Take a day trip to see one of Northern Ireland’s most iconic natural wonders, as well as historic castles and ruined abbeys
Day 5 Cave Hill Country Park: Hike to the summit of Cave Hill for panoramic views of the city
Day 6 Ulster Museum and Botanic Gardens: Learn about Northern Ireland’s history and culture at the museum, then relax among the beautiful gardens
Day 7 Day Trip to Dublin: Take a bus tour to the Irish capital for a taste of the larger city’s culture and nightlife

Food and Drink Scene of Belfast

Belfast has a thriving culinary scene that draws on both traditional Irish cuisine and international influences. Here are some of the must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Ulster Fry: This breakfast dish is a heart attack on a plate, but undeniably delicious. It typically includes bacon, sausage, black and white pudding, eggs, mushrooms, and tomatoes.

  • Boxty: This traditional potato pancake can be served as a breakfast, lunch, or dinner dish, and is often filled with meats, cheeses, or vegetables.

Local Beverages

  • Bushmills Whiskey: This famous Irish whiskey is produced just north of Belfast, and is a must-try for whiskey lovers.

  • Harp Lager: This light beer, produced in Dublin, is a refreshing option for those looking for a crisp, easy-drinking pint.

Where to Stay in Belfast: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Belfast offers a wide range of accommodation options, from luxury hotels to budget-friendly hostels. Here are some top choices in each category:

Hotels

  • The Fitzwilliam Hotel: This luxury hotel is located in the heart of the city, and offers spacious rooms, a trendy cocktail bar, and a rooftop restaurant with stunning views.

  • Grand Central Hotel: This newly renovated hotel, located in the historic Linen Quarter, offers luxury accommodations and a rooftop bar with panoramic views of the city.

Hostels

  • Global Village: This popular hostel is located near Queen’s University, and offers dormitory-style rooms as well as private options. The hostel also has a communal kitchen and a lively bar.

  • Vagabonds: This eco-friendly hostel is located near the city center, and offers both dormitory-style and private rooms. The hostel also has a communal kitchen and a cozy lounge area.

Guesthouses

  • Windermere Guesthouse: This family-run guesthouse is located in a quiet residential area, and offers comfortable rooms and a complimentary cooked breakfast.

  • The Gregory: This stylish guesthouse is located in the Queen’s Quarter, and offers chic rooms with designer features, as well as a continental breakfast.

What to Buy in Belfast: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Belfast offers a range of unique souvenirs and local products that make great gifts or mementos of your trip. Here are some top choices:

  • Irish Linen: Belfast is known for its high-quality linen products, including tablecloths, napkins, and handkerchiefs.

  • Belleek Pottery: This beautiful porcelain china is made in Ireland, and features intricate designs and patterns.

  • Bushmills Whiskey: Bring home a bottle of this famous Irish whiskey as a gift or souvenir.

Local Culture and Customs of Belfast

Northern Ireland has a complicated social and political history, and visitors should be respectful of local customs and sensitivities. Here are a few tips:

  • Respect the Peace Walls: Belfast still has several peace walls that separate Protestant and Catholic neighborhoods. Visitors should respect these walls as symbols of ongoing tension and division in the city.

  • Avoid Political Discussions: Northern Ireland politics are complex and can be sensitive topics of discussion. Avoid making political statements or picking sides in discussions about current events.

Best Time to Visit Belfast

The best time to visit Belfast is between May and September, when the weather is generally warm and dry. However, visitors should be aware that the city can be crowded during peak season, and accommodation prices may be higher.

Do’s and Don’ts of Belfast

Do:

  • Do take a Black Cab Tour: This is a great way to learn about the city’s history and culture from a local perspective.

  • Do bring an umbrella: Belfast is notorious for its rainy weather, so come prepared with rain gear.

Don’t:

  • Don’t mention politics: Belfast is still a divided city, and discussions about politics can be contentious.

  • Don’t forget to tip: Tipping is expected in restaurants and cafes, usually around 10-15% of the total bill.

Safety and Health

Safety in Belfast

Belfast is a relatively safe city, but visitors should still take common-sense precautions to avoid theft and other crime. Be aware of your surroundings and keep valuable possessions out of sight.

Health in Belfast

Northern Ireland has a public healthcare system, and visitors from the EU can access medical treatment for free or at a reduced cost with an EHIC card. Visitors to Belfast should also be aware of the risks of food poisoning and take appropriate precautions when eating out.

Getting to Belfast: Transportation Options and Logistics

Belfast is easily accessible by air, bus, and train. Here are some transportation options:

Airports Near Belfast

  • Belfast International Airport: This airport is located 18 miles northwest of the city center, and offers regular flights to destinations across Europe and beyond.

  • George Best Belfast City Airport: This airport is located just 3 miles east of the city center, and offers flights to several UK and Irish destinations.

Bus Stations Near Belfast

  • Europa Bus Station: This bus station is located in the city center and offers connections to destinations across Northern Ireland and beyond.

  • Safeway Bus Station: This bus station is located near the city center, and offers connections to several local and regional destinations.

Other Options

  • Train: Northern Ireland Railways operates train services between Belfast and other cities in Northern Ireland and the Republic of Ireland.

  • Ferry: Regular ferry services operate between Belfast and Liverpool and Cairnryan in Scotland.

How to Get Around in Belfast

Belfast is a relatively compact city, and many of its top attractions are within walking distance of each other. However, for longer journeys or for those who prefer public transport, here are some options:

  • Metro Bus: This is the local bus network, which operates services throughout the city and surrounding suburbs.

  • Translink NI Railways: This is the local train network, which operates services between Belfast and other cities in Northern Ireland and the Republic of Ireland.

  • Belfast Bike Scheme: This is a bike rental scheme that operates throughout the city center, and offers an eco-friendly way to explore the city.

Where to Go Next from Belfast: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Belfast is ideally situated for exploring the rest of Northern Ireland, as well as other parts of the UK and Ireland. Here are some nearby destinations and excursions to consider:

  • Giant’s Causeway: This world-famous natural wonder is located on the north coast of Northern Ireland, and is easily accessible from Belfast.

  • Dublin: The Irish capital is just a few hours away by train or bus, and offers a vibrant nightlife, rich history, and world-class museums.

  • Game of Thrones Filming Locations: Fans of the HBO series can visit several filming locations in Northern Ireland, including the Dark Hedges and Castle Ward.

Conclusion

Belfast is a city that offers a unique blend of past and present, tradition and innovation, and natural beauty and urban excitement. Whether you’re interested in seeing historical sites, exploring the great outdoors, or simply enjoying the city’s vibrant cultural scene, Belfast has something to offer for everyone. Use this guide to plan your next trip to this fascinating city, and discover all that it has to offer.
