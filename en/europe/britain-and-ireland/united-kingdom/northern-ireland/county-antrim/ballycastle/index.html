Introduction to Ballycastle

Ballycastle is a picturesque seaside town located in County Antrim, Northern Ireland, United Kingdom. It lies on the north coast of Northern Ireland, where the Atlantic Ocean meets the Irish Sea, making it a popular destination for beachgoers and water sports enthusiasts. The town is also known for its rich history and culture, which can be seen through its numerous museums, historical sites, and local customs. Whether you’re looking for a relaxing vacation or an adventure-filled itinerary, Ballycastle has something for everyone.

List of Places to Visit in Ballycastle

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Bonamargy Friary: Founded in the 15th century, this well-preserved Franciscan friary features a medieval tower, a crypt, and a cemetery where some of the town’s prominent figures are buried. It is situated on the outskirts of Ballycastle, near the mouth of the river.
  • Ballycastle Museum: This small museum showcases the town’s history and heritage, from the formation of the Giant’s Causeway to the local people’s way of life. The exhibits include photographs, artifacts, and interactive displays, making it a great learning experience for all ages.
  • Kinbane Castle: Located about two miles west of Ballycastle, this imposing ruin offers breathtaking views of the sea from the top. It was built in the 16th century by the MacDonnell clan and was once a strategic stronghold during the turbulent times of Northern Ireland’s history.
  • Old Bushmills Distillery: This world-renowned distillery produces some of the finest Irish whiskey, which you can sample during a guided tour of the premises. Learn about the whiskey-making process, its history, and how to appreciate its unique flavors.

Natural Attractions of Ballycastle: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Fairhead: This imposing headland rises 600 feet above sea level, making it a popular spot for hiking and rock climbing. From the top, you can enjoy panoramic views of the coastline and beyond. The nearby Murlough Bay offers a tranquil setting for picnicking and swimming.
  • Rathlin Island: Hop on a ferry from Ballycastle to Rathlin, a tiny island that packs a big punch in terms of natural beauty and wildlife. Walk along the rugged coastline, spot seals and dolphins, and visit the island’s harbor and lighthouse.
  • The Glens of Antrim: Ballycastle lies at the heart of this scenic area, which boasts nine enchanting glens dotted with waterfalls, lakes, and forests. Take a leisurely drive along the coast road and marvel at the stunning scenery.
  • Carrick-a-Rede Rope Bridge: This iconic rope bridge spans a 30-meter-deep chasm, connecting the mainland to a tiny island. If you dare to cross it, you’ll be rewarded with panoramic views of the ocean and the rugged coastline.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Ballycastle

Ballycastle and its surrounding areas offer numerous hiking and biking trails that cater to all levels of fitness and experience. Here are some of the most popular:

  • The Moyle Way: This long-distance trail runs for 26 miles, starting from Ballycastle and ending at Glenariff Forest Park. It takes you through the Glens of Antrim and offers stunning views of the landscape.
  • Ballycastle Forest: This woodland has a network of trails that take you to panoramic viewpoints, hidden waterfalls, and wildlife habitats. It’s a great place for family picnics and nature walks.
  • Fairhead: As mentioned earlier, Fairhead offers a challenging but rewarding hike for experienced walkers. The summit rewards you with spectacular views of Rathlin Island and the Mull of Kintyre in Scotland.
  • Murlough Nature Reserve: This tranquil reserve offers a network of trails that take you through sand dunes, wetlands, and grasslands, where you can spot rare bird species and other wildlife.

Best Things to Do in Ballycastle: Activities and Tours

  • Sea Fishing: The seas around Ballycastle teem with a variety of fish, including salmon, mackerel, and trout. You can charter a boat from the harbor and try your luck in catching your dinner.
  • Golfing: Ballycastle is home to a golf course that offers stunning views of the sea and the nearby mountains. The course caters to all skill levels and is open to visitors.
  • Horse Riding: There are several equestrian centers in and around Ballycastle that offer guided horseback tours along the coastline and countryside.
  • Traditional Music Sessions: Ballycastle has a vibrant music scene, with many pubs and clubs hosting traditional music sessions throughout the week. Join in the fun and soak up the local culture.

Week-Long Itinerary for Ballycastle

Day 1: Arrive in Ballycastle, settle in your accommodation, and take a stroll along the beach. Visit the Ballycastle Museum.

Day 2: Hike up Fairhead and enjoy panoramic views of the coastline. Visit the nearby Murlough Bay and have a picnic lunch.

Day 3: Take a ferry to Rathlin Island and explore its rugged coastline. Visit the Seabird Centre and spot seals and dolphins.

Day 4: Visit the Old Bushmills Distillery and learn about the whiskey-making process. Stop by the Giant’s Causeway en route.

Day 5: Take a leisurely drive along the coastal road and explore the Glens of Antrim. Stop by the waterfalls and have a picnic lunch.

Day 6: Try your hand at sea fishing or horse riding. In the evening, attend a traditional music session in one of the local pubs.

Day 7: Have a lazy morning and relax at your accommodation. Buy some local souvenirs and products to bring back home. Depart from Ballycastle.

Food and Drink Scene of Ballycastle

Must-Try Dishes

  • Fish and Chips: Ballycastle is famous for its fish and chips, which is made from fresh locally-caught fish and served with crispy chips.
  • Ulster Fry: This hearty breakfast dish consists of fried eggs, bacon, sausages, black pudding, baked beans, and potato farls. It’s a great way to start your day.
  • Irish Stew: This hearty stew is made from lamb, potatoes, carrots, and onions, cooked slowly with herbs and spices until tender and flavorful.
  • Seafood Chowder: This creamy soup is made from a mix of seafood, vegetables, and potatoes, served with a crusty bread.

Local Beverages

  • Irish Whiskey: Ballycastle is close to the Old Bushmills Distillery, which produces some of the finest Irish whiskey in the world. Sample a dram or take a bottle home with you.
  • Guinness: This iconic stout is a must-try for anyone visiting Ireland. Many pubs in Ballycastle serve it on tap, along with other local and international beers.
  • Bushmills Black Bush: This premium Irish whiskey is known for its rich and complex flavor, with hints of fruit, nut, and vanilla. It’s a great sipping whiskey.

Where to Stay in Ballycastle: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • Marine Hotel: This historic hotel overlooks the Ballycastle Bay and offers comfortable rooms, a restaurant, and a bar.
  • An Caislean: This cozy guesthouse is located in the heart of Ballycastle and offers a warm welcome, clean rooms, and a homely atmosphere.
  • The Fullerton Arms: This charming hotel is located in the nearby village of Ballintoy and offers elegant rooms, excellent food, and a lively bar.

What to Buy in Ballycastle: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Woollen Crafts: Ballycastle has a long tradition of wool spinning and knitting, so look out for handmade scarves, hats, and gloves.
  • Traditional Music CDs: Pick up a CD of local traditional music to bring home and enjoy.
  • Irish Whiskey: As mentioned earlier, the Old Bushmills Distillery produces some of the finest Irish whiskey, which makes a great souvenir or gift.
  • Ballycastle Picture Frame: Take home a local picture frame as a memory of your trip to Ballycastle.

Local Culture and Customs of Ballycastle

  • Music and Dance: Ballycastle has a vibrant music and dance scene, with many pubs hosting traditional music sessions and ceilidhs throughout the year.
  • Respect for Nature: The people of Ballycastle have a deep respect for their natural surroundings, and visitors are encouraged to do the same. Don’t litter or harm the local wildlife and respect the local customs and traditions.
  • Friendly and Welcoming: The people of Ballycastle are known for their friendly and welcoming nature, so don’t be afraid to strike up a conversation with them.

Best Time to Visit Ballycastle

The best time to visit Ballycastle is from May to September when the weather is warm and sunny, and the tourist season is in full swing. However, keep in mind that this is also the busiest time, so expect crowds and higher prices. If you prefer a quieter, off-season visit, consider coming during the autumn or winter months, when the scenery is still stunning and the local culture is in full swing.

Do’s and Don’ts of Ballycastle

Do:

  • Respect the local customs: Whether it’s in public, in someone’s home, or in a restaurant, be mindful of the local customs and traditions.
  • Take care of the environment: Ballycastle is known for its natural beauty, so help preserve it by not littering and respecting the wildlife.
  • Try the local food and drink: Ballycastle has a rich culinary scene, so take advantage of it and try some local specialties.

Don’t:

  • Be disrespectful: Ballycastle locals pride themselves on their friendliness, so be respectful in return. Avoid being rude or confrontational.
  • Litter and harm the environment: As mentioned earlier, take care of the environment and keep it clean for future generations.
  • Be afraid to ask for help: If you need help or directions, don’t hesitate to ask for assistance. The locals are happy to help visitors.

Safety and Health

Safety in Ballycastle

Ballycastle is a safe destination for travelers, with low crime rates and a strong sense of community. However, it’s always a good idea to take normal precautions and keep your valuables secure. When venturing into the outdoors, take appropriate safety gear and follow the advice of local guides.

Health in Ballycastle

Ballycastle has a modern healthcare system, with a hospital, clinics, and pharmacies available. Make sure you have appropriate travel insurance that covers any medical emergencies. If you have any pre-existing medical conditions, consult with your doctor before traveling. The tap water in Ballycastle is safe to drink.

Getting to Ballycastle: Transportation Options and Logistics

Ballycastle is easily accessible by several modes of transportation, including:

  • By Air: The closest airport is Belfast International Airport, which is about 60 miles away from Ballycastle. From there, you can rent a car or take a bus or taxi to Ballycastle.
  • By Bus: Ulsterbus operates a regular bus service between Belfast and Ballycastle, with several stops along the way. The journey takes about 2 hours.
  • By Car: Ballycastle is about a 2-hour drive from Belfast, depending on traffic. There are several car rental agencies available at the airport and in Belfast city center.

Airports Near Ballycastle

  • Belfast International Airport: Located in Aldergrove, about 60 miles away from Ballycastle. It offers direct flights to several UK and European destinations.

Bus Stations Near Ballycastle

  • Ballycastle Bus Station: Located in the town center, this station serves Ulsterbus routes between Ballycastle and Belfast, Coleraine, and other nearby towns.

Other Options

  • Ferry: A ferry service operates between Ballycastle and Rathlin Island.

How to Get Around in Ballycastle

Ballycastle is a small town, and its attractions are within walking distance from each other. However, if you prefer to explore further afield, the following transportation options are available:

  • Car: Renting a car allows you to travel at your own pace and explore the surrounding areas. There are several car rental agencies available in Ballycastle and nearby towns.
  • Bicycle: Ballycastle has several bike rental shops where you can rent bicycles for a day or longer. This is a great way to explore the coastline and countryside.
  • Public Transport: Ulsterbus operates a regular bus service between Ballycastle and nearby towns, including Coleraine and Belfast.

Where to Go Next from Ballycastle: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Giant’s Causeway: This iconic natural wonder is just a short drive away from Ballycastle.
  • Portrush: This popular seaside town offers sandy beaches, water sports, and a great nightlife scene.
  • Derry/Londonderry: This historic walled city is a cultural hub of Northern Ireland, with its impressive architecture, museums, and galleries.
  • Donegal: This rugged and unspoiled region of Ireland is just a short ferry ride away from Ballycastle.

Conclusion

Ballycastle is a charming, laid-back town that offers a rich mix of natural beauty, outdoor adventures, cultural experiences, and warm hospitality. Whether you’re a solo traveler, a couple, a family, or a group of friends, there’s something for everyone in Ballycastle. Come and explore this hidden gem on the north coast of Northern Ireland, and create memories that will last a lifetime.
