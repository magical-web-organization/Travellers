Omagh Travel Guide

Located in County Tyrone, in Northern Ireland, Omagh boasts a rich history and natural beauty. Whether you’re interested in learning about Irish culture and history, exploring the great outdoors, or indulging in local cuisine, Omagh has something for everyone.

Introduction to Omagh

Omagh is a charming town located in the heart of Northern Ireland. Known for its friendly locals and beautiful scenery, Omagh is a must-visit destination for anyone traveling to the Emerald Isle.

List of Places to Visit in Omagh

  • Ulster American Folk Park
  • Omagh Community House
  • Gortin Glen Forest Park
  • An Creagan Visitor Centre
  • Sheelin Antique Irish Lace Museum
  • Sacred Heart Church

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

One of the best ways to experience Omagh’s rich culture is by visiting its museums and historical places. Discover the history of Irish immigrants in America at the Ulster American Folk Park. Take a trip back in time at Omagh Community House, which features exhibitions on traditional Irish life. Learn about traditional Irish crafts and music at An Creagan Visitor Centre.

Natural Attractions of Omagh: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Omagh is also known for its natural beauty. Explore the great outdoors at Gortin Glen Forest Park, where you can hike, cycle, and spot local wildlife. Enjoy stunning views of Omagh and the surrounding countryside from the top of Mullaghcarn Mountain. Take a stroll through the beautiful gardens at the Parkanaur Forest Park.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Omagh

If you’re a fan of hiking or cycling, you’ll love Omagh. There are plenty of routes to choose from, including the Gortin Glen Forest Park trail, which takes you through ancient woodlands and past stunning waterfalls. Alternatively, head to the Moorlough Shore trail, a scenic route that takes you along the shores of Lough Neagh.

Best Things to Do in Omagh: Activities and Tours

From guided tours to adventure activities, there’s always something to do in Omagh. Take a tour of the Ulster American Folk Park and learn about the Irish emigration to America. Hit the water with a kayaking tour or try your hand at fishing on the local rivers and lakes. If you’re feeling adventurous, take on the high ropes course at Todds Leap Activity Centre.

Week-Long Itinerary for Omagh

Day 1: Explore the Ulster American Folk Park Day 2: Hike or cycle in Gortin Glen Forest Park Day 3: Visit An Creagan Visitor Centre and learn about traditional Irish crafts Day 4: Take a guided tour of Omagh Community House Day 5: Explore the stunning gardens at Parkanaur Forest Park Day 6: Enjoy a day of adventure activities at Todds Leap Activity Centre Day 7: Relax and explore Omagh at your own pace

Food and Drink Scene of Omagh

Omagh’s food and drink scene is not to be missed. Sample traditional Irish dishes like Irish stew, colcannon, and boxty at The Weigh Inn Restaurant. For a sweet treat, head to Sheelin Antique Irish Lace Museum for some homemade scones and tea.

Must-Try Dishes

  • Irish Stew
  • Colcannon
  • Boxty

Local Beverages

  • Guinness
  • Irish whiskey

Where to Stay in Omagh: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Omagh has a range of accommodation options, from budget-friendly hostels to luxury hotels. Stay in the heart of town at the Mellon Country Inn, or enjoy a rural retreat at the Carleton Cottage.

What to Buy in Omagh: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

If you’re looking for souvenirs to bring home, Omagh has plenty to offer. Pick up a traditional Irish tweed cap at Magee’s, or browse the handmade pottery at Castle Fayre. For something truly unique, head to Sheelin Antique Irish Lace Museum to browse their collection of handmade lace.

Local Culture and Customs of Omagh

Omagh has a rich history and welcoming locals. Be sure to show respect for Irish culture and traditions during your visit. Always ask permission before taking photographs of people or private property, and be mindful of local customs when interacting with locals.

Best Time to Visit Omagh

Omagh is a year-round destination, but the best time to visit is from May through September, when the weather is warm and the days are long. This is also peak tourist season, so be sure to book your accommodation and activities early.

Do’s and Don’ts of Omagh

Do:

  • Explore the great outdoors
  • Try traditional Irish dishes
  • Learn about Irish culture and history

Don’t:

  • Disrespect local customs and traditions
  • Overlook the stunning natural beauty of Omagh
  • Forget your rain jacket - the weather can be unpredictable!

Safety and Health

Safety in Omagh

Omagh is a generally safe destination, but visitors should take the usual precautions when traveling. Be aware of your surroundings, keep your belongings close, and avoid traveling alone at night.

Health in Omagh

Northern Ireland has a high-quality healthcare system, and visitors can access medical treatment if necessary. It is recommended to have travel insurance to cover any unexpected medical expenses.

Getting to Omagh: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Omagh

The closest airport to Omagh is the City of Derry Airport, located 42 miles away. Belfast International Airport is also within driving distance.

Bus Stations Near Omagh

Visitors can travel to Omagh via bus from Belfast, Derry, and other major cities in Northern Ireland. The Omagh bus station is located in the town center.

Other Options

Visitors can also travel to Omagh by train, taking the train to Derry or Belfast and then connecting to another train or bus to Omagh.

How to Get Around in Omagh

Omagh is a small town that can easily be explored on foot. For those who prefer to travel by car, there are several car rental companies located in town. Taxis are also available.

Where to Go Next from Omagh: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Derry/Londonderry: Explore the historic walled city of Derry/Londonderry, located less than an hour’s drive from Omagh.
  • Belfast: Spend a day exploring Northern Ireland’s capital city, located less than two hours from Omagh.
  • Donegal: Cross the border into the Republic of Ireland and explore the stunning countryside of Donegal, just a short drive from Omagh.

Conclusion

Whether you’re interested in history, nature, or adventure, Omagh has something to offer every type of traveler. Plan your visit today and discover all that this charming town has to offer.
