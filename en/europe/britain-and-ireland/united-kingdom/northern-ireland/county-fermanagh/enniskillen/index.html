Introduction to Enniskillen

Enniskillen is a town located in County Fermanagh, in the western part of Northern Ireland. It is situated on an island in the middle of Lough Erne, which makes it a popular destination for outdoor enthusiasts and nature lovers. The town has a rich history that dates back to the 16th century, and it is known for its stunning natural beauty, culture, and vibrant food and drink scene. In this travel guide, we will explore the best places to visit, things to do, where to stay, and more in Enniskillen.

List of Places to Visit in Enniskillen

Enniskillen offers a variety of attractions for visitors to explore. Here are the top places to visit in Enniskillen.

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Enniskillen is home to several museums and historical places that offer a glimpse into its past.

  • Enniskillen Castle: Built in the 16th century, this castle is now a museum that showcases the history and culture of County Fermanagh.
  • Castle Coole: Situated just outside the town, this 18th-century mansion boasts stunning architecture and beautiful gardens.
  • The Buttermarket: This Georgian building has been transformed into a cultural center that features art exhibits, concerts, and performances.

Natural Attractions of Enniskillen: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Enniskillen is surrounded by stunning natural beauty, making it an excellent destination for outdoor activities and scenic views.

  • Lough Erne: This large lake offers visitors a chance to explore its many islands, go fishing, or take a boat tour.
  • Cuilcagh Mountain: Located just outside of Enniskillen, this mountain offers excellent hiking trails and breathtaking views.
  • Marble Arch Caves Global Geopark: This unique landscape offers visitors a chance to explore underground caves and incredible rock formations.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Enniskillen

Enniskillen has several hiking and biking routes for visitors to explore. Here are some of the best routes:

  • Cuilcagh Way: This 20-mile long trail takes hikers through the beautiful Cuilcagh Mountain landscape.
  • Lough Navar Forest Drive: Visitors can explore the forest and scenic views on this stunning drive.
  • Blessingbourne Mountain Bike Trails: This mountain biking trail offers a variety of routes for different skill levels.

Best Things to Do in Enniskillen: Activities and Tours

Enniskillen offers a variety of activities and tours for visitors to explore. Here are some of the best things to do in Enniskillen:

  • Boat Tours: Take a boat tour on Lough Erne and explore the many islands and stunning scenery.
  • Fishing: Enniskillen is known for its excellent fishing opportunities, with many lakes and rivers in the area.
  • Marble Arch Caves Tour: Take a tour of the underground caves and geological wonders at the Marble Arch Caves Global Geopark.
  • Fermanagh Fun Farm: This family-friendly farm offers activities such as animal encounters and tractor rides.

Week-Long Itinerary for Enniskillen

For visitors who want to explore all that Enniskillen has to offer, here is a suggested week-long itinerary:

  • Day 1: Enniskillen Castle and the Buttermarket.
  • Day 2: Lough Erne boat tour and visit to Devenish Island.
  • Day 3: Explore the Marble Arch Caves and Global Geopark.
  • Day 4: Visit Castle Coole and the Fermanagh Lakeland Forum.
  • Day 5: Cuilcagh Mountain hike and Lough Navar Forest Drive.
  • Day 6: Fishing trip and visit to the Fermanagh County Museum.
  • Day 7: Fermanagh Fun Farm and relaxation at Lough Erne Resort.

Food and Drink Scene of Enniskillen

Enniskillen has a vibrant food and drink scene that showcases Irish cuisine and local products.

Must-Try Dishes

  • Boxty: A traditional potato pancake that is served with various savory fillings.
  • Champ: A potato dish made with scallions and butter.
  • Soda Bread: A type of bread made with baking soda instead of yeast.

Local Beverages

  • Guinness: A famous Irish beer that is brewed in Dublin.
  • Whiskey: Irish whiskey is renowned for its smooth taste and quality.
  • Cider: Enniskillen is known for producing excellent cider, made from locally grown apples.

Where to Stay in Enniskillen: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Enniskillen offers a variety of accommodation options to suit different budgets and preferences. Here are some of the best places to stay in Enniskillen:

  • Lough Erne Resort: A luxury resort that offers stunning views of Lough Erne.
  • Belmore Court and Motel: A comfortable and affordable motel in the heart of Enniskillen.
  • The Westville Hotel: A modern hotel that offers excellent amenities and room service.
  • The Enniskillen Hotel: A boutique hotel that is centrally located in the town.

What to Buy in Enniskillen: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Enniskillen offers a variety of unique and locally made products that make great souvenirs. Here are some of the best things to buy in Enniskillen:

  • Belleek Pottery: A fine porcelain pottery that has been made in Enniskillen since the 19th century.
  • Fermanagh Black Bacon: A type of bacon that is traditionally made in County Fermanagh.
  • Craft Beer: Enniskillen is known for producing some of the best craft beer in Northern Ireland.

Local Culture and Customs of Enniskillen

Enniskillen has a rich cultural heritage and traditions that visitors can experience during their stay. Here are some of the local customs and culture of Enniskillen:

  • Traditional Music: Enniskillen has a vibrant traditional music scene that can be enjoyed in local pubs and at festivals.
  • Irish Dancing: Visitors can experience traditional Irish dancing at local ceilidhs and events.
  • Festivals: Enniskillen hosts several festivals throughout the year, including the Fermanagh Live Festival and the Enniskillen Comic Fest.

Best Time to Visit Enniskillen

The best time to visit Enniskillen is during the summer months, from June to August, when the weather is warm and sunny. However, Enniskillen is a year-round destination, with plenty to do and see during the off-season as well.

Do’s and Don’ts of Enniskillen

When visiting Enniskillen, it is important to be aware of the local customs and etiquette. Here are some do’s and don’ts:

Do:

  • Respect the natural environment and wildlife.
  • Be open to experiencing local customs and traditions.

Don’t:

Safety and Health

Enniskillen is a safe and relatively crime-free town. However, visitors should take standard precautions to ensure their safety.

Safety in Enniskillen

  • Be aware of your surroundings and avoid carrying large amounts of cash.
  • Lock your doors and windows when leaving your accommodation or rental car.
  • Do not leave valuables in plain sight in your car.

Health in Enniskillen

  • Bring any necessary medication and medical supplies with you.
  • Drink bottled water or boil tap water before drinking.
  • Use sunscreen and insect repellent to protect your skin.
