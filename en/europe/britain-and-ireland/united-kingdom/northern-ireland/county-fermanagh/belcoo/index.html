Introduction to Belcoo

Belcoo is a charming village located in County Fermanagh, Northern Ireland. With its picturesque landscapes, rich history, and warm hospitality, Belcoo has become a popular destination among tourists who are looking for an authentic Irish experience. From hiking and biking to exploring museums and historical landmarks, there are plenty of things to do and see in Belcoo.

List of Places to Visit in Belcoo

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

The Museums and Historical Places in Belcoo are significant in revealing the exciting history and culture of the place. The Marble Arch Caves Global Geopark is a unique experience, embracing natural history and geology. The Enniskillen Castle and the Belleek Pottery Visitor Centre are popular for their interesting artifacts and captivating collections. The Cuilcagh Boardwalk Trail boasts panoramic views of the mountains and lakes that will leave you awestruck.

Natural Attractions of Belcoo: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Belcoo lets you experience natural beauty like no other place in the world. The Lough MacNean Sculpture Trail and the Lough Navar Forest Walks offer breathtaking views of the lakes and the hills that surround the village. The Beaghmore Stone Circles, a UNESCO World Heritage Site, provide an insight into the ancient history of the place. Belcoo is small but full of surprises, and every step you take will take you closer to Mother Nature.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Belcoo

Belcoo is known for its beautiful landscapes and trails that are ideal for hikers and bikers. From challenging mountain hikes to family-friendly strolls, Belcoo has something for everyone. Some of the popular hiking and biking trails are the Cuilcagh Boardwalk Trail, the Castlebeg Trail, the Cuilcagh Way, and the Marble Arch Caves Walking Trails.

Best Things to Do in Belcoo: Activities and Tours

Belcoo offers a range of activities and tours that will keep you entertained throughout your stay. The Marble Arch Caves are a must-visit destination, where you can see stunning underground caves and waterfalls. You can also take a boat ride on the Lough Erne, or go fishing in the nearby lakes. The village is also home to several golf courses and horse riding stables, which offer a unique experience for golfers and horse enthusiasts alike.

Week-Long Itinerary for Belcoo

If you’re planning a week-long trip to Belcoo, here’s a sample itinerary that you can follow for a memorable experience:

  • Day 1: Visit Enniskillen Castle and the Belleek Pottery Visitor Centre.
  • Day 2: Hike the Cuilcagh Way and take in the scenic views of the mountains and lakes.
  • Day 3: Explore the Marble Arch Caves and take a boat ride on the Lough Erne.
  • Day 4: Visit the Beaghmore Stone Circles and learn about the ancient history of the place.
  • Day 5: Go golfingor horse riding at one of the local courses or stables in Belcoo.
  • Day 6: Take a leisurely stroll along the Lough MacNean Sculpture Trail.
  • Day 7: Shop for souvenirs and local products at one of the many shops in the village.

Food and Drink Scene of Belcoo

Must-Try Dishes

Belcoo is not only known for its natural beauty but also for its rich culinary heritage. Here are some of the must-try dishes in Belcoo:

  • Irish Stew: A hearty traditional dish made with tender lamb, potatoes, and vegetables.
  • Boxty: A savory pancake made with grated potatoes and flour, often served with bacon or smoked salmon.
  • Soda Bread: A staple of Irish cuisine, this bread is made with flour, baking soda, and buttermilk.
  • Guinness Pie: A meat pie filled with succulent beef and vegetables, seasoned with the famous Irish stout.

Local Beverages

No trip to Belcoo is complete without tasting some of the famous local beverages. Here are some of the must-try drinks in Belcoo:

  • Guinness: A dark Irish beer that is known all around the world.
  • Bushmills: A famous Irish whiskey that has been distilled in the country for over 400 years.
  • Irish coffee: A delicious mix of whiskey, coffee, and cream, the Irish coffee is the perfect end to a meal.

Where to Stay in Belcoo: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Belcoo offers a range of accommodation options, from budget-friendly hostels to luxurious hotels. Some of the popular places to stay in Belcoo are the Customs House Country Inn, which offers stunning views of the lake, and the Killinagh House, which is known for its warm hospitality and comfortable rooms.

What to Buy in Belcoo: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Belcoo is a great place to find unique souvenirs and local products that you can bring back home. Some of the popular items to buy are:

  • Belleek Pottery: Beautifully crafted ceramics made by the famous Belleek Pottery Company.
  • Traditional Irish Woolens: Hand-woven scarves, hats, and sweaters made from the finest Irish wool.
  • Irish Whiskey: Take home a bottle of Bushmills or Jameson, some of the finest Irish whiskeys.
  • Irish Potatoes: A unique souvenir, these are small, edible potatoes hand-picked from family farms in the area.

Local Culture and Customs of Belcoo

Belcoo has a rich cultural heritage that is reflected in its festivals, traditions, and customs. The village celebrates several festivals throughout the year, including the Belcoo Agricultural Show, the Harvest Thanksgiving Festival, and the St. Patrick’s Day parade. The people of Belcoo are known for their warm hospitality and friendly nature, making visitors feel welcome and at home.

Best Time to Visit Belcoo

The best time to visit Belcoo is from May to September when the weather is mild and pleasant, and outdoor activities are in full swing. However, the village’s beauty can be experienced throughout the year, and its winter activities, including ice fishing and snowshoe trekking, are equally popular among adventure enthusiasts.

Do’s and Don’ts of Belcoo

Do:

  • Do greet people with a smile and a friendly “hello”.
  • Do try the local cuisine and beverages.
  • Do explore the natural beauty of the village.
  • Do show respect for the local customs and traditions.

Don’t:

  • Don’t litter or damage the natural surroundings.
  • Don’t be loud or disruptive in public spaces.
  • Don’t forget to dress appropriately for the weather.

Safety and Health

Safety in Belcoo

Belcoo is a safe place to visit, with low crime rates. However, visitors must still practice caution and keep an eye on their belongings when in public spaces. It is also important to be vigilant while on hiking or biking trails to avoid accidents.

Health in Belcoo

Belcoo has a good standard of healthcare facilities, and pharmacies are available for minor ailments and emergencies. Visitors are advised to carry their necessary medication and travel insurance in case of any unforeseen circumstances.

How to Get Around in Belcoo

Belcoo is a small village that can easily be explored on foot. Visitors can also hire a bicycle or a car to explore the surrounding areas. Local taxis are also available for convenient transportation within the village.

Where to Go Next from Belcoo: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Belcoo is surrounded by several popular destinations that visitors can explore, including:

  • Enniskillen: A bustling town with a rich history and stunning architecture, located just 17 km from Belcoo.
  • Donegal: A picturesque town located 90 km from Belcoo, known for its scenic beauty and friendly atmosphere.
  • Sligo: A seaside town located 123 km from Belcoo, famous for its stunning landscapes and historic landmarks.

Conclusion

Belcoo is a unique and charming village that offers visitors a refreshing break from the hustle and bustle of city life. Whether you’re looking for natural beauty, rich heritage, or warm hospitality, Belcoo has something to offer everyone. Plan your trip to Belcoo today, and explore the beauty and wonders of this hidden gem in Northern Ireland.
