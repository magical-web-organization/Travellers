Introduction to County Fermanagh

County Fermanagh is a picturesque region located in Northern Ireland, nestled between the stunning Lough Erne system and the border with the Republic of Ireland. With its rich history, magnificent landscapes, and welcoming culture, Fermanagh is a destination that you won’t want to miss.

With vast waterways, rolling hills, serene forests, and charming villages, County Fermanagh offers a variety of unforgettable experiences for visitors to explore. From championship golf courses and watersports to historical landmarks and stunning national parks, there’s something for everyone in Fermanagh.

This guide offers a detailed look at the best places to visit, things to do, food and drink scene, and local culture. Whether you’re a seasoned traveler or a first-time visitor, this guide will help you plan an unforgettable trip to County Fermanagh.

List of Places to Visit in County Fermanagh

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Fermanagh is rich in history, and there are plenty of cultural attractions to explore.

Enniskillen Castle

Enniskillen Castle is a 600-year-old fortress located in the heart of Enniskillen town. Today, it houses two museums: The Fermanagh County Museum and The Inniskillings Museum. Both exhibits showcase the history and culture of this fascinating region, with interactive displays, artifacts, and exhibits.

Devenish Island

Devenish Island is a small island located in Lower Lough Erne, an area renowned for its natural beauty. It’s home to an ancient monastery that dates back to the 6th century, as well as numerous other historic ruins, including a round tower, church, and high cross.

Natural Attractions of County Fermanagh: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

County Fermanagh offers breathtaking views and endless opportunities for outdoor enthusiasts.

Marble Arch Caves Global Geopark

The Marble Arch Caves are a natural wonder and a must-visit attraction. The caves form part of the Marble Arch Caves Global Geopark, which is a UNESCO-designated site. Visitors can take guided tours of the caves and learn about the geology and history of this fascinating region.

Lough Navar Forest

Located near the village of Derrygonnelly, Lough Navar Forest offers stunning views of Upper Lough Erne and beyond. There are several walking and biking trails throughout the forest, offering visitors a chance to explore the natural beauty of the area.

Hiking/Biking Routes of County Fermanagh

Fermanagh is a paradise for hikers and bikers, with numerous routes to choose from.

Cuilcagh Way

The Cuilcagh Way is a 33 km long-distance trail that takes visitors through the rugged landscapes of Fermanagh and Cavan. The highlight of the trail is the Cuilcagh Boardwalk, which takes hikers to the summit of the famous Cuilcagh Mountain.

Kingfisher Trail

The Kingfisher Trail is a 360 km circular route that takes visitors through the stunning landscapes of Fermanagh, Cavan, and Leitrim. The trail follows the rivers and lakes of the area, offering breathtaking views and endless opportunities for outdoor adventures.

Best Things to Do in County Fermanagh: Activities and Tours

Fermanagh offers a variety of activities and tours for visitors of all ages.

Water-Based Activities

Fermanagh is renowned for its watersports, with numerous lakes and rivers offering plenty of opportunities for activities such as boating, kayaking, paddleboarding, and fishing. There are also several marinas and boat rental companies throughout the region.

Golfing

Fermanagh is home to several championship golf courses, including the luxurious Lough Erne Resort. With its breathtaking views and challenging courses, Fermanagh is a dream destination for golfers.

Week-Long Itinerary for County Fermanagh

Here is a suggested itinerary for a week-long trip to County Fermanagh:

Day 1: Enniskillen Town

Start your trip in Enniskillen town and explore its historic landmarks and cultural attractions, including Enniskillen Castle and the Fermanagh County Museum.

Day 2: Upper Lough Erne

Take a boat tour from Enniskillen and explore the breathtaking landscapes of Upper Lough Erne, stopping off at Devenish Island and other historic landmarks.

Day 3: Cuilcagh Way

Spend a day hiking the Cuilcagh Way and exploring the rugged landscapes of the area.

Day 4: Lough Navar Forest

Explore the stunning scenery of Lough Navar Forest and take in the views of Upper Lough Erne and beyond.

Day 5: Marble Arch Caves

Take a guided tour of the Marble Arch Caves and learn about the geology and history of the area.

Day 6: Water-Based Activities

Try your hand at watersports on the lakes and rivers of Fermanagh, or take a leisurely boat trip to explore more of the stunning scenery.

Day 7: Golfing

Finish your trip with a round of golf at one of Fermanagh’s championship courses, such as the Lough Erne Resort.

Food and Drink Scene of County Fermanagh

Fermanagh boasts a vibrant food and drink scene, with plenty of locally sourced ingredients and traditional dishes.

Must-Try Dishes

Fermanagh is renowned for its traditional Irish cuisine, with dishes such as soda bread, Irish stew, and seafood chowder on many menus. Other local specialties include boxty, a type of potato pancake, and coddle, a hearty stew made with bacon, sausages, and potatoes.

Local Beverages

Fermanagh is home to several breweries and distilleries, including the Fermanagh Beer Company and Boatyard Distillery. Visitors can try the local beers and spirits at several bars and restaurants throughout the region.

Where to Stay in County Fermanagh: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Fermanagh offers a range of accommodation options to suit every budget and preference.

Lough Erne Resort

The Lough Erne Resort is a luxurious 5-star hotel located on the shores of Lower Lough Erne. With its two championship golf courses and stunning scenery, the resort offers the ultimate Fermanagh experience.

Belmore Court and Motel

The Belmore Court and Motel is a stylish and affordable option located in the heart of Enniskillen town. The hotel offers a range of rooms and suites to suit all budgets, as well as on-site parking and a fitness center.

What to Buy in County Fermanagh: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

From handcrafted pottery to locally produced beer, Fermanagh offers a range of unique souvenirs and products to take home.

Belleek Pottery

Belleek Pottery is a world-renowned brand that has been producing fine china for over 160 years. Visitors can take a guided tour of the pottery and shop for unique souvenirs and gifts.

Fermanagh Beer Company

The Fermanagh Beer Company produces a range of award-winning beers, including the popular Inishmacsaint Ale. Visitors can purchase the beers at the brewery or at several local shops and restaurants.

Local Culture and Customs of County Fermanagh

Fermanagh has a rich cultural heritage, with traditional music, dance, and storytelling still a vibrant part of the local community.

Best Time to Visit County Fermanagh

Fermanagh is a year-round destination, with something to offer visitors in every season. However, the best time to visit is usually from May to September, when the weather is warm and the water sports and outdoor activities are in full swing.

Do’s and Don’ts of County Fermanagh

Do:

  • Try traditional Irish dishes such as soda bread and coddle.
  • Take a boat tour of the Lakes.
  • Explore the local history and culture at Enniskillen Castle and other attractions.
  • Take advantage of the outdoor activities, such as hiking, biking, fishing, and watersports.

Don’t:

  • Forget to pack a raincoat and warm clothing, as the weather can be unpredictable.
  • Ignore the local customs and traditions, such as saying “hello” to fellow walkers and hikers on the trails.

Safety and Health

Safety in County Fermanagh

Fermanagh is a safe destination for travelers, with low crime rates and a friendly local community. However, visitors should take basic precautions, such as avoiding unlit areas at night and keeping valuables secure.

Health in County Fermanagh

Fermanagh has a reliable healthcare system, with several hospitals and medical clinics located throughout the region. Visitors should ensure that they have adequate travel insurance before their trip, as healthcare can be expensive for non-residents.

Getting to County Fermanagh: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near County Fermanagh

The nearest airports to Fermanagh are Belfast International Airport, Belfast City Airport, and Dublin Airport, all of which offer connections to major cities and destinations around the world.

Bus Stations Near County Fermanagh

There are several bus stations located throughout County Fermanagh, including in Enniskillen and Irvinestown. Bus Eireann and Ulsterbus both provide services to and from Fermanagh, with connections to major cities such as Belfast and Dublin.

Other Options

Visitors can also drive to Fermanagh, with several major highways connecting the region to other parts of Northern Ireland and the Republic of Ireland. Additionally, there are several ferry services that connect Northern Ireland with other parts of the UK.

How to Get Around in County Fermanagh

Visitors can rent a car, take public transportation, or hire a taxi to get around County Fermanagh. Additionally, there are several bike rental companies throughout the region, providing an eco-friendly and more active way to explore the area.

Where to Go Next from County Fermanagh: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

There are several nearby destinations and excursions that visitors can enjoy after exploring County Fermanagh, including:

  • Belfast, Northern Ireland’s capital city
  • Derry/Londonderry, a vibrant and historic city located in Northern Ireland
  • The Giant’s Causeway, a stunning natural wonder and a UNESCO World Heritage Site

Conclusion

County Fermanagh is a hidden gem in Northern Ireland, offering visitors a chance to explore its rich history, stunning landscapes, and vibrant culture. With its variety of attractions, activities, and accommodation options, Fermanagh is a destination that you won’t want to miss. Whether you’re a history buff, an outdoor enthusiast, or a foodie, this guide provides everything you need to plan an unforgettable trip.
