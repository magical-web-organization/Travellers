Introduction to Lurgan

Lurgan is a town in County Armagh, located in Northern Ireland in the United Kingdom. It is a vibrant town with a rich history, beautiful landscapes, and a friendly local community that welcomes visitors with open arms. Lurgan offers something for everyone, whether you are interested in history, culture, outdoor activities or gastronomy.

List of Places to Visit in Lurgan

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Brownlow House

Brownlow House, also known as Lurgan Castle, is a beautiful 19th-century mansion that was built in the Scottish Baronial style. It is located in the heart of Lurgan and is considered to be one of the most significant historical buildings in the town. The house has been used for a variety of purposes, including as a military hospital during World War II. Today, it serves as a venue for various events and weddings.

Craigavon Museum Services

Craigavon Museum Services, located in the town centre, is a must-visit for history buffs. The museum is home to a vast collection of artefacts, photographs, and documents that showcase the rich history and heritage of Lurgan and the surrounding area. Admission is free, and there are guided tours available.

Shankill Graveyard

Shankill Graveyard is one of the oldest graveyards in Lurgan and has been in use for more than 400 years. It is the final resting place of some of the most influential families in the town’s history, including the Brownlows and the McCanns. It is a peaceful and beautiful place to visit, with its impressive monuments and tombstones.

Natural Attractions of Lurgan: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Lurgan Park

Lurgan Park is the largest public park in the town and covers more than 60 acres. It is an ideal location for a family day out or a peaceful stroll. It has a lake, picnic areas, a play park for children, and beautiful gardens to explore. Throughout the year, the park hosts various events, including concerts, fairs, and fireworks displays.

Oxford Island Nature Reserve

Oxford Island Nature Reserve is a stunning nature reserve located on the shores of Lough Neagh, the largest freshwater lake in the British Isles. The reserve covers more than 140 acres and is home to a variety of wildlife, including birds, otters, and badgers. Visitors can enjoy walks along the shore, bird watching, and boat trips.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Lurgan

Lurgan and the surrounding area have plenty of options for hiking and biking enthusiasts. The following are some popular routes:

Craigavon Lakes

The Craigavon Lakes walk is a picturesque route that takes visitors around the lakes. The route is approximately four miles long and is perfect for a leisurely stroll or a jog. It starts and ends in the town centre and passes through Lurgan Park.

Loughgall Country Park

Loughgall Country Park is located just a few miles from Lurgan and offers a variety of walking and cycling routes. The park covers more than 200 acres and is home to a golf course, an equestrian centre, a fishing lake, and a children’s play park.

Best Things to Do in Lurgan: Activities and Tours

Lurgan has a variety of activities and tours to suit all ages and interests. Here are some of the best ways to spend your time in Lurgan:

Golf

Lurgan Golf Club is a beautiful 18-hole parkland course that offers a challenging game for golf enthusiasts. The club has an excellent reputation and has hosted several high-profile tournaments over the years.

Boat Trip on Lough Neagh

Take a boat trip on Lough Neagh and discover the beauty of the largest freshwater lake in the British Isles. Boat trips are available from Oxford Island, and visitors can enjoy views of the stunning landscapes and wildlife.

Week-Long Itinerary for Lurgan

Day 1

Explore the town centre and visit Brownlow House, Craigavon Museum Services, and Shankill Graveyard.

Day 2

Take a stroll in Lurgan Park, have a picnic, and enjoy the beautiful gardens.

Discover the beauty of Lough Neagh with a boat trip.

Day 4

Day 5

Day 7

Take a golfing lesson or play a round at Lurgan Golf Club.

Food and Drink Scene of Lurgan

Must-Try Dishes

Ulster Fry

The Ulster Fry is a popular local dish that consists of bacon, eggs, sausage, black pudding, white pudding, and fried bread. It is served with soda bread and is a hearty and delicious breakfast.

Tayto Crisps Sandwich

A Tayto Crisps Sandwich is a traditional Northern Irish snack that involves putting crisps into a sandwich. The most popular flavour is cheese and onion.

Local Beverages

Guiness

Guinness is one of the most popular beers in Ireland and is available in most pubs and restaurants in Lurgan.

Bushmills Whiskey

Bushmills Whiskey is a world-renowned whiskey that is produced in County Antrim, just a short drive from Lurgan. Many pubs in Lurgan serve Bushmills Whiskey, which is enjoyed by locals and visitors alike.

Where to Stay in Lurgan: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Lurgan has several options for accommodation, ranging from budget-friendly hostels to luxurious hotels. Some popular options include:

Glenavon Hotel

Glenavon Hotel is a four-star hotel located in the town centre. It offers comfortable rooms, excellent dining options, and a fitness centre.

Lurgan West Lodge

Lurgan West Lodge is a cosy guesthouse located just a few miles from Lurgan. It offers comfortable rooms and is ideal for those looking for a quiet and peaceful stay.

What to Buy in Lurgan: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Linen Products

Linen has a long and rich history in Northern Ireland, and Lurgan is no exception. Visitors can purchase high-quality linen products, including tablecloths, napkins, and towels, from local shops and markets.

Pottery

Handcrafted pottery is a popular product in Lurgan. Visitors can purchase unique and beautiful pieces from local artists and pottery shops.

Local Culture and Customs of Lurgan

Lurgan has a rich cultural heritage, and visitors can experience this through the town’s events, music, and local traditions. The Lurgan Show, held annually in June, is a popular event that showcases the best of local produce, crafts, and farming.

Best Time to Visit Lurgan

The best time to visit Lurgan is during the summer months, from June to August, when the weather is warm and sunny. However, Lurgan has a lot to offer all year round, and visitors can enjoy the beautiful landscapes and activities in all seasons.

Do’s and Don’ts of Lurgan

Do:

  • Respect the local traditions and customs.
  • Try the local food and drink.
  • Visit the cultural and historical attractions.
  • Be mindful of the environment and wildlife.

Don’t:

  • Litter or damage the local landscape.
  • Disrespect the local culture and traditions.
  • Be loud or disruptive in public places.
  • Leave valuables unattended in public areas.

Safety and Health

Safety in Lurgan

Lurgan is generally a safe town, and visitors can enjoy their stay without any safety concerns. However, as with any destination, it is advisable to be cautious and aware of your surroundings.

Health in Lurgan

Lurgan has several medical facilities, including a hospital and clinics. Visitors with pre-existing medical conditions should ensure that they have adequate medical insurance before travelling.

Getting to Lurgan: Transportation Options and Logistics

Lurgan is easily accessible by public transport and is located approximately one hour from Belfast and two hours from Dublin. The following are some transportation options:

Airports Near Lurgan

Belfast International Airport and George Best Belfast City Airport are both located within a 40-minute drive from Lurgan.

Bus Stations Near Lurgan

The local bus station provides regular services to Belfast and the surrounding areas.

Other Options

Visitors can also hire a car or use a taxi service to get to Lurgan.

How to Get Around in Lurgan

Lurgan is a compact town and is ideal for walking or cycling. There are also local buses and taxis available for those who prefer not to walk.

Where to Go Next from Lurgan: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Lurgan is an excellent base for exploring the surrounding area and nearby attractions, such as:

  • Belfast city centre and the Titanic Museum.
  • The Giant’s Causeway, a stunning natural attraction located on the north coast of Northern Ireland.
  • The Mourne Mountains, perfect for hiking or exploring.
  • The beautiful city of Dublin, located two hours away.
