Introduction to Newtownards

Newtownards is a small town located in County Down, in the northeast of Northern Ireland, United Kingdom. The town is situated on the east coast of the Country, and it is surrounded by beautiful countryside, mountains, and the sea. Newtownards is a popular tourist destination, and it is known for its rich history, natural beauty, and cultural attractions. Whether you’re looking to explore the town’s cultural heritage, experience the great outdoors, or simply relax, there’s something for everyone to enjoy in Newtownards.

List of Places to Visit in Newtownards

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Newtownards has a rich history, and the town boasts several museums and historical places that are worth visiting. Some of the most popular cultural attractions in Newtownards include:

  • Scrabo Tower: This iconic tower is a prominent landmark in Newtownards. It was built in 1857 and provides stunning panoramic views over the surrounding countryside.
  • Ards Arts Centre: This cultural hub is located in the old Town Hall building and showcases a diverse range of exhibitions, performances, and workshops throughout the year.
  • Somme Heritage Centre: This museum offers a fascinating insight into the history of the First World War and the role that the 36th (Ulster) Division played in the Battle of the Somme.

Natural Attractions of Newtownards: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Newtownards is blessed with stunning natural scenery, which offers endless opportunities for outdoor adventures and scenic views. Some of the most popular natural attractions in Newtownards include:

  • Strangford Lough: This vast marine reserve is the largest sea lough in the British Isles, and it is home to a rich diversity of marine life and seabirds. Visitors can explore the lough via boat tours, or walk the many coastal trails.
  • Mount Stewart: This 18th-century house and gardens are located on the shores of Strangford Lough. The gardens are internationally renowned for their rare plants and beautiful design, and the house offers a fascinating peek into the lives of the aristocracy in the 19th and 20th centuries.
  • Clandeboye Estate: This vast private estate covers over 2000 acres of woodland and countryside, and visitors can explore the many walking and cycling trails that wind through the property.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Newtownards

If you’re an outdoor enthusiast, Newtownards has plenty of hiking and biking trails to explore. Some of the best routes include:

  • The Comber Greenway: This 7-mile traffic-free route follows the old Belfast to Comber railway line. The route is suitable for walkers, cyclists, and wheelchair users, and it provides stunning views over the surrounding countryside.
  • Scrabo Country Park: This beautiful park is located on the hillside overlooking Newtownards, and it offers several walking trails of varying lengths and difficulties.
  • Mount Stewart Trails: Mount Stewart has several walking trails that wind through the estate’s gardens and woodland. Highlights include the lake walk and the red squirrel trail.

Best Things to Do in Newtownards: Activities and Tours

Newtownards offers a wide range of activities and tours for visitors to enjoy. Whether you’re interested in culture, history, or outdoor adventures, there’s something for everyone. Some of the best things to do include:

  • Boat Tour of Strangford Lough: A boat tour is one of the best ways to explore the beauty of Strangford Lough. There are several tour providers in the area, and visitors can choose from wildlife tours, heritage tours, and scenic tours.
  • Ards Peninsula Heritage Trail: This self-guided driving tour takes visitors on a journey through the history of the Ards Peninsula. The route covers over 25 miles and includes several important historical sites.
  • Mourne Mountains Tour: The Mourne Mountains are located a short drive from Newtownards, and they offer some of the best hiking and mountain biking routes in Northern Ireland. Several tour providers offer guided tours of the area.

Food and Drink Scene of Newtownards

The food and drink scene in Newtownards is varied and vibrant, with plenty of options to suit all tastes and budgets. Some of the must-try dishes and local beverages include:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Ulster Fry: This hearty breakfast dish features eggs, bacon, sausages, black and white pudding, tomatoes, mushrooms, and soda bread.
  • Irish Stew: This traditional dish is made with lamb, potatoes, onions, and carrots, and it’s often served with soda bread.
  • Fish and Chips: Newtownards is located on the coast, and there are plenty of fish and chips shops in the town serving up fresh seafood.

Local Beverages

  • Guinness: No trip to Ireland is complete without a pint of the famous black stuff, and there are plenty of pubs in Newtownards serving up a perfect pint of Guinness.
  • Bushmills Whiskey: This famous Irish whiskey is distilled in the nearby town of Bushmills, and visitors can take a tour of the distillery to learn more about the production process.
  • Cider: Newtownards is home to several local cider producers, and visitors can sample their products at local pubs and restaurants.

Where to Stay in Newtownards: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

There are plenty of accommodation options in Newtownards, ranging from budget-friendly hostels to luxury hotels. Some of the best places to stay in Newtownards include:

  • The Old Inn: This historic hotel is located in the heart of Newtownards, and it offers traditional Irish hospitality and comfortable accommodation.
  • The Portaferry Hotel: This hotel is located on the shores of Strangford Lough, and it offers stunning views and easy access to the surrounding countryside.
  • The Ballyholme Bay Guest House: This guesthouse is located close to the beach and offers comfortable accommodation at an affordable price.

What to Buy in Newtownards: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

If you’re looking for the perfect souvenir to bring home from your trip to Newtownards, there are plenty of options to choose from. Some of the best local products include:

  • Pottery: Newtownards has a rich history of pottery production, and there are several local potters still working in the area.
  • Artwork: The Ards Arts Centre and other local galleries showcase the work of many talented artists in the area.
  • Seaside-themed Gifts: With its location on the coast, Newtownards has plenty of shops selling seaside-themed gifts such as seashell jewelry, postcards, and ornaments.

Local Culture and Customs of Newtownards

The people of Newtownards are known for their warm hospitality and friendly nature. Visitors to the town should respect local customs, such as:

  • Greetings: Always greet people with a smile and a polite greeting. Handshakes are the most common form of greeting in Northern Ireland.
  • Tipping: In restaurants, it is customary to tip 10-15% of the total bill if the service was good. Tipping is not expected in pubs.
  • Language: English is the primary language spoken in Newtownards, although many locals also speak Irish.

Best Time to Visit Newtownards

The best time to visit Newtownards depends on your interests and preferences. The summer months (June-August) offer the warmest weather and longest daylight hours, making it the perfect time for outdoor activities and sightseeing. The holiday season (late November to early January) offers festive events and celebrations, particularly around Christmas and New Year. Spring (March-May) and Autumn (September-November) are generally less crowded and offer milder weather, making them ideal for hiking and exploring the countryside.

Safety and Health

Safety in Newtownards

Newtownards is a safe place to visit, with low levels of crime and a friendly and welcoming community. Visitors should still take normal precautions, such as locking doors and windows, not leaving valuables in parked cars, and being aware of their surroundings at all times.

Health in Newtownards

Northern Ireland has an excellent public healthcare system, and visitors to Newtownards can access medical care through local hospitals and clinics. Visitors should ensure that they have adequate medical insurance before traveling to the area.

Getting to Newtownards: Transportation Options and Logistics

Newtownards is located approximately 10 miles from the city of Belfast, and it is easily accessible by car, bus, and train.

Airports Near Newtownards

  • Belfast International Airport: This is the biggest airport in Northern Ireland, and it is located approximately 30 miles from Newtownards.

  • George Best Belfast City Airport: This airport is located approximately 8 miles from Newtownards.

Bus Stations Near Newtownards

  • Europa Bus Centre in Belfast

  • Bangor Bus Station

Other Options

  • Train: There are direct trains from Belfast to Newtownards, and the journey takes approximately 20 minutes.

  • Car: Newtownards is easily accessible by car, and there are several car rental options available in Belfast.

How to Get Around in Newtownards

The best way to get around Newtownards is by car, as public transportation options are limited. Taxis are also available, but they can be expensive. Visitors who prefer to walk or cycle can explore the town on foot or rent a bike.

Where to Go Next from Newtownards: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Newtownards is a great base to explore the surrounding area, which offers plenty of attractions and destinations to visit. Some of the best nearby destinations and excursions include:

  • Belfast: This vibrant city is located approximately 10 miles from Newtownards, and it offers a wide range of cultural, historical, and entertainment options for visitors.
  • The Causeway Coast: This stunning coastline is located a few hours’ drive from Newtownards, and it offers spectacular natural scenery, picturesque villages, and famous attractions such as the Giant’s Causeway.
  • The Ards Peninsula: The Ards Peninsula offers beautiful coastal landscapes, wildlife reserves, and historic sites.

Conclusion

Newtownards is a charming and welcoming town that offers something for everyone. Whether you’re interested in history, culture, outdoor adventures, or simply relaxing, you’ll find plenty to see and do in this beautiful part of Northern Ireland. With its rich heritage, stunning natural scenery, and warm hospitality, Newtownards is definitely worth a visit.
