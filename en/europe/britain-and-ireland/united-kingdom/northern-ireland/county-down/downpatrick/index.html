Introduction to Downpatrick

Located in the coastal county of Down in Northern Ireland, Downpatrick is a charming town with a rich history and plenty of outdoor adventures. With its famous St. Patrick’s Cathedral, the town is a popular destination for religious and cultural tourism. Plan your trip to Downpatrick with our comprehensive travel guide and discover the best things to do and see in the area.

List of Places to Visit in Downpatrick

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

1. St. Patrick’s Cathedral

The cathedral is the final resting place of Ireland’s patron saint. It is believed that Saint Patrick was buried here in the 5th century, and his burial site is marked by a granite slab. The cathedral houses a collection of fascinating artifacts and religious relics.

2. Downpatrick & County Down Railway

The railway is an excellent way to learn about the history of rail transport in Northern Ireland. Visitors can enjoy a steam train ride and take in the stunning views of the countryside.

3. Down County Museum

Located in the center of the town, the museum tells the story of the area from Prehistoric times to the present day. It has a wide range of exhibits and artifacts, including an ancient Irish logboat and a Viking sword.

Natural Attractions of Downpatrick: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

1. Strangford Lough

Strangford Lough is a gorgeous estuary that provides ample opportunities for bird watching, kayaking, boating, and fishing. The lough is home to a diverse range of seabirds and marine life.

2. Mourne Mountains

Rising to over 800 meters, the Mourne Mountains are a stunning range of granite peaks that offer amazing views across the surrounding countryside. Visitors can hike along the many walking trails and take in the breathtaking scenery.

3. Tyrella Beach

Tyrella Beach is a beautiful sandy beach located to the south of the town. It is a popular destination for swimming, sunbathing, and picnicking. The beach is backed by sand dunes and is home to a variety of wildlife.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Downpatrick

There are several hiking and biking trails in Downpatrick that offer visitors a chance to explore the beautiful natural scenery of the area. Some of the popular trails include:

  • Saint Patrick’s Way: A 137 km ancient pilgrimage route that runs from Armagh to Downpatrick.
  • The Mourne Way: A 40 km waymarked trail in the majestic Mourne Mountains.
  • Castle Trail: A 9.5 km circular route that leads to the ruins of Dundrum Castle.

Best Things to Do in Downpatrick: Activities and Tours

1. Saint Patrick Centre

The center is an award-winning visitor attraction that tells the story of Saint Patrick’s life and his legacy. It has interactive exhibits, a 360-degree audio-visual show, and a recreated monastic garden.

2. Downpatrick Racecourse

If you’re looking for a bit of entertainment, you can head over to the Downpatrick Racecourse. The course hosts several horse racing events throughout the year.

3. Game of Thrones Filming Locations Tour

Fans of the popular TV series can take a tour of the filming locations nearby Downpatrick. Tour highlights include the iconic Dark Hedges and Castle Ward, where many of the series’ scenes were filmed.

Week-Long Itinerary for Downpatrick

Day 1: Visit St. Patrick’s Cathedral, explore the Downpatrick & County Down Railway, and tour the Down County Museum.

Day 2: Take a hike in the Mourne Mountains, visit Tollymore Forest Park, and have lunch in Newcastle.

Day 3: Enjoy a day at Tyrella Beach, rent a kayak, and take in the stunning views of the coast.

Day 4: Take a trip to Strangford, visit the Castle Ward Estate, hike along the Lecale Way, and try some fresh seafood at one of the local restaurants.

Day 5: Play a round of golf at the Royal County Down Golf Club, take a walking tour of the town, and have an evening at Downpatrick Racecourse.

Day 6: Take a tour of the Game of Thrones filming locations or visit the Inch Abbey ruins and surrounding countryside.

Day 7: Enjoy some retail therapy and have lunch in the town center, take a walking tour of the local pubs, and sample some local brews at The Kilclief Castle Bar.

Local Culture and Customs of Downpatrick

The local culture in Downpatrick is friendly and welcoming, and visitors will often be greeted with a warm smile and a friendly hello. The area has a rich history, and locals are proud of their heritage and traditions. When visiting Downpatrick, it’s important to respect the local customs and traditions.

Best Time to Visit Downpatrick

The best time to visit Downpatrick is between June and September when the weather is warm and sunny, and there are several outdoor events and festivals happening. However, visitors can enjoy the town’s charm and beauty all year round.

Do’s and Don’ts of Downpatrick

Do:

  • Try the local cuisine and drinks.
  • Visit the historical sites and landmarks.
  • Respect the local culture and traditions.
  • Try outdoor activities, such as hiking and kayaking.

Don’t:

  • Be disrespectful towards the local culture.
  • Litter or damage the natural environment.
  • Ignore safety guidelines when doing outdoor activities.

Safety and Health

Safety in Downpatrick

Downpatrick is generally a safe place to visit. However, visitors are advised to take the usual precautions and keep valuables safe when exploring the town. The phone number for emergencies in Northern Ireland is 999.

Health in Downpatrick

The healthcare system in Northern Ireland is excellent, and visitors can access medical treatment if needed. It’s recommended that visitors have travel insurance in case of any medical emergencies.

Getting to Downpatrick: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Downpatrick

The closest airport to Downpatrick is George Best Belfast City Airport, which is approximately 45 kilometers away. Other airports within a reasonable distance include Belfast International Airport and Dublin Airport.

Bus Stations Near Downpatrick

Downpatrick has a bus station with regular services to Belfast and other nearby areas. The Ulsterbus website has up-to-date timetables and fares.

Other Options

Visitors can also take a taxi or rent a car to get to Downpatrick. It’s advisable to book taxis and rental cars in advance to avoid any inconvenience.

How to Get Around in Downpatrick

The best way to get around Downpatrick is on foot or by bicycle. Visitors can also take a taxi or rent a car if needed. The town center is compact and walkable, and there are several bike rental shops in the area.

Where to Go Next from Downpatrick: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Some suggestions for nearby destinations and excursions include:

  • Belfast: The capital city of Northern Ireland is just an hour’s drive away from Downpatrick and is known for its rich history and vibrant culture.
  • Castlewellan Forest Park: A stunning park with walking trails, mountain biking, and a lake for fishing.
  • Strangford Lough: A beautiful estuary that provides ample opportunities for bird watching, kayaking, and boating.

Conclusion

Downpatrick is a charming town with a rich history and plenty of outdoor adventures. From hiking in the Mourne Mountains to exploring the cultural landmarks, there is something for everyone in this beautiful region. Plan your trip today and experience the best that Downpatrick has to offer.
