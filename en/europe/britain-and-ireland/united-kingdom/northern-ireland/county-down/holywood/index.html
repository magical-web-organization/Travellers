Introduction to Holywood

Located in County Down, Northern Ireland, Holywood is a beautiful town filled with historical and cultural attractions. It is located just 6 miles east of Belfast and enjoys stunning views of Belfast Lough. Famous for its connections to Hollywood in California, Holywood is a charming and vibrant town that offers visitors a great blend of indoor and outdoor activities.

List of Places to Visit in Holywood

There are plenty of exciting places to visit in Holywood. Here are some of the top attractions to add to your list:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Ulster Folk Museum: Located just a short drive from Holywood, this museum offers an immersive experience of Northern Ireland’s rural way of life in the early 20th century.
  • Titanic Belfast: This museum is dedicated to the history of the Titanic and its tragic sinking on its maiden voyage.
  • North Down Museum: Learn about the story of the North Down area from prehistoric times and the arrival of Christianity to medieval and modern times.

Natural Attractions of Holywood: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Scrabo Hill: A 540-acre country park where visitors can enjoy panoramic views of Strangford Lough, the Mourne Mountains and the surrounding countryside.
  • Crawfordsburn Country Park: Enjoy scenic walks through woodland, along the coastal path or relax on the sandy beach.
  • Ballycopeland Windmill: One of Ireland’s few working windmills, visitors can see the inner workings of this ancient trade.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Holywood

Holywood is home to some beautiful hiking and biking trails; here are a few worth exploring:

  • The Lagan Towpath: A 11-mile traffic-free route from Belfast to Lisburn where visitors can enjoy beautiful scenery.
  • Cave Hill Country Park: A 370-acre park with excellent walking and hiking trails that provide stunning views of Belfast and County Down.
  • Comber Greenway: A 7-mile traffic-free route from Dee Street in East Belfast to Comber through urban and rural areas.

Best Things to Do in Holywood: Activities and Tours

Holywood is a perfect destination for those who love outdoor adventures. Here are a few must-try activities:

  • Holywood Golf Club: One of the best golf courses in Northern Ireland, this club is situated on the slopes of the Holywood Hills.
  • Holywood to Bangor Coastal Walk: A beautiful 10-mile walk that provides some of the best views of the North Down coastline.
  • Boat Tour to Copeland Islands: Take a boat tour of the Copeland Islands off the coast of Holywood and experience some of the best bird watching areas in Ireland.

Week-Long Itinerary for Holywood

Here is a suggested itinerary for a week in Holywood:

Day Activity
Day 1 Visit Ulster Folk Museum and Titanic Belfast Museum.
Day 2 Hiking the Lagan Towpath and spending the afternoon at Crawfordsburn beach.
Day 3 Comber Greenway bike ride, followed by lunch at Ballycopeland Windmill.
Day 4 Spend the day in Belfast exploring its city centre.
Day 5 Take a Boat tour to the Copeland Islands for bird watching.
Day 6-7 Spend time golfing and exploring Holywood town.

Food and Drink Scene of Holywood

Holywood is famous for its culinary delights, you will be excited to indulge in the local cuisine, and the list of must-try dishes is remarkably long.

Must-Try Dishes

  • Ulster Fry: A local breakfast comprising of bacon, sausages, black and white pudding, fried eggs, tomatoes, and mushrooms.
  • Seafood Chowder: A delicious seafood stew made with local ingredients.
  • Comber Potatoes: These locally grown potatoes are known for their unique flavour.

Local Beverages

  • Guinness: A classic Irish beverage that is a must-try when visiting Holywood.
  • Bushmills Irish Whiskey: Made in the small town of Bushmills in Northern Ireland, this whiskey is world-renowned for its distinctive flavour.
  • Crisp white wine: From the Rathfriland region, these wines are made from grapes grown in the local soil, providing an authentic taste of Northern Ireland.

Where to Stay in Holywood: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Holywood offers a host of accommodation options, ranging from luxury hotels to affordable hostels and guesthouses. Here are some of the popular accommodation options:

  • Culloden Estate & Spa: A luxurious hotel located in the heart of Holywood, offering splendid views of Belfast Lough.
  • Rayanne House: A historic guesthouse overlooking the scenic Belfast Lough that offers guests a cosy and relaxed stay.
  • The Old Inn: A charming hotel located in Crawfordsburn, one of the most picturesque villages in Northern Ireland.

What to Buy in Holywood: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Holywood offers plenty of shopping options for visitors looking to take home a piece of Northern Ireland. Here are a few recommendations:

  • Ballyholme Pottery: Handcrafted pottery made on the outskirts of Holywood.
  • Irish Linen: Known for its quality and craftsmanship, this traditional Irish product can be purchased from various stores in and around Holywood.
  • Whiskey: Bushmills Irish Whiskey is a famous souvenir to take home and enjoy.

Local Culture and Customs of Holywood

The people of Holywood are warm, hospitable and proud of their heritage. They are welcoming to visitors and often keen to share their stories and knowledge with them. One of the cultural highlights of Holywood is the annual Holywood Harmony Festival, celebrating traditional and modern music.

Best Time to Visit Holywood

The best time to visit Holywood is in the summer months (June to August), when the weather is generally warm and sunny. However, the shoulder months of April, May, September and October are also lovely, with fewer crowds and cooler temperatures.

Do’s and Don’ts of Holywood

Do:

  • Respect the local customs and traditions of Holywood.
  • Try the local food and drinks.
  • Pack a comfortable and waterproof jacket.
  • Enjoy the stunning natural landscapes of North Down.

Don’t:

  • Make loud noises or disturb the peace of the area.
  • Litter or vandalize public properties.
  • Ignore the signs and safety rules while hiking or biking.

Safety and Health

Safety in Holywood

Holywood is generally a safe destination for visitors. However, it is still advised to take necessary precautions like locking valuables in a safe and avoid wandering alone in unlit areas at night.

Health in Holywood

Northern Ireland provides free medical care to all citizens and visitors in case of emergencies. There are several pharmacies and hospitals in and around Holywood.

Getting to Holywood: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Holywood

The nearest airport to Holywood is George Best Belfast City Airport, which is only a 3-mile drive away.

Bus Stations Near Holywood

The main bus station serving Holywood is Europa Bus Centre in Belfast, which is well connected to major cities in Northern Ireland.

Other Options

Travelers can easily hire a cab or rent a car at the airport or other locations. Taxis and buses are also available in Holywood.

How to Get Around in Holywood

Holywood is a compact town, and the best way to explore it is on foot. Taxis, buses, and car rental services are also available for ease of travel.

Where to Go Next from Holywood: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Northern Ireland is a small country, and there are many excellent destinations and excursions to explore from Holywood. Here are some of the popular ones:

  • Belfast: The capital city of Northern Ireland is just 6 miles away from Holywood, with its vibrant city centre and bustling nightlife.
  • Giant’s Causeway: This natural UNESCO World Heritage site is a must-visit, located about 60 miles away from Holywood.
  • Mount Stewart: An 18th-century house and garden located on the eastern shore of Strangford Lough, 6 miles south of Holywood.

Conclusion

Holywood is a beautiful town with a charming character that boasts cultural and historical attractions, natural beauty, outdoor adventures, and a food and drink scene that is not to be missed. It provides a perfect blend of the modern and traditional, making it an ideal destination for solo travelers, couples, and families.
