Introduction to Strangford

Located in County Down in Northern Ireland, Strangford is a picturesque village that offers visitors the perfect blend of natural beauty, cultural attractions, and outdoor adventure. The village is surrounded by stunning landscapes that take in the winding waters of Strangford Lough, rolling hills, and lush forests. The village is also steeped in history and heritage, reflected in the ancient ruins, museums, and historic buildings that dot the area. In this comprehensive travel guide to Strangford, we’ll delve into the top sights and attractions of this amazing village, from museums and hikes to the best places to stay and eat.

List of Places to Visit in Strangford

Strangford has a plethora of sights, attractions, and activities to keep visitors entertained. Here is a list of some of the must-visit places in Strangford:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Strangford Castle: A 16th-century castle ruins that overlooks the picturesque Strangford Lough.
  • Castle Ward: An 18th-century mansion that offers visitors a glimpse into the lives of the aristocrats who lived there.
  • St. Patrick’s Cathedral: A 12th-century cathedral that is one of the oldest churches in Ireland.

Natural Attractions of Strangford: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Strangford Lough: A beautiful, winding body of water that offers visitors the chance to enjoy kayaking, sailing, and other water sports.
  • Tollymore Forest Park: A vast forest park that offers visitors hiking trails, camping sites, and stunning vistas of the surrounding countryside.
  • Mount Stewart Estate and Gardens: A stunning 18th-century estate that boasts leading gardens and a lake.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Strangford

Strangford is a hiker’s and biker’s paradise, with a range of trails that cater to all fitness levels. Here are some of the top hiking and biking routes in Strangford:

  • The Castle Ward Trail: A stunning trail that passes through the ancient ruins of Strangford Castle and takes visitors through breathtaking forests and countryside.
  • The Strangford Lough Trail: A popular biking route that allows visitors to take in the scenic beauty of the lough and surrounding hills.
  • The Ulster Way: A long-distance walking trail that traverses the entire length of Northern Ireland and offers hikers a unique insight into Ireland’s culture and history.

Best Things to Do in Strangford: Activities and Tours

From outdoor adventures to cultural activities, Strangford has something for everyone. Here are some of the must-do activities and tours in Strangford:

  • Take a Boat Tour of Strangford Lough: Enjoy a guided boat tour of the picturesque Strangford Lough and learn about the history and ecology of the area.
  • Visit the Ulster Museum: Located in Belfast, the Ulster Museum is home to a vast collection of art, history, and science exhibits that are sure to capture the imagination of visitors.
  • Take a Horseback Riding Tour: Explore the rugged beauty of Strangford on horseback with one of the many horseback riding tours offered in the area.

Week-Long Itinerary for Strangford

For those wanting to make the most of their visit to Strangford, here is a week-long itinerary that covers all the top sights and attractions:

Day Activities
1 Visit Strangford Castle and learn about its rich history
2 Explore the trails and stunning vistas of Tollymore Forest Park
3 Take a guided tour of Strangford Lough, and enjoy kayaking or sailing if you’re up to the challenge
4 Visit St. Patrick’s Cathedral, one of Ireland’s oldest churches
5 Take a trip to Mount Stewart Estate and Gardens for a leisurely stroll or picnic
6 Enjoy a horseback riding tour and experience the rugged beauty of Strangford
7 Visit Belfast for a day trip to the Ulster Museum and a spot of shopping

Food and Drink Scene of Strangford

No travel guide is complete without mentioning the local food and drink scene. From traditional delicacies to modern cuisine, Strangford has something for every foodie.

Must-Try Dishes

  • Boxty: A traditional Irish dish made with grated potatoes and served with bacon and cabbage.
  • Fish and Chips: You can’t visit the UK without trying this quintessential dish made with battered fish and chips.
  • Irish Stew: A hearty stew made with lamb, potatoes, and carrots.

Local Beverages

  • Guinness: Ireland’s most famous beer is a must-try for any visitor to Strangford.
  • Irish Coffee: A classic cocktail made with Irish whiskey and coffee.
  • Bushmills Whiskey: Northern Ireland’s famed whiskey is a must-try for any visitor to Strangford.

Where to Stay in Strangford: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Strangford offers a range of excellent accommodation options to suit all budgets. Here are some of the top-rated places to stay in Strangford:

  • The Cuan Guesthouse: A beautiful guesthouse located right on the waterfront and offering stunning views of the lough.
  • The Lobster Pot: This cozy pub doubles up as a guesthouse and offers visitors the chance to stay in its comfortable rooms while sampling the local cuisine.
  • The Portaferry Hotel: An excellent choice for those wanting a bit of luxury, this hotel is located in the heart of Strangford and offers guests comfortable rooms and top-quality amenities.

What to Buy in Strangford: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

No trip is complete without bringing back a few souvenirs for loved ones back home. Here are some of the local products that you can walk away with:

  • Irish wool products: From scarves to sweaters, Ireland’s woolen products make for excellent gifts.
  • Guinness merchandise: Take a piece of Ireland’s most famous beer home with you by purchasing some Guinness merchandise.
  • Irish Whiskey: Buy a bottle or two of Ireland’s famed whiskey to enjoy in the comfort of your own home.

Local Culture and Customs of Strangford

Strangford is a village steeped in history and traditions. The locals are known for their friendly and welcoming nature, so don’t hesitate to strike up a conversation and learn more about the area’s culture and customs.

Best Time to Visit Strangford

While Strangford is a year-round destination, the best time to visit is during the summer months when the weather is warm and mild. The months of July and August offer the most comfortable temperatures for outdoor activities, while May and September offer a quieter atmosphere and fewer tourists as the summer season starts to wind down.

Do’s and Don’ts of Strangford

To ensure you have a smooth and enjoyable trip to Strangford, here are some do’s and don’ts to keep in mind:

Do:

  • Respect the locals: The locals of Strangford are friendly and welcoming, so be sure to show them the same respect and courtesy.
  • Take your time and explore: Strangford is a laid-back village, so take your time and explore the area at your own pace.

Don’t:

  • Litter: Keep the village clean by disposing of your trash responsibly.
  • Overindulge in alcohol: Alcohol abuse is frowned upon in the area.

Safety and Health

Safety in Strangford

Strangford is a safe village with low crime rates. However, it is always advisable to take necessary precautions while travelling, like keeping your valuables locked away and being aware of your surroundings.

Health in Strangford

Strangford boasts excellent health care facilities and services, so visitors can rest assured that they are well taken care of in case of any medical emergencies.

Getting to Strangford: Transportation Options and Logistics

Here are the most convenient ways to get to Strangford:

Airports Near Strangford

The nearest airport to Strangford is the Belfast International Airport, which is about 44 km away from the village.

Bus Stations Near Strangford

Ulsterbus operates bus services to Strangford from Belfast and other major cities in Northern Ireland.

Other Options

Visitors can also drive to Strangford from Belfast or take a taxi.

How to Get Around in Strangford

Strangford is a small village that can easily be explored on foot or by bike. Visitors can also rent a car or book a tour that includes transportation.

Where to Go Next from Strangford: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Here are some of the top destinations and excursions to visit after exploring Strangford:

  • Belfast: This vibrant city is located just a short drive away from Strangford and offers visitors a chance to explore its rich history, culture, and lively nightlife.
  • The Giant’s Causeway: This famous natural landmark is located about 120 km from Strangford and is well worth a visit.
  • Dublin: Located about 170 km from Strangford, the capital city of Ireland is a must-visit destination that offers visitors a unique insight into Ireland’s history and culture.

Conclusion

Strangford is a charming, historic village that offers visitors a glimpse into the area’s rich history and culture. Whether you’re looking to explore the winding waters of Strangford Lough, hike in the hills and forests, or simply relax and enjoy the local food and drink scene, Strangford has something for everyone. We hope this comprehensive travel guide has given you all the information you need to plan your trip to this amazing village!
