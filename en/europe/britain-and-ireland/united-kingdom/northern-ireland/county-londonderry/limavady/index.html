Introduction to Limavady

Limavady is a small town located in County Londonderry, in the Northern Ireland country, part of the United Kingdom. The town is situated on the Roe Valley, surrounded by stunning natural beauty. Limavady has a rich cultural history and offers a wide range of attractions for tourists, including interesting historical sites, sprawling natural parks, and delicious traditional cuisine.

List of Places to Visit in Limavady

Limavady boasts several must-see attractions that are worth putting on your itinerary. Below are some of the best places to visit in Limavady:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Roe Valley Arts and Cultural Centre

This cultural center is one of the best attractions in Limavady. The center boasts a wide range of events, exhibitions and performances, including theater, music, art, and comedy. It also has a café and a gift shop.

Limavady Museum

This museum gives visitors a glimpse into the town’s history, showcasing artifacts dating back to the Paleolithic era. The museum’s displays also highlight some of the key periods in the town’s history, including the plantation, industrial revolution, and the two World Wars.

Downhill Palace

Situated on a hill overlooking the Atlantic Ocean, the Downhill Palace is a tour of quintessential Limavady. The palace was built by the Earl of Bristol as a summer home and features a beautiful library, several chapels, and lush gardens.

Natural Attractions of Limavady: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Roe Valley Country Park

Roe Valley Country Park is a paradise for outdoor enthusiasts and adventure seekers. You can enjoy a leisurely stroll along the Roe River or take a more demanding hike along the trails that wind deep into the valley.

Gortmore Viewpoint

Gortmore Viewpoint offers breathtaking views of the surrounding landscape, including the islands off the coast of County Donegal. The viewpoint is situated on top of the hill and it is the perfect spot for a romantic sunset or a picnic.

Benone Beach

Benone Beach is the jewel in Limavady’s crown. The beach is seven miles long and is perfect for a stroll, cycling, or surfing. There are plenty of picnic spots, playgrounds, and barbecue areas available on the beach.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Limavady

Limavady offers an impressive network of hiking and biking trails. Some of the most popular ones include the Boar’s Den, the East West Trail and the Lodge Trail. The trails provide stunning views of the valley and the surrounding forests.

Best Things to Do in Limavady: Activities and Tours

Limavady offers plenty of activities and tours that showcase the town’s beauty and unique spirit. Some of the best activities include:

Kayaking Down the Roe River

Kayaking is an excellent way to enjoy the natural beauty of Roe River. The calm waters make it an appropriate activity for beginners, and there are many packages to choose from.

Limavady Ghost Walk

The Limavady Ghost Walk is a perfect way to explore the spooky side of the town’s history. You will learn about the town’s haunting past, including the terrifying story of a former Town Hall execution.

Week-Long Itinerary for Limavady

If you are planning a week-long trip to Limavady, you can easily fill up your itinerary with the following:

  • Day 1: Tour Limavady Museum, visit Downhill Palace and the gardens
  • Day 2: Explore the Roe Valley Country Park
  • Day 3: Visit Roe Valley Arts and Cultural Center and the stunningly beautiful Gortmore Viewpoint
  • Day 4: Enjoy Benone Beach and the surrounding areas
  • Day 5: Hike or bike down at one of Limavady’s popular trails.
  • Day 6: Take the Limavady Ghost Walk and enjoy a live theatre performance.
  • Day 7: Try some traditional local food, buy souvenirs and explore the local culture by interacting with the locals.

Food and Drink Scene of Limavady

Limavady is home to several restaurants and cafes that specialize in local cuisine. Here are some of the best places to sample the local dishes:

Must-Try Dishes

Irish Stew

Irish stew is a meat-based dish that usually contains lamb, potatoes, carrots, and onions. The dish is usually served with soda bread or white bread.

Derry Boiled Bacon and Cabbage

Derry boiled bacon and cabbage is another popular dish in Limavady. It is served with mashed potatoes and a creamy white sauce.

Champ

Champ is a traditional Irish dish made from mashed potatoes, milk, butter, and chopped scallions.

Local Beverages

Guinness

Guinness is a dark beer with a creamy texture and a unique taste. It is the most popular beer in Ireland.

Whiskey

Irish whiskey is made from malted and unmalted barley. It is often aged in oak barrels for at least three years.

What to Buy in Limavady: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Limavady is known for its traditional woolen products, pottery, and jewelry. Some of the most popular souvenirs to buy in Limavady include woolen jumpers, scarves, and shawls. Other popular items include local pottery and handcrafted jewelry.

Local Culture and Customs of Limavady

Limavady has a rich cultural history, and the locals take pride in preserving their traditions and customs. The town is known for its love of traditional music, poetry, and literature. One of the best ways to experience local culture is by attending one of the many music festivals or cultural events held throughout the year. The locals are friendly and welcoming, and visitors are encouraged to engage in conversations with them.

Best Time to Visit Limavady

The best time to visit Limavady is during the spring or summer months (May-August), as the weather is mild, and the scenery is breathtaking. The autumn months offer stunning foliage, and the winter months are ideal for winter sports such as skiing.

Do’s and Don’ts of Limavady

Do:

  • Engage with the locals, they are extremely friendly and are happy to share their experiences with visitors.
  • Try the traditional Irish food and drinks.
  • Respect local customs and traditions.
  • Explore the natural parks, they are stunning and offer a unique escape from the hustle and bustle of daily life.

Don’t:

  • Litter or leave the environment dirty.
  • Overconsume alcohol.
  • Insult or belittle the local culture or customs.

Safety and Health

Safety in Limavady

Although Limavady is a safe place, visitors should take standard precautions, such as not leaving valuables unattended and avoiding isolated areas during the night.

Health in Limavady

Visitors should ensure they have adequate travel insurance and are vaccinated against any diseases before visiting Limavady. They should also carry essential medicines, as some of them might not be readily available.

Getting to Limavady: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Limavady

The nearest airport to Limavady is City of Derry Airport.

Bus Stations Near Limavady

Limavady bus station is the best option for those arriving from other parts of Northern Ireland. Buses run throughout the day and night, making it a convenient option for visitors.

Other Options

Visitors can also rent a car and drive to Limavady. The town is well connected to other parts of Northern Ireland via a network of roads.

How to Get Around in Limavady

The best way to get around Limavady is by car or bike. There are several car rental companies that offer their services at reasonable prices to tourists. Alternatively, visitors can hire a bike and explore the town on two wheels.
