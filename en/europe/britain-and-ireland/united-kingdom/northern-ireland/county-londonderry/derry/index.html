Introduction to Derry

Derry, also known as Londonderry, is a city in County Londonderry, in Northern Ireland, a part of the United Kingdom. It is the second-largest city in Northern Ireland and is situated on the River Foyle. Known for its vibrant cultural scene, rich history, scenic beauty, and friendly people, Derry is a must-visit destination.

List of Places to Visit in Derry

There are so many things to do and see in Derry, from exploring historical sites to experiencing outdoor adventures. Here are some of the top places to visit:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Free Derry Corner: This iconic landmark is a must-visit for history buffs. It commemorates the events of the civil rights movement of the late 1960s, which led to the conflict known as “The Troubles”.
  • Museum of Free Derry: Located in the Bogside area of Derry, this museum tells the story of the civil rights era through exhibits and multimedia displays.
  • Tower Museum: This award-winning museum explores the history of Derry, from its early settlements to present times.
  • Guildhall: This stunning 19th-century building houses a museum and art gallery showcasing Derry’s history and culture.
  • St. Columb’s Cathedral: One of the most important historical sites in Derry, this Cathedral was built in 1633 and is the first post-Reformation Cathedral in the British Isles.

Natural Attractions of Derry: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • The Peace Bridge: This 345-meter foot and cycle bridge spans the River Foyle and connects Ebrington Square with the rest of the city’s downtown area.
  • Foyle Valley Railway Museum: The museum is a perfect destination for train enthusiasts and transport lovers.
  • Bishop’s Gate: It is a beautifully restored gate that once marked the entrance to the walled city of Derry.
  • Prehen Wood: A beautiful woodland that offers amazing views of the River Foyle.
  • Grianan Of Aileach: An impressive Iron Age structure situated on a hill overlooking the city and county.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Derry

Derry offers some of the best routes for hiking and biking enthusiasts. Some popular routes include:

  • The Foyle Greenway Trail: A 26-mile trail that runs along the banks of the River Foyle, offering stunning views of the city and surrounding countryside.
  • Oak Grove Forest Park: A beautiful park with walking trails, picnic areas, and stunning views of the River Foyle.
  • Binevenagh Mountain: This mountain is a great spot for hiking and offers stunning views of the city and the surrounding countryside.

Best Things to Do in Derry: Activities and Tours

  • Walking Tours: Take a guided walking tour to explore the city’s historic sites and learn more about Derry’s rich culture and history.
  • Derry City Walls: Take a stroll along Derry’s historic city walls, which date back to the 17th century.
  • Craft Beer Tours: Derry has a thriving craft beer scene, and a tour of the city’s breweries and pubs is a great way to sample some of the best local beers.
  • Traditional Music Sessions: Derry is famous for its traditional music, and you can find live music in many pubs and bars throughout the city.
  • Murder Mystery Tour: Join a mystery tour to solve a crime in Derry’s historic landmarks, all while learning about the city’s history and culture.

Week-Long Itinerary for Derry

For a complete Derry experience, follow this itinerary:

Day Activity

Food and Drink Scene of Derry

Derry has a thriving food and drink scene, with many local specialties and traditional dishes. Here are some must-try dishes and beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Champ: A traditional dish made of mashed potatoes, butter, and scallions.
  • Irish Stew: A hearty, filling stew made with lamb or beef, potatoes, carrots, and other vegetables.
  • Boxty: A type of potato pancake that is popular throughout Ireland.
  • Seafood: Derry is situated on the coast and is known for its fresh seafood, including oysters, mussels, and salmon.

Local Beverages

  • Guinness: A classic Irish beer that is popular throughout the world.
  • Irish Whiskey: A smooth, flavorful whiskey that is a must-try while in Derry.
  • Craft Beer: Derry has a thriving craft beer scene, with many local breweries and pubs to explore.

Where to Stay in Derry: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Derry has a wide range of accommodation options to suit any budget, from luxurious hotels to budget-friendly hostels. Some of the best options are:

  • Shipquay Boutique Hotel: This stylish hotel is located in the heart of the city and offers luxurious accommodation and amenities.
  • City Hotel Derry: Located on the banks of the River Foyle, this hotel offers stunning views and comfortable accommodation.
  • Snooze Hostel: A budget-friendly hostel that is perfect for backpackers and solo travelers.
  • Groarty House & Manor Bed and Breakfast: A beautiful B&B located just outside the city, offering stunning views and comfortable accommodation.

What to Buy in Derry: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Derry offers many unique souvenirs and local products that are perfect for bringing home as a reminder of your visit. Some of the best options are:

  • Celtic Jewelry: Derry is known for its beautiful Celtic jewelry, which makes for a perfect souvenir.
  • Irish Linen: Ireland is famous for its high-quality linen, and there are many boutiques in Derry selling beautiful linen products.
  • Craft Beers: Derry has a thriving craft beer scene, and you can find many local breweries and pubs selling their unique beers.
  • Traditional Music CDs: Take home a CD of traditional Irish music to remember your trip.

Local Culture and Customs of Derry

Derry has a rich cultural heritage, and the city is known for its music, literature, and art. The people of Derry are friendly and welcoming, and visitors are encouraged to embrace the local culture and customs. Some things to keep in mind:

  • Greeting: The people of Derry are friendly and welcoming, and it is customary to greet people with a smile or a nod.
  • Language: English is the primary language spoken in Derry, although Irish Gaelic is also spoken by some locals.
  • Music and Dance: Derry is known for its traditional music and dance, and visitors are encouraged to experience this aspect of local culture.

Best Time to Visit Derry

The best time to visit Derry is between late spring and early autumn, when the weather is mild and pleasant. The peak tourist season is during the summer months, so expect higher prices and larger crowds at this time.

Do’s and Don’ts of Derry

Here are some things to keep in mind during your visit to Derry:

Do:

  • Explore the historic sites: Derry has a rich history, and exploring the city’s historic sites is a must-do.
  • Embrace the local culture: Derry is known for its music, literature, and art, and visitors are encouraged to participate in local customs and traditions.
  • Try the local cuisine: Derry has a thriving food and drink scene, and there are many local specialties that are worth trying.

Don’t:

  • Be disrespectful: Derry has a rich cultural heritage, and visitors are encouraged to be respectful of local customs and traditions.
  • Overlook the natural beauty: Derry boasts stunning natural landscapes, and visitors should take the time to explore the outdoors.

Safety and Health

Safety in Derry

Derry is a safe city, but visitors should take normal precautions to avoid any potential risks. It is always advisable to keep an eye on your belongings and be aware of your surroundings, especially when walking alone at night.

Health in Derry

Derry has excellent healthcare facilities, and visitors can access medical services easily if needed. It is advisable to take out travel insurance before visiting to cover any unexpected health costs.

Getting to Derry: Transportation Options and Logistics

Derry is easily accessible by air, road, or rail. Here are some of the best transportation options:

Airports Near Derry

  • City of Derry Airport: The airport is located just outside the city and offers flights to various destinations throughout the UK and Europe.
  • Belfast International Airport: Approximately 73 miles from Derry, the airport is the largest airport in Northern Ireland.

Bus Stations Near Derry

  • Foyle Street Bus Station: This is the main bus station in Derry, located in the heart of the city.

Other Options

  • Car Rental: Visitors can rent a car to explore the city and the surrounding countryside.
  • Rail: Derry’s rail station provides links to other cities throughout Northern Ireland, and the Republic of Ireland.

How to Get Around in Derry

Derry is a compact city and is easy to navigate on foot or by bicycle. There are also local bus services available that connect to various parts of the city.

Where to Go Next from Derry: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Derry is ideally located for exploring other destinations in Northern Ireland and beyond. Here are some of the best nearby destinations:

  • Giant’s Causeway: A UNESCO World Heritage Site and a must-visit natural wonder, located approximately 60 miles from Derry.
  • Donegal Castle: This impressive castle is located in the town of Donegal, just a short drive from Derry.
  • Belfast: The capital city of Northern Ireland is approximately two hours from Derry, and is easily accessible by car or train.

Conclusion

Derry is a vibrant, friendly, and culturally rich destination that offers something for everyone. Whether you’re interested in history, outdoor adventures, or local cuisine, Derry has it all. Explore the city’s historic sites, take in the stunning natural scenery, and immerse yourself in the local culture and customs. With excellent transportation links and a wide range of accommodation options, Derry is the perfect destination for your next adventure.
