Introduction to Magherafelt

Nestled within the breathtaking scenery of County Londonderry, Northern Ireland, Magherafelt is a small, charming town that is rich in history and culture. Located in the United Kingdom, Magherafelt is a gateway to the beauty of Ireland, nestled in the midst of the rugged landscape of the Sperrin Mountains.

Whether you-are-a-history-buff, an outdoor enthusiast, or you seek a taste of Northern Irish culture and cuisine, Magherafelt is sure to enchant you. Read on to discover all of the exciting things to do and places to see in this delightful destination.

List of Places to Visit in Magherafelt

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Seamus Heaney HomePlace

One of Magherafelt’s main attractions is the Seamus Heaney HomePlace museum. This museum pays homage to one of Ireland’s most beloved poets and scholars, Seamus Heaney. Heaney was from Mossbawn, a neighboring town to Magherafelt, and was recognized in 1995 by the Nobel Prize for Literature. The museum features exhibits on Heaney’s life and work, as well as interactive displays that engage visitors of all ages.

The Jungle NI

For those who are seeking adventure, The Jungle NI is the perfect choice. This adventure park is a testament to the natural beauty of Northern Ireland and the adventurous spirit of its people. This is a must-visit location for outdoor enthusiasts looking for an adrenaline rush through activities such as tree-top adventures, paintballing, and archery.

Natural Attractions of Magherafelt: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Sperrin Mountains

At Magherafelt’s doorstep lies the striking and rugged terrain of the Sperrin Mountains. This mountain range is home to a variety of wildlife, walking trails, and stunning views. The most notable trails here are the Ulster Way and the Sperrins Way, which offer walkers, hikers, and bikers alike a chance to explore the stunning and secluded corners of Northern Ireland.

Lough Neagh

Another natural attraction that attracts people to Magherafelt is Lough Neagh. The lake is the largest in the United Kingdom, spanning an astonishing 151 square miles. It is also home to a variety of aquatic creatures, flora, and fauna.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Magherafelt

Exploring Magherafelt’s beautiful countryside can be done by foot, bike or horseback. Here are some of the trails that you need to check out:-

The Sperrins Way

This hiking trail is a 40-kilometer track that takes you through the stunning Sperrin Mountains. It starts in Downhill Forest, just a five-minute walk from the Hillcrest House.

Davagh Forest Trails

Another hiking trail worth exploring is Davagh Forest located near Cookstown. The forest covers 1,000 hectares, with 16 km of mountain biking trails, cycling trails and walking routes.

Best Things to Do in Magherafelt: Activities and Tours

Tours

Causeway Coastal Route

Visitors to Magherafelt wanting to see more of Northern Ireland’s beautiful scenery should take a trip along the Causeway Coastal Route. It is one of the most scenic drives in the world, taking visitors along the coast from Belfast to Derry~Londonderry, passing through picturesque towns such as Bushmills, Portrush, and Portstewart.

Tours to Game of Thrones Filming Locations

Northern Ireland has become a hotspot for fans of the global sensation, Game of Thrones. Many of the show’s iconic scenes were filmed in Northern Ireland, and for those who want to experience the magic for themselves, there are several tours available that visit some of the show’s famous locations, such as The Dark Hedges and Ballintoy Harbour.

Activities

Magherafelt Water Sports

Magherafelt Water Sports provides a thrilling experience for adventure-seekers who love water-based activities such as kayaking, paddleboarding, and sailing. Located on the shores of Lough Neagh, the company offers visitors a unique perspective of the lake’s stunning scenery.

Food and Drink Scene of Magherafelt

Must-Try Dishes

Sunday Roast

Sunday roast is a local staple in Northern Ireland and is served in nearly every eatery on this side of the UK. This dish typically includes roast meat, potatoes, vegetables, and gravy.

Irish Stew

This is another local favorite that you shouldn’t miss. Irish stew is typically made with lamb or mutton, potatoes, onions, and carrots. This hearty stew is perfect for warming you up on a chilly Northern Irish day.

Local Beverages

Bushmills Whiskey

Northern Ireland is known for its whiskey, and Bushmills Whiskey is one of the country’s most popular brands. Bushmills has been producing whiskey in Northern Ireland since 1608, making it the oldest licensed whiskey distillery in the world.

Guinness

While Guinness is often associated with Dublin, this beloved Irish beer is enjoyed all over Northern Ireland, and Magherafelt is no exception. You can enjoy this classic beer at nearly any pub in town.

## Where to Stay in Magherafelt: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Hillcrest House B&B

Hillcrest House is a cozy B&B that features a breakfast room, private bedrooms, and en suite bathrooms. Located just a five-minute walk from the Seamus Heaney HomePlace museum, it is the perfect place to wake up after a restful night’s sleep.

The Terrace Hotel

Another great option is The Terrace Hotel, located right in the center of Magherafelt. This boutique hotel offers guests a comfortable stay in a characterful setting. The Terrace offers a fine dining restaurant, a pub, and a coffee shop on-site.

What to Buy in Magherafelt: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

One of the most famous local products you should bring home is the Northern Irish whiskey. Stop at your nearest liquor store to browse their impressive selection of this delightful drink. You can also bring home a souvenir from the Seamus Heaney HomePlace museum, such as a copy of one of his books or a set of postcards with some of his most famous quotes.

Local Culture and Customs of Magherafelt

The people of Magherafelt are friendly, welcoming, and proud of their local heritage. They have a rich cultural history and are proud of their celtic roots, reflected in their art, music, and literature. Don’t be surprised if you’re welcomed with open arms, as hospitality is a core Northern Irish value.

Best Time to Visit Magherafelt

The best time to visit Magherafelt is during the spring months, from March to May, and the fall, from September to November. During these months the temperature is mild, the countryside is green, and there are fewer tourists than during the summer months.

Do’s and Don’ts of Magherafelt

Do:

  • Speak to locals and learn about their culture.
  • Take advantage of the great outdoor activities in and around Magherafelt.
  • Take a Game of Thrones filming location tour.

Don’t:

  • Leave a mess in any of the hiking trails or other outdoor natural attractions in the area.
  • Be disrespectful towards local customs or cultural practices.

Safety and Health

Safety in Magherafelt

Magherafelt is a safe and friendly town, but like any other location, visitors should take reasonable precautions to keep themselves safe. Avoid walking alone late at night, especially in areas that are poorly lit or isolated. Ensure your belongings are safe and secure when out and about.

Health in Magherafelt

No major health risks are associated with Magherafelt. It is, however, a good idea to get all necessary vaccinations before travel if you are not up-to-date.

Getting to Magherafelt: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Magherafelt

The nearest international airport is in Belfast at Belfast International Airport. From there, visitors can take a bus or hire a car to get to Magherafelt.

Bus Stations Near Magherafelt

Magherafelt bus station is located in the town center and offers bus services to neighboring towns and cities.

Other Options

Northern Ireland’s rail network is efficient, one recommendation to travel to Magherafelt is by train.

How to Get Around in Magherafelt

Magherafelt’s small size makes the town easy to explore on foot. Visitors who want to venture further afield can rent bicycles or hire private taxis.

Where to Go Next from Magherafelt: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Magherafelt is located in Northern Ireland, providing easy access to other exciting destinations such as Belfast (a 40-minute drive), Portrush (an hour’s drive), and Derry~Londonderry (a 40-minute drive).

Conclusion

Magherafelt is a hidden gem, a destination that is not as well known as some of the other hotspots in Northern Ireland. But this town has so much to offer to visitors, from its natural beauty to its cultural heritage and stirring history. Whether you’re visiting for the hiking trails, adventure sports, rich heritage, or simply want to relax in stunning surroundings, Magherafelt is the perfect choice for you. With this guide, you are now well-equipped to plan an unforgettable visit to this charming town.
