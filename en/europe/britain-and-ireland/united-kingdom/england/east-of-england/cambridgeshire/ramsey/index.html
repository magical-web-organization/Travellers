Ramsey, a quaint market town situated in the heart of Cambridgeshire, is one of the hidden gems of the East of England. Known for its rich cultural history, friendly locals, and stunning natural landscapes, Ramsey is an ideal destination for travelers who seek a break from the hustle and bustle of the city.

In this travel guide, we will take you on a tour of the best places to visit, things to do, where to stay, what to eat, and more.

Introduction to Ramsey

Ramsey is a charming town located in the heart of Cambridgeshire, England. It is situated on the eastern edge of the Fenland, a flat and fertile plain that covers much of eastern England. Ramsey is known for its historic buildings, beautiful parks, and scenic countryside. The town has a population of approximately 8,000 people and is a hub of local activity.

List of Places to Visit in Ramsey

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Ramsey has a rich cultural history that is reflected in its many museums and historical places. Here are some must-visit attractions:

  • Ramsey Rural Museum: This museum showcases the history of agriculture and rural life in the Fenland.

  • Ramsey Abbey Gatehouse: This gatehouse was once part of Ramsey Abbey, a Benedictine abbey that was founded in the 10th century.

  • St. Thomas a Becket Church: This church is known for its beautiful stained glass windows, wooden carvings, and historic organ.

Natural Attractions of Ramsey: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Ramsey is surrounded by stunning natural landscapes that are perfect for outdoor adventures and scenic views. Here are some of the top natural attractions:

  • Ramsey Forty Foot: This is a perfect location for swimming, fishing, and picnicking.

  • Ramsey Heights: This is a nature reserve that offers stunning views of the Fenland.

  • Manor Farm: This is a working farm that offers tours and allows visitors to interact with the animals.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Ramsey

Ramsey and its surrounding areas are great for hiking and biking enthusiasts. Here are some of the top trails:

  • The Ramsey to Upwood Cycle Path: A scenic route that is perfect for bicycling.

  • The Ramsey to Benwick Trail: A five-mile trail that passes through the scenic countryside and is great for hiking.

Best Things to Do in Ramsey: Activities and Tours

Ramsey offers a host of activities and tours that cater to all ages and interests. Here are some of the top things to do:

  • Ramsey Beer Festival: This annual event is held in July and showcases a variety of local brews.

  • Ramsey Farmers Market: Held monthly, this market offers visitors a chance to purchase fresh, locally grown produce.

  • Ramsey Rural Museum Events: The museum hosts regular events, including craft fairs, which are perfect for families.

Week-Long Itinerary for Ramsey

Day 1: Arrival and sightseeing in Ramsey. Day 2: Hiking or cycling in the Fenland. Day 3: Exploring the museums and historical sites in Ramsey. Day 4: Shopping and taking in the sights and sounds of the local markets. Day 5: Taking a farm tour and visiting the animals. Day 6: Relaxing on the shores of Ramsey’s Forty Foot. Day 7: Departure and connecting to your next destination.

Food and Drink Scene of Ramsey

The food and drink scene in Ramsey is a reflection of the town’s rich agricultural heritage. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Fenland Lamb: This dish is made with locally sourced lamb and is seasoned with herbs and spices.

  • Toad in the Hole: A traditional English dish made with sausages and Yorkshire pudding.

Local Beverages

  • Local Ales: Ramsey’s Brewery offers a variety of locally brewed ales.

  • Cider: Fenland Cider offers some of the best cider in the region.

Where to Stay in Ramsey: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Ramsey has a variety of accommodation options, including hotels, hostels, and guesthouses. Here are some of the best places to stay:

  • The George Hotel: A historic hotel that has been in operation since the 17th century.

  • The Duke of Bedford Inn: A charming bed and breakfast that offers cozy accommodations and friendly service.

  • The Ramsey Park Hotel: A modern hotel that offers high-end amenities and stunning views of the Fenland.

What to Buy in Ramsey: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Ramsey is known for its local products, including handmade crafts, locally grown produce, and artisanal foods. Here are some of the best souvenirs to take home:

  • Fenland Honey: This honey is made by local beekeepers and is a perfect gift for any food lover.

  • Handmade Crafts: Ramsey has a variety of craft shops that sell handmade pottery, textiles, and woodwork.

  • Locally Grown Produce: Ramsey’s farmers market offers a variety of fresh, locally grown produce that is perfect for souvenirs.

Local Culture and Customs of Ramsey

Ramsey is a friendly and welcoming town that is known for its local festivals and traditions. Here are some of the local culture and customs of Ramsey:

  • The Ramsey Abbey Festival: A festival that celebrates the town’s rich religious history.

  • May Day Celebration: A festival that features traditional dancing and music.

Best Time to Visit Ramsey

The best time to visit Ramsey is during the summer months, from July to September, when the weather is warm and sunny. However, the town is beautiful year-round and offers a variety of activities and events throughout the year.

Safety and Health

Safety in Ramsey

Ramsey is a safe town that has low crime rates. However, visitors should be aware of their surroundings and take common-sense precautions to prevent theft and other crimes.

Health in Ramsey

Ramsey has a variety of health care facilities, including a hospital and medical clinics. Visitors should ensure that they have adequate health insurance coverage before traveling to the town.

Getting to Ramsey: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Ramsey

The nearest airport to Ramsey is London Stansted Airport, which is approximately 50 miles away.

Bus Stations Near Ramsey

Ramsey is serviced by the First Eastern Counties Bus company, which provides regular bus service to and from the town.

Other Options

Visitors can also reach Ramsey by car or train. Ramsey is located on the A141 highway, which connects to other major highways in the region. The nearest train station is in Huntingdon, which is approximately 10 miles away from Ramsey.

How to Get Around in Ramsey

Ramsey is a small town that can be easily explored on foot or by bicycle. Visitors can also take advantage of the local bus service.
