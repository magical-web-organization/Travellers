A Comprehensive Travel Guide to Fowlmere, Cambridgeshire

Located in Cambridgeshire, in the East of England, Fowlmere is a small village that is known for its picturesque landscapes, enchanting history, and warm hospitality. From historical landmarks to natural attractions, Fowlmere has a lot to offer to its visitors. In this comprehensive travel guide, we will explore the various places to visit, hiking/biking routes, food and drink scene, local culture and customs, safety, transportation options, nearby destinations, and more in Fowlmere.

Introduction to Fowlmere

Fowlmere is a small village that is located 10 miles southwest of the city of Cambridge. With a population of just over 1,000, Fowlmere has a peaceful and calm atmosphere, making it the perfect destination for those who want to escape the hustle and bustle of city life.

List of Places to Visit in Fowlmere

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Fowlmere Village museum – an impressive collection of artifacts that showcase the village’s history.
  • Manor Farm – a 16th-century farmhouse that provides a glimpse into Fowlmere’s agricultural past.
  • St Mary’s Church – a beautiful church that features stunning stained glass windows and intricate wooden carvings.

Natural Attractions of Fowlmere: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Fowlmere Local Nature Reserve – a peaceful nature reserve that provides opportunities for bird watching, fishing, and hiking.
  • The Shepway Meadow Nature Reserve – a lush meadow that features a wide variety of plant and animal species.
  • Fowlmere RSPB Reserve – a haven for bird watchers where you can spot different bird species like kingfishers, reed warblers, and water rails.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Fowlmere

Fowlmere provides excellent opportunities for hiking or biking. Here are some of the best routes to explore:

  • Fowlmere Circular Walk – a 5.5-mile loop around the village that provides stunning views of the surrounding countryside.
  • The Coton Footpath – a 4-mile route that takes you through the beautiful nature reserves of Cambridge.
  • The Eversden Way – a 6-mile path that connects three villages and features some of the best spots for bird watching in the area.

Best Things to Do in Fowlmere: Activities and Tours

  • Take a guided tour of Fowlmere Village Museum and learn about the village’s rich history.
  • Rent a bike and explore the beautiful countryside of Fowlmere.
  • Attend the Fowlmere Country Show, an annual event that showcases the village’s rural traditions and crafts.

Week-Long Itinerary for Fowlmere

Day 1: Visit Fowlmere Village Museum and St Mary’s Church Day 2: Go bird watching at Fowlmere RSPB Reserve and enjoy a picnic at The Shepway Meadow Nature Reserve Day 3: Rent a bike and ride the Coton Footpath Day 4: Explore the beautiful nature reserve of Fowlmere Local Nature Reserve Day 5: Take a day trip to the historic city of Cambridge Day 6: Attend the Fowlmere Country Show (if it falls on your date) Day 7: Relax and unwind in the beautiful surroundings of Fowlmere

Food and Drink Scene of Fowlmere

Fowlmere offers a variety of dining options, including local cafes, pubs, and restaurants. Here are some of the must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Cambridge sausage – a type of sausage made from minced pork and herbs.
  • Ely pork pie – a savory pie made with seasoned pork, jelly, and hot-water pastry.
  • Appleby’s Cheshire cheese – a tangy, crumbly cheese made from cow’s milk.

Local Beverages

  • Cambridge CAMRA beer – a locally brewed beer that comes in a variety of flavors.
  • Fenland cider – a sweet and tangy cider made from local apples.
  • Milton Brewery ale – a popular ale made from local hops.

Where to Stay in Fowlmere: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Fowlmere has a limited number of accommodations, but they are all cozy and welcoming. Here are some of the best options:

  • The King William IV Country Inn and Restaurant – a charming inn that features comfortable rooms, delicious food, and a friendly staff.
  • The Chequers Guest House – a Victorian-style guesthouse that offers home-cooked breakfast and comfortable rooms.
  • Sheene Mill – a stunning boutique hotel that features luxurious accommodations and a beautiful restaurant.

What to Buy in Fowlmere: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Fowlmere honey – a delicious and natural honey made by local beekeepers.
  • Cambridge Cream cheese – a soft and creamy cheese made in Cambridgeshire.
  • Fowlmere pottery – unique and handcrafted pottery made by local artisans.

Local Culture and Customs of Fowlmere

Fowlmere residents are proud of their heritage and love to share their traditions with visitors. Here are some local customs and traditions to keep in mind:

  • Always greet people with a smile and say “hello.”
  • Respect the countryside and follow the “leave no trace” policy.
  • Support local businesses and farmers by buying locally grown produce.

Best Time to Visit Fowlmere

The best time to visit Fowlmere is during the summer months of June to August. The weather is warm, and the countryside is lush, making it perfect for outdoor activities and adventures. However, visiting during the spring months of March to May offers the opportunity to see wildflowers in bloom and migratory birds in their annual fly-pasts.

Do’s and Don’ts of Fowlmere

Do:

  • Explore the beautiful countryside of Fowlmere.
  • Take a guided tour of the Fowlmere Village Museum and learn about the village’s history.
  • Support local businesses and farmers by buying locally grown produce.

Don’t:

  • Litter or damage the countryside.
  • Ignore the local customs and traditions.
  • Be disrespectful to the wildlife and birds in the nature reserves.

Safety and Health

Safety in Fowlmere

Fowlmere is a safe and friendly village, but visitors should take precautions to ensure their safety. Remember to lock your car and bicycle when leaving them unattended. Also, when walking on the countryside, wear appropriate clothing and footwear and follow the signposts to stay on the designated paths.

Health in Fowlmere

Visitors to Fowlmere do not require any special vaccinations or medications. However, it is always a good idea to bring along any prescription medications and ensure you have medical coverage.

Getting to Fowlmere: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Fowlmere

  • Stansted Airport – 25 miles away
  • London Luton Airport – 36 miles away
  • London Heathrow Airport – 68 miles away

Bus Stations Near Fowlmere

  • Royston Bus Station – 6 miles away
  • Cambridge Bus Station – 9 miles away

Other Options

  • Drive – Fowlmere is easily accessible by car, with the M11 motorway just 10 miles away.
  • Train – The nearest train station is Royston, which is a 10-minute drive from Fowlmere.

How to Get Around in Fowlmere

Fowlmere is a compact village that is easy to get around on foot or by bicycle. Taxis and car rentals are also available.

Where to Go Next from Fowlmere: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Duxford – a picturesque village famous for the Imperial War Museum.
  • Royston – a charming market town that features a variety of shops, restaurants, and cafes.

Conclusion

Fowlmere is a hidden gem in Cambridgeshire, England, that offers a unique blend of history, natural beauty, and hospitality. Whether you are interested in outdoor adventures, cultural attractions, or local cuisine, Fowlmere has something for everyone. Plan your visit today and discover the charm of this enchanting village.
