Introduction to Peterborough

Peterborough is a historic city located in the county of Cambridgeshire, East of England. This city has a rich history, dating back to the Bronze Age and has been a center of commerce and trade since Roman times. Known as the “Gateway to the Fens”, Peterborough is surrounded by beautiful countryside and waterways, making it a popular destination for outdoor enthusiasts.

List of Places to Visit in Peterborough

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Peterborough has a plethora of culturally important attractions, including museums and historical places.

Peterborough Cathedral

Peterborough Cathedral is a stunning architectural wonder that has been a symbol of the city for centuries. The cathedral boasts beautifully intricate woodwork and gorgeous stained glass windows. Visitors can join guided tours to learn about its history and take in breathtaking views from the tower.

Peterborough Museum

Peterborough Museum is home to some of the most remarkable exhibits in the region. The museum features interactive displays of the city’s history, and you can learn about the famous abbey, the civil war, and the Victorian era.

Natural Attractions of Peterborough: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Peterborough is blessed with an abundance of natural landscapes, perfect for outdoor enthusiasts and nature lovers.

Ferry Meadows Country Park

Ferry Meadows Country Park is the most popular outdoor destination in Peterborough. This park offers a variety of activities, such as cycling, walking, and fishing. Its magnificent lakes, woodlands, and meadows make it an ideal place for a family picnic.

Nene Park

Nene Park is another must-visit natural attraction in Peterborough. It spans over 1,700-acres and is filled with a wide range of habitats, including lakes, meadows, and woodlands. Visitors can explore the park on their own or join guided tours to learn about its ecosystems.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Peterborough

Whether you prefer hiking or biking, Peterborough has a variety of trails to suit all levels of experience.

The Nene Way

The Nene Way is a 177km trail that follows the river Nene from Northamptonshire to the North Sea. The route is well-marked, and visitors can break it up into stages to suit their level of fitness.

The Green Wheel

The Green Wheel is a 45km cycle and walking route that circles the city. It passes all the major parks in Peterborough and offers stunning views of the countryside.

Best Things to Do in Peterborough: Activities and Tours

There are plenty of things to do and tours to take in Peterborough to make your trip even more special.

Punting on the River Nene

One of the best ways to explore Peterborough is by punting on the River Nene. Visitors can relax and take in the beautiful views of the city while listening to the soothing sounds of the water.

Ghost Walks of Peterborough

Located in the city center, ghost walks of Peterborough are an entertaining and educational way to explore the history of this fantastic city. Tourists can join a guided tour to hear tales of the city’s most haunted spots and learn about the life and times of the prominent figures from Peterborough’s past.

Week-Long Itinerary for Peterborough

Peterborough has plenty of activities to keep you occupied for a week. Below is a suggested itinerary to help you plan your trip to Peterborough.

Food and Drink Scene of Peterborough

When it comes to food and drink, Peterborough has something for everyone. Try the local dishes and beverages to make your trip more memorable.

Must-Try Dishes

Peterborough Pudding

The Peterborough Pudding is one of the city’s most famous dishes. It is made with grated carrot, suet, sugar, and flour, which gives it a sweet and savory taste. This dessert is widely available in most local restaurants.

Raspberry Piglet

The Raspberry Piglet is a famous dessert for those with a sweet tooth. It is a delicious combination of vanilla sponge, raspberry sauce, and fresh cream, making it irresistible to taste.

Local Beverages

Peterborough Bitter

Peterborough bitter is a locally brewed ale, popular for its full-bodied taste. The blend is composed of East Kent Goldings hops, barley malt, and local spring water.

Elgood’s Brewery

Situated in the nearby village of Wisbech, Elgood’s Brewery is a must-visit for those who enjoy a good pint. It has been in the family’s ownership for over 250 years and offers a range of ales, including the famous India Pale Ale.

Where to Stay in Peterborough: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Peterborough has a wide range of accommodations to suit all budgets, from luxury hotels to guesthouses and hostels.

Best Luxury Hotels

Haycock Manor Hotel

This luxurious 16th-century manor hotel is located in Wansford and is one of the city’s most renowned hotels. It boasts 3 restaurants, an indoor pool, and a spa.

Bull Hotel

This elegant four-star hotel in the city center has been operating since the seventeenth century and is known for its sophisticated style and exceptional service.

What to Buy in Peterborough: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

If you’re looking for something to take home to remember your visit to Peterborough, there are many local products and souvenirs to choose from.

Fudge Kitchen

Fudge Kitchen is one of Peterborough’s most famous local stores. Here, visitors can discover over fifty flavors of fudge, made the old-fashioned way in copper kettles.

Hambleton Bakery

Hambleton Bakery is a local artisan bakery, famous for its bread and cakes. Visitors can take home some of their delicious baked goods, which are made from locally sourced ingredients.

Local Culture and Customs of Peterborough

Peterborough is known for its welcoming locals, and it’s vibrant and diverse community. Visitors can experience this firsthand by attending one of the many local cultural events and festivals that take place throughout the year.

Best Time to Visit Peterborough

The best time to visit Peterborough is during the spring and summer months (April to September). The weather is mild, and the parks and outdoor attractions are in full bloom.

Do’s and Don’ts of Peterborough

Do:

  • Enjoy a relaxed pace of life, take in the sights and sounds of Peterborough, and explore the countryside.
  • Take advantage of the outdoor attractions, such as the rivers, parks, and walking trails.
  • Experience the local food and drinks scene, particularly the famous Peterborough Pudding, Raspberry Piglet, and Peterborough Bitter.
  • Immerse yourself in the local culture, attend events and festivals, and take in the cultural attractions.

Don’t:

  • Forget to bring appropriate clothing for the weather. It can be chilly in the evening, even in the summer months.
  • Plan your trip during the winter months (December to February) when the weather is colder, and outdoor activities are limited.
  • Leave litter in the parks and nature reserves.

Safety and Health

Safety in Peterborough

Peterborough is generally a safe city for tourists. However, it is always necessary to be aware of your surroundings and take basic precautions, such as securing valuables.

Health in Peterborough

Peterborough has excellent medical facilities, including hospitals, clinics, and pharmacies. UK residents are eligible for free healthcare under the National Health Service (NHS). Visitors from abroad will need comprehensive travel insurance to cover any medical expenses.

Getting to Peterborough: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Peterborough

Peterborough is close to several international airports, including London Luton Airport, Birmingham Airport, East Midlands Airport, and London Stansted Airport.

Bus Stations Near Peterborough

Peterborough Bus Station is located in the city center, and visitors can catch buses to many destinations in Cambridgeshire and the neighboring counties.

Other Options

Peterborough is easily accessible by train, with direct connections from London Kings Cross Station in under an hour.

How to Get Around in Peterborough

Peterborough has an excellent public transportation system, with buses and trains running regularly throughout the city and the surrounding towns and villages. Alternatively, hiring a bike is an excellent way to explore the city and countryside at your leisure.

Where to Go Next from Peterborough: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

There are many local destinations and excursions near Peterborough that are worth a visit, including:

  • Stamford: the charming town of Stamford is a short drive from Peterborough and is renowned for its historic architecture, quaint streets, and fabulous restaurants.
  • Cambridge: the world-famous city of Cambridge is a one-hour train ride from Peterborough. Visitors can explore its stunning ancient architecture and soak up its intellectual atmosphere.

Conclusion

Peterborough is an enchanting city, well worth a visit. This travel guide offers plenty of ideas for exploring the city’s culture, food, and natural landscapes, and visitors are sure to leave with unforgettable memories.
