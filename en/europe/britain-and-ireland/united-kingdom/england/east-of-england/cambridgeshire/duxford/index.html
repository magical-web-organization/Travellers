Introduction to Duxford

Located in Cambridgeshire, in the East of England, Duxford is a picturesque village that boasts a rich history and charming architecture. It is home to the Imperial War Museum Duxford, one of the most impressive aviation museums in the world. Duxford is a popular destination for travelers interested in aviation history and outdoor activities.

List of Places to Visit in Duxford

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Imperial War Museum Duxford: This museum is dedicated to the history of aviation and military events that happened in the UK and worldwide. Visitors can explore the different exhibitions and go on a guided tour.
  • St John’s Church: The church’s architecture dates back to the 14th century, and it has been refurbished many times since then. It features beautiful stained glass windows and a stunning interior.
  • Whittlesford Bridge: This historic bridge was once a vital transport link between Cambridge and London. It played a crucial role in the transportation of goods and people during the 18th and 19th centuries.

Natural Attractions of Duxford: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Thriplow Meadows: This nature reserve covers 50 acres and features an abundance of wildflowers, butterflies, and birds. Visitors can explore the trails and enjoy the peaceful surroundings.
  • Audley End House and Gardens: This is a historic mansion and estate that has been restored and opened to the public. The lush gardens are perfect for a picnic or a relaxing walk.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Duxford

  • Duxford Circular Walk: This 6-mile trail takes visitors through the best scenery in Duxford, including the village center, countryside, and farmland.
  • Granta and Icknield Way Walk: This longer route takes hikers through the picturesque villages of Bartlow and Linton and follows part of the ancient Icknield Way.

Best Things to Do in Duxford: Activities and Tours

  • Vintage Aircraft Tours: Discover the fascinating world of vintage aircraft with an expert guide. Visit hangars that house rare and unique planes and learn about their history.
  • Flying Experiences: For those who want to experience the thrill of flying, Duxford offers flight simulators and the chance to take a real flight in a vintage plane.

Food and Drink Scene of Duxford

Must-Try Dishes

  • Cambridgeshire Cream Tea: This is a quintessential English tea experience. Scones with clotted cream and jam are served with a pot of tea.
  • Celebrate Beer: Duxford has a well-established brewing scene. Try one of the local ales, wines, or spirits.

Local Beverages

  • Cider: Cambridgeshire is known for its delicious cider. Try Old Luxters or Orchard Pig.
  • Gin: The county is home to several award-winning gin distilleries. Try Cambridge Dry Gin, Arctic Sisu, or Pinkster.

Where to Stay in Duxford: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • The John Barleycorn Inn: This inn has been serving guests since the 17th century and boasts traditional decor and comfortable rooms.
  • Holiday Inn Express Cambridge Duxford: This modern hotel is just a few minutes’ walk from the Imperial War Museum Duxford and offers comfortable rooms, free Wi-Fi, and breakfast included.
  • Duxford Lodge: This cozy guesthouse is located in a historic building and offers comfortable rooms and a friendly atmosphere.

What to Buy in Duxford: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Imperial War Museum Duxford Merchandise: Bring home a piece of aviation history with aircraft models, books, and other souvenirs available for purchase at the museum.
  • Cambridgeshire Cider: Stock up on local cider, which is made from rare apple varieties.
  • Cambridge Gin: Cambridge Distillery produces several unique gins, each with a different flavor profile.

Local Culture and Customs of Duxford

  • Dress Code: Duxford has a laid-back dress code. Casual and comfortable clothing will be suitable.
  • Tipping: Tipping is not mandatory but always appreciated.
  • Punctuality: Brits value punctuality, so arrive on time if you have an appointment or a tour reservation.
  • Small Talk: Small talk is an essential part of social interaction in the UK, so don’t be surprised if people ask you about your day or the weather.

Best Time to Visit Duxford

The best time to visit Duxford is from April to October, when the weather is warm and sunny and outdoor activities are in full swing. However, Duxford is a year-round destination, with plenty to see and do during the winter months.

Do’s and Don’ts of Duxford

Do:

  • Do try the local cider and gin.
  • Do explore the outdoors and take a hike or bike ride.
  • Do visit the Imperial War Museum Duxford and learn about aviation history.
  • Do indulge in a traditional cream tea.

Don’t:

  • Don’t forget to bring warm clothing, even in the summer months.
  • Don’t litter or disturb the natural environment.
  • Don’t be late for your tour or appointment.

Safety and Health

Safety in Duxford

Duxford is generally a safe place to visit, but visitors should take regular safety precautions, such as keeping an eye on personal belongings and avoiding poorly lit areas at night.

Health in Duxford

Healthcare is provided by the National Health Service (NHS). Visitors from outside the UK may be charged for medical treatment, so travel insurance is recommended.

Getting to Duxford: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Duxford

  • London Stansted Airport: 25 miles/40 km (approx. 40 minutes by car)
  • London Luton Airport: 38 miles/61 km (approx. 50 minutes by car)
  • London Heathrow Airport: 70 miles/112 km (approx. 1 hour and 40 minutes by car)

Bus Stations Near Duxford

  • Cambridge Bus Station: 8 miles/13 km (approx. 20 minutes by car)
  • Royston Bus Station: 11 miles/18 km (approx. 25 minutes by car)

Other Options

  • Train: Duxford railway station is on the Cambridge-London Liverpool Street line.
  • Car: Duxford is located just off the M11 motorway, and there is plenty of parking available.

How to Get Around in Duxford

Duxford is a small village, and most of the attractions are within walking distance. However, there is some public transportation, such as buses and trains, available for longer trips or when the weather is poor.

Where to Go Next from Duxford: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Cambridge: Just a short drive or train ride away, this historic university town is worth a visit. Explore the cobbled streets, take a punt on the River Cam, or visit the world-renowned museums and galleries.
  • Ely: This small city is known for its stunning cathedral, which boasts the largest collection of medieval stained glass in the world. Visit the riverside market and explore the city’s winding streets.
  • London: This world-famous city is just over an hour away by train. Take a day trip and explore the different landmarks, museums, and attractions.

Conclusion

Duxford is a charming village that has something to offer for every type of traveler. From aviation history to outdoor adventures, there is so much to see and do. Plan your trip with this guide, and discover the best of Duxford, Cambridgeshire, and the surrounding area.
