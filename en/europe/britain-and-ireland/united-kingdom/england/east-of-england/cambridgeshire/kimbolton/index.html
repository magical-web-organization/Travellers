Introduction to Kimbolton

Kimbolton is a small historic village located in Cambridgeshire in the East of England. Once a royal residence, it boasts a rich history and scenic landscapes that offer a perfect retreat for nature lovers. The village is a popular destination for tourists who love to explore stunning scenery, cultural attractions, and outdoor adventures.

List of Places to Visit in Kimbolton

Whether you are looking for a culturally fascinating attraction, stunning natural scenery, or an outdoor adventure, Kimbolton has something for everyone. Here are some places to visit in Kimbolton:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Kimbolton Castle: This is one of the most popular historical attractions in the village, located in the heart of Kimbolton. Once a royal palace, it’s now a popular tourist attraction, hosting various events and private functions.

  • St Andrew’s Church: This is a beautiful church that dates back to medieval times, with a rich history of architectural and artistic masterpieces.

  • Kimbolton School Chapel: This is a magnificent chapel located in the famous Kimbolton School, featuring intricate details and breathtaking architecture.

Natural Attractions of Kimbolton: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Kimbolton Castle Park: A beautiful park with scenic walks, a stunning lake, a well-maintained garden, and abundant wildlife.

  • Fitzwilliam Lodge Nature Reserve: A designated nature reserve featuring a dazzling collection of native flora and fauna, including rare species of birds, butterflies, and wildflowers.

  • Kimbolton Hill: This is a popular destination for hikers and mountain bikers, offering an amazing view of the village and surrounding countryside.

Hiking/Biking Routes in Kimbolton

Kimbolton is the perfect place for nature enthusiasts who love to wander in the great outdoors. Here are some of the best hiking and biking routes in Kimbolton:

  • Kimbolton Circular Walk: A picturesque 6-mile circular walk that passes through scenic countryside trails, historic bridges, beautiful woodlands, and stunning lakes.

  • Kimbolton to Molesworth Walk: A scenic 8-mile walk that links Kimbolton to Molesworth, offering panoramic views of the Nene Valley and surrounding landscapes.

Best Things to Do in Kimbolton: Activities and Tours

From cultural attractions to stunning scenery, Kimbolton offers endless opportunities to explore, enjoy, and relax. Here are some of the best things to do in Kimbolton:

  • Take a tour of Kimbolton Castle: Learn about the rich history of this fascinating palace and get an inside view of its royal legacy.

  • Take a picnic in Kimbolton Castle Park: Enjoy a relaxing picnic with friends and family in the picturesque setting of Kimbolton Castle Park.

  • Attend a concert or event in the castle: Enjoy various concerts and events hosted in the stunning setting of Kimbolton Castle.

One-Week Itinerary for Kimbolton

If you’re planning to spend a week in Kimbolton, here’s an itinerary that will help you make the most of your stay:

  • Day 1: Visit Kimbolton Castle and explore the beautiful park.

  • Day 2: Take a hiking or biking trail in the scenic countryside, explore the flora and fauna of Fitzwilliam Lodge Nature Reserve.

  • Day 3: Take a tour of St Andrew’s Church followed by a stroll in Kimbolton High Street, famous for its unique shops and cafes.

  • Day 4: Visit the nearby town of Huntingdon, rich in history and culture, and explore its museums, galleries, and parks.

  • Day 5: Explore Bedford’s town center, famous for its stunning architecture, and shopping centers.

  • Day 6: Visit Grafham Water, one of the largest reservoirs in England, known for its abundance of wildlife and water sports activities.

  • Day 7: Take a trip to Cambridge, a historical university town famous for its stunning architecture, museums, galleries, and shops.

Food and Drink Scene of Kimbolton

Kimbolton is known for its rich culinary tradition and local flavors. Here is a list of must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes:

  • Sausage Rolls: The famous sausage rolls of Kimbolton are a perfect delight, light, and crispy pastry filled with locally sourced, flavorful sausages.

  • Fudge: Kimbolton is known for its handcrafted fudge, available in various flavors, including chocolate, vanilla, and mint.

  • Jacket Potatoes: This is the perfect comfort food in Kimbolton, featuring soft, fluffy potatoes with various tasty fillings.

Local Beverages:

  • Brewery Beers: Kimbolton is home to several breweries making various craft beers, such as The Hurst Brewery, The Pheasantry Brewery and Brewery Potton, and offer tastings or brewery tours.

Where to Stay in Kimbolton: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Kimbolton offers a range of accommodation options that cater to all budgets and lifestyles.

  • Kimbolton Lodge: A comfortable guesthouse that features elegant rooms, private bathrooms, and in-room coffee/tea facilities.

  • Three Stallion Inn: A cozy inn featuring modern facilities, home-cooked meals, and a convivial atmosphere.

  • The White Horse Inn: A historic inn with stylish decor, traditional home-cooked meals and live music.

What to Buy in Kimbolton: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Kimbolton offers a range of souvenirs and local products that you can take back home as a memento of your visit. Here are some ideas:

  • Fudge: The fudge of Kimbolton is the perfect souvenir, handcrafted in the village, and available in various flavors.

  • Beers and ciders: Kimbolton is well known for its craft beer and ciders, which are perfect gifts for anyone appreciates local breweries.

  • Locally sourced cheese and meat: Cambridgeshire is well known for its dairy farms and locally sourced farms, offering an array of smoked cheese and locally reared meat.

Local Culture and Customs of Kimbolton

Kimbolton has a rich culture and vibrant community, influenced by its royal heritage and scenic landscapes. Here are some local customs and cultural traditions:

  • May Day: This is an annual festival celebrated on the first Monday of May, featuring a colorful procession around the village, led by the May king and queen.

  • Harvest Festival: This is an annual festival celebrated in October to celebrate the harvest, with an array of music, dance, and cultural events.

Best Time to Visit Kimbolton

The best time to visit Kimbolton is from August to September, offering a pleasant climate and fewer crowds. The village is also worth a visit in winter, when it takes on a magical quality, and the Christmas celebrations and lights make for a festive atmosphere.

Do’s and Don’ts of Kimbolton

Here are some do’s and don’ts while traveling to Kimbolton:

Safety in Kimbolton

  • Safety during outdoor activities: While engaging in outdoor activities, such as hiking or cycling, make sure you are prepared with appropriate clothing, supplies, and equipment.

Getting to Kimbolton: Transportation Options and Logistics

Getting to Kimbolton is easy, with several transportation options available.

Airports Near Kimbolton

  • London Luton Airport: Located approximately 45 miles from Kimbolton.

  • London Stansted Airport: Located approximately 45 miles from Kimbolton.

Bus Stations Near Kimbolton

  • Bedford Bus Station: Bus services available from and to Bedford.

  • Cambridge Bus Station: Bus services available from and to Cambridge.

Other Options

  • Train: The nearest train station to Kimbolton is St Neots station, with frequent services to and from London and Cambridge.

  • Driving: Kimbolton is easily accessible by car via the M11, A1, and A14 motorways.

How to Get Around in Kimbolton

Kimbolton is a small village, and most attractions are within walking distance. Visitors can also hire a bike or car to explore the surrounding countryside.
