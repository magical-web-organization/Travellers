Introduction to Cambridge

Cambridge is a picturesque university city with a rich history that dates back to the Roman times. The city has many beautiful buildings that showcase different architectural styles, including Gothic, Tudor, Baroque, and modern. Home to one of the most prestigious universities in the world, Cambridge is a bustling academic hub that attracts students, researchers, and visitors from all over the world. The city has a lot to offer, from world-renowned museums and galleries to tranquil parks and gardens. In this comprehensive tour guide, we will cover everything you need to know about Cambridge to make your visit unforgettable.

List of Places to Visit in Cambridge

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Cambridge is rich in history and culture, and there are many museums and historical sites that visitors can explore.

  • The Fitzwilliam Museum: A museum of art and antiquities that houses a vast collection of paintings, sculptures, and artifacts from around the world.
  • The Museum of Archaeology and Anthropology: This museum is home to an extensive collection of artifacts from different cultures and civilizations, showcasing the diversity of human history.
  • King’s College Chapel: This chapel is one of the finest examples of Gothic architecture in the world, with stunning stained glass windows and intricate stonework.
  • The Round Church: This unique building is one of only four round churches in England, and it has been standing in Cambridge since the 12th century.
  • The Cambridge University Botanic Garden: This garden is an oasis of tranquility in the heart of the city, with collections of plants from all over the world.

Natural Attractions of Cambridge: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Cambridge is also a city of natural beauty, with many parks, gardens, and waterways to explore.

  • The River Cam: This river is the heart and soul of Cambridge, and it is famous for its punting tours. Visitors can take a leisurely punting tour to see the sights of the city from the water.
  • Midsummer Common: This large park has plenty of open space for picnics, sports, and leisurely walks. The park is famous for hosting the annual Midsummer Fair in June.
  • Grantchester Meadows: This idyllic meadow is a popular spot for picnics and walks, and it inspired the famous “Grantchester” poem by Rupert Brooke.
  • Wandlebury Country Park: This park is a great place to go for a walk or bike ride, with a network of trails through woodland and parkland.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Cambridge

Cambridge is a great city for exploring on foot or by bike, with many scenic routes to choose from.

  • The Grantchester Meadows Walk: This beautiful walk takes you through the countryside to Grantchester village, where you can stop for a drink or a meal.
  • The Wandlebury Country Park Walk: This walk takes you through beautiful woodland and parkland, with plenty of opportunities to spot wildlife and take in the scenery.
  • The Cambridge Guided Walking Tour: This tour takes you to some of the best-known landmarks in the city, including the colleges and the River Cam.
  • The Cambridge to Ely Cycle Route: This 20-mile cycle route takes you through beautiful countryside to the historic city of Ely, famous for its cathedral.

Best Things to Do in Cambridge: Activities and Tours

There are plenty of activities and tours to choose from in Cambridge, catering to a wide range of interests.

  • Punting on the River Cam: Punting is a classic Cambridge activity that involves navigating the river in a traditional wooden boat. You can either hire a boat and do it yourself or take a guided tour.
  • Cambridge University Walking Tour: This tour takes you on a guided walk around the university, showcasing the history, architecture, and traditions of this world-famous institution.
  • Cambridge Ghost Tour: This tour takes you on a spooky journey through the haunted streets of Cambridge, learning about the city’s dark history and legends.
  • Cambridge Food Tour: This tour takes you on a culinary journey around the city, tasting local delicacies and learning about the history and culture of the local food scene.

Week-Long Itinerary for Cambridge

If you have a week to spare in Cambridge, here is a suggested itinerary to help you make the most of your time:

Day 1: Arrive in Cambridge and explore the city center. Start with a guided walking tour to get your bearings and see the famous landmarks.

Day 2: Visit some of the city’s museums and galleries, such as the Fitzwilliam Museum, the Museum of Archaeology and Anthropology, and the Cambridge University Botanic Garden.

Day 3: Take a punt down the River Cam and enjoy the scenic views of the city.

Day 4: Rent a bike and take a cycle route to nearby villages, such as Grantchester or Ely.

Day 5: Visit some of the city’s historic sites, such as King’s College Chapel and the Round Church.

Day 6: Take a food tour to explore the local food scene and try some of the city’s traditional dishes and beverages.

Day 7: Spend the day in some of the city’s parks and gardens, such as Midsummer Common or Wandlebury Country Park.

Food and Drink Scene of Cambridge

Cambridge has a vibrant food and drink scene, with plenty of options for foodies and travelers looking to try something new. Here are some must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Cambridge Sausages: These sausages are made with locally sourced meat and herbs, and they have a rich, savory flavor.
  • Chelsea Buns: These sweet buns are a local specialty, made with currants, spices, and sticky sugar glaze.
  • Eel Pie: This traditional pie is made with eel and vegetables, and it has been a local specialty for centuries.

Local Beverages

  • Cambridge Gin: This award-winning gin is made with botanicals grown in the Cambridge University Botanic Garden, including herbs, flowers, and citrus fruits.
  • Greene King Beer: This beer is a local favorite, brewed by the Greene King Brewery, which has been operating in Bury St Edmunds since 1799.
  • Orchard Trust Apple Juice: This refreshing apple juice is made with locally grown apples from orchards in Cambridgeshire.

Where to Stay in Cambridge: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Cambridge has a range of accommodation options to suit all budgets and needs. Here are some recommendations:

  • The University Arms Hotel: This luxurious hotel is located in the heart of the city, offering stunning views of the historic buildings.
  • YHA Cambridge Hostel: This affordable hostel is perfect for budget travelers, with clean, comfortable rooms and a central location.
  • The Varsity Hotel and Spa: This boutique hotel is located on the River Cam, with spacious rooms and a rooftop terrace with panoramic views of the city.

What to Buy in Cambridge: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Cambridge is home to many unique shops and boutiques that offer a range of souvenirs and local products. Here are some recommendations:

  • Cambridge Imprint: This shop sells beautiful hand-printed textiles, including notebooks, scarves, and tea towels, featuring designs inspired by the city’s architecture and nature.
  • The Cambridge Cheese Company: This shop offers a wide range of local and international cheeses, as well as crackers, chutneys, and other gourmet products.
  • Cambridge Gin Distillery: This distillery produces the famous Cambridge Gin, which can be purchased on-site along with gin-related gifts and accessories.

Local Culture and Customs of Cambridge

Cambridge has a rich cultural heritage, with many traditions and customs that are still observed today. Here are some of the most notable:

  • May Balls: These extravagant balls take place in May/June, and they are organized by the different colleges of the university. They feature formal attire, live music, and fireworks.
  • Punting: Punting is a traditional way of traveling on the River Cam, and it has been an integral part of Cambridge life for centuries.
  • Bumps Races: These rowing races take place on the River Cam, and they involve teams of rowers trying to “bump” their opponents.

Best Time to Visit Cambridge

The best time to visit Cambridge is between April and September, when the weather is mild and the city is buzzing with activity. This is also the peak tourist season, so it’s important to book accommodation and activities in advance.

Do’s and Don’ts of Cambridge

Do:

  • Do take a guided tour of the university to learn about its history and traditions.
  • Do go punting on the River Cam to see the city from a different perspective.
  • Do try the local food and drink specialties, such as Cambridge sausages, Chelsea buns, and Cambridge gin.

Don’t:

  • Don’t feed the swans or other wildlife in the parks and gardens.
  • Don’t take photographs inside the university buildings without permission.
  • Don’t litter or leave trash in the parks and gardens.

Safety and Health

Safety in Cambridge

Cambridge is a safe city, but visitors should be cautious and take basic safety precautions, such as:

  • Keeping valuables out of sight and in a secure place.
  • Being aware of your surroundings, especially at night.
  • Avoiding poorly lit areas and unknown places.

Health in Cambridge

Cambridge has good healthcare facilities, including hospitals, clinics, and pharmacies. Visitors should ensure that they have adequate travel insurance to cover any medical emergencies.

Getting to Cambridge: Transportation Options and Logistics

Cambridge is well connected to other parts of the UK, with several transportation options available.

Airports Near Cambridge

  • London Stansted Airport: This airport is about 30 miles south of Cambridge, and it offers international and domestic flights.
  • London Heathrow Airport: This airport is about 80 miles south of Cambridge, and it offers international and domestic flights.

Bus Stations Near Cambridge

  • Cambridge Bus Station: This is the main bus station in the city, with connections to other parts of the UK.
  • Cambridge Parkside Bus Station: This bus station is located near the city center, with connections to other parts of the county.

Other Options

  • Train: Cambridge has its own railway station, with direct connections to London, Birmingham, and other major cities.
  • Car: Cambridge is connected to the M11 motorway, which links London with the east of England.

How to Get Around in Cambridge

Cambridge is a compact city, and most of the sights and attractions are within walking distance of each other. However, there are other transportation options available, such as:

  • Bus: There are several bus routes that operate in the city, with frequent services to the city center and surrounding areas.
  • Bicycle: Cambridge is a bike-friendly city, with many cycle routes and bike rental shops available.
  • Taxi: Taxis are available from various taxi ranks in the city center, or they can be booked in advance.

Where to Go Next from Cambridge: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Cambridge is a great base for exploring other parts of the east of England, with many nearby destinations and excursions to choose from.

  • Ely: This historic city is about 15 miles north of Cambridge, famous for its beautiful cathedral and charming streets.
  • Newmarket: This town is about 15 miles east of Cambridge, and it is known as the home of horse racing.
  • Suffolk Coast: This scenic coastal region is about an hour’s drive from Cambridge, with beautiful beaches, charming villages, and nature reserves.

Conclusion

Cambridge is a charming and historic city that offers something for everyone, from culture and history to food and outdoor adventures. We hope this comprehensive tour guide has helped you plan your visit and make the most of your time in this beautiful city.
