Introduction to Huntingdon

Located in the heart of Cambridgeshire, Huntingdon is a charming market town with a rich history and vibrant culture. From its picturesque surroundings to its lively food scene, Huntingdon has something to offer every traveler.

List of Places to Visit in Huntingdon

Here are some of the must-visit places when traveling to Huntingdon.

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Huntingdon Castle - A 12th-century castle ruins that played a significant role in the English Civil War.

  • Cromwell Museum - The only museum dedicated to the life and legacy of Oliver Cromwell, one of England’s most influential leaders.

Natural Attractions of Huntingdon: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • The Ouse Valley Way - A 150-mile walking route that passes through Huntingdon’s scenic countryside.

  • Portholme Meadow - A picturesque and tranquil spot perfect for picnics and outdoor activities.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Huntingdon

Huntingdon is a haven for nature lovers with its forests, lakes, and winding footpaths. Here are some of the best hiking and biking routes to explore:

  • The Great Ouse Way - A 69-mile walking and cycling route that follows the Great Ouse river from Huntingdon to Bedford.

  • Thicket and Houghton Mill Walk - A peaceful 3-mile route that passes through Thicket Nature Reserve and Houghton Mill.

Best Things to Do in Huntingdon: Activities and Tours

Huntingdon offers plenty of activities for visitors of all ages. Here are some of the top things to do:

  • Ferry Meadows Country Park - A 500-acre park with activities such as cycling, water sports, and fishing.

  • Huntingdon Racecourse - A popular venue for horse racing, hosting 18 race days throughout the year.

Week-Long Itinerary for Huntingdon

Here’s a sample week-long itinerary for Huntingdon:

Day Activity
Monday Explore Huntingdon Castle and Cromwell Museum

Food and Drink Scene of Huntingdon

Huntingdon’s food and drink scene is a blend of traditional and modern cuisines. Here are some must-try dishes and beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Huntingdon Fidget Pie - A savory pie featuring diced pork, apples, and onions.

  • Sticky Toffee Pudding - A classic British pudding with warm caramel sauce.

Local Beverages

  • Gin from the Secret Garden Distillery - A handcrafted gin produced in a small batch distillery near Huntingdon.

  • Real ale from The Samuel Pepys - A local pub that serves traditional real ale.

Where to Stay in Huntingdon: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Here are some of the best accommodations in Huntingdon:

  • The George Hotel - A luxury hotel with a beautiful location and excellent service.

  • Oliver’s Lodge Hotel - A charming guest house with cozy rooms and warm hospitality.

What to Buy in Huntingdon: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Here are some unique souvenirs and local products to bring back from Huntingdon:

  • Oliver Cromwell Ale - A brew commemorating the legacy of Huntingdon’s famous son.

  • Local honey from Huntingdonshire Honey Company - A deliciously sweet souvenir from Huntingdon’s honey producers.

Local Culture and Customs of Huntingdon

Huntingdon is a town that celebrates its history, and its customs reflect this. Visitors should be mindful of these cultural norms, such as showing respect at historical landmarks and participating in local festivals.

Best Time to Visit Huntingdon

The best time to visit Huntingdon is during late spring and summer months when the weather is mild and outdoor activities are plentiful.

Do’s and Don’ts of Huntingdon

Here are some do’s and don’ts to follow when visiting Huntingdon:

Do:

  • Respect cultural norms and traditions

  • Appreciate the natural beauty around you

Don’t:

  • Litter or damage historical landmarks

  • Ignore safety measures when enjoying outdoor adventures

Safety and Health

Safety in Huntingdon

Huntingdon is generally a safe town, but it’s advisable to take the necessary precautions when walking in unfamiliar areas at night.

Health in Huntingdon

Huntingdon has excellent medical facilities that are accessible to all visitors. Travelers should make sure they have comprehensive travel insurance before embarking on their trip.

Getting to Huntingdon: Transportation Options and Logistics

Here are some transportation options to get to Huntingdon:

Airports Near Huntingdon

  • London Stansted Airport - Located 40 miles from Huntingdon.

  • London Luton Airport - Located 45 miles from Huntingdon.

Bus Stations Near Huntingdon

  • Huntingdon Bus Station - A central bus station that serves several routes in and out of the town.

Other Options

  • Train - Huntingdon Railway Station connects Huntingdon to several major cities, including London and Cambridge.

How to Get Around in Huntingdon

Huntingdon is a small town that can be easily explored on foot. Cycling and driving are also popular modes of transportation.

Where to Go Next from Huntingdon: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Here are some nearby destinations and excursions to consider after exploring Huntingdon:

  • Cambridge - A historic university city just 20 miles away from Huntingdon.

  • Peterborough Cathedral - One of England’s most significant medieval cathedrals located only 20 miles away from Huntingdon.

Conclusion

Huntingdon is a picturesque town with plenty to offer visitors. From its scenic surroundings to its rich history and culture, Huntingdon is the perfect destination for those seeking a unique and memorable travel experience.
