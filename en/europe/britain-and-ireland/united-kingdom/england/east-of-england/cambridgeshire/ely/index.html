Introduction to Ely

Nestled in the heart of Cambridgeshire, in the East of England, Ely is a historic city that boasts a rich and fascinating past. Synonymous with its magnificent cathedral, which dominates the skyline, Ely offers visitors an enticing blend of culture, history, and natural beauty. Whether you’re looking to explore the city’s museums and galleries, take a leisurely stroll along the riverbank, or simply relax in a quaint English pub, Ely has something to suit all tastes.

Local Culture and Customs of Ely

Ely has a proud history and strong local traditions, including the famous Eel Day festival, which takes place each May. Visitors can also experience traditional English country pursuits such as fishing and shooting in the surrounding countryside.

Best Time to Visit Ely

The shoulder seasons of spring and autumn offer pleasant temperatures and fewer crowds. Summer is the peak tourist season when the city can get crowded, and hotels may charge higher rates.

Do’s and Don’ts of Ely

Do:

  • Visit Ely Cathedral, the city’s most famous landmark.
  • Take a stroll along the riverbank and enjoy the scenic views.
  • Try the local cuisine and beverages, particularly Ely Gin and Fenland Smoked Salmon.

Don’t:

  • Disrespect local traditions or customs.
  • Leave litter or damage the environment.
  • Be rude or impolite to locals and other tourists.

How to Get Around in Ely

Ely is a small city and easy to explore on foot. Alternatively, visitors can rent bikes or hire a taxi or minibus for longer journeys.
