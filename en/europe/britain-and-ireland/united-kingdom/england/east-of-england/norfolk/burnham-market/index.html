A Comprehensive Travel Guide to Burnham Market

Located in the heart of beautiful Norfolk, Burnham Market is a picturesque village that boasts a rich history, stunning natural beauty, and a vibrant culture. Whether you’re looking for a relaxing getaway, outdoor adventure, or a glimpse into the past, Burnham Market has something to offer for everyone. In this comprehensive travel guide, we’ll explore the best places to visit, eat, stay, and more in Burnham Market.

Introduction to Burnham Market

Founded in the 17th century, Burnham Market is a charming market town that has preserved its historic homes, shops, and architecture. Its central market square features traditional market stalls and is surrounded by cozy pubs, restaurants, and shops. Visitors can take a leisurely stroll while admiring the stunning Georgian buildings, colorful flowers, and ancient church.

List of Places to Visit in Burnham Market

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • St. Mary’s Church - A grade I listed building, this ancient church is over 900 years old and is a stunning example of Norman architecture.
  • The Hoste Arms - Originally a 17th-century coaching inn, The Hoste Arms is now a boutique hotel and restaurant that has hosted many famous guests over the years.
  • Tourist Information Centre - Visitors can learn about the history and culture of Burnham Market at the information center and pick up some guides and maps for exploring the area.

Natural Attractions of Burnham Market: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Holkham National Nature Reserve - A beautiful haven for nature lovers, Holkham National Nature Reserve is home to miles of unspoiled beaches, marshes, and woodlands. Visitors can explore the reserve on foot, bike, boat, or horseback.
  • Burnham Beach - With its golden sand and crystal-clear water, Burnham Beach is one of the best beaches in Norfolk. Visitors can enjoy swimming, sunbathing, and taking long walks along the shore.
  • Scolt Head Island - A unique peninsula and nature reserve, Scolt Head Island is a popular destination for birdwatching, hiking, and relaxing. Visitors can see many rare bird species and enjoy panoramic views of the coast.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Burnham Market

Burnham Market is a paradise for hikers and bikers, with several scenic routes that offer stunning views of the countryside and coastline. Some of the best routes include:

  • Peddars Way and Norfolk Coast Path - A 93-mile trail that runs from Suffolk to Norfolk, the Peddars Way and Norfolk Coast Path offer a mix of rugged coastline, winding creeks, and rolling countryside.
  • Wells and Holkham Circuit - A 15-mile loop that starts in Wells-next-the-Sea and takes visitors through the beautiful Holkham Estate and along the stunning coastal path.

Best Things to Do in Burnham Market: Activities and Tours

  • Swimming and Water Sports - Visitors can enjoy a variety of water sports at Burnham Beach, including swimming, paddleboarding, windsurfing, and kiteboarding.
  • Horseback Riding - Burnham Market is home to several excellent riding schools that offer lessons and tours for all skill levels.
  • Food and Drink Tours - Visitors can take a guided tour of the area’s best food and drink producers, from artisan cheese makers to award-winning wineries.

Week-Long Itinerary for Burnham Market

For those who want to spend more time in Burnham Market, here’s a suggested week-long itinerary:

Day Activities
Day 1 Explore Burnham Market’s historic market square and visit St. Mary’s Church
Day 2 Take a leisurely walk along Burnham Beach and enjoy a picnic
Day 3 Discover the beauty of Holkham Estate and go birdwatching
Day 4 Take a guided food and drink tour of the local area
Day 5 Go horseback riding in the countryside
Day 6 Take a bike ride along the Norfolk Coast Path
Day 7 Relax and enjoy the local cuisine at one of Burnham Market’s cozy pubs or restaurants

Food and Drink Scene of Burnham Market

Must-Try Dishes

  • Crab and Lobster - Norfolk is known for its fresh seafood, and Burnham Market is no exception. Visitors must try the crab and lobster, which are caught daily and served in many local restaurants.
  • Norfolk Ale - There’s nothing better than a pint of locally brewed ale to accompany a tasty meal. Visitors can try different types of beer at The Hoste Arms or The Nelson Pub.
  • Arabica Coffee - For coffee lovers, Arabica is a must-visit. This cozy coffee shop serves some of the best coffee and home-made cakes in the area.

Local Beverages

  • Adnams - A well-known brewery and distillery, Adnams offers a variety of beers, wines, and spirits that visitors can taste and buy at its store in Burnham Market.
  • Norfolk Cordial - Made from locally sourced fruits and berries, Norfolk Cordial is a refreshing drink that visitors must try.
  • Birchall Tea - For tea lovers, Birchall Tea is a must-visit. This local tea company offers a variety of high-quality teas that visitors can taste and buy.

Where to Stay in Burnham Market: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • The Hoste Arms - One of the best hotels in Burnham Market, The Hoste Arms offers luxurious rooms, delicious food, and a prime location in the heart of the town.
  • The Burnham Beeches Hotel - This cozy hotel is located just a short walk from the town center and offers comfortable rooms, a beautiful garden, and a friendly atmosphere.
  • Kings Head Hotel - Located in the nearby town of Great Bircham, the Kings Head Hotel offers stylish rooms, delicious food, and a relaxed atmosphere.

What to Buy in Burnham Market: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • Art and Crafts - Burnham Market is home to several local artists and craft makers who sell their art and crafts in the town’s shops and galleries.
  • Norfolk Cheese - Made from locally sourced milk, Norfolk Cheese is a must-try for cheese lovers. Visitors can buy different types of cheese at the nearby village of Walsingham.
  • Local Produce - Visitors can buy fresh vegetables, fruits, and meat at the weekly market that takes place in the town’s market square every Wednesday.

Local Culture and Customs of Burnham Market

Although Burnham Market is a popular tourist destination, it is also a living, breathing community with its unique culture and customs. Visitors should respect the locals’ privacy and take care of the environment.

Best Time to Visit Burnham Market

The best time to visit Burnham Market is from May to September when the weather is warm and sunny. However, visitors should be aware that this is also the busiest time of the year and that accommodation prices might be higher.

Do’s and Don’ts of Burnham Market

Do:

  • Respect the locals’ privacy and traditions.
  • Take care of the environment and avoid littering.
  • Try the local food and drink and support the area’s economy.

Don’t:

  • Disturb the wildlife or enter restricted areas.
  • Ignore the tide times when going to the beach.
  • Park in unauthorized places or block the roads.

Safety and Health

Safety in Burnham Market

Burnham Market is a safe destination, but visitors should take the usual precautions to avoid becoming a victim of theft or crime. Visitors should also follow the safety guidelines when swimming, sailing, hiking, or biking.

Health in Burnham Market

Burnham Market has excellent healthcare facilities, including a medical center and a pharmacy. Visitors should also take precautions against sunburn, dehydration, and insect bites.

Getting to Burnham Market: Transportation Options and Logistics

Visitors can get to Burnham Market by car, train, or bus. The nearest airports are Norwich International Airport and London Stansted Airport.

Airports Near Burnham Market

  • Norwich International Airport - Located 31 miles from Burnham Market, Norwich International Airport offers regular flights to many destinations in the UK and Europe.
  • London Stansted Airport - Located 86 miles from Burnham Market, London Stansted Airport offers regular flights to many destinations in the UK and worldwide.

Bus Stations Near Burnham Market

  • Burnham Market Bus Station - Located in the town center, Burnham Market Bus Station offers regular buses to nearby towns and villages.
  • King’s Lynn Bus Station - Located 19 miles from Burnham Market, King’s Lynn Bus Station offers buses to many destinations in the area.

Other Options

Visitors can also get to Burnham Market by train, bicycle, or taxi.

How to Get Around in Burnham Market

Burnham Market is a small town that visitors can easily explore on foot or by bicycle. Visitors can also hire a taxi or rent a car to explore the surrounding area.

Where to Go Next from Burnham Market: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Wells-next-the-Sea - A popular seaside town located just 5 miles from Burnham Market, Wells-next-the-Sea is known for its beautiful beach, historic harbor, and charming town center.
  • Holkham Hall - A magnificent stately home and gardens located just 2 miles from Burnham Market, Holkham Hall is a popular destination for history and architecture enthusiasts.
  • Brancaster Staithe - A picturesque village located 7 miles from Burnham Market, Brancaster Staithe is a popular destination for boating, fishing, and seafood lovers.

Conclusion

Burnham Market is an enchanting destination that offers a wealth of opportunities for exploring nature, history, and culture. Whether you’re looking for a relaxing holiday or an outdoor adventure, Burnham Market is the perfect place to be. With this comprehensive travel guide, visitors can plan their trip to Burnham Market, discover the best places to visit, eat, stay, and more, and have an unforgettable experience.
