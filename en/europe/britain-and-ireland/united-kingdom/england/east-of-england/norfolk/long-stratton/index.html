Introduction to Long Stratton

Located in the heart of Norfolk in the East of England, Long Stratton is a charming market town that offers visitors a mix of cultural attractions, scenic spots, and outdoor adventures. With a rich history dating back to the 13th century, the town provides a glimpse into the region’s past with its ancient churches, museums, and historic landmarks.

Long Stratton has a friendly, small-town vibe that adds to its appeal. Visitors can enjoy a peaceful stay in the town’s many accommodations, sample local delicacies at its eateries, and engage with the local culture. Whether you are looking for a weekend getaway or a longer vacation, Long Stratton is worth exploring.

List of Places to Visit in Long Stratton

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

The Old Court House Museum

Featuring a fascinating collection of artifacts and exhibits showcasing the town’s history and culture, The Old Court House Museum is a must-visit for all visitors to Long Stratton. Here, you can explore the town’s medieval origins, learn about the life of its residents, and understand how it grew into the charming market town it is today.

All Saints’ Church

A true gem of Long Stratton, All Saints’ Church dates back to the 14th century and is the town’s oldest historic landmark. It features intricate stained-glass windows, a beautiful chancel, and a magnificent tower. Visitors can attend services or simply marvel at the church’s impressive architecture.

Natural Attractions of Long Stratton: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Stratton Stawless Common

Located just a short drive from Long Stratton, Stratton Stawless Common is a serene natural area that offers visitors the chance to enjoy scenic views and outdoor adventures. You can hike or bike along its many trails, spot wildlife, have a picnic with family and friends, or simply soak in the beauty of nature.

Tas Valley Vineyard

For wine enthusiasts, a visit to Tas Valley Vineyard is a must-do. The vineyard uses traditional methods to create some of the best wines in the region. Visitors can take guided tours of the vineyards, learn about the wine-making process, and taste some of the vineyard’s delicious wines.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Long Stratton

Long Stratton and its surrounding areas have plenty of hiking and biking routes that allow visitors to explore the region’s natural beauty. The South Norfolk Cycleway is a particularly popular route for cyclists, while the Tas Valley Way offers scenic hiking trails that range from easy to challenging.

Best Things to Do in Long Stratton: Activities and Tours

Visit a Local Farm

Long Stratton is home to several working farms that offer visitors the chance to learn about the region’s agricultural heritage and connect with the local community. You can get up close with farm animals, take part in farm tours, and buy fresh produce and homemade goods.

Take a Ghost Tour

For the brave and adventurous, a ghost tour of Long Stratton is an unforgettable experience. The town has a rich history of ghost sightings and paranormal activity. You can explore haunted sites with a knowledgeable guide, listen to spooky stories, and learn about the town’s haunted past.

Week-Long Itinerary for Long Stratton

For those planning a longer stay in Long Stratton, here’s a suggested itinerary:

Experience the region’s agricultural heritage and connect with the community by visiting a local farm.

Take a guided tour of Tas Valley Vineyard, learn about the wine-making process, and sample some of the region’s best wines.

Day 5: Tas Valley Way Hiking

Explore the scenic Tas Valley Way hiking route and take in the natural beauty of the area.

Day 6: Ghost Tour

For those brave enough, take a ghost tour of Long Stratton and learn about its haunted past.

Day 7: Relax and Enjoy the Town

Spend the final day of your trip lounging at a café, trying out local delicacies or simply exploring this charming market town.

Food and Drink Scene of Long Stratton

Must-Try Dishes

Long Stratton’s food scene is all about locally sourced ingredients, fresh produce, and a variety of flavors. Some must-try dishes include:

  • Norfolk stew: A hearty meat and vegetable stew that originates from the area.
  • Cromer crab: This classic dish features the freshest crab meat caught from the nearby North Sea.
  • Norfolk sausages: These savory sausages are made from local meats and herbs and are served in many of the town’s eateries.

Local Beverages

For drinks, there are several options for visitors to try, including:

  • Wherry Ale: A classic Norfolk beer known for its distinctive taste and high-quality ingredients.
  • Norfolk cider: Made from locally sourced apples, this cider is a refreshing drink perfect for a summer day.
  • Norfolk gin: This botanical gin is flavoured with herbs and spices found in the region and is a popular choice among locals and visitors alike.

Where to Stay in Long Stratton: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Long Stratton offers a range of accommodation options that cater to all budgets and preferences. Visitors can choose from hotels, hostels, guesthouses and even rental homes. Some popular options include:

  • The Old Ram Coaching Inn: This historic inn dates back to the 17th century and offers visitors a comfortable stay with a touch of old-world charm.
  • The Swan Hotel: This boutique hotel features beautifully appointed rooms, excellent on-site dining options and is in close proximity to many of the town’s top attractions.
  • The Long Stratton Motor Inn: For those looking for budget options, The Long Stratton Motor Inn is an affordable choice with clean and comfortable rooms.

What to Buy in Long Stratton: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Visitors to Long Stratton can take home several unique souvenirs and local products. Here are some recommendations:

  • Handmade ceramics and pottery: Made by talented artists in the area, these unique pieces make for great gifts or statement pieces in your home.
  • Local jam and chutney: Made from locally sourced fruits, these spreads are perfect for breakfast or as an accompaniment to cheese boards and crackers.
  • Norfolk lavender: Aromatic and visually pleasing, Norfolk lavender is a popular choice among visitors looking for a memorable souvenir.

Local Culture and Customs of Long Stratton

Long Stratton has a rich culture and history that is reflected in its traditions and customs. As a market town, Long Stratton is known for its bustling market which takes place every Friday. Visitors can experience the local culture by exploring the market, talking to vendors, and sampling local delicacies.

Best Time to Visit Long Stratton

The best time to visit Long Stratton is during the summer months, between June and August. This time of year offers the best weather, with warm temperatures and plenty of sunshine. Additionally, many of the town’s outdoor attractions, such as hiking trails and vineyards, are open and accessible.

Do’s and Don’ts of Long Stratton

Do:

  • Do explore the town’s rich history by visiting its many museums and historic landmarks.
  • Do hike along the South Norfolk Cycleway and Tas Valley Way for a glimpse into the region’s natural beauty.
  • Do buy local products as souvenirs from the town’s markets and specialty shops.

Don’t:

  • Don’t litter in the town or its surrounding natural areas.
  • Don’t forget to dress appropriately for the weather and outdoor activities.
  • Don’t be disrespectful or disruptive towards the local culture and customs.

Safety and Health

Safety in Long Stratton

Long Stratton is a safe town with a low crime rate. However, visitors should still exercise caution and take necessary precautions to prevent theft or other crimes.

Health in Long Stratton

Long Stratton has several medical facilities, including a hospital, clinic and pharmacy. Visitors should check with their insurance provider before traveling to make sure they are covered in case of medical emergencies.

Getting to Long Stratton: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Long Stratton

Norwich International Airport is the closest airport to Long Stratton, located just 20 miles away. Other nearby airports include Stansted Airport and Luton Airport, both of which cater to domestic and international flights.

Bus Stations Near Long Stratton

Long Stratton has a local road network that is serviced by several bus companies, including First Eastern Counties, Simonds, and National Express Coaches. There are bus stations in nearby towns such as Norwich and Diss, which run services to Long Stratton.

Other Options

Several car rental companies operate in Long Stratton, providing visitors with the option to hire a car for their travels. Additionally, Long Stratton has several taxi companies that offer a reliable and convenient mode of transportation.

How to Get Around in Long Stratton

Long Stratton is a small town that can be easily explored on foot or by bike. There are also several car rental options for those who prefer to drive, as well as local taxis for transportation to other areas.

Where to Go Next from Long Stratton: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

There are several nearby destinations and excursions that visitors to Long Stratton can explore. Some popular choices include:

  • Norwich: Just a short drive away, Norwich is a historic city known for its beautiful architecture, shopping, and dining options.
  • Thetford Forest: Located just a few miles from Long Stratton, Thetford Forest, provides visitors with the opportunity to explore nature trails, wildlife and scenic landscapes.
  • The Broads: The Broads National Park is a network of rivers, waterways and nature reserves, providing visitors with an abundance of natural beauty and wildlife.

Conclusion

Long Stratton is an underrated gem in Norfolk that offers visitors a unique blend of culture, history, and natural beauty. From hiking trails and vineyards to museums and haunted sites, there is something for everyone in this charming market town. Use this comprehensive guide to experience the best of Long Stratton and the surrounding areas.
