Introduction to King’s Lynn

King’s Lynn, located in the county of Norfolk in the East of England, is a charming medieval town steeped in history with streets lined by Georgian and Victorian buildings. The town’s rich history, stunning architecture, and a plethora of amenities make it an ideal destination for a UK holiday.

In this travel guide, we’ll explore the best places to visit, things to do, places to stay, food and drink options, local culture and customs, and more in and around King’s Lynn.

List of Places to Visit in King’s Lynn

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Lynn Museum

Lynn Museum is the ideal place to start when exploring King’s Lynn’s history. The museum’s stunning displays include medieval artefacts, historic vehicles and a life-sized scale model of the town during the 1950s. The museum also offers interactive displays and a range of family-friendly activities.

Castle Rising

Located four miles north of King’s Lynn, Castle Rising is one of the most significant 12th-century castles in the UK. The castle and its earthworks are exceptionally well-preserved, providing an opportunity for visitors to explore and learn about medieval life.

Natural Attractions of King’s Lynn: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

The Norfolk Coast Path

The 84-mile Norfolk Coast Path stretches from Hunstanton to Cromer. Walkers love it because it provides stunning coastal views, wildlife sightings, and access to some of the most beautiful beaches in the UK. A walk on the Norfolk Coast Path is an ideal way to get some fresh air and enjoy nature.

The Wash National Nature Reserve

The Wash National Nature Reserve is an ideal place to visit for bird watching. The reserve is home to many species of migratory birds such as pink-feet geese, wading birds, and birds of prey. The reserve is also home to seals, rare butterflies, and flowers.

Hiking/Biking Routes of King’s Lynn

Peddars Way and Norfolk Coast Path

The Peddars Way and Norfolk Coast Path are two of the best walking routes in King’s Lynn. The Peddars Way starts at Knettishall Heath Country Park and ends at Holme-next-the-Sea, and the Norfolk Coast Path begins at Hunstanton and ends at Cromer. Both routes offer a stunning landscape and plenty of wildlife sightings.

Marriott’s Way

Marriott’s Way is an old railway line that has been turned into a walking and cycling path. The track starts at Norwich and ends at Aylsham, passing through Reepham, Whitwell, and Lenwade on the way. The path is ideal for a leisurely walk or bike ride for all ages.

Best Things to Do in King’s Lynn: Activities and Tours

King’s Lynn Ghost Walk

The King’s Lynn Ghost Walk is a 90-minute walking tour that explores the spooky history and supernatural side of the town. Led by a guide in full costume, you’ll hear chilling tales about ghosts, murders and legends as you pass through the historic streets of the town.

Snettisham Park Farm

Snettisham Park Farm is a working farm, primarily focused on deer farming and conservation. Visitors can visit the farm to learn about the animals, feed the deer, and even attend a deer safari tour. The farm also has a picnic area, a restaurant and a small boat ride attraction.

Food and Drink Scene of King’s Lynn

Must-Try Dishes

Cromer Crab and Lobster

The Norfolk coast provides some of the freshest seafood in the UK. Try a Cromer Crab or Lobster from one of the local seafood restaurants in King’s Lynn.

Marshland Double

The Marshland Double is a rare breed of beef that is unique to the area around King’s Lynn. The beef is known for being much more flavorful than other varieties.

Local Beverages

Norfolk Gins

Norfolk is home to some of the best gin distilleries in the UK. Try a glass of the local gins, such as Norfolk Gin, Bullards Norwich Dry or Black Shuck Gin.

Where to Stay in King’s Lynn: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Dukes Head Hotel

Located in the heart of the town, the Dukes Head Hotel is a stunning Edwardian hotel that is perfect for those looking for luxury accommodation.

Bank House Hotel

Located across the river from the town’s historic quarter, the Bank House Hotel offers a tranquil and peaceful stay in King’s Lynn. The hotel has a range of rooms and suites, and guests can enjoy the hotel’s gardens and grounds.

What to Buy in King’s Lynn: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Hand-Stitched Kites

The town of King’s Lynn is known for its hand-stitched kites that are made from traditional materials. They make a unique and fun souvenir to bring home.

Britain’s Fastest Tides Book

The book Britain’s Fastest Tides is an excellent read about the UK’s tidal ranges, particularly those in and around King’s Lynn.

Local Culture and Customs of King’s Lynn

The people of King’s Lynn take great pride in their heritage and traditions. The town is famous for a range of customs such as The Walks Fair, the annual Mart Fair, The Mayors Sunday, and The Hanse Festival.

Best Time to Visit King’s Lynn

The best time to visit King’s Lynn is between late spring and early autumn, when the weather is mild and conducive to outside activities.

Do’s and Don’ts of King’s Lynn

Do:

  • Visit the historic attractions in King’s Lynn
  • Try the local food and drink
  • Walk the Peddars Way and Norfolk Coast Path
  • Attend one of the town’s festivals

Don’t:

  • Litter in public spaces
  • Trespass on private land
  • Park in restricted areas without permission

Safety and Health

Safety in King’s Lynn

King’s Lynn is generally considered a safe town. However, visitors should take precautions to protect their valuables and avoid unlit areas at night.

Health in King’s Lynn

Visitors should ensure they have access to medical services if required. The Queen Elizabeth Hospital in King’s Lynn is a leading hospital in the region and offers 24-hour medical care.

Getting to King’s Lynn: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near King’s Lynn

The closest airports to King’s Lynn are Norwich International Airport and London Stansted Airport. Regular bus and taxi services operate from both airports.

Bus Stations Near King’s Lynn

King’s Lynn has excellent links to other parts of the UK via National Express buses and local bus services.

Other Options

King’s Lynn has two railway stations: King’s Lynn railway station and Watlington railway station, operated by Great Northern. Trains run regularly to London, Cambridge, Peterborough, and other destinations.

How to Get Around in King’s Lynn

King’s Lynn is a reasonably small town, so walking or cycling is the best way to get around. The town also has local buses and taxi services, and there is a car rental service for those who want to explore the wider area.

Where to Go Next from King’s Lynn: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Cambridge

Located just 50 miles from King’s Lynn, Cambridge is a beautiful medieval city famous for its prestigious university, historic architecture and punting on the River Cam.

The Fens

The Fens are a vast area of wetland that stretch across parts of eastern England. This unique landscape is perfect for walking, cycling, and bird watching.

Conclusion

King’s Lynn is an ideal destination for anyone looking for a UK holiday that combines history, culture, and outdoor activities. With plenty of historic attractions, beautiful coastal walks, and traditional customs and festivals, visitors will find plenty to see and do in this charming medieval town.
