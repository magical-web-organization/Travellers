Introduction to Dereham

Dereham, also known as East Dereham, is a historic market town located in the Breckland district of Norfolk, England. It’s situated around 16 miles west of the county town of Norwich. Dereham has a rich history dating back to the 7th century, with a number of important historical sites and architecture. The town center is small and picturesque, with a selection of shops, pubs, and restaurants to explore.

List of Places to Visit in Dereham

Dereham has a range of cultural, natural, and historical attractions that are worth visiting. Here are a few:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Dereham Windmill: A well-restored and historic windmill that dates back to the 19th century. There is also a small museum and tearoom onsite that showcases the history of the windmill and the surrounding area.
  • St. Nicholas Church: This beautiful church is a significant historical site and has been around since the 13th century. It’s a perfect example of medieval architecture and is known for its stained glass windows.
  • Mid-Norfolk Railway: This heritage railway line is one of the best ways to explore the picturesque villages and towns of Norfolk. With its steam engines and classic carriages, it’s a nostalgic journey into the past.

Natural Attractions of Dereham: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Whitwell Common: A beautiful nature reserve that’s home to a range of flora and fauna. It’s a perfect place for a peaceful walk, picnic, or nature photography.
  • Dereham Walks: Dereham has a range of walks and hiking trails that offer beautiful views of the countryside. The most popular is the Dereham Town Walk, which covers significant historical sites and offers scenic views of the town.
  • Gressenhall Farm and Workhouse: This award-winning museum and living history attraction provides an authentic glimpse into the agricultural and rural life of Norfolk.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Dereham

One of the most popular activities in Dereham is hiking or biking along scenic trails. Here are a few recommended routes:

  • Dereham Town Walk: A circular walk that covers significant historical sites and offers scenic views of the town.
  • Neatherd Moor Loop: A circular walk starting and ending at Dereham Windmill that passes through scenic fields and woodland.
  • Bawdeswell Circular: A circular cycle/walk that starts and ends at Dereham train station and passes through pretty villages and scenic countryside.

Best Things to Do in Dereham: Activities and Tours

Dereham offers a range of activities and tours that cater to different interests. Here are a few of them:

  • Mid-Norfolk Railway Experience: Take a trip on the heritage railway and explore the villages and towns of Norfolk.
  • Art Exhibitions at The Atrium Gallery: This contemporary gallery hosts a range of exhibitions featuring local artists and art forms.
  • Dereham Town Center Market: Every Friday, Dereham’s town center hosts a vibrant open-air market that offers a range of local produce, crafts, and goods.

Food and Drink Scene of Dereham

Norfolk has a rich culinary heritage, and Dereham’s food and drink scene celebrates local produce and gastronomical delights. Here are a few must-try dishes and local beverages:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Norfolk Lamb and Pork: Reared in the lush countryside of Norfolk, the meats are the most sought-after dishes. They’re served in traditional roasts or in culinary twists like pork fritters or herbed lamb cutlets.
  • Samphire: A seasonal, nutrient-dense green that grows in the marshes and mudflats of Norfolk. The tender shoots are deliciously salty and used in salads, seafood, or cooked on its own.

Local Beverages

  • Adnams Beer: Adnams Brewery is one of the most popular breweries in Norfolk and known for its classic beers, ales, and spirits.
  • Norfolk Mead: A delicious and traditional alcoholic brew made by fermenting honey and water, Norfolk mead is a sweet and aromatic drink that’s perfect to savor on a leisurely afternoon.

Where to Stay in Dereham: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Dereham offers a range of accommodation options that cater to different budgets and preferences. Here are a few recommended places to stay:

  • The King’s Head Hotel: A historic hotel situated in the town center, the King’s Head offers comfortable and luxurious accommodation with excellent dining options.
  • The Romany Rye: A traditional, cozy pub that offers great value-for-money accommodation and hearty meals.
  • Scarning Dale: A beautiful guesthouse that offers serene countryside accommodation with picturesque views and excellent facilities.

What to Buy in Dereham: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Dereham has a range of unique and traditional products that are perfect souvenirs to take back home. Here are a few local products to look out for:

  • Norfolk Lavender Products: Norfolk’s climate is conducive to growing high-quality lavender, and the region is known for a range of lavender products like soaps, essential oils, and candles. Norfolk Lavender is one of the best-known brands in this category.
  • Salle Farm Apple Juice: Salle Farm, located near Dereham, is known for its high-quality apple juice made from apples grown in Norfolk’s orchards.

Local Culture and Customs of Dereham

Dereham, like much of Norfolk, is steeped in a rich, rural history. The town has a friendly and welcoming vibe, and the locals are always happy to share their culture and heritage with visitors. The town celebrates many festivals and events throughout the year, such as:

  • Dereham Blues Festival: A three-day long music festival that showcases some of the best blues musicians from around the world.
  • Dereham Carnival: A colorful carnival that celebrates the town’s heritage and traditions.

Best Time to Visit Dereham

Dereham is a year-round destination, but the best time to visit is during the spring (March to May) or fall (September to November) seasons. The weather during these seasons is mild, and the countryside is at its most beautiful.

Do’s and Don’ts of Dereham

Here are a few things to keep in mind while visiting Dereham:

Do:

  • Take care of the environment and avoid trampling on the wildflowers and greenery.
  • Try out the local produce and gastronomical delights.

Don’t:

Safety and Health

Dereham is a relatively safe town, with a low crime rate. However, it’s always essential to take basic precautions like locking up your valuables and keeping an eye on your belongings while in public places.

  • Dereham has a range of local medical facilities, including hospitals and clinics. It’s always a good idea to carry your medications and consult with your doctor before embarking on any adventure sports or activities.

Getting to Dereham: Transportation Options and Logistics

Dereham is well-connected to other parts of Norfolk and the surrounding areas. Here are a few transportation options:

Airports Near Dereham

  • Norwich Airport: 17 miles away
  • London Stansted Airport: 70 miles away

Bus Stations Near Dereham

  • Dereham Bus Station: The local bus station is located in the town center
  • Norwich Bus Station: 17 miles away

Other Options

  • Car rental: There are several car rental agencies available in Dereham and Norwich.
  • Rail: Dereham has a train station that connects to other parts of Norfolk, including Norwich, Cambridge, and London.

How to Get Around in Dereham

Dereham is a small town and can be easily explored on foot or by bike. There are also several local buses and taxis available for short trips and excursions.

Where to Go Next from Dereham: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Dereham is a perfect base to explore other scenic and historic towns and villages in Norfolk. Here are a few nearby destinations to plan a day trip:

  • Norwich: A vibrant and historic city that’s known for its cultural attractions, museums, and nightlife. It’s also the county town of Norfolk.
  • Wells-next-the-Sea: A pretty coastal town that offers stunning views of the North Sea and has a range of activities like beach walks, kite flying, and birdwatching.
  • Holkham Hall: A historic country house that’s known for its beautiful architecture and manicured gardens.
