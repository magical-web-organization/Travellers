Introduction to Stevenage

Stevenage is a charming town located in Hertfordshire, in the East of England. It is about 30 miles north of London and has a population of around 80,000 people. Stevenage is known for its rich history, beautiful architecture, and stunning natural beauty.

List of Places to Visit in Stevenage

Stevenage has a lot to offer visitors, from museums and historical sites to natural attractions and outdoor adventures. Here is a list of the top places to visit in Stevenage:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Stevenage Museum: This museum is located in the Old Town of Stevenage and tells the story of the town from its prehistoric origins to the present day.
  • Knebworth House: This Tudor country house is located a few miles north of Stevenage and is open to the public. It is famous for its beautiful gardens and has hosted many concerts and events over the years.
  • St. Nicholas Church: This historic church is located in the Old Town and dates back to the 12th century. It has a beautiful, medieval-style interior and is worth a visit.

Natural Attractions of Stevenage: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Fairlands Valley Park: This large park is located in the north of Stevenage and has a wide range of activities, including fishing, boating, and hiking. It also has several playgrounds for children.
  • Chiltern Hills: These stunning hills are located just a few miles west of Stevenage and offer breathtaking views of the countryside. They are perfect for hiking, biking, and exploring on foot.
  • Shephalbury Park: This beautiful park is located in the east of Stevenage and has several miles of walking trails, as well as areas for picnicking and playing sports.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Stevenage

Stevenage has several great hiking and biking trails that are perfect for exploring the town and its surroundings. Here are some of the top routes:

  • Stevenage Outer Orbital Path (STOOP): This 27-mile circular walking and cycling route circles around Stevenage, passing through nature reserves, parks, and open countryside. It is well signposted and suitable for all abilities.
  • Fairlands Valley Park Loop: This 3-mile loop around Fairlands Valley Park is perfect for a short walk or bike ride. It takes you through beautiful wooded areas and along the lake.
  • Chiltern Hills Trail: This 52-mile trail runs through the stunning Chiltern Hills and is suitable for experienced hikers and mountain bikers. It offers breathtaking views of the countryside and passes through several quaint villages along the way.

Best Things to Do in Stevenage: Activities and Tours

There’s plenty to do in Stevenage, from exploring the town’s historic sights to trying out some adrenaline-fueled activities. Here are some of the best things to do in Stevenage:

  • Go Ape!](https://goape.co.uk/locations/hertfordshire) This outdoor adventure course offers zip lining, tree-top obstacles, and other challenges that are sure to get your heart racing.
  • Stevenage Leisure Park: This entertainment complex has a variety of activities, including a cinema, bowling alley, and arcade games.
  • Old Town Walking Tour: Take a guided walking tour of Stevenage’s Old Town and learn about the town’s history and architecture.

Week-Long Itinerary for Stevenage

Day 1: Explore Stevenage’s Old Town and visit the Stevenage Museum and St. Nicholas Church. Day 2: Take a hike or bike ride along the STOOP trail or explore one of Stevenage’s parks, such as Fairlands Valley Park. Day 3: Visit Knebworth House and its beautiful gardens. Day 4: Head to the Chiltern Hills for some more challenging hikes or mountain biking. Day 5: Check out Stevenage Leisure Park and see a movie or go bowling. Day 6: Visit Shephalbury Park and have a picnic or play some sports. Day 7: Take a day trip to nearby towns such as St. Albans or Cambridge.

Food and Drink Scene of Stevenage

Stevenage has a diverse food and drink scene, with a variety of restaurants and pubs to choose from. Here are some must-try dishes and drinks:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Roast Beef and Yorkshire Pudding: This traditional English dish is a must-try while in Stevenage. Many local pubs serve this hearty meal, which consists of roasted beef, gravy, and a crispy, bread-like pudding.
  • Fish and Chips: Another classic British dish, fish, and chips is an excellent choice for dinner. There are several fish and chip shops throughout Stevenage.
  • Cream Tea: Finish off your meal with a cream tea, a popular British tradition that consists of scones, clotted cream, and jam, served with a pot of tea.

Local Beverages

  • Real Ale: England is famous for its real ale, and Stevenage has several pubs that serve it. Be sure to try a pint of one of the many locally brewed beers, such as McMullen’s AK.
  • Gin and Tonic: Gin and tonics are a popular drink in England, and Stevenage has several bars and pubs that serve them.

Where to Stay in Stevenage: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Stevenage has a variety of accommodation options to suit all budgets and preferences. Here are some of the best places to stay in Stevenage:

  • Premier Inn Stevenage Central: This budget hotel is located in the town center and has comfortable rooms and an on-site restaurant.
  • Novotel Stevenage: This mid-range hotel is located just off the A1(M) and has spacious, modern rooms and an on-site restaurant and bar.
  • Archways Lodge Hotel: This guesthouse is located in the Old Town of Stevenage and has cozy rooms and a friendly, welcoming atmosphere.

What to Buy in Stevenage: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

If you’re looking for souvenirs or local products to bring home, Stevenage has plenty to offer. Here are some great ideas:

  • Stevenage Old Town: Take a stroll through Stevenage’s Old Town and pick up some souvenirs at the local gift shops. They offer unique gifts like handcrafted pottery and glassware, and locally produced honey and jam.
  • Knebworth House Gift Shop: If you visit Knebworth House, be sure to check out its gift shop, which sells souvenirs, books, and locally produced items.
  • Farmers’ Markets: Stevenage has several farmers’ markets that sell locally produced foodie favorites like chutneys, jams, cakes, and bread.

Local Culture and Customs of Stevenage

Stevenage has a rich history and cultural heritage, and visitors can learn more about it by exploring the town’s museums and historical sites. The people of Stevenage are friendly and welcoming, and visitors are sure to feel at home. English is the primary language spoken, and the currency used is the pound sterling (GBP). Tipping is customary in restaurants and pubs, but it’s not expected in other places.

Best Time to Visit Stevenage

The best time to visit Stevenage is during the summer months (June through August) when the weather is warm and sunny, and the parks and gardens are in full bloom. Avoid visiting during the winter months when the weather can be cold and rainy.

Do’s and Don’ts of Stevenage

Do:

  • Do explore Stevenage’s Old Town and its unique mix of historic architecture and modern buildings.
  • Do try some traditional English dishes like roast beef and Yorkshire pudding and fish and chips.
  • Do take advantage of Stevenage’s parks and scenic countryside by going on hikes or bike rides.

Don’t:

  • Don’t litter in parks or public places.
  • Don’t take photographs of people without their permission.
  • Don’t forget to carry an umbrella or raincoat during the rainy months.

Safety and Health

Safety in Stevenage

Stevenage is generally a safe town, but it’s always wise to take precautions. Keep an eye on your belongings, especially in crowded areas, and avoid walking alone at night. In case of emergency, dial 999 for police, ambulance, or fire services.

Health in Stevenage

Stevenage has several hospitals and medical facilities that offer high-quality care. Visitors are advised to carry travel insurance to cover any medical emergencies or accidents.

Getting to Stevenage: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Stevenage

The nearest international airport to Stevenage is London Luton Airport (LTN), which is about 14 miles away. London Heathrow Airport (LHR) and London City Airport (LCY) are also within easy reach.

Bus Stations Near Stevenage

Stevenage Bus Station is located in the town center and offers regular services to nearby towns and cities. The National Express and Stagecoach both operate services from the station.

Other Options

Stevenage is connected to London and other major towns and cities by rail. The town has two railway stations - Stevenage Railway Station and Knebworth Railway Station.

How to Get Around in Stevenage

Stevenage is a small town that can easily be explored on foot. There are also several bus routes that connect different parts of the town and its surroundings. Taxis are readily available throughout the town.

Where to Go Next from Stevenage: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Stevenage is well-connected to several nearby towns and cities, making it an excellent base for day trips. Here are some of the top destinations to visit:

  • St. Albans: This historic city is located about 11 miles south of Stevenage and is famous for its medieval cathedral and Roman ruins.
  • Cambridge: This beautiful university town is located about 33 miles north of Stevenage and is renowned for its elegant architecture and punting on the River Cam.
  • London: The capital city of England is located about 30 miles south of Stevenage and has a wealth of world-famous attractions, including Buckingham Palace, the Tower of London, and the British Museum.

Conclusion

Stevenage is a charming town that offers visitors a unique mix of history, natural beauty, and modern amenities. Whether you’re into hiking, biking, or exploring cultural attractions, Stevenage has something to offer everyone. Plan your trip now and discover all that this beautiful town has to offer.
