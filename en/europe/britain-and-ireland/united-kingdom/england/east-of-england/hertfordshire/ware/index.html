Introduction to Ware

Nestled in the county of Hertfordshire, Ware is a charming market town that boasts of a rich history and abundant natural beauty. With easy accessibility to London, it’s a popular destination for day-trippers and weekend getaways.

In this travel guide, we’ll take you on a tour of the best places to visit in Ware, the top things to do, the best places to eat and drink, the recommended accommodations, and the essential travel tips to make the most of your trip to Ware.

List of Places to Visit in Ware

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Ware Museum

The Ware Museum located in the High St shows the history of the town from the Romans to the present day and has exhibits focussed on the local clay production and the malting industry.

Scott’s Grotto

Scott’s Grotto is a complex of six interconnected chambers measuring 65 ft and is decorated with shells, flints, bones, and coloured glass that are arranged in intricate patterns.

Van Hage Garden Centre

The Van Hage Garden Centre is a must-visit destination for anyone who’s interested in gardening and nature. You can buy plants and gardening supplies, as well as wander around the beautiful gardens and watch birds and other wildlife.

Natural Attractions of Ware: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Amwell Nature Reserve

Amwell Nature Reserve is a haven for birdwatchers, with its large stretches of reedbeds that are home to various species of ducks, herons, and other water birds.

River Lea

The River Lea is a picturesque waterway that runs through Ware. You can take a leisurely stroll or a cycle ride along its banks and enjoy the beautiful views.

Chadwell Springs Golf Course

For those who enjoy golf, the Chadwell Springs Golf Course boasts of stunning views and provides an excellent opportunity to test your skills against the rolling countryside of Ware.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Ware

The Hertfordshire Way Long Distance Footpath passes through Ware, following the River Lea upstream as far as Hertford. Some of the other popular walking and cycling routes in Ware include the Cole Green Way, the Amwell Loop, and the New River Loop.

Best Things to Do in Ware: Activities and Tours

Riverside Miniature Railway

The Riverside Miniature Railway is a delightful attraction for families and train enthusiasts. The railway runs through the beautiful Riverside Park and is an excellent way to enjoy the scenery.

Priory Lido

Priory Lido is a heated outdoor swimming pool in the heart of Ware and an excellent place to cool down during warm summer days.

Week-Long Itinerary for Ware

Day 1: Visit the Ware Museum, have lunch at Horns Café and explore the local markets.

Day 2: Take a stroll along the River Lea and stop by Van Hage Garden Centre.

Day 3: Discover Scott’s Grotto and grab lunch at The Lemon Tree.

Day 4: Hike the Hertfordshire Way Long Distance Footpath

Day 5: Play a round of golf at the Chadwell Springs Golf Course.

Day 6: Enjoy the beautiful Amwell Nature Reserve

Day 7: Visit the Riverside Miniature Railway and enjoy some pool time at Priory Lido.

Food and Drink Scene of Ware

Must-Try Dishes

If you’re looking for local delicacies to try, make sure to have a taste of the traditional Hertfordshire Pudding, which is made with suet pastry and served with roasted meat, and the Hertfordshire Clanger, which is a pastry filled with meat, potatoes, and vegetables on one end, and a sweet filling on the other.

Local Beverages

Hertfordshire is known for its excellent real ales, and the local breweries here produce some of the best beers in the country, such as Benskins, McMullen’s, and Farr Brew.

Where to Stay in Ware: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Feathers Inn

The Feathers Inn is a charming guesthouse that’s located in the heart of Ware. It offers cozy rooms, a restaurant serving traditional British dishes, and a traditional pub.

Hanbury Manor Marriott Hotel and Country Club

For those looking for a luxury stay, the Hanbury Manor Marriott Hotel and Country Club offers top-quality accommodation, spa, and golf facilities in a stunning historic mansion.

What to Buy in Ware: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Hertfordshire is known for its pottery, and you can find some beautiful handmade ceramics at the local markets in Ware. Other products to bring home include chocolate from the Hertfordshire Chocolatier and preserves from the Hertfordshire Preserving Company.

Local Culture and Customs of Ware

Ware has a rich cultural heritage, and you’ll find a lot of arts and crafts fairs, music festivals, and theatre performances taking place throughout the year. The town is also famous for its connection to the malt industry, and local festivals such as the Ware Malt festival celebrate this heritage.

Best Time to Visit Ware

The best time to visit Ware is between late spring and early autumn when the weather is mild, and the natural beauty is at its best. The summer months are particularly busy with events and festivals, so plan your trip accordingly.

Do’s and Don’ts of Ware

Do:

  • Do visit the local markets for souvenirs and local delicacies.
  • Do take a walk along the River Lea for stunning views.
  • Do try the local beer and traditional Hertfordshire dishes.

Don’t:

  • Don’t litter! Ware prides itself on its clean, green environment.
  • Don’t forget to carry a raincoat or umbrella since rain is common in the UK.
  • Don’t miss out on the local heritage trails and cultural events.

Safety and Health

Safety in Ware

Ware is a safe town to visit, but like anywhere, keep a watchful eye on your belongings in crowded places to avoid pickpocketing.

Health in Ware

Health services in Ware are of a high standard, and you’ll find plenty of medical facilities and pharmacies in the town if you need assistance.

Getting to Ware: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Ware

The nearest international airport to Ware is London Stansted Airport, which is around 24 miles away from the town.

Bus Stations Near Ware

Ware has a central bus station that connects to various destinations in Hertfordshire and London.

Other Options

You can reach Ware easily by train from London, with regular trains running from Liverpool Street Station to Ware Station.

How to Get Around in Ware

The best way to get around Ware is on foot, as the town is small and easy to navigate. You can also rent a bike from the local cycle hire stations or hop on a local bus to explore the surrounding areas.

Where to Go Next from Ware: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Some of the popular nearby destinations include:

  • Hertford, a charming town with a castle and excellent riverside walks.
  • Stevenage, a thriving new town with museums, art galleries, and shopping centers.
  • St Albans, an ancient Roman city with historic attractions such as the St Albans Cathedral and the Verulamium Museum.

Conclusion

Ware is a delightful market town that’s refreshingly free of crowds and tourists. With an abundance of natural beauty, rich history, and vibrant cultural scene, it’s a destination that’s well worth a visit. Whether you want to explore the cultural heritage and natural beauty or indulge in food and drink, Ware has something for everyone. With this travel guide, you’re all set to explore the best of Ware, England!
