Introduction to Hemel Hempstead

Hemel Hempstead is a charming town in Hertfordshire, East of England. It boasts a rich history that dates back to the Roman era, and today it’s a bustling hub for outdoor enthusiasts and history buffs alike. Hemel Hempstead’s well-preserved old town boasts an impressive array of architectural styles ranging from Tudor to Georgian, and the city’s cultural vibe is evident in its lively events calendar. Whether you’re looking for family-friendly activities, outdoor adventures, or a romantic getaway, Hemel Hempstead has got you covered.

List of Places to Visit in Hemel Hempstead

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

1. The Old Town Hemel Hempstead’s charming old town is a must-visit for anyone interested in history or architecture. The town has a wealth of unique buildings, with styles ranging from Tudor to Georgian.

2. St Mary’s Church St. Mary’s Church, situated in the old town, is one of the prettiest churches in Hertfordshire and is well worth a visit.

3. Nash Mills Nash Mills is an early mill-site museum and dates back to around 1800 when it was built to be a factory for spinning and weaving silk on a large scale. It is open to visitors on limited days, so be sure to check the website before visiting.

Natural Attractions of Hemel Hempstead: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

1. Ashridge Estate Ashridge Estate is one of the most popular outdoor destinations for both locals and tourists. It offers stunning views of the Chiltern Hills, walking trails, picnic spots, and cycling routes. If you’re lucky, you may even spot deer, red kites, or other wildlife.

2. Gadebridge Park Gadebridge Park is a lovely green space and a popular place for picnics, dog-walking, and jogging. It is also home to Hemel’s annual carnival.

3. Boxmoor Boxmoor is a picturesque area that locals love. It’s a great place for hiking and picnicking, and there are old pubs and historical buildings to discover along the way.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Hemel Hempstead

Hemel Hempstead is a fantastic destination for outdoor enthusiasts, and there are plenty of hiking and biking routes to explore.

1. The Chiltern Way The Chiltern Way is a long-distance footpath that passes through Hemel Hempstead and offers stunning scenery of the Chiltern Hills.

2. Bovingdon Circular Trail The Bovingdon Circular Trail is a popular 4.6-mile walking route that takes you through forests, farmland, and charming villages.

3. Hemel Hempstead Circular Trail The Hemel Hempstead Circular Trail is a 12-mile circular walking trail that takes hikers through Ashridge Estate and the Gade Valley.

Best Things to Do in Hemel Hempstead: Activities and Tours

1. St Albans Abbey St Albans Abbey is only a short distance from Hemel Hempstead and offers a fascinating glimpse into the city’s rich history. The abbey dates back to AD 793 and was once the site of the first Christian martyrdom in Britain.

2. The Snow Centre The Snow Centre is the perfect place to go skiing and snowboarding, no matter your skill level. The centre boasts a 160-metre slope, three ski lifts, and plenty of on-site amenities.

3. Whipsnade Zoo Whipsnade Zoo is the UK’s largest zoo and is home to over 3,800 animals. Visitors can explore a variety of habitats, including rhino, tigers, cheetahs, and more.

Food and Drink Scene of Hemel Hempstead

Must-Try Dishes

1. Hertfordshire Sausage The Hertfordshire Sausage is a local delicacy that foodies must try. It’s made from locally-sourced pork and is seasoned with herbs and spices.

2. Cream Tea Cream tea, a light meal that includes scones, clotted cream, and jam, is a popular English treat that you can enjoy in Hemel Hempstead’s tea rooms and cafes.

3. Fish and Chips Fish and chips are a classic British dish that can be enjoyed in Hemel Hempstead’s pubs and restaurants.

Local Beverages

1. Real Ale Real ale is a popular drink in Hemel Hempstead, and many of the local pubs serve it on tap. Be sure to try something from nearby Tring Brewery.

2. Cider Cider is a refreshing drink that’s perfect for summer. You can find a range of ciders, including many locally-produced varieties, at bars and pubs throughout Hemel Hempstead.

3. Tea Hemel Hempstead is a great place to drink tea, with many cafes and tea shops offering unique blends and locally-sourced ingredients.

Where to Stay in Hemel Hempstead: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

1. Holiday Inn Hemel Hempstead This comfortable hotel is situated in the city centre and boasts modern amenities and excellent service.

2. Premier Inn Hemel Hempstead Central The Premier Inn Hemel Hempstead Central is located in the heart of the city and offers comfortable rooms at affordable prices.

3. The Olde Kings Arms The Olde Kings Arms is a historic guesthouse located in the charming old town, and boasts a warm atmosphere and comfortable rooms.

What to Buy in Hemel Hempstead: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

1. Local Cheese Local cheeses are a great souvenir to bring home from Hemel Hempstead. Chesham-based Aubrey’s Traditional Creations offers a range of award-winning cheeses.

2. Pickles Pickles make great gifts and souvenirs, and Hemel Hempstead has plenty of local options. The Tartan Kitchen, based in Hemel Hempstead, offers a range of pickles and chutneys.

3. Local Artisan Goods If you’re looking for unique, locally-made souvenirs, check out the Hemel Hempstead Artisan Market. The market takes place once a month and features a variety of locally-produced goods, including food, crafts, and home decor.

Local Culture and Customs of Hemel Hempstead

Hemel Hempstead residents are known for their friendly, welcoming nature. Visitors to the town should be respectful of local customs and etiquette, such as saying “please” and “thank you.”

Best Time to Visit Hemel Hempstead

The best time to visit Hemel Hempstead is during the summer months (June to September), when the weather is warm and sunny and there are plenty of outdoor events and festivals to enjoy. Spring and fall can also be great times to visit, with mild weather and fewer crowds.

Safety and Health

Safety in Hemel Hempstead

Hemel Hempstead is a safe city, and visitors should have no problems walking around during the day or at night. As with any area, it’s important to keep an eye on your belongings and be aware of your surroundings.

Health in Hemel Hempstead

There are several hospitals and medical centres in Hemel Hempstead, so visitors should have no problems receiving medical care if needed.

Where to Go Next from Hemel Hempstead: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

St Albans

St Albans is a beautiful city that’s only a short distance from Hemel Hempstead. It’s known for its historic Roman ruins, stunning cathedral, and vibrant culture.

Dunstable Downs is a stunning nature reserve that’s perfect for hiking, biking, and outdoor activities.

Knebworth House

Knebworth House is a historic stately home that’s open to visitors. It boasts beautiful gardens, a dinosaur trail, and stunning architecture.
