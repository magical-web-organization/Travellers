Introduction to Berkhamsted

Berkhamsted is a charming market town located in the county of Hertfordshire in the East of England. Its rich history dates back to the Saxon era, with evidence of human settlement from the Neolithic period. Berkhamsted has a lot to offer its visitors from an idyllic countryside ambiance, unique heritage features, and a welcoming community.

List of Places to Visit in Berkhamsted

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Berkhamsted Castle: This famous castle holds tales from the Norman Conquest in 1066 to the dissolution of the monasteries in the 16th century.
  • The Rex Cinema: A classic 1930s cinema with a rich history and beautiful architecture.
  • Berkhamsted Local History and Museum Society: Small but fascinating museum showcasing the town’s heritage and history.
  • St Peter’s Church: A medieval church with beautiful stained glass windows, fine woodwork, and an 18th-century organ.

Natural Attractions of Berkhamsted: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • Ashridge Estate: A vast natural area of woods and rolling Chiltern hills, perfect for hiking, running, cycling and wildlife spotting.
  • Grand Union Canal: A canal running through Hertfordshire with scenic walks alongside the water.
  • Berkhamsted Common: An ancient woodland with beautiful views and paths for hiking and biking.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Berkhamsted

  • Berkhamsted Circular Walk: A 17-mile trail starting and ending in the town center, leading through the countryside, canal, and castle.
  • Ashridge Estate Trails: Several well-signed walking and biking trails with different lengths and levels, including a route up to the highest point in the Chilterns.

Best Things to Do in Berkhamsted: Activities and Tours

  • Berkhamsted Food & Drink Festival: A yearly event showcasing local produce, street food, crafts, and music.
  • Canal Boat Hire: Rent a canal boat and explore the Grand Union Canal at your own pace.
  • Berkhamsted Artisan Market: Visit the market on the third Sunday of the month, featuring local artists, makers, and independent businesses.
  • Hiring a Bike: Rent a bike and explore the local countryside and canal paths.

Food and Drink Scene of Berkhamsted

Must-Try Dishes

  • Hertfordshire Puddingstone: A type of bread pudding made with local ale, fruit, and spices.
  • Berkhamsted Beer: A locally brewed beer with a unique hoppy flavor and rich color.
  • Cream Tea: Scones, clotted cream, and jam with a cup of tea.

Local Beverages

  • Hertfordshire Vineyards: Explore local vineyards and try the different varieties of English wine.
  • Berkhamsted Brewery: Visit the brewery and taste the different beers made from locally sourced ingredients.

Where to Stay in Berkhamsted: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  • The Gatsby: A boutique hotel with luxurious rooms and a champagne bar.
  • Travelodge Berkhamsted: Affordable hotel near the town center.
  • The Pennyfarthing Hotel: Guesthouse located in an 18th century building.

What to Buy in Berkhamsted: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  • English tea: Stock up on the different varieties of loose leaf or tea bags.
  • Handmade Crafts: Browse the artisan stalls and shops for handmade jewelry, pottery, and paintings.
  • Local Food Produce: Take home some locally made cheeses, chutneys, and jams.

Local Culture and Customs of Berkhamsted

The people of Berkhamsted have preserved the town’s history and heritage through various events, traditional customs, and festivals. Visitors should be respectful of the town’s culture and engage with the locals and attend the various celebrations and ceremonies.

Best Time to Visit Berkhamsted

The ideal time to visit Berkhamsted is during the summer months (June-August) when the weather is warm and sunny, and the countryside is green and in full bloom. However, the town is beautiful all year round, with visitors enjoying the different seasons.

Do’s and Don’ts of Berkhamsted

Do:

  • Respect the natural landscapes and wildlife.
  • Take part in the local events and festivals.
  • Wander the independent shops and businesses.
  • Be courteous and polite to the locals.

Don’t:

  • Litter or disturb the natural habitats.
  • Disrespect the historic buildings and structures.
  • Be excessively loud or rowdy in public areas.

Safety and Health

Safety in Berkhamsted

Berkhamsted is generally a safe town, with low crime levels. However, visitors should take the necessary precautions, such as being mindful of valuables, and avoiding secluded areas at night.

Health in Berkhamsted

Berkhamsted has several health centers and clinics providing healthcare services to visitors. Visitors are advised to have comprehensive travel insurance that covers any medical emergencies.

Getting to Berkhamsted: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Berkhamsted

  • London Luton Airport: 20 miles northeast of Berkhamsted.
  • Heathrow Airport: 30 miles southeast of Berkhamsted.
  • London Stansted Airport: 50 miles northeast of Berkhamsted.

Bus Stations Near Berkhamsted

  • Berkhamsted Bus Station: Located in the town center with several routes servicing the area.
  • Hemel Hempstead Bus Station: Located 2 miles south of Berkhamsted with more extensive routes servicing the area.

Other Options

  • Train: Berkhamsted Station provides train services to London Euston and other towns in the area.
  • Taxi: Several local taxi companies providing transport to and from the town.

How to Get Around in Berkhamsted

The town center is easily walkable, and visitors can cycle or rent a bike to explore the surrounding areas. Taxis and buses are also available for those who prefer not to walk or cycle.

Where to Go Next from Berkhamsted: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • Tring: A charming market town with a museum, nature reserve and a traditional farmers’ market.
  • St Albans: A historic city with an impressive cathedral, Roman ruins, and a buzzing food and drink scene.
  • Chiltern Hills: An area of outstanding natural beauty with sweeping views of green hills, woods, and fields.

Conclusion

Berkhamsted is a picturesque market town with a rich heritage and stunning natural beauty. The town has something for everyone, from cultural and historical attractions to outdoor activities and entertainment. Plan your visit to discover the best of Hertfordshire and immerse yourself in friendly local culture.
