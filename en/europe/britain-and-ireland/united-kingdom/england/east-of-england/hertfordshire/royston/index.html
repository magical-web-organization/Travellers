Royston, a humble town situated in the heart of Hertfordshire, is a hidden gem that boasts beautiful countryside landscapes, rich history, and quaint charm. Royston’s strategic location, surrounded by rolling hills, makes it an ideal destination for outdoor enthusiasts who relish the peacefulness of the countryside. The town is home to various exciting attractions, including museums, historical sites, hiking and biking trails, and local breweries, that cater to different tastes and interests. This comprehensive guide will help you navigate Royston like a pro, from the places to visit, food to eat, and where to stay.

Introduction to Royston

Royston is a small town in Hertfordshire, located in the East of England. The town has a rich history that dates back to Roman times and is known for its iconic cave churches, which were built into the hills of Royston. The town is also known for its exceptional outdoor scenery, from rolling hills to lush green forests. Royston is the perfect getaway destination if you want to escape the hustle and bustle of a big city and immerse yourself in the countryside charm.

List of Places to Visit in Royston

There are numerous places to explore in Royston, including museums, historical landmarks, hiking trails, and breweries. Here are some of the top attractions:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Royston has a rich history that is ingrained into the town’s culture and traditions. One of the must-visit places is Royston Cave, an underground chamber dating back to the 13th century, housing religious carvings, and symbols that still leave visitors wondering about its purpose today. Another site that’s worth a visit is the Royston Museum; it showcases local artifacts that trace back to the town’s history.

Natural Attractions of Royston: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

For outdoor enthusiasts, Royston is an ideal destination to explore the beautiful countryside. With its scenic views and picturesque landscapes, the town offers numerous hiking and biking trails for visitors to experience. One of the most popular trails is the Therfield Heath and the Nature Reserve, dotted with wildflowers and wildlife, providing the perfect escape for a nature lover.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Royston

Royston has an extensive network of trails that lead to some of the most spectacular beauty spots in the region. Here are some of the popular routes:

  • The Royston to Cambridge Greenway is an 11-mile route that leads visitors through breathtaking landscapes, nature reserves, and banks of River Cam
  • The Icknield Way Trail, which navigates through the Chiltern Hills and provides stunning views of the surrounding countryside while tracing the Bronze Age route
  • The King James Way, a circular trail tracing the history of King James I, weaving around Royston, through villages and churches

Best Things to Do in Royston: Activities and Tours

Royston offers an array of activities for visitors to engage in, catering to different interests. Some of the recommended tours include the Royston Cave Tour, which provides historical insights into the underground cave, and the Three Hills Brewery Tour, which gives visitors an opportunity to taste the town’s local brews.

Week-Long Itinerary for Royston

Here is a suggested itinerary for those who wish to make the most out of their one-week trip to Royston:

Day 1: Explore the town’s center, visit Royston Museum

Day 2: Take a hike to Therfield Heath & Nature Reserve

Day 3: Visit Wimpole Hall, a magnificent country house and estate

Day 4: Go on a brewery tour at Three Hills Brewery

Day 5: Take a scenic bike ride to the nearby village of Meldreth and enjoy a picnic at the village green.

Day 6: Experience local cuisine and visit the town’s farmers’ market

Day 7: Visit Cambridge Greenway

Food and Drink Scene of Royston

Royston has a vibrant food and drink scene with numerous restaurants, cafes, and pubs serving delectable food. Here are some must-try dishes:

Must-Try Dishes

  • Bangers and Mash
  • Fish and Chips
  • Sunday Roast

Local Beverages

Royston has several breweries that serve locally brewed beers, ciders, and spirits. Three Hills Brewery is popular for its wide range of craft beers, while the Cambridge Cider Company offers lovely fermented apple ciders made from locally sourced ingredients.

Where to Stay in Royston: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Royston offers various accommodation options, ranging from upscale hotels to cozy guesthouses. Here are some of the recommended places to stay:

  • The Old Bull Inn - a quintessential country pub with stylish and comfortable accommodations
  • Banyers House - a luxury hotel in Royston town center
  • Templars Hotel and Restaurant - a boutique hotel set within beautiful gardens, with a highly rated restaurant

What to Buy in Royston: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

If you’re looking to take home some souvenirs for your loved ones, Royston boasts a range of local products that highlight the town’s charm and history. These include:

  • Handmade Pottery from Royston Pottery
  • A bottle of local beer or cider from Three Hills Brewery or Cambridge Cider Company

Local Culture and Customs of Royston

Royston is a small town with warm, friendly people and a rich culture. Visitors should be respectful of the customs and traditions of the town, which include:

  • Usage of “please” and “thank you”
  • Showing appreciation to hosts and locals

Best Time to Visit Royston

Royston is a year-round destination; however, summer and autumn offer the best weather and landscape for outdoor activities.

Do’s and Don’ts of Royston

Do:

  • Respect local customs and traditions
  • Try the local beer and ciders
  • Take pictures of the beautiful countryside scenery

Don’t:

  • Litter or damage natural habitats
  • Be noisy, especially at night
  • Leave the town without trying local cuisine

Safety and Health

Safety in Royston

Royston is generally a safe town, with low crime rates. However, visitors should remain vigilant and take necessary precautions, especially when exploring the town at night.

Health in Royston

Royston has a National Health Service (NHS) health center and several pharmacies on the high street. Visitors should ensure they have comprehensive travel insurance before visiting Royston.

Getting to Royston: Transportation Options and Logistics

Royston is accessible via public transport and private car. Here are some ways to get to Royston:

Airports Near Royston

  • London Luton Airport - 27 miles away
  • London Stansted Airport - 19 miles away

Bus Stations Near Royston

The town has a fast, and timely bus service connected to several neighboring towns such as Cambridge, Letchworth, and Stevenage, administered by the local bus company “Centrebus.”

Other Options

There are several car rental companies near Royston, allowing visitors to access the town and surrounding areas at their leisure.

How to Get Around in Royston

Royston is a small town that is easily accessible on foot. However, visitors can also explore the town’s surrounding areas by bus, taxi, or rental car.

Where to Go Next from Royston: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Royston is ideally located, offering visitors the opportunity to explore several neighboring towns and cities. Some of the top destinations from Royston include:

  • Hitchin, a historic market town with fantastic architecture
  • Cambridge, a university city famous for its historic buildings and museums
  • London, the capital city of England, and one of the world’s greatest cities.

Conclusion

Royston is a hidden gem that offers visitors a chance to experience the charm of a small town with the beauty of the surrounding countryside, making it an ideal getaway destination for those looking to escape the big city. From the natural scenery to the historical landmarks and delicious cuisine, Royston has something for every visitor.
