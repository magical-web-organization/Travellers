Introduction to Watford

Located in Hertfordshire, East of England, Watford lies at the heart of the Greater London Urban Area, making it a convenient base for exploring the surrounding towns and cities. Watford has a rich history and an abundance of cultural and natural attractions, making it an exciting destination for travelers. In this guide, we’ll take you through the best places to visit, things to do, food and drink scene, where to stay, transportation, and more.

List of Places to Visit in Watford

Watford has a variety of places that visitors can explore. Here are a few not-to-be-missed sites:

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  • Watford Museum: Established in 1981, this museum showcases the town’s history, including its industrial past, notable residents, and local culture.
  • St. Mary’s Church: Founded in the 12th century, this church has undergone numerous restorations and additions over the centuries. The church’s stunning architecture and peaceful atmosphere make it an ideal spot to learn about the town’s religious heritage.
  • Cassiobury Park: Once owned by the Earls of Essex, this former estate turned public park features stunning landscaped gardens, ornamental lakes, and woodland areas.

Natural Attractions of Watford: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  • The Grand Union Canal: A picturesque waterway that runs through the town, offering visitors the opportunity to explore the canal network through walks or canal boat rides.
  • West Herts Golf Course: Ideal for its stunning views of the Chilterns, the course is open to both beginners and experienced players.
  • Cassiobury Farm and Fishery: This family-friendly attraction features a farm, fishing pond, nature reserve and a lakeside café.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Watford

For those who love the great outdoors, Watford offers a variety of hiking and biking routes to explore. Some popular ones include:

  • The Ebury Way: This 3-mile linear park is a popular route for walkers and cyclists. It follows the route of the former Watford to Rickmansworth Railway Line and is home to a variety of wildlife.
  • Cassiobury Park Nature Trail: This 1.5-mile trail takes visitors through a stunning nature reserve that features an ornamental waterfall, bird hide and boardwalks over the River Gade.

Best Things to Do in Watford: Activities and Tours

Apart from the above sites, here are a few other things visitors can do in Watford:

  • Warner Bros. Studio Tour: Harry Potter fans will love this behind-the-scenes tour of the making of the films. Explore the sets, costumes, props and learn about the special effects used in the films.
  • Watford Palace Theatre: This art deco performance venue offers a programme of plays, musicals, and events throughout the year, making it the perfect spot for a night out.
  • The Grove: This luxurious 18th-century country estate features a top-rated spa, golf course, and restaurants.

Week-Long Itinerary for Watford

Day 1: Visit Watford Museum, St. Mary’s Church, and Cassiobury Park.

Day 2: Take a boat trip on the Grand Union Canal and explore nearby towns like Rickmansworth and Hemel Hempstead.

Day 3: Visit the Harry Potter Studio Tour, located just a 15-minute drive outside of Watford

Day 4: Take a nature trail walk through Cassiobury Park or visit nearby Whippendell Woods.

Day 5: Play a round of golf at West Herts Golf Course, followed by afternoon tea at The Grove.

Day 6: Catch a performance at Watford Palace Theatre or a film at Vue Cinema.

Day 7: Round off your stay with a spa day at The Grove or a meal at one of the renowned local restaurants.

Food and Drink Scene of Watford

Watford offers a vibrant food and drink scene with an array of eateries catering to all tastes.

Must-Try Dishes

  • Hertfordshire Pudding: A traditional savory dish made of suet pastry filled with vegetables, meat and gravy.
  • Sticky Toffee Pudding: A classic English dessert made of moist sponge cake covered in toffee sauce.
  • Fish and Chips: A British institution, fish and chips is a must-try in Watford.

Local Beverages

  • Real Ale: Watford has a strong tradition of brewing local ales, with popular varieties including Buntingford and Tring.
  • Gin: With an abundance of local distilleries, gin is a popular spirit in the town.

Where to Stay in Watford: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

Watford has a variety of accommodation options to suit all budgets and tastes. Here are a few that come recommended:

  • The Grove: This 18th-century country estate offers luxury accommodation, top-rated spa, golf course, and restaurants.
  • Jurys Inn: Located in the heart of Watford, this chain hotel offers modern rooms and easy access to the town’s attractions.
  • YHA: This affordable accommodation option offers both private and shared rooms in a historic building.

What to Buy in Watford: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

From artisanal foods to unique souvenirs, Watford offers plenty of options for those looking to take back a piece of the town with them. Here are a few ideas:

  • Local Ale: Try some unique local ales to take back home.
  • Hertfordshire Pudding Mix: If you enjoyed the traditional savory dish during your stay, pick up a mix to make at home.
  • Harry Potter Souvenirs: For Harry Potter fans, a souvenir from the studios or shops is a must-buy.

Local Culture and Customs of Watford

Watford has a diverse population, with people from different ethnic backgrounds. The town celebrates various festivals and has a thriving arts and culture scene, making it a perfect blend of traditional and modern British culture.

Best Time to Visit Watford

The best time to visit Watford is during the summer months of June to August, when the weather is warm and dry. However, the town has plenty of indoor attractions to enjoy year-round.

Do’s and Don’ts of Watford

Do:

  • Do visit the Warner Bros. Studio Tour if you’re a Harry Potter fan.
  • Do take the boat trip on the Grand Union Canal.
  • Do try some of the local ales.

Don’t:

  • Don’t miss out on trying the Hertfordshire Pudding.
  • Don’t leave without visiting Cassiobury Park for a nature trail walk.

Safety and Health

Safety in Watford

Watford is a relatively safe town, but as with any town, visitors should take basic precautions, such as avoiding unlit areas at night and keeping valuables safe.

Health in Watford

Watford has several medical facilities, including the The Watford General Hospital, which offers 24/7 emergency care.

Getting to Watford: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Watford

The nearest airport to Watford is London Luton Airport, which is approximately 20 miles away.

Bus Stations Near Watford

Watford has a central bus station located in the town center, offering connections to several local and regional destinations.

Other Options

Watford’s main train station links the town to London Euston station, making it an easily accessible base for exploring the capital. Alternatively, visitors can reach Watford by car via the M1 and M25 motorways.

How to Get Around in Watford

Watford has an extensive public transport network, including buses and trains. Walking and cycling are also popular options for getting around the town.

Where to Go Next from Watford: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  • London: Take a train to London, which can be reached in under 20 minutes, to explore the UK’s vibrant capital city.

  • St Albans: A historic city with a stunning cathedral, picturesque streets, and a variety of shops and restaurants.

Conclusion

Watford is a vibrant and exciting hub in the heart of the East of England. With its rich history, cultural and natural attractions, and diverse food and drink scene, it’s a must-visit destination for any traveler. Plan your trip with our comprehensive guide and discover all that Watford has to offer.
