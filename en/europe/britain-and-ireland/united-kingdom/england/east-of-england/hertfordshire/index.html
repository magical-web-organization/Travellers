Introduction to Hertfordshire

Located in the east of England, Hertfordshire is a county renowned for its beautiful countryside, rich history, and thriving cultural scene. Whether you’re looking to explore museums and historical landmarks, enjoy outdoor adventures and scenic views, or indulge in local food and drink, Hertfordshire has something for everyone.

With easy access to London and a range of transportation options, Hertfordshire is an ideal destination for a weekend getaway or a longer vacation. Read on to discover the best places to visit, eat, stay, and more in Hertfordshire.

List of Places to Visit in Hertfordshire

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

Hertfordshire is home to a wealth of museums and landmarks that tell the story of the county’s rich history and culture. Here are some must-visit attractions:

  • St Albans Cathedral: This medieval cathedral is one of the most impressive landmarks in Hertfordshire and is known for its stunning architecture and intricate carvings.
  • Knebworth House: This stately home has played host to countless iconic events, from Queen concerts to Hollywood movie shoots, and is a must-visit for history buffs and music fans.
  • Verulamium Museum: The museum showcases the Roman history of St Albans and features an impressive collection of artifacts, including mosaics, coins, and pottery.
  • Hatfield House: This magnificent Tudor palace is set within stunning parkland and is home to unique art collections and charming gardens.
  • The Henry Moore Studios and Gardens: Fans of contemporary art will love exploring the works of Henry Moore, one of Britain’s most famous sculptors.

Natural Attractions of Hertfordshire: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

Hertfordshire’s rolling countryside and picturesque villages make it an ideal destination for outdoor enthusiasts. Here are some of the best outdoor attractions to visit:

  • Ashridge Estate: This stunning woodland area is perfect for hiking and exploring, with miles of footpaths and breathtaking views stretching over the Chiltern Hills.
  • Lee Valley Regional Park: This 26-mile long park offers everything from cycling and boating to fishing and camping.
  • Hitchin Lavender: This lavender field in Hitchin is a spectacular sight during the summer months, with vibrant purple blooms as far as the eye can see.
  • The National Trust’s Shaw’s Corner: This beautiful house and garden was once home to playwright George Bernard Shaw and is a serene spot for a stroll.
  • Paradise Wildlife Park: Home to over 400 animals, Paradise Wildlife Park is a fun day out for all ages.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Hertfordshire

Hertfordshire is a haven for hikers and cyclists, with a variety of scenic routes to explore. Here are some of the best:

  • The Hertfordshire Way: This 195-mile long trail takes you through charming villages, rolling countryside, and historic landmarks.
  • The Alban Way: This former railway line is now a dedicated cycle and walking route, stretching from St Albans to Hatfield.
  • Ashridge Estate Trail: This 5.5-mile trail takes you through some of the most stunning landscapes in the Chiltern Hills.

Best Things to Do in Hertfordshire: Activities and Tours

Whether you’re looking for a fun activity with family and friends or a unique way to explore the county, Hertfordshire has plenty of options. Here are some of the best:

  • Harry Potter Studio Tour: This popular attraction takes you behind the scenes of the Harry Potter movies and is a must-visit for movie fans and aspiring wizards alike.
  • The Snow Centre: This indoor ski and snowboard center is perfect for a day of snow sports, no matter what the weather is like outside.
  • Segway Adventures: Take a tour of the woods in style with a Segway adventure, giving you a unique perspective of Hertfordshire’s natural landscapes.
  • Old Gorhambury House: This historic Tudor building is now a popular events and wedding venue, but you can also tour the house and learn about its fascinating history.
  • Hatfield Park Farm: With animals to feed, tractor rides to enjoy and play areas to explore, Hatfield Park Farm is the perfect day out for families.

Week-Long Itinerary for Hertfordshire

To make the most of your visit to Hertfordshire, here’s a one-week itinerary:

  • Day 1: St Albans Cathedral and Verulamium Museum.
  • Day 2: Knebworth House and Henry Moore Studios and Gardens.
  • Day 3: Hitchin Lavender and Paradise Wildlife Park.
  • Day 4: The Hertfordshire Way hiking trail, followed by dinner in one of the county’s charming villages.
  • Day 5: Harry Potter Studio Tour.
  • Day 6: Segway Adventure and a picnic in Ashridge Estate.
  • Day 7: Hatfield House and Hatfield Park Farm.

Food and Drink Scene of Hertfordshire

Must-Try Dishes

Hertfordshire’s location in the southeast of England means it has a range of delicious local cuisine to try. Here are some must-try dishes:

  • Hertfordshire Clangers: These unique pastry pockets are filled with savory meat at one end and sweet jam at the other.
  • Real ale: Hertfordshire is home to many local breweries, meaning there’s a range of delicious ales to try.
  • Victoria Sponge: Named after Queen Victoria herself, this classic sponge cake is a must-have with afternoon tea.

Local Beverages

There are plenty of local beverages to try in Hertfordshire, including:

  • Farr Brew Beer: This local brewery creates a range of craft beers, including pale ales and stouts.
  • Winbirri Vineyard Wine: With vineyards stretching across the county, there’s plenty of delicious wines to try in Hertfordshire.
  • Mocha Coffee: For coffee lovers, Mocha Coffee in Tring is a must-visit. Roasting their beans on-site, they have been named one of the best coffee shops in the UK.

Where to Stay in Hertfordshire: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

There are plenty of accommodation options across Hertfordshire, ranging from luxury hotels to budget hostels. Here are some top picks:

  • Sopwell House: This luxury hotel and spa is set within stunning grounds, offering a relaxing retreat from the hustle and bustle of the city.
  • Aubrey Park Hotel: This contemporary hotel is perfect for families and business travelers alike, with easy access to nearby attractions and conference facilities.
  • The Sun Hotel: Located in the heart of Hitchin, this charming hotel dates back to the 1500s and has retained many original features.
  • YHA St Albans: Perfect for budget travelers, this hostel offers comfortable accommodation in a central location.

What to Buy in Hertfordshire: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Hertfordshire is famous for its local produce and handmade goods, making it the perfect spot to pick up a souvenir. Here are some top picks:

  • Wallace Sewell Scarves: These handwoven scarves are created using natural fibers and come in a range of unique designs.
  • Rose Gold Jewellery: Hertfordshire-based designer Alison Newth creates beautiful rose gold jewelry using traditional techniques.
  • Local cheeses: Hertfordshire has a range of high-quality cheeses to try, including the soft and creamy Baron Bigod.

Local Culture and Customs of Hertfordshire

Hertfordshire is a county steeped in history and culture, with a range of customs and traditions unique to the area. Here are a few things to know:

  • Horn Dance: Every September in Abbots Bromley, the Horn Dance takes place - a traditional dance performed by six men carrying deer antlers.
  • Hertfordshire County Show: This two-day show takes place every May in Redbourn, celebrating all the best the county has to offer in terms of food, drink, and entertainment.
  • Morris Dancing: A traditional dance form popular in England, Morris Dancing is often performed in Hertfordshire at celebrations and events throughout the year.

Best Time to Visit Hertfordshire

The best time to visit Hertfordshire is during the spring and summer months, when the weather is warm and the countryside blooms with colorful flora. From April to September, there are also plenty of local events and festivals to enjoy.

Do’s and Don’ts of Hertfordshire

Do:

  • Do visit the local markets for unique handmade goods and local produce.
  • Do explore the countryside on foot or by bike.
  • Do try the local cuisine and drink.

Don’t:

  • Don’t miss out on the county’s rich history and culture.
  • Don’t forget to pack appropriate footwear and clothing for outdoor activities.
  • Don’t underestimate the traffic during peak times.

Safety and Health

Safety in Hertfordshire

Hertfordshire is generally a safe destination for travelers. However, it’s always wise to take precautions, such as staying in well-lit areas after dark and keeping an eye on your belongings in crowded places.

Health in Hertfordshire

There are no particular health concerns for visitors to Hertfordshire, but it’s always advisable to have appropriate travel insurance in case of illness or injury during your stay.

Getting to Hertfordshire: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Hertfordshire

Hertfordshire is within easy reach of several major airports, including London Luton, London Stansted, and London Heathrow.

Bus Stations Near Hertfordshire

Hertfordshire is well-served by public transport, with bus stations located in major towns and cities throughout the county.

Other Options

There are also several train stations throughout Hertfordshire, with connections to major cities and towns throughout the UK.

How to Get Around in Hertfordshire

Public transport is a convenient and affordable way to get around Hertfordshire, with frequent bus and train services available throughout the county. Taxis and car rental are also available for those who prefer to travel independently.

Where to Go Next from Hertfordshire: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

Hertfordshire’s convenient location means it’s easy to explore nearby destinations and attractions, including:

  • London: Just a short train journey away, the UK’s capital city offers a wealth of attractions, including museums, landmarks, and cultural events.
  • Cambridge: This iconic university town is known for its beautiful architecture and historic landmarks, including King’s College and the River Cam.
  • Oxford: Another famous university town, Oxford boasts stunning architecture, parks, and a thriving cultural scene.

Conclusion

Hertfordshire is a hidden gem in southeast England, offering a unique blend of history, culture, and natural beauty. With so much to see and do, it’s the perfect destination for a weekend break or longer vacation. Whatever your interests, you’re sure to find something to love in Hertfordshire.
