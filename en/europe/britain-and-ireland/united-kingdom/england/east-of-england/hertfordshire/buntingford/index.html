Introduction to Buntingford

Located in the picturesque county of Hertfordshire, Buntingford is a charming market town with a rich history dating back to the Roman period. This quaint town boasts an array of attractions, from ancient ruins to scenic countryside trails. Whether you’re looking to explore its cultural heritage or simply enjoy a relaxing retreat from the hustle and bustle of city life, Buntingford has something for everyone.

List of Places to Visit in Buntingford

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

  1. Buntingford’s Heritage Trails – Take a stroll through Buntingford’s historic streets and discover its rich heritage with the help of several heritage trails that showcase the town’s most important landmarks, including St. Peter’s Church and the former Truman’s Brewery.

  2. Wallington Hall and Gardens – Located just a short distance from the town centre, Wallington Hall and Gardens is a magnificent 18th-century building with beautifully landscaped gardens that offer breathtaking views of the Hertfordshire countryside.

  3. The Buntingford Almshouse Trust Museum – Housed in a beautifully preserved 17th-century building, this museum provides an insight into the life of the town’s residents during the past centuries.

Natural Attractions of Buntingford: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

  1. Puckeridge and Standon Walk – This scenic walk takes you through the rolling countryside surrounding Buntingford, offering stunning panoramic views along the way.

  2. Thorley Wash Nature Reserve – A peaceful wildlife haven, this nature reserve is home to a variety of rare bird species, including kingfishers, herons, and snipe.

  3. Stanstead Hall Gardens – This beautifully landscaped garden offers visitors a peaceful retreat from the hustle and bustle of town life.

Hiking/Biking Routes of Buntingford

  1. The Hertfordshire Way – This 194-kilometre-long trail takes you through some of the most scenic countryside in the county, including Buntingford and its surrounding areas.

  2. Buntingford Circular Walk – This moderate four-mile walk takes you through the town’s picturesque streets and surrounding countryside, offering stunning views of the Hertfordshire landscape.

  3. The National Cycle Route 12 – This 60-mile-long trail offers plenty of rural views and picturesque scenery, with Buntingford providing a perfect pit stop along the way.

Best Things to Do in Buntingford: Activities and Tours

  1. Buntingford Summer Carnival – Held annually in June or July, this vibrant carnival features a parade, live music, and plenty of food and drink stalls.

  2. The Buntingford Brewery Tour – Take a tour of the town’s award-winning brewery, Truman’s Brewery, to learn about the art and science of beer making.

  3. Buntingford Wine Festival – Held annually in September, this wine festival offers visitors a chance to sample some of the finest local wines and delicious cuisine.

Food and Drink Scene of Buntingford

Must-Try Dishes

  1. Buntingford Sausages – These delicious sausages are made locally and come in several flavours, including traditional pork and apple and spiced lamb.

  2. English Breakfast – Start your day like a local with a classic English breakfast, featuring bacon, eggs, sausages, baked beans, and black pudding.

  3. Potato and Leek Soup – A warming soup made with locally sourced ingredients, this dish is perfect for a cold winter day.

Local Beverages

  1. Buntingford Beer – Try the locally brewed ales from Buntingford’s renowned Truman Brewery, including the award-winning Swift IPA and Herts Hog IPA.

  2. Hertfordshire Wine – Sample the finest Hertfordshire wines at the annual Buntingford Wine Festival or stock up on local bottles at the town’s many wine shops.

Where to Stay in Buntingford: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

  1. The Crown – This charming pub and hotel is located in the heart of the town and offers cosy accommodation and delicious local cuisine.

  2. The Brewery Tap – Part of the town’s award-winning brewery, this elegant guesthouse offers luxury accommodation in a historic setting.

  3. The George Hotel – Located just a short distance from the town centre, The George Hotel is a charming boutique hotel with traditional English décor and modern amenities.

What to Buy in Buntingford: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

  1. Buntingford Tea Towel – These colourful tea towels feature illustrations of Buntingford’s most famous landmarks and are a perfect souvenir to bring back home.

  2. Local Cheese and Dairy Products – Sample some of the region’s finest artisan cheeses and other dairy products, including Buntingford’s famous gold-award-winning yoghurt.

  3. Bespoke Crafts and Gifts – Visit one of the many local craft shops or boutiques to discover unique, handmade gifts and souvenirs, from jewellery to pottery.

Local Culture and Customs of Buntingford

Buntingford has a long and rich history, dating back to Roman times, and this cultural heritage is proudly celebrated by its residents. The town is known for its friendly community spirit and lively festivals and events, including the annual summer carnival and the wine festival in September.

Best Time to Visit Buntingford

The best time to visit Buntingford depends on your preferences. The town experiences mild weather throughout the year, with warm summers and cooler winters. Those seeking outdoor adventures may prefer to visit in the summer months, while others may enjoy the cosy ambience of the town during the winter.

Do’s and Don’ts of Buntingford

Do:

  • Follow the town’s heritage trails to discover Buntingford’s rich history.
  • Try the locally brewed ales and wines.
  • Take advantage of the town’s beautiful countryside and scenic hiking trails.

Don’t:

  • Smoke in public spaces.
  • Litter or damage the town’s natural environment.
  • Ignore local customs and traditions.

Safety and Health

Safety in Buntingford

Buntingford is a safe town with low levels of crime. As with any town or city, however, visitors should take precautions, avoid leaving valuables unattended, and be aware of their surroundings when walking at night.

Health in Buntingford

Buntingford has several GP practices as well as a hospital, so visitors can rest assured that medical help is available in case of emergency.

Getting to Buntingford: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near Buntingford

Buntingford is situated in close proximity to several major airports, including London Stansted Airport (30 minutes by car) and London Luton Airport (45 minutes by car).

Bus Stations Near Buntingford

Buntingford has several bus stations located throughout the town, with regular services connecting to nearby villages and towns.

Other Options

Visitors can reach Buntingford via train to nearby towns such as Stevenage or Ware, with onward bus or taxi connections to the town itself. There are also several car rental companies located in the nearby towns.

How to Get Around in Buntingford

Buntingford is a small town, and most attractions and points of interest are within easy walking distance. Visitors can also hire bicycles or take taxis or local buses for short journeys around the town.

Where to Go Next from Buntingford: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

  1. Ware – Explore the charming historic town of Ware, just a short distance from Buntingford, with its beautiful river, antique shops, and excellent dining options.

  2. Hertford Castle – Located just a 20-minute drive from Buntingford, Hertford Castle is a magnificent fortress with over 900 years of history, including ties to royalty.

  3. Stansted Mountfitchet – This pretty village is home to a living history museum, Mountfitchet Castle, where visitors can experience what life was like in medieval England.

Conclusion

Whether you prefer to explore the town’s rich heritage or simply enjoy a peaceful retreat in the scenic Hertfordshire countryside, Buntingford has plenty to offer. With its friendly community, lively events, and charming surroundings, there’s never been a better time to discover this hidden gem in the heart of East England.
