Introduction to St Albans

St Albans is a charming city in Hertfordshire, East of England, located just 22 miles north of London. Known for its rich history, St Albans is home to a plethora of cultural and natural attractions that are sure to delight any visitor. From its ancient Roman origins to the modern-day cityscape, St Albans has something for every type of traveler.

Whether you’re interested in hiking and biking in the nearby countryside, exploring the city’s museums and historical sites, or just enjoying the food and drink scene, St Albans is a destination that should not be missed. In this comprehensive travel guide, we’ll explore the many attractions and activities that make St Albans a must-visit destination.

List of Places to Visit in St Albans

Culturally Important Attractions: Museums and Historical Places

St Albans has a rich history dating back over 2,000 years, and there are many cultural and historical attractions for visitors to explore.

St Albans Cathedral

One of the most famous landmarks in St Albans, the St Albans Cathedral has been a site of Christian worship for over 1,700 years. The cathedral is known for its impressive Gothic architecture, stunning stained glass windows, and rich history.

Verulamium Park

Verulamium Park is an expansive park that was once the site of a Roman city. Today, visitors can explore the remains of the ancient amphitheater, Roman walls, and hypocausts – an underground heating system used by the Romans.

Verulamium Museum

The Verulamium Museum houses a stunning collection of Roman artifacts, including mosaics, pottery, and jewelry. Visitors can also learn about the history of the town itself and its evolution over the centuries.

The Clock Tower

Located in the city center, the Clock Tower is a historic landmark that dates back to the 15th century. Visitors can climb to the top for stunning views of the city.

Natural Attractions of St Albans: Outdoor Adventures and Scenic Views

St Albans is surrounded by beautiful countryside, offering a range of outdoor activities for visitors to enjoy.

Heartwood Forest

Just a short drive from St Albans is Heartwood Forest, a beautiful woodland area that is perfect for hiking and picnicking. The forest is home to a range of wildlife, including deer and butterflies.

Redbournbury Mill

Redbournbury Mill is a restored watermill that dates back to the 18th century. Visitors can enjoy a guided tour of the mill and learn about the history of milling in the area.

Nomansland Common

Nomansland Common is a large nature reserve that is home to a variety of plant and animal species. Visitors can enjoy hiking, cycling, and birdwatching in this peaceful setting.

Hiking/Biking Routes of St Albans

St Albans is surrounded by picturesque countryside and offers many opportunities for hiking and biking enthusiasts. Here are some of the best routes to explore:

The Alban Way

The Alban Way is a 6.5-mile trail that follows an old railway line. The trail is perfect for walking, cycling, and jogging, and is suitable for all fitness levels.

The Nickey Line

The Nickey Line is a 7-mile trail that follows an old railway line from Hemel Hempstead to Harpenden. The trail offers stunning views of the surrounding countryside and is perfect for a leisurely bike ride.

The Ver-Colne Valley Walk

The Ver-Colne Valley Walk is a 9-mile trail that follows the River Ver and the River Colne. The trail starts in St Albans and finishes in Watford, offering stunning views of the river and surrounding countryside.

Best Things to Do in St Albans: Activities and Tours

In addition to the cultural and natural attractions, St Albans also offers a range of activities and tours for visitors to enjoy. Here are some of the best:

Food and Drink Tours

St Albans is known for its food and drink scene, and there are many tours that allow visitors to sample the best of the local cuisine. From chocolate and wine tastings to food market tours, there’s something for every taste.

Ghost Tours

For a spooky adventure, visitors can explore the city on a ghost tour. These tours take visitors to some of the most haunted sites in St Albans, sharing the history and ghost stories along the way.

Theatre Performances

The Alban Arena is a popular venue for theater performances, concerts, and comedy shows. Visitors can check the schedule to see what’s on during their visit.

Food and Drink Scene of St Albans

St Albans is known for its food and drink scene, with many local specialties and restaurants to enjoy.

Must-Try Dishes

Some of the local specialties to try include Stilton cheese made in the nearby village of Redbourn, and locally made sausages known as St Albans bangers.

Local Beverages

Local beverages to try include ale from the nearby Mcmullen Brewery, which has been brewing beer in Hertfordshire since 1827, and wines from the nearby vineyards.

Where to Stay in St Albans: Hotels, Hostels, and Guesthouses

St Albans offers a range of accommodation options to suit every budget and taste, including hotels, hostels, and guesthouses. Here are some suggestions:

The White Hart Hotel

Located in the heart of the city, The White Hart Hotel is a historic building that offers comfortable and stylish accommodation.

Premier Inn St Albans

The Premier Inn St Albans is a budget-friendly option that offers comfortable rooms and a convenient location close to the city center.

St Michael’s Manor Hotel

For a luxurious stay, St Michael’s Manor Hotel is a beautiful country house hotel located just outside the city center. The hotel offers stunning views of the gardens and lake, as well as an award-winning restaurant.

What to Buy in St Albans: Souvenirs and Local Products to Bring Home

Visitors to St Albans can take home a range of souvenirs and local products, including:

  • Locally made sausages known as St Albans bangers
  • Stilton cheese made in the nearby village of Redbourn
  • Wines from the nearby vineyards
  • Handmade chocolates from local chocolatiers

Local Culture and Customs of St Albans

Like many towns and cities in England, St Albans has a rich history and culture. Some local customs and traditions include:

  • Morris dancing, a traditional English folk dance
  • The St Albans International Organ Festival, which takes place every two years and attracts organ enthusiasts from around the world

Best Time to Visit St Albans

The best time to visit St Albans is during the summer months, from June to August, when the weather is mild and sunny. However, the city can get busy during peak season, so visitors should book accommodation and activities in advance.

Do’s and Don’ts of St Albans

Do:

  • Explore the city’s history and culture, including its Roman origins and medieval landmarks.
  • Sample the local food and drink scene, including St Albans bangers and locally made wine.
  • Take advantage of the nearby countryside, including Heartwood Forest and the Nickey Line.

Don’t:

  • Ignore the city’s rich history and cultural landmarks.
  • Visit during peak season without booking accommodation and activities in advance.
  • Miss out on the opportunity to sample some of the local specialties and products.

Safety and Health

Safety in St Albans

St Albans is a generally safe city, but visitors should still take standard precautions when traveling. Leave valuables in your hotel room, especially when visiting crowded tourist areas. When out at night, stick to well-lit areas and avoid walking alone.

Health in St Albans

There are no specific health risks in St Albans; however, visitors should still take standard precautions, such as washing hands regularly and staying hydrated during the summer months.

Getting to St Albans: Transportation Options and Logistics

Airports Near St Albans

St Albans is easily accessible from London’s major airports:

  • London Luton Airport is the closest airport to St Albans, located just 11 miles away.
  • London Heathrow and London Stansted are both located within 50 miles of St Albans.

Bus Stations Near St Albans

St Albans is well-connected by bus to London and other nearby towns and cities. The main bus station is located in the city center, providing easy access to nearby attractions.

Other Options

St Albans is also easily accessible by train, with direct services from London and other cities in the UK. The nearest train station to St Albans is St Albans City Station, located just a short walk from the city center.

How to Get Around in St Albans

St Albans is a relatively small city, and most attractions are within walking distance of each other. Buses and taxis are also available for those who prefer not to walk.

Where to Go Next from St Albans: Nearby Destinations and Excursions

St Albans is located within easy reach of other popular destinations, including:

  • London: Just a short train ride away, London offers endless opportunities for sightseeing and entertainment.

  • Hatfield House: A short drive away, Hatfield House is a stunning stately home with a rich history that dates back to the 17th century.

Conclusion

Whether you’re interested in history, culture, nature, food, or just relaxing, St Albans has something for everyone. With its beautiful surroundings, welcoming locals, and rich heritage, St Albans is a destination that should not be missed. Plan your itinerary, book your accommodation and activities, and get ready to explore this charming city in Hertfordshire.
